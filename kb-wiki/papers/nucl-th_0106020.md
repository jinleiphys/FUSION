# Spectroscopy with random and displaced random ensembles

> Due to the time reversal invariance of the angular momentum operator J^2, the average energies and variances at fixed J for random two-body Hamiltonians exhibit odd-even-J staggering, that may be especially strong for J=0. It is shown that upon ensemble averaging over random runs, this behaviour is reflected in the yrast states. Displaced (attractive) random ensembles lead to rotational spectra with strongly enhanced BE2 transitions for a certain class of model spaces. It is explained how to generalize these results to other forms of collectivity.

# Spectroscopy with random and displaced random ensembles

## Key claim

Time reversal invariance of the angular momentum operator J^2 is identified as the origin of odd-even staggering in average energies and variances at fixed J for random two-body Hamiltonians, and the attractive nature of the interaction provides a sufficient condition for BE2 enhancement associated with collective behavior; however, the authors note that time reversal invariance does not imply J=0 dominance, it only suggests its frequent observation.

## Method

The authors performed ensemble-averaged calculations using the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) derived from Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) Hamiltonians in the pf shell and a Δj=2 subspace (fp space), computing centroids and variances at fixed J for n=8 and n=9 particles. For displaced ensembles (DTBRE), they introduced a constant displacement c of all matrix elements to simulate attractive forces, running 960 fp-space calculations for four combinations of particle number and isospin (48Ca, 44Ti, 46Ti, 48Cr) and 200 pf-shell runs for 48Cr, analyzing energy ratios R=E4/E2 and BE2(2→0) transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- For 48Cr in the fp space at c=-2, 99.8% of runs produced J=0 ground states, compared to 75% in the full pf shell
- For 48Cr in the pf shell at c=-2: E4/E2 = 2.92 ± 0.56 and BE2(2→0) = 276 ± 23 e^2 fm^4 (SU3 limit is 320 e^2 fm^4)
- Maximum Q0 values in fp space: 22 for 44Ti, 25 for 46Ti, 32 for 48Cr (a-dimensional oscillator values), leading to transitions of 120, 160, and 270 e^2 fm^4 respectively
- For 48Ca in fp space, percentage of J=0 ground states decreased from 76.3% at c=0 to 53.3% at c=-3
- For 48Cr in fp space, percentage of J=0 ground states increased from 60.2% at c=0 to 100.0% at c=-3

## Context

This work extends the discovery by Johnson et al. (1998) that random interactions lead to J=0 ground and J=2 first excited states far more often than statistically expected, and the subsequent finding by Johnson et al. (1999) that odd-even staggering is often present even without pairing forces or deformed fields. The authors address a misunderstanding in Kuz'min et al. (2000) regarding boson simulations versus full fermion calculations, and build on the work of Bijker et al. (2000) who detected BE2 coherence in a randomized IBM context but not in shell model simulations. The results enable generalization to other forms of collectivity by randomizing single particle energies while fixing two-body terms, contrary to usual practice.
