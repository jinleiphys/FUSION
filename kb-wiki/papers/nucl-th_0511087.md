# Study of the effect of the tensor correlation on the alpha clustering in 8Be with the charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock method

> We study the effect of the tensor correlation in the alpha clustering in 8Be. We take as the wave function of the alpha particle the one calculated by the charge- and parity-projected Hartree- Fock method, which was proposed by us recently. We use the wave function in a cluster-model calculation. The expectation value of the potential energy from the tensor force is almost double of that in the alpha particle. The energy surface as the function of the relative distance of two alpha particles becomes much steeper in an inner region by the inclusion of the tensor correlation in the wave function of the alpha particle. This is caused by the p-state mixing induced by the tensor correlation. By superposing the wave function with different relative distances, the reasonable binding energy of 8Be is obtained.

## Key claim

The tensor correlation, treated via the charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock (CPPHF) method, makes the energy surface of 8Be as a function of the two-alpha relative distance much steeper in the inner region, primarily due to the large increase in kinetic energy from p-state mixing induced by the tensor force; this effect is stronger than the repulsion from antisymmetrization alone.

## Method

The authors constructed an alpha-cluster model for 8Be using alpha-particle wave functions pre-calculated with the CPPHF method, which mixes parity and charge states in single-particle orbitals to incorporate tensor correlations. Two alpha clusters were placed along the z-axis at a variable relative distance R, and the total wave function was antisymmetrized for all eight nucleons. The Hamiltonian used the Volkov No.1 central force and the G3RS tensor force, with the triplet-even central attraction reduced by a factor x_TE to avoid double-counting the tensor effect, and the tensor strength scaled by a factor x_T. The rotational energy was subtracted in the rigid-rotor approximation, and the generator coordinate method (GCM) was applied by superposing wave functions at different relative distances to obtain the binding energy.

## Key numbers

- For the alpha particle in the CPPHF scheme with x_T = 1.00, the tensor potential energy V_T = -13.31 MeV, the D-state probability P(D) = 3.22%, and the matter radius R_m = 1.42 fm.
- For the alpha particle in the CPPHF scheme with x_T = 1.50, V_T = -35.28 MeV, P(D) = 8.65%, and R_m = 1.36 fm.
- For 8Be at fixed relative distance R = 3 fm in the CPPHF scheme with x_T = 1.00: total energy E_TOT = -52.90 MeV, V_T = -28.91 MeV, kinetic energy K_TOT = 146.90 MeV, rotational energy Delta_E_ROT = 8.83 MeV, matter radius R_m = 2.18 fm.
- For 8Be at R = 3 fm in the CPPHF scheme with x_T = 1.50: E_TOT = -50.03 MeV, V_T = -74.72 MeV, K_TOT = 175.65 MeV, Delta_E_ROT = 11.02 MeV, R_m = 2.12 fm.
- For the simple Hartree-Fock (HF) case without tensor force (x_T = 0.00) at R = 3 fm: E_TOT = -52.84 MeV, V_T = 0.00 MeV, K_TOT = 126.65 MeV, R_m = 2.22 fm.
- GCM results for CPPHF with x_T = 1.00: E_TOT = -58.54 MeV, V_T = -25.82 MeV, K_TOT = 131.71 MeV, R_m = 2.86 fm.
- GCM results for CPPHF with x_T = 1.50: E_TOT = -59.81 MeV, V_T = -67.50 MeV, K_TOT = 153.71 MeV, R_m = 3.24 fm.
- The energy minima in the energy surfaces appear around R = 3 fm for the HF case and around R = 3.5 fm for the PPHF and CPPHF cases.

## Context

This work extends the CPPHF method previously applied to the alpha particle by Sugimoto et al. (2004) to the two-alpha cluster system 8Be, explicitly treating the tensor force that is usually renormalized into effective central and LS interactions in cluster models. It builds on earlier G-matrix calculations by Bando (1970) and Yamamoto (1978) which showed that tensor-driven attraction enhances alpha clustering, and on variational Monte Carlo results by Wiringa (2000) finding a well-developed two-alpha cluster structure in 8Be. The study enables future work including higher angular-momentum components (d-state, f-state) and the dissolution of alpha clusters at small relative distances, as well as scattering calculations using tensor-correlated wave functions.
