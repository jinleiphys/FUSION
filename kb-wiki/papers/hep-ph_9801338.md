# QCD Properties of Hot/Dense Matter

> In the introduction, we will discuss the symmetries of QCD, its breaking in the physical vacuum and its restoration at finite temperature and/or density. Then we will discuss what exact relations can be made between chiral symmetry restoration and the imaginary part of the vector vector correlation function, which is directly related to the dilepton spectrum coming from a hot and/or dense medium. Finally, we will focus on the vector meson pole appearing in the imaginary part and discuss results on how the shape changes using QCD sum rules in medium. In particular, we will discuss three characteristic changes of the vector meson peaks; namely, shift of the peak position, increase of its width and the dispersion effect due to the breaking of Lorentz invariance in medium.

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD sum rules in medium predict characteristic changes to vector meson peaks (mass shift, width increase, and dispersion effects) that are primarily driven by chiral symmetry restoration, but the dispersion effect from Lorentz invariance breaking is small and chirally symmetric, meaning it can be neglected in first approximations of universal scaling laws.

## Method

The author uses QCD sum rules with the operator product expansion (OPE) to derive constraints on the imaginary part of the vector-vector correlation function at finite baryon density, employing a Borel transformation to relate the spectral density (modeled as a pole plus continuum) to the density-dependent condensates. The nuclear matter expectation values of local operators are computed using the linear density approximation, where the nucleon expectation values are taken from known values up to dimension 6, with the four-quark condensate treated in the mean field approximation. For the dispersion effect (finite three-momentum dependence), a once-subtracted dispersion relation is used to extract the momentum-dependent parameters from a best-fit analysis of the Borel sum rule.

## Key numbers

- For the rho and omega mesons at finite density: mass shift \(\Delta m_V \sim -50 \frac{n_n}{n_0}\) MeV and width increase \(\Delta \Gamma \sim +200 \frac{n_n}{n_0}\) MeV, where \(n_0\) is nuclear saturation density.
- For the rho/omega meson mass in the zero-width limit: \(\frac{m_V(n_n)}{m_V(0)} = 1 - (0.16 \pm 0.06) \frac{n_n}{n_0}\).
- For the phi meson mass in the zero-width limit: \(\frac{m_\phi(n_n)}{m_\phi(0)} = 1 - (0.03 \pm 0.015) \frac{n_n}{n_0}\).
- For the transverse rho meson dispersion parameter \(a = -0.108\) at nuclear matter density, giving a shift less than 2% at \({\bf q} = 0.5\) GeV/c.
- For the transverse phi meson dispersion parameter \(a = 0.004\) at nuclear matter density, giving a shift less than 0.05% at \({\bf q} = 0.5\) GeV/c.
- Parameterization of the rho meson mass with momentum: \(\frac{m_\rho(n_n)}{m_\rho(0)} = 1 - (0.023 \pm 0.007) \left( \frac{{\bf q}}{0.5} \right)^2 \frac{n_n}{n_0}\) (with \({\bf q}\) in GeV).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to vector mesons in medium, building on the earlier zero-width analysis by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) which first showed the density-dependent mass shift, and later work by Mosel (1997) that included width changes. The paper also addresses the finite three-momentum (dispersion) effect, which had been neglected in earlier universal scaling laws such as Brown-Rho scaling. The results enable more realistic modeling of dilepton spectra from relativistic heavy-ion collisions and from vector meson production experiments planned at GSI and KEK, by providing QCD-based constraints on how the vector meson peak changes in hot/dense matter.
