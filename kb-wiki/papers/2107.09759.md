# Nambu-Covariant Many-Body Theory I: Perturbative Approximations

> Symmetry-breaking considerations play an important role in allowing reliable and accurate predictions of complex systems in quantum many-body simulations. The general theory of perturbations in symmetry-breaking phases is nonetheless intrinsically more involved than in the unbroken phase due to non-vanishing anomalous Green's functions or anomalous quasiparticle interactions. In the present paper, we develop a formulation of many-body theory at non-zero temperature which is explicitly covariant with respect to a group containing Bogoliubov transformations. Based on the concept of Nambu tensors, we derive a factorisation of standard Feynman diagrams that is valid for a general Hamiltonian. The resulting factorised amplitudes are indexed over the set of un-oriented Feynman diagrams with fully antisymmetric vertices. We argue that, within this framework, the design of symmetry-breaking many-body approximations is simplified.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formulation of many-body theory at non-zero temperature that is explicitly covariant with respect to Bogoliubov transformations, using the concept of Nambu tensors to factorise standard Feynman diagrams; the authors argue that this framework simplifies the design of symmetry-breaking many-body approximations, but note that the formalism is entirely equivalent to previous formulations and a perfectly efficient numerical implementation might not benefit from it.

## Method

The authors introduce a Nambu tensor algebra built over a doubled-dimension field vector space, where Nambu fields transform under the orthogonal group O(mathscr{H}^f, g) that preserves the metric tensor defined by anticommutators. They derive a factorisation of standard Feynman diagrams into un-oriented Feynman diagrams with fully antisymmetric vertices, providing explicit Feynman rules for time and energy representations along with diagrammatic rules for performing Matsubara sums. The formalism is applied to perturbative expansions of many-body Green's functions up to third order as illustrative examples.

## Key numbers

* The formalism reduces the number of Feynman diagrams compared to standard approaches, mitigating the factorial growth in diagram number (no specific numerical reduction factor given).
* The paper provides explicit examples of diagrams up to third order in the perturbative expansion of the one-body Green's function.
* The group of Bogoliubov transformations is isomorphic to O(mathscr{H}^f, g), the orthogonal group preserving the metric tensor g.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering formalisms of De Dominicis and Martin (1964) on superfluidity, Kleinert (1981, 1982) on collective excitations, and Haussmann (1993, 1999) on the BCS-BEC crossover, rooting their specific approaches into the formalism of Nambu tensors and lifting restrictive hypotheses on the choice of field basis or interaction type. The development is motivated by the need for clear and quantifiable predictions of the phase diagram of homogeneous neutron matter, particularly regarding the superfluid pairing gaps and the role of the 3PF2 gap in neutron star cooling. The formalism enables future work on self-consistent Green's function theory and non-perturbative diagrammatic summations, with a follow-up paper (Part II) analysing self-consistent approximations.
