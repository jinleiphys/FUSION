# Neutrino Rates in Color Flavor Locked Quark Matter

> We study the weak interaction rates involving Goldstone bosons in the Color Flavor Locked (CFL) quark matter. Neutrino mean free path and the rate of energy loss due to neutrino emission in a thermal plasma of CFL pions and kaons is calculated. We find that in addition to neutrino scattering off thermal mesons, novel Cherenkov like processes wherein mesons are either emitted or absorbed contribute to the neutrino opacity. Lack of Lorentz invariance in the medium and loss of rotational invariance for processes involving mesons moving relative to the medium allow for novel processes such as $π^0 \to ν\barν$ and $e^- π^+ \to ν_e$. We explore and comment on various astrophysical implications of our finding.

# Neutrino Rates in Color Flavor Locked Quark Matter

## Key claim

Despite the large energy gap in the quark excitation spectrum of Color Flavor Locked (CFL) quark matter, neutrino opacity remains large and is not exponentially suppressed even when temperature is much less than the gap, because novel Cherenkov-like processes involving Goldstone bosons dominate; this contradicts earlier expectations that neutrino cross sections would be exponentially suppressed in superconducting quark phases.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective chiral Lagrangian for the pseudo-Goldstone boson octet (pions and kaons) and the massless U(1)_B Goldstone boson in CFL quark matter, including weak interactions by gauging the Lagrangian. They computed neutrino mean free paths and energy loss rates from one-body decays (e.g., π^0 → νν̄, π^± → eν), Cherenkov emission and absorption processes (ν → Hν, νH → ν), and two-body scattering and annihilation reactions, using thermal meson distributions at finite temperature with charge neutrality enforced by an electron chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino mean free path in CFL matter at T=5 MeV: ~100 meters (from three-body Cherenkov processes); at T=30 MeV: ~70 cm.
- Neutrino mean free path from scattering (νφ → νφ) at T=5 MeV: >10 km; at T=30 MeV: 4 meters.
- Energy loss rate (emissivity) in CFL matter at T=5 MeV: ~5 × 10^33 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1}; at T=10 MeV: ~2 × 10^35 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1}.
- For comparison, unpaired quark matter emissivity at T=5 MeV: ~2 × 10^36 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1}; at T=10 MeV: ~2 × 10^38 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1} (Iwamoto).
- Meson rest energies at zero temperature: π^± ~30 MeV; K^+ ~26 MeV; K^- ~82 MeV; K^0 ~24 MeV; K̄^0 ~80 MeV (for μ=400 MeV, Δ=100 MeV, m_s=150 MeV).
- Velocity factor for Goldstone bosons: v = 1/√3.
- Decay constant f_π = 83 MeV at μ=400 MeV; f_H^2 = 3μ^2/(8π^2).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of color superconductivity and its astrophysical implications (Alford 1999, Rajagopal 2000) by providing the first detailed calculation of weak interaction rates in the CFL phase. It contrasts with earlier work on two-flavor superconducting quark matter (Carter 2000) which found exponentially suppressed neutrino cross sections due to the absence of Goldstone bosons. The results enable quantitative modeling of neutrino transport in neutron star cores that may contain CFL quark matter, and can be compared with neutrino mean free paths in nuclear matter (Reddy, Prakash, Lattimer 1997) and unpaired quark matter (Iwamoto). The emissivity results are complementary to concurrent work by Jaikumar, Prakash, and Schafer (2002) on sub-MeV temperature neutrino emission in CFL matter.
