# Coherent photoproduction of J/Psi and high-mass e+e- pairs in ultra-peripheral Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

> High energy ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) of heavy-ions generate strong electromagnetic fields which open the possibility to study gamma-gamma and gamma-nucleus processes in a kinematic regime so far unexplored. We report on preliminary PHENIX results of J/Psi and high-mass e+e- photoproduction at mid-rapidity in coherent electromagnetic Au+Au interactions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV tagged with forward neutron emission from the Au* dissociation.

## Key claim

Preliminary PHENIX results show evidence for coherent photoproduction of J/Psi and high-mass e+e- pairs in ultra-peripheral Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, but the J/Psi signal extraction carries significant systematic uncertainty dominated by the subtraction of the dielectron continuum background, yielding only 10 +/- 3 (stat) +/- 3 (syst) J/Psi candidates.

## Method

The analysis used PHENIX central-arm detectors (drift chambers, RICH, electromagnetic calorimeter) to reconstruct e+e- pairs at mid-rapidity from 6.7 M events collected with a dedicated level-1 ultra-peripheral trigger requiring a veto on beam-beam counter coincidences, an EMCal cluster above 0.8 GeV, and at least 30 GeV deposited in zero-degree calorimeters (ZDC) to tag forward neutron emission from Au Coulomb dissociation. The J/Psi yield was extracted by subtracting a fitted power-law dielectron continuum from the invariant mass distribution of unlike-sign pairs after removing like-sign background, with acceptance and efficiency corrections obtained from the Starlight Monte Carlo and a full GEANT simulation of the PHENIX detector.

## Key numbers

- Total integrated luminosity: 120 +/- 10 microbarn^-1
- J/Psi yield: 10 +/- 3 (stat) +/- 3 (syst) counts within 2.7-3.5 GeV/c^2
- J/Psi reconstructed mass: 3.096 +/- 130 MeV/c^2 (consistent with PDG value)
- Acceptance for J/Psi -> e+e- decay within |y| < 0.5: 5.7%
- Reconstruction efficiency (including all cuts and yield extraction within +/- 3 sigma): 56.4%
- Total number of e+e- and J/Psi counts: 40 +/- 6 (stat)
- Trigger efficiency for high-energy electrons from J/Psi decay: 0.9 +/- 0.1
- Equivalent photon flux enhancement factor Z^2 ~ 6.5 x 10^3 for Au beams compared to e+/- or proton beams

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent photonuclear production in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, following earlier measurements of rho meson photoproduction and low-mass e+/- pairs by the STAR collaboration (STAR, 2004). The results enable future comparisons with perturbative QCD calculations for J/Psi photoproduction that depend on the nuclear gluon distribution G_A(x,Q^2) and with QED calculations for dielectron pair production in the strong-field regime (Z*alpha_em ~ 0.6) where perturbative approaches may break down.
