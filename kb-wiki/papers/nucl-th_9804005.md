# Low-momentum effective theory for nucleons

> Starting from a precise two-nucleon potential, we use the method of unitary transformations to construct an effective potential that involves only momenta less than a given maximal value. We describe this method for an S-wave potential of the Malfliet-Tjon type. It is demonstrated that the bound and scattering state spectrum calculated within the effective theory agrees exactly with the one based on the original potential. This might open an avenue for the construction of effective chiral few-nucleon forces and for a systematic treatment of relativistic effects in few-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective two-nucleon potential, constructed via unitary transformation to involve only momenta below a chosen cutoff, reproduces the bound-state energy and scattering phase shifts of the original Malfliet-Tjon potential exactly, provided one stays below that cutoff. The abstract's claim of "exact" agreement is qualified by the fact that the effective theory is only defined for momenta below the cutoff, and the numerical solution for the operator A requires regularization and iteration to achieve this precision.

## Method

The authors start with a momentum-space Hamiltonian for two nucleons using an S-wave Malfliet-Tjon potential with attractive and repulsive parts. They introduce projection operators η and λ to separate low-momentum (|p| ≤ Λ) and high-momentum (|p| > Λ) states, then perform a unitary transformation following Okubo's method to decouple these subspaces. The operator A, which generates the transformation, is obtained by solving a nonlinear integral equation iteratively, starting from A = V(p,q)/(E_q - E_p), with regularization applied near the cutoff momentum to handle singularities.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum Λ = 400 MeV (or smaller) is used to define the low-momentum subspace.
- The Malfliet-Tjon potential parameters: V_R = 7.29, V_A = 3.18, μ_R = 614 MeV, μ_A = 306 MeV.
- The potential supports exactly one bound state at E = -2.23 MeV.
- Expectation value of |p| in the S-wave deuteron for the exact (unregularized) potential: 80.23 MeV at Λ = 200 MeV, 80.2308 MeV at Λ = 400 MeV.
- Expectation value of |p| for the effective potential (regularized): 79.90 MeV at Λ = 200 MeV, 80.2304 MeV at Λ = 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method of unitary transformations, previously used in nuclear many-body theory for model-space decoupling (e.g., Kuo, 1974; Suzuki and Okamoto, 1983), to construct a low-momentum effective two-nucleon potential from a realistic starting potential. It addresses the problem that chiral perturbation theory for nucleons (Weinberg, 1990; van Kolck, 1994) requires a cutoff to tame high-momentum components, which is not commensurate with chiral power counting; the approach differs from Lepage's (1997) effective field theory framework by aiming to match the low-momentum theory to meson-exchange pictures. The results enable future construction of effective chiral few-nucleon forces and systematic treatment of relativistic effects in few-body systems.
