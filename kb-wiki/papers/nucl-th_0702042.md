# Thermal freeze-out versus chemical freeze-out reexamined

> An alternative, to the commonly used blast-wave, model describing the freeze-out hypersurface is applied to fit the $p_{T}$-spectra of identified hadrons measured at relativistic heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4, 130$ and 200 GeV. Decays of resonances are taken into account completely. It has turned out that the fitted freeze-out temperature and baryon number chemical potential depend weakly on the centrality of the collision and their values are close to the chemical freeze-out values determined from fits to particle yield ratios.

# Thermal freeze-out versus chemical freeze-out reexamined

## Key claim
Using an alternative freeze-out hypersurface model (inspired by the single-freeze-out model but without assuming simultaneous chemical and thermal freeze-out), the fitted kinetic freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential are found to be nearly independent of collision centrality and close to chemical freeze-out values, contradicting the centrality-dependent and substantially lower temperatures obtained from blast-wave fits.

## Method
The model assumes a noninteracting gas of stable hadrons and resonances at chemical and thermal equilibrium, with the freeze-out hypersurface defined by constant proper time τ and a finite transverse size ρ_max. The four-velocity follows a scaling solution (u^μ = x^μ/τ), and the invariant distribution includes complete treatment of resonance decays. All four parameters (T_kin, μ_B, τ, ρ_max) were fitted simultaneously to p_T spectra of π^±, K^±, p, and p̄ from RHIC experiments at √s_NN = 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV using χ^2 minimization, excluding data points with p_T > 3 GeV.

## Key numbers
- PHOBOS at √s_NN = 62.4 GeV, 0-15% centrality: T_kin = 148.52 ± 1.15 MeV, μ_B = 72.60 ± 3.81 MeV
- PHENIX at √s_NN = 130 GeV, 0-5% centrality: T_kin = 166.74 ± 3.96 MeV, μ_B = 35.06 ± 8.97 MeV
- PHENIX at √s_NN = 200 GeV, 0-5% centrality: T_kin = 150.07 ± 1.34 MeV, μ_B = 24.10 ± 3.66 MeV
- STAR at √s_NN = 200 GeV, 0-5% centrality: T_kin = 159.99 ± 1.19 MeV, μ_B = 24.00 ± 2.17 MeV
- BRAHMS at √s_NN = 200 GeV, 0-10% centrality: T_kin = 150.60 ± 1.39 MeV, μ_B = 23.07 ± 3.51 MeV
- Maximum transverse flow velocity β_⊥^max ranges from 0.496 (PHENIX 130 GeV, 60-92%) to 0.791 (STAR 200 GeV, 0-5%)
- χ^2/NDF values range from 0.13 to 1.37 across all fits

## Context
This work extends the single-freeze-out model of Broniowski (2001) by removing the assumption of simultaneous chemical and thermal freeze-out and fitting all parameters directly to spectra rather than in two steps. It contradicts the blast-wave model results (Schnedermann, 1993) which yield T_kin values of 89-161 MeV with strong centrality dependence, as reported by PHENIX (Adcox, 2003; Burward-Hoy, 2002), STAR (Adams, 2003), BRAHMS (Arsene, 2005), and PHOBOS (Back, 2006). The results also challenge the hydrodynamical freeze-out criterion approach of Heinz (2006), which required an ad hoc parameter ξ = 0.295 to reproduce blast-wave temperatures. The model enables testing against Ω hyperon spectra and suggests that discrepancies between STAR and PHENIX data may explain different fitted parameter values.
