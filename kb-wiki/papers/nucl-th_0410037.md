# Effective Operator Treatment of the Lipkin Model

> We analyze the Lipkin Model using effective operator techniques. We present both analytical and numerical results for effective Hamiltonians. The accuracy of the cluster approximation is investigated.

# Effective Operator Treatment of the Lipkin Model

## Key claim

The cluster approximation, which retains only one- and two-body operators in the effective Hamiltonian, can produce substantial errors in ground-state energies and level splittings in the Lipkin Model, but these errors are likely larger than in realistic nuclear many-body problems due to the infinite-range interactions in this model. The accuracy depends strongly on the choice of P and Q spaces and on the coupling strength in ways that cannot be anticipated by perturbation theory alone.

## Method

The authors apply the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method, using the Andreozzi iteration scheme, to construct effective Hamiltonians in a truncated P-space for the Lipkin Model with N spin-1/2 fermions. They then implement the two-body cluster approximation by retaining only matrix elements of the effective Hamiltonian that correspond to operators present in the bare Hamiltonian (bold-faced elements) and setting all induced operator matrix elements to zero. The accuracy of this truncation is tested by comparing ground-state energies and ground-to-first-excited-state splittings against exact results for various choices of P-space, Q-space, and coupling strength v.

## Key numbers

- For j=8, μ=0.1, v=0.03 with Q-space containing the Jz=+8 state: the exact ground-state energy is 1.0460; the cluster approximation gives 1.1238 (approximately 7.4% error); the true splitting (0.5974) is approximated as 0.5769.
- For j=8, μ=0.1, v=0.03 with Q-space containing the Jz=0 state: the cluster approximation ground-state energy is 1.3130, overshooting the true value of 1.0460 by 25%; the splitting is 0.6228 versus the true 0.5974.
- For j=8, μ=0.1, v=0.03 with Q-space containing the Jz=-8 state: the cluster approximation ground-state energy is 1.2547 (20% error); the splitting is 0.6293 versus the true 0.5974.
- For j=8, μ=0.1, v=0.03 with a 4-dimensional Q-space (Jz=8,6,-6,-8) and 5-dimensional P-space: the cluster approximation overestimates the ground-state energy by over 41%; the splitting is 0.2134 versus the true 0.5974.
- For j=8, μ=0.1, v=0.1 with Q-space containing Jz=+8: the cluster approximation gives a 39% error in the ground-state energy, compared to under 8% at v=0.03.
- For j=4, μ=0, v=1: the analytical effective Hamiltonian contains an induced J±⁴ term with coefficient -0.1443/[j(j+1)].

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of testing many-body methods with the exactly solvable Lipkin Model (Lipkin, 1965) to evaluate the validity of the cluster approximation, which is widely used in nuclear structure calculations with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (assorted references). The effective operator method follows the Lee-Suzuki approach (Lee and Suzuki, 1980) and is related to schemes for obtaining cut-off independent low-energy two-body potentials (VlowK). The authors find that the cluster approximation works best for weak couplings and when the P-space constitutes a large fraction of the full Hilbert space, but caution that their conclusions are restricted to the Lipkin Model where interactions have unlimited range, suggesting that errors may be smaller in realistic finite-range nuclear interactions.
