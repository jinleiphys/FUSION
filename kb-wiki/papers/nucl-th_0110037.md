# A Hydrodynamic Description of Heavy Ion Collisions at the SPS and RHIC

> A hydrodynamic + cascade model of relativistic heavy ion collisions is presented and compared to available hadronic data from the SPS to RHIC. The model consistently reproduces the radial and elliptic flow data for different particles, collision energies, and impact parameters. Three ingredients are essential to the success: (a) a reasonable EOS exhibiting the hard and soft features of the QCD phase transition, (b) thermal hadronization at the phase boundary, and (c) subsequent hadronic rescattering. Some features of the RHIC data are readily explained: (i) the observed elliptic flow and its dependence on $p_{T}$ and mass, (ii) the anomalous $\bar{p}/π^{-}$ ratio for $p_{T} \approx 2.0$ GeV, (iii) the difference in the slope parameters measured by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations, and (iv) the respectively strong and weak impact parameter dependence of the $\bar{p}$ and $ϕ$ slope parameters. For an EOS without the hard and soft features of the QCD phase transition, the broad consistency with the data is lost.

## Key claim

A hydrodynamic plus cascade model, using an equation of state (EOS) that includes both the hard QGP phase and the soft mixed phase of the QCD phase transition, consistently reproduces radial and elliptic flow data from the SPS to RHIC; however, the broad consistency with data is lost for an EOS without those hard and soft features.

## Method

The authors solve relativistic hydrodynamics in the transverse plane assuming Bjorken scaling, with a switching surface at T_switch = 160 MeV where the macroscopic description is converted into hadrons via the Cooper-Frye prescription. The resulting hadrons are then fed into the hadronic cascade RQMD v2.4, which models the subsequent rescattering and freezeout stages. A family of equations of state labeled by latent heat (LH4, LH8, LH16, LH∞, and a Resonance Gas EOS) is systematically varied and compared to flow data.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy densities at approximately 1 fm/c are roughly 4 GeV/fm^3 at the SPS and 8 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC
- The switching temperature is set to T_switch = 160 MeV
- The best overall consistency with data is found with latent heat LH = 0.8 GeV/fm^3 (LH8)
- At the SPS, center-of-mass energy is sqrt(s) = 17 GeV per nucleon; at RHIC, sqrt(s) = 130 GeV per nucleon
- The QCD transition temperature is approximately T_c ≈ 160 MeV
- The energy density range where temperature and pressure are nearly constant is e = 0.5–1.3 GeV/fm^3
- Particles emerge with a collective transverse velocity of approximately 1/2 c at the SPS

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic calculations at the SPS and RHIC by Kolb, Sollfrank, Heinz, Huovinen, and Heiselberg (2000–2001) and by Teaney, Lauret, and Shuryak (2001), which gave good descriptions of radial and elliptic flows but used naive freezeout prescriptions. It also builds on the hydro+cascade approach of Bass and Dumitru (2000), who replaced the hadronic phase with the UrQMD transport model. The model enables systematic extraction of the QCD equation of state from flow data and explains features such as the anomalous antiproton-to-pion ratio at p_T ≈ 2.0 GeV and the difference in slope parameters between the STAR and PHENIX collaborations.
