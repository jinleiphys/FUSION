# Tensor-polarized structure function $b_1$ by convolution picture for deuteron

> There are polarized structure functions $b_{1-4}$ for the spin-1 deuteron. We calculated the leading-twist tensor structure function $b_1$ by using convolution description for the deuteron. We found large differences between our theoretical functions and HERMES experimental data on $b_1$. Although higher-twist effects should be considered in obtaining experimental $b_1$, it suggests a possible existence of new hadron physics mechanism for spin-1 hadrons. Furthermore, we found that there are significant distributions at large Bjorken $x$. In future, an experimental measurement is planned at JLab for $b_1$ and there is a possibility of a proton-deuteron Drell-Yan experiment at Fermilab with the tensor-polarized deuteron, so that further theoretical studies are needed for clarifying the physics origin of tensor structure in terms of quark and gluon degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The leading-twist tensor structure function $b_1$ for the deuteron, calculated using standard convolution models with D-state admixture, is much smaller in magnitude than the HERMES experimental data in the range $x<0.5$, which could indicate a new hadron physics mechanism for spin-1 hadrons, although higher-twist effects in extracting $b_1$ experimentally have not been fully accounted for.

## Method

The authors used two convolution models: a basic convolution description where $b_1$ is given by unpolarized nucleon structure functions $F_1^N$ convoluted with tensor-polarized lightcone momentum distributions of the nucleon derived from the deuteron wave function, and a virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) that includes higher-twist contributions by considering the $np$ component of the light-front deuteron wave function with one off-mass-shell interacting nucleon. Both models use the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function and MSTW2008 leading-order parton distribution functions at $Q^2=2.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- At $Q^2=2.5$ GeV$^2$, the calculated $b_1$ distributions are less than $10^{-3}$ in magnitude.
- In the measured $x$ range ($x<0.5$), the experimental magnitude from HERMES is one order larger than both theoretical estimates.
- The theoretical calculations show significant distributions at large Bjorken $x$ ($0.6<x<0.8$), where the HERMES data also show relatively large distributions.
- The average scale of the HERMES measurement is $Q^2=2.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends earlier convolution calculations of $b_1$ by Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1989) and Khan and Hoodbhoy (1991), finding that the present results differ significantly in both magnitude and $x$ dependence from those previous estimates, with the SD term having opposite sign to the result of Khan and Hoodbhoy (1991). The large discrepancy between the standard deuteron model predictions and the HERMES data suggests a possible "tensor-polarization puzzle" analogous to the nucleon spin puzzle, and motivates future measurements at JLab (planned for medium $x$ $0.3<x<0.5$) and a possible proton-deuteron Drell-Yan experiment at Fermilab (E1039) to measure tensor-polarized antiquark distributions.
