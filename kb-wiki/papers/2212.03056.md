# Impact of globally spin-aligned vector mesons on the search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions

> In high-energy heavy-ion collisions, the chiral magnetic effect (CME) is predicted to arise from the interplay between the chirality imbalance of quarks in the nuclear medium and the intense magnetic field, and will cause a charge separation along the magnetic field direction. While the search for the CME is still ongoing in experiments at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), the CME-sensitive observables need to be scrutinized to exclude the non-CME contributions. In this work, we examine the influence of globally spin-aligned $ρ$ mesons on the $γ_{112}$ correlator, the $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ correlator, and the signed balance functions, via a toy model and a multiphase transport model (AMPT). The global spin alignment of vector mesons could originate from non-CME mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions, and is characterized by the 00-component of the spin density matrix, $ρ_{00}$. We find that the CME observables show similar dependence on $ρ_{00}$, and could receive a positive (negative) contribution from $ρ$-decay pions, if the $ρ_{00}$ of $ρ$ mesons is larger (smaller) than 1/3. Since pions are the most abundant particles in such collisions, the $ρ_{00}$ measurements for $ρ$ mesons become crucial to the interpretation of the CME data.

# Impact of globally spin-aligned vector mesons on the search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Globally spin-aligned ρ mesons can produce a finite background contribution to all three major CME-sensitive observables (the γ112 correlator, the RΨ2(ΔS) correlator, and signed balance functions) that depends linearly on the spin density matrix element ρ00, and this contribution can be either positive or negative depending on whether ρ00 is larger or smaller than 1/3, meaning the effect could mimic or obscure a true CME signal.

## Method

The authors analytically derived the dependence of the γ112 correlator, the RΨ2(ΔS) correlator, and signed balance functions on the ρ-meson spin alignment parameter ρ00 by expressing the covariance terms from ρ → π+π− decays in the ρ rest frame and then applying Lorentz boost factors to the laboratory frame. They confirmed the analytical results with toy model simulations (195 π+ and 195 π− per event, 33 pairs from ρ decays, with ρ mesons generated according to a thermal spectrum and decays implemented via PYTHIA6) and with a multiphase transport model (AMPT) for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with impact parameter 8 fm, where spin alignment was implemented by redistributing decay product momenta according to the angular distribution dN/dcosθ* = (3/4)[(1−ρ00) + (3ρ00−1)cos²θ*].

## Key numbers

- The ρ-meson decay angular distribution in the transverse plane gives dN/dφ* = (1/2π)[1 − (1/2)(3ρ00−1)cos2φ*], yielding an effective elliptic flow in the ρ rest frame of v₂* = −(3ρ00−1)/4.
- In the ρ rest frame with no CME, the ρ-decay contribution to Δγ112 is Δγ112* = (Nρ/N+N−)(3ρ00−1)/4.
- Toy model simulations used TBE = 212 MeV for primordial pions (matching ⟨pT⟩ = 400 MeV) and T = 317 MeV for ρ mesons (matching ⟨pT⟩ = 830 MeV as observed in STAR data).
- AMPT simulations used ρ00 values of 0.18, 1/3, and 0.43 with one million events per setting.
- For the RΨ2(ΔS) correlator with zero v₂ρ, the sign of the concavity is Sign(Sconcavity) = Sign[−(Nρ/2N+N−)(3ρ00−1)].
- The difference variable ΔσR² for the RΨ2(ΔS) correlator has the form ΔσR² = (Nρ/N+N−)[(1/4)(fc+fs)(3ρ00−1) + (fc−fs)].

## Context

This work extends the study of non-CME backgrounds to CME-sensitive observables beyond the commonly admitted flow background, following the identification by Tang (2019) that signed balance functions could acquire contributions from globally spin-aligned vector mesons. The authors demonstrate that the global spin alignment of ρ mesons, which has been measured by the STAR Collaboration (2022) showing unexpectedly large ρ00 values for φ mesons, affects all three major CME observables (γ112, RΨ2(ΔS), and signed balance functions) in a similar linear fashion. This enables experimentalists to incorporate ρ00 measurements into background subtraction procedures for ongoing CME searches at RHIC and the LHC, and motivates further theoretical work on the possible coupling between the CME and global spin alignment through ρ-meson coalescence.
