# Saturation with chiral interactions and consequences for finite nuclei

> We explore the impact of nuclear matter saturation on the properties and systematics of finite nuclei across the nuclear chart. Using the ab initio in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG), we study ground-state energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei from $^4$He to $^{78}$Ni, based on a set of low-resolution two- and three-nucleon interactions that predict realistic saturation properties. We first investigate in detail the convergence properties of these Hamiltonians with respect to model-space truncations for both two- and three-body interactions. We find one particular interaction that reproduces well the ground-state energies of all closed-shell nuclei studied. As expected from their saturation points relative to this interaction, the other Hamiltonians underbind nuclei, but lead to a remarkably similar systematics of ground-state energies. Extending our calculations to complete isotopic chains in the $sd$ and $pf$ shells with the valence-space IM-SRG, the same interaction reproduces not only experimental ground states but two-neutron-separation energies and first excited $2^+$ states. We also calculate radii with the valence-space IM-SRG for the first time. Since this particular interaction saturates at too high density, charge radii are still too small compared with experiment. Except for this underprediction, the radii systematics is, however, well reproduced. Our results highlight the importance of nuclear matter as a theoretical benchmark for the development of next-generation chiral interactions.

# Saturation with chiral interactions and consequences for finite nuclei

## Key claim
The 1.8/2.0 (EM) chiral interaction reproduces ground-state energies of closed-shell nuclei from 4He to 78Ni to approximately 1% of experiment, but because this interaction saturates at too high density, charge radii are systematically too small; the other three Hamiltonians underbind nuclei but produce remarkably similar systematics, suggesting that saturation properties largely determine the overall energy scale while finer details of the interaction shift results by a nearly constant offset.

## Method
The authors used the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) in both closed-shell and valence-space formulations to compute ground-state energies and charge radii for closed-shell nuclei from 4He to 78Ni, based on four chiral NN+3N Hamiltonians with SRG resolution scales lambda_NN = 1.8, 2.0, 2.2 fm^-1 and two sets of c_i couplings (EM and PWA). For open-shell isotopic chains in the sd and pf shells, they employed the valence-space IM-SRG (VS-IM-SRG) with ensemble normal-ordering to decouple valence-space Hamiltonians, which were then diagonalized with the NuShellX shell-model code to obtain ground-state energies, two-neutron separation energies, first excited 2+ states, and charge radii.

## Key numbers
- The 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction reproduces closed-shell ground-state energies to approximately 1% of experiment; for 40Ca the IM-SRG gives -344.5 MeV vs. coupled-cluster -348 MeV at eMax/EMax=14/16 and hbarOmega=16 MeV.
- For 54Ca, increasing eMax from 12 to 14 at fixed EMax=14 changes the ground-state energy by only 0.6 MeV for 1.8/2.0 (EM), compared to 2.2 MeV for 2.2/2.0 (EM).
- For 78Ni, increasing EMax from 16 to 18 at eMax=14 changes the ground-state energy by 2.0 MeV for 1.8/2.0 (EM) and by 5.0 MeV for 2.0/2.0 (PWA).
- The 2.0/2.0 (PWA) interaction underbinds closed-shell nuclei by as much as 1.5 MeV per nucleon relative to experiment.
- In the nickel chain, VS-IMSRG ground-state energies become overbound in the mid-shell region by up to 10 MeV.
- For 48Ca, the first excited 2+ state calculated with IM-SRG(2) is approximately 1 MeV too high, consistent with coupled-cluster CCSD results using the same interaction.

## Context
This work extends the study of chiral NN+3N interactions from nuclear matter saturation (Hebeler 2011, Drischler 2016) to finite nuclei across the nuclear chart, using the IM-SRG method (Hergert 2016) and its valence-space formulation (Stroberg 2017). It contrasts with earlier chiral interactions that either overbind heavier nuclei (Roth 2014, Binder 2014) or fit directly to medium-mass nuclei at the expense of high-precision NN scattering (Ekstrom 2015). The results highlight that the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction, which gives realistic saturation properties, provides a good description of ground-state energies and excited states but underpredicts charge radii, motivating the development of next-generation chiral interactions that simultaneously reproduce saturation density and finite-nuclei radii.
