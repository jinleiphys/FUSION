# Equations, chromopermittivity and instabilities of the quark-gluon medium

> The quark-gluon medium described by QCD equations is considered at high energies. Within the assumptions of the linear response theory the chromopermittivity of the medium is introduced and it is argued that it exceeds 1 at TeV energies. The dispersion equations show that the proper modes of the medium reveal instability and the parton currents traversing it induce the emission of Cherenkov gluons. Their distributions at LHC can differ from those typical for lower energies of RHIC because they are determined by the high energy dependence of the chromopermittivity while the latter ones arise due to collective resonance excitations. The distinction between Cherenkov gluons and Mach waves is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chromopermittivity of the quark-gluon medium exceeds 1 at TeV energies, based on the positive real part of the forward scattering amplitude for hadron-hadron processes at high energies, which is a necessary condition for Cherenkov gluon emission; however, the author notes that the exact energy dependence of the chromopermittivity is not precisely known and the conclusions depend strongly on its realistic shape.

## Method

The author introduces a chromopermittivity for the quark-gluon medium within the linear response theory, analogous to the dielectric permittivity in electrodynamics, and writes the in-medium Yang-Mills equations by replacing the chromoelectric field **E** with **D** = ε**E**. The chromopermittivity is estimated from the real part of the forward scattering amplitude of gluons using the relation Re ε(ω) − 1 = 4π N_s Re F_0(ω)/ω^2, and a specific model is adopted where Re ε = 1 + ω_0^2/ω^2 at high energies, motivated by experimental data on hadronic elastic scattering amplitudes. The dispersion equations for the medium are then solved to find the proper modes and the conditions for Cherenkov gluon emission.

## Key numbers

- The chromopermittivity values extracted from RHIC two-particle correlation data are ε₁ ≈ 6 and ε₂ ≈ 1 (with ε₁ ranging from 5.4 to 9 and ε₂ from 0.7 to 2 in earlier data from STAR and PHENIX).
- The parameter ω₀ in the high-energy model Re ε = 1 + ω₀²/ω² is estimated as ω₀ ≈ 3 GeV, based on the total pp cross section of about 60 mb, ρ_pp ≈ 0.1 at ω ≈ 1 TeV, and N_s ≈ 3m_π³/4π.
- The maximum of Δε(ω) is estimated to be about 10⁻⁵ at ω ≈ 1 TeV.
- The instability exponent for Cherenkov gluons at k_t = ω₀ is Im δ₁ = ω₀² / [2k_z (2γ (1 + ω₀²/k_z²))^{1/3}], which decreases as γ⁻¹/³ and is about 16 times smaller at LHC compared to RHIC.
- The coherence length is positive for partons with momentum fraction x < ω₀/m ≈ 3, where m is the proton mass (≈ 1 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the analogy between QCD and electrodynamics for the quark-gluon medium, building on earlier in-medium gluodynamics equations proposed in Dremin (2004) and Dremin & Prikhodko (2005). It contrasts with the electrodynamic case where permittivity becomes less than 1 at high frequencies (Langmuir plasma formula), and instead uses the experimentally observed positive real part of forward scattering amplitudes for hadron-hadron processes (pp, Kp, πp, γp) at high energies, as measured from Coulomb-hadron interference. The paper enables predictions for LHC experiments where Cherenkov gluon distributions may differ from those at RHIC, and distinguishes Cherenkov gluons from Mach cone effects in relativistic hydrodynamics, noting that the hydrodynamical Mach cone interpretation has not yet provided quantitative fits to data.
