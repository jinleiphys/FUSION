# Hadronic vacuum polarization effect in muonic atoms

> The hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) corrections to energy levels in muonic atoms are studied systematically across the periodic table. Two nuclear charge distribution models have been considered, with partly analytical solution for an homogeneously charged sphere model and direct numerical integration for a Fermi nuclear charge distribution. Our comparison reveals a good agreement between both approaches with differences typically below a few percent. The magnitude of HVP corrections increases with nuclear charge, displaying irregularities that correlate directly with the non-monotonic behavior of nuclear radii. Despite strong relativistic effects at high $Z$, the ratio of hadronic to muonic vacuum polarization remains remarkably stable, deviating by less than $10\%$ from the established $67\%$ non-relativistic benchmark value

# Hadronic vacuum polarization effect in muonic atoms

## Key claim
The hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) correction to energy levels in muonic atoms is systematically calculated across the periodic table, showing that the ratio of HVP to muonic vacuum polarization remains stable within 0.60–0.70 across all Z, deviating by less than 10% from the established 67% non-relativistic benchmark, but only when using realistic nuclear charge distributions; simplified models can produce discrepancies up to 5–6% for heavy elements.

## Method
The authors used a semi-empirical, dispersion-relation-based approach with a parametrized hadronic polarization function fitted to experimental e+e− → hadrons cross-section data, restricting analysis to the low-energy domain (0.0–0.7 GeV). They computed first-order perturbation theory energy shifts for muonic 1s1/2, 2s1/2, 2p1/2, and 2p3/2 states across Z = 20 to Z = 100, comparing results from a 2-parameter Fermi nuclear charge distribution (direct numerical integration) and a homogeneously charged sphere model (partly analytical solution).

## Key numbers
- HVP energy shift for the 1s1/2 state increases monotonically with Z, with irregularities for Z ≥ 60 correlating with non-monotonic nuclear charge radii fluctuations.
- Ratio ΔEHVP/ΔEμVP lies within the narrow range 0.60–0.70 for all four states across Z = 20 to Z = 100.
- s-states maintain ratio values around 0.675–0.68 throughout the entire Z range from 20 to 100.
- 2p1/2 and 2p3/2 states begin with values of approximately 0.62–0.63 at Z = 20 and grow monotonically toward the non-relativistic benchmark.
- Relative difference between Fermi and homogeneous sphere models is typically below 0.03 (3%) for the 1s state across most nuclei.
- For 2s state at Z ≥ 90, discrepancies reach 0.05–0.06 (5–6%).
- For p-states, the relative difference stays constantly below 0.03 (3%) across the entire Z range.

## Context
This work extends the semi-empirical HVP evaluation method of Breidenbach et al. (2022) from hydrogenlike ions and muonic hydrogen to a systematic study across the periodic table. It provides improved theoretical predictions compared to the earlier work of PhysRevD.11.230 (1974) and fills a gap in understanding how the 67% HVP-to-μVP ratio, previously calculated only non-relativistically for hydrogen by Friar, evolves with nuclear charge. The results enable more accurate QED predictions for precision spectroscopy of muonic atoms, particularly for heavy elements where HVP corrections reach up to 68% of μVP and where nuclear model dependence becomes significant.
