# Green's Function for Nonlocal Potentials

> The single-particle nuclear potential is intrinsically nonlocal. In this paper, we consider nonlocalities which arise from the many-body and fermionic nature of the nucleus. We investigate the effects of nonlocality in the nuclear potential by developing the Green's function for nonlocal potentials. The formal Green's function integral is solved analytically in two different limits of the wavelength as compared to the scale of nonlocality. Both results are studied in a quasi-free limit. The results illuminate some of the basic effects of nonlocality in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper develops an analytic Green's function for nonlocal nuclear potentials, showing that in the low-momentum limit (nucleon wavelength much larger than the nonlocality range) the nonlocality modifies the effective mass, while in the high-momentum limit it produces an exponentially energy-dependent local equivalent potential. The authors hedge that these results rely on a factorized form of the nonlocal potential with a constant average term and a Gaussian width term, and that the analytic solutions are obtained only in two limiting cases of the dimensionless parameter \(B = \beta k^2\).

## Method

The authors start from the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation with a nonlocal potential and adopt a factorized form \(V_{NL}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{x}') = V_a((\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{x}')/2) V_w(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}')\), taking the average term \(V_a\) constant and the width term \(V_w\) as a Gaussian of range \(b\). They derive the Green's function in momentum space and evaluate the resulting integral by contour integration, solving the transcendental pole equation analytically in two limits: low momentum (\(\beta k^2 \ll 1\)) and high momentum (\(\beta k^2 \gg 1\)). The quasi-free limit (\(|A| \ll 1\)) is then applied to both cases.

## Key numbers

- Nonlocality range \(b = 0.14\) fm for the \(^{12}\text{C} + ^{12}\text{C}\) system, extracted from data.
- Nonlocality range \(b = 0.11\) fm for the \(^{12}\text{C} + ^{16}\text{O}\) system, extracted from data.
- For \(^{12}\text{C} + ^{12}\text{C}\): \(|\alpha| \beta = 0.085\).
- For \(^{12}\text{C} + ^{16}\text{O}\): \(|\alpha| \beta = 0.075\).
- Effective mass ratio: \(m^*/m = 1/(1 - \alpha \beta)\), where \(\alpha = 2 m V_0 / \hbar^2\) and \(\beta = b^2/4\).
- In the high-momentum limit, the energy-dependent local equivalent potential is \(V(E) = V_0 \exp(-m b^2 E / 2\hbar^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of nonlocal potentials in nuclear physics, building on the Perey-Buck (1962) factorized form and the Feshbach (1958) formalism for channel-coupling nonlocality. It provides analytic Green's functions that complement numerical approaches such as those of Bauhoff et al. (1984) for Pauli nonlocality and the coupled-channels methods reviewed in Ref. (1991). The results enable analytic calculation of transmission coefficients in heavy-ion collisions and allow direct comparison to phenomenological energy-dependent potentials extracted from elastic scattering data, as demonstrated for the systems \(^{12}\text{C} + ^{12}\text{C}\) and \(^{12}\text{C} + ^{16}\text{O}\) using data from Marín (1995).
