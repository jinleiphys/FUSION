# Chiral and deconfinement transitions in strong coupling lattice QCD with finite coupling and Polyakov loop effects

> We investigate chiral and deconfinement transitions in the framework of the strong coupling lattice QCD for color SU(3) with one species of unrooted staggered fermion at finite temperature and quark chemical potential. We take account of the leading order Polyakov loop terms as well as the next-to-next-to-leading order (1/g^4) fermionic terms of the strong coupling expansion in the effective action. We investigate the Polyakov loop effects by comparing two approximation schemes, a Haar measure method (no fluctuation from the mean field) and a Weiss mean-field method (with fluctuations). The effective potential is obtained in both cases, and we analytically clarify the Polyakov loop contributions to the effective potential. The Polyakov loop is found to suppress the chiral condensate and to reduce the chiral transition temperature at mu=0, and the chiral transition temperature roughly reproduces the Monte Carlo results at beta=2N_c/g^2 \lesssim 4. The deconfinement transition is found to be the crossover and first order for light (am_0 \lesssim 4 at beta=4) and heavy quark masses, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including Polyakov loop effects in strong coupling lattice QCD suppresses the chiral condensate and reduces the chiral transition temperature at zero chemical potential, with the chiral transition temperature roughly reproducing Monte Carlo results at beta = 2N_c/g^2 less than or approximately equal to 4, though the analysis is limited to the leading order Polyakov loop action combined with next-to-next-to-leading order fermionic terms.

## Method

The authors develop a Polyakov loop extended strong coupling lattice QCD (P-SC-LQCD) framework by combining the leading order Polyakov loop effective action from the pure Yang-Mills sector with the next-to-next-to-leading order (1/g^4) fermionic effective action for one species of unrooted staggered fermion in color SU(3). They derive the effective potential at finite temperature and quark chemical potential using two approximation schemes: a Haar measure method where the Polyakov loop is replaced by its mean-field value without fluctuations, and a Weiss mean-field method that includes fluctuation effects of the Polyakov loop through bosonization of the Polyakov loop action via the Extended Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation.

## Key numbers

* Chiral transition temperature at mu=0 roughly reproduces Monte Carlo results at beta = 2N_c/g^2 less than or approximately equal to 4
* Deconfinement transition is crossover for light quark masses with a m_0 less than or approximately equal to 4 at beta=4
* Deconfinement transition is first order for heavy quark masses at beta=4
* The leading order Polyakov loop action coefficient is J = (1/g^2 N_c)^(N_tau) where N_tau is the temporal lattice size
* The pure gluonic potential coefficient is beta_p = (1/g^2 N_c)^(1/T) N_c^2 d where d=3 for 3+1 dimensions

## Context

This work extends the GOIK model developed by Gocksch and Ogilvie (1984) and Ilgenfritz and Kripfganz (1984), which included both chiral condensate and Polyakov loop at leading order, by incorporating finite coupling effects from the next-to-next-to-leading order fermionic action. The P-SC-LQCD framework provides a lattice-based alternative to the Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model developed by Fukushima (2003) and Ratti (2005), with the advantage of being directly comparable to Monte Carlo simulations at zero chemical potential. The results enable future investigation of the full phase diagram at finite chemical potential, where Monte Carlo simulations suffer from the sign problem.
