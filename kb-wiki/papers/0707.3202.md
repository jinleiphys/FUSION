# Stability in the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter formalism: harmonic-oscillator reduced Salpeter equation

> A popular three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for the description of bound states in quantum field theory is the Salpeter equation, derived by assuming both instantaneous interactions and free propagation of all bound-state constituents. Numerical (variational) studies of the Salpeter equation with confining interaction, however, observed specific instabilities of the solutions, likely related to the Klein paradox and rendering (part of the) bound states unstable. An analytic investigation of this problem by a comprehensive spectral analysis is feasible for the reduced Salpeter equation with only harmonic-oscillator confining interactions. There we are able to prove rigorously that the bound-state solutions correspond to real discrete energy spectra bounded from below and are thus free of any instabilities.

## Key claim
The paper proves rigorously that bound-state solutions of the reduced Salpeter equation with harmonic-oscillator confining interactions correspond to real discrete energy spectra bounded from below and are free of instabilities, contrary to numerical studies of the full Salpeter equation with confining interactions which observed instabilities likely related to the Klein paradox.

## Method
The authors analytically investigate the reduced Salpeter equation, a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism derived by assuming instantaneous interactions and free constituent propagation. For harmonic-oscillator confining potentials, the radial integral eigenvalue equations for pseudoscalar fermion-antifermion bound states simplify to second-order homogeneous linear differential equations. The authors then transform these differential equations into zero-eigenvalue Schrodinger equations and apply spectral analysis theorems, including the minimum-maximum principle, to prove self-adjointness and discreteness of the spectrum for various Lorentz structures of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel.

## Key numbers
- For Lorentz-scalar kernel with massless constituents (m=0), the reduced Salpeter equation simplifies to (2p + a/p^2) phi(p) = M phi(p), yielding a purely continuous spectrum with no bound states.
- For Lorentz-scalar kernel with nonvanishing mass (m=1) and negative harmonic-oscillator coupling (a=-10), the derivative of auxiliary eigenvalues satisfies d epsilon_i/dM <= 1/a < 0 for all i, proving a purely discrete spectrum.
- For time-component Lorentz-vector kernel with massless constituents (m=0), the effective potential V(p) = 2p/a + 1/p^2 is singular at p=0 but bounded below for a>0.
- For Lorentz-pseudoscalar kernel, the reduced Salpeter equation with harmonic-oscillator potential reduces to a pure multiplication operator, implying no bound states exist (purely continuous spectrum).

## Context
This work extends prior numerical studies by Lagae (1992), Olsson (1995, 1996), and Lucha (2000, 2001) that observed instabilities in Salpeter equation solutions with confining interactions. The paper provides an analytic proof of stability for the reduced Salpeter equation, complementing earlier analytic work by Bohm, Joos, and Krammer (1973) and Gross (1991) on specific Lorentz structures. The results enable rigorous justification for using the reduced Salpeter equation in describing hadrons as quark-antiquark bound states within quantum chromodynamics, particularly for the Bonn group's applications (Koll 2000, Ricken 2000, Merten 2001, Ricken 2003).
