# Charge dependence of $NN\to dπ$

> We calculate the isospin symmetry violating effects to the reactions $pp\rightarrow dπ^+$ and $np\rightarrow dπ^0$ arising from the different hadron masses and from the Coulomb interaction between the positive pion and the deuteron. These effects are large enough in the cross section and analyzing power $A_y$ that they should be taken into account in comparisons of accurate experiments in different charge channels.

## Key claim

The isospin symmetry violating effects from hadron mass differences and the Coulomb interaction between the positive pion and the deuteron are large enough in the cross section and analyzing power \(A_y\) that they must be taken into account when comparing accurate experiments in different charge channels, with the caveat that the calculation only covers "trivial" mass and Coulomb effects and excludes dynamic isotensor forces.

## Method

The authors calculated the charge-dependent effects in \(pp\rightarrow d\pi^+\) and \(np\rightarrow d\pi^0\) using a coupled-channels method that includes \(p\)-wave pion rescattering through the \(\Delta(1232)\) isobar and \(s\)-wave rescattering, with the Reid soft core wave function for the deuteron. They separated the kinematic effect from mass differences by comparing reactions at the same final pion momentum in the center-of-mass system, then incorporated final-state Coulomb corrections using three approaches: Coulomb penetration factors \(|C_l|^2\), replacement of plane wave pions by Coulomb scattering wave functions, and an estimate of the finite charge distribution of the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- The \(T_z=0\) reaction \(np\rightarrow d\pi^0\) has a 5.9 MeV lower threshold (CMS) than the \(T_z=1\) reaction \(pp\rightarrow d\pi^+\).
- At threshold, the mass difference effect alone gives \(\delta R/R_{\rm av} \approx +2\%\) from the Galilean invariance term, and the \(pp\) momentum is about 2% larger than the \(np\) momentum for the same pion momentum, making the \(pp\) cross section 4% smaller than the \(np\) cross section.
- With Coulomb suppression included, \(\delta R/R_{\rm av}\) approaches 2 at threshold.
- The finite charge distribution of the deuteron increases penetration by about 1.5% for \(s\) waves and 0.5% for \(p\) waves at low momenta.
- Using the Paris deuteron wave function instead of the Reid soft core changes the threshold production ratio by about +0.6% and \(\delta A_y\) by +0.01.
- Switching off the \(\Delta\) entirely increases the threshold \(\delta R/R_{\rm ave}\) by 2 percent units to 9%.
- The estimated uncertainty of the calculation is about one percent unit for \(\delta R/R_{\rm ave}\) and 0.01 for \(\delta A_y\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin symmetry breaking in pion production by providing the first systematic calculation of mass-difference and Coulomb effects in \(NN\rightarrow d\pi\) reactions, motivated by the need for quantitative estimates in precision experiments such as those by Lacker (1990s) and threshold measurements by Bondar, Meyer, and the COSY collaboration. The results enable more accurate comparisons between charged and neutral channels, which is important for normalizing neutron beam luminosity in \(np\) scattering experiments and for determining the charged pion coupling to nucleons, a topic of controversy discussed by Ericson (1990s). The authors note that dynamic isotensor forces from meson mixings and different pion masses in intermediate propagators are outside the scope of this work and are under investigation.
