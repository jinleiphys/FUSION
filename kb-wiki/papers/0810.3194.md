# Direct photon production in d+A and A+A collisions at RHIC

> Direct photon production in minimum bias d+Cu and d+Au and central Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at center of mass energies $\sqrt{s}=62.4$ GeV and 200GeV at RHIC is systematically investigated. We study the jet quenching effect, the medium-induced photon bremsstrahlung and jet-photon conversion in the hot QGP. We account for known cold nuclear matter effects, such as the isospin effect, the Cronin effect, shadowing and cold nuclear matter energy loss. It is shown that at high $p_T$ the nuclear modification factor for direct photons $R_{AA}^γ (p_T) < 1$ is dominated by cold nuclear matter effects and there is no evidence for large cross section amplification due to medium-induced photon bremsstrahlung and jet-photon conversion in the medium. Comparison of numerical simulations to experimental data also rules out large Cronin enhancement and incoherent photon emission in the QGP but the error bars in the current experimental data cannot provide further constraints on the magnitudes of other nuclear matter effects.

## Key claim

At high transverse momentum, the nuclear modification factor for direct photons \(R_{AA}^{\gamma}(p_T) < 1\) is dominated by cold nuclear matter effects, and there is no evidence for large cross section amplification due to medium-induced photon bremsstrahlung and jet-photon conversion in the quark-gluon plasma; the error bars in current experimental data cannot provide further constraints on the magnitudes of other nuclear matter effects.

## Method

The authors performed a leading-order perturbative QCD improved parton model calculation of direct photon production in minimum bias d+Cu and d+Au and central Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=62.4\) GeV and 200 GeV at RHIC. They included hot QGP effects (jet quenching via the GLV formalism, a newly derived medium-induced photon bremsstrahlung spectrum using the Reaction Operator approach, and jet-photon conversion) and cold nuclear matter effects (isospin, Cronin effect via \(k_T\) broadening, shadowing via higher-twist collinear factorization, initial-state energy loss, and the EMC effect). The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AB}^{\gamma}(p_T)\) was compared to PHENIX experimental data.

## Key numbers

- In d+A collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, cold nuclear matter effects reduce direct photon production at \(p_T \sim 15\) GeV by about 25%.
- In d+A collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=62.4\) GeV, cold nuclear matter effects reduce direct photon production at \(p_T \sim 15\) GeV by about 40%.
- For central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, medium-induced photons contribute approximately 10% to the observed cross section for \(p_T < 5\) GeV.
- For central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, inverse Compton scattering (jet-photon conversion) of energetic quarks in the QGP is a less than 25% correction.
- The average number of jet interactions in the medium is \(\langle n \rangle = L / \lambda_q = 2-3\).
- In p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, fragmentation photons yield approximately 25-30% of all direct photons at very high \(p_T\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of direct photon production in both p+A and A+A reactions by consistently accounting for all relevant hot and cold nuclear matter effects, which had not been done before in the literature. It contradicts earlier predictions of large QGP-induced enhancement of the direct photon cross section (e.g., Srivastava (2008), Turbide (2005)) and rules out large Cronin enhancement and incoherent photon emission in the QGP. The results enable better constraints on parton dynamics in large nuclei and support the theoretical developments in the GLV formalism (Gyulassy (2003)) and the new medium-induced photon calculations by the authors.
