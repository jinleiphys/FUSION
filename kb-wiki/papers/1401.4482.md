# Marrying ab initio calculations and Halo EFT: the case of ${}^7{\rm Be} + p \rightarrow {}^8{\rm B} + γ$

> We report a leading-order (LO) calculation of $^7\mathrm{Be}(p,γ)^8\mathrm{B}$ in a low-energy effective field theory. $^8\mathrm{B}$ is treated as a shallow proton$+^7\mathrm{Be}$ core and proton$+^7\mathrm{Be}^{*}$ (core excitation) $p$-wave bound state. The couplings are fixed using measured binding energies and proton-$^7\mathrm{Be}$ $s$-wave scattering lengths, together with $^8\mathrm{B}$ asymptotic normalization coefficients from ab initio calculations. We obtain a zero-energy $S$-factor of $18.2 \pm 1.2~({\rm ANC~only})$ eV b. Given that this is a LO result it is consistent with the recommended value $S(0)=20.8\pm1.6$ eV b. Our computed $S(E)$ compares favorably with experimental data on $^7\mathrm{Be}(p,γ)^8\mathrm{B}$ for $E <0.4$ MeV. We emphasize the important role of proton-$^7\mathrm{Be}$ scattering parameters in determining the energy dependence of $S(E)$, and demonstrate that their present uncertainties significantly limit attempts to extrapolate these data to stellar energies.

# Marrying ab initio calculations and Halo EFT: the case of 7Be + p → 8B + γ

## Key claim
A leading-order Halo EFT calculation of the 7Be(p,γ)8B S-factor yields S(0)=18.2±1.2 eV b, which is consistent with the recommended value of 20.8±1.6 eV b only when the approximately 10% higher-order EFT uncertainty is included. The energy dependence of S(E) above 0.1 MeV is significantly limited by present uncertainties in proton-7Be scattering parameters, not by the asymptotic normalization coefficients.

## Method
The authors constructed a Halo EFT Lagrangian treating 8B as a shallow p-wave bound state of a proton with either a 7Be core or a 7Be* core-excited state, with Coulomb interactions included to all orders in αEM. They fixed the EFT couplings using measured binding energies, experimental s-wave scattering lengths from Angulo (2003), and asymptotic normalization coefficients from ab initio calculations by Nollett (2011) and Navratil (2011), then computed the E1 capture amplitude at leading order including initial-state multiple scattering in s-wave channels.

## Key numbers
- Zero-energy S-factor S(0)=18.2±1.2 eV b (using Nollett ANCs + Angulo scattering lengths, ANC uncertainty only)
- Recommended value from Adelberger (2010): S(0)=20.8±1.6 eV b
- 8B binding energy: 0.1375 MeV, corresponding to binding momentum γ=15.04 MeV
- High-energy scale Λ≈70 MeV set by the 7Be→3He+4He threshold, giving γ/Λ≈0.2
- s-wave scattering lengths: a(3S1)=25±9 fm and a(5S2)=-7±3 fm from Angulo (2003); a(5S2)=-15.3 fm from Navratil (2011) ab initio calculation
- ANCs from Nollett (2011): C(3P2)=-0.315±0.019 fm^{-1/2}, C(5P2)=-0.662±0.019 fm^{-1/2}
- Core-excitation ANC: C(3P2*)=-0.3485±0.0051 fm^{-1/2}
- Scattering volume a1=1054.1 fm^3 and effective range r1=-0.34 fm^{-1} extracted from ab initio ANCs
- Quadratic expansion coefficients: d1=-1.62 MeV^{-1}, d2=10.3 MeV^{-2} (No+A input)

## Context
This work extends the Halo EFT treatment of E1 proton capture by Ryberg (2013) to the case of a p-wave bound state, and combines the EFT framework with ab initio eight-body calculations from Nollett (2011) and Navratil (2011). The approach contrasts with the Taylor expansion method used by Baye (2000, 2005) for describing S(E) near zero energy, as Halo EFT preserves the non-analytic dependence from Coulomb wave functions and strong initial-state interactions. The results demonstrate that improved measurements of p-7Be scattering lengths are needed to make the extrapolation of capture data to solar energies more reliable, supporting similar conclusions from other formalisms by Davids (2003) and Descouvemont (2004).
