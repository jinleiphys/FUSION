# Nuclear deformation as a source of the non-linearity of King plot in the Yb$^+$ ion

> We perform atomic relativistic many-body calculations of the field isotope shifts and calculations of corresponding nuclear parameters for all stable even-even isotopes of Yb$^+$ ion. We demonstrate that if we take nuclear parameters of the Yb isotopes from a range of the state-the-art nuclear models which all predict strong quadrupole nuclear deformation, then calculated non-linearity of the King plot, caused by the difference in the deformation in different isotopes, is consistent with the non-linearity observed in the experiment (Ian Counts {\em et al}, Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 125}, 123002 (2020)). The changes of nuclear RMS radius between isotopes extracted from experiment are consistent with those obtained in the nuclear calculations.

# Nuclear deformation as a source of the non-linearity of King plot in the Yb⁺ ion

## Key claim

The non-linearity of the King plot observed in Yb⁺ isotopes is more likely caused by the non-monotonic variation of nuclear quadrupole deformation across the isotope chain than by quadratic field shift (QFS) or physics beyond the Standard Model. The calculated non-linearity from nuclear deformation is consistent with experiment for a range of state-of-the-art nuclear models, but only when the deformation parameters are allowed to vary between isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed atomic relativistic many-body calculations of field isotope shifts (FIS) using the correlation potential method with Brueckner orbitals, treating Yb⁺ as a one-valence-electron system above closed shells. They calculated FIS by varying the nuclear potential and used the random phase approximation (RPA) to separate the small parameter of nuclear radius change, suppressing numerical noise. Nuclear parameters (RMS charge radii and quadrupole deformation β) were taken from five nuclear models: the BETA model (from experimental B(E2) values), the Covariant Density Functional Theory with four functionals (NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, DD-PC1), and a hypothetical FIT model. The FIS constants F, G⁽²⁾, and G⁽⁴⁾ were determined by fitting atomic calculations to the formula ν_FIS = F δ⟨r²⟩ + G⁽²⁾ δ⟨r²⟩² + G⁽⁴⁾ δ⟨r⁴⟩, and King plot non-linearities were computed from the deviations of isotope shifts from linear fits.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameters β for all even-even Yb isotopes (A=168–176) range from 0.30413 to 0.34540 across the nuclear models studied, with β ∼ 0.3 for all isotopes.
- The FIS constant F for the 6s₁/₂–5d₅/₂ transition in deformed nuclei is −17.6035 GHz/fm²; for the 6s₁/₂–5d₃/₂ transition it is −18.0028 GHz/fm².
- The G⁽⁴⁾ parameter for the 6s₁/₂–5d₅/₂ transition in deformed nuclei is 0.01308 GHz/fm⁴; for the 6s₁/₂–5d₃/₂ transition it is 0.01337 GHz/fm⁴.
- The G⁽²⁾ parameter is 0.02853 GHz/fm⁴ for both transitions in deformed nuclei.
- The non-linearity of the King plot (deviations from linearity in parts of 10⁻⁶) for the isotope pair 168–170 is −0.192 in experiment; the FIT model gives −0.206, while the BETA model gives 0.642.
- For the isotope pair 174–176, the experimental non-linearity is 0.411 × 10⁻⁶; the FIT model gives 0.448 × 10⁻⁶, and the BETA model gives 3.03 × 10⁻⁶.
- The QFS contribution to non-linearity is about an order of magnitude smaller than the experimental values, e.g., for the 168–170 pair the QFS gives −0.017 × 10⁻⁶ versus experimental −0.192 × 10⁻⁶.
- The change of nuclear RMS charge radius δ⟨r²⟩ between isotopes 168 and 170 is 0.138 fm² in this work, compared to 0.156 fm² (CI) and 0.149 fm² (MBPT) from Counts et al. (2020).
- The relation ⟨r⁴⟩ = [b₀ + b₁(r_c² − r₀²) + b₂(β − β₀)] r_c² has coefficients b₀ = 1.3129, b₁ = −0.0036, b₂ = 0.1 for deformed nuclei, with r₀ = 5.179 fm and β₀ = 0.305.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Counts et al. (2020), who observed King plot non-linearity in Yb⁺ and suggested QFS or beyond-Standard-Model physics as possible explanations. The authors had previously shown in Allehabi et al. (2020) that nuclear deformation can cause King plot non-linearity. The present paper demonstrates that the observed non-linearity is consistent with nuclear deformation across multiple nuclear models (BETA, NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, DD-PC1), while QFS alone is an order of magnitude too small. The results enable using King plot non-linearity measurements to study nuclear deformation in nuclei with zero spin, where electric quadrupole moments cannot be extracted from atomic spectroscopy. The extracted δ⟨r²⟩ values differ by 8–13% from those of Counts et al. (2020) and by 12–19% from the older results of Marinova (based on single-electron estimates from 1970s), attributed to differences in the many-body treatment of FIS constants.
