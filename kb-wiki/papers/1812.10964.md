# Casimir effect for nucleon parity doublets

> Finite-volume effects for the nucleon chiral partners are studied within the framework of the parity-doublet model. Our model includes the vacuum energy shift for nucleons, which is the Casimir effect. We find that for the antiperiodic boundary the finite-volume effect leads to chiral symmetry restoration, and the masses of the nucleon parity doublets degenerate. For the periodic boundary, the chiral symmetry breaking is enhanced, and the masses of the nucleons also increase. We also discuss the finite-temperature effect and the dependence on the number of compactified spatial dimensions.

# Casimir effect for nucleon parity doublets

## Key claim

Within the parity-doublet model, finite-volume effects from compactifying one spatial dimension can either restore chiral symmetry (for antiperiodic boundary conditions) or enhance chiral symmetry breaking (for periodic boundary conditions), with the nucleon parity doublet masses becoming degenerate at small volume in the antiperiodic case. The hedge is that these results are obtained at zero chemical potential and within the mean-field approximation, and the transition length scale is comparable to that of the chiral condensate (approximately 200 MeV).

## Method

The authors place the parity-doublet model (with mirror assignment) and the Walecka model in a finite spatial box of length L with one compactified dimension, implementing the finite-volume effects through the Casimir energy for massive fermions. They solve the gap equations for the mean fields (sigma and omega) at zero and finite temperature, using the modified Bessel function expansion for the Casimir energy truncated at n=5, and compare results for antiperiodic and periodic boundary conditions with different numbers of compactified dimensions (delta=2,3,4).

## Key numbers

- Chiral invariant mass m0 = 790 MeV (parity-doublet model parameter from Zschiesche (2006))
- At T=0 with antiperiodic boundary, the transition length where masses begin to degenerate is approximately L ~ 1 fm (~0.005 MeV^{-1})
- For antiperiodic boundary, around L ~ 0.8 fm the mass splitting is dominated by the linear sigma term since sigma is finite but small
- For periodic boundary, below L ~ 0.6 fm (~0.003 MeV^{-1}) the nucleon masses become independent of temperature
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV and pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV used as input parameters
- Coupling constants in parity-doublet model: g1 = 13.0, g2 = 6.97, g_omega = 6.79
- Walecka model parameters: m_N = 939 MeV, m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, g_sigma = 10.3, g_omega = 12.7

## Context

This work extends finite-volume studies of chiral symmetry breaking/restoration previously performed with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Abreu (2006), Ebert (2010), Hayashi (2010)) and linear sigma models (Braun (2004-2011), Palhares (2009), Tripolt (2013)) to the nucleon parity-doublet framework. It is the first application of the parity-doublet model with mirror assignment (originally proposed by DeTar and Kunihiro (1988)) to finite-volume systems, and complements lattice QCD studies of nucleon parity doublet degeneracy at high temperature (Aarts (2015, 2017, 2018)). The results suggest that small-volume systems could provide a new way to extract the chiral invariant mass m0, distinct from finite-temperature or finite-density approaches.
