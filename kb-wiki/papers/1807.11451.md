# Probing the topological charge in QCD matter via multiplicity up-down asymmetry

> Relativistic heavy ion collisions provide the possibility to study the topological charge in QCD matter through the event-by-event fluctuating net axial charge or nonequal numbers of left- and right-handed quarks they generate in the produced quark-gluon plasma. Based on the chiral kinetic approach for nearly massless quarks and antiquarks in the strong vorticity field produced along the normal direction of the reaction plane of non-central heavy ion collisions, we show that a unique signal for the topological charge in QCD matter can be identified from the asymmetric distribution of particles with momenta pointing in the upper and lower hemispheres of the reaction plane as a result of the fluctuating net axial charge.

# Probing the topological charge in QCD matter via multiplicity up-down asymmetry

## Key claim

The multiplicity up-down asymmetry of particles with respect to the reaction plane in non-central heavy ion collisions is sensitive to the event-by-event fluctuating net axial charge in the produced partonic matter, providing a more promising probe of topological charge in QCD matter than the chiral magnetic effect (CME) or chiral vortical effect (CVE), because the vorticity field decays slowly with time unlike the short-lived magnetic field required for CME signals.

## Method

The authors used the chiral kinetic approach for nearly massless quarks and antiquarks, evolving them under both magnetic and vorticity fields with vector-charge-conserving scatterings. Initial parton phase-space distributions were taken from the string melting version of the AMPT model for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=62.4 GeV and 30-40% centrality, with event-by-event fluctuations in net axial charge introduced by assigning each event either more right-handed or more left-handed quarks with probability (1+p)/2, where p = sqrt(<N_5^2>)/N. The vorticity field was calculated from the local average velocity of partons, while the magnetic field was obtained from the Lienard-Wiechert potential of spectator protons.

## Key numbers

- The parton scattering cross section was chosen to reproduce eta/s = 1.5/(4pi), as determined from anisotropic flow measurements using viscous hydrodynamics.
- The evolution was stopped when the energy density decreased to epsilon_0 = 0.56 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to a switching temperature T_SW = 165 MeV from partonic to hadronic phase.
- For mid-pseudorapidity light quarks in the transverse momentum range 0.05 < p_T < 2 GeV/c, the average inverse particle number was <1/N> = 2.133 x 10^{-3} and the average CVE coefficient was <a_CVE> = +/- 9.82 x 10^{-3} for events with net axial charge fluctuation p=0.4.
- For the smaller transverse momentum range 0.15 < p_T < 2 GeV/c, <1/N> = 2.914 x 10^{-3} and <a_CVE> = +/- 8.29 x 10^{-3}.
- The magnetic field lifetime required for CME to account for experimental data is at least tau_B = 0.6 fm/c, which is not supported by lattice QCD calculations of QGP electric conductivity.

## Context

This work extends the study of topological charge effects in QCD matter beyond the chiral magnetic effect (CME) proposed by Kharzeev (2007) and the chiral vortical effect (CVE), which suffer from short magnetic field lifetimes and small net baryon densities respectively. The authors build on prior chiral kinetic approach developments (Huang 2018, Sun 2018) and use the AMPT model (Lin 2005) for realistic initial conditions. The proposed multiplicity up-down asymmetry measurement enables a new experimental probe of topological charge fluctuations that avoids the limitations of CME and CVE signals, and could be tested at RHIC energies.
