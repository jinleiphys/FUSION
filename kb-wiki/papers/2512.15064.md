# Three-dimensional imaging of hadrons with hard exclusive reactions: advances in experiment, theory, phenomenology, and lattice QCD

> Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) have emerged as a powerful framework for exploring the internal structure of hadrons in terms of their partonic constituents. Over the past three decades, the field has witnessed significant theoretical and experimental advancements. The interpretation of GPDs in impact parameter space offers a vivid three-dimensional visualization of hadron structure, correlating longitudinal momentum and transverse spatial distributions, thereby enabling tomographic imaging of hadrons. Furthermore, the link between GPDs and the matrix elements of the QCD energy-momentum tensor provides access to fundamental properties of hadrons, including spin decomposition and internal pressure distributions. Notably, recent analyses of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) data have enabled the empirical extraction of the quark pressure profile inside the proton. This white paper presents an overview of recent developments in GPD theory and phenomenology, as well as progress in lattice QCD studies. It outlines the prospects for advancing our understanding of hadron structure through the next generation of dedicated experiments, including the extension of the Jefferson Lab 12~GeV program (and its potential 22~GeV upgrade), J-PARC, COMPASS/AMBER, LHC ultra-peripheral collisions, and the future electron-ion colliders EIC and EicC.

## Key claim

This white paper argues that Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) provide a powerful framework for three-dimensional tomographic imaging of hadrons, but the field requires coordinated advances across experiment, theory, phenomenology, and lattice QCD to transform GPD studies from model-driven exploration into a precision science, with the next generation of dedicated experiments at facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Jefferson Lab 22 GeV upgrade being essential for progress.

## Method

The paper synthesizes discussions from two workshops held in 2024 (Incheon, Korea and Trento, Italy) that reviewed the current status of GPD research. It surveys developments in hard exclusive reactions such as Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) and Deeply Virtual Meson Production (DVMP), covering theoretical advances including next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) corrections, higher-twist effects, and kinematic corrections, as well as progress in lattice QCD methods using parton quasi-distributions and large momentum effective theory (LaMET). The paper also reviews experimental programs at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV, COMPASS, HERA, and future facilities including the EIC, EicC, J-PARC, and LHC ultra-peripheral collisions.

## Key numbers

* The Jefferson Lab 12 GeV program is currently underway, with a potential upgrade to 22 GeV discussed in the paper (Accardi, 2023)
* The paper references the first extraction of the proton D-term form factor from JLab DVCS data (Burkert, 2018), which revealed the quark component of the pressure distribution in the proton
* Gluonic gravitational form factors of the proton have been extracted from threshold J/psi photoproduction data (Duran, 2022)

## Context

This white paper extends the long line of GPD research initiated by Mueller (1994), Ji (1996), and Radyushkin (1996), and builds upon the review papers by Goeke (2001), Diehl (2003), and Belitsky (2005). It summarizes the outcomes of two 2024 workshops and aims to establish guidelines for upcoming experimental programs at the EIC, EicC, JLab 22 GeV upgrade, J-PARC, and COMPASS/AMBER, while also integrating novel approaches from lattice QCD and machine learning into hadron structure studies. The paper enables the community to coordinate efforts toward precision GPD extraction and hadron tomography, particularly through the Quark-Gluon Tomography (QGT) Topical Collaboration supported by the U.S. Department of Energy.
