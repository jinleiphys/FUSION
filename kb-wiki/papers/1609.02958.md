# Hydrodynamic Predictions for Pb+Pb Collisions at 5.02 A TeV

> Predictions and comparisons of hadronic flow observables for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV and 5.02 A TeV are presented using a hydrodynamics + hadronic cascade hybrid approach. Initial conditions are generated via a new formulation of the IP-Glasma model and then evolved using relativistic viscous hydrodynamics and finally fed into transport cascade in the hadronic phase. The results of this work show excellent agreement with the recent charged hadron anisotropic flow measurements from the ALICE collaboration of Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 A TeV. Event-by-event distributions of charged hadron v n , flow event-plane correlations, and flow factorization breaking ratios are compared with existing measurements at 2.76 A TeV, and are predicted at 5.02 A TeV. Further predictions of identified hadron observables (for both light and multi-strange hadrons), such as p T -spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients, are presented.

## Key claim

The hybrid hydrodynamics + hadronic cascade framework, using a new IP-Glasma implementation with shear viscosity η/s = 0.095 and a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity ζ/s(T), reproduces charged hadron anisotropic flow measurements at both 2.76 A TeV and 5.02 A TeV without changing the transport coefficients, provided the initial energy density normalization is adjusted to match the charged hadron multiplicity in 0-5% central collisions.

## Method

The authors generated initial conditions using a new numerical implementation of the IP-Glasma model, which includes sub-nucleonic color charge fluctuations and saturation physics, on a lattice with spacing dx = dy = 0.1 GeV⁻¹. These initial conditions were evolved from τ₀ = 0.4 fm using the relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code MUSIC with shear viscosity η/s = 0.095 and a parameterized temperature-dependent bulk viscosity ζ/s(T) peaking at Tp = 180 MeV, using the lattice QCD equation of state s95p-v1. The hydrodynamic evolution was matched at a switching temperature Tsw = 145 MeV to the hadronic cascade model UrQMD, which simulates microscopic hadronic scatterings and resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.095 (fixed for both collision energies)
- Peak temperature of bulk viscosity parameterization: Tp = 180 MeV
- Switching temperature between hydrodynamics and hadronic cascade: Tsw = 145 MeV
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ₀ = 0.4 fm
- Lattice spacing in IP-Glasma simulation: dx = dy = 0.1 GeV⁻¹
- Nuclear radius for ²⁰⁸Pb: R = 6.62 fm; skin depth: a = 0.546 fm
- Proton gluonic radius parameter: BG = 4.0 GeV⁻²
- Infrared regulator mass: m = λQCD = 0.2 GeV
- Overall normalization factor on initial energy density: 0.89 (to match measured dNch/dη in 0-5% centrality)
- Charged hadron v₂{2} increased by (4.1 ± 1.7)%, v₃{2} by (5.1 ± 2.2)%, and v₄{2} by (6.2 ± 2.3)% from 2.76 to 5.02 TeV
- Peak temperature in central Pb+Pb collisions: ~450 MeV at 2.76 TeV; ~20 MeV higher at 5.02 TeV
- Oversampling factor for hydrodynamic freeze-out surfaces: 100 events per hydrodynamic event
- Number of IP-Glasma events per 10% centrality bin: 1,500
- 100% centrality cutoff at 5.02 TeV: dE/dηs|ηs=0 ≈ 11 GeV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid hydrodynamics + hadronic cascade framework previously applied to Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (Ryu 2015) to the higher LHC energy of 5.02 A TeV, providing the first event-by-event flow distributions within such a hybrid approach at both energies. The IP-Glasma initial conditions build on the formulations of Schenke (2012) and the IP-Sat model of Kowalski (2003), while the viscous hydrodynamic treatment with bulk viscosity follows the Bayesian methodology of PhysRevC.94.024907. The predictions for identified hadron observables (π⁺, K⁺, p, φ, Ξ, Ω) and flow correlation observables at 5.02 TeV fill a void in the literature compared to prior work by Niemi (2015), Noronha-Hostler (2015), and Ma (2016).
