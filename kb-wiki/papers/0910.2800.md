# Charge-symmetry-breaking nucleon form factors

> A quantitative understanding of charge-symmetry breaking is an increasingly important ingredient for the extraction of the nucleon's strange vector form factors. We review the theoretical understanding of the charge-symmetry-breaking form factors, both for single nucleons and for Helium-4.

## Key claim

Charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) nucleon form factors must be calculated theoretically to reliably extract strangeness contributions from parity-violating electron scattering experiments; the CSB effects are small (at the percent level) but become essential as experimental bounds on strangeness tighten.

## Method

The author combines chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) up to order \(p^4\) for the electric and \(p^5\) for the magnetic CSB form factors with resonance saturation via vector-meson contributions (specifically \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing) to estimate the low-energy constants. The leading moments, magnetic moment \(\kappa^{u,d}\) and electric/magnetic radius terms \(\rho_E^{u,d}\), \(\rho_M^{u,d}\), are expressed in terms of the neutron-proton mass difference \(\Delta m\) and pion-loop contributions, while the vector-meson mixing amplitudes are fitted to experimental data on coupling constants. The formalism is extended to isospin mixing in Helium-4 for parity-violating electron scattering.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-violating magnetic moment at zero momentum transfer is \(\kappa^{u,d} = 0.025 \pm 0.020\) (dimensionless, from ChPT plus resonance saturation).
- The electric radius parameter \(\rho_E^{u,d} = \frac{5\pi C}{6M_\pi m_N}\) and magnetic radius parameter \(\rho_M^{u,d} = \frac{2C}{3M_\pi^2}\bigl\{1-\frac{7\pi}{4}\frac{M_\pi}{m_N}\bigr\}\), where \(C = \frac{g_A^2 m_N \Delta m}{16\pi^2F_\pi^2}\) (entirely non-analytic in quark masses).
- At \(t \approx -0.1\,\text{GeV}^2\), the CSB form factors for SAMPLE give \(G^{u,d} = 0.02\ldots 0.05\) (magnetic), for A4 give \(G_E+0.106\,G_M = 0.004\ldots 0.010\), and for HAPPEX give \(G_E+0.080\,G_M = 0.004\ldots 0.009\).
- At \(t=-0.22\,\text{GeV}^2\), isospin breaking shifts the A4 strange form factors from \(G_E^s = 0.050 \pm 0.038 \pm 0.019\) to \(0.045\) and \(G_M^s = -0.14 \pm 0.11 \pm 0.11\) to \(-0.18\), with theoretical uncertainties of \(\pm 0.002\) and \(\pm 0.01\) respectively.
- For Helium-4 at \(t=-0.077\,\text{GeV}^2\), the measured asymmetry \(A_{PV} = [+6.40 \pm 0.23_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.12_{\text{syst}}] \times 10^{-6}\) yields \(\Gamma = 0.010 \pm 0.038\); single-nucleon isospin violation contributes \(0.008\pm0.003\) to \(\Gamma\), nuclear isospin mixing contributes \(\approx 0.003\), leaving \(G_E^s = -0.001 \pm 0.016\).

## Context

This work extends earlier model calculations of CSB form factors, specifically constituent quark models by Dmitrasinovic (1995) and Miller (1997), and light-cone meson-baryon models by Ma (1997), by using chiral perturbation theory, which respects Standard Model symmetries and allows systematic uncertainty quantification. The author argues that quark models incorrectly enforce \(G_M^{u,d}(0)=0\) due to a symmetry of their wave functions, leading to underestimation of isospin-breaking effects at low momentum transfer. The results enable precision extraction of strange vector form factors from parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as SAMPLE, A4, HAPPEX, and the Helium-4 measurement by Acha (2006); the CSB corrections are now comparable to experimental uncertainties and must be included in future combined analyses.
