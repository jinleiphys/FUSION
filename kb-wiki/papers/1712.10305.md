# Hydro+: hydrodynamics with parametric slowing down and fluctuations near the critical point

> The search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collision experiments requires dynamical simulations of the bulk evolution of QCD matter as well as of fluctuations. We consider two essential ingredients of such a simulation: a generic extension of hydrodynamics by a_parametrically_ slow mode or modes ("Hydro+") and a description of fluctuations out of equilibrium. By combining the two ingredients we are able to describe the bulk evolution and the fluctutations within the same framework. Critical slowing down means that equilibration of fluctuations could be as slow as hydrodynamic evolution and thus fluctuations could significantly deviate from equilibrium near the critical point. We generalize hydrodynamics to partial-equilibrium conditions where the state of the system is characterized by the off-equilibrium magnitude of fluctuations in addition to the usual hydrodynamic variables -- conserved densities. We find that the key element of the new formalism -- the extended entropy taking into account the off-equilibrium fluctuations -- is remarkably similar to the 2PI action in quantum field theory. We show how the new Hydro+ formalism reproduces two major effects of critical fluctuations on the bulk evolution: the strong frequency dependence of the anomalously large bulk viscosity as well as the stiffening of the equation of state with increasing frequency or wave-number. While the agreement with known results confirms its validity, the fact that Hydro+ achieves this within a local and deterministic framework gives it significant advantages for dynamical simulations.

## Key claim

Hydro+ extends conventional hydrodynamics by incorporating a parametrically slow mode (or modes) that describes the relaxation of fluctuations near the QCD critical point, enabling a local and deterministic framework that captures both the frequency-dependent bulk viscosity and the stiffening of the equation of state, which ordinary hydrodynamics cannot describe when the correlation length diverges and critical slowing down occurs.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized hydrodynamic theory by adding a non-hydrodynamic scalar mode φ to the usual conserved densities (energy density e and charge density n), defining a partial-equilibrium entropy s₊(e, n, φ) and its associated thermodynamic potentials. They derive evolution equations for the conserved densities and the slow mode, constrained by the second law of thermodynamics, and then identify the slowest fluctuation mode near the QCD critical point using a 2PI (two-particle irreducible) entropy formalism that treats the magnitude of fluctuations as an additional degree of freedom. The resulting Hydro+ equations are solved in linear response to obtain the frequency-dependent bulk viscosity and sound speed, and the formalism is validated by comparing with Onuki's stochastic hydrodynamic model of critical fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* Bulk viscosity diverges as ζ ∼ ξ³ near the critical point, where ξ is the correlation length and the exponent z ≈ 3 gives the dynamic scaling.
* The relaxation rate of the slow mode Γπ vanishes as the critical point is approached, leading to Δζ(0) = β pπ²/(φπ Γπ) for the zero-frequency contribution to bulk viscosity.
* The frequency-dependent bulk viscosity follows Δζ(ω) = Δζ(0) Γπ²/(Γπ² + ω²), dropping off when ω ≳ Γπ.
* The sound speed squared increases by Δcₛ²(∞) = β pπ²/(w φπ) at high frequencies ω ≫ Γπ, related to bulk viscosity via the Landau-Khalatnikov formula ζ(0) = w Δcₛ²(∞)/Γπ.
* Sound attenuation changes from Im ω = −(k²/2) Δζ(0)/w at ω ≪ Γπ to Im ω = −(Γπ/2) Δcₛ²(∞)/c₊² at ω ≫ Γπ.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic description of heavy-ion collisions into the regime of finite baryon densities near the QCD critical point, where critical slowing down invalidates ordinary hydrodynamics because the relaxation time of fluctuations grows as ξ³ while the correlation length ξ diverges. The formalism generalizes earlier work by Andreev (1970, 1978), Lebedev (1981, 1982), and Kawasaki (1970) on kinetic equations for correlation functions near critical points, and reproduces the frequency-dependent bulk viscosity results of Onuki (1997, 2002) obtained from stochastic hydrodynamics. The Hydro+ framework enables dynamical simulations of the fireball evolution in heavy-ion collisions at the beam energy scan, where the critical point search requires treating fluctuations and bulk evolution within a single local deterministic theory.
