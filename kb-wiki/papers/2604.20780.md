# Probing QCD instantons using jet correlation observables in proton-proton collisions at the LHC

> Discovery of instantons in colliders will provide experimental evidence for the topological properties of the QCD vacuum. In this work, we propose jet correlation observables that can unambiguously discriminate between instanton-induced processes and perturbative hard scattering events in pp collisions at LHC energies. By calculating the instanton sizes and their separations in 2+1 flavor QCD with physical quark masses, we provide constraints on the center-of-mass energies of the produced hadrons in an instanton-induced process. Our proposal is directly applicable for future ep measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider, offering a cleaner environment to probe instanton-induced processes.

## Key claim

Jet acoplanarity observables, specifically the azimuthal separation \(\Delta\phi\) between leading and subleading jets and its second harmonic moment \(\langle\cos(2\Delta\phi)\rangle\), can discriminate instanton-induced processes from perturbative dijet events in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, provided the instanton sizes are constrained by lattice QCD calculations with physical quark masses.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations in 2+1 flavor QCD with physical quark masses using overlap fermions on \(32^3 \times 8\) lattices at \(T = 149\) MeV to extract instanton size distributions and inter-instanton separations from Dirac zero eigenmodes. They then used the SHERPA event generator to simulate instanton-induced events at \(\sqrt{s} = 13\) TeV for two values of the hadronic center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s^{'}} = 50\) and 100 GeV, and compared the resulting jet acoplanarity distributions and \(\langle\cos(2\Delta\phi)\rangle\) values against perturbative dijet events generated with both SHERPA and PYTHIA8.

## Key numbers

- Average instanton size \(\langle \rho \rangle = 0.65(1)\) fm from lattice QCD with physical quark masses
- Average separation between instanton and anti-instanton pairs \(\langle R \rangle \sim 2.43(4) \langle \rho \rangle\)
- Instanton-induced subprocess cross-sections in SHERPA: \(3.89 \times 10^{4}\) pb for \(\sqrt{s^{'}} = 50\) GeV and \(5.44 \times 10^{2}\) pb for \(\sqrt{s^{'}} = 100\) GeV
- Lattice box size \(\sim 5.3\) fm with lattice spacing \(a \sim 0.16\) fm, analyzed over \(\sim 84\) independent gauge configurations
- Pion and kaon masses tuned to physical values of 135 MeV and 435 MeV respectively
- Leading jet transverse momentum threshold \(p_{\rm T, jet}^{\rm L} > 25\) GeV, subleading jet threshold \(10\) GeV \(< p_{\rm T, jet}^{\rm SL} < p_{\rm T, jet}^{\rm L}\)
- Jet radius \(R = 0.3\) used for jet reconstruction

## Context

This work extends earlier instanton searches at HERA by the H1 Collaboration (2002, 2016) and ZEUS Collaboration (2003), which found no conclusive signal, by proposing new jet-based observables for LHC proton-proton collisions. The lattice QCD calculations provide the first constraints on instanton sizes in 2+1 flavor QCD with physical quark masses, improving upon earlier quenched QCD estimates used by Ringwald (1999) for fiducial cuts. The results enable future searches at the LHC and at the Electron-Ion Collider, where the cleaner environment may offer better sensitivity to instanton-induced processes.
