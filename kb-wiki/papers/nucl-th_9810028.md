# Structure of the ^3He in Backward Elastic p ^3He-Scattering

> Backward elastic $p ^3He$-scattering at incident proton kinetic energies $T_p>1$ GeV is investigated in the framework of the np-pair transfer mechanism and triangular diagram of one-pion exchange with a subprocess $pd\to ^3Heπ^0$ using a realistic three-body wave function of the $^3He$ nucleus. It is found that the $np-$pair transfer mechanism dominates owing to a rich high momentum component of the $^3He$ wave function. We show that the experimental cross section of this process is defined mainly by the values of the Faddeev component of the $^3He$ wave function, $ϕ^{23}({\bf q}_{23}, {\bf p}_1)$, at high relative momenta $q_{23}> 0.6 GeV/c$ of the NN-pair in the $^1S_0$-state and at low spectator momenta $p_1\leq 0.1$ GeV/c. The spin-spin correlation parameter is calculated in the framework of the dominating mechanism for the case of polarized target and beam. Rescatterings in the initial and final states are taken into account. Comparison with the $pd\to dp$ process is performed.

## Key claim

The absolute value of the backward elastic p^3He cross section at 180 degrees and incident proton kinetic energies above 1 GeV is determined mainly by the high momentum component of the Faddeev S-wave function of ^3He associated with the relative momentum of the NN-pair, while the "spectator" momentum remains low; this contradicts earlier claims that the sensitivity to high momentum components is weak.

## Method

The np-pair transfer mechanism (sequential transfer, nonsequential transfer, and interacting pair transfer) was calculated in the Born approximation using a realistic three-body Faddeev wave function of ^3He from the solution of Faddeev equations with the Reid soft core potential. The triangular diagram of one-pion exchange with the subprocess pd → ^3Heπ^0 was also evaluated, and rescatterings in the initial and final states were taken into account using Glauber-Sitenko theory. The spin-spin correlation parameter was calculated for the case of polarized target and beam.

## Key numbers

- The cross section at θ_cm = 180° is defined mainly by the Faddeev component φ^23(q_23, p_1) at high relative momenta q_23 > 0.6 GeV/c of the NN-pair in the ^1S_0-state and at low spectator momenta p_1 ≤ 0.1 GeV/c
- If the channel ν=1 (corresponding to L=0, S=0, T=1 of the NN-pair) is excluded from the full wave function, the cross section falls by 5-6 orders of magnitude for T_p > 1 GeV
- The cross section calculated with the complementary parts (excluding high q_23 components) is 5-6 orders of magnitude smaller for ν=1 and 4-6 times smaller for the channel ν=2
- The cutoff parameter Λ_π in the monopole πNN formfactor is 0.65 GeV/c
- The spectroscopic factor S_pd^h of ^3He in the channel d+p is approximately 1.5

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Uzikov and collaborators (1992, 1993) that found weak sensitivity of the p^3He cross section to high momentum components, and contradicts the conclusion that the triangular OPE diagram with Δ-excitation gives qualitative agreement with data as suggested by Berth (1994). The paper shows that the np-pair transfer mechanism dominates over the OPE mechanism due to the rich high momentum component of the ^3He wave function, and that the cross section can probe the short-range structure of ^3He at NN-distances r_NN ≤ 0.5 fm. This enables future extraction of information about the high momentum components of the ^3He wave function from experimental cross section data.
