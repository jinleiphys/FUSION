# Effects of Model Parameters in Thermodynamics of the PNJL Model

> The thermodynamic behavior of the two-flavor($N_f=$2) three-color ($N_c=3$) Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at the finite chemical potential is investigated. New lattice gluon data for gluon thermodynamics are used defining the effective potential within polynomial and logarithmic forms of its approximation. We study the effects of using different sets of data and different forms of the potential on thermodynamic properties of hot and dense matter. It is found that the PNJL thermodynamics depends stronger on the form of the effective potential than on the used lattice data set. Particular attention is paid to the phase diagram in the $(T,μ)$ plane.

## Key claim

The thermodynamic behavior of the two-flavor, three-color Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model depends more strongly on the chosen functional form of the effective Polyakov-loop potential (polynomial versus logarithmic) than on which set of pure-gluon lattice data (old data from Boyd (1996) or new data from Panero (2009)) is used to fix the potential's parameters.

## Method

The authors constructed the PNJL model in the mean-field approximation for two quark flavors, using both a polynomial and a logarithmic parameterization of the effective Polyakov-loop potential. For each parameterization, they fitted the potential to two different sets of pure-gauge lattice QCD data for the pressure, obtaining two parameter sets ("old" and "new"). They then solved the gap equations for the constituent quark mass, the Polyakov loop, and the meson masses, and computed thermodynamic quantities (pressure, energy density, quark number density, baryon number susceptibility) at finite temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\), comparing the results to lattice QCD data for two-flavor QCD.

## Key numbers

*   The critical temperature for the pure gauge sector was set to \(T_0 = 270\) MeV.
*   The model parameters fitted to vacuum observables are: current quark mass \(m_0 = 5.5\) MeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.639\) GeV, and coupling constant \(G = 5.227\) GeV\(^{-2}\), reproducing the pion mass \(M_\pi = 0.139\) GeV, the pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 0.092\) GeV, and the quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q} q\rangle^{1/3} = -250\) MeV.
*   At \(\mu = 0\), the pseudo-critical temperatures for the chiral crossover (\(T_\chi\)) and deconfinement crossover (\(T_p\)) differ by less than 0.013 GeV; for the new polynomial parameter set, \(T_\chi = 0.2455\) GeV and \(T_p = 0.2335\) GeV.
*   The positions of the critical endpoint (CEP) in the \((T, \mu)\) plane are: for the logarithmic form with new data, \((T_{CEP}, \mu_{CEP}) = (0.118, 0.3166)\) GeV; for the logarithmic form with old data, \((0.11, 0.3192)\) GeV; for the polynomial form with new data, \((0.10, 0.3175)\) GeV; for the polynomial form with old data, \((0.09, 0.322)\) GeV.
*   The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for the scaled pressure in the PNJL model is \(p_{SB}/T^4 \approx 4.053\).

## Context

This work extends prior PNJL model studies by Ratti (2006) and others, which used the older pure-gluon lattice data from Boyd (1996). It directly compares the impact of using newer lattice data from Panero (2009) against the older data, and systematically contrasts the polynomial and logarithmic forms of the effective Polyakov-loop potential. The results enable a more informed choice of model parameterization for future studies of the QCD phase diagram, and the authors note that further improvements, such as including entanglement interactions as in Sakai (2011) or diquark degrees of freedom as in Radzhabov (2007), are needed to bring the CEP position closer to lattice QCD predictions.
