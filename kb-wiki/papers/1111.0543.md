# Fluctuations in the number of intermediate mass fragments in small projectile like fragments

> The origin of fluctuations in the average number of intermediate mass fragments seen in experiments in small projectile like fragments is discussed. We argue that these can be explained on the basis of a recently proposed model of projectile fragmentation.

## Key claim
Fluctuations in the average number of intermediate mass fragments (IMF) at low values of \(Z_{bound}\) arise from the specific decay properties of small, fragile nuclei (especially beryllium isotopes) and from the mismatch between the experimental event-by-event integer \(Z_{bound}\) and the theoretical average non-integer \(Z_{bound}\) used in prior calculations.

## Method
The authors use a three-step model of projectile fragmentation: abrasion shears off a projectile-like fragment (PLF) with given charge \(Z_s\) and neutron number \(N_s\); the hot PLF expands to one-third normal density and breaks up via a canonical thermodynamical model (CTM) at a temperature \(T=7\) MeV; the hot composites then decay. For small PLFs, the authors replace the liquid-drop model and Fermi-gas excited states with experimental ground-state and excited-state energies (up to 7.5 MeV) and replace the evaporation code with actual decay data from tables. They compute the probability of obtaining a given integer \(Z_{bound}\) and the associated average IMF multiplicity \(M_{IMF}\) for each \((Z_s, N_s)\) combination, then weight by abrasion cross-sections for three reactions: \(^{107}\)Sn, \(^{124}\)Sn, and \(^{124}\)La on \(^{119}\)Sn.

## Key numbers
- For \(Z_{bound}=4\), the calculated \(M_{IMF}\) values are: 0.145 for \(^{107}\)Sn beam (experimental: 0.14), 0.151 for \(^{124}\)La beam (experimental: 0.178), and 0.38 for \(^{124}\)Sn beam (experimental: 0.0).
- For \(Z_{bound}=3\), \(M_{IMF}=1.000\) for all three reactions (experimental).
- For \(Z_{bound}=5\), \(M_{IMF}=1.000\) for all three reactions (experimental).
- For \(^{7}_4\)Be at \(T=7\) MeV, the probability of a particle-stable state giving \(Z_{bound}=4\) and one IMF is 0.406; the remaining states decay to \(^{4}_2\)He + \(^{3}_2\)He giving \(Z_{bound}=4\) and \(N_{IMF}=0\).
- For \(^{9}_4\)Be at \(T=7\) MeV, the ground-state occupation probability is 0.193, giving \(Z_{bound}=4\) and \(M_{IMF}=0.193\).
- For \(^{10}_4\)Be at \(T=7\) MeV, levels up to 6.26 MeV (summed occupation probability 0.604) give \(Z_{bound}=4\) and one IMF; higher levels give zero IMF, yielding \(M_{IMF}=0.604\).
- For \(^{11}_4\)Be at \(T=7\) MeV, the lowest two levels (occupation probability 0.1567) give \(Z_{bound}=4\) and one IMF; higher levels (occupation probability 0.8433) decay to \(^{10}_4\)Be + n, assigned \(M_{IMF}=0.604\), yielding a combined \(M_{IMF}=0.666\).
- For \(Z_s=4, N_s=5\), the probability of reaching \(Z_{bound}=4\) is 0.569 with \(M_{IMF}=0.165\).

## Context
This work extends the projectile fragmentation model of Mallik et al. (2011) by addressing fluctuations in \(M_{IMF}\) at low \(Z_{bound}\) that the original model missed. The authors show that using experimental nuclear data (from Ajzenberg-Selove and Tilley compilations) instead of liquid-drop and Fermi-gas approximations, and correctly handling the integer nature of \(Z_{bound}\) event-by-event, reproduces the observed dips at \(Z_{bound}=4\) and \(Z_{bound}=6\). The fragility of beryllium isotopes is identified as the key cause. This enables more precise modeling of small projectile-like fragments in heavy-ion collisions at GSI Darmstadt (SIS synchrotron) and clarifies the limitations of using average multiplicities for exclusive observables.
