# Entanglement entropy in a time-dependent holographic Schwinger pair creation

> We analyze the entanglement of a Schwinger pair created by a time-dependent pulse. In the semi-classical approximation, the pair creation by a pulse of external electric field is captured by a periodic worldline instanton. At strong gauge coupling, the gauge-gravity dual worldsheet instanton exhibits a falling wormhole in AdS. We identify the tunneling time at the boundary with the inverse Unruh temperature, and derive the pertinent entanglement entropy between the created pair using thermodynamics. The entanglement entropy is enhanced by the sub-barrier tunneling process, and partly depleted by the radiation in the post-barrier process.

# Entanglement entropy in a time-dependent holographic Schwinger pair creation

## Key claim

The entanglement entropy of a Schwinger pair created by a time-dependent electric pulse receives a positive contribution from the sub-barrier tunneling process (causal part of the worldsheet) and a negative contribution from radiation loss in the post-barrier process (non-causal part), with the net entanglement entropy enhanced for intermediate values of the dimensionless parameter gamma = M*omega/E before being overtaken by the causal contribution at larger gamma.

## Method

The authors analyze Schwinger pair creation by a time-dependent electric pulse of the form E(t) = E / cosh^2(omega*t). At weak coupling, they use the worldline instanton formalism to find periodic instanton solutions. At strong coupling (large 't Hooft coupling lambda and large N_c), they employ holographic duality where the pair creation is described by a string worldsheet in AdS_5, solving the Nambu-Goto action numerically using pseudo-spectral methods with Chebychev discretization in the radial direction and Fourier discretization in the angular coordinate. The entanglement entropy is derived from the on-shell action interpreted as free energy, using thermodynamic relations.

## Key numbers

- The period of the worldline instanton is beta = 2*pi / (a * sqrt(1 + gamma^2)), where a = E/M and gamma = omega/a
- In the static limit (omega -> 0), the on-shell action reduces to S_OS -> (sqrt(lambda)/2) * (sqrt(T_c/T) - sqrt(T/T_c))^2 with T_c = 1/(2*pi*sqrt(R^2 + z_M^2))
- The non-causal (radiation) contribution to entanglement entropy at gamma = 0 is S_EE^{NC}(0) = -(2/3)*sqrt(lambda)
- The mean number of produced pairs is bar{n} = 1/(1 - exp(-S_OS))
- The entanglement entropy is shown for T = 0.08, z_M = 1, and lambda = 12 as a function of gamma = M*omega/E

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of entanglement entropy in static electric fields (Grieninger, Kharzeev, Zahed 2023) to time-dependent electric pulses, generalizing the holographic Schwinger pair production process from constant electric fields (Semenoff 2011, Jensen 2013, Sonner 2013) to time-dependent pulses. The identification of the tunneling time with inverse Unruh temperature follows earlier work on the relation between entanglement and thermal entropy (Casini 2011). The results are relevant for understanding pair production in strong chromo-electric pulses generated in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the entanglement entropy of the pair can be converted to the Gibbs entropy of the final hadronic state, extending the QED demonstration by Florio (2021) to the strongly coupled regime of QCD.
