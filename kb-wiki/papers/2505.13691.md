# Unified nonparametric equation-of-state inference from the neutron-star crust to perturbative-QCD densities

> Perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD), while valid only at densities exceeding those found in the cores of neutron stars, could provide constraints on the dense-matter equation of state (EOS). In this work, we examine the impact of pQCD information on the inference of the EOS using a nonparametric framework based on Gaussian processes (GPs). We examine the application of pQCD constraints through a "pQCD likelihood," and verify the findings of previous works; namely, a softening of the EOS at the central densities of the most massive neutron stars and a reduction in the maximum neutron-star mass. Although the pQCD likelihood can be easily integrated into existing EOS inference frameworks, this approach requires an arbitrary selection of the density at which the constraints are applied. The EOS behavior is also treated differently on either side of the chosen density. To mitigate these issues, we extend the EOS model to higher densities, thereby constructing a "unified" description of the EOS from the neutron-star crust to densities relevant for pQCD. In this approach the pQCD constraints effectively become part of the prior. Since the EOS is unconstrained by any calculation or data between the densities applicable to neutron stars and pQCD, we argue for maximum modeling flexibility in that regime. We compare the unified EOS with the traditional pQCD likelihood, and although we confirm the EOS softening, we do not see a reduction in the maximum neutron-star mass or any impact on macroscopic observables. Though residual model dependence cannot be ruled out, we find that pQCD suggests the speed of sound in the densest neutron-star cores has already started decreasing toward the asymptotic limit; we find that the speed of sound squared at the center of the most massive neutron star has an upper bound of $\sim 0.5$ at the $90\%$ level.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that perturbative QCD constraints soften the equation of state at the central densities of the most massive neutron stars, but contrary to previous work they find no reduction in the maximum neutron-star mass or any impact on macroscopic observables when using a unified nonparametric model that extends from the crust to pQCD densities.

## Method

The authors construct a unified nonparametric equation-of-state model based on Gaussian processes that spans densities from the neutron-star crust to perturbative-QCD densities, with maximum modeling flexibility in the intermediate regime between neutron-star central densities and pQCD densities. They compare this unified approach against the traditional "pQCD likelihood" method, which applies constraints at a single termination density, and examine both the "maximized" likelihood (which checks whether any causal, thermodynamically stable extension exists) and the "modeled" likelihood (which uses Gaussian process extensions conditioned on the full pQCD prediction).

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound squared at the center of the most massive neutron star has an upper bound of approximately 0.5 at the 90% credible level
- The pQCD constraint is applied at a chemical potential of 2.6 GeV, where the relative uncertainty on the pressure is comparable to low-density chiral effective field theory uncertainty
- The termination density of 10 nuclear saturation densities (n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}) is used as a reference point for applying pQCD constraints
- The pQCD prediction covers densities from approximately 25 to 40 nuclear saturation densities, corresponding to chemical potentials above 2.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the nonparametric Gaussian-process framework for equation-of-state inference developed by Landry (2018), Essick (2019), and Landry (2020) to include pQCD constraints. It builds on the pQCD likelihood approach introduced by Komoltsev (2021) and the modeled likelihood of Komoltsev (2023), while addressing their dependence on an arbitrary termination density. The results contradict findings by Gorda (2022) and Somasundaram (2022) regarding the reduction in maximum neutron-star mass, suggesting that previous conclusions may depend on the choice of termination density and EOS model. The unified model enables self-consistent inference of the equation of state from the crust to pQCD densities without abrupt changes in the EOS representation.
