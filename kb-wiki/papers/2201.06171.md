# Charge-dependent directed flows in heavy-ion collisions by Boltzmann-Maxwell equations

> We have calculated the directed flow $v_{1}$ and charge-dependent directed flow $Δv_{1}$ for pions and protons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$GeV by solving the coupled Boltzmann-Maxwell equations self-consistently. Our numerical results show that $v_{1}$ for pions and protons are all negative in the positive mid rapidity region and have similar behavior and magnitude. In contrast we find a quite different behavior in $Δv_{1}$ for pions and protons. The difference lies in that $Δv_{1}$ for protons mainly comes from pressure gradients of the medium, while the dominant contribution to $Δv_{1}$ for pions is from electromagnetic fields. Our results indicate that the effect of the electric field will slightly exceed that of the magnetic and lead to a small negative slope of $Δv_{1}$ for pions

# Charge-dependent directed flows in heavy-ion collisions by Boltzmann-Maxwell equations

## Key claim

The authors claim that the charge-dependent directed flow Δv₁ for protons and pions arises from different physical mechanisms, pressure gradients dominate for protons while electromagnetic fields dominate for pions, but this conclusion is hedged by the caveat that their simulated v₁ magnitude is more than ten times larger than experimental data, indicating their model produces larger pressure gradients than reality.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann equation coupled self-consistently to the Maxwell equations on GPUs for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. The simulation included 2-to-2 scatterings among u, d, s quarks, their antiquarks, and gluons with thermal masses, with the electromagnetic field sourced by both external currents from fast-moving spectators and medium currents from mid-rapidity quarks. Partons were hadronized into pions and protons via a coalescence model, and the directed flow v₁ and charge-dependent directed flow Δv₁ were computed as functions of rapidity for transverse momenta pT ∈ [0.2, 1.5] GeV.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √sNN = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Impact parameter: b = 8 fm, corresponding to approximately 20-30% centrality
- Saturation time: t₀ ≈ 0.2 fm/c
- Saturation scale: Qs = 1 GeV
- Anisotropy parameter: ξ = 1.4
- Fraction of participants in mid-rapidity: rq ≈ 0.1
- Thermal masses: mu,d,ū,d̄ = 0.3 GeV, ms,s̄ = 0.5 GeV, mg = 0.5 GeV
- Strong coupling constant: αs = 0.3
- Beam rapidity: ybeam = 5.36
- The simulated v₁ magnitude is more than ten times larger than the experimental STAR data

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of directed flow by Gursoy (2018, 2020) and Dubla (2020) that treated electromagnetic fields as background without back-reactions, and a perturbative approach by Yan (2021). The authors aim to resolve the "Δv₁ puzzle" where hydrodynamic models predicted negative slopes for both pion and proton Δv₁ while STAR data (2014) showed an almost vanishing slope for pions and a positive slope for protons. The self-consistent Boltzmann-Maxwell approach enables studying the interplay between electromagnetic fields and medium evolution, providing a baseline for effects like the chiral magnetic effect.
