# Symplectic no-core configuration interaction framework for ab initio nuclear structure

> We introduce a symplectic no-core configuration interaction (SpNCCI) framework for ab initio nuclear structure calculations, in a correlated many-body basis which encodes an approximate Sp(3,R) symmetry of the nucleus. Such a scheme potentially provides a means of restricting the many-body space to include only those highly-excited configurations which dominantly contribute to the nuclear wave function. We examine the symplectic symmetry structure arising in an illustrative ab initio SpNCCI calculation for 6Li. We observe both the dominance of symplectic symmetry in individual wave functions and the emergence of families of states related by symplectic symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a symplectic no-core configuration interaction (SpNCCI) framework for *ab initio* nuclear structure calculations, but the abstract's claim of "potentially providing a means of restricting the many-body space" is hedged: the authors acknowledge that while symplectic symmetry dominates individual wave functions, the most effective truncation will require identifying the few dominant symplectic irreps from within highly degenerate subspaces, e.g., by some variant of importance truncation.

## Method

The SpNCCI framework reorganizes the NCCI many-body space into irreducible representations of the symplectic group Sp(3,R), using a basis that reduces the Sp(3,R) ⊃ U(3) ⊃ SO(3) subgroup chain combined with SU(2) spin. Matrix elements of the Hamiltonian are evaluated by decomposing the operator into SU(3)-coupled unit tensors, applying commutation relations with symplectic laddering operators to recursively express all matrix elements in terms of seed matrix elements among lowest grade irreps (LGIs), which are computed using the SU(3)-NCSM code LSU3shell. The calculation for 6Li uses the JISP16 internucleon interaction with no Coulomb interaction, truncated at 6 oscillator quanta with oscillator basis parameter ℏω = 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The SpNCCI calculation for 6Li at Nmax=6 yields a laboratory-frame M=0 space dimension of 197,822, compared to 3484 for the intrinsic-frame J=0 space.
- For the 0ℏω states with dominant U(3)×SU(2) character 0(2,0)1, the Nσ=0 symplectic contributions are in the 70%–90% range.
- For the 2ℏω states with dominant U(3)×SU(2) character 2(4,0)1, the Nσ=0 symplectic contributions are at the >70% level.
- The 0ℏω states are dominated by Nex=0 contributions at the 60%–80% level, while for the 2ℏω states, the Nex=0 contributions fall below the 15% level.
- States below an excitation energy of ∼20 MeV are dominated by Nex=0 contributions.

## Context

This work extends the symplectic shell model formalism originally proposed by Rosensteel and Rowe (1980) and builds on prior SU(3)-NCSM calculations by Dytrych et al. (2013) for 6Li. The SpNCCI framework enables future calculations that could accelerate convergence of NCCI results by truncating to dominant symplectic subspaces, though the authors note that the most effective truncation will likely require identifying "Hamiltonian preferred" symplectic irreps, similar to the importance truncation approach of Roth (2007).
