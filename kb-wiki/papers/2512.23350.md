# Fine-tuning final state interactions model in NuWro Monte Carlo event generator

> Recent experimental data from MINERvA on transverse kinematics observables across four different nuclear targets - carbon, oxygen, iron, and lead - have been utilized to refine the modeling of final state interaction effects in the NuWro Monte Carlo neutrino event generator. For this purpose, we have developed an event reweighting tool for future applications to adjust the strength of final-state interactions. This study highlights the requirement for stronger nucleon reinteractions than previously assumed, but it still falls within the uncertainty range observed in a study comparing proton transparency measurements. This conclusion has significant implications for both experimental and theoretical work involving NuWro.

## Key claim

The study finds that the strength of nucleon final state interactions in the NuWro Monte Carlo generator needs to be approximately 24% larger than previously assumed to match MINERvA data, but this adjustment remains within the ±30% uncertainty range previously established from proton transparency measurements.

## Method

The authors developed an exact event reweighting scheme that scales the nucleon mean free path by a parameter s, modifying interaction and survival probabilities at each cascade step without requiring full event resampling. They applied this reweighting to NuWro version 25.03 simulations using a local Fermi gas nuclear model, comparing the reweighted predictions against MINERvA measurements of transverse kinematic imbalance (TKI) observables for charged-current quasielastic-like events on carbon, oxygen, iron, and lead targets.

## Key numbers

- The required increase in nucleon FSI strength is approximately 24% larger than previously assumed in NuWro
- The ±30% uncertainty range on the nucleon mean free path scaling parameter s (range [0.7, 1.3]) was previously established from proton transparency measurements (PhysRevC.100.015505, 2019)
- For a nucleon with momentum ~0.6 GeV/c, nuclear transparency ranges from 0.77 for carbon to 0.61 for iron and 0.42 for lead
- The MINERvA data used average neutrino energy ⟨Eν⟩ ~ 6 GeV with a spread of ~2 GeV
- Muon selection cuts: momentum 2.0 < |pμ| < 20 GeV/c, angle θμ < 17°
- Proton selection cuts: momentum 0.5 < |pp| < 1.1 GeV/c, angle θp < 70°
- Excitation energy values used: 27.13 MeV for carbon, 24.1 MeV for oxygen, 29.6 MeV for iron, and 22.8 MeV for lead

## Context

This work extends previous NuWro FSI studies that compared to proton transparency data (PhysRevC.100.015505, 2019) and responds to findings by Filali et al. (PhysRevD.111.032009, 2025) that FSI models in multiple Monte Carlo generators should be stronger than currently implemented. The reweighting tool enables future systematic studies of FSI uncertainties in NuWro and can inform similar improvements in other generators such as GENIE, NEUT, and GiBUU.
