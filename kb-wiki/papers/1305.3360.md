# Giant dipole resonance width as a probe for nuclear deformation at finite excitation

> The systematic study of the correlation between the experimental giant dipole resonance (GDR) width and the average deformation <β> of the nucleus at finite excitation is presented for the mass region A ~ 59 to 208. We show that the width of the GDR (Γ) and the quadrupole deformation of the nucleus do not follow a linear relation, as predicted earlier, due to the GDR induced quadrupole moment and the correlation also depends on the mass of the nuclei. The different empirical values of <β> extracted from the experimental GDR width match exceptionally well with the thermal shape fluctuation model. As a result, this universal correlation between <β> and Γprovides a direct experimental probe to determine the nuclear deformation at finite temperature and angular momentum in the entire mass region.

# Giant dipole resonance width as a probe for nuclear deformation at finite excitation

## Key claim
The correlation between the giant dipole resonance (GDR) width and the average quadrupole deformation of the nucleus is non-linear, contrary to earlier predictions, because the GDR vibration itself induces a quadrupole moment that competes with thermal shape fluctuations. This universal correlation provides a direct experimental probe for nuclear deformation at finite temperature and angular momentum, but only above a critical temperature T_c = 0.7 + 37.5/A.

## Method
The authors systematically compared experimental GDR widths with average deformations calculated within the thermal shape fluctuation model (TSFM) for nuclei in the mass region A ~ 59 to 208. They used the critical temperature included fluctuation model (CTFM) to account for the GDR-induced quadrupole moment, and derived an empirical correlation formula relating the average deformation to the GDR width, centroid energy, and a mass-dependent power coefficient D(A). Experimental data for GDR widths were taken from prior measurements on ⁶³Cu, ¹¹⁹Sb, ¹²⁰Sn, ²⁰¹Tl, ²⁰⁸Pb, ⁵⁹Cu, ¹¹⁰Sn, ¹¹³Sb, ¹⁵²Gd, and ¹⁷⁶W.

## Key numbers
- The proposed correlation is: β_exp = 0.18 + β_GDR + 0.7 × ((Γ(J,T,A) − Γ₀)/E₀)^(D(A)), where β_GDR = 0.04 + 4.13/A and D(A) = 2 − 0.0036A.
- The critical temperature below which the correlation cannot be applied is T_c = 0.7 + 37.5/A MeV.
- The GDR centroid energy was calculated using E_GDR = 31.2A^(−1/3) + 20.6A^(−1/6) MeV.
- The spherical nucleus width was calculated as Γ₀ = 0.05 × E_GDR^(1.6) MeV.
- The Jacobi transition point is given as J_c ~ 1.2A^(5/6).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Mattiuzzi et al. (1997) and Kusnezov et al. (2003) that hinted at a correlation between average deformation and GDR width but failed to describe the temperature dependence. The authors resolve this discrepancy by incorporating the GDR-induced quadrupole moment, which was previously shown to cause a critical temperature in the GDR width increase (Pandit et al., 2012). The correlation enables experimental determination of nuclear deformation at finite temperature and angular momentum where the two GDR resonance energies cannot be individually resolved, and suggests that the GDR-induced quadrupole moment should be included in future TSFM calculations.
