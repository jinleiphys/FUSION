# Kinetic Equation for Gluons in the Background Gauge of QCD

> We derive the quantum kinetic equation for a pure gluon plasma, applying the background field and closed-time-path method. The derivation is more general and transparent than earlier works. A term in the equation is found which, as in the classical case, corresponds to the color charge precession for partons moving in the gauge field.

## Key claim

The authors derive a quantum kinetic equation for a pure gluon plasma that is more general and transparent than earlier derivations, and they identify a term in the equation that corresponds to the color charge precession for partons moving in the gauge field, which is necessary to guarantee gauge covariance of the resulting kinetic equation.

## Method

The authors apply the background field method of QCD (BG-QCD), decomposing the gluon field into a classical background part and a quantum fluctuation, combined with the closed-time-path (CTP) formalism for non-equilibrium dynamics. They perform a gradient expansion based on the separation of scales between hard quantum fluctuations (scale ~1/μ) and soft collective motion (scale ~1/(gμ)), and express the Dyson-Schwinger equations in terms of a gauge-covariant Wigner function defined with a Wilson link to the background field.

## Key numbers

* The separation of scales is characterized by a mass parameter μ, where the scale of collectivity ~1/(gμ) is much larger than the typical extension of hard quantum fluctuations ~1/μ, in the weak coupling limit g ≪ 1.
* The background field scales as A(X) ~ μ and the field strength as F[A(X)] ~ gμ².
* The first two terms of the kinetic equation (the drift term and the color precession term) are at leading order O(gμ²), while other terms are at subleading order O(g²μ²).
* Near equilibrium, typical scales are q ~ T (temperature), D_X ~ g²T, and gF ~ (D_X)² ~ g⁴T².

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to derive the gluon transport equation in BG-QCD by Elze (1990) and the work of Blaizot and Iancu (late 1990s) in the CTP formalism, which focused on the vicinity of equilibrium. The derivation is more general and transparent than these prior approaches, and the explicit identification of the color precession term connects the quantum kinetic equation to the classical Wong equation (Wong, 1970) and the classical transport equation for color charges (Heinz, 1980s; Elze, 1986; Jalilian-Marian, 1990s; Kelly, 1990s). The result enables further studies of gluon plasma thermalization and the connection between quantum and classical descriptions of non-Abelian gauge dynamics.
