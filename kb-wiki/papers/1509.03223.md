# Hanbury-Brown-Twiss measurements at large rapidity separations, or can we measure the proton radius in p-A collisions?

> We point out that current calculations of inclusive two-particle correlations in p-A collisions based on the Color Glass Condensate approach exhibit a contribution from Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations. These HBT correlations are quite distinct from the standard ones, in that they are apparent for particles widely separated in rapidity. The transverse size of the emitter which is reflected in these correlations is the gluonic size of the proton. This raises an interesting possibility of measuring the proton size directly by the HBT effect of particle pairs produced in p-A collisions.

## Key claim

The authors point out that current Color Glass Condensate (CGC) calculations of inclusive two-particle correlations in p-A collisions contain a Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) contribution that is distinct from standard HBT because it persists for particles widely separated in rapidity, and that this signal could allow a direct measurement of the gluonic size of the proton, provided the saturation momentum of the target satisfies \(R^2 Q_s^2 \gg 1\).

## Method

The authors derive the two-gluon correlation function from a coherent-state formalism for gluon emission, modeling the emitter as a superposition of independent color sources with random SU(N_c) phases. They then map this HBT formalism onto the glasma graph approach by identifying the source function with the classical gluon field after eikonal scattering, where the proton provides a transverse size \(R\) and the target nucleus provides a saturation scale \(Q_s\) that sets the coherence length of color domains.

## Key numbers

* The HBT correlation width in transverse momentum difference is \(\sim 1/R\), where \(R\) is the gluonic size of the proton.
* The number of independent color sources (incoherent emitters) is \(N_S \sim Q_s^2 R^2\); the HBT signal dominates when \(R^2 Q_s^2 \gg 1\).
* The correlation function for gluons takes the form \(C(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{K}) = 1 + \frac{1}{N_c^2-1}\left[ e^{-R^2 \mathbf{q}^2} + e^{-R^2 \mathbf{K}^2} \right]\) in a Gaussian model, where \(\mathbf{q} = \mathbf{p}_a - \mathbf{p}_b\) and \(\mathbf{K} = (\mathbf{p}_a + \mathbf{p}_b)/2\).
* The suppression factor in front of the correlated term is \(1/(N_c^2-1)\), with \(N_c = 3\) for QCD.

## Context

This work extends the glasma graph approach of Dumitru, McLerran, and collaborators (2008) and the Bose enhancement analysis of the same authors (2015) by identifying an additional HBT contribution within the CGC framework. It builds on standard HBT interferometry reviews by Heinz (1996) and Weiner (1999), and on prior recognition of HBT in CGC by Adrian (2014) and Yury (2014). The result enables a new experimental observable: measuring the proton's gluonic radius via two-particle correlations in p-Pb collisions at the LHC, complementing existing measurements from elastic scattering and structure functions.
