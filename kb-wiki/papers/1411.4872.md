# Effect of long range correlation on the scaling behaviors of the normalized factorial moments for first-order phase transition

> Within the framework of Ginzburg-Landau theory, the effect of multiplicity correlation between the dynamical multiplicity fluctuations is analyzed for a first-order phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadrons. Normalized factorial correlators are used to study the correlated dynamical fluctuations. A scaling behavior is found among the factorial correlators, and an approximate universal exponent, which is weakly dependent on the details of the phase transition, is obtained.

## Key claim

Within the Ginzburg-Landau framework for a first-order phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadrons, the normalized factorial correlators exhibit a scaling behavior \(F_{qq} \propto F_{22}^{\beta_q}\) that holds even when the usual intermittency power law in bin size is violated; the exponent \(\gamma\) in \(\beta_q \propto (q-1)^\gamma\) is approximately universal and only weakly dependent on the details of the interaction parameters \(s\) and \(t\).

## Method

The authors model two small phase-space bins using a Ginzburg-Landau free energy \(F(\phi_1,\phi_2)\) with a correlation term \(\lambda\delta(|\phi_1|^2-|\phi_2|^2)^2\) to parameterize long-range multiplicity correlations. They derive analytic expressions for the normalized factorial correlators \(F_{q_1 q_2}\) as functions of the bin-size parameter \(w\) and the reduced parameters \(s\) and \(t\). Numerical evaluation of these expressions for \(q_1=q_2\) (denoted \(F_{qq}\)) is performed for various \(s\) and \(t\) values to test the scaling relation \(F_{qq} \propto F_{22}^{\beta_q}\) and to extract the exponent \(\gamma\) from \(\beta_q \propto (q-1)^\gamma\).

## Key numbers

* For parameters \(s=0.2\) and \(t=0.2\), the scaling exponent \(\gamma\) in \(\beta_q \propto (q-1)^\gamma\) is found to be \(1.293\) (from a linear fit in log-log scale; a second figure caption states \(1.297\)).
* For a range of \(s\) from \(0.2\) to \(1.0\) and \(t\) from \(-0.4\) to \(1.0\), the exponent \(\gamma\) is approximately \(1.29 \pm 0.01\).
* The paper reports a universal scaling exponent \(\gamma = 1.29 \pm 0.1\) (in the Summary section) as a practical observable to characterize the first-order phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the study of factorial correlators from a second-order phase transition (Ref. "by" in the text) to a first-order phase transition within the Ginzburg-Landau model. It builds on prior applications of Ginzburg-Landau theory to multiplicity fluctuations (e.g., Refs. lab21–lab34) and introduces a correlation term \(\lambda\) to go beyond the uncorrelated-bin assumption used in earlier moment analyses (Refs. lab31–lab34). The finding of a nearly universal exponent \(\gamma\) suggests a robust observable for identifying first-order phase transitions in heavy-ion collision data, independent of the detailed interaction strength or bin size.
