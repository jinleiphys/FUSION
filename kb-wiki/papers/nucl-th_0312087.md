# Subthreshold antiproton production in proton-carbon reactions

> Data from KEK on subthreshold antiproton as well as on pi(+-) and K(+-) production in proton-nucleus reactions are described at projectile energies between 3.5 and 12.0 GeV. We use a model which considers a hadron-nucleus reaction as an incoherent sum over collisions of the projectile with a varying number of target nucleons. It samples complete events and allows thus for the simultaneous consideration of all particle species measured. The overall reproduction of the data is quite satisfactory. It is shown that the contributions from the interaction of the projectile with groups of several target nucleons are decisive for the description of subthreshold production. Since the collective features of subthreshold production become especially significant far below the threshold, the results are extrapolated down to COSY energies. It is concluded that an antiproton measurement at ANKE-COSY should be feasible, if the high background of other particles can be efficiently suppressed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that subthreshold antiproton production in proton-carbon reactions can be described by a model where the projectile interacts with groups of several target nucleons (clusters), and that these multi-nucleon interactions become decisive far below the NN threshold of 5.63 GeV, with quasi-free single-nucleon collisions contributing negligibly at the lowest energies.

## Method

The authors used the Rossendorf collision (ROC) model, a Monte Carlo generator that treats hadron-nucleus reactions as an incoherent sum over collisions of the projectile with varying numbers of target nucleons (clusters). The model calculates complete final states including both participant interactions and spectator fragmentation, using empirical matrix elements with parameters fixed from hadronic reactions and fragment spectra. The calculations were compared to KEK data on pi(+/-), K(+/-), and antiproton production at projectile energies between 3.5 and 12.0 GeV, and then extrapolated down to COSY energies of 2.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The NN threshold for antiproton production is 5.63 GeV, while the highest COSY energy is 2.8 GeV, placing antiproton production 2.83 GeV below threshold
- At 2.8 GeV, the relative contributions to total antiproton cross section from interactions with different numbers of participants are: a=1 (0.0%), a=2 (0.9%), a=3 (51.3%), a=4 (37.5%), a=5 (9.8%), a=6 (0.4%)
- The total antiproton cross section at 2.8 GeV is predicted to be 7.8 x 10^(-10) mb
- The strange quark suppression factor lambda_s = 0.15 was used for the ratio u:d:s = 1:1:0.15
- The Fermi momentum parameter p_F = 320 MeV/c was used, with the residue temperature Theta_R^max = 12 MeV

## Context

This work extends the ROC model previously applied to subthreshold K+ production (Mueller 1992) to the case of antiproton production, and is distinguished from prior transport calculations (Li 1994, Batko 1991, Cassing 1992) and thermodynamical approaches (Koch 1989, Ko 1988) by simultaneously considering all measured particle species (pi(+/-), K(+/-), and antiprotons) with a single parameter set. The results contradict the "first-chance NN collision model" interpretation of the KEK data (Sugaya 1998) and instead emphasize the importance of multi-nucleon interactions. The prediction enables feasibility assessment for antiproton measurements at the ANKE-COSY spectrometer, provided background suppression can be achieved.
