# Microscopic study of collective excitations in rotating nuclei

> We have carried out a unified microscopic study of electric monopole, quadrupole and magnetic dipole excitations in fast rotating nuclei undergoing backbending, with special attention at the magnetic excitations. We found, among other results, that the strength of the orbital magnetic dipole excitations (scissors mode) gets enhanced by more than a factor four at high rotational frequency, above the backbending region. We provide a physical explanation for such an enhancement.

## Key claim

The strength of orbital magnetic dipole excitations (scissors mode) in fast rotating nuclei is enhanced by more than a factor four at high rotational frequency above the backbending region, with the enhancement linked to a superfluid-to-normal phase transition and the associated increase in the nuclear moment of inertia.

## Method

The authors performed cranked random-phase-approximation (CRPA) calculations using a cranked Nilsson mean field plus quasiparticle RPA with separable monopole, quadrupole, and spin-spin interactions, expressed in doubly stretched coordinates to enforce self-consistency. They applied this method to the nuclei $^{156}$Dy and $^{158}$Er, computing electric monopole (E0), quadrupole (E2), and magnetic dipole (M1) strength functions at zero and high rotational frequencies, with pairing gaps treated phenomenologically as a function of rotational frequency.

## Key numbers

- The low-lying orbital M1 strength (1–4 MeV range) in $^{156}$Dy increases from 1.36 $\mu_n^2$ at $I=0$ to 8.87 $\mu_n^2$ at $I=30\hbar$ (a factor of ~6.5 enhancement).
- The total M1 strength in the 1–4 MeV range increases from 2.95 $\mu_n^2$ at $I=0$ to 12.21 $\mu_n^2$ at $I=30\hbar$.
- The critical rotational frequency for backbending is $\Omega_c = 0.32$ MeV for $^{156}$Dy and $\Omega_c = 0.33$ MeV for $^{158}$Er.
- The zero-frequency pairing gaps are $\Delta_N(0) = 0.857$ MeV and $\Delta_P(0) = 0.879$ MeV for $^{156}$Dy; $\Delta_N(0) = 0.874$ MeV and $\Delta_P(0) = 0.884$ MeV for $^{158}$Er.
- The E2 energy-weighted sum rule is exhausted to more than 98% for all rotational frequencies.
- The triaxiality contribution enhances the low-energy M1 energy-weighted sum by a factor of 1.16 for $\gamma = 50^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends earlier CRPA studies of monopole and quadrupole resonances in rotating nuclei by Shlomo (1984) and Abgrall et al. (1987), and parallels the model of Nakatsukasa et al. (1996) for octupole excitations. The results predict a stronger rotation-induced broadening of the isoscalar quadrupole resonance than found by Shlomo (1984), and provide a physical explanation for the enhancement of the scissors mode in terms of the superfluid-to-normal phase transition. The findings enable experimental searches for enhanced M1 strength in fast rotating deformed nuclei as a signature of pairing collapse.
