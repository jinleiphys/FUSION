# Two-dimensional discrete wavelet analysis of multiparticle event topology in heavy ion collisions

> The event-by-event analysis of multiparticle production in high energy hadron and nuclei collisions can be performed using the discrete wavelet transformation. The ring-like and jet-like structures in two-dimensional angular histograms are well extracted by wavelet analysis. For the first time the method is applied to the jet-like events with background simulated by event generators, which are developed to describe nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies. The jet positions are located quite well by the discrete wavelet transformation of angular particle distribution even in presence of strong background.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-dimensional discrete wavelet transformation (DWT) can locate jet positions in angular particle distributions even when strong background from heavy-ion collisions is present, but the method is recommended only for finding jet positions, not for reconstructing jet energy, because the wavelet subtraction distorts transverse momentum values.

## Method

The authors simulated jet events for pp collisions at LHC energy sqrt(s)=5500 GeV using PYTHIA, then superimposed these jets onto a high-multiplicity background from PbPb collisions generated by HYDJET (with jet production switched off). They applied a five-step DWT algorithm using Daubechies D8 wavelets: decompose the event distribution, subtract the smooth background at the largest scale (j=7), zero out large-scale coefficients (j=4-7) to remove background irregularities, apply a threshold cut (cut=0.95) to remove small-scale coefficients (j=1-3) with low intensity, and restore the distribution with the selected coefficients. Jet positions were identified from peaks in the wavelet coefficient spectrum at different scales.

## Key numbers

- The simulated pp collisions were at LHC energy sqrt(s)=5500 GeV
- The background consisted of 100 PbPb events with multiplicity (dN/dy)_{y=0} approximately 4000
- Histogram binning used 128x128 bins over intervals |eta|<2.4 and |phi|<pi, with bin sizes Delta-eta x Delta-phi = 0.0375 x 0.049, close to CMS detector granularities
- The threshold parameter for removing small-scale coefficients was cut=0.95
- Three jets with total transverse momenta pT=53 GeV, pT=45 GeV, and pT=22 GeV were simulated
- The first jet had average <pT>_{jet}=0.346 GeV/c versus background <pT>_{back}=0.330 GeV/c; the second jet had <pT>_{jet}=0.295 GeV/c versus <pT>_{back}=0.246 GeV/c
- The third jet (22 GeV/c) had <pT>_{jet}=0.143 GeV/c, less than the background <pT>_{back}=0.209 GeV/c, and was not found by the wavelet analysis
- The small-scale range j<4 corresponds approximately to sqrt((Delta-eta)^2+(Delta-phi)^2) < R_{jet}=0.3

## Context

This work extends prior applications of wavelet analysis to one-dimensional (Georg, Motal) and two-dimensional (Ast, Dremin, Uzhinskii) particle distributions in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and builds on studies of event texture using DWT (Berden, Kopytin, Adams). The method addresses the difficulty of jet reconstruction in central PbPb collisions at LHC energies, where standard cone-type algorithms (e.g., the modified UA1-algorithm studied for CMS by Pb_Pb) struggle due to large background with (dN_{ch}/dy)_{y=0}=3000-5000 charged particles. The wavelet approach enables identification of ring-like structures that conventional jet algorithms cannot extract, and could be used more broadly to study fractality and intermittency in three-dimensional phase space (Dremin, De Wolf, Kittel).
