# Debye mass at the QCD transition in the PNJL model

> We consider colour-electric screening as expressed by the quark contribution to the Debye mass calculated in a PNJL model with emphasis on confining and chiral symmetry breaking effects. We observe that the screening mass is entirely determined by the nonperturbative quark distribution function and temperature dependent QCD running coupling. The role of the gluon background (Polyakov loop) is to provide strong suppression of the number of charge carriers below the transition temperature, as an effect of confinement, while the temperature dependent dynamical quark mass contributes additional suppression, as an effect of chiral symmetry breaking. An alternative derivation of this result from a modified kinetic theory is given, which allows for a slight generalization and explicit contact with perturbative QCD. This gives the possibility to gain insights into the colour screening mechanism in the region near the QCD pseudocritical temperature and to provide a guideline for the interpretation of lattice QCD data.

## Key claim

The quark contribution to the Debye screening mass near the QCD transition can be reproduced by a PNJL model that includes confining and chiral symmetry breaking effects, but the result stays below lattice QCD data because the model lacks dynamical gluon degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors calculated the one-loop longitudinal polarization function using PNJL model propagators with QED-like vertices, then took the static long-wavelength limit to extract the Debye mass. They also derived the same result from a modified kinetic theory where the usual Fermi-Dirac distribution is replaced by a Polyakov-loop-modified distribution function. The running coupling was modelled using either a two-loop result or an analytic one-loop form with pole subtraction.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature of the model: \(T_c \approx 200\) MeV
- For high temperatures \(T \gg T_c\), the free gas behaviour \(m_D^2 = \frac{2}{3} g^2 T^2\) is reproduced
- The nonperturbative factor \(A(T) \approx 1.66\) for \(N_f = 2+1\) lattice QCD at \(T \geq 1.2 T_c\) (from Kaczmarek (2007))
- The running coupling uses \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 260\) MeV and \(\mu = \pi\) as the upper bound for the perturbative coupling
- The Debye mass calculation uses \(N_c = 3\) colours and \(N_f = 2\) flavours

## Context

This work extends earlier PNJL model calculations for massless quarks by Jankowski (2009) to include dynamical chiral symmetry breaking via a temperature-dependent quark mass. It provides a microscopic interpretation of the Debye mass extracted from lattice QCD data by Kaczmarek (2007) and contrasts with the approach of Riek and Rapp (2010), who used a screened Cornell potential with two different screening masses. The results enable a better understanding of colour screening near the QCD pseudocritical temperature and offer a guideline for interpreting lattice QCD data, though the model requires inclusion of dynamical gluons for quantitative agreement.
