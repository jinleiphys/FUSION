# Phase transition and nuclear symmetry energy from neutron star observations: Constraints in light of PSR J0614--3329

> The possible occurrence of a first-order hadron-quark phase transition (FOPT) in neutron-star interiors remains an open question. Whether such a transition can be directly tested with improved observations is a key challenge. Here, we incorporate the latest constraints, especially a new NICER radius measurement for PSR J0614--3329, into a nonparametric Gaussian Process (GP) EOS framework that explicitly includes a first-order transition. We find a Bayes factor of $B\approx2.3$ when comparing models with and without an explicit phase transition, marginally favoring its presence. At $68\%$ credibility, the transition onset density $n_{\rm PT}$ is either below $2\,n_s$ (corresponding to masses $\lesssim1\,M_\odot$, with density jump $Δn\sim0.5\,n_s$) or, more prominently, above $4\,n_s$ (near the central density of the heaviest NS, with $Δn\sim3\,n_s$), where $n_s$ represents the nuclear saturation density. In addition, by using symmetry-energy expansion at low densities ($<1.1\,n_s$), we infer a slope parameter $L=40.2^{+19.3}_{-14.3}$ MeV, in good agreement with nuclear-experiment values. Intriguingly, $L$ correlates positively with the radius difference between $1.4\,M_\odot$ and $2.0\,M_\odot$ stars.

## Key claim

The paper finds a Bayes factor of approximately 2.3 when comparing neutron star equation-of-state models with and without an explicit first-order hadron-quark phase transition, which only marginally favors the presence of such a transition and is not statistically significant under standard model-comparison criteria.

## Method

The authors construct a nonparametric Gaussian Process equation-of-state framework that explicitly includes a first-order Maxwell-type phase transition, conditioned on a low-density nuclear expansion up to 1.1 times nuclear saturation density. They perform a joint Bayesian inference using four NICER pulsar radius measurements (including the new PSR J0614--3329), gravitational-wave constraints from GW170817, a maximum-mass prior, and perturbative quantum chromodynamic constraints at high densities. The phase transition is modeled by setting the squared sound speed to zero over a density interval defined by an onset density and a density jump, with both parameters sampled from uniform priors.

## Key numbers

- Bayes factor of B ≈ 2.3 when comparing models with and without an explicit phase transition
- At 68% credibility, the transition onset density n_PT is either below 2 n_s (with density jump Δn ∼ 0.5 n_s) or above 4 n_s (with Δn ∼ 3 n_s), where n_s is nuclear saturation density
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L = 40.2^{+19.3}_{-14.3} MeV inferred from the low-density expansion
- PSR J0614--3329 radius R = 10.29^{+1.01}_{-0.86} km for a mass of 1.44^{+0.06}_{-0.07} M_sun
- Maximum nonrotating neutron star mass M_TOV ≈ 2.25 M_sun with corresponding radius R_TOV ≈ 11.6 km
- Radius of a 1.4 M_sun neutron star constrained to approximately 11.36 km with uncertainties of a few hundred meters

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Huang et al. (2023) and Kumar et al. (2024) that found a first-order phase transition at 2-3 times nuclear saturation density is disfavored by multimessenger data, now incorporating the new NICER measurement of PSR J0614--3329 which favors a relatively small radius. The results enable future tests of phase transitions through precise measurements of the maximum-mass neutron star radius, potentially via postmerger gravitational-wave signals, and through continued NICER observations of pulsars at different masses to further constrain the symmetry energy slope parameter L, which is currently in tension between the PREX-II neutron-skin result and other nuclear experimental determinations.
