# Neutron stars within the Skyrme model

> The Skyrme model is a low energy effective field theory of strong interactions where nuclei and baryons appear as collective excitations of pionic degrees of freedom. In the last years, there has been a revival of Skyrme's ideas and new related models, some of them with BPS bounds (topological lower energy bounds), have been proposed. It is the aim of this paper to review how they can be applied to the study of neutron stars allowing for a description by means of topological solitons. We will focus on different aspects as the equation of state or the mass-radius relation, where we find that high maximal masses are supported.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that the Skyrme model and its BPS extension can describe neutron stars as topological solitons, but the standard Skyrme model with simple ansätze fails to reach physical baryon numbers (~10^57) unless multilayer or ramp-profile approximations are used, while the BPS Skyrme model provides a perfect-fluid description that naturally supports high maximal masses.

## Method

The paper reviews three approaches to coupling Skyrme fields to gravity via the Einstein-Skyrme action: the hedgehog ansatz, the rational map ansatz (with single-layer, multilayer, and ramp-profile extensions), and the BPS Skyrme model where the Lagrangian contains only the sextic term (square of the baryon current) and a potential. For each approach, the field equations are derived under a spherically symmetric metric, and the mass-radius relation and equation of state are obtained either by solving the coupled differential equations numerically or, in the BPS case, by analytic integration using the constant-pressure equation.

## Key numbers

- For the hedgehog ansatz, the critical effective coupling for collapse scales as α_crit^B ≈ 0.040378/B², where α = 4πG f_π².
- For the rational map ansatz with massless pions, the integral I ≈ 1.28 B² for large baryon number (Battye 2002).
- For the rational map with two-layer configurations, the maximum baryon number is about 1.95 × 10^9, still far from the physical neutron star value of ~10^57.
- The BPS Skyrme model yields an average energy density ρ̄ = E/V and an equation of state V(P) = 2π (λ/μ) |B| ∫₀^π sin²ξ dξ / √(U + P̃), with λ and μ the coupling constants.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on gravitating Skyrmions by Glendenning (1988) and Bizon (1992) using the hedgehog ansatz, and by Piette (2007) and Nelmes (2011) using rational maps and multilayer approximations. It contrasts these with the BPS Skyrme model of Adam, Sanchez-Guillen, and Wereszczynski (2010a, 2010b), which provides a perfect-fluid description suitable for neutron star cores. The review enables future work on unified field-theoretic descriptions of nuclear matter from light nuclei to compact stars, and highlights the need to reconcile the standard Skyrme model's difficulty reaching physical baryon numbers with the BPS model's success in supporting high masses.
