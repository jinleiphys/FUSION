# Octupole deformation in the ground states of even-even nuclei: a global analysis within the covariant density functional theory

> A systematic investigation of octupole deformed nuclei is presented for even-even systems with $Z\leq 106$ located between the two-proton and two-neutron drip lines. For this study we use five most up-to-date covariant energy density functionals of different types, with a non-linear meson coupling, with density dependent meson couplings, and with density-dependent zero-range interactions. Pairing correlations are treated within relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory based on an effective separable particle-particle interaction of finite range. This allows us to assess theoretical uncertainties within the present covariant models for the prediction of physical observables relevant for octupole deformed nuclei. In addition, a detailed comparison with the predictions of non-relativistic models is performed. A new region of octupole deformation, centered around $Z\sim 98, N\sim 196$ is predicted for the first time. In terms of its size in the $(Z,N)$ plane and the impact of octupole deformation on binding energies this region is similar to the best known region of octupole deformed nuclei centered at $Z\sim 90, N\sim 136$. For the later island of octupole deformed nuclei, the calculations suggest substantial increase of its size as compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new region of octupole deformation exists centered around Z~98, N~196, predicted for the first time, and that this region is similar in size and impact on binding energies to the well-known region centered at Z~90, N~136. However, this prediction is hedged by the fact that it relies on covariant density functional theory calculations using five different functionals, and the authors note that the DD-MEδ functional often contradicts both other functionals and experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic survey of all even-even nuclei with Z≤106 between the two-proton and two-neutron drip lines using relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with five covariant energy density functionals (DD-PC1, NL3*, DD-ME2, PC-PK1, and DD-MEδ). Constrained calculations in the (β₂, β₃) deformation plane were carried out using a newly developed parallel computer code RHB-OCT, with pairing treated via a separable finite-range Gogny pairing interaction. Potential energy surfaces were mapped for β₂ values from -0.2 to 0.4 and β₃ values from 0.0 to 0.3 with a deformation step of 0.02 in each direction.

## Key numbers

- The maximum gain in binding energy due to octupole deformation |ΔE_oct| in Ra isotopes varies from approximately 1 MeV for NL3* and PC-PK1 up to 2 MeV for DD-ME2 at N~136
- In U isotopes, |ΔE_oct| ≥ 1.0 MeV for N=134, 136, and 138 for DD-ME2 and PC-PK1, and for N=136 and 138 for NL3* and DD-PC1
- In Pu isotopes, |ΔE_oct| exceeds 1.0 MeV only at N=134 for PC-PK1 and DD-ME2 and at N=136 for NL3* and DD-PC1
- In Cm isotopes, the maximum |ΔE_oct| is approximately 1.0 MeV at N~134 for NL3*, PC-PK1, DD-ME2, and DD-PC1
- The MM calculations of Ref. Moller (2008) predict |ΔE_oct| ~ 0.8 MeV at N=130 for Cm isotopes and ~ 100 keV for Rn isotopes at N=136-138
- Octupole vibrational bandheads are observed at 366.65 keV in ²³⁰U and 562.19 keV in ²³²U, and at 698, 605, and 597 keV in ²³⁶⁻²³⁰Pu isotopes (N=142-146)

## Context

This work extends previous limited CDFT studies of octupole deformation (which covered only a few nuclei per isotope chain, such as in the work of Nomura (2013) and Robledo (2011)) to a global survey across the full nuclear landscape up to Z=106. The results are compared with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using Gogny forces by Robledo (2011) and with macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Moller (2008). The study enables future investigations of octupole correlations in superheavy nuclei and provides theoretical uncertainty estimates for octupole deformation predictions, which were previously unavailable.
