# Parity doublets and chiral symmetry restoration in baryon spectrum

> It is argued that an appearance of the near parity doublets in the upper part of the light baryon spectrum is an evidence for the chiral symmetry restoration in the regime where a typical momentum of quarks is around the chiral symmetry restoration scale. At high enough baryon excitation energy the nontrivial gap solution, which signals the chiral symmetry breaking regime, disappears and the chiral symmetry should be restored. Thus one observes a phase transition in the upper part of the light baryon spectrum. The average kinetic energy of the constituent quarks in this region is just around the critical one $3T_c$.

## Key claim

The appearance of near parity doublets in the upper part of the light baryon spectrum (masses around 2 GeV and above) is evidence for chiral symmetry restoration in that regime, because the average kinetic energy of constituent quarks in these highly excited baryons reaches the critical value where the nontrivial gap solution of the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model disappears, analogous to the effect of high temperature but driven by residual interactions rather than thermal distributions.

## Method

The author uses the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field (Hartree-Fock) approximation to derive the gap equation for the dynamical quark mass, and then argues that the momentum distribution of valence quarks in highly excited baryons (obtained from solving the Schrödinger equation for three-quark systems) broadens with excitation energy, replacing the thermal Fermi-Dirac distribution in the finite-temperature gap equation. By comparing the average kinetic energy of constituent quarks in baryons to the critical kinetic energy \(3T_c\) from lattice QCD, the author estimates the excitation energy at which the gap solution vanishes and chiral symmetry is restored.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate: \(\langle \bar{\psi} \psi \rangle \simeq -(240 - 250\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- Constituent quark mass: \(M \sim 340\ \text{MeV}\).
- Root mean square momentum of constituent quarks in low-lying baryons (masses \(\leq 1.7 - 1.8\ \text{GeV}\)): 500–700 MeV.
- Average kinetic energy of constituent quarks in low-lying baryons (mass below 1.7–1.8 GeV): 260–480 MeV.
- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration from lattice QCD: \(T_c \sim 150\ \text{MeV}\) (earlier) or \(T_c \sim 170 - 190\ \text{MeV}\) (more recent).
- Critical average kinetic energy for chiral restoration: \(3T_c\).
- Splittings within parity partners in the upper baryon spectrum: typically within 5% of the baryon mass, with experimental uncertainties of order 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological observation of near parity doublets in the high-energy baryon spectrum, previously noted by Glozman and Riska (1996), by providing a microscopic foundation based on the NJL model and the concept of a momentum-dependent dynamical quark mass. It contrasts with potential models (harmonic or linear confinement, pure color Coulomb) that cannot produce systematic parity doubling and predicts that the "missing resonance" problem in constituent quark models may be resolved by chiral symmetry restoration, which renders the constituent quark description invalid above a critical excitation energy. The paper suggests that future experiments should search for the missing parity partners of states like \(N(2600), \frac{11}{2}^-\) and \(\Delta(2420), \frac{11}{2}^-\) to test the phase transition hypothesis.
