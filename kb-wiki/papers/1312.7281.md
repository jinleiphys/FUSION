# Low-energy eta-nuclear scattering parametres vs. binding properties in light nuclei

> The connection of the binding energy and width of possible bound eta-mesic states is given to the complex scattering length for s-states in the hope that, with knowledge of the final state interaction this could be useful in searches of these states. In spite of the strong direct potential dependence of both observables this connection is seen to be very model independent even for various nuclei once the influence of also the effective range is considered. The importance of this term is pointed also for data analyses. Although the nucleus considered here is 12C, extension to other nuclei is implied in the background work.

# Low-energy eta-nuclear scattering parametres vs. binding properties in light nuclei

## Key claim
The connection between the complex scattering length and the binding energy and width of possible bound eta-mesic states in 12C is very model-independent across different nuclei once the effective range term is included, even though the direct potential dependence of both observables is strong. The effective range term is essential for accurate data analyses, as omitting it can give scattering lengths less than half their true values and grossly overestimated binding energies.

## Method
The author used simple optical potentials with various nuclear charge distributions, freely varying the real and imaginary strength parameters to generate binding energy and width contours in the complex scattering length plane for s-states. The low-energy relation 1/a = -sqrt(-2μE/ħ²) - r₀ μE/ħ² was compared with exact solutions from solving the equations of motion, with the effective range r₀ taken from the calculation.

## Key numbers
- For binding energies up to |E| ≈ 10 MeV, the accuracy of the relation including effective range was a few percent for the real part and about ten percent for the imaginary part.
- For η⁴He, omitting the effective range term gives an estimated binding energy of about 4 MeV, while including it gives 1 MeV; from the contour figures the corresponding results are 10 MeV and 2 MeV.
- Using a real potential strength V_R = 0.33 fm (with V_I = 0) gives a barely bound state in ⁴He, with binding energies of 8 MeV in ¹²C and 14 MeV in ²⁴Mg.
- Using V_R = 0.47 fm with V_I = 0.5 V_R gives binding energies of 15 MeV in ¹²C and 22 MeV in ²⁴Mg, with half-widths about equal to binding energies; for ⁴He the half-width would be 12 MeV.
- The barely binding strength parametrization is V_R = 0.86 × A^(-0.7) fm for real potentials and V_R = 1.25 × A^(-0.7) fm for V_I = V_R/2.
- For a_I less than 2 fm with a_R larger than 5 fm, a bound state should be recognizable; for smaller scattering lengths, a_I < 1 fm would be necessary.

## Context
This work extends the original study of the relation in ³He (Niskanen, 2013) to heavier nuclei, particularly ¹²C, with additional results for ⁴He and ²⁴Mg in the background work. The results are compared with experimental scattering length extractions from data analyses by Smyrski (2007), Mersmann (2007), and Budzanowski (2009), showing that omitting the effective range term gives significantly different values. The findings enable interpolation to other nuclei such as A=7 systems of recent experimental interest, and suggest that bound states may be recognizable if the imaginary part of the scattering length is sufficiently small.
