# Chiral model approach to quark matter nucleation in neutron stars

> The nucleation process of quark matter in both cold and hot dense hadronic matter is investigated using a chiral approach to describe the quark phase. We use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio and the Chromo Dielectric models to describe the deconfined phase and the non-linear Walecka model for the hadronic one. The effect of hyperons on the transition phase between hadronic and quark matter is studied. The consequences of the nucleation process for neutron star physics are outlined.

## Key claim

The nucleation of quark matter in neutron stars depends strongly on which chiral quark model is used to describe the deconfined phase; the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and the Chromo Dielectric Model (CDM) give opposite trends for how the presence of hyperons shifts the phase transition pressure, contrary to the simpler MIT bag model used in prior work.

## Method

The authors computed the Gibbs energy per baryon for hadronic matter (described by the non-linear Walecka model with the GM1 parametrization) and for the non-beta-stable Q* quark phase (described by either the NJL or CDM model) as a function of pressure at zero and finite temperature. They then calculated the quantum nucleation time via the WKB approximation and the thermal nucleation rate using a prefactor dominated by shear viscosity, for different hadronic compositions (pure nucleonic matter and hyperonic matter with two choices of hyperon coupling, x_sigma = 0.6 and 0.7). The critical mass M_cr for metastable hadronic stars was defined as the mass for which the nucleation time equals 1 year (cold stars) or 10^3 seconds (proto-neutron stars with entropy per baryon 1-2 k_B).

## Key numbers

- The surface tension sigma is taken as 30 MeV fm^{-2}, within the literature range of 10-50 MeV fm^{-2}.
- The quark correlation length xi_Q is taken as 0.7 fm.
- The shear viscosity of hadronic matter is given by eta = 7.6 x 10^{26} (T/MeV)^{-2} (n_H/n_0)^2 MeV/(fm s), with n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The number of nucleation centers N_c is approximately 10^{48} within a nucleation region of radius R_nuc ~ 100 m.
- The oscillation frequency nu_0^{-1} is set to ~10^{-23} s.
- For the NJL model, the parameters are: Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G Lambda^2 = 1.835, K Lambda^2 = 12.36, m_0^u = 5.5 MeV, m_0^d = 5.5 MeV, m_0^s = 140.7 MeV.
- For the CDM model, the parameters are: f_pi = 93 MeV, f_K = 113 MeV, xi_3 = -0.75 MeV, m_sigma = 1.2 GeV, M_chi = 1.7 GeV, g = 23 MeV.
- At P = 350 MeV fm^{-3} in the CDM, the quark fractions are u: 0.33, d: 0.45, s: 0.22.
- Changing the nucleation time tau from 1 yr to 10^3 s modifies M_cr(T=0) by approximately 0.02%.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of quark matter nucleation in neutron stars that used the MIT bag model (e.g., Bombaci et al. (2000-2008) for zero temperature and thermal nucleation) by replacing the bag model with two chiral quark models: the NJL model and the Chromo Dielectric Model. The authors find that the NJL model disfavors deconfinement when hyperons are present (larger B_eff at transition), while the CDM favors it (smaller B_eff), opposite to the trend seen with a constant bag constant in the MIT bag model. This enables future calculations of the critical mass for proto-neutron stars and cold neutron stars using more realistic chiral descriptions of quark matter, and highlights the need to constrain the strange quark mass behavior in dense matter.
