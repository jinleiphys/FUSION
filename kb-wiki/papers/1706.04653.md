# The reactions $ŽĆŽĆ\rightarrowŽĆŽĆ$ and $╬│╬│\rightarrowŽĆŽĆ$ in $Žć$PT with an isosinglet scalar resonance

> The lowest-lying resonance in the QCD spectrum is the $0^{++}$ isoscalar $Žā$ meson, also known as the $f_0(500)$. We augment SU(2) chiral perturbation theory ($Žć$PT) by including the $Žā$ meson as an additional explicit degree of freedom, as proposed by Soto, Talavera, and Tarr├║s and others. In this effective field theory, denoted $Žć$PT$_S$, the $Žā$ meson's well-established mass and decay width are not sufficient to properly renormalize its self energy. At $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ another low-energy constant appears in the dressed $Žā$-meson propagator; we adjust it so that the isoscalar pion-pion scattering length is also reproduced. We compare the resulting amplitudes for the $ŽĆŽĆ\rightarrowŽĆŽĆ$ and $╬│╬│\rightarrowŽĆŽĆ$ reactions to data from threshold through the energies at which the $Žā$-meson resonance affects observables. The leading-order (LO) $ŽĆŽĆ$ amplitude reproduces the $Žā$-meson pole position, the isoscalar $ŽĆŽĆ$ scattering lengths and $ŽĆŽĆ$ scattering and $╬│╬│\rightarrow ŽĆŽĆ$ data up to $\sqrt{s} \approx 0.5$ GeV. It also yields a $╬│╬│\rightarrowŽĆŽĆ$ amplitude that obeys the Ward identity. The value obtained for the $ŽĆ^0$ polarizability is, however, only slightly larger than that obtained in standard $Žć$PT.

# The reactions $\pi\pi\rightarrow\pi\pi$ and $\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi\pi$ in $\chi$PT with an isosinglet scalar resonance

## Key claim

Augmenting SU(2) chiral perturbation theory with an explicit $\sigma$ meson degree of freedom ($\chi$PT$_S$) yields a leading-order amplitude that reproduces the $\sigma$-meson pole position, isoscalar $\pi\pi$ scattering lengths, and both $\pi\pi$ scattering and $\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi\pi$ data up to $\sqrt{s} \approx 0.5$ GeV, but only if an additional low-energy constant $f_{2p,r}$ (beyond the $\sigma$ mass and decay width) is adjusted to also reproduce the isoscalar pion-pion scattering length.

## Method

The authors augment SU(2) chiral perturbation theory by including the $\sigma$ meson as an explicit dynamical degree of freedom, following the $\chi$PT$_S$ framework of Soto, Talavera, and Tarrus (2011). They compute the $\sigma$-meson self energy at one-loop order ($\mathcal{O}(p^4)$) and resum it via a Dyson equation, using the renormalized propagator with three free parameters ($m_{S,r}$, $c_{1d,r}$, $f_{2p,r}$) constrained by the $\sigma$ pole position from the Particle Data Group and the experimental isoscalar $\pi\pi$ scattering length. The leading-order $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitude combines the standard $\mathcal{O}(p^2)$ $\chi$PT contact term with the $s$-channel $\sigma$ pole, while $t$- and $u$-channel $\sigma$ exchanges are treated as next-to-leading order. The same dressed $\sigma$ propagator is then applied to $\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi\pi$.

## Key numbers

- $\sigma$-meson pole position: $M_\sigma = (449^{+22}_{-16})$ MeV, $\Gamma = (550 \pm 24)$ MeV, from the Particle Data Group review by Pelaez (2015)
- Isoscalar $\pi\pi$ scattering length: $a_0^0 = 0.2210(47)(40) \, m_\pi^{-1}$, from Batley et al. (2010) K$_{e4}$ decay analysis
- Fitted $\chi$PT$_S$ parameters: $m_{S,r} = 221^{+5}_{-4}$ MeV; $c_{1d,r} = 0.206^{+0.001}_{-0.002}$; $f_{2p,r} = (3.4^{+0.01}_{-0.02}) \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$
- Pion decay constant: $F = 92.419$ MeV; pion mass: $m_\pi = 139.57$ MeV, from Agashe et al. (2014)
- Leading-order $\chi$PT$_S$ describes data up to $\sqrt{s} \approx 0.5$ GeV for both $\pi\pi$ scattering and $\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi\pi$

## Context

This work extends the $\chi$PT$_S$ framework of Soto, Talavera, and Tarrus (2011), who focused on static properties and lattice extrapolations, and Hansen et al. (2016), who computed one-loop corrections to pion mass, decay constant, and $\sigma$ width. It differs from Ametller and Talavera (2014, 2015) by providing a consistent treatment of the $\sigma$ width and $\sigma\pi\pi$ vertex that obeys the Ward identity for $\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi\pi$, avoiding the inconsistency in those earlier works. The approach enables a systematic effective field theory description of $0^{++}$ channel reactions from threshold through the resonance region, using a power counting with two light scales ($m_\pi \ll M_\sigma \ll \Lambda_{\chi{\rm SB}}$) analogous to the $\Delta(1232)$ treatment in baryon chiral perturbation theory by Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003) and McGovern et al. (2012).
