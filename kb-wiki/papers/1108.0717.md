# Higher-order anisotropic flows and dihadron correlations in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV in a multiphase transport model

> Using a multiphase transport model that includes both initial partonic and final hadronic scatterings, we have studied higher-order anisotropic flows as well as dihadron correlations as functions of pseudorapidity and azimuthal angular differences between trigger and associated particles in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. With parameters in the model determined previously from fitting the measured multiplicity density of mid-pseudorapidity charged particles in central collisions and their elliptic flow in mid-central collisions, the calculated higher-order anisotropic flows from the two-particle cumulant method reproduce approximately those measured by the ALICE Collaboration, except at small centralities where they are slightly overestimated. Similar to experimental results, the two-dimensional dihadron correlations at most central collisions show a ridge structure at the near side and a broad structure at the away side. The short- and long-range dihadron azimuthal correlations, corresponding to small and large pseudorapidity differences, respectively, are studied for triggering particles with different transverse momenta and are found to be qualitatively consistent with experimental results from the CMS Collaboration. The relation between the short-range and long-range dihadron correlations with that induced by back-to-back jet pairs produced from initial hard collisions is also discussed.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with parameters previously fitted to reproduce charged-particle multiplicity in central collisions and elliptic flow in mid-central collisions, the calculated higher-order anisotropic flows (v2, v3, v4) from the two-particle cumulant method approximately reproduce ALICE measurements, except at small centralities where they are slightly overestimated.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model, which combines initial particle generation from the HIJING model with parton cascade via ZPC, hadronization via spatial coalescence, and final hadronic scatterings via the ART model. Anisotropic flows were calculated using the two-particle cumulant method with a pseudorapidity gap of |Δη|>1 to reduce nonflow effects. Dihadron correlations were computed using the same-event to mixed-event ratio method with ZYAM background subtraction.

## Key numbers

- Multiplicity density of charged particles at mid-pseudorapidity in 0-5% most central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN=2.76 TeV is 2.2 times that in Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV at RHIC
- Transverse momentum integration range for anisotropic flows: 0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c
- Transverse momentum windows for dihadron correlations: trigger particles 2 < pTtrig < 3 GeV/c, 3 < pTtrig < 4 GeV/c, and 4 < pTtrig < 5 GeV/c; associated particles 1 < pTassoc < 2 GeV/c
- Pseudorapidity cuts used: |η| < 0.8 for mid-pseudorapidity charged particles; |Δη| > 1 for flow calculations; |Δη| < 1 for short-range and 2 < |Δη| < 4 for long-range correlations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMPT model study of Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN=2.76 TeV (Xu and Ko, 2011) by calculating higher-order anisotropic flows (v3, v4, v5) and dihadron correlations, comparing with ALICE data (Aamodt et al., 2011) and CMS data (Chatrchyan et al., 2011). The results confirm that the near-side ridge structure in dihadron correlations is dominated by anisotropic flows rather than jet-medium interactions, consistent with findings by Luzum (2011) and the experimental collaborations. The study enables future extraction of jet-medium interaction signals by subtracting the flow background contribution from dihadron correlations.
