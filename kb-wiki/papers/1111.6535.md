# D^- mesic atoms

> The anti-D meson self-energy is evaluated self-consistently, using unitarized coupled-channel theory, by computing the in-medium meson-baryon T-matrix in the C=-1,S=0 sector. The heavy pseudo-scalar and heavy vector mesons, anti-D and anti-D^*, are treated on equal footing as required by heavy quark spin symmetry. Results for energy levels and widths of D^- mesic atoms in 12C, 40Ca, 118Sn and 208Pb are presented. The spectrum contains states of atomic and of nuclear types for all nuclei. anti-D^0--nucleus bound states are also obtained. We find that, after electromagnetic and nuclear cascade, these systems end up with the anti-D bound in the nucleus, either as a meson or as part of a exotic anti-D N (pentaquark) loosely bound state.

# D⁻ mesic atoms

## Key claim

The authors predict that D⁻ mesic atoms exist with both atomic-type and nuclear-type bound states across a range of nuclei, but the existence and properties of these states depend sensitively on the regularization parameter α in the subtraction scheme, with the most realistic calculation (α=1) producing a weakly bound pentaquark state X(2805) that drives the attractive optical potential, while changing α to 1.2 eliminates this bound state and significantly alters the predicted spectrum.

## Method

The authors computed the anti-D meson self-energy in symmetric nuclear matter using a self-consistent unitarized coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach with an SU(8) extended Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction that treats anti-D and anti-D* mesons on equal footing as required by heavy quark spin symmetry. They solved the Schrödinger equation with an energy-dependent optical potential derived from the in-medium self-energy via the local density approximation, including the Coulomb potential with finite nuclear size and Uehling vacuum polarization, and incorporating a nucleon energy gap of 8 MeV to account for the finite energy needed to extract a nucleon from the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The X(2805) pentaquark state appears as a pole in the free-space I=0, J=1/2 amplitude at 2805 MeV, bound by about 1 MeV relative to the anti-D N threshold.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with α=1 and 8 MeV gap, the 1s atomic state has binding energy 0.036 keV and width 0.0002 keV, while the 1s nuclear-type state has binding energy 24.9 MeV and width 0.3 MeV.
- For ¹²C with α=1 and 8 MeV gap, the 1s atomic state has binding energy 0.0006 keV and width 0.0001 keV, while the 1s nuclear-type state has binding energy 10.3 MeV and width 0.9 MeV.
- The gap between nuclear and atomic states ranges from 15 to 20 MeV for all nuclei studied (¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, ¹¹⁸Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb).
- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the study of hadronic atoms from pions, anti-kaons, and anti-protons to the anti-charmed sector, where little theoretical work existed previously beyond the quark-meson coupling model of Tsushima (1998) for ²⁰⁸Pb. The authors' SU(8) approach incorporating heavy quark spin symmetry produces an attractive optical potential with a resonant structure near threshold, in contrast to the repulsive potential found in the SU(4) model of Tolos (2007) and the t-channel vector meson exchange models of Lutz (2005) and Jimenez-Tejero (2011), which ignored anti-D* coupled-channel effects. The predicted nuclear-type bound states could be studied experimentally at the future FAIR facility by the PANDA and CBM collaborations.
