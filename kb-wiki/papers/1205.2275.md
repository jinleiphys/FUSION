# A narrow $DNN$ quasi-bound state

> The energies and widths of $DNN$ quasi-bound states with isospin I=1/2 are evaluated in two methods, the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equation and the variational method approach to the effective one-channel Hamiltonian. The $DN$ interactions are constructed so that they dynamically generate the $Λ_c(2595)$ (I=0, $J^π =1/2^-$) resonance state. We find that the system is bound by about 250 MeV from the $DNN$ threshold, $\sqrt{s} \sim 3500$ MeV. Its width including both the mesonic decay and the $D$ absorption, is estimated to be about 20-40 MeV. The I=0 $DN$ pair in the $DNN$ system is found to form a cluster that is similar to the $Λ_c(2595)$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a $DNN$ three-body system with total isospin $I=1/2$ forms a narrow quasi-bound state bound by about 250 MeV from the $DNN$ threshold, with a total width of only 20-40 MeV, which is much narrower than the analogous $\bar{K}NN$ system because the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance that drives the binding has a much smaller width than the $\Lambda(1405)$.

## Method

The authors used two complementary theoretical approaches: the fixed center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, where the $NN$ pair is treated as a cluster and the $D$ meson scatters from it, and a variational method with an effective one-channel Hamiltonian. The $DN$ interactions were constructed from a coupled-channel model using the Weinberg-Tomozawa term that dynamically generates the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance, and the $D$ absorption by two nucleons ($DNN \rightarrow N\Lambda_c$) was evaluated to estimate the width.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the $DNN$ system: about 250 MeV from the $DNN$ threshold at $\sqrt{s} \sim 3500$ MeV
- Total width of the $DNN$ quasi-bound state: 20-40 MeV, including both mesonic decay and $D$ absorption
- Width of the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance: less than 1.9 MeV (experimental value)
- Width of the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ from the model without absorption: 3 MeV; with absorption included: 15 MeV
- Range parameter of the $DN$ potential: $a_s = 0.4$ fm
- Average separation of nucleons in the $NN$ cluster: $R_{NN} \simeq 2$ fm
- Subtraction constants at $\mu = 1$ GeV: $a_{DN} = -2.056$, $a_i = -2.06$ for $i \neq DN$
- A resonance state is also generated in the $I=1$ $DN$ channel at approximately 2760 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of meson-nucleus bound states from the kaon sector to the charm sector, specifically substituting the $\bar{K}$ meson by a $D$ meson in the $\bar{K}NN$ system studied by Ikeda (2007), Shevchenko (2006, 2007), Dote (2008), and others. The $DN$ interaction in $I=0$ is predicted to be attractive and dynamically generates the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance, analogous to how the $\Lambda(1405)$ is generated from $\bar{K}N$ interactions as studied by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), Lutz (2001), Jido (2003), and Hyodo (2011). The narrow width of the $DNN$ state, in contrast to the broad $\bar{K}NN$ state, makes it a promising candidate for experimental observation, and the results enable future experimental searches for charm nuclear bound states.
