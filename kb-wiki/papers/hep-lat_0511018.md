# Study of color superconductivity with Ginzburg-Landau effective action on the lattice

> We study thermal phase transitions of color superconductivity by the lattice simulations of the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) effective theory. The theory is equivalent to the SU_ f(3) cross SU_c(3) Higgs model coupled to SU_c(3) color gauge fields. From the eigenvalues of a 3-by-3 gauge-invariant diquark composite, a clear distinction between the 2-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) and the color flavor locking (CFL) phase is made in a gauge invariant manner. The thermal transitions between the normal phase and the superconducting phases are found to be first-order due to thermal gluons. The phase structure in the coupling-constant space is numerically explored and three patterns of phase transition, i.e., normal to 2SC, normal to CFL and normal to CFL via 2SC, are found in the chiral limit. These results agree qualitatively with the weak-coupling analysis of the GL theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice simulations of the Ginzburg-Landau effective theory for color superconductivity yield first-order thermal phase transitions and a phase diagram that agrees qualitatively with weak-coupling analysis, but the results are obtained in a truncated parameter space and are not directly mapped to the temperature-chemical potential plane of QCD.

## Method

The authors discretize a four-dimensional extension of the three-dimensional Ginzburg-Landau effective action for color superconductivity on a 2×12³ lattice, using a pseudo heat-bath method to update SU(3) gauge-link variables and a generalized algorithm for the Higgs-like diquark field. They identify the 2SC and CFL phases by diagonalizing the gauge-invariant 3×3 flavor matrix Σ_x Φ†_x Φ_x and extracting its eigenvalues, and determine the order of transitions by observing hysteresis loops in gauge-invariant operators and applying a boundary-shift test to locate the global minimum of the free energy.

## Key numbers

- Lattice volume used: 2×12³; a 2×8³ lattice gives qualitatively the same results.
- Gauge coupling fixed at β = 5.1, corresponding to the critical value for pure Yang-Mills with N_τ = 2.
- For (λ̄₁, λ̄₂) = (0.03, 0.01) at κ̄ = 0.34 and β = 5.1, the eigenvalue pattern shows one large eigenvalue with two degenerate small eigenvalues, identifying the 2SC phase.
- For (λ̄₁, λ̄₂) = (0.03, 0.03) at κ̄ = 0.43 and β = 5.1, three degenerate eigenvalues are observed, identifying the CFL phase.
- Hysteresis loop shown for (λ̄₁, λ̄₂) = (0.01, 0.005) at β = 5.1.

## Context

This work extends the weak-coupling analysis of the Ginzburg-Landau effective theory for color superconductivity by Iida and Baym (2001) and the studies of gluon fluctuations by Matsuura and Ren (2004) to a non-perturbative lattice simulation, avoiding the sign problem that plagues direct lattice QCD at finite density. The results enable future matching of the Ginzburg-Landau coupling constants to the temperature and chemical potential of microscopic QCD, which would allow construction of a realistic phase diagram including quark masses and color and charge neutrality constraints.
