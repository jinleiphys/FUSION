# Variations on a theme by Skyrme

> We present a survey of the phenomenological adjustment of the parameters of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model for a self-consistent description of nuclear structure and low-energy excitations. A large sample of reliable input data from nuclear bulk properties (energy, radii, surface thickness) is selected guided by the criterion that ground-state correlations should remain small. Least squares fitting techniques are used to determine the SHF parameters which accommodate best the given input data. The question of the predictive value of the adjustment is scrutinized by performing systematic variations with respect to chosen nuclear matter properties (incompressibility, effective mass, symmetry energy, and sum-rule enhancement factor). We find that the ground-state data, although representing a large sample, leave a broad range of choices, i.e. a broad range of nuclear matter properties. Information from giant resonances is added to pin down more precisely the open features. We then apply the set of newly adjusted parameterizations to several more detailed observables such as neutron skin, isotope shifts, and super-heavy elements. The techniques of least-squares fitting provide safe estimates for the uncertainties of such extrapolations. The systematic variation of forces allows to disentangle the various influences on a given observable and to estimate the predictive value of the SHF model. The results depend very much on the observable under consideration.

## Key claim

Ground-state nuclear data (energies, radii, surface thicknesses), even from a large sample, leave a broad range of choices for Skyrme-Hartree-Fock parameters, meaning that nuclear matter properties such as incompressibility, effective mass, and symmetry energy remain poorly constrained until information from giant resonances is added.

## Method

The authors selected a set of ground-state observables (binding energies, charge radii, diffraction radii, surface thicknesses) from semi-magic nuclei where ground-state correlations remain small, using the criterion that correlation effects should stay below adopted error limits (1 MeV for energy, 0.02 fm for rms radii, 0.04 fm for diffraction radii and surface thickness). They performed least-squares fits of the Skyrme energy-density functional parameters, systematically varying constraints on nuclear matter properties (incompressibility, effective mass, symmetry energy, sum-rule enhancement factor) and then adding giant resonance peak frequencies from 208Pb as additional selection criteria.

## Key numbers

- Adopted error for binding energies: 1 MeV; for rms radii: 0.02 fm; for diffraction radii and surface thickness: 0.04 fm
- Adopted error for pairing gaps: 0.12 MeV; for spin-orbit splittings: 10%
- Experimental giant resonance peak frequencies in 208Pb used as selection criteria: GMR at 13.7 MeV, GDR at 13.6 MeV, GQR at 10.9 MeV
- Pairing cutoff parameters: epsilon_cut = 5 MeV, Delta_epsilon = epsilon_cut/10
- Typical value for density-dependent pairing parameter: rho_pair = 0.16 fm^{-3} (surface pairing)

## Context

This work extends the long history of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock parameter optimization (reviewed by Bender (2003) and Stone (2007)) by systematically exploring how different nuclear matter properties affect the predictive power of the model. The authors build on earlier correlation studies by Benda (2005, 2006) and Kluepfel (2008) to select fit data minimally affected by ground-state correlations. The results enable estimation of uncertainties for extrapolations to neutron skin, isotope shifts, and super-heavy elements, while also pointing to incompatibilities in the present SHF ansatz when covering more than basic ground-state properties.
