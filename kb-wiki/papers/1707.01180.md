# Exotic tetraquark states with the $qq\bar{Q}\bar{Q}$ configuration

> In this work, we study systematically the mass splittings of the $qq\bar{Q}\bar{Q}$ ($q=u$, $d$, $s$ and $Q=c$, $b$) tetraquark states with the color-magnetic interaction by considering color mixing effects and estimate roughly their masses. We find that the color mixing effect is relatively important for the $J^P=0^+$ states and possible stable tetraquarks exist in the $nn\bar{Q}\bar{Q}$ ($n=u$, $d$) and $ns\bar{Q}\bar{Q}$ systems either with $J=0$ or with $J=1$. Possible decay patterns of the tetraquarks are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that color mixing between different color-spin configurations is relatively important for the \(J^P=0^+\) states, and that possible stable tetraquarks may exist in the \(nn\bar{Q}\bar{Q}\) (\(n=u,d\)) and \(ns\bar{Q}\bar{Q}\) systems with either \(J=0\) or \(J=1\), but these conclusions depend on the estimated masses from the threshold scheme and the approximations used for the effective coupling constants.

## Method

The authors used the color-magnetic interaction (CMI) Hamiltonian, derived from one-gluon exchange, to calculate mass splittings of tetraquark states with the \(qq\bar{Q}\bar{Q}\) configuration. They constructed diquark-antidiquark basis states with appropriate color, spin, and flavor wave functions, enforced the Pauli principle, and diagonalized the CMI matrices to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors. Masses were estimated in two schemes: one using effective constituent quark masses extracted from baryons, and a second "threshold scheme" relating the tetraquark mass to a reference meson-meson system.

## Key numbers

- \(C_{nn} = 18.3\) MeV, \(C_{ns} = 12.3\) MeV, \(C_{ss} = 6.4\) MeV, extracted from baryon mass splittings.
- \(C_{\bar{c}n} = 6.6\) MeV, \(C_{\bar{c}s} = 6.7\) MeV, \(C_{\bar{b}n} = 2.1\) MeV, \(C_{\bar{b}s} = 2.3\) MeV, extracted from meson mass splittings.
- For the \(nn\bar{c}\bar{c}\) system with \(I=1\), the lowest \(0^+\) state has an estimated mass of 3850 MeV in the threshold scheme (below the \(\bar{D}\bar{D}^*\) threshold).
- For the \(nn\bar{b}\bar{b}\) system with \(I=0\), the lowest \(1^+\) state has an estimated mass of 10483 MeV in the threshold scheme (below the \(BB^*\) threshold).
- For the \(nn\bar{c}\bar{b}\) system with \(I=0\), the lowest \(0^+\) state has an estimated mass of 7041 MeV in the threshold scheme (below the \(B\bar{D}\) threshold).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tetraquark states with the \(qq\bar{Q}\bar{Q}\) configuration using the CMI model, following earlier applications to tetraquark and pentaquark systems by Wu et al. (2016, 2017) and Chen et al. (2016). It builds on prior theoretical work using various potentials (e.g., Ader 1981, Silvestre-Brac 1993, Vijande 2006) and lattice QCD simulations (e.g., Peters 2016) that suggested possible stability of such tetraquarks. The results provide mass estimates and decay pattern predictions that could guide experimental searches for these exotic states in channels such as \(\bar{D}^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}\), \(B^{(*)}B^{(*)}\), and \(B^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}\).
