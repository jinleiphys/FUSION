# Bounds on variations of the strange quark mass from Big Bang nucleosynthesis

> We analyze the effect of a variation of the strange nucleon matrix element $\langle N| m_s \bar{s}s|N\rangle$ on the abundances of the light elements produced in the Big Bang. For that, we vary the nucleon mass in the leading eight reactions that involve neutrons, protons and the four lightest nuclei. We use various available Big Bang nucleosynthesis codes and find that the measured deuterium and $^4$He abundances set strict limits on the nucleon mass variations. This translates into an upper bound of possible variations of the strange quark mass, $\left| Δm_s/m_s \right| \leq 5.1\%$.

## Key claim

The paper derives an upper bound on possible variations of the strange quark mass from Big Bang nucleosynthesis, finding that the measured deuterium and helium-4 abundances constrain the fractional change in the strange quark mass to at most 5.1%, under the assumption that the strange quark condensate in the nucleon remains constant.

## Method

The authors varied the nucleon mass by up to ±10% in the eight leading BBN reactions involving neutrons, protons, deuterium, tritium, helium-3, and helium-4, using nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) and pionless EFT to compute how nucleon-nucleon scattering parameters and binding energies change with nucleon mass. They modeled the nucleon mass dependence of reaction rates through changes in reduced masses, Q-values, and Coulomb penetration factors, then ran multiple BBN codes (Kawano, PRyMordial, AlterBBN, PRIMAT) to compute the resulting primordial abundances and compare with observational data.

## Key numbers

- The strange sigma-term (strange quark contribution to nucleon mass) is σ_s = 44.9 ± 6.4 MeV from N_f = 2+1 lattice QCD simulations (FLAG average)
- The pion-nucleon sigma term is σ_πN = 59.0 ± 3.5 MeV from Roy-Steiner analysis
- The upper bound on strange quark mass variation is |Δm_s/m_s| ≤ 5.1%
- The deuteron binding energy K-factor from NLEFT is K^{m_N}_{B_d} = 5.276 ± 0.001
- The helium-3 binding energy K-factor from NLEFT is K^{m_N}_{B_{^3He}} = 3.662 ± 0.066
- The helium-4 binding energy K-factor from NLEFT is K^{m_N}_{B_{^4He}} = 1.685 ± 0.025
- The inverse singlet scattering length K-factor from NLEFT is K^{m_N}_{a_s} = 13.437 ± 0.001

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fundamental constant variations in BBN, particularly the variation of the fine-structure constant (Meißner 2023) and light quark masses (Burns 2024, Meyer 2024), by considering for the first time the effect of strangeness in the nucleon via the matrix element ⟨N|m_s s̄s|N⟩. The analysis uses the same formalism as earlier work on fine-structure constant variation (Meißner 2023) and relies on binding energy relations from Bedaque (2010) and Berengut (2013). The results enable future studies to incorporate strangeness effects when searching for new physics or variations of fundamental constants in BBN, and provide constraints on the strange quark mass variation that complement direct lattice QCD determinations.
