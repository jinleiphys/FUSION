# 3-D hydro + cascade model at RHIC

> We present a 3-D hydro + cascade model in which viscosity and a realistic freezeout process for the hadronic phase are taken into account. We compare our results to experimental data and discuss the finite state interaction effects on physical observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully three-dimensional hybrid model combining hydrodynamic expansion with a hadronic cascade (UrQMD) that includes viscosity and a realistic, species-dependent freezeout process, claiming that this approach resolves discrepancies between perfect hydrodynamic models and experimental data at RHIC, particularly for hadron ratios and transverse momentum spectra above 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors solve relativistic hydrodynamic equations in light-cone coordinates using Lagrangian hydrodynamics, starting at proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm with initial energy and baryon density parameterized by a combination of wounded nucleon and binary collision models. At a switching temperature of 150 MeV (below the critical temperature of 160 MeV), hadron distributions are calculated via the Cooper-Frye formula and used as initial conditions for the UrQMD cascade model on an event-by-event basis, which then handles final state interactions and viscous effects in the hadronic phase.

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamic expansion starts at tau_0 = 0.6 fm
- Maximum initial energy density for pure hydro: 55 GeV/fm^3; for hybrid model: 43 GeV/fm^3
- Maximum initial baryon number density: 0.15 fm^{-3} for both models
- Thermal freezeout temperature: 110 MeV
- Critical temperature: 160 MeV; switching temperature: 150 MeV
- Calculated P_T spectra for pi, K, and p at Au+Au sqrt(s) = 200 GeV central collisions agree with PHENIX data up to P_T ~ 2 GeV
- Impact parameters for centrality bins: b = 2.4 fm (0-6%), 4.5 fm (6-15%), 6.3 fm (15-25%), 7.9 fm (25-35%)

## Context

This work extends earlier hybrid models that had reduced dimensionality and were restricted to mid-rapidity observables (Hybrid, 2004), and addresses limitations of perfect hydrodynamic models noted by Hirano (2004) regarding freezeout treatment and by St. (2004) regarding viscous effects at AGS, SPS, and RHIC. The model enables comprehensive study of final state interaction effects on observables such as mean transverse momentum and pseudorapidity distributions across a wide rapidity range, and allows investigation of the "pion-wind" effect on proton and pion transverse momentum.
