# Spin symmetry in the Dirac sea derived from the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction

> The spin symmetry in the Dirac sea has been investigated with relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. Taking the nucleus $^{16}$O as an example and comparing the theoretical results with the data, the definition of the single-particle potential in the Dirac sea is studied in detail. It is found that if the single-particle states in the Dirac sea are treated as occupied states, the ground state properties are in better agreement with experimental data. Moreover, in this case, the spin symmetry in the Dirac sea is better conserved and it is more consistent with the findings using phenomenological relativistic density functionals.

## Key claim

Spin symmetry in the Dirac sea is better conserved when single-particle states in the Dirac sea are treated as occupied hole states rather than as empty states in relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn A, but the agreement with experimental ground-state properties is mixed: the total energy improves while charge radius and spin-orbit splittings become slightly worse.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations for the nucleus 16O using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn A, a relativistic one-boson-exchange potential fitted to NN scattering data. They compared two definitions of the single-particle potential in the Dirac sea: a "previous" definition where Dirac-sea states were treated as unoccupied (similar to particle states above the Fermi level) and a "present" definition where Dirac-sea states were treated as occupied hole states. The starting energy in the G-matrix was defined accordingly for each case, and the resulting single-particle spectra, ground-state properties, and spin-orbit splittings in the Dirac sea were analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of 16O: -120.2 MeV with the present definition, compared to -113.5 MeV with the previous definition and the experimental value of -127.6 MeV
- RMS charge radius of 16O: 2.53 fm with the present definition, compared to 2.56 fm with the previous definition and the experimental value of 2.70 fm
- Proton 1p spin-orbit splitting in 16O: 5.3 MeV with the present definition, compared to 5.4 MeV with the previous definition and the experimental value of 6.3 MeV
- Single-particle spectra in the Dirac sea with the present definition are higher by 100 to 200 MeV compared to the previous definition

## Context

This work extends previous RBHF studies by Shen et al. (2016, 2017) that developed a relativistic ab initio method for finite nuclei, and it is the first investigation of spin symmetry in the Dirac sea starting from a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction rather than phenomenological relativistic density functionals. The results are compared with phenomenological functionals PKDD (Long et al., 2004) and PKO1 (Long et al., 2006), and the present definition yields spin-orbit splittings more consistent with those functionals. The work enables future RBHF investigations using the recommended definition of the single-particle potential in the Dirac sea, and opens the question of how different bare interactions (such as relativistic chiral interactions) and different channels of the G-matrix contribute to spin symmetry in the Dirac sea.
