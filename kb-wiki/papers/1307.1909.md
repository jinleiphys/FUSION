# Systematic of isovector and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances in normal and superfluid spherical nuclei

> The isoscalar (IS) and isovector (IV) quadrupole responses of nuclei are systematically investigated using the time-dependent Skyrme Energy Density Functional including pairing in the BCS approximation. Using two different Skyrme functionals, Sly4 and SkM*, respectively 263 and 304 nuclei have been found to be spherical along the nuclear charts. The time-dependent evolution of these nuclei has been systematically performed giving access to their quadrupole responses. It is shown that the mean-energy of the collective high energy state globally reproduces the experimental IS and IV collective energy but fails to reproduce their lifetimes. It is found that the mean collective energy depends rather significantly on the functional used in the mean-field channel. Pairing by competing with parity effects can slightly affect the collective response around magic numbers and induces a reduction of the collective energy compared to the average trend. Low-lying states, that can only be considered if pairing is included, are investigated. While the approach provides a fair estimate of the low lying state energy, it strongly underestimates the transition rate $B(E2)$. Finally, the possibility to access to the density dependence of the symmetry energy through parallel measurements of both the IS- and IV-GQR is discussed.

## Key claim

The mean-field TDHF+BCS approach globally reproduces the experimental mean energies of the isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole resonances across spherical nuclei, but it fails to reproduce their lifetimes (widths) because the calculated strength is essentially a single collective peak without the fragmentation observed experimentally, except for light nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic survey of 263 (Sly4) and 304 (SkM*) even-even spherical nuclei selected from a list of 749 measured masses, using the static HF+BCS code EV8 to obtain ground states. They then applied a small initial quadrupole boost (isoscalar or isovector) to each nucleus and solved the time-dependent TDHF+BCS equations in 3D real space up to 50×10⁻²² s, extracting the strength function via Fourier transform of the quadrupole moment evolution. The mean collective energy and width of the high-lying giant resonance were obtained by fitting the main peak with a Lorentzian distribution and correcting for the artificial damping factor Γ₀.

## Key numbers

* 263 spherical nuclei were found with the Sly4 functional; 304 spherical nuclei were found with the SkM* functional.
* The mesh step was Δx = Δy = Δz = 0.8 fm, with a total mesh size of 2Lx = 2Ly = 2Lz = 22.4 fm.
* The numerical time-step was Δt = 1.5×10⁻²⁴ s, and evolutions were followed up to a maximal time T = 50×10⁻²² s.
* The Sly4 functional gives IS-GQR collective energies systematically higher than SkM* by almost 1 MeV for A ≥ 70.
* The incompressibility modulus is K∞ = 216.6 MeV for SkM* and K∞ = 229.9 MeV for Sly4.
* The fit of the proton rms radius gave a₀ = 1.22 fm for both functionals; for Sly4 aS = 2.035 fm and aI = 0.186 fm; for SkM* aS = 1.9206 fm and aI = 0.189 fm.
* The error on the total Energy Weighted Sum Rule (EWSR) was lower than 0.5% for all nuclei considered.
* The IS-GQR collective energy fit parameters (third-order polynomial in A⁻¹/³) for SkM*: a₁ = 65.03 MeV, a₂ = 52.93 MeV, a₃ = -211.67 MeV; for Sly4: a₁ = 75.50 MeV, a₂ = 3.13 MeV, a₃ = -143.69 MeV.
* The IV-GQR collective energy fit parameters for SkM*: a₁ = 177 MeV, a₂ = -255.383 MeV, a₃ = 10.05 MeV; for Sly4: a₁ = 169.36 MeV, a₂ = -223.16 MeV, a₃ = -13.06 MeV.

## Context

This work provides the first systematic investigation of both isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole resonances using a time-dependent Energy Density Functional framework with pairing (TDHF+BCS), extending the static QRPA analysis of Terasaki and Engel (2006) to a dynamical approach. The study enables quantitative comparison of different Skyrme functionals (Sly4 and SkM*) against experimental data from Bertrand (1981) and others, and it discusses the possibility of using parallel measurements of IS- and IV-GQR to access the density dependence of the symmetry energy, as proposed by Roca-Maza et al. (2013). The failure to reproduce experimental widths confirms the known limitation of mean-field theories in capturing the coupling to two-particle-two-hole states or low-lying surface modes, as discussed in Lacroix et al. (2004).
