# Improved quasiparticle nuclear Hamiltonians for quantum computing

> Quantum computing is increasingly offering concrete solutions toward the simulation of nuclear structure, with the potential to overcome the exponential scaling that limits classical diagonalization methods in large spaces. A particularly efficient encoding scheme, based on collective like-nucleon pairing modes, reduces the qubit requirements by half and avoids the non-local operator strings of standard fermion-to-qubit mappings. While this quasiparticle framework provides accurate results for semimagic nuclei, it does not adequately describe open-shell systems where proton-neutron correlations become important. In this work, we apply Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory to systematically improve the quasiparticle description of open-shell nuclei in the $sd$ shell, reaching an energy relative error below $0.2\%$ compared to the nuclear shell model. Furthermore, to make the effective Hamiltonian suitable for quantum simulation, we introduce a mean-field Hartree-Fock approximation of the non-quasiparticle resolvent, achieving ground-state energies typically within $2\%$ of the exact shell-model result. This represents a systematic improvement over the bare quasiparticle Hamiltonian while remaining within the reach of near-term quantum devices.

## Key claim

By applying Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory to systematically improve the quasiparticle description of open-shell nuclei in the sd shell, the authors achieve energy relative errors below 0.2% compared to the nuclear shell model, and with a further mean-field Hartree-Fock approximation of the non-quasiparticle resolvent they obtain ground-state energies typically within 2% of the exact shell-model result, representing a systematic improvement over the bare quasiparticle Hamiltonian while remaining within reach of near-term quantum devices.

## Method

The authors partition the nuclear many-body Hilbert space into a quasiparticle model space (spanned by collective like-nucleon pairing modes) and its orthogonal complement, then apply Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory to derive an energy-dependent effective Hamiltonian acting only within the quasiparticle sector. For quantum-device suitability, they truncate the effective Hamiltonian to two-body quasiparticle interactions by restricting to the one-quasiparticle-proton--one-quasiparticle-neutron subspace (the Nn=Zp=2 Fock sector), and further approximate the resolvent using a Hartree-Fock reference state projected onto the complementary space, replacing the full spectral decomposition with a single pole. The effective Hamiltonian is then assembled classically and passed to a quantum device for ground-state estimation without any further classical-quantum feedback loop.

## Key numbers

- Energy relative error below 0.2% compared to the nuclear shell model for open-shell nuclei in the sd shell, achieved with the full Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory approach.
- Ground-state energies typically within 2% of the exact shell-model result using the Hartree-Fock approximation of the non-quasiparticle resolvent.
- The bare quasiparticle approximation can introduce energy errors exceeding 10% for open-shell nuclei with similar numbers of protons and neutrons, as reported in prior work (Costa 2026).
- The sd shell valence space has D=12 single-particle states per species, comprising the proton and neutron 0d5/2, 1s1/2, and 0d3/2 orbitals.
- The quasiparticle encoding reduces the number of required qubits by half compared to a direct fermion encoding: the 0d5/2, 1s1/2, and 0d3/2 orbitals yield three, one, and two quasiparticle modes respectively.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle pairing encoding approach developed in prior studies (Costa 2026, 2024; PhysRevC.109.064305), which provided accurate results for semimagic nuclei but introduced energy errors exceeding 10% for open-shell systems where proton-neutron correlations are important. The Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory method is well established in nuclear physics, condensed matter, and particle physics (Brillouin 1932; Shavitt and Bartlett 2009), and the Hartree-Fock approximation follows standard mean-field techniques (Kelly 1967). This work enables the application of the efficient quasiparticle encoding to a broader range of nuclei across the nuclear chart, making it suitable for near-term quantum devices including variational quantum eigensolvers and analog quantum simulators such as Rydberg atom platforms.
