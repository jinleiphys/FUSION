# Coulomb corrections and multiple e+e- pair production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> We consider the problem of Coulomb corrections to the inclusive cross section. We show that these corrections in the limiting case of small charge number of one of the nuclei coincide with those to the exclusive cross section. Within our approach we also obtain the Coulomb corrections for the case of large charge numbers of both nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the inclusive cross section \(\sigma_T\) for \(e^+e^-\) pair production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions does contain Coulomb corrections, contrary to earlier claims by other authors; the apparent absence of these corrections in prior work arose from an illegal change of integration order before regularization of the divergent integrals.

## Method

The authors start from the eikonal-scattering-amplitude expression for the cross section \(\sigma_T\) and split it into a Born term and correction terms \(\sigma^c_A\), \(\sigma^c_B\), and \(\sigma^c_{AB}\) that isolate contributions from higher-order Coulomb effects from each nucleus and from both nuclei together. They regularize the divergent integrals by using a finite-range effective potential (with screening parameter \(a_\pm\) related to the Lorentz factor \(\gamma\)) and expand the matrix element in the small transverse momenta \(\mathbf{k},\mathbf{k}'\). The integrals over these momenta are then evaluated analytically, yielding the functions \(G_{A,B}\) expressed in terms of the digamma function \(\psi\) and the Euler constant \(C\).

## Key numbers

- Born term: \(\sigma^b = \frac{28(Z_A\alpha)^2(Z_B\alpha)^2}{27\pi m^2} \ln^3(\gamma^2)\)
- Coulomb correction from nucleus A: \(\sigma^c_A = -\frac{28(Z_A\alpha)^2(Z_B\alpha)^2}{9\pi m^2} f(Z_A\alpha) \ln^2(\gamma^2)\), where \(f(Z\alpha) = \operatorname{Re}\psi(1+i Z\alpha) + C\)
- Coulomb correction from nucleus B: \(\sigma^c_B = -\frac{28(Z_A\alpha)^2(Z_B\alpha)^2}{9\pi m^2} f(Z_B\alpha) \ln^2(\gamma^2)\)
- Mixed Coulomb correction: \(\sigma^c_{AB} = \frac{56(Z_A\alpha)^2(Z_B\alpha)^2}{9\pi m^2} f(Z_A\alpha) f(Z_B\alpha) \ln(\gamma^2)\)
- In an arbitrary frame, \(\gamma^2\) is replaced by \(\gamma_A\gamma_B\) in all expressions above.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier paper Lee and Milstein (2001) which first demonstrated that the cross-section formula of Severns, McLerran, and Greiner (and later McLerran) actually contains Coulomb corrections, contrary to those authors' claims. The present paper explicitly calculates those corrections for the inclusive cross section \(\sigma_T\) (defined as the sum over impact parameter of the mean number of produced pairs) and shows they agree with the Weizsacker-Williams approximation in the limit where one nucleus has small charge number. The results enable future calculations of multiple pair production in heavy-ion collisions with proper treatment of Coulomb effects.
