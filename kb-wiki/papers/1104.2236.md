# On Chiral Symmetry Restoration at Finite Density in Large N_c QCD

> At large N_c, cold nuclear matter is expected to form a crystal and thus spontaneously break translational symmetry. The description of chiral symmetry breaking and translational symmetry breaking can become intertwined. Here, the focus is on aspects of chiral symmetry breaking and its possible restoration that are by construction independent of the nature of translational symmetry breaking---namely spatial averages of chiral order parameters. A system will be considered to be chirally restored provided all spatially-averaged chiral order parameters are zero. A critical question is whether chiral restoration in this sense is possible for phases in which chiral order parameters are locally non-zero but whose spatial averages all vanish. We show that this is not possible unless all chirally-invariant observables are spatially uniform. This result is first derived for Skyrme-type models, which are based on a nonlinear sigma model and by construction break chiral symmetry on a point-by-point basis. A no-go theorem for chiral restoration (in the average sense) for all models of this type is obtained by showing that in these models there exist chirally symmetric order parameters which cannot be spatially uniform. Next we show that the no-go theorem applies to large N_c QCD in any phase which has a non-zero but spatially varying chiral condensate. The theorem is demonstrated by showing that in a putative chirally-restored phase, the field configuration can be reduced to that of a nonlinear sigma model.

## Key claim

The paper proves a no-go theorem: in large \(N_c\) QCD at finite density, it is impossible for all spatially-averaged chiral order parameters to vanish (i.e., for chiral symmetry to be restored in the spatially-averaged sense) unless all chirally-invariant observables are spatially uniform, and this uniformity is shown to be incompatible with the structure of Skyrme-type models and large \(N_c\) QCD with a non-zero but spatially varying chiral condensate.

## Method

The authors first prove the no-go theorem for Skyrme-type models (non-linear sigma models with stable topological solitons) by showing that chiral restoration in the spatially-averaged sense requires all chiral singlet observables to be spatially uniform, then constructing a specific chiral singlet that cannot be uniform under the conditions of chiral restoration. They then extend the theorem to large \(N_c\) QCD by demonstrating that in any putative chirally-restored phase with a non-zero but spatially varying chiral condensate, the field configuration reduces to that of a non-linear sigma model, making the same no-go theorem applicable. The analysis uses Wigner D-matrix expansions, Jacobian determinants, and properties of SU(2) field mappings.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) for large \(N_c\) QCD at finite density, where chiral restoration was argued based on Skyrme model studies. The paper contradicts the interpretation that vanishing of the spatially-averaged chiral condensate in Skyrme crystals (as seen in half-skyrmion configurations) implies full chiral restoration, and shows that the apparent chiral restoration on a hypersphere (studied by Krusch and others) is an artifact of that geometry. The no-go theorem is distinct from the Kogan-Kovner-Shifman (1996) argument for the vacuum, which relies on a real and positive functional determinant that does not hold at finite density. The result enables a clearer understanding of what conditions would be necessary for chiral restoration in dense large \(N_c\) matter.
