# Chiral symmety breaking in 3-flavor Nambu-Jona Lasinio model in magnetic background

> Effect of magnetic field on chiral symmetry breaking in a 3-flavor Nambu Jona Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature and densities is considered here using an explicit structure of ground state in terms of quark and antiquark condensates. While at zero chemical potential and finite temperature, magnetic field enhances the condensates, at zero temperature, the critical chemical potential decreases with increasing magnetic field.

## Key claim
In the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona Lasinio (NJL) model with a magnetic background, the critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry breaking decreases with increasing magnetic field at zero temperature, an effect termed "inverse magnetic catalysis," even though the chiral condensate itself is enhanced by the magnetic field before the transition.

## Method
The authors used a variational approach with a trial ground state expressed as a Bogoliubov transformation of quark-antiquark pairs, including Landau level quantization from a constant magnetic field in the z-direction. They then applied thermofield dynamics to incorporate finite temperature and chemical potential, and minimized the thermodynamic potential derived from the 3-flavor NJL Hamiltonian including the Kobayashi-Maskawa-t-Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction. The resulting gap equations for the constituent quark masses were solved numerically using a regularization procedure to handle ultraviolet divergences.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum masses (at T=0, mu=0, B=0): up/down quark mass = 368 MeV, strange quark mass = 549 MeV, using current quark masses m_u=m_d=5.5 MeV and m_s=140.7 MeV.
- NJL model parameters: three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 0.6023 GeV, dimensionless coupling G_s * Lambda^2 = 1.835, and K * Lambda^5 = 12.36.
- Magnetic field strengths considered: up to eB ~ 15 m_pi^2 at LHC, with m_pi^2 ~ 10^18 Gauss.

## Context
This work extends studies of magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, as discussed by Gorbar, Miransky and Shovkovy (2009) and Gusynin, Miransky and Shovkovy (1994-1996), to the 3-flavor NJL model at finite density. It directly contradicts the expectation that magnetic field always enhances chiral symmetry breaking, instead finding inverse magnetic catalysis at finite chemical potential, consistent with the holographic model results of Preis, Rebhan and Schmitt (2010). The results enable further studies of the equation of state and charge-neutral matter in strong magnetic fields, as detailed in the authors' companion paper Chatterjee, Mishra and Mishra (2011).
