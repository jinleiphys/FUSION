# Theoretical study of neutrino-induced coherent pion production off nuclei at T2K and MiniBooNE energies

> We have developed a model for neutrino-induced coherent pion production off nuclei in the energy regime of interest for present and forthcoming neutrino oscillation experiments. It is based on a microscopic model for pion production off the nucleon that, besides the dominant Delta pole contribution, takes into account the effect of background terms required by chiral symmetry. Moreover, the model uses a reduced nucleon-to-Delta resonance axial coupling, which leads to coherent pion production cross sections around a factor two smaller than most of the previous theoretical estimates. In the coherent production, the main nuclear effects, namely medium corrections on the Delta propagator and the final pion distortion, are included. We have improved on previous similar models by taking into account the nucleon motion and employing a more sophisticated optical potential. As found in previous calculations the modification of the Delta self-energy inside the nuclear medium strongly reduces the cross section, while the final pion distortion mainly shifts the peak position to lower pion energies. The angular distribution profiles are not much affected by nuclear effects. Nucleon motion increases the cross section by 15% at neutrino energies of 650 MeV, while Coulomb effects on charged pions are estimated to be small. Finally, we discuss at length the deficiencies of the Rein-Sehgal pion coherent production model for neutrino energies below 2 GeV, and in particular for the MiniBooNE and T2K experiments. We also predict flux averaged cross sections for these two latter experiments and K2K.

## Key claim

The authors' model predicts coherent pion production cross sections approximately a factor of two smaller than most previous theoretical estimates, primarily due to using a reduced nucleon-to-Delta resonance axial coupling (C5^A(0)=0.867) rather than the larger value (approximately 1.2) traditionally assumed from the Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic model for neutrino-induced coherent pion production off nuclei based on their earlier model for pion production off the nucleon, which includes both the dominant Delta(1232) pole contribution and background terms required by chiral symmetry. They incorporated nuclear medium corrections through the Delta self-energy in the nuclear medium and treated final pion distortion quantum mechanically by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a microscopic optical potential, while also accounting for nucleon motion by using a prescription where the initial and final nucleon momenta are set to (k_pi - q)/2 and -(k_pi - q)/2 respectively.

## Key numbers

- C5^A(0) = 0.867 and M_{A Delta} = 0.985 GeV for the nucleon-to-Delta axial form factor, fitted to ANL flux-averaged nu_mu p -> mu- p pi+ data
- Nucleon motion increases the cross section by approximately 15% at neutrino energies of 650 MeV
- Total cross section reduction from medium effects and pion distortion is around 60% for a beam energy of 600 MeV on oxygen
- For isoscalar nuclei, the approximate relation sigma_CC approximately equals 2 sigma_NC holds, with corrections of 25% at 1.3 GeV from finite muon mass effects

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach of Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2007) by properly including nucleon motion and correcting numerical inaccuracies in those earlier calculations, while using a more sophisticated optical potential for pion distortion than the Delta-dominance based potential used previously. The model is designed for the neutrino energy range of MiniBooNE and T2K experiments (peak energy around 0.6-0.7 GeV), where the authors demonstrate deficiencies of the Rein-Sehgal (1982) coherent pion production model below 2 GeV. The reduced axial coupling is consistent with results from lattice QCD (Alexandrou, 2006), quark model (Barquilla-Cano, 2007), and phenomenological studies (Graczyk, 2007).
