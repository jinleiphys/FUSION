# Dynamical Symmetry Breaking and Effective Lagrangians in U(n) Four-Fermion Models (n=2,3)

> The formation of scalar condensates and dynamical symmetry breaking in the U(n) four-fermion models (for n=2,3) with two coupling constants has been studied by the functional integration method. The bosonization procedures of the models under consideration are performed in one loop approximation. The propagators of fermions, collective Bose-fields (bound states of fermions), as well as the mass formulas for the fermions and bosons, are found. It is shown that a self-consistent consideration of four-fermion models, in the framework of dimension regularization, provides the mass relations for fermions for the case $n\geq 3$. The effective Lagrangian of interacting scalar bosons is also derived for the case n=2.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a self-consistent treatment of U(n) four-fermion models (n=2,3) using dimensional regularization, rather than cutoff regularization, leads to a specific mass relation for fermions in the U(3) case, which forces one of four conditions on the fermion masses (including the possibility that one mass equals the negative sum of the other two). This result is conditional on using dimensional regularization; the author notes that with momentum-cutoff regularization, the mass relation does not hold.

## Method

The author studies U(n) four-fermion models (n=2,3) with two coupling constants using the functional integration method. The models are bosonized in one-loop approximation by introducing auxiliary scalar fields, and the fermionic path integral is performed exactly. Gap equations for the vacuum condensates are derived and solved using dimensional regularization, and the propagators and mass matrices for the collective bosonic fields are computed from the effective action expanded to quadratic order.

## Key numbers

- For the U(3) group, the mass formula (equation 22) is: (m1 - m2)(m1 - m3)(m2 - m3)(m1 + m2 + m3) = 0, which forces one of four conditions on the dynamical fermion masses.
- For the U(2) group with m=0 and equal fermion masses (m1 = m2), the scalar boson mass is m00 = 2m1, identified with the sigma meson mass.
- For the U(2) group, the masses of the fields Phi1 and Phi2 vanish (Goldstone bosons), and the mass matrix elements include m33^2 = (m1 - m2)^2 and m00^2 = 3(m1^2 + m2^2) + 2(m1^3 + m2^3)/(2m - m1 - m2).
- For the U(3) group with the specific solution m3 = -m1 - m2 (corresponding to constituent quark masses m_u ≈ m_d ≈ 230 MeV, m_s ≈ 460 MeV), the boson mass m88^2 = 3(m1 + m2)^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of four-fermion models with dynamical symmetry breaking, particularly the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nambu, 1960) and its generalizations by Eguchi (1976), Klevansky (1992), and Volkov (1984). The author's previous work on four-fermion models with scalar-scalar, pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar, and vector-vector interactions (Kruglov, Acta Physica Polonica) and on CP-odd four-fermion models (Kruglov, 1989) provides the foundation. The key advance is the use of dimensional regularization, which yields a constraint relating the divergent integrals and leads to the mass formula (22) that does not appear in cutoff-regularized treatments. This enables a self-consistent calculation of quark masses and boson masses, and the derived effective Lagrangian for the U(2) case (equation 37) provides a starting point for constructing effective chiral Lagrangians that could be extended to include color symmetry and chiral symmetry for realistic meson phenomenology.
