# Shell Model Study of the Neutron-Rich Nuclei around N=28

> We describe the properties of the neutron rich nuclei around N=28 in the shell mode framework. The valence space comprises the $sd$ shell for protons an the $pf$ shell for neutrons without any restriction. Good agreement is found with the available experimental data. The N=28 shell closure, even if eroded due to the large neutron excess, persists. The calculations predict that $^{40}$S and $^{42}$S are deformed with $β=0.29 $ and $β=0.32$ respectively.

# Shell Model Study of the Neutron-Rich Nuclei around N=28

## Key claim

The N=28 shell closure persists in very neutron-rich nuclei, contrary to the collapse observed at N=20, although it is somewhat eroded by the large neutron excess; the calculations predict that ⁴⁰S and ⁴²S are deformed with β = 0.29 and β = 0.32 respectively, but this deformation appears to be very local with no large region of deformation expected.

## Method

The authors performed full unrestricted shell model calculations using a valence space consisting of the complete sd shell for protons and the complete pf shell for neutrons, with an effective interaction combining the USD interaction for sd-shell particles, a modified Kuo-Brown interaction (KB') for pf-shell particles, and the Lee-Kahanna-Scott G-matrix for cross-shell interactions, with monopole terms adjusted phenomenologically to reproduce known data on K isotopes and natural parity states. The shell model matrices were solved using the Lanczos algorithm code ANTOINE, and Gamow-Teller strength functions were computed using the Whitehead method in a larger 1p-1h space to recover the 3(N-Z) sum rule.

## Key numbers

- ⁴⁰S: spectroscopic quadrupole moment = -17.1 e²fm², intrinsic quadrupole moment Q₀ = 59.8 e²fm², deformation β = 0.29, E2 reduced transition probability B(E2) = 77.9 e²fm⁴
- ⁴²S: spectroscopic quadrupole moment = -19.2 e²fm², intrinsic quadrupole moment Q₀ = 67.2 e²fm², deformation β = 0.32, E2 reduced transition probability B(E2) = 93.0 e²fm⁴
- ⁴⁴S: a very low second 0⁺ state at 1.51 MeV, indicating possible shape coexistence
- ⁴²Si: identified as a new doubly magic nucleus based on 2⁺ excitation energy, S₂N behavior, and 1f₇/₂ occupation numbers
- ⁴⁸Cr: deformation parameter β = 0.30, used for comparison with ⁴²S (β = 0.32)
- Relativistic Mean Field calculations by Werner et al. predict β ≈ 0.25 for the same region

## Context

This work extends shell model studies of neutron-rich nuclei beyond the N=20 region, where earlier work by Poves (1994), Warburton (1990), and Fukunishi (1992) had demonstrated that 2p-2h intruder configurations explained the collapse of the N=20 shell closure in nuclei like ³²Mg. The calculations contradict mass systematics from the Audi-Wapstra compilation that would suggest constant S₂N around N=28, and instead support the persistence of the N=28 magic number. The deformation found in ⁴⁰S and ⁴²S is consistent with Relativistic Mean Field calculations by Werner et al. (1994), though the non-relativistic Skyrme calculations in that same work predicted nearly degenerate prolate and oblate solutions. The results enable future experimental studies of 2⁺ excitation energies in neutron-rich S, Cl, and Ar isotopes, as well as the single-particle spectrum of ³⁵Si, to test the predicted persistence of the N=28 shell closure.
