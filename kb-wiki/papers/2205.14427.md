# Thermal and thermoelectric responses of hot QCD medium in time-varying magnetic fields

> The thermal response of the hot QCD matter has been studied in the presence of a time-varying magnetic field. The impact of magnetic field, its time dependence, and the collision aspects of the medium on thermal transport have been studied within the relativistic kinetic theory. The decay time of the magnetic field in the medium seems to have a strong dependence on thermal conductivity. The applicability of the Wiedemann-Franz law for the QCD medium has been investigated in the presence of time-varying external electromagnetic fields. The phenomenological significance of thermal transport in heavy-ion collision experiments has also been investigated by relating the thermal conductivity to the elliptic flow through the Knudsen number. The investigations are extended to study the thermoelectric behavior of hot QCD medium and its dependence on the magnetic field. The time dependent magnetic field is observed to significantly influence the thermoelectric behavior of the medium.

## Key claim

The thermal and thermoelectric responses of the hot QCD medium are studied under the assumption of a *slowly varying* magnetic field, where the cyclotron frequency is comparable to the inverse of the magnetic field decay time, ensuring that collisional aspects cannot be neglected.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a weakly magnetized QGP, using a Chapman-Enskog-like ansatz for the non-equilibrium distribution function that includes terms proportional to the magnetic field, its time derivative, and the thermal driving force. Two specific profiles for the time-dependent magnetic field are considered: an exponential decay and a power-law decay. The resulting heat current is decomposed into isotropic, Hall-like, and time-decay-induced components, and the thermoelectric coefficients (Seebeck and Nernst) are derived from the steady-state condition of zero net current.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength at the early stages of heavy-ion collisions is expected to be in the order of \(10^{18}-10^{19}\) G.
- The analysis uses a magnetic field strength of \(eB_0 = 0.08\) GeV\(^2\) and a chemical potential \(\mu = 100\) MeV for quantitative estimates.
- The Knudsen number is calculated using a system size \(l = 1\) fm and relative speed \(v \approx 1\).
- The elliptic flow \(v_2\) is modeled with \(v_2^h = 0.3 \pm 0.02\) and \(Kn_0 = 0.7\).
- The magnetic field decay parameter \(\tau_B\) is varied among values of 2, 3, and 6 fm in the analysis of the Knudsen number and elliptic flow.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermal transport in the QCD medium, which had mostly considered constant magnetic fields (Das 2019) or no magnetic field (Bhalerao 2005). It also builds on recent investigations of thermoelectric effects in QGP (Bhatt 2018, Das 2020, Zhang 2020, Dey 2021, Kurian 2021) by incorporating a time-evolving magnetic field. The results enable more realistic modeling of the QGP evolution in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for lower-energy experiments at RHIC beam energy scan and FAIR where baryon chemical potential is significant.
