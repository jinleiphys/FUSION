# Mean-field analysis of ground state and low-lying electric dipole strength in $^{22}$C

> Properties of neutron-rich $^{22}$C are studied using the mean-field approach with Skyrme energy density functionals. Its weak binding and large total reaction cross section, which are suggested by recent experiments, are simulated by modifying the central part of Skyrme potential. Calculating $E1$ strength distribution by using the random-phase approximation, we investigate developments of low-lying electric dipole ($E1$) strength and a contribution of core excitations of $^{20}$C. As the neutron Fermi level approaches the zero energy threshold ($\varepsilon_F >\sim -1$ MeV), we find that the low-lying $E1$ strength exceeds the energy-weighted cluster sum rule, which indicates an importance of the core excitations with the $1d_{5/2}$ orbit.

## Key claim

The low-lying electric dipole (E1) strength in $^{22}$C can exceed the energy-weighted cluster sum rule when the neutron Fermi level approaches zero energy ($\varepsilon_F \gtrsim -1$ MeV), indicating that core excitations from the $1d_{5/2}$ orbit contribute significantly and the simple three-body model with an inert $^{20}$C core is not fully supported.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with Skyrme energy density functionals to obtain the ground state of $^{22}$C, then used the random-phase approximation (RPA) with the finite amplitude method to calculate the E1 strength distribution. To reproduce the experimentally suggested halo structure, they modified the central part of the Skyrme potential by multiplying the $t_0$ parameter by a factor $f_0$, and tested the resulting densities by calculating total reaction cross sections using the Glauber model in the optical limit approximation and nucleon-target formalism.

## Key numbers

- The experimental matter radius from Tanaka et al. (2010) is $5.4 \pm 0.9$ fm, while original Skyrme interactions give rms radii in the range $3.03 - 3.34$ fm
- The modified SIII interaction with $f_0 = 0.884$ yields $\varepsilon_F = -0.50$ MeV, a matter radius of 3.89 fm, and a $2s_{1/2}$ orbit rms radius of 7.20 fm
- The calculated total reaction cross section on a proton target at 40 MeV with the modified SIII interaction is 1040 mb, compared to the experimental value $1338 \pm 274$ mb
- With $\varepsilon_F = -0.50$ MeV, the summed E1 strengths up to 3.0, 4.0, and 5.0 MeV are 1.38, 1.65, and 1.85 $e^2$fm$^2$, respectively
- The low-lying E1 strength exhausts 6.2, 11.0, and 15.4 percent of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule when accumulated up to 5, 8, and 10 MeV, respectively
- The cumulative energy-weighted sum exceeds the cluster sum rule $S_\mathrm{clus}$ at $E_c = 3.3$ MeV for $\varepsilon_F = -0.50$ MeV, and at $E_c = 3.9$ MeV and 5.0 MeV for $\varepsilon_F = -0.70$ MeV and $-1.0$ MeV, respectively

## Context

This work extends previous three-body model studies of $^{22}$C by Horiuchi and Suzuki (2006) and Ershov (2012), which assumed an inert $^{20}$C core, by using a mean-field approach that allows core excitations. The authors find that the $1d_{5/2}$ orbit contributes significantly to the low-lying E1 strength, contradicting the assumption of a frozen core. The results enable future studies of whether such enhanced low-lying dipole strength is universal in neutron drip-line nuclei, and call for constructing new Skyrme parameter sets suitable for very neutron-rich nuclei.
