# Halo Structure Of C-19 Via The (p,d) Reaction

> We have investigated the heaviest one-neutron halo candidate C-19 nucleus. Few-body model calculations of cross section angular distributions for the C-19(p,d)C-18 reaction, together with the test calculations carried out for the C-17(p,d)C-16 reaction, at a low incident energy are presented for different possible halo-neutron configurations. We show that there is a clear distinction between in particular $\ell_{n}=0$ and $\ell_{n}=2$ halo transfers. The sensitivity of the cross sections to the assumed C-19 single neutron separation energy is discussed.

# Halo Structure Of C-19 Via The (p,d) Reaction

## Key claim

The authors show that measuring the angular distribution of the C-19(p,d)C-18 reaction at 30 MeV/nucleon out to at least 15 degrees in the center-of-mass frame can distinguish between l_n=0 and l_n=2 neutron transfer, but only if the valence neutron is in a pure single-particle state; the ground state spin-parity and neutron separation energy of C-19 remain uncertain, and the required beam intensity is currently out of reach.

## Method

The authors performed few-body model calculations of the C-19(p,d)C-18 and C-17(p,d)C-16 reactions using the Adiabatic (AD) model, the Quasi-adiabatic (QAD) approach, and standard Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) within a three-body (halo neutron + proton + core nucleus) framework. The transfer amplitude was calculated using the prior form of the (p,d) matrix element with the n-p interaction as the transition interaction, employing Woods-Saxon binding potentials with radius 1.25 fm and diffuseness 0.65 fm, and optical potentials from the Bechetti-Greenless global parameterization. Calculations were performed in zero-range approximation using a modified version of the program TWOFNR, with radial integrals from 0 to 40 fm in steps of 0.1 fm and a maximum of 30 partial waves.

## Key numbers

- C-19 one-neutron separation energy S_n: 240 keV (world average) and 530 keV (indirect determination from Riken Coulomb dissociation data)
- C-17 one-neutron separation energy S_n: approximately 730 keV
- Excitation energy of the 2+ state of C-18 core: 1.62 MeV
- Binding energy for configuration (b) (1s_{1/2} neutron bound to 2+ C-18 core): 1.86 MeV
- Incident energy for all calculations: 30 MeV/nucleon
- Angular range required to distinguish l_n=0 from l_n=2 transfer: at least theta_cm = 15 degrees
- Radial integration range: 0 to 40 fm in steps of 0.1 fm
- Maximum number of partial waves: 30

## Context

This work extends the study of one-neutron halo nuclei beyond the only established case, Be-11, to the candidate C-19, which has drawn attention from experiments at MSU (Bazin et al.), Riken (Nakamura et al.), and Ganil (Liegard et al.). The authors address the unresolved debate between a 1/2+ ground state (favoring halo formation) versus 5/2+ or 3/2+ possibilities from shell model predictions by Warburton et al. and Ridikas et al. The calculations enable future experimental design for inverse-kinematics (p,d) reactions with radioactive beams, which could resolve the ground state structure of C-19 when sufficient beam intensities become available.
