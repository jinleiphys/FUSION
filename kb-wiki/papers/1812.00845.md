# Single-particle structure of the N = 20 and N = 28 isotones within the dispersive optical model

> The neutron single-particle characteristics of the N = 20, 28 isotones at 8 < Z < 30 was calculated within the dispersive optical model. The global parameters of the spin-orbit and imaginary parts of the potential as well as surface absorption independent on neutron-proton asymmetry and increased diffuseness at large neutron excess were used in the calculations. The suitability of the global parameters to predict the evolution of the neutron single-particle structure of nuclei near the neutron drip line was investigated. The following results are in agreement with the available experimental data: the reduction of the particle-hole energy gaps, the degeneration of the 1f7/2 and 2p states and then a change in the 1f7/2 , 2p3/2 level sequence and more rapid reduction of the 2p-splitting in comparison with the 1f-splitting with decreasing Z. The predictive power of the dispersive optical model with respect to neutron-rich nuclei is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The dispersive optical model (DOM), using global parameters for the spin-orbit and imaginary parts of the potential along with surface absorption independent of neutron-proton asymmetry and increased diffuseness at large neutron excess, can predict the evolution of neutron single-particle structure in N=20 and N=28 isotones near the neutron drip line, with the caveat that the model does not explicitly account for tensor forces but captures their effects through dispersive components.

## Method

The authors constructed a spherical dispersive optical-model potential (DOMP) using global parameters KD for the imaginary and spin-orbit parts, with the Hartree-Fock type potential depth fitted to the Fermi energy. Single-particle energies were calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation with the real part of the DOMP, and occupation probabilities were evaluated using BCS theory with pairing gap parameters derived from separation energies. The calculations were performed for N=20 isotones with 8 < Z < 30 and N=28 isotones with 8 < Z < 30, using the AME16 data and HFB-24 mass formula for separation energies where experimental data were missing.

## Key numbers

- The calculated N=28 gap between 1f7/2 and 2p3/2 levels reduced by 3.4 MeV from 48Ca to 42Si
- The difference in separation energies [Sn(48Ca)-Sn(49Ca)] - [Sn(42Si)-Sn(43Si)] = 2.7 MeV (from AME16 data)
- The low experimental excitation energy E(2+1) = 742(8) keV for 42Si
- The N=20 gap between 1d3/2 and 2p3/2 states in 32Mg decreased by 1.3 MeV when diffuseness aHF = as increased from 0.67 to 0.8 fm
- Between 36S and 34Si (N=20 isotones), calculated 2p-splitting decreased by 16% and 1f-splitting decreased by 4.9%
- The 2p3/2 state of 32Mg shows Rrms almost 2 times larger than the 1d3/2 state

## Context

This work extends the dispersive optical model developed by Mahaux and Sartor (1991) to predict single-particle structure in neutron-rich N=20 and N=28 isotones, building on previous DOM applications to stable nuclei with mass number 40 ≤ A ≤ 208. The results connect to earlier theoretical studies of shell evolution including nonrelativistic and relativistic mean-field models, energy density functionals, and shell model calculations (Sorlin 2008 and references therein), as well as experimental observations of the N=28 shell closure weakening in 42Si (Takeuchi et al. 2012) and the N=20 "island of inversion" (Warburton 1990). The findings enable predictions of single-particle characteristics for nuclei far from stability and highlight the need for experimental data on surface absorption dependence on neutron-proton asymmetry at large neutron excess.
