# Constituent quark scaling violation due to baryon number transport

> In ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at $\roots\approx200$ GeV, the azimuthal emission anisotropy of hadrons with low and intermediate transverse momentum ($p_T\lesssim 4$ GeV/c) displays an intriguing scaling. In particular, the baryon (meson) emission patterns are consistent with a scenario in which a bulk medium of flowing quarks coalesces into three-quark (two-quark) "bags." While a full understanding of this number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling remains elusive, it is suggestive of a thermalized bulk system characterized by colored dynamical degrees of freedom-- a quark-gluon plasma (QGP). In this scenario, one expects the scaling to break down as the central energy density is reduced below the QGP formation threshold; for this reason, NCQ-scaling violation searches are of interest in the energy scan program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). However, as $\roots$ is reduced, it is not only the initial energy density that changes; there is also an increase in the net baryon number at midrapidity, as stopping transports entrance-channel partons to midrapidity. This phenomenon can result in violations of simple NCQ scaling. Still in the context of the quark coalescence model, we describe a specific pattern for the break-down of the scaling that includes different flow strengths for particles and their anti-partners. Related complications in the search for recently suggested exotic phenomena are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that violation of number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling in low-energy heavy ion collisions does not necessarily signal the absence of a deconfined quark-gluon plasma or the failure of the quark coalescence model; instead, such violations can arise from a less exotic mechanism where baryon number transport from beam rapidity to midrapidity creates two populations of quarks with different flow strengths, even if coalescence remains the hadronization mechanism.

## Method

The authors develop a two-component model (NCQ$_2$) in which transported $u$ and $d$ quarks (those brought from beam rapidity to midrapidity via baryon stopping) are assigned a stronger elliptic flow ($v_2$) than produced quarks (including $u$, $d$, $s$ quarks and all antiquarks created at midrapidity). They estimate the fraction of transported light quarks at midrapidity using measured hadron yields from the NA49/SPS Collaboration at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.41$ and $8.86$ GeV, then calculate hadron $v_2$ via the coalescence formula $v_2^h(p_T) = \sum_{i=1}^n [ X_{q_i^T} v_2^{q_i^T}(p_T/n) + (1-X_{q_i^T}) v_2^{q_i^P}(p_T/n) ]$, where $n=2$ for mesons and $n=3$ for baryons.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.41$ GeV, the fraction of $u$ quarks at midrapidity originating from stopping is $X_{u^T}=0.57$, and for $d$ quarks $X_{d^T}=0.63$.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.86$ GeV, the fraction of $u$ quarks at midrapidity originating from stopping is $X_{u^T}=0.50$, and for $d$ quarks $X_{d^T}=0.55$.
- For the illustrative model calculation, transported quarks are assigned $v_2(p_T)=0.07\tanh(p_T/(0.5~{\rm GeV/c}))$ and produced quarks $v_2(p_T)=0.05\tanh(p_T/(0.5~{\rm GeV/c}))$, representing a 40% stronger intrinsic flow for transported quarks.
- The rapidity loss parameter increases from approximately 1 unit at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 5$ GeV to 1.7 units at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 17$ GeV, with a smaller rise toward 2 units at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the NCQ scaling paradigm established at top RHIC energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV), where all quarks are assumed to have the same flow (NCQ$_1$ model), to lower collision energies where baryon stopping becomes significant. It provides a baseline interpretation for NCQ scaling violations observed in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (Aggarwal 2010), offering an alternative to exotic explanations such as chiral magnetic waves (Burnier 2011). The model also connects to earlier work by Lin and Ko (2002) on species-dependent quark flow and to hydrodynamic failures at low energies noted by Alt (2003). The predicted pattern of particle-antiparticle flow differences (e.g., $v_2[\pi^-] > v_2[\pi^+]$ but $v_2[K^+] > v_2[K^-]$) provides testable distinctions from chiral magnetic wave predictions.
