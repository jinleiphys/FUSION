# Charged pion condensation under parallel electromagnetic fields

> The formation of charged pion condensate under parallel electromagnetic fields is studied within the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The technique of Schwinger proper time method is extended to explore the quantity locating in the off-diagonal flavor space, i.e., charged pion. We obtain the associated effective potential as a function of the strength of the electromagnetic fields and find out that it contains a sextic term which possibly induce weakly first order phase transition. Dependence of pion condensation on model parameters is investigated.

## Key claim

Charged pion condensation can occur under parallel electromagnetic fields in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, but only when the current quark mass is sufficiently small; for physical current quark masses the condensation may not appear, depending on the model parameter set.

## Method

The authors extended the Schwinger proper time method to compute the effective potential for the charged pion condensate in the off-diagonal flavor space of the SU(2) x SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. They worked at zero temperature and zero chemical potential, with the electric field anti-parallel to the magnetic field (so that the first Lorentz invariant vanishes while the second is nonzero). The effective potential was evaluated numerically using proper time regularization with five parameter sets taken from Inagaki (2015), and the integration contour was shifted into the complex plane to handle singularities from trigonometric functions.

## Key numbers

* For parameter Set 5 (constituent quark mass 372 MeV), at small field f = 0.01 GeV^2 the system is in the usual chiral symmetry breaking phase with zero pion condensate.
* For parameter Set 5, at moderate field f = 0.2 GeV^2 an additional minimum appears in the effective potential, giving nonzero pion condensate.
* For parameter Set 5, at large field f = 0.450 GeV^2 the minimum with zero pion condensate is energetically favorable.
* For parameter Set 1 (constituent quark mass 178 MeV), the phase of pion condensation never appears at the physical current quark mass m_0 = 3.0 MeV.
* The constituent quark masses for the five parameter sets range from 178 MeV (Set 1) to 372 MeV (Set 5).

## Context

This work extends the study of pion condensation under parallel electromagnetic fields from neutral pion condensation, previously investigated by Cao (2015) using chiral perturbation theory and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, to the charged pion channel. The authors find that charged pion condensation exhibits a weakly first order phase transition as field strength increases, in contrast to the second order transition found for neutral pion condensation by Cao (2015). The results depend sensitively on the current quark mass and model parameters, and the authors note that further study via first-principle methods such as Dyson-Schwinger equations or functional renormalization group is needed.
