# Λ^{\ast}-hypernuclei in phenomenological nuclear forces

> The $Λ^{\ast}$-hypernuclei, which are bound states of $Λ(1405)$ and nuclei, are discussed as a possible interpretation of the $\bar{K}$-nuclei. The Bonn and Nijmegen potentials are extended and used as a phenomenological potential between $Λ^{\ast}$ and $N$. The $K$-exchange potential is also considered in the $Λ^{\ast}$ and $N$ interaction. The two-body ($Λ^{\ast}N$) and three-body ($Λ^{\ast}NN$) systems are solved by a variational method. It is shown that the spin and isospin of the ground states are assigned as $Λ^{\ast}N(S=1, I=1/2)$ and $Λ^{\ast}NN(S=3/2, I=0)$, respectively. The binding energies of the $Λ^{\ast}$-hypernuclei are discussed in comparison with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the so-called kaon-nuclear bound states, such as the \(ppK^{-}\) state reported by the FINUDA collaboration, can be reinterpreted as bound states of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) baryon (\(\Lambda^{\ast}\)) with nucleons, forming \(\Lambda^{\ast}\)-hypernuclei. This interpretation hinges on treating the \(\Lambda^{\ast} \Lambda^{\ast} \sigma\) coupling constant as a free parameter, with the binding energy of the \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\) system reaching 88 MeV at a specific coupling ratio, rather than directly reproducing the 115 MeV binding energy reported for \(ppK^{-}\).

## Method

The authors extended the Bonn and Nijmegen (SC89 and ESC04) one-boson-exchange potentials, originally developed for \(NN\) interactions, to the \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\) system by including \(\sigma\), \(\eta\), \(\omega\), and \(K\)-exchange terms. The \(K\)-exchange was treated with an effective kaon mass of 171 MeV/\(c^{2}\) to account for the near-threshold kinematics of \(\Lambda^{\ast}\). They solved the two-body (\(\Lambda^{\ast}N\)) and three-body (\(\Lambda^{\ast}NN\)) Schrödinger equations using a variational method with Gaussian trial wave functions, treating the \(g_{\Lambda^{\ast}\Lambda^{\ast}\sigma}\) coupling as a free parameter while fixing others from \(SU(3)\) relations or the \(NN\) interaction.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\bar{K}\) coupling constant is \(g^{2}_{\Lambda^{\ast} N K}/4\pi = 0.064\), derived from the \(\Lambda^{\ast} \rightarrow \Sigma \pi\) decay width of \(50.0 \pm 2.0\) MeV.
- The effective kaon mass used in the \(K\)-exchange potential is \(\tilde{m}_{K} = 171\) MeV/\(c^{2}\), obtained from \(\sqrt{m_K^{2} - (M_{\Lambda^{\ast}} - M_N)^{2}}\).
- For the Bonn potential with the \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\) system in the \(S=1\) channel, a binding energy of 88 MeV is obtained at a coupling ratio \(g_{\Lambda^{\ast}\Lambda^{\ast}\sigma}/g_{NN\sigma} = 0.39\).
- For the Bonn potential, the minimum coupling ratio required to bind the \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\) system is \(g_{\Lambda^{\ast}\Lambda^{\ast}\sigma}/g_{NN\sigma} \gtap 0.37\) for \(S=1\).
- For the Bonn potential, the \(\Lambda^{\ast}NN\) three-body system in the \((S=3/2, I=0)\) state requires a coupling ratio \(g_{\Lambda^{\ast}\Lambda^{\ast}\sigma}/g_{NN\sigma} \gtap 0.406\) to form a bound state.
- For the Nijmegen SC89 potential, the binding energy of 88 MeV for \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\) is reproduced at \(g_{\Lambda^{\ast}\Lambda^{\ast}\sigma}/g_{NN\sigma} = 0.832\) for \(S=1\) and \(1.128\) for \(S=0\).
- For the Nijmegen ESC04 potential, the binding energy of 88 MeV for \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\) is reproduced at \(g_{\Lambda^{\ast}\Lambda^{\ast}\sigma}/g_{NN\sigma} = 1.118\) for \(S=1\) and \(1.398\) for \(S=0\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological one-boson-exchange framework, previously applied to \(NN\) and hyperon-\(N\) systems, to the \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\) interaction, offering an alternative to the kaon-nuclear bound state interpretation of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and Dote et al. (2004). The authors find that, unlike the \(\bar{K}\)-nucleus picture where binding increases with baryon number, the \(\Lambda^{\ast}N\) two-body state is bound more easily than the \(\Lambda^{\ast}NN\) three-body state, suggesting \(\Lambda^{\ast}\)-hypernuclei may become unbound for larger systems. This work enables future studies of in-medium decay widths of \(\Lambda^{\ast}\) in compact bound states, relevant to experiments at DA\(\Phi\)NE and J-PARC.
