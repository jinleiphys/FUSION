# Hydrodynamics of holographic flavored plasmas

> We provide a short review of the hydrodynamical properties of a class of strongly coupled thermal gauge theories, coupled to massless fundamental flavor fields, having a dual supergravity description. The analysis is valid for more general classes of nearly conformal holographic plasmas, where the breaking of conformality is driven by marginally (ir)relevant deformations.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the hydrodynamical properties of strongly coupled thermal gauge theories with massless fundamental flavor fields, using holographic dual supergravity descriptions. The analysis is valid for nearly conformal holographic plasmas where conformality breaking is driven by marginally (ir)relevant deformations, and the results are obtained perturbatively in the parameter \(\epsilon_h \propto \lambda_h N_f/N_c\), which must be taken very small for the gravity description to be reliable.

## Method

The authors apply the "smearing technique" to include backreacting flavor D7-branes in the thermal \({\cal N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) plasma, working in the Veneziano limit where \(N_c\to\infty\) and \(N_f\to\infty\) with their ratio fixed. The gravity solution is found analytically to order \(\epsilon_h^2\) in a perturbative expansion, and the transport coefficients are extracted by studying gravitational fluctuations and retarded correlators of the stress-energy tensor. The effective 5-dimensional description reduces to a Chamblin-Reall single-scalar model at order \(\epsilon_h^2\), allowing all second-order transport coefficients to be obtained using results from Kanitscheider (2009).

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) (universal for all plasmas in the class)
- Bulk viscosity to shear viscosity ratio: \(\zeta/\eta = \frac{1}{9}\epsilon_h^2\) for the flavored \({\cal N}=4\) SYM plasma
- Speed of sound squared: \(c_s^2 = \frac{1}{3}\left[1 - \frac{1}{6}\epsilon_h^2\right]\) for the flavored plasma
- Entropy density: \(s = \frac{1}{2}\pi^2 N_c^2 T^3 \left[1 + \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_h + \frac{7}{24}\epsilon_h^2\right]\)
- Free energy density: \(f = -p = -\frac{1}{8}\pi^2 N_c^2 T^4 \left[1 + \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_h + \frac{1}{6}\epsilon_h^2\right]\)
- Conformality violation measure: \(\varepsilon - 3p = \frac{1}{16}\pi^2 N_c^2 T^4 \epsilon_h^2\)
- Shear relaxation time at leading order: \(\tau_{\pi,0}T_0 = (2 - \log 2)/(2\pi)\)
- Effective relaxation time for flavored plasma: \(\tau^{eff}T = \tau_{\pi,0}T_0 + \frac{16 - \pi^2}{128\pi}\epsilon_h^2\)
- At \(T \sim 1.5 T_c\) with \(c_s^2 \sim 0.26\) (QGP estimate): \(\zeta/\eta = 0.147\), \(T\tau_\pi = 0.228\), \(T\tau_\Pi = 0.208\)

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of transport coefficients from the conformal \({\cal N}=4\) SYM plasma (where \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) was established by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001)) to non-conformal plasmas with dynamical fundamental matter, using the smearing technique introduced by Bigazzi et al. (2009). The results enable numerical simulations of the hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at RHIC and LHC temperatures (\(1.5 T_c \leq T \leq 4 T_c\)), where lattice studies suggest the QGP behaves as a nearly conformal system. The paper provides explicit numerical values for transport coefficients at \(T \sim 1.5 T_c\) that could serve as benchmarks for realistic QGP simulations.
