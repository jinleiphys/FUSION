# Nuclear Parity-Violation in Effective Field Theory

> We reformulate the analysis of nuclear parity-violation (PV) within the framework of effective field theory (EFT). To order Q, the PV nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction depends on five a priori unknown constants that parameterize the leading-order, short-range four-nucleon operators. When pions are included as explicit degrees of freedom, the potential contains additional medium- and long-range components parameterized by PV piNN couplings. We derive the form of the corresponding one- and two-pion-exchange potentials. We apply these considerations to a set of existing and prospective PV few-body measurements that may be used to determine the five independent low-energy constants relevant to the pionless EFT and the additional constants associated with dynamical pions. We also discuss the relationship between the conventional meson-exchange framework and the EFT formulation, and argue that the latter provides a more general and systematic basis for analyzing nuclear PV.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates nuclear parity-violation (PV) using effective field theory (EFT), arguing that the conventional meson-exchange (DDH) framework is a model-dependent truncation, while the EFT approach provides a more general and systematic basis; the analysis is restricted to low-energy processes (center-of-mass energy less than about 50 MeV) where the hybrid approach of using phenomenological strong-interaction wave functions with EFT-organized PV operators is justified.

## Method

The authors construct the most general parity-violating nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential in two versions of EFT: a pionless theory where all interactions are short-ranged, and a pionful theory where pions are explicit degrees of freedom. They derive the form of the one-pion-exchange (OPE) and two-pion-exchange (TPE) potentials, identify the independent low-energy constants (LECs) at leading order (order Q), and relate the EFT operators to the conventional DDH meson-exchange parameters. They then outline how a set of existing and prospective few-body PV measurements could be used to determine the five LECs of the pionless EFT or the eight LECs of the pionful EFT.

## Key numbers

* The pionless EFT at leading order depends on five independent low-energy constants (LECs) that parameterize the short-range PV NN interaction.
* The pionful EFT through order Q depends on eight a priori unknown constants: five combinations of short-range constants plus the PV piNN coupling h_piNN^1, the NNLO PV piNN coupling k_piNN^{1a}, and the PV NNpi-gamma constant C_bar_pi.
* The strong piNN coupling is expressed via the Goldberger-Treiman relation as g_piNN = g_A m_N / F_pi, with F_pi = 92.4 MeV.
* The DDH "best value" for h_piNN^1 is +12 in units of g_pi = 3.8 x 10^{-8}, with a reasonable range of 0 to 30 in the same units.
* The measured photon asymmetry in the decay of polarized isomeric state of ^{180}Hf is A_gamma = -(1.66 +/- 0.18) x 10^{-2}.
* The measured asymmetry in longitudinally-polarized neutron scattering on ^{139}La is A_z = (9.55 +/- 0.35) x 10^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends the application of EFT methods, previously used for parity-conserving nuclear forces (Weinberg, 1990; van Kolck, 1994; Epelbaum et al., 2000), to the parity-violating sector, and provides a systematic alternative to the DDH meson-exchange framework (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1980). The EFT approach enables a model-independent parameterization of PV observables in few-body systems, where ab initio computations are possible, and could help resolve inconsistencies in the values of DDH couplings extracted from experiments such as pp scattering, the gamma decay of ^{18}F, and the cesium anapole moment.
