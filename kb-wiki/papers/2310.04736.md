# Candidate toroidal electric dipole mode in the spherical nucleus $^{58}$Ni

> Dipole toroidal modes appear in many fields of physics. In nuclei, such a mode was predicted more than 50 years ago, but clear experimental evidence was lacking so far. Using a combination of high-resolution inelastic scattering experiments with photons, electrons and protons, we identify for the first time candidates for toroidal dipole excitations in the nucleus $^{58}$Ni and demonstrate that transverse electron scattering form factors represent a relevant experimental observable to prove their nature.

## Key claim

The paper identifies, for the first time, candidate toroidal electric dipole excitations in the spherical nucleus \(^{58}\)Ni, but the claim is hedged: the identification is based on a combined analysis of multiple scattering experiments and QRPA calculations, and the authors state that transverse electron scattering form factors represent a "relevant experimental observable" to prove their nature, not that the nature is definitively proven.

## Method

The authors combined high-resolution inelastic scattering experiments with photons (\((\gamma,\gamma^\prime)\)), electrons (\((e,e^\prime)\)), and protons (\((p,p^\prime)\)) on \(^{58}\)Ni, using kinematics that favor dipole excitations and allowing separation of \(E1\) and \(M1\) transitions. The experimental data were compared with fully self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations within nuclear density-functional theory using the Skyrme functional SV-mas10, which predicts the toroidal, compression, and isovector dipole strengths and the corresponding electron scattering form factors.

## Key numbers

- Three low-energy dipole states predicted by QRPA at 6.18, 8.26, and 8.95 MeV have considerable toroidal strength; their energies match experimental states at 6.03, 8.24, and 8.87 MeV seen in an \((\alpha,\alpha^\prime\gamma)\) experiment.
- The \((e,e^\prime)\) experiment was performed at an incident electron energy \(E_i \simeq 50\) MeV.
- The transverse form factor for the toroidal candidate at 8.240 MeV shows a strong slope in \(\sigma/\sigma_\mathrm{Mott}\) toward larger scattering angles, in contrast to the GDR state at 18.6 MeV which does not show this behavior.
- At momentum transfers \(q_\mathrm{eff} \leq 0.6\) fm\(^{-1}\), the relative sign of Coulomb and transverse form factors \(\varPi = \mathrm{sign}[F_{E1}^C / F_{E1}^T]\) is \(+1\) for toroidal states and \(-1\) for both GDR and compression mode.

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions of the toroidal dipole resonance (TDR) dating back more than 50 years, which had lacked clear experimental evidence despite attempts using inelastic \(\alpha\)-scattering (Uchida et al., 2004) and other reactions. The paper builds on prior QRPA predictions of toroidal states in light nuclei (Nesterenko et al., 2018) and in \(^{208}\)Pb (Repko et al., 2013), and uses a unique set of high-resolution data from Darmstadt and Osaka. The results suggest that the low-energy toroidal strength, which dominates over compression strength in \(^{58}\)Ni, may be a generic feature across the nuclear chart, and the authors propose that the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich nuclei may have a toroidal nature, a question that could be resolved by future \((e,e^\prime\gamma)\) measurements at the S-DALINAC facility.
