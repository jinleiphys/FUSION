# A Parton-Hadron Cascade Approach in High-energy Nuclear Collisions

> A parton-hadron cascade model which is the extension of hadronic cascade model incorporating hard partonic scattering based on HIJING is presented to describe the space-time evolution of parton/hadron system produced by ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. Hadron yield, baryon stopping and transverse momentum distribution are calculated and compared with HIJING and VNI.

## Key claim
The Parton-Hadron Cascade (PHC) model extends a hadronic cascade by incorporating hard partonic scattering from HIJING, but the abstract glosses over the condition that at SPS energies (200 AGeV) the model effectively reduces to a pure hadronic cascade because the probability for hard scattering is very small (average number of hard scattering ~0.1/event in S+S collisions).

## Method
The PHC model uses HIJING formalism to determine hard and soft processes, with PYTHIA5.7 generating hard scattering and initial/final state radiations. On-shell partons from hard scattering are propagated in space-time, while soft hadron interactions use string excitation with DPM-type functions above c.m. energy 5 GeV and FRITIOF-type 1/x distribution below. Hadronization uses the Lund fragmentation subroutine PYSTRF of PYTHIA6.1, and low-energy baryon-baryon, baryon-meson, and meson-meson rescattering are included via resonance excitation, with constituent quarks scattering off hadrons using additive quark cross sections within a formation time.

## Key numbers
- For S+S collision at 200 AGeV/c with 20% centrality: PHC improves HIJING results for net proton and negative charged particle (π⁻, K⁻, p̄) rapidity and transverse momentum distributions, matching NA35 experimental data.
- At SPS energies (200 AGeV), the average number of hard scattering is ~0.1/event in S+S collisions when using a cut-off parameter p⊥ = 2 GeV.
- For S+S collision at RHIC energy (b = 0.0 fm): PHC charged particle yield is similar to HIJING, but the transverse momentum distribution becomes close to the parton cascade model (VNI) prediction.
- PHC predicts no baryon-free region at midrapidity for S+S at RHIC energy, similar to RQMD and UrQMD calculations, while VNI predicts transparency.

## Context
This work extends hadronic transport models such as RQMD (Sorge, 1992), ARC, QGSM, and UrQMD by incorporating hard processes from HIJING (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) to study ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC/LHC energies. The model addresses discrepancies between HIJING and VNI predictions for AA collisions, particularly in absolute particle yield and transverse momentum slope. The results enable future investigation of parton rescattering effects on thermalization and equilibration, and may help resolve model discrepancies in baryon stopping predictions.
