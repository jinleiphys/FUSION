# DSEs and pseudoscalar mesons: an aperc,u

> An hallmark of present-day Dyson-Schwinger equation applications in hadron physics is the existence of a systematic and symmetry preserving truncation scheme. This enables the proof of exact results; e.g., the leptonic decay constant of every pseudoscalar meson except the pion vanishes in the chiral limit. Calculations using the scheme's leading-order truncation are reliable in the vector and flavour nonsinglet pseudoscalar channels. In this rainbow-ladder truncation, an impulse approximation provides the consistent current for all six-point quark Schwinger functions. That is well illustrated via the anomalous process pi0 -> gamma gamma. Using two-, three- and four-point Schwinger functions calculated in rainbow-ladder truncation, the textbook value of the width is obtained algebraically and independent of model details if, and only if, the impulse approximation is used to describe the associated matrix element.

# DSEs and pseudoscalar mesons: an aperçu

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) systematic truncation scheme yields the textbook value of the π⁰ → γγ decay width algebraically and independent of model details, but only if the impulse approximation is used to describe the associated six-point quark Schwinger function; seagull terms are absent at this truncation order.

## Method

The authors employ the renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation, the leading-order term in a systematic, symmetry-preserving DSE truncation scheme, to solve the quark gap equation and the Bethe-Salpeter equations for pseudoscalar mesons. They compute the axial-vector–vector–vector (AVV) vertex via the impulse approximation using dressed quark propagators from the rainbow gap equation and dressed vertices from the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation, regularised with a Pauli-Villars scheme. The divergence of the AVV vertex is then evaluated in the chiral limit near the pion pole to extract the π⁰ → γγ amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The integrated infrared strength of the rainbow-ladder kernel defines a dressed-glue mass-scale: m_g = 720 MeV.
- Using the Maris-Tandy model, rainbow-ladder truncation yields f_{π₀} = 0.092 GeV, m_{π₀} = 0.14 GeV, ρ_{π₀} = (0.81 GeV)² at current-quark mass m_u(1 GeV) = m_d(1 GeV) = 5.5 MeV.
- For the first excited pseudoscalar meson (n=1), retaining only the E_{π₁} amplitude gives m_{π₁} = 1.1 GeV (cf. experimental m_{π₁}^{expt.} = 1.3 ± 0.1 GeV) and f_{π₁} ≤ 1.5 MeV at physical current-quark mass.
- In the chiral limit, the leptonic decay constant vanishes for every pseudoscalar meson except the ground state pion: f⁰_{π_{n≠0}} = 0.
- The π⁰ → γγ amplitude obtained is A^{π⁰→γγ}_{μν} = i (α/π f_π) ε_{μνρσ} k_{1ρ} k_{2σ}, yielding the textbook width.

## Context

This work extends the systematic DSE truncation scheme developed by Bender (1996) and Detmold (2003) to demonstrate that the rainbow-ladder truncation, combined with the impulse approximation, reproduces the π⁰ → γγ anomaly exactly and independently of model details. The result explains the numerical findings of Maris and Tandy (2002) and confirms that seagull terms identified in earlier work (Banff, 2002) are absent at this truncation order. The paper also reports ongoing rainbow-ladder calculations of excited pseudoscalar mesons (Krassnigg, 2003; Roberts, 2003) and notes a discrepancy between DSE calculations of the pion valence quark distribution, which agree with perturbative QCD predictions of u_v^π(x) ∝ (1-x)², and existing experimental data.
