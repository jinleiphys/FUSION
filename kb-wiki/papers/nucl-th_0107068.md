# Single particle properties of Lambda hypernuclei in the density dependent relativistic hadron field theory

> The density dependent relativistic hadron field theory is used to describe single particle properties of Lambda hypernuclei. The discussion focuses on the spin-orbit systematics in the relativistic mean-field formalism by discussing general effects of the Lambda continuum threshold and the delocalization of the Lambda wave function. Theoretical predictions for the hypernuclear Auger effect are presented.

## Key claim

The density dependent relativistic hadron field theory (DDRH) can describe single particle properties of Lambda hypernuclei, but the extraction of generic hyperon spin-orbit interactions from weakly bound Lambda states is complicated by threshold compression and wave function delocalization effects that are independent of the specific interaction.

## Method

The DDRH model was applied to Lambda hypernuclei, with in-medium interactions derived from a Dirac-Brueckner calculation using the free space Bonn A interaction, mapped onto density-dependent vertex functionals. The spin-orbit systematics were investigated by varying the omega-Lambda-Lambda coupling in O-16 artificially to study threshold effects, and localization coefficients were computed to quantify wave function delocalization across multiple nuclei from Ca-40 to Pb-208. Auger transition rates for neutron emission from Lambda de-excitation in Pb-209 were calculated self-consistently within the same framework.

## Key numbers

- For O-16, the 1p Lambda spin-orbit splitting was studied as a function of centroid energy approaching the continuum threshold, showing compression before becoming unbound
- Localization coefficients N_Lambda for the 1p3/2 state: 0.51 in Ca-40, 0.59 in V-51, 0.68 in Y-89, 0.79 in Pb-208
- Localization coefficients N_Lambda for the 1d5/2 state: 0.23 in Ca-40, 0.33 in V-51, 0.47 in Y-89, 0.66 in Pb-208
- Localization coefficients N_Lambda for the 1f7/2 state: 0.27 in Y-89, 0.52 in Pb-208 (not bound in lighter nuclei)
- Neutron localization coefficients N_n for the 1p3/2 state: 0.71 in Ca-40, 0.75 in V-51, 0.81 in Y-89, 0.88 in Pb-208

## Context

This work extends the DDRH model (Keil 2000) into the strangeness sector to describe Lambda hypernuclei, building on earlier Dirac-Brueckner approaches. The analysis of threshold compression effects draws on similar phenomena observed in neutron-rich dripline nuclei (Lalazissis 1998). The hypernuclear Auger effect calculations improve upon a previous non-self-consistent attempt (Likar 1986) and are motivated by a proposed experiment at JLab (JLabAuger). The results enable future analysis of global spectral features of the hypernuclear Auger effect and suggest that heavy hypernuclei should be investigated for decisive conclusions on the generic Lambda spin-orbit interaction, complementing recent gamma spectroscopic results showing almost vanishing spin-orbit splitting in C-13 (Ajim).
