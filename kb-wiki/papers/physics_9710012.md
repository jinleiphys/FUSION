# A symmetry adapted approach to vibrational excitations in atomic clusters

> An algebraic method especially suited to describe strongly anharmonic vibrational spectra in molecules may be an appropriate framework to study vibrational spectra of Na$^+_n$ clusters, where nearly flat potential energy surfaces and the appearance of close lying isomers have been reported. As an illustration we describe the model and apply it to the Be$_4$, H$_3^+$, Be$_3$ and Na$_3^+$ clusters.

## Key claim

The symmetry-adapted algebraic U(2) vibron model, previously applied to molecules, is shown to be an appropriate framework for describing strongly anharmonic vibrational spectra in atomic clusters, particularly where nearly flat potential energy surfaces and close-lying isomers have been reported. The authors illustrate the method on Be₄, H₃⁺, Be₃, and Na₃⁺ clusters, but note that the model's accuracy for H₃⁺ required four additional higher-order terms beyond the four-parameter Hamiltonian sufficient for Be₃ and Na₃⁺.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian using U(2) Lie algebras, where each interatomic bond is associated with a U_i(2) algebra whose states correspond to Morse oscillator eigenstates. Symmetry-adapted tensors are built by projecting local operators onto irreducible representations of the molecular point group (T_d for Be₄, D_{3h} for the others), and the Hamiltonian is formed by coupling these tensors to total symmetry A₁ using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. For Be₄, the algebraic Hamiltonian includes terms for vibrational angular momenta of degenerate modes, and the model parameters are fitted to ab initio spectra; for the D_{3h} molecules, least-square fits to experimental vibrational energies are performed.

## Key numbers

- Be₄ fit: r.m.s. deviation of 2.6 cm⁻¹ for levels up to V=4 quanta, using a Hamiltonian with parameters fitted to ab initio data from the literature.
- Be₃ fit: r.m.s. deviation of 1.35 cm⁻¹ using a 4-parameter Hamiltonian, with energy differences ΔE = E_th - E_exp ranging from -2.05 cm⁻¹ to +1.90 cm⁻¹ for levels up to V=3.
- Na₃⁺ fit: r.m.s. deviation of 1.33 cm⁻¹ using a 4-parameter Hamiltonian, with ΔE ranging from -2.06 cm⁻¹ to +1.95 cm⁻¹ for levels up to V=3.
- H₃⁺ fit: r.m.s. deviation of 5.84 cm⁻¹ using an 8-parameter Hamiltonian, with ΔE ranging from -5.69 cm⁻¹ to +7.48 cm⁻¹ for levels up to V=3.
- For Be₄, the fundamental vibrational energies (V=1) are: A₁ mode at 637.0 cm⁻¹, E mode at 455.0 cm⁻¹, and F₂ mode at 678.2 cm⁻¹ (all with N=44).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic U(2) vibron model, previously applied to molecules including Na₃⁺ by Frank and collaborators (1990s), to tetrahedral and D_{3h} clusters, providing a symmetry-adapted framework that incorporates anharmonicity from the outset. The approach contrasts with standard ab initio methods (e.g., those of Bonacic-Koutecky and collaborators for Naₙ⁺ clusters) that are well-suited for deep potential minima but less appropriate for the flat potential energy surfaces and close-lying isomers found in metallic clusters. The model enables systematic construction of anharmonic vibrational Hamiltonians for clusters, with the potential to study vibrational spectra of larger metallic clusters and handle isomeric shapes with different point symmetries.
