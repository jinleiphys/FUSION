# The origin of excited states of the $Λ$ baryon at the SU(3) point from Lattice QCD

> In this work we determine the finite-volume lattice QCD spectrum at the flavor symmetric $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ point in the meson-baryon singlet and octet irreducible representations. We construct the appropriate interpolation operators and perform the calculation on ensembles in quite large volume ($L=48$). We find three below-threshold energy levels, with the singlet having lower energy and the two octets being non-degenerate at one sigma, which for these large volumes ($M_π L\approx 14.5$) strongly suggests a bound state close to that energy at each of the irreducible representations. We confront this finite-volume spectrum with the prediction from UCHPT through the Lüscher method finding qualitative agreement. Finally we perform a re-fit of UCHPT free parameters to the available (experimental and lattice) data including the energy levels calculated in this work. This allows us to follow the pole trajectories to the physical point, identifying the $Λ(1405)$ as a lower octet, and $Λ(1380)$ as a singlet bound state in the $\mathrm{SU(3)}$ limit. Furthermore, $Λ(1670)$ is identified on a qualitative level as the heavier octet bound state and its relation to three-body final states is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at the SU(3) flavor symmetric point, lattice QCD calculations with meson-baryon operators reveal three below-threshold energy levels in the singlet and octet irreducible representations, which strongly suggests bound states; however, the identification of the heavier octet state with the Λ(1670) resonance is only qualitative and its relation to three-body final states remains to be clarified.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on a single CLS ensemble (C103) with Nf=2+1 dynamical fermions at the SU(3) flavor symmetric point, using a Clover-Wilson action with lattice spacing a≈0.086 fm and volume 48^3×96. They constructed meson-baryon interpolation operators projected into the singlet and two octet irreducible representations of SU(3) flavor, computed correlation functions using distillation with 100 Laplacian eigenvectors, and extracted energy levels via the GEVP method. The finite-volume spectrum was compared with predictions from chiral unitary (UCHPT) amplitudes through the Lüscher method, and a global refit of UCHPT parameters was performed including the new lattice data together with existing experimental and lattice results.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar decay constant at the SU(3) point: f_PS = 202.3(9.7) MeV (renormalized, using convention where f_π ≈ 130 MeV at physical point)
- Pseudoscalar meson mass: M = 713.42(32) MeV; baryon octet mass: m = 1613.8(2.2) MeV
- Singlet ground state energy: E_1 = 2136.2(6.3) MeV
- Octet prime ground state energy: E_8' = 2206(19) MeV
- Octet ground state energy: E_8 = 2261(33) MeV
- Non-interacting meson-baryon threshold: 2327.2(2.5) MeV
- Energy difference singlet minus octet prime: ΔE_{1-8'} = -69(19) MeV
- Energy difference singlet minus octet: ΔE_{1-8} = -125(33) MeV
- Energy difference octet prime minus octet: ΔE_{8'-8} = -56(40) MeV
- M_π L ≈ 14.5 (very large volume suppressing finite-volume effects)
- Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.086 fm; volume: 48^3×96; 800 gauge configurations from two replicas

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the Λ(1405) using single-baryon three-quark operators (Gubler 2016, Menadue 2011, Engel 2012) and recent coupled-channel scattering analyses near the physical point by the BaSc collaboration (2023). It complements the HAL QCD method study of S-wave meson-baryon interaction in the SU(3) limit (Murakami 2023, 2025) by using meson-baryon operators for the first time at the SU(3) point. The results enable following the pole trajectories from the SU(3) symmetric point to the physical point via UCHPT, identifying the Λ(1380) as a singlet bound state, the Λ(1405) as a lower octet state, and the Λ(1670) as a heavier octet bound state, while the relation of the latter to three-body final states remains an open question for future investigation.
