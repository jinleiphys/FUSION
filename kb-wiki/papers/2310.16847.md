# Maximal Mass Neutron Star as a Key to Superdense Matter Physics

> We propose a universal approximation of the equation of state of superdense matter in neutron star (NS) interiors. It contains only two parameters, the pressure and the density at the center of the maximally massive neutron star. We demonstrate the validity of this approximation for a wide range of different types of equations of state, including both baryonic and hybrid models. Combined with recently discovered correlations of internal (density, pressure, and speed of sound at the center) and external (mass, radius) properties of a maximally massive neutron star, this approximation turns out to be an effective tool for determining the equation of state of superdense matter using astrophysical observations.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a universal, two-parameter approximation of the equation of state of superdense matter in neutron star interiors, valid for densities above about three times nuclear saturation density, but the approximation is purely phenomenological and has no microscopic justification; its accuracy degrades significantly for models with first-order phase transitions or at lower densities.

## Method
The authors collected 162 equations of state (nucleonic, hyperonic, and hybrid with quark cores) from the CompOSE database and other sources, computed the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff solutions for each, and extracted the central density, pressure, and speed of sound at the maximally massive neutron star. They then fitted universal correlation formulas linking these central quantities to the maximum mass and radius, and constructed a single analytic function for the pressure-density relation above 3 times nuclear saturation density using only the central density and pressure as parameters.

## Key numbers
- The sample includes 162 equations of state: 97 nucleonic, 32 hyperonic, and 33 hybrid models.
- The correlation fit for maximum mass has a root-mean-square relative error of 0.86% and a maximal relative error of 5.4% (excluding two outlier VQCD models).
- The correlation fit for maximum radius has a root-mean-square relative error of 2.0% and a maximal relative error of 9.3%.
- The universal pressure-density approximation works above a density of 3 times the nuclear saturation density (3 x 2.8 x 10^14 g/cm^3); for nucleonic models the maximal error does not exceed 10%.
- Two illustrative observational constraints are used: a lower limit on maximum mass of 2.19 solar masses (Kandel and Romani 2023) and an upper limit of 2.33 solar masses (Rezzolla et al. 2018), plus a radius constraint of 13.6 km for a 1.4-solar-mass star (Annala et al. 2018).

## Context
This work extends the earlier two-parameter description of equations of state proposed by Lindblom (2010) and the correlations among maximally massive neutron star properties found by Ofengeim (2020) and Cai et al. (2023), confirming them on a much larger and more diverse set of models including hybrid stars with quark cores. The method enables direct transformation of astrophysical observations of the most massive neutron stars into constraints on the equation of state at densities above 3 times nuclear saturation density, which are inaccessible to terrestrial experiments. The authors note that the physical origin of the universality remains unclear and may differ from the explanation given by Cai et al. (2023).
