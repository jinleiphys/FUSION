# Renormalization of the f0(980) and a0(980) scalar resonances in a nuclear medium

> The meson meson interaction in the scalar sector in the presence of a nuclear medium is studied with particular attention to the change of the properties of the f0(980) and a0(980) resonances. By using a chiral unitary approach which generates the f0 and a0 resonances and reproduces their free properties, we find that the position of the resonances in the medium is barely changed but their widths are considerably broadened. New many body corrections generating from higher orders in the chiral Lagrangian plus the contribution from N*h excitations, not considered before in connection with the pion-pion interaction in the nuclear medium, are also investigated.

# Renormalization of the f0(980) and a0(980) Scalar Resonances in a Nuclear Medium

## Key claim

Using a chiral unitary approach that generates the f0(980) and a0(980) resonances dynamically and reproduces their free properties, the authors find that the positions of these resonances in nuclear matter are barely changed, but their widths are considerably broadened. The claim is hedged by noting that new many-body corrections from higher-order chiral Lagrangian terms and N*h excitations are also investigated, and that the results for the a0(980) depend on the specific channel considered (KbarK vs. pi-eta).

## Method

The meson-meson interaction in the scalar sector is studied using a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorization, employing the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian as the kernel. The nuclear medium is incorporated by modifying the meson propagators in the loop functions: pions are renormalized through ph and Delta h excitations, kaons through their selfenergy (with the Kbar selfenergy treated via a spectral function from a detailed model and the K selfenergy via a t-rho approximation), and the eta through a t-rho approximation using fitted scattering amplitudes. Additional medium corrections from tadpole diagrams and N*(1440) Roper-hole excitations are evaluated, with the N* coupling to N pi pi and N KbarK derived from chiral Lagrangians.

## Key numbers

- The a0(980) resonance in the KbarK channel shows an apparent mass shift of about -50 MeV at normal nuclear density rho0, and the apparent width increases from around 90 MeV (free) to about 200 MeV at rho0.
- In the pi-eta channel, the a0(980) resonance melts very fast with density; at densities of about rho0/2 there is practically no resonant trace left.
- The f0(980) resonance position barely moves with density, but its width grows from a free value of around 30 MeV to about 100 MeV at rho0.
- The K selfenergy in the medium is approximated as Pi = 0.13 mK^2 * (rho/rho0).
- The eta-nucleon scattering amplitude parameters used are a = 0.75 + 0.27i fm, r0 = -1.50 - 0.24i fm, s0 = -0.10 - 0.01i fm^3.
- The N* coupling constants used are c*1 = -7.27 GeV^{-1} and c*2 = 0, determined from pi- p -> pi+ pi- p data.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to meson-meson scattering in the nuclear medium previously applied to low-energy pion-pion interactions by Chiang et al. (1998) and Rapp et al. (1999), now focusing on the higher-energy region of the f0(980) and a0(980) resonances. It builds on the generation of these resonances from chiral Lagrangians via the inverse amplitude method and coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation developed by Oller et al. (1998, 1999), and on the selfconsistent treatment of kaon selfenergies in nuclei by Lutz (1998) and Ramos et al. (1999). The work enables predictions for two-meson production experiments in nuclei, such as (gamma,2pi) reactions, and provides a framework to test the nature of scalar resonances through their medium modifications.
