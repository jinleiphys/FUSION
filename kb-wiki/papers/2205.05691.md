# Quark formation and phenomenology in binary neutron-star mergers using V-QCD

> Using full 3+1 dimensional general-relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of equal- and unequal-mass neutron-star binaries with properties that are consistent with those inferred from the inspiral of GW170817, we perform a detailed study of the quark-formation processes that could take place after merger. We use three equations of state consistent with current pulsar observations derived from a novel finite-temperature framework based on V-QCD, a non-perturbative gauge/gravity model for Quantum Chromodynamics. In this way, we identify three different post-merger stages at which mixed baryonic and quark matter, as well as pure quark matter, are generated. A phase transition triggered collapse already $\lesssim 10\,\rm{ms}$ after the merger reveals that the softest version of our equations of state is actually inconsistent with the expected second-long post-merger lifetime of GW170817. Our results underline the impact that multi-messenger observations of binary neutron-star mergers can have in constraining the equation of state of nuclear matter, especially in its most extreme regimes.

## Key claim

Using three V-QCD equations of state in full general-relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of binary neutron-star mergers consistent with GW170817, the authors identify three distinct post-merger stages of quark formation (hot, warm, and cold quark stages), but find that the softest equation of state triggers a collapse to black hole within approximately 10 ms, which is inconsistent with the expected second-long post-merger lifetime of GW170817.

## Method

The authors performed full 3+1 dimensional general-relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of equal-mass (q=1) and unequal-mass (q=0.7) non-spinning neutron-star binaries with fixed chirp mass 1.186 solar masses, using three variants (soft, intermediate, stiff) of a finite-temperature equation of state derived from the V-QCD gauge/gravity duality model combined with nuclear theory at low densities. The simulations used the Frankfurt University/Kadath solver for initial data and the Einstein Toolkit with the Frankfurt/Illinois general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamic code for evolution, with six refinement levels and finest grid spacing of 221 meters, tracking baryon-number density, temperature, electron fraction, and quark fraction in the post-merger remnant.

## Key numbers

- Chirp mass of simulated binaries: 1.186 solar masses, consistent with GW170817
- Maximum non-rotating masses for the three equations of state: 2.02 solar masses (Soft), 2.14 solar masses (Inter), 2.34 solar masses (Stiff)
- Collapse times to black hole for Soft equation of state: 9.5 ms (q=1.0) and 5.8 ms (q=0.7); for Soft-NPT variant: >37 ms (q=1.0) and 11 ms (q=0.7)
- Tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 475 (Soft), 511 (Inter), 560 (Stiff)
- Maximum temperature during hot quark stage: approximately 60 MeV
- Maximum density during cold quark stage: well above 9 times nuclear saturation density (n_s = 0.16 fm^{-3})
- Warm quark stage occurs at intermediate temperatures of approximately 20 MeV and densities of approximately 4 n_s

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phase transitions in neutron-star mergers using polytropic equations of state with Gibbs constructions (Weih 2020) and effective particle approaches (Bauswein 2018) by employing the V-QCD gauge/gravity duality model, which provides a unified description of the deconfinement phase transition from baryonic to quark matter. The results enable future multi-messenger constraints on the equation of state at extreme densities and temperatures, particularly through the identification of characteristic post-merger gravitational-wave frequencies and the collapse timescale as discriminators between soft and stiff equations of state.
