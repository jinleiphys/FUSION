# Renormalization group analysis of color superconductivity revisited

> Color superconductivity in cold, dense quark matter is a key feature of the QCD phase diagram, whose present theoretical understanding relies predominantly on weak-coupling calculations. In this work, we revisit the evaluation of the color-superconducting gap using a renormalization group (RG) framework formulated in effective theory near the Fermi surface. By incorporating quark self-energy corrections into the RG equation, we reproduce the known weak-coupling results from the gap equation at the same perturbative order at $O(g^0)$. Within the RG approach, the angular momentum structure of the pairing channel becomes more transparent, allowing us to examine the size of the gap for various pairing patterns. We also compare our results with recent lattice QCD calculations at finite isospin density. Finally, we argue that the RG method potentially offers a simpler and more systematic route to higher-order computations of the gap, which are of order $O(g)$ and thus quantitatively important.

## Key claim

By incorporating quark self-energy corrections into the renormalization group (RG) equation formulated in effective theory near the Fermi surface, the author reproduces the known weak-coupling results for the color-superconducting gap from the gap equation at the same perturbative order at \(O(g^0)\), but the RG method does not push beyond the existing calculation and the self-energy correction contributes only at higher order.

## Method

The author applies the RG approach to Landau Fermi liquid theory for massless quarks near the Fermi surface, limiting the effective theory to a thin shell of momentum around the Fermi surface with cutoff \(\Lambda \ll \mu\). The RG equation for the four-fermion coupling function is derived by integrating out fast modes and rescaling momenta, including one-loop ladder diagrams and a tree-level beta function from long-range interactions. Quark self-energy corrections are incorporated via the resummed quark propagator in hard dense loop (HDL) effective theory, replacing the density of states \(N\) with \(Z_{\psi}(t) N\) where \(Z_{\psi}(t) = (1 + g^2 t / (9\pi^2))^{-1}\). The pairing gap is extracted from the singularity in the solution of the RG equation.

## Key numbers

- The density of states on the Fermi surface is \(N = \mu^2 / (2\pi^2)\).
- The Debye screening mass is \(m_{\mathrm{D}}^2 = N_f g^2 \mu^2 / (2\pi^2)\), and the effective gluon mass is \(m_{\mathrm{g}}^2 = m_{\mathrm{D}}^2 / 3\).
- The quark wave function renormalization factor is \(Z(k_0) = \left(1 + \frac{g^2}{18\pi^2} \ln \frac{48 e^2 m_{\mathrm{g}}^2}{\pi^2 k_0^2}\right)^{-1}\), where \(e\) is the base of the natural logarithm.
- The leading term in \(\ln(\Delta / \mu)\) scales as \(1/g\), not \(1/g^2\), consistent with Son (1998).
- The self-energy correction contributes at \(O(g^0)\) to the gap, while the leading term is \(O(1/g)\) and the next-to-leading order is \(O(g^0)\).

## Context

This work revisits the RG analysis of color superconductivity originally developed by Son (1998), Evans et al. (1998), and Schafer et al. (1998), and extends it by systematically including quark self-energy corrections. The RG approach makes the angular momentum structure of the pairing channel more transparent, enabling classification of spin and orbital angular momentum channels as in the accompanying paper by Fujimoto (2025). The results are compared with recent lattice QCD calculations at finite isospin density by Abbott et al. (2023, 2024), where qualitative agreement is found but the weak-coupling pairing gap appears overestimated relative to the lattice data. The RG method potentially offers a simpler route to higher-order computations of the gap at \(O(g)\).
