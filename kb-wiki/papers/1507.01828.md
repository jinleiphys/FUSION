# Capture reactions into borromean two-proton systems at rp-waiting points

> We investigate even-even two-proton borromean systems at prominent intermediate heavy waiting points for the rapid proton capture process. The most likely single-particle levels are used to calculate three-body energy and structure as a function of proton-core resonance energy. We establish a linear dependence between two- and three-body energies with the same slope, but the absolute value slightly dependent on partial wave structure. Using these relations we predict low-lying excited states in the isotones following the critical waiting points. The capture rate for producing a borromean bound state is described both based on a full three-body calculation and on a very simple analytic rate expression for temperatures about $1-5$~GK. This rate is valid for both direct and sequential capture paths, and it only depends on the three-body resonance energy. As a result the relevant path of the radiative capture reactions can be determined. We present numerical results for $E1$ and $E2$ photon emission, and discuss occurrence preferences in general as well as relative sizes of these most likely processes. Finally, we present narrow estimated intervals for the proton capture rates relevant for the critical waiting points.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a linear dependence between two-body (proton-core) and three-body energies for borromean two-proton systems at rp-waiting points, with the same slope of approximately two across different partial wave configurations, but the absolute value of the three-body energy depends slightly on the partial wave structure and on whether a three-body force is included.

## Method

The authors perform hyperspherical adiabatic expansion of the Faddeev equations in coordinate space to calculate three-body energies and wave functions for the system of two protons plus an even-even core (using $^{68}\text{Se} + p + p$ as the test case). They use Woods-Saxon potentials for the core-proton interaction with parameters appropriate for the mass region around $A=68$, and adjust the two-body strength parameters to place single-particle states ($p_{3/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $f_{5/2}$) at chosen energies. The radiative capture rate for producing a borromean bound state is computed both from the full three-body calculation and from a simplified analytic Breit-Wigner expression valid for narrow, well-separated resonances.

## Key numbers

- The three-body energy $E_{3b}$ depends linearly on the two-body energy $E_{2b}$ with slope approximately 2, verified within the interval $-2.0$ MeV $< E_{2b} < 2.0$ MeV.
- For the test system $^{70}\text{Kr}$, the $\beta$ decay half-life is 57(21) ms.
- The first excited states of the waiting point cores ($N=Z=32,34,36$) are at 0.9 MeV, 0.9 MeV, and 0.7 MeV, respectively.
- The simple analytic rate expression (Eq. 13) is valid for temperatures between 0.1 and 4 GK, assuming $E_R \gg T$ and width $\Gamma \ll T$.
- For $\ell=1$ transitions with $E_{\gamma}=1$ MeV and $R=10$ fm, the ratio $\Gamma_{\gamma}(\mathcal{E}1) / \Gamma_{\gamma}(\mathcal{E}2) \approx 10^4$ from a single-particle estimate.
- The three-body ground state wave function weights for the $p_{3/2}$ case show 78% in the $l_x=0, l_y=0$ component at $E_{2b}=-2.0$ MeV and 76% at $E_{2b}=2.0$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends three-body calculations of borromean two-proton systems to intermediate heavy waiting points ($^{64}\text{Ge}$, $^{68}\text{Se}$, $^{72}\text{Kr}$) in the rp-process, which had previously been limited to nuclei lighter than $^{38}\text{Ca}$ (Grigorenko 2005, Görres 1995). The paper provides a method for estimating proton capture rates given sparse experimental information, building on the few-body formalism successfully applied to neutron dripline systems (Jensen 2004, Barranco 2006) and to light proton dripline nuclei (Grigorenko 2005, Görres 1995). The results enable predictions of low-lying excited states in the isotones following the critical waiting points and allow determination of the relevant radiative capture path (E1 vs E2) based on the three-body resonance energy.
