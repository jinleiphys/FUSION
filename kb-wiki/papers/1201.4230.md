# Bulk viscosity in heavy ion collision

> The effect of a temperature dependent bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio~($ζ/s$) along with a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio~($η/s$) on the space time evolution of the fluid produced in high energy heavy ion collisions have been studied in a relativistic viscous hydrodynamics model. The boost invariant Israel-Stewart theory of causal relativistic viscous hydrodynamics is used to simulate the evolution of the fluid in 2 spatial and 1 temporal dimension. The dissipative correction to the freezeout distribution for bulk viscosity is calculated using Grad's fourteen moment method. From our simulation we show that the method is applicable only for $ζ/s<0.004$.

# Bulk viscosity in heavy ion collision

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that Grad's fourteen-moment method for calculating dissipative corrections to the freezeout distribution function is applicable only for bulk viscosity to entropy density ratios below 0.004, despite the abstract's broader claim about studying temperature-dependent bulk viscosity.

## Method
The authors used a (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant Israel-Stewart causal relativistic viscous hydrodynamics model (the "AZHYDRO-KOLKATA" code) to simulate the space-time evolution of the fluid produced in high-energy heavy ion collisions. They employed a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio (zeta/s) derived from pQCD and lattice QCD calculations, with a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s = 1/4pi), and implemented the dissipative correction to the freezeout distribution for bulk viscosity using Grad's fourteen-moment method. The initial energy density profile was calculated from a two-component Glauber model with central energy density 30 GeV/fm^3, and freezeout occurred at a constant temperature of 130 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The method is applicable only for zeta/s < 0.004 (from the abstract); the full text indicates Grad's method breaks down when the relative correction exceeds 50%, which occurs for zeta/s values greater than 0.01 times the present form considered.
- Initial central energy density: epsilon_0 = 30 GeV/fm^3.
- Freezeout temperature: T_fo = 130 MeV.
- Crossover temperature between QGP and hadronic phase: 174 MeV.
- Hadronic resonance gas includes all resonances below mass m_res = 2.5 GeV.
- Relative correction to pion invariant yield due to bulk viscosity compared to ideal fluid: within approximately 10%.
- Relative correction to elliptic flow (v_2) due to bulk viscosity compared to ideal fluid: within approximately 3%.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of shear viscosity in heavy ion collisions, where most estimates place eta/s between 1-4 times 1/(4pi), by incorporating a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity based on pQCD calculations (journal-2) and lattice QCD results (journal-3). The study uses the Wuppertal-Budapest lattice equation of state and builds on the Israel-Stewart theory of causal viscous hydrodynamics (journal-4). The results establish a practical upper limit for zeta/s in which Grad's fourteen-moment freezeout correction method remains valid, enabling future studies to identify the regime where more sophisticated non-equilibrium treatments are required.
