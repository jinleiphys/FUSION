# Phase transition and thermodynamics of a hot and dense system in a scaled NJL model

> The chiral phase transition of a hot and dense system of quarks is studied within a modified SU(3) NJL lagrangian that implements the QCD scale anomaly. The u- and s-quark condensates can feel or not the same chiral restoration depending on the considered region of the 3-dimension space T_c(μ_uc,μ_sc). The temperature behaviour of the pressure and of the energy and entropy densities of the u- and s-quark system is investigated. At high temperature, the non-vanishing bare s-quark mass only modifies slightly the usual behaviour associated with an ideal quark gas.

# Phase transition and thermodynamics of a hot and dense system in a scaled NJL model

## Key claim
The chiral phase transition for u- and s-quark condensates can occur either simultaneously or independently depending on the region of the three-dimensional parameter space (T_c, μ_uc, μ_sc), with the coupling between condensates mediated solely through the gluon condensate; the transition is always first order for one parameter set and can be second order at low chemical potentials for another.

## Method
The authors study the chiral phase transition within a modified SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that incorporates the QCD scale anomaly via a scalar dilaton field and the axial anomaly via a mass term. They work in the classical (mean field) approximation, computing the thermodynamic potential from the effective action and solving the coupled gap equations for the u-quark constituent mass, s-quark constituent mass, and gluon condensate as functions of temperature and chemical potentials. Two parameter sets are used, corresponding to large (M_u^0 = 600 MeV, χ_0 = 125 MeV) and moderate (M_u^0 = 300 MeV, χ_0 = 80 MeV) vacuum constituent u-quark masses.

## Key numbers
- For parameter set (M_u^0 = 600 MeV, χ_0 = 125 MeV): first-order transition at T_c = 168 MeV for μ_u = μ_s = 0
- For parameter set (M_u^0 = 300 MeV, χ_0 = 80 MeV): second-order transition at T_c = 150 MeV for μ_u = μ_s = 0 (u-quark), with s-quark transition at T_sc ≈ 200 MeV
- For the first parameter set at μ_uc = μ_sc = 250 MeV: ρ_u_bef/ρ_0 = 0.362, ρ_s_bef/ρ_0 = 0.050, ρ_u_aft/ρ_0 = 2.646, ρ_s_aft/ρ_0 = 0.978, where ρ_0 = 0.51 fm^{-3}
- High-temperature pressure fit for first parameter set: P ≈ 3.40 T^4 - 2.097 × 10^{-3}
- High-temperature pressure fit for second parameter set: P ≈ 3.40 T^4 - 1.195 × 10^{-3}
- Bag constant B^{1/4} ≈ 209 MeV (first set) and B^{1/4} ≈ 183 MeV (second set) from direct calculation
- High-temperature energy density fit for first set: ε ≈ 10.269 T^4 + 1.620 × 10^{-3}
- High-temperature energy density fit for second set: ε ≈ 10.271 T^4 + 0.877 × 10^{-3}
- High-temperature entropy density fit for first set: s ≈ 13.868 T^3 - 0.136 × 10^{-1}
- High-temperature entropy density fit for second set: s ≈ 13.839 T^3 - 0.912 × 10^{-2}

## Context
This work extends previous SU(2) NJL studies of the chiral transition (Jaminon 1994, Bernard 1988, Christov 1990, Asakawa 1989, Zhuang 1994) to the SU(3) flavor case with both light and strange quark chemical potentials. It contrasts with other SU(3) NJL models (Hatsuda 1987, 1994, Klimt 1990) that rarely allow first-order transitions, and with sigma-model approaches (Meyer-Ortmanns 1994). The model enables systematic mapping of the chiral transition surface in the (T, μ_u, μ_s) space and provides equations of state for hot and dense quark matter, though the authors note the need for vector meson inclusion to achieve realistic critical densities and for inclusion of gluon degrees of freedom to reach the Stefan-Boltzmann limit.
