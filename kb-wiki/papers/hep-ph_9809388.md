# Hyperon Non-leptonic Weak Decays in the Chiral Perturbation Theory I

> Hyperon non-leptonic weak decay amplitudes are studied in the chiral perturbation theory. We employ the low energy effective weak Hamiltonian which contains the perturbative QCD correction. To include the non-perturbative QCD effect, quark currents of the effective Hamiltonian are substituted with hadronic currents which are color singlet and are derived by the chiral perturbation theory. We find that the amplitudes caused by the product of hadronic currents are small. It reproduce the small amplitudes of $ΔI=3/2$, which are derived by the strong interaction correction.

## Key claim

The authors find that the amplitudes caused by the product of hadronic currents (derived from chiral perturbation theory) are small, and this reproduces the small amplitudes of the ΔI=3/2 transitions, which are derived by the strong interaction correction. The method requires fewer low-energy constants than previous chiral perturbation theory approaches, but the paper explicitly notes that the two-point vertex of the baryon operator (not included in this simple factorization) must be introduced separately to fully describe the weak interaction.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy effective weak Hamiltonian for hyperon non-leptonic decays by first including perturbative QCD corrections via the renormalization group method down to a scale where α_s=1, yielding six four-quark operators with Wilson coefficients. They then replace the quark currents in these operators with hadronic currents derived from chiral perturbation theory in the heavy baryon formalism, using Fierz transformations to rewrite the operators into forms where the currents are color singlets. The resulting hadronic weak Hamiltonian is evaluated at chiral order O(p²) for the S- and P-wave decay amplitudes of the seven main hyperon decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Two sets of Wilson coefficients are adopted for comparison: one with m_t=200 [GeV], μ₀=0.24 [GeV], Λ^(4)=0.10 [GeV]; the other with m_t=200 [GeV], μ₀=0.71 [GeV], Λ^(4)=0.316 [GeV]
- The Fermi coupling constants used are G_F=10⁻⁵ m_p⁻² and G_f=1.166×10⁻¹¹ [MeV⁻²]
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Λ_χ ∼ 1 [GeV]
- The pion decay constant f_π appears in the meson Lagrangian and in the relation between the heavy baryon amplitudes and the conventional S- and P-wave amplitudes A and B

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to describe hyperon non-leptonic weak decays using chiral perturbation theory, which had failed to simultaneously reproduce S- and P-wave amplitudes and did not explain the ΔI=1/2 enhancement (Bijnens 1985, Donoghue 1986, Jenkins 1992, Springer 1995). The approach differs from earlier chiral perturbation theory treatments (Borasoy 1998) that required many low-energy constants fitted to data; instead, the authors derive the low-energy constants from the strong-interaction Lagrangian. The method enables separate estimation of ΔI=1/2 and ΔI=3/2 amplitudes and is intended to be applied to more complicated processes such as Y+N→N+N.
