# Axial Current Generation from Electric Field: Chiral Electric Separation Effect

> We study a relativistic plasma containing charged chiral fermions in an external electric field. We show that with the presence of both vector and axial charge densities, the electric field can induce an axial current along its direction and thus cause chirality separation. We call it the Chiral Electric Separation Effect (CESE). On very general basis, we argue that the strength of CESE is proportional to $μ_Vμ_A$ with $μ_V$ and $μ_A$ the chemical potentials for vector charge and axial charge. We then explicitly calculate this CESE conductivity coefficient in thermal QED at leading-log order. The CESE can manifest a new gapless wave mode propagating along the electric field. Potential observable of CESE in heavy-ion collisions is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a relativistic plasma containing charged chiral fermions, an external electric field can induce an axial current (chirality separation) when both vector and axial charge densities are present, an effect called the Chiral Electric Separation Effect (CESE). The authors argue that the CESE conductivity is proportional to the product of the vector and axial chemical potentials, μ_V μ_A, and explicitly calculate this coefficient in thermal QED at leading-log order, noting that unlike the Chiral Magnetic Effect and Chiral Separation Effect, this effect is not related to the axial anomaly but arises from ordinary conduction in a chiral many-body environment.

## Method

The authors used the Kubo formula to compute the CESE conductivity coefficient (χ_e) for a thermal QED plasma under the condition that the chemical potentials are small compared to the temperature (μ_V, μ_A ≪ T). They derived the retarded vector-axial correlator from Feynman diagrams, incorporating an effective vertex that resums a set of ladder diagrams to capture the leading-log order contributions, and solved the resulting integral equation for the vertex using a variational method. The calculation was performed in the limit of small chemical potentials, expanding the results up to order (μ/T)^2.

## Key numbers

* The CESE conductivity coefficient is χ_e ≈ 20.499 (μ_V μ_A / T^2) * (T / [e^3 ln(1/e)]), where e is the electric charge and T is the temperature.
* The electric conductivity σ is approximately (T / [e^3 ln(1/e)]) * [15.6952 + 7.76052 (μ_V^2 + μ_A^2)/T^2].
* In the limit μ_s → 0, the electric conductivity σ ≈ 15.6964 T / [e^3 ln(1/e)], in agreement with a prior result from Arnold (2000).
* The chiral magnetic wave speed is v_χ = σ_5 √(α_V α_A) B, where σ_5 = N_c e / (2π^2) and α_V, α_A are susceptibilities.
* The chiral electric wave speed (for a background with n_V = 0) is v_e = α_A n_A √(2σ_2 χ_e α_V α_A) E, where σ_2 is the coefficient of the μ^2 term in the expansion of σ.

## Context

This work extends the known phenomena of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and Chiral Separation Effect (CSE), which are induced by magnetic fields, to the case of an electric field. The CME was proposed by Kharzeev (2004, 2007) and Fukushima (2008), and the CSE by Son (2004) and Metlitski (2005). The new CESE effect, combined with CME and CSE, allows a unified description of vector and axial current generation by both electric and magnetic fields. The authors also show that the CESE can lead to new gapless wave modes propagating along the electric field, such as the chiral electric wave (CEW) and the axial density wave (ADW), and suggest potential observable signatures in asymmetric heavy-ion collisions like Cu + Au.
