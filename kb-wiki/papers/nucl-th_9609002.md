# Kaon mass in dense matter

> The variation of kaon mass in dense, charge-neutral baryonic matter at beta-equilibrium has been investigated. The baryon interaction has been included by means of nonlinear Walecka model, with and without hyperons and the interaction of kaons with the baryons has been incorporated through the Nelson-Kaplan model. A self-consistant, one-loop level calculation has been carried out. We find that at the mean field level, the presence of the hyperons makes the density-dependence of the kaon mass softer. Thus, the kaon condensation threshold is pushed up in the baryon density. The loop diagrams tend to lower the kaon condensation point for lower values of $a_3 m_s$. We also find that the S-wave kaon-nucleon interaction plays the dominant role in determining the on-set of kaon condensation and the contribution of the P-wave interaction is insignificant.

## Key claim

The threshold density for kaon condensation is pushed higher when hyperons are included in the dense baryonic matter, but the inclusion of one-loop (oyster) diagrams lowers the condensation threshold for lower values of the parameter \(a_3 m_s\) (corresponding to higher strangeness content of the nucleon). The S-wave kaon-nucleon interaction dominates the onset of kaon condensation, while the P-wave contribution is negligible.

## Method

The authors computed the effective kaon mass in dense, charge-neutral baryonic matter at beta-equilibrium by combining two models: the nonlinear Walecka model (for baryon interactions and equation of state) and the Nelson-Kaplan \(SU(3) \times SU(3)\) Lagrangian (for kaon-baryon interactions). The kaon propagator was calculated including mean-field diagrams, one-loop oyster diagrams (S-wave), and a P-wave diagram, with the kaon mass determined from the pole of the propagator in the static limit. The calculation was performed both with and without hyperons, and the parameter \(a_3 m_s\) was varied from -134 MeV to -310 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(a_3 m_s\) was varied from -134 MeV (corresponding to zero strangeness content of the nucleon) to -310 MeV (corresponding to 20% strangeness content).
- The strange quark mass was set to \(m_s = 150\) MeV, and the pion decay constant to 93 MeV.
- The weak interaction constants used were \(D = 0.81\) and \(F = 0.44\) (with \(g_A = F + D\)).
- The parameters \(a_1 m_s = -64\) MeV and \(a_2 m_s = -134\) MeV were determined by fitting nucleon, lambda, and cascade masses.
- Hyperons (\(\Sigma^-\) and \(\Lambda\)) appear at a baryon density of about 0.4 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The nuclear compressibility was varied from 250 MeV to 350 MeV, with little effect on the kaon mass.
- At the mean-field level with \(a_3 m_s = -310\) MeV, the kaon mass was as small as 350 MeV at a baryon density of 0.1 fm\(^{-3}\) (about two-thirds of nuclear matter density).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field calculations of Nelson and Kaplan (1990s) and later authors by including one-loop corrections (oyster diagrams) and the effect of hyperons on kaon condensation in dense matter. The results contradict earlier findings (e.g., by other authors using the Walecka model) that the kaon mass is sensitive to meson-baryon coupling strengths; here the kaon mass depends more strongly on the strangeness content parameter \(a_3 m_s\). The study enables more realistic predictions for kaon condensation thresholds in neutron star cores and heavy-ion collisions, and suggests that finite-size effects of mesons and baryons may need to be incorporated to avoid unphysical condensation at normal nuclear densities.
