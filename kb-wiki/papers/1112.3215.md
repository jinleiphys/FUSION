# An anomalous hydrodynamics for chiral superfluid

> Starting from low energy effective chiral Lagrangian with gauged Wess-Zumino Witten term, we have derived a hydrodynamic theory for chiral superfluid. It is a non-abelian hydrodynamics at zero temperature with only superfluid components. With an external electromagnetic field and baryonic and axial baryonic chemical potentials turned on, we are able to identify analogs of various anomaly induced term in normal hydrodynamics, including chiral vortical effect, chiral magnetic effect and chiral electric effect. As an example, we solved the hydrodynamic equations for the ground state and observed the chiral magnetic effect and chiral separation in the confined phase.

## Key claim

The paper derives a hydrodynamic theory for chiral superfluid from the low-energy effective chiral Lagrangian with a gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten term, claiming that analogs of the chiral magnetic effect, chiral vortical effect, and chiral electric effect appear in the superfluid phase; the derivation is limited to zero temperature and includes only superfluid components, with the hedge that the external baryonic field does not appear explicitly in the hydrodynamic variables but enters implicitly through the ground state.

## Method

Starting from the chiral Lagrangian plus the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten term, the author computes the consistent currents via functional derivatives with respect to external gauge fields (baryonic, axial baryonic, and electromagnetic), then passes to covariant (gauge-invariant) currents by adding Bardeen counterterms and local corrections. The hydrodynamic equations are formulated using non-abelian superfluid velocities defined from the covariant derivatives of the chiral field, and the system is closed by including the equations of motion for the superfluid velocities and the rotated charge operators, with the anomaly entering as a source term in the current conservation equations.

## Key numbers

- The Wess-Zumino-Witten term coefficient is fixed as \(C = \frac{1}{240\pi^2}\) (implied by the normalization convention, though not explicitly stated as a numerical value in the text).
- The anomalous divergence of the axial baryonic current is given by \(dJ_{B5} = \frac{15Ci}{2}\big((dB)^2 \text{Tr}1 + 2 dB dA_{em} \text{Tr}Q + (dA_{em})^2 \text{Tr}Q^2\big) + \frac{5Ci}{2}(dB_5)^2 \text{Tr}1\).
- The chiral magnetic effect and chiral separation effect are observed in the ground state of one-flavor QCD with a constant external magnetic field \(B\) along the \(z\)-axis and axial chemical potential \(\mu_5\).

## Context

This work extends the anomalous hydrodynamics framework of Son and Surówka (2009) from normal fluids to chiral superfluids, incorporating spontaneous symmetry breaking and Goldstone bosons as additional degrees of freedom. It differs from the effective field theory approach of Lublinsky and collaborators (2010) in fine details of the Wess-Zumino-Witten term, leading to quantitative differences in the constitutive equations. The results enable the study of anomaly-induced transport (chiral magnetic effect, chiral vortical effect, chiral electric effect) in superfluid phases of QCD, such as the confined phase, and provide a zero-temperature hydrodynamic description that can be extended to include normal components or finite temperature.
