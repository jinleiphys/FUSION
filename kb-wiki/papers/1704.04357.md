# Catalysis of partial chiral symmetry restoration by Delta matter

> We study the phase structure of dense hadronic matter including $Δ(1232)$ as well as N(939) based on the parity partner structure, where the baryons have their chiral partners with a certain amount of chiral invariant masses. We show that, in symmetric matter, $Δ$ enters into matter in the density region of about one to four times of normal nuclear matter density, $ρ_B \sim 1 - 4ρ_0$. The onset density of $Δ$ matter depends on the chiral invariant mass of $Δ$, $m_{\Delta0}$: The lager $m_{\Delta0}$, the bigger the onset density. The $Δ$ matter of $ρ_B \sim 1 - 4ρ_0$ is unstable due to the existence of $Δ$, and the stable $Δ$-nucleon matter is realized at about $ρ_B \sim 4ρ_0$, i.e., the phase transition from nuclear matter to $Δ$-nucleon matter is of first order for small $m_{\Delta0}$, and it is of second order for large $m_{\Delta0}$. We find that, associated with the phase transition, the chiral condensate changes very rapidly, i.e., the chiral symmetry restoration is accelerated by Δmatter. As a result of the accelerations, there appear $N^*$(1535) and $Δ$(1700), which are the chiral partners to N(939) and $Δ$(1232), in high density matter, signaling the partial chiral symmetry restoration. Furthermore, we find that complete chiral symmetry restoration itself is delayed by $Δ$ matter. We also calculate the effective masses, pressure and symmetry energy to study how the transition to $Δ$ matter affects such physical quantities. We observe that the physical quantities change drastically at the transition density.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the presence of Δ(1232) baryons in dense hadronic matter accelerates partial chiral symmetry restoration (signaled by the appearance of chiral partners N*(1535) and Δ(1700)), but delays complete chiral symmetry restoration. This behavior depends on the chiral invariant mass of the Δ, mΔ0, and the onset density of Δ matter occurs in the range ρB ∼ 1–4ρ0, with the exact value determined by mΔ0.

## Method
The authors construct a parity doublet model extended to include the Δ(1232) baryon and its chiral partner Δ(1700), alongside the nucleon N(939) and its partner N*(1535), with chiral invariant masses. They compute the thermodynamic potential at mean-field level for symmetric nuclear matter, including scalar (σ), vector (ω), and isovector (ρ) meson mean fields, and determine the phase structure by minimizing the potential as a function of baryon chemical potential μB. The model parameters are fixed using vacuum masses of the baryons and mesons, the pion decay constant, and nuclear matter saturation properties (ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3, binding energy −16 MeV, incompressibility 240 MeV, symmetry energy 31 MeV), leaving mN0, mΔ0, gωΔΔ, and gρΔΔ as free parameters.

## Key numbers
- Δ baryons enter matter in the density region ρB ∼ 1–4ρ0, where ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3 is the normal nuclear matter density.
- For mN0 = 500 MeV and mΔ0 = 550 MeV with gωΔΔ = gωNN, stable Δ-nucleon matter is realized at about ρB ∼ 4ρ0.
- The chiral invariant mass of the nucleon mN0 is constrained to 500–700 MeV based on the requirement that the pion decay constant at ρ0 is about 0.8 times its vacuum value.
- The effective nucleon mass at saturation density satisfies μB − mN(eff) ≈ 40 MeV, independent of the choice of mN0.
- The σNN coupling constants for mN0 = 500 MeV are g1 = 9.03 and g2 = 15.49; for mN0 = 700 MeV, g1 = 7.82 and g2 = 14.28.
- The σΔΔ coupling constants for mΔ0 = 500 MeV are a = 17.5 and b = 12.2; for mΔ0 = 700 MeV, a = 16.5 and b = 11.4.

## Context
This work extends the parity doublet model of Motohiro (2015) by including the Δ(1232) baryon and its chiral partner Δ(1700), following the formalism of Jido (2001). It builds on earlier studies of Δ matter in symmetric matter by Li (1997) and Lavagno (2012), which showed that Δ appearance depends on σ–Δ coupling and can cause instabilities. The results enable future investigations of dense matter created in heavy ion collisions at a few hundred MeV per nucleon and of neutron star interiors, where Δ baryons may appear at 2–3ρ0 as noted by Schurhoff (2010) and Drago (2014).
