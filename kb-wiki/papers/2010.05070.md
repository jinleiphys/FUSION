# $ΛNN $ three-body problem within $s$-wave inverse scattering on theoretical data

> $ΛN $ potentials recovered through the application of $s$-wave inverse scattering on theoretical data are demonstrated on the $ΛNN $ three-body problem. The spin-dependent Malfliet-Tjon I/III potential, with benchmark parameters that bind the deuteron at -2.2307 MeV, represents the $NN$ interaction. The three-body problem is solved through the hyperspherical method. From spin-averaged effective $ΛN $ potentials with one-quarter spin singlet and three-quarters spin triplet contributions, the binding energy and root-mean-square radius of $Λn p$ ($J^π=1/2^+$) computed is found to be -3.0759 MeV and 7.7 fm, respectively. This is higher than the current experimental binding energy for $Λn p$ ($J^π=1/2^+$), but consistent with recent trends in high-precision measurements on the lifetime of the same hypernucleus. With charge symmetry breaking in the $ΛN $ potentials, this $Λn p$ ($J^π=1/2^+$) binding energy was found to be consistent with a bound $Λn n$ ($J^π=1/2^+$) state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that charge-asymmetric lambda-nucleon potentials recovered through s-wave inverse scattering on theoretical data produce a bound Lambda n p state with binding energy -3.0759 MeV and a bound Lambda n n state with binding energy -0.0695 MeV, without requiring artificial scaling of the potentials, though the authors note that the Lambda n p binding energy is higher than the current experimental value.

## Method

The authors constructed effective spin-averaged Lambda N potentials (GLM-YN0) by combining one-quarter spin singlet and three-quarters spin triplet potentials recovered through the Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko inverse scattering method on theoretical s-wave scattering phases. They solved the Lambda N N three-body problem using the hyperspherical method with the semi-realistic Malfliet-Tjon I/III potential for the N N interaction, which correctly binds the deuteron at -2.2307 MeV. The coupled hyperradial equations were converted to an eigenvalue problem and solved using the computer code FaCE.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy from Malfliet-Tjon I/III potential: -2.2307 MeV
- Lambda n p (J^pi = 1/2^+) binding energy: -3.0759 MeV, with root-mean-square radius 7.7 fm
- Lambda separation energy for Lambda n p: B_Lambda = 0.85 MeV (relative to deuteron binding energy)
- Lambda n n (J^pi = 1/2^+) binding energy: either -0.0695 MeV with radius 12.3 fm, or -0.6112 MeV with radius 10.1 fm
- The -0.0695 MeV Lambda n n binding energy is close to the -0.069 MeV reported by Afnan (2015) and Gibson (2019)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Meoto 2020) that used the Malfliet-Tjon V potential, now employing the more realistic Malfliet-Tjon I/III potential that correctly binds the deuteron. The results contradict earlier predictions from Dalitz (1958), Down (1959), and Gal (2014) that no bound Lambda n n state exists, and instead support the possible bound state inferred from HypHI Collaboration experiments at GSI. The computed Lambda n p binding energy is consistent with recent high-precision STAR Collaboration lifetime measurements (Adam 2019) that indicate stronger Lambda N forces than previously assumed, and the corresponding B_Lambda = 0.85 MeV is close to the value of 0.83 MeV reported by Hiyama (2014) when scaling up the tensor component in NSC1997f phase-equivalent forces. The results enable future tests through planned JLab experiments (Gogami 2020) and lifetime calculations of Lambda n p.
