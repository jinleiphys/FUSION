# Mass, radius, and composition of the outer crust of nonaccreting cold neutron stars

> The properties and composition of the outer crust of nonaccreting cold neutron stars are studied by applying the model of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland, which was extended by including higher order corrections of the atomic binding, screening, exchange and zero-point energy. The most recent experimental nuclear data from the atomic mass table of Audi, Wapstra, and Thibault from 2003 is used. Extrapolation to the drip line is utilized by various state-of-the-art theoretical nuclear models (finite range droplet, relativistic nuclear field and non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock parameterizations). The different nuclear models are compared with respect to the mass and radius of the outer crust for different neutron star configurations and the nuclear compositions of the outer crust.

## Key claim

The outer crust mass and thickness of nonaccreting cold neutron stars depend only weakly on the choice of theoretical nuclear mass model, with deviations of a few percent in diameter, but the composition is nearly universal across different neutron star configurations, provided the star has reached its absolute ground state and fall-back material is neglected.

## Method

The authors applied the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) model extended with higher-order corrections for atomic binding, screening, exchange, and zero-point energy. They used experimental nuclear masses from the 2003 Audi-Wapstra-Thibault table and five theoretical mass models (BSk8, SkM*, FRDM, NL3, and a chiral Lagrangian model) to extrapolate to the neutron drip line. The outer crust equation of state was then integrated via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation starting from a chosen inner configuration (mass \(M_0\) and radius \(R_0\)) to compute the crust mass \(\Delta M\) and thickness \(\Delta R\).

## Key numbers

- For a canonical neutron star with inner mass \(M_0 = 1.4\,M_\odot\) and inner radius \(R_0 = 12\) km, the outer crust mass \(\Delta M\) is \(3.090 \times 10^{-5}\,M_\odot\) (BSk8), \(3.088 \times 10^{-5}\,M_\odot\) (SkM*), and \(3.093 \times 10^{-5}\,M_\odot\) (BSk8 without lattice); the corresponding thickness \(\Delta R\) is 0.4509 km, 0.4408 km, and 0.4666 km.
- For the FRDM equation of state, the outer crust mass \(\Delta M\) ranges from \(7.8 \times 10^{-6}\) to \(5.6 \times 10^{-4}\,M_\odot\) and the thickness \(\Delta R\) from 0.14 to 2.57 km for inner masses \(M_0 = 1.0\)–\(2.0\,M_\odot\) and inner radii \(R_0 = 10\)–20 km.
- For a neutron star with \(M_0 = 1.4\,M_\odot\) and \(R_0 = 10\) km, the mean mass number \(\langle A \rangle\) is 105.7 (NL3), 108.3 (FRDM), and 104.8 (Chiral); the mean charge number \(\langle Z \rangle\) is 34.5 (NL3), 36.0 (FRDM), and 34.0 (Chiral).

## Context

This work extends the classical BPS model (Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland, 1971) by incorporating updated experimental masses from Audi, Wapstra, and Thibault (2003) and multiple modern theoretical mass tables, following earlier updates by Haensel, Zdunik, and Dobaczewski; Rüster, Hempel, and Schaffner-Bielich; and others. The results show that the outer crust mass and radius are insensitive to the nuclear model, but the composition can differ significantly due to the sequence of groundstate nuclei at high densities, which has implications for heat transport, magnetic field dissipation, and acoustic or shear-mode oscillations in neutron stars.
