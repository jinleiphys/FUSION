# The first quadrupole excitations in spherical nuclei and nuclear pairing

> Excitation energies and transition probabilities of the first 2+ excitations in even lead, tin and nickel isotopes are calculated within the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. A reasonable agreement with available experimental data is obtained. The effect of the density dependence of the effective pairing interaction is analyzed in detail by comparing results obtained with volume and surface pairing. The effect is found to be noticeable, especially for the 2+ energies which are systematically higher at 200-300 keV for the volume paring as compared with the surface pairing case, the latter being in a better agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the density dependence of the effective pairing interaction has a noticeable effect on the first 2+ excitation energies in semi-magic nuclei, with the surface pairing model yielding systematically lower energies (by 200-300 keV) than the volume pairing model and providing better agreement with experimental data, though this effect is less regular for nickel isotopes.

## Method

The authors solved the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) equations, which are essentially equivalent to QRPA, in the coordinate representation with exact continuum treatment, using the DF3-a energy density functional by Fayans et al. They compared two models of the effective pairing interaction: "surface pairing" where the pairing strength is much larger outside the nucleus, and "volume pairing" where the strength is equal inside and outside. Calculations were performed for even-even lead, tin, and nickel isotopes, excluding double magic nuclei and their immediate neighbors from the rms deviation analysis.

## Key numbers

- For tin isotopes, the rms deviation of calculated 2+ excitation energies from experiment is 0.16 MeV for surface pairing and 0.37 MeV for volume pairing
- For lead isotopes, the rms deviation is 0.33 MeV for surface pairing and 0.47 MeV for volume pairing
- The 2+ energies for volume pairing are systematically higher by 200-300 keV compared to surface pairing across all three isotopic chains
- In the benchmark nucleus 118Sn, the anomalous transition amplitudes for surface pairing are approximately 3 times stronger than for volume pairing
- The first 2+ state in 208Pb has excitation energy 4.08 MeV, while in the neighboring isotope 206Pb it decreases to 0.80 MeV
- The "developed pairing" approximation leads to errors of about 0.1 MeV in the values of the pairing gap Delta_lambda

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pairing effects on quadrupole excitations previously initiated by the same group (BE2), building on the density-dependent pairing ansatz proposed within TFFS by Sap-Tr (approximately 50 years prior) and the EDF method developed by Fayans et al. The surface pairing version was previously supported by analysis of odd-even staggering in charge radii by Fayans et al. and by microscopic pairing theory starting from realistic NN potentials by Pankr. The results are compared with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (SHFB) calculations using SkM* and SLy4 Skyrme forces by Bertsch1, where the present TFFS results show significantly better agreement for tin isotopes. The analysis provides new evidence favoring surface pairing over volume pairing, and the new experimental B(E2) data from GSI (2012) now favor the surface model where older data had favored volume pairing.
