# Forging the Link between Nuclear Reactions and Nuclear Structure

> A review of the recent applications of the dispersive optical model (DOM) is presented. Emphasis is on the nonlocal implementation of the DOM that is capable of describing ground-state properties accurately when data like the nuclear charge density are available. The DOM, conceived by Claude Mahaux, provides a unified description of both elastic nucleon scattering and structure information related to single-particle properties below the Fermi energy. We have recently introduced a nonlocal dispersive optical potential for both the real and imaginary part. Nonlocal absorptive potentials yield equivalent elastic differential cross sections for ${}^{40}$Ca as compared to local ones but change the $\ell$-dependent absorption profile suggesting important consequences for the analysis of nuclear reactions. Below the Fermi energy, nonlocality is essential for an accurate representation of particle number and the nuclear charge density. Spectral properties implied by $(e,e'p)$ and $(p,2p)$ reactions are correctly described, including the energy distribution of about 10\% high-momentum protons obtained at Jefferson Lab. The nonlocal DOM allows a complete description of experimental data both above (up to 200 MeV) and below the Fermi energy in $^{40}$Ca. It is further demonstrated that elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering data constrain the spectral strength in the continuum of orbits that are nominally bound in the independent-particle model. Extension of this analysis to $^{48}$Ca allows a prediction of the neutron skin of this nucleus that is larger than most predictions made so far.

## Key claim

The dispersive optical model (DOM), when implemented with a fully nonlocal potential for both the real and imaginary parts, can simultaneously describe elastic nucleon scattering data up to 200 MeV and ground-state properties such as charge density and particle number, provided that the nonlocality of the absorptive part is included to correctly represent the orbital-angular-momentum dependence of absorption.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator using a nonlocal self-energy whose real and imaginary parts are linked by a subtracted dispersion relation centered at the Fermi energy. The imaginary part of the self-energy is parameterized with nonlocal form factors and fitted to elastic-scattering differential cross sections, reaction cross sections, total cross sections, bound-state energies, spectroscopic factors, charge density, and particle number for ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca. The reducible self-energy is then iterated to all orders in momentum space to generate the full propagator and the particle spectral function, from which occupation and depletion numbers for individual orbits are extracted.

## Key numbers

- The nonlocal DOM describes elastic-scattering angular distributions for both n+⁴⁰Ca and p+⁴⁰Ca up to 200 MeV, maintaining the traditional good quality of DOM fits.
- The experimental charge density of ⁴⁰Ca (with 1% error bars) is reproduced accurately by the nonlocal DOM fit, which was not possible with local absorptive potentials.
- Occupation numbers for bound orbits in ⁴⁰Ca range from 0.926 (0s₁/₂) to 0.859 (0d₃/₂); depletion numbers integrated from 0 to 200 MeV range from 0.032 (0s₁/₂) to 0.097 (0d₃/₂), with sums (occupation + depletion) between 0.947 and 0.970.
- For the 0f₇/₂ orbit in ⁴⁰Ca, the occupation number is 0.046 and the depletion number from 0 to 200 MeV is 0.202; for the 0f₅/₂ orbit, occupation is 0.036 and depletion is 0.320.
- Spectroscopic factors for orbits near the Fermi surface in ⁴⁰Ca are 0.78 (1s₁/₂), 0.76 (0d₃/₂), and 0.73 (0f₇/₂); in ⁴⁸Ca, proton spectroscopic factors are 0.71, 0.65, and 0.59 respectively, while neutron spectroscopic factors are 0.83, 0.80, and 0.84 respectively.
- The nonlocal DOM analysis of ⁴⁸Ca predicts a neutron skin that is larger than most predictions made so far.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive optical model originally conceived by Mahaux and Sartor (1991) by introducing a fully nonlocal treatment of both the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy, overcoming limitations of earlier local implementations that could not accurately describe charge density or particle number. The nonlocal DOM for ⁴⁰Ca was first presented by Mahzoon et al. (2014) and is here compared with ab initio Faddeev random phase approximation (FRPA) calculations by Sethi (2011) that emphasize long-range correlations. The analysis of ⁴⁸Ca enables data-driven predictions of the neutron skin, which is relevant for the CREX parity-violating elastic scattering experiment at Jefferson Lab and for neutron star physics. The results also support the trend observed by Gade et al. (2004) that spectroscopic factors of valence orbits decrease for the minority nucleon and increase for the majority nucleon with increasing neutron-proton asymmetry.
