# Effect of magnetic field on the photon radiation from quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions

> We develop a formalism for the photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma with an external electromagnetic field. We then use it to investigate the effect of magnetic field on the photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma created in $AA$ collisions. We find that even for very optimistic assumption on the magnitude of the magnetic field generated in $AA$ collisions its effect on the photon emission rate is practically negligible. For this reason the magnetic field cannot generate a significant azimuthal asymmetry in the photon spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper finds that even under the most optimistic assumptions about the magnetic field strength generated in heavy-ion collisions, the effect of that magnetic field on the photon emission rate from the quark-gluon plasma is practically negligible, and therefore the magnetic field cannot generate a significant azimuthal asymmetry in the photon spectrum. This conclusion contradicts earlier qualitative suggestions that synchrotron radiation in a strong magnetic field could explain the direct photon puzzle.

## Method

The author develops a formalism based on the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach that treats on an even footing the effects of multiple scattering of quarks in the QGP and the curvature of quark trajectories in an external magnetic field. The formalism is applied to the collinear processes \(q\to \gamma q\) (bremsstrahlung) and \(q\bar{q}\to \gamma\) (pair annihilation), using an oscillator approximation for the scattering potential with a transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\propto T^3\). The photon emission rate is then integrated over a hydrodynamical fireball model with longitudinal expansion to compute the spectrum in \(AA\) collisions.

## Key numbers

- The transport coefficient is set to \(\hat{q}=0.2\) GeV\(^3\) at \(T=250\) MeV, which corresponds to \(\hat{q}\sim 14T^3\).
- For a magnetic field \(eB=c m_\pi^2\) with \(c\lesssim 1\), the ratio of the photon formation length to the quark Larmor radius for a \(u\) quark at \(E_q\gtrsim 1\) GeV satisfies \(L_f/R_L\lesssim 0.25 (m_\pi/T)^{3/2}\), confirming the collinear regime.
- The effective 2-volume for the integration over \(t\) and \(z\) in the fireball is \(\sim 5-10\) fm\(^2\), which is a factor of \(\sim 10-20\) smaller than in the earlier work of T1 (2015) that used a static QGP approximation.

## Context

This work extends the LCPI formalism previously used by Zakharov (2007) to derive the AMY photon emission rates for zero magnetic field, now incorporating an external electromagnetic field. It directly contradicts the qualitative analysis of T1 (2015), which suggested that synchrotron radiation from thermal quarks in a strong magnetic field could produce a significant photon azimuthal asymmetry comparable to the observed yield. The present results show that even with optimistic magnetic field magnitudes for RHIC and LHC conditions, the synchrotron contribution is negligible, and the direct photon puzzle remains unresolved. The analysis also addresses the overestimation issues in T1 (2015) due to neglect of longitudinal expansion, use of current quark masses instead of thermal quasiparticle masses, and failure to account for multiple scattering suppression of the synchrotron emission.
