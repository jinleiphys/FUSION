# Investigations on the equation of state of neutron star matter with density-dependent relativistic mean-field model

> The compact object with a mass of $2.50-2.67~M_\odot$ observed by LIGO Scientific and Virgo collaborations in GW190814, as well as the recent report of a light compact object with a mass and radius of $M=0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}M_{\odot}$ and $R=10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$ km within the supernova remnant HESS J1731-347, have posed a great challenge to the investigations into the supranuclear matter. In the inner core region of the neutron star, the strangeness degrees of freedom, such as the hyperons, can be present, which is also named as a hyperonic star. In this work, the neutron star consisting of nucleons and leptons, and the hyperonic star including the hyperons will be studied in the framework of the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) model. Some popular DDRMF parameterizations will be adopted to investigate the properties of nuclear matter and the mass, radius, tidal deformability, and other properties of neutron star and hyperonic stars. We find that the maximum masses of neutron star calculated by DD-MEX, DD-MEX1, DD-MEX2, DD-MEXY and DD-LZ1 sets can be around $2.5-2.6~M_\odot$ with quite stiff equations of state (EOSs) generated by their strong repulsive contributions from vector potentials at high densities. Moreover, by investigating the influence of the crust EOS and core EOS on the neutron stars, we find that the observational data from HESS J1731-347 suggest the requirement of a crust EOS with a higher $L$ parameter and a core EOS with a lower $L$ parameter, and the $M-R$ relations from the constructed EOSs can also be consistent with the observables of PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451 from NICER and the GW170817 event. With the inclusion of hyperons, the hyperonic star matter becomes softer compared to the neutron star matter. But the massive hyperonic star can also be obtained with DDRMF parameter sets if the vector coupling constants are strong.

## Key claim

The paper claims that several density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) parameter sets can produce neutron star maximum masses around 2.5–2.6 solar masses, consistent with the GW190814 secondary object, but only if the equations of state are sufficiently stiff due to strong repulsive vector potentials at high densities; the inclusion of hyperons softens the matter, yet massive hyperonic stars remain possible if vector coupling constants are strong.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff (TOV) equations using equations of state generated by the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) model for both nucleon-lepton matter and hyperonic matter including the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) and leptons (e, μ). They adopt multiple DDRMF parameter sets (DD-LZ1, DD-MEX, DD-MEX1, DD-MEX2, DD-MEXY, DD-ME2, DD-ME1, DD2, PKDD, TW99, DDV, DDVT, DDVTD) and enforce β-equilibrium and charge neutrality to compute mass, radius, and tidal deformability; for hyperonic stars, meson-baryon couplings are fixed via SU(6) symmetry relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses from DD-MEX, DD-MEX1, DD-MEX2, DD-MEXY, and DD-LZ1 sets are around 2.5–2.6 M⊙.
- The light compact object HESS J1731-347 has reported mass M = 0.77⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₁₇ M⊙ and radius R = 10.4⁺⁰·⁸⁶₋₀·₇₈ km.
- The symmetry energy slope L from DD-MEX2 is 86.8 MeV; from DDVT it is 42.3 MeV; from DD-LZ1 it is 42.5 MeV.
- The saturation density ρB0 for DD-LZ1 is 0.1581 fm⁻³; for DD-MEX it is 0.1519 fm⁻³.
- The incompressibility K0 for DD-MEXY is 367.9 MeV; for DD-LZ1 it is 231.1 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at saturation Esym for DD-MEX2 is 35.3 MeV; for DD-LZ1 it is 31.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous DDRMF studies of neutron stars (Huang 2020, Huang 2022) and addresses the "hyperon puzzle" raised by two-solar-mass pulsars such as PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2020, Miller 2021) and the GW190814 secondary (Abbott 2020). It also confronts the low-mass object HESS J1731-347 (Doroshenko 2022), finding that a crust EOS with a higher L parameter and a core EOS with a lower L parameter are needed to match its mass-radius data. The results enable future combined constraints from NICER, gravitational-wave events, and hypernuclei data to refine the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the hyperon coupling strengths.
