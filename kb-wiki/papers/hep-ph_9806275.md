# Properties of $N^*$(1535) at Finite Density in the Extended Parity-Doublet Models

> We improve so called ``naive'' and ``mirror'' models for the positive and negative parity nucleons, $N$ and $N^{*}$, by introducing nonlinear terms allowed by chiral symmetry. Both models in this improvement reproduce the observed nucleon axial charge in free space and reveal interesting density dependence of the axial charges for $N$ and $N^*$, and the doublet masses. A remarkable difference between the two models is found in the off-diagonal axial charge, $g_{ANN^*}$, which could appear either as suppression or as enhancement of $N^* \to πN$ decay in the medium.

# Properties of $N^*$(1535) at Finite Density in the Extended Parity-Doublet Models

## Key claim

The two chiral assignments for the negative-parity nucleon $N^*(1535)$, the "naive" and "mirror" models, can be minimally extended with nonlinear terms to reproduce the observed nucleon axial charge in free space, and they predict opposite density dependence for the off-diagonal axial charge $g_{ANN^*}$, which could appear either as suppression (naive model) or enhancement (mirror model) of $N^* \to \pi N$ decay in nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors extend the original naive and mirror linear sigma models for the nucleon parity doublet ($N$ and $N^*$) by adding chiral-symmetric nonlinear terms containing two meson fields and derivatives, then apply nonlinear transformations to eliminate pseudoscalar pion couplings in favor of pseudovector couplings. They perform a Hartree calculation of the chiral condensate $\sigma_0$ as a function of nuclear density, using the resulting density dependence to compute the doublet masses and axial charges in the medium.

## Key numbers

- $f_{\pi NN^*} = 1.17$ GeV$^{-1}$ (off-diagonal pseudovector coupling), obtained from $\Gamma^{exp}_{\pi N} \sim 70$ MeV for $N^* \to \pi N$ decay
- $f_{\pi NN} = 6.77$ GeV$^{-1}$ (diagonal pseudovector coupling), yielding $g_{ANN} = 1.26$ in free space
- $g_{ANN^*} = 0.217$ in free space (off-diagonal axial charge)
- $\sigma_0 = 93$ MeV (pion decay constant, vacuum expectation value)
- $M_+ = 0.939$ GeV (nucleon mass), $M_- = 1.535$ GeV ($N^*$ mass)
- $m_0 = 0.27$ GeV (mirror model parameter from DeTar and Kunihiro); authors also use $m_0 = 0.5$ GeV and $m_0 = 0.7$ GeV
- At normal nuclear density $\rho_0$, $g_{ANN}$ is quenched by 8% in the naive model
- In the mirror model with $m_0 = 0.3$ GeV, $g_{ANN^*}$ increases to 0.294 at $\rho_0$; in the naive model it decreases to 0.15 at $\rho_0$ (about a factor of 2 smaller)
- $\pi N$-sigma term $\Sigma_{\pi N} \sim 100$ MeV (naive case) and $\sim 80$ MeV (mirror case with $m_0 = 0.5$ GeV); with $m_0 = 700$ MeV, $\Sigma_{\pi N} \sim 60$ MeV
- $N^* \to \eta N$ decay becomes kinematically forbidden above $\rho/\rho_0 \sim 0.3$

## Context

This work extends the parity-doublet models of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989) for the mirror assignment and Jido et al. (1998) for the naive assignment, which previously could not simultaneously reproduce the observed nucleon axial charge $g_{ANN} = 1.26$ and the $\pi NN^*$ coupling. The extension follows the modified linear sigma model approach of Akhmedov (1989) to achieve axial charge greater than unity. The results enable experimental discrimination between the two chiral assignments by measuring $N^*$ decay properties in nuclei, particularly $\pi N$ decay, and provide an alternative explanation for the observed surface-area scaling of $\eta$ production in nuclei (Krusche et al., 1995) through the suppression of $N^* \to \eta N$ decay in the medium.
