# Quasi-static probes of the QCD plasma

> Screening correlators and masses were studied at finite temperature in QCD with two flavours of dynamical staggered quarks on a lattice. The spectrum of screening masses show a hierarchical approach to chiral symmetry restoration. Control of explicit chiral symmetry breaking through the quark mass was shown to be an important step to understanding this phenomenon. No sign of decays was found in the finite temperature scalar meson-like correlators in the confined phase.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral symmetry restoration in screening correlators occurs at different temperatures depending on the meson channel, with the scalar/pseudoscalar sector showing restoration only above approximately 1.33 T_c, while the vector/axial-vector sector shows effective restoration immediately above T_c, due to the explicit chiral symmetry breaking from the non-zero quark mass (m_pi ~ 230 MeV).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavours of dynamical staggered quarks on N_t=6 lattices, measuring meson screening correlation functions projected to zero momentum at temperatures from 0.89 T_c to 1.92 T_c. They extracted screening masses using correlated doubled-parity fits to the spatial correlators, and studied chiral symmetry restoration by examining chiral projections of the correlators and computing correlated chi-squared tests for their vanishing. The valence quark mass dependence was investigated by varying m_val while keeping the sea quark mass fixed.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass at zero temperature: m_pi ~ 230 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 1/(6T) (N_t = 6)
- Aspect ratios: zeta = N_s/N_t between 2 and 4 for main results, with additional finite volume study at T = 0.94 T_c using 4/3 <= zeta <= 5
- PS screening mass at T = 0.89 T_c: mu_1/T = 2.27(2)
- PS screening mass at T = 1.92 T_c: mu_1/T = 4.93(3)
- S screening mass at T = 1.92 T_c: mu_1/T = 5.1(6)
- Vt screening mass at T = 1.92 T_c: mu_1/T = 9.4(3)
- AVt screening mass at T = 1.92 T_c: mu_1/T = 6.1(2)
- Extrapolated mass difference at T = 1.21 T_c for m_val = 0: (mu_S - mu_PS)/T = 0.46 +/- 0.03
- Chiral symmetry restoration temperature for S/PS sector: T = 1.33 T_c (based on chi^2 values: chi^2_S = 13.5 at T = 1.33 T_c, chi^2_S = 441 at T = 1.21 T_c)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of hadron-like screening masses in QCD with dynamical quarks, including studies with 2 flavours of staggered quarks (Gavai et al., 2005) and with 2+1 flavours of p4 improved quarks (HotQCD collaboration), by reaching finer lattice spacings comparable to quenched overlap valence quark studies (Gavai et al., 2009). The finding that chiral symmetry restoration in screening masses occurs at different temperatures for different channels, with the scalar/pseudoscalar sector requiring T ~ 1.33 T_c, has implications for understanding the chiral crossover and for the applicability of dimensional reduction in high-temperature QCD. The results also suggest that in the real world with physical quark masses, chiral symmetry breaking in screening masses should not be visible above T_c.
