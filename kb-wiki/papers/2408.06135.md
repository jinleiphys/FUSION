# Nonperturbative four-gluon vertex in soft kinematics

> We present a nonperturbative study of the form factor associated with the projection of the full four-gluon vertex on its classical tensor, for a set of kinematics with one vanishing and three arbitrary external momenta. The treatment is based on the Schwinger-Dyson equation governing this vertex, and a large-volume lattice simulation, involving ten thousand gauge field configurations. The key hypothesis employed in both approaches is the ``planar degeneracy'', which classifies diverse configurations by means of a single variable, thus enabling their meaningful ``averaging''. The results of both approaches show notable agreement, revealing a considerable suppression of the averaged form factor in the infrared. The deviations from the exact planar degeneracy are discussed in detail, and a supplementary variable is used to achieve a more accurate description. The effective charge defined through this special form factor is computed within both approaches, and the results obtained are in excellent agreement.

## Key claim

The four-gluon vertex form factor in soft kinematics (one vanishing momentum) exhibits approximate "planar degeneracy," meaning it depends primarily on the single variable \(s^2 = (q^2+r^2+p^2)/2\) rather than on the full kinematic configuration, though deviations from exact degeneracy are quantified and a supplementary variable improves the description.

## Method

The authors combined two nonperturbative approaches in Landau gauge: a Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) derived from the four-loop 4PI effective action, and a large-volume quenched lattice simulation with 10,000 gauge field configurations at six \(\beta\) values (5.6 to 6.4) on \(32^4\) lattices. Both methods employed the planar degeneracy hypothesis to average over kinematic configurations with the same \(s^2\), and the SDE was solved iteratively until the relative error between consecutive averages fell below 0.1%. The 1PR contributions were subtracted from lattice data using known three-gluon vertex form factors.

## Key numbers

- The SDE iteration converged when the relative error between consecutive averages \(\overline{D}(s^2)\) was smaller than 0.1%.
- The renormalization point was set at \(\mu = 4.3\) GeV for both SDE and lattice analyses.
- The SDE used a grid of 491 kinematic configurations on the \((x,y)\) disk, with \(s^2\) sampled logarithmically over \([10^{-4}, 10^4]~\text{GeV}^2\).
- The strong coupling was taken as \(\alpha_s(\mu^2) = 0.27\).
- The lattice simulation used 10,000 configurations: \(\beta=5.6\) (2,000), 5.7 (1,000), 5.8 (2,000), 6.0 (2,000), 6.2 (2,000), and 6.4 (1,000) for \(L/a=32\).

## Context

This work extends prior nonperturbative studies of the four-gluon vertex that were restricted to collinear kinematics (Binosi 2014, Huber 2020, Aguilar 2024) by considering a wider set of soft-kinematic configurations. The planar degeneracy concept was previously explored for the three-gluon vertex (Eichmann 2014, Pinto-Gomez 2022, Aguilar 2023). The results enable improved modeling of the four-gluon interaction in continuum QCD studies and provide a benchmark for future lattice calculations of higher-point functions.
