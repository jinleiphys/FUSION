# Chiral unitary theory: application to nuclear problems

> In this talk we briefly describe some basic elements of chiral perturbation theory, $χPT$, and how the implementation of unitarity and other novel elements lead to a better expansion of the $T$ matrix for meson meson and meson baryon interactions. Applications are then done to the $ ππ$ interaction in nuclear matter in the scalar and vector channels, antikaons in nuclei and $K^-$ atoms, and how the $ϕ$ meson properties are changed in a nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper argues that combining chiral perturbation theory with exact unitarity and coupled-channel methods (the "chiral unitary approach") extends the range of applicability of chiral Lagrangians to energies where meson resonances appear, and that this framework, when combined with standard many-body techniques, provides a systematic tool for studying meson properties in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors use chiral Lagrangians up to fourth order in derivatives for meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions, then unitarize the amplitudes by expanding the inverse T-matrix rather than the T-matrix itself, using either the inverse amplitude method or the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorization. For nuclear-medium applications, they introduce medium modifications by renormalizing pion and kaon propagators, including vertex corrections, and implementing self-consistent treatments of the kaon selfenergy with Pauli blocking and baryon mean-field potentials.

## Key numbers

- The ρ meson mass shifts downward by about 50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (ρ = ρ₀), while its width becomes much broader.
- The total width of the φ meson at ρ = ρ₀ is about 22 MeV, nearly an order of magnitude larger than the free width.
- The K⁻-deuteron scattering length from the full Faddeev calculation in the physical basis is (−1.615 + i 1.909) fm.
- The theoretical K⁻-nucleus potential at ρ = ρ₀ gives an attractive potential of about 40 MeV and a width of about 100 MeV for kaons close to threshold.
- The f₀(980) resonance in nuclear medium at densities close to normal nuclear matter density acquires a width as large as 100 MeV or more.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by the authors and others (e.g., the inverse amplitude method of IAM, the Bethe-Salpeter approach of Nieves and collaborators, and the explicit-resonance method of NSD) to nuclear-medium problems. It builds on earlier studies of ππ in matter by Schuck and collaborators and of kaon selfenergies by Koch, Waas, and Weise, as well as the self-consistent treatment of Lutz. The results enable predictions for kaonic atoms, φ meson decay in nuclei, and the possibility of kaon condensates in neutron stars, while also providing experimental tests through K⁻-deuteron scattering length measurements (underway at Frascati) and φ photoproduction at Jefferson Lab.
