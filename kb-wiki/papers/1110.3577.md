# Ph.D. Thesis: Chiral Effective Field Theory Beyond the Power-Counting Regime

> Novel techniques are presented, which identify the power-counting regime (PCR) of chiral effective field theory, and allow the use of lattice quantum chromodynamics results that extend outside the PCR. By analyzing the renormalization of low-energy coefficients of the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass, the existence of an optimal regularization scale is realized. The techniques developed for the nucleon mass renormalization are then applied to a test case: performing a chiral extrapolation without prior phenomenological bias. The robustness of the procedure for obtaining an optimal regularization scale and performing a reliable chiral extrapolation is confirmed. The procedure developed is then applied to the magnetic moment and the electric charge radius of the nucleon. The consistency of the results for the value of the optimal regularization scale provides strong evidence for the existence of an intrinsic energy scale in the nucleon-pion interaction.

## Key claim

The thesis demonstrates that an optimal regularization scale exists for chiral effective field theory extrapolations of lattice QCD data, which allows reliable use of simulation results that lie outside the power-counting regime where higher-order terms would normally be considered negligible.

## Method

The author analyzed the renormalization of low-energy coefficients in the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass across multiple renormalization schemes, identifying the power-counting regime where extrapolation results become scheme-independent. The technique was then applied to the quenched rho meson mass as a blind test case without prior phenomenological bias, and subsequently extended to the magnetic moment and electric charge radius of the isovector nucleon using lattice QCD data from JLQCD, PACS-CS, CP-PACS, and QCDSF collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass from JLQCD at lattice spacing 0.118 fm and spatial length 1.90 fm: ranges from 1.615(6) GeV at m_pi^2 = 0.567 GeV^2 to 1.111(10) GeV at m_pi^2 = 0.084 GeV^2
- Nucleon mass from PACS-CS at lattice spacing 0.0907 fm and spatial length 2.90 fm: ranges from 1.583(5) GeV at m_pi^2 = 0.492 GeV^2 to 0.932(78) GeV at m_pi^2 = 0.024 GeV^2
- Nucleon mass from CP-PACS: ranges from 1.809(15) GeV at m_pi^2 = 0.940 GeV^2 (lattice spacing 0.102 fm, L = 2.45 fm) to 1.275(7) GeV at m_pi^2 = 0.272 GeV^2 (lattice spacing 0.111 fm, L = 2.66 fm)
- Quenched rho meson mass on 20^3 x 32 lattice with spacing 0.153 fm: ranges from 2.001(1) GeV at m_pi^2 = 3.150 GeV^2 to 0.913(32) GeV at m_pi^2 = 0.041 GeV^2
- Isovector nucleon magnetic moment from QCDSF preliminary data: ranges from 2.394(69) mu_N at m_pi^2 = 0.863 GeV^2 to 3.711(158) mu_N at m_pi^2 = 0.077 GeV^2
- Isovector nucleon electric charge radius from QCDSF preliminary data: ranges from 0.303(8) fm^2 at m_pi^2 = 0.591 GeV^2 to 0.586(24) fm^2 at m_pi^2 = 0.053 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory framework developed by Gasser and Leutwyler and later applied to lattice QCD by various groups including Beane and Savage, by providing a systematic method to identify when lattice data lies within the power-counting regime. The consistency of the optimal regularization scale across the nucleon mass, magnetic moment, and electric charge radius provides evidence for an intrinsic energy scale in the nucleon-pion interaction, enabling more reliable chiral extrapolations from lattice QCD simulations at larger-than-physical quark masses.
