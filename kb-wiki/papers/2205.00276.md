# Rate and ellipticity of dilepton production in a magnetized quark-gluon plasma

> Using the Landau-level representation for the imaginary part of the photon polarization tensor, we derive an explicit expression for the dilepton production rate from a hot quark-gluon plasma in a quantizing background magnetic field. We study in detail the dependence of the production rate on the dilepton invariant mass and the transverse momentum at mid-rapidity. We also investigate the angular dependence and ellipticity of dilepton emission. By comparing the result with the zero-field Born approximation, we find that the magnetic field leads to a strong enhancement of the dilepton rate at small values of the invariant mass ($M\lesssim\sqrt{|eB|}$). In the same kinematic region, the dilepton production is characterized by a sizable ellipticity. At large values of the dilepton invariant mass ($M\gtrsim\sqrt{|eB|}$), the role of the magnetic field decreases and the result approaches the isotropic zero-field Born rate. By investigating the dependence of ellipticity on the transverse momentum, we argue that the future measurements of dilepton rate in the region of small invariant masses can constrain the magnetic field produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The magnetic field causes a strong enhancement of the dilepton rate at small invariant masses (M less than about the square root of |eB|) and produces a sizable ellipticity, but the effect diminishes at large invariant masses where the rate approaches the isotropic zero-field Born result; the authors argue that future measurements of the dilepton rate in the small-invariant-mass region can constrain the magnetic field produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Landau-level representation for the imaginary part of the photon polarization tensor, derived in earlier work (Wang 2020, Wang 2021), to obtain an explicit expression for the dilepton production rate from a hot quark-gluon plasma in a quantizing background magnetic field. They treated the final lepton states as plane waves rather than Landau-level states, justified by the long mean free path of leptons compared to the plasma fireball size. The rate was calculated numerically as a sum over quark Landau levels up to a cutoff of n_max = 1000, for two magnetic field strengths (|eB| = m_pi^2 and 5 m_pi^2) and two temperatures (T = 0.2 GeV and 0.35 GeV), and the angular dependence was analyzed to extract the ellipticity parameter v_2.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field values used were |eB| = m_pi^2 ≈ (0.135 GeV)^2, corresponding to B ≈ 3.08 × 10^18 G, and |eB| = 5 m_pi^2, corresponding to B ≈ 1.54 × 10^19 G.
- The quark mass was set to m = 5 MeV for both up and down quarks.
- The temperature values used were T = 0.2 GeV and T = 0.35 GeV.
- The Landau-level sum was truncated at n_max = 1000, covering energies from about Omega_min = 0.02 GeV to Omega_max = 2 GeV.
- The invariant mass range studied was from M_min = 0.02 GeV to M_max = 1 GeV with step Delta M = 0.01 GeV.
- The transverse momentum values used in the figures were k_T = 0, 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, and 1.0 GeV.
- The analytical approximation for the rate at k_T = 0 and M much less than sqrt(|eB|) is given by Eq. (rate_conductivity_kT0-small-M), which scales as |e_f B|^3 / M^6.
- The maximum of the rate at k_T = 0 occurs near M_max ≈ |e_d B| / (6T), and the rate there is a factor of order (3T)^6 / |e_d B|^3 larger than the zero-field limit.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production in magnetized quark-gluon plasma, which used approximations such as the equivalent photon flux approximation (Tuchin 2013), lowest Landau level approximation (Bandyopadhyay 2016, Islam 2018), weak field limit (Bandyopadhyay 2017, Das 2019), Ritus method (Sadooghi 2016), and real-time formalism (Ghosh 2018). It most closely overlaps with the work of Das (2021) but adds the calculation of the angular dependence and ellipticity of the dilepton emission. The results enable future experimental measurements of dilepton rates at small invariant masses to serve as a magnetometer for the magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions, complementing the traditional use of dileptons as a thermometer of the quark-gluon plasma (Rapp 2014).
