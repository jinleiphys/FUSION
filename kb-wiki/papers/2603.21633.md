# Bayesian extraction of TMC-free collectivity in p+p and p+Pb collisions at the LHC

> A central challenge in understanding the origin of collective flow-like signatures in small collision systems calls for a reliable method to disentangle genuine collective flow from substantial background correlations, especially those arising from transverse momentum conservation (TMC). A Bayesian inference framework is developed to integrate TMC calculations with the LHC-ATLAS data on long-range multiparticle azimuthal correlation observables, thereby extracting genuine collective flow in small systems. Our analysis indicates that while the genuine elliptic and triangular flow ($v_{2}$ and $v_{3}$) are similar, the $p$+$p$ and $p$+Pb systems exhibit distinct TMC backgrounds, TMC-flow interplay, and $v_{2}$--$v_{3}$ correlations. We demonstrate that the genuine $v_{2}$ and $v_{3}$ are well described by the measured four-particle $v_2\{4\}$ and two-particle $v_3\{2\}$ in $p$+Pb collisions, whereas these measurements systematically underestimate the genuine flow in $p$+$p$ collisions, due to competing contributions from TMC effects. This establishes a robust and data-driven approach, providing renewed theoretical insight into collective behavior free from TMC contamination in small collision systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that genuine elliptic and triangular flow coefficients ($v_2$ and $v_3$) can be extracted free from transverse momentum conservation (TMC) background using Bayesian inference, but the extracted genuine flow values are only well described by standard experimental cumulant measurements in p+Pb collisions; in p+p collisions, the standard measurements systematically underestimate the genuine flow due to competing TMC effects.

## Method

The authors developed a Bayesian inference framework that integrates analytic TMC calculations with four experimental observables from ATLAS: the four-particle elliptic cumulant $c_2\{4\}$, the two- and four-particle triangular cumulants $c_3\{2\}$ and $c_3\{4\}$, and the four-particle symmetric cumulant $sc_{2,3}\{4\}$. They performed Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling using the No-U-Turn Sampler to infer posterior distributions of the flow parameters $v_2$, $v_3$, and the ratio $r = p^2 / \langle p^2 \rangle_F$, first as constants and then with a power-law dependence on charged-particle multiplicity $N_{\text{ch}}$.

## Key numbers

* In p+p collisions, the constant-parameter maximum a posteriori values were $v_2 = 0.0416^{\,+\,0.0005}_{\,-\,0.0006}$, $v_3 = 0.0001^{\,+\,0.0007}_{\,-\,0.0007}$, and $r = 1.5434^{\,+\,0.0240}_{\,-\,0.0569}$ GeV/$c$.
* In p+Pb collisions, the constant-parameter maximum a posteriori values were $v_2 = 0.0482^{\,+\,0.0002}_{\,-\,0.0001}$, $v_3 = 0.0153^{\,+\,0.0003}_{\,-\,0.0002}$, and $r = 2.0559^{\,+\,0.0740}_{\,-\,0.0554}$ GeV/$c$.
* The model results were constrained by experimental data up to $N_{\text{ch}} = 160$ for p+p and up to $N_{\text{ch}} = 300$ for p+Pb collisions.
* The relation between the TMC particle number $N$ and charged-particle multiplicity was taken as $N = (3/2) N_{\text{ch}}$ based on isospin symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the TMC theoretical framework developed by the same group (Guo et al., prior papers TMC1-TMC5) which showed that TMC effects can explain sign changes in cumulants and symmetric cumulants in small systems. The Bayesian approach enables systematic uncertainty quantification, similar to methods previously applied to extract the specific shear viscosity (BAY1-BAY8) and the equation of state (BAY9-BAY10) in heavy-ion physics. The results enable future studies of genuine collective flow in small collision systems without contamination from TMC backgrounds, and provide a data-driven method to test whether the same flow mechanism operates in p+p and p+Pb collisions.
