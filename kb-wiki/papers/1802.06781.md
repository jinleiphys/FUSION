# Theoretical analysis on the $K^{-} {}^{3} \text{He} \to Λp n$ reaction for the $\bar{K} N N$ bound-state search in the J-PARC E15 experiment

> We theoretically analyze the $K^{-} {}^{3} \text{He} \to Λp n$ reaction for the $\bar{K} N N$ bound-state search in the J-PARC E15 experiment. We find that, by detecting a fast and forward neutron in the final state, an almost on-shell $\bar{K}$ is guaranteed, which is essential to make a bound state with two nucleons from ${}^{3} \text{He}$. Then, this almost on-shell $\bar{K}$ can bring a signal of the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state in the $Λp$ invariant-mass spectrum, although it inevitably brings a kinematic peak above the $\bar{K} N N$ threshold as well. As a consequence, we predict two peaks across the $\bar{K} N N$ threshold in the spectrum: the lower peak coming from the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state, and the higher one originating from the kinematics.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $\Lambda p$ invariant-mass spectrum from the $K^{-} {}^{3} \text{He} \to \Lambda p n$ reaction will show two peaks across the $\bar{K} N N$ threshold: a lower peak from a genuine $\bar{K} N N$ bound state and a higher peak from kinematics, because the requirement of a fast forward neutron forces the intermediate $\bar{K}$ to be almost on-shell, which inevitably produces a kinematic peak above threshold even without bound-state dynamics.

## Method

The authors model the reaction as a two-step process: first, the incoming $K^{-}$ (momentum $1 \text{ GeV}/c$) scatters quasielastically off a neutron in ${}^{3} \text{He}$, producing a fast forward neutron and a slow $\bar{K}$; second, this slow $\bar{K}$ propagates and is absorbed by the remaining two nucleons. The first-step amplitude is constrained to reproduce experimental $K^{-} n \to K^{-} n$ and $K^{-} p \to \bar{K}^{0} n$ cross sections. The multiple $\bar{K}$ scattering with two nucleons is treated in the fixed center approximation, including a phenomenological two-nucleon absorption width for the $\bar{K}$, to generate the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state and its transition to $\Lambda p$.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar{K} N N$ bound-state pole position is found at $2354 - 36 i \text{ MeV}$.
- The $\bar{K} N N$ threshold is $2.37 \text{ GeV}$.
- The initial kaon momentum is $1 \text{ GeV}/c$.
- The lower tail of the experimental $\Lambda p$ invariant-mass spectrum near $\sim 2.3 \text{ GeV}$ is reproduced by the $\bar{K} N N$ bound-state contribution.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of kaonic nuclei, specifically the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state (the simplest kaonic nucleus, also called the "$K^{-} p p$" state), whose existence remains controversial as reviewed by Nagae (2016). The analysis directly addresses the J-PARC E15 experiment (Hashimoto 2014, Sada 2016), which observed a peak structure near the $K^{-} p p$ threshold in the $K^{-} {}^{3} \text{He} \to \Lambda p n$ reaction. The authors' fixed center approximation approach (following Bayar 2011, 2012) enables them to distinguish the bound-state signal from a kinematic peak, and they note that the predicted two-peak structure is consistent with higher-statistics data from the E15 second run (Iwasaki 2017).
