# Spectral functions and critical dynamics of the $O(4)$ model from classical-statistical lattice simulations

> We calculate spectral functions of the relativistic $O(4)$ model from real-time lattice simulations in classical-statistical field theory. While in the low and high temperature phase of the model, the spectral functions of longitudinal $(σ)$ and transverse $(π)$ modes are well described by relativistic quasi-particle peaks, we find a highly non-trivial behavior of the spectral functions in the cross over region, where additional structures appear. Similarly, we observe a significant broadening of the quasi-particle peaks, when the amount explicit $O(4)$ symmetry breaking is reduced. We further demonstrate that in the vicinity of the $O(4)$ critical point, the spectral functions develop an infrared power law associated with the critical dynamics, and comment on the extraction of the dynamical critical exponent $z$ from our simulations.

## Key claim

The spectral functions of the relativistic O(4) model exhibit highly non-trivial behavior in the crossover region, with additional structures beyond simple quasi-particle peaks, and develop an infrared power law near the critical point; however, the classical-statistical approximation used is strictly justifiable only at very high temperatures or in the vicinity of the critical point, so results away from criticality provide qualitative rather than quantitative insights.

## Method

Classical-statistical lattice simulations in 3+1 dimensions were performed using a Langevin prescription to generate thermal ensembles of field configurations for the O(4) model with Hamiltonian discretized on a cubic lattice. Unequal-time correlation functions were computed by evolving classical field configurations via Hamilton's equations of motion using a leap-frog scheme, and spectral functions at zero momentum were extracted from the time derivative of the statistical two-point function via the classical fluctuation-dissipation relation. The longitudinal (sigma) and transverse (pi) components were distinguished by introducing an explicit O(4) symmetry breaking term J.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at J=0: T_c = 17.3925(10), determined from finite-size scaling of the order parameter on lattices with L=48, 64, 96, 128
- Critical exponents used from Engels (2014): beta = 0.380(2), nu = 0.7377(41), gamma = 1.4531(104), delta = 4.824(9)
- Critical Binder cumulant at T_c: chi_4 = 0.63 ± 0.01
- Pseudo-critical transition temperature at J=0.05: T_pc ≈ 19.5
- Maximum evolution time for spectral function calculations: 10^4 to 10^5 a_s (lattice spacing units)
- Ensemble size for spectral functions: N_conf = 32 independent configurations
- Lattice size for spectral function calculations: L=64 (unless stated otherwise)
- Langevin update step: a_t/a_s = 0.01; leap-frog step for real-time evolution: a_t/a_s = 0.05

## Context

This work extends the classical-statistical approach previously applied to a single-component scalar field theory in 2+1 dimensions by Aarts (2001) and Berges (2009), which verified that theory belongs to the Model C dynamic universality class of Hohenberg and Halperin. The O(4) model is of particular relevance as an effective theory for low-energy QCD, since the chiral phase transition for two degenerate light-quark flavors is believed to be in the same O(4) universality class as established by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) and Rajagopal (1993). The results provide qualitative input for non-perturbative functional methods such as n-PI, Functional Renormalization Group, and Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches, which require truncations and benefit from insights into the structure of excitations.
