# Heavy flavour suppression and flow

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of charm and bottom quarks propagating through quark gluon plasma have been evaluated for conditions relevant to nuclear collisions at Large Hadron Collider and Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Both the radiative and collisional processes of energy loss are included in evaluating the {\it effective} drag and diffusion coefficients. The Landau-Pomeronchuk-Migdal (LPM) and the dead cone effects on the radiative energy loss of heavy quarks have been included. With the effective transport coefficients the Fokker Plank (FP) equation has been solved for the evolution of heavy quarks in QGP. The solution of the FP equation has been used to evaluate the nuclear suppression factor, $R_{\mathrm AA}$ and the elliptic flow, $v_2^{HF}$ for the non-photonic single electron spectra resulting from the semi-leptonic decays of hadrons containing charm and bottom quarks. It is observed that the experimental data from RHIC on $R_{\mathrm AA}$ and $v_2^{HF} $ can be reproduced simultaneously within the pQCD framework for the same set of inputs.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the experimental data from RHIC on both the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{\mathrm AA}\) and the elliptic flow \(v_2^{HF}\) for non-photonic single electrons can be reproduced simultaneously within the pQCD framework using the same set of inputs, but this agreement depends on including both collisional and radiative energy loss processes with the Landau-Pomeronchuk-Migdal (LPM) and dead cone effects.

## Method
The drag and diffusion coefficients for charm and bottom quarks in quark-gluon plasma were evaluated using pQCD, including both collisional and radiative energy loss processes with LPM and dead cone effects. These transport coefficients were used as inputs to the Fokker-Planck equation to evolve the heavy quark momentum distributions, which were then convoluted with Peterson fragmentation functions to obtain D and B meson spectra. The resulting meson distributions were decayed semi-leptonically to produce non-photonic single electron spectra, from which \(R_{\mathrm AA}\) and \(v_2^{HF}\) were computed; the background medium evolution was treated with (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature of 400 MeV and thermalization time of 0.2 fm/c were used for RHIC conditions (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV).
- Initial temperature of 700 MeV and thermalization time of 0.08 fm/c were used for LHC predictions (0-10% centrality).
- The experimental data from STAR and PHENIX show \(R_{AA}<1\) for \(p_T\geq 2\) GeV at RHIC, indicating substantial interaction of heavy quarks with the plasma.
- Lowering the velocity of sound \(c_s\) gives more suppression in the LHC predictions for \(R_{\mathrm AA}\).

## Context
This work extends the Fokker-Planck approach to heavy quark transport by including both collisional and radiative energy loss with LPM and dead cone effects, building on prior work by Das and collaborators (Das3, Das4). It enables simultaneous description of both \(R_{\mathrm AA}\) and \(v_2^{HF}\) with the same input parameters, addressing a challenge noted in earlier studies such as Rapp and collaborators (rappv2). The results provide predictions for LHC measurements and show sensitivity to the equation of state through the velocity of sound.
