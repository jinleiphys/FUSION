# Predicting hidden bottom molecular tetraquarks with a complex scaling method

> In present work, we perform a coupled-channel analysis of $B^{(*)}_{(s)}\bar{B}^{(*)}_{(s)}$ systems with the one-boson-exchange potentials. We first study the $I(J^{PC})=1(1^{+-})$ $B\bar{B}^{*}/B^{*}\bar{B}^{*}$ system to describe the $Z_{b}(10610)$ and $Z_{b}(10650)$ particles as molecular states and determine the reasonable range of cutoff parameter $Λ$. Then, other $B^{(*)}_{(s)}\bar{B}^{(*)}_{(s)}$ combinations with different quantum numbers are systematically investigated. Some bound states and resonances appear in the isoscalar systems, while only several shallow bound states exist for isovector systems. Far away from the excited conventional $P-$wave bottomium, these predicted states can be easily identified as exotic particles both theoretically and experimentally. Moreover, the $η_b(nS)/Υ(nS)$ plus light mesons are the excellent final states to search for the bound states, while the $B\bar B^*+h.c.$ and $B^* \bar B^*$ channels are suitable for the resonances. We highly recommend that the LHCb and Belle II Collaborations can hunt for these bottomonium-like states in future.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ can be described as molecular states in a coupled-channel analysis with a cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ in the range 950–1100 MeV, but the authors note that whether these states lie above or below their respective thresholds remains an open question and depends on the parametrization scheme.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel analysis of $B^{(*)}_{(s)}\bar{B}^{(*)}_{(s)}$ systems using one-boson-exchange potentials derived from effective Lagrangians satisfying heavy quark and chiral symmetries. They solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method and the complex scaling method, first calibrating the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ by reproducing the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ as molecular states in the $I(J^{PC})=1(1^{+-})$ system, then systematically investigating other quantum number combinations.

## Key numbers

- $Z_b(10610)$ mass: $10607.2^{+2}_{-2}$ MeV, width: $18.4^{+2.4}_{-2.4}$ MeV (from Belle Collaboration)
- $Z_b(10650)$ mass: $10652.2^{+1.5}_{-1.5}$ MeV, width: $11.5^{+2.2}_{-2.2}$ MeV (from Belle Collaboration)
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ range determined: $950\sim1100$ MeV for the $I(J^{PC})=1(1^{+-})$ system
- For $I(J^{PC})=1(1^{+-})$ at $\Lambda=950$ MeV: first pole at 10604 MeV with $r_{RMS}=2.75$ fm (98.91% $B\bar{B}^{*}(^{3}S_{1})$); second pole at 10649 MeV with $r_{RMS}=4.22$ fm (99.65% $B^{*}\bar{B}^{*}(^{3}S_{1})$)
- For $I(J^{PC})=1(1^{+-})$ at $\Lambda=1100$ MeV: first pole at 10599 MeV with $r_{RMS}=1.14$ fm; second pole at 10646 MeV with $r_{RMS}=1.43$ fm
- For $I(J^{PC})=0(1^{++})$ system: bound state mass range $10602\sim10586$ MeV with $r_{RMS}=1.85\sim0.91$ fm; resonance at $10643\sim10610$ MeV with width $9.72\sim2.50$ MeV; resonance at $10667\sim10668$ MeV with width $46.04\sim43.96$ MeV
- For $I(J^{PC})=0(0^{++})$ system: mass range $10522\sim10424$ MeV with $r_{RMS}=0.63\sim0.46$ fm (rejected as too compact)

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of hidden-bottom states beyond the known $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ observed by Belle (2011), which are bottom analogues of the charmonium-like $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ states. Previous single-channel calculations by Sun (2011) and Liu (2017) gave conflicting results on whether these states could be molecules; the present work emphasizes that coupled-channel effects are important. The predictions enable experimental searches at LHCb and Belle II, particularly recommending $\eta_b(nS)/\Upsilon(nS)$ plus light mesons as final states for bound states and $B\bar B^*+h.c.$ and $B^* \bar B^*$ channels for resonances.
