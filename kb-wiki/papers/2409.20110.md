# Nuclear shape / phase transitions in the N = 40, 60, 90 regions

> We investigate the isotopes of Se, Zr, Mo and Nd in the regions with N = 40, 60 and 90, where a first-order shape / phase transition, from spherical to deformed, can be observed. The signs of phase transitional behavior become evident by examining structure indicators, such as certain energy ratios and B(E2) transition rates and, in particular, how they evolve with neutron number. Microscopic mean-field calculations using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock + Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer framework also reveal structural changes when considering the evolution of the resulting potential energy curves as functions of deformation. Finally, macroscopic calculations, using the Algebraic Collective Model, specifically for $^{74}$Se, $^{102}$Mo and $^{150}$Nd, after fitting its parameters to experimental spectra, result in potentials that resemble some of the potentials proposed in the framework of the Bohr Hamiltonian to describe shape transitions in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that first-order shape/phase transitions from spherical to deformed shapes occur in the N=40, 60, and 90 regions, but only the N=90 region achieves deformed values corresponding to the X(5) critical point symmetry, while the N=40 and 60 regions lead to more moderate deformations closer to the X(3) model predictions.

## Method

The authors examined experimental energy ratios (R4/2, R2/0, R2/2) and B(E2; 2_1+ to 0_1+) transition rates as functions of neutron number for Se, Zr, Mo, and Nd isotopes. They performed microscopic mean-field calculations using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock + Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (SHF+BCS) framework with the SV-bas parametrization to obtain potential energy curves as functions of quadrupole deformation. Additionally, they carried out macroscopic calculations using the Algebraic Collective Model (ACM) for 74Se, 102Mo, and 150Nd, fitting the Hamiltonian parameters to experimental spectra.

## Key numbers

- The X(5) critical point symmetry predictions of R4/2 = 2.904 and R2/0 = 0.177 apply sufficiently well only for the N=90 isotones.
- The N=40 region shows values close to the X(3) model predictions of R4/2 = 2.44 and R2/0 = 0.35.
- For Se isotopes, the potential energy curves show a transition from oblate deformation at N=34 to prolate deformation at N=46, passing through spherical at N=40.
- For Zr and Mo isotopes, a spherical shape at N=52 evolves into a deep oblate minimum and two shallower minima (spherical and prolate) at N=64.
- For Nd isotopes, a relatively flat potential energy curve at N=84 evolves at N=90 into one with two almost degenerate minima (prolate and oblate), while at N=96 the prolate minimum has prevailed.

## Context

This work extends the study of shape/phase transitions originally proposed by Federman (1977) and formalized through the Interacting Boson Model by Iachello (1987), as well as the critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5) introduced by Iachello (2000, 2001). A recent study by JPG (2023) indicated structural similarity between the N=40 region around 74Se and the N=60 and 90 regions, which this paper investigates using a common theoretical framework. The results enable future work on establishing a correspondence between microscopic potential energy curves and those from the Algebraic Collective Model, and on understanding the preference for prolate shapes in the N=90 region versus oblate shapes in the N=40 and 60 regions.
