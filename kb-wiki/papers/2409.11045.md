# Spin kinetic theory with a nonlocal relaxation time approximation

> We present a novel relaxation time approximation for kinetic theory with spin which takes into account the nonlocality of particle collisions. In particular, it models the property of the microscopic nonlocal collision term to vanish in global, but not in local equilibrium. We study the asymptotic distribution function obtained as the solution of the Boltzmann equation within the nonlocal relaxation time approximation in the limit of small gradients and short relaxation time. We show that the resulting polarization agrees with the one obtained from the Zubarev formalism for a certain value of a coefficient that determines the time scale on which orbital angular momentum is converted into spin. This coefficient can be identified with a parameter related to the pseudo gauge choice in the Zubarev formalism. Finally, we demonstrate how the nonlocal collision term generates polarization from vorticity by studying a nonrelativistic rotating cylinder both from kinetic and hydrodynamic approaches, which are shown to be equivalent in this example.

## Key claim

The paper presents a nonlocal relaxation time approximation (NLRTA) for spin kinetic theory that models the property of the microscopic nonlocal collision term to vanish in global, but not in local equilibrium. The resulting asymptotic polarization agrees with the Zubarev formalism only for a specific value of a coefficient that controls the conversion time scale between orbital angular momentum and spin, suggesting that the pseudo-gauge choice in the Zubarev formalism can be interpreted as modeling the effectiveness of this conversion.

## Method

The authors construct a Boltzmann equation with a collision term consisting of a standard local relaxation time approximation term plus a first-order-in-hbar nonlocal term proportional to the space-time shift Delta^mu, which characterizes the finite impact parameter in collisions of spinning particles. They perform a fast-relaxation expansion (small gradients and short relaxation time) around the asymptotic distribution function, which differs from the local-equilibrium distribution function. The polarization vector is then computed from the asymptotic distribution function and compared to results from the Zubarev formalism, and the approach is tested on a nonrelativistic rotating cylinder example solved both kinetically and hydrodynamically.

## Key numbers

- The space-time shift Delta^mu is of order hbar, given by Delta^mu = -(hbar/(2m(E_p+m))) epsilon^{munualphabeta} p_nu t_alpha ms_beta
- The asymptotic polarization vector reads A^mu_infty = -(hbar/(4m)) [tilde{Omega}^{munu}p_nu + (2/(E_p+m)) xi epsilon^{mulambdarhosigma} p_rho t_sigma p^nu (partial_lambda beta_nu + Omega_{lambdanu})] f_LE^{(0)}
- Agreement between the NLRTA asymptotic polarization and the Zubarev formalism result requires setting xi = (E_p+m)/E_p and Omega^{munu} = varpi^{munu} in the NLRTA expression

## Context

This work extends spin kinetic theory developed by Weickgenannt (2020, 2021) which derived the nonlocal collision term for spin-1/2 particles from the Wigner-function formalism, and by Weickgenannt (2022) which derived second-order spin hydrodynamics. It addresses the unresolved local Lambda polarization puzzle in heavy-ion collisions, where predictions from Becattini (2013, 2017) disagreed with experimental data from STAR (2019) and ALICE (2019). The NLRTA provides a pseudo-gauge-independent alternative to the Zubarev formalism used by Becattini (2021) and Buzzegoli (2021), enabling practical approximations for spin kinetic theory and hydrodynamics without solving complicated collision integrals.
