# Problems in the derivations of the renormalization group equation for the low momentum nucleon interactions

> We carefully examine all the four derivations of the renormalization group equation (RGE) for the so-called $\Vlk$ potential, given by Bogner, \textit{et. al.}[nucl-th/0111042]. Two derivations based on the ``semi-group composition law'' are shown to be unjustified, while the other two based on the completeness relation of the model space must be modified if there are bound states. It is however shown that the RGE is unchanged if the bound state wavefunctions in the reduced theory are required to have the same low-momentum components as those in the original theory. Several aspects of the $\Vlk$ approach are also discussed.

## Key claim

All four derivations of the renormalization group equation (RGE) for the low-momentum nucleon potential V_lowk, as given by Bogner et al. (2001), contain serious defects: two derivations based on the "semi-group composition law" are unjustified, while the two based on the completeness relation of the model space must be modified if bound states exist. However, the RGE itself remains unchanged if the bound state wavefunctions in the reduced theory are required to have the same low-momentum components as those in the original theory.

## Method

The paper examines each of the four derivations of the RGE for V_lowk presented by Bogner et al. (2001). For the two derivations based on the "semi-group composition law," the author shows that the assumed relation between the Q-box at different cutoffs cannot hold because it would require an energy-independent potential to satisfy an equation that only an energy-dependent potential can satisfy. For the two derivations based on completeness relations in the model space, the author demonstrates that the completeness relation used is incorrect when bound states are present, as it omits the bound state contributions. The author then derives the correct completeness relation including bound states and shows that the extra terms in the modified RGE cancel if the bound state wavefunctions in the reduced theory are required to have the same low-momentum components as those in the original theory.

## Key numbers

- The RGE for V_lowk is given by d/dLambda V_lowk^Lambda(k',k) = (2/pi) * [V_lowk^Lambda(k',Lambda) * T^Lambda(Lambda, k; Lambda^2)] / [1 - (k/Lambda)^2], where Lambda is the floating cutoff and k, k' are relative momenta.
- The "bare" Lippmann-Schwinger equation uses units with hbar = c = M_N = 1.
- The projection operator P is defined as P = (2/pi) * integral_0^Lambda |k> k^2 dk <k|.
- The normalization condition is <k'|k> = (pi/2) * delta(k'-k)/(k'k).
- The bound state energy is denoted as -k_{B_i}^2, where k_{B_i} is the bound state momentum.

## Context

This work critically examines the foundational derivations of the V_lowk approach developed by Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators (2000-2004), which emerged as an application of model space reduction methods from shell-model theory. The paper extends the critique by showing that while the individual derivations are flawed, the RGE itself remains valid under the additional requirement that bound state matrix elements are cutoff-independent. The author contrasts V_lowk with the Wilsonian RG approach of Birse et al. (1998), noting that the V_lowk RGE is too complicated to yield simple concepts like relevant and irrelevant operators, and suggests that the convergence of realistic potentials to a universal low-energy potential may be better understood through the inverse scattering problem (Newton, 1982; Alfaro and Regge, 1965) rather than RG universality. The work also discusses the newer Wegner-Glazek-Wilson similarity RG transformation approach (Bogner et al., 2006-2008), noting that it is unclear whether it preserves the half-on-shell T-matrix.
