# Relativistic predictions of quasielastic proton-nucleus spin observables based on a complete Lorentz invariant representation of the NN scattering matrix

> Within the framework of the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), complete sets of quasielastic $(\vec{p}, \vec{p}^{'})$ and $(\vec{p}, \vec{n})$ spin observables are calculated employing a general Lorentz invariant representation of the NN scattering matrix (referred to as the IA2 representation). The use of a complete representation eliminates the arbitrariness of a previously-used five-term parameterization (commonly called the IA1 representation) and allows for the correct incorporation of effective-mass-type medium effects within the RPWIA framework and within the context of the Walecka model. For quasielastic scattering from a $^{40}$Ca target at incident proton energies between 200 and 500 MeV, we investigate the sensitivity of complete sets of spin observables to effective nucleon masses for both IA1 and IA2 representations. In general it is seen that the IA1 representation may overestimate the importance of nuclear medium effects, whereas the IA2-based predictions nearly correspond to values for free nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the previously used five-term (IA1) representation of the NN scattering matrix overestimates the importance of effective-mass-type nuclear medium effects in quasielastic proton-nucleus scattering, while predictions based on the complete 44-amplitude IA2 representation nearly correspond to free nucleon-nucleon scattering values; however, even the IA2 representation fails to describe all spin observables, particularly the analyzing power for (p,p') scattering as energy decreases from 500 to 200 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) with a complete Lorentz invariant representation of the NN scattering matrix (IA2 representation) containing 44 independent invariant amplitudes, of which five are determined from free NN scattering data and the remaining 39 from solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with one-boson exchange. They calculated complete sets of quasielastic (p,p') and (p,n) spin observables for a 40Ca target at incident proton energies between 200 and 500 MeV, treating effective projectile and target nucleon masses as free parameters varied over the range 0.50 to 1.0 times the free nucleon mass, and compared results to experimental data at the centroid of the quasielastic peak.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon masses were varied over the range (M1/M; M2/M) from (0.50; 0.50) to (1.0; 1.0), where the lower limit of 0.50 corresponds to the effective nucleon mass in infinite nuclear matter
- For 40Ca(p,p') at 500 MeV and 19 degrees, the optimal IA2 effective masses were (M1/M = 1.0, M2/M = 0.86), compared to theoretical values of (0.89, 0.82)
- For 40Ca(p,n) at 495 MeV, the optimal IA2 effective masses were (M1/M = 0.85, M2/M = 0.85), compared to theoretical values of (0.89, 0.82)
- For 40Ca(p,n) at 200 MeV, the optimal IA2 effective masses were (M1/M = 0.93, M2/M = 0.92), compared to theoretical values of (0.83, 0.75)
- Optimal effective masses for both IA1 and IA2 models agreed to within 20% with theoretical values calculated by Hillhouse and De Kock (1994)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic plane wave model (van der Ventel et al., 1999) by performing a systematic comparison of IA1 and IA2 representations against experimental quasielastic spin observable data for 40Ca at multiple energies. The study contradicts the earlier success of Horowitz and Murdock (1988) in predicting the analyzing power for 40Ca(p,p') at 500 MeV using the IA1 representation, showing that the large quenching effect was an artifact of the incomplete representation. The results indicate that effective-mass-type medium effects are insufficient to describe all spin observables, motivating the development of a relativistic distorted wave model that includes distortions, multiple scattering, and recoil effects, which the authors report is under development.
