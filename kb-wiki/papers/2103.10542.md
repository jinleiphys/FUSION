# Cluster dynamics studied with the phase-space Minimum Spanning Tree approach

> The origin of weakly bound objects like clusters and hypernuclei, observed in heavy-ion collisions, is of theoretical and experimental interest. It is in the focus of the experiments at RHIC and LHC since it is not evident how such weakly bound objects can survive in an environment whose hadronic decay products point to a temperature of the order of 150 MeV. It is as well one of the key research topics in the future facilities of FAIR and NICA which are under construction in Darmstadt (Germany) and Dubna (Russia), respectively. We present here first results on the cluster dynamics within the model-independent cluster recognition library "phase-space Minimum Spanning Tree" (psMST) applied to different transport approaches: PHQMD, PHSD, SMASH and UrQMD. The psMST is based on the "Minimum Spanning Tree" (MST) algorithm for the cluster recognition which exploits correlations in coordinate space, and it is extended to correlations of baryons in the clusters in momentum space. We show the sensitivity of the cluster formation on the microscopic realization of the n-body dynamics and on the potential interactions in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents first results from the model-independent cluster recognition library "phase-space Minimum Spanning Tree" (psMST) applied to four different transport approaches (PHQMD, PHSD, SMASH, and UrQMD), showing that cluster formation is sensitive to the microscopic realization of n-body dynamics and potential interactions in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at lower collision energies where the n-body QMD approach (PHQMD) produces significantly more clusters at mid-rapidity than mean-field or cascade approaches.

## Method

The psMST algorithm was applied to "snapshots" of baryon coordinates and momenta from four transport models (PHQMD, PHSD, SMASH, and UrQMD) at two collision energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.52 GeV and 8.8 GeV) for semi-peripheral (b = 6 fm) Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions, respectively. Three cluster recognition scenarios were studied: Scenario 1 uses only coordinate-space clustering with a radius of r_clust = 4 fm; Scenario 2 applies an additional post-clustering momentum cut of p' < 300 MeV in the cluster rest frame; Scenario 3 applies the momentum cut during the clusterization procedure itself.

## Key numbers

- Clusterization radius used in all scenarios: r_clust = 4 fm
- Momentum cut threshold for Scenarios 2 and 3: p' < 300 MeV in the cluster center of mass frame
- Collision energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.52 GeV (Au+Au) and sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 GeV (Pb+Pb)
- Impact parameter for all collisions: b = 6 fm (semi-peripheral)
- Cluster analysis performed at three time snapshots: t_clust = 40 fm/c, 90 fm/c, and 150 fm/c
- PHQMD includes mutual 2-body density dependent Skyrme type potentials with binding energy of about 8 MeV/N
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 2.52 GeV, PHQMD predicts several orders of magnitude more clusters at mid-rapidity than the other models for mass numbers A = 3 and 4 <= A <= 20
- Transverse momentum spectra calculated at mid-rapidity |y| < 0.5

## Context

This work extends the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) cluster recognition approach originally developed by Aichelin (1991) by adding momentum-space correlations to the coordinate-space clustering, creating the phase-space MST (psMST) library. It builds on previous studies using MST and the Simulated Annealing Clusterization Algorithm (SACA) within the PHQMD approach by Aichelin (2019), which demonstrated the importance of dynamical n-body description for conserving spatial and momentum correlations of nucleons. The psMST library enables systematic comparison of cluster formation across different transport models (n-body QMD, mean-field, and cascade approaches) and can be used for feasibility studies at future NICA and FAIR facilities.
