# Study of event and particle selection effects on elliptic flow background at the isobar experiments based on AMPT model

> Measurement of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) has been a popular topic of high-energy nuclear physics in the last decade. The flow correlation $γ$ between charged hadron pairs of the same and opposite charges and their difference $Δγ$ were measured to separate the CME-driven signal from the collective flow background especially second-order elliptic $v_{2}$. The STAR experiment have stepped further to the isobar experiment to compare $γ$ and $Δγ$ between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr ~\cite{PhysRevC.105.014901}, which were theoretically expected to produce the same elliptic flow background but different CME signals. However, the measured flow backgrounds also differ between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr, indicating more fine-tuning of RP and centrality definition necessary. This analysis applied the AMPT model~\cite{PhysRevC.72.064901} to simulate the same collision system and energy as the STAR isobar experiment. Since the AMPT model does not include magnetic field effects, we expect comparing its output between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collision systems can provide an insight of the possible bias of flow background definition, and help improve the measurement of CME signal in real experiments. Multiple combinations of centrality and flow definition were chosen to study how the $v_2$ and their difference would be affected, especially by varying the particles selection of charge versus neutral properties and broadening (pseudo-)rapidity regions, while STAR CME work relied on charged-only particles at central rapidity.

## Key claim

The AMPT model, which contains no magnetic field effects, shows that the choice of charged-only versus charged-plus-neutral particles for centrality and flow determination introduces a bias in the elliptic flow background between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, even though the two isobar systems are expected to produce identical flow backgrounds based on their equal total nucleon number.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model in its string-melting scenario to generate about 2 million events each for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, matching the STAR isobar experiment conditions. They varied the particle selection for centrality determination and elliptic flow measurement across multiple combinations: charged-only, neutral-only, and charged-plus-neutral particles, with and without a transverse momentum threshold of pT > 0.2 GeV/c, and within pseudorapidity acceptance |eta| < 0.5 versus no eta cut. Centrality intervals were defined using both an integration method (based on the multiplicity distribution from AMPT itself) and a designation method (scaling STAR centrality boundaries by charge-to-neutral ratios).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of charged-plus-neutral to charged particles (c+n)/c was 1.73 for both Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr within |eta| < 0.5 with pT > 0.2 GeV/c selection, and 1.76 without pT cut.
- The ratio of charged pions to neutral pions (pi+ + pi-)/pi0 was 1.65 for both systems within |eta| < 0.5 with pT > 0.2 GeV/c, and 1.69 without pT cut.
- For charged-only particles within |eta| < 0.5 and no pT cut, the mean multiplicity <N_trk> in the 0-5% centrality bin was 292.44 for Ru+Ru and 291.74 for Zr+Zr.
- For charged-only particles within |eta| < 0.5 and pT > 0.2 GeV/c, the mean multiplicity <N_trk> in the 0-5% centrality bin was 253.31 for Ru+Ru and 253.22 for Zr+Zr.
- The ratios of mean multiplicity between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr for charged-plus-neutral particles stayed within one percent of unity across all centralities, while charged-only ratios showed deviations from unity when the eta cut was released.

## Context

This work extends the STAR isobar experiment analysis (STAR Collaboration, 2022) which found that the ratio of Delta-gamma/v2 between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr was below unity, contrary to the expectation of larger CME signal in Ru+Ru. The authors use the AMPT model (Lin et al., 2005) to isolate pure flow background effects, since AMPT contains no magnetic field. The results suggest that the STAR experiment's reliance on charged-only particles at central rapidity may introduce a bias in the flow background comparison between the two isobar systems, and that expanding the particle selection to include neutral particles and broader pseudorapidity coverage could help remove this bias in future CME measurements.
