# Dynamically induced scalar quark confinement

> We employ a functional approach to investigate the confinement problem in quenched Landau gauge QCD. We demonstrate analytically that a linear rising potential between massive quarks is generated by infrared singularities in the dressed quark-gluon vertex. The selfconsistent mechanism that generates these singularities is driven by the scalar Dirac amplitudes of the full vertex and the quark propagator. These can only be present when chiral symmetry is broken. We have thus uncovered a novel mechanism that directly links chiral symmetry breaking with confinement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates analytically that a linear rising potential between massive quarks is generated by infrared singularities in the dressed quark-gluon vertex, but only when chiral symmetry is broken; if chiral symmetry is restored, the confining solution disappears and only a Coulomb-type potential remains.

## Method

The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the one-particle irreducible Green's functions of quenched Landau gauge QCD, analyzing the infrared behavior by assuming all external momenta are proportional to a single small scale \(p^2 \ll \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2\). They substitute power-law ansätze for the dressing functions of the quark-gluon vertex into a skeleton expansion of the vertex DSE and determine the infrared exponents self-consistently by matching left and right hand sides, then check that the solution persists to all orders in the skeleton expansion.

## Key numbers

* The infrared exponent for the ghost and gluon propagators is \(\kappa = (93 - \sqrt{1201})/98 \approx 0.595\), giving ghost dressing \(G(p^2) \sim (p^2)^{-\kappa}\) and gluon dressing \(Z(p^2) \sim (p^2)^{2\kappa}\).
* The infrared exponents for the four Dirac amplitudes of the quark-gluon vertex in the chirally broken phase are \(\beta_{1 \dots 4} = -1/2 - \kappa\), leading to a four-quark function \(H(p) \sim (p^2)^{-2}\) and a linear potential \(V(\mathbf{r}) \sim |\mathbf{r}|\).
* In the chirally symmetric phase the exponents become \(\beta_1 = \beta_3 = -\kappa\), giving \(H(p) \sim (p^2)^{-1}\) and a Coulomb potential \(V(\mathbf{r}) \sim 1/|\mathbf{r}|\).
* The running coupling from the quark-gluon vertex in the chirally broken phase diverges as \(\alpha_{qg}(p^2) \sim 1/(N_c p^2)\), whereas in the symmetric phase it approaches a fixed point \(\alpha_{qg}(p^2) \sim 1/N_c\).

## Context

This work extends the infrared analysis of Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge by Alkofer (2004) and others, where the running coupling from pure Yang-Mills vertices freezes to a fixed point (scenario A), to the quark sector of quenched QCD. It contradicts the older "infrared slavery" idea that a \(1/p^4\) gluon propagator confines quarks; instead, the gluon propagator vanishes at zero momentum while the quark-gluon vertex provides the necessary infrared strength. The result directly links chiral symmetry breaking with confinement, consistent with lattice observations by Karsch (2003) that the chiral and deconfinement transitions occur at similar temperatures. This enables future numerical calculations of the string tension from summing the infinite tower of diagrams in the four-quark function.
