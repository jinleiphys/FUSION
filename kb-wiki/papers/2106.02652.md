# Ladder resummation of spin 1/2 fermion many-body systems with arbitrary partial-wave content

> We resum the ladder diagrams for the calculation of the energy density $\cal{E}$ of a spin 1/2 fermion many-body system in terms of arbitrary vacuum two-body scattering amplitudes. The partial-wave decomposition of the in-medium two-body scattering amplitudes is developed, and the expression for calculating $\cal{E}$ in a partial-wave amplitude expansion is also given. The case of contact interactions is completely solved and is shown to provide renormalized results, expressed directly in terms of scattering data parameters, within cutoff regularization in a wide class of schemes. $S$- and $P$-wave interactions are considered up to including the first three-terms in the effective-range expansion, paying special attention to the parametric region around the unitary limit.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general formalism to resum ladder diagrams for the energy density of spin-1/2 fermion many-body systems using arbitrary vacuum two-body scattering amplitudes, but the resummation cannot describe the true superfluid ground state at zero temperature in the unitary limit because the ladder resummation converges only above the critical temperature for the superfluid transition.

## Method

The authors develop a many-body quantum field theory formalism based on the generating functional approach of Oller (2001), where the in-medium energy density is calculated by resumming Feynman diagrams with both particle-particle and hole-hole intermediate states (ladder diagrams). They perform a partial-wave decomposition of the in-medium two-body scattering amplitudes and derive an expression for the energy density in terms of partial-wave amplitudes, using cutoff regularization to obtain renormalized results expressed directly in vacuum scattering parameters.

## Key numbers

* Bertsch parameter ξ = 0.370(5)(8) from experimental determinations using ultracold fermion atoms (Zuern 2013)
* Bertsch parameter ξ ≈ 0.38 from quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Forbes 2010, Forbes 2012), with a specific value 0.372 ± 0.005 from auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (Carlson 2011)
* Bertsch parameter ξ ≈ 0.475 from next-to-leading order epsilon expansion around d=4 or d=2 (Nishida 2006)
* Bertsch parameter ξ = 0.377 ± 0.014 from next-to-next-to-leading order epsilon expansion with interpolators (Nishida 2008)
* Bertsch parameter ξ ≈ 0.507 from ladder resummation with contact S-wave interaction in dimensional regularization (Kaiser 2011)
* Neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn = -18.95 ± 0.40 fm (Chen 2008)
* For P-wave with only scattering volume a_1, in the limit a_1 → ∞ the energy per particle \bar{ℰ} ≈ -E_F (similar to Kaiser 2012)

## Context

This work extends the ladder resummation approach of Kaiser (2011, 2012) from S-wave contact interactions to arbitrary partial-wave content, including up to three terms in the effective-range expansion for both S- and P-waves. The formalism enables systematic inclusion of higher partial waves and effective-range corrections in calculations of neutron matter and ultracold Fermi gases, where the large scattering length requires non-perturbative treatment. The authors show that renormalized results using cutoff regularization are independent of the cutoff scheme, unlike the dimensional regularization results of Kaiser (2012) which showed scheme-dependent behavior for the unitary limit with non-zero effective range.
