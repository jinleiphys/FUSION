# Random matrices, symmetries, and many-body states

> All nuclei with even numbers of protons and of neutrons have ground states with zero angular momentum. This is ascribed to the pairing force between nucleons, but simulations with random interactions suggest a much broader many-body phenomenon. In this Letter I project out random Hermitian matrices that have good quantum numbers and, computing the width of the Hamiltonian in subspaces, find ground states dominated by low quantum numbers, e.g. J=0. Furthermore I find odd-$Z$, odd-$N$ systems with isospin conservation have relatively fewer J=0 ground states.

## Key claim

The predominance of angular momentum J=0 ground states in many-body systems with random interactions arises primarily from the width of the angular-momentum-projected Hamiltonian, which depends on simple projection integrals and subspace dimensions rather than detailed microphysics; this picture also explains why odd-Z, odd-N systems with isospin conservation have relatively fewer J=0 ground states.

## Method

The author constructs random Hermitian matrices that are projected onto subspaces with good quantum numbers, first for U(1) symmetry and then for SU(2) rotational invariance. The variance of the Hamiltonian matrix elements in each angular momentum subspace is computed from universal integrals involving Legendre polynomials, then scaled by the square root of the subspace dimension to obtain an effective width. The resulting Gaussian distributions of energies for each J are used to predict the fraction of ground states with each angular momentum, which are compared against configuration-interaction (CI) diagonalizations of ensembles of random two-body Hamiltonians in shell-model bases.

## Key numbers

- For the SU(2) variance integral (excluding the factor 4π²σ̄²), the computed values are 1.571, 0.393, 0.245, 0.178, 0.139 for J = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 respectively.
- For eight identical fermions in a j=21/2 shell, the CI simulation yields 55% J=0 ground states (f_CI) compared to 0.4% native fraction in the model space (f_space), while the random matrix model predicts 33% (f_RM).
- For the N=7 interacting boson model (IBM), the CI simulation yields 55% J=0 ground states compared to 11% native fraction, with the random matrix model predicting 81%.
- For Z=N=3 in the sd shell with isospin conservation, the CI simulation gives 15% J=0 ground states (f_CI) compared to 0.8% (T=0) and 1.6% (T=1) native fractions, with the random matrix model predicting 42%.
- For the same system with isospin broken, the CI simulation gives 32% J=0 ground states compared to 3.6% native fraction, with the random matrix model predicting 72%.

## Context

This work extends the discovery by Johnson et al. (1998) and others that rotationally invariant but otherwise random two-body Hamiltonians tend to yield J=0 ground states, a phenomenon previously attributed to the pairing interaction as described in Bohr and Mottelson (1969) and simpler models. The paper provides a simple explanation based on projection integrals and subspace dimensions, contrasting with more complicated prior analyses such as Papenbrock and Weidenmueller (2004) that found correlations between different J subspaces. The results enable future investigation of symmetry breaking, two-species systems, and correlations between states with different quantum numbers.
