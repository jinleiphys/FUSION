# Effective short-range interaction for spin-singlet P-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering

> Distorted-wave methods are used to remove the effects of one- and two-pion exchange up to order Q^3 from the empirical 1P1 phase shift. The one divergence that arises can be renormalised using an order-Q^2 counterterm which is provided by the (Weinberg) power counting appropriate to the effective field theory for this channel. The residual interaction is used to estimate the scale of the underlying physics.

# Effective short-range interaction for spin-singlet P-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering

## Key claim

The residual short-range interaction in the 1P1 channel, after removing one- and two-pion exchange up to order Q^3 using distorted-wave methods, shows a scale of approximately 260 MeV for the underlying short-distance physics, suggesting that additional low-energy degrees of freedom (such as the Delta-resonance) may be needed for a natural effective field theory in this channel.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrodinger equation with the leading-order one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential and centrifugal barrier to obtain distorted waves, then extract a residual K-matrix from the difference between the empirical and OPE phase shifts. They use the distorted-wave Born approximation to subtract matrix elements of the order-Q^2 and Q^3 two-pion-exchange potentials, regularizing the resulting divergent integrals with a radial cutoff and renormalizing by subtracting the divergent constant at a low energy of T=5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The pi-N coupling constant used is f_piNN^2 = 0.075, taken from the Nijmegen analyses
- A delta-shell cutoff radius of R_0 = 0.1 fm was used for the final results
- The renormalized short-range potential was fitted over T = 100-200 MeV with coefficients: a_0 = 0.28 fm^4, a_1 = -0.20 fm^6, a_2 = 0.0056 fm^8
- The coefficient a_1 implies a short-distance scale of approximately 260 MeV

## Context

This work extends the distorted-wave method previously applied to peripheral spin-singlet waves by Birse and McGovern (2003) and to spin-triplet waves by Birse (2007) and the 1S0 channel by Birse (2010). The 1P1 channel presents a new challenge because the two-pion-exchange matrix elements between P-wave distorted waves contain 1/r and ln r divergences, requiring renormalization with an order-Q^2 counterterm consistent with Weinberg power counting. The estimated scale of 260 MeV is similar to that found in the 1S0 channel, suggesting that including the Delta-resonance as an explicit degree of freedom may lead to more natural short-range forces.
