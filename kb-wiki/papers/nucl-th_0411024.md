# eta-Nucleon Scattering Length and Effective Range uncertainties

> The coupled eta-N, pi-N, gamma-N, 2pi-N system is described by a K-matrix method. The parameters in this model are adjusted to get an optimal fit to pi-N-->pi-N, pi-N-->eta-N, gamma-N-->pi-N and gamma-N-->eta-N data in an energy range of about 100 MeV or so each side of the eta-threshold. Compared with our earlier analysis, we now utilize recent Crystal Ball data. However, the outcome confirms our previous result that the eta-nucleon scattering length (a) is large with a value of 0.91(6)+i0.27(2) fm.

# η-Nucleon Scattering Length and Effective Range uncertainties

## Key claim

The η-nucleon scattering length is confirmed to be large at 0.91(6)+i0.27(2) fm using new Crystal Ball data, but the authors caution that the ηN amplitude drops rapidly below threshold, making it unclear whether this attraction is sufficient to produce η-nuclear quasi-bound states at the subthreshold energies (up to 40 MeV below threshold) relevant for few-nucleon systems.

## Method

The authors use a K-matrix method coupling the ηN, πN, γN, and ππN channels, with the K-matrix containing two resonant poles (N(1535) and N(1650)) plus energy-independent background terms. They perform a simultaneous fit to πN→πN, πN→ηN, γN→πN, and γN→ηN data within about 100 MeV of the η-threshold, incorporating new Crystal Ball data for πN→ηN near threshold. The effective range expansion of the on-shell T-matrix is used to extract the scattering length a, effective range r₀, and shape parameter s.

## Key numbers

- η-nucleon scattering length: a = 0.91(6) + i0.27(2) fm (from the optimal fit, set A)
- Effective range: r₀ = -1.50(13) - i0.24(4) fm (from the earlier 1997 analysis, cited for comparison)
- Shape parameter: s = -0.10(2) - i0.01(1) fm³ (from the earlier 1997 analysis)
- The ηN threshold center-of-mass energy is 1487.0 MeV
- The subthreshold region of interest for few-body systems extends down to approximately 1460 MeV center-of-mass energy
- The phenomenological ³He-η scattering length from earlier work: A(³He η) = 4.24(29) + i0.72(81) fm
- A four-body calculation based on a different ηN amplitude (a = 0.50 + i0.32 fm) gives A(³He η) = 1.82 + i2.75 fm
- The new Crystal Ball data provide 5 data points in the energy range 1488.5 ≤ E_cm ≤ 1523.3 MeV
- The overall χ²/dp for the complete set of 121 data points in set A is 1.01

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier K-matrix analyses (Green and Wycech, 1997 and 1999) by incorporating new Crystal Ball data from Kozlowska et al. (2003) for πN→ηN near threshold, which replaces older data from the Nefkens review. The new data tighten the error bars on the ηN scattering parameters and eliminate alternative "unconventional" solutions that appeared in earlier fits using preliminary Brookhaven data. The results are relevant for predicting η-nuclear quasi-bound states, which were first suggested about 20 years earlier by Haider and Liu (1986) and have been studied in the η-deuteron system (Ueda) and ³He (Wilkin et al., 1996; Maytal et al., 1996), though experimental searches have so far given negative conclusions (Chrien; Calen et al.). The authors note that the subthreshold behavior of the ηN amplitude may explain discrepancies between phenomenological and four-body calculations of the ³He-η scattering length.
