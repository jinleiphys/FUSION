# The Compactification of QCD$_4$ to QCD$_2$ in a Flux Tube

> We show from the action integral that in the special environment of a flux tube, QCD$_4$ in (3+1) dimensional space-time can be approximately compactified into QCD$_2$ in (1+1) dimensional space-time. In such a process, we find out how the coupling constant $g_{2D}$ in QCD$_2$ is related to the coupling constant $g_{4D}$ in QCD$_4$. We show how the quark and the gluon in QCD$_2$ acquire contributions to their masses arising from their confinement within the tube, and how all these quantities depend on the excitation of the partons in the transverse degrees of freedom. The compactification facilitates the investigation of some dynamical problems in QCD$_4$ in the simpler dynamics of QCD$_2$ where the variation of the gluon fields leads to a bound state.

## Key claim

The authors show that QCD in (3+1) dimensional space-time can be approximately compactified into QCD in (1+1) dimensional space-time specifically within a flux tube environment, under the conditions of longitudinal dominance and transverse confinement of partons.

## Method

Starting from the QCD₄ action integral, the authors factorize the four-dimensional Dirac fermion field into transverse wave functions and longitudinal functions, then integrate over the transverse coordinates. They relate the four-dimensional gauge fields to two-dimensional fields using the transverse wave function density, and derive the resulting two-dimensional action integral with a modified coupling constant and additional mass terms for quarks and gluons arising from transverse confinement.

## Key numbers

- The two-dimensional coupling constant is related to the four-dimensional coupling by \(g_{2D} = \frac{g_{4D}}{R_T} \sqrt{\frac{2}{9\pi}}\) for a Gaussian transverse ground state with root-mean-square radius \(R_T = \sqrt{2}\sigma_T\)
- For a sharp transverse radius \(R_{T\text{sharp}}\), the approximate relation is \(g_{2D} \sim \frac{g_{4D}}{\sqrt{\pi} R_{T\text{sharp}}}\)
- The transverse wave functions are normalized as \(\int d x^1 d x^2 \left( |G_1(\mathbf{r}_\perp)|^2 + |G_2(\mathbf{r}_\perp)|^2 \right) = 1\)

## Context

This work extends the flux tube and string fragmentation picture developed by Casher, Kogut, and Susskind (1974) and the Lund model of Andersson et al. (1983), providing a formal action-level derivation of the compactification that was previously reported briefly by Wong (2009, 2010). The compactification enables the investigation of QCD₄ dynamical problems using the simpler QCD₂ dynamics, where the gauge field equations lead to a massive bound state solution analogous to Schwinger's solution in two-dimensional Abelian gauge theory.
