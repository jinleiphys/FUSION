# Effects of Strong Magnetic Fields on Neutron Star Structure

> We study static neutron stars with poloidal magnetic fields and a simple class of electric current distributions consistent with the requirement of stationarity. For this class of electric current distributions, we find that magnetic fields are too large for static configurations to exist when the magnetic force pushes a sufficient amount of mass off-center that the gravitational force points outward near the origin in the equatorial plane. (In our coordinates an outward gravitational force corresponds to $\partial\ln g_{tt}/\partial r>0$, where $t$ and $r$ are respectively time and radial coordinates and $g_{tt}$ is coefficient of $dt^2$ in the line element.) For the equations of state (EOSs) employed in previous work, we obtain configurations of higher mass than had been reported; we also present results with more recent EOSs. For all EOSs studied, we find that the maximum mass among these static configurations with magnetic fields is noticeably larger than the maximum mass attainable by uniform rotation, and that for fixed values of baryon number the maximum mass configurations are all characterized by an off-center density maximum.

## Key claim

For static neutron stars with poloidal magnetic fields and a constant current function, the maximum mass among magnetized configurations is noticeably larger than the maximum mass attainable by uniform rotation, but the existence of static solutions is limited by a physical criterion: when magnetic forces push enough mass off-center that the gravitational force begins to point outward in the equatorial plane (∂ν/∂r > 0), no stationary configurations exist, contrary to the suggestion in Bocquet et al. (1995) that the limit was merely a numerical convergence issue.

## Method

The authors solved the Einstein-Maxwell equations for axisymmetric static neutron stars using a Green's function technique rather than the spectral method of Bocquet et al. (1995). They adopted a constant current function f(Aφ) = f0 and searched for maximum mass configurations by specifying the maximum log-enthalpy hmax (allowing for off-center density maxima) rather than central density, and found the largest f0 values for which the code converged. The critical condition for existence was identified as ∂ν/∂r = 0 at some off-center location in the equatorial plane, marking the onset of outward gravitational force.

## Key numbers

- For the Pol2 γ=2 polytropic equation of state, the maximum mass among all static magnetized configurations was found to be 4.062 M⊙ with a magnetic moment of 1.122 × 10^33 A m^2 (using the same polytropic constant as Bocquet et al. 1995)
- When the polytropic constant was adjusted so that the maximum mass of static non-magnetized configurations is 2.0 M⊙, all results were scaled accordingly
- Observed magnetar surface magnetic fields are inferred to be 2-8 × 10^14 G from measured periods and spindown rates of associated X-ray pulsars
- The largest observed white dwarf magnetic field of 5 × 10^8 G leads to a neutron star field of ~1 × 10^14 G by flux conservation
- The largest observed main sequence stellar magnetic field of 3.4 × 10^4 G suggests a possible neutron star field of a few times 10^14 G
- A dynamo mechanism could generate fields as large as 3 × 10^17 (Pi/1 ms)^(-1) G, where Pi is the initial rotation period

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculations of Bocquet, Bonazzola, Gourgoulhon and Novak (1995), who performed relativistic calculations of axisymmetric neutron star structure with comparable fluid and electromagnetic stress-energy tensors, finding maximum fields of order 10^18 G and mass increases of 13-29%. The authors identify a physical criterion for the nonexistence of static solutions (outward gravitational force in the equatorial plane) that was not recognized in the earlier work, and they obtain higher mass configurations than reported by Bocquet et al. (1995) for the same equations of state. This work enables future studies incorporating the direct effects of magnetic fields on the equation of state through Landau quantization and magnetic moment interactions, which the authors state will be reported in a subsequent paper (Cardall et al. 2000).
