# Higher Resonance Contributions to the Adler-Weisberger Sum Rule in the Large N_c Limit

> We determine the $N_c$--dependence of the resonance contributions to the Adler--Weisberger sum rule for the inverse square $1/g_A^2$ of the axial charge coupling constant and show that in the large $N_c$ limit the contributions of the Roper-like excitations scale as $O(1/N_c)$. Consistency with the $1/N_c^2$ scaling of the $1/g_A^2$ term in the sum rule requires these contributions to cancel against each other.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the large \(N_c\) limit, the coupling constants for Roper-like resonances scale as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c)\), not as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{3/2})\) as previously inferred from the Adler-Weisberger sum rule; consistency with the \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\) scaling of the left-hand side then requires the contributions of the Nucleon-Roper and \(\Delta\)-Roper resonances to cancel each other.

## Method

The authors determine the \(N_c\) scaling of the resonance coupling constants \(g_{jI}^{(\pm)}\) by comparing the non-relativistic reduction of the relativistic Rarita-Schwinger \(\pi N\)-resonance vertices with the spin-isospin structure of one-body quark-model operators in the large \(N_c\) limit. They then insert these scalings into the Adler-Weisberger sum rule in its resonance-saturated form to compute the \(N_c\) order of each resonance's contribution, and check consistency with the known \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\) scaling of \(1/g_A^2\).

## Key numbers

* The left-hand side \(1/g_A^2\) scales as \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\).
* The \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance contributes to leading order \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^0)\) to the right-hand side of the sum rule.
* The Nucleon-Roper and \(\Delta\)-Roper resonances contribute at next-to-leading order \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c)\).
* The odd-parity \((\frac12^-,\frac12)\) nucleon excitation contribution is suppressed by \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^3)\).
* The coupling constant \(g_{\frac12\frac12}^{(-)}\) of the Nucleon-Roper scales as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c)\).
* The coupling constant \(g_{\frac12\frac12}^{(+)}\) of the odd-parity nucleon-like resonance scales as \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c)\).
* The coupling constant \(g_{\frac32\frac32}^{(+)}\) of the \(\Delta(1232)\) ground state scales as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{3/2})\).
* The coupling constant \(g_{\frac32'\frac32}^{(+)}\) of the Roper-like \(\Delta\) scales as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c)\).
* The widths of all Roper-like excitations scale as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^0)\); widths of odd-parity resonances scale as \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\); the \(\Delta(1232)\) width falls at least as \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the large \(N_c\) analysis of the Adler-Weisberger sum rule previously applied to the \(\Delta(1232)\) by Mazur (1987) and to the scaling of coupling constants by Kakazu (1981). It corrects the earlier inference that Roper-like couplings scale as \(N_c^{3/2}\), showing instead that they scale as \(N_c\) due to the normalization of excited baryon states (Collins 1979). The result enables consistent treatment of Roper contributions in large \(N_c\) models such as the Skyrme model and large \(N_c\) quark models, and is qualitatively consistent with \(\pi N\) decay width data for resonances below 2.2 GeV.
