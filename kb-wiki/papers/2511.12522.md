# Simplification of chiral nuclear forces near the unitarity limit

> Modern theory approaches for describing atomic nuclei often make use of on an effective theory that constructs the interaction between nucleons systematically based on Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), exploiting constraints arising from the approximate chiral symmetry of QCD. The tensor nuclear force produced by one-pion exchange is an important feature that arises naturally in this framework. In this work we show that, however, the tensor force is suppressed by the large nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths in combination with the smallness of the pion mass. Based on this observation, we propose a new scheme for a chiral nuclear force that is able to describe $NN$ phase shifts up to the center-of-mass momenta $k \simeq 300$ MeV while treating pion exchange as a perturbation. Our much simplified leading-order force provides a microscopic explanation for the recent success of various short-range nuclear forces from the perspective of chiral effective field theory, and it shares with those approaches an approximate Wigner SU(4) symmetry, as well as the closeness to the unitarity limit (infinite nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths), as guiding principles. Compared to previous approaches to perturbative-pion interactions, our force also adjusts the ordering of short-range contact interactions, by means of which we overcome convergence problems of the expansion that were previously assumed to severely limit its usefulness. We demonstrate the performance of our approach with numerical calculations of $NN$ scattering up to fourth order, in addition to studies of $3N$ and $4N$ bound-state properties.

## Key claim
The tensor force from one-pion exchange is suppressed by the combination of large nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths and the small pion mass, allowing a perturbative treatment of pion exchange in chiral effective field theory despite previous claims that such an expansion has poor convergence.

## Method
The authors construct a new power-counting scheme (perturbative-pion interaction, PPI) where leading-order nuclear forces are purely contact interactions identical to Pionless EFT, and one-pion exchange enters as a perturbation at next-to-leading order. They promote a momentum-dependent SD-mixing contact operator to next-to-leading order to counteract the tensor force in the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel, and compute NN scattering phase shifts up to fourth order (N3LO) as well as binding energies and radii of 3H and 4He up to next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers
- NN phase shifts described up to center-of-mass momenta k ≃ 300 MeV
- Mixing angle ε1 is ≤ 2° for k ≤ 300 MeV from partial-wave analyses
- Tensor potential T(r) is 14√2 times stronger than Yukawa potential Y(r) at r = mπ^(-1) in the 3S1-3D1 channel
- 3H binding energy from MMW scheme is ≤ 5.3 MeV; 4He binding energy from MMW is ≤ 14 MeV
- PPI radius predictions: r(3H) = 1.51 ± 0.11 fm, r(4He) = 1.36 ± 0.25 fm at NLO
- Pionless EFT breakdown scale Q3 ≈ 73 MeV for 3H, Q4 ≈ 115 MeV for 4He
- PPI expansion parameter Q4απ ≈ 0.43 for 4He, Q3απ ≈ 0.27 for 3H
- Expected breakdown scales: απ^(-1) ≃ 270 MeV (KSW) and δ ≃ 290 MeV (Δ-N mass splitting)

## Context
This work extends the Pionless EFT approach of Konig (2016) and the unitarity-limit expansion, while addressing the convergence problems of the KSW scheme (Kaplan 1998) that were previously thought to limit perturbative-pion treatments (Fleming 1999). It provides a microscopic explanation for the success of simplified nuclear interactions used by Gattobigio (2019), Lu (2018), and Shen (2021), and suggests that PPI may enable controlled ab initio calculations of many-nucleon systems with explicit pions as perturbative corrections.
