# Strings In Baryons And Matrix Coordinates

## Key claim
The paper argues that the internal dynamics of a baryon, as a bound state of QCD-strings and quarks, may be captured by a theory of matrix coordinates, but only if one accepts that the relevant theory for matrix coordinates, perhaps coming from a critical string theory, should also be suitable for non-critical dimensions, a step that "needs justification."

## Method
The author reviews the two main proposals for the inter-quark potential in baryons (the Delta-shape and Y-shape ansatzes) and their lattice QCD support, then draws an analogy between the quark-string system and a bound state of D0-branes with open strings. The dynamics of the system is formulated as a matrix theory action for N x N matrix coordinates, including a commutator potential term, and the one-loop effective potential between D0-branes is computed to compare with the Delta-ansatz.

## Key numbers
- The Delta-shape potential is given by V(r1, r2, r3) = (sigma/2)(r12 + r23 + r31), where sigma is the tension of a QCD-string in a mesonic state.
- The Y-shape potential is given by V(r1, r2, r3) = sigma (r1s + r2s + r3s), where the Steiner point s minimizes the sum of distances.
- The disagreement between Delta- and Y-ansatzes is at most about 15 percent, based on geometrical reasonings (Bali).
- The size of the bound state from the matrix theory, ell, is approximately m^{-1/3} l^{2/3}, where l is of order the string length.
- The one-loop effective potential between D0-branes is V_one-loop ~ sum_{a>b=1}^N |x_a - x_b| / l^2, which matches the Delta-ansatz.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing quark-string picture of baryons (Isgur, string baryon models) and the gauge/string duality program (Maldacena 1997, Gubser-Klebanov-Polyakov-Witten). It builds on earlier matrix theory approaches to QCD (Fatollahi 2002, 2003) that reproduced linear inter-quark potentials and large-N behavior. The paper suggests that matrix coordinates become relevant only for the internal dynamics of bound states, a situation termed "confined non-commutativity," and that the non-Abelian gauge fields in a confined theory may not have an independent character.
