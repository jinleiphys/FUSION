# Application of the coherent density fluctuation model to study the nuclear matter properties of finite nuclei within the relativistic mean-field formalism

> We obtained a density-dependent analytical expression of binding energy per nucleon for different neutron-proton asymmetry of the nuclear matter (NM) with a polynomial fitting, which manifests the results of effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model. This expression has the edge over the Br$\ddot{u}$ckner energy density functional [Phys. Rev. {\bf 171}, 1188 (1968)] since it resolves the Coster-Band problem. The NM parameters like incompressibility, neutron pressure, symmetry energy, and its derivatives are calculated using the acquired expression of energy per nucleon. Further, the weight function calculated by E-RMF densities are folded with calculated NM parameters within coherent density fluctuation model to find the properties of closed/semi-closed-shell even-even $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, $^{56}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, $^{116}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb nuclei. The values obtained for the neutron pressure $P^{A}$, symmetry energy $S^{A}$ and its derivative $L_{sym}^A$ known as slope parameter, lie within a narrow domain whereas there is a large variation in isoscalar incompressibility $K^{A}$ and surface incompressibility $K_{sym}^{A}$ while moving from light to heavy nuclei. The sizable variation in $K^{A}$ and $K_{sym}^{A}$ for light and heavy nuclei depicts their structural dependence due to the peculiar density distribution of each nucleus. A comparison of surface quantities calculated in the present work has also been made with ones obtained via Br$\ddot{u}$ckner energy density functional.

## Key claim

The authors derive a density-dependent analytical expression for nuclear matter binding energy per nucleon from the E-RMF model, which resolves the "Coester-Band problem" that plagued the earlier Brückner energy density functional (Brueckner 1968) by incorporating relativistic effects that mimic three-body forces. The claim is hedged by noting that the polynomial fitting requires 10-12 terms to achieve a mean deviation of only 0.2% from the original RMF results.

## Method

The authors fitted the nuclear matter saturation curves from the E-RMF model (using NL3 and G3 parameter sets) for different neutron-proton asymmetry values with a 12-term polynomial expansion in density. They then used the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to fold the resulting nuclear matter parameters (incompressibility, symmetry energy, neutron pressure, and their derivatives) with the weight functions calculated from E-RMF densities of closed/semi-closed-shell even-even nuclei. This procedure yields the corresponding surface properties for finite nuclei: $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, $^{56}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, $^{116}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb.

## Key numbers

- For NL3 parameter set, finite nuclear incompressibility $K^{A}$ ranges from 618.05 MeV ($^{16}$O) to 411.03 MeV ($^{208}$Pb); for G3, it ranges from 258.87 MeV ($^{16}$O) to 238.85 MeV ($^{208}$Pb)
- Symmetry energy $S^{A}$ for NL3 ranges from 36.97 MeV ($^{90}$Zr) to 41.66 MeV ($^{56}$Ni); for G3, from 30.12 MeV ($^{16}$O) to 31.98 MeV ($^{56}$Ni)
- Neutron pressure $P^{A}$ for NL3 ranges from 6.68 MeV ($^{208}$Pb) to 9.70 MeV ($^{16}$O); for G3, from 2.52 MeV ($^{208}$Pb) to 3.39 MeV ($^{16}$O)
- Slope parameter $L_{sym}^{A}$ for NL3 ranges from 117.01 MeV ($^{90}$Zr) to 130.30 MeV ($^{56}$Ni); for G3, from 50.28 MeV ($^{208}$Pb) to 53.57 MeV ($^{56}$Ni)
- Surface incompressibility $K_{sym}^{A}$ for NL3 ranges from -10.92 MeV ($^{40}$Ca) to 47.99 MeV ($^{208}$Pb); for G3, from -103.23 MeV ($^{16}$O) to -89.43 MeV ($^{40}$Ca)
- Nuclear matter incompressibility at saturation is 271.76 MeV for NL3 and 243.96 MeV for G3
- The polynomial fitting achieved a mean deviation of 0.2% from the original RMF data when using 12 terms in the expansion

## Context

This work extends the coherent density fluctuation model approach previously applied with the Brückner energy density functional (Brueckner 1968) by Bhuyan (2018) and Abdul (2019), but replaces that functional with a relativistic E-RMF-based expression that resolves the Coester-Band problem (Coester 1970, Brockmann 1990). The new relativistic density functional enables more accurate estimation of surface properties for beta-stable and beta-unstable nuclei, and the calculated quantities can help constrain the nuclear equation of state for applications in neutron star physics and heavy-ion collisions.
