# Center phase transition from matter propagators in (scalar) QCD

> Novel order parameters for the confinement-deconfinement phase transition of quenched QCD and fundamentally charged scalar QCD are presented. Similar to the well-known dual condensate, they are defined via generalized matter propagators with $U(1)$-valued boundary conditions. The order parameters are easily accessible with functional methods. Their validity and accessibility is explicitly demonstrated by numerical studies of the Dyson-Schwinger equations for the matter propagators. Even in the case of heavy scalar matter, where the propagator does not show a signature of the phase transition, a discontinuity due to the transition can be extracted in the order parameters, establishing also fundamentally charged scalar matter as a probe for color confinement.

## Key claim

Novel order parameters for the confinement-deconfinement phase transition, defined via generalized matter propagators with U(1)-valued boundary conditions, can extract a discontinuity due to the transition even in the case of heavy scalar matter where the propagator itself shows no signature of the phase transition.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger equations for the scalar propagator in fundamentally charged scalar QCD at finite temperature, using a temperature-dependent gluon propagator fitted to lattice results and a model for the scalar-gluon vertex. They constructed order parameters as the Fourier transform of condensates defined from the squared propagator evaluated at vanishing three-momentum, summed over Matsubara modes with generalized boundary conditions. For QCD, they applied an analogous construction using the trace of the quark propagator dressing functions.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the deconfinement transition in quenched SU(3): Tc = 277 MeV
- Scalar mass used in the calculation: m = 1.5 GeV
- Renormalization scale: mu = 4 GeV
- Vertex model parameter: d1 = 0.53
- Gluon fit parameter c = 11.5 GeV^2
- Gluon scale parameter Lambda = 1.4 GeV
- Cutoff used for regularization: Lambda_c^2 = 5 x 10^4 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the dual condensate approach of Gattringer (2006) and Fischer (2009) to scalar QCD and to a new class of order parameters based on squared propagators rather than linear condensates. The construction enables functional methods like Dyson-Schwinger equations to probe center symmetry breaking using fundamentally charged scalar matter, which was previously demonstrated for quarks by Fischer (2009, 2010). The results enable future studies of the phase structure of scalar QCD and provide a probe for color confinement that remains finite even for non-vanishing bare masses.
