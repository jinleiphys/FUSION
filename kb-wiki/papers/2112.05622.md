# $β^-{\rm p}$ and $β^-α$ decay of the $^{11}$Be neutron halo ground state

> Beta-delayed proton emission from the neutron halo ground state of $^{11}$Be raised much attention due to the unusually high decay rate. It was argued that this may be due to the existence of a resonance just above the proton decay threshold. In this Letter, we use the lenses of real-energy continuum shell model to describe several observables including the Gamow-Teller rates for the $β^-$-delayed $α$ and proton decays, and argue that, within our model, the large $β^-{\rm p}$ branching ratio cannot be reconciled with other data.

# Wiki Page: β⁻p and β⁻α Decay of the ¹¹Be Neutron Halo Ground State

## Key claim

Within the real-energy continuum shell model (SMEC), the large β⁻p branching ratio reported for the decay of the ¹¹Be neutron halo ground state cannot be reconciled with the measured β⁻α branching ratio and the proton width of the 1/2⁺₃ resonance in ¹¹B, even though the model can consistently describe the β⁻α data and the proton width simultaneously.

## Method

The authors used the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC), an open quantum system extension of the nuclear shell model, to describe the structure of ¹¹B resonances and the β⁻-delayed decay of ¹¹Be. The SM Hamiltonian in the full psd model space (WBP⁻ interaction) was fine-tuned by adjusting two monopole terms to reproduce the experimental β⁻α branching ratio, while the continuum-coupling strength V₀ was constrained by the measured proton width of the 1/2⁺₃ resonance. Gamow-Teller transition strengths were computed between the SMEC ground state of ¹¹Be and the relevant 1/2⁺ and 3/2⁺ eigenstates of ¹¹B, and branching ratios were calculated using the phase-space integral over the resonance profile with energy-dependent widths.

## Key numbers

- The reported experimental β⁻p branching ratio is 1.3(3)×10⁻⁵ (Ayyad 2019), while the SMEC calculation yields bᵣ(β⁻p) = 0.28(3)×10⁻⁶, a factor of approximately 40 smaller.
- The experimental β⁻α branching ratio is 3.30(10)% (Refsgaard 2019), which was used to fine-tune the SMEC interaction.
- The 1/2⁺₃ resonance in ¹¹B was reported at E = 11.425(20) MeV, i.e., 197(20) keV above the proton separation energy Sₚ = 11.2286 MeV, with a width Γ = 12(5) keV (Ayyad 2019).
- The continuum-coupling strength constrained by the proton width is V₀ = −37⁺¹¹₋₁₂ MeV·fm³.
- The SMEC-predicted B(GT) value for the transition to the 1/2⁺₃ state is 0.125⁺⁰·⁰⁰⁹₋₀·₀₁₃, and to the 3/2⁺₂ and 3/2⁺₃ states are 0.240⁻⁰·⁰⁰¹⁺₀·⁰⁰² and 0.322⁺⁰·⁰⁰²₋₀·⁰⁰³, respectively.
- The total radiative width of the 1/2⁺₃ resonance is predicted to be 14.32⁻⁰·¹⁶⁺₀·²⁵ eV, with the dominant E1 transition to the 1/2⁻₁ state having a partial width of 11.7 eV (81.7% intensity).

## Context

This work extends the SMEC analysis of near-threshold resonances in ¹¹B (Okolowicz 2020) and addresses the controversy between the β⁻p branching ratio reported by Ayyad (2019) and the lower limit suggested by Riisager (2020). The study is motivated by the R-matrix analysis of Refsgaard (2019), which showed that the β⁻α branching ratio requires contributions from the 3/2⁺₃ state, and by the shell-model study of Volya (2020), which concluded the reported β⁻p rate is virtually impossible to explain. The results enable future experimental searches for the 1/2⁺₃ resonance, which has since been confirmed in proton resonance scattering and (d,n) reactions.
