# Temperature and Density Effects on the Nucleon Mass Splitting

> The finite temperature and finite density dependence of the neutron-proton mass difference is analysed in a purely hadronic framework where the $ρ-ω$ mixing is crucial for this isospin symmetry breakdown. The problem is handled within Thermo Field Dynamics. The present results, consistent with partial chiral and charge symmetry restoration, improve the experimental data fit for the energy difference between mirror nuclei.

# Temperature and Density Effects on the Nucleon Mass Splitting

## Key claim

The neutron-proton mass difference decreases with increasing nuclear density, providing a partial resolution to the Nolen-Schiffer Anomaly (NSA), but the effect is not large enough to completely remove the anomaly; temperature effects are negligible for astrophysical temperatures far from the deconfining phase transition.

## Method

The authors compute the neutron-proton mass splitting using relativistic perturbation theory within Thermo Field Dynamics, a real-time formalism for statistical field theory. The calculation incorporates the rho-omega mixing interaction as the primary charge symmetry breaking mechanism, with nucleon and vector mesons as the fundamental dynamical degrees of freedom, and includes phenomenological form factors at the meson-nucleon vertices. The self-energy correction is evaluated at finite temperature and density by separating the thermal propagators into zero-temperature Feynman parts and temperature-density dependent corrections, with the bosonic in-medium corrections neglected as requiring extreme conditions beyond the model's validity.

## Key numbers

- The experimental neutron-proton mass difference is 1.29 MeV, with an electromagnetic contribution of -0.66 to -0.76 MeV, implying a 2 MeV strong contribution
- At zero temperature and nuclear matter density delta_0 = 0.1934 fm^{-3}, the density-dependent contribution to the mass difference Delta^c M ranges from -0.1 to -0.25 MeV for baryon densities from delta_0/2 to 1.5 delta_0
- At temperatures below 15 MeV and densities below delta_0/10, the density-dependent mass difference Delta^c M is below a couple of keV
- The product of coupling constants g_rho * g_omega * lambda = 0.492 GeV^2 is chosen to saturate the 2 MeV hadronic contribution at zero temperature and density
- The estimated NSA contribution for mirror nuclei with average density approximately delta_0/2 is 0.10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature analysis (Christiansen et al., 1991) of the neutron-proton mass splitting using rho-omega mixing within a hadronic framework. The results are consistent with partial chiral and charge symmetry restoration at finite density, and the density dependence follows the same direction as predictions from Skyrme-type models (Epele et al., 1992) and other approaches (Hatsuda et al., 1991; Henley and Krein, 1989). The calculation enables improved modeling of the Nolen-Schiffer Anomaly in mirror nuclei, though the authors note that additional contributions beyond rho-omega mixing, which are minor in vacuum, become relevant inside nuclei and may be needed to fully resolve the anomaly.
