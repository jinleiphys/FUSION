# Thermalization in SU(3) gauge theory after a deconfining quench

> We determine the time evolution of fluctuations of the Polyakov loop after a quench into the deconfined phase of SU(3) gauge theory from a simple classical relativistic Lagrangian. We compare the structure factors, which indicate spinodal decomposition followed by relaxation, to those obtained via Markov Chain Monte Carlo techniques in SU(3) lattice gauge theory. We find that the time when the structure factor peaks diverges like $\sim 1/k^2$ in the long-wavelength limit. This is due to formation of competing Z(3) domains for configurations where the Polyakov loop exhibits non-perturbatively large variations in space, which delay thermalization of long wavelength modes. For realistic temperatures, and away from the extreme weak-coupling limit, we find that even modes with $k$ on the order of $T$ experience delayed thermalization. Relaxation times of very long wavelength modes are found to be on the order of the size of the system; thus, the dynamics of competing domains should accompany the hydrodynamic description of the deconfined vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper finds that after a quench into the deconfined phase of SU(3) gauge theory, the formation of competing Z(3) domains delays thermalization of long-wavelength modes, with relaxation times on the order of the system size; this effect persists for modes with wave numbers up to approximately the temperature T, away from the extreme weak-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors use Pisarski's effective model of Polyakov loops with a Z(3)-symmetric potential and a Lorentz-invariant kinetic term, solving the Euler-Lagrange equations via Molecular Dynamics on spatial cubic lattices of sizes N_s^3 = 40^3 to 96^3. Initial field configurations are prepared in the confined phase with Gaussian fluctuations (sigma = 0.04), then coarse-grained over a box of size N_cg^3 = 4^3 to set a physical length scale a = 0.184 fm, and quenched to final temperatures T_f/T_c = 1.50, 1.75, 2.00, 2.25, 2.50. Structure factors F(k,t) are computed from Fourier transforms of the Polyakov loop and compared to those from Markov Chain Monte Carlo Glauber dynamics on a 4 x 64^3 lattice.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.184 fm, corresponding to T_c = 260 MeV and aN_cg = 1/T_c = 0.736 fm.
- Time step Delta t = 0.00184 fm/c, with trajectories run from 15000 to 25000 steps (27.6 to 46 fm/c).
- For T_f/T_c = 1.50 on an 11.8^3 fm^3 lattice (64^3), F_1 reaches its maximum at t_max = 8.2(1.3) fm/c and equilibrates after approximately 20 fm/c.
- F_1,max values for T_f/T_c = 2.00 range from 15(1) at volume (7.4)^3 fm^3 to 256(17) at volume (17.7)^3 fm^3.
- For T_f/T_c = 2.0, the fit to t_max = a_0 + a_1 L_s^2 gives a_1 = 0.044 fm^{-1} and a_0 = 2.6 fm.
- The lowest mode wave numbers are |k| = sqrt(n) x 105.3 MeV for the 64^3 lattice (L_s = 11.8 fm) and sqrt(n) x 102.1 MeV for the Glauber dynamics lattice (L_s = 12.1 fm).

## Context

This work extends the study of Polyakov loop structure factors after a deconfining quench from Glauber dynamics in SU(3) lattice gauge theory (BBV 2006) to relativistic Minkowski dynamics, providing a physical time scale absent in the Monte Carlo approach. The results contradict the "bottom-up" thermalization framework of Baier, Mueller, Schiff and Son (2001) by showing that Z(3) domain formation, similar to order-order domains in the 3-state Potts model (Solyom and Zittartz 1982), delays thermalization of long-wavelength modes. The findings suggest that hydrodynamic descriptions of the deconfined vacuum should be extended to account for competing domain dynamics, and open the question of quantifying the contribution of these modes to bulk viscosity in heavy-ion collisions.
