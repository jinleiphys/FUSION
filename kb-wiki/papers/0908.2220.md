# Triton Photodisintegration with Effective Field Theory

> Effective field theory (EFT) has been recently used for the calculation of neutron-deuteron radiative capture at very low energies.We present here the use of EFT to calculate the two-body photodisintegration of the triton, considering the three-body force. The calculated cross section shows sharp rising from threshold to maximum about 0.88 mb at 13 MeV and decreasing slightly to about 0.81 mb at 19 MeV, in agreement with the experimental data. Our results are in good agreement with the experimental data and the other calculations using modern realistic two- and three-nucleon forces, like AV18/UrbanaIX potential.

## Key claim

The paper uses pionless effective field theory (EFT) to calculate the two-body photodisintegration cross section of the triton (gamma + H-3 -> n + d) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N^2LO), including a three-body force, and finds agreement with experimental data and modern potential-model calculations, but only within the low-energy regime below about 20 MeV where the EFT is valid.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev integral equation for the neutron-deuteron scattering amplitude and the triton bound state using pionless EFT, with the Lagrangian expanded to N^2LO. They computed the electric dipole (E1) transition amplitude at leading order (LO) and the magnetic dipole (M1) transition amplitude at LO, NLO, and N^2LO, then folded these with the photon interaction kernel. The three-body force parameters H0 and H2 were fixed by the doublet scattering length a3 = 0.65 fm and the triton binding energy B3 = 8.48 MeV, respectively.

## Key numbers

* Maximum cross section of about 0.88 mb at approximately 13 MeV incident photon energy (N^2LO result, combining E1 and M1 contributions).
* Cross section of about 0.81 mb at approximately 19 MeV (N^2LO result).
* At 12 MeV, the N^2LO total cross section is 0.874 mb, compared to 0.882 mb for the AV18+UrbanaIX potential with Siegert theorem and 0.949 mb for AV18 with explicit meson-exchange currents (MEC).
* At 40 MeV, the N^2LO cross section is 0.240 mb, compared to 0.180 mb for AV18+UrbanaIX-Siegert and 0.169 mb for AV18+UrbanaIX-MEC.
* The M1 contribution at N^2LO ranges from 0.063 mb at 11.54 MeV to 0.029 mb at 19.72 MeV.
* The E1 contribution at LO ranges from 0.782 mb at 11.54 MeV to 0.770 mb at 19.72 MeV.
* Cutoff variation was studied between Lambda = 150 MeV and Lambda = 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless EFT from neutron-deuteron radiative capture (previously calculated by the same authors, Sadeghi et al.) to the inverse process of triton photodisintegration. It provides a model-independent, systematically improvable calculation that agrees with results from realistic potentials such as AV18/UrbanaIX (Skibinski et al.) and CD Bonn with TM' three-body forces, and with experimental data from Faul et al. The approach enables future calculations of three-body breakup and higher-energy processes, though the authors note that E2 contributions and subnuclear currents become important above about 20 MeV.
