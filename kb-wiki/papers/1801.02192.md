# Shannon Information Entropy in Heavy-ion Collisions

> The general idea of information entropy provided by C.E. Shannon "hangs over everything we do" and can be applied to a great variety of problems once the connection between a distribution and the quantities of interest is found. The Shannon information entropy essentially quantify the information of a quantity with its specific distribution, for which the information entropy based methods have been deeply developed in many scientific areas including physics. The dynamical properties of heavy-ion collisions (HICs) process make it difficult and complex to study the nuclear matter and its evolution, for which Shannon information entropy theory can provide new methods and observables to understand the physical phenomena both theoretically and experimentally. To better understand the processes of HICs, the main characteristics of typical models, including the quantum molecular dynamics models, thermodynamics models, and statistical models, etc, are briefly introduced. The typical applications of Shannon information theory in HICs are collected, which cover the chaotic behavior in branching process of hadron collisions, the liquid-gas phase transition in HICs, and the isobaric difference scaling phenomenon for intermediate mass fragments produced in HICs of neutron-rich systems. Even though the present applications in heavy-ion collision physics are still relatively simple, it would shed light on key questions we are seeking for. It is suggested to further develop the information entropy methods in nuclear reactions models, as well as to develop new analysis methods to study the properties of nuclear matters in HICs, especially the evolution of dynamics system.

## Key claim

Shannon information entropy provides a useful tool for studying heavy-ion collisions because it can measure the chaoticity of the nuclear system without requiring the system to be in thermodynamic equilibrium, though the present applications in heavy-ion collision physics are still relatively simple.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical foundations of Shannon information entropy and its variants (joint entropy, mutual information, configurational entropy), then survey applications in heavy-ion collisions including the chaotic behavior in branching processes of hadron collisions, the liquid-gas phase transition, and the isobaric difference scaling phenomenon for intermediate mass fragments. They also briefly introduce the main characteristics of typical models used in heavy-ion collisions, including quantum molecular dynamics models, thermodynamics models, and statistical models.

## Key numbers

- The multiplicity information entropy was first defined by Y.G. Ma (1999) and applied to study nuclear reactions, showing that the information entropy (chaoticity) of the system exhibits a trend of first increasing and then decreasing along the colliding time, where a liquid-gas phase transition happens.
- For a system with equal probabilities where p_i = 1/n, the Shannon information entropy takes the form H(S) = ln N, increasing monotonically with n.
- Intermediate-mass fragments (IMFs, Z > 2) carry a major part (approximately 50%) of nucleons involved in medium-energy heavy-ion collisions from several tens to several hundreds MeV/u.

## Context

This review extends the application of Shannon information entropy, originally developed for communication theory by Shannon (1948), into nuclear physics by collecting and summarizing applications that include the work of Ma (1999) on multiplicity information entropy in nuclear reactions. The paper suggests further development of information entropy methods in nuclear reaction models and new analysis methods to study the properties of nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions, especially the evolution of dynamical systems.
