# Anisotropy Scaling Functions in Heavy-Ion Collisions: Insights into the `Ultra-Central Flow Puzzle' and Constraints on Transport Coefficients and Nuclear Deformation

> Anisotropy scaling functions derived from comprehensive measurements of transverse momentum- and centrality-dependent anisotropy coefficients \(v_2(p_T,\text{cent})\) and \(v_3(p_T,\text{cent})\) in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 and 2.76 TeV, Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV and Au+Au collisions at 0.2 TeV, offer new insights into the `ultra-central flow puzzle.' These functions integrate diverse measurements into a single curve, clarifying anisotropy attenuation throughout the entire \(p_T\) and centrality range. They reveal the influence of initial-state eccentricities (\(\varepsilon_{n}\)), dimensionless size (\(\mathbb{R}\)), radial flow, viscous correction to the thermal distribution function (\(δ_f\)), the medium's stopping power (\(\Hat{q}\)), and specific shear viscosity (\(η/s\)) on the observed anisotropies. This analysis not only enhances understanding of transport coefficients but also provides crucial constraints on nuclear deformation.

## Key claim

Anisotropy scaling functions derived from \(v_2(p_T,\text{cent})\) and \(v_3(p_T,\text{cent})\) measurements across multiple collision systems and energies can unify diverse data into a single curve, resolving the 'ultra-central flow puzzle' by demonstrating that hydrodynamic models' simultaneous failure to predict \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) in ultra-central collisions (cent \(\alt 1\%\)) arises from incomplete treatment of initial-state eccentricities, dimensionless size, radial flow, viscous corrections, stopping power, and specific shear viscosity.

## Method

The author developed scaling functions based on a dispersion relation for sound propagation (Eq. vnpc) that relates anisotropy coefficients \(v_n\) to initial-state eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\), dimensionless size \(\mathbb{R} \propto \langle N_{\text{chg}} \rangle_{|\eta|\leq 0.5}^{1/3}\), radial flow, viscous correction \(\delta_f = \kappa p_T^2\) with \(\kappa = 0.17\) (GeV/c)\(^{-2}\), and specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\). Eccentricities were calculated using a Monte Carlo quark-Glauber model (MC-qGlauber) with fluctuating initial conditions for Au, Pb, and Xe nuclei (with Xe deformation parameters \(\beta_2\) and \(\gamma\)). The scaling functions were validated across centrality ranges 0.0-0.1% to 50-60% for Pb+Pb at 5.02 and 2.76 TeV, Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV, and Au+Au at 0.2 TeV using data from ATLAS, ALICE, and PHENIX collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV: scaling exponents \(\beta=0.88\), \(\alpha=1.0\) for the scaling function validation across 0.0-0.1% to 50-60% centrality
- Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV: scaling exponents \(\beta=0.84\), \(\alpha=1.0\), \(\zeta=0\), indicating approximately 5% reduction in \(\eta/s\) relative to 5.02 TeV
- Au+Au at 0.2 TeV: scaling exponents \(\beta=0.55\), \(\alpha=1.6\), \(\zeta=0.04\), indicating approximately 37% reduction in \(\eta/s\) relative to 5.02 TeV
- Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV: scaling exponents \(\beta=0.90\), \(\alpha=0.25\), \(\zeta=0.02\) with \(\beta_2=0.16\), indicating approximately 2% increase in \(\eta/s\) relative to Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV
- Systematic uncertainty of 2-3% estimated for eccentricities based on model parameter variations
- \(\kappa = 0.17\) (GeV/c)\(^{-2}\) adopted for viscous correction \(\delta_f\)

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling efforts that previously struggled with the 'ultra-central flow puzzle' (Giannini 2022), where models either overestimated \(v_2\) or underestimated \(v_3\) in collisions with centrality below 1%. The scaling function approach builds on prior Bayesian inference constraints for QGP transport properties (Bernhard 2016, 2019; JETSCAPE 2020; Nijs 2020) and the relationship between \(\eta/s\) and \(T^3/\hat{q}\) proposed by Majumder (2007). The results enable precise constraints on nuclear deformation parameters (particularly \(\beta_2\) for Xe) and decouple initial-state from final-state effects, reducing systematic uncertainties in transport coefficient extraction.
