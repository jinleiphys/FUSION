# Precision thermodynamics of the strongly interacting Fermi gas in two dimensions

> The two-species cold atomic Fermi gas with attractive short-range interactions in two spatial dimensions undergoes a Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) to a Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC) crossover as a function of $\ln (k_F a)$, where $a$ is the scattering length. However, the nature of this crossover in the strong coupling regime $\ln(k_F a) \sim 1$ remains poorly understood. In this work we use canonical-ensemble auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo methods on discrete lattices to calculate several thermodynamical quantities in the strongly interacting regime, and eliminate systematic errors by extrapolating to continuous time and taking the continuum limit. In particular, we present results for the condensate fraction, spin susceptibility, contact, energy equation of state, and the free energy staggering gap. We identify signatures of a pseudogap regime, in which pairing correlations survive above the critical temperature for superfluidity, in the spin susceptibility and in the free energy staggering gap. These results can be used as a benchmark for future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a pronounced pseudogap regime, where pairing correlations survive above the superfluid critical temperature, exists in the strongly interacting two-dimensional Fermi gas for coupling strengths \(\ln(k_F a) \sim 1\), but the authors note that the pseudogap temperature scale \(T^\star\) at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.0\) is close to the two-body binding energy scale, leaving open the question of how much of the pseudogap is due to many-body versus two-body pairing.

## Method

The authors use canonical-ensemble auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) on discrete lattices to simulate the two-dimensional spin-1/2 Fermi gas with attractive short-range interactions. They discretize the continuum Hamiltonian on a finite lattice with periodic boundary conditions, apply a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to decouple the interaction, and sample the auxiliary-field path integral using Monte Carlo. Systematic errors from the finite Trotter time step and finite lattice spacing are removed by extrapolating to continuous time (linear in \((\Delta\beta)^2\)) and to the continuum limit (linear in filling factor \(\nu \to 0\)), respectively.

## Key numbers

- Pseudogap temperature \(T^\star / T_F = 0.31\) at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.0\); \(T^\star / T_F = 0.22\) at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.3\); \(T^\star / T_F = 0.12\) at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.8\), where \(T^\star\) is defined as the temperature at which the spin susceptibility reaches 95% of its maximum value.
- Pseudogap temperature in units of the two-body binding energy: \(T^\star = 0.91 |\epsilon_b|\) at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.0\); \(T^\star = 1.17 |\epsilon_b|\) at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.3\); \(T^\star = 1.74 |\epsilon_b|\) at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.8\).
- The spin susceptibility is significantly suppressed above \(T_c\) for all three coupling strengths, with the suppression largest at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.0\).
- The contact (in units of \(N k_F^2\)) increases monotonically with decreasing temperature in the pseudogap regime \(T_c < T < T^\star\), with the largest increase at \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.0\).
- At \(\ln(k_F a) = 1.0\), the AFMC energy at low temperatures is significantly smaller than the \(T=0\) diffusion Monte Carlo result of Bertaina (2011).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier letter (Ramachandran 2024) by providing detailed thermodynamic results for the two-dimensional BCS-BEC crossover in the strong-coupling regime. It directly addresses the open question of a pseudogap regime, which has been studied experimentally (Murthy 2018) and theoretically in three dimensions (Jensen 2020, Jensen 2024). The results provide controlled benchmarks for future experiments and theory, and the authors note that their pseudogap temperature estimates at \(\ln(k_F a) \sim 1\) are close to the two-body binding energy, suggesting that a future study of the spectral function is needed to resolve the role of many-body versus two-body pairing.
