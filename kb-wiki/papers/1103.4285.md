# Shape evolution in Yttrium and Niobium neutron-rich isotopes

> The isotopic evolution of the ground-state nuclear shapes and the systematics of one-quasiproton configurations are studied in neutron-rich odd-A Yttrium and Niobium isotopes. We use a selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism based on the Gogny energy density functional with two parametrizations, D1S and D1M. The equal filling approximation is used to describe odd-A nuclei preserving both axial and time reversal symmetries. Shape-transition signatures are identified in the N=60 isotopes in both charge radii and spin-parities of the ground states. These signatures are a common characteristic for nuclei in the whole mass region. The nuclear deformation and shape coexistence inherent to this mass region are shown to play a relevant role in the understanding of the spectroscopic features of the ground and low-lying one-quasiproton states. Finally, a global picture of the neutron-rich A=100 mass region from Krypton up to Molybdenum isotopes is illustrated with the systematics of the nuclear charge radii isotopic shifts.

## Key claim

The abrupt change in ground-state spin-parity and charge radii observed at N=60 in Yttrium and Niobium isotopes is interpreted as a signature of a shape transition, but the theoretical description for Niobium fails to reproduce the experimental spin-parities in heavier isotopes, likely due to the neglect of triaxial degrees of freedom in the calculations.

## Method

The authors performed selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny energy density functional with two parametrizations (D1S and D1M), employing the equal filling approximation (EFA) to describe odd-A nuclei while preserving both axial and time-reversal symmetries. For each isotope, multiple blocked configurations with different Kπ quantum numbers and quadrupole deformations (spherical, oblate, prolate) were explored to identify the ground state and low-lying one-quasiproton states. Constrained calculations generated potential energy curves from even-even neighbors to provide reference states for the odd-A blocking procedure.

## Key numbers

- The shape transition in Yttrium occurs at N=60 (A=99), where the ground state changes from 1/2− to 5/2+; in Niobium the transition occurs at N=60 (A=101), changing from 9/2+ to (5/2+)
- In 97Y, the calculation gives a 9/2+ oblate ground state with a 1/2− excited state at 0.7 MeV and an incipient prolate 5/2+ at 0.4 MeV
- For heavier prolate Yttrium isotopes (99-107Y), oblate 9/2+ excited states appear at energies in the range 0.6-1.2 MeV
- In Niobium, the prolate 5/2+ configurations appear at excitation energies between 0.6 and 1 MeV in 101,103,105Nb
- The charge radii show a sizable jump at N=60 in Yttrium, with the measured isotope shifts δ⟨r²c⟩ increasing abruptly between N=58 and N=60
- For Krypton isotopes, the prolate minima appear about 1 MeV higher in energy than the oblate ground states at N=60 and N=62

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of neutron-rich isotopes in the A=100 mass region, which covered even-Z elements (Sr, Zr, Mo) and odd-Z Rb isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman 2010, 2011). The results are compared with experimental data from laser spectroscopy (Cheal 2009, Baczynska 2010) and mass measurements (Audi 2003, Hager 2006). The study demonstrates that both Gogny D1S and D1M parametrizations produce similar spectroscopic results, with D1M giving slightly more compact level densities due to its larger effective mass. The smooth charge radii evolution in Krypton contrasts with the abrupt jumps in Rb, Sr, Y, and Zr, while Niobium and Molybdenum show intermediate behavior attributed to triaxiality, calling for future triaxial calculations for odd-A nuclei and configuration mixing approaches using the Generator Coordinate Method.
