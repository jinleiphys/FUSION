# Broad excitations in a 2+1D overoccupied gluon plasma

> Motivated by the initial stages of high-energy heavy-ion collisions, we study excitations of far-from-equilibrium 2+1 dimensional gauge theories using classical-statistical lattice simulations. We evolve field perturbations over a strongly overoccupied background undergoing self-similar evolution. While in 3+1D the excitations are described by hard-thermal loop theory, their structure in 2+1D is nontrivial and nonperturbative. These nonperturbative interactions lead to broad excitation peaks in spectral and statistical correlation functions. Their width is comparable to the frequency of soft excitations, demonstrating the absence of soft quasiparticles in these theories. Our results also suggest that excitations at higher momenta are sufficiently long-lived, such that an effective kinetic theory description for 2+1 dimensional Glasma-like systems may exist, but its collision kernel must be nonperturbatively determined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in 2+1D gauge theories, gluonic excitations are broad and non-Lorentzian with widths comparable to their frequency, indicating the absence of soft quasiparticles; however, excitations at higher momenta may be sufficiently long-lived to permit an effective kinetic theory description, but only if its collision kernel is determined nonperturbatively.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge theories in 2+1D and in a Glasma-like 2+1D theory (which includes an adjoint scalar field from dimensional reduction of 3+1D pure gauge theory). They introduced small instantaneous sources on top of classical fields undergoing self-similar evolution and solved linearized equations of motion to extract spectral and statistical correlation functions via a linear response framework, then Fourier-transformed these to frequency space.

## Key numbers

- Plasmon frequency for the 2+1D theory: ω_pl = 0.12 Q at Qt = 500
- Plasmon frequency for the Glasma-like 2+1D theory: ω_pl = 0.2 Q at Qt = 500
- Lattice size 1024^2 with spacing Q a_s = 1/8 for the 2+1D system
- Lattice size 512^2 with spacing Q a_s = 1/4 for the Glasma-like 2+1D system
- Maximum relative time for spectral function extraction: Δt_max = 80/Q for 2+1D theories
- Maximum relative time for scalar statistical correlator: Δt_max = 200/Q for the Glasma-like theory
- Scaling exponents extracted in prior work: β = -1/5, α = 3β

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of excitation spectra in isotropic 3+1D gluodynamics at a classical self-similar attractor (Boguslavski et al., 2018), where narrow quasiparticle peaks were observed. It also builds on their earlier equal-time correlation function study in 2+1D systems (Boguslavski et al., 2019), which found self-similar attractor behavior. The results contradict the applicability of hard-thermal loop (HTL) theory at soft scales in 2+1D, consistent with the expectation that soft-soft interactions become nonperturbative. The findings enable future studies of excitation spectra in expanding systems and with different initial conditions relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
