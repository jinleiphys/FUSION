# Production cross-sections of new superheavy elements with Z = 119-120 in fusion-evaporation reactions

> We have calculated production cross sections of new superheavy elements with atomic number Z=119,120 in the fusion-evaporation reactions of $^{48}$Ca+$^{252}$Es, $^{48}$Ca+$^{257}$Fm, $^{49}$Sc+$^{252}$Es, $^{49}$Sc+$^{251}$Cf, $^{50}$Ti+$^{247}$Bk, $^{50}$Ti+$^{251}$Cf, $^{51}$V+$^{247}$Cm, $^{51}$V+$^{247}$Cf, $^{54}$Cr+$^{243}$Am, $^{54}$Cr+$^{247}$Cm, $^{56}$Mn+$^{244}$Pu, $^{56}$Mn+$^{243}$Am, $^{60}$Fe+$^{237}$Np, $^{60}$Fe+$^{244}$Pu, $^{61}$Co+$^{238}$U, $^{61}$Co+$^{237}$Np, $^{64}$Ni+$^{231}$Pa, $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U, $^{65}$Cu+$^{232}$Th, $^{65}$Cu+$^{231}$Pa, and $^{68}$Zn+$^{232}$Th within the dinuclear system model systematically. The inner fusion barriers have been extracted from the driving potential or potential energy surface which could be used to predict the relative fusion probability roughly. The influence of mass asymmetry of the colliding partners on the production of new superheavy elements (SHE) has been investigated systematically. It is found that fusion probability increase along with the increasing mass asymmetry of colliding systems. The Ti-induced reactions have the largest cross-sections of the new SHE. The dependence of production cross-sections of new superheavy elements on the isospin of projectile nuclei has been discussed. The new SHE of $^{289-295}$119 has been predicted as the synthesis cross sections around serval picobarns in the $^{46,48,50,52}$Ti-induced reactions. Production cross-section of the element of $^{295}$120 has been evaluated as large as 1 picobarn in the reactions $^{46}$Ti ($^{251}$Cf, 2n) $^{295}$120 at $E^*$ = 26 MeV. The optimal projectile-target combinations and beam energies for producing new SHE with atomic number Z=119-120 are proposed for the forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim
The production cross-sections for new superheavy elements with Z = 119-120 are predicted to be on the order of picobarns, with the largest cross-sections found in Ti-induced reactions, but these predictions are model-dependent and rely on the dinuclear system model's treatment of fusion barriers and mass asymmetry.

## Method
The authors employed the dinuclear system (DNS) model, which decomposes the fusion-evaporation reaction into three sequential stages: capture (penetration of the Coulomb barrier), fusion (nucleon transfer between touching projectile and target calculated by solving master equations for the probability distribution of fragment proton and neutron numbers), and survival (de-excitation of the compound nucleus by evaporating neutrons, protons, and alpha particles against fission). The inner fusion barriers were extracted from the driving potential or potential energy surface for each projectile-target combination. Production cross-sections were calculated for 21 different fusion-evaporation reactions involving projectiles from Ca to Zn and targets from Th to Fm.

## Key numbers
- Production cross-section of element Z=120 isotope $^{295}$120 is evaluated as large as 1 picobarn in the reaction $^{46}$Ti($^{251}$Cf, 2n)$^{295}$120 at excitation energy $E^*$ = 26 MeV.
- The new superheavy elements $^{289-295}$119 are predicted with synthesis cross-sections around several picobarns in the $^{46,48,50,52}$Ti-induced reactions.
- The maximal angular momentum was set as $J_{\rm max}$ = 30 $\hbar$ in the calculations.
- The shell-damping energy was taken as $E_{\rm D}=5.48A^{1/3}/(1+1.3A^{-1/3})$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the systematic application of the DNS model, which has previously reproduced excitation functions for known superheavy elements (Z=104-118) synthesized at Dubna, GSI, Berkeley, and RIKEN, to predict production cross-sections for the next elements Z=119 and 120. The study systematically investigates the influence of mass asymmetry and projectile isospin on fusion probability, finding that fusion probability increases with increasing mass asymmetry of the colliding systems. The results provide optimal projectile-target combinations and beam energies for forthcoming experiments at laboratories such as IMP Lanzhou, where superheavy isotopes of Db, Bh, and Ds have already been synthesized.
