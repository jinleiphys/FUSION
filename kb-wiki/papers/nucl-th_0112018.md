# Structure of the inner crust of neutron stars: crystal lattice or disordered phase ?

> We investigate the inner crust structure of neutron stars using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with the Coulomb interaction treated beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation. Our results suggest that the shell effects associated with unbound neutrons play an important role and, in particular, lead to complicated phase transition pattern between various nuclear phases (as a function of the density). Namely, we show that the relative energies of different phases are rapidly oscillating functions of the neutron density. In the semiclassical approach this behavior is explained as an interference effect due to periodic orbits of similar lengths. We discuss also the dependence of the shell effects on pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that shell effects from unbound neutrons, which have been overlooked in previous studies, cause the relative energies of different nuclear phases in the inner crust to oscillate rapidly as a function of density, potentially reversing the phase transition order predicted by liquid-drop-based approaches and possibly leading to a disordered phase rather than a simple crystal lattice.

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock equations for neutron-proton-electron matter within a rectangular box with periodic boundary conditions, using the SLy4 Skyrme force and treating the Coulomb interaction beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation by solving the Poisson equation with periodic boundary conditions. They imposed constraints on the proton quadrupole moment to construct adiabatic paths between different nuclear phases (spherical, rod-like, slab-like) and compared energy densities at fixed total density and box size. The shell energy contribution from unbound neutrons was analyzed using a semiclassical approximation based on periodic orbits.

## Key numbers

- The energy density differences between phases are of the order of a few keV/fm³, with barriers between minima around 0.5 keV/fm³.
- At total density ρ = 0.055 fm⁻³, the spherical phase appears at an excitation energy of about 1 keV/fm³ above the ground state.
- The neutron Fermi momentum at the highest density considered (ρ_n ≈ 0.1 fm⁻³) is k_F^n ≈ 1.44 fm⁻¹.
- The mesh spacing was Δx = 1.3 fm, giving a critical momentum k_crit = π/Δx ≈ 2.42 fm⁻¹ and maximum kinetic energy ε_crit ≈ 121 MeV.
- For the spherical phase at ρ ≈ 0.055 fm⁻³, the nuclear radius R_0 ≈ 6.23 fm; for the spaghetti phase, R_1 ≈ 3.86 fm; the lattice constant d = 26 fm.
- The semiclassical estimate of the oscillation period for the energy difference between spherical and spaghetti phases is Δε_F^n ≈ 0.8 MeV; HF calculations give periods of 1.303 MeV (spherical) and 1.324 MeV (spaghetti).
- For the spherical and slab phases at ρ ≈ 0.075 fm⁻³, radii are R_0 ≈ 5.9 fm and R_2 ≈ 3.59 fm; half the oscillation period from semiclassical analysis is 0.801 MeV; HF gives 0.866 MeV (spherical) and 0.875 MeV (slab).
- The pairing gap for neutron matter at subnuclear densities reaches 3-4 MeV at the Fermi level; with a pairing gap of 3.5 MeV, the amplitude of shell energies decreases by less than 10% compared to the no-pairing regime.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron star crust structure that used liquid-drop or Thomas-Fermi models (e.g., BBP, BBA, RBP, ARP, NV, RPW, HSY, OHY, LPR, WKO, LFB, OYA1, LOR, OYA2, PRA, DHM, DHA) and semiclassical approaches by Bulgac, Magierski, and collaborators (BMA1-4, BWI) that predicted strong shell effects from unbound neutrons. The authors show that self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations confirm these shell effects are comparable to liquid-drop energy differences and can alter the phase transition pattern, potentially leading to coexistence of multiple lattice types or a disordered phase. The results enable future studies of the inner crust that must account for shell effects from unbound neutrons, which were previously neglected or treated only for bound nucleons as in Oyamatsu and Yamada (2000).
