# Realistic two-nucleon potentials for the relativistic two-nucleon Schroedinger equation

> The potentials $V (v)$ in the nonrelativistic (relativistic) nucleon-nucleon (NN) Schroedingerequation are related by a quadratic equation. That equation is numerically solved, thus providing phase equivalent v- potentials related for instance to the high precision NN potentials, which are adjusted to NN phase shift and mixing parameters in a nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation. The relativistic NN potentials embedded in a three-nucleon (3N)system for total NN momenta different from zero are also constructed in a numerically precise manner. They enter into the relativistic interacting 3N mass operator, which is needed for relativistic 3N calculations for bound and scattering states.

# Realistic two-nucleon potentials for the relativistic two-nucleon Schroedinger equation

## Key claim
The authors show that the nonrelativistic high-precision NN potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm I,II) can be transformed into phase-equivalent relativistic potentials via an iterative numerical solution of a quadratic operator equation, provided the relativistic and nonrelativistic S-matrices are compared at the same center-of-mass momentum rather than the same energy.

## Method
The quadratic operator relation V = (1/4m)(2ωv + 2vω + v²) linking the nonrelativistic potential V to the relativistic potential v is solved by an iterative scheme in momentum space, starting from a first-order approximation v^(0) = 2m V/(ω(k)+ω(k')). For the three-nucleon system, the same iterative procedure is applied to construct v_p, the NN potential in a moving frame, by replacing ω(k) with ω_p(k) = √(ω(k)² + p²/4). The phase equivalence is verified by solving both the nonrelativistic and relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equations and comparing the resulting phase shifts and mixing parameters.

## Key numbers
- For AV18 in the ¹S₀ state, V(k,k') > v(k,k') with asymptotic falloff: V ~ const/k^N (N=2 for AV18, N=3.5 for CD Bonn) and v ~ const/k^n (n=3 for AV18, n=4.5 for CD Bonn), where n = N+1.
- Iteration convergence for AV18 coupled ³S₁-³D₁ partial waves at k=k'=1.0 fm⁻¹: v^(0)(³S₁-³S₁)=0.084232 fm² converges to v^(30)=0.052126 fm²; v^(0)(³S₁-³D₁)=0.044709 fm² converges to v^(30)=0.044597 fm²; v^(0)(³D₁-³D₁)=0.016853 fm² converges to v^(30)=0.016684 fm².
- Phase shifts from relativistic and nonrelativistic calculations agree to within 0.0001 degrees or better across all tested lab energies (1.0, 10.0, 50.0, 100.0, 350.0 MeV) for the ³S₁-³D₁ coupled channels.
- The moving-frame potential v_p for AV18 ¹S₀ at p=4.0 fm⁻¹ is weaker than v, with an approximate relation v_p(k,k') ≈ v(k,k') (1 - p²/(8 ω(k) ω(k'))).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Carlson (1993) and Forest (1995), who directly fitted a relativistic potential v to NN phase shifts using the Urbana v₁₄ potential (achieving only a fair fit), and improves upon the approximate momentum-scale transformation of Kamada and Gloeckle (1998) and the earlier approximate relation of Gloeckle (1986). The resulting v and v_p potentials enable relativistic three-nucleon calculations using the instant form of dynamics as formulated by Dirac (1949) and Coester (1965), with applications to 3N bound and scattering states planned. The method is directly applicable to any high-precision NN potential adjusted in a nonrelativistic framework.
