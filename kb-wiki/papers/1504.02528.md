# Cross-checking the symmetry energy at high densities

> The nuclear symmetry energy determines the birth of neutron stars and the elementary nucleosynthesis in the universe. To constrain the nuclear symmetry energy, besides the studies in astrophysics, many terrestrial nuclear experiments are being carried out using a wide variety of advanced new facilities. In this paper, by cross-checking two unrelated experimental measurements using the newly updated transport model, we obtained a slightly soft symmetry energy at supra-saturation nuclear densities. While extrapolating to low nuclear densities, this result is compatible with the recent finding by comparing the available data on the electric dipole polarizability with the theoretical predictions. Our finding is expected to push the studies of nuclear physics and astrophysics forward.

# Cross-checking the symmetry energy at high densities

## Key claim
By simultaneously analyzing two unrelated experimental measurements (FOPI pion data and FOPI-LAND elliptic flow data) using a transport model that incorporates both nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations and in-medium inelastic baryon-baryon scattering cross sections, a mildly soft symmetry energy with slope parameter L(ρ₀) = 37 MeV at supra-saturation densities is obtained. This result is compatible with the finding from electric dipole polarizability comparisons reported in Phys. Rev. C 92, 064304 (2015), but the author notes that fully convincing constraints at high density are not easy to achieve due to numerous uncertainties in transport models and experimental measurements.

## Method
The author used the updated Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, incorporating nucleon momentum distributions with high-momentum tails (HMT) from the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with AV18 two-body interaction plus three-body force, and including in-medium reductions of baryon-baryon scattering cross sections via effective mass scaling. Central Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy were simulated, and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio (compared to FOPI data) and the ratio of neutron to proton elliptic flow V₂ⁿ/V₂ᵖ (compared to FOPI-LAND data) were calculated for different symmetry energy stiffness parameters x = 1, 0, -1, 2, 3, corresponding to slope parameters L(ρ₀) of 37, 87, 138, and other values.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L(ρ₀) = 37 MeV for the mildly soft case (x = 1) at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- The density region probed in the heavy-ion collisions is about 1 to 1.5 times saturation density
- The kinetic symmetry energy used is expressed as E_sym^kin = 12.5[(ρ/ρ₀)^(2/3) - 1] MeV
- The in-medium neutron-proton scattering cross section reduction factor R^np_medium is shown for asymmetry δ = 0.2, with experimental density and momentum region labeled as 0.5 to 0.7
- The FOPI-LAND elliptic flow experimental data was multiplied by a factor of 1.15 owing to dispersion of the reaction plane
- Free nucleons are identified by local densities ρ_free ≤ ρ₀/8, corresponding to deuteron's nucleon average density 0.02 fm⁻³
- The result L(ρ₀) = 37 MeV agrees with the range L(ρ₀) = 20 to 66 MeV from electric dipole polarizability comparisons

## Context
This work extends previous efforts to constrain the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, which had fallen into chaos due to conflicting interpretations of FOPI and FOPI-LAND data by different groups (Xiao 2009, Feng 2010, Russotto 2011, Cozma 2013, Xie 2013, Wang 2014). The author's novel inclusion of short-range correlations (SRC) and in-medium inelastic cross sections resolves some discrepancies, and the result is compatible with the electric dipole polarizability constraint from Phys. Rev. C 92, 064304 (2015). This finding enables more reliable predictions for neutron star properties and supernova nucleosynthesis, though the author notes that further theoretical and experimental studies are needed to pin down the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy.
