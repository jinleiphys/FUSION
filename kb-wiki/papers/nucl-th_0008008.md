# Extraction of electromagnetic neutron form factors through inclusive and exclusive polarized electron scattering on polarized 3He target

> Inclusive 3He(e,e') and exclusive 3He(e,e'n) processes with polarized electrons and 3He have been theoretically analyzed and values for the magnetic and electric neutron form factors have been extracted. In both cases the form factor values agree well with the ones extracted from processes on the deuteron. Our results are based on Faddeev solutions, modern NN forces and partially on the incorporation of mesonic exchange currents.

## Key claim

The magnetic and electric neutron form factors extracted from polarized inclusive and exclusive electron scattering on polarized 3He agree well with values extracted from deuteron processes, but the extraction relies on consistent Faddeev solutions and, for the inclusive case, the inclusion of mesonic exchange currents (MECs) which provide an important shift that should not be neglected.

## Method

The inclusive process 3He(e,e') was analyzed using Faddeev solutions for the 3N bound and continuum states with modern NN forces (AV18 and Bonn B), adding pi- and rho-like two-body exchange currents via the Riska prescription. The exclusive process 3He(e,e'n) was analyzed using the same Faddeev framework with a single-nucleon current operator, solving a Faddeev-type integral equation that sums all final-state interactions (FSI) to infinite order. For both processes, theoretical asymmetries were averaged over experimental acceptances (angular spreads, energy bins) via Monte Carlo and compared to measured asymmetries to extract the form factors.

## Key numbers

- For the inclusive process at Q2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)2: GMn / GMn(Dipole) = 0.966 ± 0.014 (stat) ± 0.01 (syst)
- For the inclusive process at Q2 = 0.2 (GeV/c)2: GMn / GMn(Dipole) = 0.962 ± 0.013 (stat) ± 0.01 (syst)
- For the exclusive process at Q2 = 0.40 (GeV/c)2: GEn = 0.052 ± 0.0038 (including experimental error and model dependence)
- The experimental ratio of asymmetries for the exclusive process was Vexp = (-7.26 ± 1.14)%
- The incoming electron beam energy for the inclusive experiment was E = 778 MeV, with central scattering angles theta_e = 24.44 deg (Q2=0.1) and 35.5 deg (Q2=0.2)

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical analyses of inclusive 3He(e,e') by Ishikawa et al. (1998) by adding MECs and analyzing a new, more accurate experiment at JLab (Xu et al.). The exclusive analysis is the first to use consistent Faddeev solutions for the 3N continuum and 3He bound state, going beyond the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and simple single-rescattering approximations (FSI23) used in prior work. The extracted form factors agree with results from deuteron experiments (Anklin et al., 1994 and 1998; Herberg et al., 1999) and enable future investigations of relativistic effects and MEC contributions in the exclusive channel.
