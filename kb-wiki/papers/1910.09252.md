# Feasibility study of the $K^{+} d \to K^{0} p p$ reaction for the "$Θ^{+}$" pentaquark

> We investigate theoretically the $K^{0} p$ invariant mass spectrum of the $K^{+} d \to K^{0} p p$ reaction and scrutinize how the signal of the "$Θ^{+}$" pentaquark, if it exists, emerges in the $K^{0} p$ spectrum. The most prominent advantage of this reaction is that we can clearly judge whether the "$Θ^{+}$" exists or not as a direct-formation production without significant backgrounds, in contrast to other reactions such as photoproduction and $π$-induced productions. We show that while the impulse or single-step scattering process can cover the "$Θ^{+}$" energy region with an initial kaon momentum $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.40\, \mathrm{GeV} / c$ in the laboratory frame, the contributions from double-step processes may have a potential possibility to reach the "$Θ^{+}$" energy region with a higher kaon momentum $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \sim 1 \, \mathrm{GeV} / c$. Assuming that the full decay width of the "$Θ^{+}$" is around $0.5\, \mathrm{MeV}$, we predict that the magnitude of the peak corresponding to the "$Θ^+$" is around a few hundred $μ\mathrm{b}$ to $1\, \mathrm{mb}$ with the momentum of the kaon beam $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.40\, \mathrm{GeV} / c$ while it is around $\lesssim 1\, μ\mathrm{b}$ with $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.85\, \mathrm{GeV} / c$. Thus, the "$Θ^+$" peak is more likely to be seen at $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.40\, \mathrm{GeV} / c$ than at $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.85 \,\mathrm{GeV} / c$.

## Key claim

The $K^{+} d \to K^{0} p p$ reaction can provide a definitive test for the existence of the "$\Theta^{+}$" pentaquark via direct formation with minimal backgrounds, but only if the kaon beam momentum is carefully chosen, the signal is predicted to be observable at $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.40\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ (with cross sections of a few hundred $\mu\mathrm{b}$ to $1\ \mathrm{mb}$) but is much weaker at $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.85\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ (below $1\ \mu\mathrm{b}$), assuming the "$\Theta^{+}$" exists with a full decay width of $0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Method

The authors calculate the $K^{0}p$ invariant mass spectrum of the $K^{+}d \to K^{0}pp$ reaction using a theoretical model that includes both impulse (single-step) scattering and double-step scattering processes, where intermediate kaons propagate between nucleons in the deuteron. The $KN \to KN$ scattering amplitudes are constructed from partial-wave amplitudes up to $D$ waves taken from the SAID program, with the "$\Theta^{+}$" contribution added as an $s$-channel exchange term for four spin/parity assignments ($J^{P} = 1/2^{\pm}$ and $3/2^{\pm}$). The deuteron wave function is taken in an analytic parameterization of the $s$-wave component, and the cross sections are evaluated in the laboratory frame with the deuteron at rest.

## Key numbers

- The "$\Theta^{+}$" mass is taken as $M_{\Theta} = 1524\ \mathrm{MeV}$ and its full decay width is assumed to be $\Gamma_{\Theta} = 0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$, yielding coupling constants $g_{KN\Theta} = 0.783$ for $J^{P}=1/2^{+}$, $0.101$ for $1/2^{-}$, $0.352$ for $3/2^{+}$, and $2.734$ for $3/2^{-}$.
- The impulse scattering process can reach the "$\Theta^{+}$" energy region ($\sim 1.52\ \mathrm{GeV}$) with an initial kaon momentum $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.40\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ in the laboratory frame.
- The predicted magnitude of the "$\Theta^{+}$" peak at $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.40\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ is around a few hundred $\mu\mathrm{b}$ to $1\ \mathrm{mb}$, while at $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 0.85\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ it is around $\lesssim 1\ \mu\mathrm{b}$.
- Double-step processes may reach the "$\Theta^{+}$" energy region with a higher kaon momentum $k_{\mathrm{lab}} \sim 1\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$, but at $k_{\mathrm{lab}} = 0.45\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ they contribute only a few percent to the differential cross section.

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical study by Sibirtsev et al. (2004), who considered only the impulse scattering process for the $K^{+}d \to K^{0}pp$ reaction with a "$\Theta^{+}$" width of $1$–$20\ \mathrm{MeV}$, by including double-step scattering processes and using a narrower assumed width of $0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$. The study is motivated by the ongoing controversy over the existence of the light pentaquark "$\Theta^{+}$", where experiments using photoproduction and $\pi$-induced reactions (such as those by the CLAS Collaboration, KEK-PS E533, and J-PARC E19) found no significant signal, while the LEPS and DIANA Collaborations continued to report evidence. The authors argue that the $K^{+}d$ reaction offers a cleaner direct-formation channel than previous approaches, and their results provide a quantitative guide for future experiments at facilities such as J-PARC.
