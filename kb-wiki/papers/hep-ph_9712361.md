# A general parametrization for the long-range part of neutrinoless double beta decay

> Double beta decay has been proven to be a powerful tool to constrain $B-L$ violating physics beyond the standard model. We present a representation for the long-range part of the general $0νββ$ decay rate allowed by Lorentz-invariance. Combined with the short range part this general parametrization in terms of effective $B-L$ violating couplings will provide the $0νββ$ limits on arbitrary lepton number violating theories.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general parametrization for the long-range part of neutrinoless double beta decay (0νββ) in terms of effective B-L violating couplings, but only the term where the Standard Model V-A current meets a right-handed non-SM current produces stringent limits on the couplings; the case where it meets a left-handed non-SM current is proportional to the unknown neutrino Majorana mass and yields no limits.

## Method

The authors write the general Lorentz-invariant Lagrangian for 0νββ at the Fermi scale in terms of effective couplings εαβ for hadronic and leptonic currents of defined helicity (V±A, S±P, TL, TR). They evaluate the decay amplitude "on axis" assuming only one εαβ is nonzero at a time, and identify which combinations of currents produce contributions proportional to the neutrino momentum (~100 MeV) rather than the unknown neutrino mass. Limits on the effective couplings are then derived using the half-life limit from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment and nuclear matrix elements calculated in the QRPA approach.

## Key numbers

- Half-life limit from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment: T1/20νββ > 1.1 × 10^25 y
- Limit on εV+AV+A < 8.2 × 10^-7
- Limit on εV+AV-A < 4.5 × 10^-9
- Limit on εS+PS+P = εS+PS-P < 1.2 × 10^-8, derived using nuclear matrix element M1(ν) = 2.1 calculated in the QRPA approach
- Limit on εTRTR < O(10^-9) for the special case of 76Ge

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Doi (1985) and Hirsch (1996) for left-right symmetric models to a general parametrization covering all Lorentz-invariant current combinations in the long-range part of 0νββ. Combined with the short-range part and derivative couplings, this parametrization enables the derivation of double beta decay constraints for arbitrary lepton number violating theories beyond the Standard Model. The authors note that further work should include interference terms and justify the assumptions and approximations used.
