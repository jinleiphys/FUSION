# An interpretation of saturation phenomena as Glauber-Gribov multiple parton scatterings

> We compare two formalism that describe minijet production in pA and AA collisions: pQCD supplemented by Glauber-Gribov multiple semihard parton scatterings (pQCD+Glauber), and the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC). We argue that in a suitable limit they are equivalent to each other, the PQCD+Glauber model being more accurate from a numerical point of view. Finally, we analyze RHIC data on Au-Au integrated charged multiplicities in the pQCD+Glauber framework, and conclude that at least at central rapidity there is no sign of gluon saturation.

# An interpretation of saturation phenomena as Glauber-Gribov multiple parton scatterings

## Key claim

The pQCD+Glauber model and the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism are equivalent in a kinematic region where parton densities are low enough for gluon saturation effects to be negligible but high enough for multiple scatterings and unitarity effects to become important; at central rapidity, RHIC Au-Au data on charged multiplicities show no sign of gluon saturation when described by the pQCD+Glauber model with free-nucleon PDFs.

## Method

The authors compare two formalisms for minijet production in pA and AA collisions: leading-order pQCD with Glauber-Gribov multiple semihard parton scatterings (pQCD+Glauber) and the Colour Glass Condensate effective theory. They demonstrate equivalence by separating soft and hard interactions in the CGC dipole-nucleon cross section, using the DGLAP equation to approximate the unintegrated gluon distribution, and showing that the resulting hard dipole-nucleus cross section matches the pQCD+Glauber expression with the infrared cutoff p0 set equal to the saturation scale Qs. They then apply the pQCD+Glauber model to RHIC Au-Au data on charged particle pseudorapidity densities, computing minijet multiplicities with a saturation cutoff psat and converting to charged multiplicities via isentropic expansion and parton-hadron duality.

## Key numbers

- Minimum transverse momentum exchange for semihard scattering: p0 ≈ 1–2 GeV
- Saturation momentum evolution: Qs^2(τ) = Q0^2 exp(4.84 ᾱs (τ − τ0)), where τ0 is the minimum rapidity for gluon saturation and Q0 is the corresponding saturation scale
- Charged multiplicity per participant pair computed for Au-Au collisions at √s = 56–200 GeV (central collisions used to fit soft component nsoft) and extrapolated down to 19 GeV
- Model results compared to PHOBOS collaboration data (PHOBOS02) for charged particle multiplicity per participant pair as a function of number of participants at multiple center-of-mass energies

## Context

This work extends the pQCD+Glauber model of Accardi and Triantafyllopoulos (2001) and establishes its equivalence to the CGC framework of Iancu (2002) in a specific kinematic regime. The authors compare their results to the KLN saturation model of Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2001), finding that RHIC data at central rapidity are well described without invoking gluon saturation. The paper enables future searches for novel nuclear effects beyond multiscatterings and unitarity by establishing the pQCD+Glauber model as a baseline for more differential observables, particularly the Cronin effect in dA collisions at RHIC.
