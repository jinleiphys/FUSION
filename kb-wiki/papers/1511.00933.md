# Chiral transport of neutrinos in supernovae: Neutrino-induced fluid helicity and helical plasma instability

> Chirality of neutrinos modifies the conventional kinetic theory and hydrodynamics, leading to unusual chiral transport related to quantum anomalies in field theory. We argue that these corrections have new phenomenological consequences for hot and dense neutrino gases, especially in core-collapse supernovae. We find that the neutrino density can be converted to the fluid helicity through the chiral vortical effect. This fluid helicity effectively acts as a chiral chemical potential for electrons via the momentum exchange with neutrinos and induces a "helical plasma instability" that generates a strong helical magnetic field. This provides a new mechanism for converting the gravitational energy released by the core collapse to the electromagnetic energy and potentially explains the origin of magnetars. The other possible applications of the neutrino chiral transport theory are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chirality of neutrinos modifies conventional hydrodynamics in core-collapse supernovae, allowing neutrino density to be converted into fluid helicity via the chiral vortical effect; this fluid helicity then acts as an effective chiral chemical potential for electrons and can trigger a "helical plasma instability" that generates strong helical magnetic fields, potentially explaining magnetar formation, though the authors explicitly state their argument is "schematic" and requires future quantitative verification.

## Method

The author derives the chiral kinetic theory and chiral hydrodynamics for neutrinos, incorporating the Berry curvature, quantum anomaly, chiral magnetic effect, and chiral vortical effect into the standard Boltzmann equation and relativistic hydrodynamic equations. These chiral transport equations are then applied to the neutrino gas in core-collapse supernovae, assuming the hydrodynamic regime is valid at the core where the mean free path is small, and the evolution of magnetic fields is studied via a modified magnetohydrodynamic transport equation that includes the chiral vortical and chiral magnetic contributions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum helical magnetic field at the core: \(B_{\rm core} \sim 10^{18}\) Gauss, estimated for an initial neutrino chemical potential \(\mu_{\nu} \sim 200\) MeV.
- Neutrino chemical potential at the core: \(\mu_{\nu} \sim 200\) MeV, with temperature \(T \sim 10\) MeV, satisfying \(\mu \gg T\).
- Transport coefficient for the chiral vortical effect for left-handed neutrinos: \(\xi \simeq -\mu^2/(24\pi^2)\) in the limit \(\mu \gg T\).
- Anomaly coefficient for left-handed fermions: \(C = -1/(4\pi^2)\).
- Mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly coefficient: \(D = \pm 1/12\) for right- and left-handed fermions, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory and chiral hydrodynamics developed by Son (2009), Son and Stephanov (2012), and others to the specific case of neutrinos in core-collapse supernovae, where chirality had been previously neglected. The mechanism is analogous to the proposal by Ohnishi (2014) for generating strong helical magnetic fields via a chiral chemical potential for electrons, but the author notes that fluid helicity generated in the dense neutrino medium cannot be damped by the electron mass, unlike the electron chiral chemical potential. The paper enables future three-dimensional neutrino-radiation hydrodynamics simulations that incorporate chiral effects, which could address both the supernova explosion mechanism and the origin of magnetars.
