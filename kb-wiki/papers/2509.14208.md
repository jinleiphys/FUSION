# Gluon generalized transverse momentum dependent parton distributions and Wigner functions of the Proton

> We present a calculation of the leading-twist (twist-2) generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs) for unpolarized and longitudinally polarized gluons in the proton, using a recently developed light-front gluon spectator model inspired by the soft-wall AdS/QCD prediction. We calculate the gluon GTMDs for non-zero skewness, using overlaps in terms of light-front wave functions. We also compute the gluon Wigner distributions for various polarization configurations of the proton target, including unpolarized, longitudinally polarized, and transversely polarized states. We present these Wigner distributions and the associated spin densities in both the transverse momentum plane and the transverse impact parameter plane. Furthermore, we evaluate the gluon orbital angular momentum and spin-orbit correlations, and extract the generalized parton distribution and transverse momentum-dependent distribution limits of the GTMDs.

## Key claim

The authors compute leading-twist gluon GTMDs for nonzero skewness in the DGLAP region using a light-front gluon spectator model, finding that the resulting gluon angular momentum J_z^g = 0.205 ± 0.013 is consistent with several lattice QCD and other nonperturbative determinations, though the extracted gluon kinetic orbital angular momentum L_z^g = −0.22 is larger than the value −0.123 reported by Tan (2023).

## Method

The authors model the proton as an effective two-particle system of an active spin-1 gluon and a spin-1/2 spectator, using light-front wave functions whose transverse momentum dependence follows a Gaussian form inspired by the soft-wall AdS/QCD framework. They compute the eight leading-twist (twist-2) gluon GTMDs (four F-type for unpolarized gluons and four G-type for longitudinally polarized gluons) via overlaps of these light-front wave functions in the DGLAP region (1 > x > ξ), then obtain Wigner distributions by Fourier transforming the GTMDs over transverse momentum transfer at zero skewness.

## Key numbers

*   Gluon total angular momentum: J_z^g = 0.205 ± 0.013, obtained from the Mellin moment of zero-skewness GPDs at a scale of 2 GeV.
*   Gluon kinetic orbital angular momentum: L_z^g = −0.22, extracted from zero-skewness GPDs.
*   Spectator mass: M_X = 0.985^{+0.044}_{−0.045} GeV, set larger than the proton mass to ensure stability.
*   The model parameters (κ, N_g, a, b) were determined by fitting to the gluon unpolarized PDFs using the NNPDF3.0 data set at the scale Q_0 = 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of gluon GTMDs and Wigner distributions that were largely restricted to zero skewness, such as those by Lorce (2011), Chakrabarti (2016, 2017), and More (2017). It builds on the authors' own gluon spectator model (Chakrabarti 2023) previously applied to gluon TMDs, GPDs, and gravitational form factors, and enables future phenomenological studies of gluon GTMDs in processes like diffractive vector meson production and dijet production in deep-inelastic scattering. The computed gluon angular momentum agrees with lattice QCD results from the ETM Collaboration (2020), BLFQ (Nair 2025), DSE (Tandy 2025), and χQCD (Wang 2021), while the kinetic OAM differs from the value reported by Tan (2023).
