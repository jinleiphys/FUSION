# Proton Tomography Through Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering

> In this prize talk, I recall some of the history surrounding the discovery of deeply virtual Compton scattering, and explain why it is an exciting experimental tool to obtain novel tomographic pictures of the nucleons at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV facility and the planned Electron-Ion Collider in the United States.

## Key claim

Deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) provides a practical experimental tool to measure the form factors of the quark and gluon energy-momentum tensor, thereby enabling access to the total angular momentum contributions of quarks and gluons to the proton spin, but the extraction requires careful modeling of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and reduction of model dependencies to a minimal level.

## Method

The author derived the DVCS amplitude in terms of four new nucleon structure functions, H, E, \(\tilde{H}\), and \(\tilde{E}\), called off-forward parton distributions (later renamed generalized parton distributions, GPDs). These functions depend on the Feynman parton momentum \(x\), the t-channel momentum transfer \(\Delta^2 = t\), and the longitudinal momentum transfer \(\xi\). The first moment of the sum \(H+E\) yields the form factors \(A(t)+B(t)\) of the energy-momentum tensor, and the total quark and gluon angular momentum is given by \(J_{q,g} = \frac{1}{2}[A_{q,g}(0)+B_{q,g}(0)]\).

## Key numbers

- The EMC spin result gave \(\langle S_z\rangle_{\text{quark}} = +0.06(8)\) for the proton spin fraction carried by quark spin, with \(S_{\text{proton}}=1/2\).
- After two decades of experiments, quark spin accounts for about 1/3 of the proton spin.
- The RHIC spin program measured gluon polarization \(\Delta G\) at the level of approximately \(0.2\hbar\), nearly ten times smaller than originally expected.
- The Jefferson Lab 12 GeV facility will operate with luminosities of \(10^{35}\) to \(10^{39} / \text{cm}^2 \text{s}\).
- The original DVCS paper (Ji, 1997) has received over 1400 citations; the two EMC papers each have received more than 1500 citations.

## Context

This work extends the spin crisis initiated by the EMC result (Ashman, 1987; Ashman, 1989), which showed that quark spin accounts for only a small fraction of the proton spin, contradicting simple quark model predictions. The DVCS formalism enables phase-space tomography of the nucleon, building on earlier work by Burkardt (2000) on impact-parameter-dependent parton distributions and by Belitsky, Yuan, and Ji (2003) on Wigner distributions. The approach will be tested at the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV facility and ultimately at the planned Electron-Ion Collider, which is recommended as the highest priority for new facility construction after FRIB in the 2015 long-range plan for nuclear science.
