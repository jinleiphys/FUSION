# Phases of asymmetric nuclear matter with broken space symmetries

> Isoscalar Cooper pairing in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter occurs between states populating two distinct Fermi surfaces, each for neutrons and protons. The transition from a BCS-like to the normal (unpaired) state, as the isospin asymmetry is increased, is intervened by superconducting phases which spontaneously break translational and rotational symmetries. One possibility is the formation of a condensate with a periodic crystallinelike structure where Cooper pairs carry net momentum (the nuclear Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell-phase). Alternatively, perturbations of the Fermi surfaces away from spherical symmetry allow for minima in the condensate free energy which correspond to a states with quadrupole deformations of Fermi surfaces and zero momentum of the Cooper pairs. In a combined treatment of these phases we show that, although the Cooper pairing with finite momentum might arise as a local minimum, the lowest energy state features are deformed Fermi surfaces and Cooper pairs with vanishing total momentum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, although the nuclear Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase with finite-momentum Cooper pairs may arise as a local minimum in the free energy, the lowest-energy state for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter features quadrupole-deformed Fermi surfaces and Cooper pairs with vanishing total momentum (the DFS phase), under the condition that the conformal deformation parameter is set to zero.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation for isoscalar neutron-proton pairing in the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel using the Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction, within a finite-temperature real-time Green's functions formalism in the quasiclassical approximation. They treated the total momentum of Cooper pairs \(P\) (LOFF phase) and the relative quadrupole deformation parameter \(\delta\epsilon\) (DFS phase) as variational parameters, minimizing the free energy difference between superconducting and normal states at fixed density and isospin asymmetry. The net density was fixed at the empirical saturation density \(\rho_s = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\), and computations were carried out for a fixed isospin asymmetry \(\alpha = 0.35\) at temperature \(T = 3\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap is normalized to its value \(\Delta_{00}\) in the asymmetric BCS state at \(\alpha = 0.35\); the gap increases for small perturbations in \(P\) and \(\delta\epsilon\) from the asymmetric BCS state, then falls off beyond a maximum.
- The density asymmetry was fixed at \(\alpha = 0.35\), above the critical values where the LOFF and DFS phases set in.
- The temperature was fixed at \(T = 3\) MeV, with ratios of temperature over Fermi energy and pairing gap in the symmetric state of 0.1 and 0.4, respectively.
- The net matter density was fixed at \(\rho_s = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\); the typical nucleon energies are below the laboratory energy \(E_{LAB} = 220\) MeV where the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) interaction becomes repulsive.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the nuclear LOFF phase (Müther and Sedrakian, 2000; Isaev, 2001) and the DFS phase (Müther and Sedrakian, 2002) by providing a combined treatment of both phases within the same model. The results contradict the possibility that the LOFF phase is the ground state for asymmetric nuclear matter, showing instead that the DFS phase with deformed Fermi surfaces and zero Cooper-pair momentum has lower free energy. The findings are relevant for understanding pairing in neutron star matter and supernova matter, where isospin asymmetry is significant, and they parallel studies of crystalline color superconductivity in high-density QCD (Alford, 2001; Rajagopal, 2001).
