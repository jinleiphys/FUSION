# Fluid Dynamical Prediction of Changed v1-flow at LHC

> Substantial collective flow is observed in collisions between Lead nuclei at LHC as evidenced by the azimuthal correlations in the transverse momentum distributions of the produced particles. Our calculations indicate that the Global v1-flow, which at RHIC peaked at negative rapidities (named as 3rd flow component or anti-flow), now at LHC is going to turn toward forward rapidities (to the same side and direction as the projectile residue). Potentially this can provide a sensitive barometer to estimate the pressure and transport properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma. Our calculations also take into account the initial state Center of Mass rapidity fluctuations, and demonstrate that these are crucial for v1 simulations. In order to better study the transverse momentum flow dependence we suggest a new "symmetrized" v1S flow component; and we also propose a new method to disentangle Global v1 flow from the contribution generated by the random fluctuations in the initial state. This will enhance the possibilities of studying the collective Global v1 flow both at the STAR Beam Energy Scan program and at LHC.

## Key claim

Fluid dynamical simulations predict that the Global directed flow v1 at LHC will reverse sign compared to RHIC, peaking at forward rapidities (same side as the projectile residue) rather than at negative rapidities (anti-flow), provided that initial-state center-of-mass rapidity fluctuations are taken into account; these fluctuations strongly suppress the observable v1 signal and may explain why preliminary LHC data show v1 near zero.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional perfect-fluid hydrodynamical simulations for Pb+Pb collisions at 1.38 + 1.38 A·TeV using the Particle-in-Cell method, with an initial state from an effective string rope model that produces a tilted energy-density distribution. The MIT Bag Model equation of state was used throughout, and freeze-out was taken at a constant time of 8 fm/c after formation of the hydro initial state, with transition to an ideal massless pion Juttner gas via the Cooper-Frye formula. The authors introduced a symmetrized v1^S(p_t) function and a method to separate Global v1 from random fluctuation contributions by analyzing even and odd components of v1^S.

## Key numbers

- The calculated v1(y) reaches a peak of 26% at y = ±0.5 for b = 0.7 b_max at freeze-out time 8 fm/c (before including CM rapidity fluctuations).
- At lower energies in the same FD model, v1 peaked in the "backward" direction with magnitude 5% at 158 A·GeV (SPS) and 2-3% at 65 + 65 A·GeV (RHIC).
- Experimentally at RHIC, the highest v1 values were approximately 0.6% at 62.4 + 62.4 A·GeV and 0.2% at 200 A·GeV, with peaks at |y| ≈ 1.
- The fluid cell size used was (0.438 fm)^3 for peripheral collisions with impact parameter b = 0.5 - 0.7 b_max; initially 2500-5400 fluid cells contained matter, increasing to over 100,000 by the end of calculation.
- The y-integrated v1(p_t) vanishes to an accuracy better than 10^{-16} due to antisymmetry in rapidity.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier fluid dynamical calculations at SPS and RHIC energies (Csernai and collaborators, 1999 and 2007) which predicted the "3rd flow component" or anti-flow at lower energies, and contrasts with the preliminary ALICE results from 2011 showing v1 near zero at midrapidity. The paper proposes a new method to disentangle Global v1 flow from random fluctuation contributions (which were studied by Schenke and others, 2011), and suggests that the predicted forward-directed v1 could serve as a sensitive barometer for pressure and transport properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma, potentially measurable at both the STAR Beam Energy Scan program and LHC.
