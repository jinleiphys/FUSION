# Light-front wave functions of mesons, baryons and pentaquarks, with topology-induced local 4-quark interaction

> We calculate light-front wave functions of mesons, baryons and pentaquarks in a model including constituent mass (representing chiral symmetry breaking), harmonic confining potential, and 4-quark local interaction of 't Hooft type. The model is a simplified version of that used by Jia and Vary. The method used is numerical diagonalization of the Hamiltonian matrix, with certain functional basis. We found that the nucleon wave function displays strong diquar correlations, unlike that for Delta (decuplet) baryon. We also calculate 3-quark-5-quark admixture to baryons, and the resulting antiquark sea PDF.

# Light-front wave functions of mesons, baryons and pentaquarks, with topology-induced local 4-quark interaction

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simplified quark model with constituent masses, harmonic confinement, and a topology-induced 4-quark 't Hooft interaction can reproduce hadronic properties on the light front, but only under the drastic simplification that instanton radius is much smaller than hadronic sizes (ρ ≪ R_hadrons), which causes wave functions to become singular at x→1 and requires truncation of the basis expansion by hand.

## Method

The author performs numerical diagonalization of the light-front Hamiltonian matrix using a functional basis of Jacobi polynomials in longitudinal momentum fraction variables (s, t, u, w) for N-particle sectors with N=2,3,4,5. The Hamiltonian includes constituent quark masses (M_q = 0.337 GeV), a harmonic confining term with parameter κ = 0.227 GeV, and a local 4-quark interaction of 't Hooft type that is flavor-antisymmetric and acts only in specific channels (pion, eta-prime, and ud diquark channel in nucleons). Transverse motion is ignored and replaced by a mean energy correction of 0.12 GeV² per particle.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: M_q = 0.337 GeV, confining parameter: κ = 0.227 GeV (borrowed from Jia and Vary (2018))
- Ground state ρ meson mass: approximately 700 MeV (10% lower than experimental value due to neglected transverse confinement)
- Effective mesonic coupling G ≈ 0.65 GeV², fixed by requiring the pion to be massless
- Mass squared of lowest 3-quark state (Delta baryon): 1.28 GeV²; with transverse correction (2 × 0.12 GeV²) yields M_Δ = 1.23 GeV, in agreement with experimental Breit-Wigner mass 1.230-1.234 GeV
- Mass squared of first excited Delta state: 1.679 GeV²; with transverse correction yields M_Δ' = 1.39 GeV (experimental second Delta state is 1.5-1.7 GeV)
- Effective 4-quark coupling for nucleon: G ≈ 17 GeV², fixed by requiring the lowest state mass to equal the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends the approach of Jia and Vary (2018) for pion and rho mesons to baryons (Delta decuplet and proton octet), pentaquarks (5-quark systems), and 3-quark-5-quark mixing addressing the non-perturbative antiquark sea. It builds on the instanton liquid model (ILM) of Shuryak (1981) and the 't Hooft effective Lagrangian (1976) for topology-induced multiquark interactions. The model is a simplified "bare bones" version intended to bridge the gap between lattice QCD calculations (which accurately include nonperturbative phenomena but struggle with PDFs and light-front wave functions) and light-front quantization approaches that have historically relied on perturbative QCD. The results enable calculation of parton distribution functions from first-principles-inspired models and provide a framework for studying diquark correlations in nucleons versus Delta baryons.
