# 1S0 Proton and Neutron Superfluidity in beta-stable Neutron Star Matter

> We investigate the effect of a microscopic three-body force on the proton and neutron superfluidity in the $^1S_0$ channel in $β$-stable neutron star matter. It is found that the three-body force has only a small effect on the neutron $^1S_0$ pairing gap, but it suppresses strongly the proton $^1S_0$ superfluidity in $β$-stable neutron star matter.

## Key claim

The authors find that a microscopic three-body force (TBF) has only a small effect on the neutron 1S0 pairing gap in beta-stable neutron star matter, but it suppresses strongly the proton 1S0 superfluidity, reducing the peak gap by about 50% and shifting it to a much lower density. This suppression is mainly due to the TBF renormalization of the two-body interaction rather than changes in proton fraction or single-particle energies.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS gap equation for the 1S0 channel using the Argonne AV18 two-body interaction plus a microscopic three-body force constructed via the meson-exchange current approach. The proton fraction and single-particle energies in beta-stable matter are calculated self-consistently using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with continuous choice for the auxiliary potential, including the TBF as an effective two-body interaction averaged over the third nucleon's wave function.

## Key numbers

- Neutron 1S0 pairing gap: maximal value of about 2.8 MeV at a Fermi momentum k_F = 0.8 fm^-1 (corresponding baryon density rho_B = 0.02 fm^-3), with the superfluid phase occurring only for rho_B < 0.1 fm^-3
- Proton 1S0 pairing gap without TBF: peak value of about 0.95 MeV at rho_B = 0.2 fm^-3, extending to rho_B = 0.45 fm^-3
- Proton 1S0 pairing gap with TBF: peak value reduced to about 0.55 MeV, shifted to rho_B = 0.09 fm^-3, with the superfluid phase shrinking to rho_B = 0.3 fm^-3
- Proton fractions in beta-stable matter: at rho_B = 0.4 fm^-3, Y_p = 0.0872 (AV18 only) versus Y_p = 0.1499 (AV18 + TBF)

## Context

This work extends previous BHF calculations of 1S0 pairing gaps in beta-stable matter by Baldo et al. (1998) using AV14 and Paris potentials, and by Elgaroy et al. (1996) using the Bonn potential, by adding a microscopic three-body force. The results show that TBF effects are negligible for neutron 1S0 pairing due to its low-density range, but strongly suppress proton 1S0 superfluidity, which is critical for neutron star cooling via modified URCA processes. The predicted suppression is compatible with the observational constraints from long-period precession in isolated pulsars reported by Link (2003).
