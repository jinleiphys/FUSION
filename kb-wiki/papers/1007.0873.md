# Effective contact pairing forces from realistic calculations in infinite homogeneous nuclear matter

> Non-empirical effective contact pairing forces to be used in self-consistent mean-field calculations are presented. These pairing forces, constructed so as to reproduce exactly any given microscopic pairing gaps in infinite homogeneous nuclear matter for any isospin asymmetry, are given in analytical form. As a by-product, this work provides an analytical solution of the BCS gap equations which could be applied to describe various many-body systems.

## Key claim

Non-empirical effective contact pairing forces can be constructed that exactly reproduce any given microscopic \(^1S_0\) pairing gaps in infinite homogeneous nuclear matter for any isospin asymmetry, but the analytical expression derived relies on a weak-coupling approximation that assumes the pairing gap is much smaller than both the Fermi energy and the energy cutoff.

## Method

The author derives an analytical expression for the integral \(I_q\) appearing in the HFB equations for infinite uniform matter by expanding the integrand in powers of \(\Delta_q/\varepsilon^{(q)}_{\mathrm{F}}\) and summing all coefficients, keeping lowest-order terms in \(\Delta_q/\varepsilon^{(q)}_{\mathrm{F}}\) and \(\Delta_q/\varepsilon_\Lambda\). This yields a closed-form expression for the pairing strength \(v^{\pi\,q}[\rho_n,\rho_p]\) via Eq. (3), which is then tested against numerical integration using the effective interaction underlying the HFB-17 nuclear mass model, with the pairing force adjusted to reproduce \(^1S_0\) gaps from Brueckner calculations using the Argonne \(V18\) potential.

## Key numbers

- The pairing cutoff was fixed at \(\varepsilon_\Lambda = 16\) MeV for the HFB-17 model calculations.
- The maximum of the gap function \(\Delta_q(\rho_n,\rho_p)\) is about \(4.8\) MeV in symmetric matter and \(2.6\) MeV in neutron matter for the adopted gaps.
- The largest relative error between the numerically calculated pairing strength and the weak-coupling approximation is only a few percent for \(\varepsilon_\Lambda = 8\) MeV.
- In the low-density limit \(\rho \rightarrow 0\), the pairing strength is given by \(v^{\pi\,q}[\rho\rightarrow0] = -\frac{4\pi^2}{\sqrt{\varepsilon_\Lambda}}\left(\frac{\hbar^2}{2 M_q}\right)^{3/2}\).
- The optimal value of the cutoff from experimental phase shifts is \(\varepsilon_\Lambda \sim 7-8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Garrido et al. (1999) and Margueron et al. (2008), who fitted parametric forms of the pairing interaction to microscopic gaps, by instead deriving an exact analytical expression that can reproduce any given gap function without fitting parameters. The results enable efficient implementation of non-empirical pairing forces in large-scale nuclear structure calculations such as fission barriers and neutron-star crust studies, and provide an analytical solution of the BCS gap equations applicable to other many-body systems. The method was applied in the HFB-17 nuclear mass model of Goriely, Chamel, and Pearson (2009), which achieved an rms deviation of 0.581 MeV for 2149 measured nuclear masses.
