# Spectroscopy and femtoscopic correlation function of the $B\bar{D}$, $B=(N, Δ)$ system in quark delocalization color screening model

> In this work, we systematically investigate the pentaquark systems with quark contents $qqqq\bar{c}$ with the analyzed total spin and parity quantum numbers of $J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-}$, $J^{P}=\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ and $J^{P}=\frac{5}{2}^{-}$, in the I=0, I=1 and I=2 isospin channels. The effective potentials between baryon and meson clusters are given, and the possible bound states are also investigated. Also, the study of the scattering process of the open channels is performed to identify possible resonance states. Our estimations indicate that several possible bound states and narrow baryon-meson resonances are found from corresponding the calculation processes. Furthermore, to bridge the gap between theoretical predictions and experimental measurement, we also extract the low-energy scattering parameters and compute the femtoscopic correlation functions for the $N\bar{D}$ system using the CATS framework. The results demonstrate that the predicted $I=0$ bound state manifests as a significant enhancement at low momentum accompanied by a characteristic suppression. In contrast, the $I=1$ correlations remain relatively flat as the predicted resonances are kinematically distant from the threshold. The $I=2$ sector exhibits strong spin dependence, where the bound state signal in the $J=1/2^{-}$ channel is largely masked by repulsive components in spin-averaged observables. This cancellation effect suggests that future experimental searches at ALICE and LHCb may require spin-selective measurements to identify such states. These predictions provide crucial theoretical guidance for future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the quark delocalization color screening model predicts several possible bound states and narrow baryon-meson resonances in the $B\bar{D}$ ($B=N,\Delta$) system with $qqqq\bar{c}$ quark content, but these predictions are conditional on the specific isospin and spin channels; in particular, the $I=0$ bound state signal in spin-averaged observables is largely masked by repulsive components, requiring spin-selective measurements for experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors employed the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) with a Hamiltonian including color-confining, one-gluon exchange, and Goldstone-boson exchange potentials to study $uudu\bar{c}$ pentaquark systems. They solved the five-body bound state problem using the resonating group method (RGM) for single-channel and coupled-channel calculations across $J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-},\frac{3}{2}^{-},\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ and isospin $I=0,1,2$. They extracted low-energy scattering parameters from RGM phase shifts and computed femtoscopic correlation functions for the $N\bar{D}$ system using the CATS framework.

## Key numbers

- $I(J^{P})=0(\frac{1}{2}^{-})$ system: coupled-channel calculation yields a bound state at approximately 2802 MeV, with binding energy of -1 MeV below the $N\bar{D}$ threshold
- $I(J^{P})=1(\frac{1}{2}^{-})$ system: single-channel $\Delta\bar{D}^{*}$ bound state at 3203 MeV with binding energy of -36 MeV relative to the $\Delta\bar{D}^{*}$ theoretical threshold
- $I(J^{P})=1(\frac{3}{2}^{-})$ system: $\Delta\bar{D}$ channel binding energy of -14 MeV; $\Delta\bar{D}^{*}$ channel binding energy of -26 MeV
- $I(J^{P})=1(\frac{5}{2}^{-})$ system: $\Delta\bar{D}^{*}$ bound state with binding energy of -28 MeV below the $\Delta\bar{D}^{*}$ threshold
- $I(J^{P})=2(\frac{1}{2}^{-})$ system: $\Delta\bar{D}^{*}$ bound state with binding energy of -10 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $N\bar{D}$ systems using heavy quark symmetry by Yasui (2009) and Yamaguchi (2011), which found bound states in the $I(J^{P})=0(\frac{1}{2}^{-})$ channel, and contrasts with models by Haidenbauer (2007) and Carames (2012) that suggested no significant attraction in the $N\bar{D}$ interaction. The results are consistent with the LHCb $P_{c}$ states previously described by the same QDCSM framework. The computation of femtoscopic correlation functions provides direct theoretical guidance for ongoing proton-charm femtoscopy measurements by the ALICE collaboration, enabling experimental searches at ALICE and LHCb to identify these predicted states through spin-selective measurements.
