# Single $α$-particle orbits and Bose-Einstein condensation in $^{12}$C

> The $α$-bosonic properties such as single-$α$ orbits and occupation numbers in $J^π$=$0^+$, $2^+$, $1^-$ and $3^-$ states of $^{12}$C around the $3α$ threshold are investigated with the semi-microscopic $3α$ cluster model. As in other studies, we found that the $0^+_2$ ($2^+_2$) state has dilute-$3α$-condensate-like structure in which the $α$ particle is occupied in the single $S$ ($D$) orbit with about 70% (80%) probability. The radial behaviors of the single-$α$ orbits as well as the occupation numbers are discussed in detail in comparison with those for the $0^+_1$ and $2^+_1$ states together with the $1^-_1$ and $3^-_1$ states.

## Key claim

The $0^+_2$ and $2^+_2$ states of $^{12}$C are not ideal dilute $3\alpha$ condensates (which would require 100% occupation of a single orbit), but rather have "dilute-$3\alpha$-condensate-like structure" with approximately 70% and 80% occupation probabilities, respectively, due to the perturbing effect of the antisymmetrizer among nucleons.

## Method

The authors solved the $3\alpha$ orthogonality condition model (OCM) equation for $^{12}$C using a Gaussian basis expansion, with a phenomenological $2\alpha$ potential fitted to $\alpha\alpha$ scattering phase shifts and an effective repulsive $3\alpha$ potential. They extracted single-$\alpha$ particle orbits and occupation numbers by diagonalizing the one-body density matrix constructed from the $3\alpha$ OCM wave function for the $0^+_1$, $0^+_2$, $2^+_1$, $2^+_2$, $1^-_1$, and $3^-_1$ states.

## Key numbers

- The $0^+_2$ state has an $\alpha$ particle occupied in the single $S$ orbit with about 70% probability.
- The $2^+_2$ state has an $\alpha$ particle occupied in the single $D$ orbit with about 80% probability.
- The $0^+_2$ state of $^{12}$C is located at $E_x = 7.65$ MeV, which is $E_{3\alpha} = 0.38$ MeV above the $3\alpha$ threshold.
- The $2^+_2$ state was observed at $E_{3\alpha} = 2.6 \pm 0.3$ MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha = 1.0 \pm 0.3$ MeV.
- The $3^-_1$ state is at $E_{3\alpha} = 2.37$ MeV and the $1^-_1$ state at $E_{3\alpha} = 3.57$ MeV above the $3\alpha$ threshold.
- The calculated nuclear radius for the $0^+_2$ state is about 4 fm, compared to about 2.5 fm for the ground state.
- The $0^+_2$ wave function from $3\alpha$ GCM and RGM calculations has a squared overlap of more than 90% with the single $3\alpha$ condensate wave function.
- The $2^+_2$ state has a squared overlap of about 88% with the single condensate wave function of $3\alpha$ gas-like structure.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Tohsaki (2001), who proposed the $N\alpha$ condensate-type wave function and applied it to $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, and of Matsuura (2004), who first attempted to derive $\alpha$-boson properties for $0^+$ states in $^{12}$C from a microscopic $3\alpha$ RGM model but used normalized spectroscopic amplitudes as an approximation. The present study directly uses the $3\alpha$ OCM wave function to compute single-$\alpha$ orbits and occupation numbers, enabling quantitative assessment of how close the $0^+_2$ and $2^+_2$ states are to ideal Bose-Einstein condensation, and extends the analysis to negative-parity states ($1^-_1$ and $3^-_1$) for the first time.
