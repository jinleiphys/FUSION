# Hadron-Hadron Correlation and Interaction from Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We investigate the $ΛΛ$ and $K^-p$ intensity correlations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. First, we examine the dependence of the $ΛΛ$ correlation on the $ΛΛ$ interaction and the $ΛΛ$ pair purity probability $λ$. For small $λ$, the correlation function needs to be suppressed by the $ΛΛ$ interaction in order to explain the recently measured $ΛΛ$ correlation data. By comparison, when we adopt the $λ$ value evaluated from the experimentally measured $Σ^0/Λ$ ratio, the correlation function needs to be enhanced by the interaction. We demonstrate that these two cases correspond to the two analyses which gave opposite signs of the $ΛΛ$ scattering length. Next, we discuss the $K^-p$ correlation function. By using the local $\bar{K}N$ potential which reproduces the kaonic hydrogen data by SIDDHARTA, we obtain the $K^-p$ correlation function. We find that the $K^-p$ correlation can provide a complementary information with the $K^{-}p$ elastic scattering amplitude.

## Key claim

The sign of the ΛΛ scattering length extracted from correlation data depends critically on the assumed value of the pair purity probability λ; when λ is treated as a free parameter a positive scattering length is favored, but when λ is fixed using the measured Σ⁰/Λ ratio a negative scattering length is favored.

## Method

The authors computed ΛΛ and K⁻p correlation functions using both the Koonin-Pratt (KP) formula with explicit potentials and the analytic Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) model, assuming a static spherical source. For the ΛΛ case, they fitted the STAR minimum-bias Au+Au data at √sNN = 200 GeV with either λ free or fixed at (0.67)², including feed-down from Σ⁰ decay and a residual source term. For the K⁻p case, they used a local K̅N potential that reproduces the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data and the coupled-channel scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- ΛΛ scattering length in the free λ case: a₀ = 1.10 ± 0.37⁺⁰·⁰⁸₋₀.₆₈ fm (STAR analysis)
- ΛΛ scattering length in the fixed λ case: 1/a₀ = −1.26 ± 0.74 fm⁻¹ (corresponding to a₀ ≈ −0.79 fm)
- Pair purity probability λ in the free λ case: 0.18 ± 0.05
- Fixed λ value from Σ⁰/Λ ratio: λ = (0.67)² = 0.4489
- Source size R in the fixed λ case: 1.39 ± 0.71 fm
- Source size R in the free λ case: 2.88 ± 0.38 fm
- Effective range r_eff in the free λ case: 8.51 ± 2.14 fm
- χ²/DOF for the fixed λ case: 0.64 (44 DOF)
- χ²/DOF for the free λ case: 0.55 (43 DOF)

## Context

This work resolves a contradiction between two previous analyses of the same STAR ΛΛ correlation data: Morita, Furumoto, and Ohnishi (2015) found a negative scattering length using the KP formula with λ fixed from the Σ⁰/Λ ratio, while the STAR collaboration (2015) found a positive scattering length using the LL model with λ as a free parameter. The authors show that the difference arises solely from the λ assumption, not from the choice of correlation function formula. For K⁻p, the work extends the SIDDHARTA-constrained K̅N interaction studies (Ikeda et al., 2011, 2012; Miyahara et al., 2015) by predicting the K⁻p correlation function as a new observable to further constrain the K̅N amplitude, complementing kaonic hydrogen and elastic scattering data.
