# Search of systematic behavior of breakup probability in reactions with weakly bound projectiles at energies around Coulomb barrier

> Comparing the capture cross sections calculated without the breakup effect and experimental complete fusion cross sections, the breakup was analyzed in reactions with weakly bound projectiles $^{6,7,9}$Li, $^{9,11}$Be, and $^{6,8}$He. A trend of a systematic behavior for the complete fusion suppression as a function of the target charge and bombarding energy is not achieved. The quasielastic backscattering is suggested to be an useful tool to study the behavior of the breakup probability in reactions with weakly bound projectiles.

# Search of systematic behavior of breakup probability in reactions with weakly bound projectiles at energies around Coulomb barrier

## Key claim
No systematic trend of complete fusion suppression as a function of target charge or bombarding energy could be established across reactions with weakly bound projectiles; the authors propose that quasielastic backscattering measurements, rather than direct breakup measurements, can be used to extract breakup probabilities indirectly.

## Method
The authors employed a quantum diffusion approach with a single collective variable (relative distance) to calculate capture cross sections without breakup effects, incorporating neutron transfer channels with positive Q-values and nuclear deformation. They compared these calculated capture cross sections with experimental complete fusion cross sections for reactions induced by ⁶,⁷,⁹Li, ⁹,¹¹Be, and ⁶,⁸He projectiles on various targets, extracting breakup probabilities as P_BU = 1 − σ_fus_exp / σ_c_th.

## Key numbers
- Complete fusion suppression of about 30% was found for reactions ⁶,⁷Li+²⁰⁸Pb,²⁰⁹Bi and ⁹Be+⁸⁹Y,¹²⁴Sn,²⁰⁸Pb,²⁰⁹Bi at energies from about 1.1V_b to 1.5V_b
- The ⁹Be+¹⁴⁴Sm reaction showed a much smaller suppression of about 15%
- Average normalization factors (renormalization of calculated capture to match experiment) ranged from 0.42 (⁶Li+¹⁴⁴Sm) to 1.0 (⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn)
- Breakup probabilities P_BU varied from 0% to 84% at energies above the Coulomb barrier
- In ⁸Be+²⁰⁸Pb, the fraction of σ_fus_BU in σ_fus_exp did not exceed a few percent at E_c.m. − V_b < 4 MeV, reaching about 12–20% at E_c.m. − V_b ≈ 10 MeV
- For reactions ⁹Be+¹²⁴Sn,⁸⁹Y, complete fusion suppression was 30–36%

## Context
This work extends the analysis of breakup effects in fusion reactions previously studied by Gomes and collaborators (2006, 2009) using the universal fusion function formalism, confirming the lack of systematic behavior noted in those studies. The authors propose that quasielastic backscattering, previously used by Timmers (1995), Zhang (2000), and others to study barrier distributions, can serve as an indirect probe of breakup probability by comparing reactions with weakly bound projectiles to those with tightly bound isotopes leading to the same compound nucleus. This approach would enable systematic studies of breakup without requiring direct breakup measurements, which the authors identify as a needed next step.
