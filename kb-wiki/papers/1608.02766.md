# The electromagnetic form factors of the $Λ$ in the timelike region

> The reaction $e^+e^- \to \bar ΛΛ$ is investigated for energies close to the threshold. Specific emphasis is put on the role played by the interaction in the final $\bar ΛΛ$ system which is taken into account rigorously. For that interaction a variety of $\bar ΛΛ$ potential models is employed that have been constructed for the analysis of the reaction $\bar p p \to \bar ΛΛ$ in the past. The enhancement of the effective form factor for energies close to the $\bar ΛΛ$ threshold, seen in pertinent experiments, is reproduced. Predictions for the $Λ$ electromagnetic form factors $G_M$ and $G_E$ in the timelike region and for spin-dependent observables such as spin-correlation parameters are presented.

## Key claim

The enhancement of the effective electromagnetic form factor of the Λ hyperon near the \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) threshold, observed in experiments, can be reproduced by including final-state interactions (FSI) from the \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) system, but the result depends on which of several \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) potential models is used; the model dependence is moderate for the cross section but large for the form factor ratio and phase.

## Method

The reaction \(e^+e^- \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) is calculated in the one-photon exchange approximation, with the final-state \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) interaction treated rigorously by solving a scattering equation. Five different \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) potential models (I, II, III from Haidenbauer (1991) and K, QG from Haidenbauer (1992)), originally fitted to \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) data from the PS185 experiment, are used to account for FSI effects. The bare electromagnetic form factors \(G_M^0\) and \(G_E^0\) are taken as constant and normalized to match the measured cross section at its maximum.

## Key numbers

- The \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) threshold is at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.23138\) GeV.
- The cross section for \(e^+e^- \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) rises sharply from threshold and remains roughly constant for the next 100 MeV, with a maximum value of about 0.2 nb (normalized).
- The \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) potential models describe the total cross section for \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) up to \(p_{\rm lab} \approx 1700\) MeV (corresponding to \(\sqrt{s} \approx 2.32\) GeV or excess energy \(Q \approx 90\) MeV).
- The BaBar collaboration determined the relative phase between \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) to be in the range \(-0.76 < \sin\phi < 0.98\).
- The preliminary BESIII data point suggests a large finite cross section practically at the threshold, which cannot be reproduced by the calculation because the phase-space factor \(\beta\) forces the cross section to zero linearly with the cms momentum \(k_\Lambda\).

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous study of \(e^+e^- \to \bar p p\) (Haidenbauer, 2014), which used chiral effective field theory potentials for the \(\bar N N\) interaction, to the \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) system. It contrasts with the \(\bar N N\) case, where pion exchange creates a strong tensor force; in \(\bar\Lambda\Lambda\) one-pion exchange is forbidden by isospin symmetry. The results enable predictions for spin-dependent observables (polarization, spin-correlation parameters) that can be measured by the BESIII collaboration, exploiting the self-analyzing weak decay \(\Lambda \to p\pi^-\). The work also relates to studies of \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) from the PS185 experiment (Barnes, 2000) and to analyses of \(e^+e^- \to \bar\Lambda_c\Lambda_c\) (Guo, 2010) using SU(4) flavor symmetry.
