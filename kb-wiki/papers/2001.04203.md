# On QCD strings beyond non-interacting model

> We investigate the implications of Nambu-Goto (NG), Lüscher-Weisz (LW) and Polyakov-Kleinert (PK) string actions for the Casimir energy of the QCD flux-tube at one and two loop order at finite temperature. We perform our numerical study on the 4-dim pure SU(3) Yang-Mills lattice gauge theory at finite temperature $β=6.0$. The static quark-antiquark potential is calculated using link-integrated Polyakov loop correlators. At a high temperature-close to the critical point- We find that the rigidity and self-interactions effects of the QCD string to become detectable. The remarkable feature of this model is that it retrieves a correct dependency of the renormalized string tension on the temperature. Good fit to static potential data at source separations $R \ge 0.5$ fm is obtained when including additional two-boundary terms of (LW) action. On the other-hand, at a lower temperature-near the QCD plateau- We detect signatures of two boundary terms of the Lüscher-Weisz (LW) string action. The (LW) string with boundary action is yielding a static potential which is in a good agreement with the lattice data, however, for color source separation as short as $R=0.3$ fm.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including boundary terms in the Lüscher-Weisz (LW) string action, or incorporating rigidity via the Polyakov-Kleinert (PK) action, can resolve deviations between free-string predictions and lattice data for the static quark-antiquark potential at intermediate source separations and high temperatures, but the improvement is conditional on the temperature regime and the specific form of the action used.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of 4-dimensional pure SU(3) Yang-Mills gauge theory at coupling β = 6.0, computing the static quark-antiquark potential from link-integrated Polyakov loop correlators. They compared the lattice data to analytic predictions from the Nambu-Goto (NG) action at one-loop and two-loop order, the Lüscher-Weisz (LW) action including boundary terms at order b₂ and b₄, and the Polyakov-Kleinert (PK) action with an extrinsic curvature (rigidity) term, fitting the string tension and boundary couplings to the numerical data.

## Key numbers

- Lattice simulations were performed at coupling β = 6.0 on a 4-dimensional pure SU(3) Yang-Mills lattice.
- Good fit to static potential data was obtained for source separations R ≥ 0.5 fm when including two-boundary terms of the LW action.
- At lower temperature (near the QCD plateau), the LW string with boundary action yielded good agreement with lattice data for color source separations as short as R = 0.3 fm.
- The renormalized string tension as a function of temperature at leading order is given by σ(T) = σ₀ − π(d−2)T²/6 + O(T⁴), where d = 4 and σ₀ is the zero-temperature string tension.
- At two-loop order, the string tension includes a T⁴ term: σ(T) = σ₀ − π(d−2)T²/6 − π²(d−2)²T⁴/(72σ₀) + O(T⁶).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of effective string descriptions of QCD flux tubes, particularly addressing deviations from the free Nambu-Goto string observed at intermediate distances and high temperatures by Bakry et al. (2010, 2011, 2012). It builds on the LW effective string action developed by Lüscher and Weisz (2004) and the rigid-string formulation of Polyakov (1986) and Kleinert (1986), and is motivated by recent numerical evidence for boundary corrections in U(1) gauge theories by Caselle et al. (2014, 2016) and in SU(N) theories by Brandt et al. (2017). The results enable improved modeling of the QCD string at finite temperature, relevant for mesonic spectroscopy, glueball physics, and string fireball phenomenology.
