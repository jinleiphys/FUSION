# Dissipative Effects in Photon Diagnostics of Quark Gluon Plasma

> The effects of dissipation on the space time evolution of matter formed in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collision is discussed. The thermal photon spectra for RHIC and LHC energies with viscous flow is considered. The effects of viscosity in the thermal single photon spectra is seen to be important in QGP phase as compared to the hadronic phase. Recently available data from WA80 group at SPS energies for $S+Au$ collision are compared with theoretical calculations. The experimental data do not appear to be compatible with the formation of matter in the pure hadronic phase.

## Key claim

Dissipative effects (shear and bulk viscosity) in the relativistic hydrodynamic evolution of quark-gluon plasma reduce the estimated initial temperature compared to ideal-fluid scenarios, and the WA80 data for S+Au collisions at SPS energies are incompatible with a purely hadronic initial phase even when dissipation is included, but can be described by an initial mixed phase of QGP and hadronic matter.

## Method

The authors solve the scaling Navier-Stokes equation (Bjorken flow) with shear viscosity η and bulk viscosity ζ for a first-order QCD phase transition, evolving the system through QGP, mixed, and hadronic phases. They treat the initial temperature Ti as a parameter, evolve the system forward in time to a freeze-out temperature Tf, compute the final rapidity density dN/dy from the freeze-out configuration, and match it to experimental or estimated values to determine Ti. Photon spectra are then computed by convolving the emission rates from QGP (annihilation and Compton processes) and hadronic matter (meson reactions and decays) with the space-time history.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity in QGP phase: 2 T³ ≤ η ≤ 3 T³ (τ T), with η_Q0 = η_Q/T³ = 2.5 used for estimates.
- Shear viscosity in hadronic phase: η_H0 = η_H/T³ ≈ 0.75–1.5 at T_c, with the larger value 1.5 used as a conservative case.
- For RHIC and LHC energies, the initial temperature in the dissipative case is lower than in the ideal case by about 10% or less, and total lifetimes are longer by a similar fraction.
- For SPS (S+Au, dN/dy = 225), a purely hadronic initial phase with dissipative dynamics requires an initial temperature of 262 MeV (for T_f = 100 MeV), far above the critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV.
- With an initial mixed phase at T_c = 160 MeV, the system starts with 80–90% quark matter fraction (depending on T_f) and fits the WA80 data.

## Context

This work extends the ideal-fluid Bjorken scaling hydrodynamics used by earlier authors (e.g., Alam et al., 1993; Kapusta et al., 1991) to include weak dissipation as argued feasible by Danielewicz and Gyulassy (1985). It contradicts the assumption that ideal hydrodynamics suffices for photon diagnostics, showing that dissipation lowers the inferred initial temperature and lengthens the system lifetime. The results enable more realistic estimates of initial conditions at RHIC and LHC and suggest that the WA80 data (Santo et al., 1995) provide a hint of deconfinement, consistent with the mixed-phase picture advocated by Werner (1993).
