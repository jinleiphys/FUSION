# Degeneracy in excited-state quantum phase transitions of two-level bosonic models and its influence on system dynamics

> Excited-state quantum phase transitions (ESQPTs) strongly influence the spectral properties of collective many-body quantum systems, changing degeneracy patterns in different quantum phases. Level degeneracies, in turn, affect the system's dynamics. We analyze the degeneracy dependence on the size of two-level boson models with a $u(n+1)$ dynamical algebra, where $n$ is the number of collective degrees of freedom. Below the ESQPT critical energy of these models, the energy gap between neighboring levels that belong to different symmetry sectors gets close to zero as the system size increases. We report and explain why this gap goes to zero exponentially for systems with one collective degree of freedom, but algebraically in models with more than one degree of freedom. As a consequence, we show that the infinite-time average of out-of-time-order correlators is an ESQPT order parameter in finite systems with $n=1$, but in systems with $n>1$, this average only works as an order parameter in the mean-field limit.

## Key claim

The energy gap between neighboring levels belonging to different symmetry sectors below the ESQPT critical energy decreases exponentially with system size for models with one collective degree of freedom (n=1), but algebraically for models with more than one degree of freedom (n>1); consequently, the infinite-time average of out-of-time-order correlators serves as an ESQPT order parameter in finite systems only for n=1, while for n>1 it works as an order parameter only in the mean-field limit.

## Method

The authors analyzed two-level boson models with a u(n+1) dynamical algebra, specifically the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model (n=1) and the two-dimensional limit of the vibron model (2DVM) (n=2), with extensions to n=3 (vibron model) and n=5 (interacting boson model). They computed energy gaps between states of different symmetry sectors (even/odd parity for LMG; angular momentum ℓ=0 and ℓ=1 for 2DVM) as functions of system size N and control parameter ξ, fitting the scaling behavior to exponential and power laws. They then calculated the infinite-time average of microcanonical out-of-time-order correlators (mOTOCs) using the stationary formula to avoid time-integration, evaluating how this quantity behaves across the ESQPT critical energy for different system sizes.

## Key numbers

- For the LMG model (n=1) at ξ=0.5, the energy splitting between even and odd parity states decreases exponentially with system size N (lin-log axes in Fig. 3a)
- For the 2DVM (n=2) at ξ=0.5, the energy splitting between ℓ=0 and ℓ=1 states decreases algebraically (power law) with system size N (log-log axes in Fig. 3b)
- The ESQPT critical control parameter is ξ_c = 0.2 for both the LMG model and the 2DVM
- For the 2DVM at ξ=0.5, energy differences between ℓ=0 and ℓ=1 states decrease to values between 10^{-3} and 10^{-2} (Fig. 1d)
- Calculations for mOTOC time averages were performed with system size N=300 and control parameter ξ=0.6 (Figs. 4 and 5)
- The time average for mOTOC calculations was performed over a time interval T=1000 (Fig. 5)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of ESQPT degeneracy patterns first noted by Caprio (2008) in two-level boson models, showing that the qualitative difference in scaling (exponential vs. algebraic) arises from the mean-field topology: the LMG model maps to a double-well potential with tunneling exponentially suppressed by barrier height, while the 2DVM maps to a Mexican hat potential where the centrifugal barrier for nonzero angular momentum states prevents exact degeneracy except in the large-N limit. The results explain why the mOTOC order parameter proposed by Wang (2019b) for the LMG model works only approximately for models with n>1, and they connect to experimental realizations of ESQPT precursors in molecular bending spectra (Larese 2011, 2013) and superconducting circuits (Chavez 2023).
