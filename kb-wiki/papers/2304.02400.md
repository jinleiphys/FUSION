# A+2n compound nuclei and the unitary limit in nuclear physics

> This contribution discusses a new perception of the structure of compound nuclei by introducing intermediate states of the Feshbach formalism of nuclear reactions in the Interacting Boson Model of nuclear structure. The stake is to explore the manifestation of the unitary limit in heavy, even-even nuclei. Interactions that govern Feshbach resonances of cold and dilute atomic gases suggest the formulation of an IBM-compound Hamiltonian for scattering two neutrons (2n) from a heavy, even-even target (A). The solutions of the corresponding coupled channel equations host a 2n-IBM state resonance. It turns out that the unitary limit is measurable in a heavy A+2n compound nucleus at low temperatures. That measurement is feasible through the fluctuations of the cross-sections that tune the 2n-A scattering length.

# A+2n compound nuclei and the unitary limit in nuclear physics

## Key claim

The unitary limit (infinite scattering length) is measurable in heavy A+2n compound nuclei at low temperatures through fluctuations of cross-sections that tune the 2n-A scattering length, provided that the energy of the intermediate state of the two neutrons in the compound nucleus coincides with the bound IBM state of N_b+1 bosons (i.e., at the two-neutron separation energy S_2n).

## Method

The author formulates an IBM-compound Hamiltonian for scattering two neutrons from a heavy even-even target, introducing intermediate states of the Feshbach formalism into the Interacting Boson Model. The coupled channel equations are solved with unitary interactions for both the neutron-neutron and the 2n-IBM state scattering, using boundary conditions that replace the interactions. The open channel (target with N_b bosons) is coupled to the first closed channel (target with N_b+1 bosons) through the s^†+s term, which acts as the analog of the magnetic field tuning in cold atomic gases.

## Key numbers

- The compound-elastic cross-section at resonance is given by σ_ce = (4π)^3 / k_r^2 × Γ_1^2 / [(E-E_1)^2 + (Γ_1)^2/4]
- The width of the resonance is Γ_1 = b_1^2 × (4M/ħ^2) × k_r, where b_1^2 = (N_b+1) × |∫ dr Ψ_1(r) Ψ_0(r)|^2
- The two-neutron separation energy S_2n sets the length scale via the harmonic oscillator length a_ho = ħ/√(M S_2n)
- The effective range expansion for 2n-IBM state scattering is 1/a_r(k_r) = 1/a_r - (1/2)k_r^2 r^* + ...
- The cross-section for two-neutron scattering is σ = (4π)^3 / (k_r^2 + 1/a_r^2(k))

## Context

This work extends the concept of the unitary limit, previously explored in light nuclei (Author, year) and well-established in cold atomic gases (Randeria, year; Timmermans, year), to heavy even-even nuclei within the Interacting Boson Model framework. It builds on the Feshbach formalism of nuclear reactions (Feshbach, year) and the Werner-Castin mappings (Castin, year) between zero-energy and trapped states. The approach enables experimental investigation of the unitary limit through fluctuations of cross-sections in A+2n compound nuclei and initiates the examination of conformal symmetry within the group theoretical framework of IBM, with potential connections to QCD at the limit of a large number of gluons (Gross, year).
