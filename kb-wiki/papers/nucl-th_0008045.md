# Two-step contribution to the spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse cross sections of the quasielastic (p,n) reactions

> The two-step contribution to the spin-longitudinal and the spin-transverse cross sections of ^{12}C,^{40}Ca(p,n) reactions at 494 MeV and 346 MeV is calculated. We use a plane-wave approximation and evaluate the relative contributions from the one-step and the two-step processes. We found that the ratios of the two-step to the one-step processes are larger in the spin-transverse cross sections than in the spin-longitudinal ones. Combining these results with the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) results we obtained considerable two-step contributions to the spin-longitudinal and the spin-transverse cross sections. The two-step processes are important in accounting for the underestimation of the DWIA results for the spin-longitudinal and the spin-transverse cross sections.

## Key claim

The two-step contribution to the spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse cross sections of quasielastic (p,n) reactions is calculated using a plane-wave approximation, and the ratios of two-step to one-step processes are found to be larger in the spin-transverse cross sections than in the spin-longitudinal ones; however, the estimation of two-step contributions with distortion is crude, and the results only indicate to what extent the two-step processes are important to account for the discrepancy between distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) results and experimental results.

## Method

The author calculated the two-step contribution to the spin-longitudinal cross section ID_q and the spin-transverse cross section ID_p for ^12C and ^40Ca(p,n) reactions at 494 MeV and 346 MeV using a plane-wave approximation within the Kerman-McManus-Thaler (KMT) multiple scattering formalism. To avoid the singularity in the Green's function at zero epsilon, the author used a finite epsilon in the denominator of the Green's function and performed numerical integration beyond the on-energy shell approximation in the transferred momentum space, then extrapolated the results to the limit of zero epsilon. The two-step cross sections with distortion were roughly estimated by multiplying the uncorrelated DWIA results with the ratios of the two-step to one-step cross sections from the plane-wave calculation and adding these to the DWIA results with random phase approximation (RPA) correlation.

## Key numbers

- For ^12C(p,n) at 494 MeV and omega_lab = 125 MeV, the ratio of two-step to one-step cross sections is 47% for ID_q and 114% for ID_p (with on-energy shell approximation: 24% and 59%, respectively)
- For ^12C(p,n) at 346 MeV and omega_lab = 125 MeV, the ratio is 59% for ID_q and 116% for ID_p (with on-energy shell approximation: 34% and 66%, respectively)
- For ^40Ca(p,n) at 494 MeV and omega_lab = 121 MeV, the ratio is 96% for ID_q and 192% for ID_p (with on-energy shell approximation: 46% and 88%, respectively)
- For ^40Ca(p,n) at 346 MeV and omega_lab = 121 MeV, the ratio is 92% for ID_q and 172% for ID_p (with on-energy shell approximation: 50% and 89%, respectively)
- The two-step cross sections without the on-energy shell approximation are about 1.90 to 1.93 times as large as the results with the on-energy shell approximation for ^12C(p,n) at 494 MeV and energy transfer of 85 MeV

## Context

This work extends the previous plane-wave calculation by Nakaoka and Ichimura (2000) that evaluated the relative contributions of one-step and two-step processes to ID_q and ID_p, but used an on-energy shell approximation to the Green's function. The present calculation removes that approximation and finds the two-step cross sections are about twice as large as the previous results. The two-step processes are shown to have contributions comparable to the discrepancy between the DWIA results of Kawahigashi (1999) and experimental results for ID_q in the highly excited region, and they account for a portion of the previously underestimated ID_p, addressing the long-standing R_L/R_T problem in spin-isospin excitations.
