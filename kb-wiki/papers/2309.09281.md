# Study of the effects of external imaginary electric field and chiral chemical potential on quark matter

> The behavior of quark matter with both external electric field and chiral chemical potential is theoretically and experimentally interesting to consider. In this paper, the case of simultaneous presence of imaginary electric field and chiral chemical potential is investigated using the lattice QCD approach with $N_f=1+1$ dynamical staggered fermions. We find that overall both the imaginary electric field and the chiral chemical potential can exacerbate chiral symmetry breaking, which is consistent with theoretical predictions. However we also find a non-monotonic behavior of chiral condensation at specific electric field strengths and chiral chemical potentials. In addition to this, we find that the behavior of Polyakov loop in the complex plane is not significantly affected by chiral chemical potential in the region of the parameters consider in this paper.

# Study of the effects of external imaginary electric field and chiral chemical potential on quark matter

## Key claim

The paper finds that both an imaginary electric field and a chiral chemical potential generally exacerbate chiral symmetry breaking, consistent with theoretical predictions, but a non-monotonic behavior of chiral condensation is observed at specific small electric field strengths and small chiral chemical potentials (specifically at $a^2eE_z = \pi/24$ and $a\mu_5 = 0.06$) that the abstract does not detail.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with $N_f = 1+1$ dynamical staggered fermions on an $8 \times 8 \times 24 \times 6$ lattice, using a Wick-rotated (imaginary) electric field to avoid the sign problem. The chiral chemical potential is introduced as a linear term in the action, and the electric field is implemented via $U(1)$ phases on gauge links with twisted boundary conditions. Simulations are performed at two temperatures ($\beta = 5.3$ and $\beta = 5.4$) by sequentially increasing the imaginary electric field strength from $a^2eE_z = 0$ to $8\pi/24$ and then the chiral chemical potential from $a\mu_5 = 0$ to $0.48$, with 2900 configurations measured per parameter point.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: $8 \times 8 \times 24 \times 6$ with $N_f = 1+1$ staggered fermions at $am = 0.1$
- At $\beta = 5.3$, the inverse lattice spacing is $a^{-1} = 1215 \pm 12$ MeV, corresponding to temperature $T = 202.5 \pm 2.0$ MeV
- At $\beta = 5.4$, the inverse lattice spacing is $a^{-1} = 1623 \pm 16$ MeV, corresponding to temperature $T = 270.5 \pm 2.7$ MeV
- Electric field quantization: $a^2 e E_z = k \times \pi/24$ for integer $k$ with $0 \leq k \leq 8$
- Chiral chemical potential range: $a\mu_5 = 0.06n$ for $n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots, 8$, giving $\mu_5 \leq 583.2$ MeV at $\beta = 5.3$ and $\mu_5 \leq 779.0$ MeV at $\beta = 5.4$
- Pseudo-critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: $T = 229$ MeV at $\beta = 5.34$
- $\Delta c_u^E \approx 2\Delta c_d^E$ for the change in chiral condensation due to electric field, consistent with the ratio of quark electric charges ($Q_u = 2/3$, $Q_d = -1/3$)
- $\bar{n}_{5,u} > 0$, $\bar{n}_{5,d} < 0$, and $\bar{n}_{5,u} + \bar{n}_{5,d} \approx 0$ for the chiral charge density

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of chiral chemical potential effects by Braguta et al. (2014, 2015, 2019) and Kotov et al. (2015), which found that chiral chemical potential enhances chiral symmetry breaking and chiral susceptibility. It also builds on the study of imaginary electric fields by Yang et al. (2022), which observed Roberge-Weiss-type transitions and oscillations of the Polyakov loop along the $z$-direction. The paper enables future investigations of real electric field effects on quark matter via analytical continuation, and provides a benchmark for effective model calculations such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and Polyakov loop extended NJL model.
