# Nuclear Magnetic Quadrupole Moments in Single Particle Approximation

> A static magnetic quadrupole moment of a nucleus, induced by T- and P-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction, is investigated in the single-particle approximation. Models are considered allowing for analytical solution. The problem is also treated numerically in a Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit interaction. The stability of results is discussed.

# Nuclear Magnetic Quadrupole Moments in Single-Particle Approximation

## Key claim

The static magnetic quadrupole moment (NMQM) induced by T- and P-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction is calculated in the single-particle approximation, with the result that the NMQM is much smaller than the corresponding T-even nuclear anapole moment and is more sensitive to the details of the nuclear model, making it less stable theoretically.

## Method

The authors solve the perturbed Schrodinger equation for a valence nucleon above a spherically-symmetric core, using the T- and P-odd mean field potential derived from the two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction. Analytical solutions are obtained for two simplified models: one where the nuclear density profile coincides with the nuclear potential profile, and one using a harmonic oscillator potential. Numerical calculations are performed using a Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit interaction and a realistic Fermi-type nuclear density profile, including the contribution from the spin-orbit current which only contributes for an outer neutron.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear density parameters: radius R = 1.11 A^(1/3) fm, diffuseness a = 0.54 fm, central density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^(-3)
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: U_0 = (-53.3 +/- 33.6 (N-Z)/A) MeV, spin-orbit strength U_ls = -0.263 (1+2 (N-Z)/A) U_0
- Spin-orbit interaction constant: U^(pn)_ls = 134.3 MeV * fm^5
- Analytical result for the simple model: (delta R|r|R) = (K - 1/2) / (2m U_0)
- Ratio of T-odd NMQM effect to T-even anapole moment effect: approximately 0.15 K A^(-2/3)

## Context

This work extends the earlier evaluation by Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984) which used a simple model where nuclear density and potential profiles coincide. The present paper uses a more realistic Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit interaction, similar to the approach previously applied by the same authors (Dmitriev, Khriplovich, Telitsin, 1994) to nuclear anapole moments. The results enable more reliable predictions for P- and T-odd effects in atoms and molecules, where NMQM provides a mechanism to circumvent electrostatic screening of nuclear electric dipole moments, though the authors note that many-body effects must still be included for fully reliable predictions.
