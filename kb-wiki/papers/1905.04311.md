# A First Determination of Parton Distributions with Theoretical Uncertainties

> The parton distribution functions (PDFs) which characterize the structure of the proton are currently one of the dominant sources of uncertainty in the predictions for most processes measured at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Here we present the first extraction of the proton PDFs that accounts for the missing higher order uncertainty (MHOU) in the fixed-order QCD calculations used in PDF determinations. We demonstrate that the MHOU can be included as a contribution to the covariance matrix used for the PDF fit, and then introduce prescriptions for the computation of this covariance matrix using scale variations. We validate our results at next-to-leading order (NLO) by comparison to the known next order (NNLO) corrections. We then construct variants of the NNPDF3.1 NLO PDF set that include the effect of the MHOU, and assess their impact on the central values and uncertainties of the resulting PDFs.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first extraction of proton PDFs that systematically accounts for the missing higher order uncertainty (MHOU) from fixed-order QCD calculations, demonstrating that this uncertainty can be included via a theory covariance matrix constructed from scale variations; the authors validate their approach at NLO by comparing to known NNLO corrections, finding that the NLO-NNLO shift is fully compatible with the overall uncertainty when the MHOU is included.

## Method

The authors construct a theory covariance matrix S_ij using scale variations as nuisance parameters, classifying input datasets into five process types (DIS NC, DIS CC, Drell-Yan, jet, and top production) and applying 3-point and 9-point scale variation prescriptions with specific correlation patterns between processes. They validate the covariance matrix at NLO by comparing its diagonal elements and eigenvector projections to the known NLO-to-NNLO shifts for 2819 data points, then perform global PDF fits using the NNPDF3.1 methodology with the experimental covariance matrix replaced by the sum of experimental and theory covariance matrices.

## Key numbers

- The fit uses N_dat = 2819 data points, with a lower kinematic cut increased from Q_min^2 = 2.69 GeV^2 to 13.96 GeV^2 to ensure perturbative validity when scales are varied downwards.
- For the 9-point prescription with 5 process types, the theory covariance matrix has N_S = 28 non-zero eigenvalues (independent nuisance parameters), while the 3-point prescription yields N_S = 6.
- The angle theta between the NLO-NNLO shift vector and the subspace spanned by the theory covariance matrix is 26 degrees for the complete dataset with the 9-point prescription; for individual processes the angles are 3 degrees (top), 14 degrees (jets), 22 degrees (DY), 32 degrees (NC DIS), and 16 degrees (CC DIS).
- The chi^2 per datapoint improves from 1.139 (experimental covariance only) to 1.109 (with 9-point theory covariance), while the phi estimator (average uncertainty reduction) increases from 0.314 to 0.415.

## Context

This work extends the NNPDF3.1 PDF set (Ball et al., 2017) by addressing the long-standing question of how missing higher order uncertainties affect PDF determinations, where previous PDF sets only accounted for experimental statistical and systematic errors. The approach builds on earlier proposals for including theoretical uncertainties via covariance matrices (Ball et al., 2018) and using scale variations to estimate MHOUs (Pearson et al., 2018). The results enable a fully consistent treatment of theoretical uncertainties in precision LHC phenomenology and pave the way for the upcoming NNPDF4.0 PDF set to account for MHOUs at both NLO and NNLO, as well as other theory uncertainties such as nuclear corrections.
