# Three- and Four-Body Scattering Calculations including the Coulomb Force

> The method of screening and renormalization for including the Coulomb interaction in the framework of momentum-space integral equations is applied to the three- and four-body nuclear reactions. The Coulomb effect on the observables and the ability of the present nuclear potential models to describe the experimental data is discussed.

## Key claim

The method of screening and renormalization can reliably include the Coulomb interaction in momentum-space integral equations for three- and four-body nuclear reactions, provided the screening radius is large enough for convergence; however, even after including both Coulomb and three-nucleon forces, some discrepancies between theory and experiment persist, such as the space star anomaly in p-d breakup and the A_y-puzzle in p-d and p-³He elastic scattering.

## Method

The author solves Faddeev-like Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations for three- and four-particle scattering in a momentum-space partial-wave basis, using a screened Coulomb potential with a new screening function that avoids approximations of prior implementations. The diverging screened Coulomb contributions are isolated via the two-potential formalism and renormalized by phase factors, while the short-range parts of the amplitudes are calculated numerically at finite screening radius R, with R chosen large enough for convergence.

## Key numbers

- For d-α elastic scattering at E_d = 4.81 MeV, differential cross section and deuteron vector analyzing power iT_11 are shown with and without Coulomb, comparing attractive and repulsive N-α S-wave potentials.
- For α-d breakup at E_α = 15 MeV, differential cross section in selected kinematical configurations shows shifts of αp P-wave resonance position due to Coulomb.
- Convergence of observables with screening radius R is impressively fast in most cases, becoming slower only for observables at very low energies.

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization approach of Taylor (1974) and Alt (1980) to three- and four-body nuclear reactions, improving on earlier implementations by Alt (1994, 2002) that used approximations. The results for p-d elastic scattering agree well over a wide energy range with those of Kievsky (2001) from variational solution of the three-nucleon Schrödinger equation with unscreened Coulomb. The method enables studies of three-nucleon hadronic and electromagnetic reactions, α+d, d+¹²C, p+¹¹Be, and all elastic and transfer four-nucleon reactions below three-body breakup threshold, while open questions remain regarding the space star anomaly and A_y-puzzle.
