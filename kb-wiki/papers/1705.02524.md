# Wilsonian renormalization group versus subtractive renormalization in effective field theories for nucleon--nucleon scattering

> We compare the subtractive renormalization and the Wilsonian renormalization group approaches in the context of an effective field theory for the two-nucleon system. Based on an exactly solvable model of contact interactions, we observe that the standard Wilsonian renormalization group approach with a single cutoff parameter does not cover the whole space spanned by the renormalization scale parameters of the subtractive formalism. In particular, renormalization schemes corresponding to Weinberg's power counting in the case of an unnaturally large scattering length are beyond the region covered by the Wilsonian renormalization group approach. In the framework of pionless effective field theory, also extended by the inclusion of a long-range interaction of separable type, we demonstrate that Weinberg's power counting scheme is consistent in the sense that it leads to a systematic order-by-order expansion of the scattering amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Weinberg's power counting scheme for nucleon-nucleon scattering is consistent and leads to a systematic order-by-order expansion of the scattering amplitude, contrary to claims that it is inconsistent. The authors show that the standard Wilsonian renormalization group approach with a single cutoff parameter does not cover the full space of renormalization scale parameters available in subtractive renormalization, and that Weinberg's scheme corresponds to a region of this parameter space not accessed by the Wilsonian RG analysis.

## Method

The authors construct exactly solvable, renormalizable models of the nucleon-nucleon potential using contact interactions and a separable long-range interaction, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation analytically. They compare the subtractive renormalization approach, which allows multiple independent renormalization scale parameters, with the Wilsonian RG approach that uses a single cutoff parameter, analyzing how different choices of renormalization conditions lead to either Weinberg's or Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) power counting schemes.

## Key numbers

- The inverse scattering amplitude for the contact interaction model is given by \(1/T(k) = -4\pi/m \left[-1/a + r_e k^2/2 + v_2 k^4 - ik\right]^{-1}\), where \(a\) is the scattering length, \(r_e\) the effective range, and \(v_2\) the first shape parameter
- The leading order re-scaled potential in the Wilsonian RG expansion scales as \(\hat V_0 \sim \epsilon^0\) and the next term as \(\hat V_1 \sim \epsilon^1\), where \(\epsilon \sim Q_S/\Lambda_H\) with \(Q_S\) the soft scale and \(\Lambda_H\) the hard scale
- For Weinberg's counting, the renormalized potential has couplings of natural size \(\sim \epsilon^0\), while for KSW counting the leading coupling scales as \(\sim \epsilon^{-1}\)
- The scattering length \(a\) scales as \(\sim \epsilon^{-1}\) in the case of an unnaturally large scattering length, while \(r_e \sim \epsilon^0\) and \(v_2 \sim \epsilon^0\)

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing debate about renormalization and power counting in chiral effective field theory for few-nucleon systems, which began with Weinberg (1990, 1991). It extends the analysis of Birse (1998) and others who used Wilsonian RG methods, showing that their conclusions about Weinberg's scheme being inconsistent are based on an incomplete exploration of the renormalization parameter space. The paper reconciles the Weinberg approach, which is phenomenologically successful and widely used with finite regulators, with the KSW counting of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), demonstrating that both are valid schemes corresponding to different choices of renormalization conditions. This enables future work to use either counting scheme with confidence in their formal consistency.
