# The effective field theory of extended Wilson lines

> We construct the effective theory of electrically charged, spatially extended, infinitely heavy objects at leading power. The theory may be viewed as a generalization of NRQED for particles with a finite charge distribution where the charge radius and higher moments of the charge distribution are counted as $O(1)$ rather than $O(1/M)$. We show this is equivalent to a Wilson line traced by the worldline of an extended charge distribution. Our canonical use case is atomic nuclei with large charge $Z\gg 1$. The theory allows for the insertion of external operators and is sufficiently general to allow a treatment of both electromagnetic and weak mediated lepton-nucleus scattering including charged-current processes. This provides a first step towards the factorization of Coulomb regions, including structure dependence arising from a finite charge distribution, for scattering with nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective field theory (EFT) for infinitely heavy, spatially extended charged objects where the charge radius and higher moments are counted as O(1) rather than O(1/M), which is a generalization of NRQED. The key hedge is that the theory applies at leading power in the expansion, and the canonical use case is atomic nuclei with large charge Z ≫ 1, where the inverse nuclear radius R⁻¹ ∼ 25 MeV is well separated from the nuclear mass M ∼ 200 GeV.

## Method

The author constructs the EFT by starting from NRQED and identifying operators that are coherently enhanced by the large nuclear radius, specifically terms involving ∂·E and its derivatives. The Feynman rules are derived by matching the charge form factor F(q²) in the static limit, and the theory is shown to be equivalent to a Wilson line traced by an extended charge distribution, with a gauge-invariant operator definition provided. The method of regions is applied to separate soft (q ∼ R⁻¹) and hard (q ≫ R⁻¹) momentum regions in loop integrals for lepton-nucleus scattering.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear inverse radius R⁻¹ ∼ 25 MeV, while nuclear mass M ∼ 200 GeV, establishing the parametric separation R⁻¹ ≪ M
- The charge form factor F(q²) enters the vertex Feynman rule as (Ze)v_μ + (Ze)(F(q²)-1)/q_⊥² [v_μ q_⊥² - q_{⊥μ} v·q]
- For a dipole form factor model with Λ_A and Λ_B, the one-loop structure mismatch correction gives (i/4π²) log(Λ_A/Λ_B) for the imaginary part of the amplitude
- The eikonal approximation at one loop yields a contribution proportional to Ze² times an integral over d³q/(2π)³ of F(q²)/q² times eikonal denominators

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of Wilson lines and NRQED (Caswell 1985, Manohar 1997) to composite objects where the charge radius is large compared to the Compton wavelength, which is the opposite scaling from point-like particles. The theory enables systematic treatment of structure-dependent radiative corrections in precision experiments involving nuclei, such as neutrino oscillation experiments (Branca 2021, Tomalak 2022), superallowed beta decay (Seng 2018, Czarnecki 2019, Hardy 2020), and experiments like Mu2e/COMET and LDMX (LDMX 2018, Bernstein 2019). The work provides a first step toward factorization of Coulomb regions including finite charge distribution effects for scattering with nuclei.
