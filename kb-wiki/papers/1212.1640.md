# Elastic pbar-d scattering and total pbar-d cross sections

> Elastic pbar-d scattering is studied within the Glauber theory based on the single- and double pbar-N scattering mechanisms. The full spin dependence of the elementary pbar-N scattering amplitudes is taken into account and both the S- and D-wave components of the deuteron are considered. The treatment of the spin dependence is done in a (properly modified) formalism developed recently by Platonova and Kukulin for the pd -> pd scattering process. Predictions for differential cross sections and the spin observables A_y^d, A_y^pbar, A_xx, A_yy are presented for antiproton beam energies between 50 and 300 MeV, using amplitudes generated from the Nbar-N interaction model developed by the Juelich group. Total polarized cross sections are calculated utilizing the optical theorem. The efficiency of the polarization buildup for antiprotons in a storage ring is investigated.

# Elastic pbar-d scattering and total pbar-d cross sections

## Key claim

The Glauber theory, including full spin dependence of elementary antiproton-nucleon amplitudes and both S- and D-wave deuteron components, can provide reliable predictions for elastic pbar-d scattering observables only for center-of-mass scattering angles up to 50-60 degrees; beyond this range, contributions from large-angle elementary amplitudes contradict the eikonal approximation underlying the Glauber approach.

## Method

The authors applied the Glauber multiple-scattering formalism, modified from the Platonova and Kukulin (2010) treatment of pd scattering, to compute elastic pbar-d amplitudes from single- and double-scattering mechanisms using elementary pbar-N amplitudes from the Juelich group's models A and D. The deuteron wave function was taken from the CD Bonn potential (with test calculations using the Paris potential), and the full spin structure of the pbar-N amplitude (including amplitudes A, B, C, C', G, H) was retained. The reliability of the Glauber approach was assessed by introducing smooth and sharp cutoffs on the large-angle behavior of the elementary amplitudes and examining the resulting sensitivity of pbar-d observables.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section predictions are reliable for c.m. scattering angles up to 50-60 degrees in the pbar-d system at beam energies between 50 and 300 MeV
- At 179 MeV, the first diffraction minimum in the differential cross section is located at q^2 approximately 0.12-0.13 (GeV/c)^2, corresponding to theta_c.m. approximately 55 degrees
- The D-wave component of the deuteron contributes around 30% of the absolute value of the differential cross section in the region of the first minimum at 179 MeV
- Double-scattering effects in the unpolarized total cross section were found to be on the order of 10-15% in the authors' prior work (Uzikov and Haidenbauer, 2009)
- The smooth cutoff for the elementary pbar-N amplitude at 179 MeV was applied with q_0^2 = 0.21 (GeV/c)^2, corresponding to theta_c.m. approximately 105 degrees for the pbar-N system

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior single-scattering approximation study (Uzikov and Haidenbauer, 2009) by including double-scattering mechanisms and full spin dependence for pbar-d elastic scattering, motivated by the PAX collaboration's plans to measure proton/antiproton transversity in Drell-Yan processes at FAIR. The formalism adapts the approach of Platonova and Kukulin (2010, 2011) originally developed for pd scattering, and the results are compared with experimental data from Bruge (1970s) at 179 MeV. The calculations enable predictions for the spin-filtering efficiency of antiproton polarization buildup in storage rings, relevant for planned experiments at the CERN AD ring, and provide observables that could discriminate between different pbar-N interaction models such as those from the Juelich group and the Nijmegen partial wave analysis of Timmermans (1994) and Zhou and Timmermans (2010).
