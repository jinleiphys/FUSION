# Symmetries in subatomic six-quark fermions

> The evidence of the existence of tetra-quark meson and penta-quark fermion systems has revived interest in the hunt of other multi-quark systems. We explore the possibility of six-quark cluster configurations with the two nucleon spin-isospin quantum numbers existing in the nuclear medium. The investigation of such clusters is feasible if one exploits all the symmetries involved, in particular orbital, color-spin $SU(6)_{cs}$ and isospin SU(2). We investigate whether such clusters contribute to neutrinoless double-beta decay mediated by heavy neutrinos or other exotic particles, since in conventional nuclear physics the relevant nuclear matrix elements are suppressed due to the presence of the nuclear hard core.

## Key claim

Six-quark clusters with two-nucleon spin-isospin quantum numbers may exist in the nuclear medium and could contribute to neutrinoless double-beta decay mediated by heavy neutrinos or other exotic particles, because in such clusters the interacting quarks are in the same hadron and can contribute even with a delta-function interaction, unlike conventional nuclear matrix elements which are suppressed by the nucleon-nucleon hard core.

## Method

The authors constructed six-quark basis states as products of spatial, color-spin (SU(6)cs), and isospin (SU(2)I) wave functions using a harmonic oscillator basis with up to 2-hbar-omega excitations, generalizing the SU(3) shell model approach of Elliott and Arima. They diagonalized two different quark-quark interactions (a one-gluon exchange potential and the Ohta interaction) in this basis, projected out spurious center-of-mass states using SU(3) methods, and then expanded the resulting six-quark eigenstates into two three-quark baryon clusters using SU(6)cs recoupling coefficients and orbital transformations.

## Key numbers

* For the one-gluon exchange potential with alpha_s = 1 and b_q = 0.8 fm, the ground state of the I=1, J=0 six-quark system is 99% 0s^6 configuration after projection.
* Using the Ohta interaction (parameters: A=3810, B=-12.5, C=-479.8, K=-911.1) with Delta = 500 MeV, the I=1, J=0 ground state is 93% 0s^6 and its energy is depressed by 71 MeV by the 2-hbar-omega states.
* The first excited I=1, J=0 state lies 423 MeV above the ground state for the Ohta interaction with Delta = 500 MeV.
* The I=0, J=1 state is separated from the I=1, J=0 state by over 70 MeV using the Ohta interaction.
* For the harmonic oscillator at the quark level, Model A gives hbar-omega_q = 293 MeV using b_N = 0.365 fm, while Model B gives hbar-omega_q = 188 MeV using b_N = 0.65 fm.
* The binding energy of the nucleon from the one-gluon exchange potential is estimated as -1260 MeV, and the six-quark cluster is expected to be about twice that.

## Context

This work extends the search for multi-quark systems beyond the recently observed tetra-quark mesons and penta-quark fermions (Pentaq 2015, CernCP 2016) to six-quark clusters in nuclei, building on the Jaffe MIT bag model calculations (Jaf77, Jaf77a) and the SU(3) shell model technology developed by Elliott (1958), Arima and colleagues (Inoue1, Akiyama1, Akiyama2), and Hecht (Hecht-1). The formalism enables future calculations of six-quark admixtures in neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, which are otherwise suppressed by the nucleon-nucleon hard core in conventional nuclear physics.
