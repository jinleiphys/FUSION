# Chiral three-nucleon force at N^4LO II: Intermediate-range contributions

> We derive the subleading contributions to the two-pion-one-pion exchange and ring three-nucleon force topologies emerging at next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. The resulting expressions do not involve any unknown parameters. To study convergence of the chiral expansion we work out the most general operator structure of a local isospin-invariant three-nucleon force. Using the resulting operator basis with 22 independent structures, we compare the strength of the corresponding potentials in configuration space for individual topologies at various orders in the chiral expansion. As expected, the subleading contributions from the two-pion-one-pion-exchange and ring diagrams are large which can be understood in terms of intermediate excitation of the Delta(1232) isobar.

## Key claim

The subleading contributions to the two-pion-one-pion exchange and ring three-nucleon force topologies at N⁴LO are large, which can be understood in terms of intermediate excitation of the Δ(1232) isobar; the resulting expressions do not involve any unknown parameters.

## Method

The authors derived the N⁴LO corrections to the two-pion-one-pion exchange and ring three-nucleon force topologies within chiral effective field theory using heavy-baryon formalism, evaluating all one-loop diagrams with a single insertion of subleading pion-nucleon vertices proportional to the low-energy constants c₁, c₂, c₃, and c₄. They worked out the most general operator structure of a local isospin-invariant three-nucleon force, establishing a basis of 22 independent structures, and compared the strength of the corresponding potentials in configuration space for individual topologies at various orders in the chiral expansion.

## Key numbers

- The operator basis for a local isospin-invariant three-nucleon force contains **22 independent structures**.
- The N²LO contributions to the two-pion-exchange 3NF involve the low-energy constants c₁, c₃, and c₄, with the coordinate-space functions given by expressions proportional to g_A² M_π⁶ / (128 π² F_π⁴).
- The N³LO contributions to the two-pion-one-pion exchange topology involve the loop function A(q) = (1/(2q)) arctan(q/(2M_π)), with the profile function W₁(x) = e^{-2x}/(2x²).
- The N⁴LO contributions to the two-pion-one-pion exchange topology involve the loop function L(q) = (√(q²+4M_π²)/q) log((√(q²+4M_π²)+q)/(2M_π)), with the profile function V₁(x) = (1/x) ∫₂^∞ dμ e^{-xμ}/μ² √(μ²-4).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of N⁴LO corrections to the two-pion-exchange three-nucleon force topology (Krebs, 2012) and the N³LO three-nucleon force calculations of Bernard (2007) and Bernard (2011). The leading three-nucleon force contributions at N²LO were developed by van Kolck (1994) and Epelbaum (2002). The results enable complete N⁴LO three-nucleon force calculations in the delta-less chiral EFT approach, which are needed to improve the description of few-nucleon scattering observables and to resolve long-standing puzzles such as the A_y-puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering.
