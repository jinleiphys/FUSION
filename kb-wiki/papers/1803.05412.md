# Effective field theory for the nucleon-quarkonium interaction

> We develop an effective field theory approach for the $S$-wave quarkonium-nucleon system. We adopt a natural power counting equivalent to Weinberg's power counting in nucleon-nucleon effective field theories and compute the quarkonium-nucleon potential, scattering length and effective range up to $\mathcal{O}(m^3_π/Λ^3_χ)$ accuracy, including the full light-quark mass dependence. We compare our results with lattice QCD studies of quarkonium-nucleon system, obtain an estimation of the leading order quarkonium-nucleon contact term and determine the $J/ψ$ and $η_c$ chromopolarizabilities.

## Key claim

The paper develops a model-independent effective field theory (EFT) for the S-wave quarkonium-nucleon interaction, claiming that the interaction is dominated by multigluon van der Waals forces and that the scattering length has no light-quark mass dependence at leading order, with subleading contributions much smaller than current lattice QCD uncertainties.

## Method

The authors construct a nonrelativistic chiral EFT (QNEFT) coupling S-wave quarkonium fields with nucleon doublets and pion triplets according to chiral symmetry, heavy-quark spin symmetry, and CPT symmetry, adopting Weinberg's power counting for nucleon-nucleon EFT. They then integrate out pion fields at energies below the pion mass to obtain a lower-energy potential EFT (pQNEFT), matching the coefficients up to N3LO (order mπ³/Λχ³) and computing the scattering length and effective range with full light-quark mass dependence. The results are compared with lattice QCD data from multiple groups to extract the leading-order contact term and the J/ψ and ηc chromopolarizabilities.

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach previously applied to quarkonium-quarkonium systems (Brambilla 2015) and QED van der Waals forces (Brambilla 2017) to the quarkonium-nucleon system, providing the first model-independent EFT framework for this interaction. It enables systematic chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results for charmonium-nucleon scattering, which currently show large variations between quenched simulations (Yokokawa 2006, Kawanai 2010) and full QCD simulations (Liu 2008), and between different lattice methods such as the HAL QCD potential method (Kawanai 2010) and Luscher's phase-shift formula. The framework also allows discussion of the compatibility of different lattice results and provides a path toward determining whether charmonia can form nuclear bound states, a question raised by Brodsky (1989) and recently investigated in lattice QCD by Beane (2014) and Alberti (2016).
