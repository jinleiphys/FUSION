# Derivation of spontaneously broken gauge symmetry from the consistency of effective field theory I: Massive vector bosons coupled to a scalar field

> We revisit the problem of deriving local gauge invariance with spontaneous symmetry breaking in the context of an effective field theory. Previous derivations were based on the condition of tree-order unitarity. However, the modern point of view considers the Standard Model as the leading order approximation to an effective field theory. As tree-order unitarity is in any case violated by higher-order terms in an effective field theory, it is instructive to investigate a formalism which can be also applied to analyze higher-order interactions. In the current work we consider an effective field theory of massive vector bosons interacting with a massive scalar field. We impose the conditions of generating the right number of constraints for systems with spin-one particles and perturbative renormalizability as well as the separation of scales at one-loop order. We find that the above conditions impose severe restrictions on the coupling constants of the interaction terms. Except for the strengths of the self-interactions of the scalar field, that can not be determined at this order from the analysis of three- and four-point functions, we recover the gauge-invariant Lagrangian with spontaneous symmetry breaking taken in the unitary gauge as the leading order approximation to an effective field theory. We also outline the additional work that is required to finish this program.

## Key claim

The authors derive the gauge-invariant Lagrangian with spontaneous symmetry breaking (in unitary gauge) as the unique leading-order effective field theory (EFT) of a massive scalar coupled to massive vector bosons, by imposing consistency of the constraint structure, perturbative renormalizability, and scale separation at one-loop order, but the scalar self-interaction couplings remain unfixed at this order.

## Method

The authors start with the most general Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian for three massive vector bosons (two charged, one neutral) and a neutral scalar, keeping only terms with couplings of non-negative mass dimension. They perform a canonical constraint analysis to ensure the system has the correct number of degrees of freedom for spin-one particles, then compute one-loop divergent parts of three- and four-point vertex functions using dimensional regularization, imposing that divergences be absorbable into the leading-order couplings without violating scale separation.

## Key numbers

- The constraint analysis forces \(\gamma_1 = g_5 + g_7 = 0\) and \(\gamma_2 = g_4 + g_6 - 2g_2 = 0\), i.e. \(g_7 = -g_5\) and \(2g_2 = g_4 + g_6\).
- From the \(V^3V^3 \to V^3V^3\) amplitude, the term proportional to \(u^4\) forces \(g_1 = 0\) and \(g_2 = 0\).
- The condition from the \(u^2\) term yields \(d_5 = 0\), \(g_4 = 0\), \(g_5 = -g_3\), \(g_{A2} = 0\), \(g_{A3} = -g_{A1}\), and \(d_3 = -g_3^2\).
- The \(s^2\) term gives \(d_4 = g_3^2\).
- Vanishing of the remaining divergence in \(V^3V^3 \to V^3V^3\) forces \(g_{2,ss} = -g_3^4 M_3^6/(32 M^4 g_{1,s}^2)\) and \(g_{2,s} = g_3^2 M_3^4/(4 M^2 g_{1,s})\).
- From \(V^1V^1 \to V^1V^1\) and the scalar decay into two vectors, one obtains \(d_1 = -d_2 = g_3^2/2\) and \(M_3 = M\).
- The three-vector vertex condition gives \(g_{1,s} = g_{2,s} = \pm g_3 M/2\).
- The four-vector condition then yields \(g_{1,ss} = -g_3^2/8\).
- Consistency of \(\Phi V_3 \to \Phi V_3\) forces \(g_{vss} = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivation of gauge invariance from tree-order unitarity by Llewellyn Smith (1973), Cornwall et al. (1973, 1974), and Joglekar (1973) to the modern EFT viewpoint, where tree-order unitarity is violated by higher-order terms. It is similar to the analysis of Djukanovic et al. (2010) but without assuming parity conservation, and to Gegelia et al. (2012) which considered an EFT without a scalar field. The result recovers the spontaneously broken SU(2) gauge Lagrangian in unitary gauge as the leading-order EFT, leaving the scalar self-couplings to be fixed by future two-loop or higher-point analyses.
