# Transition probabilities in the U(6) limit of the Symplectic Interacting Vector Boson Model

> The tensor properties of the algebra generators and the basis are determined in respect to the reduction chain $Sp(12,R) \supset U(6)% \supset U(3)\otimes U(2)\supset O(3)\otimes (U(1)\otimes U(1))$, which defines one of the dynamical symmetries of the Interacting Vector Boson Model. The action of the Sp(12,R) generators as transition operators between the basis states is presented. Analytical expressions for their matrix elements in the symmetry-adapted basis are obtained. As an example the matrix elements of the E2 transition operator between collective states of the ground band are determined and compared with the experimental data for the corresponding intraband transition probabilities of nuclei in the actinide and rare earth region. On the basis of this application the important role of the symplectic extension of the model is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical expressions for matrix elements of Sp(12,R) generators in a symmetry-adapted basis for the U(6) limit of the Interacting Vector Boson Model, and shows that the symplectic extension is important for reproducing intraband E2 transition probabilities in deformed nuclei, though the comparison with experimental data is presented only as an illustrative example rather than a comprehensive test.

## Method

The authors classified the Sp(12,R) algebra generators as irreducible tensor operators with respect to the reduction chain Sp(12,R) ⊃ U(6) ⊃ U(3)⊗U(2) ⊃ O(3)⊗U(1), and constructed basis states by symmetric coupling of raising operators F applied to the boson vacuum. They then applied the Wigner-Eckart theorem in two steps, using U(6) and SU(3) isoscalar factors, to evaluate matrix elements of the E2 transition operator between ground band states identified with SU(3) irreps (0,μ).

## Key numbers

- The reduced triple-barred matrix element for the SU(3) generator A in the (λ,μ) irrep is given by g_{λμ} = 2[(λ²+μ²+λμ+3λ+3μ)/3]^{1/2}, with a sign convention: positive for μ=0, negative for μ≠0.
- For the ground band with yrast condition μ=L, the SO(3) reduced matrix element of the SU(3) quadrupole operator between states differing by ΔL=2 is 2[(L-1)L/(2L-1)]^{1/2}.
- The E2 transition operator includes a term from the tensor product [F×F] that changes the number of bosons by ΔN=4 and angular momentum by ΔL=2.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) and its symplectic extension to Sp(12,R) (Ganev and Georgieva, 2006), which had successfully reproduced energy spectra of deformed nuclei. The present paper provides the necessary formalism for calculating transition probabilities, complementing the energy calculations and enabling comparison with experimental B(E2) values for actinide and rare-earth nuclei. The approach differs from the Sp(6,R) model of Rosensteel and Rowe (1980) by including an additional F_{(0,1)} raising operator that arises from the U(6) structure.
