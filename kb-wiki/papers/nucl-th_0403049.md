# Effects of momentum-dependent nuclear potential on two-nucleon correlation functions and light cluster production in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions

> Using an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model, we study the effects due to the momentum dependence of isoscalar nuclear potential as well as that of symmetry potential on two-nucleon correlation functions and light cluster production in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei. It is found that both observables are affected significantly by the momentum dependence of nuclear potential, leading to a reduction of their sensitivity to the stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy. However, the t/$^{3}$He ratio remains a sensitive probe of the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of both the isoscalar nuclear potential and the symmetry potential significantly affects two-nucleon correlation functions and light cluster yields, reducing their sensitivity to the stiffness of the nuclear symmetry energy; however, the t/³He ratio remains a sensitive probe of the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy even after including momentum dependence.

## Method

The authors used an isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate central collisions of ⁵²Ca + ⁴⁸Ca at 80 MeV/nucleon. They compared three nuclear potentials: a momentum-independent soft isoscalar potential (SBKD), a momentum-dependent isoscalar potential with a momentum-independent symmetry potential (MDYI), and a fully momentum-dependent potential from the Gogny MDI interaction that includes momentum dependence in both the isoscalar and symmetry potentials. Two-nucleon correlation functions were computed using the Koonin-Pratt formalism, and light cluster yields (deuteron, triton, ³He) were obtained from a coalescence model.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density is known to be around 30 MeV from the empirical liquid-drop mass formula.
- The MDI interaction uses compressibility K₀ = 211 MeV and symmetry energy 30 MeV at saturation.
- For the SBKD interaction with hard vs. soft symmetry energy, the correlation function for high-total-momentum nucleon pairs shows differences of about 24% for neutron-proton pairs and 9% for neutron-neutron pairs at relative momentum q = 5 MeV/c, and 21% for proton-proton pairs at q = 20 MeV/c.
- For the MDYI interaction (momentum-dependent isoscalar, momentum-independent symmetry potential), the corresponding differences are about 17% for neutron-proton pairs and 4% for neutron-neutron pairs at q = 5 MeV/c, and 12% for proton-proton pairs at q = 20 MeV/c.
- For the MDI interaction (full momentum dependence), the differences are about 11% for neutron-proton pairs at q = 5 MeV/c and 9% for proton-proton pairs at q = 20 MeV/c, with very weak sensitivity for neutron-neutron pairs.
- The fraction of total emitted nucleons before 200 fm/c is about 80% for the SBKD interaction but almost 100% for the MDYI and MDI interactions.
- The t/³He ratio for all potentials shows a very different energy dependence between soft and stiff symmetry energies: it increases with kinetic energy for the soft symmetry energy, while it decreases or increases weakly for the stiff symmetry energy.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Chen, Ko, and Li (2003-2004) that showed two-nucleon correlation functions and the t/³He ratio are sensitive to the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, but those studies used momentum-independent potentials. It also builds on earlier work by Gale, Bertsch, and Das Gupta (1987-1990) showing that momentum-dependent isoscalar potentials affect nucleon directed flow and by Li, Ko, and others (2004) on the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential. The results enable future experiments at the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) to use the t/³He ratio as a probe of both the momentum dependence of the nuclear symmetry potential and the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy.
