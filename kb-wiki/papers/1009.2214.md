# The influence of the Lande $g$-factor in the classical general relativistic description of atomic and subatomic systems

> We study the electromagnetic and gravitational fields of the proton and electron in terms of the Einstenian gravity via the introduction of an arbitrary Lande $g$-factor in the Kerr-Newman solution. We show that at length scales of the order of the reduced Compton wavelength, corrections from different values of the $g$-factor are not negligible and discuss the presence of general relativistic effects in highly ionized heavy atoms. On the other hand, since at the Compton-wavelength scale the gravitational field becomes spin dominated rather than mass dominated, we also point out the necessity of including angular momentum as a source of corrections to Newtonian gravity in the quantum description of gravity at this scale.

## Key claim

General relativistic corrections to the Coulomb and Newton potentials become significant at length scales of the order of the reduced Compton wavelength, and for the proton the Kerr-Newman solution (with gyromagnetic ratio g=2) deviates significantly from the description using the real proton g-factor of 5.585694713(46), while for the electron the real g-factor of 2.0023193043768(86) leaves the corrections practically unaffected.

## Method

The authors used Manko's generalization of the Kerr-Newman solution, which includes an arbitrary Lande g-factor as a free parameter, to describe the spacetime of a rotating charged magnetic dipole. They performed an asymptotic expansion of the Ernst complex potentials up to order r^{-5} to obtain approximate expressions for the gravitational and electromagnetic potentials. These potentials were then applied to the electron and proton using their experimentally measured g-factors, and the resulting corrections were evaluated for hydrogen-like atoms by modifying the Bohr model.

## Key numbers

- For the electron: reduced Compton wavelength = 0.386159 pm; a_e = 1.93 x 10^{-11} cm; q_e = 1.38 x 10^{-34} cm; m_e = 6.76 x 10^{-56} cm
- For the proton: reduced Compton wavelength = 0.210309 fm; g_p = 5.585694713(46)
- For hydrogen (H) ground state: energy correction = 1.50047 x 10^{-10} eV; radius correction = 8.75383 x 10^{-12} Angstrom
- For molybdenum Mo^{41+} ground state: energy correction = 0.264685 micro-eV (0.094773 micro-eV using g_KN=2)
- For gold Au^{78+} ground state: energy correction = 0.936459 micro-eV (0.335291 micro-eV using g_KN=2)
- The general relativistic corrections at the reduced Compton wavelength are of order 10^{-1}, while quantum corrections from one-loop calculations are of order 10^{-39} for the proton and 10^{-45} for the electron

## Context

This work extends the analysis of general relativistic effects in atomic systems by Martin and Pritchett (1968), Carter (1968), and Rosquist (2006), who used the Kerr-Newman solution with g=2. The authors show that for the proton, the real g-factor produces corrections that differ from the Kerr-Newman prediction, while for the electron the difference is negligible. The paper compares its results with one-loop quantum corrections from Bjerrum-Bohr (2003) and Faller (2008), finding that general relativistic corrections are stronger than quantum gravity corrections at the Compton scale. The work suggests that angular momentum should be included as a source in quantum gravity calculations and identifies specific nuclei with high spin (e.g., Mo-95 with spin 5/2, Ge-73 with spin 9/2, Hg-195m with spin 13/2) for future study.
