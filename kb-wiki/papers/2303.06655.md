# Single-Particle Spectra in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions Within the Thermal Quantum Field Theory

> A quantum generalization of the Cooper-Fry recipe is proposed. The single-particle spectrum arising from relativistic collisions of particles and nuclei is calculated within the thermal quantum field theory framework. The starting point of consideration is the solution of the initial-value problem of particle emission from a space-like hypersurface. In the following steps, we obtain the single-particle spectrum using the ``smaller'' Green's function associated with the fireball medium. Based on this result, several specific examples of particle emission are considered.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a quantum generalization of the Cooper-Frye formula for particle emission from a space-like freeze-out hypersurface, derived within thermal quantum field theory. The derivation relies on solving the Cauchy problem for the Klein-Gordon field and expresses the single-particle spectrum in terms of the "lesser" Green's function of the emitting fireball medium, rather than a classical distribution function.

## Method
The author formulates particle emission as an initial-value problem on a space-like hypersurface (initially taken as constant time $t=t_0$). The free field operator after freeze-out is obtained by solving the inhomogeneous Klein-Gordon equation with source terms representing the initial field and its derivative, using the retarded Green's function. The annihilation and creation operators for asymptotic momentum states are then projected from the donor field $\hat{\Phi}(x)$ on the hypersurface, and the single-particle spectrum $2E_p dN/d^3p$ is expressed as an integral over the hypersurface of the "lesser" Green's function $G^<(x_2,x_1)$ of the fireball. The formalism is then applied to homogeneous systems and to systems in local thermodynamic equilibrium.

## Key numbers
* Critical particle density used to define freeze-out hypersurfaces: $n_{\rm c} = 0.08$ fm$^{-3}$.
* Critical energy density used to define freeze-out hypersurfaces: $\varepsilon_{\rm c} = 0.035$ GeV/fm$^3$.
* Collision energies for which freeze-out hypersurfaces are shown: $E_{\rm kin} = 40$ AGeV (SPS), $E_{\rm kin} = 158$ AGeV (SPS), and $\sqrt{s} = 62$ AGeV (RHIC).
* For a homogeneous system in global thermodynamic equilibrium, the single-particle spectrum in the fireball rest frame reduces to $dN/d^3p = V f_{\rm BE}(E_p)$, where $V$ is the spatial emission volume.

## Context
This work extends the classical Cooper-Frye prescription (Cooper and Frye, 1974) by providing a quantum field-theoretic derivation based on the lesser Green's function, moving beyond the classical distribution function approach. It builds on prior work using thermal field theory and the spectral function formalism (Mrowczynski, 1990; Mrowczynski, 1998) and on studies of freeze-out hypersurfaces defined by critical density or energy density (Anchishkin, 2013). The result enables a systematic framework for calculating single- and two-particle spectra from a fireball while accounting for quantum statistical effects and off-shell dynamics, with the potential to incorporate interactions beyond the free-field approximation.
