# Probabilistic interpretation of compositeness relation for resonances

> Bound, antibound and resonance states are associated to poles in the on-shell partial wave amplitudes. We show here that from the residues of the pole a rank 1 projection operator associated with any of these states can be extracted, in terms of which a sum rule related to the composition of the state can be derived. Although typically it involves complex coefficients for the compositeness and elementariness, except for the bound state case, we demonstrate that one can formulate a meaningful compositeness relation with only positive coefficients for resonances whose associated Laurent series in the variable s converges in a region of the physical axis around Re(s_P), with s_P the pole position of the resonance. It is also shown that this result can be considered as an analytical extrapolation in s_P of the clear narrow resonance case. We exemplify this formalism to study the two-body components of several resonances of interest.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a meaningful compositeness relation with only positive coefficients can be formulated for resonances whose Laurent series in the Mandelstam variable \(s\) converges in a region of the physical axis around \(\operatorname{Re}s_P\), where \(s_P\) is the pole position; this condition excludes antibound states and resonances lying too close to thresholds.

## Method

Starting from the on-shell partial-wave \(T\) matrix for \(n\)-channel two-body scattering, the authors extract a rank-1 projection operator from the pole residues and derive a sum rule relating compositeness and elementariness. For resonances satisfying the convergence condition (denoted condition **I**), they apply a unitary transformation to the \(S\) matrix restricted to open channels, fixing the transformation so that the resulting partial compositeness coefficients \(X_i^R\) become positive definite by taking absolute values of the complex quantities \(- \gamma_i^2 dG(s_P)_i/ds\). The formalism is then applied to several known resonances using pole positions and residues taken from the literature.

## Key numbers

* \(f_0(500)\): total compositeness \(X^R = 0.40^{+0.02}_{-0.02}\), elementariness \(Z^R = 0.60^{+0.02}_{-0.02}\) (pole at \(\sqrt{s_P} = 442^{+4}_{-4} - i\,246^{+7}_{-5}\) MeV)
* \(f_0(980)\): \(X^R = 0.67^{+0.28}_{-0.27}\), \(Z^R = 0.33^{+0.28}_{-0.27}\) (pole at \(\sqrt{s_P} = 978^{+17}_{-11} - i\,29^{+9}_{-11}\) MeV)
* \(\rho(770)\): \(X^R = 0.08^{+0.01}_{-0.01}\), \(Z^R = 0.92^{+0.01}_{-0.01}\) (pole at \(\sqrt{s_P} = 760^{+7}_{-5} - i\,71^{+4}_{-5}\) MeV)
* \(K^*(892)\): \(X^R = 0.05^{+0.01}_{-0.01}\), \(Z^R = 0.95^{+0.01}_{-0.01}\) (pole at \(\sqrt{s_P} = 892^{+5}_{-7} - i\,25^{+2}_{-2}\) MeV)
* \(\Lambda(1405)\) narrow pole: \(X^R = 1.00^{+0.49}_{-0.25}\), \(Z^R = 0.00^{+0.49}_{-0.25}\) (pole at \(\sqrt{s_P} = 1421^{+3}_{-2} - i\,19^{+8}_{-5}\) MeV)
* \(D^*_{s0}(2317)\): \(X^R = 0.70^{+0.07}_{-0.05}\), \(Z^R = 0.30^{+0.07}_{-0.05}\) (pole at \(\sqrt{s_P} = 2321^{+6}_{-3}\) MeV)

## Context

This work extends the compositeness relation previously derived for bound states by Refs. (Weinberg, 1965) and others to the case of resonances, overcoming the problem of complex compositeness coefficients that had prevented a probabilistic interpretation. The formalism enables a model-independent determination of the two-body composition of resonances from their pole parameters, provided the Laurent series converges on the physical axis around the resonance mass. The authors apply the method to exemplify the nature of several hadron resonances including the \(f_0(500)\), \(f_0(980)\), \(\rho(770)\), \(K^*(892)\), \(\Lambda(1405)\), and \(D^*_{s0}(2317)\), among others.
