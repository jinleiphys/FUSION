# Properties of the diffusion and drift kinetic coefficients in momentum space for a cold Fermi system

> Using the methods of kinetic theory expressions for the diffusion and drift coefficients for a cold Fermi system are obtained. Their dependences on the momentum are calculated for the step distribution function as well as in the case of excitation of a particle-hole pair.

## Key claim

Using kinetic theory methods, the author obtains expressions for the diffusion and drift coefficients in momentum space for a cold Fermi system at zero temperature, showing that these coefficients are non-zero even at T=0, which implies momentum transfer processes are possible in an absolutely cold Fermi system. The paper claims that the diffusion coefficient at the Fermi surface is approximately D_p,0(p_F) ≈ 3.38 × 10⁻²² MeV²·fm⁻²·s, but this result depends on the choice of a Gaussian internucleon potential with specific free parameters and is calculated for a light nucleus with mass number A=16.

## Method

The author starts from general expressions for the diffusion and drift kinetic coefficients derived from the ninefold collision integral in kinetic theory, using the small momentum transfer approximation with particles scattered near the Fermi surface. A Gaussian form-factor potential v(r) = v₀ exp(-r²/2r₀²) is adopted for the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section in the first Born approximation, which ensures convergent integral expressions. For the cold Fermi system, the step distribution function f(p) = θ(p_F² - p²) is used, and the integration limits are rewritten to replace the distribution functions with specific integration boundaries, enabling numerical calculation of the coefficients. For the particle-hole excitation case, the initial distribution function contains 12 terms that impose conditions on the integration limits, and the coefficients are computed for two characteristic cases at three excitation energies (10, 20, and 30 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Diffusion coefficient at the Fermi surface for the cold Fermi system: D_p,0(p_F) ≈ 3.38 × 10⁻²² MeV²·fm⁻²·s, calculated for a light nucleus with A=16 and Fermi energy ε_F = 37 MeV.
- Drift coefficient at the Fermi surface for the cold Fermi system: K_p,0(p_F) ≈ -2.76 × 10⁻²³ MeV·fm⁻²·s.
- After converting to the energy-space diffusion equation used by Wambach (1982), the diffusion coefficient becomes D ≈ 24 × 10²³ MeV²·s⁻¹ and the drift coefficient becomes v ≈ -2 × 10²³ MeV·s⁻¹.
- Fitting coefficients for the quadratic dependence of D_p(p_F) on excitation energy E_ex: for case (a), c₁ = 2.97 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ and c₂ = -2.51 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻²; for case (b), c₁ = 2.27 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ and c₂ = 3.75 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (KoLu 2014, KoLu 2015) on the momentum dependence of kinetic coefficients in Fermi systems, which had previously avoided zero-temperature calculations due to technical difficulties. The results are compared with the constant-coefficient approximation used by Wambach (1982), where the diffusion coefficient obtained here is close to that value while the drift coefficient differs by a factor of two and a half. The obtained momentum-dependent diffusion and drift coefficients will be useful for further studies of the evolution of the distribution function and dissipation of collective motions using the nonlinear diffusion equation in momentum space.
