# Neutron--proton spin--spin correlations in the ground states of N=Z nuclei

> We present expressions for the matrix elements of the spin--spin operator $\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}$ in a variety of coupling schemes. These results are then applied to calculate the expectation value $\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle$ in eigenstates of a schematic Hamiltonian describing neutrons and protons interacting in a single-$l$ shell through a Surface Delta Interaction. The model allows us to trace $\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle$ as a function of the competition between the isovector and isoscalar interaction strengths and the spin--orbit splitting of the $j=l\pm \frac{1}{2}$ shells. We find negative $\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle$ values in the ground state of all even--even $N=Z$ nuclei, contrary to what has been observed in hadronic inelastic scattering at medium energies. We discuss the possible origin of this discrepancy and indicate directions for future theoretical and experimental studies related to neutron--proton spin--spin correlations.

## Key claim

The authors find that in a schematic single-\(l\) shell model with a Surface Delta Interaction, the expectation value \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle\) is negative in the ground state of all even–even \(N=Z\) nuclei for all parameter values considered, contrary to the positive values observed in hadronic inelastic scattering experiments at medium energies. The paper discusses possible origins of this discrepancy, including configuration mixing between \(s\) and \(d\) shells and the tensor component of the nuclear interaction, but does not resolve it.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the matrix elements of the spin–spin operator \(\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\) in \(LS\), \(jj\), and neutron–proton coupling schemes. They then diagonalize a schematic Hamiltonian consisting of a single-\(l\) shell with a Surface Delta Interaction (SDI) and a spin–orbit splitting term, varying two dimensionless parameters: \(x = a_0/(a_0+a_1)\) (the relative strength of isoscalar vs. isovector pairing) and \(y\) (a bounded function of the spin–orbit splitting \(\Delta\epsilon/a\)). The expectation value \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle\) is computed in the ground states of even–even and odd–odd \(N=Z\) systems for shells with \(l=1\) to \(5\).

## Key numbers

* For two neutrons and two protons in a \(d\) shell with equal isoscalar and isovector strengths and no spin–orbit splitting (\(x=1/2\), \(y=0\)), \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle = 0\) in the ground state of even–even \(N=Z\) nuclei (SU(4) limit).
* For two neutrons and two protons in a \(g\) shell (\(l=4\)) at \(x=1/2\) and \(y=0\), \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle\) is negative and more negative than for lighter shells (see Fig. 2a).
* For four neutrons and four protons in the \(d\) shell at \(x=1/2\) and \(y=0\), \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle\) is more negative than for two neutrons and two protons (see Fig. 2b).
* For two neutrons and two protons in the \(d\) shell with equal strengths and a spin–orbit splitting corresponding to \(y \approx 0.375\) (estimated from \(\Delta\epsilon/a \approx (2l+1)A^{1/3}/5\) with \(A^{1/3}\approx3\)), \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle\) is negative and lies between the SU(4) value and the single-\(j\) limit (see Fig. 4b).
* For two neutrons and two protons in a single \(g_{9/2}\) shell (\(j=l+1/2\)) with \(x=1/2\), \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle = -11/36 \approx -0.306\) for \(J=0\) and \(-95/324 \approx -0.293\) for \(J=1\) (from Eq. 30).
* For two neutrons and two protons in a single \(h_{9/2}\) shell (\(j=l-1/2\)) with \(x=1/2\), \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle = -9/44 \approx -0.205\) for \(J=0\) and \(-95/484 \approx -0.196\) for \(J=1\) (from Eq. 30).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of neutron–proton spin–spin correlations initiated by the Osaka group (Matsubara 2010, Matsubara 2015, Tamii 2015), who measured positive \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle \approx 0.1\) in \(sd\)-shell nuclei via proton inelastic scattering at 295 MeV. The present schematic model contradicts the naive expectation that increasing isoscalar (spin-triplet) pairing strength would yield positive values, and it shows that the spin–orbit interaction drives \(\langle\vec S_{\rm n}\cdot\vec S_{\rm p}\rangle\) more negative. The results call for more sophisticated models incorporating configuration mixing and the tensor force, and they motivate extending experiments to odd–odd \(N=Z\) nuclei (e.g., \(^6\)Li, \(^{14}\)N) and to \(N=Z+2\) nuclei such as \(^{46,48}\)Ti (approved at iThemba).
