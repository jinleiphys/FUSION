# Transformations between complex scattering length and binding energy

> The use of scattering length of particle-target interaction due to real- valued potential to study the bound states of the particle-target system is well known in nuclear and atomic physics. In view of the current interest in using eta-nucleus scattering length to infer the existence of eta-mesic nucleus, we derive general analytic expressions that relate the binding energy and half-width of an unstable bound state to the complex-valued scattering length due to the same particle-target interaction.

# Transformations between complex scattering length and binding energy

## Key claim
The paper derives model-independent analytic expressions that relate the binding energy and half-width of an unstable bound state to the complex-valued scattering length, provided the particle-target interaction belongs to the class of exponentially bound potentials. The authors emphasize that while the interaction dynamics determine how one calculates either observable, the functional relation between them is independent of the specific interaction model.

## Method
The authors use the low-energy effective-range expansion for s-wave scattering, truncated at the scattering-length term, and express both the complex scattering length and the complex binding energy in polar (magnitude-angle) representation. They derive the forward mapping from scattering length to binding energy and the inverse mapping from binding energy to scattering length, then identify the physical domains in the complex scattering-length, momentum, and binding-energy planes by imposing the conditions that the bound state has negative energy and positive width.

## Key numbers
- The binding energy magnitude is given by |B| = 1/(2μ|a|²), where μ is the reduced mass and a is the complex scattering length.
- The real part of the binding energy (E) is E = -(x² - y²)/(2μ|a|⁴), where x = Re[a] and y = Im[a].
- The half-width (Γ/2) is given by Γ/2 = (2xy)/(2μ|a|⁴).
- The inverse mapping yields x = (-1/(2|B|√μ)) √(-u + √(u²+v²)) and y = (1/(2|B|√μ)) √(u + √(u²+v²)), where u = E and v = -Γ/2.
- The example calculations use 2μ = 5 fm⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends the polar-representation approach used by Balakrishnan et al. for multi-channel atom-molecule collisions to the nuclear-physics problem of η-mesic nuclei. It directly addresses the relation explored by Niskanen and Machner (recent work) between binding energy, width, and complex η-nucleus scattering length. The results enable consistency tests between theoretical interaction models and experimental measurements of η-nucleus scattering lengths, and explain why lighter nuclei require larger real parts of the scattering length to bind an η meson, clarifying why the ηN model of Haider (1986) does not predict η-mesic ³,⁴He while models with larger |a_ηN| do.
