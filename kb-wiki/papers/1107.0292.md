# J/psi production at sqrt(s)=1.96 and 7 TeV: Color-Singlet Model, NNLO* and polarisation

> We study J/psi production in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=1.96 and 7 TeV using the Colour-Singlet Model (CSM), including next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections and dominant alphaS^5 contributions (NNLO*). We find that the CSM reproduces the existing data if the upper range of the NNLO* is near the actual --but presently unknown-- NNLO. The direct yield polarisation for the NLO and NNLO* is increasingly longitudinal in the helicity frame when P_T gets larger. Contrary to what is sometimes claimed in the literature, the prompt J/psi yield polarisation in the CSM is compatible with the experimental data from the CDF collaboration, when one combines the direct yield with a data-driven range for the polarisation of J/psi from chi(c).

## Key claim

The Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) can reproduce existing J/ψ production data at √s = 1.96 and 7 TeV, but only if the upper range of the NNLO* evaluation is close to the actual (presently unknown) full NNLO result. The prompt J/ψ yield polarisation in the CSM is compatible with CDF data when the direct yield is combined with a data-driven range for the polarisation of J/ψ from χc decays.

## Method

The study evaluates J/ψ production in pp collisions using the Colour-Singlet Model with NLO (αS⁴) corrections and dominant αS⁵ real-emission contributions (NNLO*), evaluated with the MADONIA code by imposing a lower bound on the invariant-mass squared of light partons (sij). The polarisation parameter α is extracted bin-by-bin in the helicity frame, and the prompt yield polarisation is obtained by combining the direct NLO/NNLO* results with a data-driven estimate of the χc feed-down polarisation, using E1 dominance relations and the measured ratio σχc1/σχc2.

## Key numbers

- Charm-quark mass: mc = 1.5 ± 0.1 GeV
- Radial wavefunction squared: |RJ/ψ(0)|² = 1.01 GeV³
- Branching ratio: Br(J/ψ → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻) = 0.0594
- Ratio of χc feed-down cross sections: R₁₂ = σχc1 Br(χc1→J/ψγ) / σχc2 Br(χc2→J/ψγ) = 2.5 ± 0.1
- Minimum polarisation from χc feed-down: α^{min}_{from χc} ≈ -0.42
- Scale variations: μR = μF varied between 0.5 mT and 2 mT, with mT² = 4mQ² + PT²
- sij^{min} cut varied between 2.25 and 9.00 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the Colour-Singlet Model calculations previously applied to Υ production at the Tevatron (Artoisenet 2008) and J/ψ at RHIC (Lansberg 2010), now covering Tevatron and LHC energies. It contradicts the common attribution of quarkonium puzzles to colour-octet mechanisms, showing that the CSM alone may suffice. The results enable further NNLO calculations to determine whether the upper or lower bound of the NNLO* prediction is correct, and whether colour-octet contributions are required at all.
