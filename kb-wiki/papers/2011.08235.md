# Transasymptotics and hydrodynamization of the Fokker-Planck equation for gluons

> We investigate the non-linear transport processes and hydrodynamization of a system of gluons undergoing longitudinal boost-invariant expansion. The dynamics is described within the framework of the Boltzmann equation in the small-angle approximation. The kinetic equations for a suitable set of moments of the one-particle distribution function are derived. By investigating the stability and asymptotic resurgent properties of this dynamical system, we demonstrate, that its solutions exhibit a rather different behavior for large (UV) and small (IR) effective Knudsen numbers. Close to the forward attractor in the IR regime the constitutive relations of each moment can be written as a multiparameter transseries. This resummation scheme allows us to extend the definition of a transport coefficient to the non-equilibrium regime naturally. Each transport coefficient is renormalized by the non-perturbative contributions of the non-hydrodynamic modes. The Knudsen number dependence of the transport coefficient is governed by the corresponding renormalization group flow equation. An interesting feature of the Yang-Mills plasma in this regime is that it exhibits transient non-Newtonian behavior while hydrodynamizing. In the UV regime the solution for the moments can be written as a power-law asymptotic series with a finite radius of convergence. We show that radius of convergence of the UV perturbative expansion grows linearly as a function of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. Finally, we compare the universal properties in the pullback and forward attracting regions to other kinetic models including the relaxation time approximation and the effective kinetic Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that solutions of the Fokker-Planck equation for gluons undergoing Bjorken expansion exhibit qualitatively different asymptotic behavior in the UV (early time, large Knudsen number) and IR (late time, small Knudsen number) regimes, and that transport coefficients can be extended to far-from-equilibrium regimes via a transseries resummation, with the caveat that this extension relies on the existence of a slow invariant manifold and the specific small-angle approximation of the collision kernel.

## Method

The authors map the Boltzmann equation in the small-angle approximation (Fokker-Planck equation) for a boost-invariant gluon plasma onto a set of nonlinear ODEs for Legendre moments \(c_l\) of the one-particle distribution function using Grad's moment method. They then analyze the stability and asymptotic resurgent properties of this non-autonomous dynamical system in two regimes: UV (early time, \(Kn \gg 1\)) and IR (late time, \(Kn \ll 1\)), employing techniques from superasymptotic and hyperasymptotic analysis as well as dynamical systems theory. The dimensionally reduced system in terms of \(w = \tau T(\tau)\) (inverse Knudsen number) is studied to reveal the transseries structure and the renormalization group flow of transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) is related to the relaxation parameter \(\Theta_0\) via \(\Theta_0 \equiv 5\eta/s\) for the conformal RTA approximation.
* The Coulomb logarithm near equilibrium is \(l_{Cb} = \log\left(\sqrt{\langle p_T^2\rangle}/m_D\right) \sim \frac{1}{2}\log\left(\mathcal{A}/\lambda_{YM}\right)\) with \(\mathcal{A} = 72\zeta(5)/\zeta(3) \approx 62.1\).
* The Debye mass near equilibrium is \(m_D^2 = 2\frac{\zeta(2)\Gamma(2)}{\pi^2}\lambda_{YM}T^2\).
* The mean transverse momentum squared near equilibrium is \(\langle p_T^2\rangle = 2\frac{\zeta(5)\Gamma(5)}{\zeta(3)\Gamma(3)}T^2\).
* The parameter \(\kappa = \pi^2/3 - 2\zeta(3)\) appears in the ODE coefficients, and \(\theta_0^{-1} = \frac{5}{8\pi^5}\lambda_{YM}^2 l_{Cb}\) sets the collision strength.
* The Lyapunov exponents for mode \(l\) are \(\lambda(l) = \frac{3}{2\theta_0}\left(\kappa + \frac{l(2l+1)}{3}\pi^2\right)\).
* The radius of convergence of the UV perturbative expansion grows linearly as a function of \(\eta/s\).
* The pressure anisotropy is \(\Delta = \frac{2}{15}c_1\), and physical constraints require \(-5/2 \leq c_1 \leq 5\).

## Context

This work extends the resurgent analysis of hydrodynamization previously applied to the relaxation time approximation (RTA) Boltzmann equation by Behtash et al. (2017, 2018, 2019) to the more realistic Fokker-Planck collision kernel for Yang-Mills plasmas. It builds on the discovery by Heller and Spalinski (2015) that hydrodynamic attractors can be understood via transseries and resurgence, and on the phase-space analysis of non-autonomous dynamical systems by Behtash et al. (2017, 2018, 2019). The results enable a systematic extension of transport coefficients to far-from-equilibrium regimes and provide a framework for comparing universal properties of attractors across different kinetic models (RTA, Fokker-Planck, and the full leading-order AMY kinetic theory of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002)).
