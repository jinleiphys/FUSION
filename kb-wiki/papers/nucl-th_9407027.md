# Simultaneous Projectile-Target Excitation in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We calculate the lowest-order contribution to the cross section for simultaneous excitation of projectile and target nuclei in relativistic heavy ion collisions. This process is, to leading order, non-classical and adds incoherently to the well-studied semi-classical Weizsäcker-Williams cross section. While the leading contribution to the cross section is down by only $1/Z_P$ from the semiclassical process, and consequently of potential importance for understanding data from light projectiles, we find that phase space considerations render the cross section utterly negligible.

## Key claim

The leading-order contribution to simultaneous projectile-target excitation in relativistic heavy ion collisions is down by only \(1/Z_P\) from the semiclassical single-excitation cross section, but phase space considerations render the cross section utterly negligible despite this potentially favorable scaling for light projectiles.

## Method

The authors calculated the lowest-order Feynman diagram for simultaneous excitation (Fig. 1a) using a fully quantum mechanical, Lorentz-covariant formalism with spin-averaged transition charge and current densities. They then derived an analytic expression for the cross section in the limit of large \(\gamma\) and low transition energies, expanding the modified Bessel functions around zero frequency and assuming dipole-dipole excitations to obtain a closed-form estimate.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}\)C projectiles on \(^{197}\)Au, the estimated simultaneous excitation cross section is 0.05 mb, compared to a measured single excitation cross section of \(50 \pm 7\) mb at 2.1 GeV/nucleon.
- The analytic estimate for dipole-dipole simultaneous excitation is \(\sigma_{E1E1} = \sigma_0 \frac{N_P Z_P}{A_P} \frac{N_T Z_T}{A_T} \left( \frac{A_P^{1/3}}{A_T^{1/3}} + \frac{A_T^{1/3}}{A_P^{1/3}} \right)\), with \(\sigma_0 \approx 1.1 \times 10^{-4}\) mb.
- The leading contribution is down by a factor of \(1/Z_P\) from the semiclassical Weizsacker-Williams cross section.

## Context

This work extends the authors' program of examining corrections to the semiclassical Weizsacker-Williams method (Benesh and Friar, 1994) for electromagnetic cross sections in heavy ion collisions. The result contradicts the naive expectation that the \(1/Z_P\) suppression might make simultaneous excitation relevant for light projectiles, showing instead that phase space factors make it negligible. The paper enables future work on higher-order processes, specifically the two-photon exchange diagram (Fig. 2), which is classically allowed and may be significant due to scaling with \(Z_P^2 Z_T^2 \alpha^2\).
