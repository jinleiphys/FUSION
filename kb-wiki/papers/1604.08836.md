# $Z_c(3900)$ as a $D\bar{D}^*$ molecule from the pole counting rule

> A comprehensive study on the nature of the $Z_c(3900)$ resonant structure is carried out in this work. By constructing the pertinent effective Lagrangians and considering the important final-state-interaction effects, we first give a unified description to all the relevant experimental data available, including the $J/ψπ$ and $ππ$ invariant mass distributions from the $e^+e^-\to J/ψππ$ process, the $h_cπ$ distribution from $e^+e^-\to h_cππ$ and also the $D\bar D^{*}$ spectrum in the $e^+e^-\to D\bar D^{*}π$ process. After fitting the unknown parameters to the previous data, we search the pole in the complex energy plane and find only one pole in the nearby energy region in different Riemann sheets. Therefore we conclude that $Z_c(3900)$ is of $D\bar D^*$ molecular nature, according to the pole counting rule method~[Nucl.~Phys.~A543, 632 (1992); Phys.~Rev.~D 35,~1633 (1987)]. We emphasize that the conclusion based upon the pole counting method is not trivial, since both the $D\bar D^{*}$ contact interactions and the explicit $Z_c$ exchanges are introduced in our analyses and they lead to the same conclusion.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that the $Z_c(3900)$ resonance is of $D\bar{D}^*$ molecular nature based on the pole counting rule, which finds only one nearby pole in the complex energy plane; this conclusion holds regardless of whether the analysis includes only $D\bar{D}^*$ contact interactions or also explicit $Z_c$ exchanges, and the authors emphasize the result is not trivial because both approaches lead to the same conclusion.

## Method

The authors constructed effective Lagrangians incorporating $D\bar{D}^*$ contact interactions and explicit $Z_c$ exchange terms, then performed a combined fit to experimental data on $J/\psi\pi$ and $\pi\pi$ invariant mass distributions from $e^+e^-\to J/\psi\pi\pi$, the $h_c\pi$ distribution from $e^+e^-\to h_c\pi\pi$, and the $D\bar{D}^*$ spectrum from $e^+e^-\to D\bar{D}^*\pi$, all at a center-of-mass energy of 4.26 GeV. They included final-state interactions for both $\bar{D}D^*$ (via bubble-chain resummation) and $\pi\pi$ (via unitarized chiral perturbation theory), then searched for poles in the complex energy plane across different Riemann sheets to apply the pole counting rule.

## Key numbers

- The $Z_c(3900)$ mass from BESIII is $3899.0 \pm 3.6 \pm 4.9$ MeV and width $46 \pm 10 \pm 20$ MeV, as observed in $J/\psi\pi^\pm$ invariant mass in $e^+e^-\to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$.
- A peak structure with mass $3883.9 \pm 4.5$ MeV and width $24.8 \pm 11.5$ MeV was observed in the $(D\bar{D}^*)^\pm$ invariant mass spectrum in $e^+e^-\to \pi^\pm(D\bar{D}^*)^{\mp}$.
- The $\chi^2/d.o.f$ for Fit I (molecular, contact interaction only) is $454/(291-28)$, and for Fit II (bare $Z_c$ exchange) is $497/(291-26)$.
- The energy resolution for the $J/\psi\pi\pi$ channel is $\sigma = 4.2$ MeV, for $h_c\pi\pi$ is $1.8$ MeV, and for $D\bar{D}^*\pi$ is $1$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of $Z_c(3900)$ as a $D\bar{D}^*$ molecule (e.g., Guo (2014), Albaladejo (2015)) by including both $D\bar{D}^*$ contact interactions and explicit $Z_c$ exchange in a unified framework, and by simultaneously fitting $J/\psi\pi$, $\pi\pi$, $h_c\pi$, and $D\bar{D}^*$ spectra. The pole counting rule, developed by Morgan (1992) and applied to $X(3872)$ by Meng (2014), is used to discriminate molecular from elementary resonance scenarios; the finding of a single nearby pole supports the molecular interpretation, enabling future studies to treat $Z_c(3900)$ as a hadronic molecule in further theoretical and experimental investigations.
