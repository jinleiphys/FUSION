# New Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer-type theory at finite temperature with particle-number conservation

> We formulate a new Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS)-type theory at finite temperature, by deriving a set of variational equations of the free energy after the particle-number projection. With its broad applicability, this theory can be a useful tool for investigating the pairing phase transition in finite systems with the particle-number conservation. This theory provides effects of the symmetry-restoring fluctuation (SRF) for the pairing phenomena in finite fermionic systems, distinctively from those of additional quantum fluctuations. It is shown by numerical calculations that the phase transition is compatible with the conservation in this theory, and that the SRF shifts up the critical temperature ($T^\mathrm{cr}$). This shift of $T^\mathrm{cr}$ occurs due to reduction of degrees-of-freedom in canonical ensembles, and decreases only slowly as the particle-number increases (or as the level spacing narrows), in contrast to the conventional BCS theory.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new BCS-type theory formulated in canonical ensembles (CE-BCS) can reconcile the pairing phase transition with particle-number conservation in finite fermionic systems, and that the symmetry-restoring fluctuation (SRF) shifts the critical temperature upward relative to conventional grand-canonical BCS theory. The authors hedge that this picture arises from an approximation on the entropy, and that exact treatment of the entropy in the degenerate model washes out the transition signatures.

## Method

The authors derive variational equations for the free energy after applying the particle-number projection operator to the trial statistical operator, introducing an approximate entropy functional that satisfies the Peierls inequality. They solve the resulting coupled equations for the quasiparticle energies and occupation probabilities, which take a form analogous to the usual BCS gap equation but with the gap parameter replaced by an averaged quantity involving integration over the Nambu-Goldstone phase variable. Numerical calculations are performed for a constant-pairing Hamiltonian with equally spaced single-particle levels, using a steepest-descent minimization of the approximate free energy.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature in GCE-BCS is approximately 0.6 (in units of the zero-temperature GCE gap parameter Δ_G(0))
- For n=26 particles, the CE-BCS critical temperature T^cr_C is appreciably higher than T^cr_G, while the π_n-projected critical temperature T^cr_π gives a far less significant shift
- The shift of T^cr_C relative to the bulk limit follows T^cr_C - T^cr_∞ ∝ n^{-0.75} ∝ d^{0.75}, with a fitted relation T^cr = T^cr_∞ + 6.8 n^{-0.75}
- The model space cutoff is set to Λ = 10 (in units of Δ_G(0)), with level spacing d = 2Λ/(Ω-1) where Ω is half the number of single-particle states

## Context

This work extends the particle-number projected BCS approach of Egido and Ring (1993) from the degenerate model to general Hamiltonians at finite temperature, and contrasts with the variation-before-projection scheme of Radionov and Rossignoli (1994) and Tanabe and Nakada (2005). The theory enables investigation of how the superfluid-to-normal phase transition develops as particle number increases in finite systems such as atomic nuclei, where recent experiments by Schiller et al. (2001) revealed an S-shape in the heat capacity. The authors note that their earlier work (Nakada and Tanabe, 2005) showed that particle-number conservation alone can produce a similar S-shape even without a phase transition.
