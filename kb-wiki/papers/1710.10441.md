# Heavy Nuclei as Thermal Insulation for Proto-Neutron Stars

> A proto-neutron star (PNS) is a newly formed compact object in a core collapse supernova. In this Letter, the neutrino emission from the cooling process of a PNS is investigated using two types of nuclear equation of state (EOS). It is found that the neutrino signal is mainly determined by the high-density EOS. The neutrino luminosity and mean energy are higher and the cooling time scale is longer for the softer EOS. Meanwhile, the neutrino mean energy and the cooling time scale are also affected by the low-density EOS because of the difference in the population of heavy nuclei. Heavy nuclei have a large scattering cross section with neutrinos owing to the coherent effects and act as thermal insulation near the surface of a PNS. The neutrino mean energy is higher and the cooling time scale is longer for an EOS with a large symmetry energy at low densities, namely a small density derivative coefficient of the symmetry energy, $L$.

## Key claim

Heavy nuclei near the proto-neutron star surface act as thermal insulation by enhancing neutrino-matter interactions through coherent elastic scattering, which keeps the surface matter hot and raises the neutrino mean energy; this effect depends on the low-density nuclear equation of state, specifically a small density derivative coefficient of the symmetry energy \(L\).

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic neutrino-radiation hydrodynamics simulations of core collapse for a \(15M_\odot\) progenitor, followed by quasi-static proto-neutron star cooling simulations using a multigroup flux-limited diffusion scheme. They compared two nuclear equations of state (Togashi EOS and Shen EOS) and constructed a hybrid T+S EOS that uses the Togashi EOS at high densities and the Shen EOS at low densities, isolating the effect of heavy nuclei. The neutrino transfer included \(\nu_e\), \(\bar\nu_e\), and \(\nu_x\) with standard interactions plus neutrino-positron scattering, plasmon decay, and nucleon bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- Cooling time to central temperature 2.2 MeV: 120.1 s (Togashi EOS), 78.9 s (T+S EOS), 56.6 s (Shen EOS).
- Proto-neutron star radius at \(t=50\) s: 11.8 km (Togashi EOS), 14.1 km (Shen EOS).
- Central baryon mass density at \(t=50\) s: \(7.73\times10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) (Togashi EOS), \(4.87\times10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) (Shen EOS).
- Incompressibility: \(K=245\) MeV (Togashi EOS), 281 MeV (Shen EOS).
- Symmetry energy and its density derivative: \((E_{\rm sym},L)=(30.0\ {\rm MeV},35\ {\rm MeV})\) for Togashi EOS; \((36.9\ {\rm MeV},111\ {\rm MeV})\) for Shen EOS.
- At \(t=50\) s, the energy sphere radius for \(\bar\nu_e\) at 10 MeV: 10.9 km with density \(6.5\times10^{13}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) (Togashi EOS); 10.6 km with density \(1.9\times10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) (T+S EOS).
- Temperature at the energy sphere at \(t=50\) s: 4.9 MeV (Togashi EOS), 4.0 MeV (T+S EOS).
- Electron fraction at the uniform-nonuniform phase boundary at \(t=50\) s: 0.034 (Togashi EOS), 0.017 (Shen EOS).

## Context

This work extends studies of proto-neutron star cooling (Suzuki 1994; Sumiyoshi 2005) by explicitly separating the roles of high-density and low-density nuclear equation of state. It contrasts the Togashi EOS, based on variational many-body theory with AV18 and UIX potentials, with the widely used Shen EOS. The finding that heavy nuclei act as thermal insulation via coherent scattering builds on earlier work by Horowitz (2016) on pasta-phase effects and by Roberts (2012) on neutrino emission. The results imply that future observations of neutrino signals from a nearby supernova could constrain the symmetry energy parameter \(L\) through its imprint on neutrino mean energy and cooling timescale.
