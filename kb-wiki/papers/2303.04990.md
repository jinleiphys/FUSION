# Deformed ground state of $^{32}$Mg and breaking of pseudo-spin symmetry

> Deformed ground state of $^{32}$Mg is investigated using the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (D-RHFB) model with the effective Lagrangian PKA1, which provides coincident description with the experimental measurements. It is illustrated that obvious breaking of the pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) given by PKA1, being consistent with the experimental observation in nearby isotone $^{40}$Ca, is crucial for describing correctly the deformed ground state by producing unique shape evolution of neutron orbit $1/2_4^+$ in $^{32}$Mg. The PSS breaking is essentially determined by characteristic in-medium balance between nuclear attractions and repulsions that is manifested as unparalleled density dependent behaviors for coupling strengths $g_σ$ and $g_ω$ in dominant $σ$-scalar and $ω$-vector channels.

## Key claim

The deformed ground state of $^{32}$Mg is correctly described by the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (D-RHFB) model with Lagrangian PKA1 only when the pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) is broken, which is determined by the unparalleled density-dependent behaviors of the coupling strengths $g_\sigma$ and $g_\omega$ in the dominant $\sigma$-scalar and $\omega$-vector channels.

## Method

The authors used the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (D-RHFB) model with the effective Lagrangian PKA1, which includes $\sigma$-scalar, $\omega$-vector, $\rho$-vector, $\rho$-tensor, $\rho$-vector-tensor, $\pi$-pseudo-vector, and photon-vector couplings. The quasi-particle spinors were expanded on a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon (DWS) base, and the finite-range Gogny force D1S was adopted as the pairing force. Binding energies and single-particle energies were calculated as functions of quadrupole deformation $\beta$ and compared with results from PKO2, PKO3, and DD-ME2 models.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of $^{32}$Mg calculated with PKA1 has prolate deformation $\beta \approx 0.51$, consistent with experimental values $\beta = 0.512(44)$ (Motobayashi 1995) and $\beta = 0.51(3)$ (Church 2005).
- The ratio $E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+) = 2.6$ for $^{32}$Mg lies between the vibration limit 2.0 and rigid rotor limit 3.3 (Takeuchi 2009).
- The spherical DWS base truncation used energy cutoffs of $E_+^C = +350.0$ MeV and $E_-^C = -100.0$ MeV.
- Maximum projection values were $m_{\max} = 11/2$ for even parity and $13/2$ for odd parity states.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean-field (RMF) and relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) studies of $^{32}$Mg that could not self-consistently describe its deformed ground state. The authors build on the D-RHFB framework developed by Geng (2020, 2022) and the finding by Geng (2019) that improved in-medium balance is crucial for PSS restoration. The results contradict predictions from PKO2, PKO3, and DD-ME2 models, which all give spherical ground states for $^{32}$Mg. The work enables future studies of how nuclear in-medium effects, particularly the density dependence of $\sigma$ and $\omega$ coupling strengths, govern deformation and shell evolution in exotic nuclei.
