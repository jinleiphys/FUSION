# Phase diagram of the rotating two-component Fermi gas including vortices

> We determine the conditions under which superfluidity with and without quantized vortices appears in a weakly interacting two-component atomic Fermi gas that is trapped in a rotating cylindrical symmetric harmonic potential. We compute the phase diagram as a function of rotation frequency, scattering length, temperature, total number of trapped atoms, and population imbalance.

## Key claim
The paper determines the full phase diagram for superfluidity with and without quantized vortices in a weakly interacting two-component Fermi gas under rotation, but the results are quantitatively reliable only for weak interactions and may require beyond-mean-field corrections for stronger couplings (e.g., at \(1/(k_{F0}|a|) = 0.8\)).

## Method
The author solves the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation self-consistently for a cylindrical harmonic trap with infinite extension in the \(z\)-direction, computing the pairing field \(\Delta(\vec x)\) and density profiles for each spin component. The Helmholtz free energy difference \(\Delta \mathcal{F} = \mathcal{F}_{k=1} - \mathcal{F}_{k=0}\) between a single central vortex (winding number \(k=1\)) and the vortex-free state (\(k=0\)) is evaluated to locate phase boundaries where \(\Delta \mathcal{F} = 0\). The upper critical frequency for superfluidity \(\Omega_s\) is obtained by finding where \(\Delta(\rho=0)\) vanishes for \(k=0\) at a small finite temperature \(T = 0.01 \hbar \omega\).

## Key numbers
- For \(\mathcal{N} = 1000\), \(T \approx 0\), \(P = 0\), vortices form for \(|a| \gtrsim 0.085 \lambda\) (where \(\lambda = \sqrt{\hbar / M \omega}\) is the harmonic oscillator length).
- The radial trapping frequency in the experiment of Zwierlein (2006) was \(\omega / (2\pi) = 110\ \mathrm{Hz}\), giving \(\lambda \sim 3.9\ \mathrm{\mu m}\) and \(\hbar \omega / k_{\mathrm B} \sim 5.3\ \mathrm{nK}\).
- For \(\mathcal{N} = 1000\), \(\Omega = 0.636 \omega\), \(a = -0.13 \lambda\) (corresponding to \(1/(k_{F0}|a|) = 0.8\)), the critical population imbalance for vortex formation \(P_c\) is weakly temperature-dependent at low \(T\).
- In the experiment of Zwierlein (2006) with \(\sim 7 \times 10^6\) atoms and \(\omega_z/\omega = 23/110\), the estimated \(\mathcal{N}(z=0) \sim 1 \times 10^5\) and \(P_c \sim 0.1\) at \(1/(k_{F0}|a|) = 0.5\).

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of vortex formation in rotating Fermi gases, which were limited to \(T=0\) and \(P=0\) (Bruun 2001, Ginzburg-Landau estimate; Warringa 2011, BdG solution for \(\Omega_l\)). It also provides a systematic comparison to the experiment of Zwierlein (2006), which measured the critical population imbalance for vortex formation at a single rotation frequency in the strongly interacting regime. The phase diagrams enable future experimental searches for superfluidity in more complex systems, such as Fermi gases with \(p\)-wave pairing, more than two components, or unequal masses.
