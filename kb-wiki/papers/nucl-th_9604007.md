# Quantum Correlations in Nuclear Mean Field Theory Through Source Terms

> Starting from full quantum field theory, various mean field approaches are derived systematically. With a full consideration of external source dependence, the stationary phase approximation of an action gives a nuclear mean field theory which includes quantum correlation effects (such as particle-hole or ladder diagram) in a simpler way than the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. Implementing further approximation, the result can be reduced to Hartree-Fock or Hartree approximation. The role of the source dependence in a mean field theory is examined.

# Quantum Correlations in Nuclear Mean Field Theory Through Source Terms

## Key claim
The stationary phase approximation (SPA) of the full quantum field theory path integral, when external source dependence is fully retained, yields a nuclear mean field theory that includes quantum correlation effects (such as particle-hole or ladder diagrams) without requiring the self-consistent Bethe-Salpeter equation used in the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach; the source dependence is the mechanism that introduces these correlations, and different truncations of the source terms reduce the result to standard Hartree-Fock or Hartree approximations.

## Method
Starting from a relativistic Lagrangian of nucleons interacting via scalar and vector meson exchange, the author introduces arbitrary external sources for both the nucleon and meson fields and applies the stationary phase approximation (SPA) to the path integral for the transition amplitude. The nucleon field is expanded as a power series in the external sources, and the resulting coupled equations are solved order by order to obtain the action up to second order in the sources. From this action, the mean fields and propagators for the nucleon and meson fields are derived, with the nucleon propagator containing a correction term \(B^{-1}(x,x')\) that differs from the RHF propagator \(A^{-1}(x,x')\) and incorporates ladder-type correlations.

## Key numbers
- The meson propagator \(i \Delta(x-x')\) contains a free meson part plus nucleon-field-dependent terms; in RMF only the second term of Eq. (13) appears, while the SPA yields five terms including one- and two-baryon loop contributions.
- The nucleon propagator is given by \(i G(x,x') = i B^{-1}(x,x') + T[U(x)\bar{U}(x')]\), where the second term is the density-dependent propagator also appearing in RMF or RHF, and the first term corrects the RHF Feynman propagator \(i A^{-1}(x,x')\).
- The mean nucleon field is \(\langle \psi(x) \rangle = U(x)\) and the mean meson field is \(\langle \varphi(x) \rangle = \int d^4 x' D(x-x') \bar{U}(x') \Gamma U(x')\), as expected for a mean field theory.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic mean field (RMF) approach of Walecka (1974) and the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation by incorporating quantum correlations through a systematic treatment of external source dependence in the path integral. It addresses the known failure of simple mean field models to reproduce nuclear saturation properties from free nucleon-nucleon interactions (the Coester band problem), which the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach of Shakin, Brockmann, Machleidt, and others partially solved for nuclear matter but found too complicated for finite nuclei. The SPA presented here includes ladder diagram correlations through the propagator \(B^{-1}(x,x')\) without requiring the self-consistent Bethe-Salpeter equation, offering a simpler alternative to DBHF. Future work requires extending the action to fourth order in the sources to study nucleon-nucleon interactions and applying the model to nuclear systems.
