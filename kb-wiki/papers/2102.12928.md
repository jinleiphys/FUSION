# Alice meets Boojums in neutron stars: vortices penetrating two-flavor quark-hadron continuity

> Alice and Boojums are both representative characters created by Lewis Carroll. We show that they possibly meet in cores of rotating neutron stars. Recent studies of quark-hadron continuity suggest that neutron superfluid matter can connect smoothly to two-flavor symmetric quark matter at high densities. We study how this can be maintained in the presence of the vortices. In the neutron matter, quantized superfluid vortices arise. In the two-flavor dense quark matter, vortices carrying color magnetic fluxes together with fractionally quantized superfluid circulations appear as the most stable configuration, and we call these as the non-Abelian Alice strings. We show that three integer neutron superfluid vortices and three non-Abelian Alice strings of different color magnetic fluxes with total color flux canceled out are joined at a junction called a Boojum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the two-flavor quark-hadron continuity picture, a single non-Abelian Alice string in the 2SC+dd quark matter phase cannot be smoothly connected to a single integer vortex in the $^3P_2$ neutron superfluid phase, contrary to what the matching of Onsager-Feynman circulations might suggest; instead, three Alice strings with different color magnetic fluxes must join at a junction point to three integer neutron vortices, forming a colorful Boojum.

## Method

The authors analyze the generalized Aharonov-Bohm (AB) phases that quarks acquire when encircling vortices in both the hadronic and quark matter phases. They compute the AB phases for light quarks ($u$, $d$) and heavy quarks ($s$) around single integer $^3P_2$ neutron vortices and around non-Abelian Alice strings carrying red, green, or blue color magnetic fluxes, then compare these phases to determine which vortex configurations can be continuously connected across the phase boundary.

## Key numbers

- The circulation of a neutron $^3P_2$ vortex is $C_{nn} = \pi / \mu_{\rm B}$, where $\mu_{\rm B}$ is the baryon chemical potential.
- The circulation of a non-Abelian Alice string is $C_{\text{NA}} = \pi /3 \mu_{\rm q} = \pi / \mu_{\rm B}$, where $\mu_{\rm q} = \mu_{\rm B}/3$ is the quark chemical potential, showing the circulations coincide.
- Non-Abelian Alice strings carry $1/6$ quantized color-magnetic fluxes, $\calF = \calF_0 / 6$, and $1/3$ quantized circulations in $\rm U(1)_B$.
- Light quarks ($u$, $d$) encircling a single integer neutron vortex acquire a phase factor $\exp(i\varphi/6)$, forming a ${\mathbb Z}_6$ group; after complete encirclement they obtain $\exp(i\pi) = -1$.
- Light quarks encircling a single Alice string form a ${\mathbb Z}_2$ group with phase factor $e^{i \varphi/2 }$ for the matching color component.
- Heavy quarks ($s$) encircling an Alice string form a ${\mathbb Z}_6$ group.

## Context

This work extends the concept of quark-hadron continuity, originally proposed by Schafer (1998) for the three-flavor CFL phase and hyperon matter, to the two-flavor case involving the 2SC+dd phase and $^3P_2$ neutron superfluid matter as developed by Fujimoto (2020). It builds on the earlier suggestion by Alford (2018) that Boojum structures may appear in three-flavor continuity, and on the finding by Chatterjee (2018) that three non-Abelian vortices with different color fluxes must join to three integer vortices in hyperonic matter. The results enable future studies of how the approximately $10^{17}$ vortices in rotating neutron stars influence pulsar glitch phenomena.
