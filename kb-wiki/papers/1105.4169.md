# Shell-model descriptions of mass 16-19 nuclei with chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions

> Shell-model calculations for several mass 16-19 nuclei are performed using the N$^3$LO two-nucleon potential $V_{2N}$ with and without the addition of an in-medium three-nucleon potential $V_{3N}^{med}$, which is a density-dependent effective two-nucleon potential recently derived from the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force $V_{3N}$ by Holt, Kaiser, and Weise. We first calculate the $V_{low-k}$ low-momentum interactions from $V_{2N}$ and $V_{3N}^{med}$. The shell-model effective interactions for both the $sd$ one-shell and $sdpf$ two-shell model spaces are then obtained from these low-momentum interactions using respectively the Lee-Suzuki and the recently developed Okamoto and Suzuki iteration methods. The effects of $V_{3N}^{med}$ to the low-lying states of $^{18}O$, $^{18}F$, $^{19}O$ and $^{19}F$ are generally small and attractive, mainly lowering the ground-state energies of these nuclei and making them in better agreements with experiments than those calculated with $V_{2N}$ alone. The excitation spectra of these nuclei are not significantly affected by $V_{3N}^{med}$. The low-lying spectra of these nuclei calculated with the $sd$ and $sdpf$ model spaces are closely similar to each other. Our shell-model calculations for $^{16}O$ indicate that the $V_{3N}^{med}$ interaction is important and desirable for the binding energy of this nucleus.

# Shell-model descriptions of mass 16-19 nuclei with chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions

## Key claim

The in-medium three-nucleon potential $V_{3N}^{med}$ provides small attractive corrections to ground-state energies of $^{18}O$, $^{18}F$, $^{19}O$, and $^{19}F$ that improve agreement with experiment, but only when the valence nucleons are embedded in a low-density medium (approximately 0.15 $\rho_0$); the excitation spectra of these nuclei are not significantly affected by $V_{3N}^{med}$.

## Method

The authors first derived low-momentum $V_{low-k}$ interactions from the N$^3$LO two-nucleon potential $V_{2N}$ and from a density-dependent effective two-nucleon potential $V_{3N}^{med}$ obtained by closing one pair of external lines of the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force and summing over the filled Fermi sea. Shell-model effective interactions for the $sd$ one-shell and $sdpf$ two-shell model spaces were then calculated using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method (for the degenerate $sd$ space) and the Okamoto-Suzuki (EKKO) iteration method (for the non-degenerate $sdpf$ space). Nuclear matter and $^{16}O$ ring-diagram calculations were also performed to determine the low-energy constants $c_D$ and $c_E$ and to study the density dependence of $V_{3N}^{med}$.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation: with $c_D = -2.7$ and $c_E = 0.7$, the ring-diagram calculation gives $E_0/A \simeq -16$ MeV and $\rho_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, in satisfactory agreement with empirical values.
- $^{16}O$ ground-state energy per nucleon: with $V_{2N}$ alone at $\rho/\rho_0 = 0.75$, the ring-diagram result is -6.97 MeV; adding $V_{3N}^{med}$ at the same density gives -8.01 MeV per nucleon, compared to the experimental value of -8.00 MeV.
- Valence nucleon local density: estimated from the density profile of $^{16}O$ and the $d_{5/2}$ radial wave function, the adopted value is $\rho_v = 0.15 \rho_0$ (approximately 0.024 fm$^{-3}$).
- Decimation scale: a common cutoff $\Lambda = 2.1$ fm$^{-1}$ was employed for both $V_{2N}^{low-k}$ and $V_{3N}^{low-k}$.
- Low-energy constants: $c_1 = -0.76$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_3 = -4.78$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_4 = 3.96$ GeV$^{-1}$; $g_A = 1.29$, $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, $\Lambda_\chi = 700$ MeV, $m_\pi = 138.04$ MeV/c$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic shell-model approach of Dong et al. (2011) by incorporating the in-medium three-nucleon force $V_{3N}^{med}$ derived by Holt, Kaiser, and Weise (2009, 2010) from leading-order chiral three-nucleon interactions. It builds on prior studies showing that $V_{2N}$ alone is inadequate for nuclear matter saturation (Siu et al., 2009; Dong et al., 2009) and that three-nucleon forces are essential for light nuclei (Nogga et al., 2000; Pieper et al., 2002; Navratil et al., 2007) and for the $^{14}C$ beta-decay half-life (Holt et al., 2009, 2010). The results enable future shell-model calculations for $sd$ and $sdpf$ nuclei that include chiral three-nucleon forces, with the caveat that the density dependence of $V_{3N}^{med}$ must be carefully determined for valence nucleons in low-density regions.
