# Evidence of sub-nucleonic degrees of freedom in J/$ψ$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We present calculations for the incoherent photoproduction of J/$ψ$ vector mesons in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions (UPC) in terms of hadronic interactions. This study was carried out using the recently developed Monte Carlo model CRISP extended to include UPCs at LHC energies. A careful study of re-scattering and destruction of the J/$ψ$ particles is presented for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV. We have also compared our method to AuAu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV measured at RHIC.

## Key claim

The CRISP hadronic model overestimates the incoherent J/ψ photoproduction cross section in PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV by nearly 100%, which the authors interpret as evidence that sub-nucleonic degrees of freedom are necessary to describe the process at LHC energies, whereas a purely nucleonic model remains satisfactory at RHIC energies (√sNN = 200 GeV).

## Method

The authors extended the Monte Carlo intranuclear cascade model CRISP to ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, using the soft dipole Pomeron model of Martynov, Predazzi and Prokudin for the γp → J/ψ cross section and the Weisskopf evaporation mechanism for the slow stage of the reaction. They calculated the virtual photon flux from the relativistic projectile nucleus using the standard Fermi-Weizsäcker-Williams method with a survival probability factor, then simulated the intranuclear cascade including final-state interactions (FSI) of the produced J/ψ mesons with the nuclear medium. The model was applied to PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and AuAu collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- In PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, the CRISP model overestimates the experimental incoherent J/ψ cross section by nearly 100%.
- For PbPb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, the photon energy at y=0 in the target frame is Eγ = 4.4 TeV; for AuAu at √sNN = 200 GeV, it is Eγ = 0.3 TeV.
- The rapidity range studied for PbPb was −3 < y < 3, corresponding to photon energies 219 GeV < Eγ < 89 TeV and γp center-of-mass energies 20 GeV < Wγp < 409 GeV.
- For AuAu at √sNN = 200 GeV, the rapidity range was −2 < y < 2, corresponding to 45 GeV < Eγ < 2.44 TeV and 9 GeV < Wγp < 68 GeV.
- Only approximately 1% of the produced J/ψ mesons escape the nucleus due to final-state interactions.
- The exponent α in the Aα scaling of the cross section is approximately 0.94 for J/ψ, compared to α ≈ 2/3 for strongly interacting particles.
- The nucleon-nucleon total cross section used was σNN = 80 mb for PbPb and σNN = 53 mb for AuAu.
- The nuclear density parameters used were R = 6.62 fm, a = 0.546 fm for Pb; R = 6.43 fm, a = 0.541 fm for Au.
- The PHENIX Collaboration estimated the incoherent contribution in AuAu at √sNN = 200 GeV to be approximately 40%, or about 30 μb.

## Context

This work extends the CRISP model, previously applied to photon-, electron-, and proton-induced reactions at lower energies (Rodrigues 2004, Deppman 2004, Andrade 2011), to ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC energies. The results contradict purely hadronic descriptions at LHC energies and support the need for partonic models such as the impact-parameter saturated dipole model (LM-fiPsat by Lappi 2013) or the partonic model RSZ-LTA, which depend on nuclear gluon distributions. The findings enable future studies of J/ψ production in collisions of different nuclear sizes (e.g., CC and UU) and at intermediate energies between √sNN = 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV to pinpoint the energy threshold where sub-nucleonic degrees of freedom become essential.
