# The behaviour of constrained caloric curves as ultimate signature of a phase transition for hot nuclei

> Simulations based on experimental data obtained from multifragmenting quasifused nuclei produced in central 129Xe + natSn collisions have been used to deduce event by event freeze-out properties on the thermal excitation energy range 4-12 AMeV. From these properties and temperatures deduced from proton transverse momentum fluctuations constrained caloric curves have been built. At constant average volumes caloric curves exhibit a monotonous behaviour whereas for constrained pressures a backbending is observed. Such results support the existence of a first order phase transition for hot nuclei.

## Key claim

Simulations of multifragmenting quasifused nuclei from central 129Xe + natSn collisions, combined with a new thermometer based on proton transverse momentum fluctuations including quantum corrections, show that caloric curves constrained at constant average volume behave monotonically while those constrained at constant pressure exhibit backbending, supporting the existence of a first-order phase transition for hot nuclei.

## Method

Event-by-event freeze-out properties (thermal excitation energy, kinetic temperature, freeze-out volume, and total thermal kinetic energy) were reconstructed from simulations that reproduce experimental observables for quasifused sources produced in central 129Xe + natSn collisions at 32–50 AMeV. Temperatures were extracted from proton transverse momentum fluctuations using a quantum-corrected thermometer that accounts for Fermi-Dirac statistics, with Fermi energies estimated from semiclassical calculations at low density (ρ ≈ 0.4ρ₀). Constrained caloric curves were then built by selecting events within narrow ranges of average freeze-out volume (expressed in units of V₀, the normal-density volume) or pressure.

## Key numbers

- Thermal excitation energy range studied: 4–12 AMeV.
- For constrained average volumes of 3.0–4.0 V₀ and 5.0–6.0 V₀, caloric curves show monotonous behaviour.
- For constrained pressures of 1.3–4.5 × 10⁻² MeV fm⁻³, backbending is clearly observed; for 4.5–7.9 × 10⁻² MeV fm⁻³, backbending is largely reduced.
- The unconstrained caloric curve with quantum-corrected temperatures exhibits a plateau at 10–11 MeV over the thermal excitation energy range 5–10 AMeV.
- The critical temperature inferred from the pressure-constrained curves is around 12–13 MeV for the selected hot nuclei (Z = 80–100, A = 194–238).
- Low density at freeze-out was estimated as ρ ≈ 0.4ρ₀, corresponding to a Fermi energy ε_f ≈ 20 MeV; systematic error on quantum temperatures from density uncertainty is ±0.6–0.5 MeV over the excitation energy range.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition signatures, including negative microcanonical heat capacity and bimodality of an order parameter (Borderie 2008, Bonnet 2009), by providing constrained caloric curves as an additional signature. It builds on theoretical predictions by Chomaz (2000) and Furuta (2006) that constrained caloric curves behave differently for first-order phase transitions. The results agree with earlier experimental indications from spinodal signals (Tabacaru 2003) and bimodality (Bonnet 2009), and with the onset of vaporization around 10 AMeV (Tsang 1993, Borderie 1996, Borderie 1999, Piesse 2000). The He/Li thermometer used in previous work (Pochodzalla 1995, Natowitz 2002) is shown to mainly reflect internal fragment temperatures rather than the kinetic/microcanonical temperature relevant for phase transition studies.
