# Nuclear Structure and Reaction Properties of Ne, Mg and Si Isotopes with RMF Densities

> We have studied nuclear structure and reaction properties of Ne, Mg and Si isotopes, using relativistic mean field densities, in the frame work of Glauber model. Total reaction cross section $σ_R$ for Ne isotopes on 12C target have been calculated at incident energy 240 MeV. The results are compared with the experimental data and with the recent theoretical study [W. Horiuchi et al., Phys. Rev. C, 86, 024614 (2012)]. Study of $σ_R$ using deformed densities have shown a good agreement with the data. We have also predicted total reaction cross section $σ_R$ for Ne, Mg and Si isotopes as projectiles and 12C as target at different incident energies.

## Key claim

The authors claim that using deformed (rather than spherical) relativistic mean field densities as input to the Glauber model yields total reaction cross sections for Ne isotopes on a 12C target at 240 MeV/nucleon that agree well with experimental data, but only for the halo nucleus 31Ne does a "core + one neutron" treatment produce a remarkable agreement with the data.

## Method

The authors calculated bulk nuclear properties (binding energy, charge radius, quadrupole deformation) for Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes using the relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the NL3* parameter set and the effective RMF (E-RMF) formalism with the G2 parameter set. They then used the resulting spherical and deformed density distributions as input to the Glauber model to compute total reaction cross sections for these projectiles on a 12C target at various incident energies, comparing their results to experimental data and to the theoretical study of Horiuchi et al. (2012).

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 18Ne: 131.8 MeV (RMF), 135.3 MeV (E-RMF), compared to experimental 132.1 MeV.
- Charge radius of 18Ne: 2.963 fm (RMF), 3.055 fm (E-RMF), compared to experimental 2.972 fm.
- For 28Ne + 12C at 240 MeV/nucleon, the experimental reaction cross section is 1273 ± 11 mb, while the spherical RMF (NL3*) calculation gives 1440 mb.
- Reaction cross sections for Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes on 12C target were predicted at multiple incident energies (figures show results at energies including 240 MeV/nucleon).

## Context

This work extends the application of relativistic mean field densities combined with the Glauber model to study reaction cross sections of neutron-rich Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes, building on prior work by Horiuchi et al. (2012) and earlier studies by the same authors (Panda et al., Sharma et al., Patra et al.). The results demonstrate that deformation effects in the nuclear density are essential for reproducing experimental reaction cross sections for stable nuclei, and that a core-plus-neutron treatment is needed for halo nuclei like 31Ne. This enables future systematic studies of reaction cross sections for exotic nuclei near the drip line using deformed RMF densities.
