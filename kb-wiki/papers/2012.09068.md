# Equilibration of the Quark-Gluon Plasma at finite net-baryon density in QCD kinetic theory

> We explore the out-of-equilibrium dynamics of the Quark-Gluon Plasma at zero and finite net-baryon density based on an effective kinetic theory of Quantum Chromo Dynamics (QCD). By investigating the isotropization of the longitudinal pressure, we determine the relevant time and temperature scales for the onset of viscous hydrodynamics, and quantify the dependence on the chemical composition of the QGP. By extrapolating our results to realistic coupling strength, we discuss phenomenological consequences regarding the role of the pre-equilibrium phase at different collision energies.

## Key claim

The paper extends the study of pre-equilibrium QGP dynamics to finite net-baryon density for the first time, finding that while viscous hydrodynamics becomes applicable at the same scaled time as in zero-density systems, the chemical composition of the plasma significantly affects the pressure evolution at early times, challenging the existence of a universal non-equilibrium attractor for multi-component plasmas.

## Method

The authors solve the leading-order QCD Boltzmann equation for a longitudinally boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous system undergoing one-dimensional Bjorken expansion, including all leading-order elastic (2↔2) and inelastic (1↔2) interactions with in-medium screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression. They parametrize the initial phase-space distributions of gluons, quarks, and anti-quarks with a momentum anisotropy parameter ξ₀ and initial abundances for gluons (f⁰_g), valence quarks (f⁰_v), and sea quarks (f⁰_s), then evolve the system for various combinations of these parameters at zero and finite net-baryon density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pre-equilibrium dynamics in pure-glue QCD (Kurkela 2014) and charge-neutral QCD (Kurkela 2018) to include finite net-baryon density, using the same effective kinetic theory framework. The results enable the inclusion of all QCD conserved charges into dynamical descriptions of the pre-equilibrium stage, such as the KoMPoST framework (Kurkela 2018), and provide a basis for future investigations of heavy flavor dynamics and electromagnetic probes of the early-time non-equilibrium phase.
