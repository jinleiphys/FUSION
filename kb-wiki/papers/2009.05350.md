# Probing Lorentz violation in $2νββ$ using single electron spectra and angular correlations

> We show that the current search for Lorentz invariance violation (LIV) in the summed energy spectra of electrons in $2νββ$ decay can be extended by investigating the single electron spectra and the angular correlation between the emitted electrons. We derive and calculate the LIV contributions to these spectra associated with the anisotropic part of the countershaded operator and controlled through the coefficient $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ and discuss possible signatures that may be probed in experiments. First, we show that some distortion occurs in the single electron spectrum, maximal at small electron energies. Then, we show that other LIV effects may be highlighted by analysing the angular correlation spectra and the ratio between the Standard Model Extension (SME) electron spectra and their Standard Model (SM) forms. We found that these LIV signatures depend on the magnitude of $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$, manifest differently for positive and negative values of this coefficient, and become more pronounced as the electron energy approaches the $Q$-value. Finally, we propose an alternative, new method to constrain $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ through the measurement of the angular correlation coefficient. Using this method, and considering only statistical uncertainties, we obtain bounds of $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ at the level of present ones, obtained from summed energy spectra. We show that future experiments can improve these limits significantly. Our study is performed for $^{100}$Mo, but the results hold qualitatively for other nuclei that undergo a double-beta decay. We hope our results will provide additional motivation for the LIV analyses performed in DBD experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that Lorentz invariance violation (LIV) in $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay can be probed not only through summed electron energy spectra (as previously done) but also through single electron spectra and the angular correlation between the two emitted electrons, with the LIV signatures depending on the sign and magnitude of the coefficient $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ and becoming more pronounced as electron energy approaches the $Q$-value.

## Method

The authors derived LIV contributions to the differential decay rate by modifying the antineutrino phase space to first order in $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$, using the Standard Model Extension (SME) framework. They computed the phase space factors (PSFs) for $^{100}$Mo under the single state dominance (SSD) hypothesis, using exact electron radial wave functions obtained by numerically solving the Dirac equation with a realistic Coulomb potential including finite nuclear size, diffuse nuclear surface, and screening effects. The LIV contributions were then incorporated into expressions for single electron spectra, summed energy spectra, angular correlation spectra, and the angular correlation coefficient $\kappa^{2\nu}$.

## Key numbers

- For $^{100}$Mo, the SM angular correlation coefficient was computed as $\kappa_{\mathrm{SM}}^{2\nu} = -0.6676$ using exact electron wave functions.
- The LIV contribution to the angular correlation coefficient was computed as $\xi_{LV}^{2\nu} = -4.285\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}$, giving $\kappa_{\mathrm{SME}}^{2\nu} = -0.6676 -4.285\times \mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ (with $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ in MeV).
- Using NEMO-3 statistics of $5\times10^{5}$ events and considering only statistical uncertainties, the angular correlation coefficient is measurable with uncertainty $\pm0.0027$, yielding a bound $|\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}| \lesssim 1.04\times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{MeV}$ at 90% CL.
- For a future experiment with $5\times10^{8}$ events (three orders of magnitude increase), the estimated bound improves to $|\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}| \lesssim 3.3\times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{MeV}$ at 90% CL.
- The EXO-200 limits used were $-2.65\times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{MeV} \le \mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)} \le 7.6\times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{MeV}$; the NEMO-3 limits were $-4.2\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{MeV} \le \mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)} \le 3.5\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- The $Q$-value for $^{100}$Mo $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay is $Q = 3.0344\ \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends previous LIV analyses in $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay, which were limited to summed energy spectra of electrons (EXO-200, CUPID-0, NEMO-3, CUORE, GERDA collaborations; Diaz 2013, 2014; Nitescu 2020), by deriving LIV signatures in single electron spectra and angular correlations. The authors improve upon earlier calculations that used non-relativistic approximations or approximate methods for electron wave functions, employing exact Dirac solutions. The proposed method of constraining $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ through the angular correlation coefficient offers an alternative to summed-energy analyses, with future experiments like SuperNEMO potentially reaching limits comparable to those from tritium decay experiments (Diaz 2014).
