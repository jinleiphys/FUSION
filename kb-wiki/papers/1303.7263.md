# Pion-mass dispersion relation in the baryon sector

> By looking at the complex plane of the pion-mass squared we establish a dispersion relation which the static quantities, such as baryon masses, magnetic moments, polarizabilities, should obey. This dispersion relation yields insight into the differences between the heavy-baryon and relativistic calculations in the baryon sector of chiral perturbation theory.

# Pion-mass dispersion relation in the baryon sector

## Key claim

The paper establishes a dispersion relation in the complex plane of the pion-mass squared that static quantities (baryon masses, magnetic moments, polarizabilities) should obey, and uses it to show that heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) exhibits a stronger cutoff dependence and less natural behavior than relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT), with the severity of the discrepancy depending on the power of the expansion parameter at which chiral loops first contribute.

## Method

The authors write a dispersion integral for chiral-loop corrections to static nucleon quantities in the complex pion-mass-squared plane, introducing a cutoff Λ on the integration along the negative real axis and a subtraction parameter n to control the chiral-limit behavior. They compute the imaginary parts of the nucleon mass, isovector anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs), and proton magnetic polarizability at chiral order p³ in both BχPT and HBχPT, then substitute these into the dispersion relation to obtain the cutoff-dependent loop contributions at the physical pion mass (mπ ≈ 139 MeV). The resulting cutoff dependence is compared between the two formulations to quantify the impact of 1/M_N suppressed terms that are dropped in HBχPT.

## Key numbers

- Physical nucleon mass: M̂_N ≈ 939 MeV, used as the scale in dimensionless variables τ = t/M̂_N² and λ = √(τ²/4 − τ)
- Physical pion mass: mπ ≈ 139 MeV, used as the fixed value for evaluating cutoff dependence
- Fine-structure constant: e²/4π ≈ 1/137, appearing in the magnetic polarizability imaginary part
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λχ ≈ 4πfπ ≈ 1 GeV, used as the natural cutoff scale for separating analytic and non-analytic contributions
- Residual cutoff dependence in HBχPT falls as 1/Λ for all considered examples, while in BχPT it falls as 1/Λ² for the nucleon mass and as 1/Λ⁴ for both AMMs and the magnetic polarizability

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach introduced by Ledwig et al. (2010) to systematically compare HBχPT and BχPT calculations. It addresses large discrepancies previously observed in calculations by Pascalutsa (2004), Geng (2008), Lensky (2009), and Strikman (2010) between the two formulations. The results suggest that BχPT may resolve problems in the two-nucleon sector where non-relativistic χPT failed to describe effective-range expansion coefficients, as noted by Kaplan (1998) and Cohen (1998), in a manner analogous to the successful BχPT treatment of nucleon polarizabilities by Lensky (2009).
