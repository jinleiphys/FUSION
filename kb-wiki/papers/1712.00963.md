# Pre-equilibrium effects of the hot nuclei de-excitation via GDR emission - theoretical approach

> The hot rotating nuclei could be formed in the complete and incomplete fusion reaction of two heavy ions. At low bombarding energies the reaction goes via compound nucleus formation and subsequent evaporation of charged particles, neutrons and $γ$-rays. However, with increasing the energy of the projectile, the emission of particles during the equilibration process becomes more and more probable. This effect can be estimated by the Heavy-Ion Phase-Space Exploration (HIPSE) code which describes the production of clusters of various size from nucleons initially in the target or projectile. This dynamic evolution finalizes with the compound nuclei, quasi-fission or multi-fragmentation products. The hot rotating nuclei produced in fusion reaction can de-excitate by evaporation of particles and emission of $γ$-rays from the Giant Dipole Resonance, or by fission into two fragments. These processes, evaporation and fission, are described within statistical codes such as GEMINI++ or in dynamical approaches by solving the transport equations of Langevin type. In the present article we will concentrate on the possible effect of the pre-equilibrium emission on the strength function of the effective Giant Dipole Resonance, which can be described within Thermal Shape Fluctuation Model (TSFM) approach.

# Pre-equilibrium effects of the hot nuclei de-excitation via GDR emission - theoretical approach

## Key claim
Pre-equilibrium particle emission before compound nucleus formation can significantly modify the effective Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) strength function compared to the standard assumption of a single compound nucleus with a triangular spin distribution. The effect is most visible at higher beam energies where pre-equilibrium emission becomes probable, and the resulting GDR strength function shows a reduced low-energy component around 8 MeV, indicating a smaller contribution from highly deformed nuclei.

## Method
The authors used the Heavy-Ion Phase-Space Exploration (HIPSE) event generator to simulate the reaction ⁴⁸Ti + ⁴⁰Ca at 300 and 600 MeV beam energies, producing an ensemble of prefragments with their masses, excitation energies, and angular momenta. The GDR strength functions for these prefragments were then calculated using the Thermal Shape Fluctuation Model (TSFM), with potential energy surfaces obtained from the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop model and a nuclear level density parameter of A_CN/7. The results were compared to the traditional approach assuming a single ⁸⁸Mo compound nucleus with a triangular spin distribution up to l_max = 64 ℏ.

## Key numbers
- Excitation energies of the compound nucleus ⁸⁸Mo: 123.8 MeV for 300 MeV beam energy and 260.7 MeV for 600 MeV beam energy.
- Angular momentum range used in the analysis: 10–64 ℏ, with the fission barrier vanishing at L_cut = 64 ℏ.
- The GDR strength function for nuclei after pre-equilibrium emission shows a reduced low-energy component around 8 MeV compared to the compound nucleus case.
- The prefragment mass range considered for pre-equilibrium effects was A > 60, with compound nucleus mass at A = 88.

## Context
This work extends previous experimental and theoretical studies of the ⁴⁸Ti + ⁴⁰Ca reaction at 300 and 600 MeV analyzed with GEMINI++ by Charity (2010) and Ciemala (2013), and compared to experimental data by Ciemala (2015, 2017) and Valdre (2016). The HIPSE code, developed by Lacroix (2004), provides a more realistic description of pre-equilibrium cluster formation than the simple compound nucleus assumption. The TSFM approach, previously successful in predicting Jacobi shape transitions in rotating nuclei at high spins by Kmiecik (2007), is here applied to assess how pre-equilibrium emission modifies the effective GDR strength function. This work enables more accurate interpretation of experimental GDR data from hot rotating nuclei formed in fusion reactions at intermediate beam energies, where pre-equilibrium effects cannot be neglected.
