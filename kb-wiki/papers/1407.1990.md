# Timelike deeply virtual Compton scattering with a linearly polarized real (or quasi-real) photon beam

> We calculate timelike virtual Compton scattering amplitudes in the generalized Bjorken scaling regime and focus on a new polarization asymmetry in the scattering process with a linearly polarized photon beam in the medium energy range, which will be studied intensely at JLab12 experiments. We demonstrate that new observables help us to access the polarized quark and gluon generalized parton distributions $\tilde H(x, ξ, t)$ and $ \tilde E(x, ξ, t)$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a new polarization asymmetry in timelike Compton scattering (TCS) with a linearly polarized photon beam can provide access to the polarized quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) \(\tilde H(x, \xi, t)\) and \(\tilde E(x, \xi, t)\), but only if the interference between the TCS and Bethe-Heitler (BH) amplitudes is isolated through specific angular observables; the BH contribution dominates the cross section over the entire kinematic range considered.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for the exclusive photoproduction of a lepton pair off a proton, \(\gamma p \to l^+ l^- p\), in the generalized Bjorken scaling regime, including both the timelike Compton scattering and Bethe-Heitler mechanisms at leading twist and next-to-leading order in \(\alpha_S\). They define two angular observables, \(\tilde{R}\) and \(\tilde{R}_3\), which project out the unpolarized and linearly polarized parts of the interference cross section, and construct the ratio \(C = \tilde{R}/\tilde{R}_3\) to isolate the Compton form factor \(\tilde{\mathcal{H}}\). Numerical estimates are performed using the GK GPD model with the gluonic contribution \(\tilde{H}_g\) varied by factors of \(\{-1, 0, 1, 2, 3\}\) relative to the GK model.

## Key numbers

- The observable \(C\) is evaluated at \(Q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\) and minimal \(t = t_0(\eta) = -4\eta^2 M^2/(1-\eta^2)\), showing effects of the order of \(30\%\) when varying the gluonic GPD \(\tilde{H}_g\).
- The observable \(C\) is also shown as a function of \(t\) for \(Q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(\eta = 0.1\), with the same \(30\%\) sensitivity to \(\tilde{H}_g\).
- The calculations are performed with NLO accuracy and \(\alpha_S = 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Goritschnig, Pire, Wagner, 2014) on accessing polarized GPDs through TCS with linearly polarized photons, and builds on the formalism for TCS developed by Berger, Diehl, and Pire (2001) and Pire, Szymanowski, and Wagner (2011). The proposed observables are designed for the medium energy range of JLab12 experiments (Nadel-Turonski, 2012), where linearly polarized photon beams are available via forward tagging at CLAS12 or coherent bremsstrahlung at GlueX (Somov, 2011). The results enable future experimental extraction of the polarized gluon GPD \(\tilde{H}_g\), which remains poorly constrained.
