# A Parallel Algorithm for Solving the 3d Schrodinger Equation

> We describe a parallel algorithm for solving the time-independent 3d Schrodinger equation using the finite difference time domain (FDTD) method. We introduce an optimized parallelization scheme that reduces communication overhead between computational nodes. We demonstrate that the compute time, t, scales inversely with the number of computational nodes as t ~ N_nodes^(-0.95 +/- 0.04). This makes it possible to solve the 3d Schrodinger equation on extremely large spatial lattices using a small computing cluster. In addition, we present a new method for precisely determining the energy eigenvalues and wavefunctions of quantum states based on a symmetry constraint on the FDTD initial condition. Finally, we discuss the usage of multi-resolution techniques in order to speed up convergence on extremely large lattices.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the parallel FDTD algorithm enables solving the 3d Schrodinger equation on extremely large spatial lattices using a small computing cluster, with compute time scaling as t ~ N_nodes^(-0.95 +/- 0.04), though the actual scaling exponents measured for specific potentials ranged from -0.91 to better than ideal, with the quoted exponent representing an average across benchmarks.

## Method

The authors discretize the Wick-rotated time-dependent Schrodinger equation on a 3d lattice using finite differences, then evolve an initial random wavefunction in imaginary time until it converges to the ground state. They parallelize the computation by slicing the N^3 lattice along one spatial direction into M pieces distributed across computational nodes, using non-blocking MPI communications to overlap boundary data exchange with interior lattice updates. Excited states are extracted either by projecting out lower-energy states from saved wavefunction snapshots or by imposing symmetry constraints on the initial wavefunction.

## Key numbers

- Compute time scales as t ~ N_nodes^(-0.95 +/- 0.04) for the general benchmark on a 512^3 lattice
- For the Coulomb potential on a 512^3 lattice with a=0.05 and Delta_tau=6.25e-4, the ground state energy obtained was E_0 = -0.499 (0.2% accuracy) and first excited state E_1 = -0.122 (2.4% accuracy)
- For the 3d harmonic oscillator on a 512^3 lattice with a=0.02 and Delta_tau=1.0e-4, the ground state energy obtained was E_0 = 1.49996 (0.0026% accuracy)
- The stability condition requires Delta_tau < a^2/3, and total compute time scales as t_total ~ N^5 for fixed lattice volume
- For a 512^3 lattice, the algorithm performs well up to N_nodes = 64, at which point communication time equals iteration time

## Context

This work extends the FDTD method previously applied to the 3d Schrodinger equation by Feagin, Roy, Wadehra, Sullivan, and Sudiarta (2007) by introducing an optimized parallelization scheme. The method contrasts with Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) techniques developed by Grimm (1971), Anderson (1975), and Foulkes (2001), which suffer from the fermion sign problem requiring the fixed-node approximation and typically only extract ground state properties. The parallel algorithm enables solving for both ground and excited states on lattices up to 1024^3 or larger using small computing clusters, and the open-source code release allows other researchers to apply the method to arbitrary 3d potentials.
