# On the question of quark confinement in the Abelian U(1) QED gauge interaction

> If we approximate light quarks as massless and apply the Schwinger confinement mechanism to light quarks, we will reach the conclusion that a light quark $q$ and its antiquark $\bar q$ will be confined as a $q\bar q$ boson in the Abelian U(1) QED gauge interaction in (1+1)D, as in an open string. From the work of Coleman, Jackiw, and Susskind, we can infer further that the Schwinger confinement mechanism persists even for massive quarks in (1+1)D. Could such a QED-confined $q\bar q$ one-dimensional open string in (1+1)D be the idealization of a flux tube in the physical world in (3+1)D, similar to the case of QCD-confined $q\bar q$ open string? If so, the QED-confined $q\bar q$ bosons may show up as neutral QED mesons in the mass region of many tens of MeV (PRC81(2010)064903 & JHEP2020(8)165). Is it ever possible that a quark and an antiquark be produced and interact in QED alone to form a confined QED meson? Is there any experimental evidence for the existence of a QED meson (or QED mesons)? The observations of the anomalous soft photons, the X17 particle, and the E38 particle suggest that they may bear the experimental evidence for the existence of such QED mesons. Further confirmation and investigations on the X17 and E38 particles will shed definitive light on the question of quark confinement in QED in (3+1)D. Implications of quark confinement in the QED interaction are discussed.

## Key claim

If light quarks are approximated as massless and the Schwinger confinement mechanism is applied, a light quark and its antiquark will be confined as a \(q\bar q\) boson in the Abelian U(1) QED gauge interaction in (1+1)D; the author proposes that such a one-dimensional open string may be the idealization of a flux tube in (3+1)D, and that the observed X17 particle at about 17 MeV and E38 particle at about 38 MeV may be experimental evidence for such QED-confined mesons.

## Method

The author applies the Schwinger confinement mechanism, originally derived for massless fermions in (1+1)D QED, to light quarks interacting in the QED interaction alone. The analysis uses the self-consistency condition between the applied gauge field \(A^\mu(x)\) and the induced quark current \(j^\mu(x)\) via the Dirac equation and Maxwell equations in (1+1)D, leading to a Klein-Gordon equation for a boson of mass \(m = g_{2D}/\sqrt{\pi}\). The author then extrapolates this (1+1)D confinement picture to (3+1)D by proposing that the QED-confined open string idealizes a flux tube, and compares predicted QED meson masses with experimental observations of anomalous particles.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger confinement mechanism gives a boson mass \(m = g_{2D}/\sqrt{\pi}\) for massless fermions in (1+1)D, where \(g_{2D}\) is the dimensional coupling constant.
- The X17 particle is observed at about 17 MeV.
- The E38 particle is observed at about 38 MeV.
- The pion mass gap \(m_\pi\) is approximately 135 MeV, which sets the energy threshold below which QCD collective excitations are not excited.
- The sum of the rest masses of a light quark and light antiquark is of order a few MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger confinement mechanism (Schwinger 1962, 1963) from massless fermions in (1+1)D to light quarks in QED, and draws on the work of Coleman, Jackiw, and Susskind (1975, 1976) who showed the mechanism persists for massive quarks in (1+1)D. The paper proposes that anomalous soft photons observed in high-energy collisions, the X17 particle (Krasznahorkay et al. 2016, 2019, 2021), and the E38 particle (Abraamyan et al. 2012, 2019) may be QED-confined \(q\bar q\) mesons. The author suggests that future lattice gauge calculations for compact QED in (3+1)D should include the Schwinger confinement mechanism, as static lattice calculations currently indicate deconfinement in contradiction with the experimental absence of fractional charges.
