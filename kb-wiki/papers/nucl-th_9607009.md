# Violation of the Ikeda sum rule and the self-consistency in the renormalized quasiparticle random phase approximation and the nuclear double-beta decay

> The effect of the inclusion of ground state correlations into the QRPA equation of motion for the two-neutrino double beta ($ββ_{2ν}$) decay is carefully analyzed. The resulting model, called renormalized QRPA (RQRPA), does not collapse near the physical value of the nuclear force strength in the particle-particle channel, as happens with the ordinary QRPA. Still, the $ββ_{2ν}$ transition amplitude is only slightly less sensitive on this parameter in the RQRPA than that in the plain QRPA. It is argued that this fact reveals once more that the characteristic behaviour of the $ββ_{2ν}$ transition amplitude within the QRPA is not an artifact of the model, but a consequence of the partial restoration of the spin-isospin $SU(4)$ symmetry. It is shown that the price paid for bypassing the collapse in the RQRPA is the violation of the Ikeda sum rule.

## Key claim

The renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) avoids the collapse that plagues ordinary QRPA near the physical particle-particle force strength, but the two-neutrino double-beta decay transition amplitude remains almost as sensitive to this parameter as in QRPA; the cost of bypassing the collapse is the violation of the Ikeda sum rule, which the authors argue is unavoidable because the scattering part of the Gamow-Teller operator acting on the RQRPA ground state lies outside the model space.

## Method

The authors formulate the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) by including ground-state correlations (GSC) via quasiparticle occupation numbers \( \mathcal{N}_t \) and renormalization factors \( D_{pn} = 1 - \mathcal{N}_p - \mathcal{N}_n \) in the QRPA equations of motion. They solve the RQRPA equations for five nuclei (\( ^{76}\mathrm{Ge} \), \( ^{82}\mathrm{Se} \), \( ^{100}\mathrm{Mo} \), \( ^{128}\mathrm{Te} \), \( ^{130}\mathrm{Te} \)) using a delta-force two-body interaction and an eleven-dimensional model space, comparing two BCS treatments: Approximation I (standard BCS constraints) and Approximation II (renormalized pairing matrix elements and self-consistent particle-number constraints). They compute the Gamow-Teller strengths, the Ikeda sum rule, and the \( \beta\beta_{2\nu} \) matrix element \( \mathcal{M}_{2\nu}(J^\pi=1^+) \) as functions of the triplet particle-particle coupling strength parameter t.

## Key numbers

* For \( ^{76}\mathrm{Ge} \), the Ikeda sum rule \( \mathsf{S}_R = s_- - s_+ \) deviates from the QRPA value of 1 more strongly when more ground-state correlations are included; the violation is quantified by \( \Delta\tilde{S}(J^\pi=1^+) = (N-Z) - [\tilde{S}_- - \tilde{S}_+] \).
* In Approximation I, the Fermi sum rule is broken by \( \lesssim 1\% \) for s=1 and by \( \gtrsim 10\% \) for s=2.
* The values of \( \mathrm{t}_{\mathrm{sym}} \) (maximal SU(4) restoration) and \( \mathrm{t}_{\mathrm{exp}} \) (reproducing experimental \( \mathcal{M}_{2\nu} \)) differ by about 10% for the nuclei studied.
* The physical value of the triplet coupling strength is \( \mathrm{t} \cong 1.5 \); the singlet coupling strength is \( \mathrm{s} \cong 1.0 \).

## Context

This work extends earlier QRPA studies of double-beta decay by Vogel (1986), Civitarese (1987), Engel (1988), and others, which showed that the \( \beta\beta_{2\nu} \) matrix element is highly sensitive to the particle-particle force and that QRPA collapses near the physical force strength. The RQRPA approach follows the self-consistent RPA formalism of Rowe (1968) and was previously applied to \( \beta\beta_{2\nu} \) decays by Toivanen and Suhonen (1995) for \( ^{100}\mathrm{Mo} \) and by Schwieger, Simkovic, and Faessler (1996) for several nuclei. The present paper systematically compares two BCS treatments and demonstrates that the Ikeda sum rule violation is inherent to RQRPA, while the strong parameter dependence of \( \mathcal{M}_{2\nu} \) persists because it arises from partial restoration of spin-isospin SU(4) symmetry rather than from the QRPA collapse. The results enable future work to incorporate additional degrees of freedom such as quadrupole and octupole charge-conserving vibrations and p-wave Coulomb corrections.
