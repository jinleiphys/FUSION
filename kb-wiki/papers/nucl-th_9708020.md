# Probing the Rho Spectral Function in Hot and Dense Nuclear Matter by Dileptons

> We present a dynamical study of $e^+e^-$ and $μ^+ μ^-$ production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN-SPS energies on the basis of the covariant transport approach HSD employing a momentum-dependent $ρ$-meson spectral function that includes the pion modifications in the nuclear medium as well as the polarization of the $ρ$-meson due to resonant $ρ$$-N$ scattering. We find that the experimental data from the CERES and HELIOS-3 Collaborations can be described equally well as within the dropping $ρ$-mass scenario. Whereas corresponding dilepton $q_T$-spectra are found to be very similar, the inclusive dilepton yield in the invariant mass range $0.85 \leq M \leq 1.0$ GeV should allow to disentangle the two scenarios experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both the "dropping ρ-mass" scenario and a hadronic model with a broadened ρ spectral function (due to medium modifications from pion and resonant ρ-N interactions) can describe the CERES and HELIOS-3 dilepton data equally well, but the inclusive dilepton yield in the invariant mass range 0.85 ≤ M ≤ 1.0 GeV should allow experimental disentanglement of the two scenarios.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical simulations of e⁺e⁻ and μ⁺μ⁻ production in p-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN-SPS energies using the covariant transport approach HSD (Hadron-String Dynamics). They employed a momentum-dependent ρ-meson spectral function that includes pion modifications in the nuclear medium and polarization of the ρ-meson due to resonant ρ-N scattering, with the selfenergy constrained by γp and γA absorption cross sections. Dilepton production was calculated perturbatively including Dalitz decays of η, ω, η′ mesons, direct decays of ρ, ω, φ mesons, and π⁺π⁻, K\bar{K}, and πρ annihilation channels.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Au at 160 A·GeV and b=5 fm, the transport model gives a charged particle multiplicity dn_{ch}/dη ≈ 260 in accordance with experimental normalization.
- The difference in inclusive dilepton yield between the "dropping mass" scenario and the hadronic spectral function approach is about a factor of 5 in the invariant mass range 0.8 ≤ M ≤ 1.0 GeV for Pb+Au at b=5 fm with mass resolution ΔM=10 MeV.
- At normal nuclear matter density, the average propagation distance of the ρ-meson in the nuclear medium is limited to less than 0.5 fm even for high momenta.
- The ρ spectral function is evaluated at temperatures of 150 MeV and baryon densities of 0, ρ₀, 2ρ₀, and 3ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport calculations by Cassing et al. (1995, 1996) and Rapp et al. (1997) that separately explored dropping-mass and hadronic spectral function scenarios for dilepton production. It directly addresses the competition between the "dropping ρ-mass" scenario proposed by Li et al. (1995) and Brown/Rho scaling versus the hadronic broadening approach of Friman and Pirner (1997) and Rapp et al. (1997). The paper enables experimental discrimination between these two theoretical pictures through precise measurement of the dilepton yield above the ω mass in Pb+Au collisions at SPS energies.
