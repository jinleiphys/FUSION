# Onset of a Pseudogap Regime in Ultracold Fermi Gases

> We show, using an ab initio approach based on Quantum Monte Carlo technique, that the pseudogap regime emerges in ultracold Fermi gases close to the unitary point. We locate the onset of this regime at a value of the interaction strength corresponding to (k_{F}a)^{-1}\approx -0.05 (a - scattering length). We determine the evolution of the gap as a function of temperature and interaction strength in the Fermi gas around the unitary limit and show that our results exhibit a remarkable agreement with the recent wave-vector resolved radio frequency spectroscopy data. Our results indicate that a finite temperature structure of the Fermi gas around unitarity is more complicated and involves the presence of the phase with preformed Cooper pairs, which however do not contribute to the long range order.

## Key claim

Using an ab initio Path Integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) approach, the authors show that a pseudogap regime emerges in ultracold Fermi gases close to the unitary point, with the onset located at an interaction strength of \((k_{F}a)^{-1}\approx -0.05\). The abstract's claim of a "remarkable agreement" with experimental data is qualified by the text's note that the experimental trap data are inhomogeneous and likely contain spatially separated and coexisting phases, making it difficult to directly relate the extracted energy distribution curves to the pseudogap phase.

## Method

The authors performed Path Integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) calculations on a \(10^{3}\) lattice with particle numbers between 90 and 110, extracting the one-particle temperature Green's (Matsubara) function at 51 imaginary time points. They used both the maximum entropy method (MEM) and the Singular Value Decomposition method (SVD) to extract the spectral weight function \(A(\bm{p},\omega)\) from the Matsubara function, then fitted the quasiparticle energy formula \(E(\bm{p}) = \sqrt{ \left ( p^2/2m^{*}+U-\mu \right )^2+\Delta^2}\) to the maxima of the spectral weight function to obtain the gap \(\Delta\), self-energy \(U\), and effective mass \(m^{*}\).

## Key numbers

- Onset of pseudogap regime at interaction strength \((k_{F}a)^{-1}\approx -0.05\).
- At unitarity (\((k_{F}a)^{-1}=0.0\)), the crossover temperature \(T^{*}/\varepsilon_{F} = 0.19(2)\) and the critical temperature \(T_{c}/\varepsilon_{F} = 0.15(1)\).
- At \((k_{F}a)^{-1}=0.2\) (BEC side), \(T^{*}/\varepsilon_{F} = 0.26(2)\) and \(T_{c}/\varepsilon_{F} = 0.19(1)\).
- At \((k_{F}a)^{-1}=-0.2\) (BCS side), \(T^{*}/\varepsilon_{F} = 0.14(2)\) and \(T_{c}/\varepsilon_{F} = 0.125(10)\).
- The gap at the critical temperature at unitarity is \(\Delta/\varepsilon_F = 0.19\) (at \(T/\varepsilon_F = 0.165\)).
- The effective mass \(m^{*}/m\) deviates from the bare mass by no more than 10% across all interaction strengths studied.
- Statistical errors were below 1%; systematic errors (finite lattice and finite range effects) were estimated at no more than 10%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier PIMC results reported in Magierski (2009) by using a larger \(10^{3}\) lattice and systematically mapping the pseudogap as a function of temperature and interaction strength. It provides the first ab initio determination of the pseudogap size and evolution, contrasting with earlier theoretical models by Randeria, Levin, Strinati, and others whose predictions varied widely without quantifiable error bars. The results enable direct comparison with wave-vector resolved radio frequency spectroscopy data from Gaebler (2010) and Stewart (2010), and challenge the Fermi liquid theory description of magnetic susceptibility measurements by Salomon (2010) that did not invoke a pseudogap model.
