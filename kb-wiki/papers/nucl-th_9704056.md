# Cross section and analyzing power of pol{p}p -> pn pi+ near threshold

> The cross section and analyzing power of the pol{p}p -> pn pi+ reaction near threshold are estimated in terms of data obtained from the pol{p}p -> d pi+ and pp -> pp pi0 reactions. A simple final state interaction theory is developed which depends weakly upon the form of the pion-production operator and includes some Coulomb corrections. Within the uncertainties of the model and the input data, the approach reproduces well the measured energy dependence of the total cross section and the proton analyzing power at a fixed pion c.m. angle of 90deg, from threshold to T_p = 330 MeV. The variation of the differential cross section with pion angle is also very encouraging.

## Key claim

The cross section and analyzing power of the $\vec{p}p \to pn\pi^+$ reaction near threshold can be estimated semi-quantitatively from data on the $\vec{p}p \to d\pi^+$ and $pp \to pp\pi^0$ reactions using a simple final state interaction model, but the agreement is within the uncertainties of the model and input data rather than being a precise prediction.

## Method

The authors developed a final state interaction (FSI) theory that relates the spin-triplet part of the $pp \to pn\pi^+$ matrix element to the $pp \to d\pi^+$ matrix element using an extrapolation theorem connecting bound-state and scattering wave functions. The spin-singlet contribution was estimated from $pp \to pp\pi^0$ data using isospin invariance, with Coulomb corrections applied to both the pion and the final-state nucleon-nucleon system. The formalism was extended to include pionic $s$- and $p$-waves, and the integration over final $np$ excitation energies was performed analytically for low energies.

## Key numbers

- The $pp \to d\pi^+$ differential cross section at $\theta=0^\circ$ is parametrized as $(13.6\,\eta + 164.0\,\eta^3)\ \mu\text{b/sr}$, and at $\theta=90^\circ$ as $(13.6\,\eta + 16.2\,\eta^3)\ \mu\text{b/sr}$ (Eqs. 4.2, 4.3).
- The $pp \to d\pi^+$ analyzing power at $\theta=90^\circ$ is given by $A_y\,d\sigma/d\Omega^* = -18.1\,\eta^2\ \mu\text{b/sr}$ (Eq. 4.4).
- The spin-singlet contribution to $pp \to pn\pi^+$ is estimated as $D' \approx 0.42\ \mu\text{b/sr}$ with $\zeta'' = 25.4$ (Eq. 4.6).
- The ratio of $s$-wave production of pions with spin-triplet to spin-singlet final states is about 1.7 at low $\eta$ and 7.8 at high $\eta$.
- The spin-triplet function $Z_t$ at $r=1.05$ fm lies 0.5% below the deuteron wave function value, and at $E_{np}=15$ MeV it lies less than 8% above the deuteron point.
- The model reproduces data from threshold to $T_p = 330$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier FSI model (Faldt and Wilkin, 1996) which used $pp \to d\pi^+$ and $pp \to pp\pi^0$ data to estimate $pp \to pn\pi^+$ total cross sections. It now includes Coulomb corrections and predictions for differential cross sections and analyzing power, enabling comparison with new IUCF data (Daehnick et al., 1995; Flammang et al., 1997). The approach is simpler than microscopic calculations (Lee and Riska, 1993; Miller and Sauer, 1991) but suggests that any microscopic model reproducing the two-body input data would give qualitatively similar results near threshold. The work enables future tests through spin-transfer experiments that could probe interference between triplet and singlet final $np$ states.
