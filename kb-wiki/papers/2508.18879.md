# Baselines for Abelian Charge Fluctuations in Nuclear Collisions:Theory and Comparison with Experimental Data

> We investigate fluctuations in the canonical ensemble of an Abelian charge, such as baryon number. Our focus is on cumulants and factorial cumulants of baryon and antibaryon multiplicity distributions, including their sum and difference, in both the full phase space and subsystems. In particular, we establish a correlation between net-baryon number fluctuations within a subsystem, which is pertinent for fluctuation analyses in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and fluctuations of baryon and antibaryon numbers in the total system. We derive analytical expressions for factorial cumulants of arbitrary order and present concise results in terms of the cumulants of the total baryon number. To account for dynamics beyond global conservation, we introduce local attractive and repulsive multi-particle interactions within a phenomenological framework. A comparison of calculated and generated cumulants with STAR and HADES data indicates that multiparticle interactions play a decisive role in the description of observed fluctuation patterns. At high collision energies, the data are well-reproduced by incorporating repulsive two-proton interactions, while at lower energies, attractive three-particle interactions become essential. Furthermore, our framework facilitates realistic event generation, enabling a direct comparison with experimental measurements.

# Baselines for Abelian Charge Fluctuations in Nuclear Collisions: Theory and Comparison with Experimental Data

## Key claim

The non-critical baseline expectations incorporating global baryon number conservation fail to reproduce experimental results for proton factorial cumulants from STAR BES-II and HADES; the observed fluctuation patterns can only be described by including both repulsive two-proton interactions at high collision energies and attractive three-particle interactions at lower energies within a canonical ensemble framework.

## Method

The authors derive analytical expressions for factorial cumulants of arbitrary order for baryon and antibaryon multiplicity distributions in a subsystem, expressed in terms of cumulants of the total-system baryon number, using the canonical ensemble with exact net-baryon number conservation and the Faà di Bruno formula. They extend this framework by introducing local attractive and repulsive multi-particle interactions among baryons within the canonical ensemble, going beyond the global conservation baseline. The model is compared with experimental data from STAR BES-II and HADES on proton number factorial cumulants.

## Key numbers

- The recurrence relation for cumulants in the full system: \( c_{k+1} = \frac{z}{2} \frac{d}{dz} c_k \), where \( z = \sqrt{z_B z_{\bar{B}}} \) is the geometric mean of single-particle partition functions.
- The first few factorial cumulants in a subsystem are given by: \( F^{(2,0)} = (\alpha_B)^2 (c_2 - \langle N_B \rangle) \), \( F^{(1,1)} = \alpha_B \alpha_{\bar{B}} c_2 \), \( F^{(3,0)} = (\alpha_B)^3 (c_3 - 3c_2 + 2\langle N_B \rangle) \), where \( \alpha_B \) and \( \alpha_{\bar{B}} \) are acceptance probabilities and \( c_k \) are cumulants of the total baryon number.
- The net-baryon cumulants in a subsystem are given by: \( \kappa_n = B k_+^{(n)} + \sum_{k=1}^n C_k B_{n,k}(k_-^{(1)}, \dots, k_-^{(n-k+1)}) \), where \( C_k \) are cumulants of \( N_B + N_{\bar{B}} \) in the full system and \( B_{n,k} \) are Bell polynomials.
- The relation between cumulants and factorial cumulants in the full system: \( F_n = \sum_{k=1}^n s(n,k) C_k \) and \( C_n = \sum_{k=1}^n S(n,k) F_k \), where \( s(n,k) \) and \( S(n,k) \) are Stirling numbers of the first and second kind, respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Barej and Bzdak (2020, 2022), who derived analytical results for factorial cumulants up to sixth order in a subsystem with global baryon number conservation, and by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2020, 2023), who investigated canonical effects on net-proton cumulants. The novel contribution is the simultaneous inclusion of both repulsive and attractive multi-particle interactions among baryons within the canonical ensemble, which was not addressed in previous analyses. The framework enables realistic event generation for direct comparison with experimental measurements from STAR BES-II and HADES, and provides baselines for isolating signals of the QCD critical endpoint in ongoing beam energy scan programs.
