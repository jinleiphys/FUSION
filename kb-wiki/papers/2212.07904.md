# Three-Body Unitary Coupled-Channel Analysis on $η(1405/1475)$

> The recent BESIII data on $J/ψ\toγ(K_SK_Sπ^0)$, which is significantly more precise than earlier $η(1405/1475)$-related data, enables quantitative discussions on $η(1405/1475)$ at the previously unreachable level. We conduct a three-body unitary coupled-channel analysis of experimental Monte-Carlo outputs for radiative $J/ψ$ decays via $η(1405/1475)$: $K_SK_Sπ^0$ Dalitz plot distributions from the BESIII, and branching ratios of $γ(ηπ^+π^-)$ and $γ(γπ^+π^-)$ final states relative to that of $γ(K\bar{K}π)$. Our model systematically considers (multi-)loop diagrams and an associated triangle singularity, which is critical in making excellent predictions on $η(1405/1475)\to πππ$ lineshapes and branching ratios. The $η(1405/1475)$ pole locations are revealed for the first time. Two poles for $η(1405)$ are found on different Riemann sheets of the $K^*\bar{K}$ channel, while one pole for $η(1475)$. The $η(1405/1475)$ states are described with two bare states dressed by continuum states. The lower bare state would be an excited $η^\prime$, while the higher one could be an excited $η^{(\prime)}$, hybrid, glueball, or their mixture. This work presents the first-ever pole determination based on a manifestly three-body unitary coupled-channel framework applied to experimental three-body final state distributions (Dalitz plots).

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\eta(1405/1475)$ puzzle is resolved by a three-body unitary coupled-channel model fitted to BESIII Monte-Carlo pseudodata (not the actual experimental data), revealing for the first time that $\eta(1405)$ corresponds to two poles on different Riemann sheets of the $K^*\bar{K}$ channel while $\eta(1475)$ corresponds to a single pole, with the caveat that the analysis uses Monte-Carlo generated pseudodata rather than direct experimental data.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-body unitary coupled-channel model for radiative $J/\psi$ decays via $\eta(1405/1475)$, incorporating dressed propagators for two bare $\eta^*$ states and two-meson resonances ($K^*$, $\kappa$, $a_0(980)$, $a_2(1320)$, $f_0$, $\rho$) with nonperturbative treatment of coupled-channel effects including triangle singularities. The model was fitted to $K_SK_S\pi^0$ Dalitz plot pseudodata generated from the BESIII Monte-Carlo $0^{-+}$ partial wave amplitude across 30 energy bins from 1300 to 1600 MeV, along with branching ratio constraints for $\gamma(\eta\pi^+\pi^-)$ and $\gamma(\gamma\pi^+\pi^-)$ relative to $\gamma(K\bar{K}\pi)$, using 25 fitting parameters and 50 bootstrap samples for statistical uncertainty estimation.

## Key numbers

- Two $\eta(1405)$ poles found: $\alpha=1$ at mass $1401.6 \pm 0.6$ MeV with width $65.8 \pm 1.0$ MeV on the $(up)$ Riemann sheet; $\alpha=2$ at mass $1401.6 \pm 0.6$ MeV with width $66.3 \pm 0.9$ MeV on the $(pp)$ Riemann sheet
- One $\eta(1475)$ pole ($\alpha=3$) at mass $1495.0 \pm 1.5$ MeV with width $86.4 \pm 1.8$ MeV on the $(up)$ Riemann sheet
- Fitted branching ratio $R_1^{\rm th} \sim 7.5$ for $\Gamma[\gamma(K\bar{K}\pi)]/\Gamma[\gamma(\eta\pi^+\pi^-)]$ compared to experimental range 6.8-11.9
- Fitted branching ratio $R_2^{\rm th} \sim 0.025$ for $\Gamma[\gamma(\rho^0\gamma)]/\Gamma[\gamma(K\bar{K}\pi)]$ compared to experimental range 0.015-0.043
- Predicted branching ratio for $\Gamma[\gamma(\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)]/\Gamma[\gamma(K\bar{K}\pi)] = 0.0045-0.0047$ compared to experimental 0.004-0.007
- Predicted branching ratio for $\Gamma[\gamma(\pi^0\pi^0\pi^0)]/\Gamma[\gamma(K\bar{K}\pi)] = 0.0015-0.0016$ compared to experimental 0.002-0.003
- $\chi^2/{\rm ndf} = 1.40-1.54$ from comparing with $\{N_{l,m}\}$ Dalitz plot bins (4496-4575 bins depending on sample)

## Context

This work extends the three-body unitary coupled-channel formalism previously applied to $a_1(1260)$ pole extraction from $\tau^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\nu_\tau$ by Guo et al. (2020) and JPAC (2019), now incorporating coupled-channels and fitting Dalitz plot distributions for the first time. The analysis addresses the long-standing $\eta(1405/1475)$ puzzle by confirming the triangle singularity mechanism proposed by Wu et al. (2012, 2013) and Aceti et al. (2012) for the large isospin-violation in $\eta(1405/1475)\to\pi\pi\pi$, and enables future direct analyses of radiative $J/\psi$ decay data with multiple $J^{PC}$ components to consistently determine pole structures of $\eta(1405/1475)$, $f_1(1420)$, and related states.
