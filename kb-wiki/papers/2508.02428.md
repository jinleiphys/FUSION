# Coulomb-Corrected Wormhole Model for Neon-20

> Building on the spatial wormhole geometry proposed by Manton and Dunajski, we develop a modified model for the Neon-20 nucleus that incorporates a repulsive Coulomb potential. This reduces the large threshold energy for cluster break-up in the original model and converts most bound states to resonances. We use generalized WKB methods to calculate the energies of bound states, and also the energies and widths of under-barrier and over-barrier resonances. The results align closely with experimental data for the 0_1^+ , 0_1^- and 0_4^+ rotational bands of Neon-20, including the large widths in the higher-nodal 0_4^+ band.

## Key claim

Adding a repulsive Coulomb potential to the wormhole geometry model for Neon-20 reduces the cluster break-up threshold from over 15 MeV to the experimental value of 4.73 MeV, converting most previously bound states into finite-width resonances whose energies and widths can be calculated using generalized WKB methods.

## Method

The authors retain the Ellis-Bronnikov wormhole metric and short-range attractive potential from Manton and Dunajski (2023) but add a repulsive Coulomb term of the form \(Q_0/(r^{\kappa}+b^{\kappa})^{1/\kappa}\) with \(\kappa=8\). They use standard WKB for bound states, Shepard's improved WKB for under-barrier resonances, and an analytically continued WKB method for over-barrier resonances, fitting parameters to reproduce the experimental threshold energy and ground-state band energies.

## Key numbers

- Experimental break-up threshold for Neon-20: 4.73 MeV (reduced from 15.19 MeV in the original model)
- Wormhole radius \(a = 5.37\) fm; energy calibration factor \(\hbar^2/2\mu a^2 = 0.2265\) MeV
- Dimensionless parameters: \(m = 8\) (reduced from \(m=9\)), \(\sigma = 0.6742\), \(\beta = 0.7119\), \(C_0 = 26.62\)
- Ground state \(0^+\) band energies: 0 MeV (0;0), 1.31 MeV (0;2), 4.38 MeV (0;4) compared to experimental 0, 1.63, 4.25 MeV
- \(1^-\) state at 5.73 MeV with width 0.0015 keV (experimental: 5.79 MeV, 0.028 keV)
- \(3^-\) state at 7.63 MeV with width 16 keV (experimental: 7.16 MeV, 8.2 keV)
- \(0^+\) (2-phonon) state at 7.82 MeV with width 850 keV (experimental: \(\approx 8.7\) MeV, \(\approx 800\) keV)
- \(6^+\) state at 9.12 MeV with width 0.19 keV (experimental: 8.78 MeV, 0.11 keV)
- \(8^+\) state at 15.58 MeV with width 38 keV (experimental: 15.87 MeV, 100 keV)
- \(9^-\) state at 22.54 MeV with width 2200 keV (experimental: 22.80 MeV, 500 keV)

## Context

This work extends the wormhole geometry model of Manton and Dunajski (2023) for Neon-20 as a five-alpha-particle bipyramid, which previously lacked Coulomb repulsion and could not describe resonance widths. The model connects to the rovibrational classification of Bijker and Iachello (2014) and the Ikeda diagram picture of cluster breakup, and enables quantitative prediction of both energies and decay widths for the \(0^+_1\), \(0^-_1\), and \(0^+_4\) rotational bands, including the broad higher-nodal states previously identified by Fujiwara et al.
