# The effect of Confined One Gluon Exchange Potential and Instanton Induced Interaction in Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> The effect of Confined One Gluon Exchange Potential and Instanton Induced Interaction potential in the singlet ($^1S_0$) and triplet ($^3S_1$) channels for Nucleon-Nucleon interaction has been investigated in the framework of Relativistic Harmonic Model using Resonating Group Method in the adiabatic limit with Born - Oppenheimer approximation. The contributions of the different components of the interaction potentials have been analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Relativistic Harmonic Model incorporating both Confined One Gluon Exchange Potential (COGEP) and Instanton Induced Interaction (III) can reproduce the short-range repulsion and intermediate-range attraction of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, but this result is conditional on the specific parameter set (ten parameters fitted to hadron spectroscopy) and the adiabatic Born-Oppenheimer approximation within the Resonating Group Method framework.

## Method

The authors model the six-quark system using the Relativistic Harmonic Model (RHM), where quarks are Dirac fermions confined by a Lorentz scalar plus vector harmonic oscillator potential. The nucleon-nucleon interaction potential is computed using the Resonating Group Method (RGM) in the adiabatic limit with the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, including both the Confined One Gluon Exchange Potential (COGEP) and the Instanton Induced Interaction (III) as the quark-quark interaction terms. The total wavefunction is antisymmetrized using the operator \(A = \frac{1}{10}(1 - 9P_{36}^{OSTC})\), and the interaction potential is separated into direct and exchange contributions.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass parameter \(M = 160.6\) MeV and energy eigenvalue \(E = 40.0\) MeV, fitted from hadron spectroscopy.
- Confinement strength \(\alpha^2 = 200.01\) MeV and harmonic oscillator size parameter \(b = 0.6\) fm, consistent with experimental results.
- Quark-gluon coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 6.5\); III strength parameter \(W = 67.67\) MeV fm\(^3\).
- COGEP provides a repulsion of about 150 MeV in the short range; without COGEP, the short-range repulsion is reduced to about 50 MeV.
- COGEP provides an attraction of about 20 MeV in the intermediate range.
- Without III, the short-range repulsion increases by about 50 MeV and the intermediate-range attraction is reduced by about 20 MeV; the attractive minimum shifts from about 1 fm (with III) to about 1.3 fm (without III).

## Context

This work extends the line of quark-model studies of nucleon-nucleon interaction by Vijaya Kumar et al. (1993) and Khadkikar et al. (1991), which incorporated gluon confinement via the Current Confinement Model, and by Oka et al. (1989), who included Instanton Induced Interaction alongside one-gluon exchange. The present study combines both COGEP and III within the Relativistic Harmonic Model, showing that gluon confinement enhances short-range repulsion compared to conventional one-gluon exchange, and that III is essential for producing intermediate-range attraction. This enables further studies of NN scattering phase shifts and the extension to other baryon-baryon systems using the same framework.
