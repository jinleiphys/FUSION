# Three-quark clusters at finite temperatures and densities

> We present a relativistic three-body equation to study correlations in a medium of finite temperatures and densities. This equation is derived within a systematic Dyson equation approach and includes the dominant medium effects due to Pauli blocking and self energy corrections. Relativity is implemented utilizing the light front form. The equation is solved for a zero-range force for parameters close to the confinement-deconfinement transition of QCD. We present correlations between two- and three-particle binding energies and calculate the three-body Mott transition.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first derivation and solution of a relativistic in-medium three-body equation for quarks at both finite temperatures and finite densities, but the results are obtained under several simplifying approximations: spin degrees of freedom are averaged over (leading to an effective one-channel bose-type equation), a zero-range force is used, and antiparticle degrees of freedom are neglected.

## Method

The authors derive a relativistic three-body equation within a Dyson equation approach using light-front dynamics, including medium effects from Pauli blocking and self-energy corrections. The equation is solved for a zero-range interaction using quark masses taken from a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Klevansky, 1992; Schmidt, 1994). The formalism yields an in-medium Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) type equation that is solved to find two- and three-quark binding energies and the three-body Mott transition.

## Key numbers

* Quark masses used in the calculation range from 300 MeV (for isolated case and at T=10 MeV, mu=100 MeV) down to 236 MeV (at T=100 MeV, mu=200 MeV), as taken from a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Schmidt, 1994).
* At T=10 MeV, the three-body bound state disappears for chemical potential mu approximately 310 MeV, even when a two-quark bound state exists.
* For weak coupling (B2/m=10^-3) at mu=100 MeV, three-body binding energies are 23 MeV at T=10 MeV, 19 MeV at T=50 MeV, and 5.0 MeV at T=100 MeV.
* The Mott transition (where B3=0) occurs at lower chemical potential for higher temperatures; for M2/m=1, the Mott line is shown in the phase diagram (Fig. 6) with values of T and mu below which three-body bound states can form.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonrelativistic in-medium few-body studies (Beyer et al., 1996-2000) to the relativistic quark phase of QCD, and contrasts with the approach of Pepin et al. (2000) who used a different blocking factor (1-n1)(1-n2)(1-n3) instead of the 1-n1-n2 form derived here. The results enable future investigations of three-quark correlations near the confinement-deconfinement transition and their possible role in color-superconductivity, though the authors note that a full treatment of spin and more realistic interactions are needed for quantitative conclusions.
