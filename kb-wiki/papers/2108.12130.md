# Super- and hyper-deformation in $^{60}$Zn, $^{62}$Zn, and $^{64}$Ge at high spins

> Background: The observation of the superdeformed (SD) bands in $^{60,62}$Zn indicates that the particle number 30 is a magic particle number, where two and four neutron single-particles are considered to be promoted to the intruder $1g_{9/2}$ shell. However, the SD-yrast band in $^{62}$Zn is assigned negative parity. Purpose: I investigate various SD configurations in the rapidly rotating $^{60,62}$Zn and $^{64}$Ge, and attempt elucidating the different roles of the energy gaps at particle numbers 30 and 32. Method: I employ a nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) method: the configuration-constrained cranked Skyrme-Kohn-Sham approach is used to describe the rotational bands near the yrast line. Results: The negative-parity SD bands appear higher in energy than the positive-parity SD-yrast band in $^{60}$Zn by about 4 MeV, which is indicative of the SD doubly-magic nucleus. However, the energy gap in $^{64}$Ge is smaller $\sim 2\text{-}3$ MeV, though the quadrupole deformation of the SD states in $^{64}$Ge is greater than that of $^{60}$Zn. The present calculation predicts the occurrence of the hyperdeformed state in $^{60}$Zn and $^{64}$Ge at a high rotational frequency $\sim 2.0$ MeV$/\hbar$ due to the occupation of the $h_{11/2}$ shell. Conclusions: An SD-shell gap at particle number 30 and 32 appears at different deformations and the energy gap at particle number 32 is low, which make the SD structures of $^{62}$Zn unique, where the negative-parity SD states appear lower in energy than the positive-parity one.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the superdeformed (SD) shell gap at particle numbers 30 and 32 appears at different deformations, and the energy gap at particle number 32 is low (approximately 2-3 MeV), which makes the SD structures of $^{62}$Zn unique where negative-parity SD states appear lower in energy than positive-parity ones, contrary to the doubly-magic behavior seen in $^{60}$Zn.

## Method

The author employed a nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) method using the configuration-constrained cranked Skyrme-Kohn-Sham approach to describe rotational bands near the yrast line in $^{60}$Zn, $^{62}$Zn, and $^{64}$Ge. The cranked Skyrme-Kohn-Sham equation was solved by diagonalizing the single-particle Routhian in a three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation with a mesh size of 1.0 fm and 12 points for each direction, imposing reflection symmetry about the (x,y), (y,z), and (z,x) planes. Various configurations were specified by occupation numbers of single-particle orbitals classified by parity and signature quantum numbers, with the SkM* functional as the primary Skyrme-type EDF.

## Key numbers

- The negative-parity SD bands appear higher in energy than the positive-parity SD-yrast band in $^{60}$Zn by about 4 MeV, indicative of SD doubly-magic behavior
- The energy gap in $^{64}$Ge is smaller, approximately 2-3 MeV, though the quadrupole deformation of SD states in $^{64}$Ge is greater than that of $^{60}$Zn
- The calculation predicts hyperdeformed states in $^{60}$Zn and $^{64}$Ge at a high rotational frequency of approximately 2.0 MeV/$\hbar$ due to occupation of the $h_{11/2}$ shell
- The deformation parameter $\beta$ of the SD band in $^{60}$Zn is 0.53 at $\omega_{\rm rot}=0$ MeV, decreasing to 0.43 at $\omega_{\rm rot}=1.4$ MeV (I ~ 27)
- The observed transition quadrupole moment in $^{60}$Zn for spin range I=12-27 is $2.75 \pm 0.45$ $e$b, corresponding to $\beta=0.47 \pm 0.07$
- In $^{62}$Zn, the deformation parameter $\beta$ around I ~ 20 is 0.40 for the well-deformed state, 0.47 for negative-parity SD, and 0.53 for positive-parity SD, corresponding to two, three, and four neutrons in the $g_{9/2}$ shell respectively
- The SD configuration $[9977]_\pi [9977]_\nu$ in $^{64}$Ge gives a large deformation of $\beta = 0.62$ at $\omega_{\rm rot}=0$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of superdeformed bands in the A~60 mass region, following experimental observations of SD bands in $^{60,62}$Zn by Svensson et al. (1999, 1997) and additional SD bands in $^{62}$Zn reported by Gellanki et al. (2009, 2012). The calculations build on earlier theoretical work using the configuration-dependent cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) method and cranked relativistic mean-field (CRMF) methods, particularly addressing the puzzle that the SD-yrast band in $^{62}$Zn has negative parity while the naturally-expected positive-parity SD band appears at higher energy as SD3. The results enable future experimental searches for hyperdeformed states in $^{60}$Zn and $^{64}$Ge at high rotational frequencies, and provide a framework for understanding how deformed shell gaps evolve with particle number and spin in this mass region.
