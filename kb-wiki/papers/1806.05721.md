# Fusion cross-section for Ni-based reactions within the relativistic mean field formalism

> In this theoretical study, we establish an interrelationship between the nucleon-nucleon interaction potential and the nuclear fusion reaction cross-sections at low energies. The axially deformed self-consistent relativistic mean field with non-linear NL3$^*$ force is used to calculate the density distribution of the projectile and target nuclei for fusion. The Wong formula is used to estimate the fusion cross-section and barrier distribution from the nucleus-nucleus optical potential for Ni-based systems, which are known for fusion hindrance phenomena. The results of the application of the so obtained nucleus-nucleus optical potential for the fusion cross-section from the recently developed relativistic $NN-$interaction (R3Y) are compared with the well-known, phenomenological M3Y effective $NN$ potential. We found a relatively good results from R3Y interactions below the barrier energies as compare to the M3Y potential concerning the experimental data. We also observe the density dependence on the nuclear interaction potential in terms of nucleon-nucleon optical potentials.

## Key claim

The recently developed relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction potential, derived from the NL3* relativistic mean field force, yields fusion cross-sections that are in relatively better agreement with experimental data below the Coulomb barrier for Ni-based reactions compared to the phenomenological M3Y potential, though the M3Y potential fits the data only at above-barrier energies.

## Method

The authors used the axially deformed self-consistent relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the non-linear NL3* force to calculate the density distributions of projectile and target nuclei (58,64Ni and 124,132Sn). The nuclear interaction potential between each pair of nuclei was obtained via the double folding procedure using both the relativistic R3Y and the phenomenological M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential for single-nucleon exchange effects. The total nucleus-nucleus optical potential (nuclear plus Coulomb) was then used in the Wong formula, which approximates the fusion cross-section from the barrier height, position, and curvature for the ℓ=0 partial wave.

## Key numbers

- For the 58Ni + 58Ni system, the R3Y+EX nuclear potential is more attractive by about 1 MeV compared to the M3Y+EX potential, with a barrier height lower by a few keV.
- The R3Y interaction produces fusion cross-sections that are always larger than those from the M3Y potential for all six Ni-based systems studied.
- The six reaction systems considered are: 64Ni + 64Ni, 64Ni + 124Sn, 64Ni + 132Sn, 58Ni + 58Ni, 58Ni + 124Sn, and 58Ni + 132Sn.
- The M3Y effective interaction is given by the expression 7999*exp(-4r)/(4r) - 2134*exp(-2.5r)/(2.5r), with ranges in fm and strengths in MeV.

## Context

This work extends the use of the relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction, previously applied to cluster radioactivity studies by Bhuyan et al. (2012, 2014), to the domain of heavy-ion fusion reactions. It directly compares the R3Y potential against the well-established phenomenological M3Y interaction of Satchler and Love (1979) for Ni-based systems known to exhibit fusion hindrance, as studied experimentally by Jiang et al. (2004), Lesko et al. (1985, 1986), and others. The results suggest that a fully microscopic approach combining RMF densities with the R3Y interaction can serve as a viable alternative to phenomenological potentials for describing sub-barrier fusion, and may enable more consistent studies of fusion dynamics across different mass regions.
