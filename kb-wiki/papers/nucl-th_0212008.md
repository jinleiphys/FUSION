# Does it make any sense to talk about a Delta-isobar?

> It is shortly investigated, on what basis an experimentally observed resonance like the Delta(1232)-isobar can be embedded into the framework of Quantum Theory (QT), i.e. Quantum Field Theory (QFT) and Quantum Mechanics (QM). After a short discussion of the particle concept in the context of the "bootstrap" idea of G. Chew and S. Mandelstam we will focus on the theoretical formalism being necessary to describe resonances in the Lagrangian or Hamiltonian formulation of QT.

## Key claim

The paper argues that it does make sense to talk about a Delta-isobar, provided one replaces standard Hermitian quantum theory with an (anti)causal formulation in which the underlying Lagrangians are non-Hermitian, allowing unstable particles like the Delta(1232) to be treated as fields with complex mass.

## Method

The author develops a consistent non-Hermitian Lagrangian and Hamiltonian framework for resonances, extending Nakanishi's "Complex-Ghost Relativistic Field Theory" for spin-0 fields to spin-1/2 and spin-1 fields. The formalism uses Laplace transforms instead of Fourier transforms to solve the field equations for complex-mass particles, introduces biorthogonal Fock spaces, and defines Lorentz boosts for unstable particles with complex four-momentum. Canonical equal-time commutation (or anticommutation) relations are imposed even for resonance fields, and the resulting propagators are obtained by standard real-time ordering.

## Key numbers

* The Delta(1232)-isobar is the specific resonance under consideration, with the paper focusing on its theoretical description as a spin-3/2, isospin-3/2 particle.
* The complex mass for a resonance is defined as \(M = m - i(\Gamma/2)\), where \(\Gamma\) is the decay width.
* The causal Nakanishi propagator for a spin-0 resonance is given by \(i \int \frac{d^4p}{(2\pi)^4} \frac{e^{-ip(x-y)}}{p^2 - M^2} \delta_{rs}\).
* The causal spin-1/2 fermion propagator is given by \(i \int \frac{d^4p}{(2\pi)^4} \frac{e^{-ip(x-y)}}{p^2 - M^2} (\not{p} + M)_{\alpha\beta} \delta_{rs}\).

## Context

This work extends the "bootstrap" idea of Chew (1961) and Mandelstam, where particles are associated with poles of the scattering matrix, by providing a concrete Lagrangian framework for unstable particles. It builds on Nakanishi's complex-ghost relativistic field theory and the author's own prior work (Kleefeld 2002, 1999, 1998) on non-Hermitian quantum theory. The formalism enables the consistent treatment of resonances like the Delta(1232) in quantum field theory without requiring perturbative loop expansions, potentially allowing such systems to be described at tree-level.
