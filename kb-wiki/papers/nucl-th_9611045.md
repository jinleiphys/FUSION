# Full-Folding Optical Potentials for Elastic Nucleon-Nucleus Scattering based on Realistic Densities

> Optical model potentials for elastic nucleon nucleus scattering are calculated for a number of target nuclides from a full-folding integral of two different realistic target density matrices together with full off-shell nucleon-nucleon t-matrices derived from two different Bonn meson exchange models. Elastic proton and neutron scattering observables calculated from these full-folding optical potentials are compared to those obtained from `optimum factorized' approximations in the energy regime between 65 and 400 MeV projectile energy. The optimum factorized form is found to provide a good approximation to elastic scattering observables obtained from the full-folding optical potentials, although the potentials differ somewhat in the structure of their nonlocality.

## Key claim

The optimum factorized (off-shell tρ) approximation provides a good description of elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering observables compared to full-folding calculations, but this conclusion is qualified by the energy regime between 65 and 400 MeV and the fact that the potentials differ somewhat in the structure of their nonlocality.

## Method

The authors calculated full-folding optical potentials by convolving fully off-shell nucleon-nucleon t-matrices (derived from two Bonn meson exchange models) with realistic target density matrices from Dirac-Hartree and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (Gogny D1S) structure calculations, using a three-dimensional Monte Carlo integration with quasi-random numbers. They then compared elastic proton and neutron scattering observables from these full-folding potentials to those obtained from the optimum factorized approximation, where the NN amplitude is expanded about a fixed momentum P₀ = 0 and the density matrix is integrated to produce the diagonal density profile.

## Key numbers

- Energy regime studied: 65 to 400 MeV projectile energy
- For ¹⁶O proton scattering at 500 MeV, the Møller factor evaluated at Θ = 90° was found to be a very good representation of the exact expression
- The harmonic oscillator basis for ¹⁶O used 12 shells; for ⁹⁰Zr it used 16 shells
- Density matrix normalization: ρ_α(q=0) equals Z (proton number) or N (neutron number)

## Context

This work extends earlier full-folding calculations by the KMT approach (Refs. FFC, FFO) and the g-matrix approach (Refs. hugo1, hugo2) by using realistic density matrices from Dirac-Hartree and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov structure models rather than simple harmonic oscillator densities. The study confirms the conclusion of Ref. FFO that the off-shell structure of the nuclear density matrix plays an insignificant role for elastic scattering observables at these energies, while extending this comparison to heavier nuclei and to energies below 100 MeV where the optimum factorized form might be expected to be less accurate. The work enables future studies of inelastic scattering, reactions with exotic nuclei far from stability, and tests of predicted density distributions for halo-like nuclei.
