# Far-from-equilibrium kinetic dynamics of $λϕ^4$ theory in an expanding universe

> We investigate the far-from-equilibrium behavior of the Boltzmann equation for a gas of massless scalar field particles with quartic (tree level) self-interactions ($λϕ^4$) in Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker spacetime. Using a new covariant generating function for the moments of the Boltzmann distribution function, we analytically determine a subset of the spectrum and the corresponding eigenfunctions of the linearized Boltzmann collision operator. We show how the covariant generating function can be also used to find the exact equations for the moments in the full nonlinear regime. Different than the case of a ultrarelativistic gas of hard spheres (where the total cross section is constant), for $λϕ^4$ the fact that the cross section decreases with energy implies that moments of arbitrarily high order directly couple to low order moments. Numerical solutions for the scalar field case are presented and compared to those found for a gas of hard spheres.

## Key claim

The authors analytically determine a subset of the spectrum and eigenfunctions of the linearized Boltzmann collision operator for massless $\lambda\phi^4$ theory in an expanding FLRW universe using a new covariant generating function method, and show that unlike the constant-cross-section hard-sphere case, the energy-dependent cross section in $\lambda\phi^4$ theory causes moments of arbitrarily high order to directly couple to low-order moments, preventing a fully analytical solution of the nonlinear moment equations.

## Method

The authors rewrite the relativistic Boltzmann equation for massless scalar particles with quartic self-interactions in a conformally flat FLRW spacetime, exploiting Weyl invariance to work in Minkowski spacetime with rescaled momenta and time. They introduce a covariant generating function for the moments of the Boltzmann distribution function to analytically determine the scalar eigenfunctions (associated Laguerre polynomials $L_n^{(1)}(E_k)$) and corresponding eigenvalues of the linearized collision operator. The same generating function technique is then extended to derive exact, infinite coupled nonlinear differential equations for the moments $c_n$ defined via $L_n^{(2)}(E_k)$, which are solved numerically and compared to the hard-sphere case.

## Key numbers

* The linearized collision operator eigenvalues for $\lambda\phi^4$ theory are $\chi_n = -\frac{\alpha\lambda}{4\pi^2} \left(\frac{n-1}{n+1}+\delta_{n0}\right)$, where $\alpha$ is the fugacity and $\lambda$ the coupling constant.
* As $n \rightarrow \infty$, the eigenvalues approach $-\frac{\alpha\lambda}{4\pi^2}$, giving a mode lifetime of $\frac{4\pi^2}{\alpha\lambda}$ for higher-order modes.
* The equilibrium distribution function is $f_k^{eq} = \alpha e^{-E_k}$, with $\int_k f_k^{eq} = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi^2}$.
* The energy density scales as $\varepsilon(\tau) = \varepsilon_0/a^4(\tau)$ and particle density as $n(\tau) = n_0/a^3(\tau)$, with temperature $T(\tau) = T_0/a(\tau)$.

## Context

This work extends the analytical moment method previously developed for constant-cross-section relativistic gases by Bazow, Denicol, and Noronha (2015, 2016), which yielded the only known analytical solution of the full nonlinear Boltzmann equation for a relativistically expanding gas. The authors go beyond those results by treating the physically distinct case of $\lambda\phi^4$ theory, where the cross section decreases with energy as $\sigma(s) \sim \lambda/s$, leading to coupling between all moment orders that prevents a fully analytical solution. This enables future comparisons between exact numerical solutions of the $\lambda\phi^4$ Boltzmann equation and approximate methods such as the relaxation time approximation (Anderson and Witting, 1974; Rocha, 2021) and linearized collision term approaches.
