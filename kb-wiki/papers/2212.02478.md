# Jet quenching for heavy flavors in $AA$ and $pp$ collisions

> We perform a global analysis of experimental data on jet quenching for heavy flavors for scenarios with and without quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions. We find that the theoretical predictions for the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for heavy flavors at the LHC energies are very similar for these scenarios, and the results for $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ agree reasonably with the LHC data. The agreement with data at the RHIC top energy becomes somewhat better for the intermediate scenario, in which the quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions occurs only at the LHC energies. Our fits to heavy flavor $R_{AA}$ show that description of jet quenching for heavy flavors requires somewhat bigger $α_s$ than data on jet quenching for light hadrons.

## Key claim

The theoretical predictions for the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for heavy flavors at LHC energies are very similar for scenarios with and without quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions, and both scenarios agree reasonably with LHC data; however, the agreement with RHIC top energy data becomes somewhat better for an intermediate scenario where quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions occurs only at LHC energies.

## Method

The author performed a global analysis of experimental data on jet quenching for heavy flavors ($D$ mesons, $B$ mesons, and heavy flavor electrons) within the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to induced gluon emission, using a temperature-dependent $\alpha_s(Q,T)$ with a plateau around $Q_{fr}=\kappa T$ motivated by lattice results. The medium-modified fragmentation functions were calculated beyond the soft gluon approximation, treating Coulomb effects accurately, and the collisional energy loss was included as a perturbation to the radiative mechanism. The analysis compared scenarios with and without mini quark-gluon plasma (mQGP) formation in $pp$ collisions, using Bjorken 1+1D expansion for the QGP fireball and fitting the free parameter $\kappa$ to LHC data on $R_{AA}$ for heavy flavors.

## Key numbers

- Optimal $\kappa$ values from fitting LHC heavy flavor data: $\kappa\approx 2$ for scenario without mQGP in $pp$ collisions ($\kappa_H$), and $\kappa\approx 1.4$ for scenario with mQGP in $pp$ collisions ($\kappa_H^{mQGP}$)
- Optimal $\kappa$ values from fitting LHC light hadron data: $\kappa\approx 3.4$ without mQGP ($\kappa_L$), and $\kappa\approx 2.5$ with mQGP ($\kappa_L^{mQGP}$)
- For heavy flavor data using $\kappa_L$ and $\kappa_L^{mQGP}$: $\chi^2/d.p.\approx 1.95$ and $1.45$ respectively
- For heavy flavor data using $\kappa_H$ and $\kappa_H^{mQGP}$: $\chi^2/d.p.\approx 0.69$ and $0.71$ respectively
- mQGP fireball radii for $pp$ collisions: $R_f\approx[1.26, 1.44, 1.49]$ fm for $\sqrt{s}=0.2, 2.76, 5.02$ TeV respectively
- mQGP initial temperatures (ideal gas): $T_0\approx[195, 217, 226]$ MeV for $\sqrt{s}=0.2, 2.76, 5.02$ TeV respectively
- Initial QGP temperatures for central $AA$ collisions: $T_0\approx 320$ MeV for Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV, and $T_0\approx 400(420)$ MeV for Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s}=2.76(5.02)$ TeV
- At $p_T\sim 10-20$ GeV, $|R_{pp}-1|$ for $\kappa_H^{mQGP}$ is larger than for $\kappa_L^{mQGP}$ by $\sim 20-25\%$

## Context

This work extends the author's previous global analysis of jet quenching for light hadrons (Zakharov, 2022) to heavy flavors, examining whether the scenario with mQGP formation in $pp$ collisions is consistent with heavy flavor data. The analysis addresses the "heavy quark puzzle" initially raised by experiments at RHIC (PHENIX, STAR) that showed strong suppression of single electrons from heavy meson decays, contradicting expectations from the dead cone model (Dokshitzer and Kharzeev, 2001). The results enable further investigation of the inconsistency between optimal $\kappa$ values for heavy flavors versus light hadrons, suggesting that approximations in the independent gluon emission treatment may need refinement, particularly for gluon contributions at LHC energies.
