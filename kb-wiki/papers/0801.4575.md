# Analyzing power in elastic scattering of the electrons off a spin-0 target

> We consider the analyzing power on a spin-0 nuclear target. This observable is related to the imaginary part of the two-photon-exchange (box) diagram. We consider the contributions of elastic and inelastic intermediate states. The former requires knowledge of the elastic nuclear form factor, while the latter uses the optical theorem as input. The elastic contribution scales as the nuclear charge $Z$, while the inelastic contribution as the ratio of the atomic number and nuclear charge, $A/Z$. We provide estimates for $^4$He and $^{208}$Pb, in the kinematics of existing or upcoming experiments. In both cases, we predict negative values of a few parts per million, and the dominant contribution is due to inelastic contributions. The analyzing power can contribute a substantial systematic error in parity-violating experiments.

# Analyzing power in elastic scattering of the electrons off a spin-0 target

## Key claim
The analyzing power (beam normal spin asymmetry) for elastic electron scattering off spin-0 nuclear targets is predicted to be negative, on the order of a few parts per million, with the dominant contribution coming from inelastic intermediate states rather than elastic ones. The authors caution that for heavy nuclei like lead, the leading-order-in-α calculation is insufficient and higher-order Coulomb distortion effects must be included.

## Method
The authors calculate the imaginary part of the two-photon-exchange (box) amplitude, which is required for a non-zero analyzing power. The elastic intermediate state contribution is computed using experimental fits for nuclear form factors and integrating over the intermediate electron's solid angle. The inelastic intermediate state contribution is estimated using the optical theorem to relate the forward Compton amplitude to the total photoabsorption cross section, with a phenomenological model for the t-dependence of the Compton amplitude based on an exponential slope parameter B ≈ 8 GeV⁻² taken from proton data.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at 850 MeV beam energy and 6° scattering angle, the total analyzing power is approximately -4 ppm
- For ⁴He at 3 GeV beam energy and 10° scattering angle, the analyzing power is approximately -10 ppm
- For ⁴He at 3 GeV and 1.0° scattering angle, the analyzing power is -0.72 ppm
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at 855 MeV and 1.0° scattering angle, the analyzing power is -0.33 ppm
- For ⁴He at 3 GeV and 20.0° scattering angle, the analyzing power is -10.73 ppm
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at 855 MeV and 20.0° scattering angle, the analyzing power is -14.97 ppm
- The inelastic contribution scales as A/Z (atomic number divided by nuclear charge), while the elastic contribution scales as Z
- The leading-order estimate for the inelastic contribution to lead gives A_n⁰ ≈ -4 ppm

## Context
This work extends theoretical treatments of two-photon-exchange effects from the proton target (where discrepancies between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of G_E/G_M motivated similar studies) to spin-0 nuclear targets used in parity-violating experiments at JLab. The authors apply their method to the HAPPEX experiment (³ GeV beam on ⁴He) and the PREX experiment (850 MeV beam on ²⁰⁸Pb). The elastic contribution is compared to the full Coulomb distortion calculation of Ref. [chuck], showing good agreement for helium but poor agreement for lead where Zα ≈ 0.6. The inelastic calculation follows the approach of Afanasev et al. and Javv for the proton target. The results indicate that the analyzing power can contribute a substantial systematic error to parity-violating asymmetry measurements, which are typically of order 1 ppm.
