# Dilepton emission in heavy ion collisions and chemical equilibrium of QCD matter

> We study thermal dilepton production and anisotropic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02 \, \mathrm{TeV}$ using next-to-leading-order (NLO) thermal QCD dilepton emission rates. A hybrid model (IP-Glasma+\kompost+MUSIC+UrQMD) simulates the collision evolution. The role of the pre-equilibrium stage in dilepton observables is examined. We also explore how chemical equilibrium in QCD matter affect dilepton observables.

## Key claim

Pre-equilibrium dileptons dominate the intermediate invariant mass region (IMR) in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, exceeding both thermal and Drell-Yan contributions, but this dominance depends on the degree of chemical equilibrium in the pre-equilibrium stage, which is modeled via an effective suppression factor.

## Method

The authors simulate Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV using a hybrid model combining IP-Glasma initial conditions, the KoMPoST framework for pre-equilibrium evolution, MUSIC for hydrodynamic evolution with shear viscosity eta/s = 0.12 and temperature-dependent bulk viscosity, and UrQMD for hadronic rescattering. Thermal dilepton production is calculated using next-to-leading-order (NLO) thermal QCD emission rates, with pre-equilibrium dileptons modeled by introducing an effective suppression factor SF(T,tau) to account for the gradual buildup of quark populations in the initially gluon-dominated system.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 0.12
- Freeze-out energy density: epsilon_frz = 0.18 GeV/fm^3
- Dilepton elliptic flow v2 exhibits a single-peak structure around M approximately 1 GeV
- In the IMR (2.0 < M < 3.0 GeV), pre-equilibrium dilepton yield consistently exceeds Drell-Yan contribution when pT > 1 GeV
- Drell-Yan scale uncertainties evaluated by varying factorization and renormalization scales from 0.5 pT to 2.0 pT

## Context

This work extends the multi-stage hydrodynamic framework of Schenke (2010), Paquet (2015), and Kurkela (2018) by incorporating NLO thermal QCD dilepton emission rates from Churchill (2023) and explicitly modeling chemical equilibration during the pre-equilibrium stage. The results build on earlier studies by Gale (2021) and Wu (2024) that examined electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions. The findings enable future experimental comparisons to quantitatively constrain the degree of chemical equilibrium in the pre-equilibrium stage of QCD matter, particularly through dilepton elliptic flow measurements.
