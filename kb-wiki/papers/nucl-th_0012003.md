# Fluctuations of the transverse energy in Pb+Pb collisions and J/psi suppression

> The observed J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions shows a drop at those large values of the transverse energy E_T which arise from fluctuations. The validity of existing models for J/psi suppression can be extended into this domain of E_T by introducing an ad hoc factor proportional to E_T. We propose a formalism in which the influence of E_T fluctuations on the J/psi suppression can be calculated and discuss the conditions under which the ad hoc factor is obtained.

## Key claim

The ad hoc factor \(\epsilon(E_T) = E_T / \bar E_T(b)\) used in existing models to extend J/psi suppression calculations into the regime of large transverse energy fluctuations is derived from first principles, but its validity depends on the product of two parameters: the ratio of variance to mean for the multiplicity distribution of comoving particles and the cross-talk correlation between rapidity intervals; the factor holds to a good approximation only when these parameters combine to give \(\alpha \approx 0.8\) or larger.

## Method

The authors construct a formalism in which the probability distribution of transverse energy \(E_T\) and the number of comoving particles \(m_0\) that suppress J/psi are correlated via Gaussian distributions for particle production and transverse energy fluctuations. They then compute the cross-talk coefficient \(\alpha_x\) between the rapidity interval of the J/psi (\(3 \leq y \leq 4\)) and the interval where \(E_T\) is measured (\(1 \leq \eta \leq 2.3\)) using string formation and decay within the dual parton model, evaluating the probability that a hadron at the J/psi rapidity and a hadron contributing to \(E_T\) originate from the same string.

## Key numbers

- The critical participant density in the cut-off model is \(n_p^c = 3.7\ \text{fm}^{-2}\).
- The cross-talk coefficient between the mean rapidities \(y_\psi = 3.5\) and \(y_{E_T} = 1.65\) is calculated to be \(\alpha_x = 0.82\).
- The factor \(e_T \bar E_T / \sigma^2_{E_T}\) (one component of \(\alpha_0\)) is found from fits to measured \(E_T\) distributions to lie between 1 and 0.7.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV, the drop in J/psi suppression occurs at \(E_T \gtrsim 100\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological prescriptions of Capella et al. (1999) and Blaizot et al. (1999) for J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions by providing a theoretical derivation of the ad hoc factor \(\epsilon(E_T)\) those authors introduced. The derivation clarifies that the strength of the fluctuation effect depends on multiplicity distribution widths and cross talk between rapidity intervals, and it enables future models to replace the ad hoc factor with a parameterized form \(\hat\epsilon(E_T) = 1 + \alpha_0 \cdot \alpha_x (E_T - \bar E_T)/\bar E_T\) that can be constrained by data on multiplicity fluctuations and rapidity correlations.
