# A short review on recent developments in TMD factorization and implementation

> In the latest years the theoretical and phenomenological advances in the factorization of several collider processes using the transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMD)has greatly increased. I attempt here a short resume of the newest developments discussing also the most recent perturbative QCD calculations. The work is not strictly directed to experts in the field and it wants to offer an overview of the tools and concepts which are behind the TMD factorization and evolution. I consider both theoretical and phenomenological aspects, some of which have still to be fully explored. It is expected that actual colliders and the Electron Ion Collider (EIC) will provide important information in this respect.

## Key claim

This review argues that the rapidity scale $\zeta$ is the essential new element in transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization, and that a consistent implementation of the $(\mu,\zeta)$ evolution, separating nonperturbative evolution effects from intrinsic TMD nonperturbative effects, is crucial for reliable predictions and extractions from data, a goal not fully realized in the traditional Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) approach.

## Method

The paper reviews the factorization theorem for Drell-Yan processes in the regime $q_T \ll Q$, where $q_T$ is the transverse momentum of the dilepton pair and $Q$ is its invariant mass. It describes how the cross section is expressed in terms of TMD distributions that depend on both a renormalization scale $\mu$ and a rapidity scale $\zeta$, with the soft function providing the rapidity anomalous dimension $\mathcal{D}$ that governs evolution. The matching of TMDs onto collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs) at small impact parameter $b_T$ (large $q_T$) is summarized, and the current status of perturbative calculations for various quark TMDs is tabulated.

## Key numbers

- The condition for the TMD regime is $q_T \ll Q$, where $q_T$ is the transverse momentum of the dilepton pair and $Q$ is its invariant mass.
- The rapidity scales satisfy $\zeta_1 \zeta_2 = Q^4$ for the Drell-Yan process.
- The unpolarized quark TMD matching coefficient is known at NNLO ($a_s^2$), with references to Gehrmann (2014) and Echevarria (2016).
- The transversity TMD matching coefficient is known at NNLO ($a_s^2$), with reference to Gutierrez-Reyes (2018).
- The Sivers function matching coefficient is known at NLO ($a_s^1$), with references including Boer (2003), Ji (2006), and Scimemi (2018).
- The helicity TMD matching coefficient is known at NLO ($a_s^1$), with references including Bacchetta (2013) and Gutierrez-Reyes (2017).
- The pretzelosity matching coefficient has been found to be zero up to NNLO, suggesting it is a twist-4 observable.
- The gluon unpolarized TMD matching coefficient is known at NNLO, with reference to Scimemi (2018).
- The linearly polarized gluon TMD matching coefficient is known at NLO, with reference to Gutierrez-Reyes (2017).

## Context

This review extends the line of TMD factorization work by Collins (2011), Becher (2010), and Echevarria (2012), focusing on the rapidity evolution framework and the $\zeta$-prescription as an alternative to the traditional CSS approach. It summarizes the current perturbative knowledge of TMD matching coefficients and identifies the need for higher-order calculations for twist-3 TMDs and gluon TMDs before the Electron Ion Collider (EIC) begins operation. The work enables future phenomenological extractions of TMDs from collider data and lattice QCD evaluations of the rapidity anomalous dimension, as recently proposed by Ebert (2018).
