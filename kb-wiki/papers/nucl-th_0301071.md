# Disentangling the transfer and breakup contributions from the inclusive 8Li+ 208Pb reaction

> An analysis of the elastic and one-neutron removal channel for the reaction 8Li+208Pb reaction at energies around the Coulomb barrier is presented. For the elastic scattering, an optical model analysis of the experimental data is performed. The observed 7Li is interpreted as the superposition of the one-neutron transfer reaction, 208Pb(8Li,7Li)209Pb, and the breakup reaction. The separate contribution of each one of these processes has been calculated within the DWBA formalism. The sum of both contributions explains adequately the experimental angular distribution of 7Li.

## Key claim

The observed 7Li yield in the 8Li+208Pb reaction at energies around the Coulomb barrier is a superposition of one-neutron transfer and breakup contributions; the DWBA calculations show that transfer accounts for about 80% of the yield, but breakup becomes the dominant mechanism at scattering angles above 120 degrees.

## Method

The authors performed an optical model analysis of elastic scattering data for 8Li+208Pb at Elab=34.4 MeV, comparing with 7Li+208Pb data at 33 MeV. They then calculated the separate contributions of one-neutron transfer (208Pb(8Li,7Li)209Pb) and breakup using the post-form Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA), with the breakup continuum discretized into energy bins up to 6 MeV relative energy and partial waves up to ℓ=6. The 8Li projectile was treated as a two-cluster model (7Li core plus valence neutron) with spectroscopic amplitudes derived from a Wigner supermultiplet scheme.

## Key numbers

- The valence neutron in 8Li has a binding energy of ε0=2.03 MeV, resulting in a positive Q value for one-neutron removal
- For 8Li+208Pb at Elab=34.4 MeV, optical potential "Pot 1" gave V0=37.1 MeV, W=154 MeV, aw=0.57 fm, reaction cross section σR=1231 mb, and χ²=1.11
- For 8Li+208Pb at Elab=34.4 MeV, optical potential "Pot 2" gave V0=15.4 MeV, W=58.4 MeV, aw=0.70 fm, reaction cross section σR=1275 mb, and χ²=1.51
- The 8Li and 7Li Coulomb barriers differ by about 4 MeV
- The 8Li ground state spin is 2+, with an excited 1+ state at εx=0.98 MeV
- The 7Li core has a ground state (3/2-) and an excited state (1/2-) at εx=0.48 MeV
- The transfer channel accounts for about 80% of the observed 7Li yield
- The breakup continuum was discretized into N=3 energy bins up to ε=6 MeV maximum relative energy between n and 208Pb

## Context

This work extends the analysis of reactions with weakly bound radioactive beams at low energies, following the observation of strong 4He groups in the 6He+209Bi reaction by Aguilera et al. (2000, 2001). The 8Li+208Pb experiment was performed at the RNB facility at Notre-Dame by Kolata et al. (2002). The authors note that previous work by Smith et al. (1991) and Becchetti et al. (1993) showed similarity between 8Li and 7Li scattering on lighter targets, but the present analysis finds a more absorptive nature for 8Li at energies around the barrier. The results enable future studies of the effect of the 1+ excited state on transfer cross sections and the influence of transfer channels on elastic cross sections.
