# On the pion electroproduction amplitude

> We analyze amplitudes for the pion electroproduction on proton derived from Lagrangians based on the local chiral SU(2) x SU(2) symmetries. We show that such amplitudes do contain information on the nucleon axial form factor F_A in both soft and hard pion regimes. This result invalidates recent Haberzettl's claim that the pion electroproduction at threshold cannot be used to extract any information regarding F_A.

## Key claim

The pion electroproduction amplitude derived from Lagrangians based on local chiral SU(2) × SU(2) symmetry does contain information on the nucleon weak axial form factor F_A in both soft and hard pion regimes, contrary to Haberzettl's claim that threshold pion electroproduction cannot be used to extract any information regarding F_A. The author argues that Haberzettl's conclusion arises from a misinterpretation of results rather than a flaw in the formalism itself.

## Method

The author constructs the pion electroproduction amplitude using two chiral Lagrangian models: a minimal Yang-Mills Lagrangian and a hidden local symmetry Lagrangian. For the minimal Lagrangian, the amplitude is built directly from Feynman diagrams including Born terms, pion pole terms, and contact terms. For the hidden local symmetry Lagrangian, the current-current amplitudes are constructed and their divergences are calculated using Eq. (pMnjl), which relates the divergence to the pion production amplitude plus the matrix element of the axial current. The contributions from π and a₁ meson exchanges in the current-current amplitudes are evaluated explicitly.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon axial coupling constant g_A = 1.267 (used in the definition of the axial current matrix element)
- The Weinberg relation m²_a₁ = 2m²_ρ is used in deriving the contact term from the minimal Lagrangian
- The contact term in the soft pion limit contains F_A(k²) plus terms of order O(q, k²)

## Context

This work extends the chiral Lagrangian approach to pion electroproduction developed by Truhlík and collaborators (STK, IT1, T, STG) and directly contradicts the recent claim by Haberzettl (H1) that the nucleon weak axial form factor cannot be extracted from threshold pion electroproduction. The author shows that earlier extractions of axial and pseudoscalar form factors by Choi (Ch) and by BNR used amplitudes containing an incorrect induced pseudoscalar term. The result enables reliable extraction of the nucleon axial form factor from pion electroproduction experiments and clarifies the role of a₁ meson exchange in producing the Kroll-Ruderman contact term, as originally noted by Adler (Ad).
