# Neutral current coherent pion production

> We investigate the neutrino induced coherent pion production reaction at low and intermediate energies. The model includes pion, nucleon and Delta(1232) resonance as the relevant hadronic degrees of freedom. Nuclear medium effects on the production mechanisms and pion distortion are taken into account. We obtain that the dominance of the Delta excitation holds due to large cancellations among the background contributions. We consider two sets of vector and axial-vector N-Delta transition form-factors, evidencing the strong sensitivity of the results to the axial coupling C5A(0). The differences between neutrino and antineutrino cross sections, emerging from interference terms, are also discussed.

## Key claim

The dominance of Delta(1232) resonance excitation in neutral-current coherent pion production holds because large cancellations among background contributions render them negligible; however, the cross section is strongly sensitive to the axial coupling C5^A(0), and using a smaller value (0.867) from a recent fit reduces the cross section by about a factor of two compared to the traditional value (1.2).

## Method

The authors extended their earlier model of charged-current coherent pion production by adding low-energy background terms (crossed Delta term, direct and crossed nucleon-pole terms) and applied it to neutral-current coherent pi0 production on isospin-symmetric nuclei (12C, 27Al, 56Fe). They used two sets of vector and axial-vector N-Delta transition form factors: set I with C5^A(0)=1.2 and M_AΔ=1.28 GeV from BNL data, and set II with C5^A(0)=0.867 and M_AΔ=0.985 GeV from a fit to ANL data. Nuclear medium effects on the Delta resonance (density-dependent selfenergy) and pion distortion (solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a Delta-hole optical potential) were included.

## Key numbers

- Cross section ratio between form-factor sets I and II: sigma(I)/sigma(II) ≈ 1.9, reflecting the squared ratio of C5^A(0) values (1.2/0.867)^2
- Crossed Delta term contributes about 0.6% of the total cross section at E_nu = 1 GeV on 12C
- Sum of direct and crossed nucleon-pole terms gives less than 0.2% of the total cross section
- NC neutrino + 27Al cross section averaged over Aachen-Padova flux: 19.9 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set I), 10.1 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set II); experimental value 29 ± 10 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- NC antineutrino + 27Al cross section averaged over Aachen-Padova flux: 19.7 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set I), 9.8 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set II); experimental value 25 ± 7 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- CC neutrino + 12C cross section averaged over K2K flux: 10.8 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set I), 5.7 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set II); experimental upper bound < 7.7 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- NC neutrino + 12C cross section averaged over MiniBooNE flux: 5.0 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set I), 2.6 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set II); preliminary experimental value 7.7 ± 1.6 ± 3.6 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- NC antineutrino + 12C cross section averaged over MiniBooNE flux: 4.6 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set I), 2.2 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (set II)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier charged-current coherent pion production model (Alvarez-Ruso et al., 2007) by adding background terms and applying it to neutral-current reactions. It builds on the nucleon-level model of Hernandez et al. (2007) for weak pion production, which derived background terms using the nonlinear sigma model and found a smaller C5^A(0) value. The results are relevant for interpreting data from MiniBooNE, K2K, and future experiments like MINERvA, where neutral-current coherent pi0 production is a background for neutrino oscillation measurements. The paper shows that coherent pion production is more sensitive to the axial N-Delta form factor than incoherent production, making it better suited to constrain C5^A(0) once experimental separation of coherent and incoherent contributions is achieved.
