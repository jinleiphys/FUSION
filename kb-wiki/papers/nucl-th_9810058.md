# Covariant formulation of pion-nucleon scattering

> A covariant model of elastic pion-nucleon scattering based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation is presented. The kernel consists of s- and u-channel nucleon and delta poles, along with rho and sigma exchange in the t-channel. A good fit is obtained to the s- and p-wave phase shifts up to the two-pion production threshold.

# Covariant formulation of pion-nucleon scattering

## Key claim
A fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation (without any 3-dimensional reduction) for elastic pion-nucleon scattering, with a kernel of s- and u-channel nucleon and Delta(1232) poles plus rho and sigma t-channel exchange, can fit the s- and p-wave phase shifts up to the two-pion production threshold, but only by using large cutoff masses that make the dressing of the vertices significant and by treating the bare Delta mass and coupling as free parameters rather than renormalizing them via analytic continuation.

## Method
The Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved in full 4-dimensional form by expanding the nucleon propagator into positive and negative energy components, sandwiching the equation between Dirac spinors, performing a partial wave decomposition to obtain 2-dimensional integral equations, and carrying out a Wick rotation for numerical solution. The kernel included tree-level s- and u-channel nucleon and Delta(1232) exchanges plus rho and sigma t-channel exchange, with derivative pion couplings and monopole form factors at each vertex; the s-channel pole diagrams used bare masses and couplings, while u-channel diagrams used physical masses and couplings.

## Key numbers
- Renormalized πNN coupling constant fixed at g_πNN^2/4π = 13.5
- Cutoff masses obtained: Λ_N = 3.12 GeV, Λ_π = 1.73 GeV, Λ_ρ = 4.67 GeV, Λ_Δ = 4.86 GeV, Λ_σ = 1.4 GeV
- Coupling constants: g_ρππ g_ρNN / 4π = 3.2, κ_ρ = 2.57, g_σππ g_σNN / 4π = -0.3, f_πNΔ^2/4π = 0.44, f_πNΔ^(0)2/4π = 0.35
- Bare Delta mass m_Δ^(0) = 2.13 GeV; sigma mass m_σ = 700 MeV
- Bare nucleon mass m_N^(0) = 1.37 GeV; bare πNN coupling g_πNN^(0)2/4π = 3.8
- Renormalized πNN cutoff mass Λ_π^R = 1.17 GeV (bare value was 1.73 GeV)
- Scattering lengths/volumes (in units of m_π^-(2ℓ+1)): S_11 = 0.184, S_31 = -0.101, P_11 = -0.079, P_31 = -0.045, P_13 = -0.037, P_33 = 0.181

## Context
This work extends the meson-exchange approach to pion-nucleon scattering pioneered by Pearce and Jennings (1991) and others, but differs by solving the full Bethe-Salpeter equation without any 3-dimensional reduction, which removes the ambiguity of choosing a particular reduction scheme. The results are compared to the SM95 partial wave analysis of Arndt et al. (1995). The model enables future covariant calculations of reactions involving pions, such as pion photoproduction, and demonstrates that vertex dressing significantly softens the πNN form factor (from Λ_π = 1.73 GeV to Λ_π^R = 1.17 GeV).
