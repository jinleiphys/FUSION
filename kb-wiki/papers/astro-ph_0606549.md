# The Upper Limit of Magnetic Field Strength in Dense Stellar Hadronic Matter

> It is shown that in strongly magnetized neutron stars, there exist upper limits of magnetic field strength, beyond which the self energies for both neutron and proton components of neutron star matter become complex in nature. As a consequence they decay within the strong interaction time scale. However, in the ultra-strong magnetic field case, when the zeroth Landau level is only occupied by protons, the system again becomes stable against strong decay.

# The Upper Limit of Magnetic Field Strength in Dense Stellar Hadronic Matter

## Key claim

The paper claims that strongly magnetized neutron stars have an upper limit of magnetic field strength beyond which the self-energies of both neutrons and protons become complex, causing the hadronic matter to decay on strong interaction timescales; however, if the field is so strong that only the zeroth Landau level is occupied by protons, the system becomes stable again.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic Landau-Fermi liquid formalism for dense neutron star matter in a strong quantizing magnetic field, with exchange of σ-ω-ρ mesons. They evaluated the quasi-particle interaction function from two-particle forward scattering diagrams (n-p exchange with σ-ω-ρ₃ mesons and direct diagram with ρ± mesons), then computed the proton and neutron self-energies by integrating the interaction functions over momentum up to the respective Fermi momenta at T=0. The traces of projection operators involving Dirac spinors in a magnetic field (with Landau quantization for protons) were evaluated to determine whether the self-energies become real or complex.

## Key numbers

- The quantum critical magnetic field for protons is B_m^(c)(p) = m_p²/q_p ≈ 1.6 × 10²⁰ G, above which Landau levels for protons become populated.
- For magnetic field strengths > 1.6 × 10²⁰ G, the hadronic matter becomes unstable due to complex self-energies, establishing this as the upper limit for stable neutron star magnetic fields.
- When the maximum Landau quantum number for protons becomes zero (only the zeroth Landau level occupied), the self-energies become real again and the system is stable.

## Context

This work extends the study of Shabad and Usov on magnetic field effects in dense matter, and builds on the authors' previous relativistic formalism of Landau theory of Fermi liquid for neutron star matter with σ-ω-ρ meson exchange (Chakrabarty, 2006). The paper shows that the stability of hadronic matter in magnetars is not monotonic with field strength: there is an unstable region between the classical regime (B_m < 1.6 × 10²⁰ G) and the ultra-strong quantum regime where only the zeroth Landau level is occupied. This result constrains the maximum possible magnetic field in neutron stars with stable hadronic cores and has implications for the equation of state and phase transitions to quark matter, which were previously shown to be forbidden for fields exceeding 10¹⁵ G.
