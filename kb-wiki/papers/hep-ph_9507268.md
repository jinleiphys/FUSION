# A New Mass Formula for NG Bosons in QCD

> An often used mass formula for Nambu-Goldstone (NG) bosons in QCD, such as the pions, involves the condensate $<\overline{q}q>$, $f_π$ and the quark current masses. We argue, within the context of the Global Colour Model to QCD, that this expression is wrong. Analysis of the interplay between the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the constituent quark effect and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the NG boson results in a new mass formula.

# A New Mass Formula for NG Bosons in QCD

## Key claim

The standard pion mass formula \(M_\pi^2 = (m_u+m_d)\langle\bar{q}q\rangle / f_\pi^2\) is incorrect within the Global Colour Model (GCM) truncation of QCD; the correct expression to first order in the quark current mass replaces the divergent condensate \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle\) with a convergent effective condensate \(\rho_{\rm eff}\) that includes a dynamical enhancement function \(\epsilon_s(s)\) and a natural cutoff function \(c(s)\), and contains an extra factor of 2.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the constituent quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for the pion mass-shell state in the GCM, a model field theory that retains only the gluon two-point function. They perform a Taylor expansion of the quark propagator functions in the current quark mass \(m\) and a small-\(P_\mu\) expansion of the BSE kernel, then derive a variational mass functional whose minimisation yields the pion mass to \(O(m)\).

## Key numbers

- The effective condensate parameter computed from the new formula is \(\rho_{\rm eff} = (0.233\ {\rm GeV})^3\).
- The pion decay constant obtained from the GCM fit is \(f_\pi = 93.0\ {\rm MeV}\).
- With a current quark mass \(m = 6.5\ {\rm MeV}\), the new formula gives \(M_\pi = 138.5\ {\rm MeV}\).
- In the infrared region the scalar enhancement function reaches \(\epsilon_s(s) \approx 4\), enhancing a current mass of \(\sim 6\ {\rm MeV}\) to about \(24\ {\rm MeV}\).
- The chiral-limit constituent quark running mass at \(s = 0.3\ {\rm GeV}^2\) is \(M(s) \approx 270\ {\rm MeV}\).

## Context

This work contradicts the standard Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner (GMOR) relation (1968) as commonly implemented in QCD, which uses the divergent condensate \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle\) with an ad hoc cutoff. The authors show that the error arises from incorrectly using the chiral-limit Bethe-Salpeter amplitude as the ansatz for the finite-mass amplitude, a mistake they trace to earlier GCM papers. The new convergent formula enables finite, cutoff-free computations of pion properties within the GCM framework and is consistent with independent results by Roberts (1995).
