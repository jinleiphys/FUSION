# Photon vortex generation in quantum level by high-order harmonic synchrotron radiations from spiral moving electrons in magnetic fields

> We explore photon vortex generation in synchrotron radiations from a spiral moving electron under a uniform magnetic field along z-axis using Landau quantization. The obtained wave-function of the photon vortecies is the eigen-state of the z-component of the total angular momentum (zTAM). In m-th harmonic radiations, individual photons are the eigen-state of zTAM of m. This is consistent with previous studies. Using the presently obtained wave-functions we calculate the decay widths and the energy spectra under extremely strong magnetic fields of 10^12 - 10^13 G, which are observed in astrophysical objects such as magnetized neutron stars and jets and accretion disks around black holes. The result suggests that photon vortices are predominantly generated in such objects. Although they have no coherency it is expected that photon vortices from the universe are measured using a detector based upon a quantum effect in future. This effect also affects to stellar nucleosynthesis in strong magnetic fields.

## Key claim

Photon vortices are predominantly generated in astrophysical objects with extremely strong magnetic fields of \(10^{12}\)–\(10^{13}\) G, but these photons have no coherency and appear as white light, requiring a quantum-effect-based detector for future measurement.

## Method

The authors used Landau quantization to solve the Dirac equation for an electron in a uniform magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis, obtaining electron wave-functions that are eigen-states of the \(z\)-component of the total angular momentum (zTAM). They then derived the wave-function of the radiated photon as a solution of the Klein-Gordon equation in cylindrical coordinates, choosing transverse magnetic (TM) and transverse electric (TE) eigen-states rather than helicity eigen-states. The decay width of the electron was calculated from the imaginary part of the electron self-energy, and numerical results were obtained for magnetic field strengths of \(10^{12}\) G and \(10^{13}\) G.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: \(10^{12}\) G and \(10^{13}\) G, which are observed in astrophysical objects such as magnetized neutron stars and accretion disks around black holes.
- Upper limit of the fundamental radiation energy: 4.1 MeV when the initial electron energy is approximately 50 MeV.
- In the energy region of 4.1–50 MeV, only photon vortices are radiated (no fundamental radiation).
- The diameter of a spiral motion of an electron is estimated as \(d \sim 1.6 \times \sqrt{10^{13}/B\ [\text{G}]} L_i^{1/2}\ [\text{pm}]\), which is shorter than \(10^{-8}\) cm under the assumed conditions.
- The maximum (minimum) energy for a radiation with zTAM \(m\) is given by \(\frac{eB m}{E_i + |p_{iz}|} \le e_q \le \frac{eB m}{E_i - |p_{iz}|}\).

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Katoh et al. (2017) that \(l\)-th harmonic photons in synchrotron radiation from spiral-moving electrons carry \(l\hbar\) total angular momentum, by providing the first calculation of photon vortex wave-functions using Landau quantization under extremely strong magnetic fields. The results are consistent with previous studies by Sasaki et al. (2008), Bahrdt et al. (2013), and Bogdanov et al. (2018, 2019), but differ from those for helical undulators. The work enables future detection of astrophysical photon vortices using Compton scattering-based detectors and predicts that photon vortices may alter isotopic abundances in stellar nucleosynthesis via the \(\gamma\)-process, as the conservation of angular momentum forbids excitation to giant dipole resonance states in even-even nuclei.
