# An effective field theory approach to two trapped particles

> We discuss the problem of two particles interacting via short-range interactions within a harmonic-oscillator trap. The interactions are organized according to their number of derivatives and defined in truncated model spaces made from a bound-state basis. Leading-order (LO) interactions are iterated to all orders, while corrections are treated in perturbation theory. We show explicitly that next-to-LO and next-to-next-to-LO interactions improve convergence as the model space increases. In the large-model-space limit we regain results from a pseudopotential. Arbitrary scattering lengths are considered, as well as a generalization to include the non-vanishing range of the interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective field theory (EFT) approach to two particles in a harmonic-oscillator trap, with interactions organized by number of derivatives and solved in truncated model spaces, systematically improves convergence of energy levels as the model space increases, provided that higher-order corrections are treated in perturbation theory and that the infinite-cutoff limit reproduces results from a pseudopotential.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation for two particles interacting via short-range forces within a harmonic-oscillator trap, using a basis of harmonic-oscillator wavefunctions truncated to a maximum number of shells N_max. The leading-order (LO) contact interaction is iterated to all orders, while next-to-leading-order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading-order (N^2LO) corrections involving derivative operators are treated in perturbation theory. The coupling constants at each order are renormalized by fixing them to reproduce selected energy levels in the trap, with the cutoff dependence controlled by the model-space size.

## Key numbers

* The ultraviolet momentum cutoff is given by Lambda = (1/b) sqrt(2N_max + 3), where b is the harmonic-oscillator length and N_max is the maximum number of shells.
* The LO coupling constant runs as C_0^(0) = -pi^2/(mu Lambda) [1 + pi/(2 Lambda a_2) + O(1/(Lambda^2 b^2))] in the large-Lambda limit, where a_2 is the scattering length.
* The NLO coupling constants in the large-cutoff limit are C_0^(1) = -pi^3 r_2/(12 mu) [1 + O(1/(r_2 Lambda), 1/(a_2 Lambda))] and C_2^(1)/C_0^(0)^2 = mu r_2/(8 pi) [1 + O(1/(r_2 Lambda))], where r_2 is the effective range.
* The transcendental equation relating trapped energy epsilon to scattering length at LO is Gamma(3/4 - epsilon/2)/Gamma(1/4 - epsilon/2) = b/(2a_2), first found by Busch et al. (1998) using a pseudopotential.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order treatment of Stetcu et al. (2007) by including explicit NLO and N^2LO corrections, showing systematic improvement in convergence as the model space increases. The approach is closely related to the no-core shell model (NCSM) method used in nuclear physics and provides a controlled EFT framework that reproduces the pseudopotential results of Busch et al. (1998) in the infinite-cutoff limit, while also generalizing to include finite effective range and higher ERE parameters. The method enables future calculations for larger trapped systems and can be applied across atomic and nuclear physics.
