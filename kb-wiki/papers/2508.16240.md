# Accurate spontaneous fission half-lives from a microscopic large-scale nuclear structure model

> We demonstrate the ability of the BSkG3 model to predict large-scale fission properties. In particular, we focus on the description of spontaneous fission half-lives, which are crucial for modeling the r-process. To assess the model accuracy, we compare predicted and experimental values for all nuclei with available data. The combination of BSkG3 predictive power for ground state and fission properties, the inclusion of triaxial and octupole degrees of freedom, the use of microscopic collective inertias, and the minimization of the action make our approach sophisticated yet practical when it comes to large-scale astrophysical applications.

## Key claim

The BSkG3 energy density functional model can reproduce all known spontaneous fission half-lives (107 ground-state and 29 isomeric decays) within less than four orders of magnitude, matching the accuracy of phenomenological microscopic-macroscopic models while simultaneously maintaining state-of-the-art predictive power for nuclear ground-state properties such as masses and charge radii.

## Method

The authors constructed two-dimensional potential energy surfaces in the collective variables (β20, β30) and (β20, β22) using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the MOCCa code, then applied the nudged elastic band method to find the least action path. The spontaneous fission half-life was obtained from the semiclassical tunneling probability using the adiabatic approximation, with the effective inertia tensor computed microscopically via the cranking approximation to the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism.

## Key numbers

- The BSkG3 model reproduces 107 ground-state spontaneous fission half-lives with an rms deviation factor f_rms = 1.1 × 10^3 and a mean deviation factor f_mean = 7.5 × 10^(-2) across all nuclei
- For the 29 isomeric decays, BSkG3 achieves f_rms = 5.3 × 10^3 and f_mean = 7.2 × 10^(-4)
- For even nuclei only (57 ground-state cases), the rms deviation is f_rms = 4.1 × 10^2 with f_mean = 1.4 × 10^0
- For odd nuclei only (50 ground-state cases), the rms deviation is f_rms = 2.9 × 10^3 with f_mean = 2.7 × 10^(-3)
- The BSkG3 model reproduces 45 primary and secondary fission barrier heights from the RIPL-3 compilation with rms errors of 0.33 MeV and 0.51 MeV respectively, and 28 isomer excitation energies with an rms of 0.36 MeV
- The model achieves global root-mean-square deviations of 0.631 MeV for all known nuclear masses and 0.024 fm for all known charge radii

## Context

This work extends the BSkG family of energy density functionals, which previously demonstrated accuracy for ground-state properties and fission barriers (Grams 2023), to the calculation of spontaneous fission half-lives across the entire chart of nuclides. The results surpass the only comparable EDF-based study by Giuliani (2018), which achieved f_rms = 1.9 × 10^4 for ground-state decays using the BCPM functional but required an a posteriori renormalization of collective inertias, and match the accuracy of microscopic-macroscopic models such as the Lublin-Strasbourg liquid drop model (Blanco 2023) which achieved f_rms = 2.8 × 10^3 for 50 even nuclei. This work enables systematic studies of the thousands of neutron-rich nuclei relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations and opens the possibility of including spontaneous fission half-lives directly in the fitting protocol of future energy density functional models.
