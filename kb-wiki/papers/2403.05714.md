# Testing effective field theory with the most general neutron decay correlations

> Built on the seminal works by Jackson-Treiman-Wyld and Ebel-Feldman, we derive the most general free neutron differential decay rate where all massive particles (neutron, proton and electron) are polarized. This introduces 33 new correlations in addition to the 18 existing ones, which over-constrain the coupling constants in the low-energy effective field theory of charged weak interactions, and thus provides stringent tests of the validity of the theory framework itself. We classify the correlation coefficients in terms of their Standard Model limit and discrete symmetries, and study their expansion with respect to the new physics coupling strengths, supplemented by the experiment-independent $\mathcal{O}(α)$ virtual electromagnetic radiative corrections.

## Key claim

Building on the seminal works by Jackson-Treiman-Wyld and Ebel-Feldman, the paper derives the most general free neutron differential decay rate where all massive particles (neutron, proton, and electron) are polarized, introducing 33 new correlations beyond the 18 existing ones; this over-constrains the coupling constants in the low-energy effective field theory of charged weak interactions and provides stringent tests of the validity of the theory framework itself, but the author notes that the paper does not propose a practical experiment for measuring proton polarization, aiming instead to inspire future experimental considerations.

## Method

The author computes the squared amplitude for free neutron beta decay using the most general Lee-Yang Hamiltonian, which includes scalar, vector, tensor, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar couplings, allowing all massive particles (neutron, proton, and electron) to be polarized. The resulting differential decay rate is expanded in terms of 51 correlation coefficients, classified by their discrete symmetries (parity and time-reversal) and their behavior under an expansion in beyond-Standard-Model (BSM) coupling strengths, with the Standard Model limit defined by the vector coupling \(G_V\) and the axial-to-vector ratio \(\lambda\). The analysis includes experiment-independent \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha)\) virtual electromagnetic radiative corrections, but excludes recoil corrections and bremsstrahlung contributions, which must be tailored to specific experimental setups.

## Key numbers

- The total number of correlation coefficients derived is 51, comprising 12 from Jackson-Treiman-Wyld (JTW), 5 from Ebel-Feldman (EF), 11 new ones linear in proton polarization \(\hat{s}_p\), and 22 new ones linear in both \(\hat{s}_n\) and \(\hat{s}_p\).
- The Standard Model parameters are defined with \(G_F = 1.1663788(6) \times 10^{-5}~\text{GeV}^{-2}\) (Fermi constant from muon decay) and \(\lambda = g_A/g_V\), with the best measurement of \(\lambda\) from the beta asymmetry parameter \(A\) by PERKEO-III giving \(\lambda = -1.27641(56)\), and from the electron-neutrino correlation \(a\) by aSPECT giving \(\lambda = -1.2668(27)\), showing a \(3.5\sigma\) discrepancy.
- The Fierz term \(b\) receives a first-order BSM correction: \((\mathcal{C}\xi)_1 = \sqrt{2}G_V \mathfrak{Re}\{C_S^+ + 3\lambda C_T^+\}\).
- The \(D\) coefficient (T-odd) receives a first-order BSM correction: \((\mathcal{C}\xi)_1 = \sqrt{2}G_V \mathfrak{Im}C_{AV}^+\), where \(C_{AV}^+ \equiv C_A^+ - \lambda C_V^+\).
- The universal part of the virtual outer radiative corrections \(\delta_v^U\) is given by \(\delta_v^U = \frac{1}{2}\ln\frac{m_p^2}{m_e^2} - \frac{11}{4} + \ln\frac{m_e^2 m_p^2}{m_\gamma^4} + \frac{2}{\beta}\tanh^{-1}\beta\left[\ln\frac{m_\gamma^2}{m_e^2} - \tanh^{-1}\beta\right] - \frac{2}{\beta}\text{Li}_2\left(\frac{2\beta}{1+\beta}\right)\), where \(m_\gamma\) is a fictitious photon mass.

## Context

This work extends the foundational formalism of Jackson, Treiman, and Wyld (1957) and Ebel and Feldman (1957), who derived neutron decay correlations with up to two or three vector quantities (neutron polarization, electron polarization, neutrino momentum), by including proton polarization for the first time. The new correlations over-constrain the coupling constants of the low-energy effective field theory (EFT) for charged weak interactions, enabling stringent consistency tests of the EFT framework and potentially revealing light new degrees of freedom that cannot be integrated out. The classification by discrete symmetries and BSM expansion order allows future experiments to probe CP-violating interactions (relevant for baryogenesis, as per Sakharov (1967)) and to cross-check the Standard Model extraction of \(\lambda\), which currently shows a \(3.5\sigma\) discrepancy between measurements from aSPECT (Beck, 2019, 2023) and PERKEO-III (Markisch, 2018).
