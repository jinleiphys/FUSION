# Internal structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance probed in chiral unitary amplitude

> The internal structure of the resonant Lambda(1405) state is investigated based on meson-baryon coupled-channels chiral dynamics. We evaluate Lambda(1405) form factors which are extracted from current-coupled scattering amplitudes in meson-baryon degrees of freedom. Using several probe currents and channel decomposition, we find that the resonant Lambda(1405) state is dominantly composed of widely spread Kbar around N, with escaping pi Sigma component.

## Key claim

The resonant Lambda(1405) state is dominantly composed of a widely spread antikaon (Kbar) around a nucleon (N), with an escaping pion-Sigma (pi Sigma) component; the Kbar N (I=0) channel generates more than 99% of the Lambda(1405) charge, consistent with the Kbar N quasibound state picture.

## Method

The authors used a chiral unitary approach with meson-baryon coupled-channels dynamics to compute the scattering amplitude T_ij, which dynamically generates the Lambda(1405) resonance. They then evaluated current-coupled scattering amplitudes T_gamma_ij^mu in a charge-conserved way by coupling probe currents to the constituent hadrons (mesons and baryons) inside the resonance, and extracted the Lambda(1405) form factors at the pole position Z_R using a residue method. The density distributions (electric, baryonic, and strangeness) were obtained by Fourier-transforming the form factors and decomposing the contributions by channel.

## Key numbers

- The Kbar N (I=0) channel contribution to the Lambda(1405) charge is 0.994 + 0.048i, corresponding to more than 99% of the total charge.
- The baryonic form factor at zero momentum transfer F_B(Q^2=0) equals 1, and the strangeness form factor F_S(Q^2=0) equals -1, satisfying the Ward identity.
- The Kbar distribution inside Lambda(1405) largely exceeds typical hadronic size of less than 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach studies of the Lambda(1405) as a dynamically generated resonance (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1997, Oller 2000, Lutz 2001, Jido 2003, Hyodo 2011) and builds on the authors' previous density distribution study (Sekihara 2008). It is consistent with the double-pole structure predicted by Oller (2000) and Jido (2003), where one pole originates from the Kbar N bound state (Hyodo 2007, Hyodo 2008). The results enable future experimental surveys of the Lambda(1405) structure, such as those proposed by Jido (2009) and Cho (2010).
