# Covariant response theory beyond RPA and its application

> The covariant particle-vibration coupling model within the time blocking approximation is employed to supplement the Relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RRPA) with coupling to collective vibrations. The Bethe-Salpeter equation in the particle-hole channel with an energy dependent residual particle-hole (p-h) interaction is formulated and solved in the shell-model Dirac basis as well as in the momentum space. The same set of the coupling constants generates the Dirac-Hartree single-particle spectrum, the static part of the residual p-h interaction and the particle-phonon coupling amplitudes. This approach is applied to quantitative description of damping phenomenon in even-even spherical nuclei with closed shells $^{208}$Pb and $^{132}$Sn. Since the phonon coupling enriches the RRPA spectrum with a multitude of ph$\otimes$phonon states a noticeable fragmentation of giant monopole and dipole resonances is obtained in the examined nuclei. The results are compared with experimental data and with results of the non-relativistic approach.

## Key claim

The covariant particle-vibration coupling model within the time blocking approximation supplements the Relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RRPA) with coupling to collective vibrations, producing noticeable fragmentation of giant monopole and dipole resonances in closed-shell nuclei; however, the spreading width of the monopole resonance is not large because of a strong cancellation between the self-energy diagrams and diagrams with the phonon exchange.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation in the particle-hole channel with an energy-dependent residual particle-hole interaction was formulated and solved in both the shell-model Dirac basis and momentum space. The same set of coupling constants from the relativistic NL3 Lagrangian generated the Dirac-Hartree single-particle spectrum, the static part of the residual interaction, and the particle-phonon coupling amplitudes. The equation for density matrix variation was solved with the additional particle-phonon coupling amplitude to enrich the calculated spectrum compared to pure RRPA.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar monopole resonance in 208Pb: RRPA mean energy 14.13 MeV, width 1.17 MeV; RRPA-PC mean energy 14.02 MeV, width 1.57 MeV; experimental mean energy 13.73(20) MeV, width 2.58(20) MeV
- Isoscalar monopole resonance in 132Sn: RRPA mean energy 16.13 MeV, width 1.96 MeV; RRPA-PC mean energy 16.07 MeV, width 2.37 MeV
- Isovector E1 resonance in 208Pb: RRPA mean energy 13.1 MeV, width 2.5 MeV, EWSR 120%; RRPA-PC mean energy 12.8 MeV, width 3.8 MeV, EWSR 114%; experimental mean energy 13.4 MeV, width 4.1 MeV
- Isovector E1 resonance in 132Sn: RRPA mean energy 14.7 MeV, width 3.0 MeV, EWSR 112%; RRPA-PC mean energy 14.3 MeV, width 3.8 MeV, EWSR 108%; experimental mean energy 16.1(7) MeV, width 4.7(2.1) MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field framework beyond the RRPA by incorporating particle-vibration coupling, addressing the known deficiency that RRPA underestimates resonance widths considerably. The approach builds on the covariant theory of particle-vibration coupling developed in Litvinova and Ring (2006) and applies the time-blocking approximation from Tselyaev (1989) to the relativistic case. The results are compared with experimental data and with non-relativistic calculations using Woods-Saxon single-particle input and Landau-Migdal forces from Litvinova and Tselyaev (2005), showing similar levels of agreement but with the advantage that the covariant approach requires no parameter adjustment beyond the NL3 Lagrangian.
