# Calculating hard probe radiative energy loss beyond soft-gluon approximation: Examining the approximation validity

> The soft-gluon approximation, which implies that radiated gluon carries away a small fraction of initial parton's energy, is a commonly used assumption in calculating radiative energy loss of high momentum partons traversing QGP created at RHIC and LHC. While soft-gluon approximation is convenient, different theoretical approaches reported significant radiative energy loss of high $p_{\perp}$ partons, thereby questioning its validity. To address this issue, we relaxed the soft-gluon approximation within DGLV formalism. The obtained analytical expressions are quite distinct compared to the soft-gluon case. However, numerical results for the first order in opacity fractional energy loss lead to small differences in predictions for the two cases. The difference in the predicted number of radiated gluons is also small. Moreover, the effect on these two variables has an opposite sign, which when combined results in almost overlapping suppression predictions. Therefore, our results imply that, contrary to the commonly held doubts, the soft-gluon approximation in practice works surprisingly well in DGLV formalism. Finally, we also discuss generalizing this relaxation in the dynamical QCD medium, which suggests a more general applicability of the conclusions obtained here.

## Key claim

Contrary to commonly held doubts that the soft-gluon approximation (assuming radiated gluon carries a small fraction of the initial parton's energy) may be invalid due to significant radiative energy loss, the authors show that within the DGLV formalism the approximation works surprisingly well in practice: numerical results for first-order-in-opacity fractional energy loss and number of radiated gluons show only small differences between the full calculation and the soft-gluon case, and these differences have opposite signs, leading to almost overlapping suppression predictions.

## Method

The authors relaxed the soft-gluon approximation (the condition \(x = \omega/E \ll 1\)) within the DGLV (Djordjevic-Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) opacity expansion formalism, calculating the first-order-in-opacity radiative energy loss for gluon jets traversing a static QCD medium of finite size. They first performed the calculation for massless gluons, then extended it to include an effective gluon mass \(m_g = \mu/\sqrt{2}\) to account for finite-temperature QCD medium effects, and finally discussed generalization to a dynamical QCD medium. The analytical expressions were obtained by computing 11 Feynman diagrams (zeroth, first, and second order in scattering centers) beyond the soft-gluon approximation, using a static screened Yukawa potential and assuming an exponential distribution of longitudinal distances between the jet production site and the rescattering site.

## Key numbers

- The fractional radiative energy loss \(\Delta E^{(1)}/E\) for gluon jets shows a maximum increase of \(\sim 3\%\) up to \(p_{\perp} \approx 10\) GeV when the soft-gluon approximation is relaxed, compared to the soft-gluon case; the difference then steeply decreases toward \(0\%\) at higher \(p_{\perp}\).
- The number of radiated gluons \(N^{(1)}_g\) decreases by up to \(5\%\) at very low transverse momenta and reaches a peak relative difference of \(-2\%\) at \(p_{\perp} \approx 10\) GeV, remaining nearly constant somewhat below \(-2\%\) for higher transverse momenta.
- For the single gluon radiation spectrum \(dN^{(1)}_g/dx\), the relative change between the beyond-soft-gluon and soft-gluon cases is smaller than \(10\%\) for \(x \lesssim 0.4\); at high \(x\) values (\(0.4 < x \leq 0.5\)), differences ascend to notable values (\(\sim 50\%\)) and increase with increasing \(p_{\perp}\).
- The calculations used parameters mimicking standard LHC conditions: constant \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(L = 5\) fm, \(\lambda = 1\) fm, and \(T = 300\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DGLV formalism (Djordjevic, Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000s) by relaxing the soft-gluon approximation, which was previously used in multiple energy loss models including ASW (Armesto, Salgado, Wiedemann), BDMPS (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff), BDMPS-Z (Zakharov), GLV, and HT (higher-twist) approaches. Prior studies introducing finite \(x\) had reached different conclusions on the importance of this relaxation, ranging from relatively small (Vitev, 2009) to moderately large (HT approach). The results here show that for the DGLV formalism the soft-gluon approximation works surprisingly well for gluon jets, implying that relaxing it for quark jets is likely redundant. This enables extending the formalism's applicability toward the intermediate momentum region (\(5 < p_{\perp} < 10\) GeV) where experimental data are most abundant, and supports the use of the dynamical energy loss formalism for QCD medium tomography.
