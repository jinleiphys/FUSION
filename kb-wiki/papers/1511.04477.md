# Transport Theory of Heavy Flavor in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> A short overview is presented for the recent progress in the theory of heavy flavor transport in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, including a summary of different transport models, their phenomenological results of heavy meson quenching and flow at RHIC and LHC, a possible solution to the $R_\mathrm{AA}$ vs. $v_2$ puzzle and predictions for heavy flavor observables beyond the current measurements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that simultaneously describing the small nuclear modification factor (R_AA) and large elliptic flow (v_2) of heavy mesons, the "heavy flavor puzzle", may be resolved by a stronger interaction strength (drag coefficient or gluon transport coefficient) near the critical temperature T_c, where the quark-gluon plasma develops strong anisotropic flow.

## Method

The paper reviews several transport approaches for heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma: the Boltzmann equation, its reduction to the Fokker-Planck equation under the assumption of small momentum transfers per scattering, and the stochastic Langevin equation. For radiative energy loss, the Langevin equation is modified with a recoil force term, using the higher-twist formalism to compute gluon radiation probabilities. Heavy quark hadronization is modeled via a hybrid of fragmentation (for high p_T) and heavy-light quark coalescence (for medium p_T), followed by hadronic rescattering in a hadron gas using either a Boltzmann cascade (UrQMD) or a Langevin approach with hadronic diffusion coefficients.

## Key numbers

* In 2.76 TeV central Pb-Pb collisions, the crossing point where quasi-elastic scattering and gluon radiation contribute equally to energy loss is around 7 GeV for charm quarks and 18 GeV for bottom quarks.
* Hadronic rescattering of D mesons in the hadron gas enhances their v_2 by another 20 to 30 percent.
* For the D-meson transverse momentum imbalance (x_T = p_T,asso / p_T,trig) in Au-Au collisions, the ratio I_AA between nucleus-nucleus and proton-proton collisions is above unity at small x_T and below unity at large x_T.
* In the study of D-meson suppression, the participant number dependence shows that B mesons are less suppressed than D mesons, consistent with the mass hierarchy of parton energy loss.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for heavy flavor transport in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier Boltzmann-based models (Molnar 2006, Zhang 2005, Uphoff 2011) and Langevin-based models (Moore 2004, He 2011, Cao 2011). It addresses the R_AA vs. v_2 puzzle by comparing temperature-dependent drag coefficients (Das 2015) and a semi-quark-gluon monopole plasma model (Xu 2014). The paper enables future measurements of two-particle correlation functions of heavy meson pairs and predictions for heavy flavor modification in small systems (proton-nucleus collisions), which could discriminate between collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms.
