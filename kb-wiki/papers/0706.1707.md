# The centrality dependence of elliptic flow at LHC

> We present predictions for the centrality dependence of elliptic flow at mid-rapidity in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the centrality dependence of elliptic flow at LHC, but not its absolute value; the predictions rely on eccentricity scaling from ideal hydrodynamics with a parameterization of viscous deviations, and the overall normalization factor remains undetermined.

## Method

The authors use a model where elliptic flow \(v_2\) is expressed as \(v_2 = h\varepsilon / (1 + K/0.7)\), with \(h\) a scale factor and \(K\) the Knudsen number derived from the partonic cross section \(\sigma\), multiplicity \(dN/dy\), and transverse overlap area \(S\). They obtain the initial-state quantities \(\varepsilon\), \(S\), and \(dN/dy\) from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approach, including fluctuations in participant nucleon positions, and extrapolate from RHIC to LHC using either fixed-coupling or running-coupling evolution of the saturation scale.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, the model fits Au-Au and Cu-Cu data with \(h = 0.22\) and \(\sigma = 5.5\) mb.
- For LHC Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV, two values of \(\sigma\) are considered: 5.5 mb (same as RHIC) and 3.3 mb (scaling as \(\sigma \sim 1/T^2\)).
- The multiplicity per participant increases by a factor of 3 with fixed-coupling evolution and by a factor of 2.4 with running-coupling evolution.
- The eccentricity \(\varepsilon\) is 10% larger with fixed-coupling evolution than with running-coupling evolution.
- For central collisions at LHC, \(v_2\) reaches 90% of the hydrodynamic limit if \(\sigma = 5.5\) mb, and 80% if \(\sigma = 3.3\) mb.
- The maximum \(v_2\) occurs for \(N_{\rm part}\) between 60 (for \(\sigma \approx\) constant) and 80 (for \(\sigma \sim 1/T^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the eccentricity-scaling approach previously applied to RHIC data (Drescher et al., 2007) by extrapolating to LHC energies using CGC-based initial conditions. It contrasts with ideal hydrodynamics by including viscous corrections parameterized via the Knudsen number, and it enables experimental tests of thermalization and initial-state models using the centrality dependence of elliptic flow as a first-day observable at LHC.
