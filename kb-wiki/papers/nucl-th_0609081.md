# Isospin Dynamics in Heavy Ion Collisions: EoS-sensitive Observables

> Heavy Ion Collisions (HIC) represent a unique tool to probe the in-medium nuclear interaction in regions away from saturation and at high nucleon momenta. In this report we present a selection of reaction observables particularly sensitive to the isovector part of the interaction, i.e. to the symmetry term of the nuclear Equation of State (EoS) At low energies the behavior of the symmetry energy around saturation influences dissipation and fragment production mechanisms. Predictions are shown for deep-inelastic and fragmentation collisions induced by neutron rich projectiles. Differential flow measurements will also shed lights on the controversial neutron/proton effective mass splitting in asymmetric matter. The high density symmetry term can be derived from isospin effects on heavy ion reactions at relativistic energies (few AGeV range), that can even allow a ``direct'' study of the covariant structure of the isovector interaction in the hadron medium. Rather sensitive observables are proposed from collective flows and from pion/kaon production. The possibility of the transition to a mixed hadron-quark phase, at high baryon and isospin density, is finally suggested. Some signatures could come from an expected ``neutron trapping'' effect.

# Isospin Dynamics in Heavy Ion Collisions: EoS-sensitive Observables

## Key claim

The symmetry term of the nuclear Equation of State can be constrained by observables from heavy ion collisions across a wide energy range, but the sensitivity depends on the specific reaction mechanism and the density region probed; the authors present predictions that are "severely constraining the existing effective interaction models" rather than providing definitive measurements, and they emphasize that the first clear evidence for a stiff symmetry energy around saturation comes from a specific correlation between neutron enrichment and alignment in neck fragmentation data.

## Method

The authors employ microscopic transport codes based on mean field theories with correlations included via hard nucleon-nucleon elastic and inelastic collisions and stochastic forces, selfconsistently evaluated from the mean phase-space trajectory. For low-energy collisions they use a non-relativistic Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) approach with a BGBD-like mean field, while for relativistic energies they solve the covariant transport equation of the Boltzmann type within the Relativistic Landau Vlasov (RLV) method using phase-space Gaussian test particles and a Monte-Carlo procedure for hadron collisions including production/absorption of Delta(1232 MeV) and N*(1440 MeV) resonances decaying into pion channels. The isovector part of the interaction is modeled with different choices: asysoft (smooth decreasing symmetry energy below saturation), asystiff (rapid decrease), NLrho (only vector rho meson field), NLrhodelta (both vector rho and scalar delta fields), and DDF (density dependent couplings with exponentially decreasing rho-coupling).

## Key numbers

- Reaction 132Sn+64Ni at 10 AMeV studied for semicentral events at impact parameters b=6, 7, 8 fm, comparing asysoft and asystiff symmetry energy behaviors
- Reaction 132Sn+64Ni at 35 AMeV shows strong correlation between neutron enrichment and alignment in neck fragmentation, reproduced only with a stiff symmetry energy
- 197Au+197Au collisions at 250 AMeV for semicentral events (b/b_max=0.5) used to test neutron/proton effective mass splitting via differential transverse and elliptic flows
- Reaction 132Sn+124Sn at 1.5 AGeV (b=6 fm) shows clear splitting in high p_t region of elliptic flow between NLrho and NLrhodelta models
- Central 197Au+197Au collisions in the 0.8-1.8 AGeV beam energy range used for pion and kaon production studies
- Pion yield freezes out at times of order 50 fm/c; kaon production saturates at around 20 fm/c
- Reaction 238U+238U at 1 AGeV and semicentral impact parameter b=7 fm produces baryon density around 3-4 rho_0, temperature 50-60 MeV, energy density approximately 500 MeV fm^{-3}, and proton fraction between 0.35 and 0.40
- For the quark EoS, the MIT bag model with B^{1/4}=150 MeV is used
- Reaction 132Sn+132Sn at 300 AMeV reaches the border of the mixed hadron-quark phase; at 1 AGeV it is well inside the mixed phase region

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining the symmetry energy of nuclear matter through heavy ion collisions, building on the framework reviewed by Baran et al. (2005) and Fuchs et al. (2001). The authors connect low-energy studies of isospin transport in deep-inelastic and fragmentation reactions (using the asysoft/asystiff parameterizations from Baran et al.) to relativistic collision studies of collective flows and particle production, where the covariant structure of isovector fields (rho vs. rho+delta) is tested following the work of Liu et al. and Gaitanos et al. (2004). The kaon production analysis extends the isoscalar EoS studies of Fuchs et al. (2001) and Hart et al. (1996) to the isovector sector, while the deconfinement discussion connects to the possibility of quark stars as proposed by Drago et al. (2005) and the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis. The results enable future experiments at facilities like SPIRAL2 and RIA to distinguish between competing effective interaction models through measurements of differential flows, isobar ratios (e.g., 3H vs. 3He), and sub-threshold kaon production.
