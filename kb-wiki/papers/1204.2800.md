# A comment on chiral restoration at finite baryon density in hyperspherical unit cells

> Prompted by recent work of Adhikari, Cohen, Ayyagari and Strother "On chiral symmetry restoration at finite density in large-N_c QCD" (Phys. Rev. C 83, 065201 (2011)), we revisit the description of dense baryonic matter in terms of hyperspherical unit cells. We focus mainly on the interpretation of the unique energy, curvature and symmetry properties which enable such S^3 cells to describe full chiral restoration in Skyrme models and which markedly distinguish them from the flat and periodic unit cells of Skyrmion crystals. These key features clarify, in particular, why an S^3 cell interpretation as a crystal-cell model in which the specific cell geometry is without physical significance, as tentatively adopted by Adhikari et al., is insufficient. The ensuing criticism does therefore not apply to the usual interpretation of S^3 cells which we describe. We also suggest a few directions in which the latter interpretation may be developed further.

## Key claim

The hyperspherical (S³) unit cell geometry is uniquely capable of describing full chiral restoration in Skyrme models due to its specific energy, curvature, and symmetry properties, and the criticism by Adhikari et al. (2011) does not apply to the standard interpretation because it incorrectly treats the cell geometry as physically insignificant.

## Method

The author reviews the mathematical and symmetry-based arguments for why S³ unit cells enable chiral restoration in Skyrme models, focusing on the Bogomol'ny bound saturation by the identity map between S³ cell space and S³ target space, and the extension of the grand-spin subgroup to SO(4) via the hedgehog coupling. The analysis contrasts these properties with flat, periodic unit cells of Skyrmion crystals and examines the implications of spatial averaging in curved versus flat geometries.

## Key numbers

- The critical radius at which the Skyrmion on S³ delocalizes and becomes the energy-minimizing identity map is the radius where the Bogomol'ny bound is saturated; for larger radii the Skyrmion localizes on S³ as on ℝ³.
- Below the critical density, the excitation spectrum includes a triplet of massless Goldstone pions; at and beyond the critical density, the former Goldstone bosons join their three parity partners in a degenerate chiral multiplet whose mass increases with density.
- In the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model on S³ cells, chiral restoration occurs at a critical density consistent with standard values, and the previously massless Goldstone pions disappear.

## Context

This work responds to Adhikari, Cohen, Ayyagari and Strother (2011), who questioned whether S³ cells faithfully model flat Skyrmion crystals and suggested chiral restoration on S³ might be a geometric artifact. The author clarifies that S³ cells were originally introduced by Manton (1986, 1987) as an independent description exploiting the unique SO(4) symmetry matching between cell geometry and chiral dynamics, not as an approximation to crystals. The paper extends earlier work by Forkel (1989) on the half-Skyrmion symmetry and chiral restoration, and suggests that S³ curvature may encode chiral interactions with ambient matter, potentially enabling a variational determination of cell geometry.
