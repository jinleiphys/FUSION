# Spin-orbit correlation and spatial distributions for spin-0 hadrons

> The spin-orbit correlation in spin-0 hadrons can be investigated through the kinetic energy-momentum tensor form factor $\tilde F^q(t)$. We observe that the latter is also related to a torque about the radial direction, which we interpret as a chiral stress. If we neglect the quark mass contribution, then $\tilde F^q(t)$ is simply proportional to the electromagnetic form factor for spin-0 hadrons, and the spin-orbit correlation is equal to minus half of the valence quark number. Given the extensive studies on the electromagnetic form factor for spin-0 hadrons such as pions, kaons, and the $α$ particle, we present the spatial distributions of chiral stress and kinetic spin-orbit correlation based on current parametrizations of the pion electromagnetic form factor.

# Spin-orbit correlation and spatial distributions for spin-0 hadrons

## Key claim
For spin-0 hadrons, the quark spin-orbit correlation is described by the P-odd energy-momentum tensor form factor \(\tilde F^q(t)\) and, if quark mass contributions are neglected, equals minus half of the valence quark number; this relation holds only when the quark mass term \(\mathcal O(m_q/M)\) is dropped, and the result depends on the renormalization scale.

## Method
The authors derive the matrix element of the P-odd energy-momentum tensor for spin-0 hadrons, parametrized by a single form factor \(\tilde F^q(t)\). Using the QCD equation of motion, they relate \(\tilde F^q(t)\) to the vector (electromagnetic) form factor \(F^q(t)\) and the tensor form factor \(H^q(t)\). They then compute three-dimensional spatial distributions of chiral stress and two-dimensional impact-parameter distributions of the kinetic spin-orbit correlation via Fourier transforms of \(\tilde F^q(t)\), using monopole, dipole, and Gaussian parametrizations of the pion electromagnetic form factor from Cui (2021).

## Key numbers
- The spin-orbit correlation for a single quark flavor in a spin-0 hadron is \(C^q_z = -\frac{1}{2} F^q(0)\) when quark mass is neglected, where \(F^q(0)\) is the valence quark number.
- For the \(\alpha\) particle, the sum over flavors gives \(C_z = -6\); for the anti-\(\alpha\) particle, \(C_z = 6\).
- The pion charge radius used is \(r_\pi = 0.659 \pm 0.004\) fm from the Particle Data Group (2020).
- The torque distributions for the \(u\) quark in \(\pi^+\) are concentrated in the region \(0.3\)–\(1.0\) fm for all three parametrizations.
- The impact-parameter distribution of the spin-orbit correlation for \(\pi^+\) concentrates in the region \(b_\perp < 1\) fm, with the area under each curve equal to \(C^u_z = -1/2\).

## Context
This work extends the study of spin-orbit correlations from spin-1/2 hadrons (Lorce 2014) to spin-0 hadrons such as pions, kaons, and the \(\alpha\) particle. It builds on prior parametrizations of the pion electromagnetic form factor by Cui (2021) and the Drell-Yan-West relation (Alberg 2024). The results enable future experimental access to spin-orbit correlations in scalar hadrons through measurements of electromagnetic form factors at JLab 12 GeV CEBAF, the Electron-Ion Collider in the US, and the Electron-ion collider in China, rather than through the more challenging deeply virtual Compton scattering measurements.
