# Low-energy dipole strength and the critical case of 48Ca

> Recent theoretical work has not led to a consensus regarding the nature of the low-energy E1 strength in the 40,44,48Ca isotopes, for which high-resolution (gamma,gamma') data exist. Here we revisit this problem using the first-order quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) and different interactions. First we examine all even Ca isotopes with N=14-40. All isotopes are predicted to undergo dipole transitions at low energy, of large and comparable isoscalar strength but of varying E1 strength. Provided a moderate and uniform energetic shift is introduced to the results, QRPA with the Gogny D1S interaction is able to account for the (gamma,gamma') data, because, up to N=28, it yields a rather pure isoscalar oscillation. A neutron-skin oscillation is anticipated for N larger or equal to 30. This contradicts existing predictions that 44,48Ca develop a neutron-skin mode. Which theoretical result is correct cannot be resolved conclusively using the available data. We propose that alpha-scattering, possibly followed by an electroexcitation experiment, could resolve the situation and thereby help to improve the different models aspiring to describe reliably the low-energy dipole strength of nuclei.

# Low-energy dipole strength and the critical case of 48Ca

## Key claim

QRPA with the Gogny D1S interaction, after applying a moderate and uniform energetic shift of about 3–4 MeV, can account for the observed (γ,γ') data on 40,44,48Ca because it predicts a predominantly isoscalar oscillation for isotopes up to N=28, rather than a neutron-skin mode; this contradicts existing predictions that 44,48Ca develop a neutron-skin mode, but which theoretical result is correct cannot be resolved conclusively using the available data.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov–Quasiparticle-RPA (HFB-QRPA) calculations for all even Ca isotopes with N=14–40, using the Gogny D1S phenomenological interaction and a unitarily-transformed AV18 realistic potential (UCOM(SRG)S,δ3N). For 40,48Ca they also performed Second-RPA (SRPA) calculations with the UCOM-transformed AV18 potential. The isoscalar dipole (ISD) and electric dipole (E1) strength distributions were computed, and transition densities were analyzed to determine the character of the low-energy dipole states.

## Key numbers

- The lowest-lying IS-LED peaks in 40,44,48Ca (N=20,24,28) with Gogny D1S carry IS strength of 832, 860, and 845 fm^6 respectively, and E1 strength of 4.5, 1.6, and 6.0 ×10^{-3} e^2fm^2 respectively.
- Between 48Ca and 50Ca, the IS-LED strength doubles and the E1 strength increases fiftyfold (from 6.0 to 315 ×10^{-3} e^2fm^2).
- The UCOM-SRPA results give E1 strength of 0.021 e^2fm^2 for 40Ca and 0.4 e^2fm^2 for 48Ca for the lowest strongly IS state, both too large compared to data.
- An energetic shift of about 3–4 MeV is required for QRPA with Gogny D1S to reproduce the experimental summed E1 strength below 10 MeV in 40,44,48Ca.
- The GDR peaks for N=20–28 isotopes are found experimentally at roughly 18–21 MeV, which all three models reproduce rather well.

## Context

This work addresses the unresolved theoretical debate about the nature of low-energy E1 strength in Ca isotopes, where high-resolution (γ,γ') data exist from Hartmann et al. (2004). Previous RPA calculations (Czaplinski et al., 1994) predicted E1 strength rising almost linearly with neutron number, while the Extended Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (ETFFS) of Tertychny et al. (2007) reproduced the data accurately but interpreted the 48Ca strength as a neutron-skin mode shifted above 10 MeV by phonon coupling. The present results contradict that interpretation and instead support a predominantly isoscalar character for 48Ca, similar to the collective IS-LED state identified in 40Ca by Papakonstantinou et al. (2011). The authors propose that alpha-scattering experiments on 48Ca, followed by electroexcitation, could resolve which theoretical scenario is correct.
