# Radiative Energy Loss in a Temperature-Evolving QGP with Dynamical Constituents

> We present a theoretical formalism for calculating first-order-in-opacity radiative energy loss that incorporates the spatial and temporal temperature evolution of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in a finite-size QCD medium with dynamical (i.e., moving) constituents. The derived expressions allow for arbitrary temperature profiles, enabling detailed evaluations of radiative energy loss across different medium-evolution scenarios. Importantly, the resulting kernel applies to both single partons (R = 0) and jets (R > 0) via an out-of-cone selection, providing a unified starting point for precision QGP tomography.

## Key claim
The paper derives a first-order-in-opacity radiative energy loss formalism that incorporates spatial and temporal temperature evolution of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) under local thermal equilibrium, applicable to both single partons (R=0) and jets (R>0) via an out-of-cone selection. The authors hedge that the derivation assumes a slowly varying temperature background and local thermal equilibrium, with the temperature profile serving as the only input while all other model parameters are fixed by theoretical considerations or standard literature values.

## Method
The authors extended their previous dynamical energy loss formalism (Djordjevic and Heinz, 2001; Djordjevic, 2009) to include space-time temperature evolution by calculating 24 Feynman diagrams using finite-temperature field theory and Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummed propagators in the Coulomb gauge, under the soft gluon and soft rescattering approximations. The derivation assumes local thermal equilibrium with temperature treated as a slowly varying background field, and generalizes the result to include finite magnetic mass and running coupling effects through a modified effective potential and running coupling constants. The resulting gluon radiation spectrum integrates over proper time along the parton trajectory, with temperature evolution determined by the path through the medium.

## Key numbers
- Transition temperature: T_c = 155 MeV, used as the threshold below which the parton is assumed to have exited the QGP
- Magnetic-to-electric mass ratio: μ_M(T)/μ_E(T) ≈ 0.6, based on lattice QCD results (Borsanyi, 2015)
- Color factors: C_2(G) = 3, C_R = 4/3, D_R = 3 for the high-p⊥ parton colors
- Running coupling defined as α_S(Q^2) = 4π / [(11 - 2/3 N_f) ln(Q^2/Λ_QCD^2)] with Λ_QCD = 0.2 GeV and n_f = 3
- For illustrative calculations: initialization at τ_0 = 0.6 fm and T_0 ≈ 500 MeV in a Bjorken 1D expansion background

## Context
This work extends the dynamical energy loss formalism of Djordjevic and Heinz (2001) and Djordjevic (2009), which assumed a constant-temperature QCD medium with global thermal equilibrium, to incorporate space-time temperature evolution under local thermal equilibrium. The derived kernel forms the theoretical foundation of the DREENA framework (Zigic, 2022; Zigic, 2021; Stojku, 2020; Stojku, 2021; Karmakar, 2024; Karmakar, 2023), which has been applied to high-p⊥ hadron data to extract bulk QGP properties. The formalism enables precision QGP tomography by allowing joint constraints from low- and high-p⊥ observables, and provides a unified description applicable to both parton and jet quenching within a single expression.
