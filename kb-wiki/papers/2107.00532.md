# The unfinished fabric of the three neutrino paradigm

> In the current 3nu paradigm, flavor oscillations probe 3 mixing angles (theta_12, theta_23, theta_13), one CP phase delta, and two squared mass differences delta m^2>0 and Delta m^2, where sign(Delta m^2)=+ (-) for normal (inverted) ordering. Absolute nu masses can be probed by the effective m_beta in beta decay, by the total mass Sigma in cosmology and, if neutrinos are Majorana, by another effective m_{beta beta} in 0nu2beta decay. Within an updated global analysis of (non)oscillation data, we constrain these 3nu parameters, both separately and in selected pairs, and highlight the concordance or discordance among different constraints. Five oscillation parameters (delta m^2, Delta m^2, theta_12, theta_23, theta_13) are consistently measured, with an overall accuracy ranging from ~1% for Delta m^2 to ~6% for sin^2(theta_23) (due to its octant ambiguity). We find overall hints for normal ordering (at 2.5 sigma), as well as for theta_23<pi/4 and for sin(delta)<0 (both at 90% C.L.), and discuss some tensions among datasets. Concerning nonoscillation data, we include the recent KATRIN constraints on m_beta, and we combine the latest 76-Ge, 130-Te and 136-Xe bounds on m_{beta beta}, accounting for NME covariances. We also discuss some variants related to CMB anisotropy and lensing data, which may affect cosmological constraints on Sigma and hints on sign(Delta m^2). The default option, including all Planck results, irrespective of the lensing anomaly, sets upper bounds on Sigma at the level of ~10^-1 eV, and further favors normal ordering up to ~3 sigma. An alternative option, that includes recent ACT results + other independent results (from WMAP and selected Planck data) globally consistent with standard lensing, is insensitive to the ordering but prefers Sigma ~(few) x 10^-1 eV, with different implications for m_beta and m_{beta beta} searches. (Abridged)

## Key claim

Within an updated global analysis of oscillation and nonoscillation data, five oscillation parameters are consistently measured with percent-level accuracy, but the three-neutrino paradigm remains "unfinished" due to unresolved issues including the mass ordering (hinted at 2.5 sigma for normal ordering), the theta_23 octant, the CP phase delta, and the absolute neutrino mass scale, with tensions between T2K and NOvA long-baseline accelerator data affecting all these unknowns simultaneously.

## Method

The authors performed a global frequentist chi-squared analysis of neutrino oscillation data including long-baseline accelerator (T2K, NOvA), solar (Super-Kamiokande-IV 2970-day), long-baseline reactor (KamLAND), short-baseline reactor (Daya Bay, RENO, Double Chooz), and atmospheric neutrino data (Super-Kamiokande-IV 2021 public chi-squared map, IceCube-DeepCore). They combined these with nonoscillation constraints from KATRIN beta-decay bounds on m_beta, neutrinoless double beta decay bounds from 76-Ge, 130-Te and 136-Xe (accounting for nuclear matrix element covariances), and cosmological constraints on Sigma from Planck and ACT CMB data under two different options for handling the A_lens anomaly.

## Key numbers

* Delta m^2 (the larger squared mass splitting) measured with 1.1% formal 1-sigma accuracy, with best fit 2.485 x 10^-3 eV^2 for normal ordering
* delta m^2 (the smaller squared mass splitting) measured with 2.3% formal 1-sigma accuracy, with best fit 7.36 x 10^-5 eV^2
* sin^2(theta_23) measured with ~6% accuracy (dominated by octant ambiguity), best fit 0.455 for normal ordering (lower octant) and 0.569 for inverted ordering (upper octant)
* sin^2(theta_13) measured with ~3% accuracy, best fit 0.0223 for both orderings
* sin^2(theta_12) measured with 4.5% accuracy, best fit 0.303
* Preference for normal over inverted ordering at 2.5 sigma (Delta chi^2 = +6.5 favoring NO)
* Hints for theta_23 < pi/4 and for sin(delta) < 0 both at 90% C.L.
* Default Planck cosmology option sets upper bounds on Sigma at ~10^-1 eV and favors normal ordering up to ~3 sigma
* Alternative option including ACT data prefers Sigma ~ (few) x 10^-1 eV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous global analyses (Capozzi 2017, Capozzi 2020) and is contemporary with similar global fits by de Salas (2020) and Esteban (2020), all based on largely common datasets from the Neutrino 2020 conference. The inclusion of new Super-Kamiokande-IV atmospheric data (publicly released in 2021) shifts the theta_23 octant preference and strengthens the hint for normal ordering compared to those earlier analyses. The paper enables future convergence studies by highlighting tensions between T2K and NOvA accelerator data, and by showing how different treatments of the Planck lensing anomaly lead to different implications for absolute neutrino mass searches in KATRIN, neutrinoless double beta decay experiments, and cosmological probes.
