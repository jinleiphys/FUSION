# Elliptic flow fluctuations in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the perfect fluid hypothesis

> We analyse the recently measured $v_2$ fluctuation in the context of establishing the degree of fluidity of the matter produced in heavy ion collisions. We argue that flow observables within systems with a non-negligible mean free path should acquire a "dynamical" fluctuation, due to the random nature of each collision between the system's degrees of freedom. Because of this, $v_2$ fluctuations can be used to estimate the Knudsen number of the system produced at RHIC. To illustrate this quantitatively, we apply the UrQMD model, with scaled cross sections, to show that collisions at RHIC have a Knudsen number at least one order of magnitude above the expected value for an interacting hadron gas. Furthermore, we argue that the Knudsen number is also bound from above by the $v_2$ fluctuation data, because too large a Knudsen number would break the observed scaling of $v_2$ fluctuations due to the onset of turbulent flow. We propose, therefore that $v_2$ fluctuation measurements, together with an understanding of the turbulent regime for relativistic hydrodynamics, will provide an upper as well as a lower limit for the Knudsen number.

## Key claim

The paper argues that event-by-event fluctuations of the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) can be used to estimate the Knudsen number of the system created in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, providing both an upper and lower bound on how close the system is to a perfect fluid. The authors hedge that while the data suggest a Knudsen number at least one order of magnitude below that of an interacting hadron gas, a quantitative answer requires further modeling of turbulence and hydrodynamic instabilities.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v2.3 transport model with scaled interaction cross sections (factors of 1/2, 1, and 3) to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. They extracted the Knudsen number from the ratio of the average number of particles to the average number of collisions, and computed the relative \(v_2\) fluctuation \(\omega_{v_2} = \sqrt{\langle (\delta v_2)^2 \rangle} / \langle v_2 \rangle\). The scaling of \(\omega_{v_2}\) with the Knudsen number was then extrapolated to compare with experimental data from Loizides and Sorensen.

## Key numbers

- The Knudsen number in UrQMD with default cross sections is in the range \(Kn \sim 0.6\) to \(1.5\).
- Extrapolating the scaling to the upper experimental error bar for \(\omega_{v_2}\) yields a lower bound on the inverse Knudsen number on the order of a hundred (\(Kn^{-1} \sim 100\)).
- For \(T = 200\) MeV, \(L = 10\) fm, and \(\langle v \rangle \sim 1/\sqrt{3}\), the Reynolds number using the ADS/CFT bound \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) is \(Re \sim 100\).
- The Reynolds number from the transport simulations is \(Re \sim 1\).

## Context

This work extends the use of elliptic flow fluctuations, previously measured by Loizides and Sorensen, to constrain the fluidity of the matter created at RHIC. It builds on the scaling of \(v_2\) with eccentricity from ideal hydrodynamics (Heinz, Shuryak, Huovinen) and viscous hydrodynamics (Romatschke), and on the Knudsen number formalism from kinetic theory (Knudsen). The paper proposes that an energy and system-size scan of \(v_2\) fluctuations could differentiate between a smooth approach to the hydrodynamic limit and a phase-transition-driven jump in fluidity, as suggested by the universal scaling of \(v_2/\epsilon\) with multiplicity density (uniscaling1, uniscaling2, uniscaling3, uniscaling4).
