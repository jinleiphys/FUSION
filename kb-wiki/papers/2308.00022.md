# Back-to-back inclusive dijets in DIS at small $x$: Complete NLO results and predictions

> We compute the back-to-back dijet cross-section in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at small $x$ to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. Our result can be factorized into a convolution of the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon transverse momentum dependent distribution function (WW gluon TMD) with a universal soft factor and an NLO coefficient function. The soft factor includes both double and single logarithms in the ratio of the relative transverse momentum $P_\perp$ of the dijet pair to the dijet momentum imbalance $q_\perp$; its renormalization group (RG) evolution is resummed into the Sudakov factor. Likewise, the WW TMD obeys a nonlinear RG equation in $x$ that is kinematically constrained to satisfy both lifetime and rapidity ordering of the projectile. Exact analytical expressions are obtained for the NLO coefficient function of transversely and longitudinally polarized photons. Our results allow for the first quantitative separation of the dynamics of Sudakov suppression from that of gluon saturation. They can be extended to other final states and provide a framework for precision tests of novel QCD many-body dynamics at the Electron-Ion Collider.

# Back-to-back inclusive dijets in DIS at small $x$: Complete NLO results and predictions

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that TMD factorization of the inclusive back-to-back dijet cross-section in deep inelastic scattering at small $x$ persists at next-to-leading order in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, valid to leading power in $q_\perp/P_\perp$ and $Q_s/P_\perp$ and to all orders in $Q_s/q_\perp$. The result allows for the first quantitative separation of Sudakov suppression dynamics from gluon saturation dynamics, though the authors note that the available kinematic window at the Electron-Ion Collider is not large and that further studies are necessary to confirm the potential of this channel.

## Method
The authors compute the one-loop correction to the inclusive back-to-back dijet cross-section in DIS at small $x$ within the CGC EFT, extracting the leading-power contributions in the limit $q_\perp, Q_s \ll P_\perp$. They identify three key components: the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon TMD satisfying a nonlinear renormalization group equation in $x$ with a kinematic constraint enforcing lifetime ordering, double and single Sudakov logarithms of $P_\perp/q_\perp$ resummed into a Sudakov soft factor, and perturbative NLO coefficient functions for transversely and longitudinally polarized photons. The numerical evaluation uses the Gaussian approximation relating the WW TMD to the dipole gluon distribution evolved with the kinematically constrained running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation.

## Key numbers
- Dijet yield computed at $\sqrt{s}=90$ GeV, $Q^2=4$ GeV$^2$, $x_{\rm Bj}=0.55\times 10^{-3}$, with $P_\perp=4-6$ GeV
- The smallest $x$ available for RG evolution is $x_c = (1.5-3.8)\times 10^{-3}$ for $P_\perp=(4-6)$ GeV
- Initial saturation scale for minimum bias: $Q_{s0,A}^2 = A^{1/3} \times 0.1$ GeV$^2$ at $x_0=2.5\times 10^{-2}$
- Infrared regulator $\Lambda = 0.24$ GeV
- The $v_2$ coefficient magnitude is less than $2\%$
- Power correction uncertainties of $\mathcal{O}(10\%)$ for $q_\perp \lesssim 1.5$ GeV and $P_\perp=4$ GeV
- Sudakov suppression is very significant for $q_\perp \lesssim 1.5$ GeV
- The Cronin enhancement at LO is washed out by Sudakov corrections alone at NLO

## Context
This work extends the LO TMD factorization result for back-to-back dijets in DIS at small $x$ established by Dominguez et al. (2011) to NLO accuracy, incorporating both Sudakov logarithms previously studied in collinear factorization by Hatta et al. (2021) and small-$x$ evolution via the JIMWLK/BK equations. The kinematic constraint on the WW TMD evolution implements lifetime ordering understood from earlier work by Andersson et al. (1995), Kwiecinski et al. (1996), and others. The results provide a framework for precision tests of gluon saturation at the future Electron-Ion Collider, and can be extended to di-hadron final states and combined with other small-$x$ processes studied by Boussarie et al. (2016), Beuf et al. (2020), and others for global analyses.
