# Pauli Blocking effects in Nilsson states of weakly bound exotic nuclei

> The description of weakly bound nuclei using deformed few-body models has proven to be crucial in the study of reactions involving certain exotic nuclei. However, these core+valence models face the challenge of applying the Pauli exclusion principle, since the factorisation of the system does not allow complete antisymmetrization. Therefore, states occupied by core nucleons should be blocked for the valence nucleons. We aim to study $^{17}$C and $^{19}$C, which are good examples of weakly bound exotic nuclei with significant deformation where the valence shell is partially filled. The structure of $^{17}$C and $^{19}$C is described with deformed two-body models where a Nilsson Hamiltonian is constructed using Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamic calculations of the cores. Different methods of blocking occupied Nilsson states are considered using the Bardeen$-$Cooper$-$Schrieffer formalism: without blocking, total blocking and partial blocking. The latter also takes into account pair correlations to some extent. These models are later used to study $^{16}$C$(d,p)^{17}$C, $^{17}$C$(p,d)^{16}$C and $^{18}$C$(d,p)^{19}$C transfer reactions within the Adiabatic Distorted Wave Approximation. In the first case, the results are compared with experimental data. A good reproduction of the structure of $^{17}$C is found, significantly improving the agreement in the $^{16}$C$(d,p)^{17}$C reaction including blocking effects. The $^{19}$C spectrum is better reproduced considering blocking, in particular, the partial blocking method that considers the pairing interaction provides the best description. Promising results are shown for the study of transfer reactions involving weakly bound exotic nuclei, by highlighting the effect of blocking occupied Nilsson states. We envision to extend the models to the study of breakup reactions and to newly discovered halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including Pauli blocking effects in deformed two-body models significantly improves the reproduction of nuclear structure and transfer reaction cross sections for weakly bound exotic nuclei, but the specific ordering of bound states depends on which blocking method is used and none perfectly matches experimental level ordering.

## Method

The authors construct a deformed two-body model (NAMD) combining Nilsson and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) approaches, where a valence neutron moves in a deformed potential generated by the core nucleus. They implement three blocking methods using the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) formalism: no blocking (WB), total blocking (TB) where the lowest Nilsson states are fully occupied, and partial blocking (PB) which includes pairing correlations. The resulting wavefunctions are used to calculate transfer reaction cross sections within the Adiabatic Distorted Wave Approximation (ADWA) for the reactions 16C(d,p)17C, 17C(p,d)16C, and 18C(d,p)19C.

## Key numbers

- For 17C, the pairing gap from the PB method is 1.58 MeV, close to the three-point formula value of 1.76 MeV for the 16C system
- The Fermi level in the PB calculation for 17C is found at -4.13 MeV
- The deformation length for 17C is delta = 1.27 fm (prolate)
- The core Hamiltonian parameter for 17C is hbar/2J = 0.295 MeV, compatible with the 16C 2+ excitation energy of 1.766 MeV
- The pairing strength G = 1.3 MeV is used between the four bound Nilsson states for 17C
- The spectroscopic factor for the 5/2+1 state of 17C improves from 0.33 (WB) to 0.66 (TB) and 0.65 (PB), compared to the experimental value of 0.62 +/- 0.13
- For 19C, the core Hamiltonian parameter is hbar/2J = 0.25 MeV, compatible with the 18C 2+ excitation energy of 1.588 MeV

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic PAMD model of Lay (2014) and the Nilsson model comparison of Punta (2023) by incorporating Pauli blocking effects through BCS theory applied to deformed Nilsson states, following the total blocking approach recently proposed by Shin (2024) for spherical states. The results enable improved predictions for transfer reactions involving weakly bound exotic nuclei and provide a framework that can be extended to breakup reactions and newly discovered halo nuclei, with the RGM calculations of Chien (2023) serving as a microscopic reference for comparison.
