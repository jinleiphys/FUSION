# Mesons in Nuclei in the Light-Front Mean Field Approximation and Deep-Inelastic Scattering

> A light-front treatment for the scalar and vector meson momentum distribution functions is developed using a model in which the nucleus is treated a static source of radius $R$. The limit $R\to \infty$ corresponds to infinite nuclear matter. In this limit, the scalar mesons are shown to carry a vanishing momentum fraction of the nuclear plus momentum $k^+$, but the vector mesons carry a significant fraction, all occuring at the experimentally inaccessible value of $k^+=0$. This confirms earlier work. A study of the $R$ dependence for sizes of real nuclei shows that a fraction of the scalar and vector mesons could be observable.

## Key claim

Scalar mesons in infinite nuclear matter carry a vanishing fraction of the nuclear plus-momentum, while vector mesons carry a significant fraction (about 35% of the nuclear momentum), but all of this vector meson momentum resides at the experimentally inaccessible value of \(k^+=0\). For finite nuclei of realistic size, a fraction of both scalar and vector mesons could be observable, contrary to the infinite-matter limit.

## Method

The authors model the nucleus as a static source of radius \(R\) and mass \(M_A\) coupled to scalar and vector meson fields via a mean-field approximation. They construct coherent ground states for the meson fields in both the rest-frame (equal-time) and light-front formulations, then compute the plus-momentum distribution functions \(f_S(x)\) and \(f_V(x)\) for scalar and vector mesons respectively. The limit \(R \to \infty\) corresponds to infinite nuclear matter, and the finite-\(R\) results are evaluated for nuclear radii of 3, 4, 5, and 6 fm.

## Key numbers

- For scalar mesons in the infinite nuclear matter limit, the ratio \(\langle k^+ \rangle / (v^+ |E_0|) = 1/(2 m_S R) \approx 1/20\) using \(m_S = 550\) MeV, showing the plus-momentum grows as surface area (\(R^2\)) while binding energy grows as volume (\(R^3\)).
- The scalar meson momentum fraction per nucleon \(\langle x_S \rangle = (g_S \rho_S)^2 / (\rho_B m_S^3) \cdot 3/(4 R M_N)\) in the limit \(m_S R \gg 1\), vanishing for infinite nuclear matter.
- For vector mesons in infinite nuclear matter, the momentum fraction \(\langle x_V \rangle = 0.348\), using parameters \(g_V^2 M_N^2 / m_V^2 = 195.9\) and baryon density \(\rho_B = 0.193\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The vector meson distribution \(x f_V(x)\) approaches a delta function \(\delta(x)\) in the infinite-matter limit, with the integrated strength \(\lim_{R\to\infty} \int_0^{x_U} dx\, x f_V(x) = (g_V/m_V)^2 \rho_B / M_N\).

## Context

This work extends the light-front treatment of nuclear matter by Burkardt and Miller (1997) to finite nuclei, addressing whether the earlier infinite-matter result, that meson momentum resides at \(k^+=0\), is an artifact of infinite size. The EMC effect (European Muon Collaboration, 1983) showed that parton distributions in nuclei differ from free nucleons, and meson-based explanations predicted enhanced antiquark distributions in Drell-Yan experiments that were not observed (Drell-Yan experiments, 1980s). The present results show that for finite nuclei, a fraction of mesons may be observable, potentially resolving the discrepancy between meson-exchange models and the experimental absence of enhanced antiquark distributions. The approach enables future calculations of nuclear structure functions using light-front methods that incorporate finite-size effects.
