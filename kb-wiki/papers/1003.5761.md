# Magic radioactivity of 252Cf

> We show that the sharp maximum corresponding to 107Mo in the fragment distribution of the 252Cf cold fission is actually a Sn-like radioactivity, similar to other decay processes in which magic nuclei are involved, namely alpha-decay and heavy cluster emission, also called Pb-like radioactivity. It turns out that the mass asymmetry degree of freedom has a key role in connecting initial Sn with the final Mo isotopes along the fission path. We suppose the cold rearrangement of nucleons within the framework of the two center shell model, in order to compute the cold valleys in the charge equilibrated fragmentation potential. The fission yields are estimated by using the semiclassical penetration approach. We consider five degrees of freedom, namely the inter-fragment distance, the shapes of fragments, the neck parameter and mass asymmetry. # Magic radioactivity of 252Cf

## Key claim

The sharp maximum at 107Mo in the cold fission fragment distribution of 252Cf is actually a Sn-like radioactivity, analogous to alpha-decay and heavy cluster emission (Pb-like radioactivity), where the mass asymmetry degree of freedom connects the initial Sn configuration to the final Mo isotopes along the fission path. The authors show that the inner cold valley of the total potential energy is connected to the double magic isotope 132Sn, and that the agreement with experimental yields depends on the fission path proceeding through a saddle configuration involving the magic numbers Z=50 and N=82.

## Method

The authors computed the cold fission path in the potential energy surface of 252Cf using the two-center shell model with a Woods-Saxon potential, considering five degrees of freedom: inter-fragment distance, fragment shapes (eccentricities), neck parameter, and mass asymmetry. The deformation energy was calculated within a microscopic-macroscopic method (Yukawa-plus-exponential model plus Strutinsky shell corrections), and the fission yields were estimated using the semiclassical penetration approach (WKB approximation) with cranking-model inertia. The adiabatic barrier in the multidimensional configuration space was determined by minimizing the action integral along the fission trajectory.

## Key numbers

- The second barrier top is located at elongation R = 11 fm and corresponds to a mass A2 ≈ 120, with the heavy fragment having A1 - Z1 = 81 and Z1 = 51 (close to magic numbers)
- The maximum theoretical penetrability and the maximum experimental yield both occur at A2 = 107 (107Mo)
- The selected experimental channels address binary partitions with light fragments ranging from 95Rb to 122Ag, with a maximum excitation energy of 7 MeV
- The step between equipotential lines in the deformation energy plots is 1 MeV
- For A2 < 110 partitions, a double-humped barrier occurs; for more symmetric channels (A2 > 110), a triple-humped barrier is obtained

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of cold fission in 252Cf, which were performed within the double folding potential method (Sandulescu 1996, 1998) and a macroscopic model (Gonnenwein 1991) that predicted a major contribution at A2 ≈ 100, contradicting experimental data. The authors extend the analysis from Delion (2007) to a more reliable microscopic approach based on a new version of the Super Asymmetric Two Center Shell Model (Mirea 2008), which was previously used to describe fusion/fission of superheavy elements. The work connects cold fission to the same class of decay processes as alpha-decay and heavy cluster emission (Pb-like radioactivity), proposing the term "Sn-like radioactivity" and grouping these as kappa (cluster) decays.
