# $Λ_c \to Λ\ell^+ ν_\ell$ form factors and decay rates from lattice QCD with physical quark masses

> The first lattice QCD calculation of the form factors governing $Λ_c \to Λ\ell^+ ν_\ell$ decays is reported. The calculation was performed with two different lattice spacings and includes one ensemble with a pion mass of 139(2) MeV. The resulting predictions for the $Λ_c \to Λe^+ ν_e$ and $Λ_c \to Λμ^+ ν_μ$ decay rates divided by $|V_{cs}|^2$ are $0.2007(71)(74)\:{\rm ps}^{-1}$ and $0.1945(69)(72)\:{\rm ps}^{-1}$, respectively, where the two uncertainties are statistical and systematic. Taking the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element $|V_{cs}|$ from a global fit and the $Λ_c$ lifetime from experiments, this translates to branching fractions of $\mathcal{B}(Λ_c\toΛe^+ν_e)=0.0380(19)_{\rm LQCD\:\:}(11)_{τ_{Λ_c}}$ and $\mathcal{B}(Λ_c\toΛμ^+ν_μ)=0.0369(19)_{\rm LQCD\:\:}(11)_{τ_{Λ_c}}$. These results are consistent with, and two times more precise than, the measurements performed recently by the BESIII Collaboration. Using instead the measured branching fractions together with the lattice calculation to determine the CKM matrix element gives $|V_{cs}|= 0.949(24)_{\rm LQCD\:\:}(14)_{τ_{Λ_c}}(49)_{\mathcal{B}}$.

## Key claim
This paper reports the first lattice QCD calculation of the form factors governing \(\Lambda_c \to \Lambda \ell^+ \nu_\ell\) decays, performed with two different lattice spacings and including one ensemble with a physical pion mass of 139(2) MeV. The resulting predictions for the decay rates are consistent with, and two times more precise than, the measurements recently reported by the BESIII Collaboration.

## Method
The calculation used \(2+1\) flavor domain-wall fermion gauge configurations from the RBC and UKQCD collaborations, with the charm quark implemented via an anisotropic clover action. Form factors were extracted from ratios of three-point and two-point correlation functions, with extrapolations to infinite source-sink separation to isolate ground-state contributions, and then fitted using modified \(z\)-expansions that included terms describing the dependence on lattice spacing, pion mass, and \(\eta_s\) mass to obtain results in the physical limit.

## Key numbers
- \(\Gamma(\Lambda_c \to \Lambda e^+ \nu_e) / |V_{cs}|^2 = 0.2007(71)(74)\ {\rm ps}^{-1}\), where the two uncertainties are statistical and systematic.
- \(\Gamma(\Lambda_c \to \Lambda \mu^+ \nu_\mu) / |V_{cs}|^2 = 0.1945(69)(72)\ {\rm ps}^{-1}\), where the two uncertainties are statistical and systematic.
- \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to \Lambda e^+ \nu_e) = 0.0380(19)_{\rm LQCD}(11)_{\tau_{\Lambda_c}}\), using \(|V_{cs}| = 0.97344(15)\) from CKM unitarity and \(\tau_{\Lambda_c} = 0.200(6)\ {\rm ps}\).
- \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to \Lambda \mu^+ \nu_\mu) = 0.0369(19)_{\rm LQCD}(11)_{\tau_{\Lambda_c}}\), using the same inputs.
- \(|V_{cs}| = 0.949(24)_{\rm LQCD}(14)_{\tau_{\Lambda_c}}(49)_{\mathcal{B}}\) from the correlated average over \(\ell = e, \mu\), combining the lattice calculation with BESIII branching fraction measurements.

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD methodology previously applied to \(\Lambda_b \to p\) and \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\) form factors by Detmold, Meinel, and others (2015, 2016) to the charmed-baryon sector, providing the first nonperturbative determination of the \(\Lambda_c \to \Lambda\) form factors. The results enable a new direct determination of \(|V_{cs}|\) from baryonic decays, consistent with CKM unitarity, and provide a stringent test of the lattice methods used for heavy-baryon decay calculations; further improvement in precision will come from more accurate measurements of the \(\Lambda_c \to \Lambda \ell^+ \nu_\ell\) branching fractions by experiments such as BESIII.
