# J/Psi Production by Charm Quark Coalescence

> Production of $c\bar c$ pairs in elementary hadron-hadron collisions is introduced in a simulation of relativistic heavy ion collisions. Coalescence of charmed quarks and antiquarks into various charmonium states is performed and the results are compared to PHENIX J$/ψ$ Au+Au data. The $χ$ and $ψ$' bound states must be included as well as the ground state J$/ψ$, given the appreciable feeding from the excited states down to the J$/ψ$ via gamma decays. Charmonium coalescence is found to take place at relatively late times: generally after $c$($\bar c$)-medium interactions have ceased. Direct production of charmonia through hadron-hadron interactions, {\it ie.} without explicit presence of charm quarks, occurring only at early times, is suppressed by collisions with comoving particles and accounts for some $\sim 5\%$ of the total J$/ψ$ production. Coalescence is especially sensitive to the level of open charm production, scaling naively as $n_{c\bar c}^2$. The J$/ψ$ transverse momentum distribution is dependent on the charm quark transverse momentum distribution and early charm quark-medium interaction, thus providing a glimpse of the initial collision history.

# J/Psi Production by Charm Quark Coalescence

## Key claim
Charmonium coalescence is the dominant mechanism for J/ψ production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, accounting for approximately 95% of the total yield, while direct production via hadron-hadron interactions contributes only about 5%. The coalescence occurs at relatively late times, after charm quark interactions with the medium have ceased, and the J/ψ yield is enhanced by more than a factor of two through feeding from excited χ and ψ' states.

## Method
The authors used the LUCIFER two-stage simulation, which models ion-ion collisions by early production of pre-hadrons followed by a relativistic cascade, and introduced explicit charm quarks produced via hadronic collisions in the initial phase. Coalescence of c and c̄ pairs into charmonium states (J/ψ, χ, ψ') was calculated using an afterburner algorithm that evaluates the quantum mechanical overlap probability between incoming charm quark wavepackets and harmonic oscillator approximations to Cornell group charmonium wavefunctions, using measured open charm production cross-sections and charmonium decay properties as input.

## Key numbers
- Direct production accounts for approximately 5% of total J/ψ production in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- Charmonium production scales as approximately n_{c\bar c}^{2.5}, where n_{c\bar c} is the number of charm quarks produced per ion-ion collision
- The PHENIX pp cross-section used is σ^{pp}_{c\bar c} = 0.57 ± 0.21 mb at √s = 200 GeV
- The STAR cross-section from D+Au measurement is σ^{NN}_{c\bar c} = 1.40 ± 0.22 mb, and from Au+Au is 1.11 ± 0.43 mb
- RMS radii for charmonium states from Cornell group: J/ψ = 0.47 fm, χ = 0.74 fm, ψ' = 0.96 fm
- Oscillator radius parameters used: r₀(ψ) = 0.384 fm, r₀(χ) = 0.49 fm, r₀(ψ') = 0.51 fm
- c̄c pairs with relative momentum |p̄| ≥ 500 MeV/c are hindered from merging into charmonium
- Direct production yield is only about 5% of coalescent yield for central collisions
- Pre-hadron decay time at rest is approximately 1.0 fm/c
- Pre-meson/pre-meson cross-section is approximately 20-25 mb

## Context
This work extends the LUCIFER simulation framework previously applied to deuteron production at the AGS (Kahana and Kahana, 1999) and to J/ψ suppression at the SPS (Kahana and Kahana, 1999; Phys. Rev. C 59, 1651). It contrasts with the SPS regime where comover suppression of directly produced charmonia was sufficient to explain observed J/ψ suppression; at RHIC, coalescence becomes the dominant mechanism. The approach is related to the coalescence ideas of Molnar and Voloshin (Phys. Rev. Lett. 91, 092301) for explaining elliptic flow, and uses charmonium wavefunctions from the Cornell group (Eichten et al., Phys. Rev. D 17, 3090; Phys. Rev. D 21, 203). The results enable using J/ψ transverse momentum distributions as a probe of the initial collision environment and early charm quark-medium interactions, and could be extended to bottomonia production at LHC energies.
