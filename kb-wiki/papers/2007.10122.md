# Lifetime of the hypertriton

> We calculate the lifetime of the hypertriton as function of the $Λ$ separation energy $B_Λ$ in an effective field theory with $Λ$ and deuteron degrees of freedom. We also consider the impact of new measurements of the weak decay parameter of the $Λ$. While the sensitivity of the total width to $B_Λ$ is small, the partial widths for decays into individual final states and the experimentally measured ratio $R=Γ_{^3\textrm{He}}/\left(Γ_{^3 \text{He}}+Γ_{pd}\right)$ show a strong dependence. For the standard value $B_Λ=(0.13\pm 0.05)$ MeV, we find $R=0.37\pm 0.05$, which is in good agreement with past experimental studies and theoretical calculations. For the recent STAR value $B_Λ=(0.41\pm0.12\pm0.11)$ MeV, we obtain $R=0.57\pm 0.11$.

## Key claim

The hypertriton lifetime puzzle can be addressed by treating the hypertriton as a weakly-bound Lambda-deuteron two-body system in pionless effective field theory, where the total width shows only a small sensitivity to the Lambda separation energy but the partial widths and the experimentally measured ratio R depend strongly on this parameter.

## Method

The authors constructed a pionless effective field theory with Lambda and deuteron degrees of freedom, treating the hypertriton as a two-body bound state. They calculated the mesonic decay widths for nucleon-deuteron and trinucleon final states, including final-state interactions between the outgoing nucleon and deuteron in the S=1/2 and S=3/2 channels, while fixing the weak interaction vertex using the free Lambda lifetime and decay parameter.

## Key numbers

- For the standard Lambda separation energy B_Lambda = (0.13 +/- 0.05) MeV, the ratio R = Gamma_He3 / (Gamma_He3 + Gamma_pd) = 0.37 +/- 0.05
- For the recent STAR value B_Lambda = (0.41 +/- 0.12 +/- 0.11) MeV, the ratio R = 0.57 +/- 0.11
- The expansion parameter of the effective theory is 0.15 for B_Lambda = 0.13 MeV, 0.25 for B_Lambda = 0.41 MeV, and reaches 0.5 for B_Lambda = 1.5 MeV
- The free Lambda lifetime used is tau_Lambda = 263.2 ps
- The deuteron binding momentum is gamma_d = 45.68 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory approach previously applied to hypertriton structure by Hildenbrand and Hammer (2019) and Hammer (2001) to the decay problem, addressing the "hypertriton puzzle" where experimental lifetime measurements range from about 100 ps to near the free Lambda value of 260 ps. The calculation improves upon the closure approximation used by Congleton (1992) and complements three-body Faddeev calculations by Kamada (1997) and the NCSM approach of Perez-Obiol (2020), while enabling future inclusion of pionic final-state interactions and extension to heavier hypernuclei.
