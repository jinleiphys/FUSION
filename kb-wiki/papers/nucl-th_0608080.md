# Effects of the neutrino electromagnetic form factors on the neutrino and antineutrino mean free paths in dense matter

> We have studied the effects of the nucleon weak magnetism and the neutrino electromagnetic properties on the neutrino and antineutrino mean free paths for all types of neutrinos. In the calculation, we consider matters with and without neutrino trapping as the target. We have found that the difference between the two mean free paths depends not only on the neutrino energy, but also on the matter density as well as matter constituents. Compared to the nucleon weak magnetism, the neutrino electromagnetic properties are found to have negligible effects on this difference.

## Key claim

The difference between neutrino and antineutrino mean free paths in dense matter depends on matter density, neutrino energy, and matter constituents; however, the contribution from neutrino electromagnetic form factors (charge radius and dipole moment) is negligible compared to the contribution from nucleon weak magnetism, even when using relatively large values for the neutrino electromagnetic properties.

## Method

The neutrino and antineutrino mean free paths were calculated by integrating differential cross sections over momentum transfer, using a linear response formalism with zero temperature approximation and a Fermi gas model for leptons. The interacting nucleons were described by a relativistic mean field model inspired by effective field theory, applied to non-strange dense matter both with and without neutrino trapping. The cross section difference was derived from a Lagrangian density that includes both weak and electromagnetic interaction vertices, with the weak magnetism of nucleons and neutrino electromagnetic form factors (charge radius and dipole moment) incorporated.

## Key numbers

- Experimental upper limits on neutrino dipole moments: electron-neutrino < 1.5 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (Beacom 1999), muon-neutrino < 7.4 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (Krakower 1990), tau-neutrino < 5.4 × 10⁻⁷ μ_B (Cooper-Sarkar 1992)
- Neutrino charge radii: electron-neutrino R = (0.54 ± 0.82) × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻¹, muon-neutrino R < 0.40 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻¹ (Vilain 1995)
- Astrophysical limit on neutrino magnetic moment: (1–4) × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (Hagiwara 2002)
- Globular-cluster star limits: μ_ν < 3 × 10⁻¹² μ_B and e_ν < 2 × 10⁻¹⁴ e (Raffelt 1999)
- Neutrino electromagnetic properties become significant for μ_ν > 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B and charge radius R > 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻¹
- For muon-neutrinos at 150 MeV in neutrinoless matter, the mean free path difference significantly increases at low densities
- For tau-neutrinos, the relatively large dipole moment used makes the mean free path difference almost negligible

## Context

This work extends the study by Horowitz and Garcia (2005), who found that muon-antineutrino mean free paths in high density matter are considerably larger than muon-neutrino ones when nucleon weak magnetism is included. The authors also build on their own previous work (Sulaksono 2006) on the sensitivity of differential cross sections to neutrino electromagnetic properties. The results enable future studies to focus on nucleon weak magnetism as the dominant mechanism for neutrino-antineutrino mean free path differences in dense matter, while confirming that neutrino electromagnetic form factors can be safely neglected in such calculations.
