# Study of excited nucleons and their structure

> Recent advances in the study of excited nucleons are discussed. Much of the progress has been achieved due to the availability of high precision meson production data in the photoproduction and electroproduction sectors, the development of multi-channel partial wave analysis techniques, and advances in Lattice QCD with predictions of the full excitation spectrum.

## Key claim

Recent progress in understanding excited nucleons has been driven by high-precision meson production data, multi-channel partial wave analysis, and Lattice QCD predictions, but the identification of new states remains conditional on the inclusion of strangeness photoproduction data and the resolution of broad, overlapping resonances through complete or nearly complete experiments.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical advances in nucleon resonance (N*) physics, focusing on photoproduction and electroproduction of mesons (e.g., π, η, K, ω, φ) on proton and neutron targets, analyzed via single-channel and coupled-channel partial wave analyses. Transition form factors for resonances such as Δ(1232)3/2+, N(1440)1/2+, N(1520)3/2−, and N(1535)1/2− were extracted from electroproduction data using Unitary Isobar Models and Dispersion Relations. Lattice QCD predictions and hybrid baryon models were compared to experimental helicity amplitudes to probe the nature of excited states.

## Key numbers

- The N(1900)3/2+ state has a Breit-Wigner mass of ≈ 1900 MeV and was upgraded to a *** state in the 2012 Review of Particle Physics (RPP) edition, based on coupled-channel analysis including CLAS data on γp → K+Λ.
- Two new N* candidates were found in ψ′ → p p̄ π° decays at BESIII, with masses at 2300 MeV and 2570 MeV.
- Transition form factors for the Roper N(1440)1/2+ state show the A1/2 amplitude drops quickly to 0 and the longitudinal amplitude S1/2 = 0 in leading order, ruling out a hybrid baryon interpretation.
- The transition to elementary quark-gluon degrees of freedom is expected at Q² ≥ 6 GeV², reachable only at the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade.

## Context

This review extends the work of Crede (2013), Aznauryan (2012), Tiator (2011), and Klempt (2009) by summarizing recent results from CLAS on strangeness photoproduction and from BESIII on charmonium decays, which have led to new or upgraded resonance ratings in the 2012 RPP. The paper enables future coupled-channel analyses incorporating vector meson photoproduction data (e.g., γp → pω, pφ) and electroproduction at higher Q² to test hybrid baryon predictions from Lattice QCD (Dudek, 2012) and quark models (Li, 1991).
