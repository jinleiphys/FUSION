# The Sphaleron Rate from 4D Euclidean Lattices

> We develop a new method to determine thermal activation rates, such as for bubble nucleation, topology change, \textsl{etc.}, using 4-dimensional Euclidean methods. This allows nonperturbative study on the lattice. We then investigate the strong sphaleron rate in pure-glue QCD at temperatures between 1.3 $\Tc$ and 1000 $T_\mathrm{c}$, making contact with previous results but extending them down close to the critical temperature. The extension to full QCD will be straightforward. Limitations of the proposal (the inability to compute a certain dynamical prefactor, puzzling large-volume behavior, and the inability to treat temperatures $T < 1.3 \, T_\mathrm{c}$) are also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors develop a new Euclidean lattice method for computing the strong sphaleron rate in pure-glue QCD that works for temperatures down to 1.3 \(T_c\), but the method cannot determine the dynamical prefactor \(\mathcal{d}\) (assumed close to 1), exhibits puzzling large-volume behavior, and fails below \(T = 1.3\,T_c\).

## Method

The method adapts Langer's thermal activation theory to QCD by identifying the separatrix between topological sectors using 3D gradient flow on Euclidean time slices. The sphaleron rate is extracted from the fraction of lattice configurations where the Chern-Simons number evaluated at \(t=0\) and \(t=\beta/2\) (with gradient-flow continuation to vacuum) differ by an integer, multiplied by \(2T\). The authors perform pure-glue SU(3) lattice simulations across a range of temperatures, checking for lattice spacing, volume, and gradient-flow depth dependence.

## Key numbers

* The strong sphaleron rate is computed for pure-glue QCD at temperatures between \(1.3\,T_c\) and \(1000\,T_c\), extending previous results down close to the critical temperature.
* The method breaks down below \(T = 1.3\,T_c\) (the critical temperature of pure-glue QCD).
* The pseudocritical temperature for full QCD is given as \(T_{pc} \simeq 155\) MeV (citing Borsanyi (2013) and HotQCD (2014)).
* At \(T = 2\,T_{pc}\), the quark number susceptibility is within 10% of the leading-order value (citing Bazavov (2013)).
* The electroweak sphaleron rate was determined using classical-field methods with coupling \(g^2 N/4\pi \simeq 1/15\), while at \(T \sim 1000\,T_{pc}\) the strong coupling is \(g^2 N/4\pi \sim 0.3\).
* Previous lattice estimates at \(T = 100\) GeV \(\sim 700\,T_{pc}\) were no better than factor-of-2 (citing Moore (2010)).

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative determination of the strong sphaleron rate from earlier lattice attempts by Moore (1997) and Moore (2010), which only achieved factor-of-2 estimates at high temperatures and did not reach near \(T_c\). The method is inspired by Langer's thermal activation theory (1969) and the Affleck-Linde formalism (1980, 1981), and adapts the Arnold-McLerran pendulum toy model (1987) to QCD. The approach enables future extension to full QCD with dynamical fermions, which the authors state will be straightforward, and could inform studies of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions and baryogenesis scenarios.
