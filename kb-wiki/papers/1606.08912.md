# Studying the P_c(4450) resonance in J/psi photoproduction off protons

> A resonance-like structure, the P_c(4450), has recently been observed in the J/psi p spectrum by the LHCb collaboration. We discuss the feasibility of detecting this structure in J/psi photoproduction in the CLAS12 experiment at JLab. We present a first estimate of the upper limit for the branching ratio of the P_c(4450) to J/psi p. Our estimates, which take into account the experimental resolution effects, predict that it will be possible to observe a sizable cross section close to the J/psi production threshold and shed light on the P_c(4450) resonance in the future photoproduction measurements.

## Key claim
The paper estimates that the P_c(4450) resonance, observed by LHCb in hadronic production, could be detectable in J/psi photoproduction at CLAS12, but only if the experimental energy resolution is sufficiently fine; the resonance peak is narrow enough that it could have escaped previous detection due to poor energy resolution.

## Method
The authors model J/psi photoproduction as a coherent sum of a nonresonant diffractive background (parametrized by an effective Pomeron exchange) and a resonant s-channel contribution from the P_c(4450) described by a Breit-Wigner amplitude. They fit this model to 137 existing differential cross section data points from ZEUS, H1, and SLAC experiments, covering photon energies from threshold to 120 TeV, using a bootstrap technique with 10^4 pseudo-data sets to extract parameter uncertainties and upper limits. The vector-meson dominance (VMD) assumption relates the photon couplings to the hadronic decay couplings, and experimental resolution effects are incorporated by Gaussian smearing of the three lowest-energy data points.

## Key numbers
- Upper limit on branching ratio B(P_c → J/psi p) at 95% CL: ≤ 29% (J=3/2, no smearing), ≤ 30% (J=3/2, σ_s=60 MeV), ≤ 23% (J=3/2, σ_s=120 MeV); ≤ 17% (J=5/2, no smearing), ≤ 12% (J=5/2, σ_s=60 MeV), ≤ 8% (J=5/2, σ_s=120 MeV)
- Upper limit on radiative width Γ_γ: ≤ 14.4 keV (J=3/2, no smearing), ≤ 11.0 keV (J=3/2, σ_s=120 MeV); ≤ 56.9 keV (J=5/2, no smearing), ≤ 26.8 keV (J=5/2, σ_s=120 MeV)
- Upper limit on photocouplings A_{1/2,3/2}: ≤ 0.007 GeV^{-1/2} (J=3/2, no smearing); ≤ 0.017 GeV^{-1/2} (J=5/2, no smearing)
- Upper limit on total cross section at resonance energy: ≤ 120 nb (J=3/2, no smearing), ≤ 14 nb (J=3/2, σ_s=120 MeV); ≤ 396 nb (J=5/2, no smearing), ≤ 14 nb (J=5/2, σ_s=120 MeV)
- P_c(4450) mass: 4449.8 ± 1.7 ± 2.5 MeV; width: 39 ± 5 ± 19 MeV (from LHCb, used as input)

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Karliner and Rosner (2015) to use photoproduction to confirm the pentaquark candidate observed by LHCb (Aaij et al., 2015), applying the resonance model of Mokeev et al. (2012) originally developed for N* electroproduction. The paper provides the first quantitative upper limits on the P_c(4450) → J/psi p branching ratio from photoproduction data, enabling future CLAS12 measurements to either confirm or constrain the resonant interpretation of the LHCb structure.
