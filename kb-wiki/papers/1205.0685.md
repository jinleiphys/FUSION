# Sensitivity of astrophysical reaction rates to nuclear uncertainties

> Sensitivities of nuclear reaction rates to a variation of nuclear properties are studied. Target nuclei range from proton- to neutron-dripline for 10<=Z<=83. Reactions considered are nucleon- and alpha-induced reactions mediated by the strong interaction. The contribution of reactions proceeding on the target ground state to the total stellar rate is also given. General dependences on various input quantities are discussed. Additionally, sensitivities of laboratory cross sections of nucleon-, alpha-, and gamma-induced reactions are shown, allowing to estimate the impact of cross section measurements. Finally, recommended procedures to explore and improve reaction rate uncertainties using the present sensitivity data are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper provides sensitivity tables for astrophysical reaction rates and laboratory cross sections to variations in nuclear input quantities (widths and level densities), but explicitly states that this approach cannot yield a full quantification of theoretical uncertainties because it still assumes the chosen theoretical model is correct, and the fundamental uncertainty of model choice itself cannot be quantified.

## Method

The author computed sensitivities of stellar reaction rates and laboratory cross sections for nucleon- and alpha-induced reactions (mediated by the strong interaction) using version 0.8.3 of the SMARAGD code, which implements the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model. The sensitivity \(^\Omega s_q\) was defined as the relative change in the final result \(\Omega\) divided by the relative change in the varied quantity \(q\), with the variation factor set to \(v_q = 2\). Target nuclei ranged from proton- to neutron-dripline for \(10 \leq Z \leq 83\), and the varied quantities were neutron-, proton-, alpha-, and gamma-widths as well as nuclear level density.

## Key numbers

- Sensitivity tables cover 24 temperatures from 0.1 GK to 10.0 GK for each reaction.
- The ground state contribution \(X\) to the stellar rate is defined such that \(X = 0.3\) implies 70% of the stellar rate comes from excited state transitions and only 30% from ground state reactions.
- For the \(^{144}\)Sm(\(\alpha\),\(\gamma\))\(^{148}\)Gd reaction, the stellar rate is only sensitive to the alpha-width across all astrophysically relevant temperatures and energies.
- For the \(^{96}\)Ru(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{97}\)Rh reaction at 5 GK, the sensitivity to the proton width is \(^r s_p = 0.5\) while the sensitivity to the gamma width is \(^r s_\gamma = 0.3\).
- For neutron capture reactions at 0.3 GK (typical s-process temperatures), sensitivities to neutron and gamma widths are basically complementary across the nuclear chart.

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity analysis approach previously outlined by Rauscher (review, 2011) and provides systematic tabulations that were missing for the full range of target nuclei from proton- to neutron-dripline. The results enable researchers to identify which nuclear properties (widths, level densities) most strongly influence specific reaction rate uncertainties, allowing them to prioritize experimental measurements or theoretical improvements. The paper also builds on earlier applicability maps for the statistical model by Rauscher, Thielemann, and Kratz (1997) and on the discussion of ground state contributions by the "xfactor" paper (Rauscher, 2003), and it explicitly notes that the reciprocity relations for stellar rates (derived from Blatt and Weisskopf, 1952) mean sensitivities are the same for forward and reverse reactions.
