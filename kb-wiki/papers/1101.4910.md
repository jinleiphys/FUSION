# Gluino production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions and nuclear shadowing

> In this article we investigate the influence of nuclear effects in the production of gluinos in nuclear collisions at the LHC, and estimate the transverse momentum dependence of the nuclear ratios $R_{pA} = {\frac{dσ(pA)}{dy d^2 p_T}} / A {\frac{dσ(pp)}{dy d^2 p_T}}$ and $R_{AA} = {\frac{dσ(AA)}{dy d^2 p_T}} / A^2 {\frac{dσ(pp)}{dy d^2 p_T}}$. We demonstrate that depending on the magnitude of the nuclear effects, the production of gluinos could be enhanced, compared to proton-proton collisions. The study of these observables can be useful to determine the magnitude of the shadowing and antishadowing effects in the nuclear gluon distribution. Moreover, we test different SPS scenarios, corresponding to different soft SUSY breaking mechanisms, and find that the nuclear ratios are strongly dependent on that choice.

# Gluino production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions and nuclear shadowing

## Key claim

The production of gluinos in proton-nucleus (pA) and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at the LHC could be either enhanced or suppressed relative to scaled proton-proton collisions, depending on the magnitude of nuclear shadowing and antishadowing effects in the nuclear gluon distribution; however, the sign and size of the effect are strongly dependent on both the choice of nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) parametrization and the specific SUSY breaking scenario (SPS point) assumed.

## Method

The authors computed leading-order (LO) partonic cross sections for inclusive gluino production (processes gg → g̃g̃, qq̄ → g̃g̃, and gq → g̃q̃) using the CTEQ6L1 proton PDF and several LO nuclear PDF parametrizations (DS, HKN, EPS08, EPS09) with the hard scale set to Q = m_g̃. They evaluated the nuclear modification factors R_pA and R_AA as functions of transverse momentum p_T at pseudorapidity |η| ≤ 2.5 for LHC energies (√s = 8.8 TeV for pA, 5.5 TeV for AA), testing ten SPS benchmark scenarios with gluino masses ranging from 595.2 GeV to 1275.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Gluino masses in the SPS scenarios range from 595.2 GeV (SPS1a) to 1275.2 GeV (SPS9); squark masses range from 539.9 GeV (SPS1a) to 1533.6 GeV (SPS2).
- For pA collisions at √s = 8.8 TeV, the HKN nPDF gives an enhancement of about 10% in R_pA for p_T > 500 GeV, while EPS08 and EPS09 show slight suppression increasing with p_T.
- For AA collisions at √s = 5.5 TeV, the EPS nPDFs show suppression of around 15% at higher p_T, while HKN shows enhancement above 20% and DS shows enhancement increasing with p_T.
- The expected pA luminosity is 7.4 × 10^29 cm^−2 s^−1, yielding an estimated 31 gluinos produced per year in the pT-integrated region using EPS09; a potential upgrade to 10^31 cm^−2 s^−1 would increase this estimate to 430 gluinos per year.
- The pp luminosity is 10^34 cm^−2 s^−1, while the AA luminosity is about 10^27 A^2 cm^−2 s^−1, seven orders of magnitude smaller.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inclusive heavy quark, quarkonium, and prompt photon production in nuclear collisions (Vogt, various years; Brenner Mariotto and collaborators, 2006-2009) to the supersymmetric sector, specifically gluino production. It uses the same nuclear PDF parametrizations (DS by de Florian and Sassot, 2004; HKN by Hirai, Kumano, and Nagai, 2007; EPS08 and EPS09 by Eskola, Paukkunen, and Salgado, 2008-2009) that have been applied to conventional QCD processes. The results show that the nuclear ratios are sensitive to both the nPDF choice and the SUSY breaking scenario, implying that future searches for gluinos in nuclear collisions will require better constraints on nuclear gluon distributions at high Q^2, and conversely that measured gluino production could provide new constraints on nPDFs in an unexplored kinematic region.
