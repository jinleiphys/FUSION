# Fragmentation, NRQCD and NNLO Factorization Analysis in Heavy Quarkonium Production

> We discuss heavy quarkonium production through parton fragmentation, including a review of arguments for the factorization of high-p_T particles into fragmentation functions for hadronic initial states. We investigate the further factorization of fragmentation functions in the NRQCD formalism, and argue that this requires a modification of NRQCD octet production matrix elements to include nonabelian phases, which makes them gauge invariant. We describe the calculation of uncanceled infrared divergences in fragmentation functions that must be factorized at NNLO, and verify that they are absorbed into the new, gauge invariant matrix elements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard NRQCD factorization of fragmentation functions for heavy quarkonium production requires a modification of NRQCD octet production matrix elements to include nonabelian phases (Wilson lines) to achieve gauge invariance, and that this modification is necessary to absorb uncanceled infrared divergences that appear at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), which were not covered by original arguments for NRQCD factorization.

## Method

The authors revisit the factorization of fragmentation functions for heavy quarkonium production through parton fragmentation, first reviewing the proof of factorization for high-transverse-momentum particles into universal fragmentation functions for both leptonic annihilation and hadronic scattering. They then test the further factorization of these fragmentation functions into perturbative coefficient functions times NRQCD matrix elements, analyzing the infrared behavior of perturbative amplitudes using cut diagram techniques and pinch surface analysis to identify uncanceled divergences at NNLO. The authors perform two-loop calculations in the infrared sector to demonstrate that these divergences can be absorbed into gauge-completed NRQCD matrix elements with added Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

* The NNLO infrared effects appear at order α_s^3 in inclusive heavy quark pair production cross sections (the type calculated to order α_s^2 in Czarnecki and Melnikov (1997) and the corresponding two-loop decay cross sections in Beneke et al. (1997))
* The factorization scale μ is naturally taken at μ = O(m_c) for charm-anticharm bound states, where m_c is the heavy quark mass
* The boundary condition for evolution of fragmentation functions is taken at μ_0 ∼ m_H, where m_H ∼ 2m_c for J/ψ

## Context

This work extends the factorization analysis of heavy quarkonium production beyond the standard NRQCD factorization approach of Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1994) and the fragmentation function formalism of Braaten et al. (1996). The paper builds on the factorization proofs for fragmentation functions in QCD by Mueller (1978), Collins and Soper (1981), and Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1985), and applies them specifically to heavy quarkonium. The results enable a consistent NNLO treatment of heavy quarkonium production through fragmentation, resolving gauge invariance issues in the NRQCD octet production matrix elements that were previously identified as problematic by Bodwin et al. (2003) and Nayak et al. (2005).
