# Chemical freeze-out in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at sqrt(s)_NN = 130 and 200 GeV

> A comprehensive and detailed analysis of hadronic abundances measured in Au-Au collisions at RHIC at sqrt(s)_NN = 130 and 200 GeV is presented. The rapidity densities measured in the central rapidity region have been fitted to the statistical hadronization model and the chemical freeze-out parameters determined as a function of centrality, using data from experiments BRAHMS, PHENIX and STAR. The chemical freeze-out temperature turns out to be independent of centrality to a few percent accuracy, whereas the strangeness under-saturation parameter gamma_S decreases from almost unity in central collisions to a significantly lower value in peripheral collisions. Our results are in essential agreement with previous analyses, with the exception that fit quality at sqrt(s)_NN = 200 GeV is not as good as previously found. From the comparison of the two different energies, we conclude that the difference in fit quality, as described by chi2 values, is owing to the improved resolution of measurements which has probably exceeded the intrinsic accuracy of the simplified theoretical formula used in the fits.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out temperature in Au-Au collisions at RHIC is independent of centrality to within a few percent, while the strangeness under-saturation parameter gamma_S decreases from near unity in central collisions to significantly lower values in peripheral collisions; however, the fit quality at sqrt(s)_NN = 200 GeV is worse than previously reported, which the authors attribute to improved measurement resolution exceeding the intrinsic accuracy of the simplified statistical hadronization model formula.

## Method

The authors fitted rapidity densities of hadrons measured in the central rapidity region by BRAHMS, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations to the statistical hadronization model using a grand-canonical formalism with four free parameters: temperature T, baryon chemical potential mu_B, strangeness under-saturation factor gamma_S, and a volume scaling factor dV/dy. They performed fits to STAR data alone and combined fits including PHENIX and BRAHMS data, with cross-normalization factors to account for inter-experiment discrepancies, and used polynomial interpolation to estimate particle yields in common centrality windows across experiments.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature is constant across centrality at the few percent level, with best-fit values around 170 MeV at both sqrt(s)_NN = 130 and 200 GeV
- Strangeness under-saturation parameter gamma_S decreases from approximately 1 in central collisions to significantly lower values in peripheral collisions; at 130 GeV gamma_S appears to exceed 1 in the most central collisions but is consistent with 1 within error bars
- Average relative deviation of calculated rapidity densities from experimental ones is 9.7% in central collisions at 130 GeV and 11.5% at 200 GeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom is less than 1 at 130 GeV but larger than 1 at 200 GeV, with the difference attributed to improved measurement resolution at the higher energy
- Cross-normalization factor f_P for PHENIX data relative to STAR data varies between 0.8 and 0.92 across the centrality range at 130 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous statistical hadronization model analyses of RHIC data by Cleymans (2004), Adams (2003, 2006), Dumitru (2005), Andronic (2005), Baran (2003), and Rafelski (2004), and completes the authors' prior studies at AGS and SPS energies (Becattini 2003, 2005). The analysis uses newly published experimental data and addresses the bias introduced by fitting particle ratios instead of multiplicities, as discussed in Becattini (2007). The results enable predictions that Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC will find T approximately 170 MeV and gamma_S approximately 1, and highlight the need for higher-order corrections to the statistical hadronization model when measurement precision exceeds the model's intrinsic accuracy.
