# Surface Tension between Kaon Condensate and Normal Nuclear Matter Phase

> We calculate for the first time the surface tension and curvature coefficient of a first order phase transition between two possible phases of cold nuclear matter, a normal nuclear matter phase in equilibrium with a kaon condensed phase, at densities a few times the saturation density. We find the surface tension is proportional to the difference in energy density between the two phases squared. Furthermore, we show the consequences for the geometrical structures of the mixed phase region in a neutron star.

# Surface Tension between Kaon Condensate and Normal Nuclear Matter Phase

## Key claim

The surface tension between normal nuclear matter and kaon condensed matter is proportional to the square of the difference in energy density between the two phases, not simply proportional to the energy density difference as had been previously assumed. The curvature coefficient is negative and comparable in magnitude to the surface tension, but the authors caution that higher-order effects may mask the curvature term in practice.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field model that treats nucleons and kaons on equal footing, coupling both to sigma, omega, and rho mesons. They solve the coupled differential equations for the kaon field and meson fields across a plane interface between semi-infinite slabs of normal nuclear matter and kaon condensed matter, using a relaxation procedure with bulk values obtained from Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions. The surface tension and curvature coefficient are computed from the mechanical Gibbs definition using the resulting density and field profiles, with the Thomas-Fermi approximation for nucleon densities.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension fit: σ_fit = (0.00786 × (ε_K − ε_N) / (MeV/fm³))² MeV/fm², valid where ε_K − ε_N decreases from 682 MeV to 358 MeV (about 50%)
- Curvature coefficient fit: γ_fit = −0.0976 × ((ε_K − ε_N) / (MeV/fm³))^(3/4) MeV/fm
- The exact and approximative surface tension calculations deviate by less than 0.4%
- The exact and approximative curvature coefficient calculations differ by about 33%
- For a spherical droplet of kaon phase with radius R = 5 fm in the χ → 0 limit, the surface energy is reduced by about 2γ_G/(σ_G R) = 18% due to curvature
- For a spherical bubble of normal matter in the χ → 1 limit, the surface energy increases by about 40% due to curvature
- The Coulomb energy is reduced less than 15% for soft boundaries compared to sharp boundaries, translating to about a 5% reduction of the sum of surface and Coulomb energies
- The overall sizes of geometrical structures decrease about 30% compared to earlier estimates

## Context

This work extends the study of mixed phase regions in neutron stars by Glendenning and Schaffner-Bielich (1999), who had previously assumed the surface tension was proportional to the energy density difference and chosen the constant arbitrarily. The authors provide the first calculation of surface tension and curvature coefficient for the kaon condensate transition, enabling more detailed studies of the crystalline structures in neutron star interiors. The results are relevant for understanding transport properties, superfluid behavior, r-mode instabilities, braking indices, and glitch phenomena in pulsars, as discussed in work by Glendenning (1997), Weber (1999), Reddy, Bertsch, and Prakash (1999), and Madsen (1999).
