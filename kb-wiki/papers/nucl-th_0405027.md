# Projected multicluster model with Jastrow and linear state dependent correlations for $12 \leq A \leq 16$ nuclei

> Variational wave functions based on a Margenau-Brink cluster model with short range and state dependent correlations, and angular momentum projection are obtained for some nuclei with $12 \leq A \leq 16$. The calculations have been carried out starting from the nucleon-nucleon interaction by using the Variational Monte Carlo method. The configuration used consists of three alpha clusters located at the apexes of an equilateral triangle, and an additional cluster, not necessarily of alpha type, forming a tetrahedron. This cluster is located at the top of its height. Short-range and state dependent correlations are included by means of a central Jastrow factor and a linear operatorial correlation factor respectively. Angular momentum projection is performed by using the Peierls-Yoccoz operators. Optimal structures are obtained for all the nuclei studied. Some aspects of our methodology have been tested by comparing with previous calculations carried out without short range correlations. The binding energy, the root mean square radius, and the one- and two-body densities are reported. The effects of correlations on both the energy and the nucleon distribution are analyzed systematically.

# Projected multicluster model with Jastrow and linear state dependent correlations for $12 \leq A \leq 16$ nuclei

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a variational wave function combining a Margenau-Brink multicluster model with Jastrow and linear state-dependent correlations, plus angular momentum projection via Peierls-Yoccoz operators, can describe ground states of $p$-shell nuclei with $A \neq 4n$ (specifically $^{13}$C, $^{14}$C, $^{14}$N, $^{15}$N) using $v_4$-type nucleon-nucleon interactions that include a strongly repulsive core. The generalization to non-spin-isospin-saturated nuclei is nontrivial because angular momentum projection introduces spin mixing, but the authors show this can be handled analytically with only a modest increase in computational cost compared to the spin-saturated case.

## Method

The trial wave function consists of a central Jastrow correlation factor, a linear operatorial correlation factor with the same spin-isospin operator structure as the $v_4$ interaction, and a model wave function based on a generalized Margenau-Brink cluster model. The spatial configuration places three alpha clusters at the vertices of an equilateral triangle and a fourth incomplete cluster (one, two, or three nucleons) at the apex of a tetrahedron; angular momentum projection is performed using Peierls-Yoccoz operators. Expectation values are evaluated via the Variational Monte Carlo method, with the linear correlation parameters determined by solving a generalized eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- For $^{12}$C ground state with the Volkov V7 potential (no correlations): binding energy 86.49(4) MeV, root mean square radius 2.31(7) fm, compared to 86.7 MeV and 2.31 fm from Dufour and Descouvemont (1996)
- For $^{16}$O ground state with BB1 potential and JLO (state-dependent) correlations: binding energy -180.61(8) MeV (Coulomb not included), root mean square radius 2.35(5) fm
- For $^{16}$O ground state with MS3 potential and JLO correlations: binding energy -135.6(3) MeV (Coulomb not included), root mean square radius 2.27(7) fm
- For $^{14}$N ground state with MS3 potential and JLO correlations: binding energy -99.3(4) MeV, root mean square radius 2.37(10) fm
- The increment in binding energy per nucleon pair due to state-dependent correlations ($\Delta_{\rm JLO-JL}$) is approximately -0.19 MeV for the MS3 potential across all nuclei studied, and ranges from -0.08 to -0.12 MeV for the BB1 potential
- For $^{12}$C with MS3 potential and JL (state-independent) correlations: binding energy -74.54(5) MeV; with JLO correlations: -87.2(4) MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of spin- and isospin-saturated $A=4n$ nuclei (Buendia et al., 2002) to the $A \neq 4n$ case, where angular momentum projection introduces spin mixing not present in saturated systems. The cluster model approach follows the multicluster descriptions of Dufour and Descouvemont (1996) and the stochastic variational method of Varga et al. (1995), but incorporates short-range correlations necessary for realistic interactions with repulsive cores. The methodology enables systematic study of how Jastrow and operatorial correlations affect binding energies, radii, and one- and two-body densities across the $12 \leq A \leq 16$ region, and can be extended to mirror nuclei $^{13}$N, $^{14}$O, and $^{15}$O since Coulomb energy was not included in the minimization.
