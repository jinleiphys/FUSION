# QCD at finite temperature and density within the fRG approach: An overview

> In this paper we present an overview on recent progress in studies of QCD at finite temperature and densities within the functional renormalization group (fRG) approach. The fRG is a nonperturbative continuum field approach, in which quantum, thermal and density fluctuations are integrated in successively with the evolution of the renormalization group (RG) scale. The fRG results for the QCD phase structure and the location of the critical end point (CEP), the QCD equation of state (EoS), the magnetic EoS, baryon number fluctuations confronted with recent experimental measurements, various critical exponents, spectral functions in the critical region, the dynamical critical exponent, etc., are presented. Recent estimates of the location of the CEP from first-principle QCD calculations within fRG and Dyson-Schwinger Equations, which passes through lattice benchmark tests at small baryon chemical potentials, converge in a rather small region at baryon chemical potentials of about 600 MeV. A region of inhomogeneous instability indicated by a negative wave function renormalization is found with $μ_B\gtrsim 420$ MeV. It is found that the non-monotonic dependence of the kurtosis of the net-proton number distributions on the beam collision energy observed in experiments, could arise from the increasingly sharp crossover in the regime of low collision energy.

## Key claim

Recent first-principle QCD calculations within the functional renormalization group (fRG) and Dyson-Schwinger Equations, which pass lattice benchmark tests at small baryon chemical potentials, converge on a critical end point (CEP) location in a rather small region at baryon chemical potentials of about 600 MeV, though the paper notes that the non-monotonic dependence of the kurtosis of net-proton number distributions observed in experiments could arise from an increasingly sharp crossover in the low collision energy regime rather than definitively from the CEP itself.

## Method

The paper applies the functional renormalization group (fRG) approach, a nonperturbative continuum field method, to QCD at finite temperature and density by integrating quantum, thermal, and density fluctuations successively with the evolution of the renormalization group scale. The truncation for the Euclidean effective action includes gluon, ghost, quark, and dynamically generated meson fields (sigma and pion) via the dynamical hadronization technique, with the effective potential decomposed into a glue (Polyakov loop) potential and a mesonic effective potential. The flow equations for propagators, strong couplings, four-quark couplings, and Yukawa couplings are derived from the Wetterich equation and solved self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- The CEP from first-principle fRG and DSE calculations converges at baryon chemical potentials of about 600 MeV.
- A region of inhomogeneous instability indicated by a negative wave function renormalization is found with mu_B greater than or approximately equal to 420 MeV.
- Lattice calculations are usually restricted to the region of mu_B/T less than or approximately equal to 2 to 3, where no signal of CEP is observed.
- A non-monotonic dependence of the kurtosis of the net-proton multiplicity distribution on collision energy is observed with 3.1 sigma significance for central gold-on-gold (Au+Au) collisions at RHIC.

## Context

This overview extends the line of work applying fRG to QCD phase structure, building on prior lattice QCD results at vanishing and small baryon chemical potential from Bazavov (2012, 2017, 2018, 2020), Borsanyi (2013, 2014, 2018, 2020), Bellwied (2015), and Ding (2019, 2020), which are restricted by the sign problem at finite chemical potentials. The fRG results converge with Dyson-Schwinger Equation calculations from Fu (2019), Gao (2020), and Gunkel (2021), and enable further searches for the CEP at facilities such as RHIC Beam Energy Scan, as well as studies of spectral functions in the critical region and the dynamical critical exponent.
