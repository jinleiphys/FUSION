# Nucleon clustering at kinetic freezeout of heavy-ion collisions via path-integral Monte Carlo

> Clustering of the four-nucleon system at kinetic freezeout conditions is studied using path-integral Monte Carlo techniques. This method seeks to improve upon previous calculations which relied on approximate semiclassical methods or few-body quantum mechanics. Estimates are given for the decay probabilities of the 4N system into various light nuclei decay channels and the strength of spatial correlations is characterized. Additionally, a simple model is presented to describe the impact of this clustering on nucleon multiplicity distributions. The effects of a possible modification of the inter-nucleon interaction due to the close critical line (and hypothetical QCD critical point) on the clustering are also studied.

## Key claim

The paper uses path-integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) to study four-nucleon clustering at kinetic freezeout conditions, claiming that the "preclusters" identified are statistical correlations in the density matrix rather than true bound states, and that their contribution to the fourth cumulant of baryon multiplicity distributions is not suppressed by the factor of 1/64 that suppresses their contribution to the mean baryon number.

## Method

The authors perform path-integral Monte Carlo simulations of four distinguishable nucleons (p↑, p↓, n↑, n↓) in a periodic box at temperatures and densities corresponding to kinetic freezeout for six beam energies from √sNN = 2.4 to 39 GeV. They use a modified Serot-Walecka potential with isoscalar central interactions plus Coulomb repulsion between protons, discretizing Euclidean time into 40 time slices. The probability distribution in hyperradius ρ (9-dimensional radial coordinate) is decomposed into ground state, thermal tail, and precluster components by subtracting the ground state distribution and a random-configuration distribution from the full kinetic freezeout distribution.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ⁴He: EB = 28.3 MeV; binding energy of deuteron: EB = 2.2 MeV
- Kinetic freezeout temperatures used: Tkin ≈ 110 MeV for higher energies, Tkin = 71 ± 8 MeV for √s = 2.4 GeV
- For √s = 19.6 GeV: ground state makes up 2.8% of the cluster; decay probabilities: ⁴He = 2.8%, p+t = 29.3%, n+³He = 21.7%, d+d = 46.2%
- For √s = 2.4 GeV: ground state makes up 15.8% of the cluster; decay probabilities: ⁴He = 15.8%, p+t = 25.8%, n+³He = 19.0%, d+d = 39.4%
- Correlation volume Vcor^(9) at √s = 7.7 GeV: 4.3 × 10⁴ fm⁹ (mσ = 500 MeV), 1.9 × 10⁵ fm⁹ (mσ = 450 MeV), 1.6 × 10⁶ fm⁹ (mσ = 400 MeV)
- Reducing mσ from 500 MeV to 450 MeV increases peak correlation by a factor of approximately 5
- Reducing mσ to 300 MeV increases binding energy by approximately a factor of 50 (to over 1.5 GeV)
- Predicted t/p ratio at √s = 7.7 GeV: approximately 0.4%

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Shuryak (2019) that used semiclassical "flucton" methods and hyperradial Schrödinger equation solutions for the 4N system, improving upon them with first-principles path-integral Monte Carlo that avoids approximations. The results connect to the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and HADES at GSI, where light nuclei production and baryon cumulants are studied as possible signals of the QCD critical point. The paper enables quantitative predictions for feed-down contributions to light nuclei yields and for the fourth cumulant K4 of proton multiplicity distributions, which the authors relate to the nonzero factorial cumulant C4 observed in STAR data by Koch (2020).
