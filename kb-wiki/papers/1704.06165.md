# A derivation of the entropy-based relativistic smoothed particle hydrodynamics by variational principle

> In this work, a second order smoothed particle hydrodynamics is derived for the study of relativistic heavy ion collisions. The hydrodynamical equation of motion is formulated in terms of the variational principle. In order to describe the fluid of high energy density but of low baryon density, the entropy is taken as the base quantity for the interpolation. The smoothed particle hydrodynamics algorithm employed in this study is of the second order, which guarantees better particle consistency. Furthermore, it is shown that the variational principle preserves the translational invariance of the system, and therefore improves the accuracy of the method. A brief discussion on the potential implications of the model in heavy ion physics is also presented.

# Entropy-Based Relativistic Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics via Variational Principle

## Key claim
The authors derive a second-order finite particle method (FPM) for relativistic smoothed particle hydrodynamics (SPH) using a variational principle, which guarantees momentum conservation through translational invariance. The method is specifically designed for high-energy-density, low-baryon-density systems in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where entropy is used as the interpolation base quantity.

## Method
The authors start from the standard SPH formalism and extend it to second order using the finite particle method (FPM), which retains terms up to the second order in a Taylor series expansion of the physical quantity before applying the kernel interpolation. The relativistic hydrodynamic equation of motion is then derived by applying the variational principle to the Lagrangian of the SPH system, where the action is expressed in terms of entropy density. The resulting equation of motion for the FPM case is obtained by substituting the FPM expression for entropy density into the variational calculation, yielding a set of force terms that automatically satisfy Newton's third law due to the translational invariance of the formulation.

## Key numbers
- 1000 SPH particles were used in the numerical demonstration of FPM interpolation accuracy, with smoothing length h = 0.002
- The FPM formalism requires solving a matrix equation of dimension D+1, where D is the spatial dimension of the system
- The standard SPH result corresponds to the specific term f^(2)_ij in the one-dimensional FPM case when the assumptions ave(1)_i → 1 and ave(1)_(i,x) → 0 are applied

## Context
This work extends the finite particle method (FPM) originally proposed by Liu et al. for improving particle consistency in SPH, and applies it to relativistic heavy ion collisions where fluctuating initial conditions are important. The variational principle approach follows earlier work by the SPH community (e.g., the first-order SPH derivation) and addresses the problem that standard symmetrization of pressure gradients for momentum conservation is not straightforward in the FPM formalism. The method is intended for implementation in realistic simulations of small systems created at RHIC and LHC, where conservation laws play a more stringent role, and builds on the observation of the "ridge" effect in two-particle correlations that motivates the use of fluctuating initial conditions.
