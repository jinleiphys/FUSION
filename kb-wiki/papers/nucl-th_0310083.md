# Three-neutrino model analysis of the world's oscillation data

> A model of neutrino oscillation experiments is constructed. The experiments incorporated are: solar neutrinos (Chlorine, Gallium, Super-K, and SNO), reactor neutrinos (Bugey and CHOOZ), beam stop neutrinos (LSND decay at rest and decay in flight), and atmospheric neutrinos. Utilizing this model and the standard three-neutrino mixing extension of the standard model, the data are analyzed. Solutions for the mixing angles and mass-squared differences are found to occur in pairs corresponding to the interchange $Δm^2_{12} \leftrightarrow Δm^2_{23}$. Two pairs of solutions are found that reasonably reproduce the data, including the LSND data. These solutions are $θ_{12}\approx 0.5$, $θ_{13}\approx 0.1$, $θ_{23}\approx 0.7$, $Δm^2_{12} \approx 5\times 10^{-5}$ eV$^2$ and $Δm^2_{23} \approx 0.2$ eV$^2$ or 2.4 eV$^2$. Other statistically significant solutions are also found which produce negligible oscillations for the LSND experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a standard three-neutrino mixing model can simultaneously fit solar, reactor, beam-stop (LSND), and atmospheric neutrino data, including the LSND anomaly, but only under the hedge that the solutions come in pairs related by the interchange of the two mass-squared differences, and that the fits are only "semi-quantitatively correct" due to simplified modeling of experimental acceptances and energy spectra.

## Method

The authors constructed a global model of neutrino oscillation experiments incorporating solar (Chlorine, Gallium, Super-K, SNO), reactor (Bugey, CHOOZ, KamLAND), beam-stop (LSND decay-at-rest and decay-in-flight), and atmospheric (Super-Kamiokande) data. They used the standard three-neutrino mixing framework with no CP violation, applied the MSW effect for solar neutrinos in the adiabatic limit, and approximated energy distributions and L/E averaging for reactor and atmospheric experiments. They performed a chi-squared minimization over mixing angles and mass-squared differences to find best-fit parameter sets.

## Key numbers

* Solar-only two-neutrino fit: tan²θ = 0.43 (bounds 0.28–0.68), Δm² = 4.0×10⁻⁵ eV² (bounds 1.0×10⁻⁵–8.6×10⁻⁵ eV²)
* Best global fit (solution 1): χ²_dof = 1.4, θ₁₂ = 0.55, θ₁₃ = 0.16, θ₂₃ = 0.79, Δm²₁₂ = 5.6×10⁻⁵ eV², Δm²₂₃ = 0.24 eV²
* Best global fit (solution 2): χ²_dof = 1.5, θ₁₂ = 0.58, θ₁₃ = 0.06, θ₂₃ = 0.66, Δm²₁₂ = 4.3×10⁻⁵ eV², Δm²₂₃ = 2.3 eV²
* LSND decay-at-rest: measured 𝒫_{ēμ̄} = (3.1 ± 1.3)×10⁻³ at L/E = 0.73 m/MeV
* LSND decay-in-flight: measured 𝒫_{eμ} = (2.6 ± 1.1)×10⁻³ at L/E = 0.40 m/MeV
* KamLAND: measured 𝒫_{ee} = 0.611 ± 0.094 at L/E = 4.1×10⁴ m/MeV
* Super-K solar: measured 𝒫_{ee} = 0.465 ± 0.094 at L/E = 2.2×10¹⁰ m/MeV

## Context

This work extends prior three-neutrino analyses that had either excluded LSND data (Gonzalez-Garcia et al., early 2000s), restricted mass-squared differences (Ohlsson, 2000), or used analytic approximations (Ahluwalia, 1997; Weiler, 1999). The authors find that their solution 7 corresponds to the LSND-excluding fit of Gonzalez-Garcia et al., validating their simplified model. The paper enables future model-independent global fits to test whether the three-neutrino framework can accommodate LSND, and identifies a new symmetry (Δm²₁₂ ↔ Δm²₂₃ interchange) that must be considered in such analyses.
