# Classification of states of single-$j$ fermions with $J$-pairing interaction

> In this paper we show that a system of three fermions is exactly solvable for the case of a single-$j$ in the presence of an angular momentum-$J$ pairing interaction. On the basis of the solutions for this system, we obtain new sum rules for six-$j$ symbols. It is also found that the "non-integer" eigenvalues of three fermions with angular momentum $I$ around the maximum appear as "non-integer" eigenvalues of four fermions when $I$ is around (or larger than) $J_{\rm max}$ and the Hamiltonian contains only an interaction between pairs of fermions coupled to spin $J=J_{\rm max}=2j-1$. This pattern is also found in five and six fermion systems. A boson system with spin $l$ exhibits a similar pattern.

# Classification of states of single-$j$ fermions with $J$-pairing interaction

## Key claim

The paper shows that a system of three fermions in a single-$j$ shell is exactly solvable for any angular momentum-$J$ pairing interaction, and that the "non-integer" eigenvalues of three fermions with angular momentum $I$ near the maximum appear as "non-integer" eigenvalues of four fermions when $I$ is around or larger than $J_{\rm max}=2j-1$, but this correspondence is asymptotic for large $j$ rather than exact, with deviations becoming significant for small $j$ values such as $j=7/2$ or $9/2$.

## Method

The authors analytically diagonalize the $J$-pairing Hamiltonian $H_J = G_J \sum_M A_M^{J\dagger} A_M^J$ for three fermions in a single-$j$ shell by constructing an overcomplete pair basis and using commutator relations to show that for each total angular momentum $I^{(3)}$ there is at most one non-zero eigenvalue. They then compare these exact three-fermion eigenvalues with shell-model diagonalization results for four-, five-, and six-fermion systems with $J=J_{\rm max}$ pairing, computing overlaps between exact eigenstates and states constructed by coupling a three-particle cluster to a single spectator particle.

## Key numbers

- For three fermions with $j=31/2$ and $H=H_{J_{\rm max}}$, the eigenvalue for $I^{(3)}_{\rm max}=87/2$ is $-\frac{267}{118} = -2.26271186440677966$.
- For four fermions with $j=31/2$ and $I^{(4)}=28$, the lowest eigenvalue from shell-model diagonalization is $-2.26271186440689$, with overlap 1.000000000000000 to the $[I^{(3)}_{\rm max} \times j]^{I^{(4)}}$ state.
- For four fermions with $j=31/2$ and $I^{(4)}=56$, the eigenvalue is $-3.66369313113292$ with overlap 1.000000000000000.
- For three fermions with $j=9/2$, the eigenvalue $E^{(3)}_{7/2, J_{\rm max}(9/2)}$ equals $-\frac{714}{715}$, which is within $10^{-2}$ of $-1$.
- For $j=31/2$ and $n=4$, out of 380 states with non-zero eigenvalues, 308 have eigenvalues located at the eigenvalues of $n=3$ within a precision of $10^{-2}$.
- For $j=19/2$ and $n=5$, the eigenvalue $\sim (E^{(3)}_{I^{(3)}_{\rm max}-5, J_{\rm max}(j)}-1) = -\frac{6469}{3410} = -1.89707$ appears in states with $I^{(5)} \ge j-7 = 5/2$.
- For $j=19/2$ and $n=6$ with $I^{(6)}=0$, eigenvalues include $-4.54286$, $-3.31055$, $-2.42027$, $-2.10284$, $-1.75573$, and $-1.23269$, approximately equal to twice the eigenvalues of three-fermion clusters with $I^{(3)} \sim I^{(3)}_{\rm max}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of four fermions with $J_{\rm max}$ pairing (Zhao and Arima, 2003), where asymptotic integer eigenvalues were found for low angular momentum states but "non-integer" eigenvalues for high angular momentum states remained unexplained. The paper provides a cluster interpretation where three-particle clusters with near-maximum angular momentum couple weakly to spectator particles, enabling approximate construction of wavefunctions without diagonalization. This connects to the broader program of exactly solvable models in nuclear physics including the Elliott model, the seniority scheme of Racah, the interacting boson model of Arima and Iachello, and the fermion dynamical symmetry model of Ginocchio. The results also yield new sum rules for six-$j$ symbols, some of which were independently derived by Talmi.
