# Issues with Search for Critical Point in QCD with Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> A systematic search for a critical point in the phase diagram of QCD matter is underway at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and is planned at several future facilities. Its existence, if confirmed, and its location will greatly enhance our understanding of QCD. In this note we emphasize several important issues that are often not fully recognized in theoretical interpretations of experimental results relevant to the critical point search. We discuss ways in which our understanding on these issues can be improved.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the widely assumed proportionality between conserved charge cumulants and powers of the correlation length near the QCD critical point does not generally hold in relativistic heavy ion collisions, because conserved charge fluctuations evolve by slow diffusion while the order parameter field evolves by faster dynamics, and the two obey different equations of motion.

## Method

The authors analyze three issues in the interpretation of conserved charge cumulant measurements from the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC: (1) whether fluctuations decouple at chemical or kinetic freeze-out, arguing that diffusion changes fluctuations until kinetic freeze-out; (2) whether the equilibrium scaling relation between cumulants and correlation length applies in dynamical collisions, concluding it does not due to critical slowing down and finite reaction time; (3) the need to map coordinate-space theoretical calculations to momentum-space experimental acceptance, requiring a full dynamical model of the collision geometry and evolution.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponents for cumulants in equilibrium are: m2 ≈ 2, m3 ≈ 4.5, m4 ≈ 7, m5 ≈ 9.5, m6 ≈ 12, where K_n ∝ ξ^{m_n} for the n-th cumulant of a particle number coupled to the order parameter.
- The STAR experimental acceptance is |η| ≤ 1.6 in pseudorapidity.
- The transverse correlation length in the Landau-Fermi picture is estimated as 2R/c_s γ, where R is the nuclear radius and c_s ≈ 0.4 is the speed of sound in baryon-rich QCD matter.
- The initial transverse correlation length from the wounded nucleon model is approximately 1 fm, comparable to the nucleon radius.

## Context

This note extends the theoretical framework of conserved charge fluctuations originally proposed by Asakawa (2000) and Jeon (2000) as probes of quark deconfinement, and the critical point scaling relations derived by Stephanov (2008). It contradicts the common assumption that cumulants decouple at chemical freeze-out, citing work by Albright (2015) showing that fluctuations frozen at a lower temperature reproduce data. The paper calls for dynamical calculations that treat both the order parameter field and hydrodynamic fields properly, as in the static calculations of Fujii (2003, 2004), and for constructing full dynamical models of the collision to enable comparison with experimental results from the Beam Energy Scan.
