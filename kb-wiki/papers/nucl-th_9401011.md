# Role of the $a_1$ meson in dilepton production from hot hadronic matter

> Dilepton production from hot hadronic matter is studied in an effective chiral Lagrangian with pions, $ρ$-mesons, and $a_1$ mesons, We find that the production rates from reactions that involve axial-vector mesons dominate over contributions from all other reactions when the dilepton invariant mass is above 1.5 GeV.

## Key claim

Dilepton production from reactions involving the $a_1$ meson (specifically $\pi^+a_1^-$ and $\pi^-a_1^+$ annihilation) dominates over all other hadronic channels for dilepton invariant masses between 1.5 GeV and 3.0 GeV, which is the window previously thought to be dominated by the quark-gluon plasma; this conclusion holds at both temperatures studied (150 MeV and 200 MeV) and is independent of the temperature.

## Method

The authors calculated dilepton production rates from hot hadronic matter using an effective chiral Lagrangian with $SU(2) \times SU(2)$ symmetry that includes pions, $\rho$ mesons, and $a_1$ mesons as massive Yang-Mills fields. They computed the cross sections for the reactions $\pi^+a_1^-(\pi^-a_1^+) \to e^+e^-$ and $a_1^+a_1^- \to e^+e^-$, using the same form factors as in prior work by Gale and Lichard, and integrated these over thermal distributions at temperatures of 150 MeV and 200 MeV to obtain differential production rates as a function of dilepton invariant mass.

## Key numbers

- At $T = 150$ MeV, the $\pi^+a_1^-(\pi^-a_1^+)\to e^+e^-$ reaction dominates over $\rho^+\rho^-\to e^+e^-$ in the invariant mass region 1.5 GeV $< M <$ 3.0 GeV.
- At $T = 200$ MeV, the same qualitative conclusion holds with an overall enhancement in the dilepton yield.
- The pion decay constant used is $F_\pi \approx 135$ MeV.
- The relevant invariant mass window is $m_\phi < M < m_{J/\psi}$, i.e., between the phi meson mass (1 GeV) and the $J/\psi$ mass (3 GeV).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Gale and Lichard (1994) that included only pseudoscalar and vector mesons, showing that $\rho^+\rho^-$ annihilation was the dominant source for dileptons in the 1.5–3.0 GeV region. The authors show that including the $a_1$ meson, which had already been found important for photon production by Song, Shin, and Ko (1993), makes the $\pi a_1$ channel even more important than $\rho\rho$ annihilation, raising questions about whether further inclusion of heavier mesons would continue to increase the predicted hadronic background. The results complicate the interpretation of dilepton signals as clean signatures for quark-gluon plasma formation in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions.
