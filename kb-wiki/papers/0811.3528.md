# Variational Theory of Hot Nucleon Matter II : Spin-Isospin Correlations and Equation of State of Nuclear and Neutron Matter

> We apply the variational theory for fermions at finite temperature and high density, developed in an earlier paper, to symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. This extension generalizes to finite temperatures, the many body technique used in the construction of the zero temperature Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall equation of state. We discuss how the formalism can be used for practical calculations of hot dense matter. Neutral pion condensation along with the associated isovector spin longitudinal sum rule is analyzed. The equation of state is calculated for temperatures less than 30 MeV and densities less than three times the saturation density of nuclear matter. The behavior of the nucleon effective mass in medium is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper extends the zero-temperature variational chain summation (VCS/2) method of Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) to finite temperatures for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, claiming that orthogonality corrections to the free energy vanish in the thermodynamic limit under a specific orthonormalization scheme, thereby enabling practical calculations without needing off-diagonal matrix elements.

## Method

The variational free energy is minimized by optimizing both the single-particle spectrum (parametrized by an effective mass m*) and the pair correlation operator (with eight operator channels) at each density and temperature, using the Gibbs-Bogoliubov variational principle. The Hamiltonian includes the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction with boost corrections (UIX*), and the expectation values are computed via the variational chain summation method generalized to finite temperature Slater functions. The four variational parameters (m*/m, d_c, d_t, alpha) are optimized using a downhill simplex routine with a constrained free energy that enforces mass and charge sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The equation of state is calculated for temperatures less than 30 MeV and densities less than three times the saturation density of nuclear matter (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3).
- At rho = rho_0 and T = 10 MeV, the deviation of the mass sum rule I_c from its exact value is approximately 0.04% and the charge sum rule I_tau deviation is approximately 0.3% in the full optimization; using the frozen-correlation method these deviations become approximately 13% and 20% respectively.
- The improved numerics yield energy changes of approximately 0.5 MeV per particle at rho = rho_0 and T = 0 compared to APR.
- The grid size for integrations uses l_t = 64 (compared to 32 in APR calculations).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature variational chain summation calculations of Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) (1998) to finite temperatures, improving upon the earlier finite-temperature variational calculation by Friedman and Pandharipande (FP) (1981) which ignored orthogonality corrections and used frozen zero-temperature correlations at high densities. The formalism enables ab initio studies of hot dense matter relevant to supernovae evolution, protoneutron star cooling, and neutron star mergers, and is benchmarked against quantum Monte Carlo calculations in neutron matter by Carlson, Morales, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (2003).
