# Franz-Keldysh effect in strong-field QED

> We studied a QED analog of the Franz-Keldysh effect, and the interplay between the non-perturbative (the Schwinger mechanism) and the perturbative particle production mechanism from the vacuum in the presence of a strong slow field superimposed by a weak field. We found that the Franz-Keldysh effect significantly affects the particle production: (i) the perturbative particle production occurs even below the threshold energy; (ii) the perturbative production becomes the most efficient just above the threshold energy; and (iii) an oscillating behavior appears in the production number above the threshold energy. These non-trivial changes are suppressed only weakly by powers of the critical field strength of QED. A relation to the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism and implications to experiments are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a QED analog of the Franz-Keldysh effect occurs when a weak perturbative field is superimposed on a strong slow electric field, significantly modifying the perturbative particle production rate. The effect is suppressed only weakly by powers of the critical field strength, unlike the exponentially suppressed Schwinger mechanism.

## Method

The author develops a perturbation theory where the weak field is treated perturbatively while the strong constant electric field is treated non-perturbatively, using the retarded Green function method (Furry picture) to solve the Dirac equation. For a specific configuration of a constant homogeneous strong electric field superimposed with a monochromatic weak electric field, the mode functions are expressed analytically using parabolic cylinder functions, and the in-vacuum expectation value of the out-state number operator is computed to first order in the weak field.

## Key numbers

- The critical field strength for QED is given by \( eE_{\rm cr} \equiv m_{\rm e}^2 \sim \sqrt{10^{28}\;{\rm W/cm^2}} \)
- The HERCULES laser achieves \( eE \sim \sqrt{10^{22}\;{\rm W/cm^2}} \), which is several orders of magnitude below the critical field
- Upcoming facilities such as ELI and HiPER may reach \( eE \sim \sqrt{10^{24}\;{\rm W/cm^2}} \), still weaker than the critical field
- The Schwinger formula for the number of produced electrons is \( n_{{\bm p},s}^{\rm (Sch)} = \frac{V}{(2\pi)^3} \exp\left[ -\pi \frac{m_{\rm e}^2 + {\bm p}_{\perp}^2}{eE} \right] \), showing exponential suppression by the inverse of the coupling constant

## Context

This work extends the concept of the Franz-Keldysh effect from semiconductor physics (Franz 1958, Keldysh 1958) to quantum electrodynamics, building on Dirac's interpretation of the vacuum as analogous to a semiconductor with a gap energy set by the electron mass. The study relates to the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism (Schutzhold 2008, Dunne 2009) where a weak fast field enhances non-perturbative pair production, but here the focus is on how a strong slow field modifies perturbative production by a weak field. The results suggest new experimental avenues for probing non-perturbative QED effects at field strengths below the critical value.
