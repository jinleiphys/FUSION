# Recent developments in quasi-elastic scattering around the Coulomb barrier

> We discuss two recent topics on heavy-ion quasi-elastic scattering at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The first topic is an application of quasi-elastic scattering at deep-subbarrier energies to extracting the surface diffuseness parameter of the nucleus-nucleus potential. The second topic is a coupled-channels analysis for the quasi-elastic barrier distribution for the $^{70}$Zn + $^{208}$Pb reaction. We show that the coupled-channels calculations which include the multi-phonon excitations in the colliding nuclei reproduce reasonably well the experimental excitation function for quasi-elastic scattering at backward angles and the barrier distribution for this reaction.

# Recent developments in quasi-elastic scattering around the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim
Quasi-elastic scattering at deep-subbarrier energies can extract the surface diffuseness parameter of the nucleus-nucleus potential in a nearly model-independent way, but the extracted value depends strongly on whether the target is spherical or deformed. For spherical systems the diffuseness parameter is around 0.55–0.60 fm, consistent with double folding potentials, whereas for deformed systems it is much larger (0.79–1.14 fm), matching values from fusion data but with an unexplained origin.

## Method
The author performed one-dimensional optical model fits to experimental quasi-elastic excitation functions at backward angles (159 degrees) for spherical systems (32S + 197Au, 34S + 197Au, 32,34S + 208Pb, 16O + 208Pb) and deformed systems (16O + 154Sm, 16O + 186W) at deep-subbarrier energies, using a Woods-Saxon potential with an imaginary part localized inside the Coulomb barrier. For the 70Zn + 208Pb reaction, coupled-channels calculations were carried out with the code CCFULL (modified for quasi-elastic scattering), including double quadrupole phonon excitations in 70Zn and triple octupole phonon excitations in 208Pb, plus mutual excitation channels, to reproduce the experimental excitation function and barrier distribution.

## Key numbers
- Surface diffuseness parameter for 32S + 197Au: a = 0.57 ± 0.04 fm
- Surface diffuseness parameter for 34S + 197Au: a = 0.53 ± 0.03 fm
- Surface diffuseness parameter for 16O + 154Sm (deformed): a = 1.14 ± 0.03 fm
- Surface diffuseness parameter for 16O + 186W (deformed): a = 0.79 ± 0.04 fm
- For 70Zn + 208Pb coupled-channels calculation: Woods-Saxon parameters V0 = 140 MeV, r0 = 1.186 fm, a = 0.69 fm for the real part; W0 = 50.0 MeV, rw = 1.0 fm, aw = 0.1 fm for the imaginary part
- Excitation energy for single phonon in 70Zn: E(2+) = 0.885 MeV with deformation parameter β2 = 0.228
- Excitation energy for single phonon in 208Pb: E(3-) = 2.614 MeV with deformation parameter β3 = 0.11

## Context
This work extends the barrier distribution concept from fusion reactions, pioneered by Rowley, Satchler, and Stelson (1991), to quasi-elastic scattering as proposed by Timmers et al. (1995) and Hagino and Rowley (2004). It addresses the "surface diffuseness anomaly" identified by Newton et al. (2004), where fusion data require larger diffuseness parameters (0.75–1.5 fm) than the conventional 0.63 fm from elastic scattering analyses by Broglia and Winther (1991) and Christensen and Winther (1976). The coupled-channels analysis for 70Zn + 208Pb demonstrates that multi-phonon excitations (double quadrupole in projectile, triple octupole in target) are necessary to reproduce experimental barrier distributions for massive systems relevant to superheavy element synthesis, consistent with findings for 100Mo + 100Mo by Rowley et al. (2006). The work enables future systematic coupled-channels studies of other cold fusion reactions (48Ti, 54Cr, 56Fe, 64Ni + 208Pb) and motivates further precision measurements for deformed systems to resolve the diffuseness discrepancy.
