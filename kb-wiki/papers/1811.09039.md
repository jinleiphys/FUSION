# Energy dependence of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction and the two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ -- are they real?

> It is shown, that the energy dependence of the chiral based $\bar{K}N$ potentials, responsible for the ocurence of two poles in the $I=0$ sector is the consequence of applying the on-shell factorization approximation [1]. When the dynamical equation is solved without this approximation, the $T$-matrix has only one pole in the region of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the widely accepted two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) resonance is an artifact of the on-shell factorization approximation applied to chiral-based \(\bar{K}N\) potentials; when the dynamical equation is solved without this approximation, the \(T\)-matrix produces only a single pole in the Λ(1405) region.

## Method

The authors start from the lowest-order Weinberg-Tomozawa (WT) term of the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian and construct a multichannel separable potential for the \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma-\pi\Lambda\) system. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation exactly for two versions of the potential: the "full" energy-independent WT potential (\(\hat{V}\)) and its on-shell factorized energy-dependent counterpart (\(\hat{U}\)). Both potentials are fitted to the same set of experimental data (six low-energy \(K^-N\) cross sections, three threshold branching ratios \(\gamma, R_n, R_c\), and the kaonic hydrogen 1s level shift \(\Delta E\)) using dipole Yamaguchi form factors with adjustable cut-off parameters \(\beta_i\).

## Key numbers

- For the full potential \(\hat{V}\): single pole at \(z1 = (1425 - 21i)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For the on-shell factorized potential \(\hat{U}\): two poles at \(z1 = (1428 - 35i)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(z2 = (1384 - 62i)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Fitted kaonic hydrogen level shift: \(\hat{V}\) gives \(\Delta E = (350 - 279i)\ \mathrm{eV}\); \(\hat{U}\) gives \(\Delta E = (302 - 294i)\ \mathrm{eV}\); experimental value is \((283 \pm 36) - (271 \pm 46)i\ \mathrm{eV}\).
- Threshold branching ratio \(\gamma\): \(\hat{V}\) gives 2.32; \(\hat{U}\) gives 2.35; experimental value is \(2.36 \pm 0.04\).
- Threshold branching ratio \(R_c\): \(\hat{V}\) gives 0.671; \(\hat{U}\) gives 0.664; experimental value is \(0.664 \pm 0.011\).
- Threshold branching ratio \(R_n\): \(\hat{V}\) gives 0.202; \(\hat{U}\) gives 0.194; experimental value is \(0.189 \pm 0.015\).

## Context

This work directly challenges the established two-pole interpretation of the Λ(1405) resonance that originated from the on-shell factorization approximation introduced by Oset and Ramos (1998). The author shows that the energy dependence responsible for the second pole is an artifact of that approximation, and that a full treatment yields only one pole. This result questions the theoretical basis for the two-pole structure and suggests that previous analyses of CLAS photoproduction data (Roca and Oset, 2013) using two-pole potentials may need re-examination; preliminary fits with the single-pole potential \(\hat{V}\) to CLAS data are shown to be promising. The new energy-independent potential also avoids difficulties in applying energy-dependent interactions to few-body (\(n>2\)) systems.
