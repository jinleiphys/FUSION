# Low-frequency $K^π=0^{+}$ modes in deformed neutron-rich nuclei: Pairing- and $β$-vibrational modes of neutron

> Low-frequency $K^π=0^{+}$ states in deformed neutron-rich nuclei are investigated by means of the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism in the coordinate space. We have obtained the very strongly collective $K^π=0^{+}$ modes not only in neutron-rich Mg isotope but also in Cr and Fe isotopes in N=40 region, where the onset of nuclear deformation has been discussed. It is found that the spatially extended structure of neutron quasiparticle wave functions around the Fermi level brings about a striking enhancement of the transition strengths. It is also found that the fluctuation of the pairing field plays an important role in generating coherence among two-quasiparticle excitations of neutron.

## Key claim

Low-frequency \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) modes in deformed neutron-rich nuclei arise from coherent superposition of neutron two-quasiparticle excitations near the Fermi level, but the paper's central hedge is that this coherence is only achieved when the fluctuation of the pairing field (dynamical pairing) is included alongside the spatially extended structure of neutron wave functions; without the pairing-field fluctuation, the coherence among two-quasiparticle excitations is not generated.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in cylindrical coordinates with a deformed Woods-Saxon mean field and a density-dependent contact pairing interaction, then used the resulting quasiparticle basis to solve the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) matrix equation. The residual interaction in the particle-hole channel was a Skyrme-type force renormalized by a factor \(f_{ph}\) to place the spurious \(K^{\pi}=1^{+}\) rotational mode at zero energy, and the particle-particle channel was renormalized by \(f_{pp}\) to place the spurious number-fluctuation \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) mode at zero energy. Calculations were performed for \(^{34}\)Mg and for Cr and Fe isotopes with neutron numbers \(N=38\)–\(46\) at fixed quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2=0.3\)–\(0.4\).

## Key numbers

- The lowest \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) state in \(^{34}\)Mg lies at 1.37 MeV excitation energy and has an intrinsic isoscalar quadrupole transition strength of about 43 Weisskopf units (1 W.u. \(\simeq 6.5\) fm\(^4\) for \(^{34}\)Mg).
- The proton \(B(E2)\) strength for this state is \(15.9\;e^{2}\text{fm}^4\) in \(^{34}\)Mg, compared to \(3.4\;e^{2}\text{fm}^4\) in \(^{40}\)Mg from prior work.
- In \(^{62}\)Cr, the \((\nu[440]1/2)^2\) two-quasiparticle excitation contributes 86 fm\(^4\) (5.9 W.u.) to the lowest \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) state; in \(^{70}\)Cr, the \((\nu[431]1/2)^2\) excitation contributes 115 fm\(^4\) (6.7 W.u.).
- The root-mean-square radius of the \(\nu[440]1/2\) level in \(^{62}\)Cr is 5.1 fm, while the total neutron r.m.s. radius is 4.2 fm.
- The energy-weighted sum rule for the quadrupole \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) excitation is satisfied to 92.9% for \(^{34}\)Mg and 97.0% for \(^{72}\)Fe within the truncated model space.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier deformed QRPA study of Mg isotopes (Yoshida 2006) by systematically applying the same coordinate-space HFB+QRPA framework to neutron-rich Cr and Fe isotopes in the \(N=40\) region, where deformation onset had been discussed by Hannawald (1999), Sorlin (2003), and Lunardi (2007). It also goes beyond prior spherical QRPA calculations of pairing vibrations in neutron-rich nuclei by Matsuo (2001, 2005) and by Paar (2003) by including deformation and the coupling between pairing vibration and neutron-skin \(\beta\)-vibration. The results enable experimental searches for low-lying \(0^{+}\) states in deformed neutron-rich nuclei as signatures of combined pairing and shape collectivity.
