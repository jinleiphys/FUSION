# Subleading contributions to the chiral three-nucleon force I: long-range terms

> We derive the long-range contributions to the tree-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. We give both momentum and coordinate space representations.

## Key claim

The paper derives the long-range contributions to the three-nucleon force (3NF) at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) in chiral effective field theory, but only for the theory with pions and nucleons (no explicit delta degrees of freedom); the authors note that the convergence is expected to improve when spin-3/2 degrees of freedom are included, which will be addressed in a subsequent paper.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with pions and nucleons, evaluating one-loop diagrams with vertices up to chiral dimension $\Delta=2$ and tree diagrams with one insertion of $\Delta=2$ interactions, following Weinberg's power counting. They identified five contributing topologies at N$^3$LO, of which three are long-range (two-pion exchange, two-pion-one-pion exchange, and ring diagrams) and free of unknown low-energy constants; these were evaluated using dimensional regularization and the method of unitary transformation, with results given in both momentum and coordinate space representations.

## Key numbers

- The shifts to the low-energy constants from two-pion exchange diagrams at N$^3$LO are: $\delta c_1 = -0.13$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\delta c_3 = -\delta c_4 = 0.52$ GeV$^{-1}$, representing 20% to 30% corrections to the corresponding LECs
- The loop function $A(q) = \frac{1}{2q} \arctan \frac{q}{2M_\pi}$ appears in the two-pion exchange and two-pion-one-pion exchange contributions
- The coordinate-space scalar functions include $U_1(x) = e^{-x}/x$, $W_1(x) = e^{-2x}/(2x^2)$, and $U_2(x) = e^{-x}$ (dimensionless distances $x = M_\pi r$)
- The ring diagram contribution involves the function $U_2(x) = e^{-x}$ from the double-pion propagator integration

## Context

This work extends the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force at N$^2$LO (van Kolck 1994, Epelbaum 2002) to N$^3$LO, providing the first systematic corrections to the two-pion exchange 3NF. The results are compared to the infrared regularization approach of Ishikawa and Robilotta (2007), with agreement found when expanding in powers of $1/m$ and keeping leading terms. The long-range contributions derived here are free of unknown constants, enabling future precision studies of few-nucleon systems and addressing discrepancies such as the $A_y$ puzzle and deuteron break-up measurements; the shorter-range terms involving contact interactions and $1/m$ corrections are deferred to a subsequent paper.
