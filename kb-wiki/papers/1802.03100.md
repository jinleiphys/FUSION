# Determination of $s$- and $p$-wave $I=1/2$ $Kπ$ scattering amplitudes in $N_{\mathrm{f}}=2+1$ lattice QCD

> The elastic $I=1/2$, $s$- and $p$-wave kaon-pion scattering amplitudes are calculated using a single ensemble of anisotropic lattice QCD gauge field configurations with $N_{\mathrm{f}} = 2+1$ flavors of dynamical Wilson-clover fermions at $m_π = 230\mathrm{MeV}$. A large spatial extent of $L = 3.7\mathrm{fm}$ enables a good energy resolution while partial wave mixing due to the reduced symmetries of the finite volume is treated explicitly.The $p$-wave amplitude is well described by a Breit-Wigner shape with parameters $m_{K^{*}}/m_π = 3.808(18)$ and $g^{\mathrm{BW}}_{K^{*}Kπ} = 5.33(20)$ which are insensitive to the inclusion of $d$-wave mixing and variation of the $s$-wave parametrization. An effective range description of the near-threshold $s$-wave amplitude yields $m_πa_0 = -0.353(25)$.

## Key claim

The elastic $I=1/2$ $s$- and $p$-wave $K\pi$ scattering amplitudes are determined from a single ensemble of lattice QCD configurations at a pion mass of 230 MeV, with the $p$-wave amplitude well described by a Breit-Wigner shape and the $s$-wave amplitude yielding a scattering length, both insensitive to the inclusion of $d$-wave mixing and variation of the $s$-wave parametrization.

## Method

The authors used a single ensemble of anisotropic lattice QCD gauge field configurations with $N_f = 2+1$ flavors of dynamical Wilson-clover fermions at $m_\pi = 230$ MeV and a spatial extent of $L = 3.7$ fm. They calculated 22 finite-volume $K\pi$ energies and applied the formalism of Morningstar (2017) to treat partial wave mixing due to reduced symmetries of the finite volume, fitting both $\ell=0$ and $\ell=1$ contributions simultaneously to extract the infinite-volume scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- $m_{K^{*}}/m_\pi = 3.808(18)$: Breit-Wigner resonance mass parameter for the $K^{*}(892)$ in the $p$-wave amplitude, with statistical error only.
- $g^{\mathrm{BW}}_{K^{*}K\pi} = 5.33(20)$: Breit-Wigner coupling for the $K^{*}(892)$ decay to $K\pi$, with statistical error only.
- $m_\pi a_0 = -0.353(25)$: effective range description of the near-threshold $s$-wave scattering length, with statistical error only.
- Pion mass $m_\pi = 230$ MeV: the light quark mass used in the simulation.
- Spatial extent $L = 3.7$ fm: the size of the lattice volume enabling good energy resolution.

## Context

This work extends prior lattice QCD calculations of the $K^{*}(892)$ resonance by Prelovsek (2013), Wilson (2015), and Bali (2015), which used different numbers of flavors, pion masses, and spatial volumes. The results enable future precision tests of lepton universality in $B$ decays at LHCb and provide input for chiral effective theory studies of the $K_0^{*}(800)$ resonance, while also serving as a proof-of-principle for simultaneous fits to different $K$-matrix elements that can be applied to coupled-channel and meson-baryon systems.
