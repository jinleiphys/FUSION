# Resonant mesomolecule formation

## Key claim
The resonant formation rates of dtμ and ddμ mesomolecules with subsequent fusion can be calculated without multipole expansion of the perturbation potential, achieving agreement with experimental data at the level of 10–15% using purely theoretical values and no fitting parameter for the matrix elements.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrödinger equation with the exact Born-Oppenheimer potential U(ρ) for DX-molecules and mesomolecular complexes, computing matrix elements for transitions between vibrational and rotational states without resorting to the dipole or multipole expansion. A computer code sums Breit-Wigner resonance contributions to the formation probability, applies Doppler broadening to account for gas motion, and averages over the mesoatom energy spectrum. For ddμ formation in D₂ gas, the collision width dominates and a Boltzmann distribution in rotational quantum numbers is assumed before back decay.

## Key numbers
- dtμ binding energy |ε̃₁₁| is known with accuracy of order 1 meV.
- Underthreshold resonance energy for dtμ formation: E_r = -14 meV.
- Matrix element squared for dtμ transition ν=0→2, K=0→1: |⟨i|V|f⟩|² = 0.60 × 10⁻⁸ a.u.
- Fusion width Γ_f for ddμ: 460(70) μsec⁻¹ theoretical; best fit gives Γ_f = 397(40) μsec⁻¹.
- ddμ bound state energy: ε̄ = -1.966 eV (compared to -0.632 eV for dtμ).
- ddμ resonance energy for K=0→1 transition: E_cʳ = 4.2 meV.
- Doppler broadening width: Δ_DQ = (4E_QLʳ T/A)^{1/2}.
- Multipole expansion parameter β₂R(MΩ)^{1/2} ≈ 0.2–0.35.
- Dipole approximation overestimates the matrix element by more than a factor of 3.
- Previous models required an additional fitting factor |C_matr|² = 0.56, overestimating λ_f^F by a factor of 1.8.
- Agreement with dtμ data at 15% level without fitting parameters.
- Agreement with ddμ data at 10% level using purely theoretical values; relative accuracy of 3% for λ_f^{3/2} and 4% for λ_f^{1/2} with Γ_f as single fitting parameter.
- Discrepancy up to 14% at T = 28.3 K attributed to non-equilibrium mesoatom spectrum.

## Context
This work extends the Vesman mechanism for resonant mesomolecule formation, originally developed in the 1970s–1980s, by removing the dipole approximation used in standard theory (Refs. 2,3) and incorporating finite resonance width and subthreshold resonances. The method builds on the authors' prior approach (Petrov and Petrov, Ref. 7) that uses only the asymptotic wave function and exact binding energy rather than the full molecular wave function. The results enable accurate predictions for muon-catalyzed fusion rates without empirical scaling factors, resolving discrepancies noted in earlier comparisons with data from IAE (1993, 2001) and PNPI (1998). The remaining open question is the effect of non-equilibrium mesoatom acceleration in ortho-para deuterium mixtures at temperatures below 70 K.
