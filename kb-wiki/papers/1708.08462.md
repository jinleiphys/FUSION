# Nucleon Axial Radius and Muonic Hydrogen - A New Analysis and Review

> Weak capture in muonic hydrogen ($μ$H) as a probe of the chiral properties and nucleon structure predictions of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is reviewed. A recent determination of the axial-vector charge radius squared, $r_A^2(z\; {\rm exp.}) = 0.46(22)\;{\rm fm}^2$, from a model independent $z$ expansion analysis of neutrino-nucleon scattering data is employed in conjunction with the MuCap measurement of the singlet muonic hydrogen capture rate, $Λ_{\rm singlet}^{\rm MuCap} = 715.6(7.4)\;{\rm s}^{-1}$, to update the induced pseudoscalar nucleon coupling: $\bar{g}_P^{\rm MuCap} = 8.23(83)$ derived from experiment, and $\bar{g}_P^{\rm theory} = 8.25(25)$ predicted by chiral perturbation theory. Accounting for correlated errors this implies $\bar{g}_P^{\rm theory}/\bar{g}_P^{\rm MuCap}= 1.00(8)$, confirming theory at the 8% level. If instead, the predicted expression for $\bar{g}_P^{\rm theory}$ is employed as input, then the capture rate alone determines $r_A^2(μ{\rm H})=0.46(24)\, {\rm fm}^2$, or together with the independent $z$ expansion neutrino scattering results, a weighted average $r_A^2({\rm ave.}) = 0.46(16)\, {\rm fm}^2$. Sources of theoretical uncertainty are critically examined and potential experimental improvements are described that can reduce the capture rate error by about a factor of 3. Muonic hydrogen can thus provide a precise and independent $r_A^2$ value which may be compared with other determinations, such as ongoing lattice gauge theory calculations. The importance of an improved $r_A^2$ determination for phenomenology is illustrated by considering the impact on critical neutrino-nucleus cross sections at neutrino oscillation experiments.

# Nucleon Axial Radius and Muonic Hydrogen - A New Analysis and Review

## Key claim

The comparison between the experimentally determined induced pseudoscalar nucleon coupling from muonic hydrogen capture and the chiral perturbation theory prediction confirms theory at the 8% level, but this agreement is weakened only slightly (from ±6%) by the larger uncertainty in the axial radius from the model-independent z expansion analysis compared to the older dipole fit. The muonic hydrogen capture rate can be used in reverse to determine a competitive value of the nucleon axial radius squared, yielding r_A^2(μH) = 0.46(24) fm^2.

## Method

The authors performed an updated analysis of the MuCap singlet muonic hydrogen capture rate measurement, Λ_singlet^MuCap = 715.6(7.4) s^{-1}, incorporating improved electroweak radiative corrections at the 0.10% uncertainty level. They combined this with a model-independent z expansion analysis of neutrino-deuteron scattering data that yields r_A^2(z exp.) = 0.46(22) fm^2, and used both inputs to extract the induced pseudoscalar coupling g̅_P and, alternatively, to determine r_A^2 from the capture rate alone using the chiral perturbation theory prediction for g̅_P as input.

## Key numbers

- MuCap singlet muonic hydrogen capture rate: Λ_singlet^MuCap = 715.6(7.4) s^{-1}, with statistical uncertainty 5.4 s^{-1} and systematic uncertainty 5.1 s^{-1}
- Induced pseudoscalar coupling from experiment: g̅_P^MuCap = 8.23(83)
- Induced pseudoscalar coupling from chiral perturbation theory: g̅_P^theory = 8.25(25)
- Ratio of theory to experiment: g̅_P^theory / g̅_P^MuCap = 1.00(8), confirming theory at 8% level
- Axial radius squared from z expansion analysis of neutrino scattering: r_A^2(z exp.) = 0.46(22) fm^2
- Axial radius squared from muonic hydrogen alone: r_A^2(μH) = 0.46(24) fm^2
- Weighted average axial radius squared: r_A^2(ave.) = 0.46(16) fm^2
- Quasielastic neutrino-neutron cross section at E_ν = 1 GeV using dipole ansatz: σ = 10.57(14) × 10^{-39} cm^2
- Quasielastic neutrino-neutron cross section at E_ν = 1 GeV using z expansion: σ = 10.1(9) × 10^{-39} cm^2
- Ortho-molecular capture rate in (ppμ)^+: Λ_ortho = 544 s^{-1}
- Para-molecular capture rate in (ppμ)^+: Λ_para = 215 s^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Meyer et al. (2016), who first applied the model-independent z expansion to neutrino-deuteron scattering data and found a dramatically larger uncertainty on r_A^2 compared to the historical dipole fit value of 0.454(13) fm^2. The paper updates the MuCap result from Andreev et al. (2012, 2015) and reconciles it with the new axial radius uncertainty, demonstrating that muonic hydrogen can provide an independent determination of r_A^2 that may be compared with ongoing lattice QCD calculations. The improved r_A^2 determination is shown to be critical for reducing uncertainties in neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for next-generation long baseline oscillation experiments such as those described by Adams et al. (2013), Acciarri et al. (2015), and Diwan et al. (2016).
