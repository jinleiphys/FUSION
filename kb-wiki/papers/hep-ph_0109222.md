# Charged Current Universality in the MSSM

> We compute the complete one-loop contributions to low-energy charged current weak interaction observables in the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM). We obtain the constraints on the MSSM parameter space which arise when precision low-energy charged current data are analyzed in tandem with measurements of the muon anomaly. While the data allow the presence of at least one light neutralino, they also imply a pattern of mass splittings among first and second generation sleptons and squarks which contradict predictions of widely used models for supersymmetry breaking mediation.

## Key claim

The paper computes the complete one-loop MSSM corrections to low-energy charged current observables and finds that, while the data allow at least one light neutralino, the combined constraints from precision charged current data and the muon anomaly imply a pattern of mass splittings among first and second generation sleptons and squarks that contradicts predictions of widely used supersymmetry breaking mediation models.

## Method

The authors computed the full one-loop MSSM electroweak radiative corrections to the Fermi constant extracted from muon decay (G_mu), the effective Fermi constant for light quark beta-decay (G_F^beta), and the pion leptonic branching ratio R_e/mu. They then compared the difference Delta r_beta - Delta r_mu (which isolates non-universal vertex, box, and external leg corrections) to precision experimental data on superallowed nuclear beta-decays, the muon anomaly, and the W boson mass, using an effective field theory framework to separate short-distance and long-distance strong interaction effects.

## Key numbers

- The 2-sigma constraint on the SUSY contribution to muon decay is: -0.0098 <= Delta r_mu^(susy) <= 0.0046, determined primarily by experimental uncertainties in M_W and m_t.
- For nearly degenerate squarks (kappa_q ~ 1), the charged current data require M_mu2 >= 3 M_mu1 (smuon mass ratio kappa_mu >= 3) to keep the non-universal SUSY correction negative.
- In the no-mixing scenario with tan(beta) > 1, the required hierarchy is M_mu^2 > 3 M_q^2 (up to logarithmic corrections) to maintain the correct sign for the SUSY contribution to G_F^beta.
- For the solution with maximal d-squark mixing and kappa_mu ~ 1 (as required by g-2), the required d-squark mass ratio is 3 <= kappa_d <= 4.5, implying M_d2 ~ 450 GeV for M_d1 ~ 115 GeV, or M_LR^2/m_d = (mu tan(beta) - A_d) ~ 10^7 GeV.
- MSSM loop effects can reduce the apparent CKM unitarity violation by approximately 0.5 sigma when parameters are chosen near the edges of allowed regions.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of supersymmetric effects on charged current universality, which had considered only R-parity violating models (Barger, 2000; Ramsey-Musolf, 2000; Dreiner, 1997), by performing the first complete analysis in the R-parity conserving MSSM. The results directly confront the 2.2-sigma deviation from CKM unitarity reported by Hardy (1999) and the 2.6-sigma deviation of the muon anomaly from the SM prediction reported by Brown (2001). The paper enables future precision measurements of the neutron lifetime (at NIST), the neutron beta-decay asymmetry parameter A (at LANSCE), and the pion beta-decay branching ratio (at PSI) to further test the MSSM parameter space and constrain models of SUSY-breaking mediation such as mSUGRA and gauge-mediated models.
