# Directed, Elliptic and Triangular Flows in Asymmetric Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this paper we propose to thoroughly investigate asymmetric nuclear collisions both in the fixed target mode at the laboratory energy below 5 GeV per nucleon and in the collider mode with a center of mass energy below 11 GeV per nucleon. Using the UrQMD transport model, we demonstrate a strong enhancement of directed and elliptic flow coefficients for the midcentral asymmetric nuclear collisions compared to symmetric collisions. We argue that such an enhancement is due to the disappearance of the nuclear shadowing effect on the side of the smaller projectile nucleus. An analysis of the energy and centrality dependencies of the directed, elliptic and triangular flows at midrapidity shows us their sensitivity to the details of the employed model of hadronic interaction. In general, the flow patters found for asymmetric nuclear collisions have a very rich and complicated structure of energy and centrality dependencies compared to the flows found for symmetric collisions and are worth to be investigated experimentally. The directed, elliptic, and triangular flow coefficients are computed for target nuclei containing high density fluctuations and thoroughly compared with that ones obtained in the absence of such fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for semi-peripheral asymmetric nuclear collisions (ANC) with a specific choice of impact parameter close to \(b_{ANC} \approx 1.1\,{\rm fm}\, \cdot (A^\frac{1}{3} - B^\frac{1}{3}) \pm 1\) fm, the nuclear shadowing effect on the side of the smaller projectile nucleus disappears, leading to a strong enhancement of directed and elliptic flow coefficients compared to symmetric collisions; this enhancement is conditional on the geometry and is not present for peripheral or most central ANC.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, supplemented with in-medium potentials, to simulate asymmetric Ne+Au collisions at laboratory energies from 2 to 10 AGeV. They computed the directed (\(v_1\)), elliptic (\(v_2\)), and triangular (\(v_3\)) flow coefficients for charged particles and pions at midrapidity as functions of impact parameter and collision energy. They also introduced a dense spot configuration by placing 20 nucleons from the Au target in a narrow Gaussian distribution to study the effect of high density fluctuations on the flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For Ne+Au ANC at \(E_{\rm lab} = 2\) AGeV, both charged particles and pions have positive \(v_2^{AS}\) for semi-peripheral collisions, while for central and peripheral collisions the pionic \(v_2^{AS}\) is negative.
- The directed flow coefficient \(v_1^{AS}\) for charged particles at midrapidity (\(|y| < 0.1\)) is essentially non-zero for ANC, whereas it is zero at \(y = 0\) in symmetric nuclear collisions.
- For the dense spot configuration at \(E_{\rm lab} \ge 5\) AGeV, the \(v_1^{AS}\) coefficient is reduced to 50% of the value without the dense spot.
- For \(E_{\rm lab} = 2-3\) AGeV, the \(v_3^{AS}\) coefficient for charged particles has a maximum in semi-peripheral collisions and is negative at impact parameters \(b \approx 1\) fm (\(b < 1\) fm) and \(b \ge 6\) fm.
- The elliptic flow in symmetric collisions is zero at \(E_{\rm lab} = 3.3\) AGeV, marking the transition from out-of-plane to in-plane elliptic flow.

## Context

This work extends the study of Fourier flow coefficients (Voloshin 1996) from symmetric heavy ion collisions to asymmetric nuclear collisions (ANC), which had only been explored in a few earlier works (Baumgardt 1975, Hofmann 1976, Stocker 1981) before systematic flow analysis was proposed. The results are relevant for the planned BMN program at JINR and the FAIR heavy ion program, where the authors suggest that ANC can be used to scan the interior of the target nucleus and study high density fluctuations. The paper enables future experimental investigations of the hadronic matter equation of state at high densities and the possible formation of the mixed quark-hadron phase or chiral quarkyonic phase in this energy range.
