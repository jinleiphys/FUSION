# Exclusive $ρ^0$ Meson Photoproduction with a Leading Neutron at HERA

> A first measurement is presented of exclusive photoproduction of $ρ^0$ mesons associated with leading neutrons at HERA. The data were taken with the H1 detector in the years $2006$ and $2007$ at a centre-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s}=319$ GeV and correspond to an integrated luminosity of $1.16$ pb$^{-1}$. The $ρ^0$ mesons with transverse momenta $p_T<1$ GeV are reconstructed from their decays to charged pions, while leading neutrons carrying a large fraction of the incoming proton momentum, $x_L>0.35$, are detected in the Forward Neutron Calorimeter. The phase space of the measurement is defined by the photon virtuality $Q^2 < 2$ GeV$^2$, the total energy of the photon-proton system $20 < W_{γp} < 100$ GeV and the polar angle of the leading neutron $θ_n < 0.75$ mrad. The cross section of the reaction $γp \to ρ^0 n π^+$ is measured as a function of several variables. The data are interpreted in terms of a double peripheral process, involving pion exchange at the proton vertex followed by elastic photoproduction of a $ρ^0$ meson on the virtual pion. In the framework of one-pion-exchange dominance the elastic cross section of photon-pion scattering, $σ^{\rm el}(γπ^+ \to ρ^0π^+)$, is extracted. The value of this cross section indicates significant absorptive corrections for the exclusive reaction $γp\toρ^0 n π^+$.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first measurement of exclusive photoproduction of ρ⁰ mesons associated with leading neutrons at HERA, interpreting the data within a one-pion-exchange framework to extract the elastic γπ⁺ → ρ⁰π⁺ cross section; the extracted cross section value indicates significant absorptive corrections, meaning the simple pion-exchange picture alone does not fully describe the reaction.

## Method

The H1 Collaboration analyzed data taken in 2006 and 2007 at a center-of-mass energy of √s = 319 GeV, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 1.16 pb⁻¹. ρ⁰ mesons with transverse momenta pT < 1 GeV were reconstructed from their decays to charged pions, while leading neutrons with xL > 0.35 were detected in the Forward Neutron Calorimeter, with the phase space defined by Q² < 2 GeV², 20 < Wγp < 100 GeV, and neutron polar angle θn < 0.75 mrad. The cross section of the reaction γp → ρ⁰ n π⁺ was measured as a function of several variables and interpreted using a double peripheral process involving pion exchange at the proton vertex followed by elastic photoproduction of a ρ⁰ meson on the virtual pion.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: √s = 319 GeV, with positron energy Ee = 27.6 GeV and proton energy Ep = 920 GeV
- Integrated luminosity: 1.16 pb⁻¹
- Photon virtuality: Q² < 2 GeV², with average value 0.04 GeV²
- Leading neutron energy fraction: xL > 0.35
- ρ⁰ meson transverse momentum: pT < 1 GeV
- Photon-proton center-of-mass energy range: 20 < Wγp < 100 GeV
- Leading neutron polar angle: θn < 0.75 mrad
- The extracted elastic γπ⁺ → ρ⁰π⁺ cross section indicates absorptive corrections quantified by Kabs = 0.44 ± 0.11

## Context

This measurement extends previous HERA studies of semi-inclusive leading neutron production in e+p → e+n+X (H1 Collaboration, 1998; ZEUS Collaboration, 2002-2007), which demonstrated dominance of pion exchange processes. The work provides the first extraction of the quasi-elastic γπ → ρ⁰π cross section, enabling tests of Regge theory and the Drell-Hiida-Deck model framework previously applied to lower-energy nucleon-nucleon and meson-nucleon collisions (ISR experiments, 1970s; low-energy kaon and pion experiments). The results enable future studies of absorptive corrections and final-state interactions in exclusive reactions at HERA.
