# Light Pseudoscalar Mesons: an Inverse Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Glimpse

> Achieving self-consistent simultaneous interpretations of pions and kaons as bound states of quark and antiquark and as the (almost) massless boson states related, according to Goldstone's theorem, to the dynamical (and explicit) breakdown of the chiral symmetries of QCD still represents a major challenge. Applying inversion techniques to conveniently simplified versions of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, governing bound states in quantum field theory, enables us to get along a straightforward route a qualitative idea of how the underlying effective interaction might look like.

## Key claim
By applying inversion techniques to a simplified, instantaneous (three-dimensional) Bethe-Salpeter equation, the authors obtain a qualitative idea of the effective interaction that could simultaneously describe pions and kaons as quark-antiquark bound states and as near-massless Goldstone bosons from chiral symmetry breaking. The hedge is that this is a "glimpse" using "conveniently simplified versions" of the full formalism, not a definitive derivation.

## Method
The authors start from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for fermion-antifermion bound states, simplified by an instantaneous (three-dimensional) reduction with a Fierz-symmetric, spherically symmetric interaction kernel. They take Euclidean-space quark propagator functions (mass and wave-function renormalization) from a prior solution (Maris and Tandy, 1999) and, using a chiral-limit identity connecting the quark propagator to the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for a zero-mass pseudoscalar meson, they obtain the ground-state Salpeter component φ₂(p) in momentum space. This is then transformed to configuration space, and the bound-state equation is inverted to extract the central potential V(r).

## Key numbers
- Average interquark distance of the pion: ⟨r⟩ = 0.483 fm.
- Root-mean-square radius of the pion from the inversion: √⟨r²⟩ = 0.535 fm.
- Experimentally determined electromagnetic charge radius of the pion: √⟨r_π²⟩ = (0.672 ± 0.008) fm.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior program of inverting the Bethe-Salpeter formalism (Lucha and Schöberl, 2013, 2015, 2016) to extract effective interactions for light mesons. It uses quark propagator functions from Maris and Tandy (1999, 2000) as input. The resulting square-well-like potential and the consistency check (where the wave-function overlap between input and output equals unity) suggest the approach can be applied further to study other mesons or to refine the effective interaction kernel.
