# Excited Baryons in Large Nc QCD: Matching the 1/Nc expansion to quark models using the permutation group S_N

> We show how to match quark models to the 1/Nc expansion of QCD. As an example we discuss in detail the mass operator of orbitally excited baryons and match it to the one-gluon exchange and the one-boson exchange variants of the quark model. The matching procedure is very general and makes use of the transformation properties of states and operators under S_N^{orb} \times S_N^{sp-fl}, the permutation group acting on the orbital and spin-flavor degrees of freedom of N quarks.

## Key claim

The paper shows that quark models for orbitally excited baryons can be matched to the 1/Nc expansion of QCD by classifying states and operators under the permutation group S_N acting on orbital and spin-flavor degrees of freedom; the matching is performed for the mass operator and compared to existing fits, finding that flavor-dependent interactions (as in the one-boson exchange model) cannot be neglected.

## Method

The authors construct baryon states with mixed-symmetric (MS) spin-flavor symmetry using Young operator basis functions for N quarks, and write the complete spin-flavor-orbital wave function as an S_N-symmetric inner product of MS orbital and MS spin-flavor components. They decompose two-body quark-quark interactions (spin-spin, quadrupole, spin-orbit) from the one-gluon exchange (OGE) and one-boson exchange (OBE) models into irreducible representations of S_N^{orb} × S_N^{sp-fl}, compute reduced matrix elements on the MS states, and match the resulting spin-flavor structure to the operator basis of the 1/Nc expansion for orbitally excited baryons.

## Key numbers

- For assignment 1 of observed L=1 baryons into contracted symmetry towers: c2^(0) = 83 ± 14 MeV, c3^(0) = -188 ± 28 MeV
- For assignment 3: c2^(0) = -12 ± 16 MeV, c3^(0) = 142 ± 38 MeV
- The OGE model predicts c3 = 0 at leading order, while the OBE model gives c3 = (2/3) g̃_A^2 K_dir (with g̃_A^2 scaling as O(N_c^0))
- The OGE model predicts c5 = - (g^2 N_c/4)(3 J_dir^s + J_dir^a) and c8 = - (g^2 N_c/2) K_dir, while the OBE model gives c5 = 0 and c8 = 0
- The OBE model predicts c4 = (1/8) g̃_A^2 (3 J_dir^s + J_dir^a) and the relation c2 = -c4

## Context

This work extends the S_3 permutation group analysis of Collins, Georgi, and others (1998) for N_c=3 to arbitrary N_c, providing a formal justification for the "core + excited quark" decomposition used in the 1/N_c expansion by Carlson, Carone, Goity, and Lebed (1998). The matching procedure enables direct comparison of quark model predictions with the general 1/N_c operator expansion for orbitally excited baryons, and the comparison with existing fits favors the Goldstone boson exchange model over the one-gluon exchange model, supporting the chiral quark picture proposed by Manohar and Georgi (1984).
