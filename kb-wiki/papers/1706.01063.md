# Point-Particle Effective Field Theory III: Relativistic Fermions and the Dirac Equation

> We formulate point-particle effective field theory (PPEFT) for relativistic spin-half fermions interacting with a massive, charged finite-sized source using a first-quantized effective field theory for the heavy compact object and a second-quantized language for the lighter fermion with which it interacts. This description shows how to determine the near-source boundary condition for the Dirac field in terms of the relevant physical properties of the source, and reduces to the standard choices in the limit of a point source. Using a first-quantized effective description is appropriate when the compact object is sufficiently heavy, and is simpler than (though equivalent to) the effective theory that treats the compact source in a second-quantized way. As an application we use the PPEFT to parameterize the leading energy shift for the bound energy levels due to finite-sized source effects in a model-independent way, allowing these effects to be fit in precision measurements. Besides capturing finite-source-size effects, the PPEFT treatment also efficiently captures how other short-distance source interactions can shift bound-state energy levels, such as due to vacuum polarization (through the Uehling potential) or strong interactions for Coulomb bound states of hadrons, or any hypothetical new short-range forces sourced by nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper extends the point-particle effective field theory (PPEFT) formalism to relativistic spin-half fermions interacting with a massive, charged finite-sized source, showing that the leading finite-size energy shift for Dirac fermions scales as \( \delta \omega_D \sim Z\alpha R^2 (m Z\alpha / n)^3 \propto (Z\alpha)^4 m^3 R^2 \), which differs from the Klein-Gordon scaling \( \delta \omega_{KG} \sim (Z\alpha)^2 R (m Z\alpha / n)^3 / m \propto (Z\alpha)^5 m^2 R \) found in prior work; this difference arises because relativistic effects are not small at radii \( r \sim R \) when \( R \lesssim Z\alpha / m \).

## Method

The authors formulate a mixed first-quantized/second-quantized effective field theory where the heavy compact source is treated as a point particle with a world-line action and the lighter fermion is described by a second-quantized Dirac field. They derive near-source boundary conditions for the Dirac field by integrating the equations of motion over a Gaussian pillbox around the source, relating the source action parameters \( c_s \) and \( c_v \) to the ratios of radial wavefunction components \( g_+/f_+ \) and \( f_-/g_- \). The renormalization group running of these couplings is obtained by demanding that physical observables remain independent of the arbitrary pillbox radius \( \epsilon \), using the small-\( r \) asymptotic forms of the Dirac solutions in a Coulomb potential.

## Key numbers

* The leading finite-size energy shift for Dirac fermions scales as \( \delta \omega_D \sim Z\alpha R^2 (m Z\alpha / n)^3 \propto (Z\alpha)^4 m^3 R^2 \), where \( R \) is the source size, \( m \) the fermion mass, \( Z\alpha \) the source charge, and \( n \) the principal quantum number.
* The corresponding Klein-Gordon shift scales as \( \delta \omega_{KG} \sim (Z\alpha)^2 R (m Z\alpha / n)^3 / m \propto (Z\alpha)^5 m^2 R \), giving a ratio \( \delta \omega_{KG} / \delta \omega_D \sim Z\alpha / (mR) \), which is order unity for electrons where \( mR \sim Z\alpha \).
* The dimensionless RG coupling \( \hat\lambda \) for both parity-even and parity-odd \( j = 1/2 \) states satisfies \( \epsilon \, d\hat\lambda / d\epsilon = (1 - \hat\lambda^2)/2 \) when \( Z\alpha = 0 \), with fixed points at \( \hat\lambda_\star = \pm 1 \).
* The parameter \( \zeta = \sqrt{(j+1/2)^2 - (Z\alpha)^2} \) appears in the small-\( r \) power-law solutions \( f_\pm, g_\pm \propto r^{\zeta-1} \) and \( r^{-\zeta-1} \).

## Context

This work extends the PPEFT formalism developed for nonrelativistic and spinless relativistic particles in Burgess et al. (PPEFT I and II, 2016-2017) to spin-half fermions, showing that the finite-size energy shifts for Dirac particles follow the standard \( R^2 \) scaling rather than the linear \( R \) scaling found for Klein-Gordon particles. The formalism provides a model-independent parameterization of finite-source-size effects in bound-state energy levels, applicable to precision measurements in muonic atoms, hadronic Coulomb bound states, and searches for new short-range forces, and connects to prior work on nuclear charge-radius effects by Friar (1979) and Zemach (1956).
