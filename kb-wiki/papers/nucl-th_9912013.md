# Effects of HBT correlations on flow measurements

> The methods currently used to measure collective flow in nucleus--nucleus collisions assume that the only azimuthal correlations between particles are those arising from their correlation with the reaction plane. However, quantum HBT correlations also produce short range azimuthal correlations between identical particles. This creates apparent azimuthal anisotropies of a few percent when pions are used to estimate the direction of the reaction plane. These should not be misinterpreted as originating from collective flow. In particular, we show that the peculiar behaviour of the directed and elliptic flow of pions observed by NA49 at low p_T can be entirely understood in terms of HBT correlations. Such correlations also produce apparent higher Fourier harmonics (of order n larger than 3) of the azimuthal distribution, with magnitudes of the order of 1%, which should be looked for in the data.

# Effects of HBT correlations on flow measurements

## Key claim

Quantum HBT correlations between identical pions produce short-range azimuthal correlations that create apparent flow anisotropies of a few percent when pions are used to estimate the reaction plane; these spurious signals can fully explain the peculiar low-transverse-momentum behavior of directed and elliptic flow observed by NA49, and should not be misinterpreted as genuine collective flow.

## Method

The authors calculate the contribution of HBT correlations to the two-particle azimuthal correlation function using a standard Gaussian parametrization of the HBT correlation function in the longitudinal comoving system (LCMS), with radii R_s, R_o, R_L and strength parameter lambda. They then substitute this HBT-induced correlation into the standard flow analysis formula (which assumes all azimuthal correlations arise from flow) to compute the spurious flow coefficients v_n^HBT. Finally, they subtract the HBT contribution from the measured NA49 correlation data to obtain corrected flow values for pions.

## Key numbers

- For a semi-peripheral Pb-Pb collision at 158 GeV per nucleon, the typical HBT radius R ~ 4 fm, giving 1/R ~ 50 MeV/c, much smaller than the average transverse momentum ~ 400 MeV/c
- The spurious flow magnitude is estimated as |v_n^HBT(D)| ~ 3% using R=4 fm, <p_T> ~ <m_T> ~ 400 MeV/c, and Delta y = 2
- After subtraction of HBT correlations, the integrated flow values change from v_1^measured(D) = -3.0 +/- 0.1% to v_1(D) = -2.5%, and from v_2^measured(D) = 3.0 +/- 0.1% to v_2(D) = 2.6%
- The spurious flow maximum occurs at p_Tmax ~ 60*sqrt(n) MeV/c, e.g., approximately 60 MeV/c for n=1 and 85 MeV/c for n=2
- Higher Fourier harmonics (n >= 3) are predicted to have spurious magnitudes of order 1%

## Context

This work extends the standard flow analysis framework of Danielewicz and Odyniec (1985) and Voloshin and Poskanzer (1998), which assumes that the only azimuthal correlations between particles arise from their correlation with the reaction plane. The paper demonstrates that HBT correlations, previously studied by Mrowczynski (1998) for their azimuthal effects, produce significant non-flow contributions that must be subtracted. This enables corrected flow measurements for pions at SPS energies and predicts that higher harmonic coefficients (v_3, v_4, etc.) reported by NA45 at Quark Matter '99 are largely due to HBT correlations rather than genuine collective flow.
