# Strongly interacting matter in a sphere at nonzero magnetic field

> We investigate the chiral phase transition within a sphere under a uniform background magnetic field. The Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is employed and the MIT boundary condition is imposed for the spherical confinement. Using the wave expansion method, the diagonalizable Hamiltonian and energy spectrum are derived for the system. By solving the gap equation in the NJL model, the influence of magnetic field on quark matter in a sphere is studied. It is found that inverse magnetic catalysis occurs at small radii, while magnetic catalysis occurs at large radii. Additionally, both magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis are observed at the intermediate radii ($R\approx4$ fm).

# Strongly interacting matter in a sphere at nonzero magnetic field

## Key claim
The chiral phase transition in quark matter confined to a sphere under a uniform magnetic field exhibits both magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis depending on the sphere radius, with the transition between these regimes occurring at intermediate radii around 4 fm. The inverse magnetic catalysis at small radii is attributed to the anomalous contribution from the lowest orbital level and the intrinsic truncation of Landau levels due to the finite size, rather than the thermal effects that drive inverse magnetic catalysis in infinite systems.

## Method
The authors employed the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit (zero current quark mass) with the MIT bag boundary condition imposed on a spherical cavity. They first solved the free Dirac equation in spherical coordinates to obtain the zero-field eigenstates, then diagonalized the full Hamiltonian including the magnetic field interaction using these eigenstates as a basis (wave expansion method). The gap equation was solved self-consistently at zero temperature and zero chemical potential to obtain the effective quark mass as a function of magnetic field strength for spheres of different radii, using a three-momentum cutoff regularization with cutoff momentum Lambda = 1000 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff momentum: Lambda = 1000 MeV
- Coupling constants considered: G = 24/Lambda^2 and G = 32/Lambda^2
- Sphere radii studied: R = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, and 10 fm
- Intermediate radius where both magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis are observed: R approximately 4 fm
- Charge fraction used for the one-flavor model: q = 1

## Context
This work extends previous studies of finite-size effects on chiral phase transitions in cylindrical geometries (Chen et al., 2016; Sadooghi et al., 2021) to the more realistic spherical confinement relevant to quark-gluon matter in heavy-ion collisions. The results contrast with the standard NJL model in infinite space (Gusynin et al., 1996; RevModPhys.88.025001; Menezes, 2008), which exhibits only magnetic catalysis at zero temperature. The findings enable more realistic modeling of QCD fireballs produced in heavy-ion collisions, where both strong magnetic fields (up to 10^15-10^18 G) and finite size effects (2-10 fm) are present, and suggest that future work should incorporate finite temperature and nonzero current quark mass in a two-flavor model.
