# Algebraic model of baryon resonances

> We discuss recent calculations of electromagnetic form factors and strong decay widths of nucleon and delta resonances. The calculations are done in a collective constituent model of the nucleon, in which the baryons are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of an oblate top.

# Algebraic model of baryon resonances

## Key claim
The collective constituent model, which interprets baryons as rotations and vibrations of an oblate top, can simultaneously describe electromagnetic form factors and strong decay widths of nucleon and delta resonances, but the model systematically underestimates the $N\eta$ decay width of the $N(1535)S_{11}$ resonance, suggesting that this state is not a conventional $q^3$ configuration.

## Method
The authors employ an algebraic collective model where baryon resonances are treated as rotations and vibrations of a string-like oblate top configuration, with wave functions spread over many oscillator shells. Electromagnetic form factors and strong decay widths are calculated by folding transition operators with a probability distribution $g(\beta) = \beta^2 e^{-\beta/a}/2a^3$ for charge and magnetization along the strings, where $a$ is a scale parameter determined from a fit to nucleon charge radii and elastic form factors. For electroproduction, the scale parameter is made energy-dependent via $a = a_0(1 + \xi(W-M)/M)$ to account for hadron swelling at higher resonance masses.

## Key numbers
- The fit to 3 and 4 star nucleon and delta resonances gives an r.m.s. deviation of 39 MeV.
- The scale parameter determined from nucleon elastic form factors is $a = 0.232$ fm.
- The calculated $N\pi$ decay width for $N(1535)S_{11}$ is 85 MeV, compared to the experimental value of $79 \pm 38$ MeV.
- The calculated $N\eta$ decay width for $N(1535)S_{11}$ is 0.1 MeV, compared to the experimental value of $74 \pm 39$ MeV.
- The calculated $N\pi$ decay width for $N(1520)D_{13}$ is 115 MeV, compared to the experimental value of $67 \pm 9$ MeV.
- The calculated $N\pi$ decay width for $N(1680)F_{15}$ is 41 MeV, compared to the experimental value of $84 \pm 9$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the algebraic collective model of Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994) to simultaneously treat electromagnetic and strong couplings, contrasting with nonrelativistic and relativized quark potential models that emphasize single-particle aspects of quark dynamics. The finding that the $N(1535)S_{11}$ resonance has a large $N\eta$ width not reproduced by the $q^3$ collective model points toward the need for additional configurations, such as a quasi-bound meson-baryon $S$ wave resonance (Kaiser, 1995) or an exotic $q^4\bar{q}$ state, which the authors identify as the next step for extending the model space.
