# A New-Old Approach to Composite Scalars with Chiral Fermion Constituents

> We develop a dynamical, Lorentz invariant theory of composite scalars in configuration space consisting of chiral fermions, interacting by the perturbative exchange of a massive "gluon" of coupling $g_0$ and mass $M_0^2$ (the coloron model). The formalism is inspired by, but goes beyond, old ideas of Yukawa and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. It yields a non-pointlike internal wave-function of the bound state, $ϕ(r)$, which satisfies a Schrödinger-Klein-Gordon (SKG) equation with eigenvalue $μ^2$. For super-critical coupling, $g_0 >g_{0c}$, we have $μ^2< 0$ leading to spontaneous symmetry breaking. The binding of chiral fermions is semiclassical, and not loop-level as in NJL. The mass scale is determined by the interaction as in NJL. We mainly focus on the short-distance, large $M_0^2$ limit, yielding an NJL pointlike interaction, but the bound state internal wave-function, $ϕ(r)$, remains spatially extended and dilutes $ϕ(0)$. This leads to power-law suppression of the induced Yukawa and quartic couplings and requires radically less fine-tuning of a hierarchy than does the NJL model. We include a discussion of loop corrections of the theory. A realistic top--condensation model appears possible.

## Key claim

The paper develops a semiclassical, configuration-space description of composite scalars bound from chiral fermions via a massive "coloron" exchange, arguing that the bound state's internal wave-function remains spatially extended even in the short-distance limit, which suppresses the induced Yukawa and quartic couplings by power laws and radically reduces the fine-tuning required for a mass hierarchy compared to the pointlike Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model.

## Method

The author introduces a bilocal field \(\Phi(x,y) \sim \bar{\psi}_R(x)\psi_L(y)\) with barycentric coordinates \(X=(x+y)/2\) and \(r=(x-y)/2\), factorizing it as \(\chi(X)\phi(r)\) for bound states. The interaction is taken from the coloron model, a massive gluon of mass \(M_0\) and coupling \(g_0\), which yields a static Yukawa potential after Fierz rearrangement. Extremalizing the Hamiltonian for \(\phi(r)\) gives a Schrödinger-Klein-Gordon (SKG) equation whose eigenvalue \(\mu^2\) determines the bound-state mass; for super-critical coupling \(g_0 > g_{0c}\) one obtains \(\mu^2 < 0\) and spontaneous symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

* Critical coupling condition: \(g_{0c}^2 N_c / (8\pi^2) = 1\) (from the NJL model, used as a reference point).
* Induced Yukawa coupling suppression: \(\phi(0) \sim \sqrt{|\mu|/M_0}\), giving power-law rather than logarithmic running.
* Quartic coupling from loops: \(\lambda \propto g_Y^4 \ln(M_0/\mu)\), with \(g_Y\) suppressed by the dilution of \(\phi(0)\).
* Example hierarchy: for a top-condensation scheme, \(v = 175\) GeV with \(M_0 \sim 5\) to \(10\) TeV yields fine-tuning at the few percent level.

## Context

This work extends the old ideas of Yukawa (bilocal fields) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (1961) by adding an explicit internal wave-function for the composite scalar, going beyond the pointlike auxiliary-field treatment. It contrasts with the NJL model as treated by Bardeen, Hill, and Lindner (1990) and by Hill (1981), where binding occurs only at loop level and the Yukawa coupling runs logarithmically. The formalism enables a realistic top-condensation model (as in Topcolor, Hill 1991) with reduced fine-tuning, and it opens the way to studying composite Higgs scenarios where the bound state is extended on the scale of the electroweak vacuum expectation value.
