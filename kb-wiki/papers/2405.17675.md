# $J/ψ$ photoproduction: threshold to very high energy

> A reaction model for $γ+ p \to J/ψ+ p$ photoproduction, which exposes the $c \bar c$ content of the photon in making the transition $γ\to c\bar c + \mathbb P \to J/ψ$ and couples the intermediate $c \bar c$ system to the proton's valence quarks via Pomeron ($\mathbb P $) exchange, is used to deliver a description of available data, viz. both differential and total cross sections from near threshold, where data has newly been acquired, to invariant mass $W \approx 300\,$GeV. The study suggests that it is premature to link existing $γ+ p \to J/ψ+ p$ data with, for instance, in-proton gluon distributions, the quantum chromodynamics trace anomaly, or pentaquark production. Further developments in reaction theory and higher precision data are necessary before the validity of any such connections can be assessed.

## Key claim

The study suggests that it is premature to link existing $\gamma + p \to J/\psi + p$ data with in-proton gluon distributions, the quantum chromodynamics trace anomaly, or pentaquark production, because further developments in reaction theory and higher precision data are necessary before the validity of any such connections can be assessed.

## Method

The authors constructed a reaction model for $\gamma + p \to J/\psi + p$ photoproduction that exposes the $c\bar c$ content of the photon in the transition $\gamma \to c\bar c + \mathbb P \to J/\psi$ and couples the intermediate $c\bar c$ system to the proton's valence quarks via Pomeron ($\mathbb P$) exchange. The $\gamma \to \bar c c + \mathbb P \to J/\psi$ transition loop was evaluated using continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) with rainbow-ladder truncation, computing the dressed quark propagator, photon-quark vertex, and vector meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, then combining them to evaluate the integral as a function of $(W,t)$. The Pomeron parameters were determined by fitting to high-energy $J/\psi$ photoproduction data ($W \geq 26$ GeV) and the ZEUS 2002 differential cross-section, with the resulting model then used to predict near-threshold behavior without further adjustment.

## Key numbers

- The near-threshold invariant mass is $W_{\rm th} = m_{J/\psi} + m_p = 4.04$ GeV; GlueX data cover $W = 4.04$ to $4.72$ GeV (photon energy range $\nu = 8.2$ to $11.4$ GeV for a target proton at rest).
- The Pomeron trajectory parameters from ZEUS 2002 are $\alpha_0 = 1.2 \pm 0.009$, $\alpha_1 = 0.115 \pm 0.0118$ GeV$^{-2}$, with $\beta_c = 0.11$ GeV$^{-1}$; the error-weighted average of three estimates yields $\alpha_0 = 1.20(1)$, $\alpha_1 = 0.112(11)$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- The dominant scalar function $A_1(W,t)$ at $W_0 = 4.5$ GeV is approximated by $m_{J/\psi} A_1(W_0,|t|) = (3.76 - 0.0110|t|)/(1+0.0845|t|) \times 10^{-3}$ for $0.5 < |t|/{\rm GeV}^2 < 8$.
- The $\chi^2$/dof for the authors' model compared to all near-threshold data is 7.0, versus 8.1 for the $\mathbb P + v_{cN}$ model and 3.6 for the GPD model (which was fitted to those data).
- The interaction parameters for charm quarks are $\varsigma = 0.6$ GeV, $\omega = 0.8$ GeV, yielding $m_{J/\psi} = 3.123$ GeV and $f_{J/\psi} = 0.278$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Pichowsky (1996), which resolved the $\gamma \to c\bar c + \mathbb P \to J/\psi$ transition loop in terms of dressed-quark degrees of freedom, by using modern continuum Schwinger function methods to compute the loop without algebraic parametrizations. The study contradicts claims that near-threshold $J/\psi$ photoproduction provides direct access to in-proton gluon distributions or gluon gravitational form factors via GPD-based descriptions (Guo 2023) or holographic models (Mamo 2022), and also challenges the interpretation that $J/\psi N$ final state interactions dominate near-threshold (Sakinah 2024). The model enables a unified description of all available $\gamma p \to J/\psi p$ data from threshold to $W \approx 300$ GeV, providing a baseline for future reaction theory developments and higher-precision measurements needed to assess connections to gluon physics.
