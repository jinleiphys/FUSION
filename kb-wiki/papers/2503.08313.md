# Suppressed Drell-Yan process by an external magnetic field

> The strongest electromagnetic fields in nature are created in high energy nuclear collisions and expected to change the dynamic scattering processes in the early stage. The magnetic field effect on the Drell-Yan process is investigated in this work. The single photon decay into quark pairs and lepton pairs in an external magnetic field leads to a significant Drell-Yan suppression in low and intermediate invariant mass region. The calculation up to the Landau level $n-1$ is complete in the energy region $s<2/3n(eB)$, and the enlarged phase space at higher landau levels may enhance the dilepton spectrum in the high mass region.

# Suppressed Drell-Yan process by an external magnetic field

## Key claim
The Drell-Yan cross section in an external magnetic field is dramatically suppressed in the low and intermediate invariant mass region due to the opening of single-photon decay channels into quark and lepton pairs, which are forbidden in vacuum. The calculation up to Landau level n-1 is complete only in the energy region s < 2/3 n (eB), and the enlarged phase space at higher Landau levels may enhance the dilepton spectrum in the high mass region.

## Method
The authors solved the Dirac equation in an external magnetic field using the Landau gauge to obtain fermion spinors quantized into Landau levels. They calculated the scattering amplitude and cross section for the Drell-Yan process q qbar -> l+ l- at leading order, incorporating the finite decay width of the intermediate photon into lepton and quark pairs in the magnetic field. The calculation was performed up to the next Landau level (NLL, n=1) and compared with the lowest Landau level (LLL, n=0) approximation, using CTEQ6 parton distribution functions to compute dilepton spectra in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- Maximum magnetic field in Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy: 5 m_pi^2 ~ 0.1 GeV^2
- Maximum magnetic field in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies: 70 m_pi^2 ~ 1 GeV^2
- Electromagnetic coupling constant: alpha_EM = 1/137
- Inelastic scattering cross section at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: sigma_inel = 71 mb
- Magnetic field values used in numerical calculation: eB = 1 GeV^2 and eB = 5 GeV^2
- Minimum energy threshold for complete calculation up to Landau level n=1: s_d^(2) = 4/3 eB, giving 4/3 GeV^2 for eB=1 GeV^2 and 20/3 GeV^2 for eB=5 GeV^2
- Lepton and quark masses used: m_e = 0.51 MeV, m_mu = 100 MeV, m_tau = 1.78 GeV, m_u = 2 MeV, m_d = 5 MeV, m_s = 93.5 MeV, m_c = 1.27 GeV

## Context
This work extends studies of magnetic field effects in heavy-ion collisions, which previously examined the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007), heavy flavor properties (Marasinghe 2011, Alford 2013, Cho 2014), and quarkonium dissociation (Singh 2017, Hasan 2018). It builds on recent investigations of dilepton production in hot QCD medium under magnetic fields (Sadooghi 2016, Bandyopadhyay 2017, Ghosh 2018, Wang 2022, Das 2021) and heavy quark production enhancement at low transverse momentum (Chen 2024). The single-photon decay channel gamma -> l'+ l'- was previously studied in the context of magnetars (Wunner 1979, 1980ApJ). This work enables future experimental tests at LHC of magnetic field effects on hard probes produced in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions.
