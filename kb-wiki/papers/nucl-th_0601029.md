# Classical Strongly Coupled QGP I: The Model and Molecular Dynamics Simulations

> We propose a model for the description of strongly interacting quarks and gluon quasiparticles at $T=(1-3)T_c$, as a classical and nonrelativistic colored Coulomb gas. The sign and strength of the inter-particle interactions are fixed by the scalar product of their classical {\it color vectors} subject to Wong's equations. The model displays a number of phases as the Coulomb coupling is increased ranging from a gas, to a liquid, to a crystal with antiferromagnetic-like color ordering. We analyze the model using Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations and discuss the density-density correlator in real time. We extract pertinent decorrelation times, diffusion and viscosity constants for all phases. The classical results when extrapolated to the sQGP suggest that the phase is liquid-like, with a diffusion constant $D\approx 0.1/T$ and a bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s\approx 1/3$.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) at temperatures between 1 and 3 times the critical temperature can be modeled as a classical, nonrelativistic colored Coulomb gas, and they argue that when their classical molecular dynamics results are extrapolated to the sQGP, the phase is liquid-like with specific transport coefficients. The hedge is that this is a classical model with quantum effects only schematically incorporated via a repulsive core potential, and the mapping to sQGP relies on heuristic arguments for parameter values.

## Method

The authors performed classical molecular dynamics (MD) simulations of a one-component plasma with SU(2) color charges, solving the equations of motion for particle positions, momenta, and classical color vectors subject to Wong's equations. They used periodic boundary conditions with mirror images to handle long-range Coulomb forces, and extracted transport coefficients via Green-Kubo relations from equilibrium time-dependent correlation functions including the velocity autocorrelation function and stress-energy tensor autocorrelation function.

## Key numbers

- The diffusion constant for the one-species classical QGP follows the approximate power law D ≈ 0.4 / Γ^(4/5), where Γ is the dimensionless Coulomb coupling parameter (ratio of potential to kinetic energy)
- For the sQGP extrapolation, the diffusion constant is D ≈ 0.1/T and the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio is η/s ≈ 1/3
- The shear viscosity η shows a minimum in the liquid phase, with the lowest measured value η ≈ 0.1 at Γ = 10 in the classical model
- The potential energy per particle follows U/(N T) ≈ -4.9 - 2Γ + 3.2 Γ^(1/4) + 2.2/Γ^(1/4), asymptoting to U/(N T) ≈ -2Γ at large Γ (crystal phase)
- The viscous decorrelation time follows τ_η ≈ 0.239 + 0.091 √Γ
- The density-density correlation function G_d was measured at three coupling values: Γ = 0.83 (gas), 31.3 (liquid), and 131 (solid)

## Context

This work extends the classical one-component plasma (OCP) and two-component plasma (TCP) studies from electrodynamics to a non-Abelian colored Coulomb gas relevant to QCD. The authors note that extensive studies of OCP using MD have revealed gas, liquid, glass, and solid regimes as Γ increases (Ichi review). The model is motivated by the observation from RHIC experiments that the produced matter cannot be a weakly coupled gas but rather a good liquid (Shuryak liquid), and by earlier work suggesting the existence of multiple marginal colored Coulomb bound states in the sQGP (Shuryak and Zahed new QGP). The MD approach provides real-time correlators that are difficult to obtain from lattice QCD or Euclidean time methods, enabling direct access to transport properties. Future work planned includes incorporating more quantum corrections, particularly the exclusion principle and the relation between classically correlated charges and quantum bound states.
