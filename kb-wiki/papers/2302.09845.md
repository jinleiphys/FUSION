# Optical potential for incident and emitted low-energy $α$ particles. III. Non-statistical processes induced by neutrons on Zr, Nb, and Mo nuclei

> The reliability of a previous $α$-particle optical-model potential (OMP) on nuclei with mass number 45$\leq$$A$$\leq$209 was proved for emitted $α$ particles as well, for proton--induced reactions on Zn isotopes [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 91}, 064611 (2015), Paper I]. However, the same was not the case of neutrons on Zr stable isotopes [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 96}, 044610 (2017), Paper II]. A recent assessment of this potential also for nucleon--induced $α$-emission on $A$$\sim$60 nuclei, including pickup direct reaction and eventual Giant Quadrupole Resonance (GQR) $α$-emission, is completed for neutrons incident on Zr, Nb, and Mo stable isotopes. Consistent sets of input parameters, determined through analysis of independent data, is involved while no further empirical rescaling factors of the $γ$ and nucleon widths have been involved. A suitable account of all competitive reaction channels is confirmed by careful uncertainty analysis, to avoid parameter ambiguities and/or error compensation. Additional validation of this potential is also supported by recently measured $(α,γ)$ and $(α,n)$ cross--sections of Zr and Mo nuclei. An increase of the $α$-emission beyond the statistical predictions, through consideration of additional reaction channels of the pickup direct interaction and {\it like}--GQR decay, makes possible the description of both absorption and emission of $α$ particles by the same optical potential.

## Key claim

The previously developed α-particle optical-model potential (OMP) for nuclei with mass number 45 ≤ A ≤ 209, which had failed to describe α-emission from neutron-induced reactions on Zr isotopes in Paper II, can be validated for neutrons incident on Zr, Nb, and Mo stable isotopes by including non-statistical reaction channels (pickup direct reaction and like-Giant Quadrupole Resonance decay) beyond the statistical Hauser-Feshbach and pre-equilibrium emission models, without using empirical rescaling factors for γ and nucleon widths.

## Method

The authors performed Hauser-Feshbach compound-nucleus and pre-equilibrium emission calculations using the codes TALYS-1.96 and the Geometry-Dependent Hybrid model, with consistent back-shifted Fermi gas nuclear level density parameters, nucleon optical-model potentials (Koning and Delaroche 2003), and γ-ray strength functions (enhanced generalized Lorentzian model). Pickup direct reaction contributions to (n,α) reactions were calculated using the distorted-wave Born approximation method with the code FRESCO, analyzing measured α-particle angular distributions from (³He,α) and (t,α) reactions on ⁸⁸Sr, ⁹⁰Zr, ⁹¹Zr, and ⁹⁶Zr to extract spectroscopic factors for the transferred neutron.

## Key numbers

- The neutron total cross-section for ⁹³Nb using the global Koning and Delaroche (2003) OMP parameters showed better agreement with measured data than the local parameter set, with adjustments to the real potential depth parameter v₁ within ~4% for near-spherical ⁹²Mo and ⁹⁴Mo.
- The ⁹⁶Zr(α,n)⁹⁹Mo reaction cross-section was measured from 6.5 to 13 MeV incident energy, spanning more than six orders of magnitude, with calculated values agreeing within measurement error bars except for ~1 MeV around 8.5 MeV incident energy.
- The ⁹⁰Zr(α,γ)⁹⁴Mo cross-section measurements extended the data energy range below the (α,n) reaction threshold, where the (α,γ) cross-section is near the α-particle total reaction cross-section, providing a strong constraint on the α-particle OMP.
- Direct inelastic-scattering cross-sections for neutrons on ⁹³Nb and ⁹⁸Mo reached ~7% of the reaction cross-section at incident energies around 4 MeV, decreasing to slightly less than 5% above 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Papers I (Avrigeanu et al. 2015, for proton-induced reactions on Zn isotopes) and II (Avrigeanu et al. 2017, for neutrons on Zr stable isotopes) to neighboring elements Nb and Mo, addressing the "α-potential mystery" (Travaglio et al. 2013) of describing both α-emission and absorption with the same OMP. The approach enables consistent nuclear data evaluations for astrophysics and fusion technology applications, and the results are compared with the TENDL-2021 evaluated-data library and default TALYS-1.96 calculations.
