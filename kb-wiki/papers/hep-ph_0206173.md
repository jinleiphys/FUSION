# Strangeness production in heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC within two-source statistical model

> The experimental data on hadron yields and ratios in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions at SPS and RHIC energies, respectively, are analysed within a two-source statistical model of an ideal hadron gas. These two sources represent the expanding system of colliding heavy ions, where the hot central fireball is embedded in a larger but cooler fireball. The volume of the central source increases with rising bombarding energy. Results of the two-source model fit to RHIC experimental data at midrapidity coincide with the results of the one-source thermal model fit, indicating the formation of an extended fireball, which is three times larger than the corresponding core at SPS.

# Strangeness production in heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC within two-source statistical model

## Key claim
The two-source statistical model (TSM) fit to midrapidity data at RHIC coincides with the standard single-source fit, indicating formation of an extended fireball three times larger than the corresponding core at SPS; however, the temperature of the central fireball at RHIC varies from 176 MeV to 185 MeV depending on whether the multiplicity of negatively charged hadrons is included in the fit.

## Method
The authors fit hadron yields and ratios from central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV (SPS) and central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV (RHIC) to a two-source statistical model of an ideal hadron gas, where a hot central fireball (source 2) is embedded in a larger, cooler outer fireball (source 1). Each source is described by four parameters (volume, temperature, net baryon density, net strangeness density), with total strangeness conservation linking the two sources, yielding seven free parameters. All hadrons with masses below 2 GeV/c^2 were included, with resonance decay feed-down but no strangeness suppression or excluded volume corrections.

## Key numbers
- At SPS (Pb+Pb, 158 AGeV): core temperature T = 155 MeV, halo temperature T = 117 MeV; core volume V = 1705 fm^3, halo volume V = 7250 fm^3; core baryon chemical potential mu_B = 45 MeV, halo mu_B = 460 MeV; chi^2/DOF = 16/5 for TSM vs 46/9 for single-source SM.
- At RHIC (Au+Au, sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV): TSM and SM fits nearly identical; temperature T = 185.7 MeV (SM) or 185.5-185.9 MeV (TSM); total volume V = 5157 fm^3 (SM) or 2560+2608 = 5168 fm^3 (TSM); baryon chemical potential mu_B = 53 MeV; chi^2/DOF = 9.6/3 for SM and 9.6/7 for TSM.
- At RHIC, when h- multiplicity is excluded from the fit, temperature drops to 176 MeV.
- At SPS, the averaged characteristics of the central zone from combined fits: temperature T = 157 +/- 2 MeV, volume V = 0.6 +/- 0.1 V_0 (where V_0 is the volume of a lead nucleus), baryon chemical potential mu_B = 31 +/- 14 MeV; halo temperature T_S1 = 117 +/- 3 MeV.
- At RHIC midrapidity: energy per particle E/N = 1.1 GeV; entropy per baryon S/A = 121.

## Context
This work extends the single-source statistical model approach (Rafelski, 1991; Becattini, 1996; Braun-Munzinger et al., 1999) by introducing two spatially separated fireballs, motivated by experimental observations from NA52 and microscopic model predictions (Bratkovskaya et al., 1999) of low net baryon densities at midrapidity at SPS. The TSM resolves discrepancies where the single-source model underestimates pion yields and overpredicts strange particle abundances. The finding that the two-source fit collapses to a single-source fit at RHIC supports the formation of an extended, homogeneous fireball at higher energies, and the authors suggest that future 4pi-data at RHIC could test the increase of the central fireball volume relative to the halo, strangeness equilibration in both sources, and possible changes in halo temperature.
