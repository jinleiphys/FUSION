# From the lineshape of the $X(3872)$ to its structure

> From a study of the lineshape of the $X(3872)$, the LHCb collaboration measures a sizeable negative effective range. This cannot be reconciled with a shallow $D\bar{D}^*$ bound state hypothesis. Based on Weinberg's compositeness criterion, together with a theorem by Smorodinsky, it follows that the $X$ has to have a compact hidden charm structure, most likely a tetraquark, interacting with unbound $D\bar{D}^*$ pairs via short-distance color forces. This conclusion is strengthened by the general pattern recently emerging for exotic mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the LHCb measurement of a sizeable negative effective range for the X(3872) cannot be reconciled with a shallow D⁰D̄*⁰ molecular bound state, and instead implies the X has a compact hidden-charm structure (most likely a tetraquark) interacting with unbound D̄D* pairs via short-distance color forces. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on the LHCb data being confirmed beyond experimental errors, noting that the error correlations give an effective range in the range -2.0 fm > r₀ > -5.3 fm.

## Method

The authors apply Weinberg's compositeness criterion and a theorem by Landau and Smorodinsky to the LHCb Flatté amplitude analysis of the X(3872) lineshape. They derive the inverse scattering length κ₀ and effective range r₀ from the LHCb fit parameters (m⁰_X = -7.18 MeV, g_LHCb = 0.108 ± 0.003) using the relation between the Flatté amplitude and the low-energy S-wave scattering amplitude expansion. The binding energy B and the compositeness parameter Z are then extracted from the effective range using the relation Z = -r₀κ/(1 - r₀κ).

## Key numbers

- Effective range r₀ = -5.34 fm from LHCb best-fit parameters (central value), with a range -2.0 fm > r₀ > -5.3 fm after accounting for error correlations from Baru (2021)
- Inverse scattering length κ₀ = 6.92 MeV
- Binding energy B = 18 keV, corresponding to inverse binding momentum κ⁻¹ = 33 fm
- Compositeness parameter Z in the range 0.052 < Z < 0.14
- LHCb fit parameters: m⁰_X = -7.18 MeV (relative to D⁰D̄*⁰ threshold), g_LHCb = 0.108 ± 0.003
- Isospin splitting δ = 8.2 MeV between charged and neutral D̄D* thresholds

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg (1965) compositeness criterion and the Landau-Smorodinsky theorem (which states that shallow bound states with purely attractive binding force always give r₀ > 0) to the X(3872) case. It contradicts the molecular interpretation of the X(3872) that has been widely advocated since its discovery in 2003, and instead supports the compact tetraquark interpretation originally proposed in the diquark model by Maiani (2004). The conclusion is strengthened by the general pattern of other exotic states, including the di-J/ψ X(6900) resonance from LHCb (2020) and the Z_cs(3985) and Z_cs(4003) from BESIII (2020) and LHCb (2021), which reproduce in the strange sector the situation observed with the X(3872) and Z_c(3900). The analysis enables future lineshape studies of the recently observed doubly charmed state from LHCb (2021).
