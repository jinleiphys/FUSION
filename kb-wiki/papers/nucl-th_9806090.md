# Double Pion Production Reactions

> We report on reactions producing two pions induced by real and virtual photons or nucleons. The role of different resonances in these reactions is emphasized. Novel results on coherent two pion photoproduction in nuclei are also reported.

# Double Pion Production Reactions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^- p$ reaction can determine the relative sign between the $s$-wave and $d$-wave decay amplitudes of the $N^*(1520) \rightarrow \Delta \pi$ transition, a quantity not accessible from $\pi N \rightarrow \pi \pi N$ data alone, and that this sign agrees with the prediction of nonrelativistic constituent quark models with recoil terms.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians for $\gamma N \rightarrow \pi \pi N$ reactions including the $N$, $\Delta(1232)$, $N^*(1440)$, and $N^*(1520)$ resonances plus $\rho$ intermediate states, reducing the full set of Feynman diagrams to 20 for photon energies up to 800 MeV. They extract the $N^*(1520) \rightarrow \Delta \pi$ decay amplitudes $A_s$ and $A_d$ from $\pi N \rightarrow \pi \pi N$ data and use the $\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^- p$ cross section to fix the global sign of these amplitudes relative to the $\gamma N \rightarrow N^*$ excitation amplitudes. The model is extended to virtual photons for electroproduction and to $NN \rightarrow NN \pi \pi$ reactions, and coherent $2\pi$ photoproduction on nuclei is analyzed using isospin arguments.

## Key numbers

- The simplified model reduces the number of Feynman diagrams from 67 to 20 for the energy range $E_\gamma \leq 800$ MeV.
- The $N^*(1520)$ helicity amplitudes yield $g_\gamma - g_\sigma = 0.157 > 0$ for the $\gamma N \rightarrow N^*$ transition operator.
- The $N^*(1520) \rightarrow \Delta \pi$ decay parameters are $\tilde{f} = 0.911$ and $\tilde{g} = -0.552$, obtained from a fit to the $s$ and $d$ wave partial decay widths.
- The $\pi^0 \pi^0$ coherent photoproduction cross section maximum is about three orders of magnitude smaller than the $\pi^+ \pi^-$ maximum for isoscalar nuclei.
- Electroproduction results for $e p \rightarrow e' \Delta^{++} \pi^-$ are averaged over the range $0.3 < Q^2 < 1.4$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the Valencia group's earlier model for $\gamma N \rightarrow \pi \pi N$ (Gomez Tejedor and Oset, 1996) and improves upon simplified models from other groups (e.g., Murphy and Laget, 1996) that used a range parameter for the $\rho$ of 200 MeV. The results provide new constraints on nonrelativistic constituent quark models (Badhuri, 1985; Silvestre-Brac, 1996) by requiring the correct $q$ dependence and sign of the $N^*(1520) \rightarrow \Delta \pi$ decay amplitudes. The findings enable further studies of $N^*$ resonance properties in two-pion production reactions at higher energies and in nuclear targets, including coherent photoproduction and electroproduction experiments at TJNAF.
