# Electric dipole moments of atoms and molecules produced by enhanced nuclear Schiff moments

> We perform calculations of the CP-violating atomic and molecular electric dipole moments (EDM) induced by the interaction of the nuclear Schiff moments with electrons. EDM of atoms Eu, Dy, Gd, Ac, Th, Pa, U, Np and Pu are of special interest since they have isotopes with strongly enhanced nuclear Schiff moments caused by the octupole nuclear deformation or soft octupole vibration mode. These atoms have open 4f or 5f shells making the calculations complicated. We use our special version of the configuration interaction method combined with the many-body perturbation theory method adopted for open f-shell case. To validate the method we perform similar calculations for simpler atoms (Xe, Hg, Tl+, Pb, Pb++, Rn, Ra) where some earlier results are available. In addition we present the estimates of the CP-violating nuclear spin - molecular axis interaction constants for molecules which may be of experimental interest including AcF, EuN, ThO, PbO and TlF. We also present updated values of the nuclear Schiff moments and atomic and molecular EDM expressed in terms of the CP-violating pi-meson - nucleon interaction constants g_0,g_1, g_2, QCD parameter theta and quark chromo-EDMs. The results may be used to test CP-violation theories and search for axion dark matter in atomic, molecular and solid state experiments.

# Electric dipole moments of atoms and molecules produced by enhanced nuclear Schiff moments

## Key claim

The authors calculate atomic and molecular electric dipole moments (EDMs) induced by nuclear Schiff moments for a range of heavy atoms and molecules, focusing on isotopes with strongly enhanced Schiff moments due to octupole nuclear deformation or soft octupole vibration modes. The results are expressed in terms of fundamental CP-violating parameters, but the enhancement factors and Schiff moment values for several nuclei (such as $^{229}$Pa, $^{233}$U, and $^{239}$Pu) remain uncertain and are presented as upper limits or estimates pending confirmation of nuclear structure properties.

## Method

The authors use a configuration interaction method combined with many-body perturbation theory (CI+MBPT) adapted for open f-shell atoms (Eu, Dy, Gd, Ac, Th, Pa, U, Np, Pu) to calculate atomic EDMs produced by the electrostatic interaction between electrons and nuclear Schiff moments. They validate their method by performing similar calculations for simpler atoms (Xe, Hg, Tl$^+$, Pb, Pb$^{++}$, Rn, Ra) where earlier results are available. Nuclear Schiff moments are estimated using an analytical formula based on octupole deformation parameters $\beta_3$, quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$, nuclear spin $I$, and the energy interval between opposite parity levels, with scaling factors $K_S$ relative to the well-studied $^{225}$Ra nucleus.

## Key numbers

- $^{225}$Ra Schiff moment: $S(^{225}{\rm Ra},g) \approx (-2.6 g\bar{g}_0 + 12.9 g\bar{g}_1 - 6.9 g\bar{g}_2)\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^3$; $S(^{225}{\rm Ra},\bar{\theta}) \approx -\bar{\theta}\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^3$; $S(^{225}{\rm Ra},\tilde{d}) \approx 10^4 (0.50 \tilde{d}_u - 0.54 \tilde{d}_d)\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^2$
- $^{199}$Hg and $^{207}$Pb Schiff moment: $S(g) \approx (0.023 g\bar{g}_0 - 0.007 g\bar{g}_1 + 0.029 g\bar{g}_2)\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^3$; $S(\bar{\theta}) \approx 0.005 \bar{\theta}\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^3$
- $^{203}$Tl and $^{205}$Tl Schiff moment: $S(g) \approx (0.13 g\bar{g}_0 - 0.004 g\bar{g}_1 - 0.27 g\bar{g}_2)\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^3$; $S(\bar{\theta}) \approx 0.027 \bar{\theta}\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^3$
- $^{129}$Xe Schiff moment: $S(g) \approx (-0.03 g\bar{g}_0 + 0.01 g\bar{g}_1 - 0.04 g\bar{g}_2)\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^3$; $S(\bar{\theta}) \approx -0.007 \bar{\theta}\, e\cdot{\rm fm}^3$
- Scaling factor $K_S$ for $^{225}$Ra is defined as 1; for $^{227}$Ac, $K_S = 6$; for $^{237}$Np, $K_S = 4$; for $^{153}$Eu, $K_S = 3.7$; for $^{229}$Pa (if confirmed), $K_S = 40$; for $^{239}$Pu, $K_S \lesssim 0.12$
- Energy interval between opposite parity levels in $^{225}$Ra: $E(1/2^-) - E(1/2^+) = 55.2$ KeV; in $^{239}$Pu: $E(1/2^-) - E(1/2^+) = 470$ KeV; in $^{233}$U: $E(5/2^-) - E(5/2^+) = 299$ KeV
- Octupole deformation parameters: $^{225}$Ra $\beta_3 = 0.099$, $\beta_2 = 0.129$; $^{227}$Ac $\beta_3 = 0.1$; $^{229}$Th $\beta_3 = 0.115$, $\beta_2 = 0.240$; $^{232}$U $\beta_3 = 0.17$, $\beta_2 = 0.238$; $^{240}$Pu $\beta_3 = 0.066$, $\beta_2 = 0.284$

## Context

This work extends the list of candidate nuclei for enhanced Schiff moments originally identified by Auerbach (1996) and Spevak (1997) to include stable isotopes such as $^{153}$Eu, $^{161}$Dy, $^{163}$Dy, $^{155}$Gd, and long-lived actinides including $^{235}$U, $^{237}$Np, $^{233}$U, $^{229}$Th, and $^{239}$Pu, building on the earlier estimates by Flambaum and Dzuba (2019). The atomic EDM calculations for open f-shell atoms (Eu, Dy, Gd, Ac, Th, Pa, U, Np, Pu) are significantly more complex than previous calculations for simpler systems like Hg, Xe, Tl, and Pb performed by Dzuba (2002, 2007, 2009) and Kozlov. The results enable experimental searches for CP-violation beyond the Standard Model and axion dark matter detection through oscillating Schiff moments, as proposed by Graham (2013) and Stadnik (2014), with the potential for resonance enhancement in molecular transitions as suggested by Flambaum (2018).
