# Polarized structure functions of the deuteron

> Physics of spin-1 hadron is an unexplored topic in the high-energy region although spin-1/2 physics has been well investigated in the last decade. It is important to test our knowledge of hadron spin structure in a quite different field of spin physics. We discuss tensor structure functions, which do not exist for the spin-1/2 nucleon, in lepton scattering and in hadron reactions such as the polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process.

# Polarized structure functions of the deuteron

## Key claim

Tensor structure functions of the deuteron, which do not exist for spin-1/2 nucleons, can be investigated both in lepton scattering and in hadron reactions such as the polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process; however, the tensor structure in the high-energy region remains experimentally unexamined, and theoretical predictions that b1/F1 is typically a few percent or much less could be wrong, as the experience with g1 has shown.

## Method

The author reviews the general formalism for deep inelastic electron-deuteron scattering, identifying eight independent helicity amplitudes from which four new structure functions (b1, b2, b3, b4) emerge beyond the usual F1, F2, g1, g2. For the polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process, the author imposes Hermiticity, parity conservation, and time-reversal invariance to derive 22 structure functions after integrating over the virtual photon transverse momentum, and constructs spin asymmetries for the tensor polarizations Q0, Q1, and Q2.

## Key numbers

- Four new structure functions (b1, b2, b3, b4) exist for the spin-1 deuteron in addition to the four usual ones (F1, F2, g1, g2)
- b2 is related to b1 by the Callan-Gross type relation b2 = 2x b1 in the Bjorken scaling limit
- A sum rule ∫ dx b1(x) = 0 was proposed by Close and Kumano (1990) if there is no tensor polarization for antiquark distributions
- b1/F1 is expected to be typically a few percent or much less
- In the polarized pd Drell-Yan, there are 22 structure functions after integrating over Q_T or taking the limit Q_T → 0
- For the pd/pp Drell-Yan ratio in the x_F → 1 limit, the expression reduces to (1/2)[1 + Δ_(T) d̄(x2)/Δ_(T) ū(x2)] under the assumption Δ_(T) u_v(x→1) ≫ Δ_(T) d_v(x→1)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of spin-1/2 nucleon structure functions to spin-1 hadrons, building on the theoretical framework of Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1989) for electron-deuteron scattering. The sum rule for b1 is analogous to the Gottfried sum rule discussed by Kumano (1998), where violation would indicate finite tensor polarization of antiquarks, similar to how Gottfried sum rule violation indicated a finite ū/d̄ asymmetry. The pd Drell-Yan approach is presented as complementary to the ongoing HERMES experiment for measuring b1, and could enable determination of antiquark tensor distributions and the ū/d̄ asymmetry for transversity distributions, analogous to how the Fermilab-E866 Drell-Yan experiment clarified the x dependence of ū/d̄.
