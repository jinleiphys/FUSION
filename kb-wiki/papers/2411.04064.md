# Astrophysical constraints on color-superconducting phases in compact stars within the RG-consistent NJL model

> We determine parameters of the renormalization group-consistent three-flavor color-superconducting Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that are suited to investigate possible compact-star configurations. Our goal is to provide quark-matter equations of state (EoS) that can be used for hadron-quark hybrid-star constructions. To that end, we mainly focus on the parameters of the quark-matter model. By varying the vector and diquark coupling constants, we analyze their impact on the EoS, the speed of sound, the maximum diquark gap, and the mass-radius relation. In almost all configurations, a stable color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase appears in the core of the maximum-mass configurations, typically spanning several kilometers in radius. In other cases, the star's two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) branch of the EoS becomes unstable before reaching the CFL transition density. At neutron-star densities, the speed of sound squared reaches up to $c_s^2 \sim 0.6$ and the CFL gap up to $Δ\sim250\,$MeV. We argue that adding a hadronic EoS at lower densities by performing a Maxwell construction does not increase the maximum mass substantially. Thus we use the $2.0 M_{\odot}$ constraint to constrain the NJL model parameters that are suited for the construction of hybrid-star EoS. We construct three examples of the hybrid-star model, demonstrating that there is room for different color-superconducting compositions. The hybrid EoSs obtained in this way can have no 2SC matter or different ratios of 2SC and CFL quark matter in the core. We show that early hadron-quark transitions are possible that can modify the tidal deformability at 1.4 $M_\odot$. We find that these EoSs are consistent with the imposed constraints from astrophysics and perturbative QCD. They allow for different hybrid-star scenarios with a hadronic EoS that is soft at low to intermediate densities ($\sim 1-3\, n_{\text{sat}}$).

## Key claim

The authors find that within the renormalization-group-consistent NJL model, a stable color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase appears in the core of maximum-mass hybrid-star configurations for almost all parameter choices, but the maximum mass of pure quark stars is used as a proxy to constrain model parameters because adding a hadronic EoS via Maxwell construction does not substantially increase the maximum mass.

## Method

The authors employ a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with color-superconducting diquark interactions, using a renormalization-group-consistent regularization with a large cutoff scale Lambda = 6023 MeV to avoid cutoff artifacts present in conventional regularization. They vary the diquark coupling eta_D between 0.90 and 1.80 and the vector coupling eta_V between 0.00 and 1.50, solving the gap equations and neutrality conditions to obtain equations of state, then solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to compute mass-radius relations for pure quark stars. Three example hybrid-star EoSs are constructed by performing a Maxwell construction with a hadronic relativistic mean-field EoS.

## Key numbers

* Speed of sound squared reaches up to c_s^2 ~ 0.6 at neutron-star densities.
* CFL diquark gap reaches up to Delta ~ 250 MeV at neutron-star densities.
* The 2.0 M_sun constraint is used to select viable NJL model parameters for hybrid-star construction.
* The conventional cutoff is Lambda' = 602.3 MeV, with scalar coupling G_S Lambda'^2 = 1.835 and 't Hooft coupling K Lambda'^5 = 12.36.
* Bare quark masses are m_u,d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 140.7 MeV.
* The RG-consistent cutoff is set to Lambda = 10 Lambda' = 6023 MeV.
* Electron mass m_e = 0.511 MeV and muon mass m_mu = 105.66 MeV are used for leptonic contributions.

## Context

This work extends the RG-consistent NJL model developed by Gholami (2024) by adding a repulsive vector interaction, enabling systematic study of color-superconducting quark-matter EoSs for hybrid-star constructions. It addresses the long-standing problem identified by Klahn (2006) that earlier NJL models with vector interactions could reach 2 M_sun but lacked stable CFL cores, and by Pagliara (2007) who constructed hybrid stars with 2SC and CFL matter. The results enable future hybrid-star constructions with different color-superconducting compositions, showing that early hadron-quark transitions can modify tidal deformability at 1.4 M_sun, which is relevant for interpreting gravitational-wave observations like GW170817.
