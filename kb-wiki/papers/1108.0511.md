# Stellar neutrino energy loss rates due to $^{24}$Mg suitable for O+Ne+Mg core simulations

> Neutrino losses from proto-neutron stars play a pivotal role to decide if these stars would be crushed into black holes or explode as supernovae. Recent observations of subluminous Type II-P supernovae (e.g., 2005cs, 2003gd, 1999br, 1997D) were able to rejuvenate the interest in 8-10 M$_{\odot}$ stars which develop O+Ne+Mg cores. Simulation results of O+Ne+Mg cores show varying results in converting the collapse into an explosion. The neutrino energy loss rates are important input parameters in core collapse simulations. Proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory has been used for calculation of neutrino energy loss rates due to $^{24}$Mg in stellar matter. The rates are presented on a detailed density-temperature grid suitable for simulation purposes. The calculated neutrino energy loss rates are enhanced up to more than one order of magnitude compared to the shell model calculations and favor a lower entropy for the core of these massive stars.

# Stellar neutrino energy loss rates due to $^{24}$Mg suitable for O+Ne+Mg core simulations

## Key claim
The pn-QRPA calculated neutrino energy loss rates due to $^{24}$Mg are enhanced up to more than one order of magnitude compared to shell model calculations, particularly during presupernova evolution around T9 ~ 1.5-2, which favors a lower entropy for the core of 8-10 solar mass stars; however, the antineutrino energy loss rates from the same calculation are much smaller than shell model results, indicating significant uncertainties in excitation energies.

## Method
The proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory with a separable interaction was used to calculate neutrino and antineutrino energy loss rates from $^{24}$Mg in stellar matter. Single-particle energies and wave functions were computed in the Nilsson model with experimental deformation from Raman et al. (2001), pairing gaps of 12/√A MeV, and particle-hole and particle-particle interaction constants of 0.001 MeV and 0.05 MeV respectively. The calculation included 132 excited states of $^{24}$Mg up to approximately 40 MeV excitation energy, with a standard Gamow-Teller quenching factor of 0.77, and incorporated all measured excitation energies with definite spin/parity assignments.

## Key numbers
- The total calculated B(GT$_{+}$) and B(GT$_{-}$) strength for $^{24}$Mg came out to be 3.34 (quenched), and the Ikeda sum rule was satisfied.
- The reported neutrino energy loss rates are more than a factor of 15 enhanced compared to shell model rates in presupernova conditions (around T9 ~ 1.5-2).
- At high temperatures (T9 ~ 30), the ratios of pn-QRPA to shell model neutrino energy loss rates are in good agreement with previous calculations.
- The calculation was performed for a total of 132 excited states of $^{24}$Mg up to excitation energies in the vicinity of 40 MeV.
- The deformation parameter for $^{24}$Mg was taken from the experimentally adopted value of Raman et al. (2001).

## Context
This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of weak interaction rates for sd-shell nuclei previously performed by Nabi and Klapdor (1999) and Nabi and Rahman (2008), specifically providing neutrino energy loss rates for $^{24}$Mg on a detailed temperature-density grid suitable for O+Ne+Mg core collapse simulations. The results are compared with the shell model calculations of Oda et al. (1994) and the earlier compilation of Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1985), showing significant enhancement in neutrino energy loss rates that could affect the outcome of simulations by Kitaura et al. (2006) and Gutierrez et al. (2005). The enhanced rates favor cooler cores with lower entropies and may influence whether 8-10 solar mass stars undergo prompt explosion, delayed explosion, or no explosion.
