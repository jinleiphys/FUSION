# Least-square approach for singular value decompositions of scattering problems

> It was recently observed that chiral two-body interactions can be efficiently represented using matrix factorization techniques such as the singular value decomposition. However, the exploitation of these low-rank structures in a few- or many-body framework is nontrivial and requires reformulations that explicitly utilize the decomposition format. In this work, we present a general least-square approach that is applicable to different few- and many-body frameworks and allows for an efficient reduction to a low number of singular values in the least-square iteration. We verify the feasibility of the least-square approach by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in factorized form. The resulting low-rank approximations of the $T$ matrix are found to fully capture scattering observables. Potential applications of the least-square approach to other frameworks with the goal of employing tensor factorization techniques are discussed.

# Least-square approach for singular value decompositions of scattering problems

## Key claim

The least-square approach provides a general strategy for reformulating few- and many-body methods to exploit tensor factorization techniques, but its practical convergence depends on the spectral radius of the integral kernel and may fail for channels with bound states (e.g., the deuteron channel) where eigenvalues of the kernel reach unity.

## Method

The authors derive factorized update equations for the singular value decomposition (SVD) components of the T matrix by minimizing the Frobenius norm of the error tensor between the full and factorized representations. They apply this SVD-factorized least-square (SVD-LS) approach to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-nucleon scattering, using a partial-wave basis with N=100 momentum mesh points up to k_max=6.0 fm^{-1} and the N3LO EMN 500 potential. The self-consistent solution is obtained by consecutive updates of the left singular vectors, singular values, and right singular vectors, with explicit orthogonalization via QR factorization to restore unitarity.

## Key numbers

- N=100 momentum mesh points used per partial-wave channel, with k_max = k'_max = 6.0 fm^{-1}
- For the uncoupled 1S0 channel, the leading singular value s1 of the T matrix is 348.67 fm at full rank (RSVD=100), enhanced by a factor of approximately ten compared to the potential due to the large scattering length
- At rank RSVD=5, the relative error of the low-rank T matrix compared to the exact solution is 2.2 x 10^{-4} in the 1S0 channel
- At rank RSVD=10, the relative error drops to 2.5 x 10^{-6} in the 1S0 channel
- Phase shifts are excellently reproduced up to laboratory energies E_lab less than or approximately 300 MeV at rank RSVD=5
- The deuteron bound state at E = -2.2245 MeV prevents convergence of the iterative approach in the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel

## Context

This work extends the observation by Tichai (2021) and Zhu (2021) that chiral two-nucleon interactions can be efficiently represented using low-rank SVD approximations, and that such approximations reproduce two-nucleon scattering and ground-state properties of medium-mass nuclei. The least-square approach builds on similar techniques used in quantum chemistry by Schutski (2017) and Hohenstein (2013, 2022) for tensor hypercontraction in many-body perturbation theory and coupled cluster calculations. The method enables future applications to nonperturbative many-body methods such as coupled cluster or in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations of medium-mass nuclei, where the algebraic equations for amplitudes or the evolution operator could benefit from factorization.
