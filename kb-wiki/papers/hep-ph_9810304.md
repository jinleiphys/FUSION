# Compton Scattering on Pion and Pion Polarizabilities

> The Compton scattering on a charged pion is studied using the dispersion relations. Unknown mass, full width and decay width into γγof the sigma meson are found from a fit to the experimental data for the process γ+γ\to π^0+π^0. These parameters are used for the calculation of the cross section of the Compton scattering on the charged pion. The analysis of the obtained results shows that the data on the elastic γ\pion scattering in the energy region up to \sqrt{s}=1 GeV (together with the data on process γγ\to π^0π^0) could be used both for a determination of the pion polarizability values with high precision and for research of the sigma meson.

# Compton Scattering on Pion and Pion Polarizabilities

## Key claim
The elastic γπ± scattering cross section in the energy region up to √s = 1 GeV, combined with data on γγ → π⁰π⁰, can determine pion polarizabilities with high precision and simultaneously constrain the properties of the σ meson, provided that corrections beyond the low-energy expression are taken into account.

## Method
Dispersion relations at fixed momentum transfer with one subtraction were constructed for the helicity amplitudes M++ and M+− of elastic γπ scattering. The amplitudes were saturated by contributions from ρ(770), b₁(1235), and a₂(1320) mesons in the s and u channels, and σ, f₀(980), and f₂(1270) mesons in the t channel. The unknown mass, full width, and γγ decay width of the σ meson were determined by fitting to experimental data for γγ → π⁰π⁰ in the energy range √t = 270 MeV to 825 MeV.

## Key numbers
- σ meson mass: 524 ± 52 MeV (variant a) and 544 ± 33 MeV (variant b)
- σ meson full width: 1075 ± 390 MeV (variant a) and 1170 ± 285 MeV (variant b)
- σ meson decay width into γγ: 0.52 ± 0.20 keV (variant a) and 0.61 ± 0.13 keV (variant b)
- (α − β)π⁰ determined from fit: −2.5 (variant a) in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³; fixed at −1.9 (variant b) from chiral perturbation theory
- (α + β)π⁰ calculated from dispersion sum rule: 0.8 in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- (α − β)π± from dispersion sum rule using σ parameters: 7.8 (variant a) and 8.1 (variant b) in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- χ² values for the fits: 0.40 (variant a) and 0.42 (variant b)

## Context
This work extends earlier dispersion relation approaches by Fil'kov and Kashevarov and by Radzhabov (1990) to simultaneously describe γγ → π⁰π⁰ and γπ± scattering. The extracted (α − β)π± values (7.8–8.1) lie between the chiral perturbation theory prediction of about 5.6 (Kirchbach, 1990) and earlier dispersion sum rule results of 10.9–10.6 (Radzhabov, 1990; Petrun'kin, 1990), while being close to the quark confinement model prediction of 7.05 (Ivanov, 1990). The results enable future high-precision extraction of pion polarizabilities from planned γπ scattering experiments and provide a method to study the σ meson through two-photon processes.
