# Newly observed D(2550), D(2610), and D(2760) as 2S and 1D charmed mesons

> We study three newly observed D(2550), D(2610), and D(2760) by the BaBar Collaboration utilizing the mass spectra and investigating the strong decays. Our calculation indicates that D(2610) is an admixture of $2^3S_1$ and $1^3D_1$ with $J^P=1^-$. $D(2760)$ can be explained as either the orthogonal partner of D(2610) or $1^3D_3$. Our estimate of the decay width for $D(2550)$, assuming it as $2^1S_0$, is far below the experimental value. the threshold effect because the strong decay of $D(2550)\to D_0^*π$ is forbidden.} The decay behavior of the remaining two 1D charmed mesons, i.e., $^3D_2$ and $^1D_2$ ($J^P=2^-$) states, is predicted, which will help future experimental search for these missing $D$-wave charmed mesons.

# Newly observed D(2550), D(2610), and D(2760) as 2S and 1D charmed mesons

## Key claim

The paper assigns D(2610) as an admixture of 2^3S_1 and 1^3D_1 states with J^P = 1^- and D(2760) as either the orthogonal partner of D(2610) or a 1^3D_3 state, but the assignment of D(2550) as 2^1S_0 is problematic because the calculated decay width (approximately 8 MeV) is far below the experimental value (127.6 MeV), suggesting either the quark model approach or the 2^1S_0 assignment may be inappropriate.

## Method

The authors used the quark pair creation (QPC) model with harmonic oscillator wave functions to calculate two-body strong decay widths of the newly observed charmed mesons, adjusting the oscillator parameter R to reproduce realistic root mean square radii from a Schrodinger equation with a potential model. The mixing angle between 2^3S_1 and 1^3D_1 states was varied to find overlap between theoretical and experimental decay widths for D(2610) and D(2760). Constituent quark masses of 1.45 GeV (charm), 0.33 GeV (up/down), and 0.55 GeV (strange) were used, with a QPC strength gamma = 6.3.

## Key numbers

- D(2550)^0 mass: 2539.4 +/- 4.5 +/- 6.8 MeV; width: 130 +/- 12 +/- 13 MeV (experimental)
- D(2610)^0 mass: 2608.7 +/- 2.4 +/- 2.5 MeV; width: 93 +/- 6 +/- 13 MeV (experimental)
- D(2760)^0 mass: 2763.3 +/- 2.3 +/- 2.3 MeV; width: 60.9 +/- 5.1 +/- 3.6 MeV (experimental)
- Theoretical decay width for D(2550) as 2^1S_0: approximately 8 MeV for R = 3.6 GeV^-1, far below the experimental width of 127.6 MeV
- Mixing angle range for D(2610) as admixture of 2^3S_1 and 1^3D_1: 0.9 to 1.5 radians
- For D(2610) in the mixing angle region 0.9 to 1.5 radians: ratio Gamma(D*pi)/Gamma(Dpi) = 0.35 to 0.47; ratio Gamma(Dpi)/Gamma(D_1(2430)pi) = 0.78 to 0.91; ratio Gamma(D*eta)/Gamma(Deta) = 0.17 to 0.33
- For D(2760) as 1^3D_3: total decay width 32.5 MeV at R = 3.6 GeV^-1, with dominant modes D*pi (12.78 MeV) and Dpi (11.71 MeV)
- For 2^- states at mass 2.76 GeV: total widths of 103 MeV (1"^3D_2") and 143 MeV (1"^1D_2")

## Context

This work extends the analysis of charmed meson spectroscopy performed by the BaBar Collaboration (2010) and prior potential model calculations by Godfrey and Isgur (GI), Matsuki et al. (MMS), and others, which predicted masses for 2S and 1D charmed mesons. The paper uses the QPC model of Micu (1968) and LeYaouanc et al. (1977) to test assignments, finding that D(2610) and D(2760) can be understood as mixed 2^3S_1 and 1^3D_1 states similar to the charmonium psi(3770) mixing suggested by Rosner (2001). The results enable future experimental searches for the missing 1D charmed mesons with J^P = 2^- and suggest a connection to charmed-strange mesons D_s1(2710) and D_sJ(2860) via flavor SU(3) symmetry.
