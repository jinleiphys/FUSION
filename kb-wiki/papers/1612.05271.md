# Event-By-Event Initial Conditions for Heavy Ion Collisions

> The early time dynamics of heavy ion collisions can be described by classical fields in an approximation of Quantum ChromoDynamics (QCD) called Color Glass Condensate (CGC). Monte-Carlo sampling of the color charge for the incoming nuclei are used to calculate their classical gluon fields. Following the recent work by Chen et al. we calculate the energy momentum tensor of those fields at early times in the collision event-by-event. This can then be used for subsequent hydrodynamic evolution of the single events.

## Key claim

The authors present a semi-analytic event generator for Color Glass Condensate (CGC) initial conditions that computes the energy-momentum tensor order-by-order in proper time, enabling event-by-event initial conditions for subsequent hydrodynamic evolution, but the power series expansion is only convergent up to roughly τ ∼ 1/Q_s.

## Method

The authors sample color charge densities for the two colliding nuclei as Gaussian fluctuations with strength gμ, then solve the Yang-Mills equations in covariant gauge using a regularized Green's function with an infrared cutoff m and a Gaussian coarse-graining function for finite resolution λ. They transform the solution to light-cone gauge via Wilson lines and compute the gluon fields, then calculate the stress-energy tensor order-by-order in proper time τ, implementing up to second order with plans to extend to fourth order.

## Key numbers

- The transverse density μ scales with nuclear size as μ ∼ A^(1/3)
- The power series expansion for the gluon field is convergent up to roughly τ ∼ 1/Q_s
- A sample charge distribution is shown for a nucleus of radius R = 3 fm with resolution scale λ = 0.2 fm
- The initial energy density at τ = 0 has only diagonal elements: T^(11)_(0) = T^(22)_(0) = ε_0 = -T^(33)_(0)
- First-order contributions to the stress-energy tensor give radial flow α^i = -∇^i ε_0 and directed flow β^i = ε^(ij)([D^j,B_0]E_0 - [D^j,E_0]B_0)

## Context

This work extends the analytical results of Chen et al. (2015) for the early-time expansion of gluon fields in heavy ion collisions, and provides a semi-analytic alternative to the fully numerical IP-Glasma event generator by Schenke et al. (2013). The event generator enables matching to viscous hydrodynamics as in Gale et al. (2012), and allows calculation of directed flow and angular momentum from first principles for use in initial conditions.
