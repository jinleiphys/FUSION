# Hyperon-nucleon interaction and baryonic contact terms in SU(3) chiral effective field theory

> In this proceeding we summarize results for baryonic contact terms derived within SU(3) chiral effective field theory. The four-baryon contact terms, necessary for the description of the hyperon-nucleon interaction, include SU(3) symmetric and explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms. They also include four-baryon contact terms involving pseudoscalar mesons, which become important for three-body forces. Furthermore we derive the leading order six-baryon contact terms in the non-relativistic limit and study their contribution to the $ΛNN$ three-body contact interaction. These results could play an important role in studies of hypernuclei or hyperons in nuclear matter.

# Hyperon-nucleon interaction and baryonic contact terms in SU(3) chiral effective field theory

## Key claim
The paper derives the complete set of four-baryon and leading six-baryon contact terms in SU(3) chiral effective field theory up to order O(q²), but the actual numerical impact on hypernuclei or neutron star matter is not computed; the results are presented as a formal framework that "could play an important role" in future studies, contingent on fitting the low-energy constants to scattering data.

## Method
The author constructs a covariant chiral baryon-baryon contact Lagrangian in flavor SU(3) up to order O(q²) using the external fields method of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985), enforcing invariance under charge conjugation, parity, time reversal, Hermitian conjugation, and local chiral transformations. For the six-baryon contact terms, the construction proceeds analogously with antisymmetrization in both initial and final states to enforce the Pauli principle, followed by a non-relativistic reduction to obtain potentials expressed in spin-space operators.

## Key numbers
- 40 terms in the minimal set of the chiral contact Lagrangian up to O(q²) for pure baryon-baryon interaction
- 28 of these 40 contact terms contribute to SU(3) symmetric potentials in partial waves ¹S₀, ³S₁, ¹P₁, ³P₀, ³P₁, ³P₂, ³D₁↔³S₁, and ¹P₁↔³P₁
- 12 low-energy constants contribute to ¹S₀ and ³S₁ partial waves and produce SU(3) symmetry breaking contributions linear in the quark masses
- 18 low-energy constants for the leading three-baryon contact force across all strangeness sectors
- For strangeness 0 (NNN system): 1 constant; for strangeness -1 (ΛNN, ΣNN): +7 constants; for strangeness -2 (ΛΛN, ΛΣN, ΣΣN, ΞNN): +9 constants; for strangeness -3: +1 constant; for strangeness -4, -5, -6: +0 constants each

## Context
This work extends the SU(2) chiral effective field theory framework for nuclear forces, as developed by Bernard (1995), Epelbaum (2009), Machleidt (2011), and Holt (2013), into the strangeness sector using SU(3) chiral effective field theory. It directly supports the hyperon-nucleon interaction calculation at next-to-leading order by Haidenbauer (2013) by providing the necessary contact-term Lagrangian. The derived six-baryon contact terms are a first step toward including hyperon-nucleon-nucleon three-body forces, which may be needed to achieve enough repulsion to explain the two-solar-mass neutron stars observed by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013).
