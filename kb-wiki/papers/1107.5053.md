# Chiral phase transitions and quantum critical points of the D3/D7(D5) system with mutually perpendicular E and B fields at finite temperature and density

> We study chiral symmetry restoration with increasing temperature and density in gauge theories subject to mutually perpendicular electric and magnetic fields using holography. We determine the chiral symmetry breaking phase structure of the D3/D7 and D3/D5 systems in the temperature-density-electric field directions. A magnetic field may break the chiral symmetry and an additional electric field induces Ohm and Hall currents as well as restoring the chiral symmetry. At zero temperature the D3/D5 system displays a line of holographic BKT phase transitions in the density-electric field plane, while the D3/D7 system shows a mean-field phase transition. At intermediate temperatures, the transitions in the density-electric field plane are of first order at low density, transforming to second order at critical points as density rises. At high temperature the transition is only ever first order.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in holographic gauge theories with mutually perpendicular electric and magnetic fields, the D3/D5 system exhibits a line of holographic BKT phase transitions in the density-electric field plane at zero temperature, while the D3/D7 system shows only mean-field transitions; however, this BKT line is independent of the electric field strength until the electric field becomes super-critical, at which point the BKT transition is entirely overcome.

## Method

The authors use the D3/D7 and D3/D5 probe brane systems in an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole geometry to model chiral symmetry breaking in gauge theories at finite temperature, density, and with perpendicular electric and magnetic fields. They compute the DBI action for the probe branes, determine the induced Ohm and Hall currents analytically from reality conditions on the action, and then solve numerically for the brane embeddings to extract the chiral condensate as a function of temperature, density, and electric field at fixed magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* D3/D7 system at zero temperature and density: chiral condensate scales as \(c = 0.225 (1 - \tE^2)^{3/4}\) near the critical electric field \(\tE_c = 1\) (with \(\tB=1\)).
* D3/D5 system at zero temperature and density: chiral condensate scales as \(c = 0.580 (1 - \tE^2)^{1/2}\) near the critical electric field \(\tE_c = 1\) (with \(\tB=1\)).
* Ohm current critical exponents at zero temperature: \(\hJ_x \sim (\tE-\tB)^{1/2}\) for D3/D5 and for D3/D7 with \(d \ne 0\); \(\hJ_x \sim (\tE-\tB)^{3/4}\) for D3/D7 with \(d=0\).
* Hall current at zero temperature for \(\tE \le \tB\): \(\hJ_y = - d \tE / \tB\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of chiral symmetry breaking in the D3/D7 and D3/D5 systems previously investigated with parallel electric and magnetic fields by Evans, Kim, and Shock (2011). It contrasts with the parallel-field case where density could not be generated, and instead reveals a richer phase structure with Hall currents. The results enable future studies of quantum critical points in holographic models relevant to condensed matter systems, particularly the BKT transition in 2+1-dimensional defect theories.
