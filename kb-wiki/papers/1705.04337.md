# Variational calculation of the ground state of closed-shell nuclei up to $A=40$

> Variational calculations of ground-state properties of $^4$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca are carried out employing realistic phenomenological two- and three-nucleon potentials. The trial wave function includes two- and three-body correlations acting on a product of single-particle determinants. Expectation values are evaluated with a cluster expansion for the spin-isospin dependent correlations considering up to five-body cluster terms. The optimal wave function is obtained by minimizing the energy expectation value over a set of up to 20 parameters by means of a nonlinear optimization library. We present results for the binding energy, charge radius, one- and two-body densities, single-nucleon momentum distribution, charge form factor, and Coulomb sum rule. We find that the employed three-nucleon interaction becomes repulsive for $A\geq16$. In $^{16}$O the inclusion of such a force provides a better description of the properties of the nucleus. In $^{40}$Ca instead, the repulsive behavior of the three-body interaction fails to reproduce experimental data for the charge radius and the charge form factor. We find that the high-momentum region of the momentum distributions, determined by the short-range terms of nuclear correlations, exhibit a universal behavior independent of the particular nucleus. The comparison of the Coulomb sum rules for $^4$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca reported in this work will help elucidate in-medium modifications of the nucleon form factors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that cluster variational Monte Carlo (CVMC) calculations can be extended to medium-mass closed-shell nuclei up to A=40 using realistic phenomenological two- and three-nucleon potentials, but finds that the repulsive behavior of the employed three-nucleon interaction (UIX) for A≥16 fails to reproduce experimental charge radius and form factor data for ⁴⁰Ca, while improving the description for ¹⁶O.

## Method

The authors employ cluster variational Monte Carlo (CVMC) to evaluate expectation values of the Hamiltonian for trial wave functions consisting of two- and three-body correlation operators acting on a Jastrow wave function built from single-particle determinants. The trial wave function is optimized by minimizing the energy expectation value over up to 20 variational parameters using a nonlinear optimization library, with the cluster expansion carried out up to five-body cluster terms. The calculations use the Argonne v18 (AV18) two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon potential, with spatial integrals evaluated via Metropolis Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies: ⁴He at 26.9 MeV (experiment 28.3 MeV), ¹⁶O at 119.6 MeV (experiment 127.6 MeV), ⁴⁰Ca at 306.2 MeV (experiment 342.1 MeV) using AV18+UIX
- Charge radii: ⁴He at 1.68 fm (experiment 1.68 fm), ¹⁶O at 2.66 fm (experiment 2.73 fm), ⁴⁰Ca at 3.66 fm (experiment 3.48 fm) using AV18+UIX
- The three-nucleon interaction contribution to the energy per particle is attractive for ⁴He (−1.5 MeV) but becomes repulsive for ¹⁶O (+1.2 MeV) and ⁴⁰Ca (+3.8 MeV)
- The cluster expansion converges to within 0.5% of the total energy for ¹⁶O and within 1% for ⁴⁰Ca when including up to five-body cluster terms

## Context

This work extends the cluster variational Monte Carlo approach previously applied to ¹⁶O by Pieper (1992) using earlier potentials, now employing the modern AV18+UIX Hamiltonian and reaching up to ⁴⁰Ca. The results enable comparison with coupled cluster calculations by Hagen (2014) and no-core shell model studies by Barrett (2013), while the computed Coulomb sum rules for ⁴He, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca will help elucidate in-medium modifications of nucleon form factors as discussed by Cloet (2016). The finding that the UIX three-nucleon force becomes repulsive for A≥16 is consistent with the need for repulsive three-body forces to explain nuclear matter saturation properties as studied by Akmal (1998) and Carbone (2014).
