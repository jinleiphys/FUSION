# Pauli resonance states in light nuclei: how they appear and how they can be eliminated

> Systematic analysis of parameters and properties of the Pauli resonance states are performed for light nuclei $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, $^{8}$Be, $^{9}$Be and $^{10}$B, which are treated as two-cluster systems. The Pauli resonance states are redundant solutions of the resonating group method appearing when one try use more advanced description of the internal structure of interacting clusters. Our calculations are performed in a standard and advanced versions of the resonating group method. The standard version employs wave functions of many-particle shell model to describe internal motion of nucleons within each cluster. The advanced version is based on three-cluster resonating group method. As in the standard version, the internal wave functions of three clusters are approximated by wave functions of many-particle shell model model. However, in advanced version one of pair of clusters forms a bound state, and third cluster is considered to interact with such state. It is found that the Pauli resonance states in nuclei under consideration have energy between 11 and 46 MeV, and their widths vary from 8 keV to 6.7 MeV. Analysis of wave functions of Pauli resonance states and matrix elements of norm kernel allowed us to formulate an effective method for eliminating Pauli resonance states. It is demonstrated that this method effectively eliminate all determined the Pauli resonance states.

## Key claim

Pauli resonance states are redundant solutions of the resonating group method that appear when using a more advanced description of the internal structure of interacting clusters, and they can be eliminated by removing specific eigenstates of the norm kernel, either by omitting almost-forbidden Pauli states (REV method) or by removing specific oscillator functions (ROF method).

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations for light nuclei (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁸Be, ⁹Be, ¹⁰B) treated as two-cluster systems using both standard and advanced versions of the resonating group method (RGM). The standard version employed many-particle shell model wave functions with a common oscillator length for both clusters, while the advanced version used a three-cluster RGM where one pair of clusters forms a bound state described by a two-cluster solution, and the third cluster interacts with that bound state. The Minnesota potential was used as the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and phase shifts were computed to extract resonance energies and widths via the Breit-Wigner formula.

## Key numbers

- Pauli resonance states in the nuclei studied have energies between 11 and 46 MeV
- Pauli resonance widths vary from 8 keV to 6.7 MeV
- For ⁶Li, the three-cluster configuration ⁴He+\(p+n\) was used with oscillator length \(b = 1.285\) fm and exchange parameter \(u = 0.863\)
- For ⁷Li and ⁷Be, the oscillator length was \(b = 1.311\) fm and exchange parameter \(u = 0.956\)
- For ⁹Be, the oscillator length was \(b = 1.285\) fm and exchange parameter \(u = 0.950\)
- For ¹⁰B, the oscillator length was \(b = 1.298\) fm and exchange parameter \(u = 0.900\)
- 100 oscillator functions were used to describe scattering of the third cluster on the two-cluster subsystem
- 4 Gaussian functions were employed to obtain energies and wave functions of two-cluster subsystems

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Pauli resonance states in the ⁴He+¹⁶O system by Wurzer and others (1983, 1985) and by Kukulin and collaborators (1992), who formulated the REV method for eliminating such states. The present paper applies a similar analysis to a broader set of light nuclei and proposes an alternative elimination method (ROF). The three-cluster model used was developed by Vasilevsky and collaborators (2009) and previously applied to ⁷Be, ⁷Li, and ¹⁰B. This work enables cleaner calculations of physical scattering observables in light nuclei by removing spurious Pauli resonance states that would otherwise distort phase shifts and cross sections.
