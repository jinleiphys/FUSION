# Holographic dilepton production in a thermalizing plasma

> We determine the out-of-equilibrium production rate of dileptons at rest in strongly coupled N=4 Super Yang-Mills plasma using the AdS/CFT correspondence. Thermalization is achieved via the gravitational collapse of a thin shell of matter in AdS_5 space and the subsequent formation of a black hole, which we describe in a quasistatic approximation. Prior to thermalization, the dilepton spectral function is observed to oscillate as a function of frequency, but the amplitude of the oscillations decreases when thermal equilibrium is approached. At the same time, we follow the flow of the quasinormal spectrum of the corresponding U(1) vector field towards its equilibrium limit.

## Key claim

The out-of-equilibrium dilepton production rate at rest in strongly coupled N=4 Super Yang-Mills plasma is computed using a quasistatic approximation for the gravitational collapse of a thin shell, meaning the calculation is valid only when the frequency of the dilepton modes is much larger than the inverse time scale of the shell's motion.

## Method

The authors model thermalization via the gravitational collapse of an infinitely thin shell of matter in AdS_5 space, with the metric inside the shell being flat AdS_5 and outside being the Schwarzschild-AdS_5 black hole solution. They impose junction conditions for a U(1) vector field at the shell radius, solve the resulting equations of motion analytically in terms of hypergeometric functions, and use the AdS/CFT prescription to extract the retarded Green's function and spectral density for dileptons at zero three-momentum.

## Key numbers

* The thermal spectral function for dileptons at rest is given by chi^thermal(w) = (N_c^2 T^2 / 4) * [pi * hat(w)^2 * sinh(pi * hat(w))] / [cosh(pi * hat(w)) - cos(pi * hat(w))], where hat(w) = w / (2 pi T).
* The thermal quasinormal mode poles of the retarded correlator are located at w = 2 pi T n ( +/- 1 - i ) = 2 r_h n ( +/- 1 - i ), for n = 0, 1, 2, ...
* The shell radius r_s is related to the horizon radius r_h = 5 (in units where the AdS curvature radius is unity) for the numerical plots, with the thermal limit approached as r_s approaches r_h.
* The parameter t_echo = (1 / (2 r_h)) * [ pi/2 + ln 2 - ln( r_s / r_h - 1 ) ] characterizes the flow of certain poles in the complex frequency plane.

## Context

This work extends the quasistatic falling-shell approach of Danielsson et al. (2000) from scalar fields to U(1) gauge fields, enabling the study of dilepton production as a probe of thermalization in strongly coupled plasma. It complements the analysis of metric perturbation correlators by Lin and Shuryak (2008) and contrasts with weakly coupled "bottom-up" thermalization scenarios such as those of Baier et al. (2001) and Kurkela et al. (2012), where soft modes thermalize before hard ones. The results enable future studies of holographic thermalization beyond the quasistatic approximation, as pursued by Erdmenger et al. (2012), and at finite chemical potential, as studied by Caceres et al. (2012).
