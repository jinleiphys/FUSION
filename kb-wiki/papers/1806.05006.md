# Exact scattering waves off nonlocal potentials under Coulomb interaction within Schrödinger's integro-differential equation

> An exact solution for the scattering wavefunction from a nonlocal potential in the presence of Coulomb interaction is presented. The approach is based on the construction of a Coulomb Green's function in coordinate space whose associated kernel involves any nonlocal optical potential superposed to the Coulomb-screened interaction. The scattering wavefunction, exact solution of the integro-differential Schrödinger's equation, poses no restrictions on the type of nonlocality of the interaction nor on the beam energy.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact solution for scattering wavefunctions from nonlocal potentials in the presence of Coulomb interaction, constructed via a Coulomb Green's function in coordinate space whose kernel involves any nonlocal optical potential superposed to the Coulomb-screened interaction, with no restrictions on the type of nonlocality or beam energy.

## Method

The authors construct a non-singular Coulomb propagator in partial-wave form as \(G_l^{c(+)}(r,r';k) = -(i/k) F_l(kr_{<}){\cal H}_l^{(+)}(kr_{>})\), where \(F_l\) and \({\cal H}_l^{(+)}\) are regular and outgoing Coulomb functions. They then formulate the scattering wavefunction as a Lippmann-Schwinger-type integral equation, \(u_{jl}(r) = (1/k)F_l(kr) + (2m/\hbar^2) \iint dr'dr'' G_l^{c(+)}(r,r';k) [r'U^{[s]}_{jl}(r',r'') r''] u_{jl}(r'')\), which is solved by direct matrix inversion on a discretized radial mesh without iterative procedures or localization approximations.

## Key numbers

* For \(p+^{40}\)Ca elastic scattering at 30.3 MeV, differences in the ratio-to-Rutherford cross section between the present approach (ESW) and DWBA98 become slightly noticeable for \(\theta_{c.m.} > 140^\circ\).
* For \(p+^{40}\)Ca elastic scattering at 300 MeV, results for both \(\sigma(\theta)/\sigma_R(\theta)\) and analyzing power \(A_y\) overlap almost completely between ESW and DWBA98 up to \(\theta_{c.m.}=60^\circ\), corresponding to a momentum transfer of 4 fm\(^{-1}\).
* The numerical implementation used \(N=150\) mesh points with spacing \(h=0.1\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the exact treatment of nonlocal potentials in the presence of Coulomb interaction beyond the finite-difference matrix method of Raynal (1998) and Gibbs (2006), which was previously the only established kernel-independent approach. The method provides an alternative to iterative procedures (Titus 2014, 2016) and Taylor-approximation methods (Upadhyay 2018) that may fail for microscopic potentials with complex off-diagonal structure, as demonstrated by the failure of the NLAT code for the microscopic model used here. The approach enables benchmark solutions for distorted-wave Born approximation calculations and is being extended to coupled-channels and inelastic processes.
