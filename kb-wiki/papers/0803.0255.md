# Brown-Rho Scaling in the Strong Coupling Lattice QCD

> We examine the Brown-Rho scaling for meson masses in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD with one species of staggered fermion. Analytical expression of meson masses is derived at finite temperature and chemical potential. We find that meson masses are approximately proportional to the equilibrium value of the chiral condensate, which evolves as a function of temperature and chemical potential.

## Key claim

In the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD with one species of staggered fermion, meson masses (except for the pion) are approximately proportional to the equilibrium value of the chiral condensate, which evolves with temperature and chemical potential; this proportionality holds only in the leading order of the 1/d expansion and under the mean field approximation.

## Method

The authors derive an analytical expression for meson masses in the strong coupling limit (g → ∞) of lattice QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential, using one species of staggered fermion. They integrate out spatial link variables in the leading order of the 1/d expansion, introduce a mesonic auxiliary field via the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, and obtain the effective potential and meson propagator from the determinant of a matrix involving the temporal link variables. Meson masses are then extracted as the pole energy of the propagator at zero momentum, with the equilibrium chiral condensate determined by minimizing the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- The lattice spacing is fixed as a⁻¹ = 497 MeV by fitting the π and ρ meson masses in vacuum.
- The current quark mass is set to m₀ = 9.5 MeV in the vacuum fit.
- In the chiral limit (m₀ = 0), the pion mass for κ = -d satisfies M ≈ 2√(σ̄ m₀) for small current quark mass, representing the PCAC relation.
- The vacuum chiral condensate is σ̄_vac ∼ a⁻¹, approximately 497 MeV.
- The meson mass formula in the finite temperature treatment is M = 2 arcsinh √[(σ̄ + m₀)((κ + d)/d σ̄ + m₀)], where κ ranges from -d to d in steps of 2.

## Context

This work extends the study of hadron masses in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD (SCL-LQCD), previously examined at zero temperature by Kluberg-Stern (1982) and Kawamoto and Smit (1984), to finite temperature and chemical potential. The Brown-Rho scaling conjecture (Brown and Rho, 1991) proposed that in-medium hadron masses scale with the pion decay constant; similar behavior was found in the NJL model (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1994) and QCD sum rules (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992). The analytical approach avoids the sign problem that hinders lattice Monte Carlo simulations at high density, and enables further study of meson mass modifications in hot and dense matter, though the authors note open questions including meson assignment, pion mass behavior near the chiral transition, and the too-high critical temperature in the strong coupling limit.
