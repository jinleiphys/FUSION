# Diquarkyonic matter: quarks, diquarks and baryons

> In this work, we investigate the color-spin interaction of a quark, a diquark and a baryon with their surrounding baryons and/or quark matter. We extend our previous work by increasing the maximum number of surrounding baryons to 5 and additionally consider all possible diquark probes that are immersed in such surroundings. This is accomplished by classifying all possible flavor and spin states of the resulting multiquark configuration in both the flavor SU(2) and SU(3) symmetric cases. We also discuss the three-body confinement potential and show that this does not contribute to the outcome. Furthermore, we find that a quark becomes more stable than a baryon when the number of surrounding baryons is three or more. Finally, when we consider the internal color-spin factor of a probe, our results show that the effects of the color-spin interaction of a multiquark configuration is consistent with the so-called diquarkyonic configuration.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous work on quarkyoniclike matter by increasing the maximum number of surrounding baryons from 3 to 5 and by considering all possible diquark probes, finding that a quark becomes more stable than a baryon when the number of surrounding baryons is three or more, and that the color-spin interaction of a multiquark configuration is consistent with the so-called diquarkyonic configuration.

## Method

The authors classify all possible flavor and spin states of multiquark configurations containing a probe (quark, diquark, or baryon) surrounded by up to five octet baryons, using both flavor SU(2) and SU(3) symmetric cases. They calculate the color-spin interaction factor \(H_{CS}\) for each configuration using group-theoretic formulas involving Casimir operators, and subtract the internal color-spin factors of the surrounding baryons and the probe to obtain the interaction energy. They also examine the three-body confinement potential from three-gluon exchange and show it does not contribute to the outcome.

## Key numbers

* For a quark probe surrounded by three or more baryons, the quark becomes more stable than a baryon (no explicit numerical energy value given for this threshold).
* The maximum number of surrounding baryons considered is 5, extending the previous work which considered up to 3.
* Neutron star radius estimates cited from NICER: \(R_{1.4}\simeq 12.45\) km and \(R_{2.08}\simeq 12.35\) km from pulsar J0740+6620 (Miller 2021, Riley 2021).
* The flavor states of 5 baryons include multiplicities such as \(\mathbf{1}_{(m=32)}\), \(\mathbf{8}_{(m=145)}\), \(\mathbf{10}_{(m=100)}\), \(\mathbf{27}_{(m=180)}\), and others up to \(\mathbf{216}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Park 2022) by increasing the maximum number of surrounding baryons from 3 to 5 and adding all possible diquark probes, building on the quarkyonic matter concept introduced by McLerran (2007) to explain stiff equations of state in neutron star cores. The color-spin interaction approach follows earlier work on the repulsive core between nucleons (Park 2019) and on quarkyoniclike phases (Park 2021), and the results enable further investigation of whether a diquarkyonic phase distinct from quarkyonic matter can appear in dense matter.
