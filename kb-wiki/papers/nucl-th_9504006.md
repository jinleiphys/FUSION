# Factorization Contributions and the Breaking of the $ΔI=1/2$ Rule in Weak $ΛNρ$ and $ΣNρ$ Couplings

> We compute the modified factorization contributions to the $Λ\rightarrow Nρ$ and $Σ\rightarrow Nρ$ couplings and demonstrate that these contributions naturally include $ΔI=3/2$ terms which are comparable ($\simeq 0.4$ to $-0.8$ times) in magnitude to the corresponding $ΔI=1/2$ terms. As a consequence, we conclude that models which treat vector meson exchange contributions to the weak conversion process $ΛN\rightarrow NN$ assuming such weak couplings to satisfy the $ΔI=1/2$ rule are unlikely to be reliable.

# Factorization Contributions and the Breaking of the ΔI=1/2 Rule in Weak ΛNρ and ΣNρ Couplings

## Key claim

The modified factorization contributions to the weak Λ→Nρ and Σ→Nρ couplings naturally include ΔI=3/2 terms that are comparable in magnitude (0.4 to -0.8 times) to the corresponding ΔI=1/2 terms, making models that assume the ΔI=1/2 rule for vector meson exchange contributions to ΛN→NN unreliable.

## Method

The authors computed the modified factorization contributions to the weak ΛNρ and ΣNρ couplings using the effective ΔS=1 non-leptonic weak Hamiltonian with Wilson coefficients evaluated at a scale of 1 GeV. They employed the factorization approximation where external contributions are separated into a product of baryon transition form factors (from CVC and semileptonic decay data) and meson decay constants, taking into account Fierz rearrangements of the operators. The penguin operator contributions (O5, O6) vanish for ρ couplings because their Fierz-rearranged forms involve only scalar and pseudoscalar currents whose vacuum-to-ρ matrix elements are zero.

## Key numbers

- For Λ→pρ⁻ couplings at q²=0: f₁^w = -4.1 × 10⁻⁷, f₂^w = -6.6 × 10⁻⁷, g₁^w = 2.9 × 10⁻⁷
- For Λ→nρ° couplings at q²=0: f₁^w = 0.36 × 10⁻⁷, f₂^w = 0.58 × 10⁻⁷, g₁^w = -0.26 × 10⁻⁷
- For Σ⁻→nρ⁻ couplings at q²=0: f₁^w = -3.3 × 10⁻⁷, f₂^w = 6.1 × 10⁻⁷, g₁^w = -1.1 × 10⁻⁷
- For ρ⁻ couplings, the leading ΔI=3/2 term is 0.52 times the leading ΔI=1/2 term and 0.41 times the net ΔI=1/2 contribution
- For ρ° couplings, the leading ΔI=3/2 contribution is -1.03 times the leading ΔI=1/2 contribution and -0.82 times the net ΔI=1/2 contribution
- Wilson coefficients at 1 GeV: c₁ = -1.90, c₂ = 0.14, c₃ = 0.10, c₄ = 0.49
- Ground state baryon pole contributions to Λ→pρ⁻ f₁^w: -10.5 × 10⁻⁷ (de la Torre model) and -11.3 × 10⁻⁷ (Nardulli model)
- Factorization contributions are approximately 1/3 of the full model predictions for f₁^w and f₂^w, and between 1/4 and 1 times the full prediction for g₁^w

## Context

This work extends the factorization approach previously applied to hyperon π decays (Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov, 1977) to the vector meson case, where the penguin operator enhancements that explain the ΔI=1/2 rule for pions are absent. The authors compare their factorization results to two existing models for ΛNρ couplings: the de la Torre model (1982), used in the meson-exchange treatment of ΛN→NN by Dubach (1986), and the Nardulli model (1988). The finding that factorization contributions are not small relative to these model predictions, combined with the large ΔI=3/2 components, implies that future treatments of weak hyperon-nucleon conversion in the meson-exchange framework must account for ΔI=1/2 rule violations in vector meson couplings.
