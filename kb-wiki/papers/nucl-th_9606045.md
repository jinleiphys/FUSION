# Transverse Shocks in the Turbulent Gluon Plasma Produced in Ultra-Relativistic A+A

> Mini-jet production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions leads to initial conditions characterized by large fluctuations of the local energy density (hot spots) and of the collective flow field (turbulence). Assuming that local equilibrium is reached on a small time scale, $\sim 0.5$ fm/c, the transverse evolution of those initial conditions is computed using hydrodynamics. We find that a new class of collective flow phenomena (hadronic volcanoes) could arise under such conditions. This could be observable via enhanced azimuthal fluctuations of the transverse energy flow, $d^2E_\perp/dϕdy$.

## Key claim

Mini-jet production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions creates highly inhomogeneous and turbulent initial conditions (hot spots and chaotic velocity fields), and when evolved hydrodynamically these conditions can produce a new class of collective flow phenomena called "hadronic volcanoes", observable via enhanced azimuthal fluctuations of the transverse energy flow, but the effect is partially obscured by the large initial anisotropy in realistic turbulent cases, requiring higher-order correlation analysis for confirmation.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING event generator to compute the local energy density and transverse momentum density of produced gluons at mid-rapidity in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV, coarse-grained on a transverse resolution scale of Δr⊥ = 1 fm (the maximal causally connected diameter at the assumed thermalization time τth = 0.5 fm/c). These initial conditions were then evolved using ideal hydrodynamics with two different equations of state: an ideal gas (p = ε/3) and a first-order Bag model equation of state. The azimuthal angle dependence of the transverse energy flow dE⊥/dφdy was computed to identify shock-induced peaks.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time assumed: τth ≈ 0.5 fm/c
- Transverse resolution scale: Δr⊥ = 1 fm (causally connected diameter at τth)
- Rapidity width for coarse-graining: Δy = 1
- Average initial energy density from Bjorken formula: ⟨ε(τ)⟩ ≈ 40 (τ0/τ) GeV/fm³
- Rapidity density of mini-jet gluons in Au+Au: dNg/dy ≈ 300–600, depending on mini-jet cut-off p0 = 1–2 GeV/c
- Soft component modeled by ≈ 1700 low-p⊥ gluons per unit rapidity with rms p⊥ = 0.3 GeV/c, contributing εs ≈ 4 GeV/fm³ at τth = 0.5 fm/c
- Deconfinement/chiral symmetry scale: εc ≈ 1 GeV/fm³
- Number of causally disconnected domains in nuclear area: Nd(τ) = (R/τ)²

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear shock waves, first proposed by Greiner (1974), to the ultra-relativistic energy regime of RHIC, where mini-jet production (Eskola, 1995) creates a turbulent gluon plasma rather than the homogeneous, boost-invariant plasma assumed in most prior QGP signature calculations (Quark Matter '93 proceedings). The authors show that the standard Bjorken formula for average energy density hides large local fluctuations, and that signatures such as high-pT direct photon and heavy-quark production will be enhanced in hot spots (Gyulassy and Wang, 1995). The results enable searches for hadronic volcanoes via higher-order transverse energy correlation functions, distinguishing this dynamical mechanism from surface instabilities (T.D. Lee).
