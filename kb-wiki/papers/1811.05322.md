# Partial dynamical symmetry and the phonon structure of cadmium isotopes

> The phonon structure and spectral properties of states in $^{110}$Cd are addressed by including proton excitations in the phonon basis and exploiting a partial dynamical symmetry that mixes only certain classes of states and maintains the vibrational character in the majority of normal states.

# Partial dynamical symmetry and the phonon structure of cadmium isotopes

## Key claim

The phonon (vibrational) interpretation of low-lying normal states in $^{110}$Cd is maintained for the majority of states, but only by invoking a partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) that preserves U(5) symmetry in class A states while allowing mixing in classes B and C; the previously rejected normal-intruder strong-mixing scenario is replaced by a PDS-based configuration mixing (CM) approach that reproduces the anomalous decay patterns of non-yrast states.

## Method

The authors construct a U(5) partial dynamical symmetry Hamiltonian within the interacting boson model (IBM) by adding terms to the U(5) dynamical symmetry Hamiltonian that annihilate class A states ($n_d=\tau$, $n_\Delta=0$) while mixing states of classes B and C. They then embed this PDS Hamiltonian in a configuration-mixing framework (IBM-CM) that couples a normal $N=7$ boson space (U(5)-PDS) with an intruder $N+2=9$ boson space (SO(6)-type), including a mixing term between the two spaces.

## Key numbers

- $B(E2; 0^+_3 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$: calculated 0.25 W.u., consistent with experimental upper limit < 7.9 W.u. (U(5)-DS value: 46.29 W.u.)
- $B(E2; 2^+_5 \rightarrow 4^+_1)$: calculated 0.19 W.u., consistent with experimental upper limit < 5 W.u. (U(5)-DS value: 19.84 W.u.)
- $B(E2; 2^+_5 \rightarrow 2^+_2)$: calculated 0.12 W.u., consistent with experimental value $0.7^{+0.5}_{-0.6}$ W.u. (U(5)-DS value: 11.02 W.u.)
- $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$: 27.0(8) W.u. experimental, fitted to 27.00 W.u. in both U(5)-DS and U(5)-PDS-CM calculations
- $B(E2; 2^+_{3;i} \rightarrow 0^+_{2;i})$: 29(5) W.u. experimental, calculated 29.00 W.u. in U(5)-PDS-CM
- Hamiltonian parameters for U(5)-PDS-CM: $t_1=767.83$, $t_2=-t_3=73.62$, $t_4=18.47$, $r_0=2.15$, $e_0=-6.92$, $\kappa=-72.73$, $\Delta=9978.86$, $\alpha=-42.78$ keV, with $N=7$ (normal) and $N=9$ (intruder) boson numbers

## Context

This work addresses the "Cd problem" raised by Garrett (2008, 2012), who showed that simple normal-intruder mixing between spherical U(5)-like and deformed SO(6)-like states fails to explain the anomalous decay patterns in $^{110}$Cd, casting doubt on the multi-phonon interpretation. The authors extend their previous PDS-based approaches for SU(3)-DS (Leviatan 1996, Leviatan and Sinai 1999, Leviatan et al. 2013) and SO(6)-DS (Ramos 2009, Leviatan and Gavrielov 2017) to the U(5) chain of the IBM. The resulting U(5)-PDS-CM framework preserves the vibrational character for class A states while explaining the anomalous decays of $0^+_3$, $0^+_4$, and $2^+_5$ states through mixing within the normal sector (via PDS) and weak coupling to intruder states. This enables a symmetry-based resolution of the long-standing cadmium puzzle without abandoning the spherical vibrator picture for the majority of normal states.
