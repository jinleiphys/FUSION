# Impact parameter dependence of the isospin effects and mass dependence of balance energy

> We study the effect of isospin degree of freedom on the balance energy (E$_{bal}$) as well as its mass dependence throughout the mass range for two different sets of isobaric systems with N/Z = 1 and 1.4 at different colliding geometries ranging from the central to peripheral ones. Our findings reveal the dominance of Coulomb repulsion in isospin effects on E$_{bal}$ as well as its mass dependence throughout the range of the colliding geometry. Our results also indicate that the effect of symmetry energy on the energy of vanishing flow is uniform throughout the mass range and throughout the colliding geometry.

# Impact parameter dependence of the isospin effects and mass dependence of balance energy

## Key claim

The dominance of Coulomb repulsion over symmetry energy in determining isospin effects on balance energy (E$_{bal}$) and its mass dependence holds throughout the full range of colliding geometry, from central to peripheral collisions, not only at semi-central geometries as previously shown. The effect of symmetry energy on the energy of vanishing flow is uniform across both mass range and colliding geometry, but only when the Coulomb potential is artificially reduced.

## Method

The authors performed simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state, momentum-dependent interactions, and an anisotropic standard isospin- and energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section (σ = 0.8 σ$_{NN}^{free}$). They simulated reactions for two sets of isobaric systems with N/Z = 1.0 and 1.4 across masses from 48 to 270, at four impact parameter bins ranging from central (0.15 < $\hat{b}$ < 0.25) to peripheral (0.75 < $\hat{b}$ < 0.85). To isolate the role of Coulomb repulsion, they repeated the calculations with the Coulomb potential reduced by a factor of 100.

## Key numbers

- Balance energy follows a power law E$_{bal}$ ∝ A$^{\tau}$ for both N/Z = 1.0 and 1.4 at all colliding geometries; the exponent τ varies with impact parameter.
- For full Coulomb calculations, the difference between τ$_{1.4}$ and τ$_{1.0}$ (Δτ) increases with impact parameter, indicating enhanced isospin effects at peripheral geometries.
- For reduced Coulomb calculations, the τ values are: τ$_{1.0}$ (τ$_{1.4}$) = -0.17 (-0.14) for BIN 1, -0.17 (-0.19) for BIN 2, -0.24 (-0.26) for BIN 3, and -0.31 (-0.29) for BIN 4; the difference Δτ is negligible, showing uniform symmetry energy effect.
- For N/Z = 1.4, E$_{bal}$ increases with impact parameter for all masses; for N/Z = 1.0, E$_{bal}$ decreases with impact parameter for heavier masses (A = 192 and 240), in contrast to previous studies.
- The saturation time for transverse flow varies from 100 fm/c for lighter masses to 300 fm/c for heavier masses.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Gautam 2010) which examined isospin effects on E$_{bal}$ only at semi-central colliding geometry (BIN 2) and found Coulomb repulsion dominance there. The present results confirm that this dominance persists across all impact parameters, from central to peripheral collisions. The study also builds on earlier work by Li (1996), Pak (1997), and Daffin (1998) who found that neutron-rich systems have higher E$_{bal}$ than neutron-deficient systems at all geometries, and that isospin effects are more pronounced at peripheral geometries. The finding that for N/Z = 1.0 heavier systems show decreasing E$_{bal}$ with increasing impact parameter contradicts the trend reported in those earlier studies. The work enables future investigations into the role of Coulomb repulsion in other isospin-dependent observables in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at peripheral geometries where the effect is largest.
