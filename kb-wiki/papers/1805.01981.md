# The center of mass motion of short-range correlated nucleon pairs studied via the A(e,e'pp) reaction

> Short-Range Correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs are a vital part of the nucleus, accounting for almost all nucleons with momentum greater than the Fermi momentum (kF). A fundamental characteristic of SRC pairs is having large relative momenta as compared to kF, and smaller center-of-mass (c.m.) which indicates a small separation distance between the nucleons in the pair. Determining the c.m. momentum distribution of SRC pairs is essential for understanding their formation process. We report here on the extraction of the c.m. motion of proton-proton (pp) SRC pairs in Carbon and, for the first time in heavier and ansymetric nuclei: aluminum, iron, and lead, from measurements of the A(e,e'pp) reaction. We find that the pair c.m. motion for these nuclei can be described by a three-dimensional Gaussian with a narrow width ranging from 140 to 170 MeV/c, approximately consistent with the sum of two mean-field nucleon momenta. Comparison with calculations appears to show that the SRC pairs are formed from mean-field nucleons in specific quantum states.

## Key claim

The center-of-mass (c.m.) momentum distribution of proton-proton short-range correlated pairs in carbon, aluminum, iron, and lead can be described by a three-dimensional Gaussian with a narrow width ranging from 140 to 170 MeV/c, approximately consistent with the sum of two mean-field nucleon momenta. The authors hedge that comparison with calculations "appears to show" that SRC pairs are formed from mean-field nucleons in specific quantum states, rather than making a definitive claim.

## Method

The authors measured the A(e,e'pp) reaction using a 5.01 GeV electron beam at Jefferson Lab's CLAS detector on carbon, aluminum, iron, and lead targets. Events were selected by requiring a leading proton with large missing momentum (300 to 600 MeV/c) and Bjorken scaling parameter x_B >= 1.2, plus a recoil proton with momentum >= 350 MeV/c, to isolate hard breakup of SRC pairs. The c.m. momentum distribution was extracted from the transverse components of p_cm = p_miss + p_recoil, corrected for detector acceptance using a GEANT3-based CLAS simulation with a 6-stage Monte Carlo procedure.

## Key numbers

- The one-dimensional Gaussian width of the c.m. momentum distribution ranges from 140 to 170 MeV/c across carbon, aluminum, iron, and lead.
- The measurement used a 5.01 GeV electron beam with Q^2 >= 1.4 GeV^2 and x_B >= 1.2.
- Missing momentum selection required 300 <= |p_miss| <= 600 MeV/c.
- Recoil proton selection required |p_recoil| >= 350 MeV/c.
- The leading proton momentum was required to satisfy 0.60 <= |p_p|/|q| <= 0.96 and be within 25 degrees of the momentum transfer direction.
- The reconstructed missing mass of the two-nucleon system was limited to m_miss <= 1.1 GeV/c^2.
- Acceptance corrections reduced the measured widths by 15-20 MeV/c for each nucleus.
- The systematic uncertainty from acceptance correction variations was 7%.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of SRC pair c.m. momentum distributions in helium-4 and carbon from Tang (2003), Shneor (2007), and Korover (2014) to heavier and asymmetric nuclei (aluminum, iron, lead) for the first time. The results are compared to theoretical calculations by Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1995) and Colle et al. (2013), as well as a Fermi-gas prediction by Moniz (1971). The factorization of SRC pair momentum distributions into relative and c.m. components, as described by Weiss (2016) and Ciofi degli Atti (2017), enables this extraction and suggests that the c.m. distribution reflects the mean-field parentage of SRC pairs.
