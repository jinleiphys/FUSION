# Search for octupole correlations in $^{147}$Nd

> Properties of excited states in $^{147}$Nd have been studied with multispectra and $γγ$ coincidence measurements. Twenty-four new $γ$-lines and three new levels have been introduced into the level scheme of $^{147}$Nd. Lifetimes of eight excited levels in $^{147}$Nd, populated in the $β$ decay of $^{147}$Pr, have been measured using the advanced time-delayed $βγγ$(t) method. Reduced transition probabilities have been determined for 30 $γ$-transitions in $^{147}$Nd. Potential energy surfaces on the ($β_{2}$,$β_{3}$) plane calculated for $^{147}$Nd using the Strutinsky method predict two single quasiparticle configurations with nonzero octupole deformation, with K=1/2 and K=5/2. We do not observe parity doublet bands with K=5/2. For pair of opposite parity bands that could form the K=1/2 parity doublet we were able only to determine lower limit of the dipole moment, $|D_0|\geq$0.02 e$\cdot fm$.

# Search for octupole correlations in $^{147}$Nd

## Key claim
The paper reports a search for octupole correlations in $^{147}$Nd and finds that, contrary to theoretical predictions of two parity doublet bands with K=1/2 and K=5/2, the K=5/2 parity doublet band is not observed and only a lower limit of the dipole moment ($|D_0|\geq$0.02 e$\cdot$fm) could be determined for the K=1/2 parity doublet candidate, suggesting octupole correlations may be weaker than expected in this odd-N isotope.

## Method
Excited states in $^{147}$Nd were populated via the $\beta$-decay of $^{147}$Pr, produced in thermal neutron-induced fission of $^{235}$U at the OSIRIS mass separator at Studsvik. Multispectra and $\beta$-gated $\gamma\gamma$ coincidence data were collected using Ge detectors, and lifetimes of eight excited levels were measured using the advanced time-delayed $\beta\gamma\gamma$(t) method with BaF$_2$ fast-timing detectors. Potential energy surfaces on the ($\beta_2$,$\beta_3$) plane were calculated using the Strutinsky macroscopic-microscopic method with a Woods-Saxon potential to predict octupole-deformed configurations.

## Key numbers
- Half-life of the 214.6 keV level: 4.59(22) ns (compared to 4.53(6) ns from prior work)
- Half-life of the 127.9 keV level: 390(23) ps (compared to 0.4(1) ns from prior work)
- Half-life of the 463.6 keV level: 26(4) ps (previously only a nanosecond upper limit was known)
- Reduced transition probability B(E2) for the 86.7 keV transition from the 214.6 keV level: 4.8(5)$\times$10$^3$ e$^2$fm$^4$
- Lower limit of the dipole moment for the K=1/2 parity doublet candidate: $|D_0|\geq$0.02 e$\cdot$fm
- Lower limit of $|D_0|$ for the 792.6 keV level (if K=1/2): $\geq$0.04 e$\cdot$fm
- Quadrupole moment $Q_0$ for the K$^\pi$=1/2$^{-}$ band: 284(16) e$\cdot$fm$^2$
- Equilibrium deformations from potential energy calculations: $\beta_{20}$ of 0.13-0.18 and $\beta_{30}\approx$0.10

## Context
This work extends prior studies of octupole correlations in the neodymium isotopic chain, following the observation of lowered 1$^{-}$ state energies and fast E1 transitions in even-even $^{146-150}$Nd that place these nuclei in the lanthanide octupole deformation region (Butler 1991, Butler 1996). The results contrast with the authors' earlier study of $^{149}$Nd (Ruchowska 2010), which also found very low $|D_0|$ values, and with theoretical calculations that predict smooth $|D_0|$ dependence versus neutron number. The non-observation of the K=5/2 parity doublet band and the low $|D_0|$ limits suggest that octupole correlations may be blocked at low excitation energies in odd-N lanthanide isotopes, similar to findings in $^{149}$Ce (Synnes 2003) and in odd-N barium isotopes $^{145}$Ba (Rząca-Urban 2012) and $^{147}$Ba (Rząca-Urban 2013). The results enable future searches for parity doublet bands at higher excitation energies in $^{147}$Nd and other odd-N isotopes to understand the behavior of dipole moments in this region.
