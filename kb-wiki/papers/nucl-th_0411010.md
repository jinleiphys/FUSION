# Kaon and Pion production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The BUU transport model is applied to study strangeness and pion production in nucleus-nucleus collisions. Starting from proton induced reactions, we further investigate Si+Au, Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions in the energy range between 2 and 40 A$\cdot$GeV and compare with data and with other transport calculations. The $q \bar q$ annihilation, or resonance, channel simulated by the string model in meson-nucleon collisions at $\sqrt{s} > 2$ GeV is introduced. The importance of this channel for a good description of the proton-nucleus data on $K^+$ production is demonstrated. We, furthermore, show that the meson-meson collisions contribute significantly to the $K \bar K$ production in heavy-ion collisions above 5 A GeV and improve an agreement with data on the $K^+/π^+$ ratio. Finally, we study the influence of the in-medium modifications of the FRITIOF model on the pion and kaon production.

## Key claim

The BUU transport model, when including a phenomenological quark-antiquark annihilation channel in meson-nucleon collisions and meson-meson strangeness production channels, can reproduce the measured K+/π+ ratio in heavy-ion collisions across the energy range 2-40 A·GeV, addressing discrepancies found in other transport models such as HSD and UrQMD.

## Method

The BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model was applied to simulate nucleus-nucleus collisions, treating baryon-baryon collisions via a resonance scenario below √s = 2.6 GeV and a FRITIOF string model above that threshold. For meson-baryon collisions, a resonance model was used below √s = 2 GeV and FRITIOF above, with a newly implemented quark-antiquark annihilation channel where a quark and antiquark of the same flavor from the interacting hadrons are annihilated and the remaining energy is put into a single string decay. Meson-meson collisions for strangeness production (ππ, πρ, ρρ → K K̅) were included with cross sections based on calculations from the literature, and the back reactions were incorporated via detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- The K+/π+ ratio at midrapidity shows a maximum around 30 A·GeV, consistent with data from the NA49 collaboration.
- For p+Be at 14.6 GeV/c, the pion yield is underestimated by approximately 20%, while kaon and antikaon yields are well described.
- The calculated kaon inverse slope parameter for p+Be overestimates data by about 20% at midrapidity.
- For Si+Au at 14.6 A·GeV/c, BUU underpredicts the inverse slope parameter for K+ by about 25% and for π+ by 15%.
- The K+ fiducial yield for Au+Au at 10.7 A·GeV agrees well with data except for a slight underprediction at peripheral collisions.
- The probability for the quark-antiquark annihilation channel is parameterized as Prob(annihilation) = max(0.85 - 0.17·√s/GeV, 0) for the energy-dependent strangeness suppression factor.

## Context

This work extends the BUU model studies of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier transport calculations with RQMD (Mattiello et al., 1989), HSD (Cassing et al., 1998), and UrQMD (Bratkovskaya et al., 2004) that failed to reproduce the peak in the K+/π+ ratio at the correct energy. The paper demonstrates that including meson-meson collisions and the quark-antiquark annihilation channel in meson-baryon collisions is crucial for describing kaon production, and it enables future comparisons with the planned CBM experiment at GSI. The results also show that the BUU model overpredicts pion production in central collisions, indicating a need for further investigation of pion production and reabsorption mechanisms.
