# Response of superfluid fermions at finite temperature

> A consistent finite-temperature microscopic theory for the response of strongly coupled superfluid fermionic systems is formulated. We start from the general many-body Hamiltonian with the vacuum (bare) two-fermion interaction and derive the equation of motion (EOM) for the thermally averaged two-time two-fermion correlation function, which determines the spectrum of the system under study. The superfluidity is introduced via the Bogoliubov transformation of the fermionic field operators, and the entire formalism is carried out in the basis of Bogoliubov's quasiparticles, keeping the complete 4x4 block matrix structure of the two-fermion EOM. Fully correlated static and dynamical interaction kernels of the resulting EOM are discussed. A special focus is then placed on the latter kernel, which is advanced to a factorized form, enabling a minimal truncation of the many-body problem while keeping important effects of emergent collectivity and mapping to the quasiparticle-vibration coupling (qPVC). As in the zero-temperature and non-superfluid cases, the qPVC can be associated with a new order parameter, qPVC vertex. In the thermal superfluid theory, the latter vertex, as well as the components of the dynamical kernel, acquire an extended form including thermally unblocked transition amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a consistent finite-temperature microscopic response theory for strongly coupled superfluid fermionic systems, extending the zero-temperature formalism by including thermally unblocked transition amplitudes in the quasiparticle-vibration coupling (qPVC) vertex and the dynamical kernel components, but the theory is derived under the assumption of an instantaneous (non-retarded) bare two-fermion interaction.

## Method

The authors start from the general many-body Hamiltonian with a bare two-fermion interaction and derive the equation of motion (EOM) for the thermally averaged two-time two-fermion correlation function in the basis of Bogoliubov quasiparticles, keeping the complete 4x4 block matrix structure. The interaction kernel of the resulting EOM is split into static and dynamical components, with the dynamical kernel advanced to a factorized form that enables mapping to quasiparticle-vibration coupling (qPVC). The finite-temperature formalism is carried out using the Matsubara imaginary-time technique, and the static kernel is benchmarked against the finite-temperature quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) of Sommermann (1983) and Ring, Robledo, Egido et al. (1984).

## Key numbers

- The strength function at finite temperature includes an additional factor \([1 - \exp(-E/T)]^{-1}\) compared to the zero-temperature case, which is singular at \(E = 0\).
- The norm matrix \(\hat{N}_{\mu\mu'\nu\nu'}\) is defined as \(\langle[\hat{\Psi}_{\mu\mu'},\hat{\Psi}^{\dagger}_{\nu\nu'}]\rangle\), with the inverse satisfying \(\frac{1}{2}\sum_{\delta\delta'}\hat{N}^{-1}_{\mu\mu'\delta\delta'}\hat{N}_{\delta\delta'\nu\nu'} = \delta_{\mu\nu}\delta_{\mu'\nu'} - \delta_{\mu\nu'}\delta_{\mu'\nu}\).
- The quasiparticle energies \(E_\mu\) appear in the diagonal matrix \(\hat{E}_{\mu\mu'}\) with entries \((E_\mu + E_{\mu'})\) for the first two components and \((E_\mu - E_{\mu'})\) for the last two components.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature superfluid response theory of Litvinova (2022) to finite temperatures, unifying the particle-hole and particle-particle channels in a single EOM with a 4x4 block matrix structure. It also builds on separate finite-temperature treatments of the ph and pp channels beyond QRPA by Litvinova and Wibowo (2018), Wibowo and Litvinova (2019), Litvinova, Robin, and Wibowo (2020), and Litvinova (2021). The formalism enables future numerical implementations for computing nuclear strength functions (e.g., electric dipole, Gamow-Teller) at finite temperatures relevant for astrophysical modeling of r-process nucleosynthesis and core-collapse supernovae.
