# Chiral potentials, perturbation theory, and the 1S0 channel of NN scattering

> We use nucleon-nucleon phase shifts obtained from experimental data, together with the chiral expansion for the long-distance part of the NN interaction, to obtain information about the short-distance piece of the NN potential that is at work in the 1S0 channel. We find that if the scale R that defines the separation between "long-" and "short-" distance is chosen to be \lsim 1.8 fm then the energy dependence produced by short-distance dynamics is well approximated by a two-term polynomial for Tlab < 200 MeV. We also find that a quantitative description of NN dynamics is possible, at least in this channel, if one treats the long-distance parts of the chiral NN potential in perturbation theory. However, in order to achieve this we have to choose a separation scale R that is larger than 1.0 fm.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a quantitative description of nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering in the 1S0 channel is possible using chiral perturbation theory if the long-distance parts of the chiral NN potential are treated perturbatively, but only when the separation scale R between long- and short-distance physics is chosen larger than 1.0 fm and less than or about 1.8 fm.

## Method

The authors used Nijmegen PWA93 phase shifts as input and integrated the radial Schrodinger equation inward from infinity to a finite radius R, using chiral potentials at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) for the long-distance region r greater than R. They extracted the energy-dependent logarithmic derivative gamma(k;R) at the matching radius R, then tested whether this quantity could be reproduced by treating the long-range chiral potential in perturbation theory using a Green's function approach.

## Key numbers

- The separation scale R must satisfy 1.0 fm less than R less than 1.8 fm for effective separation of long- and short-distance physics; at R = 2.2 fm a singularity appears in gamma(k;R) at T_lab approximately 140 MeV
- For R = 1.0 fm with the NNLO potential, the coefficient A_0 = 3.101 fm^{-1} and A_1 = -0.648 fm; for R = 1.4 fm with the same potential, A_0 = 0.496 fm^{-1} and A_1 = -0.518 fm
- The two-term polynomial approximation for the energy dependence of short-distance dynamics works for T_lab less than or equal to 200 MeV when R is less than or about 1.8 fm
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant was set to g_{pi N} = 13.1, the pion mass to m_pi = 134.98 MeV, and the nucleon mass to M = 938.918 MeV

## Context

This work extends the "outside-in" approach of Refs. (Scald, 2000; Manuel, 2003; Manuel, 2004) by including two-pion exchange contributions at NLO and NNLO, whereas those earlier works used only the leading-order chiral potential. It also contrasts with the KSW expansion (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998) which treats one-pion exchange as sub-leading and fails to converge in the 3S1 channel, and with the Weinberg approach (Weinberg, 1990) which solves the Schrodinger equation with the full chiral potential. The results enable future work to construct short-distance models for r less than R that reproduce the extracted energy-dependent boundary conditions, and to test whether perturbation theory for long-range pion exchanges converges in other partial waves.
