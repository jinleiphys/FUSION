# Hartree-Fock Approximation for Inverse Many-Body Problems

> A new method is presented to reconstruct the potential of a quantum mechanical many-body system from observational data, combining a nonparametric Bayesian approach with a Hartree-Fock approximation. A priori information is implemented as a stochastic process, defined on the space of potentials. The method is computationally feasible and provides a general framework to treat inverse problems for quantum mechanical many-body systems.

# Hartree-Fock Approximation for Inverse Many-Body Problems

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to reconstruct a quantum mechanical many-body potential from observational data by combining a nonparametric Bayesian approach with a Hartree-Fock approximation, but the reconstruction is only reliable in regions where empirical data are available; at small and large inter-particle distances the solution remains dominated by the prior reference potential due to lack of data.

## Method

The authors formulate the inverse problem of reconstructing a local two-body potential from coordinate measurements of a many-fermion system in its ground state. They use a Gaussian smoothness prior over potentials and apply a maximum a posteriori approximation, which reduces the problem to solving a stationarity equation for the potential. To make the many-body calculation tractable, they replace the exact many-body ground state with a Hartree-Fock Slater determinant, leading to an iterative scheme that alternates between solving the Hartree-Fock equations for single-particle orbitals, computing functional derivatives of the likelihood via a two-body-like equation, and updating the potential estimate.

## Key numbers

- System of two particles on a one-dimensional grid with 21 points, using Hamiltonian H = -(1/2m)Δ + V₁ + V with m = 10⁻³ and one-body potential V₁(z,z') = δ(z-z') a z² with a = 10⁻⁵
- True potential v_true(x) = b/(1+e^{-2γ(x-L/2)/L}) with b = 100, γ = 10, L = 21
- 100 data points sampled from the true probability density
- Gaussian smoothness prior with inverse covariance K₀ = λ(I-Δ)/2 and λ = 10⁻³/2
- Reference potential v₀(x) of same functional form as v_true but with γ = 1 (nearly linear in interval [1,20])

## Context

This work extends Bayesian nonparametric methods, previously applied to classification and regression problems (Neal 1996, Williams and Rasmussen 1996) and to quantum statistics (Lemm 1999), to inverse many-body problems in quantum mechanics. It contrasts with traditional inverse scattering theory and inverse spectral theory for one-body systems (Newton 1989, Chadan et al. 1997) by addressing the computationally challenging many-body case. The method enables reconstruction of inter-particle forces from finite observational data, with the Hartree-Fock approximation making the problem numerically feasible while the Bayesian framework provides principled handling of ill-posedness through prior information.
