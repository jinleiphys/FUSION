# Antikaon Production in Proton-Nucleus Reactions and the $K^-$ properties in nuclear matter

> We calculate the momentum-dependent potentials for $K^+$ and $K^-$ mesons in a dispersion approach at nuclear density $ρ_0$ using the information from the vacuum $K^+ N$ and $K^- N$ scattering amplitudes, however, leaving out the resonance contributions for the in-medium analysis. Whereas the $K^+$ potential is found to be repulsive ($\approx$ + 30 MeV) and to show only a moderate momentum dependence, the $K^-$ selfenergy at normal nuclear matter density turns out to be $\approx$ - 200 MeV at zero momentum in line with kaon atomic data, however, decreases rapidly in magnitude for higher momenta. The antikaon production in p + A reactions is calculated within a coupled transport approach and compared to the data at KEK including different assumptions for the antikaon potentials. Furthermore, detailed predictions are made for $p + ^{12}C$ and $p + ^{207}Pb$ reactions at 2.5 GeV in order to determine the momentum dependent antikaon potential experimentally.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the in-medium $K^-$ potential at normal nuclear density is strongly attractive (≈ -200 MeV at zero momentum) but decreases rapidly in magnitude for higher momenta, which could reconcile the conflicting results from kaonic atoms (which probe stopped antikaons) and heavy-ion collisions (which probe antikaons with momenta of 300-800 MeV/c). The key hedge is that the resonance contributions (particularly the Λ(1405)) are left out of the in-medium analysis, as they are assumed to dissolve in nuclear matter due to Pauli blocking.

## Method

The authors compute momentum-dependent $K^+$ and $K^-$ potentials in nuclear matter using a dispersion relation approach based on vacuum $K^\pm N$ forward scattering amplitudes, excluding the low-energy resonance contributions (Σ(1385) and Λ(1405)) for the in-medium case. They then implement these potentials in a coupled-channel transport approach (HSD) to calculate antikaon production in proton-nucleus reactions, comparing to KEK data at 3.5 and 4 GeV beam energy and making predictions for COSY at 2.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- $K^+$ potential at nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is repulsive at ≈ +30 MeV, with only moderate momentum dependence
- $K^-$ selfenergy at ρ₀ and zero momentum is ≈ -200 MeV, in line with kaonic atom data from Friedman, Gal and Batty
- $K^-$ mass scaling parameter α_K ≈ 0.2-0.22, giving m*_K⁻ = m_K (1 - α_K ρ_B/ρ₀)
- Parameterization for $K^-$ potential: U⁻(ρ_B, p_K) = -ρ_B {341 + 823 exp(-2.5 p_K)} MeV, with ρ_B in fm⁻³ and p_K in GeV/c
- Parameterization for $K^+$ potential: U⁺(ρ_B, p_K) = -167 ρ_B MeV
- At high momenta, the $K^-$ potential saturates at ≈ -60 MeV
- $K^-$ absorption gives roughly a factor of 2 reduction in cross section for ¹²C and a factor ≈ 5 for ²⁰⁷Pb at 2.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-relation approach to kaon-nucleon scattering to obtain momentum-dependent in-medium potentials, addressing the discrepancy between kaonic atom data (Friedman, Gal and Batty, 1994) indicating a 200 ± 20 MeV mass drop and heavy-ion collision studies (Li, Cassing, 1994-1997) requiring only 100-120 MeV reduction. The authors aim to resolve this by showing the potential is strongly momentum-dependent. The calculations enable experimental tests at COSY (proposed by Müller) for p + ¹²C and p + ²⁰⁷Pb reactions at 2.5 GeV, where the shape of the $K^-$ spectrum and absolute cross section should determine the momentum-dependent antikaon potential. The work also compares with the quark-meson coupling model of Tsushima et al., which predicts a $K^-$ mass decrease of ≈ 162 MeV at ρ₀ and zero momentum.
