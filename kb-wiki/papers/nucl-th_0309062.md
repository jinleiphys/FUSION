# Kaonic nuclei studied based on a new framework of Antisymmetric Molecular Dynamics

> We have developed a new framework of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD), to adequately treat the I=0 KN interaction, which is essential to study kaonic nuclei. The improved points are 1) pK$^-$/nK$^0$ mixing and 2) total spin and isospin projections. These improvements enable us to investigate various kaonic nuclei (ppnK$^-$, pppK$^-$, pppnK$^-$, $^6$BeK$^-$ and $^9$BK$^-$) systematically. We have found that they are deeply bound and extremely dense with a variety of shapes.

## Key claim

The authors claim that kaonic nuclei (systems where a K⁻ meson is bound to a nucleus) are deeply bound and extremely dense with a variety of shapes, but this conclusion depends on the use of a new Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) framework that includes pK⁻/nK̅⁰ mixing and total spin and isospin projections to properly treat the strongly attractive I=0 K̅N interaction.

## Method

The authors extended the AMD framework by introducing charge-mixed single-particle wave functions for nucleons and the K̅ meson, allowing pK⁻/nK̅⁰ mixing, and by performing simultaneous angular momentum (J) and isospin (T) projections on the intrinsic state. They employed a g-matrix method with the Tamagaki potential (OPEG) as the bare NN interaction and the Akaishi-Yamazaki K̅N potential as the bare K̅N interaction, iterating until consistency between the g-matrix and AMD results was achieved. The wave function parameters were determined by energy variation using the frictional cooling method with a constraint to fix the center of mass.

## Key numbers

* ppnK⁻: total binding energy 110.3 MeV, central density 1.50 fm⁻³, root-mean-square radius of nucleon system 0.72 fm, decay width 21.2 MeV
* pppK⁻: total binding energy 96.7 MeV, central density 1.56 fm⁻³, root-mean-square radius of nucleon system 0.81 fm, decay width 12.5 MeV
* pppnK⁻: total binding energy 105.0 MeV, central density 1.29 fm⁻³, root-mean-square radius of nucleon system 0.97 fm, decay width 25.9 MeV
* ⁶BeK⁻: total binding energy 104.2 MeV, central density 0.91 fm⁻³, root-mean-square radius of nucleon system 1.17 fm, decay width 33.3 MeV
* ⁹BK⁻: total binding energy 118.5 MeV, central density 0.71 fm⁻³, root-mean-square radius of nucleon system 1.45 fm, decay width 33.0 MeV
* For ppnK⁻, the expectation value of the pK⁻ and nK̅⁰ interaction was -255 MeV, and the pK⁻/nK̅⁰ mixing interaction was -88 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD study of kaonic nuclei (Dote et al., 2003) and the earlier predictions of deeply bound K⁻ states in light nuclei by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002). The new framework overcomes the limitation of the simple AMD version that could not treat the I=0 K̅N interaction precisely due to the lack of K̅⁰ degrees of freedom. The results enable systematic investigation of kaonic nuclei with various shapes and densities, and the method can be applied to study more complex kaonic systems beyond the five cases examined here.
