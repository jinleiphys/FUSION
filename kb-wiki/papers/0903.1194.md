# Proton-proton scattering without Coulomb force renormalization

> We demonstrate numerically that proton-proton (pp) scattering observables can be determined directly by standard short range methods using a screened pp Coulomb force without renormalization. In examples the appropriate screening radii are given. We also numerically investigate solutions of the 3-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger (LS) equation for a screened Coulomb potential alone in the limit of large screening radii and confirm analytically predicted properties for off-shell, half-shell and on-shell Coulomb t-matrices. These 3-dimensional solutions will form a basis for a novel approach to include the pp Coulomb interaction into the 3N Faddeev framework.

# Proton-proton scattering without Coulomb force renormalization

## Key claim

Proton-proton scattering observables (cross sections and spin observables) can be computed using a screened Coulomb potential at finite screening radii without any renormalization procedure, because the infinitely oscillating phase factor that appears in the screening limit cancels out when the on-shell t-matrix is multiplied by its complex conjugate to form observables. The authors demonstrate this numerically for specific screening radii and screening forms, showing that the observables become independent of the screening radius at sufficiently large values.

## Method

The authors solved the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the sum of the strong nuclear force and a screened Coulomb potential of the form V_c^R(r) = (e^2/r) exp[-(r/R)^n] with n = 1, 2, 3, and 4, projected onto partial wave basis states. They computed the full three-dimensional antisymmetrized on-shell t-matrix by combining a finite partial wave sum (where the strong force is active) with the complete three-dimensional screened Coulomb t-matrix, and then evaluated pp observables. Separately, they solved the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the screened Coulomb potential alone at three energies (E = 3 MeV, 13 MeV, and 50 MeV) to study the off-shell, half-shell, and on-shell limits of the screened Coulomb t-matrix as a function of screening radius R.

## Key numbers

- At E_p^lab = 13 MeV, the deviation between screened-Coulomb results (R = 120 fm, n = 1, 2, 3, 4) and exact Vincent-Phatak results is under approximately 1%.
- The limiting screening radius at E_p^lab = 13 MeV is R = 120 fm; at E_p^lab = 50 MeV the limiting radius is R = 60 fm.
- The on-shell momentum at E_p^lab = 13 MeV is k = 0.396 fm^{-1}.
- At forward angles (x close to 1), R = 180 fm is required for the renormalized on-shell screened Coulomb t-matrix to agree with the pure Coulomb on-shell amplitude at E_p^lab = 13 MeV.
- For negative energy E_p^lab = -13 MeV, the off-shell screened Coulomb t-matrix reaches the pure Coulomb limit already around R = 20 fm.

## Context

This work extends the analytical predictions of Chen (1972), Taylor (1974), and Alt (1978) regarding the screening limit of the Coulomb t-matrix by providing a direct three-dimensional numerical verification. It offers an alternative to the Vincent-Phatak method (1974) for treating the pp Coulomb force, avoiding the need for renormalization when computing observables. The resulting three-dimensional screened Coulomb t-matrix is intended for use in a forthcoming Faddeev treatment of proton-deuteron reactions (Witala, 2008), where the full three-dimensional structure is required because partial-wave truncated pp t-matrices are insufficient.
