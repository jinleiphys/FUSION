# Holographic Glueball Decay

> We announce new results on glueball decay rates in the Sakai-Sugimoto model, a realization of holographic QCD from first principles that has only one coupling constant and an overall mass scale as free parameters. We extend a previous investigation by Hashimoto, Tan, and Terashima who have considered the lowest scalar glueball which arises from a somewhat exotic polarization of supergravity modes and whose mass is uncomfortably small in comparison with lattice results. On the other hand, the scalar glueball dual to the dilaton turns out to have a mass of about twice the mass of the rho meson (1487 MeV), very close to the scalar meson $f_0(1500)$ that is frequently interpreted as predominantly glue. Calculating the decay rate into two pions we find a surprisingly good agreement with experimental data for the $f_0(1500)$. We have also obtained decay widths for tensor and excited scalar glueballs, indicating universal narrowness.

# Holographic Glueball Decay

## Key claim

The scalar glueball dual to the dilaton in the Sakai-Sugimoto model has a mass of 1487 MeV and a decay rate into two pions that matches the experimental data for the $f_0(1500)$ meson, which is frequently interpreted as predominantly glue. However, this nearly perfect agreement is somewhat fortuitous, as alternative parameter choices yield a slightly higher width that is still sufficiently close to be considered very interesting.

## Method

The authors used the Sakai-Sugimoto model, a top-down holographic QCD construction with flavor D8-branes, to calculate glueball decay rates. They combined dilaton fluctuations about the nontrivial dilaton background with the Dirac-Born-Infeld action for pions and mesons, integrated out the holographic coordinate, and derived an effective four-dimensional interaction Lagrangian to compute the decay width of the dilatonic scalar glueball into two pions.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the dilatonic scalar glueball: 1487 MeV, approximately twice the mass of the rho meson (which is 949 MeV from the model's mass scale $M_{\rm KK}$)
- Coupling constant $\lambda \equiv g_{\mathrm{YM}}^2 N_c \simeq 16.63$ and Kaluza-Klein mass $M_{\rm KK} \simeq 949$ MeV, fixed by matching the rho meson mass and pion decay constant $f_\pi$ to experimental values
- Decay rate of the dilatonic glueball into two pions: $\Gamma_{G\rightarrow\pi\pi}/M = 0.027$ (with the primary parameter choice)
- Experimental decay rate of $f_0(1500)$ into two pions: $\Gamma_{f_0(1500)\rightarrow\pi\pi}/M_{f_0(1500)} = 0.025(3)$
- Alternative parameter choice (matching rho meson width instead) gives $\Gamma_{G\rightarrow\pi\pi}/M = 0.034$
- Rho meson decay width from the model: $\Gamma_{\rho\rightarrow\pi\pi}/m_\rho = 0.1535$, compared to experimental value $\approx 0.19$
- Lowest scalar glueball mode from the exotic polarization: 855 MeV, only 10% heavier than the rho meson

## Context

This work extends a previous investigation by Hashimoto, Tan, and Terashima (2007), who considered the lowest scalar glueball from an exotic polarization of supergravity modes, which had an uncomfortably small mass (855 MeV) compared to lattice results. The authors instead study the dilatonic scalar glueball, which in bottom-up AdS/QCD models usually corresponds to the lowest-lying glueball (BoschiFilho 2002, Colangelo 2007, Forkel 2007). The results enable deeper investigations of glueball properties in the almost parameter-free Sakai-Sugimoto model, including systematic studies of all lowest-lying glueballs and decays into four pions, with the finding that tensor and excited scalar glueballs also show narrow widths.
