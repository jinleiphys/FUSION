# Detailed Analysis of the Chiral Unitary Model for Meson-Baryon Scattering with Flavor SU(3) Breaking Effects

> We study s-wave meson-baryon scattering using the chiral unitary model. We consider $1/2^{-}$ baryon resonances as quasibound states of the low lying mesons ($π,K,η$) and baryons ($N,Λ,Σ,Ξ$). In previous works, the subtraction constants which appear in loop integrals were found to largely depend on the channels, and it was necessary to fit these constants to reproduce the data. In order to extend this model to all channels with fewer parameters, we introduce flavor SU(3) breaking interactions in the framework of chiral perturbation theory. It is found, however, that the observed SU(3) breaking in meson-baryon scattering cannot be explained by the present SU(3) breaking interactions. The role and importance of the subtraction constants in the present framework are discussed.

## Key claim

The observed SU(3) breaking in meson-baryon scattering cannot be explained by the SU(3) breaking interactions introduced in the chiral Lagrangian, and the channel-dependent subtraction constants remain essential for reproducing data, particularly in the S=0 channel where a single common subtraction constant fails to simultaneously describe the N(1535) resonance and low-energy S11 amplitude.

## Method

The authors used the N/D method to construct a coupled-channel T-matrix for s-wave meson-baryon scattering, starting from the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction derived from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian. They first tested a common subtraction constant for all channels in both S=-1 and S=0 systems, then introduced flavor SU(3) breaking terms from the chiral Lagrangian at order O(m_q) to see if these could absorb the channel dependence of the subtraction constants.

## Key numbers

- Common subtraction constant a = -1.96 reproduces K^-p threshold branching ratios (γ = 1.80, R_c = 0.624, R_n = 0.225) compared to experimental values (γ = 2.36 ± 0.04, R_c = 0.664 ± 0.011, R_n = 0.189 ± 0.015)
- With a = -2.6 for all channels, threshold branching ratios become γ = 2.41, R_c = 0.596, R_n = 0.759, showing poor agreement with experiment
- For S=0, optimal common subtraction constant a = 0.53 reproduces low-energy S11 amplitude up to √s = 1400 MeV but fails to generate the N(1535) resonance
- Channel-dependent subtraction constants for S=-1 range from a_i = -1.84 (K̅N) to a_i = -2.67 (KΞ) at μ = 630 MeV
- Channel-dependent subtraction constants for S=0 range from a_i = 0.711 (πN) to a_i = -4.09 (KΣ) at μ = 630 MeV
- With SU(3) breaking interactions and a = -1.59, threshold branching ratios become γ = 2.19, R_c = 0.623, R_n = 0.179

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary model studies of Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), and Inoue (2001) by systematically testing whether channel-dependent subtraction constants can be replaced by a single common constant plus SU(3) breaking interactions. The authors show that while the S=-1 channel (K̅N scattering) works reasonably well with a common subtraction constant a ~ -2, the S=0 channel (πN scattering) requires channel-dependent subtraction constants to simultaneously reproduce the N(1535) resonance and low-energy data. This result contradicts the expectation of Garcia-Recio (2003) that channel-independent renormalization could work for both channels, and it motivates future work to include higher-order chiral counterterms or crossing symmetry to resolve the channel dependence.
