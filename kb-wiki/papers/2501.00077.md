# Erratum: Exact solutions to the fermion propagator Schwinger-Dyson equation in Minkowski space with on-shell renormalization for quenched QED \newline [Phys. Rev. D 96, 036021 (2017)]

> With the introduction of a spectral representation, the Schwinger--Dyson equation (SDE) for the fermion propagator is formulated in Minkowski space in QED. After imposing the on-shell renormalization conditions, numeric solutions for the fermion propagator spectral functions are obtained in four dimensions with a renormalizable version of the Gauge Technique Ansatz for the fermion-photon vertex in the quenched approximation in the Yennie gauge. Despite the limitations of this model, having an explicit solution provides a guiding example of the fermion propagator with the correct analytic structure.

# Erratum: Exact solutions to the fermion propagator Schwinger-Dyson equation in Minkowski space with on-shell renormalization for quenched QED

## Key claim

The on-shell renormalization of the fermion propagator Schwinger-Dyson equation using the Gauge Technique Ansatz with a loop-renormalizable modification is only consistent in the Yennie gauge (ξ=3), not in the Landau gauge as previously claimed in Jia (2017). The earlier work contained an algebraic mistake in the inhomogeneous term of the integral equations, and the corrected analysis shows that away from the Yennie gauge there is no consistent solution satisfying the on-shell renormalization conditions.

## Method

The authors formulate the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the fermion propagator in Minkowski space using a spectral representation, imposing on-shell renormalization conditions with an adjustable free-particle pole residue r0. They employ a renormalizable modification of the Gauge Technique Ansatz for the fermion-photon vertex in the quenched approximation. The integral equations for the spectral functions are converted into differential equations, solved analytically as series expansions and numerically for the Yennie gauge. The Euclidean space fermion propagator in the Landau gauge is then obtained from the Yennie gauge spectral functions using the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transform (LKFT).

## Key numbers

- The condition for consistent on-shell renormalization requires a = b, which occurs only in the Yennie gauge where ξ = 3.
- The parameters a and b are defined as a = (3α/4π) × r0/(1 - α/π) and b = (αξ/4π) × r0/(1 - α/π).
- For the numerical solution at α = 1 and r0 = 1, the asymptotic power-law exponent γ1 = (3 - √(1+4a))/2 = 0.7253, giving asymptotic behavior r2(s) ~ s^(-0.7253).
- The proportionality constant c0^(1) = 0.11507 for the asymptotic behavior of r2(s) at α = 1.
- The Padé approximation fitting parameters for r1(s) include c0 = 0.5017, c1 = -0.7253, c2 = 1.022; for r2(s): c0 = 0.01969, c1 = -0.7253, c2 = 1.993.
- The on-shell renormalization is only possible for 0 < α < π; at α = π there is no consistent solution even in the Yennie gauge.

## Context

This erratum corrects the earlier work by Jia (2017) which incorrectly claimed on-shell renormalization was possible in the Landau gauge. The Gauge Technique vertex was originally developed by Salam, Strathdee, and Delbourgo (1970s). The authors note that Eides (2001) had previously indicated that only the Yennie gauge was consistent for on-shell renormalization. The corrected solutions provide an explicit example of a fermion propagator with correct analytic structure in Minkowski space, and the LKFT enables transformation to other gauges despite the model's lack of gauge covariance. This enables future studies of analytic properties of propagators in non-perturbative QED.
