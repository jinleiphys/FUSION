# Electromagnetic Scattering in Degenerate and Partially Degenerate Nuclear Matter

> I calculate the rate of electromagnetic scattering in degenerate and partially degenerate plasmas composed of electrons, muons, protons, and neutrons. Correlations with strong interactions, induced by the polarizability of electromagnetically and strongly charged protons, are taken into account. At subnuclear densities induced interactions are shown to cause a drastic increase of the longitudinal scattering rate. The results obtained are particularly relevant for the electron contribution to transport in the outer core of neutron stars, and represent a first step towards a realistic calculation of transport phenomena in neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

At subnuclear densities (around 0.5–0.6 times nuclear saturation density), medium-induced interactions from the polarizability of strongly and electromagnetically charged protons cause a drastic increase in the longitudinal electromagnetic scattering rate, particularly where homogeneous nuclear matter is projected to become unstable.

## Method

The author calculates the electromagnetic scattering rate (Møller scattering) for electrons, muons, protons, and neutrons in degenerate and partially degenerate nuclear matter using the optical theorem, relating the rate to the imaginary part of the on-shell fermion self energy at two-loop order. The photon propagator is dressed via the relativistic random phase approximation (RPA), resumming one-loop polarization functions from all plasma constituents, and strong-interaction correlations are included through the polarizability of protons, which couples electromagnetic and strong screening.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 2.3 \times 10^{17} \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- Typical proton fractions in neutron star cores: below 10%
- Temperatures in neutron star mergers can reach up to 30 MeV
- Old neutron stars cool to temperatures well below 1 MeV
- Densities in neutron star cores range from \(0.5\,n_0\) to several times \(n_0\)
- The fine structure constant used: \(\alpha_f = 1/137\)
- Induced interactions are most pronounced at densities \(n \sim (0.5-0.6)\, n_0\)

## Context

This work extends the RPA-based calculation of electromagnetic scattering rates in multi-component plasmas (electrons, muons, protons) previously applied to neutron star cores by Shternin (2008, 2018) and reviewed by Schmitt (2017), by incorporating medium-induced lepton-neutron scattering via strong-interaction correlations as introduced by Bertoni (2014). The results are particularly relevant for viscous damping of r-modes in the crust-core transition region of neutron stars, where induced interactions are strongest, and represent a first step toward realistic transport calculations for neutron star merger simulations.
