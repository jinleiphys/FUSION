# Non-Perturbative Dilepton Production from a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The dilepton production rate from the quark-gluon plasma is calculated from the imaginary part of the photon self energy using a quark propagator that contains the gluon condensate. The low mass dilepton rate obtained in this way exhibits interesting structures (peaks and gaps), which might be observable at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The dilepton production rate from a quark-gluon plasma, calculated using a non-perturbative quark propagator that includes the gluon condensate, exhibits peaks and gaps at low invariant mass that might be observable at RHIC and LHC, but the absolute rate is suppressed by a factor of 5 to 10 compared to the hard thermal loop (HTL) result and does not reduce to the Born rate at large invariant mass because the residue of the quark pole approaches 1/4 instead of 1.

## Method

The authors computed the dilepton production rate from the imaginary part of the photon self energy at one-loop order, replacing the bare quark propagators with effective propagators that contain the in-medium chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates taken from lattice QCD. The spectral functions were constructed from the poles of these effective propagators, corresponding to two collective quark modes (the ordinary quark mode q+ and the plasmino mode q-), and the rate was evaluated numerically for virtual photon momentum p=0 and non-zero p at temperatures T=1.1 Tc, 2 Tc, and 4 Tc.

## Key numbers

- The effective quark mass from the gluon condensate is m_eff approximately 1.15 T for temperatures between T=1.1 Tc and 4 Tc.
- At p=0 and T=1.1 Tc, the q+ to q- decay channel opens at M=0 and terminates at M=1.01 Tc, followed by a gap from M=1.01 Tc to 2.07 Tc.
- At p=0 and T=2 Tc, the gap ranges from M=1.83 Tc to 3.73 Tc; at T=4 Tc, the gap ranges from M=2.14 Tc to 8.76 Tc.
- At p=0 and T=1.1 Tc, the q- q- annihilation process starts at M=2.07 Tc (twice the minimum of the plasmino branch), and the q+ q+ annihilation starts at M greater than or equal to 2 m_eff.
- The non-perturbative rate is smaller by a factor of 5 to 10 than the pole-pole contribution of the HTL calculation for realistic coupling g=2.5.
- At p=1 Tc and T=1.1 Tc, the decay processes open at E=1 Tc and continue to E=2.0 Tc; the q- q- annihilation turns on at E=2.1 Tc; the q+ q- annihilation opens at E=2.4 Tc; the q+ q+ annihilation opens at E=3.1 Tc.
- At p=2 Tc and T=4 Tc, the gap becomes narrower than at p=1 Tc; at T=1.1 Tc and 2 Tc the gap disappears entirely.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculations of Braaten, Pisarski, and Yuan (1990) and Wong (1992), which showed van Hove singularities and gaps in the low-mass dilepton rate from HTL-resummed quark propagators, by incorporating non-perturbative effects from the gluon condensate measured on the lattice by Boyd et al. (1996). The results enable a non-perturbative prediction for dilepton signatures of deconfined quarks at RHIC and LHC, though the authors note that the neglect of an effective quark-photon vertex (required by the Ward identity) and higher-order damping effects may modify the absolute rate and broaden the peaks. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD, which cannot yet compute dynamical quantities such as the dilepton production rate.
