# The perturbative QCD factorization approach in high energy nuclear collisions

> I discuss the systematic modifications to the perturbative QCD factorization approach in high energy l+A, p+A and A+A reactions. These include transverse momentum diffusion manifest in the Cronin effect and a small increase in the dijet acoplanarity; nuclear size enhanced power corrections that lead to shadowing in deeply inelastic scattering and suppression of single and double inclusive hadron production at forward rapidity at RHIC but disappear as a function of the transverse momentum; inelastic attenuation of the jet cross sections or jet quenching that persists to much higher pT.

# The perturbative QCD factorization approach in high energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The perturbative QCD factorization approach can be systematically extended to incorporate nuclear medium effects, including transverse momentum diffusion, nuclear-enhanced power corrections, and inelastic energy loss, provided that the corrections are organized as a twist expansion where nuclear-size enhancements and medium density can make higher-twist terms numerically important even when the hard scale Q^2 is large.

## Method

The author reviews the factorization theorem for hadronic cross sections in terms of parton distribution functions, hard-scattering parton-level cross sections, and fragmentation functions, then introduces three classes of nuclear modifications. Transverse momentum diffusion is modeled via multiple elastic scatterings characterized by a transport coefficient μ^2/λ, leading to Gaussian broadening of the parton transverse momentum distribution. Nuclear-enhanced power corrections (higher-twist terms ∼1/Q^n) are resummed by rescaling the Bjorken-x variable as x → x(1 + m_dyn^2/Q^2) with dynamical mass m_dyn^2 = ξ^2(A^{1/3}−1). Jet quenching from inelastic parton energy loss is computed using the GLV reaction-operator approach to first order in opacity, with the fractional energy loss distribution implemented via a rescaling of the fragmentation function argument z → z/(1−ε).

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient for Cronin effect in low-energy p+A reactions: μ^2/λ = 0.1–0.14 GeV^2
- Scale of power corrections per nucleon: ξ^2 ≈ 0.09–0.12 GeV^2, extracted from DIS nuclear shadowing data
- Dynamical gluon mass scale for gluons: (C_A/C_F) ξ^2 = 2.25 ξ^2 = 0.20–0.27 GeV^2
- Charm quark mass used in neutrino DIS calculations: M_c = 1.35 GeV
- Trigger pion p_T = 5 GeV and associated pion p_T = 2 GeV used in fragmentation seesaw analysis
- Predicted nuclear modification factor R_AA for π^0 at √s_NN = 200 GeV and 5500 GeV shown for SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies
- Secondary bremsstrahlung gluon transverse momentum after rescattering: ∼600 MeV

## Context

This paper extends the perturbative QCD factorization framework of Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1985) to nuclear collisions by incorporating three classes of medium modifications that were previously treated separately. The Cronin effect calculations build on the work of Cronin (1974) and Accardi (2002); the dynamical power-correction resummation for nuclear shadowing follows Qiu and Vitev (2003, 2004) and provides an alternative to the EKS98 scale-dependent shadowing parametrization; the jet quenching formalism uses the GLV reaction-operator approach of Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2000). The results enable systematic jet tomography of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, and predict the disappearance of away-side correlations in Au+Au collisions that was subsequently observed by STAR.
