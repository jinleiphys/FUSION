# Regge-plus-resonance predictions for charged-kaon photoproduction from the deuteron

> We present a Regge-inspired effective-Lagrangian framework for charged-kaon photoproduction from the deuteron. Quasi-free kaon production is investigated using the Regge-plus-resonance elementary operator within the non-relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation. The Regge-plus-resonance model was developed to describe photoinduced and electroinduced kaon production off protons and can be extended to strangeness production off neutrons. The non-resonant contributions to the amplitude are modelled in terms of K+(494) and K*+(892) Regge-trajectory exchange in the t-channel. This amplitude is supplemented with a selection of s-channel resonance-exchange diagrams. We investigate several sources of theoretical uncertainties on the semi-inclusive charged-kaon production cross section. The experimental error bars on the photocoupling helicity amplitudes turn out to put severe limits on the predictive power when considering quasi-free kaon production on a bound neutron.

# Regge-plus-resonance predictions for charged-kaon photoproduction from the deuteron

## Key claim

The Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model, previously developed for kaon photoproduction off free protons, can be extended to quasi-free charged-kaon production from the deuteron using a non-relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation; however, the predictive power for reactions involving bound neutrons is severely limited by the experimental error bars on the photocoupling helicity amplitudes of nucleon resonances.

## Method

The authors embed the Regge-plus-resonance elementary operator, which combines t-channel K+(494) and K*+(892) Regge-trajectory exchanges with s-channel resonance-exchange diagrams, into a non-relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation for the deuteron. The elementary operator is treated fully relativistically while a non-relativistic deuteron wave function (from the CD-Bonn, Paris, or Nijmegen-III potentials) is used, and the semi-inclusive threefold differential cross section is obtained by integrating over the hyperon and nucleon four-momenta in their center-of-mass frame. For neutron-target channels, the coupling constants are converted from the proton case using SU(2) isospin symmetry and photocoupling helicity amplitudes from the SAID SM95 analysis.

## Key numbers

- The Regge trajectories are defined with slopes: α_K+(t) = 0.70 (t − m_K+²) and α_K*+(t) = 1 + 0.85 (t − m_K*+²), with s₀ = 1 GeV².
- The ratio of neutron to proton EM coupling constants for the N(1650)S₁₁ resonance is −0.22 ± 0.07 (spin-1/2 case).
- The ratio of neutron to proton EM coupling constants for the N(1710)P₁₁ resonance is −0.29 ± 2.23 (spin-1/2 case).
- For the N(1720)P₁₃ resonance (spin-3/2), the two ratios are −0.38 ± 2.00 and −0.50 ± 1.08.
- For the N(1900)P₁₃ resonance, the ratios of magnetic transition moments are allowed to vary between −2 and +2 due to lack of experimental information.
- The semi-inclusive cross section is computed at photon laboratory energies from 900 MeV to 2500 MeV.
- At E_γ^lab = 1300 MeV, variations of order two are possible in the predicted Σ-production peak cross section due to helicity amplitude uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the Regge-plus-resonance formalism, previously applied to p(γ,K+)Λ and p(γ,K+)Σ⁰ reactions by Vancraeyveld et al. (2009) and to n(γ,K+)Σ⁻ by the same group, to quasi-free production on the deuteron. The approach contrasts with full coupled-channels analyses and isobar models that require ad hoc form factors and gauge-invariance restoration (as discussed by Guidal et al. (1997) and others). The results enable future experimental constraints on neutron resonance photocoupling helicity amplitudes through deuteron measurements, and provide a baseline for studying nuclear-medium effects and hyperon-nucleon interactions via final-state rescattering.
