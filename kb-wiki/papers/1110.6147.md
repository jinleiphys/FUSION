# An Analytical Evaluation For The Integral Of Two Spherical Bessel Functions With An Additional Exponential And Polynomial Factor

> The integrals $\threej{\Llo}{\Llt}{\Llth} {0}{0}{0}\,\int_0^\infty \,r^{\Llth+1}\,e^{-αr}\,j_\Llo(k_1r)\, j_\Llt(k_2r)\,dr$ and $\threej{\Llo}{\Llt}{\Llth} {0}{0}{0}\,\int_0^\infty \,r^{\Llth+2}\,e^{-αr}\,j_\Llo(k_1r)\, j_\Llt(k_2r)\,dr$ are evaluated analytically. The result is a finite sum over the associated Legendre function of the second kind.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed-form analytical expressions for two specific integrals involving two spherical Bessel functions with an additional exponential and polynomial factor, where the result is expressed as a finite sum over associated Legendre functions of the second kind, provided the convergence condition \(n + \lambda_1 + \lambda_2 \ge 0\) is satisfied.

## Method

The author uses the closure relation of spherical Bessel functions to rewrite the target integral as a double integral over a third spherical Bessel function and a known integral from Gradshteyn and Ryzhik. A previously derived result for the integral of three spherical Bessel functions (Mehrem, Londergan, and Macfarlane, 1991) is then substituted, and the integration variable is changed from \(k_3\) to \(\Delta = (k_1^2 + k_2^2 - k_3^2)/(2k_1k_2)\), which transforms the remaining integral into a standard form that yields an associated Legendre function of the second kind. The second integral is obtained by taking a derivative with respect to \(\alpha\) of the first result.

## Key numbers

- The first integral evaluated is \(\int_0^\infty r^{\lambda_3+1} e^{-\alpha r} j_{\lambda_1}(k_1 r) j_{\lambda_2}(k_2 r) dr\), with the result given by equation (8) involving a sum over associated Legendre functions \(Q_l^{\lambda_3}(y)\) where \(y = (k_1^2 + k_2^2 + \alpha^2)/(2k_1k_2)\).
- The second integral evaluated is \(\int_0^\infty r^{\lambda_3+2} e^{-\alpha r} j_{\lambda_1}(k_1 r) j_{\lambda_2}(k_2 r) dr\), with the result given by equation (11) involving \(Q_l^{\lambda_3+1}(y)\).
- A special case for \(\lambda_3 = 0\) yields \(\int_0^\infty r e^{-\alpha r} j_L(k_1 r) j_L(k_2 r) dr = \frac{1}{2k_1k_2} Q_L\left(\frac{k_1^2+k_2^2+\alpha^2}{2k_1k_2}\right)\).
- The integral \(\int_0^\infty r^{\lambda_3+1} e^{-\alpha r} j_{\lambda_3}(k_3 r) dr = \frac{(2k_3)^{\lambda_3} \lambda_3!}{(k_3^2+\alpha^2)^{\lambda_3+1}}\) is used as an intermediate result from Gradshteyn and Ryzhik (1965).

## Context

This work provides an analytical alternative to the symbolic integration method using Mathematica employed by Gebremariam, Duguet, and Bogner (2010) and to the numerical approach of Van Deun and Cools (2008) for integrals of the form \(\int_0^\infty r^n e^{-\alpha r} j_{\lambda_1}(k_1 r) j_{\lambda_2}(k_2 r) dr\). The result extends the earlier work of Mehrem, Londergan, and Macfarlane (1991) on integrals of three spherical Bessel functions. The author notes ongoing work to obtain integrals of the form \(\int_0^\infty r^{-\lambda_3} e^{-\alpha r} j_{\lambda_1}(k_1 r) j_{\lambda_2}(k_2 r) dr\), and the special case \(\alpha \rightarrow 0\) with \(\lambda_3 = 1\) exhibits a singularity when \(k_1 = k_2\) as previously noted by Maximon (1991).
