# Nonperturbative fluctuation effects of charged bosonic fields: A quark-diquark model study at nonzero density

> We study the renormalization group flow of the scale-dependent effective potential of a quark-diquark model with full field dependence at nonzero chemical potential. This includes a discussion of approximations in relation to complex bosonic fields and the Silver-Blaze property. The resulting flow equation for the scale-dependent effective potential can in principle be solved down to the infrared limit. For our quark-diquark model, which may serve as a low-energy model for dense strong-interaction matter, we find that a competition between the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer singularity and bosonic fluctuations can trigger a first-order phase transition at low temperatures that turns into a second-order phase transition at a tricritical point as the temperature increases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a competition between the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) singularity and bosonic fluctuations in a quark-diquark model can trigger a first-order phase transition at low temperatures that turns into a second-order phase transition at a tricritical point as the temperature increases, but this finding depends on the specific truncation scheme used for the local potential approximation and the treatment of temporal kinetic terms for the diquark fields.

## Method

The authors apply the functional renormalization group (fRG) approach to a quark-diquark model with two massless quark flavors, computing the full field-dependent effective potential from which the diquark condensate is deduced. They employ a local potential approximation (LPA) that includes bosonic fluctuation effects of the diquark fields beyond the mean-field approximation, using techniques borrowed from numerical fluid dynamics to solve the flow equation for the scale-dependent effective potential. The construction of truncations is discussed in relation to complex bosonic fields and the Silver-Blaze property, with two versions of LPA truncation maps examined for their treatment of temporal diquark kinetic terms.

## Key numbers

* The ultraviolet scale is set to Lambda = 1 GeV, serving as the initial condition for the renormalization group flow.
* For the mean-field approximation at zero temperature and mu = 0.5 GeV, the derivative of the scale-dependent effective potential is discontinuous at bar-Delta = 0 for k = mu, with the curvature diverging.
* At T = 5 MeV, the BCS singularity is screened and the discontinuity in the derivative of the scale-dependent effective potential at bar-Delta = 0 for k = mu disappears.
* The diquark condensate in the mean-field approximation scales as bar-Delta_gs proportional to exp(-c/mu^2), where c is a bar-nu^2-dependent positive constant.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of color superconductivity in dense QCD using functional methods, building on earlier ground-breaking studies by Son (1998), Schafer (1999), Pisarski (1999), and others, as well as more recent developments by Braun (2019, 2021), Leonhardt (2019), Fukushima (2021, 2024), and Geissel (2024, 2025). The paper also builds on discussions of renormalization of effective models for high-density QCD by Braun (2018, 2022) and subsequent studies by Andersen (2024, 2025), Gholami (2024, 2025). The fRG approach with numerical fluid dynamics techniques follows the methodology developed by Koenigstein (2021), Steil (2021), and Zorbach (2024). The results enable future studies of the phase diagram of dense strong-interaction matter including nonperturbative bosonic fluctuation effects beyond mean-field approximations.
