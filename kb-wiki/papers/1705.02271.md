# Global view on coupled dynamics of heavy and light flavor observables from EPOSHQ

> We present an analysis of the comparison between 2nd and 3rd flow harmonics of $π$, protons and D mesons produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We advocate that such an analysis could turn out to be a good strategy in order to quantify the off-equilibrium dynamics of heavy flavor at lower transverse momentum $p_T$.

## Key claim
The paper argues that comparing the event-by-event correlations between flow harmonics of D mesons and light hadrons (pions, protons) can quantify the degree of off-equilibrium dynamics of heavy quarks at low transverse momentum, but the conclusion is ambiguous: while average flow values suggest thermalization, large event-by-event fluctuations indicate incomplete thermalization of charm quarks.

## Method
The authors use the EPOSHQ model, which combines the EPOS3 viscous hydrodynamic bulk evolution with the MC@sHQ model for heavy quark energy loss (using pure elastic scattering with a K=1.5 cranking factor). They analyze PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, artificially oversampling charm quarks by a factor of 50 to reduce statistical fluctuations, and compute flow harmonics using the event plane method with a rapidity gap separation (|y|>2 for event plane, |y|<2 for flow harmonics).

## Key numbers
- sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV PbPb collisions (centrality classes 10%-30% and 30%-50%)
- D meson oversampling factor of 50 (achieved by cumulating 50 "HQ events" per single EPOS3 global event)
- p_T > 2 GeV cut applied for intermediate p_T D meson analysis
- N_c(30%-50%) approximately 0.4 times N_c(10%-30%)
- For v_2: all species (pi, p, c quarks, D mesons) roughly share the same average value
- For v_3: <v_3(D)> ≈ <v_3(c)> < <v_3(pi)> ≈ <v_3(p)>
- Standard deviations of v_n for c quarks and D mesons are larger than their averages
- D mesons are found with v_n values of opposite sign to the bulk flow

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Nahrgang 2015) which showed that heavier flavor mesons benefit less from bulk flow, especially for higher flow harmonics and larger centralities. The analysis is motivated by CMS collaboration measurements of D meson v_3 (CMS 2016). The paper contrasts with a similar analysis by Prado (2016) which found smaller fluctuations, possibly due to larger oversampling. The results suggest that correlations of heavy-light flow harmonics could provide a new experimental observable, and the authors encourage experimental collaborations to investigate these correlations further.
