# The vector MIT bag model under the light of new data

> In the present work we use the vector MIT bag model to describe quark matter and obtain the macroscopic properties of neutron stars. We also calculate the speed of sound of quark matter described by this model with specific parameter values and check if the results obey the conformal limit at high densities. We have seen that the vector MIT bag model produces stars with masses and radii consistent with recent data, suggesting the possible existence of massive quark (or strange) stars.

## Key claim

The vector MIT bag model can produce neutron star masses and radii consistent with recent NICER and Keck data for PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0952-0607, but only for specific parameter sets within the stability window; the parameters that satisfy these astrophysical constraints do not simultaneously produce a QCD phase diagram that obeys all experimental constraints, and the speed of sound at the critical chemical potential exceeds the conformal limit of 1/3.

## Method

The authors used the vector MIT bag model in the mean field approximation, which adds a repulsive mesonic vector field to the original MIT bag model Lagrangian. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations with the resulting equation of state to generate mass-radius relations for quark stars, imposing charge neutrality and beta equilibrium with electrons and muons. The speed of sound was computed as the derivative of pressure with respect to energy density, and the critical chemical potential for the deconfinement phase transition was determined from the zero-pressure condition at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Stability window for bag constant: for G_V = 0.3 fm^2 and X_V = 1.0, B^(1/4) between 139 MeV and 146 MeV; for X_V = 0.4, B^(1/4) between 139 MeV and 150 MeV
- Maximum star masses: 2.65 M_sun (X_V = 1.0, B^(1/4) = 139 MeV); 2.45 M_sun (X_V = 1.0, B^(1/4) = 146 MeV); 2.45 M_sun (X_V = 0.4, B^(1/4) = 139 MeV); 2.15 M_sun (X_V = 0.4, B^(1/4) = 150 MeV)
- Canonical star (1.4 M_sun) radius: 12.15 km (X_V = 1.0, B^(1/4) = 139 MeV); 11.40 km (X_V = 1.0, B^(1/4) = 146 MeV); 11.92 km (X_V = 0.4, B^(1/4) = 139 MeV); 10.71 km (X_V = 0.4, B^(1/4) = 150 MeV)
- Critical chemical potential at T=0: 877 MeV (X_V = 1.0); 858 MeV (X_V = 0.4)
- Squared speed of sound at critical chemical potential: 0.41 (X_V = 1.0); 0.38 (X_V = 0.4)
- Quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 4 MeV, m_s = 95 MeV

## Context

This work extends the vector MIT bag model introduced by the same group (Lobo and Menezes, 2020) to test it against the most recent neutron star observations from NICER (Riley, 2021) for PSR J0740+6620 and from Keck (2022) for PSR J0952-0607. It builds on the suggestion from sound speed studies (2020) that the conformal limit behavior of the speed of sound can indicate the presence of sizable quark matter cores in massive neutron stars. The results enable further investigation of whether hybrid stars (with hadronic outer layers and quark cores) are more consistent with observations than pure strange stars, and motivate exploration of parameter sets where the conformal limit is respected, as discussed in a companion paper (Lobo and Menezes, 2022).
