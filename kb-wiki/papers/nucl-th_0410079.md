# Globally Polarized Quark-gluon Plasma in Non-central A+A Collisions

> Produced partons have large local relative orbital angular momentum along the direction opposite to the reaction plane in the early stage of non-central heavy-ion collisions. Parton scattering is shown to polarize quarks along the same direction due to spin-orbital coupling. Such global quark polarization will lead to many observable consequences, such as left-right asymmetry of hadron spectra, global transverse polarization of thermal photons, dileptons and hadrons. Hadrons from the decay of polarized resonances will have azimuthal asymmetry similar to the elliptic flow. Global hyperon polarization is predicted within different hadronization scenarios and can be easily tested.

## Key claim

Parton scattering in non-central heavy-ion collisions polarizes quarks along the direction opposite to the reaction plane due to spin-orbital coupling, with the magnitude of polarization depending on the transverse gradient of longitudinal momentum and the screening mass; the authors estimate a quark polarization of order \(P_q \sim -0.3\) for semi-peripheral collisions at RHIC, but note that the final hyperon polarization depends on the hadronization mechanism and cannot be given model-independently.

## Method

The authors calculate the collective longitudinal momentum per parton from the participant nucleon distribution in a hard-sphere nuclear model, then compute the transverse gradient \(dp_z/dx\) and the resulting local relative orbital angular momentum. They derive the polarized parton scattering cross section using a screened potential model with Debye screening mass \(\mu\), averaging over parton impact parameters to obtain the global quark polarization. Hyperon polarization is then estimated under three hadronization scenarios: exclusive quark recombination using SU(6) wavefunctions, inclusive recombination, and quark fragmentation.

## Key numbers

* In Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the number of charged hadrons per participant nucleon is about 15, leading to \(c(s) \simeq 45\) partons per participant.
* For \(R_A = 6.5\) fm, the transverse gradient scale is \(dp_0/dx \simeq 0.34\) GeV/fm, and the orbital angular momentum scale for \(\Delta x = 1\) fm is \(L_0 \simeq 1.7\).
* For semi-peripheral collisions (\(b = R_A\)) at RHIC, with \(\mu \sim 0.5\) GeV and \(dp_0/dx = 0.34\) GeV/fm, the estimated quark polarization is \(P_q \sim -0.3\).
* In the nonrelativistic limit for massive quarks, the polarization is \(P_q \approx -\pi \mu p / 8 m_q^2\).
* For exclusive recombination with \(P_s \simeq P_q\), the hyperon polarization \(P_H = P_q\) with recombination probability \(R_H = 3(1 - P_q^2)\) for \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), and \(\Xi\).

## Context

This work extends the study of transverse hyperon polarization observed in unpolarized \(p+p\) and \(p+A\) collisions since the 1970s (PhData) to the case of non-central heavy-ion collisions where a quark-gluon plasma is formed. It contradicts earlier suggestions (Stock 1986, Panagiotou 1986) that hyperon polarization would disappear due to QGP formation. The prediction of global quark polarization enables experimental tests via hyperon polarization measurements at RHIC and LHC, and provides a new mechanism for left-right asymmetries and azimuthal asymmetries in heavy-ion collisions that are distinct from elliptic flow.
