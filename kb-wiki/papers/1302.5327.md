# Constraining the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities using isotope binding energy difference and neutron skin thickness

> We show that the neutron skin thickness $Δr_{np}$ of heavy nuclei is uniquely fixed by the symmetry energy density slope $L(ρ)$ at a subsaturation cross density $ρ_c \approx 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$ rather than at saturation density $ρ_0$, while the binding energy difference $ΔE$ between a heavy isotope pair is essentially determined by the magnitude of the symmetry energy $E_{\text{sym}}(ρ)$ at the same $ρ_c$. Furthermore, we find a value of $L({ρ_c})$ leads to a negative $E_{\text{sym}}({ρ_{0}})$-$L({ρ_{0}})$ correlation while a value of $E_{\text{sym}}({ρ_{c})}$ leads to a positive one. Using data on $Δr_{np}$ of Sn isotopes and $ΔE$ of a number of heavy isotope pairs, we obtain simultaneously $E_{\text{sym}}({ρ_{c})}=26.65\pm0.20$ MeV and $L({ρ_c})= 46.0\pm4.5$ MeV at 95% confidence level, whose extrapolation gives $E_{\text{sym}}({ρ_{0}})=32.3\pm1.0$ MeV and $L({ρ_{0}})=45.2\pm10.0$ MeV. The implication of these new constraints on the $Δr_{np}$ of $^{208}$Pb and the core-crust transition density in neutron stars is discussed.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei is uniquely fixed by the symmetry energy density slope L(ρ) at a subsaturation cross density ρ_c ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³ rather than at saturation density ρ_0, while the binding energy difference between a heavy isotope pair is essentially determined by the magnitude of the symmetry energy E_sym(ρ) at the same ρ_c. The authors show that a fixed value of L(ρ_c) leads to a negative E_sym(ρ_0)-L(ρ_0) correlation, while a fixed value of E_sym(ρ_c) leads to a positive one.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach with the standard form of Skyrme force, expressing the 9 Skyrme force parameters in terms of 9 macroscopic quantities including ρ_0, E_0(ρ_0), K_0, effective masses, E_sym(ρ_r), L(ρ_r), and gradient coefficients G_S and G_V. They performed correlation analysis by varying individual macroscopic quantities within their empirical ranges for three reference densities (0.06, 0.11, and 0.16 fm⁻³), then extracted constraints by χ² minimization using 21 data points on Δr_np of Sn isotopes and 19 data points on ΔE for heavy isotope pairs, with optimization of other parameters via simulated annealing.

## Key numbers

- E_sym(ρ_c) = 26.65 ± 0.20 MeV at 95% confidence level, extracted from binding energy differences of heavy isotope pairs at ρ_c ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³
- L(ρ_c) = 46.0 ± 4.5 MeV at 95% confidence level, extracted from neutron skin thickness data of Sn isotopes at ρ_c ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³
- Extrapolated values: E_sym(ρ_0) = 32.3 ± 1.0 MeV and L(ρ_0) = 45.2 ± 10.0 MeV
- Predicted neutron skin thickness for ²⁰⁸Pb: Δr_np = 0.170 ± 0.016 fm
- Predicted core-crust transition density in neutron stars: ρ_t = 0.082 ± 0.005 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the correlation analysis method previously applied to neutron skins by Chen (2010) and giant monopole resonances by Chen (2012), and is consistent with the finding by Khan (2012) that giant monopole resonances are constrained by the EOS at ρ_c ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³ rather than ρ_0. The results agree with constraints from Bayesian analysis of neutron star mass and radius measurements by Steiner (2012) and from pygmy dipole resonance analyses by Klimkiewicz (2007) and Carbone (2010), while providing higher precision on both E_sym(ρ_0) and L(ρ_0). The work enables more precise predictions for the neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb relevant to the PREX experiment and for the core-crust transition density in neutron stars.
