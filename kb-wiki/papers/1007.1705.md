# Surface-enhanced pair transfer in quadrupole states of neutron-rich Sn isotopes

> We investigate the neutron pair transfer modes associated with the low-lying quadrupole states in neutron-rich Sn isotopes by means of the quasiparticle random phase approximation based on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean field model. The transition strength of the quadrupole pair-addition mode feeding the $2_1^+$ state is enhanced in the Sn isotopes with $A \geq 132$. The transition density of the pair-addition mode has a large spatial extension in the exterior of nucleus, reaching far to $r\sim 12-13$ fm. The quadrupole pair-addition mode reflects sensitively a possible increase of the effective pairing interaction strength in the surface and exterior regions of neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim
The quadrupole pair-addition mode feeding the first excited 2+ state in neutron-rich Sn isotopes (A ≥ 132) shows enhanced transition strength and a transition density that extends far into the nuclear exterior (r ∼ 12–13 fm), reflecting a possible increase of the effective pairing interaction strength in the surface and exterior regions.

## Method
The authors performed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations based on a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field model with spherical symmetry, using the SLy4 Skyrme parameter set and three different density-dependent delta interactions (DDDI) for the pairing channel: DDDI-bare' (linked to bare nuclear force scattering length), volume pairing (density-independent), and mix pairing (linear density dependence). The linear response formalism was used to compute strength functions and transition densities for quadrupole pair-addition and pair-removal operators in Sn isotopes from A=100 to A=142.

## Key numbers
- The transition density of the pair-addition mode in 134Sn reaches far to r ∼ 12–13 fm, with its maximum amplitude at r ≈ 6.6 fm, about 1 fm outside the half-density radius (R1/2 ≈ 5.7 fm)
- The double ratio (Mn/Mp)/(N/Z) for the 2₁⁺ state increases suddenly for A > 132, reaching values of 1.6–1.7 (compared to 1.2–1.3 for A < 132)
- The neutron-to-proton amplitude ratio Mn/Mp itself reaches 2.7–3.1 in isotopes beyond N=82
- The DDDI-bare' pairing interaction strength in the low-density exterior region of 134Sn is more than twice larger than that of the volume pairing interaction
- The average neutron pairing gap differences between the three pairing models are less than 150 keV for A=134–138, and about 200 keV or more for A ≤ 116

## Context
This work extends previous studies of pair transfer in neutron-rich nuclei by Dobaczewski et al. (HFB ground-state pair transfer) and Khan et al. (pair transfer feeding excited 0+ states) to the quadrupole channel, specifically the 2₁⁺ state. The authors had previously found soft dipole and octupole modes with large pair transition amplitudes in the surface and exterior regions of neutron-rich nuclei (Matsuo et al., 2005; Serizawa et al., 2009), and here they show that the low-lying quadrupole mode also exhibits sensitivity to surface-enhanced pairing, enabling future experimental probes of pair correlation enhancement via pair-transfer reactions.
