# Magnetised Neutron Star Crusts and Torsional Shear Modes of Magnetars

> We discuss outer and inner crusts of neutron stars in strong magnetic fields. Here, we demonstrate the effect of Landau quantization of electrons on the ground state properties of matter in outer and inner crusts in magnetars. This effect leads to the enhancement of the electron number density in strong magnetic fields with respect to the zero field case. For the outer crust, we adopt the magnetic Baym-Pethick-Sutherland model and obtain the sequence of nuclei and equation of state (EoS). The properties of nuclei in the inner crust in the presence of strong magnetic fields are investigated using the Thomas-Fermi model. The coexistence of two phases of nuclear matter - liquid and gas, is assumed in this case. The proton number density in the Wigner-Seitz cell is affected in strong magnetic fields through the charge neutrality. We perform this calculation using the Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interaction with different parameterisations. We find nuclei with larger mass and atomic numbers in the inner crust in the presence of strong magnetic fields than those of the zero field case for all those parameter sets. Further we investigate torsional shear mode frequencies using the results of magnetised neutron star crusts and compare those with observations.

## Key claim

Landau quantization of electrons in magnetic fields above approximately \(10^{17}\) G significantly enhances the electron number density and modifies the composition of both outer and inner neutron star crusts, leading to nuclei with larger mass and atomic numbers compared to the zero-field case; this effect is conditional on the field being strong enough that electrons populate only the zeroth Landau level over a certain density regime.

## Method

The authors adopt the magnetic Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) model for the outer crust, using recent experimental and theoretical nuclear mass tables and including finite-size corrections to the lattice energy. For the inner crust, they employ the Thomas-Fermi model with the Bonche-Levit-Vautherin subtraction procedure to separate the nucleus from the coexisting neutron gas, using Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interactions (SkM, SLy4, Sk272). Torsional shear mode frequencies are then computed from the resulting magnetised crust models using a general relativistic formalism for axial perturbations in a dipole magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for electrons: \(B_c = 4.414 \times 10^{13}\) G.
- For \(B_{*} = 10^3\) (i.e., \(B \approx 4.4 \times 10^{16}\) G), the neutron drip point in the outer crust shifts to a higher density: the maximum density for the last equilibrium nucleus (\(^{118}\)Kr) is \(4.92 \times 10^{11}\) g/cm\(^3\), compared to \(4.34 \times 10^{11}\) g/cm\(^3\) at \(B=0\).
- For the inner crust with SkM interaction at \(B_{*} = 10^4\), mass and atomic numbers of equilibrium nuclei increase relative to the zero-field case; for SLy4, a jump in mass and atomic numbers occurs around baryon number density 0.05 fm\(^{-3}\) when electrons move from the zeroth to the next Landau level.
- For SGR 1806-20, calculated torsional shear mode frequencies (e.g., 15 Hz for \(n=0, \ell=2\); 93 Hz for \(n=0, \ell=12\); 626 Hz for \(n=1, \ell=29\); 1834 Hz for \(n=4, \ell=2\)) match observed QPO frequencies (18, 93, 150, 626, 1838 Hz) for a 1.4 \(M_{\odot}\) star with magnetic field \(B = 8 \times 10^{14}\) G.
- For SGR 1900+14, calculated frequencies (28 Hz for \(n=0, \ell=4\); 55 Hz for \(n=0, \ell=8\); 82 Hz for \(n=0, \ell=12\); 154 Hz for \(n=0, \ell=23\)) match observed frequencies (28, 54, 84, 155 Hz) for a 1.2 \(M_{\odot}\) star with magnetic field \(B = 4 \times 10^{14}\) G.

## Context

This work extends the magnetic BPS model of Shapiro (1992) by incorporating recent experimental and theoretical mass tables and finite-size lattice corrections, and it builds on the inner crust calculations of Nandi and Bandyopadhyay (2011) using the Thomas-Fermi model. The computed torsional shear mode frequencies are compared with quasi-periodic oscillations observed in giant flares from SGR 1806-20 and SGR 1900+14, previously identified as crustal shear modes by Duncan (1998). The results enable more precise constraints on magnetar masses and magnetic field strengths through asteroseismology, though the lowest observed frequencies (18, 26, 29 Hz in SGR 1806-20) may require Alfven mode explanations as suggested by Colaiuda et al.
