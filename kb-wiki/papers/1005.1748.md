# Meson and baryon spectrum for QCD with two light dynamical quarks

> We present results of meson and baryon spectroscopy using the Chirally Improved Dirac operator on lattices of size 16**3 x 32 with two mass-degenerate light sea quarks. Three ensembles with pion masses of 322(5), 470(4) and 525(7) MeV and lattice spacings close to 0.15 fm are investigated. Results on ground and excited states for several channels are given, including spin two mesons and hadrons with strange valence quarks. The analysis of the states is done with the variational method, including two kinds of Gaussian sources and derivative sources. We obtain several ground states fairly precisely and find radial excitations in various channels. Excited baryon results seem to suffer from finite size effects, in particular at small pion masses. We discuss the possible appearance of scattering states in various channels, considering masses and eigenvectors. Partially quenched results in the scalar channel suggest the presence of a 2-particle state, however, in most channels we cannot identify them. Where available, we compare our results to results of quenched simulations using the same action.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD spectroscopy results for mesons and baryons using the Chirally Improved Dirac operator with two dynamical light quarks, but the authors caution that excited baryon results appear to suffer from finite-size effects, particularly at the smallest pion mass, and that scattering states cannot be clearly identified in most channels despite their possible presence.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on 16³ × 32 lattices using the Chirally Improved Dirac operator (an approximate solution to the Ginsparg-Wilson equation) combined with a tadpole-improved Lüscher-Weisz gauge action, with one level of stout smearing. Hadron masses were extracted using the variational method with a basis of interpolators constructed from narrow Gaussian, wide Gaussian, and derivative quark sources, and the analysis included both dynamical and partially quenched valence quarks (including strange valence quarks). Chiral extrapolations to the physical point were performed using fits linear in the pion mass squared, with lattice spacings determined via the static quark potential using a Sommer parameter of 0.48 fm.

## Key numbers

- Three ensembles with pion masses of 322(5) MeV, 470(4) MeV, and 525(7) MeV, all on lattices of size 16³ × 32 with lattice spacings close to 0.15 fm (0.151(2) fm, 0.150(1) fm, and 0.144(1) fm respectively)
- The physical spatial volume is approximately 2.4 fm for all ensembles
- The dimensionless product m_π L is 6.4, 5.7, and 3.7 for ensembles A, B, and C respectively
- The AWI (axial Ward identity) masses are 43.0(4) MeV, 35.1(2) MeV, and 15.0(4) MeV for the three ensembles
- Computational cost per configuration ranged from approximately 60 CPU-hours (set A) to 120 CPU-hours (set C) on an SGI ALTIX-4700 system

## Context

This work extends previous quenched simulations by the BGR Collaboration using the same Chirally Improved Dirac operator (Gattringer 2003, Gattringer 2005) into the dynamical regime with two mass-degenerate light sea quarks. The results enable comparison with other contemporary dynamical lattice spectroscopy efforts (Morningstar 2008, Alexandrou 2008, Dudek 2009, Dudek 2010) and provide a foundation for future calculations with larger volumes, additional pion masses, and explicit two-particle interpolators to better resolve scattering states and finite-volume effects.
