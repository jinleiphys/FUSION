# A global extraction of the jet transport coefficient in cold nuclear matter

> Within the framework of the generalized QCD factorization formalism, we perform the first global analysis of the jet transport coefficient ($\hat q$) for cold nuclear matter. The analysis takes into account the world data on transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive electron-nucleus deep inelastic scattering, Drell-Yan dilepton and heavy quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions, as well as the nuclear modification of the structure functions in deep inelastic scattering, comprising a total of 215 data points from 8 data sets. For the first time, we clarify quantitatively the universality and probing scale dependence of the nuclear medium property as encoded in $\hat q$. We expect that the determined parametrization of $\hat q$ in cold nuclear matter will have significant impact on precise identification of the transport property of hot dense medium created in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first quantitative evidence that the jet transport coefficient \(\hat q\) in cold nuclear matter is not a constant but exhibits a clear dependence on Bjorken-\(x\) and the probing scale \(\mu^2\), based on a global fit to 215 data points from 8 data sets.

## Method

The authors perform a global analysis within the generalized QCD factorization (high-twist expansion) formalism, fitting a four-parameter functional form for \(\hat q(x,\mu^2)\) to world data on transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive electron-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (HERMES), Drell-Yan dilepton production (SPS-NA10, FNAL-E772), heavy quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions (FNAL-E772, FNAL-E866, RHIC, LHC), and nuclear modification of the structure function \(F_2\) in deep inelastic scattering (FNAL-E665). The fit uses leading-order pQCD expressions and the MINUIT package, with CT14 LO parton distribution functions and DSS fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit parameters for \(\hat q(x,\mu^2) = \hat q_0 \,\alpha_s(\mu^2) \,x^{\alpha}(1-x)^{\beta} \ln^{\gamma}(\mu^2/\mu_0^2)\) are \(\hat q_0 = 0.02\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, \(\alpha = -0.17\), \(\beta = -2.79\), and \(\gamma = 0.25\), with \(\mu_0 = 1\) GeV.
* The global fit yields \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 1.21\) for 215 data points from 8 data sets.
* The individual \(\chi^2\) contributions include: HERMES SIDIS (156 points, \(\chi^2 = 189.7\)), FNAL-E772 Drell-Yan (4 points, \(\chi^2 = 1.6\)), SPS-NA10 Drell-Yan (5 points, \(\chi^2 = 6.5\)), FNAL-E772 \(\Upsilon\) (4 points, \(\chi^2 = 2.7\)), FNAL-E866 \(J/\psi\) (4 points, \(\chi^2 = 2.4\)), RHIC \(J/\psi\) (10 points, \(\chi^2 = 31.0\)), LHC \(J/\psi\) (12 points, \(\chi^2 = 4.8\)), and FNAL-E665 DIS (20 points, \(\chi^2 = 21.5\)).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Kang (2013, 2014, 2016) which derived renormalization group equations for \(\hat q\) but left the evolution equation unclosed, requiring a data-driven approach. It contradicts the common assumption of a constant \(\hat q\) used in earlier studies by Luo (1993), Qiu (2003), and Qiu (2004). The extracted parametrization enables future precise identification of the transport property of hot dense matter in heavy ion collisions and can be tested by future measurements at a proposed Electron Ion Collider.
