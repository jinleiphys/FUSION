# In-medium QCD and Cherenkov gluons vs Mach waves at LHC

> The equations of in-medium gluodynamics are proposed. Their classical lowest order solution is explicitly shown for a color charge moving with constant speed. For nuclear permittivity larger than 1 it describes the shock wave induced by emission of Cherenkov gluons. Specific effects at LHC energies are described and compared with Mach wave predictions.

# In-medium QCD and Cherenkov gluons vs Mach waves at LHC

## Key claim
The classical lowest-order solution of the proposed in-medium gluodynamics equations describes a shock wave induced by emission of Cherenkov gluons when the nuclear permittivity exceeds 1, but the definition of the nuclear permittivity depends on the experimental geometry and the rest frame of the medium, meaning that partons moving in different directions can "feel" different states of matter in the same collision.

## Method
The author modifies the Yang-Mills equations by introducing a nuclear permittivity (analogous to electrodynamics) into the gluodynamics field equations, replacing the chromoelectric field E_a by epsilon * E_a. The classical lowest-order solution is then derived for a color charge moving with constant speed, yielding a shock-wave solution localized within a cone defined by the Cherenkov angle. The nuclear permittivity is estimated from the forward scattering amplitude of hadrons using the relation between refractive index and the real part of the forward scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The Cherenkov angle is given by cos(theta) = 1/(v*sqrt(epsilon)), where v is the parton velocity and epsilon is the nuclear permittivity.
- The position of maxima in two-particle correlations is displaced from the away-side jet by 1.05-1.23 radians, requiring Re(epsilon) ~ 2-3.
- Mach shock waves would predict a shift of 0.955 radians if the relativistic equation of state is used (cos(theta) = 1/sqrt(3)).
- In three-particle correlations, the displacement is about 1.38 radians.
- The field within the Cherenkov cone decreases with time as 1/t at any fixed point.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies of Cherenkov gluons in hadronic collisions (Dremin 2006, 2007) and provides a theoretical framework for interpreting experimental observations at SPS, RHIC, and cosmic ray energies. The asymmetry of the rho-meson shape at SPS and azimuthal correlations of in-medium jets at RHIC were previously explained by emission of low-energy Cherenkov gluons. The paper contrasts Cherenkov gluons (transverse waves) with Mach shock waves (longitudinal waves) and argues that the energy threshold of the nuclear permittivity explains why Mach waves were not observed for forward-moving partons at RHIC. The approach enables predictions for LHC experiments with both 90-degree trigger and forward-backward non-trigger geometries.
