# Generalized Polarizabilities of the Nucleon in Chiral Effective Theories

> Using the techniques of chiral effective field theories we evaluate the so called generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon, which characterize the structure dependent components in virtual Compton scattering (VCS) as probed in the electron scattering reaction e N \to e' N gamma. Results are given for both spin-dependent and spin-independent structure effects to O(p^3) in SU(2) Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory and to O(epsilon^3) in the SU(2) Small Scale Expansion. Finally we compare our calculations with results from the pioneering VCS experiment on the proton from Mainz.

## Key claim

The paper evaluates the generalized polarizabilities (GPs) of the nucleon in virtual Compton scattering (VCS) using chiral effective field theories, providing results to leading one-loop order (O(p³) in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory and O(ε³) in the Small Scale Expansion). The hedge is that these calculations are valid only in the low-momentum regime (0 < q̅² < 0.5 GeV²) and that the spin-dependent sector yields only 4 of the 7 spin GPs to this order, with the remaining 3 suppressed by 1/M_N.

## Method

The authors computed all 10 generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon in virtual Compton scattering (γ*N → γN) using SU(2) Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) to O(p³), including Nπ-loop diagrams and tree-level Born terms (nucleon pole, Thomson seagull, and anomalous pion-pole). They then extended the calculation to include explicit Δ(1232) resonance degrees of freedom via the Small Scale Expansion (SSE) to O(ε³), adding Δπ-loop diagrams and Δ-exchange Born graphs. The GPs were extracted from the VCS amplitude by expanding around ω' = 0 and retaining only terms linear in ω', yielding functions of the three-momentum transfer q̅.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability from real Compton scattering: α_E^p = (12.1 ± 0.8 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Proton magnetic polarizability from real Compton scattering: β_M^p = (2.1 ∓ 0.8 ∓ 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Baldin sum rule for proton: α_E^p + β_M^p = (13.69 ± 0.14) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Baldin sum rule for neutron: α_E^n + β_M^n = (14.40 ± 0.66) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Forward spin-polarizability γ₀^p from SAID analysis: -1.34 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Forward spin-polarizability γ₀^p from Mainz analysis: -0.80 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Backward spin-polarizability γ_π = (27.7 ± 2.3 ± 2.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Momentum range for GP predictions: 0 < q̅² < 0.5 GeV²
- SSE coupling constants: g_{πNΔ} = 1.05 ± 0.02, b₁ = 3.85 ± 0.15

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral calculations of generalized polarizabilities by Hemmert, Holstein, Knoechlein, and Drechsel (1997, 1998) that analyzed only the leading momentum dependence of the electric and magnetic GPs, and provides the first full analytical expressions for all 10 GPs in HBChPT. It also generalizes the SSE treatment of real Compton scattering by Hemmert, Holstein, and Knoechlein (1997) to the VCS case. The predictions are intended for comparison with the pioneering VCS experiment at MAMI (Mainz) and approved experiments at BATES and TJNAF, and enable extraction of the elusive Ragusa spin-polarizabilities γ₂, γ₃, γ₄ via the model-independent relations connecting them to the GPs at q̅ → 0.
