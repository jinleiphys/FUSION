# Radii and Binding Energies of Nuclei from a Model of the pn-pair Interactions

> New formulas to calculate the nuclear charge radii and the nuclear binding energies have been derived in the framework of an alpha-cluster model. The particular features of the model are two assumptions that the proton-neutron pair interactions are responsible for adherence between alpha-clusters and that the proton and the neutron from a proton-neutron pair have equal nuclear potentials. These assumptions allow one to determine the charge and Coulomb radii in dependence on the amount of proton-neutron pairs in the nucleus. Unlike the Weizsacker's formula to calculate nuclear binding energies, in the model the binding energies of alpha-clusters and excess neutrons are estimated separately. The calculated values are in a good agreement with the experimental data.

# Radii and Binding Energies of Nuclei from a Model of the pn-pair Interactions

## Key claim
New formulas for nuclear charge radii and binding energies can be derived from an alpha-cluster model where proton-neutron pair interactions are responsible for adherence between alpha-clusters, and the calculated values agree with experimental data; however, the model assumes that the proton and neutron from a proton-neutron pair have equal nuclear potentials (the EPN requirement), and the formulas contain no fitting parameters only for nuclei with N=Z, while for nuclei with excess neutrons a two-parameter phenomenological fit to experimental nn-pair separation energies is used.

## Method
The author constructs an alpha-cluster model where nuclei are composed of alpha-clusters and, for odd Z, a single proton-neutron pair on the surface. The binding energy for N=Z nuclei is calculated from the number of alpha-clusters and the number of short-range inter-cluster bonds, using empirically determined bond energies from the lightest nuclei (6Li, 10B, 12C). For nuclei with excess neutrons, the binding energy of excess neutron-neutron pairs is added using a phenomenological formula fitted to experimental separation energies. Charge radii are calculated by summing weighted square radii of a core and peripheral clusters, with the radius of the last proton position determined from Coulomb energy relations derived from the EPN requirement.

## Key numbers
- Internal binding energy of one alpha-cluster: epsilon_alpha = 28.296 MeV (taken from 4He)
- Inter-cluster bond energy: epsilon_alpha_alpha = 2.425 MeV
- Energy between a single pn-pair and a pn-pair of a nearby alpha-cluster: epsilon_pn_pn = 2.037 MeV
- Binding energy of a single pn-pair: epsilon_pn = 1.659 MeV
- Coulomb repulsion energy between two alpha-clusters: epsilon^C_alpha_alpha = 1.925 MeV
- Internal Coulomb energy of one alpha-cluster: epsilon^C_alpha = 0.764 MeV
- Coulomb repulsion energy between a single pn-pair and a nearby alpha-cluster: epsilon^C_pn_alpha = 1.001(6) MeV
- Root mean square deviation of calculated charge radii R from experimental values for nuclei with 6 <= Z <= 83: <Delta^2>^{1/2} = 0.050 Fm
- Root mean square deviation of calculated shell-model radii R_shl from experimental values for nuclei with 9 <= Z <= 82: <Delta^2>^{1/2} = 0.051 Fm
- Formula for excess nn-pair binding energy: E_nni = 22.5 MeV - 1.358 N_nn^{2/3}
- Experimental charge radius of 4He: R_exp = 1.71(4) Fm
- Experimental charge radius of 12C: R_exp = 2.470(15) Fm
- Experimental charge radius of 16O: R_exp = 2.730(25) Fm
- Experimental charge radius of 40Ca: R_exp = 3.482(25) Fm

## Context
This work extends the alpha-cluster model tradition originating with Gamow (1929) and the liquid drop model of Weizsacker (1935). It builds on prior cluster model studies of alpha-decay using alpha-core potentials, regular alpha-cluster geometries, and shell-model incorporations of clustering. The author references earlier work (Nie, 2004) for the formula counting inter-cluster bonds and for the EPN requirement. The model enables calculation of charge radii and binding energies across a wide range of nuclei without fitting parameters for the N=Z case, and provides a separate treatment of excess neutron binding energies that could be refined with more precise shell-effect corrections.
