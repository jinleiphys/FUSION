# Neutrinoless double beta decay and neutrino physics

> The connection of neutrino physics with neutrinoless double beta decay is reviewed. After presenting the current status of the PMNS matrix and the theoretical background of neutrino mass and lepton mixing, we will summarize the various implications of neutrino physics for double beta decay. The influence of light sterile neutrinos and other exotic modifications of the three neutrino picture is also discussed.

## Key claim
This review paper argues that if neutrinoless double beta decay is mediated by light Majorana neutrinos (the standard interpretation), the effective mass observable depends on seven of the nine physical parameters of low-energy neutrino physics and can provide information on the neutrino mass ordering, Majorana CP phases, and absolute mass scale, but this interpretation is conditional on the assumption that no other lepton-number-violating mechanisms contribute significantly.

## Method
The paper reviews the current status of the PMNS matrix parameters from global fits to oscillation data, presents the theoretical formalism connecting neutrino mass and lepton mixing to the neutrinoless double beta decay rate, and surveys the experimental landscape of running and planned experiments. It then analyzes the effective mass parameter as a function of the smallest neutrino mass, the sum of neutrino masses, and the kinematic neutrino mass, using the allowed ranges of oscillation parameters and considering the impact of Majorana CP phases.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit solar mass-squared difference: Δm²₂₁ = 7.62 × 10⁻⁵ eV² (with 1σ range +0.19/−0.19)
- Best-fit atmospheric mass-squared difference (normal ordering): |Δm²₃₁| = 2.53 × 10⁻³ eV² (with 1σ range +0.08/−0.10)
- Best-fit sin²θ₁₂ = 0.320 (+0.015/−0.017); sin²θ₂₃ = 0.49 (+0.08/−0.05); sin²θ₁₃ = 0.026 (+0.003/−0.004)
- Daya Bay measurement: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.092 ± 0.017, non-zero at 5.2σ
- Strongest limit on ⁷⁶Ge half-life: 1.9 × 10²⁵ years (HDM experiment), corresponding to effective mass limit range 0.21–0.53 eV
- EXO-200 limit on ¹³⁶Xe half-life: 1.6 × 10²⁵ years, corresponding to effective mass limit range 0.15–0.36 eV
- KATRIN design sensitivity: m_β = 0.2 eV (90% C.L.)
- GERDA Phase II sensitivity: 2 × 10²⁶ years for ⁷⁶Ge, corresponding to effective mass sensitivity 0.06–0.16 eV

## Context
This review extends the discussion of neutrinoless double beta decay in the context of neutrino physics, building on the framework established by earlier reviews such as Avignone (2008), Giuliani (2010), and Rodejohann (2011). It incorporates the recent measurements of θ₁₃ from Daya Bay, RENO, and Double Chooz, and discusses how the precise value of θ₁₃ is not particularly important for current and next-generation experiments. The paper enables future work on distinguishing the neutrino mass ordering and testing flavor symmetry models through their predictions for neutrino mass observables.
