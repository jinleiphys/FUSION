# On $γN \to γρN'$ at large $γρ$ invariant mass

> Photoproduction of a pair of particles with large invariant mass is a natural extension of collinear QCD factorization theorems which have been much studied for deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production. We consider the case where the wide angle Compton scattering subprocess $γ(q\bar q) \to γρ$ factorizes from generalized parton distribution. At dominant twist, separating the transverse (respectively longitudinal) polarization of the $ρ$ meson allows one to get access to chiral-odd (respectively chiral-even) GPDs. This opens a new way to the extraction of these elusive transversity GPDs.

## Key claim

The process γN → γρN' at large γρ invariant mass can be factorized into a hard scattering subprocess convolved with generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and meson distribution amplitudes, with the transverse (longitudinal) polarization of the ρ meson giving access to chiral-odd (chiral-even) GPDs; however, the authors note that "the quest for an easy extraction of chiral-odd GPDs is obviously not solved by our proposal for γρ_T photoproduction" due to the small predicted cross section.

## Method

The authors apply collinear QCD factorization to the photoproduction of a γρ pair with large invariant mass, extending the wide-angle Compton scattering formalism. They compute the leading-twist scattering amplitude at Born order using 20 Feynman diagrams (reduced to 10 by symmetry, with only 4 contributing to the chiral-odd case in Feynman gauge), then integrate over the ρ meson distribution amplitude (using its asymptotic form) and over the GPD variable x using a model for transversity GPDs. The differential cross section is evaluated numerically as a function of the Mandelstam variable -u' at fixed SγN and M²γρ.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section dσ/(dt du' dM²γρ) at |t| = t_min is evaluated at SγN = 20 GeV² and M²γρ = 6 GeV², corresponding to ξ = 0.186.
- The cross section is shown as a function of -u' with cuts requiring -t' > 1 GeV² and -u' > 1 GeV².
- The skewness parameter ξ is given by ξ = τ/(2-τ) with τ = (M²γρ - t)/(SγN - M²), where M is the nucleon mass.
- The cross section normalization is described as "rather small" (numerical values are shown in a figure but not given as explicit numbers in the text).

## Context

This work extends the factorization approach previously applied to deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production (GPD, 2000s; impact, 2000s) to a new exclusive channel. It follows the strategy of Ref. IPST and PLB for processes with two mesons in the final state, and contrasts with alternative approaches using quark mass effects (PS2015) or twist-3 distribution amplitudes (liuti) to access transversity GPDs. The results enable future experimental searches at JLab12 and other facilities, though the small cross section for the chiral-odd channel means that a complete angular analysis of the emerging π⁺π⁻ pair from ρ decay would be needed to isolate the transversity-sensitive contribution.
