# Revisiting the Possibility of a Sharp Phase Transition in Cold Neutron Stars

> First-order phase transitions (FOPTs) in cold neutron stars (NSs) have been extensively studied and have provided valuable insights into the behavior of the densest matter visible in our Universe, although a strong consensus has yet to emerge. Revisiting the possibility of a hadron-quark FOPT from a new perspective, we examine the interplay between the coupled nature of gravity and microscopic interactions in Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff (TOV) equations and the fundamental requirements of thermodynamic consistency in NSs. We demonstrate that a sharp FOPT manifested as a plateau in the equation of state (EOS) $P(\varepsilon)$, i.e., pressure $P$ versus energy density $\varepsilon$, is intrinsically incompatible with the regularity conditions of the TOV solutions. Although numerical integrations of the TOV equations with EOSs incorporating FOPTs may yield seemingly reasonable mass-radius relations consistent with current observations, such results can mask underlying inconsistencies. Our analysis thus establishes a structural consistency criterion for constraining dense-matter EOSs using NS observables, complementing existing studies of possible phase transitions in NS interiors.

## Key claim
The authors argue that a sharp first-order phase transition (FOPT) manifested as a plateau in the equation of state (pressure versus energy density) is intrinsically incompatible with the regularity conditions of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, even though numerical integrations may yield seemingly reasonable mass-radius relations that mask underlying inconsistencies.

## Method
The authors analyze the TOV equations in dimensionless form to show that the pressure gradient dP/dr must be strictly negative everywhere inside a neutron star (except at center and surface). They demonstrate that an FOPT with s²=0 would force dP/dr=0 over a finite radial interval, violating this requirement. They then perform numerical integrations of the TOV equations using piecewise polytropic equations of state with an intermediate linear segment, including cases with β=0 (FOPT) and negative β values, to illustrate that apparently reasonable mass-radius relations can emerge from physically inconsistent equations of state.

## Key numbers
- The energy density at nuclear saturation ε₀ ≈ 150 MeV/fm³ is used as the reference scale
- The transition energy density is set at ε_a/ε₀ = 2
- The pressure at the transition point P_a is randomly selected between 20-50 MeV/fm³
- Polytropic indices are taken as γ_a ≈ 2-13/6 and γ_b ≈ 5/3-2
- For the negative SSS case, β is chosen in the range -0.1 ≲ β ≲ 0
- The Seidov condition threshold is given as Δε_crit/ε_a = (1+3φ_a)/2

## Context
This work extends previous studies of first-order phase transitions in neutron stars (Alford et al., 2013; Alford, 2017; Xie, 2021) and challenges the common practice of using FOPT-based equations of state in TOV integrations. The analysis complements existing reviews (Shapiro, 1983; Oertel, 2017; Baym, 2018) by establishing a structural consistency criterion for constraining dense-matter equations of state using neutron star observables. The authors suggest that continuous crossover equations of state, supported by lattice QCD simulations (Aoki, 2006; Bazarov, 2012; Bhattacharya, 2014), provide a more physically consistent description than FOPT-based models.
