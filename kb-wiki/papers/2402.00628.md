# Cylindrically symmetric diffusion model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A relativistic diffusion model with cylindrical symmetry, which propagates an initial state based on quantum chromodynamics in time towards a thermal equilibrium limit, is derived from nonequilibrium-statistical considerations: Adapting an existing framework for Markovian stochastic processes representing relativistic phase-space trajectories, a Fokker-Planck equation is obtained for the time evolution of particle-number distribution functions with respect to transverse and longitudinal rapidity. The resulting partially-evolved distribution functions are transformed to transverse-momentum and pseudorapidity space, and compared with charged-hadron data from the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic diffusion model with cylindrical symmetry for charged-hadron production in heavy-ion collisions, where the drift coefficient is determined from a fluctuation-dissipation relation using a thermal equilibrium state that includes collective expansion and a smooth transition to power-law tails at high transverse momentum, rather than from first-principles QCD.

## Method

A Fokker-Planck equation in two-dimensional rapidity space (transverse rapidity h and longitudinal rapidity y) is obtained from a Kramers-Moyal expansion of a stochastic drift-diffusion process for relativistic phase-space trajectories, assuming constant diagonal diffusivity. The initial state is based on QCD parton distribution functions, while the asymptotic equilibrium state is modeled as a generalized Maxwell-Juttner distribution with cylindrical Bjorken-type longitudinal flow and a power-law transverse velocity profile, using a Hagedorn-type exponential sequence to replace the thermal exponential for high-momentum tails. The Fokker-Planck equation is solved numerically, and the resulting distribution functions are transformed to transverse-momentum and pseudorapidity space for comparison with LHC data.

## Key numbers

- The infinite-temperature limit of the marginal longitudinal-rapidity PDF approaches \(1 / (2 \cosh(y)^2)\), with a maximum FWHM of \(2 \arcosh(\sqrt{2})\) and a maximum variance of \(\pi^2 / 12\), which is "significantly too narrow" to describe LHC charged-hadron data.
- The power-law exponent for the transverse velocity profile is chosen as \(\nu = 0.7\), based on analyses of LHC transverse-momentum data from ALICE (2013, 2020).
- Comparisons are made with ALICE charged-hadron production data at center-of-mass energies of 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair, and with ATLAS joint distribution measurements.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic diffusion model (Wolschin, 2015, 2016) from one-dimensional rapidity space to two dimensions (transverse and longitudinal rapidity), placing it on a firmer nonequilibrium-statistical foundation using stochastic calculus (Debbasch et al., 1997; Dunkel and Hanggi, 2005, 2009). It incorporates a QCD-based initial state from the color-glass condensate framework (Mehtar-Tani and Wolschin, 2009) and a fluctuation-dissipation relation previously developed for stopping distributions (Hoelck and Wolschin, 2020). The model enables native computation of both marginal and joint particle distributions, allowing direct comparison with ALICE and ATLAS data, and can be extended to nonconstant, nondiagonal diffusivity in future work.
