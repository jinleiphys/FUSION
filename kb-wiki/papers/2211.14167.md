# Exotic properties of N^*(1895) and its impact on photoproduction of light hyperons

> In this work, we outline the findings of our recent study of the properties of $N^*(1895)$ and its consequential impacts on the cross sections of the photoproduction of $Λ(1405)$. Further, we discuss the possibility of the existence of an isovector state, with the mass similar to $Λ(1405)$, which we refer to as $Σ(1400)$. With the idea of motivating experimental investigations of $Σ(1400)$, we have studied its photoproduction process and determined the respective cross sections and polarization observables. We have studied also the coupling of $N^*(1895)$ to $KΣ(1400)$ and found that it gives an important contribution to the cross sections near the threshold. In the process, we have determined the branching ratios of the decay of $N^*(1895)$, to the final states involving $Λ(1405)$ and $Σ(1400)$, to be in the range of 6-7 MeV. Our findings can motivate consideration of alternative processes in partial wave analyses of experimental data, when studying the properties of $N^*(1895)$.

# Exotic properties of N*(1895) and its impact on photoproduction of light hyperons

## Key claim

The nucleon resonance N*(1895) couples strongly to the light hyperons Λ(1405) and a proposed isovector state Σ(1400), with branching ratios for decays to these hyperons comparable to those for other meson-baryon channels; however, the existence of Σ(1400) remains uncertain and requires further experimental confirmation, and the properties of N*(1895) itself carry large uncertainties because multiple 1/2- states near 1890 MeV are catalogued together by the Particle Data Group.

## Method

The authors employed a coupled-channel meson-baryon approach using effective Lagrangians for pseudoscalar-baryon, vector-baryon, and pseudoscalar-vector-baryon interactions, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation to generate dynamically the resonances N*(1895), Λ(1405), and Σ(1400). They then calculated the decay widths of N*(1895) to KΛ(1405) and KΣ(1400) via triangular loop diagrams involving intermediate meson-baryon channels, and used these couplings together with vector-meson dominance for radiative decays to compute photoproduction cross sections for γp → K+Λ(1405) and γp → K+Σ(1400), extending the model beyond threshold using a Regge approach for kaon and K* exchange.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratios for N*(1895) decay to KΛ(1405): 5.4 ± 0.7% (first pole) and 1.8 ± 0.1% (second pole); to KΣ(1400): 5.9 ± 0.8% (first pole) and 11.2 ± 1.1% (second pole)
- Branching ratios for N*(1895) decay to πN: 9.4% (first pole) and 10.8% (second pole), consistent with experimental range 2-18%
- Branching ratios for N*(1895) decay to ηN: 2.7% (first pole) and 18.1% (second pole), consistent with experimental range 15-40%
- Branching ratios for N*(1895) decay to KΛ: 10.9% (first pole) and 19.4% (second pole), consistent with experimental range 13-23%
- Branching ratios for N*(1895) decay to KΣ: 0.7% (first pole) and 26.0% (second pole), consistent with experimental range 6-20%
- Radiative decay width Λ(1405) → Λγ: 26.19 ± 6.93 keV, in agreement with PDG value 27 ± 8 keV
- Radiative decay width N*(1895) → pγ: 650.70 ± 65.10 keV
- Radiative decay width Σ(1400) → Λγ: 49.97 ± 8.57 keV
- Radiative decay width Σ(1400) → Σγ: 94.51 ± 9.33 keV
- Pole positions: N*(1895) at 1801 − i96 MeV and 1912 − i54 MeV; Λ(1405) at 1385 − i124 MeV and 1426 − i15 MeV; Σ(1400) at 1399 − i36 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel studies of meson-baryon dynamics (Khemchandani 2013, Khemchandani 2018, Khemchandani 2020) which dynamically generated N*(1895), Λ(1405), and the proposed Σ(1400) state. The results contradict simpler quark model predictions (Isgur 1978, Bijker 1994, Hosaka 1997, Takayama 1999) that place the third S11 nucleon resonance near 2 GeV, and instead support a molecular interpretation of these resonances. The calculated photoproduction cross sections for γp → K+Λ(1405) agree with CLAS data (Moriya 2013), and the predictions for γp → K+Σ(1400) provide testable observables for facilities such as ELSA, potentially establishing the existence of the isovector hyperon and motivating alternative interpretations in partial wave analyses of experimental data.
