# Recent Progress in Understanding Nucleosynthesis via Rapid Neutron Capture

> I discuss the recent progress in our understanding of nucleosynthesis via rapid neutron capture, the r-process, based on meteoritic data for the early solar system and observations of stars at low metallicities. At present, all data require that there be two distinct kinds of r-process events and suggest that supernovae are associated with these events. The diversity of supernova sources for the r-process may depend on whether a neutron star or black hole is formed in an individual supernova. This dependence, if substantiated by future observations discussed here, has important implications for properties of nuclear matter.

# Recent Progress in Understanding Nucleosynthesis via Rapid Neutron Capture

## Key claim
All available meteoritic and stellar data require two distinct kinds of r-process events, and the diversity of supernova sources for the r-process may depend on whether a neutron star or black hole is formed in an individual supernova, but this dependence remains conditional on future observational confirmation.

## Method
The paper analyzes meteoritic data on the radioactive nuclei 129I and 182Hf to infer injection frequencies into the interstellar medium, then compares these frequencies with supernova occurrence rates. It further examines spectroscopic abundances of r-process elements (e.g., Eu, Ba, Pd, Ag, Cd) in metal-poor stars to test whether a single or multiple r-process templates are required, and uses the inferred event frequencies to predict the r-process yield per supernova and the required duration of neutrino-driven winds.

## Key numbers
- 129I injection frequency into the ISM: f_L ~ (10^8 yr)^(-1) (low-frequency events)
- 182Hf injection frequency into the ISM: f_H ~ (10^7 yr)^(-1) (high-frequency events)
- Supernova frequency over the Galactic volume of ~10^3 kpc^3: ~(30 yr)^(-1)
- Present Galactic inventory of light (100 ≤ A ≤ 130) or heavy (A > 130) r-process nuclei: ~4 × 10^3 M_sun
- Required r-process ejecta per supernova: ~10^(-5) M_sun for H events, ~10^(-4) M_sun for L events
- Neutron star gravitational binding energy: ~10^53 erg
- Average neutrino luminosity per species: ~10^51 erg s^(-1)
- Typical mass ejection rate in neutrino-driven wind: ~10^(-5) M_sun s^(-1)
- Required wind duration: ~1 s for H events, ~10 s for L events
- Predicted Eu abundance from a single H event: log_epsilon_H(Eu) ~ -2.5
- Predicted Ag abundance from a single L event: log_epsilon_L(Ag) ~ -0.8

## Context
This work extends the foundational nucleosynthesis framework of Burbidge et al. (1957) and Cameron (1957) by using meteoritic data from Wasserburg, Busso, and Gallino (1996) and Lee and Halliday (1995) to demonstrate that two distinct r-process event classes exist. It confirms and quantifies the two-component r-process model using stellar observations of metal-poor stars such as CS 22892-052 from Sneden et al. (2000). The paper enables future observational tests linking neutron star versus black hole formation to r-process yields, and proposes searching for r-process abundance anomalies in binary companions to neutron stars or black holes as proposed by Qian (2000).
