# Charmed baryon resonances with heavy-quark symmetry

> We study charmed baryon resonances that are generated dynamically from a coupled-channel unitary approach that implements heavy-quark symmetry. Some states can already be identified with experimental observations, such as $Λ_c(2595)$, $Λ_c(2660)$, $Σ_c(2902)$ or $Λ_c(2941)$, while others need a compilation of more experimental data as well as an extension of the model to include higher order contributions. We also compare our model to previous SU(4) schemes.

# Charmed baryon resonances with heavy-quark symmetry

## Key claim
The SU(8) spin-flavor extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa meson-baryon interaction, which incorporates heavy-quark symmetry, dynamically generates charmed baryon resonances that can be identified with several experimental states, but the identification of some resonances (such as the Sigma_c(2800) and Lambda_c(2940)) remains uncertain and requires either three-body decay channels, higher-multipolarity interactions, or more experimental statistics to be confirmed.

## Method
The authors construct an SU(8) spin-flavor symmetric extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian for s-wave meson-baryon scattering, then break the symmetry by using physical hadron masses and meson decay constants in the tree-level amplitudes. They solve the coupled-channel on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation with a one-subtraction regularization scheme, where the subtraction point is fixed by reproducing the mass of the well-established Lambda_c(2595) resonance. Resonance masses, widths, and coupling constants are extracted from pole positions in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- Lambda_c(2595) resonance in I=0, J=1/2 sector: width of 0.58 MeV (experimental width is 3.6 +2.0/-1.3 MeV)
- Lambda_c(2660) resonance in I=0, J=3/2 sector: width of 38 MeV (candidate for experimental Lambda_c(2625) with width less than 2 MeV)
- Sigma_c(2902) resonance in I=1, J=3/2 sector: candidate for experimental Sigma_c(2800)
- Lambda_c(2938) resonance in I=0, J=1/2 sector: cannot correspond to experimental Lambda_c(2940) due to coupling pattern
- Sigma_c(2974) resonance in I=1, J=1/2 sector: might correspond to enhancement in D+ p histogram around 2860 MeV with width of 10 MeV
- Sigma_c(2550) resonance in I=1, J=3/2 sector: identified as charm counterpart of Sigma(1670)
- Subtraction point parameter alpha = 0.9698, adjusted to reproduce Lambda_c(2595) position

## Context
This work extends the SU(6) spin-flavor approach previously applied to strange baryon resonances by Garcia-Recio et al. (2005, 2006) and Toki et al. (2007) to four flavors including charm, improving on earlier SU(4) vector-meson exchange models by Hofmann et al. and by Angels-Mizutani et al. that were not consistent with heavy-quark symmetry. The key difference from SU(4) models is that the SU(8) scheme includes vector mesons and uses heavy-meson decay constants in transition amplitudes, leading to a different interpretation of the Lambda_c(2595) as a predominantly D* N quasibound state rather than a D N state. The work enables future studies incorporating three-body decay channels, higher-multipolarity interactions, and medium modifications to better match experimental spectra.
