# Non-equilibrium study of the Chiral Magnetic Effect from real-time simulations with dynamical fermions

> We present a real-time lattice approach to study the non-equilibrium dynamics of vector and axial charges in $SU(N) \times U(1)$ gauge theories. Based on a classical description of the non-Abelian and Abelian gauge fields, we include dynamical fermions and develop operator definitions for (improved) Wilson and overlap fermions that allow us to study real-time manifestations of the axial anomaly from first principles. We present a first application of this approach to anomalous transport phenomena such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and Chiral Separation Effect (CSE) by studying the dynamics of fermions during and after a $SU(N)$ sphaleron transition in the presence of a $U(1)$ magnetic field. We investigate the fermion mass and magnetic field dependence of the suggested signatures of the CME and CSE and point out some important aspects which need to be accounted for in the macroscopic description of anomalous transport phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper presents a real-time lattice approach to study the non-equilibrium dynamics of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and Chiral Separation Effect (CSE) from first principles, but only for a single SU(2) sphaleron transition in a constant external U(1) magnetic field, not yet for the full QCD plasma conditions relevant to heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors perform classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations of SU(2) × U(1) gauge theories with dynamical fermions, treating the non-Abelian and Abelian gauge fields as classical background fields while solving the operator Dirac equation for fermions via mode function expansion. They implement two fermion discretization schemes: improved Wilson fermions (with tree-level O(a³) and O(a⁵) improvement) and overlap fermions with exact chiral symmetry, and define vector and axial current operators for both. The dynamics of a single sphaleron transition is mimicked by constructing a smooth transition between topologically distinct classical vacua over a time scale t_sph, while a constant homogeneous U(1) magnetic field is imposed along the z-direction.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a_t = 0.02 a_s is used for the leap-frog time discretization of the Dirac equation.
- The Zolotarev rational function approximation for the overlap operator uses N_O = 20 terms, achieving |sign(H_W)² - 1| < 10⁻⁹.
- The overlap Dirac operator satisfies the Ginsparg-Wilson relation to a precision of O(10⁻⁹) without magnetic field and 10⁻⁸ with magnetic field.
- The domain wall height M is chosen in the range M ∈ [1.4, 1.6) for optimal approximation of the sign function.
- The conjugate gradient convergence criterion is set to |H_W(M)²| - 1 < 10⁻¹⁶, with a maximum of 2000 steps.

## Context

This work extends the classical-statistical lattice approach previously applied to strong field QED (Mueller 2016, Kasper 2014) and cold electroweak baryogenesis (Saffin 2011) to the study of anomalous transport in SU(N_c) × U(1) theories. It provides a detailed follow-up to the authors' earlier letter (Mueller 2016) and enables future first-principles studies of the real-time dynamics of axial charge production and CME signals in more realistic setups, including self-consistent back-reaction of fermions on gauge fields and application to the SU(3) × U(1) case relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
