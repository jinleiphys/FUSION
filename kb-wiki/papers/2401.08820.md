# Searching for the QCD critical point using Lee-Yang edge singularities

> Using $N_f=2+1$ QCD calculations at physical quark mass and purely imaginary baryon chemical potential, we locate Lee-Yang edge singularities in the complex chemical potential plane. These singularities have been obtained by the multi-point Padé approach applied to the net baryon number density. We recently showed that singularities extracted with this approach are consistent with universal scaling near the Roberge-Weiss transition. Here we study the universal scaling of these singularities in the vicinity of the QCD critical endpoint. Making use of an appropriate scaling ansatz, we extrapolate these singularities on $N_τ=6$ and $N_τ=8$ lattices towards the real axis to estimate the position of a possible QCD critical point. We find an approach toward the real axis with decreasing temperature. We compare this estimate with a HotQCD estimate obtained from poles of a [4,4]-Padé resummation of the eighth-order Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure.

# Searching for the QCD critical point using Lee-Yang edge singularities

## Key claim
Using multi-point Padé approximations applied to lattice QCD data at purely imaginary baryon chemical potential, the authors extrapolate Lee-Yang edge singularities toward the real axis to estimate the location of a possible QCD critical endpoint. The result is explicitly preliminary, with the authors stating they "currently do not have a strong reason to favor one estimate over the other" between their own and the HotQCD estimate, and that the picture "is subject to change" as lower-temperature data and refined error analysis become available.

## Method
The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2+1 flavors of HISQ quarks at physical quark mass on 36^3 x 6 lattices at purely imaginary baryon chemical potential, generating configurations at four temperatures between 136.1 and 166.6 MeV. They apply multi-point Padé approximations to the net baryon number density to locate Lee-Yang edge singularities in the complex chemical potential plane, then fit the temperature dependence of these singularities using a scaling ansatz based on the 3-d Z_2 universality class to extrapolate toward the real axis and estimate the critical endpoint location.

## Key numbers
- Temperature ensembles: 166.6, 157.5, 145.0, and 136.1 MeV, with 1800 to 16000 configurations per ensemble
- Averaged preliminary estimate: (T_CEP, mu_CEP) approximately (85 MeV, 550 MeV) with mu_CEP/T_CEP approximately 6.5
- HotQCD [4,4]-Padé extrapolation alone gives approximately (100 MeV, 600 MeV)
- Bielefeld-Parma multi-point Padé extrapolation alone gives approximately (70 MeV, 500 MeV)
- The universal critical exponent product beta*delta is approximately 1.56 for the 3-d Z_2 universality class

## Context
This work extends the multi-point Padé approach previously tested on the 1-d Thirring model (DiRenzo 2020, Dimopoulos 2021) and 2-d Ising model (DiRenzo 2023, Singh 2023), and previously applied to locate the Roberge-Weiss transition (Dimopoulos 2021). The results are compared with a HotQCD estimate obtained from [4,4]-Padé resummation of eighth-order Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure (Bollweg 2022), and with other estimates from Dyson-Schwinger equations (Gunkel 2021, Gao 2020), functional renormalization group (Fu 2019), and black hole engineering (Critelli 2017). The authors plan to add lower-temperature data points and eventually perform a continuum-limit extrapolation, as well as investigate the chiral transition with the same approach.
