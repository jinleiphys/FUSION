# Quasi-Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Reactions

> The quasi-elastic contribution of the nuclear inclusive electron scattering model developed in A. Gil, J. Nieves, and E. Oset: Nucl. Phys. A 627 (1997) 543; is extended to the study of electroweak Charged Current (CC) induced nuclear reactions at intermediate energies of interest for future neutrino oscillation experiments. The model accounts for long range nuclear (RPA) correlations, Final State Interaction and Coulomb corrections. RPA correlations are shown to play a crucial role in the whole range of neutrino energies, up to 500 MeV, studied in this work. Predictions for inclusive muon capture for different nuclei, and for the reactions $^{12}$C$(ν_μ,μ^-)X$ and $^{12}$C$(ν_e,e^-)X$ near threshold are also given.

# Quasi-Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Reactions

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously successful model of inclusive electron scattering (Gil, Nieves, and Oset, 1997) to charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions, showing that long-range RPA correlations are crucial for describing data across the entire neutrino energy range up to 500 MeV, but the model's predictions for muon capture widths in carbon disagree with experiment by about 15%, the largest discrepancy among the nuclei studied.

## Method

The model computes the inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross section from the imaginary part of the neutrino self-energy in nuclear matter, using a local Fermi gas description of the nucleus. The hadronic tensor is obtained from the W-boson self-energy in the medium, including 1p1h excitations with RPA correlations (ph and Delta-hole chains), final state interactions via dressed nucleon propagators with realistic self-energies, and Coulomb corrections for the outgoing charged lepton. The effective particle-hole interaction uses a Landau-Migdal form with explicit pion and rho exchanges in the S=1, T=1 channel, and the correct energy balance is enforced by modifying the energy-conserving delta function for the Q-value of the reaction.

## Key numbers

- Flux-averaged cross section for 12C(nu_mu,mu-)X: RPA prediction 11.9 x 10^-40 cm^2, compared to LSND 2002 measurement of 10.6 +/- 0.3 +/- 1.8 x 10^-40 cm^2
- Flux-averaged cross section for 12C(nu_e,e-)X: RPA prediction 0.14 x 10^-40 cm^2, compared to LAMPF measurement of 0.141 +/- 0.023 x 10^-40 cm^2
- Muon capture width in 12C: RPA prediction 3.21 x 10^4 s^-1, experiment 3.78 +/- 0.03 x 10^4 s^-1 (15% discrepancy)
- Muon capture width in 208Pb: RPA prediction 1310 x 10^4 s^-1, experiment 1311 +/- 8 x 10^4 s^-1 (0% discrepancy)
- Muon capture width in 40Ca: RPA prediction 257.9 x 10^4 s^-1, experiment 252.5 +/- 0.6 x 10^4 s^-1 (2% discrepancy)
- For 16O(nu_e,e-)X at 400 MeV neutrino energy: RPA+FSI cross section 303.6 x 10^-40 cm^2, compared to Pauli-only result 352.5 x 10^-40 cm^2
- For 16O(nu_e,e-)X at 220 MeV neutrino energy: RPA+FSI cross section 92.8 x 10^-40 cm^2, compared to Pauli-only result 121.2 x 10^-40 cm^2

## Context

This work extends the inclusive electron scattering model of Gil, Nieves, and Oset (1997) and the photo-nuclear model of Carrasco and Oset (1992) to charged-current neutrino reactions, building on the nuclear microscopic framework developed by Oset, Toki, and Weise (1982). The model successfully describes LSND and KARMEN measurements of neutrino-carbon reactions near threshold and provides muon capture predictions across the periodic table that agree with data to within 15% or better. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments at intermediate energies and establish that RPA correlations, not just Pauli blocking, are essential for accurate cross sections; future work could extend this approach to neutral currents and nucleon knockout reactions as done in Nieves et al. (2005).
