# The Falsification of Nuclear Forces

> We review our work on the statistical uncertainty analysis of the NN force. This is based on the Granada-2013 database where a statistically meaningful partial wave analysis comprising a total of 6713 np and pp published scattering data from 1950 till 2013 below pion production threshold has been made. We stress the necessary conditions required for a correct and self-consistent statistical interpretation of the discrepancies between theory and experiment which enable a subsequent statistical error propagation and correlation analysis

## Key claim

The paper argues that a correct statistical uncertainty analysis of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) force requires not only a good fit to experimental scattering data (not just phase-shifts) but also that the residuals between theory and experiment follow a standard normal distribution, a condition that has been overlooked in most prior NN potential work.

## Method

The authors constructed a self-consistent database of 6713 np and pp scattering data points from 1950 to 2013 below pion production threshold, using an iterative 3-sigma criterion to exclude inconsistent data sets. They fitted a delta-shell potential with one-pion exchange (OPE) for the long-range part, using 46 parameters, and then applied normality tests (including a Tail-Sensitive test) to the residuals of six different potentials fitted to this database.

## Key numbers

- The Granada-2013 database contains 6713 np and pp published scattering data points from 1950 to 2013 below pion production threshold.
- The delta-shell plus OPE fit to the self-consistent database uses 46 parameters and yields a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.04.
- The iterative 3-sigma criterion recovered 300 data points that were initially discarded by the standard 3-sigma criterion.
- Six potentials (DS-OPE, DS-chiTPE, Gauss-OPE, DS-Born, Gauss-chiTPE, Gauss-Born) were found to have standard normal residuals, qualifying them for error estimation.
- Systematic uncertainties between different potentials are typically an order of magnitude larger than the statistical error bars.

## Context

This work extends the partial wave analysis tradition of the Nijmegen group (Stoks, 1993), who first achieved a chi-squared per degree of freedom below 1 by applying a 3-sigma criterion to exclude over 1000 inconsistent data. The paper contrasts with potentials fitted only to phase-shifts, such as the local chiral potential of Gezerlis (2014), which yields a significantly large chi-squared when compared to experimental data. The results enable Monte Carlo re-sampling of experimental data for robust uncertainty propagation, as demonstrated in a calculation of the triton binding energy statistical uncertainty (Perez, 2014).
