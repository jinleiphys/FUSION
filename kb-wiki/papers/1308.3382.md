# The $γp \to n a^+_2(1320) \to n ρ^0 π^+$ reactions within an effective Lagrangian approach

> We investigate the $a_2(1320)$ meson photon production in the $γp \to n a^+_2(1320)$ and $γp \to n ρ^0 π^+$ reactions within the effective Lagrangian method. For $γp \to n a^+_2(1320)$ reaction, by considering the contributions only from the $t-$channel $π^+$ exchange, we get a fairly good description of the current experimental total cross section data. We also studied the $γp \to n ρ^0 π^+$ reaction, which mainly contribute to the $γp \to n π^+ π^+ π^-$ reaction. The latter reaction has measured by the CLAS Collaboration. The total cross sections, invariant mass distribution, and the Dalitz Plot of $γp \to n ρ^0 π^+$ reaction are shown, which can be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the $t$-channel $\pi^+$ exchange alone can provide a "fairly good description" of the existing experimental total cross section data for $\gamma p \to n a^+_2(1320)$, but this description depends on the choice of a cutoff parameter $\Lambda_\pi$ (values 0.5, 0.7, and 0.9 GeV produce different curves), and the $s$-channel and $u$-channel contributions are neglected due to scarce information.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach with the isobar model, computing tree-level Feynman diagrams for the $t$-channel $\pi^+$ exchange in $\gamma p \to n a^+_2(1320)$. They then extend this to the $\gamma p \to n \rho^0 \pi^+$ reaction by including the $a_2(1320)$ resonance decay via $a_2 \to \rho \pi$, with form factors for the exchanged pion, the $\rho$ meson, and the off-shell $a_2$ meson. The coupling constants are determined from the partial decay widths of $a_2 \to \pi \gamma$ and $a_2 \to \rho \pi \to \pi\pi\pi$, and the total cross sections are computed using a Monte Carlo multi-particle phase space integration.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $a_2(1320)$ meson: $M = 1318.3$ MeV; total decay width: $\Gamma_{a_2} = 107$ MeV.
- Branching ratio for $a_2 \to \pi \gamma$: $2.68 \times 10^{-3}$, yielding coupling constant $g_{a_2 \pi \gamma} = 1.08 \times 10^{-2}$.
- Partial decay width for $a_2 \to \rho \pi \to \pi\pi\pi$: $\Gamma_{a_2 \to \rho \pi \to \pi\pi\pi} = 75$ MeV; with cutoff $\Lambda_\rho = 1.0$ GeV, this gives $g^2_{a_2\rho\pi}/4\pi = 1.9 \times 10^{-4}$.
- The $\gamma p \to n \rho^0 \pi^+$ reaction is computed for beam energies $E_\gamma$ from the production threshold of $1.36$ GeV up to $6.0$ GeV.
- The $\rho^0 \pi^+$ invariant mass distribution and Dalitz plot are shown at beam energy $E_\gamma = 5.1$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological analysis of Szczepaniak (2001), which used vector meson dominance and predicted comparable production of the exotic $\pi_1(1600)$ and $a_2(1320)$ mesons, a result that disagrees with the CLAS measurements of Nozar (2008). The present calculation, using only $t$-channel pion exchange, provides a baseline for understanding the $a_2(1320)$ contribution to the $\gamma p \to n \pi^+ \pi^+ \pi^-$ reaction, and the predicted invariant mass distributions and Dalitz plots can be tested by future CLAS experiments.
