# The structure of $^{36}$Ca under the Coulomb magnifying glass

> Detailed spectroscopy of the neutron-deficient nucleus $^{36}$Ca was obtained up to 9 MeV using the $^{37}$Ca($p$,$d$)$^{36}$Ca and the $^{38}$Ca($p$,$t$)$^{36}$Ca transfer reactions. The radioactive nuclei, produced by the LISE spectrometer at GANIL, interacted with the protons of the liquid Hydrogen target CRYPTA, to produce light ejectiles (the deuteron $d$ or triton $t$) that were detected in the MUST2 detector array, in coincidence with the heavy residues %identified by a zero degree detection system. %States have been measured up to 9 MeV. Our main findings are: i) a similar shift in energy for the 1$^+_1$ and 2$^+_1$ states by about -250 keV, as compared to the mirror nucleus $^{36}$S, ii) the discovery of an intruder 0$^+_2$ state at 2.83(13) MeV, which appears below the first 2$^+$ state, in contradiction with the situation in $^{36}$S, and iii) a tentative 0$^+_3$ state at 4.83(17) MeV, proposed to exhibit a bubble structure with two neutron vacancies in the 2s$_{1/2}$ orbit. The inversion between the 0$^+_2$ and 2$^+_1$ states is due to the large mirror energy difference (MED) of -516(130) keV for the former. This feature is reproduced by Shell Model (SM) calculations, using the $sd$-$pf$ valence space, predicting an almost pure intruder nature for the 0$^+_2$ state, with two protons (neutrons) being excited across the $Z$=20 magic closure in $^{36}$Ca ($^{36}$S). This mirror system has the largest MEDs ever observed, if one excludes the few cases induced by the effect of the continuum.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first evidence of very large Mirror Energy Differences (MEDs) in the A=36, T=2 mirror pair 36Ca-36S, associated with shape coexistence, specifically finding that the 0+2 state in 36Ca lies below the 2+1 state (an inversion not seen in 36S) with a MED of -516(130) keV, which is among the largest ever observed if continuum-induced cases are excluded.

## Method

The neutron-deficient nucleus 36Ca was studied up to 9 MeV excitation energy using two transfer reactions: 37Ca(p,d)36Ca and 38Ca(p,t)36Ca. Radioactive beams of 37Ca and 38Ca, produced by fragmentation of a 40Ca beam at GANIL and selected by the LISE spectrometer, impinged on a liquid hydrogen target (CRYPTA); the outgoing light ejectiles (deuterons or tritons) were detected by the MUST2 detector array in coincidence with heavy residues identified by a zero-degree detection system. Excitation energies were reconstructed via the missing-mass method, and differential cross sections were compared to Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations to extract transferred angular momenta and neutron spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

* 0+2 state in 36Ca observed at 2.83(13) MeV, below the 2+1 state at 3.06(2) MeV, in contrast to 36S where the 0+2 lies at 3.346 MeV above the 2+1 at 3.291 MeV.
* Mirror Energy Difference (MED) for the 0+2 state: -516(130) keV; for the 2+1 state: -245(2) keV (using the more precise 2+1 energy of 3045(2) keV from Amthor et al. 2009); for the 1+1 state: -280(41) keV.
* Tentative 0+3 state at 4.83(17) MeV in 36Ca, proposed to exhibit a bubble structure with two neutron vacancies in the 2s1/2 orbit.
* Spectroscopic factor C2S for the ground state of 36Ca: 1.06(22) from the (p,d) reaction, matching the mirror value of 1.06 in 36S.
* Spectroscopic factors for the 2+1, 1+1, and 2+2 states: 0.66(14), 0.61(13), and 0.28(7), respectively.

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin symmetry breaking in mirror nuclei, particularly the phenomenon of large Mirror Energy Differences (MEDs) previously observed in A=13 and A=16 pairs (Thomas-Ehrmann shifts) and in rotational bands (Warner 2006, Lenzi 2001, Bentley 2006). The results contradict the situation in 36S where the 0+2 state lies above the 2+1 state, and are reproduced by Shell Model calculations using the sd-pf valence space (Caurier et al. 2014, Valiente-Dobon et al. 2018). The findings enable benchmarking of ab-initio calculations that treat all isospin-symmetry-breaking effects, and validate the use of mirror spectroscopic factors for determining unknown reaction cross sections in nuclear astrophysics (rp-process).
