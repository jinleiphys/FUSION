# Holographic description of elastic photon-proton and photon-photon scattering

> We investigate the elastic photon-proton and photon-photon scattering in a holographic QCD model, focusing on the Regge regime. Considering contributions of the Pomeron and Reggeon exchange, the total and differential cross sections are calculated. While our model involves several parameters, by virtue of the universality of the Pomeron and Reggeon, for most of them the values determined in the preceding study on the proton-proton and proton-antiproton scattering can be employed. Once the two adjustable parameters, the Pomeron-photon and Reggeon-photon coupling constant, are determined with the experimental data of the total cross sections, predicting the both cross sections in a wide kinematic region, from the GeV to TeV scale, becomes possible. We show that the total cross section data can be well described within the model, and our predictions for the photon-proton differential cross section are consistent with the data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that elastic photon-proton and photon-photon scattering in the Regge regime can be described by a holographic QCD model using Pomeron and Reggeon exchange, but only after the two adjustable parameters (Pomeron-photon and Reggeon-photon coupling constants) are fitted to total cross section data; the differential cross section predictions then follow without further adjustment.

## Method

The authors model the Pomeron exchange as a Reggeized spin-2 glueball propagator and the Reggeon exchange as a Reggeized vector meson propagator, combined with gravitational form factors for the proton and rho meson vertices. Using vector meson dominance, an energetic photon is treated as fluctuating into the lightest vector mesons, so gamma-proton and gamma-gamma scattering are approximated as rho-proton and rho-rho scattering. The total and differential cross sections are computed from the invariant amplitudes via the optical theorem, with most parameters taken from prior fits to proton-proton and proton-antiproton scattering data (Xie 2019, Liu 2022), while the two photon coupling constants are fitted to Particle Data Group total cross section data at sqrt(s) >= 5 GeV using the MINUIT package.

## Key numbers

- Pomeron intercept alpha_P(0) = 1.086, slope alpha'_P = 0.377 GeV^{-2}, and Pomeron-proton coupling lambda_{gpp} = 9.699 GeV^{-1}, taken from fits to pp and pbar-p data at high energies (Xie 2019)
- Reggeon intercept alpha_R(0) = 0.444, slope alpha'_R = 0.925 GeV^{-2}, and Reggeon-proton coupling lambda_{vpp} = 7.742, taken from fits to pp and pbar-p data at medium energies (Liu 2022)
- Pomeron-photon coupling lambda_{g gamma gamma} = 0.04078 +/- 7.6e-7 GeV^{-1}, determined in this work from total cross section data
- Reggeon-photon coupling lambda_{v gamma gamma} = 0.03447 +/- 4.9e-6, determined in this work from total cross section data
- Differential cross sections are predicted for |t| range 0.01 to 0.45 GeV^2 at sqrt(s) >= 5 GeV, with comparisons shown at sqrt(s) = 5.725 GeV and other energies

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach previously applied to elastic proton-proton and proton-antiproton scattering by Liu (2022) and Xie (2019), now applying it to photon-involved processes using vector meson dominance as formulated by Sakurai (1960). The model enables predictions for gamma-proton and gamma-gamma differential cross sections in a wide kinematic region from GeV to TeV scales, which is significant because currently available differential cross section data are quite limited; future data could further test the model and deepen understanding of the photon structure.
