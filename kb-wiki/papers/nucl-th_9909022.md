# A new resonance in K+Lambda electroproduction: the D_{13}(1895) and its electromagnetic form factors

> New SAPHIR p(gamma,K+)Lambda total cross section data show a resonance structure at a total c.m. energy around 1900 MeV. We investigate this feature with an isobar model and find that the structure can be well explained by including a new D_{13} resonance at 1895 MeV. Such a state has been predicted by a relativistic quark model at 1960 MeV with significant gamma-N and K-Lambda branching ratios. We demonstrate how the measurement of single and double polarization observables can be used to obtain additional information on this resonance. Using recent (e,e'K+) JLab data from Hall C we extract the electromagnetic form factors of this state.

# A new resonance in K+Lambda electroproduction: the D13(1895) and its electromagnetic form factors

## Key claim
The structure observed in SAPHIR p(gamma,K+)Lambda total cross section data at a center-of-mass energy around 1900 MeV can be well explained by including a new D13 resonance at 1895 MeV, though the authors note that the quantitative agreement with quark model predictions for the decay widths is "probably accidental."

## Method
The authors used an isobar model with energy-dependent widths and partial branching fractions in resonance propagators, including the S11(1650), P11(1710), and P13(1720) resonances, plus standard Born terms and K*(892) and K1(1270) vector meson poles in the t-channel. They extended the model to finite momentum transfer by fixing all hadronic couplings and photocouplings from photoproduction data, then allowing only the electromagnetic resonance transition form factors to vary when fitting the JLab Hall C p(e,e'K+)Lambda electroproduction data.

## Key numbers
- The new D13 resonance mass is determined to be 1895 MeV from the fit to SAPHIR data
- The extracted value of sqrt(Gamma_{N*N gamma} Gamma_{N*K Lambda})/Gamma_{N*} for the D13(1895) is 2.29 (+0.72/-0.20) x 10^{-3}, compared to the quark model prediction of -2.72 +/- 0.73 x 10^{-3}
- For the S11(1650), the extracted value is -4.83 +/- 0.05 x 10^{-3} versus quark model -4.26 +/- 0.98 x 10^{-3}
- For the P11(1710), the extracted value is 1.03 +/- 0.17 x 10^{-3} versus quark model -0.54 +/- 0.12 x 10^{-3}
- For the P13(1720), the extracted value is 1.17 +/- 0.04 x 10^{-3} versus quark model -1.29 +/- 0.24 x 10^{-3}
- The monopole form factor cut-offs for meson resonances are Lambda = 1.107 GeV for K*(892) and 0.525 GeV for K1(1270)
- The D13(1895) Dirac form factor parameters are Lambda_1 = 0.36 GeV with n_1 = 4, and Pauli form factor parameters are Lambda_2 = 1.21 GeV with n_2 = 4
- The JLab electroproduction data were taken at W = 1.83 GeV

## Context
This work extends the isobar model of fxlee99 to include a new D13 state, building on the quark model predictions of Capstick and Roberts (1994, 1998) which predicted a [D13]_3(1960) state with significant gamma N and K Lambda branching ratios. The results contradict the absence of such a state in previous pi N scattering analyses, supporting the idea that "missing" resonances couple strongly to K Lambda and K Sigma channels as suggested by Capstick (1998). The extracted form factors enable future experimental exploration of the small k^2 regime to provide stringent constraints, and the work suggests measurements of polarized photon asymmetry around W = 1900 MeV for the p(gamma,K+)Lambda reaction as a next step.
