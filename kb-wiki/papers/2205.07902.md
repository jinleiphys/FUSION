# Quantum Simulation of Light-Front QCD for Jet Quenching in Nuclear Environments

> We develop a framework to simulate jet quenching in nuclear environments on a quantum computer. The formulation is based on the light-front Hamiltonian dynamics of QCD. The Hamiltonian consists of three parts relevant for jet quenching studies: kinetic, diffusion and splitting terms. In the basis made up of $n$-particle states in momentum space, the kinetic Hamiltonian is diagonal. Matrices representing the diffusion and splitting parts are sparse. The diffusion part of the Hamiltonian depends on classical background gauge fields, which need to be sampled classically before constructing quantum circuits for the time evolution. The cost of the sampling scales linearly with the time length of the evolution and the momentum grid volume. The framework automatically keeps track of quantum interference and thus it can be applied to study the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect in cases with more than two coherent splittings, which is beyond the scope of state-of-the-art analyses, no matter whether the medium is static or expanding, thin or thick, hot or cold. We apply this framework to study a toy model and gluon in-medium radiation on a small lattice. The essence of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect is observed in the quantum simulation results of both the toy model and the gluon case, which is quantum decoherence caused by medium interactions that suppresses the total radiation probability.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a quantum simulation framework based on light-front Hamiltonian dynamics can study the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect for more than two coherent splittings with overlapped formation times, which is beyond the scope of state-of-the-art perturbative analyses, but the demonstration is limited to a toy model and a small lattice gluon radiation case, not full QCD jet quenching.

## Method

The author formulates jet quenching dynamics using the light-front Hamiltonian of QCD, decomposed into kinetic, diffusion, and splitting terms, with the diffusion part depending on classical background gauge fields that are sampled classically before constructing quantum circuits. The Hilbert space is built from n-particle states in momentum space, where the kinetic Hamiltonian is diagonal and the diffusion and splitting matrices are sparse, enabling Trotterized time evolution on a quantum computer. The framework is applied to a 5-qubit toy model of scalar particles in 2+1 dimensions and a 15-qubit gluon radiation system on a small 3-dimensional momentum lattice, with simulations run on the IBM Qiskit simulator.

## Key numbers

*   Toy model: 5 qubits encode a 2+1 dimensional system with one transverse direction, two levels each for longitudinal and transverse momenta, and both 1-particle and 2-particle states.
*   Gluon radiation: 15 qubits encode a 3-dimensional small momentum lattice with both 1-gluon and 2-gluon states.
*   Qubit cost estimate: approximately 50 qubits for one initial parton with one splitting, approximately 75 qubits for two splittings, and approximately 100 qubits for three splittings, assuming N^+ = N_perp = 100.
*   Medium temperature range for QGP fireball: approximately 150 to 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quantum simulation approach of Barata (2021), which studied only the jet quenching parameter (kinetic and diffusive parts without radiation), by including the splitting Hamiltonian to treat multiple coherent radiations beyond perturbation theory. The framework aims to overcome the extreme complexity of analyzing the LPM effect for more than two coherent splittings, which has been attempted in recent perturbative studies by Arnold (2020, 2021, 2022) but remains extremely challenging, especially for time-dependent media. The paper enables future studies of quantum interference in jet quenching on fault-tolerant quantum computers with a few hundred logical qubits.
