# Explaining the hints for lepton flavour universality violation with three $S_2$ leptoquark generations

> Leptoquarks are prime candidates for explaining the intriguing hints for lepton flavour universality violation. In particular, the $SU(2)_L$ doublet of scalar leptoquarks $S_2$ is capable of providing an explanation for the tensions between the measurements and the Standard Model predictions in $(g-2)_μ$, $b\to s\ell^+ \ell^-$ and $b\to cτν$ processes, as well as in non-resonant di-electron production. However, in the minimal setup with a single leptoquark generation, a common explanation for all these issues is not possible as this would lead to unacceptably large charged lepton flavour violation. We therefore propose a model with three generations of $S_2$, each coupling exclusively to a single lepton flavour, \textit{i.e.}~a model extending the Standard Model particle content by an electroquark, a muoquark and a tauquark. We show that after taking into account other constraints, such as those originating from electroweak precision observables and $ΔF=2$ processes, it is possible to provide a combined explanation for all these hints of lepton flavour universality violation. Moreover, we find that the presence of the tauquark can generate a dimension-six ${\cal O}_9^U$ operator via off-shell photon penguin diagrams, which, together with the muoquark contribution, further improves the global fit to $b \to s \ell^+ \ell^-$ data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that introducing three generations of the $S_2$ scalar leptoquark, each coupling exclusively to a single lepton flavour (electron, muon, or tau), can jointly explain the observed hints for lepton flavour universality violation in $(g-2)_\mu$, $b\to s\ell^+ \ell^-$, $b\to c\tau\nu$, and non-resonant di-electron production, while avoiding the unacceptably large charged lepton flavour violation that would arise in a minimal single-generation setup.

## Method

The authors construct a model extending the Standard Model by three copies of the $SU(2)_L$ doublet scalar leptoquark $\Phi_2$ (also called $S_2$ or $R_2$), which they name the electroquark, muoquark, and tauquark, each coupling only to the corresponding lepton flavour. They match this ultraviolet theory onto the weak effective theory at the leptoquark mass scale, compute the Wilson coefficients for the relevant operators (including tree-level and one-loop contributions), and perform a phenomenological analysis using the `flavio` and `smelli` packages, incorporating constraints from electroweak precision observables, $\Delta F=2$ processes, parity violation measurements, and lepton electric dipole moments.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of single $S_2$ leptoquark models (e.g., Becirevic et al. 2017, Crivellin et al. 2021) that could explain individual anomalies but not all simultaneously due to charged lepton flavour violation constraints. The three-generation proposal is analogous to the flavour structure of minimal $R$-parity violating supersymmetry (Barger et al. 1989, Barbier et al. 2004) and can be enforced by a $L_\mu-L_\tau$ symmetry (He et al. 1990, Foot et al. 1990). The model enables a combined explanation of all current flavour anomalies and predicts correlated signals in $B \to K^{(*)} \tau^+ \tau^-$, $B_s \to \tau^+ \tau^-$, and $D_s \to \tau \nu$ decays that can be tested at future experiments.
