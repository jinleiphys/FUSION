# Flow and interferometry in 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics

> The expansion of the fireball created in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV is described in (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics with shear and bulk viscosities. We present results for the transverse momentum spectra, the directed and elliptic flow and the interferometry radii.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model with both shear and bulk viscosities can simultaneously describe transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, directed flow, and HBT interferometry radii for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, but the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow remains too flat even when accounting for increased shear viscosity in the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors solve second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamic equations in (3+1) dimensions starting from Glauber model initial conditions at proper time 0.6 fm/c, with a tilted initial density profile to generate directed flow. The evolution uses a temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy ratio (rising from 0.08 in the QGP phase to 0.26 at freeze-out) and a bulk viscosity to entropy ratio of 0.04 in the hadronic phase, with freeze-out at 135 MeV followed by resonance decays using the THERMINATOR2 Monte-Carlo generator.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio in QGP phase: η/s = 0.08 (the conjectured lower limit from Kovtun (2004))
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio at freeze-out (135 MeV): η/s = 0.26
- Bulk viscosity to entropy ratio in hadronic phase: ζ/s = 0.04
- Freeze-out temperature: 135 MeV for viscous hydrodynamics, 145 MeV for ideal fluid hydrodynamics
- Initial maximal energy density: 33 GeV/fm³ (corresponding to temperature 380 MeV)
- Initial proper time: τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Parameter for binary collision admixture in initial density: α = 0.125
- Spatial grid spacing: Δx = Δy = 0.24 fm
- Woods-Saxon nuclear density parameters: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, R_A = 6.38 fm, a = 0.535 fm
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section: 42 mb

## Context

This work extends previous (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Bozek (2009), Song (2007)) by implementing a full (3+1)-D code that does not assume boost-invariance, following the recent development of such codes by Schenke (2010). The inclusion of bulk viscosity in the hadronic phase allows a lower freeze-out temperature (135 MeV versus 145 MeV for ideal fluid) and improves agreement with both transverse momentum spectra and HBT radii simultaneously, partially addressing the HBT puzzle. The results enable future studies of directed flow as a sensitive probe of early-time pressure imbalances and of the pseudorapidity dependence of flow observables at LHC energies.
