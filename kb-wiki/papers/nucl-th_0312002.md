# Equation of state for nuclear matter based on density dependent effective interaction

> An interesting method of obtaining equation of state for nuclear matter, from a density dependent M3Y interaction, by minimizing the energy per nucleon is described. The density dependence parameters of the interaction are obtained by reproducing the saturation energy per nucleon and the saturation density of spin and isospin symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter. The nuclear matter equation of state thus obtained is then used to calculate the pressure, the energy density, the nuclear incompressibility and the velocity of sound in nuclear medium. The results obtained are in good agreement with experimental data and provide a unified description of radioactivity, scattering and nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper derives an equation of state (EOS) for symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter using a density-dependent M3Y effective interaction, with the density dependence parameters fixed by reproducing the saturation energy per nucleon and saturation density; the resulting incompressibility of about 300 MeV agrees with experimental giant monopole resonance data, but the EOS becomes superluminal at densities above about 5.2 times the saturation density, a limitation shared with other non-relativistic EOS.

## Method

The author starts from the M3Y-Reid-Elliott effective nucleon-nucleon interaction supplemented by a zero-range pseudopotential representing the single-nucleon exchange term, and introduces a density dependence of the form \(C(1 - \beta \rho^n)\) to account for higher-order exchange and Pauli blocking effects. The parameters \(C\) and \(\beta\) are determined by solving the saturation conditions (energy per nucleon \(\epsilon_0 = -16\) MeV and density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)) for symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter. Using these parameters, the energy per nucleon, pressure, energy density, nuclear incompressibility, and velocity of sound are calculated as functions of nucleonic density, with particular focus on the case \(n = 2/3\).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and saturation energy per nucleon \(\epsilon_0 = -16\) MeV used as input for symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter.
- For \(n = 2/3\), the density dependence parameters are \(\beta = 1.6257\) fm\(^2\) and \(C = 2.06\), yielding an incompressibility \(K_0 = 306.3\) MeV.
- The nuclear mean free path derived from \(\beta = 1.6257\) fm\(^2\) and \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is about 4 fm.
- The velocity of sound becomes imaginary for densities \(\rho \le 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\) and exceeds the speed of light at densities \(\rho \ge 5.2\rho_0\).
- Using an alternative saturation energy \(\epsilon_0 = -15.25\) MeV from a Bethe-Weizsacker fit to Audi-Wapstra masses, the incompressibility becomes \(K_0 = 295.1\) MeV.
- The theoretical incompressibility of about 300 MeV agrees with the experimental value \(K_0 = 300 \pm 25\) MeV from giant monopole resonance and \(K_0 = 290 \pm 50\) MeV from hard photon production in heavy ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends the use of the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) interaction, previously applied successfully to alpha and heavy ion elastic scattering (Satchler and Love, 1979; Kobos et al., 1984) and cluster radioactivity (Basu, 2003), to the calculation of the nuclear matter equation of state. The resulting incompressibility is in close agreement with the infinite nuclear matter model result of \(K_0 = 288 \pm 20\) MeV (Satpathy et al., 1999) and with experimental determinations from giant monopole resonances (Sharma et al., 1988) and hard photon production (Schutz et al., 1996). The unified description of scattering, radioactivity, and nuclear matter properties using a single realistic effective interaction enables consistent modeling of nuclear phenomena across different density regimes.
