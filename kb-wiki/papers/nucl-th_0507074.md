# The Observable of Lambda Polarity Caused by Source Global Angular Momentum Localizing in Peripheral Au-Au Collision at RHIC

> An observable to measure the polarity of lambda that caused by source global angular momentum in peripheral AU-AU collision at RHIC is proposed.This observable's capacity of measurement is tested by Monte Carlo method.And the main factors that influence the observable are also researched.This observable will give an effective proof of the formation of deconfined matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an observable, \(P(N)\), to measure the polarization of \(\Lambda\) hyperons caused by the global angular momentum of the source in peripheral Au-Au collisions at RHIC, but the method is only expected to yield a meaningful signal at the \(3\sigma\) confidence level if the product of the decay asymmetry parameter and the \(\Lambda\) polarization, \(\alpha P_{\Lambda}\), exceeds 0.2.

## Method

The authors define a reaction plane from the beam direction and impact parameter vector. For events containing exactly \(N\) \(\Lambda\) hyperons, they count the number of protons (a decay daughter) emitted above (\(N_{\uparrow}\)) and below (\(N_{\downarrow}\)) the reaction plane in the \(\Lambda\) rest frame. The observable \(P(N)\) is defined as the average over many events of the absolute difference \(|N_{\uparrow} - N_{\downarrow}|\). The value of \(P(N)\) for unpolarized \(\Lambda\)s is calculated analytically, and the sensitivity of \(P(N)\) to polarization is tested using a Monte Carlo simulation that generates proton directions according to the decay distribution \(P(\theta) = \frac{1}{2}(1 + \alpha P_{\Lambda} \cos\theta)\).

## Key numbers

*   The decay asymmetry parameter \(\alpha\) for \(\Lambda\) is 0.642.
*   For a feasible measurement with \(N=2\) and \(\alpha P_{\Lambda}=0.2\), the resolution of the reaction plane must be smaller than \(0.4\pi\).
*   The method can give a meaningful result at the \(3\sigma\) confidence level if \(\alpha P_{\Lambda} > 0.2\), which corresponds to \(P_{\Lambda} > 0.312\).
*   The influence of momentum resolution on \(P(2)\) is unconspicuous for resolutions from 0 to 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal by Liang and Wang (2004) that global angular momentum in peripheral heavy-ion collisions can lead to hyperon polarization, which would serve as evidence for the formation of deconfined matter. The paper provides a practical observable for experimental verification at RHIC, building on evidence for deconfinement from jet quenching and elliptic flow measurements. The proposed method enables a direct experimental test of the polarization signal, with the next step being its application to real data from the STAR experiment at 200 GeV.
