# About the magnitude of the $γ^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transverse amplitudes near $Q^2=0$

> The $γ^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transition has a property that differs from the other low-lying nucleon resonance amplitudes: the magnitude of the transverse helicity amplitudes.The transition helicity amplitudes are defined in terms of square-transfer momentum $q^2$, or $Q^2=-q^2$. Near the photon point ($Q^2=0$) there is a significant difference in the magnitude of the transverse amplitudes: $A_{3/2}$ is very large and $A_{1/2}$ is very small. This atypical behavior contrasts with the relation between the amplitudes at the pseudothreshold [the limit where the nucleon and the $N(1520)$ are both at rest and $Q^2 <0$], where $A_{3/2} = A_{1/2}/\sqrt{3}$, and also in the large-$Q^2$ region, where theory and data suggest that $A_{3/2}$ is suppressed relative to $A_{1/2}$. In the present work, we look for the source of the suppression of the $A_{1/2}$ amplitude at $Q^2=0$. The result is easy to understand in first approximation, when we look into the relation between the transverse amplitudes and the elementary form factors, defined by a gauge-invariant parametrization of the $γ^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transition current, near $Q^2=0$. There is a partial cancellation between contributions of two elementary form factors near $Q^2=0$. We conclude, however, that the correlation between the two elementary form factors at $Q^2=0$ is not sufficient to explain the transverse amplitude data below $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$. The description of the dependence of the transverse amplitudes on $Q^2$ requires the determination of the scale of variation of the elementary form factors in the range $Q^2=0$...0.5 GeV$^2$,a region with almost non existent data. We conclude at the end that the low-$Q^2$ data for the transverse amplitudes can be well described when we relate the scale of variation of the elementary form factors with the nucleon dipole form factor.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the suppression of the $A_{1/2}$ transverse helicity amplitude near $Q^2=0$ for the $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transition arises from a partial cancellation between two elementary form factors $G_1$ and $G_2$, but this correlation alone is insufficient to explain the data below $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$; the scale of variation of these form factors must also be determined, and a good description is achieved when that scale is related to the nucleon dipole form factor.

## Method

The author expresses the transverse helicity amplitudes $A_{1/2}$ and $A_{3/2}$ in terms of elementary form factors $G_1$, $G_2$, and $G_3$ defined by a gauge-invariant parametrization of the $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transition current. Using experimental data at $Q^2=0$ from the Particle Data Group (PDG), the values of $G_1(0)$ and $G_2(0)$ are extracted, and then multipole parametrizations of these form factors are tested against JLab/CLAS data for $Q^2$ up to 1 GeV$^2$, comparing constant form factor approximations with those having a universal cutoff scale derived from the nucleon dipole form factor.

## Key numbers

* $A_{3/2}(0) = +(140 \pm 5) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ and $A_{1/2}(0) = -(22.5 \pm 7.5) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ from PDG (2022), giving a ratio $A_{1/2}/A_{3/2} \simeq 0.18$ at the photon point.
* At the pseudothreshold ($Q^2 \simeq -0.38$ GeV$^2$), the relation $A_{3/2} = \sqrt{3} A_{1/2}$ holds, corresponding to $G_M = 0$.
* The dimensionless scaled amplitudes at $Q^2=0$ are $\tilde A_{3/2} = +0.498 \pm 0.018$ and $\tilde A_{1/2} = -0.080 \pm 0.027$, using $F_0 = 3.67 \sqrt{M}$.
* The extracted dimensionless elementary form factors at $Q^2=0$ (with $T_1 \neq 0$) are $M G_1(0) = 2.354$ and $M^2 G_2(0) = -1.260$, with relative uncertainties of 7.8% and 10.6% respectively.
* The nucleon dipole form factor cutoff is $\Lambda_D^2 = 0.71$ GeV$^2$, used as the universal scale for the multipole parametrizations of $G_i$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transition, which differs from other low-lying nucleon resonances like the $\Delta(1232)$, $N(1440)$, and $N(1535)$ in the magnitude disparity between $A_{3/2}$ and $A_{1/2}$ near the photon point. The study builds on the formalism of Devenish (1976) for transition form factors and the pseudothreshold constraints from Siegert's theorem, and it addresses the lack of data below $Q^2 = 0.25$ GeV$^2$ noted in reviews by the Particle Data Group (2022) and others. The results enable future experimental tests at facilities like MAMI or JLab-12 GeV in the low-$Q^2$ region, and the parametrizations may be refined once uncertainties in $A_{1/2}(0)$ and $A_{3/2}(0)$ are reduced.
