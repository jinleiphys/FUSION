# Final State Interactions in Pion Production from Nuclei

> We have calculated the effect of the inclusion of final state interactions on pion production from nuclei with incident pion beams. We find that the effect of absorption, along with geometric considerations, explains much of the enhancement at low invariant mass seen in recent data for invariant mass spectra with increasing atomic number. While the variation among nuclei is well reproduced for all charge states, the enhancement for the ratio for heavy nuclei to deuterium for the pi^+ pi^- final state is only partially understood.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of the low-invariant-mass region in two-pion spectra from heavy nuclei can be largely explained by the geometry of pion absorption in the final state, without invoking modifications of the pion-pion interaction in nuclear matter; however, the enhancement for the ratio of heavy nuclei to deuterium in the π⁺π⁻ channel is only partially reproduced by this mechanism.

## Method

The authors modeled pion production as a coherent process on the nucleus, assuming the reaction occurs at a fixed point on the nuclear surface and that the two-pion pair propagates forward in the beam direction. They propagated the two pions independently through Coulomb and strong nuclear potentials using classical equations of motion, and calculated an absorption probability per unit length proportional to the square of the nuclear density, with the proportionality constant λ₀(E) fitted to experimental absorption cross sections for light nuclei and for gold. The final invariant mass spectrum was obtained by integrating over all decay angles of the pions in the two-pion rest frame, weighting by the survival probabilities of both pions.

## Key numbers

- Incident pion kinetic energy: 283 MeV (for CHAOS experiment data on ²H, ¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb).
- Woods-Saxon density parameters (c, a) in fm for the nuclear strong density: C (2.25, 0.5), Al (2.95, 0.5), Ca (3.55, 0.54), Cu (4.23, 0.55), Pb (6.5, 0.55).
- Woods-Saxon charge density parameters (γ, α) in fm: C (2.44, 0.5), Al (3.07, 0.519), Ca (3.65, 0.54), Cu (4.252, 0.589), Pb (6.6, 0.55).
- Theoretical and experimental ratios normalized to unity at 330 MeV invariant mass.
- For the π⁺π⁻ final state, the absorption-plus-geometry effect explains "only a little less than half" of the observed experimental enhancement in the lead-to-deuterium ratio.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Oset and Vicente-Vacas (1990s), who used an eikonal approximation for pion propagation and only calculated for calcium, by performing full trajectory calculations for multiple nuclei (C, Al, Ca, Cu, Pb) and including both charged and neutral pion channels. The paper directly addresses the CHAOS collaboration data (1996-2001) and Crystal Ball data (1999), which had been interpreted by Rapp et al., Vicente-Vacas and Oset, Hatsuda et al., and others as evidence for chiral symmetry restoration or medium modification of the ππ interaction. The authors argue that their parameter-free geometric absorption model reproduces the variation among heavy nuclei, leaving open the question of whether additional medium effects are needed for the π⁺π⁻ channel when compared to deuterium.
