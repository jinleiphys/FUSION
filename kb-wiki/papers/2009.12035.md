# Chiral crossover transition from the Dyson-Schwinger equations in a sphere

> Within the framework of Dyson--Schwinger equations of QCD, we study the effect of finite volume on the chiral phase transition in a sphere with the MIT boundary condition. We find that the chiral quark condensate $\langle\barψ ψ\rangle$ and pseudotransition temperature $T_{pc}$ of the crossover decreases as the volume decreases, until there is no chiral crossover transition at last. We find that the system for $R = \infty $\ fm is indistinguishable from $R=10$ fm and there is a significant decrease in $T_{pc}$ with $R$ as $R<4$ fm. When $R<1.5$ fm, there is no chiral transition in the system.

## Key claim

The chiral pseudocritical temperature \(T_{pc}\) and quark condensate decrease with decreasing spherical volume under MIT boundary conditions, and the finite-volume effects become significant only for radii below 4 fm, with no chiral crossover transition occurring for radii below 1.5 fm; the abstract's claim that the system for \(R = \infty\) fm is indistinguishable from \(R = 10\) fm is a key qualifier, meaning that finite-volume effects are negligible above that radius.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for the dressed-quark propagator at finite temperature, using a simplified Maris-Tandy interaction kernel with parameters \(\omega = 0.5\) GeV and \(D_0\omega = (0.8\ \mathrm{GeV})^3\). To impose finite spherical volume, they discretized the three-momentum integral according to the MIT boundary condition, which yields allowed momentum modes from spherical Bessel function eigen-equations, and approximated the high-momentum part of the mode sum by an integral above a cutoff \(\lambda\). The chiral condensate and chiral susceptibility were computed from the dressed propagator, and the pseudocritical temperature was identified as the maximum of the susceptibility.

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperature in infinite volume: \(T_{pc} = 137\) MeV (with current-quark mass \(m = 4.8\) MeV, fitted to pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.135\) GeV and decay constant \(f_\pi = 0.095\) GeV).
* Finite-volume effects become significant for sphere radius \(R < 4\) fm (volume \(V \sim 270\ \text{fm}^3\)).
* No chiral crossover transition occurs for sphere radius \(R < 1.5\) fm (volume \(V \sim 14\ \text{fm}^3\)).
* The system is indistinguishable from infinite volume for \(R \geq 10\) fm (volume \(V \sim 4000\ \text{fm}^3\)).
* For a cubic box with anti-periodic boundary condition, the system can be regarded as infinite for side length \(L > 3\) fm (volume \(V \sim 27\ \text{fm}^3\)).

## Context

This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of finite-volume effects on the chiral transition, which were limited to cubic boxes with anti-periodic boundary conditions (Shi 2018, Li 2019, Luecker 2010). It also complements a study using the NJL model with MIT boundary conditions (Zhang 2020), which found similar qualitative behavior but with a larger marginal radius of 14 fm. The results indicate that the choice of boundary condition (MIT sphere versus anti-periodic cube) significantly affects the sensitivity of the chiral crossover to volume, and that spherical geometry is more realistic for modeling the fireball produced in heavy-ion collisions. The findings enable more accurate predictions for the chiral transition in small collision systems at RHIC and future facilities.
