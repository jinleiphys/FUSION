# Three-Body Coulomb Final-State Interaction Effects in the Coulomb Breakup of Light Nuclei

> Coulomb breakup of a projectile in the Coulomb field of a fully stripped heavy nucleus is at present one of the most popular experimental methods to obtain information on reactions of interest in nuclear astrophysics. Its theoretical interpretation presents, however, considerable difficulties, due to the three-body nature and the infinite range of the Coulomb forces involved. Among the uncertainties affecting present analyses, the possible modification of the dissociation cross section by three-body Coulomb final-state interactions plays a major role. Various methods which have been proposed to deal with it are briefly reviewed. However, none of them is based on a consistent and mathematically satisfactory quantum mechanical treatment, with the exception of the one proposed recently. The latter, being based on the prior form of the dissociation amplitude, makes use of a genuine three-charged particle wave function for the final state which is an exact solution of the three-body Schrödinger equation, albeit only asymptotically, i.e. for large distances. Nevertheless, interesting conclusion can be drawn concerning the influence of three-body Coulomb final-state interactions on quantities of astrophysical interest.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard Distorted-Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) used in Coulomb breakup analyses employs a factorized three-body final-state wave function that is not a physically admissible solution of the three-body Schrödinger equation, and that this approximation completely eliminates the post-decay acceleration (PDA) effect. The authors present an alternative asymptotic three-body wave function that preserves the genuine three-body Coulomb correlations and claim that its use in the prior-form dissociation amplitude provides a more consistent treatment, though the wave function is only asymptotically exact (valid for large distances).

## Method
The authors start from the prior-form dissociation amplitude and introduce an approximate three-charged-particle wave function for the final state that is an asymptotically exact solution of the three-body Schrödinger equation in the domain where the fragment separation is much smaller than the distance to the target. This wave function incorporates a local relative momentum between the fragments that depends on the distance to the target, capturing the post-decay acceleration effect. The amplitude is then expanded in multipoles, and for the numerical applications the wave function is expanded in powers of the small correction term to allow separation of the six-dimensional integral into products of three-dimensional integrals.

## Key numbers
- For the reaction ⁶Li + ²⁰⁸Pb → α + d + ²⁰⁸Pb at projectile energy E_i = 156 MeV and scattering angle θ = 3°, the calculated triple-differential cross section shows good agreement with experimental data for |E_αd| < 300 keV.
- The shift of the minimum in the coincidence spectrum for the reaction ⁷Li → α + t on ²⁰⁸Pb at E_i = 63 MeV was previously observed experimentally and is attributed to post-decay acceleration due to different charge-over-mass ratios of the fragments.
- For the ⁸B → ⁷Be + p breakup, experiments have been performed at beam energies of 46.5, 51.9, 83, 90, and 254 A·MeV at RIKEN, MSU, and GSI.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical treatments of Coulomb breakup that used the DWBA (Baur 1984, Rebel 1990, Bertulani 1991), the semiclassical approach (Baur 1994), and the continuum-discretized coupled channel method (Yahiro 1984, Mortimer 2002, Matsumoto 2003). The authors specifically critique the factorized final-state wave function used in standard DWBA calculations, noting that it does not match the Redmond three-body distorted wave (Redmond 1973) in the asymptotic region and thus eliminates PDA. The proposed asymptotic wave function, derived in earlier work by Alt (1993) and Mukhamedzhanov (1996), enables a systematic estimation of three-body Coulomb final-state interaction effects on astrophysical S-factors extracted from Coulomb breakup data, particularly for reactions such as ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B relevant to solar neutrino physics.
