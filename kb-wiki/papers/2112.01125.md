# Ab initio predictions link the neutron skin of ${}^{208}$Pb to nuclear forces

> Heavy atomic nuclei have an excess of neutrons over protons, which leads to the formation of a neutron skin whose thickness is sensitive to details of the nuclear force. This links atomic nuclei to properties of neutron stars, thereby relating objects that differ in size by orders of magnitude. The nucleus ${}^{208}$Pb is of particular interest because it exhibits a simple structure and is experimentally accessible. However, computing such a heavy nucleus has been out of reach for ab initio theory. By combining advances in quantum many-body methods, statistical tools, and emulator technology, we make quantitative predictions for the properties of ${}^{208}$Pb starting from nuclear forces that are consistent with symmetries of low-energy quantum chromodynamics. We explore $10^9$ different nuclear-force parameterisations via history matching, confront them with data in select light nuclei, and arrive at an importance-weighted ensemble of interactions. We accurately reproduce bulk properties of ${}^{208}$Pb and determine the neutron skin thickness, which is smaller and more precise than a recent extraction from parity-violating electron scattering but in agreement with other experimental probes. This work demonstrates how realistic two- and three-nucleon forces act in a heavy nucleus and allows us to make quantitative predictions across the nuclear landscape.

## Key claim

The authors present ab initio predictions for the neutron skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb that are smaller and more precise than the recent PREX extraction from parity-violating electron scattering, but consistent with other experimental probes; this result is conditional on the assumption that nuclear forces constrained by nucleon-nucleon scattering data and properties of light nuclei reliably extrapolate to heavy systems.

## Method

The authors construct chiral effective field theory interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order including the $\Delta$ isobar degree of freedom, with 17 low-energy constants (LECs). They use history matching with emulators based on eigenvector continuation to explore $10^9$ different nuclear-force parameterizations, confronting them with nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts up to 200 MeV and properties of ${}^{2}\text{H}$, ${}^{3}\text{H}$, ${}^{4}\text{He}$, and ${}^{16}\text{O}$. After five waves of history matching yielding 34 non-implausible LEC samples, they perform coupled-cluster, in-medium similarity renormalization group, and many-body perturbation theory calculations for ${}^{48}\text{Ca}$ and ${}^{208}\text{Pb}$, then calibrate the samples via importance resampling using ${}^{48}\text{Ca}$ data to produce posterior predictive distributions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of ${}^{208}\text{Pb}$: $R_\text{skin} = 0.14 - 0.20$ fm (68% credibility region)
- Slope parameter of the symmetry energy: $L = 37 - 66$ MeV
- Momentum transfer for PREX experiment: $Q_\text{PREX} = 0.3978(16)$ fm$^{-1}$
- Momentum transfer for CREX experiment: $Q_\text{CREX} = 0.873$ fm$^{-1}$
- The predicted $R_\text{skin}$ shows approximately $1.5\sigma$ tension with the PREX result from Adhikari (2021)
- The authors explored $10^9$ different nuclear-force parameterisations via history matching

## Context

This work extends ab initio coupled-cluster and IMSRG methods from medium-mass nuclei like tin isotopes (Morris 2018, Arthuis 2020) to the heavy doubly-magic nucleus ${}^{208}\text{Pb}$, a significant step in mass number. The results contradict the large neutron skin extracted by the PREX collaboration (Adhikari 2021) but agree with extractions from elastic proton scattering (Zenihiro 2010), antiprotonic atoms (Trzcinska 2001), and coherent pion photoproduction (Tarbert 2013). The framework enables predictions with quantified uncertainties across the nuclear chart and links nucleon-nucleon scattering data to neutron star properties via the neutron-matter equation of state.
