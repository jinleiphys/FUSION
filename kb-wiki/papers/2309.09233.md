# Effects of nuclear matter properties in neutron star mergers

> The dynamics in mergers of binary neutron star (BNS) systems depend sensitively on the equation of state (EOS) of dense matter. This has profound implications on the emission of gravitational waves (GWs) and the ejection of matter in the merger and post-merger phases and is thus of high interest for multi-messenger astronomy. Today, a variety of nuclear EOSs are available with various underlying microphysical models. This calls for a study to focus on EOS effects from different physical nuclear matter properties and their influence on BNS mergers. We perform simulations of equal-mass BNS mergers with a set of 9 different EOSs based on Skyrme density functionals. In the models, we systematically vary the effective nucleon mass, incompressibility, and symmetry energy at saturation density. This allows us to investigate the influence of specific nuclear matter properties on the dynamics of BNS mergers. We analyze the impact of these properties on the merger dynamics, the fate of the remnant, disk formation, ejection of matter, and gravitational wave emission. Our results indicate that some aspects of the merger are sensitive to the EOS around saturation density while others are sensitive to the behavior towards higher densities, e.g., characterized by the slope of the pressure as a function of density. The detailed density dependence of the EOS thus needs to be taken into account to describe its influence on BNS mergers.

# Effects of Nuclear Matter Properties in Neutron Star Mergers

## Key claim

The influence of the nuclear equation of state (EOS) on binary neutron star merger dynamics can be systematically understood by varying individual nuclear matter properties (effective nucleon mass, incompressibility, and symmetry energy at saturation density) rather than treating the EOS only through its overall softness or stiffness; however, the detailed density dependence of the EOS must be taken into account, as some merger aspects are sensitive to the EOS around saturation density while others are sensitive to behavior at higher densities.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of equal-mass binary neutron star mergers (each with mass 1.365 solar masses) using nine different EOS tables based on Skyrme density functionals generated with the open-source SROEOS code, systematically varying the effective nucleon mass, incompressibility, and symmetry energy at saturation density one parameter at a time from a fiducial model (LS220) toward the values of the Shen EOS. They analyzed the impact of these variations on merger dynamics, remnant fate, disk formation, matter ejection, and gravitational wave emission, comparing results to existing universal relations from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The fiducial model LSm has incompressibility K = 220 MeV and effective mass m* = 1.0 m_n (where m_n = 939.5654 MeV); variations include K = 175 MeV (LSl) and K = 255 MeV (LSh), and effective masses m* = 0.8 m_n (msm) and m* = 0.634 m_n (msS)
- The dominant post-merger gravitational wave frequency f2 ranges from 2.24 kHz (Shen EOS) to 3.10 kHz (LSm); the secondary peak f1 ranges from 1.66 kHz (SkShen) to 2.14 kHz (LSm)
- Existing fit formulae predict f2 and f1 frequencies within 5-10% uncertainty across all models
- The saturation density for most models is n0 = 0.155 fm^-3; for SkShen and Shen it is 0.145 fm^-3
- The pressure at saturation density (T=0, Ye=0.1) ranges from 3.17 MeV fm^-3 (LSl) to 5.11 MeV fm^-3 (SkShen)
- The dimensionless slope of the pressure at saturation density ranges from 2.77 (LSl) to 3.43 (msKS)

## Context

This work extends previous studies that characterized EOS effects in binary neutron star mergers primarily through neutron star radii or tidal deformability (Bauswein 2016, Takami 2015, Rezzolla 2016, Nedora 2021) by instead varying individual nuclear matter properties. The approach follows the methodology of Yasin (2020) using the SROEOS code and is similar to the setup of Fields (2023), though Fields constrained the L parameter and pressure at four times saturation density independently. The results enable direct connections between nuclear physics experiments, theoretical calculations from chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2011, Drischler 2016, 2019), and multi-messenger astrophysical observations, potentially allowing future gravitational wave detections of post-merger signals to constrain nuclear matter properties.
