# Hydrodynamical attractor and thermal particle production in heavy-ion collision

> Study of thermal particle production is crucial to understand the space-time evolution of the fireball produced in high energy heavy-ion collisions. We consider thermal particle production within the framework of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics and employ recently obtained analytical solutions of higher-order viscous hydrodynamics with longitudinal Bjorken expansion to calculate the spectra of dileptons and photons. Using these analytical solutions, we constrain the allowed initial states by demanding positivity and reality of energy density throughout the evolution. Further, we compute thermal particle spectra and study the particle yield in context of hydrodynamic attractors. We find that, of all allowed solutions, the evolution corresponding to attractor solution leads to maximum production of thermal particles.

## Key claim

The authors find that, among all physically allowed hydrodynamic solutions (constrained by positivity and reality of energy density), the evolution corresponding to the hydrodynamic attractor solution leads to the maximum production of thermal particles (dileptons and photons) in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors employ recently obtained analytical solutions of higher-order viscous hydrodynamics (MIS, DNMR, and third-order theories) for one-dimensional longitudinal Bjorken expansion, using approximate analytical solutions for the normalized shear stress tensor and energy density expressed in terms of Whittaker functions. They constrain the allowed initial states by demanding positivity and reality of energy density throughout the evolution, then compute thermal dilepton and photon spectra using Chapman-Enskog type shear viscous corrections to the phase-space distribution functions, integrating over the space-time evolution of the fireball.

## Key numbers

- The approximate analytical attractor for third-order hydrodynamics using $\tau_\pi \sim 1/T_{\rm id}$ shows error $\lesssim 1\%$ during the entire evolution when compared to the exact numerical attractor.
- The percentage error between numerical and approximate attractors for the $\tau_\pi \sim 1/T_{\rm id}$ case is approximately $3\%$ for all theories, with maximum error occurring during the transition from free streaming to hydrodynamic regime.
- The allowed values of the integration constant $\alpha$ are constrained to $\alpha \in [0,\infty]$, with $\alpha=0$ corresponding to the attractor solution and $\alpha=\infty$ corresponding to the repulsor solution.
- The dilepton production rate uses the cross section relation $M^2 g^2 \sigma(M^2) = \frac{80\pi}{9} \alpha_e^2$ with $N_f=2$ and $N_c=3$.

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors, previously investigated by Heller (2015), Romatschke (2017), and Denicol (2018), to phenomenological observables in heavy-ion collisions. The authors build on analytical solutions for causal hydrodynamic evolution equations with Bjorken expansion obtained by Denicol (2017) and Jaiswal (2019), and apply them to thermal particle production, which was previously studied with dissipative effects by Bhatt (2009, 2010) and Chandra (2016). This enables future work to use attractor solutions as a benchmark for maximum thermal particle yield in hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
