# In-medium pion dispersion relation and medium correction of $Nπ\leftrightarrow Δ$ near the threshold energy of pion production

> Transport models cannot simultaneously explain very recent data on pion multiplicities and pion charged ratios of Sn+Sn in the reaction at 0.27 A GeV. This stimulates further investigations on the pion dispersion relation, in-medium $Nπ\to Δ$ cross sections and $Δ\to N π$ decay widths near the threshold energy or at subthreshold energy of pion production in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. In this study, the pion dispersion relation, in-medium $Nπ\to Δ$ cross section and $Δ\to N π$ decay width near the threshold energy are investigated in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter by using the one-boson-exchange model. With the consideration of the energy conservation effect, the in-medium $Nπ\toΔ$ cross sections are enhanced at $s^{1/2}<1.11$ GeV in nuclear medium. The prediction of pion multiplicity and $π^-/π^+$ ratios near the threshold energy can be modified if this effect is considered in transport model simulations.

## Key claim

The in-medium \(N\pi\to\Delta\) cross sections are enhanced at center-of-mass energies \(s^{1/2}<1.11\) GeV in nuclear medium when energy conservation is considered, but the amplitude of this enhancement and the energy region where it occurs are both smaller than those reported in Zhen Zhang (2017) because the present study includes the effects of effective masses on the \(\Delta\) mass distribution function.

## Method

The authors used a one-boson-exchange model with a relativistic Lagrangian to calculate the pion self-energy (including particle-hole and \(\Delta\)-hole contributions) in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium \(\Delta\to N\pi\) decay widths and \(\pi N\to\Delta\) cross sections were computed using the quasiparticle approximation, replacing free masses and energies with Dirac effective masses and effective energies from a relativistic mean-field model (NL\(\rho\delta\) parameter set), and enforcing energy conservation via the canonical momentum relation \(E_{\Delta}=E_{N}+\omega\).

## Key numbers

- The in-medium \(\pi N\to\Delta\) cross sections are enhanced at \(s^{1/2}<1.11\) GeV and suppressed at \(s^{1/2}>1.11\) GeV in symmetric nuclear matter.
- The in-medium pion energy \(\omega\) increases with density at low pion momentum \(|\mathbf{k}|/m_{\pi}<0.66\) and decreases with density at higher momentum \(|\mathbf{k}|/m_{\pi}>0.66\).
- The pion optical potential \(V_{\pi}\) is repulsive for \(|\mathbf{k}|/m_{\pi}<0.66\) and attractive for \(|\mathbf{k}|/m_{\pi}>0.66\).
- In isospin asymmetric nuclear matter with \(I=0.2\), the splitting of charged pion energies satisfies \(\omega(\pi^-)>\omega(\pi^+)\) at \(|\mathbf{k}|<0.66 m_{\pi}\) and reverses at \(|\mathbf{k}|>0.66 m_{\pi}\).
- The coupling constants used are \(g_{\pi NN}=1.008\) and \(g_{\pi N\Delta}=2.3\), with a cutoff parameter \(b=7m_{\pi}\), fitted to the free-space \(\pi^+ p\to\Delta^{++}\) cross section and a decay width \(\Gamma=0.120\) GeV at the pole mass \(m_{\Delta}=1.232\) GeV.
- In symmetric nuclear matter, the in-medium \(\Delta\to N\pi\) decay widths are reduced relative to free space for \(s^{1/2}<1.15\) GeV.
- At \(2\rho_0\) in asymmetric matter with \(I=0.2\), the medium correction factors for different channels satisfy \(R_{\pi^{-} n\to \Delta^{-}}>R_{\pi^{+} p\to \Delta^{++}}\) at \(s^{1/2}<1.11\) GeV and the opposite at \(s^{1/2}>1.11\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium pion dispersion relations and \(N\pi\leftrightarrow\Delta\) processes to isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, building on prior calculations for symmetric matter by Dmitriev (1985) and Mao (1999). It differs from the approach of Zhen Zhang (2017) by including effective mass splitting from a relativistic mean-field model and enforcing energy conservation, which reduces the magnitude of the predicted enhancement of in-medium cross sections. The results are intended to be implemented in transport model simulations to improve predictions of pion multiplicities and \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratios near threshold energy, such as those measured by the S\(\pi\)RIT collaboration for Sn+Sn reactions at 0.27 A GeV.
