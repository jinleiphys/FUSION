# Models for exclusive vector meson production in heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss coherent and incoherent photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ vector mesons in high energy heavy ion collisions. In a dipole picture for the photon both can be naturally related to the dipole cross section that is also probed in inclusive DIS. We compare results of a particular calculation to ALICE data.

# Models for exclusive vector meson production in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The dipole picture can describe both coherent and incoherent J/Ψ photoproduction in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions using the same dipole cross section probed in inclusive DIS, but the predictions overshoot ALICE data, largely due to the large skewness correction, and there remain factor-of-~5 differences between theoretical approaches.

## Method

The authors use the IPsat (KT) dipole cross section model, which is a DGLAP-evolved gluon distribution multiplied by a Gaussian impact parameter profile and unitarized by exponentiation. They generalize this to nuclei by replacing the proton impact parameter profile with a sum over A nucleon profiles at positions sampled from a Woods-Saxon distribution, then compute coherent and incoherent cross sections by averaging the scattering amplitude over nucleon coordinates differently: coherent requires the same nucleon positions in amplitude and complex conjugate, while incoherent is the difference between the total quasielastic and coherent cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Coherent and incoherent J/Ψ photoproduction cross sections are calculated as functions of rapidity for LHC kinematics, with results shown in Figure 2 of Lappi (2013).
- The incoherent cross section is suppressed compared to A × σ(γp → J/Ψ p) (labeled "IPnonsat") by a factor that depends on the dipole cross section and nuclear geometry.
- The dipole model calculation (labeled "LM") is compared to ALICE incoherent cross section data from Abbas (2013).
- Theoretical approaches differ by a factor of approximately 5 between models.

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture approach previously applied to DIS and diffractive processes (Mueller 1989, Nikolaev 1990) to exclusive vector meson production in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions measured by ALICE (Abelev 2012, Abbas 2013). It contrasts with the STARLIGHT event generator, which calculates the incoherent cross section by reducing Aσ(γp → J/Ψ p) with a shadowing correction, and with the "LTA" calculation (Rebyakova 2011) which uses a different model of nuclear breakup. The results enable further constraints on nuclear shadowing and dipole cross section models using LHC data.
