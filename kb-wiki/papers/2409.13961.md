# Description of the first order phase transition region of an equation of state for QCD with a critical point

> We map the mean-field Ising model equation of state onto the QCD phase diagram, and reconstruct the full coexistence region in the case of a first order phase transition. Beyond the coexistence line, we maintain access to the spinodal region in the phase diagram, thus providing a description of metastable and unstable phases of matter as well. In this way, our approach includes the super-heated hadronic phase and the super-cooled quark-gluon plasma, which are useful for hydrodynamic simulations of the fireball created in a heavy-ion collision at low collision energy, where a first order phase transition is expected. We discuss the features of the pressure and other thermodynamic observables as functions of temperature and baryonic chemical potential, in particular their behavior in the coexistence region. Finally, we compare our equation of state to other 3D-Ising model ones available in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors map the mean-field Ising model equation of state onto the QCD phase diagram to reconstruct the full coexistence region including metastable and unstable (spinodal) phases, but they note that the spinodals are valid only in the regime of metastability with finite lifetime and do not belong to a system in thermal equilibrium, serving instead as mathematical properties of the equation of state belonging to the broader class of Lee-Yang edge singularities.

## Method

The authors use the mean-field Ising model equation of state, where the magnetic field h(M,r) is given by h = h0 (a r M + b M^3) with a=3, b=1, and the pressure P = h0 M0 (1/2 a r M^2 + 3/4 b M^4). They map the Ising variables (reduced temperature r and magnetic field h) onto QCD variables (temperature T and baryonic chemical potential μB) using a linear mapping with six free parameters: critical point coordinates (Tc, μB,c), scaling parameters ω and ρ, and angles α1 and α2 between the Ising and QCD axes. The spinodal points in the Ising model are obtained by solving (∂h/∂M)r = 0, yielding hsp = ±2 r^(3/2)/(3√3) for r<0, and these are then mapped onto the QCD phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The mean-field Ising model critical exponents are β=1/2 and δ=3, while the 3D Ising model has (βδ)3D = 1.5648, giving a shift angle of 0.0648π for spinodal singularities into the complex plane
- The normalization constants for the Ising model are h0 = 1/(3√3) and M0 = 1/√3
- The spinodal location in the Ising model is given by hsp = ±2 r^(3/2)/(3√3) for r<0
- For the mapping with α1=0°, the spinodal in μB is given by μB,sp(T) = μB,c + (3/2^(2/3)) ρ ω^(1/3) Tc^(1/3) (T - Tc)^(2/3)

## Context

This work extends the BEST collaboration's family of equations of state with a critical point in the 3D Ising model universality class (Parotto 2018, Karthein 2021, An 2021), which was recently extended to higher chemical potential by Kahangirwe (2024). Unlike previous approaches that rely on the 3D Ising model scaling equation of state with parametric (R,θ) variables, the mean-field approach gives direct access to Ising variables (r,h) with a single mapping procedure and allows analytic solutions with rational exponents. The resulting equation of state provides input for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at low collision energies where a first order phase transition is expected, enabling studies of spinodal decomposition signatures as explored by Randrup (2009) and Steinheimer (2012).
