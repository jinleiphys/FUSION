# Information entropy of nuclear electromagnetic transitions in AdS/QCD

> Electromagnetic transitions involving nucleons and their resonances are here presented in the fermionic sector of the AdS/QCD soft-wall model, using the differential configurational entropy (DCE) as a measure of information entropy. The DCE is employed to derive adjustable parameters that define the minimal and nonminimal couplings in the nuclear interaction with a gauge vector field, in the nucleonic $γN \to N^*(1535)$ transition. The values obtained comply with phenomenological data with good accuracy.

## Key claim

The differential configurational entropy (DCE) can be used to derive the adjustable parameters that define the minimal and nonminimal couplings in the nuclear interaction with a gauge vector field for the $\gamma N \to N^*(1535)$ transition within the AdS/QCD soft-wall model, and the values obtained correspond to global minima of the DCE, representing states of maximal configurational stability that comply with phenomenological data.

## Method

The author computed the DCE for the $\gamma N \to N^*(1535)$ transition in the fermionic sector of the AdS/QCD soft-wall model, using the energy density operator as the spatially-localized scalar field. The DCE was evaluated as a function of two among five adjustable parameters ($c_4^{N^*N}$, $c_5^{N^*N}$, $d_3^{N^*N}$, $d_4^{N^*N}$, $d_5^{N^*N}$) that define the minimal and nonminimal couplings in the interaction Lagrangian, while fixing the other three parameters. The global minima of the DCE were identified as the optimal parameter values, corresponding to the dominant states of highest configurational stability of the nucleonic system.

## Key numbers

- The universal scale parameter in the dilaton background field is ${\mathsf{k}} = 383$ MeV.
- The proton mass is $M_{\text{Proton}} = 938.272$ MeV and the Roper mass is $M = 1.440$ GeV.
- The $N^*(1535)$ resonance mass used in the model is 1.510 GeV.
- The nonminimal coupling parameters fixed from magnetic moments and form factors are $\eta_V^{N^*N} = 4.28$ and $\zeta_V^{N^*N} = -0.47$.
- The parameters derived from a fit to helicity amplitudes are $c_4^{N^*N} = 25.52$, $c_5^{N^*N} = -26.90$, $d_3^{N^*N} = -1.89$, $d_4^{N^*N} = 5.64$, and $d_5^{N^*N} = -3.58$.
- The coefficients for the $N^*(1535)$ free Lagrangian are $c_4^{N^*} = 0.82$, $c_5^{N^*} = -0.63$, and $c_6^{N^*} = 0.81$.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/QCD soft-wall approach to nucleon resonances previously developed by Gutsche et al. (2012, 2017, 2019), who described electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon and the Roper resonance using minimal and nonminimal couplings. The DCE technique had been systematically applied to mass spectra of glueballs, baryons, mesons, and their resonances by da Rocha (2021), Ferreira (2020), Bazeia (2021), Braga (2017-2021), and others, corroborating experimental data and lattice QCD. This work enables the determination of coupling parameters in the $\gamma N \to N^*(1535)$ transition without relying solely on fits to helicity amplitude data, using instead the configurational stability criterion provided by the DCE.
