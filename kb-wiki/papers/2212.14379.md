# Charged-particle bound states in periodic boxes

> We consider the binding energy of a two-body system with a repulsive Coulomb interaction in a finite periodic volume. We define the finite-volume Coulomb potential as the usual Coulomb potential, except that the distance is defined as the shortest separation between the two bodies in the periodic volume. We investigate this problem in one and three-dimensional periodic boxes and derive the asymptotic behavior of the volume dependence for bound states with zero angular momentum in terms of Whittaker functions. We benchmark our results against numerical calculations and show how the method can be used to extract asymptotic normalization coefficients for charged-particle bound states. The results we derive here have immediate applications for calculations of atomic nuclei in finite periodic volumes for the case where the leading finite-volume correction is associated with two charged clusters.

## Key claim

The authors derive the leading finite-volume energy correction for bound states of two charged clusters in a periodic box, showing that the volume dependence can be expressed analytically in terms of Whittaker functions rather than the modified Bessel functions that apply for neutral clusters. The derivation is fully nonperturbative in the Coulomb interaction, meaning it remains valid for medium-mass and heavy nuclei where perturbative treatments of the Coulomb repulsion would be inadequate.

## Method

The authors consider a two-body system with a repulsive Coulomb potential plus a finite-range short-range potential in one-dimensional and three-dimensional periodic boxes. For the 3D case, they use an intermediate Hamiltonian that includes the truncated periodic Coulomb potential but leaves the short-range potential as in infinite volume, then treat the difference between the periodic and infinite Coulomb potentials as a perturbation. They construct an ansatz for the finite-volume wave function by summing shifted copies of the eigenstate of this intermediate Hamiltonian, then evaluate the leading volume dependence of the energy shift. Numerical benchmarks are performed using a lattice discretization with a generator code extended to include the Coulomb interaction, with the Coulomb potential regularized at short distances by a Gaussian regulator.

## Key numbers

- For the 3D system, the leading volume dependence is given by $\Delta E_0(L) = -\frac{3A_\infty^2}{\mu L} [W_{-\bar\eta,1/2}(\kappa L)]^2$, where $W$ is a Whittaker function, $\bar\eta = \gamma/(2\kappa_\infty)$, and $\gamma = 2\mu\alpha Z_1 Z_2$
- The correction terms $\tilde{E}_p(L)$ and $\tilde{E}_p'(L)$ both scale asymptotically as $\mathcal{O}(\bar\eta/(\kappa L)^2) \times \Delta E_0(L)$
- For the \isotope[3]{He} test case with $\gamma = 0.046\ \text{fm}^{-1}$, the ANC extracted from the finite-volume fit is $A_\infty = 1.44(1)\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}$, which would be off by about 5% if neutral-cluster formulas were used
- The direct extraction from the finite-volume wave function gives $A_\infty = 1.46(1)\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}$, in good agreement with the energy fit
- The $S$-wave ANC for \isotope[3]{He} from Ref. Friar (1988) is $1.82(1)\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}$ (converted to the authors' conventions)

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism pioneered by Luscher (1986, 1991) for neutral particles and the subsequent generalization to bound-state energy shifts by Konig (2011, 2017, 2020), which expressed the volume dependence in terms of modified Bessel functions and asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs). While previous studies of finite-volume electromagnetic corrections used perturbation theory (Davoudi 2014, Beane 2014, Borsanyi 2014, Stellin 2020, Guo 2021), this work provides the fully nonperturbative analytic form for charged clusters. The results enable extraction of ANCs from finite-volume lattice EFT calculations of bound nuclei, which is important for low-energy astrophysical capture reactions, and provide a better extrapolation for binding energies to infinite volume.
