# Fusion cross section and total kinetic energy of fission fragments by the dynamical dissipative surface-friction model

> The capture cross section, the fusion cross section, and the quasi-fission yield producing symmetric fragments ($A_{CN}/2\pm20u$) in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U reaction are analyzed by the multidimensional Langevin equation taking account of the surface friction effect. From the experimental data, the strength of the tangential friction has been determined. It is presented that tangential friction increases in proportional to the power of the relative velocity of the projectile and the target.

## Key claim

The strength of the tangential friction parameter \(\gamma_t^0\) in the dynamical dissipative surface-friction model must increase with incident energy to reproduce experimental fusion and quasi-fission cross sections, but the model cannot simultaneously reproduce the experimental total kinetic energy (TKE) of fission fragments because the conversion of internal energy into pre-scission kinetic energy is not accurately estimated.

## Method

The authors performed multidimensional Langevin trajectory calculations for the \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{238}\)U reaction using three collective coordinates (distance between centers, fragment deformation, and mass asymmetry) with a time-dependent potential that evolves from a diabatic to an adiabatic form. They varied the strength of the tangential (sliding) friction parameter \(\gamma_t^0\) and compared the calculated capture cross sections, fusion cross sections, and yields of symmetric fission fragments (\(A_{CN}/2 \pm 20\) u) against experimental data from Nishio (2015), Kozulin (2016), and Shen (2017) to determine the incident-energy dependence of \(\gamma_t^0\).

## Key numbers

- At \(E_{c.m.}=190\) MeV (\(E^{*}=30.9\) MeV), the mass distribution is reproduced with \(\gamma_t^0 = 0.1\) MeV s fm\(^{-2}\), but the calculated TKE is underestimated relative to experiment.
- At \(E_{c.m.}=210\) MeV (\(E^{*}=50.9\) MeV), the mass distribution is reproduced with \(\gamma_t^0 = 30.0\) MeV s fm\(^{-2}\), but the calculated TKE is considerably higher than the experimental value.
- At \(E_{c.m.}/E_{bass}=0.990\), the experimental fusion cross section is reproduced with \(\gamma_t^0 \approx 1\) MeV s fm\(^{-2}\).
- The value of \(\gamma_t^0\) determined from the symmetric-fragment yield and from the fusion cross section increases with \(E_{c.m.}/E_{bass}\) over the range \(E_{c.m.}/E_{bass}=0.930\) to \(1.186\).

## Context

This work extends the surface-friction model of Gross (1975) by determining the incident-energy dependence of the tangential friction strength from experimental data on the \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{238}\)U reaction, a system relevant for superheavy element synthesis. The results contradict the assumption of a constant friction strength and show that the friction increases with relative velocity, which is important for predicting fusion cross sections for unknown superheavy elements. The study also highlights that the TKE of fission fragments cannot be simultaneously reproduced, pointing to the need for improved modeling of the conversion of thermal energy into pre-scission kinetic energy from the saddle to the scission point.
