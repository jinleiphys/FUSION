# Proton emission systematics along proton drip line

> We analyze the chart containing both spontaneous and beta-delayed proton emission processes in terms of the Coulomb parameter, reduced radius and angular momentum ($χ$, $ρ$, $l$). We then compare the methods to estimate decay width $Γ$ of a resonant state in a proton mean field, namely the continuity equation for outgoing Gamow states, phase shift analysis of real scattering states and numerical integration of the Schrödinger equation in the complex plane. We show that they provide similar results in the region where it is possible to evaluate the imaginary part of the energy for a resonant (Gamow) state. We then investigate the role of the centrifugal barrier induced by Coulomb interaction and also by proton single particle orbitals. We show that the so-called universal decay law, connecting the logarithm of the monopole reduced width to the fragmentation potential, remains also valid for beta-delayed proton emission processes. This fact allows us to describe experimental data for all proton emission processes in terms of a linear dependence connecting the logarithm of the monopole Coulomb-reduced decay width to the logarithm of the monopole Coulomb penetrability and fragmentation potential within a factor of three for absolute values.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a universal decay law, linearly connecting the logarithm of the monopole Coulomb-reduced decay width to the logarithm of the monopole Coulomb penetrability and the fragmentation potential, remains valid for beta-delayed proton emission processes in addition to spontaneous proton emission, with the caveat that the linear dependence of log penetrability on the Coulomb parameter fails for Coulomb parameter values below approximately 7.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental data for spontaneous proton emitters (A>100) and beta-delayed proton emitters (A=20-21, plus additional nuclei with known Q-values) in terms of the Coulomb parameter, reduced radius, and angular momentum. They compared three methods for estimating the decay width of a resonant state in a proton mean field: the continuity equation for outgoing Gamow states, phase shift analysis of real scattering states, and numerical integration of the Schrodinger equation in the complex plane. For beta-delayed proton emission from 20Na, the authors digitized experimental spectra and performed Lorentzian fits using the R-matrix standard procedure to extract decay widths.

## Key numbers

* The barrier radius follows the linear dependence R_B = (0.93 +/- 0.06)A^(1/3) + (4.65 +/- 0.25) fm, and the Coulomb+nuclear barrier at this radius is 7% smaller than the pure Coulomb barrier.
* The linear fit for the logarithm of the monopole reduced width versus fragmentation potential yields slope a = -0.015 and intercept b = -1.246 with standard deviation sigma = 0.509.
* The universal decay law describes experimental data within a factor of three for absolute values of the decay width.
* For 20Na, the decay widths range from 6.295 keV (l=2, Q=4.053 MeV) to 116.700 keV (l=1, Q=2.320 MeV).
* For 21Na, the decay widths range from 0.016 keV (l=2, Q=1.112 MeV) to 235.000 keV (l=0, Q=3.543 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the universal decay law previously established for spontaneous proton emission by Delion (2006) and Delion (2021) to beta-delayed proton emission processes, which occur in lighter nuclei (A<100) where the Q-value lies much closer to the top of the Coulomb+nuclear barrier. The analysis builds on prior theoretical descriptions of spontaneous proton emission using semiclassical approaches (Buck 1992, Aberg 1997) and R-matrix theory (Kruppa 2004), and enables predictions of decay widths for beta-delayed proton emitters where only Q-values are currently measured (Bates 2020).
