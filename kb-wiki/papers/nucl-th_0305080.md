# Comparison of exact and approximate cross-sections in relativistic Coulomb excitation

> We present a new method of obtaining time-dependent matrix elements of the electromagnetic pulse produced by a highly-relativistic projectile. These matrix elements are used in a coupled-channel calculation to predict the cross-sections for population of 1- and 2-phonon states of the giant dipole resonance. Comparisons are made with the predictions of the long-wavelength and Born approximations.

# Comparison of exact and approximate cross-sections in relativistic Coulomb excitation

## Key claim

The Born approximation and long-wavelength approximation (LWLA) are adequate for total 1-phonon cross-sections at bombarding energies below about 2 GeV per nucleon, but at higher energies the Born approximation fails dramatically for the M=0 component of the 1-phonon state, while the exact calculation predicts appreciable cross-section where the Born approximation gives vanishingly small values.

## Method

The authors numerically integrate the time-dependent Schrödinger equation using a coupled-channel approach with matrix elements obtained from a new numerical evaluation of the Fourier transform of the exact Alder-Winther multipole expansion of the retarded electromagnetic interaction. They compare these "exact" results with the long-wavelength approximation (LWLA) and the Born approximation for the specific case of 208Pb projectiles exciting giant dipole resonance (GDR) states in 40Ca, including all states with up to two phonons.

## Key numbers

- The shell-model oscillator energy for 40Ca is taken as ħω_sm = 11.7 MeV, with ν = 0.28177 fm⁻²
- For 10A GeV 208Pb projectiles on 40Ca, γ ≈ 11.66, and the LWLA validity condition requires impact parameter b > √(γ²-1) × R ≈ 50 fm
- At b = 12 fm, the exact V_{βα}(t) differs markedly from the LWLA result; at b = 50 fm the LWLA is still only "fair"
- For the 1-phonon M=0 state at high bombarding energy, the Born approximation cross-section vanishes as 1/γ², while the exact calculation yields appreciable cross-section
- The asymptotic occupation probability of Ψ⁰¹₀ at b = 200 fm is 0.6 × 10⁻⁵, compared to the Born prediction of ~10⁻⁸
- The Born approximation and exact calculation agree well for the total 1-phonon cross-section over the entire energy range studied (up to 10A GeV)
- For 2-phonon states, the exact calculation predicts cross-sections that "far exceed" the Born approximation predictions

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical relativistic Coulomb excitation framework established by Alder and Winther (1975) and the coupled-channel methods of Lanza et al. (2000) and Bertulani et al. (1998). The authors provide the first systematic test of the LWLA and Born approximation against essentially exact calculations at bombarding energies up to 10A GeV, where previous work had assumed these approximations were adequate below 2A GeV. The results enable future studies of multiphonon giant resonance excitation at collider energies and demonstrate that measurements of separate M=0 and |M|=1 cross-sections could distinguish between the Born approximation and exact dynamics. The harmonic vibrator model predictions for 2-phonon state ratios (1/0/3/2 for states (020)/(021)/(022)/(100)) are shown to be inadequate at high energy.
