# Interplay of order and chaos across a first-order quantum shape-phase transition in nuclei

> We study the nature of the dynamics in a first-order quantum phase transition between spherical and prolate-deformed nuclear shapes. Classical and quantum analyses reveal a change in the system from a chaotic Hénon-Heiles behavior on the spherical side into a pronounced regular dynamics on the deformed side. Both order and chaos persist in the coexistence region and their interplay reflects the Landau potential landscape and the impact of collective rotations.

## Key claim
The paper shows that across a first-order quantum shape-phase transition in nuclei, the classical dynamics changes from chaotic Henon-Heiles behavior on the spherical side to regular dynamics on the deformed side, with both order and chaos coexisting in the coexistence region. This interplay is conditioned by the specific Landau potential landscape and the impact of collective rotations, and the authors use a high barrier height (V_b/h_2 = 0.231) compared to previous works where V_b/h_2 was 0.0018.

## Method
The authors employed the interacting boson model (IBM) with N (s,d) bosons representing valence nucleon pairs, resolving the Hamiltonian into intrinsic and collective parts. Classical dynamics was obtained through coherent states in the N to infinity limit, with 1/N playing the role of h-bar, and analyzed via Poincare sections for L=0 vibrations. Quantum dynamics was studied through Peres lattices {x_i, E_i} for L=0 eigenstates with N=80, where x_i is defined as sqrt(2 <i|n_d|i>/N) and the lattices distinguish regular from irregular states by ordered patterns versus disordered meshes of points.

## Key numbers
- Barrier height at the critical point: V_b/h_2 = 0.231, with beta_0 = 1.35 (compared to V_b/h_2 = 0.0018 in previous works)
- Spinodal point: rho* = 0.546
- Anti-spinodal point: xi** = 0.354
- Critical point: rho_c = beta_0^(-1) and xi_c = 0
- Number of bosons used in quantum calculations: N = 80
- Equilibrium deformation on the deformed side: beta_eq = sqrt(2) beta_0 (1 + beta_0^2)^(-1/2) > 0, gamma_eq = 0

## Context
This work extends studies of quantum phase transitions in the interacting boson model, particularly first-order transitions between spherical and axially-deformed nuclei in the Nd-Sm-Gd region, as previously studied by Cejnar (2010). The paper builds on earlier work by Whelan (1993) on chaos in the IBM and by Leviatan (1987) on the intrinsic-collective decomposition of the IBM Hamiltonian. The results enable future studies of how regular and chaotic dynamics manifest in rotational bands and gamma vibrations across the phase transition, and how the Peres lattice technique can identify bandhead states of K=0 and K=2 rotational bands.
