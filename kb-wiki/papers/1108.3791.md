# Holographic DC conductivities from the open string metric

> We study the DC conductivities of various holographic models using the open string metric (OSM), which is an effective metric geometrizing density and electromagnetic field effect. We propose a new way to compute the nonlinear conductivity using OSM. As far as the final conductivity formula is concerned, it is equivalent to the Karch-O'Bannon's real-action method. However, it yields a geometrical insight and technical simplifications. Especially, a real-action condition is interpreted as a regular geometry condition of OSM. As applications of the OSM method, we study several holographic models on the quantum Hall effect and strange metal. By comparing a Lifshitz background and the Light-Cone AdS, we show how an extra parameter can change the temperature scaling behavior of conductivity. Finally we discuss how OSM can be used to study other transport coefficients, such as diffusion constant, and effective temperature induced by the effective world volume horizon.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the open string metric (OSM) method for computing DC conductivities is equivalent to the Karch-O'Bannon real-action method in terms of the final conductivity formula, but offers a geometrical interpretation where the real-action condition corresponds to a regular geometry condition of the OSM, and this equivalence holds for both linear and nonlinear conductivities.

## Method

The authors develop a holographic method to compute DC conductivities by applying the membrane paradigm to the open string metric (OSM), which geometrizes the effects of finite charge density and background electromagnetic fields. For nonlinear conductivity, they first compute the singular shell position from the condition that the determinant of the gauge-fibered metric vanishes, then apply the same linear conductivity formula at that singular shell position instead of at the background black hole horizon. The formalism is generalized to include Wess-Zumino terms, dilaton fields, and internal flux fields in arbitrary dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For the D3/D7 system at finite density and magnetic field, the linear DC conductivity computed via OSM reproduces known results from the real-action method (no explicit numerical value given in the truncated text).
- The singular shell condition is given by \(\xi(r_s) = \det \gamma_{\mu\nu}(r_s) = 0\), where \(\mu,\nu\) are field theory direction indices.
- The current determined by the real-action condition is \(J_x = \left.\sqrt{-g_{tt} g_{xx} g_{\Omega\Omega}^2}\right|_{r=r_s(\tilde{E}, r_H)} = g_{\Omega\Omega}(r_s)\tilde{E} \equiv \sigma(r_s) \tilde{E}\).
- The Ricci scalar of the OSM near the singular shell scales as \(\mathcal{R} \sim \frac{(g_{xx} g_{tt}' + g_{tt} g_{xx}')^2}{2 \xi^2 g_{rr}}\) without current, and becomes \(\mathcal{R} \sim \frac{\chi (g_{xx} g_{tt}' + g_{tt} g_{xx}')^2}{2 \xi^2 g_{rr} g_{tt} g_{xx} g_{\Omega\Omega}^2}\) with current.

## Context

This work extends the membrane paradigm method of Iqbal and Liu (2008) to systems with finite charge density and background electromagnetic fields by using the open string metric, building on the earlier OSM-based approach of Kim and Kim (2011). It provides a geometric interpretation of the real-action method of Karch and O'Bannon (2007), where the condition for a real action is reinterpreted as a regularity condition on the OSM geometry. The method enables study of quantum Hall effect models such as the D3-D7' system of Bergman et al. (2010) and the D2-D8' system of Jokela et al. (2011), as well as strange metal properties in Lifshitz and light-cone AdS backgrounds, where it shows how an extra parameter can change the temperature scaling behavior of conductivity.
