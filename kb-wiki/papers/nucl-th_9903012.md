# Saturating Cronin effect in ultrarelativistic proton-nucleus collisions

> Pion and photon production cross sections are analyzed in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at energies 20 GeV < s^1/2 < 60 GeV. We separate the proton-proton and nuclear contributions to transverse-momentum broadening and suggest a new mechanism for the nuclear enhancement in the high transverse-momentum region.

# Saturating Cronin effect in ultrarelativistic proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The nuclear enhancement (Cronin effect) in high-transverse-momentum pion and photon production in proton-nucleus collisions can be described by a saturating mechanism where the incoming proton undergoes at most one semi-hard collision (Q² ~ 1 GeV²) prior to the hard parton scattering, after which further collisions do not increase the parton transverse momentum width. This contrasts with the standard assumption that all soft nucleon-nucleon collisions contribute cumulatively to the broadening.

## Method

The authors analyze inclusive π⁰ and γ production in pp and pA collisions using a leading-order perturbative QCD parton model supplemented by a Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum (kT) distribution for initial-state partons. They fit the average kT² (⟨kT²⟩) to pp data across multiple experiments at center-of-mass energies between 20 and 60 GeV, then extend the formalism to pA collisions by introducing an impact-parameter-dependent broadening term. The nuclear enhancement is modeled either as proportional to all soft collisions (νA(b)-1) or as a saturated step function that allows at most one semi-hard collision to contribute.

## Key numbers

- For pp → π⁰ X at √s = 27.4 GeV, the best-fit value is ⟨kT²⟩ = 3 GeV².
- For pp → γ X, ⟨kT²⟩ = 1.2 GeV² at √s = 31.6 GeV and 1.5 GeV² at √s = 38.8 GeV.
- The saturated Cronin factor C^sat = 1.2 GeV² gives a good fit for all three targets (Be, Ti, W) in pA → π⁰ X at √s = 27.4 GeV.
- The all-collisions model yields target-dependent values: C^all_pBe = 0.8 ± 0.2 GeV², C^all_pTi = 0.4 ± 0.2 GeV², and C^all_pW = 0.3 ± 0.2 GeV², inconsistent with a universal per-collision transverse momentum transfer.
- The analysis covers the energy range 20 GeV ≲ √s ≲ 60 GeV and transverse momenta 3 GeV ≲ pT ≲ 6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD treatment of the Cronin effect, first observed by Antreasyan et al. (1979), by proposing a saturation mechanism that resolves the target dependence problem in the standard multiple-soft-collision model of Wang (1997, 1998). The authors build on earlier kT-smearing approaches used by Huston et al. (1995) and the E706 Collaboration (1998), and on the observation by Zielinski (1998) that ⟨kT⟩ values increase with energy. The saturated Cronin effect provides a unified description of both π⁰ and γ production in pA collisions with a single parameter, and the authors suggest that systematic pA experiments are needed to determine the energy dependence of C^sat for extrapolation to nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC.
