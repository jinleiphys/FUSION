# The contributions of the vector-channel at finite isospin chemical potential with the self-consistent mean field approximation

> The self-consistent mean field approximation of two-flavor NJL model, which introduces a free parameter $α$ ($α$ reflects the weight of different interaction channels), is employed to investigate the contributions of the vector-channel at finite isospin chemical potential $μ_I$ and zero baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ and zero temperature $T$. The calculations show that the consideration of the vector-channel contributions leads to lower value of pion condensate in superfluid phase, compared with the standard Lagrangian of NJL model ($α=0$). In superfluid phase, we also obtain lower isospin number density, and the discrepancy is getting larger with the increase of isospin potential. Compared with the recent results from Lattice QCD, the isospin density and energy density we obtained with $α=0.5$ agree with the data of lattice well. In the phase diagram in the $T-μ_I$ plane for $μ_B=0$, we can see that the difference of the critical temperatures of phase transition between the results with $α=0$ and $α=0.5$ is up to $3\%-5\%$ for a fixed isospin potential. All of these indicate that the vector channels play an important role in isospin medium.

## Key claim
Including vector-channel contributions (via the self-consistent mean field NJL model with parameter α) lowers the pion condensate and isospin number density in the superfluid phase compared to the standard NJL model (α=0), and choosing α=0.5 yields isospin density and energy density that agree well with recent Lattice QCD data, except near μ_I ~ 1.5 m_π.

## Method
The authors employ the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a self-consistent mean field approximation that introduces a free parameter α, which weights the contributions from the original NJL Lagrangian and its Fierz-transformed form. They solve the coupled gap equations for the quark condensate σ, pion condensate π, quark number density n, and isospin number density n_I at zero baryon chemical potential and zero temperature, using a 3-momentum cutoff regularization. The parameter set (m_0 = 4.76 MeV, G = 4.78×10⁻⁶ MeV⁻², Λ = 659 MeV) is fitted to reproduce the pion mass m_π = 131.7 MeV and decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV at vacuum.

## Key numbers
- Pion condensate π/σ₀: the largest difference between α=0 and α=0.5 occurs at μ_I ~ 1.5 m_π (Fig. 3.1).
- Critical isospin chemical potential for pion condensation onset: μ_I^c = m_π = 131.7 MeV, unchanged by α.
- Isospin density n_I/m_π³ and energy density ε/m_π⁴: results with α=0.5 agree well with Lattice QCD data for μ_I ≲ 2 m_π (Figs. 3.3, 3.4).
- Phase diagram in T–μ_I plane: the difference in critical temperatures between α=0 and α=0.5 is up to 3%–5% for a fixed isospin potential, with the largest difference (5%) at μ_I ~ 1.5 m_π.
- Quark condensate in vacuum: σ₀ = –2(250 MeV)³.

## Context
This work extends previous NJL model studies of isospin matter (e.g., Barducci, He, Tao, Lian) by including vector-channel contributions via the self-consistent mean field approach of Fei Wang (2019). It directly compares with Lattice QCD results from Brandt (2018) for isospin density and energy density. The findings show that vector channels are essential for matching lattice data, and the parameter α can be constrained by future experiments or observations, analogous to how Tong Zhao (2019) used neutron star merger data to constrain α in baryonic matter. This enables more accurate modeling of the QCD phase diagram at finite isospin density, relevant for heavy-ion collisions and compact star physics.
