# Estimation of the freeze-out parameters reachable in the AFTER@LHC project

> Estimation of the freeze-out parameters as the function of rapidity in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=72$ GeV in the AFTER@LHC project is performed. The conventional hadron resonance gas model is used for the analysis of the events generated by the UrQMD model. The results indicate that one may obtain at least $2.5$ times increase of baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ in the forward rapidity range as compared to the mid-rapidity. The $μ_B$ values in the rapidity range of $0 < y < 4.5$ for AFTER@LHC are comparable to that covered by the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. Thus, a rapidity scan in the AFTER@LHC project provides a complementary approach to study the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that a rapidity scan in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=72\) GeV within the AFTER@LHC project can achieve at least a factor of 2.5 increase in baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) at forward rapidity compared to mid-rapidity, but this claim is hedged by noting that the interpretation of thermal fits at rapidities \(y \gtrsim 3\) may be questionable due to rapidly increasing \(\chi^2\) values.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD-3.4 model in cascade mode to generate \(2 \times 10^5\) events of the 10% most central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=72\) GeV. They then performed thermal fits of the mean hadron multiplicities in rapidity bins of \(\Delta y = 0.5\) using the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model implemented in the Thermal-FIST package, fitting four free parameters: temperature \(T\), baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\), strangeness saturation factor \(\gamma_S\), and system radius \(R\), with \(\mu_Q\) and \(\mu_S\) constrained by charge conservation conditions.

## Key numbers

- At least a factor of 2.5 increase in \(\mu_B\) is obtained between mid-rapidity (\(0 < y < 0.5\)) and forward rapidity (\(0 < y < 3\)) for the "conservative" rapidity range.
- The largest relative increase of \(\mu_B\) is about a factor of 5, reached between the smallest (\(0 < y < 0.5\)) and largest (\(4.0 < y < 4.5\)) rapidity bins for the particle set including \(\Xi\) and \(\Omega\).
- The parabolic fit parameters for \(\mu_B(y)\) are \(a = 75.5 \pm 5.9\) MeV and \(b = 16.6 \pm 1.0\) MeV for the full particle set, and \(a = 84.5 \pm 3.2\) MeV and \(b = 13.9 \pm 0.5\) MeV for the set without \(\Xi\) and \(\Omega\).
- The temperature values obtained are approximately \(T \sim 140\) MeV, about 20 MeV smaller than temperatures extracted from fits to real data at RHIC and LHC.
- The rapidity-integrated (4\(\pi\)) system volume is \(V = 3864 \pm 543\) fm\(^3\) (with \(\Xi\) and \(\Omega\)) and \(V = 4898 \pm 672\) fm\(^3\) (without \(\Xi\) and \(\Omega\)).
- The integrated luminosity for Pb+Pb collisions at AFTER@LHC is \(L_{int} \sim 1.6\) nb\(^{-1}\) per year, corresponding to approximately 11.5 billion minimum bias events.

## Context

This work extends the concept of a rapidity scan at fixed collision energy, previously explored by Dumitru (1993), Biedron (2006), Broniowski (2007), and Becattini (2007), to the specific energy and detector configuration of the AFTER@LHC project. The results indicate that the \(\mu_B\) values accessible in the rapidity range \(0 < y < 4.5\) are comparable to those covered by the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, providing a complementary approach to studying the QCD phase diagram with the advantages of high luminosity at the LHC. The paper enables future experimental searches for the QCD critical point by demonstrating that a rapidity scan can achieve significant variation in baryon chemical potential while maintaining similar temperatures, and it identifies the rapidity range \(y \lesssim 3\) as the most reliable for thermal interpretation.
