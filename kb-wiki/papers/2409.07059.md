# Neutron skin and its effects in heavy-ion collisions

> Neutron skin is an exotic phenomena in unstable nuclei. The various effects in nuclear reactions caused by the neutron skin and also its relation with the properties of nuclear structure are reviewed in this article. Based on numerous studies with theoretical models, strong correlations have been found between the neutron skin thickness and neutron removal cross section, neutron/proton yield ratio, t/\ch{^3He} yield ratio, neutron-proton momentum difference, isoscaling parameter, photon production, reaction cross sections for neutron induced reactions, charge-changing cross sections difference of mirror nuclei, astrophysical $S$-factor, and other quantities in nuclear reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei. Moreover, the relationships between neutron skin thickness and some properties of nuclear structure, such as $α$-cluster formation, $α$ decay, nuclear surface, nuclear temperature, and proton radii difference of mirror nuclei, have also been investigated. It also has been shown that the neutron skin plays a crucial role in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Experimentally, an unstable nucleus with neutron skin can be generated by radioactive nuclear beam facilities, and the thickness of neutron skin could be extracted by measuring the sensitive probes, which further helps giving stringent constraints on the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys the correlations between neutron skin thickness and a wide range of observables in nuclear reactions and nuclear structure, arguing that these correlations can serve as indirect probes for extracting neutron skin thickness, which in turn constrains the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron star properties; the authors note that the extracted results from different experimental methods are often model-dependent and vary greatly.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical studies using the statistical abrasion ablation (SAA) model, the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) model, and the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD-L) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions of neutron-rich projectiles (Ca, Ni, Sn, Mg, Ne, Pb isotopes) on various targets (C, Be, Sn) at incident energies from 50 MeV/nucleon to 1 GeV/nucleon. The neutron skin thickness is varied by adjusting the diffuseness parameter of the neutron density distribution in the two-parameter Fermi distribution or by employing different Skyrme parameter sets corresponding to different values of the slope of the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness is defined as the difference between the root-mean-square radius of neutrons and protons: ⟨r_n²⟩^{1/2} − ⟨r_p²⟩^{1/2}
- For Ca + C reactions at 100 MeV/nucleon, the neutron removal cross section increases and the proton removal cross section decreases with increasing neutron skin thickness (Fig. 1)
- For Ca + C and Ni + C at 50 MeV/nucleon, the neutron-to-proton yield ratio R(n/p) and triton-to-³He yield ratio R(t/³He) show linear correlations with neutron skin thickness (Fig. 2)
- For Mg, Ca, and Ne isotopes on ¹²C at 50 MeV/nucleon, the neutron-proton momentum difference decreases linearly with increasing neutron skin thickness in both initial and final states (Fig. 3)
- For ⁵⁰Ca + ¹²C and ⁴⁸Ca + ¹²C at 50 MeV/nucleon, the isoscaling parameter α decreases linearly with increasing neutron skin thickness for fragments with proton numbers 15 to 19 (Fig. 4)
- For ⁴⁸Ca + ⁹Be at 140 MeV/nucleon, the left-side width Γ_L of the parallel momentum distribution of neutron-rich sulfur fragments is sensitive to the neutron skin thickness (Fig. 5)
- For ¹²⁴,¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn at 200 MeV/nucleon, the cross sections of primary projectile-like residues with A > 100 are positively correlated with neutron skin thickness, using Skyrme parameters with symmetry energy slope L varying from 30 to 110 MeV (Fig. 6)
- For Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes on C targets at 1 GeV/nucleon, charge-changing cross sections increase linearly with increasing isospin asymmetry (Fig. 7)
- For ⁵⁰Ca + ¹²C and ⁵⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca, the yield ratio of direct hard photons between central and peripheral collisions R_cp(σ_γ) decreases with increasing neutron skin thickness, with greater sensitivity at higher incident energies

## Context

This review extends earlier work on neutron skin measurements using hadron scattering (PhysRevLett.47.1436, PhysRevC.49.2118), giant dipole resonance (PhysRevLett.66.1287), spin dipole resonance (PhysRevLett.82.3216), and antiprotonic annihilation (PhysRevC.57.2962, PhysRevLett.87.082501), which the authors note are model-dependent. It connects to studies relating neutron skin thickness to the symmetry energy parameters L and K_sym (PhysRevC.72.064309, PhysRevLett.85.5296) and to neutron star properties including the mass-radius relation (PhysRevD.82.101301), crust-core transition (PhysRevLett.86.5647), and tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 (PhysRevLett.119.161101, PhysRevLett.120.172702). The paper enables future experimental extraction of neutron skin thickness from measurable reaction observables at radioactive nuclear beam facilities, which will help constrain the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron star properties.
