# Pre-thermalization dynamics: initial conditions for QGP at the LHC and RHIC from perturbative QCD

> I discuss how the initial conditions for QGP-production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC can be computed from perturbative QCD.

## Key claim
Initial conditions for QGP production at LHC and RHIC can be computed from lowest-order perturbative QCD, but the computation depends on a cutoff parameter \(p_0\) (the minimum transverse momentum for minijets) which must be fixed by a saturation criterion or other phenomenological considerations, and the results are valid only when the parton densities are high enough that perturbative production dominates over soft processes.

## Method
The author computes the average number and transverse energy of minijets (gluons, quarks, and antiquarks) produced in central rapidity (\(|y| \le 0.5\)) in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (\(\sqrt{s}/A = 5.5\) TeV) and RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}/A = 200\) GeV) using lowest-order pQCD \(2 \to 2\) parton scattering cross sections, collinear factorization with GRV94 LO parton distributions, and the nuclear overlap function \(T_{AA}(b)\). The cutoff \(p_0\) is set to 2 GeV for both energies based on a saturation criterion comparing the transverse area occupied by partons to the nuclear transverse area, and the results are presented at the formation time \(\tau_h \sim 1/p_0 \sim 0.1\) fm/\(c\).

## Key numbers
- At LHC (Pb+Pb, \(\sqrt{s}/A = 5.5\) TeV, \(b=0\), \(p_0 = 2\) GeV, \(|y| \le 0.5\)): total average number of semihard partons \(\bar N_{\rm PbPb} = 4741\) (4350 gluons, 200 quarks, 191 antiquarks); total average transverse energy \(\bar E_{\rm T} = 14160\) GeV (12950 GeV from gluons, 619 GeV from quarks, 590 GeV from antiquarks).
- At RHIC (Pb+Pb, \(\sqrt{s}/A = 200\) GeV, \(b=0\), \(p_0 = 2\) GeV, \(|y| \le 0.5\)): total average number of semihard partons \(\bar N_{\rm PbPb} = 121\) (99.6 gluons, 13.0 quarks, 8.10 antiquarks); total average transverse energy \(\bar E_{\rm T} = 321\) GeV (263 GeV from gluons, 36.1 GeV from quarks, 21.9 GeV from antiquarks).
- The saturation criterion for LHC gives \(p_0 \approx 2\) GeV; for RHIC it gives \(p_0 \approx 1\) GeV (but the author fixes \(p_0 = 2\) GeV for both).
- For LHC, the initial gluon energy density corresponds to an ideal gas equilibrium temperature \(T_{\rm eq} \approx 1.1\) GeV.
- Initial net baryon number density at \(|y| \le 0.5\): 0.21 fm\(^{-3}\) for LHC, 0.12 fm\(^{-3}\) for RHIC.
- Net baryon-to-entropy ratio: \((B-\bar B)/S \sim 1/5000\) for LHC, \(\sim 1/2000\) for RHIC.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD approach to initial conditions for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, building on earlier minijet calculations by Blaizot and Mueller (1987), Kajantie, Landshoff, and Lindfors (1987), and Eskola, Kajantie, and Lindfors (1994). It provides specific numerical predictions for LHC and RHIC that can be used as input for hydrodynamic or parton cascade models (e.g., HIJING by Wang and Gyulassy, and the Parton Cascade Model by Geiger). The analysis highlights the dominance of gluons and the need for next-to-leading order calculations, resummation techniques, and better understanding of small-\(x\) physics (BFKL) and classical gluon field approaches (McLerran and Venugopalan) to reduce uncertainties.
