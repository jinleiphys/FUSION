# Separable Forces for $(d,p)$ Reactions in Momentum Space

> Treating $(d,p)$ reactions in a Faddeev-AGS framework requires the interactions in the sub-systems as input. We derived separable representations for the neutron- and proton-nucleus interactions from phenomenological global optical potentials. In order to take into account excitations of the nucleus, excitations need to be included explicity, leading to a coupled-channel separable representation of the optical potential.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) method can be generalized to produce separable representations of non-Hermitian optical potentials for both neutron-nucleus and proton-nucleus systems, including coupled-channel cases for deformed nuclei, enabling their use in Faddeev-AGS calculations of (d,p) reactions in momentum space. The key condition is that the separable representation exactly reproduces the original potential's wavefunctions only at the chosen support energies, with quality depending on the number and placement of those energies.

## Method
The authors generalized the EST separable representation method to non-Hermitian optical potentials and extended it further to include point Coulomb interactions by recasting the formalism in a Coulomb basis rather than a plane wave basis. For deformed nuclei, they constructed a coupled-channel separable representation of the Olsson optical potential for n+12C including the 2+ and 4+ excited states, with EST support points at 5, 16.5, and 45 MeV. The separable representations were tested by comparing cross sections from the original coordinate-space potentials against those from the momentum-space separable representations.

## Key numbers
- Rank-5 separable representations of the CH89 proton-nucleus optical potential for 48Ca and 208Pb reproduced unpolarized differential cross sections for elastic proton scattering in good agreement with coordinate-space calculations.
- For n+12C, a rank-3 separable representation of the Olsson coupled-channels optical potential was constructed with EST support points at 5, 16.5, and 45 MeV in the 0 to 50 MeV energy regime.
- Elastic and inelastic (0+ to 2+) scattering cross sections for n+12C at 17.6 MeV are shown scaled up by a factor of four in the comparison figure.

## Context
This work extends the EST separable representation method (Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler, 1974) from real potentials to non-Hermitian optical potentials, building on prior neutron-nucleus results for 48Ca, 132Sn, and 208Pb (Hlophe et al., 2015) and proton-nucleus results for 48Ca and 208Pb (Hlophe et al., 2015). The coupled-channel extension for deformed nuclei, demonstrated here for n+12C using the Olsson optical potential (Olsson, 1989), enables future Faddeev-AGS calculations of (d,p) reactions that include nuclear excitations, which is necessary for treating reactions on heavier nuclei where screening techniques for charged particles in Faddeev equations break down.
