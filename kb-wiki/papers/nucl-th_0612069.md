# Vector meson form factors and their quark-mass dependence

> The electromagnetic form factors of vector mesons are calculated in an explicitly Poincaré covariant formulation, based on the Dyson--Schwinger equations of QCD, that respects electromagnetic current conservation, and unambiguously incorporates effects from vector meson poles in the quark-photon vertex. This method incorporates a 2-parameter effective interaction, where the parameters are constrained by the experimental values of chiral condensate and $f_π$. This approach has successfully described a large amount of light-quark meson experimental data, e.g. ground state pseudoscalar masses and their electromagnetic form factors; ground state vector meson masses and strong and electroweak decays. Here we apply it to predict the electromagnetic properties of vector mesons. The results for the static properties of the $ρ$-meson are: charge radius $<r_ρ^2 > = 0.54 {\rm fm}^2$, magnetic moment $μ= 2.01$, and quadrupole moment ${\cal Q} = -0.41$. We investigate the quark mass dependence of these static properties and find that our results at the charm quark mass are in agreement with recent lattice simulations. The charge radius decreases with increasing quark mass, but the magnetic moment is almost independent of the quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the electromagnetic form factors of light vector mesons (ρ, K*) using a Poincaré-covariant Dyson-Schwinger equation framework that incorporates vector meson pole effects in the quark-photon vertex, with the hedge that the model's two parameters are fixed by chiral condensate and fπ, making all meson electromagnetic predictions parameter-free.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for vector meson bound states in rainbow-ladder truncation, using a two-parameter effective interaction (ω = 0.4 GeV, D = 0.93 GeV²) fitted to reproduce fπ and the chiral condensate. They then computed the electromagnetic form factors via the impulse approximation, solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-photon vertex (which automatically includes vector meson poles) and evaluating the triangle diagram for each photon momentum transfer Q².

## Key numbers

- ρ meson charge radius: ⟨r_ρ²⟩ = 0.54 fm²
- ρ meson magnetic moment: μ = 2.01 (in units of e/2M_ρ)
- ρ meson quadrupole moment: Q = -0.41 (in units of e/M_ρ²)
- Charged K* meson charge radius: ⟨r_K*²⟩ = 0.43 fm²
- Charged K* meson magnetic moment: μ = 2.23
- Charged K* meson quadrupole moment: Q = -0.38
- Neutral K* meson charge radius: ⟨r_K*²⟩ = -0.08 fm²
- Neutral K* meson magnetic moment: μ = -0.26
- Neutral K* meson quadrupole moment: Q = 0.01
- J/ψ meson charge radius: ⟨r_J/ψ²⟩ = 0.052(3) fm²
- J/ψ meson magnetic moment: μ = 2.13(4)
- J/ψ meson quadrupole moment: Q = -0.28(1)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach previously applied successfully to pseudoscalar meson form factors (Maris 1999, Maris 2000) and vector meson masses and decays (Maris 1999) to predict vector meson electromagnetic properties. The results improve upon earlier DSE calculations (Hawes 1998) that used a Ball-Chiu ansatz for the quark-photon vertex rather than the full inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter solution, and the authors find their ρ meson charge radius (0.54 fm²) is significantly larger than the 0.37 fm² from that earlier work. The calculations at charm quark mass agree with lattice simulations (Dudek 2006), and the framework enables future studies of vector meson form factors at finite momentum transfer and for heavier quark systems.
