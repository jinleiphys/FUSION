# Influence of vibrational excitation on surface diffuseness of inter-nuclear potential: study through heavy-ion quasi-elastic scattering at deep subbarrier energies

> We discuss the role of channel coupling in the surface properties of an inter-nuclear potential for heavy-ion reactions. To this end, we analyze the experimental quasi-elastic cross sections for the $^{12}$C + $^{105,106}$Pd and $^{13}$C + $^{105,106}$Pd systems using the coupled-channels approach by including the vibrational excitations in the target nuclei. While earlier studies have reported a negligible influence of vibrational excitation on the surface diffuseness parameter for spherical systems, we find a significant effect for the C+Pd systems. Our systematic study also reveals influence of transfer couplings on the surface diffuseness parameter.

## Key claim

While earlier studies (e.g., Gasques et al., Washiyama et al.) reported a negligible influence of vibrational excitation on the surface diffuseness parameter for spherical systems, this paper finds a significant effect for the C+Pd systems, provided the vibrational coupling is strong enough (i.e., the deformation parameter is large and the excitation energy is small).

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental quasi-elastic scattering cross sections for the 12C + 105,106Pd and 13C + 105,106Pd systems measured at 165 degrees in the laboratory frame. They performed both single-channel and coupled-channels calculations using a scattering version of the CCFULL program, including double quadrupole phonon excitations in the target nuclei in the harmonic oscillator limit, and extracted the surface diffuseness parameter via chi-square fitting of the quasi-elastic excitation functions at deep subbarrier energies.

## Key numbers

- For the 12C + 105Pd system, the single-channel best-fit surface diffuseness was 0.80 ± 0.04 fm; the coupled-channels value was 0.69 ± 0.04 fm.
- For the 12C + 106Pd system, the single-channel best-fit surface diffuseness was 0.94 ± 0.07 fm; the coupled-channels value was 0.78 ± 0.05 fm.
- For the 13C + 105Pd system, the single-channel best-fit surface diffuseness was 0.64 ± 0.05 fm; the coupled-channels value was 0.60 ± 0.05 fm.
- For the 13C + 106Pd system, the single-channel best-fit surface diffuseness was 0.76 ± 0.04 fm; the coupled-channels value was 0.68 ± 0.03 fm.
- The deformation parameter for 106Pd was β = 0.229 with excitation energy E* = 0.512 MeV; for 105Pd the averaged values were β = 0.219 and E* = 0.534 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-elastic method of Hagino et al. (Hagino2) for extracting surface diffuseness, which had previously given values around 0.6 fm for spherical systems (Washiyama et al.) but larger values for deformed systems (Gasques et al.). The paper contradicts the earlier conclusion of Gasques et al. and Washiyama et al. that vibrational coupling has a marginal effect on surface diffuseness, showing instead that the effect becomes significant when the coupling is strong (large β and low E*). The results also suggest that neutron transfer coupling (positive Q-value for 1n transfer in 13C projectiles) further reduces the extracted diffuseness, motivating future coupled-channels calculations that include both collective excitations and transfer channels.
