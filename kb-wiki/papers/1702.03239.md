# Low energy magnetic radiation enhancement in the f$_{7/2}$ shell

> Studies of the $γ$-ray strength functions can reveal useful information concerning underlying nuclear structure. Accumulated experimental data on the strength functions show an enhancement in the low $γ$ energy region. We have calculated the M1 strength functions for the $^{49,50}$Cr and $^{48}$V nuclei in the $f_{7/2}$ shell-model basis. We find a low-energy enhancement for gamma decay similar to that obtained for other nuclei in previous studies, but for the first time we are also able to study the complete distribution related to M1 emission and absorption. We find that M1 strength distribution peaks at zero transition energy and falls off exponentially. The height of the peak and the slope of the exponential are approximately independent of the nuclei studied in this model space and the range of initial angular momenta. We show that the slope of the exponential fall off is proportional to the energy of the $T=1$ pairing gap.

## Key claim

The M1 strength distribution for nuclei in the f7/2 shell peaks at zero transition energy and falls off exponentially, with the slope of the exponential proportional to the T=1 pairing gap; this low-energy enhancement is a generic feature for excited states that cannot be obtained from ground-state information, contrary to the Brink-Axel hypothesis.

## Method

Shell-model calculations using the OXBASH code with the F742 Hamiltonian in the f7/2 model space were performed for 49Cr, 50Cr, and 48V, considering states with T=Tz. The B(M1) values were sorted by transition energy Eγ, binned in 0.2 MeV intervals, and averaged to obtain the average M1 strength distribution ⟨B(M1)(Eγ)⟩. The Hamiltonian was decomposed into T=0 and T=1 parts using coefficients k0 and k1 to study the dependence of the distribution on each isospin component.

## Key numbers

- Average B(M1) value for 50Cr states between 6 and 8 MeV: 12.5 μN²
- Exponential fit parameters for the M1 distribution: B0 = 0.75 μN², TB = 1.33 MeV
- For 94-96Mo and 90Zr in prior work (M1enha8), exponential slopes for positive parity states range from TB = 0.33 to 0.41 MeV, and for negative parity states from TB = 0.50 to 0.58 MeV (0.29 MeV for 90Zr)
- Pairing gap Δ for this group of nuclei: approximately 1.5 MeV
- The constant a in the γSF formula: 11.5473 × 10⁻⁹ μN⁻² MeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of low-energy M1 enhancement previously observed in 90Zr, 94-96Mo (M1enha8) and 56,57Fe (Brown 2015) to the f7/2 shell nuclei 48V, 49Cr, and 50Cr. It contradicts the Brink-Axel hypothesis by showing that the low-energy M1 distribution peaks at zero energy for excited states, unlike the giant dipole resonance behavior. The results enable comparison with experimental γSF data for 50V (Larsen 2017) and demonstrate that the T=1 pairing interaction determines the exponential slope, while the T=0 interaction has negligible effect. The study also shows that mixing of the f7/2 orbital with other pf-shell orbitals quenches the low-energy enhancement, with the f5/2 orbital producing a spin-flip peak at Eγ = 6-8 MeV.
