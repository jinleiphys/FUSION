# Spinodal decomposition during the hadronization stage at RHIC?

> The expansion of strongly interacting matter formed in high-energy nuclear collisions drives the system through the region of phase coexistence. The present study examines the associated spinodal instability and finds that the degree of amplification may be sufficient to raise the prospect of using the spinodal pattern formation as a diagnostic tool for probing the hadronization phase transition.

## Key claim

The spinodal instability associated with a first-order hadronization phase transition may produce sufficient amplification of density perturbations during the expansion of matter formed at RHIC to generate observable spatial patterns, but the quantitative significance depends on the effective interaction range and the expansion time, with growth factors reaching only modest values (approximately 4-8) under the assumptions used.

## Method

The author constructs an equation of state for strongly interacting matter by interpolating between an ideal hadron gas (14 species) and a bag-model quark-gluon plasma using a cubic spline that matches pressure and sound speed at both phase boundaries, yielding a convex anomaly region where dp/dε < 0. Linearized relativistic fluid dynamics is then applied to a uniform static system, with the local pressure obtained by convolution with a Gaussian kernel of range a to account for finite interaction range, and the growth of harmonic perturbations is calculated as the system traverses the spinodal region during expansion.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_c = 170 MeV
- Critical pressure: p_c = 54 MeV fm^{-3}
- Hadronic critical energy density: ε_1 = 220 MeV fm^{-3}
- Plasma critical energy density: ε_2 = 2220 MeV fm^{-3}
- Latent heat: Δε = 2000 MeV fm^{-3}
- Bag constant: B^{1/4} = 250 MeV
- Sound speed squared at hadronic boundary: v_1^2 = 0.27
- Sound speed squared at plasma boundary: v_2^2 = 0.33
- Maximum negative slope of pressure in spinodal region: (dp/dε)_tilde = -0.150
- Time to traverse spinodal region: t_B - t_A ≈ 4 fm/c
- For range a = 0.5 fm: maximum growth factor G_max ≈ 8 at wavelength λ ≈ 2 fm
- For range a = 0.7 fm: maximum growth factor G_max ≈ 4 at wavelength λ ≈ 3 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of spinodal decomposition from nuclear multifragmentation (Heiselberg 1988, Borderie 2001) to the hadronization stage of high-energy nuclear collisions at RHIC. Previous fluid dynamical studies (Rischke 1995, Sollfrank 1997, Hirano 2002) suppressed the spinodal instability by using constant pressure across the mixed phase. The results suggest that if spinodal patterns could be detected experimentally via HBT interferometry or flow coefficients v_m, they would provide direct information on the equation of state in the critical region; conversely, absence of such patterns would indicate the phase transition is not first order.
