# Nucleon Electromagnetic Form Factors in the Timelike Region

> The electromagnetic form factors of the proton and the neutron in the timelike region are reviewed. In view of the forthcoming experimental projects devoted to investigate these observables, we present the current status of the field and we emphasize the relevant role, that accurate measurements, refined phenomenological analyses, as well as microscopic models will play with the goal of achieving deeper insights into the structure of the nucleon and its inner dynamics.

## Key claim

The review argues that while timelike nucleon electromagnetic form factors offer unique access to the non-perturbative regime of QCD through the physical hadronic components of the virtual photon, the experimental database remains too scarce to fully exploit this potential; only measurements of the relative phases (via polarization degrees of freedom) will fully determine the nucleon form factors in the timelike region and enable maximal phenomenological knowledge.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of timelike nucleon electromagnetic form factors obtained via three techniques: energy scan experiments at e+e- colliders (ADONE, DCI, BEPC, CESR), antiproton annihilation experiments at fixed-target facilities (LEAR, FNAL), and the initial state radiation (ISR) technique at B-factories (BaBar). On the theoretical side, the review covers the general formalism of timelike form factors, dispersion relations connecting real and imaginary parts, and several theoretical approaches including vector meson dominance models and constituent quark models.

## Key numbers

- The first measurement of a timelike nucleon form factor was performed at ADONE in Frascati in 1972 at a center-of-mass energy of sqrt(s)=2.1 GeV/c
- The BaBar experiment measured the proton form factor from threshold up to 4.5 GeV using an integrated luminosity of approximately 500 fb^-1
- The PS170 experiment at LEAR collected almost 2,000 events near threshold for the reaction p pbar -> e+e-
- The FNAL experiments E761 and E835 covered the high-q^2 range between 8.2 (GeV/c)^2 and 18.2 (GeV/c)^2
- The BES-II experiment measured the proton form factor at 10 scanning points in the range 2.0 GeV/c < sqrt(s) < 3.07 GeV/c, with systematic uncertainty close to 10% in all scan points
- The FENICE experiment measured the neutron form factor at 5 scanning points from 1.9 GeV to 2.44 GeV, collecting 74 events
- The DM2 experiment measured the proton form factor at 6 scanning points from 1.975 GeV to 2.25 GeV, with approximately 100 events total

## Context

This review extends the extensive spacelike form factor measurements initiated by Hofstadter, which achieved percent-level precision and revealed the striking fall-off of the proton electric form factor relative to the magnetic one via the polarization-transfer method. The timelike region provides complementary information through dispersion relations, as originally pointed out by Federbush, Goldberger and Treiman (1958), linking form factors to vector meson decays and hadronic transition amplitudes. The review is timely given new facilities at Novosibirsk, Beijing, and FAIR/Darmstadt that recently came into operation or are about to start, and it aims to enable a more detailed database that includes not only electric and magnetic distribution densities but also information on other physical hadronic processes.
