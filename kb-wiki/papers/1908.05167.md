# Single-Energy Partial Wave Analysis for $π^0$ Photoproduction on Proton with Fixed-$t$ Analyticity Imposed

> High precision data of the $γp \to π^0 p$ reaction from its threshold up to $W=2$~GeV have been used in order to perform a single-energy partial wave analysis with minimal model dependence. Continuity in energy was achieved by imposing constraints from fixed-$t$ analyticity in an iterative procedure. Reaction models were only used as starting point in the very first iteration. We demonstrate that with this procedure partial wave amplitudes can be obtained which show only a minimal dependence on the initial model assumptions.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that by imposing fixed-\(t\) analyticity constraints in an iterative procedure, single-energy partial wave amplitudes for \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 p\) can be obtained that show only minimal dependence on the initial reaction model used as a starting point, up to \(W = 1.7\) GeV.

## Method
The method couples two analyses in an iterative loop: a fixed-\(t\) amplitude analysis (FT AA) using Pietarinen expansions to parametrize energy-dependent helicity amplitudes, and a single-energy partial wave analysis (SE PWA) fitting multipoles to angular distributions at each energy bin. The FT AA provides continuity constraints for the SE PWA via penalty functions, and the resulting SE PWA amplitudes are fed back as constraints for the next FT AA iteration. The procedure is repeated until convergence (typically 3–4 iterations), starting from one of four energy-dependent models (BnGa, JüBo, SAID, or MAID) as the initial constraint.

## Key numbers
- Data from threshold up to \(W = 2\) GeV were used, with the majority of data covering \(W < 1.7\) GeV.
- The fixed-\(t\) amplitude analysis was performed at 20 equidistant \(t\) values in the range \(-1.00\ \text{GeV}^2 < t < -0.005\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- At energies \(W < 1.7\) GeV, up to eight observables (\(\sigma_0, \Sigma, T, P, E, F, G, H\)) were available, with a maximum of five fitted simultaneously.
- At \(W = 1.201\) GeV, four measured observables were used; at \(W = 1.481\) GeV, five measured observables; at \(W = 1.660\) GeV, four measured observables.
- The initial models were randomly scattered with 30% uncertainty to generate four different SE solutions (SE1–SE4).
- The averaged SEav solution was created by averaging over SE1–SE4 and using that as input for a final iteration.

## Context
This work extends the fixed-\(t\) analyticity method previously applied to \(\eta\) photoproduction on the proton by Osmanović et al. (2018) to the more complicated \(\pi^0\) photoproduction channel, where both isospin \(I=1/2\) and \(I=3/2\) resonances contribute. It addresses the continuum ambiguity problem in single-energy partial wave analyses identified by Omelaenko (1981), Wunderlich (2017), and Workman (2016), and builds on the comparison of energy-dependent models (BnGa, JüBo, SAID, MAID) reported by Beck et al. The results enable more model-independent extraction of resonance parameters and suggest that additional measurements of recoil observables (e.g., \(T, P, C_{x'}, O_{z'}\)) at energies above 1.7 GeV are needed to reduce remaining ambiguities.
