# NN Core Interactions and Differential Cross Sections from One Gluon Exchange

> We derive nonstrange baryon-baryon scattering amplitudes in the nonrelativistic quark model using the ``quark Born diagram" formalism. This approach describes the scattering as a single interaction, here the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) spin-spin term followed by constituent interchange, with external nonrelativistic baryon wavefunctions attached to the scattering diagrams to incorporate higher-twist wavefunction effects. The short-range repulsive core in the NN interaction has previously been attributed to this spin-spin interaction in the literature; we find that these perturbative constituent-interchange diagrams do indeed predict repulsive interactions in all I,S channels of the nucleon-nucleon system, and we compare our results for the equivalent short-range potentials to the core potentials found by other authors using nonperturbative methods. We also apply our perturbative techniques to the N$Δ$ and $ΔΔ$ systems: Some $ΔΔ$ channels are found to have attractive core potentials and may accommodate ``molecular" bound states near threshold. Finally we use our Born formalism to calculate the NN differential cross section, which we compare with experimental results for unpolarised proton-proton elastic scattering. We find that several familiar features of the experimental differential cross section are reproduced by our Born-order result.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the short-range repulsive core in the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction can be derived from perturbative one-gluon-exchange (OGE) spin-spin diagrams followed by constituent interchange, using the quark Born formalism, but only at Born order and with the caveat that the results are compared to core potentials found by other authors using nonperturbative methods.

## Method

The authors used the nonrelativistic quark model with the "quark Born diagram" formalism, calculating baryon-baryon scattering amplitudes from the OGE spin-spin hyperfine interaction Hamiltonian. They attached nonrelativistic baryon wavefunctions to external lines, evaluated eight independent spatial overlap integrals (with single-Gaussian wavefunctions for closed-form results), and summed weighted contributions from color, spin-flavor, and signature factors to derive scattering matrix elements for NN, NΔ, and ΔΔ systems.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator parameter values used: 0.25 GeV ≤ α ≤ 0.42 GeV, as cited from the baryon spectroscopy literature.
- For ΔΔ scattering in the I=3, S=3 channel, the diagram weights are { +1, -1, -1, +1 | (-) }.
- The spatial overlap integral I₁ = κ_ss exp{ t / (3α²) }, where κ_ss = (8πα_s)/(3m_q²) × 1/(2π)³.
- The spatial overlap integral I₂ = I₃ = κ_ss × (12/11)^(3/2) × exp{ -2(s-4M²)/(33α²) } × exp{ 2t/(11α²) }.
- The spatial overlap integral I₄ = κ_ss × (3/4)^(3/2) × exp{ -(s-4M²)/(12α²) }.

## Context

This work extends the quark Born formalism previously applied to meson-meson scattering (Barnes and Swanson, 1992) and KN scattering (Barnes, Swanson, and Kovarik, 1992) to the baryon-baryon sector, specifically the q⁶ system. It contrasts with nonperturbative methods such as the resonating-group method used by Oka and Yazaki (1981), Ribeiro (1980), and others, and with the variational calculation of Maltman and Isgur (1984). The results enable predictions of possible ΔΔ "molecular" bound states near threshold and provide a perturbative framework for calculating NN differential cross sections at higher energies, complementing earlier adiabatic and resonating-group approaches.
