# Alpha-Clustering in Nuclei and Its Impact on Nuclear Symmetry Energy

> Nuclear symmetry energy is a fundamental quantity currently under intense investigation in both nuclear physics and astrophysics. The {\it softness} or {\it stiffness} of symmetry energy is still under debate and the extraction of symmetry energy from neutron skin thickness $R_{\rm skin}$ remains a challenge. Parity-violating measurements PREX and CREX provide important opportunities for constraining $R_{\rm skin}$ in $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca. We investigate the occurrence of $α$-cluster at the surface of nuclei and its impact on the extraction of symmetry energy from $R_{\rm skin}$. Our result indicates that the $α$-clustering probability in $^{208}$Pb is small and the extracted density slope of symmetry energy $L$ is almost unaffected. In contrast, the $α$-clustering probability in $^{48}$Ca is sizeable and the corresponding correction to $L$ should be taken into account. This correction progressively increases with the $α$-clustering probability, leading to a modification of the $L$-$R_{\rm skin}$ correlation, a fact may have important implications in constraining nuclear symmetry energy.

# Alpha-Clustering in Nuclei and Its Impact on Nuclear Symmetry Energy

## Key claim
The density slope of nuclear symmetry energy L extracted from neutron skin thickness measurements is almost unaffected by alpha-clustering in 208Pb due to a very small formation probability (9.3×10⁻³), but in 48Ca the alpha-clustering probability is sizeable (7.3×10⁻²) and produces a correction to L of about 14% that should be taken into account when interpreting PREX-2 and CREX data.

## Method
The authors use the quartetting wave function approach (QWFA) to calculate alpha-cluster formation probabilities at the surfaces of 208Pb and 48Ca. This involves solving the in-medium Schrödinger equation for four-nucleon correlations, treating the intrinsic motion of the alpha-cluster and its center-of-mass motion separately, including complex derivative terms that had not been previously investigated. The density slope of symmetry energy L is then extracted from the neutron skin thickness using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, with the isovector potential determined from a Woods-Saxon global optical potential parameterization that reproduces alpha-decay half-lives around doubly magic nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Alpha-cluster formation probability in 208Pb: Pα = 9.3×10⁻³
- Alpha-cluster formation probability in 48Ca: Pα = 7.3×10⁻²
- Critical density for alpha-cluster dissolution: ρc = 0.03 fm⁻³
- Free alpha-cluster intrinsic energy: W^intr = -28.3 MeV at variational parameter a = 0.535 fm⁻²
- L extracted from PREX-2 data for 208Pb without alpha-clustering: 75.2⁺²⁴·³₋₂₄·₅ MeV; with alpha-clustering: 75.3⁺²⁴·³₋₂₄·₆ MeV
- L extracted from CREX data for 48Ca without alpha-clustering: 13.2⁺²⁵·⁴₋₂₄·₉ MeV; with alpha-clustering: 15.0⁺²⁵·⁶₋₂₅·₀ MeV
- Neutron skin thickness for 208Pb (PREX-2): Rskin = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- Neutron skin thickness for 48Ca (CREX): Rskin = 0.121 ± 0.050 fm
- Effective mass ratio used: m*/m = 0.70 ± 0.05
- Correction to L for lower limit of Rskin in 48Ca (0.071 fm): 100% (from 1.7 MeV to 3.4 MeV)
- Correction to L for upper limit of Rskin in 48Ca (0.171 fm): 8% (from 24.8 MeV to 26.8 MeV)

## Context
This work addresses the tension between PREX-2 results suggesting a stiff symmetry energy (thick neutron skin in 208Pb) and CREX results suggesting a soft symmetry energy (thin neutron skin in 48Ca), following earlier suggestions by Essick (2021) that alpha-clustering at nuclear surfaces could affect the extraction. The QWFA approach extends prior work by Xu (2016, 2017) and the quartet correlation studies, treating derivative terms of the intrinsic wave function for the first time. The results show that alpha-clustering cannot resolve the discrepancy between PREX-2 and CREX, motivating improved treatment of model-dependence in extracting Rskin from parity-violating asymmetry measurements and more sophisticated descriptions of shell model states and nuclear densities.
