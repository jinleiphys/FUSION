# Bremsstrahlung out of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> A systematic investigation of hard thermal photon spectra from central ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions is presented with emphasis on the effects of bremsstrahlung processes in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Bremsstrahlung photon production in the quark-gluon plasma has recently been considered within the Braaten-Pisarski method in thermal QCD, where rates have been found that exhibit the same order in the coupling constants as those describing the lowest order processes, Compton scattering and q\bar{q}-annihilation. The impact of these bremsstrahlung photon production rates on the thermal photon spectra is studied systematically within a simple, well understood one-fluid hydrodynamical model that describes an only longitudinally expanding fireball (1+1 Bjorken scaling hydrodynamics). It is found that the bremsstrahlung processes enhance the thermal photon yield from the QGP by about one order of magnitude over the complete considered p_{\perp}-range independent of the choice of the model parameters. Experimental upper limits on direct photon production in fixed target 200 A GeV S + Au collisions at the CERN SPS are also considered and used to extract upper limits for the initial temperature of the QGP, where the QGP bremsstrahlung processes are found to make a difference of about 15 to 20 MeV depending on the temperature at which the phase transition is assumed. In comparison with other theoretical studies, the importance of reaction features not described in the simple model are estimated and interesting elements for a future extension of this systematic investigation are identified, which will be of great interest in prospect of the upcoming experiments at the BNL RHIC and the CERN LHC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including bremsstrahlung processes in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) enhances the thermal photon yield by about one order of magnitude over the complete considered transverse momentum range, independent of the choice of model parameters. This enhancement is conditional on the use of the Braaten-Pisarski method in thermal QCD, which shows that bremsstrahlung rates are of the same order in the coupling constant as the previously considered Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation processes.

## Method

The author performed a systematic investigation of hard thermal photon spectra from central ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions using a one-fluid hydrodynamical model with 1+1 Bjorken scaling hydrodynamics, describing an only longitudinally expanding fireball. A first-order phase transition was implemented in which the QGP (simulated by an ideal massless parton gas of two flavors) hadronizes according to the Gibbs criteria and Maxwell construction into a hot hadronic gas (simulated by an ideal massless pion gas). The bremsstrahlung photon production rates from the Braaten-Pisarski method were integrated over the space-time history of the fireball and compared with the earlier rates for Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation from Kapusta (1991) and Baier (1991).

## Key numbers

- The bremsstrahlung processes enhance the thermal photon yield from the QGP by about **one order of magnitude** over the complete considered transverse momentum range, independent of model parameter choices.
- Experimental upper limits on direct photon production in fixed target **200 A·GeV** S + Au collisions at the CERN SPS were used to extract upper limits for the initial temperature of the QGP.
- The QGP bremsstrahlung processes make a difference of about **15 to 20 MeV** in the extracted upper limits for the initial temperature, depending on the temperature at which the phase transition is assumed.

## Context

This work extends earlier systematic investigations by Arbex (1995), Alam (1996), Sollfrank (1997), and Cleymans (1997), which implemented only Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation rates for the QGP phase, thus underestimating thermal photon emission. The new bremsstrahlung rates from Aurenche (1998) were found to be the dominant source for hard thermal photons in the QGP phase. This study enables more accurate predictions for upcoming experiments at the BNL RHIC and the CERN LHC, and identifies reaction features not described in the simple model that warrant future investigation.
