# Number conservation in odd-particle number random phase approximation and extensions

> The number conservation law in the odd-particle number random-phase approximation (oRPA) and its extension (EoRPA) is studied by applying them to a pairing model and $^{16}$O. It is found in the application to $^{16}$O that the number conservation law is not fulfilled in oRPA and EoRPA and that it is drastically improved in EoRPA due to the inclusion of ground-state correlation effects.

## Key claim

The number conservation law (Luttinger theorem) is not fulfilled in either the odd-particle number random-phase approximation (oRPA) or its extension (EoRPA) when applied to \(^{16}\)O, but the inclusion of ground-state correlation effects in EoRPA drastically improves the conservation of particle number compared to oRPA.

## Method

The author applies oRPA and EoRPA to a pairing model with \(N=4\) particles and to the nucleus \(^{16}\)O. EoRPA is formulated using a correlated ground state obtained from the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), which includes two-body correlation matrices \(C_2\). The transition amplitudes for single-particle addition and removal are calculated from the equations of motion, and the occupation probabilities are obtained from the residues of the single-particle Green's function poles. The number conservation condition is checked by evaluating whether the sum over transition amplitudes satisfies the relation \(\sum_\mu X^\mu_{\alpha'\beta':\beta}\chi^\mu_\alpha = \sum_\mu x^\mu_{\alpha'}{\cal X}^\mu_{\alpha\beta:\beta'}\).

## Key numbers

- For the pairing model with \(N=4\) and \(g/\Delta\epsilon = 0.5\), TDDM occupation probabilities for the four orbitals are: \(n_1 = 0.964\) (exact: 0.965), \(n_2 = 0.909\) (exact: 0.901), \(n_3 = 0.091\) (exact: 0.099), \(n_4 = 0.036\) (exact: 0.035).
- In the pairing model, the unperturbed energy of the \(Y^\mu_{3\bar{3}:1}\) configuration is \(4\,\Delta\epsilon\) and that of \(Y^\mu_{4\bar{4}:1}\) is \(6\,\Delta\epsilon\).
- In the pairing model, the unperturbed energy of the \(Y^\mu_{3\bar{3}:2}\) configuration is \(3\,\Delta\epsilon\) and that of \(Y^\mu_{4\bar{4}:2}\) is \(5\,\Delta\epsilon\).

## Context

This work extends the study of number conservation in approximate many-body methods, previously addressed in TDA by H. R. (1996) and FRPA by Dickhoff and collaborators (2002) for finite nuclei, and in nuclear matter by Ramos and Mahaux. The paper shows that oRPA violates the Luttinger theorem due to asymmetric treatment of particle-particle and hole-hole correlations, while EoRPA improves conservation by including ground-state correlations via TDDM. The results enable future applications of EoRPA to single-particle properties of nuclei where number conservation is critical.
