# Correlations and spectral functions in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The self-energy of nucleons in asymmetric nuclear matter is evaluated employing different realistic models for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Starting from the Brueckner Hartree Fock approximation without the usual angle-average in the two-nucleon propagator the effects of the hole-hole contributions are investigated within the self-consistent Green's function approach. Special attention is paid to the isospin-dependence of correlations, which can be deduced from the spectral functions of nucleons in asymmetric matter. The strong components of the proton - neutron interaction lead in neutron-rich matter to a larger depletion for the occupation probability of proton states below the Fermi momentum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in neutron-rich asymmetric nuclear matter, the strong components of the proton-neutron interaction lead to a larger depletion of the occupation probability for proton states below the Fermi momentum compared to neutron states, and that this effect can be deduced from the spectral functions. This result is conditional on the use of realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and a self-consistent Green's function approach that includes hole-hole scattering terms in a perturbative manner, avoiding the angle-average approximation in the two-nucleon propagator.

## Method

The authors evaluate the nucleon self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation without the usual angle-average in the two-nucleon propagator, then extend this to a self-consistent Green's function (GF) approach by including hole-hole (2h1p) scattering terms perturbatively. The Bethe-Goldstone equation is solved with the exact propagator using techniques from Schiller (1999), and the single-particle energies are determined self-consistently from the mean energy of the spectral distribution. Spectral functions for protons and neutrons are then computed from the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy, and occupation probabilities are obtained by integrating the hole spectral function up to the Fermi energy.

## Key numbers

- Total density considered: \(\rho = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (empirical saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter)
- Asymmetry parameter \(\alpha\) ranges from 0.05 to 0.75 in the BHF self-energy calculations
- For \(\alpha=0.75\), HF self-energies at \(k=0\) range from -52 MeV to -42 MeV for protons and neutrons, respectively, using the CD-Bonn interaction
- Using the Nijm2 interaction in HF approximation, self-energies at \(k=0\) range from 52 MeV to -8 MeV
- Effective \(k\)-mass values range from 0.6 m up to 0.9 m for larger \(k\) (CD-Bonn, \(\alpha=0.5\))
- GF Fermi energies are shifted by approximately 7 MeV compared to BHF Fermi energies
- Energy per nucleon for symmetric matter \(E_0\): CD-Bonn BHF = -18.8 MeV, CD-Bonn GF = -18.0 MeV; Nijm2 BHF = -16.7 MeV, Nijm2 GF = -12.3 MeV; experimental = -15.7 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient \(a_S\): CD-Bonn BHF = 31.5 MeV, CD-Bonn GF = 33.3 MeV; Nijm2 BHF = 29.3 MeV, Nijm2 GF = 34.0 MeV; experimental = 30 \(\pm\) 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Green's function approach previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter by Hassaneen and Müther (2004) to the case of asymmetric nuclear matter, using the same approximation scheme that avoids the angle-average approximation and treats hole-hole terms perturbatively. The study aims to distinguish correlation effects from tensor components versus central short-range components of the NN interaction by comparing results from the CD-Bonn potential (non-local) and the Nijm2 interaction (local). The results enable predictions for nucleon knock-out experiments on neutron-rich nuclei, such as \((e,e'p)\) reactions, and suggest that a more systematic study treating hole-hole scattering terms non-perturbatively would be useful, as noted by Bozek (2002), Frick (2004), and Bozek (2003).
