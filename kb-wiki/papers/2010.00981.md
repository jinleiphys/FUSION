# Equation of state of strongly magnetized matter with hyperons and $Δ$-resonances

> We construct a new equation of state for the baryonic matter under an intense magnetic field within the framework of covariant density functional theory. The composition of matter includes hyperons as well as $ Δ$-resonances. The extension of the nucleonic functional to the hypernuclear sector is constrained by the experimental data on $Λ$ and $Ξ$-hypernuclei. We find that the equation of state stiffens with the inclusion of the magnetic field, which increases the maximum mass of neutron star compared to the non-magnetic case. In addition, the strangeness fraction in the matter is enhanced. Several observables, like the Dirac effective mass, particle abundances, etc show typical oscillatory behavior as a function of the magnetic field and/or density which is traced back to the occupation pattern of Landau levels.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a new equation of state for baryonic matter under intense magnetic fields that includes both hyperons and Delta-resonances, finding that the equation of state stiffens with magnetic field and increases the maximum neutron star mass compared to the non-magnetic case, with the caveat that the extension of the nucleonic functional to the hypernuclear sector is constrained by experimental data on Lambda and Xi-hypernuclei.

## Method
The authors employed covariant density functional theory to construct an equation of state for baryonic matter in the presence of an intense magnetic field. The composition includes nucleons, hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi), and Delta-resonances, with the hypernuclear sector constrained by experimental data on Lambda and Xi-hypernuclei. The Dirac effective mass, particle abundances, and other observables were computed as functions of magnetic field strength and baryon density, revealing oscillatory behavior attributed to the occupation pattern of Landau levels.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutron star matter equations of state by incorporating both hyperons and Delta-resonances under strong magnetic fields, building on the covariant density functional framework. Prior work on hypernuclear matter without magnetic fields (e.g., studies of hyperon-nucleon interactions constrained by hypernuclei data) is extended to include the effects of intense magnetic fields relevant to magnetars. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star structure and composition in strongly magnetized environments, particularly for understanding the maximum mass and strangeness content of magnetars.
