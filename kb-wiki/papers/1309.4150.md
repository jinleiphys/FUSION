# Nucleon-nucleon interactions via Lattice QCD: Methodology --HAL QCD approach to extract hadronic interactions in lattice QCD--

> We review the potential method in lattice QCD, which has recently been proposed to extract nucleon-nucleon interactions via numerical simulations. We focus on the methodology of this approach by emphasizing the strategy of the potential method, the theoretical foundation behind it, and special numerical techniques. We compare the potential method with the standard finite volume method in lattice QCD, in order to make pros and cons of the approach clear. We also present several numerical results for the nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key claim

The HAL QCD method defines a non-local, energy-independent nucleon-nucleon potential from equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions in lattice QCD, but the potential itself is scheme-dependent (e.g., depends on the choice of nucleon interpolating operator) and is practically approximated via a velocity expansion whose convergence must be checked case by case.

## Method

The method extracts the equal-time NBS wave function for two nucleons from four-point correlation functions on the lattice, then defines a non-local potential through the relation (E_k - H_0) Psi = integral U Psi. In practice, the non-local potential is expanded in powers of velocity (derivatives) to obtain local coefficient functions (central, spin-spin, tensor, spin-orbit) order by order. An improved extraction uses a time-dependent Schrodinger-like equation applied to the ratio R(x,t) = F(x,t) / (exp(-m_N t))^2, which avoids requiring ground-state saturation of the correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.091 fm (a^{-1} = 2.176(31) GeV) from 2+1 flavor QCD configurations on a 32^3 x 64 lattice with Iwasaki gauge action at beta = 1.9.
- Pion mass m_pi = 701 MeV, kaon mass m_K = 789 MeV, nucleon mass m_N = 1583 MeV for the full QCD calculation.
- Spatial extent L = 32a = 2.90 fm; 390 gauge configurations with 21 source points per configuration were used.
- The third (relativistic correction) term in the time-dependent extraction was found to be negligible, confirming the non-relativistic approximation Delta W approx k^2/m_N works well at this quark mass.

## Context

This work reviews the HAL QCD potential method, which extends the standard Luscher finite-volume method (Luscher, 1990) for extracting scattering phase shifts from lattice QCD. The Luscher method gives exact phase shifts at discrete momenta but requires ground-state saturation and large volumes; the HAL QCD method instead provides a continuous phase shift via a potential, at the cost of systematic uncertainty from the velocity expansion. The approach has been applied to baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and three-nucleon systems by the HAL QCD collaboration, and enables chiral extrapolation of potentials to the physical pion mass, which is expected to be more stable than extrapolating the scattering length directly.
