# Investigating the weak charge of $^{48}$Ca using a dispersive optical model

> A new nonlocal dispersive-optical-model analysis has been carried out for neutrons and protons in $^{48}$Ca that reproduces the weak-form-factor measurement of CREX. In addition to elastic-scattering angular distributions, total and reaction cross sections, single-particle energies, the neutron and proton numbers, and the charge distribution, the CREX-measured weak form factor has been fit to extract the neutron and proton self-energies both above and below the Fermi energy. The resulting single-particle propagators yield a weak form factor of $F_W = 0.125 \pm 0.05$ and a neutron skin of $R_\mathrm{skin} = 0.152 \pm 0.05$ fm, in good agreement with CREX. The rearrangement of the neutron distribution to accommodate such a thin neutron skin results in the high-momentum content of the neutrons exceeding that of the protons, in contrast to what is expected from high-energy two-nucleon knockout measurements by the CLAS collaboration and ab initio asymmetric matter calculations. The present analysis also emphasizes the importance of neutron experimental data in constraining weak charge observables necessary for a precise description of neutron densities. Notably, the neutron reaction cross section and further parity-violating experiments weak form factor measurements are essential to generate a unique way to determine the $^{48}$Ca neutron distribution in this framework.

## Key claim

By including the CREX weak-form-factor measurement as a constraint, the dispersive-optical-model analysis of ⁴⁸Ca yields a thin neutron skin of 0.152 ± 0.05 fm, but this rearrangement of the neutron distribution produces a high-momentum content in neutrons that exceeds that of protons, contradicting expectations from CLAS knockout measurements and ab initio asymmetric matter calculations.

## Method

A nonlocal dispersive-optical-model (DOM) analysis was performed for neutrons and protons in ⁴⁸Ca, fitting the nucleon self-energy parameters through a weighted χ² minimization of elastic-scattering angular distributions, analyzing powers, total and reaction cross sections, charge density, single-particle energies, particle numbers, and the CREX-measured weak form factor at momentum transfer q = 0.8733 fm⁻¹. The Dyson equation was solved to obtain Green's functions and hole spectral functions, from which the charge density, neutron density, weak form factor, and neutron skin were calculated. The fit relaxed the error associated with neutron total cross section data above 100 MeV that had previously generated a thick skin.

## Key numbers

- Weak form factor: F_W = 0.125 ± 0.05 (DOM fit result)
- Neutron skin: R_skin = 0.152 ± 0.05 fm (DOM fit result)
- CREX measurement used as constraint: F_W = 0.1304 ± 0.0052(stat) ± 0.0020(syst) at q = 0.8733 fm⁻¹
- Previous DOM fit for ⁴⁸Ca (Mahzoon 2017): R_skin = 0.249 ± 0.023 fm
- DOM result for ²⁰⁸Pb (Atkinson 2020): R_skin = 0.25 ± 0.05 fm
- CREX experimental result: R_skin = 0.121 ± 0.026(exp) ± 0.024(model) fm
- PREX-2 result for ²⁰⁸Pb: R_skin = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm

## Context

This work extends the dispersive-optical-model approach previously applied to ⁴⁸Ca by Mahzoon (2017) and to ²⁰⁸Pb by Atkinson (2020), now incorporating the CREX weak-form-factor measurement to resolve the tension between the earlier DOM prediction of a thick skin (0.249 fm) and the CREX result (0.121 fm). The analysis demonstrates that the DOM can simultaneously describe both the thin CREX skin in ⁴⁸Ca and the thick PREX-2 skin in ²⁰⁸Pb, a feat not achieved by mean-field approaches or ab initio calculations. The results highlight the need for additional neutron reaction cross section measurements and further parity-violating experiments at different momentum transfers to uniquely determine the neutron distribution.
