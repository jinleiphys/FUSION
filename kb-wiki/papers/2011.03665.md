# Topology change and emergent scale symmetry in compact star matter via gravitational wave detection

> Topological structure has been extensively studied and confirmed in highly correlated condensed matter physics. We explore the gravitational waves emitted from binary neutron star mergers using the pseudoconformal model for dense nuclear matter for compact stars. This model considers the topology change and the possible emergent scale symmetry and satisfies all the constraints from astrophysics. We find that the location of the topology change affects gravitational waves dramatically owing to its effect on the equation of state. In addition, the effect of this location on the waveforms of the gravitational waves is within the ability of the on-going and up-coming facilities for detecting gravitational waves, thus suggesting a possible way to measure the topology structure in nuclear physics.

## Key claim
The location of a topology change (skyrmion–half-skyrmion transition) in dense nuclear matter, parameterized by the density \(n_{1/2}\), measurably alters the gravitational-wave waveform from binary neutron star mergers, provided that \(n_{1/2}\) lies in the range \(2.0 \lesssim n_{1/2}/n_0 < 4.0\) as previously constrained by astrophysics.

## Method
The authors used the pseudoconformal model (PCM) for dense nuclear matter, which incorporates a topology change at a density \(n_{1/2}\) and emergent scale symmetry, to compute equations of state (EoS) via the \(V_{lowk}\) renormalization-group approach. They then performed numerical relativity simulations of equal-mass binary neutron star mergers (masses \(1.5 M_\odot\) and \(1.7 M_\odot\)) using the Einstein Toolkit and LORENE library, with the EoS parameterized into a piecewise polytropic form and thermal effects included via a thermal adiabatic index \(\Gamma_{th}=1.8\). The simulations compared two representative values of the topology-change density: \(n_{1/2} = 2n_0\) and \(n_{1/2} = 3n_0\).

## Key numbers
- The topology-change density is constrained to the range \(2.0 \lesssim n_{1/2}/n_0 < 4.0\) from prior astrophysical constraints.
- For a \(1.5 M_\odot\) star, the central density is \(n_{\rm cent}/n_0 = 2.44\) for both \(n_{1/2}=2.0n_0\) and \(n_{1/2}=3.0n_0\); the tidal deformability is \(\Lambda/100 = 4.35\) and radius \(R = 12.85\) km for both cases.
- For a \(1.7 M_\odot\) star, the central density is \(n_{\rm cent}/n_0 = 2.83\) for \(n_{1/2}=2.0n_0\) and \(2.72\) for \(n_{1/2}=3.0n_0\); the central pressure is \(P_{\rm cent} = 103.40\) MeV/fm\(^3\) for \(n_{1/2}=2.0n_0\) and \(127.23\) MeV/fm\(^3\) for \(n_{1/2}=3.0n_0\).
- The merger with \(n_{1/2} = 3n_0\) is delayed by approximately \(5\) ms compared to that with \(n_{1/2} = 2n_0\) for equal-mass \(1.5 M_\odot\) stars.
- The initial separation between star centers in simulations was set to \(40\) km, with a grid resolution of \(dx = 0.75\) CU (computation units with \(c=G=M_\odot=1\)).

## Context
This work extends the pseudoconformal model (PCM) developed by Ma (2019) and Ma (2020), which predicts a precocious onset of conformal sound speed \(v_s^2/c^2 = 1/3\) in massive stars while maintaining confined quasifermions, in contrast to the standard scenario of Annala (2018) where deconfined quarks appear at much higher densities. The study demonstrates that gravitational-wave detection from binary neutron star mergers, using on-going and upcoming facilities, can distinguish the location of the topology change, a first direct observable of topological structure in nuclear physics, analogous to effects already confirmed in condensed matter physics as reviewed by Tong (2016).
