# Antiproton Absorption in Nuclei

> We present the analysis of experimental data on forward antiproton production on nuclei. The calculations are done in the framework of a folding model which takes properly into account both incoherent direct proton-nucleon and cascade pion-nucleon antiproton production processes as well as internal nucleon momentum distribution. The effective antiproton-nucleon cross section in nuclear matter and the imaginary part of the antiproton nuclear optical potential are estimated to be 25-45 mb and -(38-56) MeV at normal nuclear matter density, respectively. The results of the performed analysis evidence for the decreasing of antiproton absorption in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The analysis of antiproton production data on Cu and Ta nuclei within a folding model indicates that the effective in-medium antiproton-nucleon cross section is 25-45 mb, which is significantly smaller than the free antiproton-nucleon annihilation cross section, suggesting reduced antiproton absorption in the nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental data on forward antiproton production from proton-nucleus collisions at 7.2 and 9.2 GeV using a folding model that includes both direct proton-nucleon and cascade pion-nucleon antiproton production channels, as well as the internal nucleon momentum distribution of the target nuclei. The effective in-medium antiproton-nucleon cross section was treated as a free parameter and adjusted to reproduce the measured differential cross sections on Cu and Ta targets. The imaginary part of the antiproton nuclear optical potential was then derived from the extracted effective cross section using the relationship between survival probability and optical potential.

## Key numbers

- Effective in-medium antiproton-nucleon cross section: 25-45 mb (estimated from data on Cu and Ta targets at 7.2 and 9.2 GeV)
- Best-fit value of effective antiproton-nucleon cross section: 35 mb (for all considered antiproton momenta)
- Imaginary part of antiproton nuclear optical potential at normal nuclear matter density: -(38-56) MeV
- Imaginary part of antiproton optical potential at average nuclear density involved (0.36 rho_0): -14 MeV at antiproton momentum 0.73 GeV/c to -20 MeV at 2.5 GeV/c
- Ratio of calculated to measured antiproton production cross sections using free inelastic cross section: approximately 0.4

## Context

This work extends earlier observations of anomalously weak antiproton absorption in nuclei first reported by the authors' group (Ref9) and confirmed in subsequent studies (Ref10, Ref11), as well as the Glauber-type analysis of AGS data at 12.3 and 17.5 GeV (Ref12) which found antiproton absorption at least a factor of 5 smaller than the free annihilation cross section. The extracted imaginary part of the antiproton optical potential (-38 to -56 MeV at normal density) is comparable to values calculated for phi mesons (Ref26, Ref27) and antikaons (Ref30), but is significantly smaller in magnitude than the -160 MeV derived from antiprotonic atom data (Ref29), which was determined at much lower densities. The results enable further study of in-medium modification of hadron properties and the dynamics of antiproton propagation in nuclear matter.
