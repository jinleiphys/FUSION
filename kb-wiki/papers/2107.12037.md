# Presolar grain isotopic ratios as constraints to nuclear and stellar parameters of AGB nucleosynthesis

> Recent models for evolved Low Mass Stars (with $M \lesssim 3M_\odot$), undergoing the AGB phase assume that magnetic flux-tube buoyancy drives the formation of $^{13}$C reservoirs in He-rich layers. We illustrate their crucial properties, showing how the low abundance of $^{13}$C generated below the convective envelope hampers the formation of primary $^{14}$N and the ensuing synthesis of intermediate-mass nuclei, like $^{19}$F and $^{22}$Ne. In the mentioned models, their production is therefore of a purely secondary nature. Shortage of primary $^{22}$Ne has also important effects in reducing the neutron density. Another property concerns AGB winds, which are likely to preserve C-rich subcomponents, isolated by magnetic tension, even when the envelope composition is O-rich. Conditions for the formation of C-rich compounds are therefore found in stages earlier than previously envisaged. These issues, together with the uncertainties related to several nuclear physics quantities, are discussed in the light of the isotopic admixtures of s-process elements in presolar SiC grains of stellar origin, which provide important and precise constraints to the otherwise uncertain parameters. By comparing nucleosynthesis results with measured SiC data, it is argued that such a detailed series of constraints indicates the need for new measurements of weak interaction rates in ionized plasmas, as well as of neutron-capture cross sections, especially near the N = 50 and N = 82 neutron magic numbers. Nontheless, the peculiarity of our models allows us to achieve fits to the presolar grain data of a quality so far never obtained in previously published attempts.

## Key claim

The paper argues that magnetohydrodynamics-based models for low-mass AGB stars, which assume magnetic flux-tube buoyancy drives the formation of 13C reservoirs, produce fits to presolar SiC grain isotopic data of a quality "so far never obtained in previously published attempts," but only if the models' predictions are compared with both envelope abundances and a "magnetic wind" phase that mixes envelope material with 0.5% to 5% of He-shell composition.

## Method

The authors computed AGB nucleosynthesis models for low-mass stars (1-3 solar masses) using an MHD-based mixing scheme where magnetic flux tubes transport protons from the convective envelope into He-rich layers, forming 13C pockets that subsequently release neutrons via the 13C(alpha,n)16O reaction. They compared the resulting isotopic ratios of s-process elements (Sr, Zr, Mo, and others) with measurements from presolar SiC grains of the Main-Stream group, using data from the Washington University of Saint Louis presolar grain database. The comparison was performed for two scenarios: standard envelope abundances and a "magnetic wind" phase where 0.5% to 5% of He-shell material is mixed into the envelope to mimic the effects of magnetic flares or open magnetic structures.

## Key numbers

- The 13C pocket extends for 4.2 x 10^-3 solar masses in the model for a 1.5 solar mass star of solar metallicity
- The 30 keV Maxwellian-averaged neutron-capture cross section for 84Kr is 33.1 mb (KADONIS v1.0 recommended value)
- The branching ratio to the isomeric state of 85Kr is 0.586 (nearly 60% of the neutron-capture flux)
- The 85Kr ground state half-life is about 10.5 years
- The 30 keV recommended cross section for 86Sr is 60 mb
- The 30 keV recommended cross section for 87Sr is 93.8 +/- 3.8 mb
- The 30 keV recommended cross section for 88Sr is 6.3 mb (KADONIS v1.0) versus 9.4 mb (Katabuchi et al. measurement)
- The 93Zr half-life is 1.6 x 10^6 years
- The 94Nb half-life is 2 x 10^4 years
- The 93Mo half-life is 4000 years (electron capture)
- The 95Zr ground state half-life is 64.02 days
- The 85Kr isomer half-life is approximately 4.5 hours

## Context

This work extends the MHD-based mixing models previously developed by Busso et al. (2007), Nucci & Busso (2014), and Trippella et al. (2016), and recently applied by Vescovi et al. (2021) and Busso et al. (2021) to AGB nucleosynthesis. The paper contradicts earlier models that assumed exponential proton distributions producing significant 14N (a neutron poison), showing instead that the new MHD models produce much less 14N, enhancing neutron exposure per cycle. The results indicate the need for new measurements of weak interaction rates in ionized plasmas (to be enabled by the PANDORA project) and of neutron-capture cross sections near the N=50 and N=82 magic numbers, particularly for 88Sr, 84Kr, 85Kr, and the Zr-Nb-Mo branching region.
