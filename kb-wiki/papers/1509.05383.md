# Blast wave fits with resonances to $p_t$ spectra from nuclear collisions at the LHC

> We report our results for the freeze-out temperature and transverse flow profile obtained from fits to hadronic spectra measured by the ALICE collaboration. The influence of resonance decays is important and cannot be simply accounted for without the inclusion of their decays into the fits.

## Key claim

Including resonance decays in blast-wave fits to ALICE data changes the extracted freeze-out temperature and transverse flow, and the effect depends qualitatively on collision centrality: in central collisions the temperature drops by about 10 MeV when resonances are included, while in peripheral collisions it rises by about 10 MeV. There is no fiducial transverse-momentum interval where resonance contributions can be safely ignored.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo event generator DRAGON, which implements a blast-wave model identical to that used by the ALICE collaboration, to generate theoretical transverse-momentum spectra for pions, kaons, protons, K0, Lambda, Xi, Omega, K*, and Phi. They fitted the model parameters (freeze-out temperature T, mean transverse velocity ⟨v_t⟩, and velocity profile exponent n) to ALICE data for various centrality classes, both with and without including resonance decays. The chemical composition of the fireball was fixed by chemical freeze-out at T_chem = 152 MeV and baryochemical potential μ_B = 1 MeV, assuming all resonances survive until kinetic freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- In 0–5% central collisions, fits without resonances give T = 98 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.645, n = 0.73; with resonances included, T = 82 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.662, n = 0.69.
- In 70–80% peripheral collisions, fits without resonances give T = 142 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.439, n = 1.51; with resonances included, T = 170 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.423, n = 1.55.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature used for resonance abundances is 152 MeV with baryochemical potential 1 MeV.
- In 10–20% centrality, fits without resonances give T = 102 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.637, n = 0.73; with resonances, T = 90 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.649, n = 0.71.
- In 30–40% centrality, fits without resonances give T = 110 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.605, n = 0.81; with resonances, T = 102 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.616, n = 0.79.
- In 50–60% centrality, fits without resonances give T = 122 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.527, n = 1.15; with resonances, T = 126 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩ = 0.541, n = 1.03.

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave fitting approach used by the ALICE collaboration (2012) by explicitly including resonance decays via Monte Carlo simulation, rather than omitting them or applying a simple temperature shift. The results contradict the common assumption that resonance contributions are confined to low transverse momentum and can be avoided by choosing a fiducial p_t interval. The paper opens the question of whether assuming resonance abundances from chemical freeze-out at 152 MeV is justified, given the temperature drop observed in central collisions, and notes that a successful fit assuming common chemical and thermal freeze-out was recently reported by Begun (2015).
