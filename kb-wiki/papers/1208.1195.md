# Event-plane decorrelation over pseudo-rapidity and its effect on azimuthal anisotropy measurement in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Within A Multi-Phase Transport model, we investigate decorrelation of event planes over pseudorapidity and its effect on azimuthal anisotropy measurements in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The decorrelation increases with increasing η gap between particles used to reconstruct the event planes. The third harmonic event planes are found even anticorrelated between forward and backward rapidities, the source of which may root in the opposite orientation of the collision geometry triangularities. The decorrelation may call into question the anisotropic flow measurements with pseudorapidity gap designed to reduce nonflow contributions, hence the hydrodynamic properties of the quark-gluon plasma extracted from those measurements.

## Key claim

Within the AMPT model, event-plane angles reconstructed from particles in forward and backward pseudorapidity regions are not identical; the decorrelation increases with the pseudorapidity gap, and for the third harmonic the planes can become anticorrelated, which calls into question the standard method of using a pseudorapidity gap to suppress nonflow in anisotropic flow measurements.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. They reconstructed event-plane angles from both the initial parton spatial coordinates and the final-state charged-particle momenta in different pseudorapidity intervals, and calculated the correlation strength between forward and backward event planes after correcting for finite event-plane resolution.

## Key numbers

- For 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the momentum-space event-plane resolution R2 (R3) decreases from 0.302 (0.053) at eta=0-0.5 to 0.072 (0.008) at eta=3.5-4.
- The resolution-corrected correlation strength cos[2(Psi2{eta}-Psi2{-eta})] falls significantly below unity as the pseudorapidity gap Delta-eta increases from zero to about 7.
- The resolution-corrected correlation strength cos[3(Psi3{eta}-Psi3{-eta})] falls faster than the elliptic case and becomes negative (anticorrelation) at large Delta-eta.
- Both v2 and v3 measured with respect to event planes from different eta ranges decrease as the eta gap increases.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Petersen et al. (2011) that participant planes can differ across pseudorapidity, using a full transport model (AMPT) to quantify the effect. It directly challenges the standard eta-gap method used by experiments (e.g., the STAR and PHENIX collaborations) to suppress nonflow from resonance decays and jets. The results imply that extracted hydrodynamic properties of the quark-gluon plasma, such as the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, could be systematically biased if event-plane decorrelation is not accounted for.
