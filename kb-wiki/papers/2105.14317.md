# New insight on the quark condensate beyond chiral limit

> With analyzing the mass function obtained by solving Dyson-Schwinger Equations, we propose a cut-off independent definition of quark condensate beyond chiral limit. With this well-defined condensate, we then analyze the evolution of the condensate and its susceptibility with the current quark mass. The susceptibility shows a critical mass in the neighborhood of the s-quark current mass, which defines a transition boundary for internal hadron dynamics.

# New insight on the quark condensate beyond chiral limit

## Key claim
The authors propose a cut-off independent definition of the quark condensate for non-zero current quark mass by combining a subtraction of the current-quark-mass contribution with a fit to the ultraviolet asymptotic behavior of the quark mass function. This definition avoids both quadratic and logarithmic divergences and reveals a critical current quark mass near the strange quark mass where the dimensionless chiral susceptibility peaks, indicating a transition in the interference between dynamical and explicit chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator using the Qin-Chang (QC) interaction model with rainbow vertex truncation, setting the renormalization point at infinity. They define the condensate beyond chiral limit by applying the operator (1 - m ∂/∂m) to the trace of the quark propagator to remove quadratic divergence, then fit the resulting ultraviolet behavior to the asymptotic form from operator product expansion to extract a renormalization-group-invariant condensate. The current quark mass dependence of this condensate and its dimensionless susceptibility χ(m) = -∂⟨q̄q⟩^{1/3}/∂m are computed for masses from 0 to 4 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Chiral limit condensate at renormalization point ζ=2 GeV: -⟨q̄q⟩⁰_{ζ=2GeV} = (249 MeV)³, comparable to lattice results such as (252(5)(10) MeV)³
- Chiral limit fitting parameters: Λ_QCD = 0.2749 GeV, γ_m = 0.4799, -⟨q̄q⟩⁰ = (223 MeV)³
- Critical mass from dimensionless chiral susceptibility: m_crit = 0.22 GeV, in the neighborhood of the s-quark current mass
- Critical mass from in-hadron condensate ratio: m = 0.13 GeV (from Fig. 5 discussion) and m = 0.15 GeV (from summary)
- Interaction model parameters: D = 1.024 GeV², ω = 0.5 GeV, Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV, N_f = 4, m_t = 0.5 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of the quark condensate by addressing the ambiguity in defining the condensate beyond chiral limit due to quadratic divergences. It builds on subtraction schemes proposed by Fischer (2019), Braun (2020), and Bazavov (2012) that remove quadratic divergence by subtracting a fraction of the strange quark condensate, and on the method of Williams (2007) to extract condensate from the ultraviolet mass function. The critical mass found near the strange quark mass is consistent with prior studies of meson parton distribution amplitudes (PDA) where a critical mass near the strange quark makes the PDA become asymptotic (PDA1, PDA2). The results enable further investigation of the transition between dynamical and explicit chiral symmetry breaking mechanisms in hadron dynamics, with potential experimental signals in strange matter.
