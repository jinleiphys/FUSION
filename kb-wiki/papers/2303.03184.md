# Excitation functions of related temperatures of η and η0 emission sources from squared momentum transfer spectra in high-energy collisions

> The squared momentum transfer spectra of $η$ and $η^0$, produced in high-energy photon-proton ($γp$) $\rightarrow η(η^0)+p$ processes in electron-proton ($ep$) collisions performed at CEBAF, NINA, CEA, SLAC, DESY, and WLS are analyzed. The Monte Carlo calculations are used in the analysis of the squared momentum transfer spectra, where the transfer undergoes from the incident $γ$ to emitted $η(η^0)$ or equivalently from the target proton to emitted proton. In the calculations, the Erlang distribution and Tsallis-Levy function are used to describe the transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra of emitted particles. Our results show that the average transverse momentum ($\langle p_T\rangle$), the initial-state temperature ($T_i$), and the final-state temperature ($T_0$) roughly decrease from the lower center-of-mass energy ($W$) to the higher one in the concerned energy range of a few GeV, which is different from the excitation function from heavy-ion collisions in the similar energy range.

## Key claim

The average transverse momentum, initial-state temperature, and final-state temperature extracted from squared momentum transfer spectra in $\gamma p \rightarrow \eta(\eta^0) + p$ reactions roughly decrease with increasing center-of-mass energy in the few-GeV range, contrary to the increasing trend observed in heavy-ion collisions at similar energies; this difference is attributed to the absence of secondary collisions and cold nuclear effects in the small $\gamma p$ system.

## Method

The squared momentum transfer ($|t|$) spectra of $\eta$ and $\eta^0$ from $\gamma p$ collisions at CEBAF, NINA, CEA, SLAC, DESY, and WLS were analyzed using Monte Carlo calculations. Transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra were generated from the Erlang distribution (to extract initial-state temperature $T_i$) and the Tsallis-Levy function (to extract final-state temperature $T_0$), then converted to $|t|$ distributions via the Mandelstam variable relation, assuming isotropic emission in the source rest frame. The free parameters $\langle p_t\rangle$, $n_s$ (number of participant partons), and $T$ were obtained by fitting the calculated $|t|$ distributions to experimental data using the method of least squares.

## Key numbers

- For CLAS data at $W = (2.52, 2.56)$ GeV: $\langle p_t\rangle = 0.231 \pm 0.010$ GeV/$c$, $n_s = 3$, $T_i = 0.566 \pm 0.024$ GeV, $T = 0.204 \pm 0.015$ GeV.
- For CLAS data at $W = (3.08, 3.12)$ GeV: $\langle p_t\rangle = 0.133 \pm 0.006$ GeV/$c$, $n_s = 3$, $T_i = 0.326 \pm 0.015$ GeV, $T = 0.092 \pm 0.010$ GeV.
- For NINA data at $W = 2.360$ GeV: $\langle p_t\rangle = 0.172 \pm 0.003$ GeV/$c$, $n_s = 4$, $T_i = 0.544 \pm 0.010$ GeV, $T = 0.199 \pm 0.006$ GeV.
- For SLAC data at $E_\gamma = 6$ GeV: $\langle p_t\rangle = 0.118 \pm 0.005$ GeV/$c$, $n_s = 4$, $T_i = 0.373 \pm 0.016$ GeV, $T = 0.097 \pm 0.007$ GeV.
- The Tsallis-Levy function used a fixed $n = 40$ (corresponding to $q = 1.025$) for all fits.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Wang, Liu, and Olimov, 2023) of temperature extraction in small systems to a broader set of experimental facilities and a wider energy range. The observed decreasing trend of temperatures with energy in $\gamma p$ collisions contradicts the increasing excitation functions seen in heavy-ion collisions at few-GeV energies (as reported in works by various authors, e.g., 2005, 2010, 2014). The results enable future comparisons between small ($\gamma p$) and large (heavy-ion) systems to understand the role of secondary collisions and nuclear medium effects, though the authors note that higher-statistics data are needed for more solid conclusions.
