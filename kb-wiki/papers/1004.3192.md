# $n\bar{n}$ conversion in finite nuclei

> The new model of $n\bar{n}$ transitions in nuclei based on unitary $S$-matrix is considered. The $|in>$-state of nucleus is described by single-particle shell model. The dynamical process part is calculated by means of field-theoretical approach with finite time interval. The lower limit on the free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time $τ_{\rm min}$ is in the range $10^{16}\; {\rm yr}>τ_{\rm min}>1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$. \end{abstract}

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard potential model for calculating neutron-antineutron ($n\bar{n}$) transitions in nuclei is incorrect because it uses a non-hermitian Hamiltonian and gives the wrong dependence on the annihilation width; the new unitary S-matrix model yields a lower limit on the free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time in the range $10^{16}\; {\rm yr} > \tau_{\rm min} > 1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$, where the lower bound is a conservative estimate and the upper bound is an estimate from above.

## Method

The author formulates the $n\bar{n}$ transition in finite nuclei using a unitary S-matrix approach with a finite time interval $(t/2, -t/2)$ to avoid infrared divergences that arise from zero momentum transfer in the $n\bar{n}$ transition vertex. The initial neutron state is described by the single-particle shell model, and the dynamical process is calculated using a field-theoretical approach. Two models are considered: one with a bare antineutron propagator (which gives a singular amplitude in the S-matrix formulation) and one with a dressed propagator that includes antineutron self-energy $\Sigma$, which yields a non-singular amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The lower limit on the free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time from the dressed propagator model is $\tau^d_{\rm min} = 1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$, which exceeds the Grenoble reactor experiment limit by a factor of 14.
- The lower limit from the bare propagator model is $\tau^b_{\rm min} = 10^{16}\; {\rm yr}$, interpreted as an estimation from above.
- For the dressed propagator model, with $\Gamma = 100$ MeV and $\Sigma = 10$ MeV, the relation $\tau^d_{\rm min} = 5\tau_{\rm pot}$ holds, where $\tau_{\rm pot} = 2.36\cdot 10^{8}\; {\rm s}$ is the limit from the potential model.
- The free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time from the Grenoble reactor experiment is $\tau_{\rm min} = 0.86\cdot 10^{8}\; {\rm s}$.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous unitary S-matrix model for $n\bar{n}$ transitions in nuclear matter (Nazaruk, 2008, 2009) to finite nuclei, showing that the results are the same as for nuclear matter. The paper contradicts the standard potential model used in prior calculations (Refs. 4-11 in the paper), which gives an inverse dependence on the annihilation width $\Gamma$ ($W \sim 1/\Gamma$) whereas the unitary model gives $W \sim \Gamma$. The results enable a revised interpretation of experimental limits from proton-decay-type experiments, though the author notes the wide range of uncertainty ($10^{16}\; {\rm yr} > \tau_{\rm min} > 1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$) requires further investigation.
