# Superconducting multi-vortices and a novel BPS bound in chiral perturbation theory

> We derive a novel BPS bound from chiral perturbation theory minimally coupled to electrodynamics at finite isospin chemical potential. At a critical value of the isospin chemical potential, a system of three first-order differential field equations (which implies the second-order field equations) for the gauge field and the hadronic profile can be derived from the requirement to saturate the bound. These BPS configurations represent magnetic multi-vortices with quantized flux supported by a superconducting current. The corresponding topological charge density is related to the magnetic flux density, but is screened by the hadronic profile. Such a screening effect allows to derive the maximal value of the magnetic field generated by these BPS magnetic vortices, being $B_{max}=2,04 \times 10^{14}G$. The solution for a single BPS vortex is discussed in detail, and some physical consequences, together with the comparison with the magnetic vortices in the Ginzburg-Landau theory at critical coupling, are described.

## Key claim

The authors derive a novel BPS bound in chiral perturbation theory minimally coupled to electrodynamics, which can be saturated at a critical isospin chemical potential of $\mu_I^{\text{c}} = 14.1$ MeV, yielding superconducting multi-vortices with quantized magnetic flux. The bound is saturated only when using the Gibbs free energy (including a pressure term $P = 2K\mu_I^2$) rather than the free energy, meaning the BPS configurations appear at a specific external pressure rather than at zero pressure.

## Method

The authors start from the $SU(2)$ chiral perturbation theory action minimally coupled to Maxwell theory, introducing the isospin chemical potential through the covariant derivative. Using an Ansatz where the pionic field depends only on the transverse coordinates $(x_1, x_2)$ and the magnetic field points along the $x_3$ direction, they rewrite the free energy density and perform an improper gauge transformation (with a multi-valued gauge parameter) to eliminate the phase $\Phi$, which introduces Aharonov-Bohm delta-function contributions at each vortex position. By completing squares in the Gibbs free energy density, they identify a total derivative term that becomes exact only at the critical isospin chemical potential $\mu_I^{\text{c}} = \sqrt{K}$, leading to three first-order BPS equations: $\partial_1\alpha + \sin(\alpha)\widehat{A}_2 = 0$, $\partial_2\alpha - \sin(\alpha)\widehat{A}_1 = 0$, and $\widehat{B}_z - 2K\cos(\alpha) = 0$.

## Key numbers

- Critical isospin chemical potential: $\mu_I^{\text{c}} = 14.1$ MeV, derived from $\mu_I^{\text{c}} = ef_\pi/2$ with $f_\pi = 93$ MeV and $e = 0.303$
- Maximal magnetic field generated by the BPS vortices: $B_{\text{max}} = 2.04 \times 10^{14}$ G
- Pion decay constant used: $f_\pi = 93$ MeV
- Electric charge used: $e = \sqrt{4\pi\alpha} = 0.303$

## Context

This work extends the search for BPS-saturable configurations in chiral perturbation theory, following the recent result in Canfora et al. (2024) that novel BPS bounds can exist even when obvious bounds cannot be saturated. The authors compare their superconducting multi-vortices to the well-known BPS vortices in Ginzburg-Landau theory at critical coupling (Weinberg, 1979), noting that for small $\alpha$ their BPS system reduces exactly to the GL BPS system. The results enable the study of topologically stable inhomogeneous condensates at finite isospin density, relevant for heavy ion collisions and neutron star physics, and allow application of Weinberg's index theorem (Weinberg, 1981) to count fermionic zero modes supported by these vortices.
