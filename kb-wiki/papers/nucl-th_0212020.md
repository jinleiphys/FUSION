# Chiral symmetry and strangeness content in nuclear physics parametrized by a medium dependent bag constant

> Non-perturbative QCD vacuum effects at finite density are parametrized by means of a bag constant $B$. It is extracted from a Nambu- Jona Lasinio model with two or three flavors. The parameter $B$ is used in an effective quark-meson coupling model to study the nuclear phenomenology. We examine the nucleon structure and the thermodynamical properties of symmetric nuclear matter, particular attention is paid to the symmetry energy and to the eventual phase transition to deconfined quark matter. An alternative sketch of the binding mechanism of symmetric nuclear matter emerges within this approach. It is also found that the inclusion of strangeness content in $B$ is crucial for an appropriate description.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a density-dependent bag constant \(B\), extracted from a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with three flavors (including strangeness), can be used in a quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to describe nuclear matter without requiring a direct quark–sigma-meson coupling, thereby offering an alternative saturation mechanism. The hedge is that the two-flavor (SU(2)) version of this approach fails because it leads to unphysically large bag radii and violates the non-overlapping bag assumption already at normal nuclear density.

## Method

The bag constant \(B\) is computed as the energy difference between the NJL ground state (with finite quark condensates) and the state with zero condensates at the same baryon density, using both SU(2) and SU(3) versions of the NJL model. This density-dependent \(B\) is then inserted into the QMC model, where nucleons are treated as spherical bags of confined quarks that interact with mean \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) meson fields. The nucleon effective mass, bag radius, and nuclear matter equation of state are obtained by minimizing the bag energy and solving the mean-field equations for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant at zero density has two scales: \(B^{1/4} = 245\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (gluon condensation) and \(B^{1/4} = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (chiral symmetry restoration), cited from earlier work (Adami, 1995).
- At \(0.7\, n_0\) (where \(n_0 = 0.15\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) is the saturation density), the bag constant decreases by \(55\%\) in the SU(2) case and by \(22\%\) in the SU(3) case (cases b and c).
- The neutron mass at \(n_0\) drops by approximately \(10\%\) in the SU(3) treatment, \(20\)–\(25\%\) in the constant-\(B\) case, and \(40\%\) in the SU(2) case.
- The bag radius in the SU(3) case grows by \(15\%\) over its zero-density value at saturation; the non-overlapping bag assumption is violated at \(n \simeq 6\, n_0\) for SU(3) and at \(n \simeq n_0\) for SU(2).
- The nuclear compressibility at \(n_0\) is \(310\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^3\) for the constant-\(B\) case and \(335\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^3\) for the SU(3) case.
- The symmetry energy at \(n_0\) is fixed to the phenomenological value \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{sym}} = 34\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The binding energy at saturation is set to \(-16.807\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier QMC models that used ad-hoc density-dependent bag constants parametrized by the sigma-meson mean field (Jin, 1992; Aguirre and Szybisz, 1999; Lu, 1998) by instead deriving \(B\) from a dynamical NJL model that respects chiral symmetry. It contradicts the two-flavor approach, which is found inadequate, and shows that including strange-quark vacuum contributions is essential even for ordinary nuclear matter. The results enable future chiral extensions of the QMC model and suggest that no first-order deconfinement phase transition occurs in symmetric nuclear matter within this framework, though quark correlations between bags (Aguiar and Delfino, 2001) might alter that conclusion.
