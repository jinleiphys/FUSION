# Massive neutron stars and $Λ$-hypernuclei in relativistic mean field models

> Based on relativistic mean field (RMF) models, we study finite $Λ$-hypernuclei and massive neutron stars. The effective $N$-$N$ interactions PK1 and TM1 are adopted, while the $N$-$Λ$ interactions are constrained by reproducing the binding energy of $Λ$-hyperon at $1s$ orbit of $^{40}_Λ$Ca. It is found that the $Λ$-meson couplings follow a simple relation, indicating a fixed $Λ$ potential well for symmetric nuclear matter at saturation densities, i.e., around $V_Λ = -29.786$ MeV. With those interactions, a large mass range of $Λ$-hypernuclei can be well described. Furthermore, the masses of PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 can be attained adopting the $Λ$-meson couplings $g_{σΛ}/g_{σN}\gtrsim 0.73$, $g_{ωΛ}/g_{ωN}\gtrsim 0.80$ for PK1 and $g_{σΛ}/g_{σN}\gtrsim 0.81$, $g_{ωΛ}/g_{ωN}\gtrsim 0.90$ for TM1, respectively. This resolves the Hyperon Puzzle without introducing any additional degrees of freedom.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that by slightly adjusting the Λ-meson coupling strengths (specifically, increasing the vector coupling \(g_{\omega\Lambda}/g_{\omega N}\) above the naive quark model value of 0.666), the relativistic mean field (RMF) model can simultaneously reproduce the binding energies of Λ-hypernuclei and predict neutron star masses exceeding \(2 M_\odot\), thereby resolving the Hyperon Puzzle without introducing any additional degrees of freedom such as strange vector mesons or three-body forces.

## Method
The authors used the RMF model with the PK1 and TM1 effective nucleon-nucleon (N-N) interactions. The N-Λ interactions were constrained by fixing the scalar coupling \(g_{\sigma\Lambda}/g_{\sigma N}\) to reproduce the experimental binding energy of the Λ hyperon in the \(1s\) orbit of \(^{40}_{\Lambda}\)Ca (18.7 MeV), while the vector coupling \(g_{\omega\Lambda}/g_{\omega N}\) was varied from 0.666 to 1.0. For each vector coupling, the scalar coupling was adjusted to maintain a fixed Λ potential depth \(V_\Lambda = -29.786\) MeV in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density. The resulting interactions were then used to calculate the equation of state (EoS) of neutron star matter under \(\beta\)-equilibrium and charge neutrality, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation was solved to obtain the mass-radius relation of neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- The Λ potential depth for symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is fixed at \(V_\Lambda = -29.786\) MeV.
- For the PK1 interaction, the observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 (\(1.928 \pm 0.017\ M_\odot\)) and PSR J0348+0432 (\(2.01 \pm 0.04\ M_\odot\)) are attained with \(\Lambda\)-meson couplings \(g_{\sigma\Lambda}/g_{\sigma N} \gtrsim 0.73\) and \(g_{\omega\Lambda}/g_{\omega N} \gtrsim 0.80\).
- For the TM1 interaction, the same pulsar masses require \(g_{\sigma\Lambda}/g_{\sigma N} \gtrsim 0.81\) and \(g_{\omega\Lambda}/g_{\omega N} \gtrsim 0.90\).
- The onset density for Λ hyperons in neutron star matter ranges from \(0.30\) to \(0.36\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), depending on the coupling strengths.
- The scalar coupling constants that reproduce the \(^{40}_{\Lambda}\)Ca binding energy are \(g_{\sigma\Lambda}/g_{\sigma N} = 0.618\) for PK1 and \(0.620\) for TM1 when the vector coupling is fixed at the naive quark model value of \(g_{\omega\Lambda}/g_{\omega N} = 0.666\).

## Context
This work extends previous RMF studies of Λ-hypernuclei (e.g., Wang (2013), Lu (2011)) and addresses the Hyperon Puzzle, a long-standing problem where the inclusion of hyperons softens the EoS and prevents neutron star masses from reaching \(2 M_\odot\) as observed for PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest (2010), Fonseca (2016)) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis (2013)). Prior solutions introduced additional repulsive forces via strange vector mesons (Weissenborn (2012)), three-body forces (Lonardoni (2015)), or deconfinement phase transitions (Weissenborn (2011)). The present work shows that simply adjusting the Λ-meson couplings within the standard RMF framework, without new degrees of freedom, can satisfy both hypernuclear data and neutron star observations. This enables future constraints from gravitational wave events such as GW170817 (Abbott (2017)) and motivates inclusion of heavier hyperons like \(\Xi^-\).
