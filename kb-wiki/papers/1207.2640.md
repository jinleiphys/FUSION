# Effects of a spin-flavour dependent interaction on light-flavoured baryon helicity amplitudes

> This paper is a continuation of previous work about the effects of a phenomenological flavour dependent force in a relativistically covariant constituent quark model based on the Salpeter equation on the structure of light-flavoured baryon resonances. Here the longitudinal and transverse helicity amplitudes as studied experimentally in the electro-excitation of nucleon- and $Δ$ resonances are calculated. In particular the amplitudes for the excitation of three and four star resonances as calculated in a previous model $\mathcal A$ are compared to those of the novel model $\mathcal C$ as well as to existing and partially new experimental data such as e.g. determined by the CB-ELSA collaboration. A brief discussion of some improvements to model $\mathcal C$ is given after the introduction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a novel spin-flavour dependent interaction kernel (model C) improves the description of baryon helicity amplitudes compared to the previous model A, but the calculated transverse amplitudes for the S11(1535) resonance are still too small by a factor of two, and the near constancy of the proton data for 0 < Q^2 < 1 GeV^2 is not reflected by any of the calculated results.

## Method

The authors calculate longitudinal and transverse helicity amplitudes for electro-excitation of nucleon and Delta resonances using a relativistically covariant constituent quark model based on the Salpeter equation with instantaneous interaction kernels. The electromagnetic current matrix elements are computed in lowest order from the Salpeter amplitudes, which are obtained by solving an eigenvalue equation for baryon masses using an expansion into three-particle harmonic oscillator states up to excitation quantum number N_max=18. The results from model A (with confinement and instanton-induced interactions) are compared to model C (which additionally includes a phenomenological spin-flavour dependent octet and singlet exchange interaction of Gaussian form).

## Key numbers

- The electric form factor of the proton divided by dipole form is described for momentum transfers Q^2 less than 3 GeV^2 with the new parameter set of model C.
- The electric neutron form factor shows a significantly improved description with the new parameter set, reflecting sensitivity to parameter changes.
- The magnetic proton form factor shows improvements at higher momentum transfers Q^2 greater than 1 GeV^2.
- The axial form factor uses parameters M_A = 1.014 +/- 0.014 GeV and g_A = 1.267 from Bodek et al.
- The third and fourth J^pi = 1/2^- nucleon resonances are predicted in model A at 1872 MeV and 1886 MeV, and in model C at 1839 MeV and 1882 MeV.
- The S11(1535) transverse helicity amplitude at the photon point (Q^2 = 0) for both proton and neutron is accurately reproduced by model C.
- The S11(1650) neutron amplitude A_{1/2}^n calculated at the photon point corresponds to the PDG data.
- The zero crossing in the P11(1440) transverse amplitude data at Q^2 approximately 0.5 GeV^2 is not reproduced in the calculated curves.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ronniger, 2011) on baryon mass spectra in a covariant Bethe-Salpeter framework, now calculating electromagnetic transition amplitudes. It builds on earlier work by Loehning, Metsch, and Petry (1998-2005) on model A, and compares with results from the Graz group (Glozman, 1996-1998; Plessas, 2007-2008) who studied spin-flavour dependent interactions. The calculations are compared to new experimental data from Anisovich et al. (2010-2012), Aznauryan et al. (2005-2012), and the MAID analysis by Drechsel and Tiator (2007-2011). The results enable further testing of constituent quark models against electro-excitation data up to 6 GeV^2 from Jefferson Laboratory.
