# Reconciling $G_A/G_V$, $<r_n^2>$ and $\mu_{p,n}$ in Chiral Quark Model with One Gluon Generated Configuration Mixing

## Key claim

The chiral quark model with SU(3) symmetry breaking and one-gluon-exchange configuration mixing can simultaneously reproduce the spin polarization functions, $G_A/G_V$, nucleon magnetic moments, and the neutron charge radius, a compatibility that could not be achieved in either the constituent quark model or the chiral quark model alone. The resolution depends on a specific mixing angle $\phi \simeq 20^\circ$ (dictated by neutron charge radius considerations) and symmetry breaking parameters $\alpha = 0.4$, $\beta = 0.7$ obtained from a $\chi^2$ fit.

## Method

The authors calculate quark spin polarizations $\Delta u$, $\Delta d$, $\Delta s$ for the proton in the chiral quark model ($\chi$QM) by including Goldstone boson emission ($\pi$, $K$, $\eta$, $\eta'$) with SU(3) symmetry breaking via different quark and meson masses, and configuration mixing between the $(56,0^+)_{N=0}$ and $(70,0^+)_{N=2}$ harmonic oscillator bands generated by one-gluon exchange forces. Magnetic moments are evaluated using the mixed wavefunction with a Cheng-Li scale factor absorbed into effective quark masses ($m_u = m_d = 0.313$ GeV, $m_s = 0.513$ GeV), and the neutron charge radius is computed from the slope of the electric form factor using the $56$-$70$ mixing formula $<r_n^2> = \sqrt{2/3} \, R^2 (-\tan \phi)$.

## Key numbers

- $\Delta u = 0.91$ (NMC) and $0.93$ (E866) for $\phi = 20^\circ$ with SU(3) breaking and anomaly contribution included
- $\Delta d = -0.32$ (NMC) and $-0.34$ (E866) for $\phi = 20^\circ$ with SU(3) breaking and anomaly contribution included
- $\Delta s = -0.02$ (NMC and E866) for SU(3) breaking without anomaly; $-0.06$ with anomaly contribution
- $G_A/G_V = 1.22$ (NMC) and $1.26$ (E866) for $\phi = 20^\circ$ with SU(3) breaking (experimental value: $1.267$)
- $\mu(p) = 2.766$ (calculated) vs. experimental $2.793$ for $\phi = -20^\circ$
- $\mu(n) = -1.767$ (calculated) vs. experimental $-1.913$ for $\phi = -20^\circ$
- $<r_n^2> = 2.64$ GeV$^{-2}$ (calculated) vs. experimental $2.82$ GeV$^{-2}$ for $\phi = -20^\circ$, $R^2 = 8$ GeV$^{-2}$
- Symmetry breaking parameters from $\chi^2$ fit: $\alpha = 0.4$, $\beta = 0.7$
- Transition probability parameter $a = 0.1$

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model of Manohar and Georgi (1984) and Cheng and Li (1995) by incorporating one-gluon-exchange configuration mixing previously applied in constituent quark models by Isgur and Karl (1978) and others. It directly addresses the incompatibility problem identified by Gupta (1998) in constituent quark models, where $G_A/G_V$, magnetic moments, and neutron charge radius could not be simultaneously reproduced. The results enable a unified description of nucleon spin structure, electromagnetic properties, and flavor asymmetry within a single framework, and are consistent with the NMC and E866 experimental data on the Gottfried sum rule violation.
