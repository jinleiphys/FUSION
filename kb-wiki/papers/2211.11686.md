# On-shell versus curvature mass parameter fixing schemes in the three flavor quark-meson model with vacuum fluctuations

> The vacuum effective potential and phase diagram for the three (2+1) flavor quark-meson model have been computed and compared in an extended mean-field approximation (e-MFA) where the model parameters are fixed by using different renormalization prescriptions after including quark one-loop vacuum fluctuations. When the vacuum one-loop divergence is regularized in the minimal subtraction scheme and the curvature masses of the scalar and pseudo-scalar mesons are used for fixing the parameters, the setting of the quark-meson model with the vacuum term (QMVT) turns out to be inconsistent as one notes that the curvature masses are defined by the evaluation of self-energy at zero momentum. This work constitutes the first application of the consistent on-shell parameter fixing scheme to the three flavor quark-meson (QM) model. In this setting of the renormalized quark-meson (RQM) model, the physical (pole) masses of the $π, K, η$ and $η^{\prime}$ pseudo-scalar mesons and the scalar $σ$ meson,the pion decay constant and kaon decay constant are put into the relation of the running mass parameter and couplings by using the on-shell and the minimal subtraction renormalization schemes. The nonstrange direction normalized vacuum effective potential plots for both the RQM model and QMVT model, are exactly identical for the $m_σ=$ 658.8 MeV while the nonstrange direction order parameter temperature variations and phase diagrams for both the models RQM and PQMVT are identical when the $m_σ$ value is smaller by 10 MeV i.e. $m_σ=$ 648 MeV. This happens because the normalized vacuum effective potential variation in the nonstrange direction is somewhat influenced by its variation in the strange direction.

## Key claim

This work constitutes the first application of the consistent on-shell parameter fixing scheme to the three flavor quark-meson (QM) model, demonstrating that the previously used curvature mass parameter fixing scheme (QMVT) is inconsistent because curvature masses are defined by evaluating self-energy at zero momentum rather than at the physical pole. The normalized vacuum effective potential plots for the RQM and QMVT models are exactly identical for a sigma mass of 658.8 MeV, but the phase diagrams and order parameter temperature variations become identical only when the sigma mass is reduced by 10 MeV to 648 MeV.

## Method

The vacuum effective potential and phase diagram for the three (2+1) flavor quark-meson model were computed in an extended mean-field approximation (e-MFA) including quark one-loop vacuum fluctuations. Two renormalization prescriptions were compared: the QMVT model using minimal subtraction with curvature masses for parameter fixing, and the RQM model using on-shell renormalization where physical (pole) masses of the pi, K, eta, eta-prime, and sigma mesons together with the pion and kaon decay constants are related to running mass parameters and couplings. Counterterms were calculated in both the on-shell and minimal subtraction schemes, and the relations between on-shell physical quantities and running parameters were used as input for the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- The nonstrange direction normalized vacuum effective potential plots for the RQM model and QMVT model are exactly identical for m_sigma = 658.8 MeV
- The nonstrange direction order parameter temperature variations and phase diagrams for both models are identical when m_sigma = 648 MeV (10 MeV smaller)
- The normalized vacuum effective potential plots in the strange direction for both models are nearly coincident for m_sigma = 785 MeV
- For m_sigma less than 658.8 MeV, the nonstrange direction normalized vacuum effective potential is deepest for the QMVT model; for m_sigma greater than 658.8 MeV, the RQM model becomes deepest
- For m_sigma less than 785 MeV, the strange direction effective potential is deepest in the QMVT model; for m_sigma greater than 785 MeV, it becomes deepest for the RQM model

## Context

This work extends the on-shell parameter fixing scheme previously applied to the two flavor quark-meson model by Adhikari and collaborators (2018-2020) to the three flavor case, enabling investigation of both SU_A(3) chiral and U_A(1) axial symmetry breaking and restoration. It contrasts with prior three flavor QM/PQM model studies by Schaefer (2009), Mao (2010), and Tiwari (2013) that used curvature masses and neglected the inconsistency between pole and screening masses when Dirac sea contributions are included. The results enable future work to properly account for the interplay between axial U_A(1) and SU_A(3) chiral symmetry in the QCD phase diagram.
