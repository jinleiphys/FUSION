# Pi+ Pi- Emission in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Realistic vacuum $ππ$ interactions are employed to investigate thermal $π^+π^-$ emission spectra from the late stages of heavy-ion reactions at ultrarelativistic energies. Hadronic in-medium effects, including many-body $ρ$-meson spectral functions used earlier to describe the dilepton excess observed at CERN-SPS energies, are implemented to assess resulting modifications in relation to recent measurements of $π^+π^-$ invariant-mass spectra by the STAR collaboration in $p$-$p$ and $Au$-$Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV. Statistical model estimates for the $ρ^0/π^-$ and $K^*/K$ ratios close to the expected thermal freezeout are also given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent downward shift of the ρ-meson peak in π⁺π⁻ invariant-mass spectra observed by STAR in Au-Au collisions relative to p-p collisions can be largely explained by a combination of in-medium broadening of the ρ spectral function and thermal phase-space distortions, without requiring a significant shift of the ρ-meson pole mass.

## Method

The author employs microscopic in-medium ρ-meson spectral functions constructed from many-body selfenergy insertions (including pion, meson, and baryon interactions) that were previously used to describe dilepton excess at CERN-SPS energies. Thermal π⁺π⁻ emission rates are computed by integrating these spectral functions over a fireball evolution model with temperature and density profiles characteristic of RHIC freezeout conditions, and the Jülich ππ interaction model is used to evaluate S-wave ("σ") and D-wave (f₂(1270)) contributions.

## Key numbers

- The apparent ρ-meson peak shift between p-p and Au-Au collisions is approximately 30 MeV downward, as reported by STAR
- The centroid of the π⁺π⁻ mass distribution from ρ decays in Au-Au collisions is 722 MeV for an average freezeout temperature of 110 MeV and 739 MeV for 125 MeV, compared to 758-769 MeV with free ρ spectral functions
- The ρ⁰/π⁻ ratio at chemical freezeout (T=180 MeV) ranges between 0.106 and 0.125, decreasing to 0.075-0.087 at (μ_π,T)=(85,120) MeV and 0.05-0.06 at (μ_π,T)=(100,100) MeV
- The STAR preliminary measurement in p-p collisions gives ρ⁰/π⁻=0.173±0.026 at √s_NN=200 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Brown and Rho (2002) and Kolb and Prakash (2003) that addressed the STAR π⁺π⁻ data using schematic mass shifts and width effects, by employing more realistic microscopic in-medium ρ spectral functions developed for dilepton production at CERN-SPS energies (Rapp and Wambach, 2000). The results enable quantitative comparison between hadronic (π⁺π⁻) and electromagnetic (e⁺e⁻) probes of the ρ spectral function in heavy-ion collisions, and provide a framework for understanding the evolution of resonance yields from chemical to thermal freezeout at RHIC energies.
