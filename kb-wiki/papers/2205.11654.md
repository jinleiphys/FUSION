# Extended random-phase-approximation study of fragmentation of giant quadrupole resonance in $^{16}$O

> The damping of isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance in $^{16}$O is studied using extended random-phase-approximation approaches derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory. It is pointed out that the effects of ground-state correlations bring strong fragmentation of quadrupole strength even if the number of two particle--two hole configurations is strongly limited.

## Key claim

The paper claims that ground-state correlations cause strong fragmentation of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) strength in \(^{16}\)O, even when the number of two particle–two hole (2p–2h) configurations is strongly limited; the extended random-phase-approximation (ERPA) improves upon the small-amplitude limit of time-dependent density-matrix theory (STDDM) by including self-energy contributions that shift and fragment the strength.

## Method

The author applies ERPA and STDDM, both derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), to study the damping of the isoscalar GQR in \(^{16}\)O. The correlated ground state is obtained as a stationary solution of the TDDM equations using a simplified residual interaction (only the \(t_0\) and \(t_3\) terms of the Skyrme III force) and a truncation scheme that neglects the correlated part of the three-body density matrix (\(C_3\)) and retains only the 2p–2h and 2h–2p components of the correlated two-body density matrix (\(C_2\)). Realistic calculations include a large single-particle space with continuum states discretized in a 15 fm sphere and single-particle states up to 50 MeV and angular momentum \(j \le 9/2\hbar\); the residual interaction strength in the one-body channels is reduced by a factor \(f = 0.62\) to place the spurious center-of-mass mode at zero energy in RPA.

## Key numbers

- Occupation probabilities in TDDM for \(^{16}\)O: proton \(1p_{3/2}\) = 0.907, \(1p_{1/2}\) = 0.879, \(1d_{5/2}\) = 0.102; neutron \(1p_{3/2}\) = 0.908, \(1p_{1/2}\) = 0.879, \(1d_{5/2}\) = 0.102 (EDA results in parentheses: 0.910, 0.883, 0.099 for both protons and neutrons).
- Correlation energy in TDDM is \(-22.8\) MeV, largely compensated by an increase in mean-field energy; total energy in TDDM is 7.9 MeV lower than in Hartree-Fock (HF).
- In the small single-particle space (no 1p–1h quadrupole transitions allowed), the energy-weighted-sum-rule (EWSR) values exhausted are 6.7% (ERPA), 4.5% (STDDM), and 5.7% (EDA).
- In the realistic large-space calculation, EWSR values exhausted are 104% (RPA and SRPA), 106% (ERPA), and 105% (STDDM).
- The main peak in ERPA is 8.1 MeV higher than in STDDM in the small-space calculation.
- Observed quadrupole strength between \(E = 11\) MeV and 40 MeV accounts for \(53 \pm 10\)% of EWSR (Lui et al.).

## Context

This work extends beyond-RPA approaches for giant resonances, specifically comparing ERPA and STDDM to second RPA (SRPA) and particle-vibration coupling models (e.g., Kamerdzhiev et al.). The inclusion of ground-state correlations via fractional occupation probabilities and the correlated two-body density matrix produces fragmentation of the GQR in \(^{16}\)O that is absent in SRPA and RPA, and the self-energy contributions in ERPA (missing in STDDM) improve agreement with the exact diagonalization approach (EDA) and with experimental data from Lui et al. The results enable future studies of giant resonances in heavier nuclei using ERPA with a more complete treatment of three-body correlations.
