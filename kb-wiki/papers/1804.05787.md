# Phase diagram of an extended Agassi model

> Background: The Agassi model is an extension of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model that incorporates the pairing interaction. It is a schematic model that describes the interplay between particle-hole and pair correlations. It was proposed in the 1960's by D. Agassi as a model to simulate the properties of the quadrupole plus pairing model. Purpose: The aim of this work is to extend a previous study by Davis and Heiss generalizing the Agassi model and analyze in detail the phase diagram of the model as well as the different regions with coexistence of several phases. Method: We solve the model Hamiltonian through the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation, introducing two variational parameters that play the role of order parameters. We also compare the HFB calculations with the exact ones. Results: We obtain the phase diagram of the model and classify the order of the different quantum phase transitions appearing in the diagram. The phase diagram presents broad regions where several phases, up to three, coexist. Moreover, there is also a line and a point where four and five phases are degenerated, respectively. Conclusions: The phase diagram of the extended Agassi model presents a rich variety of phases. Phase coexistence is present in extended areas of the parameter space. The model could be an important tool for benchmarking novel many-body approximations.

# Phase diagram of an extended Agassi model

## Key claim
The extended Agassi model, which adds a monopole-pairing term to the original Hamiltonian, exhibits a phase diagram with up to five coexisting phases, including a line where four phases are degenerate and a point where five phases are degenerate, but only within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field approximation; the exact solution may modify the sharpness of these transitions.

## Method
The authors solved the extended Agassi Hamiltonian using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation with two variational parameters (φ and β) serving as order parameters, corresponding to Hartree-Fock and Bogoliubov transformations respectively. Two distinct energy surfaces (labeled A and B) were obtained depending on the phase selection of the Bogoliubov transformation parameters. The stability of each extremum was analyzed through the eigenvalues of the Hessian matrix, and the HFB results were compared with exact diagonalizations for large system sizes.

## Key numbers
- The spherical (symmetric) minimum has energy E_A/(jε) = -1 for χ < 1 and Σ < 1 (independently of Λ).
- The HF deformed solution has energy E_A/(jε) = -(χ² + 1)/(2χ) for χ > 1 and χ > Σ.
- The BCS deformed solution has energy E_A/(jε) = -(Σ² + 1)/(2Σ) for Σ > 1 and χ < Σ.
- The combined HF-BCS deformed solution has energy E_B/(jε) = -Λ for Λ > (1/4)(χ + √(4 + χ²)).
- The critical exponent for both the HF and BCS second-order quantum phase transitions is ε = 1/2, with order parameters scaling as √(2x) for χ = 1 + x and √(x) for Σ = 1 + x.
- The system is half-filled with 2j fermions (j fermion pairs) for a degeneracy Ω = 2j.

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Davis and Heiss (1986) by adding a monopole-pairing term (−2hA₀†A₀) to the original Agassi model from Agassi (1968), which itself combined the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (Lipkin 1965) with pairing interactions. The extended model provides a richer phase diagram with five distinct phases (spherical, HF deformed, BCS deformed, combined HF-BCS deformed, and a closed valley solution) and broad regions of phase coexistence, making it a potential benchmark for testing novel many-body approximations such as coupled cluster theory with symmetry breaking and restoration, as explored by Hermansen et al. (2017).
