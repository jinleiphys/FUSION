# Reaction and structure effects of light mass nuclei using Glauber model with relativistic and non relativistic simple effective interaction densities

> We study the structural properties of some nuclei in the region of light mass using simple effective interaction in the frame work of microscopic non relativistic Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean field formalism. For the reaction dynamics, well known Glauber model is used with the conjunctions of the densities obtained from these formalism. We observe good agreement of results by both the formalism in comparison to the experimental values. These two different approaches seems to be equally capable to reproduce the ground state properties of nuclei in this region. A careful study of reaction dynamics suggest the superiority of relativistic mean field over the Hartree-Fock with simple effective interaction densities. But good agreement of reaction cross section is appeared with simple effective interaction density for halo nuclear systems. The possibility of existence of bubble nuclei are also studied using both the formalism.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both the non-relativistic Hartree-Fock with simple effective interaction (HF(SEI)) and the relativistic mean field (RMF) with NL3 parameter set are equally capable of reproducing ground state properties of light mass nuclei, but a careful study of reaction dynamics suggests the superiority of RMF over HF(SEI), except for halo nuclear systems where HF(SEI) gives good agreement with experimental reaction cross sections.

## Method

The authors calculated ground state properties (binding energy, charge radius, quadrupole deformation) for a range of light nuclei from Be to Ar using both spherical and axially deformed RMF(NL3) and spherical HF(SEI) formalisms. They then used the resulting nucleon density distributions as input to the Glauber model with finite-range profile functions to compute total reaction cross sections for various projectile-target combinations at intermediate energies (230-955 MeV/A). The depletion factor (D.F.) was calculated from the radial density profiles to identify candidate bubble nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $^{10}$Be: 65.302 MeV (HF(SEI)), 63.49 MeV (Sph. RMF(NL3)), 64.855 MeV (Def. RMF(NL3)), compared to experimental 64.970 MeV
- Charge radius of $^{11}$Be: 2.329 fm (HF(SEI)), 2.479 fm (Sph. RMF(NL3)), 2.449 fm (Def. RMF(NL3)), compared to experimental 2.46 fm
- Total depletion factor (D.F.) for $^{22}$O: 13.21% (HF(SEI)), 14.46% (Sph. RMF(NL3)), 23.28% (Def. RMF(NL3))
- Proton depletion factor for $^{46}$Ar: 51.05% (HF(SEI)), 62.08% (Sph. RMF(NL3)), 14.73% (Def. RMF(NL3))
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section at 230 MeV/A: 3.307790 fm$^2$ with $\alpha_{NN}=0.7462136$ and $\beta_{NN}=0.1042526$ fm$^2$
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section at 955 MeV/A: 4.318853 fm$^2$ with $\alpha_{NN}=-0.2146004$ and $\beta_{NN}=0.213894$ fm$^2$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using the Glauber model with RMF densities (Patra 2009, Shukla 2007) and the recently proposed simple effective interaction (Bhuyan 2012) to a broader set of light nuclei. It also investigates bubble structure candidates previously studied by Grasso (2009) and Shukla (2011) using both relativistic and non-relativistic formalisms. The results enable systematic comparison of reaction cross section predictions from different density models and identify $^{22}$O, $^{23}$F, $^{34}$Si, and $^{46}$Ar as the most prominent bubble nuclei candidates from this study.
