# Quark Sivers Function at Small $x$: Spin-Dependent Odderon and the Sub-Eikonal Evolution

> We apply the formalism developed earlier for studying transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) at small Bjorken $x$ to construct the small-$x$ asymptotics of the quark Sivers function. First, we explicitly construct the complete fundamental "polarized Wilson line" operator to sub-sub-eikonal order: this object can be used to study a variety of quark TMDs at small-$x$. We then express the quark Sivers function in terms of dipole scattering amplitudes containing various components of the "polarized Wilson line" and show that the dominant (eikonal) term which contributes to the quark Sivers function at small $x$ is the spin-dependent odderon, confirming the recent results of Dong, Zheng and Zhou. Our conclusion is also similar to the case of the gluon Sivers function derived by Boer, Echevarria, Mulders and Zhou (see also the work by Szymanowski and Zhou). We also analyze the sub-eikonal corrections to the quark Sivers function using the constructed "polarized Wilson line" operator. We derive new small-$x$ evolution equations re-summing double-logarithmic powers of $α_s \, \ln^2 (1/x)$ with $α_s$ the strong coupling constant. We solve the corresponding novel evolution equations in the large-$N_c$ limit, obtaining a sub-eikonal correction to the spin-dependent odderon contribution. We conclude that the quark Sivers function at small $x$ receives contributions from two terms and is given by \begin{align} f_{1 \: T}^{\perp \: q} (x, k_T^2) = C_O (x, k_T^2) \, \frac{1}{x} + C_1 (k_T^2) \, \left( \frac{1}{x} \right)^0 + \ldots \end{align} with the function $C_O (x, k_T^2)$ varying slowly with $x$ and the ellipsis denoting the sub-asymptotic and sub-sub-eikonal (order-$x$) corrections.

## Key claim

The quark Sivers function at small Bjorken $x$ receives two contributions: a leading eikonal term proportional to $1/x$ from the spin-dependent odderon, and a sub-eikonal correction proportional to $(1/x)^0$ from novel double-logarithmic evolution equations, with the function $C_O(x, k_T^2)$ varying slowly with $x$ and the ellipsis denoting sub-asymptotic and sub-sub-eikonal corrections of order $x$.

## Method

The authors constructed the complete fundamental "polarized Wilson line" operator to sub-sub-eikonal order by calculating gluon field insertion operators in Feynman gauge using Brodsky-Lepage spinors in the transverse spin basis, then expressed the quark Sivers function in terms of dipole scattering amplitudes containing components of this operator. They derived new small-$x$ evolution equations re-summing double-logarithmic powers of $\alpha_s \ln^2(1/x)$ and solved these equations in the large-$N_c$ limit to obtain the sub-eikonal correction to the spin-dependent odderon contribution.

## Key numbers

* The leading eikonal small-$x$ asymptotics of the quark Sivers function is $f_{1T}^{\perp q} (x, k_T^2) \sim 1/x$ for both SIDIS and Drell-Yan cases, based on the odderon intercept being exactly zero at leading and next-to-leading logarithmic approximation in $1/x$.
* The quark Sivers function at small $x$ is given by $f_{1 \: T}^{\perp \: q} (x, k_T^2) = C_O (x, k_T^2) \, \frac{1}{x} + C_1 (k_T^2) \, \left( \frac{1}{x} \right)^0 + \ldots$, with the sub-eikonal term having no $1/x$ enhancement.
* The lowest fixed-order (one-loop) contribution to the quark Sivers function in the scalar diquark model of the proton gives $f_{1T}^{\perp q} (x, k_T^2) \sim x$ for massless quarks, falling off rapidly at small $x$.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Kovchegov and collaborators (2018, 2019) for studying transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions at small $x$ to the quark Sivers function, confirming the recent results of Dong, Zheng and Zhou (2018) that the spin-dependent odderon contributes to the quark Sivers function. The result is similar to the gluon Sivers function case derived by Boer, Echevarria, Mulders and Zhou (2015) and also studied by Szymanowski and Zhou (2016). The construction of the complete polarized Wilson line operator to sub-sub-eikonal order enables future analyses of various other quark and gluon TMDs at small $x$, particularly relevant for measurements at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
