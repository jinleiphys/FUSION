# Neutral-current Neutrino-nucleus Scattering in Quasielastic Region

> The neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering is calculated through the neutrino-induced knocked-out nucleon process in the quasielastic region by using a relativistic single particle model for the bound and continuum states. The incident energy range between 500 MeV and 1.0 GeV is used for the neutrino (antineutrino) scattering on ^{12}C target nucleus. The effects of the final state interaction of the knocked-out nucleon are studied not only on the cross section but also on the asymmetry due to the difference between neutrinos and antineutrinos, within a relativistic optical potential. We also investigate the sensitivity of the strange quark contents in the nucleon on the asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper calculates neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering on carbon-12 in the quasielastic region using a relativistic single-particle model, finding that the final-state interaction (FSI) reduces cross sections by about 50% and that the strange quark content in the nucleon produces a non-negligible effect on the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry, contrary to earlier work by Meucci, Giusti, and Pacati (2004, 2006) which found the strange quark effect to be negligible.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for bound nucleon wave functions using the sigma-omega model (Horowitz and Serot, 1981) and generate continuum wave functions for the knocked-out nucleon using the relativistic optical potential EDAD1 (Cooper, Hama, Clark, and Mercer, 1993). The neutral-current cross section is computed from the contraction of the lepton tensor with the hadron tensor, using weak vector and axial form factors that include strange quark contributions via the parameters F1^s, F2^s(0), and g_A^s. The asymmetry between neutrino and antineutrino scattering is defined as the difference over the sum of their cross sections.

## Key numbers

- FSI reduces the neutral-current cross section on 12C by approximately 50%, consistent with Meucci, Giusti, and Pacati (2004, 2006) and Martinez, Lava, Jachowicz, Ryckebusch, Vantournhout, and Udias (2006).
- For incident neutrinos at 500 MeV, the strange quark contribution reduces the total cross section by about 5%; at 1.0 GeV the reduction is about 2%.
- For incident antineutrinos at 500 MeV, the strange quark contribution enhances the total cross section by about 10%; at 1.0 GeV the enhancement is about 5%.
- For knocked-out protons at 500 MeV, the strange quark effect increases the cross section by about 15% for neutrinos and 40% for antineutrinos; at 1.0 GeV the increases are about 20% for neutrinos and 35% for antineutrinos.
- For knocked-out neutrons at 500 MeV, the strange quark effect decreases the cross section by about 30% for neutrinos and 10% for antineutrinos; at 1.0 GeV the decreases are about 20% for neutrinos and 12% for antineutrinos.
- The strange axial form factor parameter g_A^s is taken as -0.19, following Bernard, Elouadrhiri, and Meissner (2002).
- The strange magnetic moment mu_s is taken as -0.4, and F1^s = -0.53 GeV^-2.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic single-particle model previously applied to electron scattering by Kim and Wright (1999, 2003) to neutral-current neutrino-nucleus reactions, incorporating the same bound-state and optical-potential formalism. It contradicts the finding of Meucci, Giusti, and Pacati (2004, 2006) that the strange quark contribution to the asymmetry is negligible, showing instead a large effect that increases with knocked-out nucleon kinetic energy. The results enable future studies of strange quark effects using charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering with Coulomb distortion of final leptons, and suggest that the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry may serve as an effective test of strange quark contributions to the axial vector form factor.
