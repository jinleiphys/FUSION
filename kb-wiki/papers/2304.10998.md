# $О›$ and $\overlineО›$ Freeze-Out Distributions and Global Polarizations in Au+Au Collisions

> The gold--gold collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ and $11.5$GeV are simulated within the PHSD transport model. In each collision event, the spectator nucleons are separated and the fluidization procedure for the participants is performed. The local velocities are determined in the Landau frame and the kinematic and thermal vorticity fields are evaluated. We analyze the thermodynamic properties of the cells where $О›$s and $\overlineО›$s were born or had their last interaction. Such cells contribute to the formation of the observed global polarization of hyperons induced by the thermal vorticity of the medium. The $\overlineО›$ polarization signal is found to be mainly determined by hot, dense, and highly vortical cells at the earlier stage of the collision, whereas the $О›$ polarization signal is accumulated over the longer time and includes cells with lower vorticity. The calculated global polarizations for both $О›$s and $\overlineО›$s agree well with the experimental finding by the STAR collaboration at energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=11.5$GeV. For collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$GeV, we can reproduce the STAR data for $О›$ hyperons, but significantly underpredict the observed global polarization of $\overlineО›$. Furthermore, we consider the centrality dependence of the hyperon polarization in collisions at 7.7GeV. It increases with an increase of centrality, reaches a maximum at 65--75%, and then starts decreasing rapidly for peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport model reproduces the STAR collaboration's measured global polarization for both Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons at 11.5 GeV, but at 7.7 GeV it matches the Lambda data while significantly underpredicting the anti-Lambda polarization, indicating that the model's description of the early-stage vortical medium may be incomplete for anti-hyperons at the lower energy.

## Method

Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 and 11.5 GeV were simulated using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport model. In each event, spectator nucleons were separated and a fluidization procedure was applied to the participants; local velocities were determined in the Landau frame, and kinematic and thermal vorticity fields were evaluated. The thermodynamic properties of cells where Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons were born or had their last interaction were analyzed to determine the contribution of thermal vorticity to the observed global polarization.

## Key numbers

* At sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 GeV, the calculated global polarizations for both Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons agree well with STAR experimental data.
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, the model reproduces the STAR data for Lambda hyperons but significantly underpredicts the observed global polarization of anti-Lambda.
* The centrality dependence of hyperon polarization at 7.7 GeV increases with centrality, reaches a maximum at 65-75% centrality, and then decreases rapidly for more peripheral collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous PHSD-based studies of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions by specifically tracking the freeze-out cells of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons separately. The finding that anti-Lambda polarization at 7.7 GeV is underpredicted contradicts the simpler expectation that both hyperons should be equally described by the same thermal vorticity mechanism, as assumed in earlier phenomenological approaches. The results enable future refinements of transport models to better capture the early-stage dynamics that preferentially affect anti-baryon production and polarization at lower beam energies.
