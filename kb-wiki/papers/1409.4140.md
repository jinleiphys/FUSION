# Reconstructed primary fragments and symmetry energy, temperature and density of the fragmenting source in $^{64}$Zn + $^{112}$Sn at 40 MeV/nucleon

> Symmetry energy, temperature and density at the time of the intermediate mass fragment formation are determined in a self-consistent manner, using the experimentally reconstructed primary hot isotope yields and anti-symmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) simulations. The yields of primary hot fragments are experimentally reconstructed for multifragmentation events in the reaction system $^{64}$Zn + $^{112}$Sn at 40 MeV/nucleon. Using the reconstructed hot isotope yields and an improved method, based on the modified Fisher model, symmetry energy values relative to the apparent temperature, $a_{sym}/T$, are extracted. The extracted values are compared with those of the AMD simulations, extracted in the same way as that for the experiment, with the Gogny interaction with three different density-dependent symmetry energy terms. $a_{sym}/T$ values change according to the density-dependent symmetry energy terms used. Using this relation, the density of the fragmenting system is extracted first. Then symmetry energy and apparent temperature are determined in a self consistent manner in the AMD model simulations. Comparing the calculated $a_{sym}/T$ values and those of the experimental values from the reconstructed yields, $ρ/ρ_{0} = 0.65 \pm 0.02 $, $a_{sym} = 23.1 \pm 0.6$ MeV and $T= 5.0 \pm 0.4$ MeV are evaluated for the fragmenting system experimentally observed in the reaction studied.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy, temperature, and density of the fragmenting source in multifragmentation events are determined self-consistently by comparing experimentally reconstructed primary hot isotope yields with AMD simulations, yielding a density of \( \rho/\rho_0 = 0.65 \pm 0.02 \), symmetry energy \( a_{sym} = 23.1 \pm 0.6 \) MeV, and temperature \( T = 5.0 \pm 0.4 \) MeV, but these values depend on the assumption that the system temperatures from AMD events and experimental reconstructed yields are nearly identical.

## Method

The yields of primary hot fragments were experimentally reconstructed for multifragmentation events in the reaction \( ^{64}\text{Zn} + ^{112}\text{Sn} \) at 40 MeV/nucleon using a kinematic focusing technique. An improved method based on the Modified Fisher Model (MFM) was applied to extract the symmetry energy relative to the apparent temperature, \( a_{sym}/T \), taking into account a mass-dependent apparent temperature \( T(A) = T_0(1 - kA) \). The extracted \( a_{sym}/T \) values were compared with those from AMD simulations using the Gogny interaction with three different density-dependent symmetry energy terms (g0, g0AS, g0ASS), and the density of the fragmenting system was extracted from the ratios of these values, followed by a self-consistent determination of symmetry energy and temperature.

## Key numbers

* Density of the fragmenting system: \( \rho/\rho_0 = 0.65 \pm 0.02 \), extracted from the overlap of density values implied by the ratios of \( a_{sym}/T \) between AMD simulations with different symmetry energy terms.
* Symmetry energy coefficient: \( a_{sym} = 23.1 \pm 0.6 \) MeV, evaluated for the experimentally observed fragmenting system.
* Apparent temperature of the fragmenting source: \( T = 5.0 \pm 0.4 \) MeV, determined self-consistently.
* For the g0 interaction at the final round (k=0.0022): \( a_{sym} = 26.0 \pm 0.4 \) MeV.
* For the g0AS interaction at the final round: \( a_{sym} = 21.4 \pm 1.3 \) MeV.
* For the g0ASS interaction at the final round: \( a_{sym} = 18.4 \pm 0.7 \) MeV.
* Ratio of \( a_{sym}/T \) values between g0 and experimental yields: \( 1.13 \pm 0.02 \) (final round).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Wada 2012) that extracted symmetry energies at low densities using light particles from the intermediate velocity source, and by Liu (2014) who applied the improved MFM method to simulated AMD events for central collisions. The self-consistent determination of density, symmetry energy, and temperature enables more direct comparison between experimental multifragmentation data and transport model predictions, addressing the complication of secondary decay by using reconstructed primary fragment yields. The results provide constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities relevant for nuclear astrophysics and the nuclear equation of state.
