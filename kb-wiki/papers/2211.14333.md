# Quantum Entanglement and the Thermal Hadron

> This paper tests how effectively the bound states of strongly interacting gauge theories are amenable to an emergent description as a thermal ensemble. This description can be derived from a conjectured minimum free energy principle, with the entanglement entropy of two-parton subsystems playing the role of thermodynamic entropy. This allows us to calculate the ground state hadron spectrum and wavefunction over a wide range of parton masses without solving the Schrödinger equation. We carry out this analysis for certain illustrative models in 1+1 dimensions and discuss prospects for higher dimensions.

## Key claim
The paper tests whether the ground state hadrons of confining gauge theories can be described as a thermal ensemble derived from a minimum free energy principle, where the entanglement entropy of two-parton subsystems replaces thermodynamic entropy. The authors find this description works well for massless partons and for moderate to large parton masses, but has less success for small but finite parton masses in the models studied.

## Method
The authors formulate a variational ansatz where the hadron wavefunction minimizes the free energy F = E - T S of two-parton subsystems with fixed total momentum, using the free parton kinetic energy in the light-cone frame as E and the von Neumann entropy of the single-parton reduced density matrix as S. They apply this ansatz to mesonic and baryonic bound states in several exactly-solvable 1+1 dimensional models: the Schwinger model (1+1D QED), the 't Hooft model (1+1D SU(N) with N → ∞), and 1+1D SU(N) with finite N. The intrinsic temperature T is treated as a variational parameter determined by minimizing the expectation value of the full hadron Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- For massless partons in mesons, the PDF becomes uniform: f_q(x) = 1, agreeing with existing results from 't Hooft (1974).
- For massless partons in baryons with N colors, the PDF takes the form f_q(x) = N(N-1)(1-x)^{N-2}, agreeing with results from Hornbostel (1988).
- The free energy ansatz yields the PDF form f_q(x) ∝ exp[-(m_q²/𝒯²)(1/x + 1/(1-x))] for mesons, where 𝒯² = P⁻T is the combination appearing in calculations.

## Context
This work extends the use of statistical and quantum information principles to model hadron properties, building on prior proposals by Kharzeev (2017) regarding thermal bath descriptions of parton interactions and by Wang (2014) and Han (2020) on applying maximum entropy principles to hadrons. The paper tests this approach against exactly-solvable 1+1D models including the Schwinger model (Schwinger, 1962), the 't Hooft model ('t Hooft, 1974), and results from Hornbostel (1988) and Jia (2017). The approach enables calculation of ground state hadron masses and PDFs without solving the Schrödinger equation, and the authors discuss prospects for extending the method to higher dimensions.
