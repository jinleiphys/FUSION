# Probing nuclear structure with mean transverse momentum in relativistic isobar collisions

> Transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) generation in relativistic heavy ion collisions is sensitive to the initial geometry and the final-state bulk evolution. We demonstrate with hydrodynamic calculations that the mean $p_T$ ratio ($R_{\langle p_{T}\rangle}$) between the highly similar isobar $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions is insensitive to the bulk evolution and remains sensitive to the small difference in the initial nuclear structure (neutron skin and deformation) between the Ru and Zr nuclei.We further find that nuclear deformation can produce an anticorrelation between $R_{\langle p_{T}\rangle}$ and eccentricity (or elliptic flow) in central collisions. These findings suggest that the $R_{\langle p_{T}\rangle}$ between the isobar systems can be used to measure the neutron skin thickness and deformation parameters, which can in turn constrain the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter.

## Key claim

The mean transverse momentum ratio between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is insensitive to the bulk properties of the quark-gluon plasma (such as shear and bulk viscosities) but remains sensitive to the small differences in nuclear structure (neutron skin thickness and deformation) between the two isobar nuclei, provided that the ratio rather than the absolute values is used to cancel dynamical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the iEBE-VISHNU model with a hadron cascade afterburner (UrQMD), with initial conditions from the Trento model using Woods-Saxon parameterizations of nuclear densities. The nuclear densities for Ru and Zr were obtained from energy density functional theory (DFT) calculations with three different symmetry energy slope parameters (Lc20, Lc47, Lc70), and additional simulations included quadrupole deformation for Ru (beta2=0.16) and octupole deformation for Zr (beta3=0.16). Approximately 1.6 million hydrodynamic events were simulated for each collision system with 50-fold oversampling of the UrQMD afterburner.

## Key numbers

- The neutron skin thickness (Delta r_np) from DFT calculations is 0.183 fm for Zr and 0.042 fm for Ru with the Lc47 parameter set
- The Rpt ratio can reach as large as 0.5% above unity in central collisions for spherical nuclear densities
- The largest variation in Rpt from varying shear viscosity (eta/s_min = 0.04, 0.08, 0.12) and bulk viscosity (zeta/s_max = 0.025, 0.052, 0.1) is on the order of 0.001
- The statistical precision of Rpt in the simulations is 10^-5 in a given centrality
- The PREX-II measurement yields a neutron skin thickness of 0.283 +/- 0.071 fm for Pb-208, corresponding to a symmetry energy slope parameter L = 71.5 +/- 22.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Li et al. (2019) on multiplicity differences and Xu et al. (2021) on elliptic flow measurements in isobar collisions as probes of nuclear structure. The authors demonstrate that the mean transverse momentum ratio can complement these approaches to determine both neutron skin thickness and nuclear deformation parameters, which in turn constrain the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter. The results are relevant to interpreting the 2018 RHIC isobar collision data collected by the STAR collaboration (approximately 2 billion events per species) and address tensions between DFT predictions and the PREX-II measurement of the lead neutron skin.
