# Parton Energy Loss and Modified Beam Quark Distribution Functions in Drell-Yan Process in p+A Collisions

> Within the framework of generalized collinear factorization in perturbative QCD (pQCD), we study the effect of initial multiple parton scattering and induced parton energy loss in Drell-Yan (DY) process in proton-nucleus collisions. We express the contribution from multiple parton scattering and induced gluon radiation to the DY dilepton spectra in terms of nuclear modified effective beam quark distribution functions. The modification depends on the quark transport parameter in nuclear medium. This is similar to the final-state multiple parton scattering in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off large nuclei and leads to the suppression of the Drell-Yan cross section in $p+A$ relative to $p+p$ collisions. With the value of quark transport parameter determined from the nuclear modification of single-inclusive DIS hadron spectra as measured by the HERMES experiment, we calculate DY spectra in $p+A$ collisions and find the nuclear suppression due to beam parton energy loss negligible at the Fermilab energy $E_{\rm lab}$=800 GeV in the kinematic region as covered by the E866 experiment. Most of the observed nuclear suppression of DY spectra in E866 experiment can be described well by parton shadowing in target nuclei as given by the EPS08 parameterization. The effect of beam parton energy loss, however, becomes significant for DY lepton pairs with large beam parton momentum fraction $x^{\prime}$ or small target parton momentum fraction $x$. We also predict the DY cross section in $p+A$ collisions at lower beam proton energy $E_{\rm lab}$=120 GeV and show significant suppression due to initial state parton energy loss at moderately large $x^{\prime}$ where the effect of parton shadowing is very small.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear suppression of Drell-Yan dilepton spectra in proton-nucleus collisions at Fermilab E866 energies (E_lab = 800 GeV) is predominantly due to parton shadowing in target nuclei (as parameterized by EPS08), with beam parton energy loss from initial-state multiple scattering being negligible in that kinematic region; however, the beam parton energy loss becomes significant for large beam parton momentum fraction x' or small target parton momentum fraction x, and becomes dominant at lower beam energies (E_lab = 120 GeV) where parton shadowing is small.

## Method

The authors apply the generalized collinear factorization (higher-twist) framework in perturbative QCD to calculate twist-4 contributions from initial multiple parton scattering and induced gluon radiation in the Drell-Yan process, expressing the nuclear effect as a modification to the effective beam quark distribution functions via modified splitting functions that depend on the quark transport parameter q-hat. They compute the Drell-Yan cross section including both the leading-twist (single scattering) and twist-4 (double scattering) contributions, incorporating the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) interference effect, and then evaluate the nuclear suppression ratio using the quark transport parameter previously determined from HERMES data on hadron suppression in deep inelastic scattering off nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The quark transport parameter used is determined from the nuclear modification of single-inclusive DIS hadron spectra as measured by the HERMES experiment (no explicit numerical value given in the text).
- At Fermilab energy E_lab = 800 GeV, the nuclear suppression due to beam parton energy loss is found to be negligible in the kinematic region covered by the E866 experiment.
- The paper predicts the Drell-Yan cross section in p+A collisions at lower beam proton energy E_lab = 120 GeV, showing significant suppression due to initial state parton energy loss at moderately large x'.
- The Bjorken variable in the Drell-Yan process is defined as x_B = Q^2 / (2 p dot q).
- The gluon formation time is given by tau_f = 1 / (x_t p^+), where x_t = (x_B / Q^2) * (q_T^2 / (1 - z)).
- The LPM interference effectively cuts off the transverse momentum of the virtual photon at q_T^2 ~ Q^2 / R_A.

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist approach previously applied to final-state multiple parton scattering in deep inelastic scattering off nuclei (Guo 2000, Zhang 2003, Majumder 2004) to the initial-state interactions in the Drell-Yan process, where only initial-state interactions matter because the final-state dilepton pairs do not interact strongly. The study enables quantitative separation of two nuclear effects, parton shadowing (parameterized by EPS08) and beam parton energy loss, in Drell-Yan production, and provides predictions for lower-energy experiments where the energy loss effect becomes dominant, complementing earlier studies of nuclear effects in Drell-Yan at large transverse momentum (Fries, earlier higher-twist Drell-Yan work).
