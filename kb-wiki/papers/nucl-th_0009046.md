# Bridging the mass gaps at A=5 and A=8 in nucleosynthesis

> In nucleosynthesis three possible paths are known to bridge the mass gaps at A=5 and A=8. The primary path producing the bulk of the carbon in our Universe proceeds via the triple-alpha process He4(2alpha,gamma)C12. This process takes place in helium-burning of red giant stars. We show that outside a narrow window of about 0.5% of the strength or range of the strong force, the stellar production of carbon or oxygen through the triple-alpha process is reduced by factors of 30 to 1000. Outside this small window the creation of carbon or oxygen and therefore also carbon-based life in the universe is strongly disfavored. The anthropically allowed strengths of the strong force also give severe constraints for the sum of the light quark masses as well as the Higgs vacuum expectation value and mass parameter at the 1% level.

# Bridging the mass gaps at A=5 and A=8 in nucleosynthesis

## Key claim

Outside a narrow window of about 0.5% of the strength or range of the strong force, the stellar production of carbon or oxygen through the triple-alpha process is reduced by factors of 30 to 1000, strongly disfavoring carbon-based life. The anthropically allowed strengths of the strong force also give constraints for the sum of the light quark masses as well as the Higgs vacuum expectation value and mass parameter at the 1% level.

## Method

The authors calculated the resonance energy and radiative width of the 0+2 state in 12C using a microscopic 12-body cluster model with Minnesota (MN) and modified Hasegawa-Nagata (MHN) nucleon-nucleon forces. They varied the strength and range parameters of these forces by factors between 0.994 and 1.006 for strength and 0.997 and 1.003 for range, recalculated the triple-alpha reaction rates, and then performed stellar model calculations for stars of 1.3, 5, and 20 solar masses to determine changes in carbon and oxygen abundances.

## Key numbers

- The window of allowed variation in the strength parameters of the nucleon-nucleon force is about 0.5%; for range parameters the window is 0.2-0.3%.
- Outside this window, stellar production of carbon or oxygen is reduced by factors of 30 to 1000.
- The 0+2 resonance in 12C has energy epsilon = 0.3796 MeV and width Gamma = 8.5 x 10^-6 MeV (8.5 eV).
- The sum of up- and down-quark masses is fine-tuned to approximately 1%, corresponding to about 0.15 MeV.
- The non-resonant contribution to the triple-alpha rate is 7-13 orders of magnitude smaller than the resonant contribution in the temperature range T = (0.8-3) x 10^8 K.
- To make 5He, 5Li, or 8Be become bound, the strong force would need to be increased by about 10%, a factor 20 larger than the maximal variation investigated.

## Context

This work extends the anthropic fine-tuning arguments previously applied to the deuteron binding (Pochet 1971, Agrawal 1998) and the non-existence of bound diproton or dineutron (Dyson 1971) by showing that carbon and oxygen production imposes constraints about two orders of magnitude tighter. The triple-alpha reaction rate was calculated using a microscopic 12-nucleon model building on earlier work by Opik (1951) and Salpeter (1952) on the 8Be equilibrium, and Hoyle's prediction of the 0+ resonance in 12C. The results enable constraints on fundamental parameters of elementary particle physics, including light quark masses and Higgs parameters, at the 1% level.
