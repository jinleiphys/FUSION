# Diquark correlations in baryon spectroscopy and holographic QCD

> We introduce an improved mass formula for the nucleon and delta resonances and show how it emerges from AdS/QCD in a straightforward extension of the 'metric soft wall' gravity dual. The resulting spectrum depends on just one adjustable parameter, characterizing confinement-induced IR deformations of the anti-de Sitter (AdS) metric, and on the fraction of 'good' (i.e. maximally attractive) diquarks in the baryon's quark model wave function. Despite its simplicity, the predicted spectrum describes the masses of all 48 observed light-quark baryon states and their linear trajectory structure with unprecedented accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a mass formula for nucleon and delta resonances that depends on just one adjustable parameter (the confinement-induced IR deformation scale) and on the fraction of "good" (maximally attractive) diquarks in the baryon's quark model wave function; the formula describes the masses of all 48 observed light-quark baryon states with unprecedented accuracy, but the authors note that the quantum number assignments required to compare with data rely on conjectures about the equivalence of radial and orbital excitation energies and about negative-parity delta states.

## Method

The authors start from the "metric soft wall" gravity dual of AdS/QCD, which predicts linear square-mass trajectories for baryons, and add a correction term that depends on the good diquark fraction κ_gd of each resonance. They assign orbital angular momentum L, radial excitation number N, and spin S to each observed nucleon and delta resonance based on regularities in the data and harmonic oscillator shell structure, then compare the measured masses to the predictions of the combined mass formula M² = 4λ²(N + L + 3/2) - 2(M_Δ² - M_N²)κ_gd, where λ = 0.52 GeV is determined from the delta trajectory slope.

## Key numbers

- λ = 0.52 GeV: the single adjustable parameter characterizing confinement-induced IR deformations of the AdS metric, determined from the empirical slope of delta trajectories
- The mass formula reproduces masses of all 48 observed light-quark baryon states (22 nucleon and 22 delta resonances listed by the Particle Data Group, plus additional states)
- The correction term ΔM² = -2(M_Δ² - M_N²)κ_gd depends on the good diquark fraction, with κ_gd = 0 for all delta and spin-3/2 nucleon resonances, κ_gd = 1/4 for spin-1/2 nucleons in the 70_SU(6)_ representation, and κ_gd = 1/2 for nucleons in the 56_SU(6)_ multiplet
- The nucleon ground state N(940) is used as input; the delta ground state Δ(1232) is predicted at 1.27 GeV

## Context

This work extends the "metric soft wall" AdS/QCD approach of Forkel (2007) which predicted linear square-mass trajectories for baryons but worked well only in the delta sector. The paper incorporates the long-suggested role of attractive diquark correlations in baryon structure, previously emphasized by Ida and Kobayashi (1966), Jaffe (2005), and Selem and Wilczek (2006), and shows how diquark content enters holographically through the different anomalous dimensions of baryon interpolating fields. The results enable a systematic understanding of baryon spectroscopy in terms of diquark content and suggest that baryon sizes decrease with increasing good-diquark content, while also providing a framework for future lattice calculations of baryon interpolator anomalous dimensions.
