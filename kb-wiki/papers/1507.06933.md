# Algebraic Solutions for $U^{BF}(5)-O^{BF}(6)$ Quantum Phase Transition in Odd Mass Number Nuclei

> The spherical to deformed $γ-unstable$ shape- phase transition in odd-A nuclei is investigated by using the Dual algebraic structures and the affine $SU(1,1)$ Lie Algebra within the framework of the interacting boson - fermion model. The new algebraic solution for A-odd nuclei is introduced. In this model, Single $j = 1/2 $ and $ 3/2 $ fermions are coupled with an even-even boson core. Energy spectra, quadruple electromagnetic transitions and an expectation value of the d-boson number operator are presented. Experimental evidence for the $U^{BF} (5)-O^{BF} (6)$ transition in odd -A $Ba$ and $Rh$ isotopes is presented. The low-states energy spectra and $B(E2)$values for these nuclei have been also calculated and compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new algebraic solution for the \(U^{BF}(5)-O^{BF}(6)\) quantum phase transition in odd-A nuclei using the affine \(\widehat{SU(1,1)}\) Lie algebra, but only for the specific cases where a single fermion with angular momentum \(j = 1/2\) or \(j = 3/2\) is coupled to an even-even boson core; the transition is driven solely by the boson core while the fermion remains a spectator.

## Method

The authors construct a transitional Hamiltonian within the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM) using dual algebraic structures and the affine \(\widehat{SU(1,1)}\) Lie algebra. Eigenstates are obtained via a Richardson-Gaudin type Bethe-Ansatz solution, where the control parameter \(C = c_s / c_d\) is varied between 0 (the \(U^{BF}(5)\) limit) and 1 (the \(O^{BF}(6)\) limit) to describe the spherical to gamma-unstable shape-phase transition. Hamiltonian parameters are determined by least-square fitting to experimental energy levels for chains of odd-A Ba and Rh isotopes.

## Key numbers

* For Rh isotopes, the control parameter \(C\) ranges from 0.06 (for \(^{101}_{45}Rh\)) to 0.7 (for \(^{109}_{45}Rh\)), with boson numbers \(N\) from 5 to 9.
* For Ba isotopes, the control parameter \(C\) ranges from 0.65 (for \(^{135}_{56}Ba\)) to 0.8 (for \(^{129}_{56}Ba\)), with boson numbers \(N\) from 3 to 8.
* The ratio \(E(\nu_d=2)/E(\nu_d=1)\) for Rh isotopes evolves from 2.1 to 2.8, while for Ba isotopes it varies from 2.85 to 1.9; values for \(^{105}_{45}Rh\) and \(^{133-135}_{56}Ba\) are approximately 2.2-2.4.
* The standard deviation \(\sigma\) of the energy fit ranges from 62.48 keV (for \(^{127}_{56}Ba\)) to 194.78 keV (for \(^{101}_{45}Rh\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions from even-even nuclei, described by the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) with dynamical symmetries \(U(5)\), \(SU(3)\), and \(O(6)\), to odd-A nuclei within the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM). It builds on prior studies by Scholten and Blasi, Alonso et al., and Boyukata et al. on QPTs in odd-even nuclei, and on Iachello's critical point symmetries \(E(5/4)\) for a \(j=3/2\) fermion coupled to a boson core. The new algebraic solution enables calculation of energy spectra, \(B(E2)\) values, and two-neutron separation energies for transitional odd-A nuclei such as Ba and Rh isotopes, providing a framework to test against experimental data.
