# Current Issues in the Nuclear Chiral Perturbation Approach

> After outlining the basic ideas of nuclear chiral perturbation theory, I discuss its application, presenting three examples which I believe are of great current interest: (1) Exchange currents in those cases where the leading chiral-order terms dominate; (2) The near-threshold $p+p\ra p+p+π^0$ reaction, for which the leading order terms are suppressed and consequently higher-order corrections play a prominent role; (3) Radiative $μ$-capture on the proton.

## Key claim

Nuclear chiral perturbation theory provides a systematic framework for low-energy nuclear phenomena, but its application to the near-threshold $p+p \to p+p+\pi^0$ reaction reveals that higher-order chiral terms dominate because the leading-order terms are suppressed, and the calculated cross sections remain much smaller than experimental values even after including next-to-leading order contributions.

## Method

The author applies heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to three nuclear systems: (1) exchange currents in the space component of the vector current and time component of the axial current, using a hybrid treatment where phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interactions generate nuclear wavefunctions while chiral perturbation theory provides the transition operators; (2) the $p+p \to p+p+\pi^0$ reaction near threshold, where the transition amplitude is computed from irreducible diagrams up to chiral order $\nu=1$; (3) radiative muon capture on the proton. The chiral Lagrangian is expanded in powers of $Q/\Lambda_\chi$ and $Q/m_N$, with low-energy constants $c_1$, $c_2$, $c_3$ determined from pion-nucleon scattering data.

## Key numbers

- For $n({\rm thermal})+p \to d+\gamma$: experimental cross section $\sigma_{\rm exp}=334.2\pm0.5$ mb, impulse approximation prediction $\sigma_{IA}=302.5\pm4.0$ mb, and chiral perturbation theory calculation including next-to-leading order gives $\sigma=334\pm2$ mb in agreement with experiment
- The low-energy constants (LEC) determined from pion-nucleon data: $c_1=-0.87\pm0.11$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_2=3.34\pm0.27$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_3=-5.25\pm0.22$ GeV$^{-1}$
- The on-shell chiral perturbation theory value for the s-wave $\pi N$ coupling: $\kappa_0=(0.87\pm0.20)$ GeV$^{-1}$, compared to the conventional value $(0.43\pm0.20)$ GeV$^{-1}$ from $\lambda_1=0.005$
- For the typical threshold kinematics in $pp\to pp\pi^0$: $\kappa_{\rm TT}=-1.5$ GeV$^{-1}$, opposite in sign and larger in magnitude than the on-shell value
- For first-forbidden beta transitions: the ratio of exchange-current to one-body contributions $\delta_{\rm mec}^{\rm exp}=0.5$ to $0.8$, with the leading-order chiral term explaining the bulk and next-order giving approximately 10% additional enhancement

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1990) for nuclear forces and van Kolck (1992) for two-nucleon interactions to electroweak transition processes, following the approach of Rho (1991). The exchange current results build on the low-energy theorem of Crewther (1971) and the one-pion exchange current calculations of Riska and Brown (1972), while the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ analysis extends the earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations of Park et al. (1996) and Cohen et al. (1996) by using momentum-space methods to avoid kinematical approximations. The work enables future fully consistent nuclear chiral perturbation theory calculations that treat transition operators and nuclear wavefunctions on the same footing, and motivates studies of nucleon-only effective theories as a testing ground for short-range phenomena.
