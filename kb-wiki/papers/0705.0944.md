# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Limit.I

> This report concerns the energy of a zero-temperature many-body system of spin 1/2 fermions interacting via a two-body potential with a free space infinite scattering length and zero effective range; the Unitary limit. Given the corresponding phase-shift $δ(k)=π/2$ a one-term separable potential is obtained by inverse scattering assuming a momentum cut-off $Λ$ such that $δ(k)=0$ for $k>Λ$. The \it effective \rm interaction in the many-body system is calculated in a pp-ladder approximation with Pauli-blocking but neglecting mean-field (dispersion) corrections; effective mass $m^{*}=1$. Using only the zero relative momentum component of this interaction the total energy is $ξ=4/9$ (in units of the fermigas), a result reported by several previous authors. Integrating the momentum dependent interaction over the Fermi sea this energy is revised to $ξ=0.24.$ This result is independent of density and of the cut-off $Λ$ if $Λ> \sim 3k_{f}$. With $m^{*}\neq 1$ there is however a strong dependence on this cut-off. Including hh-ladders estimates give $ξ=0.4\leftrightarrow 0.6$, but a reliable result would in this case require a Green's function calculation.

# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Limit.I

## Key claim
The total energy of a zero-temperature many-body system of spin 1/2 fermions in the unitary limit (infinite scattering length, zero effective range) is ξ = 0.24 in units of the free Fermi gas energy when calculated with pp-ladder approximation and effective mass m* = 1, but this result depends critically on including the full momentum dependence of the effective interaction; neglecting that dependence gives the previously reported ξ = 4/9.

## Method
A one-term separable two-body potential was constructed by inverse scattering from the phase-shift δ(k) = π/2, with a momentum cut-off Λ such that δ(k) = 0 for k > Λ. The effective in-medium interaction was calculated in a pp-ladder (Brueckner) approximation with Pauli blocking but neglecting mean-field dispersion corrections (effective mass m* = 1). The G-matrix equation was regularized using the reactance matrix subtraction method to restrict momentum integrations to k ≤ 2k_f, and the potential energy per particle was obtained by integrating the momentum-dependent G-matrix over the Fermi sea.

## Key numbers
- ξ = 0.24: total energy per particle in units of the free Fermi gas energy, obtained with pp-ladders and m* = 1, independent of density and cut-off Λ for Λ > ~3k_f.
- ξ = 4/9: result obtained when using only the zero relative momentum component of the interaction (I(0,0) approximation), consistent with several previous authors.
- ξ = 0.515: result when allowing center-of-mass momentum dependence but using I(0,y) instead of the full I(a,y).
- ξ = 0.4 ↔ 0.6: estimated range when including hh-ladders, but stated as unreliable without a Green's function calculation.
- With m* = 0.9, ξ → 1 as Λ → 10^3 k_f, showing strong cut-off dependence when m* ≠ 1.

## Context
This work extends the Brueckner G-matrix approach to the unitary limit problem originally posed by Bertsch (1999). The result ξ = 0.24 from pp-ladders is substantially lower than the Monte Carlo result ξ = 0.44 ± 0.01 from Carlson et al. (2003) and the lattice result ξ = 0.25(3) from Lee (2006). The formalism agrees analytically with the Effective Field Theory approach of Steele (2000) for the G-matrix expression, but differs by carrying the momentum integrations over occupied states to completion. The paper identifies the need for a full Green's function calculation to reliably include hh-ladder contributions and pairing effects, which are expected to be important based on the large pairing gaps found by Carlson et al. (2003).
