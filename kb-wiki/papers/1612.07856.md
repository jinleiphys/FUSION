# Boltzmann-Langevin Approach to Pre-equilibrium Correlations in Nuclear Collisions

> Correlations born before the onset of hydrodynamic flow can leave observable traces on the final state particles. Measurement of these correlations can yield important information on the isotropization and thermalization process. Starting from a Boltzmann-like kinetic theory in the presence of dynamic Langevin noise, we derive a new partial differential equation for the two-particle correlation function that respects the microscopic conservation laws. We illustrate how this equation can be used to study the effect of thermalization on long range correlations.

# Boltzmann-Langevin Approach to Pre-equilibrium Correlations in Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

Correlations that are only partially thermalized before the onset of hydrodynamic flow can leave observable traces on final-state particles, and the degree of thermalization can be studied using a Boltzmann-Langevin equation that respects microscopic conservation laws, provided the system is treated with a linearized relaxation time approximation and dynamic Langevin noise.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, linearize it around local equilibrium, and add a Langevin noise term consistent with the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. They derive a partial differential equation for the two-particle correlation function G12 by applying the Ito product rule to the stochastic difference equation for the phase space distribution, enforcing conservation laws through projection operators onto the five conserved quantities (particle number, momentum, and energy).

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time is given by nu^{-1}, where nu is the collision frequency; the survival probability S(tau, tau_0) = exp{-integral from tau_0 to tau of nu(tau') d tau'}.
- The first five eigenfunctions of the linearized collision operator have eigenvalue zero and correspond to the conserved quantities: phi_1 = 1, phi_{2,3,4} = sqrt(n/(w T)) p, phi_5 = sqrt(n/(c_v T)) (E - e/n), where n is parton density, T is temperature, w is enthalpy density, c_v is specific heat, e is energy density.
- The equilibrium phase space correlations satisfy (langle f_1 f_2 rangle - langle f_1 rangle langle f_2 rangle)^e = langle f_1 rangle delta(1-2), corresponding to Poisson statistics for number fluctuations in the grand canonical ensemble.

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann-Langevin framework previously applied to hydrodynamic fluctuations by Gavin, Moschelli, and Zin (2016) to the pre-equilibrium kinetic regime. It builds on Baym's (1984) solution of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for boost-invariant expansion, and on the BBGKY hierarchy analysis of Dufty, Lee, and Brey (1994) who derived a similar equation for non-relativistic fluids. The approach enables analytic study of how thermalization affects long-range correlations such as transverse momentum fluctuations, complementing numerical cascade simulations that require detailed balance for all m to n body scattering processes.
