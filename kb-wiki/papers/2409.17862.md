# Medium Effects in MIT Bag Model for quark matter: Self consistent thermodynamical treatment

> The study of strange quark matter within the framework of the density-dependent MIT Bag model using the Grand Canonical ensemble is thermodynamically inconsistent. In this work, it is shown that if the medium effects are incorporated through a density-dependent Bag pressure in the Grand Canonical ensemble, then the Euler relation is violated. If Euler relation is used then the minimum of energy per baryon does not occur at zero pressure. In order to overcome this inconsistency, we propose the medium effect of the strange quark matter in the form of chemical potential dependent Bag pressure in the grand Canonical ensemble. The density dependent Bag pressure which has been used in Grand Canonical ensemble so far can however be used in Canonical ensemble without violating the laws of thermodynamics. These prescriptions will obey the Euler relation as well as the minimum energy per baryon will coincide with the zero of pressure and hence can be considered to be self consistent. These equations of state in the Grand Canonical ensemble can be further used to construct the Mass-Radius and other structural properties of the strange quark stars as well as hybrid stars. In our present work we have calculated the mass radius diagram of strange stars only using this formalism.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that using a density-dependent Bag pressure in the Grand Canonical ensemble for strange quark matter violates the Euler relation, and that thermodynamic consistency is restored only by using a chemical-potential-dependent Bag pressure in the Grand Canonical ensemble or a density-dependent Bag pressure in the Canonical ensemble.

## Method
The authors reformulate the MIT Bag model for strange quark matter by introducing medium effects through either a density-dependent Bag pressure (in the Canonical ensemble) or a chemical-potential-dependent Bag pressure (in the Grand Canonical ensemble). They consider both Gaussian and hyperbolic functional forms for the Bag pressure dependence, enforce chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality for u, d, s quarks plus electrons, and solve for the equation of state at zero temperature. The resulting equations of state are then used to compute mass-radius relations for strange quark stars by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses used: m_u = 5.0 MeV, m_d = 7.0 MeV, m_s = 95.0 MeV (from Particle Data Group 2020)
- For the Grand Canonical ensemble with Gaussian chemical-potential-dependent Bag pressure: B_0 = 100 MeV fm^{-3}, B_as = 30 MeV fm^{-3}, beta_mu = 1.0, mu_0 = 1000 MeV
- For the Canonical ensemble with Gaussian density-dependent Bag pressure: B_0 = 100 MeV fm^{-3}, B_as = 30 MeV fm^{-3}, beta_rho = 0.1, rho_0 = 0.152 fm^{-3}
- For the Canonical ensemble with hyperbolic density-dependent Bag pressure: bar_rho = 2 rho_0, Gamma_rho = 2.5 rho_0
- Stability parameter ranges for Grand Canonical Gaussian Bag pressure with B_0 = 100 MeV fm^{-3}, B_as = 30 MeV fm^{-3}: beta_mu in [0.31, 1.11]
- Stability parameter ranges for Canonical Gaussian Bag pressure with B_0 = 100 MeV fm^{-3}, B_as = 30 MeV fm^{-3}: beta_rho in [0.035, 0.23]
- Speed of sound C_s^2 tends to 1/3 at high pressure for both ensembles

## Context
This work extends the thermodynamic consistency analysis previously applied to the density-dependent quark mass model by Lugones (2020) to the MIT Bag model. It addresses the inconsistency problem identified when density-dependent Bag pressure is used in the Grand Canonical ensemble, which violates the Euler relation. The reformulation enables self-consistent equations of state for strange quark matter that can be used to compute mass-radius relations for strange stars, with comparisons to observational constraints from HESS J1731-347, GW170817, and PSR J0740+6620.
