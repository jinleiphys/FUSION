# Charge Independence and Symmetry of Nuclear Forces

> Charge independence and symmetry are approximate symmetries of nature. The observations of the small symmetry breaking effects and the consequences of those effects are reviewed. The effects of the mass difference between up and down quarks and the off shell dependence $q^2$ of $蟻^0$-$蠅$ mixing are stressed. In particular, I argue that models which predict a strong $q^2$ dependence of $蟻^0$-$蠅$ mixing seem also to predict a strong $q^2$ variation for the $蟻^0$-$纬^*$ matrix element, in contradiction with experiment.

# Charge Independence and Symmetry of Nuclear Forces

## Key claim

Charge independence and charge symmetry are approximate symmetries of nature, with small breaking effects arising from the up-down quark mass difference and electromagnetic interactions; however, models that predict a strong $q^2$ dependence of $\rho^0$-$\omega$ mixing also predict a strong $q^2$ variation for the $\rho^0$-$\gamma^*$ matrix element, which contradicts experimental data on $f_\rho(q^2)$.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental observations of charge independence breaking (CIB) and charge symmetry breaking (CSB) across multiple nuclear systems, from nucleon-nucleon scattering to heavy nuclei. It analyzes the $\rho^0$-$\omega$ mixing matrix element extracted from $e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-$ annihilation data, evaluates the $q^2$ dependence of this mixing using quark model calculations, and compares predictions for the $\rho^0$-$\gamma^*$ transition matrix element against experimental measurements from photoproduction reactions.

## Key numbers

- $a_{pp} = -17.7 \pm 0.4$ fm, $a_{nn} = -18.8 \pm 0.3$ fm, $a_{np} = -23.75 \pm 0.09$ fm (nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths in the $^1$S$_0$ channel)
- $\Delta a_{CD} = 5.7 \pm 0.3$ fm (charge independence breaking measure from scattering lengths)
- $\Delta a_{CSB} = a_{pp} - a_{nn} = 1.5 \pm 0.5$ fm (charge symmetry breaking measure)
- $<\rho^0|H|\omega> \approx -4500$ MeV$^2$ (total $\rho^0$-$\omega$ mixing matrix element from $e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-$)
- $<\rho^0|H_{em}|\omega> = 640 \pm 140$ MeV$^2$ (electromagnetic contribution), giving $<\rho^0|H_{str}|\omega> \approx -5100$ MeV$^2$ (strong contribution)
- $f^2_\rho(q^2=M^2_\rho)/4\pi = 2.11 \pm 0.06$ and $f^2_\rho(q^2=0)/4\pi = 2.18 \pm 0.22$ (no significant $q^2$ variation observed)
- $B(^3{\rm H}) - B(^3{\rm He}) = 764$ keV, with electromagnetic contribution $\Delta B(em) = 693 \pm 19 \pm 5$ keV, leaving 71 keV from strong CSB
- $\rho^0$-$\omega$ exchange potential accounts for $\Delta a_{CSB}$ with a contribution of 1.4 fm

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Henley and Morrison (1966) on charge independence breaking in nucleon-nucleon scattering and Henley's 1969 review distinguishing charge independence from charge symmetry. It addresses the controversy raised by Goldman, Henderson and Thomas (who found strong $q^2$ dependence of $\rho^0$-$\omega$ mixing using free quark propagators) and subsequent works by Krein, Thomas and Williams (1992) and others. The paper argues that such models are inconsistent with experimental data on $f_\rho(q^2)$ from the review of Bauer, Spital, Yennie and Pipkin, and that the $\rho^0$-$\omega$ exchange potential with minimal $q^2$ dependence successfully accounts for CSB in the $^1$S$_0$ channel, the $^3$He-$^3$H binding energy difference, and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in mirror nuclei.
