# Condensed vortex ground states of rotating Bose-Einstein condensate in harmonic atomic trap

> We study a system of $N$ Bose atoms trapped by a symmetric harmonic potential, interacting via weak central forces. Considering the ground state of the rotating system as a function of the two conserved quantities, the total angular momentum and its collective component, we develop an algebraic approach to derive exact wave functions and energies of these ground states. We describe a broad class of the interactions for which these results are valid. This universality class is defined by simple integral condition on the potential. Most of the potentials of practical interest which have pronounced repulsive component belong to this universality class.

## Key claim

The paper provides a rigorous analytical solution for the yrast states (minimum-energy states at fixed angular momentum) of a rotating Bose-Einstein condensate in a harmonic trap, valid for any two-body interaction potential that satisfies an integral positivity condition, not just the delta-function interaction studied previously.

## Method

The authors project the Hamiltonian onto the subspace of degenerate single-level states (the "partition subspace") in the weak-coupling limit, where the interaction energy is small compared to the oscillator level spacing. They develop an operator expansion of the projected interaction in terms of two-particle ladder operators, then apply an "algebraic decomposition" method that separates the Hamiltonian into a simple part \(V_0\) and a remainder \(V_S\), proving that \(V_S\) is non-negative definite when the potential satisfies a set of integral inequalities. The ground states are constructed from the generating function \(|L\rangle = P_S a^+_1 a^+_2 ... a^+_L |0\rangle\) projected onto sectors of definite seniority (collective angular momentum).

## Key numbers

* The condition for weak coupling: \(N\langle V \rangle \ll \hbar\omega\), where \(\langle V \rangle\) is a typical matrix element of the interaction.
* The number of basis states in the partition subspace for \(L \leq N\) is given by the partition numbers: \(p(2)=2\), \(p(3)=3\), \(p(4)=5\), \(p(5)=7\), \(p(6)=11\), \(p(7)=15\), \(p(8)=22\).
* For large \(L\), the number of states grows as \(p(L \gg 1) \propto e^{L^{1/2}}\).
* The ground-state energy for given total angular momentum \(L\) and seniority \(v\) is \({\cal E}_{min}(L,v) = L + N + \frac{N(N-1)}{2} s_0 + \frac{s_0 - s_1}{2} N (v-L)\), where \(s_0 = \int_0^\infty dt e^{-t} V(\sqrt{2t})\) and \(s_1 = \int_0^\infty dt e^{-t} \left(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{t^2}{4}\right) V(\sqrt{2t})\).
* The allowed seniority values are \(v = 0, 1, 2, ..., L-2, L\) (excluding \(v = L-1\)).

## Context

This work extends the numerical diagonalization results of Bertsch and Papenbrock (2000) for repulsive delta-interactions to a broad universality class of repulsive potentials, including Gaussian, Coulomb (\(1/r\)), and log-Coulomb (\(\log r\)) forces. The analytical solution for attractive delta-forces was previously found by Wilkin et al. (1998) and generalized to attractive interactions in a later work. The results enable exact calculation of yrast spectra for experimentally relevant trap geometries and interaction strengths, particularly in the weak-coupling regime expected to be reached via Feshbach resonance tuning.
