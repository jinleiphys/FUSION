# Attenuation of the intensity within a superdeformed band

> The attenuation of the intraband intensity of a superdeformed band, which results from mixing with normally deformed configurations, is calculated using reaction theory. It is found that the sharp increase of the attenuation is mostly due to the tunnelling through a spin dependent barrier and not to the chaotic nature of the normally deformed states.

## Key claim

The sharp drop in intraband intensity within a superdeformed band is primarily caused by tunneling through a spin-dependent barrier, not by the chaotic nature of the normally deformed states as classified by random matrix theory.

## Method

The authors model the mixing between a superdeformed (SD) state and normally deformed (ND) states using reaction theory, writing the relative intensity of two consecutive E2 gamma transitions as the sum of average and fluctuation contributions from an energy-averaged Green's function. Two models are compared: model A, where the SD state couples randomly to many ND states (equivalent to prior work with the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble), and model B, where the SD state couples to a single ND state which then mixes with other ND configurations via a residual interaction. The ND states are generated using the deformed Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (DGOE), which interpolates between Poisson and GOE statistics via a mixing parameter λ.

## Key numbers

- The electromagnetic width of the SD state is set to Γ_γ_J = 0.01 D_J, where D_J is the mean spacing of ND states at spin J.
- Calculations use N = 50 ND states, with an ensemble average over 100 realizations (Figure 1) and 1000 realizations (Figure 2).
- The sum Γ_γ_N + I (ND electromagnetic width plus energy averaging interval) is set to 3 D_J, placing the system in the overlapping resonance region.
- For model B, varying λ changes F_av_J by at most a factor of about 5.
- The approximation F_av_J ≈ Γ_γ_J / (Γ_γ_J + Γ_↓_J) is obtained when the shift Δ_↓_J(E) is ignored and Γ_↓_J(E) is taken as energy-independent.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Vigezzi et al. (1990) and the reaction-theory framework of Weidenmüller (1998) and Gu (1999) for decay out of superdeformed bands. It contradicts the suggestion in Abe (1999) that the chaotic nature of ND states causes large changes in tunneling probability, finding instead that the spin dependence of the tunneling matrix element (contained in the parameter b_J = Γ_↓_J / Γ_γ_J) drives the exponential drop in intensity. The results enable future calculations of b_J using the spin-dependent barrier, as pursued by Shimizu (2000) and Yoshida (2000).
