# Associated-quarkonium production

> We discuss the growing interest to measure associated-quarkonium production in a number of channels at the LHC. Whereas back-to-back production of quarkonium + isolated photon provides a unique way to extract gluon TMDs, observables such as quarkonium + W/Z can be of great help to better understand the quarkonium production mechanism as well as to shed light on double-parton scatterings. Along these lines, we also argue that quarkonium-pair production is a potentially rich source of information which only has started to be harvested. Finally, we discuss the relevance of studying the production of quarkonium + heavy-quark, as e.g. J/psi + charm and Upsilon + non-prompt J/psi.

## Key claim

Associated-quarkonium production channels at the LHC can provide new constraints on the quarkonium production mechanism, but the theoretical interpretation is complicated by large perturbative QCD corrections and the need to disentangle single-parton scattering from double-parton scattering contributions.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical calculations for several associated-quarkonium production channels at hadron colliders, including quarkonium-pair production, quarkonium plus heavy quarks, quarkonium plus isolated photon, and quarkonium plus W/Z bosons. The author presents leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations using the colour-singlet mechanism (CSM) and colour-octet mechanism (COM) within the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) framework, with some calculations performed using the automated matrix element generator HELAC-Onia.

## Key numbers

- For J/psi pair production at NLO*, the cross section is 8 times larger than LO for P_T greater than 5 GeV and nearly 400 times larger for P_T greater than 30 GeV
- For Upsilon + non-prompt J/psi production at the LHC, up to 400 events are expected at P_T^Upsilon approximately 10 GeV with an integrated luminosity of 20 fb^-1
- At sqrt(s)=7 TeV, the total CSM cross section for J/psi+W production is about twice as large as the COM cross section
- For back-to-back quarkonium-photon production at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, cross sections are tabulated for different pair invariant masses Q between 20 and 25 GeV and for |Y|<0.5 and |cos(theta)|<0.45

## Context

This review extends the discussion of quarkonium production puzzles that have persisted since early LHC data, where NLO corrections to both the colour-singlet mechanism (Campbell 2007, Artoisenet 2007, Gong 2008) and colour-octet mechanism (Butenschoen 2012) significantly affect predictions. The work builds on earlier studies of associated production including J/psi pair measurements by NA3 (Badier 1982, 1985) and LHCb (Aaij 2011), and provides a framework for future experimental studies at the LHC and potential fixed-target experiments such as AFTER@LHC (Brodsky 2012).
