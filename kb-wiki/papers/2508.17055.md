# Spontaneous breaking of global U(1) symmetry in an interacting Bose gas under rigid rotation

> We investigate the impact of rigid rotation on the spontaneous breaking of U(1) symmetry in a Bose gas, which is described by a self-interacting complex scalar field Lagrangian. Rigid rotation is introduced through a specific metric that explicitly depends on the angular velocity $Ω$. We begin by determining the free propagator for this model at finite temperature $T$ and chemical potential $μ$. Using this propagator, we calculate the thermodynamic potential in terms of an energy dispersion relation $ε_{k}$. It is found that in both the U(1) symmetric phase and the symmetry-broken phase, two energy branches emerge. In the symmetry-broken phase, they are identified with a massive phonon and a massless roton mode. Notably, rotation does not alter $ε_{k}$ at low momentum. Setting $μ=0$, we use the total thermodynamic potential, which includes classical, thermal, vacuum, and nonperturbative ring contributions, to explore how the condensate depends on $T$ and $Ω$. We first focus on the classical and thermal parts of the thermodynamic potential and find that the critical temperature of the U(1) phase transition scales as $Ω^{1/3}$. By identifying the (pseudo-)Goldstone and non-Goldstone modes of this model with $π$ and $σ$ mesons, we calculate the $T$ and $Ω$ dependence of masses $m_π$ and $m_σ$. We demonstrate that the Goldstone theorem holds only when the one-loop (thermal) corrections to $m_σ$ and $m_π$ are taken into account. We further explore the $T$ and $Ω$ dependence of the condensate, determine the $σ$ dissociation temperatures for fixed $Ω$, and compare them with the critical temperature of the phase transition. Additionally, we emphasize the role played by the nonperturbative ring potential, especially in altering the order of the phase transition with and without rotation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that rigid rotation modifies the critical temperature of the U(1) symmetry-breaking phase transition in an interacting Bose gas, with the critical temperature scaling as Omega^{1/3}, but only when considering the classical and thermal parts of the thermodynamic potential; the full analysis including vacuum and ring contributions shows that rotation reduces the critical temperature compared to the nonrotating case, though the critical temperature then increases as Omega rises.

## Method

The authors start from a complex scalar field Lagrangian with a quartic self-interaction term and introduce rigid rotation via a metric that depends on angular velocity Omega. They derive the free propagator using a Fourier-Bessel transformation in cylindrical coordinates, compute the thermodynamic potential including classical, thermal, vacuum, and nonperturbative ring contributions, and perform a high-temperature expansion to obtain analytical expressions. For the case of zero chemical potential, they minimize the total thermodynamic potential numerically to determine the condensate and the temperature and Omega dependence of the sigma and pi meson masses.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of the U(1) phase transition scales as Omega^{1/3} when only classical and thermal parts of the thermodynamic potential are considered.
- The minima of the classical-plus-thermal potential are proportional to (1 - t^3), where t = T/T_c is the reduced temperature, in contrast to the nonrotating case where minima scale as (1 - t_0^2).
- The sigma dissociation temperature T_diss, defined by m_sigma(T_diss) = 2 m_pi(T_diss), is found to be less than the critical temperature for fixed Omega.
- For the nonrotating case (Omega = 0), a discontinuous phase transition occurs at a specific temperature; for Omega not equal to 0, the phase transition remains continuous.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on rotating Bose gases, including the authors' own work on free Bose gases under rigid rotation (Siri 2024b), which showed that rotation modifies the critical temperature for Bose-Einstein condensation and can alter the order of the phase transition. It also builds on studies of rotating relativistic bosons (Braguta 2023a, Braguta 2023b, Ambrus 2023) and the linear sigma model with quarks (Singha 2024, Hernandez 2024, Ambrus 2025, Singha 2025). The results enable further investigation of how rotation affects the QCD phase diagram and the properties of mesons in heavy-ion collision experiments, where angular velocities up to 10^22 Hz are believed to be generated.
