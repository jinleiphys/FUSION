# From Experimental Data to Pole Parameters in a Direct Way (Angle Dependent Continuum Ambiguity and Laurent + Pietarinen Expansion)

> Unconstrained partial-wave amplitudes obtained at discrete energies from fits to complete sets of eight independent observables which are required to uniquely reconstruct reaction amplitudes do not vary smoothly with energy, and are in principle non-unique. We demonstrate how this behavior can be ascribed to the continuum ambiguity. Starting from the spinless scattering case, we demonstrate how an unknown overall phase depending on energy and angle mixes the structures seen in the associated partial-wave amplitudes making the partial wave decomposition non-unique, and illustrate it on a simple toy model. We then apply these principles to pseudo-scalar meson photoproduction and show that the non-uniqueness effect can be removed through a phase rotation generating "up-to-a-phase" unique set of SE partial wave amplitudes. Extracting pole positions from partial wave amplitudes is the next step. Up to now, there was no reliable way to extract pole parameters from SE partial waves, but a new and simple single-channel method (Laurent + Pietarinen expansion) applicable for continuous and discrete data has been recently developed. It is based on applying the Laurent decomposition of partial wave amplitude, and expanding the nonresonant background into a power series of a conformal-mapping, quickly converging power series obtaining the simplest analytic function with well-defined partial wave analytic properties which fits the input. The generalization of this method to multi- channel case is also developed and presented. Unifying both methods in succession, one constructs a model independent procedure to extract pole parameters directly from experimental data without referring to any theoretical model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that unconstrained single-energy partial-wave analyses of complete sets of observables produce non-unique multipole solutions because the continuum ambiguity introduces an arbitrary energy- and angle-dependent phase that mixes partial waves; however, this non-uniqueness can be removed by replacing the discontinuous phase with any arbitrary but continuous phase, yielding an "up-to-a-phase" unique set of partial-wave amplitudes.

## Method

The authors first illustrate the angle-dependent continuum ambiguity using a spinless toy model with two resonant partial waves, showing how a linear phase rotation mixes the partial-wave decomposition. They then perform unconstrained single-energy partial-wave analyses on a complete set of eight observables for eta photoproduction, using pseudo-data generated from the ETA-MAID15a model at 18 angles with 1% errors. The resulting multipole solutions are shown to be non-unique and discontinuous in energy, but the authors demonstrate that applying a phase rotation to the helicity amplitudes, replacing the discontinuous phase with a continuous one, makes all solutions identical up to an overall phase. Finally, they present the Laurent plus Pietarinen expansion method for extracting pole parameters from partial-wave amplitudes, which separates the resonant part via a Laurent expansion and expands the non-resonant background into a rapidly converging power series using conformal mapping.

## Key numbers

- Toy model resonance parameters: S-wave mass M_S = 1.535 GeV, width Gamma_S = 0.15 GeV, residue a_S = (0.005 + i 0.004) GeV; P-wave mass M_P = 1.440 GeV, width Gamma_P = 0.10 GeV, residue a_P = (0.004 + i 0.003) GeV.
- Pseudo-data for eta photoproduction were generated at W = 1769.8 MeV and at 18 angles using the ETA-MAID15a model, with 1% error.
- The single-energy fits used a truncation at L_max = 5 for the partial-wave expansion.
- The toy model phase rotation used was R(x) = exp[i (2.0 + 0.5 x)], where x = cos(theta).
- The Legendre expansion of this rotation was truncated at L' = 5 to a very good approximation, with all higher Legendre coefficients having moduli within 10^{-7} or less.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the continuum ambiguity from the 1970s and 1980s (Atkinson 1973, Bowcock 1975, Atkinson 1985), which was previously studied mainly in the context of pi-N elastic scattering where elastic unitarity suppresses the ambiguity. The authors show that for inelastic reactions like pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, the angle-dependent phase rotation mixes partial waves and changes their analytic structure, directly impacting resonance quantum number identification, an effect mentioned only briefly in older work (Omnès 1981). The Laurent plus Pietarinen expansion method, previously developed for single-channel analyses (Švarc 2013, 2014), is generalized to the multi-channel case, enabling a model-independent procedure to extract pole parameters directly from experimental data without constructing elaborate theoretical models.
