# Two-Nucleon Scattering without partial waves using a momentum space Argonne V18 interaction

> We test the operator form of the Fourier transform of the Argonne V18 potential by computing selected scattering observables and all Wolfenstein parameters for a variety of energies. These are compared to the GW-DAC database and to partial wave calculations. We represent the interaction and transition operators as expansions in a spin-momentum basis. In this representation the Lippmann-Schwinger equation becomes a six channel integral equation in two variables. Our calculations use different numbers of spin-momentum basis elements to represent the on- and off-shell transition operators. This is because different numbers of independent spin-momentum basis elements are required to expand the on- and off-shell transition operators. The choice of on and off-shell spin-momentum basis elements is made so that the coefficients of the on-shell spin-momentum basis vectors are simply related to the corresponding off-shell coefficients.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Argonne V18 potential can be used to compute two-nucleon scattering observables without partial-wave decomposition by expanding the interaction and transition operators in a spin-momentum basis, and that using a time-odd sixth operator in the off-shell expansion yields numerically stable solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, whereas using a sixth operator with the same symmetries as the potential leads to numerical instabilities.

## Method

The authors represent the Argonne V18 potential and the transition operator as expansions in six linearly independent spin-momentum operators, with the on-shell transition matrix expanded using five operators and the off-shell using six. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation becomes a six-channel integral equation in two variables (momentum magnitude and angle), which is solved by discretizing the integrals using quadrature points and treating the on-shell singularity via a subtraction method. The on-shell scattering amplitude is extracted directly by expanding it in the five Wolfenstein operators, avoiding the need to take on-shell limits of off-shell amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Wolfenstein amplitudes for neutron-proton (np) scattering at 100 MeV laboratory energy: the 3D calculation agrees perfectly with a partial-wave calculation using j_max=6, and even j_max=4 already converges to the 3D result.
- Wolfenstein amplitudes for proton-proton (pp) scattering at 100 MeV: the 3D calculation agrees well with a partial-wave sum up to j_max=6, with only the Real c amplitude showing a small deviation.
- Wolfenstein amplitudes for np scattering at 350 MeV laboratory energy: the partial-wave sum up to j_max=4 is clearly not converged, and backward angles of the real parts of a and h show particularly bad convergence.
- Wolfenstein amplitudes for pp scattering at 350 MeV: even a partial-wave sum up to j_max=6 is not yet converged to the full 3D calculation, and even j_max=9 is not quite converged for forward and backward angles of a, g, and h.
- The relationship between on-shell and off-shell coefficient functions when using the time-odd operator W^6 as the sixth operator is: V_1^onshell = V_1^offshell, V_2^onshell = V_2^offshell, V_3^onshell = V_3^offshell, V_4^onshell = V_4^offshell, V_5^onshell = V_5^offshell, which does not exhibit rapid variations near the on-shell point.

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space treatment of the Argonne V18 potential previously tested only for the deuteron bound state (Veerasamy, 2010) to the full scattering regime, providing a complete test of that momentum-space representation. It builds on a three-dimensional operator expansion approach proposed for chiral NN forces and one-boson-exchange potentials (Golak, 2010), and on a helicity formulation for NN scattering without partial waves (Fachruddin, 2000). The method enables future extension to three-nucleon Faddeev calculations without partial-wave projections, following similar work on three-boson systems (Lin, 2008).
