# Thermodynamic properties of gluon plasma: A q-potential approach

> In this work, we study the thermodynamic properties of quark-gluon plasma using Kramer's $q-potential$ method. We propose a modification in the first law of thermodynamics by including a temperature-dependent term when single particle energies themselves are temperature-dependent. With this modified first law, we derive an expression for pressure starting from Kramer's $q-potential$. We find that to maintain thermodynamic consistency, pressure receives an additional term solely due to medium-dependent dispersion relation. The energy density, by definition, remains a sum over all the single-particle energies with an appropriate weight factor. We confront this quasiparticle model with the lattice QCD data of SU(3) pure gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a thermodynamically consistent quasiparticle model for gluon plasma by modifying the first law of thermodynamics to include a temperature-dependent term when single-particle energies depend on temperature; the model reproduces lattice QCD data for SU(3) pure gluon plasma, but the agreement depends on fitting the parameter 'a' to the lattice results.

## Method

The author starts from Kramer's q-potential in the grand canonical ensemble and derives an expression for pressure by including an additional term in the first law of thermodynamics that accounts for temperature-dependent quasiparticle energies. The pressure is then expressed as the ideal gas pressure plus a correction term Phi(T) that depends on the temperature derivative of the quasiparticle mass. The quasiparticle mass is parametrized as a function of temperature using a two-loop running coupling constant, with one parameter 'a' fitted to lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2012).

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle mass parametrization uses m(T) = a/((T/T_c - 1)^c) + b(T/T_c)(T/T_c), with c = 0.41 for gluon plasma
- The two-loop running coupling uses T_c/Lambda_T = 0.79 from lattice QCD results of Borsanyi (2012)
- The model is compared with lattice QCD data of SU(3) pure gluon plasma from Borsanyi (2012) over a wide range of temperatures

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle model approach to quark-gluon plasma thermodynamics, which was initially developed by Goloviznin (1992) and Peshier (1994) but failed to accurately explain lattice data. Gorenstein and Yang (1992) pointed out thermodynamic inconsistencies in those early models and proposed a temperature-dependent vacuum energy as a remedy, while Bannur (2006) showed that following standard statistical mechanics could produce a thermodynamically consistent single-parameter model. The present work offers an alternative formulation that modifies the first law of thermodynamics directly, and it enables future applications to full QCD with quarks and to systems with nonzero chemical potential.
