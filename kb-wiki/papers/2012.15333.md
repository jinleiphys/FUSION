# Woods-Saxon-type of mean-field potentials with effective mass derived from the D1S Gogny force

> Analytic average mean-field potentials of the Fermi-function (Woods-Saxon) type for the whole nuclear chart with space-dependent effective mass are deduced from the D1S Gogny force. Those ready-for-use potentials are advertised as an alternative to other existing phenomenological mean-field potentials.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytic Woods-Saxon-type mean-field potentials with a space-dependent effective mass from the D1S Gogny force, but the choice of the Gogny force is not essential, a Skyrme force with effective mass could have been used instead, and the resulting potentials are intended as an alternative to existing phenomenological mean-field potentials that lack an effective mass.

## Method

The authors use the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation up to second-order ℏ corrections with finite-range Gogny interactions to compute semiclassical densities, mean-field potentials, spin-orbit potentials, and effective masses for a set of 551 even-even nuclei. They perform a restricted variational minimization using trial neutron and proton densities of modified Fermi type, then fit the resulting quantities to generalized Fermi functions whose parameters are systematized as functions of mass number A and asymmetry I = (N-Z)/A. Two sets of mean-field potentials are produced: ETF-HF potentials that include the effective mass, and phenomenological potentials where the effective mass contribution is subsumed into the potential so that only the bare mass appears in the kinetic energy operator.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy for ²⁰⁸Pb obtained with the renormalization factor β_KT = 1.9 is approximately -1625 MeV, which with a shell correction of approximately -11 MeV yields a total energy close to the experimental value of approximately -1636 MeV.
- The trial density exponents are fixed at ν = 1.5 for neutrons and ν = 2.5 for protons.
- The mean-field exponents are taken as ν = 1.5 for neutrons and ν = 3.0 for protons.
- The fits cover 551 even-even nuclei from ²⁰Ne to ²³²Ds.
- The rms residues of the fits for densities, mean fields, and effective masses are of the order of 10⁻² or smaller.

## Context

This work extends the tradition of phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials, such as those by Shlomo (1992) and the Wyss potential used in previous Mic-Mac calculations by Bhagwat (2010, 2012), by adding a space-dependent effective mass derived from the D1S Gogny force. The resulting analytic potentials are designed for use in microscopic-macroscopic calculations of nuclear masses and shell corrections via the Wigner-Kirkwood ℏ-expansion method, and have already been applied to describe the inner crust of neutron stars using Gogny forces by Mondal (2020).
