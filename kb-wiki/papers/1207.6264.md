# Mass- and field-shift isotope parameters for the $2s - 2p$ resonance doublet of lithium-like ions

> It was recently shown that dielectronic recombination measurements can be used for accurately inferring changes in the nuclear mean-square charge radii of highly-charged lithium-like neodymium [Brandau et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 100 073201 (2008)]. To make use of this method to derive information about the nuclear charge distribution for other elements and isotopes, accurate electronic isotope shift parameters are required. In this work, we calculate and discuss the relativistic mass- and field-shift factors for the two $2s ^{2}S_{1/2} - 2p ^{2}P^{o}_{1/2,3/2}$ transitions along the lithium isoelectronic sequence. Based on the multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock method, the electron correlation and the Breit interaction are taken into account systematically. The analysis of the isotope shifts for these two transitions along the isoelectronic sequence demonstrates the importance and competition between the mass shifts and the field shifts.

## Key claim

The paper provides systematic multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock calculations of relativistic mass-shift and field-shift parameters for the $2s-2p$ resonance doublet along the lithium isoelectronic sequence from $Z=4$ to $Z=90$, with the caveat that quantum electrodynamical corrections omitted in the present work become larger than the relativistic contribution for the high-$Z$ region, and that the frequency-dependent Breit interaction and QED corrections affect the field shift factors by about 1% for Li-like Nd and 3% for Li-like Th.

## Method

The authors employed the multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDF) method using the GRASP2K package, with active space expansions including single and double excitations up to principal quantum number $n=8$ for low-$Z$ ions and smaller active sets for high-$Z$ ions. The relativistic normal mass shift and specific mass shift operators were evaluated from expectation values of the relativistic recoil Hamiltonian, while field shift factors were computed from the change in electronic probability density at the nucleus using a two-parameter Fermi nuclear charge distribution model. Breit interaction was included in subsequent relativistic configuration interaction calculations.

## Key numbers

- For Be$^{+}$ $2s-2p_{1/2}$ transition: total mass shift $\Delta \tilde{K}^{RMS} = -1562$ GHz u and field shift $F = -16.98$ MHz/fm$^2$ (MCDF, $n=8$)
- For Nd$^{57+}$ $2s-2p_{1/2}$ transition: total mass shift $\Delta \tilde{K}^{RMS} = -8.341 \times 10^5$ GHz u and field shift $F = -7.885 \times 10^6$ MHz/fm$^2$ (MCDF+Breit)
- For Th$^{87+}$ $2s-2p_{1/2}$ transition: total mass shift $\Delta \tilde{K}^{RMS} = -7.832 \times 10^5$ GHz u and field shift $F = -1.518 \times 10^8$ MHz/fm$^2$ (MCDF+Breit)
- For Nd$^{57+}$ $2s-2p_{3/2}$ transition: total mass shift $\Delta \tilde{K}^{RMS} = -9.632 \times 10^5$ GHz u and field shift $F = -8.157 \times 10^6$ MHz/fm$^2$ (MCDF+Breit)
- The total isotope shifts approach zero around $Z=34$ (Se$^{31+}$) due to cancellation between mass shift and field shift
- The second term of the relativistic recoil operator contributes 10% in Fe$^{23+}$, 47% in Nd$^{57+}$, and 90% in Th$^{87+}$ to the total line mass shift for the $2s-2p_{1/2}$ transition

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework needed to interpret dielectronic recombination measurements of nuclear charge radii, following the demonstration by Brandau et al. (2008) that such measurements can infer changes in nuclear mean-square charge radii for lithium-like neodymium. The calculations provide the electronic isotope shift parameters required to apply this method to other elements and isotopes, complementing earlier work by Godefroid et al. (2001) on low-$Z$ lithium-like ions and by Kozhedub et al. (2010) on relativistic mass shifts. The results enable extraction of nuclear charge distribution information from future dielectronic recombination experiments on highly-charged lithium-like ions across the periodic table.
