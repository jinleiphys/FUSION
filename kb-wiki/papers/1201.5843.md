# Particle Spectra in Statistical Models with Energy and Momentum Conservation

> Single particle momentum spectra are calculated within three micro-canonical statistical ensembles, namely, with conserved system energy, system momentum, as well as system energy and momentum. Deviations from the exponential spectrum of the grand canonical ensemble are quantified and discussed. For mean particle multiplicity and temperature, typical for p+p interactions at the LHC energies, the effect of the conservation laws extends to transverse momenta as low as about 3 GeV/c. The results may help to interpret spectra measured in nuclear collisions at high energies, in particular, their system size dependence.

## Key claim

For mean particle multiplicity and temperature typical of p+p interactions at LHC energies, the suppression of single-particle momentum spectra due to energy and momentum conservation becomes significant (a factor of about 2) at transverse momenta as low as about 3 GeV/c, which is only about 12.5% of the threshold momentum for the ensemble with both energy and momentum conserved.

## Method

The authors calculated single-particle momentum spectra for a non-interacting gas of massless Boltzmann particles (without conserved charges) within three micro-canonical statistical ensembles: one with conserved system energy only (E,V), one with conserved system momentum only (T,P,V), and one with both energy and momentum conserved (E,P,V). Analytical formulas for the partition functions and spectra were derived using integral representations of delta functions and hypergeometric functions, and the results were compared to the exponential spectrum of the grand canonical ensemble (T,V).

## Key numbers

- For T = 160 MeV and mean multiplicity ⟨N⟩ = 100 (typical for p+p at LHC), the suppression factor (ratio of GCE spectrum to micro-canonical spectrum) is about 2 at p = 3 GeV/c.
- The threshold momentum for the (E,P=0,V) ensemble is p = E/2; at p = 3 GeV/c this is about 12.5% of the threshold momentum.
- The energy in the (E,V) and (E,P,V) ensembles was set to E = 3T⟨N⟩ = 48 GeV for the calculations with ⟨N⟩ = 100.
- The GCE relation ⟨N⟩ = VT³/π² was used to determine the volume V, which was then used in all ensembles.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of conservation law effects in statistical models, which had focused on material conservation laws (e.g., strangeness by Rafelski (1980), baryon number, and charm) and their suppression of particle yields in small systems (the canonical ensemble suppression). The results are relevant for interpreting differences between transverse momentum spectra from p+p interactions and central Pb+Pb collisions at high energies, particularly regarding system size dependence and the onset of deconfinement signals studied by Gazdzicki (2010). The paper enables quantitative assessment of when thermodynamic approximations break down for small systems.
