# Modified empirical formulas and machine learning for $α$-decay systematics

> Latest experimental and evaluated $α$-decay half-lives between 82$\leq$Z$\leq$118 have been used to modify two empirical formulas: (i) Horoi scaling law [J. Phys. G \textbf{30}, 945 (2004)], and Sobiczewski formula [Acta Phys. Pol. B \textbf{36}, 3095 (2005)] by adding asymmetry dependent terms ($I$ and $I^2$) and refitting of the coefficients. The results of these modified formulas are found with significant improvement while compared with other 21 formulas, and, therefore, are used to predict $α$-decay half-lives with more precision in the unknown superheavy region. The formula of spontaneous fission (SF) half-life proposed by Bao \textit{et al.} [J. Phys. G \textbf{42}, 085101 (2015)] is further modified by using ground-state shell-plus-pairing correction taken from FRDM-2012 and using latest experimental and evaluated spontaneous fission half-lives between 82$\leq$Z$\leq$118. Using these modified formulas, contest between $α$-decay and SF is probed for the nuclei within the range 112$\leq$Z$\leq$118 and consequently probable half-lives and decay modes are estimated. Potential decay chains of $^{286-302}$Og and $^{287-303}$119 (168$\leq$N$\leq$184: island of stability) are analyzed which are found in excellent agreement with available experimental data. In addition, four different machine learning models: XGBoost, Random Forest (RF), Decision Trees (DTs), and Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) neural network are used to train a predictor for $α$-decay and SF half-lives prediction. The prediction of decay modes using XGBoost and MLP are found in excellent agreement with available experimental decay modes along with our predictions obtained by above mentioned modified formulas.

## Key claim

The authors modify the Horoi scaling law and Sobiczewski formula by adding asymmetry-dependent terms (I and I²) and refitting coefficients to the latest experimental α-decay half-lives for nuclei with 82≤Z≤118, claiming significant improvement over 21 other formulas, but the improvement is conditional on the specific set of coefficients used (Set-1 with 24 coefficients performs better than Set-2 with 9 coefficients).

## Method

The authors refitted the Horoi scaling law (2004) and Sobiczewski formula (2005) by adding isospin asymmetry terms I and I², using experimental and evaluated α-decay half-lives for 366 nuclei with 82≤Z≤118 from the NNDC database. They also modified the Bao spontaneous fission formula (2015) by replacing shell correction energies from FRDM-1995 with shell-plus-pairing corrections from FRDM-2012, fitting to 49 nuclei with known SF half-lives. Additionally, four machine learning models (XGBoost, Random Forest, Decision Trees, and Multilayer Perceptron) were trained on the same datasets using 5-fold cross-validation for regression prediction of half-lives.

## Key numbers

- Modified Horoi formula (MHF-2020) Set-1 achieved RMSE = 0.8355 and mean deviation δ̄ = 0.5752 for α-decay half-lives across 366 nuclei (82≤Z≤118)
- Modified Sobiczewski formula (MSF-2020) achieved RMSE = 0.9833 and mean deviation δ̄ = 0.6416 for the same dataset
- The original Horoi formula (2004) gave RMSE = 2.5237 and mean deviation δ̄ = 2.3267, showing the modification improved performance by a factor of ~3 in RMSE
- Modified Bao formula (MBF-2020) for spontaneous fission achieved RMSE = 2.1103 and mean deviation δ̄ = 1.7389 for 49 nuclei, compared to the original Bao formula (2015) with RMSE = 2.8379 and δ̄ = 2.3107
- The modified formulas were used to predict decay chains for ²⁸⁶⁻³⁰²Og and ²⁸⁷⁻³⁰³119 (with neutron numbers 168≤N≤184, the predicted island of stability)

## Context

This work extends the empirical formula approach of Horoi (2004) and Sobiczewski (2005) by incorporating asymmetry-dependent terms, following the successful strategy used by Akrawy (2017, 2019) for modifying other decay formulas. The modified Bao formula updates the spontaneous fission formula of Bao (2015) using the newer FRDM-2012 mass model instead of FRDM-1995. The machine learning component applies methods previously used for nuclear mass predictions (Niu 2018) and binding energies (Tuhin 2020) to the problem of decay half-lives. The work enables more precise predictions of α-decay half-lives and decay modes for superheavy nuclei approaching the island of stability near N=184, which is relevant for ongoing synthesis experiments at Dubna and RIKEN.
