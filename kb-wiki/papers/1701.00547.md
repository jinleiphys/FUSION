# Comprehensive analysis of large $α$ yields observed in $^{6}$Li induced reactions

> Background: Large $α$ yields have been reported over the years in reactions with $^{6}$Li and $^{7}$Li projectiles. Previous theoretical analyses have shown that the elastic breakup (EBU) mechanism (i.e., projectile breakup leaving the target in its ground state) is able to account only for a small fraction of the total $α$ inclusive breakup cross sections, pointing toward the dominance of non-elastic breakup (NEB) mechanisms. Purpose: We aim to provide a systematic study of the $α$ inclusive cross sections observed in nuclear reactions induced by $^{6}$Li projectiles. In addition to estimating the total $α$ singles cross sections, it is our goal to evaluate angular and energy distributions of these $α$ particles and compare with experimental data, when available. Method: We compute separately the EBU and NEB components of the inclusive breakup cross sections. For the former, we use the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method, which treats this mechanism to all orders. For the NEB part, we employ the the model proposed in the eighties by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 432 (1982)], within the DWBA approximation. Results: Overall, the sum of the computed EBU and NEB cross sections is found to reproduce very well the measured singles cross sections. In all cases analyzed, we find that the inclusive breakup cross section is largely dominated by the NEB component. Conclusions: The presented method provides a global and systematic description of inclusive breakup reactions induced by $^{6}$Li projectiles. It provides also a natural explanation of the previously observed underestimation of the measured $α$ yields by CDCC calculations. The method used here can be extended to other weakly-bound projectiles, including halo nuclei.

# Comprehensive analysis of large α yields observed in \(^{6}\)Li induced reactions

## Key claim

The sum of elastic breakup (EBU) and non-elastic breakup (NEB) cross sections, computed separately using the CDCC method and the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model respectively, reproduces measured inclusive α singles cross sections across multiple targets and energies, with the NEB component dominating in all cases analyzed, contrary to earlier expectations that direct breakup would be the primary mechanism.

## Method

The authors computed the elastic breakup (EBU) component using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method, which treats breakup as an inelastic excitation to continuum states of the projectile to all orders. For the non-elastic breakup (NEB) component, they employed the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model within the DWBA approximation, using a two-cluster (\(\alpha\)+\(d\)) model of \(^{6}\)Li. The \(^{6}\)Li nucleus was treated with \(\alpha\) as the spectator and \(d\) as the participant in the IAV formalism, with the NEB cross section computed from the imaginary part of the \(x+A\) optical potential and the \(x\)-channel wave function satisfying an inhomogeneous equation.

## Key numbers

- \(^{6}\)Li separation energy into \(\alpha\)+\(d\): 1.474 MeV, compared to single nucleon separation energy of 5.39 MeV
- For \(^{208}\)Pb(\(^{6}\)Li,\(\alpha X\)) at energies between 29 and 39 MeV: the nominal Coulomb barrier for this system is around 29.5 MeV; the sum EBU+NEB reproduced data from Sig01 but overestimated at lowest energies
- For \(^{159}\)Tb(\(^{6}\)Li,\(\alpha X\)) at energies between 23 and 35 MeV: the summed EBU+NEB cross sections reproduced the shape and magnitude of data with slight overestimation at some energies
- For \(^{118}\)Sn(\(^{6}\)Li,\(\alpha X\)) at energies between 18 and 24 MeV: the summed EBU+NEB reproduced the shape and magnitude of data remarkably well
- \(^{7}\)Li breakup threshold: 2.468 MeV (for the \(^{7}\)Li decay into \(\alpha\)+triton channel mentioned in context of process (v))

## Context

This work extends the IAV model, originally proposed by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent (1982) for deuteron-induced reactions, to \(^{6}\)Li projectiles and provides a systematic study across multiple targets (heavy: \(^{208}\)Pb, \(^{209}\)Bi; medium: \(^{159}\)Tb; lighter: \(^{118}\)Sn). Previous CDCC calculations by Signorini (2003) and others had systematically underestimated inclusive α yields because they only accounted for the elastic breakup component. The method provides a natural explanation for this underestimation and can be extended to other weakly-bound projectiles including halo nuclei. The work also addresses the approach of Udagawa (1989) for extending the formalism to negative \(x+A\) energies (bound states) and the recent reexamination by Potel (2015).
