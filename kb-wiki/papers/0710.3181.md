# An improved calculation of the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed Fermi beta decay

> We report new shell-model calculations of the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction to superallowed nuclear beta decay. The most important improvement is the inclusion of core orbitals, which are demonstrated to have a significant impact on the mismatch in the radial wave functions of the parent and daughter states. We determine which core orbitals are important to include from an examination of measured spectroscopic factors in single-nucleon pick-up reactions. We also examine the new radiative-correction calculation by Marciano and Sirlin and, by a simple reorganization, show that it is possible to preserve the conventional separation into a nucleus-independent inner radiative term and a nucleus-dependent outer term. We tabulate new values for the three theoretical corrections for twenty superallowed transitions, including the thirteen well-studied cases. With these new correction terms the corrected Ft values for the thirteen cases are statistically consistent with one another and the anomalousness of the 46V result disappears. These new calculations lead to a lower average Ft value and a higher value of Vud. The sum of squares of the top-row elements of the CKM matrix now agrees exactly with unitarity.

## Key claim

The isospin-symmetry-breaking correction δ_C for superallowed Fermi beta decay is significantly improved by including core orbitals in shell-model calculations, guided by measured spectroscopic factors from single-nucleon pick-up reactions; this resolves the previously anomalous result for ⁴⁶V and leads to a higher value of V_ud that makes the CKM unitarity sum agree exactly with unitarity.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations of the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction δ_C for 20 superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ beta decays, extending previous work by including core orbitals (e.g., d₃/₂ and s₁/₂ for A=46) identified from experimental spectroscopic factors in neutron pick-up reactions such as (³He,α). The radial mismatch factor Ω_α^π was computed using Saxon-Woods potentials with four different treatments of surface terms (methods I through IV), and the spectroscopic amplitudes were obtained from shell-model calculations using multiple effective interactions (e.g., USD-A, USD-B, GXPF1, KB3). The radiative corrections were reorganized following Marciano and Sirlin (2006) to preserve the separation into a nucleus-independent inner term Δ_R^V and a nucleus-dependent outer term δ_R′.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁶V, the new δ_C2 calculation yields 0.58%, nearly a factor of two larger than the 2002 value of 0.33%.
- The f₇/₂ orbital contributes 0.45% to δ_C2 for ⁴⁶V (isospin-lesser) and -0.12% (isospin-greater); the d₃/₂ orbital contributes 0.39% and -0.25%; the s₁/₂ orbital contributes 0.39% and -0.28%.
- For A ≤ 38, changes in corrections are typically 0.03%; for heavier nuclei, changes can be as large as 0.2%.
- The corrected ℱt values for the thirteen well-studied cases are statistically consistent with one another, with normalized χ² = 0.8.
- The sum of squares of the top-row elements of the CKM matrix now agrees exactly with unitarity.

## Context

This work extends the shell-model calculations of Towner and Hardy (2002) and the survey of Hardy and Towner (2005), which had found a possible anomaly in ⁴⁶V after new Penning-trap Q_EC measurements by Savard et al. (2005) and Eronen et al. (2006). The inclusion of core orbitals, guided by experimental spectroscopic factors from Borlin (1967), resolves that anomaly. The new radiative correction treatment follows Marciano and Sirlin (2006). These results enable a more precise determination of V_ud and a stringent test of CKM unitarity, with implications for the electroweak standard model.
