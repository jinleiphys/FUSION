# Non-Parametric Equation of State Reveals Non-Conformal Behavior Beyond Neutron Star Densities

> We propose a non-parametric approach to construct the statistical equation of state (EOS) continuously from the nuclear crust to the asymptotic-freedom regime. Driven by the observationally required stiffening to support two-solar-mass neutron stars (NSs) with relatively small radii for low-mass NSs, this global thermodynamic constraint suggests a clear peak of squared sound speed ($c_s^2$) in massive NSs. To prevent overshooting perturbative QCD (pQCD) energy-density bounds, this early stiffening must be actively compensated by an extended density range of softening, with $c_s^2$ not approaching $1/3$ until $\sim\!30\,n_{\rm sat}$. Consistently, the trace anomaly $Δ\equiv 1/3 - p/ε$ becomes positive beyond NS densities and approaches the pQCD limit from above. This natural emergence of $Δ> 0$ organically aligns with some anticipated microphysics, likely arising from the pressure dilution in a quark-hadron mixed phase, non-conformal pQCD corrections to quark-gluon interactions, or the symmetry-breaking effects of finite strange quark mass. By measuring the degree of this non-monotonic behavior in the posterior, we find evidence for a hadron-quark phase transition in the cores of the most massive neutron stars. This indicates that the non-perturbative quark matter is intrinsically soft, fundamentally distinguishing it from the stiff scenarios associated with the quark-star picture.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a non-parametric statistical equation of state (EOS) connecting the nuclear crust to the perturbative QCD (pQCD) limit naturally produces a non-monotonic sound-speed profile with a peak followed by extended softening, and that this thermodynamic structure constitutes evidence for a hadron-quark phase transition in the cores of the most massive neutron stars, with the caveat that the conformal limit is only reached near 30 times nuclear saturation density, far above the densities probed by stable neutron star cores.

## Method

The authors construct a continuous EOS from the nuclear crust to the pQCD limit at a baryon chemical potential of 2.6 GeV using a three-segment Gaussian Process (GP) framework: a hadronic segment constrained by the BPS crust and chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) input, a pQCD segment anchored at the high-density boundary, and an intermediate action-optimized bridge segment. The bridge segment selects the most thermodynamically coherent path between the two endpoints by minimizing an action functional that penalizes deviations from a target thermodynamic trajectory, with the selected path then projected onto exact pQCD endpoint values via GP conditioning. The resulting EOS ensemble is evaluated against NICER mass-radius constraints for four pulsars, the GW170817 tidal deformability posterior, and the two-solar-mass pulsar requirement.

## Key numbers

- The squared sound speed peaks at \(c_{s,\rm peak}^2 = 0.68^{+0.14}_{-0.13}\) in the posterior
- The maximum neutron star mass is \(M_{\rm TOV} = 2.11^{+0.12}_{-0.09}\,M_\odot\) with a 95% upper limit of \(2.37\,M_\odot\)
- The squared sound speed returns toward \(1/3\) near approximately \(30\,n_{\rm sat}\) (where \(n_{\rm sat} \approx 0.16\;\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\))
- The pQCD anchor is imposed at \(\mu_{\rm pQCD} = 2.6\;\mathrm{GeV}\)
- The accumulated softening index for hadronic models from the CompOSE database reaches a maximum near \(\varsigma \simeq 1.01\times10^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends non-parametric EOS inference methods developed by Landry (2018) and Tang (2025) by introducing an action-optimized bridge that resolves the ambiguity of where to impose pQCD constraints, a problem debated by Somasundaram (2022), Fan (2023), and Brandes (2023). The results confirm the sound-speed peak structure predicted by quark-hadron crossover models from Baym (2019) and Kojo (2021), and the accumulated softening index provides evidence distinguishing this behavior from purely hadronic models with hyperons or Delta resonances. The framework enables future studies of finite-temperature properties in binary neutron-star merger simulations to identify the microphysical mechanism driving the transition to deconfined quark matter.
