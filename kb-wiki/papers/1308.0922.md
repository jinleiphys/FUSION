# Fermionic condensation in ultracold atoms, nuclear matter and neutron stars

> We investigate the Bose-Einstein condensation of fermionic pairs in three different superfluid systems: ultracold and dilute atomic gases, bulk neutron matter, and neutron stars. In the case of dilute gases made of fermionic atoms the average distance between atoms is much larger than the effective radius of the inter-atomic potential. Here the condensation of fermionic pairs is analyzed as a function of the s-wave scattering length, which can be tuned in experiments by using the technique of Feshbach resonances from a small and negative value (corresponding to the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) regime of Cooper Fermi pairs) to a small and positive value (corresponding to the regime of the Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) of molecular dimers), crossing the unitarity regime where the scattering length diverges. In the case of bulk neutron matter the s-wave scattering length of neutron-neutron potential is negative but fixed, and the condensate fraction of neutron-neutron pairs is studied as a function of the total neutron density. Our results clearly show a BCS-quasiunitary-BCS crossover by increasing the neutron density. Finally, in the case of neutron stars, where again the neutron-neutron scattering length is negative and fixed, we determine the condensate fraction as a function of the distance from the center of the neutron star, finding that the maximum condensate fraction appears in the crust of the neutron star.

# Fermionic condensation in ultracold atoms, nuclear matter and neutron stars

## Key claim

The paper shows that the condensate fraction of fermionic pairs can be calculated across three distinct superfluid systems using a unified mean-field approach, but the results for neutron stars rely on a local density approximation applied to bulk neutron matter data, and the author acknowledges that the hadronic matter in neutron stars is not made only of neutrons, so the plot "can be certainly improved."

## Method

The author applies Bogoliubov mean-field theory to uniform two-component Fermi superfluids, solving the gap equation and number equation self-consistently to extract the condensate density from the two-body density matrix via the formula n0 = (2/Ω) Σ_k u_k^2 v_k^2. For ultracold atoms, the interaction is regularized using the s-wave scattering length a, allowing a continuous BCS-BEC crossover. For bulk neutron matter, the neutron-neutron potential has fixed s-wave scattering length a = -18.5 fm and effective range r_e = 2.7 fm, and the pairing gap Δ(k) is approximated as momentum-independent; the condensate fraction is then obtained from a fitting formula for Δ/ε_F versus k_F based on numerical data from Matsuo. For neutron stars, the density profile n(r) from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation (using two different equations of state) is combined with the bulk neutron-matter condensate fraction via local density approximation.

## Key numbers

- For ultracold atoms: the condensate fraction n0/n is plotted as a function of 1/(k_F a) across the BCS-BEC crossover; the mean-field result agrees with fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo data (Astrakharchik et al., 2005) for N=66 fermions, with better agreement on the BEC side.
- For bulk neutron matter: the maximum condensate fraction is (n0/n)_max = 0.42 at neutron density n = 5.3 × 10^{-4} fm^{-3}, corresponding to Fermi wave number k_F = 0.25 fm^{-1}.
- For bulk neutron matter: the condensate fraction at n = 0.003 fm^{-3} is approximately 0.35, consistent with Path Integral Monte Carlo data of Wlazlowski and Magierski.
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: the maximum condensate fraction is approximately 0.4 at scaled distance r/R ≈ 0.96 from the center, appearing in the crust between r/R = 0.85 and r/R = 1.
- The neutron-neutron s-wave scattering length is a = -18.5 fm with effective range r_e = 2.7 fm.
- Nuclear saturation density is n_s = 0.16 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous mean-field calculations of the fermionic condensate fraction in the BCS-BEC crossover for ultracold atoms (Salasnich, Manini, and Parola, 2005) and in bulk neutron matter (Salasnich, 2011) to the astrophysical setting of neutron stars. It compares with fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo results by Astrakharchik, Boronat, Casulleras, and Giorgini (2005) for ultracold atoms and with Path Integral Monte Carlo data by Wlazlowski and Magierski for neutron matter. The neutron star density profiles are taken from Datta, Thampan, and Bhattacharya, who solved the TOV equation using the simple nuclear equation of state of Walecka and the more sophisticated model 1 of Prakash, Ainsworth, and Lattimer. The work enables future searches for observational signatures of neutron-neutron pair condensation in neutron stars and atomic nuclei.
