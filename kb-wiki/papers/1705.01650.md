# Chiral vortical effect in pionic superfluid vs spin alignment of baryons

> We consider chiral fluids, with (nearly) massless fermionic constituents, in the confining phase. Chiral vortical effect (CVE) is the flow of axial current along the axis of rotation of the fluid while the spin alignment is a non-vanishing correlation of polarizations of baryons with the axis of rotation. As the theoretical framework we use the model of pionic superfluidity induced by a non-vanishing isotopic chemical potential. We note that the average value of spin of virtual baryons reproduces the CVE. The role of defects, or vortices is crucial. The model does not apply directly to the quark-gluon plasma but might indicate existence of a mechanism to produce baryons with relatively large polarization in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a pionic superfluid model (which is not directly applicable to quark-gluon plasma), the chiral vortical effect (CVE), the flow of axial current along the axis of rotation, can be reproduced by the average spin of virtual baryons, with the role of vortices being crucial; the model "might indicate existence of a mechanism to produce baryons with relatively large polarization in heavy-ion collisions."

## Method

The authors use the effective field theory of pionic superfluidity induced by a non-vanishing isotopic chemical potential (specifically axial chemical potential μ_A with vector chemical potential μ_V = 0), at zero temperature. They evaluate the anomalous axial current generated by vortices in the superfluid, using the Goldstone-Wilczek current formalism, and interpret the radiative corrections to the superfluid current as arising from heavy particles (baryons) that act as an ultraviolet cutoff. The spatial component of the induced axial current is identified with the density of spin of baryons.

## Key numbers

- The axial current from a single vortex is given by j⁵_i = (μ n)/(2π) δ(x_{i+1}, x_{i+2}) for i = 1,2,3, where μ is the chemical potential and n is the integer winding number of the vortex.
- The winding number n is related to the angular velocity ω by n = (μ/π) ∫ d²x ω³, where ω³ is the vorticity component along the 3rd axis.
- The density of particles in the condensate is n_{V,A} = 2 f_π² μ_{V,A}, where f_π is the pion decay constant.
- The baryonic axial current from vortices is (j_B⁵)_α = (N_c)/(36π² f_π²) ε_{αβγδ} (∂^β π⁰)(∂^γ ∂^δ π⁰), where N_c is the number of colors.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the chiral vortical effect (CVE) from the deconfined quark-gluon plasma regime to the confining phase, using the pionic superfluidity model developed by Son and Stephanov (2002). It builds on prior work by Gorsky et al. (2016) on hyperon polarization estimates via vacuum expectation values, and by Son, Zhitnitsky, Metlitski, and Kirilin on vortex-related baryonic currents. The paper enables a qualitative mechanism connecting plasma rotation to baryon polarization in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors caution that the model is not directly applicable to the quark-gluon plasma and that the heavy particles in the pionic superfluid are virtual rather than real.
