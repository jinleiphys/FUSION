# Study of relativistic bound state wave functions in quasielastic (e,e'p) reactions

> The unpolarized response functions of the quasielastic $^{16}O(e,e^\prime p)^{15}N$ reaction are calculated for three different types of relativistic bound state wave functions. The wave functions are obtained from relativistic Hartree, relativistic Hartree-Fock and density dependent relativistic Hartree calculations that reproduce the experimental charge radius of $^{16}$O. The sensitivity of the unpolarized response functions to the single particle structure of the different models is investigated in the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation. Redistributions of the momentum dependence in the longitudinal and transverse response function can be related to the binding energy of the single particle states. The interference responses $R_{LT}$ and $R_{TT}$ reveal a strong sensitivity to the small component of the relativistic bound state wave function.

## Key claim

The unpolarized response functions of the quasielastic $^{16}O(e,e^\prime p)^{15}N$ reaction, calculated in the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), show a sensitivity of up to 20% in the interference responses $R_{LT}$ and $R_{TT}$ to the specific relativistic bound state wave function used, but only when the full relativistic wave function is employed; this model-dependent information is lost in the nonrelativistic limit.

## Method

The unpolarized response functions $R_L$, $R_T$, $R_{LT}$, and $R_{TT}$ for the $^{16}O(e,e^\prime p)^{15}N$ reaction were calculated in the RPWIA, using bound state wave functions from three different relativistic nuclear structure models: relativistic Hartree (RH), relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF), and a density dependent relativistic Hartree (RDDH) approach. The calculations were performed completely in momentum space with perpendicular kinematics, a four-momentum transfer $|\mathbf{q}| = 500$ MeV/c and $\omega = 131.56$ MeV (the quasielastic peak value), using the CC1 current operator with dipole form factors.

## Key numbers

*   The longitudinal response $R_L$ maximum is reduced by approximately 10% when using RHF or RDDH wave functions compared to the RH wave functions.
*   The integrated longitudinal strength $I_L$ (multiplied by 100) is 2.81 for RDDH and 2.83 for both RH and RHF, indicating a redistribution rather than a true reduction of strength.
*   The integrated transverse strength $I_T$ (multiplied by 100) is 3.30 for RH, 3.31 for RHF, and 3.12 for RDDH, showing a significant reduction for the RDDH model.
*   The maxima of the interference responses $R_{LT}$ and $R_{TT}$ show a variation of around 20% between the different models.
*   The maximum of the momentum density $n_{p1/2}(p_m)$ is 83.3 $[(GeV/c)^{-3}]$ for RH, 74.8 $[(GeV/c)^{-3}]$ for RHF, and 73.0 $[(GeV/c)^{-3}]$ for RDDH wave functions.
*   The lower component of the RDDH bound state wave function is reduced compared to the other models, with an enhancement of approximately 60% over the nonrelativistic limit for all models.

## Context

This work extends studies of relativistic effects in $(e,e^\prime p)$ reactions, such as those by Caballero et al. (1998) which used only the Horowitz and Serot bound state wave function, by comparing results from three different relativistic nuclear structure models (RH, RHF, RDDH). The findings enable future experiments to use the interference responses $R_{LT}$ and $R_{TT}$ as a direct test of the lower component of relativistic bound state wave functions and to discriminate between different relativistic many-body approximation schemes.
