# Gauge Fixing Identity in the Background Field Method of QCD in Pure Gauge

> In this paper we derive a gauge fixing identity by varying the covariant gauge fixing term in $Z[A,J,η, {\bar η}]$ in the background field method of QCD in pure gauge. Using this gauge fixing identity we establish a relation between $Z[J,η,{\bar η}]$ in QCD and $Z[A,J,η, {\bar η}]$ in background field method of QCD in pure gauge. We show the validity of this gauge fixing identity in general non-covariant and general Coulomb gauge fixings respectively. This gauge fixing identity is used to prove factorization theorem in QCD at high energy colliders and in non-equilibrium QCD at high energy heavy-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The paper derives a gauge fixing identity by varying the covariant gauge fixing term in the generating functional of the background field method of QCD, specifically when the background field is restricted to a pure gauge configuration. This identity holds only to first order in the infinitesimal gauge transformation parameter beta, with terms of order O(beta^2) neglected.

## Method

The author starts from the generating functional Z[A,J,eta,bar eta] in the background field method of QCD with a covariant gauge fixing term that depends on the background field A. By shifting the integration variable Q -> Q - A and performing an infinitesimal gauge transformation on the fields, the author compares the transformed and untransformed generating functionals to derive an identity relating the gauge-fixed expressions. The derivation is then repeated for general non-covariant gauge fixing and general Coulomb gauge fixing, showing the identity holds in all three cases. Finally, the gauge fixing identity is used to establish a relation between the standard QCD generating functional Z[J,eta,bar eta] and the background field method generating functional Z[A,J,eta,bar eta] in pure gauge.

## Key numbers

- The pure gauge condition is given by T^a A_mu^a(x) = (1/ig)(partial_mu U) U^{-1} with U = exp(ig T^a beta^a(x)), where beta^a(x) is the gauge transformation parameter.
- The infinitesimal gauge transformation parameter satisfies beta << 1 (equation b1), and terms of order O(beta^2) are systematically neglected throughout the derivation.
- The background field in the pure gauge case for infinitesimal transformations is A_mu^a(x) = partial_mu beta^a(x) (equation am).

## Context

This work extends the background field method originally formulated by 't Hooft and later extended by Abbott, which provides a formalism for constructing gauge invariant off-shell Green's functions in QCD. The derived gauge fixing identity and the resulting relation between generating functionals are used by the author in separate works to prove factorization of soft and collinear divergences at high energy colliders and to prove factorization of fragmentation functions in non-equilibrium QCD applicable at RHIC and LHC. The paper notes that unlike QED (Tucci), finding an exact relation between the generating functionals in QCD is difficult because the gauge fixing terms differ between standard QCD and the background field method.
