# Pairing correlation in nuclear matter from Skyrme force

> The properties of pairing correlation in nuclear matter are investigated by using various versions of Skyrme forces. Truncation of states involving pairing correlation, necessary due to zero range nature of the Skyrme force, is discussed in detail. A plateau appears in pairing gap versus cutoff for each force. We propose to choose the cutoff parameter in the middle of the plateau so that the parameterization is independent of nuclides.

## Key claim

The pairing gap in nuclear matter calculated with Skyrme forces depends strongly on the cutoff parameter required due to the zero-range nature of the force, but a plateau appears in the pairing gap versus cutoff for each force; the authors propose choosing the cutoff in the middle of this plateau to make the parameterization independent of nuclides, though the plateau position varies between force parameter sets.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in infinite nuclear matter with isobaric symmetry, using five parameter sets of Skyrme forces (SIII, SkM*, SGII, SkP, and SkSC4). They imposed a constraint on the density to study the pairing gap as a function of Fermi momentum, and systematically varied the cutoff energy (defining the momentum integration limits) to examine its effect on the pairing gap at the Fermi surface. The pair-scattering matrix element was derived from the Skyrme force parameters, and the BCS-type trial wavefunction was used with a Routhian including a Lagrange multiplier and a stabilizing quadratic term.

## Key numbers

* For all Skyrme forces studied, the pairing gap at the Fermi surface reaches a maximum at a Fermi momentum of approximately 0.8 fm^-1, which corresponds to about one fifth of the saturation density (k_F ~ 1.33 fm^-1).
* At saturation density, the Gogny D1 force yields a pairing gap of about 0.6 MeV, while all Skyrme forces give only the normal state solution (zero pairing gap).
* When the cutoff energy is set to 10 MeV (corresponding to a 2-hbar-omega configuration space), the pairing gap at k_F = 0.8 fm^-1 depends strongly on the cutoff; at larger cutoff values, a plateau appears for each force, extending up to about 150 MeV for some parameter sets.
* For the SkP, SGII, and SkM* forces, the peak pairing gap at k_F = 0.8 fm^-1 increases by approximately a factor of three when using the proposed cutoff (chosen at the middle of the plateau) compared to the 10 MeV cutoff, while the peak position shifts to a lower Fermi momentum of about 0.6 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing correlations in nuclear matter using Skyrme forces, following the SkP force proposed by Dobaczewski et al. (1984) for describing pairing properties, and contrasts with finite-range Gogny force calculations by Decharge and Gogny (1980). The paper addresses the previously unstudied effect of the cutoff parameter on the pairing gap, which is necessary due to the zero-range nature of the Skyrme force; prior work by Jiang and Kuo (1988) used a weak coupling approach with constant pairing gap near the Fermi surface. The proposed cutoff prescription enables consistent application of Skyrme forces in HFB calculations for finite nuclei, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei where continuum states are important, and is relevant to the Generator Coordinate Method as applied by Tajima, Flocard, and Bonche (1992).
