# Evolving Charge Correlations in a Hybrid Model with both Hydrodynamics and Hadronic Boltzmann Descriptions

> Correlations related to local charge conservation provide insight into the creation and evolution of up, down and strange charges in the quark-gluon plasma. Here, the evolution of charge correlations is overlaid onto a hydrodynamic calculation for the regions where temperature exceed 155 MeV, then transferred and carried through a microscopic model of the hadronic stage. Thus, for the first time, charge correlations are evolved consistently with a full state-of-the-art description of a heavy-ion collision. The charge correlations are projected onto charge balance functions, which characterize such correlations in the final state, and are presented as a function of relative rapidity and relative azimuthal angle for Au/Au collisions. The role of the hadronic stage is investigated. Calculation of the contribution to charge-separation observables related to the chiral magnetic effect are also presented. Calculations are compared to data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider) data when possible.

## Key claim
For the first time, charge correlations are evolved consistently through both a hydrodynamic stage (above 155 MeV) and a full hadronic Boltzmann cascade, enabling calculation of charge balance functions that can be compared directly to STAR data. The authors note that the hadronic rescattering is not expected to dramatically alter results, but might not be negligible, particularly for resonances like the rho meson whose decay daughters rescatter and alter balance function structure.

## Method
Charge correlations were overlaid onto a hydrodynamic calculation for regions where temperature exceeds 155 MeV, then transferred and carried through the hadronic Boltzmann cascade simulator B3D. The diffusion of balancing charges was modeled with Monte Carlo methods involving tagged correlated pairs, and hadrons were emitted from the hydrodynamic stage using a modified Cooper-Frye formula. Hundreds of thousands of cascade events were generated to calculate charge balance functions as a function of relative rapidity and relative azimuthal angle for Au/Au collisions.

## Key numbers
- Temperature threshold for switching from hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade: 155 MeV
- The QGP stage is estimated to last approximately 5 fm/c or more until hadronization
- Less than one percent of particles in high-energy collisions are affected by the reflection approximation used for space-like hyper-surface elements
- The correlation length in a weakly interacting QGP is effectively zero, while in a hadron gas it extends over the size of a hadron (approximately 1 fm)
- Lattice gauge theory shows a smooth transition between hadronic gas and QGP expressions for susceptibility in the temperature range 150 to 225 MeV

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic-only treatment of charge correlations by Pratt, Kim and Plumberg (2018), which evolved charge correlations through the hydrodynamic stage but emitted hadrons directly into vacuum without further hadronic evolution. The present study adds the hadronic cascade stage using B3D, addressing the fact that the hadronic stage cannot be well described with hydrodynamics because various species begin to lose thermal contact. The results are compared to STAR Collaboration data from RHIC and to earlier parametric models by Pratt, McCormack and Ratti (2015) that fit charge balance functions by assuming initial QGP formation followed by a second surge of charge production during hadronization.
