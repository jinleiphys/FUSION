# Novel transition dynamics of topological solitons

> Continuous phase transitions can be classified into ones characterized by local-order parameters and others that need additional topological constraints. The critical dynamics near the former transitions have been extensively studied, but the latter is less understood. We fill this gap in knowledge by studying the transition dynamics to a parity-breaking topological ground state called the chiral soliton lattice in quantum chromodynamics at finite temperature, baryon chemical potential, and external magnetic field. We find a slowing down of the soliton's translational motion as the critical magnetic field approaches while the local dissipation rate remains finite. Therefore, the characteristic time it takes to converge to the stationary state associated with a finite topological number strongly depends on the initial configuration: whether it forms a solitonic structure or not.

## Key claim
The paper claims that near the continuous phase transition to the chiral soliton lattice (CSL) in QCD, the translational motion of the soliton slows down critically as the magnetic field approaches its critical value, while the local relaxation rate to the solitonic state remains finite. This timescale separation means the time to reach the stationary state depends strongly on whether the initial configuration has the symmetry that avoids translational motion.

## Method
The authors constructed a dissipative hydrodynamic effective theory for two-flavor QCD at finite temperature, baryon chemical potential, and external magnetic field, using the neutral pion field and axial isospin charge density as degrees of freedom. They derived coupled nonlinear equations of motion from a Poisson bracket formalism with dissipation terms, then numerically solved the resulting partial differential equation for the pion field under two distinct initial-boundary conditions: one where a soliton is created at the boundary and must translate to its equilibrium position, and another where the initial configuration has odd symmetry about the eventual soliton center, eliminating translational motion.

## Key numbers
- The critical magnetic field separating the QCD vacuum and CSL is given by \(B_{\rm CSL}=16\pi m_{\pi}f_{\pi}^2/\mu_{\rm B}\).
- Near \(B_{\rm CSL}\) from above, the CSL period diverges logarithmically: \(l(B) \simeq -\log (\Delta B/B)\) with \(\Delta B \equiv B-B_{\rm CSL}\).
- The baryon number per unit area scales as \((N_{\rm B})_{\rm tot} \sim -1/\log(\Delta B/B)\) near the critical field.
- The local relaxation time is \(t\sim m_\pi^{-1}\) (order of inverse pion mass), while the translational completion time becomes \(t\gg m_\pi^{-1}\) near the transition.
- Numerical simulations used parameters \((\alpha,\,\beta,\,\gamma) = (2.2,\, 0.4,\,0.2)\) and system sizes \(L=8,9,10\) in units of \(m_\pi^{-1}\).
- The characteristic rate \(\tau^{-1}\) for case (I) (with translation) approaches zero as \(B/B_{\rm CSL}\rightarrow 1\), while for case (II) (no translation) it remains finite.

## Context
This work extends the static theory of the chiral soliton lattice in QCD developed by Brauner (2016), Son (2007), and Eto (2012) into the dynamical regime, providing the first study of relaxation dynamics to a topological soliton ground state. It contrasts with standard second-order phase transitions (reviewed by Hohenberg and Halperin, 1977) where both collective and local modes exhibit critical slowing down; here only the translational mode slows while local relaxation stays gapped. The results enable predictions for transient CSL formation in noncentral heavy-ion collisions and magnetar cores, and suggest analogous behavior in chiral magnets studied by Kishine (2015) and others.
