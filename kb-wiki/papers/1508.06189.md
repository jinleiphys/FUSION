# Antiproton-proton interaction and related hadron physics

> Antinucleon-nucleon interaction has been established in chiral effective field theory. The strong threshold enhancement observed in the reactions $J/ψ\toγ\overline pp$ and $e^+e^-\to\overline pp$ are interpreted by the strong $\overline pp$ interaction. Concerning the channel $J/ψ\toγ\overline pp$, the topic on the $\overline pp$ bound state is also discussed.

## Key claim
The strong threshold enhancement observed in \(J/\psi\to\gamma\bar pp\) and \(e^+e^-\to\bar pp\) can be interpreted entirely by the \(\bar pp\) final-state interaction (FSI) without requiring an intrinsic energy dependence in the bare production amplitude, provided the chiral EFT potential is refitted to simultaneously describe both the scattering data and the enhancement peak.

## Method
The \(\overline NN\) potential was constructed in chiral effective field theory (EFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), separating elastic pion-exchange contributions (related to \(NN\) via \(G\)-parity) from annihilation effects parameterized by contact terms with four low-energy constants (LECs) per partial wave. The LECs were fitted to the energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) of Zhou (2012) for \(\bar pp\) scattering below 925 MeV laboratory momentum. For \(J/\psi\to\gamma\bar pp\), the isospin-1 \(^1S_0\) LECs were refitted in a combined analysis of the PWA and the BES (2012) invariant-mass spectrum, while the isospin-0 \(^1S_0\) LECs were kept fixed. The FSI was incorporated via a distorted-wave formalism where the bare production amplitude was taken as a constant, so the entire energy dependence of the observable comes from the \(\bar pp\) \(T\)-matrix.

## Key numbers
- The \(\overline NN\) potential reproduces PWA phase shifts and inelasticities up to \(T_{\text{lab}} = 200\) MeV (NLO) and \(T_{\text{lab}} = 250\) MeV (NNLO), with very small cutoff dependence.
- The refitted isospin-1 \(^1S_0\) potential yields a \(\bar pp\) bound state in that partial wave, though the binding energy and width cannot be pinned down from above-threshold data alone.
- For \(e^+e^-\to\bar pp\), the cross section and effective form factor are reproduced up to an excess energy of 100 MeV using only one overall normalization constant.
- The differential cross section for \(e^+e^-\to\bar pp\) at an excess energy of 36.5 MeV is nicely reproduced.

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT approach to \(\overline NN\) interaction, previously applied mainly to \(NN\) scattering (reviewed in Epelbaum, Hammer, Meißner (2009)), and builds on the meson-exchange Jülich model (Hippchen et al., 1991) and the PWA of Zhou (2012). It enables a unified description of \(\bar pp\) scattering, antiprotonic hydrogen observables, and the threshold enhancements in \(J/\psi\to\gamma\bar pp\) and \(e^+e^-\to\bar pp\) without invoking exotic states. The results for \(J/\psi\to\gamma\bar pp\) are extended to other \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi'\) decays in Kang (2015). The role of \(\bar pp\) FSI in \(e^+e^-\to\) multipions in the 1750–1950 MeV region is also noted.
