# Bayesian Estimation of Thermonuclear Reaction Rates

> The problem of estimating non-resonant astrophysical S-factors and thermonuclear reaction rates, based on measured nuclear cross sections, is of major interest for nuclear energy generation, neutrino physics, and element synthesis. Many different methods have been applied in the past to this problem, almost all of them based on traditional statistics. Bayesian methods, on the other hand, are now in widespread use in the physical sciences. In astronomy, for example, Bayesian statistics is applied to the observation of extra-solar planets, gravitational waves, and type Ia supernovae. However, nuclear physics, in particular, has been slow to adopt Bayesian methods. We present astrophysical S-factors and reaction rates based on Bayesian statistics. We develop a framework that incorporates robust parameter estimation, systematic effects, and non-Gaussian uncertainties in a consistent manner. The method is applied to the d(p,$γ$)$^3$He, $^3$He($^3$He,2p)$^4$He, and $^3$He($α$,$γ$)$^7$Be reactions, important for deuterium burning, solar neutrinos, and big bang nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

Bayesian statistics provides a more consistent framework for estimating non-resonant astrophysical S-factors and thermonuclear reaction rates than traditional χ² minimization, particularly for handling systematic uncertainties, outliers, and non-Gaussian statistical uncertainties, but the method assumes that the theoretical model reliably describes the energy dependence of the S-factor while the absolute scale is determined by fitting to experimental data.

## Method

The authors implemented Bayesian models using the program JAGS (Just Another Gibbs Sampler) with Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling, incorporating lognormal priors for systematic normalization uncertainties, a mixture-model approach for robust regression to handle outliers, and lognormal likelihoods for data points where the ratio of mean value to standard deviation is ≥3. The method was applied to S-factor data for the d(p,γ)³He, ³He(³He,2p)⁴He, and ³He(α,γ)⁷Be reactions, using either polynomial representations or theoretical nuclear model results (e.g., variational wave functions from Marcucci (2005) for d(p,γ)³He) as the underlying S-factor shape.

## Key numbers

- d(p,γ)³He zero-energy S-factor: (2.156⁺⁰·⁰⁸²₋₀·₀₇₇) × 10⁻⁷ MeVb (3.7% uncertainty), compared to (2.13 ± 0.08) × 10⁻⁷ MeVb from Coc (2015) and (2.14⁺⁰·¹⁷₋₀·₁₆) × 10⁻⁷ MeVb from Adelberger (2011)
- d(p,γ)³He scale factor for Marcucci (2005) theoretical S-factor: 1.000⁺⁰·⁰³⁸₋₀·₀₃₆
- Data set normalizations for d(p,γ)³He: Ma 97 = 0.895⁺⁰·⁰⁵⁸₋₀·₀₄₈, Schmid 97 = 0.981⁺⁰·⁰⁴¹₋₀·₀₄₁, Casella 02 = 1.025⁺⁰·⁰³⁸₋₀·₀₃₇, Bystritsky 08 = 1.023⁺⁰·⁰⁷²₋₀·₀₆₈
- Outlier probability for Schmid 97 data set: 72%
- Systematic uncertainty factors used for d(p,γ)³He data sets: 1.09 (Ma 97), 1.09 (Schmid 97), 1.045 (Casella 02), 1.08 (Bystritsky 08)
- For ³He(³He,2p)⁴He, systematic uncertainties reported: 4.5% (Krauss 87), 3.7% (Junker 98), 5.7% (Bonetti 99), 3.8% (Kudomi 04)

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo-based reaction rate methodology of Longland (2010) and the STARLIB library (Sallaska 2013) by providing a Bayesian framework specifically for non-resonant reactions, which were previously treated with simpler approximations. The method contrasts with the χ² minimization approaches used by Coc (2015) for the d(p,γ)³He reaction and by Adelberger (2011) for solar model reactions, and it enables the consistent incorporation of these Bayesian rates into the STARLIB library alongside existing Monte Carlo-based resonant rates for use in nucleosynthesis sensitivity studies and stellar evolution calculations.
