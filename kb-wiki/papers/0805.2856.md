# Energy functional for the three-level Lipkin model

> We compute the energy functional of a three-level Lipkin model via a Legrendre transform and compare exact numerical results with analytical solutions obtained from the random phase approximation (RPA). Except for the region of the phase transition, the RPA solutions perform very well. We also study the case of three non-degenerate levels and again find that the RPA solution agrees well with the exact numerical result. For this case, the analytical results give us insight into the form of the energy functional in the presence of symmetry-breaking one-body potentials.

## Key claim

The random phase approximation (RPA) yields an energy functional for the three-level Lipkin model that agrees well with exact numerical results in the limits of strong and weak coupling, but fails at the boundary between these regimes because the RPA energy is not a convex function of the single-particle energies there, making the Legendre transform problematic near the phase transition.

## Method

The authors computed the exact ground-state energy of the three-level Lipkin model numerically by diagonalizing a matrix of modest dimensions. They then constructed the energy functional via a Legendre transform of the ground-state energy as a functional of external one-body potentials. Analytical expressions for the ground-state energy were obtained from Hartree-Fock (HF) and RPA calculations following the work of Hagino and Bertsch (2000), and these were Legendre-transformed to yield analytical energy functionals in both the spherical (weak-coupling) and deformed (strong-coupling) phases.

## Key numbers

- For a system of N=20 fermions, the RPA solution exhibits a cusp at the phase boundary ε = v ≡ V(N-1), where v is the effective two-body coupling strength.
- In the spherical phase (ε > v), the energy functional takes the form F(n) = -|v| √[n(n+2)], exhibiting a square-root singularity for small occupation numbers.
- In the deformed phase (ε < v), the occupation number is n(ε) = (2N-1)/4 - (N-1)/2 (ε/v) - ε/√[2(v²-ε²)], and the functional is analytical in the strong coupling limit v ≫ ε.
- For the non-degenerate case with N=10 fermions, the RPA energy functional agrees well with exact results except at the boundary between weak and strong coupling, which is a one-dimensional line in the region of small occupation numbers n₁,₂ < 1.

## Context

This work extends the approach of constructing energy functionals via Legendre transforms from the pairing Hamiltonian studied by Papenbrock (2007) to the three-level Lipkin model, a solvable model relevant for nuclei. The results provide insight into the form of occupation-number-based energy functionals for two-body interactions with continuous symmetries, and are intended to inform the development of a global nuclear energy functional based on shell-model occupations, following the success of the occupation number-based mass formula by Duflo and Zuker (1995).
