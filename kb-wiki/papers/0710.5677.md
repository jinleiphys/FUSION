# Chiral constituent quark model study of the process $γp \to ηp$

> A constituent quark model is developed for the reaction, allowing us to investigate all available data for differential cross sections as well as single polarization asymmetries (beam and target) by including {\it all} of the PDG, one to four star, nucleon resonances ($S_{11}$, $P_{11}$, $P_{13}$, $D_{13}$, $D_{15}$, $F_{15}$, $F_{17}$, $G_{17}$, $G_{19}$, $H_{19}$, $I_{1,11}$, and $K_{1,13}$). Issues related to the missing resonances are also briefly discussed by examining possible contributions from several new resonances ($S_{11}$, $P_{11}$, $P_{13}$, $D_{13}$, $ D_{15}$, and $H_{1,11}$).

## Key claim

The authors develop a chiral constituent quark model for $\gamma p \to \eta p$ that includes all PDG-listed nucleon resonances up to the $n=6$ harmonic oscillator shell, plus six proposed new resonances, and achieves a reduced $\chi^2$ of 1.8 against 1822 data points; however, the model's predictive power is limited for polarized target asymmetries due to the very small number of available data points for that observable.

## Method

The authors start from the low-energy QCD Lagrangian in the chiral quark model and compute transition amplitudes for meson photoproduction via seagull, s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel contributions. They extend previous work by deriving explicit amplitudes for harmonic oscillator shells $n=3$ through $n=6$ and include t-channel $\rho$- and $\omega$-exchanges, then fit the $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ symmetry breaking coefficients $C_{N^*}$ along with masses and widths of six new resonances to 1588 differential cross-sections, 184 polarized beam asymmetries, and 50 polarized target asymmetries using the CERN-MINUIT code.

## Key numbers

- Reduced $\chi^2$ of 1.8 for model A (all 27 resonances) and 2.1 for model B (9 dominant resonances only)
- Data base contains 1822 data points: 1588 differential cross-sections, 184 polarized beam asymmetries, and 50 polarized target asymmetries
- Extracted configuration mixing angles: $\theta_S = -35^\circ$ and $\theta_D = 15^\circ$
- Mass and width of the new $S_{11}$ resonance: $M = 1730$ MeV and $\Gamma = 100$ MeV
- Energy range covered: $E_{\gamma}^{lab} \le 3$ GeV ($W \le 2.6$ GeV)
- Nine dominant resonances: $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $S_{11}(1730)$, $S_{11}(2090)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, $P_{13}(1900)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $D_{13}(1700)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach previously applied to meson photo- and electro-production by the same group (He et al., 2006) and meson-nucleon scattering, now including higher harmonic oscillator shells up to $n=6$ and all PDG-listed nucleon resonances. The results enable future use of this elementary operator in a dynamical coupled-channel formalism (Bruno et al.) that incorporates $\pi N$, $\pi \Delta$, $\eta N$, $\sigma N$, and $\rho N$ intermediate states, and the extracted $S_{11}(1730)$ resonance parameters are compatible with findings from other groups (Saghai et al., 2006; Giannini et al.).
