# Dibaryons: Molecular versus Compact Hexaquarks

> Hexaquarks constitute a natural extension of complex quark systems like also tetra- and pentaquarks do. To this end the current status of $d^*(2380)$ in both experiment and theory is shortly reviewed. Recent high-precision measurements in the nucleon-nucleon channel and analyses thereof have established $d^*(2380)$ as an indisputable resonance in the long-sought dibaryon channel. Important features of this $I(J^P) = 0(3^+)$ state are its narrow width and its deep binding relative to the $Δ(1232)Δ(1232)$ threshold. Its decay branchings favor theoretical calculations predicting a compact hexaquark nature of this state. We review the current status of experimental and theoretical studies on $d^*(2380)$ as well as new physics aspects it may bring in the future. In addition, we review the situation at the $Δ(1232) N$ and $N^*(1440)N$ thresholds, where evidence for a number of resonances of presumably molecular nature have been found -- similar to the situation in charmed and beauty sectors. Finally we briefly discuss the situation of dibaryon searches in the flavored quark sectors.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current status of the dibaryon resonance $d^*(2380)$ and argues that its decay branchings favor theoretical calculations predicting a compact hexaquark nature, while evidence for resonances at the $\Delta(1232)N$ and $N^*(1440)N$ thresholds suggests a molecular character for those states, similar to the situation in charmed and beauty sectors.

## Method

The authors review experimental results from exclusive and kinematically complete measurements of single- and two-pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions performed at CELSIUS/WASA and WASA-at-COSY, including the double-pionic fusion reactions $pp \to d\pi^+\pi^0$, $pn \to d\pi^0\pi^0$, and $pn \to d\pi^+\pi^-$. They also review partial-wave analyses of elastic $np$ scattering data that incorporate new analyzing power measurements from WASA at COSY, and compare experimental branching ratios to theoretical calculations from quark models and hadronic interaction models.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $d^*(2380)$: $m = 2370$ MeV from Breit-Wigner fit to the $pn \to d\pi^0\pi^0$ total cross section
- Total width of $d^*(2380)$: $\Gamma = 70$ MeV from the same fit
- Pole position from partial-wave analysis of $np$ scattering: $(2380 \pm 10) - i(40 \pm 5)$ MeV in the coupled $^3D_3 - ^3G_3$ partial waves
- Binding energy relative to the nominal $\Delta\Delta$ threshold (2464 MeV): approximately 90 MeV
- Branching ratio for $d^*(2380) \to d\pi^0\pi^0$: $14 \pm 1\%$
- Branching ratio for $d^*(2380) \to d\pi^+\pi^-$: $23 \pm 2\%$
- Branching ratio for $d^*(2380) \to np$: $12 \pm 3\%$
- Upper limit for $d^*(2380) \to (NN\pi)_{I=0}$ decay: $<5\%$ at $90\%$ confidence level
- Electromagnetic branching for $d^*(2380) \to d\gamma$: approximately $10^{-4}$ (cross section about 20 nbarn in $\gamma d \to d\pi^0\pi^0$)
- Mass of the $^1D_2$ resonance near $\Delta N$ threshold: approximately 2148 MeV with width $\Gamma \approx 120$ MeV

## Context

This review extends the long history of dibaryon searches dating back to the SU(6) predictions of Dyson and Xuong (1964) and the early bag-model calculations of the Nijmegen theory group and Mulders and Thomas. It builds on the experimental discovery of $d^*(2380)$ by the CELSIUS/WASA and WASA-at-COSY collaborations around 2009-2011, and the subsequent confirmation via partial-wave analysis of $np$ scattering by the SAID group (AD14 solution). The work enables future studies of the $d^*(2380)$ transition form factor through electro-production, which could experimentally determine the size of the state and resolve the compact hexaquark versus molecular structure question.
