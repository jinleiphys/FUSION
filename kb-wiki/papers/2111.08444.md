# Quantum mechanics of radiofrequency-driven coherent beam oscillations in storage rings

> Precision searches for the electric dipole moment of protons in storage ring experiments call for beam-position monitoring in the picometer range. We present the relevant fully quantum mechanical derivation of radiofrequency-driven collective oscillations of a beam in a storage ring.

## Key claim

The functional form of the coherent beam oscillation amplitude does not change during the transition from the classical to the quantum regime, meaning that the Heisenberg uncertainty principle does not preclude sub-picometer accuracy in determining the coherent beam oscillation amplitude; the actual limitation lies in the sensitivity of the beam position monitors.

## Method

The authors derive a fully quantum mechanical master equation for radiofrequency-driven coherent betatron oscillations of a stored beam, treating the Wien filter as a time-dependent perturbation to the harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian of the vertical betatron motion. They solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation stroboscopically, summing transition amplitudes from the initial state to states created by the annihilation and creation operators over multiple turns. The result is compared to the classical treatment of the same system, and the effects of residual gas scattering and intrabeam scattering are evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The zero-point betatron oscillation amplitude for a single particle is approximately 41 nm (calculated as Q = 82/sqrt(gamma * nu_y) nm).
- The smallest measured oscillation amplitude in the COSY experiment was (1.08 +/- 0.52) micrometers.
- For the all-electric frozen-spin ring (500 m circumference, omega_rev = 2.26e6/s, nu_y = 0.45), the coherent vertical displacement from Earth's gravity is approximately 13 pm.
- For the hybrid electric ring with magnetic focusing (800 m circumference, omega_rev = 1.4e6/s, nu_y = 2.3), the coherent gravitational vertical displacement is approximately 1.3 pm, giving an average beam splitting of 2.6 pm.
- The beam lifetime due to scattering and absorption is approximately 1500 s.

## Context

This work extends the classical treatment of RF Wien filter-driven beam oscillations from the COSY experiment (Slim 2021) into the deep quantum regime relevant for proposed proton EDM searches (srEDM, CPEDM). The analysis addresses concerns about the Heisenberg uncertainty principle raised by the required picometer-level beam position monitoring accuracy, and also quantifies the gravitational beam displacement previously discussed by Silenko and Teryaev (2006), Orlov and collaborators, and Obukhov, Silenko, and Teryaev. The results enable the design of beam position monitors for the proposed frozen-spin storage ring EDM experiments and demonstrate that the quantum limit does not fundamentally prevent achieving the required sensitivity.
