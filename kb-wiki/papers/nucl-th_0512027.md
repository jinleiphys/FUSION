# Deconfinement, naturalness and the nuclear-quark equation of state

> Baryon-loops vacuum contribution in renormalized models like the Linear sigma model and the Walecka model give rise to large unnatural interaction coefficients, indicating that the quantum vacuum is not adequately described by long-range degrees of freedom. We extend such models into nonrenormalizable class by introducing an ultraviolet cutoff into the model definition and treat the Dirac-sea explicitly. In this way, one can avoid unnaturalness. We calculate the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature in a modified $σ-ω$ model. We show that the strong attraction originating from the Dirac-sea softens the nuclear matter equation of state and generates a vacuum with dynamically broken symmetry. In this model the vector-meson is important for the description of normal nuclear matter, but it obstructs the chiral phase transition. We investigate the chiral phase transition in this model by incorporating deconfinement at high density. A first-order quark deconfinement is simulated by changing the active degrees of freedom from nucleons to quarks at high density. We show that the chiral phase transition is first-order when quark decouples from the vector-meson and coincides with the deconfinement critical density.

# Deconfinement, naturalness and the nuclear-quark equation of state

## Key claim

The Dirac-sea contribution, when treated with an explicit ultraviolet cutoff in a non-renormalizable modified sigma-omega model, can avoid the unnaturalness problem that plagues renormalized models, but the vector-meson coupling obstructs chiral restoration unless quark degrees of freedom are introduced at high density; the chiral phase transition becomes first-order and coincides with the deconfinement critical density only when quarks decouple from the vector-meson.

## Method

The author extends the linear sigma model with nucleons and omega mesons into a non-renormalizable class by introducing a sharp ultraviolet cutoff Lambda, treating the Dirac-sea explicitly rather than absorbing vacuum loops via renormalization. The equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature is calculated in mean-field approximation, with parameters calibrated to reproduce empirical saturation properties (binding energy per nucleon -15.75 MeV at Fermi momentum 1.3 fm^-1). Deconfinement is simulated by switching active degrees of freedom from nucleons to quarks at a critical density rho_C, with quarks coupled only to scalar and pion fields (not directly to the vector-meson).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.148 fm^-3, with binding energy per nucleon E/A = -15.75 MeV
- Nucleon mass in vacuum: M_N(sigma_0) = 939 MeV; vector-meson mass in vacuum: m_v(sigma_0) = 783 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Cutoff values for nucleon-only parameter sets range from Lambda = 256 MeV (set A1) to 483.2 MeV (set B3)
- Compression modulus K at saturation: 370 MeV (set B3) to 490 MeV (set A1), compared to empirical K = 200-300 MeV and the no-sea approximation value of about 650 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: M_N^star/M_N(sigma_0) = 0.81-0.84, with values from 757.2 MeV (set A1) to 784.5 MeV (set B3)
- Sigma meson mass in vacuum: 805-817 MeV across all parameter sets
- Quark mass in vacuum: m_q(sigma_0) = 300 MeV, giving g_sq = 3.23
- Critical chemical potential for liquid-gas phase transition: mu_c approximately 923.25 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by Boguta (1983) to achieve nuclear matter saturation in the linear sigma model, which suffered from unacceptably high compression modulus (about 650 MeV) and absence of chiral phase transition. It also addresses the unnaturalness problem identified by Furnstahl, Serot, and collaborators (1990s), where one-baryon-loop vacuum contributions in renormalized models like the Walecka model produce large unnatural coefficients under Georgi and Manohar's naive dimensional analysis. The approach is similar to the non-renormalizable Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in using a cutoff to discard short-range physics. The inclusion of quark degrees of freedom at high density follows earlier indications of first-order deconfinement transitions and enables a unified description of the liquid-gas phase transition, chiral restoration, and deconfinement, though the compression modulus values (370-490 MeV) remain above the empirical range of 200-300 MeV.
