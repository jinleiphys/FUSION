# Isobaric Analog State in ^{96} Ag

> Preiviously, in a single j-shell calculation (j=g_{9/2}), we obtained the excitation energy of the T=2 J=0+ isobaric analog state in ^{96} Ag to be a bit below 1 MeV relative to the J=8+ T=1 ground state . We here use binding energy data and Coulomb energy estimates to obtain this same excitation energy and to see if the 2 approaches are consistent.

## Key claim
The excitation energy of the T=2 J=0+ isobaric analog state in ^96Ag is estimated at 1.142 MeV using binding energy data and Coulomb energy estimates, which is higher than the single-j-shell calculation result of about 0.9 MeV but lower than large-space calculations near 1.9 MeV; the authors note that the two approaches are not fully consistent.

## Method
The authors used binding energy differences between ^96Ag and ^96Pd combined with a Coulomb energy estimate from the Anderson et al. formula (V_C = E_1 Z/A^(1/3) + E_2, with average values E_1 = 1.441 MeV and E_2 = -1.06 MeV) to compute the excitation energy of the isobaric analog state. They compared this result to single-j-shell calculations (using INTd and CCGI interactions) and large-space calculations (using jj44b and JUN45 interactions) for ^96Ag and several other nuclei where experimental data exist for validation.

## Key numbers
- Excitation energy of the T=2 J=0+ isobaric analog state in ^96Ag: 1.142 MeV from the binding-energy/Coulomb method
- Single-j-shell calculations for ^96Ag give 0.900 MeV (INTd interaction) and 0.842 MeV (CCGI interaction)
- Large-space calculation with jj44b interaction for ^96Ag gives 1.917 MeV; with JUN45 interaction gives 1.640 MeV
- For ^94Rh: binding-energy/Coulomb method gives 2.657 MeV; single-j-shell gives 1.990 MeV (INTd) and 2.048 MeV (CCGI); jj44b gives 3.266 MeV
- For ^44Sc: binding-energy/Coulomb method gives 2.873 MeV; experiment gives 2.779 MeV
- For ^46Sc: binding-energy/Coulomb method gives 5.024 MeV; experiment gives 5.022 MeV
- For ^52Mn: binding-energy/Coulomb method gives 2.905 MeV; experiment gives 2.926 MeV
- For ^60Cu: binding-energy/Coulomb method gives 2.520 MeV; experiment gives 2.536 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous single-j-shell calculation (Zamick and Escuderos, 2012) for the g_9/2 region by incorporating binding energy data and Coulomb energy estimates to check consistency. The results show discrepancies between the simple Coulomb-energy method, single-j-shell calculations, and large-space shell model calculations (using interactions like jj44b from Brown and Lisetskiy, and JUN45 from Honma et al.), indicating that the excitation energy of the isobaric analog state in ^96Ag remains uncertain. The authors hope this will motivate experimental measurements of the J=0+ isobaric analog states in ^94Rh and ^96Ag, which have been surprisingly neglected.
