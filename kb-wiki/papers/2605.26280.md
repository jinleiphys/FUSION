# CNN-Based Online Trigger for QGP Event Selection

> Modern high-rate experiments require rare physics signatures to be identified in real time from continuous streams of reconstructed events under stringent data-throughput and storage constraints. We present a convolutional-neural-network-based trigger concept for selecting events associated with quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation. Events are encoded as compact multidimensional histograms of reconstructed particle content, including particle species, momentum magnitude, and angular information. The method is first evaluated within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) framework, where microscopic QGP-related labels are available. As an independent validation, the same event representation and network architecture are applied to Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) simulations, providing a distinct description of the collision dynamics. Cross-checks between PHSD and UrQMD are used to assess the stability of the learned response against generator-dependent effects and to quantify model-transfer robustness. For realistic deployment, a lightweight C++ inference package, ANN4FLES, is employed at the physics-analysis stage after tracking and topology reconstruction. For Au+Au collisions at 30 AGeV, the classification accuracy decreases from 95.1% on generator-level PHSD events to 83.7% after full reconstruction, while retaining practical separation power for online event selection. SHAP-based interpretability analysis is used to identify the dominant particle-species contributions to the network decision.

# CNN-Based Online Trigger for QGP Event Selection

## Key claim

The authors present a convolutional-neural-network-based trigger concept for selecting events associated with quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation, claiming that the classifier can learn stable QGP-sensitive structures that remain useful under model transfer between different event generators and under detector-level reconstruction constraints. The hedge is that the classification accuracy decreases from 95.1% on generator-level PHSD events to 83.7% after full reconstruction for Au+Au collisions at 30 AGeV, and that the method's robustness depends on cross-model validation between PHSD and UrQMD to ensure the network learns generic QGP signatures rather than generator-specific correlations.

## Method

Events from the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) framework and the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model for Au+Au collisions at 30 AGeV were encoded as compact four-dimensional histograms (28 particle species channels by 20 bins each for absolute momentum, polar angle, and azimuthal angle). A multi-output 3D convolutional neural network with two Conv3D layers (32 and 64 filters, kernel size 3x3x3), max-pooling, and a fully connected layer with 64 neurons was trained to simultaneously predict QGP classification, QGP energy fraction, number of QGP particles, and impact parameter. For realistic deployment, the lightweight C++ inference package ANN4FLES was employed at the physics-analysis stage after tracking and topology reconstruction, and SHAP-based interpretability analysis identified dominant particle-species contributions to the network decision.

## Key numbers

- Classification accuracy of 95.1% on generator-level PHSD events for Au+Au collisions at 30 AGeV
- Classification accuracy of 83.7% after full reconstruction for Au+Au collisions at 30 AGeV
- Critical energy density threshold for partonic phase onset in PHSD: approximately 0.4 GeV/fm^3
- Input event tensor dimension: 28 x 20 x 20 x 20 (28 particle species, 20 bins each for momentum, polar angle, azimuthal angle)
- Two convolutional layers: first with 32 filters, second with 64 filters, both with kernel size 3x3x3
- Fully connected layer with 64 neurons and LeakyReLU activation
- Four output neurons: QGP classification, QGP ratio, number of QGP particles, impact parameter

## Context

This work extends prior machine-learning approaches for heavy-ion collision analysis, including CNN-based studies showing that momentum-space particle distributions encode information about the QCD equation of state (Pang 2016, Du 2020, Kvasiuk 2020, Mallick 2022) and PointNet-based event classification for the CBM experiment (Omana Kuttan 2021). The method is designed for deployment in the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR, where the First-Level Event Selector (FLES) performs online reconstruction and physics selection, and builds on earlier classification-only implementations (Belousov 2023) by introducing a multi-output architecture that simultaneously predicts QGP-related observables and impact parameter. The cross-model validation between PHSD and UrQMD addresses the central challenge of generator dependence, enabling future applications for fast online event selection at CBM, RHIC, and the Electron-Ion Collider.
