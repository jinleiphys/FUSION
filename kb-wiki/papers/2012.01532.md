# Gluon distributions and their applications to Ioffe-time distributions

> We investigate unpolarized and polarized gluon distributions and their applications to the Ioffe-time distributions, which are related to lattice QCD calculations of parton distribution functions. Guided by the counting rules based on the perturbative QCD at large momentum fraction $x$ and the color coherence of gluon couplings at small $x$, we parametrize gluon distributions in the helicity basis. By fitting the unpolarized gluon distribution, the inferred polarized gluon distribution from our parametrization agrees with the one from global analysis. A simultaneous fit to both unpolarized and polarized gluon distributions is also performed to explore the model uncertainty. The agreement with the global analysis supports the $(1-x)$ power suppression of the helicity-antialigned distribution relative to the helicity-aligned distribution. The corresponding Ioffe-time distributions and their asymptotic expansions are calculated from the gluon distributions. Our results of the Ioffe-time distributions can provide guidance to the extrapolation of lattice QCD data to the region lacking precise gluonic matrix elements. Therefore, they can help regulate the ill-posed inverse problem associated with extracting the gluon distributions from discrete data from first-principle calculations, which are available in a limited range of the nucleon momentum and the spatial separation between the gluonic currents. Given various limitations in obtaining lattice QCD data at large Ioffe time, phenomenological approaches can provide important complementary information to extract the gluon distributions in the entire $x$ region. The possibility of investigating higher-twist effects and other systematic uncertainties in the contemporary first-principle calculations of parton distributions from phenomenologically well-determined Ioffe-time distributions in the large Ioffe-time region is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that a helicity-basis parametrization of gluon distributions, guided by perturbative QCD counting rules at large momentum fraction \(x\) and color coherence at small \(x\), can reproduce the unpolarized gluon distribution from global analysis and yield a polarized gluon distribution consistent with global fits, provided the helicity-antialigned distribution is suppressed by \((1-x)^2\) relative to the helicity-aligned distribution. The resulting Ioffe-time distributions can guide lattice QCD extrapolations, but the model uncertainty from the assumed \((1-x)\) power suppression leads to a factor-of-two difference in the gluon helicity \(\Delta G\) between two ansatzes.

## Method

The authors parametrize the helicity-aligned \(g^+(x)\) and helicity-antialigned \(g^-(x)\) gluon distributions using functional forms with \(x^\alpha\) small-\(x\) behavior and \((1-x)^{4+\beta}\) (for \(g^+\)) and \((1-x)^{6+\beta}\) (for \(g^-\)) large-\(x\) behavior, plus a polynomial factor \((1+\gamma\sqrt{x}+\delta x)\). They fit the unpolarized gluon distribution from the NNPDF3.1 global analysis at \(\mu=2\) GeV using 100 replicas, then compute the polarized gluon distribution \(\Delta g(x)=g^+(x)-g^-(x)\) and the corresponding Ioffe-time distributions via cosine and sine transforms. Three ansatzes are compared: fixed \((1-x)^2\) suppression (ansatz-1), fixed \((1-x)^1\) suppression (ansatz-2), and independent parameters for \(g^+\) and \(g^-\) fitted simultaneously to both unpolarized and polarized global data (ansatz-3).

## Key numbers

* Unpolarized gluon distribution fitted to NNPDF3.1 at \(\mu=2\) GeV yields small-\(x\) exponent \(\alpha = 0.036 \pm 0.058\) (ansatz-1) and \(\alpha = 0.034 \pm 0.060\) (ansatz-2).
* Gluon helicity \(\Delta G = \int_0^1 dx\,\Delta g(x)\) from ansatz-1 is \(0.451(7)\); from ansatz-2 is \(0.258(4)\); from ansatz-3 is \(0.23(41)\).
* The \((1-x)\) exponent \(\beta\) for \(g^+\) is \(0.95 \pm 1.28\) (ansatz-1), \(1.11 \pm 1.32\) (ansatz-2), and \(0.54 \pm 1.30\) (ansatz-3); for \(g^-\) in ansatz-3, \(\beta' = 0.91 \pm 2.63\).
* The polynomial parameters \(\gamma\) and \(\delta\) for ansatz-1 are \(-2.80 \pm 0.63\) and \(2.62 \pm 0.95\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Brodsky et al. (1994) that used perturbative QCD counting rules and Regge behavior to model parton distributions, and applies it specifically to gluon distributions in the helicity basis. The results are compared to the NNPDF3.1 global analysis (Ball et al., 2017) for unpolarized gluons and NNPDFpol1.1 (Nocera et al., 2014) for polarized gluons. The calculated Ioffe-time distributions provide a coordinate-space framework that can complement lattice QCD calculations of gluon parton distribution functions, particularly in the large Ioffe-time region where lattice data are limited, and can help discriminate between different parametrizations of the gluon helicity distribution.
