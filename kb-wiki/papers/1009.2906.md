# The importance of the mixed phase in hybrid stars built with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We investigate the structure of hybrid stars based on two different constructions: one is based on the Gibbs condition for phase coexistence and considers the existence of a mixed phase (MP), and the other is based on the Maxwell construction and no mixed phase is obtained. The hadron phase is described by the non-linear Walecka model (NLW) and the quark phase by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL). We conclude that the masses and radii obtained are model dependent but not significantly different for both constructions.

## Key claim
The gravitational masses and radii of hybrid stars obtained with the Gibbs condition (mixed phase) and with the Maxwell construction (no mixed phase) are not significantly different, but the central energy density always lies inside the mixed phase when it is present, whereas with the Maxwell construction the central energy density may lie in the hadron phase, making the star unstable if a phase transition occurs. The authors conclude that the Maxwell construction gives satisfactory results for macroscopic observables, but the choice between constructions affects whether a pure quark phase appears in the stellar core.

## Method
The hadron phase was described by the non-linear Walecka model (NLW) with two parameter sets (GM1 and GM3), and the quark phase by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL) in both SU(2) and SU(3) versions with two parameter sets each. Equations of state were constructed using either the Gibbs conditions (continuous neutron and electron chemical potentials, global charge neutrality in a mixed phase) or the Maxwell construction (only neutron chemical potential continuous, local charge neutrality in each phase). These equations of state were then used as input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars.

## Key numbers
- Maximum masses for hadron stars are larger than for hybrid stars (no specific numerical value given for the difference).
- For the GM3 parametrization with SU(3) NJL (HK or RKH) in hybrid stars without mixed phase, the central energy density lies within the hadron phase, meaning the star becomes unstable if a phase transition to quark matter occurs.
- For the GM1 parametrization with SU(3) NJL (HK or RKH) in hybrid stars without mixed phase, the central energy density lies in the quark phase.
- The redshift constraint from EXO0748-676 gives M/R = 0.15 solar masses per km (z = 0.35).
- The redshift constraint from 1E 1207.4-5209 gives M/R = 0.069 to 0.115 solar masses per km (z = 0.12 to 0.23).
- All calculated mass-radius curves are consistent with both observational constraints.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars that used the MIT bag model for the quark phase, such as those by Glendenning (2001) and Lugones (2003), by instead using the NJL model which incorporates chiral symmetry. The authors note that earlier calculations by Maruyama (2007) and Yasutake (2009) found that differences between Gibbs and Maxwell constructions were not significant, but those studies used the MIT bag model. The present work confirms that conclusion holds for the NJL model as well, but adds the important caveat that the central composition (whether a pure quark phase exists) depends on the construction method. The results suggest that stable neutron stars are either purely hadronic or contain a mixed phase in their core, and that colour superconductivity effects (CFL and 2SC phases) should be investigated in future work.
