# Reaction pp-> {pp}_s pi^0 in the GeV region and pi^0 p rescattering

> COSY data on the cross section of the reaction pp -> {pp}_s pi^0, where {pp}_s is the proton pair in the 1S0 state at small excitation energy E_{pp}=0-3 MeV, recently obtained for beam energies 0.5 - 2.0 GeV are analyzed within the one-pion exchange model. The model is based on the subprocess pi^0 p -> pi^0 p and final state pp-interaction. A broad maximum observed in the energy dependence of the cross section at 0.5 - 1.4 GeV in the forward direction is explained by this model as a dominant contribution of the isospin 3/2 in the pi^0 p-rescattering. The second maximum observed at 2 GeV is underpredicted within the model by one order of magnitude.

## Key claim

The one-pion exchange (OPE) model, based on the subprocess π⁰p → π⁰p and final-state pp interaction, reproduces the observed shape of the energy dependence of the pp → {pp}_sπ⁰ cross section at 0.5–1.4 GeV and demonstrates dominance of the Δ(1232)-isobar, but underpredicts the second maximum at 2 GeV by one order of magnitude and requires a scaling factor of 0.45 to match the absolute magnitude.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section of pp → {pp}_sπ⁰ using a triangle-diagram OPE mechanism where a virtual pion emitted by one proton rescatters elastically off the other proton (π⁰p → π⁰p), with the final pp pair in the ¹S₀ state. The amplitude includes direct and exchange terms, uses the πNN coupling constant f²_πNN/4π = 0.0796 with a monopole form factor, and incorporates the pp scattering wave function from the Reid soft-core potential plus Coulomb interaction. The elementary πN scattering amplitudes are taken from the SAID database, and the cross section is integrated over the final pp relative momentum up to k^max corresponding to E_pp = 3 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The calculated forward cross section (θ = 0°) multiplied by a factor of 0.45 agrees with the measured shape of the energy dependence at beam energies 0.5–1.4 GeV.
- When the isospin 3/2 amplitude (dominated by Δ(1232)) is excluded from the π⁰p scattering amplitude, the calculated cross section at 0.4–1.1 GeV diminishes by two orders of magnitude, showing strong contradiction with data.
- The second maximum observed at 1.97 GeV is underpredicted by the OPE model by one order of magnitude.
- The model predicts a smooth increase of 5–15% in the differential cross section at θ = 0°–15°.

## Context

This work analyzes recent COSY data (Kurbatov 2007, Dymov 2006) on pp → {pp}_sπ⁰ at 0.5–2.0 GeV, which showed strong disagreement with the microscopic Δ-isobar model of Niskanen (2006) that includes coupled NN–NΔ channels. The OPE approach extends earlier successful applications to pp → dπ⁺ (Yao 1969, Locher 1970) and to pd → {pp}_sn (Uzikov 2007). The results enable future measurements of the reaction pn → {pp}_sπ⁻ to distinguish between heavy Δ-isobar excitation and N* or Reggeon exchange mechanisms at higher energies.
