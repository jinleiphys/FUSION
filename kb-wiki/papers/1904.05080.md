# Delineating the properties of matter in cold, dense QCD

> The properties of dense QCD matter are delineated through the construction of equations of state which should be consistent with QCD calculations in the low and high density limits, nuclear laboratory experiments, and the neutron star observations. These constraints, together with the causality condition of the sound velocity, are used to develop the picture of hadron-quark continuity in which hadronic matter continuously transforms into quark matter (modulo small 1st order phase transitions). For hadronic matter (at baryon density nB > ~2n0 with n0 ~ 0.16 fm^(-3) being the nuclear saturation density) we use equations of state by Togashi et al. based on microscopic variational many-body calculations, and for quark matter (nB > ~5n0) we construct equations of state using a schematic quark model (with strangeness) whose interactions are motivated by the hadron phenomenology. The region between hadronic and quark matters (~2n0 < nB < ~5n0), which is most difficult to calculate, is treated by highly constrained interpolation between nuclear and quark matter equations of state. The resultant unified equation of state at zero temperature and beta-equilibrium, which we call Quark-Hadron-Crossover (QHC18 and QHC19), is consistent with the measured properties of neutron stars and in addition gives us microscopic insights into the properties of dense QCD matter. In particular to ~10n0 the gluons can remain as non-perturbative as in vacuum and the strangeness can be as abundant as up- and down-quarks at the core of two-solar mass neutron stars. Within our modeling the maximum mass is found less than ~2.35 times solar mass and the baryon density at the core ranges in ~5-8n0.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the properties of dense QCD matter in neutron stars are best described by a "hadron-quark continuity" picture, where hadronic matter continuously transforms into quark matter (with only small first-order phase transitions possible), rather than through sharp phase transitions. This conclusion is conditional on the assumption that the equation of state is of the "soft-stiff" type, meaning soft at low density (leading to neutron star radii of 10-13 km for 1.4 solar mass stars) and stiff at high density (enabling maximum masses above 2 solar masses).

## Method

The author constructs unified equations of state at zero temperature and beta-equilibrium using a three-window modeling approach: for baryon density nB below about 2n0 (where n0 ≈ 0.16 fm^(-3) is nuclear saturation density), the equations of state from Togashi et al. based on microscopic variational many-body calculations are used; for nB above about 5n0, a schematic quark model with strangeness motivated by hadron phenomenology is employed; and for the intermediate region (2n0 to 5n0), a highly constrained interpolation between the nuclear and quark matter equations of state is performed, using the pressure as a function of baryon chemical potential P(μB) as the interpolated variable. The resulting equations of state, called QHC18 and QHC19, are checked against constraints from neutron star observations (including the GW170817 merger event), nuclear laboratory experiments, and the causality condition that the speed of sound must be less than the speed of light.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 ≈ 0.16 fm^(-3)
- Hadronic matter regime: nB ≤ 2n0; quark matter regime: nB ≥ 5n0; intermediate regime: 2n0 to 5n0
- Maximum neutron star mass from modeling: less than about 2.35 solar masses
- Baryon density at the core of two-solar-mass neutron stars: about 5-8 n0
- Observed neutron star masses: PSR J1614-2230 at 1.928 ± 0.017 solar masses; PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses
- Neutron star radius estimate from X-ray analyses: R = 10-13 km
- Tidal deformability upper bound from GW170817: Λ1.4 ≤ 800 (90% confidence level), translating to R1.4 ≤ 13.6 km
- Lower bound on radius from ejecta conditions: R1.4 ≥ 11.0-11.5 km (Radice et al. 2017)
- Nuclear symmetry energy parameters: J = 31.6 ± 2.7 MeV, L = 58.9 ± 16 MeV (Lattimer 2012); J = 31.7 ± 3.2 MeV, L = 58.7 ± 28.1 MeV (Oertel 2016)
- Chirp mass from GW170817: 1.188^(+0.004)_(-0.002) solar masses, giving total mass 2.73-2.78 solar masses
- Maximum mass upper bound estimates from GW170817: about 2.16-2.17 solar masses (Margalit and Metzger 2017; Rezzolla et al. 2017; Ruiz et al. 2017); higher estimates of 2.28-2.32 solar masses from supramassive star scenarios (Yu et al. 2017)

## Context

This work extends the line of research on dense QCD matter equations of state that began with the APR (Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall 1998) nuclear matter calculations and the perturbative QCD calculations of Kurkela et al. (2009) and Fraga et al. (2001). It builds on the three-window modeling approach developed by Masuda et al. (2012) and Kojo et al. (2014-2015), and is informed by the hadron-quark continuity picture originally proposed by Schafer and Wilczek (1998) for the crossover from superfluid hadronic matter to color-flavor-locked quark matter. The paper uses the recent neutron star merger event GW170817 (LIGO-Virgo 2017) and its electromagnetic counterparts to constrain the equation of state, and provides the QHC18 and QHC19 tables available on the CompOSE repository. The work enables future studies of neutron star structure, cooling, and merger dynamics using equations of state that are consistent with both nuclear physics and QCD constraints.
