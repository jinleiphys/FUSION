# Neutron capture reactions relevant to s-process and p-process in the domain of the $N=50$ shell closure

> Radiative thermal neutron capture cross sections for nuclei participating in s-process and p-process nucleosynthesis in and around $N=50$ closed neutron shell have been calculated in statistical semi-microscopic Hauser-Feshbach approach for the energy range of astrophysical interest. A folded optical model potential is constructed utilizing the standard DDM3Y real nucleon-nucleon interaction. The folding of the interaction with target radial matter densities, obtained from the relativistic-mean-field approach, is done in coordinate space using the spherical approximation. The standard nuclear reaction code TALYS1.8 is used for cross-section calculation. The cross sections are compared with experimental results and reasonable agreements are found for almost all cases. Maxwellian-averaged cross sections (MACS) for the nuclei are presented at a single thermal energy of 30 keV relevant to s-process. We have also presented the MACS values over a range of energy from 5 to 100 keV for neutron magic nuclei with $(N=50)$.

## Key claim

The authors calculate radiative thermal neutron capture cross sections for nuclei near the N=50 shell closure using a semi-microscopic Hauser-Feshbach approach, claiming reasonable agreement with experimental data for almost all cases, though the agreement varies by nucleus and energy range (e.g., results for 87Rb agree well with Dudey et al. but underestimate Dovbenko et al. by a factor of about 2).

## Method

The authors construct a folded optical-model potential using the DDM3Y real nucleon-nucleon interaction folded with target radial matter densities obtained from relativistic-mean-field (RMF) theory using the FSU Gold parameterization, assuming spherical symmetry. They then use the statistical model code TALYS1.8 to calculate (n,gamma) cross sections in the Hauser-Feshbach approach, with nuclear level densities from Goriely's microscopic combinatorial method and E1 gamma-ray strength functions from Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations. Maxwellian-averaged cross sections (MACS) are computed by folding the energy-dependent cross sections with a Maxwell-Boltzmann neutron spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies and charge radii are calculated for 20 nuclei near N=50; for example, 88Sr has experimental binding energy 768.41 MeV and theoretical 766.13 MeV, with experimental charge radius 4.224 fm and theoretical 4.208 fm.
- MACS values are presented at a single thermal energy of 30 keV for all nuclei studied, relevant to s-process nucleosynthesis.
- MACS values are also presented over an energy range from 5 to 100 keV for neutron magic nuclei with N=50.
- For 86Kr, experimental measurements by Raman et al. suffer from 25% uncertainty near 30 keV and 30% uncertainty at 100 keV.
- For the p-nucleus 84Sr, Dillmann et al. reported a measurement at kT=25 keV with an overall 5.7% error.
- The energy window for neutron-induced reactions is given by E0 approximately 0.172 T9 (l+1/2) MeV, with width Delta approximately 0.194 T9 (l+1/2)^(1/2) MeV, where T9 is temperature in GK.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of thermal (n,gamma) cross sections near the N=82 shell closure (Dutta et al., 2015) to the N=50 region, where the weak s-process component creates elements between Fe and Sr-Y-Zr. The calculations are compared with experimental data from the National Nuclear Data Center and with theoretical MOST calculations (a Hauser-Feshbach code using global microscopic models). The results are relevant for s-process nucleosynthesis (including the strong branch point at 85Kr and weaker branch points) and p-process nucleosynthesis, where (n,gamma) reactions compete with (gamma,n) reactions and can change p-nuclei overproduction factors by orders of 10 or 100 as found by Rayet et al. The work also addresses the need for improved cross sections for unstable isotopes like 93Zr and 99Tc, which are important for nuclear transmutation of long-lived fission products.
