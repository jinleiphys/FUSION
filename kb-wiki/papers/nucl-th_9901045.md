# Analysis of correlation data of pi^+pi^- pair in p + Ta reaction using Coulomb wave function including momentum resolution and strong interaction effect

> We have proposed a new method for the Coulomb wave function correction which includes the momentum resolution for charged hadron pairs and applied it to the precise data on pi^+pi^- correlations obtained in p + Ta reaction at 70 GeV/c. It is found that interaction regions of this reaction (assuming Gaussian source function) are 9.8+-5.8 fm and 7.7+-4.8 fm for the thicknesses of the target 8 micro m and 1.4 micro m, respectively. We have also analyzed the data by the formula including strong interaction effect. The physical picture of the source size obtained in this way is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new method for Coulomb wave function correction that includes finite momentum resolution, and apply it to pi+pi- correlation data from p + Ta reactions at 70 GeV/c; the extracted source sizes depend on whether strong final-state interactions are included, with the strong-interaction-included values being systematically larger.

## Method

The authors extend their previous Coulomb wave function correction formalism (Biyajima 1995, 1996) to incorporate the finite momentum resolution of the experimental setup, using Gaussian random numbers to randomize the longitudinal and transverse components of the relative momentum q according to the resolution parameters sigma_L = 1.3 MeV/c and sigma_T = 0.6 or 0.4 MeV/c depending on target thickness. They then apply this method to pi+pi- correlation data from p + Ta reactions at 70 GeV/c for two target thicknesses (8 micrometer and 1.4 micrometer), assuming a Gaussian source function. Additionally, they include s-wave strong final-state interactions using the Bowler formulation (Bowler 1988) with pi-pi phase shifts parameterized from Suzuki (1987).

## Key numbers

- For the 8 micrometer target, the root-mean-square interaction radius (assuming Gaussian source) using only Coulomb correction is 9.8 +/- 5.8 fm.
- For the 1.4 micrometer target, the root-mean-square interaction radius using only Coulomb correction is 7.7 +/- 4.8 fm.
- For the 8 micrometer target, including strong final-state interactions gives a root-mean-square radius of 12.5 +/- 4.9 fm.
- For the 1.4 micrometer target, including strong final-state interactions gives a root-mean-square radius of 10.5 +/- 3.9 fm.
- The momentum resolution parameters used are sigma_L = 1.3 MeV/c, sigma_T = 0.6 MeV/c (8 micrometer target) and sigma_T = 0.4 MeV/c (1.4 micrometer target).
- The mean radius of the Ta nucleus is estimated as 6.8 fm from the formula 1.2 x A^(1/3) with A = 181.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Coulomb wave function correction method (Biyajima 1995, 1996) by incorporating the finite momentum resolution effect that was pointed out by Afanas'ev (1995) as necessary for analyzing the experimental data of Afanas'ev (1990, 1996). The analysis shows that the extracted source sizes are significantly larger than the Ta nuclear radius of 6.8 fm, which the authors attribute to about 60% of the unlike-sign pions emerging from short-lived resonances such as phi and Delta. The results enable future application of this theoretical formula to other data to estimate source size parameters and the fraction b of pions from short-lived resonances.
