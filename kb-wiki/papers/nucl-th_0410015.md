# Restoration of Heavy-Ion Potentials at Intermediate Energies

> The microscopic nucleus-nucleus optical potential is constructed basing on two patterns for real and imaginary parts, each calculated in the framework of microscopic models and multiplied by two normalizing factors, the free parameters, fitted to experimental data. The first supplementary model yields the real and imaginary templates for our potential, and itself reproduces the scattering amplitude of the microscopic Glauber-Sitenko theory. The other pattern, for real part only, is the standard double-folding model with the exchange term included. As a result, we obtain an acceptable agreement with elastic differential cross-sections.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that microscopic nucleus-nucleus optical potentials constructed from either the high-energy approximation (HEA) or the double-folding (DF) model, each multiplied by only two normalizing factors, can reproduce elastic differential cross-sections for heavy-ion scattering at intermediate energies, but the agreement is limited to small scattering angles where the HEA is valid (below about 5.5 degrees for the systems studied).

## Method
The authors constructed microscopic optical potentials using two patterns: the HEA potential (which provides both real and imaginary parts from the Glauber-Sitenko theory) and the standard double-folding model with exchange term (for the real part only). They multiplied each pattern by two free normalizing factors (one for the real part, one for the imaginary part) and fitted these factors to reproduce elastic differential cross-sections calculated in the HEA for phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials that had been previously fitted to experimental data. The calculations used point nucleon density distributions, nucleon-nucleon scattering parameters, and the CDM3Y6 effective NN force, with relativistic corrections applied to the velocity and center-of-mass momentum.

## Key numbers
- For the system 17O + 208Pb at 1435 MeV lab energy, the HEA is valid for scattering angles below about 5.5 degrees.
- The outer volume integral for the fitted Woods-Saxon potential for 17O + 208Pb is J_r^out = 50.3 MeV fm^3 and J_im^out = 47.3 MeV fm^3.
- For the U^B_opt potential (V^DF + iW^H) for 17O + 208Pb, the outer volume integrals are J_r^out = 18.2 MeV fm^3 and J_im^out = 12.9 MeV fm^3.
- For the U^C_opt potential (V^DF + iV^DF) for 17O + 208Pb, the outer volume integrals are J_r^out = 18.2 MeV fm^3 and J_im^out = 18.3 MeV fm^3.
- The fitted normalizing factors for U^B_opt for 17O + 208Pb are 0.5 for the real part (V^DF) and 1.3 for the imaginary part (W^H).
- The fitted normalizing factors for U^C_opt for 17O + 208Pb are 0.5 for the real part (V^DF) and 0.8 for the imaginary part (V^DF).

## Context
This work extends the method developed in the authors' preceding paper (Hanna et al., 2004) for restoring nucleus-nucleus potentials from the Glauber-Sitenko microscopic scattering theory, which had revealed an ambiguity problem where different potentials with the same tails gave acceptable fits. The present approach replaces the phenomenological Woods-Saxon form with microscopically calculated patterns (HEA and double-folding), reducing the number of free parameters from six or more to just two normalizing factors. This enables more reliable predictions of potential behavior in the sensitive surface region at intermediate energies, though the authors note that full agreement with experimental data would require solving the Schroedinger equation rather than using the HEA at larger angles.
