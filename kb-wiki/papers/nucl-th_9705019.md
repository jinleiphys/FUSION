# Hadron structure effect in finite nuclei

> The quark-meson coupling model, based on a mean-field description of non-overlapping nucleon bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons, is reviewed. In particular, I present the changes in the hadron masses of the (non-strange) vector mesons and the nucleon in a nucleus. I also give a new, simple scaling relation among the changes of the hadron masses.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which treats nucleons as composite quark bags rather than point-like particles, predicts that hadron masses in a nucleus decrease due to the scalar mean-field, with the reduction scaling approximately with the number of non-strange quarks in each hadron. The abstract's claim of a "new, simple scaling relation" is hedged by the paper's explicit statement that this relation depends on ignoring the weak dependence of the slope parameter \(a_j\) on the hadron type, and that the relation holds only up to about three times normal nuclear density.

## Method

The author reviews the QMC model, which uses the MIT bag model for nucleon and meson structure and a mean-field approximation for the \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) meson fields. For finite nuclei, the coupled non-linear differential equations from the effective Lagrangian are solved self-consistently, with the nucleon effective mass depending only on the scalar field and the meson masses also becoming field-dependent in the QMC-II variant. The coupling constants are fixed to reproduce the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy \(-15.7\) MeV at density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) and bulk symmetry energy 35 MeV), and the free hadron masses are fitted using the MIT bag model parameters.

## Key numbers

*   Effective nucleon mass in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density: 801 MeV (parameter set A), 781 MeV (set B), 775 MeV (set C), compared to 522 MeV in QHD.
*   Nuclear incompressibility \(K\): 325 MeV (set A), 382 MeV (set B), 433 MeV (set C), compared to 540 MeV in QHD.
*   Relative change of the nucleon bag radius at saturation density: \(-0.01\) (sets A, B) to \(-0.02\) (set C).
*   Relative change of the lowest quark eigenvalue at saturation density: \(-0.11\) (set A), \(-0.13\) (set B), \(-0.14\) (set C).
*   Relative change of the quark rms radius at saturation density: 0.02 for all three parameter sets.
*   Coefficient \(c_j\) for the linear density dependence of hadron mass at low density: \(c_N = 0.21\), \(c_v = 0.17\), \(c_\Lambda = 0.12\), \(c_\Sigma = 0.11\), \(c_\Xi = 0.05\).
*   Slope parameter \(a_j\) for the scalar-density ratio: ranges from \(8.6 \times 10^{-4}\) to \(9.5 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV\(^{-1}\), with a representative value \(a \simeq 9.0 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
*   Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{40}\)Ca: 6.54 MeV (QMC-II) versus experimental 8.45 MeV.
*   RMS charge radius for \(^{40}\)Ca: 3.48 fm (fitted) for both QMC-I and QMC-II, matching the experimental value 3.48 fm.
*   Difference between neutron and proton rms radii for \(^{208}\)Pb: 0.27 fm (QMC-II) versus experimental 0.16 \(\pm\) 0.05 fm.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model from infinite nuclear matter (Saito 1995, 1996) to finite nuclei, incorporating the structure effects of both nucleons and mesons (QMC-II). It contrasts with Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) (Serot and Walecka 1986), where nucleons are point-like and the \(\sigma\)-N coupling is constant, leading to a much higher incompressibility (540 MeV in QHD versus 325-433 MeV in QMC-II). The paper enables future experimental tests of the predicted scaling relation among hadron mass changes, particularly for vector mesons in nuclei, and provides a natural effective field theory for nuclei as assessed by naive dimensional analysis (Manohar and Georgi 1984).
