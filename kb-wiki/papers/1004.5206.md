# Fragmentation paths in dynamical models

> We undertake a quantitative comparison of multi-fragmentation reactions, as modeled by two different approaches: the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) and the momentum-dependent stochastic mean-field (SMF) model. Fragment observables and pre-equilibrium (nucleon and light cluster) emission are analyzed, in connection to the underlying compression-expansion dynamics in each model. Considering reactions between neutron-rich systems, observables related to the isotopic properties of emitted particles and fragments are also discussed, as a function of the parametrization employed for the isovector part of the nuclear interaction. We find that the reaction path, particularly the mechanism of fragmentation, is different in the two models and reflects on some properties of the reaction products, including their isospin content. This should be taken into account in the study of the density dependence of the symmetry energy from such collisions.

## Key claim

The fragmentation path differs significantly between the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) and the stochastic mean-field (SMF) models, with these differences reflecting on observable properties of reaction products including their isospin content; this must be accounted for when extracting the density dependence of the symmetry energy from heavy-ion collision data, rather than assuming the reaction mechanism is model-independent.

## Method

The authors performed a quantitative comparison of central collisions of 112Sn + 112Sn and 124Sn + 124Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon using two dynamical approaches: the AMD model, which employs Gaussian wave packets with full antisymmetrization and stochastic collisions including quantum branching, and the momentum-dependent SMF model, which evolves the one-body distribution function with a Boltzmann-Langevin equation where fluctuations are projected onto spatial density. Both models used soft equations of state (compressibility modulus 215 MeV for BGBD and 228 MeV for Gogny) with two parametrizations of the symmetry energy (asy-soft and asy-stiff), and fragment identification was performed via coalescence procedures on the density distribution.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility modulus: K_NM(rho_0) = 215 MeV for BGBD interaction and K_NM = 228 MeV for Gogny force in symmetric nuclear matter
- Reaction energy: 50 MeV/nucleon for both 112Sn + 112Sn and 124Sn + 124Sn central collisions
- Impact parameter: 0.5 fm for all simulated trajectories
- Initial distance between nuclei: 15 fm at time t = 0
- Average kinetic energy of pre-equilibrium emission: 20.72 MeV/nucleon in SMF and 21.95 MeV/nucleon in AMD
- Maximum cut-off for two-nucleon collision cross sections: 150 mb in both models
- Fragment recognition distance parameter: d_cl = 5 fm for late-time fragment identification (t = 200 fm/c), d_cl = 3 fm for early-time particle emission recognition
- Density threshold for "liquid" phase in SMF coalescence: rho >= (1/6) rho_0
- Gaussian wave packet width parameter: nu = (2.5 fm)^{-2} in AMD

## Context

This work extends the comparison initiated in Colonna et al. (comp_1) between molecular dynamics and mean-field approaches to multifragmentation at Fermi energies (30-50 MeV/nucleon). The study connects to earlier work on spinodal decomposition mechanisms (rep) and the thermodynamics of finite systems (Chomaz, Chomaz_new), while also addressing the ongoing debate about the density dependence of the symmetry energy (BaranPR410, EPJA_betty). The results enable more reliable extraction of nuclear matter properties from experimental multifragmentation data by identifying which observables are robust across different reaction models and which are sensitive to the specific fragmentation path.
