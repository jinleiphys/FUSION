# Quadrupole moments of low lying baryons with spin 1/2^+, spin 3/2^+, and spin 3/2^+ \to 1/2^+ transitions

> The chiral constituent quark model (\chiCQM) with general parameterization (GP) method has been formulated to calculate the quadrupole moments of the spin 3/2^+ decuplet baryons and spin 3/2^+ \to 1/2^+ transitions. The implications of such a model have been investigated in detail for the effects of symmetry breaking and GP parameters pertaining to the two- and three-quark contributions. It is found that the \chiCQM is successful in giving a quantitative and qualitative description of the quadrupole moments.

# Quadrupole moments of low lying baryons with spin 1/2^+, spin 3/2^+, and spin 3/2^+ → 1/2^+ transitions

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) with general parameterization (GP) method can give a quantitative and qualitative description of quadrupole moments for spin 3/2^+ decuplet baryons and spin 3/2^+ → 1/2^+ transitions, but the results depend on fitting the GP parameters B' and C' to the available data for nucleon charge radii and the Δ^+ N quadrupole transition moment, and on the specific SU(3) symmetry breaking parameters of χCQM previously used for magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors formulated quadrupole moment operators in spin-flavor space using a general parameterization method with two-quark (B') and three-quark (C') terms, then evaluated matrix elements using three-quark spin-flavor wave functions for the spin 1/2^+ octet and spin 3/2^+ decuplet baryons. They incorporated chiral symmetry breaking and "quark sea" effects through the χCQM mechanism, where Goldstone boson emission by constituent quarks redistributes flavor and spin structure, and substituted the modified quark flavor and spin distributions into the NQM expressions. The GP parameters were fitted via χ^2 minimization using nucleon charge radii and the Δ^+ N quadrupole transition moment as input, while the χCQM parameters were taken from previous fits to proton spin and flavor distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- GP parameters obtained from χ^2 minimization: B' = -0.047, C' = -0.008, obeying the hierarchy |B'| > |C'|
- χCQM parameters: a = 0.12, α = 0.7, β = 0.4, ζ = -0.15
- The experimental E2/M1 ratio for γ + p → Δ^+ is at least -0.025 ± 0.005
- The Δ^+ N quadrupole transition moment measured by LEGS Collaboration: -0.108 ± 0.009 ± 0.034 fm^2
- The Δ^+ N quadrupole transition moment measured by Mainz Collaboration: -0.0846 ± 0.0033 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model (χCQM), previously applied successfully to proton spin crisis, hyperon β decay, and baryon magnetic moments by the same group (Dahiya 2000s), to quadrupole moments of decuplet baryons and transitions. It builds on the general parameterization method of Morpurgo (1989, 1999) and addresses inconsistencies among prior theoretical approaches including the chiral quark soliton model (χQSM), spectator quark model, Skyrme model, light cone QCD sum rules, large N_c, chiral perturbation theory (χPT), and lattice QCD (LQCD), which disagree on the sign and magnitude of baryon deformation. The results enable future experimental tests of predicted SU(3) symmetry breaking patterns in quadrupole moments, such as Q^{Ω^-} > Q^{Ξ^{*-}} > Q^{Σ^{*-}} > Q^{Δ^-} > Q^{Δ^{++}}/2 > Q^{Δ^+} > Q^{Σ^{*+}}.
