# Neutrino astrophysics : recent advances and open issues

> We highlight recent advances in neutrino astrophysics, the open issues and the interplay with neutrino properties. We emphasize the important progress in our understanding of neutrino flavor conversion in media. We discuss the case of solar neutrinos, of core-collapse supernova neutrinos and of SN1987A, and of the recently discovered ultra-high energy neutrinos whose origin is to be determined.

# Neutrino astrophysics: recent advances and open issues

## Key claim

Recent progress in understanding neutrino flavor conversion in media has been substantial, but the final impact of collective effects (such as those from neutrino-neutrino interaction) on a supernova neutrino signal on Earth remains to be assessed, and corrections beyond the mean-field approximation require further investigation.

## Method

The paper reviews the current state of neutrino astrophysics by examining three main areas: solar neutrinos (including the pp chain, the MSW effect, and measurements from Borexino, SNO, and KamLAND), core-collapse supernova neutrinos (including SN1987A observations, flavor conversion mechanisms in massive stars, and supernova neutrino observations), and ultra-high energy neutrinos (including IceCube results and neutrino telescope capabilities). The review synthesizes experimental results with theoretical developments in neutrino flavor conversion, particularly the MSW effect, collective oscillations from neutrino self-interaction, and the impact of shock-waves and turbulence.

## Key numbers

- Solar mass-squared difference: Δm₁₂² = 2.4 × 10⁻⁵ eV²; atmospheric mass-squared difference: Δm₂₃² = 7.6 × 10⁻³ eV²
- Solar neutrino survival probability for low energy (< 2 MeV): P(νₑ → νₑ) ≈ 0.57 (with θ₁₂ = 34°); for high energy (⁸B spectrum, matter-dominated): P(νₑ → νₑ) → sin²θ₁₂ ≈ 0.31
- Sun's energy production via pp chain: 3.84 × 10³³ erg/s
- SN1987A distance: 50 kpc from Earth based on expanding photosphere method
- SN1987A neutrino events detected: 16 in Kamiokande II, 8 in IMB, 5 in Baksan
- Best fit for SN1987A electron anti-neutrino emission: total gravitational energy E = 5 × 10⁵² erg, temperature T = 4 MeV, emission time 15 s
- Limit on neutrino speed vs. light speed from SN1987A: |(c_ν − c)/c| < 2 × 10⁻⁹
- IceCube collected 37 events with deposited energies from 30 TeV to 2 PeV, consistent with discovery of high energy astrophysical neutrinos at 5.7 σ; the 2 PeV event is the highest-energy neutrino ever observed

## Context

This review extends the understanding of neutrino flavor conversion established by Pontecorvo (1957), the Super-Kamiokande discovery of neutrino oscillations (Fukuda 1998), and the MSW effect (Wolfenstein 1977, Mikheev 1986). It synthesizes results from solar neutrino experiments (Davis 1968, Bahcall 1968, SNO, KamLAND, Borexino) and supernova neutrino observations (SN1987A from Kamiokande II, IMB, Baksan) with recent theoretical developments in collective neutrino oscillations (Pantaleone 1992, Duan 2005, Hannestad 2006) and the identification of new instabilities such as LESA (Tamborra 2014). The review enables future searches for neutrino mass ordering, CP violation, sterile neutrinos, and the determination of the origin of ultra-high energy neutrinos, as well as improved supernova simulations incorporating multidimensional effects and corrections beyond mean-field approximations.
