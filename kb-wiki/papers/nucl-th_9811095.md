# Competition of electron capture and beta-decay rates in supernova collapse

> We calculate supernova electron capture and beta decay rates for various pf-shell nuclei using large-scale shell model techniques. We show that the centroid of the Gamow-Teller strength distribution has been systematically misplaced in previous rate estimates. Our total electron capture rates are significantly smaller than currently adopted in core collapse calculations, while the total beta decay rates change less. Our calculation shows that for electron-to-baryon ratios Ye=0.42-0.46 beta decay rates are larger than the competing electron capture rates.

# Competition of electron capture and beta-decay rates in supernova collapse

## Key claim

Using large-scale shell model calculations that reproduce measured Gamow-Teller strength distributions and half-lives, the authors show that previous FFN and Aufderheide et al. rates systematically misplaced the Gamow-Teller centroid, leading to electron capture rates that are significantly overestimated (by factors of 5 to orders of magnitude) and beta-decay rates that are less affected; nevertheless, the earlier qualitative conclusion that beta-decay rates exceed electron capture rates for electron-to-baryon ratios Ye = 0.42–0.46 remains correct, though the quantitative balance shifts.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model diagonalization calculations (using a modified KB3 residual interaction) for 15 key pf-shell nuclei identified by Aufderheide et al. (1994) as most important for core collapse, including even-even, odd-A, and odd-odd species. Gamow-Teller strength distributions were calculated with up to 10 million configurations, scaled by the universal quenching factor (0.74)^2, and used to compute beta-decay and electron capture rates under stellar conditions (densities ρ7 = 10–1000, temperatures T9 = 1–10) without invoking the Brink hypothesis, instead explicitly summing over ground and excited states below 1 MeV excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Shell model electron capture rate for ⁵⁶Ni at ρ7 = 4.32, T9 = 3.26: 1.3 × 10⁻² s⁻¹, compared to FFN rate of 7.4 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹ and Aufderheide et al. rate of 8.6 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹
- Shell model electron capture rate for ⁵⁵Co at ρ7 = 4.32, T9 = 3.26: 1.6 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹, compared to FFN rate of 8.4 × 10⁻² s⁻¹ (factor ~50 smaller)
- Shell model electron capture rate for ⁶⁰Co at ρ7 = 33.0, T9 = 4.24: 1.7 × 10⁻⁴ s⁻¹, compared to FFN rate of 1.1 × 10⁻¹ s⁻¹ (factor ~650 smaller)
- Shell model beta-decay rate for ⁶³Co at ρ7 = 33.0, T9 = 4.24: 1.6 × 10⁻² s⁻¹, in agreement with Aufderheide et al. rate of 1.4 × 10⁻² s⁻¹
- Shell model beta-decay rate for ⁶¹Fe at ρ7 = 33.0, T9 = 4.24: 1.7 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹, compared to Aufderheide et al. rate of 6.4 × 10⁻² s⁻¹ (factor ~38 smaller)
- Shell model beta-decay rate for ⁶¹Co at ρ7 = 33.0, T9 = 4.24: 1.6 × 10⁻⁴ s⁻¹, compared to Aufderheide et al. rate of 9.3 × 10⁻⁴ s⁻¹ (factor ~6 smaller)
- Scaling factors derived from comparison: FFN electron capture rates multiplied by 0.2 (even-even), 0.1 (odd-A), 0.04 (odd-odd); FFN beta-decay rates multiplied by 0.05 (even-even), 0.025 (odd-A), 1.5 (odd-odd)
- Beta-decay rates exceed electron capture rates for Ye = 0.42–0.455

## Context

This work extends and corrects the foundational rate compilations of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN, 1980–1985) and the subsequent extension to A > 60 by Aufderheide et al. (1994), which had been questioned by experimental (n,p) data and earlier truncated shell model calculations by Aufderheide et al. (1996). The authors show that while the phenomenological parametrization of Gamow-Teller centroids was systematically incorrect, the earlier conclusion that beta-decay can compete with electron capture at Ye ≈ 0.42–0.46 is robust. This enables future core collapse simulations using a complete set of shell-model-based rates, which are expected to yield cooler cores and larger Ye values at homologous core formation than previously assumed, as suggested by Aufderheide et al. (1994) using the older FFN rates.
