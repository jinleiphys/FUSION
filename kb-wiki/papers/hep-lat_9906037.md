# Ergodic Properties of Classical SU(2) Lattice Gauge Theory

> We investigate the relationship between the Lyapunov exponents of periodic trajectories, the average and fluctuations of Lyapunov exponents of ergodic trajectories, and the ergodic autocorrelation time for the two-dimensional hyperbola billiard. We then study the fluctuation properties of the ergodic Lyapunov spectrum of classical SU(2) gauge theory on a lattice. Our results are consistent with the notion that this system is globally hyperbolic. Among the many powerful theorems applicable to such systems, we discuss one relating to the fluctuations in the entropy growth rate.

## Key claim

The paper argues that classical SU(2) lattice gauge theory behaves as a globally hyperbolic (Anosov-like) system, based on the observed scaling of Lyapunov exponent fluctuations and their agreement with predictions from periodic orbit theory; however, the authors explicitly note that numerical calculations cannot exclude the existence of non-hyperbolic regions in the high-dimensional phase space.

## Method

The authors numerically computed the complete positive Lyapunov spectrum for classical SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on three-dimensional lattices of sizes L=1,2,3,4,6, extending prior calculations. They compared the fluctuation properties of ergodic Lyapunov exponents and their autocorrelation functions to analytical predictions from the thermodynamic formalism, using the two-dimensional hyperbola billiard as a test system where all periodic orbits up to a certain period are known. For the lattice gauge theory, they sampled the sum of positive Lyapunov exponents along single long ergodic trajectories and fitted the resulting distributions to Gaussians.

## Key numbers

- For the hyperbola billiard, the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy (positive ergodic Lyapunov exponent) was found to be h_KS = 0.575, with the arithmetic average of periodic Lyapunov exponents lambda_bar = 0.703 and topological entropy h_top = 0.5925.
- The width of the Gaussian distribution of ergodic Lyapunov exponents for the hyperbola billiard scales as Delta h_r(t_s) approximately 0.86 t_s^{-1/2}.
- For the hyperbola billiard, the autocorrelation time of local Lyapunov exponents was t_c approximately 6.
- For the SU(2) lattice gauge theory on an L=4 lattice, the variance of the sum of positive Lyapunov exponents scaled as sqrt(L^3/t_s), and the mean maximal ergodic Lyapunov exponent was of order (0.5 fm)^{-1}.
- Relative fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions were estimated to be of order sqrt((0.5 fm)^4/(5 fm)^4) = 0.01 (1 percent).

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of Lyapunov spectra in classical SU(2) gauge theory by Muller and Trayanov (1992) and Gong (1993, 1994), and connects them to the thermodynamic formalism of periodic orbit theory developed by Parry and Pollicott (1990) and Gaspard (1998). The results support the conjecture that classical Yang-Mills fields are globally hyperbolic, enabling the application of periodic orbit theory to estimate dynamical parameters such as the logarithmic entropy growth rate in the quark-gluon plasma. The paper also relates the fluctuation properties to event-by-event fluctuations observed in heavy-ion collisions by the NA49 collaboration (1999).
