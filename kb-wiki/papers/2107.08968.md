# Highest weight irreducible representations favored by nuclear forces within SU(3)-symmetric fermionic systems

> The consequences of the attractive, short-range nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction on the wave functions of nuclear models bearing the SU(3) symmetry are reviewed. The NN interaction favors the most symmetric spatial SU(3) irreducible representation (irrep), which corresponds to the maximal spatial overlap among the fermions. The consideration of the highest weight (hw) irreps in nuclei and in alkali metal clusters, leads to the prediction of a prolate to oblate shape transition beyond the mid-shell region. Subsequently, the consequences of the use of the hw irreps on the binding energies and two-neutron separation energies in the rare earth region are discussed within the proxy-SU(3) scheme, by considering a very simple Hamiltonian, containing only the three dimensional (3D) isotropic harmonic oscillator (HO) term and the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. This Hamiltonian conserves the SU(3) symmetry and treats the nucleus as a rigid rotator.

# Highest weight irreducible representations favored by nuclear forces within SU(3)-symmetric fermionic systems

## Key claim

The attractive, short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction favors the most symmetric spatial SU(3) irreducible representation (irrep), which is the highest weight (hw) irrep, not the irrep with the highest eigenvalue of the second order Casimir operator; this choice makes no difference in the lower half of each shell but predicts a prolate-to-oblate shape transition in the upper half, and the hw irrep provides theoretical justification for the dominance of prolate over oblate shapes in even-even nuclei.

## Method

The authors review the consequences of the short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction on SU(3) wave functions in fermionic systems, then calculate binding energies and two-neutron separation energies for rare earth nuclei within the proxy-SU(3) scheme using a Hamiltonian containing only the three-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator term and the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. They compare results obtained using the highest weight SU(3) irrep against those using the irrep with the highest eigenvalue of the second order Casimir operator, treating the depth of the harmonic oscillator potential V0 as a free parameter fitted to data.

## Key numbers

- The depth of the 3D isotropic harmonic oscillator potential is given approximately by V0 ≈ 50(1 − (N−Z)/A) MeV, but in the rare earths the fitting procedure gave V0 ≈ 44 MeV.
- For the Nd isotopes, the fitted V0 values used constants c1 = 42.86274, c2 = 0.04289, c3 = −2.84426 × 10⁻⁴ from a polynomial fit V0 = c1 + c2N + c3N².
- The Hamiltonian uses ℏω = 41/A^(1/3) MeV and the quadrupole-quadrupole strength χ/2 = ℏω/(8N0), where N0 is the eigenvalue of the harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian.
- For the neutron 82-126 shell, the highest weight irrep at N=104 is (54,12) with C2 = 3906, while the Casimir-maximizing irrep is (20,44) with C2 = 3408; at N=112 the hw irrep is (50,4) with C2 = 2878 versus the Casimir irrep (20,40) with C2 = 2980.

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) model (Bonatsos 2017) and the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott 1958) to binding energy calculations, building on earlier arguments in Ref. EPJAHW that the highest weight irrep is the correct choice for fermionic systems. The results contrast with the Interacting Boson Model (IBM, Arima and Iachello 1975), where the ground state band sits in the (2N,0) irrep with μ=0, because bosons lack the spin-isospin antisymmetry restrictions. The work enables future inclusion of the spin-orbit interaction, which is expected to mix the highest weight irrep with other SU(3) irreps and soften the staircase behavior seen in the two-neutron separation energies.
