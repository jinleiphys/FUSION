# The anomalous gamma -> pi^+ pi^0 pi^- form factor and the light-quark mass functions at low momenta

> The gamma -> 3 pi form factor was calculated in a simple-minded constituent model with a constant quark mass parameter, as well as in the Schwinger-Dyson approach. The comparison of these and various other theoretical results on this anomalous process, as well as the scarce already available data (hopefully to be supplemented by more accurate CEBAF data), seem to favor Schwinger-Dyson modeling which would yield relatively small low-momentum values of the constituent (dynamically dressed) quark mass function.

## Key claim

The comparison of theoretical predictions with scarce existing data suggests that Schwinger-Dyson modeling yielding relatively small low-momentum values of the dynamically dressed quark mass function is favored, but the only available experimental data point (from Serpukhov) is higher than all theoretical predictions and is probably an overestimate.

## Method

The authors calculated the gamma → 3π form factor using two approaches: (1) a simple constituent quark model with a constant quark mass parameter M and pseudoscalar Yukawa coupling, where the quark-pion coupling strength g is related to the pion decay constant fπ = 92.4 MeV through the Goldberger-Treiman relation; and (2) the Schwinger-Dyson (SD) approach with a momentum-dependent quark mass function M(k²) from the dressed quark propagator, using the Bethe-Salpeter bound-state pion-quark-antiquark vertex and the Ball-Chiu Ansatz for the dressed quark-photon vertex. The form factor was evaluated both analytically (up to order O(p⁸) relative to the soft point) and numerically for various mass parameters.

## Key numbers

- The soft-point amplitude for γ → π⁺π⁰π⁻ in the chiral limit is F_γ^{3π}(0,0,0) = e N_c / (12 π² f_π³), with f_π = 92.4 MeV
- The constant constituent quark mass values used were M = 330 MeV, M = 400 MeV, and M = 580 MeV
- The SD model adopted yields M(k²≈0) ≈ 360 MeV for light u,d quarks
- The explicit chiral symmetry breaking mass m ≈ 2 MeV in the SD model choice
- The empirical pion mass used was m_π = 138.5 MeV
- Lattice QCD studies find M(k²=0) = 298 ± 8 MeV (for m=0)
- The Ball-Chiu Ansatz is adequate for -0.4 GeV² < q² < 0.2 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the anomalous γ → 3π process beyond the chiral and soft limits, where the amplitude is exactly predicted by Adler, Terent'ev, Aviv and Zee (1971-1972). The authors compare their results with vector meson dominance predictions (Rudaz, 1984) and chiral perturbation theory (Holstein, 1996), finding that the SD form factors are lower than both. The accurate CEBAF data (Miskimen et al., 1994) should constrain the infrared SD modeling and the quark mass function at low momenta, potentially requiring reformulation of SD phenomenology toward smaller mass scales.
