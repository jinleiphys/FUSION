# Role of exact treatment of thermal pairing in radiative strength functions of $^{161-163}$Dy nuclei

> The enhancement of radiative strength function (RSF) in the region of low $γ$-rays energy ($E_γ\leq 12$ MeV), which is caused by the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR), is treated within the phonon damping model (PDM) plus exact thermal pairing (EP) without adding any extra PDR strength function. The numerical calculations performed for $^{161-163}$Dy show that, because of the effect of EP, the EP+PDM can describe reasonably well the total RSF data in both low- and high-energy regions of $γ$-rays. Consequently, as compared to the conventional description within the phenomenological generalized Lorentzian (GLO) and standard Lorentzian (SLO) models, the EP+PDM calculations can eliminate at least eight free parameters. This indicates the important role of microscopic approaches towards the precise description of the RSF. In particular, temperature is found to have significant contributions to the RSF below the neutron separation energy, questioning again the validity of the Brink-Axel hypothesis in this energy region.

## Key claim

The phonon damping model plus exact thermal pairing (EP+PDM) can describe the total radiative strength function (RSF) of \(^{161-163}\)Dy in both low- and high-energy \(\gamma\)-ray regions without adding any extra pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) strength function, eliminating at least eight free parameters compared to phenomenological models, but this agreement depends on the specific temperatures of 0.56 MeV for \(^{162}\)Dy and 0.59 MeV for \(^{161,163}\)Dy.

## Method

The total RSF was calculated as the sum of the PDM strength functions for E1, M1, and E2 resonances at a given temperature, using the phonon damping model which includes temperature-dependent GDR width and exact thermal pairing. Single-particle spectra were computed with an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential, and the EP calculation used a truncated space of 12 doubly degenerated deformed single-particle levels around the Fermi surface. The GDR and scissors resonance parameters at \(T=0\) were taken from the global parametrization of Steinwedel-Jensen and from Ref. [Ren], with GDR cross sections calculated from the EP+PDM strength functions satisfying the Thomas-Reich-Kuhn sum rule with enhancement factor \(\kappa=0.6\).

## Key numbers

- The EP+PDM RSFs agree with experimental data at temperatures of 0.56 MeV for \(^{162}\)Dy and 0.59 MeV for both \(^{161}\)Dy and \(^{163}\)Dy.
- The quadrupole deformation parameters \(\beta_2\) used are 0.271 for \(^{161}\)Dy, 0.341 for \(^{162}\)Dy, and 0.283 for \(^{163}\)Dy.
- The GDR energies for the two components (\(E_1^I\) and \(E_1^{II}\)) from the global parametrization are: for \(^{161}\)Dy, 12.50 MeV and 15.60 MeV; for \(^{162}\)Dy, 12.00 MeV and 15.90 MeV; for \(^{163}\)Dy, 12.40 MeV and 15.60 MeV.
- The EP+PDM GDR cross sections for the two components are: for \(^{161}\)Dy, 187.70 mb and 231.00 mb; for \(^{162}\)Dy, 198.20 mb and 218.70 mb; for \(^{163}\)Dy, 198.20 mb and 239.50 mb.
- The scissors resonance energies are 2.78 MeV for \(^{161}\)Dy, 2.81 MeV for \(^{162}\)Dy, and 2.84 MeV for \(^{163}\)Dy.
- The exact neutron and proton pairing gaps remain finite even at \(T=3\) MeV for all three isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the EP+PDM approach previously applied to \(^{170-172}\)Yb by Hung et al. (2020) to the dysprosium isotopes \(^{161-163}\)Dy, where recent experimental RSF data show strong dominance of E1 strengths. The results contradict the Brink-Axel hypothesis by showing significant temperature dependence of the RSF below the neutron separation energy, and improve upon the D1M+QRPA calculations of Goriely et al. (2018, 2019) which underestimate the scissors resonance due to missing thermal couplings of particle-hole, particle-particle, and hole-hole configurations. The EP+PDM eliminates the need for the two PDR strength functions required by the phenomenological GLO-SLO model of Ren et al., reducing free parameters by at least eight.
