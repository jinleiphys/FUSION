# Anomalous mapping between pionfull and pionless EFT's

> The pion contributions to the coupling $C_0$ of pionless EFT are studied via both non-relativistic and relativistic forms of chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces. A definite item in the $2N$-reducible component of the box diagram is shown to be dominant over the $2N$-irreducible (potential) ones due to the pinching of low-lying nucleon poles, and this anomalous mapping between pionless and pionfull EFT's occurs right within the non-relativistic regime. A natural strategy for renormalization of the pionfull theory emerges as a byproduct through the interactive analysis of the box diagram. Such mapping perspective may shed some light on the efficient organization of the pionfull effective field theory for nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The dominant contribution to the leading contact coupling \(C_0\) in pionless effective field theory (EFT) comes from the \(2N\)-reducible (iterated) component of the box diagram, not from the \(2N\)-irreducible (potential) components, due to pinching of low-lying nucleon poles in the non-relativistic regime; this anomalous mapping occurs right within the non-relativistic regime and is not an artifact of relativistic effects.

## Method

The author computed the one-pion exchange (OPE) and two-pion exchange (TPE) contributions to the pionless coupling \(C_0\) using both non-relativistic (heavy baryon) and relativistic chiral EFT formulations. The once-iterated OPE box diagram was evaluated in both 3-dimensional non-relativistic and 4-dimensional relativistic frameworks, with dimensional and cutoff regularization, and the contributions from the \(2N\)-reducible and \(2N\)-irreducible components were separated by identifying the low-lying nucleon pole contributions in the relativistic calculation. The BKV prescription (with separation scale \(\lambda_{\text{BKV}} = 750\) MeV) was also applied to test the robustness of the dominance.

## Key numbers

* The once-iterated OPE diagram contributes to the isospin-dependent pionless coupling \(C_{0\tau}^{(it)} = -\frac{3g_A^4 M_N m_\pi}{64\pi f_\pi^4}\) (with a 3% correction from the pionless region excluded).
* The ratio of the \(2N\)-reducible to \(2N\)-irreducible contributions from the planar box diagram is \(\frac{C_0^{(it)}}{C_0^{(irr)}} = \frac{3\pi}{4}\sqrt{\varrho} = \frac{3\pi M_N}{4m_\pi} \approx 16.03\), demonstrating dominance of the reducible component.
* The extracted scale for the isospin-dependent coupling from the iterated OPE is \(\Lambda_{(\not\pi,\tau)}^{(it)} = \frac{256\pi^2 f_\pi^4}{3g_A^4 M_N^2 m_\pi}\), which for \((f_\pi, m_\pi, M_N) = (92.4, 138, 939)\) MeV and \(g_A = 1.26\) gives \(\Lambda_{(\not\pi,\tau)} = 200.18\) MeV (\(\sim 1.45 m_\pi\)).
* The extracted scale for the isospin-independent coupling from the iterated OPE at \(g_A = 1.26\) is \(\Lambda_{(\not\pi)} = 133.45\) MeV (\(\sim 0.97 m_\pi\)).
* In the BKV prescription, the ratio of the iterated to direct OPE contribution to \(C_{0\tau}\) is \(\left|\frac{C_{0\tau}^{(it;\text{BKV})}}{C_{0\tau}^{(\text{BKV})}}\right| \approx 2.564 g_A^2\), ranging from 4.07 to 4.47 as \(g_A\) varies from 1.26 to 1.32.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the nonperturbative treatment of pion-exchange potentials in chiral EFT for nuclear forces, contrasting with the Weinberg (1990) approach and the modified power-counting schemes of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and the BKV prescription (2006). The analysis supports the conventional power counting for pionless EFT and suggests that the dominant contributions to pionless couplings arise from the region \(\tilde{U}_{(\pi)} = [\Lambda_{(\not\pi)}, \Lambda_{(\pi)})\) rather than from the pionless region itself. The results enable future extraction of pionless parameters like \(J_0\) from pion-exchange diagrams and may inform the incorporation of dibaryon-like degrees of freedom to improve the organization of pionfull EFT.
