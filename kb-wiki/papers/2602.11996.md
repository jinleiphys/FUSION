# Medium effects on light clusters from heavy-ion collisions within a relativistic mean-field description

> Central $^{136,124}$Xe$+^{124,112}$Sn collisions from INDRA data are analysed using a Bayesian inference on light nuclei multiplicities to estimate the thermodynamical parameters and in-medium modification of the cluster self-energies within a relativistic mean-field model. An excellent description of experimentally measured abundances of H and He isotopes is obtained. We examine two possible modelling of in-medium effects as an increased in-medium effective mass, or an increased vector repulsion. We show that these physical pictures cannot be discriminated by the data. In both cases, the temperature dependence of the meson couplings leads to a faster weakening of the light cluster abundances with temperature than previous studies predicted. Possible systematic errors due to out-of-equilibrium effects affecting the experimental abundances, are considered by repeating the Bayesian inference with reduced information. The abundance prediction of the species excluded from the constraint is well compatible with the experimental data, suggesting that there is no a priori need of accounting for non-equilibrium effects or finite state interactions that potentially affect the deuteron yield.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in-medium modifications of light cluster self-energies in heavy-ion collisions can be described equally well by either an increased effective mass (scalar coupling) or an increased vector repulsion (vector coupling), and that these two physical pictures cannot be discriminated by the available experimental data from INDRA.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference on experimentally measured mass fractions of hydrogen and helium isotopes (¹H, ²H, ³H, ³He, ⁴He) from central ¹³⁶,¹²⁴Xe+¹²⁴,¹¹²Sn collisions at 32 MeV/nucleon, using a relativistic mean-field model with either FSU or DD2 parametrizations. The unknown parameters, baryonic density, temperature, and cluster-meson coupling ratios, were inferred for each of 52 independent data sets (4 entrance channels × 13 velocity bins), with the scalar coupling ratio x_s first inferred while fixing x_ω=1, and then the vector coupling ratio x_ω inferred while fixing x_s=1 to test degeneracy.

## Key numbers

- Baryonic density extracted from the data is compatible with a single value of approximately 0.015 fm⁻³ across all velocity bins
- The scalar coupling ratio x_s posteriors from the quadratic fit approach range from 0.893⁺⁰·⁰¹⁸₋₀·₀₁₆ (¹²⁴Xe+¹¹²Sn) to 0.912⁺⁰·⁰⁰²₋₀·₀₀₇ (¹³⁶Xe+¹²⁴Sn) across the four entrance channels
- The uncertainty in the x_s extraction due to uncontrolled parameters and out-of-equilibrium effects is estimated as Δx_s ≈ 0.01
- The density extraction uncertainty is estimated as Δρ ≈ 0.01 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis approach of Custódio et al. (2025) by providing full methodological details and investigating the degeneracy between scalar and vector cluster-meson couplings previously noted by Ferreira et al. (2012). It contrasts with earlier studies by Pais et al. (2020) and Qin et al. (2011) that assumed an expanding cooling gas picture with increasing density as a function of surface velocity, finding instead that the baryonic density remains approximately constant. The results enable improved calibration of cluster-meson couplings for use in equations of state for supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, while the demonstrated degeneracy between scalar and vector coupling mechanisms provides guidance for future experimental design.
