# Multibin correlations in a superposition approach to relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Analysis of correlation of multiplicities between various rapidity bins is carried out in the framework of a superposition approach consisting of three phases of the ultra-relativistic nuclear collision: early partonic phase, intermediate collective evolution, and statistical hadronization. Simple relations between the moments of produced hadrons and the moments of the sources produced in the initial partonic phase are presented. They involve only a few effective parameters describing the of microscopic dynamics of the system. We illustrate the practicality of the approach with the Glauber model simulations. Our study carries direct relevance for the interpretation of the upcoming results for the multibin multiplicity correlations from the LHC, which will help in an assessment of the correlation features of the state formed in the earliest stage of the reaction.

## Key claim

The paper derives simple, centrality-independent relations between the moments of produced hadrons and the moments of initial sources in a three-stage superposition model, but the practical applicability of these relations depends on the assumption that the hydrodynamic response is approximately linear and that the emission from each source is independent and universal across rapidity and centrality classes.

## Method

The authors model relativistic heavy-ion collisions in three sequential stages: (1) initial partonic phase where sources (e.g., wounded nucleons or binary collisions) produce partons independently, (2) intermediate collective hydrodynamic evolution treated as a deterministic linear mapping, and (3) statistical hadronization where fluid cells emit hadrons independently. They derive analytic formulas relating the moments (mean, variance, covariance, third moments) of the initial source distribution to those of the final hadron distribution, involving a small set of effective parameters. The initial phase is simulated using the GLISSANDO implementation of the Glauber model for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, with the mixed model (mixing parameter a=0.1) as the default.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV are simulated using the Glauber model with NN cross section sigma_NN = 66.3 mb
- Pseudorapidity bins of width delta_eta = 0.2 are used, with separations Delta_eta = 1.8, 6, and 10 studied
- For the mixed Glauber model, the mixing parameter is a = 0.1
- The forward-backward correlation of sources rho(s_F,s_B) is essentially equal to 1 for Delta_eta = 1.8 across all centralities, and remains near 1 for Delta_eta = 10 except at very large centrality
- For centrality bins of width Delta_c = 10%, the variance of sources scales as (Delta_c)^2 and the third moment scales as a*(Delta_c)^4 where a is a small parameter
- The ratio of third-to-second moments of sources scales as a*Delta_c, making it vanish for narrow centrality bins

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-bin analysis of Olszewski and Broniowski (2013) to three rapidity bins (forward, central, backward). It builds on the superposition approach used in the wounded-nucleon model of Bialas (1976) and the forward-backward correlation studies by Konchakovski et al. (2008) using the Hadron-String-Dynamics transport approach. The paper provides a theoretical framework for interpreting upcoming LHC multibin multiplicity correlation data, which will help discriminate between different models of the initial partonic phase (wounded nucleon, mixed, or binary collision models) and extract information about the hydrodynamic response parameters.
