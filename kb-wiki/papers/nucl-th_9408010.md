# Simple classification of final state interaction effects in $^4He(e,e'p)$ scattering

> The radius of interaction between the struck proton and spectator nucleons is close to the radius of short-distance two-nucleon correlations in nuclear matter, which makes final state interaction(FSI) an important background to production of protons with large missing momentum. We present a simple classification of the dominant FSI effects in $^4He(e,e'p)$ scattering and identify parts of the phase space dominated by FSI. At large missing momentum, FSI leads to a striking angular anisotropy of the missing momentum distribution, which has a prominent peak in transverse kinematics and smaller, forward-backward asymmetric, peaks in parallel kinematics.

## Key claim

The radius of the final state interaction (FSI) between the struck proton and spectator nucleons is very close to the radius of short-distance two-nucleon correlations (ISC) in nuclear matter, making FSI an important background to the production of protons with large missing momentum; the paper provides a classification showing that at large missing momentum, FSI leads to a striking angular anisotropy of the missing momentum distribution, with a prominent peak in transverse kinematics and smaller, forward-backward asymmetric peaks in parallel kinematics.

## Method

The authors model the exclusive process \(^4He(e,e'p)\) using Glauber's multiple scattering theory for the FSI S-matrix, with a harmonic oscillator mean-field wavefunction for \(^4He\) and a Gaussian parameterization for the short-range correlation function. They expand the product of correlation and FSI operators in the closure-summed cross section, classify terms by their range (short vs. long) in transverse and longitudinal coordinates, and compute analytic estimates for the relative normalization and momentum dependence of each contribution. Numerical results are shown for the angular dependence of the missing momentum distribution at large \(p_m\).

## Key numbers

- The \(^4He\) radius \(R_o \approx 1.4\) fm (from Refs. [8,9]).
- The correlation radius \(r_c \sim 0.5\) fm (from Ref. [10]).
- The FSI radius \(b_o \approx 0.5\) fm and total cross section \(\sigma_{tot} \approx 40\) mb at \(T_{kin} \sim 1\) GeV (from Refs. [11,12,13]).
- The normalization of linear FSI terms relative to the uncorrelated distribution is \(Y = \sigma_{tot}/(4\pi R_o^2) \approx 0.17\).
- The ratio of the elastic rescattering term to the ISC term at \(p_{m,z}=0\) is \(\Gamma'\Gamma / C'C \sim 7\).
- The ratio of the ISC-FSI interference term to the elastic rescattering term is \((C\Gamma'+C'\Gamma) / \Gamma'\Gamma \sim 1\).
- The leading ISC correction to elastic rescattering has relative normalization \(C\Gamma'\Gamma / \Gamma'\Gamma \sim -0.3\).
- The FSI correction to the ISC effect has relative normalization \((C'C\Gamma+C'C\Gamma') / C'C \sim -2/3\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of FSI effects in \((e,e'p)\) scattering on heavier nuclei (e.g., C and Pb, as studied in Ref. [3]) to the specific case of \(^4He\), and provides a semi-analytic classification that was not present in prior work on FSI and ISC effects in \(A(e,e'p)\) scattering (Refs. [16,17,18]). The results enable improved interpretation of planned CEBAF experiments (Ref. [2]) and suggest that experimental separation of FSI and ISC effects is more difficult than previously thought, with implications for \(y\)-scaling analysis and color transparency studies (Ref. [4]).
