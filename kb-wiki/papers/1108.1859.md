# Cubic neutrons

> The neutron is largely spherical and incompressible in atomic nuclei. These two properties are however challenged in the extreme pressure environment of a neutron star. Our variational computation within the Cornell model of Coulomb gauge QCD shows that the neutron (and also the Delta-3/2 baryon) can adopt cubic symmetry at an energy cost of about 150 MeV. Balancing this with the free energy gained by tighter neutron packing, we expose the possible softening of the equation of state of neutron matter.

# Cubic neutrons

## Key claim

The neutron (and the Δ³/² baryon) can adopt cubic symmetry at an energy cost of about 150 MeV, which could be compensated by the free energy gained from tighter packing in the high-pressure environment of a neutron star, potentially softening the equation of state of neutron matter. This deformation is presented as a variational estimate within a specific model (Cornell model of Coulomb gauge QCD), not as a definitive prediction.

## Method

The authors performed a variational computation within the Cornell model of Coulomb gauge QCD, using a BCS approximation for the vacuum and a three-quark wavefunction for baryons. They employed a family of trial wavefunctions parameterized by an integer N that interpolates between spherical symmetry (N=2) and octahedral (cubic) symmetry (N→∞), minimizing the Hamiltonian expectation value with respect to variational parameters α_ρ and α_λ via Monte Carlo integration of the nine-dimensional three-body integrals.

## Key numbers

- Neutron mass in the model: 980 MeV (with Monte Carlo error of 40 MeV), compared to the physical mass of 940 MeV
- Energy cost to deform the neutron from spherical to nearly cubic shape (N=2 to N=18): approximately 150 MeV
- For the Δ(1232) baryon, the excitation cost to abandon spherical symmetry is very similar to that for the neutron
- 75% of the probability in the cubic wavefunction resides in the Y₀₀ spherically symmetric state, with an additional 24% in d-wave Y₂ₘ states
- The mass-energy density threshold for considering neutron structure effects: 140 MeV/fm³ (approximately 3 m_π⁴)
- Maximum packing gain from cubic deformation in an fcc lattice: 26% of the initial volume occupied by a spherical neutron

## Context

This work extends the long-standing proposal of a neutron crystal phase in neutron stars (Canuto, 1972) by considering that individual neutrons themselves can deform from spherical to cubic symmetry under directional stresses. The calculation uses the Cornell model, previously applied to heavy quarkonium (Eichten, 1978) and light mesons and baryons (Llanes-Estrada, 1999; Bicudo, 2009). The results suggest that at densities relevant for neutron star structure, neutron deformability produces a softening of the equation of state additional to other known sources, and that this deformation may compete with hyperon production (the Λ mass is about 1115 MeV, similar to the 150 MeV deformation cost added to the nucleon mass).
