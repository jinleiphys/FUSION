# Flavor and Spin Structure of $Λ$-Baryon at Large x

> It is shown that a perturbative QCD (pQCD) based analysis and the SU(6) quark-diquark model give significant different predictions concerning the flavor and spin structure for the quark distributions of the $Λ$-baryon near $x=1$. Detailed predictions for the ratios $u(x)/s(x)$ of unpolarized quark distributions, $Δs(x)/s(x)$ of valence strange quark, and $Δu(x)/u(x)$ of valence up and down quarks of the $Λ$ are given from the quark-diquark model and from a pQCD based model. It is found that the up and down quarks are positively polarized at large $x$, even though their net spin contributions to the $Λ$ might be zero or negative. The significant difference for $u(x)/s(x)$ between the two different approaches are predicted. The prediction of positively polarized up and down quarks inside the $Λ$ at large $x$ has been supported by the available data of $Λ$-polarization in $Z$ decays and also by the most recent HERMES result of spin transfer to the $Λ$ in deep elastic scattering of polarized lepton on the nucleon target.

# Flavor and Spin Structure of Λ-Baryon at Large x

## Key claim

The paper shows that a perturbative QCD (pQCD) based analysis and the SU(6) quark-diquark model yield significantly different predictions for the flavor and spin structure of quark distributions in the Λ-baryon near x=1, with the key condition that the up and down quarks inside the Λ are positively polarized at large x even though their net spin contributions integrated over all x might be zero or negative.

## Method

The authors extend the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model from the nucleon to the Λ-baryon, using the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage prescription for light-cone momentum space wave functions with specific mass parameters for quarks and diquarks. They also apply a pQCD based analysis using counting rules from minimally connected tree graphs of hard gluon exchanges, which predicts helicity retention and power-law behaviors (1-x)^p for quark distributions, with anti-parallel helicity distributions suppressed by a relative factor (1-x)^2. The two approaches are compared by calculating the ratios u(x)/s(x), Δs(x)/s(x), and Δu(x)/u(x) for the Λ at large x.

## Key numbers

- In the SU(6) quark-diquark model, the mass parameters used (in MeV) are: m_q=330 for u and d quarks, m_s=480, α_D=330, m_{S(ud)}=600, m_{S(qs)}=750, and m_{V(qs)}=950.
- In the pQCD based model, the parallel helicity distributions scale as (1-x)^3 and anti-parallel as (1-x)^5, with a Regge parameter α≈1/2 for nondiffractive valence quarks.
- The pQCD based model predicts u(x)/s(x) → 1/2 as x→1, while the quark-diquark model predicts u(x)/s(x) → 0 as x→1.
- Both models predict Δs(x)/s(x) → 1 and Δu(x)/u(x) → 1 for the Λ as x→1.
- The pQCD based model gives explicit distribution forms: u↑(x)=d↑(x)=(35/64)x^{-1/2}(1-x)^3, u↓(x)=d↓(x)=(693/1024)x^{-1/2}(1-x)^5, s↑(x)=(35/32)x^{-1/2}(1-x)^3, and s↓(x)=0.

## Context

This work extends the same mechanisms that produce different flavor and spin structure predictions for the nucleon (where d(x)/u(x) at x→1 is predicted as 0 in the quark-diquark model versus 1/5 in pQCD) to the Λ-baryon, providing a cleaner test because the Λ has three distinct valence quarks. The prediction of positively polarized u and d quarks at large x contradicts the earlier expectation from Burkardt and Jaffe (1993) that u and d quarks should be negatively polarized from SU(3) symmetry, and extends the work of Soffer and Ma (1999) on using Λ flavor and spin content to test nucleon structure. The results enable tests via Λ fragmentation functions in e+e- annihilation at the Z pole and in deep inelastic scattering, with the HERMES data on longitudinal spin transfer to the Λ providing initial support but insufficient precision to distinguish between the two models.
