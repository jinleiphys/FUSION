# Open Questions in High Energy Spin Physics

> I describe a few of the most exciting open questions in high energy spin physics. After a brief look at (g-2)_μand the muon electric dipole moment, I concentrate on QCD spin physics. Pressing questions include the interpretation of new asymmetries seen in semi-inclusive DIS, measuring the polarized gluon and quark transversity distributions in the nucleon, testing the DHGHY Sum Rule, measuring the orbital angular momentum in the nucleon, and many others which go beyond the space and time allotted for this talk.

# Open Questions in High Energy Spin Physics

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the theoretical framework for understanding nucleon spin in QCD is largely under control, several critical experimental measurements remain to be performed or fully interpreted, and the validity of foundational sum rules such as the DHGHY Sum Rule hinges on the unresolved question of whether J=1 fixed poles occur in QCD.

## Method

The paper reviews the status of several open problems in high-energy spin physics, focusing on QCD: the interpretation of azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) observed by HERMES; the measurement of quark transversity distributions via helicity-flip fragmentation functions in SIDIS and Drell-Yan processes; direct measurement of the polarized gluon distribution through photon-gluon fusion (charm pair production, dijets) and QCD Compton scattering (prompt photon plus jet); experimental tests of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn-Hosada-Yamamoto (DHGHY) sum rule using polarized real photon beams at ELSA, MAMI, and JLab; and the decomposition of nucleon angular momentum into quark and gluon spin and orbital contributions, including Ji's decomposition via deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and the more recent x-dependent decomposition into four terms.

## Key numbers

- The old CERN muon g-2 value: a_mu x 10^10 = 116 591 00(110), accuracy 10 ppm.
- The BNL E821 muon g-2 value: a_mu^BNL x 10^10 = 116 592 02(14)(6), accuracy 1.3 ppm.
- The theoretical estimate: a_mu^TH x 10^11 = 116 591 596(67), disagreeing with the weighted average of data by 2.6 standard deviations.
- Approved BNL experiments plan to reduce statistical and systematic uncertainties on a_mu to about 0.3 ppm.
- The present limit on the muon electric dipole moment is of order 10^{-18} e-cm; a proposal aims to improve this by up to six orders of magnitude, targeting d_mu ~ 10^{-22} e-cm for a CP-violating phase of order 1.

## Context

This paper extends the line of QCD spin physics launched by the European Muon Collaboration's measurement of the quark spin contribution to the nucleon spin in the mid-1980s. It reviews the status of the transversity distribution first discussed by Ralston and Soper (1979) and later developed by Artru (1990), Jaffe (1991), Cortes (1992), and Jaffe (1995). The paper discusses Ji's decomposition of nucleon helicity (1997) and the more recent x-dependent decomposition into quark and gluon orbital angular momentum by Hagler (1998), Harindranath (1999), and Bashinsky (1998). The DHGHY sum rule, originally proposed by Gerasimov (1966), Drell and Hearn (1966), and Hosoda and Yamamoto (1966), is examined in the context of possible J=1 fixed poles, following earlier work by Fox, Altarelli (1972), and Brodsky (1969). The paper enables future measurements by HERMES, COMPASS, and RHIC to test these theoretical predictions and resolve open questions about the nucleon's spin structure.
