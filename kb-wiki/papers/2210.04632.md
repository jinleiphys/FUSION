# Uncertainty quantification in electromagnetic observables of nuclei

> We present strategies to quantify theoretical uncertainties in modern ab-initio calculations of electromagnetic observables in light and medium-mass nuclei. We discuss how uncertainties build up from various sources, such as the approximations introduced by the few- or many-body solver and the truncation of the chiral effective field theory expansion. We review the recent progress encompassing a broad range of electromagnetic observables in stable and unstable nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper reviews strategies for quantifying theoretical uncertainties in ab-initio calculations of electromagnetic observables, emphasizing that while the goal is to provide uncertainty bars for all calculations, the specific approach and sophistication level depend strongly on the theoretical method and observable under investigation, and the sub-field is still developing.

## Method

The authors use chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) Hamiltonians and currents expanded in powers of (Q/Lambda), combined with few-body solvers (hyperspherical harmonics expansions for A less than 8) and many-body solvers (coupled-cluster theory for A greater than or equal to 8). For continuum states, they employ the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique to avoid explicit calculation of continuum wave functions, then perform a numerical inversion to recover response functions. Uncertainty quantification is divided into two categories: chiEFT uncertainties (truncation error, cutoff dependencies, LEC fitting) and numerical uncertainties (convergence in basis size, model space truncation, inversion procedure).

## Key numbers

* For the n p to gamma d reaction at N2LO and N3LO, the chiEFT truncation uncertainty is much smaller than experimental errors in the energy range of astrophysical relevance.
* For the gamma He-4 to X reaction at NLO, the chiEFT uncertainty at the cross section maximum is roughly 30% (half width); at N2LO it is 15% (half width); at N3LO it is roughly 5% (half width).
* For He-8, the coupled-cluster truncation uncertainty on the dipole polarizability alpha_D is around 7% of the central value; for the energy-weighted sum rule m_1, the uncertainty is below 2%.
* For He-8, the difference between the NLO and N2LO results for m_1 amounts to 3%.
* The EIHH method for He-4 achieves sub-percentage accuracy in binding energies and electromagnetic observables, with numerical inversion uncertainty of order 1-2%.

## Context

This review extends prior work on ab-initio calculations of electromagnetic observables in light and medium-mass nuclei, building on the Lorentz integral transform technique developed by Efros (1994, 2007) and the coupled-cluster method applied to electromagnetic responses by Bacca (2013). The paper discusses uncertainty quantification strategies that go beyond earlier work by incorporating Bayesian methods for chiEFT truncation errors, as developed by Furnstahl (2015), Ekstrom (2020), and Epelbaum (2020), and by applying these to a range of systems from deuteron photodisintegration to Ca-40 electron scattering. The work enables future systematic comparisons between theory and experiment with quantified uncertainties, particularly for big-bang nucleosynthesis reactions and neutron-matter equation of state parameters.
