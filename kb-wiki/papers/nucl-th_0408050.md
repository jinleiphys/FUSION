# Applicability of self-consistent mean-field theory

> Within the constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) theory, an analytic condition is derived to estimate whether a concept of the self-consistent mean field is realized or not in level repulsive region. The derived condition states that an iterative calculation of CHF equation does not converge when the quantum fluctuations coming from two-body residual interaction and quadrupole deformation become larger than a single-particle energy difference between two avoided crossing orbits. By means of the numerical calculation, it is shown that the analytic condition works well for a realistic case.

## Key claim

Within constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) theory, the self-consistent mean field concept breaks down in level repulsive regions when the quantum fluctuations from two-body residual interactions and quadrupole deformation exceed the single-particle energy difference between two avoided-crossing orbits; the paper derives an analytic condition for this breakdown, which the abstract presents without emphasizing that the condition is specifically about the sign of successive off-diagonal Hamiltonian matrix elements during iteration.

## Method

The authors performed configuration-dictated constrained Hartree-Fock calculations for the nucleus ⁶⁶Se using the Gogny D1S interaction, Coulomb force, and center-of-mass corrections, expanding single-particle wave functions on a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis up to principal quantum number N₀=8. They tracked the iterative CHF solution as a function of quadrupole deformation, monitoring the diagonal and off-diagonal components of the CHF Hamiltonian in a fixed representation (the q₀-representation) to analyze convergence behavior. A 2×2 truncated Hamiltonian model was constructed for the two specific neutron orbits responsible for nonconvergence, and analytic recurrence relations for the matrix elements were derived to explain the staggering ("ping-pong") behavior observed in the iterations.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of ⁶⁶Se has a binding energy of 544.502 MeV (HF) and 545.623 MeV (HFB), compared to the experimental value of 547.827 MeV; the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ is 0.241 (HF) and 0.234 (HFB).
- The convergence condition for the CHF iteration is that the sum of absolute differences between successive single-particle energies is ≤ 10 eV.
- The critical point where nonconvergence begins occurs at q₀ = 177.365 fm²; the missing region in the potential energy surface is around q ≈ 150 fm².
- The parabola width w is set to 1.0×10⁻³ MeV/fm⁴, and the center-of-mass constraint parameter αₓ is 1.0×10⁻⁴ MeV/fm².
- The maximum overlap criterion for the configuration-dictated method requires |⟨φᵢ(q)|φᵢ(q+Δq)⟩|² > 0.9.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions on the applicability of cranked mean-field theory near avoided level crossings (Hama 1976, Stru 1977, Naz 1993, Dob 2000) and addresses the question of when a self-consistent mean field is physically realized, which the authors state had not been previously examined. The configuration-dictated CHF method used here was developed in earlier work by the same group (Guo 2003, Guo 2002) and is related to the approach of Cusson et al. (1985). The analytic condition derived enables future studies to predict where mean-field theory fails in deformed nuclei, particularly in shape-coexistence regions, without performing the full iterative calculation.
