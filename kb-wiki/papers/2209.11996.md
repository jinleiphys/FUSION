# Anisotropic flows of charmonium in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We review recent studies about anisotropic flows ($v_1, v_2, v_3$) of charmonium in the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Collective flows of the bulk medium are developed due to the anisotropic pressure gradient of the medium. Strongly coupled with the bulk medium, charm quarks carry collective flows from the expanding medium, which will be inherited by the regenerated charmonium via the coalescence process. In event-by-event collisions where nucleon positions fluctuate from the smooth distribution, there is triangularity in the medium initial energy density. Triangular flows of the bulk medium and heavy flavor particles can be developed due to the initial fluctuations. In the longitudinal direction, the rapidity-odd distribution of the initial energy density is induced by the rotation of the medium in non-central heavy-ion collisions. Charmonium suffers biased dissociation along positive and negative $x$-directions in forward (backward) rapidity. The directed flow of charmonium becomes non-zero. The directed, elliptic and triangular flows ($v_1, v_2, v_3$) of charmonium come from the anisotropic initial distributions of the medium energy density in the transverse and longitudinal directions.

# Anisotropic flows of charmonium in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Charmonium anisotropic flows (v₁, v₂, v₃) arise from anisotropic initial distributions of the medium energy density in both transverse and longitudinal directions, combined with charm quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma; however, the magnitude of these flows depends critically on the degree of charm quark kinetic thermalization, and theoretical calculations of elliptic flow at high pT underestimate experimental data, possibly due to non-thermal distributions of charm quarks.

## Method
The authors review a Boltzmann-type transport model and a Langevin-plus-coalescence model that describe the dynamical evolution of charm quarks and charmonium in the quark-gluon plasma. Charm quarks evolve via Brownian motion described by the Langevin equation including drag, random noise, and gluon radiation, while the medium evolution is given by hydrodynamic models (MUSIC) with initial conditions from the Glauber model or Monte Carlo Glauber/fKLN models. Charmonium regeneration occurs via coalescence of charm and anti-charm quarks, with the coalescence probability calculated from the Wigner transform of the charmonium wave function.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass taken as m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Spatial diffusion coefficient estimated as 5 ≤ D_s(2πT) ≤ 7 with weak temperature dependence
- Critical energy density for QGP creation ∼ 1 GeV/fm³
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature ∼ 0.12 GeV
- Triangular deformation parameter ε₃ = 0.08, 0.1, 0.2 in centrality bins 0-10%, 10-30%, and 30-50% respectively in √s_NN = 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions
- Collision energy √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for Pb-Pb collisions in the MUSIC model calculations
- Collision energy √s_NN = 200 GeV for Au-Au collisions in directed flow calculations
- Charm pair production cross-section ratio between 5.02 TeV and 2.76 TeV drives R_AA enhancement
- Ratio N_J/ψ/(N_c\bar c)² calculated at collision energies √s_NN = 2.76, 5.02, and 39 TeV
- Charm momentum thermalization times considered: τ_t = 0.6 fm/c and τ_t = 4 fm/c

## Context
This review extends studies of anisotropic flows from light hadrons (Song 2010, Gale 2012, STAR 2004, ALICE 2011) to charmonium, building on the observation that QGP behaves as a nearly perfect fluid with small η/s. The work connects to prior transport model approaches (Yan 2006, Zhu 2004) and Langevin models for heavy quarks (Cao 2015), and to experimental observations of D-meson elliptic flows at ALICE (2013) and CMS (2012). The review enables future event-by-event calculations of charmonium triangular flow and directed flow measurements at RHIC and LHC to probe charm quark thermalization and medium initial fluctuations.
