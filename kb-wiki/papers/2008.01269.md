# Multiplicative noise and the diffusion of conserved densities

> Stochastic fluid dynamics governs the long time tails of hydrodynamic correlation functions, and the critical slowing down of relaxation phenomena in the vicinity of a critical point in the phase diagram. In this work we study the role of multiplicative noise in stochastic fluid dynamics. Multiplicative noise arises from the dependence of transport coefficients, such as the diffusion constants for charge and momentum, on fluctuating hydrodynamic variables. We study long time tails and relaxation in the diffusion of a conserved density (model B), and a conserved density coupled to the transverse momentum density (model H). Careful attention is paid to fluctuation-dissipation relations. We observe that multiplicative noise contributes at the same order as non-linear interactions in model B, but is a higher order correction to the relaxation of a scalar density and the tail of the stress tensor correlation function in model H.

## Key claim

Multiplicative noise, arising from the density dependence of transport coefficients like diffusion constants and viscosities, contributes at the same order as non-linear interactions in the diffusion of a conserved density (model B), but is a higher order correction to the relaxation of a scalar density and the tail of the stress tensor correlation function in model H (a conserved density coupled to transverse momentum density).

## Method

The authors constructed a Martin-Siggia-Rose-Janssen-de Dominicis (MSRJD) stochastic field theory for model B (diffusion of a conserved density) and model H (diffusion coupled to transverse shear modes), incorporating a field-dependent diffusion constant and shear viscosity. They derived the Feynman rules, computed one-loop corrections to response and correlation functions, and verified that the fluctuation-dissipation relations hold in the presence of multiplicative noise by including new vertex functions arising from the density-dependent transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- In model B, the one-loop self energy correction is given by Sigma(omega,k) = (lambda'/(32 pi)) ( i lambda' omega k^2 + lambda_D [i omega - D_0 k^2] k^2 ) sqrt(k^2 - 2i omega/D_0), where D_0 is the bare diffusion constant, lambda' is the effective non-linear coupling, and lambda_D parameterizes the density dependence of the diffusion constant.
- In model H, the leading correction to the density response from coupling to momentum density is Sigma(omega,k) = (1/(6 pi)) (k^2/(w(gamma_0+D_0))) sqrt(-i omega/(gamma_0+D_0)), where w is enthalpy density, gamma_0 is the bare momentum diffusion constant, and D_0 is the bare scalar diffusion constant.
- In model H, the transverse self energy for the momentum density response is Sigma_T(omega,k) = (7/(120 pi)) (k^2/(w gamma_0)) sqrt(-i omega/(2 gamma_0)), determining the leading hydrodynamic tail in the stress tensor correlation function.

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic fluctuations and long-time tails in stochastic fluid dynamics, building on the classification of dynamical critical phenomena by Hohenberg and Halperin (1977). The authors verify that multiplicative noise does not modify the leading order Kawasaki function governing the dynamical critical exponent z ~ 3 in model H, as previously studied by Kawasaki (1970) and Onuki (2002). The results enable systematic inclusion of multiplicative noise in relativistic fluid dynamics simulations of heavy ion collisions, where transport coefficients depend on fluctuating hydrodynamic variables.
