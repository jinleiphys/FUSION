# Probing high-density behavior of symmetry energy from pion emission in heavy-ion collisions

> Within the framework of the improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model, the emission of pion in heavy-ion collisions in the region 1 A GeV as a probe of nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities is investigated systematically, in which the pion is considered to be mainly produced by the decay of resonances $\triangle$(1232) and N*(1440). The total pion multiplicities and the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ yields are calculated for selected Skyrme parameters SkP, SLy6, Ska and SIII, and also for the cases of different stiffness of symmetry energy with the parameter SLy6. Preliminary results compared with the measured data by the FOPI collaboration favor a hard symmetry energy of the potential term proportional to $(ρ/ρ_{0})^{γ_{s}}$ with $γ_{s}=2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a stiff symmetry energy (potential term proportional to \((\rho/\rho_{0})^{\gamma_{s}}\) with \(\gamma_{s}=2\)) is favored by FOPI pion data, but this conclusion is preliminary and conditional: it directly contradicts a prior analysis of the same FOPI data using the IBUU04 model, which had extracted a very soft symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities.

## Method

The authors used the improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model to simulate central heavy-ion collisions at around 1 A GeV. Pion production was treated via the decay of \(\Delta\)(1232) and N*(1440) resonances created in inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions, using in-vacuum cross sections parameterized from one-boson exchange model calculations. The total pion multiplicities and \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratios were calculated for four Skyrme parameter sets (SkP, SLy6, Ska, SIII) and for different stiffnesses of the symmetry energy using the SLy6 force, then compared with FOPI collaboration data for \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at saturation density is given as \(E_{\textrm{sym}}(\rho) \approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_{0})^{\gamma}\) MeV with \(\gamma = 0.69-1.05\) for densities between \(0.1\rho_{0}\) and \(1.2\rho_{0}\) (citing prior work).
- The hard symmetry energy case uses \(\gamma_{s}=2\) with \(C_{sym}=32\) MeV in the expression \(E_{sym}(\rho)=\frac{1}{3}\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2m}\left(\frac{3}{2}\pi^{2}\rho\right)^{2/3}+ \frac{1}{2}C_{sym}\left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_{0}}\right)^{\gamma_{s}}\).
- The isobar model gives \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}=1.95\) from \(\Delta\) resonance and \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}=1.7\) from \(N^{\ast}\) resonance for the \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au system.
- A moderately soft symmetry energy with \(\gamma_{s}\simeq 0.9\pm0.3\) was extracted from neutron-proton elliptic flow analysis of FOPI/LAND data using the UrQMD model (citing prior work).

## Context

This work extends the ImIQMD model, previously applied to fusion reactions near the Coulomb barrier, to the study of pion emission at supra-saturation densities. It directly contradicts the analysis of the same FOPI data by the IBUU04 model (Xiao et al., 2009), which had extracted a very soft symmetry energy at high density. The results enable future experimental studies at facilities such as CSR (IMP Lanzhou), FAIR (GSI Darmstadt), RIKEN, SPIRAL2 (GANIL Caen), and FRIB (MSU) to further constrain the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy.
