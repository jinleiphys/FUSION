# The role of the continuum and the spurious 1- transitions in incoherent mu - e conversion rate calculations

> By using the Continuum RPA (CRPA) method, the incoherent transition strength of the exotic mu - e conversion in the 208Pb and 40Ca nuclei is investigated. The question whether excited nuclear states lying high in the continuum give an important contribution to the incoherent rate is addressed. The admixture of spurious components in the rate coming from 1- excitations is investigated in detail by using the self-consistent CRPA with Skyrme interactions as well as a less consistent version and by employing two ways to remove the spurious strength: the use of effective operators or simply the exclusion of the spurious state appearing close to zero energy. In all cases, the correction achieved is quite large.

## Key claim

High-lying continuum states contribute significantly to the incoherent muon-to-electron conversion rate in both Pb-208 and Ca-40, and more than 85% of the total 1- transition strength is spurious center-of-mass contamination that must be removed, with the correction being quite large regardless of the removal method used.

## Method

The authors employed a self-consistent Continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) method with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock ground states (SkM* and MSk7 parametrizations) to calculate the incoherent muon-to-electron conversion rate in Pb-208 and Ca-40, considering natural-parity particle-hole excitations up to L=6. They also used a non-consistent CRPA (ncRPA) with a Woods-Saxon potential and simplified Skyrme residual interaction to compare results. Spurious 1- center-of-mass contaminations were removed either by using effective dipole operators or by excluding the spurious state appearing near zero energy.

## Key numbers

- Muon binding energy in Pb-208: epsilon_b = 10.475 MeV; in Ca-40: epsilon_b = 1.0533 MeV
- Particle threshold energy for Pb-208 with SkM* force: E_thr = 8.09 MeV; for Ca-40: E_thr = 8.86 MeV
- For Pb-208 with self-consistent CRPA (SkM*), the spurious portion of total 1- strength (s^sp_tot) was: 86.9% (gamma), 96.3% (W), 90.5% (Z)
- For Pb-208 with ncRPA, s^sp_tot was: 89.8% (gamma), 96.5% (W), 88.5% (Z)
- For Ca-40 with ncRPA, s^sp_tot was: 90.8% (gamma), 93.7% (W), 88.0% (Z)
- For Pb-208 with self-consistent CRPA, spurious strength above 6 MeV (s^sp_>6MeV) was: -1.3% (gamma), 7.8% (W), 6.1% (Z)
- For Pb-208 with ncRPA, s^sp_>6MeV was: -1.6% (gamma), 12.5% (W), 16.2% (Z)
- For Ca-40 with self-consistent CRPA, s^sp_>6MeV was: 6.7% (gamma), 45.0% (W), 9.1% (Z)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CRPA study on Pb-208 (Papakonstantinou et al., 2005) to include the lighter nucleus Ca-40 and provides a detailed investigation of spurious 1- contamination. It contrasts with earlier state-by-state QRPA calculations by Kosmas et al. (1997) and Suhonen et al. (1997), which found that low-lying states dominate the incoherent rate and that the 1- channel contributes about 50% of the total rate. The results enable more reliable calculations of the experimentally measured coherent-to-total capture ratio by properly accounting for continuum contributions and removing spurious center-of-mass components.
