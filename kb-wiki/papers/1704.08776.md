# Interpretation of $Y(4390)$ as an isoscalar partner of $Z(4430)$ from $D^*(2010)\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction

> Invoked by the recent observation of $Y(4390)$ at BESIII, which is about 40 MeV below the $D^*(2010)\bar{D}_1(2420)$ threshold, we investigate possible bound and resonance states from the $D^*(2010)\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction with the one-boson-exchange model in a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach. A bound state with quantum number $0^-(1^{--})$ is produced at 4384 MeV from the $D^ (2010)\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction, which can be related to experimentally observed $Y(4390)$. Another state with quantum number $1^+(1^{+})$ is also produced at $4461+i39$ MeV from this interaction. Different from the $0^-(1^{--})$ state, the $1^+(1^{+})$ state is a resonance state above the $D^*(2010)\bar{D}_1(2420)$ threshold. This resonance state can be related to the first observed charged charmonium-like state $Z(4430)$, which has a mass about 4475 MeV measured above the threshold as observed at Belle and LHCb. Our result suggests that $Y(4390)$ is an isoscalar partner of the $Z(4430)$ as a hadronic-molecular state from the $D^*(2010)\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction.

# Interpretation of $Y(4390)$ as an isoscalar partner of $Z(4430)$ from $D^*(2010)\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction

## Key claim
The paper proposes that $Y(4390)$ and $Z(4430)$ can both be explained as hadronic molecular states arising from the $D^*(2010)\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction, with $Y(4390)$ as an isoscalar S-wave bound state and $Z(4430)$ as an isovector P-wave resonance state. This interpretation depends on tuning a single regularization cutoff parameter to $\Lambda = 1.4$ GeV to simultaneously reproduce both states.

## Method
The authors constructed a one-boson-exchange potential for the $D^*\bar{D}_1$ interaction, considering only the cross diagram of pion exchange. They solved the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation for the scattering amplitude in the center-of-mass frame, with the heavier $D_1$ meson placed on shell. Poles of the scattering amplitude were found by analytic continuation of the total energy into the complex plane and searching for zeros of $|1-V(z)G(z)|$, using an exponential regularization with cutoff $\Lambda$ to guarantee convergence.

## Key numbers
- Bound state with quantum numbers $0^-(1^{--})$ found at 4384 MeV from the $D^*\bar{D}_1$ interaction, related to $Y(4390)$ observed at BESIII with mass $4391.5^{+6.3}_{-6.8}\pm 1.0$ MeV and width $139.5^{+16.2}_{-20.6}\pm 0.6$ MeV.
- Resonance state with quantum numbers $1^+(1^{+})$ found at $4461+i39$ MeV from the $D^*\bar{D}_1$ interaction, related to $Z(4430)$ with mass $4475\pm7^{+15}_{-25}$ MeV and width $172\pm13^{+37}_{-34}$ MeV as measured at LHCb.
- The regularization cutoff used to simultaneously produce both states is $\Lambda = 1.4$ GeV.
- The $D^*\bar{D}_1$ threshold is approximately 4430 MeV, with $Y(4390)$ about 40 MeV below this threshold.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of $D^*\bar{D}_1$ molecular states by He (2014) and others, which had treated the system only for bound states below threshold. The new quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation for amplitudes, developed by He (2015, 2016), allows treatment of resonance states above threshold. The results contradict earlier S-wave molecular interpretations of $Z(4430)$ with $J^P=0^-$ by Close (2010), Liu (2008), and Lee (2008), and instead support the $1^+$ assignment from Belle and LHCb measurements. The work enables a unified molecular picture where $Y(4390)$ and $Z(4430)$ are partners from the same $D^*\bar{D}_1$ interaction, and suggests $Y(4390)$ is also related to $Y(4260)$ by replacing the $D$ meson with $D^*$ in the molecular state.
