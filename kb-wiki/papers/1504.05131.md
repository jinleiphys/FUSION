# Three-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials with virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation

> The nucleon-nucleon interaction arising from the exchange of three pions and the excitation of $Δ(1232)$-isobars in intermediate states is studied. Approximating the $Δ$-propagator by the inverse $Δ$N mass-splitting, analytical expressions are derived for the spectral-functions of the isoscalar and isovector central, spin-spin and tensor NN-potentials in momentum-space. A trans- lation of the spectral-functions into coordinate-space potentials reveals that the main effect of these specific exchange and excitation mechanisms is a repulsive isoscalar central NN-potential.

## Key claim

The main effect of three-pion exchange with virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitation is a repulsive isoscalar central nucleon-nucleon potential, in contrast to previous calculations of chiral three-pion exchange without explicit Δ-isobars which found a vanishing isoscalar central potential. This result is obtained under the approximation of replacing the non-relativistic Δ-propagator by the inverse ΔN mass-splitting Δ⁻¹, which may overestimate the potentials by about 10–20%.

## Method

The author calculates two-loop 3π-exchange diagrams with intermediate Δ(1232)-isobar excitations in the heavy nucleon mass limit, approximating the non-relativistic Δ-propagator by the inverse ΔN mass-splitting Δ⁻¹ = (293 MeV)⁻¹. Analytical expressions for the spectral functions (imaginary parts) of the isoscalar and isovector central, spin-spin, and tensor NN-potentials in momentum-space are derived using Cutkosky cutting rules, and these are transformed into coordinate-space potentials via continuous superpositions of Yukawa functions with a spectral cutoff Λ = 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Δ-nucleon mass splitting: Δ = 293 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV; pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV; nucleon axial-vector coupling: g_A = 1.29
- Spectral cutoff: Λ = 1.5 GeV
- The unscaled Yukawa potential exp(-3m_π r)/r has strength (24.1, 5.65, 1.49) MeV at distances r = (1.0, 1.5, 2.0) fm respectively
- The approximation of the Δ-propagator by Δ⁻¹ leads to an overestimation of the associated central potentials by about 10–20%

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of 3π-exchange NN-interaction by Kaiser (2001) and the subleading corrections by the same author to chiral effective field theory with explicit Δ(1232)-isobar degrees of freedom. Previous calculations of chiral three-pion exchange without Δ-isobars found a vanishing isoscalar central potential, whereas the present inclusion of Δ-excitations produces a repulsive isoscalar central component. The results can be incorporated into future fits to empirical NN phase shifts via subtracted dispersion relations, complementing the N⁴LO and N⁵LO chiral calculations by the Bochum-Bonn group.
