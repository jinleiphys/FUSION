# On interpretation of fluctuations of conserved charges at high T

> Fluctuations of conserved charges calculated on the lattice which can be measured experimentally, are well reproduced by a hadron resonanse gas model at temperatures below T_{ch} ~ 155 MeV and radically deviate from the hadron resonance gas predictions above the chiral restoration crossover. This behavior is typically interpreted as an indication of deconfinement in the quark-gluon plasma regime. We present an argument that this interpretation may be too simple. The argument is based on the scaling of quantities with the number of colors: demonstration of deconfinement and QGP requires observable that is sensitive to N_c^2 gluons while the conserved charges are sensitive only to quarks and above T_{ch} scale as N_c^1. The latter scaling is consistent with the existence of an intermediate regime characterized by restored chiral symmetry and by approximate chiral spin symmetry which is a symmetry of confining interaction. In this regime the energy density, pressure and entropy density scale as N_c^1. In the large N_c limit this regime might become a distinct phase separated from the hadron gas and from QGP by phase transitions. A natural observable that associates with deconfinement and is directly sensitive to deconfined N_c^2-1 gluons is the Polyakov loop; in the N_c=3 world it remains very close to 0 at temperatures well above chiral crossover, reaches the value 0.5 around 3T_{ch} and the value close to 1 at temperatures ~1 GeV.

# On interpretation of fluctuations of conserved charges at high T

## Key claim
The standard interpretation that deviations of conserved charge fluctuations from hadron resonance gas predictions above T_ch ~ 155 MeV signal deconfinement and quark-gluon plasma formation may be too simple; these fluctuations scale as N_c^1 and are therefore consistent with an intermediate confining regime with restored chiral symmetry and approximate chiral spin symmetry, rather than requiring a deconfined QGP phase.

## Method
The authors analyze the scaling with number of colors N_c of conserved quark number fluctuations and of the Polyakov loop in QCD at high temperature. They compare lattice data for fluctuations of u, d, s quark numbers and for the renormalized Polyakov loop against hadron resonance gas model predictions, using large-N_c scaling arguments to distinguish between hadron gas (N_c^0), an intermediate chirally symmetric confining regime (N_c^1), and QGP (N_c^2).

## Key numbers
- T_ch ~ 155 MeV: pseudocritical temperature of chiral restoration crossover in N_c=3 QCD with physical quark masses
- Polyakov loop remains very close to 0 at temperatures well above T_ch, reaches ~0.5 around ~3T_ch (~465 MeV), and approaches 1 at temperatures ~1 GeV
- Deconfinement temperature in pure Yang-Mills theory at N_c=3 is T_d ~ 270-300 MeV, where the Polyakov loop jumps from 0 to ~0.5-0.6
- In the chiral limit, the critical temperature of the second order chiral restoration phase transition is around 130 MeV (Karsch)

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Cohen and Glozman (2023) that three regimes of QCD (hadron gas, intermediate chirally symmetric confining phase, and QGP) may become distinct phases separated by phase transitions in the large-N_c limit. It contradicts the common interpretation that conserved charge fluctuations above T_ch directly indicate deconfinement, as used in lattice studies such as those by Bazavov et al. (2012). The analysis builds on lattice evidence for approximate SU(2)_CS chiral spin symmetry and its flavor extension SU(4) in the temperature range T_ch < T < ~3T_ch, found by Rafelski et al. (2018), Rothkopf et al. (2019), and Chiu et al. (2020). The paper enables reinterpretation of existing lattice data on conserved charge fluctuations as evidence for an intermediate regime rather than QGP, and motivates future lattice calculations at N_c > 3 to test the predicted N_c^1 scaling.
