# Real and Virtual Compton Scattering at Low Energies

> These lectures give a pedagogical introduction to real and virtual Compton scattering at low energies. We will first discuss real Compton scattering off a point particle as well as a composite system in the framework of nonrelativistic quantum mechanics. The concept of electromagnetic polarizabilities is introduced. We then address a description of the Compton-scattering tensor within quantum field theory with particular emphasis on the derivation of low-energy theorems. The importance of a consistent treatment of hadron structure in the use of electromagnetic vertices is stressed. Finally, the reader is introduced to the notion of generalized polarizabilities in the rapidly expanding field of virtual Compton scattering.

## Key claim

These lectures provide a pedagogical introduction to real and virtual Compton scattering at low energies, emphasizing that low-energy theorems (LETs) based on gauge invariance, Lorentz invariance, crossing symmetry, and discrete symmetries uniquely specify the scattering amplitude up to terms linear in photon momentum, while terms of second order in frequency parameterize the nucleon's electric and magnetic polarizabilities.

## Method

The paper first develops real Compton scattering in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics for point particles and composite systems, introducing electromagnetic polarizabilities via low-energy expansion of the T-matrix element. It then presents a covariant quantum field theory treatment of the Compton-scattering tensor, using Ward-Takahashi identities to derive low-energy theorems and emphasizing consistent treatment of hadron structure in electromagnetic vertices. Finally, it introduces generalized polarizabilities for virtual Compton scattering accessible via the reaction e⁻p → e⁻pγ.

## Key numbers

- Thomson cross section: σ_T = 8π/3 × α²/M², where α = e²/4π ≈ 1/137
- Wavelength shift of Compton effect: Δλ = (4π/M) sin²(Θ/2)
- Differential cross section in Thomson limit (ω→0): dσ/dΩ|_(ω=0) = (α²/M²) × (1+cos²(Θ))/2
- Nonrelativistic T-matrix element expanded to second order in photon energy: t_fi = ε'*·ε (−(Ze)²/M + 4π α_E ωω') + 4π β_M (q'×ε'*)·(q×ε)
- Differential cross section including polarizabilities: dσ/dΩ contains terms −[1+cos²(Θ)] (αZ²/M) α_E ωω' and −2cos(Θ) (αZ²/M) β_M ωω'

## Context

This work extends the foundational low-energy theorems of Low (1954) and Gell-Mann (1954) for real Compton scattering and the generalized polarizabilities framework introduced by Guichon (1995) for virtual Compton scattering. The lectures build upon pedagogical reviews by Friar (1989) and Holstein (1990, 1992) on hadron polarizabilities, and provide an introduction to the rapidly expanding field of virtual Compton scattering, which enables investigation of longitudinal current components and generalized polarizabilities in the spacelike region.
