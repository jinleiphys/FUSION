# Implications of Large-$N_c$ QCD for the NN Interaction

> We present a method for ordering two-nucleon interactions based upon their scaling with the number of QCD colors, $N_c$, in the limit that $N_c$ becomes large. Available data in the two-nucleon sector shows general agreement with this ordering, indicating that the method may be useful in other contexts where data is less readily available. However, several caveats and potential pitfalls can make the large-$N_c$ ordering fragile and/or vulnerable to misinterpretation. We discuss the application of the large-$N_c$ analysis to two- and three-nucleon interactions, including those originating from weak and beyond-the-standard-model interactions, as well as two-nucleon external currents. Finally, we discuss some open questions in the field.

## Key claim

The large-\(N_c\) scaling of QCD provides a method to order two-nucleon interactions by their relative size, but this ordering is fragile and vulnerable to misinterpretation due to caveats including renormalization scheme dependence, the need to apply the analysis before Fierz transformations, and the fact that the large-\(N_c\) and chiral limits do not commute.

## Method

The authors map the \(N_c\) scaling of QCD matrix elements onto low-energy coefficients (LECs) in effective field theories or potential models by using the contracted SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry that emerges in the large-\(N_c\) limit. They apply factorization of two-nucleon matrix elements into products of single-nucleon matrix elements, remove an overall factor of \(N_c\) to account for the scaling of the Hamiltonian, and include suppressions from relativistic momenta (where \(\pplus \sim N_c^{-1}\)) and from pion vertices (each pion gives a factor \(1/F_\pi \sim 1/\sqrt{N_c}\)). The analysis is performed on an over-complete set of operators before any Fierz reductions are applied.

## Key numbers

* In pionless EFT at leading order, the LEC \(C_S\) scales as \(O(N_c)\) and \(C_T\) scales as \(O(1/N_c)\), giving \(C_T/C_S \approx 0.08\) for \(N_c=3\).
* The ratio of spin-singlet to spin-triplet S-wave LECs at renormalization scale \(\mu = m_\pi\) is \(\Czerosing(\mu = m_\pi)/\Czerotrip(\mu = m_\pi) \approx 0.7\), a 30% deviation from the large-\(N_c\) prediction of unity.
* The spin-singlet scattering length is \(\asing \approx -23.7\) fm and the spin-triplet scattering length is \(\atrip \approx 5.41\) fm; these fine-tuned values obscure the large-\(N_c\) scaling unless a suitable renormalization point is chosen.
* Lattice QCD results at \(m_\pi \approx 806\) MeV (NPLQCD, 2013) show S-wave scattering lengths with the same sign and close in magnitude, consistent with the large-\(N_c\) expectation that \(C_T\) vanishes.

## Context

This review extends the large-\(N_c\) analysis of baryon interactions pioneered by Kaplan and Manohar (1995) and Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1993-1994) from the single-baryon sector to two-nucleon interactions, including applications to weak and beyond-the-standard-model processes. The method enables prioritization of experiments, simulations, and calculations in nuclear physics by providing a hierarchy of interaction strengths, and it can be tested against lattice QCD results such as those from the NPLQCD collaboration (2013, 2020) and the work of Horz et al. (2020).
