# Constraining neutron star properties through parity-violating electron scattering experiments and relativistic point coupling interactions

> Parity-violating electron scattering experiments on $\rm ^{48}Ca$ (CREX) and $\rm ^{208}Pb$ (PREX-2) offer valuable insight into the isovector properties of finite nuclei, providing constraints for the density dependence of the nuclear equation of state, which is crucial for understanding astrophysical phenomena. In this work, we establish functional dependencies between the properties of finite nuclei-such as weak charge form factors and neutron skin thickness-and the bulk properties of neutron stars, including tidal deformability from binary neutron star mergers and neutron star radii. The dependencies are formulated by introducing a family of $β$-equilibrated equations of state based on relativistic energy density functionals with point coupling interactions. The charge minus the weak form factors derived from CREX and PREX-2 measurements, combined with the observational constraints on tidal deformability from the GW170817 event, are used to constrain the symmetry energy and neutron star radii. Notably, the energy density expanded up to the fourth order in symmetry energy yields larger radii compared to calculations limited to the second order term. However, the results reveal a discrepancy between the constraints provided by the CREX and PREX-2 experiments. For a more quantitative assessment, higher precision parity-violating electron scattering data and neutron star observations are required.

## Key claim
The authors establish functional dependencies between finite-nuclei properties (weak charge form factors and neutron skin thickness from CREX and PREX-2 experiments) and neutron star bulk properties (tidal deformability and radii), but find a discrepancy between constraints from CREX and PREX-2 that produces a ~0.5 km gap in predicted neutron star radii at 1.4 solar masses.

## Method
The authors constructed a family of beta-equilibrated equations of state using relativistic energy density functionals with density-dependent point-coupling interactions (DD-PC family), with symmetry energies J ranging from 29 to 36 MeV. They also employed functionals specifically constrained by CREX (48Ca) and PREX-2 (208Pb) weak form factor measurements at momentum transfers of 0.0297 GeV^2 and 0.00616 GeV^2 respectively. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, with the energy density expanded up to fourth order in symmetry energy.

## Key numbers
- CREX experiment provides a neutron star radius range of 11.790 ≤ R1.4 ≤ 11.861 km and symmetry energy 24.306 ≤ J ≤ 29.037 MeV
- PREX-2 experiment provides R1.4 ≥ 12.417 km and J ≥ 34.190 MeV, with upper limit R1.4 ≤ 13.366 km from GW170817 constraints
- The GW170817 event constrains effective tidal deformability to Λ̃ ≤ 720
- The DD-PC family spans symmetry energies J = 29 to 36 MeV at saturation density
- The fit for Λ1.4 versus R1.4 yields coefficients c4 = 5.389×10^-4 and c5 = 5.357 with correlation coefficient r = 0.957
- The standard deviation for Λ1.4 fits ranges from 14.611 to 14.768 depending on the nucleus and observable

## Context
This work extends previous studies connecting PREX-2 and CREX experiments to neutron star properties (PhysRevResearch.4.L022054, PhysRevLett.127.192701, PhysRevC.104.065804) by introducing a family of relativistic point-coupling functionals (DD-PC) developed by Yuksel (2021). The results reveal a contradiction between CREX and PREX-2 constraints, producing a ~0.5 km gap in predicted radii, and show that including fourth-order symmetry energy terms yields larger radii than calculations limited to second order. Higher precision parity-violating electron scattering data and neutron star observations are required to resolve the discrepancy.
