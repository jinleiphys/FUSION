# Mirror energy differences in T=1/2 f7/2-shell nuclei within isospin-dependent DFT

> Background: Small asymmetry between neutrons and protons, caused by the differences in masses and charges of the up and down constituent quarks leads to the isospin symmetry breaking. The isospin non-conservation affects broad range of observables from superallowed Fermi weak interaction to isospin-forbidden electromagnetic rates. Its most profound and cleanest manifestation are systematic shifts in masses and excitation energies of mirror atomic nuclei. Purpose: Recently, we constructed the charge-dependent DFT that includes class II and III local interactions and demonstrated that the model allows for very accurate reproduction of Mirror and Triplet Displacement energies in a very broad range of masses. The aim of this work is to further test the charge-dependent functional by studying Mirror Energy Differences (MEDs) in function of angular momentum $I$. Methods: To compute MEDs we use DFT-rooted no core configuration interaction model. This post mean-field method restores rotational symmetry and takes into account configuration mixing within a space that includes relevant (multi)particle-(multi)hole Slater determinants. Results: We applied the model to $f_{7/2}$-shell mirror pairs of $A=43$, $45$, $47$, and $49$ focusing on MEDs in low-spin part (below band crossing) what allowed us to limit the model space to seniority one and three (one broken pair) configurations. Conclusions: We demonstrate that, for spins $I\leq 15/2$ being subject of the present study, our model reproduces well experimental MEDs which vary strongly in function of $I$ and $A$. The quality of model's predictions is comparable to the nuclear shell-model results by Bentley et al. Phys. Rev. C 92, 024310 (2015).

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their charge-dependent DFT-NCCI model, which includes short-range class III charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) interactions, reproduces experimental Mirror Energy Differences (MEDs) for spins I ≤ 15/2 in A=43, 45, 47, and 49 T=1/2 f7/2-shell mirror pairs with accuracy comparable to nuclear shell-model calculations by Bentley et al. (2015), but the agreement is limited to low-spin states below band crossing and the model systematically underestimates MEDs for the lowest spin states in some cases.

## Method

The authors use a DFT-rooted no-core configuration interaction (DFT-NCCI) model that starts from self-consistent Hartree-Fock Slater determinants built from deformed Nilsson orbitals originating from the spherical f7/2 shell. They perform angular-momentum projection on these configurations and then solve the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation to mix the projected states, using the SV_T^ISB Skyrme pseudopotential augmented with class III CSB contact interactions at next-to-leading order (NLO). The configuration space is limited to seniority one and three (one broken pair) configurations, restricting the analysis to low-spin states below band crossing.

## Key numbers

- The three NLO CSB coupling constants are t0^III = 11 ± 2 MeV fm^3, t1^III = -14 ± 4 MeV fm^5, and t2^III = -7.8 ± 0.8 MeV fm^5, adjusted to all available Mirror Displacement Energy data for A ≥ 6 from Baczyk et al. (2019)
- For the A=47 mirror pair, the DFT-NCCI calculation overestimates the experimental excitation energy of the I=31/2 terminating state by 3.180 MeV
- The fully aligned configuration for A=47 gives an excitation energy of 9.449 MeV for the I=31/2 state relative to the I=3/2 ground state, compared to the experimental value of 10.018 MeV
- Angular-momentum projection uses Gauss-Chebyshev and Gauss-Legendre quadratures with n_alpha = n_beta = n_gamma = 40 knots
- The basis consists of 12 spherical harmonic oscillator shells

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous charge-dependent DFT framework, which accurately reproduced Mirror and Triplet Displacement energies across a broad mass range (Baczyk et al., 2018, 2019), to the more challenging test of MEDs as a function of angular momentum. The results are compared directly to nuclear shell-model calculations by Bentley et al. (2015) for the same mirror pairs, showing comparable accuracy for low-spin states. The approach enables future applications to heavier N≈Z nuclei such as the A≈80 mass region, where conventional shell-model calculations face computational limitations due to large model spaces.
