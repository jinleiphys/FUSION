# Model independent study of massive lepton elastic scattering on the proton, beyond the Born approximation

> Model independent expressions for all polarization observables in $μ+p\to μ+p$ elastic scattering are obtained, taking into account the lepton mass and including the two-photon exchange contribution. The spin structure of the matrix element is parametrized in terms of six independent complex amplitudes, functions of two independent kinematical variables. General statements about the influence of the two--photon--exchange terms on the differential cross section and on polarization observables are given. Polarization effects have been investigated for the case of a longitudinally polarized lepton beam and polarized nucleon in the final state.

## Key claim

The paper derives model-independent expressions for all polarization observables in muon-proton elastic scattering, including two-photon exchange contributions and explicitly accounting for the lepton mass, which is often neglected in electron scattering. The authors show that the lepton mass introduces additional spin-flip amplitudes that become significant for muon scattering and must be included for accurate interpretation of experiments like MUSE.

## Method

The matrix element for muon-proton elastic scattering is parametrized in terms of six independent complex amplitudes (functions of two kinematical variables, momentum transfer squared and center-of-mass energy squared), extending the usual two-amplitude Born approximation. The authors derive the differential cross section and all polarization observables (T-even and T-odd) in the laboratory frame by contracting leptonic and hadronic tensors, keeping terms proportional to the lepton mass and separating the one-photon-exchange and two-photon-exchange contributions. The formalism is applied to cases of unpolarized scattering, longitudinally polarized muon beam with polarized proton target, and single-spin asymmetries for transversely polarized muon beam or polarized proton target.

## Key numbers

- The muon beam momenta chosen for the MUSE experiment are 115 MeV, 153 MeV, and 210 MeV, which are comparable to the muon mass.
- The proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy is r_c = 0.84184(67) fm, which is smaller by five standard deviations compared to the CODATA value r_c = 0.8768(69) fm.
- The smallest value of Q^2 reached with good precision in electron-proton elastic scattering is of the order of 10^{-3} GeV^2.
- The MUSE experiment aims for relative precision of cross section measurements of about few tenths of a percent.
- The target normal spin asymmetry A_p receives contributions from spin-flip amplitudes suppressed by the factor m^2/M^2, which is small even for muon scattering.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of elastic lepton-nucleon scattering beyond the one-photon-exchange approximation, which was previously applied to electron-proton scattering by Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (2003) and Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson (2003-2004). The discrepancy between polarized and unpolarized measurements of the proton form factor ratio, first observed by the GEp collaboration at JLab (Puckett, 2010), motivated the inclusion of two-photon exchange effects. The paper provides the theoretical framework needed for the MUSE experiment at Paul Scherrer Institut (Gilman, 2013), which will simultaneously measure muon-proton and electron-proton elastic scattering to test the consistency of form factor extraction and the proton charge radius determination.
