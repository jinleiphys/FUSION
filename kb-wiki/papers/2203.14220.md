# Astrophysical reaction rates with realistic nuclear level densities

> Realistic nuclear level densities (NLDs) obtained within the spectral distribution method (SDM) are employed to study nuclear processes of astrophysical interest. The merit of SDM lies in the fact that the NLDs corresponding to many body shell model Hamiltonian consisting of residual interaction can be obtained for the full configurational space without recourse to the exact diagnolization of huge matrices. We calculate NLDs and s-wave neutron resonance spacings which agree reasonably well with the available experimental data. By employing these NLDs, we compute reaction cross-sections and astrophysical reaction rates for radiative neutron capture in few Fe-group nuclei, and compare them with experimental data as well as with those obtained with NLDs from phenomenological and microscopic mean-field models. The results obtained for the NLDs from SDM are able to explain the experimental data quite well. These results are of particular importance since the configuration mixing through the residual interaction naturally accounts for the collective excitations. In the mean-field models, the collective effects are included through the vibrational and rotational enhancement factors and their NLDs are further normalized at low energies with neutron resonance data.

## Key claim
The spectral distribution method (SDM) for nuclear level densities, when combined with a parity equilibration scheme using a parameter E0 set to either the neutron separation energy Sn or 0.8Sn, can reproduce experimental neutron capture cross-sections and astrophysical reaction rates for Fe-group nuclei, with the best agreement occurring when the parity equilibration energy is taken as 0.8Sn rather than Sn.

## Method
The authors calculated nuclear level densities (NLDs) using the spectral distribution method applied to a shell-model Hamiltonian with GXPF1A residual interaction in the pf-model space (assuming 40Ca as a core). Ground state energies were computed using NushellX@MSU, with exponential convergence used for cases where the full JT-space dimension exceeded approximately 5×10^6. A parity equilibration scheme was applied to obtain NLDs for both parities, and these NLDs were then used as input to the TALYS 1.95 code to compute (n,γ) cross-sections and astrophysical reaction rates for three reactions: 50V(n,γ)51V, 54Fe(n,γ)55Fe, and 58Ni(n,γ)59Ni.

## Key numbers
- s-wave neutron resonance spacing D0 for 51V: experimental 2.3±0.6 keV; SDM* (E0=0.8Sn) gives 3.5 keV; SDM (E0=Sn) gives 6.2 keV
- s-wave neutron resonance spacing D0 for 55Fe: experimental 18±2.4 keV; SDM* gives 18.4 keV; SDM gives 31.7 keV
- s-wave neutron resonance spacing D0 for 59Ni: experimental 13.4±0.9 keV; SDM* gives 13.9 keV; SDM gives 23.8 keV
- s-wave neutron resonance spacing D0 for 49Ti: experimental 18.3±2.9 keV; SDM* gives 104.9 keV; SDM gives 174.4 keV (noted as exceptionally larger)
- Ground state energy for 51V: -125.54 MeV (full model space dimension 1,242,538)
- Ground state energy for 55Fe: -184.76 MeV (full model space dimension 25,743,302)
- Ground state energy for 59Ni: -236.37 MeV (full model space dimension 76,528,736)
- Astrophysical reaction rates computed for temperatures from 0.1 GK to 10 GK

## Context
This work extends the spectral distribution method for NLDs, previously developed by Senkov et al. (2010, 2013, 2016) and Horoi et al. (2003, 2004, 2011), to astrophysical reaction rate calculations. It contrasts with phenomenological models such as the Back-shifted Fermi gas model (Dilg 1973) and Generalized Superfluid model (Ignatyuk 1979, 1993), as well as microscopic mean-field approaches like Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Goriely 2008), which require separate vibrational and rotational enhancement factors and normalization to neutron resonance data. The SDM approach naturally accounts for collective excitations through configuration mixing. This work enables future studies of neutron-rich nuclei away from β-stability using larger model spaces, relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis.
