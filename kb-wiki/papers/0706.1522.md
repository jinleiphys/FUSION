# Viscosity Information from Relativistic Nuclear Collisions: How Perfect is the Fluid Observed at RHIC?

> Relativistic viscous hydrodynamic fits to RHIC data on the centrality dependence of multiplicity, transverse and elliptic flow for sqrt{s}=200 GeV Au+Au collisions are presented. For Glauber-type initial conditions, while data on integrated v_2 is consistent with a ratio of viscosity over entropy density up to eta/s=0.16, data on minimum bias v_2 seems to favor a much smaller viscosity over entropy ratio, below the bound from the AdS/CFT conjecture. Some caveats on this result are discussed.

## Key claim

For Glauber-type initial conditions, while data on integrated elliptic flow \(v_2\) is consistent with a viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio up to \(\eta/s \simeq 0.16\), data on minimum bias \(v_2\) seems to favor a much smaller ratio, below the AdS/CFT conjectured bound of \(1/(4\pi) \simeq 0.08\), possibly as low as \(\eta/s \simeq 0.03\); however, this result could change drastically if the minimum bias data were decreased by 20%, the estimated systematic error.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV using a set of causal shear-viscous equations (Eq. baseq) with a semi-realistic QCD equation of state from Laine (2006). They assumed boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and Glauber-type initial conditions with the energy density proportional to the binary collision density \(n_{\rm Coll}\), starting at an initial time \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm/c with zero initial shear tensor \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}=0\). Particle spectra were computed via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out mechanism at a single freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 150\) MeV, including resonance decays up to 2 GeV, and the initial entropy density was reduced by \(75\ \eta/s\) percent to account for viscous entropy production.

## Key numbers

- For integrated elliptic flow \(v_2\) data from PHOBOS, the model is consistent with \(\eta/s \sim 0.08\) and, within systematic errors, with \(\eta/s \sim 0.16\).
- For minimum bias \(v_2\) data from STAR, the model favors \(\eta/s \simeq 0.03\), which is below the AdS/CFT bound of \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi) \simeq 0.08\).
- The initial conditions used a gold nucleus Woods-Saxon profile with \(R_0 = 6.4\) fm/c and \(\chi = 0.54\) fm/c.
- The freeze-out temperature was fixed at \(T_f = 150\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier ideal hydrodynamic studies (Teaney, 2000; Huovinen, 2001; Hirano, 2002) by introducing controlled viscous corrections to determine the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC. The results contradict the AdS/CFT conjecture of Kovtun (2004) that \(\eta/s \ge 1/(4\pi)\) for all relativistic quantum field theories, but the authors note that using CGC-type initial conditions (Hirano, 2005) or including hadronic rescatterings (Bass, 2000; Hirano, 2005) could raise the extracted \(\eta/s\) values. This work enables future studies to refine the viscosity extraction by addressing these caveats and by comparing to more sophisticated hybrid models.
