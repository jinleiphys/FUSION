# A Relativistic Wave Equation for the Skyrmion

> We propose a relativistically invariant wave equation for the Skyrme soliton. It is a differential equation on the space $R^{1,3}\times S^3$ which is invariant under the Lorentz group and isospin. The internal variable valued in $SU(2)\approx S^3$ describes the orientation of the soliton. The mass of a particle of spin and isospin both equal to $j={1\over 2},{3\over 2}...$ is predicted to be $M=m\sqrt{1+K_2j(j+1)\over 1+K_1j(j+1)}$ which agrees with the known spectrum for low angular momentum. The iso-scalar magnetic moment is predicted to be $-{K_1\over 4m}{\mathbf Σ}$, where ${\mathbf Σ}$ is the spin.

# A Relativistic Wave Equation for the Skyrmion

## Key claim

The paper proposes a relativistically invariant wave equation for the Skyrme soliton that predicts a mass spectrum matching the known non-relativistic collective excitation spectrum for low angular momentum, but the equation requires two undetermined dimensionless constants (K₁ and K₂) that must be fixed by additional physical input such as the magnetic moment; the mass formula only agrees with the known spectrum in the small-j limit where K = K₂ − K₁.

## Method

The author constructs a second-order differential equation on the space R¹·³ × S³, where the internal variable g ∈ SU(2) ≈ S³ describes the soliton orientation. The wave function takes values in V±, the space of even or odd functions on SU(2) under reflection, which carries a representation of SU(2)×SU(2) for spin and isospin. Lorentz invariance is enforced by coupling the Pauli-Lubansky vector (built from the left-action spin generators Σ) to the Klein-Gordon-like equation, breaking the unwanted separate conservation of orbital and spin angular momentum. The equation is then coupled minimally to electromagnetism to extract the magnetic moment prediction.

## Key numbers

- Mass spectrum for spin and isospin both equal to j = 1/2, 3/2, ...: M = m √[(1 + K₂ j(j+1))/(1 + K₁ j(j+1))], where m is the lightest baryon mass and K₁, K₂ are dimensionless constants.
- Non-relativistic limit recovers M ≈ m + (m/2) K j(j+1) with K = K₂ − K₁.
- For large j, the mass tends to the constant m √(K₂/K₁) > m, requiring K₂ > K₁ > 0.
- Iso-scalar magnetic moment predicted to be −(K₁/4m) Σ, where Σ is the spin operator; the gyromagnetic ratio is γ = −K₁/(4m).
- In the non-relativistic collective excitation theory, the rotational energy eigenvalues are (m/2) K j(j+1).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic collective quantization of the Skyrme soliton (Skyrme; Balachandran and Tasi) to a fully relativistic wave equation, continuing the program of Harish-Chandra on relativistic wave equations for particles with internal degrees of freedom. It also parallels the work of Jackiw and Nair (Jackiw and Nair) on relativistic wave equations for particles with fractional spin in 2+1 dimensions. The equation enables future study of electromagnetic properties (isovector magnetic moments), coupling to pions, chiral symmetry, and the well-posedness of the Cauchy problem for this higher-spin wave equation.
