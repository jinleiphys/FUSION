# Pion electromagnetic form factor at spacelike momenta

> A novel method is employed to compute the pion electromagnetic form factor, F_π(Q^2), on the entire domain of spacelike momentum transfer using the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework in quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The DSE architecture unifies this prediction with that of the pion's valence-quark parton distribution amplitude (PDA). Using this PDA, the leading-order, leading-twist perturbative QCD result for Q^2 F_π(Q^2) underestimates the full computation by just 15% on Q^2>~8GeV^2, in stark contrast with the result obtained using the asymptotic PDA. The analysis shows that hard contributions to the pion form factor dominate for Q^2>~8GeV^2 but, even so, the magnitude of Q^2 F_π(Q^2) reflects the scale of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, a pivotal emergent phenomenon in the Standard Model.

# Pion electromagnetic form factor at spacelike momenta

## Key claim

Using a novel Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) method that unifies the pion form factor with its valence-quark parton distribution amplitude (PDA), the leading-order, leading-twist perturbative QCD result for Q^2 F_π(Q^2) underestimates the full computation by just 15% for Q^2 ≳ 8 GeV^2, but only when the PDA appropriate to the experimental scale is used, not the asymptotic PDA. Hard contributions dominate for Q^2 ≳ 8 GeV^2, yet the magnitude of Q^2 F_π(Q^2) still reflects the scale of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The pion electromagnetic form factor F_π(Q^2) is computed within the DSE framework using rainbow-ladder truncation, with the dressed-quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude expressed via generalized Nakanishi spectral representations. The unamputated dressed-quark-photon vertex is treated via an Ansatz that satisfies the longitudinal Ward-Green-Takahashi identity and includes a dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moment term, with the interaction kernel taken from Qin (2011) using ω = 0.5 GeV and m_G = 0.87 GeV at ζ = 2 GeV. The computation reduces to summing a series of terms each involving a single four-momentum integral, evaluated via Feynman parametrization and standard algebraic methods, enabling continuation to arbitrarily large spacelike momenta.

## Key numbers

- f_π = 0.092 GeV (pion decay constant obtained with the chosen kernel)
- m_ρ = 0.775 GeV (ρ-meson mass used for monopole comparison)
- r_π = 0.64 fm with η̃ = 0; r_π = 0.66 fm with η̃ = 0.5 (empirical r_π = 0.672 ± 0.008 fm)
- Q^2 F_π(Q^2) at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 using asymptotic PDA yields 0.15 (with n_f = 4, Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV)
- Empirical value at Q^2 = 2.45 GeV^2: 0.41^{+0.04}_{-0.03} (from Horn 2006, Huber 2008)
- The PDA at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 has the form φ_π(x) ≈ N_p x^p (1-x)^p with p = 0.3, giving w_φ^2 = 3.2 (compared to w_φ^{asy} = 1.0 for the asymptotic PDA)
- Leading-order QCD evolution requires Q^2 > 1000 GeV^2 before w_φ^2 falls below 1.6
- Variation of Λ_QCD within empirical bounds shifts curve-D by ±3% at Q^2 = 20 GeV^2
- The form factor exhibits a maximum at Q^2 ≈ 6 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the DSE prediction of Maris and Tandy (2000), which was limited to Q^2 < 4 GeV^2, to the entire spacelike domain, achieving a longstanding goal of unifying the pion's electromagnetic form factor with its valence-quark PDA. The analysis contradicts the common presumption that perturbative QCD predictions using the asymptotic PDA (Farrar 1979, Efremov 1979, Lepage 1980) fail at accessible momenta, showing instead that the appropriate PDA (broader than asymptotic) yields near-agreement with the full DSE computation. The results enable next-generation experiments (such as E12-06-101 at Jefferson Lab) to test the predicted flattening and maximum of Q^2 F_π(Q^2) around Q^2 ≈ 6 GeV^2, and demonstrate that the scale of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking imprints itself even in the hard-scattering regime.
