# Characteristics of the fission fragments in the three-body model of binary fission

> The evaluated nuclide yields, fragment mass and charge distributions, and averaged total kinetic energy of fission fragments for the neutron-induced fission of 30 actinide nuclei are well described in the new model, which considers the fissioning scission system consisting of two heavy fragments and an alpha-particle between them. The alpha-particle has its origin in the neck nucleons. The yield of fission fragments in the model is linked to the number of states over the barrier of the saddle point, which is between the contacting and well-separated fission fragments. The alpha-particle is fused with the nearest heavy fragment after passing the saddle point, therefore, two final fragments appear during fission. The quadrupole deformations of heavy fragments are taken into account in the model. The fragment yields depend on the heights of corresponding saddle points and the values of the equilibrium quadrupole deformation of fragments.

# Characteristics of the fission fragments in the three-body model of binary fission

## Key claim
The model describes evaluated nuclide yields, fragment mass and charge distributions, and average total kinetic energy for neutron-induced fission of 30 actinide nuclei by treating the scission system as two heavy fragments with an alpha-particle between them, where the alpha-particle originates from neck nucleons and fuses with the nearest heavy fragment after passing a saddle point. The fragment yields depend on the heights of corresponding saddle points and the equilibrium quadrupole deformation of fragments, with the model describing 9020 selected evaluated yields up to a factor of approximately 7.8.

## Method
The model constructs the total potential energy of a three-body scission system consisting of two deformed heavy fragments and an alpha-particle between them, including Coulomb and nuclear interactions plus liquid-drop deformation energies. The yield of primary fission fragments is calculated from the level densities above two saddle points of the three-body potential energy surface, where the alpha-particle fuses with the nearest fragment after passing each saddle point. The average total kinetic energy is computed from the interaction potentials evaluated at the saddle points, weighted by the level densities. The equilibrium quadrupole deformations of fragments are taken into account through a deformation energy approximated as a harmonic oscillator with liquid-drop and shell-correction stiffness terms.

## Key numbers
- 30 actinide nuclei are described for neutron-induced fission reactions, including isotopes of Th, Pa, Np, U, Pu, Am, Cm, Cf, Es, and Fm
- 26461 evaluated fragment yields from JENDL are considered, with 9020 selected using criteria: Y(A,Z) >= 10^-8, Y(A,Z) >= 10^-3 * Y(A), and complementary fragment yield >= 10^-8
- For n_th + ^235U -> ^236U -> f, the calculated average total kinetic energy is 172.8 MeV, compared to experimental value 170.6 +/- 0.6 MeV
- For n_0.5 MeV + ^238Pu -> ^239Pu -> f, the calculated average total kinetic energy is 177.8 MeV, compared to experimental value 179.7 +/- 0.5 MeV
- For n_th + ^229Th, calculated TKE = 167.9 MeV versus experimental 163.6 +/- 0.5 MeV
- For n_th + ^249Cf, calculated TKE = 188.3 MeV versus experimental 187.3 +/- 1.5 MeV
- The height of the saddle point for two ^118Pd fragments from ^236U fission is approximately 197 MeV, while the Q-value is approximately 193 MeV
- For the system ^76Ni + alpha + ^172Er from ^252Cf fission, the first saddle point height is approximately 160 MeV and the second approximately 167 MeV
- Ternary fission probability is approximately 10^-3 times that of binary fission

## Context
This work extends the scission-point model previously proposed by Denisov (2021, 2022) which considered two-, three-, and four-body scission systems, establishing that three-body configurations are the most important for fission. The model contrasts with the statistical scission-point model of Fong (1956) which used contacting fragments with octupole deformations, and with Gaussian-based approaches used in GEF and other evaluations. The formation of alpha-particles in the neck is supported by time-dependent density functional calculations by Ren (2020) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Han (2023), which found high-density islands in the neck with minimum radius approximately 2 fm, close to the alpha-particle radius of approximately 1.9 fm. The model enables simultaneous description of fragment yields, mass and charge distributions, and average total kinetic energy without introducing additional parameters beyond those fixed for all 30 fissioning nuclei.
