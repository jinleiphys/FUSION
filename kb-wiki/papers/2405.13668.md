# Electromagnetic moments of the odd-mass nickel isotopes $^{59-67}$Ni

> The magnetic dipole and the spectroscopic quadrupole moments of the nuclear ground states in the odd-mass nickel isotopes $^{59-67}$Ni have been determined using collinear laser spectroscopy at the CERN-ISOLDE facility. They are compared to ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations including contributions of two-body currents as well as to shell-model calculations. The two-body-current contributions significantly improve the agreement with experimental data, reducing the mean-square deviation from the experimental moments by a factor of 3 to 5, depending on the employed interaction. For all interactions, the largest contributions are obtained for the $5/2^-$ ($7/2^-$) isotopes $^{65}$Ni ($^{55}$Ni), which is ascribed to the high angular momentum of the $f$ orbitals. Our results demonstrate that the inclusion of two-body-current contributions to the magnetic moment in an isotopic chain of complex nuclei can be handled by the VS-IMSRG and can outperform phenomenological shell-model calculations using effective $g$-factors in the nickel region.

## Key claim
The inclusion of two-body-current contributions in ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations reduces the mean-square deviation from experimental magnetic moments by a factor of 3 to 5 compared to calculations without them, but the improvement depends on the specific nuclear interaction employed and is largest for isotopes with high angular momentum f orbitals.

## Method
Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at the CERN-ISOLDE facility on the odd-mass nickel isotopes 59-67Ni using the COLLAPS experiment. Hyperfine spectra of the 3d9 4s 3D3 → 3d9 4p 3P2 transition were measured, and the magnetic dipole and spectroscopic quadrupole moments were extracted relative to the reference isotope 61Ni using the hyperfine structure constants. The experimental results were compared to ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations with three different NN+3N interactions including one- and two-body-current contributions, as well as to phenomenological shell-model calculations using three different interactions (JUN45, jj44b, GXPF1A) with effective g-factors.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic dipole moment of 59Ni: -0.7439(15) μN (first determination)
- Magnetic dipole moment of 61Ni: -0.7472(6) μN (using reference value from Antusek (2020))
- Magnetic dipole moment of 63Ni: 0.4950(4) μN
- Magnetic dipole moment of 65Ni: 0.7575(7) μN
- Magnetic dipole moment of 67Ni: 0.5961(7) μN (nuclear spin unambiguously determined as 1/2)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of 59Ni: 8.8(0.9) e fm2 (first determination)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of 65Ni: 9.7(0.9) e fm2 (first determination)
- Mean-square deviation for VS-IMSRG with 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction: 0.031 μN2; with ΔN2LOGO interaction: 0.022 μN2; with N3LOlnl interaction: 0.042 μN2
- Mean-square deviation for shell model with GXPF1A interaction: 0.016 μN2; with JUN45: 0.062 μN2; with jj44b: 0.119 μN2

## Context
This work extends previous collinear laser spectroscopy measurements of nickel charge radii by Kaufmann (2020) and Malbrunot-Ettenauer (2022) to electromagnetic moments, and follows similar studies on antimony isotopes by Lechner (2023) where two-body-current contributions were identified as necessary for improving magnetic moment calculations. The results demonstrate that ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations including two-body currents can outperform phenomenological shell-model calculations using effective g-factors in the nickel region, enabling future benchmarks for nuclear interactions and electromagnetic current operators in medium-mass nuclei.
