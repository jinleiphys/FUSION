# The Folding Deuteron Optical Model Potentials

> For 52 target nuclei with deuteron as projectile, we calculate the reaction cross sections and elastic scattering angular distributions, as well as the $χ^2$ values for 11 kinds of deuteron optical model potentials: our global deuteron optical potentials and 10 folding optical potentials calculated with 2 phenomenological global nucleon optical potentials given by Koning \textit{et al}(KD) and by Varner\textit{et al}(CH89), and 8 microscopic nucleon optical potentials with the generalized Skyrme force parameters(GS1-6) and modified Skyrme force parameters(SKa, SKb). We find that for constructing the folding deuteron optical potential, both SKa and SKb are the best Skyrme force parameters of the microscopic nucleon optical potential proposed by Q. Shen \textit{et al}.

# The Folding Deuteron Optical Model Potentials

## Key claim
The microscopic nucleon optical potential with the modified Skyrme force parameters SKa and SKb are the best choices for constructing folding deuteron optical potentials, performing slightly better than the phenomenological global nucleon optical potentials KD and CH89, but only for the 52 target nuclei and energy range below 200 MeV studied in this work.

## Method
The authors calculated deuteron reaction cross sections and elastic scattering angular distributions for 52 target nuclei using 11 different deuteron optical model potentials: one global phenomenological potential (GL) and 10 folding potentials constructed by folding neutron and proton optical potentials. The folding model used a numerical deuteron ground state wave function solved with a Gaussian interaction potential, with the nucleon optical potentials taken from two phenomenological sources (Koning et al. KD; Varner et al. CH89) and eight microscopic potentials using generalized Skyrme force parameters (GS1-6) and modified Skyrme force parameters (SKa, SKb). The quality of each potential was evaluated using chi-squared values comparing calculated results to experimental data from the EXFOR database.

## Key numbers
- 52 target nuclei were studied, ranging from light to heavy masses
- The deuteron binding energy used in the wave function calculation was 2.2259 MeV, with the Gaussian potential depth adjusted to 72.194 MeV (from 72.15 MeV in the reference) to reproduce this binding energy
- The spin-orbit coupling used the pion Compton wavelength squared value of 2.0 fm^2
- Calculations were performed in the non-relativistic frame for energies below 200 MeV, with relativistic corrections considered negligible
- For experimental data without reported errors, a 10% relative error was assumed
- Weights of Wel=2.0 and Wnon=0.1 were applied for elastic scattering angular distributions and reaction cross sections, respectively
- For both the average chi-squared and the average relative chi-squared across all 52 nuclei, SKa and SKb showed the smallest values among the folding potentials, smaller than those of KD and CH89

## Context
This work extends the global deuteron optical potential approach of An and Cai (2006) by testing folding potentials that require no free parameters, making them more reliable for nuclei far from stability where experimental data are scarce. The microscopic nucleon optical potential method was developed by Shen et al. (1981) and previously applied to neutron projectiles, where GS2 and SKa were found best; this study identifies SKa as the best Skyrme parameter for both neutron and deuteron projectiles. The results enable confident predictions of cross sections and angular distributions for nuclei lacking experimental data, particularly those outside the applicability range of the global deuteron optical potential.
