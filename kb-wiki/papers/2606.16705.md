# Diffractive structure functions from JIMWLK evolution

> We compute diffractive structure functions from Wilson line configurations whose energy evolution is given by the JIMWLK equation. We use a JIMWLK evolution setup that has already been constrained with exclusive vector meson production data from HERA. We compare our results to HERA measurements and also extended to heavy nuclei. In particular we can calculate predictions for the nuclear modification factor and diffractive-to-total cross section ratios at the EIC.

## Key claim

The JIMWLK evolution, constrained by exclusive vector meson production data from HERA, provides a good description of the proton diffractive reduced cross section at low Q² but shows a faster Q² evolution than the data; the main result is the prediction of a strong nuclear suppression for diffractive structure functions of the gold nucleus in EIC kinematics.

## Method

The authors compute diffractive structure functions from Wilson line configurations on a transverse lattice, using an MV model initial condition with IP-Glasma geometry and evolving the Wilson lines via the JIMWLK equation in its Langevin formulation. The diffractive qqbar production cross section is discretized on the lattice and evaluated for longitudinally and transversely polarized virtual photons, with the proton slope parameter B extracted from an exponential fit to the t-dependence in the region |t| < 0.5 GeV². The setup contains no free parameters aside from the quark masses, having been fitted to exclusive J/psi production data by Mäntysaari (2022).

## Key numbers

* Proton reduced cross section xpom sigma_r^D(3) computed at sqrt(s) = 318 GeV² and compared to HERA combined data at four values of Q²
* Proton slope parameter B extracted from fit to |t| < 0.5 GeV² region, compared to H1 FPS data at Q² in [5,10] GeV² (HERA I) and Q² in [4,110] GeV² (HERA II)
* Nuclear modification factor for gold nucleus (A=197) computed at beta = 0.56, Q² = 15.3 GeV² and t = 0, showing strongest suppression for the transverse structure function
* Nuclear modification factor compared at two values of Q², with stronger suppression at smaller Q²

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture approach to diffractive structure functions, building on previous calculations for inclusive F₂ by Lappi (2013) that showed similar systematics in Q² evolution. The JIMWLK evolution setup had been previously constrained by exclusive J/psi production data from Mäntysaari (2022). The predictions for the nuclear modification factor and diffractive-to-total cross section ratios are directly relevant for future EIC measurements as outlined by Accardi (2012), and the same framework can be used to compute the eA-to-ep double ratio of diffractive-to-total cross sections.
