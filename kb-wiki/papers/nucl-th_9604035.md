# Excess electron pairs from heavy-ion collisions at CERN and a more complete picture of thermal production

> The low-mass dielectron signal from heavy-ion collisions at the CERN--SPS reported by the {\em CERES} collaboration is in excess of estimated hadronic decays suggestive of possible contribution from two-pion annihilation or other hadronic reactions. In the absence of dramatic medium modifications, annihilation alone is unable to account for the data. We explore the role of pion plus resonance scattering [$πρ\to a_{1}(1260) \to πe^+e^-$] which has favorable kinematics to populate masses between $2m_π$ and $m_ρ$. While it seems to account for some of the remaining excess beyond annihilation, it fails to allow quantitative interpretation of data.

## Key claim
The excess low-mass dielectron signal observed by CERES in S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/u cannot be fully explained by pion-pion annihilation alone; the additional contribution from pion-rho scattering through the a1(1260) resonance [πρ → a1(1260) → πe+e-] accounts for part of the remaining excess but still fails to provide a quantitative interpretation of the data.

## Method
The author calculates thermal dielectron production rates from the two-loop process πρ → πe+e- using an effective Lagrangian for axial-vector-vector-pseudoscalar interaction with vector dominance, folding thermal momentum distributions with transition amplitudes. The rates are integrated over a boost-invariant expanding fire cylinder with initial temperature Ti=188 MeV, critical temperature Tc=160 MeV, freeze-out temperature Tf=140 MeV, and pion chemical potential μπ=135 MeV, applying experimental kinematic cuts (pT > 200 MeV/c, pseudorapidity 2.1 < η < 2.65) to compare with CERES data.

## Key numbers
- The CERES collaboration reported an enhancement factor of 5.0 ± 2.7 over hadronic decay contributions when integrated over dielectron mass from 0.2 to 1.5 GeV for S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/u
- The coupling constants used are ga1πρ = 16.1 GeV-1 and ga1πγ = 0.81 GeV-1 for ma1 = 1230 MeV
- The predicted radiative decay width Γa1→πγ is 1.9 MeV, compared to a measured value of 0.640 ± 0.246 MeV
- At the rho mass, two-pion annihilation rates are a factor of four above πρ scattering rates at T = 160 MeV
- The initial temperature is taken as Ti = 188 MeV, with Tc = 160 MeV and Tf = 140 MeV, and μπ = 135 MeV
- The average charged particle pseudorapidity density used for normalization is ⟨dNcharged/dη⟩ = 125

## Context
This work extends calculations by Gale and Lichard (1994) on lowest-order hadronic dielectron production by including the next-to-leading order process πρ → πe+e- through a1 resonance formation. It addresses the discrepancy between the CERES data (Agakichiev et al., 1995) and estimates from hadronic decays and ππ annihilation alone. The results indicate that quantitative interpretation of the CERES data will require consideration of additional processes such as ππ → ργ*, ππ → πγ*, bremsstrahlung, and possibly medium modifications to hadron masses and widths.
