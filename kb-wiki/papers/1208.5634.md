# Decays of Fourth Generation Bound States

> We consider the decay modes of the heavy $q^\prime\bar{q^\prime}$ bound states originating from Higgs boson exchange between quark -- anti-quark pair. In case of a small coupling between the fourth and lower generation the main decay mode is $q^\prime\bar{q^\prime}$ annihilation. We show that for a vector state the dominant decay modes are Higgs-gamma and Higgs-Z decays, while for a pseudoscalar state the strong two-gluon decay mode dominates. The bound states are very narrow. The ratio of the total width to the binding energy is less than 1% if we are not extremely close to the critical quark mass where the binding energy is very small. The discussed decay modes exist for any fermion-antifermion bound states including heavy leptons and heavy neutrinos if their masses are high enough to form a bound state due to attractive Higgs boson exchange potential.

# Decays of Fourth Generation Bound States

## Key claim
For heavy fourth-generation quark-antiquark bound states formed via Higgs boson exchange, the dominant decay modes are Higgs-gamma and Higgs-Z for vector states and two-gluon decay for pseudoscalar states, but only under the condition that mixing with lower generations is small; the bound states are very narrow, with the ratio of total width to binding energy less than 1% unless extremely close to the critical quark mass where binding energy becomes very small.

## Method
The authors calculated decay widths for vector (S=1) and pseudoscalar (S=0) bound states of heavy fourth-generation quarks using non-relativistic quarkonium wave functions obtained variationally from a hydrogen-type trial function. They computed the annihilation amplitudes for Higgs-gamma, Higgs-Z, three-gluon, and two-W-boson decays at zero relative momentum, expressing the widths in terms of the wave function at the origin, and evaluated the ratios of these widths as functions of fermion mass in the range 500-1000 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For vector states, the ratio of Higgs-gamma decay width to three-gluon width dominates in the whole mass interval 500-1000 GeV for t-prime quarkonium.
- For b-prime quarkonium, the Higgs-gamma to three-gluon ratio is smaller by a factor of 4 due to the electric charge of b-prime.
- The Higgs-Z width is about one order of magnitude smaller than the Higgs-gamma width for t-prime states, suppressed by the factor (1/4 - (2/3) sin^2 theta_W)^2 which equals 0.009.
- For b-prime bound states, the corresponding factor for HZ decay is 3.25 times larger than for t-prime, making the HZ-width comparable to the H-gamma width.
- The ratio of total width to binding energy for vector states is smaller than 1% across the whole mass interval.
- For pseudoscalar states, the two-W-boson width is approximately 0.003 times the two-gluon width at all fermion masses in the discussed range.
- The Higgs mass used in calculations is 125.3 GeV.
- The non-relativistic approach can be used for quark masses up to 1000 GeV; for heavier masses relativistic effects become significant.

## Context
This work extends the study of Higgs-induced bound states of heavy fermions, which was previously considered in numerous publications including Iwaki (2011) and Fukano (2011), by calculating specific exotic decay modes for color-singlet q-prime q-prime-bar states. The authors note that the mass difference between t-prime and b-prime quarks is expected to be less than the W mass, as discussed by Kribs (2007), and that CKM matrix elements between fourth and lower generations could be small, as discussed by Barger, Gao, and Marfatia (2012). The decay of color-octet states was discussed by Enomoto, Higuchi, and Yamada (2011). These results enable experimental searches at the LHC for heavy bound states via detection of a single high-energy gamma quantum as a clear signal, and the decay modes discussed also apply to bound states of heavy leptons and heavy neutrinos if their masses are high enough to form bound states via Higgs exchange.
