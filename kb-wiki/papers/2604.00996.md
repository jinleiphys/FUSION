# Pions reloaded

## Key claim

The authors present a novel version of the pion Bethe-Salpeter equation in the chiral limit that exactly fulfills the constraints imposed by the axial Ward-Takahashi identities, both formally and numerically, using state-of-the-art QCD correlation functions as input. The approach relies on the symmetric-vertex (SV) approximation, which preserves chiral symmetry while allowing fully-dressed quark-gluon vertices with all form factors dynamically evaluated.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled system consisting of the quark-gluon vertex Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE), the quark gap equation, and the pion Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE), all within the symmetric-vertex approximation. In this truncation, the axial-vector-gluon vertex $G_5^{\mu\nu}$ is simplified by keeping only diagrams involving the axial-vector vertex $\Gamma_5^\mu$, with a compensating replacement of the quark-gluon vertex by $\gamma_\mu V(q)$, where $V(q)$ coincides with the symmetric limit of the classical form factor $\lambda_1$. The gluon propagator is taken from lattice simulations of Aguilar (2021), and the three-gluon vertex uses the planar-degeneracy property with a form factor $L_{sg}(s)$ fitted from Aguilar (2023). The resulting pion BSE contains three diagrams: a dressed rainbow-ladder contribution and two additional diagrams that ensure symmetry preservation.

## Key numbers

* The pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude component $\chi_1(p)$ satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity relation $\chi_1(p) = 2B(p)$ to better than 1% accuracy, confirming the symmetry-preserving nature of the approach.
* At $P^2 = 0$ (massless pion in the chiral limit), the eigenvalue $\lambda(P^2)$ of the BSE satisfies $\lambda(0) = 1$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Miramontes (2025) for self-consistent inclusion of dressed correlation functions in meson dynamics, and the simplified version by Ferreira (2025). It goes beyond the standard rainbow-ladder (RL) approximation of Maris (1997, 1999) and Eichmann (2025) by incorporating fully-dressed quark-gluon vertices with all tensorial structures. The approach enables future studies of massive pions and other mesons using symmetry-preserving truncations with realistic QCD correlation functions from functional methods and lattice simulations.
