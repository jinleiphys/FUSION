# Simulating Scattering of Composite Particles

> We develop a non-perturbative approach to simulating scattering on classical and quantum computers, in which the initial and final states contain a fixed number of composite particles. The construction is designed to mimic a particle collision, wherein two composite particles are brought in contact. The initial states are assembled via consecutive application of operators creating eigenstates of the interacting theory from vacuum. These operators are defined with the aid of the Møller wave operator, which can be constructed using such methods as adiabatic state preparation or double commutator flow equation. The approach is well-suited for studying strongly coupled systems in both relativistic and non-relativistic settings. For relativistic systems, we employ the language of light-front quantization, which has been previously used for studying the properties of individual bound states, as well as for simulating their scattering in external fields, and is now adopted to the studies of scattering of bound state systems. For simulations on classical computers, we describe an algorithm for calculating exact (in the sense of a given discretized theory) scattering probabilities, which has cost (memory and time) exponential in momentum grid size. Such calculations may be interesting in their own right and can be used for benchmarking results of a quantum simulation algorithm, which is the main application of the developed framework. We illustrate our ideas with an application to the $ϕ^4$ theory in $1+1\rm D$.

## Key claim

The authors develop a non-perturbative framework for simulating scattering of composite particles on classical and quantum computers, where initial and final states contain a fixed number of bound states that are eigenstates of the interacting theory. The approach is designed to mimic a particle collision by preparing composite particles separately and then bringing them into contact within a combined system, where the resulting multi-particle state is not an eigenstate of the combined interacting Hamiltonian and therefore undergoes non-trivial time evolution.

## Method

The authors construct creation operators for eigenstates of the full interacting Hamiltonian using the Møller wave operator, which can be implemented via adiabatic state preparation, unitary coupled cluster methods, or double commutator flow equations. For relativistic systems, they employ light-front quantization with boost-invariant relative coordinates, and for classical computers they describe an algorithm for calculating exact scattering probabilities with cost exponential in momentum grid size. The framework is illustrated with an application to ϕ⁴ theory in 1+1 dimensions using Discretized Light-Cone Quantization (DLCQ).

## Key numbers

- The number of elementary operator terms in second-quantized discretized Hamiltonians scales as O(N^g) for local theories in the plane wave basis, where N is the size of the discretization grid and g is the highest power of the interaction term (e.g., 4 in ϕ⁴ theory)
- The number of terms in first-quantized discretized Hamiltonians contains O(N) terms for local theories and O(N²) terms for non-local interactions such as Coulomb interactions
- In the transverse lattice formulation, the number of Hamiltonian terms scales as O(K^(g-1) N⊥), compared to O(K^(g-1) N⊥^(g-1)) in DLCQ or O(K^(g-1) N⊥^g) in BLFQ

## Context

This work extends the light-front quantization approach previously used for studying individual bound states and their scattering in external fields (Brodsky 1997) to the scattering of bound state systems. The framework builds upon digitization schemes for quantum field theory (Klco 2018, Liu 2021) and the BLFQ basis (Vary 2013), and enables quantum simulation of strongly coupled systems in both relativistic and non-relativistic settings. The approach can be used for benchmarking quantum simulation algorithms against exact classical calculations on small momentum grids.
