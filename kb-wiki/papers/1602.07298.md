# An Effective Field Theory Approach to the Stabilization of $^8$Be in a QED Plasma

> We use effective field theory to study the $\mathrmα$-$\mathrmα$ resonant scattering in a finite-temperature QED plasma. The static plasma screening effect causes the resonance state $^8$Be to live longer and eventually leads to the formation of a bound state when $m_D\gtrsim 0.3$ MeV. We speculate that this effect may have implications on the rates of cosmologically and astrophysically relevant nuclear reactions involving $\mathrmα$ particles.

# An Effective Field Theory Approach to the Stabilization of $^8$Be in a QED Plasma

## Key claim

The static screening effect of a finite-temperature QED plasma causes the $^8$Be resonance in $\alpha$-$\alpha$ scattering to live longer and eventually form a bound state when the Debye mass $m_D \gtrsim 0.3$ MeV, but the authors note this is incomplete because only static screening is considered and dynamic screening effects such as thermal width from collisions with electrons/positrons remain to be studied.

## Method

The authors construct a pionless effective field theory (EFT) for non-relativistic $\alpha$ particles with contact interactions, including Coulomb repulsion via covariant derivatives. They fit the EFT parameters (scattering length $a$, effective range $r_0$, shape parameter $P_0$) to experimental $s$-wave phase shift data up to $E_{CM}=3$ MeV, then replace the zero-temperature Coulomb potential with a screened Yukawa potential $V_C = (Z_1 Z_2 \alpha / r) e^{-m_D r}$ and compute the nuclear phase shift by numerically solving the quantum mechanical scattering problem with a delta-function plus Yukawa potential, matching asymptotic wave functions to extract the Yukawa Green's function.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energy in vacuum: $E_0 = 91.84 \pm 0.04$ keV (experimental), best fit gives $91.838$ keV
- Resonance width in vacuum: $\Gamma_0 = 5.57 \pm 0.25$ eV (experimental), best fit gives $5.715$ eV
- Resonance lifetime in vacuum: $1.2 \times 10^{-16}$ s
- $\alpha$ particle mass: $M \approx 3727.38$ MeV
- Best fit scattering length: $a = -2.029 \times 10^3$ fm
- Best fit effective range: $r_0 = 1.104$ fm
- Best fit shape parameter: $P_0 = -1.824$ fm$^3$
- Bound state formation threshold: $m_D \gtrsim 0.3$ MeV
- Debye mass for relativistic $e^-e^+$ plasma (HTL approximation): $m_D^{HTL} = \sqrt{4\pi\alpha/3} \, T$

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach to $\alpha$-$\alpha$ scattering developed by Higa et al. (2008) and the Coulomb-modified EFT formalism of Kong and Ravndal (1999) to finite-temperature QED plasmas. The results may be relevant to the "lithium problem" in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, where plasma screening effects on non-resonant reactions were previously shown negligible by Itoh et al. (1997) and Wang et al. (2010), and to stellar nucleosynthesis in the helium burning stage where longer-lived $^8$Be could increase the probability of $^{12}$C formation. The authors note that a coupled-channel EFT for the $^7$Be(n,p)$^7$Li reaction in vacuum was derived by Lensky and Birse (2011), and that including dynamic screening effects (thermal width from plasma collisions) is left for future work.
