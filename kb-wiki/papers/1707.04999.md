# Dihadron fragmentation functions in the quark-jet model: Longitudinally polarized quarks

> We study the dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFF) of longitudinally polarized quarks into pion pairs. The quark-jet framework is used to model the sequential hadronization of a polarized quark into hadrons, where the polarization transfer to the remnant quark in each hadron emission step is calculated using the spin density matrix formalism. Using Monte Carlo (MC) simulations of the hadronization process, we find a nonvanishing helicity dependent DiFF, $G_1^\perp$ , which is also related to the longitudinal jet handedness. A method is developed for extracting the angular moments of this DiFF, which enter the expressions for the azimuthal asymmetries for an electron-positron annihilation process into two pairs of hadrons from back-to-back jets and the dihadron production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering. Finally, we derive explicit integral expressions for DiFFs where only two hadrons are emitted by a quark and use them to validate our MC results for both unpolarized and helicity dependent DiFFs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a nonvanishing helicity-dependent dihadron fragmentation function $G_1^\perp$ exists for longitudinally polarized quarks fragmenting into pion pairs, but the signal decreases with increasing number of produced hadrons due to destructive interference, making it potentially measurable only in the large-$z$ region.

## Method

The authors used Monte Carlo simulations within the quark-jet framework, where sequential hadron emission from a polarized quark is modeled with polarization transfer to the remnant quark calculated using spin density matrix formalism. The input quark-to-quark splitting functions were derived from the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective theory. The dihadron fragmentation functions were extracted from polarization-dependent number densities by analyzing azimuthal modulations, and the results were validated against explicit integral expressions derived for the two-step hadronization process.

## Key numbers

* For $N_L=2$ (two hadrons produced), the number of $\pi^+\pi^-$ pairs per event is $\frac{4}{9} \approx 0.44444$, matching the analytic calculation
* For $N_L=6$, the number of $\pi^+\pi^-$ pairs per event is $\frac{3068}{729} \approx 4.2085$ without cuts, and approximately 1.18162 with cuts $z_{1,2} \geq 0.1$
* The ratio $\tilde{G}_1^\perp(z)/D_1(z)$ for $\pi^+\pi^-$ pairs with $N_L=6$ and $z_{1,2} \geq 0.1$ shows a significant signal only in the large-$z$ region, with the analyzing power slightly increased in the mid-$z$ range compared to the case without cuts
* $\pi^+\pi^+$ pairs exhibit an opposite sign and similar magnitude to $\pi^+\pi^-$ pairs in the ratio $\tilde{G}_1^\perp(z)/D_1(z)$ for $N_L=6$

## Context

This work extends the quark-jet model studies of dihadron fragmentation functions from unpolarized (Matevosyan 2013) and transversely polarized quarks (Matevosyan 2013) to longitudinally polarized quarks. It uses the self-consistent polarized quark hadronization framework developed by Bentz (2016) and the Monte Carlo implementation by Matevosyan (2016). The results are relevant for interpreting BELLE experiment measurements (Vossen 2011) that showed no significant signal for the asymmetry involving $G_1^\perp$, and COMPASS experiment results (Sirtl 2017) that also found no significant SIDIS asymmetry involving helicity-dependent DiFF. The work enables extraction of helicity parton distribution functions from two-hadron SIDIS measurements and provides predictions for the longitudinal jet handedness predicted by Efremov (1992).
