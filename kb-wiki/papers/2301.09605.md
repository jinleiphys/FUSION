# Nuclear three-body short-range correlations in coordinate space

> We study the effects of three-nucleon short-range correlations on nuclear coordinate-space densities. For this purpose, novel three-body densities are calculated for ground state nuclei using the auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo method. The results are analyzed in terms of the Generalized Contact Formalism, extended to include three-body correlations, revealing the universal behavior of nucleon triplets at short distances. We identify the quantum numbers of such correlated triplets and extract scaling factors of triplet abundances that can be compared to upcoming inclusive electron scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) to include three-nucleon short-range correlations, showing that at short distances the three-body density of nuclei factorizes into a universal, nucleus-independent part multiplied by a nucleus-specific scaling factor (the three-body contact). The authors claim that a single dominant channel with quantum numbers parity positive, total angular momentum 1/2, and total isospin 1/2 governs the behavior, but this is conditional on the dominance of zero angular-momentum components and Pauli blocking of the isospin 3/2 and spin 3/2 channels.

## Method

The authors used the auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method to calculate three-body coordinate-space densities for the ground states of ³He, ⁴He, ⁶Li, and ¹⁶O, employing the N2LO local chiral interaction with the E1 parametrization of the three-body force (primarily the R₀ = 1.0 fm cutoff, with some results for R₀ = 1.2 fm). They then analyzed these densities within the Generalized Contact Formalism extended to three-body correlations, fitting the short-distance behavior to extract three-body contact ratios that quantify the abundance of short-range correlated triplets relative to ³He.

## Key numbers

* For ⁴He, the per-nucleon t=1/2 three-body contact ratio (relative to ³He) is 3.8 ± 0.3.
* For ⁶Li, the per-nucleon t=1/2 three-body contact ratio (relative to ³He) is 3.1 ± 0.3.
* For ¹⁶O, the per-nucleon t=1/2 three-body contact ratio (relative to ³He) is 4.2 ± 0.5.
* For ¹⁶O, the per-nucleon t=3/2 three-body contact ratio (relative to ⁶Li) is 5.4 ± 0.8.
* In ⁶Li, the contribution of t=1/2 triplets for distances r < 1 fm is larger than 95% of all triplets, compared to the combinatorial fraction of 80%.
* The universal short-distance behavior of the t=1/2 three-body density holds for r ≲ 1.1 fm for all nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF), previously developed for two-nucleon short-range correlations by Weiss et al. (2015, 2016), to include three-nucleon correlations. It provides the first ab-initio verification of the three-body factorization ansatz using quantum Monte Carlo calculations, complementing ongoing experimental efforts to measure three-nucleon short-range correlations in inclusive electron scattering (Arrington E12-06-105, Ye 2018, Sargsian 2019). The extracted contact ratios provide predictions for the a₃ scaling factor that can be compared to upcoming experimental data, and the results contradict a relation proposed by Sargsian et al. (2012) that connects two-body and three-body abundances (which predicted a₃(⁴He) ≈ 3.15, smaller than the value obtained here).
