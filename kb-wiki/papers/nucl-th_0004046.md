# Why is the three-nucleon force so odd?

> By considering a class of diagrams which has been overlooked also in the most recent literature on three-body forces, we extract a new contribution to the three-nucleon interaction which specifically acts on the triplet odd states of the two nucleon subsystem. In the static approximation, this 3N-force contribution is fixed by the underlying 2N interaction, so in principle there are no free parameters to adjust. The 2N amplitude however enters in the 3NF diagram in a form which cannot be directly accessed or constrained by NN phase-shift analysis. We conclude that this new 3N-force contribution provides a mechanism which implies that the presence of the third nucleon modifies the p-wave (and possibly the f-wave) components of the 2N subsystem in the triplet-isotriplet channels.

## Key claim

The authors identify a new class of three-nucleon force (3NF) diagrams that have been overlooked in modern calculations, and they argue that this contribution specifically modifies the triplet odd states (p-waves and possibly f-waves) of the two-nucleon subsystem. The effect is not freely adjustable because it is fixed by the underlying two-nucleon interaction in the static approximation, but the two-nucleon amplitude enters in a form that cannot be directly constrained by NN phase-shift analysis.

## Method

The authors start from a formalism that explicitly includes pion dynamics in the three-nucleon system (Canton, 1998) and project out the pion degrees of freedom to obtain a modified AGS-type three-body equation. They analyze the irreducible three-nucleon force diagrams that emerge from this projection, focusing on a class where a two-nucleon correlation occurs while a pion is "in flight." They subtract the irreducible Born diagram to account for known cancellation effects from mesonic retardation (Yang, 1986; Coon, 1986), and derive the partial-wave decomposition of the resulting 3NF contribution in the channel-spin coupling scheme.

## Key numbers

- The 3NF contribution acts only when the two-nucleon pair is in a triplet state for both spin and isospin, which by the Pauli principle restricts the pair to odd orbital angular momentum (p-waves, f-waves, etc.)
- The static approximation replaces the pion propagator Green's functions with -1/ω_π, where ω_π = √(m_π² + Q²)
- The two-nucleon t-matrix in the diagram is evaluated at an energy shifted by the spectator kinetic energy and the pion mass m_π (approximately 140 MeV)
- The Fourier-Bessel transforms I_L(q,q') and I_LL'(q,q') involve integrals over Yukawa functions e^(-m_π R)/R with the pion mass m_π

## Context

This work extends the analysis of three-nucleon forces beyond the standard Fujita-Miyazawa diagram and the general pion-rescattering diagrams that have been the focus of modern calculations (Friar, 1999; Huber, 1999). The authors address the long-standing puzzle of vector analyzing powers (A_y0 and iT_11) in nucleon-deuteron scattering, which existing 3NF models fail to describe correctly. Tornow (1998) suggested that the NN interaction in the ³P_J states might need modification, while Friar (1998) concluded that a new 3NF of spin-orbit type is required; the present work offers an alternative mechanism that selectively modifies triplet odd states of the two-nucleon subsystem.
