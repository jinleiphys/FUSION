# Analysis of heavy hyperhydrogen 6^H_Lambda

> We have revisited the possible existence and the binding mechanism of heavy hyperhydrogen 6^H_Lambda. This Lambda hypernucleus is enriched in information about Lambda hypernuclear dynamics which would shed a light on significant role of coherent Lambda-Sigma coupling effect in neutron-rich nuclear medium, discussed by the authors. Recently, reports on an experimental evidence of 6^H_Lambda with theoretical comparison have appeared, wherein the significance of the coherent coupling in 6^H_Lambda has been criticized. In this report, we are revising our previous work and give further theoretical analysis to confirm the influence of the Lambda N - Sigma N coupling effect in the binding mechanism of 6^H_Lambda.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the coherent Lambda-Sigma coupling effect is essential for binding the heavy hyperhydrogen 6^H_Lambda, and that the FINUDA experimental data do not conclusively contradict their earlier prediction because one of the three candidate events is theoretically unlikely to be a ground-state weak decay.

## Method

The authors performed a three-body calculation of the t-(nn)-Lambda system using phenomenological Gaussian potentials to reproduce Dalitz's original 1963 result, then modified the interactions to reflect the known resonance nature of 5H and the accepted binding of 4_Lambda_H. They also applied an extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method with coupled Lambda-Sigma mean-fields derived from the NSC97f potential to compute the binding energy and coherent Sigma component of 6_Lambda_H.

## Key numbers

- The 5H resonance lies at 1.7 ± 0.3 MeV above the n+n+t threshold with a width of 1.9 ± 0.4 MeV.
- The FINUDA experiment determined the 6_Lambda_H binding energy relative to 5H+Lambda to be B_Lambda = 4.0 ± 1.1 MeV.
- The Myint-Akaishi (M-A) prediction from 2002 gave a bound state 2.1 MeV below the 4_Lambda_H+2n threshold.
- When the coherent Lambda-Sigma coupling term is turned off, the Lambda-separation energy from 5H reduces to 4.4 MeV, and the state is only 0.7 MeV below the threshold; the coupling adds 1.4 MeV attraction to the ground state.
- The original Dalitz prediction from 1963 was 4.2 MeV binding from the old 5H+Lambda threshold; after modifying the interactions to reflect the 5H resonance, the Dalitz value reduces to 3.26 MeV measured from the t+2n+Lambda zero level.
- The coherent Lambda-Sigma coupling effect in 6_Lambda_H was found to be 1.4 MeV with 1.6% coherent Sigma component in the extended BHF calculation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier prediction (Myint and Akaishi, 2002) of a particle-stable 6_Lambda_H based on realistic Lambda N-Sigma N dynamics, and responds to the FINUDA experimental evidence (Agnello et al., 2012) which claimed the state is close to Dalitz's (1963) prediction and deviates from the M-A result. The authors argue that the "modified Dalitz" shift used by Agnello et al. is not dynamically justified, and that the coherent Lambda-Sigma coupling effect is required to explain the data. This analysis enables future experimental tests at J-PARC and further theoretical investigations of neutron-rich hypernuclei such as 10_Lambda_Li (Saha et al., 2005), with implications for Lambda-Sigma hyperon-mixing in neutron-star matter (Shinmura et al., 2002).
