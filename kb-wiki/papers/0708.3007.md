# On the measurement of B(E2, 0+ -> 2+) using intermediate-energy Coulomb excitation

> Coulomb excitation is a standard method used to extract quadrupole excitation strengths of even-even nuclei. In typical analyses the reaction is assumed to be one-step, Coulomb only, and is treated within a semi-classical model. In this work, fully-quantal coupled-channel calculations are performed for three test cases in order to determine the importance of multi-step effects, nuclear contributions, feeding from other states and corrections to the semi-classical approximation. We study the excitation of 30S, 58Ni and 78Kr on 197Au at ~ 50 AMeV. We find that nuclear effects may contribute more than 10% and that feeding contributions can be larger than 15%. These corrections do not alter significantly the published B(E2) values, however an additional theoretical error of up to 13% should be added to the experimental uncertainty if the semi-classical model is used. This theoretical error is reduced to less than 7% when performing a quantal coupled-channel analysis.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for intermediate-energy Coulomb excitation of even-even nuclei, nuclear effects can contribute more than 10% and feeding from higher-lying states can exceed 15%, but these corrections do not significantly alter published B(E2) values; however, an additional theoretical error of up to 13% should be added to experimental uncertainties if the semi-classical model is used, reduced to less than 7% when a full quantal coupled-channel analysis is performed.

## Method

Fully-quantal coupled-channel calculations were performed for three test cases (30S, 58Ni, and 78Kr) on a 197Au target at approximately 50 AMeV, including both Coulomb and nuclear couplings for all relevant transitions between excited states. The calculations used the code FRESCO with partial waves up to Lmax=3000-6000 and radial integration to 300 fm, and cross sections were integrated over the experimental angular ranges for each case. Results were compared to the standard semi-classical Alder-Winther theory to quantify the contributions from multi-step effects, nuclear interference, feeding from higher states, and the straight-line trajectory approximation.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear effects contribute 10% for 30S, 12% for 58Ni, and 0.4% for 78Kr in the two-state (ground + 2+1) coupled-channel calculations
- Feeding contributions to the 2+1 cross section are approximately 5% for 30S and 17% for 58Ni
- The straight-line trajectory approximation alone introduces an error in the cross section of up to 6%
- Theoretical error on cross sections is up to 13% when using the first-order semi-classical theory, reduced to less than 7% with full quantal coupled-channel analysis
- For 58Ni, the feeding correction predicted by the full calculation is 33 mb, significantly larger than the 25 mb estimated in the experimental study by Yurkewicz et al.
- For 30S, a reduction of the B(E2) value from 350 e2fm4 to 303 e2fm4 is needed to bring the theoretical cross section into agreement with experiment

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison between intermediate-energy Coulomb excitation and lifetime methods performed by Cook et al. (2006), which focused on experimental accuracy but did not evaluate uncertainties from theoretical approximations. The study quantifies the corrections needed when using the semi-classical Alder-Winther theory (1975) for B(E2) extraction, and demonstrates that full quantal coupled-channel calculations reduce theoretical uncertainties. The results enable more reliable B(E2) measurements for exotic nuclei near the dripline, where nuclear effects at large impact parameters and multi-step breakup effects become important, as shown in studies of halo nuclei by Nunes (1998, 1999) and Hussein et al. (2006).
