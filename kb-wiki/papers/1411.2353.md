# 136Sn and three body forces

> New experimental data on 2+ energies of 136,138Sn confirms the trend of lower 2+ excitation energies of even-even tin isotopes with N > 82 compared to those with N< 82. However, none of the theoretical predictions using both realistic and empirical interactions can reproduce experimental data on excitation energies as well as the transition probabilities (B(E2; 6+ -> 4+)) of these nuclei, simultaneously, apart from one whose matrix elements have been changed empirically to produce mixed seniority states by weakening pairing. We have shown that the experimental result also shows good agreement with the theory in which three body forces have been included in a realistic interaction. The new theoretical results on transition probabilities have been discussed to identify the experimental quantities which will clearly distinguish between different views.

# 136Sn and three body forces

## Key claim

The new experimental data on 2+ energies of 136,138Sn can be reproduced by shell-model calculations that include three-body forces in a realistic interaction (CWG3M), but the critical test to distinguish between different theoretical approaches, specifically whether reduced pairing or three-body effects dominate, will be the measurement of B(E2; 2+ → 0+) transition probabilities, not just the excitation energies.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the Z50N82 model space (valence space above the 132Sn core) using three Hamiltonians: the empirical SMPN interaction (with 26 modified two-body matrix elements out of 2101), the CD-Bonn potential based realistic CWG interaction, and the CWG3M interaction which incorporates three-body monopole corrections into CWG following Zuker's prescription. The calculations used the OXBASH code and were compared with new experimental data from RIKEN RIBF on 2+ energies and B(E2; 6+ → 4+) values for 136,138Sn.

## Key numbers

- Experimental 2+ energy in 136Sn: 682(13) keV, identified via γ-rays in coincidence with one-proton removal from 137Sb
- Experimental B(E2; 6+ → 4+) for 136Sn: 24(4) e²fm⁴
- Experimental B(E2; 6+ → 4+) for 138Sn: approximately 17 e²fm⁴
- CWG3M predicted 2+ energy for 140Sn: 1.889 MeV, close to SMPN prediction of 1.949 MeV, indicating a shell closure at N=90
- CWG3M B(E2; 6+ → 4+) for 136Sn: 45 e²fm⁴; for 138Sn: 21 e²fm⁴
- SMPN B(E2; 6+ → 4+) for 136Sn: 14 e²fm⁴; for 138Sn: 20 e²fm⁴
- CWG B(E2; 6+ → 4+) for 136Sn: 14 e²fm⁴; for 138Sn: 12 e²fm⁴
- For 136Sn, the realistic interaction used by the experimental authors (Prl14) differed from the measured B(E2; 6+ → 4+) by a factor greater than 5; three other shell-model calculations failed by at least a factor of 2

## Context

This work extends the authors' systematic shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei above 132Sn, which began in the early 2000s with the SMPN interaction (Sarkar and Sarkar, 2004) and later incorporated three-body effects into the realistic CWG interaction (Sarkar and Sarkar, 2010). The paper directly addresses the recent experimental results from RIKEN RIBF (Prl14, 136Sn) which identified 2+ states and isomeric 6+ states in 136,138Sn. The experimental authors found that only an empirical modification weakening pairing in the 2f7/2 matrix elements could reproduce their data; the present work shows that the three-body inclusive CWG3M interaction also achieves good agreement, and identifies B(E2; 2+ → 0+) measurements as the decisive observable to distinguish between the reduced-pairing and three-body-force interpretations. This connects to broader discussions of three-body forces in nuclear structure, as demonstrated by Otsuka (2010) for oxygen isotopes and by Holt (2013) for pairing gaps.
