# Constraints on the large-x d/u ratio from electron-nucleus scattering at x>1

> Recently the ratio of neutron to proton structure functions F_2n/F_2p was extracted from a phenomenological correlation between the strength of the nuclear EMC effect and inclusive electron-nucleus cross section ratios at x>1. Within conventional models of nuclear smearing, this "in-medium correction" (IMC) extraction constrains the size of nuclear effects in the deuteron structure functions, from which the neutron structure function F_2n is usually extracted. The IMC data determine the resulting proton d/u quark distribution ratio, extrapolated to x=1, to be 0.23 +- 0.09 with a 90% confidence level. This is well below the SU(6) symmetry limit of 1/2 and significantly above the scalar diquark dominance limit of 0.

## Key claim

The IMC extraction of the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) from a phenomenological correlation between the nuclear EMC effect and inclusive electron-nucleus cross section ratios at \(x>1\) constrains the proton \(d/u\) quark distribution ratio at \(x=1\) to \(0.23 \pm 0.09\) at 90% confidence, which is well below the SU(6) symmetry limit of 1/2 and significantly above the scalar diquark dominance limit of 0, but this conclusion depends on the assumptions underlying the IMC extraction.

## Method

The authors combined the IMC-extracted \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) data with the CJ global next-to-leading order QCD fit of parton distribution functions, using a modified Kulagin-Petti parametrization of nucleon off-shell corrections in the deuteron. The off-shell corrections were parametrized by a single swelling parameter \(\delta R/R\) (the change in nucleon confinement radius), and the \(\chi^2\) of the IMC data was calculated for four different deuteron wave functions (CD-Bonn, AV18, WJC-1, WJC-2) with swelling levels ranging from 0% to 3% in steps of 0.3%. The resulting constraints on \(\delta R/R\) were then mapped onto the \(d/u\) ratio in the \(x \to 1\) limit.

## Key numbers

- The IMC extraction yields a \(d/u\) limiting value at \(x=1\) of \(0.23 \pm 0.09\) at the 90% confidence level.
- At the 99% confidence level, the uncertainty on the \(d/u\) limiting value increases to \(\pm 0.13\).
- The IMC extraction constrains the nucleon swelling levels to the range \(\delta R/R = 0.2\% - 1.4\%\) at 90% confidence.
- The minimum \(\chi^2\) occurs for the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function at \(\delta R/R = 0.9\%\).
- The average nucleon virtualities \(\delta p^2/M^2\) for the deuteron wave functions are: CD-Bonn \(-3.7\%\), AV18 \(-4.5\%\), WJC-1 \(-6.2\%\), WJC-2 \(-4.9\%\), and Paris \(-4.3\%\).
- The IMC data cover an \(x\) range of approximately \(0.35 - 0.7\) at a fixed \(Q^2 = 12\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the CJ global QCD analysis (CJ Collaboration, 2011) by incorporating the IMC extraction of \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) from the correlation between the nuclear EMC effect and short-range correlated nucleon-nucleon pairs (IMC, 2011). The results disfavor deuteron wave functions with very hard momentum distributions, such as the WJC-1 potential, and support the presence of nucleon off-shell modifications in the deuteron. Future experiments that could provide direct constraints include the BoNuS experiment at Jefferson Lab (up to \(x \approx 0.6\)), its 12 GeV extension (up to \(x \approx 0.8\)), the MARATHON experiment on \(^3\)He-\(^3\)He mirror nuclei, and parity-violating DIS on hydrogen.
