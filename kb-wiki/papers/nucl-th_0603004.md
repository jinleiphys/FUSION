# Effect of three-body elastic scattering on heavy quark momentum degradation in the quark-gluon plasma

> Heavy quark drag coefficients in the quark-gluon plasma are evaluated in the perturbative QCD. It is found that for charm quarks the contribution from three-body elastic scattering is comparable to those from two-body elastic and radiative scatterings while for bottom quarks it becomes dominant. Using a schematic expanding fireball model, effects of both two-body and three-body scatterings on the transverse momentum spectra of heavy quarks produced in Au+Au collisions at center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV are studied. Results on electrons from resulting heavy meson decays are compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim
Three-body elastic scattering contributes comparably to two-body elastic and radiative scatterings for charm quark momentum degradation in the quark-gluon plasma, and becomes the dominant contribution for bottom quarks, but this conclusion depends on an approximation that the dominant diagrams for processes involving gluons are similar to those for scattering by quarks and antiquarks.

## Method
The authors evaluate heavy quark drag coefficients in the quark-gluon plasma using perturbative QCD at leading order, including two-body elastic scattering, two-body radiative scattering, and three-body elastic scattering. For three-body scattering, they include collisional widths in intermediate quark propagators to ensure off-shell conditions, and approximate the contributions from processes involving gluons by assuming dominance of t-channel gluon-exchange diagrams. The resulting drag coefficients are then used in a Fokker-Planck equation to evolve heavy quark transverse momentum spectra through a schematic expanding fireball model of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark collisional width: approximately 75 MeV at momentum 4 GeV/c and T=175 MeV, increasing to about 130 MeV at T=350 MeV
- Bottom quark collisional width: approximately 49 MeV at momentum 4 GeV/c and T=175 MeV, increasing to about 93 MeV at T=350 MeV
- Light quark width is about 50% larger than charm quark width at the same conditions
- Three-body elastic scattering by quarks and antiquarks with different flavors increases quark widths by at most 10%
- QGP formation time tau_0 = 0.6 fm, initial temperature T_i = 350 MeV, critical temperature T_c = 175 MeV reached at proper time tau_c ~ 5 fm
- Initial transverse radius R_0 = 7 fm, transverse acceleration a = 0.1 c^2/fm
- Number of binary collisions in Au+Au collisions: approximately 960

## Context
This work extends previous studies of heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma that considered only two-body elastic scattering (Moore 2005, Molnar 2004, Zhang 2005) and two-body radiative scattering (Wicks 2005, Thoma 1991). It builds on earlier findings that three-body elastic scatterings are more efficient than two-body scattering for thermalization of light partons (Xu 2005). The results enable improved comparison with experimental data on electrons from heavy meson decays measured by PHENIX (Adler 2005) and STAR (Bielcik 2005) at RHIC, showing that including three-body scattering brings the calculated nuclear modification factor into reasonable agreement with measurements.
