# Momentum-space probability density of ${}^6$He in Halo Effective Field Theory

> We compute the momentum-space probability density of ${}^6$He at leading order in Halo EFT. In this framework, the ${}^6$He nucleus is treated as a three-body problem with a ${}^4$He core ($c$) and two valence neutrons ($n$). This requires the $nn$ and $nc$ t-matrices as well as a $cnn$ force as input in the Faddeev equations. Since the $nc$ t-matrix corresponds to an energy-dependent potential, we consider the consequent modifications to the standard normalization and orthogonality conditions. We find that these are small for momenta within the domain of validity of Halo EFT. In this regime, the ${}^6$He probability density is regulator independent, provided the cutoff is significantly above the EFT breakdown scale.

## Key claim

The momentum-space probability density of ${}^6$He computed at leading order in Halo EFT is regulator independent for momenta within the EFT's domain of validity, provided the cutoff is significantly above the EFT breakdown scale; the modifications to the standard normalization and orthogonality conditions arising from the energy-dependent $n\alpha$ t-matrix are small in this regime.

## Method

The authors treat ${}^6$He as a three-body system consisting of a ${}^4$He core and two valence neutrons, solving the Faddeev equations in momentum space at leading order in Halo EFT. They construct separable two-body potentials that reproduce the leading-order $nn$ (${}^1S_0$) and $n\alpha$ (${}^2P_{3/2}$) t-matrices exactly, using both a Heaviside step-function form factor and a Yamaguchi form factor, with energy-dependent coupling strengths derived from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The three-body wave function is obtained from the Faddeev components, and the probability density is computed as the expectation value of the projection operator onto Jacobi momentum plane-wave states, incorporating the modified normalization conditions required by the energy-dependent potentials.

## Key numbers

* The $nn$ scattering length $a_0$ and the $n\alpha$ scattering volume $a_1$ and effective range $r_1$ define the leading-order t-matrices, with the $n\alpha$ resonance momentum given by $k_R = \sqrt{2/(a_1 r_1)}$.
* The $s$-wave t-matrix has a pole at $k = i\gamma_0$ where $\gamma_0 = 1/a_0$, and the $p$-wave t-matrix uses $\gamma_1 = -r_1/2$.
* The three-body binding energy $B_3^{(0)}$ is the energy eigenvalue relative to the $\alpha nn$ threshold, with $E_3 = -B_3^{(0)}$.
* Regulator parameters $\beta_0$ and $\beta_1$ are used for the $nn$ and $n\alpha$ channels respectively, with the requirement that the cutoff be significantly above the EFT breakdown scale for regulator independence.

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT treatment of ${}^6$He by Ji et al. (2014), who solved the Faddeev equations using a dibaryon formalism but did not exactly reproduce the effective-range expansion parameters due to regulator artifacts. By using energy-dependent potentials that exactly reproduce the leading-order t-matrices, the authors eliminate these artifacts and systematically study the effects of energy dependence on the probability density. The approach enables future calculations of momentum-space probability densities for other halo nuclei and provides a framework for incorporating higher-order corrections with controlled error estimates.
