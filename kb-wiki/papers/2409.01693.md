# Various Illustrative Brief Glances at the Flavour-Cryptoexotic Tetraquark States

> Constituting the largest subset of multiquark states so far observed by experiment, tetraquark mesons carrying overall quark flavour quantum numbers identical to those of the (conventional) quark-antiquark mesons necessitate their particularly deliberate phenomenological analyses. A collection of (more or less recent) insights specific to the latter subclass of exotic hadrons bears the promise to enable even significant improvement in the understanding of these hadrons by theoretical approaches such as, among others, the framework of QCD sum rules.

# Various Illustrative Brief Glances at the Flavour-Cryptoexotic Tetraquark States

## Key claim

Flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks, the most experimentally abundant multiquark states, can be unambiguously identified in theoretical frameworks only by isolating contributions that exhibit a non-polynomial dependence on the Mandelstam variable s with a four-intermediate-quark branch cut starting at the threshold (m_a + m_b + m_c + m_d)^2; without this criterion, analyses risk contamination from contributions that provide no information about the tetraquark of interest.

## Method

The author applies a previously proposed selection criterion (Lucha, Melikhov, Sazdjian) to four-point correlation functions of quark-antiquark bilinear operators, identifying tetraquark-phile contributions via Landau equations. The large-N_c scaling behaviour of these contributions is then derived for flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks of the form [\overline q_a q_b \overline q_b q_c] with three distinct quark flavours, considering both flavour-preserving and flavour-reordering correlation functions. The analysis determines the N_c order of tetraquark-to-two-ordinary-meson transition amplitudes and the resulting total decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Tetraquark-phile contributions to correlation functions are of order O(N_c^2 α_s^2) = O(N_c^0) for flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks with three distinct flavours.
- The tetraquark-to-two-ordinary-meson transition amplitude A(T ↔ M_ab M_bc) = O(N_c^{-1}).
- The total decay width Γ(T) of flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks scales as O(N_c^{-2}) in the large-N_c limit.
- The mixing strength g_{TM_ac} between a flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquark and a conventional meson scales as O(N_c^{-1/2}).
- Conventional meson decay constants f_{M} scale as O(N_c^{1/2}).
- Conventional meson decay widths Γ(M) scale as O(N_c^{-1}).

## Context

This work extends the large-N_c QCD analysis of tetraquark decays by Lucha, Melikhov, and Sazdjian (2021), which predicted Γ(T) = O(N_c^{-2}), and contrasts with earlier results by Knecht and Peris (O(N_c^{-1})) and by Maiani, Polosa, and Riquer (O(N_c^{-3})). The discrepancies arise from either retaining non-tetraquark-phile contributions (Knecht, Peris) or neglecting flavour-rearranging contributions (Maiani, Polosa, Riquer). The criterion and large-N_c results should be generalized to all multiquark hadrons and incorporated into future quantitative predictions, particularly within QCD sum-rule frameworks as sketched by the author and collaborators.
