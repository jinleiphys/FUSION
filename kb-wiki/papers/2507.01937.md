# The Roper Resonance $N^*(1440)$ in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions and the Issue of Dibaryons

> In many reactions leading to excitations of the nucleon the Roper resonance $N^*(1440)$ can be sensed only by complex partial-wave analyses. In nucleon-nucleon collisions the isoscalar single-pion production as well as specific two-pion production channels present the Roper excitation free of competing resonance processes at a mass of 1370 MeV and a width of 150 MeV. A detailed analysis points to the formation of $N^*(1440)N$ dibaryonic systems during the nucleon-nucleon collision process similar to what is known from the $Δ(1232)N$ threshold.

# The Roper Resonance \(N^*(1440)\) in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions and the Issue of Dibaryons

## Key claim

The Roper resonance \(N^*(1440)\) appears in nucleon-nucleon collisions not as a free resonance but as part of \(N^*(1440)N\) dibaryonic systems, where the Roper is bound by roughly 70 MeV, resolving the discrepancy between its parameters extracted from \(NN\) collisions versus those from \(\pi N\) and \(\gamma N\) studies. The claim is conditional on the interpretation that the observed bump structures in isoscalar single-pion and two-pion production cross sections represent \(N^*(1440)N\) dibaryon formation rather than conventional \(t\)-channel Roper excitation.

## Method

The authors analyzed exclusive, kinematically complete measurements of \(pn \to pp\pi^-\) and \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) reactions from WASA-at-COSY in the energy range \(T_p = 1.0 - 1.35\) GeV (\(\sqrt s = 2.3 - 2.45\) GeV), extracting the isoscalar single-pion production cross section via isospin relations. They performed Breit-Wigner fits to the total cross section energy dependence and to the isoscalar \(N\pi\) invariant mass distribution. For two-pion production, they analyzed the \(pn \to d\pi^0\pi^0\) and \(pp \to pp\pi^0\pi^0\) channels, subtracting known contributions from the \(d^*(2380)\) dibaryon and \(\Delta\Delta\) excitation to isolate the \(N^*(1440)N\) signal.

## Key numbers

- Roper resonance mass extracted from isoscalar \(N\pi\) invariant mass distribution: \(m = 1370\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 150\) MeV
- Bump structure in isoscalar single-pion total cross section: \(m = 2310\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 150\) MeV (Breit-Wigner fit)
- Roper resonance pole position quoted from PDG: real part 1360 - 1380 MeV (approximately 1370 MeV), imaginary part 180 - 205 MeV (approximately 190 MeV)
- Roper Breit-Wigner mass from PDG: 1410 - 1470 MeV (approximately 1440 MeV), width 250 - 450 MeV (approximately 350 MeV)
- Binding energy of Roper in \(N^*(1440)N\) system: approximately 70 MeV (difference between 1440 MeV and 1370 MeV)
- Branching ratios for \(N^*(1440)N\) dibaryonic systems: elastic branching 0.04 for \(I(J^P) = 0(1^+)\) state and 0.15 for \(I(J^P) = 0(1^-)\) state; \(NN\pi\) branching approximately 0.8; \(NN\pi\pi\) branching approximately 0.2
- Bump in isovector two-pion production (\(pp \to pp\pi^0\pi^0\)): width 140 MeV, peaking around \(\sqrt s = 2.3\) GeV
- Peak cross section for two-pion decay of \(N^*(1440)N\) in \(pn \to d\pi^0\pi^0\): approximately 30 \(\mu b\), total estimated approximately 150 \(\mu b\)

## Context

This work extends the study of dibaryonic systems beyond the well-established \(\Delta(1232)N\) threshold states, which have been observed with multiple spin-parity combinations (\(0^-, 2^+, 2^-, 3^-\)) near the \(\Delta N\) threshold as reviewed in Refs. PPNP and CPC. The findings are consistent with the dibaryon \(NN\) interaction model of Kukulin, Platonova et al. (2020, 2022), which describes \(NN\) phase shifts up to the GeV range using \(s\)-channel exchange of intermediate dibaryons. The results enable experimental cross-checking of the dibaryons required in that model and provide a unified understanding of the Roper resonance's behavior across different reaction types, resolving the long-standing discrepancy between \(N\pi\)/\(N\gamma\) results and those from \(NN\) collisions.
