# Peculiarities of clusters formation in true ternary fission of $^{252}$Cf and $^{236}$U$^*$

> The existence of a new type of cluster decay called "collinear cluster tri-partition" (CCT) is discussed by an analysis of the landscape of the potential energy surface (PES). The total energy of the ternary system is found as a sum the binding energies of fragments and nucleus-nucleus interaction between them. The pre-scission state of the ternary system is assumed to be arranged as a chain of the three fragments along a straight line. Minima and valleys of the PES are determined by variation of the proton and neutron distributions between them. Pre-scission prompt emission of neutrons is assumed and PES is calculated for the cases of emission of 2---4 neutrons. The presence of the valley corresponding to the formation of the isotopes of Sn with masses $A$=130---136 is inherent for all PES calculated for CCT for spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf and fission induced by neutrons of $^{235}$U. There are local minima indicating the formation of Ca, Fe, Ni, Ge and Se isotopes having magic proton or/and neutron numbers, such as 20, 28, and 50. The analysis shows that the experimentally observed $^{68}$Ni is formed as the edge fragment of the ternary system connecting to Sn by Si and Ca isotopes at fission of $^{236}$U and $^{252}$Cf, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed yield of neutron-rich isotopes like \(^{68}\)Ni in fission of \(^{252}\)Cf and \(^{236}\)U\(^*\) can be explained by a collinear cluster tri-partition (CCT) mechanism, provided that the pre-scission ternary system is arranged as a straight chain of three fragments and that 2–4 prompt neutrons are emitted before scission.

## Method

The potential energy surface (PES) for a collinear ternary system was calculated as the sum of the binding energies of the three fragments, the nucleus-nucleus interaction between neighboring fragments (using a double-folding procedure with effective nucleon-nucleon forces), and the Coulomb interaction between the two edge fragments. The charge and mass numbers of the three fragments were varied over a wide range, and the distances \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) between the middle cluster and each edge fragment were minimized to find the pre-scission state. The PES was computed for spontaneous fission of \(^{252}\)Cf and for neutron-induced fission of \(^{235}\)U (forming \(^{236}\)U\(^*\)), assuming the emission of 2–4 neutrons before scission.

## Key numbers

- The PES for \(^{252}\)Cf shows a deep valley corresponding to the formation of \(^{132}\)Sn (magic numbers \(Z=50\), \(N=82\)) for various charge numbers \(Z_3\) and \(Z_1\).
- A local minimum in the PES is found at \(Z_1=28\) (Ni isotopes) crossing the \(^{132}\)Sn valley at \(Z_3=20\) (Ca), indicating the favored configuration \(^{132}\)Sn + \(^{50}\)Ca + \(^{68}\)Ni after emission of 2 neutrons from \(^{252}\)Cf.
- For the \(^{235}\)U(\(n_{\rm th},f\)) reaction, the driving potential minimum corresponds to the formation of \(^{68}\)Ni when the middle cluster is \(^{34}\)Si and the right edge fragment is \(^{132}\)Sn.
- The alternative configuration \(^{132}\)Sn + \(^{72}\)Ni + \(^{48}\)Ca has a PES minimum about 10 MeV higher, giving a much smaller probability.
- The CCT yield is estimated to be more than \(10^{-3}\) per binary fission.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of very asymmetric fission products (Ni and Fe isotopes) observed in cold fission of \(^{236}\)U by Sida and Goverd, and in the \(^{235}\)U(\(n_{\rm th},f\)) reaction and spontaneous fission of \(^{252}\)Cf by the FOBOS collaboration (Pyatkov et al., 2003–2011). It provides a theoretical basis for the collinear cluster tri-partition (CCT) mechanism, which had been proposed to explain the coincident detection of neutron-rich Ni and Ge isotopes with heavy fragments around \(A=136\)–\(140\). The calculations enable future dynamical studies of the rupture of the two necks in the ternary system and the order of scission, which depend on the pre-scission barriers \(B_{\rm sc1}\) and \(B_{\rm sc2}\).
