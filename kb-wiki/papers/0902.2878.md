# A Unified Theory of Quantum Holonomies

> A periodic change of slow environmental parameters of a quantum system induces quantum holonomy. The phase holonomy is a well-known example. Another is a more exotic kind that exhibits eigenvalue and eigenspace holonomies. We introduce a theoretical formulation that describes the phase and eigenspace holonomies on an equal footing. The building block of the theory is a gauge connection for an ordered basis, which is conceptually distinct from Mead-Truhlar-Berry's connection and its Wilczek-Zee extension. A gauge invariant treatment of eigenspace holonomy based on Fujikawa's formalism is developed. Example of adiabatic quantum holonomy, including the exotic kind with spectral degeneracy, are shown.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a unified theoretical framework that describes both the well-known phase holonomy (Berry phase) and the more exotic eigenspace and eigenvalue holonomies on an equal footing, using a gauge connection for an ordered basis that is conceptually distinct from the Mead-Truhlar-Berry connection and its Wilczek-Zee extension.

## Method

The authors develop a gauge theory for a parameterized basis, where the gauge connection AF(s) is defined for the entire frame of basis vectors rather than for individual eigenspaces. They extend Fujikawa's formalism to incorporate this connection, deriving a holonomy matrix M(C) = W(C)B(C) that combines the anti-ordered exponential of the full gauge connection with the ordered exponential of its diagonal part. The formalism is applied to three examples: Berry's spin-1/2 Hamiltonian, a quantum map spin-1/2 system exhibiting exotic holonomies without spectral degeneracy, and a degenerate case showing eigenspace holonomy.

## Key numbers

- For Berry's spin-1/2 Hamiltonian, the holonomy matrix for a loop C is M(C) = exp(-i/2 * Omega(C) * sigma_z), where Omega(C) is the solid angle of the loop
- For a meridian great circle C_theta in Berry's system, the holonomy matrix M(C_theta) = -1, obtained via the parallel transport condition with AD_theta = 0
- For a circle of latitude C_phi in Berry's system, M(C_phi) = exp{-i*pi*(1-cos(theta))*sigma_z}, with W(C_phi) = -1 and B(C_phi) = exp(i*pi*sigma_z*cos(theta))
- The quantum map spin-1/2 system has eigenvalues z_± = exp{-i*(mu*q + lambda*p)/2 ± i*Delta/2}, where Delta = 2*cos^{-1}(cos(B_mu)*cos(B_lambda) - cos(theta)*sin(B_mu)*sin(B_lambda))
- Spectral degeneracy occurs when sin(theta)*sin(B_mu)*sin(B_lambda) = 0, with degeneracy lines at {(mu, lambda, theta) | (lambda*(2-p) + mu*(2-q)*cos(theta))/(2*pi) in Integer, theta/pi in Integer} and isolated degeneracy points at (mu = 2*pi*k/(2-q), lambda = 2*pi*l/(2-p)) for integers k,l

## Context

This work extends the foundational concepts of Berry (1984) on geometric phase and Wilczek and Zee (1984) on non-Abelian gauge connections for degenerate systems, addressing the previously unformalized eigenspace holonomy reported by Cheon (1998). The unified framework enables the treatment of exotic holonomies where initial and final states can be orthogonal despite the absence of spectral degeneracy, and provides a gauge-invariant description using Fujikawa's formalism (2005, 2006) that can be applied to both Hamiltonian systems and periodically driven quantum maps.
