# Analysis of Neutron Star $f-$mode Oscillations in General Relativity with Spectral Representation of Nuclear Equations of State

> We study quasinormal $f-$mode oscillations in neutron star(NS) interiors within the linearized General Relativistic formalism. We utilize approximately 9000 nuclear Equations of State (EOS) using spectral representation techniques, incorporating constraints on nuclear saturation properties, chiral Effective Field Theory ($χ$EFT) for pure neutron matter, and perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (pQCD) for densities pertinent to NS cores. The median values of f-mode frequency, $ν_f$ (damping time, $τ_f$) for NS with masses ranging from 1.4 - 2.0 $M_\odot$ lie between 1.80 - 2.20 kHz (0.13 - 0.22 s) for our entire EOS set. Our study reveals a weak correlation between $f-$mode frequencies and individual nuclear saturation properties, prompting the necessity for more intricate methodologies to unveil multi-parameter relationships. We observe a robust linear relationship between the radii and $f-$mode frequencies for different NS masses. Leveraging this correlation alongside NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451, we establish constraints that exhibit partial and minimal overlap for observational data from Riley et al. and Miller et al. respectively with our nucleonic EOS dataset. Moreover, NICER data aligns closely with radius and frequency values for a few hadron-quark hybrid EOS models. This indicates the need to consider additional exotic particles such as deconfined quarks at suprasaturation densities. We conclude that future observations of the radius or $f-$mode frequency for more than one NS mass, particularly at the extremes of viable NS mass scale, would either rule out nucleon-only EOS or provide definitive evidence in its favour.

# Analysis of Neutron Star f-mode Oscillations in General Relativity with Spectral Representation of Nuclear Equations of State

## Key claim

The study finds only a weak correlation between f-mode frequencies and individual nuclear saturation properties, contrary to expectations that such single-parameter correlations would be strong; instead, the authors demonstrate that a robust linear relationship exists between neutron star radii and f-mode frequencies across different masses, and that NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451 show only partial or minimal overlap with nucleonic equations of state, suggesting that hadron-quark hybrid models may be needed.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 9,000 nucleonic equations of state using a relativistic mean field formalism within a Bayesian framework, imposing constraints from nuclear saturation properties, chiral Effective Field Theory for pure neutron matter, and perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics at high densities. They represented each equation of state using a spectral decomposition method with four spectral coefficients, then solved the fully general relativistic Lindblom-Detweiler perturbation equations for non-rotating neutron stars to compute quasinormal f-mode frequencies and gravitational wave damping times across a range of stellar masses from 1.4 to 2.0 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- Median f-mode frequencies for neutron stars with masses from 1.4 to 2.0 solar masses lie between 1.80 and 2.20 kHz across the entire equation of state set
- Median damping times for the same mass range lie between 0.13 and 0.22 seconds
- The spectral representation achieved a fit precision of less than 0.5 percent for all approximately 9,000 equations of state using the Levenberg-Marquardt optimization method
- Nuclear saturation parameters for the equation of state set include: binding energy e0 = -16.10 +/- 0.33 MeV, incompressibility K0 = 255 +42/-40 MeV, symmetry energy J_sym,0 = 32.07 +2.52/-2.77 MeV, and symmetry energy slope L_sym,0 = 32 +13/-21 MeV at saturation density n0 = 0.152 +0.008/-0.005 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Sotani (2021), Pradhan (2022), Kunji (2022), and Pradhan (2023) that investigated correlations between nuclear matter parameters and stellar oscillation modes, but finds that individual nuclear saturation properties are only weakly correlated with f-mode frequencies, contradicting the stronger single-parameter correlations reported by Pradhan (2022) for effective nucleon mass. The study enables future gravitational wave observations from detectors like the Einstein Telescope and Advanced LIGO to constrain neutron star equations of state by measuring f-mode frequencies, and the authors conclude that observations of radius or f-mode frequency for more than one neutron star mass, particularly at the extremes of the viable mass scale, could either rule out nucleon-only equations of state or provide definitive evidence in their favor.
