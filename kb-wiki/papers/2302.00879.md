# Nuclear response to dark matter signals in Ge and Xe odd-mass targets

> Abstract: The interaction of dark matter particles (WIMPs) with the odd-mass $^{73}$Ge and $^{131}$Xe target nuclei ,{ {that is the recoil rates corresponding to the elastic scattering of WIMPs by these nuclei}}, is analysed in the context of the minimal extensions of the SUSY model. The BCS+QRPA technique plus the quasiparticle-phonon coupling scheme is used to describe the nuclear structure part of the calculations. The resulting values for the nuclear spin content of both nuclei are compared to values previously reported in the literature.

# Nuclear response to dark matter signals in Ge and Xe odd-mass targets

## Key claim

The authors calculate spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus elastic scattering rates for odd-mass 73Ge and 131Xe targets using a BCS+QRPA plus quasiparticle-phonon coupling model, finding that the inclusion of backward-going amplitudes (via QRPA) reduces the neutron spin expectation values compared to earlier models, with the reduction factors reaching up to about 2.5 in the differential recoil rates depending on the WIMP mass.

## Method

The authors first apply BCS transformations to obtain quasiparticle states for neutron orbits in the 28-50 and 50-82 shells, then use the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) to describe monopole and quadrupole excitations of the even-mass cores 72Ge and 130Xe. They couple a quasineutron to these one-phonon states via the quasiparticle-phonon coupling scheme, diagonalizing the interaction to obtain wave functions for the odd-mass nuclei, from which they compute spin expectation values and differential recoil rates for neutralino masses between 10 and 100 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For 131Xe (Jπ = 3/2+), the calculated neutron spin expectation value ⟨Sn⟩ = -0.1349, compared to -0.236 from Engel et al. (1992) using QTDA and -0.188 from Pirinen et al. (2019) using IFBM-2
- For 73Ge (Jπ = 9/2+), the calculated neutron spin expectation value ⟨Sn⟩ = 0.2871, compared to 0.378 from Dimitrov et al. (1994) and 0.439 from Klos et al. (2013) using Chiral Effective Field Theory
- The spin-dependent cross section for 131Xe ranges from 1.60 × 10^{-45} cm^2 (mχ = 10 GeV) to 1.79 × 10^{-46} cm^2 (mχ = 100 GeV)
- The spin-dependent cross section for 73Ge ranges from 4.75 × 10^{-45} cm^2 (mχ = 10 GeV) to 5.04 × 10^{-46} cm^2 (mχ = 100 GeV)
- The ratio of differential recoil rates for 73Ge using different spin expectation values ranges from 0.512 to 0.803 depending on WIMP mass and which comparison is made
- The one-phonon energies were fixed at experimental values: 0.691 MeV (0+1) and 0.834 MeV (2+1) in 72Ge, and 0.536 MeV (2+1) in 130Xe
- The calculated B(E2) value for the 2+ → 0+ transition in 130Xe is 18.10 W.u. (with en=0.5e, ep=1.5e) compared to the experimental value of 32(3) W.u.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus scattering for odd-mass targets, specifically comparing with the Quasi Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (QTDA) results of Engel et al. (1992), the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IFBM-2) results of Pirinen et al. (2019), the Hybrid model of Dimitrov et al. (1994), and the Chiral Effective Field Theory (CEFT) results of Klos et al. (2013). The authors find that QRPA with quasiparticle-phonon coupling yields systematically lower neutron spin expectation values than these earlier approaches, which can reduce predicted detection rates by factors up to about 2.5 for lower WIMP masses. The results are relevant for interpreting data from experiments such as XENON1T, LUX, EDELWEISS, and CoGeNT that use germanium or xenon targets for dark matter direct detection.
