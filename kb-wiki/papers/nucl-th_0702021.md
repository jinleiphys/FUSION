# Threshold Effects in Multi-channel Coupling and Spectroscopic Factors in Exotic Nuclei

> In the threshold region, the cross section and the associated overlap integral obey the Wigner threshold law that results in the Wigner-cusp phenomenon. Due to flux conservation, a cusp anomaly in one channel manifests itself in other open channels, even if their respective thresholds appear at a different energy. The shape of a threshold cusp depends on the orbital angular momentum of a scattered particle; hence, studies of Wigner anomalies in weakly bound nuclei with several low-lying thresholds can provide valuable spectroscopic information. In this work, we investigate the threshold behavior of spectroscopic factors in neutron-rich drip-line nuclei using the Gamow Shell Model, which takes into account many-body correlations and the continuum effects. The presence of threshold anomalies is demonstrated and the implications for spectroscopic factors are discussed.

## Key claim

The Wigner-cusp phenomenon and associated threshold anomalies in spectroscopic factors (SFs) appear naturally in a microscopic many-body open quantum system (OQS) approach, but only if nucleon-nucleon correlations involving scattering states are treated properly; standard closed quantum system (CQS) shell-model calculations cannot reproduce these effects.

## Method

The authors applied the Gamow Shell Model (GSM), a complex-energy implementation of the continuum shell model, using a Berggren single-particle basis consisting of Gamow (bound and resonance) states and non-resonant scattering states. The GSM Hamiltonian combined a Woods-Saxon potential (representing an inert 4He core) with a finite-range surface Gaussian interaction (SGI) for valence neutrons, and the calculations were performed for the model two- and three-neutron systems 6He and 7He. To isolate the continuum coupling effects, the authors also performed simplified harmonic oscillator shell-model (HO-SM) calculations using only bound valence shells, and varied the Hamiltonian parameters to change the positions of one-neutron (1n) thresholds.

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used were: radius R0=2 fm, diffuseness d=0.65 fm, spin-orbit strength Vso=7.5 MeV, and central depth V0=47 MeV (the "5He" parameter set).
- The SGI coupling constants were V0(0)=-403 MeV fm3 and V0(2)=-392 MeV fm3, fitted to reproduce experimental ground-state binding energies of 6He and 7He.
- The maximal momentum value for the Berggren contours was kmax=3.27 fm-1, with up to 60 discretization points, giving precision on energies and widths better than 0.1 keV.
- The HO-SM results for the 6He ground-state SF in the [5He(g.s.) ⊗ p3/2]0+ channel were depressed by about 25% compared to GSM results.
- The contribution to SF from the non-resonant continuum can be as large as 25%.

## Context

This work extends the study of Wigner-cusp phenomena, originally predicted by Wigner (1948) and explained quantitatively by Breit, Baz, Newton, Meyerhof, and others in the 1950s-1960s, into the domain of many-body open quantum system calculations for exotic nuclei. The authors apply the Gamow Shell Model (Michel 2002, 2004) to demonstrate that threshold anomalies in spectroscopic factors arise naturally from continuum coupling, contradicting the standard CQS shell-model approach that ignores coupling to decay and scattering channels. The results suggest that experimental studies of threshold effects in weakly bound neutron-rich nuclei such as 6,8He and non-Borromean two-neutron halos (Timofeyuk 2003) could provide valuable spectroscopic information about single-particle structure, and that similar anomalies are expected in proton-rich mirror nuclei.
