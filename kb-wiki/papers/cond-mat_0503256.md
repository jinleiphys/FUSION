# Asymmetric Two-component Fermion Systems in Strong Coupling

> We study the phase structure of a dilute two-component Fermi system with attractive interactions as a function of the coupling and the polarization or number difference between the two components. In weak coupling, a finite number asymmetry results in phase separation. A mixed phase containing symmetric superfluid matter and an asymmetric normal phase is favored. With increasing coupling strength, we show that the stress on the superfluid phase to accommodate a number asymmetry increases. Near the infinite-scattering length limit, we calculate the single-particle excitation spectrum and the ground-state energy at various polarizations. A picture of weakly-interacting quasi-particles emerges for modest polarizations. In this regime near infinite scattering length, and for modest polarizations, a homogeneous phase with a finite population of excited quasi-particle states characterized by a gapless spectrum should be favored over the phase separated state. These states may be realized in cold atom experiments.

## Key claim

Near the infinite-scattering length limit and for modest polarizations, a homogeneous phase with a finite population of excited quasi-particle states characterized by a gapless spectrum should be favored over the phase separated state, though the calculations indicate that the mixed and homogeneous phases are essentially degenerate at \(k_F a = \infty\).

## Method

The authors used both BCS mean-field theory and fixed-node Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations to study the ground state of a dilute two-component Fermi system with attractive short-range interactions as a function of coupling strength and polarization. For the QMC calculations, they employed system sizes of 54-66 particles with a finite-range cosh potential, computing the quasiparticle dispersion by placing an unpaired spin in a state of definite momentum and calculating the ground-state energy at various polarizations.

## Key numbers

* At \(k_F a = \infty\), the QMC calculations for larger system sizes (54-66 particles) yield \(\xi = 0.42(1)\) and \(\Delta = 0.84(5) E_{FG}\), where \(\xi\) is the ratio of the energy density to that of the free Fermi gas and \(\Delta\) is the superfluid gap.
* At \(k_F a = \infty\), the ratio \(\delta\mu/\Delta = 1.00(5)\), indicating the BCS state is close to the polarization threshold.
* The QMC calculations give \(\beta \approx 1.2\) for the ratio \(\Delta/\mu\) at \(k_F a = \infty\).
* In the weak coupling BCS regime, the mixed phase is characterized by \(\delta\mu/\Delta = 1/\sqrt{2}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of asymmetric two-component Fermi systems, particularly the mean-field analysis by Bedaque, Caldas and Rupak (2003) which showed that finite polarization leads to phase separation, and the proposal of a gapless superfluid (breached-pair phase) by Liu and Wilczek (2002). The results at strong coupling near \(k_F a = \infty\) suggest that cold atom experiments may access a homogeneous gapless superfluid phase, and the findings are relevant to understanding the phase structure of dense quark matter.
