# Origin of Temperature of Quark-Gluon Plasma in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Initially produced quark-gluon matter at RHIC and LHC does not have a temperature. A quark-gluon plasma has a high temperature. From this quark-gluon matter to the quark-gluon plasma is the early thermalization or the rapid creation of temperature. Elastic three-parton scattering plays a key role in the process. The temperature originates from the two-parton scattering, the three-parton scattering, the four-parton scattering and so forth in quark-gluon matter.

## Key claim
Temperature in quark-gluon plasma originates from elastic multi-parton scattering (two-parton, three-parton, and higher-order scatterings), with elastic three-parton scattering playing a key role in achieving early thermalization within 1 fm/c, contrary to earlier assumptions that two-parton scattering alone could explain the rapid thermalization observed at RHIC and LHC.

## Method
The author derived and solved transport equations for gluon matter and quark matter that include collision terms for elastic two-parton scattering (e.g., gg→gg, gq→gq) and elastic three-parton scattering (e.g., ggg→ggg, ggu→ggu, uuu→uuu, and many other combinations of gluons, quarks, and antiquarks). Squared amplitudes for all scattering processes were calculated in perturbative QCD, with divergences regularized by a screening mass evaluated from the parton distribution functions. The transport equations were evolved from initial anisotropic momentum distributions at t=0.2 fm/c for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Parton number density in initially produced quark-gluon matter: 32 fm^{-3}
- Occurrence probability of parton-parton-parton scattering at that density: larger than 0.2
- Gluon matter achieves isotropic momentum distributions at t=0.52 fm/c, giving a thermalization time of 0.32 fm/c
- Quark matter achieves isotropic momentum distributions at t=0.86 fm/c, giving a thermalization time of 0.66 fm/c
- The resulting temperature is much higher than the QCD critical temperature of 0.175 GeV
- Hydrodynamic calculations require quark-gluon matter to begin having a temperature at 0.6 fm/c (Kolb et al. for RHIC; HSS for LHC)

## Context
This work addresses the "first RHIC puzzle" of early thermalization (Heinz and Kolb), where elliptic flow data from STAR at RHIC and from Noferini at LHC require thermalization times under 1 fm/c. Prior approaches using only two-parton scattering (Shuryak) required unrealistically large cross sections (empirically >40 mb), while including inelastic gg→ggg scattering (SM, XG) gave thermalization times larger than 1 fm/c. The author's inclusion of elastic three-parton scattering, building on earlier work on three-parton amplitudes (XSCZ, XSYX, XX, XPW, XMCW), yields thermalization times of 0.32 fm/c for gluons and 0.66 fm/c for quarks, explaining the rapid temperature creation. This enables a microscopic understanding of how the quark-gluon plasma acquires its temperature in heavy ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies.
