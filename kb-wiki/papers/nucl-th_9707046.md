# Two-particle interferometry for non-central heavy-ion collisions

> In non-central heavy ion collisions, identical two particle Hanbury-Brown/Twiss (HBT) correlations C(K,q) depend on the azimuthal direction of the pair momentum K. We investigate the consequences for a harmonic analysis of the corresponding HBT radius parameters. Our discussion includes both, a model- independent analysis of these parameters in the Gaussian approximation, and the study of a class of hydrodynamical models which mimic essential geometrical and dynamical properties of peripheral heavy ion collisions. Also, we discuss the additional geometrical and dynamical information contained in the harmonic coefficients of these HBT radius parameters. The leading contribution of their first and second harmonics are found to satisfy simple constraints. This allows for a minimal, azimuthally sensitive parametrization of all first and second harmonic coefficients in terms of only two additional fit parameters. We determine to what extent these parameters can be extracted from experimental data despite finite multiplicity fluctuations and the resulting uncertainty in the reconstruction of the reaction plane.

## Key claim

For non-central heavy-ion collisions, the azimuthal dependence of two-particle Hanbury-Brown/Twiss (HBT) correlation functions can be analyzed via harmonic expansion of the HBT radius parameters, and the leading first and second harmonic coefficients satisfy simple constraints that allow a minimal parametrization in terms of only two additional fit parameters, provided the Gaussian approximation holds and the reaction plane orientation can be reconstructed.

## Method

The author derives model-independent expressions for the six Cartesian HBT radius parameters in the "out-side-long" (osl) system in terms of space-time variances of the emission function, then expands these radii in a Fourier series in the azimuthal angle Φ of the pair momentum relative to the impact parameter direction. A class of hydrodynamical model emission functions with anisotropic geometry (elliptic spatial shape) and anisotropic transverse flow is introduced, and the harmonic coefficients of both the one-particle spectra (v_n) and the HBT radius parameters are calculated numerically for mid-rapidity symmetric collisions.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 150 MeV, pion mass m = 139 MeV, freeze-out proper time τ₀ = 5 fm/c, Δτ = 1 fm/c, Δη = 1.22, transverse size R = 5 fm (input parameters for the model calculations)
- The spatial anisotropy parameter ε_s and flow anisotropy parameter ε_f both range in -1 < ε < 1, with positive ε_f meaning the major flow direction lies in the reaction plane
- The second harmonic coefficient v₂ of the single-particle spectrum vanishes at K⊥ = 0 and grows monotonically with transverse pair momentum; for small K⊥, v₂ ∝ K⊥²
- The relation v₂ ∝ (λ_y²/ρ_y² - λ_x²/ρ_x²) ∝ 2(ε_f - ε_s)/[(1-ε_f²)(1-ε_s²)] holds for small transverse flow
- In the elliptic approximation, the second harmonic coefficients satisfy {R_{o,2}^c}² = -{R_{s,2}^c}² = -{R_{os,2}^s}² = ½ α_T \bar{g}, where α_T is the transverse spatial anisotropy and \bar{g} the average size of the homogeneity region

## Context

This work extends the standard azimuthally symmetric HBT analysis (Chapman, Scotto, and Heinz 1995) to non-central collisions, complementing earlier event generator studies (Voloshin and Csernai 1996; Fai 1996) and Lorentz-invariance-based approaches (Voloshin and Csernai 1996). The harmonic analysis of HBT radii parallels the Fourier expansion of single-particle distributions used to characterize directed flow (v₁) and elliptic flow (v₂) at AGS and SPS experiments (Barrette 1994, 1997; Wieman 1995; Seyboth 1995). The results enable experimental extraction of both geometrical and dynamical source information from azimuthally sensitive HBT measurements, with the caveat that finite multiplicity fluctuations and reaction plane uncertainty limit the precision of the extracted harmonic coefficients.
