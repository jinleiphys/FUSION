# Is a neutron halo nothing but a large neutron skin?

> We discuss the difference between neutron halos and neutron skins matching to our usual thinking of a skin as being connected to a body whereas a halo is, in some sense, disconnected from the body. We emphasize that the existence of one or more neutrons behaving incoherently with respect to the rest of the nucleons in a nucleus can match the picture of a neutron halo.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a neutron halo is qualitatively different from a large neutron skin, defined by the incoherent behavior of halo neutrons relative to the core nucleons upon interaction, rather than by quantitative measures such as binding energy or radius alone.

## Method

The author proposes a phenomenological distinction based on the factorizability of the nuclear wave function: for a halo nucleus, the total neutron wave function can be written as a product of separately antisymmetrized wave functions for the halo neutrons and the core neutrons, whereas for a skin nucleus the wave function is a single Slater determinant. This leads to the prediction that observables such as differential cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions of fragments from peripheral high-energy interactions will show two independent components with different widths and center shifts, reflecting the incoherent contributions from the halo and the core.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal momentum distribution of fragments from ordinary stable nuclei such as $^{16}O$ has Gaussian widths that are statistical averages of Fermi motions, in the range 200-350 MeV/c.
- The momentum distribution of $^{11}Li$ fragments is well fitted by the sum of two Gaussians: a narrow width of approximately 70 MeV/c and an ordinary width.
- As an example calculation, taking the binding energy of halo neutrons as $\beta_{halo} = 0.33$ MeV and the removal energy of the same number of neutrons in the core as $\beta_{core} = 1.0$ MeV, the ratio of the shifts of the centers of the momentum distributions is $p^{c}_{core} / p^{c}_{halo} = \sqrt{\beta_{core} / \beta_{halo}} = 1.7$.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of neutron halos initiated by Wilkinson (1967) and the experimental observations of $^{11}Li$ by Tanihata and collaborators in the 1980s, which showed an unexpectedly large radius and a narrow momentum distribution component. The paper explicitly addresses the ambiguity in terminology between "halo" and "skin" noted by Mizutori and others, and proposes a qualitative criterion based on incoherence and factorizability of the wave function, in contrast to purely quantitative distinctions based on binding energy or radius. This enables future experimental searches for two-component signatures in momentum distributions and differential cross sections as definitive tests for halo structure.
