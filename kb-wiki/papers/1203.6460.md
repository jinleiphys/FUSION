# A thermodynamically consistent quasi-particle model without temperature-dependent infinity of the vacuum zero point energy

> In this paper, an improved quasi-particle model is presented. Unlike the previous approach of establishing quasi-particle model, we introduce a classical background field (it is allowed to depend on the temperature) to deal with the infinity of thermal vacuum energy which exists in previous quasi-particle models. After taking into account the effect of this classical background field, the partition function of quasi-particle system can be made well-defined. Based on this and following the standard ensemble theory, we construct a thermodynamically consistent quasi-particle model without the need of any reformulation of statistical mechanics or thermodynamical consistency relation. As an application of our model, we employ it to the case of (2+1) flavor QGP at zero chemical potential and finite temperature and obtain a good fit to the recent lattice simulation results of S. Borsanyi $et$ $al$. A comparison of the result of our model with early calculations using other models is also presented. It is shown that our method is general and can be generalized to the case where the effective mass depends not only on the temperature but also on the chemical potential.

# A thermodynamically consistent quasi-particle model without temperature-dependent infinity of the vacuum zero point energy

## Key claim
The authors construct a quasi-particle model for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) that eliminates the temperature-dependent divergence of the thermal vacuum zero-point energy by introducing a classical background field, thereby obtaining a well-defined partition function that automatically satisfies thermodynamic consistency without requiring any reformulation of statistical mechanics. The model is applied to (2+1) flavor QCD at zero chemical potential and finite temperature, and it reproduces lattice simulation results from Borsanyi et al. (2010) with the gluon degeneracy factor fixed to the QCD value of 16, unlike earlier models that treated it as a free parameter.

## Method
The authors start from effective Lagrangians for quasi-particles with temperature-dependent masses and identify the temperature-dependent divergent vacuum zero-point energy in the partition function. They introduce a classical background field B(T) into the Lagrangian, chosen via a series expansion method inspired by Walecka (1974) to cancel the divergence, and compute the finite shift in the ground-state energy using dimensional regularization. From the resulting well-defined partition function, they derive the pressure and energy density using standard ensemble theory and verify that the thermodynamic consistency relation E = T dP/dT - P is automatically satisfied. They then apply the model to (2+1) flavor QGP, using hard-thermal-loop inspired thermal masses with three free parameters (lambda, T_s, B_0) fitted to lattice data for pressure, interaction measure, and speed of sound.

## Key numbers
- The gluon degeneracy factor is fixed to d_g = 16, as required by QCD, unlike earlier models where it was a fitted parameter (e.g., 16.4_{-0.2}^{+0.3} in Szabo et al. (2003)).
- The three fitted parameters are: lambda = 6.6, T_s = -0.88 T_c, and B_0^{1/4} = 80 MeV, where T_c = 170 MeV is the transition temperature.
- The finite shift in the ground-state energy for gauge bosons is Delta epsilon_0 = N/(4 pi)^2 * (1/8) m^4(T), where N is the degeneracy factor (N=16 for SU(3)).
- For scalar fields, the finite shift in the ground-state energy is Delta epsilon_0 = 1/(4 pi)^2 [ (1/2) m^4(T) ln(m(T)/m_0) + (1/2) m_0^2 m^2(T) - (3/8) m^4(T) - (1/8) m_0^4 ].
- For Dirac fields, the finite shift in the ground-state energy is Delta epsilon_0 = -2/(4 pi)^2 [ m^4(T) ln(m(T)/m_0) + m_0^3 (m_0 - m(T)) - (7/2) m_0^2 (m_0 - m(T))^2 + (13/3) m_0 (m_0 - m(T))^3 - (25/12) (m_0 - m(T))^4 ].

## Context
This work extends the quasi-particle model approach for the QGP, which was pioneered by Goloviznin and Satz (1993) and Peshier et al. (1994), and later refined by Gorenstein and Yang (1995) and Bannur (1999) to address thermodynamic inconsistencies. The key improvement over earlier models (e.g., Szabo et al. 2003) is the systematic elimination of the temperature-dependent vacuum divergence via a classical background field, which yields a well-defined partition function without needing to treat the gluon degeneracy factor as a free parameter. The model enables more reliable predictions for QGP thermodynamics and can be generalized to finite chemical potential, with potential applications to quark star phenomenology as in Tian et al. (2010).
