# Systematic study of $α$ decay for isomer related nuclei within a two-potential approach

> $α$ decay occurs in both ground states and isomers of nuclei. In this work, we use the two-potential approach to systematically study whether isomeric states play a key role on $α$ particle clustering or not. The results indicate the ratios of $α$ decay preformation probabilities of isomers to ground states are found to be around 1.

## Key claim

The ratios of α-decay preformation probabilities of isomers to those of the corresponding ground states are found to be around 1, indicating that isomeric states do not play a key role on α particle clustering for most nuclei, though significant deviations occur for specific even-even isomers and shape isomers.

## Method

The authors used the two-potential approach (TPA) to calculate α-decay half-lives for a large set of isomer-related nuclei, including odd-A, odd-odd, and even-even species. The nuclear potential was taken as a cosh-type form with fixed diffuseness a = 0.5654 fm and depth V₀ = 189.53 MeV, while the α-particle preformation factor P₀ was set to 0.7 for even-even, 0.24 for odd-A, and 0.08 for odd-odd nuclei by fitting experimental half-lives. The effective preformation probability Pα was extracted as the ratio of calculated to experimental half-lives, and the ratio P*α/Pα (isomer to ground state) was then examined.

## Key numbers

- Preformation factor P₀ = 0.7 for even-even nuclei, 0.24 for odd-A nuclei, 0.08 for odd-odd nuclei (obtained by fitting experimental half-lives).
- For the isomer ²⁰⁹ᵐRa (13/2⁺) decaying to ²⁰⁵Rn (5/2⁻) with Qα = 8.015 MeV and l = 5, the calculated half-life is 0.138 s, compared to the experimental value of 1.30 × 10⁻⁴ s; the preformation probability ratio P*α/Pα = 736.4.
- For the shape isomer ²³⁶ᵐU (0⁺) decaying to ²³²Th (0⁺) with Qα = 7.323 MeV, the calculated half-life is 6.19 × 10⁰ s, while the experimental lower limit is > 1.20 × 10⁻⁶ s; the ratio P*α/Pα = 1,499,442.686.
- For the isomer ²¹²ᵐPo (18⁺) decaying to ²⁰⁸ᵐPb (0⁺) with Qα = 11.877 MeV and l = 18, the calculated half-life is 1.72 × 10⁻¹ s versus the experimental 4.51 × 10¹ s; the ratio P*α/Pα = 0.0077.
- For the isomer ²¹⁶ᵐTh (8⁺) decaying to ²¹²Ra (0⁺) with Qα = 10.116 MeV and l = 8, the ratio P*α/Pα = 0.00828.
- For the isomer ²¹⁸ᵐU (8⁺) decaying to ²¹⁴Th (0⁺) with Qα = 10.878 MeV and l = 8, the ratio P*α/Pα = 0.00365.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic calculation of α-decay half-lives for even-even nuclei using the two-potential approach (Sun 2015) to include odd-A and odd-odd nuclei from both isomeric and ground states. The study tests whether isomeric states significantly affect α-particle clustering, a question motivated by the role of isomers as stepping stones toward the island of stability in superheavy elements (Her 2006). The results enable future refinement of preformation probability systematics and highlight cases where high angular momentum transfer or large Qα values dominate the decay channel, as seen in ²⁰⁹ᵐRa.
