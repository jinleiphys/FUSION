# Radiative and non radiative muon capture on the proton in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We have evaluated the amplitude for muon capture by a proton, mu + p --> n + nu, to O(p^3) within the context of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) using the new O(p^3) Lagrangian of Ecker and Mojzis (E&M). We obtain expressions for the standard muon capture form factors and determine three of the coefficients of the E&M Lagrangian, namely, b_7, b_{19}, and b_{23}. We describe progress on the next step, a calculation of the radiative muon capture process, mu + p --> n + nu + gamma.

# Radiative and non radiative muon capture on the proton in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The authors evaluate the muon capture amplitude to O(p^3) using the new Ecker and Mojzis Lagrangian, obtaining form factors and determining three Lagrangian coefficients (b_7, b_19, b_23); the extracted value of the induced pseudoscalar form factor G_P agrees with non-radiative capture data, but the radiative capture calculation is still in progress and may resolve a discrepancy where the TRIUMF experiment found G_P approximately 1.5 times the Goldberger-Treiman prediction.

## Method

The authors compute the non-radiative muon capture amplitude (mu + p -> n + nu) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to O(p^3) using the complete Lagrangian of Ecker and Mojzis, which includes the new O(p^3) terms. They evaluate the fully renormalized vertex functions for the weak-NN, pi-NN, and weak-pi interactions, then extract the standard form factors G_V, G_M, G_A, and G_P by matching the heavy baryon amplitude to the relativistic Dirac form. For radiative muon capture, they outline a strategy of evaluating renormalized irreducible vertex functions for weak-NN, pi-NN, gamma-NN, gamma-pi-pi, and weak-pi vertices, then combining them to obtain the full amplitude.

## Key numbers

- b_7^r(m_N) = -0.53 ± 0.02 (one of the Ecker-Mojzis Lagrangian coefficients determined from the q^2 dependence of the vector form factor)
- b_23 = -3.1 ± 0.3 (determined from the q^2 dependence of the axial form factor from antineutrino-nucleon scattering or pion electroproduction)
- b_19 = -0.7 ± 0.4 (related to the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, determined using the pion-nucleon coupling constant)
- G_P(-0.88 m_mu^2) = 8.21 ± 0.09 (the induced pseudoscalar form factor at the muon capture momentum transfer, compared to the experimental value 8.7 ± 1.9 from Bardin et al.)
- F = 92.4 MeV (pion decay constant used in the calculation)
- Mojzis reported b_19 = -1.0 ± 0.4 from pi-N scattering, consistent within errors with the authors' value

## Context

This work extends previous HBChPT calculations of muon capture form factors by using the newly constructed complete O(p^3) Lagrangian of Ecker and Mojzis (1995), which had not been applied to this process before. The extracted Lagrangian parameters are directly transferable to future calculations using the same Lagrangian, particularly the radiative muon capture process (mu + p -> n + nu + gamma) which is the authors' next step. The radiative process is especially interesting because a recent TRIUMF experiment by Jonkmans et al. found G_P approximately 1.5 times the Goldberger-Treiman prediction, in disagreement with non-radiative capture results; the HBChPT approach may resolve this by providing a microscopic calculation of the gauge term and other contributions not present in the standard tree-level calculation of Fearing (which involves four Feynman graphs with radiation from external particles and the intermediate pion).
