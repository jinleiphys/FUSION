# Polarization observables in the reaction $p n \to d ϕ$

> The reaction $p n \to d ϕ$ is studied within a covariant boson exchange model. The behavior of polarization observables being accessible in forthcoming experiments near threshold is predicted.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the beam-target asymmetry in the reaction \(p n \to d \phi\) will be \(-1\) near threshold and increase with energy, in contrast to the \(+1\) asymmetry predicted for the elementary \(pp \to pp \phi\) process; this prediction is conditional on the dominance of meson-exchange currents over nucleon currents in the model.

## Method

The authors use a covariant one-boson exchange model, incorporating meson-exchange diagrams with \(\pi NN\), \(\rho NN\), and \(\phi \rho \pi\) vertices and monopole form factors, to compute the invariant amplitude for \(p n \to d \phi\). The deuteron is described via a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude with \(S\) and \(D\) wave components, and the amplitude is evaluated by integrating over the relative momentum of the nucleons in the deuteron. Polarization observables (asymmetry, tensor analyzing power, and spin-spin correlations) are then derived from the squared amplitude summed over spin projections.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for \(p n \to d \phi\) near threshold is computed; the shape is similar to that in Nakayama (2000), but absolute values differ by roughly a factor of \(2/3\), comparable to the difference between the cross section \(\sigma = 0.26\ \mu\)b from DISTO1 and the published value \(\sigma = 0.19\ \mu\)b for \(pp \to pp \phi\) at an excess energy of 83 MeV.
- The beam-target asymmetry \(\mathcal{A}\) is predicted to be \(-1\) near threshold and increases with energy (see Fig. 3 in the paper).
- The deuteron tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) is predicted to be almost constant at \(T_{20} \approx 1 / \sqrt{2}\) over a large region of energy excess.
- The proton-phi spin-spin correlation \(W_{zz}\) is predicted to vanish near threshold and remains very small (weak dependence on energy excess), indicating almost no correlation between the incident proton and outgoing meson polarizations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(p n \to d V\) with \(V = \omega, \phi\) by Grishina (2000) and Nakayama (2000), which focused on cross sections and angular distributions, to include polarization observables. The model uses parameters from Titov (1999) adjusted to threshold-near data on \(\phi\) production in \(pp\) and \(\pi p\) reactions, combined with a Bethe-Salpeter deuteron wave function from earlier work by the authors. The predictions are directly relevant to planned experiments at COSY using the ANKE spectrometer, where a polarized proton beam and polarized targets are available, and the \(\phi\) can be identified via its \(K^+ K^-\) decay channel.
