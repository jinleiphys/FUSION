# Di-jet hadron pair correlation in a hydrodynamical model with a quenching jet

> In jet quenching, a hard QCD parton, before fragmenting into a jet of hadrons, deposits a fraction of its energy in the medium, leading to suppressed production of high-$p_T$ hadrons. Assuming that the deposited energy quickly thermalizes, we simulate the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution of the QGP fluid. Hydrodynamic evolution and subsequent particle emission depend on the jet trajectories. Azimuthal distribution of excess $π^-$ due to quenching jet, averaged over all the trajectories, reasonably well reproduce the di-hadron correlation as measured by the STAR and PHENIX collaboration in central and in peripheral Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the splitting of the away-side peak in di-hadron correlations observed in central and mid-central Au+Au collisions can be reproduced by averaging over many jet trajectories in a hydrodynamical model with a quenching jet, even though individual jet events do not produce conical flow but rather a single peak either above or below π radians.

## Method

The author solves relativistic hydrodynamic equations with a time-dependent source term representing a quenching jet that deposits energy and momentum locally along its trajectory, assuming the lost energy quickly thermalizes. The jet is produced on the surface of an ellipsoidal fireball, characterized by two angles (φ_prod and φ_jet), and the energy loss rate is proportional to the local entropy density with a reference value of 14 GeV/fm at entropy density 140 fm⁻³. The azimuthal distribution of excess π⁻ (p_T integrated between 1 and 2.5 GeV) is computed at freeze-out temperature 100 MeV using the Cooper-Frye prescription, then averaged over all possible jet trajectories.

## Key numbers

- Reference parton energy loss: dE/dx|₀ = 14 GeV/fm at reference entropy density s₀ = 140 fm⁻³
- Peak initial energy density: ε₀ = 30 GeV/fm³ at initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Critical temperature for phase transition: T_c = 164 MeV
- Gaussian smearing width for jet source: σ = 0.70 fm
- Fireball radius: R = 6.4 fm
- Impact parameters used: b = 2.3 fm (0-5% centrality), b = 4.1 fm (5-10% centrality), b = 12.1 fm (60-90% centrality)
- Normalization factor N_norm ≈ 2.5-3.5 to match experimental data
- Simulated peak positions in individual events: φ_- ∼ 2 ± 1 rad or φ_+ ∼ 4 ± 1 rad
- Peak shift between simulation and experiment: ∼ 0.4 rad

## Context

This work extends the author's previous hydrodynamic simulations with a quenching jet (Chaudhuri 2005, 2006, 2007) by correcting deficiencies including the limited p_T range and incomplete characterization of jet trajectories. The results contradict the earlier conclusion in Chaudhuri (2005) that jet quenching could explain broadening but not splitting of the away-side peak. The model reproduces both STAR data (Wang 2004) and PHENIX data (Jacak 2005) for di-hadron correlations across centralities, showing that the splitting is an average effect over many trajectories rather than a single-event conical flow. The paper notes that three-particle angular correlations could discriminate between the distorted Mach shock wave scenario and the deflected jet picture (Chiu 2006).
