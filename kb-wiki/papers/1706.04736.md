# Probing crustal structures from neutron star compactness

> With various sets of the parameters that characterize the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter, we systematically examine the thickness of a neutron star crust and of the pasta phases contained therein. Then, with respect to the thickness of the phase of spherical nuclei, the thickness of the cylindrical phase, and the crust thickness, we successfully derive fitting formulas that express the ratio of each thickness to the star's radius as a function of the star's compactness, the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter, and the density dependence of the symmetry energy. In particular, we find that the thickness of the phase of spherical nuclei has such a strong dependence on the stellar compactness as the crust thickness, but both of them show a much weaker dependence on the EOS parameters. Thus, via determination of the compactness, the thickness of the phase of spherical nuclei as well as the crust thickness can be constrained reasonably, even if the EOS parameters remain to be well-determined.

## Key claim

The thickness of the phase of spherical nuclei and the total crust thickness depend strongly on stellar compactness but only weakly on the equation of state parameters (incompressibility $K_0$ and slope parameter $L$), meaning that if the compactness can be determined to within about 10% accuracy, these thicknesses can be constrained even when the EOS parameters remain poorly determined.

## Method

The authors constructed equilibrium neutron star crust models by numerically integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations from the star's surface inward to the base of the crust, using phenomenological equations of state (OI-EOSs) derived within the Thomas-Fermi framework by Oyamatsu and Iida (2003, 2007). They considered stellar models with masses between 1.4 and 1.8 solar masses and radii between 10 and 14 km, using 16 different sets of EOS parameters characterized by $K_0$ (180 to 360 MeV) and $L$ (5.7 to 146.1 MeV). They then derived fitting formulas for the ratio of each phase thickness to the stellar radius as a function of compactness $M/R$, $K_0$, and $L$.

## Key numbers

- The typical crust thickness formula is $\Delta R / R \simeq 2.1 \times 10^{-2} (R/M)(1 - 2M/R)$, independent of $L$ and $K_0$, where the factor $2.1 \times 10^{-2}$ differs from the factor $2.57 \times 10^{-2}$ obtained from the FPS equation of state by Ravenhall and Pethick (1994)
- The fitting formulas for the spherical phase thickness coefficients $\beta_{ij}^{\rm sp}$ are given as linear functions of $K_0$, e.g., $\beta_{11}^{\rm sp} = [2.3515 - 1.5395(K_0/230\ {\rm MeV})] \times 10^{-6}$, valid for $180 \lesssim K_0 \lesssim 280$ MeV
- The SP-C transition density is approximately $n_0/3$ (about 0.058 to 0.067 fm$^{-3}$ depending on EOS parameters) and is almost independent of $L$ and $K_0$
- The fitting formulas for the cylindrical phase thickness coefficients $\beta_{ij}^{\rm cy}$ are also linear in $K_0$, e.g., $\beta_{11}^{\rm cy} = [2.9604 + 0.1490(K_0/230\ {\rm MeV})] \times 10^{-5}$, valid for $180 \lesssim K_0 \lesssim 280$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the approximate relation between crust thickness and stellar compactness derived by Zhang and Haensel (2011) and Zhang et al. (2017) by including detailed dependence on the EOS parameters $K_0$ and $L$. The results enable observational constraints on crustal structures, particularly the thickness of the spherical nuclei phase and the total crust, from determinations of neutron star compactness, independent of precise knowledge of the nuclear matter EOS. The pasta phase thicknesses (cylindrical, slab-like, cylindrical-hole, and spherical-hole) show stronger EOS dependence and are tabulated separately, with the authors noting that the coefficients for the S, CH, and SH phases are difficult to express as simple functions of $L$ and $K_0$ due to their complicated dependence on the EOS parameters.
