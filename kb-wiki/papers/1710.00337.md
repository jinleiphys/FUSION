# Two neutrino double-$β$ decay of $94\leq A\leq 150$ nuclei for the 0$^{+}\rightarrow $2$^{+}$ transition

> Within the PHFB approach, the $0^{+}\rightarrow 2^{+}$ transition of two neutrino double-$β$ decay of $^{94,96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{104}$Ru, $^{110}$Pd, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd isotopes is studied employing wave functions generated with four different parametrizations of the pairing plus multipole type of two-nucleon interaction and the summation method. In comparison to the $0^{+}\rightarrow 0^{+}$ transition, the nuclear transition matrix elements $M_{2ν}(2^{+})$ are quite sensitive to the deformation of the yrast 2$^{+}$ state. Consideration of the available theoretical and experimental results suggest that the observation of the 0$% ^{+}\rightarrow $2$^{+}$ transition of $2νβ^{-}β^{-}$ decay may be possible in $^{96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd isotopes. The effect of deformation on the $M_{2ν}(2^{+})$ is also studied.

# Two neutrino double-beta decay of 94 ≤ A ≤ 150 nuclei for the 0⁺ → 2⁺ transition

## Key claim

Within the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) approach, the 0⁺ → 2⁺ transition of two-neutrino double-beta decay is intrinsically suppressed due to the cubic dependence of the energy denominator and nuclear structure effects; however, the available theoretical and experimental results suggest that observation may be possible in ⁹⁶Zr, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹¹⁰Pd, ¹³⁰Te and ¹⁵⁰Nd isotopes, despite the fact that the 0⁺ → 2⁺ transition has not been experimentally observed so far.

## Method

The authors employed the PHFB model with wave functions generated using four different parametrizations of the pairing plus multipole type of effective two-nucleon interaction (PQQ1, PQQHH1, PQQ2, PQQHH2). The nuclear transition matrix elements M₂ν(2⁺) for the 0⁺ → 2⁺ transition were calculated using the summation method, which goes beyond the closure approximation by treating each proton-neutron excitation according to its spin-flip or non-spin-flip character. The phase space factors G₂ν(2⁺) were taken from Pahomi et al. (2013) for most isotopes, while those for ⁹⁴Zr and ¹⁰⁴Ru were calculated using the prescription of Suhonen and Civitarese (1998) with axial vector coupling constant gA = 1.2701.

## Key numbers

- Average NTMEs M̄₂ν(2⁺) for ⁹⁴Zr: (9.88 ± 4.30) × 10⁻⁵
- Average NTMEs M̄₂ν(2⁺) for ⁹⁶Zr: (9.98 ± 0.74) × 10⁻⁵
- Average NTMEs M̄₂ν(2⁺) for ¹⁰⁰Mo: (1.89 ± 0.61) × 10⁻⁵
- Average NTMEs M̄₂ν(2⁺) for ¹⁰⁴Ru: (3.63 ± 0.55) × 10⁻⁵
- Average NTMEs M̄₂ν(2⁺) for ¹¹⁰Pd: (1.19 ± 0.10) × 10⁻⁴
- Average NTMEs M̄₂ν(2⁺) for ¹²⁸Te: (2.07 ± 0.83) × 10⁻⁶
- Average NTMEs M̄₂ν(2⁺) for ¹³⁰Te: (1.34 ± 0.61) × 10⁻⁶
- Average NTMEs M̄₂ν(2⁺) for ¹⁵⁰Nd: (5.86 ± 0.33) × 10⁻⁶
- Maximum uncertainty in average NTMEs: about 45%
- Calculated G₂ν(2⁺) for ⁹⁴Zr: 6.801 × 10⁻³⁰ y⁻¹
- Calculated G₂ν(2⁺) for ¹⁰⁴Ru: 9.625 × 10⁻²⁵ y⁻¹
- Phase space factor G₂ν(2⁺) for ⁹⁶Zr: 1.494 × 10⁻¹⁸ y⁻¹
- Phase space factor G₂ν(2⁺) for ¹⁵⁰Nd: 3.253 × 10⁻¹⁷ y⁻¹
- PHFB half-life T₁/₂²ν(2⁺) for ⁹⁶Zr: 6.723 × 10²⁵ y
- PHFB half-life T₁/₂²ν(2⁺) for ¹⁰⁰Mo: 1.924 × 10²⁷ y
- PHFB half-life T₁/₂²ν(2⁺) for ¹³⁰Te: 1.201 × 10³⁰ y
- PHFB half-life T₁/₂²ν(2⁺) for ¹⁵⁰Nd: 8.940 × 10²⁶ y
- The average PHFB NTMEs are suppressed by a factor between 1 and 150 relative to those of Raduta et al. (2007), corresponding to ⁹⁶Zr and ¹²⁸Te respectively
- The NTMEs M₂ν(2⁺) calculated with QRPA models without and with deformation vary by a factor of 2–341, corresponding to ¹³⁰Te and ⁹⁶Zr respectively

## Context

This work extends the PHFB approach previously applied to the 0⁺ → 0⁺ transition of two-neutrino double-beta decay by Chandra et al. (2005) and Singh et al. (2007) to the 0⁺ → 2⁺ transition for nuclei in the mass range 94 ≤ A ≤ 150. The study was motivated by the observation of Raduta et al. (2007) that inclusion of deformation in the mean field within the pnQRPA model can reduce the NTMEs M₂ν(2⁺) by up to a factor of 341. The PHFB approach treats pairing and deformation degrees of freedom simultaneously on equal footing, and the results show further suppression of NTMEs compared to the pnQRPA calculations. The findings enable experimental planning for the observation of the 0⁺ → 2⁺ transition, which has not yet been observed, and can help constrain different nuclear models by comparing their predictions for M₂ν(2⁺), which show marked variation across models.
