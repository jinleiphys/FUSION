# Relativistic dissipative spin dynamics in the relaxation time approximation

> The concept of the Wigner function is used to construct a semi-classical kinetic theory describing the evolution of the axial-current phase-space density of spin-1/2 particles in the relaxation time approximation. The resulting approach can be used to study spin-polarization effects in relativistic matter, in particular, in heavy-ion collisions. An expression for the axial current based on the classical treatment of spin is also introduced and we show that it is consistent with earlier calculations using Wigner functions. Finally, we derive non-equilibrium corrections to the spin tensor, which are used to define, for the first time, the structure of spin transport coefficients in relativistic matter.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a semi-classical kinetic theory for spin-1/2 particles in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) that goes beyond the perfect-fluid description of spin dynamics, deriving for the first time the structure of spin transport coefficients in relativistic matter, but the formalism is restricted to small values of the spin polarization tensor (omega_mu_nu < 1).

## Method

The authors start from the Wigner function and its Clifford-algebra decomposition to obtain separate kinetic equations for the scalar and axial-vector components of spin-1/2 particles. They introduce a relaxation time approximation for the axial component, analogous to the Anderson-Witting model for the scalar component, and then switch to a classical description of spin using an extended phase-space distribution function that includes the spin vector. From this classical RTA equation, they derive dissipative corrections to the conserved current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor by expanding the distribution function around equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The spin vector length is defined by the Casimir operator eigenvalue s^2 = 3/4 (dimensionless, from spin-1/2 particles)
- The equilibrium distribution for spin-dependent functions uses the factor exp( (1/2) omega_mu_nu s^mu_nu ), valid for omega_mu_nu < 1
- The relaxation time tau_eq appears as the single timescale in all dissipative corrections (delta N^mu, delta T^mu_nu, delta S^lambda,mu_nu)
- The dissipative spin tensor correction delta S^lambda,mu_nu contains four distinct tensor structures with coefficients B_Pi, B_n, B_pi, and B_Sigma, each with multiple scalar components (e.g., B_Pi has 3 components, B_pi has 4 components, B_n has 6 components, B_Sigma has 4 components)

## Context

This work extends the perfect-fluid spin-hydrodynamic framework of Florkowski et al. (2017) by including dissipative effects through the relaxation time approximation, and builds on the kinetic theory derivation of spin tensors from the Dirac field Lagrangian by Florkowski et al. (2018). The RTA approach for spin degrees of freedom parallels the successful use of RTA in testing hydrodynamics applicability and thermalization puzzles in standard relativistic kinetic theory, as done by Florkowski et al. (2013) and Heller et al. (2015-2016). The results enable future calculations of spin transport coefficients and dissipative spin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, addressing discrepancies between experimental observations of Lambda hyperon polarization and predictions based solely on thermal vorticity.
