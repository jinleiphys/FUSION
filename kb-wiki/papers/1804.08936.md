# Nuclear interactions and net-proton number fluctuations in heavy ion collisions at the SIS18 accelerator

> The effect of nuclear interactions on measurable net-proton number fluctuations in heavy ion collisions at the SIS18/GSI accelerator is investigated. The state of the art UrQMD model including interaction potentials is employed. It is found that the nuclear forces enhance the baryon number cumulants, as predicted from grand canonical thermodynamical models. The effect however is smeared out for proton number fluctuations due to iso-spin randomization and global baryon number conservation, which decreases the cumulant ratios. For a rapidity acceptance window larger than $Δy> 0.4$ the effects of global baryon number conservation dominate and all cumulant ratios are significantly smaller than 1.

## Key claim

Nuclear interactions enhance baryon number cumulant ratios in heavy ion collisions at SIS18 energies, as predicted by grand canonical models, but the effect is significantly smaller in the microscopic transport approach and is further smeared out for net-proton fluctuations due to isospin randomization and global baryon number conservation.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD model (version 3.4) in both cascade mode (only elastic and inelastic scatterings) and with explicit nuclear interaction potentials, simulating head-on (b=0 fm) Au+Au collisions at a fixed target beam energy of 1.23 A GeV. They compared cumulant ratios of net-baryon and net-proton number distributions in coordinate space at different times during the evolution and in momentum space with acceptance cuts corresponding to the HADES experiment (transverse momentum 0.4-1.6 GeV, variable rapidity window around mid-rapidity).

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: E_lab = 1.23 A GeV (fixed target), corresponding to the SIS18 HADES experiment
- Enhancement of baryon number cumulant ratios observed for spatial boxes of length smaller than 2 fm at early times (t=15 fm/c)
- For rapidity acceptance windows larger than Delta y > 0.4, all cumulant ratios are significantly smaller than 1 due to global baryon number conservation
- The nuclear potential parameters yield incompressibility K_0 = 200 MeV for isospin symmetric nuclear matter
- Total baryon number conserved at 394 baryons in the Au+Au system

## Context

This work extends predictions from grand canonical models (Fukushima 2014, Vovchenko 2015, Mukherjee 2016) that suggested nuclear interactions significantly impact net-proton cumulants at low beam energies (sqrt(s_NN) < 20 GeV). It contrasts with a study at higher beam energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 5 GeV) by He (2016) which found no effect of nuclear interactions. The results show that while the qualitative effect agrees with grand canonical predictions, the quantitative signal is much smaller in a realistic transport model, making it harder to observe long-range correlations from nuclear interactions or critical behavior through proton number cumulants in heavy ion collisions.
