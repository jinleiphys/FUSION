# Gluon emission from heavy quarks in dense nuclear matter

> We study the medium-induced gluon emission process experienced by a hard jet parton propagating through the dense nuclear matter in the framework of deep inelastic scattering off a large nucleus. We work beyond the collinear rescattering expansion and the soft gluon emission limit, and derive a closed formula for the medium-induced single gluon emission spectrum from a heavy or light quark jet interacting with the dense nuclear medium via transverse and longitudinal scatterings. Without performing the collinear rescattering expansion, the medium-induced gluon emission spectrum is controlled by the full distribution of the differential elastic scattering rates between the propagating partons and the medium constituents. We further show that if one utilizes heavy static scattering centers for the traversed nuclear matter and takes the soft gluon emission limit, our result can reduce to the first order in opacity Djordjevic-Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev formula.

## Key claim

The authors derive a closed formula for the medium-induced single gluon emission spectrum from a heavy or light quark jet that goes beyond both the collinear rescattering expansion and the soft-gluon emission limit, showing that without these approximations the spectrum is controlled by the full distribution of differential elastic scattering rates between the propagating partons and the medium constituents.

## Method

The authors work within the framework of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus, focusing on the charged current interaction channel to treat heavy (massive) and light (massless) quarks on the same footing. They compute the hadronic tensor at twist-four level for the process where a W boson strikes a light quark from the nucleus, producing a heavy quark that subsequently emits a gluon and undergoes a single rescattering with medium constituents. The calculation retains full transverse and longitudinal momentum exchanges without performing the collinear expansion, and the gluon field correlations in the nucleus are factorized using translational invariance.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum single gluon emission spectrum from a heavy quark jet is given by \( \frac{d N_g^{\rm vac}}{d y\,d^2 \mathbf{l}_\perp } = C_F \frac{\alpha_s}{2 \pi^2} {P(y)} \frac{l_\perp^2 + \frac{y^4}{1+(1-y)^2}{M^2}}{(l_\perp^2 + y^2 {M^2})^2} \), where \(P(y) = [1+(1-y)^2]/{y}\) is the leading order splitting function, \(y = l^-/q^-\) is the gluon energy fraction, and \(M\) is the quark mass.
* The on-shell condition for the final outgoing heavy quark yields the delta function \(\delta(l_q^2-M^2)=\frac{1}{2 p^+ q^-(1-y) }\delta(x_0 - x_B - x_M -\tilde{x}_L)\), where \(x_B\) is the Bjorken fraction, \(x_M = M^2/(2p^+q^-)\), and \(\tilde{x}_L = (l_\perp^2 + y^2 M^2)/(2p^+q^- y (1-y))\).
* The color factor for the central-cut diagram with one rescattering on the radiated gluon evaluates to \(C_F C_A\).

## Context

This work extends the higher twist (HT) formalism of Guo (2000), Wang (2001), and Majumder (2009) for medium-induced gluon emission by going beyond the collinear rescattering expansion, and generalizes the authors' previous work (Zhang 2018) to treat both massive and massless quarks in the charged current DIS channel. The result reduces to the first order in opacity Djordjevic-Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (DGLV) formula when one uses heavy static scattering centers and takes the soft-gluon emission limit, thereby connecting the generalized HT formalism to the GLV/DGLV approach of Gyulassy (2000) and Djordjevic (2003). This enables future quantitative studies of jet quenching that do not rely on the collinear approximation or the soft-gluon emission limit.
