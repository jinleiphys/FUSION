# Inclusive Gluon Production in DIS at High Parton Density

> We calculate the cross section of a single inclusive gluon production in deep inelastic scattering at very high energies in the saturation regime, where the parton densities inside hadrons and nuclei are large and the evolution of structure functions with energy is nonlinear. The expression we obtain for the inclusive gluon production cross section is generated by this nonlinear evolution. We analyze the rapidity distribution of the produced gluons as well as their transverse momentum spectrum given by the derived expression for the inclusive cross section. We propose an ansatz for the multiplicity distribution of gluons produced in nuclear collisions which includes the effects of nonlinear evolution in both colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper derives the cross section for single inclusive gluon production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) including both multiple rescatterings and nonlinear quantum evolution, showing that the evolution preceding the emission of the measured gluon is only linear (single pomeron exchange), while the evolution after emission is incorporated by replacing Glauber-Mueller exponents with the evolved forward amplitude of a gluon dipole.

## Method

The authors start from the quasi-classical expression for inclusive gluon production in DIS derived by Kovchegov and Tuchin (2001), which resums multiple rescatterings via Glauber-Mueller exponents. They then include quantum evolution by analyzing diagrams in light-cone perturbation theory at large \(N_c\), separating the evolution into gluons harder than the measured one (preceding emission) and gluons softer than the measured one (following emission). The harder-gluon evolution is shown to be linear dipole/BFKL evolution, while the softer-gluon evolution is incorporated by replacing the quasi-classical saturation exponents with the evolved forward scattering amplitude of a gluon (adjoint) dipole \(N_G\).

## Key numbers

- The quasi-classical gluon saturation scale is given by \(\underline{x}^2 Q_{0s}^2 = \underline{x}^2 \frac{8\pi^2 \alpha_s N_c \sqrt{R^2 - b^2}}{N_c^2 - 1} \rho \, xG(x, 1/|\underline{x}|^2)\), where \(\rho = A/[(4/3)\pi R^3]\) is the nuclear density.
- The quasi-classical quark saturation scale is \(\underline{x}_{01}^2 Q_{0s}^{\text{quark }2} = \underline{x}_{01}^2 \frac{4\pi^2 \alpha_s \sqrt{R^2 - b^2}}{N_c} \rho \, xG(x, 1/\underline{x}_{01}^2)\), with the gluon distribution taken at two-gluon order: \(xG(x, 1/\underline{x}^2) = (\alpha_s C_F/\pi) \ln(1/(\underline{x}^2 \mu^2))\).
- The linear dipole number density satisfies \(\int d^2 B \, n(\underline{r}, \underline{x}_{01}, \underline{B}, Y) = \int \frac{d\lambda}{\pi i} e^{\overline{\alpha}_s \chi(\lambda) Y} (r/x_{01})^{2\lambda}\), where \(\overline{\alpha}_s = \alpha_s N_c/\pi\) and \(\chi(\lambda) = 2\psi(1) - \psi(1-\lambda) - \psi(\lambda)\) is the BFKL eigenvalue.
- At large transverse momentum \(k_\perp\) the cross section behaves as \(1/k_\perp^4\), while at small \(k_\perp \sim Q_s\) it softens to \(1/k_\perp^2\).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-classical calculation of inclusive gluon production in DIS by Kovchegov and Tuchin (2001) and the proton-nucleus result of Kovchegov and Mueller (1998) by including nonlinear quantum evolution described by the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. It provides the first derivation of the inclusive gluon cross section in DIS with both multiple rescatterings and nonlinear evolution, and the resulting expression differs from the earlier AGK-based result of Braun (2000) by replacing the fundamental dipole amplitude \(N\) with the gluon dipole amplitude \(N_G\). The paper enables further calculations of gluon multiplicities in nucleus-nucleus collisions by proposing an ansatz that includes nonlinear evolution in both colliding nuclei.
