# Medium polarization isotopic effects on nuclear binding energies

> There exist several effective interactions whose parameters are fitted to force mean field predictions to reproduce experimental findings of finite nuclei and calculated properties of infinite nuclear matter. Exploiting this tecnique one can give a good description of nuclear binding energies. We present evidence that further progress can be made by taking into account medium polarization effects associated with surface and pairing vibrations.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence that including medium polarization effects from surface and pairing vibrations can improve the description of nuclear binding energies beyond mean-field theory, but the improvement is modest: the rms deviation from experimental masses is reduced by only about 4.5% (to 0.691 MeV) when adding correlation energy fluctuations, and by about 8% (to 0.667 MeV) when quadrupole pairing vibrations are also included for calcium and lead isotopes.

## Method

The authors started from a Hartree-Fock (HF) mean field calculation using the Skyrme-type MSk7 interaction, with pairing treated in the BCS approximation using a zero-range interaction, for a set of 121 spherical even-even nuclei from A=16 to A=214. They then added correlation energies from low-lying surface vibrations (2+ and 3- phonons) calculated in the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and from monopole pairing vibrations calculated in RPA, followed by a perturbative linear refit of the Skyrme parameters to minimize the rms deviation from experimental binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Rms deviation of mean field (HF+BCS) binding energies from experiment: 0.724 MeV for 121 nuclei
- Rms deviation after adding surface and pairing correlation energies and refitting: 0.714 MeV (essentially unchanged from mean field)
- Rms deviation after adding only the fluctuations (delta E) of correlation energies and refitting: 0.691 MeV (4.5% reduction from mean field)
- Rms deviation after including quadrupole pairing vibrations for Ca and Pb isotopes and adding fluctuations: 0.667 MeV (8% reduction from mean field)
- Total sigma_k (within-chain rms deviation) for mean field: 0.532 MeV; after adding surface and pairing correlations: 0.502 MeV (6% reduction)
- For calcium isotopes, sigma_k improved from 0.959 MeV (MF) to 0.308 MeV after including quadrupole pairing vibrations
- For lead isotopes, sigma_k improved from 0.518 MeV (MF) to 0.296 MeV after including quadrupole pairing vibrations
- Surface correlation energies alone increased sigma_k in 16 out of 21 isotopic chains compared to mean field

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bender et al. (2005), who studied angular-momentum-projected correlation energies using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction, and of Goriely et al. (2001), who developed the MSk7 Skyrme parametrization. The authors compare their surface vibration results to those of Bender et al. (2005), finding good agreement (rms deviation of 1.46 MeV versus 1.45 MeV). The study is limited by its perturbative refitting procedure, which ties results to the starting Skyrme parametrization; the authors suggest that a full refit with recalculation of correlation energies at each step would be needed for further improvement. The work enables future investigations into systematic inclusion of pairing and surface vibrational degrees of freedom in nuclear mass formulas.
