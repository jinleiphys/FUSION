# Effects of hyperonic many-body force on $B_Œõ$ values of hypernuclei

> The stiff equation of state (EoS) giving the neutron-star mass of $2M_{\odot}$ suggests the existence of strongly repulsive many-body effect (MBE) not only in nucleon channels but also in hyperonic ones. As a specific model for MBE, the repulsive multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) is added to the two-body interaction together with the phenomenological three-body attraction. For various versions of the Nijmegen interaction models, the MBE parts are determined so as to reproduce the observed data of $B_Œõ$. The mass dependence of $B_Œõ$ values is shown to be reproduced well by adding MBE with the strong MPP repulsion assuring the stiff EoS of hyperon-mixed neutron-star matter, when $P$-state components of the adopted interaction model lead to almost vanishing contributions. The nuclear matter $Œõ\!N$ $G$-matrix interactions are derived and used in $Œõ$ hypernuclei on the basis of the averaged-density approximation (ADA). The $B_Œõ$ values of hypernuclei with $9 \le A \le 59$ are analyzed in the framework of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics with use of the two types of $Œõ\!N$ $G$-matrix interactions including strong and weak MPP repulsions. The calculated values of $B_Œõ$ reproduce the experimental data finely within a few hundred keV. The values of $B_Œõ$ in $p$-states also can be reproduced well, when ADA is modified to be suitable also to weakly-bound $Œõ$ states.

## Key claim

## Method

The authors derived ŒõN G-matrix interactions in nuclear matter from channel-coupled equations including ŒõN-Œ£N coupling, using various versions of the Nijmegen interaction models (ESC08a/b, ESC14, ESC12, ESC04a, NSC97e/f). They added the repulsive multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) and phenomenological three-body attraction (TBA) as density-dependent two-body interactions, then used the averaged-density approximation (ADA) to fold these G-matrix interactions into structure calculations of Œõ hypernuclei performed with the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for hypernuclei (HyperAMD) framework.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Yamamoto et al. (2013, 2014) who introduced the multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) to solve the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron-star physics, where hyperon mixing softens the equation of state and prevents reaching 2 M_‚äô masses. The authors build on their previous HyperAMD study (Isaka et al., 2016) using ESC12, now testing updated ESC08c versions (ESC14) and showing that strong MPP repulsion consistent with stiff neutron-star equations of state is compatible with hypernuclear B_Œõ data only when the two-body interaction has nearly vanishing P-state contributions. The work contrasts with Lonardoni et al. (2014, 2015) who extracted ŒõNN repulsions from B_Œõ data using two-body interactions without ŒõN-Œ£N coupling, finding abnormally strong repulsion. This analysis enables systematic determination of hyperonic many-body forces from hypernuclear binding energies across a wide mass range.
