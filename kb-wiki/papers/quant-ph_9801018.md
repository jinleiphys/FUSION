# Clones and other interferance effects in the evolution of angular momentum coherent states

> The aim of this article is to present the interference effects which occur during the time evolution of simple angular wave packets (WP) which can be associated to a diatomic rigid molecule (heteronuclear) or to a quantum rigid body with axial symmetry like a molecule or a nucleus. The time evolution is understood entirely within the frame of fractional revivals discovered by Averbukh and Perelman since the energy spectrum is exactly quadratic. Our objectives are to study how these interference effects differ when there is a change of the initial WP. For this purpose we introduce a two parameter set of angular momentum coherent states. From one hand this set emerge quite naturally from the three dimensional coherent states of the harmonic oscillator, from another hand this set is shown to be buit from intelligent spin states.We have also compared our coherent states to some previously constructed using boson representation of angular momentum. The time evolution of coherent states for symmetric top is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for angular momentum coherent states on a sphere, the time evolution exhibits fractional revivals where the wave packet splits into "clones" (exact copies of the initial wave packet) only for the specific case of circular states ($\eta = \pm 1$), while for other values of the parameter $\eta$ the fractional waves are "mutants" with different shapes, and only a single clone appears at particular times.

## Method

The authors construct a two-parameter family of angular momentum coherent states (exponential wave packets) depending on a localization parameter $N$ and a parameter $\eta$ that controls the asymmetry between $\Delta L_x$ and $\Delta L_y$. They expand these states in spherical harmonics and study their time evolution under a Hamiltonian with a quadratic energy spectrum $E \propto I(I+1)$, using the formalism of fractional revivals developed by Averbukh and Perelman (1991). The evolution is analyzed for three classes: circular states ($\eta = \pm 1$), linear states ($\eta = 0$), and elliptic states (other $\eta$ values), and also extended to symmetric top coherent states with two quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- The revival time is $T_{rev} = 2\pi/\omega_0 = (2\bar{I}+1)T_{cl}$, where $\omega_0 = \hbar/(2J_0)$ and $J_0$ is the moment of inertia.
- For the exponential coherent state, the average angular momentum $\langle L_z \rangle = \eta[N\coth(2N) - 1/2]$, which for large $N$ approaches $\eta[N - 1/2]$.
- The uncertainties satisfy $\Delta L_x^2 = (\eta^2/2)[N\coth(2N) - 1/2]$ and $\Delta L_y^2 = (1/2)[N\coth(2N) - 1/2]$, with large-$N$ limits $\Delta L_x^2 \rightarrow (\eta^2/2)(N-1/2)$ and $\Delta L_y^2 \rightarrow (1/2)(N-1/2)$.
- For the circular state ($\eta=1$), the partial wave expansion coefficients are $\sqrt{(2N)^I/\sqrt{(2I+1)!}}$ for the spherical harmonics $Y^I_I$.
- For the boson representation with spin $s=1/2$, the relation to the exponential parameter is $k^2/2 = N - 1/2$ for large $N$.

## Context

This work extends the universal scenario of fractional revivals discovered by Averbukh and Perelman (1991) from one-dimensional systems (such as the infinite square well studied by Aronstein and Stroud) and the two-dimensional rotor (studied by Bluhm, Kostelecky, and Tudose) to three-dimensional angular wave packets on a sphere. The paper introduces a new parameter $\eta$ that interpolates between circular states (previously studied by Mostowski for diatomic molecules) and linear states, revealing a continuous family of interference patterns. The results enable future studies of quantum rotational dynamics in heteronuclear diatomic molecules and deformed nuclei, and the symmetric top analysis extends the work of Bluhm, Kostelecky, and Tudose (1996) to systems with rational and irrational ratios of moments of inertia.
