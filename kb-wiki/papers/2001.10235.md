# In-beam gamma-ray and electron spectroscopy of $^{249,251}$Md

> The odd-$Z$ $^{251}$Md nucleus was studied using combined $γ$-ray and conversion-electron in-beam spectroscopy. Besides the previously observed rotational band based on the $[521]1/2^-$ configuration, another rotational structure has been identified using $γ$-$γ$ coincidences. The use of electron spectroscopy allowed the rotational bands to be observed over a larger rotational frequency range. Using the transition intensities that depend on the gyromagnetic factor, a $[514]7/2^-$ single-particle configuration has been inferred for this band, i.e., the ground-state band. A physical background that dominates the electron spectrum with an intensity of $\simeq$ 60% was well reproduced by simulating a set of unresolved excited bands. Moreover, a detailed analysis of the intensity profile as a function of the angular momentum provided a method for deriving the orbital gyromagnetic factor, namely $g_K = 0.69^{+0.19}_{-0.16}$ for the ground-state band. The odd-$Z$ $^{249}$Md was studied using $γ$-ray in-beam spectroscopy. Evidence for octupole correlations resulting from the mixing of the $Δl = Δj = 3$ $[521]3/2^-$ and $[633]7/2^+$ Nilsson orbitals were found in both $^{249,251}$Md. A surprising similarity of the $^{251}$Md ground-state band transition energies with those of the excited band of $^{255}$Lr has been discussed in terms of identical bands. Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations were performed to investigate the origin of the similarities between these bands.

## Key claim

The previously unobserved ground-state band of $^{251}$Md is assigned the $[514]7/2^-$ single-particle configuration based on transition intensities that depend on the gyromagnetic factor, with the alternative $[633]7/2^+$ configuration ruled out because its predicted electron intensities would exceed those of the $[514]7/2^-$ case by a factor of about 3, incompatible with the measurement.

## Method

Combined in-beam gamma-ray and conversion-electron spectroscopy was performed using the SAGE array at the University of Jyvaskyla, with $^{251}$Md produced via the $^{205}$Tl($^{48}$Ca,$2n$) reaction at 214 MeV beam energy and $^{249}$Md via $^{203}$Tl($^{48}$Ca,$2n$) at 215 MeV. Recoiling nuclei were separated with the RITU gas-filled separator and identified using recoil tagging. Gamma-gamma coincidences were used to identify rotational structures, while electron spectroscopy extended the observable rotational frequency range. Transition intensities were simulated using the particle-plus-rotor model to infer single-particle configurations and extract the orbital gyromagnetic factor.

## Key numbers

- Fusion-evaporation cross section for $^{205}$Tl($^{48}$Ca,$2n$)$^{251}$Md: about 760 nb at 214 MeV beam energy.
- Orbital gyromagnetic factor for the $^{251}$Md ground-state band: $g_K = 0.69^{+0.19}_{-0.16}$.
- Transition energy for the $13/2^- \rightarrow 9/2^-$ $E2$ transition in the $^{251}$Md ground-state band: 161.8 keV (gamma-ray), with the corresponding L and M conversion lines observed at 144 keV.
- Extrapolated energy for the unobserved $9/2 \rightarrow 7/2$ transition in the $^{251}$Md ground-state band: 61 keV, in agreement with the 62 keV value from decay spectroscopy by Asai (2015).
- Physical background in the $^{251}$Md electron spectrum: about 60% of the total population, peaking at about 80 keV.
- Average angular momentum entry point for the $^{251}$Md ground-state band: $b = 14 \hbar$, with diffuseness $c = 3 \hbar$.

## Context

This work extends in-beam spectroscopy of odd-Z transfermium nuclei, following earlier studies of $^{251}$Md by Chatillon (2007) which identified only the $[521]1/2^-$ rotational band. The identification of the $[514]7/2^-$ ground-state band and its gyromagnetic factor provides single-particle and collective properties needed to test nuclear models in the deformed midshell region approaching the predicted superheavy island of stability. The observed similarity between $^{251}$Md ground-state band transition energies and those of the excited band of $^{255}$Lr, discussed in terms of identical bands, was investigated with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations. The electron background analysis, consistent with the approach of Butler (2002) for $^{254}$No, enables future studies of high-K isomers and unresolved rotational bands in this mass region.
