# Calculation of Higher Order Effects in Electron-Positron Pair Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We present a calculation of higher order effects for the impact parameter dependent probability for single and multiple electron-positron pairs in (peripheral) relativistic heavy ion collisions. Also total cross sections are given for SPS and RHIC energies. We make use of the expression derived recently by several groups where the summation of all higher orders can be done analytically in the high energy limit. An astonishing result is that the cross section, that is, integrating over all impact parameters, is found to be identical to the lowest-order Born result for symmetric collisions. For the probability itself on the other hand we find rather large effects at small impact parameters compared to the lowest order results, which translate to large effects for the cross section for multiple pair production.

# Calculation of Higher Order Effects in Electron-Positron Pair Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Higher order effects in electron-positron pair production can be calculated analytically in the high energy limit using recently derived expressions, and while the total cross section integrated over all impact parameters is identical to the lowest-order Born result for symmetric collisions, the impact-parameter-dependent probability shows large deviations at small impact parameters that translate to substantial reductions in multiple pair production cross sections.

## Method

The authors used the analytic expression from Baltz and McLerran (1998) for the summation of higher order diagrams in the high energy limit, rewriting the complex-exponent photon propagators using a generalized Feynman parameter integration technique. They expanded the Fourier-transformed probability in polynomials of the auxiliary integration variables, performed the Fourier transform to obtain the impact-parameter-dependent probability, and then used a Poisson distribution to calculate multiple pair production probabilities. Numerical integration over final states was performed using the VEGAS Monte Carlo algorithm.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energy (gamma=10, Z=82, eta=0.59): single-pair Born cross section 4.21 kbarn, full calculation also 4.21 kbarn; two-pair Born cross section 8.61 barn, full calculation 3.88 barn; three-pair Born cross section 0.713 barn, full calculation 0.212 barn
- For Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy (gamma=100, Z=79, eta=0.57): single-pair Born cross section 34 kbarn, full calculation 34 kbarn; two-pair Born cross section 893 barn, full calculation 624 barn; three-pair Born cross section 113 barn, full calculation 53.9 barn; four-pair Born cross section 18.9 barn, full calculation 6.04 barn
- The Fourier transform of the probability for gamma=100 shows reduction of up to about 30% compared to lowest order for nonzero momentum transfer
- The Compton wavelength used as reference scale for small impact parameters is approximately 386 fm

## Context

This work extends the analytic summation of higher order effects recently developed for bound-free pair production by Baltz (1997) and for free pair production by Segev and Wells (1998) and Baltz and McLerran (1998). It provides the first numerical calculations of these higher order effects for realistic collision parameters at SPS and RHIC energies, showing that multiple pair production cross sections are substantially reduced compared to the perturbative Born results of Alscher, Hencken, Trautmann, and Baur (1997). The results enable experimental searches for higher order QED effects in relativistic heavy ion collisions through measurement of multiple pair production, which is sensitive to small impact parameters where the deviations are largest.
