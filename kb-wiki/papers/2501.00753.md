# Exploring QGP-like phenomena with Charmonia in $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV

> In ultrarelativistic collisions of nuclei at the Large Hadron Collider, the created QCD environment rapidly changes, leading to a non-adiabatic evolution of the quantum states involved. Considering this, we first examine the pre-equilibrium state of QCD matter and its effect on the initially produced charmonium using a temperature-independent Hamiltonian. As the QCD matter reaches local thermal equilibrium, this Hamiltonian transforms to its finite temperature counterpart. To model the pre-equilibrium stage, we use the bottom-up thermalization approach to determine the effective temperature of the QCD matter, followed by a Gubser-type expansion for the thermalized medium. Additionally, we consider collisional damping, gluonic dissociation, and regeneration mechanisms, which specifically modify the charmonium yield in the thermalized medium. Mainly, the gluonic dissociation and collisional damping cause a reduction in the yield conversely, regeneration through gluonic deexcitation enhances the yield of charmonium. Further, we explore the combined effects of these mechanisms on the collective yield of charmonium states with transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) and event multiplicity in the proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV. Based on our findings, we contend that the combined effects of these mechanisms can serve as a robust probe for determining the possible existence of a thermalized QCD medium in such a small collision system.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the combined effects of nonadiabatic evolution, collisional damping, gluonic dissociation, and regeneration of charmonia in proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV can serve as a probe for the possible existence of a thermalized QCD medium in small collision systems, but only if the effective temperature experienced by the charmonium states is properly angle-averaged to maintain causal consistency.

## Method

The authors model the pre-equilibrium stage of the QCD medium using the bottom-up thermalization approach to determine an effective pseudo-temperature, followed by a Gubser-type expansion with third-order viscous corrections for the thermalized hydrodynamic phase. They solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation using the Crank-Nicolson method to compute survival probabilities of charmonium states under nonadiabatic evolution, and combine this with calculations of collisional damping from the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential, gluonic dissociation cross sections, and regeneration via gluonic deexcitation using detailed balance, all within a transport equation framework.

## Key numbers

- Initial thermalization time assumed as tau_0 = 0.1 fm for p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
- Hydrodynamic temperature T_Hydro = 350 MeV at tau_Hydro = 0.3 fm used as initial condition for Gubser flow
- Maximum transverse size for high-multiplicity p+p collisions approximately 1.5 fm
- Evolution timescale tau_ev approximately 0.3 fm during thermalization phase for high-multiplicity p+p collisions
- Transition timescale tau_tr for charmonium states approximately 4.0 fm
- Collisional damping contributes approximately 90% of the total decay width for J/psi (ratio Gamma_c,nl / Gamma_D,nl varies around 0.9), while gluonic dissociation contributes approximately 10%

## Context

This work extends the unified model of quarkonia suppression (UMQS) developed by Singh et al. (2021) from heavy-ion and proton-nucleus collisions to proton-proton collisions at LHC energies. It builds on the nonadiabatic evolution framework introduced by Bagchi et al. (2023) and addresses the question raised by experimental observations from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS of QGP-like phenomena in high-multiplicity p+p collisions at 7 and 13 TeV. The study enables future experimental searches for thermalized QCD medium signatures in small collision systems by providing predictions for charmonium yield modifications as functions of transverse momentum and event multiplicity.
