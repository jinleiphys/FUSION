# Large $N_c$ QCD phase diagram at $μ_B = 0$

> Lattice studies suggest that at zero baryon chemical potential and increasing temperature there are three characteristic regimes in QCD that are connected by smooth analytical crossovers: a hadron gas regime at T < T_ch ~ 155 MeV, an intermediate regime, called stringy fluid, at T_ch < T < ~ 3 T_ch, and a quark-gluon plasma regime at higher temperatures. These regimes have been interpreted to reflect different approximate symmetries and effective degrees of freedom. In the hadron gas the effective degrees of freedom are hadrons and the approximate chiral symmetry of QCD is spontaneously broken. The intermediate regime has been interpreted as lacking spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking along with the emergence of new approximate symmetry, chiral spin symmetry, that is not a symmetry of the Dirac Lagrangian, but is a symmetry of the confining part of the QCD Lagrangian. While the high temperature regime is the usual quark-gluon plasma which is often considered to reflect "deconfinement" in some way. This paper explores the behavior of these regimes of QCD as the number of colors in the theory, N_c, gets large. In the large N_c limit the theory is center-symmetric and notions of confinement and deconfinement are unambiguous. The energy density is O(N_c^0) in the meson gas, O(N_c^1) in the intermediate regime and O(N_c^2) in the quark-gluon plasma regime. In the large N_c limit these regimes may become distinct phases separated by first order phase transitions. The intermediate phase has the peculiar feature that glueballs should exist and have properties that are unchanged from what is seen in the vacuum (up to 1/N_c corrections), while the ordinary dilute gas of mesons with broken chiral symmetry disappears and approximate chiral spin symmetry should emerge.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the large \(N_c\) limit of QCD at zero baryon chemical potential, the three regimes observed at \(N_c=3\) (hadron gas, intermediate "stringy fluid," and quark-gluon plasma) may become distinct phases separated by first-order phase transitions, but the existence of an intermediate confined phase without chiral symmetry breaking is not guaranteed and depends on assumptions about QCD dynamics.

## Method

The authors apply standard large \(N_c\) counting rules derived from planar diagram scaling to thermodynamic observables (energy density, pressure, entropy density) in each regime of QCD at zero baryon chemical potential. They analyze how correlation functions of local gauge-invariant color-singlet operators scale with \(N_c\) in the hadron gas, intermediate, and quark-gluon plasma regimes, and use these scalings to deduce whether the regimes can become distinct phases in the \(N_c \to \infty\) limit. The analysis incorporates known results from Witten (1979) and 't Hooft (1974) on meson and glueball properties at large \(N_c\), and lattice evidence from studies of chiral spin symmetry by Glozman (2019) and others.

## Key numbers

- The chiral pseudocritical temperature \(T_{ch} \sim 155\) MeV separates the hadron gas from the intermediate regime at \(N_c=3\).
- The intermediate regime extends from \(T_{ch}\) up to approximately \(3 T_{ch}\) at \(N_c=3\).
- Energy density scales as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^0)\) in the hadron gas, \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^1)\) in the intermediate regime, and \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^2)\) in the quark-gluon plasma.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s \sim 0.2\) characterizes the strongly coupled nature of matter at RHIC and LHC temperatures.
- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi \sim N_c^{1/2}\), chiral condensate \(\langle \overline{q}q \rangle \sim N_c^1\), and sigma terms for mesons and glueballs scale as \(N_c^0\).
- Meson decay amplitudes scale as \(N_c^{-1/2}\) and widths as \(N_c^{-1}\); glueball decay amplitudes scale as \(N_c^{-1}\) and widths as \(N_c^{-2}\).
- Meson-meson scattering amplitudes scale as \(N_c^{-1}\), while meson-glueball and glueball-glueball scattering amplitudes scale as \(N_c^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the lattice-based identification of three QCD regimes at \(N_c=3\) by Glozman (2019) and collaborators, who found evidence for an emergent chiral spin symmetry in the intermediate temperature range. The paper uses large \(N_c\) methods from Witten (1979) and 't Hooft (1974) to explore whether these regimes become distinct phases in the \(N_c \to \infty\) limit, where center symmetry becomes exact and confinement is unambiguous. The analysis enables future lattice studies at larger \(N_c\) to test which of the four proposed scenarios (ranging from two phases to three phases with various transition orders) is realized, and clarifies that glueballs in a putative intermediate phase would be unaffected by the medium at leading order in \(1/N_c\).
