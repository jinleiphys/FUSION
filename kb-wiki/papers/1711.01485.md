# Coupled dynamics of heavy and light flavor flow harmonics from EPOSHQ

> We pursue the study of event by event correlations between low-mass particles and heavy mesons flow harmonics in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions and clarify some ambiguities found in one of our previous work.

# Coupled dynamics of heavy and light flavor flow harmonics from EPOSHQ

## Key claim
The authors clarify that the anomalously large event-by-event fluctuations of heavy flavor flow harmonics reported in their previous work (Gossiaux et al., QM2014) were an artifact of an oversampling procedure, where 50 replications of heavy quark events per bulk event introduced spurious correlations that scaled with the number of replications. When the oversampling is removed, the fluctuations of D meson flow harmonics are dominated by the statistical 1/(2N) term rather than by genuine event-by-event fluctuations.

## Method
The authors use the EPOSHQ model to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, coupling heavy quarks to the quark-gluon plasma phase and allowing D mesons to interact with light hadrons in the hadronic phase. They perform event-by-event analysis using the event plane method, computing flow harmonics v_2 and v_3 for pions, nucleons, charm quarks, and D mesons, and decompose the variance of these harmonics into a statistical 1/(2N) contribution and a residual event-by-event contribution. They compare results with and without the previous oversampling technique (50 HQ replications per bulk event) to identify the source of spurious fluctuations.

## Key numbers
- For D mesons in 10%-30% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: var(v_2)=8.5E-2, var(v_3)=8E-2; the statistical 1/(2N) contribution accounts for 93% of v_2 fluctuations and 99% of v_3 fluctuations.
- For pions in the same centrality class: var(v_2)=8.2E-4, var(v_3)=3.3E-4; the 1/(2N) term accounts for only 16% (v_2) and 39% (v_3) of fluctuations, indicating dominance of genuine event-by-event fluctuations.
- For nucleons: var(v_2)=2.9E-3, var(v_3)=2.2E-3; the 1/(2N) term accounts for 62% (v_2) and 82% (v_3) of fluctuations.
- The residual event-by-event variance estimated from the linear response model gives var_EBE,2 approximately 6.8E-4 for pions, 1.2E-3 for nucleons, and 1.2E-3 for D mesons.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Nahrgang et al., 2015) comparing flow harmonics of light and heavy mesons, and corrects the anomalous fluctuation findings reported in Gossiaux et al. (QM2014). The results contradict the earlier conjecture of large event-by-event fluctuations specific to heavy flavor production and propagation in the quark-gluon plasma. The clarified framework enables experimental measurement of D meson-pion flow correlations using event engineering techniques at the LHC, with higher sqrt(s) collisions reducing fluctuations due to more abundant heavy quark production. The analysis follows the method of Poskanzer (1998) for decomposing flow variance.
