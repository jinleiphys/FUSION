# Deuteron P-Wave in Elastic Backward Proton-Deuteron Scattering

> The elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering is analyzed within a fully covariant approach based on the invariant expansion of the reaction amplitude. The method of calculation of any observables for any reaction mechanism is developed. The cross section and spin observables, like tensor analyzing power and polarization transfer coefficient etc., are investigated in explicit form within framework of the impulse approximation using different kinds of the relativistic deuteron wave function (DWF) with P-wave components. Our results of numerical calculation for complete set of polarization observables are compared with the experimental data and other calculations performed within both the non-relativistic and relativistic approaches. An experimental verification of the reaction mechanism is suggested by constructing some combinations of different observables.

## Key claim
The inclusion of a P-wave component in the relativistic deuteron wave function (DWF) improves the description of experimental data for the tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) and the polarization transfer \(\kappa_0\) in elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering, but the description remains unsatisfactory even with this addition, particularly at proton kinetic energies above 0.6 GeV.

## Method
The authors analyze elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering within a fully covariant formalism using an invariant expansion of the reaction amplitude into four complex helicity amplitudes. They compute the cross section and polarization observables within the impulse approximation (one-nucleon exchange, ONE) using relativistic deuteron wave functions that include both the large S- and D-wave components and the small P-wave components (\(V_s\) and \(V_t\)). The theoretical results are compared with experimental data and with non-relativistic and other relativistic calculations.

## Key numbers
- The P-wave probability \(P_V\) in the deuteron wave function that brings the polarization transfer \(\kappa_0\) close to experimental data is in the range \(0.4\% - 0.5\%\).
- The inclusion of the P-wave changes the form of \(T_{20}\) at laboratory momenta \(P_{\text{lab}} > 0.2\) GeV/c.
- The unpolarized differential cross section is proportional to \(\Sigma = 2[|\Phi_1|^2 + |\Phi_2|^2 + |\Phi_3|^2 + 2|\Phi_4|^2]\).
- The tensor analyzing power is given by \(A_{yy} = -T_{20}/\sqrt{2}\).
- The polarization transfer coefficient is \(\kappa_0 = (3/2)\kappa_t\).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of backward proton-deuteron scattering beyond the non-relativistic impulse approximation used by Ker69, which included only S- and D-wave components and failed to describe data above \(T_p > 0.6\) GeV. The authors build on the relativistic deuteron wave function formalism of Gross (1979) and Keister and Tjon (1982), and connect to the "Magic Circle" relation in the \(\kappa_0\)-\(T_{20}\) plane introduced by Kue93. The results suggest an analogy between P-wave effects and secondary interaction corrections (e.g., triangle graphs with a pion in the intermediate state) studied by Kon77, Kon81, and Lyk, and the work enables experimental verification of the ONE mechanism by measuring specific combinations of polarization observables.
