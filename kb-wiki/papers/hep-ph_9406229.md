# Why more hadronlike photons produce less particles on nuclear targets

> In hadron-nucleus interactions, the stronger is nuclear shadowing in the total cross section the higher is the multiplicity of secondary hadrons. In deep inelastic scattering, nuclear shadowing at small $x$ is associated with the hadronlike behaviour of photons as contrasted to the pointlike behaviour in the non-shadowing region of large $x$. In this paper we predict smaller mean multiplicity of secondary hadrons, and weaker fragmentation of the target nucleus, in deep inelastic leptoptoproduction on nuclei in the shadowing region of small $x$ as compared to the non-shadowing region of large $x$. This paradoxial conclusion has its origin in nuclear enhancement of the coherent diffraction dissociation of photons. We present numerical predictions for multiproduction in $μXe$ interactions studied by the Fermilab E665 collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors predict that, contrary to the naive expectation from hadron-nucleus interactions, the mean multiplicity of secondary hadrons in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei will be *smaller* in the shadowing region of small Bjorken x (where photons behave hadronlike) than in the non-shadowing region of large x (where photons are pointlike). This paradoxical conclusion arises because nuclear shadowing and diffraction dissociation of photons come together, and nuclear enhancement of coherent diffraction dissociation reduces the average multiplicity.

## Method

The authors use the dipole-cross section representation of diffractive deep inelastic scattering, where the virtual photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark pair (or higher Fock states) at large distances in front of the target. They apply multiple scattering theory to compute the coherent and incoherent diffraction dissociation cross sections on nuclear targets, using the nuclear optical thickness and the dipole cross section from Nikolaev and Zakharov (1991). The mass spectrum for diffraction dissociation is decomposed into a quark-antiquark excitation component and a triple-pomeron component, with the triple-pomeron coupling taken from real photoproduction phenomenology.

## Key numbers

- The fraction of diffraction dissociation events on free nucleons is approximately r_D ~ 10% of the total photoabsorption cross section, with the diffraction slope b_D ~ 10 GeV^{-2}
- For muon-xenon scattering at x ~ 10^{-3}, the shadowing parameter is nu_bar ~ 1.5, which would naively suggest a ~25% nuclear enhancement of mean multiplicity via the empirical relation R ~ (1/2)(1+nu_bar)
- The coherent diffraction dissociation cross section for light nuclei rises proportional to A^{1/3}, while for very heavy nuclei in the black-disc limit, coherent diffraction dissociation makes approximately 50% of the total DIS cross section
- The triple-pomeron coupling constant is A_{3Pom} ~ 0.16 GeV^{-2}, with a diffraction slope b_{3Pom} ~ 5-6 GeV^{-2}
- The condition for coherent diffraction dissociation to occur is x <~ 0.1 A^{-1/3}, corresponding to longitudinal momentum transfer kappa R_A <~ 1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dipole-cross section approach to nuclear shadowing (Nikolaev and Zakharov, 1991) and diffraction dissociation of photons (Nikolaev and Zakharov, 1992; Genovese, Nikolaev and Zakharov, 1993) to the novel prediction of suppressed multiproduction on nuclei. It contradicts the conventional wisdom from hadron-nucleus interactions, where stronger shadowing leads to higher multiplicities (as reviewed by Nikolaev, 1981), and provides specific predictions testable by the Fermilab E665 collaboration's muon-xenon data. The work enables experimental discrimination between diffractive and nondiffractive production mechanisms in nuclear deep inelastic scattering and adds to the growing interest in large-rapidity-gap physics at HERA.
