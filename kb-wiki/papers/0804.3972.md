# Toward an AdS/cold atoms correspondence: a geometric realization of the Schroedinger symmetry

> We discuss a realization of the nonrelativistic conformal group (the Schroedinger group) as the symmetry of a spacetime. We write down a toy model in which this geometry is a solution to field equations. We discuss various issues related to nonrelativistic holography. In particular, we argue that free fermions and fermions at unitarity correspond to the same bulk theory with different choices for the near-boundary asymptotics corresponding to the source and the expectation value of one operator. We describe an extended version of nonrelativistic general coordinate invariance which is realized holographically.

# Toward an AdS/cold atoms correspondence: a geometric realization of the Schroedinger symmetry

## Key claim

The paper constructs a geometric spacetime whose symmetry group is the Schroedinger group (the nonrelativistic conformal group), as a first step toward a possible gravity dual of fermions at unitarity; however, the author explicitly states that the paper does not claim to have found the actual gravity dual of the unitary Fermi gas, but only takes a possible first step toward such a duality.

## Method

The author embeds the Schroedinger group in d spatial dimensions into the relativistic conformal group O(d+2,2) by identifying the light-cone momentum with the mass operator, then constructs a d+3 dimensional metric that is invariant under the Schroedinger group but not under the full conformal group. A toy model of gravity coupled to a massive vector field with negative cosmological constant is written down, for which this metric is shown to be a solution to the coupled Einstein and Proca equations. The operator-field correspondence is then analyzed by studying a massive scalar field on this background, and the near-boundary asymptotics are related to sources and condensates in the dual nonrelativistic theory.

## Key numbers

- The cosmological constant and vector mass required for the metric to solve the field equations are: Lambda = -(1/2)(d+1)(d+2) and m^2 = 2(d+2).
- For the operator dual to the vector field A_mu, the dimension is Delta = d+3.
- The scaling dimension of an operator dual to a scalar field of bulk mass m_0 is given by Delta = (d+2)/2 + nu, where nu = sqrt(m^2 + (d+2)^2/4) and m^2 = m_0^2 + 2M^2.
- The lower bound on operator dimensions from the holographic construction is Delta > d/2.
- For the dimer operator psi_down psi_up in d=3: free fermions give Delta = 3, unitarity fermions give Delta = 2, which are symmetric about (d+2)/2 = 5/2.
- For N_up=2, N_down=1 in three spatial dimensions, the lowest value for nu is approximately -0.22728.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena 1997, Gubser 1998, Witten 1998) to nonrelativistic physics, specifically to the system of fermions at unitarity which has been studied experimentally using trapped cold atoms at Feshbach resonance (OHara, Jin, Grimm, Ketterle, Thomas, Salomon). The paper builds on the known Schroedinger symmetry of unitarity fermions (Mehen 1999) and the embedding of the Schroedinger algebra into the conformal algebra. It enables future work on constructing gravity duals of finite-density nonrelativistic systems, including black-hole metrics that realize nonrelativistic hydrodynamics and superfluid hydrodynamics, and suggests that free fermions and fermions at unitarity correspond to the same bulk theory with different choices of near-boundary asymptotics, analogous to the situation discussed by Klebanov (1999) for alternative quantizations.
