# Screening and finite size corrections to the octupole and Schiff moments

> Parity (P) and time reversal (T) violating nuclear forces create P, T -odd moments in expansion of the nuclear electrostatic potential. We derive expression for the nuclear electric octupole field which includes the electron screening correction (similar to the screening term in the Schiff moment). Then we calculate the Z alpha corrections to the Schiff moment which appear due to the finite nuclear size. Such corrections are important in heavy atoms with nuclear charge Z > 50. The Schiff and octupole moments induce atomic electric dipole moments (EDM) and P, T -odd interactions in molecules which are measured in numerous experiments to test CP-violation theories.

# Screening and finite size corrections to the octupole and Schiff moments

## Key claim

The paper derives electron screening corrections to the nuclear electric octupole moment and finite nuclear size (Z²α²) corrections to the Schiff moment, showing that these corrections are important for heavy atoms with nuclear charge Z > 50, but the Zα corrections to the Schiff moment are typically only 5-10%, with larger corrections (up to 34% for S' and 51% for L) appearing only when the main contributions are suppressed.

## Method

The authors expand the nuclear electrostatic potential including electron screening terms, then isolate the P,T-odd part to derive the screened octupole moment tensor expressed in terms of the nuclear electric dipole moment and quadrupole moment operator. For the Schiff moment, they equate matrix elements of the true nuclear P,T-odd potential with those of an effective finite-size potential, using the expansion of the electron transition density in odd powers of R to obtain analytic expressions for the Zα corrections. Numerical calculations are performed for ¹⁹⁹Hg and ²⁰⁵Tl using a finite-range P,T-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction with core polarization corrections calculated via the random phase approximation (RPA).

## Key numbers

- The Zα corrections to the Schiff moment S' are typically 5-10% for most cases in ¹⁹⁹Hg and ²⁰⁵Tl
- For ¹⁹⁹Hg with coupling constant g₀: ΔS/S₀ = 0.05 (bare), ΔS_tot/S_tot = 0.09 (with core polarization)
- For ¹⁹⁹Hg with coupling constant g₁: ΔS/S₀ = 0.05 (bare), ΔS_tot/S_tot = 0 (with core polarization)
- For ¹⁹⁹Hg with coupling constant g₂: ΔS/S₀ = 0.05 (bare), ΔS_tot/S_tot = 0.06 (with core polarization)
- For ²⁰⁵Tl with coupling constant g₀: ΔS/S₀ = 0.05 (bare), ΔS_tot/S_tot = -0.03 (with core polarization)
- For ²⁰⁵Tl with coupling constant g₁: ΔS/S₀ = 0.23 (bare), ΔS_tot/S_tot = -0.03 (with core polarization)
- For ²⁰⁵Tl with coupling constant g₂: ΔS/S₀ = 0.06 (bare), ΔS_tot/S_tot = -0.34 (with core polarization)
- The ratio b₃/b₁ for s-p₁/₂ matrix elements is -(3/5)Z²α²/R_N² and for s-p₃/₂ is -(9/20)Z²α²/R_N², with average approximately -0.5 Z²α²/R_N²
- The bare Schiff moment S₀ for ¹⁹⁹Hg ranges from -0.085 to 0.17 e·fm³ depending on coupling constant
- The bare Schiff moment S₀ for ²⁰⁵Tl ranges from -0.075 to 0.237 e·fm³ depending on coupling constant

## Context

This work extends the derivation of electron screening for nuclear moments beyond the Schiff moment (Sushkov 1984) to the nuclear electric octupole moment, following earlier work on octupole fields by Flambaum (1997). The finite nuclear size corrections to the Schiff moment build on the authors' previous calculations of the local dipole moment L (Flambaum 2002, Dmitriev 2005) and provide the corrected quantity S' that is needed for atomic EDM calculations using the finite-size Schiff moment potential (Dzuba 2002, 2007, 2009). The results enable more accurate interpretation of EDM measurements in ¹⁹⁹Hg and ²⁰⁵Tl as tests of CP-violation theories.
