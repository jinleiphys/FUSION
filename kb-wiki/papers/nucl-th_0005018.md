# Rotational Structure of T=0 and T=1 bands in the N=Z Nucleus 62Ga

> The rotational behaviour of T=0 and T=1 bands in the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 62Ga is studied theoretically using the spherical shell model (laboratory frame) and the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky model (intrinsic frame). Both models give a good description of available experimental data. The role of isoscalar and isovector pairing in the T=0 and T=1 bands as functions of angular momentum is studied in the shell model. The observed backbending in the T=0 band is interpreted as an unpaired band-crossing between two configurations with different deformation. The two configurations differ by 2p-2h and are found to terminate the rotational properties at Iπ=9+ and Iπ=17+, respectively. E2-decay matrix elements and spectroscopic quadrupole moments are calculated. From the CNS calculation, supported by shell model results, it is suggested that the low spin parts of the bands with T=0 and T=1 correspond to triaxially deformed states with the rotation taking place around the shortest axis (positive γ) and intermediate axis (negative γ), respectively. At lower spins the configuration space pf_{5/2}g_{9/2}, used in the shell model calculation, is found sufficient while also f_{7/2} becomes important above the backbending.

## Key claim

The observed backbending in the T=0 band of 62Ga is interpreted as an unpaired band-crossing between two configurations with different deformation, rather than a pairing-induced effect, with the two configurations differing by 2p-2h and terminating rotational properties at Iπ=9+ and Iπ=17+, respectively.

## Method

The authors performed spherical shell model calculations in the laboratory frame using the pf5/2g9/2 valence space with a modified effective interaction, and cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations in the intrinsic frame without pairing, allowing free minimization of quadrupole (ε and γ) and hexadecapole (ε4) degrees of freedom. Isoscalar and isovector pairing energy contributions were extracted from the shell model by subtracting a schematic L=0 pairing interaction from the full Hamiltonian. Occupation numbers in spherical j-shells were compared between the two models to understand configuration changes.

## Key numbers

- The T=0 band backbends at I≈9, with the low-spin configuration (fp)22 terminating at Iπ=9+ and the high-spin configuration (fp)20g9/22 terminating at Iπ=17+
- The 3+ T=0 state was found to be isomeric with a lifetime of 4.6(1.6) ns, corresponding to B(E2)=197(69) e2fm4
- Single-particle energies used in the shell model: εp3/2=0.0, εf5/2=0.77, εp1/2=1.113, εg9/2=2.5 MeV (modified from the experimental value of 3.7 MeV in 57Ni)
- Pairing strength parameters: G01=2.98 for T=1 pairing and G10=4.75 for T=0 pairing
- The T=1 I=0 ground state has pairing energy contributions of approximately 1.5 MeV from both T=0 and T=1 channels, totaling about 2.9 MeV; the lowest T=0 state (I=1) has about 2.1 MeV total pairing energy
- In the CNS calculation, the (fp)22 α=1 band shows γ≈25° at I=3 increasing to γ=60° at I=9; the (fp)20g9/22 α=1 band shows γ≈10° at I=7 increasing to γ=60° at I=17
- The CNS calculation predicts the 17+ state has approximately two holes in the spherical f7/2 shell, with total occupancy of 13.81 in f7/2

## Context

This work extends shell model and cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky studies previously applied to even-even nuclei such as 48Cr (Caurier et al., 1995; Juodagalvis, 1999) and 36Ar (Ar36 reference) to the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 62Ga. The results enable comparison of isoscalar and isovector pairing effects in T=0 and T=1 bands as functions of angular momentum, and suggest that the f7/2 shell becomes important above the backbending, indicating that future shell model calculations for high-spin states in this mass region may need to include the f7/2 orbital beyond the pf5/2g9/2 space.
