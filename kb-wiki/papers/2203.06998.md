# Mapping topology of skyrmions and fractional quantum Hall droplets to nuclear EFT for ultra-dense baryonic matter

> We describe the mapping at high density of topological structure of baryonic matter to a nuclear effective field theory that implements hidden symmetries emergent from strong nuclear correlations. The theory so constructed is found to be consistent with no conflicts with the presently available observations in both normal nuclear matter and compact-star matter. The hidden symmetries involved are "local flavor symmetry" of the vector mesons identified to be (Seiberg-)dual to the gluons of QCD and hidden "quantum scale symmetry" with an IR fixed point with a "genuine dilaton (GD)" characterized by non-vanishing pion and dilaton decay constants. Both the skyrmion topology for $N_f \geq 2$ baryons and the fractional quantum Hall (FQH) droplet topology for $N_f=1$ baryons are unified in the "homogeneous/hidden" Wess-Zumino term in the hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian. The possible indispensable role of the FQH droplets in going beyond the density regime of compact stars approaching scale-chiral restoration is explored by moving toward the limit where both the dilaton and the pion go massless.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a nuclear effective field theory (G$n$EFT) incorporating hidden local symmetry and hidden scale symmetry, with topological inputs from skyrmion-half-skyrmion transitions, can describe baryonic matter from normal nuclear density up to compact-star densities without explicit quark-gluon degrees of freedom, but the approach relies on the assumption that topological features extracted from skyrmion crystals are "robust" and that the baryon-quark crossover density lies in the range $2 \lesssim n_{1/2}/n_0 < 4$, which is not predicted by the theory itself.

## Method

The author maps topological properties of dense skyrmion matter, extracted from crystal lattice simulations using a chiral-scale-invariant hidden local symmetry Lagrangian (${\cal L}_{\chi{\rm HLS}}$) with vector mesons ($\rho,\omega$) and a dilaton field, to a generalized nuclear effective field theory (G$n$EFT) formulated as a density functional theory. The mapping incorporates a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density $n_{1/2}$, which is identified with the baryon-quark crossover, and implements the resulting features (cusp in symmetry energy, parity doubling, density-independent mean fields) into a Lagrangian with explicit baryon fields coupled to the $\chi$HLS fields.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter equilibrium density: $n_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- Topology change density range: $2 \lesssim n_{1/2}/n_0 < 4$ (from astrophysical constraints)
- Vector meson mass scale: $m_V \sim 700$ MeV (cutoff for hidden local symmetry)
- Dilaton mass in vacuum: $\sim 500$ MeV
- Chiral-invariant nucleon mass from parity doubling: $m_0 \sim (0.6-0.9) m_N$
- Vector manifestation fixed point density: $n_{\rm VM} \gtrsim 25 n_0$ (where $m_\rho \sim g_\rho \to 0$)
- Crystal lattice size at half-skyrmion onset: $L_{1/2} \lesssim 2.9$ fm

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and the genuine dilaton scenario of Crewther and Tunstall (2015) to dense baryonic matter, building on skyrmion crystal simulations by Park, Lee, and collaborators (2010-2018). It contrasts with standard chiral effective field theory ($s$ChEFT), which is expected to break down above $\sim 2n_0$, and with phenomenological density functional approaches that lack direct connection to QCD topology. The approach enables predictions for the equation of state of compact stars, including a pseudo-conformal sound speed, and suggests a parallel between the mapping of fractional quantum Hall effect topology to Kohn-Sham density functional theory (as in Jain, 2007) and the mapping of skyrmion topology to nuclear density functional theory.
