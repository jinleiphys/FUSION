# Pion photoproduction on the nucleon in the quark model

> We present a detailed quark-model study of pion photoproduction within the effective Lagrangian approach. Cross sections and single-polarization observables are investigated for the four charge channels, $γp\to π^+ n$, $γn\to π^- p$, $γp\to π^0 p$, and $γn\to π^0 n$. Leaving the $πNΔ$ coupling strength to be a free parameter, we obtain a reasonably consistent description of these four channels from threshold to the first resonance region. Within this effective Lagrangian approach, strongly constrainted by the quark model, we consider the issue of double-counting which may occur if additional {\it t}-channel contributions are included.

## Key claim

The quark-model-based effective Lagrangian approach, with the πNΔ coupling strength treated as a free parameter, provides a reasonably consistent description of all four charged channels of pion photoproduction from threshold to the first resonance region, though the symmetric quark model underestimates the Delta resonance contribution and the forward peaking above 500 MeV.

## Method

The authors combine a constituent quark model with an effective chiral Lagrangian for the quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling, treating the pion as an elementary particle and calculating tree-level diagrams for s-channel, u-channel, t-channel, and seagull terms. Transition amplitudes are expressed in a harmonic oscillator basis with SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry, and the πNΔ coupling strength is treated as a free parameter (CΔ = 1.7474 relative to the quark model symmetry limit) while fixing gπNN = 13.48 via the Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Key numbers

- CΔ = 1.7474: the enhancement factor for the πNΔ coupling strength relative to the quark model symmetry limit (CΔ = 1), fixed by experimental data for charged pion production
- gπNN = 13.48: the πNN coupling constant fixed as an input parameter, constraining the Born terms via the Goldberger-Treiman relation
- Energy range: Eγ ≲ 700 MeV, covering from threshold through the first resonance region including Δ(1232), S11(1535), and D13(1520)
- The forward "spike" and dip at −t = mπ² in γp → π⁺n differential cross sections are reproduced up to Eγ ≈ 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-model effective Lagrangian approach previously applied to η photoproduction (Li 1995, Zhao 2001) and K photoproduction (Li 1995, 1997) to the extensive pion photoproduction database. The model contrasts with phenomenological isobaric approaches such as MAID (Drechsel 1999) and SAID fits (Arndt 1996) by providing a microscopic framework with fewer free parameters, though it lacks unitarity. The results enable future studies of symmetry breaking effects, particularly the mixing between S11(1535) and S11(1650) states needed above the second resonance region, and suggest that self-consistent treatment of nucleon pole terms and Delta excitation is essential for viable models.
