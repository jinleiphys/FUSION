# Chiral Perturbation Theory for Staggered Sea Quarks and Ginsparg-Wilson Valence Quarks

> We study lattice QCD with staggered sea and Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks. The Symanzik effective action for this mixed lattice theory, including the lattice spacing contributions of O(a^2), is derived. Using this effective theory we construct the leading order chiral Lagrangian. The masses and decay constants of pseudoscalars containing two Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks are computed at one loop order.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the leading-order chiral Lagrangian for a mixed lattice QCD theory with staggered sea quarks and Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks, finding that the mixed theory introduces only one additional operator compared to standard staggered chiral perturbation theory, with the consequence that valence-valence pseudoscalar meson masses receive no one-loop contribution from this new operator.

## Method

The authors derive the Symanzik effective action for the mixed lattice theory, including all operators of order a^2, by constructing the allowed mixed four-fermion operators containing one sea and one valence bilinear. They then perform a spurion analysis to promote the coefficients of these operators to spurion fields that transform under the chiral flavor symmetry group, and construct the chiral Lagrangian from the pseudoscalar field Sigma and the spurion fields with the requirement of invariance under flavor rotations. The masses and decay constants of pseudoscalars containing two Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks are computed at one-loop order using this chiral Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The mixed four-fermion operators in the Symanzik action are of order a^2, with no dimension-five operators allowed by the symmetries
- The power counting adopted assumes m_q/Lambda_QCD is approximately equal to a^2 Lambda_QCD^2, where Lambda_QCD is of order 300 MeV
- The tree-level pion decay constant normalization corresponds to f approximately 131 MeV
- The leading order chiral Lagrangian contains the term a^2 V, where V = U_S + U_prime_S + U_V, with U_V containing the single new operator -C_Mix <tau_3 Sigma tau_3 Sigma^dagger>

## Context

This work extends the mixed-action chiral perturbation theory framework previously developed for Wilson sea quarks with Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks by Bar, Bernard, Rupak, and Shoresh (2003-2004) to the case of staggered sea quarks. It parallels the staggered chiral perturbation theory (SchPT) developed by Lee, Sharpe, Aubin, and Bernard (1999-2004) for unmixed staggered simulations. The results enable analysis of numerical data from mixed simulations that use existing staggered configurations (such as the MILC configurations) with domain-wall or overlap valence quarks, as reported in preliminary work by Renner (2004) and Bowler (2004).
