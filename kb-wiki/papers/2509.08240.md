# The IBM Description of the B(E2) Anomaly: Dynamical Triaxiality and Configuration Mixing

> A theoretical investigation has been carried out to examine the effects of dynamical triaxility and configuration mixing on the $B(E2)$ anomaly, characterized by $B_{4/2}=B(E2;4_1^+\rightarrow2_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+)<1.0$ and $R_{4/2}=E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)\geq2.0$, within the framework of the interacting boson model (IBM). The results indicate that the effective $γ$ deformation may undergo substantial changes with increasing angular momentum in the $B(E2)$ anomaly system governed by an IBM Hamiltonian involving rotor-like terms. Further calculations reveal that mixing between normal and intruder states may facilitate the emergence of $B(E2)$ anomaly, thereby providing additional theoretical insights into this unusual phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the B(E2) anomaly (B4/2 less than 1.0 with R4/2 at least 2.0) can arise from dynamical triaxiality and configuration mixing within the interacting boson model, but the authors explicitly state they are not providing a new fit to experimental data and instead focus on theoretical mechanisms. The effective gamma deformation may undergo substantial changes with increasing angular momentum, and mixing between normal and intruder states may facilitate the emergence of the anomaly.

## Method

The authors constructed an IBM Hamiltonian containing a consistent-Q term, a cubic d-boson term (V3), a rotor-like LQL term, and an L2 term, then calculated the classical potential using coherent states to examine mean-field geometries. They computed effective gamma deformations and their fluctuations for yrast states using two different definitions (gamma_a from quadrupole operator expectation values and gamma_b from SU(3) quantum numbers). For configuration mixing, they employed the IBM-CM scheme with a 2x2 matrix Hamiltonian mixing [N] and [N+2] boson spaces, using parameters from prior studies of Pt isotopes and exploring weak versus strong mixing between different symmetry limits (U(5), SU(3), O(6)).

## Key numbers

- For the 170Os parameter set (N=9, chi=-0.98): R4/2=2.83, B4/2=0.50, Q(2_1+)/e approximately -8.5
- For the same system without rotor-like terms: R4/2=2.99, B4/2=1.46, Q(2_1+)/e approximately -10.2
- For the cubic term plus O(6) quadrupole case (chi=0): B4/2=1.36, R4/2=2.57, Q(2_1+)/e approximately 0.0
- Effective gamma deformation for case A (with rotor terms): gamma_a increases from 10 degrees at L=0 to 31 degrees at L=8; gamma_b increases from 9 degrees to 44 degrees
- For 172Pt: experimental B4/2 approximately 0.55(19) with R4/2 approximately 2.34 (Cederwall 2018)

## Context

This work extends prior IBM analyses of the B(E2) anomaly by Zhang (2022, 2024, 2025), Wang (2020), Pan (2024), and Teng (2025) that used band-mixing mechanisms, but now explicitly examines dynamical triaxiality and configuration mixing. The study connects to mean-field calculations for neutron-deficient nuclei near N=90 and the proton dripline (Grahn 2016, Saygi 2017, Cederwall 2018, Goasduff 2019, Zhang 2021, Zanon 2025) and to prior IBM-CM studies of Pt isotopes (Dracoulis 1994, King 1998, Harder 1997, Morales 2008, Ramos 2009, 2011, 2014) that emphasized intruder configurations from 2p-2h excitations across the Z=82 shell gap. The results enable future work to determine effective gamma deformation from experimental observables and to apply configuration-mixing IBM to other B(E2) anomaly nuclei.
