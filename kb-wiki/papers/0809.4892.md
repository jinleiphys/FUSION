# Parity violation in $d \vecγ -> np$ with pionless effective theory

> We consider a pionless effective theory with dibaryon fields for the description of the weak process involving two nucleons. We construct leading order Lagrangians that contain nucleon-dibaryon weak coupling constants. We calculate the physical observable in the photodisintegration of the deuteron at threshold and obtain the result in terms of the nucleon-dibaryon weak coupling constants. Relation to existing calculations is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the parity-violating photon polarization in deuteron photodisintegration ($d\vec{\gamma} \to np$) at threshold using a pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields, but the result is expressed in terms of unknown nucleon-dibaryon weak coupling constants rather than directly predicting a numerical value.

## Method

The authors constructed leading-order parity-violating Lagrangians for nucleon-dibaryon interactions with isospin change $\Delta T = 0$ and $\Delta T = 1$, introducing dimensionless weak coupling constants $h^{0s}_{\text{dNN}}$, $h^{0t}_{\text{dNN}}$, and $h^{1}_{\text{dNN}}$. They computed the parity-violating E1 amplitude from Feynman diagrams where the weak interaction admixes $^1P_1$ or $^3P_0$ states into the initial $^3S_1$ deuteron or final $^1S_0$ scattering state, using the convection current operator for the E1 transition and the previously calculated parity-conserving M1 amplitude from the same theory.

## Key numbers

- The parity-violating photon polarization $P_\gamma$ at threshold is given by $P_\gamma = - \frac{a_0 \gamma}{6 \sqrt{2 \pi m_N \rho_d}} \frac{h^{0s}_{\text{dNN}} + h^{0t}_{\text{dNN}}}{\kappa_1 (1 - a_0 \gamma) - \frac{L_1}{2} a_0 \gamma^2}$, where $\kappa_1 = \mu_V/2 = 2.35$ and $L_1$ is a low-energy constant fixed from the $np \to d\gamma$ total cross section at threshold.
- The scattering length $a_0$ is for the $^1S_0$ scattering state, $\rho_d$ is the effective range for the $^3S_1$ bound state, and $\gamma = \sqrt{m_N B}$ with $B$ the deuteron binding energy.
- Diagram (a) is suppressed relative to diagrams (b) and (c) by $p^2$, where $p$ is the outgoing nucleon momentum.

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields, previously successful for parity-conserving processes (Ando 2005, 2006, 2008), to parity-violating observables in few-nucleon systems. It builds on earlier parity-violating EFT work by Savage (2001), which only partially accounted for the general pionless PV Lagrangian, and relates to the meson-exchange DDH potential (Desplanques, Donoghue, Holstein 1980). The result enables future comparison between pionless EFT and conventional meson-exchange calculations, but requires establishing the relation between the new weak dibaryon coupling constants and the traditional weak meson-nucleon couplings, as well as clarifying the role of electromagnetic operators beyond the convection current approximation.
