# Nucleon Form Factors from Dispersion Theory

> I review the results of a recent dispersion--theoretical analysis of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors and comment on the strangeness form factors. The need for a better data basis at low, intermediate and large momentum transfer is stressed.

# Nucleon Form Factors from Dispersion Theory

## Key claim

The dispersion-theoretical analysis of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, updated to include perturbative QCD scaling and other constraints, can describe all existing data with only three effective fit parameters; however, the extracted strange form factor values should be considered upper bounds, and the current data are not precise enough to definitively confirm the onset of pQCD scaling.

## Method

Unsubtracted dispersion relations were written for the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, with spectral functions constructed as a superposition of a hadronic part (the two-pion continuum plus three additional isovector and three isoscalar vector meson poles) and a quark contribution parametrized by a logarithmic function to enforce the pQCD asymptotic behaviour. The spectral functions were constrained by unitarity in the two-pion channel (following Frazer and Fulco), pQCD superconvergence relations, the precisely measured neutron charge radius, and a stability condition that minimized the number of additional poles while still fitting the data. This left eight free parameters, but identification of all three isoscalar and two of the isovector pole masses with physical mesons reduced the effective fit parameters to three.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: r_E^p = 0.847 fm, with about 1% uncertainty
- Proton magnetic radius: r_M^p = 0.836 fm, with about 1% uncertainty
- Neutron magnetic radius: r_M^n = 0.889 fm, with about 1% uncertainty (5% larger than the previous dispersive analysis by Hohler et al. (1976))
- omega-NN vector coupling: g_omegaNN^2/(4pi) = 34.6 +/- 0.8
- omega-NN tensor-to-vector coupling ratio: kappa_omega = -0.16 +/- 0.01
- phi-NN vector coupling: g_phiNN^2/(4pi) = 6.7 +/- 0.3
- phi-NN tensor-to-vector coupling ratio: kappa_phi = -0.22 +/- 0.01
- Strange magnetic moment: mu_s = -0.24 +/- 0.03 nuclear magnetons
- Strangeness radius: r_s^2 = 0.21 +/- 0.03 fm^2
- Strange form factor F_2^s(t) follows a dipole with cutoff mass 1.46 GeV
- The separation scale between hadronic and pQCD regimes is Lambda^2 approximately 10 GeV^2 for the best fit

## Context

This work extends the earlier dispersive analysis by Hohler et al. (1976) by incorporating pQCD superconvergence relations (from Brodsky and Farrar (1975)) and the updated neutron charge radius measurement from Kopecky et al. (1995). The analysis enables extraction of the omega-NN and phi-NN coupling constants from the pole residues, and provides upper bounds on the strange vector form factors following the approach of Jaffe (1989). The author stresses that more accurate data at low, intermediate, and large momentum transfer from facilities such as ELSA, MAMI, and CEBAF are needed to sharpen the extraction of nucleon radii, vector meson-nucleon couplings, and the onset of perturbative QCD.
