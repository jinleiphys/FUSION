# e+e- pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at intermediate impact parameters

> Using the quasiclassical Green's function in the Coulomb field, we analyze the probabilities of single and multiple $e^+e^-$-pair production at fixed impact parameter $b$ between colliding ultrarelativistic heavy nuclei. We perform calculations in the Born approximation with respect to the parameter $Z_Bα$, and exactly in $Z_Aα$, $Z_{A}$ and $Z_{B}$ are the charge numbers of the corresponding nuclei. We also obtain the approximate formulas for the probabilities valid for $Z_Aα, Z_Bα\lesssim 1$.

## Key claim

The paper provides an analytical expression for the average number of electron-positron pairs produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at intermediate impact parameters, valid in the leading logarithmic approximation for the case where the charge of one nucleus is small (Z_B alpha << 1) while the other charge can be up to order unity (Z_A alpha less than or approximately 1), and for impact parameters up to about the electron Compton wavelength (mb less than or approximately 1).

## Method

The authors use the quasiclassical Green's function of the Dirac equation in the Coulomb field of one nucleus to calculate the probability of e+e- pair production, treating the other nucleus in the Born approximation (lowest order in Z_B alpha). They derive an analytical expression for the function F(mb) that determines the average number of produced pairs W(b) = zeta * L * F(mb), where zeta = (Z_A alpha)^2 (Z_B alpha)^2 and L = ln(gamma). The calculation is performed in the leading logarithmic approximation, and the results are tabulated for various nuclear charges and impact parameters.

## Key numbers

* For mb = 0.01 (small impact parameter), the Born value of F(x) is 3.42, while for uranium (Z=92) it is 2.56, showing significant Coulomb corrections.
* For mb = 1.0, the Born value of F(x) is 0.491, while for uranium it is 0.411.
* For mb = 10, the Born value of F(x) is 1.99 x 10^{-2}, and for uranium it is 1.87 x 10^{-2}.
* For mb = 100, the Born value of F(x) is 3.43 x 10^{-4}, and for uranium it is 3.33 x 10^{-4}.
* The asymptotic form for large impact parameters (1 << mb << sqrt(gamma)) is F(mb) = (56/(9 pi^2 (mb)^2)) ln(mb).
* The Coulomb correction term for large mb is F_A(mb) = -(28/(9 pi^2 (mb)^2)) f(Z_A alpha), where f(x) = Re[psi(1+ix)+C] with C = 0.577... (Euler constant).
* The accuracy of the leading logarithmic expression for the total cross section sigma_T is better than 0.4% for RHIC and LHC colliders.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions by providing analytical results for the impact-parameter-dependent probability at intermediate impact parameters (mb less than or approximately 1), where previous numerical calculations by Hencken, Trautmann, and Baur (1995, 1999) and by Baltz (2005, 2006) required computationally intensive nine-fold integrals. The authors confirm and extend the asymptotic results of Lee, Milstein, and Strakhovenko (2002) for large impact parameters and disprove an alternative ansatz suggested by Guclu (2005). The results enable straightforward calculation of the pair production probability for any Lorentz factor gamma >> 1 using the gamma-independent function F(mb) tabulated here, which is simpler than the previous numerical approaches.
