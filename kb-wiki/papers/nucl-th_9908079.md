# Relativistic Modification of the Gamow Factor

> In processes involving Coulomb-type initial- and final-state interactions, the Gamow factor has been traditionally used to take into account these additional interactions. The Gamow factor needs to be modified when the magnitude of the effective coupling constant increases or when the velocity increases. For the production of a pair of particles under their mutual Coulomb-type interaction, we obtain the modification of the Gamow factor in terms of the overlap of the Feynman amplitude with the relativistic wave function of the two particles. As a first example, we study the modification of the Gamow factor for the production of two bosons. The modification is substantial when the coupling constant is large.

## Key claim

The traditional Gamow factor, used to account for Coulomb-type initial- and final-state interactions in particle production and reaction processes, requires a relativistic modification when the effective coupling constant is large or when the relative velocity is high; the modification is obtained by evaluating the overlap of the Feynman amplitude with the relativistic two-body wave function, rather than by taking the absolute square of the wave function at the origin, which becomes infinite in the relativistic case.

## Method

The authors derived the relativistic K-factor (the ratio of the production cross section with final-state interaction to that without) for a pair of scalar bosons produced via two-photon fusion, using the Klein-Gordon equation for two particles under a mutual Coulomb-type potential V(r) = -alpha/r. They computed the overlap integral of the relativistic Coulomb wave function (obtained from the Klein-Gordon equation) with the Feynman amplitude for the production process, and compared it to the overlap with the free wave function. The resulting K-factor is expressed analytically as a series expansion in terms of the dimensionless parameters eta = alpha/v and alpha^2, where v is the relative velocity.

## Key numbers

* For alpha = 0.01 (weak coupling), the K-factor closely follows the traditional Gamow factor; the ratio K/G(eta) is approximately 1.
* For alpha = 0.32 (strong coupling), the ratio K/G(eta) decreases significantly, indicating that the Gamow factor overestimates the final-state interaction effect.
* The K-factor decreases as eta decreases (i.e., as velocity increases) for fixed alpha; the limiting value is K = 1 as eta approaches alpha (v approaches 1).
* For a fixed velocity v, the K-factor increases as the coupling constant alpha increases.
* The critical value of alpha for the S-state (l=0) is 1/2, above which the wave function behavior changes qualitatively.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Gamow-Sommerfeld factor (Gamow 1928, Sommerfeld 1939) to the relativistic regime, addressing cases where the coupling constant is large (e.g., color-Coulomb interactions with alpha ~ 0.2-0.4 in quark-antiquark production) or where velocities become relativistic. The method avoids the divergence of the relativistic wave function at the origin, which prevents the standard approach of taking |psi(0)|^2, as noted by Crater (1991). The results enable more accurate treatment of final-state interactions in processes such as quark and gluon production, where the traditional Gamow factor would overestimate the enhancement. The authors plan to extend the formalism to fermions and gluons in future work.
