# The Continuum Structure of the Borromean Halo Nucleus 11Li

> We solve the Faddeev equations for 11Li (n+n+9Li) using hyperspherical coordinates and analytical expressions for distances much larger than the effective ranges of the interactions. The lowest resonances are found at 0.65 MeV (1/2+, 3/2+, 5/2+) and 0.89 MeV (3/2+, 3/2-) with widths of about 0.35 MeV. A number of higher-lying broader resonances are also obtained and related to the Efimov effect. The dipole strength function and the Coulomb dissociation cross section are also calculated. PACS numbers: 21.45.+v, 11.80.Jy, 21.60.Gx

## Key claim

The paper reports low-lying resonances in the Borromean halo nucleus 11Li, but the authors note that the lowest 1−-resonance at about 0.65 MeV with width 0.35 MeV is only in "fair agreement" with a recent measurement at 1.0 ± 0.1 MeV, and they "have not attempted to reproduce" that precise energy.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations for the n+n+9Li three-body system using hyperspherical coordinates, treating the angular part numerically at short distances and using analytical expressions for the large-distance behavior where the interactions are dominated by scattering lengths. The S-matrix is diagonalized to obtain eigenphases, and resonance energies and widths are extracted via the complex energy method by locating S-matrix poles. The neutron-neutron interaction reproduces low-energy nucleon-nucleon properties, while the neutron-9Li interaction uses Gaussian potentials with strengths tuned to reproduce the known structure of 10Li and the 11Li binding energy of 305 keV.

## Key numbers

- Lowest resonances: 0.65 MeV (1/2+, 3/2+, 5/2+) and 0.89 MeV (3/2+, 3/2−), each with widths of about 0.35 MeV
- 11Li ground state binding energy: 305 keV, with root mean square radius 3.34 fm
- Coulomb dissociation cross sections on lead target: 4.487 b at 42 MeV/A, 2.128 b at 180 MeV/A, 1.429 b at 280 MeV/A, and 0.971 b at 800 MeV/A
- Effective radial barrier heights: 1.7 MeV for 1/2−, 0.9 MeV for 1/2+, 0.7 MeV for 3/2+, and 0.6 MeV for 5/2+
- Sum of scattering lengths: a_nn μ_nn^{-1} + 2 a_cn μ_cn^{-1} ≈ 50 fm

## Context

This work extends the three-body Faddeev method of Fedorov, Jensen, and collaborators (1993, 1995, 1996, 1997) from zero core spin to finite core spin, enabling continuum calculations for 11Li. The computed dipole strength functions and Coulomb dissociation cross sections are compared with experimental data from Zinser et al. (1997), Sackett et al. (1993), and Shimoura et al. (1995), and with earlier limited-basis calculations by Danilin et al. (1994). The results enable future tests of three-body models against measured strength functions and provide a framework for studying the Efimov effect in nuclear halos.
