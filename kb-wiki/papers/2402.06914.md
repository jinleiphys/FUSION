# Examination of the $ ϕ-NN $ bound-state problem with lattice QCD $ N-ϕ$ potentials

> The developed Faddeev three-body equations are solved to search for bound-state solutions of a phi-meson $ \left(ϕ\right) $ and two nucleons $ \left( \textrm{NN} \right) $ system. The newly published spin $ 3/2 $ $ \textrm{N-\ensuremathϕ} $ potential based on the $ \left(\textrm{2+1}\right) $-flavor lattice QCD simulations near physical point, and realistic $ \textrm{NN} $ Malfliet-Tjon (MT) potential, are employed. Our numerical calculations for $ (I)J^π=(0)2^{-}$ $ ϕ\textrm{-d} $ system in maximum spin leads to ground state binding energy of about $ 7 $ MeV and a matter radius of about $ 8 $ fm. Our results indicates the possibility of the formation of new nuclear clusters.

## Key claim

The phi-meson and two-nucleon system forms a bound state in the $(I)J^\pi=(0)2^{-}$ channel with a binding energy of about 7 MeV, but only when the maximal spin channel is selected; no bound state is found for the $(I)J^\pi=(1)1^{-}$ channels (phi-nn and phi-pp). The result is conditional on using the newly published lattice QCD N-phi potential in the $^{4}S_{3/2}$ channel, which itself does not support a two-body N-phi bound state.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev three-body equations in coordinate space using an expansion in hyperspherical harmonics (HH method), implemented with the FaCE computational toolkit. They employed the spin-3/2 N-phi potential from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations near the physical point (pion mass 146.4 MeV, kaon mass 525 MeV) in three analytic parametrizations (A_erfc, A_exp, and B-3G), together with the semi-realistic Malfliet-Tjon (MT) NN potential. The calculations were performed for the $(I)J^\pi=(0)2^{-}$ phi-deuteron system with convergence achieved at maximum hypermomentum K_max = 70 and maximum hyperradial excitations i_max = 25.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of the $(I)J^\pi=(0)2^{-}$ phi-d system: 6.9 MeV (experimental masses) and 7.3 MeV (lattice QCD masses) for the A_erfc N-phi potential model.
- Nuclear matter radius of the phi-d system: 8.33 fm (experimental masses) and 8.35 fm (lattice QCD masses) for the A_erfc model.
- For the A_exp model: binding energy 6.8 MeV (experimental masses) and 7.2 MeV (lattice masses); matter radius 8.24 fm (experimental) and 8.25 fm (lattice).
- For the B-3G model: binding energy 6.7 MeV (experimental masses) and 7.1 MeV (lattice masses); matter radius 8.08 fm (experimental) and 8.05 fm (lattice).
- The N-phi two-body scattering length from the A_erfc potential: a_0 = -1.43(23) fm, effective range r_eff = 2.36(10) fm; no two-body bound state is formed.
- The MT NN potential supports a deuteron binding energy of -2.2307 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phi-NN bound states by Belyaev (2008, 2009) and Sofianos (2010), who used phenomenological attractive N-phi interactions and found binding energies of about 40 MeV (triplet NN) or 23 MeV (singlet NN). The present calculation replaces those phenomenological potentials with the first lattice QCD-derived N-phi potential from the HAL QCD collaboration (Yan, 2022), yielding much smaller binding energies (about 7 MeV). The results enable future experimental searches for phi-deuteron bound states via femtoscopic correlation measurements, analogous to the proton-phi correlation functions already measured by the ALICE collaboration and the hadron-deuteron correlations studied for pd, K-d, Lambda-d, and Xi-d systems.
