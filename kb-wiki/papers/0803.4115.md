# Manifestly-covariant chiral PT calculation of nucleon Compton scattering

> We compute the Compton scattering off the nucleons in the framework of manifestly covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (B$χ$PT). The results for observables differ substantially from the corresponding calculations in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$χ$PT), most appreciably in the forward kinematics. We verify that the covariant $p^3$ result fulfills the forward-Compton-scattering sum rules. We also explore the effect of the $Δ$(1232) resonance at order $p^4/\varDelta$, with $\varDelta\approx 300$ MeV, the resonance excitation energy. We find that the substantial effect of the $Δ$-excitation on the nucleon polarizabilities can naturally be accommodated in the manifestly covariant calculation.

## Key claim

The manifestly-covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) calculation of nucleon Compton scattering at order \(p^3\) fulfills forward-Compton-scattering sum rules, whereas the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) result does not; the substantial effect of the Δ(1232) resonance on nucleon polarizabilities can be naturally accommodated in the covariant calculation, in contrast to the HBχPT approach.

## Method

The authors computed the Compton scattering amplitude on nucleons using manifestly-covariant BχPT, evaluating one-loop graphs at order \(p^3\) and including Δ(1232)-resonance contributions at order \(p^4/\Delta\) (with \(\Delta \approx 300\) MeV). A field redefinition transformed the pseudovector πNN coupling into a pseudoscalar one, reducing the number of one-loop graphs and simplifying the calculation. The loop integrals were computed using the algebraic tool FORM and the LoopTools library, with on-mass-shell renormalization and standard parameter values: \(g_A=1.267\), \(f_\pi = 0.0924\) GeV, \(m_\pi = 0.139\) GeV, \(M_N = 0.9383\) GeV, \(\kappa_N = 1.79\) for the proton.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability \(\alpha^{(p)} = 6.8 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) at BχPT order \(p^3\); increases to \(10.8 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) when including the Δ(1232) contribution at order \(p^4/\Delta\).
- Proton magnetic polarizability \(\beta^{(p)} = -1.8 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) at BχPT order \(p^3\); increases to \(2.9 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) when including the Δ(1232) contribution at order \(p^4/\Delta\).
- For comparison, HBχPT at order \(p^3\) gives \(\alpha^{(p)} = 12.2 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) and \(\beta^{(p)} = 1.22 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\); the Particle Data Group experimental values are \(\alpha^{(p)} = 12.0 \pm 0.6 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) and \(\beta^{(p)} = 1.9 \pm 0.5 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\).
- The Δ(1232) resonance excitation energy is \(\Delta \approx 300\) MeV; the γNΔ transition parameters used are \(g_M = 2.95\) and \(g_E = -1.0\), with πNΔ coupling \(h_A = 2.85\) inferred from the Δ → πN decay width of 115 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original BχPT calculation of nucleon polarizabilities by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991) and contrasts with the HBχPT approach developed by Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and later applied to Compton scattering by Beane et al. (2004) and others. The authors show that the covariant calculation resolves the incompatibility of HBχPT with dispersion relations and sum rules noted by Lvov (1993) and Pascalutsa (2004), and that the Δ(1232) contribution can be included with realistic parameters, unlike the HBχPT fit by Pascalutsa (2003) which required unrealistic γNΔ parameters. This enables future calculations at order \(p^4\) without new low-energy constants.
