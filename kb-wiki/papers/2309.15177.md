# Applied nonrelativistic conformal field theory: scattering-length and effective-range corrections to unnuclear physics

> Due to an accidentally large $s$-wave scattering length, in a relatively wide range of energy, neutrons are approximately described by the nonrelativistic conformal field theory of unitarity fermions, perturbed by one relevant and an infinite number of irrelevant operators. We develop a formalism which provides a nonperturbative definition of local operators in that nonrelativistic conformal field theory. We compute the scattering-length and effective-range corrections to the two-point functions of primary charge-three operators using the technique of conformal perturbation theory. These calculations allow us to find the first corrections to the scale-invariant behavior of the rate of nuclear reactions with three neutrons in the final state in the regime when the neutrons have small relative momenta.

## Key claim

The paper develops a nonperturbative formalism for local operators in the nonrelativistic conformal field theory (NRCFT) of unitarity fermions and computes the first corrections to the scale-invariant behavior of nuclear reaction rates with three neutrons in the final state, but only in the regime where the relative kinetic energy of the neutrons lies between approximately 0.1 MeV and 5 MeV, the conformal window where the approximation holds.

## Method

The authors treat the NRCFT of fermions at unitarity as the unperturbed starting point and treat violations of scale invariance from finite scattering length and nonzero effective range as small perturbations, using conformal perturbation theory. They compute the first correction to the two-point function of primary charge-three operators by evaluating three-point functions involving the perturbation operators, using the known solutions to the three-body Schrödinger equation with Bethe-Peierls boundary conditions in hyperspherical coordinates. The formalism provides a nonperturbative definition of local operators in the NRCFT via their matrix elements between the vacuum and multiparticle states.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: \(a \approx -19\) fm; effective range: \(r_0 \approx 2.75\) fm
- Lower end of conformal window: \(\epsilon_a \equiv \hbar^2/(m_n a^2) \sim 0.1\) MeV; upper end: \(\epsilon_{r_0} \equiv \hbar^2/(m_n r_0^2) \sim 5\) MeV
- Scaling dimensions of charge-three operators: \(\Delta_3 \approx 4.27272\) (p-wave) and \(4.66622\) (s-wave)
- Coefficient \(C_{\mathcal U}\) for the scattering-length correction: \(C_{\mathcal U} = 2.64179\) for \(l=1\) (p-wave), \(C_{\mathcal U} = 4.14759\) for \(l=0\) (s-wave)
- The first effective-range correction coefficient \(C_{\mathcal U}' = 0\), meaning the leading effective-range correction appears at order \(r_0^2 m_n \omega\)

## Context

This work extends the "unnuclear physics" concept introduced by Hammer (2021), which showed that near the end point of certain nuclear reactions, the differential cross section follows a power law determined by the scaling dimension of an operator in the NRCFT of unitarity fermions. The present paper computes the deviations from this scale-invariant behavior due to finite scattering length and effective range, which were not addressed in the original work. The results enable quantitative predictions for reactions such as \(\pi^- + {}^3\text{H} \to \gamma + 3n\) within the conformal window, and the formalism developed here can be applied to other charge-three operators and to higher-order corrections.
