# Power Counting in Peripheral Partial Waves: The Singlet Channels

> We analyze the power counting of the peripheral singlet partial waves in nucleon-nucleon scattering. In agreement with conventional wisdom, we find that pion exchanges are perturbative in the peripheral singlets. We quantify from the effective field theory perspective the well-known suppression induced by the centrifugal barrier in the pion-exchange interactions. By exploring perturbation theory up to fourth order, we find that the one-pion-exchange potential in these channels is demoted from leading to subleading order by a given power of the expansion parameter that grows with the orbital angular momentum. We discuss the implications of these demotions for few-body calculations: though higher partial waves have been known for a long time to be irrelevant in these calculations (and are hence ignored), here we explain how to systematize the procedure in a way that is compatible with the effective field theory expansion.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that in peripheral singlet nucleon-nucleon partial waves, the one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential is perturbative rather than non-perturbative, and they quantify the suppression factor that grows with orbital angular momentum. This demotion of OPE from leading to subleading order is conditional on the centrifugal barrier suppressing the iteration of the potential, with the effect becoming more pronounced for higher partial waves.

## Method

The authors solved the reduced Schrodinger equation with the central OPE potential for singlet partial waves (1P1, 1D2, 1F3, 1G4, 1H5, 1I6) and compared the full non-perturbative phase shifts with perturbative expansions up to fourth order (three-loop level). They used a coordinate-space cutoff of rc = 0.3 fm and computed the perturbative wave functions iteratively from the free solution, extracting phase shifts via an integral representation. The peripheral suppression was then quantified by analyzing the ratio of diagonal momentum-space matrix elements between different partial waves and by deriving a modified expansion parameter that includes an angular-momentum-dependent factor b_l.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic momentum scale of OPE, Lambda_NN, is approximately 300 MeV, defined as Lambda_NN = 16 pi f_pi^2 / (M_N g_A^2) with g_A = 1.26 and f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- For the 1P1 wave at rc = 0.3 fm, the perturbative expansion converges quickly even for this lowest peripheral partial wave, with tree-level phase shifts already matching non-perturbative results to within a fraction of a degree except for the 1P1 wave.
- The coordinate-space cutoff rc = 0.3 fm corresponds to a momentum-space cutoff of Lambda = 1033 MeV for S-waves, 1590 MeV for P-waves, and 2127 MeV for D-waves, using the relation Lambda * rc = pi/2.
- The cut-off dependence was shown for isoscalar channels at center-of-mass momenta k_cm = 75, 150, and 300 MeV over the range rc = 0.3-2.0 fm, with the 1P1 channel showing mild cut-off dependence for rc >= 1 fm while 1F3 and 1H5 showed negligible dependence.

## Context

This work extends the consistent renormalizable version of nuclear EFT developed by Nogga (2005), Birse (2005), Valderrama (2009, 2011), and Long (2011, 2012), which improved upon the original Weinberg counting (1990, 1991) by properly renormalizing leading-order amplitudes and treating subleading contributions as perturbations. The paper quantifies the long-known empirical observation from Kaiser (1997, 1998) that pion exchanges are perturbative in peripheral partial waves, providing a systematic power-counting explanation for the suppression induced by the centrifugal barrier. This enables few-body calculations to decide which two-body partial waves to include based on power-counting arguments rather than purely numerical considerations, systematizing the truncation procedure in a way compatible with the EFT expansion.
