# Inclusive Electron Scattering And The GENIE Neutrino Event Generator

> The extraction of neutrino mixing parameters from accelerator-based neutrino oscillation experiments relies on proper modeling of neutrino-nucleus scattering processes using neutrino-interaction event generators. Experimental tests of these generators are difficult due to the broad range of neutrino energies produced in accelerator-based beams and the low statistics of current experiments. Here we overcome these difficulties by exploiting the similarity of neutrino and electron interactions with nuclei to test neutrino event generators using high-precision inclusive electron scattering data. To this end, we revised the electron-scattering mode of the GENIE event generator ($e$-GENIE) to include electron-nucleus bremsstrahlung radiation effects and to use, when relevant, the exact same physics models and model parameters, as the standard neutrino-scattering version. We also implemented new models for quasielastic (QE) scattering and meson exchange currents (MEC) based on the theory-inspired SuSAv2 approach. Comparing the new $e$-GENIE predictions with inclusive electron scattering data, we find an overall adequate description of the data in the QE- and MEC-dominated lower energy transfer regime, especially when using the SuSAv2 models. Higher energy transfer-interactions, which are dominated by resonance production, are still not well modeled by $e$-GENIE.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the revised electron-scattering mode of the GENIE event generator (e-GENIE) provides an overall adequate description of inclusive electron scattering data in the quasielastic (QE) and meson exchange current (MEC) dominated lower energy transfer regime, especially when using the SuSAv2 models, but higher energy transfer interactions dominated by resonance production remain poorly modeled.

## Method

The authors revised the electron-scattering mode of the GENIE event generator (e-GENIE) to include electron-nucleus bremsstrahlung radiation effects and to use the same physics models and parameters as the standard neutrino-scattering version where relevant. They implemented new models for quasielastic scattering and meson exchange currents based on the SuSAv2 superscaling approach, and compared e-GENIE predictions with inclusive electron scattering data for different target nuclei, beam energies, and scattering angles. The comparison used two distinct model configurations: G2018 (Rosenbluth cross section with Local Fermi Gas for QE and Empirical MEC model) and GSuSAv2 (SuSAv2 superscaling approach for both QE and MEC).

## Key numbers

- For carbon at E0=0.56 GeV and theta_e=60 degrees (Q2_QE approximately 0.24 GeV2), the GENIE v3 QE peak has about the correct integral and is at the correct energy transfer but is slightly too narrow compared to data from Barreau (1983)
- The first resonance peak in GENIE v3 is located at m_Delta - m approximately 300 MeV beyond the QE peak, as expected
- The RES and DIS cutoff in GENIE is set at W=1.93 GeV in the latest tune
- The hA FSI model is used for hadron energies up to 1.2 GeV, with CEM03 calculations for higher energies

## Context

This work extends the testing of GENIE against inclusive electron scattering data that was begun in Ankowski (2020), who tested an earlier electron version (GENIE v2.12) and found the quasielastic peak well-described but the resonance region (W > 1.1 GeV) poorly described. The paper builds on the SuSAv2 approach developed by Amaro (2019) and Megias (2016) for superscaling in lepton-nucleus interactions, and on the Empirical MEC model of Katori (2013). The work enables improved validation of neutrino interaction models used in oscillation experiments such as NOvA, T2K, and DUNE, by leveraging high-precision electron scattering data to constrain the vector components of neutrino-nucleus interactions.
