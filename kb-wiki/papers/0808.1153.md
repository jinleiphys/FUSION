# In-medium modification of P-wave charmonia from QCD sum rules

> We investigate the changes of the masses and widths of $χ_{c0}$ and $χ_{c1}$ in hot gluonic matter near $T_c$ and in nuclear medium using QCD sum rules. As in the previous works for the $J/ψ$ and $η_c$, in-medium effects are incorporated through the changes of gluon condensates. Twist-2 terms for the $^3 P_0 (χ_{c0})$ and $^3 P_1 (χ_{c1})$ are also included for the first time. The results show that larger mass shifts and width broadenings take place as compared to the $S$-wave states. As the critical change take place near $T_c$, related measurements can reveal critical phenomenon in QCD.

## Key claim

The masses and widths of the P-wave charmonia χc0 and χc1 show larger in-medium modifications than the S-wave states (J/ψ and ηc), with mass shifts reaching approximately 150 MeV at T = Tc and more than 400 MeV at T = 1.03Tc; however, the OPE convergence breaks down above T = 1.03Tc, so a definite conclusion on whether χc melts cannot be drawn from this analysis.

## Method

The authors apply QCD sum rules using the moment method, calculating the operator product expansion (OPE) for scalar and axial-vector currents with dimension-four scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates, where the twist-2 Wilson coefficients for the P-wave channels are computed for the first time. In-medium effects are incorporated through the temperature dependence of gluon condensates extracted from quenched lattice QCD, and masses and widths are extracted by equating OPE moments to a phenomenological relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral function, neglecting the continuum contribution.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass shift for χc0 and χc1 reaches approximately 150 MeV at T = Tc and more than 400 MeV at T = 1.03Tc (Figure 5).
- The OPE breaks down at T > 1.03Tc for both χc0 and χc1, as the perturbative correction term approaches 0.5 at the marginal temperature.
- In nuclear matter, the maximum mass shift is around 17 MeV, more than two times larger than for S-wave charmonia, with a difference of about 1 MeV between χc0 and χc1.
- The analysis uses ξ = 2.5, αs(ξ=1) = 0.21, and mc(ξ=1) = 1.24 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule formalism previously applied to J/ψ and ηc by Morita and Lee (2007) to the P-wave charmonia χc0 and χc1, incorporating twist-2 gluon condensate contributions for the first time in these channels. The results support the sequential suppression scenario proposed by Karsch (2006) and are consistent with lattice calculations by Datta et al. (2004) suggesting χc states dissolve before T = 1.1Tc, while showing that spectral modifications near Tc are more dramatic than for S-wave states. The findings enable experimental searches for critical QCD phenomena through measurements of χc mass shifts and width broadenings in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and future FAIR experiments.
