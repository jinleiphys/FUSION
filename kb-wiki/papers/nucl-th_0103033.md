# The coupling constants g_{ρπγ} and g_{ωπγ} as derived from QCD sum rules}

> We employ QCD sum rules to calculate the coupling constants g$_{ρπγ}$ and g$_{ωπγ}$ by studying the three point ${ρπγ}$- and ${ωπγ}$-correlation functions. Our results for the decay widths $Γ(ρ^0\to π^0γ)$ and $Γ(ω\to π^0γ)$ calculated using the obtained coupling constants are in good agreement with the experimental values of these decay widths.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule analysis yields coupling constants g_{ρπγ} and g_{ωπγ} that produce decay widths in good agreement with experiment, but the sum rule is derived in the chiral limit (m_q = 0) and relies on a specific choice of Borel parameter window (0.6 to 1.4 GeV²) where stability is observed.

## Method

The authors compute the three-point correlation functions for the ρπγ and ωπγ vertices using QCD sum rules, evaluating the perturbative contribution (which vanishes in the m_q = 0 limit) and power corrections from dimension-3 (quark condensate) and dimension-5 (mixed quark-gluon condensate) operators in the fixed point gauge. They perform a double Borel transform with respect to the squared momenta of the vector and pseudoscalar channels, then extract the coupling constants by matching the operator product expansion side to the phenomenological side saturated by the lowest-lying meson states. The overlap amplitudes for the vector mesons are obtained from experimental leptonic decay widths, and the pion overlap amplitude is derived from the pion decay constant and light quark masses.

## Key numbers

- g_{ρπγ} = 0.63 ± 0.07 (dimensionless coupling constant)
- g_{ωπγ} = 1.85 ± 0.15 (dimensionless coupling constant)
- Γ(ρ⁰ → π⁰γ) = (84 ± 15) keV, compared to experimental value (102 ± 26) keV
- Γ(ω → π⁰γ) = (740 ± 80) keV, compared to experimental value (717 ± 43) keV
- λ_ρ = 0.118 GeV², λ_ω = 0.036 GeV² (vector meson overlap amplitudes from leptonic widths)
- λ_π = 0.18 GeV² (pion overlap amplitude, using f_π = 0.132 GeV, m_π = 0.138 GeV, m_u + m_d = 0.014 GeV)
- Borel parameter variation range: 0.6 GeV² ≤ M², M'² ≤ 1.4 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the application of QCD sum rules to vector meson radiative decays, supplementing a previous analysis by Zhu, Hwang, and Yang (1998) that used the external electromagnetic field method. The extracted coupling constants are consistent with values used in photoproduction analyses of ρ⁰ and ω mesons via pseudoscalar exchange amplitudes (g_{ρπγ} = 0.54 and g_{ωπγ} = 1.82 from Oh, Titov, and Lee). The results provide model-independent input for effective Lagrangian approaches to low-energy QCD and for photoproduction reactions near threshold where scalar and pseudoscalar exchange mechanisms become important.
