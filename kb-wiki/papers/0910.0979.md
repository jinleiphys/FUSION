# Centrality dependence of elliptic flow and QGP viscosity

> In the Israel-Stewart's theory of second order hydrodynamics, we have analysed the recent PHENIX data on charged particles elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions. PHENIX data demand more viscous fluid in peripheral collisions than in central collisions. Over a broad range of collision centrality (0-10%- 50-60%), viscosity to entropy ratio ($η/s$) varies between 0-0.17.

## Key claim

PHENIX data on charged particle elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions require a viscosity-to-entropy ratio that increases from nearly zero in central collisions to about 0.17 in peripheral collisions, indicating that the fluid becomes more viscous as collisions become more peripheral.

## Method

The authors solved Israel-Stewart's second-order viscous hydrodynamics equations (boost-invariant, with only shear viscosity) using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code, with a lattice QCD + hadronic resonance gas equation of state and Glauber model initial conditions at τ_i = 0.6 fm. The fluid was initialized to reproduce STAR φ meson multiplicity in 0-5% Au+Au collisions, ensuring equal final-state entropy across different viscosities, and simulations were performed for η/s = 0, 0.08, 0.12, and 0.16 with freeze-out at T_F = 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Best fit to combined PHENIX data (0-60% centrality, p_T 1-3 GeV) yields η/s = 0.115 ± 0.005
- In 0-10% central collisions, minimum χ²/N = 11.79 is obtained for η/s = 0 (ideal fluid)
- In 50-60% peripheral collisions, minimum χ²/N = 2.75 is obtained for η/s = 0.16
- Best-fit η/s values by centrality: 0-10%: 0 ± 0.03; 10-20%: 0.051 ± 0.008; 20-30%: 0.087 ± 0.004; 30-40%: 0.109 ± 0.003; 40-50%: 0.134 ± 0.004; 50-60%: 0.169 ± 0.005
- In central collisions (N_part ≥ 100), integrated v_2 is suppressed by ~5%, 10%, and 15% for η/s = 0.08, 0.12, and 0.16 respectively
- Initial hadronic fraction increases from 20% in 0-10% collisions to 49% in 50-60% collisions
- Spatially averaged QGP initial temperature decreases from 298.7 MeV (0-10%) to 222.7 MeV (50-60%); hadronic phase temperature stays nearly constant at ~172 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous estimates of QGP viscosity from elliptic flow data, including the author's own prior analyses (Chaudhuri 2009) that gave η/s = 0.15 ± 0.06 and η/s = 0.07 ± 0.03 ± 0.14, and the scaling-violation estimate η/s = 0.12 ± 0.03 (Chaudhuri 2009). The result η/s = 0.115 ± 0.005 is consistent with the ADS/CFT lower bound of 1/4π ≈ 0.08 and with estimates from Drescher (2007) (η/s = 0.11-0.19) and Lacey (2006) (η/s = 0.09 ± 0.015). The centrality-dependent viscosity finding enables future studies of how hadronic matter contribution and the temperature dependence of η/s affect elliptic flow development, though the authors caution that neglect of bulk viscosity and systematic errors (~100%) mean the results should be treated as upper bounds.
