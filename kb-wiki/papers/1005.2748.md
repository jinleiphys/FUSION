# $J/ψ$ and $η_{c}$ masses in isospin asymmetric hot nuclear matter - a QCD sum rule approach

> We study the in-medium masses of the charmonium states $J/ψ$ and $η_{c}$ in the nuclear medium using QCD sum rule approach. These mass modifications arise due to modifications of the scalar and the twist-2 gluon condensates in the hot hadronic matter. The scalar gluon condensate, $<\frac{α_{s}}π G^a_{μν} {G^a}^{μν}>$ and the twist-2 tensorial gluon operator, $<\frac{α_{s}}π G^a_{μσ} {{G^a}_ν}^σ>$ in the nuclear medium are calculated from the medium modification of a scalar dilaton field introduced to incorporate trace anomaly of QCD within the chiral SU(3) model used in the present investigation. The effects of isospin asymmetry, density and temperature of the nuclear medium on the in-medium masses of the lowest charmonium states $J/ψ$ and $η_{c}$ mesons are investigated in the present work. The results of the present investigation are compared with the existing results on the masses of these states. The medium modifications of the masses of these charmonium states ($J/ψ$ and $η_c$) seem to be appreciable at high densities and should modify the experimental observables arising from the compressed baryonic matter produced in asymmetric heavy ion collision experiments at the future facility of FAIR, GSI.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of J/ψ and ηc charmonium states show appreciable modifications at high baryon densities in isospin-asymmetric hot nuclear matter, but these modifications are small at normal nuclear matter density; the effect arises from changes in the scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates, which are modeled via a scalar dilaton field within a chiral SU(3) effective theory.

## Method

The scalar gluon condensate ⟨(αs/π) G^a_{μν} G^{a μν}⟩ and the twist-2 gluon operator ⟨(αs/π) G^a_{μσ} G^{a σ}_ν⟩ were computed from the medium modification of a scalar dilaton field χ introduced in a chiral SU(3) model to incorporate the QCD trace anomaly. The coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields (σ, ζ, δ) and the dilaton field χ were solved in the mean-field approximation at finite temperature and baryon density, including isospin asymmetry via the scalar-isovector δ field. The resulting medium-dependent gluon condensates were then used in QCD sum rules (operator product expansion up to dimension four) to determine the masses of the J/ψ and ηc mesons.

## Key numbers

- The scalar gluon condensate is related to the dilaton field by ⟨(αs/π) G^a_{μν} G^{a μν}⟩ = (8/9)[(1-d)χ⁴ + (χ/χ₀)² (m_π² f_π σ + (√2 m_K² f_K - (1/√2) m_π² f_π) ζ)], with d = 0.064 used in the calculation.
- The running charm quark mass is taken as m_c(p² = -m_c²) = 1.26 GeV, and the running coupling constant at 4m_c² is α_s(4m_c²) ≈ 0.3.
- The twist-2 gluon operator contribution in the linear density approximation is given by φ_c = -(2π²/3) ⟨(αs/π) A_G⟩ m_N ρ_B / (4m_c²)², with A_G = 0.9 (twice the gluon momentum fraction in the nucleon) and m_N the nucleon mass.
- The parameter d is set to 6/33 for three flavors and three colors in the massless quark limit, giving an order-of-magnitude estimate consistent with the one-loop QCD β function.

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule studies of charmonium in-medium masses (Klingl et al.) and QCD second-order Stark effect calculations (Lee et al.; Kumar and Mishra) by incorporating both scalar and twist-2 gluon condensate modifications from a chiral SU(3) model that includes isospin asymmetry and finite temperature. The chiral SU(3) model had previously been applied to kaon and antikaon modifications in asymmetric matter (Mishra et al.) and to D-meson masses (Mishra et al.; Kumar and Mishra). The results are relevant for interpreting J/ψ suppression and charmonium production in compressed baryonic matter at the FAIR facility (GSI), where high densities and moderate temperatures are expected.
