# Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov nuclear mass formulas: Crossing the 0.6 MeV threshold with microscopically deduced pairing

> We present a new Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov nuclear-mass model in which the contact-pairing force is constructed from microscopic pairing gaps of symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter calculated from realistic two- and three-body forces, with medium-polarization effects included. With the pairing being treated more realistically than in any of our earlier models, the rms deviation with respect to essentially all the available mass data falls to 0.581 MeV, the best value ever found within the mean-field framework. Since our Skyrme force is also constrained by the properties of pure neutron matter, this new model is particularly well suited for application to astrophysical problems involving a neutron-rich environment, such as the elucidation of the r process of nucleosynthesis, and the description of supernova cores and neutron-star crusts.

## Key claim

The authors present a new nuclear mass model (HFB-17) that achieves an rms deviation of 0.581 MeV from experimental masses, the best ever within the mean-field framework, but only by using microscopic pairing gaps calculated without self-energy corrections and setting the effective mass equal to the bare mass in the pairing strength formula, because including self-energy effects proved incompatible with good mass fits.

## Method

The authors construct a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mass model where the contact pairing force is derived from microscopic \(^1S_0\) pairing gaps of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter calculated by Cao et al. (2006) using realistic two- and three-body forces with medium-polarization effects included. The pairing strength is made to depend on both neutron and proton densities through an interpolation ansatz for arbitrary asymmetry, and the model is fitted to 2149 measured nuclear masses from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation, with additional free parameters allowing different pairing strengths for neutrons and protons and for odd-nucleon systems.

## Key numbers

- Rms deviation for all 2149 measured masses: 0.581 MeV (compared to 0.632 MeV for the previous HFB-16 model)
- Rms deviation for 434 recently measured masses from GSI (2005): 0.363 MeV
- Rms deviation for 142 recently measured masses from JYFL (2008): 0.548 MeV
- Rms deviation for neutron separation energies (1988 nuclei): 0.506 MeV
- Rms deviation for beta-decay energies (1868 nuclei): 0.583 MeV
- Rms deviation for 782 measured charge radii: 0.0300 fm
- Neutron-skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb: 0.15 fm
- Symmetry energy J: 30.0 MeV (imposed)
- Saturation density \(\rho_0\): 0.1586 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Incompressibility \(K_v\): 241.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the series of HFB mass models by Goriely, Chamel, and Pearson, specifically improving upon HFB-16 (2008) by incorporating pairing gaps from asymmetric nuclear matter rather than only pure neutron matter, and by using the microscopic calculations of Cao et al. (2006) that include medium-polarization effects. The model enables more reliable predictions for astrophysical applications in neutron-rich environments, including r-process nucleosynthesis calculations, supernova core modeling, and neutron-star crust descriptions, while also providing ground-state spins and parities for 8508 nuclei between the proton and neutron drip lines.
