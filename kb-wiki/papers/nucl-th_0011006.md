# Shell Effects in Bubble Nuclei, Atomic Clusters, and Inhomogeneous Neutron Matter

> We analyze the character of the shell effects/Casimir energy in inhomogeneous fermion systems. We estimate magnitude of the shell effects and discuss their dependence on a number of physical parameters (geometry, fermion density, temperature).

# Shell Effects in Bubble Nuclei, Atomic Clusters, and Inhomogeneous Neutron Matter

## Key claim

The shell correction energy (Casimir energy) in inhomogeneous fermion systems depends strongly on the relative arrangement of impurities such as bubbles, voids, or other objects, and this dependence can be comparable in magnitude to the differences in total energy between competing phases in neutron star crusts. The authors hedge that their analysis is restricted to perfectly smooth objects and does not include many-body orbits beyond pairwise interactions, pairing effects, or surface corrugation.

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical periodic-orbit approach to compute the shell correction energy for fermion systems containing spherical bubbles, cylinders, or plates. They derive an analytic expression for the interaction energy between two identical spherical bubbles in an infinite Fermi system, based on the single hyperbolic periodic orbit connecting the bubble centers. The analysis is extended to finite spherical systems with a single off-center bubble, where the shell energy is evaluated as a function of bubble position and fermion density.

## Key numbers

- For two identical spherical bubbles of radius \(R\) with center-to-center distance \(d\), the Lyapunov exponent of the hyperbolic orbit is \(\lambda = 2 \ln \left [ 1 +\frac{d}{R} + \sqrt{ \frac{d}{R}\left ( \frac{d }{R}+2 \right ) } \right ]\).
- The shell correction energy for two spherical bubbles is given by \(E_{shell}= \frac{\hbar^{2}k_F^2}{2m} \frac{1}{4\pi (k_F d)^2} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{ [ 2 n k_{F} d \cos (2 n k_{F} d) - \sin (2 n k_{F} d) ]}{n^{3} \sinh ^{2}(n\lambda /2 )}\), where \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum and \(m\) the mass.
- At large separations, the interaction energy for cylinders decays as \(\propto 1/d^{5/2}\) and for plates as \(\propto 1/d^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of shell effects in bubble nuclei by Wheeler, Pomorski, and Bender, and in atomic clusters by Dietrich, by providing a unified semiclassical treatment of the Casimir energy in inhomogeneous fermion systems. The results are directly relevant to the predicted chain of phase transitions in neutron star crusts (nuclei to rods to plates to tubes to bubbles to uniform matter, as described by Pethick and collaborators). The analysis enables future investigation of more complex geometries, many-body interactions, surface corrugation, and pairing effects, which the authors note remain open questions.
