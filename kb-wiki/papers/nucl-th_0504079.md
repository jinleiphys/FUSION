# The structure of the nucleon: baryons and pentaquarks

> In these lecture notes I discuss an algebraic model of baryons and pentaquarks, in particular a stringlike collective model is considered in which the radial excitations of baryons and pentaquarks are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of the strings. The algebraic structure of the model makes it possible to derive closed expressions for physical observables, such as masses and electromagnetic and strong couplings. The model is applied to the mass spectrum and magnetic moments of baryon resonances of the nucleon and delta families and to exotic baryons of the theta family. The ground pentaquark is predicted to have angular momentum and parity J(P)=1/2(-) and a small magnetic moment of 0.382 nuclear magnetons.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algebraic, stringlike collective model of baryons and pentaquarks in which radial excitations are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of strings, predicting the ground state pentaquark to have angular momentum and parity J^P = 1/2^- and a small magnetic moment of 0.382 nuclear magnetons, though the author notes that the experimental status of pentaquarks remains controversial with some attributing signals to kinematical reflections.

## Method

The author constructs a U(7) algebraic model for the spatial degrees of freedom of three-quark (baryon) and four-quark-one-antiquark (pentaquark) systems, using bosonic quantization with dipole bosons for Jacobi coordinates and an auxiliary scalar boson. The mass-squared operator is built from S_3 permutation-invariant combinations of boson creation and annihilation operators, with special choices yielding either a harmonic oscillator quark model or a stringlike collective model where vibrations correspond to symmetric stretching (breathing mode) and bending modes. The full wave function combines the spatial part with spin-flavor-color degrees of freedom under the constraint that the total wave function is a color singlet and antisymmetric under quark permutations.

## Key numbers

- The ground state pentaquark is predicted to have J^P = 1/2^- and a magnetic moment of 0.382 μ_N (nuclear magnetons).
- The observed Θ(1540) pentaquark has a width less than 20 MeV (possibly as small as a few MeV).
- The proton's anomalous magnetic moment was measured in the 1930s to be 2.5 times that expected for a Dirac particle (actual value 2.793 μ_N).
- The proton's finite size was measured in the 1950s to be ~0.8 fm (current value 0.895 fm).
- Evidence has been reported for an exotic baryon Ξ^--(1862) with strangeness S = -2 and a heavy pentaquark Θ_c(3099).

## Context

These lecture notes extend the algebraic approach to baryon structure previously developed by Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (BIL) for three-quark systems, applying it to the newly discovered exotic pentaquark states following the 2003 LEPS Collaboration discovery of the Θ(1540). The work provides a unified framework for describing both conventional baryons (nucleon and delta families) and exotic pentaquarks (theta family) as rotational and vibrational excitations of string configurations, enabling systematic calculations of masses, electromagnetic couplings, and strong couplings within a single algebraic model.
