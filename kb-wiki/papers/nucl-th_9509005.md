# Updated resonance photo-decay amplitudes to 2 GeV

> We present the results of an energy-dependent and set of single-energy partial-wave analyses of single-pion photoproduction data. These analyses extend from threshold to 2 GeV in the laboratory photon energy, and update our previous analyses to 1.8 GeV. Photo-decay amplitudes are extracted for the baryon resonances within this energy range. We consider two photoproduction sum rules and the contributions of two additional resonance candidates found in our most recent analysis of $πN$ elastic scattering data. Comparisons are made with previous analyses.

## Key claim

The energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (SM95) of single-pion photoproduction data from threshold to 2 GeV yields photo-decay amplitudes for baryon resonances that are generally stable for states strongly coupled to the pi-N channel, but the S11(1535) gamma-neutron coupling shows large uncertainty and the gamma-proton coupling remains considerably below the value extracted from eta photoproduction analyses.

## Method

Energy-dependent and single-energy partial-wave analyses of single-pion photoproduction data were performed, extending from threshold to 2 GeV in the laboratory photon energy, updating a previous analysis that reached 1.8 GeV. The database was reexamined to correct statistical and systematic error assignments, resulting in additions and removals of data points. Photo-decay amplitudes were extracted from the multipole amplitudes using a background plus Breit-Wigner fit, with resonance masses and widths obtained from fits to the multipole amplitudes and pi-N decay width ratios taken from an elastic pi-N scattering analysis.

## Key numbers

- Energy-dependent solution SM95 has a chi-squared of 31810 for 13415 data points to 2 GeV, with an overall chi-squared per datum of about 2.4, compared to 3.6 for the previous SP93 analysis to 1.8 GeV
- The database contains 4711 pi0-p, 6359 pi+-n, 2225 pi--p, and 120 pi0-n data points in the SM95 solution
- The S11(1535) gamma-proton A1/2 coupling is 60 +/- 15 (GeV)^{-1/2} * 10^{-3}, with resonance mass W_R = 1525 +/- 10 MeV and width Gamma = 103 +/- 5 MeV
- The S11(1535) gamma-neutron A1/2 coupling is -20 +/- 35 (GeV)^{-1/2} * 10^{-3}
- The S11(1650) gamma-proton A1/2 coupling is 69 +/- 5 (GeV)^{-1/2} * 10^{-3}, with W_R = 1677 +/- 8 MeV and Gamma = 160 +/- 12 MeV
- The FFR sum rule integral for the isovector anomalous magnetic moment kappa^V was found to vary between 1.8 and 2.0, close to the predicted value

## Context

This work extends the previous partial-wave analyses of Li, Arndt, Roper, and Workman (1993) from 1.8 GeV to 2 GeV, and incorporates two new resonance candidates found in the elastic pi-N scattering analysis of Arndt, Strakovsky, Workman, and Pavan (1995). The extracted photo-decay couplings are compared with the earlier analysis of Crawford and Morton (1983) and with quark model predictions of Capstick (1992). The results enable improved tests of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti sum rules, and provide the Q^2 = 0 baseline for future pion electroproduction analyses at CEBAF.
