# Three-body molecular states composed of $D^{(*)}$ and two nucleons

> We study the three-body systems $DNN$ and $D^{*}NN$ within a hadronic molecular framework by combining a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction with a $D^{(*)}N$ potential constrained by heavy-quark symmetry. The three-body Schrödinger equation is solved with the Gaussian Expansion Method, and the analytic structure of the spectrum is investigated using the Complex Scaling Method. We find that the $DNN$ system supports a robust and compact bound state in the $I(J^{P})=\tfrac{1}{2}(1^-)$ channel over a broad range of cutoff values, even when the corresponding $DN$ subsystem is weakly bound or unbound. For $D^{*}NN$, the spin-$1$ nature of the heavy meson and the associated spin-dependent forces generate a clear spin hierarchy: deeply bound states appear in both $0^-$ and $2^-$ channels, while the $1^-$ channel exhibits a characteristic two-branch pattern with a strongly bound compact branch and a more weakly bound, spatially extended branch. The root-mean-square radii indicate pronounced spatial compression compared with the deuteron scale, highlighting the cooperative roles of realistic $NN$ correlations, the $D^{(*)}N$ interactions, and heavy-quark symmetry in forming compact heavy-flavor few-body bound states. No three-body resonances under complex scaling are found in the explored parameter space. Our results provide quantitative benchmarks for future experimental searches for such charmed-meson-nuclear bound states.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the $DNN$ system supports a robust and compact bound state in the $I(J^{P})=\tfrac{1}{2}(1^-)$ channel over a broad range of cutoff values, even when the corresponding $DN$ subsystem is weakly bound or unbound, a condition the abstract presents as a finding rather than a hedge.

## Method
The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for $DNN$ and $D^{*}NN$ systems using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) with Jacobi coordinates, combining a CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential with a $D^{(*)}N$ one-boson-exchange potential constrained by heavy-quark spin symmetry. They employed the Complex Scaling Method (CSM) to search for three-body resonances and analyzed the analytic structure of the spectrum. The $D^{(*)}N$ coupling constants were rescaled to reproduce the pole structures of $X(3872)$, $T_{cc}(3875)$, and $Z_c(3900)$ in the two-body sector.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.23 MeV with RMS radius 3.42 fm, reproduced using the CD-Bonn potential with cutoff $\tilde{\Lambda} = 0.814$ GeV.
- $DN$ system in $I(J^{P})=0(1/2^-)$ channel: binding energy range 0.35 to 4.54 MeV, with RMS radius 2.23 to 5.59 fm (cutoff $\Lambda = 1.2$ GeV).
- $D^{*}N$ system in $I(J^{P})=0(3/2^-)$ channel: binding energy range 8.35 to 43.23 MeV, with RMS radius 0.88 to 1.0 fm (cutoff $\Lambda = 1.2$ GeV).
- $D^{*}N$ system in $I(J^{P})=1(1/2^-)$ channel: binding energy range 0.82 to 25.84 MeV, with RMS radius 1.06 to 4.36 fm (cutoff $\Lambda = 1.2$ GeV).
- Gaussian basis parameters: $r_{\min}=0.1$ fm, $r_{\max}=10$ fm, with $n_{\max}=12$ basis functions per relative coordinate.

## Context
This work extends earlier variational and Faddeev-type studies of $DNN$ systems by Dote (2013) and Garcilazo (2017), which pointed to possible quasibound structures, by employing an energy-independent $D^{(*)}N$ interaction constrained by heavy-quark symmetry together with a realistic $NN$ potential. The study is motivated by analogous investigations in the strange sector, particularly the $\bar{K}NN$ ($K^-pp$) system studied by Akaishi (2002), Shevchenko (2007), and Dote (2008), where strong $\bar{K}N$ attraction can generate three-body bound states. The results provide quantitative benchmarks for future experimental searches for charmed-meson-nuclear bound states at facilities such as LHC, J-PARC, and GSI-FAIR.
