# Functional approach for pairing in finite systems: How to define restoration of broken symmetries in Energy Density Functional theory ?

> The Multi-Reference Energy Density Functional (MR-EDF) approach (also called configuration mixing or Generator Coordinate Method), that is commonly used to treat pairing in finite nuclei and project onto particle number, is re-analyzed. It is shown that, under certain conditions, the MR-EDF energy can be interpreted as a functional of the one-body density matrix of the projected state with good particle number. Based on this observation, we propose a new approach, called Symmetry-Conserving EDF (SC-EDF), where the breaking and restoration of symmetry are accounted for simultaneously. We show, that such an approach is free from pathologies recently observed in MR-EDF and can be used with a large flexibility on the density dependence of the functional.

## Key claim

The Multi-Reference Energy Density Functional (MR-EDF) approach for particle number projection can, under the specific conditions of a bilinear functional with density-independent interaction and a slight modification of the renormalization proposed in Lacroix (2009), be reinterpreted as a functional of the one- and two-body density matrices of the projected state, leading to a new Symmetry-Conserving EDF (SC-EDF) that avoids pathologies seen in standard MR-EDF.

## Method

The authors re-analyze the MR-EDF approach to particle number restoration by comparing it with the exact Hamiltonian case, where the projected energy can be expressed either as an integral over gauge angles of transition densities or as a functional of the one- and two-body density matrices of the projected state. They then derive conditions under which the MR-EDF energy, obtained by gauge-angle integration of a bilinear functional with density-independent effective vertices, becomes equivalent to a functional written directly in terms of the occupation numbers and two-body density matrix elements of the particle-number projected state. This alternative formulation, called SC-EDF, is applied to the nucleus 18O using the SLy4 effective interaction in the particle-hole channel.

## Key numbers

- The SC-EDF result for the particle-number restored deformation energy surface of 18O is shown in a figure using the SLy4 effective interaction, with the non-regularized MR-EDF result obtained using 199 points in the gauge-angle discretization as a reference.
- The MR-EDF energy kernel is constructed from effective vertices $\bar{v}^{\rho\rho}$ and $\bar{v}^{\kappa\kappa}$ in the mean-field and pairing channels, which generally differ from each other, may not be fully antisymmetric, and may be density dependent.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pathologies in MR-EDF, such as jumps and divergences in the energy, which were identified by Anguiano (2001), Dobaczewski (2007), Lacroix (2009), Bender (2009), and Duguet (2009). The proposed SC-EDF approach enables the use of density-dependent functionals, including those with non-integer powers of the density, that cannot be regularized in standard MR-EDF as noted by Duguet (2010). It also builds on the observation by Lacroix (2010) and Hupin (2011) that the two-body density matrix of the projected state is a functional of the one-body density matrix.
