# Vortex Avalanches and Collective Motion in Neutron Stars

> We simulate the dynamics of about 600 quantum vortices in a spinning-down cylindrical container using a Gross--Pitaevskii model. For the first time, we find convincing spatial-temporal evidence of avalanching behaviour resulting from vortex depinning and collective motion. During a typical avalanche, about 10 to 20 vortices exit the container in a short period, producing a glitch in the superfluid angular momentum and a localised void in the vorticity. After the glitch, vortices continue to depin and circulate around the vorticity void in a similar manner to that seen in previous point-vortex simulations. We present evidence of collective vortex motion throughout this avalanche process. We also show that the effective Magnus force can be used to predict when and where avalanches will occur. Lastly, we comment on the challenge of extrapolating these results to conditions in real neutron stars, which contain many orders of magnitude more vortices.

## Key claim

For the first time, the authors find convincing spatial-temporal evidence of avalanching behavior resulting from vortex depinning and collective motion in a Gross-Pitaevskii simulation of about 600 quantum vortices, but they note that extrapolating these results to real neutron stars remains challenging because neutron stars contain many orders of magnitude more vortices.

## Method

The authors solve the two-dimensional damped Gross-Pitaevskii equation on a square domain of size (512ξ)² with 1024² grid points, using a 4th-order Runge-Kutta method with timestep 10⁻³τ. They impose a spinning-down cylindrical container with a hard-wall confining potential at radius R_con = 230ξ, approximately 1,600 pinning sites arranged in a square lattice with separation d_p = 10ξ, and a centripetal potential to maintain uniform superfluid density. Initial conditions are prepared by evolving in imaginary time until quasi-equilibrium is achieved, then the system is evolved under a linear spin-down of the crust.

## Key numbers

- About 600 quantum vortices are simulated, significantly more than previous Gross-Pitaevskii studies which were limited to ≲200 vortices
- During a typical avalanche, 10 to 20 vortices exit the container in a period of <3000τ, producing a glitch in superfluid angular momentum
- The pinning potential height is V₀ = 2μ and width w = ξ, with pinning site separation d_p = 10ξ
- The critical velocity for vortex depinning is v_c ≃ 0.2ξ/τ
- The mean angular momentum approximation from Eq. (Lz_vor) has a relative error of ≃ 0.5%
- Initial angular velocities used are Ω₀ = 2π×10⁻³τ⁻¹ or Ω₀ = 4π×10⁻³τ⁻¹
- The spin-down rate used is Ω̇ = 2.5π×10⁻⁸τ⁻²
- The dissipation parameter γ = 5×10⁻³ for the representative simulation shown

## Context

This work extends previous Gross-Pitaevskii models of neutron star glitches by Warszawski (2011), Warszawski (2012), Melatos (2015), Lonnborn (2019), Verma (2022), Poli (2023), and Shukla (2024), which were limited to ≲200 vortices and found that glitch size decreased as vortex number increased. The results also relate to point-vortex models by Howitt (2020) and Cheunchitra (2024) which tracked up to 5,000 vortices but required ad hoc parameterization of dissipation and vortex interactions. The authors demonstrate that glitching behavior occurs only if the spin-down rate and dissipation are sufficiently small, and that the effective Magnus force can predict when and where avalanches will occur.
