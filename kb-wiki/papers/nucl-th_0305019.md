# Net charge fluctuation and string fragmentation

> We present simulation results of net charge fluctuation in $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{nn}}$=130 GeV from a dynamic model, JPCIAE. The calculations are done for the quark-gluon phase before hadronization, the pion gas, the resonance pion gas from $ρ$ and $ω$ decays and so on. The simulations of the charge fluctuation show that the discrepancy exists between the dynamic model and the thermal model for a pion gas and a resonance pion gas from $ρ$ and $ω$ decays while the simulated charge fluctuation of the quark-gluon phase is close to the thermal model prediction. JPCIAE results of net charge fluctuation in the hardonic phase are nearly 4-5 times larger than one for the quark-gluon phase, which implies that the charge fluctuation in the quark-gluon phase may not survive the hadronization (string fragmentation) as implemented in JPCIAE.

## Key claim

The net charge fluctuation in the quark-gluon phase simulated by the JPCIAE dynamic model is close to the thermal model prediction, but the fluctuation in the hadronic phase after string fragmentation is 4-5 times larger, implying that the initial charge fluctuation may not survive hadronization as implemented in the JPCIAE model.

## Method

The authors used the JPCIAE transport model, which embeds PYTHIA for high-energy hadron-hadron collisions (including string fragmentation via the Lund scheme) and conventional two-body interactions for lower energies, to simulate 10% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_nn)=130 GeV. Net charge fluctuations were computed separately for the quark-gluon phase (before hadronization, counting fractional quark charges and assuming gluon charge contribution of 2/3), for directly produced pions, and for pions from rho and omega decays, as functions of pseudorapidity interval Delta-eta. The dynamic results were compared with thermal model predictions, and the effect of hadronic rescattering was also tested by running simulations with and without rescattering.

## Key numbers

- Net charge fluctuation in the hadronic phase from JPCIAE is nearly a factor of 4-5 larger than that for the quark-gluon phase.
- The thermal model predicts net charge fluctuation of ~4 for a pion gas, ~3 for a resonance pion gas (pions from rho and omega decays), and ~0.75 for massless noninteracting quarks and gluons.
- Simulations were performed for 10% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_nn)=130 GeV.
- The parton cascade cutoff for string formation is p_T^min ~ 1 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the proposal by Jeon and others (2000) that net charge fluctuation could serve as a QGP signal. It directly compares with the thermal model predictions and with prior Monte-Carlo studies by Qinghui (2002) that found VNIb (cluster fragmentation) yields fluctuations close to QGP thermal values while string-fragmentation generators give larger values. The results suggest that string fragmentation may not preserve the partonic charge fluctuation, motivating further investigation of coalescence-type hadronization models and experimental measurements at RHIC.
