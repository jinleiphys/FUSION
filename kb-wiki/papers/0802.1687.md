# Low-lying dipole resonance in neutron-rich Ne isotopes

> Microscopic structure of the low-lying isovector dipole excitation mode in neutron-rich $^{26,28,30}$Ne is investigated by performing deformed quasiparticle-random-phase-approximation (QRPA) calculations. The particle-hole residual interaction is derived from a Skyrme force through a Landau-Migdal approximation. We have obtained the low-lying resonance in $^{26}$Ne at around 8.5 MeV. It is found that the isovector dipole strength at $E_{x}<10$ MeV exhausts about 6.0% of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn dipole sum rule. This excitation mode is composed of several QRPA eigenmodes, one is generated by a $ν(2s^{-1}_{1/2} 2p_{3/2})$ transition dominantly, and the other mostly by a $ν(2s^{-1}_{1/2} 2p_{1/2})$ transition. The neutron excitations take place outside of the nuclear surface reflecting the spatially extended structure of the $2s_{1/2}$ wave function. In $^{30}$Ne, the deformation splitting of the giant resonance is large, and the low-lying resonance is overlapping with the giant resonance.

## Key claim

The low-lying isovector dipole resonance in neutron-rich Ne isotopes is composed of several QRPA eigenmodes, with the dominant neutron transition being $\nu(2s^{-1}_{1/2}2p_{3/2})$ in $^{26}$Ne and $^{28}$Ne, but the structure changes in the well-deformed $^{30}$Ne where the low-lying resonance overlaps with the giant resonance and a collective $K^{\pi}=1^{-}$ mode appears at 6.7 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed deformed quasiparticle-random-phase-approximation (QRPA) calculations in a matrix formulation, based on a coordinate-space Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground state solved in cylindrical coordinates assuming axial and reflection symmetries. The SkM* interaction was used for the mean field, a density-dependent contact interaction for the pairing field, and the particle-hole residual interaction was derived from the Skyrme force through a Landau-Migdal approximation. The QRPA equation was solved in a two-quasiparticle space with a cutoff at 60 MeV, and renormalization factors were applied to the residual interactions to restore spurious modes to zero energy.

## Key numbers

- Low-lying resonance in $^{26}$Ne appears at around 8.5 MeV.
- Isovector dipole strength at $E_x < 10$ MeV in $^{26}$Ne exhausts about 6.0% of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn dipole sum rule.
- The $K^{\pi}=0^{-}$ state in $^{26}$Ne at 8.25 MeV has $B(E1)=2.98 \times 10^{-2}~e^{2}$fm$^{2}$ and is dominated (67.0%) by the $\nu[310]1/2 \to \nu[211]1/2$ transition.
- The $K^{\pi}=1^{-}$ state in $^{26}$Ne at 8.76 MeV has $B(E1)=1.65 \times 10^{-2}~e^{2}$fm$^{2}$ and is dominated (84.9%) by the $\nu[312]3/2 \to \nu[211]1/2$ transition.
- In $^{30}$Ne, the $K^{\pi}=0^{-}$ state at 8.1 MeV has an isovector $E1$ strength of 0.48 $e^{2}$fm$^{2}$.
- In $^{30}$Ne, the $K^{\pi}=1^{-}$ state at 6.69 MeV has $B(E1)=1.58 \times 10^{-2}~e^{2}$fm$^{2}$.
- The neutron emission threshold in $^{26}$Ne is 6.35 MeV; in $^{28}$Ne it is 4.44 MeV; in $^{30}$Ne it is 4.51 MeV.
- The ground state deformation parameter $\beta_2^{\nu}$ is 0.08 for $^{26}$Ne, 0.12 for $^{28}$Ne, and 0.32 for $^{30}$Ne.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic QRPA predictions of a low-lying dipole state in $^{26}$Ne by Cao et al. (2005) and provides the microscopic structure that those continuum-response calculations could not resolve. The results are consistent with the experimental observation at RIKEN by Gibelin et al. (2007) of a resonance around 9 MeV exhausting about 5% of the TRK sum rule. The deformed QRPA framework enables future studies of how deformation and pairing correlations affect exotic dipole modes in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly the transition from spherical to well-deformed systems where the low-lying resonance merges with the giant resonance.
