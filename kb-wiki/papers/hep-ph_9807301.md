# In-medium modification of $ρ$-mesons produced in heavy ion collisions

> The mass shift $Δm_ρ$ and width broadening $ΔΓ_ρ$ of $ρ$ mesons produced in heavy ion collisions is estimated using a general formula which relates the in-medium mass shift of a particle to the real part of the forward scattering amplitude ${\rm Re} f(E)$ of this particle on constituents of the medium and $ΔΓ$ to the corresponding cross section. It is found that in high energy ($E/A \ga 100~$GeV) heavy ion collisions the $ρ$ width broadening is large and $ρ$ (or $ω$) peak could hardly be observed in $e^+e^-(μ^+μ^-)$ effective mass distributions. In low energy collisions ($E/A \sim$ a few GeV) a broad (a few hundred MeV) enhancement is expected at the position of the $ρ$ peak.

# In-medium modification of ρ-mesons produced in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The mass shift of ρ-mesons produced in heavy ion collisions is small (Δmρ ≲ 50 MeV) across all considered energies, but the width broadening is large enough that the ρ (or ω) peak would likely be unobservable in dilepton effective mass distributions at high collision energies (E/A ≳ 100 GeV), while at low energies (E/A ∼ a few GeV) a broad enhancement of a few hundred MeV at the ρ-mass position is expected. These conclusions are semiquantitative because the medium is modeled as a noninteracting gas of nucleons and pions at the final stage of evolution, with the nucleon density being the most uncertain ingredient.

## Method

The in-medium mass shift Δmρ and width broadening ΔΓρ are calculated using general formulas that relate Δm to the real part of the forward scattering amplitude Re f(E) of the ρ-meson on medium constituents (nucleons and pions) and ΔΓ to the corresponding total cross section, averaged over the momentum distributions of constituents. The ρπ and ρN forward scattering amplitudes and cross sections are determined from resonance saturation at low energies and from vector dominance model (VDM) combined with Regge parametrizations of γπ and γN scattering at high energies. The nucleon and pion densities at the final stage of hadronic matter evolution are taken from experimental data on central heavy ion collisions, with the nucleon density assumed to be ρN⁰ = 0.3 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- High energy (E/A = 200 GeV, S+S collisions): Δmρ = 16 MeV (18 MeV from ρN, -2 MeV from ρπ); ΔΓρ = 550 MeV (150 MeV from ρN, 400 MeV from ρπ)
- Low energy (Ekin/A = 3 GeV): Δmρ = 27 MeV (37 MeV from ρN, -10 MeV from ρπ); ΔΓρ = 285 MeV (250 MeV from ρN, 35 MeV from ρπ)
- High energy: ⟨Re fρN⟩ ≈ -1.1 fm, ⟨Re fρπ⟩ ≈ 0.03 fm; ⟨σρN⟩ ≈ 43 mb, ⟨σρπ⟩ ≈ 20 mb
- Low energy: ⟨Re fρN⟩ = -0.54 fm, ⟨Re fρπ⟩ = 0.30 fm; ⟨σρN⟩ = 46 mb, ⟨σρπ⟩ = 13 mb
- Pion-to-nucleon ratio Rπ = 5.3 at high energy (E/A = 200 GeV); Rπ = 0.48 at low energy (Ekin/A = 3 GeV)
- Assumed nucleon density ρN⁰ = 0.3 fm⁻³ (about twice normal nuclear density)
- ρ-meson test momenta at high energy: kLρ = 3 GeV, kTρ = 0.5 GeV (lab frame)
- ρ-meson test energy at low energy: Eρtot = 1.2 GeV (forward direction)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Eletsky and Ioffe (1997), who derived formulas relating in-medium mass shifts to forward scattering amplitudes, to the specific case of ρ-mesons produced in heavy ion collisions. The calculation uses VDM to relate ρN and ρπ amplitudes to measured γN and γπ scattering, following the Regge parametrization of Boreskov, Kaidalov and Ponomarev (BKP) and the Donnachie-Landshoff (1992) Regge intercepts. The qualitative conclusion that the ρ-peak is absent in high-energy central collisions agrees with the measurement of e⁺e⁻ pair production in heavy ion collisions by the CERES collaboration (1997), where no ρ peak was found. The approach enables future experimental tests: observation of a ρ-peak would indicate that the hadronic density at the final stage of evolution is lower than normal nuclear density, while the predicted reappearance of a broad enhancement at low collision energies can be tested in experiments at E/A ∼ a few GeV.
