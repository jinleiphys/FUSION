# On the sensitivity of the energy of vanishing flow towards mass asymmetry of colliding nuclei

> We demonstrate the role of the mass asymmetry in the energy of vanishing flow by studying asymmetric reactions throughout the periodic table and over entire colliding geometry. Our results, which are almost independent of the system size and as well as of the colliding geometries indicate a sizable effect of the asymmetry of the reaction on the energy of vanishing flow.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the mass asymmetry of colliding nuclei has a sizable and systematic effect on the energy of vanishing flow (EVF), and this effect is almost independent of the total system size and colliding geometry, contrary to the implicit assumption in earlier studies that asymmetry could be ignored.

## Method

The authors used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions for four fixed total system masses (40, 80, 160, and 240 nucleons), each at four values of the mass asymmetry parameter eta (0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7). For each reaction, they calculated the EVF from the directed transverse momentum at semi-central collisions (b/b_max = 0.25) over an incident energy range of 90 to 350 MeV/nucleon, using both a soft equation of state with isotropic Cugnon cross-sections (Soft^iso) and a momentum-dependent soft equation of state (SMD).

## Key numbers

- For system mass = 80, the EVF varies by approximately 50 MeV as eta increases from 0.1 to 0.7.
- For system mass = 240 and eta = 0.1, the directed transverse flow changes by 50 MeV between 40 and 400 MeV/nucleon; for eta = 0.7 over the same energy range, the flow varies between -2 MeV and +18 MeV (net 20 MeV).
- The percentage difference in EVF relative to the symmetric case (eta = 0) is 0.7%, 4%, 13%, and 39% with Soft^iso for eta = 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, and 0.7, respectively; with MDI the corresponding values are 0.3%, 2%, 6%, and 20%.
- For lighter masses, varying eta can change the EVF by about 40 MeV.
- The EVF follows a power-law dependence on system mass with a power factor close to 1/3 for all asymmetries from eta = 0.1 to 0.7.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of collective flow and the energy of vanishing flow, which had focused almost exclusively on symmetric or nearly symmetric reactions (e.g., the FOPI group experiments on 40Ca+197Au and 58Ni+208Pb). The authors note that prior mass-dependence studies (e.g., by the groups cited as references 1, 3, and 4) found individual EVF values deviating from the average power-law trend, which the present work explains as arising from uncontrolled asymmetry in the colliding pairs. The results enable future experiments and calculations to account for asymmetry as a controlled parameter when extracting the nuclear equation of state from flow measurements.
