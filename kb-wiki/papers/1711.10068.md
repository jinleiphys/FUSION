# Finite Temperature Phase Diagrams of a Two-band Model of Superconductivity

> We explore the temperature effects in the superconducting phases of a hybridized two-band system. We show that for zero hybridization between the bands, there are two different critical temperatures. However, for any finite hybridization there are only one critical temperature at which the two gaps vanish simultaneously. We construct the phase diagrams of the critical temperature versus hybridization parameter $α$ and critical temperature versus critical chemical potential asymmetry $δμ$ between the bands, identifying the superconductor and normal phases in the system. We find an interesting reentrant behavior in the superconducting phase as the parameters $α$ or $δμ$, which drive the phase transitions, increase. We also find that for optimal values of both $α$ and $δμ$ there is a significant enhancement of the critical temperature of the model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for any finite hybridization between two superconducting bands, there is only one critical temperature at which both gaps vanish simultaneously, but this single critical temperature can be enhanced for optimal values of the hybridization parameter and chemical potential asymmetry, and the system exhibits a reentrant behavior as these parameters increase.

## Method

The authors start from a mean-field Hamiltonian describing two bands with intra-band s-wave pairing and a hybridization term, diagonalize it to obtain quasiparticle excitation spectra, and derive the grand thermodynamic potential. They then obtain coupled gap equations for the two superconducting order parameters by minimizing the thermodynamic potential, solve these equations self-consistently at finite temperature, and construct phase diagrams for both symmetric (k-independent) and antisymmetric (k-dependent, purely imaginary) hybridization cases.

## Key numbers

- For zero hybridization, the two bands have two different critical temperatures; for any finite hybridization parameter α > 0, there is only one critical temperature where both gaps vanish.
- The phase diagram for symmetric hybridization shows a maximum critical temperature T̃_c ~ 0.51 (in units of Fermi energy E_F) at optimal α, with parameters λ_a = 0.58, λ_b = 0.6, μ̃_a = 1.2, μ̃_b = 1.6, and cutoff ω̄ = 10.
- For antisymmetric hybridization with α = 0.3, the critical chemical potential asymmetry δμ̃_c is determined at T̃ = 0.1, with the same coupling parameters λ_a = 0.58, λ_b = 0.6, μ̃_a = 1.2.
- The phase diagram T̃_c versus δμ̃ for symmetric hybridization with α = 0.3 shows a first-order phase transition at δμ̃_{c,0} where gaps jump from zero to finite values.

## Context

This work extends the two-band superconductivity model originally studied by Suhl, Matthias, and Walker (1959), which included interband pairing interactions, by instead considering hybridization between bands with only intra-band attractive interactions. The results are relevant to understanding multiband superconductors like MgB₂ (which has T_c ~ 40 K and two gaps but one critical temperature, as experimentally shown by Nagamatsu (2001)), and the antisymmetric hybridization case produces sign-reversed gaps reminiscent of the s± gap symmetry observed in iron-based superconductors by Hanaguri (2010). The findings enable future studies of how orbital hybridization and chemical potential asymmetry can be tuned to enhance superconducting critical temperatures in multiband systems.
