# Two-Photon-Exchange effect in $ep\rightarrow enπ^+$ at small $-t$ with the hadronic model and dispersion relation approach

> In this work, the two-photon-exchange (TPE) effect in $ep\rightarrow enπ^+$ at small $-t$ is discussed. In the previous work, the TPE contribution with one $π$ intermediate state is estimated numerically within a hadronic model under the pion-dominance approximation. Here we extend the discussion to include one $ρ$ intermediate state. The TPE contribution can be described by one scalar function in the limit $m_e\rightarrow 0$, the dispersion relation (DR) satisfied by this scalar function is analysed. The analytic expressions for the imaginary parts of the TPE contributions from one $π$ or one $ρ$ intermediate state are given within the hadronic model. Combining these analytic expressions and the DR, the corresponding real parts of the TPE contributions can be estimated easily at any available region. This can help the further experimental analysis to include the TPE contributions in a convenient way. The numeric results show that the TPE correction with one $ρ$ intermediate state is much smaller than that with one $π$ intermediate state in the current energy region. These results suggest that the TPE contribution with an elastic state is the main TPE contribution in $ep\rightarrow enπ^+$ at small $-t$.

## Key claim
The two-photon-exchange (TPE) contribution from a single ρ meson intermediate state in the reaction ep → enπ⁺ at small momentum transfer −t is numerically much smaller than the TPE contribution from a single π intermediate state, suggesting that the elastic (pion) intermediate state dominates the TPE correction in the kinematic region studied.

## Method
The authors extend a previous hadronic model calculation of TPE in ep → enπ⁺, which included only a π intermediate state under the pion-dominance approximation, by adding the contribution from a ρ meson intermediate state. They derive analytic expressions for the imaginary parts of the TPE amplitudes from both π and ρ intermediate states, then use an unsubtracted dispersion relation (DR) to obtain the corresponding real parts. The numerical evaluation uses a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ = 0.77 GeV and the coupling constant g_{γπρ} = 0.103 determined from the ρ⁺ → γπ⁺ decay width.

## Key numbers
- At Q² = 6 GeV² and ν = 6 GeV², the real part of the TPE correction from the π intermediate state is about 15% of the one-photon-exchange (OPE) amplitude, while the correction from the ρ intermediate state is about 0.15%.
- At Q² = 4 GeV² and ν = 6 GeV², the real part of the TPE correction from the π intermediate state is about 4% of the OPE amplitude, while the correction from the ρ intermediate state is about 0.005%.
- At Q² = 1.6 GeV² and W = 1.95 GeV, the real part of the TPE correction from the ρ intermediate state is smaller than 10⁻³ of the OPE amplitude.
- At Q² = 1 GeV² and W = 1.95 GeV, the real part of the TPE correction from the ρ intermediate state is smaller than 10⁻⁴ of the OPE amplitude.

## Context
This work extends the earlier calculation by Zhou (2020) that estimated TPE contributions from a π intermediate state in ep → enπ⁺ at small −t, now including the ρ meson intermediate state. The results confirm that the pion-dominance approximation is safe for TPE corrections in the current experimental energy region, and the analytic expressions provided via the dispersion relation approach can be used directly in future experimental analyses, such as those from the JLab Fπ experiment.
