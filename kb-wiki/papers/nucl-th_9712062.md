# Analysis of the superdefomed rotational bands

> All available experimental data for the $ΔI=2$ transition energies in superdeformed bands are analyzed by using a new one-point formula. The existence of deviations from the smooth behavior is confirmed in many bands. However, we stress that one cannot necessarily speak about staggering patterns as they are mostly irregular. Simulations of the experimental data suggest that the irregularities may stem from the presence of irregular kinks in the rotational spectra. This could be a clue but, at the moment, where such kinks come from is an open question.

## Key claim

Deviations from smooth rotational behavior in superdeformed bands are confirmed in many cases, but the authors stress that one cannot necessarily speak about regular staggering patterns because the deviations are mostly irregular; simulations suggest the irregularities may stem from irregular kinks in the rotational spectra, though the origin of such kinks remains an open question.

## Method

The authors analyzed all available experimental data for ΔI=2 transition energies in superdeformed bands using a new extended one-point formula of seventh order (N=7), which subtracts a polynomial fit (expressed in Legendre polynomials) from the transition energies to reveal deviations from smooth behavior. A filtered version of the formula was also applied, setting deviations to zero when their absolute value is smaller than or equal to the corresponding experimental errorbar, to identify only physically significant deviations. Simulations were performed for the SD band C of ¹⁹²Tl using a simple model with a smooth rotational spectrum E(I)=AI(I+1) modified by two kinks (energy shifts at I=16 and I=32).

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁹²Tl SD band C, a simulation using a smooth rotational spectrum E(I)=AI(I+1) with kinks created by shifting energy "down" at I=16 and "up" at I=32 reproduced the basic feature of the experimental data.
- For ¹⁴⁸Gd SD band 6, two different experiments (Berkeley with Gammasphere and Legnaro with GASP spectrometer) produced results showing no similarity, with differences beyond errorbars.
- The analysis used a seventh order one-point formula (N=7) applied to all available experimental data for superdeformed bands in mass regions A~130 (¹³¹-¹³³Ce), A~150 (¹⁴⁷-¹⁴⁸Gd, ¹⁴⁸Eu, ¹⁴⁹Gd, ¹⁵⁴Er), and A~190 (¹⁹¹-¹⁹⁴Hg, ¹⁹²Tl, ¹⁹⁵Pb).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous one-point formula approach (Hara and Lalazissis, 1997) to a seventh-order formulation capable of handling higher-order global behavior in rotational spectra. It contradicts the interpretation of regular ΔI=2 staggering patterns first reported in ¹⁴⁹Gd by Flibote et al. (1995) and subsequently claimed in several other superdeformed bands, arguing that multi-point formulas (such as the five-point formula) can produce formula-dependent patterns that may lead to misinterpretation. The findings suggest that all existing data should be reconfirmed at different laboratories, and that a more careful estimate of experimental errorbars is needed; the simulation approach using kinks has also been applied to diatomic molecular rotational spectra (Hara and Lalazissis, 1997).
