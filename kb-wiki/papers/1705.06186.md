# Iso-vector axial form factors of the nucleon in two-flavour lattice QCD

> We present a lattice calculation of the nucleon iso-vector axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors on the CLS ensembles using $N_{\rm f}=2$ dynamical flavours of non-perturbatively $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions and an $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved axial current together with the pseudoscalar density. Excited-state effects in the extraction of the form factors are treated using a variety of methods, with a detailed discussion of their respective merits. The chiral and continuum extrapolation of the results is performed both using formulae inspired by Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) and a global approach to the form factors based on a chiral effective theory (EFT) including axial vector mesons. Our results indicate that careful treatment of excited-state effects is important in order to obtain reliable results for the axial form factors of the nucleon, and that the main remaining error stems from the systematic uncertainties of the chiral extrapolation. As final results, we quote $g_{\rm A} = 1.278 \pm 0.068\genfrac{}{}{0pt}{1}{+0.000}{-0.087}$, $\langle r_{\rm A}^2\rangle = 0.360 \pm 0.036\genfrac{}{}{0pt}{1}{+0.080}{-0.088}~\mathrm{fm}^2$, and $g_{\rm P} = 7.7 \pm 1.8 \genfrac{}{}{0pt}{1}{+0.8}{-2.0}$ for the axial charge, axial charge radius and induced pseudoscalar charge, respectively, where the first error is statistical and the second is systematic.

## Key claim

The lattice calculation yields values for the nucleon axial charge, axial charge radius, and induced pseudoscalar charge that are consistent with experimental determinations, but only when excited-state effects are carefully treated using multiple analysis methods; the dominant remaining uncertainty comes from the chiral extrapolation rather than from lattice discretization or finite-volume effects.

## Method

The authors computed nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions on eleven CLS ensembles with Nf=2 dynamical flavours of non-perturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson fermions at three lattice spacings (a=0.079, 0.063, 0.050 fm) and pion masses down to 190 MeV. They extracted the axial form factor GA(Q^2) and induced pseudoscalar form factor GP(Q^2) by solving an overdetermined linear system from ratios of three-point to two-point functions, using both the summation method and two-state fits to remove excited-state contamination. The chiral and continuum extrapolation was performed using Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) inspired formulae and a chiral effective theory including axial vector mesons.

## Key numbers

- Axial charge: gA = 1.278 ± 0.068 (stat) +0.000/-0.087 (sys)
- Axial charge radius squared: ⟨rA^2⟩ = 0.360 ± 0.036 (stat) +0.080/-0.088 fm^2
- Induced pseudoscalar charge: gP = 7.7 ± 1.8 (stat) +0.8/-2.0 (sys)
- Lightest pion mass in the ensembles: 190 MeV
- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.079, 0.063, and 0.050 fm
- Physical volume constraint: mπ L ≥ 4.0 for all ensembles
- Source-sink separations ranged from 0.6 fm to 1.4 fm on ensemble N6

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon axial form factors by the same collaboration (Capitani 2012, Jager 2013, Junnarkar 2014, Capitani 2015) and others (Yamazaki 2009, Bratt 2010, Alexandrou 2010, Meyer 2016, Alexandrou 2017, Green 2017, Rajan 2017) that have tended to yield lower values of gA than the experimental one at physical quark masses. The results address the tension between experimental determinations of the axial radius from neutrino scattering and pion electroproduction (Bernard 2001), and provide input for reducing uncertainties in neutrino flux determination for long-baseline neutrino experiments (Mosel 2016, Meyer 2016). The study enables more reliable lattice QCD predictions for nucleon structure by demonstrating that careful treatment of excited-state effects is essential and that the chiral extrapolation remains the dominant systematic uncertainty.
