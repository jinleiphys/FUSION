# Compton Scattering and Photo-absorption Sum Rules on Nuclei

> We revisit the photo-absorption sum rule for real Compton scattering from the proton and from nuclear targets. In analogy with the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule appropriate at low energies, we propose a new "constituent quark model" sum rule that relates the integrated strength of hadronic resonances to the scattering amplitude on constituent quarks. We study the constituent quark model sum rule for several nuclear targets. In addition we extract the $α=0$ pole contribution for both proton and nuclei. Using the modern high energy proton data we find that the $α=0$ pole contribution differs significantly from the Thomson term, in contrast with the original findings by Damashek and Gilman.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new "constituent quark model" (CQM) sum rule that extends the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule to energies above the pion production threshold, relating the integrated strength of hadronic resonances to Thomson scattering on constituent quarks. The authors find that, using modern high-energy proton data, the extracted alpha=0 pole contribution differs significantly from the Thomson term, contradicting the original findings of Damashek and Gilman (1969).

## Method

The authors derive a finite-energy sum rule (FESR) for forward real Compton scattering by combining a once-subtracted dispersion relation with the optical theorem, then separating the photo-absorption cross section into nuclear, hadronic resonance, and Regge-plus-Pomeron contributions. They parametrize the hadronic cross section as a sum of up to six Breit-Wigner resonances (P33(1232), P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1665), F15(1680), F37(1950)) plus a Regge-plus-Pomeron background, fitting this parametrization to photo-absorption data for the proton, deuteron, carbon-12, aluminum-27, copper-65, and lead-207 targets. The Regge and Pomeron intercepts are taken from fits to proton data by Breitweg (1999), and the background parameters are extracted from fits to each nuclear target.

## Key numbers

* The TRK sum rule integral is approximately 60 (NZ/A^2) mb MeV, with alpha/M_N approximately 3.03 mb MeV.
* For the proton, the Regge-plus-Pomeron background is parametrized as sigma^{R+P}(nu) = (68.0 [nu/1 GeV]^{0.097} + 99.0 sqrt(1 GeV/nu)) microbarn, with Pomeron coefficient c_P = 68.0 +/- 0.2 microbarn and Reggeon coefficient c_R = 99.0 +/- 1.15 microbarn.
* For lead-207, the Pomeron coefficient c_P = 42.08 +/- 1.96 microbarn and the Reggeon coefficient c_R = 91.43 +/- 9.14 microbarn.
* The extracted alpha=0 pole contribution for the proton is Re T_p^{alpha=0} = (-0.72 +/- 0.35) microbarn GeV, compared to the Thomson term of -3.03 microbarn GeV.
* For the deuteron, the alpha=0 pole contribution is Re T^{alpha=0} = (0.25 +/- 0.65) microbarn GeV; for carbon-12, (-1.14 +/- 0.89) microbarn GeV; for aluminum-27, (-3.68 +/- 2.31) microbarn GeV; for copper-65, (-1.71 +/- 0.77) microbarn GeV; for lead-207, (-0.48 +/- 1.68) microbarn GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule (Thomas 1925, Kuhn 1925, Levinger 1960) for nuclear photo-absorption to higher energies by proposing a new sum rule based on constituent quark degrees of freedom. It revisits the extraction of the alpha=0 pole contribution originally performed by Damashek and Gilman (1969) for the proton and by Dominguez, Gunion and Suaya (1972) for the deuteron, using modern high-energy photo-absorption data from Breitweg (1999) that were not available in those earlier analyses. The results enable future tests of the A-dependence of the Pomeron contribution and raise the question of whether nuclear photo-absorption data at energies above 200 GeV will bring the Pomeron strength per nucleon up to the free proton value.
