# Probing the cluster structure of $^6$Li with the nuclear reaction $^6$Li + $^{12}$C at 68 MeV

> This work presents a combined experimental and theoretical investigation of the nuclear reaction $^{6}$Li + $^{12}$C at a laboratory energy of 68 MeV. The reaction products are identified via the standard $Δ$E-E technique. Angular distributions are constructed for the elastic, inelastic, and deuteron transfer channels by detecting emitted particles -- $^{6}$Li and $α$. Elastic and inelastic scattering of $^{6}$Li on $^{12}$C are analyzed using both the Optical Model and Coupled channels approaches, with the interaction described by a double-folding potential. This potential is calculated based on the three-body wave function of $^{6}$Li. Pronounced coupled-channel effects are observed, which modify the potential and allow accurate reproduction of the experimental cross sections. The resulting polarized potentials provide a more precise description of the initial-state interaction for further reaction modelling. The deuteron transfer channel, $^{12}$C($^{6}$Li, $α$)$^{14}$N, is studied using the Coupled Reaction Channels method. The coupling between the transfer and elastic channels is implemented using the three-body wave function of $^{6}$Li. As an alternative, a regular wave function constructed with a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential is also employed. Comparison between the calculated differential cross sections and experimental data reveals a more complex and nuanced reaction mechanism, which supports the cluster structure of $^{6}$Li.

## Key claim

The cluster structure of $^6$Li, modeled as a three-body system of an alpha particle, proton, and neutron, is supported by the reaction data, but the comparison between calculated and experimental differential cross sections reveals a more complex and nuanced reaction mechanism than simple cluster transfer models can capture.

## Method

Angular distributions for elastic, inelastic, and deuteron transfer channels were measured using the $\Delta E$-$E$ technique with four three-layer silicon telescopes at the MAVR spectrometer. Elastic and inelastic scattering were analyzed with the Optical Model and Coupled Channels approaches using a double-folding potential derived from the three-body wave function of $^6$Li. The deuteron transfer channel $^{12}$C($^6$Li, $\alpha$)$^{14}$N was studied using the Coupled Reaction Channels method, comparing results from the three-body wave function against a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory beam energy: 68 MeV for $^6$Li on $^{12}$C target
- Target thickness: 5 $\mu$m self-supporting $^{12}$C foil with purity greater than 99%
- Beam current: 30 nA with beam spot approximately 5 $\times$ 5 mm$^2$
- Energy resolution: approximately 500 keV for ejectiles with $Z = 1$-$3$, and around 1 MeV for $Z = 4$-$6$
- Calculated rms radius of $^6$Li from three-body model: 2.49 fm (compared to literature value 2.54 $\pm$ 0.03 fm from Tanihata (1985))
- Deformation length for $^{12}$C ground state to first excited $2^+$ state at 4.44 MeV: $1.75 \pm 0.15$ fm
- Deformation length for octupole transition to 9.64 MeV state: $0.70 \pm 0.07$ fm
- Coupled-channel effects caused 13% increase in the depth of the double-folding potential and 28% reduction in the depth of the imaginary part of the optical potential
- $S$-wave component of the three-body configuration accounts for about 89% of the total weight

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Urazbekov and colleagues (2016, 2019, 2021, 2024) and Azhibekov (2024) on cluster effects in nuclear reactions, focusing specifically on the $^6$Li + $^{12}$C reaction at 68 MeV. The three-body wave function approach follows the methodology of Kukulin (1984) and Zhukov (1993), while the coupled-channels analysis builds on techniques from Thompson (1988) and Harakeh (1980). The results enable improved modeling of initial-state interactions through polarized potentials and provide a testing ground for future studies of cluster transfer mechanisms in light nuclei.
