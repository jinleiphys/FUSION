# Collective Modes in Light Nuclei from First Principles

> Results for ab initio no-core shell model calculations in a symmetry-adapted SU(3)-based coupling scheme demonstrate that collective modes in light nuclei emerge from first principles. The low-lying states of 6Li, 8Be, and 6He are shown to exhibit orderly patterns that favor spatial configurations with strong quadrupole deformation and complementary low intrinsic spin values, a picture that is consistent with the nuclear symplectic model. The results also suggest a pragmatic path forward to accommodate deformation-driven collective features in ab initio analyses when they dominate the nuclear landscape.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that collective modes in light nuclei emerge from first-principles no-core shell model calculations, but only when the basis is organized according to SU(3) symmetry; the authors hedge that the observed orderly patterns favoring strong quadrupole deformation and low intrinsic spin are "consistent with" rather than proven to be the nuclear symplectic model, and that the results "suggest" rather than establish a path forward for ab initio analyses of deformation-driven collective features.

## Method

The authors performed symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) calculations for 6Li, 8Be, and 6He using two realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions: JISP16 and chiral N3LO. Nuclear wavefunctions were expanded in a multi-shell SU(3)-based coupling scheme that labels configurations by their intrinsic quadrupole deformation (lambda, mu) and intrinsic spin quantum numbers (Sp, Sn, S) for protons and neutrons separately. The calculations used model spaces up to Nmax=12 with harmonic oscillator energies ranging from 17.5 to 25 MeV, and the authors compared results from symmetry-truncated spaces (labeled by N⊥max and N⊤max) against complete Nmax=12 spaces.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energies in the ⟨2⟩12 truncated space represent approximately 97% of the complete-space Nmax=12 binding energy for 6Li and over 98% for 6He (JISP16, ℏΩ=20 MeV)
- Excitation energies in truncated spaces differ by only 5 keV to a few hundred keV from complete-space Nmax=12 results
- The number of basis states for each 6Li state is only about 10-12% for ⟨2⟩12, ⟨4⟩12, ⟨6⟩12, 14% for ⟨8⟩12, and 30% for ⟨10⟩12 compared to the complete Nmax=12 space (which has 3.95×10^6 for J=1, 5.88×10^6 for J=2, and 6.97×10^6 for J=3)
- Magnetic dipole moments for 6Li in ⟨6⟩12 agree to within 0.3% for odd-J values and 5% for μ(2+1) compared to complete Nmax=12 results
- The experimental B(E2;1+1→3+1) for 6Li is 25.6(20) e^2fm^4
- The experimental ground-state quadrupole moment Q(1+) for 6Li is -0.0818(17) e fm^2
- Point-particle rms matter radii for 6Li in ⟨6⟩12 agree to within 1% of complete Nmax=12 results

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model (NCSM) approach of Barrett, Navratil, and Vary (2013) and others by incorporating SU(3) symmetry from the seminal work of Elliott (1958) and the symplectic Sp(3,R) model of Rosensteel and Rowe (1977, 1980). The results confirm and extend earlier findings by Dytrych et al. on symplectic structure in 12C and 16O, and demonstrate that symmetry-guided basis truncation can dramatically reduce computational cost while preserving accuracy. This enables future ab initio studies of heavier deformed nuclei in the lower ds shell that are currently inaccessible to complete NCSM calculations, and provides a framework for including three-nucleon forces in planned applications.
