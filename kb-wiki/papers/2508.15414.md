# B(E2) anomaly and triaxial deformation within a two-fluid SU(3) symmetry

> The correlation between $B(E2)$ structure and triaxial deformation has been investigated within the framework of the proton-neutron boson model. The analysis reveals that the distinctive feature, characterized by $B(E2;4_1^+\rightarrow2_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+)<1.0$ along with $E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)>2.0$, can emerge from the triaxial SU(3) symmetry inherent in two-fluid boson systems, attributed to band-mixing effects. This suggests a symmetry-based understanding of the anomalous $E2$ transitions observed in experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the anomalous B(E2) ratio \(B(E2;4_1^+\rightarrow2_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+)<1.0\) combined with \(E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)>2.0\) can arise from the triaxial SU(3) symmetry (denoted SU*(3)) of the proton-neutron boson model (IBM-2) when a three-body term is added to the Hamiltonian, but only if the system exhibits band-mixing effects; the anomaly is an intrinsic feature of the SU*(3) limit and does not depend on the absolute strength of the three-body term.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) with a Hamiltonian consisting of a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction plus a three-body term \(\eta(\hat{L}\times\hat{Q}\times\hat{L})^{(0)}\), with the SU*(3) parametrization \(\chi_\pi = -\chi_\nu = \pm\sqrt{7}/2\). They solved the Hamiltonian analytically for the \((\lambda,\mu=2)\) configuration and numerically for general SU(3) irreducible representations, computing excitation energies and B(E2) transition strengths using the quadrupole operator \(\hat{Q} = \hat{Q}_\pi^{\chi_\pi} + \hat{Q}_\nu^{\chi_\nu}\). The \(\gamma\) deformation was estimated from the SU(3) quantum numbers using the formula \(\gamma = \tan^{-1}\big(\sqrt{3}(\mu+1)/(2\lambda+\mu+3)\big)\).

## Key numbers

- For \((\lambda,\mu)=(6,2)\) (corresponding to \(\gamma \simeq 17^\circ\)), the ratio \(B(E2;4_1^+\rightarrow2_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+) = 0.97\).
- For \((\lambda,\mu)=(6,2)\), \(B(E2;6_1^+\rightarrow4_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+) = 0.61\) and \(B(E2;8_1^+\rightarrow6_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+) = 0.0\).
- For \((\lambda,\mu)=(4,2)\) (corresponding to \(\gamma \simeq 22^\circ\)), the \(B_{4/2}\) ratio is notably lower than for \((\lambda,\mu)=(6,2)\).
- Maximal triaxial deformation \(\gamma = 30^\circ\) occurs when \(N_\nu = N_\pi\).
- The \(\gamma\) deformation for \((\lambda,\mu)=(6,2)\) is \(\gamma \simeq 17^\circ\); for \((\lambda,\mu)=(4,2)\) it is \(\gamma \simeq 22^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the B(E2) anomaly in the IBM-1 framework (Zhang 2022, Zhang 2024, Teng 2025, Zhang 2025) that attributed the anomaly to band-mixing effects modeled with complex polynomial Hamiltonians. The present paper provides a simpler, symmetry-based explanation using the SU*(3) limit of IBM-2, which naturally incorporates triaxial deformation through the coupling of oblate proton and prolate neutron fluids (Dieperink 1982, Caprio 2005). The results enable future extension to odd-A systems (Teng 2025) and to other SU(3)-related microscopic frameworks (Bonatsos 2017).
