# Further evidence for a dynamically generated secondary bow in $^{13}$C+$^{12}$C rainbow scattering

> The existence of a secondary bow is confirmed for 13C+12C nuclear rainbow scattering in addition to the 16O+12C system. This is found by studying the experimental angular distribution of 13C+12C scattering at the incident 13C energy $E_L$=250 MeV with an extended double folding (EDF) model that describes all the diagonal and off-diagonal coupling potentials derived from the microscopic wave functions for 12C using a density-dependent nucleon-nucleon force. The Airy minimum at θ$ $\approx$70$^\circ$, which is not reproduced by a conventional folding potential, is revealed to be a secondary bow generated dynamically by a coupling to the excited state 2+ (4.44 MeV) of 12C. The essential importance of the quadruple {\it Y2} term (reorientation term) of potential of the excited state 2+ of 12C for the emergence of a secondary bow is found. The mechanism of the secondary bow is intuitively explained by showing how the trajectories are refracted dynamically into the classically forbidden angular region beyond the rainbow angle of the primary rainbow.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the existence of a secondary bow in 13C+12C nuclear rainbow scattering, generated dynamically by coupling to the 2+ (4.44 MeV) excited state of 12C, specifically through the quadrupole Y2 (reorientation) term of that state's potential, a feature not reproduced by conventional folding potentials.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental angular distribution data for 13C+12C scattering at incident 13C energy EL=250 MeV using a coupled channels (CC) method with an extended double folding (EDF) model. The diagonal and off-diagonal coupling potentials were derived from microscopic wave functions for 12C using a density-dependent nucleon-nucleon force (DDM3Y-FR), including coupling to the 2+ and 3- states of 12C. A phenomenological Woods-Saxon imaginary potential was added to account for absorption from other channels.

## Key numbers

- The Airy minimum of the secondary bow is observed at θ ≈ 70° in the experimental angular distribution at EL=250 MeV.
- The primary rainbow Airy minimum A1(P) appears at θ = 45° in the single-channel calculation.
- The secondary bow Airy minimum A1(S) appears at θ = 75° in CC calculations with coupling to the 2+ state only (W=0 case), shifting to θ ≈ 70° when the 3- state coupling is included.
- The volume integral per nucleon pair of the real potential JV is 331, 316, 314, and 296 MeV·fm3 for EL=200, 250, 260, and 330 MeV, respectively.
- The imaginary potential strength parameter W = 20 MeV, with radius R = 5.6 fm and diffuseness a = 0.7 fm.
- The normalization factor for the real double folding potential is NR = 1.2.

## Context

This work extends the discovery of a dynamically generated secondary bow first reported for the 16O+12C system by Ohkubo (2014) to the 13C+12C system, confirming that the phenomenon is not limited to a single projectile-target combination. The secondary bow arises from quantum coupling effects that create an additional attractive polarization potential in the nuclear interior, as previously analyzed using inversion techniques by Mackintosh (2015). The results enable future experimental searches for the energy evolution of the secondary bow Airy minimum across different incident energies, with predictions given for EL=200, 260, and 330 MeV.
