# Interference and nuclear medium effects on the eta-mesic nuclear spectrum

> The missing-mass spectrum obtained in a recoil-free transfer reaction p($^{27}$Al,$^{3}$He)$π^{-}$p'X is analyzed. We find that the observed peak structure arises from the coherent contributions from two reaction processes in the energy region corresponding to a bound eta ($η$) meson. In one of the processes the intermediate $η$ is captured by the nucleus to form the $η$-mesic nucleus $^{25}$Mg$_η$. In the other process, the $η$ does not form $η$-nucleus bound state. The interference between these two processes has caused the peak of the spectrum to appear at an $η$ binding energy stronger than the actual one. Our analysis also indicates that the data are consistent with an attractive N*(1535)-nucleus interaction at energies below the $η$N threshold.

## Key claim

The observed peak in the missing-mass spectrum from the COSY-GEM experiment does not directly correspond to the true binding energy of the eta-mesic nucleus; instead, the peak position is shifted to a stronger binding energy due to quantum interference between two reaction processes, one forming the eta-mesic nucleus and one where the eta scatters without forming a bound state.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the COSY-GEM recoil-free transfer reaction p(27Al,3He)π⁻p'X using a coupled-channel isobar (CCI) model for the η-nucleon interaction, which reproduces πN S₁₁ phase shifts. They calculated the missing-mass spectrum as the coherent sum of two amplitudes: one for direct η-nucleus scattering (Process S) and one for η capture forming the mesic nucleus ²⁵Mg_η (Process M), with the bound-state eigenenergy obtained from solving the η-nucleus Schrödinger equation with a complex optical potential. The fit used a single overall normalization parameter α while keeping the bound-state energy and width fixed from the potential calculation.

## Key numbers

- The COSY-GEM collaboration reported a peak centroid at binding energy (−13.13 ± 1.64) MeV with FWHM (10.22 ± 2.98) MeV, corresponding to half-width Γ/2 ≃ (5.1 ± 1.5) MeV.
- When assuming only Process M (mesic-nucleus formation), the required amplitude gave an unrealistic average nucleon binding energy ⟨B_N⟩ ≃ −360 MeV.
- With interference included, using ⟨B_N⟩ = −30 MeV, the actual η binding energy was E_bd = −6.5 MeV with Γ/2 = 7.1 MeV (fit a), producing a spectral peak at −10.5 MeV.
- Including an attractive N*(1535)-nucleus interaction V_N* = −24 MeV gave E_bd = −8.0 MeV and Γ/2 = 9.6 MeV (fit b), producing a spectral peak at −12.5 MeV, consistent with the COSY-GEM result.
- The effective ηN scattering lengths reproducing the bound states were (0.226 + 0.094i) fm for fit (a) and (0.250 + 0.123i) fm for fit (b), with imaginary-to-real ratios R = 0.42 and 0.49 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of eta-mesic nuclei first proposed by Haider (1986) and addresses a discrepancy between the COSY-GEM experimental result and prior theoretical models, which all gave an imaginary-to-real ratio R of the ηN scattering length at least 35% higher than the value inferred from a naive fit. The analysis demonstrates that interference between the mesic-nucleus formation process and a direct scattering process is essential, and that the N*(1535)-nucleus interaction is attractive below the ηN threshold. This enables future studies of baryon resonance N*(1535) interactions in medium-mass nuclei such as ²⁵Mg.
