# Examination of promising reactions with $^{241}$Am and $^{244}$Cm targets for the synthesis of new superheavy elements within the dinuclear system model with a dynamical potential energy surface

> Two actinide isotopes, $^{241}$Am and $^{244}$Cm, produced and chemically purified by the HFIR/REDC complex at ORNL are candidates for target materials of heavy-ion fusion reaction experiments for the synthesis of new superheavy elements (SHEs) with $Z>118$. In the framework of the dinuclear system model with a dynamical potential energy surface (DNS-DyPES model), we systematically study the $^{48}$Ca-induced reactions that have been applied to synthesize SHEs with $Z=112$--118, as well as the hot-fusion reactions with $^{241}$Am and $^{244}$Cm as targets which are promising for synthesizing new SHEs with $Z=119$--122. Detailed results including the maximal evaporation residue cross section and the optimal incident energy for each reaction are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The DNS-DyPES model predicts that several projectile-target combinations using $^{241}$Am and $^{244}$Cm targets can produce new superheavy elements with Z=119ŌĆō122, but the maximal evaporation residue cross sections are extremely small (sub-picobarn to tens of femtobarns), meaning experimental detection would be challenging even if the reactions are feasible with existing target inventories.

## Method

The authors applied the dinuclear system model with a dynamical potential energy surface (DNS-DyPES model) to calculate capture cross sections, fusion probabilities, and survival probabilities for heavy-ion fusion-evaporation reactions. The capture cross section was computed with an empirical coupled-channel approach including barrier distributions, the fusion probability was determined by solving a master equation for nucleon transfer in the dinuclear system with time-dependent dynamical deformations, and the survival probability of the excited compound nucleus was calculated with a statistical model treating competition between neutron evaporation and fission.

## Context

This work extends the DNS-DyPES model previously applied to reactions for Z=118ŌĆō120 (Wang 2012, Wang 2014) to systematically predict excitation functions for new projectile-target combinations targeting Z=119ŌĆō122. The study is motivated by the availability of $^{241}$Am and $^{244}$Cm from the HFIR/REDC complex at ORNL (Roberto 2015, Robinson 2020) and follows the unsuccessful attempt to synthesize Z=119 and 120 using $^{50}$Ti projectiles at GSI (Khuyagbaatar 2020). The results enable experimental planning by identifying the most promising reactions and their optimal incident energies, though the predicted cross sections are extremely small (tens of femtobarns), comparable to the upper limits established in recent experiments.
