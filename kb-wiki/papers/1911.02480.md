# Diffusion of heavy quarks in the early stage of high-energy nuclear collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We study the diffusion of charm and beauty in the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, considering the interaction of these heavy quarks with the evolving Glasma by means of the Wong equations. In comparison with previous works, we add the longitudinal expansion as well as we estimate the effect of energy loss due to gluon radiation. We find that heavy quarks diffuse in the strong transverse color fields in the very early stage (0.2-0.3 fm/c) and this leads to a suppression at low $p_T$ and enhancement at intermediate low $p_T$. The shape of the observed nuclear suppression factor obtained within our calculations is in qualitative agreement with the experimental results of the same quantity for $D-$mesons in proton-nucleus collisions. We compute the nuclear suppression factor in nucleus-nucleus collisions as well, for both charm and beauty, finding a substantial impact of the evolving Glasma phase on these, suggesting that initialization of heavy quarks spectra in the quark-gluon plasma phase should not neglect the early evolution in the strong gluon fields.

## Key claim

The authors find that heavy quarks (charm and beauty) diffuse in the strong transverse color fields of the evolving Glasma during the very early stage (0.2-0.3 fm/c), producing a suppression at low pT and enhancement at intermediate pT, but this effect is smaller than in previous static-box calculations because the longitudinal expansion dilutes the energy density.

## Method

The authors solve the Wong equations for charm and beauty quarks propagating in the evolving Glasma fields generated by Classical Yang-Mills equations in a box with longitudinal expansion, using the color-SU(2) case for computational simplicity. They initialize the gluon fields via the McLerran-Venugopalan model with color charge densities tuned to reproduce saturation scales at RHIC and LHC energies, and initialize heavy quark momentum distributions using FONLL perturbative QCD production. They also estimate the diffusion coefficient in momentum space and the effect of gluon radiation energy loss via a drag term related to the diffusion coefficient through the Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark formation time: tau_formation = 1/(2m_c) approximately 0.06 fm/c for m_c = 1.5 GeV
* Beauty quark formation time: tau_formation = 1/(2m_b) approximately 0.02 fm/c for m_b = 4.5 GeV
* For Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy (sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV), the saturation scale parameters are g^2mu_Pb = 3.4 GeV and g^2mu_Pb = 5 GeV for two different nuclear scaling models
* For p-Pb collisions at LHC energy, the proton saturation scale parameter is g^2mu_p = 1.41 GeV
* Diffusion coefficient D = 0.21 GeV^2/fm for g^2mu = 3.4 GeV and D = 0.35 GeV^2/fm for g^2mu = 5 GeV in Pb-Pb collisions
* The nuclear suppression factor R_pPb and R_PbPb saturate at tau approximately 0.3 fm/c due to longitudinal expansion

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Ruggieri et al. (2018) and Sun et al. (2019) that ignored longitudinal expansion of the gluon medium, and by Mrowczynski (2017) that used a simplified Fokker-Planck approach. The inclusion of longitudinal expansion makes the calculation more realistic and shows that the Glasma phase still has a substantial impact on heavy quark spectra, suggesting that initialization of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma phase should include the early evolution in strong gluon fields. The results enable more accurate initial conditions for relativistic transport codes that model the subsequent QGP phase evolution.
