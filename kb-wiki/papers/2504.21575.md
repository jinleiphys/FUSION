# Dynamical Local Tadpole-Improvement in Quantum Simulations of Gauge Theories

> We identify a new element in quantum simulations of lattice gauge theories, arising from spacetime-dependent quantum corrections in the relation between the link variables defined on the lattice and their continuum counterparts. While in Euclidean spacetime simulations, based on Monte Carlo sampling, the corresponding tadpole improvement leads to a constant rescaled value per gauge configuration, in Minkowski spacetime simulations it requires a state- and time-dependent update of the coefficients of operators involving link variables in the Hamiltonian. To demonstrate this effect, we present the results of numerical simulations of the time evolution of truncated SU(2) plaquette chains and honeycomb lattices in 2+1D, starting from excited states with regions of high energy density, and with and without entanglement.

## Key claim

In Minkowski spacetime quantum simulations of lattice gauge theories, the tadpole-improvement factor for link variables is not a constant per configuration as in Euclidean Monte Carlo calculations, but instead requires a state- and time-dependent update of the Hamiltonian coefficients at each Trotter step, iterated to self-consistency.

## Method

The authors simulate the time evolution of truncated SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in 2+1D using two lattice geometries: linear plaquette chains with periodic boundary conditions and honeycomb lattices with open boundary conditions, both truncated in the electric basis to \(j_{\rm max}=1/2\). At each time step of size \(\delta t = 0.025\), the plaquette expectation value in the current quantum state is used to compute local tadpole factors \(u_{0,i}(t)\), which are then iterated to convergence before advancing the state via the updated Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For the \(L=10\) plaquette chain at coupling \(g=0.5\), the ground-state tadpole factor is \(u_0^4 = 1.33828\) and the ground-state energy density is \(\epsilon_0 = 6.06346\) (in lattice units).
- For the \(7\times 3\) honeycomb lattice at \(g=0.5\), the ground-state tadpole factors for selected plaquettes are \(u^6_{0,(0,0)} = u^6_{0,(6,2)} = 1.37463\), \(u^6_{0,(1,0)} = 1.26440\), \(u^6_{0,(0,1)} = 1.25609\), and \(u^6_{0,(3,1)} = 1.20984\), with an average ground-state energy density of \(\epsilon_0 = 4.97765\) (in lattice units).
- The time evolution uses a Trotter step of \(\delta t = 0.025\) for both systems.

## Context

This work extends the tadpole-improvement program of Lepage (1992) from Euclidean lattice QCD to real-time Hamiltonian simulations, where prior treatments by Luo (1998) and Carlsson (2001, 2003) assumed constant and uniform tadpole factors. The paper demonstrates that for entangled initial states, the dynamical local tadpole correction produces significant deviations from both the unimproved and uniformly-improved evolutions, and it identifies this as a new element that must be incorporated into quantum algorithms for non-equilibrium gauge theory dynamics, particularly for simulating high-energy collisions and strong-field QED.
