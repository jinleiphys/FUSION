# Muon-neutrino-induced charged current cross section without pions: Theoretical analysis

> We calculate the charged current cross sections obtained at the T2K near detector for $ν_μ$-induced events without pions in the final state. The method used is quantum-kinetic transport theory. Results are shown first, as a benchmark, for electron inclusive cross sections on $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O to be followed with a detailed comparison with the data measured by the T2K collaboration on C$_8$H$_8$ and H$_2$O targets. The contribution of 2p2h processes is found to be relevant mostly for backwards angles; their theoretical uncertainties are within the experimental uncertainties. Particular emphasis is then put on a discussion of events in which pions are first created, but then reabsorbed. Their contribution is found to be essential at forward angles.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a quantum-kinetic transport theory (GiBUU) can describe T2K charged-current 0-pion cross sections on both C₈H₈ and H₂O targets without free parameters, but the agreement depends on the inclusion of "stuck-pion" events (pions produced then reabsorbed) which are essential at forward angles and contribute up to 20% of the total 0-pion cross section there.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU generator based on non-equilibrium Green's function theory to simulate neutrino-nucleus interactions, factorizing the reaction into an initial interaction and final-state interactions of hadrons. The calculations included quasielastic scattering, 2p2h excitations (using a meson exchange contribution extracted from electron-nucleus data by Bosted et al.), and pion production (via the Δ resonance using MAID analysis) with subsequent reabsorption through two-nucleon and three-nucleon processes. All results were obtained with the 2017 version of GiBUU "out of the box" with no parameter tuning, using an axial mass parameter M_A = 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For the C target at the most backward angles, explicit pion production (difference between inclusive and 0-pion cross sections) amounts to only about 15% of the total, while at the most forward angles it becomes dominant at approximately 70%
- At the two most forward angular bins on C, the stuck-pion contribution amounts to up to 20% of the total 0-pion cross section at the peak
- For the C target at backward angles, the 2p2h contribution at the peak amounts to about 25% of the total 0-pion cross section
- For the O target at the two most forward bins, the stuck-pion contribution amounts to 20% of the total 0-pion cross section at the peak
- The highest energy (1.5 GeV) electron-¹⁶O data show the largest discrepancy with calculations, up to approximately 20% in the QE peak region
- The 2p2h contribution on C peaks at p_μ ≈ 0.4 GeV at the most backward angle, moving to about 0.5 GeV at the most forward angular bin

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of T2K and MINERvA pion production data (Mosel 2017) and addresses the "pion puzzle" where MiniBooNE and MINERvA pion production data appear incompatible. The paper compares with calculations by the SUSA group (Megias 2017) and the Martini model, noting that both lack full treatment of on-shell pion reabsorption. The results enable improved neutrino energy reconstruction for T2K and future experiments like DUNE by demonstrating that generators must include stuck-pion events to avoid erroneous parameter determinations when tuning to 0-pion data.
