# Thermal Bottomonium Suppression

> I discuss recent calculations of the thermal suppression of bottomonium states in relativistic heavy ion collisions. I present results for the inclusive Upsilon(1s) and Upsilon(2s) suppression as a function of centrality. I compare with recent preliminary CMS data available at central rapidities and make predictions at forward rapidities which are within the acceptance of the ALICE dimuon spectrometer.

# Thermal Bottomonium Suppression

## Key claim
The thermal suppression of bottomonium states in relativistic heavy ion collisions, computed using a dynamical model that includes both real and imaginary parts of the heavy quark potential and accounts for plasma momentum-space anisotropies, agrees reasonably well with preliminary CMS data; however, the data have a ±14% (1s) and ±21% (2s) global uncertainty, making firm conclusions about the preferred shear viscosity to entropy ratio difficult.

## Method
The author solves the 3d Schrödinger equation numerically using a complex-valued heavy quark potential that includes both a real part derived from the internal energy and an imaginary part from leading-order perturbative calculations including gluonic Landau damping and singlet-to-octet transitions. The resulting thermal widths are integrated and exponentiated over the spatio-temporal evolution of the plasma, which is modeled using a (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamical description that provides the local momentum-space anisotropy and hard momentum scale. The final nuclear modification factor R_AA is obtained by averaging over the transverse plane weighted by the binary collision distribution from a Glauber model with Woods-Saxon nuclear profiles, with feed-down from higher excited states included using fixed fractions from pp collisions.

## Key numbers
- Bottom quark mass: m_b ≈ 4.2 GeV; charm quark mass: m_c ≈ 1.3 GeV
- Bottomonium state masses: m_Υ ≈ 10 GeV; plasma temperatures T ≲ 1 GeV
- Real potential parameters: a = 0.385, σ = 0.223 GeV²
- Feed-down fractions for inclusive Υ(1s): 0.510 direct, 0.107 from Υ(2s), 0.008 from Υ(3s), 0.27 from χ_b1, 0.105 from χ_b2
- Feed-down fractions for inclusive Υ(2s): 0.500 from Υ(2s), 0.500 from Υ(3s)
- Central temperatures used: T_0 = {520, 504, 494} MeV at τ_0 = 0.3 fm/c for 4πη/S = {1, 2, 3} respectively, to fix final charged multiplicity dN_ch/dy ≈ 1400
- CMS global uncertainties: ±14% for Υ(1s), ±21% for Υ(2s)
- ALICE forward muon spectrometer acceptance: pseudorapidity interval 2.5 ≤ y ≤ 4.0 with resolution approximately 100 MeV
- Average N_part for ALICE 0-90% centrality bin: N_part = 124

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of heavy quarkonium suppression by incorporating both real and imaginary parts of the heavy quark potential, building on the first-principles calculations of the imaginary potential by Laine et al. (2006) and the effective field theory approach of Brambilla et al. (2008). The dynamical model uses anisotropic hydrodynamical evolution following the work of Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), and Ryblewski (2010), which is important because momentum-space anisotropies reduce quarkonium binding as shown by Dumitru (2007, 2009) and Burnier (2009). The predictions enable future ALICE forward rapidity measurements to distinguish between the Bjorken-like boost-invariant plateau profile and the Landau-like Gaussian rapidity profile for the initial temperature distribution, though the author notes that initial state effects such as color glass condensate may become important at forward rapidities and the purely thermal R_AA shown should be taken as an upper bound.
