# Symmetric and anti-symmetric Landau parameters and magnetic properties of dense quark matter

> We calculate the dimensionless Fermi liquid parameters (FLPs), $F_{0,1}^{sym}$ and $F_{0,1}^{asym}$, for spin asymmetric dense quark matter. In general, the FLPs are infrared divergent due to the exchange of massless gluons. To remove such divergences, the Hard Density Loop (HDL) corrected gluon propagator is used. The FLPs so determined are then invoked to calculate magnetic properties such as magnetization $\langle M\rangle$ and magnetic susceptibility $χ_M$ of spin polarized quark matter. Finally, we investigate the possibility of magnetic instability by studying the density dependence of $\langle M\rangle$ and $χ_M$.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the spin-symmetric and spin-anti-symmetric Landau parameters for spin-polarized dense quark matter, using the Hard Density Loop (HDL) corrected gluon propagator to remove infrared divergences that arise from massless gluon exchange; the resulting magnetic susceptibility and magnetization are then used to investigate the possibility of a magnetic instability, with the condition that the critical density for the magnetic transition depends on the critical density of the para-ferro phase transition.

## Method

The authors compute the dimensionless Fermi liquid parameters \(F_{0,1}^{sym}\) and \(F_{0,1}^{asym}\) for spin-polarized quark matter by evaluating forward scattering amplitudes with both bare and HDL-corrected gluon propagators. They then express the magnetization \(\langle M \rangle\) and magnetic susceptibility \(\chi_M\) in terms of these parameters, using the spin polarization parameter \(\xi = (n_q^+ - n_q^-)/(n_q^+ + n_q^-)\). The density dependence of \(\chi_M\) and \(\langle M \rangle\) is studied numerically for various values of \(\xi\) to identify the critical density where the susceptibility diverges, indicating magnetic instability.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass \(m_D\) is given by \(m_D^2 = \frac{g^2}{4\pi^2} \sum_{s=\pm} p_f^s \varepsilon_f^s\), where \(p_f^\pm = p_f (1 \pm \xi)^{1/3}\) and \(\varepsilon_f^\pm = \sqrt{p_f^{\pm 2} + m_q^2}\).
- For numerical estimates, the coupling constant is taken as \(\alpha_c = g^2/(4\pi) = 2.2\) and the quark mass as \(m_q = 300\) MeV.
- The magnetic susceptibility \(\chi_M\) is expressed as \(\chi_M = \chi_P \left[ 1 + \frac{N_c (p_f^+ \mu^+ + p_f^- \mu^-)}{2\pi^2} \left( f_0^{asym} - \frac{1}{3} f_1^{sym} \right) \right]^{-1}\), where \(\chi_P = \overline{g}_D^2 \mu_q^2 N_c (p_f^+ \mu^+ + p_f^- \mu^-)/(8\pi^2)\).
- The magnetic dipole moments used are \(\mu_u = 1.852 \mu_N\), \(\mu_d = -0.972 \mu_N\), and \(\mu_s = -0.581 \mu_N\), with the nuclear magneton \(\mu_N = 3.152 \times 10^{-14}\) MeV/Tesla.
- Magnetization is shown for densities \(0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), and \(0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\) at a magnetic field \(B = 10^7\) G.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Fermi liquid theory (RFLT) developed by Baym and Chin (1976) for unpolarized nuclear matter to the case of spin-polarized quark matter, building on earlier studies of magnetic properties by Tatsumi (2008, 2009) and Niegawa (2005) that were restricted to unpolarized matter. It also connects to variational calculations by Tatsumi (2000) that suggested a para-ferro phase transition below a critical density. The results enable future studies of magnetic phase transitions in dense quark matter, particularly in astrophysical contexts such as neutron stars, and show qualitative similarity to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter calculations by Aguirre (2007).
