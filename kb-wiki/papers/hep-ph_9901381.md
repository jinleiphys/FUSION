# Chiral Effective Theories

> These are the proceedings of the workshop on ``Chiral Effective Theories'' held at the Physikzentrum Bad Honnef of the Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft, Bad Honnef, Germany from November 30 to December 4, 1998. The workshop concentrated on Chiral Perturbation Theory in its various settings and its relations with lattice QCD and dispersion theory. Included are a short contribution per talk and a listing of some review papers on the subject.

# Chiral Effective Theories

## Key claim
This is a workshop proceedings volume summarizing presentations at the 1998 Bad Honnef meeting on Chiral Perturbation Theory (CHPT), covering its applications to mesons, baryons, lattice QCD, and dispersion theory, with the understanding that most results presented were either already published or soon to appear in the archives, making full proceedings unnecessary.

## Method
The workshop gathered 53 participants, of whom 43 gave talks, and the proceedings consist of short contributed summaries per presentation along with a listing of review papers on the subject. The meeting followed a series of earlier topical workshops held in Ringberg (1988), Dobogoko (1991), Karrebaeksminde (1993), and Trento (1996), maintaining a format of roughly 50 participants with substantial discussion time.

## Key numbers
- SESAM collaboration found the pion-nucleon sigma term sigma_piN = 18(5) MeV from full QCD lattice simulation with n_f=2 dynamical fermions.
- SESAM estimated the flavor singlet axial coupling of the proton G_A^1 = 0.20(12), consistent with experiment and previous quenched simulations.
- The chiral condensate in the MSbar scheme at mu=2 GeV was reported as -[245 +/- 15 MeV]^3 (APE1) and -[253 +/- 25 MeV]^3 (APE2).
- Light quark masses m_{u,d} at mu=2 GeV were given as 5.7 +/- 0.8 MeV (APE1), 4.5 +/- 0.4 MeV (APE2), and 4.6 +/- 0.2 MeV (CP-PACS).
- Pion vector form factor fit yielded charge radius <r^2>^pi_V = (0.437 +/- 0.016) fm^2 and c_V^pi = (3.85 +/- 0.60) GeV^{-4}.
- Low-energy constants from pion form factors: l4 = 4.4 +/- 0.3, l6 = 16.0 +/- 0.5 +/- 0.7, l5 = 13.0 +/- 0.9.
- O(p^6) constants from pion form factors: r^r_{S3}(M_rho) ~ 1.5 x 10^{-4}, r^r_{V2}(M_rho) ~ 1.6 x 10^{-4}.

## Context
This proceedings volume documents the state of chiral effective field theory in late 1998, extending the series of workshops that began with Ringberg 1988 and continuing the tradition of the Chiral Dynamics meetings (MIT 1994, Mainz 1997). The contributions cover the generating functional at next-to-next-to-leading order (Ecker), pion form factors to two loops (Bijnens, Colangelo, Talavera), quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (Golterman, Pallante), lattice determinations of low-energy constants (Gusken, Vladikas), weak matrix elements via dispersion relations (Donoghue), the 1/N_c expansion for Delta S=1 transitions (Prades), and baryon CHPT including two-nucleon systems (Hemmert, Mojzis, Fettes, Epelbaum, Savage, Kaiser, Beane). The volume enables future work by providing a snapshot of open problems such as the role of quenching in lattice QCD, the determination of O(p^6) low-energy constants, and the matching of long- and short-distance contributions in weak non-leptonic decays.
