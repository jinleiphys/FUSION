# Dense matter equation of state and phase transitions from a Generalized Skyrme model

> Skyrmion crystals are the field configurations which minimize the energy per baryon in the infinitely large topological charge sector of the Skyrme model, at least for sufficiently high density. They are, therefore, an important tool to describe the ground state of cold, symmetric nuclear matter at high density regimes. In this work, we analyze different crystalline phases and the existence of phase transitions between them within the generalized Skyrme model, with the ultimate goal of describing symmetric nuclear matter in a wide regime of densities. Furthermore, we propose a new energy-minimizing phase for densities lower than the nuclear saturation point ($n_0$) which also presents a good qualitative behavior in the zero density limit, thereby improving the description of strongly interacting matter in the region $n<n_0$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the generalized Skyrme model, the ground state of cold symmetric nuclear matter at different densities is described by different crystalline phases (FCC, BCC, FCC half-skyrmion) with phase transitions between them, and that a new inhomogeneous phase below nuclear saturation density improves the description of matter in the region n < n0, though the authors note that quantum corrections or additional fields likely need to be included for a completely realistic description.

## Method

The authors construct crystalline Skyrme field configurations using Fourier expansions with cubic symmetries (simple cubic, body-centered cubic, face-centered cubic, and half-skyrmion phases) and minimize the static energy functional of the generalized Skyrme model (including the standard Skyrme terms, a sextic BPS term, and a pion mass term) using a Nelder-Mead algorithm with the GSL C++ library. They fix the pion mass to 140 MeV and the sextic coupling λ² = 7 MeV/fm³, then iteratively adjust the parameters fπ and e to reproduce infinite nuclear matter at saturation (E/B = 923.3 MeV at n0 = 0.16 fm⁻³) using an approximate perfect scaling parametrization of the energy per baryon as a function of lattice length.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Energy per baryon at saturation (p = 0): E/B = 923.3 MeV
- Pion mass fixed to mπ = 140 MeV
- Sextic coupling constant: λ² = 7 MeV/fm³
- For the standard Skyrme model (no sextic term, no pion mass): fπ = 137.83 MeV, e = 4.59
- For the standard Skyrme model with pion mass: fπ = 118.83 MeV, e = 4.32
- For the generalized model with sextic term (λ² = 7 MeV/fm³) and no pion mass: fπ = 160.32 MeV, e = 8.59
- For the full generalized model with sextic term and pion mass: fπ = 136.85 MeV, e = 6.46
- Minimum energy per baryon for FCC half-skyrmion phase in standard Skyrme model: E/B = 1.038 (dimensionless units) at lattice length Lt = 4.7 (dimensionless)
- Minimum energy per baryon for Klebanov cubic crystal in standard Skyrme model: E_min/B = 1.08 (dimensionless), only 8% above the Skyrme-Faddeev bound
- FCC half-skyrmion crystal minimum energy per baryon is 3.8% above the Skyrme-Faddeev bound
- Baryon number per unit cell (2L)³: B_cell = 8 for BCC phase, B_cell = 4 for both FCC phases

## Context

This work extends the study of Skyrme crystals from the standard Skyrme model (Klebanov 1985, Kugler and Shtrikman 1989, Castillejo et al. 1989) to the generalized Skyrme model including sextic and pion mass terms, building on the equation of state proposed by Adam et al. (2020) that interpolated between the standard Skyrme model and the BPS submodel. The paper enables the construction of a more complete equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter from first principles within the Skyrme model framework, with potential applications to neutron star structure calculations, though the authors note that quantum corrections and additional fields likely need to be incorporated for realistic neutron star descriptions.
