# Competition between pairing and tripling in one-dimensional fermions with coexistent s- and p-wave interactions

> We theoretically investigate in-medium two- and three-body correlations in one-dimensional two-component Fermi gases with coexistent even-parity s-wave and odd-parity p-wave interactions. We find the solutions of the stable in-medium three-body cluster states such as Cooper triple by solving the corresponding in-medium variational equations. We further feature the phase diagram consisting of the s- and p-wave Cooper pairing phase, and Cooper tripling phase, in the plane of s- and p-wave pairing strengths. The Cooper tripling phase dominates over the pairing phases when both s- and p-wave interactions are moderately strong.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a stable in-medium three-body cluster state (a "Cooper triple") can exist in a one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas when both s-wave and p-wave interactions are present, and that this Cooper triple phase dominates over the s-wave and p-wave Cooper pairing phases when both interactions are moderately strong, but the result depends on a momentum cutoff that acts as a three-body parameter.

## Method

The authors solve in-medium variational equations derived from a generalized Cooper problem for a one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas with coexistent s-wave (interspecies) and p-wave (intraspecies) interactions. They compute the ground-state energy of the three-body cluster state (Cooper triple) and compare it with the two-body s-wave and p-wave Cooper pairing energies obtained from the corresponding in-medium two-body equations. The phase diagram is constructed by comparing the energies of the three competing states.

## Key numbers

- At $1/(k_{\rm F}a_s)=1.4$, the s-wave pairing state is stable (i.e., $|E_{2,s}|\geq |E_{2,p}|$) up to $1/(k_{\rm F}a_p)\simeq 0.25$; beyond this p-wave coupling strength, the p-wave Cooper pairing becomes stabler.
- At $1/(k_{\rm F}a_s)=3$, $|E_3|$ is larger than $|E_{2,p}|$ even in the weak-coupling p-wave BCS regime at $1/(k_{\rm F}a_p)\simeq -1$.
- At $1/(k_{\rm F}a_s)=3$, $|E_3|$ starts to dominate over $|E_{2,s}|$ around $1/(k_{\rm F}a_p)=0.6$.
- The phase diagram is computed for scattering parameters in the ranges $0.5\leq 1/(k_{\rm F}a_s)\leq 3$ and $-1.5\leq 1/(k_{\rm F}a_p)\leq 1.5$, with momentum cutoffs $\Lambda/k_{\rm F}=10$ and $\Lambda/k_{\rm F}=20$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Cooper triples in three-dimensional systems (Niemann 2012, Kirk 2017, Akagami 2021, Tajima 2021, Tajima 2022) to one-dimensional fermions with hybridized s- and p-wave interactions, a situation that can be realized in ultracold $^{40}$K Fermi gases near overlapped s- and p-wave Feshbach resonances around $B=200$ G (Zhou 2017). The results are relevant to recent experiments on quasi-one-dimensional $^{40}$K Fermi gases where an emergent s-wave interaction was reported near the p-wave resonance (Jackson 2022). The work enables future studies of higher-order Cooper clusters and can be tested against exact methods such as density-matrix renormalization group or bosonization for quantitative many-body properties.
