# On the Rotational Invariance and Non-Invariance of Lepton Angular Distributions in Drell-Yan and Quarkonium Production

> Several rotational invariant quantities for the lepton angular distributions in Drell-Yan and quarkonium production were derived several years ago, allowing the comparison between different experiments adopting different reference frames. Using an intuitive picture for describing the lepton angular distribution in these processes, we show how the rotational invariance of these quantities can be readily obtained. This approach can also be used to determine the rotational invariance or non-invariance of various quantities specifying the amount of violation for the Lam-Tung relation. While the violation of the Lam-Tung relation is often expressed by frame-dependent quantities, we note that alternative frame-independent quantities are preferred.

# On the Rotational Invariance and Non-Invariance of Lepton Angular Distributions in Drell-Yan and Quarkonium Production

## Key claim

The rotational invariance of quantities like $\mathcal{F}$, $\tilde \lambda$, $\tilde \lambda^\prime$, and $\mathcal{G}$ under rotations around the $y$ axis (which connect different experimental reference frames) can be derived transparently using an intuitive picture based on the quark momentum direction in the dilepton rest frame; however, the commonly used violation measure $1-\lambda_\theta-4\lambda_\phi$ is *not* rotationally invariant, and the frame-independent quantity $(1-\lambda_\theta-4\lambda_\phi)/(3+\lambda_\theta)$ is preferred for quantifying Lam-Tung relation violation.

## Method

The authors express the angular distribution parameters $\lambda_\theta$, $\lambda_{\theta\phi}$, and $\lambda_\phi$ in terms of $\lambda_0$ (a parameter depending on the reaction mechanism) and the components ($x_1$, $y_1$, $z_1$) of the unit vector $\hat z^\prime$ of the quark momentum in the dilepton rest frame, using trigonometric relations between the lepton angles and the quark axis. They then substitute these expressions into the definitions of the rotation-invariant quantities $\mathcal{F}$, $\tilde \lambda$, $\tilde \lambda^\prime$, and $\mathcal{G}$ to show explicitly that each depends only on $\lambda_0$ and the component of $\hat z^\prime$ along the axis of rotation, thereby demonstrating invariance under rotations around that axis.

## Key numbers

- $\mathcal{F} = (1+\lambda_0-2\lambda_0 y_1^2)/(3+\lambda_0)$, where $y_1 = \sin\theta_1\sin\phi_1$ is the $y$-component of the quark momentum unit vector; for Drell-Yan ($\lambda_0=1$), $\mathcal{F} = (1-y_1^2)/2$
- $\tilde \lambda = (\lambda_0+3\lambda_0 y_1^2)/(1+\lambda_0 y_1^2)$; in the coplanar case ($y_1=0$), $\tilde \lambda = \lambda_0$
- $\tilde \lambda^\prime = \lambda_0^2(1-y_1^2)^2/(3+\lambda_0)^2$
- $\mathcal{G} = (1+\lambda_0-2\lambda_0 x_1^2)/(3+\lambda_0)$, where $x_1 = \sin\theta_1\cos\phi_1$ is the $x$-component of the quark momentum unit vector
- $\lambda_\theta = (-\lambda_0+3\lambda_0 z_1^2)/(2+\lambda_0-\lambda_0 z_1^2)$, where $z_1 = \cos\theta_1$ is the $z$-component
- The Lam-Tung relation violation measure $(1-\lambda_\theta-4\lambda_\phi)/(3+\lambda_\theta) = 1-2\mathcal{F} = (1-\lambda_0+4\lambda_0 y_1^2)/(3+\lambda_0)$ is invariant under rotations around the $y$ axis

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivations of rotation-invariant quantities by Faccioli et al. (2010-2011) and the intuitive approach for describing Drell-Yan angular distributions developed by Peng, Chang, and collaborators (2016-2017). The paper provides a simpler, more transparent derivation of the invariance properties of $\mathcal{F}$, $\tilde \lambda$, $\tilde \lambda^\prime$, and $\mathcal{G}$ that were previously obtained from covariance properties of angular momentum eigenstates. It clarifies that the commonly used violation measure $1-\lambda_\theta-4\lambda_\phi$ is frame-dependent, as first pointed out by Faccioli et al. (2011), and recommends the frame-independent combination $(1-\lambda_\theta-4\lambda_\phi)/(3+\lambda_\theta)$ for comparing Lam-Tung relation violations across experiments. The approach could be extended to other hard processes such as hadron pair production in $e^+e^-$ annihilation.
