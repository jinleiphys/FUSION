# Three-body structure of the $nnΛ$ system with $ΛN-ΣN$ coupling

> The structure of the three-body $nnΛ$ system, which has been observed recently by the HypHI collaboration, is investigated taking $ΛN-ΣN$ coupling explicitly into account. The $YN$ and $NN$ interactions employed in this work reproduce the binding energies of $^3_Λ$H, $^4_Λ$H and $^4_Λ$He. We do not find any $^3_Λn$ bound state, which contradicts the interpretation of the data reported by the HypHI collaboration.

# Three-body structure of the $nn\Lambda$ system with $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling

## Key claim
The authors find no bound state in the $^3_{\Lambda}n$ ($nn\Lambda$) system when using realistic $YN$ and $NN$ interactions that reproduce the binding energies of known $s$-shell hypernuclei, contradicting the interpretation of the HypHI collaboration's 2012 observation. A bound state can only be produced by artificially enhancing the tensor part of the $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling by 20% or the $nn$ $^1S_0$ interaction by 35%, but such adjustments cause the binding energies of $^3_{\Lambda}$H, $^4_{\Lambda}$H, $^4_{\Lambda}$He, and $^3$H to become overbound by 0.6 to 3 MeV compared to experimental data.

## Method
The authors performed three-body variational calculations using the Gaussian Expansion Method for the $nn\Lambda$ system, with the $YN$ interaction taken as a simulated NSC97f potential that includes explicit $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling via central, tensor, and spin-orbit terms, and the $NN$ interaction taken as the AV8 potential. The wavefunction was expanded in Jacobi coordinates for all rearrangement channels in an $LS$ coupling scheme, with the two neutrons antisymmetrized. The authors then systematically tuned the tensor part of the $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling and the $nn$ $^1S_0$ interaction strength to test whether a bound $^3_{\Lambda}n$ could be produced while maintaining consistency with the binding energies of $^3_{\Lambda}$H, $^4_{\Lambda}$H, $^4_{\Lambda}$He, and $^3$H.

## Key numbers
- Calculated $\Lambda$ separation energy of $^3_{\Lambda}$H: $B_{\Lambda} = 0.19$ MeV, consistent with the observed value of $0.13 \pm 0.05$ MeV.
- Calculated binding energies of $^4_{\Lambda}$H and $^4_{\Lambda}$He: 2.33 MeV and 2.28 MeV, respectively.
- When the tensor part of the $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling ($^3V^T_{N\Lambda - N\Sigma}$) was multiplied by 1.20, a very weakly bound $^3_{\Lambda}n$ state appeared at $-0.054$ MeV relative to the $nn\Lambda$ three-body breakup threshold, but $^3_{\Lambda}$H became overbound to $-0.7$ MeV (observed: $-0.13$ MeV) and a spurious $J=3/2^+$ excited state appeared at $-0.4$ MeV.
- When the $nn$ $^1S_0$ interaction was multiplied by a factor of 1.35, producing a di-neutron binding energy of $-1.269$ MeV, the $^3_{\Lambda}n$ system became bound at $-1.272$ MeV, but $^3$H became overbound to $-13.93$ MeV (observed: $-8.48$ MeV).
- The $nn$ scattering length with the unmodified AV8 potential: $a_{nn} = -23.7$ fm; with factor 1.13: $a_{nn} = 25.1$ fm; with factor 1.35: $a_{nn} = 6.88$ fm.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of the $^3_{\Lambda}n$ system by Downs (1959), who used a variational method without $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling and found no bound state, and by Garcilazo (2007), who performed Faddeev calculations with $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling and also found no bound state. The present calculation uses a more realistic $YN$ interaction (simulated NSC97f) that reproduces the binding energies of $^3_{\Lambda}$H, $^4_{\Lambda}$H, and $^4_{\Lambda}$He, unlike earlier work. The results contradict the experimental claim by the HypHI collaboration (Rappold, 2013) of a bound $^3_{\Lambda}n$. The authors suggest that further experimental measurement of the $^3_{\Lambda}n$ binding energy is urgently needed to resolve the discrepancy, and that missing elements in the calculation (such as three-body forces or more sophisticated $YN$ interactions) may need to be considered.
