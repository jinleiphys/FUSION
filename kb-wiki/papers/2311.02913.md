# The study on the multiplicity dependence of ridge behavior in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV at the LHC

> The long-range near-side ridge phenomenon in two-particle correlation is crucial for understanding the motion of partons after high-energy heavy-ion collisions. While it has been well explained by the hydrodynamic flow effect of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy-ion collisions, the recent observation of the ridge structure in small systems has led to debates about the applicability of hydrodynamic models to explain the phenomenon since the collisions in small systems could not be sufficient to produce the medium required by the QGP matter. The Momentum Kick Model (MKM), on the other hand, explains the long-range near-side ridge phenomenon by the kinematic process; the high-momentum jet particles collide with medium partons, transfer their momentum to them (called the ``kick" process), and induce collective motion of the kicked-partons resulting in the ridge phenomenon. This MKM has successfully described the ridge structure in heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC. Furthermore, since the ridge phenomenon in small systems is prominent in high-multiplicity events, the MKM with multiplicity dependence (MKMwM) has been studied in $pp$ collisions at the LHC using a relationship between the number of kicked-partons and the multiplicity through an impact parameter. In this research, we extend the previous study with more recent experimental data-driven parameters and apply them to the new measurements that have a wider multiplicity range with $p_T$ and $ΔΦ$ bins at the LHC. Simultaneously, we not only provide a theoretical basis for the ridge behavior from the new measurements but also predict the ridge structure at the energies scheduled by the LHC in the upcoming Run 3 experiments.

## Key claim

The Momentum Kick Model with multiplicity dependence (MKMwM) can describe the long-range near-side ridge phenomenon in proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV across a wider multiplicity range than previously tested, provided that the momentum transfer parameter and survival factor are treated as free parameters fitted to the new CMS data.

## Method

The authors extended the Momentum Kick Model (MKM) by incorporating a multiplicity-dependent formalism that links the average number of kicked-partons to the charged particle multiplicity through an impact parameter. They applied this model to CMS data for proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, fitting the momentum transfer parameter q and the survival factor f_R using least-squares minimization, while scaling other parameters (medium temperature, proton radius, and formation time) according to the collision energy. The model was used to compute the azimuthal angle distribution of associated particles for four transverse momentum bins and four multiplicity classes, and the results were compared to experimental data after applying the Zero-Yield-At-Minimum (ZYAM) background subtraction procedure.

## Key numbers

* Momentum transfer parameter q = 1.2 GeV/c for pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, determined by least-squares fitting.
* Medium temperature T = 0.77 GeV at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, scaled from the ratio of average transverse momenta: 0.604 GeV/c divided by 0.392 GeV/c equals 1.54.
* Proton radius R_A = R_B = 0.74 fm, derived from the inelastic cross-section sigma_inel = 68.6 mb.
* Formation time t_0 = 0.39 fm/c at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, obtained by dividing the 0.2 TeV value (0.60 fm/c) by 1.54.
* Average charged particle multiplicity <N_ch> = 79 used to compute kappa' = 236.
* Average number of kicked-partons <N_k> ranges from 0.078 for <N_ch> = 16 to 1.309 for <N_ch> = 131.

## Context

This work extends the previous application of the Momentum Kick Model with multiplicity dependence (MKMwM) by Wong and collaborators, which was applied to proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV with a limited multiplicity range up to N_ch = 120. The new CMS data at 13 TeV extend the multiplicity range to N_ch = 180 and provide four times more data points, allowing more precise testing of the model. The study also addresses two questions raised by the CMS Collaboration: the ridge yield peaks near p_T = 1 GeV/c and decreases with increasing p_T, and the ridge yield increases approximately linearly with N_ch. The authors further predict the ridge structure for proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.3 and 8.5 TeV, which are scheduled for the upcoming LHC Run 3 experiments.
