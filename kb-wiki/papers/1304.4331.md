# QCD phase diagram at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials in Polyakov loop extended quark meson model with vector interaction

> We investigate the QCD phase diagram of isospin asymmetric matter using the Polyakov loop extended quark meson (PQM) model with vector interaction. The critical point temperature is found to decrease in isospin asymmetric matter and disappear at large isospin chemical potential. We also discuss the QCD phase transition in the neutron star core. From comparison of the QCD phase diagram in PQM and corresponding baryon and isospin chemical potentials of neutron star matter in relativistic mean field models, we show that the order of the chiral phase transition in the neutron star core could be crossover because of large isospin chemical potential.

## Key claim

The critical point temperature decreases with increasing isospin chemical potential and disappears at large isospin chemical potential, but this result depends on the assumption that s-wave pion condensation does not occur in dense baryonic matter, consistent with functional renormalization group results and s-wave πN repulsion arguments.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Polyakov loop extended quark meson (PQM) model with vector interaction, including ω and ρ meson fields, to compute the QCD phase diagram in the three-dimensional space of temperature, baryon chemical potential, and isospin chemical potential. They solved the mean-field equations of motion for the σ, Polyakov loop, ω, and ρ⁰ fields, and identified the chiral phase boundary by the peak of the chiral susceptibility and the deconfinement boundary by the half-value prescription for the Polyakov loop. They then compared the resulting phase diagram with the β-equilibrium line in neutron star matter calculated using relativistic mean field models (TM1 and IOSTY).

## Key numbers

- Critical point at δμ = 0 and r = 0: (T_CP, μ_CP) = (117, 975) MeV
- At δμ = 50 MeV and r = 0, the chiral phase transition becomes crossover for δμ ≳ 50 MeV at T = 96.5 MeV
- The transition quark chemical potential shifts are 7.0 MeV for δμ = 50 MeV and 13 MeV for δμ = 70 MeV, comparable to the simple estimate of 7.2 and 14 MeV
- For r = 0.2 at δμ = 0, the critical point temperature is T_CP = 101.5 MeV
- The chiral transition becomes crossover for r ≳ 0.2 at T = 101.5 MeV and δμ = 0
- In neutron star matter, δμ is calculated to reach 100 MeV in relativistic mean field models
- Hyperons appear at μB ≃ 1100 MeV in the IOSTY model

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram in isospin asymmetric matter using chiral effective models, including the authors' own prior work (Ohnishi 2011) on critical point sweep during black hole formation. It contrasts with mean-field PNJL results (PNJL-dmu) that find s-wave pion condensation at finite δμ, and instead agrees with functional renormalization group results (FRG) that show pion condensation is suppressed at large baryon chemical potential. The comparison with neutron star matter from relativistic mean field models (TM1 by Sugahara 1993 and IOSTY by Ishizuka 2008) enables discussion of whether the chiral transition in neutron star cores is first order or crossover, which has implications for the stiffness of the equation of state and the maximum mass of neutron stars.
