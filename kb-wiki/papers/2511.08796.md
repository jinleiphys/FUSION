# Correlations between heavy mesons and the creation of the charmonia, bottomonia, and $B_c$ mesons in high energy $pp$ collisions

> The different QCD processes, which can produce a heavy quark-antiquark ($Q\bar Q$) pair, induce different correlations between the heavy quarks. Employing the EPOS4HQ event generator we study the consequences of these correlations and compare the calculation with experimental results on open and hidden heavy flavour mesons, measured in proton-proton (pp) collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. We find that the measured correlations between heavy mesons are a direct image of the different production mechanisms, which contribute also in a different way to the transverse momentum distribution of open and hidden heavy flavour mesons. The latter are calculated in a Wigner density approach which also enables to reproduce quantitatively the measured $B_c$ spectra. This agreement allows conclusions on the spatial distribution of the heavy quark creation processes.

## Key claim

The measured correlations between heavy mesons in pp collisions directly reflect the relative contributions of three distinct QCD production mechanisms (flavor creation, flavor excitation, and gluon splitting), and these same mechanisms also determine the transverse momentum distributions of open and hidden heavy flavor mesons, with the Wigner density approach enabling quantitative reproduction of the measured $B_c$ spectra.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS4HQ event generator to simulate heavy quark-antiquark pair production in pp collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, separating the contributions from flavor creation, flavor excitation, and gluon splitting processes. Quarkonium formation was modeled using a quantal density matrix approach expressed in terms of Wigner densities, where the Wigner functions were constructed from wave functions obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with a Cornell potential (parameters: $\alpha=0.513$, $\sigma=0.17$ GeV$^2$, $m_c=1.5$ GeV, $m_b=5.2$ GeV). The authors compared their calculated azimuthal correlations and transverse momentum spectra with experimental data from LHCb and ALICE for $D$ mesons, $B$ mesons, and quarkonia.

## Key numbers

- pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV used for correlation studies of $c\bar c$ and $b\bar b$ pairs
- pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV used for comparison with LHCb $D^0\bar D^0$ correlation data
- pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV used for comparison with STAR $D^0$ spectra at RHIC
- Critical energy density for QGP formation threshold: 0.57 GeV/fm$^3$
- $p_T$ cut of $p_T > 2$ GeV applied for azimuthal correlation studies of heavy quarks and mesons
- $p_T$ cut of $p_T > 3$ GeV applied for the $D^0\bar D^0$ comparison with LHCb data in the rapidity range $2<y<4$
- Cornell potential parameters: $\alpha=0.513$, $\sigma=0.17$ GeV$^2$
- Quark masses: $m_c=1.5$ GeV, $m_b=5.2$ GeV
- Theoretical masses from Schrodinger equation: $J/\psi$ = 3.071 GeV (experimental: 3.097 GeV), $\Upsilon(1S)$ = 9.390 GeV (experimental: 9.460 GeV), $B_c(1S)$ = 6.482 GeV (experimental: 6.275 GeV)
- Root-mean-square radii: $J/\psi$ = 0.182 fm$^2$, $\Upsilon(1S)$ = 0.042 fm$^2$, $B_c(1S)$ = 0.115 fm$^2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous density matrix approach for quarkonium production (Song 2017, Villar 2022, Song 2023) from charmonia to bottomonia and $B_c$ mesons, while simultaneously connecting heavy quark correlations to open heavy flavor meson observables. The study uses the EPOS4 event generator (Werner 2023) and its heavy quark extension EPOS4HQ (Zhao 2024), which reproduces open heavy flavor data in pp and heavy-ion collisions. The results enable experimental extraction of the relative importance of different QCD production mechanisms (flavor creation, flavor excitation, gluon splitting) through measurements of azimuthal correlations between heavy mesons, and provide a framework for understanding how spatial distributions of heavy quark creation affect quarkonium formation.
