# Pseudoscalar Mass Spectrum in a Soft-Wall Model of AdS/QCD

> The Anti-de Sitter Space/Conformal Field Theory (AdS/CFT) correspondence may offer new and useful insights into the non-perturbative regime of strongly coupled gauge theories such as Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Recently a modified soft-wall AdS/QCD model incorporated independent sources for explicit and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and linear confinement. This model contains a modified dilaton and higher-order interaction terms in the Lagrangian. Within this model we explore the radial pseudoscalar mass spectrum using two different representations of the pion field. We find the mass eigenvalues associated with each representation, show the equivalence between the two, and find good agreement with the pion masses. The Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation is naturally obtained.

# Pseudoscalar Mass Spectrum in a Soft-Wall Model of AdS/QCD

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two different representations of the pion field (exponential and linear) in a modified soft-wall AdS/QCD model produce equivalent equations of motion and mass eigenvalues, but only when the full coupled system is solved directly; the method of eliminating one field to obtain eigenvalues of the derivative field ∂zπe yields a spectrum that does not contain the Goldstone boson and does not match the true pseudoscalar masses.

## Method

The authors construct a 5-dimensional AdS/QCD action with a modified dilaton profile and quartic interaction terms, using a scalar vacuum expectation value v(z) parameterized by quark mass mq, chiral condensate σ, and confinement scale λ. They derive coupled differential equations for the pseudoscalar field π and the longitudinal component of the axial-vector field φ in both exponential and linear representations, then solve the system numerically using a matrix propagation method to find mass eigenvalues. For large-n excitations (n ≥ 4), they use an asymptotic large-z expansion to obtain analytic mass formulas.

## Key numbers

- Ground state pion mass: 143 MeV (calculated) vs 140 MeV (experimental)
- First excited state: 1557 MeV (calculated) vs 1300 ± 100 MeV (experimental)
- Second excited state: 1887 MeV (calculated) vs 1816 ± 14 MeV (experimental)
- Third excited state: 2095 MeV (calculated) vs 2070 MeV (experimental, listed as further state)
- Fourth excited state: 2298 MeV (calculated) vs 2360 MeV (experimental, listed as further state)
- Input parameters: mq = 9.75 MeV, σ = (204.5 MeV)^3, λ = 0.18 GeV^2, g5 = 2π, κ = 15
- Large-n mass formula: m_n^2 = (4n - 3)λ + g5^2 Γ^2 for n = 4, 5, ...
- Pion decay constant extracted from GOR relation: fπ ≈ 90 MeV

## Context

This work extends the modified soft-wall AdS/QCD model of Gherghetta (2009), which incorporated independent sources for explicit and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and linear confinement. The paper completes the calculation of radial mass spectra for spin-0 and spin-1 fields in that model, focusing on the pseudoscalar sector. It corrects an approach used by Sui (2009) that incorrectly equated eigenvalues of the derivative field ∂zπe with the true pseudoscalar masses. The results enable further holographic studies of pion properties, including the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation, and provide a benchmark for comparing bottom-up AdS/QCD models with experimental meson spectra.
