# Effects of Short Range Correlations on Ca Isotopes

> The effect of Short Range Correlations (SRC) on Ca isotopes is studied using a simple phenomenological model. Theoretical expressions for the charge (proton) form factors, densities and moments of Ca nuclei are derived. The role of SRC in reproducing the empirical data for the charge density differences is examined. Their influence on the depletion of the nuclear Fermi surface is studied and the fractional occupation probabilities of the shell model orbits of Ca nuclei are calculated. The variation of SRC as function of the mass number is also discussed.

## Key claim

Short Range Correlations (SRC) alone can reproduce the correct trend of charge density differences in Ca isotopes, but additional correlations (such as surface vibrations) are necessary to fully match experimental data, especially for the $^{48}$Ca $-$ $^{40}$Ca difference where the maximum is not reproduced well.

## Method

The authors used a Jastrow-type correlation function with a state-independent correlation parameter $\lambda$ to modify harmonic oscillator wave functions for the relative motion of nucleons. They applied the factor cluster expansion of Ristig, Ter Low, and Clark to derive analytic expressions for correlated charge form factors, densities, and moments, then extended the method from closed-shell nuclei to the Ca isotopic chain by determining the harmonic oscillator parameter $b_1$ from an isospin-dependent formula for $\hbar\omega$ and adjusting the correlation parameter $\mu$ to reproduce empirical isotope shift data. Finally, they extracted fractional occupation probabilities by mapping the correlated densities onto a natural orbital representation with two harmonic oscillator bases.

## Key numbers

- For $^{40}$Ca, the constants in the approximate MS charge radius formula are $C_{HO}=3$ and $C_{SRC}=12.4673$, with the approximate formula differing less than 0.08% from the exact expression.
- The neutron MS charge radius used is $r_n^2 = -0.116$ fm$^2$.
- The isospin-dependent oscillator spacing formula used is $\hbar\omega = 38.6 A^{-1/3} [1 + 1.646 A^{-1} - 0.191 (N-Z) A^{-1}]^{-2}$.
- The correlation parameter $\mu$ increases up to $^{44}$Ca and then decreases, following the same variation as the isotope shifts.
- The relative depletion of Ca isotopes shows a clear parabolic shape analogous to the correlation parameter variation.
- Occupation probabilities for very deep states are very close to one, while surface levels deviate significantly from unity.

## Context

This work extends a Jastrow-correlation method previously applied to closed-shell nuclei in the region $4 \leq A \leq 40$ (Lalazissis et al., 1994) to the isotopic chain of Ca, motivated by the anomalous A-dependence of charge radii observed in laser spectroscopy (Andl, 1982; Otten, 1989). The study contrasts with mean-field calculations that fail to reproduce the parabolic behavior of charge radii, and with approaches by Zamick (1971), Talmi (1984), and Barranco and Broglia (1985) that accounted for ground-state correlations but not SRC. The method enables predictions for charge form factors and density differences in other isotopic chains where SRC effects are important.
