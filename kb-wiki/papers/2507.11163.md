# Constraints on Kinetic Mixing of Dark Photons from Dilepton Spectra

> Dark photons, the hypothetical gauge bosons associated with an additional $U(1)^{\prime}$ symmetry, can couple to Standard Model particles through a small kinetic mixing parameter $\varepsilon$ with the ordinary photon. This mechanism provides a portal between the dark sector and visible matter. In this study, we present a procedure to derive theoretical upper bounds on the kinetic mixing parameter $\varepsilon^2(M_U)$ by analyzing dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions across a broad energy range, from SIS to LHC energies. Our analysis is based on the microscopic Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which successfully reproduces the measured dilepton spectra in $p+p$, $p+A$, and $A+A$ collisions across the same energy range. Besides the dilepton channels resulting from interactions and decays of Standard Model particles (such as mesons and baryons), the PHSD has been extended to include the decay of hypothetical dark photons into dileptons, $U \to e^+ e^-$. The production of these dark photons occurs via Dalitz decays of $π^0$, $η$, $ω$, $η^{\prime}$, and $Δ$ resonances; direct decays of $ρ$, $ω$, and $ϕ$; the kaon mode $K^+ \to π^+ U$; and thermal $q\bar q$ annihilation in the quark-gluon plasma. Our results show that high-precision measurements of dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions provide a sensitive and competitive probe of dark photons in the MeV to multi-GeV mass range. Furthermore, we quantify the experimental accuracy required to constrain the remaining viable parameter space of kinetic mixing in dark photon scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper derives theoretical upper bounds on the kinetic mixing parameter \(\varepsilon^2(M_U)\) by comparing the sum of Standard Model and dark photon dilepton spectra to experimental yields from heavy-ion collisions, but the bounds are conditional on the specific production channels included (Dalitz decays of \(\pi^0, \eta, \omega, \eta', \Delta\); direct decays of \(\rho, \omega, \phi\); kaon decay \(K^+ \to \pi^+ U\); and thermal \(q\bar q\) annihilation) and on the use of the PHSD transport model to reproduce the measured dilepton spectra.

## Method

The authors extended the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which solves off-shell transport equations from the Kadanoff-Baym formalism, to include dark photon production and decay into dileptons (\(U \to e^+ e^-\)). Dark photons were produced via Dalitz decays of \(\pi^0, \eta, \omega, \eta'\) and \(\Delta\) resonances; direct decays of \(\rho, \omega, \phi\); the kaon mode \(K^+ \to \pi^+ U\); and thermal \(q\bar q\) annihilation in the quark-gluon plasma, with the yield calculated by integrating over the lifetime of each emitter using the "shining" method. Theoretical upper bounds on \(\varepsilon^2(M_U)\) were obtained by comparing the summed SM+U dilepton spectra to experimental data across a broad energy range from SIS to LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The accessible dark photon mass range was extended to \(M_U = 2\) GeV/\(c^2\), covering the light vector meson sector and QGP dilepton emission.
- For typical parameters (\(\varepsilon^2 \approx 10^{-6}\), \(M_U \approx 1\) GeV/\(c^2\)), the dark photon proper lifetime is \(\tau_U \approx 10^{-16}\) s, corresponding to a decay length of \(c\tau_U \approx 10^{-8}\) m.
- The analytic expression for the branching ratio \(Br(U \to e^+ e^-)\) is valid up to \(M_U \lesssim 0.6\) GeV/\(c^2\), where hadronic channels remain closed.
- The branching ratio prediction was extended up to \(M_U < 2.5\) GeV/\(c^2\) using the vector-meson-dominance fit of Ilten.
- The critical energy density for the transition between hadronic and partonic phases is \(\varepsilon_C \simeq 0.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\), as extracted from lattice QCD.
- The pre-hadron formation time is \(\tau_F = 0.8\) fm/\(c\).
- The string fragmentation thresholds are: baryon-baryon \(\sqrt{s_{BB}^{\mathrm{th}}} = 2.65\) GeV, meson-baryon \(\sqrt{s_{mB}^{\mathrm{th}}} = 2.35\) GeV, and meson-meson \(\sqrt{s_{mm}^{\mathrm{th}}} = 1.3\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Schmidt (2021), Bratkovskaya (2022), and Romero Jorge (2024) that used only Dalitz decays of \(\pi^0, \eta\) and the \(\Delta\) resonance to derive limits on \(\varepsilon^2(M_U)\) up to \(M_U = 0.6\) GeV/\(c^2\). The present analysis adds three extensions: an extended mass range to \(M_U = 2\) GeV/\(c^2\), additional hadronic channels including \(\omega \to \pi^0 U\), direct conversions \(\rho, \omega, \phi \to U\), and the kaon decay \(K^+ \to \pi^+ U\), as well as partonic radiation from thermal \(q\bar q \to U\) annihilation at RHIC and LHC energies. The results show that high-precision dilepton measurements in heavy-ion collisions provide a sensitive and competitive probe of dark photons in the MeV to multi-GeV mass range, and the authors quantify the experimental accuracy required to constrain the remaining viable parameter space of kinetic mixing.
