# On survey of nuclei and hypernuclei in multifragmentation

> Multifragmentation reactions are dominating processes for the decomposition of highly excited nuclei leading to the fragment production in heavy-ion collisions. At high energy reactions strange particles are abundantly produced. We present a novel development of the Statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) as its generalization for the hyper-matter which is formed after the hyperon capture. In this way, it is possible to describe its disintegration into normal and hyper-nuclei. Some properties of hyper-nuclei and their binding energies can be determined from the comparison of the isotope yields. The main focus of this method is to investigate strange and multi-strange hypernuclei since their properties are not easy to measure in traditional hyper-nuclei experiments.

# On survey of nuclei and hypernuclei in multifragmentation

## Key claim

The authors present a generalization of the Statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to describe the disintegration of hyper-matter formed after hyperon capture, claiming that binding energies of hypernuclei can be determined from comparison of isotope yields. The method is presented as a way to investigate strange and multi-strange hypernuclei whose properties are difficult to measure in traditional experiments, but the authors acknowledge that experimental identification of hypernuclei remains challenging and that the method relies on the assumption of chemical equilibrium in the freeze-out volume.

## Method

The authors extend the Statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) by adding a hyper term to the internal free energy of fragments, using a liquid-drop parametrization with volume and surface contributions proportional to the fraction of Lambda hyperons (H/A). Fragment yields are calculated in the grand canonical approximation for excited nuclear residues with specified mass number A0, charge number Z0, and initial Lambda hyperon number H0, at temperatures between 3 and 5 MeV. A double ratio method is then applied to the calculated yields of hypernuclei and their normal counterparts to extract differences in hyperon binding energies between pairs of hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- Saturation yields for hyper-residues in Pb+Pb collisions at 3-5 A GeV: single hypernuclei ~100 mb, double hypernuclei ~1 mb, triple hypernuclei ~0.01 mb (from Ref. Bot17)
- Normal nuclear density used: rho0 ≈ 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Free volume parameter: kappa ≈ 2, giving V_f = kappa * V_0
- Inverse volume level density parameter for A=4 fragments: epsilon_0 ≈ 16 MeV
- Liquid-drop hyper term parametrization: F_{AH}^{hyp} = (H/A) * (-10.68 A + 21.27 A^{2/3}) MeV
- Calculations performed at temperatures T = 3, 4, and 5 MeV for initial systems with A0=200, Z0=80, H0=4
- Calculations for initial systems with A0=100, Z0=40 and A0=200, Z0=80 at T=4 MeV
- Double ratio method applied assuming T=4 MeV for the fragmenting system
- Excitation energies for cold fragments after secondary de-excitation: E* = 1.5 and 2.0 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the hyper-SMM code (Buyukcizmeci 2013, Botvina 2007, Buyukcizmeci 2018, Botvina 2016) for fragment formation mechanisms including evaporation and fission of hyper-nuclei at low energies. The double ratio method for determining hyperon binding energies was introduced in Buyukcizmeci (2018). The statistical approach builds on established multifragmentation models (SMM, Pocahontas 1997, EOS collaboration) that have been successfully applied to normal nuclear fragmentation data. The method is intended for application to future experiments at FAIR (GSI) and NICA (JINR), where hypernuclei with sufficiently large cross sections may be measured in peripheral relativistic ion-ion and hadron-ion deep-inelastic collisions.
