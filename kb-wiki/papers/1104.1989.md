# The ^4He trimer as an Efimov system

> We review the results obtained in the last four decades which demonstrate the Efimov nature of the $^4$He three-atomic system.

## Key claim

The paper reviews four decades of theoretical and experimental work to demonstrate that the excited state of the ^4He trimer is of Efimov nature, but notes that the estimate for the number of Efimov states based on the ratio of scattering length to effective range yields a value around 0.6–1.3, which neither supports nor disproves the claim without further investigation.

## Method

The authors compile and compare results from multiple computational approaches applied to realistic He-He potentials (HFD-B, LM2M2, TTY, and SAPT families), including momentum-space Faddeev integral equations, adiabatic hyperspherical expansion, and hard-core versions of two-dimensional Faddeev differential equations. They also analyze the behavior of the trimer excited state as the atom-atom potential strength is scaled by a factor λ, tracking how the excited state disappears into a virtual state for λ > 1 and how new excited states emerge from virtual states for λ < 1.

## Key numbers

- The ^4He dimer binding energy for the LM2M2 potential is −1.30348 mK, with an atom-atom scattering length of 100.23 Å
- The ^4He trimer ground-state energy for the LM2M2 potential is approximately 126.5 mK, and the excited-state energy is approximately 2.276 mK
- The ^4He trimer excited state disappears when the atom-atom potential is multiplied by a factor λ ≈ 1.2 (specifically λ ≈ 1.175 for the HFD-B potential)
- For the HFD-B potential at λ = 1.0, the atom-dimer scattering length is 160 Å and the atom-atom scattering length is 88.6 Å
- The experimental ^4He dimer bond length is 52 ± 4 Å, and the experimental ^4He–^4He scattering length is 104^{+8}_{-18} Å (Grisenti et al., 2000)
- The experimental ^4He dimer energy is 1.1^{+0.3}_{-0.2} mK (Grisenti et al., 2000)

## Context

This review extends the original prediction by Lim, Duffy, and Damert (1977) that the ^4He trimer should possess Efimov-type bound states, and consolidates subsequent confirmations by Cornelius and Glöckle (using Faddeev equations) and by Esry, Lin, and Greene (using adiabatic hyperspherical expansion). The work enables further investigations into Efimov aspects of larger He_n clusters, mixed atomic systems, and the influence of external fields, as well as providing a benchmark for universal three-body physics in ultracold atomic gases.
