# Optimization of finite-range effective interaction for in-medium cross sections

> In order to incorporate the finite range effect into effective interactions, a modification of the Skyrme force by introducing a cut-off factor for high momentum transfers is proposed. The parameters of the cut-off factor are determined by fitting the microscopic in-medium cross-sections of Li and Machleidt over a wide range of energy and nuclear density. Results for the SkM* and SLy4 forces are presented.

## Key claim

A modification of the Skyrme effective interaction by introducing a cut-off factor for high momentum transfers can reproduce the microscopic in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections of Li and Machleidt, but only when using a specific rational-function parameterization of the cut-off rather than simple Gaussian or Breit-Wigner forms.

## Method

The authors modified the Skyrme force by multiplying the zero-range \(t_0\) and \(t_3\) interaction matrix elements by a cut-off factor \(\mathcal{C}(|\langle\mathbf{q}^2\rangle|)\) that depends on the relative momentum operator. They parameterized the cut-off as a rational function \(\overline{\mathcal{C}}(q,q') = (1 + \beta_1^2 (q^2 + q'^2)/\hbar^2) / (1 + \beta_2^2 (q^2 + q'^2)/\hbar^2)\) and determined the \(\beta_1\) and \(\beta_2\) parameters by fitting the resulting in-medium cross-sections to the microscopic calculations of Li and Machleidt at densities \(\rho_0\), \(\rho_0/2\), and \(\rho_0/3\) for the SkM* and SLy4 forces.

## Key numbers

- For the (SLy4)^cut force: \(\beta_1 = 0.39\) fm and \(\beta_2 = 2.05\) fm (spin-isospin averaged values).
- For the (SkM*)^cut force: \(\beta_1 = 0.29\) fm and \(\beta_2 = 1.66\) fm (spin-isospin averaged values).
- At normal density \(\rho_0\) for SLy4: \(\mathcal{R}_{tot}(\rho) = 226.5\) (in units of \(\pi m^2 / [4\hbar (2\pi\hbar)^3]\)), with \(\beta_1 = 0.60\) fm and \(\beta_2 = 2.80\) fm for the total channel.
- At density \(\rho_0/2\) for SLy4: \(\mathcal{R}_{tot}(\rho) = 315.3\), with \(\beta_1 = 0.37\) fm and \(\beta_2 = 2.03\) fm for the total channel.
- At density \(\rho_0/3\) for SLy4: \(\mathcal{R}_{tot}(\rho) = 391.3\), with \(\beta_1 = 0.38\) fm and \(\beta_2 = 2.09\) fm for the total channel.
- For SkM* at \(\rho_0\): \(\mathcal{R}_{tot}(\rho) = 186.4\), with \(\beta_1 = 0.44\) fm and \(\beta_2 = 2.03\) fm for the total channel.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Ayik, Lacroix, and Chomaz (2000) and Lacroix, Chomaz, and Ayik (2000) to incorporate finite-range effects into Skyrme interactions for use in extended mean-field and transport models. The standard Skyrme and Gogny forces (Gogny, 1975) were shown to give poor approximations for the energy dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections, particularly at low densities where they reach unphysically large values. The proposed modification enables practical applications of Boltzmann-type semi-classical transport models (Bertsch and Das Gupta, 1988) and molecular dynamics calculations (Aichelin, 1991) that require realistic in-medium cross-sections over a wide range of energy and density.
