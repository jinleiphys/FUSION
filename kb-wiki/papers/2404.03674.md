# Target normal single-spin asymmetry in inclusive electron-nucleon scattering in the 1/Nc expansion

> The target normal single-spin asymmetry in electron nucleon scattering is studied in the framework of the 1/Nc expansion of QCD, which allows for a rigorous description in the energy range that includes the Delta resonance and below the second baryon resonance region. The asymmetry is driven by the absorptive part of the two-photon exchange component of the scattering amplitude, being therefore the most unambiguous two-photon exchange effect. Such amplitude is shown to be described up to the next to leading order in the 1/Nc expansion only in terms of the charge and magnetic form factors of the nucleons, consequence of the approximate $SU(4)$ spin flavor symmetry valid in the large Nc limit for baryons. A discussion is provided of the 1/Nc expansion framework along with the results for the asymmetries in elastic, inelastic, and inclusive electron-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

The target normal single-spin asymmetry (TSSA) in inclusive electron-nucleon scattering can be predicted up to next-to-leading order in the 1/Nc expansion using only the nucleon's charge and magnetic form factors, provided the energy range includes the Delta resonance but remains below the second baryon resonance region. The calculation is rigorous only within this restricted energy domain because the 1/Nc expansion combined with approximate SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry controls the hadronic contributions.

## Method

The TSSA is computed from the interference of the one-photon exchange amplitude with the absorptive part of the two-photon exchange amplitude, evaluated using the 1/Nc expansion of QCD. The electromagnetic current is expanded to leading and next-to-leading order in 1/Nc, with matrix elements between nucleon and Delta states determined by SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry. The hadronic tensor is constructed from products of these current matrix elements, and the phase-space integrals over the intermediate electron momentum in the box diagram are performed analytically using dipole form factors and a Breit-Wigner approximation for the Delta decay width.

## Key numbers

- The TSSA magnitude is in the range 10^-3 to 10^-2 for both proton and neutron targets.
- The dipole form factor cutoff parameter used is Lambda_EM^2 = 0.71 GeV^2.
- The Delta decay width is taken as Gamma_Delta = 0.125 GeV.
- At leading order in 1/Nc, the asymmetry scales as A_N = O(alpha N_c).
- The Delta-N mass difference is O(N_c^-1), which is 1/3 in physical units for N_c=3.

## Context

This work extends the large-Nc framework previously applied to the TSSA at leading order by Goity, Weiss, and collaborators (GWW1) to next-to-leading order, incorporating subleading terms in the electromagnetic current and the physical Delta-N mass splitting. The results enable direct experimental tests at facilities such as MAMI in Mainz, where a polarized target and electron beam in the relevant energy range are available. The TSSA is the most unambiguous two-photon exchange effect, complementing other approaches such as electron-positron scattering comparisons proposed at Jefferson Lab (Grauvogel) and DESY, and dispersion-theoretic analyses (PVdH) that require more experimental input.
