# Center-of-mass effects in electromagnetic two-proton knockout reactions

> The role of center-of mass (CM) effects in the one-body nuclear current in the description of electromagnetically induced two-nucleon knockout reactions is discussed in connection with the problem of the lack of orthogonality between initial bound states and final scattering states obtained by the use of an energy-dependent optical model potential. Results for the cross sections of the exclusive $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp)$^{14}$C and $^{16}$O($γ$,pp)$^{14}$C knockout reactions in different kinematics are presented and discussed. In super-parallel kinematics CM effects produce a strong enhancement of the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp)$^{14}$C$_{\rm g.s.}$ cross section which strongly reduces the destructive interference between the one-body and $Δ$-current and the sensitivity to the treatment of the $Δ$-current found in previous work.

## Key claim

Center-of-mass (CM) effects in the one-body nuclear current produce a strong enhancement of the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp)$^{14}$C$_{\rm g.s.}$ cross section in super-parallel kinematics, which strongly reduces the destructive interference between the one-body and $\Delta$-current and the sensitivity to the treatment of the $\Delta$-current found in previous work (Giusti et al., 2007). The authors show that the large difference between old and new results is mostly due to CM effects and not to the treatment of spuriosity or restoration of orthogonality between initial and final state wave functions.

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for exclusive $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp)$^{14}$C and $^{16}$O($\gamma$,pp)$^{14}$C knockout reactions using the Pavia model, where the two-nucleon overlap function (TOF) is obtained from a two-proton spectral function of $^{16}$O including short-range and long-range correlations. The final-state wave function is a product of two uncoupled single-particle distorted wave functions from a complex phenomenological optical potential, and the nuclear current includes one-body and two-body $\Delta$-isobar contributions. Three approaches were compared: approach A (subtracting the one-body current without correlations), approach B (subtracting without CM corrections), and approach C (explicit Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization of single-particle bound and scattering states to enforce orthogonality).

## Key numbers

- In super-parallel kinematics with $E_0=855$ MeV, $\omega=215$ MeV, and $q=316$ MeV/$c$, the one-body current cross section enhancement from CM effects is a factor of about 5 in the maximum region.
- The final cross section (OB+$\Delta$) in the new calculations is more than one order of magnitude larger than in old calculations at low recoil momentum, with an enhancement factor of about 30 in the maximum region.
- For the $^{16}$O($\gamma$,pp)$^{14}$C$_{\rm g.s.}$ reaction at $E_\gamma=400$ MeV, CM effects do not affect the final cross section for recoil momentum values up to $\sim 200$ MeV/$c$, but produce a large increase at higher $p_B$ values.
- In coplanar symmetrical kinematics with the same $E_0=855$ MeV, $\theta_e=18^\circ$, and $\omega=215$ MeV, the CM effect gives only small differences compared to previous results.

## Context

This work extends the Pavia model calculations of Giusti et al. (2002) and Schwamb et al. (2003) by properly including all center-of-mass effects in the one-body current, which were previously neglected. The results contradict the strong destructive interference between one-body and $\Delta$-current found in earlier work (Schwamb et al., 2003) for super-parallel kinematics, and show that the sensitivity to different $\Delta$-current parametrizations is drastically reduced. The findings motivate further experimental and theoretical research in pp-knockout, particularly using the super-parallel kinematics realized in recent MAMI experiments (Rosner, 2002; Middleton, 2005).
