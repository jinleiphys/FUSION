# Chiral symmetry breaking, entanglement, and the nucleon spin decomposition

> The nucleon is naturally viewed as a bipartite system of valence spin -- defined by its non-vanishing chiral charge -- and non-valence or sea spin. The sea spin can be traced over to give a reduced density matrix, and it is shown that the resulting entanglement entropy acts as an order parameter of chiral symmetry breaking in the nucleon. In the large-$N_c$ limit, the entanglement entropy vanishes and the valence spin accounts for all of the nucleon spin, while in the limit of maximal entanglement entropy, the nucleon loses memory of the valence spin and consequently has spin dominated by the sea. The nucleon state vector in the chiral basis, fit to low-energy data, gives a valence spin content consistent with experiment and lattice QCD determinations, and has large entanglement entropy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that entanglement entropy between valence and non-valence (sea) helicity degrees of freedom in the nucleon acts as an order parameter for chiral symmetry breaking within the nucleon, and that this entanglement provides a qualitative explanation for why the valence spin content of the nucleon is small. The claim is hedged by the scale dependence of the separation into valence and sea spin, and by the truncation of the chiral basis depending on an energy cutoff.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic nucleon state vector in light-cone coordinates using a chiral basis that separates valence helicity (carrying non-vanishing chiral charge) from sea helicity. They trace over the sea degrees of freedom to obtain a reduced density matrix for the valence helicity and compute the resulting entanglement entropy. A minimal two-component model extending the large-\(N_c\) result is fit to the \(\Delta\)-resonance decay width to pions, and the resulting mixing angle determines the valence spin content and entanglement entropy.

## Key numbers

- The mixing angle \(\psi = 41 \pm 4^\circ\) is obtained from fitting \(|{\cal C}_{\Delta N}|\) to the \(\Delta\)-resonance decay width to pions.
- This fit determines the valence spin content \(\Delta\Sigma = 0.14 \pm 0.13\) and the axial coupling \(g_A = 1.38 \pm 0.05\).
- The entanglement entropy is \(S_N = 0.98 \pm 0.02\) in units of the maximum entropy of \(\log 2\).
- The result for \(\Delta\Sigma\) is compared to the JAM collaboration global analysis value \(\Delta\Sigma = 0.36 \pm 0.09\) at a renormalization scale of \(Q^2 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- In the large-\(N_c\) limit, the entanglement entropy vanishes and valence spin accounts for all nucleon spin; in the maximally entangled limit (\(\psi = 45^\circ\)), the nucleon spin is given entirely by the sea.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) analysis of nucleon structure (Gervais 1983, Dashen 1993) by incorporating chiral symmetry breaking through entanglement between valence and sea helicity. It builds on prior work using light-cone coordinates and Fock-space expansions (Dirac 1949, Leutwyler 1977, Brodsky 2006) and on renormalization group arguments for integrating out low-\(x\) partons (Casher 1974, Susskind 1994). The approach enables a qualitative explanation for the small valence spin content observed in experiment and lattice QCD simulations (Alexandrou 2017, Lin 2018, Liang 2018), and suggests that lattice QCD simulations could directly access this chiral entanglement entropy.
