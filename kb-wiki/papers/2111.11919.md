# Implications of NICER for neutron star matter: the QHC21 equation of state

> The recent NICER measurement of the radius of the neutron star PSR J0740+6620, and the inferred small variation of radii from 1.4$M_\odot$ to 2.1$M_\odot$, reveal key features of the equation of state of neutron star matter. The pressure rises rapidly in the regime of baryon density $n \sim$ 2-4 times nuclear saturation density, $n_0$ -- the region where we expect hadronic matter to be undergoing transformation into quark matter -- and the pressure in the nuclear regime is greater than predicted by microscopic many-body variational calculations of nuclear matter. To incorporate these insights into the microscopic physics from the nuclear to the quark matter regimes, we construct an equation of state, QHC21, within the framework of quark-hadron crossover (QHC). We include nuclear matter results primarily based on the state-of-the-art chiral effective field theory, but also note results of using nuclear matter variational calculations based on empirical nuclear forces. We employ explicit nuclear degrees of freedom only up to $n \sim 1.5n_0$, in order to explore the possibility of further physical degrees of freedom than nucleonic here. The resulting QHC21, which has a peak in sound velocity in $\sim 2$-$4 n_0$, is stiffer than the earlier QHC19 below 2$n_0$, predicting larger radii in substantial agreement with the NICER data.

## Key claim

The NICER data's inference that neutron star radii vary little from 1.4 to 2.1 solar masses requires a rapid pressure rise at baryon densities 2-4 times nuclear saturation density, which the authors argue is naturally explained by the onset of quark degrees of freedom rather than purely nucleonic physics; the QHC21 equation of state incorporates this by using nuclear degrees of freedom only up to 1.5 times saturation density and a quark-hadron crossover framework, producing stiffer matter below 2 saturation density than the earlier QHC19.

## Method

The authors constructed the QHC21 equation of state within the quark-hadron crossover (QHC) framework, using chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) nuclear matter results at densities below approximately 1.5 times nuclear saturation density and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model quark matter equations of state above 3.5 times saturation density, with a smooth fifth-order polynomial interpolation in baryon chemical potential between these regimes. They varied the vector repulsion coupling g_V and the color superconductivity pairing coupling H in the NJL model, imposing thermodynamic stability, causality (sound speed not exceeding light speed), and the requirement that the maximum neutron star mass reach at least 2.08 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses (from Fonseca et al. 2021)
- Radius of PSR J0740+6620 from Miller et al. analysis: 13.7 +2.6/-1.5 km (NICER+XMM), dropping to 12.35 ± 0.75 km with nuclear and GW170817 constraints
- Radius of PSR J0740+6620 from Riley et al. analysis: 12.39 +1.30/-0.98 km
- Inferred radius of 1.4 solar mass neutron stars: 12.45 ± 0.65 km (Miller et al.) and 12.33 +0.76/-0.81 km (Riley et al.)
- QHC21 radii for 1.4 solar mass stars: 12.3-12.4 km across parameter sets A_chi through D_chi
- QHC21 radii for 2.08 solar mass stars: 12.0-12.5 km across parameter sets A_chi through D_chi
- Maximum masses in QHC21: 2.19-2.37 solar masses across parameter sets A_chi through D_chi
- Sound speed exceeds the conformal limit c^2/3 at densities 2.1-2.3 times nuclear saturation density in QHC21
- Central density of 1.4 solar mass stars in QHC21: 2.5-2.6 times nuclear saturation density
- Central density of 2.08 solar mass stars in QHC21: 3.3-4.3 times nuclear saturation density

## Context

This work extends the QHC framework from Baym et al. (2019) (QHC19) by incorporating new NICER radius measurements for PSR J0740+6620 and using stiffer chiEFT nuclear matter results from Drischler et al. (2020) instead of the softer variational calculations of Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) and Togashi et al. (2017) used previously. The QHC21 equation of state produces radii approximately 0.7-0.8 km larger than QHC19 for 1.4 solar mass stars, bringing predictions into agreement with NICER data, and enables future studies of neutron star cooling, neutrino emission from supernovae, and neutron star merger phenomena using a microscopically motivated equation of state that links to QCD physics.
