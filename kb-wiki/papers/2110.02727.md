# A compact formula for the quantum fluctuations of energy

> A formula to calculate the quantum fluctuations of energy in small subsystems of a hot and relativistic gas is derived. We find an increase in fluctuations for subsystems of small sizes, but we agrees with the energy fluctuations in the canonical ensemble if the size is large enough. Not only one can use our expression to find the limit of the concepts of energy density or fluid element in connection to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but also in other areas of physics where one studies matter with high temperature and velocity.

## Key claim

The paper derives a compact formula for the quantum fluctuations of energy in Gaussian-shaped subsystems of a hot, relativistic gas of spinless bosons, but the derivation explicitly neglects a temperature-independent divergent term attributed to pure vacuum energy fluctuations, and the result is only shown to agree with canonical-ensemble fluctuations in the limit of large subsystem size.

## Method

The author considers a free real scalar field in thermal equilibrium at temperature T, defines a Gaussian-smoothed Hamiltonian density operator for a subsystem of size a, and computes the variance of this operator using thermal expectation values of products of creation and annihilation operators with Bose-Einstein statistics. The resulting expression for the variance sigma^2(a,m,T) is given as a double momentum integral involving the Bose-Einstein distribution, particle energy, momentum, and mass, with Gaussian factors that depend on the subsystem size a.

## Key numbers

* Degeneracy factor g = 40 is used for numerical calculations to be consistent with heavy-ion experiments.
* Numerical calculations for the normalized fluctuation sigma_n start at subsystem size a = 0.3 fm, because sigma_n diverges as a approaches 0.
* For massless particles in the large-size limit, the quantum fluctuation scales as sigma^2 ~ T^5 / a^3.
* In the large-size limit, the product V_a * sigma_n^2 equals T^2 * c_V / epsilon^2, where V_a = a^3 * (2*pi)^(3/2) is the effective volume of the Gaussian subsystem.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum fluctuations in small subsystems of hot relativistic matter, building on prior treatments of thermal field theory and fluctuations by Huang (1987) and others. The derived formula is intended to help determine the minimum size of a fluid element (about 1 fm) for which the concept of energy density remains valid in relativistic hydrodynamics of heavy-ion collisions, as used by Fukushima (2013), Jaiswal (2016), Florkowski (2017), and Romatschke (2017). The results enable quantitative assessment of when quantum energy fluctuations in a subsystem become negligible compared to classical thermal fluctuations.
