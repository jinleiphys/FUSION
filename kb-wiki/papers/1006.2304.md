# The Nucleon Electric Dipole Form Factor From Dimension-Six Time-Reversal Violation

> We calculate the electric dipole form factor of the nucleon that arises as a low-energy manifestation of time-reversal violation in quark-gluon interactions of effective dimension 6: the quark electric and chromoelectric dipole moments, and the gluon chromoelectric dipole moment. We use the framework of two-flavor chiral perturbation theory to one loop.

## Key claim

The nucleon electric dipole form factor (EDFF) arising from dimension-six time-reversal-violating operators (quark electric and chromoelectric dipole moments, and the gluon chromoelectric dipole moment) is calculated in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory to one loop, with the caveat that the momentum dependence of the form factor appears at different chiral orders depending on the source: for the quark chromoelectric dipole moment it appears at the same order as the electric dipole moment, while for the quark electric dipole moment and gluon chromoelectric dipole moment it appears two orders down relative to the lowest-order contribution to the electric dipole moment.

## Method

The authors construct the most general effective Lagrangian for nucleons, pions, and photons that respects chiral symmetry and its breaking patterns from the dimension-six operators, using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with two flavors. They calculate the nucleon electric dipole form factor to the order where momentum dependence first appears for each source, including one-loop diagrams with virtual pions and tree-level contact interactions, using dimensional regularization and renormalization scale dependence absorbed into low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM experimental bound: $|d_n| < 2.9 \cdot 10^{-26} \, e$ cm
- Proton EDM indirect bound: $|d_p| < 7.9 \cdot 10^{-25} \, e$ cm (extracted from atomic Hg EDM)
- Pion decay constant: $F_\pi \simeq 186$ MeV
- Pion-nucleon axial-vector coupling: $g_A \simeq 1.267$
- QCD scale: $M_{\mathrm{QCD}} \sim 1$ GeV
- For the $\bar{\theta}$ term, the bound $\bar{\theta} \simle 2.5 \cdot 10^{-10}$ from the neutron EDM limit
- The isovector Schiff moment from qCEDM: $S'_{1, \mathrm{qCEDM}} = \frac{eg_A\bar{g}_0}{6m_\pi^2(2\pi F_\pi)^2}$
- The isovector momentum dependence function $f(x)$ defined with $f(x\ll 1)=x^2+\mathcal{O}(x^3)$

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of the nucleon electric dipole form factor from the QCD $\bar{\theta}$ term (Bira and Hockings, 2008; Ottnad et al., 2010) to the dimension-six operators that can arise from beyond-Standard-Model physics such as supersymmetric extensions. The results provide model-independent formulas for extrapolating lattice QCD calculations of nucleon EDMs to physical pion masses, and enable connections between nucleon EDMs, Schiff moments, and the underlying sources of time-reversal violation, complementing earlier work on the $\bar{\theta}$ term by Crewther et al. (1979) and the chiral quark model approach of Faessler et al. (2006).
