# Ground state of N=Z doubly closed shell nuclei in CBF theory

> The ground state properties of N=Z doubly closed shell nuclei are studied within correlated basis function theory. A truncated version of the Urbana v14 realistic potential, with spin, isospin and tensor components, is adopted, together with state dependent correlations. Fermi hypernetted chain integral equation and single operator chain approximation are used to evaluate density, distribution function and ground state energy of 16O and 40Ca. The results favourably compare with the available, variational MonteCarlo estimates and provide a first substantial check of the accuracy of the cluster summation method for state dependent correlations. We achieve in finite nuclei at least the same level of accuracy in the treatment of non central interactions and correlations as in nuclear matter. This opens the way for a microscopic study of medium heavy nuclei ground state using present days realistic hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Fermi Hypernetted Chain/Single Operator Chain (FHNC/SOC) method, previously applied to nuclear matter, to finite doubly closed shell nuclei (¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca) using a truncated Urbana v14 potential with state-dependent correlations, achieving a treatment of non-central interactions and correlations in finite nuclei with "at least the same level of accuracy as in nuclear matter", but only for a truncated version of the potential (v6, omitting spin-orbit and higher terms) and without three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The ground state wave function is constructed as a symmetrized product of two-body correlation operators (with spin, isospin, and tensor components) acting on a harmonic oscillator shell-model state. The FHNC integral equations, combined with the Single Operator Chain (SOC) approximation for the non-commuting operatorial correlations, are used to evaluate the one-body density, two-body distribution function, and ground state energy. The correlation functions are obtained by solving Euler-Lagrange equations at the two-body cluster level, using healing distances and quenching factors taken from previous nuclear matter calculations. The Jackson-Feenberg identity is employed for the kinetic energy expectation value.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O, the harmonic oscillator length parameter is fixed at b = 1.543 fm; for ⁴⁰Ca, b = 1.654 fm.
- The nuclear matter variational parameters used for the U14 potential are: central healing distance d_c = 2.15 fm, tensor healing distance d_t = 3.43 fm, and quenching factor α = 0.8.
- The one-body density sum rule S₁ is satisfied to within less than 1% error in all cases (Jastrow, f⁴, f⁶).
- For the operatorial correlations (f⁶), the FHNC/SOC approximation violates the sum rules S₂ and S_τ by a maximum of about 9%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CBF studies of doubly closed shell nuclei with semirealistic central interactions (Co' et al., 1990s) to include spin, isospin, and tensor components in both the interaction and correlations. It builds on the FHNC/SOC formalism developed for nuclear matter by Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988) and adapts it to finite systems. The results are compared with variational Monte Carlo calculations for ¹⁶O by Pieper et al. (1992), providing a first check of the cluster summation method for state-dependent correlations in finite nuclei. This work enables future microscopic studies of medium-heavy nuclei with realistic Hamiltonians, though the spin-orbit and three-nucleon force components remain to be included.
