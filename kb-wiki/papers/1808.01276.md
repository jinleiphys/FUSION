# Assessing saturation physics explanations of collectivity in small collision systems with the IP-Jazma model

> Experimental measurements in collisions of small systems from p+p to p/d/3He+A at RHIC and the LHC reveal particle emission patterns that are strikingly similar to those observed in A+A collisions. One explanation of these patterns is the formation of small droplets of quark-gluon plasma followed by hydrodynamic evolution. A geometry engineering program was proposed [1] to investigate these emission patterns, and the experimental data from that program in p+Au, d+Au, 3He+Au collisions for elliptic and triangular anisotropy coefficients v2 and v3 follow the pattern predicted by hydrodynamic calculations [2]. One alternative approach, referred to as initial-state correlations, suggests that for small systems the patterns observed in the final-state hadrons are encoded at the earliest moments of the collision, and therefore require no final-state parton scattering or hydrodynamic evolution [3,4]. Recently, new calculations using only initial-state correlations, in the dilute-dense approximation of gluon saturation physics, reported striking agreement with the v2 patterns observed in p/d/3He+Au data at RHIC [5]. The reported results are counterintuitive and thus we aim here to reproduce some of the basic features of these calculations. In this first investigation, we provide a description of our model, IP-Jazma, and investigate its implications for saturation scales, multiplicity distributions and eccentricities, reserving for later work the analysis of momentum spectra and azimuthal anisotropies. We find that our implementation of the saturation physics model reproduces the results of the earlier calculation of the multiplicity distribution in d+Au collisions at RHIC. However, our investigations, together with existing data, call into question some of the essential elements reported in Ref. [5].

## Key claim

The paper argues that the MSTV (Mace, Skokov, Tribedy, Venugopalan) saturation-based explanation of collective flow in small collision systems, which claims to reconcile initial-state correlations with experimental data showing larger v2 in d+Au than p+Au, contains internal inconsistencies and questionable assumptions about saturation scales and the coherent interaction of color domains over large distances.

## Method

The authors developed the IP-Jazma model, an open-source implementation that incorporates the same initial physics steps as the MSTV calculation: Monte Carlo Glauber nucleon positions, Impact Parameter Saturation (IP-Sat) profiles for gluon saturation scales with Gaussian widths, and log-normal fluctuations in the saturation scale Qs0^2 on a nucleon-by-nucleon basis. They then computed saturation scale maps, multiplicity distributions, and eccentricities for p+Au and d+Au collisions at RHIC energy sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, comparing their results to the MSTV framework and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Average three-dimensional separation between proton and neutron in the deuteron: ⟨r⟩ = 3.33 fm; average transverse separation ⟨r_T⟩ = 2.61 fm from the Hulthén wavefunction
- For 0-5% highest multiplicity d+Au events, the average transverse separation ⟨r_T⟩ = 1.66 fm under N_coll scaling
- Saturation scale at the center of the proton at x=0.01: Q_s^2 = 0.67 GeV^2 (from Kowalski and Teaney); average over radius 0.65 fm gives Q_s^2 = 0.28 GeV^2
- MSTV uses B_G = 4.25 GeV^{-2} giving sigma = 0.40 fm for the Gaussian saturation profile; the authors find only 11% overlap between neutron and proton color fields with this value
- The log-normal fluctuation width w = 0.5 is used for Q_s fluctuations, identical to the value used for pp collisions at 13 TeV
- High-multiplicity d+Au events have dN_ch/dη ≈ 18, while p+Au events have dN_ch/dη ≈ 12

## Context

This work extends the debate between hydrodynamic explanations (Nagle 2013, Aidala 2018) and initial-state correlation models (Dusling 2012, Dusling 2013) for collective behavior in small collision systems. The authors directly challenge the MSTV calculation (Mace 2018) that claimed initial-state saturation physics could reproduce the v2 ordering in p/d/3He+Au collisions, finding that the required Q_s^2 values (approximately 2.25 GeV^2) are an order of magnitude larger than the IP-Sat value of 0.28 GeV^2 from Kowalski and Teaney (2003). The IP-Jazma code is released as open source to enable further scrutiny, and the authors reserve for later work the analysis of momentum spectra and azimuthal anisotropies.
