# Neutron-Rich Nuclei in Heaven and Earth

> An accurately calibrated relativistic parametrization is introduced to compute the ground state properties of finite nuclei, their linear response, and the structure of neutron stars. While similar in spirit to the successful NL3 parameter set, it produces an equation of state that is considerably softer -- both for symmetric nuclear matter and for the symmetry energy. This softening appears to be required for an accurate description of several collective modes having different neutron-to-proton ratios. Among the predictions of this model are a symmetric nuclear-matter incompressibility of K=230 MeV and a neutron skin thickness in 208Pb of Rn-Rp=0.21 fm. Further, the impact of such a softening on the properties of neutron stars is as follows: the model predicts a limiting neutron star mass of Mmax=1.72 Msun, a radius of R=12.66 km for a ``canonical'' M=1.4 Msun neutron star, and no (nucleon) direct Urca cooling in neutrons stars with masses below M=1.3 Msun.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new relativistic parametrization (FSUGold) that produces a softer equation of state than the successful NL3 set, which appears necessary to simultaneously describe the giant monopole resonance in both 90Zr and 208Pb and the isovector giant dipole resonance in 208Pb, but the authors note that the high-density component of the equation of state relevant to neutron stars remains largely unconstrained.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic effective field theory model with Yukawa couplings of the nucleon field to isoscalar-scalar, isoscalar-vector, isovector-vector, and photon fields, supplemented by four nonlinear meson interactions including a mixed isoscalar-isovector coupling. They calibrated the parameter set (FSUGold) to binding energies and charge radii of magic nuclei using standard least-squares fitting procedures, then computed ground-state properties, relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) results for collective modes, and neutron-star structure via integration of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Symmetric nuclear-matter incompressibility: K = 230 MeV for FSUGold (compared to 271 MeV for NL3)
- Neutron skin thickness in 208Pb: Rn - Rp = 0.21 fm for FSUGold (compared to 0.28 fm for NL3)
- Limiting neutron star mass: Mmax = 1.72 solar masses for FSUGold (compared to 2.78 solar masses for NL3)
- Radius of a canonical 1.4 solar-mass neutron star: R = 12.66 km for FSUGold (compared to 15.05 km for NL3)
- No direct Urca cooling in neutron stars with masses below M = 1.3 solar masses for FSUGold (compared to 0.84 solar masses for NL3)
- GMR centroid in 208Pb: 14.04 MeV for FSUGold (experiment: 14.17 +/- 0.28 MeV)
- GMR centroid in 90Zr: 17.98 MeV for FSUGold (experiment: 17.89 +/- 0.20 MeV)
- IVGDR peak energy in 208Pb: 13.07 MeV for FSUGold (experiment: 13.30 +/- 0.10 MeV)
- Transition density from nonuniform to uniform neutron-rich matter: 0.076 fm^-3 for FSUGold (compared to 0.052 fm^-3 for NL3)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic NL3 parametrization of Lalazissis, Konig, and Ring (1997) by adding two additional coupling constants to soften both the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state and the symmetry energy. The authors build on earlier proposals by Piekarewicz (2002, 2004) that the density dependence of the symmetry energy could resolve discrepancies between nonrelativistic models predicting K around 220-235 MeV and relativistic models predicting K around 250-270 MeV. The new parametrization enables predictions for neutron-star observables that can be tested by the Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory and by pulsar mass measurements such as those of PSRJ0751+1807 by Nice, Splaver, and Stairs (2004).
