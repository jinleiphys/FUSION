# Observational constraint from the heaviest pulsar PSR J0952-0607 on the equation of state of dense matter in relativistic mean field model

> In the present work, we constrain the equation of the state of dense matter in the context of heaviest observed neutron star mass M$_{max}$ = 2.35$\pm 0.17$ M$_{\odot}$ for the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607. We propose three interactions HPU1, HPU2 and HPU3 (named after Himachal Pradesh University)for the relativistic mean field model which include different combinations of non-linear, self and cross-couplings among isoscalar-scalar $σ$, isoscalar-vector $ω$ and isovector-vector $ρ$ meson fields up to the quartic order. These interactions are in harmony with the finite nuclei and bulk nuclear matter properties. The equations of state computed by using newly generated interactions for the $β$-equilibrated nucleonic matter satisfy the heaviest observed neutron star mass M$_{max}$ = 2.35$\pm 0.17$ M$_{\odot}$ for the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607. The results for the radius ($R_{1.4}$) and dimensionless tidal deformability (${Λ_{1.4}}$) corresponding to the canonical mass are also presented and agree well with the GW170817 event and astrophysical observations. The radius of $2.08M_{\odot}$ neutron star mass is predicted to be in the range $R_{2.08}$ = 12.98 -13.09 Km which also satisfies the NICER observations by Miller et al. (2021) and Riley et al.(2021). A covariance analysis is also performed to assess the theoretical uncertainties of model parameters and to determine their correlations with nuclear matter observables.

## Key claim

The paper proposes three new relativistic mean field (RMF) parameter sets (HPU1, HPU2, HPU3) that satisfy the maximum mass constraint M_max = 2.35 ± 0.17 M_⊙ from the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607, but the HPU3 set is the only one that also respects the naturalness condition from effective field theory by including all possible self and cross-couplings among meson fields up to quartic order.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared minimization using the simulated annealing method to fit RMF model parameters to experimental data including binding energies and charge radii of closed/open-shell nuclei, the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb from PREX-II, and the maximum neutron star mass constraint from PSR J0952-0607. Three parameter sets were generated with different combinations of nonlinear self-couplings (κ̄, λ̄ for σ meson; ζ for ω meson) and cross-couplings (Λ_v for ω²-ρ²; a₁, a₂ for σ-ω²; b₁, b₂ for σ-ρ²) up to quartic order. A covariance analysis was performed to assess theoretical uncertainties and correlations among model parameters and nuclear matter observables.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass constraint used: M_max = 2.35 ± 0.17 M_⊙ for PSR J0952-0607
- Predicted radius for 2.08 M_⊙ neutron star: R_2.08 = 12.98 - 13.09 km
- Saturation density ρ₀: 0.157 fm⁻³ (HPU1), 0.150 fm⁻³ (HPU2), 0.151 fm⁻³ (HPU3)
- Binding energy per nucleon E/A: -16.298 MeV (HPU1), -16.119 MeV (HPU2), -16.062 MeV (HPU3)
- Incompressibility K: 229.88 MeV (HPU1), 225.84 MeV (HPU2), 225.56 MeV (HPU3)
- Symmetry energy coefficient J: 34.34 MeV (HPU1), 33.21 MeV (HPU2), 33.22 MeV (HPU3)
- Slope of symmetry energy L: 61.21 MeV (HPU1), 63.87 MeV (HPU2), 75.03 MeV (HPU3)
- Neutron skin thickness for 208Pb: 0.216 fm (HPU1), 0.218 fm (HPU2), 0.217 fm (HPU3)
- Neutron skin thickness for 48Ca: 0.202 fm (HPU1), 0.201 fm (HPU2), 0.203 fm (HPU3)
- RMS error in binding energies: 3.06 MeV (HPU1), 1.81 MeV (HPU2), 2.35 MeV (HPU3)
- RMS error in charge radii: 0.050 fm (HPU1), 0.016 fm (HPU2), 0.017 fm (HPU3)

## Context

This work extends previous RMF parameterizations such as NL3 (Lalazissis 1997), Big Apple (Fattoyev 2020), and DOPS1 (Thakur 2022) by incorporating the most stringent mass constraint from PSR J0952-0607 (Romani 2022) alongside PREX-II neutron skin results (Adhikari 2021). The HPU3 parameter set is the first to simultaneously satisfy the heaviest pulsar mass constraint, finite nuclei properties, and the naturalness condition from effective field theory (Furnstahl 1997), enabling more reliable predictions for neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities that can be tested against future NICER and gravitational wave observations.
