# Light-by-Light Scattering at Next-to-Leading Order in QCD and QED

> The recent experimental observation of Light-by-Light (LbL) scattering at the Large Hadron Collider has revived interest in this fundamental process, and especially of the accurate prediction of its cross-section, which we present here for the first time at Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) in both QCD and QED. We compare two radically different computational approaches, both exact in the fermion mass dependence, thus offering a strong cross-check of our results. The first approach is a fully analytic method to calculate compact and well-organized two-loop helicity amplitudes. The second one is entirely numerical and leverages the Local Unitarity construction. Our two calculations agree with each other and conclude that including the exact fermion mass contribution typically increases the size of the NLO corrections. Moreover, we find that the exact result converges slowly to the massless limit of the high-energy regime, thus emphasizing the importance of including the full mass dependence at NLO. We also compare our results with the ATLAS measurement of LbL in ultra-peripheral lead-lead collisions, and find that the inclusion of exact NLO corrections reduces, but does not eliminate, the existing tension with theoretical predictions.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first fully differential cross-section for light-by-light scattering at next-to-leading order in both QCD and QED, retaining exact fermion mass dependence. The inclusion of exact NLO corrections reduces but does not eliminate the existing tension between theoretical predictions and the ATLAS measurement in ultra-peripheral lead-lead collisions.

## Method
The computation was performed using two independent approaches: a fully analytic method deriving compact two-loop helicity amplitudes via canonical differential equations and integration-by-parts reduction, and a fully numerical method using the Local Unitarity construction. Both approaches are exact in the fermion mass dependence and were cross-checked against each other. The partonic cross-sections were convolved with photon-photon fluxes for lead-lead ultra-peripheral collisions using the charge form factor available in the gamma-UPC event generator.

## Key numbers
- The NLO' QCD+QED cross-section within the ATLAS fiducial volume increases by 6.5% (+2.1%/-1.2%) relative to the LO cross-section of 76 nb.
- The final prediction is 81.2 (+1.6/-0.9) nb, which stands 1.8 sigma below the ATLAS measurement of 120 +/- 22 nb.
- The pure NLO QCD corrections change the LO cross-section by +6%, while pure NLO QED corrections change it by -0.1%.
- The relative difference between the exact computation and the high-energy approximation reaches below 2% when sqrt(s)/m_f > 37, and below 2% for the low-energy approximation when sqrt(s)/m_f < 0.52.
- The low-energy and high-energy approximations for the K-factors of the tau, charm quark and bottom quark contributions increase the LO cross-section by 13% and 0.7% respectively.
- The top quark mass is set to 173 GeV, the muon mass to 0.1134 GeV, the tau mass to 1.777 GeV, the charm mass to 1.5 GeV, the bottom mass to 4.75 GeV, the top mass to 172.69 GeV, and the W boson mass to 80.377 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the known massless limit calculations of Bern (2001) and Binoth (2002) for NLO QCD and QED corrections to light-by-light scattering, and the low-energy Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian results of Martin (2003). It provides the first exact mass-dependent NLO predictions, which are necessary because the ATLAS measurement (2020) covers di-photon invariant masses from a few GeV to tens of GeV where the massless limit does not apply to tau leptons and heavy-flavor quarks. The results enable more precise comparisons with experimental data and demonstrate that the Local Unitarity method can successfully compute previously unknown cross-sections, opening the door to alternative approaches for multi-loop computations.
