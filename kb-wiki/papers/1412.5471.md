# P- and T-Violating Lagrangians in Chiral Effective Field Theory and Nuclear Electric Dipole Moments

> A scheme to derive hadronic interactions induced by effective multi-quark terms is presented within the framework of chiral effective field theory. It is employed to work out the list of parity- and time-reversal-symmetry-violating hadronic interactions that are relevant for the computation of nuclear contributions to the electric dipole moments of the hydrogen-2, helium-3 and hydrogen-3 nuclei. We also derive the scattering and Faddeev equations required to compute electromagnetic form factors in general and electric dipole moments in particular.

## Key claim

The paper presents a scheme within chiral effective field theory to derive hadronic interactions induced by effective multi-quark terms, showing that any chiral structure induced by an arbitrary quark multilinear is already encountered in standard pion-sector and pion-nucleon χEFT Lagrangians, with the quark multilinear only shifting the order at which a particular chiral structure appears and introducing new independent low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors employ the Gasser-Leutwyler formulation of chiral effective field theory to derive P- and T-violating low-energy Lagrangians induced by the QCD θ-term and effective dimension-six sources (FQLR-term, qCEDM, qEDM, gCEDM, and 4q-term). They then derive scattering equations for two-nucleon systems and Faddeev equations for three-nucleon systems required to compute electromagnetic form factors and electric dipole moments of the deuteron, helion, and triton nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The current upper bound on the neutron EDM is |d_n| < 2.9 × 10⁻²⁶ e·cm (Baker, 2006)
- The current upper bound on the proton EDM is |d_p| < 7.9 × 10⁻²⁵ e·cm (Griffith, 2009)
- The current upper bound on the electron EDM is |d_e| < 8.7 × 10⁻²⁹ e·cm (Baron, 2013)
- The envisioned accuracy for direct EDM measurements of charged light nuclei at storage rings is ∼10⁻²⁹ e·cm
- The pion decay constant F_π = 92.2 MeV
- The average pion mass M_π = 138.01 MeV
- The average nucleon mass m_N = 938.92 MeV
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ ∼ 4πF_π ∼ 1.2 GeV
- The QCD scale Λ_QCD ∼ 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivations of P- and T-violating chiral Lagrangians by Mereghetti (2010) and de Vries (2010, 2012) by presenting them within the Gasser-Leutwyler formulation for the first time, which simplifies the derivation of higher-order terms and generalization to three-flavor SU(3) χEFT. The formalism enables computation of nuclear EDMs for light nuclei (deuteron, helion, triton) that can be compared with upcoming storage-ring experiments, and the results for these computations were previously presented in a companion paper (edmoln, 2014).
