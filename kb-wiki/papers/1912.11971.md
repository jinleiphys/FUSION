# The electromagnetic field effects in in-out and in-in formalisms

> In this work, we compare the effects of electromagnetic (EM) fields on strong coupling systems in two formalisms: in-out and in-in, which are different from each other when a finite electric field is present. The chiral effective Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model is adopted for this study, and two kinds of EM field distributions are considered: pure electric field and parallel EM (PEM) field with equal electric and magnetic components. For both distributions, we find that the results of in-out and in-in formalisms start to diverge when the electric field $(qE)^{1/2}$ is larger than the chiral effective mass $M=[m^2+(π^0)^2]^{1/2}$, that is, when the Schwinger pair production mechanism becomes important. Besides, the chiral restorations are stiffer in the in-in formalism, especially the transition shifts to first-order instead of the second-order for the PEM field. The neutral collective modes are also explored accordingly: For the PEM field, more precise calculations show nonmonotonic features of their pole masses due to parity mixing, and then the Goldstone-like mode is found to be noneffective at the end of chiral rotation because of chiral anomaly.

## Key claim

The in-out and in-in formalisms yield different predictions for chiral symmetry restoration and meson properties in strong electromagnetic fields, with the divergence becoming significant when the electric field strength $(qE)^{1/2}$ exceeds the chiral effective mass $M$, i.e., when Schwinger pair production becomes important; the in-in formalism produces stiffer chiral restoration and can change the order of the phase transition from second-order to first-order for parallel electromagnetic fields.

## Method

The one-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model was used to study strong coupling systems under two electromagnetic field configurations: pure electric field and parallel electromagnetic (PEM) field with equal electric and magnetic components. The gap equations for the chiral condensates (dynamical quark mass $m$ and neutral pion condensate $\pi^0$) were derived and solved in both the in-out (imaginary-time) and in-in (real-time) formalisms, with the latter including the feedback of Schwinger pair production through additional integral branches in the quark propagator. The polarization functions for neutral mesonic modes were computed in the random-phase approximation, and the pole masses of the mass eigenstates (Goldstone-like $\Pi^0$ and Higgs-like $\Sigma$) were obtained by solving $\mathcal{G}^{-1}_{\rm M}(q_0=m_{\rm M},{\bf q}=0)=0$ in the in-in formalism and $\mathcal{G}^{-1}_{\rm M}(q_4=im_{\rm M},{\bf q}=0)=0$ in the in-out formalism.

## Key numbers

- NJL model parameters: $G=9.86~{\rm GeV}^{-2}$, $\Lambda=0.653~{\rm GeV}$, $m_0=5~{\rm MeV}$, fitted to $m_\pi=134~{\rm MeV}$, $f_\pi=93~{\rm MeV}$, and $\langle\bar\psi\psi\rangle=-(0.25~{\rm GeV})^3$.
- For the PEM field in the in-out formalism, the critical electric field at the end of chiral rotation is $(eE_{c1})^{1/2}=0.217~{\rm GeV}$, where the phase transition is of very weak first-order rather than second-order.
- The in-in formalism yields a first-order chiral restoration for the PEM field, while the in-out formalism gives second-order.
- For pure electric field, the in-in formalism shows stiffer variations of $m$ and $m_\pi$ around the transition region compared to the in-out formalism; in the chiral limit, the restoration is second-order in the in-out formalism but first-order in the in-in formalism.
- The Schwinger pair production parameter is $S_E=\pi/|qE|$, and the feedback terms in the gap equations contain factors $e^{-M^2S_E}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry and meson properties under electromagnetic fields using the NJL model, specifically building on the author's prior work Cao (2015) and Wang (2018) which examined neutral pion condensation under PEM fields in the in-out formalism. The study is motivated by the discovery by Copinger and Fukushima (2018) that the in-in (real-time) propagator, rather than the in-out propagator, is necessary to correctly capture chiral anomaly dynamics in the small-mass limit. The results enable indirect detection of Schwinger pair production in heavy ion collisions by comparing mesonic properties predicted by the two formalisms, and they challenge the validity of the simpler in-out formalism for studying electric field effects in strong coupling systems.
