# Cluster properties in relativistic quantum mechanics of N-particle systems

> A general technique is presented for constructing a quantum theory of a finite number of interacting particles satisfying Poincaré invariance, cluster separability, and the spectral condition. Irreducible representations and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients of the Poincaré group are the central elements of the construction. A different realization of the dynamics is obtained for each basis of an irreducible representation of the Poincaré group. Unitary operators that relate the different realizations of the dynamis are constructed. This technique is distinguished from other solutions of this problem because it does not depend on the kinematic subgroups of Dirac's forms of dynamics. Special basis choices lead to kinematic subgroups.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general method for constructing a relativistic quantum theory of N interacting particles that satisfies Poincaré invariance, cluster separability, and the spectral condition, using irreducible representations and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients of the Poincaré group as central elements. This technique is distinguished from other solutions (Sokolov 1977, and the work in Dirac's forms of dynamics) because it does not depend on the kinematic subgroups of Dirac's forms of dynamics, though special basis choices can lead to kinematic subgroups.

## Method

The construction uses Wigner's irreducible representations of the inhomogeneous SL(2,C) group (the covering group of the Poincaré group) and their Clebsch-Gordan coefficients to build N-particle Hilbert spaces as tensor products of single-particle irreducible representation spaces. A recursive N-body construction is performed using Möbius and Zeta functions of the lattice of partitions (generalizing Ursell cumulant expansions), starting from the two-body solution, to ensure cluster separability. Unitary operators called scattering equivalences, which are elements of a C* algebra of asymptotic constants, are constructed to relate different realizations of the dynamics and redistribute interactions among the Poincaré generators while preserving the S-matrix and cluster properties.

## Key numbers

- The resulting dynamics has interactions in between three and ten of the Poincaré generators (the paper does not specify exact counts for specific cases).
- The invariant relative momentum operator \(\hat{q}^2\) for two-particle systems has absolutely continuous spectrum on \([0, \infty)\).
- The two independent invariant polynomial functions of the ISL(2,C) generators are the mass-squared operator \(\hat{M}^2 = -\hat{P}^\mu \hat{P}_\mu\) and the Pauli-Lubanski squared operator \(\hat{W}^2 = \hat{W}^\mu \hat{W}_\mu\).
- The spin-squared operator is defined as \(\hat{j}^2 := \hat{W}^2 / \hat{M}^2\) when the mass spectrum is positive.

## Context

This work extends the two-body construction of Polyzou (no year given) to the N-body problem and generalizes the earlier solutions of Sokolov (1977) and the work in Dirac's forms of dynamics (Dirac 1949, and subsequent papers by Coester 1965, and others). The construction is directly motivated by Wigner's 1939 paper on unitary representations of the Poincaré group. The framework enables applications to few-hadron and few-quark systems (referenced as works by Harry, Chung, Brad, Walter, Fuda, Salme, and Klink) that were previously limited to systems where cluster properties can be trivially realized, and provides a way to relate different dynamical models through unitary scattering equivalences.
