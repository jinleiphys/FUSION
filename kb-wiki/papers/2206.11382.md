# Shear-induced anomalous transport and charge asymmetry of triangular flow in heavy-ion collisions

> Chiral anomaly implies the existence of non-dissipative transport phenomena, such as the chiral magnetic effect. At second order in the derivative expansion, novel quantum transport phenomena emerge. In this paper, we focus on the anomalous transport driven by a combination of shear, vorticity and magnetic field. We find that the corresponding transport phenomena -- shear-induced chiral magnetic and chiral vortical effects (siCME and siCVE) -- induce characteristic charge correlations among the hadrons produced in heavy ion collisions. We propose the charge asymmetry of triangular flow as a signature of the anomalous transport, and estimate the strength of the signal, as well as the background, using hydrodynamical model simulations. We find that the signal-to-background ratio for the proposed observable is favorable for experimental detection.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that second-order anomalous transport phenomena, shear-induced chiral magnetic and chiral vortical effects (siCME and siCVE), produce a charge-dependent triangular flow asymmetry in heavy-ion collisions, and estimates that the signal-to-background ratio for this observable is favorable for experimental detection, though the magnitude of the signal is about two orders of magnitude smaller than for the conventional chiral magnetic effect.

## Method

The authors compute the second-order transport coefficients ξ₁ and ξ₂ for siCVE and siCME using chiral kinetic theory at weak coupling and holographic methods at strong coupling, then implement these effects in the AVFD (Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics) simulation framework on top of 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic backgrounds from MUSIC. They evaluate the charge asymmetry of triangular flow (a₃ harmonic) for pions and protons in √s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, and estimate the background from resonance decays of K⁰_S, ρ⁰, ω, K⁰_L, and η particles.

## Key numbers

- ξ₁ ≈ -0.05 μ_A μ / T at weak coupling (assuming η/s = 1/(4π)), with strong coupling estimate ξ₁ = -N_F N_c / (√3 π³) μ_A μ / T
- ξ₂ ≈ -0.53 μ_A / T at weak coupling (assuming η/s = 1/(4π)), with strong coupling estimate ξ₂ = -N_F N_c / (√3 π³) μ_A / T
- The a₃ charge separation for pions due to siCME is of order 10⁻³, increasing with transverse momentum p_T
- The signal Δγ₃^sgn ∼ 10⁻⁷, while the resonance decay background Δγ₃^bkg ∼ 10⁻⁸ (scaled by v₆,RP ∼ 10⁻⁴)
- The magnetic field lifetime used in simulations is τ_B = 1 fm, with initial axial charge condition |n_A/s| = 0.1 (equivalently |μ_A/T| ∼ 1)
- The kinematic cuts applied are 0.5 < p_T < 2 GeV and |η| < 0.5

## Context

This work extends the classification of second-order anomalous transport coefficients from Kharzeev (2011) by computing explicit values for the shear-induced chiral magnetic and chiral vortical coefficients and proposing experimental observables. It builds on the chiral kinetic theory framework of Son (2012) and Stephanov (2012), and uses the AVFD simulation approach developed by Jiang (2016) and Shi (2017, 2019). The results enable experimental searches for these effects at RHIC (including isobar collisions) and the LHC, and suggest connections to Λ hyperon polarization measurements at lower beam energies during the beam energy scan.
