# Deduction of the quantum numbers of low-lying states of 4-nucleon systems based on symmetry

> The inherent nodal structures of the wavefunctions of 4-nucleon systems have been investigated. The quantum numbers of the excited states (resonances) have been deduced. It was found that all the T=0 even-parity resonances are dominated by {2,1,1} component with L=1, all the T=0 odd-parity resonances are dominated by {3,1} component with L=1, and all the T=1 odd-parity resonances are also dominated by {3,1} component with L=1. The understanding of the inherent nodal structure is found to be crucial to a systematic understanding of the spectrum.

## Key claim

Based solely on symmetry and the inherent nodal structure of wavefunctions, without solving the Schrödinger equation or using any dynamical model, the quantum numbers of all low-lying T=0 and T=1 states of 4-nucleon systems can be deduced, provided the states are dominated by "inherent-nodeless" spatial components that are accessible at both the equilateral tetrahedron and square configurations.

## Method

The spatial wavefunction of a 4-nucleon system is expanded into components of definite permutation symmetry (λ). Constraints on these components are derived from the equivalence between spatial rotations/inversions and particle permutations at highly symmetric configurations (equilateral tetrahedron, square). These constraints form homogeneous linear algebraic equations depending on total orbital angular momentum L, parity Π, and symmetry λ. Solving these equations identifies which λ-components can be nonzero at those configurations (ETH-accessible and square-accessible); those that are both accessible are termed "inherent-nodeless" and are expected to dominate low-lying states. The quantum numbers of the low-lying states are then deduced by combining the available inherent-nodeless components with the allowed spin-isospin symmetries from Table 1.

## Key numbers

- Seven low-lying T=0 states are predicted: one \(0^{+}\) (L=0, ground state) and six states with L=1 (an even-parity multiplet \(0^{+}, 1^{+}, 2^{+}\) and an odd-parity multiplet \(0^{-}, 1^{-}, 2^{-}\)).
- All T=0 levels of \(^{4}\)He below 28.31 MeV (the 2n+2p threshold) from an R-matrix analysis [9] match the predicted \(J^{\Pi}\) values exactly.
- The weight of the 0-\(\hbar\Omega\) component in the T=0 \(0_{2}^{+}\) state is only 0.08, from a shell-model calculation [11].
- Four odd-parity T=1 states are predicted (from the (1,-1,{3,1}) inherent-nodeless component), and all have been found in \(^{4}\)H, \(^{4}\)He, and \(^{4}\)Li with exactly the predicted J and Π [9].

## Context

This work extends the concept of inherent nodal surfaces (INS) introduced in Bao's earlier work [5] to the 4-nucleon system, providing a symmetry-based explanation for the spectrum that the shell model [10] cannot fully account for (e.g., the \(0_{2}^{+}\) state at 20.21 MeV in \(^{4}\)He is lower than odd-parity states, contrary to shell-model expectations). The approach enables a unified understanding of spectra across different few-body systems, including 4-valence-electron atoms (where it predicts a \(^{3}P^{e}\) ground state, confirmed by data for C, N\(^{+}\), O\(^{++}\), etc.) and the \(^{16}\)O nucleus as a 4-alpha system [12]. The method can be generalized to other few-body systems by investigating INS around regular shapes such as the regular octahedron for 6-body systems [13].
