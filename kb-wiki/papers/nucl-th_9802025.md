# Final State Interactions in the Near-Threshold Production of Kaons from Proton-Proton Collisions

> We analyse the $pp \to p ΛK$ cross section recently measured at COSY arguing that the enhancement of the production cross section at energies close to the reaction threshold should be due to the $Λp$ final state interaction. We find that the experimental $Λp$ elastic scattering data as well as the predictions from the Jülich-Bonn model are in reasonable agreement with the new results on $K^+$-meson production. We propose to study directly the final state interaction by measurements of the cross section as a function of the hyperon momentum in the $Λp$ cm system.

# Final State Interactions in the Near-Threshold Production of Kaons from Proton-Proton Collisions

## Key claim
The enhancement of the $pp \to p \Lambda K^+$ cross section at excess energies below 7 MeV, which is twice as large as predicted by earlier models, is due to the $\Lambda p$ final state interaction rather than deficiencies in the production amplitude. The effect is less pronounced than in the $pp \to pp \eta$ reaction because the $\Lambda N$ interaction is weaker than the $pp$ interaction at low relative momenta.

## Method
The authors extracted the average reaction amplitude $|M|$ from experimental $pp \to p \Lambda K^+$ cross section data by comparing measured cross sections with the phase-space integral expression. They then factorized the total amplitude into a production amplitude (assumed constant near threshold based on the One-Boson-Exchange model with pion and kaon exchanges) and a final state interaction amplitude. The FSI amplitude was related to the on-shell $\Lambda p$ elastic scattering amplitude, which was parameterized using experimental data and the Bonn-Julich hyperon-nucleon interaction model in the effective-range approximation.

## Key numbers
- The experimental $K^+$ yield at excess energies $\epsilon < 7$ MeV is twice as large as predicted by the OBE model of Sibirtsev and Cassing (1997) and by Faldt and Wilkin (1997), while both models reproduce data for $\epsilon > 50$ MeV.
- The FSI correction factor from Faldt and Wilkin (1997) at $\epsilon = 2$ MeV is approximately 14.
- The $\Lambda p$ scattering amplitude was fitted with parameters $\alpha = 170$ MeV, $\beta = 130$ MeV, and $C = 87$ in the parameterization $|A_{el}| = C [1 + \alpha\beta/(q_{\Lambda}^2 + \alpha^2/4)]$.
- The production amplitude was set to $|M_{prod}| = 1.05$ fm.
- The $\Lambda p$ scattering amplitude is needed for relative momenta $0 \leq q_{\Lambda} \leq 400$ MeV/c for excess energies $\epsilon < 100$ MeV.
- The $\pi^0 p \to \Lambda K^+$ production amplitude from Tsushima et al. (1997) underestimates the COSY data by approximately a factor of 10 near threshold.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Sibirtsev and Cassing (1997), who calculated the $pp \to p \Lambda K^+$ cross section within the One-Boson-Exchange model but neglected $\Lambda p$ final state interactions due to uncertainty in low-energy $\Lambda p$ scattering data. It also addresses the conflicting results between Faldt and Wilkin (1997), who used only one-pion exchange with an effective-range FSI approximation, and Tsushima et al. (1997), whose microscopic model underestimated the COSY data. The paper enables direct experimental study of the $\Lambda p$ FSI by measuring the differential cross section as a function of the hyperon momentum $q_{\Lambda}$ in the $\Lambda p$ center-of-mass system, and calls for further calculations on the hyperon-nucleon interaction under study at Julich (Haidenbauer, 1998).
