# In-medium Electromagnetic Form Factors and Spin Polarizations

> We formulate the coupling between fermion spin and background electromagnetic fields using form factors. We show that the vacuum form factors at tree level reproduce the spin polarization effects found in chiral kinetic theory. The vacuum form factors corresponding to spin couplings to perpendicular electric field, parallel and perpendicular magnetic field are degenerate. The degeneracy is expected to be lifted in medium. As an example, we calculate the in-medium QCD radiative correction to the form factors at one-loop order, where we find partial lift of the degeneracy: the spin couplings to parallel and perpendicular magnetic field are different, but the spin couplings to perpendicular electric and parallel magnetic field remain the same.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the spin polarization effects found in chiral kinetic theory (CKT) can be reproduced by a field theory calculation of correlation functions in background electromagnetic fields using vacuum form factors at tree level, but that in-medium QCD radiative corrections at one-loop order only partially lift the degeneracy among the three form factors: the spin couplings to parallel and perpendicular magnetic fields become different, while the spin couplings to perpendicular electric and parallel magnetic fields remain the same.

## Method

The authors formulate the coupling between fermion spin and background electromagnetic fields using form factors, first reproducing the CKT results by calculating fermion correlation functions in background EM fields at tree level in an equilibrium medium using the Schwinger-Keldysh real-time formalism and the KMS relation. They then parameterize the vertex function using three dimensionless in-medium electromagnetic form factors (F0, F1, F2) corresponding to spin Hall effect, spin-perpendicular magnetic coupling, and spin-parallel magnetic coupling respectively, and evaluate the in-medium QCD radiative corrections to these form factors at one-loop order for massless Weyl fermions in a QGP medium.

## Key numbers

* The tree-level vacuum form factors are all degenerate: F0 = F1 = F2 = 1, reproducing the spin polarization effects found in chiral kinetic theory.
* The in-medium QCD radiative correction at one-loop order yields partial lift of degeneracy: F1 and F2 become different (spin couplings to parallel and perpendicular magnetic fields differ), while F0 and F1 remain the same (spin couplings to perpendicular electric and parallel magnetic fields remain equal).
* The kinematic conditions for the scattering interpretation require K·Q = 0 and Q·n = 0, where Q is the momentum inflow from the EM field and n is the frame vector.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of spin polarization phenomena in relativistic heavy ion collisions beyond the weakly coupled medium assumption that underlies chiral kinetic theory (CKT) as reviewed by Hidaka (2022). The form factor formulation connects to prior work on spin-vorticity coupling by Buzzegoli (2021) and Lin (2023), and to spin-magnetic coupling and spin Hall effect studied by Liu (2020) and Mameda (2022). The results enable a non-perturbative description of spin polarization in quark-gluon plasma where the weak coupling assumption is not well justified, and provide a framework for phenomenological studies of spin polarization in heavy ion collisions.
