# Determining all thermodynamic transport coefficients for an interacting large N quantum field theory

> Thermodynamic transport coefficients can be calculated directly from quantum field theory without requiring analytic continuation to real time. We determine all second-order thermodynamic transport coefficients for the uncharged N-component massless (critical) scalar field theory with quartic interaction in the large N limit, for any value of the coupling. We find that in the large N limit, all thermodynamic transport coefficients for the interacting theory can be expressed analytically in terms of the in-medium mass and sums over modified Bessel functions. We expect our technique to allow a similar determination of all thermodynamic transport coefficients for all theories that are solvable in the large N limit, including certain gauge theories.

## Key claim

The paper claims to determine *all* second-order thermodynamic transport coefficients for the uncharged N-component massless scalar field theory with quartic interaction in the large N limit for any value of the coupling, but this result is limited to the effective field theory regime where energy scales are much below the Landau pole of the theory.

## Method

The authors calculate Euclidean two-point correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor for the O(N) model in the large N limit using the R2 resummation scheme, which involves a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and a saddle-point evaluation of the path integral. From these correlators, they extract the three thermodynamic susceptibilities f1, f2, f3 defined by Kovtun and Shukla (2018) via Kubo-like formulas that require no analytic continuation to real time. The in-medium mass m is determined self-consistently from a gap equation, and all results are expressed analytically in terms of sums over modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- f1 = 13Nm^2/(5040π^2) for the interacting theory, where m is the in-medium mass
- f3 = -11Nm^2/(6720π^2) - (Nm^2/(48π^2)) Σ_{n=1}^∞ K_2(nβm), where K_2 is a modified Bessel function
- In the weak coupling or small temperature limit (βm ≪ 1), f1 = f2 = 0 and f3 = -NT^2/144, matching the free scalar result from Moore (2012) and Kovtun and Shukla (2018)
- The speed of sound squared c_s^2 is given by an expression involving sums of K_2 and K_3 Bessel functions, and satisfies c_s^2 ≥ 1/3 for all temperatures

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of thermodynamic transport coefficients for free theories by Moore (2012) and Kovtun and Shukla (2018) to an interacting quantum field theory at intermediate coupling, filling a gap in the literature. The results enable testing of conjectured relations between thermodynamic and real-time transport coefficients, such as the possible equality f1 = κ* - κ = 2λ1, and allow evaluation of the combination c_2 which the authors find to be always negative for the interacting O(N) model, suggesting an inflationary cosmology scenario without a cosmological constant as discussed by Baier (2019).
