# Discrete and continuum spectra in the unified shell model approach

> A new version of the nuclear shell model unifies the consideration of the discrete spectrum, where the results agree with the standard shell model, and continuum. The ingredients of the method are the non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian, energy-dependent one-body and two-body decay amplitudes, and self-consistent treatment of thresholds. The results for helium and oxygen isotope chains well reproduce the data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a version of the continuum shell model (CSM) that unifies discrete and continuum nuclear spectra using a non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian, claiming that below thresholds the same bound states as the standard shell model are obtained assuming the principal-value term Δ(E) is included into renormalization of the shell-model interaction.

## Method

The authors formulate a Feshbach projection method where the effective Hamiltonian in the internal P-space includes an energy-dependent principal-value term Δ(E) from virtual excitations into the Q-space and a non-Hermitian term W(E) representing on-shell decays into open channels. One-body decay amplitudes are computed from single-particle scattering in a Woods-Saxon potential, while two-body decays are treated via sequential processes through intermediate N-1 states and direct pair emission with total angular momentum L=0. The method is applied to helium isotopes (using p-shell orbitals) and oxygen isotopes (using the full sd-shell with USD and HBUSD interactions), with self-consistent treatment of thresholds across isotope chains.

## Key numbers

- For helium isotopes, the single-particle decay width parameterization gives γ₃/₂(ε) = 0.608 ε^(3/2) MeV and γ₁/₂(ε) = 0.3652 ε^(3/2) MeV for p₃/₂ and p₁/₂ states respectively
- The direct two-body amplitude for L=0 pair emission is parameterized as a^(L=0)(ε₁, ε₂) = (ε₁+ε₂)/(3√(2π)), with the numerical constant adjusted to two-body decays of ⁶He
- For ⁶He, the 2⁺ state at 0.529 MeV (CSM) has width 0.248 MeV compared to experimental 0.113 MeV; the 0⁺₂ state at 10.803 MeV has width 12.303 MeV compared to experimental 12.1 MeV
- For ⁷He, the 3/2⁻ ground state at -1.016 MeV (CSM) has width 0.046 MeV compared to experimental 0.15 MeV; the 5/2⁻ state at 2.911 MeV has width 0.944 MeV compared to experimental 1.99 MeV
- For ⁸He, the 2⁺ state at 0.19 MeV (CSM) has width 0.53 MeV; the 0⁺₂ state at 6.489 MeV has width 15.449 MeV
- For ⁹He, the 1/2⁻ ground state at -1.992 MeV (CSM) has width 0.634 MeV
- For ¹⁰He, the 0⁺ ground state at -1.649 MeV (CSM) has width 0.746 MeV

## Context

This work extends the unified approach to nuclear reactions formulated in Mahaux and Weidenmuller (1969) and builds on recent practical continuum shell model methods by Bennaceur et al. (1999), Betan et al. (2002), and Michel et al. (2002, 2003). The results for helium isotopes agree with calculations by Michel et al. (2002, 2003) using a similar mean field and schematic residual force. The method enables parameter-free calculations of resonance properties across isotope chains, with the oxygen isotope calculations using the standard USD interaction from Wildenthal (1984) and HBUSD interaction from Brown and Richter (2004).
