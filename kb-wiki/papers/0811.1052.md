# Toroidal Super-Heavy Nuclei in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Approach

> Within the self-consistent constraint Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS model (SHF+BCS), we found equilibrium toroidal nuclear density distributions in the region of super-heavy elements. For nuclei with a sufficient oblate deformation ($Q_{20}\leq$ -200 b), it becomes energetically favourable to change the genus of nuclear surface from 0 to 1, i.e., to switch the shape from a biconcave disc to a torus. The energy of the toroidal (genus=1) SHF+BCS solution relative to the compact (genus=0) ground state energy is strongly dependent both on the atomic number Z and the mass number A. We discuss the region of Z and A where the toroidal SHF+BCS total energy begins to be a global minimum.

# Toroidal Super-Heavy Nuclei in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Approach

## Key claim

Within the self-consistent constraint Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS model (SHF+BCS), equilibrium toroidal nuclear density distributions exist in the super-heavy element region, but only for nuclei with sufficient oblate deformation (Q20 ≤ -200 b) where it becomes energetically favorable to change the nuclear surface genus from 0 to 1. The toroidal solution becomes a global minimum only for specific combinations of atomic number Z and mass number A, with the authors identifying Z=138 and A=364 as one such case.

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS equations using the HFODD code with a three-dimensional Cartesian deformed harmonic oscillator basis expansion, employing the Skyrme SkM* force. They calculated total binding energy as a function of quadrupole moment Q20 for super-heavy nuclei with Z between 114 and 138, comparing compact (genus=0) and toroidal (genus=1) self-consistent solutions. The calculations were not restricted to axial symmetry, allowing the toroidal density distributions to show characteristic sausage deformations.

## Key numbers

- For nuclei with Q20 ≤ -200 b, it becomes energetically favorable to change the nuclear surface genus from 0 to 1
- For Q20 < -250 b, only genus=1 self-consistent solutions exist
- For 316122 and 340130, toroidal solutions cease to exist at Q20 ≈ -450 b due to sausage instability
- For Z=138 and A=364, the toroidal equilibrium becomes the global minimum
- Neutron Fermi energies oscillate around -6 MeV across quadrupole deformations for all studied nuclei
- Proton Fermi energies decrease to the value of neutron Fermi energies at Q20 ≈ -400 b in the oblate deformation region

## Context

This work extends earlier liquid drop model studies of toroidal nuclei by Wong (1972, 1973, 1979) and more recent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Warda (2007) with the Gogny D1S force, as well as semiclassical extended Thomas-Fermi calculations by Vinas (2008) with the Skyrme SkM* force. The authors aim to quantitatively examine the role of shell effects in toroidal nuclei, which were not included in classical LDM and semiclassical ETF approaches. The results enable further investigation of toroidal super-heavy nuclei as a regular characteristic of strongly oblate deformed heavy and super-heavy systems, rather than an exceptional phenomenon.
