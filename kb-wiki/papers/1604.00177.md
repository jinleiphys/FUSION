# Mechanisms of the isospin-breaking decay $f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)π^0\toπ^+π^-π^0$

> Estimated are the contributions of the following mechanisms responsible for the decay $f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)π^0\toπ^+π^-π^0$: 1) the contribution of the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing, $f_1(1285)\to a_0(980)π^0\to (K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)π^0\to f_0(980)π^0\toπ^+π^-π^0$, 2) the contribution of the transition $f_1(1285)\to(K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)π^0\to f_0 (980)π^0\toπ^+π^-π^0$, arising due to the pointlike decay $f_1 (1285)\to K\bar Kπ^0$, 3) the contribution of the transition $f_1(1285)\to(K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K)\to(K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)π^0\to f_0(980)π^0\to π^+π^-π^0$, where $K^*=K^*(892)$, and 4) the contribution of the transition $f_1(1285)\to(K^*_0\bar K+\bar K^*_0K)\to(K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)π^0\to f_0(980)\toπ^+ π^-π^0$, where $K^*_0=K^*_0(800)$ (or $κ$) and $K^*_0(1430)$. These mechanisms break the conservation of the isospin due to the nonzero mass difference of the $K^+$ and $K^0$ mesons. They result in the appearance of the narrow resonance structure in the $π^+π^-$ mass spectrum in the region of the $K\bar K$ thresholds, with the width $\approx2m_{K^0}-2m_{K^+}\approx8$ MeV. The observation of such a structure in experiment is the direct indication on the $K\bar K$ loop mechanism of the breaking of the isotopic invariance. We point out that existing data should be more precise, and it is difficult to explain them using the single specific mechanism from those listed above. Taking the decay $f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)π^0 \toπ^+π^-π^0$ as the example, we discuss the general approach to the description of the $K\bar K$ loop mechanism of the breaking of isotopic invariance.

## Key claim

The isospin-breaking decay \(f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\) can proceed through four distinct \(K\bar K\) loop mechanisms, but existing experimental data cannot be explained by any single one of these mechanisms alone; the data require more precise measurements and likely involve a combination of contributions.

## Method

The authors calculated the contributions of four mechanisms that break isospin via the charged/neutral kaon mass difference: (1) \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing through \(K\bar K\) loops; (2) a pointlike \(f_1(1285)\to K\bar K\pi^0\) decay followed by \(K\bar K\to f_0(980)\) rescattering; (3) intermediate \(K^*(892)\bar K + \bar K^*(892)K\) states decaying to \(K\bar K\pi^0\); and (4) intermediate scalar \(K^*_0(800)\) (or \(\kappa\)) and \(K^*_0(1430)\) states decaying to \(K\bar K\pi^0\). They used coupling constants extracted from BESIII data on \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing intensities \(\xi_{fa}\) and \(\xi_{af}\) to evaluate the branching fractions for each mechanism.

## Key numbers

- The width of the narrow resonance structure in the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) mass spectrum near the \(K\bar K\) thresholds is \(\approx 2m_{K^0}-2m_{K^+}\approx 8\) MeV.
- The ratio \(BR(f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)/BR(f_1(1285)\to a^0_0(980)\pi^0\to\eta\pi^0\pi^0)\) from VES data is \((2.5\pm0.9)\%\).
- The same ratio from BESIII data on reaction (f) is \((3.6\pm1.4)\%\), and from BESIII data on reaction (d) is \((1.3\pm0.7)\%\).
- The \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing mechanism alone yields a ratio of approximately \(0.29\%\), an order of magnitude below the VES central value.
- The pointlike \(f_1(1285)\to K\bar K\pi^0\) mechanism gives \(\Gamma_{f_1\to(K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)\pi^0\to f_0\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0}/\Gamma_{f_1\to K\bar K\pi} = 0.224\times10^{-2}\), about 15 times lower than the experimental value of \(0.033\pm0.010\).
- The coupling constants extracted from mixing data are: \(g^2_{f_0\pi\pi}/(16\pi)=0.098\) GeV\(^2\), \(g^2_{f_0 K\bar K}/(16\pi)=0.4\) GeV\(^2\), \(g^2_{a^0_0\eta\pi^0}/(16\pi)=0.2\) GeV\(^2\), \(g^2_{a^0_0 K\bar K}/(16\pi)=0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The "mass" of the \(a_0(980)-f_0(980)\) transition at the \(K\bar K\) threshold is \(|M_{a^0_0f_0}(4m_K^2)|\approx 28\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing originally proposed by Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov (1979) and applies it to the specific decay \(f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\). It addresses recent experimental observations from VES (Doronin et al., 2008, 2011) and BESIII (Ablikim et al., 2011-2013) that show unexpectedly large isospin-breaking effects in this decay channel. The analysis enables future experimental studies to disentangle the relative contributions of different \(K\bar K\) loop mechanisms and to use polarization and interference measurements to probe the phase variation of the mixing amplitude.
