# QCD antenna radiative spectrum in dense media within the Improved Opacity Expansion

> We compute the double differential inclusive spectrum for the emission of a soft gluon from a color-singlet $q\bar q$ pair traversing a dense QCD medium. Our results extend the existing literature by simultaneously incorporating both single hard and multiple soft gluon exchanges between the jet and the medium -- an essential ingredient for a complete phenomenological description of jet quenching. Using the Improved Opacity Expansion framework, we provide an analytically tractable treatment, reducing the full cross-section to a set of simple expressions. Our analysis demonstrates that rare hard (Molière) scatterings significantly modify the gluon spectrum at large angles, accelerating the loss of color coherence between the initial quarks. We further quantify whether the modifications are driven by an overall weakening of the interference term, or by more detailed modifications to the fragmentation pattern. Our results provide a direct input for phenomenological jet quenching studies, offering new insights into the role of color decoherence in QCD matter.

## Key claim

The authors compute the double differential inclusive spectrum for soft gluon emission from a color-singlet quark-antiquark pair in a dense QCD medium, extending prior work by simultaneously incorporating both single hard (Moliere) and multiple soft gluon exchanges with the medium. The key hedge is that the Improved Opacity Expansion framework provides an analytically tractable treatment only for soft gluons (omega much less than the quark energy) and for a static, homogeneous, and isotropic medium, with the gluon treated in the eikonal approximation.

## Method

The authors start from the general form of the in-medium gluon radiation spectrum off a color-singlet q-qbar antenna, which splits into two direct terms (R_q, R_qbar) and an interference term (J). They apply the Improved Opacity Expansion (IOE) framework, which separates the Gyulassy-Wang dipole potential into a leading-order harmonic oscillator term and a next-to-leading-order logarithmic correction, using a matching scale Q_b defined by the transcendental equation Q_b^2 = qhat_0 L log(Q_b^2 / mu_*^2). The broadening kernel and the emission kernel (solution to a 2+1D Schrodinger equation) are then expanded perturbatively in the small parameter lambda = qhat_0 / qhat, allowing analytic evaluation of the spectrum up to NLO. The interference term J, which contains the decoherence factor (1 - Delta_med), is computed for the first time within this framework.

## Key numbers

* The bare jet quenching parameter qhat_0 = 4 pi alpha_s^2 C_R n(t) quantifies the mean transverse momentum accumulated per mean free path by a colored projectile.
* The Gyulassy-Wang screening mass is mu = g T, with T the medium temperature.
* The decoherence factor in the harmonic approximation is Delta_med(t) = 1 - exp[ -(1/12) Q_s0^2 r_perp^2 (t/L)^3 ], where Q_s0^2 = qhat_0 L is the bare saturation scale and r_perp = theta_qqbar L is the maximum dipole size.
* The expansion parameter lambda = qhat_0 / qhat = 1 / log(Q_b^2 / mu_*^2) is required to be much less than 1 for the IOE to be valid.
* The universal mass mu_*^2 = (1/4) e^{-1+2 gamma_E} mu^2 is approximately 0.292 mu^2.

## Context

This work extends the prior calculations of the in-medium antenna spectrum by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2012), which used the harmonic/diffusion approximation (multiple soft scattering only), and by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2011) for dilute matter. It applies the Improved Opacity Expansion framework developed by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2019) and Barata et al. (2020, 2021) to the interference term for the first time. The results provide direct input for phenomenological jet quenching studies and offer new insights into the role of color decoherence in QCD matter, particularly showing that rare hard (Moliere) scatterings significantly modify the gluon spectrum at large angles and accelerate the loss of color coherence between the initial quarks.
