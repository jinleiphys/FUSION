# The Lifshitz Regime and its Experimental Signals

> We discuss the possibility of a Lifshitz regime, where the dispersion relation for Goldstone bosons and related fields has a minimum at nonzero momenta. Studies with the Functional Renormalization Group suggest that this occurs over a wide region in the plane of temperature and baryon chemical potential. Conversely, the FRG finds that the region in which fluctuations from a critical endpoint are significant is rather small. We suggest that this is due generically to the narrowness of the tricritical region in the chiral limit. Even if particles are produced in thermal equilibrium, a dispersion relation which is non-monotonic in momenta produces what appears to be non-thermal behavior.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a Lifshitz regime, where the wave function renormalization \(Z\) becomes negative and the dispersion relation for Goldstone bosons has a minimum at nonzero momentum, can produce experimentally observable signals over a much larger region of the QCD phase diagram than the narrow critical region surrounding a critical endpoint, even without the formation of spatially inhomogeneous condensates.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for a scalar field with standard kinetic and potential terms plus higher-derivative stabilizing terms, allowing the wave function renormalization \(Z\) to become negative. They analyze the dispersion relation for fluctuations about a chiral spiral solution and use results from Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) studies by Fu, Pawlowski, and Rennecke (2019) to map the region where \(Z<0\) in the temperature-chemical potential plane. They then compute the Bose-Einstein distribution for a representative non-monotonic dispersion relation to illustrate possible experimental signatures.

## Key numbers

- The critical endpoint is located at \((\mu_{cr}, T_{cr})\); the FRG calculation applies only for \(\mu < \mu_{cr}\) and \(T > T_{cr}\).
- In the extreme example dispersion relation \(E_{\rm Lifshitz}(\vec{k}) = (\vec{k}^2 - k_0^2)^2/(2M^2) + m^2\), the parameters are taken as \(M = k_0 = 10\,m\).
- The Bose-Einstein distribution for this dispersion relation peaks at nonzero momentum approximately \(k_0\), not at zero momentum.

## Context

This work extends the possibility of a Lifshitz regime in the QCD phase diagram beyond the standard picture of a critical endpoint, which was developed by Asakawa (1989) and Stephanov (1998). The authors contrast the narrow critical region found by FRG studies (Fu, Pawlowski, and Rennecke, 2019) with the much larger region where \(Z<0\), and note that the narrowness of the tricritical region in the chiral limit (Schaefer, 2006) generically suppresses critical endpoint fluctuations. The proposed signals, oscillatory spatial correlation functions and non-thermal momentum distributions, could be tested against thermal model fits such as those by Andronic (2017).
