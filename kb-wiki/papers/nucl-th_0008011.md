# The role of $σ$-meson in $ω\to ππγ$ decays and the coupling constant $g_{ωσγ}$

> We study the $ω\toππγ$ decays by adding to the amplitude calculated within the framework of chiral perturbation theory and vector meson dominance the amplitude of $σ$-meson intermediate state. We estimate the coupling constant $g_{ωσγ}$ utilizing the experimental value of the $ω\toπ^{0}π^{0}γ$ decay rate.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between the experimental decay rate of ω→π⁰π⁰γ (0.61±0.22 KeV) and the theoretical result from chiral perturbation theory plus vector meson dominance (VDM) (approximately 227–344 eV) can be resolved by including a σ-meson intermediate state amplitude, provided the σ-meson parameters are taken from experiment.

## Method

The authors calculate the ω→ππγ decay amplitude by adding a σ-meson pole amplitude to the standard VDM amplitude (ω→ρπ→ππγ) and the chiral loop contribution. They use effective Lagrangians for the ωρπ, ρπγ, σππ, and ωσγ vertices, with coupling constants g_{ωρπ}=15 GeV⁻¹ and g_{ρπγ}²=0.485 extracted from ρ⁰→π⁰γ data. The σ-meson propagator uses a Breit-Wigner form with mass M_σ and width Γ_σ taken from the f₀(400–1200) candidate and from CLEO and Fermilab E791 experiments. The coupling constant g_{ωσγ} is estimated by fitting the experimental ω→π⁰π⁰γ decay rate, then used to predict the ω→π⁺π⁻γ decay rate.

## Key numbers

- Experimental branching ratio Br(ω→π⁰π⁰γ) = (7.2±2.5)×10⁻⁵, corresponding to decay rate Γ(ω→π⁰π⁰γ) = (0.61±0.22) KeV
- Experimental upper limit Br(ω→π⁺π⁻γ) < 3.6×10⁻³, corresponding to Γ(ω→π⁺π⁻γ) < 31 KeV
- ω full width = (8.41±0.09) MeV
- For σ parameters M_σ=478 MeV, Γ_σ=324 MeV (Fermilab E791): g_{ωσγ}=0.13 (positive) or g_{ωσγ}=-0.27 (negative); g_{σππ}=5.29
- For these parameters, VDM contribution Γ_VDM=291 eV, σ contribution Γ_σ=156 eV (positive g) or 674 eV (negative g), interference term +167 eV (positive g) or -347 eV (negative g)
- Predicted branching ratio Br(ω→π⁺π⁻γ)=14.6×10⁻⁵ for both signs of g_{ωσγ} using M_σ=478 MeV, Γ_σ=324 MeV
- For M_σ=500 MeV, Γ_σ=600 MeV: g_{ωσγ} interval from ω→π⁺π⁻γ is -1.73 < g_{ωσγ} < 1.58

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of σ-meson roles in ρ⁰→π⁺π⁻γ and ρ⁰→π⁰π⁰γ decays (Gokalp and Yilmaz, 2000) to the ω→ππγ channels. It addresses the discrepancy between the experimental ω→π⁰π⁰γ rate and earlier VDM-only calculations by Bramon et al. (1992) (235 eV), Guetta and Singer (2000) (344±85 eV), and Fajfer and Oakes (1990) (690 eV), as well as the chiral perturbation theory result where K-loop contributions give only 1.8 eV. The extracted coupling constant g_{ωσγ} is needed for meson-exchange models of ω-meson photoproduction on nucleons near threshold, as studied by Titov et al. (1999) and Oh et al. (2000).
