# $Λ_c N$ interaction in leading order covariant chiral effective field theory

> We study the $Λ_c N$ interaction in the covariant chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) at leading order. All the relevant low-energy constants are determined by fitting to the lattice QCD simulations from the HAL QCD Collaboration. Extrapolating the results to the physical point, we show that the $Λ_c N$ interaction is weakly attractive in the $^1S_0$ channel, but in the $^3S_1$ channel, it is only attractive at extremely low energies and soon turns repulsive for larger laboratory energy. Furthermore, we show that the neglect of the $^3S_1-{}^3D_1$ coupling provided by the leading order covariant ChEFT would result in an attractive interaction in the $^3S_1$ channel at the physical point, which coincides with the previous non-relatistic ChEFT study. As a byproduct, we predict the $^3D_1$ phase shifts and the mixing angel $\varepsilon_1$, which can be checked by future lattice QCD simulations. In addition, we compare the $Λ_c N$ interaction with the $ΛN$ and $NN$ interactions to study how the baryon-nucleon ($BN$) interactions evolve as a function of the baryon mass with the replacement of a light quark by a strange or charm quark in the baryon ($B$).

# ΛcN interaction in leading order covariant chiral effective field theory

## Key claim

The ΛcN interaction is weakly attractive in the ¹S₀ channel at the physical point, but in the ³S₁ channel it is only attractive at extremely low energies and soon turns repulsive for larger laboratory energy; this repulsive behavior in the ³S₁ channel originates from the coupling of ³S₁ and ³D₁, and neglecting this coupling would yield an attractive interaction consistent with previous non-relativistic chiral effective field theory studies.

## Method

The authors employed leading order covariant chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) to describe the ΛcN interaction, constructing potentials from non-derivative four-baryon contact terms and one-meson-exchange contributions. The four low-energy constants (LECs) in the contact potential were determined by fitting to lattice QCD simulations from the HAL QCD Collaboration for pion masses mπ = 410 MeV and 570 MeV, with center-of-mass energies up to 30 MeV. The coupled-channel Kadyshevsky equation was solved to obtain scattering amplitudes, and results were extrapolated to the physical point (mπ = 138 MeV) using a cutoff range of ΛF = 600-700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- ΛcN ¹S₀ scattering length at physical point: a = -1.16 fm (covariant ChEFT, LO)
- ΛcN ³S₁ scattering length at physical point: a = -0.52 fm (covariant ChEFT, LO)
- For comparison, NN ¹S₀ scattering length: a = -21.3 fm (covariant ChEFT, LO); experimental value: -23.7 fm
- For comparison, NN ³S₁ scattering length: a = 5.75 fm (covariant ChEFT, LO); experimental value: 5.42 fm
- Lattice QCD baryon masses used: for mπ = 410 MeV, mN = 1215 MeV, mΛc = 2434 MeV, mΣc = 2575 MeV; for mπ = 570 MeV, mN = 1399 MeV, mΛc = 2555 MeV, mΣc = 2674 MeV
- Meson decay constant values: f0 = 93 MeV for mπ = 138 MeV, f0 = 112 MeV for mπ = 410 MeV, f0 = 129 MeV for mπ = 570 MeV
- Axial coupling constants fixed as: gA^NN = 1.27, gA^ΣcΣc = 0.71, gA^ΛcΣc = 0.74

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon-nucleon interactions using covariant ChEFT, which had been successfully applied to strangeness S = -1 and S = -2 hyperon-nucleon systems by Li (2016), Song (2018), Ren (2018), and Li (2018). It directly addresses the lattice QCD simulations of the ΛcN interaction performed by the HAL QCD Collaboration (Miyamoto, 2017), which had been previously analyzed using non-relativistic ChEFT at next-to-leading order by Haidenbauer (2017). The present study provides an independent extrapolation to the physical point, predicts the ³D₁ phase shifts and mixing angle ε₁ for future lattice QCD verification, and enables systematic comparison of how baryon-nucleon interactions evolve as one replaces a light quark with a strange or charm quark, comparing NN, ΛN, and ΛcN interactions.
