# Progress and Opportunities in Backward angle (u-channel) Physics

> Backward angle (u-channel) scattering provides complementary information for studies of hadron spectroscopy and structure, but has been less comprehensively studied than the corresponding forward angle case. As a result, the physics of u-channel scattering poses a range of new experimental and theoretical opportunities and questions. We summarize recent progress in measuring and understanding high energy reactions with baryon charge exchange in the u-channel, as discussed in the first backward angle (u-channel) Physics Workshop. In particular, we discuss backward angle measurements and their theoretical description via both hadronic models and the collinear factorization approach, and discuss planned future measurements of u-channel physics. Finally, we propose outstanding questions and challenges for u-channel physics.

## Key claim

Backward angle (u-channel) scattering in high-energy hadron reactions offers complementary information to forward-angle studies for hadron spectroscopy and structure, but the field is less developed; the paper summarizes recent progress and identifies that a unified description of u-channel interactions remains an outstanding challenge, with evidence suggesting a transition from soft (hadronic) to hard (partonic) regimes may be observable at medium energies.

## Method

The paper is a white paper summarizing discussions from the first Backward angle (u-channel) Physics Workshop held virtually in September 2020, with twenty presentations over three days to more than 50 registered participants. It reviews existing experimental measurements from Jefferson Lab's 6 GeV program (CLAS and Hall C) for exclusive meson electroproduction in backward kinematics, and describes theoretical approaches including hadronic Regge-based models and collinear QCD factorization via Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs). The paper also outlines planned future measurements at JLab 12 GeV, GlueX, CLAS12, the Electron-Ion Collider, and PANDA/FAIR.

## Key numbers

- The CLAS collaboration reported the first cross section measurement of exclusive π+ electroproduction off the proton in near-backward kinematics at average kinematics of W=2.2 GeV and -u=0.5 GeV², with σ_U, σ_TT, and σ_LT extracted as functions of Q²
- The separated cross section ratio for ω electroproduction at Q²=2.2 GeV² indicated dominance of transverse over longitudinal cross section
- Hall C ω electroproduction data at Q²=1.60 and 2.45 GeV², at W=2.21 GeV, showed a backward-angle peak at -t>5 GeV² with strength approximately 1/10 of the forward-angle cross section
- The CLAS beam spin asymmetry A_LU^sinφ showed a sign change from positive values in the forward regime to negative values in the backward regime, occurring near 90° in the center-of-mass, independent of Q² and x_B within CLAS kinematics
- The σ_U trend above Q²=2.5 GeV² is qualitatively consistent with TDA calculations yielding a characteristic 1/Q⁸ dependence
- The maximal accessible value of -t in the CLAS beam spin asymmetry measurement was approximately 8.8 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the study of backward-angle scattering, which had been less comprehensively studied than forward-angle (t-channel) physics, with limited past photoproduction measurements predicting a small cross section (1/100 of the t-channel peak) that discouraged experimental efforts. Recent JLab results (Park 2017, Li 2019, Diehl 2020) show observed cross sections approximately 1/10 of the t-channel peak, challenging earlier expectations. The paper enables future experimental programs at JLab 12 GeV (experiments E12-20-007, E12-19-006, E12-09-011), GlueX, CLAS12, the Electron-Ion Collider, and PANDA/FAIR to test TDA scaling predictions and explore the transition between Regge and partonic descriptions.
