# Probing nuclear structure with the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation in full impact-parameter dependence

> Building on the newly available solution of the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with the full impact-parameter dependence, we extend the study of parton evolution from proton to nuclear targets. Since a key part of the scientific programme for future experimental facilities such as the EIC is to study gluon dynamics and shed new light on the phenomenon of parton saturation, we present predictions for key processes, such as deep-inelastic scattering or the diffractive production of vector mesons, on a variety of nuclear targets. Besides the standard BK equation, we employ its linearised version to identify a promising channel to search for gluon saturation in the nuclear collisions. Furthermore, we implement a tetrahedral model of oxygen to search for deviations from the standard, isotropic, Woods-Saxon approach. In addition to the future colliders, the presented results are also of interest for the current studies of nuclear vector meson production at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors extend the recently developed full-impact-parameter-dependent solution of the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation from proton to nuclear targets by modifying only the initial condition, without introducing additional free parameters, and find that the non-linear (saturating) version of the equation describes available nuclear data well while the linearised version fails, particularly for vector meson production at low Mandelstam-\(|t|\).

## Method

The authors solve the target-rapidity, collinearly-improved BK equation with full dependence on dipole size \(r\), impact-parameter magnitude \(b\), and their relative angle \(\theta\), using initial conditions adapted from the proton case. For nuclear targets, they replace the proton saturation scale \(\Qs^2\) with \(\QsA^2 = 0.1\,\mathrm{GeV}^2 A^{1/3}\) and substitute the Gaussian proton profile with nuclear thickness functions (Woods-Saxon for most nuclei, modified harmonic oscillator for carbon, and a tetrahedral \(\alpha\)-cluster model for oxygen). They then compute nuclear structure functions \(F_2\) and coherent \(J/\psi\) photoproduction cross sections, comparing results from the full BK equation (\(\kappa=1\)) with its linearised version (\(\kappa=0\), equivalent to BFKL).

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale for nuclei: \(\QsA^2 = 0.1\,\mathrm{GeV}^2 A^{1/3}\), where \(A\) is the mass number.
- Proton BK parameters: \(\Qs^2 = 0.496\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\), \(\gamma = 1.25\), \(c = 1\), \(B = 3.4\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\), infrared regulator \(C = 5\); quark masses \(m_{\mathrm{uds}} = 0.1\,\mathrm{GeV}\), \(m_c = 1.27\,\mathrm{GeV}\), \(m_b = 4.18\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
- Dipole amplitude sampling range extended to \(r_{\mathrm{max}} = 10^4\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(b_{\mathrm{max}} = 10^4\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) for nuclear targets.
- Tetrahedral oxygen model: tetrahedron side length 3.42 fm, reproducing the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) rms radius of 2.699 fm; stochastic smearing \(\sigma = 0.1\) fm on vertex positions; average over 5000 random orientations.
- \(\chi^2/\mathrm{ndof} = 6.4\) for the proton fit to structure functions and vector meson data in virtuality range \(Q^2 \in [0, 35]\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- Nuclear modification factor \(R_{\mathrm{p}A}\) predictions shown at \(Q^2 = 2\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(Q^2 = 18\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) for multiple nuclei.
- Differential \(J/\psi\) photoproduction cross sections computed at \(W = 124\,\mathrm{GeV}\) for heavy, mid-mass, and light nuclei; total cross sections integrated over \(|t| \in [0, 0.2]\) as functions of \(W\).

## Context

This work extends the full-impact-parameter BK solutions for protons from Cepila et al. (2023, 2025) to nuclear targets, following the nuclear initial-condition approach of Bendova et al. (2020) and Cepila et al. (2020). It contrasts with the impact-parameter-independent BK framework of Albacete et al. (2010) and the Glauber-based nuclear modeling used in earlier studies. The results provide predictions for the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and LHeC, and are directly comparable to current LHC measurements of nuclear vector meson production from ALICE and CMS. The tetrahedral oxygen model, based on Li et al. (2020) and Behera et al. (2021), enables searches for non-spherical nuclear structure effects.
