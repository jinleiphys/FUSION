# Chiral Hadronic Mean Field Model including Quark Degrees of Freedom

> In an approach inspired by Polyakov loop extended NJL models, we present a nonlinear hadronic SU(3) sigma-omega mean field model augmented by quark degrees of freedom. By introducing the effective Polyakov loop related scalar field Φand an associated effective potential, the model includes all known hadronic degrees of freedom at low temperatures and densities as well as a quark phase at high temperatures and densities. Hadrons in the model exhibit a finite volume in order to suppress baryons at high T and μ. This ensures that the right asymptotic degrees of freedom are attained for the description of strongly interacting matter and allows to study the QCD phase diagram in a wide range of temperatures and chemical potentials. Therefore, with this model it is possible to study the phase transition of chiral restoration and deconfinement. In this paper, the impact of quarks on the resulting phase diagram is shown. The results from the chiral model are compared to recent data from lattice QCD.

# Chiral Hadronic Mean Field Model including Quark Degrees of Freedom

## Key claim
The model demonstrates that the chiral phase transition is mainly driven by hadronic degrees of freedom even when a quark phase is included, with the quark phase only flattening the slope of the chiral condensate above the critical temperature for the Polyakov loop. The transition is a smooth cross-over for both order parameters in the whole T-μ plane, though a first-order liquid-gas phase transition for nuclear matter at the ground state up to a critical endpoint at T_c ≈ 15 MeV remains present.

## Method
The authors construct an SU(3)-flavor σ-ω mean field model with a non-linear realization of chiral symmetry, augmented by quark degrees of freedom through a Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) inspired approach. Hadrons are assigned an eigenvolume to suppress baryonic degrees of freedom at high temperatures and densities, while quarks do not carry an eigenvolume, and the effective Polyakov loop field Φ is coupled to the dilaton field to suppress the chiral condensate when deconfinement is achieved. The grand canonical potential is minimized with respect to the meson fields, the Polyakov loop, and particle densities to obtain thermodynamic quantities.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for the chiral condensate in the purely interacting hadronic resonance gas: T_c = 164 MeV, which agrees quantitatively with recent lattice data.
- Critical temperature for the Polyakov loop in the interacting HRG with quark phase: T_c = 175 MeV.
- Baryon eigenvolume in the fully interacting scenario: V_ex^B = 2.7 fm^3, corresponding to a mean baryon radius of r_B = 0.86 fm (slightly smaller than the measured proton charge radius r_p = 0.88 fm).
- Polyakov loop potential parameter T_0: 235 MeV for the non-interacting scenario, 225 MeV when all particles couple to the meson fields.
- Explicit masses: δm_{u,d} = 6 MeV, δm_s = 105 MeV, δm_N = 150 MeV.
- Coupling strengths for nucleons to meson fields: g_{Nσ} = -9.83, g_{Nζ} = 1.22, g_{Nω} = 11.56, g_{Nφ} = 0.0.
- Coupling strengths for light quarks: g_{qσ} = -3.5, g_{qω} = 4.0; for strange quarks: g_{sζ} = -3.5, g_{sφ} = 7.1.

## Context
This work extends the purely hadronic chiral SU(3) σ-ω model developed by Papazoglou (1997, 1998) and Rau (2011, 2012) by incorporating quark degrees of freedom following the PNJL model approach of Fukushima (2003), Ratti (2005), and Roessner (2006). The results are compared to lattice QCD data from the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2009, 2010) and the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2010, Aoki 2009). The model enables studies of the QCD phase diagram across a wide range of temperatures and chemical potentials, including the interplay between chiral restoration and deconfinement, and can be used to investigate the existence and location of a critical endpoint in the phase diagram.
