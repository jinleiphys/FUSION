# Chiral Susceptibility in Hard Thermal Loop Approximation

> The static and dynamic chiral susceptibilities in the quark-gluon plasma are calculated within the lowest order perturbative QCD at finite temperature and the Hard Thermal Loop resummation technique using an effective quark propagator. After regularisation of ultraviolet divergences, the Hard Thermal Loop results are compared to QCD lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The static chiral susceptibility in the quark-gluon plasma can be computed within the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation beyond leading-order perturbation theory, but the result contains a logarithmic ultraviolet divergence at order \(m_q^2\) that requires renormalization, and the HTL approach does not reproduce the peak near the critical temperature seen in lattice QCD simulations, indicating that this peak arises from truly non-perturbative chiral restoration effects not captured by HTL.

## Method

The authors computed the static chiral susceptibility from the derivative of the chiral condensate with respect to the current quark mass, using the HTL-resummed quark propagator in the one-loop tadpole diagram. They expanded the susceptibility in a power series in \(m_q^2/T^2\) up to order \(m_q^4\), applied dimensional regularization to handle ultraviolet divergences, and used minimal subtraction to cancel the pole in \(1/\epsilon\). The dynamic chiral susceptibility in the static limit was also derived from the scalar self-energy using HTL-resummed propagators, decomposed into pole-pole, pole-cut, and cut-cut contributions.

## Key numbers

- Free static chiral susceptibility (leading order): \(\chi_c^f(T) = -\frac{N_f N_c}{6} T^2\)
- HTL static chiral susceptibility expanded to order \(m_q^4\): \(\chi_c^h(T) = \chi_c^f(T) \left[ 1 + \frac{6}{\pi^2} \left( \log\frac{\mu}{4\pi T} + \gamma + 2\log 2 \right) \frac{m_q^2}{T^2} + \frac{21}{4} \frac{\zeta(3)}{\pi^4} \left(5 + \frac{8}{7}\log 2 - \frac{\pi^2}{6} \right) \frac{m_q^4}{T^4} \right]\)
- Thermal quark mass: \(m_q = g(T) T / \sqrt{6}\)
- The \(m_q^2\) term is the first finite \(\alpha_s\)-correction to the free chiral susceptibility; the \(m_q^4\) term is a small correction containing Landau damping contributions
- The temperature dependence of the HTL susceptibility is entirely determined by the temperature dependence of the running coupling constant via \(m_q(T)\)

## Context

This work extends the HTL resummation technique previously applied to the quark number susceptibility by Mustafa and Thoma (2000) and to the free energy of the quark-gluon plasma by Andersen et al. (1999, 2000). The authors show that, unlike the quark number susceptibility, the static chiral susceptibility cannot be computed consistently in usual perturbation theory beyond leading order because the two-loop contribution diverges for zero bare quark mass. The HTL result is compared to lattice QCD simulations by Karsch and Laermann (1994), but a direct quantitative comparison is difficult due to different ultraviolet behavior. The dynamic susceptibility in the static limit is related to the scalar meson correlator at \(\tau = \beta\) discussed by Karsch et al. (1998). The work enables further investigation of non-perturbative effects near the chiral phase transition temperature.
