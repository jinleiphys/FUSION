# Triton calculations with $π$ and $ρ$ exchange three-nucleon forces

> The Faddeev equations are solved in momentum space for the trinucleon bound state with the new Tucson-Melbourne $π$ and $ρ$ exchange three-nucleon potentials. The three-nucleon potentials are combined with a variety of realistic two-nucleon potentials. The dependence of the triton binding energy on the $πNN$ cut-off parameter in the three-nucleon potentials is studied and found to be reduced compared to the case with pure $π$ exchange. The $ρ$ exchange parts of the three-nucleon potential yield an overall repulsive effect. When the recommended parameters are employed, the calculated triton binding energy turns out to be very close to its experimental value. Expectation values of various components of the three-nucleon potential are given to illustrate their significance for binding.

## Key claim

The inclusion of $\rho$ exchange in the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon potential reduces the strong dependence of the triton binding energy on the $\pi NN$ cut-off parameter that was observed with pure $\pi$ exchange, and yields an overall repulsive effect that brings the calculated binding energy close to the experimental value of -8.482 MeV when the recommended parameters are employed.

## Method

The Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon bound state were solved in momentum space, expanded into 18 partial waves, with the full Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon potentials including $\pi\pi$, $\pi\rho$, and $\rho\rho$ exchange terms. These three-nucleon potentials were combined with several realistic two-nucleon potentials: the Reid soft core, Paris, Nijmegen, and Bonn OBEPQ potentials. The partial wave decomposition of the complex $\pi\rho$ and $\rho\rho$ exchange potentials was performed using a systematic procedure developed by Adam and Henning (1992).

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with the Paris potential plus full three-nucleon force: -8.494 MeV, deviating from the experimental value of -8.482 MeV by only 12 keV
- For the Bonn OBEPQ potential, the full three-nucleon force yields overbinding of more than 1 MeV relative to experiment
- The $\pi\pi$ exchange matrix elements were computed in 3.5 minutes of CPU time, the $\pi\rho$ exchange in 10 minutes, and the $\rho\rho$ exchange in 15 minutes (compared to 90 minutes for $\pi\pi$ alone with the previous method)
- Changing the $\rho$ cut-off parameters from soft to hard values altered the triton binding energy by more than 200 keV for $\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 5.8 \mu$ with the Paris potential
- The recommended $\pi NN$ cut-off parameter is $\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 5.8 \mu \approx 810$ MeV, with an estimated theoretical error bar of $\pm 200$ MeV
- The updated parameters $a$, $b$, and $c$ changed the triton binding energy from -8.494 MeV to -8.465 MeV; the subsequent modification of the factor in the $\pi\rho$ potential changed it further to -8.492 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the $\pi\pi$ exchange three-nucleon force by Coon and Gloeckle (1981) and Stadler et al. (1991), which showed strong cut-off dependence and overbinding. The inclusion of $\rho$ exchange follows the strategy of Coon and Pena (1993) who reexamined the $\pi\rho$ and $\rho\rho$ exchange potentials originally developed by the Tucson-Melbourne group. The results are in qualitative agreement with earlier findings by Hajduk et al. (1983) for the $\Delta$-mediated three-nucleon force, where $\pi\rho$ exchange also weakened the $\pi\pi$ attraction. The calculations enable future investigations of heavier meson exchanges such as $\pi\sigma$ and $\pi\omega$, which the authors suggest should be included for consistency with one-boson exchange two-nucleon potentials.
