# Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry and Heavy Baryons: Electroweak Decays

> Heavy quark spin symmetry is discussed in the context of single and doubly heavy baryons. A special attention is paid to the constraints/simplifications that this symmetry imposes on the non-relativistic constituent quark model wave functions and on the b->c semileptonic decays of these hadrons.

## Key claim

Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry (HQSS) imposes constraints on non-relativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) wave functions that greatly simplify the three-body problem for single and doubly heavy baryons, and these simplifications allow the authors to predict semileptonic decay form factors and widths that agree with lattice QCD and experiment, though the symmetry is approximate and receives corrections of order \(1/m_Q\) and \(q_{\rm light}/m_Q\).

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic three-quark Hamiltonian variationally using Jastrow-type spatial wave functions constrained by HQSS, which guarantees that the spin of the light degrees of freedom (\(S_{\rm light}\)) is well defined in the infinite heavy-quark mass limit. For single heavy baryons, they used single-particle Hamiltonians for the light quarks in the mean field of the heavy quark plus a light-light correlation function; for doubly heavy baryons, they assumed the total spin of the two heavy quarks (\(S_h\)) is well defined. They employed phenomenological quark-quark potentials (the AL1 potential of Silvestre-Brac (1996)) and computed masses, wave functions, and then used these to evaluate \(b \to c\) semileptonic decay form factors and widths for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\) and for transitions among doubly heavy baryons.

## Key numbers

- \(\Lambda_c\) mass: \(2286.46 \pm 0.14\) MeV (experimental); \(\Lambda_b\) mass: \(5620.2 \pm 1.6\) MeV (experimental)
- \(\Sigma_c\) mass: \(2453.5 \pm 0.5\) MeV; \(\Sigma^*_c\) mass: \(2517.5 \pm 0.5\) MeV (experimental)
- \(\Sigma_b\) mass: \(5811 \pm 4\) MeV; \(\Sigma^*_b\) mass: \(5832 \pm 4\) MeV (experimental)
- Extracted \(|V_{cb}| = 0.040 \pm 0.005\ (\text{stat})\ ^{+0.001}_{-0.002}\ (\text{theory})\) from \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\) semileptonic decay
- For \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\) decay: longitudinal asymmetry \(\langle a_L \rangle = -0.954 \pm 0.001\), transverse asymmetry \(\langle a_T \rangle = -0.665 \pm 0.002\), longitudinal-to-transverse ratio \(R_{L/T} = 1.63 \pm 0.02\)
- For \(\Xi_b \to \Xi_c\) decay: \(\langle a_L \rangle = -0.945 \pm 0.002\), \(\langle a_T \rangle = -0.628 \pm 0.004\), \(R_{L/T} = 1.53 \pm 0.04\)
- Hyperfine mixing angle between \(S_h\)-basis and physical \(bc\)-baryon states is approximately \(4^\circ\) in NRCQM calculations

## Context

This work extends the application of HQSS to NRCQM descriptions of heavy baryons, building on the Faddeev-equation approach of Silvestre-Brac (1996) and the lattice QCD calculations of Bowler (2001) and the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (2008). The variational method reproduces the masses obtained by solving Faddeev equations but yields simpler, more manageable wave functions. The extracted \(|V_{cb}|\) agrees with the determination from exclusive \(\bar{B}^0_d \to D^{*+} l^- \bar{\nu}_l\) decays. The analysis of hyperfine mixing in \(bc\)-baryons follows the work of Roberts and Pervin (2007) and shows that HQSS predictions for decay-width ratios could provide a model-independent way to extract the mixing angle from future experimental data.
