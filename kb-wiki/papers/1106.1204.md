# Two-Photon Exchange Effect Studied with Neural Networks

> An approach to the extraction of the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction from elastic $ep$ scattering data is presented. The cross section, polarization transfer (PT), and charge asymmetry data are considered. It is assumed that the TPE correction to the PT data is negligible. The form factors and TPE correcting term are given by one multidimensional function approximated by the feed forward neural network (NN). To find a model-independent approximation the Bayesian framework for the NNs is adapted. A large number of different parametrizations is considered. The most optimal model is indicated by the Bayesian algorithm. The obtained fit of the TPE correction behaves linearly in epsilon but it has a nontrivial Q2 dependence. A strong dependence of the TPE fit on the choice of parametrization is observed.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction extracted from elastic electron-proton scattering data using a Bayesian neural network approach behaves linearly in the kinematic variable epsilon but has a nontrivial Q² dependence; however, the fit shows a strong dependence on the choice of network parametrization, meaning the result is not uniquely determined by the data alone.

## Method

The author constructed feed-forward neural networks with one hidden layer to simultaneously approximate the proton electric and magnetic form factors (G_E, G_M) and the TPE correction term ΔC_{2γ} as functions of Q² and epsilon. A Bayesian framework was applied to compare 45 different network architectures (varying the number of hidden units in the form-factor and TPE sectors), with the optimal model selected by maximizing the evidence. The networks were trained on 27 cross-section data sets, 14 polarization transfer data sets, and 3 positron-to-electron ratio data sets, assuming the TPE correction to polarization transfer data is negligible.

## Key numbers

- The optimal network architecture was 2-(5-6)-3 (5 hidden units in the form-factor sector, 6 in the TPE sector), selected from 45 architectures with 10³ random initial weight configurations each.
- The TPE correction ΔC_{2γ}/σ_{1γ+2γ,R} shows a local minimum near Q² ≈ 0.2 GeV² and becomes a decreasing function of Q² above 2 GeV².
- At Q² around 1.5 GeV² and epsilon above 0.8, the TPE correction becomes positive.
- The form-factor ratio μ_p G_E/G_M from the best fit disagrees with the results of Arrington et al. (2007) above Q² = 4 GeV².
- The artificial constraint points were placed at Q²=0 with uncertainty Δ = 0.01 for G_M/μ_p = 1, G_E = 1, and R_±(0,1) = 1.

## Context

This work extends previous global analyses of proton form factors and TPE corrections (e.g., Alberico et al. 2008, Arrington et al. 2007) by applying Bayesian model comparison to objectively select the functional form of the TPE parametrization, rather than relying on phenomenological arguments. The approach enables model-independent extraction of the TPE correction from combined cross-section, polarization transfer, and charge asymmetry data, and provides predictions for future e⁺p/e⁻p scattering experiments. The strong dependence of the TPE fit on network architecture demonstrates that previous analyses may have underestimated the model uncertainty in the TPE correction.
