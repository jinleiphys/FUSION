# Rotationally-Invariant Circuits: Universality with the exchange interaction and two ancilla qubits

> Universality of local unitary transformations is one of the cornerstones of quantum computing with many applications and implications that go beyond this field. However, it has been recently shown that this universality does not hold in the presence of continuous symmetries: generic symmetric unitaries on a composite system cannot be implemented, even approximately, using local symmetric unitaries on the subsystems [I. Marvian, Nature Physics (2022)]. In this work, we study qubit circuits formed from k-local rotationally-invariant unitaries and fully characterize the constraints imposed by locality on the realizable unitaries. We also present an interpretation of these constraints in terms of the average energy of states with a fixed angular momentum. Interestingly, despite these constraints, we show that, using a pair of ancilla qubits, any rotationally-invariant unitary can be realized with the Heisenberg exchange interaction, which is 2-local and rotationally-invariant. We also show that a single ancilla is not enough to achieve universality. Finally, we discuss applications of these results for quantum computing with semiconductor quantum dots, quantum reference frames, and resource theories.

# Rotationally-Invariant Circuits: Universality with the exchange interaction and two ancilla qubits

## Key claim
Despite the general result that generic symmetric unitaries on a composite system cannot be implemented using local symmetric unitaries on subsystems (Marvian, Nature Physics 2022), any rotationally-invariant unitary can be realized using the Heisenberg exchange interaction (which is 2-local and rotationally-invariant) provided the system interacts with a pair of ancillary qubits; a single ancilla qubit is insufficient to achieve this universality.

## Method
The authors use a Lie-algebraic approach to characterize the group generated by k-local rotationally-invariant unitaries on n qubits. They decompose the Hilbert space into sectors of fixed total angular momentum j, with multiplicities given by Catalan numbers, and analyze the constraints on realizable Hamiltonians in terms of their inner products with operators C_l (sums of l-local exchange interactions). They then construct a Hamiltonian coupling the system to two ancilla qubits that satisfies the locality constraints and, when the ancillae are prepared in the |00⟩ state, implements the desired symmetric unitary on the system.

## Key numbers
- The dimension of the group V_k generated by k-local rotationally-invariant unitaries is dim(V_k) = (1/(n+1))*binomial(2n,n) - floor(n/2) + floor(k/2), where (1/(n+1))*binomial(2n,n) is the nth Catalan number.
- For n=4 qubits, the condition for realizability via exchange interaction is 15 Tr(H Π_0) - 5 Tr(H Π_1) + 3 Tr(H Π_2) = 0, where Π_j are projectors onto angular momentum j subspaces.
- The constraints impose floor(n/2) - floor(k/2) independent conditions on the manifold of realizable Hamiltonians.
- For n=10 qubits, the eigenvalues c_l(j) of operators C_l are tabulated for l=0,2,4,6,8,10 and j=0 through 5 (see Table 1 in the paper).
- The operator C_2 has eigenvalue c_2(j) = 2j(j+1) - 3n/2, and C_2 = 2*sum_{i1<i2} R_{i1,i2} where R_{rs} is the exchange interaction.

## Context
This work extends the no-go theorem of Marvian (Nature Physics, 2022) which showed that generic symmetric unitaries cannot be implemented using local symmetric unitaries in the presence of continuous symmetries. It builds on the encoded universality results of Bacon and DiVincenzo (2000) and Kempe (2001), who showed that universal quantum computation can be achieved using the exchange interaction with specific encodings (e.g., each qubit encoded in 3 spins). The present work establishes a stronger notion of universality: all rotationally-invariant unitaries can be implemented using the exchange interaction and two ancilla qubits, without requiring encoding. This enables applications in quantum computing with semiconductor quantum dots (Loss and DiVincenzo, 1998), quantum reference frames, and resource theories of asymmetry and thermodynamics.
