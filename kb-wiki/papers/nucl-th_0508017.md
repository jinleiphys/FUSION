# Proton polarizations in polarized 3He studied with the \vec{3He} (\vec{e},e' p)d and \vec{3He} (\vec{e},e' p)pn processes

> We study within the Faddeev framework the \vec{3He} (\vec{e},e' p)d as well as the \vec{3He} (\vec{e},e' p)pn and \vec{3He} (\vec{e},e' n)pp reactions in order to extract information on the proton and neutron polarization in polarized 3He. We achieve clear analytical insight for simplified dynamical assumptions and define conditions for experimental access to important 3He properties. In addition we point to the possibility to measure the electromagnetic proton form factors in the process \vec{3He} (\vec{e},e' p)d which would test the dynamical picture and put limits on medium corrections of the form factors.

## Key claim

Under the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA), the parallel and perpendicular helicity asymmetries measured in the \(\vec{^3{\rm He}} (\vec{e},e' p)d\) reaction directly yield the proton polarization inside polarized \(^3\)He, but final state interactions (FSI) and meson exchange currents (MEC) distort this simple picture except for small relative proton-deuteron momenta.

## Method

The authors performed full Faddeev calculations for the \(\vec{^3{\rm He}} (\vec{e},e' p)d\) and \(\vec{^3{\rm He}} (\vec{e},e' p)pn\) reactions, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential alone or combined with the UrbanaIX three-nucleon force, and including \(\pi\)- and \(\rho\)-like two-body currents. They compared these full results to PWIA predictions to assess the validity of extracting spin-dependent momentum distributions and proton polarizations from measured helicity asymmetries. The calculations used the electron kinematics from a recent MAMI experiment (Achenbach 2005) with beam energy 735 MeV, electron scattering angle 50 degrees, and three-momentum transfer 569 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

* The integrated proton polarization \(P_{\rm int}\) in polarized \(^3\)He is -0.364 with the three-nucleon force and -0.362 without it.
* The integral \(\int_0^\infty dq \, f_1(q) = 0.127\) (with 3N force) and 0.128 (without 3N force); the integral \(\int_0^\infty dq \, f_2(q) = 0.348\) (with 3N force) and 0.354 (without 3N force).
* FSI effects for strictly parallel kinematics in the \(\vec{^3{\rm He}} (\vec{e},e' p)d\) reaction amount to 5-7 percent.
* Three-nucleon force effects reach almost 20 percent at the proton angle equal to the virtual photon direction for the cross section, but are below 1 percent for the parallel helicity asymmetry \(A_\parallel\) in strictly parallel kinematics.
* For small relative momenta \(q \lesssim 50\) MeV/c, both \(P_1\) and \(P_2\) approach approximately 1/3.
* The integrands \(f_1(q)\) and \(f_2(q)\) contributing to \(P_{\rm int}\) are significant only for \(q \lesssim 350\) MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Golak et al. 2005) of spin-dependent momentum distributions in polarized \(^3\)He, which found that FSI and MEC destroy the PWIA picture for most kinematics in the \(\vec{^3{\rm He}} (e,e' \vec{p})d\) and \(\vec{^3{\rm He}} (e,e' \vec{d})p\) reactions. The present paper is motivated by a recent experiment at MAMI (Achenbach 2005) that measured electron-target asymmetries for both two- and three-body breakup of \(^3\)He. The results enable experimental access to proton polarizations in polarized \(^3\)He through the simpler \(\vec{^3{\rm He}} (\vec{e},e' p)d\) reaction, which requires only a polarized electron beam and polarized \(^3\)He target, and suggest the possibility of measuring electromagnetic proton form factors in the \(^3{\rm He} (e,e' p)d\) process to test medium corrections.
