# Excitation of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance using 6Li inelastic scattering

> The incompressibility of infinite nuclear matter (K_\infty) is a parameter in the description of the nuclear equation of state that governs the energy cost associated with density oscillations near the saturation density. The most direct experimental method for studying this property of infinite nuclear matter is to probe the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in finite nuclei. This work explores the use of 6Li as a probe to study the ISGMR in several stable nuclei: 58Ni, 90Zr, 116Sn, and 208Pb, as complementary to using inelastic scattering of α-particles, which has been used to great effect over the last several decades. Elastic and inelastic scattering data for these targets were collected with 343-MeV 6Li beams. In all nuclei studied in this work, the ISGMR strength distributions extracted from multipole decomposition analyses of the inelastic scattering spectra agree very well with the previously measured ISGMR responses from α-particle scattering, establishing the feasibility of employing 6Li inelastic scattering in investigations of the ISGMR.

# Excitation of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance using 6Li inelastic scattering

## Key claim

The ISGMR strength distributions extracted from 343-MeV 6Li inelastic scattering on 58Ni, 90Zr, 116Sn, and 208Pb agree very well with previously measured ISGMR responses from alpha-particle scattering, establishing the feasibility of 6Li as a complementary probe for ISGMR studies. However, the authors note that centroid energies for 58Ni and 116Sn are somewhat lower than previous measurements, and that agreement might improve if 6Li measurements were performed at energies similar to the 100 MeV/u used in alpha and deuteron measurements.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic scattering data were collected using a 343-MeV 6Li beam on enriched targets of 58Ni, 90Zr, 116Sn, and 208Pb at the RCNP Grand Raiden spectrometer. Optical model parameters were extracted from elastic scattering angular distributions (2.5° to 30° laboratory angle) using a real density-dependent folded potential with CDM3Y6-Paris interaction and imaginary Woods-Saxon volume and surface potentials. Multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) using a Markov-Chain Monte Carlo algorithm was performed on inelastic scattering spectra measured over 0.5° to 9.2° laboratory angle and excitation energies of 5-30 MeV, fitting experimental angular distributions to sums of DWBA cross sections for multipoles up to lambda=7.

## Key numbers

- 6Li beam energy: 343 MeV (approximately 60 MeV/u)
- Angular range for inelastic scattering: 0.5° < theta_lab < 9.2°
- Angular range for elastic scattering: 2.5° < theta_lab < 30°
- Excitation energy range: 5-30 MeV
- 58Ni ISGMR centroid: 17.5 ± 0.1 MeV, width 4.6 ± 0.1 MeV, 79 ± 2% EWSR
- 90Zr ISGMR centroid: 16.6 ± 0.1 MeV, width 4.4 ± 0.1 MeV, 95 ± 2% EWSR
- 116Sn ISGMR centroid: 15.3 ± 0.2 MeV, width 6.3 ± 0.2 MeV, 112 +9/-8% EWSR
- 208Pb ISGMR centroid: 13.6 ± 0.1 MeV, width 4.0 ± 0.2 MeV, 143 +9/-11% EWSR
- Target areal densities: 58Ni 1.50 mg/cm2, 90Zr 1.95 mg/cm2, 116Sn 4.18 mg/cm2, 208Pb 10.0 mg/cm2

## Context

This work extends previous ISGMR studies using 6Li probes at lower energies (26-40 MeV/u by Eyrich, Chen, and Dennert in the 1990s-2000s) and more recent 100 MeV/u measurements by Zamora, by covering a broader range of nuclei (58Ni, 90Zr, 116Sn, 208Pb) with a full multipole decomposition analysis over a larger angular range. The results are compared extensively with alpha-particle scattering measurements by Nayak, Lui, Gupta, Li, and Patel, as well as deuteron scattering by Patel, establishing that 6Li inelastic scattering can serve as a viable alternative probe for ISGMR studies. This is particularly relevant for future inverse-kinematics measurements with radioactive ion beams, where 6Li solid targets could avoid the sparking and quenching-gas complications associated with 4He active targets used by Vandebrouck, Monrozeau, and Bagchi.
