# Resurgence for the non-conformal Bjorken flow with Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein statistics

> We consider resurgence for the nonconformal Bjorken flow with Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein statistics on the extended relaxation-time approximation. We firstly consider full formal transseries expanded around the equilibrium and then construct the resurgent relation by looking to the structure of Borel transformed ODEs. We form a conjecture of the resurgent relation based on the considerations that Stokes constants constituting of the resurgent relation originate only from singularities of dissipative variables on the Borel plane and that the other variables such as temperature and chemical potential become Borel nonsummable through nonlinear terms with the dissipative variables. We numerically check the conjecture for fundamental variables by explicitly evaluating values of the dominant Stokes constant depending on initial conditions and a particle mass. We also make comments on some issues related to transseries structure and resurgence such as the case of broken $U(1)$ symmetry, the massless case, generalized relaxation-time, and attractor solution.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for the non-conformal Bjorken flow with Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein statistics in the extended relaxation-time approximation, the resurgent relation can be constructed from a conjecture that Stokes constants originate only from singularities of dissipative variables on the Borel plane, while temperature and chemical potential become Borel non-summable only through nonlinear coupling with those dissipative variables.

## Method
The author derives the full formal transseries expanded around equilibrium from the coupled nonlinear ODEs for temperature, chemical potential, bulk viscosity, and shear viscosity in the IR regime (late time, w >> 1). The resurgent relation is constructed by analyzing the structure of Borel-transformed ODEs and examining large-order behavior of the perturbative sector. The dominant Stokes constant is numerically evaluated for fundamental variables as a function of initial conditions and particle mass.

## Key numbers
- The leading-order inverse temperature scales as β ∼ σ_β w^2, with exponent 2/3 different from the conformal case β ∝ τ^{1/3}
- The exponential decay rate for nonperturbative transmonomials is S = 3/θ_0, with power-law exponent ρ_± = 5 − 12 C^{β̄_π}_0(σ_{R,a}) ∓ √(1285)/5
- The leading-order shear viscosity in the perturbative sector is π̄_pt ∼ (0.8889 − 0.2357 σ_{R,a}) θ_0 w^{−1}
- The leading-order bulk viscosity in the perturbative sector is Π̄_pt ∼ (2.0741 − 0.5500 σ_{R,a}) θ_0^2 w^{−2}
- The chemical potential in the IR limit gives lim_{w→+∞} μ_{pf} = −1 (= −m)

## Context
This work extends the transseries and resurgence analysis previously applied to the conformal Bjorken flow by Heller (2015), Basar (2015), Aniceto (2015), and Behtash (2018-2020) to the non-conformal case with Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein statistics. It builds on the non-conformal Bjorken flow without U(1) symmetry studied by Kamata (2022) and incorporates conserved U(1) current via the extended relaxation-time approximation of Teaney (2013) and Dash (2021). The results enable future study of nonperturbative out-of-equilibrium physics in QCD where conformal symmetry is broken at lower temperatures.
