# Theoretical description of semi-inclusive T2K, Miner$ν$A and MicroBooNE neutrino-nucleus data in the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation

> We present the results of semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections within the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) for three nuclear models: relativistic Fermi gas (RFG), independent-particle shell model (IPSM) and natural orbital shell model (NO) in comparison with the available CC0$π$ measurements from the T2K, MINER$ν$A and MicroBooNE collaborations where a muon and at least one proton are detected in the final state. Results are presented as a function of the momenta and angles of the final particles, as well as in terms of the imbalances between proton and muon kinematics. The analysis reveals that contributions beyond PWIA are crucial to explain the experimental measurements and that the study of correlations between final-state proton and muon kinematics can provide valuable information on relevant nuclear effects such as the Fermi motion and final state interactions.

## Key claim

The plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) fails to describe semi-inclusive CC0π neutrino-nucleus data from T2K, MINERνA, and MicroBooNE, and contributions beyond PWIA, particularly final state interactions and multinucleon effects, are essential to explain the experimental measurements, though the PWIA results serve as a necessary first-step benchmark.

## Method

The authors computed flux-averaged fifth-differential semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections within the PWIA using three nuclear models: relativistic Fermi gas (RFG), independent-particle shell model (IPSM), and natural orbital shell model (NO). They compared these predictions with experimental CC0π data from T2K (νμ on 12C), MINERνA (νμ on 12C), and MicroBooNE (νμ on 40Ar), presenting results as functions of natural variables (muon and proton momenta and angles), transverse kinematic imbalances (δpT, δαT, δφT), and inferred variables (Δp, Δθ, |Δp|) that compare measured proton kinematics to the quasielastic hypothesis.

## Key numbers

- For T2K inclusive CC0π data with proton momentum pN < 0.5 GeV, the PWIA results for IPSM and NO are much higher than data at cosθl near 1 (small scattering angles), while RFG results stay closer to data due to Pauli blocking (pN > kF).
- For T2K semi-inclusive data with pN > 0.5 GeV, the difference between RFG and the shell models at small θl disappears, as the experimental cut on final proton momentum effectively mimics Pauli blocking.
- T2K phase-space restrictions for semi-inclusive measurements: muon momentum k' > 0.25 GeV, cosθl > -0.6, proton momentum pN in 0.45-1.0 GeV range, cosθNL > 0.4.
- For MINERνA and MicroBooNE data, the paper presents comparisons but no specific numerical cross section values are given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PWIA formalism (Franco-Patino et al., 2020) to compare with recent semi-inclusive CC0π data from T2K, MINERνA, and MicroBooNE collaborations. The analysis shows that PWIA alone cannot describe the data, particularly at low momentum transfer where orthogonality between bound and free nucleon wave functions is violated. The study enables future work incorporating final state interactions and multinucleon excitations (2p2h), which are expected to bring shell model predictions into agreement with data, and demonstrates that transverse kinematic imbalances can help disentangle Fermi motion, final state interactions, and correlation effects.
