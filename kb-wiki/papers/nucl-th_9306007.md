# Correlation Effects in the Final-State Interaction for Quasielastic $(e,e'p)$ Scattering

> Color transparency predicts that, in $(e,e'p)$ reactions at large $Q^2$, the final-state interaction becomes weaker than the reference value predicted from the free-nucleon cross section. This reference value is usually evaluated in the dilute-gas approximation to Glauber's multiple-scattering theory. We derive the leading-order correction taking into account two-body correlations. Large cancellations are found so that the overall correlation effect is small.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading-order correction to the dilute-gas approximation of Glauber's multiple-scattering theory for quasielastic (e,e'p) reactions, finding that two-body correlation effects produce large cancellations between the "hole" effect and the "spectator" effect, so that the overall correlation effect is small and the dilute-gas approximation remains reliable.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear transparency factor Tr_A for quasielastic (e,e'p) scattering by including two-body correlations in the nuclear density within the Glauber multiple-scattering framework. They separate the correlation correction into two contributions: the "hole" effect (local reduction of nuclear density around the struck proton) and the "spectator" effect (enhancement of final-state interaction with correlated spectator nucleons), and evaluate both analytically using Gaussian approximations for the correlation function and the proton-nucleon profile function.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic proton-nucleon cross section: σ_in(pN) = 32 mb, used for numerical estimates.
- Diffraction slope for proton-nucleon elastic scattering: B_in = 0.5 fm².
- Correlation radius: r_c = 0.5 fm.
- Finite-range reduction factor for the spectator effect: r_c²/(r_c² + 2B_in) ≈ 1/5.
- Finite-range reduction factor for the hole effect: r_c²/(r_c² + B_in) ≈ 1/3.
- The analysis applies for Q² ≲ 5·A^(1/3) (GeV/c)², below the onset of color transparency.
- The multiplicity of rescatterings ν ≲ σ_in(pN) n_A(0) R_A ≈ 0.7 A^(1/3) ≪ A.

## Context

This work extends the dilute-gas approximation to Glauber's multiple-scattering theory for nuclear transparency in (e,e'p) reactions, which had previously been used as the reference value for color transparency studies. The authors derive the previously neglected "spectator" effect and show it largely cancels the "hole" effect discussed by Benhar et al. (1991), who did not consider the spectator contribution and quoted a numerically larger hole effect. The results are consistent with the findings of Kohama, Yazaki and Seki (1993) for ⁴He, and the conclusions are also relevant for (p,2p) reactions.
