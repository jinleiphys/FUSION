# Thermal QRPA equations with finite rank separable approximation for residual forces based on the Skyrme type interactions

> The approach to study properties of charge-exchange excitations in hot nuclei is presented. The approach is based on the extension of the finite rank separable approximation for Skyrme interactions to finite temperatures employing the TFD formalism. We present the formulae to obtain charge-exchange strength distributions within the Thermal Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (TQRPA).

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism to compute charge-exchange strength distributions in hot nuclei using the Thermal Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (TQRPA) with a finite rank separable approximation for Skyrme-type residual interactions, but the approach explicitly ignores the influence of temperature on the nuclear mean field and considers only the isovector part of the residual particle-hole interaction.

## Method

The authors extend the finite rank separable approximation for Skyrme interactions (previously developed for zero temperature by Giai (1998) and Severyukhin (2008, 2011)) to finite temperatures using the thermo-field dynamics (TFD) formalism. They first construct the thermal Hamiltonian by doubling the nuclear degrees of freedom, then apply a standard Bogoliubov transformation to quasiparticle operators followed by a thermal Bogoliubov transformation to thermal quasiparticle operators. The TQRPA eigenvalue equations are derived by minimizing the expectation value of the thermal Hamiltonian over thermal one-phonon states under constraints from the thermal state condition, and the separable form of the residual interaction reduces the TQRPA matrix equations to a set of equations for 2N-dimensional vectors D_+ and D_-.

## Key numbers

- The residual interaction in the isovector channel is expressed as V_res(r1,r2) = N_0^{-1}[F_0'(r1) + G'_0(r1) sigma(1)sigma(2)] tau(1)tau(2) delta(r1-r2), where N_0 = 2k_F m^* / (pi^2 hbar^2) with k_F and m^* standing for the Fermi momentum and nucleon effective mass in nuclear matter.
- The pairing interaction has the form V_pair(r1,r2) = V_0 (1 - eta (rho(r1)/rho_c)^alpha) delta(r1 - r2), where rho_0 is equal to the nuclear saturation density and V_0, eta, alpha are parameters fixed to reproduce the odd-even mass difference of nuclei in the study region.
- The thermal Bogoliubov transformation coefficients are given by y_j = [1 + exp(epsilon_j/T)]^{-1/2} and x_j = (1 - y_j^2)^{1/2}, where epsilon_j = sqrt((E_j - lambda)^2 + Delta_j^2) is the quasiparticle energy.
- The excitation energies with respect to the thermal equilibrium state of the parent nucleus are E^∓ = omega_Ji ∓ Delta_lambda for non-tilde phonon states and E^∓ = -omega_Ji ∓ Delta_lambda for tilde phonon states, where Delta_lambda = lambda_n - lambda_p is the difference between neutron and proton chemical potentials.

## Context

This work extends the finite rank separable approximation for Skyrme interactions developed by Giai (1998) and Severyukhin (2008, 2011) from zero temperature to finite temperatures using the TFD formalism previously adapted to nuclear structure problems by Dzhioev (2009). It builds on earlier TQRPA applications by Cooperstein (1984) for electron captures on neutron-rich nuclei and more recent work by Paar (2009), Dzhioev (2010), and Niu (2011) using different nuclear Hamiltonians, but replaces the schematic separable effective interactions used by Dzhioev (2010) with a self-consistent Skyrme-based residual interaction. The formalism enables calculation of charge-exchange strength distributions in hot nuclei for astrophysical applications, particularly weak-interaction mediated reactions in stellar environments.
