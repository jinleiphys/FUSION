# Towards the solution of the $C_{P}/C_{A}$ anomaly in shell-model calculations of muon capture

> Recently many authors have performed shell-model calculations of nuclear matrix elements determining the rates of the ordinary muon capture in light nuclei. These calculations have employed well-tested effective interactions in large scale shell-model studies. For one of the nuclei of interest, namely $^{28}$Si, there exists recent experimental data which can be used to deduce the value of the ratio $C_{P}/C_{A}$ by using the calculated matrix elements. Surprisingly enough, all the abovementioned shell-model results suggest a very small value ($\simeq 0$) for $C_{P}/C_{A}$, quite far from the PCAC prediction and recent data on muon capture in hydrogen. We show that this rather disturbing anomaly is solved by employing effective transition operators. This finding is also very important in studies of the scalar coupling of the weak charged current of leptons and hadrons.

# Towards the solution of the \(C_{P}/C_{A}\) anomaly in shell-model calculations of muon capture

## Key claim

The anomaly in which state-of-the-art shell-model calculations using the USD interaction yield a near-zero value for the ratio \(C_{P}/C_{A}\) in muon capture on \({}^{28}\)Si (contradicting the PCAC prediction of 6.8 and experimental data) is resolved by employing effective (renormalized) transition operators rather than bare operators. The renormalized calculations yield \(C_{P}/C_{A}\) values close to the PCAC prediction, but the result is conditional on using effective operators derived from a microscopic \(G\)-matrix approach based on the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors performed full \(1s0d\) shell-model calculations for the reaction \({}^{28}\mathrm{Si}(0^{+}_{\mathrm{gs}}) + \mu^{-} \rightarrow {}^{28}\mathrm{Al}(1^{+}_{3}) + \nu_{\mu}\) using two effective interactions: the empirical USD interaction of Wildenthal and a microscopic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn meson-exchange potential via a \(G\)-matrix and \(\hat{Q}\)-box summation of higher-order diagrams through third order. They computed nuclear matrix elements for muon capture using both bare transition operators and renormalized one-body transition operators obtained from the CD-Bonn \(G\)-matrix through second-order diagrams (including folded diagrams, up to \(6\hbar\omega\) intermediate excitations), then extracted the ratio \(C_{P}/C_{A}\) from the experimental anisotropy parameter \(x = M_{1}(2)/M_{1}(-1)\).

## Key numbers

- The PCAC Goldberger-Treiman value for the ratio is \(C_{P}/C_{A} = 6.8\).
- For muon capture in hydrogen, the experimental interval is \(6.8 \le C_{P}/C_{A} \le 10.6\) (ordinary capture) and \(8.8 \le C_{P}/C_{A} \le 10.8\) (radiative capture at TRIUMF).
- Using the Fujii-Primakoff approximation, the measured anisotropy parameters \(x = 0.254 \pm 0.034\) and \(x = 0.315 \pm 0.08\) yield \((C_{P}/C_{A})_{\mathrm{FPA}} = 7.7 \pm 1.2\) and \(9.7 \pm 2.6\), respectively.
- With bare transition operators (both USD and CD-Bonn interactions), the extracted range is \(-0.4 \le C_{P}/C_{A} \le 1.2\) for \({}^{28}\)Si, using the experimental value \(x = 0.315 \pm 0.08\).
- With renormalized transition operators, the extracted range is \(4.4 \le C_{P}/C_{A} \le 5.9\) for \({}^{28}\)Si.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter used was \(1.72\) fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of muon capture in light nuclei by Gmitro et al. (1990), Kuzmin et al. (1994), Johnson et al. (1996), and Siiskonen et al. (1997), which found anomalously small \(C_{P}/C_{A}\) values when using the USD interaction. It also addresses the contradictory results between the shell-model predictions and the anisotropy measurements of Moftah et al. (1997) and Brudanin et al. (1997) on \({}^{28}\)Si. The finding that effective transition operators resolve the anomaly enables future shell-model studies of muon capture in other light nuclei (particularly those at shell interfaces) to obtain reliable \(C_{P}/C_{A}\) values, and has implications for studies of the scalar coupling of the weak charged current.
