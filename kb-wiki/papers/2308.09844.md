# Recent developments in mathematical aspects of relativistic fluids

> We review some recent developments in mathematical aspects of relativistic fluids. The goal is to provide a quick entry point to some research topics of current interest that is accessible to graduate students and researchers from adjacent fields, as well as to researches working on broader aspects of relativistic fluid dynamics interested in its mathematical formalism. Instead of complete proofs, which can be found in the published literature, here we focus on the proofs' main ideas and key concepts. After an introduction to the relativistic Euler equations, we cover the following topics: a new wave-transport formulation of the relativistic Euler equations tailored to applications; the problem of shock formation for relativistic Euler; rough (i.e., low-regularity) solutions to the relativistic Euler equations; the relativistic Euler equations with a physical vacuum boundary; relativistic fluids with viscosity. We finish with a discussion of open problems and future directions of research.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that geometric-analytic techniques originally developed for studying the vacuum Einstein equations (particularly those exploiting the wave aspect of the equations) can be successfully applied to relativistic fluid dynamics, but that new methods are needed to handle systems with multiple characteristic speeds (sound waves, vorticity, entropy) rather than the single characteristic speed present in vacuum Einstein or perfect irrotational isentropic fluids.

## Method

The paper surveys recent mathematical results on the relativistic Euler equations and relativistic viscous fluids, focusing on: (1) a new wave-transport formulation of the relativistic Euler equations that reveals hidden structural and regularity properties; (2) the problem of shock formation for relativistic Euler; (3) existence of low-regularity (rough) solutions; (4) free-boundary problems with a physical vacuum boundary; and (5) relativistic fluids with viscosity. The presentation is schematic, omitting complete proofs but emphasizing key ideas, with the goal of providing an accessible entry point for graduate students and researchers from adjacent fields.

## Key numbers

* The new wave-transport formulation of the relativistic Euler equations is derived in the nearly hundred-page paper by Disconzi and Speck (2019), which exhibits "miraculous cancellations" in the computations.
* The paper covers results in 3+1 spacetime dimensions (three spatial dimensions plus time), with Greek indices running from 0 to 3 and Latin indices from 1 to 3.
* The signature convention for Lorentzian metrics is -+++ throughout the review.

## Context

This review extends the geometric-analytic approach pioneered by Christodoulou (2007) for perfect irrotational isentropic fluids (where the Euler equations reduce to a single quasilinear wave equation) to more general fluid regimes involving vorticity, entropy, and viscosity. It also builds on earlier work by Alinhac (1999-2001) on quasilinear wave equations. The paper enables researchers to understand how techniques from general relativity (particularly those dealing with wave propagation) can be adapted to fluid problems with multiple characteristic speeds, and identifies open problems for future work.
