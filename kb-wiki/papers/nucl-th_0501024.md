# Quantum derivation of the use of classical electromagnetic potentials in relativistic Coulomb excitation

> We prove that a relativistic Coulomb excitation calculation in which the classical electromagnetic field of the projectile is used to induce transitions between target states gives the same target transition amplitudes, to all orders of perturbation theory, as would a calculation in which the interaction between projectile and target is mediated by a quantized electromagnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper proves that a relativistic Coulomb excitation calculation using the classical electromagnetic field of the projectile to induce target transitions yields the same target transition amplitudes, to all orders of perturbation theory, as a calculation using a quantized electromagnetic field, provided the projectile charge and current densities are treated as specified functions of position and time (i.e., the projectile does not change its internal state during the collision).

## Method

The authors start from the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the target, with the interaction expressed in terms of the quantized electromagnetic field in the Coulomb gauge. They expand the transition amplitude in a perturbation series and show that, under the assumption that the projectile charge and current densities are prescribed functions, every photon creation-annihilation pair can be integrated out, leaving an effective time-dependent interaction that depends only on the target degrees of freedom. They then demonstrate that this effective interaction is identical to the classical electromagnetic interaction of the target with the projectile's classical fields, and verify the same result holds in the Lorentz gauge.

## Key numbers

- The proof extends the earlier result of Alder, Bohr, Huus, Mottelson and Winther (ABHMW), which established the equivalence only to first order in perturbation theory, to all orders of perturbation theory.
- The paper notes that Baltz, Rhoades-Brown and Weneser (BRW) estimated excitation probabilities greater than 0.5 for grazing collisions between oppositely directed 100 GeV Au nuclei, and greater than 0.1 out to impact parameters of about 50 fm.

## Context

This work extends the foundational result of Alder, Bohr, Huus, Mottelson and Winther (ABHMW), who showed the equivalence of classical and quantized-field treatments only to first order in perturbation theory, and the standard approach of Winther and Alder (1979) which used first-order perturbation theory. The paper justifies the use of classical electromagnetic potentials in modern coupled-channel calculations that go beyond first-order perturbation theory, such as those by Bertulani et al. and others. It enables future work on ultra-high-energy relativistic Coulomb excitation where both projectile and target may undergo internal transitions, which the authors note would require a more complete treatment.
