# Oscillator strength distribution in C3H6 isomers studied with the time-dependent density functional method in the continuum

> We present photoabsorption oscillator strengths for C3H6 molecules with emphasis on the difference between isomers, cyclopropane and propylene. We use an iterative numerical method based on the time-dependent local density approximation with continuum, which we have recently developed. The oscillator strengths for the two isomers differ at photon energies above their ionization thresholds. The magnitude and the shape of the oscillator strength distribution are in good agreement with recent experiments. The differences between the isomers arise from difference in symmetry of electronic states and different behaviors of continuum excitations.

## Key claim

The oscillator strength distributions of cyclopropane and propylene isomers differ significantly at photon energies below 22 eV, but become nearly identical above that threshold; the differences arise from the higher symmetry and degeneracy of electronic states in cyclopropane, which produce strong bound-to-bound transitions that create coherent anti-screening peaks in the continuum, while propylene lacks such strong bound resonances.

## Method

The authors used a time-dependent local-density approximation (TDLDA) with a continuum boundary condition, implemented on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with 0.3 Å spacing inside a sphere of 6 Å radius (33,401 grid points). They split the Kohn-Sham potential into a long-range spherical part and a short-range deformed part, constructed the Green's function for the spherical potential, and solved the linear algebraic equations iteratively using the generalized conjugate residual method. The exchange-correlation potential combined the local density part of Perdew and Zunger (1981) with the gradient correction of van Leeuwen and Baerends (1994), with a parameter β adjusted to match the experimental vertical ionization potentials.

## Key numbers

- HOMO eigenvalue for cyclopropane: −10.6 eV (with β=0.015), matching the experimental vertical IP of 10.54 eV (Pleil 1981)
- HOMO eigenvalue for propylene: −9.9 eV (with β=0.015), matching the experimental vertical IP of 9.91 eV (Krause 1978)
- Calculated peak positions in cyclopropane: 11.8 eV, 13.5 eV, and 15.7 eV, shifted lower than experiment by about 1.5 eV
- Calculated broad peak in propylene: centered at 13–18 eV, shifted lower than experiment by about 1.5 eV
- Integrated oscillator strength for 8 < ω < 60 eV: 16.2 for cyclopropane and 15.8 for propylene (about 90% of the Thomas-Kuhn-Reiche sum rule value of 18)
- Partial sum for 10–25 eV: TDLDA gives 10.0 (experiment 9.9) for cyclopropane; TDLDA gives 9.5 (experiment 9.4) for propylene
- Partial sum for 25–35 eV: TDLDA gives 2.9 (experiment 3.3) for cyclopropane; TDLDA gives 2.9 (experiment 3.1) for propylene
- Smoothing parameter Γ = 0.5 eV used for complex frequencies
- Frequency mesh: Δω = 0.25 eV for 8–20 eV, Δω = 0.5 eV for higher energies

## Context

This work extends the continuum TDLDA method previously developed by the same authors (Nakatsukasa and Yabana 2001) to study isomer effects in C3H6 molecules, addressing a long-standing demand for theoretical investigation of the experimental observations by Koizumi et al. (1985) and Kameta et al. (1999). The method improves upon the single-center expansion technique of Levine and Soven (1984), which was limited to small axially symmetric molecules, by using a three-dimensional grid representation that requires no spatial symmetry. The results enable future studies of how molecular structure influences continuum excitations in larger organic molecules and demonstrate that the dynamical screening effects from bound-to-bound transitions can qualitatively change the photoabsorption spectrum even at energies above the ionization threshold.
