# Interference Fragmentation Functions and Valence Quark Spin Distributions in the Nucleon

> We explore further applications of the twist-two quark interference fragmentation functions introduced earlier. We show that semi-inclusive production of two pions in the current fragmentation region in deep inelastic scattering of a longitudinally polarized electron on a longitudinally polarized nucleon can provide a probe of the valence quark spin (or helicity difference) distribution in the nucleon.

## Key claim

Semi-inclusive production of two pions in the current fragmentation region in deep inelastic scattering of a longitudinally polarized electron on a longitudinally polarized nucleon can provide a probe of the valence quark spin (helicity difference) distribution, but only because the asymmetry arises from interference between s-wave and p-wave production channels around the rho mass region where charge conjugation mixing occurs; the asymmetry would vanish by C-invariance if the two pions were in a charge conjugation eigenstate.

## Method

The authors factor the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering process e N -> e' pi+ pi- X into helicity density matrices representing the nucleon-to-quark distribution function, the hard partonic eq -> e'q' cross section, the quark-to-resonance (sigma, rho) fragmentation, and the resonance decay to two pions. They work in a collinear approximation at leading twist, incorporating final state interactions via pi-pi phase shifts delta_0 and delta_1, and derive a forward-backward asymmetry in the two-pion rest frame that isolates the valence quark spin distribution.

## Key numbers

- The double asymmetry A^FB_{Uparrow Downarrow} contains the factor y(2-y)/[1+(1-y)^2] times sin(delta_0) sin(delta_1) cos(delta_0 - delta_1), where y is the standard DIS inelasticity variable and delta_0, delta_1 are the strong interaction pi-pi phase shifts.
- The flavor structure of the asymmetry yields the combination (4/9) Delta u_v - (1/9) Delta d_v, where Delta q_v = Delta q - Delta bar q are the valence quark spin distributions.
- The ratio of the double asymmetry to the unpolarized forward-backward asymmetry gives the expression y(2-y)/[1+(1-y)^2] times (4 Delta u_v - Delta d_v)/(4 u_v - d_v), which is independent of the interference fragmentation functions.
- The interference factor sin(delta_0) sin(delta_1) cos(delta_0 - delta_1) peaks near the rho mass (m ~ m_rho) and is positive over the rho region.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Jaffe, Jin, Tang, 1997) of two-pion production on a transversely polarized nucleon, which targeted the quark transversity distribution. The asymmetry described here is a particular contribution to the single-pion fragmentation effects proposed by Frankfurt et al. (1989) and Close and Milner (1991) for measuring valence quark spin distributions, with the advantage that it can be characterized in terms of pi-pi phase shifts and two-particle fragmentation functions that appear in other hard processes. The measurements could be carried out at facilities such as HERMES at HERA and COMPASS at CERN.
