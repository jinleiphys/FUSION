# Resolving the structure of bound states using lattice quantum field theories

> This work presents the first lattice calculation of a two-to-two particle matrix element of a local current. This exploratory calculation is performed using a leading-order pionless effective field theory of two nucleons in a finite 3D spatial volume, where the Hamiltonian can be diagonalized exactly for moderate volumes. By considering a range of couplings where the theory supports a deuteron-like bound state, we determine the finite-volume spectra and matrix elements of the conserved local vector current. Using the Lüscher formalism, we constrain the infinite-volume, purely hadronic amplitude for this theory. Using previously derived formalism, we then map the finite-volume matrix elements to scattering amplitudes describing a reaction coupling two-particle states via a current insertion, $\2+\Jc \to \2$. We then use a recently derived relation between this class of amplitudes and the bound-state elastic form factor to directly constrain the infinite-volume form factor. By varying over a range of values of the coupling of the theory, we explore the effects of this analysis for deep-bound states and shallow-bound states. We reproduce the expected result that for deep bound states, the finite-volume formalism is largely unnecessary, while for shallow bound states, it is absolutely critical to obtain a sensible result. We present a detailed outline of the analysis of this class of matrix elements, including the determination of the charge radius of the bound state. In the shallow bound state limit, we find good agreement with the prediction stemming from the anomalous threshold.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice calculation of a two-to-two particle matrix element of a local current, using a leading-order pionless effective field theory in a finite 3D spatial volume. The authors demonstrate that for shallow bound states, the finite-volume formalism is absolutely critical to obtain a sensible result, while for deep bound states it is largely unnecessary.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the Hamiltonian of a leading-order pionless effective field theory (EFT_{\pi}) for two nucleons in a finite 3D spatial volume, using moderate volumes where exact diagonalization is possible. They tune the two-body interaction to support a deuteron-like bound state and vary the coupling to study both deep and shallow bound states. Using the Lüscher formalism, they constrain the infinite-volume purely hadronic scattering amplitude, then map finite-volume matrix elements of the conserved local vector current to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes describing a two-particle reaction via a current insertion (2+J→2). Finally, they apply a recently derived relation between these amplitudes and the bound-state elastic form factor to extract the infinite-volume form factor.

## Key numbers

- The formalism is exact up to exponentially suppressed errors scaling as e^{-mL}, where L is the spatial extent of the lattice and m is the mass gap of the theory.
- The scattering amplitude is parametrized using the effective range expansion (ERE) to next-to-leading order: q* cot δ^{NLO}(s) = -1/a + (1/2) r q*^2, where a is the scattering length and r is the effective range.
- The bound state pole condition is given by q* cot δ(s) evaluated at q* = iκ plus κ = 0, where κ is the binding momentum.
- The residue at the bound state pole, g, satisfies 1/g^2 = 1/(64π κ √s_B) * (1 - 2κ ∂/∂q*^2 q* cot δ(s)) evaluated at s = s_B.
- The single-particle dispersion relation is ω_{q*} = √(M^2 + q*^2) = M + q*^2/(2M) + O(q*^4).
- The non-relativistic two-particle energy in the CM frame is E*_{NR} = E* - 2M = q*^2/M + O(q*^4).

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Briceno (2015), Baroni (2018), and Briceno (2020) for relating finite-volume matrix elements to infinite-volume amplitudes, which had previously been tested only in perturbative limits and through Ward-Takahashi identity checks. The calculation builds on prior lattice QCD studies of simpler electroweak transitions (J→2 and 1+J→2) by groups including Lellouch (2000), Christ (2005), and Briceno (2015), and provides the first numerical implementation for the 2+J→2 class of reactions. This enables future lattice QCD calculations of electroweak processes involving two-nucleon systems, such as coherent neutrino-deuteron scattering, photodisintegration of the deuteron, and elastic form factors of the deuteron.
