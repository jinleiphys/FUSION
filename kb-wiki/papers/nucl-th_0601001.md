# Nuclei from QCD: Strategy, Challenges and Status

## Key claim

Progress toward calculating nuclear properties from QCD is possible through a multi-step matching of effective field theories, but the full program remains incomplete because lattice QCD calculations are currently limited to quark masses larger than physical values and to very light nuclei (A ≤ 4 or possibly A ∼ 8), and because the fine-tunings in nuclear systems require understanding at the level of 10⁻⁴ in the fundamental parameters.

## Method

The strategy proceeds through four matching steps: (1) lattice QCD calculations of the lightest nuclei (A ≤ 4) as a function of light-quark masses; (2) exact many-body methods such as Green Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) or the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) using chiral potentials, matched to lattice QCD for A ∼ 20–30; (3) coupled-cluster expansions for nuclei up to A ∼ 100; and (4) density functional theory for very large nuclei and nuclear matter. Each adjacent pair of theories is matched over a range of atomic numbers where both converge, allowing coefficients to be determined from the more fundamental calculation.

## Key numbers

- The number of contractions in a direct lattice QCD calculation for a nucleus with atomic number A and charge Z is (A+Z)!(2A−Z)!, making direct calculations for large nuclei impractical.
- A change of ∼0.5% in the strength of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is sufficient to yield a universe without significant amounts of ¹²C or ¹⁶O (Oberhummer 2000, Csoto 2000).
- The allowed variation in the relative location of the ¹²C** level is ∼100 keV, while the strong interaction scale is hundreds of MeV, implying a fine-tuning at the level of 10⁻⁴.
- The deuteron binding energy is ∼2.2 MeV; the scattering length in the ¹S₀ channel is ∼−24 fm.
- Preliminary NPLQCD lattice QCD results for nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths exist at pion masses of ∼500 MeV and ∼600 MeV.
- When the pion mass reaches ∼175 MeV, an additional shallow bound state appears in the triton spectrum (Braaten and Hammer 2003).
- In the scenario where both ¹S₀ and ³S₁–³D₁ scattering lengths become infinite, adjacent triton bound-state energies are related by E_{n+1}² = 515 E_n².
- The V_{low k} potentials from different modern potential models (AV₁₈, CD-Bonn, Idaho A) coincide when evolved to a low scale Λ ∼ 600 MeV (Bogner 2001, Schwenk 2004).

## Context

This paper outlines the strategy for connecting lattice QCD to nuclear structure, extending the pioneering work of Weinberg (1990, 1991) on chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon interactions and the renormalization group analyses of Kaplan (1998) and Birse (1998). It builds upon GFMC calculations with the AV₁₈ potential by Pieper (2004) and NCSM calculations with chiral potentials by Forssen (2004). The approach enables future rigorous, model-independent calculations of nuclear properties in terms of the light-quark masses, the QCD scale, and the electromagnetic coupling, and it provides a framework for exploring fine-tunings such as the triple-alpha process in ¹²C production.
