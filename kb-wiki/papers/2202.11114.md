# Twist-three cross-sections in deeply virtual Compton scattering

> We study the deeply virtual Compton scattering process with both twist-two and twist-three Compton form factors and present our cross-sections formulas with all polarization configurations. While the twist-three contributions are generally assumed to be negligible in the literature due to the kinematical suppression, we compare them with the twist-two ones at typical JLab 6 GeV and 12 GeV kinematics as well as EIC kinematics and show their kinematical suppression explicitly, justifying the leading-twist approximation made in the literature. In addition, we also estimate the twist-three Compton form factors using Wandzura-Wilczek relations and inputs of twist-two generalized parton distributions based on a reggeized spectator model. With those estimated Compton form factors, we analyze the kinematical behavior of twist-two and twist-three cross-sections in a wide range of kinematics, and discuss the optimal regions for separating the leading-twist effects from the higher-twist ones.

## Key claim

While twist-three contributions to deeply virtual Compton scattering are generally assumed negligible due to kinematical suppression, this paper explicitly quantifies that suppression at JLab 6 GeV, JLab 12 GeV, and EIC kinematics, thereby justifying the leading-twist approximation, but also identifies optimal kinematic regions where twist-three effects become measurable once leading-twist quantities are precisely determined.

## Method

The authors derived complete cross-section formulas for deeply virtual Compton scattering including both twist-two and twist-three Compton form factors for all beam and target polarization configurations, using covariant Compton tensor coefficients that satisfy current conservation. They estimated the twist-three Compton form factors via Wandzura-Wilczek relations combined with twist-two generalized parton distributions from a reggeized spectator model, then compared the kinematical behavior of twist-two and twist-three cross-sections across a wide range of kinematics for fixed-target (JLab) and collider (EIC) setups.

## Key numbers

- The kinematical suppression of twist-three relative to twist-two contributions was explicitly shown at typical JLab 6 GeV and 12 GeV kinematics as well as EIC kinematics, though no specific numerical ratios are provided in the truncated text.
- The photon virtuality \(Q^2\) is defined as \(Q^2 \equiv -q^2\), with the Bjorken scaling variable \(x_B \equiv Q^2/(2 P\cdot q)\) and the parameter \(\gamma \equiv 2M x_B/Q\) introduced with \(M\) the target mass.
- The cross-section differential in five variables is given by \(\frac{\text{d}^5\sigma}{\text{d}x_B \text{d}Q^2\text{d}|t|\text{d}\phi \text{d}\phi_S}=\frac{\alpha_{\rm{EM}}^3 x_B y^2}{16\pi^2 Q^4\sqrt{1+\gamma^2}} \left|\mathcal T\right|^2\), where \(\alpha_{\rm{EM}}\) is the fine structure constant and \(y \equiv (P\cdot q)/(P\cdot k)\).
- The twist-three Compton form factors enter the cross-section only in four combinations (e.g., \(\bar{ \mathcal {H}}_{2T}(\xi,t)\equiv  \mathcal {H}_{2T}(\xi,t)-\mathcal {H}'_{2T}(\xi,t)\)) rather than the full set of eight, due to degeneracy at leading order in \(\alpha_S\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous twist-two analysis (Guo, 2021) and builds upon the theoretical framework for twist-three DVCS developed by Anikin (2000), Penttinen (2000), Belitsky (2000, 2005), Kivel (2000), and Radyushkin (2000). It enables future experimental programs at JLab 24 GeV, the EicC (Anderle, 2021), and the EIC (AbdulKhalek, 2021) to systematically separate leading-twist from higher-twist effects, and potentially constrain twist-three generalized parton distributions that are related to the angular momentum of the nucleon (Jaffe, 1989; Lorce, 2011; Burkardt, 2012; Rajan, 2016, 2017; Guo, 2021).
