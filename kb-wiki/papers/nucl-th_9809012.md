# An investigation of interplay between dissipation mechanisms in heated Fermi systems by means of radiative strength functions

## Key claim
The gamma-decay strength function derived with a microcanonical ensemble for initial excited states leads to both a non-zero limit of the strength function for vanishing gamma-ray energy and a partial breakdown of the Brink hypothesis. The low-energy behavior of the strength function is governed by the energy dependence of the damping width, which may provide a new tool for studying the interplay between different relaxation mechanisms of collective excitations.

## Method
The authors derived a simple analytical expression for the E1 gamma-decay strength function using a microcanonical distribution for initial excited states, combined with a hydrodynamic model with friction that extends the Steinwedel-Jensen model. The Vlasov-Landau kinetic equation was completed with a source term for relaxation processes to obtain the friction coefficient of the isovector velocity, separating the damping width into two-body collisional and one-body (fragmentation) contributions. Calculations were performed for the nucleus 144Nd at an initial excitation energy equal to the neutron binding energy, using the Fermi gas model to determine the temperature of final states.

## Key numbers
- The damping width consists of two components: the two-body contribution \(\Gamma_{c}(\epsilon_{\gamma},T_{f})\) and the one-body contribution \(\Gamma_{s} = k_{s}\hbar \bar{v} R_{0}\).
- The collisional relaxation time is given by \(\tau_{c}(\epsilon_{\gamma},T_{f}) \simeq 0.9 \hbar \alpha T_{f}^{-2} / [1+ ( \epsilon_{\gamma} /2\pi T_{f})^{2}]\).
- The dipole strength function formula includes a prefactor of \(8.674 \cdot 10^{-8}\) with units MeV\(^{-3}\).
- Calculations for 144Nd used initial state energy \(E = B_{n} = 7.82\) MeV and level density parameter \(a = A/8\).
- The parameter \(\alpha = 9.2\) MeV was used, corresponding to an in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section smaller than the free-space value by a factor of 2.
- With \(\alpha \approx 9.2\) MeV, the contribution of collisional damping to the GDR width is about 15%.
- Calculations were also performed with \(\alpha = 4.6\) MeV, corresponding to the free-space nucleon-nucleon cross section.
- The EGLO model uses a parameter \(\epsilon_{0}^{\gamma} = 4.5\) MeV and \(k_{0} = 1\) for 144Nd.
- Calculations were extended to excitation energy \(E = 50\) MeV to demonstrate the breakdown of the Brink hypothesis.

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic model with friction from Steinwedel and Jensen (1950) and the Vlasov-Landau kinetic approach from Yatsyshin and Hoffmann (1981) to include both two-body and one-body dissipation mechanisms in heated nuclei. The approach is compared with the EGLO model from Kopecky and Uhl (1993) and the standard Lorentzian (SLO) model, showing good agreement with experimental data at low gamma-ray energies. The results enable a new method for studying the interplay between different relaxation mechanisms of collective excitations in heated Fermi systems, and demonstrate a partial breakdown of the Brink hypothesis that increases with excitation energy.
