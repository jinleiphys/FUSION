# Chiral kinetic approach to the chiral magnetic effect in isobaric collisions

> Based on the chiral kinetic approach using quarks and antiquarks from a multiphase transport model as initial conditions, we study the chiral magnetic effect, i.e., the magnetic field induced separation of charged particles in the transverse plane, in non-central isobaric collisions of Zr$+$Zr and Ru$+$Ru, which have the same atomic number but different proton numbers. For the observable $γ^{OS}-γ^{SS}$ related to the difference between the correlations of particles of opposite charges and of same charges, we find a difference between the two collision systems if the magnetic field has a long lifetime of 0.6 fm$/c$ and the observable is evaluated using the initial reaction plane. This signal of the chiral magnetic effect becomes smaller and comparable to the background contributions from elliptic flow if the event plane determined from particle emission angles is used. For the other observable given by the $R(ΔS)$ correlator related to the distribution of average charge separation in a collision, the signal due to the chiral magnetic effect is found to depend less on whether the reaction or event plane is used in the analysis, and their difference between the two isobaric collision systems is thus a more robust observable.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal in isobaric Zr+Zr and Ru+Ru collisions, as measured by the gamma(OS-SS) correlator, shows a difference between the two systems only if the magnetic field has a long lifetime (0.6 fm/c) and the observable is evaluated using the initial reaction plane; this difference becomes negligible when the event plane from emitted particles is used, whereas the R(Delta S) correlator provides a more robust signal that depends less on the choice of reaction plane.

## Method

The authors used the chiral kinetic approach with quarks and antiquarks from the string melting version of the AMPT model as initial conditions, evolving the partonic matter with modified equations of motion and scattering cross sections in the presence of an external magnetic field. The magnetic field was calculated from the Lienard-Wiechert potentials of spectator protons, with an assumed long lifetime of 0.6 fm/c as an upper limit, and the initial axial charge density was modeled from topological transition fluctuations using the saturation scale Q_s^2 = 1.25 GeV^2. The partonic matter was evolved until the energy density decreased to 0.56 GeV/fm^3, and charge separation was studied via the gamma(OS-SS) and R(Delta S) correlators for light quarks at mid-pseudorapidity.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field at x=0 from spectator protons: about 2.95 m_pi^2 for Zr+Zr and 3.24 m_pi^2 for Ru+Ru collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and impact parameter b=7 fm, with a short lifetime of about 0.066 fm/c.
- With a long-lived magnetic field (tau_B=0.6 fm/c), the gamma(OS-SS) correlator for Ru+Ru collisions in the transverse momentum range 0.05 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c is 1.849 x 10^{-4} when calculated from 2a_1^2.
- The charge separation 2a_1^2 changes from 1.549 x 10^{-4} for Zr+Zr to 1.849 x 10^{-4} for Ru+Ru collisions, a 19.4% increase.
- Using the initial reaction plane event-by-event, the gamma(OS-SS) correlator changes from 1.949 x 10^{-4} (Zr+Zr) to 2.242 x 10^{-4} (Ru+Ru), an increase of about 15.0 +/- 5.4%.
- The fluctuation in topological charge is sqrt(N_5^2) = 135.16 for both systems at b=7 fm.
- The correlation between event plane and initial reaction plane is <cos(2 Psi_EP - 2 Psi_RP)> = 0.27.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the chiral magnetic effect using anomalous hydrodynamics (Shi 2017) and the AMPT model with assumed initial charge separation (Deng 2018) by employing the chiral kinetic approach, which includes non-equilibrium effects and explicitly treats v_2-driven backgrounds. The isobaric collision comparison was proposed by Voloshin (2017) to separate CME signals from backgrounds. The results enable future experimental searches at RHIC using the R(Delta S) correlator as a more robust observable, though the authors note that hadronic stage effects from resonance decays and local charge conservation (Bozek 2017, Feng 2018) are not included but are expected to be similar in both systems.
