# Signatures of chiral symmetry restoration in dilepton production

> We study the structural change of the vector spectral function and integrated production rates of dileptons in the presence of the chiral mixing induced exclusively at finite density. The mixing produces multiple bumps and peaks around the vacuum masses of the $ρ, ω$ and $ϕ$ resonances in the spectral function. The arising modification becomes pronounced when the mass difference between parity partners decreases. In particular, the emergent enhancement around the vacuum $ϕ$ meson in the production rates serves as an excellent signature of the partially-restored chiral symmetry in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a density-induced chiral mixing, even with a marginal strength of about 0.1 GeV, produces multiple bumps and peaks in the vector spectral function and dilepton production rates, and that this structural change is reinforced as the mass difference between parity partners decreases, serving as a signature of partially-restored chiral symmetry in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The author introduces the order parameter (mass difference between vector and axial-vector mesons) as a function of temperature and chemical potential into the vector current correlator, using a chiral Lagrangian with a mixing term derived from the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action. The in-medium profiles of the sigma and omega expectation values are obtained from the nucleon parity-doublet model in the mean field approximation, and the vector spectral function and dilepton production rates are calculated for the ρ-ω and φ channels at fixed temperature T=50 MeV as functions of baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The mixing strength c is about 0.1 GeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀, and about 0.3 GeV at higher density.
- The chiral crossover occurs at baryon chemical potential μ_B ≈ 1.05 GeV, corresponding to net baryon density ρ_B ≈ 2.5 ρ₀.
- Vacuum masses used: m_ρ = 0.77 GeV, m_π = 0.14 GeV, m_{a1} = 1.26 GeV, f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- Vacuum on-shell decay widths: Γ_ρ(s=m_ρ²)=0.15 GeV, Γ_{a1}(s=m_{a1}²)=0.33 GeV.
- At the chiral crossover, δm/δm_vac = 0.26 for light vector states and 0.36 for strange vector mesons.
- The vector spectral function is calculated at |p|=0.5 GeV and T=50 MeV for δm/δm_vac values of 0.7, 0.4, and 0.26.
- The dilepton production rate is integrated over 0 ≤ |p| ≤ 1 GeV at T=50 MeV for μ_B/μ_B^c = 0.75 and 1.0.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of chiral mixing in dense matter, particularly the density-induced mixing found in AdS/CFT-based models (DHholo) and the phenomenological impact studied in HSdense. It contrasts with earlier finite-temperature studies (HSWhot, HRhot) where chiral mixing vanishes at restoration, and with the scenario without mass degeneracy (HSdense). The results enable experimental searches for chiral symmetry restoration signatures in dilepton measurements at FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC, with the φ meson channel identified as the most promising due to its narrow width in medium.
