# Extensive statistical mechanics based on nonadditive entropy: Canonical ensemble

> The original canonical ensemble formalism for the nonextensive entropy thermostatistics is reconsidered. It is shown that the unambiguous connection of the statistical mechanics with the equilibrium thermodynamics is provided if the entropic parameter $1/(q-1)$ is an extensive variable of the state. Based on a particular example of the perfect gas, it is proved that the Tsallis thermostatistics meets all the requirements of equilibrium thermodynamics in the thermodynamic limit. In particular, the entropy of the system is extensive and the temperature is intensive. However, for finite systems both the Tsallis and Boltzmann-Gibbs entropies are nonextensive. The equivalence of the canonical and microcanonical ensembles of Tsallis thermostatistics in the thermodynamic limit is established. The issue associated with physical interpretation of the entropic variable is discussed in detail.

# Extensive statistical mechanics based on nonadditive entropy: Canonical ensemble

## Key claim

The Tsallis thermostatistics can satisfy all requirements of equilibrium thermodynamics in the thermodynamic limit, provided the entropic parameter \(1/(q-1)\) is treated as an extensive variable of state rather than a universal constant; for finite systems, both the Tsallis and Boltzmann-Gibbs entropies remain nonextensive.

## Method

The author rederives the canonical ensemble for Tsallis nonextensive entropy by imposing the fundamental equation of thermodynamics as a constraint to obtain the equilibrium phase distribution function, avoiding the controversial Lagrange multiplier approach. The thermodynamic limit is defined as \(N \to \infty\) with \(v = V/N = \text{const}\) and \(\tilde{z} = z/N = \text{const}\), where \(z = 1/(q-1)\) is the extensive entropic variable. The formalism is then tested explicitly on the nonrelativistic ideal gas of \(N\) identical particles, using integral representations of the Euler gamma function to evaluate expectation values.

## Key numbers

- The specific energy per particle for the ideal gas in the thermodynamic limit is \(\varepsilon = \frac{3}{2} kT \frac{B}{1 + \frac{3}{2\tilde{z}}}\), where \(B\) is an intensive function of state.
- The specific entropy in the thermodynamic limit is \(s = k\tilde{z} \left[1 - \frac{B}{1 + \frac{3}{2\tilde{z}}}\right]\).
- The heat capacity at constant volume and specific \(z\) is \(C_{v\tilde{z}} = \frac{3}{2} kN \frac{B}{(1 + \frac{1}{\tilde{z}}\frac{3}{2})^{2}}\).
- The effective particle mass is \(m_{eff} = m \frac{B}{1 + \frac{1}{\tilde{z}}\frac{3}{2}}\), which reduces to the bare mass \(m\) in the Boltzmann-Gibbs limit \(\tilde{z} \to \pm\infty\).
- The relative statistical fluctuation of momentum is \(\delta_p = \sqrt{\frac{3\pi}{8} - 1} \approx 0.424\), independent of \(\tilde{z}\).
- The system is physically unstable in the region \(-3/2 < \tilde{z} < 0\).

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical ensemble treatment of Tsallis thermostatistics by Parvan (2006) to the canonical ensemble, demonstrating ensemble equivalence in the thermodynamic limit. It corrects an earlier incorrect thermodynamic limit definition by Abe (1999) and addresses the critique by Nauenberg (2003) that Tsallis statistics fails the zeroth law when \(q\) is a universal constant. The results enable a consistent physical interpretation of the entropic parameter \(z\) as an order parameter related to effective interactions among quasiparticles, with repulsive forces for \(\tilde{z} < -3/2\) and attractive forces for \(\tilde{z} > 0\).
