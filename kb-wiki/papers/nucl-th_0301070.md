# Continuum effects in reactions involving weakly bound nuclei

> The relevance of the continuum effects in transfer reactions is discussed in conection with the determination of observables of astrophysical interest. In particular, we examine the validity of the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) as a tool to extract the astrophysical \emph{(S_{17}(0))} factor. For this purpose, we present calculations for the reaction (^{14})N((^{7} )Be,(^{8})B)(^{13})C comparing the DWBA method with the more sophysticated CDCC-Born approximation

## Key claim

The Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) is a reliable tool for extracting the astrophysical S17(0) factor from the transfer reaction 14N(7Be,8B)13C, because continuum effects from the weakly bound 8B nucleus are found to be negligible in the transfer cross section, even though they significantly affect the elastic scattering of the exit channel at larger angles.

## Method

The authors compared standard DWBA calculations with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels Born Approximation (CDCC-BA) for the reaction 14N(7Be,8B)13C at 84 MeV bombarding energy. In the CDCC-BA approach, the 8B continuum was discretized into 10 bins uniformly spaced in energy up to 9 MeV, including s, p, and d partial waves for the relative motion of the 7Be-p system. The same core-core (7Be+13C) interaction was used in both methods to allow a reliable comparison.

## Key numbers

- The 8B nucleus has a binding energy of 0.137 MeV, making it weakly bound.
- The 8B continuum was discretized into N=10 bins, uniformly spaced in energy, up to a maximum energy of 9 MeV.
- The transfer reaction was measured at a bombarding energy of 85 MeV (Texas A&M Cyclotron facility) and calculated at 84 MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor for the (8B|7Be) overlap was taken as S(p3/2)=0.733, and for the (14N|13C) overlap as 0.604.
- The CDCC calculations were multiplied by a geometric factor of 5/16 due to the assumption that 7Be has zero spin.

## Context

This work extends the ANC (Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient) method developed by Azhari et al. (1999), who extracted the ANC for the (7Be,8B) overlap from the same transfer reaction using DWBA. The authors address concerns raised by the fact that 8B is weakly bound (0.137 MeV), which could invalidate the DWBA approach. The calculations confirm the validity of the DWBA extraction and thus support the astrophysical S17(0) factor derived from that analysis. The work also demonstrates that continuum effects in the elastic scattering of 8B+13C become important above 30 degrees, but these discrepancies do not affect the forward-angle transfer cross sections.
