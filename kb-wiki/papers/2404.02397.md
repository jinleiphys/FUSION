# Net proton number cumulants from viscous hydro with equation of state including a critical end point

> In the SMASH-CLVisc-hybrid framework, including SMASH for the initial conditions and the hadronic rescattering stage, and CLVisc for the quark gluon plasma (QGP) evolution, we investigate net baryon number fluctuations via considering the equation of state (EoS) with and without a critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase transition. Specifically, two distinct QCD EoS are utilized: one with smooth crossover derived from NEOS and another with a critical end point sourced from the rPNJL model. Our results show that non-monotonic behavior of $κσ^2$ is not observed above the collision energy $7.7 {\rm GeV}$, nor are there explicit differences between the EoS characterized by crossover and that by CEP. This could be attributed to the significant deviation of the freeze-out line from the location of CEP. It is also found that the pure SMASH result of $κσ^2$ is positive and close to zero at 3 GeV, which is different from the negative value observed from STAR.

## Key claim

The paper finds that non-monotonic behavior of the kurtosis times variance ($\kappa\sigma^2$) is not observed above collision energy 7.7 GeV, nor are there explicit differences between the equation of state with a smooth crossover and that with a critical end point (CEP), which the authors attribute to the significant deviation of the freeze-out line from the location of the CEP.

## Method

The authors employ the SMASH-CLVisc-hybrid framework, using SMASH for initial conditions and hadronic rescattering, and (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc viscous hydrodynamics for quark-gluon plasma evolution. Two equations of state are input into the hydrodynamics: NEOS-BQS (smooth crossover only) and rPNJL (containing a critical end point). The Cooper-Frye formula is used for particlization at freeze-out energy density 0.4 GeV/fm$^3$, and net proton number cumulants are calculated from the resulting particle distributions.

## Key numbers

- The CEP in the rPNJL model is located at ($T_{\chi}^{\rm CEP}=103$ MeV, $\mu_{B,\chi}^{\rm CEP}=679$ MeV).
- The starting proper time $\tau_0$ for hydrodynamical evolution ranges from 3.2 fm/c at 7.7 GeV to 0.7 fm/c at 62.4 GeV.
- Shear viscosity $C_{\eta_v} = 0.08$ and coefficient $C_B = 0.4$ are used for all collision energies.
- The freeze-out energy density is set to $e_{frz}=0.4$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- The pure SMASH result of $\kappa\sigma^2$ is positive and close to zero at 3 GeV, differing from the negative value observed by STAR.

## Context

This work extends hybrid model calculations of baryon number fluctuations (e.g., Vovchenko (2021) using MUSIC) by incorporating a CEP-containing equation of state from the rPNJL model into viscous hydrodynamics. The results contradict equilibrium model predictions (Li (2018)) that $\kappa\sigma^2$ would show a peak around 2-5 GeV when the freeze-out line crosses the phase boundary. The study enables future investigations of dynamical initial conditions and viscous corrections to better understand the discrepancy between theoretical predictions and STAR experimental data at low collision energies.
