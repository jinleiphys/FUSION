# D mesons in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter

> We study the in-medium properties of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons in isospin asymmetric hyperonic matter arising due to their interactions with the light hadrons. The interactions of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons with these light hadrons are derived by generalizing the chiral SU(3) model used for the study of hyperonic matter to SU(4). The nucleons, the scalar isoscalar meson, $σ$ and the scalar-isovector meson, $δ$ as modified in the strange hadronic matter, modify the masses of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons. It is found that as compared to the $\bar{D}$ mesons, the $D$ meson properties are more sensitive to the isospin asymmetry at high densities. The effects of strangeness in the medium on the properties of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons are studied in the present investigation. The $D$ mesons ($D^0$,$D^+$) are found to undergo larger medium modifications as compared to $\bar{D}$ mesons ($\bar {D^0}$, $D^-$) with the strangeness fraction, $f_s$ and these modifications are observed to be more appreciable at high densities. The present study of the in-medium properties of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons will be of relevance for the experiments in the future Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research, GSI, where the baryonic matter at high densities will be produced. The isospin asymmetric effects in the doublet $D = (D^{0}, D^{+})$ in the strange hadronic matter should show in observables like their production and flow in asymmetric heavy-ion collisions as well as in $J/ψ$ suppression.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons are modified in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter through interactions with nucleons and scalar mesons, with the chiral SU(3) model generalized to SU(4) flavor symmetry; the abstract's claim that $D$ mesons undergo larger medium modifications than $\bar{D}$ mesons is conditional on the strangeness fraction $f_s$ and is more appreciable at high baryon densities.

## Method

The authors generalize the chiral SU(3) model, previously used for hyperonic matter, to SU(4) flavor symmetry to derive interactions of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons with light hadrons. They solve coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields $\sigma$, $\zeta$, and $\delta$ in the mean-field approximation at zero temperature, incorporating nucleons and hyperons ($\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$) in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter. The dispersion relations for $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons are then solved using self-energies that include vectorial Weinberg-Tomozawa terms, scalar exchange terms, and range terms.

## Key numbers

- The resonance $\Lambda_c(2593)$ in the I=0 channel is generated 45 MeV below its free space position at nuclear matter density and zero temperature.
- The resonance $\Sigma_c(2770)$ in the I=1 channel is generated 40 MeV below its free space position at nuclear matter density and zero temperature.
- At finite temperature $T = 100$ MeV, the $\Lambda_c$ resonance position shifts to 2579 MeV and the $\Sigma_c$ resonance position shifts to 2767 MeV, moving closer to their free space values.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $D$ meson properties in symmetric hot nuclear matter using the SU(4) model (Kumar and Mishra, 2010) and in asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature (Kumar and Mishra, 2010) to include strangeness via hyperons. It contrasts with coupled channel approaches (Mizutani and others, 2006) that found repulsive mass shifts for $\bar{D}$ mesons, whereas the present model yields small attractive mass shifts that could allow charmed mesic nuclei. The results are relevant for interpreting $J/\psi$ suppression and $D$ meson production in asymmetric heavy-ion collisions at the future Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research at GSI.
