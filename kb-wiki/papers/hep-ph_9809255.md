# Valence Quark Spin Distribution Functions

> The hyperfine interactions of the constituent quark model provide a natural explanation for many nucleon properties, including the Delta-N splitting, the charge radius of the neutron, and the observation that the proton's quark distribution function ratio d(x)/u(x)->0 as x->1. The hyperfine-perturbed quark model also makes predictions for the nucleon spin-dependent distribution functions. Precision measurements of the resulting asymmetries A_1^p(x) and A_1^n(x) in the valence region can test this model and thereby the hypothesis that the valence quark spin distributions are "normal".

## Key claim

The hyperfine-perturbed constituent quark model makes specific predictions for the spin-dependent asymmetries A1^p(x) and A1^n(x) in the valence region (x > 0.3), and precision measurements of these asymmetries can test whether the valence quark spin distributions are "normal", meaning consistent with the conservative scenario where valence quarks carry the spin expected by the quark model, with relativistic quenching reducing the valence spin sum from 1 to about 0.75.

## Method

The author assumes that hyperfine interactions (the Fermi contact part of the spin-spin interaction responsible for the Delta-N splitting) are responsible for SU(6)-breaking in the nucleon wavefunction, and normalizes predictions to the observed d(x)/u(x) ratio as x approaches 1. Using the hyperfine-perturbed quark model wavefunction with a mixing angle sin theta_m approximately -0.23 between the symmetric [56,0+] and mixed-symmetry [70,0+] states, the author derives the spin-dependent valence quark distributions and the resulting asymmetries A1^p(x) and A1^n(x) using rough parameterizations d(x)/u(x) approximately kappa(1-x) with kappa between 0.5 and 0.6 and c_A(x)=nx(1-x)^n with n between 2 and 4.

## Key numbers

- The integrated valence spin sum Sigma_v is approximately 0.75, representing a 25% relativistic quenching from the naive quark model value of unity
- The mixing angle sin theta_m is approximately -0.23, as calculated in the hyperfine-perturbed quark model by Isgur, Karl, and Koniuk (1978)
- As x approaches 1, both A1^p(x) and A1^n(x) tend to 1, and the ratio d(x)/u(x) approaches 0 with the parameter kappa between 0.5 and 0.6
- The Bjorken sum rule is satisfied with Delta u_v approximately +4/5 G_A and Delta d_v approximately -1/5 G_A, where the nucleon expectation value of (1-c_A(x)) is approximately 0.75

## Context

This work extends the hyperfine-perturbed quark model approach of Isgur, Karl, and Koniuk (1978) and the earlier SU(6)-breaking arguments of Feynman (1972), Close (1973), and Carlitz and Kaur (1977), as well as the mixed-symmetry wavefunction formalism of Le Yaouanc, Oliver, Pene, and Raynal (1975). The paper argues that previous attempts at "absolute" calculations of structure functions produced a wide range of predictions, whereas normalizing to the observed d(x)/u(x) ratio yields narrow prediction bands for the asymmetries. Precision measurements of A1^p(x) and A1^n(x) in the valence region at Jefferson Lab (CEBAF) can determine whether the valence spin structure is anomalous, thereby guiding the search for the resolution of the "proton spin crisis."
