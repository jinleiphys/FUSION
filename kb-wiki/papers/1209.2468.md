# The nuclear electric polarizability of 6He

> We present an estimate of the nuclear electric polarizability of the 6He halo nucleus based on six-body microscopic calculations. Wave functions are obtained from semi-realistic two-body interactions using the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method. The polarizability is calculated as a sum rule of the dipole response function using the Lanczos algorithm and also by integrating the photo-absorption cross section calculated via the Lorentz integral transform method. We obtain alpha_E=1.00(14) fm^3, which is much smaller than the published value 1.99(40) fm^3 extracted from experimental data. This points towards a potential disagreement between microscopic theories and experimental observations.

## Key claim

The microscopic six-body calculation yields a nuclear electric polarizability for ⁶He of α_E = 1.00(14) fm³, which is about a factor of two smaller than the published experimental value of 1.99(40) fm³ extracted from Coulomb breakup data, pointing to a potential disagreement between microscopic theory and experiment.

## Method

Wave functions for the six-body ⁶He system were obtained using the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method with semi-realistic two-body interactions (Minnesota, MTI-III, and AV4' potentials). The polarizability was calculated both as a sum rule of the dipole response function using the Lanczos algorithm and by integrating the photo-absorption cross section obtained via the Lorentz integral transform method, with the final estimate derived from a linear correlation between α_E and the neutron skin radius extrapolated to infinite model space.

## Key numbers

- α_E(⁶He) = 1.00(14) fm³ (theoretical estimate from this work)
- α_E_exp(⁶He) = 1.99(40) fm³ (experimental value from Moro (2009) extracted from Aumann et al. Coulomb breakup data)
- α_E(⁴He) from Minnesota potential (u=1) at K_max=12/13: 0.06360 fm³ (Lanczos method) and 0.06336 fm³ (integrating σ_γ to 120 MeV)
- α_E(⁴He) from realistic forces: 0.0683(8)(14) fm³ (Stetcu (2007) with EFT potentials) and 0.0655(4) fm³ (Gazit (2006) with Argonne v18 + Urbana IX)
- For ⁶He with Minnesota potential u=1.05 at K_max=12/13: α_E = 0.7542 fm³ (Lanczos); integrating σ_γ to 40 MeV gives 0.7711 fm³, to 60 MeV gives 0.7827 fm³
- Extrapolated neutron skin radius r_skin(∞) = 0.87(5) fm for ⁶He with Minnesota u=1.05
- Experimental skin radius range for ⁶He: 0.52 ≤ r_skin_exp ≤ 0.62 fm (inferred from Tan92, Alk97, Kis05 matter radii and Maxime proton radius)
- Two-neutron separation energy S_2n for Minnesota u=1.05: 1.15 MeV (calculated); experimental S_2n ≈ 0.975 MeV
- At ω̄ = 8 MeV the m_(-2) sum rule for ⁶He is exhausted only to 75%; at 12.3 MeV to 90%; at 40 MeV to 98%

## Context

This work extends microscopic calculations of nuclear electric polarizabilities from ³He and ⁴He (Stetcu 2007; Gazit 2006) to the halo nucleus ⁶He, which had no previous ab-initio prediction. The result contradicts the experimental extraction by Moro (2009) from Aumann et al. Coulomb breakup data, similar to the known discrepancy for ³He where ion scattering data (Goe91) disagree with photo-absorption estimates by about a factor of two. The correlation found between α_E and the neutron skin radius for ⁶He parallels studies for ²⁰⁸Pb (Pb_theory; Shlomo) within nuclear density functional theory. Future work should extend these calculations to realistic potentials including three-body forces and seek additional experimental measurements of α_E.
