# Elastic photon-initiated production at the LHC: the role of hadron-hadron interactions

> We analyse in detail the role of additional hadron-hadron interactions in elastic photon-initiated (PI) production at the LHC, both in $pp$ and heavy ion collisions. We first demonstrate that the source of difference between our predictions and other results in the literature for PI muon pair production is dominantly due to an unphysical cut that is imposed in these latter results on the dimuon-hadron impact parameter. We in addition show that this is experimentally disfavoured by the shape of the muon kinematic distributions measured by ATLAS in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions. We then consider the theoretical uncertainty due to the survival probability for no additional hadron-hadron interactions, and in particular the role this may play in the tendency for the predicted cross sections to lie somewhat above ATLAS data on PI muon pair production, in both $pp$ and PbPb collisions. This difference is relatively mild, at the $\sim 10\%$ level, and hence a very good control over the theory is clearly required. We show that this uncertainty is very small, and it is only by taking very extreme and rather unphysical variations in the modelling of the survival factor that this tension can be removed. This underlines the basic, rather model independent, point that a significant fraction of elastic PI scattering occurs for hadron-hadron impact parameters that are simply outside the range of QCD interactions, and hence this sets a lower bound on the survival factor in any physically reasonable approach. Finally, other possible origins for this discrepancy are discussed.

## Key claim

The dominant source of difference between the authors' predictions and other results in the literature for photon-initiated muon pair production is an unphysical cut on the dimuon-hadron impact parameter imposed in those other results; this cut is experimentally disfavored by ATLAS data on muon kinematic distributions in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions, and the remaining mild tension between the authors' predictions and ATLAS data (at the ~10% level) cannot be resolved by reasonable variations in the survival factor modeling.

## Method

The authors calculate elastic photon-initiated (PI) production using the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) at the amplitude level in impact parameter space, accounting for the survival factor (probability of no additional inelastic hadron-hadron interactions) via the opacity function. They implement a cut on the individual impact parameter between the produced dimuon system and each hadron (requiring b_i⊥ > R_A) to reproduce the approach used in the STARlight generator and other literature, and compare the resulting predictions to ATLAS data on muon pair production in both pp and PbPb collisions. The survival factor uncertainty is evaluated by varying the modeling of the opacity and considering extreme variations out to impact parameters of ~3 R_A.

## Key numbers

- ATLAS fiducial cross section for exclusive dimuon production in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV: 34.1 ± 0.4 (stat.) ± 0.7 (syst.) μb, with muon requirements pT^μ > 4 GeV, |η^μ| < 2.4, m_μμ > 10 GeV, pT^μμ < 2 GeV
- The b_i⊥ > R_A cut reduces the SuperChic prediction (excluding survival effects) by ~30%, from 52.2 μb to 37.1 μb
- Including survival effects with the cut gives 29.9 μb; without the cut gives 38.9 μb; including QED final-state radiation gives 37.3 μb
- The survival factor for PbPb collisions is ~70-80%, with precise value depending on process and kinematics
- Reasonable model variations in the survival factor affect predictions at the less than 1% level
- Only by including survival probability corresponding to inelastic interactions occurring with unit probability out to impact parameters b_i⊥ ~ 3 R_A does the ATLAS data begin to be matched

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of survival factors in photon-initiated production at the LHC (Harland-Lang, 2020 for pp collisions; Harland-Lang, 2018 for PbPb collisions) implemented in the SuperChic MC generator. It directly addresses discrepancies between SuperChic predictions and results from the STARlight MC generator (Baltz, 2009) and the approach of Dyndal (2014), demonstrating that the dominant difference arises from an unphysical impact parameter cut in those approaches. The findings enable improved precision for BSM constraints using photon-initiated processes at the LHC, particularly for measurements of lepton pair production and light-by-light scattering, and clarify that the survival factor uncertainty is too small to explain the ~10% tension with ATLAS data, motivating investigation of other sources such as higher-order QED effects and final-state photon emission.
