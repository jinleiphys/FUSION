# Astrophysically Relevant Neutron Capture near the Border of Stability

> The neutron capture cross section on $^{26}$Mg was measured in the astrophysically relevant energy region from 25 keV to 220 keV. The experimental results agree well with a calculation using the Direct Capture (DC) model together with systematic folding potentials. This experiment confirms for the $^{26}$Mg(n,$γ$)$^{27}$Mg reaction that the DC process is at least comparable to the Compound--Nucleus (CN) process. The reliability of DC calculations is discussed, and we present some ideas for future experiments which could reduce the theoretical uncertainties of DC calculations.

## Key claim

The neutron capture cross section on $^{26}$Mg was measured from 25 keV to 220 keV, and the experimental results agree well with a Direct Capture (DC) model calculation using systematic folding potentials; this confirms that the DC process is at least comparable to the Compound-Nucleus (CN) process for this reaction, though the reliability of DC calculations depends on input parameters such as spectroscopic factors, spins, parities, and excitation energies of final states.

## Method

The activation method was used, employing the fast cyclic activation technique at the Van de Graaff accelerator in Karlsruhe: a 98.79% enriched $^{26}$MgO sample was irradiated with neutrons generated by the $^7$Li(p,n)$^7$Be reaction at proton energies near threshold (1881 keV) to produce quasi-thermal spectra (kT ≈ 25 keV) and at higher energies for spectra with 20–30 keV width. The cross section was measured relative to the well-known $^{197}$Au(n,γ)$^{198}$Au cross section using thin gold foils, and the γ-rays from the decay of $^{27}$Mg (halflife 9.458 ± 0.012 min) were detected with a high-purity germanium detector. The theoretical analysis used the DC model with systematic folding potentials, where the potential strength parameter λ was adjusted to the binding energy and scattering length, and spectroscopic factors were taken from a $^{26}$Mg(d,p)$^{27}$Mg experiment.

## Key numbers

- Neutron capture cross section on $^{26}$Mg measured in the energy range from 25 keV to 220 keV.
- The $^{27}$Mg residual nucleus has a halflife of 9.458 ± 0.012 min.
- γ-ray energies from $^{27}$Mg decay: 844 keV (branching 71.8 ± 0.4%) and 1014 keV (28.0 ± 0.4%).
- The $^{26}$Mg sample was 98.79% enriched, with a weight of 90 mg and diameter 6 mm.
- The $^7$Li(p,n)$^7$Be reaction threshold is at proton energy 1881 keV.
- The p-wave potential had to be reduced by about 15% in strength relative to the s-wave potential to describe the data.
- Spectroscopic factors for the ground state (1/2$^+$) of $^{27}$Mg range from 0.57 to 1.07 (experimental) and 0.43 to 0.70 (shell-model theoretical).
- Spectroscopic factors for the 984.7 keV state (3/2$^+$) range from 0.37 to 0.80 (experimental) and 0.28 to 0.45 (theoretical).
- Spectroscopic factors for the 1698.0 keV state (5/2$^+$) range from 0.13 to 0.31 (experimental) and 0.02 to 0.14 (theoretical).
- Spectroscopic factor for the 3559.5 keV state (3/2$^-$) is 0.40–0.56 (experimental) and 0.37 (derived from (n,γ)).
- Spectroscopic factor for the 3760.4 keV state (7/2$^-$) is 0.55–0.80 (experimental).
- Spectroscopic factor for the 4149.8 keV state ((5/2$^+$)) is 0.03 (experimental) and 0.001–0.06 (theoretical).
- Spectroscopic factor for the 4827.3 keV state ((1/2$^-$)) is 0.32 (experimental) and 0.19 (derived from (n,γ)).

## Context

This work extends a series of DC measurements on neutron-rich light and intermediate nuclei (e.g., $^{15}$N, $^{18}$O, $^{36}$S, $^{48}$Ca) performed at the Karlsruhe Van de Graaff accelerator, and it confirms the importance of the DC process for neutron capture near the border of stability. The results contradict the assumption in statistical Hauser-Feshbach (HF) model calculations for r-process nucleosynthesis (as noted by Goriely (1996)) that compound-nucleus formation dominates, showing instead that DC can be at least comparable. The paper enables future experiments using (d,p) reactions with accelerated neutron-rich fission fragments in inverse kinematics to determine spins, parities, excitation energies, and spectroscopic factors for unstable nuclei, which would reduce theoretical uncertainties in DC calculations for r-process capture cross sections.
