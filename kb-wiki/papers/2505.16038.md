# Initial-State Charge Density Predicts Final-State Net Charge Flow in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We propose a new class of charge-conjugation-odd flow observables and use them to investigate the dynamics of conserved currents in simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Inspired by the success of the initial energy and momentum distributions at predicting final-state anisotropic flow, we construct systematically-improvable initial-state estimators for final net-charge flow observables, which we validate with numerical simulations. This opens the possibility of a multitude of new charge-dependent probes of heavy-ion collisions of different systems and energies.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the event-by-event net-proton elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions is well predicted by the initial baryon density distribution, using a new class of charge-conjugation-odd flow observables and systematically-improvable initial-state estimators. The claim is hedged by the condition that the estimator performance decreases for more peripheral centralities, possibly due to non-linear corrections and large statistical uncertainties from few antiprotons.

## Method
The authors constructed initial-state estimators for net-charge flow by decomposing a generating function into discrete cumulants ordered by length scale, using a ratio of antisymmetrized (charge conjugation odd) and symmetrized cumulants. They validated the framework using event-by-event hybrid hydrodynamic simulations with the superMC+MUSIC+UrQMD model, which includes fluctuating Glauber initial conditions with nonzero baryon currents. The quality of the estimator was quantified via the linear Pearson correlation coefficient between the proposed net eccentricity estimator and the net proton elliptic flow vector.

## Key numbers
- Linear correlation coefficient Q2 between net elliptic flow and the proposed net eccentricity estimator is greater than 0.9 for central and mid-central collisions.
- The traditional eccentricity estimator shows a very small Q2 value for net elliptic flow, in contrast to the proposed estimator.
- The estimator performance decreases for more peripheral centralities, with large statistical uncertainty attributed to the small number of antiprotons.

## Context
This work extends the successful initial-state estimator paradigm previously applied to energy density eccentricities (Teaney 2010, Gardim 2011, Noronha-Hostler 2015) and initial momentum degrees of freedom (Sousa 2024) to encompass conserved charges like baryon number. It builds on recent relativistic viscous hydrodynamic calculations exploring baryon diffusion at low center-of-mass energy (Denicol 2018, Du 2024) and fluctuations of BSQ charges at high energy (Carzon 2019, Martinez 2019). The framework enables new charge-dependent probes of heavy-ion collisions across different systems and energies, and provides a baseline to isolate signatures of the QCD critical point.
