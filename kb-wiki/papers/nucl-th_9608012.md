# Quasielastic K-nucleus scattering

> Quasielastic K^+ - nucleus scattering data at q=290, 390 and 480 MeV/c are analyzed in a finite nucleus continuum random phase approximation framework, using a density-dependent particle-hole interaction. The reaction mechanism is consistently treated according to Glauber theory, keeping up to two-step inelastic processes. A good description of the data is achieved, also providing a useful constraint on the strength of the effective particle-hole interaction in the scalar-isoscalar channel at intermediate momentum transfers. We find no evidence for the increase in the effective number of nucleons participating in the reaction which has been reported in the literature.

# Quasielastic K-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
The puzzling increase in the effective number of nucleons participating in the reaction reported in prior analyses is very likely an artifact of the naive fitting procedure, and a realistic theoretical framework without free parameters gives an accurate description of the experimental cross sections. The authors find no evidence for the enhancement of the in-medium K+N cross section that had been inferred from earlier elastic scattering data.

## Method
The authors analyzed quasielastic K+-nucleus double-differential cross section data at fixed momentum transfers of 290, 390, and 480 MeV/c using a finite nucleus continuum random phase approximation (RPA) framework with a density-dependent particle-hole interaction derived from a G-matrix calculation. The reaction mechanism was treated consistently according to Glauber theory, including up to two-step inelastic processes, and the nuclear response functions were calculated using a surface response formalism rather than the effective nucleon number approximation.

## Key numbers
- Momentum transfers analyzed: q = 290, 390, and 480 MeV/c, corresponding to K+ laboratory momentum of 705 MeV/c and scattering angles of 24°, 34°, and 43°
- The effective total K+N cross section varies from approximately 14 mb to 13 mb for energy losses up to 250 MeV at k = 705 MeV/c
- At q = 500 MeV/c, the relativistic position of the quasielastic peak is approximately 8 MeV below the non-relativistic one
- At q = 480 MeV/c, relativistic kinematics produces an enhancement of approximately 10% at the peak position of the scalar-isoscalar response
- For the S=T=0 mode at q = 480 MeV/c, the variation of the response function at the left of the quasielastic peak is typically 20-30%, growing to approximately 50% in the very low energy region (omega less than 30 MeV)
- For the other spin-isospin modes at the left of the quasielastic peak, the variation is always much smaller, approximately 5-10%

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous RPA+Glauber framework applied to (p,p') and (p,n) quasifree scattering (DePace 1993, 1995) to the case of K+-nucleus scattering. It directly addresses the puzzling results from BNL experiments (Kor 1993, 1995a) where the effective number of participating nucleons was found to be approximately 30% higher than Glauber theory predictions, and also relates to earlier observations that multiple scattering theory underestimates nuclear elastic scattering data (Mar 1982, Sie 1984). The analysis enables future use of K+-nucleus quasielastic scattering as a clean probe of the scalar-isoscalar particle-hole interaction at intermediate momentum transfers, since the K+N interaction is much weaker than other hadron-nucleon interactions and the scalar-isoscalar channel is largely dominant.
