# Experimental constraints on shallow heating in accreting neutron-star crusts

> The observed thermal relaxation of transiently accreting neutron stars during quiescence periods in low-mass X-ray binaries suggests the existence of unknown heat sources in the shallow layers of neutron-star crust. Making use of existing experimental nuclear data, we estimate the maximum possible amount of heat that can be deposited in the outer crust of an accreting neutron star due to electron captures and pycnonuclear fusion reactions triggered by the burial of X-ray burst ashes.

# Experimental constraints on shallow heating in accreting neutron-star crusts

## Key claim
Using existing experimental nuclear data, the maximum possible heat deposited in the outer crust of accreting neutron stars from electron captures and pycnonuclear fusion can be up to about an order of magnitude larger than previously estimated, but these are upper limits that depend on uncertain initial compositions, neutrino losses, and reaction rates.

## Method
The authors derived analytical formulas for the Gibbs free energy per nucleon at fixed pressure, including electron capture thresholds, electrostatic corrections, and electron exchange/polarization effects. They applied these formulas to experimental nuclear masses from the Atomic Mass Evaluation and excitation energies from nuclear data services, considering both ground-state-to-ground-state and ground-state-to-excited-state transitions for various X-ray burst ash compositions.

## Key numbers
- Electron capture by 18O releases about 0.4 MeV per one accreted nucleon
- Pycnonuclear fusion of 12C + 12C at pressure 6.97 × 10^28 dyn cm^-2 and density 4.15 × 10^10 g cm^-3 releases up to 1.41 MeV per nucleon (0.64 MeV from fusion alone)
- Pycnonuclear fusion of 16O + 16O at pressure 2.72 × 10^28 dyn cm^-2 and density 2.05 × 10^10 g cm^-3 releases up to 1.16 MeV per nucleon (0.58 MeV from fusion alone)
- Pycnonuclear fusion of 16O + 16O via excited-state transitions at pressure 7.96 × 10^28 dyn cm^-2 and density 4.59 × 10^10 g cm^-3 releases up to 1.39 MeV per nucleon (0.60 MeV from fusion alone)
- Most heating is concentrated at densities 10^10 - 10^11 g cm^-3
- Analytical formulas agree with exact numerical solutions to within about 10^-3% for transition pressure and density, and 3.5 × 10^-4% for heat released (tested for 56Fe to 56Cr transition)

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Haensel and collaborators (1990, 2003, 2008) and Fantina (2018) that considered only pure 56Fe or 106Pd ashes, finding that individual heat sources can be an order of magnitude larger when realistic X-ray burst ash compositions are used. The results provide upper limits for the shallow heating required by cooling simulations of quasipersistent soft X-ray transients such as those studied by Brown (2009), Degenaar (2011-2019), and Parikh (2017-2019), but the large heat required for Aql X-1 and MAXI J0556-332 remains unexplained.
