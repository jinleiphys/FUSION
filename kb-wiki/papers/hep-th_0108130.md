# Character Expansions for the Orthogonal and Symplectic Groups

> Formulas for the expansion of arbitrary invariant group functions in terms of the characters for the Sp(2N), SO(2N+1), and SO(2N) groups are derived using a combinatorial method. The method is similar to one used by Balantekin to expand group functions over the characters of the U(N) group. All three expansions have been checked for all N by using them to calculate the known expansions of the generating function of the homogeneous symmetric functions. An expansion of the exponential of the traces of group elements, appearing in the finite-volume gauge field partition functions, is worked out for the orthogonal and symplectic groups.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit formulas for expanding arbitrary invariant group functions in terms of characters of the symplectic and orthogonal groups using a combinatorial method, but the expansions for SO(2N+1) and SO(2N) do not include the spinor representations, and the SO(2N) expansion is expressed in terms of double characters rather than simple characters when the last partition index is nonzero.

## Method

The authors extend Balantekin's combinatorial method for U(N) character expansions to the groups Sp(2N), SO(2N+1), and SO(2N). They define a generating function G(x,t) expanded as a power series, construct a function F from the Weyl denominator and products of G over eigenvalues and their reciprocals, and manipulate the resulting determinants to express F as a sum over characters with coefficients given by sums over products of the expansion coefficients A_n. The derivations proceed in parallel for all three groups, with the specific choices of a parameter q and sign in the Weyl denominator distinguishing the groups.

## Key numbers

- For Sp(2N): the character expansion coefficient is given by d_{n_j+N-j+1,i} = sum_{p=-∞}^{∞} A_p (A_{n_j+i-j+p} - A_{-n_j-2N-2+i+j+p}), with a simplified form for Taylor series: d = sum_{p=0}^{∞} A_p (A_{p+|n_j+i-j|} - A_{p+n_j+2N+2-i-j})
- For SO(2N+1): the coefficient is d_{n_j+N-j,i} = sum_{p=-∞}^{∞} A_p (A_{n_j+i-j+p} - A_{-n_j-2N-1+i+j+p}), with Taylor series simplification: d = sum_{p=0}^{∞} A_p (A_{p+|n_j+i-j|} - A_{p+n_j+2N+1-i-j})
- For SO(2N): the coefficient is d_{n_j+N-j,i} = sum_{p=-∞}^{∞} A_p (A_{n_j+i-j+p} + A_{-n_j-2N+i+j+p}), with Taylor series simplification: d = sum_{p=0}^{∞} A_p (A_{p+|n_j+i-j|} + A_{p+n_j+2N-i-j})
- For the generating function G(x,t)=1/(1-xt) of homogeneous symmetric functions, the Sp(2N) expansion yields det[d] = x^{n_1} for one-row representations, giving the known identity sum_{n=0}^{∞} x^n h_n(t_i, t_i^{-1})
- For the same generating function with SO(2N+1), the coefficient becomes det[d] = x^n/(1+x) for one-row representations, yielding sum_{n=0}^{∞} x^n h_n(t_i, t_i^{-1}, 1)
- For SO(2N), the coefficient becomes det[d] = 2x^n/(1-x^2) for one-row representations, with the factor of 1/2 in the expansion canceling to give the same result as SO(2N+1)

## Context

This work extends Balantekin's combinatorial method for U(N) character expansions (Balantekin, 1984; Balantekin, 2000) to the orthogonal and symplectic groups, which are needed for finite-volume QCD partition functions with Dyson index β=1 and β=4. For β=4, the Goldstone modes belong to the coset space SU(N_f)/SO(N_f) (Leutwyler, 1992; Smilga, 1995), and for β=1 the coset is SU(2N_f)/Sp(2N_f) (Leutwyler, 1992; Smilga, 1995). The derived expansions enable calculation of massive partition functions for random matrix ensembles with β=1 and β=4 (Akemann, 2000; Nagao, 2000) and may help find solutions of Virasoro constraints, which so far exist only for the β=2 case (Damgaard, 2000; Akemann, 2001; Dalmazi, 2001).
