# $E(5)$-like emerging $γ$-softness in $^{82}$Kr

> Recently the interacting bosom model with $SU(3)$ higher-order interactions was proposed by one of the authors, wherein unexpected $γ$-softness can be emerged in this new model. This stimulates further discussion on the connections of the new $γ$-softness and the realistic $γ$-soft nuclei. In this paper, $E(5)$-like $γ$-softness arises when the $SU(3)$ fourth-order interaction $\hat{C}_{2}^{2}[SU(3)]$ is considered. And the corresponding transitional behaviors are similar to that from the $U(5)$ limit to the $O(6)$ limit in previous IBM-1, which provides a novel perspective for understanding the new model. Low-lying spectra, $B(E2)$ values between the low-lying states and quadrupole moment of the first $2_{1}^{+}$ state are investigated. Experimental data of the $E(5)$-like nucleus $^{82}$Kr are compared with the calculated theoretical results, where the calculated low-lying level energies and the associated $B(E2)$ values fit very well with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper claims that adding the fourth-order SU(3) Casimir operator squared \(\hat{C}_{2}^{2}[SU(3)]\) to the SU3-IBM Hamiltonian can produce \(E(5)\)-like \(\gamma\)-softness, with transitional behaviors similar to the \(U(5)\) to \(O(6)\) evolution in standard IBM-1, but only under specific parameter choices (\(\xi=0.2232\), \(N=6\)) and with the hedge that the \(0_{2}^{+}\) and \(0_{3}^{+}\) state crossing occurs only when the third-order interaction \(\kappa\) is zero.

## Method
The authors used the interacting boson model with SU(3) higher-order interactions (SU3-IBM), adding the fourth-order Casimir operator \(\hat{C}_{2}^{2}[SU(3)]\) to the Hamiltonian that already contained the second-order and third-order Casimir operators. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian for boson number \(N=6\) and varied the control parameter \(\eta\) from 0 (spherical U(5) limit) to 1 (SU(3) limit), with fixed \(\xi=0.2232\) and either \(\kappa=0\) or \(\kappa=0.2\). They computed low-lying energy spectra, \(B(E2)\) transition rates using the quadrupole operator \(\hat{T}(E2)=e\hat{Q}\), and the quadrupole moment of the \(2_{1}^{+}\) state, then fitted the results to experimental data for \(^{82}\)Kr at \(\eta=0.41\).

## Key numbers
- The \(0_{2}^{+}\) and \(0_{3}^{+}\) states cross at \(\eta=0.5\) when \(\kappa=0\) and \(\xi=0.2232\) with \(N=6\).
- For \(^{82}\)Kr fitting, the control parameter is \(\eta=0.41\) with \(N=6\) and \(\xi=0.2232\).
- Total fitting parameter \(c = 1517.4\) keV for \(\kappa=0\) and \(c = 1486.6\) keV for \(\kappa=0.2\).
- Effective charges: \(e = 1.8037\) (W.u.)\(^{1/2}\) for \(\kappa=0\) and \(e = 1.8525\) (W.u.)\(^{1/2}\) for \(\kappa=0.2\).
- Calculated \(B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\rightarrow0_{1}^{+}) = 21.3\) W.u. for both \(\kappa=0\) and \(\kappa=0.2\), matching the experimental value of 21.3(10) W.u.
- Calculated \(B(E2;2_{2}^{+}\rightarrow2_{1}^{+}) = 34.6\) W.u. (\(\kappa=0\)) and 35.8 W.u. (\(\kappa=0.2\)), compared to experimental 34.6(63) W.u.
- Quadrupole moment of \(2_{1}^{+}\) state: \(-0.388\) eb for \(\kappa=0\) and \(0.071\) eb for \(\kappa=0.2\) at \(\eta=0.41\).

## Context
This work extends the SU3-IBM framework proposed by Wang (2022), which previously described \(\gamma\)-softness in \(^{110}\)Cd and \(^{196}\)Pt but suffered from excessive energy separation between the \(0_{2}^{+}\) and \(0_{3}^{+}\) states. The addition of the fourth-order interaction \(\hat{C}_{2}^{2}[SU(3)]]\) reduces this gap and produces transitional behavior analogous to the \(U(5)\) to \(O(6)\) critical point symmetry \(E(5)\) of Iachello (2000). The successful fit to \(^{82}\)Kr, previously identified as an \(E(5)\) candidate by Rajban (2021), enables future studies of traditional \(O(6)\)-like \(\gamma\)-soft nuclei such as \(^{196}\)Pt and of \(\gamma\)-soft behaviors in Xe and Zr isotopes that resist standard descriptions.
