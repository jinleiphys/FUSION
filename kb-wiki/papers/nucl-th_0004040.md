# On a Theory of Superfluidity of Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> Influence of asymmetry on superfluidity of nuclear matter with triplet-singlet pairing of nucleons (in spin and isospin spaces) is considered within the framework of a Fermi-liquid theory. Solutions of self-consistent equations for the critical temperature and the energy gap at T=0 are obtained. It is shown, that the backward influence of pairing correlations through the normal distribution functions of nucleons leads to the qualitative change: instead of two-valued behavior the energy gap has universal one-valued behavior. At T=0 superfluidity arises as a result of a first order phase transition in density.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when the feedback of pairing correlations on the normal distribution functions of nucleons is included self-consistently, the energy gap in asymmetric nuclear matter with triplet-singlet pairing changes from double-valued to universal one-valued behavior, and at T=0 superfluidity arises and disappears via a first-order phase transition in density.

## Method

The authors use Landau's semiphenomenological Fermi-liquid theory with Skyrme effective forces (SkP potential) to study triplet-singlet (S=1, T=0) pairing of nucleons in asymmetric nuclear matter. They solve self-consistent equations for the critical temperature and the energy gap at T=0, first with chemical potentials determined in the ideal Fermi-gas approximation (uncoupled calculation) and then with the full self-consistent treatment where the finite gap feeds back into the normal distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature phase curves show double-valued behavior for asymmetry parameter α < α_c ≈ 0.071; for α > α_c the curves become closed ovals, and TS superfluidity disappears for α > α_m ≈ 0.092.
- In the uncoupled calculation at T=0, the maximum asymmetry parameter for which TS superfluidity exists is α_m' ≈ 0.179.
- In the self-consistent calculation at T=0, the threshold asymmetry for superfluidity is reduced to α_m' ≈ 0.029.
- The ratio Δ/ε_F ≤ 0.12 for all densities considered.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of np pairing in nuclear matter that used the Thouless criterion (e.g., with Graz II and Paris potentials in Refs. [AFR, ARS]) or BCS theory with bare interactions (e.g., Ref. [SAL] with the Paris potential). The authors note that their results for the critical temperature qualitatively agree with Ref. [ARS] on ³D₂ pairing. The self-consistent treatment reveals new features, specifically the first-order phase transition in density and the drastic reduction of threshold asymmetry, that were not captured by earlier approaches using the Thouless criterion or bare interactions. This enables future study of multi-gap superfluidity in asymmetric nuclear matter and suggests possible experimental observation in intermediate-energy heavy ion reactions.
