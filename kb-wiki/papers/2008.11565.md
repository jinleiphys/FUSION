# Jacobi no-core shell model for $p$-shell hypernuclei

> We extend the recently developed Jacobi no-core shell model to hypernuclei. Based on the coefficients of fractional parentage for ordinary nuclei, we define a basis where the hyperon is the spectator particle. We then formulate transition coefficients to states that single out a hyperon-nucleon pair which allow us to implement a hypernuclear many-baryon Hamiltonian for $p$-shell hypernuclei. As a first application, we use the basis states and the transition coefficients to calculate the ground states of $^{4}_Λ$He, $^{4}_Λ$H, $^{5}_Λ$He, $^{6}_Λ$He, $^{6}_Λ$Li, and $^{7}_Λ$Li and, additionally, the first excited states of $^{4}_Λ$He, $^{4}_Λ$H, and $^{7}_Λ$Li. In order to obtain converged results, we employ the similarity renormalization group (SRG) to soften the nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions. Although the dependence on this evolution of the Hamiltonian is significant, we show that a strong correlation of the results can be used to identify preferred SRG parameters. This allows for meaningful predictions of hypernuclear binding and excitation energies. The transition coefficients will be made publicly available as HDF5 data files.

## Key claim

The Jacobi no-core shell model (J-NCSM) is extended to hypernuclei up to the $p$-shell, enabling efficient calculation of hypernuclear binding and excitation energies; however, the results show a significant dependence on the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution of the Hamiltonian, and the authors use a strong correlation between results to identify preferred SRG parameters rather than claiming fully converged, parameter-independent predictions.

## Method

The authors construct a translationally invariant many-baryon Hamiltonian in Jacobi relative coordinates, using fully antisymmetrized nucleonic states for the $(A-1)$N core coupled to a hyperon spectator state. They formulate transition coefficients that single out either a nucleon-nucleon pair or a hyperon-nucleon pair from the basis, enabling evaluation of the Hamiltonian matrix elements. The nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions are softened via the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution, and the resulting Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a harmonic oscillator basis constrained by a maximum total oscillator quantum number $\mathcal{N}_{max}$.

## Key numbers

- Ground states calculated for $^{4}_{\Lambda}$He, $^{4}_{\Lambda}$H, $^{5}_{\Lambda}$He, $^{6}_{\Lambda}$He, $^{6}_{\Lambda}$Li, and $^{7}_{\Lambda}$Li, plus first excited states of $^{4}_{\Lambda}$He, $^{4}_{\Lambda}$H, and $^{7}_{\Lambda}$Li.
- The hyperon-nucleon interaction used is the next-to-leading order (NLO) chiral EFT potential from Polinder (2006) and Haidenbauer (2013, 2019).
- The basis states are constrained by $\mathcal{N} = \mathcal{N}_{A-1} + 2n_Y + l_Y \leq \mathcal{N}_{max}$, where $\mathcal{N}_{max}$ is the model space size.
- The total rest mass of the system is $M(t_Y) = (A-1)m_N + m(t_Y)$, with $m(t_Y=0) = m_{\Lambda}$ and $m(t_Y=1)$ the average of $\Sigma^+$, $\Sigma^-$, and $\Sigma^0$ masses.

## Context

This work extends the Jacobi NCSM approach previously applied to $s$-shell hypernuclei by Gazda (2015) and Wirth (2014) to the $p$-shell hypernuclei, building on the antisymmetrized nucleonic basis developed by Liebig (2015). It enables efficient repeated calculations needed for fitting interaction parameters to hypernuclear data, complementing methods such as Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for very light systems (Miyagawa 1993, Nogga 2001) and the standard NCSM with importance truncation (Wirth 2014, 2016, 2017). The transition coefficients will be made publicly available as HDF5 data files, facilitating future studies of hypernuclear structure with chiral EFT interactions.
