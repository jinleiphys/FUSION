# Error estimates for the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model

> There are many complementing strategies to estimate the extrapolation errors of a model which was calibrated in least-squares fits. We consider the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model for nuclear structure and dynamics and exemplify the following five strategies: uncertainties from statistical analysis, covariances between observables, trends of residuals, variation of fit data, dedicated variation of model parameters. This gives useful insight into the impact of the key fit data as they are: binding energies, charge r.m.s. radii, and charge formfactor. Amongst others, we check in particular the predictive value for observables in the stable nucleus $^{208}$Pb, the super-heavy element $^{266}$Hs, $r$-process nuclei, and neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while statistical error estimates from least-squares fitting provide a useful lower bound for extrapolation uncertainties in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, systematic errors, which are harder to quantify, can only be explored piecewise through complementary strategies such as varying fit data, comparing with experiment, and comparing different model variants.

## Method

The authors performed a series of least-squares fits of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model to a standard pool of nuclear data (binding energies, charge formfactor parameters, pairing gaps, and spin-orbit splittings) and then systematically omitted groups of fit data to produce variant parametrizations (SV-min, "no surf", "no rms", "no Formf", "E only"). They applied five error-estimation strategies: statistical uncertainty propagation from the covariance matrix, covariance analysis between observables, trends of residuals, dedicated variation of nuclear-matter properties (NMP) as model parameters, and variation of the fit data pool itself.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolation uncertainties for binding energy along the Sn isotope chain grow by a factor of 6–10 when moving from fit nuclei to neutron-rich r-process nuclei.
- For the fit "E only" (using only energy data), the uncertainty on charge r.m.s. radius is an order of magnitude larger than for the full fit SV-min.
- The neutron equation of state shows a "fixpoint" at density approximately 0.13 fm⁻³ where all parametrizations agree; the extrapolation uncertainty has a pronounced minimum near this density.
- The average deviation of the diffraction radius R_diffr,C for the "no Formf" and "E only" fits grows to about 0.07 fm⁻³, far larger than the allowed uncertainty from SV-min of about 0.02 fm⁻³.
- The fission barrier B_f in ²⁶⁶Hs shows the most pronounced correlation with the effective mass m*/m among all nuclear-matter properties.

## Context

This work extends the systematic error-analysis framework introduced by Dobaczewski et al. (2014) and applies it specifically to the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, building on earlier parametrization efforts such as Klüpfel et al. (2009) and Kortelainen et al. (2010). The paper enables more reliable extrapolations to exotic nuclei (r-process nuclei, super-heavy elements) and neutron-star properties by quantifying both statistical and systematic uncertainties, and it identifies specific deficiencies in the SHF model, such as the incompatibility between charge r.m.s. radius and diffraction radius data.
