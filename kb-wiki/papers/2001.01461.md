# Stringy excited baryons in holographic QCD

> We analyze excited baryon states using a holographic dual of QCD that is defined on the basis of an intersecting D4/D8-brane system. Studies of baryons in this model have been made by regarding them as a topological soliton of a gauge theory on a five-dimensional curved spacetime. However, this allows one to obtain only a certain class of baryons. We attempt to present a framework such that a whole set of excited baryons can be treated in a systematic way. This is achieved by employing the original idea of Witten, which states that a baryon is described by a system composed of $N_c$ open strings emanating from a baryon vertex. We argue that this system can be formulated by an ADHM-type matrix model of Hashimoto-Iizuka-Yi together with an infinite tower of the open string massive modes. Using this setup, we work out the spectra of excited baryons and compare them with the experimental data. In particular, we derive a formula of the nucleon Regge trajectory assuming that the excited nucleons lying the trajectory are characterized by the excitation of a single open string attached on the baryon vertex.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a complete set of excited baryon states in holographic QCD can be systematically described not by the topological soliton (instanton) approach, which only yields baryons with spin equal to isospin for Nf=2, but by a many-body quantum mechanics derived from open strings attached to a baryon vertex D4-brane, incorporating an infinite tower of massive open string modes.

## Method

The authors start from the D4/D8-brane holographic QCD model and introduce a baryon vertex as a D4-brane wrapping an S4 with Nc units of RR flux, forcing Nc open strings to stretch between it and the D8-branes. They take the large 't Hooft coupling limit (λ≫1) to approximate the curved background as flat spacetime, then perform light-cone quantization of the 9-5 strings (between D9 and D5 branes after T-duality) and 5-5 strings on the baryon vertex. The mass spectrum of open string states is classified by representations of SO(4)123z × SO(5)ỹ6789, and only states that are SO(4)6789 singlets, τ-parity even, and carry no momentum along the compactified directions are retained as candidates for QCD baryons.

## Key numbers

- The string length is set to α' = ls² = 27/(4λ) in units of MKK = 1, so stringy excitation masses are of order λ^(1/2)
- The 9-5 string ground state (N95=0) has mass squared = 0, belonging to (1/2,0) singlet of SO(4)123z × SO(5)ỹ6789
- The first excited 9-5 string states (N95=1/2) have mass squared = 1/(2ls²), classified as (0,1/2) vector ⊕ (1,1/2) singlet under SO(4)123z × SO(5)ỹ6789
- The first excited 5-5 string states (N55=1) have mass squared = 1/ls², and are rearranged into representations including (1,0)⊕(0,1) vector, (1/2,1/2) antisymmetric, and (0,0) rank-3 antisymmetric of SO(5)ỹ6789

## Context

This work extends the holographic baryon analysis of HSSY (2005) and HSS (2007), which used moduli space quantum mechanics of instanton solutions on D8-branes but only produced baryons with I=J for Nf=2. It follows the approach of ISS (2009) for mesons, where excited open string modes on D8-branes gave linear Regge trajectories. The framework builds on Witten's original baryon vertex idea (1998) and the ADHM-type matrix model of Hashimoto, Iizuka, and Yi (2010). This enables systematic treatment of baryons with I≠J and higher-spin excitations, including derivation of the nucleon Regge trajectory from single open string excitation on the baryon vertex.
