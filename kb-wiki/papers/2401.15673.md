# Using the Transition-Density Formalism in the First Computation of 4He Compton Scattering

> 6 pages LaTeX2e (pdflatex) including 4 figures as 6 .pdf files using includegraphics and webofc class. Contribution to the Proceedings of the 16th International Conference on Meson-Nucleon Physics and the Structure of the Nucleon (MENU 2023), Mainz 16-20 October 2023

## Key claim

The first theory description of 4He Compton scattering at nuclear energies is presented, using the transition-density formalism within Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChiEFT) with explicit Delta degrees of freedom, applicable between about 50 and 130 MeV. The results compare well to data from HIgammaS, MAXlab and Illinois, but only in the range where the intermediate four-nucleon system predominantly propagates incoherently with minor rescattering effects, which is justified at omega greater than about 50 MeV.

## Method

The authors factorise the Compton scattering interaction on 4He into an interaction kernel between photons and active nucleons (one or two nucleons that directly interact) and a backdrop of spectator nucleons described by one- and two-body transition densities. The Compton kernels are the same as those used previously for proton, neutron, deuteron and 3He Compton scattering in ChiEFT at order O(e^2 delta^3) [N3LO], where delta is approximately 0.4. The one- and two-body densities for 4He are generated from several chiral semilocal momentum-space regularised (SMS) potentials with cutoffs of 400, 450, 500 and 550 MeV, and the kernel convolutions involve sums and integrals over the undetected one- and two-nucleon quantum numbers and momenta.

## Key numbers

* Isoscalar scalar polarisabilities extracted from deuteron data: alpha_E1^(s) = 11.1 +/- 0.6_stat +/- 0.2_BSigmaR +/- 0.8_th, beta_M1^(s) = 3.4 -/+ 0.6_stat +/- 0.2_BSigmaR +/- 0.8_th (units not stated explicitly but standard are 10^(-4) fm^3)
* The ChiEFT treatment at N3LO has less-than-10% uncertainties from residual dependence on chiral 2N and 3N interactions, with an even smaller lower-bound uncertainty of about +/-6% at the highest energies
* The incoherent-propagation assumption is justified at omega greater than about 50 MeV
* alpha_E1^(s) + beta_M1^(s) = 14.5 +/- 0.4 from the Baldin Sum Rule
* The small expansion parameter delta = sqrt(m_pi/Lambda_chi) approximately equal to Delta/Lambda_chi approximately 0.4, with Delta approximately 300 MeV and Lambda_chi approximately 700 MeV
* At 60 MeV, the average 4He cross section is nearly 7 times that of the proton and deuteron; at 120 MeV, nearly 5 times

## Context

This work extends the ChiEFT Compton scattering program from the proton, deuteron, and 3He targets (previously computed by Griesshammer (2012), Choudhury (2007), Shukla (2008), and Margaryan (2018)) to the 4He nucleus, using the transition-density method introduced by Griesshammer (2020). The results enable experimental planning at HIgammaS and other facilities for extracting scalar-isoscalar nucleon polarisabilities from 4He data, which is a perfect scalar-isoscalar target free of contamination from spin polarisabilities. Work is under way to include rescattering effects (Walet, 2023) and update Compton kernels to N4LO for high-accuracy polarisability extractions.
