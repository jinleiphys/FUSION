# Finite-size scaling in nucleon axial charge from 2+1-flavor DWF lattice QCD

> We report the current status of the on-going lattice-QCD calculations of nucleon isovector axial charge, g_A, using the RBC/UKQCD 2+1-flavor dynamical domain-wall fermion ensembles at lattice cutoff of about a^{-1}=1.4 GeV in a spatial volume (L = 4.6 fm)^3. The result from the ensemble with m_π= 250 MeV pion mass, corresponding to the finite-size scaling parameter m_πL \sim 5.8, agrees well with an earlier result at a^{-1}=1.7 GeV, L = 2.8 fm, and m_π= 420 MeV, with similar m_πL. This suggests the systematic error from excited-state contamination is small in both ensembles and about 10-% deficit in g_A we are observing is likely a finite-size effect that scales with m_πL. We also report the result from the lighter, m_π= 170 MeV ensemble.

# Finite-size scaling in nucleon axial charge from 2+1-flavor DWF lattice QCD

## Key claim

The observed approximately 10% deficit in the nucleon isovector axial charge \(g_A\) relative to the experimental value is likely a finite-size effect scaling with the dimensionless parameter \(m_\pi L\), rather than being caused by excited-state contamination. This claim is supported by the agreement between two ensembles with very different lattice parameters but similar \(m_\pi L \sim 5.8\).

## Method

The authors used RBC/UKQCD 2+1-flavor dynamical domain-wall fermion (DWF) ensembles with a lattice cutoff of \(a^{-1} = 1.371(8)\) GeV and spatial volume \((L = 4.6\ \mathrm{fm})^3\), generated with a combined Iwasaki gauge action and dislocation-suppressing determinant ratio (DSDR) factor. Nucleon three-point and two-point correlators were computed using Gaussian-smeared sources with optimized smearing width and source-sink separation to control excited-state contamination, and the isovector axial charge was extracted from the ratio of axial to vector local currents.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: \(m_N = 1.054(7)\) GeV for the \(m_\pi = 250\) MeV ensemble; \(m_N = 0.984(8)\) GeV for the \(m_\pi = 170\) MeV ensemble.
- Isovector vector charge: \(g_V = 1.450(4)\) for the 250 MeV ensemble; \(g_V = 1.447(9)\) for the 170 MeV ensemble; chiral-limit extrapolation gives \(g_V^{-1} = 0.692(7)\), consistent with meson-sector renormalization factors \(Z_V = 0.673(8)\) and \(Z_A = 0.6878(3)\).
- Ratio \(g_A/g_V = 1.15(5)\) for the 250 MeV ensemble (\(m_\pi L \sim 5.8\)); \(g_A/g_V = 1.12(10)\) for the 170 MeV ensemble (\(m_\pi L \sim 4\)).
- Earlier result at \(a^{-1} = 1.7\) GeV, \(L = 2.8\) fm, \(m_\pi = 420\) MeV gives \(g_A/g_V = 1.19(4)\) at similar \(m_\pi L \sim 5.8\).
- Experimental value of \(g_A/g_V\) is 1.2701(25); the observed deficit is approximately 10%.

## Context

This work extends the RBC/UKQCD collaboration's ongoing lattice-QCD calculations of nucleon structure (Yamazaki 2008, Yamazaki 2009, Aoki 2010, Ohta 2010, Lin 2011, Ohta 2011) to lighter pion masses and larger volumes. The results contradict the suggestion by Wittig (2012) and Capitani (2012) that the observed deficit in \(g_A\) could be explained by excited-state contamination. The finite-size scaling interpretation provides the first concrete lattice evidence for the virtual pion cloud surrounding the nucleon, and motivates future calculations at physical pion mass with even larger spatial extents to fully contain the axial current.
