# Two-loop massive scalar three-point function in a dispersive approach

> We present a dispersion relation formalism to calculate a massive scalar two-loop vertex function. Such calculation is of direct relevance in the evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon's anomalous magnetic moment due to meson poles. The discontinuity of the two-loop diagram is obtained by a sum of two- and three-particle cut contributions, which involve a phase space integration over the physical intermediate states. The real part of the vertex function is subsequently reconstructed through evaluation of a dispersion integral. We explicitly demonstrate that the dispersive formalism yields exactly the same result as the direct two-loop calculation.

# Two-loop massive scalar three-point function in a dispersive approach

## Key claim

The dispersive formalism, which reconstructs the real part of a massive scalar two-loop vertex function from its absorptive parts via dispersion integrals, yields exactly the same result as the direct two-loop calculation when all two- and three-particle cut contributions are summed, provided the internal lines have different masses. This equivalence holds for the scalar vertex case and serves as a validation step toward applying the method to the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon's anomalous magnetic moment, where off-shell ambiguities in composite vertices are avoided.

## Method

The authors compute the absorptive parts of two topologically distinct scalar two-loop three-point functions by applying Cutkosky rules to obtain two-particle and three-particle cut contributions. The two-particle cuts involve one-loop triangle and box subdiagrams, while the three-particle cuts involve phase-space integrations over physical intermediate states. The real part of each vertex function at zero momentum transfer is then reconstructed via dispersion integrals over the cut thresholds, and the results are compared with a direct two-loop evaluation using a hyperspherical (Gegenbauer polynomial) approach.

## Key numbers

- The two-particle cut thresholds are at $(\L_1 + M)^2$ for the first diagram and $(\L_3 + M)^2$ for the second diagram, where $\L_1$, $\L_2$, $\L_3$ are photon masses and $M$ is the meson mass.
- The three-particle cut thresholds are at $(\L_1 + \L_2 + \L_3)^2$ for both diagrams, and additionally at $(M + 2m)^2$ for the "lepton-lepton-meson" cut in the second diagram, where $m$ is the lepton mass.
- The demonstration mass configuration used is $\L_1 = 0$, $\L_2 = m$, $\L_3 = 3m$, and $M = 5m$.
- Cusps in the three-particle cut contributions appear exactly at the positions where the two-particle cuts start, corresponding to the opening of new two-particle ("meson-photon") channels.

## Context

This work extends the unitarity-based dispersion method for two-loop vertex functions, previously applied to massless diagrams by van Neerven (1985) and to the Higgs decay $H \to \gamma\gamma$ by Korner (1995), to the case of massive diagrams with different masses for photons, muons, and mesons. The formalism is a first step toward a dispersive evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon's anomalous magnetic moment, where meson-pole exchanges with form factors introduce off-shell ambiguities in direct loop calculations. The approach enables future calculations that avoid such ambiguities by using only on-shell intermediate states, as will be presented in a subsequent work by the same authors.
