# Phenomenological Lambda-Nuclear Interactions

> Variational Monte Carlo calculations for ${_Λ^4}H$ (ground and excited states) and ${_Λ^5}He$ are performed to decipher information on $Λ$-nuclear interactions. Appropriate operatorial nuclear and $Λ$-nuclear correlations have been incorporated to minimize the expectation values of the energies. We use the Argonne $\upsilon_{18}$ two-body NN along with the Urbana IX three-body NNN interactions. The study demonstrates that a large part of the splitting energy in ${_Λ^4}H$ ($0^+-1^+$) is due to the three-body $Λ$ NN forces. $_Λ^{17}O$ hypernucleus is analyzed using the {\it s}-shell results. $Λ$ binding to nuclear matter is calculated within the variational framework using the Fermi-Hypernetted-Chain technique. There is a need to correctly incorporate the three-body $Λ$ NN correlations for $Λ$ binding to nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that a large part (approximately 50% to 75%) of the 0⁺–1⁺ splitting energy in ⁴_ΛH is due to three-body ΛNN forces, contrary to earlier studies that attributed most of the splitting to the spin dependence of the two-body ΛN potential. This conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of spin-average strength \(\bar V\) = 6.15 or 6.20 MeV and the inclusion of appropriate ΛNN correlations.

## Method

Variational Monte Carlo calculations were performed for ⁴_ΛH (ground and excited 1⁺ states) and ⁵_ΛHe using the Argonne υ₁₈ two-body NN interaction and the Urbana IX three-body NNN interaction, together with phenomenological two-body ΛN and three-body ΛNN potentials. The trial wave function incorporated operatorial nuclear and Λ-nuclear correlations including spin, tensor, spin-orbit, and three-body terms, with variational parameters optimized to minimize energy expectation values. A χ² fit to experimental B_Λ values was used to determine the Λ-nuclear interaction parameters \(\bar V\), V_σ, C_p, and W_o.

## Key numbers

- Experimental B_Λ values used as constraints: B_Λ(⁴_ΛH) = 2.22 ± 0.04 MeV, B_Λ(⁴_ΛH*) = 1.12 ± 0.06 MeV, B_Λ(⁵_ΛHe) = 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV.
- Best-fit parameters for \(\bar V\) = 6.15 MeV: V_σ = 0.176 ± 0.05 MeV, C_p = 1.64 ∓ 0.15 MeV, W_o = 0.026 ∓ 0.003 MeV.
- Best-fit parameters for \(\bar V\) = 6.20 MeV: V_σ = 0.125 ± 0.05 MeV, C_p = 1.52 ∓ 0.15 MeV, W_o = 0.025 ∓ 0.003 MeV.
- The core nuclei shrink by about 20% in all hypernuclei due to the presence of the Λ particle.
- Without three-body ΛNN interactions, ⁵_ΛHe is overbound by about 2.34 MeV for \(\bar V\) = 6.20 MeV and by about 1.39 MeV for \(\bar V\) = 6.15 MeV.
- The calculated B_Λ for ¹⁷_ΛO using the s-shell parameters is 23.3 ± 1.6 MeV, considerably larger than the empirical estimate of about 13.0 ± 0.4 MeV.
- The empirical well depth D for Λ in nuclear matter is 29 ± 1 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends earlier variational studies by Bodmer and Usmani (1990s) that used simplified NN interactions, by employing the realistic Argonne υ₁₈ and Urbana IX interactions. It contradicts the earlier findings of Bando et al. (1980s) and Shinmura et al. (1990s) regarding the role of tensor forces in the overbinding problem, and also disagrees with the results of Usmani, Pieper, and Usmani (1990s) for ¹⁷_ΛO. The study enables future work on incorporating realistic three-body ΛNN correlations in nuclear matter calculations, which the authors identify as a challenging but necessary next step.
