# Thermodynamics of (2+1)-flavor QCD: Confronting Models with Lattice Studies

> The Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model, which combines chiral as well as deconfinement aspects of strongly interacting matter is introduced for three light quark flavors. An analysis of the chiral and deconfinement phase transition of the model and its thermodynamics at finite temperatures is given. Three different forms of the effective Polyakov loop potential are considered. The findings of the (2+1)-flavor model investigations are confronted to corresponding recent QCD lattice simulations of the RBC-Bielefeld, HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations. The influence of the heavier quark masses, which are used in the lattice calculations, is taken into account. In the transition region the bulk thermodynamics of the PQM model agrees well with the lattice data.

## Key claim
The Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model for (2+1) quark flavors, when evaluated in mean-field approximation with heavier quark masses matching those used in lattice simulations, reproduces the bulk thermodynamics of QCD lattice data in the transition region, but only for specific parameter choices where the chiral and deconfinement transitions coincide.

## Method
The authors constructed a (2+1)-flavor Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model combining the chiral linear sigma model with the Polyakov loop, coupling quarks to a uniform temporal background gauge field via covariant derivatives. They evaluated the thermodynamic potential in mean-field approximation, minimizing it with respect to four order parameters (non-strange condensate, strange condensate, Polyakov loop, and its conjugate) to determine the phase structure. Three different forms of the effective Polyakov loop potential were considered: a polynomial Ginzburg-Landau ansatz, a logarithmic form, and a strong-coupling-inspired potential by Fukushima.

## Key numbers
- For physical pion masses with m_sigma = 600 MeV and T_0 = 270 MeV, all three Polyakov loop potentials yield coinciding chiral and deconfinement transitions at temperatures ranging from 189-207 MeV depending on the potential.
- With heavier meson masses matching lattice simulations (m_pi ~ 220 MeV, m_K ~ 503 MeV), the polynomial potential gives T_chi = 216 MeV and T_d = 215 MeV; the logarithmic potential gives T_chi = 215 MeV and T_d = 208 MeV; the Fukushima potential gives T_chi = 200 MeV and T_d = 196 MeV.
- The Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration finds a chiral transition at T_chi ~ 146 MeV and deconfinement at T_d ~ 170 MeV for physical quark masses, which the PQM model cannot reproduce except for one extreme parameter set (polynomial potential, T_0 = 270 MeV, m_sigma = 500 MeV).
- The pure gauge transition temperature T_0 is estimated as 187 MeV for (2+1) flavors, down from 270 MeV for N_f = 0.

## Context
This work extends previous two-flavor Polyakov-loop NJL and PQM model studies (Ratti 2005, Roessner 2006, Schaefer 2007) to three light quark flavors, confronting model predictions with recent lattice data from the RBC-Bielefeld, HotQCD, and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations. The RBC-Bielefeld and HotQCD collaborations observe coincident chiral and deconfinement transitions around 185-195 MeV, while the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration finds a lower chiral transition temperature (146 MeV) than deconfinement (170 MeV). The PQM model can reproduce the coincident-transition scenario but not the Wuppertal-Budapest scenario with well-separated transitions, indicating limitations of the mean-field treatment and the need for beyond-mean-field fluctuations or improved Polyakov loop potentials.
