# Effect of chemical freeze out on identified particle spectra at 200AGeV Au-Au Collisions at RHIC using SPheRIO

> We investigate the effect of chemical freeze-out on identified particle spectra at 200AGeV Au-Au Collisions at RHIC, by utilizing a full three-dimensional hydrodynamical calculation. The hydrodynamical code SPheRIO we employed is based on the smoothed particle hydrodynamic algorithm. In order to describe the spectra of strange hadrons, the code has been further improved by explicitly incorporating the strangeness conservation and a chemical freeze-out mechanism. In our model, strange hadrons such as Lambda, Xi, Omega and phi undergo the chemical freeze-out immediately after the hadronization, and their multiplicities are fixed thereafter. At a lower temperature the thermal freeze-out takes place for all the particles. It is shown that the present model provides a reasonably good description for the spectra of identified particles, in particular, considerable improvement is observed for those of strange hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that incorporating a chemical freeze-out mechanism for strange hadrons (Lambda, Xi, Omega, phi) immediately after hadronization, without introducing a specific chemical freeze-out temperature as a free parameter, significantly improves the description of strange hadron transverse momentum spectra in a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic model, while leaving pion, kaon, and proton spectra essentially unchanged.

## Method

The authors used the SPheRIO code, a smoothed particle hydrodynamic (SPH) algorithm, to perform full three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of 200 AGeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC. They implemented strangeness conservation by incorporating a strangeness chemical potential and assuming local strangeness neutrality, and introduced a chemical freeze-out mechanism where strange hadrons cease inelastic collisions immediately after hadronization, with their abundances fixed by the partonic equation of state. Initial conditions were generated using the NEXUS event generator with event-by-event fluctuations, and an additional parameter alpha provided initial transverse velocity.

## Key numbers

- Thermal freeze-out temperature: T_f = 110 MeV, used to reproduce the transverse momentum distribution of all charged particles
- Initial transverse velocity parameter: alpha = 0.04 fm^-1, used to match the charged particle transverse momentum distribution
- Collision system: Au-Au at 200 AGeV (center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair)
- Rapidity interval for particle spectra: -1.0 < eta < 1.0

## Context

This work extends earlier statistical model and hydro-inspired calculations (e.g., those by Rafelski and collaborators, and by Braun-Munzinger and collaborators) that suggested early chemical freeze-out for strange hadrons, by implementing this scenario in a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic evolution. The approach differs from hybrid models (e.g., those by Bass and Dumitru, and by Teaney and Shuryak) that couple hydrodynamic evolution with hadronic cascade codes, and from simpler hydrodynamic calculations that assumed Bjorken scaling or zero baryon chemical potential. The results enable further studies of chemical freeze-out effects at large rapidity where baryon density differs significantly from zero.
