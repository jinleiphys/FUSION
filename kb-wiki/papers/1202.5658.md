# Lorentz covariant nucleon self-energy decomposition of the nuclear symmetry energy

> Using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, we derive analytical expressions for the nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$ and its density slope $L(ρ)$ in terms of the Lorentz covariant nucleon self-energies in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. These general expressions are useful for determining the density dependence of the symmetry energy and understanding the Lorentz structure and the microscopic origin of the nuclear symmetry energy in relativistic covariant formulism. As an example, we analyze the Lorentz covariant nucleon self-energy decomposition of $E_{sym}(ρ)$ and $L(ρ)$ and derive the corresponding analytical expressions within the nonlinear $σ$-$ω$-$ρ$-$δ$ relativistic mean field model.

## Key claim

Using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, the authors derive general analytical expressions for the nuclear symmetry energy and its density slope in terms of Lorentz covariant nucleon self-energies, but the decomposition is only valid for infinite nuclear matter and the explicit formulas depend on the specific relativistic mean field model chosen for illustration.

## Method

The authors start from the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, which equates nucleon chemical potential to Fermi energy in asymmetric nuclear matter, and expand the equation of state in powers of isospin asymmetry. By expanding the single-nucleon energy (expressed in terms of scalar, vector, and space-like self-energies) in powers of asymmetry and matching coefficients, they derive analytical expressions for the symmetry energy E_sym(ρ) and its slope L(ρ) as sums of kinetic, momentum-dependent, first-order, second-order, and cross terms involving the self-energies. They then apply these general formulas to the nonlinear σ-ω-ρ-δ relativistic mean field model, where the self-energies become momentum-independent and the space-like component vanishes, yielding explicit analytical expressions for E_sym(ρ) and L(ρ) in that model.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at saturation density E_sym(ρ₀) is decomposed into kinetic, first-order scalar, and first-order vector contributions; for the four interactions studied (FSUGold, IU-FSU, NLρδ, HA), the kinetic contribution is roughly the same across models while the scalar and vector contributions differ significantly.
- The slope parameter L(ρ) is decomposed into kinetic, first-order, and second-order contributions; the second-order contribution L^(2nd)(ρ) from second-order symmetry self-energies generally cannot be neglected, consistent with non-relativistic calculations (Che12).
- For the nonlinear RMF model, the first-order symmetry self-energies are given analytically: Σ_S^(sym,1)(ρ) = -g_δ² M₀* ρ / (ℰ_F* Q_δ) and Σ_V^(sym,1)(ρ) = +g_ρ² ρ / Q_ρ, where Q_δ and Q_ρ depend on meson masses, coupling constants, and densities.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic decomposition of the symmetry energy in terms of single-nucleon potentials (XuC10, XuC11, Che12) to a fully relativistic covariant framework, which is important because the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach (Ana83, Hor87, Bro90, Dal07, Dal10, Sam10) can describe saturation properties without three-nucleon forces. The derived general expressions enable future determination of the symmetry energy from QCD sum-rule techniques (Coh91, Dru10) or from experimental Dirac phenomenology of nucleon-nucleus scattering, and the analytical RMF formulas are useful for fixing isovector parameters in the RMF model by fitting empirical asymmetric nuclear matter properties.
