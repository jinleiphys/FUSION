# Description of Double Giant Dipole Resonance within the Phonon Damping Model

> In a recent Letter [1] an overall agreement with the experimental data for the excitation of the single and double giant dipole resonances in relativistic heavy ion collision in 136Xe and 208Pb nuclei has been reported. We point out that this agreement is achieved by a wrong calculation of the DGDR excitation mechanism. We also argue that the agreement with the data for the widths of resonances is achieved by an unrealistically large value of a model parameter. [1] Nguyen Dinh Dang, Vuong Kim Au, and Akito Arima, Phys. Rev. Lett. 85 (2000) 1827.

# Description of Double Giant Dipole Resonance within the Phonon Damping Model

## Key claim
The agreement with experimental data for double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) excitation in relativistic heavy ion collisions reported by Dang, Au, and Arima (2000) is achieved by a wrong calculation of the DGDR excitation mechanism, specifically by incorrectly reducing a two-step process to a one-step process, and by using an unrealistically large value of the model parameter F_ph.

## Method
The authors critique the Phonon Damping Model (PDM) calculation of DGDR cross sections in relativistic heavy ion collisions for 136Xe and 208Pb. They point out that the PDM uses an expression similar to photoabsorption cross section with the GDR strength function replaced by the DGDR one, treating DGDR excitation as a direct one-step process. The authors argue that multi-step processes with sequential absorption of virtual photons, described by second-order perturbation theory, are the correct physical mechanism and are stronger by at least two orders of magnitude based on microscopic calculations by Bertulani and Ponomarev (1999).

## Key numbers
- The two-step process for DGDR excitation is stronger than the one-step process by at least two orders of magnitude, based on microscopic calculations by Bertulani and Ponomarev (1999)
- The strength parameter F_ph changes by two orders of magnitude when contributions of higher-order processes to GDR damping are included, as in the extension of the model by Dang, Tanabe, and Arima (1998)
- The PDM yields the value of F_ph in the heavier double-magic 208Pb larger than in the lighter semi-magic 136Xe, which the authors argue should be the opposite

## Context
This comment extends the ongoing debate about the mechanism of DGDR excitation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, specifically challenging the Phonon Damping Model results of Dang, Au, and Arima (2000). It contradicts the claim that the PDM can reproduce the factor-of-two enhancement of DGDR cross section in 136Xe observed by Schmidt et al. (1993), which has remained an open problem despite attempts by Aumann, Bortignon, and Emling (1998) and Bertulani and Ponomarev (1999). The experimental proof that DGDR is excited in a two-step process was provided by Boretzky et al. (1996). The comment enables further scrutiny of the PDM approach and supports microscopic calculations by Ponomarev, Bortignon, Broglia, and Voronov (2000) that yield smaller anharmonicities than the PDM.
