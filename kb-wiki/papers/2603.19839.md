# A dual description of quarks and baryons: Quarkyonic matter within a relativistic quark model

> We investigate quarkyonic matter within a relativistic quark model by combining the dual quarkyonic picture with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. Using relativistic gaussian quark wavefunctions for the nucleon, we construct the quarkyonic QMC (QQMC) model and study the properties of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. We find that the quark saturation density depends sensitively on the nucleon size parameter and that nuclear interactions quantitatively modify the high-density behavior of the equation of state (EoS) and the sound velocity. In particular, the QQMC model yields an earlier onset of quark saturation than the noninteracting gaussian quarkyonic (GQ) model, indicating that nuclear interactions enhance the stiffening of the EoS in the quarkyonic regime.

## Key claim

The quarkyonic QMC (QQMC) model yields an earlier onset of quark saturation than the noninteracting gaussian quarkyonic (GQ) model, indicating that nuclear interactions enhance the stiffening of the equation of state in the quarkyonic regime; however, the singular behavior of the sound velocity at the quark saturation density is an artifact of the sharp Fermi surface assumption and should not be interpreted as a physical phenomenon.

## Method

The authors construct the quarkyonic QMC (QQMC) model by combining the dual quarkyonic picture with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, using relativistic gaussian quark wavefunctions for the nucleon obtained from a scalar-vector harmonic oscillator confinement potential. In symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, the nucleon momentum-space distribution is separated into a bulk Fermi sea and a shell structure above the quark saturation density, with the boundary condition determined by the Pauli blocking at the quark level. The mean fields of sigma, omega, and rho mesons interact with the confined quarks, and the effective quark mass and energy replace the vacuum quantities in the gaussian wavefunction.

- Quark saturation density for r_p = 0.7 fm: 2.21 rho_0 in QQMC model, 2.53 rho_0 in GQ model
- Quark saturation density for r_p = 0.8 fm: 1.46 rho_0 in QQMC model, 1.63 rho_0 in GQ model
- Constituent quark mass fixed at m = 300 MeV
- Meson masses: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV
- For r_p = 0.7 fm: nucleon size parameter a = 0.669 fm, confinement strength c = 1.102 fm^-3, single-particle quark energy epsilon = 592.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept introduced by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) and the IdylliQ model (PhysRevLett.132.112701) by incorporating nuclear interactions through the QMC framework developed by Saito, Guichon, and collaborators (1994-2018). The relativistic treatment shifts the quark saturation density to higher density than in the nonrelativistic case (PhysRevC.110.025201), while nuclear interactions shift it back to lower density. The next step is to develop a smoother description that avoids the divergence at the quark saturation density, as noted by Saito (2025).
