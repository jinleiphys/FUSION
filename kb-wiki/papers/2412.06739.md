# $f$-mode oscillations of dark matter admixed quarkyonic neutron star

> We systematically investigate $f-$mode oscillations ($\ell$ = 2) in quarkyonic neutron stars with dark matter, employing the Cowling approximation within the framework of linearized general relativity. The relativistic mean-field approach is used to compute various macroscopic properties of neutron stars. The analysis focuses on three key free parameters in the model: transition density, QCD confinement scale, and dark matter (DM) Fermi momentum, all of which significantly affect the properties of $f-$mode oscillations. The inclusion of dark matter in quarkyonic equations of state leads to notable variations in $f-$mode frequencies. Despite these changes, several universal relations among the oscillation properties are found to hold, demonstrating their robustness in the presence of dark matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that universal relations among f-mode oscillation properties of neutron stars remain robust even when dark matter is included in quarkyonic equations of state, although the specific f-mode frequencies themselves show notable variations depending on the chosen values of transition density, QCD confinement scale, and dark matter Fermi momentum.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with the effective RMF (E-RMF) model to compute macroscopic properties of neutron stars, incorporating a quarkyonic matter model based on the McLerran-Reddy framework as extended by Zhao and Lattimer. They added dark matter particles (Neutralinos with mass 200 GeV) interacting with nucleons and quarks via Higgs exchange, and computed f-mode oscillations (l=2) using the Cowling approximation within linearized general relativity, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for equilibrium configurations.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter particle mass (Neutralino) set to 200 GeV, with coupling constants y=0.07 and f=0.35, and Higgs boson mass 125 GeV with vacuum expectation value 246 GeV
- Dark matter Fermi momentum approximated as k_f^DM ≈ 0.03 GeV, based on the assumption that nucleon density is 1000 times larger than average DM density, giving a mass ratio M_χ/M_NS = 1/6
- The Cowling approximation yields oscillation frequencies with error less than 20% compared to fully general relativistic calculations
- Central density of neutron stars can exceed 5 to 10 times the nuclear saturation density of ~0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter model of McLerran and Reddy (2019) and its refinement by Zhao and Lattimer (2020) by adding dark matter, building on earlier studies of dark matter effects in neutron stars by various authors. The study uses the Cowling approximation following the approach of Andersson and Kokkotas for relating f-mode frequencies to global neutron star properties, and it enables future gravitational wave asteroseismology to potentially constrain dark matter properties in neutron stars through observed f-mode frequencies.
