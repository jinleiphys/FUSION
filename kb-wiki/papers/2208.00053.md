# Ignition of carbon burning from nuclear fission in compact stars

> Type-Ia supernovae (SN Ia) are powerful stellar explosions that provide important distance indicators in cosmology. Recently, we proposed a new SN Ia mechanism that involves a nuclear fission chain reaction in an isolated white dwarf (WD) [PRL 126, 1311010]. The first solids that form as a WD starts to freeze are actinide rich and potentially support a fission chain reaction. In this letter we explore thermonuclear ignition from fission heating. We perform thermal diffusion simulations and find at high densities, above about 7x10^8 g/cm^3, that the fission heating can ignite carbon burning. This could produce a SN Ia or another kind of astrophysical transient.

## Key claim
Fission heating from a naturally occurring nuclear chain reaction in an actinide-rich crystal can ignite carbon burning in an isolated white dwarf, but only at densities above approximately 7×10^8 g/cm^3; at lower densities the heat simply diffuses away without initiating thermonuclear burning.

## Method
The author performed thermal diffusion simulations of a spherical actinide-rich crystal (the "pit") embedded in a 50/50 carbon-oxygen liquid, using a first-order implicit scheme with spherical symmetry. The fission heating rate per baryon was taken from prior nuclear reaction network simulations, and carbon-carbon fusion rates were calculated using the REACLIB database with strong screening. The simulations tracked temperature evolution as a function of radius and time, varying density, pit mass, and total fission energy to determine ignition thresholds.

## Key numbers
- Carbon ignition requires densities above approximately 7×10^8 g/cm^3; at 2×10^8 g/cm^3 the simulation fails to ignite.
- At ρ = 8×10^8 g/cm^3, carbon ignition occurs with a pit mass of 16 mg and radius 1.7×10^(-4) cm, consistent with the predicted pit mass of 20 mg from Table 2.
- The minimum total fission heating required for carbon ignition decreases from 0.66 MeV/nucleon at ρ = 6×10^8 g/cm^3 to 0.19 MeV/nucleon at ρ = 4×10^9 g/cm^3.
- Oxygen ignition is possible at ρ = 5×10^9 g/cm^3 in a 60/30/10% O/Ne/Mg composition, reaching a temperature of approximately 1.5 MeV.
- The total fission energy released in the reference simulation is 0.679 MeV/baryon.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior proposal of a fission chain reaction mechanism for Type-Ia supernovae (Horowitz, PRL 126, 131101, 2021) by providing the first thermal diffusion simulations of the ignition stage. It contrasts with traditional single-degenerate and double-degenerate SN Ia models (Hillebrandt 2013, Ruiz-Lapuente 2014) and with alternative isolated white dwarf ignition mechanisms such as dark matter interactions or pycnonuclear fusion of impurities. The results enable future hydrodynamic simulations of the resulting deflagration or detonation and suggest that ultra-massive white dwarfs formed through mergers (as in the double-degenerate channel) could host this mechanism after cooling, potentially producing SN Ia without a detectable gravitational wave signal or ex-companion star.
