# Omega-Phi mixing at finite temperature

> We compute the mass shifts and mixing of the Omega and Phi mesons at finite temperature due to scattering from thermal pions. The Rho and b_1 mesons are important intermediate states. Up to a temperature of 140 MeV the Omega mass increases by 12 MeV and the Phi mass decreases by 0.6 MeV. The change in mixing angles is negligible.

## Key claim

The mass of the Omega meson increases by 12 MeV and the mass of the Phi meson decreases by 0.6 MeV at a temperature of 140 MeV due to scattering from thermal pions, but the authors note that multiple pion scattering and scattering from other thermal mesons should become important above 100 MeV, so the results above that temperature are not complete.

## Method

The authors compute the self-energies of the Omega and Phi mesons at finite temperature using a virial expansion, where the self-energy is obtained from the forward scattering amplitude of the vector meson from a pion with a Bose-Einstein momentum distribution. They use effective Lagrangian techniques to model the interactions coupling pions, Omega, and Phi mesons, with the Rho and b_1 mesons as important intermediate states, fitting parameters to known physical quantities including decay rates. The mass shifts and mixing angles are found by diagonalizing the inverse propagator matrix for Omega and Phi mesons at rest in the many-body system.

## Key numbers

- Omega mass increases by 12 MeV at a temperature of 140 MeV
- Phi mass decreases by 0.6 MeV at a temperature of 140 MeV
- The vacuum mixing angle is fitted to theta_V = 40.1 degrees, close to ideal mixing of approximately 35.3 degrees
- The b_1 meson has a width of 142 +/- 8 MeV and a measured D/S ratio of 0.26 +/- 0.04, used to determine coupling constants g_{b_1} = -9.471 GeV and h_{b_1} = 6.642 GeV^{-1}
- A "maximum effect" set of couplings (g_{b_1} = -11.401 GeV, h_{b_1} = 7.647 GeV^{-1}) increases the mass shift of the Omega by about 30% and the Phi by about 25%

## Context

This work extends the calculation of Haglin and Gale (1996) for the Rho meson self-energy to the Omega and Phi mesons, using a virial expansion approach with thermal pions as the scattering medium. The authors note that Shuryak (1993) previously identified the importance of the b_1 meson for the Omega mass shift at finite temperature. The results suggest that the Omega mass shift may be detectable in future heavy ion experiments, while the Phi mass shift and mixing angle changes are negligible, providing a baseline for interpreting dilepton measurements from experiments such as CERES, HELIOS, and PHENIX.
