# Non-relativistic expansion of Dirac equation with spherical scalar and vector potentials by reconstituted Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation

> Inspired by the reconstituted similarity renormalization group method, the reconstituted Foldy-Wouthuysen (FW) transformation is proposed. Applied to the Dirac equation in the covariant density functional theory, the reconstituted FW transformation shows a fast convergence of the spectrum of the single-particle energy. The single-particle densities and the single-particle scalar densities obtained by this new method are also investigated. In particular, the relativistic corrections to the densities from the picture-change error between the Schrödinger and Dirac pictures are discussed in detail. Taking these relativistic corrections into account, both the single-particle densities and the single-particle scalar densities are almost identical to their exact values.

## Key claim

The reconstituted Foldy-Wouthuysen (FW) transformation, inspired by the reconstituted similarity renormalization group (SRG) method, achieves fast convergence of the single-particle spectrum from the Dirac equation by expanding in powers of \(1/M^\ast\) (where \(M^\ast = M + S\) is the Dirac mass) rather than \(1/M\), and the resulting single-particle densities and scalar densities become almost identical to exact values only after including relativistic corrections from the picture-change error between the Schrödinger and Dirac pictures.

## Method

The authors start from the Dirac Hamiltonian with spherical scalar potential \(S\) and vector potential \(V\), replace the bare mass \(M\) with the Dirac mass \(M^\ast = M + S\), and perform a unitary Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation using the operator \(\tilde{\Lambda} = -\frac{i\beta}{4}( \frac{1}{M^\ast}\tilde{O} + \tilde{O}\frac{1}{M^\ast})\) with \(\tilde{O} = \boldsymbol{\alpha}\cdot\mathbf{p}\). The transformation is iterated recursively to raise off-diagonal parts to at least the \(1/{M^\ast}^4\) order, yielding a non-relativistic Hamiltonian \(\mathcal{H}_{\rm rFW}\) up to the \(1/{M^\ast}^3\) order. The single-particle densities and scalar densities are then computed by transforming the delta function and the \(\gamma_0\) matrix through the same unitary transformations, incorporating relativistic corrections from the picture-change error.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleus \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) with Woods-Saxon potentials (\(\Sigma_0 = -66.0\) MeV, \(\Delta_0 = 650.0\) MeV, \(r_0 = 7.0\) fm, \(a_0 = 0.6\) fm) and nucleon mass \(M = 939.0\) MeV, the last occupied orbital is the \(3p_{1/2}\) state at energy \(-6.999\) MeV.
- At the \(1/{M^\ast}^3\) order, the discrepancy between reconstituted FW single-particle energies and exact values is less than \(0.1\) MeV for deeply bound states and around \(0.2\) MeV for weakly bound states.
- The three orbitals with the largest negative deviations are: \(1g_{9/2}\) at \(-28.072\) MeV (deviation \(-152\) keV), \(1h_{11/2}\) at \(-18.784\) MeV (deviation \(-239\) keV), and \(1i_{13/2}\) at \(-9.267\) MeV (deviation \(-325\) keV).
- The orbital with the largest positive deviation is the \(1h_{9/2}\) state at \(-14.027\) MeV (deviation \(+89\) keV).

## Context

This work extends the conventional Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation (Foldy 1950, Tani 1951) and the reconstituted SRG method (Guo 2019) to achieve faster convergence for the non-relativistic expansion of the Dirac equation in covariant density functional theory. It also generalizes the relativistic corrections to densities (the picture-change error) from quantum chemistry (Douglas 1974, Hess 1986) to the nuclear physics context of spherical scalar and vector potentials. The results enable accurate non-relativistic calculations of single-particle energies and densities that closely match exact Dirac solutions, bridging relativistic and non-relativistic density functional frameworks.
