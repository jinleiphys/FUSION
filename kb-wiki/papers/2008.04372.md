# Inclusive prompt photon-jet correlations as a probe of gluon saturation in electron-nucleus scattering at small $x$

> We compute the differential cross-section for inclusive prompt photon$+$quark production in deeply inelastic scattering of electrons off nuclei at small $x$ ($e+A$ DIS) in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. The result is expressed as a convolution of the leading order (in the strong coupling $α_{\mathrm{s}}$) impact factor for the process and universal dipole matrix elements, in the limit of hard photon transverse momentum relative to the nuclear saturation scale $Q_{s,A}(x)$. We perform a numerical study of this process for the kinematics of the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), exploring in particular the azimuthal angle correlations between the final state photon and quark. We observe a systematic suppression and broadening pattern of the back-to-back peak in the relative azimuthal angle distribution, as the saturation scale is increased by replacing proton targets with gold nuclei. Our results suggest that photon+jet final states in inclusive $e+A$ DIS at high energies are in general a promising channel for exploring gluon saturation that is complementary to inclusive and diffractive dijet production. They also provide a sensitive empirical test of the universality of dipole matrix elements when compared to identical measurements in proton-nucleus collisions. However because photon+jet correlations at small $x$ in EIC kinematics require jet reconstruction at small $k_\perp$, it will be important to study their feasibility relative to photon-hadron correlations.

## Key claim

The authors compute the leading-order cross-section for inclusive prompt photon+quark production in electron-nucleus deeply inelastic scattering at small Bjorken x, showing that in the limit of hard photon transverse momentum relative to the nuclear saturation scale, the cross-section depends only on universal dipole matrix elements rather than quadrupole correlators. However, the authors caution that photon+jet correlations at small x in Electron-Ion Collider kinematics require jet reconstruction at small transverse momentum, making it important to study their feasibility relative to photon-hadron correlations.

## Method

The computation is performed in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory framework, where the amplitude for virtual photon-nucleus scattering producing a quark, antiquark, and prompt photon is expressed as a convolution of a leading-order impact factor (in the strong coupling alpha_s) with universal dipole matrix elements. The cross-section for photon+quark production is obtained by integrating over the phase space of the unobserved antiquark, with collinear divergences regulated by a cutoff procedure and absorbed into quark-to-photon fragmentation functions. The azimuthal angle correlations between the final-state photon and quark are studied numerically for Electron-Ion Collider kinematics using the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov renormalization group equation to evolve the dipole correlator.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale scales as Q_s^2 proportional to A^{1/3}, where A is the nuclear mass number
- The computation is valid for Bjorken x_Bj less than or equal to 10^{-2}
- The virtuality Q^2 is taken to be much greater than the nucleon mass squared M_N^2, and W^2 is much greater than M_N^2
- The longitudinal photon flux f_L = (alpha_em)/(pi Q^2 s y) (1-y) and transverse photon flux f_T = (alpha_em)/(4 pi Q^2 s y) [1+(1-y)^2], where y = (W^2+Q^2)/s is the inelasticity

## Context

This work extends the formalism for inclusive prompt photon+quark-antiquark dijet production at next-to-leading order developed by Roy et al. (2018, 2019) to the study of photon+quark correlations in electron-nucleus scattering. It complements prior studies of dijet/dihadron production in proton-nucleus collisions by Kharzeev (2004), Marquet (2007), and Dominguez (2011), as well as studies of dijet production in electron-nucleus scattering by Zheng (2014), Dumitru (2015, 2018), and Mantysaari (2019). The results enable comparisons with identical measurements in proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC to test the universality of dipole matrix elements and isolate initial versus final state contributions to gluon saturation.
