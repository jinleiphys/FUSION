# The relativistic kinetic dispersion relation: Comparison of the relativistic Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook model and Grad's 14-moment expansion

> In this paper, we study the Cauchy problem of the linearized kinetic equations for the models of Marle and Anderson-Witting, and compare these dispersion relations with the 14-moment theory. First, we propose a modification of the Marle model to improve the resultant transport coefficients in accord with those obtained by the full Boltzmann equation. Using the modified Marle model and Anderson-Witting model, we calculate dispersion relations that are kinetically correct within the validity of the BGK approximation. The 14-moment theory that includes the time derivative of dissipation currents has causal structure, in contrast to the acausal first-order Chapman-Enskog approximation. However, the dispersion relation of the 14-moment theory does not accurately describe the result of the kinetic equation. Thus, our calculation indicates that keeping these second-order terms does not simply correspond to improving the physical description of the relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The second-order Israel-Stewart (14-moment) theory, while causal unlike the acausal first-order Chapman-Enskog approximation, does not accurately reproduce the dispersion relations obtained from the kinetic BGK models (modified Marle and Anderson-Witting), indicating that including second-order time derivatives of dissipation currents does not simply improve the physical description of relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors linearize the relativistic BGK kinetic equations for the Marle model (modified to correct its high-temperature transport coefficients) and the Anderson-Witting model around a global equilibrium rest state, then solve the resulting homogeneous Fredholm integral equations to obtain dispersion relations for longitudinal and transverse modes. They compare these kinetic dispersion relations with those derived from Grad's 14-moment expansion (Israel-Stewart theory) by numerically solving for the complex frequency ω as a function of wavenumber k.

## Key numbers

- The modification factor for the Marle model relaxation time τ_M is given by τ_M = (K_1(ζ)/K_2(ζ)) τ_relax, where ζ = m/T; this factor becomes 1 in the low-temperature limit (large ζ) and ζ/2 in the high-temperature limit (ζ near 0)
- The transport coefficients of the original Marle model differ from those of the full Boltzmann equation for hard-sphere particles by a factor of 1/ζ in the high-temperature limit
- Units are set with c = 1 and k_B = 1 throughout

## Context

This work extends the boundary-value dispersion relation studies of Cercignani and Majorana (1985) to a Cauchy problem formulation, solving for ω rather than k. It tests the applicability of the Israel-Stewart second-order theory (based on the 14-moment method of Grad) against kinetic BGK models, following recent interest in relativistic dissipative fluids for quark-gluon plasma studies at RHIC (Baier et al., 2008; Molnar et al., 2009). The results enable assessment of whether second-order terms in relativistic hydrodynamics provide physically accurate descriptions, and inform the choice of kinetic coefficients for numerical simulations.
