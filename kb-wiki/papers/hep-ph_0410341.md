# Multiparticle production processes from the nonextensive point of view

> We look at multiparticle production processes from the nonextensive point of view. Nonextensivity means here the systematic deviations in exponential formulas provided by the usual statistical approach for description of some observables like transverse momenta or rapidity distributions. We show that they can be accounted for by means of single parameter q with |q-1| being the measure of nonextensivity. The whole discussion will be based on the information theoretical approach to multiparticle processes proposed by us some time ago.

# Multiparticle production processes from the nonextensive point of view

## Key claim
Multiparticle production data on transverse momentum and rapidity distributions, which show systematic deviations from the exponential formulas of standard statistical models, can be accounted for by a single nonextensivity parameter q, where |q-1| measures the degree of nonextensivity. The authors argue that this parameter provides information on dynamical fluctuations present in the hadronizing system, but emphasize that their approach is not a model but rather the least biased and most probable distribution from information theory.

## Method
The authors apply information theory using both Shannon entropy and Tsallis entropy to derive single-particle distributions in rapidity and transverse momentum. They maximize entropy under constraints of normalization and energy conservation, replacing exponential functions with q-exponential functions (exp_q) to account for nonextensivity. The resulting distributions are fitted to experimental data from proton-proton, antiproton-proton, nucleus-nucleus (PbPb, Au+Au, S+S), and electron-positron collisions at various energies.

## Key numbers
- For antiproton-proton and proton-proton data, the nonextensivity parameter q shows energy dependence that agrees with the Negative Binomial distribution parameter 1/k, with the relation q = 1 + 1/k.
- For NA49 central PbPb collisions, rapidity distributions were fitted with q = 1.2, 1.16, and 1.04 for different energies (from top to bottom in the figure).
- For PHOBOS most central Au+Au collisions, rapidity distributions were fitted with q = 1.27, 1.26, and 1.29 (from top to bottom).
- For UA1 antiproton-proton transverse momentum spectra, q = 1.11, 1.105, and 1.095 for energies decreasing from 900 to 200 GeV.
- For S+S collisions transverse momentum spectra, q = 1.015, corresponding to a temperature fluctuation of Delta T/T approximately 0.12.
- For electron-positron annihilations at 91.2 GeV, the best fit used q = 0.6.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier information-theoretical approach to multiparticle production (Utyuzh, Wilk, Wlodarczyk, cited as MaxEnt and MaxEntq) by incorporating nonextensive Tsallis statistics. It builds on the observation that the usual exponential (Boltzmann-Gibbs) distributions fail when the "heat bath" is finite and inhomogeneous, and connects to the concept of superstatistics developed by Beck and Cohen (2003). The paper shows that the nonextensivity parameter q can be interpreted as arising from fluctuations of the inverse temperature parameter, with q-1 = 1/C where C is the heat capacity, and demonstrates that q values from hadronic collisions are larger than those from nuclear collisions, consistent with the volume dependence of heat capacity. The results enable a systematic search for phase transition phenomena, which should be accompanied by nonmonotonic fluctuations.
