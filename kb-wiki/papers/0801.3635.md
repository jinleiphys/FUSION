# X(2175) as a resonant state of the $ϕK \bar{K}$ system

> We perform a Faddeev calculation for the three mesons system, $ϕK \bar{K}$, taking the interaction between two pseudoscalar mesons and between a vector and a pseudoscalar meson from the chiral unitary approach. We obtain a neat resonance peak around a total mass of 2150 MeV and an invariant mass for the $K \bar{K}$ system around 970 MeV, very close to the $f_0(980)$ mass. The state appears in I=0 and qualifies as a $ϕf_0(980)$ resonance. We enlarge the space of states including $ϕππ$, since $ππ$ and $K \bar{K}$ build up the $f_0$ (980), and find moderate changes that serve to quantify theoretical uncertainties. No state is seen in I=1. This finding provides a natural explanation for the recent state found at BABAR and BES, the X(2175), which decays into $ϕf_0(980)$.

## Key claim

The X(2175) resonance can be explained as a dynamically generated resonant state of the $\phi K \bar{K}$ system, where the $K \bar{K}$ subsystem forms the $f_0(980)$ resonance, rather than as an exotic tetraquark or hybrid state; the theoretical mass of 2150 MeV differs from the experimental X(2175) mass by about 25 MeV, which the authors consider a remarkable agreement for a hadronic model.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations for the three-meson system $\phi K \bar{K}$ using two-body scattering matrices obtained from the chiral unitary approach: the pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar interaction (generating the $f_0(980)$ pole) and the vector-pseudoscalar interaction (generating axial-vector resonances) were taken from prior work by Napsuciale et al. (2007) and Roca et al. (2005). The calculation was performed in isospin I=0, with the invariant mass of the $K \bar{K}$ subsystem varied around the $f_0(980)$ mass, and the space of states was later enlarged to include the $\phi \pi \pi$ channel as a coupled channel to quantify theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The resonance peak appears at a total mass of **2150 MeV** with a full width at half maximum of **16 MeV** in the $\phi K \bar{K}$ calculation.
- After folding with an experimental resolution of **10 MeV**, the Breit-Wigner width becomes **27 MeV**.
- The invariant mass of the $K \bar{K}$ subsystem at the peak is **970 MeV**, close to the $f_0(980)$ mass.
- Including the $\phi \pi \pi$ channel shifts the peak downward by **38 MeV** to **2112 MeV**, with the $K \bar{K}$ invariant mass at **965 MeV**.
- The experimental X(2175) mass from BABAR is **2175 ± 10 MeV** with width **58 ± 16 ± 20 MeV**; from BES the mass is **2186 ± 10 ± 6 MeV** with width **65 ± 25 ± 17 MeV**.
- Changing the cutoff in the two-body $t$ matrices by **20 MeV** shifts the peak position by **8 MeV**.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev approach previously applied to $\pi \bar{K} N$ and $\pi \pi N$ systems by Martinez Torres et al. (2007) and Khemchandani et al. (2008), which successfully reproduced many low-lying baryon resonances. The study contradicts interpretations of X(2175) as a tetraquark (Wang, 2006) or a gluon hybrid (Ding, 2007; Close, 2007), and instead provides a natural dynamical explanation for the resonance observed by BABAR (Aubert, 2006; Aubert, 2007) and BES (2007). The results enable future experimental tests of the prediction that no analogous resonance exists in I=1, which would correspond to $\phi a_0(980)$.
