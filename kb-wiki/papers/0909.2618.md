# Color symmetrical superconductivity in a schematic nuclear quark model

> In this note, a novel BCS-type formalism is constructed in the framework of a schematic QCD inspired quark model, having in mind the description of color symmetrical superconducting states. The physical properties of the BCS vacuum (average numbers of quarks of different colors) remain unchanged under an arbitrary color rotation. In the usual approach to color superconductivity, the pairing correlations affect only the quasi-particle states of two colors, the single particle states of the third color remaining unaffected by the pairing correlations. In the theory of color symmetrical superconductivity here proposed, the pairing correlations affect symmetrically the quasi-particle states of the three colors and vanishing net color-charge is automatically insured. It is found that the groundstate energy of the color symmetrical sector of the Bonn model is well approximated by the average energy of the color symmetrical superconducting state proposed here.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a BCS-type formalism for color-symmetrical superconducting states in quark matter, where the pairing correlations affect all three colors symmetrically and vanishing net color-charge is automatically ensured. The authors show that the groundstate energy of the color symmetrical sector of the Bonn model is well approximated by the average energy of the proposed color symmetrical superconducting state, but note that the BCS state cannot be a strict color singlet, only satisfying the singlet condition "in the average."

## Method

The authors develop a generalized Bogoliubov transformation that treats all three colors on an equal footing, constructing a BCS vacuum state as the vacuum of quasi-particles defined by operators that mix creation and annihilation operators across all colors. The formalism is applied to the Bonn model, a schematic QCD-inspired quark model with an SU(3)-invariant pairing interaction expressed in terms of SU(4) generators, and the energy expectation value of the color-symmetrical BCS state is computed analytically and compared with the exact solution of the color-symmetrical sector obtained via a Schwinger-type boson representation of SU(4).

## Key numbers

- For the color-symmetrical BCS state, the groundstate energy is given by \( \mathcal{E}/G = (N/9)(6\Omega - N + 1 + 4N/(3\Omega)) \), where \( N \) is the quark number (\( 0 \leq N \leq 6\Omega \)) and \( 2\Omega \) is the level degeneracy for a fixed color.
- The exact groundstate energy of the color symmetrical sector is \( \mathcal{E}/G = (N/3)(2\Omega + 3 - N/3) \) for \( 0 \leq N \leq 6\Omega \).
- The color symmetry constraint for the BCS state requires \( p \cos 2\theta - q \cos 2\tilde\theta = p - q \), where \( p = \Omega'/\Omega \) and \( q = 1-p \).
- At the extremum, the quark number satisfies \( N = 6\Omega q \), and the leading-order energy is \( \mathcal{E}/G \approx \Omega^2[1 - (1 - N/(3\Omega))^2] \).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity in the Bonn model previously studied by Bohr (2009) and provides a method to construct color-singlet BCS states without resorting to projection techniques. It contrasts with the approach of Amore (2003), which uses the Peierls-Yoccoz projection method that the authors argue is unreliable unless the mean-field state is properly chosen, and with Iida (2004), which imposes color neutrality only through average color charges without ensuring full color-singlet conditions. The formalism enables the description of color-symmetrical superconducting states that are distinct from color-flavor-locking (Alford, 1999) and remain meaningful even when only u and d flavors are relevant.
