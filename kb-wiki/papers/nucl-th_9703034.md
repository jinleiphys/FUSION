# Symmetry energy of nuclear matter and properties of neutron statrs in a relativistic approach

> Asymmetric nuclear matter is treated in the formalism of Dirac-Brueckner approach with Bonn one-boson-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction. We extract the symmetry energy coefficient at the saturation to be about 31 MeV, which is in good agreement with empirical value of $30\pm 4$ MeV. The symmetry energy is found to increase almost linearly with the density, which differs considerably from the results of non-relativistic approaches. This finding also supports the linear parameterization of Prakash, Ainsworth and Lattimer. We find, furthermore, that the higher-order dependence of the nuclear equation of state on the asymmetry parameter is unimportant up to densities relevant for neutron stars. The resulting equation of state of neutron-rich matter is used to calculate the maximum mass of neutron star, and we find it to be about 2.1$M_\odot$. Possible mechanisms for the softening of the equation of state are also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that in a relativistic Hartree approach with a linear σ-ω-ρ model, strong magnetic fields (≥ 10²⁰ G) cause additional binding of nuclear matter and can convert neutron star matter into a stable proton-rich system, but the maximum neutron star mass is practically insensitive to the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic Hartree theory with a linear σ-ω-ρ meson model to compute the equation of state of cold symmetric nuclear matter and n-p-e (neutron-proton-electron) matter in beta equilibrium under strong magnetic fields. The Dirac equation for protons is solved in a uniform magnetic field with Landau quantization, while neutrons are treated as plane waves; the effective nucleon mass is obtained self-consistently, and the resulting equation of state is used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter at B_m = 10 B_m^(p)(c), the binding energy per baryon is E/A ≈ -41 MeV.
- The maximum neutron star masses for B_m = 0, 10⁴ B_m^(e)(c), B_m^(p)(c), and 10 B_m^(p)(c) are M_max = 3.10 M_⊙, 2.99 M_⊙, 2.91 M_⊙, and 2.86 M_⊙, respectively.
- The corresponding radii at maximum mass are R = 15.02 km, 14.95 km, 12.25 km, and 12.00 km for the same field values.
- For B_m = 10 B_m^(p)(c), the proton fraction Y_p saturates to 0.98 at n_B ≥ 2 n_0.
- The critical magnetic field for electrons is B_m^(e)(c) = 4.4 × 10¹³ G, and for protons it is B_m^(p)(c) ~ 10²⁰ G.
- The nuclear matter saturation density is n_0 = 0.1484 fm⁻³ with binding energy 15.75 MeV and bulk symmetry energy 35 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dense matter in strong magnetic fields by Lai (1991) and others, who examined free electron gas and neutron matter, by incorporating a full relativistic Hartree treatment of nuclear matter with meson exchange. The results enable future investigations of neutron star cooling via the direct URCA process and the possible existence of stable proton-rich matter in neutron star interiors, and the authors suggest extending the approach to quark matter in a relativistic Hartree-Fock model.
