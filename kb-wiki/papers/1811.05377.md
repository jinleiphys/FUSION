# Thermodynamic Behaviour of Magnetized QGP within the Self-Consistent Quasiparticle Model

> The self-consistent quasiparticle model has been successful in studying QCD thermodynamics. In this model, the medium effects are taken into account by considering quarks and gluons as quasiparticles with temperature-dependent masses which are proportional to the plasma frequency. The present work involves the extension of this model in the presence of magnetic fields. We have included the effect of the magnetic field by considering relativistic Landau Levels. The quasiparticle masses are then found to be dependent on both temperature and magnetic field. The thermomagnetic mass thus defined allows obtaining the thermodynamics of magnetized quark matter within the self-consistent quasiparticle model. The model then has been applied to the case of 2-flavor Quark-Gluon Plasma and the equation of state obtained in the presence of magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The self-consistent quasiparticle model, previously successful for QCD thermodynamics at zero magnetic field, is extended to include magnetic fields by redefining quasiparticle masses via relativistic Landau level quantization; the resulting thermodynamic quantities (energy density, pressure, entropy density) show qualitatively correct increases with magnetic field strength, but a fully quantitative comparison with lattice data requires a coupling constant whose exact functional dependence on both temperature and magnetic field is not yet known.

## Method

The authors modify the self-consistent quasiparticle model by replacing the standard three-momentum phase-space integral with a sum over Landau levels weighted by the magnetic field factor \(|q_f eB|/(2\pi)\), and by using the Landau-level energy eigenvalues \(E_j = \sqrt{m^2 + k_z^2 + 2j|q_f eB|}\). The quark number density equation is solved self-consistently to obtain a thermo-magnetic mass \(m_{qj}(T,B)\); gluons acquire an indirect thermo-magnetic mass through their coupling to quarks. Thermodynamic quantities (energy density, pressure, entropy density) are then computed from the modified distribution functions, using a temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent running coupling constant parametrized as in Farias (2017) for the SU(2) NJL model.

## Key numbers

- Energy density, pressure, and entropy density are computed for a 2-flavor system in the temperature range **170–400 MeV** for magnetic field strengths up to **eB ≈ (1–5) m_π²** (where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass).
- The energy density at a given temperature increases with magnetic field; for example, the difference \(\Delta\varepsilon/T^4\) between magnetized and unmagnetized cases is plotted for two magnetic field values (figures 4–6 show \(\Delta\varepsilon/T^4\), \(\Delta P/T^4\), and \(\Delta s/T^3\) as functions of temperature).
- The longitudinal pressure \(P_\parallel\) is computed; the transverse pressure \(P_\perp\) is not calculated because the functional dependence of the coupling constant on the magnetic field is unknown.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent quasiparticle model of Bannur (2007, 2008) to include magnetic fields, following prior studies of magnetized QGP thermodynamics using lattice QCD (Bali 2014; Levkova 2014), the SU(2) NJL model (Farias 2017), the Bag Model (Fraga 2012), and an effective fugacity quasiparticle model (Kurian 2017). The results are qualitatively consistent with those approaches. The model enables future quantitative studies of the equation of state for 2+1 flavor QGP in magnetic fields and calculation of the transverse pressure, as well as investigation of how the modified equation of state affects transport coefficients.
