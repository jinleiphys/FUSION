# Full jet tomography of high-energy nuclear collisions

> Parton energy loss in the hot QCD medium will manifest itself not only in leading hadron spectra but also in reconstructed jet productions in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. In this paper we report on recent theoretical efforts in studying full jet observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by discussing the modifications of jet shapes, inclusive jet cross section and the vector boson accompanied jet production in the presence of the QGP-induced jet quenching.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions is well-established through single-hadron suppression, full jet observables (jet shapes, inclusive jet cross sections, and vector-boson-tagged jet production) provide complementary and more detailed probes of the quark-gluon plasma, but the modifications to jet shapes in medium are surprisingly modest because the broadening from medium-induced radiation is offset by the steepening of the jet profile due to energy loss.

## Method

The author calculates jet shapes at leading order (LO) in perturbative QCD using DGLAP splitting kernels, with Sudakov resummation to handle collinear divergences and inclusion of initial-state radiation. Medium-induced gluon radiation is computed within the GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) formalism, and the total in-medium jet shape is obtained as a probabilistic superposition of vacuum and medium-induced contributions weighted by the energy loss probability distribution. For inclusive jet cross sections and Z0/gamma*-tagged jet production, next-to-leading order (NLO) calculations at O(alpha_s^3) are performed using the MCFM numerical code, with medium modifications applied via a similar probabilistic convolution.

## Key numbers

* Jet shapes in p+pbar collisions at sqrt(s)=1960 GeV at Tevatron are compared to CDF II data, with the theoretical model describing the data well away from r=0.
* For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV at LHC, the mean relative jet radii <r/R> show modest broadening: for cone size R=0.4, the vacuum value is approximately 0.22 and the medium value is approximately 0.24; for R=0.7, vacuum is approximately 0.19 and medium is approximately 0.20.
* The nuclear modification factor R_AA^jet for inclusive jets in central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV at RHIC varies continuously with jet radius R, with smaller radii (R <= 0.2) showing suppression similar to inclusive high-p_T particles.
* For Z0/gamma*-tagged jet production in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=4 TeV with cone size R=0.2, a sharp transition in I_AA^jet from enhancement below p_T(Z) to suppression above p_T(Z) is predicted.
* Non-perturbative corrections from hadronization at RHIC energies can shift jet p_T by about 8% at p_T ~ 12 GeV in p+p collisions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of jet quenching from single-hadron observables (pioneered by Wang (1991), Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev, and others) to full jet observables, providing predictions for the first reconstructed jet measurements at RHIC and LHC. The calculations build on the GLV formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation and use the MCFM code for NLO baseline calculations. The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental data from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC and future LHC measurements, and the tagged-jet channel offers a clean way to measure parton energy loss directly by comparing the jet p_T to the vector boson p_T.
