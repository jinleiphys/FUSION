# Coulomb-nuclear interference in the breakup of $^{11}$Be

> Within a theory of breakup reactions formulated in the framework of the post form distorted wave Born approximation, we calculate contributions of the pure Coulomb and the pure nuclear breakup as well as those of their interference terms to a variety of cross sections in breakup reactions of the one-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Be on a number of target nuclei. In contrast to the assumption often made, the Coulomb-nuclear interference terms are found to be non-negligible in case of exclusive cross sections of the fragments emitted in this reaction on medium mass and heavy target nuclei. The consideration of the nuclear breakup leads to a better description of such data.

# Coulomb-nuclear interference in the breakup of $^{11}$Be

## Key claim
Contrary to the common assumption that Coulomb-nuclear interference (CNI) terms are negligible in halo breakup reactions, the authors find that these terms are non-negligible for exclusive cross sections of fragments emitted from $^{11}$Be breakup on medium-mass and heavy target nuclei. The claim is qualified by the beam energy (41 and 72 MeV/nucleon) and by the observation that CNI effects are small (2-8%) on the total breakup cross section, with the sign of interference depending on the target nucleus.

## Method
The authors use a post-form distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) theory of breakup reactions that treats Coulomb and nuclear fragment-target interactions to all orders while treating the fragment-fragment interaction in first order. Finite-range effects are included via the local momentum approximation (LMA). The ground state wave function of $^{11}$Be is constructed using a single-particle potential model with a Woods-Saxon neutron-core interaction, and the partial wave expansion is converged by treating the high-angular-momentum contributions analytically through a pure Coulomb Bremsstrahlung integral.

## Key numbers
- Ground state binding energy of $^{11}$Be: 504 keV, with a spectroscopic factor of 0.78 for the $2s_{1/2}$ valence neutron coupled to the $^{10}$Be($0^+$) core
- Beam energy of 41 MeV/nucleon for neutron angular distribution measurements on Be, Ti, and Au targets
- Beam energy of 72 MeV/nucleon for relative energy spectrum measurements on a $^{208}$Pb target
- Partial wave convergence required $L_i^{max}$ = 500 for Au and Ti targets, and 150 for the Be target
- CNI terms contribute 2-8% to the total breakup cross section, with constructive interference for Be and Ti targets and destructive interference for the Au target
- For the Be target, pure Coulomb contributions are down by at least an order of magnitude at forward angles and by 2-3 orders of magnitude at backward angles relative to nuclear contributions

## Context
This work extends the post-form DWBA theory previously developed by Chatterjee, Banerjee, and Shyam (2000) for pure Coulomb breakup of halo nuclei to include nuclear and interference terms consistently. It contrasts with prior analyses by Anne et al. (1994) and others that neglected CNI terms in halo breakup data. The authors note that prior-form DWBA calculations by Shyam and Thompson (1999) for $^8$B breakup were found to be insufficient due to higher-order coupling effects (Tostevin, Nunes, and Thompson, 2001), and that the present post-form approach may avoid some of those limitations for neutron halo nuclei. The work enables more accurate analysis of exclusive breakup data on medium-mass and heavy targets, which are now becoming available with higher-intensity secondary beams.
