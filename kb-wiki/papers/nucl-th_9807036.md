# ${\barΛ}/{\bar p}$ ratios in heavy ion collisions at 11.6 AGeV/c

> We attempt to explain the ${\bar Λ}/\bar p$ ratios measured in heavy ion collisions at $11.6~{\rm A\cdot GeV}/c$ beam momentum within a hadronic framework. This ratio is enhanced relative to corresponding ratios in $pp$ collisions, and is large when compared with thermal fits to heavy ion data. Using a detailed cascade calculation, we show that different annihilation cross--sections of $\bar Λ$'s and $\bar p$'s, and the net conversion of $\bar p$'s to $\bar Λ$'s, do not account for the enhancement in central collisions. For larger impact parameters, however, hadronic mechanisms may well suffice to produce the observed enhancement. Uncertainties in elementary cross--sections and formation times are considered.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of the $\bar \Lambda/\bar p$ ratio in heavy ion collisions at 11.6 AGeV/c cannot be explained by hadronic mechanisms (differential annihilation and conversion processes) for central collisions, but may suffice for larger impact parameters; the authors hedge that uncertainties in elementary cross-sections and formation times prevent a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors performed a cascade model calculation for Au+Au and Si+Pb collisions at 11.6 AGeV/c beam momentum, using experimental $p\bar p$ annihilation cross-sections and parameterized $\bar \Lambda$ annihilation cross-sections with a variable exponent $\alpha$ controlling the energy dependence. They inserted $\bar p$ and $\bar \Lambda$ particles by hand whenever a collision had sufficient energy for pair production, assuming the initial $\bar \Lambda/\bar p$ ratio from $pp$ data ($r_p \approx 0.25$), and calculated survival probabilities and conversion rates to obtain final ratios. A thermal model with chemical equilibration and feed-down contributions was also fitted to particle ratios from central Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The thermal model yields $\bar \Lambda/\bar p \lesssim 1.9$ for central Au+Au, below the E864 98% CL lower bound of 2.3.
- For central Au+Au with $\alpha=0$ (extreme differential annihilation) and zero formation time, the cascade gives $\bar \Lambda/\bar p = 2.4^{+0.2}_{-1.0}$.
- With more realistic $\alpha=0.4$ and $\tau=0$, the cascade gives $\bar \Lambda/\bar p = 0.7^{+0.1}_{-0.3}$ for central Au+Au.
- The $p\bar p$ annihilation cross-section is parameterized as $\sigma_{p\bar p}^{annih}(p_{lab}) = 67\, p_{lab}^{-0.7}$ mb.
- The $\bar \Lambda$ annihilation cross-section is parameterized as $\sigma_{p\bar \Lambda}^{annih}(p_{lab}) = 15\, (p_{lab}/10)^{-\alpha}$ mb, with best fit $\alpha=0.5$.
- The initial $\bar \Lambda/\bar p$ ratio in $pp$ collisions at AGS energies is inferred to be $\sim 0.2$, used as $r_p \approx 0.25^{+0.0}_{-0.1}$.
- E864 reports $\bar \Lambda/\bar p > 2.3$ at 98% CL for Au+Au, with most probable value 3.5.
- E859 reports $\bar \Lambda/\bar p = 3 \pm 1 \pm 1$ in central Si+Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends thermal model studies by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1995, 1996) and Heinz (1994) that found thermal fits could not reproduce the large $\bar \Lambda/\bar p$ ratios, and challenges the conclusion by Geiss et al. (1998) that a quark-gluon plasma scenario is required at AGS energies. The cascade calculation shows that hadronic mechanisms fail for central collisions but may explain peripheral data, leaving open the possibility that QGP formation in central events could be responsible. The results enable future work to focus on measuring the poorly known $\bar \Lambda$ annihilation cross-section and understanding medium effects on production and annihilation.
