# Density-matrix functionals for pairing in mesoscopic superconductors

> A functional theory based on single-particle occupation numbers is developed for pairing. This functional, that generalizes the BCS approach, directly incorporates corrections due to particle number conservation. The functional is benchmarked with the pairing Hamiltonian and reproduces perfectly the energy for any particle number and coupling.

## Key claim

The authors develop a functional theory for pairing based on single-particle occupation numbers that generalizes the BCS approach by directly incorporating particle-number conservation corrections, and they claim it reproduces the pairing Hamiltonian energy perfectly for any particle number and coupling, though the functional requires a linear approximation for the \(\alpha_i\) parameters that introduces a slight underestimation of energy in the intermediate coupling regime.

## Method

The authors start from a Projected BCS (PBCS) trial state with good particle number and derive expressions for occupation numbers \(n_i\) and two-body correlation matrix elements \(R_{ij}\) as functionals of the parameters \(\{x_i\}\). They then perform a \(1/N\) expansion to express the \(\alpha_i\) parameters (which encode particle-number restoration effects) as explicit functionals of the occupation numbers, and further approximate these with a linear form \(\alpha_i = a_0 + a_1 n_i\) to obtain a practical energy functional. The functional is minimized directly with respect to occupation numbers using quadratic programming, with a Lagrange multiplier to enforce particle number conservation.

## Key numbers

- For the pairing Hamiltonian with 8 particles (\(N=8\)) and 32 single-particle states (\(\Omega = 4N\)), the functional with successive corrections (first, second, third order) systematically improves upon BCS, converging to the exact Richardson solution; the BCS result deviates significantly from exact in both weak and strong coupling.
- For systems with various particle numbers (tested up to 400 particles) and coupling strengths \(g/\Delta\varepsilon\) ranging from weak to strong, the linear-approximation functional yields energies in very good agreement with exact results, with a slight underestimation in the intermediate coupling regime (e.g., for \(A=16\) at \(g/\Delta\varepsilon = 0.16\) and \(0.56\)).
- The linear approximation coefficients are given by \(a_1 = -\frac{1}{N} \frac{1 - s_2^N}{1 - s_2}\) and \(a_0 = 1 - (s_2 - s_3) \frac{\partial a_1}{\partial s_2}\), where \(s_p = \frac{1}{N} \sum_i (n_i)^p\).

## Context

This work extends the Gilbert functional approach (Gilbert 1975) to pairing in finite systems, addressing difficulties with particle-number projection in density functional theories that were identified by Dobaczewski (2007), Lacroix (2009), and others. The new functional avoids the divergences and jumps observed when combining projection techniques with standard functionals, and it can be directly implemented in existing functional theories for nuclei, ultrasmall metallic grains, and other mesoscopic superconductors. The approach enables future studies of finite-temperature and time-dependent properties of finite systems with pairing, and has already been applied to nuclei to resolve broken-symmetry difficulties.
