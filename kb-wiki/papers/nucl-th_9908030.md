# Statistical Mechanics of semi-classical colored Objects

> A microscopic model of deconfined matter based on color interactions between semi-classical quarks is studied. A hadronization mechanism is imposed to examine the properties and the disassembly of a thermalized quark plasma and to investigate the possible existence of a phase transition from quark matter to hadron matter.

## Key claim

A semi-classical model of deconfined quark matter, using a color interaction potential in an Abelian approximation of SU(3) and a dynamical hadronization criterion, produces a smooth crossover transition rather than a sharp first-order phase transition, with the critical temperature scaling as T_C ~ sqrt(F_cut * kappa) and requiring an in-medium string tension kappa > 2 kappa_0 to match lattice results.

## Method

The authors treat quarks as semi-classical particles interacting via a Cornell potential (linear confining term plus color Coulomb term) with color matrix elements derived from the diagonal Gell-Mann matrices lambda_3 and lambda_8 in an Abelian approximation. A Metropolis Monte Carlo algorithm generates thermal ensembles of up to 400 quarks and antiquarks in a spherical volume (radius 4 fm) at fixed temperature; quark clusters (q-qbar or qqq) whose net color force on the remaining system falls below a threshold F_cut * kappa are declared hadronized and mapped to mesonic or baryonic resonances via Breit-Wigner mass distributions.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature T_C ≈ 90 MeV for the natural choices kappa = kappa_0 ≈ 1 GeV/fm and F_cut = 0.01, compared to lattice predictions of T_C ≈ 150 MeV.
* To approach lattice results of T_C ≈ 140 MeV at mu = 0, values kappa ≳ 2 kappa_0 (i.e., ≳ 2 GeV/fm) are required.
* At T → 0, the chemical potential for the quark phase is about 350 MeV, placing normal nuclear matter (mu ≈ 300 MeV) within the hadronic region.
* The residual energy from field cut-off in hadronization is of order delta E/E ≲ 10^{-2}.
* The standard deviation of radial color force fluctuations in the center of the blob is sigma = 0.5 kappa.
* The hadron fraction xi drops from 1 to 0 within about 0.2 T_C, indicating a mixed phase.
* The model discriminates 29 mesonic and 36 baryonic states.

## Context

This work extends the line of effective models for the quark-gluon plasma phase transition, such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio approach of Rehberg (1998) (which lacks confinement) and the chromodielectric model of Traxler (1998) (which is numerically too expensive for heavy-ion simulations). The authors aim to unify partonic cascade models (e.g., VNI by Geiger, 1992) with hadronic transport models (e.g., UrQMD by Bass, 1999) into a single framework that dynamically includes a QGP phase transition. The results enable future dynamical simulations of complete heavy-ion collisions with a semi-classical deconfinement mechanism.
