# Parity inversion, breakdown of shell closure and particle-vibration coupling in Be-isotopes

> The coupling of single-particle motion and of vibrations in $^{11}_4$Be produces dressed neutrons which spend only a fraction of the time in pure single-particle states, and which weighting differently from the bare neutrons, lead to parity inversion. The interaction of the two least bound neutrons in the ground state of $^{12}_4$Be mediated by the $v_{14}$ Argonne nucleon-nucleon potential and by the exchange of the phonon cloud, give rise to a strongly correlated state, where the neutrons spend more than half of the time in (s$^2$+d$^2$)-configurations, resulting in a breaking of the N=8 shell closure.

# Parity inversion, breakdown of shell closure and particle-vibration coupling in Be-isotopes

## Key claim
The coupling of single-particle motion to collective surface vibrations in ¹¹Be produces dressed neutrons whose effective masses and spectroscopic factors differ sufficiently from the bare single-particle picture to invert the ordering of the 1s₁/₂ and 0p₁/₂ levels. In ¹²Be, the induced interaction between the two least bound neutrons, mediated by phonon exchange, generates a ground state where the neutrons spend more than half of their time in (s²+d²) configurations, breaking the N=8 shell closure.

## Method
The authors applied nuclear field theory (NFT) using Bloch-Horowitz perturbation theory, diagonalizing energy-dependent matrices of order 10²×10² for ¹¹Be and approximately 10³×10³ for ¹²Be. Single-particle states were obtained from a Schrodinger equation with a Saxon-Woods potential in a 30 fm spherical box, with continuum states discretized up to 350 MeV. The bare nucleon-nucleon interaction was the Argonne v₁₄ potential, and collective 2⁺ and 3⁻ vibrations were calculated in the random phase approximation (RPA) using a separable multipole-multipole interaction with self-consistent coupling constants adjusted to reproduce the quadrupole vibration energy.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies in ¹¹Be: 1/2⁺ state at -0.504 MeV (experiment) vs -0.48 MeV (theory); 1/2⁻ state at -0.18 MeV (experiment) vs -0.27 MeV (theory)
- Spectroscopic factors for ¹¹Be: S[1/2⁺] = 0.73±0.06 (experiment) vs 0.76 (theory); S[1/2⁻] = 0.63±0.15 (experiment) vs 0.79 (theory)
- Two-neutron separation energy for ¹²Be: -3.673 MeV (experiment) vs -3.58 MeV (theory)
- Spectroscopic factors for ¹²Be: S[1/2⁺] = 0.42±0.10 (experiment) vs 0.27 (theory); S[1/2⁻] = 0.37±0.10 (experiment) vs 0.32 (theory)
- Ground state wavefunction composition of ¹²Be: 23% s², 29% p², 48% d²
- Normalization factor N(¹²Be) = 0.47; N(¹¹Be) = 0.48
- Estimated mass enhancement factor λ ≈ 1.2 from experimental spectroscopic factors Z_ω ≈ 0.3-0.5
- Quadrupole vibration energy in ¹⁰Be: ω_C ≈ 3.37 MeV

## Context
This work extends the particle-vibration coupling formalism previously applied to nuclei along the stability valley to the neutron-rich halo nuclei ¹¹Be and ¹²Be. The results are consistent with large shell model calculations reported by Navin (2000) for ¹²Be and Otsuka (1993) for ¹¹Be, and with experimental knockout reaction data from Navin (2000). The calculations show that the induced interaction from phonon exchange dominates over the bare Argonne v₁₄ potential in ¹²Be because the low-angular-momentum phase space (s₁/₂, p₁/₂, d₅/₂) cannot exploit the high-multipole pairing components of the bare interaction. This work enables a microscopic understanding of how shell closures break in light neutron-rich nuclei and provides a framework for predicting spectroscopic factors and occupation probabilities in exotic systems.
