# Paramagnetic squeezing of a uniformly expanding quark-gluon plasma in and out of equilibrium

> The plasma of quarks and gluons created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions turns out to be paramagnetic. In the presence of a background magnetic field, this paramagnetism thus leads to a pressure anisotropy, similar to anisotropies appearing in a viscous fluid. In the present paper, we use this analogy, and develop a framework similar to anisotropic hydrodynamics, to take the pressure anisotropy caused, in particular, by the nonvanishing magnetization of a plasma of quarks and gluons into account. We consider the first two moments of the classical Boltzmann equation in the presence of an electromagnetic source in the relaxation-time approximation, and derive a set of coupled differential equations for the anisotropy parameter $ξ_0$ and the effective temperature $λ_0$ of an ideal fluid with nonvanishing magnetization. We also extend this method to a dissipative fluid with finite magnetization in the presence of a strong and dynamical magnetic field. We present a systematic method leading to the one-particle distribution function of this magnetized dissipative medium in a first-order derivative expansion, and arrive at analytical expressions for the shear and bulk viscosities in terms of the anisotropy parameter $ξ$ and effective temperature $λ$. We then solve the corresponding differential equations for $(ξ_0,λ_0)$ and $(ξ,λ)$ numerically, and determine, in this way, the proper time and temperature dependence of the energy density, directional pressures, speed of sound, and the magnetic susceptibility of a longitudinally expanding magnetized quark-gluon plasma in and out of equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper develops a framework analogous to anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) to describe the pressure anisotropy in a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma that arises from paramagnetic magnetization in a background magnetic field, rather than solely from viscous dissipation as in standard aHydro treatments.

## Method

The authors start from the classical Boltzmann equation with an electromagnetic source term in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) and take its first two moments to derive coupled differential equations for the anisotropy parameter ξ₀ and effective temperature λ₀ of an ideal magnetized fluid. They then extend the method to a dissipative fluid with finite magnetization in a strong dynamical magnetic field, obtaining the one-particle distribution function in a first-order derivative expansion and deriving analytical expressions for shear and bulk viscosities in terms of ξ and λ. The coupled equations for (ξ₀, λ₀) and (ξ, λ) are solved numerically to determine the proper-time evolution of thermodynamic quantities for a longitudinally expanding magnetized QGP under Bjorken flow conditions.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field evolves as B(τ) ∝ τ⁻¹ in ideal transverse MHD, with B = B̄ (τ̄/τ) where τ is proper time and τ̄ is the initial time.
- For vanishing magnetic susceptibility (χₘ = 0), the pressure evolves as p = p̄ (τ̄/τ)^(1+1/κ) and the temperature as T = T̄ (τ̄/τ)^(1/κ), where κ is the constant from the ideal gas equation of state ε = κp.
- The entropy density evolves as s = s̄ (τ̄/τ) in the Bjorken flow.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework of Florkowski (2010) and Strickland (2010) by incorporating pressure anisotropy from paramagnetic magnetization of the QGP, which was previously studied in static systems by Ferrer (2010), Martinez (2014), and Fayazbakhsh (2014). The paper also builds on the transverse magnetohydrodynamics formalism developed by Rischke (2015, 2016) and Shokri (2017, 2018) for Bjorken-expanding plasmas. The results enable future studies of how paramagnetic squeezing contributes to elliptic flow v₂ in heavy-ion collisions, complementing the lattice QCD findings of D'Elia (2013) and Bali (2013) on the temperature dependence of magnetic susceptibility.
