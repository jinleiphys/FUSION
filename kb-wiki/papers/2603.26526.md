# Non-eikonal corrections to dijet production in DIS

> We compute non-eikonal corrections to dijet production in deep inelastic scattering off a nucleus. Such corrections are expected to be quantitatively important at the energies of the future Electron Ion Collider. We focus on those corrections stemming solely from the finite longitudinal size of the nucleus. For both longitudinally and transversely polarized photons, we provide general, all-order expressions in terms of two-dimensional path integrals. To proceed further, we use the harmonic oscillator approximation for the target averages of Wilson lines. We then expand the general expressions order by order beyond the shockwave limit which provides the eikonal results, up to next-to-next-to-eikonal accuracy. We observe that next-to-eikonal corrections to this observable vanish for the mentioned approximation for target averages, as previously found for single gluon production in proton-nucleus collisions. Finally, we calculate the back-to-back of correlation limit of our expressions.

## Key claim

Non-eikonal corrections to dijet production in deep inelastic scattering off a nucleus, stemming solely from the finite longitudinal size of the nucleus, are computed to all orders using a path integral formalism; next-to-eikonal corrections vanish under the harmonic oscillator approximation for target averages of Wilson lines, as previously found for single gluon production in proton-nucleus collisions.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitude for virtual photon-nucleus scattering into a quark-antiquark pair, separating contributions where the photon splits before entering the medium, inside the medium, and after traversing the medium. They upgrade the eikonal Wilson lines to in-medium propagators given by two-dimensional path integrals that account for transverse diffusion, and evaluate the medium averages of two- and four-point functions in the Gaussian approximation using the harmonic oscillator (multiple soft scattering) model. The general expressions are then expanded order by order beyond the shockwave limit up to next-to-next-to-eikonal accuracy, and the back-to-back correlation limit is studied.

## Key numbers

- The non-eikonal effects are significant for centre-of-mass energies sqrt(s_NN) less than or approximately 100 GeV, which are the energies of interest for the future Electron Ion Collider.
- The dipole cross section in the harmonic oscillator approximation is given by C_F n(t) sigma(r(t)) approximately equal to (Q_s^2)/(4L^+) r^2(t), where Q_s is the saturation scale and L^+ is the longitudinal extent of the nucleus.
- The two-point function in the harmonic oscillator approximation is S^(2)_ij(t,t_0) = exp[-(Q_s^2)/(4L^+) integral from t_0 to t of dxi |r_i(xi) - r_j(xi)|^2].
- The transition amplitude T(xi) in the four-point function is (Q_s^2)/(2L^+) (r_1 - r_bar_1) dot (r_2 - r_bar_2).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of non-eikonal corrections to CGC calculations by Altinoluk et al. (2014, 2015) and Agostini et al. (2024) by using an all-order formalism from jet quenching (Casalderrey-Solana 2007, Mehtar-Tani 2013, Blaizot 2015) that resums all orders of non-eikonal corrections via path integrals, going beyond the next-to-eikonal order considered earlier. It enables more precise theoretical predictions for dijet production at the Electron Ion Collider, where non-eikonal effects are expected to be quantitatively important, and establishes a connection between CGC calculations and transverse momentum distributions in the correlation limit.
