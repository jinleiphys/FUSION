# X17: A new force, or evidence for a hard $γ+γ$ process?

> It is investigated whether the "X17 puzzle" might be explained by a nuclear decay chain and a conversion of the two resulting highly energetic $γ$s into an electron-positron pair. It is found that the corresponding kinematics fits perfectly to the experimental result. Also the conversion rates of this process are reasonable. However, the assumed nuclear chain reaction is not favored in the established nuclear models and no explanation for the isospin structure of the signal can be given. Thus, it has to be concluded that the process studied in this paper does not give a completely satisfying explanation of the "X17 puzzle".

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the X17 anomaly can be explained by a modified Bethe-Heitler process involving two sequential gamma emissions from a nuclear decay chain converting into an electron-positron pair, rather than by a new particle. The kinematic match is nearly perfect and conversion rates are reasonable, but the required nuclear chain reaction is not favored in established nuclear models and cannot explain the observed isospin structure, so the proposed process does not give a completely satisfying explanation.

## Method

The author considers a modified Bethe-Heitler process where an excited 8Be nucleus emits two sequential gamma rays via a broad intermediate nuclear state, and these two gammas convert into an electron-positron pair. The kinematics are calculated in an on-shell approximation using conservation of energy and momentum, with the angular distribution estimated from classical multipole radiation formulas. The conversion probability is estimated from the gamma-gamma to electron-positron cross section combined with a geometric overlap factor and a suppression factor from the finite width of the intermediate state.

## Key numbers

- The broad intermediate state is 8Be*(J^pi=4+, T=0) at 11.35 MeV above ground state with width Gamma = 3.5 MeV
- The kinematic relation m_X^2 = 4 (Delta M_12)(E_13 - Delta M_12) sin^2(theta/2) gives curves that cross the observed data point almost perfectly with no tuned parameters
- The predicted most likely relative angles are (144 +/- 14) degrees for N=3 and (152 +/- 11) degrees for N=4
- The total cross section for gamma+gamma to e+e- is 3.5 x 10^-6 MeV^-2
- The conversion probability for the MBH process is estimated as p = 7 x 10^-4, compared to the background internal pair creation probability p_bg = 25 x 10^-4
- The angular width from off-shell contributions is delta' theta_rel = +/- 8 degrees, smaller than the measured Gamma_theta = 26 degrees
- The Gaussian fit to the angular distribution gives theta_rel = 145 degrees with halfwidth Gamma_theta = 26 degrees and statistical error of 18%

## Context

This work extends the critical analysis of the X17 puzzle by considering alternative explanations within standard nuclear physics, following the experimental results of Krasznahorkay (2015) which found a 17 MeV resonance in electron-positron pairs from excited 8Be. The paper contrasts with the dominant theoretical approach of invoking new mediator particles (Feng 2016, Fayet 2016, and others) and builds on the effective field theory treatment of Zhang (2017) and the critical revision by Fornal (2017). The proposed mechanism cannot simultaneously explain the similar 4He result from Krasznahorkay (2019), and the author suggests that measuring the angular distribution of the unconverted gamma rays would provide a smoking gun test.
