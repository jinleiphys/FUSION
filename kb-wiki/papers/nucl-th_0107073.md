# Kinematical versus dynamical relativistic effects in A(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})B

> The relativistic mean field approach is applied to the description of coincidence $A(e,e'p)B$ reactions where both the incident electron beam and knockout proton are polarized. Effects introduced by the dynamical enhancement of the lower components of the bound nucleon wave function are analyzed within RPWIA for the polarized responses and transferred polarizations. Results obtained by projecting out the negative-energy components are also compared with various nonrelativistic reductions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), the dynamical enhancement of the lower components of the bound nucleon wave function (negative-energy projections) produces significant modifications to polarized response functions and transferred polarization observables in \(A(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})B\) reactions, particularly at high missing momenta and forward electron scattering angles, but the magnitude of these effects depends on the choice of current operator (CC1 vs. CC2) and kinematics.

## Method

The authors applied the relativistic mean field approach within RPWIA to the reaction \(^{16}\text{O}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})^{15}\text{N}\), using a Dirac-Hartree bound state wave function from the Walecka model (HS parameter set, TIMORA code). They computed the four polarized hadronic response functions (\(R^{T'}_l\), \(R^{TL'}_l\), \(R^{T'}_s\), \(R^{TL'}_s\)) and the transferred polarization components (\(P'_l\), \(P'_s\)) for two kinematics: (\(q-\omega\)) constant kinematics (q=500 MeV/c, \(\omega\)=131.56 MeV) and parallel kinematics (outgoing nucleon kinetic energy 120.2 MeV). They decomposed each response into positive-energy, crossed, and negative-energy contributions to isolate dynamical relativistic effects, and compared fully relativistic results with positive-energy projected calculations and with nonrelativistic reductions expanded in powers of \(p_m/M_N\).

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer \(q = 500\) MeV/c and energy transfer \(\omega = 131.56\) MeV for (\(q-\omega\)) constant kinematics, corresponding to the quasielastic peak.
- Outgoing nucleon kinetic energy fixed at 120.2 MeV for parallel kinematics.
- For forward electron scattering (\(\theta_e = 30^\circ\)), the electron beam energy is \(\varepsilon_e = 1\) GeV; for backward scattering (\(\theta_e = 150^\circ\)), \(\varepsilon_e = 324\) MeV.
- Dynamical relativistic effects from negative-energy projections become visible for missing momenta \(p_m > 300\) MeV/c in the transferred polarizations.
- In (\(q-\omega\)) constant kinematics, the crossed term contribution to \(R^{TL'}_l\) is similar in magnitude to the positive-energy projection contribution.
- For forward angle (\(\theta_e = 30^\circ\)), the sideways transferred polarization \(P'_s\) from nonrelativistic reductions exceeds 1 in absolute value at high \(p_m\), indicating unphysical negative cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic analysis of Caballero et al. (1998) on unpolarized responses in \(A(e,e'p)B\) reactions to the polarized case with both beam and recoil polarization, and builds on earlier studies of relativistic effects in unpolarized observables by Udi et al. (1999, 2000). The results are relevant to interpreting recent polarization transfer measurements on \(^{16}\text{O}\) by Malov et al. (2000) and on \(^{4}\text{He}\) by Dieterich et al. (2001), where standard nonrelativistic calculations disagreed with data. The paper enables future work incorporating final-state interactions within a relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) to test whether the sensitivity to negative-energy projections persists in more realistic calculations.
