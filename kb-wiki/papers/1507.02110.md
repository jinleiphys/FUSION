# Spontaneous magnetization of quark matter in the inhomogeneous chiral phase

> Considering the density wave of scalar and pseudoscalar condensates, we study the response of quark matter to a weak external magnetic field. In an external magnetic field, the energy spectrum of the lowest Landau level becomes asymmetric about zero, which is closely related to chiral anomaly, and gives rise to the spontaneous magnetization. This mechanism may be one of candidates for the origin of the strong magnetic field in pulsars and/or magnetars.

# Spontaneous magnetization of quark matter in the inhomogeneous chiral phase

## Key claim

Quark matter in the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase exhibits spontaneous magnetization in the limit of a vanishing external magnetic field, arising from spectral asymmetry of the lowest Landau level which is closely related to chiral anomaly. This magnetization only appears when both the scalar condensate amplitude m and the wavevector q are nonzero, meaning it is a property exclusive to the inhomogeneous chiral phase and not present in the homogeneously chiral-broken or chiral-restored phases.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation with the DCDW condensate form Δ(r) = Δ e^{iqz}, where the scalar and pseudoscalar condensates spatially modulate along the z-axis. They expand the thermodynamic potential in powers of the external magnetic field B, compute the linear response coefficient Ω^(1) by summing over Landau levels, and carefully treat the spectral asymmetry of the lowest Landau level using the η-invariant regularization. The spontaneous magnetization M_0 is obtained as M_0 = -e Ω^(1) evaluated at the zero-field order parameters m^(0) and q^(0) that minimize the B=0 thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff in proper time regularization is Λ = 660 MeV with GΛ² = 6.35, which reproduce pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV and constituent quark mass ≈ 330 MeV in vacuum.
- For a sphere of quark matter with uniform magnetization in the DCDW phase at zero temperature, the surface magnetic field is estimated B ∼ 10¹⁶ G, comparable to magnetar observations.
- The spontaneous magnetization vanishes with the disappearance of the DCDW phase at T ∼ 100 MeV.
- At T ≈ 30 MeV, a temperature relevant for newly born neutron stars, the magnetic field remains of similar order of magnitude to the zero-temperature estimate.
- In the limit where no valence quarks are present (μ < m - q/2 at T=0), the magnetization reduces to the Wess-Zumino-Witten term contribution M_0 = μ N_c q / (4π²).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the DCDW phase previously studied in the NJL model by Nakano and Tatsumi, and builds on the finding by Frolov et al. that the DCDW wavevector aligns parallel to an external magnetic field. The mechanism differs from earlier proposals for spontaneous magnetization in quark matter via spin alignment (Tatsumi, 2000) or via axial anomaly acting on pion domain walls in nuclear matter (Son and Eto). The authors show that the magnetization arises from a combination of the anomalous Wess-Zumino-Witten term and valence quark contributions, making the fermion degrees of freedom indispensable. This work enables future studies of magnetic properties such as magnetic susceptibility and Nambu-Goldstone spin-wave modes in the DCDW phase, and motivates investigations of whether the DCDW phase survives under realistic neutron star conditions including charge neutrality, chemical equilibrium, and finite current quark mass.
