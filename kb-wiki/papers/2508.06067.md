# Examining potential energy surface through Chebyshev shape parametrization

> The present study introduce a novel approach, the Chebyshev shape parametrization, to describe the geometric configurations of atomic nuclei, with a particular emphasis on fission dynamics. In this framework, the nuclear surface is represented by a profile function expanded in a Chebyshev polynomial series, with deformation parameters derived analytically under volume conservation and centre-of-mass constraints. The proposed parametrization is shown to be universal robust, and we establish transformation equations that connect it to other widely used shape parametrizations. In the macroscopic approach, the potential energy surface (PES) is computed using the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) model, incorporating deformation-dependent energy coefficients expressed in terms of Chebyshev parameters. This enables a detailed investigation of the structural evolution and fission pathways of the nucleus Pa-227 across various deformations, depicting the influence of shape parameters on elongation, asymmetry, and neck formation. Complementing this, microscopic analysis is carried out by calculating single-particle energy levels through diagonalization of the Yukawa-folded mean-field Hamiltonian in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis. The nuclear shape parameters are provided by the Chebyshev parametrization, allowing us to examine shell structure effects at specific deformations. Together, these macroscopic and microscopic studies provide comprehensive insight into the nuclear energy landscape and shape evolution during fission.

# Examining potential energy surface through Chebyshev shape parametrization

## Key claim

The Chebyshev shape parametrization provides a universal, rapidly converging framework for describing nuclear shapes across the fission process, but its practical application to potential energy surface calculations for 227Pa uses only three Chebyshev parameters (a2, a3, a4) plus a non-axiality parameter eta, with higher-order coefficients shown to contribute negligibly (a6 approximately 0.01 compared to a2 of 0.2).

## Method

The nuclear surface is represented by a profile function expanded in Chebyshev polynomials of the first kind in cylindrical coordinates, with deformation parameters derived analytically under volume conservation and center-of-mass constraints. Macroscopic potential energy surfaces are computed using the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) model with deformation-dependent energy coefficients expressed in terms of Chebyshev parameters, while microscopic single-particle energy levels are calculated through diagonalization of a Yukawa-folded mean-field Hamiltonian in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis. Transformation equations are established connecting the Chebyshev parametrization to Bohr-Mottelson, Funny-Hills, TKS, and Fourier shape parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- For 227Pa, the scission point in the (a2, a4) deformation subspace is reached at a2 = 0.5 and a4 = -0.26, with the scission region spanning a4 from -0.2 to -0.28
- In convergence analysis, the a2 coefficient has a value of 0.2, while a6 drops to approximately 0.01 (over an order of magnitude lower); a5 at 0.02 is more than an order of magnitude smaller than a3 at approximately 0.3
- The LSD model parameters used include: b_surf = 16.9707 MeV, k_surf = 2.2938, b_curv = 3.8602 MeV, k_curv = -2.3764, r0 = 1.21725 fm
- The congruence energy strength parameters are C3 = 10 MeV and W2 = 42 MeV
- For spheroidal shapes, the relation between elongation c and a2 is c = -1/(2a2), with spherical shapes at a2 = -1/2, prolate shapes for -1/2 < a2 < 0, and oblate shapes for a2 < -1/2

## Context

This work extends the line of nuclear shape parametrization methods including Bohr-Mottelson (beta,gamma) parametrization, Funny-Hills shapes, modified Funny-Hills, Trentalange-Koonin-Sierk (TKS) parametrization, and Fourier shape parametrization, addressing limitations such as the complexity of Bohr-Mottelson for highly deformed structures and the unphysical negative rho-squared solutions in Funny-Hills parametrization. The Chebyshev parametrization enables investigation of fission pathways for 227Pa, a nucleus previously studied by Reddy and Saneesh for symmetric and asymmetric fission modes, and provides a framework that can be extended to other nuclei and to include higher-order deformation parameters for more detailed studies of the fission process.
