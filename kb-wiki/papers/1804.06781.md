# Pion-kaon femtoscopy in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV modeled in (3+1)D hydrodynamics coupled to Therminator 2 and the effect of delayed kaon emission

> Non-identical particle femtoscopy measures the size of the system emitting particles ("radius") in heavy-ion collisions as well as the difference between mean emission space-time coordinates of two particle species ("emission asymmetry"). The system created in such collisions at the LHC behaves collectively and its dynamics is well described by hydrodynamic models. A significant emission asymmetry between pions and kaons, coming from collective flow, enhanced by contribution from flowing resonances is predicted. We present calculations within the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model coupled to statistical hadronization code Therminator 2, corresponding to Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV. We obtain femtoscopic radii and emission asymmetry for pion-kaon pairs as a function of collision centrality. The radii grow linearly with cube root of particle multiplicity density. The emission asymmetry is negative and comparable to the radius, indicating that pions are emitted closer to the center of the system and/or later than kaons. Recent ALICE Collaboration measurements of identical kaon femtoscopy shows that kaons are emitted, on average, 2.1~fm/$c$ later than pions. We modify our calculation by introducing such delay and find that the system source size is only weakly affected. In contrast the pion-kaon emission asymmetry is directly sensitive to such delays and the modified calculation shows significantly lower values of asymmetry. Therefore we propose the measurement of the pion-kaon femtoscopic correlation function as a sensitive probe of the time delays in particle emission.

# Pion-kaon femtoscopy in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV modeled in (3+1)D hydrodynamics coupled to Therminator 2 and the effect of delayed kaon emission

## Key claim
The pion-kaon emission asymmetry extracted from femtoscopic correlation functions is directly and linearly sensitive to any delays in kaon emission time, with the default (3+1)D hydrodynamic model predicting an asymmetry of approximately -6 fm for central collisions that reduces to approximately -4 fm when a 2.1 fm/c kaon emission delay is introduced. This sensitivity provides a cross-check of the interpretation that kaons are emitted later than pions due to hadronic rescattering via the K* resonance, as suggested by ALICE identical kaon femtoscopy data.

## Method
The authors used a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model (second order Israel-Stewart equations, eta/s=0.08, zeta/s=0.04, freeze-out temperature 140 MeV) with smooth Glauber initial conditions, coupled to the Therminator 2 statistical hadronization and resonance decay code, to generate events for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV at five impact parameters corresponding to 0-10% through 40-50% centrality. Pion-kaon femtoscopic correlation functions were calculated via an afterburner procedure using only the Coulomb interaction (Gamov factor and confluent hypergeometric function), and fitted with a Gaussian source model assuming sigma_side = sigma_out and sigma_long = 1.3 sigma_out to extract the system size sigma_out and emission asymmetry mu_out. Additional calculations were performed by adding a Gaussian-distributed time delay to each kaon's emission time, with mean values of 1.0, 2.1, and 3.2 fm/c and widths of 0.3 or 2.0 fm/c.

## Key numbers
- The default calculation yields a pion-kaon emission asymmetry of approximately -6 fm for central (0-10%) Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV.
- Introducing a kaon emission time delay of 2.1 fm/c (with width 2 fm/c) reduces the asymmetry to approximately -4 fm for central collisions.
- A time delay of 3.2 fm/c causes the emission asymmetry to become positive for the most peripheral collisions (40-50% centrality).
- The Bohr radius for the pion-kaon pair is 248.52 fm (sign depending on charge combination).
- Pions and kaons were selected in the transverse momentum range 0.15 to 2.5 GeV/c and pseudorapidity range |eta| < 1.0.
- The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is 0.6 fm/c.
- The system size grows by approximately 0.5 fm when the kaon time delay is introduced, with the increase being larger for larger delay values.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous identical particle femtoscopy calculations (Kisiel, 2014) using the same (3+1)D hydrodynamic model coupled to Therminator 2, which were compared to ALICE data on identical kaon femtoscopy (Acharya, 2017). Those data showed that kaons are emitted on average 2.1 fm/c later than pions, attributed to rescattering via the K* meson, and could not be reproduced by the Therminator 2 model alone but required a hadronic rescattering code (Shapoval, 2014). The present calculations provide predictions for pion-kaon emission asymmetry that can be directly compared to ALICE measurements, enabling an independent test of the existence of the hadronic rescattering phase at LHC energies. The formalism follows earlier work on non-identical particle femtoscopy at RHIC (Kisiel, 2009) which established that emission asymmetry arises from collective flow enhanced by resonance decay effects.
