# Quantum corrections to the ground state energy of inhomogeneous neutron matter

> We estimate the quantum corrections to the ground state energy in neutron matter (which could be termed as well either shell correction energy or Casimir energy) at subnuclear densities, where various types of inhomogeneities (bubbles, rods, plates) are energetically favorable. We show that the magnitude of these energy corrections are comparable to the energy differences between various types of inhomogeneous phases. We discuss the dependence of these corrections on a number of physical parameters (density, filling factor, temperature, lattice distortions).

## Key claim

The quantum corrections (shell or Casimir energy) to the ground state energy of inhomogeneous neutron matter are comparable in magnitude to the energy differences between various inhomogeneous phases (bubbles, rods, plates), but the authors use a simplified hard-wall potential model that likely overestimates the magnitude of these shell effects while preserving the qualitative pattern.

## Method

The authors compute the shell correction energy for plate-like, rod-like, and spherical bubble-like phases in neutron matter using a semiclassical approximation with a hard-wall potential model for the inhomogeneities. For the plate-like phase the shell energy is evaluated exactly, while for rod-like and spherical phases the Gutzwiller trace formula is used to sum contributions from the shortest periodic orbits between nearest neighbors on simple cubic and simple square lattices. The chemical potential is determined self-consistently by requiring the average neutron density to match a given value.

## Key numbers

- Shell energy amplitudes in the density range ρ₀ ≈ 0.04–0.07 fm⁻³ are of order 10 keV/fm³ for plate-like, 3 keV/fm³ for rod-like, and 0.05 keV/fm³ for bubble-like phases.
- The density range for phase transitions between nuclei, rods, plates, tubes, bubbles, and uniform matter is 0.04–0.1 fm⁻³, citing Lorenz (1993) and Pethick (1995).
- These phases exist up to temperatures of about 10 MeV, citing Lattimer (1985).
- The bubble-bubble interaction energy at large separations (a = L − 2R ≫ R) is approximately E_∘∘ ≈ (ℏ² k_F R²)/(8π m) × cos(2k_F a)/a³.
- For the plate-like phase, the shell energy per volume is E_shell/L³ = (ℏ² k_F²)/(40 a² L m) × G(k_F a/π), where G(x) is an approximate periodic function with −1 ≤ G(x) ≤ 1.

## Context

This work extends earlier liquid drop model and Thomas-Fermi calculations (Lattimer 1985, Lorenz 1993, Pethick 1995) that predicted small energy differences between inhomogeneous phases but omitted quantum corrections. It contradicts the conclusion of Oyamatsu (1993) that shell corrections from bound nucleons only are smaller than phase energy differences; the authors find larger corrections from delocalized states. The results enable future studies of how shell effects influence the sequence of nuclear shape transitions in neutron star crusts and the positional ordering of voids, and raise open questions about the role of resonant states and the accuracy of the hard-wall approximation for realistic neutron skin thicknesses.
