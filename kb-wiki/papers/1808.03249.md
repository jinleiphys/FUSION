# Neutrino induced reactions in core-collapse supernovae: effects on the electron fraction

> Neutrino induced reactions are a basic ingredient in astrophysical processes like star evolution. The existence of neutrino oscillations affects the rate of nuclear electroweak decays which participates in the chain of events that determines the fate of the star. Among the processes of interest, the production of heavy elements in core-collapse supernovae is strongly dependent upon neutrino properties, like the mixing between different species of neutrinos. In this work we study the effects of neutrino oscillations upon the electron fraction as a function of the neutrino mixing parameters, for two schemes: the $1+1$ scheme (one active neutrino and one sterile neutrino) and the $2+1$ scheme (two active neutrinos and one sterile neutrino). We have performed this analysis considering a core-collapse supernovae and determined the physical conditions needed to activate the nuclear reaction chains involved in the r-process. We found that the interactions of the neutrinos with matter and among themselves and the initial amount of sterile neutrinos in the neutrino-sphere might change the electron fraction, therefore affecting the onset of the r-process. We have set constrains on the active-sterile neutrino mixing parameters. They are the square-mass-difference $Δm^2_{14} $, the mixing angle $\sin^2 2θ_{14}$, and the hindrance factor $ξ_s $ for the occupation of sterile neutrinos. The calculations have been performed for different values of $X_α$, which is the fraction of $α$-particles. For $X_α=0$ the r-process is taking place if $Δm^2_{14} \geq 2 \, {\rm eV}^2 $, $\sin^2 2θ_{14} < 0.8 $ and $ξ_s < 0.5$. For larger values of $X_α$ the region of parameters is strongly reduced. The present results are compared to results available in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the inclusion of sterile neutrinos can significantly reduce the electron fraction in core-collapse supernovae, but only under specific conditions: the r-process is favored only when the hindrance factor for sterile neutrino occupation is small ($\xi_s < 0.5$) and the alpha-particle fraction is zero or very small; for larger $X_\alpha$ values the allowed parameter region is strongly reduced or eliminated.

## Method

The authors solved the differential equations for neutrino and antineutrino distribution functions as a function of stellar radius, using both a 1+1 scheme (one active plus one sterile neutrino) and a 2+1 scheme (two active plus one sterile neutrino). The Hamiltonian included vacuum oscillations, neutrino-matter interactions, and neutrino-neutrino interactions in the single-angle approximation. The electron fraction was computed under weak equilibrium conditions for the late cooling phase of the neutrino-driven wind (post-bounce time ~10 seconds), using Fermi-Dirac and power-law neutrino distribution functions with mean energies taken from Qian (2003) and Balantekin (2004).

## Key numbers

* For $X_\alpha=0$ in the 1+1 scheme, the r-process requires $\Delta m^2_{14} \geq 2 \, {\rm eV}^2$, $\sin^2 2\theta_{14} < 0.8$, and $\xi_s < 0.5$.
* For the 1+1 scheme at $R=150$ km with $X_\alpha=0$, the allowed region requires $\Delta m_{14}^2 > 1.5 \, {\rm eV}^2$; at $R=200$ km, $\Delta m_{14}^2 > 1 \, {\rm eV}^2$; at $R=250$ km, $\Delta m_{14}^2 > 0.5 \, {\rm eV}^2$.
* For the 2+1 scheme at $R=150$ km with $X_\alpha=0$, the condition $\Delta m_{14}^2 > 2 \, {\rm eV}^2$ is consistent with the r-process at small radius.
* For the 2+1 scheme with $\Delta m_{14}^2 = 2 \, {\rm eV}^2$, the mixing angle must satisfy $\sin^2 2\theta_{14} < 0.55$ and $\xi_s < 0.1$ to obtain $Y_e < 0.5$.
* The mean neutrino energies used were: $<E_{\nu_e}>= 10\, {\rm MeV}$, $<E_{\overline{\nu}_e}>= 15\, {\rm MeV}$, $<E_{\nu_x}>= <E_{\overline{\nu}_x}>= 24\, {\rm MeV}$.
* The neutrino-sphere radius was taken as $R_\nu = 10 \, {\rm km}$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of active-sterile neutrino mixing in supernovae performed by Nunokawa (1997) and Tamborra (2012), and uses the formalism of Balantekin (2004) for the supernova environment. The results are compared to those of Keil (2003), Tamborra (2012), and Pllumbi (2015), particularly regarding the use of power-law versus Fermi-Dirac neutrino distributions. The study enables constraints on sterile neutrino parameters ($\Delta m^2_{14}$, $\sin^2 2\theta_{14}$, $\xi_s$) from the requirement that the electron fraction remain below 0.48 for r-process nucleosynthesis, and shows that the presence of alpha particles strongly suppresses the allowed parameter space.
