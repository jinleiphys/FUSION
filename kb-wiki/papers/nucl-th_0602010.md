# Time-Reversal Violation in Threshold $\vec{n}$ $\vec{p}$ Scattering

> We investigate parity and time-reversal violation in neutron-proton scattering in the optical regime. We calculate the neutron spin rotation and analyzing power in scattering on polarized protons. This allows us to quantify the sensitivity that such experiments should aim for in order to be competitive to present-day measurements of the neutron electric dipole moment in constraining the $P$- and $T$-odd two-nucleon interaction. While state-of-the-art techniques fall short by some three orders of magnitude for the neutron-proton case, specific neutron-nucleus experiments look promising, provided certain experimental and theoretical challenges are met.

## Key claim

While state-of-the-art neutron spin rotation experiments with polarized hydrogen targets fall short by some three orders of magnitude compared to neutron electric dipole moment measurements in constraining the P- and T-odd two-nucleon interaction, specific neutron-nucleus experiments exploiting p-wave resonances could become competitive if certain experimental and theoretical challenges are met.

## Method

The authors calculate the neutron spin rotation and analyzing power for scattering of polarized neutrons on polarized protons using first-order Born approximation, with distorted wave functions obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with high-quality local nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, Reid93, Nijm-II). The parity- and time-reversal-violating interactions are described by a one-meson-exchange model parametrized by meson-nucleon coupling constants, and the observables are evaluated at thermal neutron energy across a range from epithermal to very cold energies.

## Key numbers

- For thermal neutrons (E_n = 0.025 eV) with target density N = 0.4 × 10^23 /cm^3, the differential spin rotation dφ_z/dz using the AV18 potential is: 1.130 h_π^1 - 0.283 h_ρ^0 + 0.008 h_ρ^1 + 0.250 h_ρ^2 - 0.269 h_ω^0 - 0.024 h_ω^1 rad/m
- Using DDH "best values" for the PV couplings, dφ_z/dz ≈ 6.5 × 10^(-7) rad/m
- The proposed PV neutron spin rotation experiment in para-hydrogen aims for an accuracy of ~2.7 × 10^(-7) rad/m
- The current most stringent upper limit on the neutron EDM is d_n < 6.3 × 10^(-26) e-cm (Harris, 1999)
- A PV neutron transmission measurement exploiting the 0.734 eV p-wave resonance of 139La gave dφ_z/dz = (7.4 ± 1.1) × 10^(-1) rad/m (Haseyama, 2001)
- The differential spin rotation dφ̃_y/dz for the PVTV case at thermal energy with the AV18 potential gives coefficients: 7.758 for ḡ_π^0, 1.131 for ḡ_π^1, -28.180 for ḡ_π^2 (in rad/m per coupling constant)
- The differential analyzing power dP̃_y/dz at thermal energy with AV18 gives coefficients: 2.830 × 10^(-3) for ḡ_π^0, -0.106 × 10^(-3) for ḡ_π^1, -11.589 × 10^(-3) for ḡ_π^2 (in 10^(-3)/m per coupling constant)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for parity violation in neutron optics, developed by Stodolsky (1981) and Kabir (1981, 1987, 1988), to time-reversal violation in neutron-proton scattering. The calculations use the DDH potential for parity violation (Desplanques, 1980) and the complete PVTV potential from Liu (2004). The authors show that while neutron spin rotation in polarized hydrogen cannot compete with neutron EDM experiments (Harris, 1999), the 10^6 enhancement factor observed in PV neutron-nucleus experiments with 139La (Haseyama, 2001) suggests that analogous PVTV measurements could become competitive with planned EDM experiments targeting the 10^(-27) to 10^(-28) e-cm level, provided experimental sensitivity improves by 3-4 orders of magnitude and theoretical interpretation of nuclear observables in terms of fundamental couplings is developed to comparable accuracy.
