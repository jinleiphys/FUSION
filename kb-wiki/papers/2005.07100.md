# Spectrum of the Hidden-Bottom and the Hidden-Charm/Strange Exotics in the Dynamical Diquark Model

> The lightest hidden-bottom tetraquarks in the dynamical diquark model fill an $S$-wave multiplet consisting of 12 isomultiplets. We predict their masses and dominant bottomonium decay channels using a simple 3-parameter Hamiltonian that captures the core fine-structure features of the model, including isospin dependence. The only experimental inputs needed are the corresponding observables for $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$. The mass of $X_b$, the bottom analogue to $X(3872)$, is highly constrained in this scheme. In addition, using lattice-calculated potentials we predict the location of the center of mass of the $P$-wave multiplet and find that $Y(10860)$ fits well but the newly discovered $Y(10750)$ does not, more plausibly being a $D$-wave bottomonium state. Using similar methods, we also examine the lowest $S$-wave multiplet of 6 $c\bar c s\bar s$ states, assuming as in earlier work that $X(3915)$ and $Y(4140)$ are members, and predict the masses and dominant charmonium decay modes of the other states. We again use lattice potentials to compute the centers of mass of higher multiplets, and find them to be compatible with the masses of $Y(4626)$ ($1P$) and $X(4700)$ ($2S$), respectively.

## Key claim

The dynamical diquark model predicts the masses and dominant decay channels of the lightest hidden-bottom tetraquark multiplet (12 isomultiplets) using only the experimental masses of Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) as inputs, but the model's success depends on the assumption that internal diquark spin couplings dominate over inter-diquark couplings and that isospin dependence couples to light quarks rather than to pointlike diquarks.

## Method

The authors used a 3-parameter Hamiltonian (common multiplet mass M_0, diquark spin-spin coupling κ_qb, and isospin-dependent light-quark spin coupling V_0) applied to the Σ^+_g(1S) Born-Oppenheimer potential multiplet, solving the Schrödinger equation with lattice-calculated potentials for the diquark-antidiquark system. For the hidden-bottom sector, they fixed parameters using the masses of Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650); for the hidden-charm/strange sector, they used a 2-parameter Hamiltonian (M_0 and κ_sc) assuming X(3915) and Y(4140) are members of the S-wave multiplet. They also computed centers of mass for higher multiplets (1P, 2S) using lattice potentials.

## Key numbers

- Z_b(10610) mass: 10607.2 ± 2.0 MeV (I^G J^PC = 1^+ 1^+-)
- Z_b(10650) mass: 10652.2 ± 1.5 MeV (I^G J^PC = 1^+ 1^+-)
- Y(10860) mass: 10889.9^{+3.2}_{-2.6} MeV, fits well as a Σ^+_g(1P) state
- Y(10750) mass: 10752.7^{+5.9}_{-6.0} MeV, does not fit as a Σ^+_g(1P) state and is argued to be a D-wave bottomonium state
- X(3915) mass: 3918.4 ± 1.9 MeV, assumed as lightest c\bar c s\bar s S-wave state
- Y(4140) mass: 4146.8 ± 2.4 MeV, identified as the unique J^PC = 1^++ c\bar c s\bar s state
- Y(4626) mass: 4624 ± 5 MeV, compatible with the 1P multiplet center of mass
- X(4700) mass: 4704^{+17}_{-26} MeV, compatible with the 2S multiplet center of mass

## Context

This work extends the dynamical diquark model previously applied to hidden-charm tetraquarks (Giron and Lebed, 2019) into the hidden-bottom and hidden-charm/strange sectors, building on the framework developed by Brodsky, Lebed, and collaborators (2014, 2017). The hidden-bottom analysis parallels the hidden-charm study where X(3872), Z_c(3900), and Z_c(4020) were identified as S-wave multiplet members. For the hidden-charm/strange sector, the paper follows the assignment of X(3915) and Y(4140) as tetraquark states proposed by Lebed and collaborators (2016), and uses lattice potential calculations from Juge, Kuti, and Morningstar (1999, 2002) to compute higher multiplet centers of mass. The results enable future searches for the predicted X_b (the bottom analogue of X(3872)) and for the remaining unobserved members of both multiplets.
