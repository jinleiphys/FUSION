# Quark Spin Properties at High x

> A general three quark bound state satisfying the Pauli principle, and conserving angular momentum and isospin, is used to investigate the spin structure of nucleons at high $x$. It is shown if {$F_{1n}/F_{1p}$$\to$1/4}, then both $A_{1p}$ and $A_{1n}$, as well as quark spin distribution $Δu/u$, will approach 1 as $x$$\to$1 in polarized deep inelastic scattering. The spin distribution $Δd/d$ does not approach 1, but is bound by the limits $-{1/3}\leΔd/d\le 0$ for any $x$.

# Quark Spin Properties at High x

## Key claim
If the unpolarized structure function ratio $F_{1n}/F_{1p}$ approaches 1/4 as $x \to 1$, then the asymmetry parameters $A_{1p}$ and $A_{1n}$, as well as the quark spin distribution $\Delta u/u$, will all approach 1 as $x \to 1$ in polarized deep inelastic scattering, but this result is conditional on the validity of a general three-quark bound state satisfying the Pauli principle, angular momentum conservation, and isospin conservation. The spin distribution $\Delta d/d$ does not approach 1, but is bound by $-1/3 \le \Delta d/d \le 0$ for any $x$, independent of the behavior of $F_{1n}/F_{1p}$.

## Method
The author constructs the most general spin-1/2, isospin-1/2 bound state of three valence quarks (u and d) obeying the Pauli principle, using a single three-body spatial function $\phi(123)$ with the quarks in a uud ordering. From this wave function, the four quark spin distributions ($u\up$, $u\dn$, $d\up$, $d\dn$) are derived by integrating over spectator quarks, and these are used to compute the unpolarized structure functions $F_{1p}$ and $F_{1n}$, the spin-dependent structure functions $g_{1p}$ and $g_{1n}$, and the asymmetry parameters $A_{1p}$ and $A_{1n}$ as functions of Bjorken $x$.

## Key numbers
- If $F_{1n}/F_{1p} \to 1/4$ as $x \to 1$, then $A_{1p} \to 1$ and $A_{1n} \to 1$ as $x \to 1$.
- The spin distribution $\Delta d/d$ is bounded by $-1/3 \le \Delta d/d \le 0$ for any $x$.
- For SU(6) symmetry, the predicted ratios are $g_{1p}/F_{1p} = 5/9$ and $g_{1n}/F_{1n} = 0$.
- The unpolarized ratio $F_{1n}/F_{1p}$ would be constant at $2/3$ for all $x$ under SU(6) symmetry, but experimental data show it falling toward $1/4$ as $x$ nears 1.

## Context
This work extends the model-independent analysis of nucleon spin structure at high $x$ beyond earlier model-dependent predictions by Close (1973), Kaur (1977), and Isgur (1999), who had previously concluded that $A_1 \to 1$ as $x \to 1$ under specific models. The paper contradicts a perturbative QCD prediction by Farrar and Jackson (1975) that $\Delta d/d \to 1$ as $x \to 1$, showing this violates the general bound derived from the Pauli principle. The results provide a challenge for upcoming experiments at higher $x$ to test whether $A_{1p}$ and $A_{1n}$ actually reach 1, and to determine how high an $x$ is sufficient to test the $x \to 1$ predictions.
