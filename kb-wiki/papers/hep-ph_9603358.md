# 1/N_c expansion for the partition function in four fermion models

> We present a derivation of the bosonic contribution to the thermodynamical potential of four fermion models by means of a $1/N_c$-expansion of the functional integral defining the partition function. This expansion turns out to be particularly useful to correct the mean field approximation expecially at low temperatures, where the relevant degrees of freedom are low-mass bosonic excitations (pseudogoldstones).

## Key claim

The paper presents a functional integral method for computing the $1/N_c$ expansion of the partition function in four-fermion models, which corrects the mean field approximation by including bosonic fluctuations; the method is particularly useful at low temperatures where low-mass pseudogoldstone bosons dominate, but the authors note that the bosonic free energy term may be difficult to evaluate in its sum-over-Matsubara-frequencies form and requires transformation into an integral representation.

## Method

The authors start from a four-fermion Lagrangian with $N_c$ colors and $N_f$ flavors, introduce auxiliary scalar and pseudoscalar fields $\sigma$ and $\vec{\pi}$ via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations, integrate out the fermion fields to obtain a purely bosonic effective action, then expand the bosonic field around its vacuum expectation value and perform a $1/N_c$ expansion of the functional integral. The effective potential (free energy density) is separated into a fermionic mean-field term ${\cal F}^F$ from the $N_c\to\infty$ limit and a bosonic fluctuation term ${\cal F}^B_j$ of order $1/N_c$, with the latter expressed as a sum over Matsubara frequencies that is subsequently transformed into an integral over the spectral function using the residue theorem and the Bose-Einstein distribution.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is $1/N_c$, where $N_c$ is the number of colors (with $N_c=3$ in QCD)
- The bosonic contribution $W_1$ is of order $1/N_c$ relative to the mean-field term $W_0$, which survives in the $N_c\to+\infty$ limit
- The inverse bosonic propagator $D^{-1}_j(p)$ contains a term $\epsilon^2_M = 4\bar{\varphi}^2$ for the scalar channel and $\epsilon^2_M = 0$ for the pseudoscalar channels
- The Matsubara frequencies for bosons are $p_{0_n}=i\nu_n=2n\pi i/\beta$

## Context

This work extends the large-$N$ expansion methods previously applied to the $O(N)$ symmetric field theory (Zinn-Justin) and the Gross-Neveu model (Gross and Neveu) to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature. It provides an alternative to the diagrammatic $1/N_c$ calculation by Klevansky (1993) and the coupling constant integration method of Fetter and Walecka, with the advantage of automatically preserving symmetries through the effective action formalism of Jackiw. The results enable systematic calculation of bosonic fluctuation contributions to the equation of state of four-fermion models, which are expected to dominate low-temperature thermodynamics through pseudogoldstone excitations, and can be applied to study chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature relevant to heavy-ion collisions and early universe cosmology.
