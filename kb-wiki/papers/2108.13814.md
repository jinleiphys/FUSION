# Pionic depth of the hadron gas after a heavy-ion collision

> The final stage of a relativistic heavy-ion collision is a hadron gas. Final-state interactions therein distort the $p_T$ spectrum of particles coming from the phase transition upon cooling the quark-gluon plasma. Using recent state-of-the-art parametrizations of pion interactions we provide theoretical computations of the pionic depth of the gas: how likely is it that a given pion rescatters in it (we find a high probability around $p_T=0.5$ GeV at midrapidity, corresponding to the formation of the $ρ$ resonance), a comparison of the collision and Bjorken expansion rates, and how many pions make it through without interacting as a function of $p_T$. This is in the range 10-24$\%$ and shown in this plot, the main result of the contribution.

# Pionic depth of the hadron gas after a heavy-ion collision

## Key claim
The fraction of pions that escape without interacting in the hadron gas formed after a heavy-ion collision is in the range 10-24%, with the lowest value around p_T = 0.5 GeV at midrapidity, corresponding to the formation of the rho resonance. This result depends on the value of the hadronization temperature T_had employed, and the escape fraction is computed by integrating the collision rate over the Bjorken expansion of the gas.

## Method
The authors compute the pionic depth (optical depth) of the pion gas by integrating the momentum-dependent collision rate over the proper time of a Bjorken longitudinal expansion model, using recent parametrizations of pi-pi cross sections broken down by isospin channels (I=0,1,2) that satisfy elastic unitarity. The expansion rate is derived from the Bjorken model with a linear equation of state (squared sound speed c_s^2 = 0.27), and the freeze-out condition is determined by comparing the collision rate with the expansion rate. The fraction of pions that escape without interaction is then obtained as exp[-alpha(p_T)], where alpha(p_T) is the pionic depth.

## Key numbers
- The fraction of pions that escape without interaction is in the range 10-24%, with the lowest value around p_T = 0.5 GeV (order 10-16% depending on T_had).
- The kinetic freeze-out temperature is T_f = 120 MeV, taken from experimental fits to the pion spectrum.
- The transition temperature from quark-gluon plasma to hadron gas is T_0 = 170 MeV, with the Bjorken evolution starting at tau_0 = 3 fm.
- The freeze-out system size from Hanbury Brown-Twiss interferometry is r_f = 11 fm (approximately equal to tau_f at zero rapidity).
- The squared sound speed of the pion gas is c_s^2 = 0.27, less than 1/3.

## Context
This work extends earlier investigations of the pion gas final state in heavy-ion collisions, such as Tomasik (2002), by providing quantitative computations of the pionic depth and escape fraction using state-of-the-art parametrizations of pion interactions from Kaminski (2012). The results enable corrections to be applied to measured particle spectra that are distorted by final-state interactions in the hadron gas, complementing the use of penetrating probes (photons, dileptons, charmonia, jets) to study the quark-gluon plasma. The Bjorken expansion model with a linear equation of state follows the approach of Rapp (1995), and the thermal pion distribution is compared to ALICE data from the TPC detector.
