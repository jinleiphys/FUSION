# Thermal dilepton polarization and dynamics of the QCD plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present the first theoretical study of the polarization of lepton pairs produced in $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, using next-to-leading order (NLO) dilepton emission rates. These calculations employ a multi-stage framework to simulate the evolution of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and to explore the sensitivity of polarization to early times. It is found that the intermediate invariant-mass dileptons are indeed probes of the thermal equilibration process, and go beyond the reach of hadronic observables. We compute the polarization anisotropy coefficient obtained with LO dilepton rates, and show that the LO and NLO results differ radically, both in trend and in magnitude, at low and intermediate lepton pair invariant masses.

# Thermal Dilepton Polarization and Dynamics of the QCD Plasma in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The polarization anisotropy coefficient lambda_theta of thermal dileptons in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, computed with next-to-leading order (NLO) emission rates, differs radically from leading-order (LO) results both in trend and magnitude at low and intermediate invariant masses; this difference is attributed to gluon-mediated processes that are absent at LO and makes intermediate-mass dileptons sensitive probes of the thermal equilibration process, going beyond the reach of hadronic observables.

## Method

The authors simulate Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV using the iEBE-MUSIC multistage framework, which provides event-by-event hydrodynamical evolution with local temperature and flow velocity fields. Thermal dilepton emission rates are computed at both LO (quark-antiquark annihilation) and NLO (including Compton scattering and modified annihilation with gluons, combined with LPM resummation for small invariant masses), using a strong coupling alpha_s = 0.3. The polarization coefficient lambda_theta is obtained as a weighted average over all fluid cells, integrating the fully differential rates over the collision history.

## Key numbers

- The collision energy is sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, in the 0%-20% centrality class.
- The intermediate mass range (IMR) is defined as 1 GeV less than or approximately equal to M less than or approximately equal to 3 GeV; the low mass range (LMR) is M less than or approximately equal to 1 GeV; the high mass range (HMR) is M greater than or approximately equal to 3 GeV.
- In the LMR, lambda_theta shifts from a near-zero negative value at LO to a sizable positive value at NLO, indicating a qualitative change in polarization behavior.
- For M much less than T, the NLO result approaches lambda_theta approximately equal to 1 for a plasma at rest, while the LO result cannot capture this real-photon limit.
- The polarization coefficient lambda_theta satisfies -1/3 less than or equal to lambda_theta (M to 0) less than or equal to 1, with the upper bound saturated for chi = 1 (fluid at rest in the helicity frame).
- Lattice QCD results from the HotQCD Collaboration (Ali 2024) at T approximately equal to 220 MeV with spatial momenta k approximately equal to 0.5 to 1.4 GeV show rho_Delta decreasing from positive at omega = k to negative for omega greater than k.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermal dilepton spectra by Churchill (2023) and Wu (2024) to include polarization observables, using the same multistage framework calibrated to hadronic observables at LHC energies. The polarization coefficient lambda_theta is directly connected to the spectral function difference rho_Delta = rho_T - rho_L, which has been studied on the lattice by Brandt (2017), Ce (2022), Meyer (2023), and the HotQCD Collaboration (Ali 2024). The results demonstrate that NLO corrections, which include gluon-mediated processes, are essential for capturing the correct polarization behavior, particularly in the low and intermediate mass ranges where LO calculations fail to reproduce the real-photon limit. The sensitivity of lambda_theta to preequilibrium dynamics, especially in the transverse momentum dependence in the IMR, opens a new avenue for studying the early-time evolution of the QGP that is complementary to hadronic observables.
