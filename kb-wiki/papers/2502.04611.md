# Investigating effects of the electrical conductivity of QCD matter on charge-dependent directed flow

> Charge dependent directed flow is an important observable of electromagnetic fields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We demonstrate how the difference in charge dependent directed flows between protons and antiprotons is sensitive to the resistivity, inverse of quark-gluon plasma's electric conductivity, over different collision centralities. Our model numerically solves the 3+1D relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamic (RRMHD) equations, assuming the electric conductivity to be a scalar. For this work, we focus on symmetric Au + Au collisions at the top RHIC energy of $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV. We illustrate the time evolution of the electromagnetic fields in our model and connect that to the charge dependent directed flow results. Our results highlight the importance of modeling quark-gluon plasma's electric conductivity for charge dependent observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the difference in charge-dependent directed flow between protons and antiprotons in symmetric Au+Au collisions is sensitive to the electric conductivity of quark-gluon plasma, but the authors emphasize this is a qualitative first step because the initial non-equilibrium dynamics of QGP have not been settled, making an exact quantitative role of initial positive charges difficult to establish.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the 3+1D relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamic (RRMHD) equations coupled with Maxwell's equations and Ohm's law, assuming a constant scalar electric conductivity and an ideal-gas equation of state. They initialize the electromagnetic fields using analytic in-medium Maxwell equation solutions from Tuchin (2013), adopt the optical Glauber model for the initial energy density profile with a longitudinal tilt, and compute final-state hadron distributions via the Cooper-Frye formula on a freezeout hypersurface at energy density 0.15 GeV/fm³.

## Key numbers

- Collision system: Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV (top RHIC energy), with impact parameter b = 10 fm for peripheral collisions
- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c, initial energy density ε₀ = 55.0 GeV/fm³
- Freezeout energy density ε_f = 0.15 GeV/fm³, corresponding to freezeout temperature T_f ≈ 0.140 GeV
- Grid size: 168 × 168 × 72, spanning -16.8 fm < (x,y) < 16.8 fm and -7.2 < η < 7.2
- Inelastic cross-section σ_NN = 41.3 mb at this energy
- Electric conductivity values studied range from σ = 0.0294 fm⁻¹ to σ = 100 fm⁻¹, with the physically relevant range limited to σ < 5 fm⁻¹ based on lattice QCD and pQCD calculations

## Context

This work extends previous RRMHD studies by Nakamura et al. (2023) that only considered peripheral centralities and final-state pions, now enabling study of different hadron species across collision centralities. It contrasts with approaches that explain the positive slopes of Δv₁ solely through transported quark effects, instead demonstrating that the initial net positive charge of the plasma from protons can also produce positive slopes, as suggested by Gursoy et al. (2018). The results highlight the need for future improvements including temperature-dependent conductivity, pre-equilibrium dynamics, and spectator charges as ongoing EM sources to move from qualitative to quantitative predictions.
