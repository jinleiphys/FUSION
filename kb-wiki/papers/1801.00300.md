# Goldstone Modes and Clebsch-Gordan Coefficients

> We solve explicitly the Goldstone modes in spontaneously symmetry breaking models with supersymmetry. We find that, when more than one fields or representations contribute to the symmetry breaking, there exist identities among the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients which can be used as consistent checks on the calculations.

# Goldstone Modes and Clebsch-Gordan Coefficients

## Key claim
The paper claims that in supersymmetric models with spontaneous symmetry breaking involving multiple Higgs fields or representations, the Goldstone mode components are explicitly proportional to the vacuum expectation values (VEVs) and depend only on Clebsch-Gordan coefficients (CGCs), not on superpotential parameters. This result is conditional on the SUSY framework and the assumption that the F-flatness conditions can be used to eliminate mass parameters.

## Method
The authors derive zero-eigenvalue equations for Goldstinos (the fermionic partners of Goldstones) from the superpotential mass matrix, using F-flatness conditions to eliminate mass parameters. They analyze both real and complex representations under a symmetry group G1 breaking to a subgroup G2, constructing matrix equations whose entries are CGCs and whose solutions give the Goldstino content. Special cases are treated for identical fields, real fields among complex representations, and fields lacking certain subgroup components. The method is then applied to SU(2) as an explicit test case, translating the general CGC identities into standard angular-momentum notation.

## Key numbers
- For the SU(2) case with real representations, the 3x3 determinant identity is given as:
  \[
  \left|
  \begin{array}{ccc}
  C^{ij}_{0,0;k,0} & C^{ij}_{1,-1;k,0} & -C^{ij}_{1,0;k,1} \\
  C^{ij}_{-1,1;k,0} & C^{ij}_{0,0;k,0} & -C^{ij}_{0,1;k,1} \\
  -C^{ij}_{-1,0;k,-1} & -C^{ij}_{0,-1;k,-1} & C^{ij}_{0,0;k,0}
  \end{array}
  \right| = 0
  \]
- For the SU(2) case with complex representations, a 6x6 determinant identity is given in equation (6x6su2), involving CGCs with indices \(i_0, j_0, k_0\) and shifted indices \(i_\pm = i_0 \pm 1\).
- For the special case where \(J = K\) in real representations, the matrix reduces to a 2x2 with entries \(C^{ij}_{0,0;j,0}\), \([1+(-1)^{i+2j}] C^{ij}_{1,-1;j,0}\), \(C^{ij}_{-1,1;j,0}\), and \(C^{ij}_{0,0;j,0} - C^{ij}_{0,1;j,1}\).
- For the special case where \(I = J = K\) in real representations, the matrix reduces to a 2x2 with entries \(C^{ii}_{0,0;i,0}\), \([1+(-1)^{3i}] C^{ii}_{1,-1;i,0}\), \(C^{ii}_{-1,1;i,0}\), and \(C^{ii}_{0,0;i,0} - C^{ii}_{0,1;i,1}\).

## Context
This work extends the general treatment of spontaneous symmetry breaking in supersymmetric grand unified theories (SUSY GUTs), particularly for models beyond minimal SU(5) such as SO(10) models studied by He, Lee, Sato, and others (various years). Prior approaches often relied on numerical checks to verify whether SSB occurs, as in works by Sato, Fuku, and others. The paper provides an analytic method using CGC identities that eliminates the need for such numerical tests. The results enable systematic construction of Goldstone modes in complicated gauge groups where explicit broken-generator matrix elements are unavailable, and the derived CGC identities serve as consistency checks for calculations. The authors note that detailed application to SUSY SO(10) models will appear in a separate publication.
