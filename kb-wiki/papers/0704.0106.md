# Multiple Parton Scattering in Nuclei: Quark-quark Scattering

> Modifications to quark and antiquark fragmentation functions due to quark-quark (antiquark) double scattering in nuclear medium are studied systematically up to order \cal{O}(α_{s}^2)$ in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclear targets. At the order $\cal{O}(α_s^2)$, twist-four contributions from quark-quark (antiquark) rescattering also exhibit the Landau-Pomeranchuck-Midgal (LPM) interference feature similar to gluon bremsstrahlung induced by multiple parton scattering. Compared to quark-gluon scattering, the modification, which is dominated by $t$-channel quark-quark (antiquark) scattering, is only smaller by a factor of $C_F/C_A=4/9$ times the ratio of quark and gluon distributions in the medium. Such a modification is not negligible for realistic kinematics and finite medium size. The modifications to quark (antiquark) fragmentation functions from quark-antiquark annihilation processes are shown to be determined by the antiquark (quark) distribution density in the medium. The asymmetry in quark and antiquark distributions in nuclei will lead to different modifications of quark and antiquark fragmentation functions inside a nucleus, which qualitatively explains the experimentally observed flavor dependence of the leading hadron suppression in semi-inclusive DIS off nuclear targets. The quark-antiquark annihilation processes also mix quark and gluon fragmentation functions in the large fractional momentum region, leading to a flavor dependence of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Quark-quark (antiquark) double scattering in nuclei modifies quark and antiquark fragmentation functions at order O(α_s^2), with the modification being smaller than quark-gluon scattering by a factor of C_F/C_A = 4/9 times the ratio of quark to gluon distributions in the medium; however, this modification is not negligible for realistic kinematics and finite medium size, and the asymmetry between quark and antiquark distributions in nuclei leads to different modifications of quark and antiquark fragmentation functions.

## Method

The authors systematically calculated twist-four contributions from quark-quark (antiquark) double scattering in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclear targets using the generalized factorization framework of Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (LQS). They computed the hard partonic parts for all cut diagrams (central, left, and right) of quark-antiquark annihilation and quark-quark scattering processes at order O(α_s^2), including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) interference effects, and factorized the results into convolutions of twist-four two-parton correlation matrix elements with parton fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The modification from quark-quark (antiquark) scattering relative to quark-gluon scattering is smaller by a factor of C_F/C_A = 4/9 times the ratio of quark and gluon distributions in the medium.
- The twist-four corrections are suppressed by powers of 1/Q^2 but enhanced by at least a factor of A^{1/3} due to multiple scattering in a large nucleus, and by A^{2/3} (quadratic in nuclear size) due to LPM interference.
- The momentum fraction x_L is defined as x_L = ℓ_T^2 / (2 p^+ q^- z (1-z)), where ℓ_T is the transverse momentum of the radiated parton.

## Context

This work extends the LQS twist-expansion framework previously applied to quark-gluon double scattering by Wang and Guo (2001) and Zhang and Wang (2003), which described parton energy loss and nuclear modification of fragmentation functions in cold nuclear matter and successfully explained HERMES data on leading hadron suppression. The results provide a mechanism for the experimentally observed flavor dependence of leading hadron suppression in semi-inclusive DIS off nuclear targets (HERMES, 2000, 2003) and for flavor-dependent jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, complementing the dominant quark-gluon scattering picture.
