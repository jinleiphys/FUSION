# Coulomb Form Factors of $^{27}$Al and $^{31}$P Nuclei Using Coulomb valance Tassie model and Bohr-Mottelson Collective Models with Different Potentials

> The longitudinal Coulomb C0, C2 and C4 form factors with core-polarization effects have been studied using shell model calculations for 3/2$^+_1$ state with excitation energy of 1.069 MeV, 5/2$^+_2$ state with excitation energy of 2.706 MeV and 7/2$^+_1$ state with excitation energy of 2.304 MeV state in $^{27}$Al nucleus and 1/2 state in $^{31}$P nucleus. The two-body effective interaction Wildenthal and universal sd-shell interaction A (USDA) are used for sd-shell orbits. The Coulomb valance Tassie model (CVTM) and Bohr-Mottelson (BM) collective model have been used to calculated the Core-polarization effects. Three potentials are Harmonic Oscillator (HO) potential, Wood-Saxon (WS) and SKX potential,which have been used to calculated the wave functions of radial single particle matrix elements. The results for these potentials are compared with final update experimental data. The calculations with inclusions of core-polarization effects give a good agreements of experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including core-polarization effects via the Coulomb Valance Tassie Model (CVTM) and the Bohr-Mottelson (BM) collective model, combined with the SKX potential, produces good agreement with experimental Coulomb form factor data for specific states in \(^{27}\)Al and \(^{31}\)P, but the authors note that the importance of core-polarization effects in some longitudinal components of form factors remains an open question and that all calculations were performed without adjusting any parameter.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using the Nushellx@MUS code with the USDA effective interaction for sd-shell orbits to generate one-body density matrix (OBDM) elements. They computed longitudinal Coulomb C0, C2, and C4 form factors for the \(^{27}\)Al nucleus (3/2\(^+_1\) state at 1.069 MeV, 5/2\(^+_2\) state at 2.706 MeV, and 7/2\(^+_1\) state at 2.304 MeV) and the C0 form factor for the 1/2 state in \(^{31}\)P. Core-polarization effects were included using the CVTM and BM collective models, with radial single-particle wave functions generated from three different potentials: Harmonic Oscillator (HO), Wood-Saxon (WS), and SKX.

## Key numbers

- C2 form factor for the 3/2\(^+_1\) state at excitation energy 1.069 MeV in \(^{27}\)Al: best agreement with experimental data using the SKX potential across all momentum transfer regions.
- C2+C4 form factor for the 7/2\(^+\) state at excitation energy 2.304 MeV in \(^{27}\)Al: good agreement in the first minimum region and second maximum region of momentum transfer using the BM collective model.
- C0+C2+C4 form factor for the 5/2\(^+_2\) state at excitation energy 2.706 MeV in \(^{27}\)Al: BM collective model results are closer to experimental data in the momentum transfer region between 1.2 and 2.0 fm\(^{-1}\) compared to CVTM results.
- C0 form factor for the 1/2 state at 0.0 MeV in \(^{31}\)P: WS potential gives good agreement in the region 1.25–2.1 fm\(^{-1}\); SKX potential gives agreement in the region 2.1–3.0 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of electron scattering form factors by Khalid S. Jassim and colleagues (various years) on light and medium nuclei, and builds on studies of Coulomb C2 and C4 form factors in \(^{18}\)O, \(^{20,22}\)Ne using the BM model by Ajeel and colleagues. The paper compares three different potentials (HO, WS, SKX) and two core-polarization models (CVTM and BM) to determine which combination best reproduces experimental data for \(^{27}\)Al and \(^{31}\)P, enabling future work to select optimal potentials and models for electron scattering calculations in the sd-shell region.
