# Unified description of pairing and quarteting correlations within the particle-hole-boson approach

> We study the description of single-species and isovector pairing correlations in the framework of the projected-BCS (PBCS) and the Quartet Condensation Model (QCM) from a particle-hole perspective and we introduce the representation of the QCM quartet condensate state in terms of particle-hole excitations with respect to the Hartree-Fock state. We also present a new bosonic approximation for both PBCS and QCM. In each case, the starting point is the reformulation of the pair/quartet condensate state in terms of particle-hole excitations with respect to the Hartree-Fock state. The main simplification of our approach is the assumption that the pair operators corresponding to both particle and hole states obey bosonic commutation relations. This simplifies tremendously the computations and allows for an analytic derivation of the averaged Hamiltonian on the condenstate state as a function of the mixing amplitudes. We study both the pure bosonic approach and the renormalized version, and compare the particle-hole bosonic version to the naive prescription of applying the boson approximation directly to the original condensate state. We compare the fermionic and the renormalized particle-hole bosonic approach in the case of a picket fence model of doubly degenerate states and in a realistic shell model space with an effective interaction for the $N=Z$ nuclei above $^{100}$Sn.

# Unified description of pairing and quarteting correlations within the particle-hole-boson approach

## Key claim

The authors present a new bosonic approximation for both projected-BCS (PBCS) and Quartet Condensation Model (QCM) states, based on reformulating the condensate states in terms of particle-hole excitations with respect to the Hartree-Fock state, under the assumption that pair operators for both particle and hole states obey bosonic commutation relations. The approach is claimed to work well only when a renormalized version of the bosonic commutation relations is used, as the pure bosonic approximation fails to reproduce finite level occupancy in the strong pairing regime.

## Method

The authors first reformulate the PBCS pair condensate and the QCM quartet condensate as particle-hole expansions with respect to the Hartree-Fock state, decomposing collective pair operators into components below and above the Fermi level. They then introduce a bosonic approximation where particle and hole pair operators are replaced by bosonic operators, and compute the average of the pairing Hamiltonian on the bosonic condensate state analytically as a function of the mixing amplitudes. Two versions are studied: a pure bosonic case with unit commutator coefficients, and a renormalized case where the commutator coefficients are determined self-consistently to account for Pauli exclusion principle effects on level occupancy.

## Key numbers

- For the picket fence model with 20 equally spaced doubly-degenerate levels (ε_k = k-1 MeV) and constant pairing force V_ij = -G, the critical pairing strengths are: G_cr^(n_p=6) = 0.238 MeV, G_cr^(n_p=10) = 0.234 MeV for PBCS; G_cr^(q=1) = 0.144 MeV, G_cr^(q=2) = 0.132 MeV, G_cr^(q=3) = 0.127 MeV, G_cr^(q=4) = 0.123 MeV for QCM.
- The renormalized bosonic approximation (rbPBCS and rbQCM) reproduces the fermionic ground state energy and average level occupation fractions with "almost perfect agreement" across weak, medium, and strong pairing regimes (G/G_cr from 0 to 4).
- The pure bosonic approximation (bPBCS) yields average level occupation fractions exceeding unity for states near the Fermi surface in the strong pairing regime at G/G_cr = 4.
- The "naive" renormalized bosonic approach (applying bosonization directly to the original condensate rather than the particle-hole expansion) shows "strong discrepancies" with the fermionic case for all interaction strengths except G = 0.

## Context

This work extends the particle-hole treatment of pairing correlations developed by Dukelsky et al. (2016) to the more complicated case of quartet correlations in the QCM framework. The QCM was originally proposed by Sandulescu et al. (2012) for isovector pairing and quarteting correlations in N=Z nuclei, and the present paper provides a unified bosonic description of both pairing and quarteting correlations that yields the same functional form of the energy for both cases. The approach enables analytic computation of ground state correlations in both PBCS and QCM formalisms, and the authors note that the particle-hole bosonic method could potentially be extended to introduce a quasiparticle representation for quarteting correlations.
