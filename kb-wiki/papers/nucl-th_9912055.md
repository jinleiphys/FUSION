# Statistical aspects of nuclear coupling to continuum

> Various global characteristics of the coupling between the bound and scattering states are explicitly studied based on realistic Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum. In particular, such characteristics are related to those of the scattering ensemble. It is found that in the region of higher density of states the coupling to continuum is largely consistent with the statistical model. However, assumption of channel equivalence in the statistical model is, in general, violated.

# Statistical aspects of nuclear coupling to continuum

## Key claim

The coupling to continuum in the region of higher density of states is largely consistent with the statistical model of non-Hermitian random matrices, but the assumption of channel equivalence in that statistical model is generally violated in realistic calculations using the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC).

## Method

The authors performed SMEC calculations for 325 Jπ=0+, T=0 states in 24Mg, using a 16O core with the sd-shell interaction among valence nucleons and a Woods-Saxon average potential for radial single-particle wave functions. They constructed the effective Hamiltonian HeffQQ = HQQ + W acting in the space of quasibound states, where W contains the coupling to continuum, and analyzed the statistical distributions of the real and imaginary parts of the matrix elements Wij for one and multiple open channels.

## Key numbers

- 325 Jπ=0+, T=0 states in 24Mg were computed in the SMEC framework
- After discarding 60 states on each end of the spectrum, 205 states in the middle showed good agreement with the Λ=1 statistical distribution for both real and imaginary parts of W
- For 10 randomly selected channels at E=20 MeV particle energy, the best fit to the statistical distribution gave Λeff=3.1 for the imaginary part and Λeff=4.4 for the real part of W
- At E=40 MeV particle energy with the same 10 channels, the best fit gave Λeff=4.8 for the imaginary part and Λeff=3.1 for the real part
- The residual interaction coupling strength was V12(0)=650 MeV·fm3 with spin-exchange parameter β=0.05
- When the overall coupling strength was multiplied by a factor f=7, all 10 states became unambiguously separated from the remaining trapped states

## Context

This work extends the statistical description of open quantum systems using scattering ensembles of non-Hermitian random matrices, as developed by Sokolov and Zelevinsky (1989) and Drozdz, Trellakis, and Wambach (1996), by testing these statistical assumptions against realistic SMEC calculations for the first time. The SMEC computational scheme was developed by Bennaceur, Nowacki, Okolowicz, and Ploszajczak (1999). The finding that channel equivalence is violated and that the effective number of channels (Λeff) is much smaller than the actual number of open channels points toward the need for improved scattering ensembles that account for this non-equivalence, similar to how various random matrix ensembles were developed for bound states by Brody et al. (1981), Wong and French (1972), and Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998).
