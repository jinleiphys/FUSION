# In-beam $γ-$spectroscopy of the transitional nucleus $^{217}$Ac

> High-spin states in the transitional $^{217}$Ac nucleus are established up to 3.8 MeV excitation energy and $I^π =$ 41/2$^+$ with the addition of around 20 new transitions. The structure of the yrast and near-yrast states below the 29/2$^+$ isomer is revisited. The inconsistencies in the level schemes reported earlier are resolved. The level structure above the 29/2$^+$ isomer is established for the first time. Large-basis shell-model calculations with the KHPE interaction are performed to compare the experimentally observed level energies with the theoretical predictions. A comparison with the systematics of the N = 128 isotones suggests that the yrast structures result from a weak coupling of the odd proton to the even-even 216Ra core, which is consistent with the shell-model configurations. Furthermore, alpha decay of the 29/2$^+$ isomer is revisited and the decay scheme established from this work is discussed in the framework of the shell model.

# In-beam γ-spectroscopy of the transitional nucleus ²¹⁷Ac

## Key claim

The level scheme of ²¹⁷Ac is extended up to 3.8 MeV excitation energy and spin-parity 41/2⁺ with around 20 new transitions, but the spin-parity assignments above the 29/2⁺ isomer rely on DCO ratio and linear-polarization measurements that are only possible for the four strongest transitions; the remaining new transitions above the isomer have no measured multipolarity.

## Method

High-spin states in ²¹⁷Ac were populated via the ²⁰⁹Bi(¹²C,4n)²¹⁷Ac fusion-evaporation reaction at 72 MeV beam energy, using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) of 18 Compton-suppressed HPGe clover detectors. Prompt γ-γ and γ-γ-γ coincidence matrices, together with an early-delayed matrix (200–1200 ns time window), were constructed from time-stamped data to identify transitions below and above the 29/2⁺ isomer. Multipolarities were determined from directional correlation from oriented states (DCO) ratios and linear-polarization measurements using the 90° clover detectors.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the highest observed state: 3.8 MeV (3880.9 keV level), with spin-parity 41/2⁺.
- Isomeric state at 2012.4 keV with half-life 740(40) ns and spin-parity 29/2⁺.
- New transitions above the isomer: 328-keV (E2), 372-keV (E2), 380-keV (E1), 501-keV (E1), with DCO ratios of 1.97(14), 2.03(14), 0.97(7), and 1.10(8) respectively in dipole gates.
- Relative intensity of the 660-keV transition (13/2⁻ → 9/2⁻) normalized to 100.0(57); the 328-keV transition above the isomer normalized to 100.0(56).
- α-decay branch of the 29/2⁺ isomer determined to be ∼5 %.
- Unobserved low-energy transitions inferred: 10 keV (between 11/2⁻ and 13/2⁻), 31 keV (between levels at 1528 and 1497 keV), and 60 keV (between (13/2⁺) and 11/2⁻).

## Context

This work extends the level scheme of ²¹⁷Ac beyond the 29/2⁺ isomer, which was previously studied only up to that state by Decman et al. (1980s) using in-beam g-factor and α-γ coincidence measurements. The results resolve inconsistencies in the placement of transitions reported by Gono et al. and confirm the ordering established by Decman et al. The yrast structures are interpreted via large-basis shell-model calculations with the KHPE interaction and compared with systematics of N=128 isotones, suggesting weak coupling of the odd proton to the ²¹⁶Ra core. The new high-spin data enable future tests of shell-model predictions for transitional nuclei near the N=126 shell closure and provide a link between spherical and octupole-deformed regions in the actinium isotopic chain.
