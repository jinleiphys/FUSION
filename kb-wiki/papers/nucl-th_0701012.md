# Electroexcitation of the Roper resonance in the relativistic quark models

> The amplitudes of the transition gamma* N -> P11(1440) are calculated within light-front relativistic quark model assuming that the P11(1440) is the first radial excitation of the 3q ground state. The results are presented along with the predictions obtained in other relativistic quark models. In comparison with the previous calculations, we have extended the range of Q^2 up to 4.5 GeV^2 to cover the kinematic interval of the forthcoming experimental data. Using approach based on PCAC, we have checked the relative sign between quark model predictions for the N and P11(1440) contributions to the pion electroproduction found in previous investigations.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the electroexcitation amplitudes of the Roper resonance (P11(1440)) within a light-front relativistic quark model, assuming the resonance is the first radial excitation of the 3-quark ground state, and extends the Q^2 range up to 4.5 GeV^2 to cover the kinematic interval of forthcoming experimental data. The relative sign between the NNπ and P11(1440)Nπ amplitudes is confirmed using a PCAC-based approach, matching the sign found in prior work using the 3P0 model.

## Method

The author computed the γ* N → P11(1440) helicity amplitudes using the light-front relativistic quark model of Ref. [20], formulated in the infinite momentum frame (IMF) with the plus component of the electromagnetic current in the q+=0 frame. The radial part of the nucleon wave function was taken in Gaussian form with a harmonic-oscillator parameter, and the P11(1440) wave function was defined as a radial excitation orthogonal to the nucleon ground state. The relative sign between the NNπ and P11(1440)Nπ coupling constants was determined by relating the divergence of the axial-vector current to the pion field via PCAC, using Eq. (30) and the axial matrix element of Eq. (33).

## Key numbers

- Quark mass m_q = 0.22 GeV and harmonic-oscillator parameter α_HO = 0.38 GeV, found from description of static nucleon properties in Ref. [20].
- Calculations extended to Q^2 up to 4.5 GeV^2, covering the kinematic interval of forthcoming CLAS data (1.7 < Q^2 < 4.2 GeV^2).
- The ratio g_A^{RN}/g_A^{NN} = -ζ α_HO^2/(3√6 m_q^2) in the limit α_HO ≪ m_q, with the same sign confirmed numerically for 0 < m_q < m/3 and a wide range of α_HO.
- The pion decay constant f_π = 132 MeV used in the PCAC relation.
- The transverse amplitude A_{1/2} at Q^2=0 is negative; the longitudinal amplitude S_{1/2} at small Q^2 is positive.
- All light-front approaches predict a change of sign of A_{1/2} at small Q^2.

## Context

This work extends previous light-front relativistic quark model calculations of the γ* N → P11(1440) transition (Refs. [12,13,14,15,16]) by covering a wider Q^2 range up to 4.5 GeV^2 and by resolving inconsistencies in the relative sign between the NNπ and P11(1440)Nπ amplitudes. The sign obtained via PCAC agrees with that found in Ref. [13] using the 3P0 model of Ref. [17]. The results enable comparison with forthcoming CLAS data at 1.7 < Q^2 < 4.2 GeV^2, which combined with previous CLAS data at Q^2 = 0.4 and 0.65 GeV^2 and information at Q^2=0 will help determine the nature of the Roper resonance. All approaches describe nucleon form factors well but give different predictions for the Roper transition amplitudes, with the light-front approaches using the plus component of the electromagnetic current considered most reliable.
