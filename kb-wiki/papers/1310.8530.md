# Peripheral NN scattering from subtractive renormalization of chiral interactions

> We apply five subtractions in the Lippman-Schwinger (LS) equation in order to perform a non-perturbative renormalization of chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interactions. Here we compute the phase shifts for the uncoupled peripheral waves at renormalization scales between $0.1~ \rm{fm}^{-1}$ and $1 ~ \rm{fm}^{-1}$. In this range, the results are scale invariant and provide an overall goof agreement with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis up to at least $E_{\rm{lab}} = 150 ~ \rm{MeV}$, with a cutoff at $Λ= 30~\rm{fm}^{-1}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that applying five subtractions in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation yields approximately scale-invariant phase shifts for uncoupled peripheral nucleon-nucleon waves from chiral N3LO potentials, even with an extremely large momentum cutoff of 30 fm⁻¹, but the agreement with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis is limited to laboratory energies up to about 200 MeV, with F-waves only well described up to 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors implement a subtractive renormalization scheme with five subtractions at a renormalization scale μ in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the K-matrix, using the recursive driving term method rather than applying a cutoff regularizing function directly to the potential. They compute phase shifts for uncoupled peripheral waves up to J=6 using two state-of-the-art N3LO chiral potentials: the Entem-Machleidt (N3LO-EM) and the Epelbaum-Glöckle-Meissner (N3LO-EGM) potentials. The numerical solution is carried out by discretizing momentum space with 200 points up to 30 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- Renormalization scales μ between 0.1 fm⁻¹ and 1 fm⁻¹ are used, over which results show approximate scale invariance
- Cutoff Λ = 30 fm⁻¹ is employed in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation
- Phase shifts agree with Nijmegen partial wave analysis up to at least Elab = 150 MeV for most waves
- F-waves show good description only up to 100 MeV
- Results are computed up to laboratory energies of about 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the subtractive renormalization approach previously developed by the same group (Timóteo et al., 2010-2012) to chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interactions, contrasting with the standard cutoff regularization method used by Epelbaum (2005) and Machleidt (2003) which fails for cutoffs above 1 GeV. The method enables non-perturbative renormalization of chiral interactions without imposing a momentum cutoff on the potential, and the authors suggest it is suitable for handling the nuclear force at arbitrarily large cutoffs.
