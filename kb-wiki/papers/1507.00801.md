# Spontaneous magnetization in high-density quark matter

> It is shown that the spontaneous magnetization occurs due to the anomalous magnetic moments of quarks in the high-density quark matter under the tensor-type four-point interaction. The spin polarized condensate for each flavor of quark appears at high baryon density, which leads to the spontaneous magnetization due to the anomalous magnetic moments of quarks. The implications to the strong magnetic field in the compact stars is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that spontaneous magnetization in high-density quark matter occurs only when both the tensor-type four-point interaction (which produces spin polarization) and the anomalous magnetic moments of quarks are present; without the anomalous magnetic moment, the thermodynamic potential has no linear term in the magnetic field B, so the spontaneous magnetization is zero even though spin polarization exists.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type model with a tensor-type four-point interaction, introducing a spin-polarized condensate F in the mean-field approximation. They derive the thermodynamic potential under an external magnetic field B for the cases F > mu, 0 < F < mu, and F = 0, then approximate the sum over Landau levels by an integral for small B. The spontaneous magnetization is obtained from the thermodynamic relation M = -dPhi/dB at B=0, first without and then with the inclusion of the anomalous magnetic moments of quarks.

## Key numbers

- The spin polarization condensate F appears at a critical chemical potential mu_c approximately 0.407 GeV, corresponding to a baryon density of 3.53 times the normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3.
- For mu >= mu_F approximately 0.5605 GeV, the spin polarization condensate satisfies F >= mu.
- The anomalous magnetic moments used are mu_u = 1.85 mu_N and mu_d = -0.97 mu_N, where mu_N = e/(2m_p) = 3.15 x 10^-17 GeV/T.
- The coupling constant G = 20 GeV^-2 is used, with a note that G = 11.1 GeV^-2 gives similar results under a three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 0.631 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of spin-polarized phases in high-density quark matter under tensor-type interactions (Tsue et al., 2013; Tsue et al., 2014), which considered competition with two-flavor color superconducting and color-flavor locked phases but did not include magnetic features. The paper also builds on earlier work by Tatsumi (2000) on strong magnetic fields in compact stars and by Niegawa et al. (1975) on quark spin polarization under pseudovector interactions. The results suggest that the spontaneous magnetization from quark anomalous magnetic moments could explain the strong surface magnetic fields observed in magnetars and hybrid compact stars.
