# Mean pt scaling with m/nq at the LHC: Absence of (hydro) flow in small systems?

> In this work, a study of the average transverse momentum (pt) as a function of the mid-rapidity charged hadron multiplicity (Nch) and hadron mass (m) in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies is presented. For the events producing low Nch, the average pt is found to scale with the reduced hadron mass, i.e., mass divided by the number of quark constituents (m/nq), this scaling also holds for inelastic pp collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The scaling is broken in high multiplicity p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions, where, for <Nch/deta> < 60 the average pt is higher for baryons than that for mesons, though they increase linearly with m/nq. This behavior is qualitatively well reproduced by Pythia 8, but not by hydro calculations, where an universal scaling with the hadron mass (and not with m/nq) is predicted for all the multiplicity event classes. Only the central (0-60%) Pb-Pb collisions behave as expected from hydro.

# Mean pT scaling with m/nq at the LHC: Absence of (hydro) flow in small systems?

## Key claim

The average transverse momentum scales with the reduced hadron mass (mass divided by number of quark constituents, m/nq) in low-multiplicity p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions, but this scaling is broken in high-multiplicity events where baryons show higher average pT than mesons; only central (0-60%) Pb-Pb collisions exhibit the universal scaling with hadron mass predicted by hydrodynamics, while Pythia 8 qualitatively reproduces the m/nq scaling and its breaking.

## Method

The study analyzed published LHC data for average transverse momentum as a function of charged hadron multiplicity and hadron mass in p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, comparing with inelastic pp collisions at 0.2, 0.9, and 7 TeV from RHIC and LHC. The author also performed Pythia 8.201 simulations (tune Monash 2013) for pp collisions at 7 TeV, with and without color reconnection, extracting pT spectra for multiple hadron species at |y|<1 and |eta|<1. The mass dependence was examined by plotting average pT against both hadron mass m and reduced mass m/nq for different multiplicity event classes.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at 0.2 TeV, the average multiplicity density is approximately 2.25 charged particles per unit pseudorapidity; at 0.9 TeV it is approximately 3.81
- Two multiplicity classes in p-Pb collisions were studied: average dNch/deta = 4.4 and average dNch/deta = 45
- The m/nq scaling holds for low-multiplicity p-Pb events (dNch/deta = 4.4) but is broken at high multiplicity (dNch/deta = 45)
- For Pb-Pb collisions, the universal mass scaling is observed only for 0-60% centrality classes, while it is broken for 60-90% centrality
- At the same multiplicity density of approximately 35, the slope of average pT versus mass is higher in p-Pb than in Pb-Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends studies of QGP-like effects in small systems, where long-range angular correlations (CMS, 2012) and flow-like patterns (Ortiz Velasquez, 2014) have been observed in high-multiplicity pp and p-Pb collisions. The results contradict the universal mass scaling predicted by hydrodynamic calculations (Bozek, 2014) for all multiplicity classes in p-Pb collisions, while supporting alternative explanations based on color reconnection and multi-parton interactions in Pythia 8 (Sjostrand, 2007; Corke, 2010). The findings enable discrimination between hydrodynamical and fragmentation-based interpretations of collectivity in small systems, and suggest that hydro models should be applied with caution given that mass scaling is not universal in the data.
