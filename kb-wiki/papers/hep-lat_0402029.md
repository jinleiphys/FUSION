# Hadronic Electromagnetic Properties at Finite Lattice Spacing

> Electromagnetic properties of the octet mesons as well as the octet and decuplet baryons are augmented in quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory to include O(a) corrections due to lattice discretization. We present the results for the SU(3) flavor group in the isospin limit as well as the results for SU(2) flavor with non-degenerate quarks. These corrections will be useful for extrapolation of lattice calculations using Wilson valence and sea quarks, as well as calculations using Wilson sea quarks and Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks.

## Key claim

The paper extends quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (QχPT and PQχPT) to include O(a) corrections from lattice discretization for electromagnetic properties of octet mesons, octet and decuplet baryons, and decuplet-to-octet transitions, but the actual finite-lattice-spacing corrections to the observables at the order calculated appear only implicitly through the a-dependence of meson masses, while explicit O(a) operator insertions are shown to contribute at higher orders in the chiral expansion and are therefore neglected.

## Method

The authors construct the low-energy effective Lagrangian for partially quenched QCD at O(a) by including the Sheikholeslami-Wohlert (SW) term and vector-current correction operators, then match these onto PQχPT and QχPT using a dual expansion in quark mass and lattice spacing with small parameters ε² ~ m_q/Λ_χ, aΛ_χ, and p²/Λ_χ². They compute one-loop chiral corrections to charge radii, magnetic moments, electric quadrupole moments, and transition form factors for the octet mesons, octet baryons, decuplet baryons, and decuplet-to-octet transitions, working to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion and leading order in the heavy baryon expansion, with the lattice spacing dependence entering through the meson masses in loop diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f = 132 MeV is used as the scale in the meson Lagrangian
- The meson mass formula including O(a) corrections: m_QQ'² = (4/f²)[λ_m(m_Q + m_Q') + aλ_a(c_Q + c_Q')]
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ is taken as the scale for the singlet field mass μ₀ → Λ_χ
- The octet-decuplet mass splitting Δ appears in loop functions F(m_X,Δ,μ) and G(m_X,Δ,μ)
- The charge radius of octet mesons to O(ε²): ⟨r²⟩ = α₉(24Q/f²) + (1/(16π²f²)) Σ_X A_X log(m_X²/μ²)
- The quenched meson charge radii have coefficients A_X = 0 for all loop mesons, giving no quark mass or lattice spacing dependence at this order

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of electromagnetic properties in QχPT and PQχPT (Arndt 2003) to include O(a) lattice spacing corrections, following the framework for mesons developed by Rupak (2002) and Bar (2002) and for baryons by Beane (2003). The results enable extrapolation of lattice QCD calculations using Wilson valence and sea quarks, as well as mixed actions with Wilson sea quarks and Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks, to the physical continuum limit. The paper provides the necessary chiral perturbation theory expressions that lattice practitioners can use to remove finite-lattice-spacing artifacts from their numerical data.
