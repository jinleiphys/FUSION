# Kinematical and nonlocality effects on the nonmesonic weak hypernuclear decay

> We derive in detail the transition potential for nonmesonic lambda-hypernuclear decay in a one-meson-exchange model involving the full pseudoscalar and vector meson octets and including two effects that have been systematically omitted in the literature. These are the kinematical effects due to the difference between the lambda and nucleon masses and the first-order nonlocality corrections. Numerical results for $^{12}_Λ$C and $^5_Λ$He are presented and they show that the combined kinematical plus nonlocal corrections have an appreciable influence on the partial decay rates. However, this is somewhat diminished in the main decay observables: the total nonmesonic rate, the neutron-to-proton branching ratio, and the asymmetry parameter. The latter two still cannot be reconciled with the available experimental data. The existing theoretical predictions for the sign of the asymmetry parameter in $^5_Λ$He are confirmed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a one-meson-exchange transition potential for nonmesonic lambda-hypernuclear decay that includes two previously omitted effects, kinematical corrections from the lambda-nucleon mass difference and first-order nonlocality, and finds that while these corrections appreciably influence partial decay rates, their impact on the main observables (total nonmesonic rate, neutron-to-proton branching ratio, and asymmetry parameter) is diminished, and the latter two still cannot be reconciled with experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed a nonrelativistic reduction of the Feynman amplitudes for one-meson-exchange involving the full pseudoscalar and vector meson octets (π, η, K, ρ, ω, K*), deriving the transition potential in coordinate space with explicit treatment of the lambda-nucleon mass difference and first-order nonlocal terms (up to first-order differential operators). They applied this potential to calculate partial decay rates, total nonmesonic rates, neutron-to-proton branching ratios, and asymmetry parameters for ¹²_ΛC and ⁵_ΛHe using the extreme particle-hole model of Barbero (2002).

## Key numbers

- The effective pion mass is reduced to \(\tilde{\mu}_\pi/\mu_\pi = 0.752\), increasing the range of the pion contribution by approximately 35%
- The kinematical mass \(M_0 = 92.18\) MeV, with \(1/M_q = 2.196 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV⁻¹ and \(1/M_Q = 8.800 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV⁻¹, using \(M = 938.92\) MeV and \(M_\Lambda = 1115.68\) MeV
- The correction factor \((1 + M_0/M_q)^{-1} = 0.998\) and \(\check{M}/\bar{M} = 0.996\) for the pion potential
- The ratio \((M_\Lambda - M)/\bar{M} = 0.17\), justifying the need for kinematical corrections compared to the neutron-proton mass difference ratio of 0.0014

## Context

This work extends the one-meson-exchange model approach previously used by Parreño (1997) and others, who had neglected the lambda-nucleon mass difference and nonlocality effects. The paper confirms the sign of the asymmetry parameter in ⁵_ΛHe predicted by earlier theoretical work, but shows that even with these corrections, the neutron-to-proton branching ratio and asymmetry parameter cannot be reconciled with experimental data, pointing to the need for additional physics beyond the one-meson-exchange framework, such as two-pion exchange (Shimmura 1994, Itonaga 2002) or quark models (Cheung 1983, Sasaki 2000).
