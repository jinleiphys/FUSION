# Three-body Halos. IV. Momentum Distributions after Fragmentation

> Momentum distributions of particles from nuclear break-up of fast three-body halos are calculated incorporating effects of final state interactions. The same two-body interactions between the particles are used to calculate both the ground state structure and the final state of the reaction processes. The ground state wave function reproduces size and energy of the halo nucleus. First we give a general and detailed description of the method. Then we investigate the effect of final state interactions in neutron removal and core break-up reactions for one- and two-dimensional momentum distributions. We compute specifically core and neutron momentum distributions from 11Li fragmentation and compare those with available experimental data. We conclude that 11Li must have a neutron-core relative state containing a p-state admixture of 20\%-30\%. The fragmentation data also strongly suggest that 10Li has an s-state at about 50 keV and a p-state around 500 keV.

## Key claim

The momentum distributions from fragmentation of three-body halos are significantly affected by final-state interactions (FSI) between the two remaining particles, especially when low-lying resonances are present; the authors conclude that \(^{11}\)Li must have a neutron-core relative state containing a \(p\)-state admixture of 20%-30%, and that \(^{10}\)Li has an \(s\)-state at about 50 keV and a \(p\)-state around 500 keV, based on comparison with experimental data.

## Method

The authors use the sudden approximation for high-energy fragmentation reactions, where one particle is instantaneously removed from the three-body projectile. The initial three-body wave function is constructed by solving Faddeev equations with hyperspherical coordinates, using the same two-body interactions for both the ground state and the final state. The final-state two-body wave function is obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with the appropriate two-body potential, replacing the plane wave with a distorted wave to account for FSI. Momentum distributions are computed by squaring the transition matrix and integrating over unobserved variables, then transformed to the center-of-mass system of the projectile for comparison with experiment.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{11}\)Li binding energy is \(295 \pm 35\) keV and its root mean square radius is \(3.1 \pm 0.3\) fm.
- For a neutron removal reaction, the FWHM of the core momentum distribution decreases from 68 MeV/c (no FSI) to 55 MeV/c (with FSI) when the virtual \(s_{1/2}\)-state in \(^{10}\)Li is at 100 keV; for a \(p_{1/2}\)-resonance at 100 keV, the FWHM decreases from 92 MeV/c (no FSI) to 87 MeV/c (with FSI).
- The neutron-neutron scattering lengths used are: \(a(^1S_0)=18.8\) fm, \(a(^3P_0)=3.6\) fm, \(a(^3P_1)=-2.0\) fm, and \(a(^3P_2)=0.30\) fm; the \(s\)-wave effective range is \(r_e(^1S_0)=2.76\) fm.
- The \(s\)-wave content in the neutron-core subsystem of \(^{11}\)Li ranges from 97% (virtual \(s_{1/2}\)-state at 100 keV) to 22% (\(p_{1/2}\)-resonance at 100 keV).

## Context

This paper is the fourth in a series on three-body halos, extending earlier work on Borromean systems (Garrido, Fedorov, Jensen, previous papers in the series) and general three-body systems. It addresses the problem that momentum distributions from fragmentation reactions mix effects from the projectile structure and the reaction mechanism, as noted in earlier studies by Zhukov (1993), Korsheninnikov (1994), and Barranco (1994). The method enables extraction of information about unbound subsystems like \(^{10}\)Li from fragmentation data, and the results suggest specific \(s\)- and \(p\)-state energies in \(^{10}\)Li that can guide future experiments and theoretical models.
