# Multiplicities and bulk thermodynamic quantities at \mathversion{bold}{$\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$} GeV with SHARE

> We introduce the Statistical Hadronization with Resonances (SHARE) suite of programs and perform a study of particle multiplicities as well as bulk thermodynamic quantities for the RHIC Au--Au reactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV. We also show that the statistical hadronization model, with parameters fitted to match pion, proton and hyperon ratios, in turn correctly and consistently reproduces rapidity particle multiplicity.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model, when fitted to particle ratios (pion, proton, hyperon) at RHIC Au–Au \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130\) GeV, correctly and consistently reproduces rapidity particle multiplicity, but the highest statistical significance is achieved only when the full chemical non-equilibrium ansatz (allowing both light and strange quark phase space occupancies \(\gamma_q\) and \(\gamma_s\) to vary) is used, rather than assuming chemical equilibrium.

## Method

The authors used the Statistical Hadronization with Resonances (SHARE) suite to fit particle ratios and total charged multiplicities from the seven most central STAR bins at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130\) GeV. They compared three model variants: full chemical non-equilibrium (\(\gamma_q\) and \(\gamma_s\) free), full chemical equilibrium (\(\gamma_q=\gamma_s=1\)), and partial non-equilibrium (\(\gamma_q=1\), \(\gamma_s\) free), each with and without finite resonance width treatment. The fitted thermal parameters (temperature \(T\), light and strange quark fugacities \(\lambda_q,\lambda_s\), isospin fugacity \(\lambda_{I_3}\), and phase space occupancies \(\gamma_q,\gamma_s\)) were then used to compute bulk thermodynamic quantities such as entropy per baryon, energy per particle, and strangeness content.

## Key numbers

* Temperature in the full non-equilibrium fit (with finite width): \(T = 135 \pm 12\) MeV.
* Light quark phase space occupancy in full non-equilibrium (with finite width): \(\gamma_q = 1.65 \pm 0.030\).
* Strange quark phase space occupancy in full non-equilibrium (with finite width): \(\gamma_s = 2.28 \pm 0.46\).
* \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for the full non-equilibrium fit (with finite width): 0.4554 (16 data points, 5 free parameters).
* \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for the full equilibrium fit (with finite width): 0.8832 (16 data points, 3 free parameters).
* Entropy per net baryon \(S/(B-\overline{B})\) in full non-equilibrium (with finite width): 369.857.
* Entropy per net baryon \(S/(B-\overline{B})\) in full equilibrium (with finite width): 252.001.
* Energy per charged hadron \(E/(h^++h^-)\) in full non-equilibrium (with finite width): 0.882.
* Energy per charged hadron \(E/(h^++h^-)\) in full equilibrium (with finite width): 1.004.
* Fraction of particles from weak decays in full non-equilibrium (with finite width): 0.326.
* Fraction of particles from weak decays in full equilibrium (with finite width): 0.427.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization approach pioneered by Fermi (1950) and Pomeranchuk (1951), and later refined by Hagedorn (1965) to include the full resonance spectrum. It directly compares three chemical (non)equilibrium scenarios previously discussed in the literature, notably the under-saturation of strangeness via \(\gamma_s\) (Jan Rafelski, 1990s) and the over-saturation of light quarks via \(\gamma_q\) (Jan Rafelski, 2000s). The SHARE code provides an open-source standard for such analyses, enabling future studies to test these models against higher-statistics data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and to couple the statistical parameters with dynamical flow models (e.g., Blast Wave) to resolve the HBT puzzle and test full energy conservation.
