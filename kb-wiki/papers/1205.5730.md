# Three-body breakup within the fully discretized Faddeev equations

> A novel approach is developed to find the three-body breakup amplitudes and cross sections within the modified Faddeev equation framework. The method is based on the lattice-like discretization of the three-body continuum with a three-body stationary wave-packet basis in momentum space. The approach makes it possible to simplify drastically all the three- and few-body breakup calculations due to discrete wave-packet representations for the few-body continuum and simultaneous lattice representation for all the scattering operators entering the integral equation kernels. As a result, the few-body breakup can be treated as a particular case of multi-channel scattering in which part of the channels represents the true few-body continuum states. As an illustration for the novel approach, an accurate calculations for the three-body breakup process $n+d\to n+n+p$ with non-local and local $NN$ interactions are calculated. The results obtained reproduce nicely the benchmark calculation results using the traditional Faddeev scheme which requires much more tedious and time-consuming calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel approach to computing three-body breakup amplitudes and cross sections by fully discretizing the three-body continuum using a stationary wave-packet basis in momentum space, which simplifies calculations compared to traditional Faddeev schemes. The authors claim the method reproduces benchmark results for the n+d to n+n+p process, but the validation is limited to s-wave spin-dependent pair interactions only, not yet extended to higher partial waves or more realistic interactions.

## Method

The authors construct a three-body lattice basis from direct products of two-body stationary wave packets (WPs) built by integrating plane waves over momentum bins for both Jacobi coordinates, yielding orthonormalized square-integrable states. They then diagonalize the two-body subHamiltonian matrix in the free WP basis to obtain pseudostates that approximate scattering WPs for the channel Hamiltonian, and use these to build three-body channel WP states. The Faddeev equation for the transition operator U is solved in this finite-dimensional WP representation, where the channel resolvent G1 has explicit analytical diagonal matrix elements, and the permutation operator matrix is computed via one-dimensional numerical integration over hypermomentum.

## Key numbers

- The three-body breakup process n+d to n+n+p is calculated at low and moderate energies E_n approximately 10 to 30 MeV.
- The free WP basis uses M bins for the p-momentum and N bins for the q-momentum, with bin widths d_i = p_i - p_{i-1} and bar-d_j = q_j - q_{j-1}.
- The deuteron binding energy epsilon_0* is obtained from diagonalization of the NN spin-triplet channel, with the first pseudostate approximating the deuteron wave function.
- The NN spin-singlet channel has no bound states, so all pseudostates correspond to scattering WP states.
- The total three-body spin Sigma takes values 1/2 and 3/2 in the s-wave case.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the wave-packet lattice technique for elastic 2-to-2 scattering (Rubtsova et al., 2010-2011) to the treatment of three-body breakup, which is a more challenging problem within L2-type approaches. The method offers an alternative to traditional momentum-space Faddeev calculations (Gloeckle et al., 1996) that require time-consuming multi-dimensional interpolations at each iteration step, and differs from the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) method (Thompson, 1988) by discretizing both Jacobi coordinates globally rather than only the projectile inner motion. The approach enables simpler calculations of breakup observables and could facilitate testing of new three-nucleon interaction models, though the present work only demonstrates the method for s-wave interactions.
