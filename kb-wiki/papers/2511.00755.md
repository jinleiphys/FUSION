# High-order cumulants and correlation functions near the critical point from molecular dynamics

> We present a systematic investigation of particle number fluctuations in the crossover region near the critical endpoint of a first-order phase transition using molecular dynamics simulations of the classical Lennard-Jones fluid. We extend our prior studies to third- and fourth-order cumulants in both coordinate- and momentum-space acceptances and integrated correlation functions (factorial cumulants). We find that, even near the critical point, non-Gaussian cumulants equilibrate on time scales comparable to those of the second-order cumulants, but show stronger finite-size effects. The presence of interactions and of the critical point leads to strong deviations of the cumulants from the ideal-gas baseline in coordinate space; these deviations are expected to persist in momentum space in the presence of collective expansion. In particular, the kurtosis becomes strongly negative, $κσ^2 \ll -1$, on the crossover side of the critical point. However, this signal is significantly diluted once an efficiency cut used to distinguish protons from baryons is applied, leading to $|κσ^2| \lesssim 1$ even in the presence of the critical point. We discuss our results in the context of ongoing measurements of proton number cumulants in heavy-ion collisions in RHIC-BES-II.

## Key claim

The authors find that, even near the critical point, non-Gaussian cumulants (skewness and kurtosis) equilibrate on time scales comparable to those of the second-order cumulant, but show stronger finite-size effects; however, the strongly negative kurtosis signal ($\kappa\sigma^2 \ll -1$) observed on the crossover side of the critical point in coordinate space is significantly diluted once an efficiency cut used to distinguish protons from baryons is applied, leading to $|\kappa\sigma^2| \lesssim 1$ even in the presence of the critical point.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics simulations of a classical Lennard-Jones fluid in a box with periodic boundary conditions, using the Velocity-Verlet integration method for N = 400 particles. They studied particle number fluctuations along the supercritical isotherm at T = 1.06 Tc at three densities: dilute (n = 0.32 nc), near-critical (n = 0.95 nc), and dense (n = 1.9 nc), computing third- and fourth-order cumulants and factorial cumulants in both coordinate-space and momentum-space acceptances. The Subensemble Acceptance Method was used to correct for global baryon number conservation and extract grand-canonical ensemble estimates from the microcanonical simulations.

## Key numbers

*   The critical point of the Lennard-Jones fluid is located at reduced temperature Tc* = 1.321 ± 0.007 and reduced density nc* = 0.316 ± 0.005.
*   For the near-critical density (n = 0.95 nc) at acceptance fraction α = 0.5, the kurtosis in coordinate space reaches κσ² ≈ -17.5.
*   The relaxation time τ for the scaled variance ω near the critical density (n = 0.95 nc) is τ = 3.70 ± 0.02 (in reduced time units), showing critical slowing down compared to τ = 0.98 ± 0.14 in the dense phase (n = 1.9 nc).
*   After correction for baryon number conservation, the scaled variance at α = 0.4 reaches ω̃ ≈ 1.52 for the dilute case (n = 0.32 nc) and ω̃ ≈ 0.25 for the dense case (n = 1.9 nc), compared to grand-canonical estimates of ω_gce = 1.9 and 0.33, respectively.
*   The corrected skewness S̃σ̃ saturates at approximately +2 in the dilute region and -0.5 in the dense region, compared to grand-canonical predictions of (Sσ)_gce = 3.27 and -1.53, respectively.

## Context

This work extends prior molecular dynamics studies of the Lennard-Jones fluid by Kuznietsov et al. (2022, 2023, 2024), which were limited to second-order fluctuations, to third- and fourth-order cumulants and factorial cumulants. The results are discussed in the context of ongoing measurements of proton number cumulants in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC-BES-II, where the STAR collaboration has reported possible nonmonotonic trends in fourth-order cumulants of net-proton distributions (STAR 2021, 2025). The finding that the critical point signal is strongly diluted when applying efficiency cuts to distinguish protons from baryons provides a quantitative constraint for interpreting experimental fluctuation measurements and suggests that large negative kurtosis may still serve as a potential signal of the critical point, though finite-size effects in systems of N = 400 particles are significant.
