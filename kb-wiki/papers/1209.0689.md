# Beta decays in the LHC era: from ultracold neutrons to colliders

> In this talk I review the New Physics reach of semileptonic beta decay experiments, and their interplay with LHC searches. Assuming the new particles are heavy enough we can use an Effective Field Theory approach to analyze the LHC searches, what allows us to perform a direct and model-independent comparison with low-energy experiments.

## Key claim

Semileptonic beta decay experiments and LHC searches in the \(pp\to e\nu\) channel probe the same underlying partonic process (\(\bar{u}d\to e\bar{\nu}\)), and assuming new particles are heavy enough, an Effective Field Theory (EFT) approach allows a direct, model-independent comparison of their New Physics reach.

## Method

The author uses a quark-level effective Lagrangian with non-standard couplings \(\epsilon_i\) and \(\tilde{\epsilon}_i\) to describe semileptonic transitions at the 1 GeV scale, then maps hadronic-level coefficients \(C\) to these quark-level couplings via form factors. For the LHC analysis, the cross-section for \(pp\to e\nu\) events with high transverse mass is expressed as a function of the same \(\epsilon_i\) coefficients, allowing direct comparison of bounds from low-energy experiments (nuclear, neutron, and meson decays) with those from collider data.

## Key numbers

- Superallowed nuclear decays (Hardy 2004) provide 90% C.L. limits on scalar and tensor couplings \(\epsilon_{S,T}\), shown as green region in Figure 1 (left).
- Radiative pion decay (Bychkov 2008) provides 90% C.L. limits on \(\epsilon_{S,T}\), shown as yellow region in Figure 1 (left).
- CMS data with 5 fb\(^{-1}\) at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV in the \(pp\to e\nu\) channel yields 90% C.L. bounds on scalar and tensor couplings, shown as blue solid line in Figure 1 (right).
- Previous CMS bound using 1 fb\(^{-1}\) of data (Bhattacharya 2011) is shown for comparison as red solid line in Figure 1 (right).
- The ratio \(R_\pi = \Gamma(\pi\to e\nu)/\Gamma(\pi\to \mu\nu)\) (Britton 1992) strongly constrains pseudo-scalar and (axial)vector couplings.

## Context

This work extends the EFT-based comparison of low-energy beta decay constraints and LHC searches initiated by Bhattacharya (2011) and others, using the same quark-level Lagrangian framework. The analysis shows that for scalar and tensor interactions, low-energy and high-energy searches compete, while for pseudoscalar couplings low-energy probes are more powerful, and for interactions involving right-handed neutrinos the LHC will dominate. The combination of both search strategies is necessary for a complete picture of non-standard charged current interactions.
