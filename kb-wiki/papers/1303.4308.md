# MCNP6 Fission Cross Section Calculations at Intermediate and High Energies

> MCNP6 has been Validated and Verified (V&V) against intermediate- and high-energy fission cross-section experimental data. An error in the calculation of fission cross sections of 181Ta and a few nearby target nuclei by the CEM03.03 event generator in MCNP6 and a "bug: in the calculation of fission cross sections with the GENXS option of MCNP6 while using the LAQGSM03.03 event generator were detected during our V&V work. After fixing both problems, we find that MCNP6 using CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 calculates fission cross sections in good agreement with available experimental data for reactions induced by nucleons, pions, and photons on both subactinide and actinide nuclei at incident energies from several tens of MeV to about 1 TeV.

## Key claim

After fixing an error in CEM03.03 and a bug in the GENXS option for LAQGSM03.03, MCNP6 calculates fission cross sections in good agreement with experimental data for reactions induced by nucleons, pions, and photons on both subactinide and actinide nuclei at incident energies from several tens of MeV to about 1 TeV, but only after these specific corrections were applied.

## Method

The authors validated and verified MCNP6 against intermediate- and high-energy fission cross section experimental data using the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM03.03) and the Los Alamos Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM03.03) event generators. They detected an error in CEM03.03 introduced during a 2005 code upgrade that affected fission cross sections for 181Ta and nearby nuclei, and a bug in the GENXS output option for LAQGSM03.03 that miscounted fissioning nuclei. Both problems were fixed by refitting the ratio of level-density parameters (af/an) in the GEM2 fission model for CEM03.03 and correcting the counting routine in the GENXS portion of MCNP6.

## Key numbers

- Fission cross sections for p + 181Ta showed "very bad results at proton energies above ~200 MeV" before the CEM03.03 error was fixed.
- The corrected CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators in MCNP6 produce fission cross sections in good agreement with data for targets from 165Ho to 239Pu.
- Incident energy range validated: from several tens of MeV to about 1 TeV.
- The level-density parameter ratio used in GEM2 for subactinide nuclei is given by af = an (1.08926 + 0.01098 (χ - 31.08551)^2), where χ = Z^2/A.

## Context

This work extends prior validation of CEM and LAQGSM event generators (Mashnik et al., 2003) which had shown good agreement for fission cross sections when used as standalone codes, but had not been tested within MCNP6 or its precursor MCNPX. The detected error in CEM03.02/03.03 explains poor agreement with measured nuclide yields from proton interactions with 181Ta above 250 MeV reported by Titarenko (2011). The corrected MCNP6 now enables reliable fission cross section calculations for applications in transport codes at intermediate and high energies.
