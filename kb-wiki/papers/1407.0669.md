# High-resolution search for the $Θ^{+}$ pentaquark via a pion-induced reaction at J-PARC

> The pentaquark $Θ^+$ has been searched for via the $π^-p \to K^-X$ reaction with beam momenta of 1.92 and 2.01 GeV/$c$ at J-PARC. A missing mass resolution of 2 MeV (FWHM) was achieved but no sharp peak structure was observed. The upper limits on the production cross section averaged over the scattering angle from 2$^{\circ}$ to 15$^{\circ}$ in the laboratory frame were found to be less than 0.28 $μ$b/sr at the 90\% confidence level for both the 1.92- and 2.01-GeV/$c$ data. The systematic uncertainty of the upper limits was controlled within 10\%. Constraints on the $Θ^+$ decay width were also evaluated with a theoretical calculation using effective Lagrangian. The present result implies that the width should be less than 0.36 and 1.9 MeV for the spin-parity of $1/2^+$ and $1/2^-$, respectively.

## Key claim
The J-PARC E19 Collaboration searched for the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark via the $\pi^-p \to K^-X$ reaction at beam momenta of 1.92 and 2.01 GeV/$c$ and found no sharp peak structure, placing upper limits on the production cross section that are an order of magnitude more stringent than previous measurements. The abstract's claim of "no sharp peak structure" glosses over the fact that a bump structure at 1530 MeV/$c^2$ had been previously reported in the same reaction by the KEK E522 experiment, but with only 2.5--2.7$\sigma$ significance; the present higher-resolution measurement did not confirm that bump.

## Method
The experiment used the K1.8 beam line at J-PARC to deliver pion beams of 1.92 and 2.01 GeV/$c$ onto a liquid hydrogen target. The missing mass of the $\pi^-p \to K^-X$ reaction was reconstructed using a beam spectrometer (with multiwire proportional chambers and drift chambers) and the Superconducting Kaon Spectrometer (SKS), achieving a missing mass resolution of approximately 2 MeV (FWHM). The analysis included particle identification via time-of-flight and Cherenkov counters, vertex reconstruction, and calibration using $\Sigma^{\pm}$ production data and $\pi^{\pm}$ beam-through data to correct the momentum scale and evaluate the mass resolution.

## Key numbers
- Missing mass resolution: 2.13 $\pm$ 0.15 MeV (FWHM) for the 2.01 GeV/$c$ data, averaged over scattering angles 2$^{\circ}$ to 15$^{\circ}$; 1.72 MeV (FWHM) for the 1.92 GeV/$c$ data.
- Upper limit on production cross section: less than 0.28 $\mu$b/sr at 90% confidence level, averaged over scattering angles 2$^{\circ}$ to 15$^{\circ}$ in the laboratory frame, for both the 1.92 and 2.01 GeV/$c$ data.
- Systematic uncertainty on the upper limits: controlled within 10%.
- Constraint on $\Theta^+$ decay width: less than 0.36 MeV for spin-parity $1/2^+$ and less than 1.9 MeV for spin-parity $1/2^-$, evaluated with a theoretical calculation using effective Lagrangian.
- Beam flux: $7.8 \times 10^{10}$ pions at 1.92 GeV/$c$ (2010 run) and $8.1 \times 10^{10}$ pions at 2.01 GeV/$c$ (2012 run).
- Momentum resolution of beam spectrometer: $\Delta p_{\rm B}/p_{\rm B} = (1.4 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-3}$ (FWHM); SKS spectrometer: $\Delta p_{\rm S}/p_{\rm S} = (2.0 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-3}$ (FWHM).

## Context
This work extends the search for the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark via meson-induced reactions, following the KEK E522 experiment (Miwa, 2006) which reported a bump at 1530 MeV/$c^2$ with 2.5--2.7$\sigma$ significance and a mass resolution of 13 MeV (FWHM). The present result improves the mass resolution by an order of magnitude and achieves an order of magnitude higher sensitivity than E522. The null result is consistent with the Belle Collaboration's upper limit on the $\Theta^+$ width (Mizuk, 2006) of $\Gamma_{\Theta}<0.64$ MeV at 90% C.L., and with the DIANA Collaboration's claimed observation (2014) of a width $0.34\pm0.10$ MeV. The constraints on the decay width from this experiment provide input for theoretical models of the $\Theta^+$ structure, such as the diquark correlation models of Jaffe-Wilczek (2003) and Karliner-Lipkin (2003).
