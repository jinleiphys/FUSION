# Strangeness and Particle Freeze-out in Nuclear Collisions at 14.6 Gev a

> We study the chemical conditions at freeze-out associated with the production of strange-particles in Si-Au collisions at 14.6 GeV A. We obtain freeze-out chemical potentials and temperature, and determine the entropy as well as the final particle abundance. We also consider in detail the alternative evolution scenarios involving the hadronic gas and the deconfined phase.

## Key claim

The freeze-out conditions in Si-Au collisions at 14.6 GeV A are well described by an equilibrium hadronic gas model, with the strange-quark fugacity λ_s = 1.71 (+0.20, -0.15) differing by more than four standard deviations from the CERN value of λ_s ≈ 1.03, suggesting either that the quark-gluon plasma phase was not formed at AGS energies or that complete re-equilibration occurred during hadronization.

## Method

The authors employed a thermal fireball model assuming local equilibrium, using state-of-the-art equations of state for both hadronic gas (HG) and quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phases. For the HG they used a refined Hagedorn approach treating all known hadronic resonances as independent particles, with pions and mesons as bosons and nucleons and Δ-resonances as fermions. For the QGP they used an ideal quark-gluon gas with perturbative QCD corrections and non-perturbative thermal masses. The strange particle ratios K⁻/K⁺ and Λ̅/Λ from the E802/E859 experiment were used to determine chemical potentials, temperature, and entropy at freeze-out, with strangeness conservation as a constraint and allowing for a strangeness phase space occupancy factor γ_s.

## Key numbers

- K⁻/K⁺ = 0.22 ± 0.02 (central rapidity range 1 < y < 1.6)
- Λ̅/Λ = (2.0 ± 0.8) × 10⁻³ (central rapidity, 1.1 < y < 1.7)
- λ_s = 1.71 (+0.20, -0.15) (strange-quark fugacity at freeze-out)
- λ_q = 3.62 (+0.39, -0.26) (light-quark fugacity at freeze-out)
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 127 ± 8 MeV
- Freeze-out baryon chemical potential μ_B,f = 485 ± 70 MeV
- Freeze-out strange chemical potential μ_s,f = 68 (+20, -16) MeV
- Specific entropy at freeze-out S/B = 13 ± 1
- Meson-to-baryon ratio R_m/b = 1.6 ± 0.2
- Freeze-out baryon density ρ_f = (0.7 ± 0.3) ρ_N, where ρ_N = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Initial temperature T_0 = 190 ± 30 MeV (for both HG and QGP scenarios)
- Initial energy density in HG scenario: ε_0^HG = 2.3 GeV fm⁻³
- Initial baryon density in HG scenario: ρ_0^HG = 6 ρ_N
- Initial energy density in QGP scenario: ε_0^QGP = 1.2 GeV fm⁻³
- Initial baryon density in QGP scenario: ρ_0^QGP = 2.9 ρ_N
- Ratio λ_q/λ_s = 2.12 ± 0.12 at AGS, compared to ≈ 1.5 at CERN (200 GeV A)

## Context

This work extends the thermal fireball analysis previously applied to CERN SPS collisions at 200 GeV A (Rafelski 1991, Letessier 1993) down to AGS energies of 14.6 GeV A, where the authors find freeze-out conditions consistent with equilibrium hadronic gas rather than the deconfined phase suggested at higher energies. The results contradict the expectation that ARC cascade models (Gong 1994) could describe strange particle production without novel mechanisms, and enable predictions for upcoming Au-Au collisions at 11-12 GeV A, where the authors suggest that multi-strange anti-baryon abundances could serve as a definitive test for QGP formation.
