# Isospin Effects on Meson Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We show that the phenomenology of isospin effects on heavy ion reactions at intermediate energies (few AGeV range) is extremely rich and can allow a ``direct'' study of the covariant structure of the isovector interaction in a high density hadron medium. We work within a relativistic transport frame, beyond a cascade picture, consistently derived from effective Lagrangians, where isospin effects are accounted for in the mean field and collision terms. We show that rather sensitive observables are provided by the pion/kaon production (π^-/π^+, K^0/K^+ yields). Relevant non-equilibrium effects are stressed. The possibility of the transition to a mixed hadron-quark phase, at high baryon and isospin density, is finally suggested. Some signatures could come from an expected ``neutron trapping'' effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the isospin content of meson production (specifically the \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) and \(K^0/K^+\) yield ratios) in relativistic heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies (few AGeV range) can serve as a direct probe of the covariant structure of the isovector nuclear interaction in high-density hadronic matter, but only when non-equilibrium effects are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic transport calculations using the Relativistic Landau Vlasov (RLV) method, which goes beyond a simple cascade picture and is consistently derived from effective Lagrangians. They simulated central \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au collisions at beam energies from 0.8 to 1.8 AGeV, comparing four different models for the isovector part of the equation of state: \(NL\) (no isovector interactions), \(NL\rho\) (vector \(\rho\) meson only), \(NL\rho\delta\) (both vector \(\rho\) and scalar \(\delta\) mesons), and \(DDF\) (density-dependent couplings). The collision term included elastic and inelastic processes involving \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(N^*(1440)\) resonances and their decays into pions.

## Key numbers

*   For central Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV, the pion yield freezes out at times of the order of 50 fm/c, while kaon production saturates at around 20 fm/c.
*   In simulations of \(^{238}\)U+\(^{238}\)U collisions at 1 AGeV with semicentral impact parameter \(b = 7\) fm, a local equilibration is achieved after about 10 fm/c, with baryon density around \(3-4\rho_0\), temperature \(50-60\) MeV, energy density \(500\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), and proton fraction between 0.35 and 0.40.
*   For the \(^{132}\)Sn+\(^{132}\)Sn system, the path in the \((\rho, Z/A)\) plane reaches the border of the mixed hadron-quark phase at 300 AMeV and is well inside it at 1 AGeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin effects in heavy ion reactions, previously explored by Baran et al. (2005) and others, from equilibrium properties to the non-equilibrium dynamics of relativistic collisions. It builds on relativistic mean field models (Quantum-Hadro-Dynamics) and prior transport approaches by Fuchs et al. (1995) and Ferini et al. (2005). The paper enables future experimental searches for a mixed hadron-quark phase at high baryon and isospin density, suggesting that a "neutron trapping" effect in the quark phase could be observable through an inversion in the trend of \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) or \(K^0/K^+\) yield ratios for high-transverse-momentum particles.
