# Consistency check of charged hadron multiplicities and fragmentation functions in SIDIS

> We derived the conditions on certain combinations of integrals of the fragmentation functions of pion using HERMES data of the sum for the charged pion multiplicities from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) off the deuteron target. In our derivation the nucleon parton distribution functions (PDFs) are assumed to be isospin SU(2) symmetric. Similar conditions have also been obtained for the fragmentation functions (FFs) of kaon by the sum of charged kaon multiplicities as well. We have chosen several FFs to study the impact of those conditions we have derived. Among those FFs, only that produced in the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NL$χ$QM) constantly satisfy the conditions. Furthermore, the ratios of the strange PDFs $S(x)$ and the nonstrange PDFs $Q(x,Q^2)$ extracted from the charged pion and kaon multiplicities differ from each other significantly. Finally, we demonstrate that the HERMES pion multiplicity data is unlikely to be compatible with the two widely-used PDFs, namely CTEQ6M and NNPDF3.0.

## Key claim

The authors derive consistency conditions relating integrals of fragmentation functions (FFs) to charged hadron multiplicities measured in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS), and show that under the assumption of isospin SU(2) symmetric parton distribution functions (PDFs), only the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLχQM) fragmentation functions satisfy the conditions for both pions and kaons simultaneously, while the HERMES pion multiplicity data are incompatible with the widely-used CTEQ6M and NNPDF3.0 PDF sets.

## Method

The authors used leading-order QCD formulas for charged pion and kaon multiplicities from SIDIS off a deuteron target, assuming isospin SU(2) symmetry for the nucleon PDFs. They derived positivity conditions on combinations of integrated fragmentation functions \(D^{\pi}_{Q}(Q^2)\) and \(D^{\pi}_{S}(Q^2)\) (and similarly for kaons) by requiring that the ratio of strange to nonstrange PDFs \(S(x,Q^2)/Q(x,Q^2)\) remain positive. They then tested nine different fragmentation function parametrizations and models against HERMES data at nine kinematic points, and compared the extracted \(S/Q\) ratios with those from CTEQ6M and NNPDF3.0 PDFs.

## Key numbers

- HERMES data points cover \(x\) from 0.03349 to 0.45147 and \(Q^2\) from 1.1931 to 10.2355 GeV\(^2\), with charged pion multiplicities \(M^{\pi}_{D}\) ranging from 0.676 to 0.800 and charged kaon multiplicities \(M^{K}_{D}\) ranging from 0.103 to 0.132.
- The integration range for fragmentation functions is \(z_{\text{min}} = 0.2\) to \(z_{\text{max}} = 0.8\).
- For NLχQM at \(Q^2 = 1.1931\) GeV\(^2\): \(D^{\pi}_{Q} = 4.50\), \(D^{\pi}_{S} = 0.28\); at \(Q^2 = 10.2355\) GeV\(^2\): \(D^{\pi}_{Q} = 3.53\), \(D^{\pi}_{S} = 0.21\).
- For NLχQM at \(Q^2 = 1.1931\) GeV\(^2\): \(D^{K}_{Q} = 0.20\), \(D^{K}_{S} = 0.87\); at \(Q^2 = 10.2355\) GeV\(^2\): \(D^{K}_{Q} = 0.33\), \(D^{K}_{S} = 0.81\).
- For DSS (LO) at \(Q^2 = 1.1931\) GeV\(^2\): \(D^{K}_{Q} = 0.43\), \(D^{K}_{S} = 1.41\); at \(Q^2 = 10.2355\) GeV\(^2\): \(D^{K}_{Q} = 0.44\), \(D^{K}_{S} = 1.13\).
- The \(S/Q\) ratio extracted from kaon multiplicities using NLχQM FFs is approximately 0.5 across most data points, while the ratio from CTEQ6M and NNPDF3.0 PDFs is much smaller.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of fragmentation functions in SIDIS by deriving model-independent consistency conditions, building on earlier extractions of strange-quark PDFs from kaon multiplicities (e.g., the work cited as strange1, strange2, strange3 in the paper). The authors test nine fragmentation function sets including HKNS (Hirai, Kumano, Nagai, Sudoh, 2007), DSS (de Florian, Sassot, Stratmann, 2007), AKK08 (Albino, Kniehl, Kramer, 2008), SKMA (Soleymaninia, Khorramian, Moosavinejad, Arbabifar), DSEHS (de Florian, Sassot, Epele, Hernandez-Pinto, Stratmann), NJL-Jet model (Matevosyan et al., 2010-2011), and NLχQM (Nam et al., 2011-2013). The results enable future work to better constrain fragmentation functions and strange-quark PDFs, while highlighting the strong dependence of strange PDF extraction on the choice of fragmentation functions.
