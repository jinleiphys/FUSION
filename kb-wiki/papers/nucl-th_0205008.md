# Nuclear modifications of fragmentation functions and rescaling models

> We discuss nuclear modification of fragmentation functions in the context of the so-called ``rescaling models''. These models implement partial deconfinement inside nuclei by modifying the fragmentation functions perturbatively. We apply these models to the analysis of nuclear hadron production in deep inelastic scattering processes at the HERMES and EMC experiments.

## Key claim

Rescaling models, which implement partial deconfinement inside nuclei by modifying fragmentation functions perturbatively, can describe nuclear hadron production data from both HERMES and EMC experiments only when supplemented by nuclear absorption effects; the maximal deconfinement model is ruled out by the data, while the partial deconfinement model provides a good description.

## Method

The authors compute the multiplicity ratio for charged hadrons in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) using leading-order pQCD with rescaled parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs), where the scale factor ξ_A(Q) accounts for a change in confinement scale λ_A > λ_0 inside nuclei. They implement two variants: the maximal deconfinement model (NP) with λ_A = R_A (nuclear radius) and the partial deconfinement model (CJRR) with λ_A proportional to nucleon overlap. Nuclear absorption is included via a formation time τ_F and a survival probability S_A, using σ_h = 20 mbarn for charged hadrons and κ = 0.25 GeV/fm, with GRV98 PDFs and Kretzer's leading-order FF parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Scale factor in maximal deconfinement model: ξ_A(Q) = R_A/R_p, where R_A is nuclear radius
- Scale factor in partial deconfinement model: ξ_A(Q) = (λ_A/λ_0)^{(1/2)(α_s(Q_0)/α_s(Q))}
- Nuclear absorption cross-section for charged hadrons: σ_h = 20 mbarn
- Formation time parameter: κ = 0.25 GeV/fm
- Nuclear density radius parameterization: R_A = 1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3} fm

## Context

This work extends the rescaling models of Nachtmann and Pirner (1984), Close et al. (1984), and Diaz de Deus et al. (1986) by applying them to nuclear fragmentation functions and comparing with HERMES and EMC data. The analysis shows that the partial deconfinement model (CJRR) combined with nuclear absorption from Bialas and Chmaj (1983) and Bialas and Gyulassy (1987) can describe both experiments, while the maximal deconfinement model (NP) is ruled out. The results enable future disentanglement of rescaling and formation time effects through precise data at moderate and high ν for light and heavy targets.
