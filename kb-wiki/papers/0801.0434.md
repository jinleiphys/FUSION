# The Transverse-momentum-dependent Parton Distribution Function and Jet Transport in Medium

> We show that the gauge-invariant transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) quark distribution function can be expressed as a sum of all higher-twist collinear parton matrix elements in terms of a transport operator. From such a general expression, we derive the nuclear broadening of the transverse momentum distribution. Under the maximal two-gluon correlation approximation, in which all higher-twist nuclear multiple-parton correlations with the leading nuclear enhancement are given by products of twist-two nucleon parton distributions, we find the nuclear transverse momentum distribution as a convolution of a Gaussian distribution and the nucleon TMD quark distribution. The width of the Gaussian, or the mean total transverse momentum broadening squared, is given by the path integral of the quark transport parameter $\hat q_F$ which can also be expressed in a gauge invariant form and is given by the gluon distribution density in the nuclear medium. We further show that contributions from higher-twist nucleon gluon distributions can be resummed under the extended adjoint two-gluon correlation approximation and the nuclear transverse momentum distribution can be expressed in terms of a transverse scale dependent quark transport parameter or gluon distribution density. We extend the study to hot medium and compare to dipole model approximation and ${\cal N}=4$ Supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory in the strong coupling limit. We find that multiple gluon correlations become important in the strongly coupled system such as ${\cal N}=4$ SYM plasma.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariant transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) quark distribution function in a nucleus can be expressed as a sum of all higher-twist collinear parton matrix elements via a transport operator, and under the maximal two-gluon correlation approximation, where higher-twist nuclear multiple-parton correlations with leading nuclear enhancement factorize into products of twist-two nucleon parton distributions, the nuclear transverse momentum distribution becomes a Gaussian convolution with the nucleon TMD quark distribution, with the broadening given by a path-integral of the quark transport parameter.

## Method

The authors start from the gauge-invariant definition of the TMD quark distribution function in a nucleus, which includes both longitudinal and transverse gauge links. They express this distribution in terms of a transport operator acting on the quark fields, then expand in powers of the operator and evaluate the nuclear matrix elements under the maximal two-gluon correlation approximation for a large, weakly bound nucleus. In this approximation, higher-twist nuclear multiple-parton correlations are reduced to products of twist-two nucleon gluon distribution functions, and the resulting nuclear TMD quark distribution is obtained as an exponential of the Laplacian in transverse momentum acting on the nucleon TMD distribution. The derivation is performed in light-cone gauge and then generalized to arbitrary gauge, and the study is extended to hot medium with comparison to the dipole model and N=4 SYM theory.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of jet transverse momentum broadening in nuclear matter, such as those by Baier et al. (1996) and Guo and Wang (1998), by providing a fully gauge-invariant derivation from the TMD quark distribution function. It also generalizes the recent calculation by Majumder and Müller (2007) of nuclear transverse momentum broadening in DIS, which used direct summation of twist diagrams in covariant gauge. The results enable a rigorous connection between the jet transport parameter ȓq_F and the gluon distribution density in the nuclear medium, and the extension to hot medium allows comparison with the dipole model approximation and with N=4 SYM theory in the strong coupling limit, where multiple gluon correlations become important.
