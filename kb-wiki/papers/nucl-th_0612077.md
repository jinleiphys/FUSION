# In-medium properties of D-mesons at FAIR

> We obtain the D-meson spectral density at finite temperature for the conditions of density and temperature expected at FAIR. We perform a self-consistent coupled-channel calculation taking, as a bare interaction, a separable potential model. The $Λ_c$ (2593) resonance is generated dynamically. We observe that the D-meson spectral density develops a sizeable width while the quasiparticle peak stays close to the free position. The consequences for the D-meson production at FAIR are discussed.

# In-medium properties of D-mesons at FAIR

## Key claim
The D-meson spectral density at finite temperature develops a considerable width while the quasiparticle peak stays close to its free position, contrary to previous expectations of a strong attractive mass shift. The broadening of the D-meson, rather than a mass shift, is identified as the dominant source of open-charm enhancement at FAIR.

## Method
A self-consistent coupled-channel calculation was performed at finite temperature using a separable potential model for the s-wave DN interaction, with parameters fitted to dynamically generate the Λc(2593) resonance. The in-medium DN interaction was solved including Pauli blocking on nucleons, Bose distributions for pions, and dressing of D-mesons, nucleons, and pions in the intermediate states. The D-meson self-energy and spectral density were obtained self-consistently for conditions of density and temperature expected at FAIR.

## Key numbers
- At T=0 and ρ0, the D-meson potential is -23 MeV when D-mesons and nucleons are dressed; for the full self-consistent calculation including pion dressing, it is -5 MeV at ρ0.
- At T=0 and 3ρ0, the D-meson potential is -76 MeV with nucleon dressing only, and -48 MeV with full self-consistent calculation.
- At T=120 MeV, the width increases from 52 MeV at ρ0 to 163 MeV at 3ρ0 for the first approach; from 36 MeV at ρ0 to 107 MeV at 3ρ0 for the second approach.
- The in-medium transition rates for D+n (D0p) at T=120 MeV show maximum enhancement reduced by a factor four as density increases from ρ0 to 3ρ0.
- Cross sections close to the DN threshold are expected on the order of 1 to 20 mb in the range of densities studied.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature coupled-channel calculations (Tolos 2004) to finite temperature conditions relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR. It contradicts earlier mean-field predictions from the quark-meson coupling model (QMC), QCD sum-rule calculations (Arata), and chiral models (Amruta) that claimed attractive mass shifts of -50 to -200 MeV at saturation density. The results are consistent with recent zero-temperature coupled-channel calculations (Lutz 2005, Angels) that also find reduced attraction. The findings imply that D-meson production at FAIR should be modeled using off-shell transport with in-medium cross sections rather than assuming a strong mass shift.
