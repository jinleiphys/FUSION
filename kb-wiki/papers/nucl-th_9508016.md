# Distillation of Strangelets for low initial mu/T

> We calculate the evolution of quark-gluon-plasma droplets during the hadronization in a thermodynamical model. It is speculated that cooling as well as strangeness enrichment allow for the formation of strangelets even at very high initial entropy per baryon $S/A^{\rm init}\approx 500$ and low initial baryon numbers of $A_{\rm B}^{\rm init}\approx 30$. It is shown that the droplet with vanishing initial chemical potential of strange quarks and a very moderate chemical potential of up/down quarks immediately charges up with strangeness. Baryon densities of $\approx 2ρ_0$ and strange chemical potentials of $μ_s>350$~MeV are reached if strangelets are stable. The importance of net--baryon and net--strangeness fluctuations for the possible strangelet formation at RHIC and LHC is emphasized.

## Key claim

The authors argue that strangelets (metastable strange quark matter droplets) can form from quark-gluon plasma even at the very high entropy per baryon (S/A ≈ 500) and low baryon number (A_B ≈ 30) expected at RHIC and LHC collider energies, provided that local fluctuations in net baryon and net strangeness numbers create suitable initial conditions, a possibility previously dismissed because average baryon densities at midrapidity were thought to vanish.

## Method

The authors model the hadronization of a quark-gluon plasma droplet using a two-phase thermodynamic equilibrium description (bag model for the QGP, relativistic Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac gases for hadrons) combined with a non-equilibrium freeze-out of hadrons from the outer layer of the hadron phase. They solve coupled differential equations for baryon number, entropy, and net strangeness of the total system alongside Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions for temperature and chemical potentials as functions of time, for various initial conditions and bag constants.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions explored: entropy per baryon S/A^init = 50, 100, 200, and 500; initial baryon number A_B^init = 30; initial strangeness fraction f_s^init = 0 or 0.7
- For bag constant B^{1/4} = 160 MeV and S/A^init = 200 with f_s^init = 0: initial quark chemical potential μ_q^i = 16.36 MeV, final μ_q^f > 200 MeV; strange chemical potential rises from μ_s^i = 0 to μ_s^f ≈ 400 MeV
- Final baryon density reached: ≈ 0.35 fm^{-3} (approximately 2ρ_0, where ρ_0 is ground state nuclear matter density) for B^{1/4} = 160 MeV across multiple initial conditions
- Final strange chemical potential μ_s^final > 350 MeV (specifically 385.8–396.9 MeV) for B^{1/4} = 160 MeV cases
- For B^{1/4} = 160 MeV, S/A^init = 200, f_s^init = 0.7: final baryon number A_B^final = 3.37; for B^{1/4} = 145 MeV under same conditions: A_B^final = 9.33
- For B^{1/4} ≥ 180 MeV, the droplet completely hadronizes (A_B^final = 0)
- Estimated probability: 0.5% of events have net baryon number |B| > 30; about 0.1% of events reach |B| > 30 with strangeness fraction |f_s| > 0.7
- Estimated quark number in a rapidity interval Δy ∼ 1: roughly 500 for RHIC, up to 2000 for LHC

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Greiner et al. (1987, 1992) and Lee and Heinz (1993) that addressed strangelet formation from baryon-rich QGP with moderate entropy per baryon, to the low baryon density, high entropy conditions expected at collider energies (RHIC and LHC). The authors emphasize that local fluctuations in net baryon and net strangeness, neglected in most prior calculations, can provide suitable initial conditions even when average midrapidity baryon densities vanish. The results enable experimental searches for strangelets at collider facilities, particularly within the ALICE experiment at LHC, and connect to broader questions about baryon inhomogeneities in the early universe and primordial nucleosynthesis as discussed by Witten (1984) and Applegate and Hogan (1985).
