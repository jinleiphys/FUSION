# Jet quenching in anisotropic flowing matter

> We study the interplay between the flow and hydrodynamic gradients in jet quenching at first order in opacity. We find that the mixed flow-gradient contributions in jet quenching are enhanced by the medium length, and survive in the eikonal limit, dominating over other medium evolution effects. The resulting modification to the jet quenching parameter and energy loss rate can be substantial, leading to ample phenomenological implications. We also compute the leading corrections to the jet broadening due to the flow velocity gradients, and consider the leading gradient effects in the medium-induced branching for general kinematics, extending the recent considerations of jets in inhomogeneous media. These results can be straightforwardly coupled to matter simulations, providing new opportunities for jet tomography in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The mixed flow-gradient contributions to jet quenching are enhanced by the medium length and survive in the eikonal limit, dominating over other medium evolution effects; the resulting modification to the jet quenching parameter and energy loss rate can be substantial, with the jet quenching parameter in flowing anisotropic matter taking the form $\hat{q}(z)=[1 - z\,\hat{\g} \cdot \frac{\u}{1-u_z}]\hat{q}_0(z)$, where the gradient operator $\hat{\g}$ acts on temperature, longitudinal velocity, and transverse velocity components.

## Method

The authors work within the opacity expansion at first order in opacity, extending the gradient expansion formalism developed in Sadofyev (2021) to include the interplay between flow and hydrodynamic gradients. They compute the single-Born and double-Born contributions to momentum broadening for a scalar quark interacting with a stochastic color field modeled by Gyulassy-Wang potentials, expanding the matter properties in transverse gradients about the parton trajectory. They also derive the leading gradient corrections to medium-induced gluon radiation for general kinematics, treating the emission of spin-1 gluons.

## Key numbers

* The jet quenching parameter in flowing anisotropic matter is given by $\hat{q}(z)=[1 - z\,\hat{\g} \cdot \frac{\u}{1-u_z}]\hat{q}_0(z)$, where $z$ is the path length of the energetic parton and $\u$ is the transverse velocity
* The single-source potential in the Gyulassy-Wang model is $v_i(q)=g^2/(q^2-\mu_i^2)$, where $\mu_i$ is the Debye mass at the position of the source
* The color averaging yields $\langle t^a_i t^b_j\rangle = \frac{1}{2C_{\bar R}}\delta_{ij}\delta^{ab}$, with $C_{\bar R}=N_c$ for sources in the fundamental representation
* The pole $Q^+_{p-q}$ is of order $\mathcal{O}(E)$, while the dominant pole $Q^-_{p-q}$ is given by $Q^-_{p-q} \simeq \frac{\q\cdot\u}{1-u_z} + \frac{(\p-\q)^2-\p^2}{2(1-u_z)E}$

## Context

This work extends the gradient expansion approach of Sadofyev (2021) for jet quenching in evolving media, which previously considered flow and anisotropy effects separately, by computing the mixed flow-gradient contributions that arise when both are present simultaneously. It goes beyond the momentum broadening discussion in Sadofyev (2021) and Barata (2022) by including all hydrodynamic gradients at first order, and extends the medium-induced radiation results available in Sadofyev (2021) and Barata (2023) to general kinematics. The results can be coupled to matter simulations, enabling new jet tomography opportunities in heavy-ion collisions.
