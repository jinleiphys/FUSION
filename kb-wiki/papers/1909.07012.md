# Constraining the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C astrophysical S-factors with the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C measurements at very low energies

> We use an underground counting lab with an extremely low background to perform an activity measurement for the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C system with energies down to $E\rm_{c.m.}$=2.323 MeV, at which the $^{12}$C($^{13}$C,$p$)$^{24}$Na cross section is found to be 0.22(7) nb. The $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C fusion cross section is derived with a statistical model calibrated using experimental data. Our new result of the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C fusion cross section is the first decisive evidence in the carbon isotope systems which rules out the existence of the astrophysical S-factor maximum predicted by the phenomenological hindrance model, while confirming the rising trend of the S-factor towards lower energies predicted by other models, such as CC-M3Y+Rep, DC-TDHF, KNS, SPP and ESW. After normalizing the model predictions with our data, a more reliable upper limit is established for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion cross sections at stellar energies.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first decisive experimental evidence in carbon isotope systems that rules out the existence of an astrophysical S-factor maximum predicted by the phenomenological hindrance model for $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C, while confirming the rising trend of the S-factor towards lower energies predicted by other models; after normalizing these models with the new data, a more reliable upper limit is established for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion cross sections at stellar energies.

## Method

The $^{12}$C($^{13}$C,$p$)$^{24}$Na cross section was measured by irradiating a natural carbon target with a $^{13}$C beam from the 3 MV Tandem accelerator at IFIN-HH, then transporting the activated samples to an underground counting lab in the SLANIC salt mine, Romania for offline gamma-ray detection of the $^{24}$Na decay (1369 keV line) using a well-shielded HPGe detector. The total $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C fusion cross section was derived from the measured proton channel cross section using a statistical model (Talys and Empire) calibrated against experimental branching ratios from prior work. The resulting S-factors were compared to predictions from the hindrance model, CC-M3Y+Rep, DC-TDHF, SPP, KNS, and ESW models.

## Key numbers

- $^{12}$C($^{13}$C,$p$)$^{24}$Na cross section at $E_{\rm c.m.}=2.323$ MeV: $0.22(7)$ nb.
- $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C total fusion cross section at the lowest measured energy: $0.9$ nb with uncertainty $\le 30\%$.
- The hindrance model predicted a S-factor maximum for $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C at $E_{\rm c.m.}=3.45\pm0.37$ MeV; the data show a plateau instead of a maximum in the range 3 to 4 MeV.
- At $E_{\rm c.m.}<3$ MeV, the three global models (CC-M3Y+Rep, DC-TDHF, SPP) are higher than the data by up to 35%, 55%, and 55% respectively; after normalization, their predictions at $E_{\rm c.m.}=1$ MeV agree within $\pm12\%$.
- The reduced-$\chi^2$ of the hindrance model fit to the new data below 3 MeV is 3.05; at the three lowest energies, the deviation between data and hindrance prediction ranges from 2.3 to 2.5$\sigma$.
- The reduced-$\chi^2$ of the ESW model fit to all $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C data is 0.69.

## Context

This work extends the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C fusion measurements of Notani et al. (2012) down to cross sections of 0.22 nb, more than an order of magnitude lower than previous measurements. The results contradict the hindrance model of Jiang et al. (2007, 2018) and Back et al. (2014), which predicted a S-factor maximum for carbon isotopes, and instead support the rising trend predicted by CC-M3Y+Rep (Esbensen 2011), DC-TDHF (Umar 2012), SPP (Gasques 2007), KNS (Aguilera 2006), and ESW models. The new upper limit for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion rules out the greatly suppressed rates from the hindrance model that would affect type Ia supernova ignition and superburst models, and is significantly lower than the Trojan Horse Method result of Tumino et al. (2018), indicating a need for further direct measurements below $E_{\rm c.m.}=2.7$ MeV.
