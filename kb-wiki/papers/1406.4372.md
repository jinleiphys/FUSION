# Does neutron transfer with positive Q-values enhance sub-barrier fusion cross section?

> [Background] Significant enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections owing to neutron rearrangement with positive $Q$-values were found for many combinations of colliding nuclei. However several experimental results on fusion reactions were reported recently in which such enhancement has not been observed in spite of a possibility for neutron rearrangement with positive $Q$-values. [Purpose] We aim to clarify much better the mechanism of neutron rearrangement in sub-barrier fusion reactions to find the other requirements (beside positive $Q$-value) which favour (or prevent) sub-barrier fusion enhancement. [Method] Channel coupling approach along with the semi-classical model for neutron transfer have been used for analysis of available experimental data. [Results] (1) Only 1n and 2n transfers with positive $Q$-values have a noticeable impact on sub-barrier fusion. Positive $Q$-value for neutron rearrangement is necessary but not sufficient requirement for additional sub-barrier fusion enhancement takes place. (2) "Rigidity" of colliding nuclei in respect of collective excitations is important that the sub-barrier fusion enhancement due to neutron rearrangement with positive $Q$-value be clearly visible. (3) Neutron binding energy in "donor" nucleus has a strong impact only in the case of fusion of light weakly bound nuclei.

## Key claim

Positive Q-value for neutron rearrangement is necessary but not sufficient for sub-barrier fusion enhancement; the effect is clearly visible only when the colliding nuclei are "rigid" with respect to collective excitations, and for light weakly bound nuclei the neutron binding energy in the donor nucleus also plays a strong role.

## Method

The authors used the empirical coupled channel (ECC) approach combined with a semiclassical model for neutron transfer probabilities to analyze available experimental fusion cross section data. The model averages the barrier penetration probability over dynamic surface deformations and/or orientations, and includes up to four neutron transfer channels via a modified Hill-Wheeler formula that accounts for the energy gain from positive Q-value transfers. Calculations were performed using the NRV Fusion code with Woods-Saxon potentials fitted to reproduce experimental data for nearest projectile-target combinations.

## Key numbers

* For $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr: $Q_{1n} = +0.51$ MeV, $Q_{2n} = +5.53$ MeV; barrier height $B_{sph} = 99.0$ MeV at $R_B = 10.8$ fm with $\hbar\omega_B = 3.90$ MeV
* For $^{58}$Ni+$^{130}$Te: $Q_{1n} = +0.58$ MeV, $Q_{2n} = +5.89$ MeV; barrier height $B_{sph} = 169.0$ MeV at $R_B = 11.5$ fm with $\hbar\omega_B = 3.64$ MeV
* For $^{60}$Ni+$^{100}$Mo: $Q_{2n} = +4.20$ MeV; barrier height $B_{sph} = 142.9$ MeV at $R_B = 11.0$ fm with $\hbar\omega_B = 3.88$ MeV
* For $^{18}$O+$^{74}$Ge: $Q_{2n} = +3.74$ MeV; barrier height $B_{sph} = 35.6$ MeV at $R_B = 9.7$ fm with $\hbar\omega_B = 3.78$ MeV
* For $^{6}$He+$^{64}$Zn: $Q_{1n} = 6.11$ MeV, $Q_{2n} = 18.06$ MeV; enhancement reaches about one order of magnitude at 1 MeV below the Coulomb barrier
* For $^{4}$He+$^{64}$Zn: barrier height $B_{sph} = 9.7$ MeV at $R_B = 8.2$ fm with $\hbar\omega_B = 4.26$ MeV
* For $^{6}$He+$^{64}$Zn: barrier height $B_{sph} = 9.4$ MeV at $R_B = 8.4$ fm with $\hbar\omega_B = 3.43$ MeV
* For $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm: barrier heights for limit orientations $B_1 = 53.6$ MeV and $B_2 = 60.7$ MeV; $B_{sph} = 59.5$ MeV at $R_B = 11.2$ fm with $\hbar\omega_B = 3.99$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical model of Zagrebaev (2003) for neutron rearrangement in sub-barrier fusion, which successfully described enhancement in systems like $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr and $^{18}$O+$^{58}$Ni. The authors address recent contradictory experimental results from $^{58,64}$Ni+$^{130}$Te (PRL 2011), $^{16,18}$O+$^{76,74}$Ge (PRC 2012), and $^{60,64}$Ni+$^{100}$Mo (Fusion_Ni_Mo) where no enhancement was observed despite positive Q-values. The analysis clarifies that collective excitation properties (surface vibrations and rotations) can mask or suppress the transfer-induced enhancement, and identifies specific combinations where the effect should be experimentally observable.
