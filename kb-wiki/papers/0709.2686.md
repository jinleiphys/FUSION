# A Stochastic Mean-Field Approach For Nuclear Dynamics

> We propose a microscopic stochastic approach to improve description of nuclear dynamics beyond the mean-field approximation at low energies. It is shown that, for small amplitude fluctuations, the proposed model gives a result for the dispersion of a one-body observable that is identical to the result obtained previously through a variational approach. Furthermore, by projecting the proposed stochastic mean-field evolution on a collective path, a generalized Langevin equation is derived for collective variable, which incorporate one-body dissipation and one-body fluctuation mechanism in accordance with quantal fluctuation-dissipation relation.

# A Stochastic Mean-Field Approach For Nuclear Dynamics

## Key claim
The paper proposes a stochastic mean-field approach that incorporates initial correlations as Gaussian random fluctuations in the single-particle density matrix, yielding a description of nuclear dynamics beyond the mean-field approximation at low energies (around 10 MeV per nucleon). The model is shown to reproduce the dispersion of one-body observables from a previous variational approach only for small amplitude fluctuations, and derives a generalized Langevin equation for collective variables that satisfies the quantal fluctuation-dissipation relation.

## Method
The author introduces a stochastic Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) framework where the initial single-particle density matrix elements are treated as Gaussian random numbers with mean values given by occupation numbers and variances determined by the Fermi-Dirac distribution. For each stochastic event, the time-dependent single-particle wave functions evolve under their own self-consistent mean-field Hamiltonian according to the TDHF equation. The approach is then linearized around the average evolution to obtain the time-dependent RPA equation for small fluctuations, and projected onto a collective path to derive a generalized Langevin equation for a collective variable.

## Key numbers
- The mean-field approximation is stated to be valid at sufficiently low energies around 10 MeV per nucleon, where two-body dissipation does not play an important role.
- The variance of initial density matrix fluctuations is given by: $\overline{\delta\rho^{\lambda}_{ij}(t_0) \delta\rho^{\lambda}_{j'i'}(t_0)} = \frac{1}{2} \delta_{ii'} \delta_{jj'}[n_i(1-n_j) + n_j(1-n_i)]$.
- The correlation time of the coupling matrix elements is defined as $\tau_c = \hbar / \Delta$, where $\Delta$ is the energy range of the form factor.
- The friction coefficient $\gamma(\Omega)$ is expressed as a sum over states involving matrix elements $|<\Psi_k|\partial h/\partial q|\Psi_j>|^2$ and occupation factors $\rho_k(1-\rho_j)$, with a Lorentzian energy dependence parameterized by $\eta$.
- The autocorrelation function of the stochastic force satisfies the quantum fluctuation-dissipation relation: $\overline{\xi(t) \xi(t_1)} = \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \frac{d\omega}{2\pi} e^{-i\omega(t-t_1)} \hbar\omega \coth(\hbar\omega/2T) \gamma(\omega)$.
- At high temperature limit ($T \gg \Delta$), the autocorrelation reduces to the classical result: $\overline{\xi(t) \xi(t_1)} \approx 2T \gamma_0 \delta(t-t_1)$, where $\gamma_0 = \gamma(0)$.

## Context
This work extends the mean-field description of nuclear dynamics by incorporating one-body fluctuation mechanisms, building on prior stochastic transport theories that addressed two-body dissipation and fluctuation at intermediate energies (Ayik and Gregoire, Randrup, Abe). The approach is contrasted with the variational method of Balian (1970s) for computing dispersions of one-body observables, and with the linear response treatment of Hofmann (1970s) for collective motion. The derived generalized Langevin equation enables microscopic calculations of fluctuation dynamics in low-energy nuclear collisions, such as induced fission, and is applicable to other fields including electron dynamics in atomic and condensed matter physics.
