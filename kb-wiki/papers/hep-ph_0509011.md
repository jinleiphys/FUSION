# Target mass effects in deep-inelastic scattering on the deuteron

> We calculate the effects of the target mass on deep-inelastic electron scattering on targets of spin one such as the deuteron, focusing on the novel structure functions that enter. It is important to understand these effects given the recent HERMES measurements of the $b_1(x,Q^2)$ structure function of the deuteron. We also derive the spin-one analogues of the Wandzura-Wilczek relations and discuss possible calculations in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

Target mass corrections (TMCs) to the spin-one structure functions of the deuteron are significant at the relatively low momentum transfers of the HERMES measurements (where 0.75 < M_d^2/Q^2 < 6.9) and must be included in any extraction of the underlying twist-two parton distributions, though the author notes that without extensive studies of QCD and higher-twist effects, a reliable extraction remains difficult.

## Method

The author applies the Georgi-Politzer formalism for target mass corrections to the operator product expansion of the forward Compton amplitude for spin-one targets, using the representation theory of the Lorentz group to treat trace subtractions exactly. The matrix elements of twist-two operators are parameterized by coefficients a_n, d_n, and r_n, where the novel d_n terms encode the spin-one structure. The author then performs inverse Mellin transforms to obtain target mass corrected expressions for all structure functions (F_1, F_2, g_1, g_2, b_1, b_2, b_3, b_4) in terms of the Nachtmann variable ξ and the scaling variable r.

## Key numbers

- HERMES measurements of b_1d(x,Q^2) cover the range 0.01 < x < 0.45 and 0.5 GeV^2 < Q^2 < 5 GeV^2
- The ratio M_d^2/Q^2 for the HERMES data lies between 0.75 and 6.9
- The deuteron mass used is M_d = 1.876 GeV; the proton mass used is M_p = 0.938 GeV
- For the b_1d structure function, two parameterizations are fit to data: Fit (I) with A_I = 0.00159, b_I = -1.828; Fit (II) with A_II = 0.0586, b_II = -1.532, c_II = 2.86, d_II = -4.32
- Target mass corrected structure functions are shown at Q^2 = 1, 3, and 10 GeV^2 in the figures

## Context

This work extends the target mass correction formalism of Nachtmann (1973) and Georgi and Politzer (1976) from spin-half targets to spin-one targets such as the deuteron, focusing on the novel b_i structure functions first formalized by Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1988). The calculations are motivated by the first measurements of b_1d by the HERMES collaboration (Airapetian, 2005). The author derives spin-one analogues of the Wandzura-Wilczek relations (Wandzura, 1977) connecting moments of b_2, b_3, and b_4, and discusses how lattice QCD calculations of twist-two operator matrix elements (as explored for the rho-meson by Best, 1997) could enable extraction of deuteron parton distributions.
