# Bound on the variation in the fine structure constant implied by Oklo data

> Dynamical models of dark energy can imply that the fine structure constant $α$ varies over cosmological time scales. Data on shifts in resonance energies $E_r$ from the Oklo natural fission reactor have been used to place restrictive bounds on the change in $α$ over the last 1.8 billion years. We review the uncertainties in these analyses, focussing on corrections to the standard estimate of $k_α\!=\!α\,dE_r/dα$ due to Damour and Dyson. Guided, in part, by the best practice for assessing systematic errors in theoretical estimates spelt out by Dobaczewski et al. [in J. Phys. G: Nucl. Part. Phys. 41, 074001 (2014)], we compute these corrections in a variety of models tuned to reproduce existing nuclear data. Although the net correction is uncertain to within a factor of 2 or 3, it constitutes at most no more than 25% of the Damour-Dyson estimate of $k_α$. Making similar allowances for the uncertainties in the modeling of the operation of the Oklo reactors, we conclude that the relative change in $α$ since the Oklo reactors were last active (redshift $z\simeq 0.14$) is less than $\sim 10$ parts per billion. To illustrate the utility of this bound at low-$z$, we consider its implications for the string theory-inspired runaway dilaton model of Damour, Piazza and Veneziano.

# Bound on the variation in the fine structure constant implied by Oklo data

## Key claim
The relative change in the fine structure constant α since the Oklo natural fission reactors were last active (redshift z ≈ 0.14) is less than about 10 parts per billion, but this bound depends on corrections to the standard Damour-Dyson estimate of k_α that are uncertain to within a factor of 2 or 3, though they constitute at most 25% of that estimate.

## Method
The authors compute nuclear physics corrections to the Damour-Dyson estimate of k_α = α dE_r/dα, which relates shifts in the resonance energy of neutron capture on 149Sm to changes in α. They use deformed Fermi profiles fitted to Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations and include the increase in surface diffuseness with excitation energy, evaluating excitation, external Coulomb, and deformation corrections across four different models of nuclear charge densities. The corrected bound on k_α is then combined with the measured resonance energy shift Δ_r = 7.2 ± 9.4 meV from Oklo data to derive a statistical upper limit on Δα/α.

## Key numbers
- The preferred Damour-Dyson estimate k_DD is −2.51 ± 0.20 MeV, compared to the original Damour and Dyson value of −1.1 ± 0.1 MeV (the difference arises from using a physically realistic nuclear radius of 6.50 ± 0.01 fm instead of 8.11 fm).
- The net correction k_corr = 0.33 ± 0.16 MeV, leading to a corrected bound k_Bd = k_DD + k_corr = −2.18 ± 0.26 MeV.
- The resonance energy shift from Oklo Sm data is Δ_r = 7.2 ± 9.4 meV (adopted from PhysRevC.74.024607).
- The 95% confidence level upper bound on the relative change in α is |Δα|/α_now < 1.1 × 10^(−8).
- The bound on the dilaton field speed in the runaway dilaton model is |Φ_0′| ≲ 0.03.

## Context
This work revisits the foundational method of Damour and Dyson (Nucl. Phys. B 480, 37) for relating Oklo data to variations in the fine structure constant, following best practices for systematic error assessment outlined by Dobaczewski et al. (J. Phys. G 41, 074001, 2014). The analysis extends earlier Oklo studies by Shlyakhter (Nature 264, 340) and subsequent independent analyses, and demonstrates that the revised Damour-Dyson estimate remains reliable for order-of-magnitude purposes. The resulting bound on Δα/α is applied to the string theory-inspired runaway dilaton model of Damour, Piazza and Veneziano (Phys. Rev. D 66, 046007), showing that for redshifts z < 5 the predicted behavior of α in that model becomes almost indistinguishable from ΛCDM.
