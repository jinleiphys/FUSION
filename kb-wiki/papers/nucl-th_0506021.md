# Medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> We use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model as an effective quark theory to investigate the medium modifications of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors. By using the equation of state of nuclear matter derived in this model, we discuss the results based on the naive quark-scalar diquark picture, the effects of finite diquark size, and the meson cloud around the constituent quarks. We apply this description to the longitudinal response function for quasielastic electron scattering. RPA correlations, based on the nucleon-nucleon interaction derived in the same model, are also taken into account in the calculation of the response function.

## Key claim

The paper uses the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective quark theory to investigate medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, but the authors explicitly note that this is only a first step toward a realistic description because axial vector diquarks (important for spin-dependent quantities) and the pion cloud (important for magnetic moments and nucleon size) are not fully included.

## Method

The nucleon is described as a quark-scalar diquark bound state using the static approximation to the relativistic Faddeev equation in the NJL model, with nuclear matter treated in the mean field approximation. The electromagnetic current is evaluated from Feynman diagrams including quark and diquark contributions, with on-shell approximations for the quark and diquark electromagnetic vertices. The model incorporates effects of finite diquark size, pion cloud around constituent quarks, and vector meson dominance corrections, with RPA correlations included for the response function calculation.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass at zero density: \(M_0 = 0.4\) GeV
- Free nucleon mass: \(M_{N0} = 0.94\) GeV
- Pion mass at zero density: \(M_{\pi} = 0.14\) GeV; pion decay constant: \(f_{\pi} = 93\) MeV
- Coupling constant ratio \(G_s/G_{\pi} = 0.508\) (determined to reproduce free nucleon mass)
- Coupling constant ratio \(G_{\omega}/G_{\pi} = 0.37\) (determined so binding energy curve passes through empirical saturation point at \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(E_B/A = 15\) MeV)
- Coupling constant ratio \(G_{\rho}/G_{\pi} = 0.091\) (determined to reproduce symmetry energy coefficient \(a_4 = 35\) MeV at \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\))
- Infrared cutoff: \(\Lambda_{\rm IR} = 0.2\) GeV; ultraviolet cutoff: \(\Lambda_{\rm UV} = 0.6385\) GeV
- At saturation density \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\): nucleon mass \(M_N = 0.707\) GeV, quark mass \(M = 0.308\) GeV, diquark mass \(M_s = 0.413\) GeV, pion mass \(M_{\pi} = 0.159\) GeV
- Quark wave function renormalization factor: \(Z_Q = 0.808\) at \(\rho=0\), increasing to \(0.869\) at \(\rho=0.24\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the NJL model approach previously applied to nucleon and nuclear structure functions for deep inelastic scattering (Bentz and Thomas, 2001; Mineo, Bentz, and Yazaki, 1999) to the calculation of electromagnetic form factors in the nuclear medium. The model builds on earlier Faddeev-type descriptions of the free nucleon using Schwinger-Dyson methods and provides a basis for future inclusion of axial vector diquarks and pion cloud effects, which were already incorporated for structure functions by Mineo, Bentz, and Yazaki (2004). The results enable calculations of the longitudinal response function for quasielastic electron scattering, including RPA correlations based on the nucleon-nucleon interaction derived in the same model.
