# Impact of shell model interactions on WIMP-nucleus scattering observables for silicon and germanium targets

> The nature of dark matter (DM) remains one of the biggest mysteries in physics today. Dark matter direct detection experiments look for nuclear recoil signals from DM-nucleus elastic scattering, which can be used to characterise DM. Nuclear modelling of the target nucleus may impact the predicted DM-nucleus scattering rates, and affect interpretation of experimental signals. In this work, we investigate the impact of nuclear shell model interactions on DM nuclear responses for silicon and germanium targets using a SuperCDMS-like experimental parameters. Nuclear uncertainties resulting from shell model interaction choice in the nuclear form factors are roughly retained at the scattering rate and exclusion limit levels for certain nuclear responses.

## Key claim

Nuclear uncertainties from shell model interaction choice in the nuclear form factors are roughly retained at the scattering rate and exclusion limit levels for certain nuclear responses, but only for specific non-relativistic operators; the degree of propagation depends on the target nucleus and the operator channel considered.

## Method

Large-scale nuclear shell model calculations were performed using NuShellX for silicon isotopes (28,29,30Si) in the full sd model space with USD and USDB interactions, and for germanium isotopes (70,72,73,74,76Ge) in the full f5pg9 valence space with JUN45 and jj44b interactions, compared against the GCN2850 interaction from Fitzpatrick et al. (2012, 2013). The nuclear form factors were computed within the non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) framework, and scattering rates and exclusion limits were evaluated using SuperCDMS-like detector parameters with the standard halo model (SHM) for the dark matter velocity distribution.

## Key numbers

- For germanium, the largest scattering rate differences due to shell model interaction choice exist between GCN2850 and jj44b calculations for the O3NR and O12NR operators, with a factor of approximately 2 uncertainty.
- For germanium exclusion limits, a factor of approximately 2 uncertainty is present for O3NR and a factor of approximately 2.5 for O12NR between GCN2850 and jj44b.
- For silicon, the maximum nuclear difference in the rates for O4NR and O10NR operators is of order approximately 70 percent, larger than the neutron IFF silicon SD channel uncertainties of order 30-40 percent from AbdelKhaleq et al. (2023).
- Silicon exclusion limits for O4NR and O10NR display a nuclear difference of order 75-80 percent.
- All rates were plotted for m_chi = 10 GeV/c^2 and sigma_p = 10^{-40} cm^2.
- SuperCDMS-like parameters include: silicon detector mass 2.4 kg, germanium detector mass 11.12 kg; flat background 0.1 cpd/kg/keV for silicon and 0.003 cpd/kg/keV for germanium; energy resolution parameters C = 5 x 10^{-3}, D = 0.7 eV, sigma_E(Si) = 5 eV, sigma_E(Ge) = 10 eV; 85 percent flat efficiency; 5-year run time.

## Context

This work extends the NREFT framework of Fitzpatrick et al. (2012, 2013) and Anand et al. (2013) by quantifying how nuclear shell model interaction choice propagates to experimental observables for silicon and germanium targets, using SuperCDMS-like parameters. It builds on the form factor calculations of AbdelKhaleq et al. (2023) by demonstrating that the nuclear uncertainties identified at the form factor level are roughly retained in scattering rates and exclusion limits, particularly for spin-dependent (SD) and longitudinal spin-dependent (LSD) channels. The results enable more accurate interpretation of future SuperCDMS signals by showing which nuclear responses require improved nuclear modeling to reduce systematic uncertainties in dark matter searches.
