# Hydrodynamics near the QCD Phase Transition: Looking for the Longest-Lived Fireball

> We propose a new strategy for the experimental search of the QCD phase transition in heavy ion collisions: One may tune collision energy around the point where the lifetime of the fireball is expected to be longest. We demonstrate that the hydrodynamic evolution of excited nuclear matter does change dramatically as the initial energy density goes through the "softest point" (where the pressure to energy density ratio reaches its minimum). For our choice of equation of state, this corresponds to epsilon_i approx. = 1.5 GeV/fm^3 and collision energy E_lab/A approx. = 30 GeV (for Au+Au). Various observables seem to show distinct changes near the softest point.

# Hydrodynamics near the QCD Phase Transition: Looking for the Longest-Lived Fireball

## Key claim
The hydrodynamic evolution of excited nuclear matter changes dramatically when the initial energy density passes through the "softest point" (minimum of the pressure-to-energy-density ratio), and tuning the collision energy to this point should produce the longest-lived fireball, with a lifetime increase by a factor of 2.5 in a narrow energy interval. The authors hedge that this result depends on their specific choice of equation of state and that more sophisticated models would modify the exact numbers, but they assert the qualitative feature of a lifetime maximum should persist.

## Method
The authors performed 2+1 dimensional ideal (non-viscous, baryon-free) relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using a Lax finite-difference scheme, with a smoothed equation of state interpolating between a virial hadron gas (speed of sound squared 0.19) and a quark-gluon plasma with bag constant 0.32 GeV/fm^3, and a phase transition temperature of 160 MeV. Initial conditions assumed a uniform disk with Lorentz-contracted longitudinal thickness, zero initial velocity, and total energy equal to half of sqrt(s), for central Au+Au collisions at energies from 10 GeV/A to 200 GeV/A.

## Key numbers
- The "softest point" occurs at initial energy density epsilon_i ≈ 1.5 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to collision energy E_lab/A ≈ 30 GeV for Au+Au.
- The maximal lifetime of the mixed phase at the center (z, r_t = 0) reaches τ_M ≈ 25 fm/c at the softest point.
- The lifetime increases by a factor of 2.5 in a narrow interval of collision energies around the softest point.
- The phase transition occurs in a narrow temperature interval ΔT ∼ 5 MeV << T_c, with T_c = 160 MeV.
- The energy density changes by about an order of magnitude across the mixed phase region, from epsilon_min to epsilon_max.

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions by Shuryak and collaborators (1980s) that near the QCD phase transition the equation of state becomes especially soft, reducing transverse expansion compared to an ideal pion gas. The authors propose a new experimental strategy of scanning collision energies downward from the nominal SPS energy (200 GeV/A) using existing detectors (WA80, CERES) to locate the softest point via dilepton and photon production measurements. This approach contrasts with the alternative strategy of reaching very high energies at RHIC and LHC to produce quark-gluon plasma well above the critical region.
