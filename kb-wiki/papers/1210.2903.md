# S wave bottomonium states moving in a quark-gluon plasma from lattice NRQCD

> We extend our study of bottomonium spectral functions in the quark-gluon plasma to nonzero momentum. We use lattice QCD simulations with two flavours of light quark on highly anisotropic lattices and treat the bottom quark with nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD). We focus on S wave (Upsilon and eta_b) channels and consider nonrelativistic velocities, v/c < 0.2. A comparison with predictions from effective field theory is made.

# S wave bottomonium states moving in a quark-gluon plasma from lattice NRQCD

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of bottomonium S wave correlators and spectral functions in the quark-gluon plasma is effectively temperature independent for nonrelativistic velocities (v/c < 0.2), with the ground state peak position increasing linearly with v^2 and the width showing no significant velocity dependence within the precision of the lattice calculation.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavours of dynamical light Wilson-type quarks on highly anisotropic lattices (a_s/a_tau = 6, a_s ~ 0.162 fm, a_tau^{-1} = 7.35 GeV), treating the bottom quark with O(v^4) improved nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD). They computed Euclidean correlation functions at seven nonzero momenta up to |p| = 1.73 GeV (corresponding to v ~ 0.18) across seven temperatures from 0.42 T_c to 2.09 T_c, and reconstructed spectral functions using the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract the momentum-dependent position and width of the ground state peak.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures studied: 90, 230, 263, 306, 368, 408, and 458 MeV, corresponding to T/T_c = 0.42, 1.05, 1.20, 1.40, 1.68, 1.86, and 2.09 (T_c accuracy ~10%)
- Momenta used: |p| = 0.634, 0.900, 1.10, 1.23, 1.38, 1.52, and 1.73 GeV, giving velocities v = 0.067 to 0.183 for both Upsilon and eta_b ground states
- Ground state masses used: M_Upsilon = 9.460 GeV, M_eta_b = 9.438 GeV (from Aarts (2010))
- The double ratio [G(tau,p;T)/G(tau,0;T)]/[G(tau,p;T_0)/G(tau,0;T_0)] shows remaining momentum dependence less than 0.5% at the largest Euclidean time
- The width Gamma/T shows no significant velocity dependence; the EFT prediction for the velocity-dependent width gives Gamma_v/Gamma_0 = 1 - 2v^2/3 + O(v^4), which is a percent-level correction for v^2 < 0.04

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice NRQCD studies of bottomonium at zero momentum (Aarts (2011)) to nonzero momentum, where they had found that S wave ground states survive up to 2.09 T_c while P wave states melt quickly above T_c (Aarts (2010)). The results provide a lattice QCD benchmark for comparison with effective field theory predictions for moving quarkonia in a thermal medium, particularly the velocity-dependent width calculations of Escobedo (2011) and the complex potential approach of Laine (2006). The temperature independence of the momentum dependence offers a clear prediction that can be contrasted with potential model calculations and EFT approaches, and contributes to interpreting CMS sequential Upsilon suppression measurements in PbPb collisions at the LHC.
