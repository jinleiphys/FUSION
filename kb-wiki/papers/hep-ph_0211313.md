# Jet and Dijet Rates in pA Collisions

> Jet and dijet rates in pA collisions at LHC energies are computed using a NLO Monte Carlo code. Uncertainties due to the choice of scale, jet-finding algorithm, nucleon parton densities and nuclear modifications of nucleon parton densities are analyzed. Predictions for different pA collisions at LHC energies are presented.

## Key claim

Jet and dijet rates in pA collisions at LHC energies can be reliably computed using NLO QCD, but the predictions carry significant theoretical uncertainties from scale choice (up to ±10%), jet algorithm choice (up to 15%), and nuclear PDF modifications (small but non-negligible for pseudorapidity distributions at reduced energies). The authors caution that several physical effects not included in their computations, such as the underlying event, multiple hard parton collisions, and rescatterings, may complicate comparison with experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the NLO Monte Carlo code of the subtraction method (based on the work of the original code developers) adapted to include isospin effects and nuclear modifications of nucleon parton densities via the EKS98 parameterization. They computed jet and dijet cross sections for pp, pAr, and pPb collisions at various LHC energies, using MRST98 central gluon as default nucleon PDF, a factorization/renormalization scale μ = E_T/2, and the k_T-clustering algorithm with D=1 for jet reconstruction. The kinematical acceptance was set to |η_i| < 2.5 (CMS central detector) with jet transverse energy thresholds E_Ti > 20 GeV for single-jet distributions and E_T1 > 20 GeV, E_T2 > 15 GeV for dijet angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at 14 TeV with luminosity 10^34 cm^-2 s^-1: 10^10 jets with E_Ti ~ 70 GeV (cross section 1 μb/(AB)) and 10^6 jets with E_Ti ~ 380 GeV (cross section 10^-4 μb/(AB)) expected per month within |η_i| < 2.5.
- For pPb at 8.8 TeV, 10^3 jets correspond to a cross section of 4.8 × 10^-5 μb/(AB), cutting the solid curve at E_Ti ~ 325 GeV.
- Scale variation between μ = E_T/4 and μ = E_T gives differences of order ±10% in jet cross sections.
- Cone algorithm with R=1 gives results ~15% larger than the default k_T-clustering algorithm with D=1; cone with R=0.7 gives results ~1-2% smaller.
- The K-factor (NLO/LO ratio) for default options is around 1.2, varying between ~1.15 (μ = E_T/4) and ~1.35 (μ = E_T).
- For dijet angular distributions, scale variation can make results up to ~50% smaller for μ = E_T compared to μ = E_T/4; jet algorithm choice produces differences of order 20%.
- Nuclear PDF modifications (EKS98) produce an extra asymmetry in pseudorapidity distributions for pPb at 5.5 TeV, but this asymmetry disappears at 8.8 TeV.
- Multiple hard scattering from different nucleon-nucleon collisions is negligible: the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions involved is 1.0 for all E_T0 values (20, 100, 200 GeV) in pPb at 8.8 TeV.

## Context

This work extends NLO QCD jet calculations, previously tested at the TeVatron (CDF collaboration), to the nuclear environment of pA collisions at LHC energies. It provides the first systematic NLO study of theoretical uncertainties for jet and dijet observables in pA collisions, addressing issues raised by the EMC effect (EMC collaboration, 1983) and parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter. The results enable future experimental studies at the LHC (particularly with the CMS detector) to disentangle nuclear PDF modifications from other cold-nuclear-matter effects, and serve as a baseline for identifying possible new physics (such as quark-gluon plasma formation) in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The authors note that full event simulators incorporating NLO QCD are needed before definitive comparisons with data can be made.
