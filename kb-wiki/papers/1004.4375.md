# Accessibility of color superconducting quark matter phases in heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss a hybrid equation of state (EoS) that fulfills constraints for mass-radius relationships and cooling of compact stars. The quark matter EoS is obtained from a Polyakov-loop Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with color superconductivity, and the hadronic one from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with density-dependent couplings (DD-RMF). For the construction of the phase transition regions we employ here for simplicity a Maxwell construction. We present the phase diagram for symmetric matter which exhibits two remarkable features: (1) a "nose"-like structure of the hadronic-to-quark matter phase border with an increase of the critical density at temperatures below T ~ 150 MeV and (2) a high critical temperature for the border of the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase, T_c > 160 MeV. We show the trajectories of heavy-ion collisions in the plane of excitation energy vs. baryon density calculated using the UrQMD code and conjecture that for incident energies of 4 ... 8 A GeV as provided, e.g., by the Nuclotron-M at JINR Dubna or by lowest energies at the future heavy-ion collision experiments CBM@FAIR and NICA@JINR, the color superconducting quark matter phase becomes accessible.

# Accessibility of color superconducting quark matter phases in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that color superconducting quark matter (the 2SC phase) becomes accessible in heavy-ion collisions at incident energies of 4–8 A GeV, as provided by the Nuclotron-M at JINR Dubna or by the lowest energies at future experiments CBM@FAIR and NICA@JINR, based on trajectories from UrQMD simulations overlaid on a model phase diagram constructed using a Maxwell construction for the phase transition.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid equation of state combining a Polyakov-loop Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with color superconductivity for quark matter and a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model for hadronic matter, with phase transitions built using a Maxwell construction. They calculated the phase diagram for symmetric matter and then overlaid trajectories of central heavy-ion collisions at various energies from UrQMD simulations onto the phase diagram to determine which regions of the phase diagram are accessible.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the border of the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase is \(T_c > 160\) MeV.
- The hadronic-to-quark matter phase border exhibits a "nose"-like structure with an increase of the critical density at temperatures below \(T \sim 150\) MeV.
- The saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), and at low temperatures the chiral restoration transition occurs at densities of order \(2-3\, n_0\).
- The incident energy range for accessing the 2SC phase is \(4 < E < 8\) A GeV, with energies beyond 10 A GeV required to reach the QCD critical point.
- The Polyakov-loop meanfield in the accessible region is \(\Phi < 0.25\), indicating a quarkyonic phase.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the QCD phase diagram using NJL models with Polyakov-loop coupling (Roessner 2006, Gomez Dumm 2008) that showed increased critical temperatures for the 2SC phase, and builds on the suggestion by Andronic (2009) that the "horn" in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio may indicate an approximate triple point in the QCD phase diagram. The results suggest that the Nuclotron-M and future NICA and CBM experiments can explore color superconducting and quarkyonic matter phases, complementing the energy scan program of NA49 and planned studies at NA61-SHINE and low-energy RHIC. The authors propose experimental signatures including an enhancement of the two-photon invariant mass spectrum around \(M_{2\gamma} \sim 300\) MeV from sigma meson decay as a signal of chiral restoration, and lepton-pair enhancements as precursors to color superconductivity.
