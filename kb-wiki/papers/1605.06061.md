# Momentum transport in strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas in the presence of strong magnetic fields

> We present a holographic perspective on momentum transport in strongly coupled, anisotropic non-Abelian plasmas in the presence of strong magnetic fields. We compute the anisotropic heavy quark drag forces and Langevin diffusion coefficients and also the anisotropic shear viscosities for two different holographic models, namely, a top-down deformation of strongly coupled $\mathcal{N} = 4$ Super-Yang-Mills (SYM) theory triggered by an external Abelian magnetic field, and a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) model which is able to provide a quantitative description of lattice QCD thermodynamics with $(2+1)$-flavors at both zero and nonzero magnetic fields. We find that, in general, energy loss and momentum diffusion through strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas are enhanced by a magnetic field being larger in transverse directions than in the direction parallel to the magnetic field. Moreover, the anisotropic shear viscosity coefficient is smaller in the direction of the magnetic field than in the plane perpendicular to the field, which indicates that strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas become closer to the perfect fluid limit along the magnetic field. We also present, in the context of the EMD model, holographic predictions for the entropy density and the crossover critical temperature in a wider region of the $(T,B)$ phase diagram that has not yet been covered by lattice simulations. Our results for the transport coefficients in the phenomenologically realistic magnetic EMD model could be readily used as inputs in numerical codes for magnetohydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas under strong magnetic fields, energy loss and momentum diffusion are enhanced and are larger in directions transverse to the magnetic field than parallel to it, while the shear viscosity is smaller along the field direction; these results are derived from two holographic models, with the bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) model providing predictions that could be used in magnetohydrodynamics codes, though the quantitative agreement with lattice QCD is limited to magnetic fields up to about eB ~ 0.3 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors used holographic gauge/gravity duality to compute anisotropic heavy quark drag forces, Langevin diffusion coefficients, and shear viscosities in two models: a top-down magnetic brane deformation of N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory triggered by an external Abelian magnetic field, and a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) model whose free parameters were fixed by matching to (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero magnetic field. For the EMD model, the equations of motion were solved numerically from the horizon to the boundary to obtain the background geometry, and then the transport coefficients were extracted using standard holographic formulas for string worldsheet fluctuations and metric perturbations.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for the strongly coupled QGP near crossover is approximately eta/s ~ 0.095 from hydrodynamic simulations (Ryu et al., 2015), which is close to the holographic bound eta/s = 1/(4pi).
- Magnetic fields at RHIC reach eB ~ 0.02 GeV^2 and at LHC reach eB ~ 0.3 GeV^2; magnetars produce fields up to eB ~ 1 MeV^2, while the primordial Universe may have had fields up to eB ~ 4 GeV^2.
- In the magnetic brane model, the drag force for motion perpendicular to the magnetic field shows a linear dependence on B/T^2 in the infrared limit (B/T^2 >> 1), while the parallel drag force approaches a constant in that limit.
- The EMD model predictions for the equation of state at finite B show reasonable agreement with lattice data for magnetic fields up to eB ~ 0.3 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of anisotropic momentum transport in plasmas with an external magnetic field, going beyond the analytical strong-field limit (B/T^2 >> 1) studied by Li et al. (2016) and providing full numerical results for arbitrary B/T^2. It also updates the EMD model of Rougemont et al. (2015) by using more recent lattice QCD data from Borsanyi et al. (2013) to fix parameters, enabling predictions for transport coefficients in a phenomenologically realistic QCD-like holographic dual that could be used as inputs for magnetohydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions.
