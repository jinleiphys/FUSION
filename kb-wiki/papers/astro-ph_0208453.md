# Multipair contributions to the spin response of nuclear matter

> We analyse the effect of non-central forces on the magnetic susceptibility of degenerate Fermi systems. These include the presence of contributions from transitions to states containing more than one quasiparticle-quasihole pair, which cannot be calculated within the framework of Landau Fermi-liquid theory, and renormalization of the quasiparticle magnetic moment, as well as explicit non-central contributions to the quasiparticle interaction. Consequently, the relationship between the Landau parameters and the magnetic susceptibility for Fermi systems with non-central forces is considerably more complicated than for systems with central forces. We use sum-rule arguments to place a lower bound on the contribution to the static susceptibility coming from transitions to multipair states.

## Key claim

Non-central nuclear forces (tensor and spin-orbit) introduce contributions to the static magnetic susceptibility from multipair excitations that cannot be calculated within Landau Fermi-liquid theory, and sum-rule arguments place a lower bound on these contributions, though quantitative estimates remain uncertain due to difficulties in calculating long-wavelength structure factors.

## Method

The authors analyze the spin-density response function for degenerate Fermi systems with non-central forces, using sum-rule arguments to derive a lower bound on the multipair contribution to the static susceptibility. They expand the quasiparticle interaction in Legendre polynomials, include the tensor form of the interaction (parametrized by a function \(k\) rather than the earlier \(h\) parametrization), and apply the inequality \(\chi(q, 0) \ge 2n S(q)/\bar{\omega}\) in the \(q \rightarrow 0\) limit to bound the multipair part. Numerical estimates are obtained using static structure factor and mean excitation energy values from Akmal et al. (1998) and susceptibility from Fantoni et al. (2001).

## Key numbers

- At saturation density \(n = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for pure neutrons, the static structure factor at \(q=0\) is \(S_{\mathrm{M}}(q=0) \approx 0.19\ \mu_0^2\) and the mean excitation energy is \(\bar{\omega}(q=0) \approx 63\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from Akmal et al. 1998).
- The total susceptibility from Fantoni et al. (2001) is \(\chi \approx 0.38\ \chi_{\mathrm{F}}\), where \(\chi_{\mathrm{F}} = 3n\mu_0^2 / 2\epsilon_{\mathrm{F}}\) and \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{F}} \approx 59\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at this density.
- The sum-rule bound gives \(\chi_{\mathrm{M}}(q,0)/\chi \ge 0.63\), implying multipair states could contribute more than about 60% of the total static response.
- Cowell and Pandharipande (2002) find magnetic moments are reduced by approximately 10% for nuclear matter with proton fractions 0.2–0.5 and densities 0.08–0.24 fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the Landau Fermi-liquid framework of Leggett (1965) and Pines and Nozieres (1966) to systems with non-central forces, where spin is not conserved. It contrasts with earlier treatments by Haensel and Dabrowski (1975) and Haensel and Jerzak (1979) that neglected multipair contributions and magnetic moment renormalization. The results are relevant for calculating neutrino emission, absorption, and scattering in dense matter (e.g., Prakash 1994, Sawyer 1975, Iwamoto 1982), and the authors identify the need for improved long-wavelength spin structure factor calculations to make quantitative estimates reliable.
