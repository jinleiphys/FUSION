# Temperature dependence of the static quark diffusion coefficient

> The energy loss pattern of a low momentum heavy quark in a deconfined quark-gluon plasma can be understood in terms of a Langevin description. In thermal equilibrium, the motion can then be parametrized in terms of a single heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient kappa, which needs to be determined nonperturbatively. In this work, we study the temperature dependence of kappa for a static quark in a gluonic plasma, with a particular emphasis on the temperature range of interest for heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim
The paper finds that the static quark momentum diffusion coefficient κ_E/T^3 shows a mild decrease with increasing temperature in the range 1.2–3.5 T_c, but the authors caution that their χ^2 analysis treats the error band as a statistical 1σ band while the actual error band is dominated by systematic errors, so the standard 1σ interpretation should not be attributed to the quoted fit parameter errors.

## Method
The authors computed the color-electric field correlator G_EE(τ) for a gluonic plasma using lattice QCD with the Wilson gauge action and the multilevel algorithm, across temperatures from 1.2 to 3.5 T_c. They performed continuum extrapolations at T/T_c = 2, 2.5, and 3 using three lattice spacings each, then extracted κ_E by fitting the correlator to model spectral functions that combine a leading-order ultraviolet form with a linear infrared transport term.

## Key numbers
- κ_E/T^3 at T/T_c = 1.2: range 2.1–3.5
- κ_E/T^3 at T/T_c = 1.5: range 1.5–2.8
- κ_E/T^3 at T/T_c = 2.0: range 1.0–2.3
- κ_E/T^3 at T/T_c = 2.5: range 0.9–2.1
- κ_E/T^3 at T/T_c = 3.0: range 0.8–1.8
- κ_E/T^3 at T/T_c = 3.5: range 0.75–1.5
- For the spatial diffusion coefficient D_s of charm quarks, a linear fit D_s ∼ α + γ (T/T_c − 1) yields α = 3.61(30) and γ = 2.57(43)
- For bottom quarks, the same fit yields α = 3.99(35) and γ = 3.08(54)
- For static quarks, the fit yields α = 4.27(29) and γ = 3.60(33)

## Context
This work extends the temperature range studied in Banerjee et al. (2012) for the static quark diffusion coefficient in gluonic plasma, covering the full range relevant to heavy ion collision experiments (up to 3.5 T_c). It improves upon earlier lattice calculations by Francis et al. (2015) at 1.5 T_c and Brambilla et al. (2020) at higher temperatures, and agrees well with those results within uncertainties. The results enable phenomenological modeling of heavy quark thermalization in the quark-gluon plasma, and when combined with the 1/M correction from Banerjee et al. (2022), provide estimates of the spatial diffusion coefficients for charm and bottom quarks that can be compared with Bayesian analyses of experimental data such as Xu et al. (2018).
