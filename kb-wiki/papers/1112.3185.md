# On the extraction of the probability of two- and three-nucleon short range correlations in nuclei

> Recent experimental data on inclusive and exclusive lepton and hadron scattering off nuclei have renewed the interest in theoretical and experimental studies of Short Range Correlations (SRC), due to the relevant impact they may have not only on the structure of ordinary nuclei but on the structure of hadronic matter at high densities as well. One of the ultimate aim of these studies is the determination of the probability of two- and three-nucleon correlations in nuclei. To this end, we have studied the possibility to extract these probabilities from a novel analysis of inclusive A(e, e')X processes in terms of relativistic scaling variables which incorporate effects from two- and three-nucleon SRC, with a resulting scaling function strictly related to longitudinal momentum distributions; such an approach led to a satisfactory explanation of the cross section ratios recently found at JLab and interpreted as strong evidence of SRC in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the probability of two- and three-nucleon short range correlations (SRC) in nuclei can be extracted from inclusive A(e,e')X processes by using novel relativistic scaling variables (y2 and y3) that incorporate the average excitation energy of the residual (A-1)-nucleon system generated by SRC, rather than the traditional mean-field scaling variable y which sets that excitation energy to zero.

## Method

The author introduces scaling variables y2 and y3 by placing in the energy conservation law the momentum-dependent average excitation energies <E*(A-1)(k)>2NC and <E*(A-1)(k)>3NC, calculated from the nucleon spectral function. The inclusive cross section is then expressed as a sum of integrals over the momentum distributions generated by the mean field, two-nucleon correlations, and three-nucleon correlations, with the scaling function F^A(q,Y) becoming approximately equal to the longitudinal momentum distribution f^A(Y). The approach is tested by comparing the resulting scaling functions and cross section ratios with experimental data from JLab.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer range of the JLab data under analysis is 2.5 < Q^2 < 7.4 GeV^2.
- For the Deuteron, the minimum removal energy E_min = 2.22 MeV.
- The constants C^A used to scale the scaling functions to the Deuteron are C^4 = 2.7 for ^4He, C^12 = 4.0 for ^12C, and C^56 = 4.6 for ^56Fe.
- The scaling variable y2 (for 2NC) is defined only for x_Bj ≤ 2, while y3 (for 3NC) is defined only for x_Bj up to 3.
- The average Q^2 used for the calculation of scaling variables versus x_Bj for ^56Fe is <Q^2> = 2.8 (GeV/c)^2.
- The 2NC model reproduces the exact spectral function of ^3He in the removal energy range 50 < E < 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the traditional Y-scaling approach of Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators, which used the mean-field scaling variable y, by introducing new scaling variables that incorporate SRC effects as first proposed by Ciofi degli Atti and West (CW). The analysis provides a framework to explain the cross section ratios measured at JLab (Fomin, Egyian) that were interpreted as evidence of SRC, and enables the extraction of two- and three-nucleon SRC probabilities from inclusive electron scattering data. The approach also allows separation of mean-field, 2NC, and 3NC contributions to the nucleon momentum distribution, with calculations for additional nuclei reported as in progress.
