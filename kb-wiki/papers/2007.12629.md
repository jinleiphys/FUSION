# Two-neutron transfer reactions as a tool to study the interplay between shape coexistence and quantum phase transitions

> The goal of this study is to find an observable that could distinguish between both phenomena, shape coexistence and quantum phase transitions. The selected observable to be analyzed is the two-neutron transfer intensity between the 0+ states in the parent and daughter nuclei. The framework in which the study is done is the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), including its version with configuration mixing (IBM-CM). In order to generate the wave functions of the isotope chains of interest, needed for calculating transfer intensities, previous systematic studies with IBM and IBM-CM are taken without changing the parameters. Results for two-neutron transfer intensities are presented for Zr, Hg and Pt isotopic chains using IBM-CM and, moreover, the same is done for Zr, Pt and Sm isotopic chains using IBM with just a single configuration, i.e., without using configuration mixing. In the case of Zr, the two-neutron transfer intensities between the ground states provide a clear observable indicating that normal and intruder configurations coexist in the low-lying spectrum and that they cross at A=98->100, and this could allow to disentangle whether or not shape coexistence is inducing a given QPT. In the case of Pt, where shape coexistence is present and the regular and the intruder configurations cross for the ground state, there is almost no influence in the value of the two-neutron transfer, neither in the case of Hg where the ground state always has regular nature. For the Sm isotope chain that is one of the quantum phase transition paradigms, the value of the two-neutron transfer is strongly affected.

## Key claim

Two-neutron transfer intensities between 0⁺ states can distinguish shape coexistence from quantum phase transitions (QPTs), but the observable's behavior depends critically on whether the crossing of normal and intruder configurations occurs in the ground state and on the degree of configuration mixing; the distinction is clearest when the configurations cross sharply (as in Zr) rather than when they are strongly mixed (as in Pt) or never cross (as in Hg).

## Method

The authors used the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), including its configuration-mixing extension (IBM-CM), to calculate two-neutron transfer intensities between 0⁺ states in parent and daughter nuclei. Wave functions were taken from previous systematic IBM and IBM-CM studies of Zr, Hg, Pt, and Sm isotope chains without any parameter adjustment. The transfer operator was taken as the simplest possible form, proportional to the s-boson creation or annihilation operator, and intensities were computed for (t,p) reactions (and implicitly for (p,t) reactions) using the squared reduced matrix elements between initial and final 0⁺ states.

## Key numbers

*   For Zr isotopes using IBM-CM, the (t,p) transfer intensity from the ground state of the parent to the ground state of the daughter and to the first excited 0⁺ state cross at A=98, where the regular and intruder configurations also cross; at this point, I(0₁⁺(A) → 0₁⁺(A+2)) becomes smaller than I(0₁⁺(A) → 0₂⁺(A+2)).
*   For Hg isotopes using IBM-CM, the dominant (t,p) transfer intensity is always I(0₁⁺(A) → 0₁⁺(A+2)), with transfer to any other low-lying 0⁺ state being "very small" for all isotopes (no specific numerical values given for the small intensities).
*   For Pt isotopes using IBM-CM, the (t,p) transfer intensity I(0₁⁺(A) → 0₁⁺(A+2)) remains dominant throughout the chain, with only a "minor lowering" and a "modest increase" in transfer to the 0₂⁺ state around the crossing points; the mixing between regular and intruder configurations reaches approximately 50% in both the 0₁⁺ and 0₂⁺ states for many midshell isotopes.
*   For Zr isotopes using a single-configuration IBM, the (t,p) transfer intensity I(0₁⁺(A) → 0₁⁺(A+2)) shows a "certain dropping" and I(0₁⁺(A) → 0₂⁺(A+2)) shows an "associated rising" at A=100, but I(0₁⁺(A) → 0₁⁺(A+2)) remains larger than I(0₁⁺(A) → 0₂⁺(A+2)) for all isotopes, unlike the configuration-mixing case.
*   For Sm isotopes using a single-configuration IBM, the value of the two-neutron transfer is "strongly affected" (no specific numerical values given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends earlier schematic studies by Vitturi et al. (2018) that suggested differences in two-neutron transfer fragmentation between QPT and shape coexistence, and by Fossan et al. (2007) and Zhang et al. (2017) who first used two-neutron transfer to explore QPTs. The present paper applies the method to realistic calculations in four isotopic chains (Zr, Hg, Pt, Sm) using previously fitted IBM and IBM-CM Hamiltonians. The results show that the two-neutron transfer intensity can serve as a diagnostic tool: in Zr, the crossing of configurations at A=98→100 produces a clear signature in the transfer intensities, while in Pt the strong mixing conceals the crossing, and in Hg the configurations never cross. The approach enables future experimental tests of whether rapid structural changes in nuclei arise from shape coexistence, a QPT, or a QPT induced by shape coexistence.
