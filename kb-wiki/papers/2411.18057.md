# Impact of tensor forces on quasifission product yield distributions

> We employ the microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory to study the 48Ca+249Bk and 48Ti+238U systems, taking into account the dependence on orientation for deformed nuclei and full range of impact parameters. By analyzing fragment distributions of neutron and proton numbers, we assess the influence of different isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constants of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The quasifission yield distributions of 48Ca + 249Bk collision system utilizing SLy5t and T31 parametrizations exhibit more pronounced spherical shell effects compared to those using SLy5, T44 and T62 sets. Furthermore, within each parametrization group, the distributions for SLy5t and T31 are closely aligned, as are those for SLy5, T44, and T62. Similarly, the yield distributions for the 48Ti + 238U system using SLy5t and T31 also reflect the more pronounced spherical shell effects relative to SLy5 and T62, while the charge distribution shows much better agreement with experimental results for the SLy5t and T62 parametrizations compared to SLy5 and T31. The yield distributions for the 48Ca+249Bk and 48Ti+238U systems, when compared across the SLy5, SLy5t, T31, T44, and T62 parametrizations, indicate that the influence of tensor forces on quasifission fragments is reflected in the prominence of shell effects. This influence appears to be sensitive only in specific regions within the isoscalar and isovector coupling constant parameter space. In the 48Ti + 238U system, the prominence of shell effects is manifested not only through shifts in peak positions but also through narrower yield distributions.

## Key claim

Tensor forces influence quasifission fragment yield distributions primarily by altering the prominence of spherical versus deformed shell effects, but this influence is sensitive only in specific regions of the isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constant parameter space, rather than being a universal effect across all Skyrme parametrizations.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations for the 48Ca+249Bk system at Ec.m.=234 MeV and the 48Ti+238U system at Ec.m.=229.68 MeV, using five Skyrme parametrizations (SLy5, SLy5t, T31, T44, T62) with different isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constants. For each system, they varied the orientation angles of the deformed target nuclei (249Bk and 238U) at β=0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90° and scanned impact parameters from b=0 fm with 0.5 fm increments until quasielastic reactions occurred, then constructed fragment yield distributions from the TDHF outcomes.

## Key numbers

- 48Ca+249Bk system studied at center-of-mass energy Ec.m.=234 MeV
- 48Ti+238U system studied at center-of-mass energy Ec.m.=229.68 MeV
- T31 parametrization has isoscalar coupling constant C0J=0 MeV fm5 and isovector coupling constant C1J=-60 MeV fm5
- T62 parametrization has C0J=120 MeV fm5 and C1J=-120 MeV fm5
- T44 parametrization has C0J=120 MeV fm5 and C1J=0 MeV fm5
- Contact time threshold of 5 zs (zeptoseconds) identified for significant fragment accumulation near shell gaps
- Numerical box dimensions: 60×28×48 fm3; time step: 0.2 fm/c; grid spacing: 1.0 fm
- Initial separation of target and projectile: 26 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Li et al., Phys. Lett. B 833, 137349, 2022) which first identified tensor force effects on shell gap competition in quasifission for the 48Ca+249Bk system using only SLy5 and SLy5t forces. By incorporating the TIJ family of Skyrme parametrizations (Lesinski et al., 2007) and the 48Ti+238U system with experimental data from Morjean et al. (2017), the paper demonstrates that tensor effects on quasifission shell effects are confined to specific regions of the coupling constant parameter space. This enables future studies to identify which tensor coupling regimes most strongly influence superheavy element synthesis reactions.
