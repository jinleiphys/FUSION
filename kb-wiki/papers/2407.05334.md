# Pion photoproduction of nucleon excited states with Hamiltonian effective field theory

> We refine our previous calculation of multipole amplitude $E_{0+}$ for pion photoproduction process, $γN\rightarrowπN$. The treatment of final-state interactions is based upon an earlier analysis of pion-nucleon scattering within Hamiltonian effective field theory, supplemented by incorporating contributions from the $N^*(1650)$ and the $KΛ$ coupled channel. The contribution from the bare state corresponding to the $N^*(1650)$ significantly enhances our results. Additionally, we also compute the multipole amplitude $M_{1-}$, which is of direct relevance to the Roper resonance. The results are comparable with other dynamical coupled channel models, even though the contribution from the bare state (interpreted as a 2$s$ excitation) in this channel is small because of its large mass.

## Key claim

The authors refine their previous calculation of the multipole amplitude \(E_{0+}\) for pion photoproduction by incorporating contributions from the \(N^*(1650)\) resonance and the \(K\Lambda\) coupled channel, finding that the bare state corresponding to the \(N^*(1650)\) significantly enhances the results; for the \(M_{1-}\) amplitude relevant to the Roper resonance, the bare state contribution is small because of its large mass, and the results are comparable with other dynamical coupled channel models despite some model-dependent discrepancies.

## Method

The calculation splits the \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi N\) scattering T-matrix into electromagnetic transition amplitudes (derived from effective Lagrangians following the ANL-Osaka model) and final-state interactions (FSI) obtained from earlier Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT) analyses of pion-nucleon scattering. For the \(S_{11}\) partial wave, three coupled channels (\(\pi N\), \(\eta N\), \(K\Lambda\)) and two bare states mixing to form the \(N^*(1535)\) and \(N^*(1650)\) are included; for the \(P_{11}\) partial wave, three coupled channels (\(\pi N\), \(\sigma N\), \(\pi\Delta\)) and a bare state for the \(N^*(1440)\) are considered. The coupled channel equations are solved to obtain the T-matrix, and the multipole amplitudes \(E_{0+}\) and \(M_{1-}\) are extracted from the partial wave amplitudes.

## Key numbers

*   The dipole regulator cutoff for the \(S_{11}\) electromagnetic form factor is \(\Lambda_{-}^{\rm em} = 1.007^{+0.150}_{-0.150}\).
*   The fitted coupling constant for the proton target to the first bare state (\(N^*(1535)\)) is \(g_{p\gamma N^{*+}_1} = 1.049^{+0.019}_{-0.022}\); for the neutron target it is \(g_{n\gamma N^{*0}_1} = -0.349^{+0.011}_{-0.010}\).
*   The fitted coupling constant for the proton target to the second bare state (\(N^*(1650)\)) is \(g_{p\gamma N^{*+}_2} = 1.064^{+0.125}_{-0.095}\); for the neutron target it is \(g_{n\gamma N^{*0}_2} = -0.188^{+0.167}_{-0.198}\).
*   The exponential form factor cutoff for the \(P_{11}\) partial wave is \(\Lambda^{\rm em}_{+} = 1.942^{+0.150}_{-0.150}\) GeV.
*   The fitted coupling constant \(g_{\sigma NN}\) for the \(P_{11}\) partial wave is \(0.359^{+0.746}_{-0.000}\).
*   The bare state masses for the two low-lying odd-parity resonances are 1.6301 GeV and 1.8612 GeV, respectively.
*   The pion decay constant used is \(f_{\pi} = 92.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HEFT analysis of the \(N^*(1535)\) (Liu, 2015) and the \(N^*(1440)\) (Wu, 2017) by incorporating the \(N^*(1650)\) and the \(K\Lambda\) channel into the photoproduction calculation, building on a recent HEFT study that jointly described both low-lying odd-parity nucleon resonances (Abell, 2023). The results are compared with the ANL-Osaka model (Kamano, 2013), the Juelich-Bonn model (Ronchen, 2014), the Bonn-Gatchina model (Anisovich, 2011), and SAID partial wave analysis (Workman, 2012); the approach enables future connections between lattice QCD finite-volume spectra and experimental photoproduction observables through the HEFT framework.
