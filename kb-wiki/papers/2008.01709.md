# Hanbury Brown--Twiss Interferometry and Collectivity in Small Systems

> Hanbury Brown--Twiss interferometry (HBT) provides crucial insights into both the space-time structure and the momentum-space evolution of ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions at freeze-out. In particular, the dependence of the HBT radii on the transverse pair momentum $K_T$ and the system charged multiplicity $dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/dη$ may reflect the mechanisms driving collective behavior in small systems. This paper argues that certain features observed in the multiplicity dependence of the HBT radii can be naturally understood if small systems evolve hydrodynamically at high-multiplicity. This study thus establishes a baseline for the multiplicity dependence of HBT in hydrodynamics which may prove useful in discriminating between competing models of collectivity in nuclear collisions.

## Key claim
This paper argues that the non-universal slopes and slope hierarchy observed in the multiplicity dependence of HBT radii across different collision systems (pp, p-Pb, Pb-Pb) can be naturally understood if small systems evolve hydrodynamically at high multiplicities, though the simplified hydrodynamic model used limits the analysis to a qualitative rather than quantitative level.

## Method
The author used the 2+1D iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic package with specific viscosities η/s = 0.08 and ζ/s = 0, initialized at τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c using MC-Glauber initial conditions for pp, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions. Event-averaged initial conditions were generated in 10% centrality-class intervals, with overall normalization adjusted to match experimental benchmark values of dNch/dη. The hydrodynamic evolution was terminated at a freeze-out temperature of Tfo = 120 MeV, and HBT correlation functions were evaluated using Cooper-Frye integrals over the freeze-out surface, then fitted to extract the HBT radii as functions of transverse pair momentum KT and charged multiplicity.

## Key numbers
- Specific viscosities used: η/s = 0.08, ζ/s = 0
- Hydrodynamics initialized at proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: Tfo = 120 MeV
- Target multiplicities: pp at 7 TeV with dNch/dη = 6.0 (0-100% class); p-Pb at 5.02 TeV with dNch/dη = 17.5 (0-100% class); Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV with dNch/dη = 1601 (0-5% class)
- In AA collisions, the slope of Ro is approximately 0.7, while Rs and Rl have slopes of approximately 0.6
- In pp collisions, the Ro slope is approximately 0.3, Rl slope is approximately 0.3, and Rs slope is approximately 0.5

## Context
This work extends previous experimental analyses of HBT multiplicity dependence in pp (Aamodt 2011), p-Pb (Adam 2015), and Pb-Pb (Aamodt 2011) collisions, as well as STAR results for Au+Au (Adams 2004, Abelev 2009). The study builds on the observation by Heinz (2019) that initially more compact systems need to develop stronger transverse flow to reach the same final freeze-out volume. It contrasts with prior theoretical work by Hirono (2014) that explored KT-dependence but not multiplicity scaling, and by Graef (2012) and Li (2012) who studied multiplicity dependence using UrQMD rather than hydrodynamics. The results establish a hydrodynamic baseline for multiplicity dependence of HBT that may help discriminate between competing models of collectivity, including CGC-type models, escape mechanism models, and string hadronization approaches.
