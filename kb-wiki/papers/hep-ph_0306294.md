# On the resonant and non-resonant contributions to B\toρπ

> We discuss the importance of the background in order to understand the scattering in the J^{PC}=0^{++} low and intermediate energy region and in particular regarding the σmeson. In order to appreciate better its importance we compare with the ρmeson in the P-wave ππscattering. We also point out that in present analyses of three-body heavy meson decays, like those of D^+ and B, the role of this background is still not properly settled although it happens to be considerably smaller.

# On the resonant and non-resonant contributions to B→ρπ

## Key claim
The background (non-pole) contribution to the J^PC = 0^++ ππ scattering amplitude is as important as the σ meson pole contribution itself, completely distorting the σ pole shape; however, in B and D decays to 3π, the scalar form factor lacks chiral zeros, so the σ meson pole may dominate with only a slight background, explaining why the σ is clearly seen in D decays but not in ππ scattering.

## Method
The author performs a Laurent expansion of the ππ partial wave amplitudes f_0 (I=0 S-wave) and f_1 (P-wave) around the pole positions on the unphysical Riemann sheet, using partial waves calculated from the Chiral Unitary Approach. The pole term (γ_0^2/(s−s_P)) is isolated as the resonant contribution, while the remaining terms (γ_1, γ_2, etc.) constitute the background. The scalar ππ form factor Γ(s) from ref. [meisso] is also examined via Laurent expansion to compare the background size in production versus scattering processes.

## Key numbers
- σ meson pole position: s_σ = (0.466 − i 0.224)^2 GeV^2; ρ meson pole position: s_ρ = (0.758 − i 0.075)^2 GeV^2
- σ pole residue: γ_0^2 = 5.3 + i 7.7 GeV^2; ρ pole residue: γ_ρ^2 = −5.6 + i 2.6 GeV^2
- σππ coupling: g_σππ = 1.4 + i 2.7 GeV (phase 62.5°); ρππ coupling: g_ρππ = 2.4 − i 0.5 GeV (phase −12°)
- Background coefficient for σ: γ_1 = −8.1 + i 36.9; for ρ: γ_1 = −11.3 + i 1.7
- CLEO ratio: R = Br(B̅^0 → ρ^± π^∓) / Br(B^− → ρ^0 π^−) = 2.7 ± 1.2

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Deandrea (2003) and Gardner (2003) on the role of the σ meson in B → ρπ → 3π decays, which was motivated by the E791 Collaboration observation of the σ in D^+ → π^−π^+π^+. The author shows that the Breit-Wigner parameterization used by Deandrea (2003) and E791 fails to reproduce the ππ scattering data for the σ, as previously noted by Gardner (2003), who employed the parameterization of [meisso] that respects chiral symmetry and unitarity. The key advance is demonstrating quantitatively via Laurent expansion that the large background in ππ scattering arises from chiral zeros required by Goldstone boson nature, and that production processes like B and D decays are free of this requirement, enabling cleaner σ signals. This clarifies how to properly treat the σ contribution in future B → 3π Dalitz plot analyses for measuring the CKM angle α.
