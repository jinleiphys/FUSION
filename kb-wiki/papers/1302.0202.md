# Three-body force effect on off-shell mass operator and spectral functions in nuclear matter

> Within the framework of the Brueckner theory, the off-shell behaviors of the mass operator $M(k,ω)=V(k,ω)+iW(k,ω)$, i.e., its dependence upon the momentum $k$ and upon the nucleon frequency $ω$, are investigated by including nuclear three-body force (TBF). The first two terms of the hole-line expansion of the mass operator are taken into account. The TBF effects on their off-shell properties are discussed. A comparison is made between the on-shell and off-shell values of $M_{1}$. The nucleon spectral function and nucleon momentum distribution are also calculated, and the calculation shows that they are hardly affected by the TBF effect at the saturation density. At a high density two times greater than the saturation density, inclusion of the TBF may lead to a visible effect on the spectral function and may enhance the depletion of the hole states.

## Key claim

Within the Brueckner theory extended to include a microscopic three-body force (TBF), the TBF effect on the nucleon spectral function and momentum distribution is negligible at saturation density (0.17 fm⁻³) but becomes sizable at twice that density (0.34 fm⁻³), where it enhances the depletion of hole states; the TBF also significantly modifies the off-shell mass operator for nucleons with both low momentum and large frequency.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation using the Argonne V18 two-body interaction plus an effective two-body force derived from a microscopic meson-exchange TBF model (Grangé et al.), with self-consistent iteration. They computed the first two terms of the hole-line expansion of the mass operator: the BHF term M₁ and the Pauli rearrangement term M₂, both off-shell as functions of momentum k and frequency ω. The spectral function S(k,ω) was then obtained from the real and imaginary parts of the mass operator, and the momentum distribution n(k) was integrated from the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the depletion of the zero-momentum hole state is about 16.4%, consistent with previous predictions and experimental results.
- At ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³ (twice saturation density), inclusion of the TBF enhances the depletion of the zero-momentum state from about 17% to about 22%.
- The TBF effect on V₁(k,ω) and W₁(k,ω) was studied at ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³ for fixed frequencies ω = 20 MeV and ω = 160 MeV, with corresponding on-shell momenta approximately k = 2.15 fm⁻¹ and k = 3.15 fm⁻¹.
- The ω-dependence of V₁ and W₁ was calculated at ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³ for fixed momenta k = (3/4)k_F and k = (5/4)k_F.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Brueckner-Hartree-Fock study of the off-shell mass operator by Baldo et al. (1990, 1991) and the spectral function calculation by Frick et al. (2002), which used the Argonne V14 interaction without TBF. It also complements the in-medium T-matrix study by Soma et al. (2008), who used the Urbana TBF and found similarly small TBF effects at low densities. The results enable more accurate off-shell transport descriptions of heavy-ion reactions beyond the quasiparticle approximation, and provide microscopic input for dispersion-relation approaches to the nuclear mean field and for y-scaling analyses of inclusive electron scattering.
