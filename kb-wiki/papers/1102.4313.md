# Chemical and kinetic equilibrations via radiative parton transport

> A hot and dense partonic system can be produced in the early stage of a relativistic heavy ion collision. How it equilibrates is important for the extraction of Quark-Gluon Plasma properties. We study the chemical and kinetic equilibrations of the Quark-Gluon Plasma using a radiative transport model. Thermal and Color-Glass-Condensate motivated initial conditions are used. We observe that screened parton interactions always lead to partial pressure isotropization. Different initial pressure anisotropies result in the same asymptotic evolution. Comparison of evolutions with and without radiative processes shows that chemical equilibration interacts with kinetic equilibration and radiative processes can contribute significantly to pressure isotropization.

# Chemical and kinetic equilibrations via radiative parton transport

## Key claim
Radiative (2↔3) processes contribute significantly to pressure isotropization in a gluon plasma, and when they are included, the kinetic equilibration (pressure anisotropy evolution) depends mainly on the coupling constant and loses memory of the initial pressure anisotropy, whereas with only elastic collisions it depends on both the coupling constant and the initial energy density.

## Method
The authors simulate a system of gluons in a box and in an expanding central cell using a parton cascade model with medium-dependent 2→2 cross sections (regulated by a Debye screening mass) and 2↔3 radiative processes (with σ₂₃ taken as 50% of σ₂₂, following Xu and Greiner (2004)). The 3→2 rate is determined by detailed balance, and all outgoing particles are isotropic in the center-of-mass frame. Thermal (exponential) and Color-Glass-Condensate-motivated (step-function) initial momentum distributions are compared.

## Key numbers
- In the box simulation with 2000 gluons at initial temperature 1 GeV in a 5×5×5 fm³ volume, chemical equilibrium is reached at about 3 fm/c.
- At 3 fm/c, the 3→2 rate is about the same as the 2→3 rate and both are close to the equilibrium rate.
- The initial energy density and particle rapidity used for the heavy-ion collision simulations are about the same as those expected at RHIC.
- For the radiative case, the pressure anisotropy evolution shows an approximate αₛ scaling, qualitatively different from the exact αₛ ε₀ scaling observed in the elastic-only case.

## Context
This work extends parton cascade studies of thermalization (e.g., Xu and Greiner (2004), Zhang (2010)) by explicitly comparing evolutions with and without radiative processes and by examining the interplay between chemical and kinetic equilibration. It shows that radiative processes can significantly enhance thermalization in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and that the memory of initial pressure anisotropy is quickly lost, enabling simpler modeling of the asymptotic evolution. The results motivate further study of radiative effects on experimental observables.
