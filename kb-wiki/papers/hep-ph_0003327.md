# Diquark composites in the color superconducting phase of two flavor dense QCD

> We study the Bethe-Salpeter equations for spin zero diquark composites in the color superconducting phase of N_f=2 cold dense QCD. The explicit form of the spectrum of the diquarks, containing an infinite tower of narrow (at high density) resonances, is derived. It is argued that there are five pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons (pseudoscalars) that remain almost massless at large chemical potential. These five pseudoscalars, along with the massless quarks of the third color should play an important role in the infrared dynamics of N_f=2 dense QCD.

# Diquark composites in the color superconducting phase of two flavor dense QCD

## Key claim
The spectrum of spin-zero diquark bound states in the color superconducting phase of two-flavor dense QCD contains five nearly massless pseudoscalar pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons and an infinite tower of massive narrow resonances, but only in the hard dense loop improved ladder approximation and at asymptotically high chemical potential where the coupling is weak.

## Method
The authors derived and solved Bethe-Salpeter equations for diquark composites in the color superconducting phase of N_f=2 cold dense QCD, using the hard dense loop improved ladder approximation. They introduced Majorana spinors for the first two colors and Weyl spinors for the third color, constructed the quark propagators and gluon vertices, and solved the BS equations analytically by matching logarithmic derivatives of the wave functions in three momentum regions. The Ward identities were used to fix the wave functions of the massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons.

## Key numbers
- The mass formula for the massive singlet diquark states: M_n^2 ≃ 4|Δ|^2 (1 − α_s^2 κ / (2n+1)^4), where κ ≃ 0.27, n = 1,2,..., |Δ| is the dynamical Majorana mass of quarks, and α_s is the running coupling at the scale of the chemical potential μ.
- The ultraviolet cutoff in the BS equation is Λ = (4π)^(3/2) μ / α_s^(5/2).
- The binding energy of massive states scales as E_bind ∼ α_s^2 Δ.
- The heavy gluon mass scale is M_gl ∼ (α_s μ^2 Δ)^(1/3) ≫ Δ.

## Context
This work extends the earlier studies of color superconductivity in dense QCD by Pisarski and Rischke (1999), Son (1999), and the authors' own prior work (1999) on the gap equation, by providing the first explicit Bethe-Salpeter treatment of the diquark bound state spectrum. It builds on the observation by the authors (1999) that long-range magnetic gluon interactions could produce an infinite tower of diquark states. The results enable future studies of the infrared dynamics of two-flavor dense QCD, where the five pseudoscalar pseudo-NG bosons and the massless quarks of the third color are the relevant degrees of freedom, and the massive narrow resonances serve as a signature of unscreened long-range forces.
