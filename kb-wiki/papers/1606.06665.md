# $P$-wave coupled-channel scattering of $B_sπ,\,B_s^*π,\,B\overline K,\,B^{*}\overline K$ and the puzzling $X(5568)$

> The D0 Collaboration has recently reported a narrow peak structure in the $B_s^0π^\pm$ invariant-mass spectrum, that is called $X(5568)$. Its proposed quantum numbers $J^P=0^+/1^+$ are not determined unambiguously, and we show that one can fit the data assuming $J^P=1^-$ with the same mass and width for the resonance signal. The corresponding isovector $P$-wave coupled-channel scattering involving the states $B_sπ$, $B_s^*π$, $B \overline{K}$ and $B^*\overline K$ is studied employing leading-order Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory, which is then unitarized making use of standard techniques from Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory. The subtraction constants that appear in the unitarization process are determined by a $u$-channel crossing symmetry constraint or by naturalness arguments, being the numerical values obtained in both cases quite closed, and no further freedom is left in the model. A crucial point is that if the $X(5568)$ is $1^-$ and decaying into $B_s^0π^\pm$ then it should appear as a pole in the $T$-matrix elements. It finally turns out that no $X(5568)$ pole is found that can be qualified as dynamically generated from the isovector $P$-wave coupled-channel dynamics. This result disfavors the interpretation of the $X(5568)$ within a "molecular" picture.

## Key claim

The authors show that the D0 Collaboration's data on the $X(5568)$ peak can be fitted equally well assuming $J^P=1^-$ (vector) instead of the originally proposed $0^+/1^+$, but when they perform a full coupled-channel $P$-wave scattering calculation using Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory, no dynamically generated pole corresponding to the $X(5568)$ is found, disfavoring a molecular interpretation.

## Method

The authors studied isovector $P$-wave coupled-channel scattering involving the channels $B_s\pi$, $B_s^*\pi$, $B\overline K$, and $B^*\overline K$ using leading-order Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory (HMChPT). The tree-level amplitudes were projected into $P$-wave and then unitarized using the Bethe-Salpeter equation solved on-shell, with subtraction constants determined either by a $u$-channel crossing symmetry constraint or by naturalness arguments (three-momentum cutoff around 1 GeV). The authors searched for poles in the $T$-matrix on the unphysical Riemann sheet relevant for the $X(5568)$ mass region.

## Key numbers

- The $X(5568)$ mass and width from D0: $m_X = 5567.8 \pm 2.9^{+0.9}_{-1.9}$ MeV, $\Gamma_X = 21.9 \pm 6.4^{+5.0}_{-2.5}$ MeV
- Mass thresholds for the four channels: $B_s^0\pi^+$ at 5506.36 MeV, $B_s^*\pi^+$ at 5554.97 MeV, $B^+\overline K^0$ at 5772.29 MeV, $B^{*+}\overline K^0$ at 5818.10 MeV
- The $B_s^*\pi^+$ threshold is only about 10 MeV below the $X(5568)$ mass
- The coupling constant used: $g = 0.58 \pm 0.07$ (from $D^{*+}$ decay width)
- The pion decay constant: $f_\pi \approx 92.4$ MeV
- Subtraction constants determined from $u$-channel crossing symmetry are around $-4$ at renormalization scale $\mu = 770$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the $S$-wave coupled-channel study of $B_s\pi \to B\overline K$ by Swanson (2016) and Oset et al. (2016), which already disfavored a molecular interpretation for the $X(5568)$. The authors go beyond previous work by considering $P$-wave scattering (required for $J^P=1^-$) and including the $B_s^*\pi$ channel, whose threshold is very close to the $X(5568)$ mass. The result that no dynamically generated pole is found further disfavors the molecular picture, consistent with the LHCb non-observation of the state and with tetraquark model calculations by Chen et al. (2016) and Qiao et al. (2016) that found masses much higher than the experimental value.
