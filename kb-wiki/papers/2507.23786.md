# Scale-anomaly-induced binding pressure in hadrons

> The effect of the QCD scale anomaly on the internal pressure distribution of hadrons is studied based on the trace-traceless decomposition of the energy-momentum tensor. Using recent model-independent results of gravitational form factors as input, the pressure distributions of both pions and nucleons are analyzed in the instant form and the light-front form. It is found that, in all cases, the scale anomaly dominantly generates the negative binding pressure. This result suggests that the phenomenon is a universal feature, independent of models, types of hadrons, and the choice of form.

## Key claim

The QCD scale anomaly dominantly generates the negative binding pressure in both pions and nucleons, and this result appears to be a universal feature independent of the type of hadron, the specific model used for the gravitational form factors, and whether the analysis is performed in the instant form or the light-front form.

## Method

The authors performed a trace-traceless decomposition of the energy-momentum tensor to isolate the contribution of the scale anomaly (the trace part) from the traceless part. Using model-independent gravitational form factors for the pion and nucleon from Cao (2024), which were determined via dispersion relations, they computed the pressure distributions in both the instant form (three-dimensional Breit frame densities) and the light-front form (two-dimensional transverse densities). The form factors were extrapolated to the asymptotic momentum-transfer region using a tripole (m=3) multipole model, with the scale parameter Lambda determined by fitting to the form factor data.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass fixed at m_pi = 140 MeV for the pressure distribution calculations.
* Nucleon mass fixed at m_N = 940 MeV for the pressure distribution calculations.
* Forward limit values used: A^pi(0) = 1, A^N(0) = 1, J^N(0) = 1/2.
* D-term values at zero momentum transfer: D^pi(0) = -0.935, D^N(0) = -3.352.
* Multipole fit parameters (Lambda^2): A^pi(t) = 6.344 GeV^2, D^pi(t) = 2.593 GeV^2, A^N(t) = 3.213 GeV^2, J^N(t) = 3.394 GeV^2, D^N(t) = 0.767 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Fujii 2025, Tanaka 2025) that first observed the scale anomaly driving binding pressure in nucleons using a topological soliton model, and it also builds on the analysis of the massless pion in the light-front form (Fujii 2025). The use of model-independent gravitational form factors from Cao (2024) removes model dependence, and the simultaneous analysis in both instant and light-front forms addresses the known ambiguity in defining spatial densities for light hadrons (Miller 2018, Jaffe 2020). The results enable future investigations of the scale anomaly's role in other hadrons to distinguish universal confinement features from hadron-specific properties.
