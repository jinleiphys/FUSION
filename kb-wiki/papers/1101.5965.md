# Higher order proton structure corrections to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen

> The recent conundrum with the proton charge radius inspires reconsideration of the corrections that enter into determinations of the proton size. We study the two-photon proton-structure corrections, with special consideration of the non-pole subtraction term in the dispersion relation, and using fits to modern data to evaluate the energy contributions. We find that individual contributions change more than the total, and present results with error estimates.

## Key claim

The total two-photon proton-structure correction to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen is similar in magnitude to previous calculations, but individual contributions (subtraction, inelastic, elastic) change more than the total, with the main changes arising from using a larger magnetic polarizability and from a dispersive treatment that excludes elastic non-pole contributions.

## Method

The authors used dispersion relations to separate the two-photon exchange correction into elastic, inelastic, and subtraction terms, with the subtraction function evaluated at zero photon energy using a pion-loop model for its Q² dependence. The inelastic contributions were computed using fits to electron-proton scattering data from Christy and Bosted (2007) for the resonance region and Capella et al. (1994) for higher energies, while the elastic contributions used modern nucleon form factor parametrizations (Kelly, AMT, Mainz 2010).

## Key numbers

- Total two-photon correction: ΔE = -36.9 ± 2.4 μeV (this work), compared to -35.1 μeV from Pachucki (1996,1999) and -34.5 μeV from Martynenko (2005)
- Subtraction term: ΔE_subt = 5.3 ± 1.9 μeV (this work), scaling as 5.3 μeV × (β_M / (3.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm³))
- Inelastic contribution: ΔE_inel = -12.7 ± 0.5 μeV (this work), compared to -13.9 μeV (Pachucki) and -13.8 μeV (Martynenko)
- Elastic contribution: ΔE_el = -29.5 ± 1.3 μeV using AMT form factors, with values ranging from -27.8 μeV (Kelly) to -30.8 μeV (Mainz 2010)
- Magnetic polarizability values considered: β_M = (1.9 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (PDG), (4.0 ± 0.7) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, and (3.4 ± 1.2) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from recent analyses
- Half of the subtraction term contribution comes from Q² ≲ 0.04 GeV²

## Context

This work addresses the proton radius puzzle arising from the muonic hydrogen measurement by Pohl (2010), which gave R_E = 0.84184(67) fm, discrepant from the CODATA value of 0.8768(69) fm and the electron scattering value of 0.879(8) fm from Bernauer (2010). The paper extends the dispersive treatment of Pachucki (1996,1999) and Martynenko (2005) by using a pion-loop model for the subtraction function's Q² dependence rather than assuming a form factor falloff, and by properly separating elastic non-pole contributions. This enables improved error estimates for the two-photon correction, which is essential for extracting the proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements.
