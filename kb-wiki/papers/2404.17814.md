# Three-body analysis reveals the significant contribution of minor $^{5}$He $s$-wave component in $^{6}$Li$(p,2p)^{5}$He cross section

> $^6$Li is usually treated as an $α+p+n$ three-body system, and the validity of this picture is important for understanding $^6$Li reactions. The ($p$,$2p$) reaction is a powerful method to study the structure of valence nucleons in $^6$Li. Recently, the new experimental data of the $^{6}$Li($p$,$2p$)$^{5}$He reaction have been obtained and should be analyzed. We investigate the $^{6}$Li($p$,$2p$)$^{5}$He reaction using the $α+p+n$ three-body wave function of $^6$Li and study the validity of this model. We calculate the $^6$Li wave function by using Gaussian expansion method, and the function is used to obtain the relative wave function between $p$ and $^5$He. We combine the relative wave function with the distorted wave impulse approximation. Our results reproduce the experimental data of the triple differential cross section within about 10\% difference in the absolute values, and contributions from both $p$- and $s$-wave states of $^5$He in $^6$Li are found to be important. We can qualitatively understand the $^{6}$Li($p$,$2p$)$^{5}$He reaction by describing $^6$Li with the three-body model. Contribution from the $s$-wave component is important in reproducing the experimental data in the zero recoil-momentum region.

## Key claim

The three-body model of 6Li as an α+p+n system reproduces the experimental triple differential cross section of the 6Li(p,2p)5He reaction within about 10% difference in absolute values, but only when the minor s-wave component of 5He (with a probability of about 10% in 6Li) is included; the s-wave contribution is essential to fill the dip in the cross section at the recoilless condition where the recoil momentum of 5He is nearly zero.

## Method

The authors calculated the 6Li ground state wave function using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with the Minnesota interaction for p-n and the KKNN potential for nucleon-α interactions, plus a phenomenological three-body force. They then obtained the overlap function between 6Li and 5He and combined it with the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using the asymptotic momentum approximation. The quadruple differential cross section (QDX) and triple differential cross section (TDX) were computed for the 6Li(p,2p)5He reaction at 392 MeV, with the Franey-Love effective interaction for the p-p t-matrix and optical potentials from the Melbourne g matrix (for p-6Li) and the EDAD1 Dirac phenomenology (for p-5He).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 6Li: -3.77 MeV (calculated) vs. -3.6989 MeV (experimental)
- Root-mean-square radius of 6Li: 2.36 fm (calculated) vs. 2.44 ± 0.07 fm (experimental)
- Probability of 5He(s1/2) in 6Li: 0.102 (10.2%)
- Probability of 5He(p3/2) in 6Li: 0.551 (55.1%)
- Probability of 5He(p1/2) in 6Li: 0.275 (27.5%)
- Incident proton energy: 392 MeV
- Polar angles of emitted protons: 32.21° (particle 1) and 51.62° (particle 2)
- Recoilless condition: T1L ≈ 260 MeV
- Integration ranges for ε: (0.4, 1.8) MeV and (0.4, 14.8) MeV
- Three-body force parameters: V3 = -0.4 MeV, γ = 0.036 fm-2

## Context

This work extends the three-body description of 6Li previously used by Watanabe (2015, 2021) and others (Kukulin 1990, Hlophe 2017, Tursunov 2016) by applying it to the (p,2p) reaction. It directly tests the three-body model against the kinematically complete experimental data of Yoshida (2010) from RCNP, Osaka University, which had previously been analyzed only with a phenomenological single-particle model. The results enable future analysis of the 6Li(p,pd)α reaction using the CDCCIA method (Chazono 2022) to probe the α+d cluster structure of 6Li, and provide a benchmark for studying s-wave probabilities in other halo nuclei such as 11Li (Kubota 2020).
