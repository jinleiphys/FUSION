# Quantified Gamow Shell Model interaction for $psd$-shell nuclei

> The structure of weakly bound and unbound nuclei close to particle drip lines is one of the major science drivers of nuclear physics. A comprehensive understanding of these systems goes beyond the traditional configuration interactions approach formulated in the Hilbert space of localized states (nuclear shell model) and requires an open quantum system description. The complex-energy Gamow Shell Model (GSM) provides such a framework as it is capable of describing resonant and non-resonant many-body states on equal footing. To make reliable predictions, quality input is needed that allows for the full uncertainty quantification of theoretical results. In this study, we carry out the optimization of an effective GSM (one-body and two-body) interaction in the $psdf$ shell model space. The resulting interaction is expected to describe nuclei with $5 \leqslant A \leqslant 12$ at the $p-sd$-shell interface. The optimized one-body potential reproduces nucleon-$^4$He scattering phase shifts up to an excitation energy of 20 MeV. The two-body interaction built on top of the optimized one-body field is adjusted to the bound and unbound ground-state binding energies and selected excited states of the Helium, Lithium, and Beryllium isotopes up to $A=9$. A very good agreement with experiment was obtained for binding energies. First applications of the optimized interaction include predictions for two-nucleon correlation densities and excitation spectra of light nuclei with quantified uncertainties. The new interaction will enable comprehensive and fully quantified studies of structure and reactions aspects of nuclei from the $psd$ region of the nuclear chart.

## Key claim

The authors optimize a quantified Gamow Shell Model (GSM) interaction for light nuclei in the $psdf$ model space, but the resulting interaction is expected to describe nuclei with $5 \leqslant A \leqslant 12$ at the $p$-$sd$-shell interface, not the full $psdf$ space implied by the model space definition.

## Method

The one-body core-nucleon potential for the $^4$He core was modeled as a Woods-Saxon plus spin-orbit and Coulomb potential and optimized to reproduce experimental nucleon-$^4$He scattering phase shifts up to 20 MeV. The two-body interaction between valence nucleons, consisting of central, spin-orbit, tensor, and Coulomb terms based on the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki interaction (1979, 1980), was then optimized to bound and unbound ground-state binding energies and selected excited states of Helium, Lithium, and Beryllium isotopes up to $A=9$. The chi-square optimization employed the Gauss-Newton algorithm augmented by singular value decomposition, with statistical uncertainties evaluated via the covariance matrix within the linear regression approach.

## Key numbers

- Optimized neutron Woods-Saxon potential depth: $V_0 = 41.9(10)$ MeV; proton depth: $V_0 = 44.4(11)$ MeV
- Optimized spin-orbit strength: $V_{\ell s} = 7.2(2)$ MeV fm$^2$ for both neutrons and protons
- Optimized Woods-Saxon radius: $R_0 = 2.15(4)$ fm (neutrons), $R_0 = 2.06(4)$ fm (protons)
- Optimized diffuseness: $a = 0.63(2)$ fm (neutrons), $a = 0.64(2)$ fm (protons)
- Calculated $^5$He $3/2^-$ ground state energy: $E = 0.755$ MeV (experiment: 0.798 MeV), width: $\Gamma = 651$ keV (experiment: 648 keV)
- Calculated $^5$Li $3/2^-$ ground state energy: $E = 1.627$ MeV (experiment: 1.69 MeV), width: $\Gamma = 1351$ keV (experiment: 1230 keV)
- Calculated $^6$He $0^+$ ground state binding energy relative to $^4$He: $E = -1.063$ MeV (experiment: $-0.973$ MeV)
- Calculated $^6$Li $1^+$ ground state binding energy relative to $^4$He: $E = -3.724$ MeV (experiment: $-3.699$ MeV)
- Calculated $^8$Be $0^+$ ground state binding energy relative to $^4$He: $E = -28.572$ MeV (experiment: $-28.204$ MeV)
- Momentum cutoff for Berggren basis: $k_{\text{max}} = 2$ fm$^{-1}$
- Natural orbital basis achieved energy convergence within $\Delta E < 15$ keV using 5-7 states per partial wave instead of 30 Berggren basis states

## Context

This work extends the Gamow Shell Model framework (Michel et al., 2002-2004; Betan et al., 2002-2003) by providing the first quantified, uncertainty-controlled effective interaction for light nuclei at the $p$-$sd$-shell interface. The approach builds on the pioneering shell-model work of Lane and Kurath (1955, 1956) and the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki interaction (1979, 1980), while incorporating modern uncertainty quantification methods developed for nuclear density functional theory (Dobaczewski et al., 2014) and chiral interactions (Ekstrom et al., 2013). The optimized interaction enables comprehensive and fully quantified studies of structure and reaction aspects of nuclei from the $psd$ region of the nuclear chart, including predictions for two-nucleon correlation densities and excitation spectra with quantified uncertainties.
