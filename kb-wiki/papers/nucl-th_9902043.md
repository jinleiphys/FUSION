# Nuclear Phase Transitions in Transport Theory

> Questions concerning nuclear phase transitions are addressed within the transport theory. The issue of thermal equilibrium in the region of the liquid-gas phase transition is investigated. Signatures of the transition to the quark-gluon plasma are looked for.

# Nuclear Phase Transitions in Transport Theory

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether thermal equilibrium can be reached in nuclear reactions in the liquid-gas phase transition region and whether clear signals of the quark-gluon plasma transition can be observed, concluding that in the neck region of low-energy reactions the system is not near equilibrium and that elliptic flow measurements may detect the QGP transition at high density.

## Method

The author uses a relativistic Boltzmann transport model with scalar and vector potentials, including light clusters (A ≤ 3) whose production is handled via time-reversal invariance relating production to breakup cross sections. For the liquid-gas transition, simulations of Xe-Sn reactions at 55 MeV/nucleon are performed with isospin-dependent interactions and cluster formation. For the QGP transition, a quasiparticle model with density-dependent hadron masses is constructed, and Au + Au reaction simulations from 0.5 to 11 GeV/nucleon are carried out to compute elliptic flow excitation functions.

## Key numbers

- Mean free path in the neck region at density ρ ∼ ρ₀/3: λ ∼ 6 fm
- Distance from nuclei to center of neck: L ∼ 5 fm
- Characteristic time for both isospin and density adjustments in the neck: ∼30 fm
- Lattice QCD transition temperature for baryonless matter: T_c ∼ 170 MeV
- Pion density at transition: ρ_π ∼ 2 ρ₀
- At T=0, the QGP transition takes matter from baryon density ρ ≈ 3.5 ρ₀ to 7 ρ₀
- Mass modification factor parametrization: S = (1 − 0.54 (fm³/GeV) ρ_s)²
- Vector potential parameters: a = 146.32 MeV, b = 0.4733, c = 51.48
- Elliptic flow simulations cover beam energies: (0.5–11) GeV/nucleon
- Present data point to EOS stiffness variation in the region of ∼(2–3) GeV/nucleon, corresponding to baryon densities of ∼4 ρ₀

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of liquid-gas phase coexistence in asymmetric nuclear matter by Müller and Serot (1995), Barranco and Buchler (1980), and Glendenning (1992), and builds on experimental observations by Dempsey et al. (1996) of enhanced neutron-rich fragment yields in Xe-Sn reactions. The transport simulations by Sobotka et al. (1997) are used to test whether these enhancements reflect phase equilibrium. The QGP model follows the quasiparticle approach of Gosset (1998), and the elliptic flow calculations are compared to data from the EOS, E895, and E877 Collaborations (1999). The work enables future experimental searches for the QGP transition via the elliptic flow excitation function.
