# Origin of a maximum of astrophysical $S$ factor in heavy-ion fusion reactions at deep subbarrier energies

> The hindrance phenomenon of heavy-ion fusion cross sections at deep subbarrier energies often accompanies a maximum of an astrophysical $S$ factor at a threshold energy for fusion hindrance. We argue that this phenomenon can naturally be explained when the fusion excitation function is fitted with two potentials, with a larger (smaller) logarithmic slope at energies lower (higher) than the threshold energy. This analysis clearly suggests that the astrophysical $S$ factor provides a convenient tool to analyze the deep subbarrier hindrance phenomenon, even though the $S$ factor may have a strong energy dependence for heavy-ion systems unlike that for astrophysical reactions.

## Key claim

The maximum of the astrophysical $S$ factor in heavy-ion fusion reactions at deep subbarrier energies arises naturally from a change in the logarithmic slope of the fusion excitation function at the threshold energy for hindrance, rather than from any intrinsic property of the $S$ factor itself.

## Method

The authors fitted experimental fusion excitation functions for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni and $^{28}$Si+$^{64}$Ni systems using two separate Woods-Saxon potentials: one for the subbarrier region (cross sections between $10^{-2}$ and $10^0$ mb) and another for the deep subbarrier region (cross sections below $10^{-3}$ mb). They then computed the astrophysical $S$ factor and its first energy derivative, decomposing the derivative into a nuclear contribution (the logarithmic slope $L(E)$) and a Coulomb contribution ($\pi\eta/E$), to show how the cancellation between these two terms changes at the threshold energy.

## Key numbers

- For $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni: subbarrier potential parameters are $V_0 = 180$ MeV, $r_0 = 1.15$ fm, $a = 0.676$ fm; deep subbarrier parameters are $V_0 = 98.0$ MeV, $r_0 = 1.1$ fm, $a = 1.1$ fm.
- For $^{28}$Si+$^{64}$Ni: subbarrier potential parameters are $V_0 = 70.5$ MeV, $r_0 = 1.2$ fm, $a = 0.71$ fm; deep subbarrier parameters are $V_0 = 46.5$ MeV, $r_0 = 1.19$ fm, $a = 0.99$ fm.
- The astrophysical $S$ factor is scaled with $\eta_0 = 75.23$ for $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni and $\eta_0 = 41.25$ for $^{28}$Si+$^{64}$Ni, and is given in units of (mb MeV).
- The subbarrier region is defined as cross sections between $10^{-2}$ and $10^0$ mb; the deep subbarrier region is defined as cross sections below $10^{-3}$ mb.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of deep subbarrier fusion hindrance first observed by Jiang et al. (2002) for the $^{60}$Ni+$^{89}$Y system and subsequently found in many other systems. The two-potential fit method was introduced earlier by the same group (ELH12), and the present paper clarifies the origin of the $S$ factor maximum that had been used empirically by Jiang et al. (Back14) to define the threshold energy for hindrance. The analysis enables future microscopic calculations, such as those by Ichikawa et al. (2013) for vibrational excitations, to determine the amount of logarithmic slope change for each system.
