# Electromagnetic Form Factors and the hCQM

> We briefly report on results about the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon obtained with different models and then we concentrate our attention on recent results obtained with the hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM).

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM), with its three free parameters fixed to the baryon spectrum, reproduces the observed decrease of the proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio with $Q^2$ as a relativistic effect, but only when boosts to the Breit frame and a relativistic quark current are included; without these relativistic corrections the decrease disappears.

## Method

The authors used the hCQM, which employs a linear-plus-Coulomb hypercentral potential in the hyperradius $x$ (the collective coordinate of the three-quark system), with a perturbative hyperfine interaction to break SU(6) symmetry. They calculated elastic nucleon form factors and helicity amplitudes for electromagnetic transitions to negative-parity baryon resonances, applying boosts from the initial and final baryon rest frames to the Breit frame and using a relativistic quark current (first expanded to lowest order in quark momenta, then without expansion). The potential parameters were refitted with relativistic kinetic energy for the fully relativistic version.

## Key numbers

- Proton r.m.s. radius from the hCQM potential parameters: 0.48 fm
- Potential parameters from spectrum fit: $\alpha = 1.61\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$, $\tau = 4.59$ (dimensionless)
- Quark mass taken as $1/3$ of the nucleon mass
- For the Delta resonance, pion cloud contribution to transverse amplitudes $A_{1/2}$ and $A_{3/2}$ is about 50% at low $Q^2$
- In the isospin-dependent extension, hyperfine interaction contributes only about 35% to the $N-\Delta$ mass difference, spin-isospin term 50%, and isospin term 15%
- The original Iachello-Jackson-Lande VMD model (1973) showed the ratio $G_E/G_M$ crossing zero at about $8\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$
- The Frank et al. (1996) light-cone constituent quark model gave a zero in the proton electric form factor at $Q^2 = 6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$
- The Holzwarth (1996) Skyrme soliton model gives $G_E^p$ crossing zero at $10\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$
- The MIT Bag model expects a zero at $Q^2 = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$

## Context

This work extends the hCQM program, which had previously been applied to photocouplings, helicity amplitudes, and elastic form factors, by presenting a fully relativistic version with no expansion in quark momenta. The results are compared to the earlier nonrelativistic hCQM calculations by the same group (De Sanctis et al., 1999) and to other approaches including the VMD model of Iachello, Jackson and Lande (1973), the light-front constituent quark model of Cardarelli et al. (1995) and Frank et al. (1996), the chiral CQM of the Pavia-Graz group (Wagenbrunn et al., 2000; Boffi et al., 2001), the Skyrme soliton model of Holzwarth (1996), the cloudy bag model (Lu et al., 1997-1999), and the spectator-diquark model of Ma et al. The model enables systematic calculation of baryon properties from a single parameter set, with remaining discrepancies at low $Q^2$ attributed to missing quark-antiquark (pion cloud) contributions.
