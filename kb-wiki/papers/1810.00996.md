# Recent Results on Heavy Quark Production in High Energy Nucleus Collisions

> The goal of the ultra-relativistic heavy ion program is to study Quantum Chromodynamics under finite temperature and density conditions. After a couple of decades of experiment, the focus at the top RHIC and the LHC energy has evolved to quantitative understanding of properties of the hot and dense medium, namely the strongly-coupled Quark Gluon Plasma (sQGP) created in these heavy-ion collisions, and to constrain transport parameters of the sQGP medium. Heavy quarks offer unique insights towards detailed understanding of the sQGP properties due to their large masses. Recent collider and detector advances have enabled precision measurements of heavy quark hadron production in heavy-ion collisions. In this talk, I will review recent results from heavy quark production measurements at RHIC and the LHC. These high quality data will offer stringent constraints on theoretical model calculations and help precision determination of QGP medium transport parameters. Finally, I look forward to a more prospective future of the heavy quark program with further improved detectors at both RHIC and the LHC.

# Recent Results on Heavy Quark Production in High Energy Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim
Heavy quark measurements at RHIC and LHC now provide precision constraints on QGP transport parameters, but no single theoretical model can simultaneously describe both the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow data for charm hadrons at both collider energies, indicating that the heavy quark diffusion coefficient remains poorly constrained despite high-quality data.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental measurements of open heavy flavor hadrons (D⁰, Dₛ⁺, Λc⁺, B⁺) and their decay leptons in p+p, p/d+A, and A+A collisions at RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV) and LHC (√sNN = 5.02 TeV). Observables include the nuclear modification factor RAA, elliptic flow v₂, and hadron yield ratios (Dₛ⁺/D⁰, Λc⁺/D⁰), compared to theoretical models incorporating Langevin or Boltzmann transport, coalescence hadronization, and pQCD energy loss calculations.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for QGP phase transition: Tc = 154 ± 9 MeV from lattice QCD with (2+1) flavors and physical quark masses
- D⁰ meson RAA is significantly suppressed at pT > 4 GeV/c in 0–10% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, with suppression level similar to light hadrons
- D⁰ meson v₂ in 10–40% centrality Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV follows the same Number-of-Constituent-Quark scaling trend as light flavor hadrons (K⁰S, Λ, Ξ⁻)
- Dₛ⁺/D⁰ ratio in mid-central heavy-ion collisions is significantly higher than the p+p reference value of 0.15–0.2 (PYTHIA), with enhancement persisting to high pT
- Λc⁺/D⁰ ratio in mid-central Au+Au collisions is significantly larger than PYTHIA expectation and world average fragmentation ratio from elementary collisions
- Λc⁺/D⁰ ratio in p+p and p+Pb collisions by ALICE is about 0.4–0.6 between 1 < pT < ~10 GeV/c
- B⁺-meson RAA in 0–100% centrality Pb+Pb at √sNN = 5.02 TeV is similar to charged hadrons and D⁰ mesons at pT = 8–40 GeV/c
- Non-prompt J/ψ RAA shows less suppression than light hadrons and D⁰ mesons at pT = 4–10 GeV/c in Pb+Pb at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- Bottom-electron RAA is higher than charm-electron RAA with about 2σ significance in Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV (STAR)
- STAR MAPS pixel detector achieves < 40 μm pointing resolution at pT = 1 GeV/c with 21×21 μm² pitch and 0.4% X₀ per layer thickness

## Context
This review extends the heavy quark program from earlier RHIC and LHC results (StarWhitePaper, PhenixWhitePaper, LhcSummary) that established jet quenching and partonic collectivity as evidence for QGP formation. The measurements now enable quantitative comparison to lattice QCD calculations of the charm-quark diffusion coefficient (Banerjee 2011, Kaczmarek 2014, Ding 2015) and to Bayesian analysis fits (Xu 2017). Key open questions identified include disentangling radiative versus collisional energy loss mechanisms, comparing Langevin versus Boltzmann approaches for heavy quark in-medium evolution (Cao 2016), and understanding the role of coalescence versus fragmentation hadronization (Oh 2009, Plumari 2017). The next phase requires precision bottom measurements at low pT and heavy flavor baryon studies, enabled by detector upgrades at both RHIC (sPHENIX MVTX) and LHC (ALICE ITS-upgrade) using fast MAPS sensors with 10–40× improved read-out integration time.
