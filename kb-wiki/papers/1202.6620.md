# Collision Energy Dependence of Viscous Hydrodynamic Flow in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Using a (2+1)-d viscous hydrodynamical model, we study the dependence of flow observables on the collision energy ranging from sqrt(s)=7.7 A GeV at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) to sqrt(s)=2760 A GeV at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). With a realistic equation of state, Glauber model initial conditions and a small specific shear viscosity eta/s = 0.08, the differential charged hadron elliptic flow v_2^{ch}(p_T,sqrt(s)) is found to exhibit a very broad maximum as a function of sqrt(s) around top RHIC energy, rendering it almost independent of collision energy for 39 < sqrt(s) < 2760 A GeV. Compared to ideal fluid dynamical simulations, this "saturation" of elliptic flow is shifted to higher collision energies by shear viscous effects. For color-glass motivated MC-KLN initial conditions, which require a larger shear viscosity eta/s = 0.2 to reproduce the measured elliptic flow, a similar "saturation" is not observed up to LHC energies, except for very low p_T. We emphasize that this "saturation" of the elliptic flow is not associated with the QCD phase transition, but arises from the interplay between radial and elliptic flow which shifts with sqrt(s) depending on the fluid's viscosity and leads to a subtle cancellation between increasing contributions from light and decreasing contributions from heavy particles to v_2 in the sqrt(s) range where v_2^{ch}(p_T,sqrt(s)) at fixed p_T is maximal. By generalizing the definition of spatial eccentricity epsilon_x to isothermal hyper-surfaces, we calculate epsilon_x on the kinetic freeze-out surface at different collision energies. Up to top RHIC energy, sqrt(s)=200 A GeV, the fireball is still out-of-plane deformed at freeze out, while at LHC energy the final spatial eccentricity is predicted to approach zero.

## Key claim

Using a (2+1)-d viscous hydrodynamical model with a realistic equation of state, the differential charged hadron elliptic flow v₂ᶜʰ(p_T, √s) exhibits a very broad maximum as a function of collision energy around top RHIC energy, rendering it almost independent of collision energy for 39 ≤ √s ≤ 2760 A GeV, but this "saturation" is not associated with the QCD phase transition, arising instead from the interplay between radial and elliptic flow that shifts with √s depending on the fluid's viscosity.

## Method

The authors employed the (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model VISH2+1 with longitudinal boost invariance, using a lattice QCD based equation of state (s95p-PCE) with chemical freeze-out at T_chem = 165 MeV and kinetic decoupling at T_dec = 120 MeV. Initial entropy density profiles were generated from both MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models, with specific shear viscosities η/s = 0.08 and η/s = 0.2 respectively, and evolved from an assumed thermalization time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c. Final hadron distributions were computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription with strong resonance decays up to 2 GeV in particle mass.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy range studied: from √s = 7.7 A GeV (RHIC) to √s = 2760 A GeV (LHC)
- For MC-Glauber initial conditions with η/s = 0.08, the charged hadron elliptic flow v₂ᶜʰ(p_T, √s) at fixed p_T is almost independent of collision energy for 39 ≤ √s ≤ 2760 A GeV
- For MC-KLN initial conditions with η/s = 0.2, the "saturation" of elliptic flow is not observed up to LHC energies except for very low p_T (p_T < 200 MeV/c)
- Mean p_T ⟨p_T⟩ increases by 43% (from 0.48 to 0.68 GeV/c) for MC-KLN and by 140% (from 0.29 to 0.63 GeV/c) for MC-Glauber from √s = 7.7 to 2760 A GeV
- Initial peak temperatures range from 269.2 MeV (MC-Glauber at 7.7 A GeV) to 485.2 MeV (MC-Glauber at 2760 A GeV); MC-KLN gives about 30 MeV lower peak temperatures
- Fireball lifetime ranges from 9.3 fm/c (MC-Glauber at 7.7 A GeV) to 14.2 fm/c (both models at 2760 A GeV)
- Final charged hadron multiplicity dN_ch/dη for 0-5% central collisions ranges from 212.3 (MC-Glauber at 7.7 A GeV) to 1597.2 (MC-KLN at 2760 A GeV)
- For MC-Glauber, the overlap area S in Pb+Pb at 2760 A GeV decreases from 22.8 fm² (0-5% central) to 6.5 fm² (60-70% peripheral); for MC-KLN, from 23.6 fm² to 4.7 fm²
- Up to √s = 200 A GeV, the fireball is still out-of-plane deformed at freeze-out; at LHC energy the final spatial eccentricity approaches zero

## Context

This work extends earlier ideal hydrodynamic studies by Kestin and Heinz (2008) by incorporating viscous effects and a modern lattice QCD-based equation of state, and by comparing two different initialization models (MC-Glauber and MC-KLN). The study provides systematic predictions for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and LHC experiments, showing that the "saturation" of elliptic flow with collision energy is a viscous effect not requiring a soft point in the equation of state. The results enable experimental discrimination between initialization models through the predicted violation of √s-scaling in the centrality dependence of charged multiplicity for MC-KLN initial conditions, and through the different behavior of eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow as a function of multiplicity density.
