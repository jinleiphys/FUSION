# Probing the equation of state with pions

> The influence of the isospin-independent, isospin- and momentum-dependent equation of state (EoS), as well as the Coulomb interaction on the pion production in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions (HICs) is studied for both isospin-symmetric and neutron-rich systems. The Coulomb interaction plays an important role in the reaction dynamics, and strongly influences the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of charged pions. It even leads to the $π^-/π^+$ ratio deviating slightly from unity for isospin-{\it symmetric} systems. The Coulomb interaction between mesons and baryons is also crucial for reproducing the proper pion flow since it changes the behavior of the directed and the elliptic flow components of pions visibly. The EoS can be better investigated in neutron-rich system if {\it multiple} probes are measured simultaneously. For example, the rapidity and the transverse momentum distributions of the charged pions, the $π^-/π^+$ ratio, the various pion flow components, as well as the {\it difference} of $π^+$-$π^-$ flows. A new sensitive observable is proposed to probe the symmetry potential energy at high densities, namely the transverse momentum distribution of the elliptic flow difference [$Δv_2^{π^+ - π^-}(p_t^{\rm c.m.})$].

## Key claim

The Coulomb interaction between mesons and baryons is the leading cause of the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio deviating from unity even in isospin-symmetric systems, and its inclusion is crucial for reproducing proper pion flow; the transverse momentum distribution of the elliptic flow difference $\Delta v_2^{\pi^+ - \pi^-}(p_t^{\rm c.m.})$ is proposed as a new sensitive observable to probe the symmetry potential energy at high densities, but only when multiple pion observables are measured simultaneously in neutron-rich systems.

## Method

The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, version 1.3, with updated mean-field potentials including isospin-independent Skyrme terms (hard and soft equations of state with and without momentum dependence), isospin-dependent symmetry potentials (four parameterizations: F15, Fa3, DDH$\rho^*$, DDH3$\rho\delta^*$), and hadronic Coulomb interactions (including meson-baryon Coulomb potentials). They simulated central ($b=0$ fm) $^{40}{\rm Ca}+^{40}{\rm Ca}$ collisions at $0.4A$ GeV and semi-peripheral ($b=7-9$ fm) $^{208}{\rm Pb}+^{208}{\rm Pb}$ collisions at $0.8A$ GeV, comparing rapidity and transverse momentum distributions, $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratios, directed flow, elliptic flow, and flow differences for charged pions.

## Key numbers

- For central $^{40}{\rm Ca}+^{40}{\rm Ca}$ at $E_b=0.4A$ GeV with SM-EoS and F15 symmetry potential, the integrated $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio is about $1.05$; at $E_b=0.8A$ GeV it is reduced to $1.02$.
- For central $^{197}{\rm Au}+^{197}{\rm Au}$ reactions at beam energies from $0.4A$ to $1.5A$ GeV, calculations with a soft EoS ($K=200$ MeV) are in good agreement with FOPI preliminary data, while cascade mode deviates at beam energies below $0.8A$ GeV.
- The hard EoS has incompressibility $K=300$ MeV, the soft EoS $K=200$ MeV, the hard momentum-dependent EoS $K=380$ MeV, and the soft momentum-dependent EoS $K=200$ MeV; all use symmetry energy coefficient $S_0=34$ MeV.
- For semi-peripheral $^{208}{\rm Pb}+^{208}{\rm Pb}$ at $E_b=0.8A$ GeV and $b=7-9$ fm, the $\Delta v_2^{\rm pm}$ (elliptic flow difference) shows pronounced sensitivity to the density dependence of the symmetry potential at transverse momenta $\sim 0.2-0.5$ GeV$/c$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of pion production in heavy ion collisions using transport models such as QMD and BUU, particularly building on the findings of Bass (1995), Li (2005), and the FOPI Collaboration (Pelizaeus 1997, Hong 2005) regarding pion flows and ratios. It also follows the symmetry energy investigations of Tsang (2001), Furnstahl (2002), Chen (2004), and Khoa (2005), and the relativistic mean-field calculations of Gaitanos (2004) for DDH$\rho$ and DDH3$\rho\delta$ parameterizations. The proposed $\Delta v_2^{\pi^+ - \pi^-}(p_t^{\rm c.m.})$ observable enables future experimental extraction of the high-density symmetry potential by comparing multiple pion observables simultaneously in neutron-rich systems.
