# A real-time lattice simulation of the thermalization of a gluon plasma: first results

> To achieve an understanding of the thermalization of a quark-gluon plasma, starting from QCD without using model assumptions, is a formidable task. We study the early stage dynamics of a relativistic heavy ion collision in the framework of real time simulations of classical Yang-Mills theory in a static box with the color glass condensate as initial condition. Our study generalizes a previous one by Fukushima and Gelis from SU(2) to the realistic case of SU(3). We calculate the chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic energy densities as well as the ratio of longitudinal and transverse pressure as a function of time as probes for thermalization. Our preliminary results on coarse lattices show the occurrence of Weibel instabilities prior to thermalization.

# A real-time lattice simulation of the thermalization of a gluon plasma: first results

## Key claim
The authors provide preliminary evidence that classical Yang-Mills theory with SU(3) gauge group, starting from color glass condensate initial conditions in a static box, exhibits chromo-Weibel instabilities and approaches hydrodynamization, but the results are on coarse lattices and the hydrodynamization time depends on an artificial fluctuation seed parameter.

## Method
The authors perform real-time lattice simulations of classical Yang-Mills theory on a 40^3 lattice, using the McLerran-Venugopalan model to construct initial gauge fields and chromo-electric fields for SU(3). They add small random fluctuations to the initial chromo-electric fields controlled by a seed parameter Delta, then solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in a static box. They compute the chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic energy densities and the ratio of longitudinal to transverse pressure as functions of time.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: 40^3, averaged over 30 independent measurements
- Coupling constant: g = 2
- System size parameter: g^2 mu L = 120, chosen to conform to RHIC physics expectations
- Fluctuation seed: Delta = 0.025, which changes the initial total energy density by less than 10%
- Filamentation observed at g^2 mu t = 1666, clearly visible at g^2 mu t = 3333, and almost dissolved at g^2 mu t = 23333
- Hydrodynamization level set at P_L/P_T approximately 0.7

## Context
This work generalizes a previous study by Fukushima and Gelis from SU(2) to the realistic SU(3) gauge group. It extends the approach of real-time classical Yang-Mills simulations of heavy-ion collision dynamics, contrasting with other works using Bjorken (expanding) coordinates. The next steps will be an analysis of finite volume and discretization effects as well as studies of an expanding system.

Review-type paper: no original numerics.
