# Structure of A=7 iso-triplet $Λ$ hypernuclei studied with he four-body model

> The structure of the T=1 iso-triplet hypernuclei, $^7_Λ$He, $^7_Λ$Li and $^7_Λ$Be within the framework of an $α+Λ+N+N$ four-body cluster model is studied. Interactions between the constituent subunits are determined so as to reproduce reasonably well the observed low-energy properties of the $αN$, $αΛ$, $αNN$ and $αΛN$ subsystems. Furthermore, the two-body $ΛN$ interaction is adjusted so as to reproduce the $0^+$-$1^+$ splitting of $^4_Λ$H. Also a phenomenological $ΛN$ charge symmetry breaking(CSB) interaction is introduced. The $Λ$ binding energy of the ground state in $^7_Λ$He is predicted to be 5.16(5.36) MeV with(without) the CSB interaction. The calculated energy splittings of the $3/2^+$-$5/2^+$ states in $^7_Λ$He and $^7_Λ$Li are around 0.1 MeV. We point out that there is a three-layer structure of the matter distribution, $α$ particle, $Λ$ skin, proton or neutron halo, in the $^7_Λ{\rm He}(J=5/2^+)$, $^7_Λ{\rm Li}(J=5/2^+)$ and $^7_Λ{\rm Be}(J=1/2^+)$ states.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the Λ binding energy of the ground state in ⁷ΛHe is 5.16 MeV with the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) interaction and 5.36 MeV without it, with the CSB interaction introduced phenomenologically to reproduce the experimental binding energy difference between ⁴ΛHe and ⁴ΛH.

## Method

The authors employed an α+Λ+N+N four-body cluster model, solving the Schrödinger equation using the Rayleigh-Ritz variational method with Gaussian basis functions. Interactions between constituent subunits (αN, αΛ, αNN, αΛN, and ΛN) were determined to reproduce observed low-energy properties of the subsystems, with the ΛN interaction adjusted to reproduce the 0⁺-1⁺ splitting of ⁴ΛH and a phenomenological ΛN charge symmetry breaking interaction introduced to fit the experimental binding energy difference between ⁴ΛHe and ⁴ΛH.

## Key numbers

- Λ binding energy of the ground state in ⁷ΛHe: 5.16 MeV (with CSB interaction) and 5.36 MeV (without CSB interaction)
- Calculated energy splitting of the 3/2⁺-5/2⁺ states in ⁷ΛHe and ⁷ΛLi: around 0.1 MeV
- Calculated BΛ of ⁷ΛLi* (T=1, J=1/2⁺): 5.28 MeV, compared to experimental value 5.26 MeV
- Calculated BΛ of ⁷ΛBe (J=1/2⁺): 5.21 MeV, compared to experimental value 5.16 MeV
- Calculated BΛ of 0⁺ state in ⁴ΛH: 1.99 MeV; in ⁴ΛHe: 2.35 MeV
- Calculated BΛ of 1⁺ state in ⁴ΛH: 0.98 MeV; in ⁴ΛHe: 1.17 MeV
- Experimental ΔCSB = BΛ(⁴ΛHe) - BΛ(⁴ΛH): 0.35 ± 0.06 MeV for ground (0⁺) state and 0.24 ± 0.06 MeV for excited (1⁺) state
- Calculated rms distance between α and valence nucleon (r̄α-N) in ⁷ΛHe (J=1/2⁺): 3.66 fm
- Calculated rms distance between α and Λ (r̄α-Λ) in ⁷ΛHe (J=1/2⁺): 2.81 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body ⁵ΛHe+N+N model study (Hiyama 1996) to a full four-body α+Λ+N+N framework, and extends their earlier four-body analysis of ⁷ΛLi T=0 states (Hiyama 2006) to the T=1 iso-triplet (⁷ΛHe, ⁷ΛLi*, ⁷ΛBe). The calculations provide predictions for upcoming experiments such as the ⁷Li(e,e'K⁺)⁷ΛHe reaction at Jefferson Lab, and enable systematic study of charge symmetry breaking effects in p-shell hypernuclei, including the A=8 iso-doublet (⁸ΛLi and ⁸ΛBe) using α+t+Λ and α+³He+Λ cluster models.
