# Comment on "No-core calculations" of the spectra of light nuclei

> We comment upon a recent work by Zheng, Barrett, Jaqua, Vary and McCarthy, concerning calculations of spectra of light nuclei with no core. It is demonstrated that the omission of particle-particle ladder diagrams in their calculations, explains the large differences between results obtained with various model spaces.

# Comment on "No-core calculations" of the spectra of light nuclei

## Key claim

The large differences between results obtained with various model spaces in the no-core calculations of Zheng et al. (1993) are not due to an inherent need for large model spaces, but rather to the omission of particle-particle ladder diagrams in their effective interaction. When these ladder diagrams are included, low-order perturbation theory within a small model space reproduces the same results as the G-matrix calculation in a large model space.

## Method

The authors performed two-particle calculations using the Bonn B nucleon-nucleon potential with a harmonic oscillator basis (ħΩ = 14 MeV) and a fixed starting energy of -100 MeV, following the no-core approach of Zheng et al. (1993). They computed eigenvalues for the lowest JT=10 state using only the G-matrix (as done by Zheng et al.) and then repeated the calculations including two-particle ladder diagrams up to third order in G, for model spaces ranging from the 0s-shell only up through the 1p0f-shell. The Pauli operator was defined with no holes, as in the no-core approach.

## Key numbers

- Eigenvalue for the JT=10 state with G-matrix only in the 0s-shell model space: -7.83 (scaled units)
- Eigenvalue for the JT=10 state with G-matrix only in the 0s-1p0f model space: -12.35 (scaled units), showing a difference of approximately 50% or more
- Eigenvalue for the JT=10 state with G-matrix plus two-particle ladder diagrams (G+2P) in the 0s-shell model space: -12.37 (scaled units), closely matching the large-space G-matrix result
- Eigenvalue for the JT=10 state with G+2P in the 0s-1s0d model space: -12.30 (scaled units)
- Harmonic oscillator energy ħΩ = 14 MeV, with a negative shift Δ = -71 MeV applied to single-particle energies
- Fixed starting energy ω = -100 MeV was used throughout

## Context

This comment directly addresses the work of Zheng, Barrett, Jaqua, Vary and McCarthy (1993) on no-core shell-model calculations for light nuclei. The authors demonstrate that the claimed need for large model spaces in that work is an artifact of omitting particle-particle ladder diagrams, and that low-order perturbation theory with proper diagrammatic inclusion works well. The comment also criticizes the use of a fixed starting energy for multi-shell model spaces, noting that Suzuki et al. (1994) proposed a scheme to handle the starting energy dependence. The non-hermiticity problem that arises when ladder diagrams are included is discussed, with reference to approaches by Lindgren (1991) and Kuo et al. (1993) for constructing hermitian effective interactions.
