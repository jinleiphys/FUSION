# Exciting gauge unstable modes of the quark-gluon plasma by relativistic jets

> We present a study of the properties of the collective modes of a system composed by a thermalized quark-gluon plasma traversed by a relativistic jet of partons. We find that when the jet traverses the system unstable gauge field modes are excited and grow on very short time scales. The aim is to provide a novel mechanism for the description of the jet quenching phenomenon, where the jet crossing the plasma loses energy exciting colored unstable modes. In order to simplify the analysis we employ a linear response approximation, valid for short time scales. We assume that the partons in the jet can be described with a tsunami-like distribution function, whereas we treat the quark-gluon plasma employing two different approaches. In the first approach we adopt a Vlasov approximation for the kinetic equations, in the second approach we solve a set of fluid equations. In both cases we derive the expressions of the dispersion law of the collective unstable modes and compare the results obtained.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that when a relativistic jet of partons traverses a thermalized quark-gluon plasma, unstable gauge field modes are excited and grow on very short time scales, providing a novel mechanism for jet quenching. However, this analysis is limited to a linear response approximation valid only for short time scales, and the jet is described with a simplified tsunami-like distribution function rather than a more realistic momentum spread.

## Method

The authors study the collective modes of a system composed of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma traversed by a relativistic jet of partons using two complementary approaches. In the first approach, they employ a Vlasov approximation for the kinetic transport equations of quarks, antiquarks, gluons, and jet partons, treating the gauge fields as classical and neglecting collision terms. In the second approach, they use a chromohydrodynamical fluid description derived from kinetic theory by expanding transport equations in moments of momenta and truncating at the second moment level, closing the system with an equation of state relating pressure and energy density fluctuations. In both cases, the polarization tensor of the combined system is computed and the dispersion laws of the collective unstable modes are derived and compared.

## Key numbers

* Instabilities develop on time scales of 1-2 fm/c, estimated in the weak coupling limit at a temperature T ~ 350 MeV.
* The parameter b = ω²_jet / ω²_t, which measures the relative contribution of the jet to the total plasma frequency, is set to values of b=0.1 and b=0.02 in the numerical results shown.
* In the fluid approach, instabilities develop for jet velocities v > c_s^a, where c_s^a is the sound speed parameter; results are shown for the conformal value c_s^a = 1/√3 and for c_s^a = 1/2.
* In the kinetic theory approach, for sufficiently small jet velocities v < 0.6, there is no preferred unstable direction, while for larger velocities the most unstable modes correspond to large angles between the wave vector k and the jet velocity v.

## Context

This work extends the study of chromo instabilities in the quark-gluon plasma, a field reviewed by Mrowczynski (2006), by considering a relativistic jet traversing the plasma as a source of instability. The authors previously developed the fluid approach in Manuel (2006) and applied it to this problem in Mannarelli (2007), while the kinetic theory treatment is presented in Mannarelli (2007). The mechanism provides a novel description of jet quenching in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the jet loses energy by exciting colored unstable gauge modes rather than through radiative processes alone, and the comparison between the two theoretical approaches enables assessment of the fluid approximation's validity for describing short-time plasma dynamics.
