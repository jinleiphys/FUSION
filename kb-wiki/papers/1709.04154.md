# Prompt photon production and photon-jet correlations at the LHC

> Next-to-leading order predictions matched to parton showers are compared with recent ATLAS data on inclusive photon production and CMS data on associated photon and jet production in pp and pPb collisions at different centre-of-mass energies of the LHC. We find good agreement and, as expected, considerably reduced scale uncertainties compared to previous theoretical calculations. Predictions are made for the ratio of inclusive photons over decay photons $R_γ$, an important quantity to evaluate the significance of additional photon sources, e.g. thermal radiation from a Quark-Gluon-Plasma, and for distributions in the parton momentum fraction in lead ions $x_{\rm Pb}^{\rm obs}$, that could be determined by ALICE, ATLAS, CMS and LHCb in ongoing analyses of photon+jet production in pPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. These data should have an important impact on the determination of nuclear effects such as shadowing at low $x$.

# Prompt photon production and photon-jet correlations at the LHC

## Key claim

Next-to-leading order predictions matched to parton showers (NLO+PS) using POWHEG+PYTHIA agree well with ATLAS inclusive photon data and CMS photon-jet data in pp and pPb collisions, with considerably reduced scale uncertainties compared to previous theoretical calculations, but the agreement depends on the tuning of the parton shower cutoff scale and the choice of parton distribution functions.

## Method

The authors implemented a NLO calculation of direct photon production (including the processes qqbar → γg, qg → γq, and gg → γqqbar) in the POWHEG framework, matched it to the PYTHIA 8 parton shower with pT-ordered evolution and string hadronisation, and compared the resulting predictions to published ATLAS data on inclusive photon production at √s = 13 TeV and CMS data on photon-jet correlations in pp collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and pPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV. The NLO+PS results were also compared to LO+PS predictions from PYTHIA 8 and to fixed-order NLO predictions from JETPHOX using the BFG II photon fragmentation function.

## Key numbers

- ATLAS inclusive photon data at √s = 13 TeV with integrated luminosity 3.2 fb⁻¹, compared in four rapidity bins; NLO+PS predictions agree with data within experimental error bars, while NLO JETPHOX predictions globally underestimate the data
- CMS photon-jet data in pp collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (5.3 pb⁻¹) and pPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV (30.4 nb⁻¹); photon isolation with fixed cut on total energy E^iso at 5 GeV, photons required to have pTγ > 40 GeV and |ηγ| < 1.44, jets with anti-kT algorithm (R = 0.3) required to have pTjet > 30 GeV and |ηjet| < 1.6
- The ratio Rγ of inclusive over decay photons at √s = 13 TeV shows central values at NLO and NLO+PS agreeing well for pTγ > 7 GeV, while LO central values are larger and fall outside the NLO error band
- For pPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the observable region of the parton momentum fraction in lead ions xPb^obs ranges from 0.005 to 0.25; the shadowing region below x ~ 0.02 exhibits nuclear PDF uncertainties of up to a factor of two with nCTEQ15(np) PDFs

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NLO+PS calculations for prompt photon production at RHIC (Jezo, 2016) to LHC energies, confronting them for the first time with ATLAS and CMS data. The matched NLO+PS approach combines the advantages of fixed-order NLO calculations (which stabilise scale dependences) with parton shower Monte Carlo generators (which provide detailed final-state information), improving upon earlier inclusive NLO calculations with JETPHOX (Catani, 2002) and LO Monte Carlo simulations with PYTHIA 8 (Sjostrand, 2007). The predictions for Rγ and xPb^obs distributions enable future experimental determinations of nuclear PDF effects such as shadowing at low x and the extraction of thermal photon signals from the Quark-Gluon-Plasma in heavy-ion collisions.
