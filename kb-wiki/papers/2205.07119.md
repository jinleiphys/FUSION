# Uncertainty Quantification in Breakup Reactions

> Breakup reactions are one of the favored probes to study loosely bound nuclei, particularly those in the limit of stability forming a halo. In order to interpret such breakup experiments, the continuum discretized coupled channel method is typically used. In this study, the first Bayesian analysis of a breakup reaction model is performed. We use a combination of statistical methods together with a three-body reaction model (the continuum discretized coupled channel method) to quantify the uncertainties on the breakup observables due to the parameters in the effective potential describing the loosely bound projectile of interest. The combination of tools we develop opens the path for a Bayesian analysis of not only breakup processes, but also a wide array of complex processes that require computationally intensive reaction models.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first Bayesian analysis of a breakup reaction model, using Gaussian process emulators to enable Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling that would otherwise be computationally prohibitive. The analysis is limited to quantifying uncertainties from the core-valence effective interaction parameters only, while fixing the optical potentials to those from a prior study (Mortimer 2002, Capel 2007).

## Method

The authors couple a Gaussian process emulator with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method implemented in the code FRESCOX to model the breakup reaction ⁸B + ²⁰⁸Pb → ⁷Be + p + ²⁰⁸Pb at 80 MeV per nucleon. They train the emulator on 500 Latin hypercube samples of four Woods-Saxon potential parameters (Coulomb radius, Woods-Saxon radius, diffuseness, and spin-orbit depth), using principal component analysis to reduce the high-dimensional angular and energy distribution outputs, then perform Bayesian calibration with mock data generated from the parameters of Capel (2007).

## Key numbers

- The CDCC simulation for a single parameter set takes approximately 25 hours on a single core.
- The emulator is trained with n=500 samples (reduced to n=393 after filtering energy distributions exceeding 1000 mb/MeV).
- The angular distribution output dimension is reduced from d_Ω=401 to q_Ω=39 principal components, capturing 99% of the variance.
- The combined angular and energy emulator achieves a test r² value of 0.99.
- The extracted ⁸B ANC squared is C² = 0.49 ± 0.01 fm⁻¹.
- The 95% credible interval for breakup cross sections corresponds to roughly 20% uncertainty (two standard deviations of the predicted mean).

## Context

This work extends Bayesian uncertainty quantification methods previously applied to elastic scattering within the optical model (Lovell 2017, 2018; King 2019; Catacora 2019, 2021; Whitehead 2021) to the more computationally demanding three-body CDCC framework for breakup reactions. The combination of Gaussian process emulation with Bayesian calibration opens the path for uncertainty quantification in other complex reaction models that are too expensive for direct Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling, including the eigenvector continuation method for two-body elastic scattering (Furnstahl 2020, Drischler 2021, Melendez 2021) and preliminary three-body work (Zhang 2021).
