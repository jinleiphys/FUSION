# Effective field theory and cold Fermi gases near unitary limit

> We study a dynamics of ultracold Fermi-gases near the unitary limit in the framework of Effective Field Theory. It is shown that, while one can obtain a reasonable description of the universal proportionality constant both in the narrow and the broad Feshbach resonance limits, the reguirement of the reparametrisation invariance leads to appearance of the three body forces needed to cancel the otherwise arising off-shell uncertainties. The size of the unsertainties is estimated.

# Effective field theory and cold Fermi gases near unitary limit

## Key claim

While effective field theory can provide a reasonable description of the universal proportionality constant ξ for cold Fermi gases in both the narrow and broad Feshbach resonance limits, the requirement of reparametrisation invariance forces the introduction of three-body forces to cancel off-shell uncertainties that otherwise arise in the presence of a medium.

## Method

The author solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the fermion-fermion T-matrix in vacuum using an effective Lagrangian with short-range interactions, then extends the calculation to the in-medium case by solving the Feynmann-Galitskii equation for the particle-particle/hole-hole ladder approximation. The universal constant ξ is extracted by computing the ground state energy density of the interacting Fermi gas from the in-medium T-matrix, both in the strict unitary limit with vanishing effective range and for finite effective range corresponding to narrow Feshbach resonances.

## Key numbers

- ξ(UL) ≃ 0.33 in the unitary limit with vanishing effective radius
- ξ = 0.48 at p_F r ∼ 1 (finite effective range)
- ξ ≃ 0.68 for neutron matter with scattering length −18.5 fm and effective radius 2.7 fm
- ξ_{C_4} ≃ 0.73 when including the shape parameter correction for neutron matter
- The "world average" for ξ is given as 0.42 ± 0.002

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Weinberg (1990) and Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for fermion-fermion interactions with large scattering length to the many-body problem of ultracold Fermi gases near the unitary limit. The author builds on the analysis of reparametrisation invariance in many-fermion systems from Krippa (2002) and shows that three-body forces, analogous to those required in few-body systems by Van Kolck (1999), are necessary to cancel off-shell ambiguities in standard many-body approximations such as the particle-particle/hole-hole ladder and parquet approximations. The results enable future systematic EFT studies of strongly interacting Fermi systems including dilute atomic Fermi gases and neutron/nuclear matter, though the author notes that a consistent power counting for the in-medium case remains an open problem.
