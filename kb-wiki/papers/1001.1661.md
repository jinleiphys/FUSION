# Investigation of the existence of hybrid stars using Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models

> We investigate the hadron-quark phase transition inside neutron stars and obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars. The equation of state for the quark phase using the standard NJL model is too soft leading to an unstable star and suggesting a modification of the NJL model by introducing a momentum cutoff dependent on the chemical potential. However, even in this approach, the instability remains. In order to remedy the instability we suggest the introduction of a vector coupling in the NJL model, which makes the EoS stiffer, reducing the instability. We conclude that the possible existence of quark matter inside the stars require high densities, leading to very compact stars.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that stable hybrid stars (neutron stars with a deconfined quark matter core) are possible only in a very narrow range of central densities, making their existence "remote," and that even with a vector coupling added to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to stiffen the equation of state, the instability of the quark phase is reduced but not eliminated.

## Method

The authors used the SU(2) NJL model with a repulsive vector coupling term to describe the quark phase, solving the gap equations for dynamical mass and renormalized chemical potential in the Hartree approximation with a momentum cutoff regularization. For the hadronic phase, they employed the GM1 (Glendenning and Moszkowski) equation of state. They applied the Gibbs criterion for phase equilibrium (equal chemical potentials, temperatures, and pressures) and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius and mass-central density relations for hybrid stars for different ratios of vector to scalar coupling (\(g_V/g_S\)).

## Key numbers

- The central density of the most massive neutron stars is estimated to be \(\sim 7-10\) times the nuclear matter saturation density \(\rho_0\).
- For a vector coupling ratio \(g_V/g_S = 0.5\), the stable hybrid star branch appears in a small range of central densities around \(\rho_{\rm c}/\rho_0 \sim 9\).
- The plateau in the mass versus central density plot decreases from \(g_V/g_S = 0\) to \(g_V/g_S = 0.25\) and disappears at \(g_V/g_S = 0.5\).
- For higher vector couplings (\(g_V/g_S > 0.5\)), the phase transition starts at densities much higher than \(10\rho_0\), which were not considered.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Baldo et al. (2003), who found that the standard NJL model yields an equation of state too soft to produce stable hybrid stars and proposed a chemical-potential-dependent cutoff that still left an instability. The authors here show that adding a vector coupling to the NJL model, as previously studied for pure quark stars in their own work (Coelho et al., 2009), can reduce but not remove the instability, and that the resulting hybrid stars are very compact. This enables future studies to constrain the vector coupling strength needed for a viable hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars, and it contrasts with approaches using the MIT bag model or the chromodielectric model (CDM) cited in the text.
