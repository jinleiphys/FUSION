# Isospin violation in pion-nucleon scattering

> We construct the complete effective chiral pion-nucleon Lagrangian in the presence of virtual photons to one loop. As an application, we consider strong and electromagnetic isospin violation for scattering of neutral pions off nucleons. We show that for the scattering lengths these isospin violating terms are of the same size as the purely hadronic ones. We also analyze isospin-violating effects for the $σ$--term. These can be as large as 10% for the absolute value but are negligible for the shift to the Cheng-Dashen point.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the complete effective chiral pion-nucleon Lagrangian including virtual photons to one-loop order (third order in small momenta) and shows that for neutral pion-nucleon scattering lengths, the isospin-violating terms (both strong and electromagnetic) are of the same size as the purely hadronic isospin-conserving ones. However, the authors caution that these results are indicative only and need to be supplemented by higher-order (fourth order) calculations to achieve high precision.

## Method

The authors extend heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) for two flavors by constructing the generating functional in the presence of virtual photons, performing renormalization via heat kernel techniques, and building all finite terms up to and including third chiral dimension (counting the electric charge as order one in small momenta). They then compute the S-wave scattering lengths for neutral pions off protons and neutrons to one-loop order, including both strong isospin violation from the quark mass difference (via the dimension-two operator proportional to c5) and electromagnetic isospin violation from the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference (via the LEC C). They also evaluate the scalar form factor (sigma-term) for the proton and neutron.

## Key numbers

- The dimension-two isospin-conserving contribution to the π⁰p scattering length: a_IC^(2) = -1.33 GeV⁻¹
- The dimension-two strong isospin-violating contribution: a_IV^(2) = +0.12 GeV⁻¹
- The dimension-three isospin-conserving contribution: a_IC^(3) = +0.84 GeV⁻¹
- The dimension-three electromagnetic contribution: a_em^(3) = -0.30 GeV⁻¹
- Total π⁰p scattering length: a(π⁰p) = -8.8 × 10⁻³ / M_π (with M_π the neutral pion mass)
- Decomposition: a(π⁰p)_str,IC = -4.8 × 10⁻³ / M_π; a(π⁰p)_str,IV = -1.1 × 10⁻³ / M_π; a(π⁰p)_em = -2.9 × 10⁻³ / M_π
- Difference a(π⁰p) - a(π⁰n) = -2.2 × 10⁻³ / M_π (25% strong isospin violation in the difference)
- Proton sigma-term at t=0: σ_p(0) = 47.2 MeV (isospin-conserving) - 3.9 MeV (isospin-violating) = 43.3 MeV (8% reduction)
- Shift to the Cheng-Dashen point: σ_p(2M_π⁺²) - σ_p(0) = 7.5 MeV (compared to 7.9 MeV in the isospin limit, a 5% difference)
- Electromagnetic LEC f2 = -(0.45 ± 0.19) GeV⁻¹, fixed from the electromagnetic proton mass shift of -(0.7 ± 0.3) MeV
- Strong LEC c5 = -0.09 ± 0.01 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the seminal analysis by Weinberg (1977) who first pointed out that the up-down quark mass difference could produce a 30% effect in the difference of π⁰p and π⁰n scattering lengths, and the subsequent reformulation by Weinberg (1994). It builds upon the chiral perturbation theory framework for pion-nucleon systems developed by Bernard et al. (1995) and the heavy baryon formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991). The paper provides the machinery to simultaneously treat electromagnetic and strong isospin violation, enabling systematic calculations for neutral pion photoproduction (relevant to experiments at MAMI and SAL) and for resolving claims by Gibbs et al. (1995) and Matsinos (1997) that existing pion-nucleon data exhibit isospin violation at the few percent level. The authors note that a complete fourth-order calculation is needed for high precision.
