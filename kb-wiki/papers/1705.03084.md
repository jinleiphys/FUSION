# Calculations of Shear, Bulk viscosities and Electrical conductivity in Polyakov-Quark-Meson model

> We have evaluated the transport coefficients of quark and hadronic matter in the frame work of Polyakov-Quark-Meson model. The thermal widths of quarks and mesons, which inversely control the strength of these transport coefficients, are obtained from the imaginary part of their respective self-energies at finite temperature. Due to the threshold conditions of their self energies, some limited temperature regions of quark and hadronic phase become relevant for our numerical predictions on transport coefficients, which are grossly in agreement with earlier results.

## Key claim

The authors calculate shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and electrical conductivity for quark and hadronic matter using the Polyakov-Quark-Meson model, but the numerical predictions are restricted to limited temperature regions of each phase due to threshold conditions in the self-energies of quarks and mesons, and the results are only "grossly in agreement" with earlier work rather than providing precise quantitative matches.

## Method

The authors evaluate transport coefficients using the Kubo formalism within the two-flavor Polyakov-Quark-Meson (PQM) model at zero baryon chemical potential. Thermal widths of quarks and mesons, which control the relaxation times, are obtained from the imaginary parts of their one-loop self-energies at finite temperature, corresponding to 1-to-2 inelastic scattering processes (e.g., Q to QM and M to MM). The thermodynamic potential is treated in mean-field approximation for mesons and Polyakov loop fields, while retaining quantum and thermal fluctuations of quarks, with meson masses determined from the curvature of the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity near transition temperature from different models varies widely: approximately 0.001 GeV^3 (Itakura, Weise1), 0.002-0.003 GeV^3 (Nicola, Prakash, SSS), and 0.4 GeV^3 (Dobado).
* Bulk viscosity values from different model calculations range from 10^{-5} GeV^3 (Sarkar) to 10^{-2} GeV^3 (Redlich_NPA).
* The ratio sigma/T for electrical conductivity ranges approximately from 10^{-3} to 10^{-2} for hadronic phase and 10^{-3} to 10^{-1} for quark phase across different studies.
* The model parameters are fixed using vacuum values: pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV, sigma mass m_sigma = 600 MeV, constituent quark mass M_Q = 300 MeV, leading to lambda = 19.7 and Yukawa coupling g = 3.3.
* The Polyakov loop potential uses T_0 = 192 MeV for two flavors.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of transport coefficients in the PQM model by Singha et al. (HM_PQM2) that used 2-to-2 scattering processes, replacing them with 1-to-2 inelastic diagrams from self-energies. It connects to a large body of work on shear viscosity (Arnold, Nills, Meyer_eta for quark matter; Itakura, Dobado, Nicola, Weise1 for hadronic matter), bulk viscosity (Arnold_bulk for HTL; Redlich_NPA, Marty, Deb for NJL model; Purnendu, Paech1 for linear sigma model), and electrical conductivity (Lattice QCD by Ding, Arts, Buividovich; transport codes by Cassing, Puglisi, Greif). The authors note that quark-meson interactions near the transition temperature may help produce the near-perfect fluid behavior suggested by RHIC and LHC experiments, and that inelastic scatterings in specific temperature ranges can contribute to this perfect fluid nature.
