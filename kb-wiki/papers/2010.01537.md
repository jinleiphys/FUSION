# Second order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics within an effective description of hot QCD medium

> The second-order hydrodynamic equations for evolution of shear and bulk viscous pressure have been derived within the framework of covariant kinetic theory based on the effective fugacity quasiparticle model. The temperature-dependent fugacity parameter in the equilibrium distribution function leads to a mean field term in the Boltzmann equation which affects the interactions in the hot QCD matter. The viscous corrections to distribution function, up to second-order in gradient expansion, have been obtained by employing a Chapman-Enskog like iterative solution of the effective Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation. The effect of mean field contributions to transport coefficients as well as entropy current has been studied up to second-order in gradients. In contrast to the previous calculations, we find non-vanishing entropy flux at second order. The effective description of relativistic second-order viscous hydrodynamics, for a system of interacting quarks and gluons, has been quantitatively analyzed in the case of the $1+1-$dimensional boost invariant longitudinal expansion. We study the proper time evolution of temperature, pressure anisotropy, and viscous corrections to entropy density for this simplified expansion. The second order evolution of quark-gluon plasma is seen to be affected significantly with the inclusion of mean field contributions and the realistic equation of state.

## Key claim

The authors derive second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamic equations for shear and bulk viscous pressure within an effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) of hot QCD matter, finding that the temperature-dependent fugacity introduces a mean field term in the Boltzmann equation that significantly affects the second-order evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. In contrast to previous calculations with vanishing chemical potential, the authors report a non-vanishing entropy flux at second order, which arises purely from the effective fugacity and vanishes in the ideal equation-of-state limit.

## Method

The authors employ a Chapman-Enskog-like iterative solution of the effective Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation (RTA) to obtain viscous corrections to the distribution function up to second order in gradient expansion. The thermal QCD medium effects are incorporated via the EQPM, where a temperature-dependent fugacity parameter in the equilibrium distribution function encodes the lattice QCD equation of state for (2+1) flavors. The second-order evolution equations for shear stress tensor and bulk viscous pressure are derived from the covariant kinetic theory, and the resulting transport coefficients are expressed in terms of thermodynamic integrals involving polylogarithm functions. The formalism is then applied to the 1+1-dimensional boost-invariant Bjorken expansion to study the proper time evolution of temperature, pressure anisotropy, and viscous corrections to entropy density.

## Key numbers

* Initial conditions for Bjorken evolution: initial temperature \(T_0 = 600\) MeV at initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/c, corresponding to LHC initial conditions.
* Relaxation time taken as constant and equal for both shear and bulk parts: \(\tau_\pi = \tau_\Pi = \tau_R = 0.25\) fm/c.
* Degeneracy factors: gluon degeneracy \(g_g = N_s \times (N_c^2 - 1)\) and quark degeneracy \(g_q = 2 \times N_s \times N_c \times N_f\), with \(N_s = 2\), \(N_f = 3\), \(N_c = 3\) (working at zero baryon chemical potential).

## Context

This work extends the first-order dissipative hydrodynamic analysis within the EQPM by Bhadury et al. (2019) to second order in gradients, and complements an earlier second-order study of shear tensor within the EQPM using Grad's 14-moment method by Mitra et al. (2019). The results reduce to those of Florkowski et al. (2015) in the non-interacting ideal case where the fugacity parameter \(z_k \rightarrow 1\) and the energy shift \(\delta\omega_k \rightarrow 0\). The formalism enables more realistic modeling of QGP evolution in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating the realistic lattice QCD equation of state and mean field contributions into second-order causal relativistic hydrodynamics.
