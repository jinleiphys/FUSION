# Rapidity dependence of initial state geometry and momentum correlations in p+Pb collisions

> Event geometry and initial state correlations have been invoked as possible explanations of long range azimuthal correlations observed in high multiplicity p+p and p+Pb collisions. We study the rapidity dependence of initial state momentum correlations and event-by-event geometry in $\sqrt{s}=5.02~\rm{TeV}$ p+Pb collisions within the 3+1D IP-Glasma model~\cite{Schenke:2016ksl}, where the longitudinal structure is governed by JIMWLK rapidity evolution of the incoming nuclear gluon distributions. We find that the event geometry is correlated across large rapidity intervals whereas initial state momentum correlations are relatively short range in rapidity. Based on our results, we discuss implications for the relevance of both effects in explaining the origin of collective phenomena in small systems.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in 5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions simulated with the 3+1D IP-Glasma model, the event geometry (eccentricities) is correlated across large rapidity intervals, whereas initial state momentum correlations are relatively short range in rapidity; this distinction may help experimentally separate initial-state from final-state contributions to collective phenomena in small systems.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D classical Yang-Mills simulations using the IP-Glasma model with JIMWLK rapidity evolution of Wilson lines for the proton and lead nucleus, starting from initial conditions at rapidity Y=-2.4 and evolving to Y=+2.4 in steps of 0.2. They generated 4096 events from 32 proton and 8 lead configurations with 16 impact parameters each, classified into centrality bins by gluon multiplicity at mid-rapidity, and computed eccentricities, momentum anisotropies, and their correlation functions as functions of rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Gluon multiplicity at mid-rapidity for the 0-5% centrality class: g² dN_g/dy = 141.1 (m=0.2 GeV) and 152.3 (m=0.8 GeV)
- Gluon multiplicity at mid-rapidity for the 80-90% centrality class: g² dN_g/dy = 9.2 (m=0.2 GeV) and 16.6 (m=0.8 GeV)
- Transverse energy per unit rapidity at mid-rapidity for 0-5% centrality: g² dE⊥/dy = 457.1 GeV (m=0.2 GeV) and 697.1 GeV (m=0.8 GeV)
- Transverse energy per unit rapidity at mid-rapidity for 80-90% centrality: g² dE⊥/dy = 20.1 GeV (m=0.2 GeV) and 66.2 GeV (m=0.8 GeV)
- The simulation used a transverse lattice of 1024×1024 sites with spacing a_s = 0.02 fm, and classical Yang-Mills evolution was run up to τ = 0.2 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the 3+1D IP-Glasma model of Schenke (2016) to study the rapidity dependence of initial-state correlations, relaxing the boost-invariance assumption used in earlier calculations such as Schenke (2019) and Giacalone (2020). The results provide input for distinguishing initial-state Glasma correlations from final-state hydrodynamic response in small collision systems, and can be compared to experimental measurements of rapidity decorrelation in p+Pb collisions at the LHC.
