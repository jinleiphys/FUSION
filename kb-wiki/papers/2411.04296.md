# Cluster and anti-cluster production in heavy-ion collisions and pA reactions

> We investigate light cluster and anti-cluster production in heavy-ion collisions from SIS to RHIC energies within the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) microscopic transport approach which propagates (anti-)baryons using n-body QMD dynamics. In PHQMD the clusters are formed dynamically by potential interactions between baryons - and recognized by the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithm - as well as by kinetic reactions in case of deuterons. We present the novel PHQMD results for different observables such as excitation functions of the multiplicity of deuterons, anti-deuterons and tritons, as well as their transverse momentum spectra. Moreover, we investigate the system size dependence of proton and deuteron production in p+A collisions and show the PHQMD results for p+A collisions (A = Be, Al, Cu, Au) at 14 AGeV/c, as well as for asymmetric Au+A collisions (A = Al, Cu, Pb) at a bombarding energy of about 10 AGeV.

## Key claim

The PHQMD transport approach reproduces experimental data for light cluster and anti-cluster production across a wide energy range (SIS to RHIC) and for various system sizes, with the dominant production mechanism at midrapidity being potential interactions between baryons (identified by the aMST algorithm) rather than kinetic reactions, for both symmetric and asymmetric collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) microscopic n-body transport model, which propagates baryons using QMD dynamics and incorporates partonic and hadronic degrees of freedom from the PHSD approach. Clusters were formed dynamically via potential interactions between baryons and identified by the advanced Minimum Spanning Tree (aMST) algorithm, which selects bound clusters with negative binding energies; additionally, a kinetic mechanism for deuterons was included via catalytic hadronic reactions (πNN ↔ πd, NNN ↔ Nd) with quantum effects modeled by finite-size excluded volume and momentum-space projection onto the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron, anti-deuteron, and triton midrapidity yields (dN/dy) were computed for 0-10% central Au+Au collisions across √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV, reproducing STAR collaboration data
- Transverse momentum distributions for deuterons and anti-deuterons were shown at √sNN = 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV for central Au+Au collisions
- Proton and deuteron rapidity distributions were calculated for p+Be, p+Al, p+Cu, and p+Au collisions at 14.6 AGeV/c, compared to E802 Collaboration data
- Proton and deuteron rapidity distributions were calculated for Au+Al and Au+Cu collisions at Ebeam = 9.9 AGeV and Au+Pb collisions at 10.6 AGeV, compared to E878 and E864 Collaboration data

## Context

This work extends the PHQMD approach (Aichelin 2019, Glässel 2021, Kireyeu 2022, Coci 2023) to systematically compare cluster and anti-cluster production with experimental data from STAR, E802, E878, and E864 collaborations across multiple collision systems and energies. The results are consistent with UrQMD calculations combined with a coalescence model (Sombun 2018) for the excitation functions, and demonstrate that the potential mechanism dominates deuteron production at midrapidity for all studied systems, while the kinetic mechanism becomes important in the target region for p+A collisions. This enables future studies of cluster production mechanisms in asymmetric systems and provides a benchmark for understanding how weakly bound objects form in hot, dense nuclear environments.
