# In-medium omega meson mass and quark condensate in a Nambu Jona-Lasinio model constrained by recent experimental data

> We have determined the relation between the in-medium $ω$ meson mass and quark condensate in the framework of a Nambu Jona-Lasinio model constrained by some recent experimental data on the meson properties in nuclei. In addition to the usual four-quark interactions, we have included eight-quark terms in the Lagrangian. The parameters of this model have been determined using the meson properties in the vacuum as well as in the medium. More particularly, we have constrained both the in-medium pion decay constant to the value measured in experiments on deeply bound pionic atoms and the in-medium $ω$ meson mass to the experimental value obtained either by the TAPS collaboration or by the E325 experiment at KEK. Our results are compared to several scaling laws and in particular to that of Brown and Rho.

# In-medium omega meson mass and quark condensate in a Nambu Jona-Lasinio model constrained by recent experimental data

## Key claim

The relation between the in-medium omega meson mass and the quark condensate depends on which experimental constraint is used: the TAPS data approximately recover the Brown-Rho scaling law (mass proportional to the square root of the condensate), while the KEK/E325 data favor a scaling with the third root of the condensate. Both cases produce a drop of the omega meson mass at saturation density close to 10%, which is lower than the 15-25% decrease typically found in QCD sum rule calculations.

## Method

The authors used a two-flavor Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with four-quark and eight-quark interaction terms, solving the gap equation for the dynamical constituent quark mass and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the omega meson mass in the quark-antiquark channel. The six model parameters (cutoff, bare quark mass, and four coupling constants) were fixed using vacuum meson properties (pion mass 135 MeV, pion decay constant 92.4 MeV, omega mass 782 MeV) plus two in-medium constraints: the in-medium pion decay constant ratio f*²/f² = 0.64 at saturation density from deeply bound pionic atoms, and the in-medium omega mass from either the TAPS collaboration (722 MeV at 0.6 times saturation density) or the E325 experiment at KEK (711 MeV at saturation density). The regularization used a three-momentum cutoff, and the constituent quark mass in vacuum was varied between 400 and 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- In-medium omega meson mass from TAPS collaboration: m*_omega = 722^(+4)_(-4) (stat) ^(+35)_(-5) (syst) MeV at 0.6 times nuclear saturation density.
- In-medium omega meson mass from E325/KEK experiment: 9% decrease of the omega mass at saturation density (central value 711 MeV at rho_0).
- In-medium pion decay constant ratio from deeply bound pionic atoms: f*²/f² = 0.64 at saturation density.
- Vacuum omega meson mass: m_omega = 782 MeV.
- Vacuum pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- Vacuum pion mass: m_pi = 135 MeV.
- Quark condensate in vacuum from the model: <u-bar u>^(1/3) = -240 MeV, compared to lattice result -(231 ± 4 ± 8 ± 6) MeV.
- For the TAPS parametrization (m_vac = 450 MeV): Lambda = 575 MeV, m_0 = 5.6 MeV, g1 Lambda² = 2.53, g2 Lambda² = 5.20, g3 Lambda^8 = 62.5, g4 Lambda^8 = 2.27.
- For the KEK parametrization (m_vac = 450 MeV): Lambda = 575 MeV, m_0 = 5.6 MeV, g1 Lambda² = 2.58, g2 Lambda² = 4.12, g3 Lambda^8 = 12.4, g4 Lambda^8 = 1.22.
- Drop of omega meson mass at saturation density: 9% for KEK, 12% for TAPS.
- Critical density from the model: close to four times saturation density.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations of in-medium vector meson masses (Bernard and Meissner, 1988) by including eight-quark interaction terms and by enforcing constraints from recent experimental data on deeply bound pionic atoms (Suzuki et al., 2004) and on omega meson photoproduction (TAPS collaboration, Trnka et al., 2005) and proton-nucleus reactions (E325/KEK, Naruki et al., 2006). The results are compared to the Brown-Rho scaling law (Brown and Rho, 2004) derived from the vector manifestation of chiral symmetry in hidden local symmetry theory, and to finite-density QCD sum rule calculations (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992; Jin and Leinweber, 1995; Klingl, Kaiser and Weise, 1997) which generally predict a larger 15-25% drop of the omega mass at saturation density. The findings enable more precise tests of the relation between chiral symmetry restoration and vector meson mass modification in nuclear matter.
