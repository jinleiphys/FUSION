# Chiral-Imbalance Density Wave in Baryonic Matters

> We propose a new chirality-imbalance phenomenon arising in baryonic/high dense matters under a magnetic field. A locally chiral-imbalanced (parity-odd) domain can be created due to the electromagnetically induced $U(1)_A$ anomaly in high-dense matters. The proposed local-chiral imbalance generically possesses a close relationship to a spacial distribution of an inhomogeneous chiral (pion)-vector current coupled to the magnetic field. To demonstrate such a nontrivial correlation, we take the skyrmion crystal approach to model baryonic/high dense matters. Remarkably enough, we find the chirality-imbalance distribution takes a wave form in a high density region (dobbed ``chiral-imbalance density wave''), when the inhomogeneous chiral condensate develops to form a chiral density wave. This implies the emergence of a nontrivial density wave for the explicitly broken $U(1)_A$ current simultaneously with the chiral density wave for the spontaneously broken chiral-flavor current. We further find that the topological phase transition in the skyrmion crystal model (between skyrmion and half-skyrmion phases) undergoes the deformation of the chiral-imbalance density wave in shape and periodicity. The emergence of this chiral-imbalance density wave could give a crucial contribution to studies on the chiral phase transition, as well as the nuclear matter structure, in compact stars under a magnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a local chirality imbalance (parity-odd domain) can arise in high-density baryonic matter under a magnetic field through the electromagnetic \(U(1)_A\) anomaly, and they demonstrate that this imbalance takes the form of a spatial wave, dubbed a "chiral-imbalance density wave", when an inhomogeneous chiral condensate (chiral density wave) develops, but only within the specific framework of the skyrmion crystal model.

## Method

The authors use the skyrmion crystal approach to model baryonic/high-density matter, constructing a face-centered-cubic (FCC) crystal from static pion fields expanded in Fourier series. They introduce a constant magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis via a symmetric gauge and a chiral chemical potential \(\mu_5\) into the chiral Lagrangian, then derive the chiral imbalance density \(\rho_5\) from the covariantized Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term. The Fourier coefficients of the skyrmion configuration are determined by minimizing the per-baryon energy for a given crystal size \(L\) and magnetic field scale \(\sqrt{eB}\), using \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\) and \(g = 5.93\) as inputs.

## Key numbers

- Crystal size \(L = 2.0\ \text{fm}\) (low density, skyrmion phase) and \(L = 1.0\ \text{fm}\) (high density, half-skyrmion phase) are used for numerical analysis.
- Magnetic field scales \(\sqrt{eB} = 400\ \text{MeV}\) and \(800\ \text{MeV}\) are applied to both crystal sizes.
- The magnitude of the chiral imbalance density \(\rho_5\) is amplified by a factor of 10 in the figures due to its smallness compared to the baryon number density \(\rho_B\).
- The space-averaged value of \(\rho_5\) over the crystal cube is zero: \(\int_{\text{cube}} d^3 x \rho_5 = 0\).

## Context

This work extends studies of the \(U(1)_A\) anomaly in hot QCD matter (e.g., Kharzeev 2001, Fukushima 2008) to cold, high-density environments, where gluonic topological configurations are suppressed, by instead using the electromagnetic anomaly. It builds on prior skyrmion crystal analyses of inhomogeneous chiral condensates (Kawaguchi 2018) and topological phase transitions between skyrmion and half-skyrmion phases (Lee 2003, Ma 2013). The results enable future investigations of chiral phase transitions and nuclear matter structure in compact stars under magnetic fields, linking the \(U(1)_A\) anomaly to spontaneously broken chiral symmetry.
