# Kaon Condensation in Dense Stellar Matter

> This article combines two talks given by the authors and is based on works done in collaboration with G.E. Brown and D.-P. Min on kaon condensation in dense baryonic medium treated in chiral perturbation theory using heavy-baryon formalism. It contains, in addition to what was recently published, astrophysical backgrounds for kaon condensation discussed by Brown and Bethe, a discussion on a renormalization-group analysis to meson condensation worked out together with H.K. Lee and S.-J. Sin, and the recent results of K. M. Westerberg in the bound-state approach to the Skyrme model. Negatively charged kaons are predicted to condense at a critical density $2\lsim ρ/ρ_0\lsim 4$, in the range to allow the intriguing new phenomena predicted by Brown and Bethe to take place in compact star matter.

## Key claim

Negatively charged kaons are predicted to condense in dense stellar matter at a critical density in the range $2\lsim \rho/\rho_0 \lsim 4$, but this result depends on the assumption that nuclear matter can be described as a Fermi-liquid fixed point and that the parameters of the chiral Lagrangian can be fixed from kaon-nucleon scattering data, with the critical density being "extremely robust against changes in the parameters."

## Method

The authors use heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to next-to-next-to-leading (NNL) order, computing kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes to one-loop order and fixing the Lagrangian parameters from experimental scattering lengths for $K^+p$, $K^-p$, $K^+n$, and $K^-n$ channels. They then apply a renormalization-group analysis to study instability in the strangeness direction of dense nuclear matter, and calculate the critical density for kaon condensation by examining the kaon dispersion relation near the electron chemical potential, assuming charge neutrality and that the kaon chemical potential equals the electron chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for kaon condensation: $2\lsim \rho/\rho_0 \lsim 4$, where $\rho_0$ is normal nuclear matter density
- Kaplan-Nelson tree-order prediction: critical density $2.3 \rho_0 \le \rho_c \le 3 \rho_0$
- Kaon mass in free space: $\sim 495$ MeV
- Experimental scattering lengths: $a_0^{K^+p} = -0.31$ fm, $a_0^{K^-p} = -0.67 + i 0.63$ fm, $a_0^{K^+n} = -0.20$ fm, $a_0^{K^-n} = +0.37 + i 0.57$ fm
- One-loop finite contributions: $L_s M_K \approx -0.109$ fm, $L_v M_K \approx +0.021$ fm
- Determined parameters: $\bar d_s \approx 0.201$ fm, $\bar d_v \approx 0.013$ fm, $\bar g_s M_K \approx 0.008$ fm, $\bar g_v M_K \approx 0.002$ fm
- Maximum neutron star mass with kaon condensate equation of state: $< 1.5 M_\odot$
- Core mass range for SN1987A: $1.44 M_\odot < M_{core} < 1.56 M_\odot$
- Ratio of helium to metal enrichment: $\Delta Y/\Delta Z = 4 \pm 1.3$

## Context

This work extends the original Kaplan-Nelson (1986) prediction of kaon condensation in neutron matter by going beyond tree-order chiral perturbation theory to next-to-next-to-leading order, confirming their result while uncovering new physical elements. It provides the theoretical basis for the Brown-Bethe (1994) scenario for low-mass black hole formation, where kaon condensation softens the nuclear equation of state enough to lower the maximum neutron star mass below $1.5 M_\odot$, explaining the observed narrow range of neutron star masses and the possible delayed collapse of SN1987A into a black hole. The renormalization-group analysis follows Shankar (1994) and Polchinski (1992) and shows that pion condensation is unlikely at low density, contrary to earlier work by Maxwell et al. (1977), making kaon condensation the leading candidate for the required phase transition.
