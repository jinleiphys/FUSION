# Diquarks: condensation without bound states

> We employ a bispinor gap equation to study superfluidity at nonzero chemical potential: mu .neq. 0, in two- and three-colour QCD. The two-colour theory, QC2D, is an excellent exemplar: the order of truncation of the quark-quark scattering kernel: K, has no qualitative impact, which allows a straightforward elucidation of the effects of mu when the coupling is strong. In rainbow-ladder truncation, diquark bound states appear in the spectrum of the three-colour theory, a defect that is eliminated by an improvement of K. The corrected gap equation describes a superfluid phase that is semi-quantitatively similar to that obtained using the rainbow truncation. A model study suggests that the width of the superfluid gap and the transition point in QC2D provide reliable quantitative estimates of those quantities in QCD.

## Key claim

Diquark condensation (superfluidity) can occur in three-colour QCD even when the quark-quark scattering kernel is improved beyond rainbow-ladder truncation to eliminate unphysical diquark bound states, and the resulting superfluid phase is semi-quantitatively similar to that obtained using the defective rainbow truncation.

## Method

The authors employed a bispinor Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) to study superfluidity at nonzero chemical potential in both two-colour (QC2D) and three-colour QCD, using a confining model for the dressed gluon propagator (a contact interaction proportional to a delta function in momentum space). For three-colour QCD, they solved the gap equation in rainbow truncation and then at next order by including a 1/Nc-suppressed dressed-ladder vertex correction, comparing the pressure of the quark-condensed and diquark-condensed phases to determine the stable ground state.

## Key numbers

- In the QC2D model at zero chemical potential and zero current-quark mass, the bag constants for quark and diquark condensation are equal: B_B(0) = B_Delta(0) = (0.092 eta_hat)^4 = (0.13 m_{J=1})^4.
- In the QC2D model, chiral symmetry restoration in the absence of diquark condensation occurs at mu_{2c}^{B,Delta=0} = 0.28 eta_hat.
- In the QC2D model with nonzero current-quark mass m = 0.013 m_{J=1}, the minimum chemical potential for diquark condensation is mu^{Delta != 0} = 0.051 m_{J=1}; for m = 0.13 m_{J=1}, it is mu^{Delta != 0} = 0.092 m_{J=1}.
- In the rainbow-truncation QCD model, the transition to the superfluid phase occurs at mu_{c, rainbow}^{B=0,Delta} = 0.25 eta = 0.89 mu_{c, rainbow}^{B,Delta=0}.
- In the vertex-corrected QCD model, the transition to the superfluid phase occurs at mu_{c}^{B=0,Delta} = 0.63 mu_{c}^{B,Delta=0}.
- In the vertex-corrected QCD model at the transition, the diquark condensate is (0.51)^3 times the quark condensate at mu=0.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark matter superfluidity that used the rainbow-ladder truncation, which has the defect of producing unobserved diquark bound states (as found by Cahill et al. and others). The authors show that including a 1/Nc-suppressed dressed-ladder vertex correction, following the truncation scheme of Bender, Roberts, and Schmidt (1996), eliminates these bound states while preserving a superfluid phase. The results enable reliable quantitative estimates of the superfluid gap and transition point in QCD by using lattice simulations of QC2D, where the order parameter is gauge invariant and the fermion determinant is real and positive, as suggested by Hands et al.
