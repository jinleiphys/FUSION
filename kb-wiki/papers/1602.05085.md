# Electric Conductivity of a hot hadron gas from a kinetic approach

> We calculate the electric conductivity of a gas of relativistic particles with isotropic cross sections using the Boltzmann equation as the starting point. Our analyses is restricted to elastic collisions. We show the perfect agreement with previously published numerical results for a massless quark- gluon plasma, and give results for the electric conductivity of an interacting hadron gas, employing realistic resonance cross sections. These results for the electric conductivity of a hot hadron gas, as created in (ultra-)relativistic heavy-ion collisions, are of rich phenomenological as well as theoretical interest and can be compared to, e.g., lattice quantum field theory calculations.

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytic expression for the electric conductivity of a hot hadron gas from kinetic theory using the linearized Boltzmann equation with elastic collisions only, showing that the method converges to within about 99% of previously published numerical results for a massless quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation for a multicomponent relativistic gas in the presence of a small static electric field, using a polynomial expansion of the off-equilibrium distribution function in powers of energy truncated at second order. They compute the retarded Green's function at vanishing frequency and wavenumber to extract the static electric conductivity, solving a matrix equation that involves momentum integrals over the linearized collision term for elastic 2-to-2 scatterings with isotropic cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For a massless seven-species gas mimicking a quark-gluon plasma (charges ±1/3, ±2/3, 0; degeneracies 6 for charged, 16 for neutral; cross section 3 mb), the electric conductivity is σ_el = 0.000832737 GeV² / T, matching numerical BAMPS results to about 99%.
- For a three-species test system (charges +1, -1, 0; degeneracies 1, 1, 9; cross section 10 mb; temperature 140 MeV; chemical potential μ_q = 0), the conductivity depends strongly on the mass ratio of charged to uncharged species.
- The analytic expansion truncated at n=2 shows convergence toward the numerical value for the massless quark-gluon plasma case.

## Context

This work extends analytic kinetic theory calculations of transport coefficients (e.g., shear viscosity in Denicol (2013)) to the electric conductivity of a hadron gas, which had previously only been computed numerically via partonic cascades such as BAMPS (Greif, 2014) or via lattice QCD (Aarts, 2014; Brandt, 2013). The results enable direct comparison with lattice calculations and provide input for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the diffusion of magnetic fields (Baym, 1997; Tuchin, 2013) and soft dilepton production rates (Moore, 2003).
