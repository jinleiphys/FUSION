# Chiral $π$-exchange NN-potentials: Results for diagrams proportional to g_A^4 and g_A^6

> We calculate in (two-loop) chiral perturbation theory the local NN-potentials generated by the three-pion exchange diagrams proportional to g_A^4 and g_A^6. Surprisingly, we find that the total isoscalar central $3π$-exchange potential vanishes identically. The individually largest $3π$-exchange potentials are of isoscalar spin-spin, isovector central and isoscalar tensor type. For these potentials simple analytical expressions can be given. The strength of these dominant $3π$-exchange potentials at r=1.0 fm is 4.6 MeV, 2.9 MeV and 1.4 MeV, respectively. Furthermore, we observe that the spin-spin and tensor potentials due to the diagrams proportional to g_A^6 do not exist in the infinite nucleon mass limit.

## Key claim

The total isoscalar central three-pion-exchange potential vanishes identically, contrary to what might be expected from individual diagram contributions; the largest chiral three-pion-exchange potentials are of isoscalar spin-spin, isovector central, and isoscalar tensor type, but they are likely too weak to be of practical relevance in nucleon-nucleon phenomenology.

## Method

The author calculates the local nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials generated by two-loop three-pion-exchange diagrams in chiral perturbation theory, specifically those proportional to the axial-vector coupling constants g_A^4 and g_A^6. The imaginary parts of the NN amplitudes are obtained via Cutkosky cutting rules and integrated over the three-pion phase space in the heavy-nucleon (static) limit, then transformed to coordinate-space potentials using spectral representations with Yukawa functions. Diagrams are grouped into classes V through IX based on their topology and coupling structure, with iterative (reducible) parts subtracted to isolate the irreducible potential contributions.

## Key numbers

* The isoscalar spin-spin potential from class VII at r=1.0 fm is 4.62 MeV (repulsive).
* The isovector central potential from class IX at r=1.0 fm is 2.91 MeV (repulsive).
* The isoscalar tensor potential from class VII at r=1.0 fm is 1.39 MeV (repulsive).
* The isovector spin-spin potential from class V at r=1.0 fm is 1.06 MeV (repulsive).
* The isovector tensor potential from class VI at r=1.0 fm is -0.689 MeV (attractive).
* The isoscalar central potentials from class VIII and class IX cancel exactly at all distances, with values at r=1.0 fm of +3.58 MeV and -3.58 MeV respectively.
* The isovector spin-spin potential from class VI at r=1.0 fm is 0.322 MeV (repulsive).
* The isovector tensor potential from class V at r=1.0 fm is 0.302 MeV (repulsive).

## Context

This work completes the calculation of chiral three-pion-exchange NN potentials begun by Kaiser (1999) for diagrams proportional to g_A^2, which gave potentials of order ±0.1 MeV or less at 1.0 fm. The present results show that the g_A^4 and g_A^6 contributions are larger by factors of 3 to 10, but still much weaker than chiral two-pion-exchange potentials from the earlier work of the same group. The vanishing of the total isoscalar central three-pion-exchange potential implies that only explicit omega-meson exchange (a resonant three-pion state) can provide the phenomenological isoscalar central repulsion needed in NN models. The finding that spin-spin and tensor potentials from g_A^6 diagrams do not exist in the static limit points to a limitation of the heavy-baryon approach when the number of nucleon propagators exceeds a critical value.
