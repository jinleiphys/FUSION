# Recent Theoretical Studies on Hadronic Atoms

> A report contains a review of recent theoretical investigations on kaonic atoms carried out at Stefan Meyer Institute of the Austrian Academy of Sciences. We discuss (1) a phenomenological quantum field theoretic model for bar-K N interactions at threshold of the reactions K^-p -> K^-p, K^-n -> K^-n and K^-d ->K^-d, and (2) the energy level displacements of the ground and nP excited states of kaonic hydrogen, (3) the contribution of the sigma-term to the shift of the energy level of the ground state of kaonic hydrogen, (4) the isospin-breaking and dispersive corrections to the energy level displacement of the ground state of kaonic hydrogen, (5) the radiative transitions nP -> 1S + γ, induced by strong low-energy interactions and enhanced by the Coulomb interaction, in kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium, (6) Perspectives and (7) Comments on the approach, where we adduce our recent results obtained after the Workshop EXA05.

# Recent Theoretical Studies on Hadronic Atoms

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological quantum field theoretic model for low-energy $\bar{K}N$ interactions that successfully describes the energy level displacements of kaonic hydrogen and deuterium, but the model's predictions depend on specific assumptions about the treatment of baryon resonances (particularly $\Lambda(1405)$, $\Lambda(1800)$, and $\Sigma(1750)$) and exotic scalar mesons, with the authors later revising their scattering length values to satisfy a low-energy theorem relating $K^-N$ and $\pi^-N$ scattering.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological Effective Field Theory with Effective Chiral Lagrangians, treating baryon resonances and exotic scalar mesons as elementary particles represented by local interpolating fields. They calculate complex S-wave scattering lengths for $\bar{K}N$ scattering with isospin $I=0$ and $I=1$, then apply the Deser-Goldberger-Baumann-Thirring-Trueman (DGBTT) formula to obtain energy level displacements of kaonic hydrogen and deuterium ground states. The imaginary parts of scattering amplitudes are determined from experimental cross sections for inelastic reactions $K^-p \to Y\pi$ and two-body production rates $K^-d \to NY$, while real parts receive contributions from strange baryon resonances in the s-channel and exotic scalar mesons $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ in the t-channel.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Effective Field Theory approach to $\bar{K}N$ interactions developed by the authors (Ivanov et al., 2003-2005), building upon Chiral Perturbation Theory by Gasser and Leutwyler (1983) and the DGBTT formula by Deser et al. (1954). The model successfully describes experimental data from the DEAR Collaboration (2003) for kaonic hydrogen and provides predictions for kaonic deuterium to guide the DEAR/SIDDHARTA experiments at Frascati. The authors compare their results with the KEK Collaboration (1997) data, the Dalitz and Deloff (1991) analysis of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, and the Ericson-Weise formalism for scattering on deuterium. The work enables extraction of the $\sigma_{KN}^{I=1}(0)$-term from future precision measurements and provides a framework for calculating energy level displacements of kaonic helium isotopes and antiprotonic atoms.
