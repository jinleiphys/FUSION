# Dynamics of the conserved net-baryon density near QCD critical point within inhomogeneous quark-gluon plasma profile

> This paper investigates the dynamics of the net-baryon multiplicity fluctuations near the QCD critical point, within the inhomogeneous temperature and baryon chemical potential profile of quark-gluon plasma from the hydrodynamics. The Langevin dynamics of conserved net-baryon density are solved numerically within the temperature and chemical potential profile borrowing from hydrodynamic simulation. It is found that the local systems at different rapidities reach the critical point at different proper times, owing to the inhomogeneous temperature and chemical potential. As a result, it is observed the pronounced enhancement of the magnitude for the net-baryon multiplicity fluctuations with large rapidity acceptance at the freeze-out surface, which is the consequence of the combined effect of critical slowing down and inhomogeneous profile.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the inhomogeneous temperature and baryon chemical potential profile of the quark-gluon plasma from hydrodynamic simulations causes local systems at different rapidities to reach the QCD critical point at different proper times, leading to a pronounced enhancement of net-baryon multiplicity fluctuations at large rapidity acceptance on the freeze-out surface, which the abstract presents as a combined effect of critical slowing down and the inhomogeneous profile.

## Method

The author solves the 1+1-dimensional stochastic diffusion equation (Langevin dynamics) for the conserved net-baryon density in Milne coordinates, using the Saul'yev numerical scheme for unconditional stability. The temperature and chemical potential profiles are extracted from 3+1-dimensional MUSIC hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at 19.6 GeV with 0-5% centrality, with the critical point placed at (T_c, μ_c) = (0.16 GeV, 0.155 GeV). The effective potential includes second-, third-, and fourth-order terms with coefficients containing both regular parts from lattice QCD and singular parts mapped from the three-dimensional Ising model, and the dynamics are evolved from τ₀ = 3 fm until freeze-out at switching energy density e_sw = 0.1 GeV/fm³.

## Key numbers

- Critical point position: (T_c, μ_c) = (0.16 GeV, 0.155 GeV)
- Starting proper time for dynamics: τ₀ = 3 fm
- Switching energy density for freeze-out: e_sw = 0.1 GeV/fm³
- Starting proper time for AMPT initial condition: τ_0I = 0.4 fm
- Starting time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ_0h = 1.2 fm
- Specific shear viscosity: η/s = 0.08
- Specific bulk viscosity: ζ/s = 0.01
- Transverse area set to A = 1 fm² for the 1+1-dimensional evolution
- Second-order susceptibility χ₂ shown in range from 0.1 to 9 GeV² in figures, with white regions corresponding to χ₂ > 9 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the study of conserved net-baryon density dynamics near the QCD critical point, previously investigated by Sakaida (2017) and Pihan (2022) in uniform temperature and chemical potential profiles with Bjorken expansion, to the more realistic inhomogeneous profiles from hydrodynamic simulations. The results show that the inhomogeneous profile significantly affects fluctuations at large rapidity, which is essential for comparison with experimental measurements from the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, where the STAR collaboration has observed preliminary non-monotonic behavior of net-proton multiplicity fluctuations as a function of colliding energy. The work enables future incorporation of these dynamics into full hydrodynamical simulations with additional slow modes as developed by Stephanov (2017) and extension to non-boost-invariant backgrounds for lower collision energies.
