# Probing nuclear bubble configuration by the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions

> It is theoretically and experimentally argued that there may exist bubble or toroid-shaped configurations in some nucleus systems. Based on the nuclear transport model, it is shown that compared with the collision of normal nuclei, there is a depletion of central density of compression with bubble configurations in projectile and target nuclei. This depletion of central compression density may affect some observables in heavy-ion collisions.

# Probing nuclear bubble configuration by the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The π⁻/π⁺ ratio, especially its kinetic energy distribution in head-on collisions at intermediate energies, is sensitive to the presence of bubble configurations in projectile and target nuclei. However, the variation of this ratio can also be caused by other factors such as fragmentation of participant nuclei and inelastic nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections, so an enhancement of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio alone cannot definitively prove the existence of bubble configurations before reducing theoretical uncertainties.

## Method
The author used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with Skyrme-type mean field parametrization and in-medium baryon-baryon cross sections. Bubble nuclei were constructed by conserving volume: for a compact nucleus of radius R, a bubble nucleus with inner bubble radius R_bubble has outer radius R_b given by R_b³ = R³ + R_bubble³. Simulations were performed for head-on collisions of the fictitious super-heavy nucleus ⁹⁰⁰₂₇₄X at a beam energy of 0.4 GeV/nucleon, with bubble radii R_bubble = 0 (normal), R/3, and R/2.

## Key numbers
- The effect of bubble configurations on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio reaches about 23% for R_bubble = R/3 and about 68% for R_bubble = R/2, compared to normal nuclei collisions.
- For energetic pion mesons (from denser matter), the bubble effects on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio reach about 70% for R_bubble = R/3 and about 300% for R_bubble = R/2.
- The beam energy used was 0.4 GeV/nucleon for ⁹⁰⁰₂₇₄X + ⁹⁰⁰₂₇₄X head-on collisions.
- The ground-state compressibility coefficient of nuclear matter was set to K = 230 MeV, with symmetry energy at saturation density of 31.6 MeV.
- Two symmetry energy stiffness parameters were used: γ = 0.3 (soft) and γ = 1.5 (stiff).

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of bubble and toroid-shaped nuclear configurations dating back to Wilson (1946) and Siemens and Bethe (1967), and more recent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Decharge et al. (1999, 2003). It builds on transport model simulations of nuclear collisions at lower beam energies by Bauer (1992), Li and Bauer (1992, 1993), Xu (1993), Zhang (2014), and Yong (2015). The method is motivated by experimental data from Najman et al. (2015) on the ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au reaction at 23 MeV/nucleon, which suggested possible toroid-shaped objects. The work proposes the π⁻/π⁺ ratio as a new observable to probe bubble configurations, complementing electronic hyperfine configuration measurements of nuclear density profiles suggested by Decharge (2003), Suda (2009), and Antognini (2011).
