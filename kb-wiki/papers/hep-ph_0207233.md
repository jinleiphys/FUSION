# Spin Structure Functions in Chiral Quark Soliton Models

> In this talk I review studies of hadron structure functions in bosonized chiral quark models. Such models require regularization and I show that the two--fold Pauli--Villars regularization scheme not only fully regularizes the effective action but also leads the scaling laws for structure functions. This scheme is consistent with other computations of the pion structure function in that model. For the nucleon structure functions the present approach serves to determine the regularization prescription for structure functions whose leading moments are not given by matrix elements of local operators. Some numerical results are presented for the spin structure functions and the role of strange quarks is addressed.

## Key claim

The two-fold Pauli-Villars regularization scheme fully regularizes the effective action of the bosonized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model and preserves the scaling laws for structure functions, but numerical results for the full structure functions including the properly regularized vacuum piece are not yet available; the presented numerical results rely on the valence quark contribution as a reliable approximation because the axial charges are saturated to 95% or more by that contribution.

## Method

The author computes hadron structure functions from the forward virtual Compton amplitude in the bosonized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, using a two-fold Pauli-Villars regularization that separates the functional into γ5-even and γ5-odd pieces. For the nucleon, soliton solutions of the meson action are obtained from the hedgehog ansatz, and states with good spin and isospin are generated by quantizing the collective rotational coordinates. The structure functions are extracted in the Bjorken limit, transformed to the infinite momentum frame, and evolved via leading-order DGLAP evolution from a model scale of Q²₀ = 0.4 GeV² to the experimental scale.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: fπ = 93 MeV, used to fix model parameters.
- Pion mass: mπ = 138 MeV, used to fix model parameters.
- In the chiral limit (mπ = 0), the pion structure function F(x) = 5/9 for 0 ≤ x ≤ 1 at the model scale.
- The low energy scale at which the model approximates QCD is estimated as Q²₀ = 0.4 GeV² from a best fit to experimental data for the unpolarized structure function F₂(x).
- The strange quark contribution to the nucleon spin structure function g₁(x) is shown at Q² = 3 GeV² after evolution from the model scale.

## Context

This work extends studies of hadron structure functions in bosonized chiral quark models, building on the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model (Nambu, 1961) and the large-Nc picture of QCD (’t Hooft, 1974). It differs from approaches by Diakonov et al. (1996), Wakamatsu and Tsutsui (1998), and Wakamatsu (2000), which assumed model predictions for constituent quark distributions could be directly identified with QCD quark distributions, whereas the present work computes structure functions from the gauged meson action. The results enable comparison with experimental data from SLAC-E143 (Abe et al., 1998) and E155 (Anthony et al., 2002), and the approach can be generalized to three flavors to study strangeness contributions to nucleon spin structure.
