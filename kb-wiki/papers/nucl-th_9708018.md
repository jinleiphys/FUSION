# The Skyrme Energy Functional and Naturalness

> Recent studies show that successful relativistic mean-field models of nuclei are consistent with naive dimensional analysis and naturalness, as expected in low-energy effective field theories of quantum chromodynamics. The nonrelativistic Skyrme energy functional is found to have similar characteristics.

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic Skyrme energy functional, when its coefficients are rescaled using naive dimensional analysis (NDA) with the pion-decay constant and a scale around the nucleon mass, yields dimensionless coefficients of order unity, indicating naturalness similar to that previously found in relativistic mean-field models. The authors caution that this result is not obvious a priori because many-body correlation effects absorbed into the parameters by fits to nuclei could disrupt the power counting.

## Method

The authors applied Georgi and Manohar's naive dimensional analysis to the Skyrme energy density H(r) for N=Z nuclei, rewriting the conventional Skyrme parameters (t0, t1, t2, t3, W0) into dimensionless coefficients c_i using the pion-decay constant fπ ≈ 93 MeV and a scale Λ taken as the nucleon mass M = 939 MeV. They then computed these dimensionless coefficients for six standard Skyrme parameter sets (Skyrme 1 through 6) and compared the resulting values to the naturalness expectation of order unity.

## Key numbers

- The pion-decay constant used for scaling: fπ ≈ 93 MeV
- The scale Λ was taken as the nucleon mass M = 939 MeV (with a range 500 MeV < Λ < 1000 MeV considered)
- The dimensionless coefficient c_1 = 0.5 for all six Skyrme forces (arising from the nucleon kinetic energy scale)
- The "worst case" dimensionless coefficient was c_4 = 5.53 for Skyrme 5; all other coefficients across all six forces were between -2.89 and 3.97
- The expansion parameter ρ₀/fπ²Λ for nuclear matter at saturation density lies between 1/4 and 1/7
- The dimensionless expansion parameter for gradients in finite nuclei is 1/Λσ ≤ 1/5, where σ is the nuclear surface thickness
- The total binding energy at saturation density is ε₀ = 16.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the naturalness analysis previously applied to relativistic point-coupling models by Friar, Madland, and Lynn (1996) and to relativistic meson-nucleon models by Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997). It shows that the long-established nonrelativistic Skyrme approach, originally proposed by Skyrme (1956) and developed by Vautherin and Brink (1972) and Negele and Vautherin (1972), is consistent with QCD effective field theory expectations. The results suggest that current Skyrme models are truncated prematurely and encourage further application of effective field theory methods to finite-density nuclear systems, including consideration of all possible nonredundant terms organized according to NDA.
