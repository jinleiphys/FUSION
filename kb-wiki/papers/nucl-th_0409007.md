# Microscopic study of 240Pu, mean-field and beyond

> The influence of exact angular-momentum projection and configuration mixing on properties of a heavy, well-deformed nucleus is discussed for the example of Pu240. Starting from a self-consistent model using Skyrme interactions, we analyze the resulting modifications of the deformation energy, the fission barrier height, the excitation energy of the superdeformed minimum associated with the fission isomer, the structure of the lowest rotational bands with normal deformation and superdeformation, and the corresponding quadrupole moments and transition moments. We present results obtained with the Skyrme interactions SLy4 and SLy6, which have slightly different surface tensions.

# Microscopic study of 240Pu, mean-field and beyond

## Key claim

Exact angular-momentum projection and configuration mixing modify the deformation energy, fission barrier height, and excitation energy of the superdeformed fission isomer in 240Pu, but the overall structure of the potential energy curve is not altered from the mean-field approximation. The results depend on the Skyrme interaction used (SLy4 vs SLy6), which differ primarily in their surface tension, and the predicted superdeformed band-head energy falls between the two parameterizations relative to experimental values.

## Method

The authors start from a set of self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HF+BCS) wave functions generated with a constraint on the axial quadrupole moment, using the Skyrme interactions SLy4 and SLy6 with a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction. They then perform exact restoration of particle-number and angular-momentum symmetries, followed by variational configuration mixing of the symmetry-restored states using a discretized version of the generator coordinate method. Spectroscopic quadrupole moments and B(E2) transition probabilities are computed directly in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- Angular-momentum projection on J=0 lowers the total energy by about 3.0 MeV for both SLy4 and SLy6 interactions at spherical shape.
- The fission isomer excitation energy after angular-momentum projection is 3.3 MeV for SLy4 (down from 4.3 MeV) and 1.6 MeV for SLy6 (down from 2.6 MeV).
- The superdeformed 0+ band-head excitation energy is 2.99 MeV with SLy4 and 1.25 MeV with SLy6; experimental values range from 2.25 +/- 0.20 MeV to 2.8 MeV.
- The ground-state 2+ excitation energy is 0.083 MeV for both interactions; experimental value is 42.824 keV.
- The B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) value is 2.80 e^2 b^2 for SLy4 and 2.81 e^2 b^2 for SLy6; experimental value is 26660 +/- 360 e^2 fm^4 (equivalent to 2.666 e^2 b^2).
- The ground-state deformation beta_2 is 0.30 from both spectroscopic and transition quadrupole moments, consistent with the experimental value of 0.29.
- The E0 transition matrix element M(E0; 0+_3 -> 0+_1) in the normal-deformed well is -29 e fm^2 (rho(E0) = -0.52); in the superdeformed well, M(E0; 0+_4 -> 0+_2) is 87 e fm^2 (rho(E0) = 1.6).
- Angular-momentum projection lowers the inner fission barrier by about 0.6 MeV, the fission isomer by about 1 MeV, and the outer barrier by about 2 MeV relative to the ground state.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of fission barriers in actinide nuclei, including calculations with Skyrme forces (Flo74), the Gogny force (Gir83), and relativistic mean-field methods (Blu94a). It also builds on the authors' previous generator-coordinate-method applications to neutron-deficient Pb isotopes (Dug03pb, Ben03pb). The results show that the schematic rotational correction used in mass formulae (Gor03) overestimates the reduction of fission barrier heights by at least 2 MeV compared to exact projection. The study enables future work using cranked mean-field states as a starting point for projection and configuration mixing, which the authors note is currently underway, to improve the description of excited-state energies within rotational bands.
