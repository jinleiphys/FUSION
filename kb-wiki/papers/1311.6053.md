# Discrimination of parametrizations for nuclear effects in neutrino scattering through comparisons of low (~ 700 MeV) and medium (~ 3 GeV) energy cross-section data

> High-quality charged current quasielastic scattering data have recently been reported for both muon neutrinos and antineutrinos from several accelerator-based neutrino experiments. Measurements from MiniBooNE were the first to indicate that more complex nuclear effects, now thought to be the result of nucleon pair correlations, may contribute to neutrino quasielastic samples at a much higher significance than previously assumed. These findings are now being tested by MINER$ν$A and other contemporary neutrino experiments. Presented here is a comparison of data from MiniBooNE and MINER$ν$A to a few example parametrizations of these nuclear effects. It has been demonstrated that such effects may bias future measurements of neutrino oscillation parameters and so this issue continues to press the neutrino community. A comparison of data over a large range of neutrino energies is one approach to exploring the extent to which such nucleon correlations may influence our understanding and subsequent modeling of neutrino quasielastic scattering.

## Key claim
Comparing MiniBooNE (low energy, ~700 MeV) and MINERvA (medium energy, ~3 GeV) charged-current quasielastic scattering data on carbon shows that two different parametrizations of nuclear effects, an increased axial mass and the Transverse Enhancement Model, produce nearly indistinguishable effects at MiniBooNE energies but diverge at MINERvA energies, where the Transverse Enhancement Model is more strongly favored.

## Method
The authors recast MiniBooNE neutrino and antineutrino charged-current quasielastic data into the same normalized ratio form (data divided by a nominal Relativistic Fermi Gas model with axial mass MA = 0.99 GeV) that MINERvA had previously published, using the NuWro event generator for consistency. They compare the shape of the Q²_QE distributions (squared four-momentum transfer reconstructed assuming quasielastic kinematics) for two alternative parametrizations: an increased axial mass parameter and the Transverse Enhancement Model, focusing on the region Q²_QE > 0.2 GeV² to avoid low-momentum-transfer uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- MiniBooNE neutrino energy range: 0.4 < Eν < 2 GeV; MINERvA neutrino energy range: 1.5 < Eν < 10 GeV.
- The nominal Relativistic Fermi Gas model uses axial mass MA = 0.99 GeV.
- The low-Q² cut applied to all comparisons is Q²_QE > 0.2 GeV², corresponding to momentum transfer q < 450 MeV.
- The separation energy EB used in neutrino energy reconstruction is 34 MeV for neutrinos and 30 MeV for antineutrinos on carbon.

## Context
This work extends the comparison of MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arevalo et al., 2010, 2013) and MINERvA data (Fiuza et al., 2013) on charged-current quasielastic scattering, which had previously shown that simple Relativistic Fermi Gas models fail to describe the data. The paper demonstrates that comparing data across different energy regimes can discriminate between competing parametrizations of nuclear effects (increased axial mass versus transverse enhancement from nucleon pair correlations), which is critical because these effects could bias future neutrino oscillation parameter measurements. The approach enables future experiments such as MicroBooNE, NOvA, and T2K to test more sophisticated microscopic models of multi-nucleon knockout mechanisms (Martini et al., Nieves et al.) that are currently being developed.
