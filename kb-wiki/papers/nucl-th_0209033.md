# Spectral Functions of Quarks in Quark Matter

> We present a simple albeit self-consistent approach to the spectral function of light quarks in infinite quark matter. Relations between correlation functions and collision rates are used to calculate the spectral function in an iterative procedure. The quark interactions are described by the SU(2) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Calculations were performed in the chirally restored phase at zero temperature.

## Key claim

The paper presents a self-consistent calculation of light quark spectral functions in infinite quark matter using the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, but only in the chirally restored phase at zero temperature with mean-field effects neglected and only lowest-order Born diagrams considered for collisional self-energies.

## Method

The authors use relations between correlation functions and collision rates to construct an iterative, self-consistent calculation of the quark spectral function beyond the quasiparticle approximation. Quark interactions are described by the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a pointlike interaction, and the collisional self-energies are computed from Born diagrams using full propagators. The calculation is initialized with a constant width of 1 MeV and achieves self-consistency after two iterations.

## Key numbers

- Fermi energy of quark matter at nuclear-matter-like density: ω_F = 0.268 GeV, corresponding to quark matter density ρ_qm = 3 × 0.17 fm⁻³
- Higher-density calculation: ω_F = 0.387 GeV, corresponding to ρ_qm = 1.53 fm⁻³
- Width of spectral function peaks increases from 0.1–1 MeV to 10 MeV when coupling is increased up to four times the standard NJL value
- Depletion of quark momentum distribution at lowest coupling: 0.1%; at twice the coupling: below 1%; only at four times the coupling does depletion reach a few percent
- For ω_F = 0.387 GeV, depletion grows by almost one order of magnitude compared to ω_F = 0.268 GeV

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent approach to spectral functions previously applied to nucleons in nuclear matter by Lehr et al., who used a simple pointlike nucleon interaction with constant scattering amplitude. The authors find that short-range correlation effects on quarks are much smaller than those seen in nuclear matter, which they suggest may be an artifact of using the NJL model with vacuum parameters in the Born approximation. The model is being extended to include the chirally broken phase and dynamically generated mesons via higher-order diagrams.
