# Anomalous pairing vibration in neutron-rich Sn isotopes beyond the N=82 magic number

> Two-neutron transfer associated with the pair correlation in superfluid neutron-rich nuclei is studied with focus on low-lying $0^+$ states in Sn isotopes beyond the N=82 magic number. We describe microscopically the two-neutron addition and removal transitions by means of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field model and the continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation formulated in the coordinate space representation. It is found that the pair transfer strength for the transitions between the ground states becomes significantly large for the isotopes with $A \ge 140$, reflecting very small neutron separation energy and long tails of the weakly bound $3p$ orbits. In $^{132-140}$Sn, a peculiar feature of the pair transfer is seen in transitions to low-lying excited $0^+$ states. They can be regarded as a novel kind of pair vibrational mode which is characterized by an anomalously long tail of the transition density extending to far outside of the nuclear surface, and a large strength comparable to that of the ground-state transitions. The presence of the weakly bound neutron orbits plays a central role for these anomalous behaviors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a novel type of pairing vibrational mode emerges in neutron-rich Sn isotopes with A=132-140 (just beyond the N=82 magic number), characterized by an anomalously long tail of the transition density extending to far outside the nuclear surface and a pair-addition strength comparable to that of the ground-state transitions, which deviates from the conventional picture where pairing vibration strength is significantly smaller than pairing rotation strength.

## Method

The authors employed the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field model with the SLy4 Skyrme parameter set and a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) as the effective pairing force, solved in coordinate space representation. Excited 0+ states were described using the continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with outgoing-wave boundary conditions for isotopes with A >= 132 and box boundary conditions for A < 132. The two-neutron transfer strength functions and transition densities were calculated for both pair-addition and pair-removal modes across the Sn isotopic chain from A=100 to A=150.

## Key numbers

- The pair-addition strength B(Pad0) for the low-lying pairing vibration in 134Sn is 3.16, compared to 1.18 in 102Sn and 0.40 in 120Sn
- The ground-state transition strength B(Pad/rm0) in 134Sn is 3.61, making the pair vibrational strength comparable to the ground-state transition strength
- The excitation energy of the pair vibrational state in 134Sn is 3.73 MeV, above the one-neutron separation energy S1n = 3.31 MeV
- The resonance width of the pair vibrational state in 134Sn is less than 1 keV (FWHM of peak minus smoothing width of 100 keV)
- The transition density for the anomalous pair vibrational mode in 134Sn extends up to r approximately 15 fm, while in 102Sn and 120Sn the amplitude diminishes by r approximately 8 fm
- The root mean square radius of total nucleon density is 4.84 fm in 134Sn and 4.98 fm in 142Sn
- The pair-addition strength B(Pad0) reaches 4.615 in 138Sn and 3.683 in 136Sn
- The unperturbed two-quasiparticle excitations [2f7/2]^2 and [3p3/2]^2 in 134Sn have B(Pad0) = 0.18 and 0.15 respectively, compared to the collective QRPA value of 3.16 (enhancement factor of approximately twenty)
- The Hartree-Fock single-particle energies for neutrons in 132Sn are -1.99 MeV for 2f7/2, -0.25 MeV for 3p3/2, and 0.26 MeV for 3p1/2 (unbound resonance)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pairing collective modes in neutron-rich Sn isotopes by Khan et al. (2009) and Matsuo (2010), providing more systematic and detailed analyses that reveal a novel type of pairing vibration not identified in those earlier studies. The paper contradicts the conventional picture established by Bes and Broglia (1966), Broglia (1973), and Brink and Broglia, where pairing vibration strength is significantly smaller than pairing rotation strength, by showing that in neutron-rich isotopes beyond N=82 the pair-addition vibrational strength can become comparable to ground-state transitions. The findings enable future experimental studies of two-neutron transfer reactions using radioactive beams of neutron-rich Sn isotopes, particularly for investigating the role of weakly bound neutron orbits and the surface enhancement of pairing correlations in heavy neutron-rich nuclei.
