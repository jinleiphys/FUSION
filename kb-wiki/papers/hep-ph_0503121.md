# QCD Plasma Instabilities and Isotropization

> We study the coupled Wong Yang-Mills equations for both U(1) and SU(2) gauge groups and anisotropic particle momentum distributions numerically on a lattice. For weak fields with initial energy density much smaller than that of the particles we confirm the existence of plasma instabilities and exponential growth of the fields which has been discussed previously. Also, the SU(2) case is qualitatively similar to U(1), and we do find strong ``abelianization'' of the non-Abelian fields during the period of exponential growth. However, the effect nearly disappears when the fields are strong. This is because of the very rapid isotropization of the particle momenta by deflection in a strong field on time scales comparable to that for the development of Yang-Mills instabilities. This happens without entropy production which makes it very interesting for the phenomenology of high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

For weak initial fields, the SU(2) non-Abelian case is qualitatively similar to U(1) and exhibits significant "abelianization" during exponential growth, but this effect nearly disappears when the fields are strong because rapid isotropization of particle momenta by deflection in a strong field occurs on time scales comparable to the development of Yang-Mills instabilities.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Wong Yang-Mills equations numerically on a lattice for both U(1) and SU(2) gauge groups, using the test-particle method to represent the continuous phase-space distribution of hard gluons. The fields were assumed to depend only on time and one spatial coordinate (1+1 dimensions), while hard particles propagated in three spatial dimensions. Initial anisotropic particle distributions were taken as quasi-thermal in two dimensions with zero longitudinal momentum, and initial field amplitudes were sampled from a Gaussian distribution.

## Key numbers

- For weak-field simulations: lattice physical size L=40 fm, hard scale p_hard=10 GeV, particle density n/g^2=10 fm^{-3}
- For strong-field simulations: lattice physical size L=10 fm, hard scale p_hard=1 GeV, particle density n/g^2=500 fm^{-3}, initial field energy density about 20 GeV/fm^3
- Total energy conserved to within 5x10^{-4} for weak field and 5x10^{-3} for strong field initial conditions
- Maximal violation of Gauss' law was 10^{-9} for SU(2) and 10^{-33} for U(1) in lattice units
- For strong fields, the average commutator bar-C dropped by about a factor of 5 and saturated at approximately 10%, an order of magnitude larger than for the weak field case
- For strong fields, t/L=0.1 corresponds to t=1 fm in physical units

## Context

This work extends the hard-loop effective action approach to anisotropic QCD plasmas developed by Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore (2004) and Rebhan, Romatschke, and Strickland (2004), which predicted abelianization of non-Abelian fields during instability growth. The authors confirm these predictions for weak fields but show that for strong fields, where the initial particle-to-field energy density ratio is smaller (as expected in the color glass condensate model of high-energy collisions), rapid isotropization via particle deflection in strong fields inhibits the development of Yang-Mills instabilities. This mechanism for isotropization without entropy production may be relevant for understanding the early stages of high-energy heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where data from the PHOBOS collaboration suggest that equilibration does not produce large amounts of entropy.
