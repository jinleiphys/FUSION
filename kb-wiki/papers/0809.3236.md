# Chiral Logarithms Tamed

> We derive non-linear recursion relations for the leading chiral logarithms (LLs). These relations not only provide a very efficient method of computation of LLs (e.g. the 33-loop contribution is calculated in a dozen of seconds on a PC) but also equip us with a powerful tool for the summation of the LLs. Our method is not limited to the chiral perturbation theory only, it is pertinent for any non-renormalizable effective field theory such as, for instance, the theory of critical phenomena, the low-energy quantum gravity, etc.

## Key claim

The authors derive nonlinear recursion relations for the leading chiral logarithms (LLs) that allow computation to essentially unlimited loop order (e.g., 33-loop contribution in seconds on a PC), but the method is limited to the leading logarithmic approximation and does not address subleading logarithms or full chiral corrections.

## Method

The method reduces the computation of leading chiral logarithms to solving nonlinear recursion relations for coefficients ω_{nj} that encode the action of the renormalization group operator on the coupling constants of the all-order counterterm Lagrangian. The recursion relations are derived from the one-loop β-functions of the O(N+1) σ-model, which are expressed in terms of integrals over Legendre polynomials. The LL coefficients for the forward ππ scattering amplitude A(s) and B(s) are then obtained by summing the ω_{nj} coefficients with appropriate combinatorial factors.

## Key numbers

- 33-loop chiral logarithm computed in "a dozen of seconds on a PC"
- For N=3 (two-flavor QCD), the 6-loop LL coefficient for amplitude A(s) is 33744493/15876000
- For N=3, the 1-loop LL coefficient for amplitude A(s) is 2/3
- For N=3, the 7-loop LL coefficient for amplitude B(s) is 2433747559349/3780710640000
- For arbitrary N, the 1-loop LL coefficient for amplitude A(s) is (N/2)(1 - 5/(3N))
- For arbitrary N, the 1-loop LL coefficient for amplitude B(s) is 2/3 (independent of N)
- Large-N asymptotic: A_n = (N/2)^n [1 - (π²/3 - 8(1 - ln 2)) n/N + ...]
- Large-N asymptotic: B_n = (N/2)^(n-1) × 2/(2+n) [1 + O(1/N)]

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of leading chiral logarithms that had reached two-loop accuracy for ππ-scattering (Colangelo, 2001), five-loop accuracy for scalar current correlators (Bissegger, 2007), and three-loop accuracy for generalized parton distributions (Kivel, 2008). The recursion relation method enables computation to arbitrarily high loop order and provides a tool for summing the LL series, which is particularly important for generalized parton distributions where the small chiral expansion parameter is compensated by 1/x_Bj^n. The method is applicable to any non-renormalizable effective field theory, including the theory of critical phenomena and low-energy quantum gravity.
