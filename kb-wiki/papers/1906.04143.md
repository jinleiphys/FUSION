# Bayesian analysis of capture reactions $^3\mathrm{He}(α,γ)^7\mathrm{Be}$ and $^3\mathrm{H}(α,γ)^7\mathrm{Li}$

> Bayesian analysis of the radiative capture reactions $^3\mathrm{He}(α,γ)^7\mathrm{Be}$ and $^3\mathrm{H}(α,γ)^7\mathrm{Li}$ are performed to draw inferences about the cross sections at threshold. We do a model comparison of two competing effective field theory power countings for the capture reactions. The two power countings differ in the contribution of two-body electromagnetic currents. In one power counting, two-body currents contribute at leading order, and in the other they contribute at higher orders. The former is favored for $^3\mathrm{He}(α,γ)^7\mathrm{Be}$ if elastic scattering data in the incoming channel is considered in the analysis. Without constraints from elastic scattering data, both the power countings are equally favored. For $^3\mathrm{H}(α,γ)^7\mathrm{Li}$, the first power counting with two-body current contributions at leading order is favored with or without constraints from elastic scattering data.

# Bayesian analysis of capture reactions $^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$ and $^3\mathrm{H}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Li}$

## Key claim

Bayesian model comparison favors an effective field theory power counting where two-body electromagnetic currents contribute at leading order (Model A) over one where they contribute at higher orders (Model B) for both capture reactions, but the preference for $^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$ is conditional on including elastic scattering data in the analysis; without such constraints, both power countings are equally favored for that reaction.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian parameter estimation and model comparison using nested sampling (MultiNest algorithm) for halo effective field theory calculations of the radiative capture cross sections. They considered two competing power countings differing in whether the s-wave scattering length is large (Model A, two-body currents at leading order) or small (Model B, two-body currents at next-to-leading order), fitting to capture S-factor data, branching ratio data, and elastic scattering phase shift data where available.

## Key numbers

- For $^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$ with capture data up to 1000 keV and phase shift data: $\ln[P(M_A|D,H)/P(M_B|D,H)] \approx 2.6 \pm 0.8$, slightly favoring Model A
- For $^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$ with capture data up to 1000 keV and no phase shift data: $\ln[P(M_A^\ast|D,H)/P(M_B^\ast|D,H)] \approx 4.9 \pm 0.6$, favoring Model A
- For $^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$ with capture data up to 2000 keV and phase shift data: $\ln[P(M_A|D,H)/P(M_B|D,H)] \approx 31.6 \pm 0.9$, strongly favoring Model A
- For $^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$ with capture data up to 2000 keV and no phase shift data: $\ln[P(M_A^\ast|D,H)/P(M_B^\ast|D,H)] \approx 0.2 \pm 0.8$, both models equally favored
- For $^3\mathrm{H}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Li}$ with phase shift data: $\ln[P(M_A|D,H)/P(M_B|D,H)] \approx 19.7 \pm 0.7$, overwhelmingly favoring Model A
- For $^3\mathrm{H}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Li}$ without phase shift data: $\ln[P(M_A^\ast|D,H)/P(M_B^\ast|D,H)] \approx 18.4 \pm 0.6$, overwhelmingly favoring Model A
- Binding energies: $^7\mathrm{Be}$ ground state $B_0^\mathrm{(Be)} = 1.5866$ MeV, first excited state $B_1^\mathrm{(Be)} = 1.1575$ MeV; $^7\mathrm{Li}$ ground state $B_0^\mathrm{(Li)} = 2.467$ MeV, first excited state $B_1^\mathrm{(Li)} = 1.989$ MeV
- Prior ranges: s-wave scattering length $a_0 \sim U(1\ \mathrm{fm}, 70\ \mathrm{fm})$, effective range $r_0 \sim U(-5\ \mathrm{fm}, 5\ \mathrm{fm})$, two-body current couplings $L_1^{(\pm)} \sim U(-10, 10)$

## Context

This work extends the halo EFT analysis of Higa et al. (2016) for $^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$ by applying Bayesian methods to both that reaction and the related $^3\mathrm{H}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Li}$ reaction, which is important for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis calculations of $^7\mathrm{Li}$ abundances (Kawano, 1991; Smith, 1993). The Bayesian approach enables quantitative comparison of competing EFT power countings and provides posterior distributions for poorly constrained parameters like p-wave effective ranges, which are difficult to determine from capture data alone. The results enable more reliable extrapolation of cross sections to astrophysically relevant low energies (Gamow energy ~20 keV for $^3\mathrm{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$) with quantified theoretical uncertainties.
