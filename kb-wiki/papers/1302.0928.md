# Collision Rate and Symmetry Factor in Gluon Plasma

> The elastic and inelastic collision rates in a gluon gas are calculated. The symmetry factor and the phase space integral are discussed in detail. With a symmetry factor and well constrained phase space, the same result can be obtained as that of the full phase space without the factor. Such an equivalence is illustrated by analytic and numerical calculations for $gg \rightarrow gg$ and $gg\rightarrow ggg$ processes.

# Collision Rate and Symmetry Factor in Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that the elastic and inelastic collision rates in a gluon plasma can be calculated equivalently either by integrating over the full phase space without a symmetry factor or by integrating over a constrained phase space with a symmetry factor, provided the phase space is properly constrained. This equivalence resolves a discrepancy in the literature about the relative importance of the 22 (elastic) and 23 (inelastic) processes, with the 22 process being more important than the 23 process for coupling strengths where perturbative QCD is reliable.

## Method
The authors calculate elastic (gg → gg) and inelastic (gg → ggg) collision rates in a gluon gas using the Boltzmann equation with detailed balance. For the 22 process, they compare analytic results using a small-q approximation with numerical integration over full phase space. For the 23 process, they use the Gunion-Bertsch (GB) formula in the limit where one gluon is soft, applying a symmetry factor of 6 for the six equivalent configurations, and compare this semi-analytic method to full numerical integration without the symmetry factor. They also incorporate the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect as an infrared regulator.

## Key numbers
- Elastic collision rate for a Boltzmann gas: \( R_{22} = 3\alpha_s T \) (analytic result using small-q approximation).
- Elastic collision rate for a boson gas: \( R_{22} = \frac{\pi^2}{2\zeta(3)}\alpha_s T \approx 4.105\alpha_s T \) (with Debye mass \( m_D^2 = 4\pi \alpha_s T^2 \)).
- Inelastic collision rate from semi-analytic method with symmetry factor: \( R_{23} = 4.818 \alpha_s^{3/2} \) (for a boson gas).
- Inelastic collision rate with LPM cutoff from self-consistent equation: \( R_{23}^{LPM} \approx 0.99 \alpha_s T \) (solution of \( R_{23}^{LPM} = 6.52\alpha_s^2/(R_{22}+2.5R_{23}^{LPM}) \)).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Chen 2009, Chen 2010, Chen 2011) which suggested that a multiple-counting error in phase space integrals could explain the discrepancy between the perturbative QCD results of Xu (2007) and Wesp (2011), where the 23 process was found to be 7 times more important than the 22 process, and the results of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY) (2000, 2003), where the 23 process gave only about a 10% correction. The current results confirm that with proper treatment of symmetry factors, the 22 process dominates over the 23 process for perturbative couplings. The scheme can be applied to calculate other transport coefficients such as the dragging coefficient of heavy quarks and the jet quenching parameter.
