# Estimating higher order perturbative coefficients using Borel transform

> A new method of estimating higher order perturbative coefficients is discussed. It exploits the rapid, asymptotic growth of perturbative coefficients and the information on the singularities in the complex Borel plane. A comparison with other methods is made in several Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) expansions.

# Estimating higher order perturbative coefficients using Borel transform

## Key claim
The paper presents a method for estimating unknown higher-order perturbative coefficients from known lower-order coefficients, which works when the perturbative coefficients grow rapidly and the nature and location of the first singularity in the complex Borel plane is known. The method relies on the condition that the expansion coefficients of a suitably defined function R(b(w)) in the conformally mapped w-plane are much smaller than the constant terms in the corresponding expansion, a condition that cannot be verified a priori and must be judged from circumstantial information.

## Method
The authors define the Borel transform of the amplitude and construct the function R(b) = (1 - b/b0)^(1+ν) Ã(b) to remove the leading singularity at b = b0. They then apply a conformal mapping that places the first singularity inside the unit circle and all other singularities on the unit circle, expand R in powers of the mapped variable w, and set the unknown expansion coefficients r_n to zero to obtain estimates for the perturbative coefficients a_n = -p_n/q_n. The method is applied to the double-well potential in quantum mechanics and to three QCD expansions: the Bjorken polarized sum rule, the Adler function, and the on-shell quark mass relation.

## Key numbers
- Double-well potential: estimated coefficient a_5 = 2.266 × 10^5 versus exact a_5 = 2.288 × 10^5, giving accuracy better than 1% at n=5
- Bjorken sum rule at N_f=3: estimated d_3 = 156, with a range 149 ≤ d_3 ≤ 163 from assuming |r_3| ≤ 2.0
- Bjorken sum rule at N_f=3: estimated d_4 = 1565 versus PMS estimate d_4 = 933
- Adler function at N_f=3: estimated d_3 = 24.4 versus PMS estimate d_3 = 27.5
- Quark mass expansion at N_f=3: estimated r_3 = 1357 versus residue method value r_3 = 1364
- Quark mass expansion at N_f=3: estimated r_4 = 27098

## Context
This work extends the use of Borel transform and conformal mapping techniques for estimating perturbative coefficients, building on prior knowledge of renormalon singularities from Mueller (1985) and earlier instanton analysis by Lee (2001). The method is compared with the principle of minimal sensitivity (PMS) and effective charge (ECH) methods of Kataev and Starshenko (1995) for the Bjorken sum rule and Adler function, and with the residue-based method of Pineda (2001) for the quark mass expansion. The approach differs from a prior conformal mapping attempt by Caprini and Fischer (1999), which did not remove the first Borel singularity and concluded the method works only for large orders (N ~ 7), whereas the present method claims applicability at low orders relevant for current QCD calculations.
