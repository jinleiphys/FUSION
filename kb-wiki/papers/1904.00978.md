# Complete Matching for Quasi-distribution Functions in Large Momentum Effective Theory

> We complete the procedure of extracting parton distribution functions (PDFs) using large momentum effective theory (LaMET) at leading power accuracy in the hadron momentum. We derive a general factorization formula for the quasi PDFs in the presence of mixing, and give the corresponding hard matching kernel at $\mathcal O(α_s)$, both for the unpolarized and for the polarized quark and gluon quasi-PDFs. Our calculation is performed in a regularization-independent momentum subtraction scheme. The results allow us to match the nonperturbatively renormalized quasi-PDFs to normal PDFs in the presence of mixing, and therefore can be used to extract flavor-singlet quark PDFs as well as gluon PDFs from lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper completes the procedure for extracting parton distribution functions (PDFs) using large momentum effective theory (LaMET) at leading power accuracy in the hadron momentum, by deriving a general factorization formula for quasi-PDFs in the presence of mixing between quark and gluon operators, and providing the corresponding hard matching kernel at one-loop order in a regularization-independent momentum subtraction (RI/MOM) scheme.

## Method

The authors derived a general factorization formula for renormalized quasi-PDFs in the presence of mixing, following the operator product expansion method. They performed a one-loop calculation of the hard matching kernel connecting RI/MOM renormalized quasi-PDFs to PDFs in the MSbar scheme, for both unpolarized and polarized quark and gluon quasi-PDFs. The calculation was performed in dimensional regularization, with power divergences tracked by expanding at d less than 4, and the renormalization was studied using the auxiliary field approach.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop renormalization factor for the quark quasi-PDF auxiliary field operators is Z = 1 + alpha_s/(2*pi*epsilon) + O(alpha_s^2)
- The linear divergence in the gluon quasi-PDF operator appears as a pole at d=3 in dimensional regularization, with coefficient proportional to alpha_s*C_A/pi
- The effective mass counterterm for the Wilson line is delta m ~ O(1/a) where a is the lattice spacing

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework beyond isovector quark PDFs, which were previously calculated without mixing (Chen 2018, Lin 2018, Alexandrou 2018), to enable extraction of flavor-singlet quark PDFs and gluon PDFs from lattice QCD. It builds on earlier studies of gluon quasi-PDF renormalization (Wang 2017, Zhang 2018) and provides the complete matching kernels needed for lattice implementations, enabling future lattice calculations of the full flavor-singlet and gluon PDFs.
