# Coulomb corrections in quasi-elastic scattering: tests of the effective-momentum approximation

> Coulomb corrections for quasi-elastic scattering of electrons by nuclei are calculated using eikonal distorted waves. Corrections to the lowest-order eikonal approximation are included in order to obtain accurate results. Spin-dependent eikonal phase shifts are evaluated and they yield very small corrections to the longitudinal and transverse cross sections at electron energies of 500 MeV or higher. Because of this the Rosenbluth procedure is accurate for separation of the longitudinal and transverse response functions. The effective-momentum approximation is also found to be accurate with regard to removal of the remaining Coulomb effects from the distorted waves. Calculations are presented for electron scattering from Pb and Fe nuclei at energies of 500 MeV and 800 MeV and momentum transfers q = 550 MeV/c and 900 MeV/c.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spin-dependent Coulomb corrections to quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering are negligible (below one part per thousand) at electron energies of 500 MeV or higher, so the Rosenbluth procedure accurately separates longitudinal and transverse response functions, and the effective-momentum approximation (EMA) reliably removes the remaining Coulomb effects from distorted waves.

## Method

The authors used eikonal distorted waves for the Dirac equation, including systematic corrections beyond the lowest-order eikonal approximation, to compute Coulomb corrections for quasi-elastic electron scattering. They evaluated spin-dependent eikonal phase shifts and compared full distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations with the effective-momentum approximation (EMA) using a simplified Coulomb potential fitted to empirical charge densities for 208Pb and 56Fe. The EMAr approximation was introduced, which applies the effective-momentum shift to the photon propagator and nucleon form factors while retaining the full r-dependence of eikonal phases and focusing factors, reducing the computation to a three-dimensional integration.

## Key numbers

- For 500 MeV electrons on 208Pb, the spin-dependent Coulomb corrections change the longitudinal response function by less than one part per thousand; the ratio of response functions with and without spin corrections differs from unity by less than 0.001 for energy loss up to 300 MeV (final electron energy 200 MeV).
- The momentum-shift parameter f_EMA = δk/V_c(0) was found to be approximately 0.7 near the peak of the response function for 500 MeV e- scattering from 208Pb, with values up to about 1 at the smallest and largest ω values.
- Calculations were performed for electron energies of 500 MeV and 800 MeV and momentum transfers q = 550 MeV/c and 900 MeV/c for 208Pb and 56Fe nuclei.
- The spin-dependent eikonal phase γ^(+) + iδ^(+) has magnitude 0.03 or less for 500 MeV electrons on 208Pb, vanishing at zero impact parameter.

## Context

This work extends the systematic eikonal expansion developed by Tjon and Wallace (2006) by including spin-dependent Coulomb corrections that were previously omitted, and by using shell-model nuclear wave functions instead of a simple model. It addresses the controversy about Coulomb corrections in the interpretation of quasi-elastic scattering data, where different experiments (e.g., at MIT Bates Laboratory, Saclay Laboratory, and SLAC) have produced significantly different values of the Coulomb sum rule as reviewed by Morgenstern (2001) and Benhar (2008). The results enable the use of the EMA with a constant momentum shift δk to remove Coulomb effects from experimental data, allowing extraction of the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) response functions with minimal reliance on nuclear models.
