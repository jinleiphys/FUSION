# Left-right splitting of elliptic flow due to directed flow in heavy ion collisions

> Recently the splitting of elliptic flow $v_2$ at finite rapidities has been proposed as a result of the global vorticity in non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this study, we find that this left-right (i.e., on opposite sides of the impact parameter axis) splitting of the elliptic flow at finite rapidities is a result of the non-zero directed flow $v_1$, with the splitting magnitude $\approx 8v_1(1-3v_2)/(3π)$. We also use a multi-phase transport model, which automatically includes the vorticity field and flow fluctuations, to confirm the $v_2$ splitting. In addition, we find that the analytical expectations for the $v_2$ splitting work for the raw $v_2$ and $v_1$ (i.e., before event plane resolutions are applied) measured relative to either the first- or second-order event plane. Since the $v_2$ splitting is mostly driven by $v_1$, it vanishes at zero transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$), and its magnitude and sign may have non-trivial dependencies on $p_{\rm T}$, centrality, collision energy, and hadron species.

## Key claim

The left-right splitting of elliptic flow $v_2$ at finite rapidities, previously proposed as a result of global vorticity, is actually a direct consequence of non-zero directed flow $v_1$, with the splitting magnitude approximately given by $8v_1(1-3v_2)/(3\pi)$; the analytical expectation works for raw (uncorrected) $v_2$ and $v_1$ before event plane resolutions are applied.

## Method

The authors derived an analytical relation between the left-right $v_2$ splitting and the directed flow $v_1$ from a Fourier expansion of the azimuthal particle distribution. They then tested this relation using the string melting version of the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies (11.5A and 200A GeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy (5.02A TeV), calculating flows both with the theoretical reaction plane and with experimental event plane methods (first-order $\Psi_1$ and directional second-order $\Psi_{2\rm r}$).

## Key numbers

- The analytical splitting magnitude is $\approx 8v_1(1-3v_2)/(3\pi)$.
- For minimum bias Au+Au at 200A GeV, the $v_2$ splitting becomes obvious for partons at $p_{\rm T} > 1$ GeV/$c$ and for hadrons at $p_{\rm T} > 1.5$ GeV/$c$.
- For Au+Au at 11.5A GeV, the first-order event plane resolutions used were $R_1\{\Psi_1\}=0.574$, $R_2\{\Psi_1\}=0.188$, and $R_3\{\Psi_1\}=0.047$.
- For Au+Au at 11.5A GeV relative to $\Psi_{2\rm r}$, the subevent resolutions were $R_1^{\rm sub}\{\Psi_{2\rm r}\}=0.715$, $R_2^{\rm sub}\{\Psi_{2\rm r}\}=0.433$, and $R_3^{\rm sub}\{\Psi_{2\rm r}\}=0.357$.
- The $v_2$ splitting integrated over all $p_{\rm T}$ shows a very small splitting, while the splitting integrated over $p_{\rm T}>1.5$ GeV/$c$ shows a significant left-right splitting.

## Context

This work extends the recent proposal by Chen (2021) that attributed the $v_2$ splitting to global vorticity, showing instead that the splitting is primarily driven by directed flow $v_1$. The study uses the AMPT model, which has been shown to describe bulk matter including elliptic flow (Lin 2001, Ma 2016) and vorticity field (Jiang 2016, Li 2017). The results enable experimental extraction of raw $v_1$ and $v_2$ from $v_2$ splitting measurements using either first-order or directional second-order event planes, and suggest that the splitting can be observed even at LHC energies at large rapidities.
