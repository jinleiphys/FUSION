# Compositeness of the strange, charm and beauty odd parity $Λ$ states

> We study the dependence on the quark mass of the compositeness of the lowest-lying odd parity hyperon states. Thus, we pay attention to $Λ-$like states in the strange, charm and beauty, sectors which are dynamically generated using a unitarized meson-baryon model. In the strange sector we use an SU(6) extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa meson-baryon interaction, and we further implement the heavy-quark spin symmetry to construct the meson-baryon interaction when charmed or beauty hadrons are involved. In the three examined flavor sectors, we obtain two $J^P=1/2^-$ and one $J^P=3/2^-$ $Λ$ states. We find that the $Λ$ states which are bound states (the three $Λ_b$) or narrow resonances (one $Λ(1405)$ and one $Λ_c(2595)$) are well described as molecular states composed of $s$-wave meson-baryon pairs. The $\frac{1}{2}^-$ wide $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ_c(2595)$ as well as the $\frac{3}{2}^-$ $Λ(1520)$ and $Λ_c(2625)$ states display smaller compositeness and so they would require new mechanisms, such as $d$-wave interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the degree to which odd-parity Lambda hyperon states can be described as molecular (composite) states of s-wave meson-baryon pairs depends strongly on the heavy quark mass and on the specific state, with the caveat that states with larger widths or higher spin require additional mechanisms such as d-wave interactions to fully account for their structure.

## Method

The authors use a unitarized meson-baryon model based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation with an s-wave contact interaction kernel. In the strange sector, they employ an SU(6) extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction; for charm and beauty sectors, they incorporate heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) to construct the meson-baryon interaction. The model includes pseudoscalar and vector mesons coupled to 1/2+ and 3/2+ baryons, with symmetry breaking introduced through physical masses and decay constants, and the loop function is renormalized by a subtraction point regularization.

## Key numbers

- The first Lambda(1405) state (strange, J^P=1/2^-) has compositeness 1-Z close to unity for the default subtraction point (alpha=1), indicating it is well described as an s-wave meson-baryon molecule.
- The Lambda(1520) (strange, J^P=3/2^-) has compositeness 1-Z = 0.27 for alpha=1.
- The Lambda_c(2625) (charm, J^P=3/2^-) has compositeness 1-Z = 0.37 for alpha=1.
- The Lambda_b(5920) (beauty, J^P=3/2^-) has compositeness 1-Z = 0.82 for alpha=1.
- The three Lambda_b states (beauty) are bound states, while the Lambda(1405) and Lambda_c(2595) states include narrow resonances.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of compositeness in the strange sector, such as Sekihara (2012, 2014) for the two Lambda(1405) states and Aceti (2014) for the Lambda(1520), by systematically analyzing the quark mass dependence across strange, charm, and beauty sectors. The results enable future investigations into the role of d-wave interactions and other missing mechanisms for states with smaller compositeness, particularly the second Lambda(1/2^-) and Lambda(3/2^-) resonances in the strange and charm sectors.
