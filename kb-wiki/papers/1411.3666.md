# Exact solution of the (0+1)-dimensional Boltzmann equation for massive Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac gases

> We present the exact solution of the (0+1)-dimensional Boltzmann equation for massive Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac gases. For the initial conditions used typically in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, we find that the effects of quantum statistics are very small for thermodynamics-like functions such as the effective temperature, energy density or transverse and longitudinal pressures. Similarly, the inclusion of quantum statistics affects very little the shear viscosity. On the other hand, the quantum statistics becomes important for description of the phenomena connected with the bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

For massive gases with initial conditions typical of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, quantum statistics effects are very small for thermodynamic-like quantities (effective temperature, energy density, pressures) and shear viscosity, but become important for the description of bulk viscosity.

## Method

The authors solve the (0+1)-dimensional relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for massive Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac gases, generalizing their earlier classical Boltzmann solution (Florkowski 2014). They assume a transversely homogeneous, boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow and use the Romatschke-Strickland form for the initial anisotropic distribution function. The Landau matching condition determines the effective temperature, and the integral equation for the temperature is solved iteratively. Shear and bulk viscosities are computed both from standard kinetic-theory integrals and from the exact solution's pressure anisotropy.

## Key numbers

* Initial effective temperature \(T_0 = 300\) MeV, initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.5\) fm/c, equilibration time \(\tau_{\rm eq} = 0.5\) fm/c, and particle mass \(m = 300\) MeV.
* Evolution is followed from \(\tau_0 = 0.5\) fm/c to \(\tau = 10.5\) fm/c.
* Initial anisotropy parameter \(\xi_0\) is set to either 0 (isotropic) or 100 (highly oblate).
* Degeneracy factor \(g_0 = 16\) (its specific value cancels in ratios or appears as an overall factor).

## Context

This work extends the exact RTA Boltzmann solution for classical (Boltzmann) statistics (Florkowski 2014) to massive quantum gases obeying Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac statistics. It confirms that the standard kinetic-theory formulas for shear viscosity (Sasaki 2008) and bulk viscosity (Sasaki 2008) are supported by the exact solutions at late times. The results enable more accurate hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for higher-order hydrodynamics that must reproduce the time evolution of bulk viscous pressure at early times.
