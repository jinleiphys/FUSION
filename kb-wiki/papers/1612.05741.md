# EPPS16: Nuclear parton distributions with LHC data

> We introduce a global analysis of collinearly factorized nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) including, for the first time, data constraints from LHC proton-lead collisions. In comparison to our previous analysis, EPS09, where data only from charged-lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (DIS), Drell-Yan (DY) dilepton production in proton-nucleus collisions and inclusive pion production in deuteron-nucleus collisions were the input, we now increase the variety of data constraints to cover also neutrino-nucleus DIS and low-mass DY production in pion-nucleus collisions. The new LHC data significantly extend the kinematic reach of the data constraints. We now allow much more freedom for the flavour dependence of nuclear effects than in other currently available analyses. As a result, especially the uncertainty estimates are more objective flavour by flavour. The neutrino DIS plays a pivotal role in obtaining a mutually consistent behaviour for both up and down valence quarks, and the LHC dijet data clearly constrain gluons at large momentum fraction. Mainly for insufficient statistics, the pion-nucleus DY and heavy gauge boson production in proton-lead collisions impose less visible constraints. The outcome - a new set of next-to-leading order nuclear PDFs called EPPS16 - is made available for applications in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The EPPS16 analysis introduces the first global fit of nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) that directly includes data from LHC proton-lead collisions, but the constraints from heavy gauge boson production (W and Z) and pion-nucleus Drell-Yan data remain weak due to limited statistical precision, while the LHC dijet data clearly constrain gluons at large momentum fraction and neutrino-nucleus DIS plays a pivotal role in obtaining mutually consistent behavior for up and down valence quarks.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of collinearly factorized nuclear PDFs at next-to-leading order, parametrizing nuclear modifications \(R_i^A(x,Q^2_0)\) relative to the CT14NLO free proton baseline at the charm pole mass scale \(Q^2_0 = m_c^2 = 1.3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), then evolving with DGLAP equations using 2-loop splitting functions. The fit included charged-lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (DIS), Drell-Yan dilepton production in proton-nucleus and pion-nucleus collisions, inclusive pion production in deuteron-gold collisions, neutrino-nucleus DIS from CHORUS, and LHC proton-lead data for dijets, W, and Z boson production, with the LHC data included as forward-to-backward rapidity ratios to reduce sensitivity to the free-proton baseline and cancel luminosity uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The parametrization scale is fixed to \(Q^2_0 = m_c^2\) with \(m_c = 1.3\ \mathrm{GeV}\); bottom quark mass is \(m_b = 4.75\ \mathrm{GeV}\); strong coupling constant is \(\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.118\)
- The total dataset comprises 1811 data points with a global \(\chi^2\) of 1789 at the minimum
- The CHORUS neutrino-Pb DIS data contribute 824 data points with \(\chi^2 = 998.6\)
- The LHC CMS dijet data (7 data points) contribute \(\chi^2 = 5.5\); CMS W data (10 points) contribute \(\chi^2 = 8.8\); CMS Z data (6 points) contribute \(\chi^2 = 5.8\); ATLAS Z data (7 points) contribute \(\chi^2 = 9.6\)
- The pion-nucleus DY data contribute: NA10 (10 points, \(\chi^2 = 11.6\)), E615 (11 points, \(\chi^2 = 10.2\)), NA3 (7 points, \(\chi^2 = 4.6\))
- The kinematic cuts for neutrino DIS data are \(Q^2 > 4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(W^2 > 12.25\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\); for charged-lepton DIS data the cut is \(Q^2 > m_c^2\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier EPS09 analysis (Eskola et al., 2009) by adding neutrino-nucleus DIS data from CHORUS, pion-nucleus Drell-Yan data from NA3, NA10, and E615, and LHC proton-lead data for dijets, W, and Z production, while also relaxing the assumption of flavor-independent nuclear modifications for quarks at the parametrization scale and removing the isoscalar corrections applied by some experiments. The new EPPS16 set supersedes EPS09 and enables more objective flavor-by-flavor uncertainty estimates; the LHC dijet data resolve a tension between EPS09/nCTEQ15 and DSSZ (de Florian et al., 2011) regarding nuclear gluon modifications, favoring the EPS09 and nCTEQ15 (Kovarik et al., 2015) descriptions over DSSZ.
