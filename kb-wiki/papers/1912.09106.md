# Basic properties of GPDs and modelling of the latter

> We present here a new method based on the Radon transform to model Generalised Parton Distributions (GPDs). It allows to fulfil all theoretical constraints applying on GPDs, especially polynomiality and positivity at the same time. More specifically, we show how polynomiality can be systematically restored within the framework of the overlaps of Lightfront Wave Functions (LFWFs). It provides a systematic way to extend models defined solely in the DGLAP kinematical region to the ERBL one. We then exemplify our approach using LFWFs models.

# Basic properties of GPDs and modelling of the latter

## Key claim
The authors present a new method based on the Radon transform that allows GPD models to fulfill both positivity and polynomiality constraints simultaneously, by using the DGLAP region alone to reconstruct Double Distributions via the inverse Radon transform, thereby extending models defined only in the DGLAP region to the ERBL region.

## Method
The method computes GPDs in the DGLAP kinematical region from overlaps of Lightfront Wave Functions (LFWFs), which guarantees positivity via the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality. The inverse Radon transform is then applied to the DGLAP-region GPDs to reconstruct the Double Distributions, which automatically enforce polynomiality; the Radon transform of those Double Distributions then yields GPDs in the ERBL region. The inverse Radon transform is implemented using a finite-element algorithm, and the approach is tested on a Nakanishi-based model for the pion GPD.

## Key numbers
- Positivity bound for pion GPDs: |H(x,ξ,t)| ≤ √[q((x−ξ)/(1−ξ)) q((x+ξ)/(1+ξ))], where q is the pion PDF.
- Mellin moments of GPDs satisfy polynomiality: M_n(ξ,t) = ∫_{-1}^{1} dx x^n H(x,ξ,t) = Σ_{i=0}^{[n/2]} c_{i,n}(t) ξ^{2i} + mod(n,2) ξ^{n+1} c_{n+1,n}(t).
- The D-term is defined such that ∫_{-|ξ|}^{|ξ|} dx x^n D(x/ξ,t) = mod(n,2) sign(ξ) ξ^{n+1} c_{n+1,n}(t).

## Context
This work extends the Radon-transform relation between GPDs and Double Distributions first noted by Teryaev (2001) and generalises earlier results obtained within specific functional forms by Hwang (2007) and Mueller (2014). The method builds on the overlap representation of GPDs from LFWFs developed by Diehl (2000) and the positivity constraints derived by Pire (1998). It enables systematic construction of GPD models that respect all theoretical constraints, with generalisation to the nucleon case considered as a next step, particularly using nucleon LFWFs models such as those by Mezrag (2017).
