# Exact relations between the conductivities and their connection to the chemical composition of QCD matter

> We present exact relations between the diffusion coefficients or conductivities, $κ_{qq'}/T = σ_{qq'}$, of strongly-interacting matter. We show that once the diagonal entries are known in two different charge representations, the off-diagonal coefficients are functions of the diagonal entries once isospin symmetry applies. As an important example, we infer the conductivities on the basis of available calculations from lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) and argue that these computations suffer under the approximations made to achieve them. Further, we argue that the representation of the conductivities w.r.t. to the conserved quark-flavors may deliver more insight into the chemical composition of strongly-interacting matter.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact transformation laws that relate the diffusion coefficients (conductivities) of strongly-interacting matter in different charge representations, showing that under isospin symmetry the off-diagonal coefficients become functions of the diagonal entries alone. The authors argue that lattice QCD calculations of these conductivities suffer from the approximation that off-diagonal quark-flavor current-current correlations are neglected, which may cause significant deviations from the true values near the pseudo-critical temperature.

## Method

The authors derive the transformation law for the conductivity matrix under changes of charge representation, showing that the matrix transforms as \(\hat{\kappa}' = \hat{\mathcal{M}} \hat{\kappa} \hat{\mathcal{M}}^T\) where \(\hat{\mathcal{M}}\) is the transformation matrix between charge bases. They apply this formalism to transform between the hadronic charge representation (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) and the quark-flavor representation (up, down, strange) for QCD matter with \(N_f = 2+1\) flavors. Using these relations together with the assumption of isospin symmetry, they express all off-diagonal conductivities in terms of the diagonal entries and extract the full conductivity matrix from available lattice QCD results for the isospin conductivity.

## Key numbers

- For \(N_f = 2\) (light quarks only): \(C_B = \frac{2}{9}\), \(C_{\text{el}} = \frac{5}{9}e^2\), \(C_S = 0\); the off-diagonal conductivities become \(\kappa_{BQ} = \frac{e}{9} \kappa_{\ell\ell}\) while \(\kappa_{ud} = 0\), \(\kappa_{\ell s} = 0\), \(\kappa_{BS} = 0\), \(\kappa_{QS} = 0\)
- For \(N_f = 3\) (up, down, strange): \(C_B = \frac{1}{3}\), \(C_{\text{el}} = \frac{2}{3}e^2\), \(C_S = 1\); the off-diagonal conductivities become \(\kappa_{BS} = -\frac{1}{3} \kappa_{\ell\ell}\) and \(\kappa_{QS} = \frac{e}{3} \kappa_{\ell\ell}\), while \(\kappa_{ud} = 0\) and \(\kappa_{\ell s} = 0\)
- The pseudo-critical temperature used for hadronic and partonic approaches is set to \(T_c = 158\) MeV where the approaches do not incorporate a cross-over

## Context

This work extends the study of charge transport in strongly-interacting matter by providing exact transformation relations between different charge representations, building on prior kinetic theory calculations by Greif et al. (2017) and Fotakis et al. (2019, 2021), as well as hadronic transport approaches by Hammelmann et al. (2023). The paper enables the extraction of the full conductivity matrix from lattice QCD results that currently only provide the isospin conductivity, and it suggests that future lattice calculations should relax the approximation of neglecting off-diagonal quark-flavor current-current correlations to capture the non-zero cross-conductivities expected from hadronic calculations near the pseudo-critical temperature.
