# Off-shell extrapolation of Regge-model NN scattering amplitudes describing final state interactions in $^2H(e,e'p)$

> In this work, an off-shell extrapolation is proposed for the Regge-model $NN$ amplitudes of \cite{FVO_Reggemodel}. A prescription for extrapolating these amplitudes for one nucleon off-shell in the initial state are given. Application of these amplitudes to calculations of deuteron electrodisintegration are presented and compared to the limited available precision data in the kinematical region covered by the Regge model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a smooth, self-regulating off-shell extrapolation for Regge-model nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes, but acknowledges that the prescription is "somewhat arbitrary and incomplete" and that the off-shell terms (five additional Fermi invariants) are set to zero because they cannot be determined from on-shell data alone.

## Method

The authors construct an off-shell extrapolation by analytically continuing the on-shell Regge-model Fermi invariants from the t-channel center-of-momentum frame to the s-channel center-of-momentum frame, using a modified definition of the t-channel scattering angle z that is continuous in the off-shell parameter v = p₁² - m². The five off-shell Fermi invariants (F_S0, F_V0, F_T0, F_P0, F_A0) are set to zero, and the on-shell invariants are evaluated using the Regge parameterization from their prior work (Ford and Van Orden, 2013). The off-shell amplitudes are then applied to deuteron electrodisintegration calculations, including a cutoff function f(v) = (Λ² - m²)² / ((Λ² - m²)² + v²) to regulate the integral.

## Key numbers

- The Regge model fits NN scattering for Mandelstam s from 5.4 GeV² to 4000 GeV².
- For deuteron electrodisintegration at x=1, beam energy 5 GeV, Q²=3.5 GeV², s=7.0 GeV², and φ=180°, the off-shell contribution reaches a maximum of about 25 percent at missing momentum p_m = 1.0 GeV.
- For x=1.3, beam energy 12 GeV, Q²=7.5 GeV², s=7.54 GeV², and φ=180°, the off-shell contribution at p_m = 1.0 GeV is about 130 percent above the on-shell contribution.
- The cutoff mass Λ is typically taken as 1 GeV for SAID amplitudes and 10 GeV for Regge amplitudes to represent saturated off-shell contributions.
- The data compared span approximately 0.8 < x < 1.5 and 4.8 GeV² < s < 8.5 GeV², with missing momenta of 0.2, 0.4, and 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Regge-model NN amplitudes of Ford and Van Orden (2013) to off-shell kinematics, enabling calculations of final-state interactions in deuteron electrodisintegration at higher invariant masses than possible with the SAID helicity amplitudes used in earlier work by Van Orden and collaborators (2008, 2009). The off-shell prescription is compared to data from Boeglin (high-momentum transfer measurements), and the results suggest that off-shell effects may be small, though the authors caution that more data are needed. The approach enables future calculations of the d(e,e'p) reaction in kinematic regions where Regge models apply, providing a range of possible off-shell contributions.
