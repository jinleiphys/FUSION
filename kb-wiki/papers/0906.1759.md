# Can one measure nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double beta decay?

> By making use of the isospin conservation by strong interaction, the Fermi $0νββ$ nuclear matrix element $M_{F}^{0ν}$ is transformed to acquire the form of an energy-weighted double Fermi transition matrix element. This useful representation allows reconstruction of the total $M_{F}^{0ν}$ provided a small isospin-breaking Fermi matrix element between the isobaric analog state in the intermediate nucleus and the ground state of the daughter nucleus could be measured, e.g. by charge-exchange reactions. Such a measurement could set a scale for the $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements and help much to discriminate between different nuclear structure models in which calculated $M_{F}^{0ν}$ may differ by as much as the factor of 5.

## Key claim

The Fermi component of the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element can be reconstructed from a measurement of the isospin-forbidden Fermi matrix element between the ground state of the daughter nucleus and the isobaric analog state in the intermediate nucleus, provided the isospin-conserving strong interaction assumption holds and small corrections from isospin-breaking strong interactions (estimated at 2–3%) can be accounted for.

## Method

The authors exploit isospin conservation by the strong interaction to transform the Fermi 0νββ operator into a double commutator of the isospin lowering operator with the total nuclear Hamiltonian, which reduces to the isotensor component of the Coulomb interaction. This representation expresses the total Fermi matrix element as an energy-weighted sum over intermediate 0⁺ states, dominated by the isobaric analog state, and the key measurable quantity is the ΔT=2 Fermi matrix element ⟨0_f|T⁻|IAS⟩, which could be extracted from (n,p)-type charge-exchange reactions.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-forbidden matrix element ⟨0_f|T⁻|IAS⟩ is estimated from QRPA calculations as ~0.005, about a thousand times smaller than the first-leg matrix element ⟨IAS|T⁻|0_i⟩ ≈ √(N-Z).
- The isospin-breaking part of the strong two-body interaction is estimated at 2%–3% of the Coulomb interaction, leading to a residual uncertainty of about 10% in M_F⁰ν if evaluated with 30% accuracy.
- The vacuum polarization correction to the photon propagator is about 0.5% and can be accounted for by renormalization of the electron charge.
- The ratio M_GT⁰ν/M_F⁰ν is estimated as approximately -2.5 from simple arguments based on the spin-isospin structure of the contributing nucleon pairs.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Frekers (2008) for reconstructing two-neutrino double beta decay amplitudes from charge-exchange reactions, which works only under single-state dominance, to the neutrinoless case where many intermediate states contribute. The proposed measurement would discriminate between QRPA calculations (Rodin 2003, Kortelainen 2007) and nuclear shell model calculations (Caurier 1996), which differ by up to a factor of 5 in the Fermi matrix element, corresponding to about 20% difference in the total matrix element.
