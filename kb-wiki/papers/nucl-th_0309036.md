# Coupled collective motion in nuclear reactions

> In this paper, we review the roles of collective modes in nuclear reactions. We emphaize the strong couplings of various collective states with the monopole and quadrupole motions. In inelastic excitation, these couplings can be seen as an important source of anharmonicity in the multiphonon spectrum. In fusion reaction, the breathing and quadrupole motions strongly affects the oscillation of protons against neutrons. Finally, the modification of the collective properties induced by a large amplitude dilution might be the origin of the nuclear multifragmentation, directly related to the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. In the three cases we derive a coupling matrix element which appears to be in good agreement.

# Coupled collective motion in nuclear reactions

## Key claim

The paper argues that the strong coupling between collective vibrations (particularly monopole and quadrupole modes) and other collective states is a unifying mechanism that explains anharmonicities in multiphonon spectra, pre-equilibrium giant dipole resonance behavior in fusion reactions, and the onset of nuclear multifragmentation via the liquid-gas phase transition. The authors derive coupling matrix elements from three different approaches (time-dependent mean-field, boson mapping, and dilution-dependent RPA) that show qualitative agreement, though the TDHF values are systematically about 50% larger than the boson mapping results.

## Method

The authors use three complementary theoretical frameworks: (1) Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations with the SGII Skyrme force for $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, extracting linear and quadratic responses to collective boosts; (2) boson mapping techniques that map the fermionic Hamiltonian into a bosonic one truncated at four-boson interactions, followed by RPA transformation to extract coupling matrix elements between one- and two-phonon states; (3) RPA calculations on scaled and heated density matrices using the SLy4 Skyrme force to study the dilution dependence of giant resonance frequencies and the onset of instabilities.

## Key numbers

- GMR energy in $^{40}$Ca: 22.9 MeV; GQR energy: 18.6 MeV; GDR energy: 17.2 MeV (from TDHF linear response)
- GMR energy in $^{208}$Pb: 15.7 MeV; GQR energy: 11.1 MeV; GDR energy: 13.0 MeV (from TDHF linear response)
- Coupling matrix element $\langle \nu | V | \nu 0 \rangle$ for GDR on GMR in $^{40}$Ca: -4.58 MeV; in $^{208}$Pb: -2.40 MeV (from TDHF quadratic response)
- Coupling matrix element $\langle \nu | V | \nu 2 \rangle$ for GDR on GQR in $^{40}$Ca: -3.92 MeV; in $^{208}$Pb: -0.70 MeV (from TDHF quadratic response)
- Coupling matrix element $\langle \nu | V | \nu 0 \rangle$ for GQR on GMR in $^{40}$Ca: -4.28 MeV; in $^{208}$Pb: -2.17 MeV (from TDHF quadratic response)
- Energy shift of two-phonon states due to coupling with three-phonon states: about 2 MeV for Ca and 0.5 MeV for Pb
- Quadrupole deformation parameter $\varepsilon = 0.23$ for the fused $^{40}$Ca system from $^{20}$O+$^{20}$Mg at 1 MeV per nucleon
- Dipole oscillation period in fusion: approximately 150 fm/c, compared to about 75 fm/c for ground-state $^{40}$Ca
- Mathieu equation coupling strength $\delta = -0.32$ for the dipole-deformation coupling in fusion
- Coupling matrix element extracted from fusion analysis: approximately -0.5 MeV for the GDR coupling to monopole or quadrupole
- Derivative of GQR frequency with dilution for $^{40}$Ca: $\partial_\alpha \omega \approx -40$ MeV
- Coupling matrix element from dilution analysis: approximately -2 MeV for GQR coupling to GMR in $^{40}$Ca
- Limiting temperature for octupole instabilities: about 6 MeV for $^{120}$Sn and 4.5 MeV for $^{40}$Ca
- Instability growth time $\tau = 100$ fm/c and $\tau = 50$ fm/c shown for $^{120}$Sn octupole modes

## Context

This review extends the authors' prior work on nonlinear coupling in collective nuclear motion (Chomaz, 1990s) and the phonon anharmonicity puzzle discussed by many authors (Chomaz, 1993; Emling, 1990; Aumann, 1999). The TDHF results are compared with boson mapping calculations from Beaumel and collaborators (2000) that use a completely different approach to infer the same matrix elements. The fusion reaction analysis builds on experimental indications of pre-equilibrium GDR excitation reported by Fliessbach (1990), Aumann (1998), and Pierroutsakou (2001). The dilution studies connect to spinodal decomposition experiments reported by Beaulieu and Borderie (2000s), and to prior theoretical work on spinodal instabilities by Bertsch, Heiselberg, Norenberg, Colonna, and Jacquot (1990s). The work enables a unified understanding of how collective mode couplings govern phenomena from multiphonon anharmonicity through fusion dynamics to multifragmentation, with the coupling to monopole and quadrupole motions identified as the common mechanism.
