# Nuclear Physics of Neutron Stars

> Understanding the equation of state (EOS) of cold nuclear matter, namely, the relation between the pressure and energy density, is a central goal of nuclear physics that cuts across a variety of disciplines. Indeed, the limits of nuclear existence, the collision of heavy ions, the structure of neutron stars, and the dynamics of core-collapse supernova, all depend critically on the equation of state of hadronic matter. In this contribution I will concentrate on the special role that nuclear physics plays in constraining the EOS of cold baryonic matter and its impact on the properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The properties of neutron stars are determined almost entirely by the equation of state (EOS) of cold, neutron-rich matter, and robust correlations exist between the neutron skin thickness of a single heavy nucleus (such as 208Pb) and the radius of a neutron star; however, the paper emphasizes that the existence of exotic "pasta" phases in the inner crust is not universal and depends critically on the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which remains poorly constrained.

## Method

The author uses relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, specifically the NL3 and FSUGold parameter sets, to compute the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations of general relativity are then solved with these EOS inputs to generate mass-versus-radius relations for neutron stars. The models incorporate scalar and vector meson fields, including isoscalar-isovector coupling (Λ_v) and nonlinear vector self-couplings (ζ), to explore how the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the high-density stiffness affect both finite-nuclei observables and neutron-star structure.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 2.48 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³ (0.148 fm⁻³).
- Outer crust spans densities from about 10⁴ g/cm³ to 4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³.
- Neutron-drip density: approximately 4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³.
- For FSUGold: symmetry energy at saturation J = 32.59 MeV, slope parameter L = 60.5 MeV, incompressibility K₀ = 230.0 MeV.
- For NL3: J = 37.29 MeV, L = 118.2 MeV, K₀ = 271.5 MeV.
- For a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star, the reduction in radius between FSUGold and NL3 predictions is close to 2 km.
- The crust thickness is approximately 1 km, containing about 2% of the star's mass.

## Context

This work extends the line of research connecting nuclear physics observables to neutron-star properties, building on the relativistic mean-field framework of Walecka (1974), Serot (1984, 1997), and the NL3 parameter set of Lalazissis (1996, 1999). It directly builds on the correlation between neutron skin thickness and neutron-star radius established by Horowitz (2000, 2001) and Steiner (2004). The paper highlights that the Parity Radius Experiment (PREx) at Jefferson Laboratory will provide a model-independent measurement of the neutron skin of 208Pb, which will constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The discussion of pasta phases references the work of Ravenhall (1983), Hashimoto (1984), and Oyamatsu (2006), who showed that pasta formation requires a stiff symmetry energy and may not be universal. The paper enables future work where simultaneous measurements of neutron-star radii and masses, combined with laboratory measurements of neutron skins, could reveal phase transitions to exotic matter such as meson condensates or quark matter.
