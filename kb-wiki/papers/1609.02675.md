# Brueckner-Hartree-Fock and its renormalized calculations for finite nuclei

> We have performed self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) and its renormalized theory to the structure calculations of finite nuclei. The $G$-matrix is calculated within the BHF basis, and the exact Pauli exclusion operator is determined by the BHF spectrum. Self-consistent occupation probabilities are included in the renormalized Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF). Various systematics and convergences are studies. Good results are obtained for the ground-state energy and radius. RBHF can give a more reasonable single-particle spectrum and radius. We present a first benchmark calculation with other {\it ab initio} methods using the same effective Hamiltonian. We find that the BHF and RBHF results are in good agreement with other $\it{ab}$ $\it{initio}$ methods.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) and renormalized Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations, using a \(V_{\text{low-}k}\) softened interaction derived from the Argonne \(\upsilon_{18}\) potential, produce ground-state energies and radii for closed-shell nuclei that are in good agreement with other *ab initio* methods, though the RBHF ground-state energies are higher than BHF for all nuclei studied, contrary to most past RBHF calculations.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent BHF and RBHF calculations for the closed-shell nuclei \(^{4}\text{He}\), \(^{16}\text{O}\), and \(^{40}\text{Ca}\). The \(G\)-matrix was solved exactly in the self-consistent BHF basis via matrix inversion, using the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the Pauli exclusion operator determined by the BHF spectrum, while the strong short-range correlations were handled by the \(V_{\text{low-}k}\) renormalization technique rather than by the \(G\)-matrix itself. In the RBHF approach, self-consistent occupation probabilities were included to account for the depletion of normally occupied single-particle states, and the single-particle potential was defined with an off-energy-shell prescription for particle-particle matrix elements.

## Key numbers

*   Ground-state energy of \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) in BHF: -551.51 MeV at \(N_{\text{shell}}=11\) and \(\hbar\Omega=22\) MeV; -552.14 MeV at \(N_{\text{shell}}=12\) and \(\hbar\Omega=22\) MeV, showing convergence within 0.5-1.0 MeV.
*   Ground-state energy of \(^{4}\text{He}\) in BHF: -25.90 MeV; in RBHF: -25.79 MeV (both at \(N_{\text{shell}}=12\), \(\hbar\Omega=14\) MeV, \(\Lambda=1.9\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
*   Ground-state energy of \(^{16}\text{O}\) in BHF: -134.16 MeV; in RBHF: -130.04 MeV (both at \(N_{\text{shell}}=12\), \(\hbar\Omega=22\) MeV, \(\Lambda=2.1\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
*   Ground-state energy of \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) in BHF: -552.14 MeV; in RBHF: -530.68 MeV (both at \(N_{\text{shell}}=12\), \(\hbar\Omega=22\) MeV, \(\Lambda=2.1\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
*   Point-nucleon rms radius for \(^{16}\text{O}\): 1.92 fm (BHF), 2.05 fm (RBHF); for \(^{40}\text{Ca}\): 2.20 fm (BHF), 2.30 fm (RBHF).
*   Single-particle energy for the neutron \(1p_{3/2}\) orbital in \(^{16}\text{O}\): -37.38 MeV (BHF), -27.85 MeV (RBHF), compared to the experimental value of -21.839 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the traditional BHF approach for finite nuclei by using a \(V_{\text{low-}k}\) softened interaction (rather than the \(G\)-matrix) to handle short-range correlations and by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation exactly in the self-consistent BHF basis, reducing approximations such as the angle-averaged Pauli operator used in earlier work by Sauer (1970) and others. It provides a first benchmark comparison of BHF and RBHF results with other *ab initio* methods, including Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) calculations by Nogga (2004), importance-truncated NCSM (IT-NCSM) by Roth and Navratil (2007), and coupled-cluster (CCSD, CCSD(T)) calculations by Hagen (2007), using the same effective Hamiltonian. The findings enable future systematic comparisons of BHF-based methods with modern *ab initio* approaches and clarify the role of the single-particle potential prescription in determining whether RBHF yields higher or lower ground-state energies than BHF.
