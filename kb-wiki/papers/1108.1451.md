# Pole analysis on unitarized $SU(3)\times SU(3)$ one loop $χ$PT amplitudes

> We analyze $ππ-K\bar{K}$ and $πη-K\bar{K}$ couple channel [1,1] matrix Padé amplitudes of $SU(3)\times SU(3)$ chiral perturbation theory. By fitting phase shift and inelasticity data, we determine pole positions in different channels ($f_0(980)$, $a_0(980)$,$f_0(600)$, $K_0^*(800)$, $K^*(892)$, $ρ(770)$) and trace their $N_c$ trajectories. We stress that a couple channel Breit--Wigner resonance should exhibit two poles on different Riemann sheets and meet each other on the real axis when $N_c=\infty$. Poles are hence classified using this criteria and we conclude that $K^*(892)$ and $ρ(770)$ are unambiguous Breit--Wigner resonances. For scalars the situation is much less clear. We find that $f_0(980)$ is a molecular state rather than a Breit--Wigner resonance, while $a_0(980)$, though behaves oddly when varying $N_c$, does maintain a twin pole structure.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a genuine coupled-channel Breit-Wigner resonance must exhibit two poles on different Riemann sheets that meet on the real axis when the number of colors \(N_c\) is taken to infinity; using this criterion, they classify the poles extracted from unitarized chiral perturbation theory amplitudes, concluding that \(K^*(892)\) and \(\rho(770)\) are unambiguous Breit-Wigner resonances while \(f_0(980)\) is a molecular state and \(a_0(980)\) maintains a twin pole structure despite odd behavior under \(N_c\) variation.

## Method

The authors construct [1,1] matrix Pade approximants from one-loop \(SU(3)\times SU(3)\) chiral perturbation theory amplitudes for the coupled channels \(\pi\pi-K\bar{K}\) and \(\pi\eta-K\bar{K}\). They fit the low-energy constants to experimental phase shift and inelasticity data for several isospin-spin channels, then extract pole positions on different Riemann sheets and trace their trajectories as the number of colors \(N_c\) is varied.

## Key numbers

* \(\pi\pi\) scattering length in the \((I,J)=(0,0)\) channel: \(a^0_0 = 0.213\, m_{\pi}^{-1}\).
* \(\sigma(600)\) pole on sheet II: \(\sqrt{s_{\sigma}} = (0.444 - 0.245i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
* \(f_0(980)\) pole on sheet II: \(\sqrt{s_{f_0}} = (0.974 - 0.026i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
* \(\rho(770)\) poles: sheet II at \((0.740 - 0.069i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\), sheet III at \((0.782 - 0.056i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
* \(K^*(892)\) poles: sheet II at \((0.895 - 0.026i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\), sheet III at \((0.921 - 0.021i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
* \(a_0(980)\) poles: sheet III at \((0.640 - 0.002i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\), sheet IV at \((1.131 - 0.079i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
* \(\kappa(800)\) pole on sheet II: \((0.673 - 0.254i)\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
* Virtual state in \((I,J)=(2,0)\) channel: \(s_v = 0.045\, m_{\pi}^2\) on sheet II.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) pole-trajectory analysis of Pelaez (2003) by explicitly examining the twin-pole structure of resonances in coupled-channel Pade amplitudes, which had not been emphasized previously. The classification of \(f_0(980)\) as a molecular state rather than a Breit-Wigner resonance is consistent with the pole-counting argument of Morgan (1992), while the twin-pole finding for \(a_0(980)\) supports the picture of Kalashnikova et al. that \(a_0(980)\) is more elementary than \(f_0(980)\). The results enable future studies to use the \(N_c\) behavior of shadow poles as a model-independent tool for distinguishing molecular states from conventional \(\bar{q}q\) resonances.
