# Exact Solution of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model and Liquid-Gas Phase Transition in Nuclear Matter

> An exact analytical solution of the statistical multifragmentation model is found in thermodynamic limit. The system of nuclear fragments exhibits a 1-st order liquid-gas phase transition. The peculiar thermodynamic properties of the model near the boundary between the mixed phase and the pure gaseous phase are studied.

## Key claim

The statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) exhibits a first-order liquid-gas phase transition in the thermodynamic limit, but the specific heat per nucleon at the boundary between the mixed phase and the pure gaseous phase shows a finite discontinuity rather than the divergent peak expected from finite-system studies.

## Method

The authors solved the SMM analytically in the grand canonical ensemble by introducing an isobaric partition function (IPF) via a Laplace transform over volume, which reduces the phase transition analysis to studying singularities of the IPF. The gaseous phase corresponds to a simple pole singularity, while the liquid phase corresponds to a singularity of the function F(s,T,μ) itself, representing an infinite fragment with k=∞. The excluded volume effects were treated self-consistently using the Van der Waals approximation with normal nuclear density ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Bulk binding energy per nucleon: W₀ = 16 MeV
- Surface tension parameter: σ₀ = 18 MeV, with critical temperature T_c = 18 MeV (surface tension vanishes at T ≥ T_c)
- Fermi-gas excitation parameter: ε₀ = 16 MeV
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, giving excluded volume parameter b = 1/ρ₀
- Liquid phase density: ρ_l = 1/b = ρ₀ (fixed at normal nuclear density)
- Critical point C exists at T_c = 18 MeV; above this temperature only the pure gaseous phase exists

## Context

This work provides the first rigorous analytical proof of a first-order liquid-gas phase transition in the SMM, which had previously only been studied numerically in the canonical ensemble by Chomaz (1995) and Gulminelli (1998, 1999). The exact solution enables systematic investigation of the phase diagram structure and resolves whether the specific heat peak diverges in the thermodynamic limit, showing it remains finite with a finite discontinuity at the mixed-gas boundary.
