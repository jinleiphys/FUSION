# Neutrino emission, Equation of State and the role of strong gravity

> Neutron-star mergers are interesting for several reasons: they are proposed as the progenitors of short gamma-ray bursts, they have been speculated to be a site for the synthesis of heavy elements, and they emit gravitational waves possibly detectable at terrestrial facilities. The understanding of the merger process, from the pre-merger stage to the final compact object-accreting system involves detailed knowledge of numerical relativity and nuclear physics. In particular, key ingredients for the evolution of the merger are neutrino physics and the matter equation of state. We present some aspects of neutrino emission from binary neutron star mergers showing the impact that the equation of state has on neutrinos and discuss some spectral quantities relevant to their detection such as energies and luminosities far from the source.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the stiffness of the equation of state (EoS) directly affects neutrino emission from binary neutron star mergers, with softer EoS producing higher neutrino energies and luminosities, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific post-processing methodology used to define neutrino surfaces via scattering optical depth rather than effective absorption surfaces.

## Method

The authors post-processed 3D fully relativistic binary neutron star merger simulations (originally from Palenzuela 2015) with three different equations of state (NL3, DD2, SFHo) by computing neutrino surfaces defined as the two-dimensional hypersurfaces where the optical depth τ = 2/3 along the z-axis. They calculated neutrino mean free paths from charged-current and neutral-current processes (scattering from protons, neutrons, and electrons; absorption on nucleons; neutrino-antineutrino annihilation) and then derived average neutrino energies at emission and after applying gravitational redshift to obtain observed quantities at infinity.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star mass for all simulations: 1.35 M⊙
- SFHo EoS predicts radius ≈ 12 km for 1.35 M⊙; DD2 predicts ≈ 13 km; NL3 predicts ≈ 15 km
- Post-merger neutrino luminosities can reach 10^54 erg/s
- For DD2 EoS at 2.5 ms after merger: observed electron antineutrino average energy 18.3 MeV (emitted 22.1 MeV); observed electron neutrino average energy 14.6 MeV (emitted 17.4 MeV)
- For DD2 EoS at 7.4 ms: observed electron antineutrino 13.2 MeV (emitted 16.1 MeV); observed electron neutrino 10.2 MeV (emitted 12.4 MeV)
- For DD2 EoS at 12.3 ms: observed electron antineutrino 12.6 MeV (emitted 15.3 MeV); observed electron neutrino 10.1 MeV (emitted 12.3 MeV)
- For DD2 EoS at 17.2 ms: observed electron antineutrino 10.7 MeV (emitted 13 MeV); observed electron neutrino 8.9 MeV (emitted 10.8 MeV)
- For SFHo EoS at 3.2 ms: observed electron antineutrino 24.6 MeV; observed electron neutrino 23.5 MeV
- For NL3 EoS at 2.5 ms: observed electron antineutrino 18.5 MeV; observed electron neutrino 15.2 MeV
- For NL3 EoS at 8.4 ms: observed electron antineutrino 13.4 MeV; observed electron neutrino 9.8 MeV
- Electron antineutrino surface temperatures reach around 18 MeV at early times (2.5 ms) for DD2, decreasing to ~12 MeV at 7.4 ms

## Context

This work extends the fully relativistic binary neutron star merger simulations of Palenzuela (2015) by providing a detailed post-processing analysis of neutrino surface evolution and its dependence on the equation of state. The results connect to prior work on neutrino-driven nucleosynthesis (Lattimer 1976, Surman) and the role of strong gravity in altering neutrino fluxes and electron fractions (Caballero). The finding that softer EoS (SFHo) produce more energetic neutrinos than stiffer EoS (NL3) enables future discrimination between nuclear matter models using neutrino detection from mergers, and distinguishes merger neutrino signals (lasting tens of milliseconds) from supernova signals (estimated around 10 seconds).
