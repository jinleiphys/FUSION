# Microscopic potential model analysis of the radiative $(n,γ)$ cross sections near the $Z=50$ shell closure taking part in the main s-component

> The neutron capture cross sections have been studied near the $Z=50$ closed shell for a number of nuclei those take part in heavy element nucleosynthesis, the slow and the rapid neutron capture processes and the proton capture process. An optical model potential is constructed in theoretical approach by folding the density dependent M3y purely real nucleon-nucleon interaction with the target radial matter density in relativistic mean field (RMF) approach. The standard code TALYS1.8 is used for cross-section calculation. We have presented the Maxwellian-averaged capture cross-section (MACS) values and stellar neutron capture reaction rates at astrophysically relevant thermal energies and temperatures.

# Microscopic potential model analysis of the radiative (n,γ) cross sections near the Z=50 shell closure taking part in the main s-component

## Key claim

The authors construct a fully theoretical microscopic optical model potential, using the density-dependent M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction folded with relativistic mean-field target densities, to predict neutron capture cross sections and Maxwellian-averaged cross sections (MACS) for nuclei near Z=50. The model reproduces experimental MACS values within approximately 5–25% for most studied nuclei, but the abstract does not highlight that for several isotopes (115In, 128Te, 129I) the predictions either overestimate or underestimate measurements by factors of roughly two, and that the model has known limitations near shell closures due to low level densities.

## Method

The authors construct a microscopic optical model potential by folding the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) real nucleon-nucleon interaction with target radial matter densities obtained from relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the FSU Gold Lagrangian. A spin-orbit interaction term is included according to Scheerbaum's prescription. The compound nuclear cross sections are calculated in the Hauser-Feshbach statistical formalism using the TALYS1.8 reaction code, with level densities from Goriely's microscopic calculations and E1 gamma-ray strength functions from Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations. Width fluctuation corrections are included according to Moldauer's formula.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the same microscopic optical model potential for elastic scattering and capture reactions at low energies (Dutta et al., prior papers on nuclei near N=50, N=82, and other regions). The calculations are compared with experimental data from the KADoNiS database, the MOST2005 theoretical predictions, and the BRUSLIB reaction rate library. The results provide MACS values over a range of thermal energies (5–100 keV) for several unstable branch-point nuclei (121Sn, 122Sb, 127Te, 133Xe) that are inaccessible to experiment and have no prior theoretical estimates (e.g., 127Te). The work also presents reaction rates for rare p-only tin isotopes (112,114,115Sn), which can be used via detailed balance to obtain photodisintegration rates needed for p-process models. The authors note that their fully theoretical approach, using no experimental input except nuclear binding energies, makes the model applicable to nuclei far from stability where experimental data are scarce.
