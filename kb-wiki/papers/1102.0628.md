# Stellar weak decay rates in neutron-deficient medium-mass nuclei

> Weak decay rates under stellar density and temperature conditions holding at the rapid proton capture process are studied in neutron-deficient medium-mass waiting point nuclei extending from Ni up to Sn. Neighboring isotopes to these waiting point nuclei are also included in the analysis. The nuclear structure part of the problem is described within a deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS + QRPA approach, which reproduces not only the beta-decay half-lives but also the available Gamow-Teller strength distributions, measured under terrestrial conditions. The various sensitivities of the decay rates to both density and temperature are discussed. In particular, we study the impact of contributions coming from thermally populated excited states in the parent nucleus, as well as the competition between beta decays and continuum electron captures.

## Key claim

The paper calculates stellar weak decay rates for neutron-deficient medium-mass waiting-point nuclei (Ni to Sn) under rapid proton capture process conditions, finding that while ground-state decays dominate at relevant temperatures (1-3 GK), contributions from thermally populated excited states can become comparable for mid-shell nuclei with very low-lying 2+ states, and that the competition between beta-plus decay and continuum electron capture depends sensitively on the nucleus, temperature, and density.

## Method

The nuclear structure was described using a deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS + QRPA approach with the SLy4 Skyrme force, including both particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions. The phase space factors for beta-plus decay and continuum electron capture were computed as functions of density and temperature, assuming fully ionized atoms with a Fermi-Dirac electron distribution and zero neutrino and positron blocking. Decay rates were calculated for even-even waiting-point nuclei and their neighboring even-even isotopes, considering thermal population of low-lying excited states below 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The most significant conditions for the rp process are T=1.5 GK and ρ=10^6 g cm^-3
- At T=1.5 GK, the maximum population of a low-lying excited state is 12% (for the 2+ state at 261 keV in ^76Sr), while the ground state contributes 88%
- The effective axial-vector coupling ratio used is (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.74 (g_A/g_V)_bare
- The coupling strengths used are χ^ph_GT = 0.15 MeV and κ^pp_GT = 0.03 MeV
- The D value in the decay rate expression is D=6146 s
- The Coulomb displacement energy formula uses ΔE_C = 1.4144 Z̄/A^(1/3) - 0.9127 MeV for T=1 isobaric analog states
- The rp process temperature range of interest is T=1-3 GK

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1980s) for stellar weak-interaction rates by applying a deformed Skyrme-HF+BCS+QRPA approach that has been previously tested against experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions and half-lives for Kr and Sr isotopes (Sarriguren, 2010). The calculations cover waiting-point nuclei identified in rp-process network studies by Schatz (2001) and others, and the results enable more accurate nuclear reaction network calculations for type I X-ray bursts and potential rp-process scenarios in core-collapse supernovae as discussed by Wanajo and Frohlich.
