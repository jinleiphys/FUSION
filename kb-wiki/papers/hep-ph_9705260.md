# Analysis of DCC Domain Structure

> Wavelet-type methods are employed for the analysis of pion field configurations that have been obtained by dynamical simulations in idealized scenarios relevant to the formation of disoriented chiral condensates. It is illustrated how the measurement of the isospin domain structure depends on the ability to zoom in on limited parts of the phase space, due to the interplay between the pion correlation length and the effective source geometry. The need for advanced analysis methods is underscored by the fact that the extracted neutral-fraction distribution would differ significantly from the ideal form, even under perfect experimental conditions, and, moreover, by the circumstance that thermal sources with suitably adjusted temperatures can lead to distributions that may be practically indistinguishable from those arising from DCC-type non-equilibrium evolutions.

## Key claim

Wavelet-type analyses of simulated pion fields show that the extracted neutral-fraction distribution depends strongly on the scale of observation relative to the pion correlation length, and that even under perfect experimental conditions the distribution will differ significantly from the ideal \(1/2\sqrt{f}\) form. Moreover, thermal equilibrium sources at suitably adjusted temperatures can produce neutral-fraction distributions that are practically indistinguishable from those arising from non-equilibrium DCC-type evolutions.

## Method

The authors generated 100 field configurations each for rope (32 fm long, 4×4 fm² cross section) and cube (8×8×8 fm³) geometries using the linear sigma model at the semi-classical level, with initial thermal sampling at 400 MeV followed by rapid cooling via a Rayleigh dissipation term mimicking Bjorken-type expansion in D=2 or D=3 dimensions. They applied Haar and Daubechies (DAUB4, DAUB20) discrete wavelet transforms to the spatial dependence of the neutral pion fraction, extracting neutral-fraction distributions \(P_L(f)\) at successive scales by bisection (rope) or octopartition (cube), and computed the width \(\Delta f\) and wavelet power spectrum \(W_j\) for each scale.

## Key numbers

- Correlation lengths measured by full width at half maximum of the pion correlation function: approximately 2.8 fm for D=3 cooling and 1.6 fm for D=2 cooling.
- For rope events, the effective number of independent sources per slice was estimated as roughly 2 for D=3 and 6 for D=2, based on comparison with analytical results from Randrup (1996).
- The width of the neutral-fraction distribution for the idealized fully coherent case is \(\Delta_0 = 2/\sqrt{45} \approx 0.30\).
- The most rapid growth of \(\Delta f\) with scale reduction occurred for \(4 \leq L \leq 7\), corresponding to physical scales between 1 and 8 fm.
- Thermal equilibrium samples at T=100 MeV and T=200 MeV produced neutral-fraction distributions practically indistinguishable from those of D=3 and D=2 non-equilibrium evolutions, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering wavelet analysis of DCC domain structure by Huang et al. (1995), who performed one-dimensional schematic applications, to fully three-dimensional pion field configurations generated by dynamical simulations of the linear sigma model. The results underscore the need for advanced analysis methods because even perfect experimental conditions cannot yield the ideal \(1/2\sqrt{f}\) distribution, and because thermal sources can mimic DCC signals. The authors suggest that resolving this degeneracy may require combining pion measurements with electromagnetic probes such as photons and dileptons, as studied by other groups.
