# Quasi-SU(3) truncation scheme for odd-even sd-shell nuclei

> The quasi-SU(3) symmetry, as found in shell model calculations, refers to the dominance of the single particle plus quadrupole-quadrupole terms in the Hamiltonian used to describe well deformed nuclei, and to the subspace relevant in its diagonalization. It provides a very efficient basis truncation scheme. It is shown that a small number of SU(3) coupled irreps, those with the largest C_2 values within the direct product of the proton and neutron SU(3) irreps with spin 0 and 1 (for even number of particles), and spin 1/2 and 3/2 for (for odd number of nucleons), are enough to describe the low energy spectra and B(E2) transition strengths of 21Ne, 23Na and 25Mg. A simple but realistic Hamiltonian is employed. Results compare favorably both with experimental data and with full shell model calculations. Limitations and possible improvements of the schematic Hamiltonian are discussed.

## Key claim

The quasi-SU(3) truncation scheme, which selects only the SU(3) coupled irreps with the largest second-order Casimir operator (C₂) values and spins 1/2 and 3/2 for odd-mass nuclei, is sufficient to reproduce low-energy spectra and B(E2) transition strengths in ²¹Ne, ²³Na, and ²⁵Mg, but the agreement is conditional on the use of a schematic Hamiltonian whose rotor terms must be tuned per nucleus and which lacks a proton-neutron pairing term.

## Method

The authors constructed a truncated Hilbert space by selecting the three or four proton and neutron SU(3) irreps with the largest C₂ values and spins 1/2 and 3/2, then coupling them to form proton-neutron SU(3) irreps and keeping only the 16-20 coupled irreps with the largest total C₂ values. They diagonalized a Hamiltonian containing spherical Nilsson single-particle terms, a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, pairing terms, and three rotor terms (a K_J², b J², and A_sym C₂), with parameters fixed from systematics except for the rotor terms which were fitted per nucleus.

## Key numbers

- ²¹Ne: 17 coupled irreps used out of 137 possible in the full SU(3) basis; B(E2; 3/2₁⁺ → 5/2₁⁺) = 1.215 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 1.239 ± 0.155 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- ²¹Ne: B(E2; 5/2₁⁺ → 7/2₁⁺) = 0.710 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 0.505 ± 0.183 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- ²¹Ne: B(E2; 7/2₁⁺ → 9/2₁⁺) = 0.348 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 0.387 ± 0.215 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- ²¹Ne: B(E2; 9/2₁⁺ → 11/2₁⁺) = 0.239 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 0.248 ± 0.165 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- ²³Na: 20 coupled irreps used out of 670 possible; B(E2; 3/2₁⁺ → 5/2₁⁺) = 1.677 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 1.573 ± 0.233 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- ²³Na: B(E2; 5/2₁⁺ → 7/2₁⁺) = 1.123 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 0.777 ± 0.207 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- ²³Na: B(E2; 1/2₁⁺ → 5/2₂⁺) = 2.103 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 2.681 ± 0.583 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- ²⁵Mg: 16 coupled irreps used out of 1821 possible; B(E2; 5/2₁⁺ → 7/2₁⁺) = 1.968 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 1.621 ± 0.289 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- ²⁵Mg: B(E2; 1/2₁⁺ → 5/2₂⁺) = 2.323 × 10⁻² e²b² (experiment: 2.345 ± 1.042 × 10⁻² e²b²)
- Effective charges used for all three nuclei: e_π_eff = 1.56 e, e_ν_eff = 0.56 e
- Hamiltonian parameters for ²¹Ne: χ = 0.1063 MeV, G_π = G_ν = 0.4524 MeV, a = -0.200 MeV, b = -0.020 MeV, A_sym = 0.010 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quasi-SU(3) truncation scheme, previously applied to even-even sd-shell nuclei (Vargas, Hirsch, and Draayer, 2000) and odd-odd sd-shell nuclei, to odd-mass nuclei ²¹Ne, ²³Na, and ²⁵Mg. It builds on the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) and the pseudo-SU(3) model (Hecht and Adler, 1969), and directly improves upon earlier SU(3) studies of these nuclei (Naqvi and collaborators, 1992, 1995) by including spin-3/2 irreps for the first time. The results enable future application of the quasi-SU(3) truncation to heavy deformed nuclei with intruder orbits, as envisioned by Zuker and collaborators (1994), though the authors note that an isospin-invariant Hamiltonian including proton-neutron pairing is needed for full predictive power.
