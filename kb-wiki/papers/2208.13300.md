# Nuclear fragmentation reactions as a probe of neutron skins in nuclei

> We investigate the contributions of various reaction channels to the interaction, reaction, charge-changing and neutron-changing cross sections. The goal is to investigate the relation between microscopic interactions and the symmetry energy component of the equation of state (EoS) of interest for the structure of neutron stars. We have made a comparison of the neutron skins extracted from diverse experimental techniques with those obtained with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with 23 Skyrme and with 8 density-dependent interactions used in the relativistic mean field method. We have shown that no particular conclusion can be drawn on the best EoS in view of the wide range of uncertainty in the experimental data. We have further investigated the prospects of using neutron-changing reactions to assess the isospin dependence of the neutron-skin in neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether nuclear fragmentation reactions can reliably probe neutron skins and the symmetry energy, but concludes that no particular equation of state can be favored due to the wide range of uncertainty in experimental data, despite comparing 23 Skyrme and 8 relativistic mean field interactions.

## Method

The authors used the abrasion-ablation model within Glauber scattering theory to calculate interaction, reaction, charge-changing, and neutron-changing cross sections for nucleus-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions. They systematically evaluated corrections to the first-order eikonal approximation including Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, higher-order eikonal corrections up to 3rd order, Coulomb repulsion, and relativistic effects. Neutron skins extracted from diverse experimental techniques were compared with those obtained from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using 23 Skyrme interactions and 8 density-dependent relativistic mean field interactions.

## Key numbers

* The PREX-2 experiment at Jefferson Laboratory reported a neutron skin value for 208Pb of Δr_np = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm, implying a slope parameter L = 106 ± 37 MeV
* A contrasting measurement reported Δr_np = 0.156 +0.025/-0.021 fm for 208Pb
* The symmetry energy at saturation density J ≈ 30 MeV is compatible with numerous theoretical predictions, while the slope parameter L varies from about 30 MeV to 130 MeV across 23 popular Skyrme interactions
* For 12C + 12C collisions at 30 MeV/nucleon, Fermi motion increases the interaction cross section by about 3%, decreasing to less than 0.1% above 200 MeV/nucleon
* Higher-order eikonal corrections for 12C + 12C collisions are less than 1% even below 100 MeV/nucleon (e.g., 1139 mb to 1133 mb at 50 MeV/nucleon)
* Pauli blocking corrections become important for proton + 208Pb collisions below 50 MeV bombarding energy

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to constrain the symmetry energy term of the nuclear equation of state through laboratory experiments, following studies by Baym (2018), Lattimer (2021), and others. The paper directly addresses the tension between the large neutron skin reported by the PREX collaboration (2021) and smaller values from other experimental techniques and astronomical observations from NICER and LIGO/Virgo. The analysis enables future experiments with radioactive beams at facilities like FAIR to use total neutron-changing cross section measurements as proposed by Aumann (2017) to study neutron skins across isotopic chains, though the authors emphasize that model-dependent systematic uncertainties must be included in error budgets.
