# In-medium properties of pseudoscalar $D_s$ and $B_s$ mesons

> We calculate the shift in masses and decay constants of $D_s(1968)$ and $B_s(5370)$ mesons in hot and dense asymmetric strange hadronic matter using QCD sum rules and chiral SU(3) model. In-medium strange quark condensates $\left\langle \bar{s}s\right\rangle_{ρ_B}$, and gluon condensates $\left\langle \frac{α_{s}}π {G^a}_{μν} {G^a}^{μν} \right\rangle_{ρ_B}$ to be used in the QCD sum rules for pseudoscalar $D_s$ and $B_s$ mesons are calculated using chiral SU(3) model. As an application of our present work, we calculate the in-medium decay widths of the excited (c$\bar{s}$) states $D_s^*(2715)$ and $D_s^*(2860)$ decaying to ($D_s(1968)$,$η$) mesons. The medium effects in their decay widths are incorporated through the mass modification of the $D_s(1968)$ and $η$ mesons. Results of the present investigation may be helpful to understand the possible outcomes of the future experiments like CBM and PANDA under the FAIR facility.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the shift in masses and decay constants of pseudoscalar $D_s(1968)$ and $B_s(5370)$ mesons in hot and dense asymmetric strange hadronic matter, using a combination of QCD sum rules and the chiral SU(3) model, with the caveat that the results are obtained within the heavy quark limit (neglecting the strange quark mass relative to the charm/bottom mass) and using mean-field approximation for the baryonic medium.

## Method

The authors first solve the coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields ($\sigma$, $\zeta$, $\delta$, $\chi$) of the chiral SU(3) model in mean-field approximation to obtain the in-medium strange quark condensate $\langle \bar{s}s \rangle_{\rho_B}$ and gluon condensate $\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^a_{\mu\nu} G^{a\mu\nu} \rangle_{\rho_B}$ as functions of baryon density, temperature, strangeness fraction, and isospin asymmetry. These condensates are then used as input in Borel-transformed QCD sum rules for the pseudoscalar currents of $D_s$ and $B_s$ mesons, where the forward scattering amplitude is parameterized in terms of unknown coefficients $a$ and $b$ that determine the mass and decay constant shifts via linear density approximation. As an application, the in-medium partial decay widths of $D_s^*(2715)$ and $D_s^*(2860)$ decaying to ($D_s(1968), \eta$) are calculated using the $^3P_0$ model, incorporating the medium-modified masses of $D_s(1968)$ and $\eta$ mesons.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum masses used: $D_s(1968)$ meson at 1968 MeV and $B_s(5370)$ meson at 5370 MeV (from the title and abstract)
- Excited states considered: $D_s^*(2715)$ with full decay width approximately 115 MeV and $D_s^*(2860)$ with full decay width approximately 48 MeV (from the introduction)
- The constant $d$ in the chiral model is set to $2/11$, determined through the QCD beta function at one-loop level for three colors and three flavors
- The parameter $\lambda^2$ appears in the relation $\langle\bar{s}g_s\sigma Gs\rangle_{\rho_B} = \lambda^{2}\left\langle \bar{s}s \right\rangle_{\rho_{B}} + 3.0 \textrm{GeV}^{2}\rho_{B}$ (no numerical value for $\lambda^2$ given in the truncated text)
- The relation $\langle \bar{s} i D_0 i D_0s\rangle_{\rho_B} + \frac{1}{8}\langle\bar{s}g_s\sigma Gs\rangle_{\rho_B} = 0.3 \textrm{GeV}^{2}\rho_{B}$ is used for the mixed condensate

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of vector and axial-vector $D_1$ and $B_1$ mesons (Chhabra and Kumar, 2017) using the same QCD sum rule and chiral SU(3) model approach, and builds upon earlier calculations of $D_s$ and $B_s$ meson properties in asymmetric strange hadronic matter by Pathak et al. using a generalized SU(4) chiral model. The results are intended to inform future experiments at the CBM and PANDA facilities under the FAIR project, particularly regarding the interpretation of $J/\psi$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions and the understanding of heavy-flavor transport coefficients in hadronic matter.
