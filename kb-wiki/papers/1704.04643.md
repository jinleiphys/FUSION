# Revealing the collision energy dependence of $η/s$ in RHIC-BES Au+Au collisions using Bayesian statistics

> We investigate the collision energy dependence of $η/s$ in a transport + viscous hydrodynamics hybrid model. A Bayesian analysis is performed on RHIC beam energy scan data for $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6,39,$ and $62.4$ GeV. The resulting posterior probability distributions for the model parameters show a preference for a larger value of $η/s$ at $19.6$ GeV compared to $62.4$ GeV, indicating dependence on baryon chemical potential $μ_B$.

## Key claim

The Bayesian analysis of Au+Au collisions at RHIC beam energy scan energies shows a preference for a larger value of η/s at 19.6 GeV compared to 62.4 GeV, indicating dependence on baryon chemical potential μB, but the present uncertainties prohibit precise statements about the correct parameter values.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining the UrQMD hadron-string cascade for initial non-equilibrium evolution with (3+1)-D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics using a constant η/s and a chiral model equation of state. They performed a Bayesian analysis with Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling and Gaussian process emulators, calibrating approximately 100 training data points per collision energy to experimental data on charged particle multiplicity, pseudorapidity distribution, elliptic flow, identified particle HBT radii, and particle yields.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 19.6 GeV, the posterior probability distribution for η/s shows a visible preference towards values over 0.2
- At √sNN = 62.4 GeV, the probability distribution for η/s peaks at η/s ≈ 0
- At √sNN = 39 GeV, the weighted posterior distribution develops a peak at η/s ≈ 0.1
- The hydro-to-transport switching energy density εSW has both peak and median values in the range ≈ 0.3 – 0.4 GeV/fm³ for all investigated collision energies
- The analysis used approximately 100 training data points produced for each collision energy
- The analysis used O(1000) random walkers for MCMC sampling

## Context

This work extends the earlier RHIC beam energy scan study by Karpenko (2015) which found that best fits required larger η/s at lower collision energies, and applies the Bayesian statistical approach previously used by Bernhard (2016) for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. The results suggest that η/s depends on baryon chemical potential μB, but the large uncertainties, especially from poorly constrained initial state parameters like the transverse and longitudinal smearing widths, prevent precise quantification. The authors expect improvement once the dynamics of initial fluidization are better understood.
