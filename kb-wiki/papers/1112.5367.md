# Partial dynamical symmetries in quantum systems

> We discuss the the notion of a partial dynamical symmetry (PDS), for which a prescribed symmetry is obeyed by only a subset of solvable eigenstates, while other eigenstates are strongly mixed. We present an explicit construction of Hamiltonians with this property, including higher-order terms, and portray their significance for spectroscopy and shape-phase transitions in nuclei. The occurrence of both a single PDS, relevant to stable structures, and of several PDSs, relevant to coexistence phenomena, are considered.

## Key claim

Partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) provide a middle ground between exact dynamical symmetries (where all states are solvable and have good quantum numbers) and complete symmetry breaking (where solvability is lost entirely), by ensuring that a prescribed symmetry is obeyed by only a subset of solvable eigenstates while other eigenstates are strongly mixed.

## Method

The paper constructs Hamiltonians with partial dynamical symmetries using the interacting boson model (IBM) for nuclei, where the U(6) spectrum generating algebra contains U(5), SU(3), and O(6) dynamical symmetry chains. For type I PDS, the algorithm identifies n-boson annihilation operators that annihilate all states in a chosen irreducible representation of the symmetry group, then builds number-conserving interactions from these operators and their Hermitian conjugates; the resulting Hamiltonian preserves solvability and good quantum numbers for that subset of states while mixing others. For type II PDS, the Hamiltonian contains terms from two different chains with a common symmetry subalgebra, so that all states preserve part of the symmetry (the common subalgebra labels) but mix the incompatible higher labels. Type III PDS combines both properties, where only selected states preserve part of the symmetry.

## Key numbers

- For SU(3)-PDS in the IBM, the solvable ground band states \(|[N](2N,0)K=0,L\rangle\) have energies \(E_{PDS}=CL(L+1)\) for \(L=0,2,4,\ldots,2N\) (Eq. 2N0).
- For SU(3)-PDS, the solvable \(\gamma^k(K=2k)\) band states \(|[N](2N-4k,2k)K=2k,L\rangle\) have energies \(E_{PDS}=h_2\,6k(N-2)(2N-2k+1)+CL(L+1)\) for \(k>0\) (Eq. 2N4k2k).
- For \(^{168}\)Er (\(N=16\)), the SU(3)-PDS Hamiltonian uses parameters \(h_2=4\), \(\eta=4\), \(C=13\) keV (Fig. 1 caption).
- For O(6)-PDS, the ground band corresponds to O(6) irrep \(\Sigma=N\) and the spectrum resembles a \(\gamma\)-unstable deformed rotovibrator with eigenenergies \(E_{DS}=4h_0(N-v+2)v + B\tau(\tau+3) + CL(L+1)\) where \(\Sigma=N-2v\) (Eq. eDSo6v).

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetries introduced by Leviatan (2011) and builds on earlier algorithms by Alhassid and Leviatan (1992) and by Ramos, Leviatan, and Van Isacker (2009). The SU(3)-PDS construction is applied to the spectroscopy of \(^{168}\)Er, where it reproduces experimental B(E2) branching ratios for \(\gamma\to g\) transitions without free parameters, improving upon the exact SU(3) dynamical symmetry which predicts degenerate \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) bands. The framework enables description of shape-phase transitions and coexistence phenomena in nuclei where a single dynamical symmetry is insufficient, and provides analytic expressions for matrix elements between solvable states.
