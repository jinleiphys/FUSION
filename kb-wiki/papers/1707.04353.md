# Effective field theory for triaxially deformed nuclei

> Effective field theory (EFT) is generalized to investigate the rotational motion of triaxially deformed even-even nuclei. A Hamiltonian, called the triaxial rotor model (TRM), is obtained up to next-to-leading order (NLO) within the EFT formalism. Its applicability is examined by comparing with a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) for the description of the energy spectra of the ground state and $γ$ band in Ru isotopes. It is found that by taking into account the NLO corrections, the ground state band in the whole spin region and the $γ$ band in the low spin region are well described. The results presented here indicate that it should be possible to further generalize the EFT to triaxial nuclei with odd mass number.

## Key claim
The paper generalizes effective field theory (EFT) to triaxially deformed even-even nuclei, constructing a triaxial rotor model (TRM) Hamiltonian up to next-to-leading order (NLO). The approach works well for ground-state bands across the full spin region studied and for gamma bands at low spin, but the abstract's claim of applicability is hedged by the finding that deviations remain for high-spin states in the gamma band, which the authors attribute to the neglect of vibrational degrees of freedom.

## Method
The authors constructed an EFT Lagrangian for triaxial nuclei using the coset space SO(3)/D₂, where D₂ is the discrete symmetry of a triaxial rotor, and derived the Hamiltonian up to NLO by including terms of order ξ⁴ (fourth power of angular velocity components). They solved the resulting TRM Hamiltonian by diagonalization in a basis of symmetrized Wigner D-functions and fitted the parameters (moments of inertia parameters J₀ and non-rigidity parameters M₀, plus the triaxial deformation angle γ) to experimental energy spectra of Ru isotopes. The results were compared with calculations from a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) based on covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the PC-PK1 effective interaction.

## Key numbers
- For ¹¹²Ru at LO: J₀ = 25.43 ℏ²/MeV (fitted to 0₁⁺, 2₁⁺, 4₁⁺, 6₁⁺ states with γ fixed from CDFT at 38.4°)
- For ¹¹²Ru at NLO with J₀ fixed: M₀ = 1.59 ℏ⁴/MeV³
- For ¹¹²Ru at NLO with J₀ and M₀ simultaneously fitted: J₀ = 24.76 ℏ²/MeV, M₀ = 4.97 ℏ⁴/MeV³
- For ¹¹²Ru with all three parameters fitted (strategy two): J₀ = 24.76 ℏ²/MeV, M₀ = 7.77 ℏ⁴/MeV³, γ = 32.1°
- CDFT-predicted deformation for ¹¹²Ru: β = 0.26, γ = 38.4°
- CDFT-predicted γ values across isotopes range from 19.2° (¹⁰²Ru) to 38.4° (¹¹²Ru)
- The study is restricted to spins I ≤ 10 based on quasi-particle alignment analysis using Harris parameters J₀ = 16.0 ℏ²/MeV and J₁ = 50.0 ℏ²/MeV³

## Context
This work extends the EFT approach for nuclear rotations, previously applied to axially deformed nuclei by Papenbrock and collaborators (2011-2016), to triaxially deformed systems. The EFT results are compared with the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) approach of Niksic (2011), which includes vibrational degrees of freedom and reproduces the data more accurately for high-spin gamma-band states. The paper enables future generalization of the EFT to triaxial nuclei with odd mass number and suggests that vibrational degrees of freedom should be systematically included in the EFT formulation.
