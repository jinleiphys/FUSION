# Solar fusion III: New data and theory for hydrogen-burning stars

> In stars that lie on the main sequence in the Hertzsprung-Russel diagram, like our sun, hydrogen is fused to helium in a number of nuclear reaction chains and series, such as the proton-proton chain and the carbon-nitrogen-oxygen cycles. Precisely determined thermonuclear rates of these reactions lie at the foundation of the standard solar model. This review, the third decadal evaluation of the nuclear physics of hydrogen-burning stars, is motivated by the great advances made in recent years by solar neutrino observatories, putting experimental knowledge of the proton-proton chain neutrino fluxes in the few-percent precision range. The basis of the review is a one-week community meeting held in July 2022 in Berkeley, California, and many subsequent digital meetings and exchanges. The relevant reactions of solar and stellar hydrogen burning are reviewed here, from both theoretical and experimental perspectives. Recommendations for the state of the art of the astrophysical S-factor and its uncertainty are formulated for each of them. Several other topics of paramount importance for the solar model are reviewed, as well: recent and future neutrino experiments, electron screening, radiative opacities, and current and upcoming experimental facilities. In addition to reaction-specific recommendations, also general recommendations are formed.

## Key claim
This review provides the third decadal evaluation of nuclear reaction rates for hydrogen-burning stars, updating the recommendations from Solar Fusion I (1998) and Solar Fusion II (2011), with the caveat that the recommended astrophysical S-factors and their uncertainties represent community consensus based on a July 2022 workshop and subsequent exchanges, rather than new experimental measurements.

## Method
The authors organized a four-day community workshop in July 2022 in Berkeley, California, with 47 participants divided into nine working groups who reviewed experimental and theoretical work completed since the 2011 Solar Fusion II review. Each working group evaluated the astrophysical S-factor and its uncertainty for specific reactions of the proton-proton chain and CNO cycles, producing updated recommendations for use in standard solar models.

## Key numbers
- The pp-chain reaction $^{1}\text{H}(p,e^+\nu)^{2}\text{H}$ has a recommended S(0) value of $4.09 \times 10^{-25}$ MeV b
- The $^{3}\text{He}(^{3}\text{He},2p)^{4}\text{He}$ reaction has S(0) = 5.21 MeV b with S'(0) = -4.90 b and S''(0) = 22.42 MeV$^{-1}$ b
- The $^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}$ reaction has S(0) = $2.05 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV b
- The $^{14}\text{N}(p,\gamma)^{15}\text{O}$ reaction has S(0) = $1.68 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV b
- The predicted pp neutrino flux from standard solar models is $5.95 \times 10^{10}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$ with 0.6% uncertainty (MB22p composition)
- The predicted $^{8}$B neutrino flux is $5.07 \times 10^{6}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$ with 15% uncertainty (MB22p composition)

## Context
This review extends the previous decadal evaluations Solar Fusion I (1998) and Solar Fusion II (2011), motivated by advances from solar neutrino observatories that have achieved few-percent precision on pp-chain neutrino fluxes. The updated reaction rates will serve as input for large-scale asteroseismic surveys such as ESA's PLATO mission and for interpreting results from next-generation neutrino detectors including Hyper-Kamiokande, JUNO, and DUNE.
