# Nuclear Physics of Double Beta Decay

> Study of the neutrinoless $ββ$ decay allows us to put a stringent limit on the effective neutrino Majorana mass, a quantity of fundamental importance. To test our ability to evaluate the nuclear matrix elements that govern the decay rate, it is desirable to be able to describe the allowed two-neutrino decay. It is argued that only low-lying virtual intermediate states are important for that process, and thus it appears that the large-scale shell model evaluation, free from the various difficulties of QRPA, is the preferred method. In the $0ν$ decay, it is shown that there is a substantial cancellation between the paired and broken pair pieces of the nuclear wave function. Thus, despite the popular claim to the contrary, one expects that the $0ν$ rate is also sensitive to the details of nuclear structure. This is reflected in the spread of the theoretical values, leading to the uncertainty of about factor 2-3 in the deduced $<m_ν>$ limit. Finally, brief comment about the $0ν$ decay with the exchange of a very heavy neutrino are made.

# Nuclear Physics of Double Beta Decay

## Key claim

The uncertainty in the deduced effective neutrino Majorana mass limit from neutrinoless double beta decay is about a factor of 2-3 due to the spread in theoretical nuclear matrix elements, contradicting the popular claim that the 0ν rate is insensitive to nuclear structure details.

## Method

The paper reviews the nuclear structure aspects of both two-neutrino (2ν) and neutrinoless (0ν) double beta decay, comparing the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) and large-scale shell model approaches. For 2ν decay, the analysis focuses on the Gamow-Teller amplitudes and the convergence of the sum over low-lying intermediate 1+ states. For 0ν decay, the matrix elements are decomposed into paired (0+) and broken-pair (higher seniority) multipole contributions to demonstrate cancellation effects.

## Key numbers

- The 2ν decay halflives have been observed in ten cases, typically determined to better than 10% accuracy, with values ranging from 1.0×10^19 y (100Mo) to 2.0×10^24 y (128Te)
- The latest Heidelberg-Moscow 76Ge experiment gives a halflife limit of T1/2 > 1.3×10^25 y (90% CL) based on 41.55 kg y of exposure, improving to T1/2 > 5.7×10^25 y (90% CL) with pulse shape analysis
- For 76Ge with ⟨mν⟩ = 1 eV, calculated halflives range from 2.3×10^24 y (QRPA by Staudt) to 1.7×10^25 y (shell model by Caurier97), a factor of 6-7 spread
- The 2ν matrix element exhausts only 10^-5 to 10^-7 of the double Gamow-Teller sum rule
- The exchanged neutrino in 0ν decay has momentum q ~ 50-100 MeV, corresponding to the inverse nucleon-nucleon distance

## Context

This invited talk at the 1999 Workshop on Beta-Decay in Strasbourg reviews the nuclear physics challenges in double beta decay, extending the earlier comprehensive review by Suhonen and Civitarese (1998) on QRPA methods. The paper contrasts the QRPA approach, criticized for its sensitivity to the particle-particle force strength gpp and proximity to collapse, with the large-scale shell model calculations by Caurier and collaborators that avoid these difficulties. The work enables interpretation of the then-current experimental limits from Heidelberg-Moscow and IGEX collaborations, and sets the stage for next-generation ton-scale experiments (GENIUS, CUORE) aiming to probe neutrino masses below 0.1 eV.
