# Charge conservation and the shape of the ridge of two-particle correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We demonstrate that in the framework of the event-by-event hydrodynamics followed by statistical hadronization, the proper charge conservation in the mechanism of hadron production provides the crucial non-flow component and leads to agreement with the two-dimensional two-particle correlation data in relative azimuthal angle and pseudorapidity at soft transverse momenta (p_T<2GeV). The fall-off of the same-side ridge in relative pseudorapidity follows from the fact that a pair of particles with balanced charges is emitted from the same fluid element, whose collective velocity collimates the momenta of the pair. We reproduce basic experimental features of the two-dimensional correlation function, such as the dependence on the relative charge and centrality, as well as the related charge balance functions and the harmonic flow coefficients as functions of the relative pseudorapidity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that local charge conservation in statistical hadronization, implemented as a non-flow component on top of event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics, quantitatively explains the fall-off of the same-side ridge in relative pseudorapidity and the charge dependence of two-particle correlation functions, without requiring (mini)jet contributions.

## Method

The authors used GLISSANDO to generate Glauber-model initial conditions, then ran event-by-event 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics with shear and bulk viscosities, and finally performed statistical hadronization with THERMINATOR at freeze-out temperature T_f. Direct charge balancing was implemented by enforcing that same-species charged hadron-antihadron pairs are produced at the same space-time location, with momenta determined independently via the Cooper-Frye formula, while additional balancing came from neutral resonance decays. Simulations incorporated STAR kinematic cuts (|eta| < 1, p_T < 2 GeV) and 90% detector efficiency.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV (preferred) and 150 MeV (tested)
- Transverse momentum cut for soft physics: p_T < 2 GeV
- For unlike-sign pairs with direct charge balancing, C(+-)-1 drops from the central region to |Delta eta| = 2 by about a factor of 2
- Centrality range shown: 30-40% for the main correlation function results
- Detector efficiency estimated at 90% for registered charged particles in STAR
- For the charge balance function comparison, STAR measurement at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with 0.2 < p_T < 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the nature of the same-side ridge in two-particle correlations, where prior arguments by Trainor (2011) suggested (mini)jets were essential and questioned the applicability of hydrodynamics. The paper builds on the collective harmonic flow framework of Takahashi (2009) and Luzum (2010), and implements the charge balancing mechanism previously studied by Bass (2000), Jeon (2001), and Bozek (2003). The results enable a unified hydrodynamic explanation of the correlation function, charge balance functions, and harmonic flow coefficients v_n^2 as functions of relative pseudorapidity, resolving the ridge puzzle without invoking jet contributions.
