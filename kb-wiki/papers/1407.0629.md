# System-size and energy dependence of particle momentum spectra: The UrQMD analysis of p+p and Pb+Pb collisions

> The UrQMD transport model is used to study a system-size and energy dependence of the pion production in high energy collisions. New data of the NA61/SHINE Collaboration on spectra of negatively charged pions in proton-proton interactions at SPS energies are considered. These results are compared with the corresponding data of the NA49 Collaboration in central Pb+Pb collisions at the same collision energies per nucleon. Mean pion multiplicity per participant nucleon, inverse slope parameter of the transverse momentum spectra, and width of rapidity distribution are investigated. A role of isospin effects is discussed. We find that the UrQMD model predicts a non-monotonous behavior of mean transverse mass with collision energy for positively charged pions at the mid-rapidity in inelastic proton-proton interactions. This will be checked soon experimentally by NA61/SHINE Collaboration.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model predicts a non-monotonous behavior of mean transverse mass with collision energy for positively charged pions at mid-rapidity in inelastic proton-proton interactions, but this prediction is based on a model that underestimates the yield of negatively charged pions in p+p collisions at lower SPS energies, meaning the prediction requires experimental verification by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration and may change with model improvements.

## Method

The cascade version of the UrQMD-3.3p2 transport model (without an intermediate hydro stage) was used to simulate inelastic p+p reactions (approximately 2 million events) and central Pb+Pb collisions (approximately 50,000 events) at SPS energies corresponding to laboratory momenta of 20A, 30A, 40A, 80A, and 158A GeV/c. The model results were compared with NA61/SHINE data on negatively charged pion spectra in p+p interactions and NA49 data on central Pb+Pb collisions at the same collision energies per nucleon. Isospin effects were accounted for by decomposing nucleus-nucleus collisions into weighted contributions from p+p, p+n, and n+n collisions using combinatoric probabilities based on the atomic number and charge of the colliding nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Mean multiplicity of negatively charged pions per participant nucleon in central Pb+Pb collisions is larger than in inelastic p+p collisions at all SPS energies studied (20A to 158A GeV/c).
- The difference in mean total pion multiplicity per participant nucleon between Pb+Pb and p+p collisions changes sign at approximately Elab ≈ 40A GeV in experimental data, but at approximately Elab ≈ 10A GeV in UrQMD results.
- The inverse slope parameter T of the transverse mass spectra for negatively charged pions satisfies T(p+p) < T(Pb+Pb) for the whole SPS energy range, with UrQMD systematically overestimating T in p+p collisions at all SPS energies.
- The rapidity width σ of the π⁻ distribution satisfies σ(Pb+Pb) > σ(p+p) for the whole SPS energy range, with UrQMD overestimating σ(p+p) at low SPS energies.
- At plab = 158A GeV/c, the inverse slope parameters in central Pb+Pb collisions satisfy Tπ < TK < Tp, while in inelastic p+p collisions Tπ ≈ TK ≈ Tp.
- The UrQMD model predicts a non-monotonous behavior of mean transverse mass for π⁺ in inelastic p+p collisions in the laboratory energy range 20-40 GeV, arising from the interplay between baryonic resonance decays and string decays.

## Context

This work extends the NA49 Collaboration's studies of the onset of deconfinement in central Pb+Pb collisions (observed at about 30A GeV/c) by comparing with the first results from the NA61/SHINE system-size scan program at SPS energies. The analysis uses the UrQMD transport model to interpret differences between p+p and Pb+Pb collisions, finding that isospin effects account for a significant part of the observed differences in pion multiplicities. The results enable future experimental checks by NA61/SHINE of the predicted non-monotonous behavior of mean transverse mass for positively charged pions, and motivate improvements to the UrQMD model's treatment of baryonic resonances and string excitation at SPS energies, as discussed in recent work by Uzhinsky (no year given in text).
