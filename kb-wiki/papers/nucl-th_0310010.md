# Superheavy nuclei in relativistic effective Lagrangian model

> Isotopic and isotonic chains of superheavy nuclei are analyzed to search for spherical double shell closures beyond Z=82 and N=126 within the new effective field theory model of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang for the relativistic nuclear many-body problem. We take into account several indicators to identify the occurrence of possible shell closures, such as two-nucleon separation energies, two-nucleon shell gaps, average pairing gaps, and the shell correction energy. The effective Lagrangian model predicts N=172 and Z=120 and N=258 and Z=120 as spherical doubly magic superheavy nuclei, whereas N=184 and Z=114 show some magic character depending on the parameter set. The magicity of a particular neutron (proton) number in the analyzed mass region is found to depend on the number of protons (neutrons) present in the nucleus.

## Key claim

The effective field theory (EFT) model predicts spherical doubly magic superheavy nuclei at N=172 and Z=120, and at N=258 and Z=120, while N=184 and Z=114 show only partial magic character that depends on the specific parameter set used; the magicity of a given neutron or proton number in this mass region is not absolute but depends on the number of the other type of nucleon present.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean field equations derived from the chiral effective Lagrangian of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (EFT model) in spherical symmetry, using the G1 and G2 parameter sets and comparing with the conventional NL3 set. Pairing correlations were treated with a constant pairing matrix element BCS approach that includes quasibound levels to mock up continuum effects, with pairing strengths fitted to known binding energies of Ni and Sn isotopes and N=28 and N=82 isotones. Shell closures were identified using four indicators: two-nucleon separation energies, two-nucleon shell gaps (second differences of binding energies), average pairing gaps, and shell correction energies from the Strutinsky method.

## Key numbers

- For the Z=120 isotopic chain with the G2 parameter set, sharp peaks in the two-neutron shell gap δ₂ₙ appear at N=172, N=184, and N=258; the peak at N=184 is only about one-third the height of the peak at N=172.
- The two-proton shell gap δ₂ₚ for the N=172 isotonic chain with G2 is approximately 5 MeV for Z=120, nearly twice the corresponding neutron gap δ₂ₙ of about 3 MeV.
- For the N=184 isotonic chain, the two-proton shell gap δ₂ₚ shows similar magnitude for Z=114 and Z=120, but both are small compared to the jump observed for (N=172, Z=120).
- The neutron pairing gap Δₙ vanishes at N=172, 184, and 258 for the G2 set in the Z=120 chain, but does not vanish at N=184 for the G1 and NL3 sets.
- The bulk incompressibility K is 215 MeV for the G1 and G2 parameter sets, compared to 271 MeV for the NL3 set.
- The effective mass at saturation M*∞/M is about 0.65 for the EFT sets, larger than the typical value of about 0.60 in conventional RMF parameter sets.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean field studies of superheavy nuclei (Rutz et al., Bender et al., Kruppa et al., Reinhard et al.) by applying the newer effective field theory model of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, which includes additional non-linear scalar-vector and vector-vector meson interactions beyond the conventional σ-ω model. The results confirm the preference for Z=120 and N=172 found in earlier RMF calculations, but also identify N=258 as a candidate spherical shell closure, consistent with earlier work by Bender et al. The finding that magicity depends on the proton-neutron composition contrasts with macroscopic-microscopic predictions (Moller et al.) that favor Z=114 and N=184, and with Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations (Rutz et al., Bender et al.) that often prefer Z=124-126. The spherical calculations provide a baseline for future deformed calculations that would be needed to describe the known deformed superheavy nuclei around Z=108 and N=162 (Moller et al., Smolanczuk et al., Burvenich et al.).
