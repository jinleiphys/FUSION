# Neutral baryonic systems with strangeness

> We review the status as regards the existence of three- and four-body bound states made of neutrons and $Λ$ hyperons. For interesting cases, the coupling to neutral baryonic systems made of charged particles of different strangeness has been addressed. There are strong arguments showing that the $Λnn$ system has no bound states. $ΛΛnn$ strong stable states are not favored by our current knowledge of the strangeness $-1$ and $-2$ baryon-baryon interactions. However, a possible $Ξ^- t$ quasibound state decaying to $ΛΛnn$ might exist in nature. Similarly, there is a broad agreement about the nonexistence of $ΛΛn$ bound states. However, the coupling to $ΞNN$ states opens the door to a resonance above the $ΛΛn$ threshold.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical evidence that three- and four-body neutral baryonic systems made of neutrons and Λ hyperons are generally unbound, but a possible Ξ⁻ t quasibound state decaying to ΛΛnn might exist, and coupling to ΞNN states opens the door to a resonance above the ΛΛn threshold.

## Method

The authors review Faddeev equation calculations with separable potentials and constituent quark cluster models for the Λnn system, and generalized Gaussian variational methods with Yukawa-type Malfliet-Tjon potentials for the ΛΛnn system. They also examine constraints from known hypernuclear binding energies (³ΛH, ⁴ΛHe, ⁴ΛH) and Λp scattering data, and discuss a three-body model treating the dineutron as an elementary particle for the ΛΛnn – Ξ⁻pnn coupled system.

## Key numbers

- The Λnn system is unbound by at least 5–10 MeV, compared to the 0.13 MeV binding energy of hypertritium (³ΛH)
- Fredholm determinant at zero energy for Λnn ranges from 0.38 to 0.42 (quark model) and 0.46 to 0.59 (separable Nijmegen potentials), all positive and far from zero
- To bind Λnn, the ΛN spin-triplet scattering length would need values giving σ_Λp = 943–954 mb at p_Λ=145 MeV/c, versus the experimental value of 180 mb
- The ΛΛnn system remains unbound; a multiplicative factor g_ΛΛ ≥ 1.8 on the ΛΛ attractive strength would be needed to bind it, which would produce a_ΛΛ = −29.15 fm (nearly a bound ΛΛ state)
- With the HAL QCD ΛΛ interaction, a factor g_ΛΛ = 1.6 is needed to bind ΛΛnn
- The ⁴ΛHe 0⁺ ground state (experimental binding 2.21 MeV) becomes overbound to 5.55 MeV when the ΛN tensor coupling is increased to bind Λnn

## Context

This review extends the longstanding conclusion from Dalitz and Downs (1958) that the Λnn system is unbound, confirmed by Garcilazo (1987) and more recent work by Garcilazo et al. (2014), Hiyama et al. (2014), Gal et al. (2014), and Afnan et al. (2015). For ΛΛnn, it contradicts the suggestion by Bleser et al. (2019) that a ⁴_ΛΛn double hypernucleus might explain BNL AGS-E906 data, and disagrees with the bound state found by Richard et al. (2015) using chiral effective field theory parameters. The work enables future studies of the Ξ⁻t quasibound state and resonances in the ΛΛn – ΞNN coupled system, and highlights the need to consider medium corrections when combining scattering data with hypernuclear information.
