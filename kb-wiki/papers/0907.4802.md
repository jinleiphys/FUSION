# High-P_T Physics with Identified Particles

> The suppression of high-P_T particles in heavy ion collisions was one of the key discoveries at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. This is usually parameterized by the average rate of momentum-transfer squared to this particle, qhat. Here we argue that measurements of identified particles at high P_T can lead to complementary information about the medium. The leading particle of a jet can change its identity through interactions with the medium. Tracing such flavor conversions could allow us to constrain the mean free path. Here we review the basic concepts of flavor conversions and discuss applications to particle ratios and elliptic flow. We make a prediction that strangeness is enhanced at high P_T at RHIC energies while its elliptic flow is suppressed.

## Key claim

Measurements of identified particles at high transverse momentum can provide complementary information about the quark-gluon plasma beyond the standard energy-loss parameter \(\hat q\), specifically by constraining the mean free path \(\lambda\) of fast partons through flavor conversion processes.

## Method

The authors model flavor conversions of the leading jet parton via binary collisions with thermal partons from the quark-gluon plasma, such as \(q+\bar q \leftrightarrow g+g\) and \(q+g \leftrightarrow g+q\). They compute the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and azimuthal asymmetry \(v_2\) for identified hadrons (pions, protons, kaons) using perturbative elastic rates with a \(K\) factor of 4, comparing results with and without conversion processes switched on.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of nuclear modification factors for protons and pions approaches one when flavor conversions are allowed, compared to a larger suppression without conversions.
- For neutral kaons at RHIC, a clear enhancement in \(R_{AA}\) is predicted extending above 10 GeV/\(c\) when conversion processes are included.
- Strangeness contributes only a small fraction (\(< 5\%\)) to the jet sample at RHIC, while it is equilibrated in the quark-gluon plasma.
- No significant yields of charm or bottom quarks at high \(P_T\) are found from conversion processes, even at LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of jet energy loss parameterized by \(\hat q\) (Wang 1991, BDMPS 1996, Zakharov 1996, Wiedemann 2000, Gyulassy 2003, AMY 2002) by proposing that the mean free path \(\lambda\) can be separately constrained via hadro-chemistry changes. It builds on earlier discussions of jet conversions for photons and dileptons (Fries 2003, Fries 2005, Simon 2005, Turbide 2007) and on calculations showing that photons from jet-medium interactions produce a negative contribution to \(v_2\) (Turbide 2005, Chatterjee 2005). The predictions enable future experimental tests at RHIC of strangeness enhancement and suppressed elliptic flow for kaons at high \(P_T\).
