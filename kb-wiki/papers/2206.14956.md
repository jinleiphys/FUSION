# Uncertainty quantification of transition operators in the empirical shell model

> While empirical shell model calculations have successfully described low-lying nuclear data for decades, only recently has significant effort been made to quantify the uncertainty in such calculations. Here we quantify the statistical error in effective parameters for transition operators in empirical calculations in the $sd$ ($1s_{1/2}$-$0d_{3/2}$-$0d_{5/2}$) valence space, specifically the quenching of Gamow-Teller transitions, effective charges for electric quadrupole (E2) transitions, and the effective orbital and spin couplings for magnetic dipole (M1) transitions. We find the quenching factor for Gamow-Teller transitions relative to free-space values is tightly constrained and that the isoscalar coupling of E2 is much more tightly constrained than the isovector coupling. For effective M1 couplings, we found isovector components more constrained than isoscalar, but that to get any sensible result we had to fix one of four couplings. This detailed quantification of uncertainties, while highly empirical, nonetheless is an important step towards interpretation of experiments.

## Key claim

The quenching factor for Gamow-Teller transitions relative to free-space values is tightly constrained to Q = 0.762 ± 0.025, but this result depends on the assumption that the covariance of Hamiltonian parameters with respect to energies is the same as their covariance with respect to transition observables.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian parameter estimation using Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling with the emcee library, marginalizing over the 66 parameters of the USDB empirical shell-model interaction in the sd valence space. They approximated the posterior distribution for transition operator parameters by summing likelihoods over 2,500 samples of the Hamiltonian parameters drawn from a multivariate normal distribution derived from a prior sensitivity analysis of energy data.

## Key numbers

- Gamow-Teller quenching factor: Q = 0.762 ± 0.025, based on 185 low-lying beta-plus, beta-minus, and electron-capture transitions
- Prior estimate from Richter et al. (2008) gave Q = 0.764 ± 0.114, showing the new result is more tightly constrained
- The USDB interaction has 66 parameters, of which only 10 linear combinations account for 99.9% of the Hamiltonian variance
- E2 data set was reduced from 544 to 483 transitions by excluding strengths below 0.1 times and above 150 times the Weisskopf single-particle estimate
- M1 data set was reduced from 575 to 281 transitions and from 73 to 72 moments by excluding strengths below 0.01 times the Weisskopf estimate and states above 6 excitations

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity analysis of the USDB interaction performed by Fox et al. (previous work) to transition operators, and builds on the foundational empirical shell-model calculations of Brown and Richter (2006). The results enable more meaningful comparisons between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements at facilities like FRIB, DUNE, and neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments, though the authors note that a fully Bayesian analysis treating Hamiltonian and operator parameters jointly would be preferable but is computationally challenging.
