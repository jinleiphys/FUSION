# α-decay half-lives for even-even isotopes of W to U

> We investigate α-decay half-lives for 74 {\le} Z {\le} 92 even-even nuclei within the semiclassical WKB approximation in deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). The α-particle preformation factors are estimated from cluster-formation model using both empirical AME2020 binding energies and numerical ones obtained by a deep neural network (DNN) study in which available DRHBc binding energies are used as training set. We find that our estimated α-decay half-lives are qualitatively in agree with experimental results. We also compare our results with the empirical formulae, ZZCW and UNIV. Based on these observation, we extend our predictions of α-decay half-lives for the isotopes whose experimental data are not available.

## Key claim

The authors find that their calculated α-decay half-lives for even-even nuclei (Z=74 to 92) are "qualitatively in agree with experimental results" within a standard deviation of σ=0.437 in log₁₀(T₁/₂), but this agreement depends on using binding energies from a deep neural network (DNN) trained on DRHBc results and AME2020 data, since the DRHBc framework itself cannot yet provide binding energies for odd-A nuclei needed to compute preformation factors.

## Method

The authors calculate α-decay half-lives using the semiclassical WKB approximation with nuclear density distributions obtained from deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 density functional. The α-particle preformation factors are estimated from the cluster-formation model using binding energies from either empirical AME2020 data or a deep neural network (DNN) study that uses available DRHBc binding energies as a training set. The nuclear and Coulomb potentials between the daughter nucleus and α-particle are constructed via the double-folding model with M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the normalization factor λ is determined by the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation σ = 0.437 in log₁₀(T₁/₂) between calculated and experimental α-decay half-lives for 67 nuclei with α-decay branching ratio > 99%
- Preformation factor Pα for ²¹²Po estimated as 0.221 in the cluster-formation model (compared to 0.3 from microscopic calculations)
- Calculated α-decay half-lives for ¹⁸⁰W: 10²⁵·⁷ s, for ¹⁸⁶Os: 10²²·⁸ s, for ¹⁹⁰Pt: 10¹⁹·¹⁸³ s (the longest half-lives in this study)
- Quadrupole deformation parameters from DRHBc: β₂,ₚₐᵣ = 0.243 for ¹⁸⁰W, β₂,ₚₐᵣ = 0.209 for ¹⁸⁶Os, β₂,ₚₐᵣ = 0.163 for ¹⁹⁰Pt
- Pairing strength V₀ = -325 MeV·fm³ and angular momentum cutoff Jₘₐₓ = 23/2 ħ used in DRHBc calculations

## Context

This work extends the application of DRHBc theory, previously used to study deformed halo structure (Sun 2018), neutron drip line shifts (In 2020), and bubble structure (Choi 2022), to systematic α-decay half-life predictions. The approach combines DRHBc density distributions with the cluster-formation model for preformation factors (SalehAhmed 2013, Ahmed 2015) and compares results with empirical formulae ZZCW and UNIV. The study enables predictions of α-decay half-lives for even-even isotopes of W to U where experimental data are not available, particularly for nuclei far from β-stability that will be accessible at new radioactive ion beam facilities such as RIBF at RIKEN, FRIB, HIAF, FAIR, and RAON.
