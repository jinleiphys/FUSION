# Effective Field Theory Approach To \vec{n} + \vec{p} -> d + γAt Threshold

> Previously, in an effective field theory formulated by us, we have carried out parameter-free calculations of a large number of low-energy two-nucleon properties. An experiment at the Institut Laue-Langevin is currently measuring spin-dependent effects in the polarized np capture process \vec{n}+\vec{p} -> d +γat threshold. Noting that spin-dependent observables for this reaction are sensitive to terms of chiral orders higher than hitherto studied, we extend our effective theory approach to this process and make parameter-free predictions on the spin-dependent observables.

## Key claim

The authors extend their effective field theory (EFT) approach to make parameter-free predictions for spin-dependent observables in the polarized neutron-proton capture process \(\vec{n}+\vec{p}\rightarrow d+\gamma\) at threshold, noting that these observables are sensitive to higher chiral-order terms not previously studied. The predictions are genuine and unbiased by pre-existing numbers, but rely on a hard-core cutoff scheme whose reliability is less certain for these "unprotected" isoscalar matrix elements than for previously studied chiral-filter-protected quantities.

## Method

The authors adopt the Weinberg scheme of chiral perturbation theory, using a hybrid approach where one-body and two-body transition operators derived from EFT are sandwiched between initial and final two-nucleon wavefunctions obtained from the Argonne \(v_{18}\) phenomenological potential. They compute the isoscalar M1 (M1S) and isoscalar E2 (E2S) matrix elements up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiral counting, including one-loop and counter-term contributions, and fix the remaining unknown parameter \(g_4'\) using the deuteron magnetic moment as input.

## Key numbers

* \(\MS = 5.78 \pm 0.03\) fm (isovector M1 amplitude), compared to the empirical value \(\sqrt{\sigma_{unpol}^{exp}} = 5.781 \pm 0.004\) fm.
* One-body M1S amplitude: \(\mut_{\rm 1B} = -4.297 \times 10^{-3}\) fm.
* One-body E2S amplitude: \(\Qt_{\rm 1B} = 1.394 \times 10^{-3}\) fm.
* Unpolarized cross section: \(\sigma_{unpol}^{th} = 334 \pm 3\) mb, compared to the experimental value \(\sigma_{unpol}^{exp} = 334.2 \pm 0.5\) mb (Cox).
* Deuteron binding energy: \(B_d \simeq 2.224575\) MeV; asymptotic \(D/S\) ratio: \(\eta_d \simeq 0.0250\); \(^3S_1\) scattering length: \(a_t \simeq 5.4192\) fm; deuteron normalization factor: \(A_s \simeq 0.8850\) fm\(^{-1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous EFT calculations of low-energy two-nucleon properties (Park et al., 1999) to spin-dependent observables in polarized np capture, which are not protected by the "chiral filter" hypothesis (Kubodera et al., 1978; Rho, 1991). The predictions are designed to be tested by the ongoing ILL experiment (Muller et al.), which will measure the photon circular polarization \(P_\gamma\) and the anisotropy \(\eta_\gamma\), potentially allowing extraction of the individual M1S and E2S amplitudes. The approach contrasts with the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme of Kaplan et al. (1998), which the authors argue sacrifices predictivity for systematic power counting.
