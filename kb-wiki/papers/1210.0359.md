# What does it mean to have `seen' the quark-gluon plasma?

> Identifying the quark-gluon plasma requires convincing experimental evidence that partons move independently throughout the environment created in a heavy ion collision and with densities expected from equilibrium considerations. In lattice calculations, charge correlations suggest that quarks exist independently, and are not merely exchanged from hadronic object to another. Many experimental signatures (J/Psi suppression, quark number scaling, etc.) suggest that quarks are not confined to their original singlets, but these signatures do not make a clear case that quarks move independently or that they have the expected densities. I discuss a class of measurements that parallel lattice observables and has the prospect of investigating whether partonic charges move independently.

# What does it mean to have `seen' the quark-gluon plasma?

## Key claim
Identifying the quark-gluon plasma requires not only evidence that quarks are not confined to their original singlets (as suggested by J/Psi suppression and quark number scaling), but also convincing experimental evidence that partons move independently throughout the environment with densities expected from equilibrium considerations. The paper proposes that charge-charge correlation measurements between identified hadron species can provide this evidence by paralleling lattice observables of charge fluctuations.

## Method
The author derives a formalism connecting the charge-charge correlation matrix from lattice QCD, $\chi_{ab} = \langle Q_a Q_b \rangle / V$, to experimentally measurable hadronic correlators $g_{\alpha\beta}(\Delta\eta)$ between identified hadron species. The method assumes two distinct scales of charge creation: a broad diffusive component from the quark-gluon plasma phase with width $\sigma_{\rm(QGP)}$ and a narrow component from hadronization with width $\sigma_{\rm(HAD)}$, then converts the charge correlators to hadronic correlators using thermal weights and a blast wave model with Monte Carlo decays.

## Key numbers
- Lattice calculations show the transition from hadrons to partons occurs in a temperature window around 175 MeV.
- The diffusive widths are estimated as $\sigma_{\rm(QGP)} \approx 1$ unit of rapidity and $\sigma_{\rm(HAD)} \approx 0.2$ units of rapidity for the two-wave model.
- For a partonic gas, the charge correlation matrix satisfies $\chi_{ab}^{\rm(QGP)} = 2\delta_{ab} n_a$, where $n_a$ is the density of up, down, or strange quarks.
- The integral of the charge-charge correlator over rapidity difference satisfies $\int d\Delta\eta~g_{ab}(\Delta\eta) = -\chi_{ab}^{\rm(HAD)}$, where $\chi^{\rm(HAD)}$ is defined with densities per unit spatial rapidity taken from final-state yields.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD calculations of charge fluctuations by the Wuppertal-Budapest group (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2012) and prior theoretical work on charge balance functions (Koch 2005). It provides a framework to test whether the quark-gluon plasma observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC is a plasma of independently moving partons with equilibrium densities, rather than merely a state where quarks are not confined to their original singlets. The method enables quantitative tests of chemical evolution models, including whether strangeness enhancement results from a chemically equilibrated plasma, and can be applied to recent high-statistics data from STAR and ALICE with good time-of-flight measurements.
