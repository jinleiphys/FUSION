# Nucleon exchange in heavy-ion collisions within stochastic mean-field approach

> Nucleon exchange mechanism is investigated in deep-inelastic symmetric heavy-ion collisions in the basis of the Stochastic Mean-Field approach. By extending the previous work to off-central collisions, analytical expression is deduced for diffusion coefficient of nucleon exchange mechanism. Numerical calculations are carried out for ${}^{40}$Ca + ${}^{40}$Ca and ${}^{90}$Zr + ${}^{90}$Zr systems and the results are compared with the phenomenological nucleon exchange model. Also, calculations are compared with the available experimental results of deep-inelastic collisions between calcium nuclei.

## Key claim
The Stochastic Mean-Field (SMF) approach, when applied to off-central deep-inelastic symmetric heavy-ion collisions, yields a nucleon diffusion coefficient whose time-integrated width of the fragment mass distribution is consistent with the empirical formula from the phenomenological nucleon exchange model, but only for initial angular momenta outside the orbiting region where the drift term can be neglected.

## Method
The authors extended their previous SMF work from central to off-central collisions by employing a geometric projection procedure to extract the nucleon diffusion coefficient. They used 3D time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations to generate time-dependent single-particle wave functions for the symmetric systems, then applied a smoothing procedure to the quantal phase-space distributions to obtain semi-classical reduced Wigner functions on the reaction plane. The diffusion coefficient was computed from these smoothed distributions using an analytical expression derived from the SMF approach, and the width of the fragment mass distribution was obtained by time-integrating the diffusion coefficient.

## Key numbers
- For the ${}^{40}$Ca + ${}^{40}$Ca system at $E_{\text{cm}}=110$ MeV and initial orbital angular momentum $l=70\hbar$, the diffusion coefficient and mass distribution width were calculated as functions of time.
- For the ${}^{90}$Zr + ${}^{90}$Zr system at $E_{\text{cm}}=300$ MeV, calculations were performed for three different initial orbital angular momenta.
- The asymptotic dispersion $\sigma_{AA}(\infty)$ for ${}^{40}$Ca + ${}^{40}$Ca at $E_{\text{cm}}=110$ MeV was computed for four impact parameters corresponding to initial angular momenta $L>69.5\hbar$ (outside the orbiting region).
- Experimental data for ${}^{40}$Ca + ${}^{40}$Ca at $E_{\text{cm}}=128$ MeV show fragment mass dispersions between $\sigma_{AA}=2.8$ amu and $4.6$ amu over the measured angular range; SMF calculations are consistent with these measurements.

## Context
This work extends the SMF approach from central collisions (Washiyama, Yilmaz, earlier works) to off-central deep-inelastic collisions, providing a more realistic comparison with experiment. The SMF-derived diffusion coefficient has a similar form to that of the phenomenological nucleon exchange model (Randrup, 1982; Frei, Adamian, later works) but offers a more refined microscopic description. The results enable future extension to asymmetric systems, particularly relevant for understanding two-particle transfer channels in reactions such as ${}^{40}$Ca + ${}^{96}$Zr, where pairing alone (Sca13, Cor11) cannot explain experimental enhancements.
