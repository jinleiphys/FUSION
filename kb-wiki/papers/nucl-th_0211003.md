# Neutrino mean free path and in-medium nuclear interaction

> Neutrinos produced during the collapse of a massive star are trapped in a nuclear medium (the proto-neutron star). Typically, neutrino energies (10-100 MeV) are of the order of nuclear giant resonances energies. Hence, neutrino propagation is modified by the possibility of coherent scattering on nucleons. We have compared the predictions of different nuclear interaction models. It turns out that their main discrepancies are related to the density dependence of the k-effective mass as well as to the onset of instabilities as density increases. This last point had led us to a systematic study of instabilities of infinite matter with effective Skyrme-type interactions. We have shown that for such interactions there is always a critical density, above which the system becomes unstable.

# Neutrino mean free path and in-medium nuclear interaction

## Key claim
The neutrino mean free path in dense nuclear matter is sensitive to the density dependence of the k-effective mass and to the onset of spin instabilities; for Skyrme-type interactions, a ferromagnetic transition always occurs at some critical density (below about 3.5 times saturation density), but this prediction is not confirmed by microscopic calculations using realistic bare interactions.

## Method
The authors calculated neutrino mean free paths in pure neutron matter and asymmetric nuclear matter in beta equilibrium using effective Skyrme (SLy230a) and Gogny (D1P) interactions, both in the mean-field approximation and within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) to include nuclear correlations. They also performed a systematic stability analysis of infinite nuclear matter with general Skyrme interactions by imposing Landau parameter inequalities, reducing the ten-parameter problem to three free parameters (t1x1, t2x2, t3x3) constrained by empirical saturation properties.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino energies considered: 10-100 MeV
- Density range studied: 1 to 3 times ρ0 (saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter)
- Temperatures used in mean free path calculations: 5 MeV and 20 MeV
- All Skyrme interactions predict ferromagnetism for densities below approximately 3.5 ρ0
- The critical density for Skyrme interactions does not exceed 3.5-4 times ρ0 for a reasonable choice of empirical inputs
- Stability domains were computed at densities of ρ0, 1.5ρ0, 2.0ρ0, 2.5ρ0, and 3.0ρ0 (in steps of 0.5ρ0)

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of neutrino propagation in proto-neutron stars by Iwamoto (1982) and Navarro (1999), and compares predictions from Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions. The finding that Skyrme interactions inevitably predict ferromagnetic instabilities at high density contradicts recent microscopic calculations using diffusion Monte Carlo (Fantoni, 2001) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Vidaña, 2002; Lombardo, 2002), which find no such instability. The systematic stability analysis (Margueron, 2002) provides a framework for identifying Skyrme parametrizations that can simultaneously describe finite nuclei and avoid unphysical high-density behavior, enabling more reliable predictions of neutrino mean free paths for supernova and neutron star physics.
