# BCS-BEC Crossover in Symmetric Nuclear Matter at Finite Temperature: Pairing Fluctuation and Pseudogap

> By adopting a $T$-matrix based method within $G_0G$ approximation for the pair susceptibility, we studied the effects of pairing fluctuation on the BCS-BEC crossover in symmetric nuclear matter. The pairing fluctuation induces a pseudogap in the excitation spectrum of nucleon in both superfluid and normal phases. The critical temperature of superfluid transition was calculated. It differs from the BCS result remarkably when density is low. We also computed the specific heat which shows a nearly ideal BEC type temperature dependence at low density but a BCS type behavior at high density. This qualitative change of the temperature dependence of specific heat may serve as a thermodynamic signal for BCS-BEC crossover.

## Key claim

Pairing fluctuations in symmetric nuclear matter induce a pseudogap in the nucleon excitation spectrum both below and above the superfluid transition temperature, and the critical temperature differs significantly from the BCS result at low density, with the specific heat showing a qualitative change from BEC-type to BCS-type temperature dependence across the crossover.

## Method

The author adopted a T-matrix-based method within the G0G (one free and one full nucleon propagator) approximation for the pair susceptibility, extending the BCS mean-field theory to include pairing fluctuation effects. The coupled equations for the total excitation gap, pseudogap, and nucleon chemical potential were solved self-consistently at finite temperature using a density-dependent contact interaction (DDCI) with parameters reproducing realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials in the 3S1 (np pairing) channel. The formalism was applied separately for temperatures below and above the critical temperature Tc, with the pseudogap above Tc determined by the effective pair chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature Tc at low density limit follows Tc ≈ 0.218 εF, the BEC transition temperature for bosons of mass mb ≈ 2m.
* The chemical potential changes sign at density ρ/ρ0 ∼ 0.002, marking the boundary between BEC and crossover regions.
* The unitary point where 1/(kF a) = 0 occurs at ρ/ρ0 ∼ 0.06, where the np scattering length diverges.
* At density ρ = 0.0001 ρ0, the BCS critical temperature Tc^BCS is almost ten times larger than the Tc obtained from the G0G formalism.
* The right vertical line separating BCS from crossover region is at ρ/ρ0 ∼ 0.5, where the scaled pair size ξ/d = 1.
* At low density ρ ≲ 0.01 ρ0, the pseudogap Δpg(T=Tc) is roughly equal to the zero-temperature excitation gap Δ(T=0).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter by Alm (1993), Stein (1994), Baldo (1995), Lombardo (2001), Isayev (2004), Sedrakian (2005), and Mao (2008) by including pairing fluctuation effects that were neglected in those mean-field treatments. The T-matrix formalism follows the approach developed by the Chicago group (Chen, 1998; Levin, 1999-2009) for high-Tc superconductors and cold atomic gases, and generalizes the early works of Kadanoff and Martin (1961) and Patton (1971). The results enable future studies of strongly coupled low-density nuclear matter relevant to the surface of nuclei, expanding nuclear matter from heavy-ion collisions, and collapsing stars.
