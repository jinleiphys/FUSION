# Diffusion of $Λ_c$ in hot hadronic medium and its impact on $Λ_c/D$ ratio

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of the $Λ_c(2286$ MeV) have been evaluated in the hadronic medium which is expected to be formed in the later stages of the evolving fire ball produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The interactions between the $Λ_c$ and the hadrons in the medium have been derived from an effective hadronic Lagrangian as well as from the scattering lengths, obtained in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$χ$PT). In both the approaches, the magnitude of the transport coefficients are turn out to be significant. A larger value is obtained in the former approach with respect to the latter. Significant values of the coefficients indicate substantial amount of interaction of the $Λ_c$ with the hadronic thermal bath. Furthermore, the transport coefficients of the $Λ_c$ is found to be different from the transport coefficients of $D$ meson. Present study indicates that the hadronic medium has a significant impact on the $Λ_c/D$ ratio in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The drag and diffusion coefficients of the \(\Lambda_c\) baryon in a hot hadronic medium are significant, but their magnitude depends strongly on the theoretical approach used: the effective hadronic Lagrangian yields larger values than the scattering-length method based on heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT). The impact on the \(\Lambda_c/D\) ratio in heavy ion collisions is therefore subject to a band of uncertainty.

## Method

The drag and diffusion coefficients of the \(\Lambda_c\) (mass 2286 MeV) were computed in a hadronic medium of pions, kaons, \(\eta\) mesons, and nucleons at temperatures between \(T_F \sim 120\) MeV and \(T_c \sim 170\) MeV. Two interaction models were used: (1) momentum-independent \(T\)-matrix elements extracted from scattering lengths obtained by Liu et al. within HB\(\chi\)PT at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO); (2) momentum-dependent matrix elements from an effective hadronic Lagrangian with \(SU(4)\) flavor symmetry, including \(s\)- and \(u\)-channel diagrams via \(\Sigma_c\) and \(D\) resonances. The Fokker-Planck equation was then solved to evolve the momentum distributions of \(\Lambda_c\) and \(D\) mesons through the hadronic phase.

## Key numbers

* Drag coefficient of \(\Lambda_c\) at \(T=170\) MeV: the maximum and minimum values from the scattering-length approach give \(R_{AA} \sim 16\%\) and \(4\%\) respectively for a hadronic lifetime \(\Delta\tau = 5\) fm/c.
* Drag coefficient of \(D\) meson at \(T=170\) MeV: yields \(R_{AA} \sim 14\%\) for \(\Delta\tau = 5\) fm/c.
* The \(\Lambda_c/D\) ratio after the hadronic phase can vary by up to \(12\%\) depending on the minimum to maximum values of the \(\Lambda_c\) drag coefficients.
* Scattering lengths from HB\(\chi\)PT: \(\Lambda_c\pi\) = 0.06 fm; \(\Lambda_cK\) = -0.032 \(\pm\) 0.038 fm; \(\Lambda_c\overline{K}\) = (0.79 + 0.27i) \(\pm\) 0.044 fm; \(\Lambda_c\eta\) = (0.35 + 0.19i) \(\pm\) 0.044 fm.
* Coupling constant in the effective Lagrangian: \(g = 0.37\) for the \(\Lambda_c\Sigma_c\pi\) vertex, with a form-factor cutoff \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy-flavor transport in the hadronic phase, which had focused on \(D\) and \(B\) mesons (e.g., Das et al. (2013), Min He et al., Abreu et al.), by providing the first evaluation of \(\Lambda_c\) transport coefficients. The results enable more realistic modeling of the \(\Lambda_c/D\) ratio in heavy ion collisions, which is crucial for interpreting non-photonic electron spectra and the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) measured at RHIC and LHC. The enhancement of the \(\Lambda_c/D\) ratio in the hadronic phase found here complements earlier work on quark coalescence in the QGP phase by Lee et al. and Greco et al.
