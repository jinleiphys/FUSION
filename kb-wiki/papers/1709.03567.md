# Collective long-range particle correlations in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC with the CMS detector

> Measurements of two-particle angular correlations of inclusive charged particles as well as identified strange hadron in pp and pPb collisions are presented over a wide range in pseudorapidity and full azimuth. The data were collected using the CMS detector at the LHC, with nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV for pPb collisions and 5, 7, 13 TeV for pp collisions. The results are compared to semi-peripheral PbPb collision data at center-of-mass energy of 2.76 TeV, covering similar charged-particle multiplicity ranges of the events. For high-multiplicity pp and pPb events, a clear particle species dependence of $v_2$ is observed for $p_{T} \lesssim 2$ GeV/c. When divided by the number of constituent quarks and compared at the same transverse kinetic energy per quark, a universal scaling over a broad range of particle transverse kinetic energy has been observed. For high-multiplicity pp collisions at 13 TeV, the $v_2$ values obtained for inclusive charged particles with two-, four- and six-particle correlations are found to be comparable within uncertainties. These measurements provide strong evidence for the collective nature of the long-range correlations observed in pp collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed particle-species dependence of the second-order Fourier harmonic \(v_2\) and the constituent-quark-number scaling of \(v_2\) in high-multiplicity pp and pPb collisions provide strong evidence for the collective nature of long-range correlations, but the abstract hedges by noting that the underlying mechanism in small collision systems remains poorly understood and that alternative models (AMPT, UrQMD, Color Glass Condensate + fragmentation) can also qualitatively explain the observations.

## Method

Two-particle angular correlations of inclusive charged particles and identified strange hadrons (\(K^0_S\) and \(\Lambda/\bar{\Lambda}\)) were measured over a wide range in pseudorapidity and full azimuth using the CMS detector at the LHC. The data included pp collisions at center-of-mass energies of 5, 7, and 13 TeV and pPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, with results compared to semi-peripheral PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV covering similar charged-particle multiplicity ranges. The azimuthal correlations at large relative pseudorapidity were used to extract the second-order (\(v_2\)) and third-order (\(v_3\)) Fourier harmonics as functions of event multiplicity and particle transverse momentum (\(p_T\)), with residual jet contributions subtracted using low-multiplicity pp events.

## Key numbers

- For high-multiplicity pp and pPb events, a clear particle species dependence of \(v_2\) is observed for \(p_T \lesssim 2\) GeV/c, with \(K^0_S\) particles (lighter) showing larger \(v_2\) values than \(\Lambda/\bar{\Lambda}\) particles (heavier) at the same \(p_T\).
- When divided by the number of constituent quarks and compared at the same transverse kinetic energy per quark, \(v_2\) for \(K^0_S\) and \(\Lambda/\bar{\Lambda}\) particles are consistent over a broad range of particle transverse kinetic energy in pp and pPb collisions.
- For high-multiplicity pp collisions at 13 TeV, the \(v_2\) values obtained for inclusive charged particles with two-, four-, and six-particle correlations are comparable within uncertainties.
- The total integrated luminosity was 35 nb\(^{-1}\) for pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, and 1.0 pb\(^{-1}\), 6.2 pb\(^{-1}\), and 0.7 pb\(^{-1}\) for pp collisions at 5, 7, and 13 TeV, respectively.
- In pp collisions, \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) values of inclusive charged particles increase with multiplicity for \(N_{\text{trk}}^{\text{offline}} \lesssim 100\) and reach a relatively constant value at higher multiplicities.

## Context

This work extends the study of long-range two-particle correlations (the "ridge") from relativistic nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at RHIC and the LHC to smaller systems (pp and pPb), where the phenomenon was first observed at the LHC. The results are compared to semi-peripheral PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV from CMS, and the observed mass ordering and constituent-quark-number scaling are reminiscent of similar findings in AA collisions by the STAR and ALICE collaborations, which are interpreted as evidence of hydrodynamic collective flow and quark recombination. The measurements enable future searches for jet quenching in small systems and tests of whether collectivity extends to non-hadronic collisions such as electron-proton or electron-positron collisions.
