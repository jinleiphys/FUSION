# Beyond integrability: Baryon-baryon backward scattering in the massive Gross-Neveu model

> Due to integrability, baryon-baryon scattering in the massless Gross-Neveu model at large N features only forward elastic scattering. A bare mass term breaks integrability and is therefore expected to induce backward elastic scattering as well as inelastic reactions. We confirm these expectations by a study of baryon-baryon scattering in the massive Gross-Neveu model near the non-relativistic limit. This restriction enables us to solve the time-dependent Hartree-Fock equations with controlled approximations, using a combination of analytical methods from an effective field theory and the numerical solution of partial differential equations.

## Key claim

Baryon-baryon scattering in the massive Gross-Neveu model, which is non-integrable due to a bare mass term, is shown to exhibit backward elastic scattering and inelastic reactions near the non-relativistic limit, in contrast to the massless integrable case where only forward elastic scattering occurs.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic reduction of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations for the massive Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions at large N, expanding to leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in a small parameter epsilon characterizing baryon velocity and inverse size. They combined analytical methods from an effective no-sea theory that integrates out negative energy states with numerical solution of the resulting inhomogeneous, linear partial differential equations using a higher-order stable operator-difference scheme based on the Magnus expansion.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter epsilon characterizes the regime where baryon velocity v ~ epsilon and inverse baryon size parameter y ~ epsilon.
- The LO TDHF equation reduces to the multi-component non-linear Schrodinger equation, independent of the confinement parameter gamma.
- The NLO correction S^(4) contains terms proportional to 1/(12m^2) * 1/(1+gamma) * partial_x^2 S^(2) and -1/(2m) * 1/(1+gamma) * (S^(2))^2 from vacuum polarization effects.
- The inhomogeneous term F in the NLO equations for two-baryon scattering is given by F = 16 v^4 y^4 U_1 U_2 / D^2, where D = v^2(1+U_1)(1+U_2) + y^2 U_2.
- Numerical solutions used 250 points on the time axis and 250 points on half the space axis.

## Context

This work extends previous analytical results for the massless Gross-Neveu model, where integrability yields only elastic forward scattering with factorized transmission amplitudes (Thies, 2003). It builds on static properties of the massive Gross-Neveu model (Dashen, Hasslacher, and Neveu, 1975) and an effective low-energy theory derived by integrating out negative energy states (Thies, 2015). The results enable future studies of non-equilibrium thermodynamics and bulk viscosity in non-integrable quantum field theories, and may have applications to condensed matter systems such as trans- and cis-polyacetylene.
