# Fermi liquid theory: A brief survey in memory of Gerald E. Brown

> I present a brief review of Fermi liquid theory, and discuss recent work on Fermi liquid theory in dilute neutron matter and cold atomic gases. I argue that renewed interest in transport properties of quantum fluids provides fresh support for Landau's approach to Fermi liquid theory, which is based on kinetic theory rather than effective field theory and the renormalization group. I also discuss work on non-Fermi liquids, in particular dense quark matter.

## Key claim

This review argues that Landau's original kinetic-theory approach to Fermi liquid theory remains a vital framework for understanding transport in strongly correlated quantum fluids, and that recent work on transport properties provides fresh support for this approach over the purely effective-field-theory and renormalization-group formulations that have become popular.

## Method

The paper surveys the formal structure of Landau Fermi liquid theory, including the Boltzmann equation for quasi-particle distribution functions and the expansion of the effective interaction in Legendre polynomials (Landau parameters \(F_l, G_l\)). It then reviews the renormalization-group derivation of Fermi liquid theory by Polchinski and Shankar, and applies the effective-field-theory approach to two specific systems: dilute neutron matter (using the functional renormalization group with \(V_{\rm low k}\) as the bare interaction) and dense quark matter (using a high-density effective theory with hard dense loop resummation for gluon interactions). Finally, it presents a kinetic-theory calculation of bulk viscosity in a dilute Fermi gas near unitarity using a fugacity expansion.

## Key numbers

* In dilute neutron matter, the effective mass ratio \(m^*/m = 1 + F_1/3\) exceeds unity for Fermi momenta in the range \(0.6\ \text{fm}^{-1} < p_F < 1.4\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
* For the unitary Fermi gas just above the superfluid transition, Nascimbene et al. find \(m^*/m = 1.13 \pm 0.03\).
* The bulk viscosity of a dilute Fermi gas near unitarity is \(\zeta = \frac{1}{96\pi^{5/2}} (mT)^{3/2} \left( \frac{z\lambda}{a} \right)^2\), where \(\lambda\) is the thermal de Broglie wavelength and \(z\) is the fugacity.
* In dense quark matter, the dynamical gluon mass is \(m^2 = N_f g^2 \mu^2/(4\pi^2)\).
* The color-superconducting gap in dense quark matter is parametrically \(\Delta \sim \mu \exp(-1/g)\).

## Context

This review extends the line of work by Schwenk, Friman, and Brown (2001) on applying the functional renormalization group to neutron matter, and by Polchinski (1992) and Shankar (1993) on the renormalization-group formulation of Fermi liquid theory. It contrasts the effective-field-theory approach with Landau's original kinetic theory, and discusses how the latter has been reinvigorated by recent calculations of transport properties in strongly correlated Fermi gases, such as the bulk viscosity calculation by Schaefer (2013). The review also connects to holographic models of non-Fermi liquids by Faulkner et al. (2010) and to the anomalous transport theory of Son and Yamamoto (2012).
