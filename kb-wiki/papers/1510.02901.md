# A stochastic approach to the reconstruction of spectral functions in lattice QCD

> We present a Stochastic Optimization Method (SOM) for the reconstruction of the spectral functions (SPFs) from Euclidean correlation functions. In this approach the SPF is parameterized as a sum of randomly distributed boxes. By varying the width, location and height of the boxes stochastically an optimal SPF can be obtained. Using this approach we reproduce mock SPFs fairly well, which contain sharp resonance peaks, transport peaks and continuum spectra. We also analyzed the charmonium correlators obtained from $N_τ$=96, 48, 32 lattices using SOM and found similar conclusion on the dissociation temperatures of charmonium ground states as that obtained using the Maximum Entropy Method.

# A stochastic approach to the reconstruction of spectral functions in lattice QCD

## Key claim

The Stochastic Optimization Method (SOM) can reliably reconstruct spectral functions from Euclidean correlation functions without requiring a Bayesian prior or default model, producing results comparable to the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) for charmonium ground state dissociation temperatures, though the accuracy of the transport peak reconstruction depends on the relative size of the intercept compared to the continuum contribution.

## Method

The spectral function is parameterized as a sum of randomly distributed boxes, each defined by a width, height, and center position. Eight elementary update operations (shift, lengthen/shorten, broaden/narrow, and swap height/width between boxes) are applied stochastically to minimize a likelihood function, with a Simulated Annealing Algorithm controlling acceptance to avoid local minima. The procedure is repeated many times (typically 100) and the resulting solutions are averaged to obtain the final optimal spectral function.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium in the vector channel at 0.73 T_c, SOM gives a first peak location of 3.44 GeV with peak height 1.75, compared to MEM giving 3.48 GeV with peak height 0.98.
- At 1.46 T_c, SOM gives a first peak location of 4.8 GeV with peak height 0.72, compared to MEM giving 4.7 GeV with peak height 0.58.
- At 2.20 T_c, SOM gives a first peak location of 4.1 GeV with peak height 0.57; MEM did not report a peak at this temperature.
- The screening mass extracted from the corresponding spatial correlation function at 0.73 T_c is 3.472 GeV, close to the SOM peak location of 3.44 GeV.
- Mock data tests used N_tau = 96 with Gaussian-distributed errors of 0.0001 * G(tau) * tau.
- The simulated annealing stopping temperature was set to 0.0001 in practice.

## Context

This work extends the Stochastic Optimization Method, previously used in condensed matter physics (Mishchenko), to lattice QCD spectral function reconstruction, offering an alternative to Bayesian approaches such as the Maximum Entropy Method (Asakawa, 2001; Jarrell), the new Bayesian approach (Burnier, 2013), Stochastic Analytical Inference (Ohno), and the Backus-Gilbert Method (Francis, 2015). The method enables extraction of spectral functions from finite-temperature lattice QCD correlators without requiring a default model, and the authors plan to apply it to finer lattices in future work.
