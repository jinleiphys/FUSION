# Statistics of thermalization in Bjorken Flow

> The apparent early thermalization of quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC and LHC has motivated a number of studies of strongly coupled ${\mathcal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory using the AdS/CFT correspondence. Here we present the results of numerical simulations of Bjorken flow aimed at establishing typical features of the dynamics. This is done by evolving a large number of far from equilibrium initial states well into the hydrodynamic regime. The results strongly suggest that early thermalization is generic in this theory, taking place at proper times around $0.6$ in units of inverse local temperature. We also find that the scale which determines the rate of hydrodynamic cooling is linearly correlated with the entropy of initial states defined by the area of the apparent horizon in the dual geometry. Our results also suggest that entropy production during the hydrodynamic stage of evolution is not negligible despite the low value of $η/s$.

## Key claim

The paper strongly suggests that early thermalization (hydrodynamization) is generic in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory under Bjorken flow, occurring at proper times around 0.6 in units of inverse local temperature, based on simulations of over 600 far-from-equilibrium initial states.

## Method

The authors numerically solve Einstein's equations in asymptotically AdS spacetime using Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, evolving a large ensemble of randomly generated initial geometries that satisfy the constraint equations. The metric coefficients are expanded in Chebyshev polynomials using a spectral collocation method, and time evolution is performed with a 4th order Adams-Bashforth-Moulton predictor-corrector scheme. The apparent horizon is tracked dynamically to define the coordinate range and to compute entropy via the Bekenstein-Hawking relation.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization occurs at proper times around w = 0.6 in units of inverse local temperature, based on a criterion that the function f(w) deviates from the hydrodynamic form by less than 0.5 percent.
- The distribution of thermalization times w_th peaks near 0.6, with all 600+ cases showing hydrodynamization significantly before w = 1.2.
- Initial effective temperature is normalized to T_i = 1/pi at tau = 0 for all initial states.
- The pressure anisotropy at thermalization is typically about Delta = 0.35.
- Initial entropy values range from below 0.3 up to higher values, with a linear correlation between the hydrodynamic scale Lambda and initial entropy for entropies below approximately 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the earlier numerical studies of Heller et al. (2011, 2012) which analyzed only 29 initial conditions and suggested early thermalization, by demonstrating the result is generic across a much larger ensemble of over 600 initial states. The approach builds on the numerical framework of Chesler and Yaffe (2009) but uses a different metric parameterization that allows setting initial conditions at tau = 0. The results enable systematic study of entropy production during both pre-hydrodynamic and hydrodynamic stages, and show that the final entropy scale Lambda is linearly correlated with initial entropy for low-entropy states, while this correlation breaks down for higher initial entropies.
