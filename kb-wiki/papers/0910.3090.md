# Running sums for $2νββ$-decay matrix elements within the QRPA with account for deformation

> The $2νββ$-decay running sums for $^{76}$Ge and $^{150}$Nd nuclei are calculated within a QRPA approach with account for deformation. A realistic nucleon-nucleon residual interaction based on the Brueckner G matrix (for the Bonn CD force) is used. The influence of different model parameters on the functional behavior of the running sums is studied. It is found that the parameter $g_{pp}$ renormalizing the G matrix in the QRPA particle-particle channel is responsible for a qualitative change in behavior of the running sums at higher excitation energies. For realistic values of $g_{pp}$ a significant negative contribution to the total $2νββ$-decay matrix element is found to come from the energy region of the giant Gamow-Teller resonance. This behavior agrees with the results of other authors.

## Key claim

The parameter \(g_{pp}\) renormalizing the nucleon-nucleon residual interaction in the particle-particle channel of the QRPA is responsible for whether high-lying \(1^+\) states contribute constructively or destructively to the total \(2\nu\beta\beta\)-decay matrix element; for realistic (unquenched) values of \(g_{pp}\), a significant negative contribution comes from the giant Gamow-Teller resonance region, contradicting some earlier QRPA calculations that found no such destructive contribution.

## Method

The authors used a QRPA approach that accounts for nuclear deformation and employs a realistic residual interaction based on the Brueckner \(G\) matrix derived from the Bonn CD nucleon-nucleon force, as well as a schematic separable force for comparison. They calculated the \(2\nu\beta\beta\) running sums \(M^{2\nu}_{GT}(\omega)\) for \(^{76}\)Ge and \(^{150}\)Nd by truncating the sum over intermediate \(1^+\) states at excitation energy \(\omega\), and studied the effect of varying the particle-hole renormalization factor \(g_{ph}\) and the particle-particle renormalization factor \(g_{pp}\) (or their schematic counterparts \(\chi\) and \(\kappa\)) between their fitted values and half those values.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{76}\)Ge with spherical deformation (\(\beta_2 = 0.0\) for both initial and final nuclei), the fitted particle-particle strength parameter is \(g_{pp}^0 = 0.94\) for the realistic force and \(\kappa^0 = 0.087\) MeV for the separable force.
* For \(^{150}\)Nd with deformation set Def. I (\(\beta_2 = 0.37\) for initial, 0.23 for final), the fitted \(g_{pp}^0 = 0.78\) and \(\kappa^0 = 0.033\) MeV; for set Def. II (\(\beta_2 = 0.24\) for initial, 0.21 for final), \(g_{pp}^0 = 1.35\) and \(\kappa^0 = 0.053\) MeV.
* The particle-hole renormalization factor is fixed at \(g_{ph}^0 = 1.15\) for the realistic force and \(\chi^0 = 3.73/A^{0.7}\) MeV for the separable force for both nuclei.
* For quenched strengths \(g_{pp} = g_{pp}^0/2\) (\(\kappa = \kappa^0/2\)), the running sums grow monotonically and the total \(M^{2\nu}_{GT}\) is strongly overestimated; for unquenched \(g_{pp} = g_{pp}^0\), a negative contribution from the GTR region at \(\omega \approx 10\) MeV compensates an overshoot at low excitation energies.
* The total \(M^{2\nu}_{GT}\) for \(^{48}\)Ca from shell-model calculations in Horoi (2007) is 0.054 MeV\(^{-1}\), in agreement with the experimental value \(0.05 \pm 0.01\) MeV\(^{-1}\) from Barabash (cited as barab).

## Context

This work extends the QRPA calculations of Rodin (2005) for \(^{76}\)Ge and \(^{100}\)Mo, which also found a destructive contribution from the GTR region, and contrasts with the systematic QRPA calculations by Moreno (2008) that used a schematic residual interaction and found no such destructive contribution. The results agree with shell-model calculations for \(^{48}\)Ca by Zhao (1990), Horoi (2007), Nakada (1996), and Caurier (2004), which consistently show an overshoot at about 7 MeV compensated by negative GTR contributions. The study enables a more reliable determination of the \(g_{pp}\) parameter in QRPA calculations, which is necessary before conclusions about single-state dominance in \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay can be drawn.
