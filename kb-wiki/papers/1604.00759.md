# Self-consistent continuum random-phase approximation with finite-range interactions for charge-exchange excitations

> The formalism of the continuum random-phase approximation theory which treats, without ap- proximations, the continuum part of the single-particle spectrum, is extended to describe charge- exchange excitations. Our approach is self-consistent, meaning that we use a unique, finite-range, interaction in the Hartree-Fock calculations which generate the single-particle basis and in the con- tinuum random-phase approximation which describes the excitation. We study excitations induced by the Fermi, Gamow-Teller and spin-dipole operators in doubly magic nuclei by using four Gogny- like finite-range interactions, two of them containing tensor forces. We focus our attention on the importance of the correct treatment of the continuum configuration space and on the effects of the tensor terms of the force.1

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously developed continuum random-phase approximation (CRPA) formalism with finite-range interactions to charge-exchange excitations, achieving the first self-consistent CRPA calculations for these modes with finite-range forces. The work demonstrates that the correct treatment of the continuum part of the single-particle spectrum is important for describing strength above the particle emission threshold, particularly in neutron-rich nuclei, and that the tensor force affects charge-exchange responses primarily through its influence on single-particle energies rather than through the RPA interaction itself.

## Method

The authors solve Hartree-Fock equations with finite-range Gogny-like interactions to generate the single-particle basis, then solve the CRPA secular equations rewritten in terms of channel functions expanded on a basis of orthonormal Sturm functions. They study excitations induced by Fermi, Gamow-Teller, and spin-dipole operators in eight doubly magic nuclei (12C, 16O, 22O, 24O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 56Ni, and 68Ni) using four Gogny-like interactions: D1M, D1S, D1MT2c, and D1ST2c, with the latter two containing tensor terms. The CRPA results are compared with discrete RPA (DRPA) calculations where the continuum is artificially discretized by imposing boundary conditions at the edge of the radial integration box.

## Key numbers

* For 48Ca, the total DRPA T- Gamow-Teller strength with the D1M interaction is 24.17, with the contribution above the main peak being 1.05, compared to the CRPA integrated strength of 1.08 in that region.
* For 48Ca with the D1MT2c interaction, the total DRPA T- Gamow-Teller strength is 24.04, with 1.18 beyond the main peak, while the CRPA total strength in that region is 1.58.
* The experimental total Gamow-Teller strength in 48Ca is 15.36 plus or minus 2.16, corresponding to about 60 percent of the theoretical strength, with measurements stopping at about 30 MeV excitation energy.
* The DRPA calculations exhaust the Ikeda sum rule at the 1 percent level for the D1MT2c force and to one part in over 100,000 when the D1M interaction is used.
* For 24O, convergence of DRPA results is reached already for epsilon_max equal to 40 MeV.
* Stability of CRPA results up to the fifth significant figure is achieved with ten expansion coefficients, independent of multipolarity and excitation energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CRPA formalism for charge-conserving excitations (De Donno et al., 2011) to charge-exchange modes, and is the natural continuation of their discrete RPA study of charge-exchange responses with finite-range interactions (De Donno et al., 2014). The self-consistent approach using finite-range Gogny interactions contrasts with most prior self-consistent studies of charge-exchange excitations, which used zero-range Skyrme interactions (Hamamoto et al., 2000; Fracasso and Colo, 2007) and more recently included tensor terms (Bai et al., 2009-2011; Minato and Bai, 2013). The work enables parameter-free predictions for charge-exchange excitations in unstable nuclei relevant to astrophysical nucleosynthesis, particularly r-process calculations, and highlights the need for including 2p-2h excitations beyond RPA to reproduce experimental widths and detailed spectral structure.
