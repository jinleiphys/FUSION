# Decay out of a Superdeformed Band

> Using a statistical model for the normally deformed states and for their coupling to a member of the superdeformed band, we calculate the ensemble average and the fluctuations of the intensity for decay out of the superdeformed band and of the intraband decay intensity. We show that both intensities depend on two dimensionless variables: The ratio $Γ^{\downarrow}/Γ_S$ and the ratio $Γ_N/d$. Here, $Γ^{\downarrow}$ is the spreading width for the mixing of the superdeformed and the normally deformed states, $d$ is the mean level spacing of the latter, and $Γ_S$ ($Γ_N$) is the width for gamma decay of the superdeformed state (of the normally deformed states, respectively). This parametric dependence differs from the one predicted by the approach of Vigezzi et al. where the relevant dimensionless variables are $Γ_N/Γ_S$ and $Γ^{\downarrow}/d$. We give analytical and numerical results for the decay intensities as functions of the dimensionless variables, including an estimate of the error incurred by performing the ensemble average, and we present fit formulas useful for the analysis of experimental data. We compare our results with the approach of Vigezzi et al. and establish the conditions under which this approach constitutes a valid approximation.

## Key claim

The authors show that the ensemble-averaged intensities for decay out of a superdeformed band and for intraband decay depend on two dimensionless variables: the ratio of the spreading width to the gamma-decay width of the superdeformed state (Γ↓/Γ_S) and the ratio of the gamma-decay width of the normally deformed states to their mean level spacing (Γ_N/d). This parametric dependence differs from the approach of Vigezzi et al. (1990), where the relevant variables are Γ_N/Γ_S and Γ↓/d, and the authors establish the conditions under which the Vigezzi approach constitutes a valid approximation.

## Method

The authors model the normally deformed (ND) states using the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) of random matrices, with the superdeformed (SD) state coupled to them via Gaussian-distributed matrix elements. They calculate the ensemble average of the intraband decay intensity using the supersymmetry approach of Verbaarschot et al. (1985), obtaining a fourfold integral that is evaluated numerically. They supplement this analytical work with numerical simulations using matrices of dimension K = 100 or larger with N = 10^4 or more realizations, and estimate the width of the probability distribution of the intraband intensity to assess the error incurred by using the ensemble average.

## Key numbers

* For Γ_N/d = 10^{-2}, the peak value of the fluctuating part of the average intraband intensity (I_in^fluc) is approximately 0.6.
* The value of Γ↓/Γ_S where the average intraband intensity equals 1/2 shrinks from approximately 5 × 10^4 for Γ_N/d = 10^{-6} to approximately 1 for Γ_N/d = 10.
* In the mass region A ~ 190, the ratio d/Γ_N is typically of order 10^2.
* For Γ_N/d = 0, the fluctuating part of the average intraband intensity equals (Γ↓/Γ_S) / (1 + Γ↓/Γ_S).
* The total transmission coefficient for E1 decay is T_N = 2π Γ_N/d, which for nuclei with mass A ~ 190 where Γ_N/d ≈ 10^{-2} gives T_N ≈ 0.05.

## Context

This work extends the re-examination by Weidenmüller (1998) of the statistical model for decay out of superdeformed bands originally proposed by Vigezzi et al. (1990). The authors show that the fluctuating part of the intraband decay intensity cannot be neglected in situations of practical interest, contrary to claims in Weidenmüller (1998), and that the approach of Vigezzi et al. (1990) is a valid approximation only when Γ_S and Γ_N are sufficiently small compared to the mean level spacing d. The results enable more reliable determination of the spreading width Γ↓ from experimental data, which is used to infer properties of the barrier separating the first and second minima of the deformation potential.
