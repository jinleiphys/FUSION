# Reaction Theory and Advanced CDCC

> The Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) has been successfully employed to describe elastic and breakup of nuclear reactions induced by weakly bound projectiles. In this contribution, we review some other, less widespread applications of the CDCC wavefunction, some of them in combination with other reaction formalisms, which are being currently employed in the analysis of reactions involving three or more fragments in the initial or final state.

## Key claim

The Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method, originally developed for two-body projectiles, can be extended and combined with other reaction formalisms to describe reactions involving three or more fragments, including transfer, knockout, and non-elastic breakup processes, but the accuracy of these combined approaches depends on the specific reaction and energy regime.

## Method

The authors review extensions of the CDCC method where the three-body wavefunction is expanded in eigenstates of the projectile Hamiltonian, including both bound and discretized continuum states, and then inserted into transition amplitudes for transfer or knockout reactions. For non-elastic breakup, the CDCC wavefunction is used within the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) formalism to compute inclusive breakup cross sections via an inhomogeneous equation containing the imaginary part of the fragment-target optical potential.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{10}\text{Be}(d,p)^{11}\text{Be}$ reaction at two deuteron incident energies, the separate contributions from elastic transfer, inelastic transfer, elastic breakup transfer, and inelastic breakup transfer are shown; the full calculation is the coherent sum of these paths.
- For the $^{18}\text{C}(p, pn)^{17}\text{C}^*$ reaction below 100 MeV/u, the contribution from the $(p,d)$ transfer reaction is significant for populating the excited state at $E_x = 0.33$ MeV, affecting the extracted spectroscopic factor.
- For the $^{9}\text{Li}(d,p)^{10}\text{Li}$ reaction at 2.4 MeV/u and 11.1 MeV/u, the $s$-wave virtual state contribution is very small at the higher energy and not apparent in the data, attributed to different angular ranges in the two experiments.
- For the $^{209}\text{Bi}(^{6}\text{Li},\alpha)X$ reaction at 36 MeV, the non-elastic breakup (NEB) contribution dominates the inclusive cross section, while elastic breakup (EBU) is only dominant at small angles; the EBU+NEB sum reproduces the data shape and magnitude with some overestimation at the maximum.

## Context

This work extends the original CDCC method of Rawitscher (1974) and the Pittsburgh-Kyushu collaboration (Yahiro 1986, Austern 1987) by combining it with transfer and knockout formalisms, and by implementing the IAV model (Ichimura, Austern, Vincent 1985) for non-elastic breakup. The extended CDCC with target excitations (XCDCC) has been applied to reactions induced by $^{11}\text{Be}$ (Summers 2007, Moro 2012, Di Pietro 2019) and $^{19}\text{C}$ (Lay 2016), showing that core excitation is important for light targets but small for heavy targets due to dominant dipole Coulomb couplings. The IAV-CDCC implementation (Jin 2019) enables calculation of inclusive breakup cross sections that were previously inaccessible due to numerical complexity.
