# $X(3872)$ production from reactions involving $D$ and $D^*$ mesons

> In this proceeding we show the results found for the cross sections of the processes $\bar D D\toπX(3872)$, $\bar D^* D\to πX(3872)$ and $\bar D^* D^*\toπX(3872)$, information needed for calculations of the $X(3872)$ abundance in heavy ion collisions. Our formalism is based on the generation of $X(3872)$ from the interaction of the hadrons $\bar D^0 D^{*0} - \textrm{c.c}$, $D^- D^{*+} - \textrm{c.c}$ and $D^-_s D^{*+}_s - \textrm{c.c}$. The evaluation of the cross section associated with processes having $D^*$ meson(s) involves an anomalous vertex, $X\bar D^* D^*$, which we have determined by considering triangular loops motivated by the molecular nature of $X(3872)$. We find that the contribution of this vertex is important. Encouraged by this finding we estimate the $X\bar D^* D^*$ coupling, which turns out to be $1.95\pm 0.22$. We then use it to obtain the cross section for the reaction $\bar D^* D^*\toπX$ and find that the $X\bar D^* D^*$ vertex is also relevant in this case. We also discuss the role of the charged components of $X$ in the determination of the production cross sections.

## Key claim

The cross sections for producing the exotic charmonium state X(3872) from reactions involving D and D* mesons are significantly enhanced (by factors of 100-150 for one channel and 8-10 for another) when previously neglected anomalous vertices involving D*D*pi and X D* D* couplings are included, under the assumption that X(3872) is a molecular state of D and D* mesons.

## Method

The authors calculate the isospin-spin averaged production cross sections for the reactions D D -> pi X, D* D -> pi X, and D* D* -> pi X in the center-of-mass frame using effective Lagrangians with PPV, VVP, and VVV vertices. For processes involving D* mesons, the anomalous X D* D* vertex is determined by evaluating triangular loop diagrams motivated by the molecular nature of X(3872), and the resulting coupling is then used to compute the cross section for D* D* -> pi X via a point-like effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The X D* D* coupling constant is determined to be 1.95 ± 0.22 (dimensionless).
- The strong coupling g_PPV for the D* D pi vertex is approximately 9, derived from heavy quark symmetry using f_pi = 93 MeV, giving a D*+ -> D0 pi+ decay width of 71 keV (consistent with the experimental result of 65 ± 15 keV).
- The cross section for D* D -> pi X, including the anomalous X D* D* vertex, is larger than that for D D -> pi X by a factor of 100-150.
- The cross section for D* D* -> pi X, including the X D* D* vertex contribution, is raised by a factor of 8-10 compared to the case without this vertex.
- The cross section for D D -> pi X increases by a factor of 2-3 when charged components of X are included compared to considering only neutral components.
- The couplings of X to its constituent pseudoscalar-vector pairs are: g_{X D- D*+} = 3638/sqrt(2) MeV, g_{X D0 D*0} = 3663/sqrt(2) MeV, and g_{X Ds- Ds*+} = 3395/sqrt(2) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Cho et al. (2011) on X(3872) hadronic absorption and production cross sections in a hadron gas, which were used to model X abundance in heavy ion collisions. The key improvement is the inclusion of anomalous vertices (pi D* D* and X D* D*) that were neglected in that prior work, as well as the charged components of the D and D* mesons coupling to X. The importance of anomalous couplings in related contexts was previously shown by Oh et al. for J/psi absorption and by Nagahiro et al. for radiative decays of scalar resonances. The results enable more precise calculations of X(3872) abundance in heavy ion collisions, potentially distinguishing between molecular and tetraquark interpretations of X(3872) through comparison with experimental yields.
