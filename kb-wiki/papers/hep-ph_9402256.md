# Pair Production in the Quantum Boltzmann Equation

> A source term in the quantum Boltzmann equation, which accounts for the spontaneous creation of $e^+e^-$-pairs in external electric fields, is derived from first principles and evaluated numerically. Careful analysis of time scales reveals that this source term is generally non-Markovian. This implies in particular that there may be temporary violations of the $H$-theorem.

## Key claim

The source term for spontaneous $e^+e^-$-pair creation in the quantum Boltzmann equation is generally non-Markovian, meaning it involves an integration over the entire history of the system; this implies that there may be temporary violations of the $H$-theorem, particularly when the electric field strength reaches or exceeds $E \ge m^2/\hbar q$.

## Method

The author derives the source term from first principles using the projection method of Nakajima, Zwanzig, and others, applied to a model of $e^+e^-$-pair creation in a homogeneous, time-independent electric field (the Schwinger mechanism). The evolution of the system is described by a time-dependent Bogoliubov transformation of the field operators, and the source term is expressed as an integral over the history of the system involving a kernel $R(\vec p,\tau)$ obtained from the projection onto irrelevant degrees of freedom. In the weak-field limit ($E \ll m^2/\hbar q$), the source term is evaluated explicitly and numerically, yielding the longitudinal momentum distribution of produced electrons.

## Key numbers

* The weak-field condition is $E \ll m^2/\hbar q$; at $E = m^2/\hbar q$, the production interval and memory time become of the same order $\tau \sim \hbar/m$.
* The total production rate per unit volume in the dilute limit ($S=1$) is $w = (qE)^2 / (4\pi^3 \hbar^2) \exp(-\pi m^2 / \hbar qE)$, consistent with the leading term of the Schwinger formula.
* The memory time is $\tau_{\rm mem} \sim \hbar/\epsilon_\perp + \epsilon_\perp/qE$, combining a quantum time $\hbar/\epsilon_\perp$ and a classical time $\epsilon_\perp/qE$.
* The production interval for $p_{\|}=0$ in the weak-field limit is $\tau_{\rm prod}(0,\vec p_\perp) \sim (\epsilon_\perp/qE) \exp(\pi\epsilon_\perp^2 / 2\hbar qE)$.
* The dimensionless quantity $\eta(a,p_{\|}/\epsilon_\perp)$ (with $a = \hbar qE/\epsilon_\perp^2$) is evaluated numerically; for weak fields ($a<1$) the momentum distribution of produced electrons is not a $\delta$-function or thermal distribution but shows oscillations with period scaling with $a$, and electrons are produced predominantly in the direction of the external field ($p_{\|}>0$).

## Context

This work extends the Wigner function approach to pair creation used by Eisenberg and Kaelbermann, Rafelski et al., and Kluger et al. (early 1990s), which could not fully determine the source term or the longitudinal momentum distribution and was not suited for discussing irreversibility. The projection method provides a clear definition of memory time and coarse-grained entropy, enabling a systematic analysis of non-Markovian effects. The results enable future studies of memory effects at large field strengths and can be extended to include binary collisions, where pair creation and collisions do not interfere to lowest order perturbation theory.
