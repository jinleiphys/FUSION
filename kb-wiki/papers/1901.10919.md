# Evolution of multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

> The evolution of multiplicity distribution of a species which undergoes chemical reactions can be described with the help of a master equation. We study the master equation for a fixed temperature, because we want to know how fast different moments of the multiplicity distribution approach their equilibrium value. We particularly look at the 3rd and 4th factorial moments and their equilibrium values from which central moments, cumulants and their ratios can be calculated. Then we study the situation in which the temperature of the system decreases. We find out that in the non-equilibrium state, higher factorial moments differ more from their equilibrium values than the lower moments and that the behaviour of the combination of the central moments depends on the combination we choose. If one chooses to determine the chemical freeze-out temperature from the measured values of higher moments, these effects might jeopardise the correctness of the extracted value.

# Evolution of multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
If chemical equilibrium is broken during the cooling of a fireball in heavy ion collisions, higher factorial moments of the multiplicity distribution deviate more from their equilibrium values than lower moments, and extracting the chemical freeze-out temperature from measured higher moments may yield different apparent temperatures depending on which moment is used, potentially jeopardising the correctness of the extracted value.

## Method
The authors solve a master equation for a binary reversible reaction (a1 a2 <-> b1 b2) to study the time evolution of the multiplicity distribution of b1 b2 pairs across an ensemble of fireballs. For constant temperature, the master equation is cast into dimensionless form using relaxation time tau = t L/V; for a cooling fireball, the original master equation is solved numerically using a parametrisation of the cross-section for the specific reaction pi+ n <-> K+ Lambda, with temperature and volume evolving according to a 1D longitudinally boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion scenario from T=165 MeV down to T=100 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature for the cooling scenario: T = 165 MeV; final temperature: T = 100 MeV
- Final time set to 10 fm/c, leading to initial time t0 = 2.2 fm/c
- Initial volume V0 = 125 fm^3
- Initial average multiplicity N0 = 0.005 for the binomial initial conditions
- Parameter epsilon = 0.1 used for the constant-temperature evolution of factorial moments
- Baryon density at start of calculation: rho0 = 0.08 fm^{-3}
- Cross-section parametrisation for pi N -> Lambda K: threshold sqrt(s0) with coefficients 0.054, 0.091, and 0.0045 in units of fm^2 and GeV

## Context
This work extends the master equation approach previously applied to second factorial moments (references cited as [1] and [2]) to the third and fourth factorial moments, building on earlier work by Sochorova (2018). The study demonstrates a potential systematic error in the common practice of extracting chemical freeze-out temperatures from measured higher moments of multiplicity distributions in heavy ion collisions. The results suggest that non-equilibrium effects must be accounted for when interpreting experimental data on skewness, kurtosis, and their volume-independent combinations (S sigma and kappa sigma^2), and that future work should include additional reaction channels that may further modify the moments.
