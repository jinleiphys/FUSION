# Vector-type four-quark interaction and its impact on QCD phase structure

> Effects of the vector-type four-quark interaction on QCD phase structure are investigated in the imaginary chemical potential region, by using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with the extended Z3 symmetry. In the course to this end, we clarify analytically the Roberge-Weiss periodicity and symmetry properties of various quantities under the existence of a vector-type four-quark interaction. In the imaginary chemical potential region, the chiral condensate and the quark number density are sensitive to the strength of the interaction. Based on this result, we propose a possibility to determine the strength of the vector-type interaction, which largely affects QCD phase structure in the real chemical potential region, by comparing the results of lattice simulations and effective model calculations in the imaginary chemical potential region.

## Key claim

The vector-type four-quark interaction significantly affects the chiral condensate and quark number density in the imaginary chemical potential region, and comparing these quantities from lattice QCD simulations and PNJL model calculations in that region could determine the interaction strength, which then enables reliable predictions for the QCD phase diagram in the real chemical potential region.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with an added vector-type four-quark interaction term, treated in the mean-field approximation. They analytically proved that the extended Z3 symmetry and Roberge-Weiss periodicity hold even with the vector interaction present, and then numerically solved the stationary conditions for the chiral condensate, quark number density, and modified Polyakov loop as functions of imaginary chemical potential μ = iTθ for various vector coupling strengths Gv.

## Key numbers

- The scalar coupling constant Gs = 5.498 GeV^{-2} and the three-dimensional momentum cutoff Λ = 0.6315 GeV were used, with current quark mass m0 = 5.5 MeV, reproducing pion decay constant fπ = 93.3 MeV and pion mass Mπ = 138 MeV.
- The vector coupling Gv was varied in the range 0 ≤ Gv ≤ Gs, with the critical endpoint disappearing at approximately Gv ≈ 0.38 Gs.
- The Polyakov potential parameter T0 was rescaled to 190 MeV, yielding a deconfinement temperature TD = 176 MeV at μ = 0.
- At T = 300 MeV, the chiral condensate and the imaginary part of the quark number density showed clear sensitivity to Gv across the Roberge-Weiss period 0 ≤ θ ≤ π/3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PNJL model analysis (Sakai, 2008) that established the extended Z3 symmetry and Roberge-Weiss periodicity without vector interactions. It also builds on findings by KKN, Kashiwa, and Fukushima (various years) that the vector-type four-quark interaction shifts the critical endpoint toward lower temperature and higher chemical potential in the real-μ region. The proposed method of determining Gv by comparing lattice QCD results with effective model calculations in the imaginary-μ region offers a way to resolve the open question of the physical strength of the vector interaction, which is essential for reliable predictions of the QCD phase diagram at finite real chemical potential.
