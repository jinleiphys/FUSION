# Four-body Faddeev-type equations for $\bar{K}NNN$ quasi-bound state calculations

> The paper is devoted to the $\bar{K}NNN$ system, which is an exotic system consisting of an antikaon and three nucleons. Four-body Faddeev-type AGS equations, which are being used for evaluation of the possible quasi-bound state in the system are described.

## Key claim

The paper describes the formal derivation of four-body Faddeev-type AGS equations for the \(\bar{K}NNN\) system, but no numerical results for a quasi-bound state are yet obtained; the work is still in progress, with only the equations, antisymmetrization, and necessary three-body subsystem calculations completed.

## Method

The author writes down the four-body AGS equations in momentum representation for separable two-body potentials, then specifies the 18 channels for the \(\bar{K}NNN\) system with all three nucleons treated as distinguishable, followed by antisymmetrization to reduce the system to a \(5 \times 5\) matrix equation. The three-body subsystems (\(\bar{K}NN\) and \(NNN\)) are solved separately using the three-body AGS equations with separable potentials, and the resulting effective three-body transition operators are separabelized via Hilbert-Schmidt expansion for use as input to the four-body equations.

## Key numbers

- The three-nucleon system \(NNN\) (\({}^{3}\)H and \({}^{3}\)He) was calculated with a separable two-term \(NN\) potential, yielding a binding energy of \(9.95\) MeV for both nuclei (Coulomb interaction not included).
- The \(\bar{K}NNN\) system is studied with total isospin \(I^{(4)} = 0\) (corresponding to \(K^- ppn\)), total spin \(S^{(4)} = 1/2\), and orbital momentum zero.
- Three models of the \(\bar{K}N\) interaction are used: two phenomenological potentials (one-pole and two-pole structure of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance) and a chirally motivated model; all three describe low-energy \(K^- p\) scattering and the \(1s\) level shift of kaonic hydrogen with equally high accuracy.

## Context

This work extends previous three-body Faddeev-type calculations of the \(\bar{K}NN\) system (reviewed in Shevchenko, reference "review") to the four-body \(\bar{K}NNN\) system, where only less accurate calculations existed before. The author uses the same separable two-body potentials (a two-term \(NN\) potential reproducing Argonne v18 phase shifts and the three \(\bar{K}N\) models) and the same numerical programs from the three-body \(\bar{K}NN\) calculations. The completed formalism and subsystem results enable future numerical solution for the quasi-bound state pole position in the \(K^- ppn\) system.
