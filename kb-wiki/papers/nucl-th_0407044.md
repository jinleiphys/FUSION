# Optimal Fermi-averaging t-matrix for ($π^+$,$K^+$) reactions in $Λ$ quasi-free region

> We propose an optimal Fermi-averaging for an elementary $π^+$+$n$$\to$$K^+$+$Λ$ t-matrix under the on-energy-shell condition, in order to describe ($π^+$,$K^+$) reactions on a nuclear target in the framework of a distorted-wave impulse approximation. We apply it to calculate $Λ$ quasi-free spectra from $^{12}$C($π^+$,$K^+$) reactions at $p_π$= 1.20 GeV/c and 1.05 GeV/c, and compare them with experimental data. The results show that the calculated spectra are in excellent agreement with the data, because the energy-dependence originates from the nature of the optimal Fermi-averaging t-matrix.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an "optimal Fermi-averaging" procedure for the elementary π⁺+n→K⁺+Λ t-matrix under the on-energy-shell condition, which introduces an energy dependence that is essential to reproduce the observed quasi-free Λ spectra from ¹²C(π⁺,K⁺) reactions at two different incident pion momenta, resolving a puzzle where a simple Fermi gas model predicts the opposite trend.

## Method

The authors implement a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework where the elementary π⁺+n→K⁺+Λ t-matrix is averaged over the neutron Fermi momentum distribution, but only for those kinematic configurations that satisfy the on-energy-shell condition in the nucleus (energy and momentum conservation). This "optimal" t-matrix t^opt(ω,θ_K) acquires a dependence on the energy transfer ω, and is then used in a factorized cross section formula together with a strength function calculated via Green's function method, using a Woods-Saxon Λ-nucleus potential and eikonal approximation for meson-nucleus distorted waves.

## Key numbers

- Incident pion momenta studied: p_π = 1.20 GeV/c and 1.05 GeV/c, at K⁺ forward angle θ_K = 6° ± 2°
- Momentum transfer to Λ: q ≃ 300–500 MeV/c, exceeding Fermi momentum p_F ≃ 270 MeV/c
- For p_π = 1.20 GeV/c: calculated QF peak at ω ≃ 275 MeV (about 80 MeV above ¹¹C_g.s.+Λ threshold)
- For p_π = 1.05 GeV/c: calculated QF peak at ω ≃ 240 MeV (about 45 MeV above threshold), with width about 80 MeV
- Magnitude of QF peak at 1.05 GeV/c is about 2/3 times smaller than at 1.20 GeV/c
- Λ-nucleus potential parameters: V_Λ⁰ = −28.0 MeV, a = 0.60 fm, r_0 = 1.128+0.439A^(−2/3) fm, R = 2.695 fm for A=12
- Neutron Woods-Saxon potential strength: V_N⁰ = −64.8 MeV, reproducing charge radius of 2.46 fm
- Calculated QF spectrum at 1.20 GeV/c is slightly smaller than data by about 15%

## Context

This work extends earlier DWIA analyses of (π⁺,K⁺) reactions (Motoba 1988, Hausmann 1989) and addresses a puzzle identified in KEK E438 and E521 experiments (Saha 2003), where the observed quasi-free spectra at different incident momenta showed opposite behavior to predictions from a simple Fermi gas model (Dalitz 1976). The optimal Fermi-averaging approach enables simultaneous description of Λ bound and quasi-free regions, and the authors suggest it provides a powerful way to calculate spectra beyond the ordinary DWIA (Harada 2004), with potential implications for understanding hyperon-nucleus and meson-nucleus interactions.
