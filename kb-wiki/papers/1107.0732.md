# Effective field theory for hydrodynamics: Wess-Zumino term and anomalies in two spacetime dimensions

> We develop the formalism that incorporates quantum anomalies in the effective field theory of non-dissipative fluids. We consider the effect of adding a Wess-Zumino-like term to the low-energy effective action to account for anomalies. In this paper we restrict to two spacetime dimensions. We find modifications to the constitutive relations for the current and the stress-energy tensor, and, more interestingly, half a new propagating mode (one-and-a-halfth sound): a left- or right-moving wave with propagation speed that goes to zero with the anomaly coefficient. Unlike for the chiral magnetic wave in four dimensions, this mode propagates even in the absence of external fields. We check our results against a more standard, purely hydrodynamical derivation. Unitarity of the effective field theory suggests an upper bound on the anomaly coefficient in hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors propose that quantum anomalies in non-dissipative fluids can be incorporated into an effective field theory by adding a Wess-Zumino-like term to the fluid Lagrangian. In two spacetime dimensions, this term generates a new propagating mode (one-and-a-halfth sound) that propagates even without external fields, unlike the chiral magnetic wave in four dimensions, and unitarity of the effective field theory suggests an upper bound on the anomaly coefficient.

## Method

The authors construct a Wess-Zumino term for the fluid Lagrangian in (1+1) dimensions that is invariant under the chemical shift symmetry only at the level of the action, with the Lagrangian density changing by a total derivative. They establish the dictionary between field theory variables and hydrodynamic observables (velocity, entropy density, charge density, temperature, chemical potential) and derive the modified constitutive relations for the current and stress-energy tensor. The spectrum of linear perturbations around a static homogeneous fluid ground state is analyzed, and the results are cross-checked against a purely hydrodynamic derivation following the approach of Son and Surowka (2009).

## Key numbers

* The Wess-Zumino term gives a correction to the current: Δj^μ = -2C μ ũ^μ, where C is the anomaly coefficient and μ is the chemical potential.
* The Wess-Zumino term gives a correction to the stress-energy tensor: ΔT^{μν} = -C μ^2 (u^μ ũ^ν + u^ν ũ^μ).
* The one-and-a-halfth sound mode has propagation speed v_WZ = 2C s^2 F_bb / [s^2 F_yy F_bb - (2s F_by + F_y)^2] + O(C^2) for small anomaly coefficient C.
* The null energy condition imposes the bound |C| ≤ (ρ + p)/(2μ^2).
* The ghost-free condition at zero momentum gives 4C^2 < (μ F_y - s F_b) F_yy / μ^2 = (ρ + p)/μ^2 (∂n/∂μ)_s.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to hydrodynamics developed by Dubovsky, Gregoire, Nicolis, and Rattazzi (2006) and by Endlich, Nicolis, Rattazzi, and Wang (2010) to include quantum anomalies, building on the hydrodynamic derivation of anomalous transport by Son and Surowka (2009). The results enable a field-theoretic treatment of anomalous hydrodynamics that can reveal constraints (such as ghost-free conditions) not obvious in the purely hydrodynamic language. The paper focuses on two spacetime dimensions, where the Wess-Zumino term appears at the same derivative order as the leading-order fluid Lagrangian, and notes that concurrent work by Kharzeev and Yee (2011) and by Loganayagam (2011) derived anomalous constitutive relations in even spacetime dimensions at the hydrodynamic level without studying the spectrum.
