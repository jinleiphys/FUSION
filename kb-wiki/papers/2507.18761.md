# Freezeout at constant energy density and spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions

> Using the local equilibrium density operator, we develop a geometry-informed linear response theory that takes into account the parameterization of the freezeout hypersurface before the gradient expansion is carried out. Assuming local equilibrium on an iso-energy density hypersurface, we study the $Λ$ hyperon spin polarization, and compute corrections to the isothermal case due to finite density. We argue that corrections to isothermal freezeout should be small, and that even in heavy ion collisions with energy as low as $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 11.5$ GeV they constitute at most a $10\%$ effect. They may, however, become relevant for local polarization observables.

## Key claim

The paper argues that assuming freezeout occurs on a constant-energy-density hypersurface (rather than an isothermal one) introduces corrections to the computed \(\Lambda\) hyperon spin polarization that are at most a \(10\%\) effect even at collision energies as low as \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 11.5\) GeV, but these corrections may become relevant for local polarization observables.

## Method

The authors develop a linear response theory that incorporates the geometry of the freezeout hypersurface before performing a gradient expansion, using the local equilibrium density operator. They assume freezeout occurs on an iso-energy density hypersurface and derive thermodynamic constraints relating variations of temperature and chemical potential along that surface via the coefficient \(\mathcal{C} = (\partial e/\partial \mu|_T)/(\partial e/\partial T|_\mu)\). They then compute modified expressions for the \(\Lambda\) spin polarization in two regimes (\(|\mathcal{C}|>1\) and \(|\mathcal{C}|<1\)) and evaluate the magnitude of geometric corrections using two different equations of state.

## Key numbers

* The correction to isothermal freezeout is at most a \(10\%\) effect even at collision energies as low as \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 11.5\) GeV.
* The isothermal freezeout approximation breaks down at collision energies below \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 39\) GeV, where the chemical potential becomes comparable to the temperature.
* The coefficient \(\mathcal{C}\) is defined as \(\mathcal{C}=(\partial e/\partial \mu|_T)/(\partial e/\partial T|_\mu)\) and determines which thermodynamic variation is larger on the hypersurface.

## Context

This work extends the isothermal freezeout framework of Becattini et al. (2021) which resolved the local polarization sign puzzle in high-energy collisions by assuming constant temperature on the freezeout surface. The present paper generalizes that approach to lower collision energies where chemical potentials are non-negligible, using the more realistic assumption of constant energy density freezeout common in hydrodynamic simulations. The results enable future full hydrodynamic implementations of spin polarization that properly account for freezeout geometry at moderate collision energies.
