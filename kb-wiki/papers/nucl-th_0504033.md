# Chiral dynamics of baryon resonances and hadrons in a nuclear medium

> In these lectures I make an introduction to chiral unitary theory applied to the meson baryon interaction and show how several well known resonances are dynamically generated, and others are predicted. Two very recent experiments are analyzed, one of them showing the existence of two $Λ(1405)$ states and the other one providing support for the $Λ(1520)$ resonance as a quasibound state of $Σ(1385) π$. The use of chiral Lagrangians to account for the hadronic interaction at the elementary level introduces a new approach to deal with the modification of meson and baryon properties in a nuclear medium. Examples of it for $\bar{K}$, $η$ and $ϕ$ modification in the nuclear medium are presented.

# Chiral dynamics of baryon resonances and hadrons in a nuclear medium

## Key claim

Chiral unitary theory dynamically generates baryon resonances (including the Λ(1405), Λ(1520), N*(1535), and Σ(1620)) as multiple-scattering states of mesons and baryons, not as preexisting quark-model states; the Λ(1405) is shown to correspond to two distinct poles (a singlet and an octet state) rather than a single resonance, and the Λ(1520) is described as a quasibound state of Σ(1385)π.

## Method

The authors apply chiral perturbation theory at lowest order to the meson-baryon interaction, then impose unitarity in coupled channels via the N/D method or Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorized kernel. They solve the resulting algebraic equations for the T-matrix in ten coupled channels for strangeness S=-1 (including K⁻p, πΣ, ηΛ, KΞ) and search for poles in the complex energy plane on the second Riemann sheet. For the decuplet-octet interaction, they extend the same formalism to the 3/2⁺ baryon decuplet interacting with the pseudoscalar meson octet.

## Key numbers

- The two Λ(1405) poles are found at (1390 + 66i) MeV and (1426 + 16i) MeV for isospin I=0, with the lower pole coupling more strongly to πΣ (|g| = 2.9) and the higher pole coupling more strongly to K̄N (|g| = 2.7)
- A third I=0 pole appears at (1680 + 20i) MeV, corresponding to the Λ(1670) resonance, with dominant coupling to KΞ (|g| = 3.5)
- The resonant part of the Λ(1405) in the K⁻p → π⁰π⁰Σ⁰ reaction has a width Γ = 38 MeV, narrower than the PDG average width of 50 ± 2 MeV
- The peak of the Λ(1405) in the K⁻p → π⁰π⁰Σ⁰ reaction appears at M_I = 1420 MeV (M_I² = 2.02 GeV²), higher than the nominal 1405 MeV
- The cut-off momentum used in the regularization is 630 MeV for all channels
- The SU(3) symmetric limit uses M₀ = 1151 MeV, m₀ = 368 MeV, and a₀ = -2.148

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to meson-meson scattering (Oller, Oset, 1997) and to meson-baryon systems (Kaiser, 1995; Oset, Ramos, 1998) by systematically analyzing the pole structure of dynamically generated resonances. The two-pole nature of the Λ(1405) was predicted in Jido (2003) and is here supported by comparison with the K⁻p → π⁰π⁰Σ⁰ experiment of Prakhov (2004), complementing the earlier π⁻p → K⁰πΣ study of Thomas (1973). The Λ(1520) as a Σ(1385)π quasibound state is tested against the K⁻p → π⁰π⁰Λ reaction of Prakhov (2004). The decuplet-octet interaction follows the work of Kolomeitsev (2003) and predicts multiple 3/2⁻ resonances, including an exotic S=1, I=1 state from ΔK channels whose existence remains uncertain due to sensitivity to the subtraction constant.
