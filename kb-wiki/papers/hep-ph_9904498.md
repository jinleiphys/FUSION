# Three-Flavor Symmetry of Hadrons Consistent With the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka Rule

> It is argued that the only three-flavor symmetry which is consistent with the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka (OZI) rule, and is therefore dictated by the gluon gauge dynamics, is the heavy c quark limit of a certain U(4)_F subgroup (called S_OZI here) and defined as S_OZI= \lim_(m_c\to Λ_c) SU(2)_ud * SU(2)_cd * U(1) with the two SU(2) groups acting in turn onto the 1st and 2nd quark generations rather than Gell-Mann's eightfold SU(3)_F. Within this scheme the presence of non-strange quarkonium components in the wave function of the physical eta and f_1(1285) mesons appears necessarily through the violation of the OZI rule for the pseudoscalar and axial vector mesons by the anomalous axial U(1)_A baryon number current and not by a primordial octet flavor symmetry. The physical observable which selects the S_OZI over the SU(3)_F scheme is the non-isotriplet part of the neutral axial current. In the case of the S_OZI symmetry the only non-isotriplet neutral axial current having a well defined chiral limit is purely strange, while within the SU(3)_F framework it is the octet axial current which is supposed to be partially conserved. Accordingly, while in the latter case the tree level eta N coupling is of significant strength, its almost vanishes in the former, in agreement with data, thus confirming the relevance of the S_OZI symmetry.

# Three-Flavor Symmetry of Hadrons Consistent With the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka Rule

## Key claim

The three-flavor symmetry of hadrons that is consistent with the OZI rule, and therefore with gluon gauge dynamics, is the heavy charm quark limit of a U(4)_F subgroup called S_OZI = lim_{m_c→Λ_c} SU(2)_ud ⊗ SU(2)_cs ⊗ U(1), rather than Gell-Mann's SU(3)_F eightfold way. The physical observable that selects S_OZI over SU(3)_F is the non-isotriplet part of the neutral axial current, which in the S_OZI scheme is purely strange and has a well-defined chiral limit, whereas in the SU(3)_F framework it is the octet axial current that is supposed to be partially conserved.

## Method

The author constructs the S_OZI symmetry by starting from the four-flavor unitary group U(4)_F and taking the heavy charm quark limit, freezing out the charm degree of freedom. The Weyl basis for the diagonal generators of SU(4) is used to define the neutral meson states, and the transformation properties of the electromagnetic and axial currents are analyzed in this basis. The structure of the vector meson sector (where the OZI rule is respected) is compared to the pseudoscalar and axial vector meson sectors (where the OZI rule is violated by the U(1)_A anomaly), and the consequences for the ηN coupling constant are derived from the Goldberger-Treiman relations in both the SU(3)_F and S_OZI frameworks.

## Key numbers

- The physical η meson wave function contains a non-strange quarkonium component with mixing angle ε = 45.3° relative to the purely strange quarkonium state, indicating OZI rule violation.
- The ideal mixing angle for vector mesons in the SU(3)_F basis is θ = 35.3°, which restores the Weyl four-flavor basis.
- The octet η meson mixing angle in the SU(3)_F scheme is α = cos(−10.1°), giving the physical η as a mixture of octet and singlet states.
- A cutoff value of Λ_c ≈ 1.5 GeV is suggested for freezing out the charm quark degree of freedom.
- The axial vector meson f_1(1285) shows OZI rule violation with a mixing angle of ε ≈ −17°.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Kirchbach, 1990s) showing that baryon excitation spectra reveal Lorentz covariant spin clustering incompatible with the SU(3)_F ⊗ SU(2)_S × O(3)_L classification scheme, and that the octet Goldberger-Treiman relation predicting the ηN coupling is seriously violated (Kirchbach and Weise, 1990s). The paper contradicts Gell-Mann's eightfold way (Gell-Mann, 1961; Zweig, 1964) by proposing that the correct three-flavor symmetry arises from truncating a four-flavor symmetry that respects the OZI rule (Okubo, Zweig, Iizuka, 1960s-1970s). This enables a new understanding of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD via the chain [SU(2)^L_ud ⊗ SU(2)^L_cs] ⊗ [SU(2)^R_ud ⊗ SU(2)^R_cs] / SU(2)_{I,V,U} rather than SU(3)^L ⊗ SU(3)^R / SU(3)_F, with pions and kaons as Goldstone bosons and the η meson acting as a "masked" strange Goldstone boson through its strange quarkonium component.
