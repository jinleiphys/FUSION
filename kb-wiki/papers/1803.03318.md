# Off-equilibrium corrections to energy and conserved charge densities in the relativistic fluid in heavy-ion collisions

> Dissipative processes in relativistic fluids are known to be important in the analyses of the hot QCD matter created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. In this work, I consider dissipative corrections to energy and conserved charge densities, which are conventionally assumed to be vanishing but could be finite. Causal dissipative hydrodynamics is formulated in the presence of those dissipative currents. The relation between hydrodynamic stability and transport coefficients is discussed. I then study their phenomenological consequences on the observables of heavy-ion collisions in numerical simulations. It is shown that particle spectra and elliptic flow can be visibly modified.

## Key claim

The paper shows that off-equilibrium corrections to energy and conserved charge densities, conventionally set to zero via Landau matching conditions, can be finite and visibly modify particle spectra and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions, provided the transport coefficients satisfy the condition of positive entropy production and the off-equilibrium phase-space distribution at freeze-out is properly included.

## Method

The author derives Israel-Stewart-type second-order constitutive equations for the scalar dissipative currents \(w\) (energy density correction) and \(v_J\) (charge density correction) alongside the bulk pressure \(\Pi\), using the Grad momentum expansion of the phase-space distribution. A (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic model with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions and a lattice QCD-based equation of state is then used to numerically simulate Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, with the effective transport coefficients parametrized in terms of dimensionless factors \(C_\Pi\), \(C_w\), and \(C_v\) motivated by gauge-gravity correspondence.

## Key numbers

- The effective transport coefficients are parametrized as \(\zeta_{\Pi} = C_\Pi (1/3 - c_s^2) (e+P)/(4\pi T)\), \(\zeta_{w} = C_w (1/3 - c_s^2) (e+P)/(4\pi T)\), and \(\zeta_{v} = C_v (1/3 - c_s^2) n_B/(4\pi T)\), with the conjectured lower boundary for bulk viscosity corresponding to \(C_\Pi = 2\).
- For positive energy density correction with \(C_w = 6\) and including the \(\delta f\) correction at freeze-out, the mean \(p_T\) of positive pions is reduced by 1.6% and the number of particles is increased by 2% relative to the ideal case.
- Without the \(\delta f\) correction, the same positive energy density correction reduces mean \(p_T\) by 4.4% and reduces particle number by 2.5%.
- The effect of the baryon number density correction (\(C_v = \pm 6\)) is found to be negligible at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV due to the small baryon density.
- The freeze-out energy density used is \(e_f = 0.14\) GeV, corresponding to \(T \approx 0.14\) GeV at vanishing chemical potential; the initial hydrodynamic time is \(\tau_\mathrm{th} = 0.4\) fm/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the standard relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic framework (Israel and Stewart, 1979) by relaxing the Landau matching conditions that conventionally set \(w\) and \(v_J\) to zero. It builds on prior discussions of generalized matching conditions by Eckart (1940), Osada (2009, 2011, 2014), and Bemfica (2017) regarding causality at non-linear order. The numerical results show that the effects of \(w\) partially cancel with those of bulk viscosity, implying that extraction of the bulk viscous coefficient from experimental data could be complicated by the presence of these off-equilibrium density corrections. Future work is suggested for lower beam energies (Beam Energy Scan at RHIC) and for electromagnetic probes that may distinguish the two types of dissipative currents.
