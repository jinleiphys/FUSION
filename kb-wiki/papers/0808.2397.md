# Target mass corrections for spin-dependent structure functions in collinear factorization

> We derive target mass corrections (TMC) for the spin-dependent nucleon structure function g1 and polarization asymmetry A1 in collinear factorization at leading twist. The TMCs are found to be significant for g1 at large xB, even at relatively high Q^2 values, but largely cancel in A1. A comparison of TMCs obtained from collinear factorization and from the operator product expansion shows that at low Q^2 the corrections drive the proton A1 in opposite directions.

## Key claim

Target mass corrections (TMCs) for the spin-dependent nucleon structure function g1 are significant at large xB even at relatively high Q2 values, but largely cancel in the polarization asymmetry A1; however, at low Q2 the corrections drive the proton A1 in opposite directions when comparing the collinear factorization (CF) approach to the operator product expansion (OPE) approach.

## Method

The authors derived TMCs for the spin-dependent structure functions g1 and g2 and the polarization asymmetry A1 using the collinear factorization (CF) formalism, which avoids the threshold problem inherent in the OPE approach by directly implementing four-momentum conservation in the handbag diagram. They compared these CF results numerically with the standard OPE-based TMC prescription, using leading-order GRSV2000 polarized parton distributions for g1 and GRV98LO unpolarized distributions for F1, evaluating the ratios of target-mass-corrected to massless structure functions and asymmetries at Q2 values of 1, 3, 5, and 10 GeV2.

## Key numbers

- At Q2 = 1 GeV2, the OPE-corrected g1 structure function can be up to ~20% larger than the CF-corrected g1 at xB ~ 0.4.
- At Q2 = 10 GeV2, the CF and OPE methods give essentially the same results at the 2% level for all xB less than or equal to 0.8.
- The residual TMC effects in A1 can be up to 20% at Q2 = 1 GeV2 for xB ~ 0.8-0.9, decreasing to ~2-3% at Q2 = 10 GeV2.
- At xB less than or equal to 0.5, the size of TMCs is less than or equal to 2-3% for Q2 greater than 10 GeV2.
- The polarization asymmetry A1 is found to be up to ~20-30% larger within the OPE approach at xB greater than or equal to 0.7 for Q2 = 1 GeV2.

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization approach to TMCs, previously applied to spin-averaged structure functions by Accardi and Qiu (2008), to the spin-dependent case for the first time. It contrasts with the standard OPE-based TMC formalism developed by Georgi and Politzer (1976) and later extended to spin-dependent scattering by Matsuda (1979), Piccione and Ridolfi (1997), and Blumlein and Tkabladze (1999), which suffers from a threshold problem where corrected structure functions remain nonzero at xB greater than or equal to 1. The CF framework naturally avoids this issue and can be extended to semi-inclusive DIS and other hard scattering processes where the OPE is not available.
