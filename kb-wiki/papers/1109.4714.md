# Penta-quark states with hidden charm and beauty

> More and more hadron states are found to be difficult to be accommodated by the quenched quark models which describe baryons as 3-quark states and mesons as antiquark-quark states. Dragging out an antiquark-quark pair from the gluon field in hadrons should be an important excitation mechanism for hadron spectroscopy. Our recent progress on the penta-quark states with hidden charm and beauty is reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that penta-quark (5-quark) components in baryons, arising from dragging out a quark-antiquark pair from the gluon field, can explain the mass reverse problem and strange decay properties of the lowest negative parity baryons, and that extending this picture to hidden charm and beauty sectors predicts super-heavy N* and Lambda* resonances that cannot be accommodated by three-quark models.

## Method

The author reviews the unquenched quark model approach where baryons are mixtures of three-quark (qqq) and five-quark (qqqqq-bar) components, with a breathing mode of qqq ↔ qqqqq-bar transitions. For the hidden charm and beauty sectors, the Valencia approach using local hidden gauge formalism with meson-baryon coupled channel unitary dynamics is applied, along with a conventional Schrodinger Equation approach to check consistency of predictions for bound states.

## Key numbers

- The lowest negative parity N* resonance N*(1535) is heavier than two other spatial excited baryons: Lambda*(1405) and N*(1440)
- Quenched quark models predict the 1/2- Sigma* and Xi* around 1650 MeV and 1760 MeV respectively; unquenched models predict them around 1400 MeV and 1550 MeV; meson-baryon dynamical models predict around 1450 MeV and 1620 MeV
- Several meson-baryon dynamically generated narrow N* and Lambda* resonances with hidden charm are predicted with mass around 4.3 GeV and width smaller than 100 MeV
- Two N*_{b\bar b} states and four Lambda*_{b\bar b} states are predicted with masses above 11 GeV and widths of only a few MeV
- For pp → pp eta_b reaction, the best center-of-mass energy for observing N*_{b\bar b} is 13-25 GeV with production cross section about 0.01 nb
- For e-p → e-p Upsilon reaction, when center-of-mass energy is larger than 14 GeV, production cross section should be larger than 0.1 nb

## Context

This work extends the unquenched quark model approach previously applied to strangeness (Helminen, Zhu et al., 2000s) to the hidden charm and beauty sectors, following the Valencia approach of Ramos (2000s) and the local hidden gauge formalism studies by Wu et al. (2010). The predicted super-heavy N* and Lambda* states with hidden charm and beauty are distinguishable from three-quark model predictions and could be searched for at PANDA/FAIR, JLab 12-GeV upgrade, and future electron-ion collider facilities. The recent observation of Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) by Belle Collaboration provides supporting evidence for the existence of such exotic states.
