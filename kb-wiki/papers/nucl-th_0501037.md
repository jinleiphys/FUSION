# Residual interaction effects on deeply bound pionic states in Sn and Pb isotopes

> We have studied the residual interaction effects theoretically on the deeply bound pionic states in Pb and Sn isotopes. We need to evaluate the residual interaction effects carefully in order to deduce the nuclear medium effects for pion properties, which are believed to provide valuable information on nuclear chiral dynamics. The s- and p-wave $π-N$ interactions are used for the pion-nucleon residual interactions. We show that the complex energy shifts are around [(10-20)+i(2-7)]keV for 1s states in Sn, which should be taken into account in the analyses of the high precision data of deeply bound pionic $1s$ states in Sn isotopes.

## Key claim

The residual interaction between the pion and the neutron-hole in the final state of (d,3He) reactions produces complex energy shifts for deeply bound pionic 1s states in Sn isotopes that are comparable to the experimental errors of around 20 keV, meaning these effects cannot be automatically neglected in analyses aiming to extract chiral dynamics parameters, although they are still slightly smaller than the current experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the Hamiltonian for the pion plus neutron-hole system, including both s-wave and p-wave pion-nucleon residual interactions with parameters fixed from the pion-nucleus optical potential. They considered six pionic states (1s, 2s, 2p, 3s, 3p, 3d) and four neutron-hole configurations for Pb isotopes or five for Sn isotopes, using Woods-Saxon neutron wavefunctions and two-parameter Fermi density distributions for the nuclear density. The complex energy shifts were calculated as the difference between the eigenenergies of the coupled pion-nucleus system and the unperturbed energies.

## Key numbers

- For the dominant configuration [(1s)π ⊗ s1/2-1]1/2 in Sn isotopes, the real energy shift is -15.4 keV for 115Sn, -13.5 keV for 119Sn, and -12.3 keV for 123Sn, with imaginary shifts of -4.2 keV, -3.3 keV, and -2.4 keV respectively.
- The experimental errors for the 1s states in Sn isotopes are around ΔE ~ 20 keV for the real part and ±36 to ±44 keV for the imaginary part, as reported in Suzuki et al. (2004).
- For 207Pb, the complex energy shifts for the [(1s)π ⊗ p1/2-1]1/2 configuration are (-13.3 - 2.9i) keV with both s- and p-wave interactions, compared to (-14.2 - 3.1i) keV with only s-wave interaction.
- For 205Pb, the largest shift for the [(2p)π ⊗ f5/2-1]3/2 configuration is (-22.6 - 7.3i) keV, approximately half the experimental error of ±45 keV for the real part.
- The complex energy shifts for 1s states in Sn isotopes are typically around [(10-20) + i(2-7)] keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Hirenzaki et al. (1999) which considered only s-wave residual interactions for 207Pb, by now including p-wave interactions and applying the formalism to Sn isotopes. The paper directly addresses the analysis of the high-precision (d,3He) experiments on Sn targets performed by Suzuki et al. (2004), which observed deeply bound pionic 1s states with errors comparable to the residual interaction effects. The results enable more accurate extraction of the s-wave pion-nucleus potential parameters, which are connected through the Tomozawa-Weinberg theorem to the pion decay constant fπ and the quark condensate via the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation.
