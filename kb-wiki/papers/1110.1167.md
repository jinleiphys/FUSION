# Special 6j and 9j symbols for a single-j shell

> In this Brief Report, we propose general expressions for 6j and 9j coefficients arising in the study of the J-pairing interaction of identical fermions in a single-j shell. In the literature such coefficients are available only for a number of given angular momenta.

## Key claim

The paper provides general, unified analytical expressions for specific 6j and 9j coefficients that arise in J-pairing interactions of identical fermions in a single-j shell, where previously only formulas for fixed angular momenta were available. The 9j coefficient for J=2j-1 is presented as a conjecture based on empirical fitting and numerical checks, not a proven derivation.

## Method

The author derives a closed-form expression for the 6j coefficient {j j 2j-1; j I 2j-1} by applying a known formula from Varshalovich (1988) with a=b=j and e=I. For the 9j coefficient with J=2j, the author applies the general formula of Bandzaitis et al. (1964) and Jucys (1977) that expresses the 9j symbol in terms of a 3jm coefficient, then evaluates the 3jm coefficient using the standard formula from Edmonds (1957). The 9j coefficient with J=2j-1 is found empirically and checked numerically with a Fortran code for various j and I up to j=100, I=156.

## Key numbers

- The 6j coefficient {j j 2j-1; j I 2j-1} equals (-1)^(3j+I) (9j^2 - 5j - I(I+1)) × (2j)!/(4j)! × (j+I-1)!/(I-j+1)!
- The sum rule over even J: Σ_even J (2J+1) {j j J; j I J} equals 3/2 [(2I+3)/6] - I/2 - 1/4 for I ≤ j, and 3/2 [(3j-3-I)/6] + (3/2)δ_{I,j} - 1/2 [(3j+1-I)/2] for I ≥ j
- The sum rule over odd J: Σ_odd J (2J+1) {j j J; j I J} equals -1 if 2j=3k, 0 if 2j=3k+1, 1 if 2j=3k+2
- The 9j coefficient {j j 2j-1; j j 2j-1; 2j-1 2j-1 I} equals (-1)^(2j+I/2)/2 × (I-1)!! (4j-I-3)!!/(4j+1)!! × (2j+I/2-1)! (2j-1)!/((I/2)! (4j-1)!) × (8j^2 - 6j - I(I+1)/2), for even integer I
- The 9j coefficient {j j 2j; j j 2j; 2j 2j I} equals (-1)^(2j+I/2) × ((2j)!/(4j+1)!)^2 × I! (4j-I)! (2j+I/2)! / (((I/2)!)^2 (2j-I/2)!), for even integer I

## Context

This work extends the series of papers by Zhao and Arima (2003, 2004, 2005a, 2005b) and Zhang (2008) on Hamiltonians with attractive J-pairing interactions in single-j shells, where analytical formulas for the relevant 6j and 9j coefficients were previously given only for fixed values of angular momentum I. The unified formulas enable calculations for arbitrary I values in studies of three-fermion and four-fermion systems with J-pairing interactions, and the sum rules with weighting factor (-1)^J(2J+1) connect to earlier work by Vanagas and Batarunas (1961) on characters of the symmetric group SO(3). The 9j result for J=2j-1 remains a conjecture warranting mathematical proof.
