# Event-by-Event Simulations of Jet Modification Using the MATTER Event Generator

> The modification of hard jets in the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) is studied using the MATTER event generator. Based on the higher twist formalism of energy loss, the MATTER event generator simulates the evolution of highly virtual partons through a medium. These partons sampled from an underlying PYTHIA kernel undergo splitting through a combination of vacuum and medium induced emission. The momentum exchange with the medium is simulated via the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$, which is assumed to scale with the entropy density at a given location in the medium. The entropy density is obtained from a relativistic viscous fluid dynamics simulation (VISH2+1D) in 2+1 space time dimensions. Results for jet and hadron observables are presented using an independent fragmentation model. These proceedings will focus on the physics input and simulation details of the MATTER event generator as compared to a variety of test observables.

## Key claim

The MATTER event generator, based on the higher-twist formalism, can simulate jet modification in the quark-gluon plasma with results consistent with experimental data for the nuclear modification factor R_AA, but only in the regime where the jet transport coefficient times the formation time divided by the parton virtuality is less than or approximately equal to 1, limiting its applicability to high-energy, high-virtuality partons.

## Method

The simulation uses PYTHIA to generate initial hard partons at midrapidity, the OSU (Ohio State University) 2+1D relativistic viscous fluid dynamics code (VISH2+1D) to provide the medium entropy density, and the MATTER event generator to simulate parton shower evolution via the higher-twist formalism. The jet transport coefficient q-hat is assumed to scale with the local entropy density from the hydrodynamic simulation, and the Sudakov form factor including a medium-induced emission kernel is sampled to determine parton virtualities and splitting kinematics. Final partons with virtuality above 1 GeV^2 are showered iteratively; those within 1 fm of the medium edge are kept, and the Kniehl-Kramer-Potter (KKP) fragmentation function is applied to obtain leading hadron spectra, while Fastjet with the anti-k_T algorithm (R=0.4) is used for full jet reconstruction.

## Key numbers

- Leading pion R_AA for 200 GeV Au+Au collisions compared to PHENIX data, shown for varying q-hat_0 values (q-hat_0 = 2.4 GeV^2/fm used as baseline)
- Leading hadron R_AA for 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions compared to CMS data, with q-hat_0 = 2.4 GeV^2/fm
- Hard p_T bins from 2.5-52.5 GeV for Au+Au and 2.5-227.5 GeV for Pb+Pb, in 5 GeV wide bins
- Parton shower cutoff at virtuality 1 GeV^2, below which the higher-twist formalism is no longer applicable
- Partons removed if further than 1 fm from the edge of the medium

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist formalism of energy loss (Majumder 2009, 2011) into a Monte-Carlo event generator, complementing other approaches such as Q-PYTHIA based on the Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann scheme (Armesto 2009), MARTINI based on the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe scheme (Schenke 2009), JEWEL (Zapp 2012), YaJEM (Renk 2012), and PYQUEN (Lokhtin 2005). The MATTER generator enables event-by-event simulations with fluctuating space-time structure of the shower, which previous approaches did not address consistently; future plans include coupling to a multiple-scattering-per-emission generator like MARTINI for low-virtuality partons and incorporating medium response and thermal-shower recombination hadronization.
