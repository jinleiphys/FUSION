# Synchrotron radiation in strongly coupled conformal field theories

> Using gauge/gravity duality, we compute the energy density and angular distribution of the power radiated by a quark undergoing circular motion in strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory. We compare the strong coupling results to those at weak coupling, and find the same angular distribution of radiated power, up to an overall prefactor. In both regimes, the angular distribution is in fact similar to that of synchrotron radiation produced by an electron in circular motion in classical electrodynamics: the quark emits radiation in a narrow beam along its velocity vector with a characteristic opening angle $α\sim 1/γ$. To an observer far away from the quark, the emitted radiation appears as a short periodic burst, just like the light from a lighthouse does to a ship at sea. Our strong coupling results are valid for any strongly coupled conformal field theory with a dual classical gravity description.

## Key claim

Using gauge/gravity duality, the authors compute the energy density and angular distribution of power radiated by a quark in circular motion in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory and find that, contrary to expectations from prior studies of isotropization at strong coupling, the radiation pattern is nearly identical to classical synchrotron radiation, a narrow beam along the velocity vector with opening angle α ∼ 1/γ, with the same angular distribution at weak and strong coupling up to an overall prefactor.

## Method

The authors use gauge/gravity duality to map the strongly coupled quantum field theory problem onto a classical gravitational calculation in AdS5 spacetime, where a rotating string with its endpoint attached to the 4d boundary represents the quark; the resulting gravitational perturbation induces a stress tensor on the boundary that gives the expectation value of the quantum field theory stress tensor. At weak coupling, they use a classical effective field theory with a U(1) gauge field and a real scalar field, with effective couplings e_eff^2 = λ/2, solving the retarded Green's function equations for the fields sourced by the quark in circular motion.

## Key numbers

- Total radiated power at strong coupling: P = (√λ / 2π) a², where a = γ² v ω₀ is the proper acceleration (Mikhailov 2003 result)
- Effective coupling at weak coupling: e_eff² = g² (N_c² − 1)/(2 N_c) ≈ λ/2
- Characteristic opening angle of the radiation beam: α ∼ 1/γ for large γ
- Radial width of the spiral of radiation at large γ: Δ ∼ R₀/γ³
- Condition for vacuum-like radiation at nonzero temperature: ω₀² γ³ ≫ π² T²

## Context

This work extends Mikhailov's (2003) calculation of total power radiated by an arbitrarily moving test quark in strongly coupled N=4 SYM to the full angular distribution for circular motion. It contrasts with findings by Hofman and Maldacena (2008) that radiation from off-shell boson decays in strongly coupled holographic CFTs is isotropic with no spin correlation, and with studies by Hatta et al. (2008) and Chesler et al. (2008) suggesting isotropization of radiation at strong coupling. The results enable future extension to nonzero temperature, where the narrow synchrotron beam could be studied as it interacts with and thermalizes in a strongly coupled plasma, potentially providing insight into jet quenching in heavy ion collisions.
