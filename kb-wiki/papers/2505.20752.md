# Electromagnetic tomography of radial flow in the quark-gluon plasma

> We present a novel multimessenger approach to extract the effective radial flow of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by jointly analyzing thermal photon and dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions. A key feature of this method is that it circumvents the need for a directly unmeasurable reference -- the photon temperature in the absence of flow -- by establishing, within a calibrated model framework, a stable, approximately linear correlation with the dilepton-inferred temperature. This construction defines an experimentally constructible quantity, $v_r^\mathrm{eff}$, which reflects early-time collectivity and exhibits a strong correlation with the spacetime-averaged radial velocity of the QGP. Together with previous results linking dilepton slopes to the initial QGP temperature, our work establishes a consistent framework for electromagnetic tomography of the QGP. Our framework quantifies the experimental precision target, thereby providing a concrete roadmap for future measurements at RHIC and the LHC and opening a new avenue to probe the early-time dynamics of hot QCD matter.

## Key claim

The authors propose a multimessenger method to extract the effective radial flow of the quark-gluon plasma by jointly analyzing thermal photon and dilepton spectra, circumventing the need for the directly unmeasurable photon temperature in the absence of flow by exploiting a stable, approximately linear correlation between the dilepton-inferred temperature and the no-flow photon temperature within a calibrated model framework.

## Method

The authors used a (3+1)-dimensional multistage hydrodynamic framework with parametric initial conditions, the MUSIC hydrodynamics code, the iS3D sampler for particlization, and UrQMD for the hadronic afterburner, calibrated to hadronic observables across nine beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. Thermal photon and dilepton spectra were computed by integrating emission rates over the full hydrodynamic history, with effective temperatures extracted from fits to the photon transverse momentum spectra (exponential form for pT in [0.8, 2] GeV) and dilepton invariant mass spectra (parametrization for M in [1, 3] GeV). The effective radial flow velocity was then inferred using the relativistic blue-shift relation between the measured photon temperature and the no-flow baseline estimated from the dilepton temperature via a calibrated linear correlation.

## Key numbers

* The global linear fit (excluding sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV) relating dilepton temperature T_ll to no-flow photon temperature T_gamma0 gives T_ll = (1.78 +/- 0.01) T_gamma0 - (10.5 +/- 0.2) x 10^(-2) GeV.
* A constant offset Delta T = 4.5 MeV reproduces T_gamma0 from T_ll for sqrt(s_NN) > 7.7 GeV, while Delta T = 4 MeV suffices at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV.
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV in 0-10% Au+Au collisions, the effective radial flow v_r^eff = 0.036 +/- 0.017 c, while the pre-freezeout radial flow v_r^prefrz = 0.108 +/- 0.116 c and the freezeout radial flow v_r^frz = 0.449 +/- 0.156 c.
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV in 20-30% Au+Au collisions, T_ll = 200 +/- 4 MeV, T_gamma0 = 169 +/- 0 MeV, and T_gamma = 171 +/- 0 MeV.
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV in 20-30% Au+Au collisions, T_ll = 295 +/- 2 MeV, T_gamma0 = 224 +/- 3 MeV, and T_gamma = 233 +/- 2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the electromagnetic tomography program by building on previous results linking dilepton slopes to the initial QGP temperature (Churchill 2023, Du 2024) and earlier time-resolved analyses showing that T_ll and T_gamma0 evolve in parallel with constant offset (Du 2025). The method establishes a consistent framework for extracting early-time collectivity from electromagnetic probes, providing a concrete precision target for future measurements at RHIC and the LHC, analogous to how unexpectedly large photon anisotropic flow observed at RHIC and the LHC (PHENIX 2015, ALICE 2018) revealed richer dynamics than initially anticipated.
