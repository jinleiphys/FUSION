# Exact Relations for Two-Photon-Exchange Effect in Elastic $ep$ Scattering by Dispersion Relation and Hadronic Model

> The two-photon-exchange (TPE) effect plays a key role to extract the form factors (FFs) of the proton. In this work, we present some exact properties on the TPE effect in the elastic $ep$ scattering based on four types of typical and general interactions. The possible kinematical singularities, the asymptotic behaviors and the branch cuts of the TPE amplitudes are analyzed. The analytic expressions clearly indicate some exact relations between the dispersion relation (DR) method and the hadronic model (HM) method. It suggests that the two methods should be modified to general forms, respectively. After the modifications the new forms give the same results. Furthermore, they automatically and correctly include the contributions due to the seagull interaction, the meson-exchange effect, the contact interactions and the off-shell effect. To analyze the elastic $e^{\pm}p$ scattering data sets, the new forms should be used.

## Key claim

The dispersion relation (DR) method and the hadronic model (HM) method for calculating the two-photon-exchange (TPE) effect in elastic electron-proton scattering must be modified to include a non-trivial term with two singularities; after this modification, both methods give exactly the same results and automatically incorporate contributions from seagull interactions, meson-exchange effects, contact interactions, and off-shell effects.

## Method

The authors analyzed the analytic structures, kinematical singularities, asymptotic behaviors, and branch cuts, of TPE amplitudes using four low-energy toy interactions: a minimal electromagnetic coupling (ℒ_E), a Pauli-type magnetic coupling (ℒ_M), a seagull-type polarizability interaction (ℒ_S), and a tensor-meson exchange interaction (ℒ_T). They computed the TPE amplitudes for box, crossed-box, seagull, and meson-exchange diagrams using dimensional regularization with FeynCalc and PackageX, then compared the results from the standard dispersion relation (DR1 and DR2) and hadronic model methods to derive exact relations between them.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach of Borisyuk and Kobushkin (2008) and Tomalak and Vanderhaeghen (2015) by showing that the standard DR1 and DR2 forms are incomplete. It resolves the long-standing discrepancy between the DR method and the hadronic model method by demonstrating that both require modification to include polynomial terms in ν that account for seagull interactions, meson-exchange effects (previously studied by Zhou (2014, 2017), Afanasev (2017), Borisyuk (2017), and Dorokhov (2018)), contact interactions, and off-shell effects. The new modified DR forms (DR3) with two additional unknown functions f_i(t) provide a unified framework for analyzing elastic e±p scattering data to extract proton form factors.
