# Importance of non-flow in mixed-harmonic multi-particle correlations in small collision systems

> Recently CMS Collaboration measured mixed-harmonic four-particle azimuthal correlations, known as symmetric cumulants SC(n,m), in pp and pPb collisions, and interpreted the non-zero SC(n,m) as evidence for long-range collectivity in these small collision systems. Using the PYTHIA and HIJING models which do not have genuine long-range collectivity, we show that the CMS results, obtained with standard cumulant method, could be dominated by non-flow effects associated with jet and dijets, especially in $pp$ collisions. We show that the non-flow effects are largely suppressed using the recently proposed subevent cumulant methods by requiring azimuthal correlation between two or more pseudorapidity ranges. We argue that the reanalysis of SC(n,m) using the subevent method in experiments is necessary before they can used to provide further evidences for a long-range multi-particle collectivity and constraints on theoretical models in small collision systems.

## Key claim
Using PYTHIA8 and HIJING models that contain only non-flow correlations (jets, dijets, resonance decays) and no genuine long-range collectivity, the authors show that the CMS symmetric cumulant measurements in pp and pPb collisions, obtained with the standard cumulant method, could be dominated by non-flow effects; they argue that reanalysis using subevent methods is necessary before these measurements can be interpreted as evidence for long-range multi-particle collectivity.

## Method
The authors generated pp events at √s = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8 and pPb events at √sNN = 5.02 TeV using HIJING. They calculated symmetric cumulants SC(2,3) and SC(2,4) for charged particles with |η| < 2.5 in two pT ranges (0.3 < pT < 3 GeV and 0.5 < pT < 5 GeV) using the standard cumulant method and compared results to CMS data. They then recalculated the same observables using two-subevent, three-subevent, and four-subevent methods, which require azimuthal correlations between particles from different pseudorapidity intervals to suppress non-flow.

## Key numbers
- In pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV, both SC(2,3) and SC(2,4) from PYTHIA8 using the standard method are larger than the CMS data over the full Nch range (Figure 1, left panel).
- In pPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, SC(2,3) and SC(2,4) from HIJING using the standard method are larger than CMS data for Nch < 70, but much smaller than data for Nch > 100 (Figure 1, right panel).
- Non-flow in pPb collisions is approximately ten times smaller than in pp collisions at comparable Nch for Nch > 100.
- For pp collisions with 0.5 < pT < 5 GeV, the four-subevent method gives SC(2,3) and SC(2,4) values closest to zero, followed by three-subevent and then two-subevent methods (Figure 3).
- SC(2,3) from subevent methods in PYTHIA8 shows a small negative value that cannot be fully suppressed to zero even with the four-subevent method.

## Context
This work extends the application of subevent cumulant methods, previously shown necessary for reliable c2{4} measurements in small systems by Jia (2016) and Aaboud (2017), to the symmetric cumulant observables SC(2,3) and SC(2,4) measured by CMS (Sirunyan 2017). The results demonstrate that the standard cumulant method is contaminated by non-flow in pp collisions over the full Nch range and in pPb collisions at low Nch, while subevent methods suppress these contributions. This enables future experimental reanalyses of symmetric cumulants using subevent methods to provide cleaner constraints on theoretical models of long-range collectivity in small collision systems.
