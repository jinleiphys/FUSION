# Meron Pairs and Fermion Zero Modes

> Merons, conjectured as a semiclassical mechanism for color confinement in QCD, have been described analytically by either infinite action configurations or an Ansatz with discontinuous action. We construct a smooth, finite action, stationary lattice solution corresponding to a meron pair. We also derive an analytical solution for the zero mode of the meron pair Ansatz, show that it has the qualitative behavior of the exact zero mode of the lattice solution, and propose the use of zero modes to identify meron gauge field configurations in stochastic evaluations of the lattice QCD path integral.

## Key claim

The authors construct a smooth, finite-action, stationary lattice solution for a meron pair, and they derive the analytic fermion zero mode for the meron pair Ansatz; however, the lattice solution is not a strict minimum of the action and will coalesce into an instanton under repeated relaxation, so the claim is that meron pairs are quasi-stationary configurations whose logarithmic interaction may allow them to dominate the path integral and confine color charge.

## Method

The authors put the patched meron-pair Ansatz (with instanton caps) onto a space-time lattice by replacing gauge fields with parallel transport link variables, integrating analytically within the caps and numerically outside. They then apply a cooling algorithm (iterative local minimization of the lattice action) to smooth out the unphysical discontinuities in the action density at the cap boundaries, producing a stationary lattice solution. The fermion zero mode is computed numerically using the Arnoldi method and compared to the analytic solution derived from the continuum Ansatz.

## Key numbers

- The action of the patched Ansatz is \( S = S_0 \left( 1 + \frac{3}{8} \ln \frac{R}{r} \right) \), where \( S_0 = 8\pi^2/g^2 \), showing a logarithmic dependence on the ratio of the outer cap radius \(R\) to the inner cap radius \(r\).
- The lattice calculation used a \(32^3 \times 40\) lattice with lattice spacing \(a\), with specific parameters \(r = 9\) and \(d = 20.8\) (in lattice units) for the comparison shown in Fig. 2.
- The maximum action density within the caps is \( S_{\rm max} = (48/g^2) (R+r)^4/d^8 \).
- The normalization constant for the analytic zero mode is \( {\cal N}^{-1} = 2\pi d^2 \left[2 - \sqrt{r/R} \right]^{1/2} \).
- The smooth adiabatic lattice meron pair solutions exhibit a logarithmic action curve that is about 0.25 lower than the analytic Ansatz (in units of \(S_0\)).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical study of merons as a mechanism for color confinement, which was originally proposed by Callan, Dashen, and Gross (1977) using a patched Ansatz with discontinuous action. The authors build on lattice techniques previously used to study instantons (Chu, Grandy, Huang, and Negele, 1994) and the use of fermionic zero modes to identify topological objects (Ivanenko and Negele, 1998). The results enable future investigations of whether meron pairs can be identified in stochastic evaluations of the lattice QCD path integral, potentially providing a semiclassical explanation for confinement analogous to the Kosterlitz-Thouless phase transition.
