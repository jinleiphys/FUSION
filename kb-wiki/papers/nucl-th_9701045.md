# ``Pressure Equilibration'' in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the time scale for pressure equilibration in heavy ion collisions at AGS energies within the three-fluid hydrodynamical model and a microscopic cascade model (UrQMD). We find that kinetic equilibrium is reached in both models after a time of 5 fm/c (center-of-mass time). Thus, observables which are sensitive to the early stage of the reaction differ considerably from the expectations within the instant thermalization scenario (one-fluid hydrodynamical model).

## Key claim
The paper finds that kinetic (pressure) equilibration in heavy ion collisions at AGS energies (11 AGeV) requires approximately 5 fm/c of center-of-mass time, contradicting the instant thermalization assumption of one-fluid hydrodynamics and showing that observables sensitive to the early stage differ from that scenario.

## Method
The authors compare a three-fluid hydrodynamical model (projectile nucleons, target nucleons, and a net-baryon-free fireball of produced particles) with the microscopic cascade model UrQMD for central Au+Au collisions at 11 AGeV. In the three-fluid model, local thermal equilibrium is assumed within each fluid but not among fluids, with coupling between nucleonic fluids parametrized via free binary NN-collisions and merging when relative velocities become comparable to thermal velocities.

## Key numbers
- Pressure equilibration time is approximately 5 fm/c = 2R_Au/gamma_CM for Au(11 AGeV)+Au at b=0.
- The longitudinal pressure exceeds the transverse pressure until approximately 5 fm/c, as shown by UrQMD results averaged over a cylindrical volume of transverse radius 6 fm and length 2 fm.
- The ratio of total nucleonic fluid pressure to equilibrium pressure reaches unity at about 5 fm/c in the three-fluid model.

## Context
This work extends earlier one-fluid hydrodynamic models (e.g., Yaris) that assumed instantaneous thermalization and predicted maximum energy deposition and possible QGP formation at AGS energies. The three-fluid approach follows prior work by Dumitru and collaborators (1990s) and Satarov (1990s) on multi-fluid dynamics, and the UrQMD comparison follows the cascade model of Bass (1990s). The results enable more realistic modeling of early-stage dynamics and show that directed nucleon flow is considerably lower than in instant-equilibration models, even without a first-order phase transition.
