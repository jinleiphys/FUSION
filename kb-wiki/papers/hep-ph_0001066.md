# Hadronic Physics

> I review the novel results and developments presented at the Third Workshop on Physics and Detectors for DA$Φ$NE that deal with hadronic physics. Topics discussed include: the scalar quark condensate, kaon decays, the sector of scalar and vector mesons, kaon-nucleon scattering, pion- and kaon-nucleon sigma terms, and strange nuclear physics.

# Hadronic Physics

## Key claim

This paper reviews results from the Third Workshop on Physics and Detectors for DAΦNE, arguing that nonperturbative methods such as chiral perturbation theory combined with dispersion relations can determine fundamental QCD parameters like the scalar quark condensate and light quark mass ratios, but that the actual value of the condensate remains uncertain and could be significantly smaller than the standard value of approximately (-230 MeV)^3, which would alter predictions for pion-pion scattering lengths by up to 30%.

## Method

The author surveys conference presentations on hadronic physics at energies around 1 GeV and below, covering chiral perturbation theory analyses of the scalar quark condensate, kaon decay data from BNL E865 and SERPUKHOV, dispersion-relation extractions of the pion-nucleon sigma term from scattering data, coupled-channel SU(3) chiral Lagrangian calculations for kaon-nucleon scattering, and experimental results on hypernuclear decays and kaonic atoms from KEK, VEPP-2M, and other facilities.

## Key numbers

- The standard scalar quark condensate value is approximately (-230 MeV)^3, corresponding to B approximately 1.4 GeV; a small-B scenario could allow B as low as F_pi approximately 100 MeV
- The pion-nucleon sigma term sigma(0) is approximately (45 +/- 10) MeV, translating to y = 2<p|bar s s|p> / <p|bar u u + bar d d|p> approximately 0.2 +/- 0.1
- The VPI/GW group's alternative analysis gives Sigma approximately 90 +/- 8 MeV, which would imply sigma(0) approximately 200 MeV if their amplitudes are used
- The omega-prime meson from VEPP-2M has mass M_omega' = (1170 +/- 10) MeV with width Gamma_omega' = (197 +/- 15) MeV
- The sigma meson from D+ decay analysis has M_sigma = 486 MeV and Gamma_sigma = 351 MeV
- The DIRAC experiment aims to measure the pionium lifetime to 10%, giving the scattering length difference |a_0^0 - a_0^2|^2 to 5%
- The DEAR experiment aims to determine kaonic hydrogen strong interaction shift to 1% and width to 3%

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral perturbation theory for meson-meson and meson-baryon systems developed by Weinberg (1979), Gasser (1980s), and collaborators, and addresses the ongoing debate about the size of the scalar quark condensate initiated by Jan (1990s). The results enable improved determinations of the pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon sigma terms, which are proton matrix elements of the explicit chiral symmetry breaking part of the QCD Hamiltonian, and support the interpretation of scalar mesons as non-simple q\bar q states. The paper also discusses how hypernuclear studies at DAΦNE, KEK, and BNL can probe the fundamental hyperon-nucleon interaction and weak non-mesonic decay mechanisms.
