# Studies on photo- and electro-productions of $Λ(1405)$ via $γ^{(*)} p\to K^{*+}π^0Σ^0$

> We study the photo- and electro-productions of the vector kaon off the proton, i.e., $γ^{(*)}p\to K^{*+}π^0Σ^0$, and investigate the line shape of the $π^0Σ^0$ invariant mass in an effective Lagrangian approach with the inclusion of a $K^*NΛ^*$ interaction. Relevant electromagnetic form factors for the neutral hyperons and charged strange mesons are constructed by considering experimental and theoretical information. We find that the $Λ^*$ peak is clearly observed for the photo- and electro-productions with the finite $K^*NΛ^*$ interaction, whereas the clear peak signals survive only for the electro-production, when we ignore the interaction. These different behaviors can be understood by different $Q^2$ dependences in the $K^*$ electromagnetic and $K^*\toγK$ transition form factors. We suggest a photon-polarization asymmetry $Σ$ to extract information of the $K^*NΛ^*$ interaction. It turns out that $Σ$ near the $Λ^*$ peak region becomes negative with a finite $K^*NΛ^*$ interaction while positive without it for $Q^2 = 0$, due to the different naturalities of $K$ and $K^*$ exchanges. For $Q^2\ne 0$, we observe more obvious signals in the peak region due to the additional contribution of the longitudinal virtual photon for $Λ^*$.

# Studies on photo- and electro-productions of Λ(1405) via γ⁽*⁾ p → K*⁺ π⁰ Σ⁰

## Key claim

The Λ(1405) peak is clearly observed in both photo- and electro-production when a finite K*NΛ* interaction is included, but when this interaction is ignored, a clear peak signal survives only for electro-production, not photo-production. This difference arises from the different Q² dependences of the K* electromagnetic form factor and the K* → γK transition form factor.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach at tree-level Born approximation, computing Feynman diagrams for the reaction γ⁽*⁾p → K*⁺π⁰Σ⁰ including s-channel proton-pole, u-channel hyperon (Λ, Σ, and the two poles of Λ(1405) labeled H and L), t-channel K* and K exchange, and Kroll-Rudermann and Weinberg-Tomozawa contact interactions. Electromagnetic form factors for neutral hyperons are parameterized using electric and magnetic mean-square charge radii from chiral unitary model calculations, while the K* electromagnetic form factor and K* → γK transition form factor are constructed from Dyson-Schwinger results and light-cone sum rules respectively.

## Key numbers

- Higher-pole (H) mass: 1430 MeV with width 30 MeV; lower-pole (L) mass: 1376 MeV with width 126 MeV (from chiral unitary model)
- Ratio of coupling products: |g_{K*NL} g_{πΣL}| ≈ 0.9 × |g_{K*NH} g_{πΣH}| for the K* production
- For K production, the corresponding ratio is |g_{KNL} g_{πΣL}| ≈ 1.6 × |g_{KNH} g_{πΣH}|
- K* → γK coupling constant: g_{γK*⁺K⁺} = 0.254 GeV⁻¹
- K* electromagnetic form factor mean-square charge radius: ⟨r²⟩_{K*} = 0.54 fm²
- Transition form factor dipole parameter: Λ_{K*→γK} = 1.1 GeV
- Strong cutoff parameter: Λ_strong = 0.9 GeV
- Center-of-mass energy: √s = 2.35 GeV
- For photo-production with K* exchange (g=1), the H peak increases by about 0.1 μb due to constructive interference between K and K* exchanges
- For electro-production at Q² = 2 GeV², the cross section is smaller than photo-production by a factor ~10⁻²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of γp → K⁺πΣ (Nam, 2017) which explained the two-bump structure in the πΣ invariant mass from CLAS electro-production data (Lu, 2013) via constructive interference of the two Λ(1405) poles. The present reaction avoids contamination from Σ*(1385) which does not couple to the neutral π⁰Σ⁰ channel. The K*NΛ* coupling strengths are estimated using the chiral unitary model approach of Khemchandani (2011) with Kroll-Rudermann interaction. The results provide predictions for future experiments at LEPS/SPring-8 and CLAS, and suggest the photon-polarization asymmetry Σ as an observable to determine the K*NΛ* interaction strength.
