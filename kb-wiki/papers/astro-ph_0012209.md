# Superfluidity in Neutron Star Matter

> Lecture notes of the workshop on "Physics of Neutron Star Interiors" held at the ECT*, June 2000. To be published in Lecture Notes in Physics, eds. D. Blaschke, N. K. Glendenning, and A. Sedrakian (Springer, 2001)

# Superfluidity in Neutron Star Matter

## Key claim
The BCS approximation for pairing gaps in neutron matter is unreliable even at very low density, because polarization corrections suppress the gap by a factor of approximately 0.45 independent of interaction strength; at higher densities the precise amount and density dependence of this suppression remain unknown and vary substantially between different theoretical approaches.

## Method
The authors solve the BCS gap equation for pure neutron matter using partial-wave expansions of realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Paris, Argonne V14, Nijmegen, Bonn) with a free single-particle spectrum, obtaining gaps in the 1S0 and 3P2-F2 partial waves. They then extend the analysis beyond BCS by incorporating first-order polarization diagrams in the low-density limit, and review the Babu-Brown induced interaction model for general polarization effects, which requires self-consistent determination of the particle-hole interaction F and transformation to the particle-particle channel.

## Key numbers
- Maximum 1S0 pairing gap in BCS approximation: about 3 MeV
- Maximum 3P2-F2 pairing gap in BCS approximation: of order 1 MeV
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18.8 fm
- Low-density asymptotic BCS gap formula: Δ(k_F) → (8/e²)(k_F²/2m) exp[π/(2k_F a_nn)]
- Polarization correction factor in low-density limit: Δ/Δ_0 → (4e)^{-1/3} ≈ 0.45
- Polarization coefficient c = (2/3π)(1 + 2 ln 2) ≈ 0.506
- Validity limit of low-density asymptotic result: k_F << 1/|a_nn| ≈ 0.05 fm^{-1}
- 3P2-F2 gap predictions diverge between potentials above k_F ≈ 2 fm^{-1}, corresponding to neutron density ≈ 0.3 fm^{-3}

## Context
This lecture note reviews and extends work on neutron superfluidity following Migdal's original proposal that neutron stars are macroscopic superfluid systems. The BCS-level calculations extend earlier systematic surveys by Tamagaki (1970) and Takatsuka (1993), and the correlated basis function approach of Chen et al. (1993). The beyond-BCS analysis builds on the Babu-Brown induced interaction model (1973) and the low-density analytical result of Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov (1961). The results enable more accurate predictions for neutron star cooling and post-glitch timing observations, but the authors emphasize that the large uncertainties in polarization effects at relevant densities remain an open problem requiring further theoretical development.
