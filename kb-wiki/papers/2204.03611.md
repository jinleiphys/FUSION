# Density functional approach to quark matter with confinement and color superconductivity

> We present a novel relativistic density-functional approach to modeling quark matter with a mechanism to mimic confinement. The quasiparticle treatment of quarks provides their suppression due to a large quark selfenergy already at the mean-field level. We demonstrate that our approach is equivalent to a chiral quark model with medium-dependent couplings. The dynamical restoration of the chiral symmetry is ensured by construction of the density functional. Supplemented with the vector repulsion and diquark pairing the model is applied to construct a hybrid quark-hadron EoS of cold compact-star matter. We study the connection of such a hybrid EoS with the stellar mass-radius relation and tidal deformability. The model results are compared to various observational constraints including the NICER radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817. The model is shown to be consistent with the constraints, still allowing for further improvement by adjusting the vector repulsion and diquark pairing couplings.

## Key claim
The paper presents a relativistic density-functional approach to quark matter that mimics confinement through a large scalar self-energy, and claims that selected parametrizations allow hybrid star sequences to reach masses in the range 2.5–2.67 \(M_\odot\), potentially interpreting the lighter object in the GW190814 merger as the heaviest neutron star known to date.

## Method
The authors construct a density functional for two-flavor quark matter with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and diquark interaction channels, using a mean-field approximation with a Gaussian formfactor regulator. The confining interaction is modeled via a density functional proportional to \((\langle \overline{q}q \rangle_0^2 - (\overline{q}q)^2 - (\overline{q}i\gamma_5\vec\tau q)^2)^{1/3}\), following the string-flip model. The model is normalized to vacuum pion mass and decay constant, then applied to construct hybrid quark-hadron equations of state for cold compact-star matter, with the hadronic phase described by a separate model and the transition treated via a Maxwell construction.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass \(M_\pi = 140\) MeV and decay constant \(F_\pi = 92\) MeV are used to fix model parameters.
- Vacuum chiral condensate per flavor \(\langle \overline{l}l \rangle_0^{1/3} = -267\) MeV.
- Scalar meson mass \(M_\sigma = 980\) MeV (identified with \(f_0(980)\)) is used to determine the scalar coupling.
- The asymptotic value of scalar and pseudoscalar couplings at high density/temperature is \(G_\infty = 6.6\) GeV\(^{-2}\), corresponding to \(G_\infty \Lambda^2 = 2.16\).
- Hybrid star sequences reach masses in the range 2.5–2.67 \(M_\odot\), relevant for the lighter object in GW190814.
- Model parameters: current quark mass \(m = 4.2\) MeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 573\) MeV, \(\alpha = 1.43\), \(D_0 \Lambda^{-2} = 1.39\).

## Context
This work extends the density-functional approach of Kaltenborn et al. (2017) by incorporating diquark pairing and color superconductivity, building on the string-flip model of Horowitz (1985) and Ropke (1986). It enables the construction of hybrid neutron-star equations of state that can be compared to observational constraints from NICER (radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620) and GW170817 (tidal deformability), and suggests that the GW190814 secondary object may be a heavy neutron star rather than a black hole.
