# The Chiral Phase Transition

> I review the current understanding of the chiral phase transition in QCD, with particular emphasis on recent results obtained in the instanton liquid model.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in QCD can be understood as a transition from a disordered instanton liquid to a correlated phase of instanton-antiinstanton molecules, but this picture is based on the assumption that the QCD partition function is dominated by instanton configurations, and the transition temperature found in this model (T_c ≃ 130 MeV) is lower than the generally accepted value.

## Method

The author reviews the chiral phase transition using the instanton liquid model, where chiral symmetry breaking is caused by delocalization of quark zero modes associated with instantons, and chiral restoration occurs when instantons and antiinstantons form polarized molecules. Numerical simulations of the instanton ensemble are performed, and both temporal (related to spectral functions in energy) and spatial (related to screening masses) correlation functions are computed at finite temperature. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations, chiral perturbation theory predictions, and QCD sum rule analyses.

## Key numbers

- The chiral phase transition occurs at T_c ≃ 150 MeV, while the pure gauge deconfinement transition occurs at T_c ≃ 260 MeV
- The latent heat associated with the pure gauge transition is approximately 1.5 GeV/fm³, while the chiral decondensation energy is Δε ≃ 250 MeV/fm³
- The vacuum pressure from the trace anomaly is p = 500 MeV/fm³
- For N_f=2 and B=500 MeV/fm³, the bag model estimate gives T_c = [(90B)/(37π²)]^{1/4} ≃ 180 MeV
- The instanton density from lattice cooling is (N/V) ≃ (1.4-1.6) fm⁻⁴ with typical size ρ ≃ 0.35 fm, compared to the instanton liquid parameters (N/V) = 1 fm⁻⁴, ρ = 1/3 fm
- In the instanton liquid model, the chiral phase transition occurs at T_c ≃ 130 MeV
- The hadron density near T ≃ 200 MeV is of order 1 fm⁻³, while if T_c is 150 MeV then n_had(T_c) ≃ 0.15 fm⁻³
- In pure gauge QCD, the particle density below T_c is on the order of n ≃ 0.005 fm⁻³

## Context

This review extends the instanton liquid model approach of Shuryak (1982) and the work on instanton-antiinstanton molecules by Ilgenfritz and Shuryak (1994) and Schaefer, Shuryak, and Verbaarschot (1995). It contrasts with lattice results discussed by Laermann and with the Pisarski-Yaffe (1981) prediction that instantons are significantly suppressed near T_c, which lattice calculations by Chu and Schramm (1995) and others show is incorrect. The work enables further study of the U(1)_A anomaly at finite temperature, which has implications for experimental signatures in heavy ion collisions such as the η/π ratio measured by WA80 (1994) and low mass dileptons seen by CERES (1995).
