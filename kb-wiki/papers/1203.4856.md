# Generalized seniority for the shell model with realistic interactions

> The generalized seniority scheme has long been proposed as a means of dramatically reducing the dimensionality of nuclear shell model calculations, when strong pairing correlations are present. However, systematic benchmark calculations, comparing results obtained in a model space truncated according to generalized seniority with those obtained in the full shell model space, are required to assess the viability of this scheme. Here, a detailed comparison is carried out, for semimagic nuclei taken in a full major shell and with realistic interactions. The even-mass and odd-mass Ca isotopes are treated in the generalized seniority scheme, for generalized seniority v<=3. Results for level energies, orbital occupations, and electromagnetic observables are compared with those obtained in the full shell model space.

## Key claim

The generalized seniority scheme with at most one broken pair ($v \leq 3$) reproduces the qualitative features of full shell model calculations for Ca isotopes across the $pf$ shell, but the quantitative accuracy depends on the interaction and the specific state, with deviations in excitation energies reaching up to approximately 1 MeV for some states.

## Method

The authors constructed a generalized seniority basis for neutrons in the $pf$-shell model space ($0f_{7/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$ orbitals) using collective $S$ pairs defined by variational minimization of the energy functional. They performed diagonalizations in the $v=2$ space for even-mass Ca isotopes and $v=1$ and $v=3$ spaces for odd-mass Ca isotopes, using the FPD6 and GXPF1 realistic interactions, and compared the results with full shell model calculations from the code NuShellX.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy residual $\Delta E$ for even-mass Ca isotopes: maximum $0.48$ MeV at mid-shell for FPD6 interaction, maximum $0.21$ MeV for GXPF1 interaction
- Root-mean-square deviation of $0^+_1$ ground state energy across $21 \leq N \leq 39$: $0.31$ MeV (FPD6), $0.13$ MeV (GXPF1)
- Root-mean-square deviation of $2^+_1$ excitation energy: $0.48$ MeV (FPD6), $0.25$ MeV (GXPF1)
- Root-mean-square deviation of $4^+_1$ excitation energy: $0.62$ MeV (FPD6), $0.34$ MeV (GXPF1)
- Maximum deviation of $J=2$ excitation energy: $0.41$ MeV for FPD6, $0.23$ MeV for GXPF1
- Maximum deviation of $J=4$ excitation energy: $0.58$ MeV for FPD6, $0.53$ MeV for GXPF1
- Largest deviation for first excited $J=0$ state: $\lesssim 1$ MeV
- $v=2$ model space dimensions in $pf$ shell: $4$ states for $J=0$, $8$ states for $J=2$, $6$ states for $J=4$
- $v=3$ model space dimensions: $27$ states for $J=7/2$, $28$ for $J=5/2$, $25$ for $J=3/2$, $12$ for $J=1/2$

## Context

This work provides the first systematic benchmark of generalized seniority truncation against full shell model calculations for a full major shell with realistic interactions, extending earlier limited comparisons for light Sn isotopes by Sandulescu (1997) and light Ca isotopes by Lei (2010). The results establish the viability of generalized seniority as a truncation scheme for semimagic nuclei and support its use as a basis for boson mapping to collective models such as the interacting boson model of Otsuka (1978) and Iachello (1987). The approach may enable tractable calculations for nuclei with both valence protons and neutrons, where full shell model calculations remain computationally prohibitive.
