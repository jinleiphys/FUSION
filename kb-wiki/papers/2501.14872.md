# Effects of sub-nucleonic fluctuations on the longitudinal structure of heavy-ion collisions

> Sub-nuclear fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions impact not only transverse long-range correlations of small systems, but also the creation of longitudinal structures, seen in particle detectors as longitudinal decorrelation observables. In this work, we study the emergence of long-range rapidity correlations in nuclear collisions based on the 3D resolved McDIPPER initial state model, and for the first time, connect it to experimental observables using the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics framework CLVisc. We include different sources of fluctuations at the nucleon and subnucleon level and study the effects of these additional fluctuation sources on the longitudinal structure of relevant observables, such as the flow decorrelations and directed flow.

## Key claim

Sub-nucleonic fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions significantly affect longitudinal observables such as flow decorrelations and directed flow, but the magnitude of these effects depends on the specific implementation of fluctuations (hotspot number, thickness fluctuation strength) and the centrality class; the paper demonstrates this for the first time by connecting a 3D resolved initial state model to a 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics framework.

## Method

The authors extended the McDIPPER initial state model (version 1.2) to include sub-nucleonic fluctuations by implementing a hotspot model where each nucleon's transverse density profile is a weighted superposition of \(N_q=3\) Gaussian hotspots with positions sampled from a Gaussian distribution and thickness fluctuations sampled from a log-normal distribution with width \(\sigma\). They generated \(10^4\) minimum bias initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, then evolved them using the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics code CLVisc with Israel-Stewart-like equations for shear tensor, bulk pressure, and baryon diffusion, followed by particlization via the Cooper-Frye prescription at freezeout density \(\epsilon_\mathrm{frz}=0.266477\) GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Key numbers

- Five simulation sets were compared: smooth nucleons, nucleons with thickness fluctuations (\(\sigma=0.637\)), 3 hotspots, hotspots with thickness fluctuations (\(\sigma=0.637\)), and hotspots with stronger thickness fluctuations (\(\sigma=1.2\)).
- The phenomenological normalization factor \(K_g\) was tuned for each scenario to match charged particle multiplicity at mid-rapidity in 0-5% centrality: \(K_g=2.23\) (nucleon), \(K_g=2.34\) (nucleon fluctuations \(\sigma=0.637\)), \(K_g=2.61\) (hotspots), \(K_g=2.71\) (hotspots fluctuations \(\sigma=0.637\)), \(K_g=3.20\) (hotspots fluctuations \(\sigma=1.2\)).
- The hotspot width parameter was set to \(B_q=0.04\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) and the nucleon width to \(B_G=0.156\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
- The initialization time for hydrodynamics was \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm.
- The freezeout energy density was \(\epsilon_\mathrm{frz}=0.266477\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The baryon diffusion coefficient was set to \(C_B=0.4\).

## Context

This work extends the McDIPPER initial state model (Garcia-Montero, 2023) by incorporating sub-nucleonic fluctuations previously used in the IP-Glasma framework (Schenke, 2012) and the hotspot model (Mantysaari, 2016) for small systems and ultra-peripheral collisions. It connects these initial conditions to experimental observables for the first time using the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics code CLVisc (Wu, 2021; Pang, 2018), enabling studies of longitudinal decorrelation observables such as those measured by CMS (2015). The results contradict the assumption that nucleon-level fluctuations alone suffice for heavy-ion collisions, showing that sub-nucleonic fluctuations are necessary to reproduce the high-multiplicity tail in p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7\) TeV (ALICE, 2010) and that they significantly affect baryon stopping and longitudinal energy deposition.
