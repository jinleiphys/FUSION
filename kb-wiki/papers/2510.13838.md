# Efficiency correction of particle-averaged quantities

> We derive analytic formulas to reconstruct particle-averaged quantities from experimental results that suffer from the efficiency loss of particle measurements. These formulas are derived under the assumption that the probabilities of observing individual particles are independent. The formulas do not agree with the conventionally used intuitive formulas.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic formulas for reconstructing particle-averaged quantities and their higher-order correlations from experimental data affected by detector efficiency loss, under the assumption that detection probabilities of individual particles are mutually uncorrelated. These formulas do not agree with the conventionally used intuitive formulas that appear in the literature without derivation.

## Method

The authors introduce generating functions for the observed particle distributions after efficiency loss, relating them to the true underlying distributions through integrals over a parameter sigma. For the case of uniform efficiency r, they derive closed-form correction factors involving powers of alpha = (r-1)/r. For multiple or particle-dependent efficiencies, they express the correction as an integral over sigma of products of terms (sigma/r_b + alpha_b) raised to the observed multiplicities n_b, which can be evaluated using elementary symmetric polynomials of the quantities (r_j - 1).

## Key numbers

- The correction factor for the first-order average with uniform efficiency is (1 - alpha^n), where alpha = (r-1)/r and n is the observed multiplicity; the true average equals the expectation value of (q/n)(1 - alpha^n) over observed events with n not equal to 0.
- For the second-order correlation with uniform efficiency, the correction factor kappa_{2;n} = 1 - n*alpha^{n-1} + (n-1)*alpha^n, where alpha = (r-1)/r.
- For the third-order correlation with uniform efficiency, the correction factor kappa_{3;n} = 1 - n(n-1)/2 * alpha^{n-2} + n(n-2)*alpha^{n-1} - (n-1)(n-2)/2 * alpha^n.
- For r less than 1/2, the parameter |alpha| is greater than or equal to 1, which can cause the correction terms involving alpha^n to become divergent for large n with flipping signs.

## Context

This work addresses a gap in the efficiency correction literature: while corrections for sums of particle quantities (such as total momentum or charge) have been well established by Kitazawa (2011, 2012), Asakawa (2015), Nonaka (2016, 2017), and others, no analytic formulas existed for particle-averaged quantities of the form (1/N) sum xi_i. The conventional formulas used by ATLAS (2013, 2022) and STAR (2024) for flow anisotropy and mean pT analyses are shown to be incorrect. The derived formulas enable proper correction of event-by-event averages and their fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments, including mean transverse momentum and flow-anisotropy parameters, without requiring numerically intensive unfolding methods.
