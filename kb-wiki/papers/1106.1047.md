# Polarized electric dipole moment of well-deformed reflection asymmetric nuclei

> The expression for polarized electric dipole moment of well-deformed reflection asymmetric nuclei is obtained in the framework of liquid-drop model in the case of geometrically similar proton and neutron surfaces. The expression for polarized electric dipole moment consists of the first and second orders terms. It is shown that the second-order correction terms of the polarized electric dipole moment are important for well-deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper derives an expression for the polarized electric dipole moment (PEDM) of well-deformed reflection-asymmetric nuclei that includes both first-order (proportional to βℓβℓ+1) and second-order (proportional to βℓβℓ′βℓ″) terms in the multipole deformation parameters, showing that the second-order corrections are important for well-deformed nuclei where the first-order approximation alone is insufficient.

## Method

The author uses the liquid-drop model with the assumption of geometrically similar proton and neutron surfaces (proton and neutron radii have the same angular dependence). The PEDM is separated into volume and surface contributions, which are evaluated by solving the Poisson equation for the Coulomb potential and the density variation equation using perturbation theory up to second order in the deformation parameters β2, β3, β4, β5, and β6. The macroscopic PEDM is then expressed as the sum of six terms: first-order volume and surface contributions, second-order volume and surface contributions from the zeroth-order Coulomb potential, and second-order volume and surface contributions from the first-order Coulomb potential correction.

## Key numbers

* For the hyperdeformed state of 152Dy (β2=0.61, β3=0.1, β4=0.11, β5=0.05, β6=0), the macroscopic PEDM in first order is D1 macro = 0.46 e fm and in second order is Dmacro = 0.67 e fm.
* For 152Dy, the total PEDM including the microscopic shell-correction (Dshell = -0.34 e fm) is 0.12 e fm using first-order macroscopic part and 0.33 e fm using second-order macroscopic part.
* The droplet model parameters used are J = 32.5 MeV, Q = 29.4 MeV, and r0 = 1.16 fm.
* The first-order volume contribution for axial nuclei includes terms such as (9 β2 β3)/(56 √35) and (11 β3 β4)/(105 √7) in the expression for Dv1.

## Context

This work extends the original PEDM derivation by Strutinsky (1956) and Bohr and Mottelson, which only included first-order terms proportional to βℓβℓ+1, and addresses the numerical finding by Skalski that the first-order approximation strongly underestimates the PEDM for well-deformed nuclei. The new second-order expressions are intended for practical use in describing E1 transitions in well-deformed reflection-asymmetric nuclei, including fission fragments and hyperdeformed states, and can improve the macroscopic part of semi-microscopic approaches such as those by Butler and Nazarewicz.
