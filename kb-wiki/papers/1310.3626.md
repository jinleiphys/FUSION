# Lattice QCD at the physical point meets SU(2) chiral perturbation theory

> We perform a detailed, fully-correlated study of the chiral behavior of the pion mass and decay constant, based on 2+1 flavor lattice QCD simulations. These calculations are implemented using tree-level, O(a)-improved Wilson fermions, at four values of the lattice spacing down to 0.054 fm and all the way down to below the physical value of the pion mass. They allow a sharp comparison with the predictions of SU(2) chiral perturbation theory (ПҮPT) and a determination of some of its low energy constants. In particular, we systematically explore the range of applicability of NLO SU(2) ПҮPT in two different expansions: the first in quark mass (x-expansion), and the second in pion mass (Оҫ-expansion). We find that these expansions begin showing signs of failure around M_ПҖ=300 MeV for the typical percent-level precision of our N_f=2+1 lattice results. We further determine the LO low energy constants (LECs), F=88.0 \pm 1.3\pm 0.3 and B^\msbar(2 GeV)=2.58 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.02 GeV, and the related quark condensate, ОЈ^\msbar(2 GeV)=(271\pm 4\pm 1 MeV)^3, as well as the NLO ones, l_3=2.5 \pm 0.5 \pm 0.4 and l_4=3.8 \pm 0.4 \pm 0.2, with fully controlled uncertainties. We also explore the NNLO expansions and the values of NNLO LECs. In addition, we show that the lattice results favor the presence of chiral logarithms. We further demonstrate how the absence of lattice results with pion masses below 200 MeV can lead to misleading results and conclusions. Our calculations allow a fully controlled, ab initio determination of the pion decay constant with a total 1% error, which is in excellent agreement with experiment.

## Key claim
NLO SU(2) chiral perturbation theory begins showing signs of failure for pion masses around 300 MeV and breaks down completely around 450 MeV, when applied to lattice QCD results with approximately 1% precision; this conclusion holds for both the standard quark-mass expansion and the pion-mass expansion.

## Method
The authors performed 2+1 flavor lattice QCD simulations using tree-level O(a)-improved Wilson fermions at four lattice spacings down to 0.054 fm, with pion masses as low as 120 MeV. They carried out fully-correlated fits of the pion mass and decay constant to NLO and NNLO SU(2) chiral perturbation theory expressions in both the x-expansion (in quark mass) and the Оҫ-expansion (in squared pion mass), systematically varying the maximum pion mass included in the fits to determine the range of applicability.

## Context
This work extends previous N_f вүҘ 2+1 lattice studies by groups such as Allton (2008), Aoki (2008), Bazavov (2009), and Borsanyi (2012) by reaching pion masses below the physical point (down to 120 MeV) and systematically testing the range of applicability of SU(2) ПҮPT. The results enable a precise, ab initio determination of the pion decay constant and low-energy constants with fully controlled uncertainties, and demonstrate that lattice results without pion masses below 200 MeV can lead to misleading conclusions about the validity of chiral perturbation theory.
