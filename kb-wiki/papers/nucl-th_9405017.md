# Collective Flow from the Intranuclear Cascade Model

> The phenomenon of collective flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions is studied using the hadronic cascade model ARC. Direct comparison is made to data gathered at the Bevalac, for Au+Au at $p=1-2$ GeV/c. In contrast to the standard lore about the cascade model, collective flow is well described quantitatively without the need for explicit mean field terms to simulate the nuclear equation of state. Pion collective flow is in the opposite direction to nucleon flow as is that of anti-nucleons and other produced particles. Pion and nucleon flow are predicted at AGS energies also, where, in light of the higher baryon densities achieved, we speculate that equation of state effects may be observable.

## Key claim

The hadronic cascade model ARC, using unmodified free-space cross-sections and no mean field, quantitatively describes sideward collective flow in Au+Au collisions at Bevalac energies (1-2 GeV/c), contradicting the standard lore that pure cascade models produce little or no collective flow. The authors hedge that mean fields may still play a role at lower collision energies or in very soft collisions, and that equation of state effects may become observable at the higher baryon densities achieved at AGS energies.

## Method

The authors used the ARC (A Relativistic Cascade) model, a pure hadronic cascade that simulates ion-ion collisions via Monte Carlo methods using experimentally measured free-space hadron-hadron cross-sections. They calculated sideward flow following the Danielewicz-Odyniec method, defining a reaction plane from a weighted average of outgoing transverse momenta and projecting proton-like momenta into that plane. Two modifications to the standard cascade treatment were applied: preserving the two-body reaction plane for final-state momenta (rather than randomizing the azimuthal angle) and eliminating attractive orbits in favor of only repulsive orbits, consistent with the repulsive nature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction at these energies.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at beam momentum 1.7 GeV/c, preserving the final-state plane adds approximately 20% to the average flow, while eliminating attractive orbits produces an additional approximately 10%.

## Context

This work extends the ARC cascade model, previously successful at AGS energies (Pang, Schlagel, and Kahana, 1992), down to Bevalac energies, directly comparing to Plastic Ball data from Doss et al. (1986) and Kampert (1989). It contradicts the conclusion of earlier cascade studies such as Beavis et al. (1986) that pure cascades generate insufficient flow, and argues against the necessity of including mean field terms as in BUU-type models (Bertsch, DasGupta, and Kruse, 1984) or RQMD (Mattiello et al., 1989; Sorge et al., 1992). The work enables predictions for pion and nucleon flow at AGS energies (11.6 GeV/c) that can be tested by the upcoming E895 collaboration, and suggests that deviations from the pure cascade picture in very high multiplicity events might reveal equation of state effects.
