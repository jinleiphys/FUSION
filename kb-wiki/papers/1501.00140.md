# NLO Dispersion Laws for Slow-Moving Quarks in HTL QCD

> We determine the next-to-leading order dispersion laws for slow-moving quarks in hard-thermal-loop perturbation of high-temperature QCD where weak coupling is assumed. Real-time formalism is used. The next-to-leading order quark self-energy is written in terms of three and four HTL-dressed vertex functions. The hard thermal loops contributing to these vertex functions are calculated ab initio and expressed using the Feynman parametrization which allows the calculation of the solid-angle integrals involved. We use a prototype of the resulting integrals to indicate how finite results are obtained in the limit of vanishing regularizer.

## Key claim

The authors determine the next-to-leading order (NLO) dispersion relations (both real energy and damping rate) for slow-moving quarks in high-temperature QCD using hard-thermal-loop (HTL) perturbation theory, but the calculation is not fully completed analytically; instead, a prototype integral is used to indicate how finite results can be obtained in the limit of vanishing regularizer, and the work is mainly numerical.

## Method

The authors use the closed-time-path (CTP) formulation of the real-time formalism of finite-temperature quantum field theory to avoid analytic continuation from Matsubara frequencies. The NLO quark self-energy is expressed in terms of one-loop diagrams with fully HTL-dressed three-point and four-point vertex functions and propagators, all with soft momenta. The HTL-dressed vertex functions are rederived ab initio due to discrepancies in the literature, and a Feynman parametrization is introduced to evaluate the solid-angle integrals appearing in the hard thermal loops. The resulting multi-dimensional integrals are handled numerically, with a prototype integral used to demonstrate how a stable finite result is obtained down to \(10^{-8}\) in units of the quark thermal mass \(m_f\).

## Key numbers

- The lowest-order quark thermal mass is \(m_f = \sqrt{C_F/8}\,gT\) with \(C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c)\).
- The lowest-order quark energies for soft momentum \(\bar{p} \equiv p/m_f < 1\) are given by the series: \(\omega_\pm(p) = m_f \left(1 \pm \frac{1}{3}\bar{p} + \frac{1}{3}\bar{p}^2 \mp \frac{16}{135}\bar{p}^3 + \mathcal{O}(\bar{p}^4)\right)\).
- The gluon thermal mass to lowest order is \(m_g = \sqrt{(N_c + N_f/2)/6}\,gT\).
- The NLO pure-gluon plasma frequency in the long-wavelength limit (from prior work) is \(\omega_g(0) = \frac{\sqrt{N_c}}{3}gT\left(1 - 0.09\sqrt{N_c}g + \dots\right)\) for \(N_f = 0\).
- The NLO quark mass at zero momentum (from prior work) is \(\omega_q(0) = \frac{gT}{\sqrt{6}}\left(1 + \frac{1.87}{4\pi}g + \dots\right)\) for \(N_c = 3, N_f = 2\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of NLO damping rates for slow-moving quarks and gluons (Abada et al., several papers 2006-2013) to the real part of the dispersion relations, which is significantly more difficult. It builds on the determination of the NLO fermion mass at zero momentum by Carrington et al. (2007, 2008) and the NLO gluon plasma frequency by Schulz (1994). The use of the real-time formalism avoids analytic continuation issues encountered in earlier imaginary-time calculations. The results enable a complete NLO description of quark quasiparticle properties in the quark-gluon plasma, which is relevant for understanding transport and thermodynamic properties at high temperature.
