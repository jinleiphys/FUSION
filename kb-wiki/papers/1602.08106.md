# Equation of state for neutron stars with hyperons by the variational method

> We investigate the effects of the odd-state part of bare $ΛΛ$ interactions on the structure of neutron stars (NSs) by constructing equations of state (EOSs) for uniform nuclear matter containing $Λ$ and $Σ^-$ hyperons with use of the cluster variational method. The isoscalar part of the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential are employed as the interactions between nucleons, whereas, as the bare $ΛN$ and even-state $ΛΛ$ interactions, two-body central potentials that are determined so as to reproduce the experimental data on single- and double-$Λ$ hypernuclei are adopted. In addition, the $Σ^- N$ interaction is constructed so as to reproduce the empirical single-particle potential of $Σ^-$ in symmetric nuclear matter. Since the odd-state part of the $ΛΛ$ interaction is not known owing to lack of experimental data, we construct four EOSs of hyperonic nuclear matter, each with a different odd-state part of the $ΛΛ$ interaction. The EOS obtained for NS matter becomes stiffer as the odd-state $ΛΛ$ interaction becomes more repulsive, and correspondingly the maximum mass of NSs increases. It is interesting that the onset density of $Σ^-$ depends strongly on the repulsion of the odd-state $ΛΛ$ interaction. Furthermore, we take into account the three-baryon repulsive force to obtain results that are consistent with observational data on heavy NSs.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how uncertainty in the odd-state part of the bare ΛΛ interaction affects neutron star structure, finding that the equation of state becomes stiffer and the maximum neutron star mass increases as the odd-state ΛΛ interaction becomes more repulsive, but the results are conditional on the lack of experimental data to determine this interaction component.

## Method

The authors extended a cluster variational method previously developed for asymmetric nuclear matter to calculate energies of hyperonic nuclear matter containing Λ and Σ⁻ hyperons, using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and Urbana IX three-nucleon potential for nucleon interactions, with ΛN and even-state ΛΛ interactions fitted to hypernuclear data and a Σ⁻N potential tuned to reproduce the empirical single-particle potential of Σ⁻ in symmetric nuclear matter. Four different models (Types 1-4) for the unknown odd-state part of the ΛΛ interaction were constructed with monotonically increasing repulsion, and the resulting equations of state were used to compute neutron star mass-radius relations under charge neutrality and beta equilibrium at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The empirical saturation density reproduced by the nucleonic part of the calculation is n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, with saturation energy E₀ = -16.09 MeV, incompressibility K = 245 MeV, and symmetry energy E_sym = 30.0 MeV.
- The single-particle potential of Λ in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is U_Λ⁰ = -43 MeV, consistent with the empirical value.
- The single-particle potential of Σ⁻ in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is U_Σ⁻⁰ = +12 MeV, consistent with experimentally suggested values.
- The p-wave volumes J_p-wave for the four odd-state ΛΛ interaction models are -313, -100, +112, and +430 MeV fm⁵ for Types 1 through 4, respectively.
- The onset density of Σ⁻ with free (noninteracting) hyperons is approximately 0.39 fm⁻³, which is much lower than the onset densities obtained with interacting hyperons.

## Context

This work extends the cluster variational method previously developed by Togashi and Takano (2014) for asymmetric nuclear matter to include hyperons, building on the Fermi Hypernetted Chain calculations of Akmal et al. (1998) for nucleonic equations of state. The study addresses the well-known "hyperon puzzle" where hyperon mixing softens the equation of state and reduces maximum neutron star masses below observed values of approximately 2 solar masses, as reported by Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013). The results enable future inclusion of three-baryon repulsive forces to reconcile the calculated maximum masses with observational data on heavy neutron stars.
