# Small Quadrupole Deformation for the Dipole Bands in 112In

> High spin states in $^{112}$In were investigated using $^{100}$Mo($^{16}$O, p3n) reaction at 80 MeV. The excited level have been observed up to 5.6 MeV excitation energy and spin $\sim$ 20$\hbar$ with the level scheme showing three dipole bands. The polarization and lifetime measurements were carried out for the dipole bands. Tilted axis cranking model calculations were performed for different quasi-particle configurations of this doubly odd nucleus. Comparison of the calculations of the model with the B(M1) transition strengths of the positive and negative parity bands firmly established their configurations.

## Key claim
The positive-parity dipole band in \(^{112}\)In is established as a magnetic rotational (shears) band with a small quadrupole deformation of \(\epsilon_2 = 0.08\), contrary to the triaxial deformation predicted by earlier Relativistic Mean Field calculations (Meena 2006). The TAC calculations reproduce the measured B(M1) trend only when using this lower deformation rather than the global minimum at \(\epsilon_2 = 0.12\).

## Method
High-spin states in \(^{112}\)In were populated via the \(^{100}\)Mo(\(^{16}\)O, p3n) reaction at 80 MeV using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) of 18 Compton-suppressed clover detectors. Directional correlation of oriented states (DCO) ratios and integrated polarization direction correlation (IPDCO) analysis were performed to assign spins and parities. Lifetimes of excited states in bands A and C were measured using the Doppler-shift attenuation method (DSAM) with the LINESHAPE code, and the resulting B(M1) transition strengths were compared with tilted axis cranking (TAC) model calculations for specific quasiparticle configurations.

## Key numbers
- Excited levels observed up to 5.6 MeV excitation energy and spin \(\sim 20\hbar\).
- Lifetimes measured for band A: \(15^+\) state \(\tau = 0.83^{+0.04}_{-0.04}\) ps, \(16^+\) state \(\tau = 0.49^{+0.03}_{-0.03}\) ps, \(17^+\) state \(\tau = 0.22^{+0.02}_{-0.03}\) ps, \(18^+\) state \(\tau < 0.25\) ps.
- Corresponding B(M1) strengths for band A: \(15^+\) state \(3.28^{+0.14}_{-0.14}\) \(\mu_N^2\), \(16^+\) state \(1.89^{+0.11}_{-0.10}\) \(\mu_N^2\), \(17^+\) state \(1.52^{+0.20}_{-0.14}\) \(\mu_N^2\), \(18^+\) state \(>0.63\) \(\mu_N^2\).
- Lifetimes for band C: \(15^-\) state \(\tau = 0.72^{+0.25}_{-0.16}\) ps, \(16^-\) state \(\tau < 0.60\) ps; B(M1) for \(15^-\) state \(1.87^{+0.55}_{-0.48}\) \(\mu_N^2\), \(16^-\) state \(>1.97\) \(\mu_N^2\).
- TAC calculations used proton pairing gap \(\Delta_\pi = 0.99\) MeV and neutron pairing gap \(\Delta_\nu = 0.85\) MeV.
- Best-fit deformation for band A: \(\epsilon_2 = 0.08\), \(\gamma = 5^\circ\); for band C: \(\epsilon_2 = 0.13\), \(\gamma = 0^\circ\).

## Context
This work extends the study of magnetic rotational (shears) bands in odd-odd In isotopes, previously reported in \(^{108}\)In and \(^{110}\)In (Chiara 2001), to \(^{112}\)In. The positive-parity band A is assigned the same \(\pi g_{9/2}^{-1} \otimes \nu (d_{5/2}/g_{7/2})(h_{11/2})^2\) configuration as those lighter isotopes, but with a notably smaller deformation (\(\epsilon_2 = 0.08\) vs. typical values). The absence of a band crossing near \(\hbar\omega = 0.6\) MeV, seen in \(^{108,110}\)In, is explained by the increased neutron number (N=63) making the \(g_{7/2}\) orbitals unavailable near the Fermi surface. The results contradict the triaxial deformation predicted for \(^{112}\)In by Relativistic Mean Field calculations (Meena 2006), instead supporting a weak prolate shape. Future recoil distance measurements (RDM) for lower states of band C and measurement of crossover E2 transitions in band A are suggested to further test the shears mechanism.
