# Three-nucleon contact forces in the Jacobi partial-wave basis

> We construct the three-nucleon contact potentials in the Jacobi partial-wave basis. The potentials are built in the separable form as the products of the antisymmetrized three-nucleon states in which the nucleons are arbitrarily close to each other. We compile the three-nucleon contact potentials up to $\mathcal{O}(Q^2)$. These contact potentials are by construction independent operators under exchanges of any nucleon pairs.

## Key claim

The paper constructs three-nucleon contact potentials in the Jacobi partial-wave basis up to order Q², but the method relies on solving eigenvector equations for the cyclic permutation operator P₁₂₃ in finite-dimensional subspaces, meaning the resulting operators are only independent under the specific constraint that the zero-range states are expanded in channels with definite total orbital angular momentum L, total spin S, and total isospin T.

## Method

The authors construct antisymmetrized zero-range three-nucleon states in the Jacobi partial-wave basis using the uncoupled LS scheme, then impose permutation invariance under P₁₂₃ by solving eigenvalue equations for the cyclic permutation operator in finite-dimensional subspaces classified by ν = l + λ (the sum of pair and spectator orbital angular momenta). The contact potentials are built as outer products of these permutation-invariant states, with the momentum-space wave functions taking the form ψ_α p^l q^λ p^{2m} q^{2n} where the powers of momenta determine the EFT order. The method is first demonstrated on the two-nucleon sector before being extended to three nucleons, with the invariant subspaces tabulated for ν = 0, 1, 2 and for cases where m or n are nonzero.

## Key numbers

- The contact potentials are constructed up to O(Q²), where Q denotes generic external momenta
- For ν = l + λ = 0, the invariant subspace Γ₁ has dimension 2 (l=0, λ=0, s=0 and l=0, λ=0, s=1) with L=0, S=1/2, T=1/2
- For ν = 1, the subspace Γ₂ has dimension 4 (four combinations of l=0,1 and λ=1,0 with s=0,1) with L=1, S=1/2, T=1/2
- Nine distinct invariant subspaces (Γ₁ through Γ₉) are tabulated for m=n=0, with dimensions ranging from 1 (Γ₇) to 6 (Γ₈)
- Five additional subspaces (Γ₁₀ through Γ₁₅) are listed for cases with m>0 or n>0, including Γ₁₃ with dimension 2 (l=1, λ=1, s=0 and s=1) for L=0, S=1/2, T=1/2

## Context

This work extends the approach of constructing three-nucleon contact interactions from Lagrangian terms and Fierz transformations used by Girlanda (2011, 2020), Witala (2022), and Nasoni (2023), but introduces a novel method based on separable outer products of antisymmetrized zero-range states in the Jacobi partial-wave basis. The results enable systematic construction of three-nucleon contact potentials up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, complementing the pion-exchange three-nucleon forces derived by van Kolck (1994), Epelbaum (2002), Bernard (2007, 2011), Krebs (2012, 2013), and providing a framework that can be extended to higher orders and applied in few-body calculations using the Jacobi partial-wave basis.
