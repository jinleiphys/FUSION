# Geometric scaling in ultrahigh energy neutrinos and nonlinear perturbative QCD

> It is shown that in ultrahigh energy inelastic neutrino-nucleon(nucleus) scattering the cross sections for the boson-hadron(nucleus) reactions should exhibit geometric scaling on the single variable tau_A =Q2/Q2_{sat,A}. The dependence on energy and atomic number of the charged/neutral current cross sections are encoded in the saturation momentum Q_{sat,A}. This fact allows an analytical computation of the neutrino scattering on nucleon/nucleus at high energies, providing a theoretical parameterization based on the scaling property.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that ultrahigh energy neutrino-nucleon and neutrino-nucleus cross sections for charged and neutral current interactions should exhibit geometric scaling in the single variable tau_A = Q^2/Q^2_sat,A, provided the scaling property extends from the lepton-hadron case to the electroweak boson-hadron case via the color dipole formalism. The claim is conditional on the validity of the geometric scaling window extending to virtualities up to Q^2 ~ Q^4_sat/Lambda^2_QCD, which is enhanced by a factor of 10-100 for nuclear targets.

## Method
The author uses the color dipole formalism to relate the electroweak boson-nucleus cross sections to the photon-nucleus cross section through coupling constant ratios, then applies the known geometric scaling property of the gamma*A cross section to compute the W^+- and Z^0-nucleus cross sections as functions of the scaling variable tau_A. The charged and neutral current structure functions are obtained from these boson-nucleus cross sections, and the total neutrino-nucleus cross sections are computed by integrating over the available phase space using the scaling function parameterized from DESY-HERA data on the proton structure function F_2.

## Key numbers
- Saturation momentum scaling: Q_sat^2(x) = (x_0/x)^lambda GeV^2 with x_0 = 3.04e-4, lambda = 0.288, and m_f = 0.14 GeV
- Nuclear saturation scale exponent: delta = 0.79 +/- 0.02, giving Q_sat,A^2 proportional to A^(4/9)
- Proton radius squared from fit: pi R_p^2 = 1.55 +/- 0.02 fm^2
- Scaling function parameters: a = 1.868, b = 0.746, bar_sigma_0 = 40.56 microbarns
- Ratio of exact to approximate cross section: R_cor = 0.82 for E_nu >= 10^8 GeV
- Power-like rise exponent: omega_scal = b*lambda = 0.2, giving cross section proportional to E_nu^0.227
- Numerical total cross section: sigma_nu,bar_nu^tot = 1.48e-34 * A^alpha * (E_nu/GeV)^0.227 cm^2
- Nuclear dependence exponent alpha = b/delta approximately 1, giving approximately linear A dependence

## Context
This work extends the geometric scaling property observed in DESY-HERA ep data (Stasto, Golec-Biernat, Kwiecinski, 2000) and electron-nuclei processes (Freund, 2002) to ultrahigh energy neutrino scattering on nucleons and nuclei. It builds on the color dipole framework (Nikolaev, Zakharov, 1991) and the traveling wave solutions of nonlinear QCD evolution equations (Munier, Peschanski, 2003). The results enable analytical parameterizations for neutrino cross sections at energies above 10^8 GeV, which are relevant for neutrino observatories detecting ultrahigh energy neutrinos through air showers. The predicted cross section is a factor of 2 smaller than both NLO DGLAP and unified BFKL-DGLAP approaches at energies above 10^12 GeV, with implications for event rates in neutrino telescopes.
