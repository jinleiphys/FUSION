# Low-energy M1 excitations in $^{208}$Pb and the spin channel of the Skyrme energy-density functional

> We investigate the spin dependent part of the Skyrme energy-density functional, in particular its impact on the residual particle-hole interaction in self-consistent calculations of excitations. Test cases are the low-energy M1 excitations in $^{208}$Pb treated within the self-consistent random-phase approximation based on the Skyrme energy-density functional. We investigate different parametrizations of the functionals to find out which parameters of the functional have strongest correlations with M1 properties. We explore a simple method of the modification of the spin-related parameters which delivers a better description of M1 excitations while basically maintaining the good description of ground state properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the spin-dependent parameters of the Skyrme energy-density functional, particularly those governing the spin-spin residual interaction, can be adjusted to simultaneously describe the low-energy M1 excitations in 208Pb without spoiling the good description of ground state properties, but only if the spin-orbit splitting is also properly tuned.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations for M1 excitations in 208Pb using 30 different Skyrme energy-density functional parametrizations. They systematically varied the spin-related constants (C_T^s, C_T^{s,alpha}, C_T^{Delta s}) and the spin-orbit coupling strength to study correlations with M1 properties, and developed a modified parametrization (SV-bas_mx) by tuning these parameters while preserving ground-state quality.

## Key numbers

- The experimental low-energy M1 state in 208Pb lies at E_1 = 5.84 MeV with integrated B_1(M1) strength; the upper M1 resonance is centered at E_2 = 7.39 MeV in the interval 6.6-8.1 MeV
- The neutron spin-orbit doublet energy difference epsilon^nu_ph (1i_11/2 - 1i_13/2) and proton difference epsilon^pi_ph (1h_9/2 - 1h_11/2) are the key single-particle energies driving M1 properties
- The RPA energy-weighted sum rule for isoscalar EL excitations in 208Pb is fulfilled within 0.1% for L <= 8 with the chosen numerical parameters (box radius 18 fm, particle energy cutoff 100 MeV)
- The effective M1 operator uses renormalization constants xi_s = 0.1 and xi_l = -0.03 to simulate quenching of M1 strength

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent RPA studies of M1 excitations in 208Pb by Cao (2009), Vesely (2009), Nesterenko (2010), and Wen (2014), which failed to simultaneously describe both the lower and upper M1 modes. It is guided by the large body of experience from the Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) by Migdal (1967) and subsequent work by Speth (1991) and Borzov (1984), where the Landau-Migdal parameters g and g' for the spin-spin channel were successfully tuned. The paper enables future beyond-RPA treatments including phonon coupling, which the authors previously attempted in Tselyaev (2017, 2018) but found ineffective until the RPA-level problem is solved.
