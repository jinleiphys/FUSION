# The Likelihood of DCC Formation

> We estimate the probability that a disoriented chiral condensate forms during the spherical expansion of a hot medium described by the linear sigma model.

## Key claim

The probability that a disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) forms during spherical expansion of hot matter is estimated to be small, with a conditional probability of roughly 4 × 10⁻³ for an observable signal in a central Pb-Pb collision at CERN SPS, assuming the initial plasma droplet was formed.

## Method

The authors combine the mean-field (Hartree-type) approximation of the linear sigma model, as developed by Lampert et al., with a Monte Carlo sampling strategy for the initial values of the classical mean-field and its time derivative. The initial conditions are sampled from a Gaussian probability distribution derived from the assumption of local thermal equilibrium in a spherical droplet of radius R, with variances computed analytically for R larger than the correlation length. The time evolution is solved in hyperbolic coordinates (τ, η) to model spherical expansion at the speed of light, and the amplification factor A_s for the softest (s=0⁺) mode is computed from the Bogoliubov transformation between initial and final particle operators.

## Key numbers

- The correlation length λ = 1.17 fm at T = 200 MeV and λ = 0.68 fm at T = 400 MeV.
- The amplification factor A is calculated at τ_f = 10 fm, where the system is in stationary regime.
- For T = 200 MeV and R = λ, the probability distribution P(A) is fitted as P(A) = 0.805 / (1 + 0.27 A)^(3.21).
- For T = 400 MeV and R = λ, the fit is P(A) = 0.213 / (1 + 0.032 A)^(6.959).
- For A > 30, the average constant c in the invariant spectrum E dn/d³p = c A is roughly c = 5 GeV⁻² for R = λ at both T = 200 MeV and T = 400 MeV.
- In a central Pb-Pb collision at CERN SPS, the invariant momentum space density at very small transverse momentum is roughly 1900 GeV⁻², with expected fluctuation of order 75 GeV⁻².
- The conditional probability for A > 45 (signal more than three times the fluctuation) is roughly 4 × 10⁻³ for R = λ.
- The overall probability estimate for an observable DCC signal in a central Pb-Pb collision at CERN SPS is at best of order 10⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Lampert et al., who studied the time evolution of the chiral phase transition during spherical expansion, by adding a systematic method to sample initial conditions from local thermal equilibrium rather than assigning them ad hoc. The results are qualitatively similar to the analytical result obtained by Bjorken et al. using a 1+1 dimensional toy model, except that here P(A) has a power-law tail at large A rather than an exponential fall-off. The estimate provides a crude probability for DCC formation that experimenters designing DCC search strategies should take into account, and it highlights the need for modeling the collision environment to obtain absolute multiplicities.
