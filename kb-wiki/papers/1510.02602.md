# Reexamining closed-form formulae for inclusive breakup: Application to deuteron and $^6$Li induced reactions

> The problem of the calculation of inclusive breakup cross sections in nuclear reactions is reexamined. For that purpose, the post-form theory proposed by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 431 (1985) is revisited, and an alternative derivation of the non-elastic breakup part of the inclusive breakup is presented, making use of the coupled-channels optical theorem. Using the DWBA version of this model, several applications to deuteron and $^6$Li reactions are presented and compared with available data. The validity of the zero-range approximation of the DWBA formula is also investigated by comparing zero-range with full finite-range calculations.

## Key claim

The paper reexamines the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) post-form theory for inclusive breakup cross sections, providing an alternative derivation of the non-elastic breakup (NEB) part using the coupled-channels optical theorem, and tests the DWBA version of this model for deuteron and ⁶Li induced reactions, including the first application to ⁶Li scattering.

## Method

The authors revisit the IAV model and derive a new expression for the NEB component of inclusive breakup using the coupled-channels optical theorem, within a post-form DWBA framework. They implement both zero-range and full finite-range versions of the DWBA formula, and apply the model to several deuteron-induced reactions and, for the first time, to ⁶Li-induced reactions, comparing the calculated cross sections with available experimental data. The validity of the zero-range approximation is assessed by comparing zero-range with finite-range calculations.

## Key numbers

- For deuteron-induced reactions, the paper presents comparisons with experimental data at various energies, though specific numerical cross section values are not given in the truncated text.
- For ⁶Li-induced reactions, the method is applied for the first time, with comparisons to available data, but explicit numerical results are not provided in the truncated text.
- The zero-range approximation is investigated by comparing zero-range with full finite-range calculations, but no specific numerical differences are quoted in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the IAV formalism (Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent, 1985) and the earlier post-form DWBA model of Austern and Vincent (1981), which had fallen into disuse despite encouraging early comparisons with data. The paper is motivated by recent renewed interest in inclusive breakup calculations, as evidenced by two contemporary works: Carlson et al. (2015), who used the zero-range post-form IAV model for deuteron reactions, and Potel et al. (2015), who used the finite-range prior-form version. The present work enables future applications to incomplete fusion calculations for weakly-bound projectiles and to surrogate reaction methods for determining neutron-induced cross sections on short-lived nuclei.
