# Bohr Hamiltonian with Davidson potential for triaxial nuclei

> A solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian appropriate for triaxial shapes, involving a Davidson potential in beta and a steep harmonic oscillator in gamma, centered around gamma=30 degrees, is developed. Analytical expressions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates ranging from a triaxial vibrator to the rigid triaxial rotator are obtained and compared to experiment. Using a variational procedure it is pointed out that the Z(5) solution, in which an infinite square well potential in beta is used, corresponds to the critical point of the shape phase transition from a triaxial vibrator to the rigid triaxial rotator.

# Bohr Hamiltonian with Davidson potential for triaxial nuclei

## Key claim
The Z(5)-D model, which replaces the infinite square well potential in β of the Z(5) solution with a Davidson potential, can describe the entire range from a triaxial vibrator to a rigid triaxial rotator, but the Z(5) critical point solution is **not** a special case of Z(5)-D obtained for any specific parameter value, the Davidson potential is not flat enough to reproduce the critical point exactly.

## Method
The authors solved the Bohr Hamiltonian for triaxial shapes by assuming a separable potential: a Davidson potential in the β degree of freedom (u(β) = β² + β₀⁴/β²) and a steep harmonic oscillator in the γ degree of freedom centered at γ = 30°. The β-equation was solved exactly using Laguerre polynomials, while the γ-equation reduced to a simple harmonic oscillator. A variational procedure was applied to the ground-state band energy ratios E(L)/E(2) to locate the maximum rate of change as a function of β₀, identifying the critical point.

## Key numbers
- Ground state band R₄/₂ = E(4)/E(2) ratios range from 2.150 (triaxial vibrator, β₀=0) to 2.667 (rigid triaxial rotator, β₀→∞)
- γ₁ bandhead (normalized to 2₁⁺) ranges from 1.734 (β₀=0) to 2.000 (β₀→∞)
- β₁ bandhead (normalized to 2₁⁺) is above 2.528
- For β₀=0 (Z(5)-β² model), the ground state band energies are parameter-free up to overall scale factors
- The variational procedure showed that the Z(5) ground state band agrees very well with the band determined at the critical point β₀,m for each angular momentum L

## Context
This work extends the Z(5) solution of Bonatsos et al. (Z5) by replacing the infinite square well in β with a Davidson potential, analogous to earlier extensions of E(5) (Elliott, Fort) and X(5) (varPLB, varPRC) with Davidson potentials. The variational procedure, previously applied to E(5) and X(5) frameworks (varPLB, varPRC), demonstrates that Z(5) corresponds to the critical point of a shape phase transition from a triaxial vibrator to the rigid triaxial rotator (DavFil, DavRos). The model enables analytical comparison to experimental data for nuclei such as ¹²⁸–¹³²Xe, though the predicted strong triaxial γ-rigid staggering is not observed in these isotopes, which instead exhibit γ-soft staggering.
