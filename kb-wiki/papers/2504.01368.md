# Proton cumulants from hydrodynamics in light of new STAR data

> New measurements of proton number cumulants from the Beam Energy Scan Phase II (BES-II) program at RHIC by the STAR Collaboration provide unprecedented precision and insights into the properties of strongly interacting matter. This report discusses the measurements in the context of predictions from hydrodynamics, emphasizing the enhanced sensitivity of factorial cumulants and their implications for the search for the QCD critical point. The experimental data shows enhancement of second-order factorial cumulants and suppression of third-order factorial cumulants relative to the non-critical baseline at $7.7 < \sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \lesssim 10$ GeV. We discuss implications of this observation for the possible location of the critical point in the QCD phase diagram and opportunities for future measurements of acceptance dependence of factorial cumulants.

## Key claim

The new STAR BES-II data show enhancement of second-order factorial cumulants and suppression of third-order factorial cumulants relative to the non-critical hydrodynamics baseline at collision energies 7.7 < √sNN ≲ 10 GeV, which could be interpreted as a critical point signal but may also arise from non-critical effects such as volume fluctuations or baryon stopping.

## Method

The authors compare new STAR BES-II measurements of proton number factorial cumulants with a non-critical baseline from (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamics (MUSIC) simulations of Au-Au collisions, which incorporate global baryon number conservation via the subensemble acceptance method and baryon repulsion via excluded volume effects with parameter b = 1 fm³. They analyze deviations of the measured factorial cumulants ω₂, ω₃, and ω₄ from this baseline across collision energies √sNN = 7.7–27 GeV, and use a van der Waals model for nuclear matter to map the sign structure of factorial cumulants onto the QCD phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The non-critical baseline describes data quantitatively at √sNN ≳ 20 GeV, strongly disfavoring a critical point at μB ≲ 200 MeV.
- At 7.7 < √sNN ≲ 10 GeV, the deviation ω₂ − ω₂^baseline > 0 (enhancement) and ω₃ − ω₃^baseline ≤ 0 (suppression) relative to the baseline.
- The allowed region for freeze-out of fluctuations consistent with the data (Ĉ₂ > 0, Ĉ₃ < 0) corresponds to the QGP side of the transition near the crossover region.
- Theoretical estimates place the critical point at Tc ∼ 100–120 MeV and μB,c ∼ 400–650 MeV, accessible at √sNN ≲ 7.7 GeV.
- The nuclear liquid-gas critical point is estimated at Tc ∼ 20 MeV and μB ∼ 900 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamics-based baseline developed by Vovchenko (2021) for describing proton cumulants at RHIC-BES energies, which successfully described BES-I data at √sNN ≳ 20 GeV. The new BES-II data confirm trends seen in BES-I for second and third-order cumulant ratios and provide new insights into higher-order cumulants. The analysis enables future studies of acceptance dependence of factorial cumulants to distinguish critical fluctuations from non-critical effects, and motivates measurements at lower energies from the RHIC fixed target program and the CBM experiment at FAIR.
