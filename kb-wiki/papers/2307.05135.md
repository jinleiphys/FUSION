# Interplay of effects of neutron skins in coordinate space and proton skins in momentum space on hard photons emission in heavy-ion collisions around Fermi Energy

> Within an isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, we investigate the hard photons emission from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung in reaction system of $^{208}Pb+^{208}Pb$ around Fermi energy. Effects of neutron skins in coordinate ($r$) space and proton skins in momentum ($k$) space on the time evolution, the angular distribution, and the transverse momentum spectra of hard photons with different energies are studied. It is shown that the emission of direct hard photons is sensitive to the neutron skin, which has larger effects for more energetic hard photons. Meanwhile, we find that the proton skins have an important influence on the emission of direct hard photons, and its effect is actually even larger than that of neutron skins. It needs to take the effect of proton skins into account when we determine the size of neutron skins by comparing transport mode predictions of hard photons with the corresponding experiment measurements.

## Key claim
The emission of direct hard photons from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung in peripheral heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to both neutron skins in coordinate space and proton skins in momentum space, with the proton-skin effect being larger than the neutron-skin effect; therefore, proton skins must be accounted for when extracting neutron skin thickness from hard photon measurements.

## Method
The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with an extended Thomas-Fermi approximation for the nucleon kinetic-energy density that incorporates short-range correlations (SRCs) and a high-momentum tail (HMT) in the nucleon momentum distribution. They simulated peripheral collisions of 208Pb+208Pb at a beam energy of 45 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter b=11 fm, varying the neutron skin thickness (S=0.1 fm and S=0.3 fm) and including or excluding the proton skin in momentum space via the surface term in the kinetic-energy density. Hard photon production was calculated from the p+n→p+n+γ elementary cross section based on a neutral scalar σ meson exchange model.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 45 MeV/nucleon for 208Pb+208Pb peripheral collisions with impact parameter b=11 fm.
- Neutron skin thicknesses studied: S=0.1 fm and S=0.3 fm.
- Hard photon energies examined: 100, 150, 200, and 250 MeV.
- The high-momentum tail parameters used: xHMT_SNM=28%, xHMT_PNM=4%, with C0=0.161, C1=-0.25, φ0=2.38, φ1=-0.56.
- For isospin asymmetry δ=0.21, the enhancement factors in the bulk kinetic-energy density were Φp=2.09 and Φn=1.60.
- The elementary photon production probability fitted as pγ=2.1×10-6 (1-y2)α/y, where y=Eγ/Emax and α=0.7319-0.5898βi.

## Context
This work extends prior studies using hard photons as probes of neutron skin thickness (Wei 2015, Wang 2022) and builds on the IBUU04 transport model (Li 2004, 2005). It incorporates the coexistence of proton skins in momentum space and neutron skins in coordinate space predicted by Cai (2016) using an extended Thomas-Fermi model with SRC effects. The results indicate that future experiments using hard photons to extract neutron skin thickness must account for proton skin effects, and the transverse momentum spectrum of hard photons is identified as a particularly promising observable for investigating nuclear surface properties.
