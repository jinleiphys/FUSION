# QCD Phase Diagram for Large $N_f$ : Analysis from Contact Interaction Effective Potential

> In this paper, we discuss the impact of a higher number of light quark flavors, $N_f$, on the QCD phase diagram under extreme conditions. Our formalism is based on the Schwinger-Dyson equation, employing a specific symmetry-preserving vector-vector flavor-dressed contact interaction model of quarks in Landau gauge, utilizing the rainbow-Ladder truncation. We derive expressions for the dressed quark mass $M_f$ and effective potential $Ω^{f}$ at zero, at finite temperature $T$ and the quark chemical potential $μ$. The transition between chiral symmetry breaking and restoration is triggered by the effective potential of the contact interaction, whereas the confinement and deconfinement transition is approximated from the confinement length scale $\tildeτ_{ir}$. Our analysis reveals that at $(T = μ= 0)$, increasing $N_f$ leads to the restoration of chiral symmetry and the deconfinement of quarks when $N_f$ reaches its critical value, $N^{c}_{f} \approx 8$. At this critical value, In the chiral limit ($m_f = 0$), the global minimum of the effective potential occurs at the point where the dressed quark mass approaches zero ($M_f \rightarrow 0$). However, when a bare quark mass of $m_f = 7$ MeV is introduced, the global minimum shifts slightly to a nonzero value, approaching $M_f \rightarrow m_f$. At finite $T$ and $μ$, we illustrate the QCD phase diagram in the $(T^{χ,C}_{c} -μ)$ plane, for various numbers of light quark flavors, noting that both the critical temperature $T_c$ and the critical chemical potential $μ_c $ for chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement decrease as $ N_f $ increases. Moreover, the critical endpoint $(T_{EP}, μ_{EP})$ also shifts to lower values with increasing $N_f $. Our findings are consistent with other low-energy QCD approaches.

## Key claim
The paper claims that increasing the number of light quark flavors \(N_f\) drives the QCD phase diagram toward chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement, with a critical value \(N_f^c \approx 8\) at zero temperature and chemical potential. However, this critical value is derived from a specific symmetry-preserving contact interaction model with rainbow-ladder truncation, and the transition is only a crossover when a finite bare quark mass (\(m_f = 7\) MeV) is included, rather than a true second-order transition.

## Method
The authors employ a Schwinger-Dyson equation approach in Landau gauge, using a vector-vector flavor-dressed contact interaction model with rainbow-ladder truncation. They derive the dressed quark mass \(M_f\) and effective thermodynamic potential \(\Omega^f\) at zero temperature and chemical potential, then extend the formalism to finite temperature \(T\) and quark chemical potential \(\mu\) using Matsubara frequencies. The chiral transition is determined from the effective potential, while the confinement-deconfinement transition is approximated via the confinement length scale \(\tilde{\tau}_{ir}\), which diverges when chiral symmetry is restored.

## Key numbers
- Critical number of light quark flavors at \((T = \mu = 0)\): \(N_f^c \approx 8\) (in the chiral limit \(m_f = 0\), the global minimum of the effective potential occurs at \(M_f \rightarrow 0\)).
- With a bare quark mass \(m_f = 7\) MeV, at \(N_f^c \approx 8\), the global minimum shifts to \(M_f \rightarrow m_f\) (partial restoration).
- For \(N_f = 2\) at finite temperature and \(\mu = 0\), the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration is \(T_c \approx 207\) MeV.
- Model parameters: infrared cutoff \(\tau_{ir} = (240\ \text{MeV})^{-1}\), ultraviolet cutoff \(\tau_{uv} = (905\ \text{MeV})^{-1}\), \(\alpha_{ir} = 0.93\pi\), and gluon mass scale \(\mathcal{M}_g = 800\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior Schwinger-Dyson equation studies (e.g., Ahmad 2020, Ahmad 2022) and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model calculations (Ahmad 2022) that showed increasing \(N_f\) lowers the critical temperature and chemical potential for chiral restoration. It is consistent with lattice QCD simulations (e.g., LSD 2014, Hayakawa 2010) and other continuum approaches (e.g., Appelquist 1998, Binosi 2016) that predict a conformal window for \(N_f\) between the critical value and the loss of asymptotic freedom (\(N_f^{c,AF} = 16.5\) for \(N_c = 3\)). The results enable mapping the QCD phase diagram for multiple light flavors, which is relevant for understanding early-universe matter, neutron star interiors, and experimental searches at facilities like RHIC, FAIR, and NICA.
