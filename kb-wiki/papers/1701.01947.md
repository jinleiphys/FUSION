# Spin-dependent sum rules connecting real and virtual Compton scattering verified

> We present a detailed derivation of the two sum rules relating the spin polarizabilities measured in real, virtual, and doubly-virtual Compton scattering. For example, the polarizability $δ_{LT}$, accessed in inclusive electron scattering, is related to the spin polarizability $γ_{E1E1}$ and the slope of generalized polarizabilities $P^{(M1,M1)1}-P^{(L1,L1)1}$, measured in, respectively, the real and the virtual Compton scattering. We verify these sum rules in different variants of chiral perturbation theory, discuss their empirical verification for the proton, and prospect their use in studies of the nucleon spin structure.

## Key claim

The paper derives two new sum rules that connect spin polarizabilities measured in real Compton scattering (RCS), virtual Compton scattering (VCS), and doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS), and verifies them in chiral perturbation theory; the sum rules relate the slope of the first moment of the spin structure function \(g_1\) and the slope of the third moment of \(g_2\) to combinations of spin polarizabilities and generalized polarizability slopes, with the caveat that empirical verification for the proton remains partially prospective.

## Method

The authors start from the forward double-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) tensor, expressed in terms of four invariant amplitudes \(T_1, T_2, S_1, S_2\) as functions of photon virtuality \(Q^2\) and energy \(\nu\). They perform low-energy expansions (LEX) in \(\nu\) and \(Q\) for these amplitudes, relating the expansion coefficients to nucleon polarizabilities from RCS and to generalized polarizabilities (GPs) from VCS, specifically the slopes at \(Q^2=0\) of the spin GPs \(P^{(M1,M1)1}\) and \(P^{(L1,L1)1}\). Dispersion relations for the non-pole parts of the amplitudes are then matched to the LEX at \(\nu=0\) to derive sum rules connecting the \(Q^2\)-slopes of the spin structure function moments \(I_1(Q^2)\) and \(I_2^{(3)}(Q^2)\) to the polarizabilities and GP slopes. The sum rules are verified in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) and covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT).

## Key numbers

* The GDH sum rule at \(Q^2=0\) gives \(I_1(0) = -\kappa_N^2 / 4\), where \(\kappa_N\) is the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon.
* The new sum rule for the slope of the first moment of \(g_1\) is: \(I_1^\prime(0) = \frac{\kappa_N^2}{12} \langle r_2^2 \rangle + \frac{M^2}{2} \left\{ \frac{1}{\alpha_\mathrm{em}} \gamma_{E1 M2} - 3 M \left[ P^{\prime (M1,M1)1}(0) + P^{\prime (L1,L1)1}(0) \right] \right\}\), where \(\langle r_2^2 \rangle\) is the nucleon mean squared Pauli radius.
* The new sum rule for the slope of the third moment of \(g_2\) is: \(I_2^{(3)\prime}(0) = \frac{M^2}{2} \left\{ -\frac{1}{\alpha_\mathrm{em}} \left[ \gamma_0 + \gamma_{E1 E1} \right] + 3 M \left[ P^{\prime (M1,M1)1}(0) - P^{\prime (L1,L1)1}(0) \right] \right\}\).
* The forward spin polarizability \(\gamma_0\) is related to the four lowest-order spin polarizabilities as \(\gamma_0 = -(\gamma_{M1 E2} + \gamma_{E1 M2} + \gamma_{M1 M1} + \gamma_{E1 E1})\).
* The Baldin sum rule at \(Q^2=0\) gives \(\alpha_E + \beta_M = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int_{\nu_0}^\infty \frac{d\nu}{\nu^2} \sigma(\nu)\), where \(\sigma(\nu)\) is the total photoabsorption cross section.

## Context

This work extends the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule (Gerasimov 1965, Drell 1966) and the Baldin sum rule (Baldin) to finite photon virtuality by connecting VVCS observables with VCS generalized polarizabilities, building on the framework of Drechsel et al. (2002) and the recent relations proposed by Pascalutsa et al. (2014). The sum rules enable empirical extraction of the slopes of the spin generalized polarizabilities \(P^{(M1,M1)1}\) and \(P^{(L1,L1)1}\) from inclusive electron scattering data on spin structure functions, and provide cross-checks for dispersive evaluations and chiral perturbation theory calculations of nucleon spin structure.
