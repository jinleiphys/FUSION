# Charge-Independence Breaking in the Two-Pion-Exchange Nucleon-Nucleon Force

> Charge-independence breaking due to the pion-mass difference in the (chiral) two-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon force is investigated. A general argument based on symmetries is presented that relates the charge-symmetric part of that force to the proton-proton case. The static potential linear in that mass difference is worked out as an explicit example by means of Feynman diagrams, and this confirms the general argument.

## Key claim

The paper argues that charge-independence breaking (CIB) in the two-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon force due to the pion-mass difference can be derived from a general symmetry argument, and that to first order in the mass difference the effective pion mass to use in the isoscalar force or the T=0 force is the average pion mass, while charged-pion mass should be used for pp or nn cases and neutral-pion mass for the T=1 np case.

## Method

The authors present a general symmetry argument based on the isotensor structure of the charge-symmetry-breaking interaction, showing that the CIB potential takes the form V_II = T_20(1,2) ΔV^CIB. They then verify this argument by explicit Feynman diagram calculation of the static (leading-order chiral) two-pion-exchange potential, expanding the pion propagators to first order in the pion-mass difference δm_π about the average pion mass. The derivative of the isovector potential with respect to the average pion mass yields the CIB correction.

## Key numbers

* The pion-mass difference δm_π = m_π± - m_π0 produces an isospin-violating effect of order (δm_π/m_π) ~ 3% in the one-pion-exchange potential.
* The separate contributions to Δa = |a_np| - ½|a_pp + a_nn| from box, triangle, and football diagrams are [0.98, -0.31, -0.02] fm respectively, giving a total of 0.65 fm.
* The average pion mass is defined as m̄_π = (2/3)m_π± + (1/3)m_π0.
* The pion-decay constant f_π ≈ 92 MeV and the axial-vector coupling constant g_A ≈ 1.25 are used in the chiral Lagrangian.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of isospin violation in nuclear forces (van Kolck, 1998) to the specific case of two-pion-exchange CIB from pion-mass differences. The result provides the leading-order CIB correction that can be incorporated into partial-wave analyses such as those by the Nijmegen group (Stoks et al., 1994), who had already shown that the charge-independent TPEP improves fits to pp data. The calculation enables a re-analysis of np scattering data with proper treatment of CIB, and the numerical estimate of 0.65 fm for the scattering-length difference updates earlier calculations by Li and Machleidt (1998) and Ericson and Miller (1998).
