# Open and hidden charm in proton-nucleus and heavy-ion collisions

> We review the collectivity and the suppression pattern of charmed mesons - produced in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS (158 AGeV) and RHIC energies (21 ATeV) - in comparison to dynamical and thermal models. In particular, we examine the charmonium `melting' and the `comover dissociation' scenarios - implemented in a microscopic transport approach - in comparison to the available data from the SPS and RHIC. The analysis shows that the dynamics of c, c-bar quarks at RHIC are dominated by partonic or `pre-hadronic' interactions in the strongly coupled plasma stage. Both the `charmonium melting' and the hadronic `comover absorption and recreation model' are found, however, to be compatible with the experimental observation at SPS energies; the experimental ratio of Psi'/J/Psi versus centrality clearly favors the `hadronic comover' scenario. We find that the collective flow of charm in the purely hadronic Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) transport appears compatible with the data at SPS energies, but substantially underestimates the data at top RHIC energies. Thus, the large elliptic flow v2 of D-mesons and the low R_AA(p_T) of J/Psi seen experimentally have to be attributed to early interactions of non-hadronic degrees of freedom. Simultaneously, we observe that non-hadronic interactions are mandatory in order to describe the narrowing of the J/Psi rapidity distribution from pp to central Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy. We demonstrate additionally that the strong quenching of low-pT J/Psi's in central Au+Au collisions indicates that a large fraction of final J/Psi mesons is created by a coalescence mechanism close to the phase boundary. Throughout this review we, furthermore, provide predictions for charm observables from Au+Au collisions at FAIR energies of 25-35 AGeV.

## Key claim

The dynamics of charm quarks at RHIC energies are dominated by partonic or "pre-hadronic" interactions in the strongly coupled plasma stage, while at SPS energies both the "charmonium melting" and hadronic "comover absorption and recreation" models are compatible with data; however, the experimental ratio of Psi'/J/Psi versus centrality clearly favors the hadronic comover scenario.

## Method

The authors employ the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) transport approach to simulate charm production and propagation in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS (158 AGeV) and RHIC (21 ATeV) energies. They implement both the charmonium "melting" scenario and the "comover dissociation" scenario within this microscopic transport framework, using phenomenological parametrizations of elementary charm production cross sections from NN and pi N reactions fitted to experimental data. The space-time evolution of energy density is calculated from the energy-momentum tensor in the local rest frame, with noninteracting nucleons discarded in the rapidity intervals of projectile and target.

## Key numbers

- Energy densities above 4 GeV/fm^3 are reached in the early overlap phase of Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV (SPS), dropping below 1 GeV/fm^3 within a few fm/c in the center
- Energy densities above 16 GeV/fm^3 are reached in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV (RHIC), dropping below 1 GeV/fm^3 after about 6 fm/c from contact
- The Bjorken estimate gives energy density about 5/0.13 greater than 30 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC using time-scale t_r = 0.13 fm/c
- The c, c-bar formation time tau_c is about 0.05 fm/c for a transverse mass of 4 GeV
- The transient time for central Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV is t_r approximately 2 R_A/gamma_cm approximately 0.13 fm/c
- The experimental ratio B(chi_c to J/Psi) sigma_{chi_c} / sigma^{exp}_{J/Psi} = 0.344 plus or minus 0.031 measured in pp and pi N reactions
- The averaged pp and pA ratio (B_{mu mu}(Psi') sigma_{Psi'}) / (B_{mu mu}(J/Psi) sigma_{J/Psi}) approximately 0.0165
- The parameter b_{p_T} = 2.08 GeV^{-1} for the exponential p_T distribution at SPS energies
- The parameters b_{p_T} = 3.5 GeV/c and c_{p_T} = -8.3 for the power law parametrization at RHIC energies

## Context

This review extends the work of the authors and collaborators (Cass01, brat03, brat04, Olena.SPS, Olena.RHIC) on charm dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, comparing transport model results to data from the NA38, NA50, NA60 experiments at SPS and PHENIX, STAR experiments at RHIC. The analysis shows that purely hadronic HSD transport underestimates the elliptic flow v2 of D-mesons and the low R_AA(p_T) of J/Psi at top RHIC energies, indicating that non-hadronic interactions are mandatory. The work provides predictions for charm observables at FAIR energies of 25-35 AGeV and identifies open questions about the transport properties of the partonic phase that require further development of nonperturbative parton-hadron transport approaches.
