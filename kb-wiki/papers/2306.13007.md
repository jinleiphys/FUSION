# Generative modeling of nucleon-nucleon interactions

> Developing high-precision models of the nuclear force and propagating the associated uncertainties in quantum many-body calculations of nuclei and nuclear matter remain key challenges for ab initio nuclear theory. In the present work we demonstrate that generative machine learning models can construct novel instances of the nucleon-nucleon interaction when trained on existing potentials from the literature. In particular, we train the generative model on nucleon-nucleon potentials derived at second and third order in chiral effective field theory and at three different choices of the resolution scale. We then show that the model can be used to generate samples of the nucleon-nucleon potential drawn from a continuous distribution in the resolution scale parameter space. The generated potentials are shown to produce high-quality nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts. This work provides an important step toward a comprehensive estimation of theoretical uncertainties in nuclear many-body calculations that arise from the arbitrary choice of nuclear interaction and resolution scale. Source code for this project can be found at https://github.com/pswen2019/Glow-nuclear-potential.git.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a generative machine learning model (Glow) can learn the salient features of nucleon-nucleon potentials from chiral effective field theory and generate novel, physically reasonable instances of the potential across a continuous range of resolution scales, but the generated potentials are benchmarked only by scattering phase shifts and not by many-body observables beyond the neutron matter equation of state.

## Method

The authors adapt the Generative Flow (Glow) model, originally developed for computer vision, to treat momentum-space partial-wave matrix elements of the nucleon-nucleon potential as images. They train the model on sets of chiral potentials at second and third order in chiral effective field theory with three different momentum-space cutoffs (450, 500, 550 MeV), as well as on Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolved potentials at eight different resolution scales. The trained model generates new potentials via latent space sampling (LSS) or latent space interpolation (LSI), and a separate Vision Transformer (ViT) model is trained to extract the low-energy constants and cutoff values from the generated potential matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Training set includes SRG potentials at eight values of lambda: 2, 3, 4, 7, 8, 10, 11, 12 inverse femtometers
- SRG potential experiments use 3 partial-wave channels with a 32 by 32 momentum-space mesh grid; chiral potential experiments use 14 partial-wave channels with a 48 by 48 mesh grid
- Chiral potentials trained at cutoffs of 450, 500, and 550 MeV at second and third order in the chiral expansion
- Glow model generates potentials with cutoffs continuously in the range 450 to 550 MeV, with extrapolation demonstrated at 435 MeV
- Phase shift training performed up to laboratory energy of 300 MeV in the neutron-proton channel
- The Glow model can generate realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials in a matter of seconds

## Context

This work extends the application of generative machine learning models to nuclear force modeling, building on chiral effective field theory frameworks for nuclear interactions (Weinberg 1979, Epelbaum 2009, Machleidt 2011) and prior Bayesian uncertainty quantification studies (Wesolowski 2016, Furnstahl 2015). The approach is related to other recent investigations exploring generative models in the context of the renormalization group and uncertainty quantification (PhysRevResearch.2.023369, Abdar 2021, Yang 2024). The work enables more comprehensive estimation of theoretical uncertainties in nuclear many-body calculations that arise from the arbitrary choice of nuclear interaction resolution scale, and the authors note that extension to three-body forces is a straightforward next step.
