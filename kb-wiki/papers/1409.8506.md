# Study of the $B\bar{B}^*/D\bar{D}^*$ bound states in a Bethe-Salpeter approach

> In this work the $B\bar{B}^*/D\bar{D}^*$ system is studied in the Bethe-Salpeter approach with quasipotential approximation. In our calculation both direct and cross diagrams are included in the one-boson-exchange potential. The numerical results indicate the existence of a isoscalar bound state $D\bar{D}^*$ with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$, which may be related to the $X(3872)$. In the isovector sector, no bound state is produced from the interactions of $D\bar{D}^*$ and $B\bar{B}^*$, which suggests the molecular state explanations for $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_c(3900)$ are excluded.

# Study of the $B\bar{B}^*/D\bar{B}^*$ bound states in a Bethe-Salpeter approach

## Key claim

The paper finds an isoscalar $D\bar{D}^*$ bound state with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ that may be related to the $X(3872)$, but **only when the cutoff $\Lambda$ is tuned to values between 2.0 and 2.4 GeV**; in the isovector sector, **no bound state is produced from either $D\bar{D}^*$ or $B\bar{B}^*$ interactions for any cutoff $\Lambda<5$ GeV**, which disfavors the molecular-state explanations for $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_c(3900)$.

## Method

The $B\bar{B}^*$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ systems are studied using the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) with a quasipotential approximation based on covariant spectator theory, treating the vector meson as on-shell. Both direct and cross diagrams are included in the one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential derived from heavy-quark effective theory Lagrangians, with $\pi$, $\eta$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\sigma$ exchange. The BSE is reduced to a one-dimensional integral equation via partial-wave expansion and solved numerically by a recursion method.

## Key numbers

- $D\bar{D}^*$ isoscalar $0^+(1^{++})$ bound state: binding energy $E = 0.2$ MeV at cutoff $\Lambda = 2.0$ GeV; $E = 1.4$ MeV at $\Lambda = 2.2$ GeV; $E = 4.1$ MeV at $\Lambda = 2.4$ GeV.
- $B\bar{B}^*$ isoscalar $0^+(1^{++})$ bound state: $E = 0.2$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.3$ GeV; $E = 3.0$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV; $E = 7.4$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.7$ GeV.
- $D\bar{D}^*$ isoscalar $0^-(1^{+-})$ bound state: $E = 0.2$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.3$ GeV; $E = 6.0$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.4$ GeV.
- $B\bar{B}^*$ isoscalar $0^-(1^{+-})$ bound state: $E = 0.6$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.1$ GeV; $E = 7.8$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.2$ GeV.
- $B\bar{B}^*$ isoscalar $0^-(0^{--})$ bound state: $E = 1.6$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV; $E = 6.7$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.9$ GeV.
- $B\bar{B}^*$ isoscalar $0^-(1^{--})$ bound state: $E = 1.4$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.6$ GeV; $E = 6.4$ MeV at $\Lambda = 1.8$ GeV.
- No isovector bound states found for either $D\bar{D}^*$ or $B\bar{B}^*$ for any cutoff $\Lambda < 5$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior nonrelativistic OBE model studies of the $B\bar{B}^*/D\bar{D}^*$ system by Sun (2011, 2012), which found a $B\bar{B}^*$ isovector bound state assigned to $Z_b(10610)$ and no $D\bar{D}^*$ bound state. The present BSE approach includes both direct and cross diagrams (including $\pi$ exchange) and treats the cross diagram correctly, unlike the earlier coordinate-space potential models. The results contradict the molecular-state interpretation of $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_c(3900)$ and are more consistent with lattice QCD results from Prelovsek (2013) and Chen (2014), which disfavor shallow $D\bar{D}^*$ bound states. The work enables future studies of hadronic molecules using relativistic BSE methods rather than simple Fourier-transformed potentials.
