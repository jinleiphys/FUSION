# Hadrons in Medium

> In these lectures I first give a motivation for investigations of in-medium properties of hadrons. I discuss the relevant symmetries of QCD and how they might affect the observed hadron properties. I then discuss at length the observable consequences of in-medium changes of hadronic properties in reactions with elementary probes, and in particular photons, on nuclei. Here I put an emphasis on new experiments on changes of the sigma and omega mesons in medium.

## Key claim

In-medium changes of hadronic properties, particularly for vector mesons, can be studied in reactions with elementary probes on normal nuclei as a cleaner and independent testing ground compared to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, though the nuclear densities probed are always at or below equilibrium density (rho_0) and any observable effects are expected to be smaller than in heavy-ion reactions.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical approaches to in-medium hadron properties, including chiral symmetry and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for the density dependence of the chiral condensate, QCD sum rules that relate hadronic spectral functions to quark and gluon condensates, and low-density expansions of meson selfenergies via forward scattering amplitudes. For reactions with elementary probes, the author describes a coupled-channel transport model based on the BUU equation that propagates spectral phase space distributions of particles, allowing off-shell production and transport of hadrons with correct spectral functions, with final state interactions treated semiclassically including elastic and inelastic scattering, particle production, and absorption.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate in medium drops as approximately (1 - 0.3 rho/rho_0) times the vacuum condensate at zero temperature, based on the linear density approximation
- At normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, the specific volume per baryon is about 6.7 fm^3, compared to a baryon volume of about 2.1 fm^3 (assuming baryon radius 0.8 fm), indicating significant overlap at about 3 times nuclear equilibrium density
- The STAR collaboration observed a downward shift of the rho-meson pole mass by about 70 MeV in Au+Au collisions, with a similar but less pronounced shift of about 40 MeV in p+p data
- For the S_{11}(1535) resonance, the collisional broadening is about 35 MeV at rho_0 from selfconsistent calculations
- The CERES experiment found a dilepton excess in the invariant mass range from about 300 MeV to about 700 MeV compared to expectations from freely radiating mesons

## Context

This lecture extends the long-standing work on in-medium hadron properties from the Delta-hole model (Ericsson-Weise) and the original QCD sum rule predictions of Hatsuda and Lee for vector meson mass dropping, while noting that more recent work by Leupold and Mosel showed that QCD sum rules give only restrictions on mass and width, not fixed values. The author's coupled-channel approach builds on earlier treatments by Brat-Cass and Fuchs that transported mesons with pole mass only, and enables future experiments with elementary probes (photons, protons, pions) on nuclei to provide a baseline for disentangling in-medium effects from phase-space distortions and hot matter effects seen in heavy-ion experiments at CERN SPS, RHIC, and the planned CBM experiment at FAIR.
