# Equation of state and speed of sound of (2+1)-flavor QCD in strangeness-neutral matter at non-vanishing net baryon-number density

> We update results on the QCD equation of state in (2+1)-flavor QCD with non-zero conserved charge chemical potentials obtained from an eighth-order Taylor series. We present results for basic bulk thermodynamic observables of strangeness-neutral strong-interaction matter, i.e. pressure, number densities, energy and entropy density, and resum Taylor series results using Padé approximants. Furthermore, we calculate the speed of sound as well as the adiabatic compression factor of strangeness-neutral matter on lines of constant entropy per net baryon number. We show that the equation of state ($P(n_B), ε(n_B)$) is already well described by the $4^{\rm th}$-order Taylor series in almost the entire range of temperatures accessible with the beam energy scan in collider mode at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the equation of state (pressure as a function of net baryon-number density and energy density as a function of net baryon-number density) is already well described by a fourth-order Taylor series for almost the entire range of temperatures accessible with the beam energy scan in collider mode at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, but this conclusion is based on results obtained from an eighth-order Taylor series with Padé resummation used to check consistency.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with physical light and strange quark masses (m_l/m_s = 1/27), generating gauge configurations on lattices with temporal extents N_tau = 6, 8, 12, and 16. They computed Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure up to eighth order in the baryon chemical potential, using continuum extrapolations for the second and fourth order coefficients and spline interpolations of high-statistics N_tau = 8 data for the sixth and eighth order coefficients, then constructed Padé approximants to resum the series and calculated thermodynamic observables for strangeness-neutral, isospin-symmetric matter (mu_Q = 0, n_S = 0).

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature from the maximum of P'_2: T_pc,P2 = 153.8(7)(5) MeV
- Pseudo-critical temperature from the zero of P'_4: T_pc,P4 = 155.3(2)(5) MeV
- For temperatures T > 250 MeV, deviations from the ideal gas limit are only about 12% for P_2 and P_4
- At T = 135-175 MeV, the [4,4] Padé approximants for the pressure have poles only in the complex plane, providing an estimate for the range of validity of the Taylor series
- The curvature coefficient kappa_2^B extracted from scaling analysis lies in the range 0.012 to 0.022

## Context

This work extends previous HotQCD results on the QCD equation of state from sixth-order Taylor expansions (Bazavov 2017) to eighth order, using new high-statistics data sets with more than an order of magnitude larger statistics than earlier calculations. The results enable improved hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions at beam energy scan energies and provide constraints on the cosmic trajectory of matter in the expanding early universe, building on prior work by Borsanyi (2012), Guenther (2017), and the HotQCD collaboration (2014, 2018).
