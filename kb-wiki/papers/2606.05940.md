# Seed-Robust PINN Determination of $s$-Wave Bound States and Jost-Function-Based vertex constants in $_Λ^{208}$Pb

> We investigate physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) for computing the $s$-wave bound states of the hypernucleus $_Λ^{208}$Pb, modeled as a two-body system composed of a $Λ$ hyperon and a $^{207}$Pb core. The interaction is described by a Woods--Saxon potential without spin--orbit coupling. In the PINN formulation, the radial bound-state wave function is represented by an artificial neural network, and the eigenenergies are obtained from the Rayleigh--Ritz variational quotient. Because PINN eigenvalue calculations can depend on the residual-loss formulation and random-seed initialization, two residual losses are compared across four independent random seeds. Their performance is assessed using eigenvalue accuracy, coefficient of variation, signal-to-noise ratio, bias--variance decomposition, and Hermitian spectral-ordering consistency. The normalized residual loss gives the most stable and physically consistent bound-state spectrum for the four seeds considered. With this loss, the computed bound-state energies and root-mean-square radii are in very good agreement with the corresponding theoretical values. The resulting wave functions are used to construct the Jost functions through the Wronskian with incoming and outgoing Riccati--Hankel functions. From these Jost functions, the residue of the partial-wave $S$-matrix at the bound-state pole, the Asymptotic Normalization and the Nuclear Vertex Constants are extracted. These quantities show reasonable agreement with theoretical values, although with larger standard deviations than those obtained for the eigenenergies and radii. The results indicate that the Rayleigh--Ritz formulation combined with a seed-robust normalized residual loss provides a stable PINN framework for bound-state and Jost-function-based calculations in hypernuclear two-body systems.

# Seed-Robust PINN Determination of s-Wave Bound States and Jost-Function-Based Vertex Constants in $_\Lambda^{208}$Pb

## Key claim

The normalized residual loss (Eq. 14) provides a seed-robust PINN framework for bound-state calculations that yields stable eigenvalues and wave functions across random initializations, whereas the standard unnormalized residual loss produces seed-dependent results that often fail to recover the correct spectral ordering. The Jost-function-derived quantities (ANC, NVC, S-matrix residue) show reasonable agreement with theoretical values but exhibit larger standard deviations than the eigenenergies and radii.

## Method

The authors model $_\Lambda^{208}$Pb as a two-body system of a $\Lambda$ hyperon and a $^{207}$Pb core interacting via a Woods-Saxon potential without spin-orbit coupling (strength $V_0 = 31.20$ MeV, diffuseness $d = 0.6$ fm, radius $R = r_0(A-1)^{1/3}$ with $r_0 = 1.1$ fm). They use a physics-informed neural network (PINN) with three hidden layers of 32 nodes each and $\sin(\cdot)$ activation to represent the radial bound-state wave function, with eigenenergies obtained from the Rayleigh-Ritz variational quotient. Two residual loss formulations (standard and normalized) are compared across four independent random seeds using eigenvalue accuracy, coefficient of variation, signal-to-noise ratio, bias-variance decomposition, and Hermitian spectral-ordering consistency. The Jost functions are constructed from the wave functions via the Wronskian with Riccati-Hankel functions, from which the residue of the partial-wave S-matrix, asymptotic normalization constant (ANC), and nuclear vertex constant (NVC) are extracted.

## Key numbers

- Ground state (1s) energy: $-27.00094$ MeV (seed 5678, standard residual loss); mean over four seeds with normalized residual loss agrees with reference value $-27.00$ MeV (blokhintsev2007vertex) and $-27.00085$ MeV (Mmusi)
- First excited state (2s) energy: $-16.42320$ MeV (seed 5678, standard residual loss); reference values $-16.43$ MeV (blokhintsev2007vertex) and $-16.42311$ MeV (Mmusi)
- Second excited state (3s) energy: $-3.40045$ MeV (seed 5678, standard residual loss); reference values $-3.41$ MeV (blokhintsev2007vertex) and $-3.40020$ MeV (Mmusi)
- RMS radius for 1s state: $3.5755987 \pm 2.27 \times 10^{-5}$ fm (this work); reference $3.58$ fm (blokhintsev2007vertex)
- RMS radius for 2s state: $4.3630880 \pm 6.724 \times 10^{-5}$ fm (this work); reference $4.36$ fm (blokhintsev2007vertex)
- RMS radius for 3s state: $5.9270186 \pm 3.851 \times 10^{-4}$ fm (this work); reference $5.92$ fm (blokhintsev2007vertex)
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: $V_0 = 31.20$ MeV, $d = 0.6$ fm, $r_0 = 1.1$ fm
- Training used 5000 collocation points over domain $[0, 35]$ fm, Gauss-Legendre quadrature with 512 points, loss weight $w_{\mathrm{res}} = 150$

## Context

This work extends the unsupervised PINN (uPINN) approach of Jin (2020) for bound-state calculations, correcting the failure of that earlier method to reproduce the correct second excited state in $_\Lambda^{208}$Pb as reported by Tshipi (2025). The authors address the seed-sensitivity problem identified in prior PINN eigenvalue calculations by introducing a normalized residual loss inspired by Rayleigh (1899) and Lagaris (1997). The Jost-function-based extraction of ANC, NVC, and S-matrix residues follows the framework of Mmusi and blokhintsev (2007), enabling future applications to hypernuclear peripheral radiative capture and providing empirical constraints on hyperon-nucleon interactions relevant to neutron-star equation-of-state calculations.
