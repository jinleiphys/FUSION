# Alternate Derivation of Ginocchio-Haxton relation [(2j+3)/6]

> We address the problem, previously considered by Ginocchio and Haxton (G-H), of the number of states for three identical particles in a single j-shell with angular momentum J=j. G-H solved this problem in the context of the quantum Hall effect. We address it in a more direct way. We also consider the case J=j+1 to show that our method is more general, and we show how to take care of added complications for a system of five identical particles.

# Alternate Derivation of Ginocchio-Haxton relation [(2j+3)/6]

## Key claim

The number of states with total angular momentum J=j for three identical fermions in a single j-shell is given by the largest integer less than (2j+5)/6, which is equivalent to the Ginocchio-Haxton relation [(2j+3)/6]; the derivation uses a counting method that does not require constructing explicit wave functions, and the same approach can be extended to J=j+1 and to systems of five identical particles with additional care for states where the lowest magnetic projection reaches -j.

## Method

The authors count states by first enumerating all basis states (M1, M2, M3) with M=j+1 for three identical fermions in a single j-shell, then lowering M3 by one unit to obtain states with M=j. The number of additional states with M=j that cannot be obtained by this lowering procedure, those with the form M3 = M2 - 1, gives the number of J=j states. The same technique is extended to J=j+1 by starting from J=j+2, M=j+2 states and lowering M3, and to five-particle systems by subtracting J=j+1 states that have M5 = -j.

## Key numbers

- For three identical fermions in a single j-shell, the number N of J=j states satisfies N < (2j+5)/6, meaning N equals the largest integer less than (2j+5)/6, which is equivalent to [(2j+3)/6].
- For J=j+1, the number of states N_{j+1} satisfies N_{j+1} < (2j+1)/6, which is the same as [j/3].
- For the sum over even J0 of (2J0+1) times the 6j-symbol {j j J0; j j J0}, the values are (-0.5, 0.5, 0) for j = (1/2, 3/2, 5/2), repeating for (7/2, 9/2, 11/2), (13/2, 15/2, 17/2), etc.
- For the sum over even J0 of (2J0+1) times the 6j-symbol {j j J0; j j+1 J0}, the values are (0, 1/2, 1) for j = (1/2, 3/2, 5/2), repeating for (7/2, 9/2, 11/2), etc.
- For five identical fermions in the g_{9/2} shell, there are 5 states with M=j=9/2 and M5=M4-1, but 2 of these belong to J=11/2 states, leaving 3 J=9/2 states.

## Context

This work provides an alternate, more direct derivation of the counting result originally obtained by Ginocchio and Haxton (1993) in the context of the fractional quantum Hall effect, using the rotation group. The method follows the advice of Talmi (1993) on counting states via magnetic projections, and extends the approach to J=j+1 and to five-particle systems. The results connect to the work of Rosensteel and Rowe (2003) on four-fermion J=0 states, and to Zhao et al. (2003, 2004, 2005) on modular behavior of 6j-symbol sums; the authors note that Bayman and Lande (1966) served as a useful guide for handling the five-particle case.
