# Longitudinal collective modes in relativistic asymmetric magnetized nuclear matter within the covariant Vlasov approach

> The neutron-proton-electron (npe) matter under strong magnetic field is studied in the context of the covariant Vlasov approach. A covariant relativistic approach based on the Vlasov equation is applied to the study of infinite asymmetric magnetized nuclear matter. We use several relativistic mean-field nuclear models with non-linear terms. The dispersion relations for the longitudinal modes are obtained, and the isovector and isoscalar collective modes are determined in a wide range of densities as a function of the isospin asymmetry, momentum transfer, and magnetic field. A strong magnetic field gives rise to the appearance of low-lying isovector modes that propagate in nuclear matter, not present in non-magnetized matter. Neutron-like modes are essentially not affected by the presence of a strong magnetic field. In the presence of a strong magnetic field, Landau quantization modifies the proton-like collective modes, leading to the emergence of new branches associated with distinct Landau levels. These new modes can propagate even at high densities and exhibit isoscalar or isovector character.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in asymmetric magnetized nuclear matter, a strong magnetic field (corresponding to occupation of only a few Landau levels) gives rise to low-lying isovector collective modes not present in non-magnetized matter, while neutron-like modes remain essentially unaffected; however, this result is conditional on the neglect of the anomalous magnetic moment of nucleons and on restricting the analysis to longitudinal modes with wave vector parallel to the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors employ the covariant Vlasov approach, the semiclassical limit of the relativistic transport equation, linearized around a magnetized ground state constructed within relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory using the NL3, NL3ωρ, and FSU parameter sets. The ground state accounts for Landau quantization of protons and electrons in an external magnetic field (Landau gauge), while the neutron distribution remains the standard Fermi sphere. Dispersion relations for longitudinal collective modes (wave vector parallel to the magnetic field) are derived by Fourier-transforming the linearized Vlasov equation and applying the Oberman-Ron transform to handle the cyclotron motion, then solving for the self-consistent perturbations of the meson fields.

## Key numbers

- Saturation densities for the three RMF models: NL3 and NL3ωρ at 0.148 fm⁻³, FSU at 0.1505 fm⁻³
- Nuclear matter incompressibilities: NL3 and NL3ωρ at 271 MeV, FSU at 230 MeV
- Symmetry energies at saturation: NL3 at 37.4 MeV, NL3ωρ at 31.5 MeV, FSU at 32.59 MeV
- Slope parameter L of the symmetry energy: NL3 at 118 MeV, NL3ωρ at 55 MeV, FSU at 60.5 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass ratio M*/M at saturation: NL3 and NL3ωρ at 0.596, FSU at 0.611
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: NL3 and NL3ωρ at -16.24 MeV, FSU at -16.30 MeV
- Magnetic field strengths considered range from 10¹⁴ to 10¹⁵ G on the surface of magnetars, potentially exceeding 10¹⁸ G in interiors

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the covariant Vlasov approach by Avancini (2018) on the stability of npe matter under strong magnetic fields, and by Rabhi (2025) on collective modes in cold asymmetric nuclear matter without magnetic fields. The formalism enables future extensions to finite-temperature beta-equilibrated matter, inclusion of anomalous magnetic moments, leptonic or hyperonic components, and transverse modes, which are essential for understanding neutron star oscillations, cooling, and magnetic field decay as studied by Pons (2013).
