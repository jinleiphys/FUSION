# Chiral anomaly and nucleon properties in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with vector mesons

> We consider the extended SU(3) Nambu and Jona-Lasinio model with explicit vector couplings in the presence of external fields. We study the chiral anomaly in this model and its implications on the properties of the nucleon described as a chiral soliton of three valence quarks bounded in mesonic background fields. For the model to reproduce the QCD anomaly it is necessary to subtract suitable local and polynomial counterterms in the external and dynamical vector and axial-vector fields. We compute the counterterms explicitly in a vector gauge invariant regularization, and obtain modifications to the total effective action and vector and axial currents. We study the numerical influence of those counterterms in the two flavour version of model with dynamical $σ,π, ρ, A $ and $ω$ mesons. We find that, for time independent hedgehog configurations, the numerical effects in the nucleon mass, the isoscalar nucleon radius and the axial coupling constant are negligibly small.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with vector mesons does not reproduce the QCD chiral anomaly unless suitable local polynomial counterterms are subtracted; however, for time-independent hedgehog configurations in the two-flavor version, the numerical effects of these counterterms on nucleon mass, isoscalar nucleon radius, and axial coupling constant are negligibly small.

## Method

The authors start from the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector couplings, bosonize it by introducing auxiliary mesonic fields, and integrate out the quarks to obtain an effective action. They compute the chiral anomaly arising from the regularized fermion determinant using a vector gauge invariant Pauli-Villars regularization, identify the discrepancy with the QCD anomaly, and construct explicit counterterms (polynomial in the fields) that restore the correct Bardeen form of the anomaly. The counterterms are then applied to the two-flavor version with sigma, pion, rho, A1, and omega mesons, and nucleon properties are computed in the chiral soliton picture with three valence quarks bound in mesonic background fields.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Arriola and Salcedo, 1994) which showed that the NJL model with vector couplings fails to reproduce the QCD anomaly, and addresses the solution proposed by Bijnens and Prades (1994) that counterterms exist and are unique under CP invariance. The paper provides explicit counterterm expressions and demonstrates that, despite the formal necessity of these terms, their numerical impact on static nucleon observables in the soliton picture is negligible, thus validating previous soliton calculations (e.g., by the authors and others in 1993-1994) that omitted them.
