# Magnetism in Compact Ultra Dense Objects

> This paper summarizes some of the recent results on magnetism in high dense mediums, where the phenomenon of color superconductivity can be present, and its possible implications for the astrophysics of compact objects. The presentation will be organized through the answers to three fundamental questions.

## Key claim
Strong magnetic fields can fundamentally alter the color-superconducting pairing pattern in dense quark matter, splitting the single gap of the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase into three distinct gaps in a new magnetic-CFL (MCFL) phase; moreover, at sufficiently high field strengths, charged gluons become tachyonic and condense into a vortex state that antiscreens the applied magnetic field, producing the paramagnetic-CFL (PCFL) phase.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical results from quantum field theory applied to dense QCD in a magnetic field. It examines the effect of a strong magnetic field on the CFL phase by comparing the magnetic length to the Cooper-pair coherence length, leading to a symmetry reduction from the full CFL group to \(SU(2)_{C+L+R}\). It then analyzes the Schwinger rest-energy spectrum for spin-1 charged gluons to identify a tachyonic instability, and solves the resulting Ginzburg-Landau-type equations for the gluon condensate and the rotated magnetic field to demonstrate antiscreening.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic length is given by \(l_0 = 1/\sqrt{2eB}\); when this becomes smaller than the pairs' coherence length, the magnetic field modifies the condensate structure.
- The critical field for the gluon zero-mode instability is \(H_{\text{cr}} = m^2/e\), where \(m\) is the gluon mass.
- Neutron star magnetic fields are estimated to reach up to \(10^{20}\) G in the inner core if composed of quark matter.
- The energy density of the MCFL phase is compared to that of the iron nucleus (\(\sim 930\) MeV) to assess stability of magnetized strange quark stars.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of color superconductivity, specifically the CFL phase introduced by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and the rotated electromagnetism formalism of Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (2000). It builds on earlier studies of magnetic effects in the CFL phase (Ferrer, de la Incera, and Manuel, 2005–2006) to propose the MCFL and PCFL phases. The results enable future investigations of transport properties in magnetized quark stars and offer an alternative mechanism for magnetic field enhancement in magnetars, potentially resolving conflicts with the standard rapid-spinning magnetar model.
