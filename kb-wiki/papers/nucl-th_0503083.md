# Approximate Coulomb distortion effects in (e,e'p) reactions

> In this paper we apply a well-tested approximation of electron Coulomb distortion effects to the exclusive reaction (e,e'p) in the quasielastic region. We compare the approximate treatment of Coulomb distortion effects to the exact distorted wave Born approximation evaluated by means of partial wave analysis to gauge the quality of our approximate treatment. We show that the approximate Möller potential has a plane-wave-like structure and hence permits the separation of the cross section into five terms which depend on bilinear products of transforms of the transition four current elements. These transforms reduce to Fourier transforms when Coulomb distortion is not present, but become modified with the inclusion of Coulomb distortion. We investigate the application of the approximate formalism to a model of 208Pb(e,e'p) using Dirac-Hartree single particle wave functions for the ground state and relativistic optical model wave functions for the continuum proton. We show that it is still possible to extract, albeit with some approximation, the various structure functions from the experimentally measured data even for heavy nuclei.

# Approximate Coulomb Distortion Effects in (e,e'p) Reactions

## Key claim
The authors show that an approximate treatment of electron Coulomb distortion, using a local effective momentum approximation (LEMA) with additional phase corrections, can reproduce full distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) results for the exclusive (e,e'p) reaction to within 2% around the first peak, but only for momentum transfers above approximately 350 MeV/c and for radial coordinates out to about three to four nuclear radii.

## Method
The authors developed approximate analytic Coulomb distorted electron wavefunctions by replacing the exact partial wave phase shifts with an expansion in powers of κ² (the Dirac quantum number) up to fourth order, and by replacing the angular momentum operator J² with its classical equivalent (r × p')². They then used the Knoll technique to derive an approximate Møller potential with plane-wave-like structure, enabling the cross section to be separated into five terms with modified Fourier transforms of the nuclear current. The approximate formalism was applied to ²⁰⁸Pb(e,e'p) using Dirac-Hartree single particle wavefunctions for bound states and relativistic optical model wavefunctions for the continuum proton, with three-dimensional numerical integration replacing the multipole expansion.

## Key numbers
- For parallel kinematics on ²⁰⁸Pb, the approximate DWBA reproduces the full DWBA reduced cross section to within 2% around the first peak, with the primary effect of Coulomb distortion being a shift of about 20 MeV/c in missing momentum to the right compared to plane wave results.
- For perpendicular kinematics on ²⁰⁸Pb at q = 444 MeV/c, the approximate results agree with full DWBA to within 2% around the first peak and within 5% in the side regions.
- The EMA-κ² approximation underestimates the full DWBA result by about 30% around the first peak for both kinematics.
- Coulomb distortion changes the cross section by approximately 30% for the 3s₁/₂ shell knockout from ²⁰⁸Pb, but only 10% for the 2d₃/₂ shell knockout.
- The apparent fourth structure function R_LT differs from the directly calculated structure function by a factor of 3 at the peaks when Coulomb distortion is included, compared to exact agreement in PWBA.
- For the fifth structure function R_LT' in ²⁰⁸Pb, Coulomb distortion changes the magnitude by approximately 30% compared to the plane wave result.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous approximate treatment of Coulomb distortion for inclusive (e,e') reactions (Kim and Wright, 2005) to the exclusive (e,e'p) reaction, and provides a computationally efficient alternative to the full DWBA partial wave analysis developed by the Ohio University group (Jin, 1994). The approximate formalism enables the separation of the cross section into five structure functions, which is not possible in the exact DWBA approach. The authors compare their results to the EMA-κ² approximation used by Traini et al. (1992) and Giusti and Pacati (1988), finding their LEMA+Δ approach significantly more accurate. The work enables extraction of modified structure functions from experimental data even for heavy nuclei like ²⁰⁸Pb, and can be extended to higher energies available at Jefferson Lab.
