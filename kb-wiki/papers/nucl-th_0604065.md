# Production and Width of the Pentaquark Theta+(1540) in Strangeness Production in the Proton-Proton System

> We analyze recent experimental information on the excitation of the Theta+(1540) pentaquark in the hadronic reaction pp -> p Sigma+ K0. Upon describing the conventional production process - which serves as a normalization - in a meson exchange model, we estimate the resonant Theta+ induced cross section and the width of the pentaquark via K - exchange in a meson-baryon and a quark-gluon model. From a comparison with experiment we extract information on the width of the Theta+, the p Theta+ K0 coupling constant in the meson exchange and the relative s or p wave function for a nucleon-kaon cluster in the Theta+.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the width of the pentaquark Theta+(1540) and its production cross section in the reaction pp -> p Sigma+ K0 by comparing meson-exchange and quark-gluon models with experimental data from the COSY-TOF collaboration, but the authors note that the very restricted experimental information and the smooth dependence of the cross-section ratio on the coupling constant prohibit quantitative conclusions for the moment.

## Method

The authors first model the nonresonant pp -> p Sigma+ K0 cross section using a meson-exchange framework with effective scalar meson exchange and averaged baryon resonance contributions, normalized to the experimental data point at an excess energy Q = 126 MeV. They then calculate the resonant Theta+ induced cross section and width via K-exchange in two parallel approaches: a meson-baryon model with an effective N Theta+ K coupling constant, and a constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator wave functions for the nucleon-kaon cluster in the pentaquark, incorporating spectroscopic factors for the KN configuration.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ratio of nonresonant to resonant cross sections: sigma_NR / sigma_R ~ 12.4 microbarn / 0.4 microbarn ~ 30, from COSY-TOF at beam momentum 2.95 GeV/c
- Excess energy for the data point: Q = 126 MeV
- For a Theta+ with positive parity in the meson-exchange model, the coupling constant range is 2.4 <= g_{N Theta+ K} <= 4.5, which restricts the width to Gamma <= 15 MeV
- For the quark model with positive parity, the width parameter range is 0.4 <= a_R <= 0.7 fm, corresponding to Gamma <= 8 MeV
- The kaon momentum in Theta+ decay: k_0 ~ 260 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier meson-exchange calculations for associated strangeness production in pp collisions, such as those by Tsush and Edep for Lambda K+ and Sigma0 K+ final states, and addresses the surprising narrow width of the Theta+ pentaquark (Gamma <= 20 MeV) compared to conventional baryon resonances (Gamma ~ 100-300 MeV). The results enable future experimental constraints from further data on pp -> p Sigma+ K0 to pin down the pentaquark structure and width, and the authors note that their findings contradict naive dimensional analysis estimates of the N Theta+ K coupling constant (which would give g ~ 4 pi) as discussed by Carlson and Liupgam.
