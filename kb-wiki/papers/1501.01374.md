# Half-lives of Double $β^+$-decay with Two Neutrinos

> Nuclear double $β^-$-decays with two neutrinos were observed for many years and a systematic law describing the relation between their half-lives and decay energies was also proposed recently [Phys. Rev. C89, 064603 (2014)]. However, double $β^+$-decay ($β^+β^+)$ with emission of both two positrons and two neutrinos has not been observed up to date. In this article, we perform a systematic analysis on the candidates of double $β^+$-decay, based on the 2012 nuclear mass table. Eight nuclei are found to be the good candidates for double $β^+$-decay and their half-lives are predicted according to the generalization of the systematic law to double $β^+$-decay. As far as we know, there is no theoretical result on double $β^+$-decay of nucleus $^{154}Dy$ and our result is the first prediction on this nucleus. This is also the first complete research on eight double $β^+$-decay candidates based on the available data of nuclear masses. It is expected that the calculated half-lives of double $β^+$-decay in this article will be useful for future experimental search of double $β^+$-decay.

## Key claim

The authors generalize a systematic law previously established for two-neutrino double β⁻-decay half-lives to the case of double β⁺-decay, predicting half-lives for eight candidate nuclei. The key hedge is that the generalization relies on reversing the sign of the Coulomb correction term from the β⁻ case, and the predictions are made without introducing new adjustable parameters, but the law itself has not been experimentally validated for β⁺β⁺ decay.

## Method

The authors first screened all 40 possible double β⁺-decay emitters listed in the 2012 nuclear mass table, calculating their decay energies using the mass-excess formula Q₂β = [M(A,Z) – M(A,Z-2) – 4Mₑ]c². They identified eight nuclei with significantly positive decay energies. They then generalized a previously published systematic law for two-neutrino double β⁻-decay half-lives (Ren, 2014) by reversing the sign of the Coulomb correction term (from –2lg(2πZ/137) to +2lg(2πZ/137)) to account for positron instead of electron emission, and set the shell-correction term S = 0 for all candidates since none have a magic proton number.

## Key numbers

- Eight candidate nuclei for double β⁺-decay were identified: ⁷⁸Kr, ⁹⁶Ru, ¹⁰⁶Cd, ¹²⁴Xe, ¹³⁰Ba, ¹³⁶Ce, ¹⁴⁸Gd, and ¹⁵⁴Dy.
- Decay energies range from 0.334 MeV (¹³⁶Ce) to 1.267 MeV (¹⁵⁴Dy).
- Predicted half-life for ¹⁵⁴Dy: 2.35 × 10⁵ Ey (where 1 Ey = 10¹⁸ years), corresponding to lg(T₁/₂) = 5.371.
- Predicted half-life for ¹⁴⁸Gd: 4.22 × 10⁶ Ey, corresponding to lg(T₁/₂) = 6.625.
- Predicted half-life for ¹³⁶Ce: 1.09 × 10²⁰ Ey, the longest among the eight.
- Predicted half-life for ⁷⁸Kr: 6.73 × 10⁷ Ey, consistent with prior calculations by Suhonen (2012) which gave (4.94–15.8) × 10⁷ Ey.
- Branching ratio of double β⁺-decay relative to α-decay for ¹⁵⁴Dy: BR = 1.28 × 10⁻¹⁷.

## Context

This work extends the systematic law for two-neutrino double β⁻-decay half-lives proposed by Ren (2014) to the unobserved double β⁺-decay channel. It provides the first complete theoretical survey of eight candidate nuclei using updated 2012 nuclear mass data, and gives the first prediction for ¹⁵⁴Dy. Prior theoretical work by Haxton et al. (1980s) estimated half-lives for six nuclei from A=78 to A=136, and Suhonen (2012) calculated half-lives for ⁷⁸Kr and ⁹⁶Ru using different nuclear-structure methods. The results are intended to guide future experimental searches for double β⁺-decay, particularly for ¹⁴⁸Gd and ¹⁵⁴Dy, which have the shortest predicted half-lives.
