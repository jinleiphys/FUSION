# Program for Generating Tables of SU(3) Coupling Coefficients

> A C-Language program which tabulates the isoscalar factors and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for products of representations in SU(3) is presented. These are efficiently computed using recursion relations, and the results are presented in exact precision as square roots of rational numbers. Output is in LaTeX format.

## Key claim

The paper presents a C-language program that generates tables of SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and isoscalar factors using recursion relations, with results given in exact precision as square roots of rational numbers; however, the program is limited by computer memory and maximum integer size, with the largest product successfully computed being **27** ⊗ **27** on a DEC VAX with 32-bit integers.

## Method

The program uses recursion relations derived from SU(3) ladder operators to construct tables of isoscalar factors for products of two irreducible representations, then combines these with SU(2) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients in the Condon-Shortley phase convention to produce the full SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The algorithm determines which representations appear in the Clebsch-Gordan series using algebraic degeneracy conditions, then computes isoscalar factors by stepping through lattice sites in a three-dimensional space defined by the quantum numbers k₁, k₂, and s = i₁ + i₂. For cases with outer degeneracy greater than one, the program resolves degeneracy by forcing specific isoscalar factors to zero and enforcing orthogonality between successive sets.

## Key numbers

- Memory required on the VAX: 934 k words for **3** ⊗ **3**, 998 k words for **27** ⊗ **27**
- Disk space for output: 2 k words for **3** ⊗ **3**, 182 k words for **27** ⊗ **27**
- CPU time on VAX: 1.2 seconds for **3** ⊗ **3**, 13 minutes 11 seconds for **27** ⊗ **27**
- CPU time on Alpha: 1 minute 13 seconds for **27** ⊗ **27**
- Program contains 2601 lines of C code

## Context

This work extends prior tabulations of SU(3) coupling coefficients by de Swart (1963), Chilton (1967), and others, and replaces earlier programs by Akiyama (1973) and Williams (1993) with a faster, more memory-efficient implementation. The program enables nuclear and particle physics calculations requiring SU(3) symmetry to generate exact tables of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for arbitrary product representations, limited only by computer memory and integer size constraints.
