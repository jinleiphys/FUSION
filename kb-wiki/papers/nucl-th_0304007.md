# Narrow Resonances in Effective Field Theory

> We discuss the power counting for effective field theories with narrow resonances near a two-body threshold. Close to threshold, the effective field theory is perturbative and only one combination of coupling constants is fine-tuned. In the vicinity of the resonance, a second, ``kinematic'' fine-tuning requires a nonperturbative resummation. We illustrate our results in the case of nucleon-alpha scattering.

## Key claim

For effective field theories describing narrow resonances near a two-body threshold, a perturbative expansion is valid for generic low momenta, but a second "kinematic" fine-tuning occurs in a narrow momentum window around the resonance pole, requiring a nonperturbative resummation of the unitarity-cut term even when the underlying short-distance physics requires only one fine-tuned coupling.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for two-body scattering using a "dimeron" auxiliary field carrying the quantum numbers of the resonance, and derive the scattering amplitude in partial waves l ≥ 0. They analyze two possible scalings of the effective-range parameters: one requiring two fine-tuned combinations of coupling constants (as in prior work), and a new scaling requiring only one fine-tuning, where the scattering length a_l is anomalously large for l ≥ 1 while the effective range r_l retains its natural size. The theory is applied to neutron-alpha scattering, comparing perturbative (non-resummed) calculations at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order with the resummed approach of Bedaque et al. (2003).

## Key numbers

- For the p_{3/2} resonance in nα scattering: Γ(E₀)/2 ≃ 0.3 MeV ≪ E₀ ≃ 0.8 MeV ≪ E_α ≃ 20 MeV, or k_I ≃ −6 MeV ≪ k_R ≃ 34 MeV ≪ √(m_N E_α) ≃ 140 MeV
- The first term in the expansion of the resonance energy E₀ is 0.96 MeV, compared to 0.80 MeV found at next-to-leading order in the resummed approach
- The first term in the expansion of the width Γ(E₀) at the resonance is 0.82 MeV, compared to 0.55 MeV in the resummed approach
- From the phase-shift analysis of Arndt et al. (1973), the extracted scales are M_hi ∼ 100 MeV and M_lo ∼ 30 MeV
- The momentum window requiring resummation is of order M_lo²/M_hi

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of shallow bound states (Kaplan 1997, van Kolck 1999) to narrow resonances in any partial wave, clarifying the power counting for the p_{3/2} resonance in nα scattering previously studied by Bedaque et al. (2003). The authors show that the nonperturbative resummation performed in that prior work is only strictly necessary in a narrow kinematic region around the resonance, but improves the description throughout the low-energy region when the scales M_lo and M_hi are not well separated. The approach enables systematic EFT treatments of other narrow resonances such as the Delta resonance in πN scattering and various low-energy nuclear reactions.
