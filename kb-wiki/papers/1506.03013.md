# Generalized perturbations in neutrino mixing

> We derive expressions for the neutrino mixing parameters that result from complex perturbations on (1) the Majorana neutrino mass matrix (in the basis of charged lepton mass eigenstates) and on (2) the charged lepton mass matrix, for arbitrary initial (unperturbed) mixing matrices. In the first case, we find that the phases of the elements of the perturbation matrix, and the initial values of the Dirac and Majorana phases, strongly impact the leading order corrections to the neutrino mixing parameters and phases. For experimentally compatible scenarios wherein the initial neutrino mass matrix has $μ-τ$ symmetry, we find that the Dirac phase can take any value under small perturbations. Similarly, in the second case, perturbations to the charged lepton mass matrix can generate large corrections to the mixing angles and phases of the PMNS matrix. As an illustration of our generalized procedure, we apply it to a situation in which nonstandard scalar and nonstandard vector interactions simultaneously affect neutrino oscillations.

## Key claim

The paper derives general analytic expressions for leading-order corrections to all neutrino mixing parameters (angles and phases) under complex perturbations to either the Majorana neutrino mass matrix or the charged lepton mass matrix, for arbitrary initial mixing matrices; the key hedge is that the corrections to the Dirac and Majorana phases depend strongly on the phases of the perturbation matrix elements and the initial phase values, and that for experimentally compatible scenarios with initial mu-tau symmetry, the Dirac phase can take any value under small perturbations.

## Method

The authors start with an initial Majorana neutrino mass matrix (in the basis where the charged lepton mass matrix is diagonal) and add a complex perturbation matrix, then use sequential rotations to block-diagonalize the resulting mass matrix to leading order in the perturbation size relative to the large mass-squared difference. They derive analytic formulas for the corrections to the three mixing angles, the Dirac phase, and the two Majorana phases. Separately, they treat perturbations to the charged lepton mass matrix, where the initial charged lepton mixing matrix is unconstrained, and compute how the resulting corrections propagate into the PMNS matrix. As an illustration, they apply the formalism to a model with both nonstandard scalar and nonstandard vector interactions affecting neutrino oscillations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous real-perturbation analysis (Liao, 2012) to complex perturbations, which is necessary because real perturbations have no effect on the Dirac and Majorana phases. The paper generalizes beyond the mu-tau symmetric initial conditions considered in earlier work (Babu, 2002; Grimus, 2003) to arbitrary initial mixing matrices, and also treats perturbations in the charged lepton sector, which was not covered in the prior real-perturbation study. The results enable systematic calculation of how nonstandard interactions (both scalar and vector) modify neutrino oscillation probabilities through their effects on the effective mass matrix.
