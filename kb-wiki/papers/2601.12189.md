# Asymptotic Long-Distance Expansion of Euclidean Correlators in Lattice Parton Applications

> Bilinear Euclidean quark and gluon correlators with Wilson links have been used widely for applications of large-momentum effective field theories to computing non-perturbative collinear and soft parton physics. Due to color confinement, these correlators decay exponentially at large spatial distances, a behavior crucial for computing momentum-space Fourier transformations with controlled errors from lattice QCD data. Using heavy-quark effective theory reduction, dispersive analysis, Lorentz symmetry, and heavy-flavor spectra, we determine the leading and next-to-leading asymptotic behaviors and relate the expansion parameters to binding energies of heavy-flavor hadrons. We demonstrate the results through two-loop calculations in $ϕ^3$ theory and from the perspective of locality and analyticity. We also study the impact of the asymptotic analysis on realistic lattice QCD data and demonstrate reliable error estimates.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading and next-to-leading asymptotic exponential decay forms of Euclidean correlators with Wilson links at large spatial distances, relating the expansion parameters to binding energies of heavy-flavor hadrons, but the actual mass gap parameter \(\Lambda\) may deviate from the physical value up to \(O(\Lambda_{\rm QCD})\) due to unphysical pion mass, discretization effects, or renormalon scheme choice on the lattice.

## Method

The authors use heavy-quark effective theory (HQET) reduction to represent the gauge link with dynamical heavy quarks, then perform a dispersive analysis along timelike separation by inserting a complete basis of QCD eigenstates and applying stationary-phase analysis to obtain the large-time asymptotic form. Lorentz symmetry converts this timelike form to the spacelike separation relevant for lattice QCD, and the authors further validate the exponential decay from locality and analyticity perspectives. Two-loop calculations in \(\phi^3\) theory and numerical tests on realistic lattice data for pion and nucleon quasi-PDFs, quasi-DAs, and quasi-GPDs demonstrate the controlled precision of the Fourier transform using the derived asymptotic forms.

## Key numbers

- The mass gap \(\Lambda\) for light-quark correlators is related to the binding energy of a vector or pseudoscalar heavy-light meson (\(Q \bar{q}\)), estimated as \(\sim 500\) MeV using physical states.
- On the lattice, the mass gap may deviate from the physical value up to \(O(\Lambda_{\rm QCD})\), with a physics-motivated constraint range of \([500\,{\rm MeV}-O(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}), 500\,{\rm MeV}+O(\Lambda_{\rm QCD})]\).
- The leading asymptotic decay is proportional to \(e^{-\Lambda |z|}\), and the next-to-leading asymptotic includes an additional factor of \(1/|z|\) multiplying the same exponential.
- With current lattice data quality, the uncertainty from asymptotic analysis is just a few percent compared to the center value at \(O(1)\) in the moderate \(y\) range.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological parametrization proposed by Ji (2020) for large-distance asymptotic forms and the HQET-based derivation by Gao (2021) that assumed constant form factors and analytic continuation. It directly responds to concerns raised by Dutrieux (2025) about the LaMET formalism, arguing that LaMET is a forward-problem formalism with systematically improvable precision. The results enable reliable error estimates for Fourier transforms in lattice QCD calculations of parton distribution functions, distribution amplitudes, transverse-momentum-dependent distributions, and generalized parton distributions within the LaMET framework.
