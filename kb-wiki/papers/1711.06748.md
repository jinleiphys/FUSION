# Methodology study of machine learning for the neutron star equation of state

> We discuss a methodology of machine learning to deduce the neutron star equation of state from a set of mass-radius observational data. We propose an efficient procedure to deal with a mapping from finite data points with observational errors onto an equation of state. We generate training data and optimize the neural network. Using independent validation data (mock observational data) we confirm that the equation of state is correctly reconstructed with precision surpassing observational errors. We finally discuss the relation between our method and Bayesian analysis with an emphasis put on generality of our method for underdetermined problems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a deep neural network can reconstruct the neutron star equation of state from discrete mass-radius observational data points with precision surpassing the input observational errors, provided the training data includes multiple noisy realizations per equation of state to avoid overfitting.

## Method

The authors generate 1948 random equations of state using a five-segment piecewise polytrope parametrization of the sound velocity, with each segment assigned a uniform random value between 0.02 and 0.98. For each equation of state, they solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain the mass-radius relation, then sample 15 mass-radius points with Gaussian errors (0.1 solar mass for mass, 0.5 km for radius) to create 100 noisy observations per equation of state. They train a five-layer feedforward neural network (30-60-40-40-5 nodes) with ReLU and tanh activation functions, using the mean square logarithmic error as the loss function, to map the 15 noisy mass-radius points onto the five sound-velocity parameters characterizing the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- 1948 equations of state used for training (52 of 2000 rejected for not reaching 1.97 solar masses)
- 15 mass-radius points sampled per observation, with errors of 0.1 solar masses (mass) and 0.5 km (radius)
- 100 observations per equation of state, yielding 194800 total training data sets
- Root mean square of radius deviations between reconstructed and true mass-radius relations: 0.16 km at 0.6 solar masses, 0.12 km at 0.8 solar masses, 0.10 km at 1.0 solar masses, 0.099 km at 1.2 solar masses, 0.11 km at 1.4 solar masses, 0.11 km at 1.6 solar masses, 0.12 km at 1.8 solar masses
- Input observational radius error was 0.5 km, while reconstruction RMS is approximately 0.1 km

## Context

This work extends Bayesian approaches to equation of state inference from neutron star observations, such as those by Ozel (2010), Raithel (2017), Steiner (2010), and Alvarez-Castillo (2016), by introducing a machine learning method that does not require explicit prior distributions. The authors show that their neural network approach encompasses Bayesian maximum a posteriori estimation as a limiting case, and argue the method can be generalized to other underdetermined inverse problems in physics. The technique is designed for application to forthcoming neutron star observations from facilities such as NICER.
