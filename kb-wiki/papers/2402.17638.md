# Dual chiral density wave induced oscillating Casimir effect

> The Casimir effect is known to be induced from photon fields confined by a small volume, and also its fermionic counterpart has been predicted in a wide range of quantum systems. Here, we investigate what types of Casimir effects can occur from quark fields in dense and thin quark matter. In particular, in the dual chiral density wave, which is a possible ground state of dense quark matter, we find that the Casimir energy oscillates as a function of the thickness of matter. This oscillating Casimir effect is regarded as an analog of that in Weyl semimetals and is attributed to the Weyl points in the momentum space of quark fields. In addition, we show that an oscillation is also induced from the quark Fermi sea, and the total Casimir energy is composed of multiple oscillations.

## Key claim

The authors find that the Casimir energy from quark fields in the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase of dense quark matter oscillates as a function of the thickness of matter, but this oscillating behavior is conditional on the DCDW parameter regime where the amplitude M is less than half the wave number q (M < q/2), and the total Casimir energy is a superposition of multiple oscillations from both the Dirac sea and the Fermi sea contributions.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective model of QCD, applying a mean-field ansatz for the DCDW condensates with a wave number vector along the z-direction. They calculate the Casimir energy for quark fields confined between periodic boundary conditions at z=0 and z=L_z using two approaches: (i) a Lifshitz formula derived for the DCDW phase, which provides an analytical expression for the Casimir energy from the Dirac sea contribution, and (ii) a lattice regularization method that discretizes the momentum and allows calculation of both Dirac sea and Fermi sea contributions. The dispersion relations of quarks in the DCDW phase exhibit Weyl points and Fermi points, which are identified as the origin of the oscillating behavior.

## Key numbers

- The DCDW phase is a candidate ground state for quark/nuclear matter in the density region of ρ/ρ₀ = 3-6, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (normal nuclear density)
- For the virtual parameter set (M/Λ, q/Λ) = (0.1, 0.6), the oscillation period is estimated as L_z^osc ≈ 5.1 fm with k_WP/Λ ≈ 0.283
- In the zero-density region at L_z = 1 fm, the Casimir energy is E_Cas ≈ 241 MeV/fm² ≈ 3.86 × 10⁴ N/fm; for massless quarks at the same thickness, E_Cas ≈ 519 MeV/fm² ≈ 8.32 × 10⁴ N/fm
- In the intermediate-density region (μ/Λ = 0.485), the sign of the total Casimir coefficient flips around L_z ≈ 2.3 fm and 8.8 fm, with an oscillation period estimated as L_z^osc ≈ 9.4 fm from k_FP/Λ ≈ 0.154
- In the high-density DCDW phase (M/Λ, q/Λ, μ/Λ) = (0.09, 0.62, 0.52), the Dirac-sea oscillation period is L_z^osc ≈ 4.8 fm (k_WP/Λ ≈ 0.298), while the Fermi-sea contributions have periods of L_z^osc ≈ 1.7 fm (k_FP/Λ ≈ 0.851) and L_z^osc ≈ 7.6 fm (k_FP/Λ ≈ 0.190)

## Context

This work extends the oscillating Casimir effect previously predicted in Weyl semimetals by Nakayama (2022) to the QCD context, specifically the DCDW phase of dense quark matter first proposed by Dautry (1979). The DCDW phase has been extensively studied in the NJL model by Tatsumi (2004) and Nakano (2004), who determined the order parameters M and q as functions of chemical potential. The results enable future investigations of Casimir effects in finite-volume lattice QCD simulations, relativistic heavy-ion collision fireballs, and neutron star interiors where dense quark matter may exist in small regions.
