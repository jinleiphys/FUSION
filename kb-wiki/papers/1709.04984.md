# Integral transforms of the quantum mechanical path integral: hit function and path averaged potential

> We introduce two new integral transforms of the quantum mechanical transition kernel that represent physical information about the path integral. These transforms can be interpreted as probability distributions on particle trajectories measuring respectively the relative contribution to the path integral from paths crossing a given spatial point (the hit function) and the likelihood of values of the line integral of the potential along a path in the ensemble (the path averaged potential).

## Key claim

The paper introduces two new integral transforms of the quantum mechanical transition kernel, the hit function and the path averaged potential, that can be interpreted as probability distributions on particle trajectories, but the authors hedge that these transforms are primarily motivated by their usefulness for numerical sampling of the path integral, with wider applications anticipated but not yet demonstrated.

## Method

The authors define the hit function as a path integral weighted by a delta function that counts worldlines crossing a given spatial point, and the path averaged potential as a constrained path integral over the line integral of the potential along a path. They derive inverse transforms relating these functions back to the standard kernel, provide asymptotic formulas for large Euclidean time T using spectral decompositions, analyze gauge transformations for electromagnetic interactions, and compute explicit analytic expressions for the free particle, linear potential, harmonic oscillator, and constant magnetic field. The analytic results are verified against numerical sampling using worldline numerics.

## Key numbers

- Free particle hit function in one dimension: \(\mathcal{H}_{0}(z | y, x; T) = \sqrt{ \frac{\pi}{2T} } e^{ \frac{(x - y)^2}{2T} } \textrm{Erfc}\left[ \frac{|z - x| + |z - y|} { \sqrt{2T}} \right]\)
- Path averaged potential for the linear potential \(V(x)=kx\) in one dimension: \(\mathcal{P}(v | y, x; T) = \sqrt{\frac{6}{\pi k^{2}T^{3}}}e^{-\frac{3}{2T}(x + y)^{2}}e^{-\frac{6}{k^{2}T^{3}}\left(v^{2} - kT(x +y)v\right)}\)
- Path averaged potential for constant magnetic field B (Fock-Schwinger gauge, diagonal elements): \(\mathcal{\hat{P}}(v | x, x; T) = \frac{\pi}{2 e B T} \textrm{sech}^{2}\left( \frac{\pi v}{e B T} \right)\)
- Free particle kernel normalization: \(K_{0}(y, x; T) = \left(\frac{m}{2\pi T}\right)^{\frac{D}{2}} e^{-\frac{m\left(y-x\right)^{2}}{2T}}\)

## Context

This work extends concepts from quantum field theory, specifically the "local time" of Levy (1939) and the proper time formalism of Polyakov (1987) and Schubert (2001), into non-relativistic quantum mechanics, while also adapting the relativistic path averaged potential introduced by Gies et al. (2003) to the quantum mechanical setting. The authors provide the inverse transformations that were lacking in those prior works and analyze gauge dependence. The results enable numerical estimation of the quantum mechanical kernel through finite-dimensional integration of these distributions, with applications to more complex systems reported separately by the authors.
