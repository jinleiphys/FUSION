# Leptophobic dark photon interpretation of the $η^{\left(\prime\right)}\rightarrowπ^0\left(η\right)γγ$ puzzle

> The decays of $η$ and $η^\prime$ mesons provide unique opportunities for testing the properties of low energy Quantum Chromodynamics and for the search of new physics beyond the Standard Model. However, recent experimental results on the rare decays of $η^{\left(\prime\right)}\rightarrowπ^0\left(η\right)γγ$ cannot be self-consistently described by the combination of the Vector Meson Dominance and Linear Sigma Model employing the same set of parameters. We show that this tension can be attributed to the presence of a leptophobic dark photon $\mathcal{B}$, and find representative values of the parameters which provide consistent description of these three decays, simultaneously. Unlike existing strategies of Dalitz analysis searching for the bump at $m_\mathcal{B}$, we propose the usage of mismatch between these decays to constrain the parameters of the hypothetical dark photon.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the approximately 5-sigma tension between the experimental branching ratios of the three decays η→π⁰γγ, η′→π⁰γγ, and η′→ηγγ, which cannot be simultaneously described by the Vector Meson Dominance plus Linear Sigma Model framework, can be resolved by introducing a leptophobic dark photon (B boson) that couples predominantly to quarks rather than leptons, provided one accepts "unnatural" values of the coupling constants that violate the usual naturalness expectations.

## Method

The author adopts the VMD+LσM framework developed by Escribano et al. (2020) and modifies the VMD part of the amplitude by including a hypothetical B boson with the same quantum numbers as the ω meson (I^G(J^PC)=0^-(1^--)). The B boson couplings to mesons are parameterized without reference to SU(3) flavor symmetry, and the total matrix element is computed as a coherent sum of VMD, LσM, and B boson contributions. A fit is performed to simultaneously describe the η→π⁰γγ and η′→π⁰γγ decays, using a new parameterization of the ρ⁰-meson decay width from Lichard (2006) instead of the previously used Roos (1969) parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The discrepancy between the simultaneous fit of all three decays and experimental data is approximately 5σ
- For η→π⁰γγ, vector mesons contribute approximately 93% of the total decay width, with constructive interference from scalar mesons contributing approximately 7%
- For η′→π⁰γγ, the interference between vector and scalar mesons is approximately -0.4%, with vector mesons contributing approximately 100% of the total decay width
- The mass of the hypothetical B boson is estimated to be in the range of 140 MeV to 1 GeV
- The naturalness bound on the quark coupling is g_B ≲ 10^(-2) × (m_B / 100 MeV)
- The leptonic coupling parameter ε is constrained by BaBar and KLOE-2 experiments to be ε < 10^(-3) to 10^(-5)
- The invisible decay branching ratio of η′ is bounded as Br(η′ → invisible) < 6 × 10^(-4)

## Context

This work extends the combined analysis of Escribano et al. (2020), who found that the VMD+LσM framework cannot simultaneously reproduce the branching ratios of η→π⁰γγ, η′→π⁰γγ, and η′→ηγγ. Unlike existing "bump-hunting" strategies searching for a resonance in Dalitz plots as proposed by Tulin (2014), the author proposes using the mismatch between these three decays to constrain the parameters of a leptophobic dark photon. The paper enables future experimental searches at facilities such as the JLab Eta Factory (GlueX apparatus) and provides a framework for constraining B boson couplings from the three decay channels simultaneously.
