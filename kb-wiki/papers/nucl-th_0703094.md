# B- to light-meson transition form factors

> We report a comprehensive set of results for B-meson heavy-to-light transition form factors calculated using a truncation of, and expression for, the transition amplitudes in which all elements are motivated by the study of Dyson-Schwinger equations in QCD. In this relativistic approach, which realises confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, all physical values of momentum transfer in the transition form factors are simultaneously accessible. Our results can be useful in the analysis and correlation of the large body of data being accumulated at extant facilities, and thereby in probing the Standard Model and beyond.

## Key claim

The paper reports a comprehensive set of B-meson heavy-to-light transition form factors calculated using a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) motivated truncation, where all physical values of momentum transfer are simultaneously accessible, but the results depend on simple one-covariant models for meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and a momentum-partitioning choice that would be unnecessary in a fully ab initio study.

## Method

The authors calculate transition form factors for B-meson decays to light pseudoscalar and vector mesons using a generalised impulse approximation derived from a symmetry-preserving truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. Dressed-quark propagators are parametrised algebraically for light quarks (u, d, s) using entire functions that realise confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, while heavy quarks (c, b) are treated via constituent-quark propagators. Meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are represented by single-covariant forms: light pseudoscalars use amplitudes related to the dressed-quark mass function via the Goldberger-Treiman relation, light vector mesons use a transverse ansatz with a Gaussian momentum-space width, and heavy mesons use Gaussian forms with flavour-dependent width parameters. The dressed-quark-photon vertex is implemented via the Ball-Chiu ansatz, which satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity.

- Heavy constituent-quark masses: M_c = 1.32 GeV, M_b = 4.65 GeV

- Ratio f_Ds/f_D = 1.26, f_Bs/f_B = 1.20
- Heavy-quark symmetry limit: Isgur-Wise function slope parameter rho^2 = 1.98 for the B to D transition
- Vacuum quark condensate: -langle u u rangle_{zeta=1 GeV} = (0.22 GeV)^3

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based approach of Ivanov et al. (2006) and earlier studies (misha1, misha2, mishasvy) to cover a fuller range of rare exclusive B decays, while remedying the threshold problem that plagued earlier relativistic constituent quark model calculations (Faessler 2002, Ivanov 2006) by implementing confinement for light quarks. The results are comparable to those from light-cone sum rules (Khodjamirian 2006, Ball 2004), light-front quark models (Lu 2007), and lattice QCD calculations (HPQCD, UKQCD), and can be used to analyse data from existing facilities to probe the Standard Model and beyond.
