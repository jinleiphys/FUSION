# The 25th anniversary for nuclear chirality

> The brief history for the prediction of the nuclear chirality is provided. The theoretical and experimental investigations of the nuclear chirality are reviewed, including the verification of chiral doublet bands, the chiral conundrum and its resolution, and the prediction and observation of the multiple chiral doublets (M$χ$D). Some recent theoretical progresses are highlighted, including the chiral collective Hamiltonian, the A-plot and the K-plot, the nuclear chirality-parity (ChP) violation, the chiral rotation induced by the pairing correlations, as well as the chiral dynamics. The possibly emerging area, challenges that lie ahead, and opportunities for progress in the context of the nuclear chirality are discussed.

# The 25th anniversary for nuclear chirality

## Key claim
Nuclear chirality, predicted 25 years ago in an ideal model of a high-j particle and high-j hole coupled to a triaxial rotor with γ=30°, has been experimentally confirmed in multiple mass regions, but the original chiral conundrum in ¹³⁴Pr, where measured in-band B(E2) values showed large differences between candidate partner bands, was later resolved by measurements in other nuclei such as ¹²⁸Cs and ¹³⁵Nd, suggesting the problem in ¹³⁴Pr may be a special case.

## Method
The paper reviews the history and current status of nuclear chirality research, covering both theoretical approaches (triaxial particle rotor model, three-dimensional cranking covariant density functional theory, projected shell model, chiral collective Hamiltonian, time-dependent CDFT) and experimental efforts (lifetime measurements, g-factor measurements, identification of chiral doublet bands and multiple chiral doublets). The authors highlight recent developments including the A-plot and K-plot for visualizing angular momentum orientation, nuclear chirality-parity violation, pairing-induced chiral rotation, and chiral dynamics from time-dependent CDFT.

## Key numbers
- More than 59 chiral doublet bands in 47 nuclei (including 8 nuclei with MχD) reported by 2019 in the A=80, 100, 130, and 190 regions (Xiong 2019)
- First experimental evidence for MχD identified in ¹³³Ce in 2013 (Ayangeakaa 2013)
- For ¹³⁵Nd, the experimental energies of two pairs of chiral doublet bands were well reproduced by TDCDFT without any adjustable parameters beyond the well-defined density functional (Ren 2022)
- In ¹²⁸Cs, the A-plot analysis shows evolution from chiral vibration at I=11ℏ to static chiral rotation at I=14ℏ and to another chiral vibration at I=18ℏ (Chen 2017)
- For ¹³⁵Nd with pairing correlations included, the azimuthal angle becomes nonzero at rotational frequencies of 0.52, 0.49, or 0.47 MeV for enhanced proton pairing (50%, 100%, 150% strength), and at 0.54, 0.53, or 0.52 MeV for enhanced neutron pairing (Wang 2023)

## Context
This review marks the 25th anniversary of the nuclear chirality concept introduced by Frauendorf (1997), extending the earlier work on magnetic rotation in near-spherical Pb isotopes (Frauendorf 1993) and the tilted axis cranking model validated against the particle rotor model (Frauendorf 1996). The paper summarizes progress from the initial candidate chiral doublet bands in ¹³⁴Pr (Petrache 1996) through the resolution of the chiral conundrum (Tonev 2006, Grodner 2006) to the prediction and observation of multiple chiral doublets (Meng 2006, Ayangeakaa 2013). It enables future searches for chirality-parity quartet bands, g-factor measurements for excited states, and studies of chirality in soft triaxial nuclei and chiral wobblers.
