# PWA tools in Hadronic Spectroscopy

> The mini-proceedings of the Workshop on PWA tools in Hadronic Spectroscopy held in Mainz from February 18th to 20th, 2013.

## Key claim

This paper is a workshop summary (mini-proceedings) that reports on discussions about the need for common, modular partial wave analysis (PWA) tools in hadronic spectroscopy, rather than presenting a single new result; the key claim is that combining data from different experiments (ELSA, JLAB, MAMI, BES-III, COMPASS, HADES) requires mutual understanding of techniques and model assumptions used by different communities.

## Method

The workshop brought together experts to discuss specific PWA-related topics, including an improved Breit-Wigner formula with a single additional phase parameter for model-independent resonance extraction, coupled-channel dynamics in meson-baryon scattering using unitarized amplitudes, the Bonn-Gatchina PWA framework applied to p+p collisions for kaonic cluster searches, truncated PWA for two-pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, and the design of a common PWA software framework for the PANDA experiment. Each talk presented either a new formalism, an analysis of existing data, or a software development plan.

## Key numbers

- The HADES experiment extracted 21,000 events (two data samples of 13,000 and 8,000 events) of the final state p + K⁺ + Λ from 1.2 × 10⁹ p+p collisions at a beam kinetic energy of 3.5 GeV.
- For neutral pion photoproduction near threshold, an upper limit of approximately 170 MeV photon energy in the laboratory frame was established for the applicability of Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory.
- The coupled-channel analysis of pion-induced πN, ηN, KΛ, and KΣ production determines resonance poles and residues up to Jᴾ = 9/2±.
- The truncated PWA for two-pseudoscalar meson photoproduction with J_max = 1/2 requires eight independent partial wave amplitudes, determined from a complete set including unpolarized cross section, helicity beam asymmetry, and nucleon recoil polarization.

## Context

This workshop summary extends the ongoing efforts in hadron spectroscopy by addressing the challenge of combining high-precision data from multiple facilities (ELSA, JLAB, MAMI, BES-III, COMPASS, HADES) into a global analysis. The work builds on prior approaches such as the Bonn-Gatchina PWA framework (Anisovich et al., 2012), the SAID analysis of pion photoproduction (Workman et al., 2012), and chiral perturbation theory calculations (Bernard et al., 1995; Fernández-Ramírez and Bernstein, 2012). The workshop enables future development of common, modular PWA software for experiments like PANDA at FAIR, and addresses open questions about the dynamical nature of baryonic resonances and the need for model-independent resonance parameter extraction.
