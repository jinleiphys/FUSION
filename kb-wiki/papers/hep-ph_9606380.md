# Large-x d/u Ratio in W-boson Production

> Recent analysis of proton and deuteron deep-inelastic scattering data have suggested that the extracted d/u quark distribution ratio at large x may be significantly larger than previously believed, if the data are corrected for nuclear binding effects in the deuteron. We examine the sensitivity to the large-x d/u ratio of lepton asymmetries from W-boson production in p-pbar and p-p collisions at large rapidity, which do not suffer from nuclear contamination.

## Key claim

The ratio of W+ to W- cross sections in proton-antiproton and proton-proton collisions at large Feynman x (x_F > 0.5) is highly sensitive to the d/u quark distribution ratio at large x, and this method avoids the theoretical ambiguities from nuclear binding corrections that affect deuteron deep-inelastic scattering analyses.

## Method

The authors compute the ratio R_ph(x_F) of W+ to W- differential cross sections in p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s) = 1.8 TeV and pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 500 GeV, using leading-order parton model expressions with CTEQ parameterizations. They modify the d quark distribution by adding a correction term Delta(x,Q^2) parameterized from the analysis of Melnitchouk and Thomas (1996) to allow a non-zero d/u ratio at x -> 1, then compare the resulting cross section ratios and lepton charge asymmetries with those from standard parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- The correction term at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 is parameterized as Delta(x) = 0.2 x^2 exp(-(1-x)^2), giving d/u -> 1/5 as x -> 1
- At Q^2 = M_W^2, the correction evolves to Delta(x) = 0.18 x^1.2 exp(-(1-x)^2)
- The modified distribution produces a ~30% reduction in R_ppbar at x_F = 0.6, and more than 50% reduction for x_F > 0.7
- For pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 500 GeV, x_1 > 0.6 at large x_F implies x_2 < 0.04
- With a transverse energy cut E_T > 35 GeV at sqrt(s) = 1.8 TeV, the lepton asymmetry differences become noticeable for lepton rapidity y_l > 2, with effects of order 50% at y_l = 2.5

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Melnitchouk and Thomas (1996), who showed that including nuclear EMC corrections in deuteron deep-inelastic scattering data raises the asymptotic d/u ratio from zero to values consistent with the perturbative QCD prediction of 1/5 from Farrar and Jackson (1975). The W-boson production method, previously used by Berger et al. (1989) and Martin, Roberts, and Stirling (1989) to study sea quark asymmetries at small x, is here applied to probe valence quark distributions at large x without nuclear contamination. The approach enables experimental tests at the upgraded Tevatron and RHIC that could distinguish between the standard parameterizations (Eichten et al., 1984; CTEQ, 1995) and the modified d/u ratio.
