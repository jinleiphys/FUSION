# Phase transition and critical end point driven by an external magnetic field in asymmetric quark matter

> The location of the critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram is determined under different scenarios. The effect of strangeness, isospin/charge asymmetry and an external magnetic field is investigated. The discussion is performed within the 2+1 flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loop. It is shown that isospin asymmetry shifts the CEP to larger baryonic chemical potentials and smaller temperatures. At large asymmetries the CEP disappears. However, a strong enough magnetic field drives the system into a first order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in highly isospin-asymmetric quark matter where the critical end point (CEP) is absent at zero magnetic field, a sufficiently strong external magnetic field can drive the system into a first-order phase transition, restoring a CEP. This result is conditional on the specific choice of the 2+1 flavor PNJL model and the regularization scheme used.

## Method

The authors use the 2+1 flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loop (PNJL) in the mean-field approximation, with a sharp 3-momentum cutoff regularization. They compute the thermodynamic potential as a function of temperature, baryonic chemical potential, isospin asymmetry, and external magnetic field, then locate the CEP by minimizing the potential with respect to the chiral condensates and the Polyakov loop. The model parameters are fixed by fitting vacuum pion and kaon properties, and the pure gauge sector is described by a standard Polyakov loop potential with T0 = 270 MeV.

## Key numbers

* For symmetric matter (μu = μd = μs) at zero magnetic field, the CEP in the PNJL model is at T = 155.4 MeV, μB = 873.8 MeV, and ρB = 1.87 ρ0 (where ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3).
* For β-equilibrium matter at zero magnetic field, the CEP in the PNJL model is at T = 140.1 MeV, μB = 915.1 MeV, and ρB = 1.73 ρ0.
* The threshold for CEP disappearance with isospin asymmetry is μd = 1.45 μu (with μs = 0), corresponding to |μI| = 130 MeV; at this threshold the CEP occurs at T ∼ 0 MeV, μB = 1126 MeV, and ρB = 1.91 ρ0.
* For symmetric matter (μu = μd = μs) at eB = 1.0 GeV2, the CEP shifts to T = 244.6 MeV, μB = 775.9 MeV, and ρB = 13.85 ρ0.
* For the threshold isospin asymmetry (μd = 1.45 μu, μs = 0), two CEPs appear for magnetic fields in the range 0.03 ≲ eB ≲ 0.07 GeV2; above eB = 0.07 GeV2 only one CEP remains.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram under isospin asymmetry by Ueda (2013) and Abuki (2013), who showed that the CEP disappears at sufficiently high asymmetry, and generalizes the magnetic field results of Avancini (2012) from symmetric to asymmetric matter. The paper enables future investigations of how strong magnetic fields in magnetars or heavy-ion collisions could induce first-order phase transitions in systems that would otherwise exhibit only crossover transitions, and highlights the need to incorporate inverse magnetic catalysis effects (as seen in lattice QCD by Bali (2012)) into effective model calculations.
