# Bimodality as a signal of Liquid-Gas phase transition in nuclei?

> We use the HIPSE (Heavy-Ion Phase-Space Exploration) Model to discuss the origin of the bimodality in charge asymmetry observed in nuclear reactions around the Fermi energy. We show that it may be related to the important angular momentum (spin) transferred into the quasi-projectile before secondary decay. As the spin overcomes the critical value, a sudden opening of decay channels is induced and leads to a bimodal distribution for the charge asymmetry. In the model, it is not assigned to a liquid-gas phase transition but to specific instabilities in nuclei with high spin. Therefore, we propose to use these reactions to study instabilities in rotating nuclear droplets.

## Key claim

The bimodality in charge asymmetry observed in nuclear reactions around the Fermi energy, previously interpreted as a signature of a liquid-gas phase transition, may instead originate from the sudden opening of decay channels when the angular momentum (spin) transferred into the quasi-projectile exceeds a critical value, and is not assigned to a liquid-gas phase transition in the HIPSE model.

## Method

The authors used the HIPSE (Heavy-Ion Phase-Space Exploration) model to generate 10^6 collisions for the Xe+Sn system at 50 MeV/nucleon, covering the full impact parameter distribution from grazing to head-on collisions. Events were filtered using the same experimental protocol as the INDRA 4π array, selecting quasi-projectile events with 80% of the projectile detected and sorting by transverse energy of light charged particles from the quasi-target. The correlation between the largest fragment charge (Z1) and the charge asymmetry η_Z = (Z1-Z2)/(Z1+Z2) was analyzed for different transverse energy bins, and the properties of the quasi-projectile source before secondary decay were traced back to identify the origin of the bimodal behavior.

## Key numbers

- The Xe+Sn system was studied at 50 MeV/nucleon beam energy.
- Events were selected with 80% of the projectile detected (completeness criterion).
- For the evaporation residue (ER) class, the quasi-projectile thermal energy was Eth/A ≈ 1.5 MeV and spin J ≈ 30 ħ.
- For the multifragmentation (MF) class, the quasi-projectile thermal energy was Eth/A ≈ 4 MeV and spin J ≈ 80 ħ.
- The quasi-projectile source sizes were Z_QP ≈ 50 for ER and Z_QP ≈ 45 for MF, with strong overlap between the two distributions.
- In statistical simulations of a 120Sn nucleus, bimodality appeared at spin values J = 60-70 ħ, approximately corresponding to the limit of stability against prompt fission for Z ≈ 50.

## Context

This work extends the experimental studies of bimodality in charge asymmetry reported by Tamain (2003) and Pichon (2005), which were presented as evidence for a liquid-gas phase transition in nuclei. The authors use the HIPSE model (Lacroix, 2004) to show that the bimodality can instead be explained by high-spin instabilities in the quasi-projectile, specifically the sudden opening of decay channels when the spin approaches the fission barrier limit (Frobrich, 1996). The results suggest that heavy-ion reactions at Fermi energies may be used to study shape bifurcations associated with high spin, such as the Jacobi transition (Rin, 1980; Maj, 2004), rather than liquid-gas phase transitions.
