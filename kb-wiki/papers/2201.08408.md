# Event-by-event jet anisotropy and hard-soft tomography of the quark-gluon plasma

> Suppression of jet spectra or jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is caused by jet energy loss in the dense medium. The azimuthal anisotropy of jet energy loss in non-central heavy-ion collisions can lead to jet anisotropy which in turn can provide insight into the path-length dependence of jet quenching. This is investigated within the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model which simulates both elastic scattering and medium-induced gluon radiation based on perturbative QCD for jet shower and medium recoil partons as well as radiated gluons as they propagate through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The dynamical evolution of the QGP in each event of heavy-ion collisions is provided by the (3+1)D CLVisc hydrodynamic model with fully fluctuating initial conditions. This framework has been shown to describe the suppression of single inclusive jet spectra well. We calculate in this study the elliptic ($v_{2}^{\rm jet}$) and triangular ($v_{3}^{\rm jet}$) anisotropy coefficients of the single inclusive jet spectra in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energies. We investigate the colliding energy, centrality, jet transverse momentum dependence of the jet anisotropy, as well as their event-by-event correlation with the flow coefficients of the soft bulk hadrons. An approximate linear correlation between jet and bulk $v_2$ is found. Effect of the bulk $v_n$ fluctuation on $v_n^{\rm jet}$ is found negligible. The jet-induced medium excitation, which is influenced by radial flow, is shown to enhance $v_{2}^{\rm jet}$ and the enhancement increases with the jet cone size. The jet elliptic anisotropy $v_{2}^{\rm jet}$ is also found to be slightly enhanced by the shear viscosity of the bulk medium in comparison to the LBT results when jets propagate through an ideal hydrodynamic QGP medium.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that jet azimuthal anisotropy in non-central heavy-ion collisions arises from the path-length dependence of jet energy loss, and that the jet elliptic anisotropy ($v_{2}^{\rm jet}$) is enhanced by both jet-induced medium excitation (influenced by radial flow) and the shear viscosity of the bulk medium, while the effect of event-by-event bulk $v_n$ fluctuations on $v_n^{\rm jet}$ is found to be negligible.

## Method

The authors employ the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which simulates both elastic scattering and medium-induced gluon radiation based on perturbative QCD for jet shower and medium recoil partons as they propagate through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The dynamical evolution of the QGP is provided by the (3+1)D CLVisc hydrodynamic model with fully fluctuating initial conditions from the AMPT model. Jets are reconstructed using the anti-$k_\mathrm{T}$ algorithm via the FASTJET package, and the elliptic ($v_{2}^{\rm jet}$) and triangular ($v_{3}^{\rm jet}$) anisotropy coefficients are extracted from the azimuthal distributions of single inclusive jets relative to the event plane of soft hadrons.

## Key numbers

* Soft hadron elliptic flow $\langle v_2^{\rm soft}\rangle$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV ranges from $0.047\pm 0.007$ in the $5-10\%$ centrality bin to $0.078\pm 0.009$ in the $50-60\%$ centrality bin.
* Soft hadron elliptic flow $\langle v_2^{\rm soft}\rangle$ at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV ranges from $0.054\pm 0.008$ in the $5-10\%$ centrality bin to $0.078\pm 0.009$ in the $50-60\%$ centrality bin.
* Soft hadron triangular flow $\langle v_3^{\rm soft}\rangle$ at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV ranges from $0.031\pm 0.007$ in the $5-10\%$ centrality bin to $0.035\pm 0.007$ in the $50-60\%$ centrality bin.
* Soft hadron triangular flow $\langle v_3^{\rm soft}\rangle$ at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV ranges from $0.027\pm 0.007$ in the $5-10\%$ centrality bin to $0.032\pm 0.008$ in the $50-60\%$ centrality bin.
* The event-by-event fluctuation of soft hadron elliptic flow $\delta v_2^{\rm soft}/v_2^{\rm soft}$ is approximately $10\%$ to $15\%$, while for triangular flow $\delta v_3^{\rm soft}/v_3^{\rm soft}$ is approximately $20\%$ to $25\%$.
* A fixed strong coupling constant $\alpha_\mathrm{s}=0.15$ is used in the LBT model, tuned to describe experimental data on single inclusive jet spectra.
* The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is set to $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm, and the Gaussian smearing parameters for initial conditions are $\sigma_r=0.6$ fm and $\sigma_{\eta_s}=0.6$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet quenching and jet anisotropy by combining the LBT model with event-by-event (3+1)D CLVisc hydrodynamics, building on the framework that previously described single inclusive jet suppression (He, 2018) and gamma/Z-jet correlations (Luo, 2018; Zhang, 2018). The study addresses the longstanding "v2 puzzle" where observed hadron anisotropy at intermediate transverse momentum exceeds simple jet quenching model predictions, and it enables future hard-soft tomography of the QGP by correlating jet anisotropy with bulk flow coefficients. The approach contrasts with earlier work by Betz (2011), Zigic (2018, 2019), Andres (2019), and Noronha-Hostler (2016) that focused on simultaneous description of hadron suppression and anisotropies, and with proposals involving magnetic monopoles (Xu, 2014, 2015; Shi, 2019) or singular temperature dependence of the jet transport coefficient.
