# Nuclear dynamics in time-dependent picture

> Using the time-dependent theory of quantum mechanics, we investigate nuclear electric dipole responses. The time evolution of a wave function is explicitly calculated in the coordinate-space representation. The particle continuum is treated with the absorbing boundary condition. Calculated time-dependent quantities are transformed into those of familiar energy representation. We apply the method to a three-body model for 11Li and to the mean-field model for 22O, then discuss properties of E1 response.

## Key claim

The time-dependent method with an absorbing boundary condition provides an efficient and practical approach for calculating nuclear response functions over a wide energy range, particularly for continuum states, but the energy resolution is approximately inversely proportional to the propagation time (ΔE ≈ ħ/T), and the absorbing boundary condition is only an approximate outgoing boundary condition.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation in coordinate-space representation, using an absorbing complex potential (-iW(r)) to impose an approximate outgoing boundary condition for continuum states. For the ¹¹Li three-body model, they propagate the state |Ψ̃^μ(t)⟩ = e^{-iHt/ħ} M_{1μ} |Ψ₀⟩ and compute the B(E1) strength via Fourier transform of the time-correlation function. For ²²O, they perform time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations with the Skyrme SGII functional on a 3D Cartesian mesh, applying an instantaneous E1 external field and Fourier transforming the resulting dipole moment with a complex energy (E + iΓ, Γ = 1 MeV) to smooth the discretized continuum.

## Key numbers

- ¹¹Li: two-neutron separation energy of 0.3 MeV used to adjust the depth of the neutron-⁹Li potential.
- ¹¹Li: calculated E1 peak position at E_x = 0.8 MeV, compared to experimental peak at E_x = 0.6 MeV from Nak06.
- ²²O: time evolution calculated up to t = 20 ħ/MeV.
- ²²O: lowest calculated peak at 13 MeV, compared to experimental low-energy peak around E_x = 9 MeV from Leis01.
- ²²O: main peak position around E_x = 20 MeV, consistent with relativistic RPA calculation from VPRL01.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent approach with absorbing boundary conditions previously developed by Nakatsukasa and Yabana (2001, 2005) to nuclear response calculations. For ¹¹Li, it addresses inconsistencies among earlier Coulomb breakup measurements at MSU (Ieki 1993), RIKEN (Shim 1995), and GSI (Zins 1997), and compares with new high-statistics data from RIKEN (Nak 2006). For ²²O, the TDHF results are compared with relativistic RPA calculations (VPRL 2001) and experimental data from GSI (Leis 2001). The method enables efficient calculation of strength functions over broad energy ranges without diagonalizing large RPA matrices, which is computationally expensive in 3D coordinate space (Muta 2002, IH 2003, Ina 2004).
