# Isospin-symmetry restoration within the nuclear density functional theory: formalism and applications

> Isospin symmetry of atomic nuclei is explicitly broken by the charge-dependent interactions, primarily the Coulomb force. Within the nuclear density functional theory, isospin is also broken spontaneously. We propose a projection scheme rooted in a mean field theory, that allows the consistent treatment of isospin breaking in both ground and exited nuclear states. We demonstrate that this scheme is essentially free from spurious divergences plaguing particle-number and angular-momentum restoration approaches. Applications of the new technique include excited high-spin states in medium-mass N=Z nuclei, such as superdeformed bands and many-particle-many-hole terminating states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an isospin-projected density functional theory (DFT) scheme that allows consistent treatment of isospin breaking in both ground and excited nuclear states, and demonstrates that this scheme is essentially free from spurious divergences that plague particle-number and angular-momentum restoration approaches, though the method cannot fully account for the ground-state isospin of odd-odd N=Z nuclei without simultaneous angular-momentum projection and residual proton-neutron interactions.

## Method

The authors implement an isospin projection-after-variation technique within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) framework, where the total effective nuclear Hamiltonian (including kinetic energy, Skyrme interaction, and Coulomb interaction decomposed into isoscalar, isovector, and isotensor components) is rediagonalized in a good-isospin basis generated by acting with the one-dimensional isospin-projection operator on the mean-field product state. The Hamiltonian and norm kernels are calculated using the generalized Wick's theorem for non-orthogonal Slater determinants, with the overlap matrix inverted analytically via singular value decomposition to demonstrate that singularities only occur at β=π and are compensated by the Jacobian sinβ dβ. The method is applied to superdeformed bands in ⁵⁶Ni and terminating states in N=Z isotopes of Ca, Sc, Ti, V, and Cr.

## Key numbers

- The isospin projection scheme retains states having |b_{T,T_z}|² > 10⁻¹⁰, which sets the limit of T ≤ |T_z| + 5
- The isovector kinetic energy is quenched relative to its isoscalar counterpart by a factor ΔM/M ∼ 0.001 due to the small mass difference between neutron and proton relative to mean nucleonic mass
- Band 2 in ⁵⁶Ni becomes yrast around spin I ∼ 12ħ
- The π[440]1/2 band in ⁵⁶Ni crosses Band 1 above spin I ∼ 16ħ in HF calculations, well above the empirical crossing
- The isospin of ground states changes from T=0 in light (A < 40) odd-odd N=Z nuclei to T=1 in heavier (A > 40) odd-odd N=Z nuclei

## Context

This work extends the nuclear DFT framework by addressing isospin symmetry breaking that is both explicit (primarily from Coulomb force) and spontaneous (from the mean-field approximation), building on earlier projection techniques by Cauvin (1980, 1982) and the authors' own recent work (Rafalska 2009, Satula 2009). The method enables consistent treatment of excited high-spin states in medium-mass N=Z nuclei such as superdeformed bands in ⁵⁶Ni (observed by Rudolph 1999) and terminating states in the A∼40 region (studied by Zaluska 2007), where previous mean-field approaches predicted spurious degenerate proton and neutron excitations that are not observed experimentally.
