# Is the analysis of flow at the CERN SPS reliable?

> Several heavy ion experiments at SPS have measured azimuthal distributions of particles with respect to the reaction plane. These distributions are deduced from two-particle azimuthal correlations under the assumption that they result solely from correlations with the reaction plane. In this paper, we investigate other sources of azimuthal correlations: transverse momentum conservation, which produces back-to-back correlations, resonance decays, HBT correlations and final state interactions. These correlations increase with impact parameter: most of them vary with the multiplicity N like 1/N. When they are taken into account, the experimental results of the NA49 collaboration at SPS are significantly modified. These correlations might also explain an important fraction of the pion directed flow observed by WA98. Data should be reanalyzed taking into account carefully these non--flow correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that non-flow azimuthal correlations (transverse momentum conservation, resonance decays, HBT correlations, and final state interactions) are typically of order 1/N and comparable in magnitude to the flow signals measured at SPS, so that published flow results from NA49 and WA98 may be significantly contaminated or even reversed in sign when these correlations are properly subtracted.

## Method

The authors decompose measured two-particle azimuthal correlations into a flow term (product of single-particle Fourier coefficients v_n) and a non-flow term from direct two-particle correlations. They analytically calculate the contribution from transverse momentum conservation using the condition that total transverse momentum is zero, estimate resonance decay correlations from Δ→pπ and ρ→ππ decays using Monte Carlo simulations with exponential transverse mass spectra, and evaluate small-angle correlations from HBT and final state interactions based on published correlation measurements. They then subtract these non-flow contributions from the published NA49 and WA98 correlation data to obtain corrected flow values.

## Key numbers

- Non-flow correlations are typically of order 1/N, where N ~ 2500 for central Pb-Pb at SPS, giving c_n^(non-flow) ~ 4×10^(-4)
- NA49 measured directed and elliptic flow of ~3%, giving c_n^(measured) ~ 9×10^(-4), only a factor of 2 larger than non-flow
- For NA49 protons, subtracting momentum conservation alone reverses the sign of directed flow v_1 from positive to negative for almost all p_T
- For Δ→pπ decays, the average ⟨cos(φ_1-φ_2)⟩ is large and positive for inverse slope parameter T ~ 300 MeV, while for ρ→ππ decays with T ~ 270 MeV both c_1 and c_2 are slightly negative
- For WA98 semi-central collisions, |v_1| ~ 0.1 for both pions and protons, corresponding to |c_1^(measured)| ~ 10^(-2), an order of magnitude larger than for NA49
- For WA98 protons, momentum conservation gives c_1^(Σp_T) ~ -4×10^(-4) for p_T ~ 400 MeV, much smaller than c_1^(measured) ~ 10^(-2)
- WA98 pion v_1 reaches values as high as 0.3 for very peripheral collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of HBT correlations (Borghini, Dinh, Ollitrault 2000) to include momentum conservation, resonance decays, and final state interactions as additional sources of non-flow correlations. It directly challenges the standard flow analysis assumption dating back to Danielewicz and Odyniec (1985) that two-particle azimuthal correlations arise solely from correlations with the reaction plane. The paper enables reanalysis of NA49 data (Data 1999) and WA98 data (WA98 1999) with proper subtraction of non-flow effects, and suggests that the centrality dependence of flow proposed as a quark-gluon plasma signature by Sorge (1999), Voloshin (1999), and Bravina (1999) may be contaminated by non-flow correlations that increase with impact parameter.
