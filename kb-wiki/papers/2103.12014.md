# Cluster structure of 21Ne and 21Na

> We study the cluster structure of 21Ne and 21Na within the framework of the cluster shell model (CSM) and show that they have a complex cluster structure with the coexistence of a 20Ne+n, 20}Ne+p structure and a 19Ne+2n, 19}F+2p structure. Seven rotational bands are identified in 21Ne and four in 21Na and assigned to single-particle cluster states, single-hole cluster states and vibrational states. The single-particle states are associated with the 20Ne+n and 20Ne+p cluster structure, while the single-hole states are associated with the 19Ne+2n and 19F+2p structure.

## Key claim

The paper argues that 21Ne and 21Na exhibit a complex coexistence of cluster structures (20Ne+n and 19Ne+2n for 21Ne; 20Ne+p and 19F+2p for 21Na), but the assignments of several rotational bands differ from prior work by Wheldon et al., particularly for two bands where the reinterpretation has a major effect on the cluster interpretation.

## Method

The authors applied the cluster shell model (CSM) to calculate single-particle intrinsic states by solving the Schrödinger equation in the potential generated by a bi-pyramidal 20Ne core with D3h symmetry. They assigned experimentally known energy levels in 21Ne and 21Na into rotational bands using the rotational formula with decoupling parameters, then compared extracted intrinsic energies and decoupling parameters to CSM calculations and to Nilsson model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Seven rotational bands identified in 21Ne and four in 21Na
- Ground state band in 21Ne: K^P = 3/2^+ with inertial parameter B = 138(4) keV, observed up to J^P = 15/2^+
- K^P = 1/2^- (2789) band in 21Ne: B = 148(12) keV, decoupling parameter a = 0.54(6), intrinsic energy ε = 2824(4) keV
- K^P = 1/2^+ (2794) band in 21Ne: B = 216(8) keV, decoupling parameter a = 1.86(4), intrinsic energy ε = 3088(29) keV
- K^P = 5/2^+ (4526) band in 21Ne: B = 131(4) keV, intrinsic energy ε = 4199(32) keV
- K^P = 1/2^- (5690) band in 21Ne: B = 174(12) keV, decoupling parameter a = -2.85(6), intrinsic energy ε = 5107(58) keV

## Context

This work extends the cluster shell model approach previously applied to 9Be, 9B (Della Rocca and Iachello, 2020) and 13C (Bijker and Iachello, 2020) to the more complex case of kα+x nuclei with x=2. It builds on the recent analysis of 20Ne as a bi-pyramidal configuration (Bijker and Iachello, 2020) and uses experimental data compiled by Firestone (2017) and Wheldon et al. (2019). The assignments differ from Wheldon et al. for bands 2, 5, and 7, and the paper enables future comparison between cluster features and large-scale shell model calculations using the SDPF interaction (Caurier et al., 2005), to be presented elsewhere (Mengoni et al., in preparation).
