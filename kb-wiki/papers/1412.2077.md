# Unitary neutron matter in the on-shell limit

> We compute the Bertsch parameter for neutron matter by using nucleon-nucleon interactions that are fully diagonal in momentum space. We analyze the on-shell limit with the similarity renormalization group and compare the results for a simple separable toy model to realistic calculations with high precision $NN$ potentials.

## Key claim

The Bertsch parameter for neutron matter is not uniquely determined by two-body scattering information alone unless the extreme on-shell limit (similarity cutoff lambda -> 0) is taken; at finite similarity cutoff, the parameter shows strong cutoff dependence, and the real unitary limit (scattering length -> -infinity and lambda -> 0) remains an open challenge.

## Method

The authors evolved nucleon-nucleon interactions using the similarity renormalization group (SRG) with the Wilson generator toward the infrared region of the similarity cutoff lambda, eventually reaching the fully on-shell limit lambda=0 via an energy shift prescription. They computed the Bertsch parameter in the Hartree-Fock approximation for a simple S-wave gaussian separable potential toy model (fitted to the np scattering length and effective range) and compared it to results from high-precision potentials (Argonne v18, Nijmegen II, N3LO 2003, N3LO 2005) including contributions from S-waves only and summing up to G-waves.

## Key numbers

- The nn scattering length is -18.5 fm and the effective range is 2.7 fm; the np scattering length in the same channel is -23.7 fm.
- The toy model parameters are C = -1.916 fm and L = 1.2 fm^-1, fitted to reproduce the scattering length and effective range.
- At lambda = 1 fm^-1, the minimum Bertsch parameter lies between xi = 0.42 and xi = 0.45 for both the toy model and high-precision potentials, occurring at Fermi momenta between 1.1 fm^-1 and 1.3 fm^-1.
- Quantum Monte Carlo calculations give xi = 0.44 (Carlson et al., 2003).

## Context

This work extends the study of the unitary Fermi gas problem originally posed by Bertsch (1999) to neutron matter, using SRG-evolved interactions to isolate on-shell physics. The results show that a simple separable potential reproduces the Bertsch parameter from sophisticated high-precision potentials at lambda = 1 fm^-1, but the strong lambda-dependence demonstrates that off-shell ambiguities must be eliminated by taking the on-shell limit lambda -> 0. The approach enables future studies of the true unitary limit (infinite scattering length and zero effective range) in neutron matter, which is relevant for understanding BCS-BEC crossover and superfluidity in low-density neutron matter.
