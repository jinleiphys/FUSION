# Search for baryon junctions in photonuclear processes and isobar collisions at RHIC

> During the early development of Quantum Chromodynamics, it was proposed that baryon number could be carried by a non-perturbative Y-shaped topology of gluon fields, called the gluon junction, rather than by the valence quarks as in the QCD standard model. A puzzling feature of ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is the apparent substantial baryon excess in the midrapidity region that could not be adequately accounted for in most conventional models of quark and diquark transport. The transport of baryonic gluon junctions is predicted to lead to a characteristic exponential distribution of net-baryon density with rapidity and could resolve the puzzle. In this context we point out that the rapidity density of net-baryons near midrapidity indeed follows an exponential distribution with a slope of $-0.61\pm0.03$ as a function of beam rapidity in the existing global data from A+A collisions at AGS, SPS and RHIC energies. To further test if quarks or gluon junctions carry the baryon quantum number, we propose to study the absolute magnitude of the baryon vs. charge stopping in isobar collisions at RHIC. We also argue that semi-inclusive photon-induced processes ($γ+p$/A) at RHIC kinematics provide an opportunity to search for the signatures of the baryon junction and to shed light onto the mechanisms of observed baryon excess in the mid-rapidity region in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. Such measurements can be further validated in A+A collisions at the LHC and $e+p$/A collisions at the EIC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed exponential dependence of mid-rapidity net-baryon density on beam rapidity in A+A collisions is consistent with the baryon-junction picture, but the authors hedge that the extracted exponent of 0.61±0.03 is only "qualitatively consistent" with the Regge theory prediction of α_J ≈ 0.42–1, and that the small difference could arise from multiple hadronic interactions in central collisions.

## Method

The authors compiled global data on inclusive net-proton yields at mid-rapidity from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC experiments and performed exponential fits to the rapidity density per participant pair as a function of beam rapidity. They then used Monte Carlo simulations with PYTHIA 6.4, BeAGLE, and UrQMD event generators to model photon-induced processes in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) at RHIC kinematics, evaluating detector acceptance and triggering strategies for the STAR experiment. The paper also proposes comparing baryon stopping versus charge stopping in isobar collisions of Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The exponential fit to mid-rapidity net-proton density per participant pair in central A+A collisions yields a slope of α_B = 0.61 ± 0.03, with normalization N_B = 1.1 ± 0.1.
- The exponent α_B is consistent across centralities: −0.63 ± 0.052 (10–20%), −0.67 ± 0.059 (30–40%), and −0.65 ± 0.082 (50–60%).
- The Regge theory prediction for the baryon-junction intercept is α_J ≈ 0.42–1, with specific values of α_J = 1 for double-baryon stopping and α_J = 0.42 for single-baryon stopping.
- For RHIC UPCs, the quasi-real photon energy range is E_γ = 0.8–2.8 GeV, and the center-of-mass energy of the photon-nucleon system is W_γN ≈ 9–34 GeV for √s_NN = 54–200 GeV.
- The virtuality of photons in UPCs is Q² < 0.0008 GeV², and the typical range is Q² < 0.01 (GeV/c)² for the simulated photoproduction processes.

## Context

This work extends the baryon-junction conjecture originally proposed by Rossi and Veneziano (1977) and Artru (1974), and later developed by Kharzeev (1996), which posits that baryon number is carried by a non-perturbative Y-shaped gluon field configuration rather than valence quarks. The paper argues that conventional models such as PYTHIA, UrQMD, AMPT, and HIJING, which use quark and diquark transport mechanisms, cannot adequately explain the observed baryon excess at mid-rapidity in heavy-ion collisions. The proposed measurements in photonuclear processes at RHIC and isobar collisions would provide new tests of the baryon-junction picture, and could be further validated at the LHC and the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).
