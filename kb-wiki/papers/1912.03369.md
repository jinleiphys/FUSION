# Production of the (predicted) $K^*(4307)$ in $B$ decays

> In this work we study the production of $K^*(4307)$ in $B$ decays by determining the $J/ψπ^{+(0)}K^{0}$ and $J/ψπ^- K^+$ invariant mass distributions of the processes $B^+\to J/ψπ^+π^0 K^0$ and $B^+\to J/ψπ^+π^- K^+$, respectively. Such $K^*(4307)$ has been recently predicted as a three-body state originated from the dynamics involved in the $K D\bar D^*$ system, with the $KD$ subsystem forming the $D^{*}_{s0}(2317)$ in isospin 0, and the $D\bar D^*$ subsystem generating the $X(3872)$ in isospin 0 and the $Z_c(3900)$ in isospin 1. The hidden charm content of $K^*(4307)$ favors its decay to a state like $J/ψπK$ and the study of $B$-decays with these particles in their final states can constitute a way of finding experimental evidences for such an exotic vector meson, whose width, in spite of its large mass, is still quite narrow (around 18 MeV).

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the exotic vector meson \(K^*(4307)\), a three-body state arising from dynamics in the \(K D \bar D^*\) system, should be observable in existing experimental data on \(B^+ \to J/\psi \pi^+ \pi^0 K^0\) and \(B^+ \to J/\psi \pi^+ \pi^- K^+\) decays, with a branching ratio of order \(10^{-8}\), provided the \(J/\psi \pi K\) invariant mass distributions are reconstructed.

## Method

The authors calculate the \(J/\psi \pi K\) invariant mass distributions for the two \(B^+\) decay channels using a formalism based on the quantum chromodynamics factorization approach for non-leptonic \(B\)-meson decays. The weak vertex \(B^+ \to J/\psi K^+\) is described by an amplitude fixed to reproduce the measured branching ratio, while the strong vertices for \(Z_c(3900) \to J/\psi \pi\) and \(K^*(4307) \to K Z_c(3900)\) are included via coupling constants obtained from previous models (Aceti 2014, Ren 2018). The loop integrals involved in the triangular diagrams are regularized with a cut-off of 700 MeV for the center-of-mass momentum of the \(K\)-\(Z_c\) system.

## Key numbers

- The predicted mass of the \(K^*(4307)\) state is around 4300 MeV, with a width of approximately 18 MeV.
- The branching ratio for the process \(B^+ \to \pi^{0(+)} K^{*+(0)}(4307) \to \pi^{0(+)} K^{0(+)} Z_c^{+(0)}(3900) \to \pi^{0(+)} K^0 J/\psi \pi^{+(0)}\) is \(\mathcal{BR} = (1.05 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-8}\).
- The coupling constant used for \(Z_c(3900) \to J/\psi \pi\) is \(g_{Z_c \to J/\psi \pi} = 3715\) MeV, and for \(K^*(4307) \to K Z_c(3900)\) is \(g_{K^* \to K Z_c} = 22143\) MeV.
- The measured branching ratio for \(B^+ \to J/\psi K^+\) used to fix the weak vertex is \((1.01 \pm 0.028) \times 10^{-3}\) (Tanabashi 2018).

## Context

This work extends the prediction of the \(K^*(4307)\) as a three-body state from the \(K D \bar D^*\) system (Ma 2017, Ren 2018) by proposing a concrete experimental search channel in \(B\) decays. The study is motivated by the success of \(B\)-decay analyses in discovering other exotic states such as \(X(3872)\) (Choi 2003, Aaij 2013) and \(Z_c(3900)\) (Aceti 2014), and it suggests that reconstructing \(J/\psi \pi K\) invariant mass distributions from existing data could reveal this narrow, hidden-charm vector meson. The work enables experimental verification of a six-quark meson state and opens exploration of the energy region above 3100 MeV where no excited kaon states are currently known.
