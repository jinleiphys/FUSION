# Study of N = 16 shell closure within RMF+BCS approach

> We have employed RMF+BCS (relativistic mean-field plus BCS) ap- proach to study behaviour of N = 16 shell closure with the help of ground state properties of even-even nuclei. Our present investigations include sin- gle particle energies, deformations, separation energies as well as pairing energies etc. As per recent experiments showing neutron magicity at N = 16 for O isotopes, our results indicate a strong shell closure at N = 16 in 22C and 24O. A large gap is found in between neutron 2s1/2 and 1d3/2 states for 22C and 24O. These results are also supported by a sharp increase in two neutron shell gap, zero pairing energy contribution and with excellent agreement with available experimental data. Moreover, our calculations of N = 16 isotones are however found at variance for higher Z isotones like 36Ca, where experiments show high lying first excited 2+ state indicating shell closure at N = 16.

# Study of N = 16 shell closure within RMF+BCS approach

## Key claim
The RMF+BCS calculations indicate a strong shell closure at N = 16 for 22C and 24O, supported by a large gap between neutron 2s1/2 and 1d3/2 states, a sharp increase in two-neutron shell gap, and zero pairing energy. However, the same calculations are at variance with experimental data for higher-Z isotones such as 36Ca, where experiments show a high-lying first excited 2+ state indicating shell closure at N = 16.

## Method
The authors employed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with nonlinear terms for sigma and omega mesons, using the TMA force parameter, combined with a state-dependent BCS calculation where the continuum was replaced by a set of positive energy states generated by enclosing the nucleus in a spherical box. Single-particle energies, deformations, separation energies, and pairing energies were calculated for even-even nuclei along the N = 16 isotonic chain and for carbon and oxygen isotopes.

## Key numbers
- The gap between neutron 2s1/2 and 1d3/2 states is approximately 3.5 MeV for 22C and 3.3 MeV for 24O.
- Two-neutron shell gap shows an abrupt increase moving from N = 14 to N = 16 for both carbon and oxygen isotopes, in accord with experimental data for oxygen isotopes.
- Pairing energy vanishes for 12C, 14C, 22C and 14O, 16O, 24O at N = 6, 8, and 16 respectively.

## Context
This work extends the study of new magic numbers emerging in neutron-rich nuclei, following experimental observations of N = 16 magicity in 24O by Robert (2008) and Kanungo (2009), and the N = 32 sub-shell closure in 52Ca by Wien (2013). The RMF+BCS approach used here was previously developed by Singh (2014) and Saxena (2015). The results support N = 16 shell closure for 22C and 24O but disagree with experimental findings for 36Ca by Door (2013) and for 30Si and 32S by Wang (2014), indicating that further investigations are needed for a consistent description of the isotonic chain in terms of parameters, pairing, and isospin.
