# Rapidly Spinning Massive Pulsars as an Indicator of Quark Deconfinement

> We study rotating hybrid stars, with particular emphasis on the effect of spin on the deconfinement phase transition and star properties. Our analysis is based on a hybrid equation of state with a phase transition from hadronic matter containing hyperons to color-superconducting quark matter, where the quark phase is modeled within a relativistic density functional approach. By varying the strength of the vector repulsion and diquark pairing couplings in the microscopic quark Lagrangian, we construct a set of hybrid star sequences with different quark-matter onset densities. This framework ensures consistency with astrophysical and gravitational wave constraints on mass, radius, and tidal deformability.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the fastest-spinning and most massive known Galactic neutron star, PSR J0952–0607, can be explained as a hybrid star containing a color-superconducting quark-matter core, but only for the most favorable parameter sets with strong vector coupling; this pulsar cannot be explained by the purely hadronic equation of state considered in this study.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state using the Maxwell construction for a phase transition from hadronic matter (DD2npY-T, including nucleons and hyperons) to two-flavor color-superconducting quark matter modeled with a relativistic density functional approach. They vary the vector repulsion strength (eta_V) and diquark pairing coupling (eta_D) to generate hybrid star sequences with different quark-matter onset densities, then solve the Einstein equations for rotating stars using the RNS code to compute properties up to the Kepler (mass-shedding) frequency.

## Key numbers

- The maximum gravitational mass of static hybrid stars reaches up to 2.2 solar masses for the chosen parameter sets (eta_V=0.30, eta_D=0.733-0.76)
- Using the 716 Hz spin frequency of PSR J1748-2446ad, the authors derive an upper limit on the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R_1.4 ≤ 14.90 km
- The same analysis yields an upper limit on the radius of a 0.7 solar mass star: R_0.7 < 11.49 km
- The non-axisymmetric f-mode instability threshold is at T/W ≈ 0.08, above which rotating neutron star configurations are ruled out
- The accretion model uses a typical accretion rate of 10^{-7} solar masses per year and a magnetic field decay time of 10^8 years

## Context

This work extends the hybrid equation of state framework developed by Gärtlein et al. (2023, 2024) to rapidly rotating neutron stars, building on earlier studies of hybrid stars by Ippolito et al. (2007), Dhiman et al. (2010), Ayvazyan et al. (2013), Bhattacharyya et al. (2017), and Largani et al. (2021). The results are constrained by NICER observations (Salmi et al., 2024; Dittmann et al., 2024; Choudhury et al., 2024; Vinciguerra et al., 2023; Miller et al., 2019) and gravitational wave data. The paper enables future tests with the Square Kilometre Array (SKA), which will expand the known pulsar population and allow precise measurements of masses and spin frequencies to distinguish hybrid from purely hadronic neutron stars.
