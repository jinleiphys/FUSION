# Lorentz Boosted Nucleon-Nucleon T-matrix and the Triton Binding Energy

> The phase equivalent relativistic NN potential, which is related by a nonlinear equation to the original nonrelativistic potential, is used to construct the mass operator (rest Hamiltonian) of the 3-nucleon system. Employing the CD Bonn NN potential, the solution of the relativistic 3N Faddeev equation for $^3$H shows slightly less binding energy than the corresponding nonrelativistic result. The effect of the Wigner spin rotation on the binding is very small.

## Key claim

The relativistic calculation of the triton binding energy using the CD Bonn potential yields a value about 100 keV smaller than the nonrelativistic result, which is significantly less than a previously published reduction of about 400 keV that was attributed to an incorrect construction of the relativistic off-shell t-matrix.

## Method

The authors constructed a phase-equivalent relativistic NN potential from the nonrelativistic CD Bonn potential using a nonlinear operator relation, then derived the corresponding boosted two-body potential for use in the three-body mass operator. They solved the relativistic three-nucleon Faddeev equation for the triton bound state using the boosted off-shell t-matrix obtained from a relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and compared the result to the nonrelativistic Faddeev calculation with the same input interaction.

## Key numbers

- Nonrelativistic triton binding energy with np force only (34 channels): -8.247 MeV
- Relativistic triton binding energy with np force only (34 channels): -8.147 MeV
- Difference between nonrelativistic and relativistic results with np force only: 0.100 MeV (100 keV)
- Relativistic triton binding energy including charge independence breaking (np+nn forces): -7.916 MeV
- Difference between nonrelativistic and relativistic results including CIB: 0.089 MeV (89 keV)
- Wigner spin rotation effect on relativistic binding energy: approximately 2 keV reduction (from -7.916 to -7.914 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré-invariant approach to three-nucleon systems pioneered by Coester and collaborators, and corrects an earlier overestimate of relativistic effects by Kamada (2002) that gave a 400 keV reduction due to an incorrect scaling transformation. The results contradict field-theory-based calculations by Stadler (2005) that showed an increase in binding energy. The formulation has been applied to low-energy neutron-deuteron scattering (Witala, 2008) and to exclusive proton-deuteron scattering at 508 MeV (Lin, 2007, 2008), and can be extended to electromagnetic processes.
