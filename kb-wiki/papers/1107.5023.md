# The I=2 pipi S-wave Scattering Phase Shift from Lattice QCD

> The pi+pi+ s-wave scattering phase-shift is determined below the inelastic threshold using Lattice QCD. Calculations were performed at a pion mass of m_pi~390 MeV with an anisotropic n_f=2+1 clover fermion discretization in four lattice volumes, with spatial extent L~2.0, 2.5, 3.0 and 3.9 fm, and with a lattice spacing of b_s~0.123 fm in the spatial direction and b_t b_s/3.5 in the time direction. The phase-shift is determined from the energy-eigenvalues of pi+pi+ systems with both zero and non-zero total momentum in the lattice volume using Luscher's method. Our calculations are precise enough to allow for a determination of the threshold scattering parameters, the scattering length a, the effective range r, and the shape-parameter P, in this channel and to examine the prediction of two-flavor chiral perturbation theory: m_pi^2 a r = 3+O(m_pi^2/Lambda_chi^2). Chiral perturbation theory is used, with the Lattice QCD results as input, to predict the scattering phase-shift (and threshold parameters) at the physical pion mass. Our results are consistent with determinations from the Roy equations and with the existing experimental phase shift data.

## Key claim

The I=2 ππ S-wave scattering phase shift is determined from lattice QCD at a single pion mass of mπ ~ 390 MeV, and chiral perturbation theory is then used to predict the phase shift at the physical pion mass, with results consistent with Roy equation determinations and experimental data, though the lattice calculations themselves are performed at only one unphysical pion mass.

## Method

The authors used nf=2+1 anisotropic clover fermion discretization on four lattice volumes with spatial extents L ~ 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, and 3.9 fm and a spatial lattice spacing of bs ~ 0.123 fm. Energy eigenvalues of π+π+ systems with zero and non-zero total momentum were computed on these lattices, and Luscher's finite-volume method was applied to extract the s-wave scattering phase shift from the measured energy levels.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: mπ ~ 390 MeV
- Spatial lattice spacing: bs = 0.1227 ± 0.0008 fm
- Anisotropy parameter: ξ = 3.469(11)
- Scattering length from Fit A (k²/mπ² < 0.5): mπ a = 0.230(10)(16)
- Effective range from Fit A: mπ r = 12.9(1.5)(2.9)
- Product mπ² a r from Fit A: 2.95(20)(42)
- Scattering length from Fit B (k²/mπ² < 1): mπ a = 0.226(10)(16)
- Effective range from Fit B: mπ r = 18.1(2.4)(4.7)
- Shape parameter from Fit B: P = -0.00123(30)(55)
- Phase shift at k²/mπ² = 0.0715(32)(48) on the 16³×128 ensemble: δ = -4.03(25)(35) degrees
- Phase shift at k²/mπ² = 1.582(9)(16) on the 32³×256 ensemble: δ = -46.5(2.3)(3.5) degrees

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD determinations of π+π+ scattering by the CP-PACS collaboration (Yamazaki 2003, 2004) and the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Dudek 2010), using chiral perturbation theory to interpolate to the physical pion mass rather than polynomial extrapolation. The results are compared with Roy equation determinations by Colangelo (2001) and Garcia-Martin (2011), and the lattice data allow determination of the shape parameter P for the first time in this channel, enabling next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory predictions of the phase shift up to the inelastic threshold at the physical pion mass.
