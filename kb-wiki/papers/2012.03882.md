# Emergence of N-body tunable interactions in universal few-atom system

> A three-atom molecule AAB, formed by two identical bosons A and a distinct one B, is studied by considering coupled channels close to a Feshbach resonance. It is assumed that the subsystems AB and AA have, respectively, one and two channels, where, in this case, AA has open and closed channels separated by an energy gap. The induced three-body interaction appearing in the single channel description is derived using the Feshbach projection operators for the open and closed channels. An effective three-body interaction is revealed in the limit where the trap setup is tuned to vanishing scattering lengths . The corresponding homogeneous coupled Faddeev integral equations are derived in the unitarity limit. The s-wave transition matrix for the AA subsystem is obtained with a zero-range potential by a subtractive renormalization scheme with the introduction of two finite parameters, besides the energy gap. The effect of the coupling between the channels in the coupled equations is identified with the energy gap, which essentially provides an ultraviolet scale that competes with the van der Waals radius - this sets the short-range physics of the system in the open channel. The competition occurring at short distances exemplifies the violation of the ``van der Waals universality" for narrow Feshbach resonances in cold atomic setups. In this sense, the active role of the energy gap drives the short-range three-body physics.

# Emergence of N-body tunable interactions in universal few-atom systems

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a three-atom AAB system (two identical bosons A and a distinct atom B) near a Feshbach resonance, the coupling between open and closed channels generates an effective three-body interaction that persists even when the two-body scattering length is tuned to zero, contradicting the expectation that only two-body interactions matter in that limit. The energy gap between open and closed channels provides an ultraviolet scale that competes with the van der Waals radius, violating "van der Waals universality" for narrow Feshbach resonances.

## Method

The authors formulate the three-body bound state problem using Faddeev equations with coupled open and closed channels, applying Feshbach projection operators to derive the effective single-channel description. They derive the s-wave transition matrix for the AA subsystem using a zero-range potential with a subtractive renormalization scheme, introducing two finite parameters (the coupling strength η and the closed-channel interaction strength λ) plus the energy gap e. The coupled Faddeev integral equations are then derived explicitly for the AAB system in the unitarity limit (scattering lengths a → ±∞), with the AA subsystem having one open and one closed channel while the AB subsystem has only a single open channel.

## Key numbers

- The effective range expansion parameter R* is given by R* = 1/(4π⁴ η² e√e), where η is the coupling strength between open and closed channels and e is the energy gap
- The three-body binding energy at unitarity in the single-channel framework is proportional to μ², where μ ∼ 1/ℓ_vdW is the ultraviolet scale
- The ratio a₀/ℓ_vdW ∼ -9 is cited as the verified value for broad Feshbach resonances from prior experimental work (BerPRL2011, 2017chin)
- The subtracted kernel uses μ² >> e as the regularization condition

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework sketched in Yamashita et al. (2006) by providing the full Faddeev formalism for the AAB bound state with coupled open-closed channels. It addresses experimental observations from 2017chin showing that the position of Efimov recombination resonances in ⁶Li-¹³³Cs mixtures deviates from single-channel "van der Waals universality" predictions for narrow Feshbach resonances, which the authors interpret as a manifestation of induced three-atom attraction. The formalism enables future studies of how the parameters η and e can be used to control Efimov states and potentially extend to N-body tunable interactions in cold atomic systems.
