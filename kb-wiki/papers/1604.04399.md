# Magnetic hexadecapole gamma transitions and neutrino-nuclear responses in medium heavy nuclei

> Neutrino-nuclear responses in the form of squares of nuclear matrix elements,NMEs, are crucial for studies of neutrino-induced processes in nuclei. In this work we investigate magnetic hexadecapole (M4) NMEs in medium-heavy nuclei. The experimentally derived NMEs, $M_{\rm EXP}$(M4), deduced from observed M4 $γ$ transition half-lives are compared with the single-quasiparticle (QP) NMEs, $M_{\rm QP}$(M4), and the microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model (MQPM) NMEs $M_{\rm MQPM}$(M4). The experimentally driven M4 NMEs are found to be reduced by a coefficient $k \approx 0.29 $ with respect to $M_{\rm QP}$(M4) and by $k \approx 0.33$ with respect to $M_{\rm MQPM}$(M4). The M4 NMEs are reduced a little by the quasiparticle-phonon correlations of the MQPM wave functions but mainly by other nucleonic and non-nucleonic correlations which are not explicitly included in the MQPM. The found reduction rates are of the same order of magnitude as those for magnetic quadrupole $γ$ transitions and Gamow-Teller (GT) and spin-dipole (SD) $β$ transitions. The impact of the found reduction coefficients on the magnitudes of the NMEs involved in astroneutrino interactions and neutrinoless double beta decays are discussed.

## Key claim

The experimentally derived magnetic hexadecapole (M4) nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) in medium-heavy nuclei are reduced by a coefficient of approximately 0.29 relative to single-quasiparticle (QP) NMEs and by approximately 0.33 relative to microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model (MQPM) NMEs, with the reduction attributed mainly to nucleonic and non-nucleonic spin-isospin correlations not explicitly included in the MQPM, rather than to the quasiparticle-phonon correlations themselves.

## Method

The authors extracted experimental M4 gamma transition NMEs from measured half-lives for stretched M4 transitions in odd-mass nuclei across two mass regions (A=70-120 and A=130-150), where the dominant single-quasiparticle transitions are 1g9/2-2p1/2 and 1h11/2-2d3/2, respectively. They compared these experimental NMEs with single-quasiparticle NMEs calculated using BCS wave functions with Woods-Saxon single-particle energies, and with MQPM NMEs that include quasiparticle-phonon coupling via QRPA phonons built on even-even reference nuclei, using a residual Hamiltonian derived from the Bonn-A one-boson-exchange potential.

## Key numbers

- Experimental M4 NMEs for the A=70-120 region are around (0.6 ± 0.1) × 10³ fm³; for the A=130-150 region they are around (1.0 ± 0.1) × 10³ fm³.
- The reduction coefficient k for experimental NMEs relative to single-QP NMEs is k ≈ 0.29 ± 0.05, with k_p ≈ 0.3 and k_n ≈ 0.3 for odd-proton and odd-neutron transitions respectively.
- The reduction coefficient for experimental NMEs relative to MQPM NMEs is k ≈ 0.33.
- MQPM NMEs are 10-20% smaller than the corresponding single-QP NMEs.
- The M4 reduction coefficient k(M4) ≈ 0.29 is compared to k(M2) ≈ 0.24 for magnetic quadrupole transitions, k(GT) ≈ 0.235 for Gamow-Teller beta decays, and k(SD) ≈ 0.18 for spin-dipole beta decays.
- The experimental M4 NMEs are approximately described by M_EXP(M4) ≈ 6 × A fm³, where A is the mass number.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quenching in axial-vector nuclear matrix elements, specifically the reductions observed for magnetic quadrupole (M2) gamma transitions and Gamow-Teller (GT) and spin-dipole (SD) beta transitions reported by Ejiri et al. (2014, 2015). The finding that M4 NMEs are reduced by a similar factor (k ≈ 0.2-0.3) suggests a universal reduction mechanism for axial-vector NMEs across multipolarities λ=1-4, relevant for both neutral-current and charged-current processes. This enables improved estimates of higher-multipole contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay NMEs and astro-neutrino interactions, where the effective axial-vector coupling g_A^eff may need to be quenched to approximately 0.3 (in units of bare g_A=1.26 g_V) when using the quasiparticle model, or to approximately 0.6 when using models that explicitly include nucleonic spin-isospin correlations.
