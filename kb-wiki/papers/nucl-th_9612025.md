# Color Transparency at ELFE

> Report on the Color Transparency parallel session held during the second ELFE workshop, Saint Malo, France, September 1996, to be published in the Proceedings, N. d'Hose et al ed.(Nuclear Physics A). e-mail: pire@orphee.polytechnique.fr and SICK@ubaclu.unibas.ch

## Key claim

Color Transparency (CT) effects can be observed in deuteron electrodisintegration by measuring a reduction of final-state interactions (FSI) between the recoiling "small" Fock state and the spectator neutron, rather than by measuring the overall nuclear transparency. This approach avoids the problem of the short lifetime of the small Fock state, which at practical momentum transfers is comparable to the internucleon distance rather than the nuclear size.

## Method

The authors review a proposed (e,e'p) experiment on the deuteron, selecting kinematics where the Bjorken scaling variable x=1 and the unobserved neutron carries a momentum of ~600 MeV/c. The signal for CT is a reduction of the FSI between the recoiling proton (initially in a small Fock state) and the neutron, compared to the FSI calculated from the standard nucleon-nucleon interaction. Laget and Voutier studied the accessible kinematic range at a high-energy, high-luminosity facility, introducing the change of the interaction cross section due to CT and the effects of finite coherence length using standard parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic region studied for the D(e,e'p) reaction covers momentum transfers Q² = 6–16 GeV²/c².
- At these Q² values, the normal nucleonic FSI changes the cross section by factors of 2 to 3.
- The proposed experiment requires an electron beam energy in the several-10 GeV range and a luminosity of 10³⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹.
- Expected count rates are of the order of several 10 per hour.
- The spectator neutron momentum is selected at ~600 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier CT studies (e.g., Anklin et al., first letter of intent for ELFE) that focused on (e,e'p) transparency in heavier nuclei, and complements theoretical developments by Piller and Kopeliovich on vector meson electroproduction. The proposed deuteron experiment enables an unambiguous CT signal because the deuteron wave function is well known up to 350 MeV/c (with limits being extended by ongoing experiments at NIKHEF, MAMI, and CEBAF), and only single and double scattering need be considered. Future experience from CEBAF and HERMES runs will inform the design of ELFE experiments.
