# Theoretical study of the direct $־±+d$ $\rightarrow$ $^6$Li + $־³$ astrophysical capture process in a three-body model II. Reaction rates and primordial abundance

> The astrophysical S-factor and reaction rate of the direct capture process $־±+d$ $\rightarrow$ $^6$Li + $־³$, as well as the abundance of the $^6$Li element are estimated in a three-body model. The initial state is factorized into the deuteron bound state and the $־±+d$ scattering state. The final nucleus $^6$Li(1+) is described as a three-body bound state $־±+n+p$ in the hyperspherical Lagrange-mesh method. Corrections to the asymptotics of the overlap integral in the S- and D-waves have been done for the E2 S-factor. The isospin forbidden E1 S-factor is calculated from the initial isosinglet states to the small isotriplet components of the final $^6$Li(1+) bound state. It is shown that the three-body model is able to reproduce the newest experimental data of the LUNA collaboration for the astrophysical S-factor and the reaction rates within the experimental error bars. The estimated $^6$Li/H abundance ratio of $(0.67 \pm 0.01)\times 10^{-14}$ is in a very good agreement with the recent measurement $(0.80 \pm 0.18)\times 10^{-14}$ of the LUNA collaboration.

## Key claim

The three-body model, with corrected asymptotics of the overlap integral for the E2 transition and a properly calculated isospin-forbidden E1 transition, reproduces the newest LUNA experimental data for the astrophysical S-factor and reaction rates within experimental error bars, yielding a primordial ^6Li/H abundance ratio consistent with the LUNA measurement and lower than predictions from models using the exact mass prescription.

## Method

The initial state is factorized into the deuteron bound state (solved with the Minnesota NN potential on a Lagrange-Laguerre mesh) and the ־±-d scattering state (calculated with a deep Dubovichenko potential modified in the S-wave). The final ^6Li(1+) ground state is described as a three-body ־±+n+p bound state using the hyperspherical Lagrange-mesh method with two different ־±-N potentials (Voronchev et al. Model A and Kanada et al. Model B). Corrections to the asymptotics of the S-wave and D-wave overlap integrals are applied at a matching distance of 5.5 fm to improve the E2 S-factor, while the isospin-forbidden E1 S-factor is calculated from initial isosinglet states to the small isotriplet components of the final ^6Li(1+) bound state.

## Key numbers

- S-wave asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for Model A: C_־±d^(0) = 2.116 fm^(-1/2) at matching point R_0 = 5.5 fm; for Model B: C_־±d^(0) = 2.051 fm^(-1/2).
- D-wave ANC for Model A: between 2.160ֳ—10^(-2) and 2.175ֳ—10^(-2) fm^(-1/2) for matching points from 5.5 to 7.5 fm; for Model B: between 2.179ֳ—10^(-2) and 2.188ֳ—10^(-2) fm^(-1/2).
- Squared norm of the isotriplet component in the ^6Li ground state: 5.3ֳ—10^(-3) for Model A; 4.2ֳ—10^(-3) for Model B.
- Matter r.m.s. radius of the ^6Li ground state: approximately 2.25 fm (Model A) or 2.24 fm (Model B), compared to the experimental value 2.32 ֲ± 0.03 fm.
- Reaction rate at T_9 = 0.010: Model A gives N_a(ֿƒv) = 1.79ֳ—10^(-13) cm^3 mol^(-1) s^(-1); Model B gives 1.25ֳ—10^(-13) cm^3 mol^(-1) s^(-1).
- Reaction rate at T_9 = 1.000: Model A gives N_a(ֿƒv) = 6.56ֳ—10^(-2) cm^3 mol^(-1) s^(-1); Model B gives 5.70ֳ—10^(-2) cm^3 mol^(-1) s^(-1).
- Estimated ^6Li/H abundance ratio: (0.67 ֲ± 0.01)ֳ—10^(-14) from Model A, consistent with the LUNA measurement of (0.80 ֲ± 0.18)ֳ—10^(-14).
- Estimated ^6Li/^7Li ratio: (1.30 ֲ± 0.12)ֳ—10^(-5), agreeing with the standard BBN model estimate from Serpico (2004).

## Context

This work extends the three-body model developed by Tursunov et al. (2016) and improved by Tursunov et al. (2018) for the ־±+d ג†’ ^6Li+־³ capture process, correcting the asymptotics of the overlap integral to enhance the E2 S-factor. The results contradict earlier theoretical estimates based on the exact mass prescription (e.g., Dubovichenko 1995, Mukhamedzhanov 2011) and are consistent with the newest direct measurements from the LUNA collaboration (2014, 2017). The calculated reaction rates and abundance ratio, lower than those from exact-mass models, further increase the discrepancy between BBN predictions and astronomical observations for primordial ^6Li, motivating future ab-initio calculations including NN-tensor forces.
