# Tensor force role in $β$ decays analyzed within the Gogny-interaction shell model

> Background: The half-life of the famous $^{14}$C $β$ decay is anomalously long, with different mechanisms: the tensor force, cross-shell mixing, and three-body forces, proposed to explain the cancellations that lead to a small transition matrix element. Purpose: We revisit and analyze the role of the tensor force for the $β$ decay of $^{14}$C as well as of neighboring isotopes. Methods: We add a tensor force to the Gogny interaction, and derive an effective Hamiltonian for shell-model calculations. The calculations were carried out in a $p$-$sd$ model space to investigate cross-shell effects. Furthermore, we decompose the wave functions according to the total orbital angular momentum $L$ in order to analyze the effects of the tensor force and cross-shell mixing. Results: The inclusion of the tensor force significantly improves the shell-model calculations of the $β$-decay properties of carbon isotopes. In particular, the anomalously slow $β$ decay of $^{14}$C can be explained by the isospin $T=0$ part of the tensor force, which changes the components of $^{14}$N with the orbital angular momentum $L=0,1$, and results in a dramatic suppression of the Gamow-Teller transition strength. At the same time, the description of other nearby $β$ decays are improved. Conclusions: Decomposition of wave function into $L$ components illuminates how the tensor force modifies nuclear wave functions, in particular suppression of $β$-decay matrix elements. Cross-shell mixing also has a visible impact on the $β$-decay strength. Inclusion of the tensor force does not seem to significantly change, however, binding energies of the nuclei within the phenomenological interaction.

# Tensor force role in β decays analyzed within the Gogny-interaction shell model

## Key claim

The anomalously slow β decay of ¹⁴C can be explained specifically by the isospin T=0 part of the tensor force, which changes the sign of the L=0 component in the ¹⁴N ground-state wave function and causes a cancellation between L=0 and L=1 contributions to the Gamow-Teller transition strength; the T=1 component is also needed to simultaneously describe nearby β decays, but the tensor force does not significantly change binding energies.

## Method

The authors added a Gogny-type tensor force (with separate isospin T=0 and T=1 channels) to the D1S Gogny interaction, derived an effective Hamiltonian, and performed shell-model calculations in a full p-sd model space (with ⁴He as the core) for carbon and nitrogen isotopes. They decomposed the initial and final wave functions into components of total orbital angular momentum L to isolate how the tensor force and cross-shell mixing affect the Gamow-Teller transition matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The ¹⁴C β decay half-life is about 5730 years, corresponding to an experimental B(GT) value of 0.354 × 10⁻⁵.
- The fitted tensor force strengths are V_T0 = 26.0 MeV and V_T1 = 34.8 MeV, determined by fitting experimental B(GT) values of ¹⁴C(0⁺₁) → ¹⁴N(1⁺₁), ¹²N(1⁺₁) → ¹²C(0⁺₁), and ¹²N(1⁺₁) → ¹²C(2⁺₁) decays.
- With both T=0 and T=1 tensor forces included (D1S+T1+T0), the calculated B(GT) for ¹⁴C → ¹⁴N is 0.400 × 10⁻⁵, compared to 1.38 without tensor force (D1S) and 0.411 with the WBP interaction.
- For ¹²N(1⁺₁) → ¹²C(2⁺₁), the D1S+T1+T0 calculation gives B(GT) = 0.0288, close to the experimental value of 0.0276, while D1S alone gives 0.112.
- For ¹⁵C(1/2⁺) → ¹⁵N(3/2⁺), the D1S+T1+T0 calculation gives B(GT) = 0.0530, compared to the experimental value of 0.501 × 10⁻³.
- The tensor force range is μ_T = 1.2 fm, and the Gaussian central force ranges in D1S are μ_i = 0.7 and 1.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of the ¹⁴C puzzle that used realistic interactions in the p-shell (Holt 2008, Holt 2009, Robson 2011, Fayache 1999) and larger p-sd space calculations with YSOX and WBP interactions (Yuan 2017). It contrasts with ab initio no-core shell model (Maris 2011) and coupled-cluster (Ekstrom 2014) calculations that found three-nucleon forces reduce the transition matrix element but still gave lifetimes an order of magnitude too large. The L-decomposition method (Johnson 2015) enables analysis of cross-shell effects in large basis spaces, and the results suggest that similar L decompositions applied to ab initio frameworks could help reconcile the roles of tensor and three-nucleon forces in explaining the ¹⁴C half-life.
