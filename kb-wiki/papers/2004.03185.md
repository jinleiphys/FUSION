# Transverse momentum dependent decorrelation in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC

> Based on A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model simulations, the transverse momentum dependent decorrelation has been studied in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, respectively. It has been found that the mix-order factorization ratio $r_{m, n}$ value deviates significantly from unity in noncentral collisions. Such effect becomes stronger with an increase in the $p_{T}$ difference $p_{T}^{a}-p_{T}^{b}$. These decorrelations are not only between the same order harmonic but also between the different order harmonic, which as a result of the initial fluctuations appear between the different phase spaces. It has also been found that the correlations involving higher powers of the flow vector yield stronger decorrelation, $r_{m|n;3}<r_{m|n;2}<r_{m|n;1}~(m=2,3; n=2,3,4,5)$, except for the weighted factorization ratio $r_{3|4;k}$. The breaking phenomenon of these factorization ratios indicated that it provides a possible observation for studying the initial fluctuation properties of heavy-ion collisions.

# Transverse momentum dependent decorrelation in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC

## Key claim
Based on A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model simulations with a specific shear viscosity of eta/s = 0.273, the mix-order factorization ratio r_{m,n} deviates significantly from unity in noncentral Pb-Pb collisions, with the effect becoming stronger as the transverse momentum difference p_T^a - p_T^b increases. The decorrelation is observed not only between same-order harmonics but also between different-order harmonics, and the correlations involving higher powers of the flow vector yield stronger decorrelation (r_{m|n;3} < r_{m|n;2} < r_{m|n;1}) except for the weighted factorization ratio r_{3|4;k}.

## Method
The study used the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model with a specific shear viscosity eta/s = 0.273 and Lund string fragmentation parameters a = 0.5, b = 0.9 GeV^{-2}, alpha_s = 0.33, and mu = 3.2 fm^{-1} to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. The mix-order factorization ratio r_{m,n} and weighted factorization ratios r_{m|n;k} were calculated using the scalar-product method with a pseudorapidity gap |Delta eta| > 2 between triggered particles (positive pseudorapidity) and associated particles (negative pseudorapidity) to suppress nonflow effects. The analysis focused on 40-50% centrality collisions and examined the p_T-dependent decorrelation for harmonics m = 2,3 and n = 2,3,4,5.

## Key numbers
- The factorization ratio r_{m,n} was studied in 40-50% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV.
- The specific shear viscosity used in the AMPT model was eta/s = 0.273.
- The Lund string fragmentation parameters were a = 0.5, b = 0.9 GeV^{-2}, alpha_s = 0.33, and mu = 3.2 fm^{-1}.
- The pseudorapidity gap applied was |Delta eta| > 2.
- The trigger particles were selected in the range 2.4 < p_T^a < 3.0 GeV/c.
- The ordering of weighted factorization ratios was r_{m|n;3} < r_{m|n;2} < r_{m|n;1} for m = 2,3 and n = 2,3,4,5, except for r_{3|4;k}.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of flow vector fluctuations using factorization ratios in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the CMS measurements at 2.76 TeV (Khachatryan 2015) and hydrodynamic calculations by Gardim (2013), Heinz (2013), and Bozek (2018). The study uses the AMPT model to investigate mix-order harmonic decorrelation, which has not been completed in previous AMPT calculations that focused only on same-order harmonics (Acharya 2017). The results indicate that the factorization ratio breaking provides a possible observable for studying initial fluctuation properties of heavy-ion collisions, though the anomalous behavior of r_{3|4;k} requires further investigation.
