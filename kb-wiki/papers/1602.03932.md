# Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of the electromagnetic and neutral-weak response functions in the quasi-elastic sector

> A quantitative understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions is demanded to achieve precise measurement of neutrino oscillations, and hence the determination of their masses. In addition, next generation detectors will be able to detect supernovae neutrinos, which are likely to shed some light on the open questions on the dynamics of core collapse. In this context, it is crucial to account for two-body meson-exchange currents along within realistic models of nuclear dynamics. We summarize our progresses towards the construction of a consistent framework, based on the Green's function Monte Carlo method, that can be exploited to accurately describe neutrino interactions with atomic nuclei in the quasi-elastic sector.

## Key claim

The Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) framework, combined with realistic two- and three-nucleon potentials and consistent one- and two-body meson-exchange currents, can accurately describe neutrino-nucleus interactions in the quasi-elastic sector at low and moderate momentum transfer (|q| less than about 500 MeV), but the nonrelativistic approximation limits applicability at higher momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors used the GFMC method to compute Euclidean response functions, Laplace transforms of the standard electromagnetic and neutral-weak response functions, for 4He and 12C at moderate momentum transfer. The nuclear Hamiltonian employed the AV18+IL7 two- and three-nucleon potentials, and the electroweak currents included both one-body operators (from nonrelativistic expansion of covariant single-nucleon currents) and two-body meson-exchange currents derived from the conventional meson-exchange formalism. The energy-dependent response functions were then recovered from the Euclidean responses via the maximum entropy technique for Bayesian inversion.

## Key numbers

- For 12C at q = 570 MeV, the electromagnetic longitudinal and transverse Euclidean response functions were computed and compared to experimental data from Jourdan (1996); the transverse response showed substantial enhancement from two-body currents over the entire imaginary-time region considered.
- For 4He at q = 500 MeV, the electromagnetic longitudinal and transverse response functions obtained from inversion of Euclidean responses showed excellent agreement with experimental data from Carlson (2002); two-body currents significantly enhanced the transverse response in the quasi-elastic peak, dip, and threshold regions.
- The 12C response function calculation at q = 570 MeV required approximately 60 million core hours on the Mira supercomputer at Argonne National Laboratory.
- The nonrelativistic framework was applied for momentum transfers |q| less than about 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier GFMC calculations of electroweak sum rules in 12C (Lovato 2013, Lovato 2013b), which showed that roughly 30% of transverse strength arises from two-body currents, and that interference between one- and two-body matrix elements is important, effects only partially captured in perturbative or mean-field studies such as Martini (2009) and Nieves (2011). The results enable first-principles calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for light nuclei up to 12C, providing a framework to reduce systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments like DUNE and in neutrino-less double beta decay searches.
