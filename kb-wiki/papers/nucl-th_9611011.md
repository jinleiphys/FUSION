# New QCD Sum Rules for Nucleon Axial Vector Coupling Constants

> Two new sets of QCD sum rules for the nucleon axial coupling constants are derived using the external-field technique and generalized interpolating fields. An in-depth study of the predicative ability of these sum rules is carried out using a Monte-Carlo based uncertainty analysis. The results show that the standard implementation of the QCD sum rule method has only marginal predicative power for the nucleon axial coupling constants, as the relative errors are large. The errors range from approximately 50 to 100% compared to the nucleon mass obtained from the same method, which has only 10% to 25% error. The origin of the large errors is examined. Previous analyses of these coupling constants are based on sum rules that have poor OPE convergence and large continuum contributions. Preferred sum rules are identified and their predictions are obtained. We also investigate the new sum rules with an alternative treatment of the problematic transitions which are not exponentially suppressed in the standard treatment. The new treatment provides exponential suppression of their contributions relative to the ground state. Implications for other nucleon current matrix elements are also discussed.

## Key claim

The standard QCD sum rule method has only marginal predictive power for nucleon axial vector coupling constants, with relative errors of approximately 50% to 100%, compared to the nucleon mass obtained from the same method which has only 10% to 25% error. The large errors originate from poor operator product expansion convergence and large continuum contributions in previously used sum rules.

## Method

Two new sets of QCD sum rules for the nucleon axial coupling constants were derived using the external-field technique with generalized spin-1/2 interpolating fields (parameterized by a mixing parameter β) and mixed correlators of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 fields. A Monte-Carlo based uncertainty analysis was performed by generating 1000 randomly-selected, Gaussianly-distributed QCD parameter sets, applying χ² minimization to extract phenomenological fit parameters, and constructing uncertainty distributions for the extracted coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The relative errors for nucleon axial coupling constants from standard QCD sum rules range from approximately 50% to 100%
- The nucleon mass obtained from the same method has relative errors of only 10% to 25%
- The quark condensate was taken as a = 0.52 ± 0.05 GeV³
- The gluon condensate was adopted as b = 1.2 ± 0.6 GeV⁴ with 50% uncertainty
- The mixed condensate parameter was placed at m²₀ = 0.72 ± 0.08 GeV²
- The QCD scale parameter Λ_QCD was restricted to 0.15 ± 0.04 GeV, with bounds 0.10 GeV ≤ Λ_QCD ≤ 0.20 GeV
- For the isovector g_A, the vacuum susceptibilities were χ_v a = 0.70 ± 0.05 GeV² and κ_v a = 0.14 ± 0.14 GeV⁴
- For the flavor singlet g⁰_A, the susceptibilities were χ_v a = 0.14 ± 0.07 GeV² and κ_v a = 0.01 ± 0.01 GeV⁴
- The optimal mixing parameter was β = -1.2 for spin-1/2 sum rules and β = 0 for mixed sum rules

## Context

This work extends the Monte-Carlo based uncertainty analysis introduced by Leinweber (1996) for ground state ρ-meson and nucleon spectral properties, applying it for the first time to a three-point function calculation of axial coupling constants. Previous analyses of these coupling constants by Belyaev (1984), Chiu (1985), Gupta (1989), Henley (1992), and Ioffe (1992) were based on the Ioffe current and the invalid nucleon-mass sum rule, with poor OPE convergence and large continuum contributions. The new sum rules and alternative treatment of off-diagonal transitions provide exponential suppression of problematic contributions relative to the ground state, enabling more reliable extraction of nucleon axial coupling constants and having implications for other nucleon current matrix elements.
