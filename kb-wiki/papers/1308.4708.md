# Light front distribution of the chiral condensate

> The pseudoscalar projection of the pion's Poincare'-covariant Bethe-Salpeter amplitude onto the light-front may be understood to provide the probability distribution of the chiral condensate within the pion. Unlike the parton distribution amplitudes usually considered and as befitting a collective effect, this condensate distribution receives contributions from all Fock space components of the pion's light-front wave-function. We compute this condensate distribution using the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework and show the result to be a model-independent feature of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Our analysis establishes that this condensate is concentrated in the neighbourhood of the boundaries of the distribution's domain of support. It thereby confirms the dominant role played by many-particle Fock states within the pion's light-front wave function in generating the chiral condensate on the light-front and verifies that light-front longitudinal zero modes do not play a material role in that process.

# Light front distribution of the chiral condensate

## Key claim
The pseudoscalar projection of the pion's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude onto the light front provides the probability distribution of the chiral condensate within the pion, and this distribution is a model-independent feature of QCD. The chiral condensate is concentrated near the boundaries of the distribution's domain of support, confirming that many-particle Fock states dominate its generation and that light-front longitudinal zero modes do not play a material role.

## Method
The authors computed the light-front distribution of the chiral condensate using the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework, employing both rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation and DCSB-improved (DB) kernels that preserve the one-loop renormalisation group behaviour of QCD. They used generalised spectral representations to continue from Euclidean metric to Minkowski space, where the light front is defined, and reconstructed the distribution from its moments using a Gegenbauer-polynomial method. The computation was performed in the chiral limit (vanishing current-quark masses) at two renormalisation scales, 2 GeV and 19 GeV.

## Key numbers
- At renormalisation scale ζ₂ = 2 GeV, the distribution parameters are ν = 1.05 and a₂ = 0.48, with the form ω(x;ζ₂) = N_ν [x(1-x)]^{ν-1} [1 + a₂ C₂^{(ν-1/2)}(x - (1-x))].
- The lowest three moments of the distribution at ζ₂: ∫dx (2x-1)^{2j} ω(x,ζ₂) = 0.39 (j=1), 0.25 (j=2), 0.18 (j=3).
- The central value ω(1/2, ζ₂) = 0.76, compared to the asymptotic value ω^{asy}(1/2) = 3/4.
- The asymptotic distribution (ν=1) is ω^{asy}(x) = 1 + (1/2) C₂^{(1/2)}(x - (1-x)).
- The pion's asymptotic parton distribution amplitude is φ^{asy}(x) = 6 x (1-x).

## Context
This work extends the light-front projection method introduced by Chang, Roberts, and Schmidt (2013) for the pion's valence-quark parton distribution amplitude to the pseudoscalar projection, which images the chiral condensate. It confirms the hypothesis of Brodsky et al. (2010) that the chiral condensate is confined within the pion and demonstrates that the distribution is model-independent, unlike earlier QCD sum rules estimates by Ball and colleagues (1998, 2006) which only constrained low-order moments. The result enables lattice QCD verification of the predicted moments and provides input for light-cone sum rule analyses of hard exclusive processes such as B-meson pionic decays.
