# The imprints of nonextensive statistical mechanics in high energy collisions

> To describe high energy collisions one widely uses thermodynamical methods and concepts which follow the classical Boltzmann-Gibbs (BG) approach. In many cases, however, either some deviations from the expected behaviour are observed experimentally or it is known that the conditions necessary for BG to apply are satisfied only approximately. In other branches of physics where such situations are ubiquitous, the popular remedy is to resort, instead, to the so called nonextensive statistics, the most popular example of which is Tsallis statistics. We shall provide here an overview of possible imprints of non-extensitivity existing both in high energy cosmic ray physics and in multiparticle production processes in hadronic collisions, in particular in heavy ion collisions. Some novel proposition for the interpretation of the nonextensitivity parameter q present in such circumstances will be discussed in more detail.

## Key claim

The paper argues that deviations from Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics observed in high-energy collision data can be described by Tsallis nonextensive statistics with a single parameter q, and proposes that this q parameter can be interpreted as a direct measure of fluctuations present in the system, specifically the relative variance of the fluctuating parameter (such as mean free path or temperature).

## Method

The authors review experimental data from cosmic ray cascade measurements, heavy ion collisions, e+e- annihilation, and rapidity distributions, fitting them with power-law distributions derived from Tsallis statistics. They then derive analytically that the Tsallis parameter q equals 1 plus or minus the relative variance ω of fluctuations in the exponential parameter 1/λ, using gamma function representations and a Langevin equation approach with white Gaussian noise to show that gamma distributions naturally arise from stochastic processes described by Fokker-Planck equations.

## Key numbers

- For cosmic ray cascade data from the Pamir experiment, the depth distribution of cascade starting points is fitted with q = 1.3 and λ = 18.85 ± 0.66 cascade units (1 c.u. = 6.4 g/cm² = 0.56 cm of lead)
- For transverse momentum distributions in heavy ion collisions, the deviation from exponential behavior occurs at the level q = 1.015
- The relative variance ω of fluctuations in 1/λ equals q - 1 for q > 1 and 1 - q for q < 1
- For the heavy ion case with q = 1.015, the relative temperature fluctuations are of order ΔT/T ≈ 0.12
- For e+e- annihilation, the transverse momentum distribution can be fitted with constant temperature T₀ and varying q parameter
- For rapidity distributions with M = 100 GeV and m_T = 0.4 GeV, the condition β_q ≥ 0 requires N ≥ N_q0 ≈ 2(ln(M/m_T))^q
- For the Φ-measure of fluctuations in heavy ion collisions at T = 140 MeV and μ = 0, Φ vanishes for q = 1.015
- The Φ-measure prediction for p_T-dependent contributions should become negative for p_T > 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis nonextensive statistics, originally developed for systems with long-range interactions and fractal phase space structure (Tsallis, 1988), to high-energy multiparticle production processes in both cosmic ray and accelerator experiments. It builds on earlier analyses of the "long flying component" in cosmic ray cascades (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 1999) and on studies of transverse momentum distributions in heavy ion collisions (Alberico et al., 2000; Utyuzh et al., 2000). The paper's novel interpretation of q as a measure of fluctuations provides a physical mechanism for the success of Tsallis statistics in these contexts, and enables future experimental tests: event-by-event analyses at RHIC and LHC can determine whether power-law p_T distributions arise from thermal fluctuations within single events or from event-to-event temperature variations.
