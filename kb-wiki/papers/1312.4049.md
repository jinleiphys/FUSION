# Quark confinement mechanism for baryons

> The confinement mechanism proposed earlier and then successfully applied to meson spectroscopy by the author is extended over baryons. For this aim the wave functions of baryons are built as tensorial products of those corresponding to the 2-body problem underlying the confinement mechanism of two quarks. This allows one to obtain the Hamiltonian of the quark interactions in a baryon and, accordingly, the possible energy spectrum of the latter. Also one may construct the electric and magnetic form factors of baryon in a natural way which entails the expressions for the root-mean-square radius and anomalous magnetic moment. To ullustrate the formalism in the given Chapter for the sake of simplicity only symmetrical baryons (i.e., composed from three quarks of the same flavours) $Δ^{++}$, $Δ^{-}$, $Ω^-$ are considered. For them the masses, the root-mean-square radii and anomalous magnetic moments are expressed in an explicit analytical form through the parameters of the confining SU(3)-gluonic field among quarks and that enables one to get a number of numerical estimates for the mentioned parameters from experimental data. We also discuss chiral limit for the baryons under consideration and estimate the purely gluonic contribution to their masses. Further the problem of masses in particle physics is shortly discussed within the framework of the given approach. Finally, a few remarks are made about the so-called Yang-Mills Millennium problem and a possible way for proving it is outlined.

## Key claim

The confinement mechanism previously applied to mesons is extended to baryons by constructing baryon wave functions as tensorial products of the two-quark wave functions that underlie the meson description, but the paper explicitly restricts this extension to symmetrical baryons (Δ⁺⁺, Δ⁻, Ω⁻) composed of three quarks of the same flavour, leaving baryons with mixed flavours for future work.

## Method

The author builds baryon wave functions as tensorial products of the two-body wave functions that describe quark-antiquark bound states in the previously developed confinement mechanism, where the confining SU(3)-gluonic field is given by the unique nonperturbative spherically symmetric solutions of the Yang-Mills equations. The Dirac equation is solved in this confining field to obtain the two-quark wave functions parametrized by constants a_j, b_j, B_j describing the gluon condensate, and the baryon Hamiltonian and energy spectrum are then derived from the tensorial product construction. For the three symmetrical baryons considered, the masses, root-mean-square radii, and anomalous magnetic moments are expressed analytically in terms of the confining field parameters, and numerical estimates for these parameters are extracted from experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the author's previously developed confinement mechanism from meson spectroscopy (Goncharov, 2001-2010) to baryons, building on studies of heavy quarkonia and the pseudoscalar meson nonet. The approach is based on unique nonperturbative solutions of the Dirac-Yang-Mills system that satisfy the Wilson confinement criterion (Wilson, 1974), and it offers an alternative to the standard quark model treatment of baryons as three-body problems. The formalism enables future calculation of baryon electromagnetic form factors and provides a framework for addressing the Yang-Mills Millennium problem.
