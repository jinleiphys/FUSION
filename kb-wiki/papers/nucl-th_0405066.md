# Two neutrino positron double beta decay of $^{106}$Cd for $0^+ \to 0^+$ transition

> The two neutrino positron double beta decay of $^{106}$Cd for 0$^{+} \to $ 0$^{+}$ transition has been studied in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model in conjunction with the summation method. In the first step, the reliability of the intrinsic wave functions of $^{106}$Cd and $^{106}$Pd nuclei has been tested by comparing the theoretically calculated results for yrast spectra, reduced $B(E2$:$0^{+}\to 2^{+})$ transition probabilities, quadrupole moments $Q(2^{+})$ and gyromagnetic factors $g(2^{+})$ with the available experimental data. In the second step, the nuclear transition matrix element $M_{2ν}$ and the half-life $T_{1/2}^{2ν}$ for 0$^{+}\to$ 0$^{+}$ transition have been calculated with these wave functions. Moreover, we have studied the effect of deformation on nuclear transition matrix element M$_{2ν}.$

## Key claim

The authors calculate the two-neutrino positron double beta decay half-life of \(^{106}\)Cd for the \(0^+ \to 0^+\) transition using the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model with the summation method, finding that the nuclear transition matrix element \(M_{2\nu}\) is quenched by a factor of approximately 2 due to deformation effects, and that the predicted half-lives are larger than most previous calculations but close to experimentally observable limits for the \(\beta^+EC\) and \(ECEC\) modes.

## Method

The authors first generate intrinsic wave functions for \(^{106}\)Cd and \(^{106}\)Pd using the PHFB model with a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole (PPQQ) effective interaction, fixing the proton-neutron quadrupole strength \(\chi_{pn}\) to reproduce the experimental \(2^+\) excitation energies. They then test the wave functions by calculating yrast spectra, reduced \(B(E2:0^+ \to 2^+)\) transition probabilities, quadrupole moments \(Q(2^+)\), and gyromagnetic factors \(g(2^+)\) and comparing with available data. Finally, they compute the nuclear transition matrix element \(M_{2\nu}\) using the summation method (avoiding the closure approximation) and the half-life \(T_{1/2}^{2\nu}\) for the three positron double beta decay modes (\(\beta^+\beta^+\), \(\beta^+EC\), \(ECEC\)), and study the effect of deformation by varying the strength of the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical \(E_{2^+}\) for \(^{106}\)Cd is 0.6220 MeV (experimental 0.6327 MeV) for \(\chi_{pn}=0.0151\) MeV \(b^{-4}\); for \(^{106}\)Pd it is 0.5036 MeV (experimental 0.5119 MeV) for \(\chi_{pn}=0.0145\) MeV \(b^{-4}\).
- The calculated \(B(E2:0^+ \to 2^+)\) values for both nuclei agree excellently with experiment for effective charge \(e_{eff}=0.5\).
- The calculated \(g(2^+)\) is 0.370 nm for \(^{106}\)Cd and 0.466 nm for \(^{106}\)Pd; the latter is 0.047 nm above the experimental upper limit from Raghavan (1989).
- The phase space factors used are \(G_{2\nu}=4.991\times10^{-26}\) yr\(^{-1}\) for \(\beta^+\beta^+\), \(1.970\times10^{-21}\) yr\(^{-1}\) for \(\beta^+EC\), and \(1.573\times10^{-20}\) yr\(^{-1}\) for \(ECEC\) modes, from Doi et al. (1992).
- The deformation quenching factor \(D_{2\nu}=M_{2\nu}(\chi_{qq}=0)/M_{2\nu}(\chi_{qq}=1)=2.09\), indicating the matrix element is reduced by approximately a factor of 2 due to deformation.
- The predicted half-lives are of the order \(10^{21-23}\) years for the \(\beta^+EC\) mode.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of positron double beta decay in \(^{106}\)Cd using the PHFB model, which treats pairing and deformation on equal footing, unlike the QRPA-based calculations of Suhonen and Civitarese (2001) and others. The authors compare their results with earlier theoretical predictions from QRPA (Staudt et al., 1991; Suhonen et al., 1993; Toivanen et al., 1997), SU(4)\(\sigma\tau\) (Rumyantsev et al., 1998), and SSDH (Civitarese et al., 1998), as well as experimental limits from the Osaka-OTO collaboration (Itoh et al., 1997) and others. The calculated half-lives are close to the observable range for the \(\beta^+EC\) and \(ECEC\) modes, suggesting that planned experiments (e.g., the larger Osaka-OTO setup) may detect these decays, which would help discriminate between Majorana neutrino mass and right-handed current scenarios in neutrinoless double beta decay.
