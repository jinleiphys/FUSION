# A renormalizable extension of the NJL-model

> The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is supplemented by the quark interaction generated by the one-gluon exchange. The employed gluon propagator exhibits the correct large momentum behavior of QCD, whereas the Landau-pole at low energies is screened. The emerging constituent quark model is one-loop renormalizable and interpolates between the phenomenologically successful Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at low energies and perturbative QCD at high momenta. Consequently, the momentum dependence of the quark self-energy at high energy coincides with the prediction from perturbative QCD. The chiral phase transition is studied in dependence on the low energy four quark interaction strength in the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach. The critical exponents of the quark self-energy and the quark condensate are obtained. The latter exponent deviates from the NJL-result. Pion properties are addressed by means of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The validity of the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation is verified. Finally, we study the conditions which are necessary to allow for an accurate estimate of observables by the NJL-model as well as the shortcoming of the NJL-model due to its inherent non-renormalizability.

## Key claim

The authors propose a one-loop renormalizable extension of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that interpolates between the NJL model at low energies and perturbative QCD at high momenta, provided the gluon propagator's Landau pole is screened by the NJL-type interaction at an energy scale \(\Lambda_I > \Lambda_{QCD}\).

## Method

The model supplements the NJL four-quark interaction with a one-gluon exchange term, using a gluon propagator that is a step function: a constant NJL coupling \(G\) for momenta below a scale \(\Lambda_I\), and the perturbative QCD running coupling for momenta above \(\Lambda_I\). The Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark self-energy is solved numerically and analytically in the ladder approximation, and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion is treated via a derivative expansion to extract pion properties.

## Key numbers

* Critical coupling for chiral symmetry breaking: \(G_c \approx (0.26 \pm 0.011) \times 16\pi^2 / \Lambda_I^2\).
* Critical exponent for the quark mass function near the phase transition: \(\delta_\Sigma = 0.47\), compatible with \(1/2\).
* Critical exponent for the quark condensate near the phase transition: \(\delta_\chi \approx 0.27\), deviating from the NJL result of \(1/2\).
* For \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 500\) MeV and \(G = 2.22 \times 16\pi^2 / \Lambda_I^2\): constituent quark mass \(M_c = 312\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 130\) MeV, and quark condensate \([- \langle \bar{q}q \rangle (\mu = 1\ \text{GeV})]^{1/3} = 214\) MeV.
* Asymptotic behavior of the quark mass function in the chiral limit: \(\Sigma(p^2) \approx c / [p^2 (\ln p^2/\Lambda_{QCD}^2)^{\beta}]\) with \(\beta = 1 - d_m = 5/9\) for \(N_f=3\).

## Context

This work extends the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) by incorporating the one-gluon exchange interaction, addressing the NJL model's non-renormalizability. It contrasts with the gauged NJL model (Bardeen, 1986; Yamawaki, 1989), which does not reduce to one-gluon exchange at high energies. The model enables a consistent study of chiral symmetry breaking and pion properties with a momentum-dependent quark self-energy that matches perturbative QCD at high momenta, and it provides a framework to assess the accuracy of the standard NJL model as an approximation.
