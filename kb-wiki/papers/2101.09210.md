# QCD Critical Point and High Baryon Density Matter

> We report the latest results on the search for the QCD critical point in the QCD phase diagram through high energy heavy-ion collisions. The measurements discussed are based on the higher moments of the net-proton multiplicity distributions in heavy-ion collisions. A non-monotonic variation in the product of kurtosis times the variance of the net-proton distribution is observed as a function of the collision energy with 3$σ$ significance. We also discuss the results of the thermal model in explaining the measured particle yield ratios in heavy-ion collisions and comparison of the different variants of hardon resonance gas model calculation to the data on higher moments of net-proton distributions. We end with a note that the upcoming programs in high baryon density regime at various experimental facilities will complete the search for the QCD critical point through heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
A non-monotonic variation in the product of kurtosis times variance (κσ²) of the net-proton distribution is observed as a function of collision energy with 3σ significance, but the authors state that a more robust conclusion requires reduced uncertainties and significance above 5σ.

## Method
The authors analyze higher moments (skewness Sσ and kurtosis κσ²) of net-proton multiplicity distributions measured in Au+Au collisions at RHIC across a range of center-of-mass energies (√sNN from 7.7 to 200 GeV). They compare experimental data to predictions from several variants of the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model (ideal grand canonical, excluded volume, canonical ensemble) and the UrQMD transport model, using χ² tests to quantify deviations. They also compare cumulant ratios to lattice QCD inspired fits up to next-to-leading order in μB/T.

## Key numbers
- The product κσ² of net-proton distributions in 0-5% central Au+Au collisions shows a non-monotonic variation with collision energy, deviating from the statistical baseline of κσ² = 1 at a significance of approximately 3σ.
- χ² tests between experimental data and models for √sNN range 7.7 to 27 GeV yield p-values less than 0.05 for all models (HRG GCE, HRG EV, UrQMD) except HRG CE for Sσ (p = 0.0754); for κσ², p-values are 0.00553 (HRG GCE), 0.0145 (HRG EV), 0.0450 (HRG CE), and 0.0221 (UrQMD).
- The measurements cover a baryon chemical potential range of 20-450 MeV.
- The transition from grand canonical to canonical ensemble in thermal models occurs below collision energy of 5 GeV, based on φ/K⁻ yield ratios.
- The ground state of nuclear matter is at T ∼ 0 and μB ∼ 925 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the search for the QCD critical point in the QCD phase diagram using heavy-ion collision data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, following theoretical frameworks by Stephanov (2008, 2011) and Hatta (2003). The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations by Bazavov (2017) that disfavor a critical point at μB/T ≤ 2. The paper enables future high-statistics searches at RHIC Beam Energy Scan Phase-II and upcoming facilities including NICA at JINR, CBM at FAIR, and CEE at HIAF, which will probe the high baryon density region (μB between 500 and 800 MeV).
