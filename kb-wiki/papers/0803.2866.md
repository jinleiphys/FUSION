# Charmonium in strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma

> The growing consensus that a strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) has been observed at the SPS and RHIC experiments suggests a different framework for examining heavy quark dynamics. We present both semi-analytical treatment of Fokker-Planck (FP) evolution in pedagogical examples and numerical Langevin simulations of evolving charm quark-antiquark pairs on top of a hydrodynamically expanding fireball. In this way, we may conclude that the survival probability of bound charmonia states is greater than previously estimated, as the spatial equilibration of pairs proceeds through a ``slowly dissolving lump'' stage related to the pair interaction.

## Key claim

The survival probability of bound charmonia states in strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma is greater than previously estimated, because the spatial equilibration of charm-anticharm pairs proceeds through a "slowly dissolving lump" stage related to the pair interaction, rather than through rapid dissociation.

## Method

The authors performed both semi-analytical Fokker-Planck evolution calculations in pedagogical examples and numerical Langevin simulations of evolving charm quark-antiquark pairs on top of a hydrodynamically expanding fireball. The simulations used a parametrization of the heavy quark-antiquark potential above deconfinement fitted to quenched-QCD lattice data, with the internal energy (rather than free energy) as the effective potential. The initial charm pair distributions were generated using PYTHIA event generation at 200 GeV, and the Langevin dynamics included drag, stochastic forces, and the mutual interaction between charm quarks, with pairs allowed to exist as either color singlets or color octets in thermal equilibrium in color space.

## Key numbers

- Charm diffusion constant range used: D_c (2 pi T) = 1.5 to 3, based on analysis of RHIC single electron data by Moore and Teaney
- AdS/CFT estimate for charm diffusion constant: D_c (2 pi T) = 4 / sqrt(g^2 N_c) ~ 0.5 to 1
- Drag coefficient from AdS/CFT value: eta_c ~ 0.6 GeV
- J/psi survival through the QGP phase duration tau ~ 5 fm/c is about a half
- Initial projection probability of a random c-cbar pair into J/psi state: 0.8%, on the same order as the experimental value of 1% for sigma_J/psi / sigma_c-cbar
- Potential parametrization: sigma = 0.22 GeV^2, mu(T) = (0.03 + 0.006 T/T_c) GeV^2, C(T) = 0.15 GeV / (T/T_c - 0.98)
- Root mean square distance for diffusive motion: <x^2> = 6 D_c tau
- Spatial correlation length in liquid regime estimate: xi(T=0.4 GeV) ~ 0.05 fm
- Perturbative gas correlation length estimate: lambda/2 ~ 0.6 fm at T=0.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Zahed and Shuryak (2006) who argued that the internal energy rather than free energy should be used as the effective potential for dynamical quark-antiquark pairs, and follows the Langevin simulation framework of Moore and Teaney for single charm quarks. The paper contradicts the perturbative calculations of Kharzeev and Satz (1997) which predicted near-total charmonium destruction at RHIC, and instead supports the lattice findings from the Bielefeld-BNL group and Aarts et al. (2007) that charmonium states may survive to higher temperatures. The results enable a dynamical explanation for why J/psi survival probability can be close to one at RHIC without requiring fine-tuned recombination from unrelated charm pairs, and provide a natural mechanism for the observed thermal ratios of charmonium states at SPS.
