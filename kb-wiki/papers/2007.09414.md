# An efficient solution for Dirac equation in 3D lattice space with the conjugate gradient method

> An efficient method, preconditioned conjugate gradient method with a filtering function (PCG-F), is proposed for solving iteratively the Dirac equation in 3D lattice space for nuclear systems. The filtering function is adopted to avoid the variational collapsed problem and a momentum-dependent preconditioner is introduced to promote the efficiency of the iteration. The PCG-F method is demonstrated in solving the Dirac equation with given spherical and deformed Woods-Saxon potentials. The solutions given by the inverse Hamiltonian method in 3D lattice space and the shooting method in radial coordinate space are reproduced with a high accuracy. In comparison with the existing inverse Hamiltonian method, the present PCG-F method is much faster in the convergence of the iteration, in particular for deformed potentials. It may also provide a promising way to solve the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equation iteratively in the future.

## Key claim

The preconditioned conjugate gradient method with a filtering function (PCG-F) solves the Dirac equation in 3D lattice space for nuclear systems without the variational collapse problem, and is much faster in convergence than the existing inverse Hamiltonian method, especially for deformed potentials. The method avoids calculating the inverse of the Hamiltonian, which is the main bottleneck of the inverse Hamiltonian method.

## Method

The PCG-F method iteratively minimizes the Rayleigh quotient for the Dirac Hamiltonian using a locally optimal block preconditioned conjugate gradient scheme. A filtering function \(F(\hat{h}) = (\hat{h} - C)^2 / D^2\) is applied to the trial wavefunctions and residuals to suppress negative-energy components and avoid variational collapse, while a momentum-dependent preconditioner \(\hat{T}_l = [\hat{\mathbf{p}}^2 + g_l^2 m_N^2]^2\) damps high positive-energy components to accelerate convergence. The method was tested on the Dirac equation with spherical and deformed Woods-Saxon potentials in a 3D lattice discretized with 28 grids per axis and 1 fm mesh size, using fast Fourier transformation for spatial derivatives.

## Key numbers

*   The PCG-F method reduces the maximum energy dispersion to \(10^{-8}~\mathrm{MeV}^2\) in 15 iterations for a spherical Woods-Saxon potential, compared to more than 1200 iterations without the preconditioner and more than 30 iterations for the inverse Hamiltonian method.
*   After 18 iterations, the PCG-F method reaches energy dispersions around \(10^{-10}~\mathrm{MeV}^2\), while the inverse Hamiltonian method requires 37 iterations to reach this level.
*   Absolute energy deviations between the PCG-F method and the inverse Hamiltonian method are less than \(10^{-12}~\mathrm{MeV}\) for all bound states in spherical and deformed potentials.
*   Absolute energy deviations between the PCG-F method and the shooting method in radial coordinate space are in the range of \(10^{-5}\) to \(10^{-3}~\mathrm{MeV}\) for spherical potentials.
*   For deformed potentials, the PCG-F method takes less than 30 iterations to reduce the maximum energy dispersion to around \(10^{-10}~\mathrm{MeV}^2\), while the inverse Hamiltonian method requires more than 100 iterations.

## Context

This work extends the inverse Hamiltonian method (IHM) for solving the Dirac equation in 3D lattice space, previously developed by Tanimura (2010) and Ren (2017), which was time-consuming for heavy nuclei and difficult to apply to Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations. The PCG-F method is inspired by the conjugate gradient method with filtering for electron systems (Lin 2013) but adapts it to nuclear systems where the energy gap between positive- and negative-energy states is much smaller due to large spin-orbit interactions. The method enables future development of covariant density functional theory in 3D lattice space for exotic deformations, super-heavy nuclei, fission, and fusion dynamics, and provides a promising way to solve the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equation iteratively.
