# Anomalous suppression of $π^0$ production at large transverse momentum in Au + Au and d + Au collisions at $\sqrt{{\bf s}_{\bf NN}} =$ 200 GeV

> We propose a model of suppression of large $p_T$-pions in heavy ion collisions based on the interaction of the large $p_T$ pion with the dense medium created in the collision. The model is practically the same as the one previously introduced to describe $J/ψ$ suppression. Both the $p_T$ and the centrality dependence of the data are reproduced. In deuteron-gold collisions, the effect of the final state interaction with the dense medium turns out to be negligibly small. Here the main features of the data are also reproduced both at mid and at forward rapidities.

## Key claim

The suppression of large-$p_T$ $\pi^0$ production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV can be described by final-state interactions of the pion with the dense medium, using a model nearly identical to one previously applied to $J/\psi$ suppression; the effect in d+Au collisions is negligibly small, and the data at both mid and forward rapidities are reproduced only when dynamical shadowing is combined with low-$p_T$ effects from string models.

## Method

The authors model the interaction of a high-$p_T$ $\pi^0$ with the soft medium (all charged and neutral secondaries) using gain-and-loss differential equations in cylindrical space-time coordinates with longitudinal boost invariance. The suppression factor $S_{\pi^0}$ is obtained by integrating the evolution equation from an initial proper time $\tau_0$ to freeze-out time $\tau_f$, where the ratio $\tau_f/\tau_0$ is set equal to the ratio of the medium density in AA to that in pp collisions. The $\pi^0$ $p_T$ distribution in pp is taken as $(1 + p_T/p_0)^{-n}$ with $p_0 = 1.219$ GeV/c and $n = 9.99$, and the $p_T$ broadening in AuAu is implemented by adjusting $p_0$ to match the measured centrality-dependent average $p_T$ while keeping $n$ fixed. The final-state interaction cross-section $\widetilde{\sigma}$ is treated as a free parameter, and the $p_T$ shift $\delta p_T$ is taken either as constant or proportional to $(p_T - \langle p_T \rangle)$.

## Key numbers

- In pp collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV, the $\pi^0$ $p_T$ distribution is described by $(1 + p_T/p_0)^{-n}$ with $p_0 = 1.219$ GeV/c and $n = 9.99$, giving $\langle p_T \rangle = 0.349$ GeV/c.
- In central ($n_{part} = 350$) AuAu collisions at the same energy, $\langle p_T \rangle = 0.453$ GeV/c for $\pi^+$ and $\pi^-$.
- For the 10% most central AuAu collisions ($n_{part} = 325$), using $\widetilde{\sigma} = 1.03$ mb and neglecting the gain term, the suppression factor $S_{\pi^0} = 0.143$.
- The density of charged and neutral particles per unit rapidity at $y^* \sim 0$ in pp collisions is $N_{pp}(0) = 2.24$ fm$^{-2}$.
- In central AuAu collisions, the density of the medium is about 5 times larger than in pp; this factor is predicted to be practically unchanged at LHC energies.
- For dAu collisions at forward rapidities, the combined effect of shadowing and low-$p_T$ nuclear attenuation produces a suppression factor of about 1.7 between $R_{dAu}(p_T)$ at $\eta^* = 0$ and at $\eta^* = 3.2$, independent of $p_T$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model for $J/\psi$ suppression (Capella et al., 2004) to the case of large-$p_T$ $\pi^0$ production, where the particle does not disappear but its $p_T$ is shifted to smaller values. The approach contrasts with radiative parton energy loss (jet quenching) models, and the authors note that the density increase factor of about 5 between peripheral and central AuAu collisions is consistent with a recent finding by another group (2004). The model incorporates dynamical shadowing determined from diffractive cross-sections, which is smaller than saturation-regime shadowing at both RHIC and LHC energies; when combined with low-$p_T$ effects from the Dual Parton Model and Quark Gluon String Model, it reproduces the BRAHMS measurements of $R_{dAu}$ at forward rapidities.
