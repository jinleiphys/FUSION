# Heavy baryons in holographic QCD with higher dimensional degrees of freedom

> We try to introduce heavy flavors to Sakai-Sugimoto model by regarding higher dimensional components of gauge fields as heavy mesons. Using the Forgács and Manton approach, we obtain a theory composed of heavy mesons and the instanton of light flavors. Applying the collective coordinate quantization method, we derived a mass formula of heavy baryons. In the leading order of $1/m_{H}$ expansion ($m_{H}$ the mass of a heavy quark), we find singlet and doublet states in the heavy quark symmetry (HQS). Also, we obtain the degenerate Roper like and odd parity excitations. By virtue of heavy meson degrees of freedom, our mass formula reproduces the mass ordering of $Σ^*_c$ and $Λ_c^*$ correctly.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by treating extra-dimensional components of gauge fields in the Sakai-Sugimoto model as heavy mesons, they derive a mass formula for heavy baryons that reproduces the correct mass ordering of Sigma_c* and Lambda_c* states, but only in the leading order of a 1/m_H expansion (where m_H is the heavy quark mass) and with the addition of an ad hoc mass term that is not derived from the holographic framework.

## Method

The authors extend the SU(2) Sakai-Sugimoto model to SU(3) flavor by identifying the gauge field components on the S^4 sphere (A_psi and A_varphi) as heavy meson fields, using the Forgacs and Manton dimensional reduction method to obtain a five-dimensional effective theory. They then apply the collective coordinate quantization method to an instanton solution representing light-flavor baryons, coupled to the heavy meson fields, and derive a mass formula for heavy baryons by expanding in 1/lambda (the t'Hooft coupling) and 1/m_H.

## Key numbers

- Instanton mass M_0 = -572 MeV (parameter value used in the model)
- Kaluza-Klein mass M_KK = 500 MeV (parameter value used in the model)
- Mass parameter m/M_KK = 4.385 for charm and 10.62 for bottom (used to set heavy meson masses to D(1870) and B(5279) respectively)
- Pion decay constant f_pi/M_KK = 0.122 (parameter value used in the model)
- The mass formula contains a term proportional to sqrt((l+1)^2/6 + (2N_c^2/15)(1 - (40a pi^2 A/N_c)(N_Q - N_anti-Q))) M_KK, where A is a numerical constant from the heavy meson coupling

## Context

This work extends the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) by incorporating heavy flavors through extra-dimensional gauge field components, following the dimensional reduction approach of Forgacs and Manton (1980). The method is analogous to the bound state approach for hyperons developed by Callan and Klebanov (1985), where heavy mesons are treated as bound states of a soliton. The resulting framework enables the study of heavy baryons including excited states such as Roper-like and odd parity excitations, and could be extended to exotic states like the P_c pentaquarks.
