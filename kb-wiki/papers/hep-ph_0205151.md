# Gluon Shadowing and Heavy Flavor Production off Nuclei

> Gluon shadowing which is the main source of nuclear effects for production of heavy flavored hadrons, remains unknown. We develop a light-cone dipole approach aiming at simplifying the calculations of nuclear shadowing for heavy flavor production, as well as the cross section which does not need next-to-leading and higher order corrections. A substantial process dependence of gluon shadowing is found at the scale of charm mass manifesting a deviation from QCD factorization. The magnitude of the shadowing effect correlates with the symmetry properties and color state of the produced c-cbar pair. It is about twice as large as in DIS, but smaller than for charmonium production. The higher twist shadowing correction related to a nonzero size of the c-cbar pair is not negligible and steeply rises with energy. We predict an appreciable suppression by shadowing for charm production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and a stronger effect at LHC. At the same time, we expect no visible difference between nuclear effects for minimal bias and central collisions, as is suggested by recent data from the PHENIX experiment at RHIC. We also demonstrate that at medium high energies when no shadowing is possible, final state interaction may cause a rather strong absorption of heavy flavored hadrons produced at large x_F.

## Key claim

Gluon shadowing for heavy flavor production is substantially process-dependent at the charm mass scale, manifesting a deviation from QCD factorization; the magnitude of the shadowing effect correlates with the symmetry properties and color state of the produced c-cbar pair, being about twice as large as in deep-inelastic scattering but smaller than for charmonium production.

## Method

The authors develop a light-cone dipole approach to calculate nuclear shadowing for heavy flavor production, representing the process in terms of dipole cross sections for multiparton systems (c-cbar, c-cbar-G, c-cbar-G-G) interacting with nucleons. They classify production amplitudes according to the color state (color-singlet or color-octet) and symmetry (odd or even under permutation) of the c-cbar pair, and use light-cone Green functions to describe the propagation of the c-cbar-G system through nuclear matter, solving a two-dimensional Schrodinger-type equation with a nonperturbative light-cone potential.

## Key numbers

* About 20% of produced c-cbar pairs are in a color-singlet state, with the rest being color-octets (ratio sigma^(1) : sigma^(8)_O : sigma^(8)_E = 1 : 5/2 : 117/70).
* For fixed target experiments at the Tevatron (x_2 ~ 10^-2, x_F ~ 0.5), the higher-twist shadowing correction from c-cbar pair size gives 1 - R_A less than or similar to 0.05.
* The higher-twist shadowing correction reaches about 10% at x_2 = 10^-3 and about 30% at x_2 = 10^-5.
* Predictions are made for charm production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV) and LHC (sqrt(s) = 5500 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the light-cone dipole approach previously applied to deep-inelastic scattering (Kopeliovich et al., 2000) and charmonium production (Kopeliovich et al., 2001) to open charm production, finding that gluon shadowing is process-dependent and deviates from QCD factorization. The results enable predictions for nuclear suppression in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and explain the PHENIX observation of nearly identical shadowing for minimal bias and central collisions as a natural consequence of the formalism rather than an indication of weak nuclear effects.
