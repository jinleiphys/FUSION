# Parametrization of the Relativistic ($σ-ω$) Model for Nuclear Matter

> We have investigated the zero-temperature equation of state (EoS) for infinite nuclear matter within the $(σ-ω)$ model at all densities $n_{\mathrm{B}}$ and different proton-neutron asymmetry $η\equiv(N-Z)/A$. We have presented an analytical expression for the compression modulus and found that nuclear matter ceases to saturate at $η$ slightly larger than $0.8$. Afterward, we have developed an analytical method to determine the strong coupling constants from the EoS for isospin symmetric nuclear matter, which allow us to reproduce all the saturation properties with high accuracy. For various values of the nucleon effective mass and the compression modulus, we have found that the quartic self-coupling constant $G_4$ is negative, or positive and very large. Furthermore, we have demonstrated that it is possible (a) to investigate the EoS in terms of $n_{\mathrm{B}}$ and $η$; and (b) to reproduce all the known saturation properties without $G_4$. We have thus concluded that the latter is not necessary in the $(σ-ω)$ model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the quartic self-coupling constant \(G_4\) is not necessary in the relativistic (\(\sigma-\omega\)) model because all known saturation properties of isospin symmetric nuclear matter can be reproduced without it; however, this conclusion is hedged by the finding that for various values of the nucleon effective mass and compression modulus, \(G_4\) is either negative, or positive and very large, indicating that the necessity of \(G_4\) depends on the chosen parameter set.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation to the (\(\sigma-\omega\)) model (Walecka model) extended with nonlinear scalar self-coupling terms (three- and four-body interactions) and including the \(\rho\) meson for isospin dependence. They derived the zero-temperature equation of state (EoS) for infinite nuclear matter at all baryon densities \(n_{\mathrm{B}}\) and proton-neutron asymmetry \(\eta \equiv (N-Z)/A\), and developed an analytical method to determine the strong coupling constants from the EoS for isospin symmetric nuclear matter by using the saturation properties as input parameters rather than fitting them as output.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: \(n_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{sat}} \approx 0.153\ \mathrm{Nucleon/fm^3}\) (from empirical estimate using \(r_0 \approx 1.16\ \mathrm{fm}\)).
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation for isospin symmetric matter: \(E_{\mathrm{iso}}^{\mathrm{sat}} \approx -16.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Asymmetry energy coefficient at saturation: \(a_{\mathrm{sym}}^{\mathrm{sat}} \approx 32.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Nucleon effective mass range at saturation: \(0.7 \lesssim m_{\mathrm{N}}^*/m_{\mathrm{N}} \lesssim 0.8\).
- Compression modulus range: \(230.0 \lesssim \mathcal{K} \lesssim 250.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with a supported value \(\mathcal{K} \simeq 234.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from Myers and Swiatecki (1998) and \(\mathcal{K} = 231 \pm 5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from Youngblood et al.
- Nuclear matter ceases to saturate at \(\eta\) slightly larger than \(0.8\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Walecka (1974) and the nonlinear extension by Boguta and Bodmer (1977) by providing an analytical parametrization method that inverts the usual procedure: instead of fitting coupling constants to reproduce saturation properties, the saturation properties are used as input to determine the coupling constants analytically. The paper enables systematic investigation of the EoS for asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter without requiring the quartic self-coupling term, which simplifies the model and clarifies the role of nonlinear scalar interactions. The results are relevant for studies of neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions, and the method contrasts with numerical fitting approaches used in prior work such as that of Glendenning (2000).
