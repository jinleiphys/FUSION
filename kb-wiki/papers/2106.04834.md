# Wigner SU(4) symmetry, clustering, and the spectrum of $^{12}$C

> We present lattice calculations of the low-lying spectrum of $^{12}$C using a simple nucleon-nucleon interaction that is independent of spin and isospin and therefore invariant under Wigner's SU(4) symmetry. We find strong signals for all excited states up to $\sim 15$~MeV above the ground state, and explore the structure of each state using a large variety of $α$ cluster and harmonic oscillator trial states, projected onto given irreducible representations of the cubic group. We are able to verify earlier findings for the $α$ clustering in the Hoyle state and the second $2^+$ state of $^{12}$C. The success of these calculations to describe the full low-lying energy spectrum using spin-independent interactions suggest that either the spin-orbit interactions are somewhat weak in the $^{12}$C system, or the effects of $α$ clustering are diminishing their influence. This is in agreement with previous findings from {\it ab initio} shell model calculations.

## Key claim

Using a simple spin- and isospin-independent nucleon-nucleon interaction that obeys Wigner SU(4) symmetry, the authors reproduce the full low-lying spectrum of $^{12}$C up to about 15 MeV excitation energy, suggesting that either spin-orbit interactions are weak in this system or that alpha clustering diminishes their influence.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) calculations using projection Monte Carlo (PMC) simulations with a simplified SU(4)-symmetric two-nucleon interaction containing only two free parameters (the coupling strength and the local smearing range), fitted to the ground-state energies of $^{4}$He and $^{12}$C. They used a large variety of three-alpha cluster trial states (isosceles right triangles, bent-arm configurations, linear chains, and acute isosceles triangles) and harmonic oscillator shell-model trial states, projected onto irreducible representations of the cubic group, to extract the energies of excited states via adiabatic transfer matrix methods.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of $^{4}$He: $-28.299(9)$ MeV at lattice spacing $a = 1.97$ fm and $-28.290(15)$ MeV at $a = 1.64$ fm, compared to the experimental value of $-28.296$ MeV
- Ground state energy of $^{12}$C: $-92.15(3)$ MeV at $a = 1.97$ fm and $-92.12(4)$ MeV at $a = 1.64$ fm, compared to the experimental value of $-92.162$ MeV
- Hoyle state ($0_2^+$) energy: $-85.20(15)$ MeV at $a = 1.97$ fm and $-85.23(22)$ MeV at $a = 1.64$ fm, compared to the experimental value of $-84.508$ MeV
- Second $2^+$ state ($2_2^+$) energy: $-83.5(2)$ MeV at $a = 1.97$ fm and $-83.1(5)$ MeV at $a = 1.64$ fm, compared to the experimental value of $-82.29(6)$ MeV
- $3_1^-$ state energy: $-84.9(2)$ MeV at $a = 1.97$ fm and $-83.3(5)$ MeV at $a = 1.64$ fm, compared to the experimental value of $-82.521(5)$ MeV
- The spatial lattice spacings used were $a = 1.97$ fm (momentum cutoff 314 MeV) and $a = 1.64$ fm (momentum cutoff 378 MeV), with periodic box size $L = 9$ in each dimension

## Context

This work extends earlier NLEFT studies by Epelbaum et al. (2011, 2012) that focused exclusively on even-parity alpha cluster states in $^{12}$C, now including both positive and negative parity states up to about 15 MeV excitation energy using an extended basis of cluster and shell-model trial states. The results confirm previous findings from ab initio shell model calculations by Hayes (2003) and Johnson (2014) that spin-orbit splittings are not strong in $^{12}$C. The study provides a benchmark for future ab initio chiral effective field theory calculations on the lattice and suggests that similar SU(4)-symmetric studies of $^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne would be valuable to test whether such simple interactions can describe the low-energy spectra of heavier nuclei.
