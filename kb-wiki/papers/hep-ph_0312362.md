# Magnetic Moments of $J^P={3/2}^+$ Pentaquarks

> If the $J^P$ of $Θ_5^+$ and $Ξ_5^{--}$ pentaquarks is really found to be ${1\over 2}^+$ by future experiments, they will be accompanied by $J^P={3\over 2}^+$ partners in some models. It is reasonable to expect that these $J^P={3\over 2}^+$ states will also be discovered in the near future with the current intensive experimental and theoretical efforts. We estimate $J^P={3/2}^+$ pentaquark magnetic moments using different models.

## Key claim

If the $J^P$ of the $\Theta_5^+$ and $\Xi_5^{--}$ pentaquarks is experimentally determined to be ${1\over 2}^+$, then $J^P={3\over 2}^+$ partner states are expected to exist in several models; the authors calculate the magnetic moments of these $J^P={3\over 2}^+$ states as a way to discriminate between competing models, but they do not argue that any of the models are correct.

## Method

The authors computed the magnetic moments of $J^P={3\over 2}^+$ pentaquarks belonging to the $\bf{\bar{10}}$ and $\bf{8}$ flavor multiplets using three distinct constituent models: Jaffe and Wilczek's model (two scalar diquarks plus an antiquark with $L=1$ between the diquarks), Shuryak and Zahed's model (one scalar diquark, one tensor diquark, and an antiquark with no orbital excitation), and Karliner and Lipkin's model (a scalar diquark and a triquark with $L=1$ between them). For each model, the total magnetic moment was obtained by summing the spin and orbital contributions of the constituents, using standard Clebsch-Gordan coefficients to project onto the $J={3\over 2}$ state, and evaluated numerically with fixed quark and diquark masses.

## Key numbers

* In Jaffe and Wilczek's model (Set I parameters: $m_u=m_d=0.36$ GeV, $m_s=0.54$ GeV, $m_{ud}=0.72$ GeV, $m_{us}=m_{ds}=0.90$ GeV), the magnetic moment of the $\Theta^{\ast +}$ ($Y=2, I=0, I_3=0$) is $1.01\ \mu_N$; for $\Xi_5^{\ast --}$ ($Y=-1, I=3/2, I_3=-3/2$) it is $-2.43\ \mu_N$; for $\Xi_5^{\ast +}$ ($Y=-1, I=3/2, I_3=3/2$) it is $1.22\ \mu_N$.
* In Shuryak and Zahed's model (with $m_{ud}^T=0.57$ GeV, $m_{us}^T=m_{ds}^T=0.72$ GeV, $m_u=m_d=0.36$ GeV, $m_s=0.54$ GeV), the magnetic moment of $\Theta^{\ast +}$ is $1.23\ \mu_N$; for $\Xi_5^{\ast --}$ it is $-2.84\ \mu_N$; for $\Xi_5^{\ast +}$ it is $1.85\ \mu_N$.
* In Karliner and Lipkin's model (with $m_u=m_d=0.36$ GeV, $m_s=0.54$ GeV, $m_{ud}=0.72$ GeV, $m_{us}=m_{ds}=0.90$ GeV, triquark mass $m_{ud\bar u}=1.08$ GeV), the magnetic moment of $\Theta^{\ast +}$ is $0.84\ \mu_N$; for $\Xi_5^{\ast --}$ it is $-1.20\ \mu_N$; for $\Xi_5^{\ast +}$ it is $0.89\ \mu_N$.
* For comparison, the $J={1\over 2}$ $\Theta^+$ magnetic moment in the same three models is $0.08\ \mu_N$ (JW), $0.23\ \mu_N$ (SZ), and $0.19\ \mu_N$ (KL).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of magnetic moments for $J={1\over 2}$ pentaquarks (Li et al., 2003) to the $J={3\over 2}$ partners, using the same three constituent models (Jaffe and Wilczek, Shuryak and Zahed, Karliner and Lipkin). It provides predictions that can be compared with future experimental measurements of pentaquark magnetic moments, which would help distinguish between the competing internal structures. The paper also notes that the $J={3\over 2}$ states are expected to be heavier than their $J={1\over 2}$ partners by no more than about 300 MeV, based on the analogy with the Delta-nucleon mass splitting.
