# Net-proton number kurtosis and skewness in nuclear collisions: Influence of deuteron formation

> We explore the influence of deuteron formation in the late stage of nucleus-nucleus reactions on the fluctuations observed in the final net-proton yields around midrapidity. At each investigated energy, the produced (anti-)proton yield at chemical freeze-out is assumed to fluctuate according to a Poisson distribution and in each event the probability for deuteron formation by coalescence is proportional to $(dN_{\mathrm{proton}}/dy)^2$. The protons that are then clustered in deuterons are usually not included in the experimental measurement of the net-proton fluctuations, therefore, we subtract these clustered protons from the final state proton number for the calculation of the net-proton fluctuations (the same is done in the anti-proton sector). Due to the non-linear deuteron formation probability the resulting distribution is not a Skellam distribution, but shows the interesting feature of a decrease in the kurtosis $κσ^2$ and a local maximum in the skewness $S σ$ observables as the collision energy decreases.

## Key claim

Deuteron formation via coalescence in the late stage of heavy-ion collisions, where the formation probability is proportional to the square of the proton yield, can substantially modify the net-proton fluctuation observables kurtosis κσ² and skewness Sσ, producing deviations from the Skellam distribution that could mimic or obscure signals of a QCD phase transition.

## Method

The authors assume that the initial (anti-)proton yield at chemical freeze-out follows a Poisson distribution, and that in each event the probability for deuteron formation by coalescence is proportional to the square of the initial proton yield. Protons bound into deuterons are subtracted from the final-state proton count (and analogously for antiprotons) to obtain the observed net-proton distribution. The model parameters (mean initial proton number and coalescence factor B) are fixed from experimental midrapidity yields of protons and deuterons, using a thermal-model parametrization of the deuteron-to-proton ratio. For energies above √sNN ≈ 10 GeV, antiprotons and antideuterons are included via independent Poisson sampling, with 10⁹ events generated per beam energy.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron-to-proton multiplicity ratio at midrapidity is parametrized as d/p = 0.8 [√sNN / 1 GeV]⁻¹·⁵⁵ + 0.0036, based on data from Andronic (2005).
- For central Au+Au collisions, the scaled kurtosis κσ² and skewness Sσ show strong deviations from unity (the Poisson/Skellam expectation) across the beam energy range from AGS to top RHIC energies.
- The deviation becomes most pronounced at low collision energies where the deuteron fraction is largest; the kurtosis κσ² decreases and the skewness Sσ exhibits a local maximum as collision energy decreases.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing investigation of net-baryon fluctuation observables as potential signatures of the QCD critical endpoint and first-order phase transition, following lattice QCD results on higher-order susceptibilities (Allton, 2005) and the STAR Beam Energy Scan program (Aggarwal, 2010). It introduces a previously overlooked effect, deuteron formation via coalescence, that must be accounted for before drawing conclusions about critical phenomena, complementing prior studies of conservation laws (Bzdak, 2012), hadronic interactions (Kitazawa, 2011), and acceptance effects (Bzdak, 2012). The results suggest that experimental data on net-proton cumulants may require correction for cluster formation before comparison with lattice QCD or critical-point predictions.
