# The Theory of Parity Violation in Few-Nucleon Systems

> We review recent progress in the theoretical description of hadronic parity violation in few-nucleon systems. After introducing the different methods that have been used to study parity-violating observables we discuss the available calculations for reactions with up to five nucleons. Particular emphasis is put on effective field theory calculations where they exist, but earlier and complementary approaches are also presented. We hope this review will serve as a guide for those who wish to know what calculations are available and what further calculations need to be completed before we can claim to have a comprehensive picture of parity violation in few nucleon systems.

## Key claim

This review compiles and compares theoretical calculations of hadronic parity violation in few-nucleon systems (up to five nucleons), emphasizing that a comprehensive understanding requires a consistent analysis of a suite of observables, but that comparisons between different theoretical approaches are complicated by scale-dependent restrictions and differing basis choices for the unknown parity-violating low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical methods applied to parity-violating observables in few-nucleon systems, including meson-exchange models (particularly the DDH model of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980)), effective field theories (pionless EFT and chiral EFT), and hybrid approaches that combine model and EFT treatments. They catalog available calculations for systems with one through five nucleons, covering observables such as longitudinal analyzing powers, photon asymmetries in radiative capture, circular photon polarization, neutron spin rotation, and anapole moments, and provide translations between different parameter sets where possible.

## Key numbers

- Parity-violating components are typically suppressed relative to parity-conserving ones by a factor of \(10^{-6}\) to \(10^{-7}\) in few-nucleon systems.
- The nucleon axial-vector coupling constant is given as \(g_A = 1.27\) (Beringer et al., 2012).
- The scattering lengths are \(a^{(^1S_0)} = -1/(8\ \mathrm{MeV})\) and \(a^{(^3S_1)} = 1/(36\ \mathrm{MeV})\) for the spin-singlet and spin-triplet channels, respectively.
- The NPDGamma experiment at the Spallation Neutron Source at Oak Ridge National Laboratory measures the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in \(\vec{n}p\to d\gamma\) (Gericke et al., 2011).
- Neutron spin rotation in \(^4\)He was measured at NIST (Snow et al., 2011), yielding an upper bound consistent with earlier results (Markoff, thesis).

## Context

This review extends and updates earlier reviews of low-energy parity violation in few-nucleon systems, particularly Ramsey-Musolf and Page (2006), and the foundational DDH meson-exchange analysis by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980). It addresses the shortcomings of the DDH model identified by Zhu et al. (2004) and describes the development of effective field theory treatments following Weinberg (1990, 1991, 1992). The work enables future systematic analyses that combine experimental results from high-intensity neutron and photon sources with lattice QCD and few-body theory to extract the underlying parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions.
