# Nucleon-Quark Diquark-exchange Interaction

> In this note the nucleon-quark diquark-exchange interaction is derived using the Feynman-propagator for axial-vector diquark (D) exchange. The Feynman diquark propagator is derived and the result is a (-)-sign difference w.r.t. quark-antiquark exchange. This is due to the (-)-sign for a closed fermion loop, present in for example vector and axial-vector quark-antiquark exchange, but absent in the case of D-exchange. The calculations in these notes follow closely those for vacuum polarisation in the literature. Taking into account that the diquark D is a color $\left{\bar{3}_c\right\}$-state giving a factor +2. The result is an effective axial-vector diquark propagator. Application to $QN \rightarrow NQ$ gives a repulsive potential, which has been used in mixed nuclear-quark matter calculation.

## Key claim

The nucleon-quark diquark-exchange interaction derived using the Feynman propagator for an axial-vector diquark (D) shows a (-)-sign difference compared to quark-antiquark exchange, because the (-)-sign from a closed fermion loop present in vector and axial-vector quark-antiquark exchange is absent in D-exchange; the resulting effective axial-vector diquark propagator gives a repulsive potential for the process QN → NQ, which has been used in mixed nuclear-quark matter calculations.

## Method

The author derives the Feynman propagator for an axial-vector diquark field using standard quantum field theory second-quantization formalism, starting from the triquark representation of the nucleon current from QCD sum rules (Ioffe 1981, Ioffe 1983). The diquark propagator is computed from its composite quark-quark structure using the Wick theorem, with color factors giving a factor +2 because the diquark is a color {3̄_c} state. The momentum-space integral is evaluated using Pauli-Villars regularization (Pauli and Villars 1949), following the treatment of vacuum polarization in Bjorken and Drell (1965), and the propagator is renormalized analogously to QED vacuum polarization.

## Key numbers

- The color factor from the diquark being a color {3̄_c}-state gives a factor +2 in the propagator.
- The unrenormalized diquark propagator in momentum space is proportional to (η_μν k² − k_μ k_ν) times a logarithmic term containing ln(M²/m²), where M is a Pauli-Villars cutoff mass and m is the quark mass.
- The renormalized propagator takes the form i(Δ̃)_μν^{ab}(k) = +2i δ_{ab} (η_μν − k_μ k_ν/m_D²) / (k² − m_D² + iϵ), where m_D is the diquark mass.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of diquark exchange interactions in mixed nuclear-quark matter, following the introduction of the composite diquark field in YYR24. The derivation follows closely the vacuum polarization calculations in Bjorken and Drell (1965) and uses the Pauli-Villars regularization method (Pauli and Villars 1949). The result provides a repulsive axial-vector interaction for NQ → QN processes, which enables further calculations of mixed nuclear-quark matter equations of state.
