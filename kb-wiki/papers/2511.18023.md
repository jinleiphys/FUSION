# Two-pion exchange contributions to the relativistic chiral nuclear force at N$^3$LO

> We present the two-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction up to next-to-next-to-next-to leading order (N$^3$LO) in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. Both one-loop and two-loop diagrams are calculated with the spectral functional regularization. We show that the phase shifts for partial waves with total angular momentum $3\le J\le 5$ are in better agreement with the partial wave analysis from the Nijmegen or the SAID group than their N$^2$LO counterparts. In addition, the relativistic chiral force exhibits better convergence than its non-relativistic counterpart, suggesting the importance of relativistic corrections.

## Key claim

The two-pion exchange contributions to the relativistic chiral nuclear force at N³LO improve the description of peripheral partial waves (J ≥ 3) compared to N²LO, but the authors focus only on perturbative partial waves where non-perturbative effects are less pronounced, and they explicitly neglect three-pion exchange contributions which are argued to be negligible.

## Method

The authors calculated one-loop and two-loop Feynman diagrams for two-pion exchange in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory using spectral function regularization with a Gaussian form factor cutoff. They computed the spectral functions via Cutkosky's cutting rules applied to the corresponding \(\bar{N}N \rightarrow \pi\pi \rightarrow \bar{N}N\) annihilation processes, then transformed these to the t-channel NN amplitudes through dispersion integrals. The phase shifts and mixing angles for partial waves with total angular momentum \(3 \le J \le 5\) were obtained perturbatively, using the relativistic scattering equation rather than the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is set to \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, the nucleon mass to \(m = 939\) MeV, the pion decay constant to \(f = 92.4\) MeV, and the axial coupling constant to \(g_A = 1.276\) (with \(g_A = 1.290\) used for parts of the calculation and \(g_A = 1.267\) for the renormalized \(\pi N\) amplitude).
- The cutoff for the dispersion integrals is varied from \(0.5\) GeV to \(0.8\) GeV, covering typical choices in chiral nuclear force construction; results remain almost unchanged even with a cutoff of \(1.5\) GeV.
- The low-energy constants \(c_i\) (in GeV\(^{-1}\)) are: \(c_1 = -1.39\), \(c_2 = 4.01\), \(c_3 = -6.61\), \(c_4 = 3.92\); the \(d_j\) (in GeV\(^{-2}\)) are: \(d_1+d_2 = 4.40\), \(d_3 = -3.02\), \(d_5 = -0.62\), \(d_{14}-d_{15} = -7.15\), \(d_{18} = -0.56\).
- Phase shifts are computed up to laboratory energy \(T_{\text{lab}} = 200\) MeV, with comparisons to Nijmegen partial-wave analysis (PWA93) and SAID data.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral nuclear force framework previously developed up to N²LO by the same group (Xiao 2018, Xiao 2020, Wang 2021, Lu 2021) to N³LO, following the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory approach. It contrasts with the non-relativistic (heavy baryon) scheme of Weinberg (1990, 1991, 1992) where N⁴LO and N⁴LO+ forces have been achieved by Epelbaum (2014), Reinert (2017), and Entem (2017). The results demonstrate better convergence than non-relativistic counterparts and enable future work to pin down low-energy constants for partial waves with \(J \le 2\) and perform \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.}\) comparisons with NN scattering observables.
