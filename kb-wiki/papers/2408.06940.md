# Spherical-oblate shape coexistence in $^{94}$Zr from a model-independent analysis

> Low-lying states of $^{94}$Zr were investigated via low-energy multi-step Coulomb excitation. From the measured $γ$-ray yields, 13 reduced transition probabilities between low-spin states were determined, together with the spectroscopic quadrupole moments of the $2_{1,2}^+$ states. Based on this information, for the first time in the Zr isotopic chain, the shapes of the $0_{1,2}^+$ states including their deformation softness were inferred in a model-independent way using the quadrupole sum rules approach. The ground state of $^{94}$Zr possesses a rather diffuse shape associated with a spherical configuration, while the $0_2^+$ state is oblate and more strongly deformed. The observed features of shape coexistence in $^{94}$Zr are in agreement with Monte-Carlo shell-model predictions, and the present results are vital to refine the IBM-CM description of the Zr isotopes around $A\approx 100$ in terms of an intertwined quantum phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper establishes, for the first time in the Zr isotopic chain, model-independent shape parameters for the $0_{1,2}^+$ states in $^{94}$Zr using quadrupole sum rules, showing that the ground state has a diffuse, quasi-spherical shape while the $0_2^+$ state is oblate and more strongly deformed. The abstract's claim of "shape coexistence" is qualified by the finding that the ground state's deformation softness is so large that its shape is not well defined, with the dispersion in $\langle Q^2 \rangle$ comparable to the mean value itself.

## Method

Low-energy multi-step Coulomb excitation of a $^{94}$Zr beam at 370 MeV on a $^{208}$Pb target was performed at INFN Legnaro National Laboratories. Gamma rays were detected by the GALILEO spectrometer and LaBr$_3$:Ce detectors, while scattered $^{94}$Zr ions were detected by the SPIDER silicon array. The measured gamma-particle coincidence yields were analyzed with the GOSIA code to extract 13 reduced $E2$ transition probabilities and spectroscopic quadrupole moments of the $2_{1,2}^+$ states, from which quadrupole sum rules were applied to obtain rotationally invariant quantities $\langle Q^2 \rangle$, $\langle Q^3 \cos 3\delta \rangle$, and $\langle Q^4 \rangle$ for the $0_{1,2}^+$ states.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of $2_1^+$ state: $Q_s = +0.099_{-0.025}^{+0.010}$ $e$b
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of $2_2^+$ state: $Q_s = +0.28(3)$ $e$b
- $\langle Q^2 \rangle$ for $0_1^+$ state: $0.112(4)$ $e^2$b$^2$, with dispersion $\sigma(Q^2) = 0.143(4)$ $e^2$b$^2$ (dispersion exceeds the mean)
- $\langle Q^2 \rangle$ for $0_2^+$ state: $0.305(12)$ $e^2$b$^2$, with dispersion $\sigma(Q^2) = 0.14(3)$ $e^2$b$^2$
- $\langle \cos(3\delta) \rangle$ for $0_2^+$ state: $-0.8(2)$, corresponding to $\langle \delta \rangle = 48^{+12}_{-5}$ degrees (oblate)
- $B(E3; 3_1^- \to 0_1^+) = 33(3)$ W.u.
- $B(E2; 2_2^+ \to 0_2^+) = 18.5(9)$ W.u.
- $B(E2; 4_2^+ \to 2_2^+) = 40(3)$ W.u.

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of shape coexistence in Zr isotopes, where previous measurements in $^{94,96}$Zr (Chakraborty 2013, Kremer 2016) had inferred deformed $0_2^+$ states from enhanced $B(E2)$ values but could not distinguish between vibrational and rotational interpretations. The model-independent sum-rules approach, previously applied to other mass regions, here provides the first definitive shape determination in the Zr chain. The results agree with Monte-Carlo shell-model calculations (Togashi 2016) and refine the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) calculations of Gavrielov (2022) by fixing the $\chi$ parameter to $+0.6$, establishing the oblate nature of the deformation. This enables future studies of the intertwined quantum phase transition (IQPT) at $N=60$ and beyond, though reaching similar detail for neutron-rich isotopes will require radioactive-ion beam facilities.
