# Electromagnetic deuteron form factors in point form relativistic quantum mechanics

> A study of electromagnetic structure of the deuteron in the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics is presented. The deuteron form factors dependencies on the transferred 4-momentum Q are calculated. We compare results obtained with different realistic deuteron wave functions stemming from Nijmegen-I, Nijmegen-II, JISP16, CD-Bonn, Paris and Moscow (with forbidden states) potentials. A nucleon form factor parametrization consistent with modern experimental analysis was used as an input data.

## Key claim

The point-form relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) spectator approximation, when applied to elastic electron-deuteron scattering using non-relativistic deuteron wave functions from realistic potentials, yields deuteron electromagnetic form factors that agree well with experiment for momentum transfers up to about 5 Fm⁻¹, but discrepancies at larger Q are comparable to the differences between results from different potentials and may require inclusion of meson exchange currents.

## Method

The author calculates the deuteron charge monopole (G_C), charge quadrupole (G_Q), and magnetic dipole (G_M) form factors as functions of the transferred 4-momentum Q using the point-form RQM spectator approximation of the electromagnetic current operator, without expanding it in powers of the vector-parameter h. The internal deuteron wave functions in momentum space are taken from seven realistic potentials (Nijmegen-I, Nijmegen-II, JISP16, CD-Bonn, Paris, Argonne18, and two Moscow-type potentials with forbidden states), and the nucleon electromagnetic form factors are taken from a phenomenological parametrization consistent with modern experimental analysis.

## Key numbers

- Form factors are calculated for transferred 4-momentum Q up to 7.5 Fm⁻¹.
- Static magnetic moment: experimental value G_M(0) = 1.7148; calculated values range from 1.696 (Paris, Argonne18) to 1.720 (JISP16) in the relativistic calculation.
- Static quadrupole moment: experimental value G_Q(0) = 25.83; calculated values range from 24.5 (Moscow06) to 26.3 (JISP16) in the relativistic calculation.
- The S-wave functions for all potentials except JISP16 change sign at q ≈ 2 Fm⁻¹; D-wave functions change sign at q ≈ 6-8 Fm⁻¹.
- The relativistic correction to the deuteron binding energy is a factor of (1 - ε/4m) relative to the nonrelativistic relationship, where ε = 2.2233 MeV (effective) versus experimental 2.2246 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous point-form RQM studies of NN scattering (Khokhlov, 2013), pp bremsstrahlung, deuteron photodisintegration, and exclusive electrodisintegration to the elastic eD scattering channel. The results are compared with experimental data compilations from Garson and with model calculations by Arenhovel showing that meson exchange currents may give significant effects. The approach enables future calculation of neutron electromagnetic form factors compatible with the Moscow potential model, which has not previously been used for their extraction.
