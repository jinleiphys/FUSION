# Hybrid Stars in an SU(3) Parity Doublet Model

> We apply an extended version of the SU(3) parity model, containing quark degrees of freedom, to study neutron stars. The model successfully reproduces the main thermodynamic features of QCD which allows us to describe the composition of dense matter. Chiral symmetry restoration is realized inside the star and the chiral partners of the baryons appear, their masses becoming degenerate. Furthermore, quark degrees of freedom appear in a transition to a deconfined state. Performing an investigation of the macroscopic properties of neutron stars, we show that observational constraints, like mass and thermal evolution, are satisfied and new predictions can be made.

## Key claim
The SU(3) parity doublet model, extended with quark degrees of freedom and a Polyakov loop for deconfinement, can simultaneously satisfy neutron star mass constraints (up to 1.96 M⊙) and reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon chemical potential, but only when the nuclear matter compressibility is allowed to be high (525 MeV); tuning to a more realistic compressibility (330 MeV) reduces the maximum mass to 1.64 M⊙, which falls short of the 1.97 M⊙ observational constraint.

## Method
The authors construct a unified equation of state by coupling an SU(3) parity doublet model for baryons (including the full baryon octet and their chiral partners) to a quark phase via a Polyakov-loop potential, with excluded-volume suppression of hadrons at high density. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for charge-neutral, chemically equilibrated matter at T=0 and T=30 MeV, and compute thermal evolution using standard neutrino emission processes (direct Urca, modified Urca, Bremsstrahlung) with neutron superfluidity and optional quark pairing.

## Key numbers
- Maximum cold star mass for Model A: 1.96 M⊙ (radius 10.70 km, central baryon density 1.16 fm⁻³, central quark fraction ~60%).
- Maximum cold star mass for Model B: 1.64 M⊙ (radius 9.30 km, central baryon density 1.58 fm⁻³, central quark fraction ~50%).
- Maximum warm star (T=30 MeV) mass for Model A: 2.15 M⊙ (radius 15.17 km, central baryon density 0.90 fm⁻³, central quark fraction ~50%).
- Maximum warm star (T=30 MeV) mass for Model B: 1.75 M⊙ (radius 14.28 km, central baryon density 1.18 fm⁻³, central quark fraction ~40%).
- Quark appearance thresholds: 1.56 ρ₀ for Model A, 2.16 ρ₀ for Model B (ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³).
- Nuclear saturation properties: binding energy ~ -16 MeV, saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³; symmetry energy 32.6 MeV (Model A) and 32.4 MeV (Model B); slope L = 99 MeV (Model A) and 92 MeV (Model B).
- Compressibility: 525 MeV (Model A), ~330 MeV (Model B).
- Bare mass parameter m₀ = 810 MeV; strange quark mass mₛ = 150 MeV; quark explicit mass δm_q = 5 MeV; quark mass parameter m₀q = 200 MeV.
- Excluded volume: 1 fm³ (Model A), 0.5 fm³ (Model B).
- Quark pairing gap Δ = 10 MeV (for paired cooling calculations).

## Context
This work extends the SU(3) parity doublet model of Steinheimer (2011) from symmetric matter to neutron star conditions (charge neutrality, chemical equilibrium), and builds on prior parity-model neutron star studies by Dexheimer (2007, 2008). It addresses the tension between reproducing the high mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 M⊙, Demorest 2010) and maintaining realistic nuclear compressibility, a known issue in the parity model. The cooling results show that chiral partners can invert the usual mass-cooling relation (heavier stars cool slower), similar to effects seen in color-superconducting hybrid star models by Noda (2011). The approach enables future studies of proto-neutron star evolution, black-hole-neutron-star mergers, and the interplay between chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement in dense matter.
