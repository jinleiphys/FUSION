# Transition densities and form factors in the triangular $α$-cluster model of $^{12}$C with application to $^{12}$C+$α$ scattering

> Densities and transition densities are computed in an equilateral triangular alpha-cluster model for $^{12}$C, in which each $α$ particle is taken as a gaussian density distribution. The ground-state, the symmetric vibration (Hoyle state) and the asymmetric bend vibration are analyzed in a molecular approach and dissected into their components in a series of harmonic functions, revealing their intrinsic structures. The transition densities in the laboratory frame are then used to construct form-factors and to compute DWBA inelastic cross-sections for the $^{12}$C$(α, α')$ reaction. The comparison with experimental data indicates that the simple geometrical model with rotations and vibrations gives a reliable description of reactions where $α$-cluster degrees of freedom are involved.

# Transition densities and form factors in the triangular α-cluster model of ¹²C with application to ¹²C+α scattering

## Key claim

The equilateral triangular α-cluster model, with rotations and vibrations of three α particles treated as Gaussian density distributions, provides a reliable description of both structure observables and inelastic scattering cross-sections for ¹²C, but only when the radial parameter β and vibrational amplitudes are tuned to reproduce a minimal set of known matrix elements or electromagnetic transition rates. The model reproduces the shape and magnitude of DWBA cross-sections for the ¹²C(α,α') reaction at 240 MeV, though the agreement for the 0₁⁺ → 1₁⁻ transition depends sensitively on the choice of the χ₂ parameter, which cannot be independently fixed from available data.

## Method

The authors construct the ¹²C density as a sum of three Gaussian α-particle densities (α = 0.56(2) fm⁻²) placed at the vertices of an equilateral triangle with radial parameter β = 1.82 fm. They expand the intrinsic densities in spherical harmonics, transform to the laboratory frame, and compute transition densities for the ground-state band (A symmetry), the symmetric breathing vibration (Hoyle state, A-type), and the asymmetric bend vibration (E-type, doubly degenerate). These transition densities are folded with the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction to produce double-folding form factors, which are then used in DWBA calculations of inelastic α-scattering cross-sections at 240 MeV bombarding energy.

## Key numbers

- α-particle Gaussian width parameter: α = 0.56(2) fm⁻²
- Radial parameter for the equilateral triangle: β = 1.82 fm (chosen to simultaneously reproduce ground-state radius and B(E2))
- Ground-state root-mean-square radius: ⟨r²⟩¹/² = 2.45 fm
- B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) = 7.86 e²fm⁴
- B(E3; 3₁⁻ → 0₁⁺) = 65.07 e²fm⁶
- B(E4; 4₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) = 96.99 e²fm⁸
- Hoyle state (0₂⁺) root-mean-square radius: 3.44 fm (with Δβᴬ = 1.2 fm)
- B(E2; 2₂⁺ → 0₁⁺) = 0.58 e²fm⁴ (Hoyle band to ground band)
- B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) = 2.90 e²fm⁴
- B(E3; 3₂⁻ → 0₁⁺) = 70.42 e²fm⁶
- M(E0; 0₂⁺ → 0₁⁺) = 5.4 e fm² (used to fix χ₁ = 0.247255)
- Isoscalar dipole matrix element M(IS1; 0₁⁺ → 1₁⁻) ≈ 0.31 e fm³ (with χ₂ = 0.136)
- Bombarding energy for DWBA calculations: 240 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Algebraic Cluster Model approach of Bijker and Iachello (2002, 2014) and the geometric α-cluster descriptions of Delion (2017) by connecting structure calculations directly to reaction observables via transition densities and double-folding form factors. It builds on earlier folding-model analyses by Kanada-En'yo and Ogata (2019) using Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics wavefunctions, and on the coupled-channel work of Ito (2018) linking inelastic scattering to the extended nature of the 2₂⁺ state in the Hoyle band. The results enable future coupled-channels calculations that include continuum effects and three-body dynamics, which are necessary to properly describe the unbound nature of states above the three-α threshold and to resolve ambiguities in the population of states like the 2₂⁺ via two-step processes.
