# Numerical Evidence for Multiplicative Logarithmic Corrections from Marginal Operators

> Field theory calculations predict multiplicative logarithmic corrections to correlation functions from marginally irrelevant operators. However, for the numerically most suitable model - the spin-1/2 chain - these corrections have been controversial. In this paper, the spin-spin correlation function of the antiferromagnetic spin-1/2 chain is calculated numerically in the presence of a next nearest neighbor coupling J2 for chains of up to 32 sites. By varying the coupling strength J2 we can control the effect of the marginal operator, and our results unambiguously confirm the field theory predictions. The critical value at which the marginal operator vanishes has been determined to be at J2 = 0.241167 +/- 0.000005J.

## Key claim

The paper provides unambiguous numerical confirmation that field theory predictions of multiplicative logarithmic corrections to spin-spin correlation functions from marginally irrelevant operators are valid for the spin-1/2 chain, but only when the bare coupling constant is sufficiently small (i.e., near the critical next-nearest-neighbor coupling J2) and when the analysis uses a fixed ratio r = L/2 rather than extrapolating to infinite system size.

## Method

The author performed exact diagonalization using a modified Lanczos method on periodic antiferromagnetic spin-1/2 chains of up to 32 sites with a next-nearest-neighbor coupling J2. The spin-spin correlation function G(r) was computed at fixed ratio r = L/2 for various J2 values, and the multiplicative correction r G(r) was extracted via cubic spline interpolation between even and odd r data. The results were fit to the renormalization-group predicted form r G(r) = sqrt(a λ0 ln(r/r0)) to extract the bare coupling λ0 and the length scale r0.

## Key numbers

* Critical value of next-nearest-neighbor coupling: J2_crit = 0.241167 ± 0.000005 J, determined from the energy difference between the first two excited states (S=0 and S=1) scaling as 1/L^3
* Ultraviolet cutoff: r1 = 0.85 (lattice spacing units), determined from the relation λ0 ln(r1/r0) = 1
* Normalization constant: a = 0.0296, independent of λ0
* Bare coupling expansion coefficients: c1 ≈ 1.723/J, c2 ≈ -1.35/J^2, c3 ≈ 1.76/J^3 for λ0 = c1 ΔJ2 + c2 ΔJ2^2 + c3 ΔJ2^3
* Correlation function values for J2 = 0 at r = 16: <Sz(0) Sz(16)> = 0.0151987 (for r = L/2 = 16)
* Correlation function values at J2 = J2_crit for r = 16: <Sz(0) Sz(16)> = 0.0105511

## Context

This work resolves a controversy in the spin-1/2 chain literature where previous numerical studies by Hallberg, Horsch, and Martinez (1995) and Sandvik and Scalapino (1993) found different or no multiplicative logarithmic corrections, contradicting the field theory predictions of Affleck, Gepner, Schulz, and Ziman (1989). By introducing a next-nearest-neighbor coupling J2 to control the marginal operator strength and using a novel fixed-ratio analysis (r = L/2) instead of extrapolating to infinite system size, the author demonstrates that the field theory calculations are correct. The results enable accurate predictions of correlation functions for the spin-1/2 chain and carry over to other models with marginal operators such as the 1-D Hubbard model, other half-integer spin chains, and φ^4 models.
