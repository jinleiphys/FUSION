# Pseudogap regime of the unitary Fermi gas with lattice auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo in the continuum limit

> The unitary Fermi gas (UFG) is a strongly correlated system of two-species (spin-1/2) fermions with a short-range attractive interaction modeled by a contact interaction and has attracted much interest across different disciplines. The UFG is considered a paradigm for strongly correlated superfluids and has been investigated extensively, with generally good agreement found between theory and experiment. However, the extent of a pseudogap regime above the critical temperature $T_c$ for superfluidity is still debated both theoretically and experimentally. Here we study thermodynamic properties of the UFG across the superfluid phase transition using finite-temperature lattice auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) methods in the canonical ensemble of fixed particle numbers. We extrapolate our lattice AFMC results to the continuous time and continuum limits, thus removing the systematic error associated with the finite filling factor of previous AFMC studies. We determine the critical temperature to be $T_c=0.16(1)\, T_{F}$. For the largest particle number studied $N=114$, the energy-staggering pairing gap is suppressed above a pairing scale temperature of $T^{*}\approx 0.2\,T_F$. The spin susceptibility displays moderate suppression above $T_c$ with a spin gap temperature of $T_s\approx 0.2 \,T_F$. We also calculate a free energy-staggering pairing gap, which shows substantially reduced statistical errors when compared with the energy-staggering gap, allowing for a clear signature of pairing correlations in the finite-size system. All results indicate that the pseudogap regime is narrow, with pseudogap signatures emerging at temperatures below $T^{*}\approx 0.2 \, T_F$. The reduced statistical errors of the free energy gap enable an extrapolation at low temperatures, allowing an estimate of the zero-temperature pairing gap $Δ_E = 0.576(24) \, ε_F$.

## Key claim

The pseudogap regime of the unitary Fermi gas (UFG) is narrow, with pairing signatures emerging only below approximately 0.2 \(T_F\), and the critical temperature for superfluidity is \(T_c = 0.16(1)\, T_F\); this result is conditional on extrapolating lattice auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) results to the continuous time and continuum limits, removing the systematic error from finite filling factor present in previous AFMC studies.

## Method

The authors used finite-temperature lattice auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) in the canonical ensemble of fixed particle numbers for spin-balanced homogeneous UFG with a contact on-site attractive interaction on a cubic lattice. They performed simulations for particle numbers \(N = 40, 66, 114\) and extrapolated results to the continuum limit (zero filling factor) and continuous imaginary-time limit, using a linear fit in \(\nu^{1/3}\) where \(\nu\) is the filling factor. Observables calculated include the condensate fraction, energy-staggering pairing gap, spin susceptibility, and a free energy-staggering pairing gap, with finite-size scaling applied to the condensate fraction to determine \(T_c\).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for superfluidity: \(T_c = 0.16(1)\, T_F\), determined from finite-size scaling of the condensate fraction.
- Pairing scale temperature for \(N=114\): \(T^{*} \approx 0.2\, T_F\), above which the energy-staggering pairing gap is suppressed.
- Spin gap temperature for \(N=114\): \(T_s \approx 0.2\, T_F\), from moderate suppression of the spin susceptibility above \(T_c\).
- Zero-temperature pairing gap: \(\Delta_E = 0.576(24)\, \varepsilon_F\), estimated from extrapolation of the free energy-staggering pairing gap.
- Largest particle number simulated: \(N = 114\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice AFMC studies by the same authors (Jensen et al., 2020) that used finite filling factor \(\nu \approx 0.6\) and found a negligible pseudogap regime, but had sizable lattice effects with effective range \(k_F r_e \simeq 0.41\). The present continuum-limit results resolve those systematic errors and enable direct comparison with experiments. The findings support the self-consistent Luttinger-Ward diagrammatic approach (Haussmann et al., 2009; Enss et al., 2012) which found \(T^{*}\) and \(T_c\) very close for the UFG, in contrast to non-self-consistent diagrammatic approaches (Perali et al., 2011; Kashimura et al., 2012; Tajima et al., 2014) and earlier spherical-cutoff AFMC studies (Magierski et al., 2009, 2011; Wlazlowski et al., 2013) that found more pronounced pseudogap signatures. The narrow pseudogap regime reported here helps reconcile theoretical predictions with experiments that saw no gapped signatures (Ku et al., 2012; Navon et al., 2009, 2011; Sommer et al., 2011).
