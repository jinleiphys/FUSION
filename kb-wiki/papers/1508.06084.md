# Chiral dynamics of S-wave baryon resonances

> As the pion mass approaches a critical value $m_π^\star$ from below, an $S$-wave resonance crosses pion-baryon threshold and becomes a bound state with arbitrarily small binding energy, thus driving the scattering length to diverge. I explore the consequences of chiral symmetry for the values of $m_π$ close to $m_π^\star$. It turns out that chiral symmetry is crucial for an $S$-wave resonance to be able to stand very near threshold and in the meantime to remain narrow, provided that the mass splitting is reasonably small. The effective range of pion-baryon scattering is unexpectedly large, proportional to $ 4πf_π^2/m_π^3$ when $m_π$ is around $m_π^\star$. As a result, this unexpected large length scale causes universality relations to break down much sooner than naively expected.

# Chiral dynamics of S-wave baryon resonances

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry eliminates the need for a second fine-tuning parameter to keep an S-wave baryon resonance narrow and near threshold, provided the mass splitting between the resonance and its decay products is much smaller than the intermediate scale √(4π)fπ ≈ 328 MeV. The effective range becomes unexpectedly large, proportional to 4πfπ²/mπ³, which causes universality relations based solely on a large scattering length to break down much sooner than naively expected.

## Method

The author constructs a chiral effective field theory for S-wave pion-baryon scattering near threshold, using the charmed baryon Λc⁺(2595) coupled to πΣc(2455) as the physical example. The leading-order Lagrangian includes the s-channel exchange of the excited baryon and the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, with the nonrelativistic nature of the pion systematically accounted for in the power counting. The scattering amplitude is obtained by resumming the geometric series of connected s-channel exchanges, yielding an effective range expansion form, and next-to-leading order corrections from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term and pion recoil effects are computed.

## Key numbers

- The critical pion mass mπ* is approximately equal to the mass splitting Δ = Λc⁺(2595) - Σc(2455) ≈ 138 MeV (the physical pion mass)
- The intermediate mass scale √(4π)fπ = 328 MeV, with fπ = 92.4 MeV
- The leading-order effective range r0 = -4πfπ²/(h²mπ³), where h is the dimensionless coupling constant (assumed of order unity)
- From PDG values for Λc⁺(2595), the effective range r = 19.5 fm, giving h = 0.64
- The condition for chiral symmetry to facilitate a narrow near-threshold resonance is Δ ≪ √(4π)fπ ≈ 328 MeV
- The validity range of universality is |(mπ - mπ*)/mπ| ≪ (Δ/328 MeV)⁴
- The lower bound of δ for the resonance to remain narrow in the Λc⁺(2595) case is around a couple of MeV
- The leading-order binding energy B0 = (h⁴/2)ε²mπ (√(1 - 2δ/(h⁴ε²mπ)) - 1)², where ε = (mπ/√(4π)fπ)²

## Context

This work extends the study of near-threshold S-wave resonances beyond the general two-body scattering theory, which normally requires two-parameter fine-tuning for an S-wave resonance to approach threshold (Hyodo 2013, Hanhart 2014). The author shows that chiral symmetry provides the extra constraint, making the Λc⁺(2595) system a natural realization. The results enable lattice QCD studies with variable quark masses to explore the transition from resonance to bound state, and demonstrate that the large effective range invalidates standard universality relations (Braaten 2004) that account only for a large scattering length. The work contrasts with descriptions of X(3872) as a weakly-bound molecular state decaying through three-body channels (Fleming 2007, Hanhart 2010, Guo 2013).
