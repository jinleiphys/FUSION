# New Procedure for Evaluation of U(3) Coupling and Recoupling Coefficients

> A simple method to calculate Wigner coupling coefficients and Racah recoupling coefficients for U(3) in two group-subgroup chains is presented. While the canonical U(3)->U(2)->U(1) coupling and recoupling coefficients are applicable to any system that respects U(3) symmetry, the U(3)->SO(3) coupling coefficients are more specific to nuclear structure studies. This new procedure precludes the use of binomial coefficients and alternating sums which were used in the 1973 formulation of Draayer and Akiyama, and hence provides faster and more accurate output of requested results. The resolution of the outer multiplicity is based on the null space concept of the U(3) generators proposed by Arne Alex et al., whereas the inner multiplicity in the angular momentum subgroup chain is obtained from the dimension of the null space of the SO(3) raising operator. A C++ library built on this new methodology will be published in a complementary journal that specializes in the management and distribution of such programs.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for calculating U(3) coupling and recoupling coefficients that avoids binomial coefficients and alternating sums used in the 1973 formulation by Draayer and Akiyama, but the method is limited to two specific group-subgroup chains: the canonical U(3) to U(2) to U(1) chain and the U(3) to SO(3) chain relevant to nuclear structure.

## Method

The authors compute U(3) Wigner coupling coefficients by solving the null space of a matrix constructed from the action of raising generators on the highest weight state, using the null space concept of U(3) generators proposed by Arne Alex et al. (2011). For lower weight states, the coefficients are obtained by applying lowering generators and solving a system of linear equations that must be reduced to a square invertible matrix. Recoupling coefficients (6-U(3) and 9-U(3)) are then derived by fixing the highest weight states of intermediate and final irreps and solving linear equations with the coupling coefficients as known quantities.

## Key numbers

* The dimension of a U(3) irrep [n13, n23, n33] is given by (1+n13-n23)(1+n23-n33)(1+(n13-n33)/2).
* The dimension of an SU(3) irrep (lambda, mu) is given by (1+lambda)(1+mu)(1+(lambda+mu)/2).
* The outer multiplicity rho_max for coupled irreps is determined by the dimensionality of the null space of the matrix P(HW''), with the number of linearly independent solutions giving the multiplicity.
* For the SU(3) to SO(3) reduction, the angular momentum L values follow the rule: K = min(lambda, mu), min(lambda, mu)-2, ..., 0 or 1; for K>0, L = K, K+1, ..., K+max(lambda, mu); for K=0, L = max(lambda, mu), max(lambda, mu)-2, ..., 0 or 1.

## Context

This work extends the 1973 formulation of Draayer and Akiyama for U(3) coupling and recoupling coefficients by replacing the earlier method that used binomial coefficients and alternating sums with a null-space approach adapted from Alex et al. (2011). The new procedure enables faster and more accurate computation, which is expected to facilitate shell-model descriptions of heavier and more complex atomic nuclei, and the authors anticipate a follow-up paper focused on U(4) coefficients needed for Wigner's Supermultiplet Symmetry.
