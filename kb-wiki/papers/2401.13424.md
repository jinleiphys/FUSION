# Revisiting the helium isotope-shift puzzle with improved uncertainties from nuclear structure corrections

> Measurements of the difference between the squared charge radii of the helion ($^3$He nucleus) and the $α$-particle ($^4$He nucleus) have been characterized by longstanding tensions, recently spotlighted in the 3.6 $σ$ discrepancy of the extractions from ordinary atoms versus those from muonic atoms. Here, we present a novel analysis of uncertainties in nuclear structure corrections that must be supplied by theory to enable the extraction of the difference in radii from spectroscopic experiments. We use modern Bayesian inference techniques to quantify uncertainties stemming from the truncation of the chiral effective field theory expansion of the nuclear force for both muonic and ordinary atoms. With the new nuclear structure input, the helium isotope-shift puzzle cannot be explained, rather it is reinforced to a 4 $σ$ discrepancy.

## Key claim

The helium isotope-shift puzzle, a 3.6 sigma discrepancy between extractions of the squared charge radius difference from ordinary atoms versus muonic atoms, cannot be explained by improved nuclear structure corrections; rather, the discrepancy is reinforced to a 4 sigma level after applying Bayesian uncertainty quantification to chiral effective field theory truncation errors.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear structure corrections for both muonic and ordinary helium atoms using the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method to solve the few-nucleon Schrodinger equation with chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) interactions at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), and next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO). They applied Bayesian inference techniques to quantify uncertainties from the truncation of the chiEFT expansion, using the AV18+UIX phenomenological potential as the reference for the expansion coefficients. For muonic atoms, they computed the two-photon exchange correction delta^A_TPE,mu; for ordinary atoms, they computed the dipole polarizability contribution tilde_alpha_pol,e.

## Key numbers

- For muonic helium-3: delta^A_TPE,mu = -14.868(357) meV at N3LO, compared to previous value -14.72(31) meV
- For muonic helium-4: delta^A_TPE,mu = -8.751(303) meV at N3LO, compared to previous value -8.49(39) meV (a 22% uncertainty reduction)
- Extracted charge radii: r_ch(^3He) = 1.9704(10) fm and r_ch(^4He) = 1.6793(10) fm
- For ordinary atoms: tilde_alpha_pol = 3.514(65) fm^3 for ^3He and 1.909(78) fm^3 for ^4He at N3LO
- Muonic-atom extraction of delta r^2 = 1.0626(29) fm^2, improving the previous determination 1.0636(31) fm^2 by about 6%
- Ordinary-atom extraction of delta r^2 = 1.0758(15) fm^2, nearly unchanged from the value 1.0757(15) from van der Werf et al. (2023)
- To resolve the puzzle, delta mu would need to change by 0.00875 fm^2, which is excluded at the 95% confidence level

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of nuclear structure corrections for muonic helium by Ji et al. (2013, 2018) and for ordinary helium by Pachucki (2007), but provides the first consistent order-by-order chiEFT treatment with Bayesian uncertainty quantification for both systems simultaneously. The results reinforce the discrepancy first highlighted by Schumann et al. (2023) between muonic and ordinary atom extractions, now at 4 sigma, suggesting that the puzzle likely originates from underestimated systematic errors in experiments or neglected correction terms rather than from nuclear structure theory. The extracted charge radius for ^4He constitutes the most precise value to date.
