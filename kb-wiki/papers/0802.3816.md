# Study of $η$ photoproduction on the proton in a chiral constituent quark approach via one-gluon-exchange model

> A formalism based on a chiral quark model ($χ$QM) approach complemented with a one-gluon exchange model, to take into account the breakdown of the $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ symmetry, is presented. The configuration mixing of wave functions for nucleon and resonances are derived. % With few adjustable parameters, differential cross-section and polarized beam asymmetry for the $γp \to ηp$ process are calculated and successfully compared with the data in the centre-of-mass energy range from threshold up to 2 GeV. The known resonances $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$, as well as two new $S_{11}$ and $D_{15}$ resonances are found to be dominant in the reaction mechanism. Besides, connections among the scattering amplitudes of the $χ$QM approach and the helicity amplitudes, as well as decay widths of resonances are established. Possible contributions from the so-called "missing resonances" are investigated and found to be negligible.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model, when augmented with one-gluon-exchange configuration mixing to account for SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking, can reproduce η photoproduction data from threshold to 2 GeV with significantly fewer free parameters than previous approaches, but only if three new resonances (S11, D13, D15) are included, reducing the reduced χ² from 12.37 to 2.31.

## Method

The authors start from an effective chiral Lagrangian and derive CGLN amplitudes for γp → ηp within the SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry. They then introduce configuration mixing of baryon wave functions via a one-gluon-exchange (OGE) hyperfine interaction Hamiltonian, which generates mixing angles and coefficients for nucleon and resonance states. The model is fitted simultaneously to 1220 differential cross-section data points and 184 polarized beam asymmetry data points from MAMI, CLAS, CB-ELSA, LNS, and GRAAL, plus the PDG mass spectrum of 12 known nucleon resonances, using the CERN MINUIT code.

## Key numbers

- Reduced χ² improves from 12.37 (model A, without new resonances) to 2.31 (model B, with three new resonances)
- New S11 resonance: mass 1717 MeV, width 217 MeV, symmetry breaking coefficient 0.59
- New D13 resonance: mass 1943 MeV, width 139 MeV, symmetry breaking coefficient -0.19
- New D15 resonance: mass 2090 MeV, width 328 MeV, symmetry breaking coefficient 2.89
- η-nucleon coupling constant gηNN = 0.449 (model B)
- Constituent quark mass mq = 304 MeV (model B)
- Harmonic oscillator strength α = 285 MeV (model B)
- QCD coupling constant αs = 1.977 (model B)
- Switching off S11(1535) increases χ² to 162; switching off new S11 increases χ² to 12.69
- Switching off D13(1520) increases χ² to 9.83; switching off F15(1680) increases χ² to 4.82
- Switching off P13(1720) increases χ² to 4.15
- Extracted masses for known resonances: S11(1535) = 1473 MeV, S11(1650) = 1620 MeV, D13(1520) = 1511 MeV, F15(1680) = 1723 MeV, P13(1720) = 1718 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach of Li (1997) and Saghai (2001), which previously required one free parameter per resonance to account for SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking. By replacing those adjustable parameters with configuration mixing derived from the one-gluon-exchange model of Isgur and Karl (1978, 1979), the number of free parameters is significantly reduced. The results support the need for new S11 and D15 resonances, consistent with findings from the Bonn-Gatchina coupled-channel approach of Sarantsev (2005) and the CB-ELSA data of Crede (2005), while finding negligible contributions from the "missing" resonances predicted by the OGE spectrum. The formalism also establishes explicit connections between the chiral quark model scattering amplitudes and the photoexcitation helicity amplitudes and partial decay widths of resonances.
