# Density distributions of superheavy nuclei

> We employed the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model to investigate the density distributions and their dependence on nuclear shapes and isospins in the superheavy mass region. Different Skyrme forces were used for the calculations with a special comparison to the experimental data in $^{208}$Pb. The ground-state deformations, nuclear radii, neutron skin thicknesses and $α$-decay energies were also calculated. Density distributions were discussed with the calculations of single-particle wavefunctions and shell fillings. Calculations show that deformations have considerable effects on the density distributions, with a detailed discussion on the $^{292}$120 nucleus. Earlier predictions of remarkably low central density are not supported when deformation is allowed for.

## Key claim

Deformation effects significantly alter the density distributions of superheavy nuclei, and earlier predictions of remarkably low central density (semi-bubble structures) in nuclei such as \(^{292}\)120 are not supported when deformation is allowed for; only a weak central depression remains in the deformed case.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model with axially symmetric shapes, using the SkI4, SLy4, SLy7, and SkI3 parameter sets. Pairing correlations were treated in the BCS scheme with a delta-pairing force. Ground-state deformations (\(\beta_2\) and \(\beta_4\)) were determined by energy minimization, and density distributions were calculated from single-particle wavefunctions and occupation probabilities for even-even superheavy nuclei with Z=104–120.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{208}\)Pb, the SkI4 force gives proton rms radius 5.43 fm, neutron rms radius 5.61 fm, and neutron-skin thickness 0.18 fm; the SLy4 and SLy7 forces give 0.16 fm, and the experimental value from antiprotonic atoms is 0.15±0.02 fm.
- For the predicted spherical doubly magic nucleus \(^{298}\)114, the SkI4 force gives neutron-skin thickness 0.16 fm, with neutron rms radius 6.33 fm and proton rms radius 6.16 fm.
- For the experimentally known even-even superheavy nuclei, the SkI4 force reproduces \(\alpha\)-decay energies within a few hundred keV (largest difference 640 keV in \(^{266}\)Hs) and binding energies within ≈4 MeV.
- For \(^{270,280,290}\)110, the SkI4 force gives equilibrium deformations \(\beta_2\) = 0.25, 0.19, and 0.05, respectively; the SLy7 force gives \(\beta_2\) = 0.25, 0.17, and 0.05.
- For \(^{292}\)120, the SkI4 force predicts three energy minima at \(\beta_2\) = 0.11, –0.12, and 0.52; the spherical case shows a significant central depression, but at the small deformations only weak central depressions remain.

## Context

This work extends earlier spherical SHF calculations by Bender et al. (1999) and RMF predictions of semi-bubble nuclei by Decharge et al. (1999) by systematically including axially symmetric deformation. The results show that deformation competes with central bubble formation, reducing the central depression in \(^{292}\)120. The study enables more realistic predictions of density distributions for superheavy nuclei, which are important for understanding reaction cross sections and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, as discussed by Horowitz et al. (2001) in the context of neutron-star crusts.
