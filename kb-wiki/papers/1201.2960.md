# On the lifetime of the 2+ state in 10C

> The lifetime of the J=2+ state in 10C was measured using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method following the inverse kinematics p(10B,n)10C reaction at 95 MeV. The 2+ state, at 3354 keV, has tau = 219\pm(7)stat \pm(10)sys fs corresponding to a B(E2) # of 8.8(3) e2fm4. This measurement,combined with that recently determined for 10Be (9.2(3) e2fm4), provides a unique challenge to abinitio calculations, testing the structure of these states, including the isospin symmetry of the wave functions. Quantum Monte Carlo calculations using realistic two- and three-nucleon Hamiltonians that reproduce the 10Be B(E2) value generally predict a larger 10C B(E2) probability but with considerable sensitivity to the admixture of different spatial symmetry components in the wave functions, and to the three-nucleon potential used.

## Key claim

The lifetime of the 2+ state in 10C was measured to be substantially longer than the previous value from Fisher et al. (1968), yielding a B(E2) value only 2% different from its mirror transition in 10Be, which Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations using the best available Hamiltonian fail to reproduce despite successfully reproducing the 10Be value.

## Method

The 2+ state at 3354 keV in 10C was populated via the inverse kinematics p(10B,n)10C reaction at 95 MeV using beams from the ATLAS accelerator at Argonne National Laboratory. Recoiling 10C nuclei were selected by the Fragment Mass Analyzer and gamma rays were detected with the Gammasphere array of 100 HPGe detectors; the lifetime was extracted using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method by measuring the average recoil velocity at the time of gamma-ray emission from centroid shifts across 16 Gammasphere angle rings, with five separate target and backing combinations.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the 2+ state in 10C: tau = 219 +/- 7 (stat) +/- 10 (sys) fs
- B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) in 10C: 8.8 +/- 0.3 e2fm4
- B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) in 10Be from the same group's prior work: 9.2 +/- 0.3 e2fm4
- Excitation energy of the 2+ state in 10C: 3354 keV
- Recoil velocity at production: beta ~ 0.131; average beta at gamma-ray emission: 0.12422 +/- 0.00024
- Range of forward-shifted to backward-shifted gamma-ray peaks: more than 800 keV

## Context

This measurement extends the work of McCutchan et al. (2009) on 10Be and supersedes the pioneering but imprecise DSAM measurement by Fisher et al. (1968), which had only ~20% precision. The near-equality of the B(E2) values in the 10C-10Be mirror pair provides a stringent test for ab initio calculations; the Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations with the AV18+IL7 Hamiltonian that reproduce the 10Be value overpredict the 10C value, indicating sensitivity to the three-nucleon potential and the admixture of spatial symmetry components in the wave functions. This result can guide development of improved three-nucleon potentials and tests of other approaches such as no-core shell model calculations with the CD-Bonn 2000 potential, which show better agreement for 10C.
