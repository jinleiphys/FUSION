# Evidence of the mixed phase formation in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Searchers for various irregularities in the behavior of thermodynamic quantities at chemical freeze-out (CFO) are rather important in a view of experimental studies of quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Using the multicomponent hadron resonance gas model (HRGM), developed in (Sagun, 2014; Bugaev et al., 2015), we performed a high-quality fit of 111 hadronic ratios measured for 14 values of the center of mass collision energies between 2.7 GeV and 200 GeV with the overall fit quality $χ^2/dof \simeq 0.95$. In addition to previously reported singularities (Bugaev et al., 2015) at CFO we found that the hadron yield ratios $\fracΛ{p}$, $\frac{K^{+}}{p}$, $\frac{K^{+}}Λ$, $\frac{Ω^{-}}{p}$ and $\frac{Ξ^{-}}{p}$ measured in central nuclear collisions demonstrate a significant change of slope in the same range of center of mass collision energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3-4.9$ GeV (Bugaev et al., 2015). This change of slopes is accompanied by a dramatic increase of resonance decays at CFO. Also at CFO the trace anomaly and baryonic density demonstrate the pronounced peaks at the collision energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.9 $ GeV. We argue that all these and previously found irregularities provide an evidence for the QGP formation in nuclear collisions at about $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.9 $ GeV.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a set of irregularities observed in hadron yield ratios and thermodynamic quantities at chemical freeze-out in the narrow center-of-mass collision energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3-4.9\) GeV provide evidence for the formation of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) mixed phase in nucleus-nucleus collisions, but they hedge that a second set of peaks observed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2\) GeV may indicate another phase transformation requiring higher-precision data for confirmation.

## Method

The authors used the multicomponent hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) with different hard-core radii for pions, kaons, \(\Lambda\)-hyperons, other mesons, and other baryons to fit 111 hadronic yield ratios measured at midrapidity in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at 14 center-of-mass energies from 2.7 GeV to 200 GeV. The fit parameters were temperature \(T\), baryonic chemical potential \(\mu_B\), and isospin chemical potential \(\mu_{I3}\), with the strange chemical potential \(\mu_S\) determined from the condition of vanishing strangeness and a \(\gamma_s\) factor accounting for possible strangeness non-equilibrium. The effect of resonance decays on final hadronic multiplicities was included using branching ratios from the THERMUS code.

## Key numbers

* Overall fit quality: \(\chi^2/dof \simeq 0.95\) for 111 hadronic ratios at 14 collision energies.
* Hard-core radii used in the fit: \(R_b = 0.355\) fm, \(R_m = 0.4\) fm, \(R_{\pi} = 0.1\) fm, \(R_K = 0.38\) fm, \(R_\Lambda = 0.11\) fm.
* The effective number of degrees of freedom \(p/T^4\) increases by 70% while the collision energy changes by 15% and the CFO temperature changes by 30% in the range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3-4.9\) GeV.
* Sharp peaks in the trace anomaly \(\delta = (\varepsilon - 3p)/T^4\) and baryonic density are observed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.9\) GeV.
* A sudden change of slope in the \(\Lambda/p\), \(K^{+}/p\), \(K^{+}/\Lambda\), \(\Omega^{-}/p\), and \(\Xi^{-}/p\) ratios occurs in the range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3-4.9\) GeV.
* A second peak of the trace anomaly is observed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous findings of quasi-plateaus in entropy per baryon and pion number per baryon at CFO (Bugaev et al., 2015) and a sudden pressure jump (Bugaev et al., 2015), which were predicted as signals of QGP-hadron mixed phase formation (Bugaev et al., 1989; Bugaev et al., 1990; Bugaev et al., 1991). The results contradict the inability of traditional thermal models (Andronic et al., 2006) to simultaneously describe the Strangeness Horn \(K^{+}/\pi^{+}\) and the Lambda hyperon horn \(\Lambda/\pi^{-}\). The findings enable future experiments at JINR-NICA and GSI-FAIR to search for deconfinement signatures in the \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3-4.9\) GeV range and potentially a second phase transformation near \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2\) GeV.
