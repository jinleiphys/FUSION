# Baryon Asymmetry of the Proton Sea at Low $x$

> We predict a nonvanishing baryon asymmetry of the proton sea at low $x$. It is expected to be about $7\%$ and nearly $x$-independent at $x < 0.5 \times 10^{-3}$. The asymmetry arises from the baryon-antibaryon component of the Pomeron, rather than from the valence quarks of the proton, which are wide believed carriers of baryon number. Experimental study of $x$-distribution of the baryon asymmetry of the proton sea can be performed in $ep$ or $γp$ interactions at HERA, where $x\sim 10^{-5}$ are reachable, smaller than at any of existing or planned proton colliders.

## Key claim

The authors predict a nonvanishing baryon asymmetry of the proton sea at low Bjorken x, expected to be about 7% and nearly x-independent at x < 0.5 × 10⁻³, arising from the baryon-antibaryon component of the Pomeron rather than from valence quarks.

## Method

The authors analyze the Fock-state decomposition of the proton light-cone wave function and interpret baryon number flow in terms of string junction configurations and Regge trajectories. They derive the baryon asymmetry observable A_B(x) by relating it to the baryon-antibaryon annihilation cross section in the proton rest frame, using the Lorentz invariance of the observable. The gluonic contribution is evaluated using the asymptotic annihilation cross section σ^{B\bar B}_{ann} ≈ 1-2 mb, while the valence quark contribution is obtained from the intercept α⁰(M^J_2) = 1/2 and data on pp collisions.

## Key numbers

- Predicted baryon asymmetry A_B⁰ ≈ 7% (0.07), nearly x-independent at x < 0.5 × 10⁻³
- Asymptotic baryon-antibaryon annihilation cross section σ^{p\bar p}_{ann} = 1.5 ± 0.1 mb from analysis of multiplicity distribution data
- Valence quark contribution to baryon asymmetry: Δ_B^(q) = δ_q √x with δ_q ≈ 0.6 from ISR data or δ_q ≈ 1 from annihilation cross section
- Net baryon number yield per unit rapidity: Δ_B(x) ≈ 0.02 (2% of total photoabsorption cross section)
- Unitarity corrections suppress A_B(x) by about 20% for the energy range of HERA
- Smallest x reachable at HERA: x ≈ 10⁻⁵
- Quark mechanism dominates down to x ≈ 5 × 10⁻⁴

## Context

This work extends the string junction approach to baryon number transfer developed by the authors and collaborators (Kopeliovich and Zakharov, various years) and contrasts with earlier multiperipheral bootstrap models (Egger and Hüfner, various years; Rossi and Veneziano, various years) which predicted lower intercepts for the relevant Regge trajectories. The paper provides a framework for experimental study of baryon number distribution at HERA, where x values as low as 10⁻⁵ are accessible, smaller than at any existing or planned proton colliders including RHIC, LHC, or SSC.
