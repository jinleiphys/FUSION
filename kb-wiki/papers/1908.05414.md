# Probing $ΩΩ$ and $pΩ$ dibaryons with femtoscopic correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The momentum correlation functions of baryon pairs, which reflects the baryon-baryon interaction at low energies, are investigated for multi-strangeness pairs ($ΩΩ$ and $NΩ$) produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We calculate the correlation functions based on an expanding source model constrained by single-particle distributions. The interaction potentials are taken from those obtained from recent lattice QCD calculations at nearly physical quark masses. Experimental measurements of these correlation functions for different system sizes will help to disentangle the strong interaction between baryons and to unravel the possible existence of strange dibaryons.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that momentum correlation functions for $\Omega\Omega$ and $p\Omega$ pairs, calculated using lattice QCD potentials at nearly physical quark masses and an expanding source model, can reveal the existence of shallow dibaryon bound states through characteristic depletion or enhancement patterns, particularly when comparing correlation functions from different collision centrality (system sizes) via the small-to-large ratio.

## Method

The authors compute two-particle momentum correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formula, where the relative wave function is obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with lattice QCD-derived potentials for the $\Omega\Omega$ ($J=0$) and $p\Omega$ ($J=2$) channels. The emission source is modeled as a cylindrically expanding boost-invariant fireball with parameters (freeze-out temperature $T_f=155$ MeV, proper time $\tau_0$, transverse radii $R_T$, and flow parameters $\alpha,\beta$) fitted to experimental transverse momentum spectra of $\Omega$ and protons from Pb-Pb collisions at LHC. The correlation functions are calculated for five centrality bins (0-10% through 60-80%) and the small-to-large ratio $C_{SL}(q)$ is constructed by dividing correlation functions from peripheral collisions by those from central collisions to cancel Coulomb effects.

## Key numbers

- $\Omega\Omega$ scattering length from lattice QCD (without Coulomb): $a_0 \simeq 4.6$ fm, effective range $r_{\text{eff}} \simeq 1.27$ fm, binding energy $E_B \simeq 1.6$ MeV
- $\Omega\Omega$ scattering length with Coulomb repulsion ranges from $a_0 = 65.28$ fm ($t/a=16$) to $a_0 = 11.69$ fm ($t/a=18$), with binding energies from 0.1 MeV to 1.0 MeV
- $p\Omega$ scattering length from lattice QCD (without Coulomb): $a_0 \simeq 5.3$ fm, effective range $r_{\text{eff}} \simeq 1.26$ fm, binding energy $E_B \sim 1.54$ MeV
- $p\Omega$ scattering length with Coulomb attraction ranges from $a_0 = 3.38$ fm ($t/a=12$) to $a_0 = 3.49$ fm ($t/a=13$), with binding energies from 2.08 MeV to 2.27 MeV
- Source model parameters include $\tau_0 = 10.0$ fm/$c$ for 0-10% centrality, $R_T^{\Omega} = 8.0$ fm and $R_T^{p} = 6.8$ fm for the same centrality bin
- Freeze-out temperature fixed at $T_f = 155$ MeV from fits to LHC multiplicity data

## Context

This work extends previous femtoscopic correlation studies by Morita et al. (2014, 2016) and Ohnishi et al. (2016) by using the latest lattice QCD potentials at nearly physical quark masses from Gongyo et al. (2017) for $\Omega\Omega$ and Iritani et al. (2018) for $p\Omega$, rather than earlier potentials with heavier quark masses. The small-to-large ratio method, introduced by Morita et al. (2016) to cancel Coulomb effects, is applied to both systems. The predictions enable experimental searches for strange dibaryons via momentum correlations in heavy-ion collisions, with the STAR collaboration at RHIC having already reported a first measurement of $p\Omega$ correlation that shows similar tendency to the present calculations.
