# Chiral dynamics in "gamma p --> pi^0 eta p" and related reactions

> Using a chiral unitary approach for meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness zero sector, where the $N^*(1535)$ and $Δ^*(1700)$ resonances are dynamically generated, we study the reactions $γp \to π^0 ηp$ and $γp \to π^0 K^0 Σ^+$ at photon energies at which the final states are produced close to threshold. Among several reaction mechanisms, we find the most important is the excitation of the $Δ^*(1700)$ state which subsequently decays into a pseudoscalar meson and the $N^*(1535)$. Hence, the reaction provides useful information with which to test current theories of the dynamical generation of the low-lying $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ states. Predictions are made for cross sections and invariant mass distributions which can be compared with forthcoming experiments at ELSA.

## Key claim

The reaction $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \eta p$ is dominated by the excitation of the dynamically generated $\Delta^*(1700)$ resonance, which subsequently decays into a pseudoscalar meson and the $N^*(1535)$; the cross section predictions are relatively independent of the chosen model for the $N^*(1535)$, but the theoretical error is estimated by comparing a full and a reduced model.

## Method

The authors use a chiral unitary approach for meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness zero sector, where the $N^*(1535)$ and $\Delta^*(1700)$ resonances are dynamically generated from the interaction of the octet of pseudoscalar mesons with the octet of stable baryons and the decuplet of $3/2^+$ baryons, respectively. They calculate the photoproduction amplitudes for $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \eta p$ and $\gamma p \to \pi^0 K^0 \Sigma^+$ by including several mechanisms: contact terms, pole terms for gauge invariance, Kroll-Ruderman terms, and explicit resonance contributions from the $\Delta^*(1700)$ and $N^*(1520)$, with the final state interaction provided by the dynamically generated $N^*(1535)$ in coupled channels including $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K \Lambda$, and $K \Sigma$.

## Key numbers

- The integrated cross section for $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \eta p$ is predicted up to photon lab energies $E_\gamma$ around 2.0 GeV, with the full and reduced model giving similar results; the phenomenological potential from the PWA of Arndt (2003) is only available up to $E_\gamma \sim 1.2$ GeV.
- The cross section for $\gamma p \to \pi^0 K^0 \Sigma^+$ is much smaller than for the $\pi^0 \eta p$ final state, as shown in Fig. 4 (right side) for photon energies up to about 2.5 GeV.
- The invariant mass spectrum $\frac{d\sigma}{dM_I(\eta p)}$ is shown in units of $\mu$b GeV$^{-1}$ as a function of $M_I(\eta p)$ in MeV for several photon lab energies $E_\gamma$, with a clear $N^*(1535)$ peak and a second peak from the $\Delta(1232)$ in the tree level process at higher energies.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Kaiser (1995), Kaiser (1996), and Inoue (2001) for dynamically generated resonances, and builds on the photoproduction formalism of Nacher (1998) for $\gamma p \to K^+ \pi \Sigma$ and Nacher (2000) for $\gamma p \to \pi \pi N$. It incorporates the $\Delta^*(1700)$ as a dynamically generated resonance from the meson-baryon decuplet interaction of Kolomeitsev (2003) and Sarkar (2004). The predictions are directly testable by forthcoming experiments at the ELSA facility, and the reactions probe decay channels of the $\Delta^*(1700)$ (such as $\Delta^*(1700) \to \eta \Delta$ and $\Delta^*(1700) \to K \Sigma^*$) that are predicted by the model but not yet measured.
