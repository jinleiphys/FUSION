# Pygmy resonance and torus mode within Vlasov dynamics

> The pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich nuclei is studied within the framework of the Vlasov equation which is solved numerically. The interaction used in the Thomas-Fermi ground state and in the Vlasov equation is derived from an energy functional which correctly describes the equation of state of nuclear matter and neutron matter. It is found that the pygmy resonance appears in the electric dipole response of all nuclei with strong neutron excess, the energies and transition probabilities being in reasonable agreement with experimental results. Since the Vlasov equation does not account for any shell effects, this indicates that the existence of the pygmy resonance is a generic phenomenon and does not rely on the specific shell structure. Besides the electric dipole response, the isoscalar toroidal response is calculated. The transition densities and velocity fields are discussed. A comparison of the peak positions and velocity fields suggests that the pygmy resonance can be identified with one of the low-lying modes excited by the isoscalar toroidal operator.

## Key claim

The pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) appears in the electric dipole response of all nuclei with strong neutron excess, and its existence is a generic phenomenon that does not rely on specific shell structure, since the Vlasov equation (which averages over shell effects) reproduces it. The paper further suggests that the PDR can be identified with one of the low-lying modes excited by the isoscalar toroidal operator, based on comparison of peak positions and velocity fields.

## Method

The Vlasov equation was solved numerically using the test-particle method, with the ground state calculated in the Thomas-Fermi (TF) approximation. The mean field was derived from the bulk part of the Barcelona-Catania-Paris (BCP) energy functional, which reproduces the equation of state of nuclear matter and neutron matter. The electric dipole response and the isoscalar toroidal response were computed by applying a delta-function perturbation and Fourier-transforming the time-dependent expectation values, with the strength function folded with a Lorentzian of width 0.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- In \(^{132}\)Sn, the PDR peak appears at 8.6 MeV, contributing about 4% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR), in agreement with the experimental value from Adrich (2005) for \(^{132}\)Sn.
- In \(^{132}\)Sn, the GDR peak is at 14.5 MeV; for \(^{116}\)Sn, the GDR is at 15.8 MeV with a fitted width of 2.4 MeV (including the artificial 0.5 MeV width), compared to the experimental width of 4.19 MeV from Fultz.
- In \(^{22}\)O, the low-lying strength contributes 8% of the EWSR when integrated up to 15 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 7% from Leistenschneider.
- In \(^{208}\)Pb, the PDR peak is at 7.6 MeV, with a total strength \(B(E1;\uparrow)\) integrated up to 8 MeV of 2 \(e^2\)fm\(^2\), compared to the experimental value of 1.32 \(e^2\)fm\(^2\) from Ryezayeva.
- For \(^{132}\)Sn with a reduced Gaussian width \(d = 0.5\) fm (instead of 0.7 fm), the GDR shifts from 14.5 to 15.3 MeV, while the PDR position shifts only from 8.6 to 8.7 MeV and still contributes about 4% of the EWSR.

## Context

This work extends semiclassical studies of collective modes, such as the Steinwedel-Jensen model (Steinwedel and Jensen), by solving the full Vlasov equation without fluid-dynamical approximations, using a self-consistent mean field from the BCP functional (Baldo). It contrasts with quantum mechanical RPA calculations (Catara, Vretenar) by showing that the PDR is a generic collective mode not dependent on shell structure, and it connects the PDR to the isoscalar toroidal mode previously studied in nuclear fluid dynamics (Bastrukov 1993) and relativistic RPA (Vretenar). The results enable further investigation of the PDR as a mixed isoscalar-isovector mode in neutron-rich nuclei.
