# Lambda polarization in heavy ion collisions: from RHIC BES to LHC energies

> STAR collaboration at RHIC has recently measured the polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in non-central heavy ion collisions in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) program. The magnitude of the polarization was found to decrease from few percents at the lowest BES energies to $\approx0.2$% at the top RHIC energy. The polarization signal has been reproduced in different hydrodynamic calculations assuming a thermodynamic spin-vorticity coupling mechanism at the Cooper-Frye hypersurface. In this work an extension of our existing calculations of the $Λ$ polarization in the RHIC BES program to the top RHIC and 2.76 TeV LHC energies is presented. The longitudinal component of the $Λ$ polarization, which is the dominant component of the polarization at the LHC energies, is discussed. Finally we show that the global polarization of $Λ$ originates dominantly from the relativistic analogue of the classical vorticity, whereas the quadrupole longitudinal component originates from the gradients of temperature and acceleration of the medium when the $Λ$s are produced out of the fluid.

## Key claim

The global polarization of Λ hyperons decreases from RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies to LHC energies, reaching ≤0.1% at 2.76 TeV, while the longitudinal (beam-direction) component of polarization decreases much more slowly and originates from temperature gradients and acceleration rather than from classical vorticity.

## Method

The authors extended their existing 3D viscous hydrodynamic model (vHLLE+UrQMD) to calculate Λ polarization at √sNN = 2.76 TeV LHC energy, using a Monte Carlo Glauber initial state with parametrized rapidity dependence instead of the UrQMD cascade used at lower energies. The polarization was computed via the thermodynamic spin-vorticity coupling mechanism at the Cooper-Frye particlization hypersurface, and the contributions from temperature gradients, relativistic vorticity, and acceleration were decomposed separately.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization of Λ hyperons in 20-50% central collisions decreases from a few percent at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to ≈0.2% at top RHIC energy (200 GeV), and further to ≤0.1% at 2.76 TeV LHC energy.
- The second-order Fourier harmonic coefficient f₂ of the longitudinal polarization component decreases by only 35% between √sNN = 7.7 GeV and 200 GeV, whereas the global polarization decreases by about a factor of 10 over the same energy range.
- The shear viscosity over entropy ratio in the calculations varies between η/s = 0.08 and 0.2.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic calculations of Λ polarization by Karpenko (2016) from the RHIC BES program to top RHIC and LHC energies, and builds on the spin-vorticity coupling mechanism proposed by Becattini (2013). The results show that global polarization is dominated by classical vorticity, while the quadrupole longitudinal component arises from temperature gradients and acceleration, which are related in ideal hydrodynamics. The authors note a discrepancy with preliminary STAR data at √sNN = 200 GeV (presented by Takafumi at Quark Matter 2018), where the measured longitudinal polarization has the same sin(2φ) dependence but opposite sign and smaller amplitude than the hydrodynamic calculations.
