# Ab initio calculation of the $np \to d γ$ radiative capture process

> Lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon systems are used to isolate the short-distance two-body electromagnetic contributions to the radiative capture process $np \to dγ$, and the photo-disintegration processes $γ^{(\ast)} d \to np$. In nuclear potential models, such contributions are described by phenomenological meson-exchange currents, while in the present work, they are determined directly from the quark and gluon interactions of QCD. Calculations of neutron-proton energy levels in multiple background magnetic fields are performed at two values of the quark masses, corresponding to pion masses of $m_π\sim 450$ and 806 MeV, and are combined with pionless nuclear effective field theory to determine these low-energy inelastic processes. Extrapolating to the physical pion mass, a cross section of $σ^{lqcd}(np\to dγ)=332.4({\tiny \begin{array}{l}+5.4 \\ - 4.7\end{array}})\ mb$ is obtained at an incident neutron speed of $v=2,200\ m/s$, consistent with the experimental value of $σ^{expt}(np \to dγ) = 334.2(0.5)\ mb$.

# Ab initio calculation of the $np \to d\gamma$ radiative capture process

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations, combined with pionless effective field theory, determine the short-distance two-body electromagnetic contributions to $np \to d\gamma$ directly from quark and gluon interactions, yielding a cross section consistent with experiment. The result depends on a naive extrapolation of the quantity $\overline{L}_1$ to the physical pion mass using only two pion masses ($m_\pi \sim 450$ and 806 MeV), and the authors note that systematic uncertainties from the single lattice spacing and volume are expected to be small ($\mathcal{O}(a^2\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2, e^{-m_\pi L}, e^{-\gamma_0 L}) \lesssim 4\%$) but remain to be verified.

## Method

Lattice QCD calculations of neutron-proton energy levels in multiple background magnetic fields were performed on two ensembles of gauge-field configurations with clover-improved fermions and Lüscher-Weisz gauge action, at pion masses of $m_\pi \sim 450$ MeV and 806 MeV. The energy splittings between the coupled $^3S_1$ and $^1S_0$ channels in the $I_z = j_z = 0$ sector were extracted from principal correlators and combined with single-nucleon correlation functions to isolate the two-nucleon electromagnetic contribution $\overline{L}_1$. Pionless nuclear effective field theory was then used to relate $\overline{L}_1$ to the $M1$ amplitude and the radiative capture cross section.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated value of $\overline{L}_1$ at the physical pion mass: $\overline{L}_1^{\rm lqcd} = 0.285\ (^{+63}_{-60})$ natural nuclear magnetons (nNM).
- Corresponding short-distance parameter: $l_1^{\rm lqcd} = -4.48\ (^{+16}_{-15})$ fm.
- Lattice QCD cross section at incident neutron speed $v = 2,200$ m/s: $\sigma^{\rm lqcd}(np \to d\gamma) = 332.4\ (^{+5.4}_{-4.7})$ mb.
- Experimental cross section at the same speed: $\sigma^{\rm expt}(np \to d\gamma) = 334.2(0.5)$ mb.
- At $m_\pi \sim 806$ MeV, the predicted cross section using only lattice QCD inputs is $\sigma^{\rm 806\ MeV} \sim 5$ mb at $v = 2,200$ m/s.
- Background magnetic fields used: $e|\mathbf{B}| \sim 0.05 |\tilde n|$ GeV$^2$, with $\tilde n = 0, 1, -2, 4$ (and additional $\tilde n = 3, -6, 12$ at the SU(3) symmetric point).
- Lattice spacing: $a \sim 0.12$ fm for both ensembles.
- Volume: $32^3 \times 48$ at $m_\pi \sim 806$ MeV; $32^3 \times 96$ at $m_\pi \sim 450$ MeV.
- Approximately 1,000 gauge configurations at the SU(3) point and 650 at the lighter pion mass.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of two-nucleon systems (Beane 2012, Beane 2013, Beane 2014) to electroweak processes, providing the first direct QCD determination of meson-exchange-current contributions that in nuclear potential models are described phenomenologically (Riska 1972, Hockert 1974). The result enables future QCD calculations of weak processes such as $pp \to d e^+ \nu$ (proton-proton fusion in the Sun) and $\nu d \to p p e^+$ (neutrino disintegration of the deuteron), which currently have the largest theoretical uncertainties from two-nucleon weak currents (Kong 2000, Butler 2001, Ando 2007, Adelberger 2010, Marcucci 2013, Rupak 2014). The background field technique also opens the way for calculating nuclear matrix elements relevant for dark matter direct detection and searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
