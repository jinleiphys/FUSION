# Electromagnetic Form Factors of Charged and Neutral Kaons

> The charged and neutral kaon form factors are calculated as a phenomenological application of the QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations. The results are compared with the pion form factor calculated in the same framework and yield \mbox{$F_{K^\pm}(Q^2) > F_{π^\pm}(Q^2)$} on \mbox{$Q^2\in[0,3]$~GeV$^2$}; and a neutral kaon form factor that is similar in form and magnitude to the neutron charge form factor. These results are sensitive to the difference between the kaon and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the $u$- and $s$-quark propagation characteristics.

## Key claim

The charged kaon form factor is larger than the pion form factor on the momentum-transfer range \(Q^2 \in [0,3]\) GeV\(^2\), but this ordering reverses for \(Q^2 > 3\) GeV\(^2\) where the difference becomes small and sensitive to the unconstrained form of the kaon Bethe-Salpeter amplitude; the neutral kaon form factor is similar in form and magnitude to the neutron charge form factor.

## Method

The authors calculate the kaon electromagnetic form factors using a generalized-impulse approximation within the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) framework of QCD, employing dressed quark propagators, dressed quark-photon vertices that satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity, and approximate Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the kaon and pion. The quark propagator parameters are fixed by fitting pion observables, and the two additional \(s\)-quark parameters are determined by requiring the model to reproduce experimental values of \(f_K/f_\pi\), \(r_{K^\pm}/r_{\pi^\pm}\), and \(m_K/f_K\). The form factors are then evaluated numerically in the Breit frame via three-dimensional quadrature.

## Key numbers

- \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV (calculated, used as input to set mass scale \(D = 0.160\) GeV\(^2\))
- \(f_K = 0.113\) GeV (calculated; experimental value \(0.113 \pm 0.001\) GeV)
- \(m_K = 0.4936\) GeV (calculated; experimental value \(0.4937\) GeV)
- \(f_K/f_\pi = 1.22\) (calculated; experimental value \(1.22 \pm 0.01\))
- \(r_{K^\pm} = 0.49\) fm (calculated; experimental value \(0.583 \pm 0.041\) fm)
- \(r_{K^\pm}/r_{\pi^\pm} = 0.87\) (calculated; experimental value \(0.88 \pm 0.06\))
- \(r_{K^0}^2 = -0.020\) fm\(^2\) (calculated; experimental value \(-0.054 \pm 0.026\) fm\(^2\))
- \(m_K/f_K = 4.37\) (target experimental value used for parameter fixing)
- \(\alpha = 0.49\) (momentum partitioning parameter required to satisfy \(F_{K^0}(Q^2=0)=0\))

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach previously applied to the pion by Roberts (1995) to the kaon, providing the first calculation of both charged and neutral kaon form factors within this framework. The results are qualitatively consistent with the earlier calculation by Buck, Williams, and Ito (1995) but yield uniformly smaller form factor magnitudes. The calculation enables experimental tests at CEBAF (experiments E-91-016 and E-93-018) and demonstrates that measurement of kaon form factors probes both the bound-state Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and nonperturbatively generated differences between \(u\)- and \(s\)-quark propagation characteristics.
