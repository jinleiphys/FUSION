# Hadronic Parity-Violation on the Lattice

> We motivate lattice QCD studies of the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant and extend flavor-conserving hadronic parity-violation from QCD to partially-quenched QCD. The parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling and the anapole form factor (and moment) of the proton are computed to one-loop order in the partially-quenched chiral expansion. For the parity-violating pion-nucleon interaction, we include the contributions from total derivative operators necessary to match the kinematics that will be used in lattice simulations.

# Hadronic Parity-Violation on the Lattice

## Key claim

The parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}$ and the anapole moment of the proton can be computed to one-loop order in partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQ$\chi$PT), but the extraction of $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}$ from lattice simulations requires the inclusion of total derivative operators because lattice kinematics involve on-shell particles with energy injected at the weak vertex, unlike the $P_\pi^\mu=0$ limit typically used in chiral expansions.

## Method

The authors extend the flavor-conserving hadronic parity-violating four-quark operators from QCD to partially-quenched QCD (PQQCD) with two non-degenerate light quarks, using the $SU(4|2)_L \otimes SU(4|2)_R$ chiral symmetry group. They compute the $\Delta I=1$ parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}$ to one-loop order in PQ$\chi$PT for two kinematic regimes: the standard $P_\pi^\mu=0$ case and the lattice-appropriate case where energy is injected at the weak vertex, including contributions from total derivative operators. They also compute the anapole form factor and moment of the proton at one-loop order in PQ$\chi$PT.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used in the chiral expansion is $f = 132\ \text{MeV}$.
- The coefficients of the four-quark operators at the chiral symmetry breaking scale $\Lambda_\chi$ are $C^{(1)}(\Lambda_\chi) = (1.10, 0.068, 0.234, -0.697)$ and $S^{(1)}(\Lambda_\chi) = (5.61, -1.90, 4.74, -2.67)$.
- The one-loop correction to the parity-violating vertex in QCD with $P_\pi^\mu=0$ is $\Gamma_{\overline{n}p\pi^+} = -h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} - 2\overline{m}(\tilde c_1+\tilde c_2) + \frac{1}{16\pi^2 f^2} \left[ h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} \left(\frac{2}{3}+\frac{3}{2} g_A^2\right) L_\pi + h_{\pi\Delta\Delta}^{(1)} \frac{20}{9} g_{\Delta N}^2 J_\pi \right]$, where $L_\pi = m_\pi^2 \log(m_\pi^2/\mu^2)$.
- The anapole form factor of the proton at leading order in QCD $\chi$PT is $A_p(q^2) = \frac{e g_A h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} M_N^2}{48 \pi f} \tilde A_{\pi^+}(q^2)$, with $\tilde A_{\pi^+}(q^2) \rightarrow \frac{1}{m_{\pi^+}} + \frac{q^2}{20 m_{\pi^+}^3} + \dots$ in the $q^2\rightarrow 0$ limit.
- For lattice kinematics at threshold, the injected energy must satisfy $E \ge m_\pi + M_n - M_p$, and when $E = \pm m_\pi$, the function $\tilde L(m,\pm m,\mu) = -4 m^2 \log(m^2/\mu^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the study of flavor-conserving hadronic parity-violation from QCD to partially-quenched QCD, building on the development of partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory for baryons by Chen and Savage (2001) and Beane and Savage (2002). The calculation of $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}$ at one-loop in QCD was performed previously by Zhu, Puglia, Holstein, and Ramsey-Musolf (2001), and the authors' results agree with that work once conventions are accounted for. The inclusion of total derivative operators for lattice kinematics follows the approach used for $K\rightarrow \pi\pi$ matrix elements in lattice QCD. This work enables future lattice QCD simulations to extract $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}$ using quark masses larger than those of nature, with chiral extrapolation guided by the PQ$\chi$PT expressions provided, and it provides the necessary framework for matching lattice data to the physical couplings needed for interpreting parity-violating experiments such as SAMPLE, cesium atomic parity violation, and the $\vec n p \rightarrow d \gamma$ angular asymmetry measurement.
