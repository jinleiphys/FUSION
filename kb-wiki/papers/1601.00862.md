# Resonance on top of thresholds: the $Λ_c(2595)^+$ as an extremely fine-tuned state

> A dedicated study of the $πΣ_c$ scattering around its threshold is carried out in this work to probe the nature of $Λ_c(2595)^+$. We first demonstrate that the effective range expansion approach fails to work near the $Λ_c(2595)^+$ pole position, due to the presence of a nearby CDD pole around the $πΣ_c$ thresholds. We then develop a general framework to properly handle the situation with a CDD pole accompanied by nearby thresholds, which is first elaborated for the single-channel case and then generalized to the coupled-channel study. The isospin breaking effects of the three $πΣ_c$ channels with different thresholds are specially taken into account in our study. The finite-width effects from the $Σ_c$ baryons are considered and found to be relevant to give the $Λ_c(2595)^+$ width fully compatible to its experimental value. Through the compositeness analysis, our robust conclusion is that the $π^0Σ_c^+$ component is subdominant inside the $Λ_c(2595)^+$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the effective range expansion (ERE) fails to describe the $\Lambda_c(2595)^+$ resonance because a nearby Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole lies extremely close to the $\pi\Sigma_c$ thresholds, severely restricting the ERE convergence radius before reaching the resonance pole position; the authors instead develop a framework that explicitly includes the CDD pole and isospin-breaking effects among the three $\pi\Sigma_c$ channels.

## Method

The authors first show that the ERE parameters (scattering length and effective range) vary dramatically when different isospin-limit masses are chosen for the $\pi\Sigma_c$ system, indicating fine-tuning and a nearby CDD pole. They then construct a single-channel $S$-wave amplitude using the N/D method with one explicit CDD pole, and generalize this to a $3\times 3$ coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation that incorporates the three physical $\pi\Sigma_c$ channels with their distinct thresholds and the finite widths of the $\Sigma_c$ baryons. The parameters are fixed by requiring the amplitude to reproduce the experimental pole position of the $\Lambda_c(2595)^+$ for both the lowest and highest threshold channels.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $\Lambda_c(2595)^+$: $M_R = 2592.25 \pm 0.28$ MeV; width: $\Gamma_R = 2.6 \pm 0.6$ MeV (from PDG).
- Threshold of $\pi^0\Sigma_c^+$: 2587.9 MeV; thresholds of $\pi^+\Sigma_c^0$ and $\pi^-\Sigma_c^{++}$: approximately 2593.5 MeV.
- For the single-channel ERE analysis, the scattering length $a$ and effective range $r$ vary from $a = 1.66 \pm 0.38$ fm, $r = -40.1 \pm 9.1$ fm (using $\pi^0\Sigma_c^+$ masses) to $a = 16.5 \pm 1.4$ fm, $r = -9.7 \pm 0.8$ fm (using $\pi^-\Sigma_c^{++}$ masses).
- In the single-channel CDD analysis (first solution), the CDD pole masses are $M_{CDD}^{(i)} = 2592.3(2)$ MeV (for channel $i$) and $M_{CDD}^{(iii)} = 2590.0(6)$ MeV (for channel $iii$); the ERE convergence radius is limited to $|k| > 33.6$ MeV (channel $i$) and $|k| > 30.6$ MeV (channel $iii$), both smaller than the resonance momentum $|k_R|$.

## Context

This work extends the unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UChPT) studies of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance in the strange sector to the charmed baryon $\Lambda_c(2595)^+$, which previous UChPT calculations (e.g., Lutz (2003), Hofmann (2005), Geng (2014), and Nieves (2008, 2012, 2015)) had difficulty reproducing simultaneously. The paper contradicts the assumption in Hyodo (2013) and Long (2015) that the ERE is applicable to this resonance, and it enables a proper treatment of near-threshold resonances with nearby CDD poles, particularly for states where isospin-breaking mass differences are comparable to the resonance width.
