# Precision boson-jet azimuthal decorrelation at hadron colliders

> The azimuthal angular decorrelation of a vector boson and jet is sensitive to QCD radiation, and can be used to probe the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions. By using a recoil-free jet definition, the sensitivity to contamination from soft radiation on the measurement is reduced, and the complication of non-global logarithms is eliminated from our theoretical calculation. Specifically we will consider the $p_T^n$ recombination scheme, as well as the $n\to \infty$ limit, known as the winner-take-all scheme. These jet definitions also significantly simplify the calculation for a track-based measurement, which is preferred due to its superior angular resolution. We present a detailed discussion of the factorization in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory, revealing why the transverse momentum $\vec q_T$ is more complicated than the azimuthal angle. We show that potential glauber contributions do not spoil our factorization formalism, at least up to and including order $α_s^3$. The resummation is carried out using the renormalization group, and all necessary ingredients are collected or calculated. We conclude with a detailed phenomenological study, finding an enhanced matching correction for high jet $p_T$ due to the electroweak collinear enhancement of a boson emission off di-jets. We also compare with the Pythia event generator, finding that our observable is very robust to hadronization and the underlying event.

## Key claim
The azimuthal decorrelation between a vector boson and a jet, when measured using a recoil-free jet definition such as the Winner-Take-All (WTA) scheme, eliminates non-global logarithms and enables resummation beyond NLL accuracy, whereas the radial decorrelation (the other component of the transverse momentum imbalance) retains these complications even with a recoil-free axis.

## Method
The authors derive a factorization formula in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the azimuthal decorrelation δφ = arcsin(q_x/|p_T,V|) in V+jet production at hadron colliders, using the WTA recombination scheme (p_T^n scheme with n→∞) to define the jet axis. They collect or calculate all necessary ingredient functions (hard, beam, jet, and soft functions) and their anomalous dimensions, then perform resummation of logarithms of δφ via renormalization group evolution to NNLL accuracy. The resummed results are matched to fixed-order calculations from MCFM and compared with the Pythia event generator.

## Key numbers
- Resummation achieved at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy, as first reported in Chien (2020)
- Potential Glauber contributions first appear at order α_s^4 (α_s^3 is explicitly safe)
- Cusp anomalous dimension given up to three loops: Γ_0 = 4, Γ_1 = (268/9 - 4π²/3)C_A - (80/9)T_F n_f
- Non-cusp anomalous dimension for quark hard function at one loop: γ_0^q = -3 C_F
- Non-cusp anomalous dimension for gluon hard function at one loop: γ_0^g = -β_0 = -(11/3)C_A + (4/3)T_F n_f
- Beta function coefficients: β_0 = (11/3)C_A - (4/3)T_F n_f, β_1 = (34/3)C_A² - (20/3)C_A T_F n_f - 4 C_F T_F n_f

## Context
This work extends the program of precision resummation for boson-jet correlations, building on fixed-order calculations at NNLO by Chen (2014), Ridder (2015), Boughezal (2015), and others, and on earlier NLL resummation by Banfi (2008) and Chen (2018). The use of a recoil-free axis was first considered for azimuthal angles in Banfi (2008) using the H1 scheme, but the present work achieves NNLL accuracy by eliminating non-global logarithms (Dasgupta, 2001) through the WTA scheme. The results enable precision comparisons with experimental data from ATLAS and CMS, particularly for heavy-ion collisions where the boson provides an inert reference for studying the quark-gluon plasma (Kartvelishvili, 1995). The authors note that N³LL resummation is within reach, and the observable is robust to hadronization and underlying event effects.
