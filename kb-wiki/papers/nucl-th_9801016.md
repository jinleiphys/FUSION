# $J/ψ$ suppression in heavy ion collsions and the QCD phase transition

> We suggest that the new regime of $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb-Pb collisions found by the NA50 experiment at CERN is the result of non-trivial space-time evolution due to specific behavior of the Equation of State (EOS) near the QCD phase transition. We also study another suppression channel, the conversion of $J/ψ$ into $η_c$ during the late cool hadronic stage, and find it rather inefficient.

## Key claim

The new regime of $J/\psi$ suppression in Pb-Pb collisions observed by NA50, which appears as a sharp onset at impact parameter $b \approx 8$ fm, may be explained by an increased QGP lifetime due to the non-trivial equation of state near the QCD phase transition (the "softest point"), rather than by increased absorption mechanisms such as melting or gluonic excitation; however, the authors note that their model does not reproduce the very sharp jump seen in the data, and the discontinuity is softened by $J/\psi$ leakage from the plasma region.

## Method

The authors constructed a small event generator for Pb(158 GeV-A)-Pb collisions at a given impact parameter, generating charmonium events that have survived nuclear absorption and are in a hot deconfined medium. They modeled the survival probability as $e^{-\Gamma t_p}$ where $t_p$ is the time spent traversing the medium and $\Gamma$ is the ionization rate chosen to match the observed 25% suppression immediately following the jump, using Glauber theory for spatial distributions and a Bjorken estimate for longitudinal size. Separately, they calculated spin-exchange reaction rates for $J/\psi + \pi \rightarrow \eta_c + \rho$ and $J/\psi + \rho \rightarrow \eta_c + \pi$ in a hadronic gas at T=150 MeV using a non-relativistic quark model with a point-like spin-spin interaction.

## Key numbers

- The softest point of the EOS occurs at energy density $\epsilon_{max} \sim 1-2$ GeV/fm$^3$, where the ratio $p/\epsilon$ is only about 0.05 (compared to 0.2 for resonance gas and 1/3 for high density QGP).
- The QGP lifetime at the softest point was found to be 2-3 times longer than for more energetic central collisions at the SPS (Heinz and Schäfer, 1997).
- The jump in suppression is observed for impact parameter $b \simeq 8.5$ fm, corresponding to average wounded nucleon density $\langle n_w(b=8.5) \rangle \simeq 2.6$ fm$^{-2}$.
- The equilibration time chosen to match the softest point is $\tau = 1.9$ fm, giving $\epsilon_{max} = 1.6$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- The spin-exchange cross section for $J/\psi + \pi \rightarrow \eta_c + \rho$ is about 1.2 mb at pion energy 800 MeV, with threshold at $E_\pi \simeq 710$ MeV.
- Reaction rates in a pion gas at T=150 MeV: $W_{J/\psi + \pi \rightarrow \eta_c + \rho} = 0.29$ MeV and $W_{J/\psi + \rho \rightarrow \eta_c + \pi} = 0.30$ MeV; with reduced $\rho$ mass (385 MeV) these rates increase to approximately 1.1 MeV each.
- The $\eta_c$ branching ratio to $\phi\phi$ is 0.7%.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework of Heinz and Schäfer (1997) and Rischke (1996), who found that the QCD phase transition's "softest point" in the equation of state leads to a non-monotonous dependence of QGP lifetime on initial conditions. It contrasts with the "melting" scenario (Matsui and Satz, 1986; Karsch and Satz, 1992) where charmonium states are destroyed by Debye screening above a critical energy density, and with the gluonic excitation ("photoeffect") mechanism (Peskin, 1979; Kharzeev, Satz, 1994). The authors also introduce a previously uninvestigated suppression channel via spin-exchange conversion of $J/\psi$ to $\eta_c$ in the hadronic stage. The work enables experimental discrimination between suppression mechanisms through measurements of hadronic observables sensitive to transverse expansion (e.g., $p_t$ slopes of heavy secondaries, HBT transverse sizes) and the $p_T$ dependence of $J/\psi$ suppression as a function of centrality.
