# Instability induced pressure isotropization in a longitudinally expanding system

> In two previous works [arXiv:1009.4363,arXiv:1107.0668], we studied the time evolution of a system of real scalar fields with quartic coupling which shares important features with the Color Glass Condensate description of heavy ion collisions. Our primary objective was to understand how such a system, when initialized with a non-perturbatively large classical field configuration, reaches thermal equilibrium. An essential goal of these works was to highlight the role played by the quantum fluctuations. However, these studies considered only a system confined within a box of fixed volume. In the present paper, we extend this work to a system that expands in the longitudinal direction thereby more closely mimicking a heavy ion collision. We conclude that the microscopic processes that drive the system towards equilibrium are able to keep up with the expansion of the system; the pressure tensor becomes isotropic despite the anisotropic expansion.

## Key claim

The microscopic instabilities (parametric resonance) in a longitudinally expanding scalar field theory are able to keep up with the expansion rate, causing the pressure tensor to become isotropic despite the anisotropic expansion, but this conclusion is demonstrated only for a scalar analog theory, not for QCD itself.

## Method

The authors study a massless real scalar field theory with quartic coupling, initialized with a boost-invariant classical background field plus Gaussian quantum fluctuations derived from the linearized mode functions (Hankel functions of imaginary order). They discretize the system on a lattice with periodic boundary conditions in the transverse plane and rapidity direction, evolve the classical field equations in proper time, and compute the energy-momentum tensor components to track the transverse and longitudinal pressures.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant used in all plots (except one appendix figure): \(g = 4\).
- Initial times tested for independence: \(\tau_0 = 10^{-2}\) and \(\tau_0' = 10^{-1}\) (in units of transverse lattice spacing \(a_\perp = 1\)).
- Lattice sizes used: \(20 \times 20 \times 20\) with 256 field configurations for the initial-time independence check.
- The resonance condition for unstable modes in the expanding case is \(R_- < k_\perp^2 + \nu^2/\tau^2 < R_+\), where \(R_\pm\) are proportional to the squared amplitude of the background field.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of scalar field thermalization in a fixed-volume box (Dusling et al., 2010; Epelbaum, 2011) to a longitudinally expanding geometry that more closely mimics heavy ion collisions. It builds on the Color Glass Condensate framework and the resummation scheme for secular divergences developed for QCD (Gelis et al., 2008; Fukushima et al., 2009; Dusling et al., 2010). The results show that parametric resonance instabilities can isotropize the pressure despite expansion, supporting the possibility that Weibel instabilities in QCD might similarly enable early hydrodynamization. The work enables future application of this classical-statistical approach to Yang-Mills theory in expanding geometry.
