# Influence of pairing and deformation on charge exchange transitions

> We describe the importance of charge-exchange reactions, and in particular Gamow-Teller transitions, in astrophysical processes and double beta decay, and in understanding of nuclear structure. We first provide an overview of the central role played by the isovector pairing and the quadrupole-quadrupole channels in the description of energy spectra and in the manifestation of collective modes, some associated with deformation of the nuclear shape. We then turned our focus to Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions in relatively light nuclei, especially in the 2p1f shell, where isoscalar pairing may be playing a role in competition with the isovector pairing that dominates in heavier regions. Following a summary of the progress made in recent years on this subject, we report a systematic shell model study aimed at providing further clarification as to how these pairing modes compete. In this study, we use a schematic Hamiltonian that contains a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction as well as both isoscalar and isovector pairing interactions. We first find an optimal set of Hamiltonian parameters for the model, to provide a starting point from which to vary the relevant pairing strengths and thus assess how this impacts the behavior of GT transitions and the corresponding energy spectra and rotational properties of the various nuclei involved in the decays. The analysis includes as an important theme a comparison with experimental data. The need to suppress the isoscalar pairing mode when treating nuclei with a neutron excess to avoid producing spurious results for the ground state spin and parity with the simplified Hamiltonian is highlighted. Varying the strength parameters for the two pairing modes is found to exhibit different but systematic effects on GT transition properties and on the corresponding energy spectra, which are detailed. (abridged)

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a schematic shell-model Hamiltonian for the 2p1f shell, isoscalar pairing must be suppressed (set to zero) for nuclei with a neutron excess (N ≠ Z) to avoid producing spurious ground-state spin and parity, while for N=Z nuclei both isoscalar and isovector pairing can be active; varying these pairing strengths produces systematic but different effects on Gamow-Teller transition fragmentation and energy spectra.

## Method

The authors performed systematic shell-model calculations using the ANTOINE code for even-mass nuclei in the 2p1f shell (A=42–48) with a schematic Hamiltonian containing a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction plus isoscalar (T=0, J=1) and isovector (T=1, J=0) pairing terms, with single-particle energies taken from the KB3 interaction. They first fixed an optimal parameter set (χ = -0.065 MeV, a = b = 6 for N=Z; a = 0 for N≠Z) by requiring good reproduction of low-energy spectra and GT fragmentation, then varied the isovector (b) and isoscalar (a) pairing strengths independently to assess their impact on GT transition strengths and energy spectra.

## Key numbers

- Optimal quadrupole-quadrupole strength: χ = -0.065 MeV.
- Optimal pairing strengths for N=Z nuclei: a = b = 6 (dimensionless).
- Single-particle energies from KB3 interaction: ε(7/2) = 0.0 MeV, ε(3/2) = 2.0 MeV, ε(1/2) = 4.0 MeV, ε(5/2) = 6.5 MeV.
- Quenching factor applied to B(GT) values: (0.74)².
- For A=42 (⁴²Ca → ⁴²Sc), the optimal a=b=6 produces a single strong peak at almost the exact experimental energy and strength; increasing a to 12 makes the lowest 1⁺ state become the ground state.
- For A=44 (⁴⁴Ca → ⁴⁴Sc), with a=0 and b=6, the lowest excitation is roughly five times more strongly populated than the next few, while experiment shows a relative enhancement of roughly two.
- For A=46 (⁴⁶Ti → ⁴⁶V), the optimal a=b=6 produces strong fragmentation with the lowest state having slightly more strength than the next few, but individual strengths are substantially larger than experiment.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of pairing competition in N≈Z nuclei, particularly the approach of Lei (2011) which used a similar Hamiltonian but with different single-particle energies. It also builds on the extensive body of work on Gamow-Teller transitions in the 2p1f shell using realistic interactions like KB3G and GXPF1J (Caurier, 1999; Honma, 2005; Suzuki, 2011). The results enable systematic understanding of how isoscalar versus isovector pairing affects GT fragmentation and rotational properties, and highlight a trade-off between reproducing GT fragmentation and rotational spectra when varying pairing strengths.
