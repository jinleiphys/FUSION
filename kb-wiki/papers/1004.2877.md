# Triaxial Angular Momentum Projection and Configuration Mixing calculations with the Gogny force

> We present the first implementation in the $(β,γ)$ plane of the generator coordinate method with full triaxial angular momentum and particle number projected wave functions using the Gogny force. Technical details about the performance of the method and the convergence of the results both in the symmetry restoration and the configuration mixing parts are discussed in detail. We apply the method to the study of $^{24}$Mg, the calculated energies of excited states as well as the transition probabilities are compared to the available experimental data showing a good overall agreement. In addition, we present the RVAMPIR approach which provides a good description of the ground and gamma bands in the absence of strong mixing.

## Key claim

The authors present the first implementation of the generator coordinate method with full triaxial angular momentum and particle number projection using the Gogny force, but the quality of the results depends critically on using variation-after-particle-number-projection (VAP-PN) rather than plain HFB or Lipkin-Nogami approaches to handle pairing correlations across the entire (β,γ) plane.

## Method

Intrinsic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states were obtained by minimizing the particle-number projected energy functional (VAP-PN) under constraints on the quadrupole deformation operators Q20 and Q22, using the Gogny D1S interaction expanded in a cartesian harmonic oscillator basis. These states were then projected onto good particle number and angular momentum via integration over gauge and Euler angles, and the resulting projected states were mixed using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) to solve the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation. A restricted VAMPIR approximation (RVAMPIR) was also implemented, where particle number projection is treated variationally but angular momentum projection is performed only at the energy-minimizing deformation for each angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The VAP-PN potential energy surface for ²⁴Mg shows two minima separated by a barrier of ~7.7 MeV, with the lowest at prolate deformation β=0.5 and another at oblate β=-0.2.
- After angular momentum projection, the prolate minimum shifts to β=0.6 for I=0,2,4,6,8, and the correlation energy gain from rotational symmetry restoration is ~3.9 MeV for the ground state.
- The energy difference between the VAP-PN and the I=0 oblate minima is ~4.6 MeV.
- Axial calculations with Nshells=7 versus Nshells=11 show a relative error of less than 10% for all levels in the spectrum of ²⁴Mg.
- For the axial projection, Npoints=31 intrinsic wave functions were distributed in the interval -1.5 ≤ β ≤ 1.5, with N_Fom=9 gauge angle points and N_b=16 Euler angle points, yielding expectation values for N, Z, N², Z², and I² differing by less than 10⁻⁸ from eigenvalues.

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field calculations with the Gogny force from axial symmetry (K=0) to full triaxial angular momentum projection and configuration mixing in the (β,γ) plane, which was previously implemented only with Skyrme interactions (Bender and Heenen, 2008) and Relativistic Mean Field (Yao et al., 2009, 2010). The use of VAP-PN distinguishes this approach from those earlier implementations, which used Lipkin-Nogami or BCS treatments of pairing. The method enables the study of γ-bands, γ-softness, shape coexistence, and shape transitions in nuclei where triaxiality is important, and the RVAMPIR approximation provides a computationally cheaper alternative for nuclei without strong configuration mixing.
