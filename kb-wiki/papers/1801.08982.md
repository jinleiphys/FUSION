# Tilted-axis wobbling in odd-mass nuclei

> A triaxial rotor Hamiltonian with a rigidly aligned high-$j$ quasiparticle is treated by a time-dependent variational principle, using angular momentum coherent states. The resulting classical energy function have three unique critical points in a space of generalized conjugate coordinates, which can minimize the energy for specific ordering of the inertial parameters and a fixed angular momentum state. Due to the symmetry of the problem, there are only two unique solutions, corresponding to wobbling motion around a principal axis and respectively a tilted-axis. The wobbling frequencies are obtained after a quantization procedure and then used to calculate $E2$ and $M1$ transition probabilities. The analytical results are employed in the study of the wobbling excitations of $^{135}$Pr nucleus, which is found to undergo a transition from low angular momentum transverse wobbling around a principal axis toward a tilted-axis wobbling at higher angular momentum.

# Tilted-axis wobbling in odd-mass nuclei

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a triaxial rotor Hamiltonian with a rigidly aligned high‑j quasiparticle, treated semiclassically, yields three critical points in the classical energy function, but only two unique solutions survive after accounting for symmetry: wobbling around a principal axis and wobbling around a tilted axis. The transition from transverse (principal‑axis) wobbling to tilted‑axis wobbling at higher angular momentum is used to explain the anomaly in the wobbling excitation energy around I=29/2 in ¹³⁵Pr.

## Method

A triaxial rotor Hamiltonian with a fully aligned quasiparticle (angular momentum j fixed along the first principal axis) is treated via a time‑dependent variational principle using angular‑momentum coherent states. The classical energy function is expressed in canonical variables (r, φ) derived from the stereographic parametrization of the coherent state. The stationary points of this classical energy are found and classified as minima under specific ordering conditions of the inertial parameters A_k. The energy function is then expanded harmonically around each minimum, quantized via the correspondence principle, and the resulting wobbling frequencies are used to compute E2 and M1 transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The critical spin where the transition from transverse to tilted‑axis wobbling occurs in ¹³⁵Pr is around I = 29/2.
- For hydrodynamic moments of inertia, the transversal wobbling regime with A₁ > A₃, A₂ exists only for I = 13/2 and I = 15/2, with γ intervals: 90° < γ < 103.71° (Mod π) and 136.29° < γ < 150° (Mod π) for I = 13/2; 90° < γ < 92.45° (Mod π) and 147.55° < γ < 150° (Mod π) for I = 15/2.
- The wobbling frequency for the transversal regime (mode 1) is given by ω₁(I) = √{[(2I‑1)(A₃‑A₁)+2A₁j] [(2I‑1)(A₂‑A₁)+2A₁j]}, and for the tilted‑axis modes (modes 2 and 3) by ω₂(I) = (2I‑1)√{(A₃‑A₂)(A₁‑A₂)} sin α₂ and ω₃(I) = (2I‑1)√{(A₂‑A₃)(A₁‑A₃)} sin α₃.
- The tilting angle α₂ (or α₃) is defined by cos α₂,₃ = 2A₁j / [(2I‑1)(A₁‑A₂,₃)]; for asymptotically high angular momentum, sin α₂,₃ → 1, meaning the average rotation axis aligns perpendicular to the alignment axis.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical description of wobbling in odd‑mass nuclei introduced by Frauendorf and Dönau (2014), which explained the decreasing wobbling excitation energy as transverse wobbling. It also builds on the Bohr‑Mottelson (1975) wobbling frequency for even‑even systems and on earlier studies of wobbling in ¹⁶³Lu by Ødegård et al. (2001) and in ¹³⁵Pr by Matta and Garg (2015). The paper provides a unified phase diagram showing the domains of principal‑axis and tilted‑axis wobbling, and it enables the interpretation of the termination of transverse wobbling bands as a transition to tilted‑axis wobbling, which can be tested in future experiments on ¹³⁵Pr and similar nuclei.
