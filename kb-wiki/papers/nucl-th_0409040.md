# On the correlation between the binding energies of the triton and the alpha-particle

> We consider the correlation between the binding energies of the triton and the alpha-particle which is empirically observed in calculations employing different phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interactions. Using an effective quantum mechanics approach for short-range interactions with large scattering length |a| >> l, where l is the natural low-energy length scale, we construct the effective interaction potential at leading order in l/|a|. In order to renormalize the four-nucleon system, it is sufficient to include a SU(4)-symmetric one-parameter three-nucleon interaction in addition to the S-wave nucleon-nucleon interactions. The absence of a four-nucleon force at this order explains the empirically observed correlation between the binding energies of the triton and the alpha-particle. We calculate this correlation and obtain a prediction for the alpha-particle binding energy. Corrections to our results are suppressed by l/|a|.

## Key claim

The empirically observed correlation between the triton and alpha-particle binding energies (the Tjon line) follows from the fact that, at leading order in an expansion in ℓ/|a|, no four-nucleon force is needed to renormalize the four-nucleon system; only a SU(4)-symmetric three-nucleon interaction is required in addition to S-wave nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective quantum mechanics approach with short-range contact interactions, expanded to leading order in ℓ/|a| where ℓ is the natural low-energy length scale and a is the scattering length. They solved the Yakubovsky equations numerically in momentum space for the four-nucleon system, fixing the two-body couplings from the spin-singlet and spin-triplet scattering lengths (or the deuteron binding energy) and the three-body parameter from the triton binding energy. The cutoff independence of the alpha-particle binding energy was checked by varying the regulator cutoff Λ.

## Key numbers

- The spin-singlet scattering length a_s = -23.5 fm and spin-triplet scattering length a_t = 5.42 fm, both significantly larger than the natural low-energy length scale ℓ ∼ 1.4 fm.
- The three-body parameter L_3 = 21.2 fm^{-1} when using a_t as input, or L_3 = 19.8 fm^{-1} when using the deuteron binding energy B_d = 2.22 MeV as input.
- The alpha-particle binding energy B_α = 29.5 MeV when using a_t as input, or B_α = 26.9 MeV when using B_d as input, compared to the experimental value B_α^{exp} = (29.0 ± 0.1) MeV.
- The triton binding energy used to fix the three-body parameter was B_t = 8.48 MeV.
- The cutoff independence of the alpha-particle binding energy was demonstrated to within 5% for Λ in the range 8 fm^{-1} ≤ Λ ≤ 10 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the four-boson system (Platter, 2004) to the four-nucleon system, and explains the Tjon line originally discovered by Tjon (1975) using separable interactions and later confirmed for modern phenomenological potentials by Nogga (2000). The result shows that the Tjon line is a universal feature of few-body systems with large scattering length, analogous to the Phillips line in the three-nucleon system (Phillips, 1968; Efimov and Tkachenko, 1985; Bedaque, 1999). This enables systematic inclusion of higher-order corrections (suppressed by ℓ/|a|) and extension to scattering observables.
