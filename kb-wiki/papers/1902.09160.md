# Twist-2 Pseudoscalar and Vector Meson Distribution Amplitudes in Light-Front Quark Model with Exponential-type Confining Potential

> We study the twist-2 distribution amplitudes (DAs) and the decay constants of pseudoscalar light ($π$, $K$) and heavy ($D$, $D_s$, $B$, $B_s$) mesons as well as the longitudinally and transversely polarized vector light ($ρ$, $K^*$) and heavy ($D^*$, $D_s^*$, $B^*$, $B_s^*$) mesons in the light-front quark model with the Coulomb plus exponential-type confining potential $V_{\rm {exp}} = a + b e^{αr}$ in addition to the hyperfine interaction. We first compute the mass spectra of ground state pseudoscalar and vector light and heavy mesons and fix the model parameters necessary for the analysis, applying the variational principle with the trial wave function up to the first three lowest order harmonic oscillator (HO) wave functions $Φ(x, \textbf{k}_\bot) = \sum_{n=1}^{3} c_n ϕ_{nS}$. We then obtain the numerical results for the corresponding decay constants of light and heavy mesons. We estimate the DAs, analyze their variation as a function of momentum fraction and compute the first six $ξ$-moments of the $B$ and $D$ mesons as well. We compare our results with the available experimental data as well as with the other theoretical model predictions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that an exponential-type confining potential, combined with a trial wave function expanded over the first three harmonic oscillator basis states, yields predictions for meson mass spectra, decay constants, and twist-2 distribution amplitudes that are comparable to those obtained with linear and harmonic oscillator confining potentials, provided sufficient basis functions are used to achieve efficacy of the model.

## Method

The authors apply the variational principle to a QCD-motivated Hamiltonian with a Coulomb plus exponential-type confining potential \(V_{\rm exp} = a + b e^{\alpha r}\) and a smeared hyperfine interaction, using a trial wave function expanded over the three lowest harmonic oscillator basis states (\(1S\), \(2S\), \(3S\)). They fix model parameters by fitting the \(\pi\) and \(\rho\) meson masses and requiring a common strong coupling constant \(\kappa\) for all ground-state pseudoscalar and vector mesons, then compute mass spectra, decay constants, and twist-2 distribution amplitudes for light (\(\pi\), \(K\), \(\rho\), \(K^*\)) and heavy (\(D\), \(D_s\), \(B\), \(B_s\), \(D^*\), \(D_s^*\), \(B^*\), \(B_s^*\)) mesons.

## Key numbers

* Strong coupling constant \(\kappa = 0.4220\) obtained from the variational fit, compared to \(\kappa = 0.4829\) from the linear confining potential case.
* Constituent quark masses: \(m_q = 0.202\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.405\) GeV, \(m_c = 1.725\) GeV, \(m_b = 5.182\) GeV.
* Gaussian parameters \(\beta\) for pseudoscalar mesons range from \(\beta_{qq} = 0.3387\) GeV (\(\pi\)) to \(\beta_{bs} = 0.3290\) GeV (\(B_s\)); for vector mesons from \(\beta_{qq} = 0.2308\) GeV (\(\rho\)) to \(\beta_{bs} = 0.3250\) GeV (\(B_s^*\)).
* Predicted meson masses (in GeV): \(M_K = 0.521\), \(M_{K^*} = 0.826\), \(M_D = 1.803\), \(M_{D^*} = 1.884\), \(M_{D_s} = 1.929\), \(M_{D_s^*} = 1.971\), \(M_B = 5.212\), \(M_{B^*} = 5.242\), \(M_{B_s} = 5.313\), \(M_{B_s^*} = 5.329\).

## Context

This work extends previous light-front quark model analyses by Choi and Ji (2015, 2016) that used linear and harmonic oscillator confining potentials with up to two lowest harmonic oscillator basis functions, by introducing an exponential-type confining potential and requiring three basis functions for variational convergence. The results enable further studies of hard exclusive processes involving pseudoscalar and vector mesons, and provide constraints for future experiments on the role of variational parameters in light-front quark models.
