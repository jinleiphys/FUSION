# Electroexcitation of nucleon resonances in a light-front relativistic quark model

> We report the predictions for the 3q core contributions to the electroexcitation of the resonances Delta(1232)3/2+, N(1440)1/2+, N(1520)3/2-, N(1535)1/2-, and N(1675)5/2- on the proton obtained in the light-front relativistic quark model (LF RQM). For these states, experimental data on the electroexcitation transition amplitudes allow us to make comparison between the experiment and LF RQM predictions in wide range of Q2 and also to quantify the expected meson-baryon contributions as a function of Q2.

# Electroexcitation of nucleon resonances in a light-front relativistic quark model

## Key claim
The light-front relativistic quark model (LF RQM) predicts the three-quark (3q) core contributions to the electroexcitation of five nucleon resonances on the proton, and comparison with experimental data allows quantification of the meson-baryon contributions as a function of Q², under the assumption that at Q² > 2-3 GeV² the transitions are determined only by the 3q core term.

## Method
The authors employed a light-front relativistic quark model with two forms of spatial wave functions (exponential in invariant mass and exponential in quark momenta) and running quark masses parameterized as functions of Q². For each resonance, the weight of the 3q component in the nucleon resonance wave function was extracted from experimental transition amplitudes at high Q², where meson-baryon contributions are assumed negligible. The meson-baryon contributions were then inferred as the difference between the LF RQM predictions and experimental data across the full Q² range.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon wave function composition: |N> = 0.95|3q> + 0.313|πN>
- 3q core coefficients c_N*: Δ(1232)3/2+ = 0.88 ± 0.04, N(1440)1/2+ = 0.93 ± 0.05, N(1520)3/2- = 0.93 ± 0.06, N(1535)1/2- = 0.91 ± 0.03; for N(1675)5/2-, c_N* ≃ 1
- Running quark mass parameterizations: m_q^(1)(Q²) = 0.22 GeV / (1 + Q²/56 GeV²), m_q^(2)(Q²) = 0.22 GeV / (1 + Q²/18 GeV²)
- At Q² = 5 (12) GeV², running quark mass increases transition helicity amplitudes by 25%-35% (100%-140%) for wave function Φ₁ and 10%-15% (55%-65%) for Φ₂
- Correspondence between Q² and mean invariant mass: Q² = 0, 5, 10, 20 GeV² correspond to <M₀²> = 1.35, 2.66, 3.1, 3.5 GeV²
- At photon point (Q² = 0), inferred meson-baryon contributions for N(1440)1/2+ proton A₁/₂: -31 ± 4 (in units of 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²)
- At photon point, inferred meson-baryon contributions for N(1520)3/2- proton A₃/₂: -174 ± 10 (in units of 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²)
- At photon point, inferred meson-baryon contributions for N(1535)1/2- proton A₁/₂: -54 ± 15 (in units of 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²)
- At photon point, inferred meson-baryon contributions for N(1675)5/2- proton A₁/₂: 16 ± 8, A₃/₂: 15 ± 5 (in units of 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²)

## Context
This work extends the LF RQM framework previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Aznauryan 2012, 2015, 2016, 2017) to the electroexcitation of five nucleon resonances. The results for the Δ(1232) magnetic-dipole form factor are compared with dynamical reaction model calculations (Lee), where the bare 3q contribution gives 40-70% of the total at Q²=0. The identification of N(1440)1/2+ as the first radial excitation of the nucleon, based on CLAS data (Aznauryan 2008, 2009), is confirmed by the LF RQM description. The inferred meson-baryon contributions show peaks at Q²=0 similar to those from coupled-channels analyses (Lee), and the helicity asymmetry for N(1520)3/2- reproduces the rapid switch predicted by nonrelativistic quark models (Close, Isgur). The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted meson-baryon contributions and the suppression of quark core contributions for N(1675)5/2- on the proton versus neutron.
