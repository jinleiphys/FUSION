# Mutual boosting of the saturation scales in colliding nuclei

> Saturation of small-x gluons in a nucleus, which has the form of transverse momentum broadening of projectile gluons in pA collisions in the nuclear rest frame, leads to a modification of the parton distribution functions in the beam compared with pp collisions. The DGLAP driven gluon distribution turns out to be suppressed at large x, but significantly enhanced at x<<1. This is a high twist effect. In the case of nucleus-nucleus collisions all participating nucleons on both sides get enriched in gluon density at small x, which leads to a further boosting of the saturation scale. We derive reciprocity equations for the saturation scales corresponding to a collision of two nuclei. The solution of these equations for central collisions of two heavy nuclei demonstrate a significant, up to several times, enhancement of Q_{sA}^2, in AA compared with pA collisions.

## Key claim

The saturation scale in nucleus-nucleus collisions is boosted by a factor of up to several times compared to proton-nucleus collisions, because participating nucleons on both sides become enriched in gluon density at small x, leading to mutual enhancement of the saturation scales through reciprocity equations.

## Method

The authors derive reciprocity equations for the saturation scales in collisions of two nuclei, where the gluon distribution in each nucleus is evaluated at a scale shifted by the saturation scale of the other nucleus. They solve these equations numerically for central collisions of identical heavy nuclei using LO gluon distributions from MSTW2008, with an infrared cutoff Q₀² fixed by matching to the soft dipole cross section parametrization. The boosting factor is calculated as the ratio of the boosted saturation scale to the standard one, and the results are applied to predict J/ψ broadening in AA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy √s = 200 GeV, the boosting factor for central heavy ion collisions reaches about 1.5
- At LHC energy √s = 5.5 TeV, the boosting factor for central heavy ion collisions reaches about 3
- The saturation scale for heavy nuclei at LHC may be as large as about 10 GeV²
- The infrared cutoff Q₀² is 1.84 GeV² at √s = 200 GeV and 1.7 GeV² at √s = 5.5 TeV
- The Bjorken x values used are x = 3.25 × 10⁻³ at RHIC and x = 1.18 × 10⁻⁴ at LHC
- The mean transverse momentum of gluons is ⟨k_T⟩ ≈ 0.65 GeV

## Context

This work extends the treatment of saturation and broadening from pA collisions (Kopeliovich et al., 2002) to AA collisions, where the mutual boosting of saturation scales is a new effect not present in the Color Glass Condensate or Balitsky-Kovchegov approaches. The reciprocity equations (Eq. 380) are the main theoretical result, and the predicted boosting effect can be tested by measuring J/ψ transverse momentum broadening in AA collisions at LHC, where final state interactions do not affect the pT distribution. The authors note that a similar enhancement observed in NA50/NA60 experiments at lower energy is likely due to a different mechanism involving interactions with gluons radiated in preceding collisions.
