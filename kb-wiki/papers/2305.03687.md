# Multi-charmed and singled charmed hadrons from coalescence: yields and ratios in different collision systems at LHC

> We study the production of charmed and multi-charmed hadrons in ultra-relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions coupling the transport approach for charm dynamics in the medium to an hybrid hadronization model of coalescence plus fragmentation. In this paper, we mainly discuss the particle yields for single charmed and multi-charmed baryons focusing mainly on the production of $Ξ_{cc}$ and $Ω_{ccc}$. We provide first predictions for PbPb collision in 0-10% centrality class and then we explore the system size dependence through KrKr, to ArAr and OO collisions, planned within the ALICE3 experiment. In these cases, a monotonic behavior for the yields emerges which can be tested in future experimental data. We found about three order of magnitude increase in the production of $Ω_{ccc}$ in PbPb collisions compared with the yield in small collision systems like OO collisions. Furthermore, we investigate the effects on the $Ω_{ccc}$ particle production and spectra coming from the modification of the charm quark distribution due to the different size of the collision systems comparing also to the case of thermalized charm distributions. These results suggest that observation on the $Ω_{ccc}$ spectra and their evolution across system size can give information about the partial thermalization of the charm quark distribution as well as to its wave function width.

## Key claim

The paper provides first predictions for multi-charmed baryon yields (Ξ_cc and Ω_ccc) in PbPb collisions at LHC and across smaller collision systems (KrKr, ArAr, OO), showing that the yields depend sensitively on the degree of thermalization of the charm quark distribution and on the hadron wave-function width, with the caveat that the Ω_ccc yield range spans an order of magnitude depending on whether a realistic or fully thermalized charm distribution is assumed.

## Method

The authors couple a relativistic Boltzmann transport approach for charm quark dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma to a hybrid hadronization model that combines coalescence (using a Gaussian Wigner function formalism) with Peterson fragmentation for high-pT charm quarks. The fireball parameters (volume, radial flow, lifetime) for each collision system (PbPb, KrKr, ArAr, OO) are fixed by matching total multiplicity and transverse energy to experimental data, while the initial charm quark spectra are taken from FONLL pQCD calculations and evolved via the Boltzmann equation. The coalescence probability is forced to unity at zero momentum, and the Wigner function widths for multi-charmed hadrons are scaled from single-charmed baryon frequencies using reduced masses.

## Key numbers

- In PbPb collisions at 0-10% centrality and √s = 5.02 TeV, the Ξ_cc yield is about (8 ± 2) × 10⁻³, the Ω_cc yield is about (0.5 ± 0.02) × 10⁻³, and the Ω_ccc yield is between 0.12 × 10⁻⁴ and 1.01 × 10⁻⁴ (range from realistic to thermalized charm distribution).
- The Λ_c yield in PbPb collisions is dN^(Λ_c)/dy = 3.28 ± 0.42(stat) ± 0.44(syst) ± 0.16(BR), in agreement with ALICE measurements.
- The number of charm quarks scales with system size: N_c^(PbPb) ~ 15, N_c^(KrKr) ~ 4.35, N_c^(ArAr) ~ 1.5, N_c^(OO) ~ 0.4.
- The fireball volumes for |y| < 0.5 are 5000 fm³ (PbPb), 2000 fm³ (KrKr), 920 fm³ (ArAr), and 345 fm³ (OO).
- The Ω_ccc yield increases by about three orders of magnitude from OO to PbPb collisions.
- The J/ψ yield in PbPb is in the range (1-6) × 10⁻², about a factor 2 smaller than the Statistical Hadronization Model result.

## Context

This work extends the Catania coalescence model, previously applied to single-charmed hadrons in pp and AA collisions (Minissale 2020, Plumari 2017), to multi-charmed baryons (Ξ_cc, Ω_ccc) for the first time. It provides predictions for the ALICE3 experiment at the HL-LHC, complementing calculations from the Statistical Hadronization Model (Andronic 2021) and earlier studies on multi-charmed production (Becattini 2005, Cho 2019, He 2014). The results enable experimental tests of charm quark thermalization by comparing Ω_ccc spectra across different collision system sizes, and can constrain the wave-function width of multi-charmed baryons.
