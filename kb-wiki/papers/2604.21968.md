# Gradient-Produced Neutrinos

> Sufficiently strong electric fields can produce charged-particle pairs via the Schwinger effect. We argue that steep matter-density gradients, as can arise in neutron star interiors, would analogously produce neutrino-antineutrino pairs. We then discuss observational signatures of these gradient-produced (anti)neutrinos and how they could provide new probes of neutron-star structure and baryon-dense QCD.

## Key claim

Steep matter-density gradients in neutron star interiors can produce neutrino-antineutrino pairs via an analog of the Schwinger effect, but the production rate depends on the gradient sharpness and the mass of the neutrino, with the zero-mixing approximation valid only when the gradient length scale is much smaller than the inverse of the potential jump.

## Method

The authors reduce the Dirac equation for a fermion coupled to a spatially varying vector and axial-vector background to Schrodinger-like second-order differential equations, then solve exactly for a Sauter potential profile V(z) = (ΔV/2) tanh(z/l). They use the Bogoliubov method to relate asymptotic in/out plane-wave solutions via transmission and reflection amplitudes, obtaining the mean occupation number of produced pairs per mode. The calculation is performed in the small-mass limit where chiral mixing is neglected, and the results for general vector and axial-vector couplings (c_V, c_A) are obtained by rescaling the known pure-vector (c_V=1, c_A=0) Sauter-potential solution.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino-absorption mean free path for the process ν_e s → u e^- in free space is (n_s σ_{ν_e s})^{-1} ∼ 0.5 m, based on n_s ∼ 1 fm^{-3} and σ_{ν_e s} ∼ G_F^2 θ_C^2 m_s^2 ∼ 2 × 10^{-41} cm^2 with θ_C = 0.23 and m_s = 100 MeV.
- Accounting for Pauli blocking with μ_u ∼ μ_e ∼ 100 MeV and T_core ∼ 10 keV increases the mean free path by a factor of (μ_u/T_core)(μ_e/T_core) ∼ 10^8, yielding λ_{ν_e n} ∼ 5 × 10^4 km.
- The volume-integrated rate of chemical equilibration through strange-quark Urca processes is Γ_{s,Urca} ∼ 10^{40} s^{-1} (T_core/keV)^5 (δμ/T_core), giving a relaxation time of n_n R^3/Γ_{s,Urca} ∼ 10^{23} yr (T_core/keV)^{-5} (δμ/T_core)^{-1}.
- The transport mean free path for neutrino scattering on relativistic quarks is λ_{ν q} ∼ [G_F^2 E_ν^2 n_q (T_core/μ_q)]^{-1} ∼ 2 × 10^{13} km (T_core/10 keV)^{-1} for E_ν ∼ 10 eV, n_q ∼ 1 fm^{-3}, and μ_q ∼ 100 MeV.
- The effective neutrino production rate per neutron star is Ṅ_ν^{grad} F ≲ 2 × 10^{38} s^{-1}.
- The local flux from the nearest plausible undetected NS at ∼10 pc is ≲ 0.02 cm^{-2} s^{-1}.
- The aggregate Galactic contribution from ∼10^9 NSs at ∼10 kpc yields a local flux of ≲ 20 cm^{-2} s^{-1}.
- The extragalactic contribution from ∼5 × 10^{17} neutron stars yields a diffuse background flux of φ ≲ 0.1 cm^{-2} s^{-1}.
- The cross section for electron neutrino capture on tritium is σ ≈ 4 × 10^{-45} cm^2 for E_ν ≪ keV, yielding an event rate of ∼9 yr^{-1} for the CνB and 2 × 10^{-9} yr^{-1} for Galactic gradient-produced neutrinos with a 100 g tritium target.
- Thermal neutrino luminosities follow L_{νν̄}^{th} = a (T_core/100 eV)^b, with Urca processes having a ∼ 1 × 10^{27}–3 × 10^{28} erg/s and b = 6, MUrca processes having a ∼ 8 × 10^{15}–2 × 10^{17} erg/s and b = 8, and quark matter processes having a ∼ 10^{24}–10^{25} erg/s and b = 6.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger effect for charged particles in electric fields (Fedotov 2022, Gelis 2015) to neutral particles (neutrinos) in matter-density gradients, analogous to how a spatially varying potential can produce particle-antiparticle pairs. The authors build on the Sauter-potential solution for the Dirac equation (Chervyakov 2009, Chervyakov 2011, 1999 Physics Reports) and generalize it to arbitrary vector and axial-vector couplings. This enables new probes of neutron star structure and baryon-dense QCD, potentially explaining observed neutron star cooling that exceeds standard thermal neutrino emission (Yakovlev 2004, Page 2004). The mechanism could dominate over rotochemical heating from Urca processes (Reisenegger 1996) for certain core temperature and chemical-potential-imbalance ranges, and the resulting neutrino flux could be detectable by PTOLEMY-like experiments (Chacko 2018, McKeen 2018) or other low-threshold detectors.
