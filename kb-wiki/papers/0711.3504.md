# Non-Uniform Structure of Matter and the Equation of State

> We investigate the non-uniform structures and the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter in the context of the first-order phase transitions (FOPT) such as liquid-gas phase transition, kaon condensation, and hadron-quark phase transition. During FOPT the mixed phases appear, where matter exhibits non-uniform structures called ``Pasta'' structures due to the balance of the Coulomb repulsion and the surface tension between two phases. We treat these effects self-consistently, properly taking into account of the Poisson equation and the Gibbs conditions. Consequently, they make the EOS of the mixed phase closer to that of Maxwell construction due to the Debye screening. This is a general feature of the mixed phase consisting of many species of charged particles.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that when the Coulomb interaction is treated self-consistently (including the Poisson equation and Gibbs conditions) rather than perturbatively, the equation of state of the mixed phase in first-order phase transitions approaches that of the Maxwell construction due to Debye screening, contrary to the earlier naive view that a broad structured mixed phase must appear when multiple chemical components are present.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for low-density nuclear matter and kaon-condensed matter, and a nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach combined with the MIT bag model for hadron-quark mixed phase. They solve the coupled equations of motion for meson fields and the Poisson equation for the Coulomb potential self-consistently within Wigner-Seitz cells of spherical (3D), cylindrical (2D), and slab (1D) geometries, searching for the minimum-energy configuration among droplet, rod, slab, tube, and bubble structures.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ (abstract) and $\rho_0 \equiv 0.153$ fm$^{-3}$ (text, from parameter fit)
- Minimum energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter: $-16.3$ MeV at $\rho = \rho_0$
- Incompressibility: $K(\rho_0) = 240$ MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: $m_N^{*}(\rho_0) = 0.78 m_N$ with $m_N = 938$ MeV
- Isospin-asymmetry coefficient: $a_{\rm sym} = 32.5$ MeV
- Critical density for kaon condensation in single phase: $\rho_B \simeq 0.46$ fm$^{-3}$
- Surface tension parameter for hadron-quark interface: $\sigma = 40$ MeV/fm$^2$
- Strange quark mass in MIT bag model: $m_s = 150$ MeV
- Cell radius and droplet radius at $\rho_B = 0.4$ fm$^{-3}$ for hadron-quark mixed phase: $R_W = 26.7$ fm, $R = 17.3$ fm
- Energy reduction from non-uniform structures compared to uniform matter: up to about 15 MeV per baryon

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear pasta structures by Ravenhall (1983) and others, treating the Coulomb interaction self-consistently rather than perturbatively. The authors show that the Debye screening effect makes the equation of state approach the Maxwell construction, contradicting the earlier argument by Glendenning (1992) and others that a broad structured mixed phase must appear when multiple chemical components are present. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star crusts and cores, including the effects of kaon condensation and hadron-quark phase transitions on the equation of state.
