# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov description of deformed light nuclei

> The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model is applied in the analysis of ground-state properties of Be, B, C, N, F, Ne and Na isotopes. The model uses the NL3 effective interaction in the mean-field Lagrangian, and describes pairing correlations by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction D1S. Neutron separation energies, quadrupole deformations, nuclear matter radii, and differences in radii of proton and neutron distributions are compared with recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the NL3 effective interaction and Gogny D1S pairing can describe global trends of ground-state properties for light Be, B, C, N, F, Ne, and Na isotopes, but it does not reproduce the sudden increase of matter radii at N=15 in Nitrogen and Fluorine (interpreted as evidence for one-neutron halo formation) and cannot describe radii of halo nuclei because the calculation is performed in a harmonic oscillator basis rather than coordinate space.

## Method

The RHB equations were solved self-consistently by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential, using 14 oscillator shells for fermion fields and 20 shells for boson fields. The NL3 effective interaction was used for the mean-field Lagrangian, and pairing correlations were described by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction D1S. A simple blocking procedure without breaking time-reversal symmetry was used for odd-proton and/or odd-neutron systems.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron drip-line nucleus 14Be: prolate deformation beta_2 = 0.36, ratio of neutron to proton quadrupole moments Q_n / Q_p = 3.6, and neutron skin r_n - r_p = 0.71 fm.
- For 19C: calculated neutron separation energy 510 keV, compared with experimental value 530 +/- 130 keV; predicted large oblate deformation beta_2 approximately -0.4.
- 13Be predicted to be unbound by 180 keV.
- Heaviest particle stable Nitrogen isotope predicted: 23N; heaviest particle stable Fluorine isotope predicted: 31F.
- For 22N: calculated one-neutron separation energy slightly lower than experimental value 1.22 +/- 0.22 MeV; theoretical matter radius coincides with the one deduced from experimental interaction cross section.
- For mirror nuclei with Delta T_z = 2 and 3, proton-rich nuclei have almost 0.1 fm larger radii than their T_z > 0 mirror partners.

## Context

This work extends previous spherical RHB calculations of neutron-rich isotopes (Lalazissis, Vretenar, and Ring, 1998) and deformed RHB calculations of the A = 20 isobaric sequence (Lalazissis, Vretenar, and Ring, 2001) to a broader set of light isotopic chains. The results are compared with experimental data from Suzuki et al. (1999), Ozawa et al. (2001), and Audi and Wapstra (1995), and with predictions of the finite-range droplet model (Moller and Nix, 1995). The study demonstrates that the NL3+D1S combination can describe ground-state properties of light nuclei near the low-mass limit of mean-field applicability, but the harmonic oscillator basis prevents accurate description of halo structures, which require coordinate-space calculations.
