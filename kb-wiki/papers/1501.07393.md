# Neutron star equations of state with optical potential constraint

> Nuclear matter and neutron stars are studied in the framework of an extended relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with higher-order derivative and density dependent couplings of nucleons to the meson fields. The derivative couplings lead to an energy dependence of the scalar and vector self-energies of the nucleons. It can be adjusted to be consistent with experimental results for the optical potential in nuclear matter. Several parametrisations, which give identical predictions for the saturation properties of nuclear matter, are presented for different forms of the derivative coupling functions. The stellar structure of spherical, non-rotating stars is calculated for these new equations of state (EoS). A substantial softening of the EoS and a reduction of the maximum mass of neutron stars is found if the optical potential constraint is satisfied.

## Key claim

The paper shows that incorporating an energy-dependent optical potential constraint into relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, via derivative couplings that reproduce the experimentally observed saturation of the optical potential at high kinetic energies, leads to a substantial softening of the neutron star equation of state and a reduction of the maximum neutron star mass, potentially below the observed two-solar-mass threshold.

## Method

The authors extended a standard density-dependent RMF model by introducing derivative coupling operators (Lorentzian or exponential functions of nucleon energy) that make the scalar and vector self-energies energy-dependent. They fixed the model parameters to reproduce identical saturation properties of nuclear matter (saturation density, binding energy, compressibility, symmetry energy, effective mass) for three choices of the derivative coupling function (D1: constant, D2: Lorentzian, D3: exponential) and then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for spherical, non-rotating neutron stars to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density set to \(n_{\rm sat} = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy per nucleon \(B = 16\) MeV, compressibility \(K = 240\) MeV, symmetry energy \(J = 32\) MeV, symmetry energy slope \(L = 60\) MeV, effective nucleon mass \(m_{\rm eff} = 0.5625 m_{\rm nuc}\)
- Meson masses used: \(m_{\sigma} = 550\) MeV/c\(^2\), \(m_{\omega} = 783\) MeV/c\(^2\), \(m_{\rho} = 763\) MeV/c\(^2\)
- Cut-off parameter \(\Lambda\) values tested: 400, 500, 600, and 700 MeV for the D2 and D3 parametrizations
- The standard DD2 parametrization (without derivative couplings) predicts a maximum neutron star mass of \(2.4 M_{\rm sol}\)
- A previous NLD model (Gaitanos et al., 2012) yielded a maximum neutron star mass of \(2.03 M_{\rm sol}\) barely satisfying observational constraints

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear derivative (NLD) model approach of Gaitanos et al. (2009, 2011, 2012) by adding density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings and using energy-dependent rather than momentum-dependent derivative operators, making the formalism more suitable for future applications to finite nuclei. It directly addresses the "hyperon puzzle" and similar softening problems (Lonardoni, 2014; Fortin, 2014) by showing that even purely nucleonic equations of state can yield reduced maximum masses when constrained by the experimentally observed saturation of the optical potential (Hama, 1990; Cooper, 1993), in contrast to standard RMF models like DD2 (Typel, 2009) which predict higher maximum masses. The results enable future studies of finite nuclei with the DD-NLD approach and highlight the need to reconcile optical potential constraints with the observed two-solar-mass neutron stars.
