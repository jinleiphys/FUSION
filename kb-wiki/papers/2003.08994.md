# Baryon production probability via the nuclear configurational entropy

> We study the net-baryon production at forward rapidities within the Color Glass Condensate paradigm. At high energy regime, the leading baryon production mechanism is shown to change from recombination to independent fragmentation. The nuclear configurational entropy (NCE) constructed upon forward scattering amplitudes, allows to predict the two free parameters that govern the anomalous dimension of the target gluon distribution. The global minimum of the NCE indicates a point of stability in pp/pA/AA collisions at LHC energies, corroborating and matching HERA data for hadron spectra measured in pp and dAu collisions at RHIC energies, with accuracy between 1.2\% and 1.8\%, respectively for the two free parameters.

## Key claim

The nuclear configurational entropy (NCE) constructed from forward scattering amplitudes predicts the two free parameters that govern the anomalous dimension of the target gluon distribution, with the global minimum of the NCE indicating a point of stability in pp/pA/AA collisions at LHC energies. The abstract glosses over the fact that these predictions are derived specifically for the baryon production probability and are validated against HERA and RHIC data with stated accuracy margins of 1.2% to 1.8%.

## Method

The author computed the nuclear configurational entropy (NCE) from the forward scattering amplitude for baryon production, using the Glauber-like formula with an anomalous dimension parameterized by two free parameters \(a\) and \(b\). The differential cross section for leading baryon production was expressed via the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, incorporating the unintegrated gluon distribution and the net-baryon fragmentation function. The NCE was then calculated as a function of \(a\) and \(b\) by taking the Fourier transform of the squared forward scattering amplitude, constructing the modal fraction, and applying the Shannon entropy formula; the global minimum of the NCE was found numerically using Mathematica's NMaximize routine.

## Key numbers

- Global minimum of the nuclear configurational entropy: CE(2.77, 166.02) = 0.476, at parameter values \(a = 2.77\) and \(b = 166.02\).
- These predicted values match the fitted parameters from Duraes (2.82 and 168) with accuracy of 1.8% for parameter \(a\) and 1.2% for parameter \(b\).
- The saturation scale exponent \(\lambda = 0.252\) was obtained from prior CE work (Karapetyan 2019) and matches experimental data within approximately 0.4%.
- The parameter \(\Lambda^2 = 0.04\ \text{GeV}^2\) is used in the saturation scale expression.

## Context

This work extends the configurational entropy approach previously applied to hadron-nucleus collisions (Karapetyan 2019) and to scalar and tensor meson production (Ferreira 2019, Bernardini 2018, Braga 2018) into the domain of net-baryon production at forward rapidities. The results corroborate the fitted parameters from Duraes for hadron spectra in pp and dAu collisions at RHIC energies, and provide a first-principles prediction of those parameters via the NCE global minimum. This enables future applications of the NCE to other nuclear configurations and wavefunctions, as proposed in work by da Rocha (2005, 2013) and Bazeia (2013).
