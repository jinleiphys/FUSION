# Momentum dependent potentials from a parity doubling CMF model in UrQMD: Results on flow and particle production

> The quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) part of the UrQMD model is extended to allow implementation of momentum dependent potentials from a parity doubling chiral mean field (CMF) model. Important aspects like energy conservation and effects on particle production and flow are discussed. It is shown, that this new implementation reproduces qualitatively and quantitatively available data over a wide range of beam energies and improves the description of observables without exception. In particular the description of hyperon and pion production at SIS18 energies is improved. From a comparison with HADES data one could conclude that the present parametrization of the CMF model leads to a slightly too weak momentum dependence. However, a more firm conclusion will require a systematic comparison with flow and multiplicity data over a range of beam energies and system sizes. Our work serves as an important step towards such future studies where the properties of dense QCD matter, through parameters of the CMF model, can be constraint using a comparison of the UrQMD model with high precision heavy ion data, finally also allowing direct comparisons with neutron star and neutron star merger observables.

## Key claim

The paper claims that implementing momentum-dependent potentials from a parity-doubling chiral mean field (CMF) model into the UrQMD transport model improves the description of heavy-ion observables without exception, but the present parametrization leads to a slightly too weak momentum dependence when compared with HADES data, requiring further systematic comparison before firm conclusions can be drawn.

## Method

The authors extended the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) part of the UrQMD model to include momentum-dependent single-particle potentials derived from the relativistic CMF model, which provides consistent density and momentum dependence for all baryon species including nucleons, hyperons, Delta baryons, and their parity partners. They implemented these potentials by numerically inverting the relation between the single-particle potential and the potential energy per baryon at fixed momentum, then used the resulting momentum- and density-dependent potentials in the non-relativistic equations of motion. The simulations were performed for three scenarios: purely density-dependent potentials, momentum-dependent potentials using only nucleon potentials for all baryons, and full species-dependent (ID-dependent) momentum-dependent potentials.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 with binding energy E/A - mN = -16.6 MeV, nuclear incompressibility 278 MeV, symmetry energy 34 MeV, and symmetry energy slope L = 42 MeV
* Hyperon potentials at saturation density and zero momentum: U_Lambda = -30.8 MeV, U_Sigma = -1.5 MeV, U_Xi = -16.5 MeV
* The CMF model predicts a first-order phase transition at mu_B approximately equal to mN - 16 MeV with critical temperature T_CP approximately 17 MeV, and a chiral transition at approximately 4.7 n0
* Energy conservation violation with momentum-dependent potentials is less than 2.4 percent for all beam energies considered
* Excluded volume parameters: v_j = 0.72 fm^3 for baryons, v_j = 1/10 fm^3 for mesons

## Context

This work extends previous UrQMD simulations that used only density-dependent CMF potentials (Omana Kuttan 2022, Steinheimer 2022) by adding momentum dependence, which earlier studies (Aichelin 1987, Hong 2013, Hartnack 2005, Welke 1988, Gale 1989) had highlighted as important. The approach enables future statistical inference of not only the density dependence but also the momentum and particle-type dependence of interactions in dense QCD matter, allowing direct comparisons with neutron star and neutron star merger observables.
