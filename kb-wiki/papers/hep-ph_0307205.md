# Neutrino mixing and Lorentz invariance

> We use previous work on the Hilbert space for mixed fields to derive deformed dispersion relations for neutrino flavor states. We then discuss how these dispersion relations may be incorporated into frameworks encoding the breakdown of Lorentz invariance. We consider non-linear relativity schemes (of which doubly special relativity is an example), and also frameworks allowing for the existence of a preferred frame. In both cases we derive expressions for the spectrum and end-point of beta decay, which may be used as an experimental probe of the peculiar way in which neutrinos experience Lorentz invariance.

## Key claim

Neutrino flavor states, defined as superpositions of mass eigenstates, do not satisfy the standard dispersion relation \(E^2 - k^2 = m^2\); this deformed dispersion can be incorporated either into a non-linear realization of Lorentz invariance (such as doubly special relativity) or into a framework with a preferred frame, and each choice leads to distinct predictions for the beta-decay spectrum and endpoint.

## Method

The authors start from the Pontecorvo approximation for two-flavor mixing, defining flavor states as linear combinations of mass eigenstates with mixing angle \(\theta\). They compute the expectation value of the Hamiltonian on these flavor states to obtain deformed dispersion relations \(E_e = \omega_{k,1}\cos^2\theta + \omega_{k,2}\sin^2\theta\) (and similarly for \(\nu_\mu\)). They then recast these relations into the form \(E^2 f^2(E) - k^2 g^2(E) = M^2\) and identify the non-linear Lorentz transformations that leave them invariant, or alternatively treat the deformed dispersion as selecting a preferred frame. Finally, they derive energy-conservation laws for beta decay under both scenarios and compute the resulting electron kinetic-energy spectra and endpoints.

## Key numbers

- For tritium beta decay, the available energy is \(Q = 18.6\) KeV.
- The rest mass of the electron neutrino (minimum energy at zero momentum) is \(m_e = m_1 \cos^2\theta + m_2 \sin^2\theta\).
- The kinematic invariant \(M_e^2\) for electron neutrinos is given by \(M_e^2 = (m_1^2\cos^4\theta - m_2^2\sin^4\theta) / \cos(2\theta)\); it becomes negative when \(\tan\theta > \sqrt{m_1/m_2}\).
- For maximal mixing (\(\theta = \pi/4\)), \(M_e^2 = (m_1^2 + m_2^2)/2\).
- The endpoint of beta decay in the non-linear relativity case (no preferred frame) is \(K_{\text{max}} = Q - M_e\) (for \(M_e^2 \ge 0\)); in the preferred-frame case it is \(K_{\text{max}} = Q - m_e\).
- For \(M_e^2 < 0\), the endpoint becomes \(K_{\text{max}} = Q\) (unbounded below in the non-linear scheme without a preferred frame).

## Context

This work extends the QFT treatment of flavor states developed by Blasone, Vitiello, and collaborators (1995, 1999) and by Hannabuss (2000), which resolved the problem of defining a Hilbert space for flavor states despite Bargmann super-selection rules. It connects neutrino oscillations to the phenomenological framework of deformed dispersion relations used in quantum gravity by Amelino-Camelia (2001) and by Magueijo and Smolin (2002). The results enable experimental tests of Lorentz invariance via precise measurements of the beta-decay spectrum, such as those planned by the KATRIN collaboration, and distinguish between scenarios where flavor states obey non-linear Lorentz transformations or select a preferred cosmological frame.
