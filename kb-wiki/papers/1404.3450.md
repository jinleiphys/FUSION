# Exotic Four Quark Matter: $Z_1(4475)$

> Motivated by the LHCb's recent confirmation of $Z_1(4475)$ as the $J^P=1^+$ resonance, we investigate various exotic interpretations of $Z_1(4475)$, which may be an axial vector tetraquark state, the P-wave excitation of the S-wave $D_1 {\bar D}^\ast$ or $D_2 {\bar D}^\ast$ molecule, the S-wave molecule composed of a $D$ or $D^*$ meson and a D-wave vector $D$ meson, or the cousin molecular state of $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ composed of a $D$ or $D^*$ meson and their radial excitations. With the help of the heavy quark symmetry, we predict the typical radiative and hidden-charm and open-charm strong decay patterns of $Z_1(4475)$, which are crucial to further identify the molecular state assignment of $Z_1(4475)$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed decay patterns of $Z_1(4475)$ can discriminate among several molecular interpretations, but notes that the non-observation of $Z_1(4475)$ in the $J/\psi\pi$ mode remains a serious challenge for the P-wave molecule and tetraquark schemes, with no manifest symmetry forbidding that mode.

## Method

The authors apply heavy quark spin symmetry to decompose the total angular momentum of $Z_1(4475)$ into conserved heavy and light spin components, then use spin recoupling formulas with 6-j and 9-j symbols to predict the relative strengths of radiative ($M1$, $E1$, $E2$) and strong (hidden-charm and open-charm) decay modes for each molecular assignment. They consider three molecular scenarios: P-wave excitation of $D_1\bar{D}^\ast$ or $D_2\bar{D}^\ast$ molecules, S-wave molecules containing a D-wave vector $D$ meson, and S-wave molecules containing radially excited $D$ or $D^\ast$ mesons.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $Z_1(4475)$: 4475 MeV; width: 172 MeV (from LHCb).
- LHCb confirmed $Z_1(4475)$ as $J^P=1^+$ with a significance of $13.9\sigma$.
- LHCb observed a second charged state $Z_0(4239)$ with $J^P=0^-$ and width around 220 MeV.
- For the S-wave molecule containing a D-wave vector $D$ meson, the predicted $M1$ decay width ratio $\Gamma(\chi_{c0}\gamma):\Gamma(\chi_{c1}\gamma):\Gamma(\chi_{c2}\gamma)$ is $20:15:1$ ignoring phase space.
- For the S-wave molecule containing radially excited $D$ or $D^\ast$ mesons, the predicted $M1$ decay width ratio is $1:3:5$.
- The binding energy of $Z_0(4239)$ as a possible ground state of the $D_1\bar{D}^\ast$ molecule would be as large as 190 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of charged charmonium-like states, following the discovery of $Z_1(4475)$ by Belle (Choi, 2007) and its spin-parity confirmation by LHCb (Aaij, 2014). It builds on earlier molecular studies by Meng (2007), Liu (2007, 2008), and Ding (2007), and connects to the $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4025)$ states observed by BESIII (2013). The predicted decay patterns enable experimental tests to distinguish between the P-wave molecule, D-wave molecule, and radially-excited molecule assignments, and could guide searches in processes such as $e^+e^- \to Y(4660) \to Z_1(4475)\pi \to D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}\pi$.
