# Determination of the pion-nucleon coupling constant and scattering lengths

> We critically evaluate the isovector GMO sum rule for forward pion-nucleon scattering using the recent precision measurements of negatively charged pion-proton and pion-deuteron scattering lengths from pionic atoms. We deduce the charged-pion-nucleon coupling constant, with careful attention to systematic and statistical uncertainties. This determination gives, directly from data a pseudoscalar coupling constant of 14.11+-0.05(statistical)+-0.19(systematic) or a pseudovector one of 0.0783(11). This value is intermediate between that of indirect methods and the direct determination from backward neutron-proton differential scattering cross sections. We also use the pionic atom data to deduce the coherent symmetric and antisymmetric sums of the negatively charged pion-proton and pion-neutron scattering lengths with high precision. The symmetric sum gives 0.0012+-0.0002(statistical)+-0.0008 (systematic) and the antisymmetric one 0.0895+-0.0003(statistical)+-0.0013(systematic), both in units of inverse charged pion-mass. For the need of the present analysis, we improve the theoretical description of the pion-deuteron scattering length.

## Key claim

The charged-pion-nucleon coupling constant is determined directly from experimental data via the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme (GMO) sum rule, yielding a value intermediate between indirect methods and direct backward np scattering determinations, with the caveat that the systematic uncertainty in the pion-proton scattering length extraction from pionic atoms must be increased from ±0.5% to ±1.0% due to inconsistencies in the treatment of range terms.

## Method

The authors evaluate the isovector GMO sum rule for forward pion-nucleon scattering using precision measurements of π⁻p and π⁻d scattering lengths from pionic atoms, combined with total cross section data for π⁺p and π⁻p. They reorganize the sum rule to separate the dominant contributions and use the π⁻d scattering length, together with charge symmetry, to extract the small isoscalar scattering length. The theoretical description of the pion-deuteron scattering length is improved by including corrections for non-locality of the πN s-wave interaction, Fermi motion, non-static effects, and dispersion contributions.

## Key numbers

- Charged-pion pseudoscalar coupling constant: g²_c(GMO)/4π = 14.11 ± 0.05 (statistical) ± 0.19 (systematic)
- Charged-pion pseudovector coupling constant: f²_c/4π = 0.0783(11)
- Symmetric sum of scattering lengths: (a_π⁻p + a_π⁻n)/2 = (-12 ± 2 (statistical) ± 8 (systematic)) × 10⁻⁴ m_π⁻¹
- Antisymmetric sum of scattering lengths: (a_π⁻p - a_π⁻n)/2 = (895 ± 3 (statistical) ± 13 (systematic)) × 10⁻⁴ m_π⁻¹
- π⁻p scattering length: (883 ± 2 (statistical) ± 10 (systematic)) × 10⁻⁴ m_π⁻¹
- π⁻d scattering length: (-252 ± 5 (statistical) ± 5 (systematic) + i63(7)) × 10⁻⁴ m_π⁻¹
- Static double scattering term for π⁻d: D = -254(4) × 10⁻⁴ m_π⁻¹
- Inverse deuteron radius: ⟨1/r⟩ = 0.456(7) fm⁻¹ = 0.645(10) m_π

## Context

This work resolves discrepancies between earlier determinations of the pion-nucleon coupling constant, which ranged from the Koch-Pietarinen (1980) value of 14.28(18) to the lower values of 13.47(11) from the Nijmegen group (1993-1997) and 13.45(14) from Timmermans (1997). The result is intermediate between indirect methods and the direct extrapolation from backward np differential cross sections by Rahm et al. (1998) giving 14.52(26). The improved determination enables more accurate tests of the Goldberger-Treiman relation, where the coupling constant uncertainty was the main obstacle to matching the experimental precision of other quantities.
