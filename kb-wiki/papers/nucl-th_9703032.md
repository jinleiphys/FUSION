# Nonequilibrium fluid-dynamics in the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> To describe ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions we construct a three-fluid hydrodynamical model. In contrast to one-fluid hydrodynamics, it accounts for the finite stopping power of nuclear matter, i.e. for nonequilibrium effects in the early stage of the reaction. Within this model, we study baryon dynamics in the BNL-AGS energy range. For the system Au+Au we find that kinetic equilibrium between projectile and target nucleons is established only after a time $t_{CM}^{eq}\approx 5~fm/c\simeq 2R_{Au}/γ_{CM}$. Observables which are sensitive to the early stage of the collision (like e.g. nucleon flow) therefore differ considerably from those calculated in the one-fluid model.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a three-fluid hydrodynamical model that accounts for finite stopping power and nonequilibrium effects in the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, finding that kinetic equilibrium between projectile and target nucleons is established only after a time \(t_{CM}^{eq}\approx 5~fm/c\simeq 2R_{Au}/\gamma_{CM}\), which is a significant hedge against the instantaneous thermalization assumed in one-fluid models.

## Method

The authors developed a three-fluid relativistic ideal hydrodynamics model where fluid 1 corresponds to projectile nucleons, fluid 2 to target nucleons, and fluid 3 to newly produced particles (the fireball). The coupling between fluids was derived from binary nucleon-nucleon collision cross sections using experimental data for elastic and inelastic \(NN\) scattering, with source terms parametrized by moments \(\sigma_E\) and \(\sigma_P\) that describe mean energy and longitudinal momentum loss per collision. The hydrodynamical equations were solved on an Eulerian grid using the SHASTA algorithm, with projectile and target fluids merged into one fluid when their Møller velocity became comparable to thermal or Fermi velocities.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic equilibrium time for Au+Au: \(t_{CM}^{eq}\approx 5~fm/c\simeq 2R_{Au}/\gamma_{CM}\)
- Ground state baryon density: \(n_0\simeq 0.16~fm^{-3}\)
- Ground state energy density: \(\epsilon_0\simeq 0.15~GeV/fm^{3}\)
- Maximum projectile energy density in three-fluid model: does not exceed \(10\epsilon_0\) (i.e., \(\approx 1.5~GeV/fm^{3}\))
- Maximum projectile density in one-fluid limit is almost a factor of two higher than in the three-fluid model
- Grid spacing: \(\Delta x=\Delta y=\Delta z=0.15~fm\)
- Time step: \(\Delta t = 0.4\,\Delta x\)
- Ground-state incompressibility: \(k_c=300~MeV\)
- Binding energy: \(W_0=-16~MeV\)
- Phase transition temperature for QGP: \(T_c = 160~MeV\)

## Context

This work extends earlier two-fluid models by Amsden (1975), Clare (1986), and Barz (1988) by adding a third fluid for produced particles that is propagated hydrodynamically, and by treating interactions locally in space-time. The model contradicts the one-fluid hydrodynamical approach at AGS energies, which assumes instantaneous thermalization and overestimates baryon and energy densities at midrapidity. This enables future studies of nucleon flow, baryon stopping, and spectra of produced particles like thermal pions or photons, with a more refined investigation of various particle spectra in preparation.
