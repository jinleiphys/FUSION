# Scattering in strong electromagnetic fields: transverse size effects in tBLFQ

> The framework of 'time-dependent basis light-front quantisation' (tBLFQ) offers a non-perturbative approach to scattering problems in external fields, based on Fock space truncation. Here we extend tBLFQ to include spatio-temporal field inhomogeneities in multiple spacetime directions. This extension is necessary for the proper modelling of e.g. intense laser fields. We focus on the example of nonlinear Compton scattering of an electron on an axicon-type laser, with an emphasis on the transverse structure of the beam. We analyse the impact of field intensity and particle energy, as well as basis truncation effects, on the radiation spectrum of the scattered electron.

## Key claim

The paper extends time-dependent basis light-front quantisation (tBLFQ) to include spatio-temporal field inhomogeneities in multiple spacetime directions, specifically transverse structure, which is necessary for modeling intense laser fields; the authors demonstrate this for nonlinear Compton scattering of an electron on an axicon-type laser, but note that the CM motion is mixed with relative motion in the background field, requiring an approximate factorization to isolate the relative excitation spectrum.

## Method

The authors work in light-front coordinates and use a truncated Fock space containing only the \(|e\rangle\) and \(|e\gamma\rangle\) sectors, with a 2D harmonic oscillator basis for transverse degrees of freedom and discretized longitudinal momentum. They evolve the quantum state using the MSD2 (second-order difference) scheme for the time-evolution operator in the interaction picture, where the background field is a toy axicon laser potential with only a longitudinal component \(\mathcal{A}^\LCm\), proportional to the lowest 2D-HO eigenstate \(\Phi_{00}^{b_l}(x^\LCperp)\). To separate center-of-mass (CM) motion from relative motion in the truncated basis, they introduce a modified CM Hamiltonian with a Lagrange multiplier \(\lambda\) to lift nonzero CM excitations.

## Key numbers

* For \(a_0 > 0\) (repulsive case), the peak of the electron probability distribution shifts to \(|x^\LCperp| \approx 5.5\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\) at \(x^\LCp = 8\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\) and to \(|x^\LCperp| \approx 8\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\) at \(x^\LCp = 12\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\), in agreement with classical trajectory calculations.
* The 2D harmonic oscillator basis truncation parameters are \(N_{\rm max}=200\) and \(K_{\rm total}=10.5\), with basis width \(b = m_e = 0.511\ \text{MeV}\) and background width \(b_l = m_e\).
* The dimensionless background amplitude is \(a_0 = 10\) (repulsive) or \(a_0 = -10\) (attractive), and the initial electron longitudinal momentum is \(p^{\LCp} = 10.5\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous tBLFQ studies (Zhao 2013) that only considered backgrounds with univariate spacetime dependence, enabling the modeling of focused laser fields with transverse structure. The approach complements particle-in-cell (PIC) simulations (Gonoskov 2014) by providing a fully quantum mechanical, non-perturbative framework that avoids the classical approximations inherent in PIC codes. The factorization method using a Lagrange multiplier (Gloeckner 1974, Li 2013) allows the authors to isolate relative motion from CM motion in the truncated basis, which is necessary for studying photon emission spectra in nonlinear Compton scattering.
