# Comment on "Partial conservation of seniority in semi-magic nuclei" by Chong Qi

## Key claim
The review by Qi misrepresents parts of Neergård (2022) and fails to mention the most remarkable result of that study: that in the space spanned by the Escuderos-Zamick multiplets and the I = 10 and 12 multiplets, every rotationally invariant two-body interaction acts as a linear combination of a constant and the square of the total angular momentum.

## Method
The author defines a subspace Φ₄ of the four-body state vector space, spanned by states created by acting with a product of one creation and one annihilation operator on the I = 0 subspace. He shows that the Escuderos-Zamick multiplets (I = 4 and 6 multiplets with seniority v = 4 in the j = 9/2 shell) are orthogonal to Φ₄ by their definition, and verifies by computer algebra that Φ₄ is invariant under every rotationally invariant two-body interaction, providing an alternative proof of universal stationarity of these multiplets.

## Key numbers
- The Escuderos-Zamick multiplets have angular momenta I = 4 and 6 in the j = 9/2 shell.
- The space Φ₄⊥ spanned by the Escuderos-Zamick multiplets and the I = 10 and 12 multiplets is four-dimensional.
- Diagonalising the matrix C (equation 22 of Neergård 2022) representing the restriction to Φ₄₀⊥ of the square of total angular momentum yields exactly the coefficients listed in Table 4 of the review by Qi.
- Two of the four energies of the Escuderos-Zamick multiplets and the I = 10 and 12 multiplets determine the other two.

## Context
This comment corrects the review by Qi (2026), which discusses the partial seniority conserved multiplets first found numerically by Escuderos and Zamick (2006). Neergård (2022) provides an alternative computer algebraic proof of universal stationarity of these multiplets, extending earlier proofs based on interaction matrix elements by Isacker et al. (2008), Qi (2012), and Isacker et al. (2014). The result that every rotationally invariant two-body interaction acts as a linear combination of a constant and I² in the relevant four-dimensional space enables experimental tests of energy relations among the multiplets and implies the existence of a three-dimensional space of interactions that vanish on these states.
