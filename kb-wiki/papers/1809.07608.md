# Pion constituent quarks couplings strong form factors: a dynamical approach

> Form factors for pions interactions with constituent quarks are investigated as the leading effective couplings obtained from a one loop background field method applied to a global color model. Two pion field definitions are considered and the resulting eleven form factors are expressed in terms of components of the quark and gluon propagators that compose only two momentum dependent functions. A momentum dependent Goldberger Treiman relation is also obtained as one of the ratios between the form factors. The resulting form factors with pion momenta up to 1.5 GeV are exhibited for different quark effective masses and two different non-perturbative gluon propagators and they present similar behavior to fittings of experimental data from nucleons form factors. The corresponding pseudoscalar averaged quadratic radii (a.q.r.) and correction to the axial a.q.r. are presented as functions of the sea quark effective mass, being equal respectively to the scalar and vector ones at the present level of calculation.

## Key claim

The paper derives momentum-dependent strong form factors for pion-constituent quark couplings from a one-loop background field method applied to a global color model, but the results are obtained under a large quark and gluon effective mass expansion and depend on the specific choice of pion field definition (Weinberg field versus the usual U-field parameterization), with the resulting form factors showing similar behavior to experimental nucleon form factor data rather than directly reproducing them.

## Method

The authors performed a one-loop background field method calculation on a global color model, splitting the quark field into sea quarks (integrated out to produce pions and the chiral condensate) and constituent background quarks. They expanded the resulting sea quark determinant in a large quark and gluon effective mass expansion to extract the leading momentum-dependent pion-constituent quark couplings, considering two different pion field definitions (Weinberg field and the usual U-field parameterization). The resulting eleven form factors were expressed in terms of only two momentum-dependent functions, F1(K,Q) and F2(K,Q), and numerical results were obtained using two different non-perturbative gluon propagators: a transversal one from Tandy-Maris and an effective longitudinal confining one from Cornwall.

## Key numbers

- Pion momenta were considered up to 1.5 GeV for the form factor calculations
- For the axial form factor fitting, a dipole parameterization with MA = 1.1 GeV was used for comparison
- The axial coupling constant at the constituent quark level was argued to be close to gA ≈ 3/4 or gA ≈ 1
- Two gluon propagators were used: D_I(k) with parameters ω = 0.5 GeV, D = 0.55^3/ω (GeV^2), Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV; and D_II(k) with K_F = (2π M_k/(3 k_e))^2 where k_e = 0.15 and M_k = 220 MeV
- An ultraviolet cutoff Λ = 2 GeV was used for the M3(Q) calculation
- Quark effective masses M* = 310 MeV and M* = 350 MeV from the gap equation were used in the numerical results
- The multiplicative factors 1/4 and 3/4 were applied to M3(Q) to fit the curves into a suitable scale

## Context

This work extends the author's previous derivations of the zero-momentum limit of pion-constituent quark couplings (Braghin, EPJA 2016, EPJA 2018) and momentum-dependent couplings to light vector mesons (Braghin, PRD 2018a, PRD 2018b) by now computing the full momentum-dependent form factors for pion-constituent quark interactions. The results are compared with experimental data for nucleon form factors and with a dipole parameterization from experimental fits (Bernard and Meissner, Alexandrou et al.), and they enable future comparisons between constituent quark model predictions and lattice QCD calculations or experimental data to elucidate the role of internal degrees of freedom in hadron structure.
