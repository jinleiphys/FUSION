# Optimal collision-energy range for realizing macroscopic high-baryon-density matter

> We investigate the volume and lifetime of the high baryon-density matter created in heavy-ion collisions and estimate the optimal collision-energy range to realize the high baryon-density region over a large spacetime volume. We simulate central collisions of gold ions for the center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4 - 19.6\;{\rm GeV}$ with a microscopic transport model JAM. We discover that the optimal range is around $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3 - 5\;{\rm GeV}$, where a baryon density exceeding three times the normal nuclear density is realized with a substantially large spacetime volume. Higher and lower energies are disfavored due to short lifetime and low density, respectively. We also point out that event-by-event fluctuations of the spacetime density profile are large, indicating the importance of the event selection in the experimental analysis.

# Optimal collision-energy range for realizing macroscopic high-baryon-density matter

## Key claim
The optimal collision-energy range for creating high baryon-density matter with a substantially large spacetime volume in central gold-gold collisions is around √sNN = 3–5 GeV, which is significantly lower than previous estimates. The dense region exceeding three times normal nuclear density can be realized with macroscopic spatial and temporal sizes, but regions exceeding five times normal nuclear density have suppressed volume and lifetime and are no longer macroscopically large.

## Method
The authors simulated central collisions of gold ions (impact parameter less than 3 fm, corresponding to top 5% centrality) for center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair √sNN = 2.4–19.6 GeV using the microscopic transport model JAM (version 2.5743) with the RQMDv covariant cascade algorithm and a soft momentum-dependent mean field. They defined three measures from the baryon density in the local rest frame: the spatial volume V3(ρth;t) exceeding a threshold density, the Lorentz-invariant four-volume V4(ρth), and the typical lifetime τ(ρth) = V4 / max[V3]. One thousand events were generated for each energy, and the baryon current was constructed by smearing particle baryon densities with a Gaussian width of 1 fm.

## Key numbers
- The optimal energy range for V4(3ρ0) is √sNN ≈ 3–5 GeV, where [V4(3ρ0)]^(1/4) ≈ 5.8 fm.
- The optimal energy range for V4(4ρ0) is √sNN ≈ 5–7 GeV, where [V4(4ρ0)]^(1/4) ≈ 4.9 fm.
- For ρth/ρ0 = 3, the crossing point of max[V3] and τ occurs at √sNN ≈ 2.6 GeV, with max[V3]^(1/3) = τ ≈ 5.5 fm.
- For ρth/ρ0 = 4, the crossing point is at √sNN ≈ 2.8 GeV, with max[V3]^(1/3) = τ ≈ 4 fm.
- The upper and lower bounds of the 2σ band of V4(3ρ0) at √sNN ≲ 3 GeV are 1250 and 850 fm^4, respectively, giving a factor of approximately 1.5 between top and bottom 2.5% events.
- The maximum spatial volume max[V3(3ρ0;t)]^(1/3) ≈ 6 fm for all collision energies studied.
- The normal nuclear density is ρ0 ≈ 0.17 fm^(-3).

## Context
This work extends previous transport-model studies (Li 1995, Danielewicz 1998, Ohnishi 2015, Bhaduri 2022) that predicted maximum baryon densities at the center exceeding 8ρ0 for √sNN ≳ 5 GeV, but did not quantify the spatial and temporal extent of the dense region. The results contradict earlier estimates from chemical freezeout analyses (Randrup 2006, Begun 2012, Blaschke 2024) that suggested optimal energies around √sNN ≈ 6–10 GeV. The findings are directly relevant to the design and interpretation of experiments at RHIC Beam-Energy Scan, NA61/SHINE, NICA, FAIR, HIAF, and J-PARC-HI. The large event-by-event fluctuations of the spacetime volume (factor ~1.5) indicate that event selection could be used to study density-dependent observables, but also that volume fluctuations may contaminate conserved-charge fluctuation signals used for QCD critical point searches.
