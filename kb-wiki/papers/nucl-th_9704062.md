# Breakup probabilities and the optical potential in elastic deuteron scattering

> We investigate the effect of the deuteron breakup on the optical potential for the elastic scattering of 56 MeV deuterons from $^{51}$V. The breakup probabilities calculated within the post-form distorted wave Born-approximation theory of breakup reactions are fitted to derive the contribution to the optical potential from the breakup channels. The breakup potentials are found to have large real as well as imaginary parts. Thus the dynamical polarization potential due to the breakup process is expected to modify strongly the real part of the optical potential calculated by the double folding model.

## Key claim

The breakup process for 56 MeV deuterons on $^{51}$V produces a dynamical polarization potential with both large real and imaginary parts, which strongly modifies the real part of the optical potential calculated by the double folding model; however, the authors note that there are ambiguities in the shape of the breakup potential obtained from the fitting procedure.

## Method

The authors first calculated deuteron breakup probabilities for the (d,p) and (d,n) channels on a $^{51}$V target at 56 MeV using the post-form distorted wave Born-approximation (PFDWBA) theory. They then fitted these calculated breakup probabilities using an optical model approach where the breakup potential was expanded in a Fourier-Bessel series, varying the coefficients to reproduce the PFDWBA probabilities. The fitting was performed separately for the total breakup (including both elastic and inelastic breakup) and for the elastic breakup (dissociation) channel alone.

## Key numbers

- Total breakup cross section for 56 MeV deuterons on $^{51}$V: 627 mb, composed of inelastic (d,p) 290 mb, inelastic (d,n) 214 mb, and elastic (d,pn) 122 mb
- Total (d,p) breakup cross section: 412 mb, in reasonable agreement with the measured value of 481 mb reported by Matsuoka et al.
- Reaction cross section for the full optical potential: 1571 mb
- With the real dissociation potential removed, the reaction cross section for the bare potential $U(r)-U_{bu}(r)$ was 1405 mb, giving a difference of 166 mb compared to the elastic breakup cross section of 122 mb
- With the complex dissociation potential removed, the reaction cross section for the bare potential increased to 2173 mb

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied by Ingemarsson (1996) to $\alpha$-particle breakup on $^{62}$Ni at 172.5 MeV, where only the ($\alpha$,$^{3}$He) channel was considered. The deuteron case was chosen as a simpler test with only two breakup channels (d,p) and (d,n), for which comprehensive experimental data exist from Matsuoka et al. (1980). The results contradict the weaker dynamical polarization potentials found by Yabana et al. (1992) for 80 MeV deuterons on $^{58}$Ni, which neglected Coulomb breakup. The findings enable future calculations of breakup probabilities for all $\alpha$-particle breakup channels, which the authors plan to perform for energies above 40 MeV/A.
