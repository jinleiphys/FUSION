# Jets propagation through a hadron-string medium

> Di-jet correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied within the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach taking into account the reaction of the medium on the jet energy loss nonperturbatively. A comparison with the STAR and PHENIX data in central Au+Au collisions at the RHIC energy $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV is performed differentially, i.e. with respect to correlations in azimuthal angle $Δϕ$ and pseudorapidity $Δη$. The HSD results do not show enough suppression for the `away-side' jets in accordance with earlier perturbative studies. Furthermore, the `Mach-cone' structure for the angle distribution in the `away-side' jet as well as `ridge' long rapidity correlations in the `near-side' jet - observed by the STAR and PHOBOS Collaborations - are not seen in the HSD results, thus suggesting a partonic origin.

## Key claim

The Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model, even when including non-perturbative medium response to jet energy loss, does not reproduce the experimentally observed "Mach-cone" structure for away-side jets or the "ridge" long-range pseudorapidity correlations for near-side jets in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, suggesting that these phenomena require explicit partonic degrees of freedom beyond the hadron-string description.

## Method

The authors used the HSD transport approach, which describes inelastic hadron-hadron collisions via the Fritiof model (including Pythia v5.5 with Jetset v7.3 for jet production and fragmentation), to simulate di-jet correlations in p+p and central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. They calculated the associated particle distribution C(Delta-eta, Delta-phi) as a function of azimuthal angle and pseudorapidity between trigger and associated particles, using mixed events to subtract the background, and compared results to STAR and PHENIX data for various transverse momentum cuts.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor R_AA from HSD: 0.35 to 0.4 at p_T > 4 GeV/c, compared to experimental R_AA of 0.2 to 0.25
- Approximately 30 million p+p inelastic collision events and 0.5 million central Au+Au collisions with impact parameter b=0 were used in the simulations
- For STAR cuts (4 < p_T^trig < 6 GeV/c, 2 < p_T^assoc < 4 GeV/c): on average 0.5 trigger particles per event in Au+Au
- For PHOBOS cuts (p_T^trig > 2.5 GeV/c, p_T^assoc > 0.02 GeV/c): on average 5 trigger particles per event in Au+Au

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative HSD studies of high-p_T spectra (Konchakovski et al., 2008) by incorporating for the first time the full non-perturbative medium response to jet energy loss. The results contradict experimental observations from the STAR and PHOBOS Collaborations of Mach-cone and ridge structures, and support the need for explicit partonic degrees of freedom as implemented in the recently proposed parton-HSD (PHSD) model. The findings enable future investigations into whether the PHSD approach can reproduce the observed jet correlation patterns.
