# Fluid property of QGP in presence of magnetic field

> Fluidity of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is studied where interaction between quark and gluon is mapped through fugacity in particle distribution function using lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) results.

## Key claim
The paper maps QCD interactions in the presence of an external magnetic field using a temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent fugacity parameter Z(T,B) extracted from lattice QCD entropy density data, and finds that both magnetic field and QCD interaction push the quark-gluon plasma toward a nearly perfect fluid, as quantified by the KSS bound of 1/(4π).

## Method
The authors first parameterize the QCD interaction by fitting lattice QCD data for entropy density s(T) at different magnetic field strengths (eB = 0, 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4 GeV²) using a temperature-dependent fugacity Z(T) inserted into Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distribution functions. They then compute the normal (η₂) and Hall (η₄) shear viscosity components from standard kinetic theory relations that include a magnetic-field-dependent time scale τ_B = ω/(eB) alongside the collisional relaxation time τ_c, and evaluate the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s.

## Key numbers
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for entropy density is s ≈ 20.8 T³.
- Lattice QCD data for entropy density s(T) are fitted at magnetic field strengths eB = 0, 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, and 0.4 GeV².
- Quark masses used in the calculation: m_u = 0.005 GeV, m_d = 0.005 GeV, m_s = 0.100 GeV; gluon mass m_g = 0 GeV.
- The KSS bound for η/s is 1/(4π), drawn as a reference line in the results.

## Context
This work extends the fugacity-based quasi-particle mapping approach of Chandra and Ravisankar (2007), originally applied to quark-hadron phase transitions, to the case of QGP in a magnetic field using lattice QCD data from Bali et al. The study builds on prior calculations of anisotropic shear viscosity components by Dey et al. and shows that both the magnetic field and the QCD interaction (via reduced fugacity) lower η/s, pushing the system closer to the perfect fluid limit. The results enable further investigation of magnetohydrodynamic properties of QGP in heavy-ion collisions.
