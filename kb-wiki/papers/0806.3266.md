# Probing the Reheating Temperature at Colliders and with Primordial Nucleosynthesis

> Considering gravitino dark matter scenarios with a long-lived charged slepton, we show that collider measurements of the slepton mass and its lifetime can probe not only the gravitino mass but also the post-inflationary reheating temperature TR. In a model independent way, we derive upper limits on TR and discuss them in light of the constraints from the primordial catalysis of lithium-6 through bound-state effects. In the collider-friendly region of slepton masses below 1 TeV, the obtained conservative estimate of the maximum reheating temperature is about TR=3\times 10^9 GeV for the limiting case of a small gluino-slepton mass splitting and about TR=10^8 GeV for the case that is typical for universal soft supersymmetry breaking parameters at the scale of grand unification. We find that a determination of the gluino-slepton mass ratio at the Large Hadron Collider will test the possibility of TR>10^9 GeV and thereby the viability of thermal leptogenesis with hierarchical heavy right-handed Majorana neutrinos.

## Key claim

Collider measurements of a long-lived charged slepton's mass and lifetime can probe not only the gravitino mass but also the post-inflationary reheating temperature TR, with the derived upper limits on TR depending on the gluino-slepton mass ratio and subject to constraints from catalyzed big bang nucleosynthesis of lithium-6.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the gravitino mass upper limit from the slepton lifetime and mass using supergravity predictions, then combines this with the thermally produced gravitino density from SUSY QCD contributions to obtain a conservative upper limit on the reheating temperature TR. The analysis uses the gauge-invariant calculation of thermal gravitino production from Bolz (2000) and Pradler (2006), and applies the dark matter density constraint to bound TR. The CBBN constraint from lithium-6 overproduction via bound-state effects with helium-4, as identified by Pospelov (2006), is translated into lifetime limits that feed into the TR bounds.

## Key numbers

- Maximum reheating temperature TR = 3 × 10^9 GeV for the limiting case of a small gluino-slepton mass splitting (c = 1) with slepton masses below 1 TeV
- Maximum reheating temperature TR = 10^8 GeV for the case typical of universal soft supersymmetry breaking parameters at the grand unification scale (c = 7)
- The slepton lifetime bound from CBBN of lithium-6 is τ_slepton ≲ 5 × 10^3 seconds
- The gravitino mass upper limit is mgravitino_max = 0.41 GeV × (τ_slepton / 10^4 s)^(1/2) × (m_slepton / 100 GeV)^(5/2)
- The conservative TR limit expression: TR_max = (2.37 × 10^9 GeV / c^2) × (Ω_DM h^2 / 0.1) × (τ_slepton / 10^4 s)^(1/2) × (m_slepton / 100 GeV)^(1/2)
- The reduced Planck mass MPl = 2.4 × 10^18 GeV is used throughout

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gravitino dark matter scenarios with a charged slepton NLSP beyond constrained frameworks such as the CMSSM studied by Pradler (2006, 2007) and Kersten (2007), providing model-independent results valid for gauge-mediated SUSY breaking and non-standard mass spectra. The paper builds on the CBBN lithium-6 constraint from Pospelov (2006) and the thermal gravitino production calculations of Bolz (2000) and Pradler (2006). The results enable testing the viability of thermal leptogenesis with hierarchical heavy right-handed Majorana neutrinos, which requires TR > 10^9 GeV, through LHC measurements of the gluino-slepton mass ratio.
