# Radiative heavy quark energy loss in a dynamical QCD medium

> The computation of radiative energy loss in a dynamically screened QCD medium is a key ingredient for obtaining reliable predictions for jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We calculate, to first order in the opacity, the energy loss suffered by a heavy quark traveling through an infinite and time-independent QCD medium and show that the result for a dynamical medium is almost twice that obtained previously for a medium consisting of randomly distributed static scattering centers. A quantitative description of jet suppression in RHIC and LHC experiments thus must correctly account for the dynamics of the medium's constituents.

## Key claim

The radiative energy loss of a heavy quark in a dynamical QCD medium is almost twice that obtained for a medium consisting of randomly distributed static scattering centers, but this result is computed to first order in the opacity for an infinite and time-independent medium, neglecting Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) interference effects.

## Method

The authors computed the medium-induced radiative energy loss for a heavy quark to first order in the opacity (single gluon emission accompanied by one collisional interaction) using Hard-Thermal Loop (HTL) resummed gluon propagators for a dynamical QCD medium in thermal equilibrium. They compared this with the corresponding calculation for a static medium modeled by Gyulassy-Wang color-screened Yukawa potentials, performing analytical integrations over transverse momenta of the exchanged and emitted gluons under the soft gluon and soft rescattering approximations.

## Key numbers

- For a dynamical QCD medium, the radiative energy loss is almost 70% higher than for a static medium, for light, charm, and bottom quarks at RHIC conditions (T=225 MeV, n_f=2.5, alpha_s=0.3, L=5 fm).
- The dynamical mean free path is defined as lambda_dyn^{-1} = 3 alpha_s T, while the static mean free path is lambda_stat = lambda_dyn / c(n_f) with c(2.5) approximately 0.84, giving an approximately 20% increase in energy loss from the shorter mean free path.
- The remaining approximately 50% increase arises from the replacement of the static gluon exchange kernel mu^2/(bq^2+mu^2)^2 with the dynamical kernel mu^2/(bq^2(bq^2+mu^2)).
- The enhancement of the emitted gluon spectrum in the dynamical medium exceeds a factor of 2.5 at low transverse momentum |bk| less than about 1 GeV, and settles to an approximately factor of 1.3 enhancement at high |bk| greater than about 1 GeV.
- The charm quark mass is taken as M=1.2 GeV, the bottom quark mass as M=4.75 GeV, and the Debye screening mass mu is approximately 0.5 GeV for the RHIC conditions.

## Context

This work extends the static scattering center approximation of Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (GLV) and the Gyulassy-Wang model by incorporating dynamical medium constituents, showing that the static approximation underestimates radiative energy loss by nearly a factor of two. The results enable more reliable predictions for jet quenching in RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that finite medium size corrections (the LPM effect) and inclusion of collisional energy loss (as studied by Mustafa, Dutt-Mazumder, and others) are needed for a complete phenomenological description.
