# Hyperons in Two Flavor Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We use two-flavor chiral perturbation theory to describe hyperons. We focus on the strangeness conserving sector, and, as an example, calculate hyperon masses. Convergence of this two-flavor chiral expansion for observables is improved over the three-flavor theory. The cost, however, is a larger number of low-energy constants that must be ultimately determined from lattice QCD data. A formula for the mass of the omega baryon is derived to sixth order in this expansion, and will aid lattice practitioners in scale setting or tuning the strange quark mass.

## Key claim

Two-flavor chiral perturbation theory provides a better-converging expansion for hyperon observables than the three-flavor theory, but at the cost of introducing a larger number of low-energy constants that must be determined from lattice QCD data; the improved convergence is demonstrated explicitly for the omega baryon mass, which can be computed to sixth order in the pion mass expansion.

## Method

The authors construct an SU(2) chiral Lagrangian for hyperons with strangeness S=1 (Lambda, Sigma, Sigma*), S=2 (Xi, Xi*), and S=3 (Omega), treating baryons as heavy fields and removing their chiral-limit masses via field redefinitions. They calculate hyperon masses to fourth order in the chiral expansion for the S=1 and S=2 baryons, and to sixth order for the Omega baryon, using one-loop and two-loop diagrams with axial couplings and mass spurion insertions. The SU(2) Lagrangian is matched onto the SU(3) heavy baryon Lagrangian at tree level to relate the low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter for two-flavor chiral perturbation theory is epsilon ~ 0.1, compared to epsilon ~ 0.5 for three-flavor theory.
- The physical mass splitting between Lambda and Sigma is Delta_{LS} ~ 77 MeV; between Lambda and Sigma* is Delta_{LS*} ~ 270 MeV; between Xi and Xi* is Delta_X ~ 215 MeV.
- The Gell-Mann–Okubo relation gives Delta_GMO = 10.3(2) MeV, and normalized by the centroid octet baryon mass gives delta_GMO = 0.0090(2).
- The lattice-extrapolated axial charge relation delta_{g_piBB} = g_{piNN} - g_{piSS} - g_{piXiXi} = 0.23 at the physical point.
- Experimental axial charges used: g_{piNN} = 1.27, g_{piLS} = 1.47; lattice-extrapolated values: g_{piSS} = 0.78, g_{piXiXi} = 0.24.
- Axial transition couplings from decay widths: g_{piDN} = 1.48, g_{piXi*Xi} = 0.69, g_{piLS*} = -0.91, g_{piS*S} = 0.76.
- SU(3) violations in decuplet axial couplings are on the order of 5-10% from ratios such as (g_{piDN} - sqrt(3) g_{piXi*Xi}) / (g_{piDN} + sqrt(3) g_{piXi*Xi}) = 0.11.
- The O(e^3) correction to the Xi mass is less than or equal to 5% for pion masses up to m_pi ~ 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) treatment of strange baryons previously applied to kaon-pion scattering (Roessl, 1999) and nucleon strange matrix elements (Chen, 2002), and to spin-1/2 hyperons at tree level (Beane, 2003), by including both spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 strange baryons with full loop calculations. The SU(2) approach is motivated by lattice QCD studies (Lin, 2007; Walker-Loud, 2008) that found SU(3) chiral perturbation theory insufficient for extrapolating hyperon results to the physical point. The derived Omega mass formula to sixth order enables lattice practitioners to use the Omega baryon for scale setting or tuning the strange quark mass with controlled chiral extrapolations.
