# The early life of millisecond magnetars

> Some neutron stars may be born spinning fast and with strong magnetic fields---the so-called \emph{millisecond magnetars}. It is important to understand how a star's magnetic axis moves with respect to the spin axis in the star's early life, as this effects both electromagnetic and gravitational wave emission. Previous studies have highlighted the importance of viscous dissipation within the star in this process. We advance this program by additionally considering the effect of the electromagnetic torque. We find an interesting interplay between the viscous dissipation, which makes the magnetic axis orthogonalise with respect to the spin, verses magnetic torques that tend to make the magnetic axis align with the spin axis. We present some results, and highlight areas where our model needs to be made more realistic.

## Key claim

The paper finds an interplay between viscous dissipation, which tends to make the magnetic axis orthogonalise with respect to the spin axis, and electromagnetic torques, which tend to make the magnetic axis align with the spin axis; the outcome depends on the star's birth spin frequency and magnetic field strength, but the model is highly idealised and the authors caution that firm conclusions require addressing several outstanding issues.

## Method

The authors model a millisecond magnetar as a spherical background star with superimposed perturbations from a toroidal magnetic field and rotation, using a two-parameter perturbative scheme in the rotational deformation and magnetic deformation. They solve the coupled Euler, continuity, induction, and Poisson equations to compute the non-rigid displacement field (the "xi-motions") that damp precessional motion, then derive evolution equations for the wobble angle and spin rate including bulk viscosity from modified Urca reactions and a vacuum dipole electromagnetic torque.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic deformation ellipticity scales as epsilon_B ~ 1.9 x 10^-6 B_15^2 R_6^4 / M_1.4^2, where B_15 is the magnetic field in units of 10^15 G, R_6 is the radius in units of 10^6 cm, and M_1.4 is the mass in units of 1.4 solar masses.
- The rotational deformation scales as epsilon_alpha ~ 0.21 R_6^3 f_kHz^2 / M_1.4, where f_kHz is the spin frequency in kHz.
- For the toroidal magnetic field used, the ellipticity is epsilon_B = -0.019 Lambda^2 / G, where Lambda is a coefficient determined by the magnetic field configuration.
- The bulk viscosity relaxation timescale from modified Urca reactions is tau ~ 0.2 / T_9^6 (rho / rho_nuc)^(2/3) yr, where T_9 is temperature in units of 10^9 K.
- The critical spin frequency for orthogonalisation is given by f^crit_kHz ~ 2.8 T_10^6 / [4.1 x 10^3 tilde_lambda(chi) T_10^6 - 0.155 B_15^4 cos^2 chi]^(1/2), where T_10 is temperature in units of 10^10 K.
- Below a magnetic field strength of about 10^14 G, all stars orthogonalise regardless of birth spin frequency; above 10^14 G, there is a field-dependent spin frequency threshold below which stars align and above which they orthogonalise.

## Context

This work extends the program of Lander and Jones (2017, 2018) on non-rigid precession of magnetic stars by additionally incorporating the effect of electromagnetic torques on the evolution of the inclination angle. The results enable future comparisons with the observed distribution of inclination angles in the Galactic magnetar population and inform models of millisecond magnetars as possible gravitational wave sources (Dall'Osso et al., 2009; Cutler, 2002) or as engines for gamma-ray bursts (Metzger et al., 2011). The authors note that the model needs to be made more realistic by including magnetospheric spin-down torques, buoyancy effects, neutrino opacity, and more detailed temperature evolution.
