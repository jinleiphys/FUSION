# Psi to N Nbar m

> In this invited talk I discuss two recent applications of charmonium (Psi) decays to N Nbar m final states, where N is a nucleon and m is a light meson. There are several motivations for studying these decays: 1) They are useful for the study of N* spectroscopy; 2) they can be used to estimate cross sections for the associated charmonium production processes p pbar to Psi m, which PANDA plans to exploit in searches for charmonium hybrid exotics; and 3) they may allow the direct experimental measurement of NNm (meson-nucleon) strong couplings, which provide crucial input information for meson exchange models of the NN force. The latter two topics are considered in this talk, which will also compare results from a simple hadron pole model of these decays to recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The crossing relation between charmonium decays \(\Psi \to N \bar N m\) and the PANDA reaction \(p \bar p \to \Psi m\) can provide useful cross section estimates, but the simple constant-amplitude formula overpredicts the only measured case (\(p \bar p \to J/\psi \pi^0\)) by a factor of 2–3, and a more detailed hadron pole model suggests that C=(+) charmonia like \(\eta_c\) and \(\chi_{c0}\) may have much larger associated production cross sections than the C=(-) \(J/\psi\).

## Method

The author applies a crossing relation between the three-body charmonium decay \(\Psi \to p \bar p m\) and the two-body annihilation \(p \bar p \to \Psi m\), using a constant-amplitude approximation to convert measured or calculated partial widths into cross sections. A hadron pole model with effective hadron Lagrangians, assuming dominance by sequential Feynman diagrams, is then used to compute both Dalitz plot densities for \(\Psi \to p \bar p m\) and differential cross sections for \(p \bar p \to \Psi m\). The \(NNm\) coupling constants are extracted from the ratio \(\Gamma(\Psi \to p \bar p m) / \Gamma(\Psi \to p \bar p)\), which cancels the unknown \(\Psi NN\) coupling.

## Key numbers

- The constant-amplitude formula for \(p \bar p \to J/\psi \pi^0\) gives results about a factor of 2–3 larger than the two E760 data points (Armstrong, 1992).
- The predicted cross sections for \(\chi_{c0}\) and \(\eta_c\) production are much larger than the 0.1 nb scale of the only experimentally studied case, \(p \bar p \to J/\psi \pi^0\).
- The estimate of the pion-nucleon coupling from \(J/\psi \to p \bar p \pi^0\) is \(g_{NN\pi} = 13.3 \pm 0.6\), consistent with the standard value of about 13.
- The same calculation using \(\psi'\) decays gives a somewhat smaller estimate of \(g_{NN\pi} = 9.9 \pm 0.7\).

## Context

This work extends the crossing-based approach of Lundborg (2005) and the hadron pole model of Gaillard (1982) to estimate PANDA cross sections for associated charmonium production and to extract meson-nucleon couplings. It contradicts the simple sequential decay picture for some channels, as CLEO data (Onyisi, 2010) show that \(\chi_{c0} \to p \bar p \pi^0\) is dominated by a low \(p \bar p\) invariant mass enhancement rather than the pole diagrams. The results enable PANDA to prioritize searches for C=(+) charmonium hybrids and suggest that \(J/\psi \to p \bar p \omega\) may allow measurement of the \(NN\omega\) couplings \(g_\omega\) and \(\kappa_\omega\), which are crucial for meson-exchange models of the NN force.
