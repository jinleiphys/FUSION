# The Lipkin Model in the su(M+1)-Algebra for Many-Fermion System and its Counterpart in the Schwinger Boson Representation

> Following the Schwinger boson representation for the su(M+1)- and the su(N,1)-algebra presented by two of the present authors (J. da P. and M. Y.) and Kuriyama, a possible counterpart of the Lipkin model in the su(M+1)-algebra formulated in the fermion space is presented. The free vacuum, which plays a fundamental role in the conventional treatment of the Lipkin model, is generalized in a quite natural way, and further, the excited state generating operators such as the particle-hole pairs are also given in a natural scheme. As concrete examples, the cases of the su(2)-, su(3)- and the su(4)-algebra are discussed. Especially, the case of the su(4)-algebra is investigated in detail in relation to the nucleon pairing correlations and the high temperature superconductivity.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a boson-space counterpart of the Lipkin model in the su(M+1)-algebra using the Schwinger boson representation, which allows the treatment of intrinsic states more general than the conventional free vacuum, but the connection to the fermion-space model requires introducing the quantity (Ω−n) as an external parameter because the boson representation lacks an independent total-fermion-number quantum number.

## Method

The authors first recapitulate the Schwinger boson representation for the su(M+1)-algebra and its associated su(N,1)-algebra, then restrict to the case N = M−1 to obtain intrinsic states specified by exactly M quantum numbers (the eigenvalues σ_i of the diagonal operators Ŝ_i^i). They construct the intrinsic state |m⟩ as a product of powers of determinant-type boson creation operators b̂_k(M)* acting on the boson vacuum, and show that this state satisfies the conditions Ŝ_i|m⟩ = 0, Ŝ_i^j|m⟩ = 0 (j>i), T̂_p|m⟩ = 0, and T̂_q^p|m⟩ = 0 (q>p). The eigenvalues σ_i are given by sums of the occupation numbers m_k, and the fermion-number-related quantities n_i and total fermion number 𝒩 are then defined using the external parameter (Ω−n).

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic state |m⟩ = ∏_{k=0}^{M−1} (b̂_k(M)*)^{m_k} |0⟩ is specified by M quantum numbers m_0, m_1, …, m_{M−1}.
- The eigenvalues of Ŝ_i^i are σ_i = ∑_{k=0}^{i−1} m_k (i = 1, …, M).
- The eigenvalues of T̂_p^p are τ_p = ∑_{k=0}^{M−p} m_k + (M+1) (p = 1, …, M−1).
- The fermion occupation numbers are n_i = Ω − n − σ_i = Ω − n − ∑_{k=0}^{i−1} m_k.
- The total fermion number is 𝒩 = (M+1)(Ω−n) − ∑_{k=0}^{M−1} (M−k) m_k.
- For the free-vacuum case with n=0, m_l = Ω, and m_k = 0 (k≠l), the state reduces to |m⟩ = (b̂_l(M)*)^Ω |0⟩, corresponding to full occupation of levels i=0,…,l.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Schwinger boson representation for su(M+1)- and su(N,1)-algebras developed by da Providencia, Yamamura, and Kuriyama (2002) and applies it to the Lipkin model, which was originally formulated for su(2) by Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick (1965) and later generalized to su(3) and su(4). The paper enables the description of intrinsic states beyond the simple free vacuum, which is necessary for studying finite-temperature effects and for treating cases such as the su(4)-algebra model relevant to nucleon pairing correlations and high-temperature superconductivity, where the conventional free vacuum is insufficient.
