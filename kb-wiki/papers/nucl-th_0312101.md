# Breakup of 8B and the S17 astrophysical factor revisited

> Existing experimental data for the breakup of 8B at energies from 30 to 1000 MeV/nucleon on light through heavy targets are analyzed in detail in terms of an extended Glauber model. The predictions of the model are in excellent agreement with independent reaction data (reaction cross sections and parallel momentum distributions for core like fragments). Final state interactions have been included in the Coulomb dissociation component. We extract asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) from which the strophysical factor S17(0) for the key reaction for solar neutrino production, 7Be(p,gamma)8B, can be evaluated. Glauber model calculations using different effective interactions give consistent, though slightly different results. The differences give a measure of the precision one can expect from the method. The unweighted average of all ANCs extracted leads to S17(0)=18.7+/-1.9 eVb. The results of this new analysis are compared with the earlier one. They are consistent with the values from most direct measurements and other indirect methods.

## Key claim

The unweighted average of all asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) extracted from breakup data using three different nucleon-nucleon effective interactions yields S17(0) = 18.7 ± 1.9 eV·b, but the individual averages from each interaction differ by up to 13%, indicating that the precision of the indirect method is limited not only by experimental uncertainties but also by the accuracy of the reaction calculations.

## Method

Existing experimental data for the breakup of 8B at energies from 30 to 1000 MeV/nucleon on C, Al, Si, Sn, and Pb targets were analyzed using an extended Glauber model that includes final-state interactions in the Coulomb dissociation component. The one-proton removal cross sections were calculated as an incoherent superposition of single-particle contributions from stripping, diffraction dissociation, and Coulomb dissociation, using three different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions: the Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux (JLM) interaction, a "standard" finite-range interaction, and the Ray interaction. Asymptotic normalization coefficients were extracted by comparing the calculated and measured cross sections after subtracting the contribution from 7Be core excitation.

## Key numbers

- Unweighted average of all ANCs extracted leads to S17(0) = 18.7 ± 1.9 eV·b for the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction.
- ANC from JLM interaction: C_tot^2 = 0.454 ± 0.048 fm^{-1}, giving S17(0) = 17.5 ± 1.8 eV·b.
- ANC from "standard" interaction: C_tot^2 = 0.503 ± 0.032 fm^{-1}.
- ANC from "Ray" interaction: C_tot^2 = 0.517 ± 0.041 fm^{-1}.
- Ratio of core-excitation contribution to total ANC: C_e^2/C_tot^2 = 0.16(4), determined from σ(exc.) = 12(3) mb and σ(tot) = 94(9) mb at 936 MeV/nucleon.
- Ratio of p1/2 to p3/2 contributions in the mirror nucleus 8Li: A_p1/2^2 / A_p3/2^2 = 0.13(2).
- For breakup on C at 76 MeV/nucleon: calculated σ_str = 80 mb, σ_diff+C = 50 mb; experimental σ_str = 93(16) mb, σ_diff+C = 37(13) mb.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Trache et al. (2001) by including newer experimental data from Enders et al. and Warner et al., incorporating final-state interactions in the Coulomb dissociation component, and testing three different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions to assess the systematic uncertainty of the method. The extracted ANC values agree well with those from peripheral proton transfer reactions (Azhari et al.) and from mirror neutron transfer reactions (Trache et al. 2003). The final S17(0) value is consistent with the most probable values from statistical analyses by Schumann et al. (18.6 ± 1.2 ± 1.0 eV·b) and Davids et al. (18.6 ± 0.4 ± 1.1 eV·b), but disagrees with the larger value claimed by Junghans et al. The results demonstrate that breakup reactions can determine ANCs to approximately ±10% accuracy, enabling indirect determinations of astrophysical S-factors for radiative proton capture reactions relevant to solar neutrino production.
