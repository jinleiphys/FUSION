# Neutrino-induced pion production at low energies and in the small $Q^2$ region

> We analyse neutrino-induced reactions in the small $Q^2$ region and for energies covering the production and decay of the delta resonance. One of our results is the agreement with the MiniBooNE data for $1π^+$ and $1π^0$ final states. In addition we present differential cross sections for charged and neutral currents and for proton and neutron targets. Finally, we present cross sections induced by muon and electron type neutrinos, where effects of the lepton masses are visible.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their PCAC-based method, which uses experimental pion-nucleon cross sections as input, reproduces the MiniBooNE data for both \(1\pi^+\) and \(1\pi^0\) final states after including nuclear corrections, but the agreement for the \(\pi^0\) yield is less precise than for the \(\pi^+\) yield, with the theoretical curves lying a little lower than the data.

## Method

The authors compute neutrino-induced single-pion production cross sections in the small \(Q^2\) region (below approximately 0.4 GeV\(^2\)) and for neutrino energies covering the delta resonance (0.5–2.0 GeV). The axial current contribution is obtained via the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) relation, which connects the neutrino cross section to measured \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi N\) scattering data; the vector and vector-axial interference contributions are computed using the formalism of Lalakulich et al. (2005) with vector form factors from Paschos et al. (2011). For comparison with the MiniBooNE experiment on a CH\(_2\) target, the authors apply a nuclear transport model based on Adler (1974) and Paschos (2004) that includes an effective absorption factor \(A(Q^2)\) and a charge-exchange matrix for pion rescattering in carbon.

## Key numbers

- The dominant axial form factor for the nucleon-\(\Delta\) transition is taken as \(C_5^A(0) = 1.10 \pm 0.08\), from simultaneous fits to ANL and BNL data.
- The differential cross section for \(\nu_\mu p \rightarrow \mu^- p \pi^+\) shows a peak in the axial contribution at \(Q^2 \approx 0.03\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the carbon target, the effective absorption factor is \(A(Q^2 = 0.05\ \text{GeV}^2) = 0.71\), \(A(Q^2 = 0.20\ \text{GeV}^2) = 0.79\), and \(A(Q^2 = 0.40\ \text{GeV}^2) = 0.81\).
- The \(\pi^0\) channel receives approximately a 45% contribution from charge exchange (feeding from the \(\pi^+\) channel).
- The analysis covers neutrino energies in the range \(0.50\ \text{GeV} \leq E_\nu \leq 2.00\ \text{GeV}\), averaged over the MiniBooNE flux.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier PCAC-based calculations (Paschos et al., 2011) to a wider set of charged-current and neutral-current reactions on proton and neutron targets, and applies them to the MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arevalo et al., 2010). The approach contrasts with the GiBUU transport code (Lalakulich et al., 2011, 2012; Mosel, 2012), which the authors note has difficulties reproducing the MiniBooNE data, lying far below the experimental results. The method enables reliable cross-section estimates in the low-\(Q^2\) region that can serve as a benchmark for parameterizing form factors at larger \(Q^2\) and for testing isospin relations in new experiments such as MINERvA.
