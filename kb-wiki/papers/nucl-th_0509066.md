# Local demands on sterile neutrinos

> In a model independent manner, we explore the local implications of a single neutrino oscillation measurement which cannot be reconciled within a three-neutrino theory. We examine this inconsistency for a single region of baseline to neutrino energy $L/E$. Assuming that sterile neutrinos account for the anomaly, we find that the {\it local} demands of this datum can require the addition to the theory of one to three sterile neutrinos. We examine the constraints which can be used to determine when more than one neutrino would be required. The results apply only to a given region of $L/E$. The question of the adequacy of the sterile neutrinos to satisfy a global analysis is not addressed here. Finally, using the results of a 3+2 analysis, we indicate values for unknown mixing matrix elements which would require two sterile neutrinos due to local demands only.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a single anomalous neutrino oscillation measurement (one that cannot be explained by a three-neutrino theory) can locally require the addition of one to three sterile neutrinos, but only for a specific region of baseline-to-energy ratio (L/E). The authors explicitly state that the question of whether these sterile neutrinos can satisfy a global analysis of all data is not addressed.

## Method

The authors use a model-independent mathematical framework based on contraction-dilation theory: the non-unitary mixing matrix T that relates active flavors to mass eigenstates is treated as a contraction, and its defect rank determines the minimal number of sterile neutrinos needed locally. They derive a lower bound on the number N of required sterile neutrinos from the sum over active flavor deficits: N ≥ 3 − Σα cα, where cα is the total measured flux of active flavors from a source of flavor α. They then apply this bound to the LSND anomaly using best-fit parameters from a 3+2 neutrino analysis of short-baseline data.

## Key numbers

- The LSND experiment reports a ν̅μ → ν̅e oscillation probability of (0.264 ± 0.081)% at an average L/E around 1 m/MeV.
- Three-neutrino analyses predict Pν̅μ→ν̅e ≈ 10⁻⁷ at the LSND DAR baseline, six orders of magnitude below the measured value.
- Best-fit mass-squared differences from the 3+2 SBL analysis: Δ₄₁ = 0.92 eV² and Δ₅₁ = 22 eV².
- Best-fit mixing matrix elements: Ue4 = 0.121, Ue5 = 0.036, Uμ4 = 0.204, Uμ5 = 0.224.
- Oscillation probabilities at LSND baseline (1 m/MeV) from best-fit parameters: Pee = 0.95, Pμμ = 0.78, Peμ = 0.29%.
- For a specific example point Uτ4 = 0.6 and Uτ5 = −0.3: Uτ4² + Uτ5² = 0.45, 3 − Σα cα = 1.008, Pττ = 0.17, Pμτ = 0.032, Peτ = 0.015, and cτ = 0.22.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for sterile neutrino phenomenology beyond the standard 3+1 and 2+2 schemes, which have been found to be in tension with existing data (as reviewed by the authors). The paper builds on prior 3+2 neutrino analyses by Sorel et al. (2004) that showed improved global fits to short-baseline data including LSND. The key advance is providing a mathematical criterion (the defect rank and the sum-over-flavors inequality) to determine whether a single anomalous measurement locally requires more than one sterile neutrino, independent of global fits. This enables future experiments to characterize the "order" of an anomaly if all active flavor channels can be measured at a given L/E.
