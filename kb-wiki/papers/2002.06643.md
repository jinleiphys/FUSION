# Multi-stage evolution of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma

> The interaction of heavy flavor with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied using JETSCAPE, a publicly available software package containing a framework for Monte Carlo event generators. Multi-stage (and multi-model) evolution of heavy quarks within JETSCAPE provides a cohesive description of heavy flavor quenching inside the QGP. As the parton shower develops, a model becomes active as soon as its kinematic region of validity is reached. Two combinations of heavy-flavor energy-loss models are explored within a realistic QGP medium, using parameters which were tuned to describe {\it light-flavor} partonic energy-loss.

## Key claim

The JETSCAPE framework's multi-stage heavy-quark energy-loss model, using parameters tuned exclusively on light-flavor observables without any additional tuning, achieves good agreement with CMS data for the D0-meson nuclear modification factor in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but only when combining MATTER and LBT models; MATTER alone produces an RAA close to unity that does not match the data.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV in the 0-10% centrality class using TRENTo initial conditions, free-streaming, and (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamics calibrated via Bayesian model-to-data comparison. Hard partons from PYTHIA were propagated through the QGP using either MATTER (for high virtuality Q > Q_s) or LBT (for low virtuality Q ≤ Q_s), with the switching virtuality Q_s set to 2 GeV based on light-flavor tuning. Two combinations of energy-loss models were explored: MATTER alone down to Q_s=1 GeV, and the MATTER+LBT combination with switching at Q_s=2 GeV and Q_s=sqrt(2) GeV.

## Key numbers

- Switching virtuality Q_s = 2 GeV between MATTER and LBT, tuned using light-flavor observables and also used for heavy flavor evolution
- Effective strong coupling alpha_s = 0.25 used to determine the jet transport coefficient q-hat, tuned on light-flavor observables
- Charm quark production cross-section in p+p collisions: PYTHIA+MATTER with a K-factor of 1.5 shows better agreement with CMS data than PYTHIA alone
- D0-meson RAA: MATTER alone (down to Q_s=1 GeV) yields RAA closer to 1 than to CMS data; MATTER+LBT combination with Q_s=2 GeV gives results much closer to experimental data
- Reducing switching virtuality from Q_s=2 GeV to Q_s=sqrt(2) GeV produces only a small effect on the D0 RAA results

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework's multi-stage approach, previously applied to light-flavor partonic energy-loss (A-K_tp), to heavy flavor for the first time. It builds on prior heavy-quark studies using LBT alone (Cao 2018) and the Bayesian calibration of hydrodynamic parameters (Bernhard 2019). The results enable future calculations of D0-meson elliptic flow v2 and extension to bottom hadrons, as well as further refinement of the switching virtuality and model combinations for heavy quarks.
