# Origin of symmetry energy in finite nuclei and density dependence of nuclear matter symmetry energy from measured alpha-decay energies

> Based on the Skyrme energy density functional, the spatial distribution of the symmetry energy of a finite nucleus is derived in order to examine whether the symmetry energy of a finite nucleus originates from its interior or from its surface. It is found that the surface part of a heavy nucleus contributes dominantly to its symmetry energy compared to its inner part. The symmetry energy coefficient $a_{\text{sym}}({A})$ is then directly extracted and the ratio of the surface symmetry coefficient to the volume symmetry coefficient $κ$ is estimated. Meanwhile, with the help of experimental alpha decay energies, a macroscopic method is developed to determine the symmetry energy coefficient of heavy nuclei. The resultant $a_{\text{sym}}({A})$ is used to analyze the density dependence of the symmetry energy coefficient of nuclear matter around the saturation density, and furthermore, the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}\text{Pb}$ is deduced which is consistent with the pygmy dipole resonance analysis. In addition, it is shown that the ratio $κ$ obtained from the macroscopic method is in agreement with that from the Skyrme energy density functional. Thus the two completely different approaches may validate each other to achieve more compelling results.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy of a heavy nucleus originates dominantly from its surface region rather than its interior, and the ratio of surface-to-volume symmetry coefficients obtained from experimental alpha-decay energies agrees with that from the Skyrme energy density functional, validating both approaches.

## Method

The authors derived the spatial distribution of the symmetry energy density for finite nuclei using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach with the extended-Thomas-Fermi approximation for kinetic energy densities, after subtracting Coulomb polarization effects. Separately, they extracted the symmetry energy coefficient of heavy nuclei from experimental alpha-decay energies of 162 heavy and superheavy nuclei (Z >= 92, N >= 140) using a previously developed Q_alpha formula, fitting the ratio kappa of surface-to-volume symmetry coefficients with S_0 fixed at 31.6 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the symmetry energy coefficient a_sym(A) calculated with Skyrme interactions SIII, SLy4, SLy5, and SkM* ranges from 20.8 to 23.6 MeV.
- The ratio kappa of surface-to-volume symmetry coefficients from the Skyrme method for 208Pb is 1.8 to 2.6.
- From experimental alpha-decay energies, kappa = 2.21 (+0.56 / -0.55) with S_0 = 31.6 (+2.2 / -2.2) MeV.
- The slope parameter L = 61 (+22 / -22) MeV and curvature parameter K_sym = -109 (+71 / -70) MeV.
- The neutron skin thickness of 208Pb deduced from alpha-decay energies is Delta R_np = 0.191 +/- 0.032 fm.
- The gradient term contributes less than 2% and the second-order kinetic term contributes less than 3% to a_sym for 208Pb.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy in finite nuclei using liquid-drop models with nuclear masses (Liu et al.) and leptodermous expansions based on self-consistent mean-field theory (Centelles et al.). The authors introduce a novel macroscopic method using alpha-decay energies to extract symmetry energy coefficients, which avoids complications from shell effects and less important terms in mass formulas. The results enable tighter constraints on the density dependence of nuclear matter symmetry energy and neutron skin thickness, with the deduced neutron skin thickness for 208Pb consistent with pygmy dipole resonance analyses (PDR, 2010) and mean-field predictions (Dong et al., 2012).
