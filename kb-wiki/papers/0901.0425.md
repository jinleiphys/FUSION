# Extended Siegert Theorem in the Relativistic Investigation of the Deuteron Photodisintegration Reaction

> The contribution of the two-body exchange current is investigated for the reaction of the deuteron photodisintegration in the framework of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism and with using extended Siegert theorem. This theorem allow to express the reaction amplitude in terms of extended electric and magnetic dipole moments of the system. The resultant analytical expression is faultless with respect to both translation and gauge invariance. It permits to perform calculations of the deuteron photodisintegration cross section and polarization observables taking into account two-body exchange current implicitly.

# Extended Siegert Theorem in the Relativistic Investigation of the Deuteron Photodisintegration Reaction

## Key claim

The extended Siegert theorem allows the two-body exchange current contribution to the deuteron photodisintegration amplitude to be expressed in terms of extended electric and magnetic dipole moments, enabling calculations that implicitly include two-body currents without explicit meson-exchange current operators; however, the paper presents calculations that omit final state interactions, and the agreement with experimental data is demonstrated only for specific observables and energies.

## Method

The authors compute the deuteron photodisintegration cross section and polarization observables within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism using the extended Siegert theorem, which expresses the reaction amplitude through generalized electric and magnetic dipole matrix elements. Calculations are performed with the Graz II nucleon-nucleon potential in both nonrelativistic (Schrodinger equation) and relativistic (Bethe-Salpeter equation) frameworks, comparing results with and without the effective inclusion of two-body currents via the extended Siegert theorem, while final state interactions are not included.

## Key numbers

- At photon energy 20 MeV, the one-body approximation gives a differential cross section 30% less than experimental data; calculations using the extended Siegert theorem bring agreement with experiment.
- Relativistic effects give a larger contribution to two-body current than to one-body current, particularly visible for the tensor target asymmetry T22 at 200 MeV.
- For the tensor target asymmetry T20, large contributions from relativistic effects are seen even at photon energy equal to 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deuteron photodisintegration within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism using one-body approximation (KaShi, KaShu, BoBuKaShu), which were insufficient for complete description of experimental data over a wide energy range. The extended Siegert theorem approach (Siegert, extended) provides a method to incorporate meson exchange current contributions without explicit current operators, enabling improved calculations of differential cross sections and polarization observables such as tensor target asymmetries T20 and T22.
