# Viscosity and thermal conductivity effects at first-order phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions

> Effects of viscosity and thermal conductivity on the dynamics of first-order phase transitions are studied. The nuclear gas-liquid and hadron-quark transitions in heavy-ion collisions are considered. We demonstrate that at non-zero thermal conductivity, $κ\neq 0$, onset of spinodal instabilities occurs on an isothermal spinodal line, whereas for $κ=0$ instabilities take place at lower temperatures, on an adiabatic spinodal.

# Viscosity and thermal conductivity effects at first-order phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

For any non-zero thermal conductivity (κ ≠ 0), spinodal instabilities at first-order phase transitions begin when the system crosses the isothermal spinodal line, not the adiabatic spinodal line; this means instabilities occur at significantly higher temperatures than the κ = 0 case would predict, favoring observable signals of spinodal decomposition in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized non-relativistic non-ideal hydrodynamic equations (Navier-Stokes, continuity, and heat transport) for density and entropy fluctuations near first-order phase transitions, using a Van der Waals equation of state as a mean-field model. They derive the growth increment γ(p) for fluctuations and analyze the unstable modes in the spinodal region for both the nuclear gas-liquid (NGL) and hadron-quark gluon plasma (HQGP) transitions, comparing the cases κ ≠ 0 and κ = 0.

## Key numbers

- For the HQGP phase transition, the fog size (where density and thermal relaxation times equal) is R_fog ∼ 0.1–1 fm; for the NGL transition, R_fog ∼ 1–10 fm, which is less than or comparable to the fireball size at freeze-out.
- For the NGL phase transition, the inertia parameter β ∼ 0.01; for the HQGP transition, β ∼ 0.02–0.2 even for η/s ≃ 1/(4π).
- Numerical simulations at T/T_cr = 0.85, T_cr = 162 MeV, n/n_sat = 1.3 (n_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³) with η ≃ 45 MeV/fm² and β = 0.2 show undercritical seeds dissolve in ∼ several fm, while overcritical seeds grow very slowly.
- The characteristic time scale for density wave growth in the spinodal region at T/T_cr = 0.85 is t_ch ∼ 10 fm, comparable to the fireball expansion time; far from the critical point, t_ch ∼ 1.5 T_cr/|δT| fm.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical studies of first-order phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions previously performed by the same authors (SV1, SV2, SV3) and by Randrup and collaborators, emphasizing the role of non-ideal hydrodynamics. The key result, that non-zero thermal conductivity shifts the onset of spinodal instabilities from the adiabatic to the isothermal spinodal line, implies that the equilibrium critical temperature may be significantly higher than the value manifested in fluctuation growth in experiments. This enables predictions of specific fluctuation signatures with characteristic spatial size that could be observed at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA, potentially serving as a signal of the QCD first-order phase transition.
