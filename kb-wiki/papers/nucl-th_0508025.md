# Muonic Hydrogen and the Third Zemach Moment

> We determine the third Zemach moment of hydrogen (<r^3>_(2)) using only the world data on elastic electron-proton scattering. This moment dominates the O (Z alpha)^5 hadronic correction to the Lamb shift in muonic atoms. The resulting moment, <r^3 >_(2) = 2.71(13) fm^3, is somewhat larger than previously inferred values based on models. The contribution of that moment to the muonic hydrogen 2S level is -0.0247(12) meV.

## Key claim

The third Zemach moment of hydrogen, ⟨r³⟩₂ = 2.71(13) fm³, is determined from world electron-proton scattering data alone and is somewhat larger than previously inferred model-based values; this moment contributes -0.0247(12) meV to the muonic hydrogen 2S level, removing most of the uncertainty from the O(Zα)⁵ elastic finite-size term.

## Method

The authors analyzed the world data on elastic electron-proton scattering for momentum transfers q ≤ 4 fm⁻¹, parameterizing the electric and magnetic Sachs form factors G_E(q²) and G_M(q²) using a Continued Fraction expansion. The longitudinal/transverse separation was performed during a global fit of cross sections, including Coulomb distortion in second-order Born approximation, and systematic uncertainties were treated by shifting data sets by their quoted errors and adding resulting changes quadratically.

## Key numbers

- ⟨r³⟩₂ = 2.71(13) fm³, determined from world electron-proton scattering data with error bar including both random and systematic uncertainties (systematic dominating by far)
- Contribution to muonic hydrogen 2S level: -0.0247(12) meV
- For the "standard" dipole parameterization (rms charge radius 0.811 fm): ⟨r³⟩₂ = 2.02 fm³, giving energy shift -0.0185 meV
- When dipole model scaled to rms radius 0.895 fm: ⟨r³⟩₂ = 2.72 fm³, in good agreement with the data-derived value
- Two-photon exchange correction increases ⟨r³⟩₂ by only 0.02 fm³, a small fraction of the error bar

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive analysis of Sick (2003) of the world electron-proton scattering data, which separated charge from magnetic scattering and incorporated Coulomb corrections. The result addresses concerns raised by Borie (2005) that two simple charge-distribution models with the same charge radius produced differences of 0.002 meV in the 2S energy shift; the model-independent determination removes most of the uncertainty from the O(Zα)⁵ elastic finite-size contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, which is critical for the PSI experiment aiming to determine the proton charge radius to 1 part per thousand.
