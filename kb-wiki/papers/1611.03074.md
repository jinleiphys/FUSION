# Analytical On-shell Calculation of Higher Order Scattering: Massless Particles

> We demonstrate that the use of on-shell methods involving calculation of the discontinuity across the t-channel cut associated with the exchange of a pair of massless particles can be used to evaluate loop contributions to the electromagnetic and gravitational scattering of both massive and massless particles. In the gravitational case the use of factorization permits a straightforward and algebraic calculation of higher order scattering results, which were obtained previously by considerably more arduous Feynman diagram techniques.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that on-shell methods, specifically calculating the discontinuity across the t-channel cut from two-massless-particle exchange, can evaluate loop contributions to electromagnetic and gravitational scattering of both massive and massless particles, with the gravitational case simplified by factorization into products of electromagnetic amplitudes times a kinematic factor, avoiding the more arduous Feynman diagram techniques used previously.

## Method

The authors use the unitarity relation to compute the discontinuity of the scattering amplitude across the two-photon or two-graviton t-channel cut, expressed as a product of tree-level Compton annihilation and creation amplitudes. For electromagnetic scattering, they evaluate the angular integrals either directly or by relating them to discontinuities of scalar box, triangle, and bubble Feynman integrals. For gravitational scattering, they exploit the factorization property that gravitational Compton amplitudes equal a kinematic factor times the square of the corresponding electromagnetic Compton amplitudes, allowing algebraic calculation of the discontinuity.

## Key numbers

- For massive-mas sive electromagnetic scattering: the second-order effective potential is \(V_2^{em}(r) = -\frac{\alpha_{em}^2(m_A+m_B)}{2 m_A m_B r^2} - \frac{7\alpha_{em}^2\hbar}{6\pi m_A m_B r^3}\) (Equation labeled dz).
- For massive-massless electromagnetic scattering: the effective potential is \(V_2^{0-em}(r) = \frac{\alpha_{em}^2}{2 E r^2} + \frac{2\alpha_{em}^2\hbar}{\pi m_B E r^3} \ln\frac{r}{r_0}\), where \(E\) is the laboratory energy of the massless particle.
- The gravitational Compton amplitude factorizes as \(\text{Amp}_{C}^{grav}(S=0) = \frac{\kappa^2}{8 e^4} F (\text{Amp}_C(S=0))^2\) with \(F = \frac{p_i\cdot k_i p_i\cdot k_f}{k_i\cdot k_f}\).
- For massive-mas sive gravitational scattering, the discontinuity is given by Equation (labeled zx) involving angular integrals \(I_{00}, I_{11}, J^A_{00}, J^B_{00}\) and parameters \(\tau_A, \tau_B, y\).

## Context

This work extends the on-shell method previously applied to massive spinless particles (Holstein 2016) to the case where one scattered particle becomes massless, providing a simpler algebraic alternative to the Feynman diagram calculations of Iwasaki (1971), Feinberg and Sucher (1988), and Bjerrum-Bohr et al. (2014). The results enable calculation of quantum corrections to light-bending in the vicinity of a massive object, as recently considered by Bjerrum-Bohr et al. (2015), and the factorization property connects gravitational scattering to known electromagnetic results.
