# Strangeness Photoproduction on Quasifree Neutrons

> Strangeness photoproduction in the elementary reactions $γN\rightarrow KY$ offers the possibility to study nucleon resonances in the mass region above 1.7 GeV, where the number of states and their properties are still under debate. These reactions could allow the first 'complete' experiment in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction due to the easier access to recoil polarization observables in experiments. In addition to measurements on proton targets, a full isospin decomposition of the $I=1/2$ electromagnetic amplitudes requires also data obtained from quasifree neutron targets. This contribution shows first preliminary results on feasibility studies for such measurements at the A2 experiment at MAMI.

## Key claim

Strangeness photoproduction on quasifree neutrons using a deuterium target at the A2 experiment at MAMI is feasible for identifying the three isospin reactions γn→KY, but the results are very preliminary and require further refinements and corrections before cross sections or recoil polarization observables can be extracted.

## Method

The A2 experiment at MAMI used a 1.5 GeV electron beam to produce a bremsstrahlung photon beam impinging on a liquid deuterium target. The Crystal Ball calorimeter (672 NaI(Tl) crystals) and TAPS detector wall (366 BaF₂ and 72 PbWO₄ crystals) detected final-state particles from the reactions γn→K⁰Λ, γn→K⁰Σ⁰, and γn→K⁺Σ⁻, with K⁺ mesons identified via their in-crystal muonic decay and hyperons reconstructed from their decay products (Λ→pπ⁻, Σ⁰→γΛ, Σ⁻→nπ⁻). Event selection used kinematic fits, invariant mass cuts, and missing mass techniques to separate signal from background.

## Key numbers

- The K⁺ meson mean lifetime of 12.38 ns was used for in-crystal decay identification, with the muonic decay branch (Γᵢ/Γ = 63.55%) and pionic decay branch (Γᵢ/Γ = 20.66%) distinguished via decay cluster energy distributions.
- The K⁰ was identified via the K⁰_S→π⁰π⁰ decay with branching ratio Γᵢ/Γ = 30.69%.
- The Λ hyperon was detected via Λ→pπ⁻ decay with branching ratio Γᵢ/Γ = 63.9%.
- The Σ⁰→γΛ radiative decay (Γᵢ/Γ ≈ 100%) produced a clear peak at 76 MeV in the decay photon energy distribution in the Σ⁰ rest frame, consistent with the Σ⁰-Λ mass difference.
- Neutron detection efficiencies in the Crystal Ball are below 40%.
- The experimental trigger required total energy E_tot > 300 MeV in the Crystal Ball and more than 2 activated logical sectors.

## Context

This work extends the sparse experimental database for strangeness photoproduction on neutrons (reactions γn→K⁰Λ, γn→K⁰Σ⁰, γn→K⁺Σ⁻), which is needed for full isospin decomposition of I=1/2 electromagnetic amplitudes. Prior measurements from CLAS and other facilities (KPSM 2006, KPSM 2010, K0N 2008, K0N 2010, K0N 2012) have limited precision and coverage near threshold, where theoretical interpretation is more straightforward and Chiral Perturbation Theory including strangeness (CHPT 1997, CHPT 2007) can be tested. The results enable future extraction of cross sections and recoil polarization observables, which could help constrain u- and t-channel background terms in theoretical models (MART 2011, MART 2014) and study KN and YN potentials via final state interactions (KYD 2013).
