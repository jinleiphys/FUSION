# On the nuclear states of K^- mesons

> The KNNN bound states recently discovered at KEK are studied. It is shown that the Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1385) resonant states coupled to the KN system may generate an attraction strong enough to form such bound states.

# On the nuclear states of K^- mesons

## Key claim
The Λ(1405) and Σ(1385) resonant states coupled to the KN system can together generate sufficient attraction to form the strongly bound KNNN states observed at KEK, but only if the Λ(1405) and Σ(1385) in a nuclear medium are located above the KN threshold and the binding of K^- is strong enough to block the main πΣ and πΛ decay modes.

## Method
The authors model the KN S-wave scattering amplitude using a resonance formula for the Λ(1405) and a P-wave resonance formula for the Σ(1385), generating an optical potential for K^- in nuclear matter. They solve a variational problem for the kaonic energy level in the K^-pnn and K^-ppn systems using a square-well trial wave function, with self-consistent iteration between the kaon binding energy and the energy-dependent Σ(1385) potential. The coupling parameters for the Σ(1385) to the KN channel are taken from SU(3) relations and experimental data from K^- capture in deuterium.

## Key numbers
- The K^-pnn state found at KEK is bound by 195 MeV.
- For the K^-pnn system, the calculated binding energy is 169 MeV using the SU(3) value for the Σ(1385)-KN coupling (γ²_ΣKN / γ²_ΣπΛ = 2/3), and 192 MeV when this coupling is enhanced by 20%.
- For the K^-ppn system, the calculated binding energy is 155 MeV with the SU(3) coupling, and 175 MeV with the 20% enhanced coupling.
- The I=0 scattering amplitude at subthreshold energies is f₀ = 1.48 fm; the I=1 amplitude is f₁ = 0.34 fm.
- The S-wave potential well depth for K^- at the center of tritium is -110 MeV, insufficient alone to produce the experimental binding.
- The Σ(1385) resonance has an experimental width of 36 MeV, with decays to πΣ constituting only 13% of the total.
- The experimental ratio γ²_ΣKN / γ²_ΣπΛ = 0.51 ± 0.18 from K^- capture in deuterium is consistent with the SU(3) value of 2/3.
- The average nuclear potential for the Λ(1405) at nuclear centers is estimated at V_Λ ≈ 200 MeV; for the Σ(1385) in nuclear matter it is about -30 MeV depth, and in the K^-pnn system about -20 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the predictions of Wycech (1986), who showed that K^- mesons could be bound so strongly that decay channels would be blocked, and of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), who predicted narrow states in light nuclei such as ^4He. Both earlier predictions attributed the attraction to the Λ(1405) alone, but the KEK experiment showed that attraction from the Λ(1405) is not strong enough to generate the observed binding in the K^-pnn system, which has only one proton. The present paper introduces the Σ(1385) resonance as an additional, dominant source of attraction in the I=1 channel, enabling strong binding even in neutron-rich systems. This work enables further searches for K^-nn, K^-nnn, and other neutron-excess objects, and suggests a proximity to kaon condensation at higher densities.
