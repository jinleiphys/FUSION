# Negative Elliptic Flow of $J/ψ$'s: A Qualitative Signature for Charm Collectivity at RHIC

> We discuss one of the most prominent features of the very recent preliminary elliptic flow data of $J/Ψ$ meson from the PHENIX collaboration \cite{Silvestre:2008tw}. Even within the the rather large error bars of the measured data a negative elliptic flow parameter ($v_2$) for $J/Ψ$ in the range of $p_T=0.5-2.5 \GeV/c$ is visible. We argue that this negative elliptic flow at intermediate $p_T$ is a clear and qualitative signature for the collectivity of charm quarks produced in nucleus-nucleus reactions at RHIC. Within a parton recombination approach we show that a negative elliptic flow puts a lower limit on the collective transverse velocity of heavy quarks. The numerical value of the transverse flow velocity $β_T$ for charm quarks that is necessary to reproduce the data is $β_T(charm)\sim 0.55-0.6c$ and therefore compatible with the flow of light quarks.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the negative elliptic flow ($v_2$) observed in preliminary PHENIX data for $J/\psi$ mesons at intermediate transverse momentum ($p_T = 0.5-2.5$ GeV/c) is a qualitative signature that charm quarks participate in the collective expansion of the partonic system at RHIC, but the authors acknowledge that the data have large error bars and the conclusion requires verification with more precise measurements.

## Method

The authors employ a parton recombination approach combined with a blast-wave parametrization of the freeze-out hypersurface to model the quark emission function. For charm quarks, the same emission function is used as for light quarks, but with the transverse collective velocity as a free parameter. The elliptic flow of $J/\psi$ mesons is then calculated from the charm quark emission function using coalescence, where the hadron inherits its azimuthal asymmetry directly from its constituent quarks.

## Key numbers

- The transverse flow velocity for charm quarks needed to reproduce the PHENIX data is $\beta_T(charm) \sim 0.55-0.6c$, compatible with the flow of light quarks.
- The best fit to the data is obtained with a mean charm flow velocity of $\beta_T = 0.55c$, equal to the light quark flow velocity extracted from previous fits within the same model.
- The analysis places a lower bound on the charm quark transverse velocity of $\beta_T \geq 0.5c$ (stated as $\beta_T \geq 0.4c$ in the conclusion).
- The charm quark mass used in the calculation is $m_c = 1.5$ GeV/$c^2$; the bottom quark mass is $m_b = 4.7$ GeV/$c^2$.
- The negative elliptic flow for $J/\psi$ is observed in the $p_T$ range $0.5-2.5$ GeV/c; above $p_T \sim 2.5$ GeV, the elliptic flow values turn positive.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of elliptic flow and parton recombination at RHIC, particularly the constituent quark number scaling observed for light hadrons (Fries 2003, Krieg 2007). It contradicts previous assumptions from parton cascade simulations (Molnar 2001) that charm quarks could not achieve local equilibrium, and differs from approaches assuming incomplete thermalization of charm (Greco 2003, Yan 2006, Ravagli 2007, Linnyk 2008). The findings enable future tests with the heavy-flavor tracker for STAR and suggest that direct measurements of heavy meson elliptic flow will allow better analysis of charm collectivity.
