# Interaction of antiproton with nuclei

> We performed fully self-consistent calculations of $\bar{p}$-nuclear bound states within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. The G-parity motivated $\bar{p}$-meson coupling constants were adjusted to yield potentials consistent with $\bar{p}$-atom data. We confirmed large polarization effects of the nuclear core caused by the presence of the antiproton. The $\bar{p}$ absorption in the nucleus was incorporated by means of the imaginary part of a phenomenological optical potential. The phase space reduction for the $\bar{p}$ annihilation products was taken into account. The corresponding $\bar{p}$ width in the medium significantly decreases, however, it still remains considerable for the $\bar{p}$ potential consistent with experimental data.

## Key claim

Fully self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations confirm that the antiproton width in the nuclear medium is significantly reduced by phase space suppression, but remains considerable (around 180 MeV) for potentials consistent with antiproton-atom data, even when the additional downward energy shift from the momenta of the annihilating partners is included.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent calculations of antiproton-nuclear bound states within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, using the TM1 parametrization for heavy nuclei and TM2 for light nuclei. The antiproton-meson coupling constants were scaled from the nucleon values by a factor ξ (with ξ=0.2 for potentials consistent with antiproton-atom data), and the antiproton self-interaction was subtracted. The imaginary part of the optical potential was taken in a tρ form with parameter Im b0 = 1.9 fm, and the phase space suppression for annihilation products was evaluated using both an analytic two-body formula and the Monte Carlo simulation tool PLUTO for multi-body channels.

## Key numbers

- The real part of the antiproton-nucleus potential derived using G-parity transformation is approximately -650 MeV deep at normal nuclear density, while experiments with antiproton atoms favor a shallower real part in the range -(100 - 300) MeV in the nuclear interior
- The nuclear core density in an antiproton nucleus reaches 2-3 times the nuclear matter density, localized within r ≤ 1.5 fm
- Total binding energy increases from B = -344.3 MeV for 40Ca to B = -485.4 MeV for 40Ca+antiproton (ξ=0.2)
- For 16O+antiproton with ξ=0.2, the dynamical calculation gives antiproton single particle energy ε = -191.6 MeV and width Γ = 179.9 MeV when including phase space suppression with the momentum-dependent downward energy shift
- The antiproton width is reduced from 552.3 MeV (complex potential without suppression) to 179.9 MeV (with full phase space treatment), corresponding to a lifetime of approximately 1 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF calculations of antiproton-nuclear bound states by Mishustin and collaborators, and incorporates the phenomenological optical potential parameters fitted to antiproton-atom data by Friedman, Gal, and Mareš. The authors go beyond previous static calculations by including full self-consistency with nuclear core polarization, proper treatment of antiproton self-interaction, and a self-consistent evaluation of the center-of-mass energy √s that includes the momenta of the annihilating antiproton and nucleon. The results enable more realistic predictions for antiproton-nuclear bound states that could be studied at the FAIR facility.
