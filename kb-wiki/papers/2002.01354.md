# Fate of Light Scalar Mesons

> 10 pages, 14 figures, Invited talk at the International Conference "Problems of Theoretical and Mathematical Physics" dedicated to the 110th anniversary of the birth of the outstanding Russian scientist - mathematician and physicist Nikolai Nikolaevich Bogolyubov, JINR, Dubna, September 11 - 13, 2019. arXiv admin note: text overlap with arXiv:1807.00179

## Key claim

All predictions for the light scalar mesons based on their four-quark nature are supported by experiment, but the present statistics for semileptonic D decays are not adequate for conclusions about details of the πη production.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental evidence for the four-quark structure of the light scalar mesons a0(980), f0(980), and σ(600) (also called f0(500)) by analyzing their production in γγ collisions, φ(1020) radiative decays, ππ scattering, and semileptonic D and B decays. The author compares these data with predictions from the four-quark model and the conventional qq̄ model, using fits to Belle, KLOE, CLEO, and BESIII data, and also examines K0S K+ correlations in Pb-Pb interactions from ALICE.

## Key numbers

- Predicted two-photon widths: Γ_{a0γγ} ≈ Γ_{f0γγ} ≈ 0.27 keV (1982 prediction, confirmed by experiment)
- The qq̄ model prediction g²_{f0γγ} : g²_{a0γγ} = 25:9 is excluded by experiment, while the analogous ratio for tensor mesons g²_{f2γγ} : g²_{a2γγ} = 25:9 agrees with experiment
- The φ(1020) → K+K− → γ a0(980)/f0(980) decays require virtual kaon momenta greater than 2 GeV, while loose molecules with binding energy about 20 MeV would require momenta about 100 MeV
- The ππ scattering model describes data up to 1.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the four-quark model of light scalar mesons originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) and by Shuryak and Wambach, and the author's own predictions from 1982 for γγ decays. The paper contradicts the conventional qq̄ interpretation of a0(980) and f0(980) as P-wave quark-antiquark states, showing that the qq̄ model predictions for their two-photon couplings and their absence in J/ψ radiative decays are at variance with experiment. The results enable future studies of light scalar mesons in semileptonic D and B decays, with BESIII having recently performed first measurements of D0 → a0− e+ ν and D+ → a00 e+ ν decays, though higher statistics are needed.
