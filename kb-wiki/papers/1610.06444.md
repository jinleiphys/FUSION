# Theoretical approaches to low energy $\bar{K}N$ interactions

> We provide a direct comparison of modern theoretical approaches based on the SU(3) chiral dynamics and describing the low energy $\bar{K}N$ data. The model predictions for the $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes and pole content of the models are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper directly compares several modern chiral unitary approaches to low-energy \(\bar{K}N\) interactions, showing that while all models reproduce the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data equally well, they yield very different predictions for sub-threshold \(K^{-}p\) and \(K^{-}n\) amplitudes and disagree on the pole positions of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance, particularly on the imaginary part of the higher-energy pole and the location of the second pole.

## Method

The authors compare six NLO models from four theoretical approaches (Kyoto-Munich, Murcia, Bonn, and Prague), all based on SU(3) chiral dynamics with coupled-channel re-summation techniques. The models are fitted to \(K^{-}p\) scattering and reaction data including the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurement, and their predictions for scattering amplitudes, pole content, and pole origins in the zero-coupling limit (ZCL) are analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Kaonic hydrogen \(1s\) level shift \(\Delta E(1s)\) and width \(\Gamma(1s)\) from SIDDHARTA: the rectangular box covering one standard deviation of the experimental data is shown in Fig. 1 (left panel); all models reproduce these data well.
- Coupled-channel thresholds range from about 1250 to 1810 MeV for \(\pi\Lambda\), \(\pi\Sigma\), \(\bar{K}N\), \(\eta\Lambda\), \(\eta\Sigma\), and \(K\Xi\).
- The higher-energy \(\Lambda(1405)\) pole is generated at about 1420 MeV by all models, with varying imaginary parts.
- In the ZCL, the \(\Lambda_1(1405)\) pole is found in the \(\pi\Sigma\) channel for all six models; the \(\Lambda_2(1405)\) pole origin is \(\bar{K}N\) for \(\CSnlo\), \(\IHWnlo\), \(\GOi\), and \(\MMiv\), but \(\eta\Lambda\) for \(\GOii\) and \(\MMii\).
- The \(\Lambda(1670)\) pole originates from \(K\Xi\) in the ZCL for \(\CSnlo\), \(\GOi\), \(\GOii\), and \(\MMiv\); it is missing in \(\IHWnlo\) and \(\MMii\).
- The \(\Sigma(1750)\) pole originates from \(K\Xi\) in the ZCL for \(\CSnlo\), \(\GOi\), \(\GOii\), and \(\MMiv\); missing in \(\IHWnlo\) and \(\MMii\).

## Context

This work extends the direct comparison of chiral unitary approaches initiated in Cieply (2016) and builds on the Kyoto-Munich model of Ikeda (2012), the Murcia model of Guo (2012), the Bonn model of Mai (2014), and the Prague model of Cieply (2011). The results show that additional constraints from CLAS photoproduction data (Moriya 2013) help narrow pole positions, but the models still struggle to explain HADES \(\pi\Sigma\) mass spectra from \(pp\) collisions (Agakishiev 2012). The analysis enables future work to discriminate between models using sub-threshold amplitude measurements and to clarify the nature of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) as either a \(\bar{K}N\) bound state or a dynamically generated resonance.
