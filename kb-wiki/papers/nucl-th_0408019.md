# Calculation of the Alpha--Particle Ground State within the Hyperspherical Harmonic Basis

> The problem of calculating the four--nucleon bound state properties for the case of realistic two- and three-body nuclear potentials is studied using the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) approach. A careful analysis of the convergence of different classes of HH functions has been performed. A restricted basis is chosen to allow for accurate estimates of the binding energy and other properties of the 4He ground state. Results for various modern two-nucleon and two- plus three-nucleon interactions are presented. The 4He asymptotic normalization constants for separation in 2+2 and 1+3 clusters are also computed.

## Key claim

The hyperspherical harmonic (HH) expansion can achieve converged binding energies for the alpha particle at the level of 20–30 keV, provided the basis is carefully restricted to specific classes of HH states ordered by the number of correlated particles and by low values of the orbital angular momentum quantum numbers ℓ₁, ℓ₂, ℓ₃, rather than by including all states up to a maximum grand angular quantum number K.

## Method

The four-nucleon wave function was expanded in antisymmetrized hyperspherical harmonic functions constructed from Jacobi coordinates of set A, with the hyperradial functions determined by the Rayleigh–Ritz variational principle. The HH states were divided into six classes (C1–C6) based on the values of ℓ₁+ℓ₂+ℓ₃ and the quantum numbers n₂, n₃, with class C1 containing only the potential-basis states (two-body correlations) and successive classes adding three-body and four-body correlations with increasing ℓ₁+ℓ₂+ℓ₃. Convergence was studied by sequentially increasing the maximum K within each class while keeping lower classes fixed, and the matrix elements of local two-nucleon potentials were reduced to one-dimensional integrals using a jj-coupling scheme.

## Key numbers

* The experimental alpha-particle binding energy is 28.30 MeV.
* For the Malfliet-Tjon V central potential, the binding energy calculated with only the potential basis (PB) is approximately 1 MeV smaller than the exact value.
* The number of linearly independent antisymmetrical spin-isospin-HH states for the J=0, T=0, π=+ ground state is much smaller than the full degeneracy; for example, at K=0 there is only one such state.
* Class C1 (potential basis) has asymptotic growth M₁(K₁) ≈ (3/2) K₁; class C2 has M₂(K₂) ≈ (3/4) K₂²; class C3 has M₃(K₃) ≈ 5 K₃²; class C4 has M₄(K₄) ≈ (57/4) K₄²; class C5 has M₅(K₅) ≈ (109/4) K₅²; class C6 (T>0 states with ℓ₁+ℓ₂+ℓ₃ ≤ 2) has M₆(K₆) ≈ (45/4) K₆².
* The complete calculation includes about 8,000 states.
* States with K ≥ 22 not belonging to classes C1–C3 contribute rather little to the binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the HH approach previously applied to the three-nucleon system by Viviani, Kievsky, and Rosati (1996) to the four-nucleon bound state, addressing the slow convergence that had hindered earlier HH calculations for A=4 (e.g., Demin et al. 1973, Fonseca 1983). The results are compared with those from the Faddeev–Yakubovsky method (Nogga et al. 2000), the Green Function Monte Carlo method (Wiringa et al. 2000), and the stochastic variational method (Varga and Suzuki 1995). The technique is intended to be extended to four-nucleon scattering states, enabling tests of modern chiral perturbation theory interactions and resolving discrepancies between different methods for the AV18+UIX potential.
