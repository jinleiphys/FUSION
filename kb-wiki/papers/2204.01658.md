# Kaon, Nucleon and $Δ^*$ Resonances with Hidden Charm

> In this presentation we discuss the generation of hadrons with an exotic quark content and hidden charm emerging from three-body interactions. To be more specific, we have studied the $KD\bar{D}^*/K\bar D D^*$ and $ND\bar{D}^*/N\bar{D}D^*$ systems and predicted states generated from the respective three-body dynamics. In the case of the $KD\bar{D}^*/K\bar D D^*$ system we predict the formation of a meson state with mass around $4307$ MeV and quantum numbers $I(J^P) = 1/2(1^-)$, $K^*(4307)$, and from the study of the $ND\bar{D}^*/N\bar{D}D^*$ system we found $N^*$ states with masses in the range $4400\sim 4600$ MeV, width of $2\sim 20$ MeV and positive parity.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that three-body dynamics in the $KD\bar{D}^*/K\bar D D^*$ and $ND\bar{D}^*/N\bar{D}D^*$ systems generate exotic hadrons with hidden charm, but the results depend on treating the $D\bar{D}^*$ subsystem as a fixed cluster (either the $X(3872)$ or $Z_c(3900)$ state) and on the choice of cut-off parameters between 700 and 770 MeV.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the three-body systems using a fixed-scattering-center approximation, where the $D\bar{D}^*$ pair forms a cluster (either $X(3872)$ with isospin 0 or $Z_c(3900)$ with isospin 1). The two-body $t$-matrices for the $KD$, $K\bar{D}^*$, $ND$, and $ND^*$ subsystems were obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with kernels from effective Lagrangians based on heavy-quark spin symmetry, using either $SU(8)$ spin-flavor symmetry or $SU(4)$ plus heavy-quark spin symmetry models.

## Key numbers
- $K^*(4307)$ meson state predicted with mass around 4307 MeV and quantum numbers $I(J^P) = 1/2(1^-)$
- $N^*$ states found with masses in the range 4400–4600 MeV, widths of 2–20 MeV, and positive parity
- For $I(J^P) = 1/2(1/2^+)$: two $N^*$ states with masses 4404–4410 MeV (width ~2 MeV) and 4556–4560 MeV (width ~4–20 MeV)
- For $I(J^P) = 1/2(3/2^+)$: two $N^*$ states with masses 4467–4513 MeV (width ~3–6 MeV) and 4558–4565 MeV (width ~5–14 MeV)
- $\Delta^*$ states with hidden charm predicted at masses 4359 MeV (width ~1.5 MeV) and 4512 MeV (width ~4 MeV), with $I(J^P) = 3/2(1/2^+)$
- Cut-off variation from 700 to 770 MeV causes shifts of 3–5 MeV in the masses of the $N^*$ states

## Context
This work extends the study of exotic hadrons with hidden charm from two-body to three-body dynamics, building on prior observations of $X$, $Y$, and $Z$ states (Hosaka 2016, Eberhard, Eulogio 2016, Stephen, Richard) and the LHCb announcement of possible hidden charm pentaquarks with non-zero strangeness near 4459 MeV (Mwang). The subsystems $D\bar{D}^*$, $KD$, $KD^*$, $ND$, and $ND^*$ are known to have attractive interactions generating states like $X(3872)$, $Z_c(3900)$, $D_{s0}(2317)$, $D_{s1}(2460)$, and $\Lambda_c(2595)$ (Albaladejo 2018, Bali 2017, Martinez Torres 2014, van Beveren 2003, Guo 2006, Gamermann 2007, Barnes 2003, Gamermann 2006, Nieves 2019, Liang 2017, Romanets 2012, Garcia-Recio 2008, Mizutani 2006, Hofmann 2005). The predicted $K^*(4307)$ and $N^*$ states provide targets for experimental searches at facilities exploring the 4–5 GeV energy region.
