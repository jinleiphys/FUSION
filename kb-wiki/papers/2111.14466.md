# Competition between diffusion and rapid expansion and its impact on critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the impact of the competition between the expansion of the medium created in heavy-ion collisions and its diffusive properties on the critical fluctuations of the net-baryon density. As the relaxation time of the fluctuations is connected with the diffusive properties, the latter determine the in- or out-of-equilibrium nature of the net-baryon density fluctuations during the fireball evolution. This may result in important consequences for the phenomenological interpretation of heavy-ion collision data. We study three possible situations that can occur as a result of the competition between diffusion and expansion and discuss the impact of this competition on the integrated second and fourth order cumulants of net-baryon density fluctuations at freeze-out in these situations.

## Key claim

The competition between diffusion and rapid expansion in heavy-ion collisions determines whether net-baryon density fluctuations near the QCD critical point are close to equilibrium (large critical enhancement but short-lived) or out of equilibrium (smaller critical signal but longer survival), and this ambiguity can lead to misinterpretation of experimental data regarding the location of the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors study a non-linear stochastic diffusion equation in Milne coordinates for a Bjorken-type expanding medium, where the effective mass and non-linear coupling are derived from second and fourth order susceptibilities per unit of rapidity. These susceptibilities are parametrized by adding singular contributions from a matching to the 3D Ising model scaling equation of state to regular contributions from lattice QCD, following the procedure proposed by Sakaida (2017). The deterministic evolution of an initial Gaussian net-baryon density profile is used to classify three regimes based on the diffusion length D, and then numerical simulations of the full stochastic equation are performed to compute integrated second and fourth order cumulants at freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- For D ≤ 0.5 fm, the normalized difference between system size and density profile width remains close to 1 in the scaling region, indicating expansion dominates diffusion and fluctuations remain out of equilibrium.
- For D ≥ 0.7 fm, the normalized difference becomes negative before the critical proper time τ_c is reached, indicating diffusion wins over expansion and fluctuations are close to equilibrium in the critical region.
- The critical proper time occurs at τ_c - τ_0 = 2.3 fm/c (vertical dashed line in the deterministic evolution analysis).
- Freeze-out temperatures studied are T_f = 145 MeV and T_f = 130 MeV, with trajectories at constant μ_B = 350 MeV.
- The expansion lasts for a total duration of 5 fm/c, starting from an initial equilibrium state at T = 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical study of critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions beyond equilibrium predictions, which were proposed by Asakawa (2015) as crucial observables for the QCD phase transition. The authors build on the framework of Bluhm (2020) regarding out-of-equilibrium fluctuations and use the stochastic diffusion equation in Milne coordinates developed by Kitazawa (2020). The results show that uncertainty in the diffusion length D, combined with uncertainty in freeze-out conditions, broadens the range of possible locations for the QCD critical point, and the authors note that realistic calculations of diffusive properties were recently performed by Fotakis (2019) and are under investigation for use in fluctuation dynamics.
