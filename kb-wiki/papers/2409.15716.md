# Nucleon thermalization hindered by isospin symmetry: Violation of eigenstate thermalization hypothesis in atomic nuclei

> Bohr's compound nucleus theory is one of the most important models in nuclear physics, with far-reaching applications in nuclear science and technology. This model generally assumes that the participating nucleons attain a thermal equilibrium characterized by the microcanonical ensemble before subsequent decays. However, from a theoretical viewpoint, it remains uncertain whether this assumption is universally valid. In this Letter, we critically examine this longstanding assumption through the lens of the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH), a cornerstone of the modern quantum thermalization theory. Utilizing the time-dependent configuration interaction shell model, it is found that, in certain cases, the long-time averages of nucleon occupation numbers can exhibit significant deviations from the microcanonical ensemble averages, in contrast to the conventional expectation. We attribute this discrepancy primarily to the violation of the ETH in the presence of isospin symmetry and discover that incorporating a substantial isospin-breaking term into the shell-model Hamiltonian can effectively restore the nucleon thermalization.

## Key claim

Bohr's assumption that nucleons in a compound nucleus reach thermal equilibrium described by the microcanonical ensemble is not universally valid; in certain cases, the long-time averages of nucleon occupation numbers deviate significantly from microcanonical ensemble averages, with the violation of the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) attributed primarily to the presence of isospin symmetry in the nuclear Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors used a time-dependent configuration interaction shell model to simulate compound nucleus formation via a quantum quench, where an incident neutron in a specific single-particle state fuses with a target nucleus in an excited eigenstate. They computed the time evolution of nucleon occupation numbers for two fusion processes (n+⁴⁹Ca* and n+²³Mg*) using exact diagonalization within the pf-shell (KB3G interaction) and sd-shell (USDB interaction) model spaces, then compared long-time averages to microcanonical ensemble averages. To study isospin symmetry breaking, they rescaled the two-body matrix elements between protons and neutrons (TBME_pn) by factors ranging from 1.02 to 1.5.

## Key numbers

- In the n+⁴⁹Ca* fusion process, relative deviations between long-time averages and microcanonical ensemble averages for all four pf-shell orbits were less than 0.5%.
- In the n+²³Mg* fusion process, relative deviations were approximately 60% for the 0d₃/₂ orbit, 24% for the 0d₅/₂ orbit, and 20% for the 1s₁/₂ orbit, one order of magnitude larger than in the n+⁴⁹Ca* case.
- For the n+²³Mg* fusion process, the microcanonical ensemble energy window was ΔE_c = 0.1 MeV; for n+⁴⁹Ca*, ΔE_c = 0.05 MeV.
- The Hilbert space dimension was 17,276 for the n+⁴⁹Ca* system (pf-shell, 10 active nucleons) and 28,503 for the n+²³Mg* system (sd-shell, 8 active nucleons).

## Context

This work extends the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis framework, previously validated in condensed matter and ultracold atomic systems and recently in (2+1)-dimensional SU(2) gauge theory (Yao 2023, Ebner 2023), to the domain of nuclear physics and Bohr's compound nucleus theory (Bohr 1936). It contradicts the conventional expectation that nuclear forces always drive nucleons to thermal equilibrium, and it challenges the universal validity of the Bohr independence hypothesis tested experimentally by Ghoshal (1950) and others. The findings enable a reassessment of statistical models used in nuclear astrophysics (r-process modeling), nuclear data evaluation, and applications in nuclear power and medical imaging, and suggest that symmetric nuclear systems may exhibit stronger hindrance of thermalization than asymmetric ones.
