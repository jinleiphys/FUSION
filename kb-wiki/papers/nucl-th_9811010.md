# Dispersion relations in Kbar N - pi Y coupled channel analyses

> Rapidly convergent, unsubtracted dispersion relations have been used to test coupled channel analyses of low-energy S = -1 meson-baryon pi Y --> pi Y' channels. We conclude that the low-energy S = -1 meson-baryon sector is less well known than currently believed, and that it deserves more accurate experiments and analyses.

# Dispersion relations in \(\bar K N\) – \(\pi Y\) coupled channel analyses

## Key claim
The low-energy \(S=-1\) meson-baryon sector is less well known than currently believed, and the coupling constants \(G_{\pi Y\Sigma}\) cannot be determined with the accuracy claimed by previous calculations (e.g., Chan and Meiere) because the results show large cancellations between P\(_{11}\) and D\(_{13}\) waves, leading to essential instability in unconstrained analyses such as Kim’s 1967 parametrization.

## Method
The authors used rapidly convergent, unsubtracted forward dispersion relations applied to the three \(\pi Y \to \pi Y'\) reactions (elastic \(\pi\Lambda\) and \(\pi\Sigma\) scattering, and inelastic \(\pi\Lambda\to\pi\Sigma\)), saturating the dispersive integrals with amplitudes from Kim’s 1967 coupled-channel K-matrix analysis (the only published parametrization extending down to the \(\bar K N\) threshold and including S-, P-, and D-waves). They corrected Kim’s D-waves for correct threshold behavior and included estimates of intermediate- and high-energy contributions to the dispersive integrals, then evaluated the resulting coupling constants as a function of energy both with and without the P\(_{11}\) and D\(_{13}\) waves.

## Context
This work extends the dispersion-relation approach of Chan and Meiere (1970s) by testing the stability of coupling constants extracted from Kim’s 1967 K-matrix analysis when P- and D-waves are included. It contradicts the assumption that the low-energy \(S=-1\) sector is well determined, showing that the cancellations between P\(_{11}\) and D\(_{13}\) waves prevent accurate coupling extraction. The results enable future experiments at DA\(\Phi\)NE to improve knowledge of the \(\bar K N\) – \(\pi Y\) coupled-channel amplitudes, particularly around the \(\bar K^0 n\) charge-exchange threshold, and call for more stringent constraints from unitarity, analyticity, and crossing symmetry beyond those used in forward dispersion relations by A.D. Martin.
