# Magnetic-Field Induced Deformation in Hybrid Stars

> The effects of strong magnetic fields on the deconfinement phase transition expected to take place in the interior of massive neutron stars are studied in detail for the first time. For hadronic matter, the very general density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model is employed, while the simple, but effective vector-enhanced bag model (vBag) model is used to study quark matter. Magnetic-field effects are incorporated into the matter equation of state and in the general-relativity solutions, which also satisfy Maxwell's equations. We find that for large values of magnetic dipole moment, the maximum mass, canonical mass radius, and dimensionless tidal deformability obtained for stars using spherically symmetric Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations and axisymmetric solutions attained through the LORENE library differ considerably. The deviations depend on the stiffness of the equation of state and on the star mass being analyzed. This points to the fact that, unlike what was assumed previously in the literature, magnetic field thresholds for the approximation of isotropic stars and the acceptable use of TOV equations depend on the matter composition and interactions.

## Key claim

For hybrid stars with strong magnetic fields, the deviations between spherically symmetric TOV solutions and axisymmetric LORENE solutions for maximum mass, canonical mass radius, and dimensionless tidal deformability become considerable at large magnetic dipole moments, and the magnetic field thresholds at which the isotropic star approximation breaks down depend on the stiffness of the equation of state and on the star mass, contrary to previous assumptions in the literature.

## Method

The authors employed the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model for hadronic matter (including the full baryon octet) and the vector-enhanced bag model (vBag) for quark matter, connecting them via a Gibbs construction for a mixed phase with global charge neutrality. Magnetic field effects were incorporated into both the equation of state (via Landau quantization of charged particles) and into the general-relativity solutions, solving both spherically symmetric Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations and axisymmetric solutions from the LORENE library that satisfy the coupled Einstein-Maxwell equations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass from DD-RMF parameter sets DD-ME1 and DD-ME2: 2.3–2.5 solar masses
- Hyperon optical potentials used: U_Λ^N(ρ₀) = -30 MeV, U_Σ^N(ρ₀) = +30 MeV, U_Ξ^N(ρ₀) = -14 MeV
- Effective bag constant values used: B_eff^(1/4) = 130 MeV and 160 MeV (with 180 MeV also considered in final discussion)
- Vector coupling constant parameter K_ν fixed at 4 GeV⁻² for three-flavor quark matter
- Quark masses: m_u = m_d = 5 MeV, m_s = 100 MeV
- Surface magnetic fields in magnetars: 10¹⁴–10¹⁵ G; interior fields suggested by 4U 0142+61 data: ~10¹⁶ G; theoretical maximum from virial theorem: ~10¹⁸ G
- Canonical mass considered: 1.4 solar masses

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetic field effects on neutron stars (e.g., Gomes (2019), Pili et al. (2017), Dexheimer et al. (2021)) by incorporating quark deconfinement via a Gibbs construction into axisymmetric magnetized stellar models for the first time. It contradicts the common assumption that TOV equations remain valid for magnetized stars below certain field thresholds, showing that the threshold depends on matter composition. The results enable more accurate modeling of hybrid stars with strong magnetic fields, relevant for interpreting observations of magnetars and neutron star mergers.
