# $K^-/K^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions at GSI with an antikaon self-energy in hot and dense matter

> The $K^-/K^+$ ratio produced in heavy-ion collisions at GSI energies is studied. The in-medium properties at finite temperature of the hadrons involved are included, paying a special attention to the in-medium properties of antikaons. Using a statistical approach, it is found that the determination of the temperature and chemical potential at freeze-out conditions compatible with the ratio $K^-/K^+$ is very delicate, and depends very strongly on the approximation adopted for the antikaon self-energy. The use of an energy dependent $\bar{K}$ spectral density, including both s and p-wave components of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction, lowers substantially the freeze-out temperature compared to the standard simplified mean-field treatment and gives rise to an overabundance of $K^-$ production in the dense and hot medium. Even a moderately attractive antikaon-nucleus potential obtained from our self-consistent many-body calculation does reproduce the ``broad-band equilibration'' advocated by Brown, Rho and Song due to the additional strength of the spectral function of the $K^-$ at low energies.

## Key claim

The determination of freeze-out temperature and chemical potential compatible with the experimental $K^-/K^+$ ratio depends very strongly on the approximation adopted for the antikaon self-energy; using an energy-dependent $\bar{K}$ spectral density with both s- and p-wave components lowers the freeze-out temperature substantially compared to a standard mean-field treatment and produces an overabundance of $K^-$ in hot, dense matter.

## Method

The authors employed a statistical thermal model with canonical treatment of strangeness conservation to compute the $K^-/K^+$ ratio for heavy-ion collisions at GSI energies. They incorporated in-medium properties of hadrons at finite temperature, using three different prescriptions for the antikaon self-energy: non-interacting $K^-$, an on-shell (mean-field) self-energy from a self-consistent coupled-channel calculation based on the Julich meson-exchange potential, and a full energy-dependent $\bar{K}$ spectral density including both s-wave and p-wave components of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction, with the p-wave contribution added from hyperon-hole excitations ($\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Sigma^*$).

## Key numbers

* The $K^-$ single-particle energy at zero momentum at $T=70$ MeV changes from 434 MeV to 375 MeV when density grows from $1.2\rho_0$ to $2.1\rho_0$, where $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ is nuclear matter saturation density.
* The baryonic chemical potential $\mu_B$ changes from 873 MeV to 962 MeV over the same density range ($1.2\rho_0$ to $2.1\rho_0$) at $T=70$ MeV.
* The $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance is described with mass $m_{\Sigma^*}=1385$ MeV and width $\Gamma=37$ MeV.
* The $\Lambda$ potential is parameterized as $U_{\Lambda}(\rho)=-340\rho+1087.5\rho^2$ (units not specified but density in fm$^{-3}$).
* The $\Sigma$ potential is taken as $U_{\Sigma}(\rho)=30 \rho/\rho_0$ (repulsive).
* The $K^+$ potential is $U_{K^+}(\rho)= 32 \rho/\rho_0$.

## Context

This work extends the "broad-band equilibration" concept introduced by Brown, Rho and Song (2001), which proposed that the $K^-/K^+$ ratio remains constant with density because the increase in baryonic chemical potential is roughly cancelled by the reduction of the $K^-$ mass. The present study shows that this cancellation is incomplete when using a microscopic on-shell self-energy, but the full spectral density (especially including p-wave contributions from hyperon-hole excitations) does produce a nearly density-independent ratio. The results contrast with earlier statistical model analyses by Cleymans and collaborators (1998-2000) that used free masses and found freeze-out parameters $\mu_B \simeq 720$ MeV and $T \simeq 70$ MeV. The work enables future investigation into what fraction of the low-energy strength in the $K^-$ spectral function emerges as real antikaons at freeze-out.
