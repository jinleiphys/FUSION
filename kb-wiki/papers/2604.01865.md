# Tetraquark-Jet Systems at the High-Luminosity LHC

> We investigate the high-energy production of tetraquark-jet systems at the LHC and its forthcoming High-Luminosity upgrade. In this review, we examine the leading-power fragmentation of fully heavy tetraquarks ($T_{4Q}$) in hadronic collisions, highlighting their relevance as novel probes of multiquark dynamics in QCD. Our analysis relies on the hadron-structure-oriented TQ4Q1.1 fragmentation functions, built within a nonrelativistic QCD framework that incorporates both gluon- and heavy-quark-initiated channels. Threshold-consistent DGLAP evolution is performed through the HF-NRevo scheme, enabling a unified treatment of mass thresholds and scale variations. We also provide a systematic discussion of uncertainties arising from color-composite long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) and from perturbative hard- and fragmentation-scale inputs (H- and F-MHOUs). Phenomenological predictions are obtained using the (sym)Jethad framework at NLL/NLO$^+$ accuracy for semi-inclusive tetraquark-jet production at the LHC and beyond. This review connects the emerging spectroscopy of fully heavy exotics with modern fragmentation-based approaches to hadron structure and high-energy QCD.

## Key claim

This review argues that fully heavy tetraquark-jet production at the High-Luminosity LHC can be described via leading-power fragmentation using the TQ4Q1.1 fragmentation functions, but the predictive power is conditional on systematic propagation of both perturbative missing higher-order uncertainties and nonperturbative long-distance matrix element uncertainties, which are treated here for the first time as independent sources of theoretical error.

## Method

The analysis constructs collinear fragmentation functions for fully heavy tetraquarks (T_{4Q}) within a nonrelativistic QCD framework, incorporating both gluon- and heavy-quark-initiated channels at the initial scale. These functions are evolved through threshold-consistent DGLAP evolution using the HF-NRevo scheme, which enables unified treatment of mass thresholds and scale variations. Phenomenological predictions for semi-inclusive tetraquark-jet production are obtained using the (sym)Jethad framework at NLL/NLO^+ accuracy within the hybrid collinear and high-energy factorization scheme, which merges NLO collinear factorization with resummation of high-energy logarithms.

## Key numbers

- The CMS Collaboration reported three resonant structures in the 4m_c < M < 7 GeV region for fully charmed tetraquarks, with an angular analysis favoring a J^{PC}=2^{++} assignment for the leading peak
- ATLAS extracted double-parton scattering effective cross sections from prompt J/psi pairs at sqrt(s)=8 TeV
- CMS measured total and differential double J/psi cross sections at sqrt(s)=7 TeV
- CMS observed double J/psi production in pPb at 8.16 TeV
- An ANDY observation at RHIC reported a resonance near 18.15 GeV in Cu-Au collisions, consistent with predictions for T_{4b} tetraquarks

## Context

This review extends the phenomenological study initiated in Celiberto (2025) by providing comprehensive analysis of collinear fragmentation into fully heavy tetraquarks through the TQ4Q1.1 family of functions. It builds upon prior NRQCD-based fragmentation frameworks for quarkonia (Braaten 1993, Zheng 2019) and earlier tetraquark fragmentation work (Feng 2020, Bai 2024), while connecting the emerging spectroscopy of fully heavy exotics with modern fragmentation-based approaches to hadron structure. The framework enables future multimodal fragmentation studies where perturbative and nonperturbative ingredients can be explored and validated in a unified, uncertainty-aware setting, and sets the stage for quantitative cross-validation of heavy-flavor observables at current and next-generation hadron colliders including the HL-LHC, EIC, and FCC.
