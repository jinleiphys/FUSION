# Emergence of a Brunnian neutron state

> We discuss a quantum-statistical feature of non-relativistic identical fermions whose interaction is predominantly attractive at low energies. Specifically, we consider exotic, multi-neutron nuclei. From the enhancement of an arbitrarily small P-wave interaction between two nucleons, we infer the existence of a particle-stable nucleus composed entirely of neutrons. While we cannot specify the number of neutrons in the system, we predict that none of its substructures is bound. The independence of this deduction from the short-distance structure of the nuclear interaction and its consistency with deuteron, triton, and helium-4 properties is established.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a particle-stable nucleus composed entirely of neutrons must exist, even though none of its substructures (di-neutron, tri-neutron, etc.) are individually bound. The claim is conditional on the assumption that the enhancement of an arbitrarily small P-wave interaction between two neutrons scales with the number of neutron pairs in the system, and the authors cannot specify the exact number of neutrons required.

## Method

The author uses pion-less effective field theory (EFT) with contact interactions and a spin-orbit perturbation term to describe neutron-neutron interactions. The strength of the spin-orbit term is varied to selectively enhance P-wave channels while keeping the theory consistent with deuteron, triton, and helium-4 binding energies. The resonating-group method is employed to solve the Schrodinger equation for three- and four-neutron systems, and the critical spin-orbit strengths at which bound states appear are compared across systems of different sizes.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy used for calibration is 2.22 MeV at physical pion mass.
- The triton binding energy used for calibration is 8.48 MeV at physical pion mass.
- The singlet neutron-proton scattering length used for calibration is -23.8 fm at physical quark masses.
- For the heavy-pion world (pion mass approximately 806 MeV), the di-neutron binding energy is approximately 16 MeV and the triton binding energy is 53.9 MeV.
- The critical spin-orbit parameter hierarchy is eps(4n) < eps(3n) < eps(2n), meaning bound tetra-neutrons appear at weaker spin-orbit strength than bound tri-neutrons.
- The predicted binding energy of the multi-neutron state is of order 10 MeV, comparable to the deuteron and triton ground states.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Borromean and Brunnian nuclear states, previously studied in systems like helium-6 (a helium-4 core plus two neutrons where neither helium-5 nor the di-neutron is bound). It contrasts with high-accuracy calculations by Gandolfi (2016) and others that do not find bound neutron nuclei, and with earlier work by Migdal (1972) who considered stable di-neutrons in the field of a lithium-9 core. The paper also relates to studies by Baz (1972) and Ogloblin (1989) that suggested bound neutron nuclei with more than 64 neutrons based on analogies with liquid helium. The result enables future experimental tests in cold-atom systems where two-fermion P-wave interactions can be tuned, and suggests that nuclear P-wave observables (such as the first excited state of helium-4 or the instability of beryllium-8) could constrain the critical neutron number.
