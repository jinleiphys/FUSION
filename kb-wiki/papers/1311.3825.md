# Tensor parameters in Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions: Trends from a ground-state-focused study

> Recent ground--state--focused studies of the tensor effects in the mean--field framework are our starting point. On the basis of phenomenological arguments, we indicate regions for acceptable values of the parameters that are associated with the tensor effective forces within both the Skyrme and the Gogny models. We identify acceptable signs and values of the parameters by making an adjustment on the neutron $1f$ spin--orbit splitting for the nuclei $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca and $^{56}$Ni. The first nucleus is not used to adjust the tensor parameters because it is spin--saturated, but is employed to tune the spin--orbit strength. One of the main conclusions of this work is that some existing Skyrme parametrizations containing the tensor force should not be employed because the wrong sign of the tensor parameters does not lead to the correct behavior (by comparing with the experimental results). This study also allows us to better constrain the tensor parameters in the Gogny case, where much less work is published and boundaries and signs for the parameters have not been analyzed so far.

## Key claim

Based on phenomenological arguments and a ground-state-focused fitting protocol, the authors identify that the tensor parameters governing like-particle contributions (\(\alpha\) in Skyrme, \(V_{T1}+V_{T2}\) in Gogny) must be negative and those governing neutron-proton contributions (\(\beta\) in Skyrme, \(V_{T2}\) in Gogny) must be positive to reproduce experimental neutron \(1f\) spin-orbit splittings; several existing Skyrme parametrizations with the wrong sign of these parameters should not be employed.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock calculations (neglecting pairing and particle-vibration coupling) for the neutron \(1f_{7/2}-1f_{5/2}\) spin-orbit splitting in three nuclei: \(^{40}\)Ca (spin-saturated, used to tune the spin-orbit strength), \(^{48}\)Ca (proton-saturated, used to adjust the like-particle tensor parameter), and \(^{56}\)Ni (spin-unsaturated, used to adjust the neutron-proton tensor parameter). Starting from six existing Skyrme parametrizations (SLy5\(_{\rm T}\), SIII\(_{\rm T}\), T\(_{41}\), T\(_{11}\), T\(_{14}\), T\(_{44}\)) and one Gogny parametrization (D1ST2a), they sequentially refitted the spin-orbit parameter \(W\), then the like-particle tensor parameter (\(\alpha_{\rm T}\) or \(V_{T1}+V_{T2}\)), and finally the neutron-proton tensor parameter (\(\beta_{\rm T}\) or \(V_{T2}\)) to match experimental centroid energies.

## Key numbers

- The experimental neutron \(1f\) spin-orbit splitting centroid in \(^{40}\)Ca is 6.8 MeV (evaluated from 24 states between 4.9 and 9.1 MeV).
- The spin-orbit parameter \(W\) was reduced from 126.00 to 101.00 MeV fm\(^5\) for SLy5\(_{\rm T}\), from 120.00 to 95.50 for SIII\(_{\rm T}\), from 138.15 to 103.00 for T\(_{41}\), from 103.74 to 100.00 for T\(_{11}\), from 128.51 to 101.00 for T\(_{14}\), from 161.37 to 105.00 for T\(_{44}\), and from 130.00 to 103.00 for D1ST2a.
- The refitted like-particle tensor parameter \(\alpha_{\rm T}\) (in MeV fm\(^5\)) was -170.00 for SLy5\(_{\rm T}\), -155.00 for SIII\(_{\rm T}\), -215.00 for T\(_{41}\), -175.00 for T\(_{11}\); the refitted \(V_{T1}+V_{T2}\) for D1ST2a was -75.00 MeV.
- The refitted neutron-proton tensor parameter \(\beta_{\rm T}\) (in MeV fm\(^5\)) was 122.00 for SLy5\(_{\rm T}\), 50.00 for SIII\(_{\rm T}\), 55.00 for T\(_{41}\); the refitted \(V_{T2}\) for D1ST2a was 60.00 MeV.
- The ratio of like-particle to neutron-proton tensor strengths was -1.4 for SLy5\(_{\rm T}\), -3.1 for SIII\(_{\rm T}\), -3.9 for T\(_{41}\), and -1.2 for D1ST2a.

## Context

This work extends the fitting protocol of Zalewski et al. (2008, 2009), who used single-particle properties to adjust tensor coupling constants in the Energy Density Functional framework, by applying it to both Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions. It contradicts the use of several Skyrme parametrizations from Lesinski et al. (2007) where \(\alpha\) is positive or \(\beta\) is negative, and provides the first systematic constraints on the signs and approximate values of tensor parameters in the Gogny model, where much less work has been published compared to the Skyrme case (e.g., Stancu et al. 1977, Colo et al. 2007, Brink and Stancu 2007). The identified parameter regions enable future global fits of full Gogny parametrizations including both pure tensor and tensor-isospin terms.
