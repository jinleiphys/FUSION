# The Role of the Coherence Length in Exclusive Electroproduction of Vector Mesons off Nuclei

> The variation of the coherence length $l_c=2ν/(Q^2+m_V^2)$ in virtual photoproduction of vector mesons off nuclei as a function of the photon energy $ν$ or virtuality $Q^2$ results in dramatic changes in the values of the nuclear transparency. Color transparency effects can be easily mixed up with the effects of the coherence length in incoherent photoproduction on nuclei.

# The Role of the Coherence Length in Exclusive Electroproduction of Vector Mesons off Nuclei

## Key claim
The variation of the coherence length $l_c = 2\nu/(Q^2 + m_V^2)$ with photon energy $\nu$ or virtuality $Q^2$ produces steep changes in nuclear transparency that can mimic or obscure color transparency (CT) signals, particularly at moderate energies where straightforward application of high-energy CT predictions is unreliable.

## Method
The authors derive a new Glauber-approximation formula for incoherent exclusive (virtual) photoproduction of vector mesons off nuclei that correctly incorporates coherence-length effects, which was previously missing in the literature. They compute nuclear transparency for both incoherent and coherent production of $\rho$ and $J/\Psi$ mesons as functions of $\nu$ and $Q^2$, using the nuclear density $\rho(b,z)$ and thickness function $T(b)$, and compare their results to existing data from the NMC experiment and earlier predictions.

## Key numbers
- Coherence length defined as $l_c = 2\nu/(Q^2 + m_V^2)$, where $\nu$ is photon energy, $Q^2$ is virtuality, and $m_V$ is vector meson mass.
- For charmonium photoproduction, the transition region where coherence-length effects become important covers energies from a few tens to a few hundreds of GeV.
- For light vector mesons ($\rho, \omega, \phi$), the transition energy range is an order of magnitude lower than for charmonium.
- The authors predict a steep $Q^2$ dependence of nuclear transparency at moderate energy, in variance with results of BZNF (1994) which expected nearly $Q^2$-independent nuclear transparency for $\phi$-meson electroproduction at $\nu = 8$ GeV.
- Nuclear transparency for incoherent $\rho$ production on iron saturates at high $Q^2$ at a level below unity, even in the Glauber approximation without CT.

## Context
This work extends the earlier high-energy predictions of Kopeliovich et al. (1994) for color transparency in vector meson photoproduction, but warns that those predictions cannot be straightforwardly applied at moderate energies accessible to CEBAF, HERMES, and ELFE. The authors derive a new Glauber formula for incoherent production that corrects an earlier incorrect formula by Bauer (1978), which underestimated data on real photoproduction of $\rho$ off nuclei. The paper enables experimental searches for color transparency to properly separate coherence-length effects from genuine CT signals, and concludes that coherent virtual photoproduction at moderate energies is a better reaction for CT studies than incoherent production.
