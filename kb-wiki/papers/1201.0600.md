# An efficient method to evaluate energy variances for extrapolation methods

> The energy variance extrapolation method consists in relating the approximate energies in many-body calculations to the corresponding energy variances and inferring eigenvalues by extrapolating to zero variance. The method needs a fast evaluation of the energy variances. For many-body methods that expand the nuclear wave functions in terms of deformed Slater determinants, the best available method for the evaluation of energy variances scales with the sixth power of the number of single-particle states. We propose a new method which depends on the number of single-particle orbits and the number of particles rather than the number of single-particle states. We discuss as an example the case of ${}^4He$ using the chiral N3LO interaction in a basis consisting up to 184 single-particle states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method for evaluating energy variances that scales with the number of single-particle orbits and the number of particles rather than the sixth power of the number of single-particle states, but the method is demonstrated only for the specific case of ${}^4$He using a renormalized chiral N3LO interaction in a basis of up to 184 single-particle states.

## Method

The author reformulates the computation of the third term in the expression for $\langle V|\HH^2|U\rangle / \langle V|U\rangle$ (the $Q$ term) by factorizing the density matrix into low-rank products of rectangular matrices $U$ and $W$, where $U$ contains the single-particle wave functions of the Slater determinants and $W$ is derived from the inverse overlap matrix. The expressions are then recoupled into angular-momentum-coupled form using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, separating the contributions into 2-body, 3-body, and 4-body terms ($C_2$, $C_{3a}$, $C_{3b}$, $C_4$) that are evaluated using matrix multiplications over particle indices and orbit indices rather than over all single-particle states. The method is applied to ${}^4$He using the Hybrid Multi-Determinant (HMD) method to generate sequences of wave functions, with the N3LO interaction renormalized either by a sharp momentum cutoff at $K_{max}=2.5\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ followed by Lee-Suzuki renormalization, or by direct Lee-Suzuki renormalization alone.

## Key numbers

- The largest single-particle space used consists of **184 single-particle states** (for $N_{ho}=7$), corresponding to **32 single-particle orbits**.
- The computational cost for the 2-body contribution $C_2$ scales as $N_{JM} N_{orb}^2 N_n^2$, where $N_{orb}$ is the number of orbits and $N_n$ the number of neutrons.
- The 3-body contribution $C_3$ scales as $N_{sp} N_n^3$ plus an additional cost of $N_{JM} N_{sp} N_{orb} N_n^3$ for auxiliary matrices.
- The 4-body contribution $C_4$ scales as $N_n^4$, with the matrix $\CM$ evaluation scaling as $N_{JM} N_{orb}^2 N_n^4$.
- Harmonic oscillator frequencies considered: $\hbar\Omega = (21, 24, 27, 30, 33, 36)\ \mathrm{MeV}$ for $N_{ho}=5,6$, and $\hbar\Omega = (27, 30)\ \mathrm{MeV}$ for $N_{ho}=7$.
- The linear fit quality criterion $\mathcal{E}_{n_0}$ is kept at the order of **10 keV** or smaller.
- The sharp momentum cutoff used in the first renormalization method is $K_{max}=2.5\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the energy variance extrapolation method previously applied in Monte Carlo shell model calculations (Refs. [9,10] in the paper) and in shell model truncation studies (Ref. [6]) to a computationally cheaper evaluation scheme. The prior best method scaled as the sixth power of the number of single-particle states, making systematic application prohibitive. The new method enables variance extrapolation for variational methods like the Hybrid Multi-Determinant method (Puddu, earlier work) and Monte Carlo shell model (Otsuka and collaborators, earlier work) with larger single-particle bases, and the ${}^4$He test case shows results approaching the exact Faddeev-Yakubovski energy.
