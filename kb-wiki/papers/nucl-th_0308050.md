# Effective field theory: A complete relativistic nuclear model

> We analyzed the results for finite nuclei and infinite nuclear and neutron matter using the standard $σ-ω$ model and with the effective field theory. For the first time, we have shown here quantitatively that the inclusion of self-interaction of the vector mesons and the cross-interaction of all the mesons taken in the theory explain naturally the experimentally observed softness of equation of state without loosing the advantages of standard $σ-ω$ model for finite nuclei. Recent experimental observations support our findings and allow us to conclude that without self- and cross-interactions the relativistic mean field theory is incomplete.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including self-interactions of vector mesons and cross-interactions of all mesons (as in effective field theory, EFT) naturally explains the experimentally observed softness of the equation of state (EOS) at high densities, whereas the standard nonlinear σ-ω model without these terms fails. The hedge is that this conclusion is drawn specifically for the G2 parameter set, which yields a positive Φ⁴ coupling constant (κ₄) and avoids the unphysical negative lower bound of the energy spectrum present in many standard parametrizations.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for finite nuclei and infinite nuclear and neutron matter using the standard nonlinear σ-ω model (with NL3 and TM1 parameter sets) and an EFT-based Lagrangian truncated at fourth order (with G1 and G2 parameter sets). They solved the Dirac equation for nucleons and the mean-field equations for scalar (σ), vector (ω), isovector (ρ), and photon (A) fields, including nonlinear scalar-vector and vector-vector couplings, tensor couplings, and electromagnetic structure terms. The resulting EOS and nuclear properties were compared to experimental data from heavy-ion collisions and to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density, total energy, incompressibility modulus, and effective mass were imposed to fix parameters; the bulk symmetry energy coefficient J fixed the value of g_ρ²/m_ρ².
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the charge radius r_ch = 5.50 fm for G2 (experimental: 5.50 fm); binding energy per nucleon E/A = -7.86 MeV for G2 (experimental: -7.87 MeV).
- Neutron star radius R = 10.04 km for G2, compared to experimental range 10.0–12.0 km; mass ratio M/M_⊙ = 2.04 for G2, compared to experimental range 1.5–2.5.
- For ¹⁶O, spin-orbit splitting ΔE_SO (n,1p) = 5.9 MeV for G2 (experimental: 6.2 MeV); for ⁴⁸Ca, ΔE_SO (n,1d) = 5.6 MeV for G2 (experimental: 3.6 MeV).
- Surface energy coefficient E_s = 17.8 MeV for G2 (experimental range: 16.5–21.0 MeV); surface thickness t = 2.08 fm for G2 (experimental range: 2.2–2.5 fm).

## Context
This work extends the EFT-based RMF approach of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1996) and the authors' own prior studies (Es99, estal1) by testing the G1 and G2 parameter sets against new experimental EOS data from heavy-ion collisions (Sci02). It contradicts the standard nonlinear σ-ω model (e.g., NL3 from Lalazissis et al., 1997) which gives a too stiff EOS at high densities, and shows that EFT with the G2 set reproduces both finite-nuclei properties and the soft EOS required by neutron star observations and kaon production measurements (PRL01). The work enables a unified relativistic description of finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter without ad hoc parameter adjustments.
