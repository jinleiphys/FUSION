# Electric charge fluctuations from lattice QCD in the continuum limit

> Electric charge fluctuations $χ_n^Q$ allow comparisons between theory and experiment, but are elusive on the lattice due to severe cutoff effects. We use a 4HEX action to obtain $χ_2^Q$ and, for the first time ever, $χ_4^Q$ in the continuum limit. We find disagreement with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, which we cannot explain with finite volume effects. We include light meson interactions in the HRG model via the S-matrix, reducing the tension for $χ_4^Q$, but worsening the agreement for $χ_2^Q$. We propose measuring the ratio $χ_4^Q/χ_2^Q$ at the LHC to investigate this tension.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results for the fourth-order electric charge susceptibility χ₄^Q, finding disagreement with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model that cannot be explained by finite volume effects; including light meson interactions via the S-matrix reduces the tension for χ₄^Q but worsens agreement for χ₂^Q, leaving the discrepancy unresolved.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the 4HEX action with N_f=2+1 rooted staggered dynamical quarks at physical masses, employing temporal resolutions N_τ=8,10,12,16 covering T=120-165 MeV with aspect ratio LT=4. Continuum extrapolations were performed combining two scale settings (f_π and w_1) with linear and parabolic fits, using the histogram method of Borsanyi (2020) to estimate systematic uncertainties. The HRG model was extended via the S-matrix formulation including elastic π-π and π-K scattering phase shifts from Garcia-Martin (2011) and Pelaez (2016), and finite volume effects were studied through dedicated lattice runs at LT=2,3 and HRG calculations in finite boxes.

## Key numbers

- χ₂^Q and χ₄^Q continuum-extrapolated results are presented in the temperature range T=120-160 MeV
- At T=130 MeV, the 4HEX action achieves at N_τ=12 the same discretization error that the 4stout action requires N_τ=20 to reach
- Finite volume effects at LT=4 are of order 1-2% for χ₂^Q
- The S-matrix HRG model includes π-π I=2 S-wave (repulsive) and D-wave channels up to √s=0.85 GeV and 1.42 GeV respectively, and π-K S-wave I=3/2 and I=1/2 channels up to √s=1.74 GeV
- The effective mass model (EMM) uses f_π=133 MeV in the gap equation

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD results for electric charge fluctuations from the HotQCD collaboration with the HISQ action (Bollweg 2021) and from the authors' earlier 4stout action results (Bellwied 2015), which were limited to χ₂^Q or had incomplete continuum extrapolations for χ₄^Q. The disagreement with the ideal HRG model and its S-matrix extension motivates experimental measurement of the ratio χ₄^Q/χ₂^Q at the LHC, as existing RHIC measurements (Adamczyk 2014, Adare 2015) lack sufficient precision. The results also connect to studies of in-medium pion spectral properties above the chiral crossover (Lowdon 2022) and to functional approaches such as FRG (Gao 2020, Fu 2021) and DSE (Isserstedt 2019, Bernhardt 2022).
