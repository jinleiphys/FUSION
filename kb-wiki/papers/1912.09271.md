# Survey of deformation in nuclei in order to estimate the enhancement of sensitivity to atomic EDM

> The observed baryon asymmetry of the universe (BAU) cannot be explained by the known sources of charge-parity (CP)-violation in the Standard Model (SM). A non-zero permanent electric-dipole-moment (EDM) for fundamental particles, nuclei or atoms, violates CP. Measuring a non-zero EDM allows us to gain a handle on additional sources of CP-violation required to explain the observed BAU. The EDM of an atom with an octupole and quadrupole deformed nucleus is enhanced. Therefore, the search for such atoms has become important in the quest to measure an EDM. Viability of EDM searches in $^{225}$Ra atoms with a deformed nucleus have already been demonstrated. We have performed a comprehensive survey for possible EDM candidates from a list of octupole deformed nuclei predicted by various theoretical models. Our search of long-lived isotopes with nuclear deformations comparable to or better than $^{225}$Ra results in a handful of viable candidates for future atomic EDM experiments based out of the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB): $^{221}$Rn, $^{221,223,227}$Fr, $^{221,223,225}$Ra, $^{223,225,227}$Ac, $^{229}$Th, and particularly $^{229}$Pa. Furthermore, nuclei of $^{223,225}$Rn, $^{225}$Fr and $^{226}$Ac are also highly quadrupole and octupole deformed, but their ground state parity doublet energy difference has not yet been measured.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a comprehensive survey of octupole-deformed nuclei identifies several viable candidates for atomic EDM experiments at FRIB, but they hedge that the theoretical enhancement of atomic EDM relative to $^{225}$Ra depends strongly on the ground-state parity doublet energy difference, which for the most promising candidate ($^{229}$Pa) has a large relative uncertainty that makes the existence of the doublet itself uncertain.

## Method

The authors surveyed theoretical predictions of octupole and quadrupole deformation parameters ($\beta_3$, $\beta_2$) from models such as the Moller-Nix model, Hartree-Fock model, and Nilsson model, combined with measured ground-state parity doublet energy differences ($\Delta E$) and FRIB beam rate estimates. They computed a normalized theoretical EDM expectation $d^{\mathcal{T}} \propto (\beta_2 \beta_3^2 Z^3 A^{2/3})/(E_- - E_+)$ and a relative impact factor $\mathcal{I} \propto d^{\mathcal{T}} \cdot \sqrt{W^{\text{(FRIB)}}}$, then ranked candidates by these figures of merit.

## Key numbers

* $^{225}$Ra: $\beta_3 = 0.124$, $\beta_2 = 0.164$, $\Delta E = 55.16(6)$ keV, normalized $d^{\mathcal{T}} = 1.00$, normalized $\mathcal{I} = 1.00$ (reference isotope)
* $^{229}$Pa: $\beta_3 = 0.082$, $\beta_2 = 0.190$, $\Delta E = 0.06(5)$ keV, normalized $d^{\mathcal{T}} = 521.13$, normalized $\mathcal{I} = 938.03$ (most enhanced candidate, but with large uncertainty on $\Delta E$)
* $^{227}$Ac: $\beta_3 = 0.105$, $\beta_2 = 0.172$, $\Delta E = 27.369(11)$ keV, normalized $d^{\mathcal{T}} = 1.56$, normalized $\mathcal{I} = 1.56$
* $^{223}$Ra: $\beta_3 = 0.142$, $\beta_2 = 0.156$, $\Delta E = 50.128(9)$ keV, normalized $d^{\mathcal{T}} = 1.36$, normalized $\mathcal{I} = 1.40$
* $^{223}$Ac: $\beta_3 = 0.151$, $\beta_2 = 0.147$, $\Delta E = 64.62(4)$ keV, normalized $d^{\mathcal{T}} = 1.49$, normalized $\mathcal{I} = 1.34$
* $^{225}$Ac: $\beta_3 = 0.127$, $\beta_2 = 0.164$, $\Delta E = 40.09(5)$ keV, normalized $d^{\mathcal{T}} = 1.49$, normalized $\mathcal{I} = 1.33$
* $^{229}$Th: $\beta_3 = 0.240$, $\beta_2 = 0.115$, $\Delta E = 146.3569(14)$ keV, normalized $d^{\mathcal{T}} = 1.07$, normalized $\mathcal{I} = 1.05$
* $^{221}$Rn: $\beta_3 = 0.142$, $\beta_2 = 0.119$, $\Delta E = 30(10)$ keV, normalized $d^{\mathcal{T}} = 1.61$, normalized $\mathcal{I} = 0.97$
* For $^{225}$Ra, the value of $\beta_3$ varies between theoretical models: 0.124 (Moller-Nix), 0.146 (Hartree-Fock), and 0.104 (Nilsson model)
* The neutron EDM upper limit is $d_n < 3\times10^{-26}\,e\cdot cm$ (90% C.L.), constraining the QCD-$\theta$ parameter to $\theta < 5\times10^{-10}$
* The electron EDM is constrained to $d_e < 1.1\times10^{-29}\,e\cdot cm$

## Context

This work extends the search for atomic EDM candidates beyond $^{225}$Ra, whose EDM experiment at Argonne National Laboratory (Parker et al.) demonstrated feasibility. The survey uses deformation parameters from the Moller-Nix model (1995), Hartree-Fock model, and Nilsson model, and FRIB beam rate estimates from Refs. [32-1,32-2]. The paper identifies $^{229}$Pa as a candidate with potentially enormous enhancement, but notes that an earlier measurement by the same group gave $\Delta E = 0.22(5)$ keV, which would reduce the impact factor by a factor of 4. The authors call for new measurements of parity doublet energy differences for isotopes in Table 2 ($^{223,225}$Rn, $^{225}$Fr, $^{226}$Ac) and for independent theoretical calculations of deformation parameters for isotopes in Table 3.
