# Evidence for chiral symmetry restoration in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the effect of the chiral symmetry restoration (CSR) on heavy-ion collisions observables in the energy range $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3-20 GeV within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The PHSD includes the deconfinement phase transition as well as essential aspects of CSR in the dense and hot hadronic medium, which are incorporated in the Schwinger mechanism for particle production. Our systematic studies show that chiral symmetry restoration plays a crucial role in the description of heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3-20 GeV, realizing an increase of the hadronic particle production in the strangeness sector with respect to the non-strange one. Our results provide a microscopic explanation for the "horn" structure in the excitation function of the $K^+/π^+$ ratio: the CSR in the hadronic phase produces the steep increase of this particle ratio up to $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx $ 7 GeV, while the drop at higher energies is associated to the appearance of a deconfined partonic medium. Furthermore, the appearance/disappearance of the "horn" structure is investigated as a function of the system size. We additionally present an analysis of strangeness production in the ($T,μ_B$)-plane (as extracted from the PHSD for central Au+Au collisions) and discuss the perspectives to identify a possible critical point in the phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chiral symmetry restoration (CSR) in the hadronic phase, not deconfinement alone, is responsible for the steep increase of the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio up to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7\) GeV, while the drop at higher energies is due to the appearance of a deconfined partonic medium; this explanation is conditional on the specific implementation of CSR within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach.

## Method

The authors used the PHSD transport approach, which includes partonic degrees-of-freedom as strongly interacting quasiparticles and a dynamical hadronization scheme, to simulate central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3-20\) GeV. CSR was incorporated by modifying the Schwinger mechanism for string decay: the effective masses of light and strange quarks were made dependent on the in-medium scalar quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle\), calculated via a non-linear \(\sigma-\omega\) model for nuclear matter with three parameter sets (NL1, NL2, NL3). The resulting particle ratios were compared to experimental data, and the system-size dependence was studied by also simulating Ca+Ca and C+C collisions.

## Key numbers

* The string tension used in the Schwinger formula is \(\kappa \approx 0.176\) GeV\(^2\).
* The vacuum constituent (dressed) quark masses are \(m_u^v \approx 0.33\) GeV and \(m_s^v \approx 0.5\) GeV, giving a vacuum strangeness suppression factor \(\gamma_s \approx 0.3\).
* The bare quark masses used are \(m_q^0 \approx 7\) MeV and \(m_s^0 \approx 100\) MeV.
* The pion-nucleon \(\Sigma\)-term is \(\Sigma_\pi \approx 45\) MeV.
* The "horn" in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio peaks near \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7\) GeV for Au+Au collisions; the peak becomes less pronounced for Ca+Ca and disappears for C+C collisions.

## Context

This work extends the study of Cassing (2015) by providing a microscopic explanation for the "horn" structure in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio, which had previously been attributed solely to deconfinement (Rafelski, Stock; Greiner and Gaitanos, 1999, 2011). The results contradict conventional hadronic transport theories (Geiss, Bratkovskaya, Weber) that could not reproduce the experimental data. The paper enables future searches for a critical point in the QCD phase diagram, though it notes that the large spread in temperature and baryon chemical potential at all bombarding energies makes experimental identification difficult.
