# Strange Antibaryon Production Data and Reduction of Strangeness Suppression in Sulphur-Nucleus Collisions at 200A GeV

> We have used the event generator LUCIAE to analyse NA35 data of the $\bar p$ and $\barΛ$ yields, the ratio $\barΛ/\bar p$, and the transverse mass distributions of $\bar p$ and $\barΛ$ in pp and central sulphur-nucleus collisions at 200A GeV. The NA35 data could be reproduced reasonably if one assumes that the s quark pair suppression factor and concerns in nucleus-nucleus collisions are larger than the nucleon-nucleon collision. It seems to indicate that NA35 data might imply the reduction of strangeness suppression in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions comparing to the nucleon-nucleon collision at the same energy. However, the ratio $\barΛ/\bar p$ approaching to unity in AA collisions comparing to the pp collision does not necessarily mean a flavor symmetry since hadronic rescattering plays a role as well.

# Strange Antibaryon Production Data and Reduction of Strangeness Suppression in Sulphur-Nucleus Collisions at 200A GeV

## Key claim

The NA35 data on antiproton and antilambda yields in central sulphur-nucleus collisions at 200A GeV can be reproduced by the LUCIAE event generator only if the strange quark pair suppression factor in nucleus-nucleus collisions is assumed to be larger than in nucleon-nucleon collisions, which may indicate a reduction of strangeness suppression in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions compared to nucleon-nucleon collisions at the same energy. However, the authors caution that the antilambda-to-antiproton ratio approaching unity in AA collisions compared to pp collisions does not necessarily imply flavor symmetry, because hadronic rescattering also contributes to this effect.

## Method

The authors used the LUCIAE event generator to analyze NA35 experimental data on antiproton and antilambda yields, the antilambda-to-antiproton ratio, and the transverse mass distributions of antiprotons and antilambdas in pp and central sulphur-nucleus collisions at 200A GeV. They varied the strange quark pair suppression factor in the model to reproduce the measured data, comparing results between nucleon-nucleon and nucleus-nucleus collision systems.

## Key numbers

- Antilambda-to-antiproton ratio approaches unity in central sulphur-nucleus collisions at 200A GeV, compared to a smaller value in pp collisions at the same energy
- The strange quark pair suppression factor required to reproduce NA35 data is larger in nucleus-nucleus collisions than in nucleon-nucleon collisions at 200A GeV
- Transverse mass distributions of antiprotons and antilambdas in pp and central sulphur-nucleus collisions at 200A GeV were reproduced by the LUCIAE model with the adjusted strangeness suppression parameters

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of strangeness production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions by using the LUCIAE event generator to interpret NA35 experimental data. The finding that strangeness suppression is reduced in nucleus-nucleus collisions compared to nucleon-nucleon collisions at the same energy is relevant for understanding whether a quark-gluon plasma or other novel state of matter is formed in these collisions. The authors note that hadronic rescattering effects must be considered before drawing conclusions about flavor symmetry from the antilambda-to-antiproton ratio.
