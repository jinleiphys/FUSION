# Translation-invariant relativistic Langevin equation derived from first principles

> The relativistic Langevin equation poses a number of technical and conceptual problems related to its derivation and underlying physical assumptions. Recently, a method has been proposed in [A. Petrosyan and A. Zaccone, J. Phys. A: Math. Theor. 55 015001 (2022)] to derive the relativistic Langevin equation from a first-principles particle-bath Lagrangian. As a result of the particle-bath coupling, a new ``restoring force'' term appeared, which breaks translation symmetry. Here we revisit this problem aiming at deriving a fully translation-invariant relativistic Langevin equation. We successfully do this by adopting the renormalization potential protocol originally suggested by Caldeira and Leggett. The relativistic renormalization potential is derived here and shown to reduce to Caldeira and Leggett's form in the non-relativistic limit. The introduction of this renormalization potential successfully removes the restoring force and a fully translation-invariant relativistic Langevin equation is derived for the first time. The physically necessary character of the renormalization potential is discussed in analogy with non-relativistic systems, where it emerges due to the renormalization of the tagged particle dynamics due to its interaction with the bath oscillators (a phenomenon akin to level-repulsion or avoided-crossing in condensed matter). We discuss the properties that the corresponding non-Markovian friction kernel has to satisfy, with implications ranging from transport models of the quark-gluon plasma, to relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations, and to electrons in graphene.

## Key claim

The paper derives a fully translation-invariant relativistic Langevin equation from a first-principles particle-bath Lagrangian by introducing a relativistic renormalization potential, which removes the "restoring force" term that broke translation symmetry in the prior derivation by Petrosyan and Zaccone (2022). The authors show that this renormalization potential reduces to the Caldeira-Leggett form in the non-relativistic limit.

## Method

The authors start from the Caldeira-Leggett particle-bath Lagrangian without a renormalized potential, derive the equations of motion for the tagged particle and the relativistic oscillator bath, and solve for the bath oscillator dynamics using a frequency renormalization and phase-drift correction approach developed in Petrosyan and Zaccone (2022). They then apply the Caldeira-Leggett renormalization protocol: starting with the non-renormalized CL model, solving for the EOMs, computing an ad-hoc potential to cancel the restoring force, adding it to the Lagrangian, and solving the new EOMs. The relativistic renormalization potential is derived and shown to successfully remove the translation-symmetry breaking term.

## Key numbers

- The condition for dropping the interaction term in the tagged particle EOM is given by the inequality: norm of position vector x is much less than sqrt(2/g_i) * c, where g_i is approximately 10^0 (order unity coupling constant)
- The frequency shift for the bath oscillators is proportional to gamma^(-3/4)(q_dot), where gamma is the Lorentz factor of the oscillator velocity
- The phase-drift correction function is given by xi(t,x) = A * t * (x - B), where A and B are parameters fitted to numerical solutions of the relativistic oscillator dynamics

## Context

This work extends the first-principles derivation of the relativistic Langevin equation by Petrosyan and Zaccone (2022), which produced a translation-symmetry breaking "restoring force" term. The authors adopt the renormalization potential protocol originally suggested by Caldeira and Leggett to remove this term, analogous to how the Caldeira-Leggett model handles translation invariance in non-relativistic systems. The resulting translation-invariant relativistic Langevin equation enables applications to transport models of the quark-gluon plasma, relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations, and electrons in graphene, where the non-Markovian friction kernel cannot be reduced to a Dirac delta.
