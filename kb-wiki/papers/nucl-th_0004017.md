# Shell model description of 16O(p,gamma)17F and 16O(p,p)16O reactions

> We present shell model calculations of both the structure of 17F and the reactions 16O(p,gamma)17F, 16O(p,p)16O. We use the ZBM interaction which provides a good description of the properties of 16O and neighbouring nuclei and, in particular it takes account for the complicated correlations in coexisting low-lying states of 16O.

## Key claim

The Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) approach, using the ZBM effective interaction that includes 2p-2h and 4p-4h correlations in the 16O ground state, can simultaneously describe the structure of 17F and the 16O(p,γ)17F and 16O(p,p)16O reactions, but the quality of the description depends critically on the realistic internal configuration mixing in the shell model wavefunctions to avoid unphysical resonant contributions from bound states at positive energies.

## Method

The authors used the SMEC formalism, which couples the Q subspace of quasi-bound many-body states (calculated with the ZBM interaction using the ANTOINE code) to the P subspace of scattering states via a density-dependent residual interaction of Landau-Migdal type. The coupled channel equations were solved self-consistently to determine the effective Hamiltonian H_QQ^eff(E), which becomes non-Hermitian above the particle emission threshold, yielding complex eigenvalues for resonance states. The resulting wavefunctions were used to calculate the astrophysical S-factor for radiative proton capture and elastic scattering phase shifts and cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- B(E2) transition between 1/2_1^+ and 5/2_1^+ bound states in 17F: 79.61 e^2fm^4 (SMEC with ZBM and DDSM0, effective charges δ=0.2), compared to experimental value 64.92 e^2fm^4
- RMS radius of the 1s_1/2 orbit in SMEC: 5.24 fm, compared to 3.03 fm in the shell model
- Zero-energy astrophysical S-factor for 16O(p,γ)17F(J^π=1/2_1^+): S(0) = 9.32×10^(-3) MeV·b, with logarithmic derivative S'(0)/S(0) = -4.86 MeV^(-1) (DDSM1 interaction)
- Zero-energy astrophysical S-factor for 16O(p,γ)17F(J^π=5/2_1^+): S(0) = 3×10^(-4) MeV·b, with S'(0)/S(0) = 0.649 MeV^(-1) (DDSM1 interaction)
- Ratio σ^E2/σ^E1 for the 1/2_1^+ branch at 20 keV: 1.622×10^(-4) (DDSM1)
- Ratio σ^M1/σ^E1 for the 1/2_1^+ branch at E_CM→0: 3.90×10^(-4) (DDSM1) and 1.087×10^(-3) (DDSM0)
- Reference Woods-Saxon potential parameters: R_0 = 3.214 fm, a = 0.58 fm, V_SO = 3.683 MeV, V_0 = -52.46 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SMEC approach previously applied in the 0p1s0d shell model space (Bennaceur et al., 2000, Nucl. Phys. A 671, 203) by incorporating the ZBM interaction, which includes the 2p-2h and 4p-4h correlations in 16O that were missing in earlier calculations. The earlier SMEC calculations using simplified wavefunctions for 16O and 17F failed to reproduce the experimental elastic cross-section at low energies and produced unphysical resonant behavior in the 5/2^+ partial wave. The present results demonstrate that the SMEC approach can simultaneously describe structure and reaction data, enabling future applications to nuclei far from stability where experimental data are limited.
