# Sum Rules for B(M1,0+ -> 1+) Strength in IBM-3 and IBM-4

> Sum rules for $B(M1,0^+_1\rightarrow1^+_i)$ strength are derived for even-even nuclei in the isospin-invariant forms of the IBM, IBM-3 and IBM-4, in the cases where the respective natural internal symmetries, isospin $U$(3) and $U(6)\supset SU$(4), are conserved. Subsequently, the total strength is resolved into its component partial sums to the allowed isospins (and $SU(4)$ representations in IBM-4). In cases where the usual IBM dynamical symmetries are also valid, a complete description of all $B(M1,0^+_1\rightarrow1^+_i)$ is given. In contrast to IBM-2, there is fragmentation of the strength even in the dynamical symmetry cases, for $T\neq 0$, over two states in IBM-3, and over three states in IBM-4. The presence of $pn$ bosons in the ground state of the extended versions reduces the expected strength from that for IBM-2, allowing in principle the possibility of using $B(M1)$ data for a given nucleus to infer which version is the most appropriate.

## Key claim

Sum rules for B(M1,0⁺→1⁺) strength are derived for even-even nuclei in the isospin-invariant IBM-3 and IBM-4, under the condition that the respective natural internal symmetries (isospin U(3) for IBM-3 and U(6)⊃SU(4) for IBM-4) are conserved. The presence of pn bosons in the ground state reduces the expected strength compared to IBM-2, allowing in principle to infer which version is most appropriate from B(M1) data, but the authors note that undetected strength, SU(4) breaking, or g-boson extensions could complicate this inference.

## Method

The authors derived sum rules by equating the summed B(M1) strength to a ground-state expectation value of the magnetic dipole operator squared, then evaluated this expectation value for fully symmetric ground states in IBM-3 and IBM-4 using boson quasispin SU(1,1) techniques and analytic continuations of SU(2) 3-j symbols. They resolved the total strength into partial sums to allowed isospins (IBM-3) and SU(4) representations (IBM-4) using group coupling coefficients, and provided closed-form expressions for the dynamical symmetry limits (U(5), SO(6), SU(3)) by substituting known analytic values for ⟨n_d⟩.

## Key numbers

- IBM-3 total sum rule: ∑_i B(M1) = (9/8π) g_{l1}² ⟨n_d⟩ (N−T)(2N(T+1)+T(2T+3)) / [(N−1)N(2T+3)]
- IBM-4 total sum rule: ∑_i B(M1) = (9/8π) g_{l1}² ⟨n_d⟩ (N−T)(N(T+1)+T(T+3)) / [(N−1)N(T+3)]
- IBM-2 sum rule (Ginocchio, prior work): ∑_i B(M1) = (9/4π) g_{l1}² ⟨n_d⟩ 2N_π N_ν / [N(N−1)]
- For IBM-3, strength fragments over two states when T≠0; for IBM-4, over three states when T≠0; only one transition when T=0
- In dynamical symmetries: U(5) gives ⟨n_d⟩ = 0; SO(6) gives ⟨n_d⟩ = N(N−1)/[2(N+1)]; SU(3) gives ⟨n_d⟩ = 4N(N−1)/[3(2N−1)]

## Context

This work extends the IBM-2 M1 sum rule derived by Ginocchio (prior work) to the isospin-invariant IBM-3 and IBM-4, which include pn bosons (δ and σ bosons) that are absent in IBM-2. The results provide benchmark values for the dynamical symmetry limits (U(5), SO(6), SU(3)) that have often proved useful in IBM work. The paper enables future studies to use B(M1) data to test which version of the IBM is most appropriate for a given nucleus, though the authors caution that undetected strength, SU(4) breaking, or g-boson extensions (prior work) could complicate such inferences.
