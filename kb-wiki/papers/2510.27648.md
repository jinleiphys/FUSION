# Beyond Leading Logarithms in $g_V$: The Semileptonic Weak Hamiltonian at $\mathcal{O}(α\,α_s^2)$

> We present the first next-to-leading-logarithmic QCD analysis of the electromagnetic corrections to the semileptonic weak Hamiltonian, including the mixed $\mathcal{O}(α\,α_s^2)$ corrections to the vector coupling $g_V$. The analysis combines the evaluation of three-loop anomalous dimensions and two-loop matching corrections with a consistent factorization of short-distance QCD effects. The latter is implemented through a scheme change based on a $d$-dimensional operator product expansion performed inside the loop integrals. The resulting renormalization-group--improved expression for the radiative correction $Δ^V_R = 2.436(16)\%$ can be systematically refined using input from lattice QCD and perturbation theory and improves the consistency of first-row CKM unitarity tests.

# Beyond Leading Logarithms in $g_V$: The Semileptonic Weak Hamiltonian at $\mathcal{O}(\alpha\,\alpha_s^2)$

## Key claim

The paper presents the first next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) QCD analysis of electromagnetic corrections to the semileptonic weak Hamiltonian, including mixed $\mathcal{O}(\alpha\,\alpha_s^2)$ corrections to the vector coupling $g_V$, but the analysis is restricted to the isoscalar contribution (proportional to $Q_+ = Q_d + Q_u$) which is the only component that receives QCD corrections; the nonisoscalar contributions are matched directly onto chiral perturbation theory without NLL QCD improvement.

## Method

The authors combine three-loop anomalous dimensions and two-loop matching corrections for the weak effective theory operator $O = (\bar{d}\gamma^{\mu}P_{L}u)(\bar{\nu}_{l}\gamma_{\mu}P_{L}l)$, using an intermediate $\mu_0$-subtracted renormalization scheme based on a $d$-dimensional operator product expansion performed inside loop integrals to factorize short-distance QCD effects. The matching to Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory is achieved through a scheme change that cancels the $\log \mu_{\chi}$ dependence, while the long-distance isoscalar contribution $\overline{\Box}^V_{\rm Had}(\mu_0)$ is expressed as an integral over the Nachtmann moment split into low-$Q^2$ (dispersive or lattice input) and high-$Q^2$ (perturbative DIS) regions.

## Key numbers

- The radiative correction $\Delta^V_R = 2.436(16)\%$ at NLL QCD order, combining the $\mu_0$-subtracted scheme with lattice input $\Box^{V<}_{\gamma W}(Q_0^2) = 1.490(73)\cdot 10^{-3}$ from Ma (2023)
- The NLL result for the vector coupling: $(g_V - 1) \cdot 10^6 = 11355^{+24+74}_{-23-74}$, where the first uncertainty is from scale variation and the second from lattice input
- The LL result for comparison: $(g_V - 1) \cdot 10^6 = 11426^{+60}_{-57}$
- Extraction of $|V_{ud}|$ from superallowed nuclear beta decays: $|V_{ud}|^{0^+\to 0^+}_{\rm NLLs} = 0.97382(30)$
- Extraction of $|V_{ud}|$ from neutron decay: $|V_{ud}|^{n}_{\rm NLLs} = 0.97410(41)$
- The $\mathcal{O}(\alpha\,\alpha_s^2)$ anomalous dimension coefficient for the isoscalar contribution: $[a (308/9 - 56 n_f/27) + 10 n_f/9 - 33] \alpha \alpha_s^2(\mu)/(4\pi)^3$, where $a$ is the evanescent scheme parameter

## Context

This work extends the leading-order QED analysis of Cirigliano (2023) by incorporating NLL QCD corrections to the electromagnetic effects in semileptonic weak decays, and provides the two-loop $\mathcal{O}(\alpha\alpha_s)$ matching coefficients that were previously unknown (only the one-loop $\mathcal{O}(\alpha)$ Wilson coefficient from Gambino (2001) and Brod (2008) was available). The framework enables systematic refinement using lattice QCD input from Ma (2023) and reduces the theoretical uncertainty in $\Delta^V_R$, thereby improving the consistency of first-row CKM unitarity tests that currently show a 2-3 sigma tension with the world averages from the Particle Data Group (2024).
