# Uncertainty Principle and Angular Momentum Generation in Microscopic Fission Models

> The generation of angular momentum (intrinsic spin) in fission fragments has recently attracted renewed attention. While several microscopic approaches reproduce the spin distribution qualitatively using projection techniques, the physical origin of the fragments' angular momentum in density functional theory remains unclear. In this work, we investigate the mechanisms responsible for the spin distribution of fission fragments within a microscopic TDDFT framework. We compare spin distributions obtained from projection operators with those predicted by a simple expression derived from the uncertainty relation between angle and angular momentum, where angular fluctuations are estimated using a Monte Carlo sampling of nucleon positions. We find that a large portion of the spin distribution obtained from projection methods can be explained by the uncertainty principle. Our results thus show that, within microscopic approaches, the spin of fission fragments originates primarily from quantum uncertainty associated with their orientation angle with respect to the fission axis, mainly due to quadrupole deformation and, to a lesser extent, octupole deformation.

# Uncertainty Principle and Angular Momentum Generation in Microscopic Fission Models

## Key claim
Within a time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) framework, a large portion of the spin distribution of fission fragments obtained from exact projection methods can be explained by the uncertainty principle relating angular orientation and angular momentum. However, the spin cut-off parameter deduced from angular fluctuations of the principal axis underestimates the full spin obtained from projection, indicating that additional mechanisms (such as octupole deformation and intrinsic spin fluctuations) contribute beyond the simple quadrupole-deformation picture.

## Method
The authors used the Gogny-TDHFB code with the Gogny D1S interaction in a hybrid basis (two-dimensional harmonic oscillator eigenfunctions plus one-dimensional spatial mesh along the fission axis) to simulate fission of 230Th, 240Pu, and 250Cf. They estimated angular fluctuations of fragment orientation by sampling nucleon positions from the many-body density using a Markov Chain Monte Carlo algorithm (code NucleoScope), then applied the angle-angular momentum uncertainty relation to predict spin cut-off parameters. These predictions were compared to spin distributions obtained from exact angular momentum projection operators using the Pfaffian method and three-dimensional grid rotations.

## Context
This work extends the macroscopic-quantal picture of fragment spin generation proposed by Mikhailov (1999), Bonneau (2007), and Scamps (2022, 2023), and the collective Hamiltonian approach of Shneidman (2002, 2025). It provides a microscopic foundation within TDDFT for the connection between fragment deformation and spin that was previously discussed only qualitatively in projection-based studies such as those by Bertsch (2019), Marevic (2021), and Bulgac (2021). The results enable a clearer interpretation of the saw-tooth pattern of fragment spins observed experimentally by Wilson (2021) and suggest that static constraint-based calculations would yield similar qualitative features, with dynamics primarily serving to excite fragments and break nucleon pairs.
