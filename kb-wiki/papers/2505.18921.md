# Microscopic constraints for the equation of state and structure of neutron stars: a Bayesian model mixing framework

> Bayesian model mixing (BMM) is a statistical technique that can combine constraints from different regions of an input space in a principled way. Here we extend our BMM framework for the equation of state (EOS) of strongly interacting matter from symmetric nuclear matter to asymmetric matter, specifically focusing on zero-temperature, charge-neutral, $β$-equilibrated matter. We use Gaussian processes (GPs) to infer constraints on the neutron star matter EOS at intermediate densities from two different microscopic theories: chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) at baryon densities around nuclear saturation, $n_B \sim n_0$, and perturbative QCD at asymptotically high baryon densities, $n_B \geqslant 20 n_0$. The uncertainties of the $χ$EFT and pQCD EOSs are obtained using the BUQEYE truncation error model. We demonstrate the flexibility of our framework through the use of two categories of GP kernels: conventional stationary kernels and a non-stationary changepoint kernel. We use the latter to explore potential constraints on the dense matter EOS by including exogenous data representing theory predictions and heavy-ion collision measurements at densities $\geqslant 2n_0$. We also use our EOSs to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and their uncertainties. Our framework, whose implementation will be available through a GitHub repository, provides a prior distribution for the EOS that can be used in large-scale neutron-star inference frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper extends a Bayesian model mixing (BMM) framework from symmetric nuclear matter to charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated neutron star matter, using Gaussian processes to combine constraints from chiral effective field theory at low densities and perturbative QCD at high densities, with the caveat that the resulting equation of state is conditional on the chosen model-space prior and kernel assumptions.

## Method

The authors construct a Gaussian process (GP) that interpolates between the chiral EFT equation of state at baryon densities around nuclear saturation (n_B ~ n_0) and the perturbative QCD equation of state at asymptotically high densities (n_B >= 20 n_0), using the BUQEYE truncation error model to quantify uncertainties in both regimes. They explore two categories of GP kernels: conventional stationary kernels and a non-stationary changepoint kernel that allows different covariance structures in different density regions, and they update the changepoint kernel with exogenous data representing theory predictions and heavy-ion collision measurements at densities >= 2 n_0. The resulting equations of state are propagated to neutron star mass-radius relations by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 ≈ 0.164 fm^{-3} (from Drischler (2024))
- Chiral EFT is used at baryon densities ≤ 2 n_0
- Perturbative QCD is used at baryon densities ≥ 20 n_0
- The beta-equilibrium proton fraction is < 8% for n_B ≤ 2 n_0

## Context

This work extends the BMM framework developed in Semposki (2024) (denoted Paper I) from symmetric nuclear matter to asymmetric, charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated neutron star matter, and expands the kernel choices beyond the stationary squared-exponential radial basis function kernel used in that prior work. The framework provides a prior distribution for the equation of state that can be used in large-scale neutron-star inference frameworks, and the implementation is made available through a GitHub repository.
