# Light clusters production as a probe to the nuclear symmetry energy

> Using an isospin-dependent transport model for heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei at intermediate energies, we study the production of light clusters such as deuteron, triton, and $^{3}$He via coalescence of nucleons. We find that both the yields and energy spectra of these light clusters are affected significantly by the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, with a stiffer symmetry energy giving a larger yield.

## Key claim

Using an isospin-dependent transport model with a coalescence model for light cluster production, the authors find that both the yields and energy spectra of deuterons, tritons, and \({}^{3}\)He in heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei are significantly affected by the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, with a stiffer symmetry energy giving a larger yield; this effect is particularly large for clusters with lower kinetic energies, and the isobaric ratio t/\({}^{3}\)He shows a strong sensitivity to the symmetry energy, especially for higher energy clusters.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate central collisions of \({}^{52}\)Ca + \({}^{48}\)Ca at 80 MeV/nucleon, with nucleon freeze-out defined when local densities fall below \(\rho_0/8\). Light clusters (deuteron, triton, \({}^{3}\)He) were produced via a coalescence model using Wigner phase-space densities derived from the Hulthén wave function for deuterons and harmonic oscillator wave functions for triton and \({}^{3}\)He, with parameters adjusted to reproduce measured root-mean-square radii. The density dependence of the symmetry energy was parameterized as \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho) = 30\,\mathrm{MeV} \cdot u^{\gamma}\) with \(\gamma = 0.5\) (soft) and \(\gamma = 2\) (stiff), and the sensitivity to the isoscalar equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections was tested by varying the nuclear incompressibility from \(K_0 = 380\) MeV to 201 MeV and using Dirac-Brueckner parameterized cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Final multiplicities of deuteron, triton, and \({}^{3}\)He for the stiff symmetry energy are larger than those for the soft symmetry energy by 51%, 73%, and 100%, respectively, for central collisions of \({}^{52}\)Ca + \({}^{48}\)Ca at 80 MeV/nucleon.
- The symmetry energy effect on the yield of deuteron, triton, and \({}^{3}\)He is about 60%, 100%, and 120%, respectively, for clusters with kinetic energies around 10 MeV, but about 30%, 40%, and 85%, respectively, for clusters with kinetic energies around 100 MeV.
- The t/\({}^{3}\)He ratio increases with kinetic energy for the soft symmetry energy and decreases with kinetic energy for the stiff symmetry energy; the ratio is larger than the N/Z ratio of the reaction system (N/Z = 1.5) for both cases.
- The deuteron root-mean-square radius used in the Wigner function is 1.96 fm; the root-mean-square radii for triton and \({}^{3}\)He are 1.61 fm and 1.74 fm, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used the neutron/proton ratio (Li 1997), isospin fractionation (Xu 2000, Tan 2001), isoscaling (Betty), proton differential elliptic flow (Li 2002), neutron-proton differential transverse flow (Li 2000), the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio (Li 2002), and two-nucleon correlation functions (Chen 2002) as probes of the nuclear symmetry energy. It also contrasts with earlier studies of cluster production using the lattice gas model (Chomaz 1999) or a hybrid of IBUU and statistical fragmentation model (Tan 2001), which were at lower energies where multifragmentation may play a role. The results enable the use of light cluster yields and the t/\({}^{3}\)He ratio as a new, sensitive probe of the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, particularly at future Rare Isotope Accelerator facilities.
