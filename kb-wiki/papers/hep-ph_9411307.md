# Bose-Einstein Correlations for Systems with Large Halo

> Model-independent considerations are presented for the calculation of Bose-Einstein correlation functions and momentum distributions which describe boson-emitting systems containing a central part surrounded by a large halo. If the characteristic geometrical size of the halo is sufficiently large, the contributions of central part and the halo to the invariant momentum distribution are shown to be separable. The momentum-dependence of the intercept parameter of the correlation function plays a central role. Almost all high energy reactions including lepton-lepton, lepton hadron, hadron-hadron and nuclear reactions are shown to be interpretable as boson emitting systems with large halo. The results are applied to certain high energy heavy ion data at CERN SPS. New insights are obtained for the description of low transverse momentum enhancement of pions.

## Key claim

If the characteristic size of the halo surrounding a boson-emitting source is sufficiently large (greater than the maximum length-scale resolvable by the intensity interferometry measurement, approximately 20 fm), the contributions of the central core and the halo to the invariant momentum distribution become separable, and the only effect of the halo on the measured Bose-Einstein correlation function is a momentum-dependent reduction of the intercept parameter.

## Method

The authors model the boson-emitting system as a sum of two independent, completely chaotic emission functions: a compact central core (resolvable by HBT measurements) and an extended halo (unresolvable because its Fourier transform vanishes for relative momenta above the experimental resolution threshold, taken as Q_min ≈ 10 MeV/c). Using the Wigner-function formalism, they derive expressions for the measured two-particle correlation function and the invariant momentum distribution, showing that the effective intercept parameter λ_*(p) equals the square of the fraction of core particles at momentum p. They apply this formalism to NA44 data for central S+Pb collisions at 200 AGeV/c at CERN SPS.

## Key numbers

- The halo length-scales are larger than 20 fm, giving a sharp peak in the correlation function in the Q ≤ ℏ/R ≈ 10 MeV/c region.
- For the ω resonance: 1/Γ_ω = 23.5 fm/c; for η′: 1/Γ_η′ = 986.5 fm/c; for η: 1/Γ_η = 164400 fm/c; for K_s^0: 1/Γ_Ks = 2.76 cm/c.
- For short-lived resonances: 1/Γ_ρ ≈ 1.3 fm/c, 1/Γ_N* ≈ 0.56 fm/c, 1/Γ_Δ ≈ 1.6 fm/c, 1/Γ_K* ≈ 3.9 fm/c.
- NA44 data for central S+Pb at 200 AGeV/c show λ_π+ = 0.56 ± 0.02 at mean transverse momentum 150 MeV and 0.55 ± 0.02 at 450 MeV.
- The halo contains 1 − √λ_* = 25 ± 2% of all pions within the NA44 acceptance.
- The effective volume factor from the core scales as V_* ∝ (m_t)^(−3/2), giving a factor of 10 enhancement of low-p_t pions from the core compared to low-p_t heavy resonances.

## Context

This work extends the core/halo picture previously discussed in halo1, halo2, halo3, and halo4 (1994 references) and in the Wigner-function formalism of Pratt and Csorgo (1990) and Zajc (1987). It provides a model-independent derivation showing that the phenomenological parametrization of Bose-Einstein correlation functions with a reduced intercept parameter (often criticized as a fudge factor) follows naturally from quantum statistics when a large halo is present. The results enable separation of core and halo contributions to the invariant momentum distribution using combined measurements of the correlation function intercept and the single-particle spectrum, and they explain the observed constancy of λ_* with transverse mass in NA44 data as requiring a hydrodynamic enhancement of low-p_t pions in the core, rather than a dominant halo contribution at low p_t as predicted by earlier calculations such as SPACER (Lorstad, 1994), RQMD (Sorge et al., 1994), HYLANDER (halo2, 1994), and the Regensburg hydrodynamic calculations (uli_h, 1994).
