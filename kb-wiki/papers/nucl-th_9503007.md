# Short-range correlations in (e,e$'$p) and (e,e$'$pp) reactions on complex nuclei

> The influence of short-range correlations (SRC) on the triple-coincidence (e,e$'$pp) reactions is studied. The non-relativistic model uses a mean-field potential to account for the distortions that the escaping particles undergo. Apart from the SRC, that are implemented through a Jastrow ansatz with a realistic correlation function, we incorporate the contribution from pion exchange and intermediate $Δ_{33}$ currents. The (e,e$'$pp) cross sections are predicted to exhibit a sizeable sensitivity to the SRC. The contribution from the two-nucleon breakup channel to the semi-exclusive $^{12}$C(e,e$'$p) cross section is calculated in the kinematics of a recent NIKHEF-K experiment. In the semi-exclusive channel, a selective sensitivity in terms of the missing energy and momentum to the SRC is found.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) produce a sizeable but not dominant effect on (e,e'pp) cross sections, increasing them by less than a factor of two in the peak, and the sensitivity is highly dependent on the choice of correlation function; in semi-exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, SRC contributions are significant only at forward proton angles, while at backward angles the cross section is dominated by pion-exchange and isobaric currents.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic model with a Jastrow-type central correlation function (Gaussian parametrization from Fermi Hypernetted Chain calculations) to incorporate short-range correlations into the nuclear wave functions, and treat final-state interactions via a mean-field potential for the escaping particles. They coherently add the SRC contribution to two-body currents from pion exchange and intermediate Δ₃₃ excitation, and compute cross sections for both triple-coincidence (e,e'pp) and semi-exclusive (e,e'p) reactions on ¹²C using a first-order cluster expansion of the effective transition Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C(e,e'pp)¹⁰Be in coplanar symmetrical kinematics, the SRC effect increases the cross section by less than a factor of two in the peak, both at longitudinal polarization ε_L = 0.30 and ε_L = 0.85.
- The Gaussian correlation function for ¹⁶O has parameters α = 0.51 and β = 1.52 fm⁻², obtained from FHNC calculations.
- The πNN form factor uses a monopole cutoff mass Λ_π = 1250 MeV/c².
- For the semi-exclusive ¹²C(e,e'p) data at beam energy ε = 478 MeV, energy transfer ω = 212 MeV, and momentum transfer q = 270 MeV/c, the TNC model predicts maximized SRC likelihood at missing energies: E_m ≈ 85 MeV (θ_p = 27°), E_m ≈ 95 MeV (θ_p = 42°), E_m ≈ 130 MeV (θ_p = 74°), E_m ≈ 155 MeV (θ_p = 107°), E_m ≈ 165 MeV (θ_p = 131°), and E_m ≈ 170 MeV (θ_p = 162°).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of short-range correlations in photon-induced two-nucleon knockout (Ryckebusch 1994) to virtual photon (e,e'NN) reactions, where the longitudinal component provides cleaner access to SRC than real-photon reactions dominated by pion exchange. The results contradict the much larger SRC effects predicted by Giusti et al. (1992, 1993) using the "omy" hard-core correlation function (Clark 1974), which the authors show is unrealistically strong compared to modern Variational Monte Carlo and FHNC calculations. The semi-exclusive analysis enables future experiments to distinguish between SRC and meson-exchange contributions by comparing forward and backward proton angle data, and confirms the kinematical predictions of the two-nucleon correlation model of Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1991, 1993).
