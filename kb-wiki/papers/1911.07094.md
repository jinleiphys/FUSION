# QGP droplet formation in small asymmetric collision systems

> The journal Nature recently published a letter titled "Creating small circular, elliptical, and triangular droplets of quark-gluon plasma" [1]. The basis for that claim is a combination of measured Fourier amplitudes $v_2$ and $v_3$ from collision systems $p$-Au, $d$-Au and $h$-Au (helion $h$ is the nucleus of atom $^3$He), Glauber Monte Carlo estimates of initial-state transverse collision geometries for those systems and hydro Monte Carlo descriptions of the $v_n$ data. Correspondence between hydro $v_n$ trends and data trends is interpreted as confirmation of "collectivity" occurring in the small collision systems, further interpreted to indicate QGP formation. QGP formation in small systems runs counter to pre-RHIC theoretical assumptions that QGP formation should require large collision systems (e.g. central A-A collisions). There is currently available a broad context of experimental data from $p$-$p$, A-A and $p$-Pb collisions at the RHIC and LHC against which the validity of the Nature letter claims may be evaluated. This talk provides a summary of such results and their implications. [1] Nature Phys. 15, no. 3, 214 (2019).}

## Key claim

The claim that small asymmetric collision systems (p-Au, d-Au, h-Au) produce QGP droplets is based on five critical assumptions about hydrodynamical modeling and data interpretation that are contradicted by a broad array of published experimental data from p-p, A-A, and p-Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Method

The author confronts each of five assumptions underlying the Nature letter's QGP droplet claim with evidence from published experimental data analyzed using the two-component (soft + hard) model (TCM) of hadron production. The analysis examines PID v2(p_t) spectra, centrality trends for jet quenching and v2 from 200 GeV Au-Au 2D angular correlations, ensemble mean-p_t data from 5 TeV p-Pb collisions, proton and K0_S p_t spectra from Au-Au and p-Pb collisions, and 2D angular correlations from p-p and Au-Au collisions to test whether hydrodynamical descriptions correspond to actual flow mechanisms or instead accommodate jet-related correlation structures.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole spectra for pions, kaons, and Lambdas from 200 GeV Au-Au collisions fall on a single Levy distribution with slope parameter T = 92 MeV after transformation assuming a fixed boost Delta y_t0 = 0.6 from lab to boost frame
- The nonjet quadrupole amplitude in 200 GeV p-p collisions increases proportional to rho_s^3 (soft component cubed) over a thousand-fold amplitude increase
- Jet modification in 200 GeV Au-Au shows a sharp transition near 50% centrality, while the nonjet quadrupole trend shows no deviation from a fixed trend from peripheral to central collisions
- MC Glauber estimates without exclusivity fail to describe ensemble mean-p_t data from 5 TeV p-Pb collisions, while TCM estimates describe the data accurately
- Hydro theory for 0-5% central 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions shows large discrepancy with pion spectrum data below y_t = 2 (p_t = 0.5 GeV/c), falsifying the hydro model

## Context

This work extends the author's previous challenges to QGP interpretations (Trainor, various years) and directly responds to the Nature letter by Acharya et al. (Nature Phys. 15, no. 3, 214, 2019) claiming QGP droplet formation in small systems. The analysis contradicts the conventional interpretation that Fourier amplitudes v_n measure collective flow and that hydrodynamical models confirm QGP formation, instead arguing that the two-component model provides an alternative description consistent with basic QCD and jet measurements without requiring exceptional mechanisms such as flows or jet quenching. The work enables quantitative assessment of jet modification in A-A collisions by providing a stable TCM reference against which deviations from linear superposition can be measured.
