# Observing light-by-light scattering at the Large Hadron Collider

> Elastic light-by-light scattering, $γγ\toγγ$, is open to study at the Large Hadron Collider thanks to the large quasi-real photon fluxes available in electromagnetic interactions of protons (p) and lead (Pb) ions. The $γγ\toγγ$ cross sections for diphoton masses $m_{γγ} > 5$ GeV amount to 105 fb, 260 pb, and 370 nb in p-p, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 14 TeV, 8.8 TeV, and 5.5 TeV respectively. Such a measurement has no substantial backgrounds in Pb-Pb collisions where one expects about 70 signal events per run, after typical detector acceptance and reconstruction efficiency selections.

## Key claim

Elastic light-by-light scattering ($\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma$) can be observed at the LHC using the large quasi-real photon fluxes in electromagnetic interactions of protons and lead ions, but only in Pb-Pb collisions is the signal unambiguous because the dominant background from central exclusive production via gluon exchange can be reduced to a controllable level; in p-p collisions the signal is swamped by the gluon-induced background even after tight kinematic cuts.

## Method

The authors calculate the exclusive $\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma$ cross section by factorizing the process into the elementary QED cross section (including all quark and lepton loops at leading order) convoluted with equivalent photon approximation (EPA) spectra from the colliding beams, using the MadGraph v.5 Monte Carlo framework. For protons, the photon spectrum is obtained from the elastic form factor; for lead ions, an impact-parameter dependent expression is integrated with a minimum impact parameter equal to the nuclear radius to ensure no hadronic overlap. The expected event yields are then estimated by applying detector acceptance (both photons with $|\eta^\gamma| < 2.5$ and $p_T^\gamma > 2$ GeV), reconstruction efficiencies (80% per photon), and trigger conditions to the cross sections multiplied by the integrated luminosities for each collision system.

## Key numbers

- Exclusive $\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma$ cross section for diphoton masses $m_{\gamma\gamma} > 5$ GeV in p-p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 14$ TeV: $105 \pm 10$ fb
- Exclusive $\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma$ cross section for diphoton masses $m_{\gamma\gamma} > 5$ GeV in p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.8$ TeV: $260 \pm 26$ pb
- Exclusive $\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma$ cross section for diphoton masses $m_{\gamma\gamma} > 5$ GeV in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV: $370 \pm 70$ nb
- Expected number of signal events per year after acceptance and efficiency cuts: 12 in p-p (with 1 fb$^{-1}$ at low pileup), 6 in p-Pb (with 200 nb$^{-1}$), and 70 in Pb-Pb (with 1 nb$^{-1}$)
- The central exclusive production (CEP) $gg\to\gamma\gamma$ background in p-p at 14 TeV after acceptance cuts: $\sigma_{gg\to\gamma\gamma}^{\rm CEP} = 20^{\times 3}_{\times 1/3}$ pb
- The QED exclusive $\gamma\gamma\to e^+e^-$ cross section in Pb-Pb for $m_{ee} > 5$ GeV: 5.4 mb

## Context

This work extends the possibility of directly observing light-by-light scattering, which has never been directly detected despite being tested indirectly through the anomalous magnetic moments of the electron and muon. The authors build on prior studies of ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC (Baltz 2007, d'Enterria 2008) and central exclusive production calculations (Khoze 2004), and provide the first calculations of the $\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma$ cross sections for p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions. The measurement in Pb-Pb collisions would constitute the first direct observation of this fundamental quantum electrodynamic process and could be sensitive to new physics effects such as anomalous gauge couplings, charged supersymmetric partners, monopoles, unparticles, low-scale gravity, and non-commutative interactions.
