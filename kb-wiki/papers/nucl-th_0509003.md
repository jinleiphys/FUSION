# X(5) Critical-Point Structure in a Finite System

> X(5) is a paradigm for the structure at the critical point of a particular first-order phase transition for which the intrinsic energy surface has two degenerate minima separated by a low barrier. For a finite system, we show that the dynamics at such a critical point can be described by an effective deformation determined by minimizing the energy surface after projection onto angular momentum zero, and combined with two-level mixing. Wave functions of a particular analytic form are used to derive estimates for energies and quadrupole rates at the critical point.

# X(5) Critical-Point Structure in a Finite System

## Key claim

For a finite system at the X(5) critical point of a first-order shape-phase transition, the dynamics can be described by an effective deformation determined by minimizing the energy surface after projection onto angular momentum zero and combined with two-level mixing, rather than by the infinite square-well potential used in the original geometric X(5) model. The procedure provides faithful estimates to exact finite-N calculations of the critical IBM Hamiltonian, but the agreement for the non-yrast 0⁺₂ state is less precise (81.9% overlap) because it is affected by additional states beyond the two-state mixing considered.

## Method

The study uses the interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian for the U(5)-SU(3) transition at the critical point, with parameters ε = (9/4)κ(2N-3). L-projected states of good O(3) symmetry are constructed from the intrinsic condensate |β,γ=0;N⟩, and an effective β-deformation is determined by minimizing the lowest eigenpotential obtained from a 2×2 mixing matrix between the spherical |s^N⟩ state and the L=0 projected deformed state. Energies and B(E2) values are then evaluated using these projected states at the equilibrium β value.

## Key numbers

- The critical Hamiltonian parameters satisfy ε = (9/4)κ(2N-3)
- For N=10, the minimum of the lowest eigenpotential E⁽⁻⁾_{L=0}(β) occurs at β = 0.591, compared to β = 0.528 for the unmixed L=0 projected energy surface and β = 0.619 for the L=2 projected energy surface
- Overlaps between calculated and exact eigenstates for N=10: 99.6% for 0⁺₁, 99.1% for 2⁺₁, 98.0% for 4⁺₁, 97.0% for 8⁺₁, 96.6% for 10⁺₁, and 81.9% for 0⁺₂
- The intrinsic energy surface has degenerate minima at β=0 and at (β=1/(2√2), γ=0)

## Context

This work extends the geometric X(5) benchmark of Iachello (2001) to finite systems by incorporating the effects of a finite number of bosons N, building on earlier finite-N studies of phase transitions in the IBM by Iachello, Zamfir, Arias, and others. The approach uses L-projection and two-level mixing, similar in spirit to the matrix coherent-state method of Frank et al. (2004), but with states of good angular momentum and fixed N. The results are directly applicable to nuclei such as ¹⁵²Sm, ¹⁵⁰Nd, ¹⁵⁶Dy, and ¹⁵⁴Gd where X(5) behavior has been observed, and the same method can be extended to the coexistence region between the spinodal and antispinodal points, as well as to other mesoscopic systems like polyatomic molecules described by the vibron model.
