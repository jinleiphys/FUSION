# Functional reduction of the S matrix in the canonical formalism

> The Low equation is derived in a functional approach to the reduction of the S matrix in the canonical formalism. This establishes the vacuum expectation value of the scattering matrix as the generating functional of non-forward Green functions, without reference to external currents. The method provides an alternate derivation of non-perturbative results of field theory, such as the Low equation, and considerably simplifies their derivation as well as that of the rules of perturbation theory, the LSZ reduction formula, the Dyson-Schwinger equations and crossing symmetry. The approach is employed to further develop the Low equation via reduction of the fermionic sector to obtain a reduced Dyson-Schwinger equation for boson-fermion scattering.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the vacuum expectation value of the S matrix serves as the generating functional for non-forward Green functions within the canonical formalism, without introducing external currents or classical fields, thereby providing an alternative to the path integral approach for deriving non-perturbative results.

## Method

The author replaces the annihilation operator algebra of canonical field theory with a functional calculus, using the isomorphism between commutators and functional derivatives expressed as \(\comm{F[a,a^\dag]}{a^\dag_i(q)} = \delta F[a,a^\dag] / \delta a_i(q)\). This functional reduction is applied to the S matrix in the Dirac (interaction) representation, specifically to derive the Low equation for meson-nucleon scattering by evaluating functional derivatives of the S matrix with respect to bosonic annihilation operators and their adjoints. The method is then extended to the fermionic sector to obtain a reduced Dyson-Schwinger equation for boson-fermion scattering.

## Key numbers

* The meson wave function normalization includes the factor \((2\pi)^{-3/2}(2\omega_q)^{-1/2}\) in the relation \(\delta \phi_i(x) / \delta a_j(q) = \delta_{ji} e^{-iq\cdot x} / [(2\pi)^{3/2}(2\omega_q)^{1/2}]\).
* The interaction Hamiltonian density includes a quartic coupling \(\frac{1}{4} \lambda [\phi_i(x) \phi_i(x)]^2\), a mass counterterm \(-\frac{1}{2} \delta\mu^2 \phi_i(x) \phi_i(x)\), and a fermion mass counterterm \(-\delta m \bar{\psi}(x)\psi(x)\).

## Context

This work extends the functional-derivative representation of the field commutator previously considered by Schweber (1955) in the Kallen-Yang-Feldman formalism, and is related to functional S-matrix studies by Rzewuski (1970) and Garbaczewski (1974) on convergence and asymptotic behavior, as well as Efimov (1975) on S-matrix unitarity. The approach simplifies derivations of the Low equation (Low, 1955), the LSZ reduction formula, the Dyson-Schwinger equations, and crossing symmetry, and enables further development of the Low equation through reduction of the fermionic sector for boson-fermion scattering.
