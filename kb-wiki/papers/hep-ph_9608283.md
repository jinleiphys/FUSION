# Binding of Quarks and the pi-N sigma-Term

> It is shown that the binding effect that is associated with the short range part of the Goldstone boson exchange interaction between constituent quarks provides a good description of the $πN$ $σ$-term.

## Key claim
The short-range part of the Goldstone boson exchange interaction between constituent quarks, which provides the binding of quarks in the nucleon, yields a pion-nucleon sigma-term of about 50 MeV, in good agreement with the empirical value of 45 ± 10 MeV. The result depends on the product of the average current quark mass and the derivative of constituent quark masses with respect to current masses, so the specific value of 7 MeV for the average current quark mass is not uniquely determined.

## Method
The author applies the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to the nucleon mass expressed as a sum of constituent quark masses, kinetic energy, confining interaction, and Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) interaction. The derivatives of the kinetic and GBE terms with respect to current quark masses are evaluated using their known dependence on constituent quark mass (m^{-1} and m^{-2} respectively), while the confining term is assumed independent of current quark masses. Numerical expectation values for the kinetic and GBE contributions are taken from three-body Faddeev calculations in prior work (Glozman et al., GPP).

## Key numbers
- Empirical pion-nucleon sigma-term: σ_{πN} = 45 ± 10 MeV, extracted from pion-nucleon scattering data (SA).
- Calculated sigma-term: σ_{πN} ≃ 50.1 MeV, obtained using average current quark mass m̂⁰ = 7 MeV and constituent quark mass m = 340 MeV.
- Expectation values from Faddeev calculations: ⟨N|H_kin|N⟩ = 844 MeV, ⟨N|H_χ|N⟩ = -1130 MeV, ⟨N|H_conf|N⟩ = 204 MeV.
- Lattice QCD result cited for comparison: σ_{πN} = 47-53 MeV (Liu).

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model with chiral dynamics (Glozman 1996, Glozman et al. GPP) by showing that the binding effect from Goldstone boson exchange, not just the additive quark ansatz, is essential for reproducing the pion-nucleon sigma-term. Prior approaches such as the naive constituent quark model and the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Bernard 1988, Vogl 1991) gave much smaller values, indicating missing physics. The result enables a consistent description of both baryon spectra and the sigma-term within the same framework, and is compatible with lattice QCD calculations (Liu).
