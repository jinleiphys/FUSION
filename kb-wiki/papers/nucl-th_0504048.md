# Short-range correlations in two-nucleon knockout reactions

> A theory of short-range correlations in two-nucleon removal due to elastic breakup (diffraction dissociation) on a light target is developed. Fingerprints of these correlations will appear in momentum distributions of back-to-back emission of the nucleon pair. Expressions for the momentum distributions are derived and calculations for reactions involving stable and unstable nuclear species are performed. The signature of short-range correlations in other reaction processes is also studied.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations between nucleons can be identified in two-nucleon knockout reactions through back-to-back emission patterns in momentum distributions, but the cross sections predicted by Feshbach and Zabek (1977) are unrealistically large (approximately 1 barn for oxygen-oxygen collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon) because they did not properly account for strong absorption at small impact parameters.

## Method

The author develops a Glauber multiple-scattering formalism for diffraction dissociation (elastic breakup) of a projectile into a core plus two correlated nucleons on a light target. The cross section is derived using eikonal wavefunctions with S-matrices for the core and each nucleon, incorporating absorption through the imaginary part of the optical potential. The formalism is applied to two-nucleon removal from a three-body projectile (core plus correlated pair) using a Jastrow-type correlation function that vanishes as the two-particle correlation distance approaches zero.

## Key numbers

- For light nuclei, the grazing impact parameter window has width approximately 1-2 fm, yielding a geometric cross section factor of roughly 300-600 mb
- The effective potential felt by a nucleon in the middle of a nucleus is only about 40-50 MeV deep, compared to a cumulative sum of approximately 1400 MeV from 16 nucleon-nucleon potentials in oxygen-16
- The healing distance for correlations is approximately 1 fm, beyond which the true ground-state wavefunction coincides with the independent particle wavefunction
- The two-particle correlation distance is composed of four contributions: Pauli correlations, short-range dynamical correlations, a combined Pauli-short-range term, and center-of-mass correlations

## Context

This work extends the phonon-like excitation mechanism proposed by Feshbach and Zabek (1977) for correlated pair emission in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, but corrects their neglect of strong absorption which led to cross sections violating unitarity. The formalism connects diffraction dissociation with DWBA and semiclassical methods, showing they are perturbative expansions of the same eikonal approach. The results enable interpretation of knockout experiments with exotic nuclear beams, where quenching of spectroscopic factors relative to shell-model predictions (as seen in Hansen and Tostevin (2004) and the systematic A(e,e'p) studies of Pandharipande (1997)) may indicate short-range correlation effects.
