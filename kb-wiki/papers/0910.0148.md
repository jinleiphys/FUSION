# Shell-model calculations in 132Sn and 208Pb regions with low-momentum interactions

> We discuss shell-model calculations based on the use of low-momentum interactions derived from the free-space nucleon-nucleon potential. A main feature of this approach is the construction of a smooth potential, V-low-k, defined within a given momentum cutoff. As a practical application of the theoretical framework, we present some selected results of our current study of nuclei around doubly magic 132Sn and 208Pb which have been obtained starting from the CD-Bonn potential. Focusing attention on the similarity between the spectroscopy of these two regions, we show that it emerges quite naturally from our effective interactions without use of any adjustable parameter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the striking spectroscopic similarity between nuclei around doubly magic \(^{132}\)Sn and \(^{208}\)Pb emerges naturally from shell-model effective interactions derived from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential (CD-Bonn) via the \(V_{\rm low-k}\) approach, without introducing any adjustable parameters. The authors hedge that the agreement is "very good" with discrepancies "well below 100 keV for most of the states," rather than claiming exact reproduction of all data.

## Method

The authors start from the CD-Bonn free-space nucleon-nucleon potential and renormalize it into a smooth low-momentum potential \(V_{\rm low-k}\) with a cutoff momentum \(\Lambda = 2.2\) fm\(^{-1}\) using the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method, then make it Hermitian via Cholesky decomposition. They derive the shell-model effective interaction \(V_{\rm eff}\) through a \(\hat{Q}\)-box folded-diagram expansion including all diagrams up to second order in the interaction (plus Coulomb force for protons), using a harmonic-oscillator basis with intermediate states restricted to five proton and neutron shells above the Fermi surface. Calculations are performed with the NUSHELLX code for six nuclei: \(^{134}\)Te, \(^{134}\)Sn, \(^{134}\)Sb (around \(^{132}\)Sn) and \(^{210}\)Po, \(^{210}\)Pb, \(^{210}\)Bi (around \(^{208}\)Pb), using experimental single-particle energies.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum for \(V_{\rm low-k}\): \(\Lambda = 2.2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- Oscillator parameter \(\hbar\omega = 7.88\) MeV for the \(A=132\) region and 6.88 MeV for the \(A=208\) region, from the expression \(\hbar\omega = 45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3}\).
- Discrepancies between calculated and experimental excitation energies are "well below 100 keV for most of the states" across all six nuclei.
- The calculated states are dominated by a single configuration with percentages ranging from 80% to 100%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nuclei beyond doubly magic \(^{132}\)Sn (Coraggio 2005, 2006; Covello 2007; Simpson 2007) using the \(V_{\rm low-k}\) approach, and is motivated by the close resemblance between the spectroscopy of the \(^{132}\)Sn and \(^{208}\)Pb regions first pointed out by Blomqvist (1981). The results confirm and extend the similarity noted by Fornal (2001), Isakov (2006), and Korgul (2007) for two-valence-particle nuclei. The paper enables further studies to investigate whether this resemblance extends beyond two-valence-particle nuclei, and demonstrates that \(V_{\rm low-k}\) provides an advantageous alternative to the traditional Brueckner \(G\)-matrix method for deriving realistic shell-model interactions.
