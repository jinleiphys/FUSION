# QCD in the heavy dense regime: Large $N_c$ and quarkyonic matter

> After combined character and hopping expansions and integration over the spatial gauge links, lattice QCD reduces to a three-dimensional $SU(3)$ Polyakov loop model with complicated interactions. A simple truncation of the effective theory is valid for heavy quarks on reasonably fine lattices and can be solved by linked cluster expansion in its effective couplings. This was used ealier to demonstrate the onset transition to baryon matter in the cold and dense regime. Repeating these studies for general $N_c$, one finds that for large $N_c$ the onset transition becomes first-order, and the pressure scales as $p\sim N_c$ through three consecutive orders in the hoppoing expansion. These features are consistent with the formal definition of quarkyonic matter given in the literature. We discuss the implications for $N_c=3$ and physical QCD.

## Key claim
For large $N_c$, the baryon onset transition becomes first-order and the pressure scales as $p \sim N_c$ through three consecutive orders in the hopping expansion, consistent with the formal definition of quarkyonic matter; however, these results are obtained in the strong coupling limit and require gauge corrections to correspond to the 't Hooft limit, and the ordering of limits (continuum first, then large $N_c$) matters for continuum physics.

## Method
The authors start from lattice QCD in the Wilson formulation and derive an effective three-dimensional $SU(3)$ Polyakov loop model by performing combined character and hopping expansions, then integrating over spatial gauge links analytically. The effective theory, valid for heavy quarks on fine lattices, is solved using linked-cluster expansion methods in its effective couplings, with calculations carried out through orders $\kappa^8 u^5$. The study is repeated for general $N_c$ by modifying the partition function to account for $N_c$-dependent baryon spin degeneracy and computing thermodynamic functions order by order in the hopping expansion.

## Key numbers
- The effective couplings are functions of original QCD parameters: $\lambda = u^{N_\tau} \exp[N_\tau(4u^4+\ldots)]$, $h_1 = (2\kappa e^{a\mu})^{N_\tau}(1+\ldots) = e^{(\mu-m)/T}(1+\ldots)$, and $h_2 = \kappa^2 N_\tau/N_c(1+\ldots)$.
- In the static strong coupling limit ($\beta=0$) at zero temperature, the quark number density is a step function: $a^3 n = 0$ for $\mu < m$, and $a^3 n = 2N_c$ for $\mu > m$, reflecting saturation at $2N_c$ quarks per flavour per lattice site.
- For $N_f=2$ with $h_1>1$ (beyond onset), the pressure scales as $a^4 p \sim (4\ln(h_1)/N_\tau) N_c$ at leading order, $\sim -12 N_c$ at next order, and $\sim 198 N_c$ at third order in the hopping expansion.
- The binding energy per baryon in units of baryon mass for $N_f=1$ is $\epsilon = -(4/3)(1/(a^3 n_B))((6h_1^3+3h_1^6)/z_0)^2 \kappa^2 + \ldots$, vanishing in the static limit.
- For $N_c=3$, the nuclear liquid gas transition terminates at $T_c \sim 16$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the onset transition to baryon matter in cold and dense QCD using effective lattice theories (Fromm et al., 2013; Philipsen and Scheunert, 2019) to general $N_c$, testing the quarkyonic matter conjecture of McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The results show that the phase diagram for heavy quarks gradually evolves toward the conjectured rectangular large-$N_c$ shape as $N_c$ increases, with the deconfinement transition straightening and the onset transition becoming more strongly first-order. For physical QCD with $N_c=3$, the authors argue that the nuclear liquid gas transition plays the role of the quarkyonic transition, with no separate phase transition to quarkyonic matter, and suggest that the shell picture of quarkyonic matter (inner quark sphere surrounded by baryonic shell) may apply as a smooth crossover rather than a sharp transition.
