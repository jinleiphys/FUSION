# Hadron tomography through Wigner distributions

> We study the Wigner functions of the nucleon which provide multidimensional images of the quark distributions in phase space and combine in a single picture all the information contained in the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and the transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs). In particular, we present results for the distribution of unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon obtained in a light-cone constituent quark model. Treating the Wigner distribution as it was a classical distribution, we obtain the quark orbital angular momentum and compare it with alternative definitions given in terms of the GPDs and the TMDs.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first model calculation of quark Wigner distributions within the light-cone formalism, showing that treating the Wigner distribution as a classical distribution yields a quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) that disagrees with Ji's sum rule for separate flavor contributions, even though the total quark OAM is the same for all definitions.

## Method

The authors computed Wigner distributions for unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon using a light-cone constituent quark model (LCCQM) and the light-cone version of the chiral quark-soliton model (LCχQSM). They obtained the quark orbital angular momentum from the Wigner distributions via the phase-space integral of the distribution multiplied by the naive OAM operator (b_perp x k_perp)_z, and compared it with alternative definitions from generalized parton distributions (GPDs) via Ji's sum rule and from the transverse-momentum dependent distribution h_{1T}^perp.

## Key numbers

- Total quark OAM from LCCQM: sum_q L^q_z = 0.126 (dimensionless)
- Total quark OAM from LCχQSM: sum_q L^q_z = 0.069 (dimensionless)
- For LCCQM, u-quark OAM from Wigner distributions: ell^u_z = 0.131; from Ji's sum rule: L^u_z = 0.071
- For LCCQM, d-quark OAM from Wigner distributions: ell^d_z = -0.005; from Ji's sum rule: L^d_z = 0.055
- For LCχQSM, u-quark OAM from Wigner distributions: ell^u_z = 0.073; from Ji's sum rule: L^u_z = -0.008
- For LCχQSM, d-quark OAM from Wigner distributions: ell^d_z = -0.004; from Ji's sum rule: L^d_z = 0.077
- Fixed transverse momentum for impact-parameter plots: k_perp = 0.3 GeV
- Fixed impact parameter for transverse-momentum plots: b_perp = 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Wigner distributions in QCD first explored by Ji (2003) and Belitsky (2003), and builds on the authors' previous work connecting Wigner distributions to generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) (Lorce 2011). The model calculations use light-cone wave functions previously applied to GPDs (Boffi 2007, Lorce 2011), TMDs (Pasquini 2008, Boffi 2009, Pasquini 2010, Lorce 2011), and nucleon electroweak properties (Lorce 2006, 2007, Pasquini 2007). The finding that different OAM definitions disagree for separate flavor contributions challenges the common belief that Jaffe-Manohar and Ji's OAM should coincide in the absence of gauge degrees of freedom, as noted by Burkardt (2010), and opens questions about the reference point dependence of OAM definitions.
