# Three-body force for baryons from the D0-D4/D8 matrix model

> This is an extensive work to our previous paper \cite{key-08} studied on the D0-D4/D8 holographic system. We compute the three-body force for baryons with the D0-D4/D8 matrix model derived in \cite{key-08} with considering the non-zero QCD vacuum. We obtain the three-body force at short distances but modified by the appearance of the smeared D0-branes i.e. considering the effects from the non-trivial QCD vacuum. We firstly test our matrix model in the case of 't Hooft instanton and then in two more realistic case: (1) three-neutrons with averaged spins and (2) proton-proton-neutron (or proton-neutron-proton). The three-body potential vanishes in the former case while in two latter cases it is positive i.e. repulsive and makes sense only if the constraint for stable baryonic state is satisfied. We require all the baryons in our computation aligned on a line. These may indicate that the cases in dense states of neutrons such as in neutron stars, Helium-3 or Tritium nucleus all with the non-trivial QCD vacuum.

## Key claim

The three-body force for baryons in the D0-D4/D8 holographic matrix model vanishes for three neutrons with averaged spins but is repulsive for proton-proton-neutron (or proton-neutron-proton) configurations, provided the constraint for stable baryonic states (zeta less than or equal to 2) is satisfied and all baryons are aligned on a line with equal spacings.

## Method

The authors extend their previous D0-D4/D8 matrix model (Li and Jia, 2015) from the two-body case to the three-body case (k=3) by solving the ADHM constraint for 't Hooft instantons, substituting the solution into the matrix model action, integrating out the auxiliary field A0, and evaluating the Hamiltonian for specific baryonic states. They consider two realistic cases: three neutrons with averaged spins and proton-proton-neutron (or proton-neutron-proton), with all baryons aligned on a line with equal spacings.

## Key numbers

* The three-body potential vanishes for three neutrons with averaged spins, independent of the non-zero QCD vacuum parameter.
* For proton-proton-neutron (or proton-neutron-proton), the three-body potential is positive (repulsive) and makes sense only when the constraint zeta = U_Q0^3 / U_KK^3 is less than or equal to 2.
* The three-body potential becomes totally complex if zeta = U_Q0^3 / U_KK^3 is greater than 2, which is the same constraint for stable baryonic states found in the single-baryon and two-body cases.
* The three-body potential is suppressed compared to the two-body force computed in Li and Jia (2015).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of two-body forces in the D0-D4/D8 holographic system (Li and Jia, 2015) to the three-body case, and is a parallel computation to the work of Hashimoto, Iizuka, and Yi (2010) for three-body forces in the original Sakai-Sugimoto model. The results are consistent with the soliton approach of Park, Min, and Rho (2002) for the classical spin/isospin case. The findings may have implications for dense neutron states in neutron stars, Helium-3, or Tritium nuclei with non-trivial QCD vacuum.
