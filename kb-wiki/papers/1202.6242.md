# Probing the Equation of State of Nuclear Matter via Neutron Star Asteroseismology

> We general relativistically calculate the frequency of fundamental torsional oscillations of neutron star crusts, where we focus on the crystalline properties obtained from macroscopic nuclear models in a way depending on the equation of state of nuclear matter. We find that the calculated frequency is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, but almost independent of the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter. By identifying the lowest-frequency quasi-periodic oscillation in giant flares observed from soft gamma-ray repeaters as the fundamental torsional mode and allowing for the dependence of the calculated frequency on stellar models, we provide a lower limit of the density derivative of the symmetry energy as $L\simeq 50$ MeV.

## Key claim

By identifying the lowest-frequency quasi-periodic oscillation (QPO) observed in giant flares from soft gamma-ray repeaters as the fundamental torsional mode of neutron star crusts, and allowing for the dependence of the calculated frequency on stellar models, the authors provide a lower limit of the density derivative of the symmetry energy as L approximately 50 MeV. This constraint is derived under the assumption that the shear modulus vanishes in the pasta phase (n > n1), which tends to underestimate the calculated frequencies, making the constraint potentially conservative.

## Method

The authors general relativistically calculated the frequencies of fundamental torsional oscillations of neutron star crusts using the relativistic Cowling approximation, solving the perturbation equation for the angular displacement variable. They constructed crust models by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations inward from the stellar surface for given mass M and radius R, using nuclear equations of state derived from a Thomas-Fermi model that reproduces the bulk energy per nucleon near saturation as a function of density and neutron excess. The shear modulus for the bcc Coulomb lattice of spherical nuclei was taken from Monte Carlo calculations, while the pasta region (n > n1) was treated as having zero shear modulus.

## Key numbers

- The lowest QPO frequency observed from SGR 1806-20 is 18 Hz (horizontal dot-dashed line in Figure 1).
- The density derivative of the symmetry energy is constrained to L approximately 50 MeV as a lower limit.
- The parameter range adopted for the symmetry energy density derivative coefficient is 0 < L < 160 MeV.
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter is varied over 180 MeV <= K0 <= 360 MeV.
- For a stellar model with M = 1.4 solar masses and R = 12 km, the fitting formula for the fundamental torsional frequency (l=2) gives coefficients: c0 = 34.87 Hz, c1 = 32.33 Hz, c2 = 10.14 Hz.
- The transition density from spherical to cylindrical nuclei (n1) ranges from 0.058 fm^-3 to 0.072 fm^-3 across the parameter sets.
- The density at which nuclear matter becomes uniform (n2) ranges from 0.066 fm^-3 to 0.134 fm^-3.
- The pasta region (interval between n1 and n2) vanishes at L approximately 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Steiner and Watts (2009) and Gearheart et al. (2011) that noted the sensitivity of torsional oscillation frequencies to the symmetry energy, but provides the first systematic general relativistic calculation with a Thomas-Fermi treatment of nuclei to derive a quantitative lower limit on L. The constraint L approximately 50 MeV is currently stringent because experimental constraints on L have yet to converge, and it suggests that pasta phases in neutron stars, if present, would occur in a narrow density region. The authors note that neutron superfluidity and pasta elasticity would make the constraint on L and the pasta region even more stringent, and that the first overtone frequency depends significantly on both L and K0, offering potential for future constraints on the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter.
