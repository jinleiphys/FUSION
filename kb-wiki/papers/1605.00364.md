# Accessing proton generalized parton distributions and pion distribution amplitudes with the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process at J-PARC

> Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) encoding multidimensional information of hadron partonic structure appear as the building blocks in a factorized description of hard exclusive reactions. The nucleon GPDs have been accessed by deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production with lepton beam. A complementary probe with hadron beam is the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process. In this paper, we discuss recent theoretical advances on describing this process in terms of nucleon GPDs and pion distribution amplitudes. Furthermore, we address the feasibility of measuring the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process $π^- p \to μ^+μ^- n$ via a spectrometer at the High Momentum Beamline being constructed at J-PARC in Japan. Realization of such measurement at J-PARC will provide a new test of perturbative QCD descriptions of a novel class of hard exclusive reactions. It will also offer the possibility of experimentally accessing nucleon GPDs at large timelike virtuality.

## Key claim

The exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process $\pi^- p \to \mu^+\mu^- n$ can serve as a complementary timelike probe to access nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and pion distribution amplitudes (DAs), provided the measurement is feasible at the High Momentum Beamline at J-PARC; the factorization formalism is assumed to apply by swapping initial and final states and replacing spacelike virtuality with timelike virtuality, but this universality of GPDs between spacelike and timelike processes remains to be verified experimentally.

## Method

The authors present the leading-twist factorization formalism for the exclusive Drell-Yan process $\pi^- p \to \gamma^* n$, expressing the cross section in terms of convolution integrals of nucleon GPDs ($\tilde{H}^{du}$ and $\tilde{E}^{du}$) and the twist-two pion distribution amplitude $\phi_\pi(z)$. They compute the differential cross section $d\sigma_L/(dt dQ'^2)$ for longitudinally polarized virtual photons at leading order in $\alpha_s$, using four different models for the pion DA (asymptotic, Chernyak-Zhitnitsky, Goloskokov-Kroll, and Dyson-Schwinger equation) and the pion-pole dominance approximation for the GPD $\tilde{E}^q$. The feasibility of detecting the exclusive Drell-Yan events is then assessed using the spectrometer of the E50 experiment at the J-PARC Hadron Hall.

## Key numbers

* The leading-twist cross section for $\pi^- p \to \gamma^* n$ scales as $d\sigma_L/(dt dQ'^2) \propto \tau^2 / Q'^8$, where $\tau = Q'^2/(2p\cdot q) \approx Q'^2/s$ and $Q'^2$ is the timelike virtuality of the final-state photon.
* The skewness variable $\xi$ is related to $\tau$ by $\xi \approx \tau/(2-\tau)$, and the scaling variable $\tilde{x}$ is approximately $-\xi$.
* The pion decay constant $f_\pi$ appears in the cross section formula, and the pion DA normalization is $\int_0^1 dx \, \Phi_\pi(x,\mu)=1$.
* The Chernyak-Zhitnitsky (CZ) model for the pion DA has $a_2(\mu\simeq 0.5\ {\rm GeV}) = 2/3$, while the Goloskokov-Kroll (GK) model uses $a_2(\mu=2\ {\rm GeV})=0.22$, and the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) model gives $a_2=0.20$, $a_4=0.093$, $a_6=0.055$ at $\mu^2=4\ {\rm GeV}^2$.
* The asymptotic form of the pion DA at $\mu\to\infty$ is $\Phi_\pi^{\rm as}(x)=6x(1-x)$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for accessing nucleon GPDs via timelike processes, complementing the spacelike probes of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) that have been measured by HERMES, H1, ZEUS, HALL-A, and CLAS. The exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process was previously suggested by Berger (2001) and Goloskokov (2015) as a complementary probe, and the present paper provides a feasibility study for the upcoming J-PARC high-momentum beamline, building on the factorization proof for DVMP by Collins (1996, 1998). A successful measurement would test the universality of GPDs across spacelike and timelike processes and enable access to nucleon GPDs at large timelike virtuality, as well as provide constraints on pion distribution amplitudes that are currently debated between measurements from BABAR and Belle.
