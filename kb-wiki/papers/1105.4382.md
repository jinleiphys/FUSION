# QED Effective Actions in Space-Dependent Gauge and Electromagnetic Duality

> We develop the in-out formalism for one-loop effective actions in electromagnetic fields in the space-dependent gauge. We further advance a method using the inverse scattering matrix to calculate the effective actions in pure magnetic fields, find the effective actions in a constant magnetic field and a localized Sauter-type magnetic field and apply the uniform semiclassical approximation to the effective action in a general magnetic field. In the in-out formalism we show that the one-loop effective actions in constant fields and Sauter-type fields exhibit the electromagnetic duality.

## Key claim

The paper develops an in-out formalism for one-loop QED effective actions in space-dependent gauge fields, and advances a method using the inverse scattering matrix to compute effective actions in pure magnetic fields, showing that the one-loop effective actions in constant fields and Sauter-type fields exhibit electromagnetic duality.

## Method

The author applies the second quantized field theory for barrier tunneling in the space-dependent gauge to find Bogoliubov coefficients from Jost functions for electric fields, then generalizes this approach to pure magnetic fields by interpreting the inverse scattering matrix (the ratio of exponentially increasing to decreasing solution amplitudes) as the analog of the Bogoliubov coefficient. The method is applied to a constant magnetic field and a spatially localized Sauter-type magnetic field \(B(x) = B \, \text{sech}^2(x/L)\), using gamma-function regularization and the Schwinger prescription to obtain renormalized effective actions.

## Key numbers

- Mean number of produced pairs per Landau level and spin state: \(\mathcal{N}_n^{(\sigma)} = e^{-\pi (m^2 + qB(2n+1-2\sigma))/(qE)}\)
- Total mean number for spinor QED: \(\mathcal{N}_{\text{sp}} = \frac{(qB)(qE)}{(2\pi)^2} e^{-\pi m^2/(qE)} \coth(\pi B/E)\)
- Total mean number for scalar QED: \(\mathcal{N}_{\text{sc}} = \frac{(qB)(qE)}{2(2\pi)^2} e^{-\pi m^2/(qE)} \frac{1}{\sinh(\pi B/E)}\)
- Vacuum persistence for spinor QED: \(2\,\text{Im}(\mathcal{L}^{(1)}_{\text{sp}}) = \frac{(qB)(qE)}{(2\pi)^2} \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{1}{k} e^{-k\pi m^2/(qE)} \coth(k\pi B/E)\)
- Vacuum persistence for scalar QED: \(2\,\text{Im}(\mathcal{L}^{(1)}_{\text{sc}}) = \frac{(qB)(qE)}{2(2\pi)^2} \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k} e^{-k\pi m^2/(qE)} \frac{1}{\sinh(k\pi B/E)}\)

## Context

This work extends the in-out formalism previously applied to time-dependent and space-dependent electric fields by Kim, Lee and Yoon (2008, 2009, 2010) to the case of pure magnetic fields, where bound states (Landau levels) make the standard in-out formalism apparently inapplicable. The inverse scattering matrix method provides an alternative to the zeta-function regularization (which does not easily apply to general magnetic fields such as the Sauter-type) and to the Green's function resolvent method of Dunne and Hall (1998). The electromagnetic duality shown between constant electric and magnetic fields and between Sauter-type electric and magnetic fields generalizes the known duality of the Heisenberg-Euler and Schwinger effective action found by Chopak and Batalin-Chernov-Polivanov.
