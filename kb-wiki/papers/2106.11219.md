# Studying VLOCV twin compact stars with binary mergers

> GW170817 has provided valuable constraints on the equations of state of merging binary neutron stars, which can be considered as the most probable candidate for the source of gravitational waves. On the other hand, these natural laboratories of extreme temperature and density may lead to the estimation of some exotic matter like {deconfined quark matter in their cores}. In this paper, we investigate the neutron star matter equation of state (EoS) with the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method considering the excluded volume effect (VLOCV) for nucleons {to} compute the tidal deformability of binary neutron star mergers (BNSMs). Within this approach, the size of nucleons makes the EoS {so} stiff that requires a phase transition in order to avoid causality violation. Therefore, this phase transition {may lead} to the appearance of the third family of compact stars {including} ``twin star'' configurations. {Our EoS models are confronted with observations from GW170817, GW190814, GW190425, and also NICER. We find out that regarding all these constraints, the EoS models having the {transition} pressure$\approx$30-100 MeV/fm$^{3}$ and the energy density discontinuity $Δ\varepsilon$$\lesssim$300 MeV/fm$^{3}$ are preferable.

# Studying VLOCV twin compact stars with binary mergers

## Key claim

The paper finds that equations of state incorporating the excluded volume effect (VLOCV) for nucleons become so stiff that they require a first-order phase transition to avoid causality violation, and that EoS models with transition pressure approximately 30-100 MeV/fm³ and energy density discontinuity less than or approximately 300 MeV/fm³ are preferable when confronted with observations from GW170817, GW190814, GW190425, and NICER.

## Method

The authors compute the neutron star matter equation of state using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the AV18 two-body potential, incorporating the excluded volume effect (VLOCV) from finite nucleon size and, for comparison, three-nucleon interaction (TNI). They model the high-density quark phase using the constant speed of sound (CSS) parametrization with a first-order phase transition, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for binary neutron star merger configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass from VLOCV (AV18) approach: 2.19 M_sun (before causality violation at density 0.45 fm^-3)
- Maximum mass from LOCV (AV18+TNI) approach: 2.11 M_sun
- Maximum mass from LOCV (AV18) without TNI or finite size effects: 1.623 M_sun
- Transition pressure ranges for twin star categories: Category II: 108-127 MeV/fm³; Category III: 31-107 MeV/fm³; Category IV: 5-30 MeV/fm³
- Energy density discontinuity ranges: Category II: 420-700 MeV/fm³; Category III: 250-415 MeV/fm³; Category IV: 250-430 MeV/fm³ (for VLOCV AV18)
- Preferred parameter range: transition pressure approximately 30-100 MeV/fm³ with energy density discontinuity less than or approximately 300 MeV/fm³
- Combined dimensionless tidal deformability for GW190425 chirp mass 1.44 M_sun: 55-100 (for EoS with p_trans=60 MeV/fm³ and Delta epsilon=350 MeV/fm³) and 83-138 (for p_trans=60 MeV/fm³ and Delta epsilon=300 MeV/fm³)

## Context

This work extends the study of twin compact star configurations arising from strong first-order phase transitions, building on the classification scheme of four twin star categories introduced by Erik1 and Erik2. The authors compare their VLOCV and LOCV+TNI approaches against multimessenger constraints from GW170817, GW190814, GW190425, and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (Miller and Riley analyses). The results enable further investigation of whether twin star solutions can resolve potential tensions between nuclear EoS calculations and multimessenger observations, as discussed by Biswas and Tan (2020).
