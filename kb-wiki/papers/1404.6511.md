# Additional Strange Hadrons from QCD Thermodynamics and Strangeness Freeze-out in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We compare lattice QCD results for appropriate combinations of net strangeness fluctuations and their correlations with net baryon number fluctuations with predictions from two hadron resonance gas (HRG) models having different strange hadron content. The conventionally used HRG model based on experimentally established strange hadrons fails to describe the lattice QCD results in the hadronic phase close to the QCD crossover. Supplementing the conventional HRG with additional, experimentally uncharted strange hadrons predicted by quark model calculations and observed in lattice QCD spectrum calculations leads to good descriptions of strange hadron thermodynamics below the QCD crossover. We show that the thermodynamic presence of these additional states gets imprinted in the yields of the ground-state strange hadrons leading to a systematic 5--8 MeV decrease of the chemical freeze-out temperatures of ground-state strange baryons.

## Key claim

Supplementing the conventional hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with additional, experimentally uncharted strange hadrons predicted by quark model calculations leads to good descriptions of strange hadron thermodynamics below the QCD crossover, and the thermodynamic presence of these additional states imprints on ground-state strange hadron yields, causing a systematic 5-8 MeV decrease in the chemical freeze-out temperatures of ground-state strange baryons.

## Method

The authors compared lattice QCD results for net strangeness fluctuations and their correlations with net baryon number fluctuations against predictions from two HRG models: one based on experimentally established strange hadrons (PDG-HRG) and one including additional strange hadrons predicted by quark model calculations (QM-HRG). They calculated cumulants of strangeness fluctuations and correlations with baryon number and electric charge in (2+1)-flavor QCD using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action, with strange quark mass tuned to its physical value and light quark masses set to m_l = m_s/20, on lattices of size 6x24^3 and 8x32^3 in the temperature range 145 MeV to 170 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The QCD crossover temperature is T_c = (154 +/- 9) MeV (HotQCD Collaboration, 2012).
- Lattice QCD calculations were performed in the temperature range 145 MeV <= T <= 170 MeV.
- The light quark mass was set to m_l = m_s/20, corresponding to a pion mass of about 160 MeV in the continuum limit.
- Additional calculations used physical light quarks with m_l = m_s/27.
- Fits of strange anti-baryon to baryon yields from NA57 at sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV gave (mu_S^f/mu_B^f, mu_B^f/T^f) = (0.213(10), 1.213(30)).
- Fits of strange anti-baryon to baryon yields from STAR preliminary results at sqrt(s) = 39 GeV gave (mu_S^f/mu_B^f, mu_B^f/T^f) = (0.254(7), 0.697(20)).
- The PDG-HRG-based analysis resulted in freeze-out temperatures for strange baryons that are larger by about 8 MeV for the smaller value of T^f and about 5 MeV for the larger value of T^f, compared to QM-HRG and lattice QCD results.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between lattice QCD and HRG models for light (Redlich, 2004), strange (Bellwied, 2013), and charm (charm, 2012) degrees of freedom, where discrepancies near the QCD crossover had been noted. The authors build on prior evidence that additional unobserved hadron resonances contribute to thermodynamics (Majumder, 2007; Noronha-Hostler, 2009) and on lattice QCD spectrum calculations (LGT, 2013) and quark model predictions (Isgur, 1978; Ebert, 2009) for additional strange hadrons. The results enable improved determination of freeze-out parameters in heavy ion collisions and suggest that including further unobserved light quark hadrons may resolve the current discrepancy between lattice QCD and PDG-HRG results for the trace anomaly.
