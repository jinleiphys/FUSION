# Analytical solutions of causal relativistic hydrodynamic equations for Bjorken and Gubser flows

> We obtain general analytical solutions of third-order viscous hydrodynamic equations for Bjorken and Gubser flows in systems with vanishing bulk viscosity and chemical potential, and having a constant shear relaxation time. We also analytically characterize and determine the hydrodynamic attractors for such systems by studying the universal behavior of these solutions at both early and late times. Finally we study the properties of these hydrodynamic attractors for transport coefficients obtained from relativistic kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation.

## Key claim

The authors obtain general analytical solutions of third-order viscous hydrodynamic equations for both Bjorken and Gubser flows, but only under the restrictive assumptions of vanishing bulk viscosity and chemical potential, and a constant shear relaxation time; the attractor behavior is demonstrated analytically for Bjorken flow and presented for Gubser flow, with the claim that these attractors exist and can be characterized by early-time singularities in the derivative with respect to the initial condition parameter.

## Method

The authors start from the third-order evolution equations for the shear stress tensor in one-dimensional Bjorken flow and in Weyl-rescaled variables for Gubser flow, both with constant shear relaxation time. They transform these equations into second-order linear ordinary differential equations for the normalized shear variable \(\bar{\pi} \equiv \pi/(\epsilon+P)\) (or \(\hat{\bar{\pi}}\) for Gubser flow), which admit solutions in terms of Whittaker functions (Bjorken) and Heun general functions (Gubser). The hydrodynamic attractor is identified by setting the integration constant \(\alpha = 0\) for Bjorken flow, and its characterization is performed by studying the divergence of \(\partial \bar{\pi}/\partial \alpha\) in the early-time limit. For Gubser flow, the analytical solution is presented but the attractor characterization is left for future work.

## Key numbers

* For Bjorken flow, the third-order transport coefficients are \(\lambda = 10/21\) and \(\chi = 72/245\), with \(\beta_\pi = 4P/5\) and \(\gamma = 412/147\).
* For Bjorken flow, the separation between solutions with different initial conditions damps as \(\partial \bar{\pi}/\partial \alpha \sim e^{-\bar{\tau}}/\bar{\tau}\) in the limit \(\bar{\tau} \to \infty\), where \(\bar{\tau} \equiv \tau/\tau_\pi\).
* For Gubser flow, the third-order transport coefficients are \(\hat{\lambda} = 46/21\) and \(\hat{\chi} = 72/245\), with \(\hat{\beta}_\pi = 4\hat{P}/5\) and \(\gamma = 412/147\).
* For Gubser flow, the zeroth-order gradient expansion term is \(\hat{\bar{\pi}}_0 = a \, \hat{\tau}_\pi \tanh \rho\) with \(a = 4/15\), and the first-order term is \(\hat{\bar{\pi}}_1 = a \, \hat{\tau}_\pi^2 [\tanh^2 \rho (1+\lambda) - 1]\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical solutions of higher-order dissipative hydrodynamics previously obtained for Bjorken flow by Denicol (2017) and Hatta (2014), and presents for the first time analytical solutions for Gubser flow at third order. The analytical characterization of hydrodynamic attractors using early-time singularities provides a new method to identify attractor solutions, complementing the late-time analysis of Denicol (2017). The results enable systematic studies of the domain of validity of hydrodynamics in systems with large gradients, and the demonstration that gradient expansions diverge for both flow profiles motivates the need for non-perturbative approaches to describe far-from-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions.
