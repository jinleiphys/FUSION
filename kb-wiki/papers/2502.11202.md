# Stochastic baryon charge transport in relativistic hydrodynamics

> We utilize 3+1D stochastic hydrodynamics to study correlations and fluctuations of baryon charge in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The baryon charge fluctuations are important observables to probe the QCD phase diagram, yet a dynamical description with stochastic hydrodynamics remains challenging due to numerical instabilities and high computational demands. In this work, we employ a linearized approach, allowing us to separately simulate the background energy-momentum evolution of a charge-neutral fluid and the stochastic baryon transport processes, thereby largely reducing computational cost while maintaining sufficient accuracy. We implement this linearized stochastic charge evolution in the viscous hydrodynamic code MUSIC, and find that it nicely describes the two-point correlation of 1+1D analytical solutions for various equations of state and transport coefficients. In particular, the hydrodynamic calculations demonstrate how different rapidity separations probe charge fluctuations originating at different times of the evolution. We also investigate the net baryon correlations after the Cooper--Frye freeze out, which show good consistency with the analytical calculations and indicate that these fluctuation-induced correlations are sensitive to the baryon diffusion coefficient.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a linearized stochastic approach to baryon charge transport in relativistic hydrodynamics can accurately reproduce analytical 1+1D two-point correlation functions while drastically reducing computational cost compared to full event-by-event stochastic hydrodynamic simulations, provided a momentum cutoff filter is applied to avoid numerical instabilities from high-frequency noise modes.

## Method

The authors implement linearized stochastic baryon charge evolution equations in the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic code MUSIC, separately simulating a charge-neutral background energy-momentum evolution and the fluctuating baryon transport on top. They solve the coupled stochastic differential equations for baryon density and dissipative current in Fourier space, using the fluctuation-dissipation relation with white noise whose magnitude is determined by the baryon diffusion constant κ_B and the grid cell volume. The numerical scheme runs multiple fluctuating baryon-diffusion events in parallel on a single hydrodynamic background, outputting baryon density and chemical potential at freeze-out for correlation analysis.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time τ₀ = 1 fm/c and freeze-out time τ_F = 4 fm/c used for Green's function calculations (with τ_F = 11 fm/c for correlation studies)
- Diffusion coefficient D_s = 0.3 fm used in verification calculations
- Relaxation time τ_q varied from 0.001 fm/c (near Navier-Stokes limit) to values where non-hydrodynamic oscillations become visible
- Grid spacing Δη_s = 0.05 for N_η = 200 and Δη_s = 0.0125 for N_η = 800 over rapidity range (-5, 5)
- Noise covariance in numerical implementation: ⟨ξ^μ ξ^ν⟩ = 2 κ_B Δ^{μν} / (τ Δτ Δx Δy Δη)

## Context

This work extends stochastic hydrodynamic approaches first proposed by Landau for non-relativistic fluids and generalized to relativistic frameworks by Kapusta (2011), addressing the computational challenges noted in recent reviews by Bluhm (2020) and Basar (2024). The linearized method builds on prior efforts to implement fluctuations in hydrodynamic codes including those by Nahrgang (2018), Singh (2018), and Chattopadhyay (2024), but avoids the numerical instabilities and high computational demands of full event-by-event simulations. The approach enables direct comparison of baryon charge fluctuations from dynamical hydrodynamic evolution with equilibrium lattice QCD calculations and experimental measurements from the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, particularly for studying the QCD critical point through cumulants of conserved charges.
