# Phenomenological predictions of 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We make phenomenological predictions for particle spectra and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions using 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) including the effects of both shear and bulk viscosities. The dynamical equations necessary are derived by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation allowing for three distinct (diagonal) momentum-space anisotropy parameters. The formulation is based on relaxation-time approximation for the collisional kernel and a lattice-QCD-based equation of state. Evolving the system to late times, we calculate particle production using THERMINATOR 2, modified to account for an ellipsoidal distribution function. We obtain particle spectra for different particle species such as pions, kaons, and protons, and elliptic flow $v_2$ as a function of centrality, transverse momentum, and rapidity. In our model, we have four free parameters, i.e. freeze-out temperature, initial central energy density, initial momentum-space anisotropies, and shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. Using a multidimensional fit to LHC experimental data, we make a preliminary extraction of these parameters. We find reasonable agreement between 3+1d aHydro and available experimental data for $η/s\sim 0.23$.

# Phenomenological predictions of 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) model, including shear and bulk viscosities, yields reasonable agreement with LHC experimental data for particle spectra and elliptic flow in 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, but only for a preliminary extraction of parameters using a smooth optical Glauber profile and a fixed freeze-out temperature of 0.13 GeV; the extracted shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of η/s ≈ 0.23 differs significantly from a recent viscous hydrodynamics estimate of η/s ≈ 0.095.

## Method
The authors derived dynamical equations by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation under the relaxation-time approximation, using a distribution function with three distinct diagonal momentum-space anisotropy parameters and a lattice-QCD-based equation of state (Krakow parametrization). They evolved the system to late times and calculated particle production using THERMINATOR 2, modified for an ellipsoidal distribution function, with a constant energy density freeze-out hypersurface defined by an effective temperature. A multidimensional fit to LHC data for 20-30% centrality class was performed to extract four free parameters: initial central temperature, freeze-out temperature, initial momentum-space anisotropies, and shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio extracted: η/s ≈ 0.23 (equivalently 4πη/s = 3), compared to a viscous hydrodynamics estimate of η/s ≈ 0.095 from Ryu (2015).
- Best-fit initial central temperature: T₀ = 0.56 GeV.
- Best-fit freeze-out temperature: TFO = 0.13 GeV.
- Particle spectra and elliptic flow v₂ computed for π⁺, K⁺, and protons as functions of transverse momentum pT up to 2 GeV for the 20-30% centrality class at LHC 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions.
- 20,100 hadronic events were sampled using THERMINATOR 2 at freeze-out.

## Context
This work extends the 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics formalism developed by Nopoush, Strickland, and collaborators (2014-2015) to phenomenological predictions for heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier aHydro work by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), and others. The extracted η/s ≈ 0.23 differs substantially from the viscous hydrodynamics estimate of η/s ≈ 0.095 by Ryu (2015), highlighting the sensitivity of viscosity extraction to the hydrodynamic framework used. The results enable future studies with fluctuating initial conditions (already implemented in the code) and inclusion of off-diagonal anisotropy components and chemical potentials to improve accuracy.
