# The Phase Diagram of the Quark-Meson Model

> Within the proper-time renormalization group approach, the chiral phase diagram of a two-flavor quark-meson model is studied. In the chiral limit, the location of the tricritical point which is linked to a Gaussian fixed point, is determined. For quark chemical potentials smaller than the tricritical one the second-order phase transition belongs to the O(4) universality class. For temperatures below the tricritical one we find initially a weak first-order phase transition which is commonly seen in model studies and also in recent lattice simulations. In addition, below temperatures of T <~ 17 MeV we find two phase transitions. The chiral restoration transition is initially also of first-order but turns into a second-order transition again. This leads to the possibility that there may be a "second tricritical" point in the QCD phase diagram in the chiral limit.

## Key claim

Within the proper-time renormalization group approach, the chiral phase diagram of a two-flavor quark-meson model in the chiral limit exhibits not only the expected tricritical point at T = 52 MeV and mu = 251 MeV, but also a possible "second tricritical" point at temperatures below approximately 17 MeV where the chiral restoration transition changes from first-order back to second-order.

## Method

The authors employed the proper-time renormalization group (PTRG) method with an optimized smearing function in d=3 dimensions to derive analytic flow equations for the scale-dependent grand canonical thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and quark chemical potential. They solved the resulting flow equation numerically on a grid of the field phi^2, using initial conditions at a UV scale Lambda = 500 MeV with a Yukawa coupling g = 3.2 and lambda = 10, evolving toward k = 0 to obtain the effective action containing all thermodynamically relevant information.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at mu = 0: T_c ~ 142 MeV for the second-order chiral phase transition in the chiral limit
- Tricritical point location: T_c,tri = 52 MeV and mu_c,tri = 251 MeV
- Splitting temperature where the first-order line splits into two transitions: T_s ~ 17 MeV, with mu_s ~ 263 MeV
- Curvature of the transition line at mu = 0: T_c * d^2 T_c / d mu^2 = -0.23 (PTRG full result)
- Vacuum pion decay constant in the chiral limit: f_pi ~ 87 MeV
- Quark number density jump at mu ~ 242 MeV: n_q = 0.1 fm^{-3}, corresponding to normalized baryon density rho_B/rho_0 ~ 0.18

## Context

This work extends previous PTRG studies of O(N)-models (Bohr, 2001) and quark-meson models (Papp, 2000) to finite temperature and chemical potential, providing a nonperturbative RG treatment beyond mean-field approximations. The results are compared with lattice simulations (Allton, 2002; Fodor, 2002) and NJL mean-field calculations (Buballa, 2003), finding that the curvature of the transition line in the chiral limit is larger by a factor of two than lattice analyses. The discovery of a possible second tricritical point at low temperatures opens new questions about the QCD phase diagram structure in the chiral limit, with implications for future studies of the universality class of this additional critical point.
