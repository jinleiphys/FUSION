# Three-body hadronic structure of low-lying $1/2^+$ $Σ$ and $Λ$ resonances

> We discuss the dynamical generation of some low-lying $1/2^+$ $Σ$'s and $Λ$'s in two-meson one-baryon systems. These systems have been constructed by adding a pion in $S$-wave to the $\bar{K} N$ pair and its coupled channels, where the $1/2^-$ $Λ$(1405)-resonance gets dynamically generated. We solve Faddeev equations in the coupled-channel approach to calculate the $T$-matrix for these systems as a function of the total energy and the invariant mass of one of the meson-baryon pairs. This squared $T$-matrix shows peaks at the energies very close to the masses of the strangeness -1, $1/2^+$ resonances listed in the particle data book.

## Key claim

The authors claim that several low-lying strangeness -1, \(1/2^+\) \(\Sigma\) and \(\Lambda\) resonances listed by the Particle Data Group can be dynamically generated as three-body (two-meson one-baryon) bound states by solving Faddeev equations with on-shell two-body \(t\)-matrices, but the match is approximate: the calculated peak positions and widths differ from the PDG ranges, and the identification is based on proximity rather than exact agreement.

## Method

The authors constructed coupled two-meson one-baryon systems by adding an \(S\)-wave pion to the \(\bar{K}N\) pair and its coupled channels (which dynamically generate the \(1/2^-\) \(\Lambda(1405)\)), resulting in twenty-two coupled channels for fixed total charge. They solved the Faddeev equations in a coupled-channel approach using on-shell two-body \(t\)-matrices, after verifying that off-shell contributions and three-body forces cancel to within 5% of the total on-shell contribution. The squared \(T_R\)-matrix was projected onto the isospin basis and plotted as a function of total energy \(\sqrt{s}\) and the invariant mass of one meson-baryon pair to identify resonant peaks.

## Key numbers

- \(\Lambda(1810)\): peak at 1740 MeV with a full width at half maximum of \(\sim\) 20 MeV (PDG range: 1750–1850 MeV, width 50–250 MeV).
- \(\Lambda(1600)\): peak at 1568 MeV with width 60 MeV (PDG width range: 50–250 MeV).
- \(\Sigma(1560)\): peak at 1590 MeV with width 70 MeV (PDG width range: 10–100 MeV).
- \(\Sigma(1620)\): peak at 1630 MeV with width 39 MeV (PDG width range: 10–100 MeV).
- \(\Sigma(1660)\): peak at 1656 MeV with width 30 MeV (PDG width range: 40–200 MeV).
- \(\Sigma(1770)\): peak at 1790 MeV with width 24 MeV (PDG width range: 60–100 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary dynamics approach, previously used to dynamically generate the \(\Lambda(1405)\) with a two-pole structure (Jido et al., 2005), to three-body systems for the first time. It provides a theoretical interpretation for several poorly understood low-lying \(\Sigma\) and \(\Lambda\) resonances, such as the \(\Sigma(1560)\) (whose spin-parity was unknown) and the \(\Sigma(1620)\) (where partial-wave analyses and production experiments were kept separate in the PDG). The results enable future studies of three-body hadronic structure and may guide experimental searches for these resonances in three-body final states like \(\pi^0\pi^0\Lambda\) and \(\pi^0\pi^0\Sigma\).
