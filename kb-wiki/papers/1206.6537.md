# A T-Matrix Calculation for in-Medium Heavy-Quark Gluon Scattering

> The interactions of charm and bottom quarks in a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) are evaluated using a thermodynamic 2-body T-matrix. We specifically focus on heavy-quark (HQ) interactions with thermal gluons with an input potential motivated by lattice-QCD computations of the HQ free energy. The latter is implemented into a field-theoretic ansatz for color-Coulomb and (remnants of) confining interactions. This, in particular, enables to discuss corrections to the potential approach, specifically hard-thermal-loop corrections to the vertices, relativistic corrections deduced from pertinent Feynman diagrams, and a suitable projection on transverse thermal gluons. The resulting potentials are applied to compute scattering amplitudes in different color channels and utilized for a calculation of the corresponding HQ drag coefficient in the QGP. A factor of ~2-3 enhancement over perturbative results is obtained, mainly driven by the resummation in the attractive color-channels.

## Key claim

The authors extend a thermodynamic T-matrix approach to heavy-quark (HQ) scattering with thermal gluons, finding a factor of ~2-3 enhancement over perturbative results for the HQ drag coefficient, but this enhancement is conditional on using the internal energy (U) rather than the free energy (F) as the in-medium potential, and is mainly driven by resummation in the attractive color channels.

## Method

The authors construct in-medium potentials for heavy-quark-gluon scattering by fitting a microscopic model with color-Coulomb and confining terms to lattice-QCD free-energy data at temperatures from 1.2 Tc to ~2 Tc. They derive relativistic corrections from Feynman diagrams for the Coulomb and string terms, project onto transverse gluon polarizations, and implement hard-thermal-loop vertex corrections (finding them subleading for hard thermal gluons). The resulting potentials are used in a 3-dimensional reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation (Thompson scheme) to compute T-matrices in partial-wave and color projections, which are then integrated to obtain the heavy-quark friction coefficient via a Fokker-Planck approach.

## Key numbers

- Charm-quark bare mass: m_c^0 = 1.264 GeV; bottom-quark bare mass: m_b^0 = 4.662 GeV (adjusted to ground-state quarkonium mass in vacuum using the Thompson scheme)
- Thermal gluon mass: m_g = sqrt(3/4) g T, with fixed coupling g = 2.3 and N_f = 3 active light flavors
- Fixed combined width of heavy quark and gluon: 0.2 GeV (0.1 GeV each)
- Running coupling correction parameters: Delta = 1 GeV, Lambda = 0.2 GeV
- At low temperatures using the U-potential, the sextet channel drag coefficient gamma_c = 0.027 fm^{-1} and the triplet channel gamma_c = 0.018 fm^{-1}
- Factor of ~2-3 enhancement over perturbative results for the HQ drag coefficient

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic T-matrix approach previously applied to heavy-light quark scattering (Riek and Rapp, 2010) and quarkonia (Cabrera and Rapp, 2006; van Hees and Rapp, 2007) to the heavy-quark-gluon channel. It builds on lattice-QCD computations of the static HQ free energy (Kaczmarek, 2007; Bazavov, 2009) and a microscopic model for color-Coulomb and confining terms (Megias, 2005, 2007). The results enable improved calculations of heavy-quark transport coefficients in the Quark-Gluon Plasma for application to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and can be compared to perturbative evaluations to quantify the relevance of nonperturbative effects.
