# Proton radii and two-photon exchange

> We investigate the effect of two-photon exchange processes upon the rms- and Zemach radii extracted from electron-proton scattering. We find that the changes are small and do not help to explain the discrepancy between experimental and calculated HFS in the hydrogen atom.

# Proton radii and two-photon exchange

## Key claim
Two-photon exchange corrections to electron-proton scattering produce only small changes to the extracted rms charge radius and Zemach moment, and these changes go in the wrong direction to explain the 3.6(5) ppm discrepancy between the calculated and experimental hyperfine structure (HFS) in hydrogen.

## Method
The authors calculated the two-photon exchange box and crossed-box amplitudes with an intermediate nucleon, using hadronic form factors consistent with measured charge and magnetic form factors G_E(q^2) and G_M(q^2). They applied these corrections to the world cross section data on elastic electron-proton scattering up to a maximum momentum transfer of 4 fm^{-1}, then fitted the corrected data with 5-parameter continued fraction expansions for both form factors to extract the radii.

## Key numbers
- Change in Zemach moment ⟨r⟩_(2) when removing two-photon exchange contribution: +0.0052 fm (approximately 40% of the error bar)
- Zemach moment after two-photon correction: 1.091 ± 0.012 fm
- Change in charge rms radius: +0.0015 fm
- Charge rms radius after two-photon correction: 0.897 ± 0.018 fm
- The calculated HFS disagrees with experiment by 3.6(5) ppm, where the uncertainty is dominated by the uncertainty in the Zemach moment

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Sick (2003) for the charge rms radius and Friar (2004) for the Zemach moment, which extracted these quantities from world e-p data under one-photon exchange assumptions. The two-photon exchange corrections were previously shown by Blunden (2003) to have considerable effects on the proton charge form factor at very large momentum transfers. The results show that the HFS discrepancy cannot be resolved by two-photon exchange effects in electron scattering, pointing instead toward the uncertain nuclear polarization correction calculated by Faustov (2002) using experimental spin structure functions, or potentially not yet calculated higher-order QED contributions to the HFS.
