# Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking on the Light Front.II. The Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model

> An investigation of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking on the light front is made in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with one flavor and N colors. Analysis of the model suffers from extraordinary complexity due to the existence of a "fermionic constraint," i.e., a constraint equation for the bad spinor component. However, to solve this constraint is of special importance. In classical theory, we can exactly solve it and then explicitly check the property of ``light-front chiral transformation.'' In quantum theory, we introduce a bilocal formulation to solve the fermionic constraint by the 1/N expansion. Systematic 1/N expansion of the fermion bilocal operator is realized by the boson expansion method. The leading (bilocal) fermionic constraint becomes a gap equation for a chiral condensate and thus if we choose a nontrivial solution of the gap equation, we are in the broken phase. As a result of the nonzero chiral condensate, we find unusual chiral transformation of fields and nonvanishing of the light-front chiral charge. A leading order eigenvalue equation for a single bosonic state is equivalent to a leading order fermion-antifermion bound-state equation. We analytically solve it for scalar and pseudoscalar mesons and obtain their light-cone wavefunctions and masses. All of the results are entirely consistent with those of our previous analysis on the chiral Yukawa model.

## Key claim

Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking on the light front in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model requires solving the "fermionic constraint" (the constraint equation for the bad spinor component) nonperturbatively via the 1/N expansion; only by choosing a nontrivial solution of the resulting gap equation does one enter the broken phase, and the results are entirely consistent with those of the authors' previous analysis on the chiral Yukawa model.

## Method

The authors quantized the NJL model with one flavor and N colors on the light front, then rewrote the fermionic constraint in terms of color-singlet bilocal operators. They applied the Holstein-Primakoff type boson expansion method to perform a systematic 1/N expansion of these bilocal operators, which allowed the leading-order fermionic constraint to become a gap equation for the chiral condensate. The leading-order eigenvalue equation for a single bosonic state, equivalent to a leading-order fermion-antifermion bound-state equation, was solved analytically for scalar and pseudoscalar mesons to obtain their light-cone wavefunctions and masses.

## Key numbers

* The leading (bilocal) fermionic constraint becomes a gap equation for a chiral condensate; choosing a nontrivial solution places the system in the broken phase.
* The leading-order eigenvalue equation for a single bosonic state is equivalent to a leading-order fermion-antifermion bound-state equation, solved analytically for scalar and pseudoscalar mesons.
* The authors worked with a nonzero bare mass m0 (m0 ≠ 0) throughout; if m0 = 0, the canonical light-front Hamiltonian P^- of the Gross-Neveu model vanishes entirely in 1+1 dimensions.
* The antiperiodic boundary condition was used for fermionic fields: ψ_a(x^- = -∞, x^i_⊥) = -ψ_a(x^- = ∞, x^i_⊥) and similarly for χ_a.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the chiral Yukawa model (Itakura and Maedan, 1999), where dynamical chiral symmetry breaking was described indirectly via scalar zero-mode constraints; here the NJL model is treated directly without introducing auxiliary scalar fields. The approach contrasts with earlier attempts by Heinzl et al. (1990s), who treated the model within mean-field approximation and emphasized the delicacy of the infrared cutoff, and by Bentz et al. (1990s), who introduced auxiliary fields and solved constraint equations by 1/N expansion. The results enable a full understanding of how light-front chiral symmetry breaking works in a simple fermionic theory, providing a foundation for extending such analysis to QCD.
