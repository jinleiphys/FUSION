# Calculation of multidimensional potential energy surfaces for even-even transuranium nuclei: Systematic investigation of the triaxiality effect on fission barrier

> Static fission barriers for 95 even-even transuranium nuclei with charge number $Z=94-118$ have been systematically investigated by means of pairing self-consistent Woods-Saxon-Strutinsky calculations using the potential energy surface approach in multidimensional ($β_2$, $γ$, $β_4$) deformation space. Taking the heavier $^{252}$Cf nucleus (with the available fission barrier from experiment) as an example, the formation of the fission barrier and the influence of macroscopic, shell and pairing correction energies on it are analyzed. The results of the present calculated $β_2$ values and barrier heights are compared with previous calculations and available experiments. The role of triaxiality in the region of the first saddle is discussed. It is found that the second fission barrier is also considerably affected by the triaxial deformation degree of freedom in some nuclei (e.g., the $Z=112-118$ isotopes). Based on the potential energy curves, general trends of the evolution of the fission barrier heights and widths as a function of the nucleon numbers are investigated. In addition, the effects of Woods-Saxon potential parameter modifications (e.g., the strength of the spin-orbit coupling and the nuclear surface diffuseness) on the fission barrier are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate static fission barriers for 95 even-even transuranium nuclei (Z=94-118) using a multidimensional potential energy surface approach in (β₂, γ, β₄) deformation space, finding that triaxiality lowers the inner fission barrier height and also considerably affects the second fission barrier in some nuclei (e.g., Z=112-118 isotopes), with the claim qualified by the model dependence of the Woods-Saxon potential parameters.

## Method

The authors performed macroscopic-microscopic calculations using the Woods-Saxon-Strutinsky method with pairing treated self-consistently via the Lipkin-Nogami approach. The potential energy surfaces were computed in a three-dimensional deformation space (β₂, γ, β₄) for each nucleus, with the macroscopic energy given by the standard liquid-drop model of Myers and Swiatecki (1966) and the universal Woods-Saxon parameter set of Cwiok (1987). The inner fission barrier heights were extracted from the resulting potential energy curves after minimization with respect to γ and β₄ at each β₂ point.

## Key numbers

- The triaxiality lowers the inner fission barrier height of ²⁵²Cf by up to 2.66 MeV compared to the axially symmetric result.
- For ²⁵²Cf, the present calculated inner fission barrier agrees with experimental data to within approximately 30 keV.
- The CDFT calculation of Lu (2014) for ²⁵²Cf underestimates the experimental barrier by 40 keV.
- The study covers 95 even-even transuranium nuclei with charge numbers Z=94-118.
- The Woods-Saxon central potential depth parameters used are V₀=49.6 MeV and κ=0.86.
- The diffuseness parameters for both central and spin-orbit parts are a₀(p)=a₀(n)=a₀₋ₛₒ(p)=a₀₋ₛₒ(n)=0.70 fm.
- The spin-orbit strength parameters are λ(p)=36.0 and λ(n)=35.0.

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of fission barriers in transuranium nuclei by including triaxial deformation degrees of freedom, which were often neglected in earlier macroscopic-microscopic calculations. The authors had previously investigated octupole effects on outer fission barriers for even-even nuclei with 102≤Z≤112 (Wang, 2012) and triaxial effects in specific isotope chains such as Ba (Yang, 2015), Nd (Chai, 2015), and W (Yang, 2015). The present systematic study across all synthesized even-even transuranium nuclei provides a reference for experiments and for more microscopic calculations such as covariant density functional theory (CDFT) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov methods, and may help constrain the r-process nucleosynthesis calculations that depend on fission barrier heights.
