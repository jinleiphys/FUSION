# From $\bar{K}N$ Interactions to $\bar{K}$-Nuclear Quasi-Bound States

> We review the current status of our study of $K^-$-nuclear interactions and $K^-$-nuclear quasi-bound states. The adopted $K^-$-nuclear optical potential consists of two parts -- the single-nucleon one constructed microscopically from chirally motivated $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes, and a phenomenological multi-nucleon one constrained in fits to kaonic atoms data. The inclusion of multi-nucleon absorption in our calculations of $K^{-}$ quasi-bound states in many-body systems leads to huge widths, considerably exceeding the binding energies. If this feature is confirmed the observation of such states is unlikely. Finally, a development of a new microscopical model for in-medium $K^-NN$ absorption is discussed as well.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a phenomenological multi-nucleon term in the K⁻-nuclear optical potential, required to fit kaonic atom data, leads to calculated widths of K⁻-nuclear quasi-bound states that considerably exceed their binding energies; if this feature is confirmed, experimental observation of such states is unlikely, though the conclusion does not necessarily apply to few-body K⁻-nucleon systems.

## Method

The authors construct a K⁻-nuclear optical potential from two parts: a single-nucleon term built microscopically from chirally motivated \(\bar{K}N\) scattering amplitudes with self-consistent treatment of the subthreshold energy shift, and a phenomenological multi-nucleon term of the form \(V^{mN}_{K^-} \sim B (\rho/\rho_0)^\alpha \rho\) whose complex strength parameter \(B\) and exponent \(\alpha\) are fitted to 65 kaonic atom data points. The Klein-Gordon equation is solved self-consistently for K⁻-atomic and K⁻-nuclear states, with the multi-nucleon term applied either at full density (FD) or fixed above half density (HD). A separate microscopical model for in-medium K⁻NN absorption is developed using meson-exchange and chirally motivated amplitudes with Pauli blocking.

## Key numbers

- The self-consistent energy shift for K⁻-nuclear states is typically 50–100 MeV below the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold.
- The kaonic atom data set comprises 65 experimental results for strong interaction level shifts, widths, and relative yields.
- For the KM1 model in \(^{208}\)Pb, the imaginary part of the multi-nucleon optical potential reaches close to 160 MeV (FD variant).
- For the 1s state in \(^{208}\)Pb with the KM model and HD1 option: binding energy \(B_{K^-} = 64\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_{K^-} = 108\) MeV.
- For the 1s state in \(^{208}\)Pb with the KM model and FD1 option: binding energy \(B_{K^-} = 33\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_{K^-} = 273\) MeV.
- For the 1s state in \(^{16}\)O with the P model and HD1 option: binding energy \(B_{K^-} = 49\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_{K^-} = 94\) MeV.
- The AMADEUS measurement of the branching ratio \(R = \text{BR}(K^-pp \rightarrow \Lambda p) / \text{BR}(K^-pp \rightarrow \Sigma^0 p) = 0.7 \pm 0.2(\text{stat.})^{+0.2}_{-0.3}(\text{syst.})\) is reproduced by the microscopical \(\bar{K}NN\) model when Pauli-blocked amplitudes are used.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of K⁻-nuclear interactions (Cieplý 2011, Friedman 2016, Hrtánková 2017) by systematically comparing multiple chirally motivated \(\bar{K}N\) models (Kyoto-Munich, Prague, Barcelona, Bonn, Murcia) and demonstrating that only the Prague, Kyoto-Munich, and Barcelona models satisfy both kaonic atom data and single-nucleon absorption fraction constraints from bubble-chamber experiments (Davis 1968, Moulder 1972, Vandervelde 1977). The finding that multi-nucleon absorption produces widths exceeding binding energies contradicts earlier phenomenological analyses (Mareš 2006) that predicted deeper real potentials and narrower widths. The new microscopical \(\bar{K}NN\) absorption model, following the approach of Nagahiro (2011) for \(\eta'NN\), enables future calculations of in-medium absorption rates and comparison with AMADEUS data (Del Grande 2018).
