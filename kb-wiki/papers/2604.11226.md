# Inclusive breakup reactions with non-spectator fragments: Generalization of the IAV sum rules

> The Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) sum rule formalism for inclusive breakup reactions $a + A \to b + \mathrm{anything}$ treats the detected fragment $b$ as a spectator by replacing its interaction with the target by an optical potential. This assumption becomes questionable when $b$ is a loosely bound composite particle such as a deuteron. I derive a generalization that removes the spectator approximation and retains $b$'s internal degrees of freedom, providing state-resolved inclusive cross sections. Within the DWBA, all non-spectator effects enter through the source function via the operator $V_{bA} - U_{bA}$. The exact sum rule involves the full $x + A$ resolvent $(E_{x,0}^+ - H_{xA})^{-1}$, while a single-channel IAV-like expression is recovered only when the explicit target dependence of $V_{bA}$ is neglected; post-prior equivalence is preserved in both cases. A key conceptual finding is that the standard IAV result for structureless $b$ corresponds, under closure, to the \emph{total} inclusive cross section summed over all of $b$'s internal states, rather than the cross section for $b$ in a specific state. An operator-level estimate for $b = d$ on ${}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ shows that the non-spectator correction is not a small perturbation at the nuclear surface. The present work is purely formal: it establishes the theoretical framework and identifies the relevant operators, while quantitative assessment of the cross-section impact awaits a full numerical evaluation of the source integrals.

## Key claim

The standard IAV sum rule for inclusive breakup reactions treats the detected fragment \(b\) as a spectator by replacing its interaction with the target by an optical potential, but this assumption fails when \(b\) is a loosely bound composite particle such as a deuteron. The paper derives a generalization that removes the spectator approximation and retains \(b\)'s internal degrees of freedom, providing state-resolved inclusive cross sections; however, the work is purely formal and quantitative assessment of the cross-section impact awaits full numerical evaluation.

## Method

The author derives a post-form DWBA sum rule for the inclusive breakup reaction \(a + A \to b + \text{anything}\) starting from the full Hamiltonian that includes \(b\)'s internal Hamiltonian \(h_b(\zeta)\) and the full interaction \(V_{bA}\) rather than an optical potential \(U_{bA}\). The source function is decomposed into a spectator part \(|\rho_0^{(\mathrm{sp})}\rangle\) and a nonspectator part \(|\rho_0^{(\mathrm{nsp})}\rangle\) controlled by the operator \(V_{bA} - U_{bA}\), and the exact sum rule involves the full \(x + A\) resolvent \((E_{x,0}^+ - H_{xA})^{-1}\) rather than the optical Green's function. A prior-form sum rule is also derived, and post-prior equivalence is established in the reduced single-channel limit where the target dependence of \(V_{bA}\) is neglected.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron binding energy is 2.224 MeV and its root-mean-square proton-neutron separation is approximately 3.9 fm, motivating the need for the nonspectator treatment for \(b = d\).
* An operator-level estimate for \(b = d\) on \({}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) shows that the nonspectator correction is not a small perturbation at the nuclear surface (no numerical value given for the correction magnitude).
* The projectile binding energy \(\epsilon_a > 0\) appears in the bound-state equation for \(\varphi_a(\mathbf{r}_{bx}, \zeta)\).
* The energy available to the \(x + A\) system when \(b\) is in its ground state is \(E_{x,0} = E - E_b - \varepsilon_0\), where \(E_b = \hbar^2 k_b^2/(2\mu_b)\).

## Context

This work extends the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) formalism (Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent, 1985) by removing the spectator approximation that replaces \(V_{bA}\) with an optical potential \(U_{bA}\), a limitation noted by the original authors. It enables future numerical calculations of inclusive breakup cross sections for reactions where the detected fragment is a loosely bound composite particle such as a deuteron from \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) or \({}^{7}\mathrm{Li}\) projectiles, or an unbound fragment like \({}^{8}\mathrm{Be}\) from \({}^{9}\mathrm{Be}\)-induced reactions (Villanueva, 2024). The formalism also clarifies that the standard IAV result for structureless \(b\) corresponds to the total inclusive cross section summed over all of \(b\)'s internal states, not the state-resolved cross section.
