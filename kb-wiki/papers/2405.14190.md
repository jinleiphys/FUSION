# Strangelets at finite temperature

> We study the properties of strangelets at finite temperature $T$, employing an equivparticle model that incorporates both linear confinement and leading-order perturbative interactions with density-dependent quark masses. The shell effects are analyzed by solving the Dirac equations for quarks within the mean-field approximation. As temperature increases, these effects weaken due to the occupation probability of single-particle levels being governed by the Fermi-Dirac statistics, a phenomenon known as shell dampening. Surprisingly, the surface tension, derived from a liquid-drop formula, does not decrease with temperature but instead rises until it peaks at $T \approx 20-40$ MeV. At this temperature, shell corrections become negligible, and the formula provides a reasonable approximation for the free energy per baryon of strangelets. However, the curvature term decreases with $T$ despite the presence of shell effects. The neutron and proton emission rates are determined microscopically by the external nucleon gas densities that are in equilibrium with strangelets. These emission rate generally increases with $T$ for stable strangelets, but decrease for those that are unstable to nucleon emission at $T$ = 0. The other properties of $β$-stable strangelets obtained with various parameter sets are presented as well. The results indicated in this work are useful for understanding the products of binary compact star mergers and heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The surface tension of strange quark matter, derived from a liquid-drop fit to the free energy per baryon, does not decrease with temperature as typically expected but instead rises until it peaks at approximately T ≈ 20–40 MeV, where shell corrections become negligible; this counterintuitive behavior is attributed to shell dampening effects rather than a genuine material property.

## Method

The authors employ an equivparticle model with density-dependent quark masses that incorporate both linear confinement and leading-order perturbative interactions. They solve the Dirac equations for quarks within the mean-field approximation for spherically symmetric strangelets, using the Fermi-Dirac distribution to determine occupation probabilities at finite temperature. The surface tension and curvature term are extracted by fitting the free energy per baryon to a liquid-drop formula, and nucleon emission rates are calculated from the equilibrium densities of external neutron and proton gases using standard nuclear reaction rate formulae.

- Shell structures persist up to about T = 10 MeV in the MIT bag model (citing Mustafa 1996, Mustafa 1997).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strangelets at zero temperature (Xia 2018) and finite-temperature studies using the MIT bag model (Mustafa 1996, Mustafa 1997) by employing an equivparticle model that incorporates linear confinement and density-dependent quark masses. It also builds on earlier finite-temperature equivparticle model calculations that used the multiple reflection expansion method (Wen 2005, Chen 2022). The results are relevant for understanding the products of binary compact star mergers and heavy-ion collisions, and they identify certain extremely stable strangelets that may serve as key waiting points during the decay of larger strangelets.
