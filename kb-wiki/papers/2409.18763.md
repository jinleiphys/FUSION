# Hydrodynamics in the Carrollian regime

> Carroll hydrodynamics arises in the $c\to 0$ limit of relativistic hydrodynamics. Instances of its relevance include the Bjorken and Gubser flow models of heavy-ion collisions, where the ultrarelativistic nature of the flow makes the physics effectively Carrollian. In this paper, we explore the structure of hydrodynamics in what can be termed as the Carrollian regime, where instead of keeping only the leading terms in the $c\to 0$ limit of relativistic hydrodynamics, we perform a small-$c$ expansion and retain the subleading terms as well. We do so both for perfect fluids as well as viscous fluids incorporating first order derivative corrections. As apposite applications of the formalism, we utilize the subleading terms to compute modifications to the Bjorken and Gubser flow equations which bring in, in particular, dependence on rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper extends the strict $c\to 0$ (Carroll) limit of relativistic hydrodynamics by performing a small-$c$ expansion that retains subleading terms, arguing that these subleading corrections can be comparable to or larger than leading dissipative terms in the derivative expansion, and using them to compute modifications to the Bjorken and Gubser flow equations that introduce rapidity dependence.

## Method

The authors start from the Papapetrou-Randers parametrization of the spacetime metric and fluid four-velocity, which makes powers of $c$ manifest. They expand the relativistic perfect-fluid and first-order viscous energy-momentum tensors in powers of $c^2$, keeping terms up to ${\cal O}(c^2)$ beyond the leading Carroll limit, and then impose energy-momentum conservation order by order in $c$. The resulting subleading hydrodynamic equations are specialized to the geometric structures that map to Bjorken and Gubser flows, yielding corrected flow equations.

## Key numbers

- The fluid velocity expansion uses coefficients $C_n \equiv \frac{(2n-1)!!}{2^n n!}$ for the series $\mathcal{F} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} C_n \beta^{2n} c^{2n}$.
- The leading-order perfect Carroll fluid energy-momentum components are $\ost{T}{0}_{(0)}\mbox{}^{\!\rm t}_{\;\rm t} = -\e_{(0)}$, $\ost{T}{0}_{(0)}\mbox{}^{\!i}_{\,j} = p_{(0)} \delta^i_{\,j}$, $\ost{T}{0}_{(0)}\mbox{}^{\!\rm t}_{\;i} = \frac{b_i+\beta_i}{\Omega}(\e_{(0)}+p_{(0)})$, and $\ost{T}{0}_{(0)}\mbox{}^{\!i}_{\;\rm t} = 0$.
- The next-to-leading order perfect fluid components include $\ost{T}{2}_{(0)}\mbox{}^{\!i}_{\;\rm t} = -\Omega\beta^i(\epsilon_{(0)}+p_{(0)})$ and $\ost{T}{2}_{(0)}\mbox{}^{\!\rm t}_{\;\rm t} = -\epsilon_{(2)} - (\epsilon_{(0)}+p_{(0)})(\beta^2 + \vec{\beta}\cdot\vec{b})$.

## Context

This work extends the strict Carroll hydrodynamics developed by Ciambelli (2018), Petkou (2022), and Bagchi (2023) by including subleading $c^2$ corrections, similar in spirit to the approach of de Boer (2023). It builds on the mapping discovered by Bagchi (2023) between Carroll hydrodynamics and boost-invariant Bjorken/Gubser flow models for the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions. The new subleading terms enable modeling of departures from exact boost-invariance, which the exact Bjorken (1982) and Gubser (2010) flow models cannot capture.
