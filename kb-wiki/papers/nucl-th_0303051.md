# Roles des contraintes sur les signaux de transition de phase

> Ce papier presente la notion de signal de transition de phase pour un systeme de taille finie. Il se concentre sur le role des contraintes physiques sur ces signaux et la robustesse quant au changement d'ensemble statistique. Des resultats obtenus avec un modele de gaz sur reseau sont presentes en analogie avec les observations effectuees en collisions d'ions lourds aux energies intermediaires.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ability of an observable to serve as an order parameter for a phase transition in finite systems depends critically on the statistical ensemble (i.e., the physical constraints applied), and that the largest fragment size, while a valid order parameter in the grand-canonical ensemble, loses this property in the canonical ensemble due to the fixed total mass constraint.

## Method

The author performed Monte Carlo simulations of a three-dimensional lattice-gas model (an extension of the Ising model) with an attractive nearest-neighbor interaction and kinetic energy terms. The Metropolis algorithm was used to sample microstates in both the grand-canonical ensemble (fixed chemical potential and temperature) and the canonical ensemble (fixed particle number and temperature). The distributions of the largest fragment size were analyzed for bimodality and for scale invariance (Delta-scaling) across a range of temperatures on a 6x6x6 lattice.

## Key numbers

- The simulations were performed on a 6x6x6 lattice, corresponding to 108 particles in the canonical ensemble.
- The critical chemical potential used for grand-canonical simulations was μ = 3ε (where ε is the interaction strength).
- In the grand-canonical ensemble, the transition from a bimodal to a unimodal distribution of the largest fragment size signals the critical point; in the canonical ensemble, this bimodality disappears entirely.
- For the Delta-scaling analysis, the expected exponent is Δ = 1 in the disordered phase and Δ = 1/2 at the critical point; the canonical ensemble shows no true scaling, but a limited temperature range can be falsely interpreted as a change from Δ = 1 to Δ = 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Gulminelli and Chomaz (1999) to use bimodality of the order parameter distribution as a phase transition signal in finite systems, and the Delta-scaling method of Botet and Ploszajczak (2000) for critical phenomena. The paper shows that these signals are not robust across statistical ensembles, contradicting the assumption that the largest fragment size can serve as a universal order parameter for nuclear multifragmentation experiments. The results enable future work to focus on asymmetry-based observables of the largest fragment distribution rather than simple order parameter identification.
