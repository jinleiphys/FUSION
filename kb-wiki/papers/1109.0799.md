# Quark Number Susceptibility and Thermodynamics in HTL approximation

> In HTL perturbation theory we obtain leading order quark number susceptibility as a response to an external disturbance, viz., chemical potential (μ) that generates density fluctuation, which is related to the correlation function through the thermodynamic sum rule associated with the symmetry of the system. We also obtain various thermodynamic quantities in leading order.

## Key claim

The authors show that leading-order hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) yields thermodynamically consistent results for quark number susceptibility and other thermodynamic quantities when an external chemical potential is incorporated, and that the thermodynamic sum rule relating susceptibility to the correlation function is satisfied at leading order, which had not been demonstrated previously within HTL approximation.

## Method

The authors reformulate HTL perturbation theory by introducing an external chemical potential (μ) as a source term in the HTL Lagrangian, then expand the Lagrangian to second order in δμ to obtain effective N-point functions. They compute the quark number density from the first derivative of the pressure with respect to μ, and the quark number susceptibility (QNS) from the second derivative, using the thermodynamic sum rule χ = ∂ρ/∂μ = ∂²𝒫/∂μ². They separately evaluate QNS from the correlation function expression χ = −lim_{p→0} Re Π₀₀(0,p) to verify the sum rule, decomposing both calculations into quasiparticle and Landau damping contributions.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order perturbative QNS for massless QCD at μ=0 is given by χ_p/χ_f = 1 − ½(g/π)² + √(1+N_f/6)(g/π)³ − ¾(g/π)⁴ log(1/g) + O(g⁴)
* The ratio R ≡ (χ_htl − χ_f)/(χ_p(g²) − χ_f) approaches unity in the weak coupling limit, confirming correct inclusion of order g² in the HTL approach
* The LO HTL quark number density and entropy density are shown for 2 flavours as functions of T/T_c, with lattice data from Alton (year not given in text) for comparison
* The 3-flavour scaled QNS is shown as a function of T/T_c with lattice data from multiple lattice groups (year not given in text)

## Context

This work extends the HTL perturbation theory framework developed by Andersen (year not given in text) and Blaizot (year not given in text) by incorporating an external chemical potential and verifying the thermodynamic sum rule for quark number susceptibility, which had not been shown earlier within HTL approximation. The authors note that various HTL approaches and methods by Blaizot (year not given in text), Mustafa (year not given in text), and Jiang (year not given in text) had led to different results for QNS, and their reformulation resolves these discrepancies at leading order. The results enable consistent leading-order calculations of conserved charge fluctuations in quark-gluon plasma, which serve as probes for QGP in heavy-ion collisions.
