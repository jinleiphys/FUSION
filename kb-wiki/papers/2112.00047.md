# Noether's theorems and the energy-momentum tensor in quantum gauge theories

> Noether's first and second theorems both imply conserved currents that can be identified as an energy-momentum tensor (EMT). The first theorem identifies the EMT as the conserved current associated with global spacetime translations, while the second theorem identifies it as a conserved current associated with local spacetime translations. This work obtains an EMT for quantum electrodynamics and quantum chromodynamics through the second theorem, which is automatically symmetric in its indices and invariant under the expected symmetries (e.g., BRST invariance) without the need for introducing an ad hoc improvement procedure.

## Key claim

Noether's second theorem, applied to local spacetime translations with careful accounting of the transformation properties of non-scalar fields (vector and spinor fields), yields an energy-momentum tensor for quantum electrodynamics and quantum chromodynamics that is automatically symmetric in its indices and gauge-invariant, without requiring the Belinfante improvement procedure. However, the paper contains an erratum noting that the treatment of spinor fields under local translations was erroneous because a finite linear double cover of GL(4,R) does not exist, meaning the spinor transformation formulas used in the derivation are incorrect.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bessel-Hagen (1921), Felsager (1981), Munoz (1996), Gamboa Saravi (2002, 2003), and Montesinos (2006), who applied Noether's second theorem to classical electromagnetism and Yang-Mills theory, to include spinor fields and the quantized gauge theories QED and QCD. The paper aims to resolve ambiguities in the hadron physics community regarding different versions of the QCD energy-momentum tensor operator (reviewed by Leader and Lorc'e, 2013) by providing an unambiguous derivation. The erratum notes that the spinor transformation formulas are incorrect and refers to Freese (2025) for a corrected derivation yielding an asymmetric but gauge-invariant EMT, and to Barroso Sa (2025) for an alternative approach using vierbein transformations that produces the symmetric EMT.
