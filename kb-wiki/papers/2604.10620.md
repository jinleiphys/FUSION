# Complementary Approach to Anisotropic Flows in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We introduce a no-reaction-plane (no-RP) method for extracting directed (\(v_1\)) and elliptic (\(v_2\)) flows in heavy-ion collisions, which eliminates the need for event-plane reconstruction. %by scanning over fixed test angles and using simple count asymmetries. The method is validated with PHSD model simulations of Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2\) GeV at freeze-out (impact parameter \(b = 4\) fm). We demonstrate that the two asymmetries for each harmonic contribute equally, i.e., \(\langle A_{\mathrm{ud}}^2\rangle \approx \langle A_{\mathrm{lr}}^2\rangle\) and \(\langle A_1^2\rangle \approx \langle A_2^2\rangle\), so that a single asymmetry measurement suffices for a good flow estimate. Event-by-event comparisons with direct calculations using the true reaction plane yield Pearson correlation coefficients of 0.985 for \(v_2\) and 0.883 for \(v_1\), confirming that the no-RP method captures flow fluctuations well enough.

# Complementary Approach to Anisotropic Flows in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The no-reaction-plane (no-RP) method can extract directed flow \(v_1\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) on an event-by-event basis without event-plane reconstruction, achieving Pearson correlation coefficients of 0.834 for \(v_1\) and 0.956 for \(v_2\) when compared to direct calculations using the true reaction plane in PHSD model simulations, though the method's accuracy is better for \(v_2\) because the higher harmonic \(v_4\) is expected to be very small.

## Method

The authors define fixed-plane count asymmetries (up-down \(A_{\mathrm{ud}}\), left-right \(A_{\mathrm{lr}}\) for directed flow; in-out \(A_1\) and rotated \(A_2\) for elliptic flow) and extend them to event-by-event measurements by scanning over a set of test angles \(\psi\) from 0 to \(\pi\), averaging the squared asymmetries over \(\psi\). They validate the method using Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) model simulations of Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2\) GeV at freeze-out for impact parameters \(b = 4-8\) fm, comparing the no-RP estimates against direct calculations that reconstruct the event plane from the \(Q\)-vector of particles with \(p_T > 0.2\) GeV/\(c\).

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2\) GeV at freeze-out, impact parameter \(b = 4-8\) fm
- Pearson correlation coefficient between no-RP method and direct calculation: \(r = 0.956\) for \(v_2\) (elliptic flow)
- Pearson correlation coefficient between no-RP method and direct calculation: \(r = 0.834\) for \(v_1\) (directed flow)
- The squared asymmetry ratios \(\langle A_{\mathrm{ud}}^2 \rangle_{\psi} / (\langle A_{\mathrm{ud}}^2 \rangle_{\psi} + \langle A_{\mathrm{lr}}^2 \rangle_{\psi})\) and \(\langle A_{1}^2 \rangle_{\psi} / (\langle A_{1}^2 \rangle_{\psi} + \langle A_{2}^2 \rangle_{\psi})\) cluster around 0.5, demonstrating equal contribution from each asymmetry pair

## Context

This work extends the fixed-plane asymmetry approach for event-averaged flow measurements to event-by-event extraction by scanning over test angles, eliminating the need for event-plane reconstruction that is challenging in experimental environments with finite acceptance and detector effects. The method is validated against PHSD model simulations, which previous work by the PHSD collaboration (referenced as "Prev" in the text) used to demonstrate the rise of azimuthal anisotropies as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation. The no-RP technique provides a simpler experimental alternative that relies only on counting particles in predefined azimuthal regions relative to a laboratory orientation, with potential applications across a wide range of collision systems and energies.
