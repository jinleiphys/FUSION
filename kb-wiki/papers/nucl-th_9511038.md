# Deep Inelastic Lepton Scattering in Nuclei at x > 1 and the Nucleon Spectral Function

> The nuclear structure function F_2A(x) has been studied in the Bjorken limit for (l, l') scattering on nuclei in the region of x > 1 and was found to be very sensitive to the information contained in the nucleon spectral function in nuclei, particularly the correlations between momenta and energies in the region of large momenta. Calculations were done in a local density approximation using two different spectral functions for nuclear matter. Results are compared to those obtained for a spectral function which has been evaluated directly for the finite nucleus, ^{16}O, under consideration. For values of x around 1.5 and larger the quasiparticle contribution is negligible, thus stressing the sensitivity of the present reaction to the dynamical properties of nuclei beyond the shell model approach. Several approximations which are usually employed in studies of the EMC effect have been analyzed and their inaccuracy in this region is demonstrated. The results stress the fact that the nuclear structure function contains important information on nuclear dynamical correlations. Therefore further measurements of F_2A(x) in that region and for many nuclei would be most welcome.

## Key claim

The nuclear structure function \(F_{2A}(x)\) in the Bjorken limit for \(x > 1\) is extremely sensitive to the nucleon spectral function, particularly the correlations between nucleon momenta and energies at large momenta; the quasiparticle contribution becomes negligible for \(x\) around 1.5 and larger, meaning that measurements in this region probe nuclear dynamical correlations beyond the shell model, but the authors stress that approximations neglecting energy-momentum correlations (such as using only momentum distributions) can produce results in error by two or more orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear structure function \(F_{2A}(x)\) for deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering in the Bjorken limit using a convolution formula that folds the free nucleon structure function with the hole spectral function of the nucleus. They employed a local density approximation with two different spectral functions for infinite nuclear matter (a semiphenomenological one based on NN scattering data and a microscopic one from a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation with a One-Boson-Exchange potential) and also compared results to a spectral function evaluated directly for the finite nucleus \({}^{16}\)O. Relativistic corrections were included via a formalism using positive-energy spinors and a modified nucleon propagator.

## Key numbers

* At \(x = 1.5\), the value of the structure function \(F_{2A}(x)\) decreases by about three orders of magnitude relative to its value at \(x = 1\), but remains within measurable range.
* For \(x > 1\), approximations using only the momentum distribution (ignoring energy-momentum correlations) produce results that differ from the accurate spectral-function results by two or more orders of magnitude.
* A 10% increase in \(Im \Sigma(\omega,k)\) leads to increases in \(F_{2A}(x)\) of about 2% for \(0 < x < 0.6\) and always below the 10% level for large \(x\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the EMC effect and the ratio \(R(x)\) of nuclear to deuteron structure functions, where Fermi motion was identified as important near \(x \simeq 1\) (e.g., works by Bodek and Ritchie (1981) and others). Previous investigations of \(x > 1\) (e.g., by Frankfurt and Strikman (1988) and others) used phenomenological momentum distributions and concluded that a tail in the momentum distribution for \(k > k_F\) was required; the present paper demonstrates that such momentum-only treatments are dangerously incomplete because they neglect energy-momentum correlations encoded in the spectral function. The results enable future experimental measurements of \(F_{2A}(x)\) for \(x > 1\) across many nuclei to serve as a direct probe of nuclear dynamical correlations beyond the shell model.
