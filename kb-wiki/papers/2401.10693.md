# Fine-tuning of the quasi-bound $K^- pp$ state

> Characteristics of the quasi-bound state in the $K^- pp$ system strongly depend on the model of antikaon-nucleon interaction and weakly - on the nucleon-nucleon potential. In the present paper, dynamically exact Faddeev-type calculations with coupled $\bar{K}NN$ and $πΣN$ were performed using different models of the $ΣN$ and $πN$ interactions to study the influence of these "less important" interaction on the three-body result. In addition, dynamically exact three-body Faddeev-type AGS calculations with three coupled particle channels $\bar{K}NN - πΣN - πΛN$ were performed.

## Key claim

The binding energy and width of the $K^- pp$ quasi-bound state depend strongly on the antikaon-nucleon interaction model and weakly on the nucleon-nucleon potential, but the present work shows that the "less important" $\Sigma N - \Lambda N$ interaction can change the three-body result quite strongly, while dependence on the $\pi N$ potential is weaker; the effect of directly including the third $\pi \Lambda N$ channel in three-body calculations yields different changes depending on the antikaon-nucleon model, but the reason for the differences (new potentials versus the third channel) requires further study.

## Method

Dynamically exact Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations were solved in momentum representation for the $K^- pp$ system with spin $S^{(3)} = 0$ and isospin $I^{(3)} = 1/2$, using separable two-body potentials with orbital momentum $l=0$. Calculations were performed first with two coupled particle channels ($\bar{K}NN - \pi \Sigma N$) using different models of the $\Sigma N - \Lambda N$ interaction and $\pi N$ potential, and then for the first time with three coupled channels ($\bar{K}NN - \pi \Sigma N - \pi \Lambda N$) using newly constructed antikaon-nucleon potentials coupling all three channels.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy $B_{K^-pp}$ with the chirally motivated potential and the most reliable hyperon-nucleon model ($V_{\Sigma N-\Lambda N}^{\rm ScL,SDep}$) and $\pi N$ switched off: $27.9$ MeV; width $\Gamma_{K^-pp}$: $42.8$ MeV (two-channel calculation).
- Binding energy with the one-pole phenomenological potential $V_{\bar{K}N}^{\rm 1,SIDD}$ and the same hyperon-nucleon model: $34.3$ MeV; width: $65.6$ MeV (two-channel calculation).
- Binding energy with the two-pole phenomenological potential $V_{\bar{K}N}^{\rm 2,SIDD}$ and the same hyperon-nucleon model: $34.7$ MeV; width: $53.4$ MeV (two-channel calculation).
- Three-channel calculation with $V_{\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma - \pi \Lambda}^{\rm 1,SIDD}$ and $V_{\pi N}^{\rm ScL-1}$: $B_{K^-pp} = 34.5$ MeV, $\Gamma_{K^-pp} = 52.0$ MeV.
- Three-channel calculation with $V_{\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma - \pi \Lambda}^{\rm Chiral}$ and $V_{\pi N}^{\rm ScL-1}$: $B_{K^-pp} = 26.8$ MeV, $\Gamma_{K^-pp} = 59.6$ MeV.
- Experimental binding energy from the E15 experiment at J-PARC: $42 \pm 3 (\rm stat.) {}^{+3}_{-4} (\rm syst.)$ MeV; experimental width: $100 \pm 7 (\rm stat.) {}^{+19}_{-9} (\rm syst.)$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous dynamically exact AGS calculations of the $K^- pp$ system (Shevchenko, 2023) by systematically studying the influence of the $\Sigma N - \Lambda N$ and $\pi N$ interactions that were previously treated with simpler models or neglected. The new three-channel $\bar{K}NN - \pi \Sigma N - \pi \Lambda N$ calculations are performed for the first time, using newly constructed antikaon-nucleon potentials that directly couple all three channels and reproduce the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data and threshold branching ratios. The results show that the theoretical predictions for the $K^- pp$ state remain sensitive to the choice of subleading interactions, and the experimental width measured by the E15 collaboration at J-PARC ($100$ MeV) remains much larger than all theoretical predictions obtained here (ranging from $39.3$ to $96.5$ MeV depending on the model).
