# Angular structure of many-body correlations in atomic nuclei: From nuclear deformations to diffractive vector meson production in $γA$ collisions

> There is growing evidence that high-energy scattering processes involving nuclei can offer unique insights into the many-body correlations present in nuclear ground states, in particular those of deformed nuclei. These processes involve, for instance, the collective anisotropic flows in heavy-ion collisions, or the diffractive production of vector mesons in photo-nuclear ($γA$) interactions. In this paper, we use a classical approximation and simple analytical models in order to exhibit characteristic and universal features of ground-state correlation functions that result from the presence of a deformed intrinsic state. In the case of a small axial quadrupole deformation, we show that the random rotation of the intrinsic density of the nucleus leads to a specific quadrupole modulation of the lab-frame two-body density as a function of the relative azimuthal angle. As a phenomenological, albeit academic application, we analyze the diffractive production of vector mesons in high-energy $γ+^8$Be collisions. This demonstrates with the simplest deformed nucleus how the two-body correlations impact the $|t|$ dependence of the incoherent cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for nuclei with a small axial quadrupole deformation, the random rotation of the intrinsic deformed density produces a universal quadrupole modulation of the lab-frame two-body density-density correlation function as a function of the relative azimuthal angle, with the caveat that this result relies on a classical approximation and simple analytical models rather than a full quantum-mechanical treatment.

## Method

The authors use a classical rotor-model approximation where ground-state correlations are generated by averaging over random orientations of an intrinsic deformed state, assuming nucleons are uncorrelated within the intrinsic state. They analytically compute the density-density correlation function for several model systems: a thin rod, a dumbbell with Gaussian blobs, and a Gaussian density mimicking a deformed nucleus. As a phenomenological application, they analyze coherent and incoherent diffractive vector meson production in high-energy gamma-beryllium-8 collisions using a harmonic-oscillator model for the beryllium-8 nucleus.

## Key numbers

* For small quadrupole deformations, the density-density correlation function behaves as delta^2 r_1^2 r_2^2 cos(2 phi_12), where delta is a measure of the deformation and phi_12 = phi_1 - phi_2 is the relative azimuthal angle.
* For the dumbbell model with deformation parameter w/L = 0.2, the connected correlation function G_c(r,r,phi_12) shows sharp peaks at phi_12 = 0 and phi_12 = pi, with a plateau region between them; for w/L = 0.5, the peaks soften into a cos(2 phi_12) modulation.
* For the dumbbell model with large deformation (w/L much less than 1), the connected correlation function at phi_12 = pi/2 and r = L is approximately -exp(-(L-r)^2/w^2) / (8 pi^3 w^2 L r).
* The eccentricity parameter epsilon = (x^2 - y^2)/(x^2 + y^2) for the dumbbell thickness function is given by epsilon = (L^2 sin^2 theta / w^2) / (2 + L^2 sin^2 theta / w^2), ranging from 0 to 1.

## Context

This work extends the rotor-model approach to nuclear correlations previously used in heavy-ion collision analyses by Jia (2021) and Jia (2021), and connects to the framework of Glauber theory and eikonal approximation for high-energy scattering. The paper shows that the same density-density correlation function probed in diffractive vector meson production in gamma-A collisions (as studied by Caldwell and collaborators in 2010) can reveal nuclear deformation effects, with maximal sensitivity at momentum transfers where the coherent cross section exhibits a diffractive minimum. This enables future experiments (such as those proposed by Kesler in 2025) to extract nuclear deformation information from high-energy scattering data.
