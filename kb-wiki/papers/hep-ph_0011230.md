# Measuring the P-odd Pion-Nucleon Coupling h_{πNN}^{(1)} in π^+ Photoproton Production Near the Threshold

> We show that polarized gamma p -> π^+ n in the threshold region is an excellent candidate for measuring the leading parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling h_{πNN}^{(1)} to an uncertainty of 20% if it has a natural size from dimensional analysis. The conclusion is based on a large unpolarized cross section, a new low-energy theorem for the photon polarization asymmetry at the threshold A_γ|_{th} = \sqrt{2} f_π(μ_p-μ_n) h_{πNN}^{(1)}/g_A m_N \sim h_{πNN}^{(1)}/2, and its strong dominance at forward and backward angles in the threshold region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the photon helicity asymmetry in polarized $\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ n$ near threshold can determine the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}$ to 20% uncertainty, *provided* that coupling has a natural size from dimensional analysis (around $5 \times 10^{-7}$). The hedge is that the 20% estimate relies on higher-order chiral corrections being of order $\epsilon/m_N$ (about 20%), which the authors argue is likely but not rigorously proven at the level of the paper.

## Method

The authors derive a low-energy theorem for the photon helicity asymmetry at threshold in the chiral limit, then compute the asymmetry at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$\chi$PT), including explicit $\Delta$-resonance degrees of freedom. They compare the leading-order unpolarized cross section to existing data up to $E_\gamma = 200$ MeV to validate the chiral expansion, then use the parity-violating effective Lagrangian at ${\cal O}(p^0)$ to compute the P-odd T-matrix and the resulting asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy theorem gives the threshold asymmetry: $A_\gamma|_{\rm th} = \sqrt{2} f_\pi (\mu_p - \mu_n) h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} / g_A m_N \sim h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}/2$, with the numerical coefficient 0.52 when plugging in known physical constants.
- The expected asymmetry magnitude is $\sim 2 \times 10^{-7}$ for a natural-sized $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} \sim 5 \times 10^{-7}$.
- The unpolarized total cross section in the threshold region is $\sim 100\ \mu$b ($10^{-28}\ \text{cm}^2$).
- The threshold region is defined as laboratory photon energies from threshold up to $E_\gamma = 200$ MeV, where the leading-order HB$\chi$PT describes unpolarized cross section data within 10%.
- At $E_\gamma = 200$ MeV and $\theta = 90^\circ$, the asymmetry deviates from the threshold value by less than 40%.
- The required luminosity is $10^{37}/(\text{cm}^2\ \text{s})$, yielding a $\pi^+$ production rate of $10^8/\text{s}\cdot\text{rad}$, requiring $\sim 10^6$ seconds of beam time.

## Context

This work extends earlier meson-exchange model calculations by Woloshyn (1982) and Li, Henley, and Hwang (1991), who noted the dominance of the $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}$-type P-odd coupling in the asymmetry near threshold. The present paper sharpens those findings by deriving a low-energy theorem and using modern HB$\chi$PT to defend the dominance in the threshold region. The process is proposed as an alternative to parity-violating measurements in few-body systems (e.g., $\vec{n}p \rightarrow d\gamma$ at LANSCE, $\gamma d \rightarrow np$ at JLab) and single-nucleon Compton scattering proposed by Butler and Savage (1999), which suffers from small cross sections. A precise measurement of $h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}$ would resolve discrepancies in extractions from $^{18}$F and $^{133}$Cs many-body systems and test the $s$-quark dominance prediction from QCD.
