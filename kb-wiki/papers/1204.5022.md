# Dineutron and the three-nucleon continuum observables

> We investigate how strong a hypothetical 1S0 bound state of two neutrons would affect different observables in the neutron-deuteron reactions. To that aim we extend our momentum space scheme of solving three-nucleon Faddeev equations to incorporate in addition to the deuteron also the 1S0 dineutron bound state. We discuss effects induced by dineutron on the angular distribution of the neutron-deuteron elastic scattering and cross sections of the deuteron breakup. A comparison to the available data for neutron-deuteron total cross sections and elastic scattering angular distributions cannot decisively exclude a possibility that the two neutrons can form 1S0 bound state. However, the strong modifications of a final-state-interaction peak of the neutron-deuteron breakup when changing from negative to positive values of the neutron-neutron scattering length seems to exclude existence of dineutron.

# Dineutron and the three-nucleon continuum observables

## Key claim

The available neutron-deuteron total cross section data are incompatible with a dineutron bound state having binding energy greater than approximately 100 keV, but the elastic scattering angular distribution data alone cannot decisively exclude the existence of a 1S0 dineutron. However, the strong modifications of the final-state-interaction peak in neutron-deuteron breakup when changing from negative to positive values of the neutron-neutron scattering length seems to exclude existence of dineutron.

## Method

The authors extended their momentum-space scheme for solving three-nucleon Faddeev equations to incorporate, in addition to the deuteron, a hypothetical 1S0 bound state of two neutrons (dineutron). They modified the 1S0 neutron-neutron component of the CD Bonn potential by multiplying it with a factor λ (ranging from 0.9 to 1.5) to generate forces with different scattering lengths and, for λ > 1, dineutron binding energies. They also performed calculations with a chiral next-to-leading-order (NLO) potential to test potential dependence of the conclusions.

## Key numbers

- For λ = 1.21, the dineutron binding energy is ε_nn = -144 keV; the predicted total cross sections for energies up to ≈ 10 MeV differ from data by about three standard deviations.
- The quartet nd scattering length remains practically constant under modifications of the 1S0 nn force, staying close to the experimental value ⁴a_nd = (6.35 ± 0.02) fm.
- The average value of the neutron-neutron scattering length extracted from four FSI configurations is a_nn = -18.7 ± 0.7 fm (negative sign).
- For the NLO chiral potential, a negative scattering length a_nn = -17.6 fm and a positive scattering length a_nn = +17.5 fm were used for comparison.
- The cross section for the n + d → p + dineutron reaction is by an order of magnitude smaller than for nd elastic scattering.

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of discrepancies between data and theoretical predictions based on modern nucleon-nucleon potentials such as AV18, CD Bonn, and Nijmegen potentials, as well as chiral perturbation theory forces (Epelbaum, 2005). The authors previously identified that the nn quasi-free-scattering and symmetrical space-star configurations in nd breakup are dominated by 1S0 and 3S1 contributions and show ≈ 20% discrepancies with data (Witala, 2006; Witala, 2008). The present study enables future Monte Carlo analyses of experimental proton spectra to search for dineutron signals, and provides constraints on the maximum allowed dineutron binding energy from existing nd data.
