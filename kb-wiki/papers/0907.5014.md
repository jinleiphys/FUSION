# Applicability of viscous hydrodynamics at RHIC

> In an earlier work (arXiv:0808.0953) we established that causal Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics is only accurate in RHIC applications at very low shear viscosities 4 pi eta_s / s < ~ 1.5-2. We show here that the region of applicability is significantly reduced if bulk viscosity plays a role in the dynamics.

# Applicability of viscous hydrodynamics at RHIC

## Key claim
Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics is only accurate for RHIC applications when shear viscosity is very low (4π η_s/s ≲ 1.5-2), and the region of applicability shrinks significantly if bulk viscosity plays a role in the dynamics. The authors show that even for local thermal equilibrium initial conditions, proper times earlier than τ ≈ 0.6 fm are not accessible unless bulk viscosity is negligible or shear viscosity is below the conjectured minimal value of η_s = s/(4π).

## Method
The authors solved the complete set of Israel-Stewart equations for a boost-invariant Bjorken scenario with axial and transverse translational symmetry, using a massless e = 3p equation of state merged with lattice QCD results at low temperatures. They mapped entropy production relative to initial entropy as a function of thermalization time, initial shear stress, and initial bulk pressure for various combinations of shear and bulk viscosity parameters, imposing ΔS/S₀ ≤ 0.2 as the condition for validity.

## Key numbers
- For minimal shear viscosity η_s/s = 1/(4π) with vanishing bulk viscosity, local equilibrium initial conditions require τ₀ ≳ 0.3 fm for applicability, Navier-Stokes initial conditions require τ₀ ≳ 0.6 fm, and CGC initial conditions require τ₀ ≳ 1 fm
- With the default bulk viscosity parameterization (ζ_m = 0.2, ΔT = 0.03 GeV, T_c = 0.192 GeV), local equilibrium initial conditions require τ₀ ≳ 1 fm
- With half the default bulk viscosity (ζ_m = 0.1), local equilibrium initial conditions are suitable when τ₀ ≳ 0.6 fm, and Navier-Stokes initial conditions require τ₀ ≳ 1 fm
- Default initial condition for Au+Au at RHIC: e₀ = 15 GeV/fm³ (T₀ ≈ 0.297 GeV) at thermalization time τ₀ = 0.6 fm
- The 20% rule of thumb: dissipative corrections to pressure and entropy must not exceed about 20% for 10% accuracy in computing dissipative effects

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Molnar and Huovinen, 2008) that established the validity region for Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics with only shear viscosity, now incorporating bulk viscosity. It contradicts the earlier study by Fries et al (2008) by showing that entropy production was overestimated in that work due to thermodynamic inconsistency. The results enable more realistic assessments of whether viscous hydrodynamics can describe early-time dynamics at RHIC, and suggest that unless shear viscosity is significantly below the conjectured minimal value of η_s = s/(4π) from Son (2002), or bulk viscosity is negligible, hydrodynamics cannot be applied at proper times earlier than about 0.6 fm.
