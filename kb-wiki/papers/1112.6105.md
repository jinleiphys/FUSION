# On the near-threshold peak structure in the differential cross section of ϕ-meson photoproduction: indication of a missing resonance with non-negligible strangeness content

> The details of the analysis of the near-threshold bump structure in the forward differential cross section of the ϕ-meson photoproduction to determine whether it is a signature of a resonance are presented. The analysis is carried out in an effective Lagrangian approach which includes Pomeron and (π,η) exchanges in the t channel, and contributions from the s- and u-channel excitations of a postulated nucleon resonance. In addition to the differential cross sections, we use the nine spin-density matrix elements (SDME) as recently measured, instead of the phi-meson decay angular distributions which depend only on six SDMEs as was done before, to constrain the resonance parameters. We conclude that indeed the nonmonotonic behavior, along with the other experimental data as reported by LEPS, can only be explained with an assumption of the excitation of a resonance of spin 3/2, as previously reported. However, both parities can account for the data equally well with almost identical mass of 2.08 +/- 0.04 GeV and width of 0.501 +/- 0.117 and 0.570 +/- 0.159 for 3/2^+ and 3/2^-, respectively. The ratio of the helicity amplitudes A_{1/2}/A_{3/2} calculated from the resulting coupling constants differs in sign from that of the known D_{13}(2080). More experimental data on single and double polarization observables are needed to resolve the parity. We further find that with an assumption of large values of the OZI-evading parameters x_{OZI} = 12 for J^P = 3/2^- and x_{OZI} = 9 for J^P = 3/2^+, the discrepancy between the recent experimental data on ω-meson photoproduction and theoretical model can be considerably reduced. We argue that the large value of x_{OZI} indicates that the postulated resonance contains non-negligible amount of strangeness content.

## Key claim

The near-threshold bump structure in forward differential cross sections of φ-meson photoproduction can only be explained by assuming excitation of a spin-3/2 nucleon resonance, but both positive and negative parity assignments fit the data equally well, with the resonance mass near 2.08 GeV. The authors argue that the large OZI-evading parameters required (x_OZI = 12 for 3/2⁻ and x_OZI = 9 for 3/2⁺) indicate the postulated resonance contains non-negligible strangeness content, though the parity cannot be resolved with current data.

## Method

The analysis uses an effective Lagrangian approach including t-channel Pomeron and (π, η) exchanges as background, plus s- and u-channel excitations of a postulated nucleon resonance. The model is fitted simultaneously to LEPS data on forward differential cross sections as a function of photon energy, differential cross sections as a function of t at eight photon energies, and nine spin-density matrix elements (SDMEs) at three photon energies, using the MINUIT package to determine resonance mass, width, and coupling constant products.

## Key numbers

- Resonance mass: 2.08 ± 0.04 GeV for both 3/2⁺ and 3/2⁻ assignments
- Resonance width: 0.501 ± 0.117 GeV for 3/2⁺; 0.570 ± 0.159 GeV for 3/2⁻
- χ²/N: 0.891 for 3/2⁺; 0.821 for 3/2⁻
- OZI-evading parameter x_OZI = 12 for J^P = 3/2⁻; x_OZI = 9 for J^P = 3/2⁺
- Helicity amplitude ratio A_{1/2}/A_{3/2} = 1.05 for the postulated 3/2⁻ resonance, compared to -1.18 for the known D_{13}(2080)
- Pomeron strength factor C_P = 3.65, with threshold factor s_th = 1.3 GeV²
- Cut-off parameter Λ = 1.2 GeV for all resonances and for π, η exchanges

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Kiswandhi, 2010) by using the full set of nine spin-density matrix elements from newer LEPS data (Chang, 2010) rather than only the six SDMEs accessible through φ-meson decay angular distributions, providing more stringent constraints on resonance parameters. The study contradicts earlier coupled-channel analysis by Ozaki (2009) which preferred J^P = 1/2⁻, and finds that spin-1/2 resonances cannot fit the data (χ²/N ≈ 5–9). The results enable future experimental searches using single and double polarization observables to resolve the parity ambiguity, and suggest that the resonance may have significant strangeness content, potentially reducing discrepancies in ω-meson photoproduction models.
