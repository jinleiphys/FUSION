# Temporal Entanglement Entropy as a probe of Renormalization Group Flow

> The recently introduced concept of timelike entanglement entropy has sparked a lot of interest. Unlike the traditional spacelike entanglement entropy, timelike entanglement entropy involves tracing over a timelike subsystem. In this work, we propose an extension of timelike entanglement entropy to Euclidean space ("temporal entanglement entropy"), and relate it to the renormalization group (RG) flow. Specifically, we show that tracing over a period of Euclidean time corresponds to coarse-graining the system and can be connected to momentum space entanglement. We employ Holography, a framework naturally embedding RG flow, to illustrate our proposal. Within cutoff holography, we establish a direct link between the UV cutoff and the smallest resolvable time interval within the effective theory through the irrelevant $T\bar T$ deformation. Increasing the UV cutoff results in an enhanced capability to resolve finer time intervals, while reducing it has the opposite effect. Moreover, we show that tracing over a larger Euclidean time interval is formally equivalent to integrating out more UV degrees of freedom (or lowering the temperature). As an application, we point out that the temporal entanglement entropy can detect the critical Lifshitz exponent $z$ in non-relativistic theories which is not accessible from spatial entanglement at zero temperature and density.

## Key claim

The authors propose that tracing over a Euclidean time interval corresponds to coarse-graining in quantum field theory and can be connected to momentum space entanglement entropy; they show this specifically within holography using the irrelevant \(T\bar T\) deformation, where the UV cutoff is directly linked to the smallest resolvable Euclidean time interval.

## Method

The authors work in holographic AdS\(_3\)/CFT\(_2\) and compute the area of minimal surfaces (geodesics) that connect two points separated by a Euclidean time interval \(T_0\) at the boundary, using the Ryu-Takayanagi formula. They implement a finite radial cutoff \(\eta_c\) in the bulk, which is the holographic dual of a \(T\bar T\) deformation of the boundary field theory, and derive the entanglement entropy as a function of \(T_0\) and \(\eta_c\). The calculation is extended to finite temperature (thermal AdS and BTZ black hole) and to non-relativistic Lifshitz geometries with dynamical critical exponent \(z\) and hyperscaling violation exponent \(\theta\).

## Key numbers

* For undeformed AdS\(_3\) (no \(T\bar T\) deformation), the temporal entanglement entropy at large cutoff expands as \(S_\text{EE} = \frac{c}{3} \log\!\left(\frac{\sqrt{\delta T_0} R}{L}\right)\) where \(\delta T_0 = \cosh(T_0)-1\) and \(R = L\sinh(\eta_c)\).
* For the \(T\bar T\) deformed theory, the condition for a minimal surface to exist is \(T_0 > 2 \log((\sqrt{L^2+R^2}+L)/R)\); in the limit \(R\to\infty\) this becomes \(T_0 R > \sqrt{c\mu/(6\pi)}\).
* For finite temperature in the BTZ black hole (\(\mu>1\)), the temporal entanglement entropy at large cutoff is \(S_\text{EE} = \frac{L}{2G_N} \log \left(\frac{\eta_c \sin (\pi T T_0)}{\pi L^2 T}\right)\).
* For thermal AdS (\(\mu=0\)), the temporal entanglement entropy at large cutoff is \(S_\text{EE} = \frac{L}{2G_N} \log \left(\frac{2 \eta_c \sinh (T_0/(2L))}{L}\right)\).
* For non-relativistic geometries with \(d_e=2\) and dynamical exponent \(z\), the temporal entanglement entropy at large cutoff expands as \(S_\text{EE} = \frac{1}{2 G_N}\left(\log\left(z^z \frac{T_0^{1/z}}{\varepsilon}\right) + \ldots\right)\) where \(\varepsilon = 1/\eta_c\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of timelike entanglement entropy introduced by Doi et al. (2023) from Minkowski space to Euclidean space, and connects it to momentum space entanglement originally studied by Balasubramanian et al. (2011) and Costa et al. (2022). The holographic implementation builds on the cutoff holography proposal of McGough, Mezei, and Verlinde (2016) for \(T\bar T\) deformations, and on the surface/state correspondence of Miyaji et al. (2015). The results enable a new probe of the dynamical critical exponent \(z\) in non-relativistic theories, which is inaccessible from spatial entanglement at zero temperature and density, and suggest that temporal entanglement entropy can serve as a diagnostic of renormalization group flow.
