# Signatures of octupole correlations in neutron-rich odd-mass barium isotopes

> Octupole deformation and the relevant spectroscopic properties of neutron-rich odd-mass barium isotopes are investigated in a theoretical framework based on nuclear density functional theory and the particle-core coupling scheme. The interacting-boson Hamiltonian that describes the octupole-deformed even-even core nucleus, as well as the single-particle energies and occupation probabilities of an unpaired nucleon, are completely determined by microscopic axially-symmetric $(β_{2},β_{3})$-deformation constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations for a specific choice of the energy density functional and pairing interaction. A boson-fermion interaction that involves both quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom is introduced, and their strength parameters are determined to reproduce selected spectroscopic data for the odd-mass nuclei. The model reproduces recent experimental results both for the even-even and odd-mass Ba isotopes. In particular, for $^{145,147}$Ba our results indicate, in agreement with recent data, that octupole deformation does not determine the structure of the lowest states in the vicinity of the ground state, and only becomes relevant at higher excitation energies.

## Key claim

For the neutron-rich odd-mass isotopes \(^{145,147}\)Ba, octupole deformation does not determine the structure of the lowest states near the ground state, and only becomes relevant at higher excitation energies, in agreement with recent experimental data.

## Method

The authors combine nuclear density functional theory with the particle-core coupling scheme. The even-even core nucleus is described by an interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian that includes \(s\), \(d\), and \(f\) bosons (the \(sdf\)-IBM), with all parameters determined by mapping a microscopic axially-symmetric \((\beta_2,\beta_3)\)-deformation constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculation onto the boson Hamiltonian. The odd neutron is coupled to the core via an interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) Hamiltonian that includes quadrupole and octupole boson-fermion interactions, with strength parameters adjusted to reproduce selected spectroscopic data for \(^{145}\)Ba and then applied with minimal variation to \(^{143}\)Ba and \(^{147}\)Ba.

## Key numbers

- The SCMF calculation for \(^{144}\)Ba and \(^{146}\)Ba yields an equilibrium minimum with non-zero octupole deformation \(\beta_3 \approx 0.1\), while for \(^{142}\)Ba the minimum is at \(\beta_3 = 0\) with \(\beta_2 \approx 0.14\).
- In \(^{145}\)Ba, the experimental \({15/2}^-\) state at 1226 keV is identified as a candidate octupole state; the calculation places a \({15/2}^-_4\) state at 1167 keV dominated by one \(f\)-boson configuration.
- Predicted \(B(E3)\) values for transitions from the octupole band in \(^{145}\)Ba include \(B(E3; {15/2^-_4}\rightarrow {9/2^+_1}) = 25\) W.u. and \(B(E3; {19/2^-_2}\rightarrow {13/2^+_1}) = 31\) W.u., comparable to the \(B(E3; 3^-_1\rightarrow 0^+_1) = 23\) W.u. in the even-even core \(^{144}\)Ba.
- For \(^{143}\)Ba, predicted \(B(E3)\) values include \(B(E3; {15/2}^+_1\rightarrow {9/2}^-_1) = 11\) W.u. and \(B(E3; {17/2}^+_1\rightarrow {11/2}^-_1) = 13\) W.u.
- The boson E2 effective charge is set to \(e^{(2)}_B = 0.1108\,eb\), determined to reproduce the experimental \(B(E2; 2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)\) in \(^{144}\)Ba.

## Context

This work extends the method of Nomura (2016) for odd-mass nuclei to include octupole degrees of freedom via the \(sdf\)-IBM framework, which was previously applied to even-even nuclei by Nomura (2013, 2014). The study focuses on neutron-rich barium isotopes \(^{143,145,147}\)Ba, where recent experiments by Rząca-Urban (2012) and Zhu (1999) suggested that octupole deformation does not dominate the ground-state structure, in contrast to the neighboring even-even nucleus \(^{144}\)Ba, which has long been considered an example of pronounced octupole correlations (Leander, 1985). The results enable future comparisons with experimental \(B(E2)\) and \(B(E3)\) transition rates, which are currently unavailable for these odd-mass systems.
