# What different variants of chiral EFT predict for the proton Compton differential cross section - and why

> We compare the predictions of different variants of chiral effective field theory for the gamma-p elastic scattering differential cross section. We pay particular attention to the role of pion loops, and the impact that a heavy-baryon expansion has on the behavior of those loops. We also correct erroneous results for these loops that were published in Phys. Rev. C 67, 055202 (2003) [ arXiv:nucl-th/0212024 ].

## Key claim

The paper shows that different variants of chiral effective field theory (χEFT) for proton Compton scattering produce similar differential cross sections once the scalar dipole polarizabilities α_E1^(p) and β_M1^(p) are adjusted to the same values, but the heavy-baryon (HB) expansion produces a cusp at the πN threshold approximately twice as strong as the covariant calculation, and the HB calculation overshoots forward-angle data at higher energies.

## Method

The authors compare six variants of χEFT for γp elastic scattering: tree-level graphs only; tree plus Δ(1232) pole graphs; tree plus πN loops in heavy-baryon χEFT without explicit Δ; tree plus πN and πΔ loops with relativistic nucleon propagators; tree plus Δ poles and HB πN and πΔ loops; and tree plus Δ poles with relativistic πN and πΔ loops. All calculations include counterterm contributions adjusted to yield the same best-fit values of α_E1^(p)=10.5×10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_M1^(p)=2.7×10⁻⁴ fm³, and the unpolarized differential cross section is computed for lab energies from threshold to 350 MeV at fixed angles.

## Key numbers

- α_E1^(p) = 10.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_M1^(p) = 2.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ are the best-fit scalar dipole polarizabilities from Ref. Gr12, used as input for all calculations
- Counterterm contributions to α_E1^(p) range from -9.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (for +πHB,Δ) to +10.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (for +Δ); counterterm contributions to β_M1^(p) range from -7.1 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (for +πHB,Δ) to +4.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (for +π)
- The cusp from HB πN loops at πN threshold is approximately twice as strong as in the covariant calculation, measured relative to the +Δ calculation
- At ω_lab = 149 MeV, there is about a 10% difference between HB and covariant predictions at backward angles
- The +πHB,Δ calculation overshoots forward-angle data at ω_lab = 300-350 MeV

## Context

This work extends and corrects the χEFT calculations of Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003) for γp scattering, which contained erroneous results for πN loops above the πN threshold. It compares the heavy-baryon approach of Bernard et al. (1991, 1995) with the manifestly covariant approach of Lensky and Pascalutsa (2009), showing that differences between the two are consistent with expected higher-order effects once polarizabilities are fixed. The results enable interpretation of ongoing Compton scattering experiments at MAMI (Mainz) and HIγS (TUNL), and the authors note that examining χEFT predictions for asymmetries will be the next step.
