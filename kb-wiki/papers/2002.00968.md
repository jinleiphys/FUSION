# Free energy of a Holonomous Plasma

> At a nonzero temperature T, a constant field $\overline{A}_0 \sim T/g$ generates nontrivial eigenvalues of the thermal Wilson line. We discuss contributions to the free energy of such a holonomous plasma when the coupling constant, $g$, is weak. We review the computation to $\sim g^2$ by several alternate methods, and show that gauge invariant sources, which are nonlinear in the gauge potential $A_0$, generate novel contributions to the gluon self energy at $\sim g^2$. These ensure the gluon self energy remains transverse to $\sim g^2$, and are essential in computing contributions to the free energy at $\sim g^3$ for small holonomy, $\overline{A}_0 \sim T$. We show that the contribution $\sim g^3$ from off-diagonal gluons is discontinuous as the holonomy vanishes. The contribution from diagonal gluons is continuous as the holonomy vanishes, but sharply constrains the possible sources which generate nonzero holonomy, and must involve an infinite number of Polyakov loops.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while the free energy of a holonomous plasma can be computed perturbatively to order g^2, the contribution at order g^3 from off-diagonal gluons is discontinuous as the holonomy vanishes, and the contribution from diagonal gluons requires sources involving an infinite number of Polyakov loops for the holonomy to turn on smoothly.

## Method

The authors compute the free energy of a constant background field A0 ~ T/g in SU(N) gauge theory at nonzero temperature using background field gauge, expanding about a diagonal classical field with eigenvalues theta_a. They review the one-loop and two-loop computations using both an external source method and a constrained functional integral that fixes the Polyakov loop expectation value, then extend the analysis to order g^3 using hard thermal loop techniques and matrix model methods for the diagonal sector.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop holonomous potential is V_1(Theta) = - (T^3/pi^2) sum_{n=1}^infinity (1/n^4) |tr L^n|^2 = (2 pi^2 T^3/3) sum_{a,b=1}^N B_4((theta_a - theta_b)/(2 pi)), where B_4(x) = -1/30 + x^2(1-|x|)^2
- The two-loop perturbative potential involves Bernoulli polynomials B_1, B_2, and B_3, with B_2(x) = 1/6 - |x| + x^2 and B_3(x) = x/2 - (3/2) sign(x) x^2 + x^3
- The correction to the second-order Polyakov loop from off-diagonal gluons is (3 - xi) (g^2/(8 pi)) sum_{a != b} i r exp(i r theta_a) B_1((theta_a - theta_b)/(2 pi))

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative computations of the QGP free energy (Kapusta 1979, Arnold 1994, Zhai 1995) to include a nontrivial holonomy for Polyakov loops, building on the background field approach of Gross (1980), Weiss (1980), and later work by Elze (1989), Enqvist (1990), Belyaev (1991), Bhattacharya (1991, 1992), Korthals Altes (1993), Giovannangeli (2002, 2004), Dumitru (2013), and Guo (2014, 2018). The results enable refinement of effective theories for the semi-QGP (Pisarski 2000, Dumitru 2002-2012, Hidaka 2009, Korthals Altes 2019) and constrain the form of non-perturbative holonomous potentials used in those models.
