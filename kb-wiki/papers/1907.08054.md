# New thermonuclear reaction rate equations for radiative neutron capture

> The radiative neutron capture reaction rates have been studied at very low energies which are of interest for nuclear astrophysics. The rates for many of these reactions have remained independent of temperature so far. The temperature dependence of the thermonuclear reaction rates have been explored within the statistical model. Apart from the compound nuclear contribution, the pre-equilibrium as well as the direct effects have been taken into account. The corresponding Maxwellian-averaged thermonuclear reaction rates of relevance in astrophysical plasmas at temperatures in the range from 10$^6$ K to 10$^{10}$ K have been calculated. Analytical expression as a function of $T_9$ has been provided for $^6$Li(n,$γ$)$^7$Li by fitting the calculated reaction rate.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the thermonuclear reaction rates for four neutron-capture reactions previously treated as temperature-independent in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) networks actually show temperature dependence when calculated with the TALYS code including compound, pre-equilibrium, and direct contributions; however, for three of the four reactions (¹⁰B, ¹²C, ¹⁴N targets) the rates remain constant within the studied temperature range, while only ⁶Li(n,γ)⁷Li exhibits a clear monotonic increase with temperature.

## Method

The authors used the TALYS nuclear reaction code to calculate Maxwellian-averaged thermonuclear reaction rates for ⁶Li(n,γ)⁷Li, ¹⁰B(n,γ)¹¹B, ¹²C(n,γ)¹³C, and ¹⁴N(n,γ)¹⁵N over a temperature range from 10⁶ K to 10¹⁰ K (T₉ = 0.0001 to 10). The calculations employed the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model for compound nuclear contributions, with additional inclusion of pre-equilibrium and direct reaction mechanisms, and accounted for target excited states via temperature-dependent partition functions. The resulting rates for ⁶Li(n,γ)⁷Li were fitted to a third-order polynomial in T₉ to provide an analytical expression.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶Li(n,γ)⁷Li, the reaction rate at T₉ = 0.0001 is 408.43 cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹, rising to 1397.99 cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹ at T₉ = 10.0
- The new analytical fit for ⁶Li(n,γ)⁷Li is: N_A⟨σv⟩ = (387.75 ± 0.95) + (219.45 ± 1.91)T₉ - (20.45 ± 0.59)T₉² + (0.867 ± 0.045)T₉³ cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹
- For ¹⁰B(n,γ)¹¹B, the constant rate is (5.3318 × 10³ ± 8.67) cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹
- For ¹²C(n,γ)¹³C, the constant rate is (2.8749 × 10³ ± 5.81) cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹
- For ¹⁴N(n,γ)¹⁵N, the constant rate is (4.2081 × 10⁴ ± 3.97 × 10¹) cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹
- The previous Malaney-Fowler rate for ⁶Li(n,γ)⁷Li was 5.10 × 10³ cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹ (temperature-independent); a recent calculation by Su et al. (2010) gave (8.5 ± 1.7) × 10³ cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the reaction rate calculations of Malaney and Fowler (1989) and Wagoner (1969) by introducing temperature dependence for ⁶Li(n,γ)⁷Li, which had previously been treated as constant in BBN network codes such as the Kawano/Wagoner code. The new rates were incorporated into the BBN code modified by Singh et al. (2019), but primordial elemental abundances remained unchanged because these reactions do not play a significant role in standard BBN due to the scarcity of free neutrons; the authors suggest the rates may find usefulness in other domains such as stellar burning and stellar nucleosynthesis.
