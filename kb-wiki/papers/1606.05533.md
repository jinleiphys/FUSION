# Excited Scalar Mesons and the Search for Glueballs

> We discuss the importance of mixing between glueballs -- bound states of gluons -- and excited $\bar{q}q$ states for the glueball search. A preliminary study of the excited states in the Extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) suggests their masses in the scalar channel to be in the vicinity of the scalar-glueball mass found in Lattice QCD. This could have implications for future glueball searches.

# Excited Scalar Mesons and the Search for Glueballs

## Key claim

Mixing between the scalar glueball and excited (first radial excitation) $\bar{q}q$ states, rather than only ground-state $\bar{q}q$ mesons, may alter glueball properties and is therefore important for experimental glueball searches, but this is contingent on the excited $\bar{q}q$ masses being reasonably close to the scalar glueball mass found in Lattice QCD, which the authors present as a preliminary study.

## Method

The authors use the Extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM), which incorporates chiral symmetry and its breaking, dilatation symmetry and its breaking, discrete QCD symmetries, ground-state scalar/pseudoscalar/vector/axial-vector $\bar{q}q$ states in three flavours, a pure scalar dilaton (glueball) state, and first excitations in the scalar and pseudoscalar channels. They assign four experimentally known resonances to excited $\bar{q}q$ states, $\pi(1300)$, $\eta(1440)$, $K(1460)$, and $f_0(1790)$, to determine the model parameters, then predict the masses of the remaining three excited scalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- $m_{\pi^E} = 1300 \pm 100$ MeV (excited pseudoscalar isotriplet, used as input from PDG)
- $m_{\eta_S^E} = 1432 \pm 10$ MeV (excited pseudoscalar $\bar{s}s$ isosinglet, used as input from eta1440 analysis)
- $m_{K^E} = 1460 \pm 73$ MeV (excited pseudoscalar kaon, used as input from PDG with $\pm 5\%$ uncertainty)
- $m_{\sigma_N^E} = 1790 \pm 35$ MeV (excited scalar $\bar{n}n$ isosinglet, assigned to $f_0(1790)$ from BES, used as input)
- Predicted $m_{\sigma_S^E} = (1911 \pm 39)$ MeV (excited scalar $\bar{s}s$ isosinglet)
- Predicted $m_{K_{0}^{\star E}} = (1851 \pm 37)$ MeV (excited scalar kaon)
- Predicted $m_{a_0^E} = (1790 \pm 35)$ MeV (excited scalar $\bar{n}n$ isotriplet, equal to $m_{\sigma_N^E}$ due to setting $\kappa_2 = 0$)
- Pure-gauge Lattice QCD scalar glueball mass $\simeq 1.7$ GeV (Morningstar)

## Context

This work extends previous mixing scenarios (Janowski 2011, PGR, PKWGR) that assumed the scalar glueball mixes only with ground-state $\bar{q}q$ mesons, by considering mixing with first-excited $\bar{q}q$ states. The predicted masses for $\sigma_S^E$ and $K_{0}^{\star E}$ are close to the unconfirmed $f_0(2020)$ and $K_0^\star(1950)$ resonances from the PDG. The proximity of the excited isosinglet $\bar{q}q$ masses to the Lattice QCD scalar glueball mass (Morningstar) suggests that mixing effects could influence glueball phenomenology, which the authors state is under investigation. The work also highlights the potential of antiproton data from PANDA@FAIR for future meson discoveries, following the historical success of $\bar{p}p$ collisions (Amsler 1995).
