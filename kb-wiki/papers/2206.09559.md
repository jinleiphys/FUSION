# Near-BPS Skyrmions

> We consider the Skyrme model in the near-BPS limit. The BPS part is made of the sextic term plus a potential and the deformation is made of the standard massive Skyrme model controlled by a small parameter $ε\ll1$. In order to keep the perturbation under theoretical and computational control, we find a model for which BPS Skyrmions have compact support, henceforth denoted as compactons, and the spherically symmetric $B=1$ Skyrmion represents the most stable solution. We use the $ε$-expansion scheme to systematically calculate the corrections to the energy and compare with the exact numerical computations in the $B=1$ sector. Finally, we use the $ε$-expansion scheme to calculate the bound state of two $B=1$ Skyrmions and its binding energy, which corresponds, prior to quantization, to the deuteron in our model.

## Key claim

The paper develops a semi-analytic epsilon-expansion scheme for the near-BPS Skyrme model, where the BPS sector consists of the sextic term plus a potential that yields compacton solutions (solitons with compact support), and the deformation is the standard massive Skyrme model controlled by a small parameter epsilon much less than 1; the authors claim that this approach overcomes the numerical intractability that previously limited full numerical studies to epsilon approximately 0.2, whereas physically relevant binding energies require epsilon approximately 0.01.

## Method

The authors construct a BPS Skyrme model with a sextic derivative term and a potential chosen such that the BPS solutions have compact support (compactons), specifically using potentials with parameters (s,p) = (1,2) or (2,2) that yield finite leading-order energy corrections. They then apply an epsilon-expansion scheme around the BPS solution, computing corrections to the energy at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (N^2LO) using linearized perturbation fields, and solve the resulting equations for the spherically symmetric B=1 Skyrmion and for the bound state of two B=1 Skyrmions in the attractive channel.

## Key numbers

- The BPS mass for the (s,p)=(1,2) potential is M_BPS^(1,2) = 2*pi^2*sqrt(2*c_6)*mu*N, where N is the topological degree
- The BPS mass for the (s,p)=(2,2) potential is M_BPS^(2,2) = (3/2)*pi^2*sqrt(c_6)*mu*N
- The BPS mass for the (s,p)=(1,1) potential (standard pion mass) is M_BPS^(1,1) = (128*pi/15)*sqrt(2*c_6)*mu*N
- The BPS mass for the (s,p)=(2,1) potential is M_BPS^(2,1) = (16*pi/3)*sqrt(2*c_6)*mu*N
- Previous full numerical studies could only reach epsilon approximately 0.2, while physically relevant binding energies require epsilon approximately 0.01
- The compacton radius for the (s,p)=(2,2) potential with axial symmetry is R = cube_root(6*pi*sqrt(c_6)*N/mu)

## Context

This work extends the epsilon-expansion scheme previously developed by the same authors for the 2D baby Skyrme model (Gudnason 2020, Gudnason 2021) to the 3D Skyrme model, building on the BPS-Skyrme model introduced by Adam (2010). The paper addresses the restricted harmonic problem identified by Speight (2014) and the numerical difficulties encountered by Gillard (2015) who found that full numerical solutions develop spike-like singularities for epsilon below 0.2. The approach enables systematic calculation of binding energies for multi-Skyrmion configurations, with the bound state of two B=1 Skyrmions corresponding to the deuteron prior to quantization.
