# Confronting the nucleonic hypothesis with current neutron star observations from GW170817 and PSR J0740+6620

> The nuclear matter equation of state is relatively well constrained at sub-saturation densities thanks to the knowledge from nuclear physics. However, studying its behavior at supra-saturation densities is a challenging task. Fortunately, the extraordinary progress recently made in observations of neutron stars and neutron star mergers has provided us with unique opportunities to unfold the properties of dense matter. Under the assumption that nucleons are the only constituents of neutron star cores, we perform a Bayesian inference using the so-called meta-modeling technique with a nuclear-physics-informed prior. The latest information from the GW170817 event by the LIGO-Virgo Collaboration (LVC) and from the radius measurement of the heaviest known neutron star PSR J0740+6620 by the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) telescope and X-ray Multi-Mirror (XMM-Newton) are taken into account as likelihoods in the analysis. The impacts of different constraints on the equation of state as well as on the predictions of neutron star properties are discussed. The obtained posterior reveals that all the current observations are fully compatible with the nucleonic hypothesis. Strong disagreements between our results with future data can be identified as a signal for the existence of exotic degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

All current neutron star observations (GW170817, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0030+0451) are fully compatible with the hypothesis that neutron star cores contain only nucleons, electrons, and muons; however, this does not eliminate the possibility of exotic degrees of freedom, and strong disagreements with future data could signal their existence.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference using the meta-modeling technique, which expands the nuclear matter energy per nucleon as a Taylor series around saturation density truncated at fourth order, characterized by 13 bulk parameters. A nuclear-physics-informed prior was constructed from nuclear mass measurements (AME2016) and chiral effective field theory calculations, and likelihoods were applied from the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement, the mass of PSR J0348+0432, and the mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 from NICER and XMM-Newton. Four distributions were compared: Prior (nuclear masses only), LD (adding chiral EFT constraints), HD+LVC (adding mass and tidal deformability constraints), and All (adding NICER mass-radius measurements).

## Key numbers

- PSR J0740+6620 mass: \(2.08 \pm 0.07 M_{\odot}\) (Fonseca 2021)
- PSR J0348+0432 mass: \(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot}\) (Antoniadis 2013)
- Nuclear saturation density: \(\rho_{\rm sat} = 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)
- Pearson correlation coefficient between \(R_{1.4}\) and \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) in the All distribution: 0.97
- Final relative uncertainties in crustal thickness in the All distribution: up to \(\sim 10\%\)
- Chiral EFT filter applied in density interval \([0.02, 0.2]\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the meta-modeling approach of Margueron et al. (2018) and the Bayesian analysis of Dinh Thi et al. (2021) by incorporating the latest NICER+XMM-Newton radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 and the GW170817 tidal deformability constraints. The results are consistent with similar conclusions by Pang et al. (2021) and Legred et al. (2021), who found that hadronic composition is favored over strong phase transitions to quark matter. The analysis provides a null hypothesis for the nucleonic composition of neutron star cores, enabling future observations to identify signals of exotic degrees of freedom if they deviate from these predictions.
