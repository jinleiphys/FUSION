# Nuclear dependences of azimuthal asymmetries in the Drell-Yan process

> We study nuclear dependences of azimuthal asymmetries in the Drell-Yan lepton pair production in nucleon-nucleus collisions with polarized nucleons.We use the "maximal two-gluon correlation approximation", so that we can relate the transverse momentum dependent quark distribution in a nucleus to that in a nucleon by a convolution with a Gaussian broadening.We use the Gaussian ansatz for the transverse momentum dependence of such quark distribution functions,and obtain the numerical results for the nuclear dependences.These results show that the averaged azimuthal asymmetries are suppressed.

## Key claim

Under the "maximal two-gluon correlation approximation" for large, weakly-bound nuclei, the nuclear dependence of azimuthal asymmetries in the Drell-Yan process can be expressed via a Gaussian convolution of the nucleon-level transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distributions, leading to a suppression of the \(q_T\)-integrated azimuthal asymmetries.

## Method

The authors extended the TMD factorization formalism for the Drell-Yan process from nucleon-nucleon to nucleon-nucleus collisions, relating nuclear TMD quark distributions to nucleon TMD distributions via a Gaussian broadening with width \(\Delta_{2F}\). They adopted a Gaussian ansatz for the transverse momentum dependence of all relevant TMD parton distribution and correlation functions (e.g., \(f_1\), \(h_1^\perp\), \(f_{1T}^\perp\), \(h_{1L}^\perp\), \(h_1\), \(h_{1T}^\perp\)) and computed the resulting azimuthal asymmetries and their nuclear modification factors analytically.

## Key numbers

* The total average squared transverse momentum broadening for a gold nucleus is estimated as \(\Delta_{2F} \sim 0.12\ \text{GeV}^2\), using \(\hat{q} \approx 0.025\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\) and an average transport distance \(L_A = 3R_A/4 = 3 R_N A^{1/3}/4\).
* For the special case \(\alpha = \beta\), the nuclear modification factor for the \(\cos 2\phi\) asymmetry is \(R^{\cos 2\phi} = \left(2\alpha/(2\alpha+\Delta_{2F})\right)^2\), independent of the lepton-pair transverse momentum \(q_T\).
* The \(q_T\)-integrated nuclear modification factor for the \(\cos 2\phi\) asymmetry is \(\bar{R}^{\cos 2\phi} = 2\beta/(2\beta+\Delta_{2F})\).
* For the special cases \(\sigma_1 = \sigma_3 = \sigma_4 = 2\alpha - \beta\) and \(\sigma_2 = \alpha\), the \(q_T\)-integrated nuclear modification factors are: \(\bar{R}^{\sin 2\phi} = (2\alpha/(2\alpha+\Delta_{2F}))^2\), \(\bar{R}^{\sin \phi_s} = 2\alpha/(2\alpha+\Delta_{2F})\), \(\bar{R}^{\sin(2\phi-\phi_s)} = 2\alpha/(2\alpha+\Delta_{2F})\), and \(\bar{R}^{\sin(2\phi+\phi_s)} = (2\alpha/(2\alpha+\Delta_{2F}))^3\).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear dependence of azimuthal asymmetries from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS), as previously investigated by Gao et al. (2010) and Song et al. (2010), to the Drell-Yan process in nucleon-nucleus collisions. The approach builds on the "maximal two-gluon correlation approximation" developed by Liang et al. (2008) and the diffusion-equation derivation by Majumder et al. (2007). The results enable future experimental tests of nuclear medium effects on TMD distributions using Drell-Yan data, and the authors note that existing measurements of \(\langle\cos 2\phi\rangle\) in \(\pi A\) collisions by Badier (1981), Conway (1989), Guanziroli (1988), and Falciano (1986) are not yet precise enough to determine the \(A\) dependence.
