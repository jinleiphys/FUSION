# Constraining the trend of the $N = 50$ shell gap towards $^{100}$Sn with the masses of $^{96-98}$Cd

> We present the first determination of the $N = 50$ empirical shell gap at $Z = 48$ by precise mass measurements of the neutron-deficient cadmium isotopes $^{96-98}$Cd with the ISOLTRAP mass spectrometer at ISOLDE-CERN, including the first precise determination of the excitation energy of the $25/2^+$ isomer in $^{97}$Cd. Through the systematics of Coulomb Displacement Energies, we further deduce the empirical shell gap in the higher-$Z$ isotopic chains, tightly constraining the $^{100}$Sn mass-surface region. The new experimental data suggest an enhancement of the gap towards $^{100}$Sn, which is discussed in comparison to state-of-the-art calculations using energy-density functional and new ab initio approaches.

## Key claim

The first determination of the N=50 empirical shell gap at Z=48, based on precise mass measurements of 96-98Cd, suggests an enhancement of the gap towards 100Sn, though the authors note this is discussed in comparison to calculations rather than claimed as a definitive measurement at 100Sn itself.

## Method

The masses of 96-98Cd were measured using the ISOLTRAP multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer (MR-ToF MS) at ISOLDE-CERN, with cadmium isotopes selectively ionized by the resonance ionization laser ion source (RILIS). The excitation energy of the 25/2+ isomer in 97Cd was determined from its time-of-flight difference relative to the ground state in the same ToF spectrum. The empirical shell gap was then derived from one- and two-neutron separation energies, and extended to higher Z using the linear systematics of Coulomb Displacement Energies (CDE) for T=1/2 isobaric analogues.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 96Cd: -55682(30) keV, compared to AME2020 extrapolation of -55570(410) keV
- Mass excess of 97Cd: -60520(24) keV, compared to AME2020 value of -60730(420) keV
- Mass excess of 98Cd: -67662(11) keV, compared to AME2020 value of -67640(50) keV
- Excitation energy of the 25/2+ isomer in 97Cd: 2246(23) keV, compared to literature value of 2620(580) keV from Park et al.
- Extrapolated mass excess of 95Cd via CDE systematics: -46765(140) keV
- Extrapolated mass excess of 97In via CDE systematics: -47336(142) keV
- Extrapolated mass excess of 99Sn via CDE systematics: -48044(141) keV
- Mass resolving power of MR-ToF MS: R = t/(2*delta_t_FWHM) approximately 2*10^5
- Total of 40 counts of 96Cd detected over 3.5 days of measurement time

## Context

This work extends recent mass measurements in the silver isotopic chain that reached N=48 (Ge and Ag, 2023) and indium measurements at N=50 (Nies et al., 2023), enabling the first probe of the N=50 shell gap evolution into the N approximately Z region. The new data are compared to self-consistent calculations using the Generator Coordinate Method with SLy4 interaction (Bender et al.), Density Functional Theory with BSkG4 and BSkG5 Skyrme interactions (Grams et al., 2025, 2026), and new ab initio calculations using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) with EM(1.8/2.0), DeltaNNLO_GO, and N3LO_LNL chiral interactions. The results enable benchmarking of ab initio calculations for systematic trends of empirical shell gaps and constrain the mass-surface region around 100Sn, which is relevant for the rp-process of nucleosynthesis.
