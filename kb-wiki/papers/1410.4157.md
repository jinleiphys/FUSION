# Jet quenching beyond the energy loss approach

> We study the jet quenching effect in heavy ion collisions, based on medium-induced splitting functions calculated from Soft Collinear Effective Theory with Glauber Gluons. Our method is formulated in the language of DGLAP evolution equations with medium-induced splitting functions. In the small-$x$ soft gluon approximation we analytically solve the evolution equations and find an intuitive connection to the energy loss approach. For central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC we quantify the effect of finite-$x$ corrections for the nuclear modification factor and compare to data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that jet quenching can be formulated using DGLAP evolution equations with medium-induced splitting functions from Soft Collinear Effective Theory with Glauber Gluons (SCETG), and that the finite-x corrections beyond the small-x (soft gluon) approximation are not large, changing the extracted in-medium coupling by less than 10% compared to the energy loss approach.

## Method

The author uses medium-induced splitting functions for all four parton splitting channels (q→qg, g→gg, g→qq̅, q→gq) calculated to first order in opacity expansion using SCETG, and incorporates them into DGLAP evolution equations for fragmentation functions. The evolution equations are solved numerically for the full-x case and analytically in the small-x soft gluon approximation, and the resulting medium-modified fragmentation functions are used to compute the nuclear modification factor R_AA for central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV, the nuclear modification factor R_AA is computed for a quark and a gluon jet with p_Tc = Q = 40 GeV.
- The extracted in-medium coupling from comparing the full-x evolution and energy loss approaches to data differs by less than 10%.
- The full-x DGLAP evolution method is closer in shape to the data at low p_T < 20 GeV where the difference between approaches is visible.

## Context

This work extends the energy loss approach of Gyulassy (2003) and others by formulating jet quenching through DGLAP evolution equations with medium-induced splitting functions from SCETG, as developed by Ovanesyan (2011) and Kang (2014). The method enables consistent incorporation of finite-x corrections beyond the soft gluon approximation, and the analytical solution in the small-x limit reveals an explicit connection to the energy loss formalism through the factorization of medium and vacuum radiative effects.
