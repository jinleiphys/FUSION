# Rearrangements of interacting Fermi liquids

> The stability condition of Landau Fermi liquid theory may be broken when the interaction between particles is strong enough. In this case, the ground state is reconstructed to have a particle distribution different from the Fermi-step function. For specific instances, one case with the vector boson exchange and another with the relativistic heavy-ion collision are taken into consideration. With the vector boson exchange, we find that the relative weak interaction strength can lead to the ground-state rearrangement as long as the fermion mass is large enough. It is found that the relativistic heavy-ion collision may also cause the ground-state rearrangement, affecting the statistics of the collision system.

## Key claim

The necessary stability condition of Landau Fermi liquid theory (that the derivative of single-particle energy with respect to momentum must be positive near the Fermi surface) can be broken by relatively weak repulsive interactions if the fermion mass is sufficiently large, or by the enormous deceleration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, leading to a ground-state rearrangement with a multi-connected momentum distribution.

## Method

The authors examine the stability condition by computing the derivative dε/dk of the single-particle energy in the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation, searching for zero points in the interval 0.95 k_F to 1.05 k_F. For the vector boson exchange case, they add an additional repulsive vector boson to the SLC nuclear model and compute the critical coupling constant g_vc as a function of density and fermion mass. For the heavy-ion collision case, they replace the non-inertial effect (deceleration) with an equivalent Yukawa potential from a postulated vector meson, then compute the critical coupling g_c and corresponding critical deceleration A_c.

## Key numbers

- For nucleon systems at saturation density (ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³) with vector boson mass m_v = 0.010 GeV, the ratio g_vc/m_v ranges from 352.44 GeV⁻¹ for fermion mass M = 0.10 GeV down to 10.76 GeV⁻¹ for M = 300.00 GeV.
- For the U-boson scenario, the ratio g_vc/m_v for nucleons is always larger than 40 GeV⁻¹, while the regular U-boson ratio is around 10 GeV⁻¹, making ground-state rearrangement impossible for ordinary nuclear matter.
- For the collision case at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ with postulated meson mass m_v = 0.5478 GeV, the critical coupling constant is g_c = 31.91 and the critical deceleration is A_c = 0.0946 fm⁻¹ (approximately 8.50 × 10³⁰ m/s²).
- For the collision case, critical decelerations range from 0.0441 fm⁻¹ (m_v = 0.1350 GeV, g_c = 7.19) to 0.200 fm⁻¹ (m_v = 0.9578 GeV, g_c = 78.71).

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Khodel et al. (1990) that fermion condensation or multi-connected momentum distributions arise when the Landau stability condition is broken. The authors apply this concept to two new scenarios: weakly interacting massive particles (dark matter candidates) and relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The vector boson case connects to studies of the U-boson by Kr09, WDH09, and Zh11, which examined its effects on neutron star properties. The collision case uses the equivalence principle to relate deceleration to a local gravitational field, following Eda11, and suggests that ground-state rearrangement could affect fermion systems during supernova explosions and near black hole horizons.
