# Azimuthal asymmetry in unpolarized $πN$ Drell-Yan process

> Taking into account the effect of final state interaction, we calculate the non-zero (naïve) $T$-odd transverse momentum dependent distribution $h_1^{\perp}(x,\kp^2)$ of the pion in a quark-spectator-antiquark model with effective pion-quark-antiquark coupling as a dipole form factor. Using the model result we estimate the $\cos 2ϕ$ asymmetries in the unpolarized $π^- N$ Drell-Yan process which can be expressed as $h_1^{\perp}\times\bar{h}_1^{\perp}$. We find that the resulting $h_{1π}^\p(x,\kp^2)$ has the advantage to reproduce the asymmetry that agrees with the experimental data measured by NA10 Collaboration. We estimate the $\cos2ϕ$ asymmetries averaged over the kinematics of NA10 experiments for 140, 194 and 286 GeV $π^-$ beam and compare them with relevant experimental data.

# Azimuthal asymmetry in unpolarized πN Drell-Yan process

## Key claim

The authors calculate the Boer-Mulders function $h_1^{\perp}$ for the pion using a quark-spectator-antiquark model with a dipole form factor for the pion-quark-antiquark coupling, and find that this treatment (unlike a constant coupling) yields a $\cos 2\phi$ asymmetry in unpolarized $\pi^- N$ Drell-Yan that agrees with NA10 experimental data, though the estimation is crude and several approximations are adopted.

## Method

The authors compute the $T$-odd transverse momentum dependent distribution $h_{1\pi}^{\perp}(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp^2)$ of the pion in a quark-spectator-antiquark model, including final state interaction via one-gluon exchange modeled by expanding the Wilson line to first order. They treat the effective pion-quark-antiquark coupling $g_\pi$ as a dipole form factor $g_\pi(k^2) = N_\pi (k^2 - m^2)/(\Lambda^2 - k^2)^2$, in contrast to a previous constant-coupling treatment. Using this result together with a similarly computed $h_1^{\perp}$ for the nucleon from a quark-scalar diquark model with dipole coupling, they evaluate the $\cos 2\phi$ asymmetry coefficient $\nu$ in the Collins-Soper frame for unpolarized $\pi^- N$ Drell-Yan, averaging over the kinematics of the NA10 experiments at beam energies of 140, 194, and 286 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The dipole form factor parameters are chosen as: $\Lambda = 0.6$ GeV, $M_\pi = 0.137$ GeV, $m = 0.1$ GeV (quark/antiquark mass inside the pion), $M_N = 0.94$ GeV, $\lambda_S = 0.8$ GeV (scalar diquark mass), and $m_N = 0.3$ GeV (quark mass inside the nucleon).
- The strong coupling is taken as $\alpha_s = 0.3$ with $C_F = 4/3$, extrapolating $|e_1 e_2|/4\pi \rightarrow C_F \alpha_s$.
- The center-of-mass energies for the pion-nucleon system are: $\sqrt{s} = 16.2$ GeV for 140 GeV beam, $\sqrt{s} = 19.1$ GeV for 194 GeV beam, and $\sqrt{s} = 23.2$ GeV for 286 GeV beam.
- The data-selecting condition for the NA10 experiments is: $\bar{x} < 0.7$, $4.0 \leq Q \leq 8.5$ GeV ($4.05 \leq Q \leq 8.5$ GeV for the 194 GeV data) and $Q \geq 11$ GeV.
- The experimental data show a large $\nu$ value near 30% in the Collins-Soper frame.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Boer (1999) that the product of two $T$-odd chiral-odd $h_1^{\perp}$ functions can produce the $\cos 2\phi$ asymmetry in unpolarized Drell-Yan without suppression by the lepton pair momentum. It builds on calculations of $h_1^{\perp}$ for the proton in a quark-scalar diquark model by Gamberg et al. (2003) and Bacchetta et al. (2003), and on the authors' previous constant-coupling calculation of $h_{1\pi}^{\perp}$ (Lu and Ma, 2004) which failed to reproduce the $Q_T$ dependence of the asymmetry. The dipole form factor treatment follows the approach of Jakob et al. (1997) for T-even nucleon distributions and of Brodsky et al. (2004) for the Sivers function. The results enable further tests of the Boer-Mulders function mechanism and can be discriminated by future experiments comparing the $Q_T$ behavior of the asymmetry.
