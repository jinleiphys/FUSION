# Investigation of the ground-state spin inversion in the neutron-rich 47,49Cl isotopes

> A first gamma-ray study of 47,49Cl spectroscopy was performed at the Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory with 50Ar projectiles at 217 MeV/nucleon, impinging on the liquid hydrogen target of the MINOS device. Prompt de-excitation gamma-rays were measured with the NaI(Tl) array DALI2+. Through the one-proton knockout reaction 50Ar(p,2p), a spin assignment could be determined for the low-lying states of 49Cl from the momentum distribution obtained with the SAMURAI spectrometer. A spin-parity J = 3/2+ is deduced for the ground state of 49Cl, similar to the recently studied N = 32 isotope 51K.

## Key claim

The ground state of the neutron-rich isotope ⁴⁹Cl is assigned spin-parity \(J^{\pi}=3/2^{+}\), similar to the recently studied \(N=32\) isotope ⁵¹K, but this assignment is based on the momentum distribution from the one-proton knockout reaction ⁵⁰Ar(p,2p) and is compared to theoretical predictions that show a very small energy difference between the \(3/2^{+}\) and \(1/2^{+}\) states, leaving open the possibility of spin inversion in neighboring isotopes.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory (RIBF) using a ⁵⁰Ar beam at 217 MeV/nucleon impinging on a liquid hydrogen target (151 mm thick) of the MINOS device. Prompt de-excitation gamma rays were measured with the NaI(Tl) array DALI2+, and the momentum distributions of reaction residues were obtained with the SAMURAI spectrometer to determine the angular momentum of the knocked-out proton via comparison with distorted-wave impulse-approximation (DWIA) and transfer to continuum (TC) reaction models.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of ⁴⁹Cl is assigned \(J^{\pi}=3/2^{+}\); the first excited state at 350(6) keV is assigned \(1/2^{+}\).
- Transitions observed in ⁴⁹Cl from the ⁵⁰Ar(p,2p) reaction: 350(6) keV, 630(15) keV, 970(27) keV, and 1515(32) keV.
- The inclusive cross section for the ⁵⁰Ar(p,2p)⁴⁹Cl reaction is \(\sigma_{incl} = 4.55(15)\) mb.
- The exclusive cross section for the \(1/2^{+}_{1}\) state at 350 keV is 1.25(9) mb.
- The exclusive cross section for the \(5/2^{+}_{2}\) state at 1515(32) keV is 0.55(4) mb.
- The one-neutron separation energy for ⁴⁹Cl is \(S_{1n} = 3050(640)\) keV.
- The ⁵⁰Ar beam intensity was 2.9 particles/s, with total beam intensity on target about 212 particles/s.

## Context

This work extends the study of ground-state spin inversion in neutron-rich odd-Z nuclei beyond the potassium isotopic series, where spin inversion (\(1/2^{+}\) ground state) was observed for ⁴⁷K and ⁴⁹K but a \(3/2^{+}\) ground state was restored for ⁵¹K (Papuga et al., 2013, 2014). The results for ⁴⁹Cl are compared to shell-model calculations with the SDPF-MU interaction and ab initio calculations using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) and Gorkov self-consistent Green's function (GGF) approaches, enabling tests of nuclear Hamiltonians derived from chiral effective field theory in the neutron-rich region near \(N=32\).
