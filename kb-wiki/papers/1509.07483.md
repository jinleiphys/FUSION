# Radiative energy loss and radiative p_T-broadening of high-energy partons in QCD matter

> I give a self-contained review on radiative p_T-broadening and radiative energy loss of high-energy partons in QCD matter. The typical p_T^2 of high-energy partons receives a double logarithmic correction due to the recoiling effect of medium-induced gluon radiation. Such a double logarithmic term, averaged over the path length of the partons, can be taken as the radiative correction to the jet quenching parameter qhat and hence contributes to radiative energy loss. This has also been confirmed by detailed calculations of energy loss by radiating two gluons.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections to the typical transverse momentum broadening and energy loss of high-energy partons in QCD matter contain a universal double logarithmic term proportional to \(\alpha_s \ln^2(L/l_0)\), where \(L\) is the medium length and \(l_0\) the size of its constituents; this term can be resummed and interpreted as a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\).

## Method

The author extends the BDMPS-Z formalism to include medium-induced gluon radiation beyond leading order, analytically evaluating the double logarithmic contributions from one-gluon and multi-gluon emission in the regime where the gluon formation time is bounded by the mean free path of the coherent parton-gluon pair. The calculation uses a Schrödinger-type evolution equation for the dipole and integrates the differential cross section for single-scattering gluon emission over the phase space region where the formation time lies between the constituent size \(l_0\) and the medium length \(L\). Resummation of leading double logarithms from arbitrary numbers of gluons is performed by induction, and the results are applied to both radiative \(p_\perp\)-broadening and radiative energy loss.

## Key numbers

* The double logarithmic correction to the typical transverse momentum squared is \(\Delta p_\perp^2(L) = \hat{q}_R L \Delta(L)\) with \(\Delta(L) \equiv \frac{\alpha_s N_c}{2\pi} \ln^2(L/l_0)\) (Equation 4.12).
* The leading double logarithmic contribution from two-gluon emission to \(\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle\) is \(\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle_2 = \frac{(\hat{q}_R L)^2}{3!2!} [2\Delta(L)]^2\) (Equation 4.15).
* The resummed typical transverse momentum squared is \(\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle = \hat{q}_R L [2\Delta(L)]^{-1/2} I_1([8\Delta(L)]^{1/2})\) (Equation 4.16).
* The renormalized jet quenching parameter is \(\hat{q}_R^{ren}(L) \approx \hat{q}_R [1 + \Delta(L)]\) (Equation 4.17).
* The double logarithmic correction to the average energy loss from two-gluon emission is \(\Delta E_2 \approx \frac{\alpha_s N_c}{12} \hat{q} L^2 \Delta(L)\) (Equation 5.5).
* The total resummed energy loss is \(\Delta E = \frac{\alpha_s N_c}{12} L \langle p_\perp^2 \rangle\) (Equation 5.7).

## Context

This work provides a self-contained review of radiative corrections to \(p_\perp\)-broadening and energy loss, extending the leading-order BDMPS-Z formalism of Baier (1998) and Zakharov (1996, 1997). The double logarithmic correction to \(\hat{q}\) was previously identified by Blaizot (2014) and Iancu (2014), and the energy loss results confirm the calculations of Wu (2014) and Arnold (2015). The approach differs from the NLO (\(O(g)\)) correction to transverse momentum broadening obtained by Caron-Huot (2008) using a perturbative-kinetic method, which arises from soft collisions rather than radiation; it also contrasts with the formalism of Casalderrey-Solana and Wang (2007), which does not include multiple soft scattering. The results enable a resummed treatment of radiative corrections to jet quenching observables in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
