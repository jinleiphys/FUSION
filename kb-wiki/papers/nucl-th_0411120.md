# Tensor coupling and pseudospin symmetry in nuclei

> In this work we study the contribution of the isoscalar tensor coupling to the realization of pseudospin symmetry in nuclei. Using realistic values for the tensor coupling strength, we show that this coupling reduces noticeably the pseudospin splittings, especially for single-particle levels near the Fermi surface. By using an energy decomposition of the pseudospin energy splittings, we show that the changes in these splittings come by mainly through the changes induced in the lower radial wave function for the low-lying pseudospin partners, and by changes in the expectation value of the pseudospin-orbit coupling term for surface partners. This allows us to confirm the conclusion already reached in previous studies, namely that the pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is of a dynamical nature.

# Tensor coupling and pseudospin symmetry in nuclei

## Key claim

The isoscalar tensor coupling reduces pseudospin splittings noticeably, especially for single-particle levels near the Fermi surface, but the effect is not primarily due to the tensor potential terms themselves; instead, it arises through changes induced in the lower radial wave function for low-lying pseudospin partners and through changes in the expectation value of the pseudospin-orbit coupling term for surface partners, confirming that pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is of a dynamical nature.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation numerically with Woods-Saxon mean-field scalar and vector potentials fitted to reproduce the neutron single-particle spectrum of 208Pb, including an isoscalar tensor coupling term derived from the derivative of the vector potential. They performed an energy decomposition based on the second-order differential equation for the lower component of the Dirac spinor, computing expectation values of kinetic, potential, Darwin, and pseudospin-orbit terms for pseudospin partner pairs at varying tensor coupling strengths.

## Key numbers

- For the surface pseudospin doublet [2g9/2 - 1i11/2] at f_v = 0.0, the pseudospin-orbit expectation values are -6.4555 MeV and +3.3530 MeV respectively; at f_v = 1.3 they become -7.2110 MeV and +4.1391 MeV.
- For the low-lying doublet [2s1/2 - 1d3/2] at f_v = 0.0, the pseudospin-orbit expectation values are -0.5852 MeV and +0.0966 MeV; at f_v = 1.3 they become -0.5632 MeV and +0.1559 MeV.
- The tensor coupling strength f_v was varied from 0.0 to 1.3, consistent with values found in RMF and point coupling model fittings by Furnstahl et al.
- For the [2f5/2 - 3p3/2] pair at f_v = 1.3, the levels become almost degenerate; for [1i11/2 - 2g9/2] the order reverses.
- Woods-Saxon parameters used: R = 7 fm, Delta_0 = 650 MeV, Sigma_0 = -66 MeV, a = 0.6 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Alberto, Lisboa, Malheiro, de Castro, 2001-2003) that established the dynamical nature of pseudospin symmetry in nuclei using Woods-Saxon potentials without tensor coupling. It also builds on the work of Furnstahl et al. (1996) and Mao (2003), who showed that tensor coupling increases spin-orbit splitting in nuclei. The results enable improved understanding of pseudospin symmetry breaking mechanisms and suggest that tensor coupling may help reproduce experimental energy ordering, such as the order reversal seen in the [1i11/2 - 2g9/2] doublet in 208Pb, which calculations without tensor coupling fail to reproduce.
