# Closing the equations of motion of anisotropic fluid dynamics by a judicious choice of moment of the Boltzmann equation

> In Molnár et al. [Phys. Rev. D 93, 114025 (2016)] the equations of anisotropic dissipative fluid dynamics were obtained from the moments of the Boltzmann equation based on an expansion around an arbitrary anisotropic single-particle distribution function. In this paper we make a particular choice for this distribution function and consider the boost-invariant expansion of a fluid in one dimension. In order to close the conservation equations, we need to choose an additional moment of the Boltzmann equation. We discuss the influence of the choice of this moment on the time evolution of fluid-dynamical variables and identify the moment that provides the best match of anisotropic fluid dynamics to the solution of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the choice of which moment of the Boltzmann equation is used to close the equations of anisotropic fluid dynamics significantly affects the time evolution of fluid-dynamical variables, and identifies a specific moment that provides the best match to the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation for a boost-invariant expanding system.

## Method

The authors consider 0+1 dimensional Bjorken flow (boost-invariant expansion) of a massless Boltzmann gas using the Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution function. They derive the conservation equations and then close them by selecting different scalar moments from the infinite hierarchy of moment equations of the Boltzmann equation, using the relaxation-time approximation for the collision term. The time evolution of the anisotropy parameter and temperature is computed for each closure choice and compared to the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers

- For the case without particle-number conservation, the moment equation corresponding to the projection of the second moment of the Boltzmann equation (the equation for the longitudinal pressure \(\hat{P}_l\)) provides the best match to the Boltzmann solution
- For the case with particle-number conservation, the moment equation for the particle diffusion current \(\hat{n}_l\) gives the best agreement with the Boltzmann solution
- The relaxation time is parametrized as \(\tau_{eq}(\tau) = 5\beta_0(\tau)\frac{\eta}{s}\), where \(\eta/s\) is the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (assumed constant)
- The Landau matching conditions relate the anisotropic distribution parameters to equilibrium temperature via \(\beta_0 = \beta_{RS} / [R_{200}(\xi)]^{1/4}\) when particle number is not conserved

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic fluid dynamics framework developed by Molnár, Niemi, and Rischke (2016) by addressing the closure problem left open in that earlier paper. It builds upon the Romatschke-Strickland distribution function (Romatschke and Strickland, 2003) and the anisotropic hydrodynamics approach pioneered by Florkowski and Ryblewski (2008-2012) and Martinez and Strickland (2009-2010). The results enable systematic improvement of anisotropic fluid dynamics by identifying which moment equation should be used for closure, allowing more accurate modeling of systems with large momentum-space anisotropies such as those created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.
