# A fast and accurate method for perturbative resummation of transverse momentum-dependent observables

> We propose a novel strategy for the perturbative resummation of transverse momentum-dependent (TMD) observables, using the $q_T$ spectra of gauge bosons ($γ^*$, Higgs) in $pp$ collisions in the regime of low (but perturbative) transverse momentum $q_T$ as a specific example. First we introduce a scheme to choose the factorization scale for virtuality in momentum space instead of in impact parameter space, allowing us to avoid integrating over (or cutting off) a Landau pole in the inverse Fourier transform of the latter to the former. The factorization scale for rapidity is still chosen as a function of impact parameter $b$, but in such a way designed to obtain a Gaussian form (in $\ln b$) for the exponentiated rapidity evolution kernel, guaranteeing convergence of the $b$ integral. We then apply this scheme to obtain the $q_T$ spectra for Drell-Yan and Higgs production at NNLL accuracy. In addition, using this scheme we are able to obtain a fast semi-analytic formula for the perturbative resummed cross sections in momentum space: analytic in its dependence on all physical variables at each order of logarithmic accuracy, up to a numerical expansion for the pure mathematical Bessel function in the inverse Fourier transform that needs to be performed just once for all observables and kinematics, to any desired accuracy.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a perturbative resummation scheme for transverse momentum-dependent (TMD) observables that avoids integrating over the Landau pole in impact parameter space by choosing the virtuality factorization scale in momentum space, while still using a rapidity scale that depends on impact parameter in a way designed to yield a Gaussian form in ln(b) for the exponentiated rapidity evolution kernel, guaranteeing convergence of the b integral.

## Method
The authors introduce a hybrid scale-setting scheme where the virtuality scale μ is chosen in momentum space (after the b integration) rather than as a function of impact parameter b, while the rapidity scale ν is chosen as a function of b to produce a Gaussian exponent in ln(b) for the rapidity evolution kernel. They apply this scheme to Drell-Yan and Higgs production at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy. The b integral is then evaluated semi-analytically by expanding the Bessel function J0 in a Mellin-Barnes representation and expanding the resulting gamma function products in a Hermite polynomial series, yielding analytic dependence on all physical variables up to a numerical expansion performed once for the pure mathematical Bessel function.

## Key numbers
- The cross section is computed for Drell-Yan (γ* channel) and Higgs production in pp collisions at NNLL accuracy, with the gauge boson invariant mass Q and transverse momentum qT in the perturbative regime qT ≪ Q.
- The rapidity evolution kernel VΓ is expressed as a Gaussian in ln(b): VΓ = C exp[-A ln²(μL b0 χ)], where A ∼ Γ[αs(μL)] and the coefficients C, A, χ are functions of the scales μL, νL, νH and the rapidity anomalous dimension (Eq. 4.14 in the paper).
- The basic b integral I_b⁰ = ∫₀^∞ db b J0(b qT) exp[-A ln²(Ω b)] is evaluated analytically in terms of Hermite polynomial integrals, with the result given by Eq. (4.24): I_b⁰ = (2/(π qT²)) Σₙ Im{ c₂ₙ H₂ₙ(α,a₀) + (iγ_E/β) c₂ₙ₊₁ H₂ₙ₊₁(β,b₀) }, where the Hermite integrals Hₙ have closed-form analytic expressions (Eq. 4.27).
- The series expansion for Γ(1-ix)² requires only a few (3 or 4) terms in each series (real and imaginary parts) to achieve numerical accuracy acceptable for NNLL precision.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative resummation of TMD observables in the soft collinear effective theory (SCET) framework, building on the rapidity renormalization group formalism of Chiu et al. (2011, 2012). It provides an alternative to the standard Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) approach (Collins, 1984) and recent SCET treatments (e.g., Neill, 2015) that require a cutoff or nonperturbative model to handle the Landau pole in the b integral. The semi-analytic result enables fast computation of resummed cross sections and their scale uncertainties, and the method can be extended to other TMD observables and higher logarithmic orders.
