# Effect of a Zeeman field on the superconductor-ferromagnet transition in metallic grains

> We investigate the competition between pairing correlations and ferromagnetism in small metallic grains in the presence of a Zeeman field. Our analysis is based on the universal Hamiltonian, valid in the limit of large Thouless conductance. We show that the coexistence regime of superconducting and ferromagnetic correlations can be made experimentally accessible by tuning an external Zeeman field. We compare the exact solution of the model with a mean-field theory and find that the latter cannot describe pairing correlations in the intermediate regime. We also study the occurrence of spin jumps across the phase boundary separating the superconducting and coexistence regimes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the coexistence regime of superconducting and ferromagnetic correlations in small metallic grains can be made experimentally accessible by tuning an external Zeeman field, which shifts the critical exchange coupling values to lower, more realistic values than those required in the absence of a field.

## Method

The authors use the universal Hamiltonian, valid for grains with large Thouless conductance, and solve it exactly using Richardson's solution for the pairing interaction combined with the known solution of the exchange model. They construct the ground-state phase diagram by finding the lowest energy for each total spin S and minimizing with respect to S, using an equidistant single-particle spectrum at half filling. They compare these exact results with a mean-field approximation based on a trial BCS-like wave function with spin-dependent pairing gap.

## Key numbers

- For an even grain with no Zeeman field, the intermediate regime disappears above \(\Delta/\delta \sim 3\).
- For \(\Delta/\delta < 0.6\), the ground-state spin increases in steps of \(\Delta S=1\) versus exchange coupling; for \(0.6 < \Delta/\delta < 0.8\), a spin jump from \(S=0\) to \(S=2\) occurs within \(0.87 < J_s/\delta < 0.9\).
- At \(\Delta/\delta=0.7\) and zero Zeeman field, a spin jump of \(\Delta S=2\) occurs at \(J_s \approx 0.89\delta\); at \(\Delta/\delta=0.9\), a spin jump of \(\Delta S=3\) occurs at \(J_s \approx 0.92\delta\).
- With a Zeeman field \(g\mu_B H/\delta = 2\), a spin jump of \(\Delta S=2\) occurs at \(J_s/\delta=0.55\) and \(\Delta/\delta=2\); a spin jump of \(\Delta S=3\) occurs at \(J_s/\delta=0.64\) and \(\Delta/\delta=2.3\).
- For niobium (\(J_s/\delta \approx 0.4\)), at Zeeman field \(g\mu_B H/\delta=2.6\), a spin jump of \(\Delta S=2\) occurs at \(\Delta/\delta=2.15\), corresponding to a grain radius of approximately \(3.48\) nm and Zeeman field \(g\mu_B H=3.69\) meV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pairing and exchange competition in metallic grains by Ying (2000) and Falci (2004), who found a narrow coexistence regime and suggested enhancement by mesoscopic fluctuations or asymmetric bandwidth. The authors show that a Zeeman field can enlarge this regime and make spin jumps observable at exchange coupling values typical of real materials like niobium, in contrast to the mean-field approach of Delft (2001) which fails to capture pairing correlations in the partially polarized regime. The results enable experimental searches for spin jumps in metallic grains by tuning an external magnetic field rather than relying on intrinsic material properties.
