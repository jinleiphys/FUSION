# A modified proximity approach in the fusion of heavy-ions

## Key claim
The authors claim that by selecting a specific combination of the surface energy coefficient, nuclear radius, and universal function from the literature, their modified proximity potential (Prox 2010) reproduces experimental fusion barrier heights within 1.4% on average, reducing the overestimate of 6.7% reported for symmetric nuclei with the original proximity potential 1977. The improvement is conditional on using the universal function from Blocki et al. (1981) rather than the 1977 version, and the latest surface energy coefficient from Dutt and Bansal (2010).

## Method
The authors constructed a modified proximity potential (Prox 2010) by combining the universal function Φ-1981 from Blocki et al. (1981), the nuclear charge radius formula of Royer and Rousseau (2009), and the surface energy coefficient with γ₀ = 1.25284 MeV/fm² and k_s = 2.345 from Dutt and Bansal (2010). They calculated fusion barrier heights and positions for 390 reactions with combined mass between A = 19 and A = 294, using the conditions dV_T/dr = 0 and d²V_T/dr² ≤ 0 at the barrier radius R_B. Fusion cross sections were computed using the Wong model for six selected reactions.

## Key numbers
- Average deviation for fusion barrier heights over 390 reactions: 0.77% for Prox 2010, compared to 3.99% for Prox 1977 and 4.45% for Prox 2000.
- Prox 2010 reproduces experimental fusion barrier heights within 1.4% (stated as a general result, not per-reaction).
- Original proximity potential 1977 overestimates data by 6.7% for symmetric colliding nuclei.
- The universal function Φ-1981 reduces the average deviation over 390 reactions by 1% for fusion barriers compared to Φ-1977.
- Surface energy coefficient parameters: γ₀ = 1.25284 MeV/fm² and k_s = 2.345, obtained from least-squares adjustment to ground-state masses of 1654 nuclei from ¹⁶O to ²⁶³106 and fission-barrier heights.
- Nuclear charge radius formula: R₀₀ᵢ = 1.2332 Aᵢ^{1/3} {1 + 2.348443/Aᵢ - 0.151541[(Nᵢ - Zᵢ)/Aᵢ]} fm, derived from 2027 masses with N, Z ≥ 8 and mass uncertainty ≤ 150 keV.
- Fusion barrier heights reproduced within ±5% on average; barrier positions within ±10%.

## Context
This work extends the line of proximity potential modifications that began with Blocki et al. (1977) and continued with Blocki et al. (1981) and Myers and Swiatecki (2000). The authors address the known overestimate of fusion barrier heights by the original proximity potential 1977, which was reported as 4% by Myers and Swiatecki (2000) and as 6.7% for symmetric nuclei by Dutt and Bansal (2010). The study enables more reliable predictions of fusion barrier heights and cross sections for heavy-ion reactions, particularly for superheavy element formation, by providing a systematic selection of the three key parameters (surface energy coefficient, nuclear radius, universal function) that were previously chosen arbitrarily in the literature.
