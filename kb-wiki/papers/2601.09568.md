# Long Range Outlook for Short-Range Correlations

> Short range correlated (SRC) N N pairs are pairs of nucleons with high relative momentum (prel > kF where kF ~ 250 MeV/c is the Fermi momentum in medium to heavy nuclei) and lower center of mass momentum. The motivation for studying SRC pairs ranges from a desire to achieve a more comprehensive understanding of the many-body nuclear wave-function at high-resolution to searching for explicit QCD-dynamics effects within the nuclear medium, not to mention connections to many other open problems in nuclear physics. Exploring short-range correlations was one of the physics motivations for building CEBAF (now Jefferson Lab). Scientists used the high luminosity and high energy of this cutting-edge machine to find kinematics that cleanly showed the signals of short-range correlations. This paved the way in the last two decades for tremendous progress understanding these correlations. This paper reviews recent progress and highlights outstanding questions and areas that need further study.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs have been extensively studied and their dominance by proton-neutron pairs is well established, a complete understanding of the nuclear wave function at short distances remains elusive, and definitive measurements of three-nucleon SRCs (3N-SRCs) have not yet been achieved.

## Method

The paper reviews inclusive quasielastic electron scattering $(e,e')$ and semi-inclusive proton knockout $(e,e'p)$ and two-nucleon knockout $(e,e'pp)$ and $(e,e'pn)$ measurements at Jefferson Lab, using kinematics with $Q^2 \ge 1.5$ GeV$^2$ and $x_B > 1$ to isolate high-momentum nucleons ($p_{miss} > k_F \sim 250$ MeV/c). Cross-section ratios of nucleus $A$ to deuterium are analyzed within the factorized impulse approximation framework, where the cross-section factorizes into an electron-nucleon cross-section and a nuclear spectral function, with corrections for final-state interactions and meson-exchange currents applied at high $Q^2$.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum in medium to heavy nuclei: $k_F \sim 250$ MeV/c
- Minimum momentum for SRC dominance in inclusive scattering: $k_{min} \ge 300$ MeV/c, corresponding to $1.5 \le x_B \le 1.9$ at $Q^2 \ge 1.5$ GeV$^2$
- Per-nucleon inclusive cross-section ratio $a_2$ for $^{12}$C/deuterium is approximately 5, increasing from about 2 for $^3$He/deuterium
- Measured per-nucleus inclusive cross-section ratio of $^{48}$Ca to $^{40}$Ca is $1.165(14)$, showing increased SRC pairs with added neutrons
- Tritium to helium-3 inclusive cross-section ratio is $0.854 \pm 0.010$ for $1.4 < x_B < 1.7$ in the SRC-dominated region at $Q^2 \sim 1.4$ and $1.9$ GeV$^2$
- Transition from mean-field to SRC dominance in $(e,e'p)$ occurs in the missing momentum range from approximately 250 to 350 MeV/c
- $pp$ to $np$ SRC pair ratio is approximately 8-10% across nuclei from $^4$He to Pb
- Threshold for 3N-SRC contribution requires $\alpha_{3N} > 1.6$, with $\alpha_{3N} > 1.8$ as a more realistic threshold for a second scaling plateau

## Context

This review extends the experimental program initiated at SLAC (Frankfurt 1993) and continued at Jefferson Lab through experiments such as E02-019 (Fomin 2012), CLAS 2006 (Egiyan 2006), and CLAS 2020 (Schmookler 2019), which established the universality of 2N-SRC scaling ratios. The paper identifies open questions including the isospin dependence of SRCs, the role of nuclear density versus mass number in saturation effects, and the definitive observation of 3N-SRCs, which remains an experimental challenge despite predictions of $a_3 \approx a_2^2$. Future experiments at Jefferson Lab (E12-06-105 XEM2) are currently analyzing data at $Q^2$ of 2.3 and 3.2 GeV$^2$ to probe 3N-SRCs up to $\alpha_{3N} \approx 1.8$.
