# Entanglement and seniority

> We study mode-entanglement in the seniority model, derive analytic formulas for the one-body reduced density matrix of states with seniority $ν= 0,\;1,\;2$, and $ν=3$, and also determine the particle number dependence of the one-body reduced density matrix for arbitrary seniority. We carry out numerical calculations for the lightest calcium isotopes and for $^{94}{\rm Ru}$ nucleus, and investigate the structure of their ground and low energy yrast states. We investigate the fulfillment of several predictions of the seniority model regarding the behavior of one-mode entropies, which we compare with the results of configuration interaction (CI) and density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) computations. For $^{94}{\rm Ru}$, the seniority model accounts for the $0g_{9/2}$ mode entropies, but seniority mixing is important for certain yrast states. Interaction induced quantum fluctuations decrease the occupation of the $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$ and $1p_{1/2}$ shells, and amount in finite mode entropies on these shells, too, clearly outside the scope of the simple $(0g_{9/2})^4$ seniority model. The $0f_{7/2}$ shell based seniority model is more accurate for the light ${\rm Ca}$ isotopes, but seniority mixing is substantial for some $^{44}{\rm Ca}$ yrast states, too.

## Key claim

The seniority model accurately predicts one-mode entropies for the $0g_{9/2}$ shell in $^{94}$Ru and the $0f_{7/2}$ shell in light calcium isotopes, but seniority mixing becomes substantial for certain yrast states, particularly the $J=4$ state of $^{44}$Ca and some yrast states of $^{94}$Ru, where simple seniority-only descriptions fail.

## Method

The authors derived analytic formulas for the one-body reduced density matrix (1B-RDM) of seniority $\nu=0$, $1$, $2$, and $3$ states within the single-$j$ shell seniority model using quasi-spin formalism and the Wigner-Eckart theorem. They then performed configuration interaction (CI) calculations using the BIGSTICK code and density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) computations for the lightest calcium isotopes ($^{42}$Ca, $^{43}$Ca, $^{44}$Ca, $^{46}$Ca) and $^{94}$Ru, using the GXPF1 interaction for calcium and realistic effective interactions for $^{94}$Ru, and compared the resulting one-mode entropies and one-body entanglement entropies with seniority model predictions.

## Key numbers

- For $^{44}$Ca ground state, the four valence neutrons occupy the $0f_{7/2}$ shell with occupation number 3.89, while $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, and $1p_{1/2}$ shells have occupations 0.06, 0.05, and 0.01 respectively
- The $J=4$ state of $^{44}$Ca achieves overlap 0.953 with a mixed seniority $\nu=2$ and $\nu=4$ state
- In the seniority-zero $J=0$ ground state of $^{44}$Ca, mode entropies reach the maximal value $\ln 2 \approx 0.6931$, as predicted for a half-filled shell
- For $^{43}$Ca ground state ($J=7/2$, $\nu=1$), only modes $m=-7/2$ and $m=7/2$ have ${\cal S}_m \approx 0$
- For seniority $\nu=0$ states, the one-body entropy is ${\cal S}_{\rm 1B}(00,j^n0)=(2j+1)\, h(n/(2j+1))$, maximized at half-filling $n=j+1/2$
- For seniority $\nu=1$ states, ${\cal S}_{1B}(jM,j^n1)=(2j-1)\,h((n-1)/(2j-1))$
- For half-filled shell with $j+1/2$ even, $M=0$, and $n=j+1/2$, seniority $\nu=2$ states give ${\cal S}_{\rm 1B}=(2j+1)\,\ln 2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on entanglement of angular-momentum coupled two-nucleon states (Kruppa, 2021) to many-nucleon states within the seniority model, and complements studies of seniority-zero electron pair states in quantum chemistry (Boguslawski, 2016). The seniority model, originally developed by Talmi and others for describing pairing phenomena in nuclei, is here used as a benchmark for entanglement measures that can detect delicate structures in correlated quantum states. The results enable future investigations of seniority mixing in more complex nuclei and provide a foundation for using one-mode entropies as a diagnostic tool for characterizing the seniority structure of nuclear states, complementing traditional shell model studies (Kvaal, 2014; Legeza, 2015; Kruppa, 2021) and ab initio no-core shell model investigations (Sawyer, 2019).
