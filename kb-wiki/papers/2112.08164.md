# Aspects of finite temperature QCD towards the chiral limit

> QCD under extreme conditions has been studied for a long time, and the chiral limit has been a grey area mostly. In this write-up of my talk, I review some of the recent developments made by the community to unveil various features of QCD towards the chiral limit, which includes calculation of the chiral critical temperature and determination of the order of chiral phase transition for various numbers of flavors. Acknowledging the importance of the studies regarding the effective restoration of $U_A(1)$, I try to give a comprehensive overview about the various studies done in the last few years in a comparative manner to realize the current status of the community in this regard. I also discuss very recent efforts about the relevance of various energy-like observables w.r.t. the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

This review argues that the chiral critical temperature for (2+1)-flavor QCD is now consistently determined around 132-134 MeV from lattice calculations, but the effective restoration of $U_A(1)$ near the chiral limit remains unresolved, with lattice evidence divided between restoration and persistent breaking, especially for temperatures close to the chiral critical temperature.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of the chiral phase transition, including scaling analyses of pseudo-critical temperatures using the chiral order parameter and its susceptibility, finite-size scaling of kurtosis to determine the order of the transition, and eigenvalue spectrum analyses of the Dirac operator to probe effective $U_A(1)$ restoration. Results from multiple fermion discretizations (HISQ, domain-wall, overlap, twisted mass Wilson) are compared across different lattice spacings and volumes.

## Key numbers

- Chiral critical temperature for (2+1)-flavor QCD: $T_c = 132^{+3}_{-6}$ MeV (continuum extrapolated, HISQ action, HotQCD 2019)
- Chiral critical temperature for (2+1)-flavor QCD: $T_c = 134^{+6}_{-4}$ MeV (twisted mass Wilson fermions, Kotov 2021)
- Chiral critical temperature for three-flavor QCD: $T_c = 98^{+3}_{-6}$ MeV (HISQ action, Dini 2021)
- For three-flavor QCD, no evidence of first order transition down to pion mass of 80 MeV, with scaling arguments pushing the bound on critical pion mass to 50 MeV (Bazavov 2017)
- At $T = 1.6 T_c$, the disconnected chiral susceptibility $\chi_{\text{disc}}$ extrapolates to a non-vanishing value with more than 95% confidence (HISQ action, Ding 2020)

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program to determine the QCD phase diagram in the chiral limit, building on the seminal work of Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) who argued for an $O(4)$ second-order transition for two massless flavors and a first-order transition for three massless flavors. The results for $T_c$ from HotQCD (2019) and Kotov (2021) agree within uncertainties and are consistent with $O(4)$ scaling. The unresolved status of $U_A(1)$ restoration near $T_c$ directly impacts whether the Columbia plot shows a second-order or first-order chiral transition for two flavors, and the review highlights the difficulty of chiral extrapolations for $U_A(1)$ breaking measures at temperatures just above $T_c$, where non-monotonic mass dependence appears.
