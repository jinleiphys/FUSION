# Unitarized pion-nucleon scattering within Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> By means of the Inverse Amplitude Method we unitarize the elastic pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes obtained from Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory to O(q^3). Within this approach we can enlarge their applicability range and generate the Delta(1232) resonance. We can find a reasonable description of the pion nucleon phase shifts with (q^2) parameters in agreement with the resonance saturation hypothesis. However, the uncertainties in the analysis of the low energy data as well as the large number of chiral parameters, which can have strong correlations, allow us to obtain very good fits with rather different sets of chiral constants.

## Key claim

By unitarizing the Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) amplitude to O(q^3) with the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM), the authors can describe pion-nucleon phase shifts and generate the Delta(1232) resonance, but the large number of chiral parameters and their strong correlations allow very good fits with rather different sets of chiral constants, making individual parameter determinations meaningless without additional assumptions like resonance saturation.

## Method

The authors applied the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) to the elastic pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes derived from SU(2) Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory to O(q^3). They fitted the resulting unitarized partial waves to extrapolated phase shifts from the VPI group, starting at sqrt(s)=1130 MeV and extending up to inelastic thresholds, using the MINUIT minimization routine with assigned errors (3% uncertainty plus 1 degree systematic error). The IAM generates the Delta(1232) resonance dynamically as a pole in the second Riemann sheet without introducing additional fields.

## Key numbers

- Delta(1232) pole position in the second Riemann sheet: sqrt(s) = (1212 - i 47) MeV, stable across all fits and in good agreement with Particle Data Group data.
- Fits started at sqrt(s) = 1130 MeV, following the approach of Fet98.
- S-wave scattering length predictions: a0^+ between -0.07 GeV^-1 and 0.04 GeV^-1; a0^- between 0.6 GeV^-1 and 0.67 GeV^-1, in rough agreement with Fet98.
- Unconstrained IAM fits achieved chi^2/d.o.f. less than or approximately equal to 1.
- HBChPT convergence limited to pion momentum q_pi less than or approximately 200 MeV (Fet98).
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi = 4 pi f_pi approximately 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Inverse Amplitude Method, previously successful in meson-meson scattering (IAM, 1990s; Oller, Oset, and Pelaez, 1998), to the baryon sector by unitarizing Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory amplitudes. It contrasts with the approach of Jido and Arima (1999), who unitarized lowest-order HBChPT with explicit Delta(1232) and N*(1440) resonances using the N/D method. The results enable further work on SU(3) extensions and coupled-channel formalisms, though the slow convergence of HBChPT and strong parameter correlations remain open challenges.
