# Virtual photons in the pion form factors and the energy-momentum tensor

> We evaluate the vector and scalar form factor of the pion in the presence of virtual photons at next-to-leading order in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory. We also consider the scalar and tensor pion form factors of the energy-momentum tensor. We find that the intrinsic electromagnetic corrections are of the expected size for the vector form factor and very small for the charged pion scalar form factor. Detector resolution independent photon corrections reduce the vector radius by about one percent. The scalar radius of the neutral pion is reduced by two percent by isospin-breaking contributions. We perform infrared regularization by considering electron-positron annihilation into pions and the decay of a light Higgs boson into a pion pair. We discuss the detector resolution dependent contributions to the various form factors and pion radii.

## Key claim
The intrinsic electromagnetic corrections to the vector form factor are of the expected size, and detector-resolution-independent photon corrections reduce the vector radius by about one percent; the scalar radius of the neutral pion is reduced by two percent by isospin-breaking contributions, but the charged pion scalar form factor receives very small intrinsic electromagnetic corrections.

## Method
The authors evaluate the vector and scalar form factors of the pion, as well as the scalar and tensor pion form factors of the energy-momentum tensor, at next-to-leading order in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory including virtual photons. They perform infrared regularization by introducing a finite photon mass and considering the processes of electron-positron annihilation into pions and the decay of a light Higgs boson into a pion pair. The calculation uses the effective chiral Lagrangian with strong and electromagnetic terms up to fourth order in the chiral expansion, counting the electric charge as a small parameter.

## Key numbers
- Detector-resolution-independent photon corrections reduce the vector radius by about one percent.
- The scalar radius of the neutral pion is reduced by two percent by isospin-breaking contributions.
- The low-energy constant Z = 0.89, obtained using the pion decay constant in the chiral limit F₀ = 88 MeV.
- The scale-independent low-energy constants for the energy-momentum tensor are: \bar{l}_{11} = 8.7, \bar{l}_{12} = -0.4, \bar{l}_{13} = 9.4.

## Context
This work extends the one-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) for pion form factors by including virtual photon effects and isospin breaking. It builds on previous studies of electromagnetic corrections to pion-pion scattering by Müller, Meißner, and Steininger (1997) and by Kubis and Meißner (1999). The results enable more precise extraction of the pion charge radius from experimental data and improve the theoretical treatment of the scalar form factor for dispersive analyses of the pion-nucleon sigma-term and Higgs decay into pion pairs.
