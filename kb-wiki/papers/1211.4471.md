# The phase diagram of N_f=2 and N_f=2+1 QCD from quark and gluon propagators

> In this talk we present results for the propagators and the phase diagram of QCD, obtained from a truncated set of Dyson-Schwinger equations at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential mu. We include back-coupling effects of the dressed quark and gluon propagators, which also allows us to study the influence of strange quarks. For the phase diagram we find a critical end-point at mu/T = 1.9 and coinciding chiral and deconfinement phase transitions.

# The phase diagram of N_f=2 and N_f=2+1 QCD from quark and gluon propagators

## Key claim
The authors find a critical end-point (CEP) at μ/T ≈ 1.9 for N_f=2+1 QCD using a truncated set of Dyson-Schwinger equations, but note that the vertex ansatz overestimates chiral symmetry breaking above T_c, meaning the quantitative position of the CEP is subject to this systematic uncertainty.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled set of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the quark and gluon propagators at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ, using a quenched gluon propagator from lattice QCD as input and adding the quark-loop contribution to the gluon DSE to include back-coupling effects. The quark-gluon vertex is modeled with a Ball-Chiu construction plus a dressing function that is constant in T and μ, with parameters fixed to reproduce the pion decay constant in vacuum. The chiral condensate and the dressed Polyakov loop (dual condensate) are used as order parameters for chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement, respectively.

## Key numbers
- Critical end-point for N_f=2+1 QCD: (μ, T) ≈ (190 MeV, 100 MeV), corresponding to μ/T ≈ 1.9.
- Critical temperature for N_f=2+1 QCD at zero chemical potential: T_c ≈ 156 MeV, in good agreement with lattice results from Borsanyi (2010).
- Critical temperature for N_f=2 QCD at zero chemical potential: T_c ≈ 200 MeV, about 50 MeV higher than the N_f=2+1 case.
- Vertex parameters: d_1 = 7.5 GeV², d_2 = 0.5 GeV², Λ = 1.4 GeV.

## Context
This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of QCD at finite temperature and density (Fischer 2009, Fischer 2011) by including explicit back-coupling of quarks onto the gluon propagator, enabling the study of strange quark effects. The results are compared to lattice QCD simulations by Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2011), which are limited to zero chemical potential due to the fermion sign problem. The approach enables exploration of the phase diagram at finite μ, finding a CEP outside the range accessible by lattice extrapolations, and provides a continuum functional method to complement effective model studies such as Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio models (Fukushima 2003, Ratti 2005) and quark-meson models (Schaefer 2007, Skokov 2010, Herbst 2010).
