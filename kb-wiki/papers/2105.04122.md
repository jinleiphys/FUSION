# Macroscopic transports in a rotational system with an electromagnetic field

> In this work, we explore macroscopic transport phenomena associated with a rotational system in the presence of an external orthogonal electromagnetic field. Simply based on the lowest Landau level approximation, we derive nontrivial expressions for chiral density and various currents consistently by adopting small angular velocity expansion or Kubo formula. While the generation of anomalous electric current is due to the pseudo gauge field effect of the spin-rotation coupling, the chiral density and current can be simply explained with the help of Lorentz boosts. Finally, Lorentz covariant forms can be obtained by unifying our results and the magnetovorticity effect.

# Macroscopic transports in a rotational system with an electromagnetic field

## Key claim
Under the condition of a weak rotation (angular velocity much smaller than the Lorentz invariant field strength) and an orthogonal electromagnetic field, novel macroscopic transport phenomena, termed the chiral electromagnetic vortical effect (CEMVE), emerge from the lowest Landau level approximation, including a vector current that does not require a magnetic field for its generation and arises solely from spin-rotation coupling acting as a pseudo gauge field.

## Method
The author considers a one-flavor massless fermion system under global rotation with constant orthogonal electric and magnetic fields, using the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation for the fermion propagator. The calculation proceeds by expanding the full propagator to first order in small angular velocity, then evaluating expectation values of chiral density and currents via trace operations in momentum space, with dimensional regularization and bosonization rules applied to handle two-dimensional current correlators. The results are cross-checked using a Kubo formula approach that relates the transport coefficients to retarded Green's functions of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers
- Chiral density: \( n_5 = \frac{|q| (\mathbf{E} \times \boldsymbol{\Omega}) \cdot \mathbf{B}}{4\pi^2 I_1} \), where \( I_1 = (\mathbf{B}^2 - \mathbf{E}^2)^{1/2} \)
- Axial current: \( \mathbf{J}_5 = -\frac{|q| \mathbf{E}^2}{4\pi^2 I_1} \boldsymbol{\Omega} \)
- Vector current: \( \mathbf{J} = \frac{q \mathbf{E} \times \boldsymbol{\Omega}}{4\pi^2} \)
- The vector current coefficient from Kubo formula: \( \lambda_3 = \frac{qE}{4\pi^2} \)
- The chiral density coefficient from Kubo formula: \( \lambda_5 = -\frac{|q|EB}{4\pi^2 I_1} \)
- In the presence of a boundary-induced mass gap \( \delta m \), the chiral density becomes \( n_5(z) = -\Omega \frac{|q|EB}{4\pi^2 I_1} \left[1 + 2x(\psi_0(z) - \ln z)\right] \) with \( z = \delta m^2/(2|qI_1|) \); in the large-mass limit, \( n_5 \rightarrow \Omega \frac{q^2 EB}{12\pi^2 \delta m^2} \)

## Context
This work extends the family of chiral anomaly transport phenomena, including the chiral magnetic effect (CME) of Kharzeev (2007) and Fukushima (2008), the chiral vortical effect of Erdmenger (2008) and Son (2009), and the magnetovorticity effect of Hattori (2016), to the novel configuration of orthogonal rotation and electromagnetic fields. The vector current (termed chiral electric vortical effect, CEVE) constitutes a new member of the chiral anomaly transport family, distinct from classical Hall effects. The results enable future studies of these effects in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, where strong electromagnetic fields and vorticity coexist, and suggest that the axial current can be greatly enhanced by reducing the Lorentz invariant field strength \( I_1 \).
