# Exact Analytical Solution of the Constrained Statistical Multifragmentation Model and Phase Transitions in Finite Systems

> We discuss an exact analytical solution of a simplified version of the statistical multifragmentation model with the restriction that the largest fragment size cannot exceed the finite volume of the system. A complete analysis of the isobaric partition singularities of this model is done for finite volumes. It is shown that the real part of any simple pole of the isobaric partition defines the free energy of the corresponding state, whereas its imaginary part, depending on the sign, defines the inverse decay/formation time of this state. The developed formalism allows us, for the first time, to exactly define the finite volume analogs of gaseous, liquid and mixed phases of this model from the first principles of statistical mechanics and demonstrate the pitfalls of earlier works. The finite size effects for large fragments and the role of metastable (unstable) states are discussed.

# Exact Analytical Solution of the Constrained Statistical Multifragmentation Model and Phase Transitions in Finite Systems

## Key claim

The paper provides an exact analytical solution of a simplified version of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) with the constraint that the largest fragment size cannot exceed the finite volume of the system, but only for the case where the largest fragment size is fixed (volume-independent) does the model exhibit no phase transition; when the largest fragment size grows monotonically with volume, an infinite number of simple poles approach the liquid-phase singularity and generate a genuine phase transition in the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors introduce a Laplace-Fourier transform method to decouple the volume dependence of the constraint that the largest fragment size K(V) depends on the system volume V, reducing the grand canonical partition to a sum over residues of simple poles of the isobaric partition. The singularities are defined by the equation lambda_n = F(V, lambda_n), where F is a sum over fragment species with an upper limit K(V), and the real and imaginary parts of each pole are analyzed separately to identify gaseous, metastable, and liquid-like states. The Euler-MacLaurin formula is used to handle sums over fragment sizes when K(V) takes real values, and the mechanical pressure is derived from the volume derivative of the grand canonical partition.

## Key numbers

- The bulk binding energy per nucleon is W_o = 16 MeV, with the excited states contribution T^2/epsilon_o where epsilon_o = 16 MeV.
- The surface tension is parameterized as sigma(T) = sigma_o [(T_c^2 - T^2)/(T_c^2 + T^2)]^{5/4} with sigma_o = 18 MeV and T_c = 18 MeV (sigma = 0 at T >= T_c).
- The normal nuclear density is rho_o = 0.16 fm^{-3}, giving the excluded volume parameter b = 1/rho_o.
- For the case K(V) = alpha V/b with 0 < alpha <= 1, the parameter alpha can be set to pi/6 to account for geometric packing of a spherical fragment in a cubic volume.
- The decay/formation time of a metastable state with imaginary part I_n is tau_n = 1/(I_n b T), and for large K(V) the closest poles have I_n approximately pi n/(b K(V)), giving tau_n approximately K(V)/(pi n T).

## Context

This work extends the exact analytical solution of a simplified SMM found by Bugaev (2000, 2001) and Reuter (2001) from infinite to finite volumes, correcting earlier approaches such as Hill's treatment of finite-system thermodynamics which assumed the mixed phase consists of just two pure phases. The formalism enables first-principles study of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in finite systems without taking the thermodynamic limit, and it reveals that the finite-volume mixed phase consists of a stable gaseous phase plus a set of metastable states with different free energies, each containing the same fragments but with different weights. The method opens the possibility to study finite-size effects for large fragments and the role of metastable states in experimental multifragmentation data.
