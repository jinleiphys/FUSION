# Particle-number projected Bogoliubov coupled cluster theory. Application to the pairing Hamiltonian

> While coupled cluster theory accurately models weakly correlated quantum systems, it often fails in the presence of strong correlations where the standard mean-field picture is qualitatively incorrect. In many cases, these failures can be largely ameliorated by permitting the mean-field reference to break physical symmetries. Symmetry-broken coupled cluster, e.g. Bogoliubov coupled cluster, theory can indeed provide reasonably accurate energetic predictions, but the broken symmetry can compromise the quality of the resulting wave function and predictions of observables other than the energy. Merging symmetry projection and coupled cluster theory is therefore an appealing way to describe strongly correlated systems. Independently, two different but related formalisms have been recently proposed to achieve this goal. The two formalisms are contrasted in this manuscript, with results tested on the Richardson pairing Hamiltonian. Both formalisms are based on the disentangled cluster representation of the symmetry-rotated coupled cluster wavefunction. However, they differ in the way that the disentangled clusters are solved. One approach sets up angle-dependent coupled cluster equations, while the other involves first-order ordinary differential equations. The latter approach yields energies and occupation probabilities significantly better than those of number-projected BCS and BCS coupled cluster and, when the disentangled clusters are truncated at low excitation levels, has a computational cost not too much larger than that of BCS coupled cluster. The high quality of results presented in this manuscript indicates that symmetry-projected coupled cluster is a promising method that can accurately describe both weakly and strongly correlated finite many-fermion systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that particle-number projected Bogoliubov coupled cluster (PBCC) theory, when implemented via first-order ordinary differential equations for the disentangled cluster amplitudes, yields energies and occupation probabilities significantly better than number-projected BCS and BCS coupled cluster, provided the disentangled clusters are truncated at low excitation levels. The alternative approach of solving angle-dependent BCC equations for the disentangled clusters is shown to be energetically equivalent to unprojected BCC and must be discarded.

## Method

The authors contrast two formalisms for merging symmetry projection with Bogoliubov coupled cluster theory, both based on a disentangled cluster representation of the symmetry-rotated coupled cluster wavefunction. One approach sets up angle-dependent coupled cluster equations for the gauge-rotated cluster amplitudes, while the other involves solving first-order ordinary differential equations derived from the particle-number operator's action on the projected state. Both methods are tested on the Richardson pairing Hamiltonian at the single and double excitation levels (PBCS-CCSD), using a variation-after-projection Bogoliubov transformation obtained by minimizing the projected mean-field energy.

## Context

This work extends the independent formalisms of Duguet (2014, 2015) and Qiu (2017) for symmetry-projected coupled cluster theory, clarifying their structural identity while highlighting the critical difference in how the disentangled clusters are solved. The paper demonstrates that the angle-dependent BCC equation approach is energetically equivalent to unprojected BCC and must be discarded, whereas the ODE-based approach provides a practical route to accurate symmetry restoration. The results enable future applications of symmetry-projected coupled cluster to more general Bogoliubov coupled cluster problems in finite many-fermion systems, with the formalism applicable to other symmetries such as rotational (spin) symmetry.
