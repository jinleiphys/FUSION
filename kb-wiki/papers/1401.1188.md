# Anisotropic Hydrodynamics: Motivation and Methodology

> In this proceedings contribution I review recent progress in our understanding of the bulk dynamics of relativistic systems that possess potentially large local rest frame momentum-space anisotropies. In order to deal with these momentum-space anisotropies, a reorganization of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics can been made around an anisotropic background, and the resulting dynamical framework has been dubbed "anisotropic hydrodynamics." I also discuss expectations for the degree of momentum-space anisotropy of the quark gluon plasma generated in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC from second-order viscous hydrodynamics, strong-coupling approaches, and weak-coupling approaches.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon plasma created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions may be thermal but strongly anisotropic in momentum-space, implying that the system has at least two temperatures (transverse and longitudinal), even though ideal hydrodynamics had previously assumed complete isotropy.

## Method

The author reviews the development of anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro), which reorganizes relativistic viscous hydrodynamics around an anisotropic background distribution function of Romatschke-Strickland form, characterized by a single anisotropy parameter ξ. The framework is derived from kinetic theory by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, and has been extended to next-to-leading order (viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics, vaHydro) by treating dissipative corrections δf̃ perturbatively while keeping the spheroidal leading-order form non-perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC-like initial conditions (T₀ = 400 MeV at τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c) with η̄ = 1/4π, first-order Navier-Stokes gives (P_L/P_T)_NS ≈ 0.5
- For LHC-like initial conditions (T₀ = 600 MeV at τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c) with η̄ = 1/4π, first-order Navier-Stokes gives (P_L/P_T)_NS ≈ 0.35
- In the strong coupling limit of N=4 SYM, the shear relaxation time τ_π = (2 - log 2)/2πT, giving τ_π ∼ 0.1 fm/c for RHIC-like and τ_π ∼ 0.07 fm/c for LHC-like initial conditions
- AdS/CFT numerical GR simulations find early-time pressure anisotropies P_L/P_T ∼ 0.31 or smaller
- Weak-coupling CGC simulations with resummation find early-time pressure anisotropies in the range 0.01 to 0.5, depending on the strong coupling constant g_s = 0.1 to 0.4

## Context

This proceedings paper reviews progress in understanding momentum-space anisotropies in the QGP, extending earlier work on ideal hydrodynamics (Huovinen 2001, Hirano 2002, Kolb 2003) which assumed complete isotropy. The findings from second-order viscous hydrodynamics (Muronga 2003, Baier 2006, Song 2008, Denicol 2010, Schenke 2010), strong-coupling AdS/CFT approaches (Heller 2011, van der Schee 2013), and weak-coupling CGC simulations (Epelbaum and Gelis 2013) all consistently indicate large anisotropies, particularly at early times (τ ≲ 2 fm/c). The vaHydro framework (Bazow 2013) provides a superior approximation scheme compared to Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics, especially for early-time dynamics and near transverse edges of the overlap region.
