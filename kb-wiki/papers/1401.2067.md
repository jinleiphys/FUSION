# NLO QCD corrections for DVCS and TCS

> The inclusion of QCD corrections to the Born amplitude of deeply virtual Compton scattering in both spacelike (DVCS) and timelike (TCS) regimes modifies the extraction process of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from observables. In particular, gluon contributions are by no means negligible even in the medium energy range accessible at JLab12. We emphasize the complementarity of spacelike and timelike measurements and raise the question of factorization scale dependence of the O(alpha_S) results.

## Key claim

NLO QCD corrections to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) are large, but this is primarily due to the gluonic contribution which is absent at LO, not necessarily due to a slow convergence of the perturbative expansion; the true convergence rate cannot be assessed until NNLO contributions are computed.

## Method

The authors compute the next-to-leading order (NLO, O(α_S)) coefficient functions for the Compton form factors (CFFs) of both spacelike DVCS and timelike TCS, using the analyticity relation between the two channels. They evaluate the CFFs using two GPD models based on double distributions: the Goloskokov-Kroll (GK) model and a model based on the MSTW08 PDF parametrization, for a factorization scale μ_F² = Q² = 4 GeV² and t = -0.1 GeV².

## Key numbers

- For μ_F² = Q² = 4 GeV² and t = -0.1 GeV², gluonic NLO contributions change the sign of the real part of the CFF ℋ for almost all values of the skewness parameter ξ (DVCS) or η (TCS).
- The gluonic contribution to the imaginary part of the CFF remains sizeable for skewness parameter values up to 0.3.
- The factorization scale dependence is explored by varying μ_F² between Q² and Q²/4, with α_s = 0.3, for Q² = 4 GeV² and t = -0.1 GeV².
- DVCS cross section calculations are shown for E_e = 11 GeV, μ_F² = Q² = 4 GeV², and t = -0.2 GeV².
- TCS cross section calculations are shown for E_γ = 10 GeV, Q² = μ² = 4 GeV², and t = -0.1 GeV², integrated over θ ∈ (π/4, 3π/4).

## Context

This work extends the NLO QCD calculations for DVCS from earlier GPD studies (Moutarde 2013) to the timelike TCS channel, using the analyticity relation established by Pire, Szymanowski, and Wagner. The results demonstrate the complementarity of spacelike and timelike measurements for extracting GPDs, and raise the question of factorization scale dependence. The large gluonic NLO contributions motivate future NNLO calculations to assess the true convergence of the perturbative expansion, and the results are relevant for the medium energy range accessible at JLab12.
