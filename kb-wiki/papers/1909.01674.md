# A Particle Emitting Source From an Accelerating, Perturbative Solution of Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> The quark gluon plasma is formed in heavy-ion collisions, and it can be described by solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics. In this paper we utilize perturbative hydrodynamics, where we study first order perturbations on top of a known solution. We investigate the perturbations on top of the Hubble flow. From this perturbative solution we can give the form of the particle emitting source and calculate observables of heavy-ion collisions. We describe the source function and the single-particle momentum spectra for a spherically symmetric solution.

# A Particle Emitting Source From an Accelerating, Perturbative Solution of Relativistic Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The paper presents a first-order perturbative solution of relativistic hydrodynamics on top of the Hubble-flow that includes acceleration and a pressure gradient, from which the particle emitting source function and single-particle momentum spectra can be calculated; however, the perturbations cause only small deviations from the original quantities in the Gaussian saddlepoint approximation, and the product of the perturbation parameter δ and the exponent j defines the scale of the perturbations rather than these parameters acting independently.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics equations with a simple equation of state ε = κp and a conserved charge density n. They take the known 1+3D relativistic Hubble-flow solution (Csorgo 2003) as the zeroth-order background and add first-order perturbations to the four-velocity, pressure, and charge density fields. For a concrete solution they choose a scale parameter S = r^j / t^j, a Gaussian shape for the original charge density scale function, and a constant freeze-out hypersurface at proper time τ₀, then calculate the source function from a relativistic Jüttner-distribution and the single-particle momentum distribution using a Gaussian saddlepoint approximation.

## Key numbers

- The equation of state parameter κ is a constant in the relation ε = κp, with no specific numerical value assigned in the paper.
- The perturbation parameters are δ (dimensionless), a (dimensionless), and j (exponent of the scale parameter); the observables depend only on the product δj, not on δ or j separately.
- The source function becomes a two-component Gaussian with widths R and Rδ, where R² = T₀τ₀²(T_eff − T₀)/(E T_eff) and Rδ² = T₀τ₀²(T_δ − T₀)/(E T_δ).
- The effective temperatures are T_eff = T₀ + (T₀E Ṙ₀²)/(2b(T₀ − E)) and T_δ = T₀ + (T₀E Ṙ₀²)/(2b(2T₀ − E)).
- The perturbative terms ε₁ through ε₅ contain factors such as δj, τ₀, r, and combinations of the model parameters; for example, ε₁ = δj (2abκτ₀⁴)/((κ−3)Ṙ₀² r (τ₀² + r²)³/²).

## Context

This work extends the exact relativistic Hubble-flow solution (Csorgo 2003) by adding acceleration and pressure gradient perturbations, building on a perturbative framework developed in Kurgyis (2017). The calculated observables are compared to those from the exact solution (Csanad 2009), and the HBT-radius scaling R ∝ 1/√m_T reproduces the experimentally observed transverse mass scaling seen in data from Adler (2004) and Afanasiev (2009). The results enable future calculation of elliptic flow in non-spherically symmetric cases and more detailed studies of the role of acceleration in heavy-ion collision observables.
