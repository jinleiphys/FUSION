# Where is the Baseline for Color Transparency Studies with Moderate Energy Electron Beams?

> Study of color transparency (CT) effects at moderate energies is more problematic than is usually supposed. Onset of CT can be imitated by other mechanisms, which contain no explicit QCD dynamics. In the case of the $(e,e'p)$ reaction the standard inelastic shadowing well known in the pre-QCD era, causes a substantial growth of nuclear transparency with $Q^2$ and a deviation from the Glauber model, analogous to what is assumed to be a signal of CT. In the case of exclusive virtual photoproduction of vector mesons, CT is expected to manifest itself as an increase of nuclear transparency with $Q^2$ in production of the ground states and as an abnormal nuclear enhancement for the radial excitations. We demonstrate that analogous $Q^2$-dependence can be caused at moderate energies by the variation of so called coherence length, which is an interference effect, even in the framework of the vector dominance model. One should disentangle the real and the mock CT effects in experiments planned at CEBAF, at the HERMES spectrometer, or at the future electron facility ELFE.

## Key claim

The onset of color transparency (CT) at moderate energies can be imitated by standard inelastic shadowing (in the case of (e,e'p) reactions) and by variation of the coherence length (in exclusive vector meson photoproduction), both of which contain no explicit QCD dynamics and produce a Q²-dependent rise in nuclear transparency that mimics the expected CT signal.

## Method

The authors calculate the first-order inelastic correction (IC) to the Glauber model for the (e,e'p) reaction, using the forward diffraction dissociation cross section from NN data and realistic nuclear densities, to compute the Q²-dependent nuclear transparency. For exclusive virtual photoproduction of vector mesons, they apply the Glauber model with coherence length effects incorporated, deriving expressions for both coherent and incoherent production of ground-state and radially excited vector mesons, and compare these with predictions that include CT dynamics.

## Key numbers

- In Pb(e,e'p), the first-order IC and the CT model predict about the same magnitude of deviation from the Glauber model up to Q² ≈ 20 GeV².
- For coherent ρ-meson photoproduction on heavy nuclei, nuclear transparency is strongly suppressed at CEBAF energies but becomes sizeable at HERMES-ELFE energies.
- For incoherent ρ-meson photoproduction, nuclear transparency decreases dramatically from low to high energies, with the high-energy value approximately the square of the low-energy value.
- For φ-meson photoproduction at ν = 8 GeV, coherence length effects produce substantial Q²-dependence in nuclear transparency despite the small φ absorption cross section.
- The ratio R²(Ψ'/Ψ) at Q²=0 is approximately 0.25 from the two-channel VDM calculation, close to the measured value of 0.15 ± 0.05 in real photoproduction.
- For ρ'/ρ production at Q²=0, the predicted R² ranges from 0.005 (relativized quark model with leptonic width ratio 0.064) to 0.22 (using experimental leptonic width estimate of 2 KeV).

## Context

This work extends the pre-QCD understanding of inelastic shadowing, originally formulated by Gribov (1969) and confirmed by measurements of neutron-nucleus total cross sections (Murthy et al., 1975) and neutral K-meson cross sections (Gsponer et al., 1979), to the context of color transparency searches. The authors demonstrate that the first-order IC, which is independent of QCD dynamics, produces a Q²-dependent rise in nuclear transparency that can mimic CT up to Q² ≈ 20 GeV² in (e,e'p) reactions, and that coherence length variation in vector meson photoproduction produces analogous effects even within the vector dominance model. This work establishes a baseline for CT studies at CEBAF, HERMES, and ELFE, and suggests that reliable disentanglement of real CT from mock effects requires Q² of a few tens of GeV², or alternatively the use of asymmetry measurements in missing momentum (as proposed by Kopeliovich and Nemchik, 1994) which are free from the inelastic shadowing background.
