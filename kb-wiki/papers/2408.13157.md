# Generating ultra-compact neutron stars with bosonic dark matter

> In this work we investigate the properties of neutron stars admixed with selfinteracting scalar bosonic dark matter. The dark matter interaction is described by a generalized $ϕ^n$ power-law potential. We perform a stability analysis of these two-fluid objects by studying the onset of the unstable radial modes. We find ultra-compact neutron star-dark matter configurations where the neutron star matter is confined to a core radius of values below $7$ km which is unreachable for pure neutron stars. The total gravitational maximum mass of these ultra-compact configurations can have values of $3.4 \, M_\odot$. With our general ansatz of the power-law potential we show that the compactness of these solutions can be extreme, i.e. the compactness is $C = 1/3$ or even larger, making them compact enough to have a light-ring mimicking black holes. These ultra-compact objects are stable and possess a dark matter halo while having a hadronic matter core. With the addition of dark matter to neutron stars recent unusual mass-radius measurements of compact stars can be explained. We conclude that apparently contradictory measurements of neutron star masses and radii could be not only an indication of the presence of dark matter around a the hadronic matter core which is stabilized by the gravitational potential of dark matter but could also serve to disentangle the selfinteraction strength of dark matter. Our work points to a stiff equation of state for the dark matter fluid, rather than a soft one.

## Key claim
The paper claims that stable ultra-compact neutron star configurations with a hadronic matter core confined to radii below 7 km and total gravitational masses up to 3.4 solar masses can exist when admixed with self-interacting scalar bosonic dark matter, but only if the dark matter self-interaction potential is sufficiently stiff (high power-law exponent n), specifically n=40 rather than the standard n=4 case.

## Method
The authors solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations coupling ordinary nuclear matter (described by piecewise polytropes EoS1 and EoS2 from Kurkela (2014)) with bosonic dark matter described by a complex scalar field with a generalized φ^n power-law self-interaction potential. They perform a stability analysis by studying radial density perturbations of both fluids simultaneously, computing eigenvalues of the matrix of partial derivatives of total particle numbers with respect to central energy densities, following the method of Hippert (2022). The ordinary matter equation of state combines the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland outer crust, Negele-Vautherin inner crust, chiral effective field theory results from Hebeler (2013), and the piecewise-polytrope core.

## Key numbers
- Total gravitational maximum mass of ultra-compact configurations can reach 3.4 solar masses
- Neutron star matter confined to a core radius below 7 km, unreachable for pure neutron stars
- Compactness C = 1/3 or larger, with maximum compactness approaching the causality limit C = 0.354
- For n=40 with boson mass m_b = 200 MeV, stable configurations exist with masses above 3 solar masses and DM central pressure in the range 5000-6000 MeV/fm^3
- For n=40 with m_b = 300 MeV, XTE J1814-338 constraints (M = 1.21 ± 0.05 solar masses, R = 7.0 ± 0.4 km) are satisfied with DM central pressure 590-980 MeV/fm^3 and DM fraction 51-57%
- For n=40 with m_b = 200 MeV, XTE J1814-338 constraints are satisfied with DM central pressure 30-35 MeV/fm^3 and DM fraction 84-90%
- For n=4, maximum compactness is C = 0.16 for pure boson stars (Pau Amaro-Seoane, 2010), and C = 0.29 when combined with EoS1
- Pure EoS1 maximum mass is 2.03 solar masses with radius 10.4 km and compactness 0.29
- Pure EoS2 maximum mass is 2.45 solar masses with radius 13.3 km and compactness 0.27

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutron stars with bosonic dark matter by Zhang (2020) which considered φ^n potentials up to n=10 with compactness below C < 0.3, and the authors' own prior work on ultra-compact boson stars (Pitz, 2023). It contrasts with standard φ^4 studies by Rafiei Karkevandi (2021), Shakeri (2022), and Diedrichs (2023) which found insufficient stiffness to produce ultra-compact configurations. The results enable interpretation of contradictory mass-radius measurements such as HESS J1731-347 (2022) with M = 0.77 solar masses and R = 10.4 km, and XTE J1814-338 (Kini, 2024) with M = 1.21 solar masses and R = 7.0 km, as potential signatures of stiff dark matter rather than exotic hadronic physics. The work also addresses the mass-gap object GW230529 from LIGO-Virgo (2024) in the range 2.5-4.5 solar masses.
