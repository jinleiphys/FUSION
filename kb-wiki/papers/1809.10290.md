# Three-Body Decay of $Λ_c^{*}(2595)$ and $Λ_c^{*}(2625)$ with the Inclusion of Direct Two-Pion Coupling

> We investigate the three-body decays of $Λ_c^*(2595)$ and $Λ_c^*(2625)$ into $Λ_c ππ$ by including a direct process going through the two-pion coupling of $Λ_c^*Λ_c ππ$ in addition to the sequential processes going through $Σ_c(2455)$ and $Σ_c^*(2520)$. The strength of the direct coupling is related to that of the Yukawa coupling by the chiral partner structure between $Λ_c$'s and $Σ_c$'s, while the strength of the Yukawa coupling is estimated by the quark model. It is found that the direct process gives a considerable contribution especially in the decay of $Λ_c^*(2625)$. A clear indication of the direct process is seen in an asymmetric pattern in the angular correlation between the two pions. We discuss the usefulness of the detailed analysis of the decays for the study of the structure of $Λ_c^*$.

## Key claim

The direct two-pion coupling process, whose strength is related to the Yukawa coupling via the chiral partner structure between Λ_c and Σ_c baryons, gives a considerable contribution to the three-body decay of Λ_c*(2625) but only a small contribution to Λ_c*(2595), with the clearest signature being an asymmetric pattern in the angular correlation between the two pions.

## Method

The authors computed three-body decay amplitudes for Λ_c*(2595) and Λ_c*(2625) → Λ_c ππ by combining sequential processes through Σ_c(2455) and Σ_c*(2520) intermediate states with a direct Λ_c*Λ_c ππ coupling term. The direct coupling strength was estimated by equating it to the Yukawa coupling of the second vertex in the sequential decay, following the chiral partner classification of Kawakami and Harada (2018), while the Yukawa couplings for sequential processes were taken from the quark model. Non-relativistic reductions of the amplitudes were performed, and the decay widths, Dalitz plots, invariant mass distributions, and angular correlations were calculated including interference effects.

## Key numbers

- Total decay width of Λ_c*(2595) with λ-mode assignment: 2.222 MeV (compared to experimental value 2.6 ± 0.6 MeV from PDG)
- Direct process contribution to Λ_c*(2595) total width: 0.004 MeV (charged channels) and 0.005 MeV (neutral channel)
- Total decay width of Λ_c*(2625) with λ-mode assignment: 0.570 MeV (compared to experimental upper limit < 0.97 MeV from PDG)
- Direct process contribution to Λ_c*(2625) total width: 0.061 MeV (charged channels) and 0.045 MeV (neutral channel)
- Branching fraction B(Σ_c(2455)^0 π^+) for Λ_c*(2595): 0.082 (compared to Belle measurement 0.125 ± 0.035)
- Branching fraction B(Σ_c(2455)^0 π^+) for Λ_c*(2625): 0.065
- Pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV used in direct coupling amplitudes

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Arifi, Nagahiro, Hosaka, 2017) of three-body decays of Λ_c* resonances, which considered only sequential processes through Σ_c(2455) and Σ_c*(2520). It incorporates the chiral partner classification proposed by Kawakami and Harada (2018), which relates the direct two-pion coupling to the Yukawa coupling. The results enable experimental discrimination between λ-mode and ρ-mode quark model assignments for Λ_c*(2625) through detailed Dalitz plot and angular correlation measurements, particularly the asymmetric pattern in the ππ angular correlation that serves as a clear signature of the direct process.
