# Dynamical and statistical description of multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions

> To explore the roles of dynamical model and statistical model in the description of multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, the fragments charge distributions of $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at 35 MeV/u are analyzed by using the hybrid model of improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model plus the statistical model GEMINI. We find that, the ImQMD model can well describe the charge distributions of fragments produced in central and semi-central collisions. But for the peripheral collisions of Au+Au at 35 MeV/u, the statistical model is required at the end of the ImQMD simulations for the better description of the charge distribution of fragments. By using the hybrid model of ImQMD+GEMINI, the fragment charge distribution of Au+Au at 35 MeV/u can be reproduced reasonably well. The time evolution of the excitation energies of primary fragments is simultaneously investigated.

## Key claim

The hybrid model of improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) plus the statistical model GEMINI can reproduce the fragment charge distribution of 197Au+197Au at 35 MeV/u reasonably well, but the dynamical model alone is sufficient for central and semi-central collisions, while the statistical model is required only for peripheral collisions to account for the yield of intermediate mass fragments.

## Method

The authors simulated 197Au+197Au collisions at 35 MeV/u using the ImQMD dynamical model with the IQ3 parameter set, then applied the MST algorithm to identify primary fragments at a switch time of 500 fm/c. The excitation energies of these primary fragments were calculated and used as input for the GEMINI statistical decay model, which simulates sequential binary decays including nucleon emission, light particle evaporation, asymmetric fission, and gamma emission until all fragments are stable.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 35 MeV/u for 197Au+197Au collisions
- MST cluster parameters: Rc = 3.5 fm and Pc = 300 MeV/c for fragment recognition
- Switch time for hybrid model: t = 500 fm/c, where ImQMD results are passed to GEMINI
- For head-on test reactions, the excitation energy EQ* values were 65.7 MeV for 40Ca+40Ca (Ecm = 80 MeV), 50.3 MeV for 16O+46Ti (Ecm = 38 MeV), and 46.1 MeV for 16O+92Zr (Ecm = 50 MeV)
- Impact parameters studied: b = 1, 6, and 10 fm for central, semi-central, and peripheral collisions respectively

## Context

This work extends the use of hybrid dynamical-plus-statistical models for multifragmentation, building on prior transport model studies such as the QMD approach by Aichelin (1991) and the GEMINI model by Charity (1988, 2001). The authors compare their results to experimental data from Lavaud (2002) for central collisions (INDRA+ALADIN apparatus) and Agostino (1999) for peripheral collisions (MULTICS apparatus). The study demonstrates that for heavy systems like Au+Au, the ImQMD model alone cannot describe peripheral collisions due to missing quantum correlations, and the hybrid approach enables simultaneous reproduction of data from two different experiments without parameter readjustment.
