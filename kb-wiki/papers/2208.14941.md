# Coupled fission fragment angular momenta

> Nuclear fission produces fragments whose spins are coupled to the relative angular motion via angular momentum conservation. It is shown how ensembles of such spins can readily be obtained by either direct microcanonical sampling or by sampling of the associated normal modes of rotation. The resulting distribution of the spin-spin opening angle is illustrated in various three- and two- dimensional scenarios and it is demonstrated how recent mutually conflicting model calculations can be well reproduced with different assumptions about the scission geometry.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the widely different predictions for the distribution of the spin-spin opening angle between fission fragments can be reproduced by a statistical sampling framework using different assumptions about the scission geometry, specifically the relative magnitude of the moment of inertia for the relative motion.

## Method

The author develops two equivalent methods for sampling ensembles of coupled angular momenta: direct microcanonical sampling and sampling of normal modes of rotation. Both methods enforce angular momentum conservation (with total angular momentum set to zero) while allowing the rotational energies to follow canonical distributions characterized by the scission temperature. The methods are applied to calculate the distribution of the opening angle between the two fragment spins, \(P_{12}(\psi)\), for both three-dimensional and two-dimensional spin scenarios using different ratios of the moments of inertia for the fragments and the relative motion.

## Key numbers

- For the 2D scission scenario with moments of inertia ratios \({\cal I}_1:{\cal I}_2:{\cal I}_0 = 0.75:1.25:17\), the first-order Fourier coefficient for the opening-angle distribution is \(f_1 = -0.086\).
- For the 2D touching-sphere scenario with ratios \(1:1:5\), the Fourier coefficients are \(f_1 = -0.264\) and \(f_2 = 0.028\).
- The scission scenario uses a center separation that exceeds the sum of the fragment radii by \(d = 4\) fm, with moment of inertia ratios \({\cal I}_1:{\cal I}_2:{\cal I}_0 = 0.75:1.25:17\).
- The compact scenario that reproduces the results of Bulgac et al. uses ratios \({\cal I}_1:{\cal I}_2:{\cal I}_0 = 1:1:2\), corresponding to a relative moment of inertia that is only 40% of that for touching spheres.

## Context

This work extends the statistical framework for fission fragment angular momenta previously developed for the fission event generator FREYA (Randrup, 2022; Vogt, 2021) and addresses conflicting predictions from time-dependent density functional theory calculations by Bulgac et al. (2022) and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics simulations by Chen, Ishizuka, and Chiba (2022). The analysis enables understanding of how different assumptions about the scission geometry and the relative moment of inertia produce the observed discrepancies, and it highlights the need for experimental measurements of the spin-spin opening angle to clarify the scission physics.
