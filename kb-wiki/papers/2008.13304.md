# Gradient tomography of jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We illustrate with both a Boltzmann diffusion equation and full simulations of jet propagation in heavy-ion collisions within the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model that the spatial gradient of the jet transport coefficient perpendicular to the propagation direction can lead to a drift and asymmetry in the transverse momentum distribution. Such an asymmetry depends on both the spatial position along the transverse gradience and the propagating length. It can be used to localize the initial jet production positions for more detailed studies of jet quenching and properties of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the spatial gradient of the jet transport coefficient \(\hat q\) perpendicular to a jet's propagation direction induces a measurable drift and asymmetry in the jet's transverse momentum distribution, which can be used to localize the initial jet production position in heavy-ion collisions. This claim is conditional on the existence of a non-uniform medium with a transverse gradient of \(\hat q\), and the effect depends on both the spatial position along the gradient and the propagation length.

## Method

The authors first derive an analytical solution to a drift-diffusion equation for parton propagation in a static, uniform medium, showing a correlation between transverse momentum and spatial position. They then perform full simulations using the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes both elastic and radiative processes, with a 3+1D hydrodynamic medium of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to model jet propagation in realistic heavy-ion collisions. A toy model with a longitudinally expanding medium and varying ellipticity is used to isolate the effect of the transverse gradient of \(\hat q\) on the transverse asymmetry \(A_N^{\vec n}\).

## Key numbers

- The toy model uses \(\hat q_0 = 5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm and \(t_0 = 0.5\) fm/\(c\) for the jet transport coefficient.
- The LBT simulations are performed for 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) TeV.
- The trigger photon has \(p_T^\gamma = 60-80\) GeV/\(c\) and the jet has \(p_T^{\rm jet} = 80-90\) GeV/\(c\).
- The transverse asymmetry \(A_{E_\perp}\) values used for localization are \(0.2\), \(0\), and \(-0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the standard eikonal approximation for jet propagation, which neglects transverse diffusion, by showing that the gradient of \(\hat q\) produces a drift term in the Boltzmann equation. It builds on the JET Collaboration's extraction of \(\hat q\) from single inclusive hadron suppression (Burke 2013) and prior LBT model studies (He 2015, Luo 2018) that described jet and \(\gamma\)-jet suppression. The gradient tomography technique enables future detailed studies of the length dependence of jet energy loss and the spatial distribution of \(\hat q\) in the quark-gluon plasma.
