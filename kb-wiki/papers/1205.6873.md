# Thermalization from gauge/gravity duality: Evolution of singularities in unequal time correlators

> We consider a gauge/gravity dual model of thermalization which consists of a collapsing thin matter shell in asymptotically Anti-de Sitter space. A central aspect of our model is to consider a shell moving at finite velocity as determined by its equation of motion, rather than a quasi-static approximation as considered previously in the literature. By applying a divergence matching method, we obtain the evolution of singularities in the retarded unequal time correlator $G^R(t,t')$, which probes different stages of the thermalization. We find that the number of singularities decreases from a finite number to zero as the gauge theory thermalizes. This may be interpreted as a sign of decoherence. Moreover, in a second part of the paper, we show explicitly that the thermal correlator is characterized by the existence of singularities in the complex time plane. By studying a quasi-static state, we show the singularities at real times originate from contributions of normal modes. We also investigate the possibility of obtaining complex singularities from contributions of quasi-normal modes.

## Key claim

The number of singularities in the retarded unequal time correlator decreases from a finite number to zero as the gauge theory thermalizes, which may be interpreted as a sign of decoherence; this result depends on considering a shell moving at finite velocity rather than using the quasi-static approximation used in prior work.

## Method

The authors model thermalization via gauge/gravity duality as a collapsing thin matter shell in asymptotically Anti-de Sitter space, with the shell moving at finite velocity determined by its equation of motion. They apply a divergence matching method to study the singularities of the retarded unequal time correlator \(G^R(t,t')\) for a probe scalar field in this background, analyzing how light rays bounce between the AdS boundary and the collapsing shell. The method determines the singular part of the correlator without solving the full scalar wave equation in the bulk, using WKB solutions and matching conditions on the shell derived from the Israel junction conditions.

## Key numbers

* For a conformal equation of state with \(a=1/3\), the shell trajectory satisfies \(\sqrt{1+\dot{z}^2}-\sqrt{f+\dot{z}^2}=bz^4\), with \(b\) a parameter and \(z_h=1\) setting the temperature scale.
* The critical times \(T_n(z_s)\) separate regions with different numbers of bouncings; for a given intrinsic scale \(z_s\), smaller \(t'\) and larger \(1/z_s\) lead to more bouncings.
* The recursion relation for the singularity amplitude \(B_n\) in the gravitational collapse model is \(B_1=B_0\frac{d-1}{4z_0(c-1)}\frac{f(\sqrt{1+\dot{z}^2}-\sqrt{f+\dot{z}^2})}{\sqrt{f+\dot{z}^2}+\dot{z}}\left(\frac{\sqrt{f+\dot{z}^2}+\dot{z}}{\sqrt{f+\dot{z}^2}-\dot{z}}\right)^c\), where \(c=(d+1)/2\) and \(d\) is the spacetime dimension.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of unequal time correlators using a moving mirror toy model in Anti-de Sitter space (Erdmenger, Lin, and Hoyos, 2011; Erdmenger, Hoyos, and Lin, 2012), where the divergence matching method was developed. It goes beyond the quasi-static approximation used in shell collapse models (shell, 2011) by including finite velocity motion. The results enable future study of how quasi-normal modes evolve during thermalization and how complex singularities in the thermal correlator relate to the approach to equilibrium.
