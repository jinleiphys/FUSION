# On the effect of secondary protons on baryon and proton number cumulants in event-by-event analysis

> We investigate the effects of secondary (knockout) protons, which constitute about 20% of the observed protons at STAR, on the higher order cumulants of proton and baryon numbers measured by event-by-event analyses in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We argue that the contribution of this background effect on the cumulants is expressed by a simple formula, and that hence their effects can be removed in the experimental analysis. It is discussed that this background effect has non-negligible contribution to recently observed proton number cumulants at STAR, especially the third-order one, and that the removal of this effect is crucial to investigate the thermodynamical properties of the primordial hot medium appropriately.

## Key claim

Secondary (knockout) protons, which constitute about 20% of observed protons at STAR, have a non-negligible effect on higher-order cumulants of net-proton and net-baryon numbers, especially the third-order cumulant at large collision energies; however, this background can be removed using a simple additive formula because the secondary proton distribution is Poissonian and uncorrelated with the genuine proton number when events are binned by charged-particle multiplicity.

## Method

The authors derived analytic relations showing that the cumulants of the experimentally observed proton number are the sum of the cumulants of the genuine (collision-origin) proton number and the mean number of secondary protons, based on the Poissonian nature of the secondary proton distribution and the assumption that correlations between secondary and genuine protons vanish under narrow centrality binning. They applied these relations to net-proton and net-baryon number cumulants, and estimated the modification to cumulant ratios using the approximate value ⟨N_p^2nd⟩ ≈ 0.2 ⟨N_p^exp⟩ from STAR data at √s_NN = 62 GeV AuAu collisions for the transverse momentum range 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Secondary protons constitute about 20% of observed protons at STAR for the typical p_T range 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV in 62 GeV AuAu collisions.
- The probability r that a particle from the hot medium creates a misidentified secondary proton is of order 10^(-2), significantly smaller than unity.
- At STAR, R^exp_32 ≈ 0.2 at √s_NN ≈ 200 GeV; after correction for secondary protons, R^col_32 ≈ R^exp_32 - 0.1, representing about a 50% modification.
- At STAR, R^exp_42 ≈ 0.8 at √s_NN ≈ 20 GeV is the smallest value; the correction gives 1 - R^col_42 ≈ 1.1 (1 - R^exp_42), about a 10% modification in the deviation from unity.
- In the HRG model, the parameter r (defined as 0.2 ⟨N_p^exp⟩ / ⟨(δN_p-p̅^exp)^m⟩) approaches 0.1 for large √s_NN and r ≈ 0.2 for √s_NN ≲ 20 GeV for even m.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for relating proton number fluctuations to baryon number cumulants developed by Kitazawa and Asakawa (2012) (Refs. KA1, KA2 in the text), by adding the treatment of secondary proton backgrounds that had not been accounted for in existing experimental fluctuation analyses at STAR. The results enable experimentalists to remove this background effect from measured cumulants, which is crucial for comparing experimental data with lattice QCD predictions and hadron resonance gas model baselines, particularly for the beam energy scan program at RHIC where deviations from Poissonian behavior are expected to signal non-hadronic properties of the hot medium.
