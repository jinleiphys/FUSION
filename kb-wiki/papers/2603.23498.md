# Hall Viscosity in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study the Hall viscosity of the quark gluon plasma (QGP) created in non-central heavy-ion collisions. In the presence of a strong magnetic field or vorticity, rotational symmetry is broken from O(3) to O(2), allowing for two independent Hall viscosities associated with shear deformations transverse and parallel to the symmetry-breaking direction. We find the corresponding constitutive relations by extending the kinetic-theory mechanism to three spatial dimensions and provide parametric estimates of the Hall viscosities under realistic QGP conditions. Both kinetic-theory and holographic estimates indicate that Hall viscosities are comparable in magnitude to the shear viscosity at zero magnetic field. We further show that Hall viscous stresses at hydrodynamic initialization can be as large as standard viscous corrections and identify observable consequences in flow and event-plane correlations.

# Hall Viscosity in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

Hall viscosities in the quark-gluon plasma created in non-central heavy-ion collisions are comparable in magnitude to the shear viscosity at zero magnetic field, and Hall viscous stresses at hydrodynamic initialization can be as large as standard viscous corrections, but the analysis is limited to controlled order-of-magnitude estimates rather than a full relativistic magneto-hydrodynamic simulation.

## Method

The authors extend a kinetic-theory mechanism originally proposed by Alekseev (2016) for two-dimensional charged fluids to three spatial dimensions, deriving constitutive relations for the stress tensor in the presence of a magnetic field that breaks rotational symmetry from O(3) to O(2). They obtain parametric estimates of the two independent Hall viscosities (perpendicular and parallel to the symmetry-breaking direction) using both weak-coupling kinetic theory with quasiparticle relaxation times and strong-coupling holographic methods based on a five-dimensional asymptotically AdS charged magnetic black-brane geometry within Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory.

## Key numbers

- Zero magnetic field shear viscosity estimate: η₀ ≈ 1.95 T³, giving η₀/s ≈ 0.12 at T ≈ 300 MeV
- At T ≈ 300 MeV with qB ≈ 10¹⁹ Gauss and thermal quark mass m ∼ gT: transverse Hall viscosity η̃_⟂ ≈ 0.48 η₀ and longitudinal Hall viscosity η̃_∥ ≈ 0.93 η₀
- At T ≈ 300 MeV with massless quark assumption: transverse Hall viscosity η̃_⟂ ≈ 0.43 η₀ and longitudinal Hall viscosity η̃_∥ ≈ 0.99 η₀
- Cyclotron frequency parameter estimate: ϖ = 2 ω_c τ₂ ≈ 1.34 for thermal mass quarks, or ϖ ≈ 1.77 for massless quarks
- Zero magnetic field shear viscosity numerical value: η₀ ≈ 5.26 × 10⁷ MeV³ at T ≈ 300 MeV
- Energy density of QGP: ε ≈ 12.17 T⁴, with entropy density s ≈ 16.23 T³ using conformal equation of state
- Relaxation time estimate: τ₂ ≈ 0.4 fm/c or τ₂ ≈ 0.6/T for T ≈ 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the kinetic-theory mechanism for Hall viscosity from two-dimensional Dirac semimetals studied by Alekseev (2016) to three-dimensional quark-gluon plasma, and complements holographic computations of Hall viscosities in strongly coupled plasmas by Landsteiner (2016) and Ammon (2020). The results provide order-of-magnitude benchmarks that motivate future quantitative simulations incorporating Hall viscosity into full magneto-hydrodynamic descriptions of heavy-ion collisions, where previous work by Heinz (2013), Gale (2013), and Bernhard (2015) has established the success of viscous relativistic hydrodynamics but has not included Hall viscous terms.
