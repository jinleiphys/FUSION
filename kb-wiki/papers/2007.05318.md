# Charge Distributions of Moving Nucleons

> Relativistic charge distributions of targets with arbitrary average momentum are introduced. They provide an interpolation between the usual Breit frame and infinite-momentum frame distributions. We find that Breit frame distributions can be interpreted from a phase-space perspective as internal charge quasi-densities in the rest frame of a localized target, without any relativistic correction. We show also that the apparent discrepancies between Breit frame and infinite-momentum frame distributions simply result from kinematical artifacts associated with spin.

## Key claim

The paper shows that relativistic charge distributions for moving nucleons can be defined as quasi-densities in a phase-space approach, and that the apparent negative center of the neutron charge distribution in the infinite-momentum frame does not contradict the rest-frame picture but simply results from relativistic kinematical effects associated with spin.

## Method

The author introduces elastic-frame (EF) charge distributions for targets with arbitrary average momentum, defined via Wigner phase-space distributions and the off-forward matrix elements of the charge four-current operator. For spin-0 and spin-1/2 targets, the formalism is applied to derive explicit expressions for the charge distributions in terms of Sachs form factors, including a kinematical factor M/P^0 that was previously omitted. The unpolarized part of the EF charge distribution is decomposed into convection and magnetization contributions, and the evolution with target momentum P_z is computed using a phenomenological parametrization of nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron, the center of the charge distribution vanishes when the target momentum reaches P_z = 1.31 GeV.
- The Sachs charge distributions differ from the Breit frame quasi-densities by a kinematical factor M/P^0 = (1+tau)^{-1/2}, where tau = Q^2/4M^2.
- The total charge integral yields G_E(0) for any frame, confirming Lorentz scalar behavior.
- The differential elastic cross section in first Born approximation contains the factor 1/(1+tau) multiplying the Sachs form factor combination.

## Context

This work extends the phase-space approach previously applied to the nucleon energy-momentum tensor by Lorce (2017, 2018) to the electromagnetic charge current. It resolves the long-standing discrepancy between Breit frame and infinite-momentum frame charge distributions noted by Miller (2007), who found a negative center for the neutron IMF distribution in conflict with gluon-exchange and meson-cloud models. The formalism provides a unified interpolation between the Breit frame and infinite-momentum frame distributions, and clarifies the role of the kinematical factor that was debated by Yennie (1957) and Friar (1975). The results enable a consistent relativistic interpretation of charge distributions extracted from elastic scattering data without ambiguous relativistic corrections.
