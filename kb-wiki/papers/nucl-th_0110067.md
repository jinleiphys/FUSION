# Interplay of pairing and multipole interactions in a simple model

> The interplay of pairing and other interactions is addressed in this work using a simple single-j model. We show that enhancements in pairing correlations observed through studies of the spectra of deformed systems, moments of inertia, changes in transitional multipole amplitudes, and direct calculations of the pairing component in the wave function, indicate that even without explicit matrix elements responsible for pairing, a paired state can still appear from the kinematic coupling of pairing to deformation and from other geometrical restrictions that are of extreme importance in mesoscopic systems. Furthermore, we demonstrate that macroscopic transitions such as oblate to prolate shape changes can lead to strong dynamic enhancements of pairing correlations. In this work we emphasize that the pairing condensate has an important dynamic and kinematic effect on other residual interactions.

# Interplay of pairing and multipole interactions in a simple model

## Key claim
Even without explicit pairing matrix elements, a paired state can still emerge from kinematic coupling of pairing to deformation and from geometrical restrictions in mesoscopic systems; macroscopic transitions such as oblate-to-prolate shape changes can produce strong dynamic enhancements of pairing correlations.

## Method
The author uses a single-j shell model with one species of particle, where the rotationally invariant two-body interaction Hamiltonian is expressed in terms of pair operators coupled to angular momentum L. The Hamiltonian is transformed between particle-particle and particle-hole channels using the Pandya transformation, and the quasispin SU(2) algebra of pair operators is employed to evaluate expectation values of all residual interactions on paired states with definite seniority. Perturbative treatment of non-pairing interactions in the basis of paired states is performed, and the stability of the pairing condensate is examined through the separation energy of a particle pair.

## Key numbers
- The expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the paired (seniority s=0) state is: ⟨N,0|H|N,0⟩ = (V₀/2) × N(Ω−N)/(Ω−2) − Ṽ₀ × N(N−2)/(Ω−2), where Ω = 2j+1.
- For the s=1 state: ⟨N,1|H|N,1⟩ = (N−1)/(Ω−2) × [(Ω−N−1)V₀/2 − (N−1)Ṽ₀].
- The effective interaction between two unpaired particles is reduced by a factor of [1 − 2Nₚ(Ω−Nₚ−4)/((Ω−6)(Ω−4))], where Nₚ = N−s, with maximum weakening by about a factor of 1/2.
- The separation energy S = E(N,s=0) − E(N,s=2) in the large-Ω limit gives the stability condition for pairing: V₀ < V_L for any L≠0 at the edges of occupancy, and V₀ < V_L/2 − Ṽ_L/(2L+1) for any even L≠0 at half-filling (Nₚ/Ω = 1/2).

## Context
This work extends the understanding of pairing in nuclear systems beyond the traditional attribution to strong short-range residual interactions, building on Belyaev's demonstration that pure pairing interactions would violate gauge invariance. It connects to studies of two-body random interactions by Mulhall and Horoi (2001) showing that paired ground states do not appear under random interactions, and to Kadmenskii's (1987) argument that finiteness is key to the superconducting state in nuclei. The results enable a more complete treatment of the pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) model by incorporating kinematic pairing effects that are typically ignored, and provide a framework for evaluating the stability of pairing against multipole-multipole correlations in mesoscopic systems.
