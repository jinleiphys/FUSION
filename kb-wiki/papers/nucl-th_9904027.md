# Extraction of nucleon momentum distributions from inclusive electron scattering on nuclei

> We address the problem of extracting single-nucleon momentum distributions $n(p)$ from inclusice scattering data. A model for these relates nuclear and nucleon structure functions (SF) through an intermediate SF $f^{PN}$ for a nucleus of point-particles. In addition to the asymptotic limit (AL) which depends on $n(p), f^{PN}$ contains, generally $q$-dependent Final State Interactions (FSI) parts. In the inverse problem one wishes to separate $q$-dependent FSI from the AL. In general it suffices to know the structure of the theory, but not numerical results. It appears, that in the $q$-range of the analyzed electron scattering data, FSI are only weakly $q$-dependent, making it virtually impossible to obtain parameters in a free fit of the parametrized components of $f$. Imposing a restriction, we obtain $n(p)$ for Fe and $^4$Fe.

# Extraction of nucleon momentum distributions from inclusive electron scattering on nuclei

## Key claim

The authors attempt to extract single-nucleon momentum distributions from inclusive electron scattering data on Fe and 4He, but find that final state interactions are only weakly q-dependent in the analyzed kinematic range, making it virtually impossible to obtain parameters in a free fit of the parametrized components of the structure function. Only by imposing a restriction on the central value n(0) can a well-determined momentum distribution be obtained.

## Method

The authors relate nuclear structure functions to nucleon structure functions through an intermediate structure function fPN for a nucleus of point-particles, using a convolution formula. They parametrize the momentum distribution as a sum of two centered Gaussians and fit the reduced cross sections hA for Fe data at E=4.05 GeV and 4He data at E≤3.6 GeV, using a relativistic scaling variable yG. Because free fits failed, they imposed a fixed central value n(0) near the theoretical value to extract the remaining parameters.

## Key numbers

- For Fe, the extracted width of the dominant Gaussian p1 ranges from 0.797±0.041 fm⁻¹ to 0.837±0.011 fm⁻¹ depending on the fixed n(0) value (58.3, 69.8, or 76.8 fm⁻³)
- For Fe, the extracted root mean square momentum √⟨p²⟩ ranges from 1.013±0.021 fm⁻¹ to 1.108±0.035 fm⁻¹
- For 4He, the extracted p1 is 0.821 fm⁻¹ and the rms momentum is 1.005 fm⁻¹ with ε set to 0
- The even FSI part Δφeven reaches 35% of F0(yG) for |yG|≈0.2 GeV and up to 15% for |yG|≈0.4 GeV
- Fits for hFe show relative deviations from data rarely exceeding 5% and usually staying under 2%

## Context

This work extends the successful method previously applied to extract single-atom momentum distributions from neutron scattering on liquid 4He (Glyde 1994, Azuah et al. 1995) and Ne (Azuah et al. 1997) to the nuclear case. The authors build on the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith theory (1973) and their own prior work on relativistic structure functions (Rinat and Taragin 1996, 1997). The paper identifies a fundamental difficulty for nuclei: unlike atomic systems where final state interactions have a discernible q-dependence enabling clean separation, nuclear FSI from diffractive NN interactions are barely q-dependent, forcing restrained fits. This contrasts with the approach of Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1991, 1994) who extracted scaling functions assuming a clear Q²-signature for FSI. The extracted momentum distributions enable comparison with theoretical calculations such as those by Kohama, Yazaki and Seki (1992).
