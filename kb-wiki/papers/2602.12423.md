# Low-energy $^{3}$He($α,γ$)$^{7}$Be reaction within the Skyrme potential framework

> \textbf{Background:} The $^{3}$He($α,γ$)$^{7}$Be reaction plays a crucial role in the proton-proton chain and Big Bang nucleosynthesis, affecting solar neutrino fluxes and primordial element abundances. Experimental data at astrophysical energies remain uncertain due to the extremely low cross sections. \\ \textbf{Purpose:} This work uses a microscopic potential-model approach to construct the $^{3}$He+$α$ potential from the nucleon+$α$ interaction, aiming to describe low-energy elastic scattering and to calculate the astrophysical $S$ factor of the $^{3}$He($α,γ$)$^{7}$Be reaction. \\ \textbf{Method:} The nucleon-nucleus potential is derived from self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations extended to the continuum. The $^{3}$He+$α$ potential is then obtained by folding the HF potential with the $^{3}$He density. A small number of scaling parameters is constrained by elastic-scattering data.\\ \textbf{Result:} The scaled Skyrme HF potential and folded potential simultaneously reproduce the low-energy $p$+$α$ and $^{3}$He+$α$ $s$-wave phase shifts, respectively. The calculated astrophysical $S$ factor of $^{3}$He($α,γ$)$^{7}$Be shows good agreement with experimental data, yielding the recommended value $S_{34}(0) = 0.610 \pm 0.024$~keV~b. A moderate sensitivity of $S_{34}(0)$ to the choice of projectile density is also observed in the folding procedure. \\ \textbf{Conclusion:} The Skyrme HF-based potential provides a unified and predictive microscopic framework for describing both elastic scattering and radiative capture in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a microscopic potential derived from Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations, with only a small number of scaling parameters constrained by elastic-scattering data, can simultaneously describe low-energy \(p+\alpha\) and \(^{3}\text{He}+\alpha\) elastic scattering and the astrophysical \(S\) factor of the \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction; however, the extrapolated \(S_{34}(0)\) value shows moderate sensitivity to the choice of the \(^{3}\text{He}\) density distribution used in the folding procedure, and the recommended value of \(0.610 \pm 0.024\) keV b is obtained only with the Ngo density, not with the Schechter-Canto density.

## Method

The nucleon-nucleus potential is derived from self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations using the SLy4 interaction, extended to the continuum to describe scattering states. The \(^{3}\text{He}+\alpha\) potential is then obtained by single-folding the HF potential with a two-parameter Fermi density distribution for \(^{3}\text{He}\), using either the Schechter-Canto or Ngo parametrization. A scaling factor \(\lambda_0\) is applied to the central potential and \(\lambda_{\text{ls}}\) to the spin-orbit term, constrained by reproducing experimental \(p+\alpha\) and \(^{3}\text{He}+\alpha\) elastic-scattering phase shifts; the same folded potentials are then used to calculate the \(E1\) radiative-capture cross section and astrophysical \(S\) factor.

## Key numbers

- Recommended zero-energy astrophysical \(S\) factor: \(S_{34}(0) = 0.610 \pm 0.024\) keV b, obtained with the Ngo density; the SC density yields \(S_{34}(0) = 0.666 \pm 0.026\) keV b.
- Scaling factor for the central potential in \(^{3}\text{He}+\alpha\) \(s\)-wave scattering: \(\lambda_0 = 0.85 \pm 0.02\) (Ngo density) and \(\lambda_0 = 0.88\) (SC density).
- Spin-orbit scaling factor for \(^{3}\text{He}+\alpha\) scattering states: \(\lambda_{\text{ls}} = 0.14\) for both densities.
- Binding energies of \(^{7}\text{Be}\) cluster states: ground state (\(3/2^-\)) at \(-1.587\) MeV and first excited state (\(1/2^-\)) at \(-1.158\) MeV relative to the \(^{3}\text{He}+\alpha\) threshold.
- Asymptotic normalization coefficients for the Ngo density: \(C_{p_{3/2}} = 4.766\) fm\(^{-1/2}\) and \(C_{p_{1/2}} = 4.056\) fm\(^{-1/2}\).
- Slope parameter: \(S'_{34}(0)/S_{34}(0) = -0.754 \pm 0.051\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- Calculated \(S\) factor at the Gamow peak: \(S_{34}(23 \pm 6~\text{keV}) = 0.600 \pm 0.027\) keV b.
- Branching ratio \(R = \sigma_{429}/\sigma_0 = 0.43\) at very low energies for both densities.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme Hartree-Fock potential framework, previously applied to nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering and nucleon radiative capture by Anh et al. (2021, 2022, 2023), to describe for the first time a low-energy nucleus-nucleus system (\(^{3}\text{He}+\alpha\)). The results are consistent with state-of-the-art theoretical predictions including the no-core shell model with continuum by Dohet-Eraly et al. (2016), the fermionic molecular dynamics model by Neff (2011), and halo effective field theory by Zhang et al. (2020), and lie near the upper limit of the most recent evaluation from Solar Fusion III by Acharya et al. (2025). The approach enables a unified microscopic description of both elastic scattering and radiative capture in light nuclei with minimal empirical input, and the authors state it will be applied to the \(^{7}\text{Li}+\alpha\) channel and can be extended to heavy-ion systems of astrophysical relevance.
