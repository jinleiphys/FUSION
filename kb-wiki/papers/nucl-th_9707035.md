# Quantum Signature of the Chaos-Order Transition in a Homogeneous SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs System

> We analyze a spatially homogeneous SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system both in classical and quantum mechanics. By using the Toda criterion of the Gaussian curvature we find a classical chaos-order transition as a function of the Higgs vacuum, the Yang-Mills coupling constant and the energy of the system. Then, we study the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution of the energy levels, which shows a Wigner-Poisson transition by increasing the value of the Higgs field in the vacuum. This transition is a clear quantum signature of the classical chaos-order transition of the system.

# Quantum Signature of the Chaos-Order Transition in a Homogeneous SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs System

## Key claim

The classical chaos-order transition in a spatially homogeneous SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system, predicted analytically using the Toda criterion of Gaussian curvature, has a clear quantum signature in the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution of energy levels, which transitions from Wigner (chaotic) to Poisson (integrable) as the Higgs vacuum expectation value increases. However, the curvature criterion guarantees only local instability and should be combined with Poincare sections for full confirmation.

## Method

The author analyzed a spatially homogeneous SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system reduced to two degrees of freedom with Hamiltonian H = (1/2)(p1^2 + p2^2) + g^2 v^2 (q1^2 + q2^2) + (1/2) g^2 q1^2 q2^2, where v is the Higgs vacuum expectation value. The classical chaos-order transition was studied analytically using the Toda criterion of Gaussian curvature of the potential energy surface, and the quantum energy levels were computed by numerical diagonalization of the truncated Hamiltonian matrix (1156 x 1156) in the basis of two harmonic oscillators, using the first 100 converged energy levels to compute the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution P(s) and fit it to the Brody distribution.

## Key numbers

* Critical energy for chaos-order transition: E_c = 6 g^2 v^4, derived analytically from the zero-Gaussian-curvature condition on the potential energy surface.
* For E = 10 and g = 1, the critical Higgs vacuum value for onset of chaos is v_c = (E / 6 g^2)^(1/4) approximately 1.14, confirmed by numerical Poincare sections.
* Brody parameter omega values for different Higgs vacuum v (with g=1, E=10): v=1.00 gives omega=0.99 (near-Wigner); v=1.05 gives omega=0.47; v=1.10 gives omega=0.34; v=1.15 gives omega=0.12; v=1.20 gives omega=0.01 (near-Poisson).
* The first 100 energy levels were converged to 8 digits using a matrix dimension of 1156 x 1156.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of classical chaos in spatially uniform Yang-Mills and scalar electrodynamics systems, which had been studied numerically with Lyapunov exponents and Poincare sections by Savvidy (1984), Gorski (1984), and others. The author applies the Toda curvature criterion (Toda, 1974) as an analytical method for the chaos-order transition, and connects it to quantum spectral statistics following the framework of Seligman, Verbaarschot and Zirnbauer (1984) for two-dimensional anharmonic oscillators. The results enable quantitative study of the quantum signature of classical chaos in field-theoretic systems and connect to random matrix theory approaches used by Halasz and Verbaarschot (1995) for QCD lattice spectra.
