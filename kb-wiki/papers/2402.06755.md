# Radiative neutron capture rate of $^{11}$B$(n,γ)^{12}$B reaction from the Coulomb dissociation of $^{12}$B

> We calculate the $^{11}$B$(n,γ)^{12}$B reaction rate, an important constituent in nucleosynthesis networks, contributed by resonant as well as non-resonant capture. For the resonant rate, we use the narrow resonance approximation whereas the non-resonant contribution is calculated with the Coulomb dissociation method for which we use finite-range distorted wave Born approximation theory. We then compare our calculated rate of $^{11}$B$(n,γ)^{12}$B reaction with those reported earlier and with other charged particle reactions on $^{11}$B.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the total reaction rate for $^{11}$B$(n,\gamma)^{12}$B by combining a resonant contribution (using the narrow resonance approximation for low-energy narrow resonances) with a non-resonant contribution derived from the Coulomb dissociation of $^{12}$B on a heavy target, using finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory. The abstract does not specify that the method assumes the non-resonant continuum is dominated by a single electromagnetic multipolarity and that the resonant contribution is added separately via the Breit-Wigner formula.

## Method

The non-resonant capture cross-section was obtained by first calculating the Coulomb breakup of $^{12}$B on a $^{208}$Pb target at a beam energy of 60 MeV/nucleon using the post-form finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory. The relative energy spectrum from the breakup was related to the photodisintegration cross-section via virtual photon numbers, and then the radiative capture cross-section was obtained through the principle of detailed balance. The resonant contribution to the reaction rate was computed separately using the Breit-Wigner formula for narrow resonances in the $^{11}$B$-n$ system, and the total rate was obtained by summing resonant and non-resonant parts.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy of $^{12}$B: $S_n = 3.3696(13)$ MeV.
* Ground state spin-parity of $^{12}$B: $J^\pi = 1^+$, obtained by coupling the $^{11}$B core ($3/2^-$) with a $1p_{1/2}$ neutron.
* Woods-Saxon potential parameters for the ground state wave function: radius = 1.35 fm, diffuseness = 0.65 fm.
* Coulomb dissociation calculation performed at a beam energy of 60 MeV/nucleon on a $^{208}$Pb target.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to determine the $^{11}$B$(n,\gamma)^{12}$B reaction rate, which is a key reaction in nucleosynthesis networks leading to carbon isotopes (e.g., Dubo (2020), Lee (2010), Liu (2001), Lin (2003)). The rate is important for evaluating the primitive CNO mass fraction as noted by Coc (2012). The authors compare their calculated rate with those from earlier studies and with charged-particle capture rates on $^{11}$B (proton and alpha capture) from the literature, which is relevant for understanding nucleosynthesis in homogeneous and inhomogeneous Big-Bang nucleosynthesis models (Wang (1991), Rauscher (1994)) and for potential proton-Boron fusion reactor applications (Chirkov (2023), HB11 (2023)).
