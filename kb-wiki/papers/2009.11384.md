# Contact interaction analysis of pion GTMDs

> A contact interaction is used to calculate an array of pion twist-two, -three and -four generalised transverse light-front momentum dependent parton distribution functions (GTMDs). Despite the interaction's simplicity, many of the results are physically relevant, amongst them a statement that GTMD size and shape are largely prescribed by the scale of emergent hadronic mass. Moreover, proceeding from GTMDs to generalised parton distributions (GPDs), it is found that the pion's mass distribution form factor is harder than its electromagnetic form factor, which is harder than the gravitational pressure distribution form factor; the pressure in the neighbourhood of the pion's core is commensurate with that at the centre of a neutron star; the shear pressure is maximal when confinement forces become dominant within the pion; and the spatial distribution of transversely polarised quarks within the pion is asymmetric. Regarding transverse momentum dependent distribution functions (TMDs), their magnitude and domain of support decrease with increasing twist. The simplest Wigner distribution associated with the pion's twist-two dressed-quark GTMD is sharply peaked on the kinematic domain associated with valence-quark dominance; has a domain of negative support; and broadens as the transverse position variable increases in magnitude.

## Key claim

Using a simple contact interaction (CI) model, the authors calculate a full array of pion twist-two, -three, and -four generalised transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (GTMDs), finding that despite the interaction's simplicity, the results are physically relevant because the GTMD size and shape are largely prescribed by the scale of emergent hadronic mass (EHM), though the CI's momentum-independent nature means the results should be interpreted judiciously as a qualitative guide rather than a precise QCD prediction.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector×vector contact interaction (CI) as the foundation for their analysis, using the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) with a momentum-independent kernel. The pion's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is obtained from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the generalised parton correlation function (GPCF) formalism of Meissner et al. (2008) is used to compute the GTMDs via appropriate Dirac-matrix projections. The CI is regularised with an ultraviolet scale Λ_uv and an infrared scale Λ_ir = 0.24 GeV to provide a rudimentary expression of confinement.

## Key numbers

- Dressed-quark mass M is determined from the gap equation: M = m + M (4α₀)/(3π m_G²) C₀(M²), with α₀/π = 0.36 and m_G = 0.5 GeV.
- Infrared regularisation scale: Λ_ir = 0.24 GeV.
- The twist-two GTMD E₂ is given by E₂(x, k_⊥², ξ, t) = P̅_T [E_π² F^EE + E_π F_π F^EF + F_π² F^FF], where P̅_T = [θ_{ξ̄ξ} + P_T(-t)(1-θ_{ξ̄ξ})].
- The twist-two GTMD Ẽ₂ is identically zero: Ẽ₂(x, k_⊥², ξ, t) = 0.
- The twist-two GTMD H₂ involves terms with T_71^EF and T_72^EE, where T_72^EE = (3 N_c)/(4π³) (4m_π² - t) ∫₀¹ dα θ_{αξ}/[σ₃ʳ]² C̄₃(σ₃ʳ).
- The twist-two GTMD H̃₂ has no E_π² term: H̃₂ = P̅_T [E_π F_π F^EF + F_π² F^FF].
- The twist-three GTMD G₂^k includes a term with (4m_π² - t) in F^FF: F^FF = 4 T_51^EE + (N_c)/(4π³) (1/σ₂ʳ) C̄₂(σ₂ʳ) θ_{ξ̄ξ}(4m_π² - t)/M².
- The twist-four GTMD F₃ involves terms with (4m_π² - t) and t in F^EE: F^EE = T̃_11^EE + T̃_12^EE + (N_c)/(16π³) (1/σ₂ʳ) C̄₂(σ₂ʳ) θ_{ξ̄ξ} x(4m_π² - t)/(M² ξ) + (N_c)/(8π³) (1/σ₂ʳ) C̄₂(σ₂ʳ) θ_{ξ̄ξ} x t/(M² ξ³).

## Context

This work extends the contact interaction (CI) approach previously applied to pion parton distribution functions and form factors by Gutierrez-Guerrero et al. (2010), Roberts et al. (2011), and Chen et al. (2012), now providing the first complete CI calculation of all twist-two, -three, and -four pion GTMDs. The results enable the unified derivation of generalised parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum dependent distribution functions (TMDs) from the same GPCF framework, allowing future comparisons with more sophisticated DSE-based calculations and with experimental data from facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and Jefferson Laboratory (JLab) at 12 GeV.
