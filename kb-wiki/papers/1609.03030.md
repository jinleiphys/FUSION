# Excited State Mass spectra of doubly heavy baryons $Ω_{cc}$, $Ω_{bb}$ and $Ω_{bc}$

> We discuss the mass spectrum of $Ω$ baryon with two heavy quarks and one light quark (\textit{ccs, bbs and bcs}). The main goal of the paper is to calculate the ground state masses and after that, the positive and negative parity excited states masses are also obtained within a Hypercentral Constituent quark model, using coulomb plus linear potential framework. We also added first order correction to the potential. The mass spectra upto 5S for radial excited states and 1P-5P, 1D-4D and 1F-2F states for orbital excited states are computed for $Ω_{cc}$, $Ω_{bb}$ and $Ω_{bc}$ baryons. Our obtained results are compared with other theoretical predictions which could be a useful complementary tool for the interpretation of experimentaly unknown heavy baryon spectra. The Regge trajectory is constructed in both ($n_r$, $M^{2}$) and ($J$, $M^{2}$) planes for $Ω_{cc}$,$Ω_{bb}$ and $Ω_{bc}$ baryons and their slopes and intercepts are also determined. Magnetic moments of doubly heavy $Ω'$s are also calculated.

## Key claim

The paper calculates ground and excited state masses of the doubly heavy Omega baryons (Omega_cc, Omega_bb, Omega_bc) using a hypercentral constituent quark model with a Coulomb-plus-linear potential plus a first-order correction, but the abstract does not highlight that the results for Omega_bc orbital excited states are claimed as the first such calculation because other approaches consider the bc diquark excited states unstable.

## Method

The authors solve the six-dimensional hyperradial Schrodinger equation numerically within the hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM), using Jacobi coordinates and a hypercentral potential V(x) that includes a color Coulomb term (tau/x), a linear confining term (beta x), a first-order correction term proportional to -C_F C_A alpha_s^2 / (4 x^2), and a spin-dependent part V_SD(x) containing spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms. Quark masses are fixed at m_s = 0.500 GeV, m_c = 1.275 GeV, m_b = 4.67 GeV, with alpha_s = 0.4155 GeV. The hyperradial equation is solved numerically using Mathematica, and masses are computed for radial excitations up to 5S and orbital excitations up to 5P, 4D, and 2F for all three baryon systems.

## Key numbers

* Ground state mass of Omega_cc with J^P = 1/2^+: 3.650 GeV (without first-order correction).
* Ground state mass of Omega_cc with J^P = 3/2^+: 3.810 GeV (without first-order correction).
* Ground state mass of Omega_bb with J^P = 1/2^+: 10.446 GeV (without first-order correction).
* Ground state mass of Omega_bb with J^P = 3/2^+: 10.467 GeV (without first-order correction).
* Ground state mass of Omega_bc with J^P = 1/2^+: 7.136 GeV (without first-order correction).
* Ground state mass of Omega_bc with J^P = 3/2^+: 7.187 GeV (without first-order correction).
* 2S radial excited state mass of Omega_cc (J^P = 1/2^+): 4.028 GeV (without correction), 4.041 GeV (with first-order correction).
* 2S radial excited state mass of Omega_bb (J^P = 1/2^+): 10.730 GeV (without correction), 10.736 GeV (with first-order correction).
* 2S radial excited state mass of Omega_bc (J^P = 1/2^+): 7.473 GeV (without correction), 7.480 GeV (with first-order correction).
* Magnetic moment of Omega_cc^+ (J^P = 1/2^+): 0.692 nuclear magnetons.
* Magnetic moment of Omega_bb^- (J^P = 1/2^+): 0.108 nuclear magnetons.
* Magnetic moment of Omega_bc^0 (J^P = 1/2^+): 0.439 nuclear magnetons.

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral constituent quark model previously applied to singly charm baryons by Shah et al. (2016) and to doubly heavy baryons via a variational approach by Bhavin et al. It provides mass predictions for states not yet experimentally observed, with comparisons to results from the relativistic three-quark model of Ebert et al., the Salpeter model of Giannuzzi, QCD sum rules of Wang et al. and Aliev et al., lattice QCD calculations by Padmanath et al., Brown et al., and others. The computed Regge trajectories and magnetic moments are intended to guide future experimental searches at LHCb, PANDA, and other facilities, and the authors state they are the first to calculate orbital excited states for Omega_bc because other approaches consider the bc diquark excited states unstable due to soft gluon emission.
