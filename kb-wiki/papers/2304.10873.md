# Moments of inertia in light deformed nuclei: pairing and mean-field impacts

> The dependence of the moment of inertia $\cal J$ on the pairing and axial quadrupole deformation $帣$ in $^{24}$Mg and $^{20}$Ne was investigated. The study is based on quadrupole-constrained calculations with three cranking approaches for $\cal J$ (Inglis-Belyaev, Thouless-Valatin, adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock) and a representative set of Skyrme forces (SVbas, SkM*, SLy6). At variance with macroscopic collective models, the calculations predict the specific regime $d{\cal J}/d帣<0$ at $帣\ge 0.5$ ($^{24}$Mg) and $帣\ge 0.6$ ($^{20}$Ne), where the pairing breaks down. This regime is explained by two effects: full break up of the pairing and specific evolution of a {\it single} dominant particle-hole (1ph) configuration with $帣$. The analysis of experimental data for the ground-state rotational bands in $^{24}$Mg and $^{20}$Ne shows that such regime is possible at low spins.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in light deformed nuclei \(^{24}\)Mg and \(^{20}\)Ne, the moment of inertia can decrease with increasing axial quadrupole deformation (regime \(d{\cal J}/d\beta < 0\)) at deformations \(\beta \ge 0.5\) and \(\beta \ge 0.6\) respectively, contrary to the usual growth predicted by macroscopic collective models; this regime occurs only after pairing has fully collapsed and is driven by the evolution of a single dominant particle-hole configuration.

## Method

The authors performed quadrupole-constrained calculations using three cranking approaches for the moment of inertia: Inglis-Belyaev (IB), Thouless-Valatin (TV), and adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF), all based on Skyrme mean-field calculations with three parametrizations (SVbas, SkM*, SLy6). Pairing was treated within the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) scheme, and the calculations used a two-dimensional grid in cylindrical coordinates with all single-particle levels up to +40 MeV and all two-quasiparticle \(K^\pi = 1^+\) states up to 60 MeV. Experimental moments of inertia were extracted from ground-state rotational band energies using the formula \({\cal J}(I) = \hbar^2 (2I-1) / \Delta E_I\).

## Key numbers

- In \(^{24}\)Mg, the ATDHF calculation with SkM* shows a maximum in \({\cal J}(\beta)\) at \(\beta \sim 0.4\) followed by a decrease; the experimental moment of inertia at \(I=2\) is 2.19 \(\hbar^2\)/MeV and at \(I=6\) is 2.78 \(\hbar^2\)/MeV.
- In \(^{20}\)Ne, the ATDHF calculation with SkM* shows a maximum in \({\cal J}(\beta)\) at \(\beta \sim 0.6\) followed by a decrease; the experimental moment of inertia at \(I=2\) is 1.84 \(\hbar^2\)/MeV and at \(I=6\) is 2.43 \(\hbar^2\)/MeV.
- The experimental squared intrinsic quadrupole moment \(Q_0^2\) in \(^{24}\)Mg decreases from 0.44 \(e^2 b^2\) at \(I=2\) to 0.22 \(e^2 b^2\) at \(I=6\); in \(^{20}\)Ne it decreases from 0.33 \(e^2 b^2\) at \(I=2\) to 0.093 \(e^2 b^2\) at \(I=6\).
- The calculated equilibrium deformation \(\beta_{\mathrm{eq}}\) for \(^{24}\)Mg is 0.49 with SkM* (experimental 0.61); for \(^{20}\)Ne it is 0.37 with SkM* (experimental 0.72).
- The pairing gaps for \(^{24}\)Mg with SkM* are \(\Delta_p = 0.01\) MeV and \(\Delta_n = 0.01\) MeV; for \(^{20}\)Ne with SkM* they are \(\Delta_p = 1.15\) MeV and \(\Delta_n = 1.07\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Hinohara et al. (2011) who observed a \({\cal J}(\beta)\)-maximum in \(^{24}\)Mg using the CHFB+LQRPA method, attributing it to pairing collapse, but did not explore the mean-field origin. The present study provides a microscopic interpretation in terms of single-particle spectra, showing that a single dominant two-quasiparticle configuration (proton and neutron \([211\uparrow, 202\uparrow]\) in \(^{24}\)Mg and \([211\uparrow, 220\uparrow]\) in \(^{20}\)Ne) drives the anomalous decrease. The results enable experimental searches for the \(d{\cal J}/d\beta < 0\) regime at low spins by simultaneously measuring intraband energy intervals and \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities in light deformed nuclei.
