# Pair correlation of giant halo nuclei in continuum Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

> The giant halos predicted in neutron-rich Zr isotopes with $A=124-138$ are investigated by using the self-consistent continuum Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach, in which the asymptotic behavior of continuum quasiparticle states is properly treated by the Green's function method. We study in detail the neutron pair correlation involved in the giant halo by analyzing the asymptotic exponential tail of the neutron pair condensate (pair density) in addition to that of the neutron particle density. The neutron quasiparticle spectra associated with these giant halo nuclei are examined. It is found that the asymptotic exponential tail of the neutron pair condensate is dominated by non-resonant continuum quasiparticle states corresponding to the scattering states with low asymptotic kinetic energy. This is in contrast to the asymptotic tail of the neutron density, whose main contributions arise from the resonant quasiparticle states corresponding to the weakly-bound single-particle orbits and resonance orbits in the Hartree-Fock potential.

## Key claim

The asymptotic exponential tail of the neutron pair condensate (pair density) in giant halo nuclei is dominated by non-resonant continuum quasiparticle states (scattering states with low asymptotic kinetic energy), in contrast to the neutron density tail which arises mainly from resonant quasiparticle states corresponding to weakly-bound single-particle orbits and resonance orbits in the Hartree-Fock potential.

## Method

The authors applied the self-consistent continuum Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with the Green's function method to properly treat the asymptotic behavior of continuum quasiparticle states in neutron-rich Zr isotopes with A=124-138. They employed the SkI4 Skyrme functional and a density-dependent delta interaction for the pairing field, with parameters reproducing the experimental neutron pairing gap along the Sn isotopic chain. The neutron pair density and particle density were analyzed through their asymptotic exponential tails, and the quasiparticle spectra were examined using the Green's function formalism to distinguish resonant and non-resonant continuum contributions.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron separation energy S2n drops from 4.81 MeV at 122Zr to 0.64 MeV at 124Zr, then gradually decreases to 0.04 MeV at 138Zr, which is the neutron drip-line nucleus in this model.
* The average pairing gap Delta_uv is approximately 0.59-0.68 MeV and Delta_vv is approximately 0.53-0.61 MeV for isotopes A=126-138, while at A=124 the values are approximately 0.42-0.47 MeV.
* The neutron r.m.s. radius shows a steep increase with neutron number for A>=124 compared to the extrapolation r_r.m.s. approximately 0.87 N^(1/3) fm for A<=122.
* The pair r.m.s. radius is significantly larger than the neutron density r.m.s. radius and exhibits a sudden jump at 124Zr, remaining almost constant until the drip-line.

## Context

This work extends the study of giant neutron halos first predicted by the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory for neutron-rich Zr and Ca isotopes (Meng 1998, Zhang 2003), and later reproduced by non-relativistic Skyrme HFB models (Grasso 2005, Terasaki 2006). The present paper goes beyond previous investigations by focusing on the pair density rather than just the particle density, using the continuum HFB approach with Green's function method (Zhang 2011, Oba 2011) to properly treat continuum quasiparticle states. This enables the identification of non-resonant continuum states as the dominant contributors to the pair density tail, a mechanism not accessible in standard box-discretized HFB calculations.
