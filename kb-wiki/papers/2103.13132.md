# Influence of the treatment of initialization and mean-field potential on the neutron to proton yield ratios

> In this work, we firstly investigate how to reproduce and how well one can reproduce the Woods-Saxon density distribution of initial nuclei in the framework of the improved quantum molecular dynamics model. Then, we propose a new treatment for the initialization of nuclei which is correlated with the nucleonic mean-field potential by using the same potential energy density functional. In the mean field potential, the three-body force term is accurately calculated. Based on the new version of the model, the influences of precise calculations of the three-body force term, the slope of symmetry energy, the neutron-proton effective mass splitting, and the width of the wave packet on heavy ion collision observables, such as the neutron to proton yield ratios for emitted free nucleons [$R(n/p)$] and for coalescence invariant nucleons [$R_{ci}(n/p)$] for $^{124}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn at the beam energy of 200 MeV per nucleon, are discussed. Our calculations show that the spectra of neutron to proton yield ratios [$R(n/p)$] can be used to probe the slope of symmetry energy ($L$) and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting. In detail, the $R(n/p)$ in the low kinetic energy region can be used to probe the slope of symmetry energy ($L$). With a given $L$, the inclination of $R(n/p)$ to kinetic energy ($E_k$) can be used to probe the effective mass splitting. In the case where the neutron-proton effective mass splitting is fixed, $R(n/p)$ at high kinetic energy can also be used to learn the symmetry energy at suprasaturation density.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the neutron to proton yield ratio [$R(n/p)$] in heavy ion collisions can simultaneously probe both the slope of the symmetry energy ($L$) and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting, but only if the initialization of nuclei and the mean-field potential are treated consistently using the same energy density functional and if the three-body force term is calculated precisely rather than approximated.

## Method

The authors modified the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model by implementing a new initialization procedure where neutron and proton centroids are sampled within half-density radii ($R_n$, $R_p$) obtained from a restricted density variational (RDV) method using the same Skyrme energy density functional as in the mean-field propagation. The three-body force term in the mean-field potential was calculated exactly using an 11-point Gauss-Legendre quadrature method (ImQMD-L version) instead of the traditional approximation. The model was then applied to simulate $^{124}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn collisions at 200 MeV per nucleon with impact parameter b=2 fm, varying the slope of symmetry energy $L$ from 30 to 110 MeV while keeping other nuclear matter parameters fixed ($K_0=240$ MeV, $S_0=30$ MeV, $m_v^*/m=0.7$, $m_s^*/m=0.8$).

## Key numbers

- The wave packet width $\sigma_r=1.287$ fm was selected as a compromise between reproducing the initial density distribution and achieving nuclear stability, giving a probability of stability $P_{stab}\approx 87\%$ for $^{124}$Sn at 200 fm/$c$
- The binding energy deviation for $^{124}$Sn using $\sigma_r=1.287$ fm (corresponding to diffuseness $a=0.743$ fm) is about 0.65 MeV per nucleon (8.2% deviation) from the ground state energy obtained with RDV
- For $^{124}$Sn, the proton half-density radius $R_p$ ranges from 5.656 fm (for $L=110$ MeV) to 5.733 fm (for $L=30$ MeV), while the neutron half-density radius $R_n$ ranges from 5.777 fm ($L=30$ MeV) to 5.909 fm ($L=110$ MeV)
- The $R(n/p)$ values for emitted free nucleons with kinetic energy $E_k<80$ MeV follow the ordering $R(n/p)_{L=30}>R(n/p)_{L=70}>R(n/p)_{L=110}$, with the soft symmetry energy case ($L=30$ MeV) giving the largest ratio

## Context

This work extends previous quantum molecular dynamics approaches (UrQMD, PHQMD, TuQMD) that struggled to reproduce Woods-Saxon density distributions in initial nuclei, as discussed in the transport model comparison project by Xu et al. (2016). The new consistent treatment of neutron skin and mean-field potential addresses the separate handling of the slope parameter $L$ and neutron skin thickness $\Delta r_{np}$ in earlier calculations by Hartnack et al. (2018) and Wei et al. (2014). The results enable more reliable constraints on the symmetry energy at suprasaturation density using $R(n/p)$ at kinetic energies above 100 MeV, and on the neutron-proton effective mass splitting through the kinetic energy dependence of $R(n/p)$.
