# Deformations and clustering correlations in $p$- and $sd$-shell nuclei using the Gogny and Skyrme interactions

> Structures of $p$- and $sd$-shell nuclei are studied with the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics method using the Gogny D1S and Skyrme SLy7 forces as effective interactions. By the energy variation with a constraint, energy curves as functions of quadrupole deformation parameter $β$ are obtained. The energy curves for $sd$-shell nuclei show structure change as a function of $β$, and suggest shape coexistence. Nuclear structures in the deformed region are discussed focusing on deformations and clustering. It is found that the deformations often involve cluster structures. Effective-interaction dependence is also discussed comparing the results obtained with the two effective interactions. Although the two forces give similar results when cluster structures do not develop clearly, they give different energies in the largely deformed region when an $α$ cluster develops.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in deformed states of $p$- and $sd$-shell nuclei, deformations often involve cluster structures, but the degree of clustering and its energy impact depend on the effective interaction used: the Gogny D1S and Skyrme SLy7 forces give similar results for mean-field-type structures without clustering, yet they yield different energies in the largely deformed region where an $\alpha$ cluster develops.

## Method

The authors used the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method, where the wave function is a Slater determinant of triaxially deformed Gaussian wave packets, and performed energy variation with a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$ to obtain energy curves for positive-parity states (and negative-parity states for $^{20}$Ne) of $p$- and $sd$-shell $N=Z=\text{even}$ nuclei plus $^{24}$O. They employed the Gogny D1S and Skyrme SLy7 effective interactions, and analyzed clustering correlations by calculating overlaps with Margenau-Brink-type cluster wave functions and comparing the energies of ideal cluster configurations.

## Key numbers

* $^8$Be: ground state minimum at $\beta \sim 0.7$ with $2\alpha$ cluster structure; distance between two $\alpha$ clusters approximately 2 fm for the D1S force; binding energy of the ground state for D1S is approximately 2 MeV larger than for SLy7.
* $^{12}$C: D1S gives oblate ground state minimum at $\beta = 0.28$; SLy7 gives spherical minimum; energy difference between $\beta \sim 0$ and 0.5 is only 3 MeV for SLy7.
* $^{20}$Ne (positive-parity): prolate minima at $\beta \sim 0.4$ for both forces; binding energy for D1S is larger than for SLy7; compared to HFB using D1S, binding energy of ground state is approximately 3 MeV larger.
* $^{20}$Ne (negative-parity): $\alpha$-$^{16}$O cluster structure energy for D1S is approximately 2 MeV lower than for SLy7.
* $^{24}$Mg: mean-field-type and $\alpha$-$^{20}$Ne cluster structure minima at $\beta \sim 0.4$ and 0.8, respectively; excitation energies of $\alpha$-$^{20}$Ne local minima are 19.9 MeV (D1S) and 21.5 MeV (SLy7); $\alpha$-$^{20}$Ne cluster energies for D1S are approximately 2 MeV lower than for SLy7.
* $^{28}$Si: oblate ground state at $\beta \sim 0.3$; prolate ND and SD local minima at $\beta \sim 0.4$ and 0.8; excitation energies of ND and SD minima are 9.5 and 16.1 MeV relative to ground state for D1S.
* $^{32}$S: prolate ground state at $\beta \sim 0.2$; SD local minima at $\beta \sim 0.7$; excitation energies of SD minima are 12.7 MeV (D1S) and 14.6 MeV (SLy7); compared to HFB using D1S, binding energies at ground state and SD minimum are 4 MeV and 1 MeV larger, respectively.
* $^{36}$Ar: oblate ground state at $\beta \sim 0.2$; prolate SD local minima at $\beta \sim 0.5$; excitation energies of SD minima are 9.2 MeV (D1S) and 10.0 MeV (SLy7).
* $^{24}$O: spherical ground state; SD local minima at $\beta \sim 0.7$; excitation energies of SD minima are 20.0 MeV (D1S) and 18.1 MeV (SLy7).
* Infinite nuclear matter properties: D1S gives saturation density $\rho_{0,\text{eq}} = 0.160$ fm$^{-3}$, incompressibility $K_\infty = 209$ MeV, effective mass $m^*_0/m = 0.67$; SLy7 gives $\rho_{0,\text{eq}} = 0.158$ fm$^{-3}$, $K_\infty = 230$ MeV, $m^*_0/m = 0.69$.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of deformations in $sd$- and $pf$-shell nuclei using Hartree-Fock with Skyrme forces by Inakura et al. (2002), and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (or BCS) plus generator coordinate method calculations by Rodriguez-Guzman et al. (2000, 2002, 2003, 2004) and Egido et al. (2004). It also builds on previous deformed-basis AMD studies of $sd$-shell nuclei by Kimura et al. (2004a, 2004b), Kanada-En'yo et al. (2005), and Taniguchi et al. (2004, 2007). The results enable systematic investigation of how clustering correlations affect deformed states across the $p$- and $sd$-shell regions, and highlight the need for beyond-mean-field approaches (such as angular momentum projection and superposition of $\beta$ values) to describe soft energy curves and shape coexistence, particularly in nuclei like $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si.
