# Final state interaction in inclusive and exclusive quasi-elastic processes

> We discuss a new approach to final state interactions, that keeps explicitly into account the virtuality of the ejected nucleon in quasi-elastic $A(e,e'p)X$ scattering at very large $Q^2$, and we present some recent results, at moderately large $Q^2$ values, for the nuclear transparency in $^4He$, $^{16}O$ and $^{40}Ca$ and for the momentum distributions of $^4He$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new approach to final state interactions (FSI) in quasi-elastic electron scattering that explicitly accounts for the virtuality of the ejected nucleon, which at very large momentum transfers introduces a finite formation time that can be mapped onto a two-channel Glauber model; the key hedge is that the formalism is derived under the assumption of a factorized form for the off-shell nucleon-nucleon amplitude and a specific spectral function for the virtuality dependence.

## Method

The authors incorporate the virtuality of the ejected nucleon into the Feynman diagram description of quasi-elastic A(e,e'p)X scattering by replacing the standard theta function in the nucleon propagator with a J(z) function that depends on a formation length l(Q^2) = Q^2/(xmM^2). They show that at the single-rescattering level this formalism coincides with a two-channel Glauber model where the propagating nucleon and its excited state of mass squared m*^2 = m^2 + M^2 are the two channels, with the transparency condition f_{11}γ_1 + f_{12}γ_2 = 0 and f_{21}γ_1 + f_{22}γ_2 = 0. For complex nuclei at moderately large Q^2, they apply a number-conserving linked cluster expansion for the distorted one-body mixed density matrix using Glauber multiple scattering theory with correlated nuclear wave functions containing central and non-central correlations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency for 16O with realistic correlations: T = 0.52 (compared to T_SM = 0.51 for the shell model uncorrelated case)
- Nuclear transparency for 40Ca with realistic correlations: T = 0.42 (compared to T_SM = 0.41 for the shell model uncorrelated case)
- The effect of NN correlations on nuclear transparency amounts to approximately 3% for both 16O and 40Ca
- Double rescattering terms amount to 14% of the leading order single rescattering contribution
- For the exclusive process 3He(e,e'p)3H, at Q^2 approximately 20 GeV^2 the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation result is almost completely recovered when finite formation time effects are included
- Excited nucleon masses considered for the deuteron calculations: m* = 1.4 GeV, 1.8 GeV, and 2.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Gribov (1969) approach to final state interactions by incorporating virtuality effects through a finite formation time formalism, and builds upon the two-channel Glauber model for color transparency conditions discussed by Frankfurt, Greenberg, Miller, and Strikman (1992). The calculations for complex nuclei use realistic wave functions from Morita, Akaishi, Endo, and Tanaka (1987) for 4He and a linked cluster expansion developed by Ciofi degli Atti and Treleani (1999). The formalism enables predictions for both inclusive structure functions and exclusive processes at high Q^2, where standard Glauber approaches become inadequate due to finite formation time effects.
