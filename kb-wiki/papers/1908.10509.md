# Comprehensive study of mass modifications of light mesons in nuclear matter in the three-flavor extended Linear Sigma Model

> We present a comprehensive study of mass modifications of scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons in nuclear matter using the three-flavor extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) and the two-flavor Parity Doublet Model (PDM). The meson masses in nuclear matter are determined by calculating the one-loop nucleon corrections to the meson mean fields. As a result, we find all spin-$0$ meson masses except those of the pion, kaon, and the lightest scalar-isoscalar mesons decrease at finite baryon density. For spin-$1$ mesons, masses of all axial-vector mesons decrease in medium, and the density dependences of the $ρ$ and $ω$ meson masses strongly depend on the value of chiral invariant mass ($M_0$). Also, our results suggest $M_0\approx0.8\, {\rm GeV}$ is preferable.

## Key claim

The paper claims that all spin-0 meson masses except the pion, kaon, and lightest scalar-isoscalar mesons decrease at finite baryon density, and that the density dependence of the ρ and ω meson masses strongly depends on the value of the chiral invariant mass M₀, with M₀ ≈ 0.8 GeV being preferable, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of the free parameters M₀ and k₁ (the direct U(1)A anomaly effect to nucleons), and the allowed range of M₀ is 0.6 GeV ≲ M₀ ≲ 0.8 GeV.

## Method

The authors combined the three-flavor extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) for mesons with the two-flavor Parity Doublet Model (PDM) for nucleons, constructing nuclear matter at the one-loop nucleon level. Meson masses in nuclear matter were determined by calculating one-loop nucleon corrections to the meson mean fields, solving gap equations for the σN, σS, and ωN mean fields, and fitting remaining parameters to reproduce vacuum nucleon properties (masses, axial charges, N*(1535)→Nη decay width) and nuclear matter properties (saturation density ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy per nucleon −16 MeV, incompressibility K=0.24 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: (E/N)|ρ₀ − m̂₊ = −16 MeV
- Incompressibility: K = 0.24 GeV
- Chiral invariant mass preferred value: M₀ ≈ 0.8 GeV (allowed range 0.6 GeV ≲ M₀ ≲ 0.8 GeV)
- Reduction of pion decay constant at ρ₀: (fπ)med/(fπ)vac ≈ 85% for M₀=0.8 GeV, k₁=0
- Reduction of kaon decay constant at ρ₀: (fK)med/(fK)vac ≈ 87% for M₀=0.8 GeV, k₁=0
- Nucleon mass (N(939)): m̂₊ = 0.939 GeV
- N*(1535) mass: m̂₋ = 1.535 GeV
- N*(1535)→Nη decay width: Γ̂ = 0.065 GeV
- Chemical potential at saturation: μB|ρ₀ = 0.923 GeV

## Context

This work extends the three-flavor eLSM established by Parganlija et al. (2012) and the two-flavor PDM originally developed by Detar and Kunihiro (1988) and Jido et al. (2001) to a comprehensive study of meson mass modifications in nuclear matter. It builds on previous nuclear matter studies using the eLSM by Gallas et al. (2011) and the PDM by Motohiro et al. (2015) and Suenaga et al. (2017). The results provide predictions for ongoing and planned experiments on meson mass modifications in nuclei, including the E16 experiment at J-PARC, η′ mesic nuclei experiments at GSI and SPring-8, and the pp→ppη′ reaction experiment at COSY.
