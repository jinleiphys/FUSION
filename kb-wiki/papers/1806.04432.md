# Renormalization group consistency and low-energy effective theories

> Low-energy effective theories have been used very successfully to study the low-energy limit of QCD, providing us with results for a plethora of phenomena, ranging from bound-state formation to phase transitions in QCD. These theories are consistent quantum field theories by themselves and can be embedded in QCD, but typically have a physical ultraviolet cutoff that restricts their range of validity. Here, we provide a discussion of the concept of renormalization group consistency, aiming at an analysis of cutoff effects and regularization-scheme dependences in general studies of low-energy effective theories. For illustration, our findings are applied to low-energy effective models of QCD in different approximations including the mean-field approximation. More specifically, we consider hot and dense as well as finite systems and demonstrate that violations of renormalization group consistency affect significantly the predictive power of the corresponding model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low-energy effective theories (LEFTs) of QCD require "renormalization group (RG) consistency", the condition that the bare action's cutoff dependence cancels that of loop diagrams, and that violations of this condition, which occur when external parameters (temperature, density, volume) are not asymptotically small compared to the UV cutoff, significantly reduce predictive power; the authors demonstrate this by showing that cutoff artefacts can be removed via "pre-initial flows" that provide an RG-consistent UV completion of the LEFT.

## Method

The authors use the functional renormalization group (FRG) framework, specifically the Wetterich equation, to analyze RG consistency in low-energy effective theories of QCD. They derive formal conditions for RG consistency (Eq. 1: Λ dΓ/dΛ = 0) and show how violations arise when external scales (temperature T, chemical potential μ, inverse volume V^{-1/3}) are not negligible compared to the UV cutoff Λ. They then apply a practical strategy: starting from an initial effective action Γ_Λ' fixed at vacuum benchmark values (T=0, μ=0, infinite volume), they integrate the FRG flow from a lower scale Λ' up to a higher scale Λ (chosen so that external parameters satisfy m_ext,i/Λ ≪ 1) to obtain a corrected initial condition Γ_Λ that restores RG consistency. This is illustrated for four specific models: a quark-meson model in vacuum, a diquark model at finite density, a quark-meson-diquark model at finite temperature and density, and a quark-meson model in a finite box.

## Key numbers

- The gluonic sector of QCD decouples from the matter sector at scales Λ_phys ∼ 0.4 to 1 GeV, based on FRG studies of fundamental QCD (citing Pawlowski 2010, Herbst 2010, Haas 2013, among others).
- The paper defines a "thermal range" Λ_T[r] ≡ Λ[r; T] as the minimal cutoff value for which the RG-consistency condition holds to a given accuracy, and similarly defines "density range" Λ[r; μ] and "volume range" Λ[r; V]; no explicit numerical values for these ranges are given in the provided text.
- The condition for asymptotically large cutoff is s_i/Λ ≪ 1, where s includes physical masses m_phys and external scales m_ext = {T, V^{-1/3}, μ}; no specific numerical thresholds are provided.

## Context

This work extends the general concept of RG consistency (also called cutoff independence) from perturbative renormalization to non-perturbative functional methods like FRG, Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE), and nPI methods, which have been applied to strongly-correlated systems from condensed matter to quantum gravity. It directly addresses the problem of cutoff artefacts in QCD low-energy effective theories such as Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models and quark-meson (QM) models (citing Hatsuda 1985, Klevansky 1992, Jungnickel 1995, Berges 1997, Fukushima 2011, among many others), as well as quark-meson-diquark models (citing Rajagopal 2000, Buballa 2003, Strodthoff 2011) and Polyakov-loop extended models (citing Meisinger 1997, Pisarski 2000, Ratti 2005, Fukushima 2003, Schaefer 2007, Herbst 2010). The paper enables more reliable LEFT computations by providing a systematic method to remove cutoff artefacts, and suggests that using first-principles results from lattice or functional studies at finite temperature and small chemical potential as benchmarks (rather than vacuum values) could further improve reliability, a direction termed "QCD-assisted LEFTs."
