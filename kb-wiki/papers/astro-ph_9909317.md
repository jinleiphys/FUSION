# A Measurement of the Coulomb Dissociation of 8B at 254 MeV/nucleon and the 8B Solar Neutrino Flux

> We have measured the Coulomb dissociation of 8B into 7Be and proton at 254 MeV/nucleon using a large-acceptance focusing spectrometer. The astrophysical S17 factor for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction at E{c.m.} = 0.25-2.78 MeV is deduced yielding S17(0)=20.6 \pm 1.2 (exp.) \pm 1.0 (theo.) eV-b. This result agrees with the presently adopted zero-energy S17 factor obtained in direct-reaction measurements and with the results of other Coulomb-dissociation studies performed at 46.5 and 51.2 MeV/nucleon.

## Key claim
The Coulomb dissociation of 8B at 254 MeV/nucleon yields an extrapolated zero-energy astrophysical S17 factor of 20.6 ± 1.2 (exp.) ± 1.0 (theo.) eV-b, which agrees with the presently adopted value from direct-reaction measurements and with other Coulomb-dissociation studies at lower energies, but the extraction relies on fitting only data points below approximately 0.45 MeV relative energy to avoid uncertainties from nuclear excitations.

## Method
A 254 MeV/nucleon 8B beam, produced by fragmentation of a 350 MeV/nucleon 12C beam on a beryllium target and separated in the FRS, was directed onto a 199.7 mg/cm2 enriched 208Pb target at the KaoS magnetic spectrometer at GSI. The breakup products (7Be and proton) were detected in coincidence using silicon micro-strip detectors for angle measurement and multi-wire proportional chambers plus a scintillator wall for momentum and time-of-flight analysis, enabling reconstruction of the p-7Be relative energy and scattering angle. The astrophysical S17 factor was deduced by scaling a theoretical E1 calculation (Bertulani 1996) to match the observed yield after subtracting the simulated M1 resonance contribution, with Monte-Carlo simulations (GEANT) used to model detector response and efficiency.

## Key numbers
- Extrapolated zero-energy S17 factor: S17(0) = 20.6 ± 1.2 (exp.) ± 1.0 (theo.) eV-b, from fitting data points below E_rel ≈ 0.45 MeV.
- Beam energy: 254.0 MeV/nucleon at the center of the breakup target.
- Target thickness: 199.7 (± 0.2) mg/cm2 enriched 208Pb.
- Relative-energy resolution: σ(E_rel) = 0.11 MeV at E_rel = 0.6 MeV and 0.22 MeV at E_rel = 1.8 MeV.
- 3σ upper limits for E2/E1 transition amplitude ratio: 0.06 × 10^(-4) for E_rel = 0.3-0.5 MeV, 0.3 × 10^(-4) for 0.5-0.7 MeV, and 0.6 × 10^(-4) for 1.0-1.2 MeV.
- Angular acceptance of KaoS: 140 mrad horizontal and 280 mrad vertical; momentum acceptance p_max/p_min ≈ 2.

## Context
This work extends the Coulomb dissociation approach for the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction to a higher incident energy (254 MeV/nucleon) compared to earlier studies by Motobayashi et al. (1994) at 46.5 MeV/nucleon and Kikuchi et al. (1998) at 51.9 MeV/nucleon, with the advantage of reduced E2 admixtures and higher-order contributions. The result supports the lower normalization group of direct-reaction measurements (Vaughn 1970, Filippone 1983, Hammache 1998) and the standard-model prediction for the high-energy solar neutrino flux measured by Homestake, Super Kamiokande, and SNO. The paper enables future work to clarify the magnitude of the E2 component in Coulomb dissociation, which remains an open question given the discrepancy with the upper limits derived by Davids et al. from 7Be momentum spectra.
