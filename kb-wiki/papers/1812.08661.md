# Bottom Production in $pp$ Collisions at Large Hadron Collider Energies using Parton Cascade Model

> We study the production of bottom quarks in $pp$ collisions at Large Hadron Collider energies using previously developed parton cascade model to explore the impact of Landau Pomeranchuk Midgal (LPM) effect on their dynamics. In contrast to the case for charm quarks reported recently, we find only a marginal impact of the suppression of multiple scatterings of partons due to the LPM effect on their production. It is felt that this happens as they are only produced in very hard collisions.

## Key claim

The authors find that, in contrast to charm quarks, the production of bottom quarks in pp collisions at LHC energies is only marginally affected by multiple parton scatterings once the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Midgal (LPM) effect is accounted for, because bottom quarks are produced only in very hard collisions due to their large mass.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo parton cascade model VNI/BMS, which solves the Boltzmann equation for the time evolution of quarks and gluons, including 2→2 scatterings and 2→3 reactions via time-like branchings. They implemented the LPM effect by assigning a formation time to radiated gluons and removing those that would interact during that formation time. Calculations were performed for pp collisions at √s = 7 and 13 TeV, comparing three scenarios: neglecting the LPM effect, including only primary parton interactions with radiations, and including multiple scatterings with the LPM effect.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark production at √s = 13 TeV is about 1.4 times larger than at 7 TeV as a function of transverse momentum at y=0
- The pT-integrated rapidity spectra ratio between 13 and 7 TeV is close to 1.5 at central rapidities, rising to about 2 at forward/backward rapidities
- The cross-section ratio between 7 and 13 TeV is approximately 0.9, obtained by multiplying the production ratios with σ_in(7 TeV)/σ_in(13 TeV)
- Calculations were performed for bottom quark masses of 4.00, 4.75, and 5.00 GeV, with a pT cut-off of 2 GeV and a virtuality cut-off μ0 of 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of charm quark production in pp collisions (Srivastava 2018), which found significant sensitivity to multiple scatterings and the LPM effect. The present results show that bottom quarks, due to their larger mass, are insensitive to these effects for realistic mass values (4.75-5.00 GeV). This enables the continued use of the nuclear modification factor R_AA for bottom quarks as a reliable measure of medium modification in heavy-ion collisions, while charm quarks remain better suited for detecting the onset of an interacting medium in pp collisions.
