# Reaction mechanism in odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections

> It was recently suggested that the odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections is an evidence of the pair- ing anti-halo effect on projectile radii. We define the dimensionless staggering parameters, Γrds and ΓR, for projectile radii and reaction cross sections, respectively, and analyze the relation between Γrds and ΓR for the scattering of 14,15,16C from a 12C target at 83 MeV/A by taking account of projectile-breakup and nuclear- medium effects newly with the microscopic version of the continuum discretized coupled-channels method. The value of ΓR is deviated from that of Γrds by the projectile-breakup effect, the nuclear-medium effect and an effect due to the fact that the scattering are not the black-sphere scattering (BSS) exactly. The projectile-breakup and nuclear medium effects are nearly canceled for ΓR. The remaining non-BSS effect becomes small as an incident energy decreases, indicating that nucleus-nucleus scattering at lower incident energies are a good probe of evaluating Γrds from measured reaction cross sections.

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections (Γ_R) is not a direct measure of the staggering of projectile radii (Γ_rds); it is deviated by projectile-breakup, nuclear-medium, and non-black-sphere-scattering (non-BSS) effects, but at lower incident energies (50–80 MeV/A) the breakup and medium effects nearly cancel and the remaining non-BSS effect is small, making low-energy scattering a good probe of radius staggering.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic continuum discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with three-body (n + ¹⁴C + ¹²C) and four-body (n + n + ¹⁴C + ¹²C) models to describe scattering of ¹⁴,¹⁵,¹⁶C from a ¹²C target at 83 MeV/A. The optical potentials were constructed by folding the Melbourne g-matrix nucleon-nucleon interaction with densities of the projectile constituents and the target, thereby including the nuclear-medium effect. Three models were compared: Model I (no medium or breakup effects), Model II (medium effect included, no breakup), and Model III (both medium and breakup effects included via CDCC).

## Key numbers

- The experimental staggering parameter γ₃ for ¹⁴,¹⁵,¹⁶C is 163 ± 52 mb, about 10% of σ_R(¹⁵C) = 1319 ± 40 mb (Fang 2004).
- The calculated matter radii are 2.51 fm (¹⁴C), 2.87 fm (¹⁵C), and 2.83 fm (¹⁶C), yielding Γ_rds = 1.3.
- Model III (full CDCC) gives Γ_R = 0.77 at 83 MeV/A, deviating from Γ_rds = 1.3.
- Switching off the breakup effect (Model II) reduces Γ_R to 0.56; switching off the medium effect (Model I) increases Γ_R to 0.83.
- The nuclear-medium effect enhances σ_R by about 10% for all three projectiles (13% for ¹⁴,¹⁶C; 15% for ¹⁵C).

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Hagino and Sagawa (2011, 2012) that odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections is evidence of the pairing anti-halo effect, which was previously analyzed using the Glauber model. The present study uses the more sophisticated microscopic CDCC approach to separate the contributions of projectile breakup, nuclear-medium effects, and non-black-sphere-scattering effects for the first time. The results enable future experiments at lower incident energies (50–80 MeV/A) to reliably extract projectile radius staggering from measured reaction cross sections, and they show that the large Γ_R observed for ³⁰,³¹,³²Ne scattering at 250 MeV/A implies an even larger radius staggering, supporting the pairing anti-halo effect.
