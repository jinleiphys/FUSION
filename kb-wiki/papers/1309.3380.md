# Lowest-lying octet baryon masses in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We report on a systematic study of the ground-state octet baryon masses in the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell renormalization scheme up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, taking into account the contributions of the virtual decuplet baryons. A reasonable description of the lattice results is achieved by fitting simultaneously all the publicly available $n_f = 2+1$ lattice QCD data. It confirms that the various lattice simulations are consistent with each other. We stress that a self-consistent treatment of finite-volume corrections is important to obtain a $χ^2/\rm{d.o.f.}$ about 1.

# Lowest-lying octet baryon masses in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim
The covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell renormalization scheme, including virtual decuplet baryon contributions, can describe all publicly available n_f = 2+1 lattice QCD data simultaneously with a chi-squared per degree of freedom of about 1.0, but only when the lattice data are restricted to pion masses squared below 0.25 GeV^2 and M_phi L greater than 4, and when finite-volume corrections are treated self-consistently.

## Method
The authors performed a simultaneous chi-squared fit of 19 low-energy constants (LECs) in the extended-on-mass-shell baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N^3LO) to all publicly available n_f = 2+1 lattice QCD data from the PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, QCDSF-UKQCD, and NPLQCD Collaborations, together with experimental octet baryon masses. Finite-volume corrections were calculated self-consistently within the same framework, and fits were performed both with and without explicit virtual decuplet baryon contributions.

## Key numbers
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom at N^3LO without decuplet baryons is 1.0, and with decuplet baryons is also 1.0, indicating consistent lattice simulations across five collaborations.
- The fitted chiral limit baryon mass m_0 at N^3LO with decuplet baryons is 908(24) MeV.
- The octet-decuplet mass splitting delta is approximately 0.3 GeV.
- Lattice data were selected using the criteria M_pi^2 less than 0.25 GeV^2 and M_phi L greater than 4.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom at lower orders without decuplet baryons is 11.8 at O(p^2) and 8.6 at O(p^3), showing order-by-order improvement to 1.0 at O(p^4).

## Context
This work extends previous baryon chiral perturbation theory studies of octet baryon masses (Jenkins 1991, Bruns 2012) by performing the first systematic simultaneous fit to all publicly available n_f = 2+1 lattice QCD data with self-consistent finite-volume corrections. The study confirms that covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory in the three-flavor sector converges with clear order-by-order improvement, and that lattice results from PACS-CS (Aoki 2008), LHPC (Walker-Loud 2008), HSC (Lin 2008), QCDSF-UKQCD (Bietenholz 2011), and NPLQCD (Beane 2011) are mutually consistent. The results enable future work to disentangle virtual decuplet contributions from octet and tree-level contributions using additional observables beyond octet baryon masses alone.
