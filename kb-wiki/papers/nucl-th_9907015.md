# Nonlinear Effects in Nuclear Cluster Problem

> Some nonlinear aspects of a cluster phenomenon in nuclei are considered using of cubic Nonlinear Schroedinger Equation and Korteveg de Vries Equation. We discuss the following possible nonlinear effects: i) the decribing clusters as solitons; ii) an anomalous large angle scattering of $α$-particles by light and intermediate nuclei; iii) stable vortical objects; iv) and dynamical clusterization in the presence of instability.

## Key claim

The paper argues that diverse nuclear cluster phenomena, including anomalous large-angle alpha scattering, stable vortical excitations, and dynamical clusterization during nuclear breakup, can be described using the cubic Nonlinear Schroedinger Equation (NoSE) and the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation, but the authors explicitly state that their work is "only the first approximation to this problem" because they used only one dimension.

## Method

The authors apply the cubic Nonlinear Schroedinger Equation (NoSE) derived from hydrodynamical considerations with a compressibility constant C = K/9 to model alpha-nucleus scattering and cluster formation. They also use the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation to describe the time evolution of nuclear surface perturbations, solving it via the inverse scattering method (Gelfand-Levitan-Marchenko equation) for reflectless symmetric potentials. For vortical excitations, they consider two-dimensional analogs of nuclear disks with constant vorticity regions on a nuclear surface.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear compressibility constant K ≈ 270 MeV, obtained from experiments using the NoSE approach
- For alpha-particle scattering on 12C with kinetic energies between 4 and 12 MeV, the number of solitons N ∼ 5, corresponding to states with K+ = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8
- Eigenvalues (in MeV) for the soliton-shaped potential example: -15.57, -8.65, -1.75, 5.19, 12.11 for n = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8 respectively
- The critical parameter value σ = √12 for the KdV equation, above which perturbations break into multiple solitons
- The hard core radius estimate from the NoSE approach: R0 = √2 ξ = √2 ℏ² / √(2m(E-V)) = ℏ² / √(m(E-V))
- For alpha-alpha scattering, the repulsive core boundary occurs at r ≤ 4 fm (impact parameter b ≈ 4 fm at Ecm = 1 MeV) and r ≤ 3 fm (b ≈ 3 fm at Ecm = 6 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of anomalous large-angle scattering (ALAS) of alpha particles using effective surface potentials, and connects to prior nonlinear approaches including the work of Bialynsky-Birula and Mysielsky on nonlinear quantum mechanics, and the soliton-based coexistence model of Sandulesky and Greiner for cluster decays. The paper also references the spinodal instability framework for multifragmentation (introduced around 1983) and the more recent Rayleigh-Taylor type sheet instability proposed by other authors around 1992. The approach enables future application of the Kadomtsev-Petviashvily equation (a two-dimensional generalization) to nuclear cluster problems, and application of the similarity principle to linear alpha-chain states in nuclei.
