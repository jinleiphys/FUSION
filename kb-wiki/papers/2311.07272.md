# Hydrodynamic theories for a system of weakly self-interacting classical ultra-relativistic scalar particles: causality and stability

> We investigate the causality and stability of three different relativistic dissipative fluid-dynamical formulations emerging from a system of classical, ultra-relativistic scalar particles self-interacting via a quartic potential. For this particular interaction, all transport coefficients of Navier-Stokes, Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun and second-order transient theories can be computed in analytical form. We first show that Navier-Stokes theory is acausal and unstable regardless of the matching conditions. On the other hand, BDNK theory can be linearly causal and stable for a particular set of matching choices that does not contain the so-called exotic Eckart prescription. In particular, using the Liénard-Chipart criterion, we obtain a set of sufficient conditions that guarantee the stability of the theory. Last, second-order transient hydrodynamic theory in Landau matching is shown to be linearly causal and stable.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a system of weakly self-interacting classical ultra-relativistic scalar particles, the BDNK theory can be linearly causal and stable only for a particular set of matching conditions that excludes the exotic Eckart prescription, while second-order transient theory in Landau matching is linearly causal and stable, and Navier-Stokes theory is acausal and unstable regardless of matching conditions.

## Method

The authors analytically computed all transport coefficients for Navier-Stokes, BDNK, and second-order transient hydrodynamic theories using the exact spectrum of the linearized collision operator for a classical massless scalar field with a quartic interaction potential. They then performed a linear stability and causality analysis by considering small perturbations around global equilibrium in Fourier space, decomposing the linearized fluid-dynamical equations into transverse and longitudinal modes, and applying the Liénard-Chipart criterion to obtain sufficient conditions for stability in the BDNK case.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity coefficient for all theories: eta = 48/(g beta^3), where g = lambda^2/(32 pi) and beta = 1/T
- Diffusion coefficient in Navier-Stokes: nu^mu = z * (3/(g beta^2)) * nabla^mu alpha, with z a free matching parameter
- BDNK transport coefficients: xi = (12/(g beta^2))*(q-1)*(s-1), chi = (36/(g beta^3))*(q-2)*(s-2), kappa = (12/(g beta^2))*z, lambda = (48/(g beta^3))*(z-1)
- Relaxation times for transient theory: tau_nu = 60/(g n_0 beta^2), tau_pi = 72/(g n_0 beta^2)
- Shear viscosity in transient theory: eta = 48/(g beta^3)

## Context

This work extends the analytical transport coefficient calculations of Rocha (2023) for the same system of weakly self-interacting scalar particles, where the exact spectrum of the linearized collision operator was first derived by Denicol (2022). The causality and stability analysis builds on prior work by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2020) showing that BDNK theory can be causal and well-posed, and by Hiscock (1983, 1985) demonstrating that Navier-Stokes theory is acausal and unstable. The results enable future applications of these hydrodynamic formulations to relativistic heavy-ion collisions and other ultra-relativistic systems where the microscopic interaction is known.
