# Charged pions asymmetry from the decay of light neutral axial mesons due to interference

> Effective three meson couplings, based in flavor U(3) quark-antiquark interactions, are considered to describe two light neutral axial meson Strong decays, $A \sim f_1(1285)$ and $f_{1S} (1420)$, and the charged rho meson decay in the following channels: $A \to ρ^\mp (770)+ π^\pm$, $A\to a_0^\mp (980) +π^\pm$ and $ρ^\pm \to π^\pm π^0$. By considering neutral meson mixings, such as $f_1(1285) - a_1^0(1260)$, $f_{1S}(1420) - a_1^0(1260)$ and $η-π^0$ for the channels investigated, the leading three-meson interactions may lead to decay amplitudes that undergo interference and a resulting ratio of decay rate into charged pions, $Γ_{π^+}/Γ_{π^-}$, slightly smaller than one. For that it will be assumed that the produced charged vector mesons or charged scalar mesons undergo absorption, decay into channels without charged pions, or any other inelastic process that may suppress their decays into charged pions.

## Key claim

The decay of neutral axial mesons \(f_1(1285)\) and \(f_{1S}(1420)\) into charged pions can produce a ratio \(\Gamma_{\pi^+}/\Gamma_{\pi^-}\) slightly smaller than one due to interference between decay amplitudes, but only if the produced charged vector or scalar mesons undergo absorption, decay into channels without charged pions, or other inelastic processes that suppress their decays into charged pions.

## Method

Effective three-meson couplings were derived from a flavor U(3) quark-antiquark interaction model using a large quark mass expansion of the quark determinant in the presence of background constituent quark currents, with local auxiliary meson fields introduced via the auxiliary field method. The leading vertices for axial-vector-pseudoscalar (VPA), axial-scalar-pseudoscalar (ASP), and vector-pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (VPP) channels were computed in the zero-momentum-exchange limit, and neutral meson mixings including \(f_1(1285)-a_1^0(1260)\), \(f_{1S}(1420)-a_1^0(1260)\), and \(\eta-\pi^0\) were incorporated into the decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(\Gamma_{\pi^+}/\Gamma_{\pi^-}\) is predicted to be slightly smaller than one for decays of \(f_1(1285)\) and \(f_{1S}(1420)\) into charged pions plus \(\rho^\pm(770)\) or \(a_0^\pm(980)\).
* The \(f_1(1285)\) meson has mass \(M_{f_1} = 1.252\;\text{GeV}\) and width \(\Gamma = 0.023\;\text{GeV}\).
* The \(f_{1S}(1420)\) meson has mass \(M_{f_{1S}} = 1.426\;\text{GeV}\) and width \(\Gamma = 0.055\;\text{GeV}\).
* The \(\rho^\pm(770)\) meson has width \(\Gamma = 0.147\;\text{GeV}\).
* The \(a_1(1260)\) meson has width \(\Gamma = 0.420\;\text{GeV}\).
* The \(a_0(980)\) meson has width \(\Gamma = 0.075\;\text{GeV}\).
* The \(\eta(548)\) meson has mass \(M_{\eta} = 548\;\text{GeV}\) and width \(\Gamma_\eta = 1.3 \times 10^{-6}\;\text{GeV}\).
* The branching ratio for \(\rho^\pm \to \pi^\pm \eta\) is \(\Gamma_i/\Gamma \sim 6 \times 10^{-3}\%\).

## Context

This work extends the derivation of effective three-meson interactions from quark models such as the Global Color Model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, following the approach of Braghin (2024b). It addresses charged pion asymmetries in light meson decays, complementing studies of \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratios in heavy ion collisions by experiments such as FOPI and analyses by Zhang-Rho and JunXu et al. The results enable further investigation of CP-breaking effects in light meson decays and motivate experimental searches for the predicted asymmetry at facilities including LHCb, BESIII, COMPASS, and FAIR.
