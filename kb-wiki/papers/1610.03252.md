# Resonance poles and threshold energies for hadron physical problems by a model-independent universal algorithm

> We show how complex resonance poles and threshold energies for systems in hadron physics can be accurately obtained by using a method based on the Padé-approximant which was recently developed for the calculation of resonance poles for atomic and molecular auto-ionization systems. The main advantage of this method is the ability to calculate the resonance poles and threshold energies from \emph{real} spectral data. In order to demonstrate the capabilities of this method we apply it here to an analytical model as well as to experimental data for the squared modulus of the vector pion form factor, the S0 partial wave amplitude for $ππ$ scattering and the cross section ratio $R(s)$ for $e^+e^-$ collisions. The extracted values for the resonance poles of the $ρ(770)$ and the $f_0(500)$ or $σ$ meson are in very good agreement with the literature. When the data are noisy the prediction of decay thresholds proves to be less accurate but feasible.

## Key claim

The Resonances Via Padé (RVP) method, based on the Schlessinger point method, can converge beyond a singular point and thus identify resonance poles and threshold energies from real spectral data, but the prediction of decay thresholds from noisy experimental data is less accurate than from clean numerical data.

## Method

The RVP method constructs a truncated continued fraction (Schlessinger point method) from a finite set of real data points located in the analytic domain of the function, then performs an analytic continuation into the complex energy plane by taking the energy variable as complex. The method is applied to an analytical model function, to numerically calculated spectral functions from the quark-meson model with Functional Renormalization Group (FRG), and to experimental data on the vector pion form factor, the S0 partial wave amplitude for ππ scattering, and the cross section ratio R(s) for e+e- collisions.

## Key numbers

- Complex pole of the f0(500) (σ) meson: √s_σ = 450.2^(+9.6)_(-10.6) - i(299.2^(+9.8)_(-11.3)) MeV, extracted from the S0 partial wave amplitude using the CFD parametrization of the δ0^(0)(s) phase shift
- Three decay thresholds identified from the sigma meson spectral function at T=60 MeV: approximately 275 MeV (two-pion decay), 600 MeV (quark-antiquark decay), and 980 MeV (two-sigma decay)
- The RVP method applied to R(s) data for e+e- collisions indicates a singularity near √s ≈ 4 GeV, corresponding to the charm quark production threshold

## Context

This work extends the RVP method, originally developed for auto-ionization resonances in atomic and molecular quantum chemistry by Landau (2015), Landau (2016), and Haritan (2016), to hadron physics. It contrasts with standard Padé approximants as used by Masjuan and Sanz-Cillero (2013), which fail to converge beyond singular points when derivatives are computed numerically. The extracted σ meson pole agrees with dispersive determinations from Colangelo, Gasser, and Leutwyler (2001), Caprini, Colangelo, and Leutwyler (2006), Garcia-Martin, Kaminski, Pelaez et al. (2011a), Moussallam (2011), Pelaez (2015), and Masjuan, Ruiz de Elvira, and Sanz-Cillero (2014). The method enables future applications such as determining temperature dependence of hadron resonance poles in hot dense media or obtaining real-time correlation functions from imaginary-time counterparts.
