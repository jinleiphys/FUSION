# Spectral convexity for attractive SU(2N) fermions

> We prove a general theorem on spectral convexity with respect to particle number for 2N degenerate components of fermions. The number of spatial dimensions is arbitrary, and the system may be uniform or constrained by an external potential. We assume only that the interactions are governed by an SU(2N)-invariant two-body potential whose Fourier transform is negative definite. The convexity result implies that the ground state is in a 2N-particle clustering phase. We discuss implications for light nuclei as well as asymmetric nuclear matter in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for any number of spatial dimensions, the ground-state energy of SU(2N)-invariant fermions with a two-body potential whose Fourier transform is negative definite is a convex function of particle number for even particle numbers modulo 2N, with odd-A energies bounded below by the average of the two neighboring even-A energies. The abstract does not emphasize that the convexity result applies only to even A modulo 2N, with a separate weaker bound for odd A.

## Method

The proof uses a transfer-matrix formalism on a hypercubic lattice, introducing an auxiliary field to rewrite powers of the transfer matrix as a path integral over determinants. By considering states where j components fill one set of orbitals and the remaining 2N−j components fill another set, the author applies Hölder’s inequality to the resulting determinant expressions and takes the large-time limit to derive convexity inequalities for the ground-state energies. The argument works for any SU(2N)-invariant two-body potential with a negative-definite Fourier transform, with no restriction on spatial dimension or external potential.

## Key numbers

- The convexity constraints are verified for the most stable light nuclei with up to 16 nucleons, as shown in Fig. 2 of the paper.
- For the special case K=0 (particle numbers between 0 and 2N), the convexity holds for all A, not just even A, because the relevant determinant is strictly positive for sufficiently small temporal lattice spacing.
- The quasiparticle energy gaps satisfy δ₁ ≥ δ₂ ≥ δ₄ ≥ … ≥ δ₂ₙ for adding j fermions of different components, and similarly δ₁ʰ ≥ δ₂ʰ ≥ δ₄ʰ ≥ … ≥ δ₂ₙʰ for quasiholes.

## Context

This work extends a weaker form of the convexity inequality previously proven by Chen, Lee, and Schäfer (2004) for A ≤ 2N and zero-range attractive interactions, now generalized to any particle number and any SU(2N)-invariant potential with negative-definite Fourier transform. The results provide sufficient conditions for the onset of multiparticle clustering phases, which have been studied in nuclear matter by Röpke (1998), Tohsaki (2001), and others, and in other systems by Wu (2005, 2006) and Lecheminant (2005). The convexity bounds are satisfied by experimental light-nuclei data up to A=16, suggesting that an approximate SU(4)-symmetric attractive potential may describe light nuclei, which could enable sign-problem-free Monte Carlo simulations of dilute neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron-star crusts.
