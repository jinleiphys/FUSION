# $τ^-\to K^-η^{(\prime)}ν_τ$: a primer analysis

> The decays $τ^-\to K^-ην_τ$ and $K^-η^\primeν_τ$ are studied in the context of Chiral Perturbation Theory supplemented with the explicit inclusion of resonances. For the required vector-form factors we have explored three different possibilities according to the treatment of final-state interactions: Breit-Wigner parametrisation, exponential resummation and dispersive representation. In the first part of the analysis, we predict the invariant mass spectrum and the integrated branching ratio for both decays from a fit to data on $τ^-\to K_Sπ^-ν_τ$ decays. In the second part, we take advantage of existing data on $τ^-\to K^-ην_τ$ from BABAR and Belle collaborations and perform a fit to extract the pole position of the $K^\ast(1410)$ resonance which is seen to be in agreement and competitive with the values obtained before. From the comparison to data we conclude that the Breit-Wigner parametrisation of form factors is too na\"ıve and consequently the use of more advanced treatments such as exponential resummation or dispersive representation is essential.

## Key claim
The Breit-Wigner parametrisation of the vector-form factors for these decays is too naive, and more advanced treatments such as exponential resummation or dispersive representation are essential to reproduce experimental data; the analysis predicts branching ratios and extracts the pole position of the K*(1410) resonance from fits to tau- -> K- eta nu_tau data.

## Method
The vector-form factors for K eta and K eta' were calculated in Resonance Chiral Theory including K*(892) and K*(1410) resonances, with three treatments of final-state interactions: Breit-Wigner parametrisation, exponential resummation (JPP), and dispersive representation (BEJ). The scalar-form factors were taken from a coupled-channel dispersion-relation analysis. Predictions for the invariant mass spectra and integrated branching ratios were obtained from fits to tau- -> K_S pi- nu_tau data, then a second fit to tau- -> K- eta nu_tau data from Belle and BABAR was performed to extract the K*(1410) pole position.

## Key numbers
- Predicted branching ratio for tau- -> K- eta nu_tau: (1.52 ± 0.08) × 10^{-4} (PDG value, consistent with exponential resummation and dispersive representation results)
- Measured branching ratio for tau- -> K- eta nu_tau from Belle: (1.58 ± 0.05 ± 0.09) × 10^{-4}
- Measured branching ratio for tau- -> K- eta nu_tau from BABAR: (1.42 ± 0.11 ± 0.07) × 10^{-4}
- Upper bound for tau- -> K- eta' nu_tau from BABAR: < 2.4 × 10^{-6} at 90% CL
- Predicted branching ratio for tau- -> K- eta' nu_tau: approximately 1 × 10^{-6} (all parametrisations)
- Estimated branching ratio from Pich (1987) in ChPT at lowest order: 1.2 × 10^{-4}
- Estimated branching ratio from Li (1996) in Vector Meson Dominance: 2.2 × 10^{-4}

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical calculations by Pich (1987) using lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory and Li (1996) using Vector Meson Dominance, and provides the first analysis of the K- eta' decay mode. It uses experimental data from Belle (Inami, 2008) and BABAR (del Amo Sanchez, 2010) for tau- -> K- eta nu_tau, and the KLOE measurement of the eta-eta' mixing angle (Ambrosino, 2006). The results enable improved determination of the K*(1410) resonance pole position through joint fits to both K_S pi- and K- eta invariant mass distributions, which is important for the TAUOLA Monte Carlo generator and low-energy QCD studies.
