# On nuclear matrix element uncertainties in short range 0vBB decay

> The evaluation of short range contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay has been challenged due to critics of the ansatz of the nuclear matrix element calculations. We comment on the critics and uncertainties of these calculations and the effect on the derived limits.

## Key claim

The paper argues that criticisms of short-range nuclear matrix element calculations for neutrinoless double beta decay are overstated, and that even if one accepts the critics' estimated suppression factor, the resulting limits on superheavy neutrinos and supersymmetric couplings remain competitive with or only moderately worse than those from the standard nucleon-picture approach.

## Method

The authors review the standard pn-QRPA (proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation) approach for short-range nuclear matrix elements, which uses nucleon form factors with a cutoff mass m_A = 0.85 GeV and a hard-core correlation function with correlation length l_c ≈ 0.7 fm fitted to nucleon-nucleon scattering data. They then compare this approach to the quark-picture criticism of Minkcrit (1996), which estimates a suppression factor of 1/40 or less from quark-quark repulsion, and evaluate whether that criticism correctly applies the suppression to the derived limits.

## Key numbers

- The standard pn-QRPA approach yields a total suppression of short-range matrix elements compared to long-range matrix elements of 1/20 to 1/30.
- The quark-picture criticism estimates a suppression factor of 1/40 or less, based on a spin singlet requirement (≈ 2/3), color Coulomb repulsion (≈ 1/3), and a factor from remaining quark interactions (≈ (1/3)^2 or less).
- Even assuming the critics' suppression, the limit on the superheavy Majorana neutrino mass ⟨m_H⟩ becomes 2000 TeV, still competitive with a 1 TeV linear collider.
- For supersymmetric contributions, the estimated suppression would worsen the limit on λ'_{111} by only a factor of about 5.
- The standard approach predicted the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element with an accuracy of √2.
- A relativistic quark model calculation (Faes, year not given) confirms the standard matrix element calculations within a factor of three.

## Context

This paper responds to criticism by Minkcrit (1996) of the short-range nuclear matrix element calculations used in earlier work by Hirsch (year not given) and others, which had derived stringent limits on superheavy neutrino masses and R-parity violating SUSY couplings from neutrinoless double beta decay half-life limits. The authors argue that the quark-picture criticism is not based on a generally accepted approach and that the only serious relativistic quark model calculation (Faes, year not given) confirms the standard results within a factor of three. The paper enables continued use of the nucleon-picture limits while noting that even under the critics' assumptions, the derived constraints remain competitive with future collider experiments.
