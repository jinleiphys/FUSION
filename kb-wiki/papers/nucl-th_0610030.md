# Alpha-decay lifetimes semiempirical relationship including shell effects

> A new version of the semiempirical formula based on fission approach of alpha decay is derived, by using the optimum values of the fitting parameters determined for even-even nuclei, combined with hindrance factors for even-odd, odd-even, and odd-odd nuclides. The deviations from experimental data for two regions of nuclear chart (493 alpha emitters with Z=52-118 and 142 transuranium nuclei including superheavies (Z=92-118), respectively) are compared with those obtained by using the universal curve and the Viola-Seaborg semiempirical relationship.

# Alpha-decay lifetimes semiempirical relationship including shell effects

## Key claim
The new SemFIS formula, which multiplies the action integral by a second-order polynomial in variables representing distance from magic proton and neutron numbers, improves the description of alpha-decay half-lives near magic numbers compared to the universal curve and Viola-Seaborg relationship, but only when the six fitting parameters are determined from even-even nuclei and combined with separate hindrance factors for other parity groups.

## Method
The authors derived a semiempirical formula (SemFIS) based on the fission approach to alpha decay, where the decimal logarithm of the half-life is expressed as log T = 0.43429 χ(x,y) · K − 20.446 + H^f, with K being the action integral and χ a second-order polynomial in reduced variables x = (N−N_i)/(N_{i+1}−N_i) and y = (Z−Z_i)/(Z_{i+1}−Z_i) that encode distance from magic numbers. The six polynomial coefficients were fitted to 163 even-even emitters, and separate hindrance factors H^f were applied for even-odd, odd-even, and odd-odd nuclei. The formula was tested on 493 alpha emitters with Z=52-118 and on 142 transuranium nuclei with Z=92-118, and compared against the universal curve (UNIV) and the Viola-Seaborg relationship.

## Key numbers
- For 162 even-even emitters (Z=52-118): standard deviation σ = 0.233 for SemFIS, compared to 0.352 for UNIV
- For 122 even-odd emitters: σ = 0.515 for SemFIS, compared to 0.612 for UNIV
- For 115 odd-even emitters: σ = 0.497 for SemFIS, compared to 0.546 for UNIV
- For 94 odd-odd emitters: σ = 0.709 for SemFIS, compared to 0.841 for UNIV
- For 47 even-even transuranium nuclei (Z=92-118): σ = 0.164 for SemFIS, compared to 0.267 for UNIV and 0.282 for Viola-Seaborg
- For 45 even-odd transuranium nuclei: σ = 0.540 for SemFIS, compared to 0.554 for UNIV and 0.562 for Viola-Seaborg
- For 25 odd-even transuranium nuclei: σ = 0.514 for SemFIS, compared to 0.543 for UNIV and 0.608 for Viola-Seaborg
- For 25 odd-odd transuranium nuclei: σ = 0.492 for SemFIS, compared to 0.456 for UNIV and 0.597 for Viola-Seaborg
- Hindrance factors for the full set: H^f_{ee} = −0.025, H^f_{eo} = 0.420, H^f_{oe} = 0.280, H^f_{oo} = 0.810
- Polynomial coefficients for the full set: B_1 = 0.987389, B_2 = 0.009520, B_4 = 0.033711, B_3 = 0.035249, B_6 = −0.038941, B_5 = 0.019451

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier SemFIS formula (Poenaru 1983, 1982) and the analytical superasymmetric fission (ASAF) model (Poenaru 1996, 1991) by reducing the number of fitting parameters while maintaining accuracy. It directly addresses the systematic underestimation of half-lives near neutron magic numbers (N_d = 126) that persists in the universal curve (UNIV) and in the Viola-Seaborg relationship (Viola 1966), particularly for lighter nuclei. The improved description of shell effects enables more reliable predictions for superheavy nuclei with Z up to 118, where magic numbers such as Z=114, N=184 and Z=126 remain under investigation, and the formula's dependence on magic numbers may help determine unknown shell closures.
