# Deformed Base Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics and its Application to ^{20}Ne

> A new theoretical framework named as deformed base antisymmetrized molecular dynamics that uses the localized triaxially deformed Gaussian as the single particle wave packet is presented. The model space enables us to describe sufficiently well the deformed mean-field structure as well as the cluster structure and their mixed structure within the same framework. The improvement over the original version of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics which uses the spherical Gaussian is verified by the application to $^{20}{\rm Ne}$ nucleus. The almost pure $α+ ^{16}{\rm O_{g.s}}$ cluster structure of the $K^π$=$0^-$ band, the distortion of the cluster structure in the $K^π$=$0^+_1$ band and the dominance of the deformed mean-field structure of the $K^π$=$2^-$ band are confirmed and their observed properties are reproduced. Especially, the intra-band E2 transition probabilities in $K^π$=$0^+_1$ and $2^-$ bands are reproduced without any effective charge. Since it has been long known that the pure $α+ ^{16}{\rm O}_{g.s.}$ cluster model underestimates the intra-band $E2$ transitions in the $K^π$=$0^+_1$ band by about 30%, we consider that this success is due to the sufficient description of the deformed mean-field structure in addition to the cluster structure by the present framework. From the successful description of $^{20}{\rm Ne}$, we expect that the present framework presents us with a powerful approach for the study of the coexistence and interplay of the mean-field structure and the cluster structure.

## Key claim

The deformed-basis AMD framework improves upon spherical-basis AMD by enabling a simultaneous description of deformed mean-field structure and cluster structure within the same model space, and this improvement is verified by reproducing intra-band E2 transition probabilities in the K^π=0^+_1 and 2^- bands of ^20Ne without any effective charge, whereas pure α+^16O cluster models have long been known to underestimate these transitions by about 30%.

## Method

The authors introduced a Slater determinant of single-particle wave packets using triaxially deformed Gaussian spatial functions with independent width parameters ν_x, ν_y, ν_z common to all nucleons, along with complex centroid parameters Z_i and spin parameters α_i, β_i optimized by frictional cooling. They performed variational calculations with parity projection before variation, angular momentum projection after variation, and generator coordinate method (GCM) superposition of wave functions with different quadrupole deformation parameters β_0 and K values, using the Gogny D1S effective interaction. The method was applied to the low-lying rotational bands of ^20Ne, including K^π=0^+_1, 0^+_4, 0^-_1, and 2^- bands.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the ground state of ^20Ne: -159.8 MeV, underestimating the observed value by about 0.8 MeV
- Excitation energy of the 1^- state in the K^π=0^-_1 band: 6.72 MeV (experimental value is 5.78 MeV, 1.05 MeV above the α+^16O threshold)
- Expectation value of spin-orbit force in ^24Mg: -19.0 MeV for deformed-basis AMD versus -11.6 MeV for spherical-basis AMD
- α reduced width (θ_α^2 × 100) for the 1^- state of the K^π=0^-_1 band at channel radius a=6 fm: 31.0 (experimental lower limit >16)
- α reduced width (θ_α^2 × 100) for the 3^-_2 state of the K^π=0^-_1 band: 29.1 (experimental value 26)
- Squared amplitude of α+^16O component W^J for the 1^-_1 state: 0.95; for the 0^+_1 state: 0.70
- Expectation value of spin-orbit force for the 2^-_1 state: -12.9 MeV; for the 1^-_1 state: -0.8 MeV
- Intra-band B(E2; 3^- → 1^-) in the K^π=0^-_1 band: 151.2 e^2fm^4 (experimental value 164±26 e^2fm^4)
- Minimum energy point of the J^π=2^- curve in deformed-basis AMD: around β=0.45; in spherical-basis AMD: around β=0.25 with energy underestimated by about 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics framework by introducing deformed Gaussian wave packets, improving upon spherical-basis AMD which had been shown by Sugawa (year not specified in text) to provide inadequate descriptions of deformed heavier nuclei and smaller spin-orbit force expectation values compared to Hartree-Fock theory. The method addresses the long-standing problem that pure α+^16O cluster models (referenced as RGM and OCM in the text) underestimate intra-band E2 transitions in the ground band of ^20Ne by about 30%, and it enables future studies of the coexistence and interplay of mean-field and cluster structures in heavier stable and unstable nuclei where the spin-orbit force becomes increasingly important.
