# A new consistent Neutron Star Equation of State from a Generalized Skyrme model

> We propose a new equation of state for nuclear matter based on a generalized Skyrme model which is consistent with all current constraints on the observed properties of neutron stars. This generalized model depends only on two free parameters related to the ranges of pressure values at which different submodels are dominant, and which can be adjusted so that mass-radius and deformability constraints from astrophysical and gravitational wave measurements can be met. Our results support the Skyrme model and its generalizations as good candidates for a low energy effective field-theoretic description of nuclear matter even at extreme conditions such as those inside neutron stars.

# A new consistent Neutron Star Equation of State from a Generalized Skyrme model

## Key claim

The authors propose a hybrid equation of state for neutron star matter that combines a standard Skyrme crystal at low pressures with a BPS Skyrme fluid at high pressures, achieving consistency with all current observational constraints on neutron star properties. The model depends on only two free parameters (the transition pressures p_* and p_PT) and assumes a smooth crossover rather than a sharp phase transition between the crystalline and fluid regimes.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized equation of state by interpolating between the standard Skyrme crystal energy per baryon (derived from cubic lattice solutions of B=4 Skyrmions) and the BPS Skyrme fluid equation of state ПҒ = p + constant, using a smooth interpolating function with exponent ОІ = 0.9. They then glue this generalized EoS to the BCPM nuclear matter EoS at a low-density transition pressure p_* using a faster interpolation with ОІ = 2, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for neutron stars.

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to describe neutron stars using Skyrme models, which either produced maximum masses too small (pure Skyrme crystals, as in Nelmes and Piette) or too large (BPS Skyrmion stars, as in Adam (2015a)). The authors explicitly note that no previous Skyrmion star model achieved good agreement with observational data (Naya (2019)). The hybrid approach enables consistency with constraints from GW170817 (Abbott (2017)), heavy pulsar masses from NICER (PSR J0740+6620 at 2.14 M_вҠҷ), and the GW190814 event which may indicate a neutron star of about 2.6 M_вҠҷ. The sextic term is physically motivated by its relation to Пү meson repulsion, with the empirical Пү meson mass m_Пү = 783 MeV and coupling g_Пү^2/(4ПҖ) вҲј 12 yielding О»^2 вҲј 10 MeVВ·fm^3, consistent with the authors' extracted effective values.
