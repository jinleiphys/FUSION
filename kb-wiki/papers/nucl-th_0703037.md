# Calculation of the $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$,$n$) reaction for searching the deeply-bound $K^-pp$ state

> The formation of a deeply-bound $K^-pp$ state by the $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$,$n$) reaction is investigated theoretically in the distorted-wave impulse approximation using the Green's function method. The expected inclusive and semi-exclusive spectra at $p_{K^-}$ = 1.0 GeV/c and $θ_n = 0^{\circ}$ are calculated for the forthcoming J-PARC E15 experiment. We employ optical potentials between the $K^-$ and ``$pp$'' core-nucleus, and demonstrate systematically the dependence of the spectral shape on $V_0$ and $W_0$, which are the real and imaginary parts of the strength for the optical potential, respectively. The necessary condition to observe a distinct peak of the $K^-pp$ bound state with $I=1/2$, $J^π=0^-$ in the spectrum turns out to be that the value of $V_0$ is deeper than $\sim-100$ MeV and $W_0$ shallower than $\sim-100$ MeV, of which the strength parameters come up to recent theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The necessary condition to observe a distinct peak of the $K^-pp$ bound state with $I=1/2$, $J^\pi=0^-$ in the inclusive spectrum of the $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$,$n$) reaction is that the real part $V_0$ of the $K^-$-$pp$ optical potential is deeper than approximately $-100$ MeV and the imaginary part $W_0$ is shallower than approximately $-100$ MeV, which the abstract presents as a condition that "comes up to recent theoretical predictions" but the full text shows is a systematic parameter study that also identifies parameter sets that would make the peak invisible.

## Method

The authors calculated the inclusive and semi-exclusive missing-mass spectra for the $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$,$n$) reaction at $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/c and $\theta_n=0^\circ$ using the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with the Green's function method. They employed a Gaussian-form $K^-$-$pp$ optical potential with variable real and imaginary strength parameters $V_0$ and $W_0$, solved the Klein-Gordon equation for the $K^-pp$ system, and decomposed the strength function into $K^-$ conversion and escape components. The distorted waves for the incoming $K^-$ and outgoing neutron were evaluated within the eikonal approximation using isospin-averaged total cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The elementary $K^-+n\rightarrow n+K^-$ forward scattering cross section in free space is approximately 27 mb/sr, reduced to approximately 15 mb/sr with Fermi-averaging at $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/c.
- Using the Yamazaki-Akaishi (YA) potential with ($V_0$, $W_0$) = ($-300$ MeV, $-70$ MeV), the $K^-pp$ bound state peak appears at $p_n\approx 1280$ MeV/c with $B.E.\sim 50$ MeV and $\Gamma\sim 70$ MeV, and the integrated formation cross section is 3.5 mb/sr.
- The effective neutron number for the $K^-pp$ bound state is $N^{\rm DW}_{1s_n \to 1s_{K^-}}=0.23$ with distortion factor $D_{\rm dis}=0.47$, more than ten times larger than the effective proton number $P^{\rm DW}_{1p_p \to 1s_{K^-}}=0.013$ for the $K^-$ nuclear $1s$ state in $^{12}$C.
- The $K^-pp$ bound state predicted by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002) has $B.E.=51$ MeV and $\Gamma=68$ MeV when solving the Klein-Gordon equation with the YA potential.
- The Shevchenko, Gal, and Mares (2006) Faddeev results are simulated with ($V_0$, $W_0$) = ($-350$ MeV, $-100$ MeV), yielding $B.E.=72$ MeV and $\Gamma=115$ MeV.
- The r.m.s. distance of the "$pp$" pair in the $K^-pp$ bound state is 1.90 fm, reduced from 2.25 fm in $^3$He, but this contraction suppresses the cross section by only about 4\%.
- Including the isoscalar transition amplitude enhances the $K^-pp$ bound state cross section by about 18\% with Fermi-averaging.

## Context

This work provides the first theoretical calculation of the $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$,$n$) reaction for the J-PARC E15 experiment proposed by Iwasaki et al. (2006), extending prior DWIA-Green's function studies by Yamagata et al. (2006) on $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O targets to a light $s$-shell nucleus. The calculation systematically tests the $K^-$ optical potential parameters from Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002) and Shevchenko, Gal, and Mares (2006), and demonstrates that the $s$-shell target provides a major advantage in effective nucleon number over $p$-shell nuclei. The results enable experimental planning for the J-PARC E15 experiment and establish the parameter conditions under which a $K^-pp$ bound state peak would be observable, while also identifying the need for future full coupled-channel calculations.
