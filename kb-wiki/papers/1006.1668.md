# Transport Theoretical Description of Collisional Energy Loss in Infinite Quark-Gluon Matter

> We study the time evolution of a high-momentum gluon or quark propagating through an infinite, thermalized, partonic medium utilizing a Boltzmann equation approach. We calculate the collisional energy loss of the parton, study its temperature and flavor dependence as well as the the momentum broadening incurred through multiple interactions. Our transport calculations agree well with analytic calculations of collisional energy-loss where available, but offer the unique opportunity to address the medium response as well in a consistent fashion.

## Key claim

The authors validate that a Parton Cascade Model (PCM) based on the Boltzmann equation reproduces analytic calculations of collisional energy loss for high-momentum partons in an infinite, thermalized quark-gluon plasma, but only for temperatures above approximately \(2 T_C\) and in the weak coupling limit with a fixed coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.3\).

## Method

The authors simulate an infinite, homogeneous medium by placing a thermalized gas of gluons (or quarks and gluons) in a box with periodic boundary conditions at fixed temperature. They inject a single high-momentum gluon or quark (initial energies 50–500 GeV) and track its evolution via the Boltzmann transport equation, using pQCD matrix elements for elastic \(2\to2\) scattering processes (gluon radiation is excluded). The hard probe's energy and transverse momentum are recorded after each scattering event, and the results are compared to analytic expressions for collisional energy loss and the transport coefficient \(\hat q\).

## Key numbers

- Gluon mean free path in a gluon plasma: 1.1 fm at \(T=300\) MeV and 0.76 fm at \(T=400\) MeV.
- Gluon mean free path in a quark-gluon plasma: 0.83 fm at \(T=300\) MeV and 0.59 fm at \(T=400\) MeV.
- Quark mean free paths are about \(9/4\) times longer than those of gluons at the same temperature.
- Initial values of \(\hat q\) for a 50 GeV gluon in a gluon plasma range from approximately 1.5 GeV\(^2\)/fm at \(T=300\) MeV to about 9 GeV\(^2\)/fm at \(T=600\) MeV.
- At \(T=400\) MeV, the PCM result for \(\hat q\) agrees with calculations by Schenke (2008) and Fochler (2010).
- A GP and a QGP at identical entropy density \(s = 87.5\) fm\(^{-3}\) (corresponding to \(T=457\) MeV for GP and \(T=318\) MeV for QGP) inflict nearly identical elastic energy loss on a hard probe.

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model (PCM), previously applied to bulk properties and reaction dynamics of heavy-ion collisions (Geiger 1991, Molnar 2000, Bass 2002), to the study of hard probe energy loss. It provides a benchmark validation against analytic calculations by Bjorken (1982) and Thoma (1992) for collisional energy loss, and against analytic expressions for \(\hat q\) by Arnold (2009). The validated PCM enables future studies of full jet evolution and the medium response (e.g., Mach-cone formation) in realistic, dynamic heavy-ion collision scenarios, which cannot be addressed by leading-particle energy-loss schemes or Monte Carlo generators that neglect medium response.
