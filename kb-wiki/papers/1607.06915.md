# Formation of eta'(958) Meson Bound States by the 6Li(gamma,d) reaction

> We have investigated the 6Li(gamma,d) reaction theoretically for the formation of the eta'(958) mesic nucleus close to the recoilless kinematics. We have developed the theoretical formula and reported the quantitative results of the formation spectra for various cases in this article. We have found that the formation cross sections are reduced by the effects of the fragile deuteron form factor.

## Key claim

The formation cross sections for η'(958) mesic nuclei via the ⁶Li(γ,d) reaction are significantly reduced by the effects of the fragile deuteron form factor, particularly at forward angles where the momentum transfer to the deuteron is largest.

## Method

The authors developed a theoretical model using the effective number approach, factorizing the formation cross section into an elementary γ+d→d+η' cross section and an effective number describing the nuclear response. The elementary cross section was calculated using a contact-interaction model with the deuteron form factor from the Bonn potential, calibrated to existing η' total cross section data with coupling constant c=0.1 fm. The η'–α bound states were obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with an optical potential proportional to the α density distribution, using a realistic α density from the Gaussian expansion method. Distortion effects on the emitted deuteron were included via the Eikonal approximation with σ_dN=60 mb.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron separation energy in ⁶Li is S_d=1.47 MeV, calculated as the mass gap (M_α+M_d)-M_⁶Li.
- The η' meson at threshold (B_η'=0) is produced in recoilless kinematics by the (γ,d) reaction with E_γ≈1.46 GeV; bound η' with B_η'=24.7 and 96.8 MeV require E_γ≈1.40 and 1.23 GeV, respectively.
- The elementary cross section at θ_γd=0° is 0.019 nb/sr in the center-of-mass frame and 0.208 nb/sr in the laboratory frame at E_γ=1.4 GeV.
- For the weakest attractive potential (V_0=-50 MeV, W_0=-5 MeV), the 1s state binding energy is 22.4 MeV with width 11.6 MeV; for the strongest (V_0=-200 MeV, W_0=-20 MeV), the 1s state binding energy is 325.4 MeV with width 124.0 MeV.
- The formation cross section for the 1s state with V_0=-50 MeV is on the order of 0.1 pb/sr/MeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-nucleon pick-up reaction approach of Ikeno et al. (2015) for heavy meson bound state formation on ⁶Li targets, improving the theoretical method by including deuteron distortion effects, phenomenological elementary cross sections, realistic α density distributions, and center-of-mass corrections. The study is motivated by the ongoing debate about the η'–nucleus potential strength, with predictions ranging from strong attraction around -150 MeV from NJL model calculations (Nagahiro et al., 2005, 2006) to weak attraction from recent experimental data (Czerwinski et al., 2014; Nanova et al., 2012, 2013) and theoretical work (Bass et al., 2014). The results enable experimental searches for η' mesic nuclei using photon beams at facilities such as SPring-8 or ELPH, with the calculated spectra providing guidance for optimal incident photon energies and expected cross section magnitudes.
