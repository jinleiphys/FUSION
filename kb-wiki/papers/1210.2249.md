# Effect of charged particle's multiplicity fluctuations on flow harmonics in even-by-event hydrodynamics

> In nucleon-nucleon collisions, charged particle's multiplicity fluctuates. We have studied the effect of multiplicity fluctuation on flow harmonics in nucleus-nucleus collision in event-by-event hydrodynamics. Assuming that the charged particle's multiplicity fluctuations are governed by the negative binomial distribution, the Monte-Carlo Glauber model of initial condition is generalised to include the fluctuations. Explicit simulations with the generalised Monte-Carlo Glauber model initial conditions indicate that the multiplicity fluctuations do not have large effect on the flow harmonics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in event-by-event hydrodynamics for 20-30% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, charged particle multiplicity fluctuations (modeled via a negative binomial distribution) do not have a large effect on flow harmonics, with integrated flow coefficients increasing by less than 5% for v2 and v3 and by 10-15% for v4 and v5, contrary to expectations from earlier work by Dumitru (2012) that suggested higher moments of eccentricity could increase substantially.

## Method

The authors generalized the Monte-Carlo Glauber model for initial energy density by assigning each participant nucleon a random weight ni drawn from a negative binomial distribution with average ⟨n⟩ = 3 and width parameter k, using two models for k: Model I with k = kpp · min(TA(r⊥), TB(r⊥))σ0 and Model II with k = kpp = 2. They then solved boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic equations using the code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA with a lattice-based equation of state (Wuppertal-Budapest crossover at Tc = 174 MeV joined to hadronic resonance gas), starting at τi = 0.6 fm with zero initial fluid velocity, and computed flow harmonics from π− spectra via Cooper-Frye freeze-out at TF = 130 MeV for 100 events at impact parameters 7-8 fm.

## Key numbers

- Average charged particle multiplicity per participant in 20-30% Au+Au collisions: dNch/dη ≈ 276, with (1/0.5Npart)(dNch/dη) ≈ 3.3
- Negative binomial distribution parameters: ⟨n⟩ = 3 (fixed), kpp = 2 (from √s = 540 GeV pp collisions)
- Initial thermalization time: τi = 0.6 fm; freeze-out temperature: TF = 130 MeV; Gaussian smoothing width: σ = 0.5 fm
- Event-averaged flow coefficients increased by less than 5% for v2 and v3, and by 10-15% for v4 and v5 with multiplicity fluctuations
- Correlation measure Cmeasure for v2: 0.980 (no fluctuations), 0.974 (Model I), 0.971 (Model II); for v3: 0.873, 0.865, 0.869; for v4: 0.633, 0.538, 0.466; for v5: 0.712, 0.727, 0.689
- Differential elliptic flow decreased by less than 2% in pT range 1-2 GeV; triangular flow increased by 4-5% (Model I) and ~8% (Model II) in same pT range
- Simulations with ⟨n⟩ = 10 produced flow coefficients nearly indistinguishable from those with ⟨n⟩ = 3

## Context

This work extends the study of multiplicity fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions from the Color Glass Condensate approach of Dumitru (2012), who found that eccentricity moments increase substantially, to a Monte-Carlo Glauber model framework where the effect on actual flow harmonics had not been studied. The results contrast with those of Schenke (2012) and Bozek (2012), who found that Glasma initial conditions with negative binomial fluctuations produce harder pT spectra and increased high-pT particle production; here, multiplicity fluctuations smoothed rather than sharpened hot spots in the initial density distribution. The work enables future studies to determine whether the weak effect of multiplicity fluctuations on flow harmonics is specific to the Glauber model or holds for other initial condition models, and leaves open the question of why the v4-ε4 correlation is particularly sensitive to such fluctuations.
