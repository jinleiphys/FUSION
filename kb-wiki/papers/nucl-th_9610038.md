# Electromagnetic Production of the Hypertriton

> Kaon photoproduction on He3, gamma + He3 --> K+ + Hypertriton, is studied in the framework of the impulse approximation. Realistic He3 wave functions obtained as solutions of Faddeev equations with the Reid soft-core potential are used along with different Hypertriton wave functions. Results are compared for several elementary operator models, which can successfully describe the elementary kaon production off the proton up to a photon lab energy of k = 2.2 GeV. It is found that the corresponding cross sections are small, of the order of several nanobarns. It is also shown that the influence of Fermi motion is important, while the effect of different off-shell assumptions on the cross section is not too significant.

## Key claim

The cross section for kaon photoproduction on He3 to form the hypertriton is predicted to be small, on the order of several nanobarns, but the calculation relies on the impulse approximation and neglects final-state interactions, which could reduce the cross section by an additional 5–10%.

## Method

The reaction γ + He3 → K+ + hypertriton was calculated in momentum space using the impulse approximation, where the photon interacts with only one nucleon in He3 while the other two nucleons act as spectators. Realistic He3 wave functions from Faddeev equations with the Reid soft-core potential were combined with a simple hypertriton model consisting of a deuteron and a lambda moving in an effective Λ-d potential, with the lambda part parametrized as a Gaussian divided by a Yukawa form. The elementary kaon production operator was taken from two models (Williams and Terry) that fit data up to 2.2 GeV, and the nuclear matrix elements were evaluated numerically using partial-wave expansions.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer at forward angles for photon lab energies k = 1.4–2.2 GeV is Q ≃ 1.29–1.54 fm⁻¹.
- At k = 1.8 GeV, the ratio of the He3 cross section to the elementary proton cross section is approximately 5.9 × 10⁻³.
- Using the elementary model of Williams, which yields a maximum cross section of about 500 nb/sr on the proton at k = 1.8 GeV, the predicted He3 cross section is about 3 nb/sr.
- The lambda binding energy in the hypertriton is 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV, and the lambda part of the wave function has a range parameter Q_Λ = 1.17 fm⁻¹ (∼ ±10%) and α = (6.8 ± 1.3) × 10⁻² fm⁻¹.
- The branching ratio R = Γ(³_ΛH → π⁻ + He3) / Γ(³_ΛH → π⁻ + all) = 0.35 ± 0.04.
- The ΣNN component in the hypertriton wave function has a probability of only 0.36% (Janus) or 0.5% (Miyagawa using Nijmegen potentials).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hypernuclear electromagnetic production, such as the DWIA calculations for Ca(γ,K+) and Pb(γ,K+) by Bennhold and collaborators, and is the first analysis of hypertriton photoproduction on He3. The calculation is motivated by the start of experimental activities at Jefferson Lab, where such reactions could become feasible. The results enable comparison with the hadronic production reaction p + d → K+ + hypertriton studied by Komarov et al., which estimated maximum differential cross sections well below 1 nb/sr, and with eta photoproduction on He3 by Tiator et al., which found cross sections around 100 nb/sr at threshold. Future work could incorporate more advanced hypertriton wave functions from Faddeev calculations, such as those by the Bochum group using the Jülich or Nijmegen hyperon-nucleon potentials.
