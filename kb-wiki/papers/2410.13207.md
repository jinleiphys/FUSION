# The Theoretical Study of $Σ^{+} p \toΛa_0^{+} p$ Reaction

> We conducted a theoretical study on the process $Σ^{+} p \toΛa_0^{+} p$ based on an effective Lagrangian approach. This model encompasses the excitation of intermediate states leading to the production of $Δ(1920)$ through $π^{+}$ and $K^{+}$ meson exchanges between the initial $Σ^{+}$ baryon and the initial proton $p$, as well as the generation of $Δ(1940)$ via $π^{+}$ and $ρ^{+}$ meson exchanges. We provide predictions for the total and differential cross sections and discuss the potential impacts of cutoff parameters, off-shell effects, and branching ratios on the $Δ^{\ast} \to a_0 p$ decay. Given the dominance of $Δ(1940)$ in the region close to the reaction threshold, this reaction is considered an ideal platform for deeply exploring the unique properties of the $Δ(1940)$ resonance. Through this approach, we can gain a more precise understanding of the intrinsic characteristics of this particle.

## Key claim

The reaction Σ⁺ p → Λ a₀⁺ p is predicted to be dominated by the Δ(1940) resonance near threshold, making it an ideal platform to study this resonance, but the quantitative predictions depend on free parameters (cutoff parameters, off-shell parameters, and branching ratios) that require future experimental data to be constrained.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach to compute the total and differential cross sections for the Σ⁺ p → Λ a₀⁺ p reaction. The model includes t-channel and u-channel exchanges: Δ(1920) is produced via π⁺ and K⁺ meson exchanges, while Δ(1940) is produced via π⁺ and ρ⁺ meson exchanges between the initial Σ⁺ baryon and the initial proton. Coupling constants were determined from partial decay widths using branching ratios from the Particle Data Group, and form factors with cutoff parameters were introduced to account for the internal structure of hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy of the a₀p channel is approximately 1.918 GeV, closely aligned with the mass ranges of Δ(1920) and Δ(1940) resonances.
- For Δ(1920) (width 300 MeV): adopted branching ratios of 12.5% to Nπ, 4% to ΣK, and 4% to Na₀(980), yielding coupling constants g²/4π of 5.26×10⁻³, 8.92×10⁻², and 4.12 respectively.
- For Δ(1940) (width 400 MeV): adopted branching ratios of 80% to Nρ, 10.5% to Nπ, and 2% to Na₀(980), yielding coupling constants g²/4π of 5.39, 3.85×10⁻², and 1.06 respectively.
- Cutoff parameters used: Λ_ρ = 1.2 GeV for ρ meson exchange; Λ_Δ(1920) = Λ_Δ(1940) = 1.5 GeV for intermediate baryons.
- The total cross section was calculated from the reaction threshold up to 3.9 GeV center-of-mass energy.
- Differential cross sections were studied at a center-of-mass energy W = 3.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of Δ* hyperon resonances, particularly following observations of parity doublets with J = 3/2 for Δ(1920) and Δ(1940) in the γ p → π⁰ η reaction by CBELSA/TAPS (2007) and Wang (2023). The reaction Σ⁺ p → Λ a₀⁺ p is proposed as a cleaner probe than photon-induced reactions or Nπ scattering because charge conservation eliminates interference from N* resonances with isospin 1/2. The predictions enable future experimental comparisons to determine the free parameters (cutoff parameters, off-shell parameters, and branching ratios) and to better characterize the Δ(1940) resonance and its coupling to the a₀p channel.
