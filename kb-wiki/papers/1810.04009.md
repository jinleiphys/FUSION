# Deep learning: Extrapolation tool for ab initio nuclear theory

> Ab initio approaches in nuclear theory, such as the no-core shell model (NCSM), have been developed for approximately solving finite nuclei with realistic strong interactions. The NCSM and other approaches require an extrapolation of the results obtained in a finite basis space to the infinite basis space limit and assessment of the uncertainty of those extrapolations. Each observable requires a separate extrapolation and most observables have no proven extrapolation method. We propose a feed-forward artificial neural network (ANN) method as an extrapolation tool to obtain the ground state energy and the ground state point-proton root-mean-square (rms) radius along with their extrapolation uncertainties. The designed ANNs are sufficient to produce results for these two very different observables in $^6$Li from the ab initio NCSM results in small basis spaces that satisfy the following theoretical physics condition: independence of basis space parameters in the limit of extremely large matrices. Comparisons of the ANN results with other extrapolation methods are also provided.

## Key claim
The authors propose a feed-forward artificial neural network (ANN) as an extrapolation tool for ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) results, claiming it can predict ground-state energies and point-proton root-mean-square radii in $^6$Li from calculations in small basis spaces, provided the ANN results satisfy the condition of independence of basis space parameters in the limit of extremely large matrices.

## Method
The authors performed NCSM calculations for $^6$Li using the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon interaction with harmonic-oscillator basis spaces up through cutoff $N_{\rm max} = 18$, producing a dataset at 19 selected values of $\hbar\Omega$ from 8 to 50 MeV. They designed separate feed-forward ANNs with one input layer (two neurons for $\hbar\Omega$ and $N_{\rm max}$), one hidden layer (eight neurons with hyperbolic tangent sigmoid activation), and one output layer, trained using Bayesian regularization with mean-square-error performance function. Multiple datasets were constructed by successively including NCSM results up through $N_{\rm max} = 10, 12, 14, 16$, and 18, with networks filtered by performance thresholds on independent test sets.

## Key numbers
- The largest NCSM matrix diagonalized for $^6$Li had dimension approximately $2.8 \times 10^9$ at cutoff $N_{\rm max} = 18$
- NCSM basis spaces for $^6$Li have been achieved up through $N_{\rm max} = 22$ in prior work by Forssen (2017)
- Networks were retained if mean-square-error on the 3/19 test set was below 0.002 MeV$^2$ for ground-state energy and below $5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ fm$^2$ for point-proton root-mean-square radius
- Retained networks required mean-square-error on their design set below 0.0002 MeV$^2$ for ground-state energy and below $5.0 \times 10^{-7}$ fm$^2$ for point-proton root-mean-square radius
- The original dataset consisted of 19 selected $\hbar\Omega$ values: 8, 9, 10 MeV and then in 2.5 MeV increments covering 10 to 50 MeV

## Context
This work extends the preliminary study by Negoita (2018) which considered only one dataset up through $N_{\rm max} = 10$ and did not report uncertainty assessments. The ANN approach is compared with established extrapolation methods including "Extrapolation B" (2009, 2013), "Extrapolation A5", "Extrapolation A3", and "Extrapolation based on L$_{\rm eff}$" (2017), which were developed for taking the infinite basis space limit in NCSM calculations. The method enables extrapolation of observables that lack proven analytical extrapolation formulas, potentially extending to other nuclei and observables beyond ground-state energy and radius.
