# Decreasing elliptic flow at the LHC: Calculations in a parton recombination approach

> We apply the parton recombination approach to study the energy dependence of the elliptic flow, v_2 in heavy ion collisions from AGS to LHC energies. The relevant input quantities ($T, μ_B, η_T$) at the various center of mass energies are obtained from fits to the available data. The model yields a good description of the integrated v_2 data for charged particles at midrapidity from AGS to RHIC energies. In stark contrast to the current expectations, we observe a decrease of the integrated v_2 values above the highest RHIC energy. Thus, we predict a decrease of v_2 at LHC energies compared to the RHIC results. This drop is attributed to negative v_2 values for the underlying parton distributions at low to moderate transverse momenta that develops if the transverse flow velocity is high enough. At energies above the LHC regime, the present approach predicts even negative values for the integrated v_2.

# Decreasing elliptic flow at the LHC: Calculations in a parton recombination approach

## Key claim

The integrated elliptic flow v2 of charged hadrons at midrapidity will decrease from RHIC to LHC energies, and may even become negative at energies above the LHC regime, due to negative v2 values developing in the underlying parton distributions at low to moderate transverse momenta when the transverse flow velocity is sufficiently high. This prediction contradicts the linear extrapolations toward LHC by Borghini and Wiedemann (2007) and the general expectation in the heavy ion community.

## Method

The authors apply a collinear parton recombination approach, using a thermal spectrum for recombining quarks without a power-law tail, to compute elliptic flow for hadrons in the low to intermediate pT range (0 to about 3 GeV at RHIC and 5-6 GeV at LHC). The input parameters (temperature T, baryo-chemical potential μB, and transverse flow rapidity ηT) at the hadronization surface are obtained from fits to experimental data at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, then extrapolated to LHC energies; for √sNN = 5.5 TeV the values are T = 175 MeV, μB ≈ 0 MeV, and βT = 0.75c. The spatial asymmetry of non-central collisions is translated into a velocity asymmetry in the parton phase, and the elliptic flow is calculated from the angular modulation of the parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 5.5 TeV (LHC), the input parameters are T = 175 MeV, μB ≈ 0 MeV, and transverse flow velocity βT = 0.75c.
- The recombination formalism is applied in the pT range from 0 to about 3 GeV at RHIC and about 5-6 GeV at LHC; the lower limit of applicability is approximately pT ∼ 1 GeV at RHIC and pT ∼ 1.2 GeV at LHC.
- The parameter p0 in the function f(pT) = 1/(1 + (pT/p0)^2) is taken as p0 = 1.1 GeV from Fries (2003).
- At RHIC (√s = 200 GeV), the transverse flow velocity is vT = 0.54, consistent with Fries (2003).
- The scaling factor from freeze-out transverse rapidity to hadronization surface transverse rapidity is k = 0.85.

## Context

This work extends the parton recombination approach of Fries (2003) to study the energy dependence of elliptic flow from AGS to LHC energies, using parameterizations of T, μB, and ηT fitted to experimental data. The prediction of decreasing v2 at LHC contradicts the linear extrapolations by Borghini and Wiedemann (2007) and the general expectation in the heavy ion community. The negative v2 values for heavy particles at low pT have been previously observed in blast-wave fits by Voloshin (1996), Huovinen (2001), Retiere (2003), and Pratt (2004), and experimentally by the NA49 collaboration (2003); the present work shows this effect becomes quantitatively strong at LHC energies. The results enable experimental tests at LHC that could distinguish between recombination-dominated and other hadronization scenarios.
