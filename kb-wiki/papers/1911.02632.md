# Pions in hot dense matter and their astrophysical implications

> We study the role of pions in hot dense matter encountered in astrophysics. We find that strong interactions enhance the number density of negatively charged pions and that this enhancement can be calculated reliably for a relevant range of density and temperature using the virial expansion. We assess the influence of pions and muons on the equation of state (EOS) and weak interaction rates in hot dense matter. We find that thermal pions increase the proton fraction and soften the EOS. We also find that charged current weak reactions involving pions and muons $\barν_μ+μ^- \rightarrow π$ and $ν_μ+π^- \rightarrow μ^-$ make an important contribution to the opacity of muon neutrinos. This could influence the dynamics of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers. Finally, we note that pion-nucleon reactions can alter the evolution of the proton fraction when weak interactions are not in equilibrium.

## Key claim

Strong interactions between pions and nucleons, calculated reliably using the virial expansion at modest densities and high temperatures, enhance the number density of negatively charged pions in hot dense matter, which in turn increases the proton fraction, softens the equation of state, and introduces new charged-current weak reactions that significantly reduce the mean free path of muon neutrinos.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid model combining a Skyrme mean-field treatment of nucleon-nucleon interactions (using the NRAPR parameter set) with a virial expansion for pion-nucleon interactions, where the second-virial coefficient is calculated directly from experimentally measured pion-nucleon phase shifts in the s-wave and p-wave channels. The composition of charge-neutral matter in beta-equilibrium is solved self-consistently at fixed baryon density and temperature, and a pseudo-potential model proportional to the phase shifts is constructed to obtain the in-medium pion dispersion relation needed for neutrino mean free path calculations.

## Key numbers

- At baryon density n_B = n_0 (0.16 fm^-3) and T = 30 MeV, the number density of negatively charged pions is approximately equal to that of muons (n_π- ≈ n_μ-)
- At n_B = 1.4 n_0 and T = 30 MeV, n_π- ≈ 2 n_μ-
- At n_B = 1.2 n_0 and T = 60 MeV, pions decrease the total pressure by about 9%
- The second-virial coefficient b_2^(nπ-) at T = 15 MeV is 2.14 × 10^-4 fm^-3; at T = 30 MeV is 4.09 × 10^-3 fm^-3; at T = 45 MeV is 1.87 × 10^-2 fm^-3
- The second-virial coefficient b_2^(pπ-) at T = 15 MeV is 4.24 × 10^-4 fm^-3; at T = 30 MeV is 4.68 × 10^-3 fm^-3; at T = 45 MeV is 2.02 × 10^-2 fm^-3
- The fudge factor α needed to match the virial expansion result for the pion self-energy ranges from 0.139 to 0.216 across densities from 0.5 n_0 to 1.0 n_0 and temperatures from 30 to 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pion condensation in dense matter by Migdal (1973) and Sawyer (1972), and is motivated by recent work showing that muons could facilitate supernova explosions through the neutrino-driven mechanism. The virial expansion approach for pion-nucleon interactions follows the formalism of Dashen (1969) and Venugopalan (1992), previously applied to hot hadronic gas in heavy-ion collisions and to dilute nucleon gas in proto-neutron stars by Horowitz (2005). The results enable future studies of neutrino transport in core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, where temperatures of 20-50 MeV are realized at densities near nuclear saturation, as shown in simulations by Perego (2019).
