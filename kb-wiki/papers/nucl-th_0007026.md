# Anomalous radial expansion in central heavy-ion reactions

> The expansion velocity profile in central heavy-ion reactions in the Fermi energy domain is examined. The radial expansion is non-hubblean and in the surface region it scales proportional to a higher exponent ($α> 1$) of the radius. The anomalous expansion velocity profile is accompanied by a power law nucleon density profile in the surface region. Both these features of central heavy-ion reactions disappear at higher energies, and the system follows a uniform Hubble expansion ($α\simeq 1$).

# Anomalous radial expansion in central heavy-ion reactions

## Key claim
The radial expansion velocity profile in central heavy-ion reactions in the Fermi energy domain is non-Hubblean, scaling as $v \propto r^\alpha$ with $\alpha > 1$ in the surface region, but this anomalous behavior disappears at higher energies where the system reverts to uniform Hubble expansion ($\alpha \simeq 1$). The claim is conditional on bombarding energy and on the distinction between surface and bulk matter, with the anomalous profile appearing only in the surface region for energies around and below the Fermi energy.

## Method
The authors used a nonlocal quantum kinetic theory based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation with Enskog-type gradient corrections (shifts proportional to derivatives of the scattering phase shift) to simulate central $Ta+Au$ collisions at various bombarding energies. They extracted the radial velocity profile exponent $\alpha$ and the nucleon density profile exponent $\beta$ from angular-averaged quantities as functions of time, separately analyzing the bulk region ($R < 10$ fm) and the surface region ($R > 10$ fm). Dynamical trajectories in the temperature-density plane were constructed using the Fermi liquid relation to connect the expansion pattern to the spinodal instability region.

## Key numbers
- At $E_{lab}/A = 33$ MeV for $Ta+Au$, the surface velocity exponent stabilizes asymptotically at $\alpha_{surf} \simeq 1.75 \pm 0.05$ for $t > 80$ fm/c
- At $E_{lab}/A = 33$ MeV, the surface density falls as a power law $n(r) \sim r^{-\beta}$ with $\beta \simeq 3 \pm 0.2$ for $t > 120$ fm/c
- At $E_{lab}/A = 15$ MeV, the limiting density exponent is $\beta_{lim} \sim 3.5$; at $E_{lab}/A = 33$ MeV, $\beta_{lim} \sim 3.1$
- The anomalous power-law density tail and non-Hubblean velocity profile are present for $15$ MeV $\lesssim E_{lab}/A \lesssim 50$ MeV, with the smallest $\beta_{lim}$ value reaching about 2
- At $E_{lab}/A = 60$ MeV, the shell-like structure forms at $t_{sh} \simeq 280$ fm/c; at $E_{lab}/A = 90$ MeV, at $t_{sh} \simeq 170$ fm/c
- For $E_{lab}/A = 15$ MeV, a giant resonance with period $T = 60$ fm/c (energy $2\pi/T = 20.6$ MeV) is excited
- No anomalous behavior ($\alpha_{surf} > 1$) is observed for energies higher than $90$ MeV/A
- The system in the spinodal region at $E_{lab}/A = 15$ and 33 MeV reaches $n \approx n_0/3$ and $T \approx 7-8$ MeV between 150 fm/c and 200 fm/c

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological finding of Le Fevre et al. (1999), who postulated a non-Hubblean velocity profile with $\alpha$ between 1.5 and 2 to explain fragment kinetic energies in $Xe+Sn$ collisions at 50 MeV/A within a statistical microcanonical model. The present kinetic theory confirms that such a profile is a plausible freeze-out configuration for symmetric heavy-ion collisions. The results contradict the common assumption of linear Hubble expansion ($\alpha=1$) from scaling hydrodynamics (BGZ 1978, CCL 1998) for the Fermi energy domain. The work enables predictions for non-Gaussian Bose-Einstein correlations and suggests that proton and neutron source parametrizations may differ, extending the analysis of Helgesson et al. (1997) who studied asymmetric reactions at 30 MeV/A assuming linear scaling. The predicted power-law correlations at small momenta for energies $35$ MeV $\lesssim E_{lab}/A \lesssim 50$ MeV provide a testable signature for future interferometry experiments.
