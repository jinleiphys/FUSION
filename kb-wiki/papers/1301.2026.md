# Odd-particle number random phase approximation and extensions: Applications to particle and hole states around $^{16}$O

> The hole-state random phase approximation (hRPA) and the particle-state random phase approximation (pRPA) for systems like odd $A$ nuclei are discussed. These hRPA and pRPA are formulated based on the Hartree-Fock ground state. An extension of hRPA and pRPA based on a correlated ground state is given using time-dependent density-matrix theory. Applications to the single-particle states around $^{16}$O are presented. It is shown that inclusion of ground-state correlation affects appreciably the results of hRPA and pRPA. The question of the coupling of the center of mass motion of the core to the particle (hole) is also discussed.

# Odd-particle number random phase approximation and extensions: Applications to particle and hole states around $^{16}$O

## Key claim

The hole-state RPA (hRPA) and particle-state RPA (pRPA) formulations, when extended using a correlated ground state from time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), produce appreciably different results for single-particle states around $^{16}$O compared to the Hartree-Fock based versions, but the extended formulation (EoRPA) requires careful truncation of the configuration space to avoid unphysical mixing between hole and particle states.

## Method

The authors derive hRPA and pRPA using the equation-of-motion approach starting from the Hartree-Fock ground state, then extend these formulations using a correlated ground state obtained as a stationary solution of the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) equations. The extended odd-RPA (EoRPA) is formulated with an excitation operator containing both one-body and three-body terms, and the coupled equations are solved for the four amplitude types $y_h^\mu$, $y_p^\mu$, $Y_{pp':h}^\mu$, and $Y_{hh':p}^\mu$ in applications to $^{16}$O. The ground-state correlation matrix $C_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'}$ and occupation matrix $n_{\alpha\alpha'}$ are determined from the stationary TDDM solution using the gradient method.

## Key numbers

- For $^{16}$O, the single-particle energies of the $0p_{1/2}$ hole state and $0d_{5/2}$ particle state are calculated using oRPA and EoRPA; the inclusion of ground-state correlations shifts the $0p_{1/2}$ hole strength distribution compared to the HF-based oRPA result
- The occupation number $n_{\alpha\alpha} = \sum_\mu x^\mu_\alpha(x^\mu_\alpha)^*$ is shown to approximately hold in the applications to $^{16}$O, with the sum rule relating transition amplitudes to the occupation matrix
- The spurious center-of-mass mode in the odd system is shown to have zero excitation energy from the ground state $|\Phi_0\rangle$ and energy $\epsilon_p$ from the HF state, provided all components of $x^\mu_{\alpha\alpha'}$ are included

## Context

This work extends the earlier hRPA formulation of Ref. (hrpa73) and its applications in Ref. (hrpa96) by incorporating ground-state correlations beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation. The approach contrasts with the particle-phonon coupling model of Bortignon (1985) and Giai (1981), where the spurious translational mode is simply discarded; the authors argue that using 2h-1p and 2p-1h TDA amplitudes in the mass operator may give more realistic results than the particle-vibration coupling model. The work also relates to the Faddeev random-phase approximation (FRPA) of Dickhoff (2002) and the Tamm-Dancoff approximation for odd systems. The extension enables future investigation of rotational modes in odd nuclei and analogous questions in cold fermionic atom systems coupled to collective Kohn modes.
