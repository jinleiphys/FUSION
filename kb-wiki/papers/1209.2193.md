# Medium effects in direct reactions

> We discuss medium corrections of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross sections and their influence on direct reactions at intermediate energies $\gtrsim 50$ MeV/nucleon. The results obtained with free NN cross sections are compared with those obtained with a geometrical treatment of Pauli-blocking and with NN cross sections obtained with Dirac-Bruecker methods. We show that medium corrections may lead to sizable modifications for collisions at intermediate energies and that they are more pronounced in reactions involving weakly bound nuclei.

## Key claim

Medium corrections to nucleon-nucleon cross sections can lead to sizable modifications in direct reactions at intermediate energies (≥50 MeV/nucleon), and these modifications are more pronounced for reactions involving weakly bound nuclei; however, the effects do not lead to sizable modifications on the shapes of momentum distributions, only on the absolute cross sections.

## Method

The authors compared three treatments of medium corrections to nucleon-nucleon cross sections: a geometrical Pauli-blocking model, a Dirac-Brueckner (G-matrix) approach, and a phenomenological parametrization combining free cross sections with Brueckner results. They implemented these medium-modified cross sections within the eikonal approximation using the t-ρρ formalism to compute total reaction cross sections and knockout cross sections for several systems, comparing with experimental data for p+¹²C total reaction cross sections, ¹²C(¹⁷C,¹⁶B)X at 35 MeV/u, and ⁹Be(¹¹Be,¹⁰Be)X at 60 MeV/u.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹²C(¹⁷C,¹⁶B)X system at 35 MeV/nucleon, medium corrections change the total cross section by about 5% (from 24.78 mb without medium to 25.98 mb with medium).
- For the ⁹Be(¹¹Be,¹⁰Be)X system at 60 MeV/nucleon, medium corrections change the total cross section by about 50% (from 261.4 mb without medium to 172.1 mb with medium).
- The free nucleon-nucleon cross section parametrizations cover energies from 10 MeV to 5 GeV, with separate formulas for pp and np collisions in three energy intervals each.
- The geometrical Pauli-blocking formula uses a saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and includes a threshold at E = 46.27 ρ̃²/³ MeV where ρ̃ = (ρ_p + ρ_t)/ρ₀.
- The Brueckner parametrization for σ_np is valid only below 300 MeV (pion production threshold).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of medium effects in knockout reactions by Bertulani and collaborators (2010) and builds on the t-ρρ approximation framework developed by Hussein, Rego, and Bertulani (1991). The authors compare their results with experimental data from Aumann et al. (2000) for ¹¹Be knockout and from Lecouey et al. (2009) for ¹⁷C knockout. The work enables more accurate extraction of spectroscopic factors from knockout reactions, which is important for nuclear structure studies and for indirect methods in nuclear astrophysics. The authors note that medium corrections have been frequently ignored in past experimental analyses and should be included for better accuracy, especially at lower energies around 50 MeV/nucleon and for weakly bound nuclei.
