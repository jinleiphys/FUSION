# Near-threshold $K^- d$ scattering and properties of kaonic deuterium

> We calculated the $1s$ level shifts and widths of kaonic deuterium, corresponding to accurate results on near-threshold antikaon - deuteron scattering. The Lippmann-Schwinger eigenvalue equation with a strong $K^- - d$ and Coulomb potentials was solved. The two-body $K^- - d$ potentials reproduce the near-threshold elastic amplitudes of $K^- d$ scattering obtained from the three-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations with the coupled channels using four versions of the $\bar{K}N - πΣ$ potentials. Both new $\bar{K}N - πΣ$ potentials reproducing the very recent SIDDHARTA data on kaonic hydrogen and our older potentials reproducing KEK data have one- or two-pole versions of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance and reproduce experimental data on $K^- p$ scattering.

# Near-threshold $K^- d$ scattering and properties of kaonic deuterium

## Key claim

The $1s$ level shift and width of kaonic deuterium are calculated using a two-body $K^- - d$ potential that reproduces three-body AGS results, but the calculated widths are large enough to question whether accurate experimental measurement will be possible. The results show no correlation between the one- or two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance and the three-body $K^- d$ scattering or kaonic deuterium observables, meaning that future experiments on kaonic deuterium cannot resolve the question of the number of poles forming the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance.

## Method

The author solved the Lippmann-Schwinger eigenvalue equation with a strong $K^- - d$ potential and directly included Coulomb potential to calculate the $1s$ level energy of kaonic deuterium. The two-body $K^- - d$ potentials were constructed to reproduce the near-threshold elastic $K^- d$ amplitudes, scattering lengths, and effective ranges obtained from three-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations with coupled $\bar{K}NN$ and $\pi \Sigma N$ channels, using four versions of $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ potentials (one- and two-pole versions reproducing either SIDDHARTA or KEK kaonic hydrogen data).

## Key numbers

- Kaonic hydrogen $1s$ level shift from SIDDHARTA: $\Delta E_{1s}^{\rm SIDD} = -283 \pm 36 \pm 6$ eV; width: $\Gamma_{1s}^{\rm SIDD} = 541 \pm 89 \pm 22$ eV
- Kaonic hydrogen $1s$ level shift from KEK: $\Delta E_{1s}^{\rm KEK} = -323 \pm 63 \pm 11$ eV; width: $\Gamma_{1s}^{\rm KEK} = 407 \pm 208 \pm 100$ eV
- $K^- d$ scattering length with $V^{\rm 1,SIDD}_{\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma}$: $a_{K^- d} = -1.48 + i\,1.22$ fm; with $V^{\rm 2,SIDD}_{\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma}$: $a_{K^- d} = -1.51 + i\,1.23$ fm
- $K^- d$ effective range with $V^{\rm 1,SIDD}_{\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma}$: $r^{\rm eff}_{K^- d} = 0.68 - i\,1.33$ fm; with $V^{\rm 2,SIDD}_{\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma}$: $r^{\rm eff}_{K^- d} = 0.67 - i\,1.35$ fm
- Kaonic deuterium $1s$ level shift with $V^{\rm 1,SIDD}_{K^- d}$: $\Delta E_{1s}^{K^- d} = -781$ eV; width: $\Gamma_{1s}^{K^- d} = 1010$ eV
- Kaonic deuterium $1s$ level shift with $V^{\rm 2,SIDD}_{K^- d}$: $\Delta E_{1s}^{K^- d} = -794$ eV; width: $\Gamma_{1s}^{K^- d} = 1012$ eV
- Kaonic deuterium $1s$ level shift with $V^{\rm 1,KEK}_{K^- d}$: $\Delta E_{1s}^{K^- d} = -767$ eV; width: $\Gamma_{1s}^{K^- d} = 810$ eV
- Kaonic deuterium $1s$ level shift with $V^{\rm 2,KEK}_{K^- d}$: $\Delta E_{1s}^{K^- d} = -809$ eV; width: $\Gamma_{1s}^{K^- d} = 902$ eV
- PDG values for $\Lambda(1405)$: mass $M^{PDG}_{\Lambda(1405)} = 1406.5 \pm 4.0$ MeV, width $\Gamma^{PDG}_{\Lambda(1405)} = 50 \pm 2.0$ MeV
- Deuteron binding energy used: $E_{\rm deu} = 2.2246$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous three-body calculations of the $K^- d$ scattering length (Shevchenko, 2012) by constructing explicit two-body $K^- - d$ potentials that reproduce the full near-threshold elastic amplitudes, enabling calculation of kaonic deuterium atomic level characteristics. The new $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ potentials are fitted to the more precise SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data (SIDDHARTA Collaboration, 2011), superseding the older KEK data fits, while the older potentials from Shevchenko (2012) are retained for comparison. The results enable comparison with the upcoming SIDDHARTA-2 experiment planning measurements of kaonic deuterium, and demonstrate that the corrected Deser formula (used by Barret and Deloff, 1999) underestimates the width by about 30% compared to the full calculation.
