# Isospin Breaking in the Pion-Nucleon Coupling from QCD Sum Rules

> We use QCD sum rules for the three point function of a pseudoscalar and two nucleonic currents in order to estimate the charge dependence of the pion nucleon coupling constant $g_{NNπ}$ coming from isospin violation in the strong interaction. The effect can be attributed primarily to the difference of the quark condensates $<{\bar u}u>$ and $<{\bar d}d>$. For the splitting $(g_{ppπ_0} - g_{nnπ_0}) / g_{NNπ}$ we obtain an interval of $1.2 * 10^{-2}$ to $3.7 * 10^{-2}$, the uncertainties coming mainly from the input parameters. The charged pion nucleon coupling is found to be the average of $g_{ppπ_0}$ and $g_{nnπ_0}$. Electromagnetic effects are not included.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules for the three-point function, the authors estimate that the strong-interaction contribution to the isospin splitting between the neutral pion-nucleon couplings lies in the range \(1.2 \times 10^{-2}\) to \(3.7 \times 10^{-2}\), with the charged coupling being exactly the average of the two neutral ones; the result is conditional on omitting electromagnetic effects and on truncating the operator product expansion at order 4.

## Method

The authors construct QCD sum rules for the three-point correlation function of two nucleonic Ioffe currents and a pseudoscalar isovector current, keeping terms up to first order in isospin violation (\(m_d - m_u\)). They perform the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-4 condensates (quark condensates only), use a Borel transformation to suppress higher resonances, and extract the coupling splitting by comparing the OPE side with the phenomenological side saturated by nucleon and pion intermediate states. The \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing is handled by using a linear combination of SU(3) octet pseudoscalar currents to isolate the physical pion state, and the unknown \(N \to N^*\) transition contributions are treated as an effective parameter fitted to achieve Borel stability.

## Key numbers

- The splitting \((g_{pp\pi_0} - g_{nn\pi_0}) / g_{NN\pi}\) is found in the interval \(1.2 \times 10^{-2}\) to \(3.7 \times 10^{-2}\) (final range after including estimated higher-order uncertainties).
- The \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing angle \(\theta\) is \((10 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-3}\), obtained from lowest-order chiral perturbation theory using quark mass ratios.
- The quark mass ratio \((m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) = 0.29 \pm 0.05\) is taken from the analysis of Leutwyler (1996).
- The strong-interaction contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference \(\delta M_N\) is taken as an interval \(1.6\ \text{MeV} < \delta M_N < 2.4\ \text{MeV}\).
- The gluon condensate \(g_c^2 \langle G^2 \rangle\) is set to \(0.474\ \text{GeV}^4\).
- The range for the isospin-breaking parameter \(\gamma = \langle \bar{d}d \rangle / \langle \bar{u}u \rangle - 1\) is \(0.002 < -\gamma < 0.010\), taken from previous analyses.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum-rule approach for the pion-nucleon coupling constant developed by Reinders, Rubinstein, and Yazaki (1985) and Reinders (1984) to the isospin-violating case, focusing on strong-interaction effects from the up-down quark mass difference and condensate splitting. The result is compared with earlier model estimates: the quark-gluon model of Henley and Zhang (1987) gives \(\approx 0.010\)–\(0.014\); the quark-pion model used by Piekarewicz (1995) gives \(\approx 0.006\); the chiral bag model of Cao and Hwang (1984) gives \(\approx 0.0067\); and the cloudy bag model of Thomas, Bickerstaff, and Gersten (1981) gives \(\approx 0.006\) with opposite sign. The analysis enables future inclusion of electromagnetic corrections and higher-order condensates, and provides a strong-interaction baseline for interpreting charge-symmetry-breaking phenomena in nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering.
