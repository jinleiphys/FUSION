# Global and local spin polarization in heavy ion collisions: a brief overview

> elsarticle style, 8 pages, 1 figure. Contribution to the proceedings of Quark Matter 2017 based on the plenary talk. More discussions on the characteristics of theoretical models and their relationships are made. Some references are added, the text is improved and typos are corrected

## Key claim

The paper provides a brief overview of theoretical models and experimental measurements of global and local spin polarization in heavy ion collisions, arguing that the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) created in these collisions is the most vortical fluid ever produced in the laboratory, based on STAR collaboration data that show positive global polarization of Lambda hyperons at all collision energies below 62.4 GeV.

## Method

The paper reviews three theoretical approaches to spin polarization: the microscopic spin-orbital coupling model, the Wigner function method (a quantum kinetic approach), and the statistical-hydro model (a generalization of the statistical model to rotating fluids). It then describes how the STAR collaboration measured global polarization of Lambda hyperons via their weak decay into a proton and a negatively charged pion, using the decay's parity-violating nature where the proton is emitted preferentially along the Lambda's spin direction, with the polarization extracted from the event-averaged angular distribution of the daughter proton relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization for Lambda hyperons averaged over all collision energies: Π_Λ = (1.08 ± 0.15)%
- Global polarization for anti-Lambda hyperons averaged over all collision energies: Π_Λ̄ = (1.38 ± 0.30)%
- Lambda decay constant used in polarization measurement: α_H = 0.642 ± 0.013
- Estimated fluid vorticity from polarization data: (9 ± 1) × 10^21 s^(-1)
- Positive polarization observed at each collision energy at the level of (1.1–3.6)σ for both Λ and Λ̄

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for spin polarization in heavy ion collisions originally proposed by Liang and Wang (2004) and Voloshin (2004), and later developed by Becattini and collaborators (2009–2016) through the statistical-hydro model and by Gao, Chen, and Fang (2012–2016) through the Wigner function approach. The paper reviews the STAR collaboration's Beam Energy Scan measurements (2017) which provide the first experimental evidence for global polarization, enabling future studies of vorticity in the quark-gluon plasma and comparisons between different theoretical models, as well as investigations of possible differences between Lambda and anti-Lambda polarization that could arise from magnetic field effects or Pauli blocking.
