# The three-body problem with short-range forces: renormalized equations and regulator-independent results

> We discuss effective field theory treatments of the problem of three particles interacting via short-range forces. One case of such a system is neutron-deuteron scattering at low energies. We demonstrate that in attractive channels the renormalization-group evolution of the 1+2 scattering amplitude may be complicated by the presence of eigenvalues greater than unity in the kernel. We also show that these eigenvalues can be removed from the kernel by one subtraction, resulting in an equation which is renormalization-group invariant. A unique solution for 1+2 scattering phase shifts is then obtained. We give an explicit demonstration of our procedure for both the case of three spinless bosons and the case of the doublet channel in nd scattering. After the contribution of the two-body effective range is included in the effective field theory, it gives a good description of the nd doublet phase shifts below deuteron breakup threshold.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the non-uniqueness and cutoff-dependence of the three-body scattering amplitude with zero-range forces can be removed by a single subtraction at threshold, without introducing an explicit three-body force at leading order, provided one piece of three-body data (the 1+2 scattering length) is used as input.

## Method

The authors start from the Amado model for three spinless bosons and take the zero-range limit to obtain the Faddeev-type integral equation for boson-dimer scattering. They analyze the kernel's eigenvalue spectrum and show that eigenvalues greater than unity cause cutoff dependence; they then apply a subtraction procedure at threshold (originally developed by Hammer and Mehen (2000)) that removes these eigenvalues from the kernel. The same formalism is extended to the doublet channel in neutron-deuteron scattering, where the two-body effective range is included as a sub-leading correction.

## Key numbers

- The transcendental equation for the exponent \(s\) in the asymptotic solution has complex roots \(s = \pm i s_0\) with \(s_0 = 1.0062\) for the three-boson case (\(\lambda = 1\)).
- A new eigenvalue of one appears in the kernel each time the cutoff is increased by a factor of \(e^{\pi/s_0} = 22.7\).
- The number of Efimov bound states grows as \(N = (1.0062/\pi) \ln(\Lambda/\gamma)\).
- The critical coupling for which the RG evolution becomes non-trivial is \(\lambda_c = 3\sqrt{3}/(4\pi)\).
- The boson-dimer scattering length \(a_3\) is used as input; the example calculation uses \(a_3 \gamma = -2\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of the three-body problem with short-range forces, building on the foundational studies of Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (1957) and Danilov (1961), and the more recent EFT formulations by Bedaque et al. (1999). The subtraction method provides an alternative to introducing an explicit three-body force at leading order, as done by Bedaque et al. (1999), and yields regulator-independent predictions for 1+2 phase shifts below breakup threshold. This enables systematic EFT calculations for three-nucleon systems and related systems such as helium-4 atoms and Bose-Einstein condensates near Feshbach resonances.
