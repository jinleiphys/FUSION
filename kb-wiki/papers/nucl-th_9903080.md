# Infinite Nuclear Matter on the Light Front: Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations

> A relativistic light front formulation of nuclear dynamics is developed and applied to treating infinite nuclear matter in a method which includes the correlations of pairs of nucleons: this is light front Brueckner theory. We start with a hadronic meson-baryon Lagrangian that is consistent with chiral symmetry. This is used to obtain a light front version of a one-boson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential (OBEP). The accuracy of our description of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) data is good, and similar to that of other relativistic OBEP models. We derive, within the light front formalism, the Hartree-Fock and Brueckner Hartree-Fock equations. Applying our light front OBEP, the nuclear matter saturation properties are reasonably well reproduced. We obtain a value of the compressibility, 180 MeV, that is smaller than that of alternative relativistic approaches to nuclear matter in which the compressibility usually comes out too large. Because the derivation starts from a meson-baryon Lagrangian, we are able to show that replacing the meson degrees of freedom by a NN interaction is a consistent approximation, and the formalism allows one to calculate corrections to this approximation in a well-organized manner. The simplicity of the vacuum in our light front approach is an important feature in allowing the derivations to proceed. The mesonic Fock space components of the nuclear wave function are obtained also, and aspects of the meson and nucleon plus-momentum distribution functions are computed. We find that there are about 0.05 excess pions per nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors develop a light front formulation of nuclear dynamics that includes nucleon-nucleon correlations via Brueckner theory, and show that this approach reproduces nuclear matter saturation properties reasonably well while yielding a compressibility of 180 MeV that is smaller than alternative relativistic approaches, which typically produce too large a value.

## Method

The authors start from a hadronic meson-baryon Lagrangian consistent with chiral symmetry and derive a light front version of a one-boson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential (OBEP) using six mesons (π, η, ρ, ω, σ, δ/a₀). They then derive the Hartree-Fock and Brueckner Hartree-Fock equations within the light front formalism and apply them to infinite nuclear matter, solving the Weinberg-type integral equation (transformed to the Blankenbecler-Sugar equation) to obtain the scattering amplitude and nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility of nuclear matter: 180 MeV, obtained from the light front Brueckner calculation
- Excess pions per nucleon: about 0.05, computed from the mesonic Fock space components of the nuclear wave function
- The nucleon-nucleon potential uses six meson types with masses below 1 GeV/c², with form factors of the form F_α(q²) = ((Λ²_α − m²_α)/(Λ²_α − q²))^{n_α}, where n_α = 1 for pseudoscalar and scalar mesons and n_α = 2 for vector mesons
- The two-nucleon scattering data are reproduced below the inelastic threshold (about 300 MeV laboratory kinetic energy)

## Context

This work extends previous light-front mean-field calculations by Miller (1997) to include nucleon-nucleon correlations via Brueckner theory, representing one step beyond the earlier mean-field treatment of infinite nuclear matter. The light front approach is motivated by deep inelastic scattering experiments (the EMC effect) which show differences between parton distributions of free nucleons and nucleons in a nucleus, and the formalism enables calculation of light-front momentum distributions that are directly relevant to such experiments. The method also allows for systematic calculation of corrections from mesonic degrees of freedom, which is not straightforward in alternative relativistic approaches.
