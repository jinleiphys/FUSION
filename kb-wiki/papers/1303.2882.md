# Exclusive $p p \to p p π^{0}$ reaction at high energies

> The amplitudes for the $p p \to p p π^{0}$ process applicable at high energy are discussed in detail. Both diffractive bremsstrahlung (Drell-Hiida-Deck--type model), photon-photon and photon-omega exchange mechanisms are included in the calculation. Large cross sections of the order of mb are predicted. The corresponding differential cross sections in rapidities and transverse momenta of outgoing protons and pions as well as relative azimuthal angle between outgoing protons are calculated for RHIC and LHC energies. The hadronic bremsstrahlung contributions dominate at large (forward, backward) pion rapidities. The diffractive nonresonant background contributes at small $π^0 p$ invariant mass and could be therefore misinterpreted as the Roper resonance. We predict strong dependence of the slope in $t$ (squared four-momentum transfer between ingoing and outgoing proton) on the mass of the supplementary excited $π^{0}p$ system. At high energies and midrapidities, the photon-photon contribution dominates over the diffractive components, however, the corresponding cross section is rather small. The photon-odderon and odderon-photon contributions are included in addition and first estimates (upper limits) of their contributions to rapidity and transverse momentum distribution of neutral pions are presented. We suggest a search for the odderon contribution at midrapidity and at $p_{\perp,π^{0}} \sim$ 0.5 GeV. Our predictions are ready for verification at RHIC and LHC. The bremsstrahlung mechanisms discussed here contribute also to the $pp \to p(n π^{+})$ reaction. Both channels give a sizable contribution to the low-mass single diffractive cross section and must be included in extrapolating the measured experimental single diffractive cross section.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the exclusive $pp \to pp\pi^{0}$ reaction at high energies (RHIC and LHC) has large cross sections of the order of millibarns, but this result is conditional on the dominance of diffractive bremsstrahlung (Drell-Hiida-Deck-type) mechanisms at forward and backward pion rapidities, while at midrapidity the photon-photon contribution dominates but with a rather small cross section.

## Method

The authors constructed amplitudes for the $pp \to pp\pi^{0}$ process including diffractive bremsstrahlung mechanisms (pion exchange, proton exchange, and direct production) driven by pomeron and reggeon exchanges, as well as photon-photon and photon-omega exchange mechanisms. They used Regge-inspired parametrizations for the energy dependence of elastic scattering amplitudes, with parameters from the Donnachie-Landshoff analysis of total $NN$ and $\pi p$ cross sections, and included absorptive corrections via a quasieikonal approximation. The calculations were performed for RHIC and LHC energies, producing differential cross sections in rapidities, transverse momenta, and relative azimuthal angle between outgoing protons.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections of the order of mb are predicted for the $pp \to pp\pi^{0}$ process at high energies.
- The total cross section for single pion production near threshold grows to about 10 $\mu$b at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s} \approx 3$ GeV.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is $g_{\pi NN}^{2}/4\pi = 13.5$.
- The slope parameters used are $B_{I\!\!P}^{NN} = 9$ GeV$^{-2}$, $B^{\pi N}_{I\!\!P} = 5.5$ GeV$^{-2}$, $B_{I\!\!R}^{NN} = 6$ GeV$^{-2}$, and $B^{\pi N}_{I\!\!R} = 4$ GeV$^{-2}$ for pomeron and reggeon exchange respectively.
- The cutoff parameter for off-shell form factors is $\Lambda_{N} \sim 1$ GeV, and the form factor parameter for pion vertices is $\Lambda_{\pi} = 1$ GeV.
- The pion Regge trajectory slope is $\alpha'_{\pi} = 0.7$ GeV$^{-2}$, and the nucleon trajectory is $\alpha_{N}(p^{2}) = -0.3 + \alpha'_{N} \, p^{2}$ with $\alpha'_{N}=0.9$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- The authors suggest searching for the odderon contribution at midrapidity and at $p_{\perp,\pi^{0}} \sim 0.5$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of exclusive pion pair production in $pp \to pp\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ (Lebiedowicz and Szczurek, 2010) and $pp \to nn\pi^{+}\pi^{+}$ (Lebiedowicz and Szczurek, 2011) to the single neutral pion channel. The diffractive bremsstrahlung mechanism is based on the Drell-Hiida-Deck model for diffractive production of $\pi N$ final states in $NN$ collisions (Deck, 1968), and is similar to mechanisms used for $pp \to pp\omega$ and $pp \to pp\gamma$ processes. The paper provides predictions ready for verification at RHIC and LHC, and notes that these bremsstrahlung mechanisms also contribute to the $pp \to p(n\pi^{+})$ reaction, both giving sizable contributions to the low-mass single diffractive cross section that must be included in extrapolating measured experimental single diffractive cross sections.
