# Trace preserving quantum dynamics using a novel reparametrization-neutral summation-by-parts difference operator

> We develop a novel numerical scheme for the simulation of dissipative quantum dynamics following from two-body Lindblad master equations. All defining continuum properties of the Lindblad dynamics, hermiticity, positivity and in particular trace conservation of the evolved density matrix are preserved. The central ingredient is a new spatial difference operator, which not only fulfils the summation by parts (SBP) property but also implements a continuum reparametrization property. Using the time evolution of a heavy-quark anti-quark bound state in a hot thermal medium as an explicit example, we show how the reparametrization neutral summation-by-parts (RN-SBP) operator preserves the continuum properties of the theory.

## Key claim

The paper develops a novel numerical scheme for simulating dissipative quantum dynamics from two-body Lindblad master equations that preserves all defining continuum properties (hermiticity, positivity, and trace conservation) by introducing a reparametrization-neutral summation-by-parts (RN-SBP) difference operator, which the abstract claims is demonstrated using heavy-quarkonium time evolution.

## Method

The authors discretize the one-dimensional Lindblad master equation for a heavy quark-antiquark bound state in a hot thermal medium using a newly constructed spatial difference operator that satisfies both the summation-by-parts (SBP) property and a continuum reparametrization neutrality property, allowing derivatives in the original coordinates (x,y) to be re-expressed in terms of relative (z=x-y) and center-of-mass (z'=x+y) coordinates. The RN-SBP operator is applied to the partial differential equation for the reduced density matrix ρ(x,y,t), and the time evolution is computed while monitoring the conservation of trace, hermiticity, and positivity. The numerical scheme uses periodic boundary conditions and the Simultaneous Approximation Term (SAT) technique for boundary treatment.

## Key numbers

* The heavy-ion collision temperatures exceed 200,000 times the temperature in the core of the sun (no explicit numerical value in Kelvin given).
* The spatial discretization uses N+1 equidistant grid points with spacing Δx = (x_R - x_L)/N, where the standard second-order SBP operator has H = Δx diag[1/2, 1, ..., 1, 1/2] for non-periodic boundaries.
* The dissipation kernel D(x) is symmetric around the origin with a maximum at the origin, ensuring F_2(0)=0 for the first and third derivatives vanishing at the origin.
* The Lindblad operators depend on a continuous momentum variable k and a discrete color index a, with the damping rates γ_k and Lindblad operators L_k derived from the dissipation kernel D(x) and the real-time heavy-quark potential V(x).

## Context

This work extends the development of summation-by-parts (SBP) difference operators, previously applied to Navier-Stokes and Schrödinger-like equations (Svärd and Nordström 2014, Fernández 2014, Lundquist and Nordström 2014), to the Lindblad master equation framework for open quantum systems. The specific physical application builds on the derivation of the quarkonium Lindblad equation by Akamatsu (2012, 2014) and the relative-coordinate reduction by Miura (2019), and it enables numerically stable simulations of dissipative quantum dynamics that preserve unit trace, which is essential for studying energy, momentum, and particle exchange between a quantum subsystem and its environment without introducing artificial loss channels.
