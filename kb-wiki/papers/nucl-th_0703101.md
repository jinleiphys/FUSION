# Fission and Nuclear Liquid-Gas Phase Transition

> The temperature dependence of the liquid-drop fission barrier is considered, the critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter being a parameter. Experimental and calculated data on the fission probability are compared for highly excited $^{188}$Os. The calculations have been made in the framework of the statistical model. It is concluded that the critical temperature for the nuclear liquid--gas phase transition is higher than 16 MeV.

# Fission and Nuclear Liquid-Gas Phase Transition

## Key claim
The critical temperature for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is higher than 16 MeV, based on a comparison of experimental and calculated fission probabilities for highly excited 188Os. The analysis uses the temperature dependence of the liquid-drop fission barrier, with the critical temperature as a free parameter, and the result depends on the chosen parameterization of the surface tension.

## Method
The authors calculated the fission probability of 188Os compound nuclei produced in 4He + 184W collisions at excitation energies up to 117 MeV, using the statistical model with a temperature-dependent liquid-drop fission barrier. The surface tension coefficient was parameterized as a function of reduced temperature T/Tc using four different approximations: equation (4) from Ravenhall (1983), equation (5) from Lima (1992), a linear approximation, and a quadratic approximation. The calculated fission probabilities were compared to experimental data from Moretto (1972) to extract the critical temperature by fitting the highest excitation energy point.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature extracted using the surface tension parameterization of equation (4): Tc ≈ (23.5 ± 2.5) MeV
- Critical temperature extracted using the surface tension parameterization of equation (5): Tc ≈ (17.5 ± 1.5) MeV
- Excitation energy of the compound nucleus 188Os at the highest beam energy: 117 MeV
- Mean number of emitted neutrons at the highest excitation energy: 11-12
- Critical temperature from the FASA collaboration analysis of p(8.1 GeV)+Au data: Tc = (20 ± 3) MeV (Karnaukhov 2003)
- Critical temperature from the FASA collaboration reanalysis: Tc = (17 ± 2) MeV (Karnaukhov 2004)
- Critical temperature from semi-empirical analysis of limiting temperatures: 16.6 ± 0.86 MeV (Natowitz 2002)
- Critical temperature from Fisher model analysis of π (8 GeV/c) + Au collisions: (6.7 ± 0.2) MeV (Elliott 2002)
- Critical temperature from Fisher model analysis of Au, La, Kr (1.0 GeV per nucleon) + C: (7.6 ± 0.2), (7.8 ± 0.2), and (8.1 ± 0.2) MeV respectively (Elliott 2003)

## Context
This work extends the approach suggested in Karnaukhov (1997) of using fission probability as an alternative observable to estimate the critical temperature, motivated by the large discrepancies in previous estimates from multi-fragmentation data. The results support the higher critical temperature values obtained by the FASA collaboration (Karnaukhov 2003, 2004) and by Natowitz (2002), in contrast to the lower values (around 6-8 MeV) extracted using Fisher droplet model analyses by Elliott (2002, 2003) and the earlier estimate of about 5 MeV by Hirsh (1984). The analysis enables a cross-check of critical temperature determinations using a different physical observable than intermediate mass fragment distributions.
