# The E2 contribution to the 8B -> p + 7Be Coulomb dissociation cross section

> We have calculated the E1 and E2 contributions to the low-energy B-8 + Pb-208 -> p + Be-7 + Pb-208 Coulomb dissociation cross sections using the kinematics of a recent experiment at RIKEN. Using a potential model description of the Be-7 (p,gamma) B-8 reaction, we find that the E2 contributions cannot a priori be ignored in the analysis of the data. Its inclusion reduces the extracted Be-7 (p,gamma) B-8 S-factor at solar energies by about 25%.

# The E2 contribution to the 8B -> p + 7Be Coulomb dissociation cross section

## Key claim

The E2 contribution to the 8B + 208Pb Coulomb dissociation cross section cannot be ignored a priori in the analysis of the RIKEN experiment data, contrary to the assumption of a pure E1 break-up process; including the E2 component reduces the extracted 7Be(p,gamma)8B S-factor at solar energies by about 25%, but this estimate is based on a potential model whose E2 cross section is not directly tested against experimental data and may be somewhat uncertain.

## Method

The authors calculated the E1 and E2 contributions to the double-differential Coulomb dissociation cross section for 8B + 208Pb -> p + 7Be + 208Pb using the semi-classical formalism of Baur et al. (1986), adopting the kinematics of the RIKEN experiment at 46.5 MeV/u. They used the E1 and E2 capture cross sections from the potential model calculation of Kim et al. (1987) for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction, and fitted the RIKEN data to their (E1+E2) yield curves by multiplying the calculated yields with a parameter determined by chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers

* The E2/E1 ratio of virtual photon numbers exceeds 100 at theta_R = 2 degrees for relative energies E_17 <= 1 MeV.
* The E2 process dominates the total Coulomb dissociation cross section at angles theta_R >= 4 degrees.
* The best-fit parameter alpha at the resonance energy (E_17 = 0.6 MeV) is 0.66 +/- 0.08, with chi-squared reduced by 30% compared to a pure E1 fit.
* The extracted partial E1 S-factors are 11.2 +/- 1 eV-b at E_17 = 0.6 MeV, 11.5 +/- 2.5 eV-b at 0.8 MeV, and 12.3 +/- 3 eV-b at 1.0 MeV.
* The extrapolated S-factor at solar energies (20 keV) is 12 +/- 3 eV-b, about 25% smaller than the value derived in the original RIKEN analysis by Gai et al. (1994).
* This S-factor is 55% of the value adopted in Bahcall and Pinsonneault (1992) and 62% of the value in Turck-Chieze et al. (1993).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the RIKEN Coulomb dissociation experiment by Gai et al. (1994), which assumed a pure E1 break-up process, by demonstrating that E2 contributions are significant at low energies due to the strong enhancement of virtual E2 photons relative to E1 photons. The finding contradicts the assumption that E2 contributions can be ignored, as was valid for the direct radiative capture reaction where E1 dominates E2 by nearly three orders of magnitude (Kim et al., 1987). The result enables a reinterpretation of the Coulomb dissociation data that brings the predicted high-energy solar neutrino flux into agreement with the Kamiokande III observation, but calls for improved theoretical treatment via multichannel resonating group model calculations (Csoto, in progress) and experimental determination of the E2 contribution through triple-differential cross section measurements sensitive to E1-E2 interference.
