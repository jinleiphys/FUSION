# Sequential decays of the triple strange process $\bar{p} p \rightarrow \barΩ^{+} Ω^{-} \rightarrow K^{+} \barΛ K^{-} Λ\rightarrow K^{+} \bar{p} π^{+} K^{-} p π^{-}$: a model-independent approach

> A model-independent irreducible tensor formalism is developed to analyse $\barΩΩ$ production in $\bar{p}p$ collisions and its subsequent decay via the triple-strange decay process, $\barΩ^{+} Ω^{-} \rightarrow K^{+} \barΛ K^{-} Λ\rightarrow K^{+} \bar{p} π^{+} K^{-} p π^{-}$. These processes are relevant for the upcoming experiments at PANDA. We not only provide expressions for the density matrix of the $Ω\barΩ$ system but also for the products at each stage of the decay. The Fano statistical tensors so obtained not only completely characterize the relevant final systems but also provide expressions for joint angular distributions. Finally, we show which of the Fano statistical tensors characterizing the $\barΩΩ$ system can be inferred from the Fano statistical tensors characterizing the final $\bar{p}p$ system, wherein we also obtain the relation between the production cross sections for $Ω\barΩ$ and the final $p\bar{p}$ system.

# Sequential decays of the triple strange process \(\bar{p} p \rightarrow \bar{\Omega}^{+} \Omega^{-} \rightarrow K^{+} \bar{\Lambda} K^{-} \Lambda \rightarrow K^{+} \bar{p} \pi^{+} K^{-} p \pi^{-}\): a model-independent approach

## Key claim
A model-independent irreducible tensor formalism can completely characterize the spin state of the \(\Omega\bar{\Omega}\) system produced in \(\bar{p}p\) collisions and relate it to the measurable Fano statistical tensors of the final \(p\bar{p}\) system, provided the decay amplitudes are known. The formalism shows that for unpolarized beam and target (as at PANDA), the maximum rank of the Fano tensor is 4, and the production cross section for \(\Omega\bar{\Omega}\) can be determined from the final \(p\bar{p}\) system.

## Method
The author develops an irreducible tensor formalism using channel-spin and partial-wave expansions for the reaction \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \bar{\Omega}^{+}\Omega^{-}\), with initial spins \(s_a=s_A=1/2\) and final spins \(s_b=s_B=3/2\), and channel spins \(s_i=0,1\) and \(s_f=0,1,2,3\). The density matrices for the \(\Omega\bar{\Omega}\) system, the \(\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\) system after the first decay stage, and the final \(p\bar{p}\) system after the second decay stage are derived using Racah recoupling techniques, incorporating parity-conserving and parity-nonconserving decay amplitudes for \(\Omega \rightarrow \Lambda K\) and \(\Lambda \rightarrow p\pi\) decays. The Fano statistical tensors at each stage are expressed in terms of irreducible tensor reaction amplitudes and spherical harmonics of the decay product momenta.

## Key numbers
- Initial spins: \(s_a=s_A=1/2\) for \(\bar{p}\) and \(p\); final spins: \(s_b=s_B=3/2\) for \(\bar{\Omega}\) and \(\Omega\)
- Channel spins: \(s_i=0,1\) in the entrance channel; \(s_f=0,1,2,3\) in the exit channel
- Maximum rank of Fano statistical tensor for the \(\Omega\bar{\Omega}\) system is 6 with polarized beam and target, but reduces to 4 for unpolarized beam and target (as at PANDA)
- For unpolarized beam and target, the differential cross section is \(\mathrm{Tr}(\rho_{\Omega,\bar{\Omega}}) = 16 \sum_{\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda} \sum_{\text{even }\mu} |M^\lambda_\mu(\lambda_1,\lambda_2)|^2\), with possible even \(\mu\) values: \(\mu=0\) for \(\lambda=0,1,2,3,4\); \(\mu=2\) for \(\lambda=2,3,4\); \(\mu=4\) for \(\lambda=4\)
- The Fano statistical tensors satisfy \(t^{k}_{q}(k_1,k_2)=0\) for all odd \(q\) due to parity conservation in the transverse frame

## Context
This work extends the polarization analysis of hyperon production from \(e^+e^-\) annihilation contexts (Perotti et al., Ablikim et al.) and from earlier work by Thomé on \(\Omega\) decays at PANDA, which did not address the spin structure of \(\Omega\bar{\Omega}\) production. The formalism is specifically designed for the upcoming PANDA experiments, where the \(\Omega^-\) hyperon has never been studied with antiproton beams and the production cross section is unknown. The results enable model-independent determination of the \(\Omega\bar{\Omega}\) production cross section and Fano statistical tensors from measurements of the final \(p\bar{p}\) system in kinematically complete experiments.
