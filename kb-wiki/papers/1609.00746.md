# Revisiting the equivalence of light-front and covariant QED in the light-cone gauge

> We discuss the equivalence between light-front time-ordered-perturbation theory and covariant quantum field theory in light-front quantization, in the case of quantum electrodynamics at one-loop level. In particular, we review the one-loop calculation of the vertex correction, fermion self-energy and vacuum polarization. We apply the procedure of integration by residue over the light-front energy in the loop to show how the perturbative expansion in covariant terms can be reduced to a sum of propagating and instantaneous diagrams of light-front time-ordered perturbation theory. The detailed proof of equivalence between the two formulations of the theory resolves the controversial question on which form should be used for the gauge-field propagator in the light-cone gauge in the covariant approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the equivalence between light-front time-ordered perturbation theory and covariant QED in the light-cone gauge at one-loop level requires using the two-term photon propagator (without the instantaneous interaction term) or, alternatively, the three-term propagator together with an explicit instantaneous interaction in the Hamiltonian, resolving a long-standing ambiguity in the literature.

## Method

The authors performed one-loop calculations of the vertex correction, fermion self-energy, and vacuum polarization in QED using both light-front time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) and the covariant formulation with light-front coordinates. They applied integration by residues over the light-front energy component in the loop to show how covariant Feynman diagrams reduce to sums of propagating and instantaneous diagrams from light-front TOPT. The photon propagator was treated in the light-cone gauge $n \cdot A = 0$, comparing the two-term expression $D^{\mu\nu}(q) = (-i/q^2)(g^{\mu\nu} - (q^\mu n^\nu + q^\nu n^\mu)/q^+)$ with the three-term expression $D_T^{\mu\nu}(q) = (-i/q^2)(g^{\mu\nu} - (q^\mu n^\nu + q^\nu n^\mu)/q^+ + q^2 n^\mu n^\nu/(q^+)^2)$.

## Key numbers

- The two-term photon propagator is given by $D^{\mu\nu}(q) = (-i/q^2)(g^{\mu\nu} - (q^\mu n^\nu + q^\nu n^\mu)/q^+)$ (Eq. 2)
- The three-term photon propagator is given by $D_T^{\mu\nu}(q) = (-i/q^2)(g^{\mu\nu} - (q^\mu n^\nu + q^\nu n^\mu)/q^+ + q^2 n^\mu n^\nu/(q^+)^2)$ (Eq. 3)
- The vertex correction calculation involves integration over longitudinal momentum fraction $x$ in the range $-\xi < x < 1$ for the non-vanishing contribution, where $\xi = \Delta^+/2P^+$ is the skewedness parameter
- The fermion propagator splits into propagating and instantaneous parts as $S_F(k) = i(\tilde{k}\!\!\!/+m)/(k^2 - m^2 + i\epsilon) + i\gamma^+/(2k^+)$ (Eq. 16)
- The photon propagator splits as $D^{\mu\nu}(q) = i d^{\mu\nu}(\tilde{q})/(q^2 + i\epsilon) + i n^\mu n^\nu/(q^+)^2$ (Eq. 17)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of light-front versus covariant equivalence in scalar $\phi^3$ theory (Sawicki, 1991), Yukawa theory (Schoonderwoerd, 1998), scalar QED (Ji, 2014), and standard QED (Misra, 2005; Patel, 2010), but finds results partially at variance with those earlier calculations. The paper clarifies the correct form of the gauge-field propagator in the light-cone gauge, resolving confusion about whether to use the two-term or three-term expression. This enables consistent calculations of QED processes in light-front quantization, which is important for applications to parton distribution functions and other non-perturbative QCD phenomena where light-front methods offer advantages over equal-time approaches.
