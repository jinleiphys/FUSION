# Quantum states from normalizing flows

> We introduce an architecture for neural quantum states for many-body quantum-mechanical systems, based on normalizing flows. The use of normalizing flows enables efficient uncorrelated sampling of configurations from the probability distribution defined by the wavefunction, mitigating a major cost of using neural states in simulation. We demonstrate the use of this architecture for both ground-state preparation (for self-interacting particles in a harmonic trap) and real-time evolution (for one-dimensional tunneling). Finally, we detail a procedure for obtaining rigorous estimates of the systematic error when using neural states to approximate quantum evolution.

## Key claim

Normalizing flows can be modified to represent quantum wavefunctions (including phase information) for many-body systems, enabling efficient uncorrelated sampling from the probability distribution defined by the wavefunction. The authors demonstrate this architecture for both ground-state preparation and real-time evolution, but note that the approach cannot represent wavefunctions with nonzero winding number, limiting its applicability in dimensions higher than one or for excited states.

## Method

The authors construct neural quantum states by modifying two normalizing flow architectures: RealNVP (called QuantumNVP) and continuous normalizing flows. In QuantumNVP, the scaling function in affine coupling layers is made complex to encode phase information; in the continuous flow, the neural network defining the differential equation is given an additional output for the phase derivative. For ground-state preparation, the wavefunction parameters are optimized by minimizing the Hamiltonian expectation value via stochastic gradient descent. For real-time evolution, the authors minimize a loss function measuring violation of the discretized time-dependent Schrodinger equation, using a Crank-Nicolson-like integration scheme.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of three distinguishable particles in a harmonic trap with Yukawa interaction: with 6 QuantumNVP layers, the ground-state energy is approximated to within 10% for coupling strengths up to g^2 = 10.
- Time-evolution simulation: 50 steps of real-time evolution under the tunneling Hamiltonian, each with step size Delta t = 0.1, using a quantum continuous normalizing flow.
- Training uses B = 2^10 samples per gradient step, with 2^15 samples for evaluating the Hamiltonian expectation value in the optimized state.
- Learning rate for ground-state optimization: 3 x 10^-4 using the Adam optimizer.
- Training duration: 3 x 10^4 steps for ground-state preparation.

## Context

This work extends the neural quantum state approach originally proposed by Carleo and Troyer (2017) for solving quantum many-body problems, which has been applied to spin liquids, Fermi gases, the Bose-Hubbard model, atomic systems, and light nuclei. The key innovation over prior neural quantum state architectures is the use of normalizing flows, which provide efficient parallel sampling from the wavefunction probability distribution, mitigating a major computational cost. The authors note that autoregressive networks (PhysRevLett.124.020503) were previously proposed to address this sampling challenge. The error analysis procedure for time-evolution builds on the method of Gutierrez and Mendl (2022), which removed the need for pseudo-inverse computations and allowed larger time-steps. The work enables future applications to systems with fermionic statistics (extending FermiNet of Pfau et al., 2020) and to wavefunctions with winding numbers, which the current architecture cannot represent.
