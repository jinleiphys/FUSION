# Consistent chiral limit in on-shell renormalized quark-meson model with ChPT scaling

## Key claim

Using ChPT-predicted scaling of pion and kaon decay constants and the sum of squared eta masses, the renormalized 2+1 flavor quark-meson (RQM) model achieves a consistent path to the chiral limit without heuristic parameter adjustment, but only when the on-shell renormalization of parameters and the quark one-loop vacuum fluctuations are treated consistently; the resulting first-order chiral transition region in the Columbia plot is larger than in a recent functional renormalization group study, whose softening effect from vacuum fluctuations appears overestimated.

## Method

The authors use the on-shell renormalized quark-meson model with 2+1 flavors, where the vacuum effective potential is constructed after canceling divergences in the MS-bar scheme and relating counter-terms to the on-shell scheme, using pole masses of pseudoscalar mesons and the sigma meson as input. The chiral limit path is defined by scaling both pion and kaon masses by a common factor gamma, with the corresponding decay constants and the quantity M_eta^2 = m_eta^2 + m_eta'^2 determined from ChPT at O(1/f^2). The grand potential is minimized to obtain the temperature and chemical potential dependence of the nonstrange and strange condensates, and the order of the chiral transition is mapped in the m_pi-m_K and mu-m_K planes.

## Key numbers

- At the physical point (gamma=1), the pion curvature mass m_pi,c = 135.95 MeV is 2.05 MeV smaller than its pole mass m_pi = 138 MeV; the kaon curvature mass m_K,c = 467.99 MeV is 28.01 MeV smaller than its pole mass m_K = 496 MeV.
- The tricritical point in the m_pi-m_K plane at mu=0 occurs at m_K^TCP = 242.6 MeV (with U_A(1) anomaly included).
- The critical pion mass for the SU(3) symmetric chiral limit path (m_pi = m_K) is m_pi^c = 134.16 MeV.
- The terminal pion mass on the strange chiral limit line is m_pi^t = 168.39 MeV.
- For the light chiral limit (m_pi=0) at mu=0, the transition is second order for m_K >= 496 MeV and first order below m_K^TCP = 242.6 MeV.
- The critical end point at physical masses occurs at mu_CEP = 262.8 MeV.
- Without the U_A(1) anomaly, the first-order region disappears entirely in the m_pi-m_K plane at mu=0.
- The chiral limit values: f_pi = 88 + 7.283e-6 MeV, f_K = 88 + 16.574e-6 MeV for gamma = 2.02e-4, with m_pi* = 0.1 MeV and m_K* = 0.028 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the renormalized quark-meson model developed in Tiwari (2022) and Rai and Tiwari (2024) by incorporating ChPT scaling of decay constants and eta masses, following the approach of Herpay and Kovacs (2005) for the tree-level parameter fixing. It contrasts with the fixed-UV scheme used in earlier QM model studies by Schaefer and Wagner (2009) and with the fixed-f_pi scheme of the FRG study by Resch et al., where heuristic adjustment of the UV scale is required to retain spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The results show that the first-order region is larger than in the FRG study of Resch et al., and the softening effect of quark one-loop vacuum fluctuations is more moderate than in that work, consistent with the authors' earlier findings that the on-shell renormalization gives a stronger U_A(1) anomaly strength and weaker explicit symmetry breaking strengths.
