# Hard probes of non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma

> Jets and photons could play an important role in finding the transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma. To this end we analyze their interaction with a non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma. Using new field-theoretical tools we derive two-point correlators for the plasma which show how instabilities evolve in time. This allows us, for the first time, to derive finite rates of interaction with the medium. We furthermore show that coherent, long-wavelength instability fields in the Abelian limit do not modify the rate of photon emission or jet-medium interaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that coherent, long-wavelength instability fields in the Abelian limit do not modify the rate of photon emission or jet-medium interaction, but only under the condition that the anisotropy parameter \(\xi\) is much smaller than the coupling constant \(g\) (\(\xi \ll g\)), ensuring a kinetic theory description where the instability grows slower than the typical time for medium-induced bremsstrahlung.

## Method

The authors derive two-point correlators for a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma using non-equilibrium quantum field theory, starting from coupled Boltzmann and Yang-Mills equations for quasiparticles and long-wavelength background fields. They assume a slightly anisotropic initial momentum distribution at time \(t_0 = 0\) with \(\xi \ll g\), then compute the retarded propagator and the \(rr\) correlator (density of field modes) to evaluate medium-induced bremsstrahlung rates. For the Abelian limit, they expand in the small parameter \(k\Delta t \sim \xi/g \ll 1\) and resum diagrams with crossed gluon rungs from the coherent background field.

## Key numbers

- The growth rate of instabilities is \(\gamma \sim \xi g \Lambda\), where \(\xi\) is the anisotropy of the momentum distribution and \(\Lambda\) is the quasiparticle energy scale.
- The condition for kinetic theory validity is \(\xi \ll g\), ensuring the instability grows slower than the typical bremsstrahlung time scale \(1/g^2\Lambda\).
- The fluctuating field poles are at energy scale \(g\Lambda\), while the coherent background field operates at energy scale \(\xi g \Lambda\).
- The expansion parameter for the Abelian calculation is \(k\Delta t \sim \xi/g \ll 1\).

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory framework of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY) for jet and photon interactions in equilibrium QGP to non-equilibrium plasmas with Weibel instabilities, as previously studied by Romatschke and Strickland (2003) and Mrowczynski (2016). The authors resolve a long-standing divergence in naive non-equilibrium rate calculations by properly accounting for the finite initial time of instability growth. This enables future calculations of jet-medium interaction and photon emission rates in non-equilibrium QGP, which can be used to extract transport coefficients from heavy-ion collision data at RHIC and LHC.
