# Toward a simultaneous description of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ for heavy quarks

> The two key observables related to heavy quarks that have been measured in RHIC and LHC energies are the nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$. Simultaneous description of these two observables is a top challenge for all the existing models. We have highlighted how a consistent combination of four ingredients i.e the temperature dependence of the energy loss, full solution of the Boltzmann collision integral for the momentum evolution of heavy quark, hadronization by coalescence, then the hadronic rescattering, responsible to address a large part of such a puzzle. We have considered four different models to evaluate the temperature dependence of drag coefficients of the heavy quark in QGP. All these four different models are set to reproduce the same $R_{AA}$ as of the experiments. We have shown that for the same $R_{AA}$, the $v_2$ could be quite different depending on the interaction dynamics as well as other ingredients.

# Toward a simultaneous description of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ for heavy quarks

## Key claim

For the same nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ as measured in experiments, the elliptic flow $v_2$ can be quite different depending on the temperature dependence of the drag coefficient, the use of full Boltzmann dynamics versus Fokker-Planck approximation, hadronization by coalescence, and hadronic rescattering. The paper demonstrates that a consistent combination of these four ingredients is responsible for addressing a large part of the simultaneous description puzzle, but does not claim to have achieved a full simultaneous description.

## Method

The authors solve the Fokker-Planck equation stochastically via the Langevin equation for heavy quark propagation in a QGP fireball at RHIC energy (Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, initial central temperature $T_i=340$ MeV, initial time $\tau_i=0.6$ fm/c). They consider four different models for the temperature dependence of the drag coefficient: pQCD with Debye screening, AdS/CFT with $\Gamma_{conf}=C T_{QCD}^2/M_{HQ}$, a quasi-particle model (QPM) with temperature-dependent masses and running coupling, and a massless QPM variant. Each model is rescaled to reproduce the same $R_{AA}$ as experiment. The study also examines the effect of full Boltzmann dynamics versus Fokker-Planck, hadronization by coalescence, and hadronic rescattering of D mesons in a medium of pions, kaons, and eta treated via chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- RHIC energy: Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, with initial central temperature $T_i=340$ MeV and initial time $\tau_i=0.6$ fm/c
- Drag coefficients are compared at $p_T=100$ MeV across the four models
- The hadronic medium enhances $v_2$ by approximately $20\%$ while leaving $R_{AA}$ almost unchanged
- $R_{AA}$ saturates within 3-4 fm of evolution, while $v_2$ develops at later stages near $T_c$
- AdS/CFT drag coefficient parameter: $C=2.1\pm0.5$ in $\Gamma_{conf}=C T_{QCD}^2/M_{HQ}$
- QPM coupling parameters: $\lambda=2.6$ and $T/T_s=0.57$ in $g^2(T)=\frac{48\pi^2}{[(11N_c-2N_f)ln[\lambda(\frac{T}{T_c}-\frac{T_s}{T_c})]^2}$

## Context

This work extends prior efforts using Fokker-Planck approaches (Rapp and collaborators, 2000s; Das and collaborators, 2015) and relativistic Boltzmann approaches (Gossiaux and collaborators; Scardina and collaborators, 2010s) that showed difficulties describing both $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ simultaneously. The paper builds on the finding that $R_{AA}$ is sensitive to the drag coefficient at early times (high temperature) while $v_2$ is sensitive to the drag near $T_c$, making the temperature dependence of the interaction a key factor. It enables future studies that incorporate radiative processes and pre-equilibrium phase effects (Das, 2015) within a single framework to further resolve the simultaneous description puzzle.
