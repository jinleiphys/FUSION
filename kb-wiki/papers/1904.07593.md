# Spontaneous generation of spin current from the vacuum by strong electric fields

> We discuss spontaneous spin current generation from the vacuum by strong electric fields as a result of interplay between the Schwinger mechanism and a spin-orbit coupling. By considering a homogeneous slow strong electric field superimposed by a fast weak transverse electric field, we explicitly evaluate the vacuum expectation value of a spin current (the Bargmann-Wigner spin current) by numerically solving the Dirac equation. We show that a non-vanishing spin current polarized in the direction perpendicular to the electric fields flows mostly in the longitudinal direction. We also find that a relativistic effect due to the helicity conservation affects direction/polarization of spin current.

## Key claim

Spontaneous spin current can be generated from the vacuum by superimposing a homogeneous slow strong electric field with a fast weak transverse electric field, provided the relative angle between the total electric field and the induced U(1) charge current is non-zero, which requires the frequency of the transverse field to be fast enough that the current lags behind the field direction.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Dirac equation in momentum space for a QED system with a time-dependent external electromagnetic potential consisting of a strong constant field along the z-direction and a weak oscillating field along the x-direction. They computed the in-vacuum expectation value of the Bargmann-Wigner spin current operator at asymptotic times using the canonical operator formalism, regularizing by subtracting the out-vacuum contribution to remove ultraviolet divergences.

## Key numbers

- The strong field strength is set to \( e\bar{E}/m^2 = 1 \), the weak field strength to \( e\mathcal{E}/m^2 = 0.2 \), and the frequency to \( \Omega/m = 1 \) for the main numerical results.
- The largest spin current component is \( (J_{s^y})^z \), polarized in the y-direction and flowing in the z-direction.
- The spin current is exponentially suppressed by mass as \( N \propto \exp[-\pi m^2/e\bar{E}] \), requiring \( e\bar{E} \gtrsim m^2 \) for observable effects.
- The spin current vanishes as \( \Omega \to 0 \) and increases with increasing frequency \( \Omega \).
- The ratio \( (J_{s^z})^y / (J_{s^y})^z \) approaches zero in the massless limit due to helicity conservation.

## Context

This work extends the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism (Schutzhold et al. 2008, Dunne et al. 2009, Monin and Voloshin 2010) by adding spin dynamics, which had been neglected in previous studies of pair production. It contrasts with conventional spin current generation mechanisms such as the spin-Hall effect (Dyakonov and Perel 1971, Hirsch 1999) and the Rashba-Edelstein effect (Edelstein 1990), which require pre-existing spin carriers or a Fermi surface, whereas this mechanism works from the vacuum. The results suggest new observables for upcoming laser experiments (ELI, HiPER) and may apply to condensed matter systems such as Dirac semi-metals and graphene.
