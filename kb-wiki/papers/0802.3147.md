# Continued fraction approximation for the nuclear matter response function

> We use a continued fraction approximation to calculate the RPA response function of nuclear matter. The convergence of the approximation is assessed by comparing with the numerically exact response function obtained with a typical effective finite-range interaction used in nuclear physics. It is shown that just the first order term of the expansion can give reliable results at densities up to the saturation density value.

## Key claim

The continued fraction approximation for the RPA response function converges rapidly for the Gogny D1 interaction, with first-order terms giving reliable results at densities up to saturation density, but convergence deteriorates at higher densities (e.g., 2ρ₀) where second or higher orders become necessary.

## Method

The authors applied a continued fraction expansion to the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole Green's function in infinite symmetric nuclear matter, defining an effective interaction V_eff as a continued fraction whose terms V_i are obtained by expanding the RPA response in powers of V_i χ_HF and matching to the iterative solution. They used a multipole expansion of both the HF propagator and the particle-hole interaction to reduce the angular integrations, and compared the continued fraction results at first and second order to numerically exact RPA calculations with the finite-range Gogny D1 force at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer values used: q = 27 MeV and q = 270 MeV (approximately k_F/10 and k_F respectively).
- At saturation density ρ₀ and q = 27 MeV, the first-order approximation (V_1) reliably reproduces the exact RPA strength for all spin-isospin channels except (S,T) = (0,0), where second order (V_2) is required.
- At density ρ = 2ρ₀ in the (0,0) channel, V_2 χ_HF is no longer small compared to V_1 χ_HF, requiring at least third-order terms for reliable results.
- Direct and exchange monopole contributions (ℓ=0) at the Fermi surface for ρ = ρ₀: in the (0,0) channel, direct term = 885 MeV·fm⁻³ (q=27 MeV) and 1129 MeV·fm⁻³ (q=135 MeV), exchange term = -1147 MeV·fm⁻³ (both q values).

## Context

This work extends the continued fraction method for response functions (Schuck 1989; Pace 1998) to finite-range Gogny-type interactions (Gogny 1975), which are computationally heavier than zero-range Skyrme forces (Vautherin 1972; Garcilazo 1992) for RPA calculations. The method enables self-consistent RPA calculations with finite-range forces in finite nuclei without diagonalizing large configuration-space matrices (Blaizot 1977; Peru 2005), and offers a simpler way to evaluate neutrino propagation in dense matter such as neutron stars.
