# Deuteron-alpha scattering: separable vs nonseparable Faddeev approach

> {\bf Background} Deuteron induced reactions are widely used to probe nuclear structure and astrophysical information. Those (d,p) reactions may be viewed as three-body reactions and described with Faddeev techniques. {\bf Purpose} Faddeev-AGS equations in momentum space have a long tradition of utilizing separable interactions in order to arrive at sets of coupled integral equations in one variable. However, it needs to be demonstrated that observables calculated based on separable interactions agree exactly with those based on nonseparable forces. {\bf Methods} Momentum space AGS equations are solved with separable and nonseparable forces as coupled integral equations. {\bf Results} Deuteron-alpha scattering is calculated via momentum space AGS equations using the CD-Bonn neutron-proton force and a Woods-Saxon type neutron(proton)-$^4$He force, for which the Pauli-forbidden S-wave bound state is projected out. Elastic as well as breakup observables are calculated and compared to results in which the interactions in the two-body sub-systems are represented by separable interactions derived in the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) framework. {\bf Conclusions} We find that the calculations based on the separable representation of the interactions and the original interactions give results that are in excellent agreement. Specifically, integrated cross sections and angular distributions for elastic scattering agree within $\approx$ 1\%, which is well below typical experimental errors. In addition, the five-fold differential cross sections corresponding to breakup of the deuteron agree extremely well.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that solving the Faddeev-AGS equations for deuteron-alpha scattering using separable interactions (via the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler framework) yields elastic and breakup observables that agree within approximately 1% with calculations using the original nonseparable forces, provided the separable expansion is sufficiently converged (rank-4 to rank-6 depending on the observable).

## Method

Momentum-space Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations were solved as coupled integral equations for deuteron-alpha scattering at three beam energies (10, 20, and 50 MeV). The two-body interactions used were the CD-Bonn neutron-proton force and a Woods-Saxon type neutron(proton)-⁴He force (Bang potential) with the Pauli-forbidden S-wave bound state projected out. Separable representations of these interactions were constructed using the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) framework with ranks ranging from 3 to 7, and the resulting observables were compared to calculations using the original nonseparable interactions solved via the method of Deltuva and Fonseca (2006).

## Key numbers

- Integrated cross sections for elastic scattering and breakup at 10 and 20 MeV deuteron beam energy agree within ≈ 1% between separable and nonseparable calculations when using rank-6 separable potentials.
- For rank-4 separable potentials, the discrepancy between separable and nonseparable integrated cross sections is already better than ≈ 1.5%.
- Elastic differential cross sections at 10, 20, and 50 MeV show relative differences of ≈ 1% throughout the angular range between converged separable and nonseparable calculations.
- Five-fold differential breakup cross sections at 10 and 20 MeV show that rank-4 separable potentials already yield results in very good agreement with the nonseparable approach, while rank-3 potentials are inadequate for this observable.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark of separable interactions in Faddeev-type calculations from the three-body bound state of ⁶Li (Hlophe 2017) to scattering observables, and is consistent with earlier findings for neutron-deuteron scattering (Cornelius 1990) where rank-3 EST potentials reproduced integrated cross sections to about 2%. The study is a necessary step toward implementing the AGS equations in the Coulomb basis as proposed by Mukhamedzhanov and collaborators (2012), which would enable applications to deuteron-induced reactions on heavier targets beyond the mass A ≤ 58 limit of the screening method used by Deltuva and Fonseca (2006, 2009).
