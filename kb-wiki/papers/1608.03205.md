# Event-shape-engineering study of charge separation in heavy-ion collisions

> Recent measurements of charge-dependent azimuthal correlations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions have indicated charge-separation signals perpendicular to the reaction plane, and have been related to the chiral magnetic effect (CME). However, the correlation signal is contaminated with the background caused by the collective motion (flow) of the collision system, and an effective approach is needed to remove the flow background from the correlation. We present a method study with simplified Monte Carlo simulations and a multi-phase transport model, and develop a scheme to reveal the true CME signal via the event-shape engineering with the flow vector of the particles of interest.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the event-shape-engineering (ESE) approach using the squared magnitude of the flow vector (\(q^2\)) as the event-shape handle can remove flow-related backgrounds from charge-separation observables, but the extracted signal at zero \(q^2\) must be corrected by a factor of \(1+2v_2\) to recover the true event-ensemble-averaged chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal.

## Method

The authors performed simplified Monte Carlo simulations with fixed input \(v_2 = 5\%\) and \(|a_{1,\pm}| = 2\%\) (no non-flow effects) and multi-phase transport model (AMPT) simulations of 10 million Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV (which contain flow backgrounds but no CME signal). They divided each event into sub-events by pseudorapidity, reconstructed the flow vector magnitude \(q\) and \(q^2\) for the particles of interest, and studied the dependence of the elliptic flow \(v_2\) and the three-point correlator \(\gamma \equiv \langle \langle \cos(\phi_\alpha + \phi_\beta - 2\Psi_{\rm RP}) \rangle \rangle\) on \(q^2\), using second-order polynomial fits to project \(\Delta\gamma = \gamma_{\rm OS} - \gamma_{\rm SS}\) to zero \(q^2\).

## Key numbers

* In AMPT simulations of 20–60% Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV, the intercept of \(N_{\rm part}\Delta\gamma^{\rm A}\) at \(q^2 = 0\) was \((-4.5 \pm 6.7) \times 10^{-4}\), consistent with zero, demonstrating background disappearance.
* In the same AMPT simulation, the intercept of the corrected observable \(N_{\rm part}\Delta\gamma^{\rm observe}/R^{\rm B1(B2)}\) at \(q^2 = 0\) was \((-3.3 \pm 10.6) \times 10^{-4}\), also consistent with zero.
* In simplified Monte Carlo simulations with input \(v_2 = 5\%\) and \(|a_{1,\pm}| = 2\%\), the event-ensemble average of \(\gamma_{\rm SS}\) was \(-4 \times 10^{-4}\) and of \(\gamma_{\rm OS}\) was \(4 \times 10^{-4}\), but the apparent value at zero \(q^2\) was exaggerated by a relative \(2v_2\) (10% in this case).

## Context

This work extends previous experimental measurements of charge-dependent azimuthal correlations by the STAR and ALICE Collaborations (LPV_STAR1–LPV_STAR5, LPV_ALICE, LPV_UU) and theoretical studies of flow backgrounds from Pratt (2010), Pratt and Sorensen (2011), and others. The paper identifies shortcomings in an earlier ESE attempt using event-by-event observed \(v_2\) (LPV_STAR5) and proposes a corrected scheme using \(q^2\) as the event-shape handle, which enables future experimental extraction of the true CME signal after removing flow backgrounds and correcting for the artificial correlation between \(\gamma\) and \(v_2\).
