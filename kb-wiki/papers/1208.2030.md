# Long range order in gauge theories. Deformed QCD as a toy model

> We study a number of different ingredients, related to long range order observed in lattice QCD simulations, using a simple "deformed QCD" model. This model is a weakly coupled gauge theory, which however has all the relevant crucial elements allowing us to study difficult and nontrivial problems which are known to be present in real strongly coupled QCD. In the present study, we want to understand the physics of long range order in form of coherent low dimensional vacuum configurations observed in Monte Carlo lattice simulations. We demonstrate the presence of double-layer domain wall structures in the deformed QCD, and study their interaction with localized topological monopoles. Furthermore, we show that there is in fact an attractive interaction between the two, such that the monopole favors a position within the domain wall.

## Key claim

Using a weakly coupled "deformed QCD" model that preserves confinement and topological structure, the authors demonstrate that domain walls exhibit a double-layer structure of topological charge density and that monopoles experience an attractive interaction toward these domain walls, suggesting that topological charge originally localized on monopoles can spread out along extended surfaces.

## Method

The authors construct domain wall solutions from the sine-Gordon effective Lagrangian that emerges from the dual description of deformed QCD, where the theory is formulated on ℝ³ × S¹ with a center-stabilizing deformation potential. They compute the topological charge density and magnetic field distributions for the domain wall profile χ(z) = 4 arctan[exp(m_χ z)] and then study the interaction between these domain walls and fractionally charged monopoles using the operator formalism for monopole creation.

## Key numbers

- Topological charge of fundamental monopoles: Q = ±1/N, where N is the number of colors
- Domain wall tension: σ = m_χ/L² · (g/2π)² ∼ √(ζ/L³), where ζ is monopole fugacity and L is the compactification length
- Mass of the χ field: m_χ = 2m_σ, where m_σ² = Lζ(2π/g)² is the Debye correlation length
- Average number of monopoles in a Debye volume: 𝒩 = m_σ⁻³ζ = (g/2π)³/√(L³ζ) ≫ 1

## Context

This work extends the deformed QCD framework developed by Yaffe (2008) to study long-range order phenomena observed in lattice QCD simulations by Horvath et al. (2003-2005) and Ilgenfritz et al. (2007-2008), which revealed coherent low-dimensional sheets of topological charge. The authors build on earlier studies of monopole-domain wall attraction in cosmological contexts by Dvali (1997) and apply similar ideas to Euclidean configurations describing tunnelling processes. The results enable a semiclassical understanding of how topological charge density can become delocalized from point-like instantons onto extended surfaces, potentially connecting to holographic descriptions using D2 branes as proposed by Gorsky et al. (2007-2009) and Zhitnitsky (2011).
