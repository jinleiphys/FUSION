# Factorized QED and QCD Contribution to Deeply Inelastic Scattering

> We present the first calculation of next-to-leading order (NLO) factorized QED and QCD contributions to the short-distance hard coefficients of inclusive lepton-hadron deep inelastic scattering (DIS) in a joint QED and QCD factorization approach. Unlike the traditional radiative correction approach to handle the collision-induced QED contributions to DIS, QED radiation from all charged leptons and quarks are treated equally, and their collinear sensitivities are systematically factorized into corresponding universal lepton and parton distribution functions. We demonstrate that the NLO factorized QED contribution is completely infrared safe and calculable without the need of any parameters other than the standard factorization scale in the same way as the factorized QCD contribution. We discuss the potential impact of this joint factorization approach on the extraction of partonic information from lepton-hadron DIS.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete calculation of next-to-leading order (NLO) factorized QED and QCD contributions to the short-distance hard coefficients of inclusive lepton-hadron deep inelastic scattering (DIS) within a joint QED and QCD factorization framework, demonstrating that the NLO factorized QED contribution is completely infrared safe and calculable using only the standard factorization scale, without the need for additional parameters that traditional radiative correction approaches require.

## Method

The authors computed NLO QED and QCD corrections to the short-distance hard parts of the leading electron-quark subprocess, \(e+q\to e+X\), in inclusive DIS using a joint QED and QCD factorization formalism. Collinear-sensitive QED radiation from all charged leptons and quarks was systematically factorized into universal lepton distribution functions (LDFs) and lepton fragmentation functions (LFFs), while QCD radiation was factorized into standard parton distribution functions (PDFs). The NLO QED contributions were evaluated by calculating real and virtual Feynman diagrams at \({\cal O}(\alpha_{em}^3)\) and demonstrating cancellation of all collinear divergences, while NLO QCD contributions at \({\cal O}(\alpha_{em}^2\alpha_s)\) were obtained by applying the factorization formula to asymptotic partonic states and expanding order-by-order in the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

* The lowest order hard part for the subprocess \(e+q\to e+q\) is \(\widehat{H}^{(2,0)}_{eq\to eX} = e_q^2 (4\alpha_{em}^2) [(\hat{s}^2 + \hat{u}^2)/\hat{t}^2] \delta(\hat{s}+\hat{t}+\hat{u})\), where \(e_q\) is the quark electric charge fraction and \(\alpha_{em}\) is the fine-structure constant.
* The NLO QCD contribution to the hadronic tensor on a quark state includes a term proportional to \(C_F [(1+\hat{x}_B^2)[\ln(1-\hat{x}_B)/(1-\hat{x}_B)]_+ - (3/2)[1/(1-\hat{x}_B)]_+ - ((1+\hat{x}_B^2)/(1-\hat{x}_B))\ln(\hat{x}_B) + 3 - \hat{x}_B]\), where \(C_F\) is the QCD color factor and \(\hat{x}_B\) is the partonic Bjorken variable.
* The NLO QCD calculation yields a collinear divergence proportional to \((-1/\epsilon) P^{(0,1)}_{q/q}(\hat{x}_B)\) in \(d=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions, which is cancelled by factorization into PDFs, leaving infrared-safe hard parts.

## Context

This work extends the joint QED and QCD factorization approach proposed by Liu et al. (2021) by providing the first explicit NLO calculations of both QED and QCD contributions to the short-distance hard coefficients for inclusive DIS. Unlike traditional radiative correction approaches (Mo and Tsai, 1968; Bardin et al., 1989; Badelek et al., 1994; Kripfganz et al., 1990; Spiesberger, 1994; Blumlein, 2002) that require introducing unknown parameters to handle collinear photon radiation, this joint factorization systematically absorbs collinear sensitivities into universal LDFs and LFFs. The results enable more precise extraction of partonic information from lepton-hadron DIS experiments at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider, and provide a foundation for extending the approach to semi-inclusive DIS and other processes where QED radiation can alter azimuthal angular distributions used to isolate transverse momentum dependent PDFs.
