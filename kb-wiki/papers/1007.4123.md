# Study of $^{6}$He+$^{12}$C Elastic Scattering Using a Microscopic Optical Potential

> The $^6$He+$^{12}$C elastic scattering data at beam energies of 3, 38.3 and 41.6 MeV/nucleon are studied utilizing the microscopic optical potentials obtained by a double-folding procedure and also by using those inherent in the high-energy approximation. The calculated optical potentials are based on the neutron and proton density distributions of colliding nuclei established in an appropriate model for $^6$He and obtained from the electron scattering form factors for $^{12}$C. The depths of the real and imaginary parts of the microscopic optical potentials are considered as fitting parameters. At low energy the volume optical potentials reproduce sufficiently well the experimental data. At higher energies, generally, additional surface terms having form of a derivative of the imaginary part of the microscopic optical potential are needed. The problem of ambiguity of adjusted optical potentials is resolved requiring the respective volume integrals to obey the determined dependence on the collision energy. Estimations of the Pauli blocking effects on the optical potentials and cross sections are also given and discussed. Conclusions on the role of the aforesaid effects and on the mechanism of the considered processes are made.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a microscopic optical potential combining a double-folding real part with an imaginary part derived from the high-energy approximation (Glauber theory) can describe $^{6}$He+$^{12}$C elastic scattering data across a wide energy range, but only if additional surface terms are added to the imaginary part at higher energies and the volume integrals of the fitted potentials are constrained to follow a physically reasonable energy dependence to resolve the ambiguity of multiple parameter sets.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential was calculated using a double-folding procedure with CDM3Y6 effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, including both direct and exchange terms, based on the large-scale shell model density for $^{6}$He and a symmetrized Fermi-type density for $^{12}$C. The imaginary part was taken either as the same folded form or from the high-energy approximation (HEA) formalism, with the depths of both real and imaginary parts treated as fitting parameters ($N_R$ and $N_I$). At higher energies (38.3 and 41.6 MeV/nucleon), surface terms proportional to the derivative of the imaginary potential were added, and the volume integrals $J_V$ and $J_W$ were required to follow a systematic energy dependence to select among ambiguous parameter sets.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic optical potential approach from $^{6,8}$He+$p$ scattering (Lukyanov 2007, 2009) to a heavier target, $^{12}$C, where both projectile and target densities contribute. It addresses the ambiguity problem noted in phenomenological analyses by Milin (2004) and Kucuk (2009), who obtained different Woods-Saxon parameters for the same data, and by Lapoux (2002) and El-Azab (2008) who used semi-microscopic models with phenomenological imaginary parts. The method enables a more constrained extraction of dynamic polarization potentials that may reflect breakup channel effects, and the results can be compared with coupled-channel calculations by Khalili (1996) and Matsumoto (2009) that explicitly include breakup channels.
