# Hadron Spectra, Decays and Scattering Properties within Basis Light Front Quantization

> We survey recent progress in calculating properties of the electron and hadrons within the Basis Light Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach. We include applications to electromagnetic and strong scattering processes in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We present an initial investigation into the glueball states by applying BLFQ with multigluon sectors, introducing future research possibilities on multi-quark and multi-gluon systems.

## Key claim

The paper surveys recent progress in the Basis Light Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach for calculating hadron properties, but the results for light mesons, baryons, and glueballs are presented as initial investigations or demonstrations that require further development, particularly noting that the light meson spectra lack chiral symmetry breaking and the glueball sector is restricted to a small basis space of Nmax = Lmax = 6.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front mass eigenvalue problem by diagonalizing an effective Hamiltonian expressed in a basis of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator states for transverse modes and discretized plane waves for longitudinal modes. For heavy quarkonium, they employ a Hamiltonian incorporating quark masses, transverse and longitudinal confinement terms, and one-gluon exchange with either fixed or running coupling. For baryons, they generalize this approach to three unequal-mass quarks using Jacobi coordinates, and for glueballs they include multiple gluon Fock sectors with color singlet projection.

## Key numbers

- Ground state B_c meson mass: 6.258 GeV from BLFQ, compared to experimental 6.2749(8) GeV and lattice result 6.304(12) GeV
- Charmonium rms radii: eta_c = 0.170 fm and J/psi = 0.175 fm from BLFQ with running coupling at Nmax = 32
- Charmonium rms radii: eta_c = 0.199 fm and J/psi = 0.212 fm from BLFQ with fixed coupling at Nmax = 24
- Baryon model parameters: equal quark masses m_j = 0.35 GeV, confining strength kappa = 0.49 GeV
- Proton charge radius from BLFQ baryon model: 0.9 fm
- Glueball basis space: Nmax = Lmax = 6, Fock space includes 2 through 6 gluons, Hamiltonian matrix dimension of 1987 after color singlet restriction
- Color charge density for CGC scattering: g^2 mu = 0.5 GeV, infrared scale Lambda = 0.1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ framework previously applied to QED problems such as the electron anomalous magnetic moment (Honkanen 2010, Zhao 2014) and positronium spectrum (Wiecki 2014) into QCD hadron physics. The heavy quarkonium results build on earlier BLFQ studies (Li 2015, Li 2017) and are compared with lattice QCD calculations (Dudek 2006, Dudek 2009, Becirevic 2014, Hughes 2015) and quark model results (Ebert 2002). The baryon results are compared with AdS/QCD predictions (Brodsky 2014) and experimental data from the Particle Data Group. The glueball investigation introduces multi-gluon Fock sectors for the first time in BLFQ, enabling future studies of multi-quark and multi-gluon systems.
