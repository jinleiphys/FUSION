# Electromagnetic radiation from Quark-Gluon Plasma at finite baryon density

> Using the Bayesian calibrated iEBE-MUSIC framework, we compute the production of electromagnetic radiation from hot hadronic matter at finite baryon density. Results for thermal photon and thermal dilepton yields are obtained by folding in-medium emission rates with posterior-sampled backgrounds evolved hydrodynamically. We consider different photon sources and analyze the collision-energy dependence of the thermal-to-prompt photon ratio. The sensitivity of the dilepton spectra to the pre-equilibrium stage is explored by considering different initialization procedures. Finally, we examine the impact on dilepton spectra of choosing parameter sets stemming from different Bayesian data analyses.

## Key claim

Using the Bayesian calibrated iEBE-MUSIC framework, thermal photon and thermal dilepton yields at finite baryon density are computed by folding in-medium emission rates with posterior-sampled hydrodynamic backgrounds; the results show that at low collision energies thermal photons dominate over prompt photons even at high transverse momentum, and that dilepton spectra in the intermediate mass region are sensitive to the pre-equilibrium stage and to the hotspot size in the initial state.

## Method

The spacetime evolution of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density is simulated using the iEBE-MUSIC framework, which combines 3D dynamical initial conditions with viscous fluid dynamics (MUSIC) and a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD). Model parameters are calibrated via Bayesian inference using hadronic data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. Thermal photon and thermal dilepton production are calculated by convolving in-medium emission rates with local fluid cell information (space-time volume, temperature, flow velocity, and baryon chemical potential), with the electromagnetic rates switching from QCD-based to hadronic-based at T = 160 MeV and the hadronic phase ending at T = 120 MeV; prompt photons are added using INCNLO based on NLO pQCD.

## Key numbers

- Direct photon yields are computed for 0–20% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, with prompt photons dominating at high pT and thermal emission dominating for pT less than about 2.5 GeV.
- The ratio of thermal to prompt photon yields is shown for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 14.6–200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 17.3 GeV in the 0–20% centrality class; for pT less than about 2.5 GeV, thermal photons dominate at all collision energies.
- For dilepton invariant-mass excess spectra in In+In collisions at 17.3 GeV (dN_ch/deta > 30), the default dynamical initialization deposits energy and momentum during tau = 0.5–1.5 fm/c; instantaneous initialization at tau = 0.5 fm/c produces more dileptons at large invariant mass, while initialization at tau = 1.5 fm/c limits emission in the intermediate mass region.
- Posterior 2 from a recent Bayesian analysis uses a switching energy density e_sw ~ 0.16 GeV/fm^3 and a larger hotspot size (0.3 fm vs. 0.1 fm for Posterior 1), resulting in a colder early-time medium and lower dilepton yield in the intermediate mass region.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian-calibrated iEBE-MUSIC framework (Jahan 2024) to compute electromagnetic radiation at finite baryon density, complementing prior studies that focused on hadronic observables. The results demonstrate that thermal dileptons are sensitive to the pre-equilibrium stage description and to the hotspot size in the initial state, as explored by comparing dynamical deposition with instantaneous initialization scenarios (Shen 2023). The findings motivate the inclusion of electromagnetic probes in future Bayesian analyses to improve constraints on QGP properties in the high-temperature regime, building on the Bayesian calibration approach of Jahan (2024) and Jahan (2025).
