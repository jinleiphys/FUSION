# Positron supercritical resonances and spontaneous positron creation in slow collisions of heavy nuclei

> We present a theoretical and computational study of positron supercritical resonances in systems consisting of two highly-charged bare nuclei. The resonance positions and widths depending on the internuclear separation are calculated with the help of the complex-scaling generalized pseudospectral method in modified prolate spheroidal coordinates. The results are applied to estimate the probability of spontaneous positron creation in slow U$^{92+}$--U$^{92+}$ and Cm$^{96+}$--Cm$^{96+}$ collisions.

# Positron supercritical resonances and spontaneous positron creation in slow collisions of heavy nuclei

## Key claim
Spontaneous positron creation in slow collisions of bare uranium and curium nuclei can be observed experimentally via impact-sensitive measurements, with the spontaneous mechanism contributing about 27% of total positron production in U-U collisions and becoming dominant at about 78% in Cm-Cm collisions, provided that only collisions with small impact parameters (and large scattering angles) are selected. The authors qualify that the total pair-creation probability for a given trajectory does not exhibit threshold effects when crossing from subcritical to supercritical regime, so the spontaneous contribution must be estimated by integrating the resonance width over the supercritical regime along the nuclear trajectory.

## Method
The authors solve the time-independent Dirac equation for a one-positron system with two identical bare nuclei using the complex-scaling generalized pseudospectral (GPS) method in modified prolate spheroidal coordinates. The nuclear potential is modeled with a Gaussian charge distribution, and complex energy eigenvalues of the form E = E_r - i Gamma/2 are obtained by diagonalizing non-Hermitian matrices with uniform complex scaling. The resonance widths Gamma(R) as functions of internuclear distance are then integrated over time along classical Rutherford scattering trajectories for head-on collisions with minimum internuclear distance R_min = 17.5 fm to obtain spontaneous positron creation probabilities.

## Key numbers
- Total charge number threshold for supercritical regime: larger than 173 (the combined charge of two nuclei must exceed this value for the 1s sigma_g state to cross into the upper continuum)
- For U-U system: the 1s sigma_g energy level crosses into the upper continuum at internuclear distance R approximately 35 fm; the 2p_1/2 sigma_u level would do so at R approximately 11 fm (unreachable as smaller than R_min = 17.5 fm)
- For Cm-Cm system: the 1s sigma_g level crosses into the upper continuum at R approximately 52 fm; the 2p_1/2 sigma_u level reaches the upper continuum at R approximately 20 fm
- Maximum spontaneous positron energy (from resonance position at closest approach): approximately 270 keV for U-U system; approximately 550 keV for Cm-Cm system
- Spontaneous contribution to total positron production in head-on collisions with R_min = 17.5 fm: about 27% for U-U system; about 78% for Cm-Cm system
- Nuclear root-mean-square radii used: 5.8571 fm for uranium isotope 238U; 5.8429 fm for curium isotope 244Cm (from Angeli 2013)
- Numerical grid parameters: 160 grid points for coordinate lambda, 16 grid points for coordinate eta, mapping parameter R_l = 1/Z_n, complex rotation angle Theta = 0.3

## Context
This work extends the St. Petersburg group's previous studies (Maltsev 2019, Shabaev 2019, Popov 2020, Popov 2023) which showed that signatures of the subcritical-to-supercritical transition appear in pair-creation probabilities and positron spectra for collisions along trajectories with a given minimum internuclear distance. The results agree with earlier calculations by Maltsev (2020) using a one-center approach and by Marsman and Horbatsch (2011), but the present method does not require searching for an optimal complex rotation angle. The Frankfurt group (Rein 2005) had concluded that vacuum decay could only be observed with nuclear sticking, which has not been evidenced; this work provides an alternative experimental path using coincidence schemes to select small-impact-parameter collisions. The approach enables quantitative estimation of spontaneous versus dynamical pair creation contributions in supercritical heavy-ion collisions.
