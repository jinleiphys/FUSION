# Hyperon suppression in hadron-quark mixed phase

> We investigate the property of the hadron-quark mixed phase using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model for hadron (hyperon) phase and the MIT bag model for quark phase. To satisfy the Gibbs conditions, charge density as well as baryon number density becomes non-uniform in the mixed phase, accompanying phase separation. We clarify the roles of the surface tension and the charge screening effect. We show that the screened Coulomb interaction tends to make the geometrical structure of the mixed phase less stable, and the resultant EOS becomes similar to the one given by the Maxwell construction. The composition of the mixed phase, however, is very different from that of the Maxwell construction; in particular, hyperons are completely suppressed in the mixed phase, because hadron phase is positively charged. This is a novel mechanism of hyperon suppression in compact stars.

# Hyperon suppression in hadron-quark mixed phase

## Key claim

Hyperons are completely suppressed in the hadron-quark mixed phase during the deconfinement transition in dense matter, because the hadron phase is positively charged and hyperons are hardly mixed in positively charged matter. This suppression occurs even though the resulting equation of state (EOS) is similar to that given by the Maxwell construction, and the effect depends on the interplay between surface tension and Coulomb screening.

## Method

The authors use the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) model for the hadron (hyperon) phase and the MIT bag model for the quark phase, with a surface tension parameter of 40 MeV/fm². They employ the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation with spherical (3D), cylindrical (2D), and slab (1D) geometries to describe pasta structures, solving the Gibbs conditions with finite-size effects including Coulomb interaction and surface tension. The optimum cell configuration, cell size, lump size, and density profiles are found by minimizing the total energy density for a given baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The mixed phase exists between baryon density ρ_B = 0.326 fm⁻³ (quark droplet onset) and ρ_B = 0.666 fm⁻³ (quark bubble termination).
- The bag constant is set to B = 100 MeV/fm³ with α_s = 0; the strange quark mass is m_s = 150 MeV.
- The surface tension parameter used is τ_surf = 40 MeV/fm²; the mixed phase EOS coincides with the Maxwell construction for τ_surf ≈ 60 MeV/fm².
- The maximum mass of a hybrid star is around 1.5 M_⊙, larger than the purely hyperonic star maximum of ≈1.3 M_⊙.
- Hyperons (Σ⁻) appear in charge-neutral hadronic matter at ρ_B = 0.34 fm⁻³, but are completely suppressed in the mixed phase up to very high density.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the hadron-quark mixed phase with pasta structures (Ravenhall et al.; Maruyama et al.) by incorporating hyperons and using a microscopic BHF approach for the hadronic phase. It contrasts with the bulk Gibbs calculation (Glendenning 1992), which predicts a broad mixed-phase region, and with the Maxwell construction, which yields a similar EOS but different composition. The results have implications for neutrino opacity via coherent scattering off lumps (Reddy et al.) and for thermal evolution of compact stars through hyperon Urca processes (Pethick et al.; Tatsumi et al.).
