# Energy--level splitting in antiprotonic helium atoms

> Recent experiments on the laser-induced resonant annihilation provide a precise measurement of transition energies of antiprotonic helium atoms. Relativistic corrections of an order of $α^2$ to the pure Coulomb interaction will be taken into account in the theoretical description of energy spectra of antiprotonic helium atoms. The splitting of energy levels arising due to the spin-dependent part of the relativistic interaction is considered for a number of states of $^{3,4}\! H\! e\bar pe$ systems.

## Key claim
Relativistic corrections of order α² to the pure Coulomb interaction produce a spin-dependent splitting of energy levels in antiprotonic helium atoms; the largest splitting arises from the interaction with the electron spin, while the interaction with heavy-particle spins gives a much smaller secondary splitting.

## Method
The author used a variational method with up to 600 trial functions containing up to 15 bispherical harmonics to solve the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation for the three-body system (helium nucleus, antiproton, electron). The energy-level splitting due to the spin-dependent part of the Breit interaction was then computed in first-order perturbation theory using the variational wave functions. Calculations were performed for states of the ³He\(\bar p\)e and ⁴He\(\bar p\)e systems in the range of total angular momenta L = 31–37.

## Key numbers
- Splitting values ΔE_LN for the ⁴He\(\bar p\)e system range from −1.10 × 10⁻⁶ au (L=32, N=1) to −0.81 × 10⁻⁶ au (L=37, N=5).
- Splitting values ΔE_LN for the ³He\(\bar p\)e system range from −1.20 × 10⁻⁶ au (L=31, N=1) to −0.81 × 10⁻⁶ au (L=36, N=4).
- Spectral line splitting Δν for the transition (33,1) → (32,3) in ⁴He\(\bar p\)e is −0.92 GHz; for (34,1) → (33,3) it is −0.86 GHz.
- Spectral line splitting Δν for the transition (33,2) → (32,4) in ³He\(\bar p\)e is −1.22 GHz; for (34,2) → (33,4) it is −1.35 GHz.
- The contribution of the last two terms in the splitting interaction (electron magnetic moment interacting with heavy-particle magnetic field) to ΔE_LN for ⁴He\(\bar p\)e ranges from −1.41 × 10⁻⁶ au (L=32, N=1) to −0.95 × 10⁻⁶ au (L=37, N=5).
- The experimental resolution in current experiments is about 10 GHz and can be improved to 1 GHz (as noted in the paper).

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical calculations on antiprotonic helium atoms (Kartavtsev 1995) by including relativistic corrections of order α² to the pure Coulomb interaction, which are necessary to match the precision of recent laser-induced resonant annihilation experiments (Morita 1994, Hayano 1994, Maas 1994). The calculated splitting values are of order 1 GHz, making them measurable with improved experimental resolution, and the results enable tests of antiproton properties (mass, charge, magnetic moment) and fundamental symmetry principles. The paper specifically identifies unfavoured transitions (L,N) → (L−1,N+2) as the most promising for experimental observation of the splitting.
