# Qubit regularization of asymptotic freedom

> We provide strong evidence that the asymptotically free (1+1)-dimensional non-linear O(3) sigma model can be regularized using a quantum lattice Hamiltonian, referred to as the "Heisenberg-comb", that acts on a Hilbert space with only two qubits per spatial lattice site. The Heisenberg-comb consists of a spin-half anti-ferromagnetic Heisenberg-chain coupled anti-ferromagnetically to a second local spin-half particle at every lattice site. Using a world-line Monte Carlo method we show that the model reproduces the universal step-scaling function of the traditional model up to correlation lengths of 200,000 in lattice units and argue how the continuum limit could emerge. We provide a quantum circuit description of time-evolution of the model and argue that near-term quantum computers may suffice to demonstrate asymptotic freedom.

## Key claim

The paper provides strong evidence that the asymptotically free (1+1)-dimensional nonlinear O(3) sigma model can be regularized using a quantum lattice Hamiltonian (the "Heisenberg comb") that acts on a Hilbert space with only two qubits per spatial lattice site, but the continuum limit emerges only in the limit where the ratio of coupling constants J/J_p goes to infinity, meaning the asymptotically free critical point is recovered only as J approaches infinity.

## Method

The authors constructed a spin-half ladder Hamiltonian called the "Heisenberg comb" consisting of an antiferromagnetic Heisenberg chain coupled antiferromagnetically to a second local spin-half particle at every lattice site, with Hamiltonian H = sum_i [J_p H_{(i,1),(i,2)} + J H_{(i,1),(i+1,1)}]. They computed the finite-size correlation length xi(L) using continuous-time world-line Monte Carlo methods for lattice sizes between 12 and 512, and extracted the universal step-scaling function F(z) = xi(2L)/xi(L) as a function of z = xi(L)/L. The inverse temperature beta was tuned as a function of L to restore space-time rotational symmetry by matching spatial and temporal correlation functions.

## Key numbers

* The Heisenberg comb reproduces the universal step-scaling function of the traditional model up to correlation lengths of 200,000 in lattice units (achieved at J=10).
* For J=3, 5, and 10, the minimum lattice size L_min at which universal physics emerges is approximately 30, 100, and 400 lattice units respectively, with corresponding correlation lengths xi of approximately 25, 600, and 200,000.
* The symmetric ladder (J1=J2=1) was studied at J_p = 0.10 and 0.20; the asymmetric ladder (J1=1, J2=0.5) at J_p = 0.05 and 0.10; the Heisenberg comb at J=3, 5, 10 with J_p=1.
* Lattice sizes studied ranged from 12 to 512, with fine-tuned beta values shown for each L.

## Context

This work extends the D-theory formulation of field theories introduced by Brower (1997) and Brower (2003), which proposed qubit regularization but required an extra spatial dimension whose size grows to recover asymptotic freedom. It contrasts with earlier attempts by Niedermayer (2016) using a Nienhuis-type action and by PhysRevD.99.074501 (2019) using truncated Hilbert space methods, both of which suggested that 2-qubit models might not reach the continuum limit. The paper demonstrates that the Heisenberg comb, unlike symmetric or asymmetric spin ladders, reproduces the step-scaling function previously computed for the traditional model by PhysRevLett.75.1891 (1995), and provides a quantum circuit implementation that may enable near-term quantum computers to demonstrate asymptotic freedom.
