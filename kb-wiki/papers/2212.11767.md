# Analysis of rescattering effects in $3π$ final states

> Decays into three particles are often described in terms of two-body resonances and a non-interacting spectator particle. To go beyond this simplest isobar model, crossed-channel rescattering effects need to be accounted for. We quantify the importance of these rescattering effects in three-pion systems for different decay masses and angular-momentum quantum numbers. We provide the amplitude decompositions for four decay processes with total $J^{PC} = 0^{--}$, $1^{--}$, $1^{-+}$, and $2^{++}$, all of which decay predominantly as $ρπ$ states. Two-pion rescattering is described in terms of an Omnès function, which incorporates the $ρ$ resonance. Inclusion of crossed-channel effects is achieved by solving the Khuri-Treiman integral equations. The unbinned log-likelihood estimator is used to determine the significance of the rescattering effects beyond two-body resonances; we compute the minimum number of events necessary to unambiguously find these in future Dalitz-plot analyses. Kinematic effects that enhance or dilute the rescattering are identified for the selected set of quantum numbers and various masses.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies how much experimental data (minimum number of Dalitz-plot events) is needed to detect crossed-channel rescattering effects beyond the simple isobar model in three-pion decays, for specific quantum numbers and decay masses, assuming the amplitude can be reduced to a single subtraction constant that serves as a mere normalization.

## Method

The authors decompose the decay amplitudes for four sets of quantum numbers (JPC = 0--, 1--, 1-+, 2++) into single-variable amplitudes using reconstruction theorems that retain only P-wave pi-pi interactions. Two-pion rescattering is described by an Omnes function incorporating the rho resonance, while full crossed-channel effects are included by solving the Khuri-Treiman integral equations iteratively. The unbinned log-likelihood estimator and Kullback-Leibler divergence are used to compare the Omnes (spectator) model to the full KT solution, computing the minimum number of events needed to reject the simpler model at a given confidence level.

## Key numbers

- For omega -> 3pi, the WASA-at-COSY dataset of 44,080 events is sensitive to rescattering effects only at 2.1 sigma.
- For omega -> 3pi, the BESIII dataset of 260,520 events reaches a 5 sigma level for the solution containing one subtraction.
- For phi -> 3pi, KLOE provides a Dalitz plot analysis using 2 x 10^6 events, and CMD-2 studied almost 8 x 10^4 decays; for both, rescattering effects are clearly observable.

## Context

This work extends previous Khuri-Treiman analyses of three-pion decays such as omega/phi -> 3pi (Niecknig 2012, Danilkin 2014, Dax 2018, JPAC 2020) and the general e+e- -> 3pi reaction (Hoferichter 2014, 2018, 2019). It also relates to studies of exotic 1-+ hybrids like the pi1(1600) (COMPASS 2018) and 0-- states predicted as hybrid mesons (Jaffe 1985, Dudek 2011). The paper enables experimentalists to determine the required statistics for future Dalitz-plot analyses to unambiguously detect rescattering effects, and identifies kinematic conditions that enhance or dilute these effects for different quantum numbers and masses.
