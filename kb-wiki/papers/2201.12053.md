# Unified nuclear matter EOSs constrained by the in-medium balance in density-dependent covariant density functionals

> Considering the effects of charge screening, we propose a new numerical recipe within the framework of Thomas-Fermi approximation, where the properties of nuclear matter throughout a vast density range can be obtained self-consistently. Assuming spherical and cylindrical approximations for the Wigner-Seitz cell, typical nuclear matter structures (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble, and uniform) are observed. We then investigate the EOSs and microscopic structures of nuclear matter with both fixed proton fractions and $β$-equilibration, where two covariant density functionals DD-LZ1 and DD-ME2 are adopted. Despite the smaller slope $L$ of symmetry energy obtained with the functional DD-LZ1, the curvature parameter $K_\mathrm{sym}$ is much larger than that of DD-ME2, which is attributed to the peculiar density-dependent behavior of meson-nucleon couplings guided by the restoration of pseudo-spin symmetry around the Fermi levels in finite nuclei. Consequently, different mass-radius relations of neutron stars are predicted by the two functionals. Different microscopic structures of nonuniform nuclear matter are obtained as well, which are expected to affect various physical processes in neutron star properties and evolutions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new numerical recipe within the Thomas-Fermi approximation that includes charge-screening effects to obtain nuclear matter properties self-consistently across a vast density range, but the results depend on the specific covariant density functional used (DD-LZ1 or DD-ME2), and the predicted neutron star mass-radius relations differ between the two functionals despite both being consistent with existing nuclear matter constraints around saturation density.

## Method

The authors adopt the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, using the Thomas-Fermi approximation at zero temperature to solve the Klein-Gordon equations for the mean fields within Wigner-Seitz cells. They impose spherical and cylindrical approximations to reduce the problem to one-dimensional differential equations, and search for the ground state among five types of nuclear pasta structures (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) and uniform matter by minimizing the energy per baryon at fixed average baryon number density and proton fraction.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for DD-LZ1: \(n_0 = 0.158\) fm\(^{-3}\); for DD-ME2: \(n_0 = 0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Binding energy at saturation for DD-LZ1: \(B = -16.06\) MeV; for DD-ME2: \(B = -16.13\) MeV
- Incompressibility for DD-LZ1: \(K = 230.7\) MeV; for DD-ME2: \(K = 250.8\) MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation for DD-LZ1: \(S = 32.0\) MeV; for DD-ME2: \(S = 32.3\) MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy for DD-LZ1: \(L = 42.5\) MeV; for DD-ME2: \(L = 51.2\) MeV
- Curvature parameter of symmetry energy for DD-LZ1: \(K_\mathrm{sym} = -20\) MeV; for DD-ME2: \(K_\mathrm{sym} = -87\) MeV
- Root-mean-square deviation from experimental binding energies for DD-LZ1: \(\Delta = 1.923\) MeV; for DD-ME2: \(\Delta = 2.400\) MeV
- Constraint from finite nuclei at \(n_\mathrm{on} = 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\): \(B_\mathrm{PNM} = 11.4 \pm 1.0\) MeV and \(B_\mathrm{SNM} = -14.1 \pm 0.1\) MeV (Brown 2013)

## Context

This work extends the unified treatment of nuclear matter equations of state using covariant density functionals, building on the RMF models of Typel (1999) and the DD-ME2 functional of Lalazissis (2005). The new DD-LZ1 functional, developed by Wei (2020) guided by pseudo-spin symmetry restoration, is compared with DD-ME2 to show how different density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling behaviors affect nuclear pasta structures and neutron star properties. The results enable more consistent calculations of neutron star crust and core properties, addressing the issue raised by Dinh Thi (2021) that using surface tension inconsistent with the bulk functional leads to underestimation of pasta property uncertainties.
