# Kaon production from 1 to 40 A GeV

> Kaon production is studied within the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model. Results are compared with experiment and with other models. The influence of the kaon potential on the kaon azimuthal distributions at SIS energies is considered. We also discuss the role of the many-body collisions at high-density phase of reaction.

## Key claim

The GiBUU model provides a better description of the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio at AGS-SPS energies than UrQMD or HSD, but still underpredicts the ratio at 10–30 A GeV due to overestimated pion production; the authors argue that neglecting many-body collisions, which become important at high densities, is the major limitation of standard transport calculations.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU transport model, which simulates heavy-ion collisions as a time-ordered sequence of binary collisions and resonance decays, with string-model treatment for high-energy collisions (\(\sqrt{s}>2.6\) GeV for baryon-baryon, \(>2\) GeV for meson-baryon). At SIS energies, strangeness production was treated perturbatively with mean-field potentials for baryons (soft momentum-dependent, compressibility \(K=215\) MeV) and kaons, using either the Brown-Rho (BR) or Ko-Li (KL) parametrization of the kaon single-particle energy. Kaon propagation was described by Hamiltonian equations of motion with in-medium thresholds for production cross sections.

## Key numbers

- \(K^+\) potential at zero momentum for BR parametrization: \(U_K(\rho_0) \simeq 30\) MeV.
- Maximum baryon density in central Au+Au at 20 A GeV: \(\rho_B \simeq 10 \rho_0 = 1.6\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Gas parameter \(\gamma_{gas} = (\sigma/\pi)^{3/2} \rho_B \simeq 2\) for total baryon-baryon cross section \(\sigma \simeq 40\) mbarn.
- Kaon-nucleon sigma term: \(\Sigma_{KN} = 0.450\) GeV (BR) and 0.350 GeV (KL); in-medium pion decay constant ratio \((f_\pi^*/f_\pi)^2 = 0.6\) (BR) and 1 (KL).
- Bare kaon mass \(m_K = 0.496\) GeV; vacuum pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 0.093\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior GiBUU studies (Larionov and Mosel 2005; Wagner et al. 2005) and compares with Tübingen QMD (Zheng et al. 2004), UrQMD, and HSD (Weber et al. 2003) calculations. At SIS energies, the analysis confirms that the BR parametrization of the kaon potential is needed to describe the out-of-plane squeeze-out of \(K^+\) measured by the KaoS Collaboration (Uhlig et al. 2005), consistent with earlier FOPI flow data (Herrmann et al. 1999; Crochet et al. 2000). The failure to reproduce the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio peak and the too-soft kaon transverse mass spectra (also reported for UrQMD and HSD by Bratkovskaya et al. 2004) motivates future inclusion of many-body collision effects in transport models.
