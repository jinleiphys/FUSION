# Entanglement Maximization in Low-Energy Neutron-Proton Scattering

> The entanglement properties of neutron-proton scattering are investigated using a measure that counts the number of entangled pairs produced by the action of a scattering operator on a given initial neutron-proton state. All phase shifts relevant for scattering at laboratory energies up to 350 MeV are used. Entanglement is found to be maximized in very low energy scattering. At such energies the Hamiltonian obeys Wigner SU(4) symmetry, and an entanglement maximum is a sign of that symmetry. At higher energies the angular dependence of entanglement is strong and the entanglement is large for many scattering angles. The tensor force is shown to play a significant role in producing entanglement at lab kinetic energies greater than about 50 MeV.

## Key claim
Entanglement in neutron-proton scattering is maximized at very low energies (around 1 MeV lab kinetic energy) where the Hamiltonian obeys Wigner SU(4) symmetry, contrary to earlier claims that nucleon-nucleon scattering is characterized by entanglement suppression at such energies.

## Method
The author computed the entanglement measure E(C) of Bennett (1996) by starting with an initial pure product state |up down> with zero entanglement, then applying the full neutron-proton scattering operator M expressed in terms of five independent amplitudes (a, c, m, g, h) derived from phase shift data. The scattering amplitudes were obtained from the NN online database using the measured phase shifts of the Nijmegen group (PhysRevC.48.792) for laboratory energies up to 350 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Entanglement is maximized at a lab kinetic energy of approximately 1 MeV, where the phase shift difference delta_1 - delta_0 equals pi/2
- The entanglement power E(S) of Beane (2018) reaches its maximum at a lab energy of about 8.7 MeV, where the phase shift difference passes through pi/4
- The maximum possible value of the entanglement power E(S) is 1/6, so a value of 0.05 corresponds to E/E_max approximately 0.3
- At 1 MeV lab kinetic energy, entanglement is maximized at all scattering angles due to s-wave dominance
- For lab kinetic energies below about 50 MeV, the initial state |up up> yields zero entanglement in the s-wave limit

## Context
This work extends the study of entanglement in nucleon-nucleon scattering initiated by Beane (2018), who proposed that entanglement suppression correlates with Wigner SU(4) symmetry. The author argues that the entanglement power measure used by Beane (2018) cannot distinguish between maximally entangled Bell states and the completely unentangled maximum-entropy state. The results contradict the interpretation of entanglement suppression at low energies and instead show that maximum entanglement is a signature of Wigner SU(4) symmetry. The tensor force is identified as playing a significant role in producing entanglement at lab kinetic energies above about 50 MeV, consistent with the known importance of one-pion exchange at these energies.
