# Anisotropic hydrodynamics and the early-thermalization puzzle

> The framework of anisotropic hydrodynamics is used in 3+1 dimensions to analyze behavior of matter produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The model predictions for the hadronic transverse-momentum spectra, directed and elliptic flows, and the HBT radii are presented. We show that the effects of the initial anisotropy of pressure may be compensated by appropriate adjustment of the initial energy density. In this way, the final hadronic observables become insensitive to the early stage dynamics and the early thermalization puzzle may be circumvented.

## Key claim

The effects of initial pressure anisotropy on final hadronic observables can be compensated by adjusting the initial energy density, making the observables insensitive to the early-stage dynamics and thereby circumventing the early-thermalization puzzle, provided the equilibration time scale is fixed to 1 fm.

## Method

The authors solved the 3+1 dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics equations, which include energy-momentum conservation and an entropy production law with a source term controlled by a constant equilibration time parameter τ_eq = 1 fm. They initialized the system at proper time τ_0 = 0.25 fm with two extreme momentum-space anisotropies (oblate with x_0 = 100 and prolate with x_0 = 0.032) and adjusted the initial energy density separately for each case to match the measured multiplicity. The evolution was run until the entropy density dropped to σ_f = 1.79 fm^{-3}, after which particle production and resonance decays were simulated using the THERMINATOR code with the Cooper-Frye formalism.

## Key numbers

- Initial starting time: τ_0 = 0.25 fm
- Equilibration time parameter: τ_eq = 1.0 fm (constant value)
- Initial anisotropy parameters: x_0 = 100 (oblate) and x_0 = 0.032 (prolate), chosen because r(100) = r(0.032)
- Initial energy density at center for most central collisions: ε_i = 48.8 GeV/fm^3 for x_0 = 100, and ε_i = 80.1 GeV/fm^3 for x_0 = 0.032
- Freeze-out entropy density: σ_f = 1.79 fm^{-3}, corresponding to temperature T_f = 150 MeV for x=1
- Collision system: Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC)
- Centrality classes studied: c = 0-6%, 0-5%, 5-40%, 15-25%, and 20-40%

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework previously developed by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), and Ryblewski (2010-2012) to 3+1 dimensions, addressing the early-thermalization puzzle that arises in perfect-fluid and viscous hydrodynamics approaches. The results show that final hadronic observables (pseudorapidity distributions, transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic and directed flow, and HBT radii) are insensitive to the initial pressure anisotropy when the initial energy density is appropriately adjusted, which enables the use of hydrodynamic models without requiring unrealistically short thermalization times.
