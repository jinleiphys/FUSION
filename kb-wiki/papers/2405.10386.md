# Proto-neutron stars with quark cores

> This work investigates the evolution of proto-neutron stars (PNSs) from birth as neutrino-rich objects to maturity as cold-catalyzed neutrino-poor objects with nucleonic and non-nucleonic degrees of freedom. The focus is on the star's core where the nucleons, hyperons, and the $Δ$-isobars are expected to dissolve into a ``soup" of deconfined quarks, at higher baryon densities, to establish a possible hadron-quark phase transition. We separately calculate the nuclear equations of state (EoS) for the hadronic matter (composed of all the baryon octet and $Δ$-isobars) and the strange quark matter (SQM) under the same thermodynamic conditions characteristic of PNS and proto-strange star (PSS) evolution and construct the hybrid EoS using Maxwell's construction. The study allows us to determine the hadron-quark phase transitions along the evolution lines of the star. We observed a phase transition from hadronic matter to quark matter (QM) phase when the neutrinos have completely escaped from the star's core. The EoSs utilized are constrained to meet the $2\,\rm M_\odot$ threshold in accordance with the observational data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hadron-quark phase transition in proto-neutron stars occurs only after all neutrinos have completely escaped from the stellar core, meaning that deconfined quark matter does not appear during the neutrino-trapped early stages of the star's evolution.

## Method

The authors separately calculated nuclear equations of state for hadronic matter (composed of the full baryon octet and Delta-isobars) using a relativistic mean-field model with density-dependent DDME2 couplings, and for strange quark matter using a density-dependent quark model with parameters determined through Bayesian inference. They constructed hybrid equations of state by applying Maxwell's construction to establish chemical and mechanical equilibrium between the two phases under thermodynamic conditions characteristic of proto-neutron star evolution from neutrino-rich to neutrino-poor states.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density used is \(n_0 = 0.152\,\rm fm^{-3}\), with binding energy \(E_B = -16.4\,\rm MeV\), compressibility modulus \(K_0 = 251.9\,\rm MeV\), symmetry energy \(J = 32.3\,\rm MeV\), and symmetry energy slope \(L_0 = 51.3\,\rm MeV\)
- The equations of state are constrained to meet the \(2\,\rm M_\odot\) threshold from observations of pulsars PSR J0740+6620, PSR J2215+5135, and PSR J0952-0607
- Quark model parameters used are \(\sqrt{D}=128.4\,\rm MeV\) and \(C=0.8\), with bare quark masses \(m_u = 2.16\,\rm MeV\), \(m_d = 4.67\,\rm MeV\), and \(m_s = 93.4\,\rm MeV\)
- Hyperon potentials used for coupling fitting are \(U_\Lambda = -28\,\rm MeV\), \(U_\Sigma = 30\,\rm MeV\), \(U_\Xi = -4\,\rm MeV\), and \(U_\Delta \approx -98\,\rm MeV\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid neutron stars at zero temperature (Issifu 2023, Karimi 2022, Cierniak 2021) to include finite-temperature conditions relevant for proto-neutron star evolution. It contrasts with the findings of PhysRevC.100.015803, which concluded there are no free quarks in hot stars within their model framework, and differs from Shao (2011) by using Maxwell construction rather than Gibbs construction for the phase transition. The results enable future studies of how quark cores appear during the cooling and deleptonization of neutron stars from birth to maturity.
