# Structure of the Nucleon and its Excitations

> The structure of the ground state nucleon and its finite-volume excitations are examined from three different perspectives. Using new techniques to extract the relativistic components of the nucleon wave function, the node structure of both the upper and lower components of the nucleon wave function are illustrated. A non-trivial role for gluonic components is manifest. In the second approach, the parity-expanded variational analysis (PEVA) technique is utilised to isolate states at finite momenta, enabling a novel examination of the electric and magnetic form factors of nucleon excitations. Here the magnetic form factors of low-lying odd-parity nucleons are particularly interesting. Finally, the structure of the nucleon spectrum is examined in a Hamiltonian effective field theory analysis incorporating recent lattice-QCD determinations of low-lying two-particle scattering-state energies in the finite volume. The Roper resonance of Nature is observed to originate from multi-particle coupled-channel interactions while the first radial excitation of the nucleon sits much higher at approximately 1.9 GeV.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance of Nature originates predominantly from multi-particle coupled-channel interactions (πN, πΔ, σN rescattering), while the first radial excitation of the nucleon sits much higher at approximately 1.9 GeV; the quark-model-like basis state at 2.0 GeV makes only a small but nontrivial contribution to the finite-volume state observed in the Roper resonance regime.

## Method

The authors used three complementary approaches on PACS-CS 2+1 flavour dynamical-fermion lattice QCD configurations at mπ = 156 MeV. First, they extracted relativistic components of the nucleon wave function via gauge-fixed non-local interpolating fields with correlation-matrix optimised sources. Second, they applied the parity-expanded variational analysis (PEVA) technique to isolate states at finite momenta and compute electromagnetic form factors of low-lying odd-parity nucleon excitations. Third, they performed a Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT) analysis incorporating lattice-QCD determinations of two-particle scattering-state energies in finite volume, constraining coupled πN, πΔ and σN channels to experimental scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The first radial excitation of the nucleon sits at approximately 1.9 GeV (1.9(1) GeV) in lattice QCD
- The bare quark-model-like basis state in the HEFT analysis has a mass of 2.0 GeV
- The HEFT fit provides a pole in the Roper resonance regime at 1393 - i 167 MeV
- The first negative-parity excitation of the nucleon (N*(1535)) is almost 600 MeV above the nucleon
- Lattice QCD calculations were performed at light quark masses providing mπ = 156 MeV
- The PEVA technique used Euclidean-time values t0 = 1 after the source and Δt = 3 for the generalised-eigenvalue equation

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of nucleon structure by introducing new wave function techniques and the PEVA method for excited-state form factors, building on prior work by Mahbub (2013) and Menadue (2013). The HEFT analysis resolves a long-standing puzzle where every lattice QCD collaboration using local three-quark interpolating fields (Mahbub 2010, Edwards 2011, Engel 2013, Alexandrou 2014, Kiratidis 2015, Liu 2016) failed to observe a low-lying positive-parity nucleon excitation near the Roper resonance. The results confirm and extend the independent work of Lang (2016) using non-local five-quark interpolators, and demonstrate that the Roper resonance arises from coupled-channel dynamics rather than being a simple three-quark radial excitation. This enables future studies of the quark mass dependence of these electromagnetic form factors and further understanding of nucleon excitation structure.
