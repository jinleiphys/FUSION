# Pairing in a two-component ultracold Fermi gas: phases with broken space symmetries

> We explore the phase diagram of a two-component ultracold atomic Fermi gas interacting with zero-range forces in the limit of weak-coupling. We focus on the dependence of the pairing gap and the free energy on the variations in the number densities of the two species while the total density of the system is held fixed. As the density asymmetry is increased, the system exhibits a transition from a homogenous Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) phase to phases with spontaneously broken global space symmetries. One such realization is the deformed Fermi surface superfluidity (DFS) which exploits the possibility of deforming the Fermi-surfaces of the species into ellipsoidal form at zero total momentum of Cooper pairs. The critical asymmetries at which the transition from the DFS to the unpaired state occurs are larger than those for the BCS phase. In this precritical region the DFS phase lowers the condensation energy of the asymmetric BCS state. We compare quantitatively the DFS phase to another realization of superconducting phases with broken translational symmetry - the single plane wave Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF)phase, which is characterized by a non-vanishing center-of-mass momentum of the Cooper pairs. The possibility of the detection of the DFS phase in the time-of-flight experiments is discussed and quantified for the case of $^6$Li atoms trapped in two different hyperfine states.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a weakly-coupled two-component ultracold Fermi gas with zero-range interactions, pairing correlations can persist for density asymmetries larger than the critical asymmetry of the homogeneous BCS phase if the Fermi surfaces of the two species are deformed into ellipsoids (DFS phase) or if Cooper pairs carry finite center-of-mass momentum (LOFF phase); the DFS phase is found to be the ground state in a wider range of asymmetries than the LOFF phase for the specific case of \(^6\)Li atoms.

## Method

The authors solve the mean-field (BCS) gap equation and the number density equations self-consistently for a two-component Fermi gas with zero-range \(s\)-wave interactions, using a regularized contact interaction characterized by the scattering length \(a_S\). They compute the pairing gap and free energy as functions of the density asymmetry \(\alpha = (\rho_{\uparrow} - \rho_{\downarrow})/(\rho_{\uparrow} + \rho_{\downarrow})\) for three phases: the homogeneous BCS phase, the plane-wave LOFF phase (where Cooper pairs have finite total momentum \(\mathbf{P}\)), and the DFS phase (where the Fermi surfaces are quadrupole-deformed with deformation parameter \(\delta\epsilon\)).

## Key numbers

* The critical asymmetry for the homogeneous BCS phase is \(\alpha_{\rm c}^{\rm BCS} \simeq 0.055\) for \(^6\)Li at density \(\rho = 3.8 \times 10^{12}\) cm\(^{-3}\) (corresponding to \(k_F |a_S| = 0.55\)).
* The LOFF phase is preferred over the normal and homogeneous BCS phases in a narrow window of asymmetries \(0.04 \le \alpha \le 0.057\) for total pair momentum \(P/k_F \sim 0.05\).
* The DFS phase is the ground state in a wider range of asymmetries, \(0.03 \le \alpha \le 0.06\), for deformation parameters \(0.12 \le \delta\epsilon \le 0.16\).
* The Fermi temperature for the system is \(T_F = 942\) nK, and calculations are performed at constant temperature \(T = 10\) nK.
* The Fermi momentum for the symmetric case is \(k_F \approx 4.83 \times 10^4\) cm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pairing in asymmetric Fermi systems, particularly the BCS prediction that the gap vanishes for asymmetries \(\alpha > \alpha_{\rm c}^{\rm BCS}\) as derived by Mur and Lombardo. It builds on the concept of deformed Fermi surface superfluidity introduced in a prior paper by the same group (Sedrakian et al., 2005) for the strong-coupling regime, and applies it to the weak-coupling regime relevant to ultracold atomic gases. The paper also compares the DFS phase quantitatively with the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase, which was previously studied in condensed matter and hadronic contexts (Larkin and Ovchinnikov, 1964; Fulde and Ferrell, 1964). The results enable experimental detection of the DFS phase in time-of-flight experiments by measuring the anisotropic momentum distribution, which would distinguish it from the homogeneous BCS and LOFF phases.
