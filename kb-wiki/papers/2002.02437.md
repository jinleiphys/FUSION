# Constraints and correlations of nuclear matter parameters from a density-dependent van der Waals model

> A recently proposed density-dependent van der Waals model, with only $4$ free parameters adjusted to fix binding energy, saturation density, symmetry energy, and incompressibility, is analyzed under symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter constraints. In a previous paper, it was shown that this model is fully consistent with the constraints related to the binary neutron star merger event named GW170817 and reported by the LIGO and Virgo collaboration. Here, we show that it also describes satisfactorily the low and high-density regions of symmetric nuclear matter, with all the main constraints satisfied. We also found a linear correlation between the incompressibility and the skewness parameter, both at the saturation density and show how it relates to the crossing point presented in the incompressibility as a function of the density. In the asymmetric matter regime, other linear correlations are found, namely, the one between the symmetry energy ($J$) and its slope ($L_0$), and other one establishing the symmetry energy curvature as a function of the combination given by $3J-L_0$.

## Key claim
The density-dependent van der Waals (DD-vdW) model, with only four free parameters, satisfies all major symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter constraints from low to high densities, provided the incompressibility is restricted to the range 242.4 MeV ≤ K₀ ≤ 255 MeV to meet the flow constraint; the model fails the MIX4 constraint on the ratio of symmetry energy at half saturation density to its value at saturation density, but the deviation is less than 5% for most parametrizations.

## Method
The authors extended a previously proposed density-dependent van der Waals model (DD-vdW) by introducing a density-dependent attractive interaction a(ρ) = a/(1+bρ)ⁿ, with the power n adjusted to fix the incompressibility at saturation density. The repulsive interaction used the Carnahan-Starling excluded volume method, and the model was generalized to asymmetric nuclear matter by adding a term dρ₃² to the energy density. The four free parameters (a, b, n, d) were fitted to reproduce the binding energy B₀ ≈ 16 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, the symmetry energy J in the range 25–35 MeV, and the incompressibility K₀, then tested against the set of constraints (SET2a) used in Dutra et al. (2014) for 263 relativistic mean-field parametrizations.

## Key numbers
- Incompressibility range satisfying the flow constraint: 242.4 MeV ≤ K₀ ≤ 255 MeV, consistent with the SM1 constraint of 190–270 MeV and overlapping with the range 250–315 MeV from Stone (2015).
- Skewness coefficient Q₀ for flow-consistent parametrizations: −740 MeV ≤ Q₀ ≤ −569 MeV, compatible with ranges from other calculations (−690 to −208 MeV, −790 to −330 MeV, and −1200 to −200 MeV).
- Symmetry energy slope L₀ for flow-consistent parametrizations: 63.4 MeV ≤ L₀ ≤ 97.1 MeV, within the MIX2a constraint of 25–115 MeV.
- Symmetry energy curvature K_sym⁰ for flow-consistent parametrizations: −20.6 MeV ≤ K_sym⁰ ≤ 2.5 MeV.
- Crossing density in the incompressibility as a function of density: ρ_cross/ρ₀ = 0.8 (fitted value 0.798 from linear correlation Q₀ = 9.79K₀ + a₂).
- Ratio r = S(ρ₀/2)/J for flow-consistent parametrizations: 0.53–0.56, below the MIX4 constraint range of 0.57–0.83 but within 5% for most cases.

## Context
This work extends the DD-vdW model introduced in Dutra et al. (2019) by testing it against the comprehensive nuclear matter constraints used in Dutra et al. (2014) to evaluate 263 relativistic mean-field parametrizations. The model reproduces the linear correlation between incompressibility K₀ and skewness Q₀ previously found for Boguta-Bodmer models with effective mass 0.6M (Bianca et al., 2018) and for Skyrme and Gogny parametrizations (Margueron et al., 2018), but not for general relativistic mean-field models. The linear correlations between symmetry energy J and its slope L₀, and between K_sym⁰ and 3J−L₀, confirm relationships observed across 500 relativistic and nonrelativistic parametrizations (Mondal et al., 2017) and specifically in Skyrme models (Mondal et al., 2016). The model enables further studies of neutron star properties and high-density nuclear matter using a simple four-parameter framework that satisfies both terrestrial constraints and the GW170817 binary neutron star merger constraints.
