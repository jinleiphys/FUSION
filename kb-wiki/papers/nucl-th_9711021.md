# Dynamics of a Simple Quantum System in a Complex Environment

> We present a theory for the dynamical evolution of a quantum system coupled to a complex many-body intrinsic system/environment. By modelling the intrinsic many-body system with parametric random matrices, we study the types of effective stochastic models which emerge from random matrix theory. Using the Feynman-Vernon path integral formalism, we derive the influence functional and obtain either analytical or numerical solutions for the time evolution of the entire quantum system. We discuss thoroughly the structure of the solutions for some representative cases and make connections to well known limiting results, particularly to Brownian motion, Kramers classical limit and the Caldeira-Leggett approach.

## Key claim

The authors derive a quantum evolution equation for a slow degree of freedom coupled to a complex many-body environment modeled by parametric random matrices, showing that in the high-temperature Markovian limit the influence functional differs qualitatively from the Caldeira-Leggett form and leads to a Kramers equation in the classical limit, but only when the initial state is wider than the thermal de Broglie wavelength.

## Method

The intrinsic many-body system is modeled as a Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) random matrix whose elements depend parametrically on the slow coordinate X, with an exponentially increasing average density of states ρ(ε) = ρ₀ exp(βε) that defines the reservoir temperature T = 1/β. Using the Feynman-Vernon double path integral formalism, the authors compute the influence functional in the adiabatic limit (reservoir time scale ℏ/κ₀ much shorter than the slow-system time scale) and expand to first order in β, obtaining a Schrödinger-like evolution equation for the reduced density matrix ρ(X,Y,t) of the slow subsystem. The Wigner transform of this equation yields the classical Kramers equation in the limit where the correlation function G(x) is expanded to quadratic order.

## Key numbers

- The average density of states of the intrinsic system is ρ(ε) = ρ₀ exp(βε), where β = 1/T is interpreted as the thermodynamic temperature of the reservoir.
- The spreading width Γ↓ and the correlation width X₀ characterize the random matrix coupling; the correlation function is taken as G(x) = exp(-x²/2) with G(0)=1.
- The friction coefficient in the small-velocity limit is γ = βΓ↓ℏ/(2MX₀²).
- The diffusion constant D = 2X₀²/(β²Γ↓ℏ) satisfies the classical Einstein relation D = T/(γM).
- The thermal de Broglie wavelength is λ_T = 2πℏ√(β/M); the evolution equation is valid only for initial states wider than this scale.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier random-matrix approach to nuclear collective motion (Bulgac et al., 1995-1996) by deriving a full quantum evolution equation for the density matrix, going beyond the classical Langevin-type equations studied previously. The influence functional differs qualitatively from the quadratic Caldeira-Leggett form (Caldeira and Leggett, 1983), and the high-temperature limit recovers the Kramers equation (Kramers, 1940) with a friction coefficient that has a clear microscopic interpretation. The formalism enables future study of memory effects beyond the Markovian limit and can be extended to include particle evaporation or coupling to electromagnetic fields.
