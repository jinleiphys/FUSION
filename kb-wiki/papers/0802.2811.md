# Effective-interaction approach to the many-boson problem

> We show that the convergence behavior of the many-body numerical diagonalization scheme for strongly interacting bosons in a trap can be significantly improved by the Lee-Suzuki method adapted from nuclear physics: One can construct an effective interaction that acts in a space much smaller than the original Hilbert space. In particular for short-ranged forces and strong correlations, the method offers a good estimate of the energy and the excitation spectrum, at a computational cost several orders of magnitude smaller than that required by the standard method.

## Key claim
The Lee-Suzuki method, adapted from nuclear physics, can significantly improve the convergence of numerical diagonalization for strongly interacting bosons in a trap, but the resulting energies are not variational bounds because the effective Hamiltonian is approximated as a two-body operator rather than the exact N-body operator.

## Method
The authors apply the Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation to construct an effective two-body interaction from the two-body problem, then use this effective interaction to build an N-body Hamiltonian acting in a truncated model space (P-space) defined by a maximum energy cutoff parameter N_max. The method is tested on harmonically trapped spinless bosons in quasi-two dimensions with a Gaussian repulsive interaction of range sigma=0.1 oscillator units, comparing results to standard configuration interaction calculations for systems of N=4, 9, and 20 particles.

## Key numbers
- For N=4 bosons with g=10 and sigma=0.1, the effective interaction gives converged energies at N_max around 5 (basis size ~10 states), while standard calculations require N_max around 25 (basis size ~10^4 states)
- For N=9 bosons with g=10, a calculation with N_max=6 requires 12 basis states, while N_max=20 would correspond to 81097 states
- The scattering length for g=10, sigma=0.1 is a=0.0871 oscillator units; for g=1, sigma=0.1 it is a=0.000283 oscillator units
- For N=9 bosons with sigma=1 (larger range), the effective interaction does not improve convergence compared to standard calculations

## Context
This work extends the Lee-Suzuki method, previously used in nuclear no-core shell model calculations for fermions (Caurier 2005), to bosonic cold atom systems for the first time. It contrasts with mean-field approaches like Gross-Pitaevskii theory and with quantum Monte Carlo methods (Harju 2005), which have limited access to excitation spectra. The method enables study of larger strongly correlated bosonic systems than standard configuration interaction, though for N=20 with strong interactions (g=10) neither method achieves convergence within the N_max range considered.
