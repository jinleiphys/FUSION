# QCD topological susceptibility from the nonlocal chiral quark model

> We investigate the QCD topological susceptibility $χ_t$ by using the nonlocal chiral quark model (NL$χ$QM). This model is based on the liquid instanton QCD-vacuum configuration in which $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ flavor symmetry is explicitly broken by the current quark mass $(m_{u,d},m_s)\approx(5,135)$ MeV. To compute $χ_t$, the local topological charge density operator $Q_t(x)$ is derived from the effective partition function of NL$χ$QM. We take into account the contributions from the leading-order (LO) ones $\sim\mathcal{O}(N_c)$ in the $1/N_c$ expansion. We also verify that the analytical expression of $χ_t$ in NL$χ$QM satisfy the Witten-Veneziano (WV) and the Leutwyler-Smilga (LS) formulae. Once the average instanton size and inter-instanton distance are fixed with $\barρ=1/3$ fm and $\bar{R}=1$ fm, respectively, all the associated model parameters are all determined self-consistently within the model, including the $η$ and $η'$ weak decay constants. We obtain the results such as $F_η=96.77$ MeV and $F_{η'}=102.53$ MeV for instance. Numerically we observe that $χ_{t}=(165.57\,\mathrm{MeV})^4$ in our full calculation. This value is comparable with its empirical one $χ_t=(175\pm5\,\mathrm{MeV})^4$. We also find that our $χ^\mathrm{WV}_t=χ^\mathrm{QL}_t=(194.30,\mathrm{MeV})^4$ in the quenched limit and $χ^\mathrm{LS}_t=(162.54\,\mathrm{MeV})^4$ in the chiral limit. Consequently, we conclude that $χ_{t}<χ^\mathrm{QL}_t$. Our result also implies that the $(10\sim20)\,\%$ decrease with the dynamical quark contributions.

## Key claim

The nonlocal chiral quark model (NLχQM) yields a QCD topological susceptibility of χ_t = (165.57 MeV)^4, which is about 10% smaller than the empirical value of (175 ± 5 MeV)^4, with the dynamical quark contributions reducing the susceptibility by 10–20% compared to the quenched limit.

## Method

The authors derived the local topological charge density operator Q_t(x) from the effective partition function of the NLχQM, which is based on a liquid instanton QCD-vacuum configuration with explicit SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking via current quark masses (m_{u,d} ≈ 5 MeV, m_s ≈ 135 MeV). They computed the topological susceptibility at leading order in the 1/N_c expansion by evaluating the two-point correlation function of Q_t(x), including contributions from quark loops, pseudo-scalar meson propagators, and a constant term from the small difference between instanton and anti-instanton numbers. All model parameters were determined self-consistently after fixing the average instanton size ρ̄ = 1/3 fm and inter-instanton distance R̄ = 1 fm.

## Key numbers

- χ_t = (165.57 MeV)^4 in the full calculation with dynamical quarks
- χ_t^WV = χ_t^QL = (194.30 MeV)^4 in the quenched limit (Witten-Veneziano formula)
- χ_t^LS = (162.54 MeV)^4 in the chiral limit (Leutwyler-Smilga formula)
- Empirical value χ_t = (175 ± 5 MeV)^4
- F_η = 96.77 MeV and F_η' = 102.53 MeV for the η and η' weak decay constants
- Constituent quark mass M_0 = 341 MeV determined from the self-consistent gap equation
- Quark condensates: Σ_{u,d} = (251.31 MeV)^3 and Σ_s = (242.93 MeV)^3
- Pseudo-scalar meson masses: m_π = 138.43 MeV, m_K = 493.16 MeV, m_η = 563.82 MeV, m_η' = 954.76 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the topological susceptibility using effective QCD models, particularly the NJL model approach of Fukushima (2001) which gave χ_t = (166 MeV)^4, and provides a nonlocal alternative to lattice QCD simulations such as those by the CP-PACS collaboration (1999) and the SESAM/TχL collaborations (2001). The NLχQM result reproduces the Witten-Veneziano and Leutwyler-Smilga formulae analytically and enables further studies of the U_A(1) anomaly and η' meson properties within a self-consistent instanton-based framework.
