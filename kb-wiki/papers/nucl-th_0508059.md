# Improving the Feshbach-Rubinow approximation

> The binding energy of three identical bosons is estimated by coupled differential equations which generalise the Feshbach--Rubinow approximation. This method turns out to be rather efficient, especially in the limit of vanishing binding.

# Improving the Feshbach-Rubinow approximation

## Key claim

The Feshbach-Rubinow approximation for three identical bosons can be systematically improved by expanding the wave function in orthogonal polynomials of the interparticle distances, yielding coupled differential equations whose solution with just two terms provides a dramatic improvement over the original single-equation method, especially in the limit of vanishing binding.

## Method

The author generalizes the Feshbach-Rubinow ansatz by writing the three-body wave function as a sum of terms $u_n(r) P_n(x,y,z) / r^{(n+7/2)}$, where $P_n$ are real, homogeneous, symmetric polynomials of the interparticle distances $x$, $y$, $z$ and $r = (x+y+z)/2$. For a truncation at $n=2$, the coefficients $u_1(r)$ and $u_2(r)$ obey a set of two coupled ordinary differential equations with known constant coefficients and effective potentials obtained by projecting the pairwise interaction onto the polynomial basis. The coupled equations are solved numerically by mapping $r \in [0,\infty)$ to $x \in [0,1)$ and expanding each radial function in a Fourier sine series, reducing the system to a matrix eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- Harmonic oscillator potential $v(x)=x^2$: exact energy $E = 6\sqrt{3/2} \simeq 7.3485$; FR1 gives $E_1 = (36/7)\sqrt{15/7} \simeq 7.5284$; FR2 gives $E_2 = 7.3537$, a remarkable improvement.
- Harmonic oscillator: ratio of short-range correlation $\delta$ to exact value improves from $\delta_1/\delta_{\rm ex} = 1.57$ for FR1 to $\delta_2/\delta_{\rm ex} = 0.968$ for FR2.
- Linear potential $\sum_{i<j} r_{ij}/2 = r$: FR1 gives $E_1 \simeq 3.906$; FR2 gives $E_2 \simeq 3.8635$; hyperspherical expansion with one partial wave gives $E_{L=0} \simeq 3.8647$ and with two partial waves $E_{L\le4} \simeq 3.8633$.
- Linear potential: correlation coefficient $\delta_1 = 0.08488$ for FR1, $\delta_2 = 0.05592$ for FR2; four coupled hyperspherical waves give $\delta \simeq 0.05689$ and 176 correlated Gaussians give $\delta \simeq 0.05702$.
- Exponential potential $v(x) = -g \exp(-x)$ at coupling $g=1.4$: FR1 gives $-E_3 = 0.03468$; FR2 gives $-E_3 = 0.03542$; Gaussian expansion with 40 terms (G8) gives $-E_3 = 0.03586$.
- Exponential potential at $g=1.4$: correlation coefficient $\delta = 0.01212$ for FR1, $\delta = 0.01091$ for FR2; G8 gives $\delta = 0.01000$.
- Critical coupling $g_3$ for three-body binding in exponential potential: FR1 gives $g_3 = 1.1751$; FR2 gives $g_3 = 1.1644$; G8 gives $g_3 = 1.1563$.

## Context

This work extends the original Feshbach-Rubinow approximation (Feshbach and Rubinow, 1955) by providing a systematic expansion that can be truncated at increasing order, unlike previous improvements such as the integro-differential equation approach of Rosenthal and Haracz (1982) which remained a single-channel method. The FR2 approximation with two coupled equations is shown to be roughly equivalent to an expansion involving 40 correlated Gaussians (using the method of Hiyama et al., 2003) for the exponential potential, while being significantly simpler. The method enables accurate calculation of both binding energies and short-range correlation coefficients (which enter decay and production rates) for three-boson systems, and the author indicates forthcoming applications to the positronium ion and two-electron atoms.
