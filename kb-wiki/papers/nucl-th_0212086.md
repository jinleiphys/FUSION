# Cranking in Isospace

> The response of isovector and isoscalar pairing to generalized rotation in isospace is studied. Analytical expressions for non-selfconsistent solutions for different limiting cases of the model are derived. In particular, the connections between gauge relations among pairing gaps and the position of the isocranking axis are investigated in $N$=$Z$ nuclei. The two domains of collective and non-collective rotation in space are generalized to isospace. The amplitudes for pair-transfer of T=0 and T=1 pairs are also calculated. It is shown that the structure of the T=0 state in odd-odd nuclei prevents any enhancement of pairing transfer also in the presence of strong T=0 correlations. The energy differences of the T=0 and T=1 excitations in odd-odd nuclei are qualitatively reproduced by Total Routhian Surface calculations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, within the BCS approximation, mixed collective solutions combining isovector (t=1) and isoscalar (t=0) pairing cannot be obtained without additional symmetry-breaking mechanisms such as approximate number projection; consequently, the structure of the T=0 state in odd-odd N=Z nuclei is a two-quasiparticle excitation, which prevents enhancement of isoscalar pair transfer regardless of the strength of T=0 correlations.

## Method

The authors derive analytical non-selfconsistent BCS solutions for a model Hamiltonian with isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions under two-dimensional isocranking (rotation in isospace) in N=Z nuclei. They classify solutions into collective and non-collective types based on the relative orientation of the isocranking axis to the pairing field vector in isospace, and calculate quasiparticle routhians, eigenvectors, pairing tensors, and gap equations for various limiting cases. Pair-transfer amplitudes are evaluated using the structure of T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd nuclei, and Total Routhian Surface (TRS) calculations are performed to study deformation effects.

## Key numbers

- The first crossing frequency for non-collective isorotation is given by ω_τ ≤ 2E₁ ≡ ω_τ^(1), where E₁ is the lowest quasiparticle energy at zero frequency.
- For the pure t=0 pairing model, quasiparticle energies are E_i± = E_i ± ½ ω_τ, with E_i = √(ẽ_i² + Δ₀²), valid below ω_τ ≤ 2E₁.
- The critical ratio for mixing of t=1 and t=0 pairing phases via Lipkin-Nogami projection is x^(t=0) = G_(t=0)/G_(t=1) ≥ x_crit ~ 1.1.
- For the equidistant level model with symmetric cut-off, particle-hole symmetry leads to X^((-))=0 for routhians of Goodman-type (Eq. 17), automatically satisfying the particle number condition.

## Context

This work extends the isocranking formalism previously developed by Satula et al. (2001) for describing isobaric analogue states and Wigner energies in N=Z nuclei, and builds on earlier studies of proton-neutron pairing by Engel et al. (1996), Dean et al. (1997), and Frauendorf et al. (1999). The analytical solutions clarify the geometrical interpretation of collective versus non-collective isorotation, analogous to spatial rotation, and show that pair-transfer experiments (as proposed by Bes et al., 2000) may not be reliable indicators of T=0 pairing strength due to the intrinsic two-quasiparticle structure of T=0 states in odd-odd nuclei. The results enable future studies of isospin symmetry breaking and the role of particle-hole isovector fields in determining the moment of inertia in isospace.
