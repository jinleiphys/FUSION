# Collective nature of $0^{+}$- states in deformed rare-earth nuclei

> The description of the energiy spectra of $0^{+}$ states for rare-earth nuclei has been done involving the degree of the collectivity of corresponding $0^{+}$-state as a systematics parameter. Holshtein-Primakoff representation leads to very good agreement with experiment. Within the framework of this approach the parameter of the collectivity is mainly determined by pairs of particles constructed on single ''effective'' level. The results may be helpful both for nuclear structure experimentalists and theorists in their investigations of low-lying states structure and transition probabilities.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the energy spectra of \(0^{+}\) states in deformed rare-earth nuclei can be described by a simple quadratic formula \(E_n = A k - B k^2\), where the degree of collectivity \(k\) (treated as a systematics parameter) is mainly determined by pairs of particles on a single "effective" level. The hedge is that this is a simplified pairing vibrational model using the Holstein-Primakoff representation, and the agreement with experiment is achieved by minimizing Chi-square values for different permutations of possible \(k\) values.

## Method
The author uses a pairing Hamiltonian for \(N\) particles on a single effective level \(j\), expressed in terms of monopole pair operators \(R_+, R_-, R_0\). The Holstein-Primakoff transformation maps these operators to ideal boson creation and annihilation operators \(b^\dagger, b\), yielding a boson Hamiltonian \(H = A b^\dagger b - B b^\dagger b b^\dagger b\). The energy of any monopole excited state \(|n\rangle\) is then given by \(E_n = A n - B n^2\), where \(n\) corresponds to the degree of collectivity \(k\). The parameters \(A\) and \(B\) are determined by fitting experimental \(0^{+}\) state energies via Chi-square minimization.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{194}\mathrm{Pt}\): predicted one-phonon \(0^{+}\) state at 0.75 MeV, six-phonon state at 2.38 MeV, and seven-phonon state at 2.1 MeV.
- For \(^{196}\mathrm{Pt}\): predicted one-phonon \(0^{+}\) state at 0.6 MeV, three-phonon state at 1.5 MeV, and twelve-phonon state at 1.0 MeV.
- For \(^{188}\mathrm{Os}\): predicted one-phonon \(0^{+}\) state at 0.75 MeV and two-phonon state at 1.3 MeV.
- For \(^{158}\mathrm{Er}\): predicted one-phonon \(0^{+}\) state at 1.2 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the pairing vibrational model to deformed rare-earth nuclei, using the Holstein-Primakoff representation (originally applied in other contexts, as cited in the paper). It provides a simple systematics parameter \(k\) for the degree of collectivity of \(0^{+}\) states, which can be used to predict additional \(0^{+}\) states and guide experimental measurements of \(E0\) transition probabilities. The results are intended to complement more sophisticated analyses of low-lying nuclear structure, such as studies of \(B(E2; 2^+_{K=0_2^+} \rightarrow 0^+_{g.s.})\) transitions in rare-earth isotopes (referenced as work by other authors).
