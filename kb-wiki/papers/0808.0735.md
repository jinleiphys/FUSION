# Neutrino Astrophysics

> I review the current status of neutrino astrophysics, including solar neutrinos; atmospheric neutrinos; neutrino mass and oscillations; supernova neutrinos; neutrino nucleosynthesis (Big Bang nucleosynthesis, the neutrino process, the r-process); neutrino cooling and red giants; and high energy neutrino astronomy.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys the state of neutrino astrophysics as of 2008, arguing that the discovery of neutrino mass and oscillations through solar and atmospheric neutrino experiments has profound implications for particle physics beyond the Standard Model, while also noting that recent downward revisions in solar metallicity abundances have created tensions between standard solar model predictions and helioseismology measurements.

## Method

The paper reviews the experimental programs and theoretical frameworks across multiple subfields: solar neutrino detection via radiochemical (chlorine and gallium) and real-time water Cerenkov detectors (Super-Kamiokande and SNO); atmospheric neutrino measurements; neutrino oscillation phenomenology including the MSW matter-enhanced mechanism; supernova neutrino emission and nucleosynthesis; Big Bang nucleosynthesis; neutrino cooling in red giants; and high-energy neutrino astronomy. The review synthesizes results from the Homestake chlorine experiment, SAGE, GALLEX/GNO, Kamiokande, Super-Kamiokande, SNO, and KamLAND.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on solar neutrinos initiated by Davis (2002 Nobel Prize) and the Homestake chlorine experiment, which first revealed the solar neutrino deficit. It describes how the SNO experiment resolved this deficit by demonstrating that neutrinos change flavor during transit, confirming the MSW mechanism proposed by Mikheyev, Smirnov, and Wolfenstein (1985). The review also discusses tensions between standard solar models and helioseismology following the 30% downward revision in photospheric metal abundances from improved three-dimensional models. The results enable precise tests of neutrino oscillation parameters and motivate next-generation experiments like Borexino for measuring low-energy pp and ^7Be neutrinos.
