# Participant number fluctuations for higher moments of a multiplicity distribution

> The independent participant model is generalized for skewness and kurtosis. The obtained relations allow to calculate the fluctuations of an arbitrarily high order. From the comparison with the SPS and the LHC data it is found that the participants are not nucleons. The contribution of the participant fluctuations increases with the order of fluctuations. The 5% centrality bins selected for the analysis at the LHC by ALICE seems to be too large. The fluctuations measures are dominated by the fluctuations of participants there. The method to quantify the value of participant number fluctuations experimentally is proposed.

## Key claim

The independent participant model can describe fluctuations of charged particles in Pb+Pb at the LHC only if the fluctuations from one participant are much smaller than the fluctuations of charged particles in p+p reactions; therefore, the participants are not nucleons.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the third and fourth moments of a multiplicity distribution under the assumptions that participants are identical and independent, generalizing the independent participant model beyond the scaled variance to skewness and kurtosis. These formulas are then applied to ALICE data on charged-particle fluctuations in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, using the measured p+p fluctuations as a proxy for single-participant fluctuations, to extract the participant number fluctuations via the acceptance-corrected scaled variance relation.

## Key numbers

- The scaled variance of charged particles in Pb+Pb at ALICE within |eta|<0.8 is approximately 3, while the scaled variance in p+p within |Delta eta|<0.8 is approximately 7.
- The acceptance q in Pb+Pb at ALICE is about 15%, giving a reconstructed full-acceptance p+p scaled variance of approximately 41.
- The p+p scaled variance values in different rapidity intervals are approximately 4.6 (|Delta eta|=0.5), 8.46 (|Delta eta|=1.0), 11.36 (|Delta eta|=1.5), and 13.74 (|Delta eta|=2.4).
- The 5% centrality bins selected for analysis at the LHC by ALICE are too large, causing fluctuation measures to be dominated by participant fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends the independent participant model of Bialas (1976) from the scaled variance to higher moments, addressing a gap noted in the literature where the influence of participant number fluctuations on skewness and kurtosis had not been considered. The analysis contradicts the expectation from transport models like HIJING and AMPT that participant fluctuations should grow with energy, and instead finds that participants cannot be nucleons at LHC energies. The results enable experimental methods to quantify participant number fluctuations by studying how fluctuations change with progressively narrower centrality bins, which is essential before searching for the QCD critical point via higher moments as pursued by STAR and NA61/SHINE.
