# Self-bound dense objects in holographic QCD

> We study a self-bound dense object in the hard wall model. We consider a spherically symmetric dense object which is characterized by its radial density distribution and non-uniform but spherically symmetric chiral condensate. For this we analytically solve the partial differential equations in the hard wall model and read off the radial coordinate dependence of the density and chiral condensate according to the AdS/CFT correspondence. We then attempt to describe nucleon density profiles of a few nuclei within our framework and observe that the confinement scale changes from a free nucleon to a nucleus. We briefly discuss how to include the effect of higher dimensional operator into our study. We finally comment on possible extensions of our work.

## Key claim

Using the hard wall model of holographic QCD, the authors claim that the confinement scale (the IR cutoff \(1/z_m\)) may change from a free nucleon (\(\sim 300\) MeV) to a nucleus (\(\sim 100\) MeV), but they hedge that this conclusion is qualitative and that typical errors in holographic QCD studies are \((10-30)\%\), so the \(z_m\)-dependence on nucleon number \(A\) should not be taken seriously.

## Method

The authors analytically solve the separable partial differential equations for the bulk gauge field \(A_t\) (dual to baryon number density) and the bulk scalar field \(X_0\) (dual to chiral condensate) in the hard wall model with a slice of AdS metric. They impose various IR boundary conditions at the hard wall cutoff \(z = z_m\) (e.g., \(A_t(r,z_m)=0\) or \(v'(r,z_m)=0\)) and fix integration constants by requiring regularity at the center \(r=0\) and specifying density and condensate values at the center and surface of a spherical self-bound object. They then compare their resulting radial density profiles to empirical nucleon density profiles of nuclei using a Fermi-type distribution, adjusting \(z_m\) to match the plateau region.

## Key numbers

* The IR cutoff in the standard hard wall model is fixed by the \(\rho\)-meson mass: \(1/z_m \simeq 320\) MeV.
* For a nucleus with \(A=20\) nucleons, the best-fit IR cutoff is \(1/z_m \simeq 72.8\) MeV; for \(A=50\), \(1/z_m \simeq 79.0\) MeV; for \(A=70\), \(1/z_m \simeq 78.5\) MeV; for \(A=100\), \(1/z_m \simeq 77.0\) MeV.
* The empirical nuclear radius is taken as \(R \simeq r_0 A^{1/3}\) with \(r_0 \sim 1.2\) fm, and the empirical density profile uses \(a = 0.54\) fm and \(R_{1/2} = R_s - 0.89 A^{-1/3}\) fm with \(R_s = 1.128 A^{1/3}\) fm.
* The central baryon number density for nuclei is taken as \(\rho_c \sim 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) with \(\rho_R \sim 0\) at the surface.

## Context

This work extends holographic QCD studies of dense matter (e.g., dense_hQCD) and holographic neutron stars (BPV) to the description of self-bound objects like nuclei, using the hard wall model of Erlich, Katz, Son, and Stephanov (EKSS) and Da Rold and Pomarol (PR). It is a first step toward a realistic holographic description of nuclei, and the authors note that the ambiguity in IR boundary conditions and the lack of stability analysis are major limitations. The observation that the confinement scale may change from a free nucleon to a nucleus is linked to the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect, where the quark confinement scale is claimed to change in nuclei. The work enables future studies of nuclear collective excitation modes and connections to chiral nuclei in chiral liquids (Lynn) or Q-stars (Qstar).
