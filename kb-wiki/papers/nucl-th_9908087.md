# Reaction rates for Neutron Capture Reactions to C-, N- and O-isotopes to the neutron rich side of stability

> The reaction rates of neutron capture reactions on light nuclei are important for reliably simulating nucleosynthesis in a variety of stellar scenarios. Neutron capture reaction rates on neutron-rich C-, N-, and O-isotopes are calculated in the framework of a hybrid compound and direct capture model. The results are tabulated and compared with the results of previous calculations as well as with experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper calculates neutron capture reaction rates for neutron-rich C-, N-, and O-isotopes using a hybrid compound and direct capture model, claiming improved accuracy over previous calculations by incorporating shell-model-derived spectroscopic factors, electromagnetic transition strengths, and resonance parameters; however, the authors acknowledge that the reliability of the shell model calculations decreases when approaching the neutron dripline.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining non-resonant direct capture (DC) calculations with resonant Breit-Wigner cross sections for isolated resonances, including interference terms where necessary (specifically for the 143 keV resonance in 13C(n,γ)14C). Spectroscopic factors and electromagnetic transition strengths were obtained from shell model calculations using the OXBASH code with the WBN interaction, while scattering and bound-state wave functions were computed using real folding potentials with the DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. For stable target nuclei, experimental data from transfer reactions and cross section measurements were used where available, while for β-unstable nuclei the calculations relied primarily on shell model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Thermal capture cross section for 13C(n,γ)14C: calculated value of 6.33 mb versus experimental absorption cross section of 1.37 mb
- 13C(n,γ)14C: 2+ p-wave resonance at neutron energy E_n^cm = 143 keV with calculated neutron width Γ_n = 3.15 keV (experimental: 3.4 ± 0.6 keV)
- 15N(n,γ)16N: 1+ resonance at E_res = 0.869 MeV with calculated Γ_n = 21.85 keV (experimental: 15 ± 5 keV)
- 18O(n,γ)19O: 3/2- resonance at E_res = 0.625 MeV with calculated Γ_n = 28.5 keV (experimental: 52 ± 3 keV)
- 14C(n,γ)15C: Maxwellian averaged cross section of 10.14 μb at 30 keV, compared to experimental data from Bee92
- 16C(n,γ)17C: Maxwellian averaged cross section at 30 keV differs by about 10% from the calculation by Mengoni et al.
- 21O(n,γ)22O: spectroscopic factor for ground state transition is 5.222, much higher than the 0.2 estimated in Rau94

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Rau94 (direct capture model) and the Hauser-Feshbach model calculations of CTT91 and RTK97, incorporating improved nuclear structure input from shell model calculations. The paper provides a consistent set of stellar neutron capture reaction rates for neutron-rich C-, N-, and O-isotopes needed for nucleosynthesis simulations in stellar helium burning, AGB stars, and inhomogeneous Big Bang models, and the authors state these rates should replace those given in previous compilations by Rau94, TSO93, and OKB97.
