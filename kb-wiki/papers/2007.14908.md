# Neutrinoless Double-Electron Capture

> Double-beta processes play a key role in the exploration of neutrino and weak interaction properties, and in the searches for effects beyond the Standard Model. During the last half century many attempts were undertaken to search for double-beta decay with emission of two electrons, especially for its neutrinoless mode ($0\nu2β^-$), the latter being still not observed. Double-electron capture (2EC) was not in focus so far because of its in general lower transition probability. However, the rate of neutrinoless double-electron capture ($0\nu2$EC) can experience a resonance enhancement by many orders of magnitude in case the initial and final states are energetically degenerate. In the resonant case, the sensitivity of the $0\nu2$EC process can approach the sensitivity of the $0\nu2β^-$ decay in the search for the Majorana mass of neutrinos, right-handed currents, and other new physics. We present an overview of the main experimental and theoretical results obtained during the last decade in this field. The experimental part outlines search results of 2EC processes and measurements of the decay energies for possible resonant $0ν$2EC transitions. An unprecedented precision in the determination of decay energies with Penning traps has allowed one to refine the values of the degeneracy parameter for all previously known near-resonant decays and has reduced the rather large uncertainties in the estimate of the $0\nu2$EC half-lives. The theoretical part contains an updated analysis of the electron shell effects and an overview of the nuclear structure models, in which the nuclear matrix elements of the $0\nu2$EC decays are calculated. One can conclude that the decay probability of $0ν$2EC can experience a significant enhancement in several nuclides.

# Neutrinoless Double-Electron Capture

## Key claim

Neutrinoless double-electron capture (0ν2EC) can achieve a sensitivity approaching that of neutrinoless double-beta decay (0ν2β⁻) in searches for Majorana neutrino mass and other new physics, but only if the initial and final atomic states are energetically degenerate to within the electromagnetic decay width of the daughter atom (typically ~10 eV), a condition that requires mass measurements with relative uncertainty of about 10⁻⁹.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review combining high-precision Penning-trap mass spectrometry of candidate nuclide pairs with relativistic calculations of electron shell effects and nuclear structure models for nuclear matrix elements (NMEs). Experimentally, decay energies for possible resonant 0ν2EC transitions were measured using Penning traps to determine the degeneracy parameter Δ = M_parent − M_daughter with unprecedented precision. Theoretically, the half-lives of near-resonant transitions were estimated using the Breit-Wigner resonance factor Γ_f/(Δ² + Γ_f²/4), updated electron wave functions at the nucleus, and NMEs calculated within various nuclear-theory frameworks including new calculations performed specifically for this review.

## Key numbers

- The maximum resonant enhancement factor for 0ν2EC is approximately 10⁶, derived from a typical atomic mass splitting Δ ~ 10 keV and a typical electromagnetic decay width Γ_f ~ 10 eV.
- The best experimental half-life limits for 0ν2EC processes are T₁/₂ ~ 10¹⁹–10²² yr, compared to T₁/₂ ~ 10²⁴–10²⁶ yr for 0ν2β⁻ decay.
- The effective Majorana neutrino mass from 0ν2β⁻ experiments is constrained to |m_ββ| ≲ 0.1–0.7 eV.
- The neutron Majorana mass is constrained to Δm < 0.8 × 10⁻³³ m (where m = 939.57 MeV/c² is the neutron Dirac mass), derived from the experimental limit on neutron-antineutron oscillations τ_vac > 2.7 × 10⁸ s.

## Context

This review extends the earlier theoretical work on resonant 0ν2EC by Bernabeu et al. (1983), who first specified a dozen candidate nuclide pairs, and by Sujkowski and Wycech (2004) and Karpeshin and Trzhaskovskaya (2008), who provided updated lists of near-resonant transitions. The key experimental advance enabling this work is the development of Penning-trap mass spectrometry at facilities such as ISOLTRAP at CERN, which now routinely achieves relative mass uncertainties of about 10⁻⁹, sufficient to determine the degeneracy parameter for candidate transitions. The review enables future experimental searches for 0ν2EC in specific nuclides where resonance conditions are favorable, and provides a framework for comparing 0ν2EC sensitivity to that of 0ν2β⁻ decay in probing lepton number violation and Majorana neutrino mass.
