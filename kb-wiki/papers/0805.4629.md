# Hadronic Interactions from Lattice QCD

> We present an overview of recent efforts to calculate the interactions among hadrons using lattice QCD. After outlining the techniques that are used to extract scattering parameters, we detail the latest calculations of meson-meson scattering, baryon-baryon scattering and multi-meson systems obtained with domain-wall valence quarks on the staggered MILC lattices by the NPLQCD collaboration. Estimates of the computational resources required to achieve precision results in the baryon sector are presented.

## Key claim

This review summarizes the NPLQCD collaboration's lattice QCD calculations of hadronic interactions using domain-wall valence quarks on staggered MILC ensembles, but the results are obtained at unphysical pion masses (mπ ∼ 300 MeV) and finite lattice spacings (b ∼ 0.125 fm on coarse lattices), requiring extrapolation to physical quark masses and the continuum limit using effective field theories.

## Method

The authors extract scattering parameters from finite-volume Euclidean-space correlation functions using Lüscher's method, which relates the energy shift of two-particle states in a finite cubic volume to the infinite-volume scattering phase shift. For multi-meson systems, they compute the ground-state energy of n identical bosons in a finite volume and extract both two-body scattering parameters and three-body interactions from the volume dependence. The calculations use a mixed-action approach with domain-wall valence quarks on staggered MILC sea-quark configurations, and the results are analyzed using mixed-action chiral perturbation theory (MAχ-PT) to account for finite lattice spacing effects.

## Key numbers

- Typical pion masses in current calculations: mπ ∼ 300 MeV, compared to physical mπ ∼ 140 MeV
- Lattice volumes have spatial extent L ∼ 2.5–3.5 fm on current ensembles
- Lattice spacings: b ∼ 0.125 fm (coarse MILC), b ∼ 0.09 fm (fine MILC), b ∼ 0.06 fm (super-fine MILC)
- Coefficients for the two-particle energy shift expansion: c1 = −2.837297, c2 = +6.375183
- Geometric constants for n-boson energy shift: I = −8.9136329, J = 16.532316, K = 8.4019240, L = 6.9458079
- Threshold for bound-state identification: x0 = −0.0959006, where x = (pL/2π)²

## Context

This work extends the foundational formalism of Lüscher (1986, 1990) for extracting scattering amplitudes from finite-volume lattice calculations, and applies it to multi-hadron systems using the mixed-action approach developed by the NPLQCD collaboration. The calculations build upon the staggered MILC lattice ensembles and the Chroma software infrastructure from USQCD and Jefferson Laboratory. The results enable future calculations of nuclear interactions directly from QCD, including three-nucleon forces and hyperon-nucleon scattering that are difficult to access experimentally, with the next five years expected to bring calculations at physical quark masses (mπ ∼ 140 MeV) in volumes L ≳ 6 fm with lattice spacings b ≲ 0.06 fm.
