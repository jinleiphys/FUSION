# Comment on "Efimov States and their Fano Resonances in a Neutron-Rich Nucleus"

> By introducing a mass asymmetry in a non-Borromean three-body system, without changing the energy relations, the virtual state pole cannot move from the negative real axis of the complex energy plane (with nonzero width) and become a resonance, because the analytical structure of the unitarity cuts remains the same.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in a non-Borromean three-body system (where at least one two-body subsystem is bound), varying the mass asymmetry or two-body binding energies cannot turn a bound Efimov state into a continuum resonance; the pole can only move to a virtual state on the negative real axis of the complex energy plane, because the analytical structure of the unitarity cuts remains unchanged.

## Method

The authors used a renormalized zero-range three-body model for the $n-n-^{18}$C system (representing $^{20}$C), extending the bound-state equations to the second energy sheet through the $n-^{19}$C elastic scattering cut via the method of Gloeckle. They fixed the $n-n$ virtual-state energy at -143 keV and the three-body ground-state energy of $^{20}$C at -3.5 MeV, then varied the binding energy of $^{19}$C to compute the excited-state energies and elastic scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- $n-n$ virtual-state energy fixed at -143 keV.
- Three-body ground-state energy of $^{20}$C fixed at -3.5 MeV.
- For $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}| = 200$ keV, the excited-state energy $|E_{^{20}{\rm C}}-E_{^{19}{\rm C}}| \simeq 668$ eV (virtual state).
- For $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}| = 170$ keV, the excited-state energy $|E_{^{20}{\rm C}}-E_{^{19}{\rm C}}| \simeq 14$ eV (virtual state).
- For $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}| = 150$ keV, the excited-state energy $|E_{^{20}{\rm C}}-E_{^{19}{\rm C}}| \simeq 115$ eV (bound state).
- $n-^{19}$C scattering lengths: 448.5 fm for $E_{^{19}{\rm C}} = -150$ keV, -411.3 fm for $E_{^{19}{\rm C}} = -180$ keV, and -8.6 fm for $E_{^{19}{\rm C}} = -500$ keV (minus signs indicate virtual states).

## Context

This comment directly challenges the claim by Mazumdar et al. (2007) that a bound Efimov state in $^{20}$C turns into a continuum resonance due to mass asymmetry. The results instead agree with earlier studies of Efimov physics with three equal-mass particles by Adhikari et al. (1982) and Amado and Noble (1972), as well as with the parametric mapping of asymmetric two-body energies by Amorim et al. (1997). The work clarifies that for non-Borromean systems, Efimov excited states become virtual rather than resonant, and that true Efimov resonances require a Borromean system (all subsystems unbound), as seen in the Innsbruck cesium experiment by Kraemer et al. (2006).
