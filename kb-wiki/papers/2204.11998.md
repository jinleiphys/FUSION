# BeAGLE: Benchmark $e$A Generator for LEptoproduction in high energy lepton-nucleus collisions

> The upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) will address several outstanding puzzles in modern nuclear physics. Topics such as the partonic structure of nucleons and nuclei, the origin of their mass and spin, among others, can be understood via the study of high energy electron-proton ($ep$) and electron-nucleus ($e$A) collisions. Achieving the scientific goals of the EIC will require a novel electron-hadron collider and detectors capable to perform high-precision measurements, but also dedicated tools to model and interpret the data. To aid in the latter, we present a general-purpose $e$A Monte Carlo (MC) generator - BeAGLE. In this paper, we provide a general description of the models integrated into BeAGLE, applications of BeAGLE in $e$A physics, implications for detector requirements at the EIC, and the tuning of the parameters in BeAGLE based on available experimental data. Specifically, we focus on a selection of model and data comparisons in particle production in both $ep$ and $e$A collisions, where baseline particle distributions provide essential information to characterize the event. In addition, we investigate the collision geometry determination in $e$A collisions, which could be used as an experimental tool for varying the nuclear density.

## Key claim

BeAGLE is presented as a general-purpose Monte Carlo generator for electron-nucleus collisions at the future Electron-Ion Collider, but the paper acknowledges that coherent diffraction is not currently included in the model and that the lepton-nucleus cross section is assumed to be A times the lepton-nucleon cross section rather than calculated from first principles.

## Method

BeAGLE is a hybrid model combining modules from DPMJet for nuclear geometry and intra-nuclear cascade, PYTHIA-6 for partonic interactions and fragmentation, PyQM for optional partonic energy loss via Salgado-Wiedemann quenching weights, and FLUKA for nuclear remnant breakup including evaporation, fission, and de-excitation. The generator uses EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions and a Glauber-type model to select struck nucleons, with three optional modes for multiple nucleon scattering and shadowing controlled by the parameter genShd. The formation-zone intra-nuclear cascade uses a characteristic time scale tau_0, tuned to E665 neutron multiplicity data from muon-lead collisions.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range for the EIC: 20 to 140 GeV
- EIC luminosities: 10^33-34 cm^-2 s^-1
- EIC beam polarization: 70% for electron, proton, and light-ion beams
- Default tau_0 parameter in BeAGLE: 10 fm, determined from E665 muon-lead data where average neutron multiplicity was 4.7 +/- 0.5
- Coherent diffractive fraction assumed for tuning: 24% (f = 0.24), inspired by HERA measurements where diffractive events contribute about 15% to total deep inelastic cross-section in ep collisions
- PYTHIA-6 tuned parameters: PARJ(170) = 0.32, PARJ(21) = PARP(91) = 0.4 (default settings)
- ZEUS leading proton data kinematic range: Q^2 > 3 GeV^2, 45 < W < 225 GeV, y > 0.03, p_T^2 < 0.5 GeV^2, x_L > 0.32
- E665 muon-xenon data kinematic cuts: theta > 3.5 mrad, Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, 8 < W < 30 GeV, 0.1 < nu/E_mu < 0.85, muon beam energy 490 GeV

## Context

This work extends the BeAGLE generator from previous studies focused on exclusive observables in the far-forward region (Chang 2021) to inclusive particle production in both forward and central regions, with comparisons to fixed-target data from the E665 experiment at Fermilab and leading proton data from the ZEUS experiment at HERA. The generator has already been used for exploring diffractive and spectator-tagging physics and detector requirements for the far-forward region at the EIC (AbdulKhalek 2021). The paper enables future studies of collision geometry determination via neutron detection from nuclear breakup, which could serve as an experimental tool for varying nuclear density at the EIC, and identifies the need to implement coherent lepton-nucleus interactions as a future development.
