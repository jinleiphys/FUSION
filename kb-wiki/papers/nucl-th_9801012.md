# Electron capture on iron group nuclei

> We present Gamow-Teller strength distributions from shell model Monte Carlo studies of fp-shell nuclei that may play an important role in the pre-collapse evolution of supernovae. We then use these strength distributions to calculate the electron-capture cross sections and rates in the zero-momentum transfer limit. We also discuss the thermal behavior of the cross sections. We find large differences in these cross sections and rates when compared to the naive single-particle estimates. These differences need to be taken into account for improved modeling of the early stages of type II supernova evolution.

# Electron capture on iron group nuclei

## Key claim

The authors find that shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations of Gamow-Teller strength distributions for fp-shell nuclei yield electron-capture cross sections and rates that differ substantially from the naive single-particle estimates used in previous supernova models, but the agreement with currently recommended total rates is reasonable for even-even parent nuclei while the Gamow-Teller contribution to odd-A nuclei is found to be negligible at low temperatures, contrary to prior compilations.

## Method

The authors performed shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations in the full 0ħω fp-shell space using the KB3 residual interaction to obtain Gamow-Teller strength distributions for iron-group nuclei at finite nuclear temperatures. They then used these strength distributions to compute electron-capture cross sections in the zero-momentum transfer limit, folding the cross sections with a relativistic degenerate electron gas to obtain capture rates as a function of electron chemical potential. The spin operator was renormalized by a factor of 0.8 to account for observed quenching, and the odd-A nuclei were treated via a number-projection technique from neighboring even-even systems.

## Key numbers

- Total Gamow-Teller strength for ⁴⁵Sc from SMMC: 0.52 ± 0.08, compared to diagonalization result of 0.60 and experimental value of 0.48 up to 9 MeV excitation
- SMMC calculations for odd-A nuclei limited to temperatures T_N ≥ 0.8 MeV; even-even nuclei calculated at T_N = 0.5 MeV
- Uncertainty in Gamow-Teller centroid position: ±0.5 MeV; decreasing (increasing) centroid by 0.5 MeV changes the rate by a factor of approximately 1.4 (1.3)
- For ⁵⁵Co, SMMC predicts Gamow-Teller contribution to capture rate is negligible (only 13% of the rate given in Aufderheide compilation), where Aufderheide attributed 87% to Gamow-Teller transitions
- Typical presupernova conditions: μ_e ≈ 1.5–2 MeV at T_N ≈ 0.4 MeV and ρ_7 ≈ 6
- Gamow-Teller peak position: ~2 MeV daughter excitation for even-even parents, ~5 MeV for odd-A parents
- KB3 interaction describes masses of pf-shell nuclei within typically 500 keV of data

## Context

This work extends the SMMC Gamow-Teller studies of Radha (1997) to additional nuclei important for presupernova collapse (⁵⁵,⁵⁷Co, ⁵⁶Ni, ⁵⁰,⁵²Cr, ⁵⁵,⁵⁸Fe, ⁵⁰Ti) and provides the first consistent treatment of thermal effects on Gamow-Teller strength distributions for electron-capture calculations. The results contradict the parametrization of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN) and the subsequent update by Aufderheide, which placed the Gamow-Teller resonance at too high excitation for even-even nuclei and too low for odd-A nuclei. The findings enable improved modeling of the early stages of type II supernova evolution by providing microscopically determined Gamow-Teller contributions, and suggest that for odd-A nuclei only the non-Gamow-Teller fraction of compiled rates should be used.
