# New extraction of alpha_s(MZ) from proton DIS data

> An exploratory study for a new determination of the strong coupling constant alpha_s(MZ) from existing world data on the proton structure function F2p in the Q**2-range from ~ 5 to ~ 120 (GeV/c)**2 is presented. The main features of our approach are: 1) the use of low-order Nachtmann moments evaluated with a direct contribution from data larger than 70% of the total; 2) the inclusion of high-order perturbative effects through the soft gluon resummation technique; 3) a direct control over higher-twist effects; and 4) the independence from any specific choice of the x-shape of the input parton distributions. At next-to-leading order we get alpha_s(MZ) = 0.1209 +/- 0.0010 (stat.) +/- 0.0015 (syst.) with a significant dependence upon the order of the moment used. Including soft gluon effects we obtain alpha_s(MZ) = 0.1188 +/- 0.0010 (stat.) +/- 0.0014 (syst.) with a remarkable better stability against the order of the moment. Our findings are compared with recent DIS determinations of alpha_s(MZ) and directions for future improvements are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors extract the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s(M_Z)\) from proton deep inelastic scattering data using low-order Nachtmann moments, claiming that their method avoids dependence on the assumed \(x\)-shape of input parton distributions and provides direct control over higher-twist effects; however, the result at next-to-leading order (NLO) shows a significant dependence on the order of the moment used, and the inclusion of soft gluon resummation is required to achieve stability across moments.

## Method

The authors evaluate low-order Nachtmann moments (n = 4, 6, 8) of the proton structure function \(F_2^p\) from world data in the \(Q^2\) range from approximately 5 to 120 (GeV/c)^2, using a direct experimental contribution larger than 70% of the total moment. They perform a perturbative QCD analysis of the scaling violations of these moments at NLO and then include high-order perturbative effects via soft gluon resummation at next-to-leading-log (NLL) accuracy, while controlling higher-twist contributions through a phenomenological power-series ansatz.

## Key numbers

- At NLO: \(\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1209 \pm 0.0010 \text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.0015 \text{ (syst.)}\), with a significant dependence upon the order of the moment used.
- Including soft gluon resummation: \(\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1188 \pm 0.0010 \text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.0014 \text{ (syst.)}\), with better stability against the order \(n\) of the moment for \(n < 8\).
- The analysis covers the \(Q^2\) range from approximately 5 to 120 (GeV/c)^2, with the \(b\)-quark threshold fixed at \(Q_b^2 = 25\) (GeV/c)^2.
- The direct experimental contribution to the moments is larger than 70% of the total; at \(Q^2 \gsim 75\) (GeV/c)^2 for n = 4 and n = 6, and at \(Q^2 \approx 20\) (GeV/c)^2 for n = 8, the contribution drops to between 50% and 70%.

## Context

This work extends the method of truncated moments used by Forte et al. (2002) and the neural network parameterization approach, but avoids restricting the \(Q^2\) range to above 20 (GeV/c)^2, instead extending down to approximately 5 (GeV/c)^2 while controlling higher-twist effects. The results are compared with recent DIS determinations of \(\alpha_s(M_Z)\) by Santiago and Yndurain (2001) and Alekhin (2003), and the authors suggest that future improvements require more precise data in the \(Q^2\) range from 5 to 15 (GeV/c)^2, consideration of more Nachtmann moments, and inclusion of full NNLO effects and NNLL resummation.
