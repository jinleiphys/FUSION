# On shielding of nuclear electric dipole moments in atoms

> We demonstrate explicitly that some recent calculations of atomic electric dipole moments (EDM) are incomplete. A contribution overlooked therein is pointed out. When included, it cancels exactly the result of those calculations, and thus restores the standard conclusions for nuclear EDM in atoms.

# On shielding of nuclear electric dipole moments in atoms

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that recent calculations by Fujita and collaborators (2004) claiming large atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) directly proportional to the neutron EDM are incomplete; when a previously overlooked third-order contribution from the electron-nucleus interaction is included, it exactly cancels the claimed effect, restoring the standard conclusion that nuclear EDMs in atoms are strongly suppressed by the Schiff shielding mechanism.

## Method

The authors analyze the deuterium atom as a simplified case, using nonrelativistic quantum mechanics to separate the Hamiltonian into atomic, nuclear, and perturbation terms. They calculate the second-order contribution to the deuteron EDM from the P-odd, T-odd neutron-proton interaction combined with an external electric field, then compute the previously omitted third-order contribution involving the electron-nucleus Coulomb perturbation, and demonstrate exact cancellation between the two terms using completeness relations for atomic states.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on $^{199}$Hg atomic EDM: $d(^{199}{\rm Hg}) < 2.1 \times 10^{-28}\; e$ cm (from Regan et al., 2001)
- Corresponding bound on neutron EDM from $^{199}$Hg: $|d_n| < 4.0 \times 10^{-25}\; e$ cm
- Direct neutron EDM measurements give: $|d_n| < (0.6 - 1.0) \times 10^{-25}\; e$ cm
- Schiff suppression factor for mercury: $\eta \sim 10^{-4}$, derived from $\eta \sim (A^{1/3}r_0 / (a/Z))^2$ with $a=0.5\times 10^{-8}$ cm and $r_0 =1.2\times 10^{-13}$ cm
- Fujita et al. (2004) claimed relations: $d(^{199}{\rm Hg}) \simeq -2.8 d_n$, $d(^{129}{\rm Xe}) \simeq 1.6 d_n$, $d({\rm D}) \simeq 0.017 d_n$
- Fujita et al. (2004) extracted neutron EDM bound: $d_n \simeq (0.37\pm 0.17 \pm 0.14)\times 10^{-28}\; e$ cm

## Context

This work restores the standard shielding theorem originally formulated by Purcell and Ramsey (1950) and Schiff (1963), which states that for a neutral atom of point particles in electrostatic equilibrium, the internal field exactly cancels the external field at each charged particle, making nuclear EDMs unobservable. The finite-size Schiff moment mechanism, which gives the actual small observable effect, was developed by Khriplovich and collaborators in prior work. The paper corrects the erroneous claims of Fujita and collaborators (2004) that would have implied orders-of-magnitude larger atomic EDMs, and reaffirms that the deuteron Schiff moment vanishes if the strong proton-neutron potential is spin-independent, providing a specific condition for future calculations of nuclear EDM effects in atoms.
