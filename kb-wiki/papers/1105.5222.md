# Equation of state of the neutron star matter, and the nuclear symmetry energy

> The nuclear mean-field potentials obtained in the Hartree-Fock method with different choices of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction have been used to study the equation of state (EOS) of the neutron star (NS) matter. The EOS of the uniform NS core has been calculated for the np$eμ$ composition in the $β$-equilibrium at zero temperature, using version Sly4 of the Skyrme interaction as well as two density-dependent versions of the finite-range M3Y interaction (CDM3Y$n$ and M3Y-P$n$), and versions D1S and D1N of the Gogny interaction. Although the considered effective NN interactions were proven to be quite realistic in numerous nuclear structure and/or reaction studies, they give quite different behaviors of the symmetry energy of nuclear matter at supranuclear densities that lead to the \emph{soft} and \emph{stiff} scenarios discussed recently in the literature. Different EOS's of the NS core and the EOS of the NS crust given by the compressible liquid drop model have been used as input of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to study how the nuclear symmetry energy affects the model prediction of different NS properties, like the cooling process as well as the gravitational mass, radius, and moment of inertia.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy at supranuclear densities, which determines whether the equation of state is "soft" or "stiff," strongly affects predictions of neutron star properties such as mass, radius, moment of inertia, and cooling via the direct Urca process; however, the authors note that both groups of interactions can describe some empirical mass-radius data, while the comparison with data from Steiner et al. (2010) favors the stiff-type interactions.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter using five effective nucleon-nucleon interactions (SLy4 Skyrme, CDM3Yn and M3Y-Pn finite-range M3Y, and D1S and D1N Gogny) to obtain the energy density and pressure of neutron star matter with np e mu composition in beta-equilibrium at zero temperature. They combined these core equations of state with a crust equation of state from the compressible liquid drop model (using SLy4 parameters) and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to predict gravitational mass, radius, and moment of inertia for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy coefficient at saturation density is J = 29 ± 3 MeV for all interactions considered.
- For soft-type interactions (M3Y-Pn, D1S, D1N), the proton fraction reaches a maximum of about 4% at n_b ≈ 0.2 fm^{-3} and then decreases to zero at n_b > 0.6 fm^{-3}.
- For stiff-type CDM3Yn interactions, the proton fraction exceeds 30% at the maximum central density n_c ≈ 1.3 fm^{-3}.
- The direct Urca threshold for the proton fraction has its lowest value of 11.1% for the muon-free case; for CDM3Yn interactions this threshold is crossed at n_b ≈ 0.45 fm^{-3}, well below the maximum central densities of 1.2-1.3 fm^{-3}.
- The maximum gravitational masses from stiff-type interactions (SLy4 and CDM3Y6) are close to about 2 solar masses.
- For the D1N interaction, the maximum gravitational mass radius R_G is almost 1 km smaller than the minimum empirical limit R ≳ 3.6 + 3.9(M/M_⊙) km.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Hartree-Fock study (Than et al., 2009) that identified two families of effective interactions giving soft or stiff symmetry energy behavior, and it applies those interactions to neutron star structure calculations. The results are compared with empirical mass-radius constraints from Ozel et al. (2010) and Steiner et al. (2010), and with ab-initio calculations by Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (1998) and Gandolfi et al. (2010). The findings enable future studies to discriminate between soft and stiff symmetry energy scenarios using combined mass, radius, and cooling observations of neutron stars.
