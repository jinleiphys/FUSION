# Nucleon axial, tensor and scalar charges and $σ$-terms in lattice QCD

> We determine the nucleon axial, scalar and tensor charges within lattice Quantum Chromodynamics including all contributions from valence and sea quarks. We analyze three gauge ensembles simulated within the twisted mass formulation at approximately physical value of the pion mass. Two of these ensembles are simulated with two dynamical light quarks and lattice spacing $a=0.094$~fm and the third with $a=0.08$~fm includes in addition the strange and charm quarks in the sea. After comparing the results among these three ensembles, we quote as final values our most accurate analysis using the latter ensemble. For the nucleon isovector axial charge we find $1.286(23)$ in agreement with the experimental value. We provide the flavor decomposition of the intrinsic spin $\frac{1}{2}ΔΣ^q$ carried by quarks in the nucleon obtaining for the up, down, strange and charm quarks $\frac{1}{2}ΔΣ^{u}=0.431(8)$, $\frac{1}{2}ΔΣ^{d}=-0.212(8)$, $\frac{1}{2}ΔΣ^{s}=-0.023(4)$ and $\frac{1}{2}ΔΣ^{c}=-0.005(2)$, respectively. The corresponding values of the tensor and scalar charges for each quark flavor are also evaluated providing valuable input for experimental searches for beyond the standard model physics. In addition, we extract the nucleon $σ$-terms and find for the light quark content $σ_{πN}=41.6(3.8)$~MeV and for the strange $σ_{s}=45.6(6.2)$~MeV. The y-parameter that is used in phenomenological studies we find $y=0.078(7)$.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have computed the nucleon axial, scalar, and tensor charges, as well as sigma-terms, using lattice QCD at the physical pion mass with all quark flavors included in the sea, achieving agreement with the experimental value of the isovector axial charge only after careful treatment of excited-state contamination using multiple source-sink separations and multi-state fits.

## Method

The authors used three gauge ensembles generated with the twisted mass fermion formulation at approximately the physical pion mass: two with two dynamical light quarks (Nf=2) at lattice spacing a=0.094 fm and one with four dynamical quarks (Nf=2+1+1) at a=0.08 fm. They computed two- and three-point correlation functions for the nucleon, including both connected and disconnected contributions, and extracted the charges by constructing ratios that plateau at large Euclidean times. To control excited-state contamination, they applied plateau fits, two-state fits, and three-state fits to the correlators, using the Nf=2+1+1 ensemble with the highest statistics for their final results.

## Key numbers

* Isovector axial charge g_A^(u-d) = 1.286(23) from the Nf=2+1+1 ensemble, in agreement with the experimental value.
* Flavor decomposition of intrinsic spin: 1/2 Delta Sigma^u = 0.431(8), 1/2 Delta Sigma^d = -0.212(8), 1/2 Delta Sigma^s = -0.023(4), 1/2 Delta Sigma^c = -0.005(2).
* Nucleon sigma-term for light quarks: sigma_piN = 41.6(3.8) MeV.
* Strange sigma-term: sigma_s = 45.6(6.2) MeV.
* y-parameter = 0.078(7).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies that had systematically underestimated the isovector axial charge, such as those summarized in the FLAG report (Aoki 2019), by using ensembles at the physical pion mass and including strange and charm quarks in the sea. The computation provides first-principles results for the flavor-diagonal tensor and scalar charges needed for beyond-the-Standard-Model searches, and the sigma-terms are relevant for dark matter direct detection experiments. The results enable more reliable input for experimental programs including DUNE, COHERENT, and the Electron Ion Collider.
