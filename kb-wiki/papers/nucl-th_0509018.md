# Cold dilute neutron matter on the lattice II: Results in the unitary limit

> This is the second of two papers which investigate cold dilute neutron matter on the lattice using pionless effective field theory. In the unitary limit, where the effective range is zero and scattering length is infinite, simple scaling relations relate thermodynamic functions at different temperatures. When the second virial coefficient is properly tuned, we find that the lattice results obey these scaling relations. We compute the energy per particle, pressure, spin susceptibility, dineutron correlation function, and an upper bound for the superfluid critical temperature.

## Key claim

When the second virial coefficient is properly tuned, lattice results for cold dilute neutron matter in the unitary limit obey the expected scaling relations, but the authors can only provide bounds on the universal parameter xi (between 0.07 and 0.42) and an upper bound on the superfluid critical temperature (less than 0.14 T_F) rather than precise values.

## Method

The authors used pionless effective field theory on a lattice with spatial spacing a = (50 MeV)^{-1} and temporal spacing a_t = (24 MeV)^{-1}, employing the hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm to generate Hubbard-Stratonovich field configurations. They tuned the four-fermion coupling constant C so that the second virial coefficient b_2(T) equals 3 * 2^{-5/2} at each temperature, rather than tuning to the zero-temperature scattering length. They computed the density, energy per particle, pressure, spin susceptibility, and dineutron correlation function for temperatures from 1 to 4 MeV on lattice volumes ranging from 4^3 x 6 to 6^3 x 24.

## Key numbers

- The constant eta defining the coupling in the unitary limit is eta = 3.957, giving |U|/t = 2*eta = 7.914 for the equivalent attractive Hubbard model.
- Spatial lattice spacing a = (50 MeV)^{-1} and temporal lattice spacing a_t = (24 MeV)^{-1}.
- The quarter-filled lattice corresponds to a density of 0.0081 fm^{-3}.
- The second virial coefficient is tuned to b_2(T) = 3 * 2^{-5/2} = 0.530.
- The coupling constant C ranges from -0.971 x 10^{-4} MeV^{-2} at T=4 MeV to -1.128 x 10^{-4} MeV^{-2} at T=0.5 MeV.
- The energy per particle divided by (3/5)E_F has an intercept of 0.07 at the lowest T/T_F values, giving the bound 0.07 <= xi <= 0.42 for the zero-temperature Bertsch parameter.
- The superfluid critical temperature satisfies T_C/T_F < 0.14.

## Context

This paper is the second of two papers (the first is referred to as [I]) investigating cold dilute neutron matter on the lattice using pionless effective field theory. It extends the virial expansion results of Part I to the unitary limit and demonstrates improved scaling behavior by tuning the second virial coefficient rather than the zero-temperature scattering length. The results provide bounds on the universal parameter xi that are marginally consistent with the Green function Monte Carlo result xi = 0.44 from Carlson (2003), and the upper bound on T_C/T_F of 0.14 is lower than the result T_C = 0.22(3) T_F from Bulgac (2005) but consistent with T_C = 0.035(4) T_F from Wingate (2005).
