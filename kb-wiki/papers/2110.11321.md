# Nuclear medium effects in neutrino- and antineutrino-nucleus scattering

> In this paper we study the influence of nuclear medium effects on quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering processes. We focus on effects provided by the nuclear mean field and random phase correlations and pay special attention to differences between neutrino- and antineutrino-induced reactions. We confront our results with the T2K and MiniBooNE data for both neutrinos and antineutrinos and the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry. In view of the recently published ab initio results we provide a careful comparison between our cross section predictions and the ab-initio calculations.

# Nuclear medium effects in neutrino- and antineutrino-nucleus scattering

## Key claim

Nuclear medium effects from the mean field and random phase approximation (RPA) correlations significantly influence quasi-elastic neutrino- and antineutrino-nucleus cross sections, with the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry being largely insensitive to these medium effects except at low incoming energies and small momentum transfers, where Coulomb effects on the outgoing lepton and binding energy differences become important.

## Method

The authors use a Hartree-Fock continuum random phase approximation (HF-CRPA) approach with the SkE2 Skyrme effective interaction to calculate inclusive charged-current quasi-elastic cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering off nuclei. The CRPA equations are solved in coordinate space, allowing full treatment of the continuum, with relativistic effects included via the prescription of Jeschonnek (1997) and Coulomb distortion of the outgoing lepton treated using a combined Fermi function and modified effective momentum approximation (MEMA) scheme. Short-range correlations and meson-exchange currents are not included in the HF-CRPA model itself; instead, the SuSAv2 2p2h predictions from Megias et al. (2017) are added when comparing to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb energy for a sphere with charge Z and radius 1.2A^(1/3) is approximately 4.5 MeV for carbon and 10 MeV for calcium, producing an observed shift in cross sections around twice as large for calcium compared to carbon
- For the T2K target composition (87% carbon, 7.4% hydrogen, 3.7% oxygen, 2.7% heavier elements by mass), there is approximately 1 hydrogen particle per carbon nucleus
- The asymmetry for symmetric even-even nuclei is practically independent of the nucleus except at small incoming energies (100 MeV), where nuclear medium effects and binding-energy differences become larger
- For 40Ca at 800 MeV incoming energy, the difference between neutrino and antineutrino responses is due solely to the Coulomb potential, with the effect decreasing for larger momentum transfers

## Context

This work extends the HF-CRPA modeling previously applied to electron scattering (Pandey 2016, Nikolakopoulos 2019) and neutrino scattering to a systematic comparison of neutrino versus antineutrino induced reactions, confronting predictions with T2K data from 2020 and MiniBooNE data. The authors provide a careful comparison with ab-initio Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) results from Lovato et al. (2016), showing that CRPA one-body results match GFMC one-body results almost perfectly for forward bins, while adding SuSAv2 MEC contributions yields excellent agreement with GFMC 1b+2b results. This enables benchmarking of more efficient nuclear models against exact ab-initio calculations for use in neutrino oscillation analyses, particularly for the T2K and MiniBooNE experiments seeking to determine the CP-violating phase delta.
