# Extraction of Polarization Parameters in the $\mathrm{\bar{p} p} \rightarrow \barΩ Ω$ Reaction

> A method to extract the polarization of $Ω$ hyperons produced via the strong interaction is presented. Assuming they are spin 3/2 particles, the corresponding spin density matrix can be written in terms of seven non-zero polarization parameters, all retrievable from the angular distribution of the decay products. Moreover by considering the full decay chain $Ω\rightarrow ΛK\rightarrow pπK$ the magnitude of the asymmetry parameters $β_Ω$ and $γ_Ω$ can be obtained. This method, applied here to the specific $Ω$ case, can be generalized to any weakly decaying hyperon and is perfectly suited for the PANDA experiment where hyperon-antihyperon pairs will be copiously produced in proton-antiproton collisions. The aim is to take a step forward towards the understanding of the mechanism that reigns strangeness production in these processes.

## Key claim

A method is presented to extract the seven non-zero polarization parameters of the spin-3/2 Ω hyperon produced in the strong reaction p̅p → Ω̅Ω, using the angular distributions of the decay products in the full decay chain Ω → ΛK → pπK; the method also allows determination of the magnitude of the asymmetry parameters β_Ω and γ_Ω, which are currently unknown, though the extracted polarization parameters can only be determined up to a sign because γ_Ω appears in their expressions.

## Method

The spin density matrix for a spin-3/2 hyperon produced via the strong interaction is reduced by parity conservation to seven non-zero polarization parameters r_M^L. The angular distribution of the first decay (Ω → ΛK) is expressed in terms of these parameters and the asymmetry parameter α_Ω, using the Method of Moments to extract three of them. The full decay chain (Ω → ΛK → pπK) is then analyzed by applying the T-matrix for the Λ → pπ weak decay, yielding a joint angular distribution that depends on all four decay angles and contains the remaining four polarization parameters as well as β_Ω and γ_Ω, which are extracted via additional moment integrals.

## Key numbers

- α_Ω = 0.0180 ± 0.0024 (for Ω → ΛK decay, from Olive 2016)
- α_Λ = 0.642 ± 0.013 (for Λ → pπ decay, from Olive 2016)
- The seven non-zero polarization parameters are: r_{-3}^3, r_{-2}^3, r_{-1}^3, r_2^2, r_1^2, r_0^2, and r_{-1}^1
- The asymmetry parameters satisfy α^2 + β^2 + γ^2 = 1
- The total polarization of the Ω hyperon is defined as d(ρ) = √(∑_{L=1}^{2j} ∑_{M=-L}^{L} (r_M^L)^2)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Doncel et al. (1973, 1972) for spin density matrices to the specific case of Ω hyperons produced in p̅p collisions, and is designed for the upcoming PANDA experiment where hyperon-antihyperon pairs will be copiously produced. The method enables the first determination of the asymmetry parameters β_Ω and γ_Ω, which can be used for testing CP violation in hyperon weak decays as discussed by Donoghue (1986) and He (1991), and can be generalized to any weakly decaying hyperon.
