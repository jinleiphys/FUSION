# Charmonia formation in quark-gluon plasma

> Using the color evaporation model, the cross section for charmonium production in p+p collision is calculated in quark-gluon plasma. The threshold energy for open charms is given by the free energy potential from lattice calculations, the initial charm quark pairs by the Pythia simulations, and their time evolution by solving the Langevin equation. It is found that the threshold energy which decreases with temperature reduces the cross section while the invariant mass of charm pair which decreases by collisions enhances it. As a result, charmonia production is suppressed by $30$\sim$50\%$ while $J/ψ$ production is similar or enhanced compared to in vacuum.

# Charmonia formation in quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

Using the color evaporation model generalized to the quark-gluon plasma phase, charmonium production in p+p collisions is suppressed by 30–50% in QGP compared to vacuum, but the J/ψ yield specifically is similar or enhanced because excited charmonium states dissolve above the critical temperature.

## Method

The color evaporation model was modified for QGP by replacing the D-meson threshold energy for open charm with the sum of dressed charm and anticharm quark masses from the lattice free energy potential, and by evolving the initial charm quark pair invariant mass distribution (generated by Pythia at √s = 200 GeV) via the Langevin equation with drag and diffusion coefficients from lattice QCD and pQCD. The formation time of charmonium was calculated from dispersion relations and the lattice free energy potential, and the invariant mass distribution at the formation time was used to compute the hidden charm cross section.

## Key numbers

- Bare charm quark mass taken as 1.25 GeV; critical temperature Tc = 170 MeV
- No charmonium bound state found above 1.13 Tc using the free energy potential from lattice QCD
- J/ψ formation time: about 0.6 fm/c at Tc, increasing to 30 fm/c near the dissociation temperature
- The spatial diffusion constant used: 2πTD = 2 from lattice QCD
- Cross section ratio for charmonium production in QGP vs. vacuum: 70% at 1.0 Tc and 50% at 1.1 Tc
- J/ψ production cross section ratio in QGP vs. vacuum: between 1.0 and 1.4 (enhanced or similar)

## Context

This work extends the color evaporation model of Amundson (1996) to finite-temperature QGP, building on the Matsui and Satz (1986) proposal that quarkonium suppression signals QGP formation. The study uses lattice free energy potentials from Kaczmarek (2005) and the Langevin approach of Moore and Teaney (2004). It enables refined interpretation of J/ψ suppression measurements at RHIC and LHC by separating the competing effects of threshold energy reduction (which suppresses production) and spectral softening (which enhances it), and by accounting for the dissolution of excited charmonium states above Tc.
