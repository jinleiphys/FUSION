# Confinement of $N$-body systems and non-integer dimensions

> The squeezing process of a three-dimensional quantum system by use of an external deformed one-body oscillator potential can also be described by the $d$-method, without external field and where the dimension can take non-integer values. In this work we first generalize both methods to $N$ particles and any transition between dimensions below $3$. Once this is done, the use of harmonic oscillator interactions between the particles allows complete analytic solutions of both methods, and a direct comparison between them is possible. Assuming that both methods describe the same process, leading to the same ground state energy and wave function, an analytic equivalence between the methods arises. The equivalence between both methods and the validity of the derived analytic relation between them is first tested for two identical bosons and for squeezing transitions from 3 to 2 and 1 dimensions, as well as from 2 to 1 dimension. We also investigate the symmetric squeezing from 3 to 1 dimensions of a system made of three identical bosons. We have in all the cases found that the derived analytic relations between the two methods work very well. This fact permits to relate both methods also for large squeezing scenarios, where the brute force numerical calculation with the external field is too much demanding from the numerical point of view, especially for systems with more than two particles.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the squeezing of an N-body quantum system from a higher to a lower integer dimension (e.g., 3D to 2D) can be equivalently described either by an external deformed oscillator potential or by the d-method using non-integer dimensions, and that for harmonic oscillator particle-particle interactions an exact analytic translation exists between the two methods. The hedge is that this equivalence is derived and tested only for the specific case of harmonic oscillator interactions between particles, and the authors note that for realistic short-range potentials the translation requires an appropriately adjusted oscillator frequency parameter.

## Method

The authors first generalize both the external squeezing method and the d-method to N particles for transitions between dimensions below 3. They then introduce harmonic oscillator two-body interactions between particles, which allows complete analytic solutions of the Schrodinger equation for both methods. By equating the ground state energies and wave functions from the two formulations, they derive an analytic relation between the squeezing frequencies and the non-integer dimension parameter d. The equivalence is tested numerically for two identical bosons undergoing 3D-to-2D, 3D-to-1D, and 2D-to-1D transitions, and for three identical bosons undergoing symmetric 3D-to-1D squeezing.

## Key numbers

* For the ground state of identical bosons, the relation between dimension d and squeezing frequencies is d = sqrt(1 + omega_x^2/omega_pp^2) - omega_x/omega_pp + sqrt(1 + omega_y^2/omega_pp^2) - omega_y/omega_pp + sqrt(1 + omega_z^2/omega_pp^2) - omega_z/omega_pp, which is independent of the particle number N.
* For symmetric squeezing from 3D to 2D, the relation between squeezing frequency and dimension is omega_ho/omega_pp = (3-d)(d-1)/(2(d-2)).
* For symmetric squeezing from 3D to 1D, the relation is omega_ho/omega_pp = (3-d)(d+1)/(2(d-1)).
* For symmetric squeezing from 2D to 1D, the relation is omega_ho/omega_pp = (2-d)d/(2(d-1)).
* The scaling factors relating the d-method wave function to the external field wave function are 1/s_x^2 = sqrt(1 + omega_x^2/omega_pp^2), with analogous expressions for y and z directions.

## Context

This work extends the d-method previously developed by Nieves (2001) and Garrido (2019a, 2019b) for two and three particles to an arbitrary number N of particles, and provides analytic translations for any transition between integer dimensions below 3. The method enables calculations for large squeezing scenarios where brute-force numerical calculations with external fields become prohibitively demanding, especially for systems with more than two particles. The results connect to experimental work on magnetic-optical trapping and deformed geometries by Greiner (2001), Bloch (2005), and others, and to theoretical studies of the Efimov effect under dimensional reduction by Sandoval (2018) and Garrido (2021, 2023).
