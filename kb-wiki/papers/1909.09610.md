# Flavor structures of charged fermions and massive neutrinos

> Most of the free parameters in the Standard Model (SM) -- a quantum field theory which has successfully elucidated the behaviors of strong, weak and electromagnetic interactions of all the known fundamental particles, come from the lepton and quark flavors. The discovery of neutrino oscillations has proved that the SM is incomplete, at least in its lepton sector; and thus the door of opportunity is opened to exploring new physics beyond the SM and solving a number of flavor puzzles. In this review article we give an overview of important progress made in understanding the mass spectra, flavor mixing patterns, CP-violating effects and underlying flavor structures of charged leptons, neutrinos and quarks in the past twenty years. After introducing the standard pictures of fermion mass generation, flavor mixing and CP violation in the SM extended with the presence of massive Dirac or Majorana neutrinos, we briefly summarize current experimental knowledge about the flavor parameters of quarks and leptons. Various ways of describing flavor mixing and CP violation are discussed, the renormalization-group evolution of flavor parameters is illuminated, and the matter effects on neutrino oscillations are interpreted. Taking account of possible extra neutrino species, we propose a standard parametrization of the $6\times 6$ flavor mixing matrix and comment on the phenomenological aspects of heavy, keV-scale and light sterile neutrinos. We pay particular attention to those novel and essentially model-independent ideas or approaches regarding how to determine the Yukawa textures of Dirac fermions and the effective mass matrix of Majorana neutrinos, including simple discrete and continuous flavor symmetries. An outlook to the future development in unravelling the mysteries of flavor structures is also given.

## Key claim

This review argues that while the Standard Model extended with massive neutrinos successfully describes observed flavor phenomena, it contains too many free parameters (twenty or twenty-two depending on whether neutrinos are Dirac or Majorana) that must be experimentally determined rather than theoretically predicted, leaving the fundamental origin of fermion mass hierarchies, flavor mixing patterns, and CP violation unexplained.

## Method

The paper systematically reviews the standard formalism of fermion mass generation, flavor mixing, and CP violation in the Standard Model extended with massive Dirac or Majorana neutrinos, including the parametrization of the 3x3 PMNS and CKM matrices. It surveys current experimental constraints on quark and lepton flavor parameters, discusses renormalization-group evolution of flavor parameters, matter effects on neutrino oscillations, and proposes a standard parametrization for the 6x6 flavor mixing matrix that includes possible sterile neutrino species. The review also examines model-independent approaches to determining Yukawa textures and effective Majorana mass matrices using discrete and continuous flavor symmetries.

## Key numbers

- Twenty (or twenty-two) flavor parameters exist in the three-flavor scheme of quarks and leptons, depending on whether massive neutrinos are Dirac or Majorana fermions
- The charged fermion mass spectrum spans nearly six orders of magnitude, with a "flavor desert" of about six orders of magnitude between neutral and charged fermion masses
- The Planck constraint on the sum of three neutrino masses sets an upper bound of 0.12 eV at the 95% confidence level (Aghanim 2018)
- Quark mixing angles: ϑ₁₂ ≈ 13°, ϑ₁₃ ≈ 0.21°, ϑ₂₃ ≈ 2.4°, with Wolfenstein expansion parameter λ ≈ sinϑ₁₂ ≈ 0.22
- The CKM matrix shows hierarchical structure V = I + O(λ) + O(λ²) + ..., with quark mass ratios following m_u/m_c ~ m_c/m_t ~ λ⁴ and m_d/m_s ~ m_s/m_b ~ λ²
- Current neutrino oscillation data and electroweak precision measurements constrain possible PMNS unitarity violation below O(10⁻²)

## Context

This review extends the comprehensive treatment of flavor physics in the Standard Model, building on the foundational work of Cabibbo (1963), Kobayashi and Maskawa (1973), and the Pontecorvo-Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata framework for neutrino mixing. It synthesizes progress from the past twenty years following the discovery of neutrino oscillations, connecting to specific model-building approaches such as the Georgi-Jarlskog mass relations (1979) at the GUT scale and the canonical seesaw mechanism (Minkowski 1977; Yanagida 1979; Gell-Mann, Ramond, and Slansky 1980; Glashow 1979; Mohapatra and Senjanovic 1979). The review enables future work by cataloging open puzzles, the flavor hierarchy problem, the flavor desert, the mechanism of neutrino mass generation, and the interpretation of observed mixing patterns, and by providing a standardized parametrization framework for incorporating sterile neutrinos into phenomenological analyses.
