# Leading logarithms for the nucleon mass

> Within the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory approach, we have studied the leading logarithm behaviour of the nucleon mass up to four-loop order exactly and we present some results up to six-loop order as well as an all-order conjecture. The same methods allow to calculate the main logarithm multiplying the terms with fractional powers of the quark mass. We calculate thus the coefficients of $m^{2n+1}\log^{(n-1)}(μ^2/m^2)$ and $m^{2n+2}\log^n(μ^2/m^2)$, with $m$ the lowest-order pion mass. A side result is the leading divergence for a general heavy baryon loop integral.

## Key claim

Within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, the leading logarithmic (LLog) contributions to the nucleon mass can be computed to all orders using only one-loop beta functions, despite the fact that the renormalization group order (RGO) is not in one-to-one correspondence with the chiral order; the paper presents exact results up to four loops and a conjecture for the all-order behavior, with the caveat that odd-power terms in the quark mass are actually next-to-leading logarithms (NLLogs) from an RGO perspective.

## Method

The authors extend the renormalization group method of Buchler and Colangelo (2003) from bosonic chiral perturbation theory to the nucleon-pion system in the heavy baryon formulation. They compute the leading logarithms for the nucleon mass by constructing the one-loop beta functions of the low-energy constants (LECs) and applying the renormalization group operator \(\hat H\) to the tree-level contributions, using two different parametrizations of the heavy baryon Lagrangian (Bernard et al. 1992 and Ecker and Mojžiš 1995) and three different parametrizations of the pion field as cross-checks. The pole equation is solved perturbatively in the pion mass \(m\), separating even and odd powers to handle the non-analytic terms that arise from loop integrals.

## Key numbers

* \(k_3 = -\frac{3}{2}g_A^2\): coefficient of the \(\pi m^3/(4\pi F)^2\) term (one-loop, non-analytic in quark mass).
* \(k_4 = \frac{3}{4}(g_A^2 + (c_2+4c_3-4c_1)M) - 3c_1M\): coefficient of the \(m^4/((4\pi F)^2 M) \log(\mu^2/m^2)\) term (one-loop LLog).
* \(k_5 = \frac{3g_A^2}{8}(3 - 16g_A^2)\): coefficient of the \(\pi m^5/(4\pi F)^4 \log(\mu^2/m^2)\) term (two-loop LLog for odd power).
* \(k_7 = g_A^2(-18g_A^4 + \frac{35}{4}g_A^2 - \frac{443}{64})\): coefficient of the \(\pi m^7/(4\pi F)^6 \log(\mu^2/m^2)\) term (three-loop LLog for odd power).
* \(k_9 = \frac{g_A^2}{3}(-116g_A^6 + \frac{2537}{20}g_A^4 - \frac{3569}{24}g_A^2 + \frac{55609}{1280})\): coefficient of the \(\pi m^9/(4\pi F)^8 \log(\mu^2/m^2)\) term (four-loop LLog for odd power).
* The even-power coefficients \(k_6, k_8, k_{10}\) are also tabulated, involving the LECs \(c_1, c_2, c_3\) and \(g_A\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-logarithm program from bosonic chiral perturbation theory (Bijnens et al. 2009–2013) to the nucleon sector, where the presence of odd-chiral-order operators breaks the simple relation between loop order and chiral order. The results enable the evaluation of nucleon parton distributions at small \(x \sim m_\pi/M_N\) (Moiseeva et al. 2013) and provide a check on full two-loop calculations of the nucleon mass in the EOMS scheme (Schindler et al. 2007). The observed regularity in the coefficients when expressed in the physical pion mass leads to a conjecture for the all-order LLog behavior, which can be combined with known pion LLogs up to seven loops (Bijnens et al. 2012).
