# The 27-plet Baryons from Chiral Soliton Models

> We use the perturbation method to calculate the masses and widths for 27-plet baryons with spin 3/2 from chiral soliton models. According to the masses and quantum numbers, we find all the candidates for non-exotic members of 27-plet. The calculation of the widths shows that these candidates manifest an approximate symmetry of the 27 representation of the SU(3) group, and the quantum numbers of $Ξ(1950)$ seem to be $I(J^P)={1/2}({3/2}^+)$. Up to leading order of the strange quark mass, we find that the exotic members have widths much larger than those of the anti-decuplet members.

## Key claim

The authors use perturbation theory within chiral soliton models to calculate masses and widths for all 27-plet baryons with spin 3/2, finding that the non-exotic members can be identified with known resonances and that the exotic members have widths much larger than those of the anti-decuplet members, up to leading order in the strange quark mass.

## Method

The collective Hamiltonian of the SU(3) Skyrme model is diagonalized, with symmetry-breaking terms treated perturbatively via an operator proportional to the strange quark mass. The model parameters (classical soliton mass, moments of inertia, and symmetry-breaking coefficients) are fixed model-independently using two methods: (I) using the mass and width of the Theta+ (1540) as an anti-decuplet member, and (II) using both Theta+ and the candidate for Xi_{3/2} from NA49 as anti-decuplet members. Decay widths to octet baryon plus pseudoscalar meson are computed using a pseudoscalar Yukawa coupling with constants G0 and G1 fixed by experiment.

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model treatment of baryon multiplets beyond the octet, decuplet, and anti-decuplet to the 27-plet with spin 3/2, following earlier studies of the anti-decuplet by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and of the 27-plet and 35-plet by Walliser and Kopeliovich (2003). The results are consistent with the NA49 (2003) observation of a narrow Xi-pi- resonance at 1.862 GeV, and the authors identify Xi(1950) as a non-exotic 27-plet member with J^P = 3/2+. The predictions for exotic members with larger widths suggest they will be more difficult to observe experimentally than the anti-decuplet members.
