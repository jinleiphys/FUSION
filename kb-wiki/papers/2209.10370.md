## Context
This work provides the first complete analytical solution for the eigenvalue problem of a relativistic linearized Boltzmann collision operator, extending the non-relativistic results for Maxwell molecules by Wang Chang and Uhlenbeck (1940s) where eigenfunctions were known but eigenvalues were not given in simple analytical form. The results enable exact computation of transport coefficients without numerical inversion of the collision operator, and provide the foundation for deriving the correct relaxation time approximation, improving upon the Anderson-Witting approximation (Anderson and Witting, 1974) which was shown by Rocha (2021) to violate conservation laws. The method builds on the scalar (в„“=0) spectrum calculation by Mullins (2022) and can be applied to studies of hydrodynamic behavior, causal relativistic hydrodynamics, and higher-order moment methods.
