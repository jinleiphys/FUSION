# Exact results for the Boltzmann collision operator in $λϕ^4$ theory

> We analytically determine all the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the linearized Boltzmann collision operator in massless scalar $λϕ^4$ theory in the high-temperature (classical) regime. This is used to exactly compute the shear viscosity and particle diffusion transport coefficients of this system. The corresponding relaxation time approximation for this linearized Boltzmann equation is also derived.

# Exact results for the Boltzmann collision operator in λφ⁴ theory

## Key claim
The authors analytically determine all eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the linearized Boltzmann collision operator for massless scalar λφ⁴ theory in the high-temperature (classical) regime, showing the spectrum is discrete and bounded from below, but only for this specific interaction and only in the classical (non-quantum) limit, not for general quantum field theories or for massive particles.

## Method
The linearized collision operator is separated into gain and loss terms, and the eigenfunctions are shown to be products of associated Laguerre polynomials L_n^(2ℓ+1)(βE_k) and irreducible tensors k^⟨μ₁...μ_ℓ⟩ constructed from the particle 4-momentum. The gain term is evaluated using generating functions for Laguerre polynomials, Kummer's confluent hypergeometric functions, and integral representations, while the loss term is computed directly using orthogonality relations. The resulting eigenvalues are then used to compute shear viscosity and particle diffusion coefficients via Chapman-Enskog theory, and to derive the exact relaxation time approximation.

## Key numbers
- The eigenvalues are χ_nℓ = −(gM/2)[(n+ℓ−1)/(n+ℓ+1) + δ_ℓ0δ_n0], where g = λ²/(32π) and M = exp(α)/(2π²β²) is the equilibrium particle number density.
- The eigenvalue spectrum is bounded from below: χ_nℓ ≥ −gM/2.
- The shear viscosity is η = 48/(gβ³), where β = 1/T.
- The particle diffusion coefficient is κ_n = 3/(gβ²).
- The relaxation time in the Anderson-Witting approximation is τ_R = 2E_k/(gM).

## Context
This work provides the first complete analytical solution for the eigenvalue problem of a relativistic linearized Boltzmann collision operator, extending the non-relativistic results for Maxwell molecules by Wang Chang and Uhlenbeck (1940s) where eigenfunctions were known but eigenvalues were not given in simple analytical form. The results enable exact computation of transport coefficients without numerical inversion of the collision operator, and provide the foundation for deriving the correct relaxation time approximation, improving upon the Anderson-Witting approximation (Anderson and Witting, 1974) which was shown by Rocha (2021) to violate conservation laws. The method builds on the scalar (ℓ=0) spectrum calculation by Mullins (2022) and can be applied to studies of hydrodynamic behavior, causal relativistic hydrodynamics, and higher-order moment methods.
