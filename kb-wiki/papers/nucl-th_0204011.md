# Mass Dependence of M3Y-Type Interactions and the Effects of Tensor Correlations

> The mass dependence of the M3Y-type effective interactions and the effects of tensor correlations are examined. Two-body nuclear matrix elements are obtained by the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) technique with and without tensor correlations. We have found that the tensor correlations are important especially in the triplet-even (TE) and tensor-even (TNE) channels in order to reproduce the G-matrix elements obtained previously. Then M3Y-type potentials for inelastic scattering are obtained by fitting our two-body matrix elements to those of a sum of Yukawa functions for the mass numbers A=24, A=40 and A=90.

## Key claim

The M3Y-type effective interactions for inelastic scattering are extended to mass numbers A=24, A=40, and A=90 using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) technique, but the agreement with G-matrix results is model-dependent and requires tensor correlations to be switched on, particularly in the triplet-even (TE) and tensor-even (TNE) channels, while significant discrepancies persist in the odd angular momentum channels (SO, TO, LSE) regardless of tensor correlation strength.

## Method

Two-body nuclear matrix elements were calculated using the LOCV technique with Reid soft-core potential and harmonic oscillator basis, both with and without tensor correlations parametrized by a strength parameter αλ(A) that decreases monotonically with mass number A. The matrix elements were then fitted to sums of Yukawa functions with ranges 0.25, 0.4, 0.7, and 1.414 fm to produce M3Y-type potentials for inelastic scattering, separately for A=24, A=40, and A=90 systems.

## Key numbers

- For A=24: harmonic oscillator energy ħω = 12.7 MeV, tensor correlation strength α = 0.075; TE matrix element for (n'=0, n=0) is -10.37 with α on versus 3.69 with α=0.0
- For A=40: ħω = 11.0 MeV, α = 0.070; TE matrix element for (n'=0, n=0) is -8.26 with α on versus 2.85 with α=0.0, compared to G-matrix value of -8.040 (difference less than 12 percent)
- For A=90: ħω = 8.8 MeV, α = 0.060; TE matrix element for (n'=0, n=0) is -5.43 with α on versus 1.89 with α=0.0, compared to G-matrix value of -6.4335
- For A=40, SE matrix element for (n'=0, n=0) is -5.96 compared to G-matrix value -5.2187 (agreement within 25 percent)
- For A=40, SO matrix element for (n'=2, n=2) is 4.86 compared to G-matrix value 2.5781 (difference over 80 percent)
- For A=40, TNE matrix element for (n'=0, n=0) is -3.49 compared to G-matrix value -3.8897 (difference less than 10 percent)
- Tensor correlation strength α ranges from 0.09 at beginning of p shell to 0.07 for upper end of sd shell, extrapolating to 0.06 for A=90

## Context

This work extends the LOCV-based effective interaction approach previously applied to A=16 by Fiase et al. (2002) to include mass dependence across A=24, A=40, and A=90, and systematically investigates the role of tensor correlations. The results are compared with G-matrix calculations by Hosaka et al. (2002) using the Paris potential, and the approach follows the pioneering M3Y interaction framework of Bertsch and collaborators (1977). The findings enable systematic study of mass-dependent effective interactions for inelastic scattering across the nuclear chart, though the persistent discrepancies in odd angular momentum channels echo the problem noted by Bertsch et al. (1977), who used Elliott matrix elements for those channels, and by Hosaka et al. (2002), who attributed the issue to harmonic oscillator wave function vanishing near the origin for odd forces.
