# On the Connection Between the Charged and Neutral Pion-Nucleon Coupling Constants in the Yukawa Model

> In the Yukawa model for nuclear forces, a simple relation between the charged and neutral pion-nucleon coupling constants is derived. The relation implies that the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant is larger than the neutral one since the np interaction is stronger than the pp interaction. The derived value of the charged pion-nucleon constant shows a very good agreement with one of the recent experimental values. The relative splitting between the charged and neutral pion-nucleon coupling constants is predicted to be practically the same as that between the charged and neutral pion masses. The charge dependence of the NN scattering length arising from the mass difference between the charged and neutral pions is also analyzed.

# On the Connection Between the Charged and Neutral Pion-Nucleon Coupling Constants in the Yukawa Model

## Key claim

Within the Yukawa meson-field model, the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant is predicted to be larger than the neutral one because the neutron-proton interaction is stronger than the proton-proton interaction in the spin-singlet state; the relative splitting between the charged and neutral coupling constants is practically the same as that between the charged and neutral pion masses.

## Method

The authors derive relations between the charged and neutral pion-nucleon coupling constants by equating the parameters of the Yukawa potential (depth and range) for proton-proton and neutron-proton interactions, using measured low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths and effective ranges. The depth of the Yukawa potential is expressed through the pseudovector coupling constant and pion mass, while the range is inversely proportional to the pion mass. The neutron-proton potential parameters are obtained using averaged pion mass and averaged coupling constant, and the resulting equations yield explicit factors relating the charged and neutral coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Proton-proton scattering length: a_pp = -17.3(4) fm; effective range: r_pp = 2.85(4) fm
- Neutron-proton scattering length: a_np = -23.715(8) fm
- Derived Yukawa potential depth for pp: V0^pp = 44.8259 MeV; for np: V0^np = 48.0706 MeV
- Derived factor relating pseudoscalar coupling constants: C_g^2 = 1.0744
- Predicted charged pseudoscalar coupling constant: g_pi±^2 = 14.53(25)
- Predicted neutral pseudoscalar coupling constant (from experiment): g_pi0^2 = 13.52(23)
- Relative charge independence breaking: Delta g_CIB^2 / g_pi0^2 = 0.0744 (7.4%)
- Ratio of pseudovector coupling constants: f_pi± / f_pi0 = 1.0358, compared to pion mass ratio m_pi± / m_pi0 = 1.0340
- Difference between pp and np scattering lengths from pion mass difference: Delta a_CIB^pi = 5.59 fm, which is about 90% of the experimental value 6.42(41) fm

## Context

This work extends the classical Yukawa model (Yukawa, 1935) and the effective range theory of nucleon-nucleon scattering (Bethe, 1949; Blatt and Jackson, 1949) to derive a relation between charged and neutral pion-nucleon coupling constants. It contradicts the charge independence assumption that would make the coupling constants equal, and it supports the experimental results of the Uppsala Neutron Research Group (Rahm et al., 1998) which found g_pi±^2 = 14.52(26), while disagreeing with the Nijmegen group (de Swart et al., 1998) value of 13.54(5). The model enables a quantitative explanation of charge dependence in nuclear forces, predicting that 90% of the scattering length difference between pp and np systems arises from the pion mass difference, compared to only about 50% in previous analyses (Machleidt and Banerjee, 2000).
