# A Factorization of the S-Matrix into Jost Matrices

> An effective algebraic approach to $S$--matrix factorization into Jost matrices is developed in the case of coupled channels. The Jost matrix is given as a solution of boundary value Riemann -- Hilbert problem. A rational form is assumed for tangent of mixing angle, while there is no limitations for approximation of phase shifts.

## Key claim

The paper develops an algebraic method for factorizing the two-channel S-matrix into Jost matrices that avoids solving systems of singular integral equations, under the condition that the tangent of the mixing angle is approximated by a rational function of momentum squared, while no such approximation is required for the phase shifts.

## Method

The authors formulate the Jost matrix as a solution to a Riemann–Hilbert boundary value problem on the real axis. They first perform a "prefactorization" by diagonalizing the S-matrix via an orthogonal transformation involving the mixing angle, factorizing the diagonal phase-shift part using scalar Jost functions, and multiplying by a matrix containing the rational approximation for the mixing angle tangent. Nonphysical zeros introduced in the determinant of this preliminary matrix are then removed iteratively by multiplying from the right by rational "correcting matrices" built from projection operators, yielding the final Jost matrix.

## Key numbers

- The rational approximation for the mixing angle tangent is given by \(\tan\varepsilon(k^2) = P_M(k^2)/Q_N(k^2)\), where \(P_M\) and \(Q_N\) are polynomials in \(k\) of degree \(2M\) and \(2N\), respectively.
- The number of projection operators \(P_j\) needed is \(L = \max\{M, N\}\).
- In the example of elastic two-channel neutron–proton scattering (\(^3S_1\), \(^3D_1\)) in the effective radius approximation: \(\tan\varepsilon(k^2) = k^2/(2\chi^2)\), so \(M=1\), \(N=0\), and \(L=1\); the bound state point is at \(k = i\kappa\) with \(m=1\) bound state.
- The nonphysical zeros in the example are at \(k = \pm\lambda_1, \pm\lambda_1^*\) with \(\lambda = \chi(1-i)\).

## Context

This work extends the scalar (single-channel) Jost function factorization of Newton (1982) and Gakhov (1966) to the coupled-channel case with tensor interactions, where the scalar solution fails because the S-matrix does not commute at different momenta. The method generalizes earlier approaches by Fuda and Newton (1956) and Troitsky (1978), and the example reproduces the Jost matrix result of Newton and Feshbach (1957) in a more compact form. The obtained explicit Jost matrices can be used in inverse scattering problems and in solving nonlinear differential equations.
