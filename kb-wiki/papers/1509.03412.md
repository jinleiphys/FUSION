# Further study on mechanism of production of light complex particles in nucleon-induced reactions

> The Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model incorporated with the statistical decay model is used to investigate the intermediate energy nucleon-induced reactions. In our last work, the description on light complex particle emission has been great improved with a phenomenological mechanism called surface coalescence and emission introduced into ImQMD model. In this work, taking account of different specific binding energies and separation energies for various light complex particles, the phase space parameters in surface coalescence model are readjusted. By using the new phase space parameters set with better physical fundament, the double differential cross sections of emitted light complex particles are found to be in better agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that adjusting the phase space parameter \(h_0\) in the surface coalescence model to be specific to each light complex particle (LCP) type, based on its binding energy and separation energy, yields better agreement with experimental double differential cross sections than using a single energy-dependent \(h_0\) value for all LCPs, although the improvement is not uniform across all reactions and particle types.

## Method

The Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model, merged with the GEM2 statistical decay model, was used to simulate nucleon-induced reactions. A surface coalescence mechanism was applied, where a leading nucleon leaving a compound nucleus can pick up other nucleons in a surface layer (defined by radius \(R_0 = 1.4A^{1/3}\) and width \(D_0 = 2.3\) fm) to form LCPs (\(^2\)H, \(^3\)H, \(^3\)He, \(^4\)He) if the phase-space condition \(R_{im} \times P_{im} \leq h_0\) is met. The parameter \(h_0\) was assigned a distinct value for each LCP type (setII), based on their specific binding energies and separation energies, and the results were compared to those from a previous energy-dependent parameter set (setI).

## Key numbers

*   The surface layer parameters were fixed as \(R_0 = 1.4A^{1/3}\) and \(D_0 = 2.3\) fm.
*   The new phase space parameters (setII) for each LCP are: \(h_0 = 300\) fm MeV/\(c\) for \(^2\)H, \(h_0 = 260\) fm MeV/\(c\) for \(^3\)H, \(h_0 = 230\) fm MeV/\(c\) for \(^3\)He, and \(h_0 = 200\) fm MeV/\(c\) for \(^4\)He.
*   The specific binding energies used to motivate the new parameters are: 1.1 MeV for \(^2\)H, 2.9 MeV for \(^3\)H, 2.6 MeV for \(^3\)He, and 7.1 MeV for \(^4\)He.
*   The separation energies used are: 2.24 MeV for \(^2\)H, 6.24 MeV for \(^3\)H, 5.48 MeV for \(^3\)He, and 19.82 MeV (for \(p+^3\)H) and 20.58 MeV (for \(n+^3\)He) for \(^4\)He.
*   The model was tested on nucleon-induced reactions on targets including \(^{63}\)Cu, \(^{56}\)Fe, \(^{58}\)Ni, \(^{27}\)Al, \(^{197}\)Au, and \(^{209}\)Bi, with incident energies from 62 MeV to 1200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Wei et al., 2015) where a surface coalescence mechanism was first introduced into the ImQMD model, improving LCP description but with a single energy-dependent phase space parameter. The new parameter set (setII) is motivated by nuclear structure considerations of binding and separation energies, similar to the approach used in Letourneau et al. The improved model enables more accurate predictions of LCP double differential cross sections for spallation reactions, which is important for applications such as accelerator-driven systems and nuclear waste transmutation.
