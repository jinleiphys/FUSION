# The eccentricity in heavy-ion collisions from Color Glass Condensate initial conditions

> The eccentricity in coordinate-space at midrapidity of the overlap zone in high-energy heavy-ion collisions predicted by the $k_\perp$-factorization formalism is generically larger than expected from scaling with the number of participants. We provide a simple qualitative explanation of the effect which shows that it is not caused predominantly by edge effects. We also show that it is quite insensitive to ``details'' of the unintegrated gluon distribution functions such as the presence of leading-twist shadowing and of an extended geometric scaling window. The larger eccentricity increases the azimuthal asymmetry of high transverse momentum particles. Finally, we point out that the longitudinal structure of the Color Glass Condensate initial condition for hydrodynamics away from midrapidity is non-trivial but requires understanding of large-$x$ effects.

## Key claim

The eccentricity predicted by Color Glass Condensate (CGC) models within the k_perp-factorization formalism is generically larger than that from Glauber-type models, and this property is not predominantly caused by edge effects but rather by the scaling of the produced gluon density with the smaller of the two local saturation scales.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the k_perp-factorization formula for gluon production using several models for the unintegrated gluon distribution function: the original KLN parameterization, an extension including a smooth anomalous dimension and leading-twist shadowing, and a unitarized dipole profile. They computed the spatial eccentricity at midrapidity for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV as a function of centrality, and compared results to Glauber-type initial conditions where energy density scales with the number of participants or binary collisions.

## Key numbers

- The average saturation momentum at midrapidity for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV is about 1.5 GeV, using the parameterization Q_s^2(x) ~ x^{-lambda} with lambda approximately 0.2-0.3.
- The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV is sigma_NN = 42 mb.
- The anomalous dimension at leading-order BFKL saturation is gamma_s = 0.627.
- The formation time of the medium used in the opacity calculation is l_0 = 0.2 fm.
- The color factor for quark versus gluon coupling to the medium is C_F/C_A = 4/9.

## Context

This work extends the observation of Hirano (2005) that CGC initial conditions produce larger eccentricity than the wounded nucleon model, and shows that this result is robust against variations in the unintegrated gluon distribution. The larger eccentricity increases the azimuthal asymmetry of high transverse momentum particles and has implications for hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC. The authors point out that understanding large-x effects within the CGC formalism is needed for quantitative calculations of initial conditions away from midrapidity.
