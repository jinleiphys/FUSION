# Recent progress on the accurate determination of the equation of state of neutron and nuclear matter

> The problem of accurately determining the equation of state of nuclear and neutron matter at density near and beyond saturation is still an open challenge. In this paper we will review the most recent progress made by means of Quantum Monte Carlo calculations, which are at present the only ab-inito method capable to treat a sufficiently large number of particles to give meaningful estimates depending only on the choice of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. In particular, we will discuss the introduction of density-dependent interactions, the study of the temperature dependence of the equation of state, and the possibility of accurately studying the effect of the onset of hyperons by developing an accurate hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interaction.

# Recent progress on the accurate determination of the equation of state of neutron and nuclear matter

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations with density-dependent interactions can produce equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter that reproduce saturation properties, but the inclusion of hyperons remains uncertain because three-body hyperon-nucleon-nucleon forces, which appear repulsive in preliminary calculations, may significantly alter the predicted maximum neutron star mass.

## Method

The authors used Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) to compute the zero-temperature equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, employing a density-dependent two-body potential fitted to reproduce saturation energy, saturation density, and compressibility. Finite-temperature corrections were estimated using Fermi Hyper-Netted Chain (FHNC) variational calculations with a correlated basis formalism, and these thermal corrections were added to the zero-temperature AFDMC results. For hypernuclei, AFDMC simulations were performed on systems containing one Lambda hyperon plus A nucleons, using a Hamiltonian that includes both two-body hyperon-nucleon and three-body hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent interaction approach of Gandolfi (2010) and Lovato (2011) to provide a unified treatment of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter equations of state, with finite-temperature corrections computed via FHNC following the variational method of Schmidt (1979). The hypernucleus calculations build on the Lambda-nucleon interaction model of Usmani (1995) and address the tension between observed neutron star masses above 2 solar masses (Demorest, 2010) and existing hyperonic equations of state that predict lower maximum masses (Vidana, 2011; Schulze, 2011). The results enable more realistic neutron star structure calculations and provide a framework for constraining hyperon-nucleon interactions using hypernuclear separation energy data.
