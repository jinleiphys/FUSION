# Gravitational form factors of light mesons from Basis Light-Front Quantization

> We compute the gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the pion and kaon using their light-front wave functions within the Basis Light-Front Quantization framework. The wave functions are obtained by solving a light-front effective Hamiltonian that incorporates three-dimensional confinement along with a color-singlet Nambu--Jona-Lasinio interaction between the constituent quark and antiquark. The form factor $A(Q^2)$ is found to be in overall agreement with recent lattice QCD and dispersive results. In contrast, $D(Q^2)$ is enhanced in magnitude at low $Q^2$ relative to both lattice QCD and dispersive determinations. This behavior arises from extracting the $D$-term using transverse components of the QCD energy--momentum tensor, which are more sensitive to the small-$x$ region and to light-front zero-mode effects in the present truncated framework. Using the resulting GFFs, we determine the mass (matter) and mechanical radii of the pion and kaon and analyze their mechanical structure through the corresponding pressure and shear-force distributions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the gravitational form factor $D(Q^2)$ for both pion and kaon is enhanced in magnitude at low $Q^2$ relative to lattice QCD and dispersive determinations, but this enhancement is a model-dependent feature arising from extracting the $D$-term using transverse components of the QCD energy-momentum tensor, which are more sensitive to the small-$x$ region and to light-front zero-mode effects in the present truncated valence-sector framework.

## Method

The authors compute gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the pion and kaon using light-front wave functions obtained by solving a light-front effective Hamiltonian within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework, restricted to the valence Fock sector. The effective Hamiltonian incorporates three-dimensional confinement and a color-singlet Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) interaction, with wave functions expanded in a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis in the transverse direction and a Jacobi-polynomial basis in the longitudinal direction. The GFFs $A(Q^2)$ and $D(Q^2)$ are extracted from matrix elements of the fermionic part of the QCD energy-momentum tensor using the overlap representation of light-front wave functions, with $A(Q^2)$ obtained from the $T^{++}$ component and $D(Q^2)$ from the $T^{12}$ component.

## Key numbers

*   Pion $D(0)$ extrapolated value: $-4.247$ for basis truncation $\{N_{\text{max}},L_{\text{max}}\}=\{8,32\}$, with dipole mass parameter $m_D = 0.620$ GeV.
*   Kaon $D(0)$ extrapolated value: $-3.941$ for basis truncation $\{N_{\text{max}},L_{\text{max}}\}=\{8,32\}$, with dipole mass parameter $m_D = 0.717$ GeV.
*   Pion dipole mass parameter $m_A$ for $A(Q^2)$: $1.589$ GeV for $\{N_{\text{max}},L_{\text{max}}\}=\{8,32\}$.
*   Kaon dipole mass parameter $m_A$ for $A(Q^2)$: $1.814$ GeV for $\{N_{\text{max}},L_{\text{max}}\}=\{8,32\}$.
*   Model parameters include confinement strength $\kappa = 227$ MeV for the pion and $\kappa = 276$ MeV for the kaon, with quark masses $m_q = 337$ MeV for the pion and $m_u = 308$ MeV, $m_{\bar s} = 445$ MeV for the kaon.

## Context

This work extends prior BLFQ-NJL applications to light mesons that successfully computed distribution amplitudes, electromagnetic form factors, parton distribution functions, and generalized parton distributions (Jia 2018, Lan 2019, Mondal 2021, Adhikari 2021). The results for $A(Q^2)$ show overall agreement with lattice QCD results from Hackett (2023) and dispersive analyses from Cao (2025), while the enhanced $D(Q^2)$ at low $Q^2$ contrasts with these same lattice and dispersive determinations as well as chiral perturbation theory values from Donoghue (1991). The work enables future studies of mechanical properties of light mesons, including pressure and shear-force distributions, and highlights the need for extending the model space beyond the leading Fock sector to reconcile with other nonperturbative approaches.
