# Baryons in/and Lattice QCD

> Anchoring our understanding of low-energy nuclear and hadronic physics to the fundamental theory of strong interactions, QCD, remains and outstanding challenge for physicists. Lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory are the most powerful theoretical tools we have at our disposal to make this connection. These tools share a symbiotic relationship as chiral perturbation theory is used to understand the light quark mass dependence of observables, while lattice QCD is used to determine the values of the unknown operator coefficients appearing in the chiral Lagrangian. In this talk, I review our present understanding of select single baryon properties from lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory, highlighting some of the challenges and discussing some unresolved puzzles.

# Baryons in/and Lattice QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of single baryon properties have not yet achieved the precision of meson calculations, and apparent conflicts with experiment or chiral perturbation theory are not justified because lattice calculations currently lack sufficient study of systematic effects including finite volume, excited state contamination, and continuum limit.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations of nucleon matrix elements (axial charge g_A, electromagnetic form factors) and the light quark mass dependence of the nucleon mass, comparing results from multiple lattice collaborations with chiral perturbation theory (χPT) predictions. The talk also examines the neutron-proton mass splitting using a dispersion-relation approach (Cottingham formula) to separate electromagnetic and strong isospin-breaking contributions, and discusses combined large-N_c and SU(3) baryon χPT analyses of the octet and decuplet spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon axial charge g_A: lattice calculations with m_π ~ 300 MeV from LHPC (2005) agreed with experiment, but more recent calculations with lighter pion masses are all further from the experimental value
- Nucleon mass from SU(2) baryon χPT NNLO fit: 941 ± 42 ± 17 MeV; from "ruler approximation" (linear in m_π): 938 ± 9 MeV
- Nucleon mass linear fit: m_N [MeV] = 800 + m_π, persisting over range 170 ≲ m_π ≲ 760 MeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term from ruler approximation: σ_πN = 67 ± 5 MeV
- Finite volume correction to nucleon mass at m_π L ≃ 3.9: 2.5% correction observed (Beane 2011)
- Neutron-proton mass splitting experimental value: m_n - m_p = 1.29333217(42) MeV
- Electromagnetic contribution to n-p mass splitting: δM_{n-p}^γ = -1.30 ± 0.03 ± 0.47 MeV (first uncertainty from elastic/inelastic, second from unknown subtraction function)
- SU(3) baryon χPT fit to M_3 and M_4 returned axial couplings: D = 0.75(5), F = 0.47(3), C = -1.4(1), H = -2.1(2)

## Context

This talk reviews the status of single baryon properties from lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory as presented at the 7th International Workshop on Chiral Dynamics (2012). It extends the work of the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal Collaboration (Durr 2008) who first computed the ground state hadron spectrum with controlled systematics, and addresses the "ruler approximation" puzzle identified by Walker-Loud (2008) where the nucleon mass shows unexpectedly linear dependence on pion mass. The analysis of the neutron-proton mass splitting revisits the Cottingham formula approach of Gasser and Leutwyler (1974), correcting a technical oversight in their subtracted dispersion integral. The talk enables future lattice QCD calculations to focus on controlling excited state contamination and finite volume effects, particularly for the nucleon axial charge and the isovector magnetic polarizability needed to improve the neutron-proton mass splitting prediction.
