# Long- and medium-range components of the nuclear force in quark-model based calculations

> Quark-model descriptions of the nucleon-nucleon interaction contain two main ingredients, a quark-exchange mechanism for the short-range repulsion and meson-exchanges for the medium- and long-range parts of the interaction. We point out the special role played by higher partial waves, and in particular the 1F3, as a very sensitive probe for the meson-exchange part employed in these interaction models. In particular, we show that the presently available models fail to provide a reasonable description of higher partial waves and indicate the reasons for this shortcoming.

## Key claim
The paper argues that quark-model descriptions of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, while successful for low partial waves (S, P, D), fail to describe higher partial waves (F, G) because their medium-range attractive (sigma-like) components are too strong and too long-ranged, and because they omit vector-meson exchanges (omega, rho) that are still relevant at internucleonic distances around 1 fm.

## Method
The authors solved the Schrodinger equation for the NN system using only the "direct" effective NN interaction kernel (V^D_NN) from three quark models (Tokyo group TOK, Kyoto-Niigata group KYO-NII, and Tuebingen-Salamanca group TUEB-SAL), omitting the quark-exchange short-range contributions. They compared the resulting phase shifts for the ^1F_3 and ^1G_4 partial waves with those from the full quark models and with a conventional one-boson-exchange potential (Bonn OBEPR). They also applied a step-function cutoff to the NN potential to quantify the sensitivity of higher partial waves to different radial ranges.

## Key numbers
- For the ^1F_3 partial wave at E_lab = 300 MeV, applying a cutoff radius r_c = 0.7 fm to the TOK potential produced deviations of the order of 10% in the phase shift, while for r_c smaller than about 1 fm the results were generally insensitive to the cutoff.
- For G waves (specifically ^1G_4), the phase shifts were insensitive to the NN interaction for internucleon distances up to r_c ≈ 1.5 fm.
- The sigma-like medium-range potential in the TOK model was exceptionally large compared to the sigma exchange in the Bonn OBEPR model for internucleonic distances r ≥ 1 fm.
- The KYO-NII model, which came closest to the sigma exchange in the OBE model, gave the best results for ^1F_3 among the quark models.

## Context
This work extends earlier quark-model studies of the NN interaction by the Tokyo group (TOK, 1996), Kyoto-Niigata group (KYO-NII, 1999), and Tuebingen-Salamanca group (TUEB-SAL, 1990s), which focused on low partial waves. It is complementary to a recent investigation by the Paris group (1999) that combined the short-range part of the Tokyo quark model with the Paris potential and found poor data description (chi^2/data ranging from 20 to 160). The paper suggests that future work should incorporate reliable medium- and long-range NN interactions from dispersion theory (Paris potential), extended meson-exchange models (Bonn potential), or chiral perturbation theory, and supplement them with the short-range piece from quark models.
