# Gluon saturation in $pA$ collisions at the LHC: KLN model predictions for hadron multiplicities

> The upcoming p+Pb run at the LHC will probe the nuclear gluon distribution at very small Bjorken x (from $x \sim 10^{-4}$ at mid-rapidity down to $x \sim 10^{-6}$ in the proton fragmentation region) and will allow to test approaches based on parton saturation. Here, we present the predictions of the KLN model for hadron multiplicities and multiplicity distributions in p+Pb collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 4.4 TeV. We also compare the model to the existing pp, dA and AA data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim
The KLN model, updated with a reduced energy-dependence intercept for the saturation momentum (by about 20%) and a Monte-Carlo Glauber treatment of participant number fluctuations, predicts hadron multiplicities in p+Pb collisions at 4.4 TeV that can test parton saturation, but the model involves several adjustable parameters and a 10% overall normalization uncertainty remains.

## Method
The authors compute charged hadron multiplicities per unit rapidity using a factorized formula involving unintegrated gluon distributions that encode saturation via a simple ansatz: below the saturation momentum Q_s the distribution scales as p_t^2, above it as Q_s^2. The saturation momentum for each nucleus depends on the number of participants and rapidity via Q_s^2(y) = Q_0^2 N_part (x_0 W/Q_0 e^{∓y})^{λ̄} with λ̄ = 0.205. A Monte-Carlo Glauber simulation generates the distribution of participant nucleons in the target nucleus, and intrinsic multiplicity fluctuations are modeled with a negative binomial distribution whose parameter k is estimated from the saturation scale.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy for p+Pb predictions: 4.4 TeV
- Saturation momentum intercept parameter: λ̄ = 0.205 (reduced by about 20% from the earlier value in Kharzeev et al. (2004))
- Saturation momentum for a proton at midrapidity at RHIC energy: Q_s ≈ 0.68 GeV
- Reference parameters: Q_0 = 0.6 GeV, x_0 = 0.01
- Inelastic pp cross sections used: 42 mb at W=200 GeV, 52 mb at 900 GeV, 60 mb at 2360 GeV, 65.75 mb at 4400 GeV, 70.45 mb at 7000 GeV
- The KLN model overestimates LHC Pb-Pb data by about 10-15% when using the earlier energy dependence from Kharzeev et al. (2004)
- The factor for converting d+Au to equivalent p+Au collisions at W=200 GeV: 1.52
- Effective area for pp collisions: S_pp ≈ 0.7 S_pA
- Parameter κ for negative binomial fluctuations: κ ≈ 0.05

## Context
This work extends the KLN model previously applied to RHIC data (Kharzeev et al., 2001; Kharzeev et al., 2004) and to earlier LHC predictions, updating it with a reduced saturation momentum energy dependence and Monte-Carlo participant sampling. The model successfully describes RHIC d+Au and Au+Au data as well as LHC pp data at 0.9, 2.36, and 7 TeV, and LHC Pb-Pb data at 2.76 TeV. The predictions for p+Pb at 4.4 TeV will allow testing saturation ideas; deviations from the predictions could signal viscous entropy production in the quark-gluon fluid, which the present zero-viscosity treatment neglects.
