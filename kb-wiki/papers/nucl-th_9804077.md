# Nuclear Interference effects in 8B sub-Coulomb breakup

> The breakup of $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni below the Coulomb barrier was measured recently with the aim of determining the Coulomb breakup components. We reexamine this reaction, and perform one step quantum-mechanical calculations that include E1, E2 and nuclear contributions. We show that the nuclear contribution is by no means negligible at the intermediate angular range where data was taken. Our results indicate that, for an accurate description of this reaction, Coulomb E1, E2 and nuclear processes all have to be taken into account.

# Nuclear Interference effects in 8B sub-Coulomb breakup

## Key claim
Nuclear contributions to the breakup of 8B on 58Ni below the Coulomb barrier are by no means negligible in the intermediate angular range where data were taken (39°–51°), contrary to the assumption that pure Coulomb E1 and E2 processes could be cleanly measured at those angles. The authors show that Coulomb-nuclear interference produces a pronounced dip between 25° and 50°, which coincides with the measured angular range and may explain the discrepancy between earlier semiclassical predictions and the Notre Dame data.

## Method
The authors performed first-order distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations for single-proton excitations from the 8B ground state to a discretised continuum of s, p, d, and f partial waves up to 3 MeV relative energy, using the continuum binning method (CDCC-style). They included E1, E2, and nuclear multipole transitions coherently, folding realistic projectile-target interactions with the extended 8B ground-state wavefunction (binding energy 0.137 MeV), and used the code Fresco with partial waves up to lmax = 600 ħ and Rmax = 300 fm for convergence.

## Key numbers
- The 8B ground-state binding energy is only 0.137 MeV, giving a long tail in the wavefunction that invalidates the point-projectile approximation for angles larger than 20°.
- The Notre Dame experiment measured integrated breakup cross sections of 8B on 58Ni at 26 MeV beam energy for 7Be fragment angles between 39° and 51°, corresponding to a distance of closest approach of 15 fm.
- The pure nuclear differential cross section peaks at approximately 70°, while the Coulomb-nuclear interference minimum lies between 25° and 50°.
- For unambiguous extraction of E1 and E2 Coulomb amplitudes, measurements at 3 MeV/A should be performed at angles of 15° or more forward, corresponding to a distance of closest approach of 40 fm or more.

## Context
This work reexamines the Notre Dame experiment (1997) on Coulomb dissociation of 8B on 58Ni at 26 MeV, which had disagreed with semiclassical predictions from Alder and Winther theory by factors of two to three (Shyam, Puri). The authors extend prior semiclassical treatments (Vitturi) by performing full quantum-mechanical DWBA calculations that include finite-size effects of the 8B halo. The results indicate that nuclear effects and Coulomb-nuclear interference must be included to interpret low-energy breakup data, and that multistep processes (higher-order Coulomb-Coulomb couplings) still need to be fully analysed before the E2 component can be reliably extracted for astrophysical S17 determinations.
