# Polarization phenomena in the reaction NN to NNpi near threshold

> First calculations for spin-dependent observables of the reactions $pp \to ppπ^0$, $pp \to pnπ^+$ and $pp \to dπ^+$ near threshold are presented, employing the Jülich model for pion production. The influence of resonant (via the excitation of the $Δ(1232)$) and non-resonant p-wave pion production mechanisms on these observables is examined. For the reactions $pp \to pnπ^+$ and $pp \to dπ^+$ nice agreement of our predictions with the presently available data on spin correlation coefficents is observed whereas for $pp \to ppπ^0$ the description of the data is less satisfying.

# Polarization phenomena in the reaction NN to NNpi near threshold

## Key claim

The Jülich model for pion production provides the first calculations of spin-dependent observables for the reactions pp → ppπ⁰, pp → pnπ⁺, and pp → dπ⁺ near threshold, but while the model shows good agreement with data for the charged-pion channels, the description of the pp → ppπ⁰ channel is less satisfying, with a serious disagreement for the spin correlation coefficient combination A_xx − A_yy that may reflect either a known deficiency in the total cross section or a separate problem with p-wave contributions.

## Method

The authors employed the Jülich model for pion production, which includes direct production, pion rescattering, pair diagrams, and explicit Δ(1232) resonance excitation, treating the reaction in a distorted wave Born approximation in momentum space. All NN partial waves up to orbital angular momentum L_NN = 2 and all states with relative orbital angular momentum l ≤ 2 between the NN system and the pion were included, along with all πN partial waves up to L_πN = 1 for rescattering diagrams. The NN distortions used the coupled-channel CCF potential derived from the full Bonn model, and the πN → πN T-matrix came from a microscopic meson-exchange model, with nucleon and Δ pole terms removed to avoid double counting with the direct production diagrams.

## Key numbers

- For pp → ppπ⁰, the integrated spin correlation coefficient A_Δ is overestimated by approximately a factor of 2 compared to the data from Mey (1998).
- For pp → ppπ⁰ at energies corresponding to η ≈ 1, the model underestimates the total π⁰ production cross section by a factor of about 2.
- For pp → pnπ⁺ and pp → dπ⁺, non-resonant p-wave rescattering yields a change in sign of A_Δ for energies η ≤ 0.6.
- Results are presented at T_lab = 400 MeV as a function of pion angle and angle between nucleons for all three reactions.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich group's previous calculations (Hanhart 1998a, 1998b) for total cross sections and analyzing powers to spin-dependent observables, and is one of only two model calculations (alongside the Osaka group's work by Mae 1997 and Tam 1998) that consider all relevant pion production channels with higher partial waves. The predictions enable comparison with newly available data from polarized beam and polarized target experiments (Mey 1998, Mey 1999, Sah 1999, Prz 1999, Jan 2000), and the results suggest that spin correlation coefficients are well suited for testing the treatment of Δ resonance contributions at energies far below the resonance regime.
