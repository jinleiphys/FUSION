# Ab initio computation of charge densities for Sn and Xe isotopes

> We present the first ab initio calculations for open-shell nuclei past the tin isotopic line, focusing on Xe isotopes as well as doubly-magic Sn isotopes. We show that, even for moderately hard interactions, it is possible to obtain meaningful predictions and that the NNLOsat chiral interaction predicts radii and charge density distributions close to the experiment. We then make a new prediction for ${}^{100}$Sn. This paves the way for ab initio studies of exotic charge density distributions at the limit of the present ab initio mass domain, where experimental data is becoming available. The present study closes the gap between the largest isotopes reachable by ab initio methods and the smallest exotic nuclei accessible to electron scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first *ab initio* calculations of charge densities and radii for open-shell nuclei beyond the tin isotopic line, specifically for Xe isotopes up to mass 138, using self-consistent Green's function theory with the NNLOsat chiral interaction. The authors hedge that while the NNLOsat interaction predicts radii and charge density distributions close to experiment, the ground-state energy could not be converged due to the restricted single-particle basis and cut on three-body matrix elements, so energy results are not discussed.

## Method

The authors used self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory, solving the Dyson equation via the algebraic diagrammatic construction approach at the ADC(2) truncation level for open-shell nuclei (Gorkov SCGF) and at ADC(3) for doubly-magic nuclei. Calculations were performed in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with frequencies from 10 to 16 MeV, using up to 14 major shells (Nmax = 13) for one- and two-body operators, while three-body excitations were limited to E3max = 16. The NNLOsat chiral interaction was used bare, incorporating two-body forces and three-nucleon forces at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

* Charge radius of 132Xe: 4.824 ± 0.124 fm from SCGF, compared to experimental value 4.79 +0.11/-0.08 fm from SCRIT
* Charge radius of 132Sn: 4.725 – 4.956 fm from SCGF, compared to experimental value 4.7093 fm
* Charge radius of 136Xe: 4.715 – 4.928 fm from SCGF, compared to experimental value 4.7964 fm
* Charge radius of 138Xe: 4.724 – 4.941 fm from SCGF, compared to experimental value 4.8279 fm
* Charge radius of 100Sn: 4.525 – 4.707 fm (first *ab initio* prediction, no experimental value available)
* Neutron skin of 132Sn with NNLOsat: 0.168 – 0.197 fm; with NN+3N(lnl) interaction: 0.180 – 0.275 fm
* Neutron skin of 132Xe with NNLOsat: 0.103 – 0.128 fm; with NN+3N(lnl): 0.120 – 0.152 fm

## Context

This work extends *ab initio* methods from the light and mid-mass region (up to A ~ 100, as in Binder (2013) and Morris (2018)) to heavier nuclei up to A = 138, closing the gap between the largest isotopes reachable by *ab initio* methods and the smallest exotic nuclei accessible to electron scattering experiments at facilities like SCRIT at RIKEN. The results enable future comparisons with experimental charge density distributions from SCRIT for neutron-rich Xe isotopes, and motivate measurements at higher momentum transfer to probe nuclear interior structure. The study also provides the first *ab initio* prediction for the charge radius of 100Sn, which sits near the proton dripline and is expected to be the heaviest doubly-magic N=Z nucleus.
