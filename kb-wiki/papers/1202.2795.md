# Relation between Wigner energy and proton-neutron pairing

> The linear term proportional to $|N-Z|$ in the nuclear symmetry energy (Wigner energy)is obtained in a model that uses isovector pairing on single particle levels from a deformed potential combined with a $\vec T^2$ interaction. The pairing correlations are calculated by numerical diagonalization of the pairing Hamiltonian acting on the six or seven levels nearest the $N=Z$ Fermi surface. The experimental binding energies of nuclei with $N\approx Z$ are well reproduced. The Wigner energy emerges as a consequence of restoring isospin symmetry. We have found the Wigner energy to be insensitive to the presence of moderate isoscalar pair correlations.

# Relation Between Wigner Energy and Proton-Neutron Pairing

## Key claim

The Wigner energy (the linear term proportional to |N-Z| in the nuclear symmetry energy) emerges as a consequence of restoring isospin symmetry through isovector proton-neutron pair correlations, and is insensitive to the presence of moderate isoscalar pair correlations. The model reproduces experimental binding energies for nuclei with N approximately equal to Z without introducing new parameters beyond the isovector pairing strength and a T^2 interaction term.

## Method

The authors numerically diagonalize an isorotational invariant isovector pairing Hamiltonian acting on the six or seven single-particle levels nearest the N=Z Fermi surface, using single-particle energies derived from the Nilsson potential at equilibrium deformation. The Hamiltonian includes a C T·T term to account for the isospin dependence of the single-particle levels, and the two parameters (isovector pairing strength G_V and symmetry interaction parameter C) are determined by fitting the even-even odd-odd mass differences and the energy difference between T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei. The experimental Coulomb energy is subtracted from binding energies using a fit to mirror nuclei data from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation.

## Key numbers

- The isovector pairing strength is parameterized as G_V = 13.9 / A^(3/4) MeV, determined by fitting the even-even odd-odd mass differences which follow approximately 2Δ = 24 / A^(1/2) MeV.
- The symmetry interaction parameter is C = 58.9 / A MeV, determined by fitting the energy difference E(T=1) - E(T=0) in odd-odd N=Z nuclei.
- The Coulomb energy fit to mirror nuclei gives ΔE_C/ΔZ = 0.706 (±0.007) A^(2/3) - 0.876 (±0.068) MeV, with a root mean squared deviation of 104 keV.
- The experimental Wigner X values derived from the 2012 AME have an average value of X = 1.64 for 24 ≤ A ≤ 100.
- The Coulomb energy fit yields r_0 = 1.224 fm and d = 0.281 fm for the equivalent radius and diffuseness parameters.

## Context

This work extends the isorotational band interpretation of Frauendorf and Sheikh (1999, 2000) and the mean-field plus random-phase approximation approach of Neergaard (2002, 2003, 2009), who showed that the Wigner energy appears without new parameters when isorotational invariance is respected. The authors improve upon earlier treatments by numerically diagonalizing the pairing Hamiltonian, avoiding limitations near the critical coupling strength for the isovector pair field. The results contradict the need for an ad-hoc phenomenological Wigner term in mean-field approaches such as the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations of the HFB-21 mass model. The findings enable systematic inclusion of Wigner energy in nuclear mass calculations and show consistency with large-scale shell model calculations by Poves (1998) and Langanke (1997) that find moderate isoscalar pair correlations coexisting with strong isovector correlations.
