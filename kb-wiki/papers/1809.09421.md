# Multipole sensitivity to phase variation in pion photo-and electroproduction analyses

> We use the Athens Model Independent Analysis Scheme (AMIAS) to examine the validity of using the Fermi-Watson theorem in the multipole analyses of pion photoproduction and electroproduction data. A standard practice in this field is to fix the multipoles' phases from $πN$ scattering data, making use of the Fermi - Watson theorem. However, these phases are known with limited accuracy and the effect of this uncertainty on the obtained multipole extraction has not been fully explored yet. Using AMIAS we constrain the phases within their experimentally determined uncertainty. We first analyze sets of pseudodata of increasing statistical precision and subsequently we apply the methodology for a re-analysis of the Bates/Mainz electroproduction data. It is found that the uncertainty induced by the $πN$ phases uncertainty to the extracted solutions would be significant only in the analysis of data with much higher precision than the current available experimental data.

## Key claim

The uncertainty in pion-nucleon scattering phases, when propagated through multipole analyses of pion photoproduction and electroproduction data using the Athens Model Independent Analysis Scheme (AMIAS), would only significantly affect the extracted multipole solutions if the experimental data had much higher precision than currently available data.

## Method

The authors used the AMIAS framework, which is based on Monte Carlo techniques and Bayesian statistics, to incorporate the experimentally determined uncertainties of pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts into multipole analyses. They first created pseudodata for pion photoproduction observables at three levels of statistical precision (2.0, 1.0, and 0.16 times the uncertainty of the most precise current data) using the MAID07 model as generator, then performed multipole extractions with phases fixed to the generator values, fixed to the SAID-WI08 solution, and allowed to vary with Gaussian weighting according to the WI08 uncertainties. The methodology was subsequently applied to a re-analysis of the Bates/Mainz electroproduction data at Q^2 = 0.127 GeV^2/c^2 and W = 1232 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pseudodata were created at three precision levels: relative uncertainties of 2.0, 1.0, and 0.16 times the statistical uncertainty of the most precise pion photoproduction data from Adlarson (2015).
- Each pseudodata set contained 288 datapoints: 18 angular measurements evenly spaced over theta_cm in [5 degrees, 175 degrees] for each of eight polarization observables for each of two proton target reactions.
- The Bates/Mainz electroproduction data were measured at Q^2 = 0.127 GeV^2/c^2 and W = 1232 MeV.
- For the M_{1+}^{1/2} multipole from Set C (highest precision), the extracted value changed from 1.829 +/- 0.032 (phases fixed to MAID07) to 1.896 +/- 0.031 (phases fixed to WI08) to 2.043 +/- 0.071 (phases varied), all in units of 10^{-3}/m_pi.
- The uncertainty in multipole amplitudes derived from Set A was reduced by a factor of 2.0 when derived from Set B and by a factor of 12.5 when derived from Set C.

## Context

This work extends the standard practice in pion photoproduction and electroproduction multipole analyses, as used by Beck (2000), Sparveris (2005), and others, where multipole phases are fixed using the Fermi-Watson theorem from pion-nucleon scattering data as if known with infinite precision. The study uses the AMIAS method previously developed by Stiliaris (2007) and Papanicolas (2012) to quantify the effect of phase uncertainties for the first time. The results enable future analyses to determine whether improved experimental precision would require explicitly incorporating phase uncertainties, and validate the current standard practice for existing data.
