# Interplay between soft and hard hadronic components for identified hadrons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the transverse dynamics in Au+Au collisions at \sqrt{s_NN}=200 GeV by emphasis upon the interplay between soft and hard components through p_T dependences of particle spectra, ratios of yields, suppression factors, and elliptic flow for identified hadrons. From hydrodynamics combined with traversing minijets which go through jet quenching in the hot medium, we calculate interactions of hard jets with the soft hydrodynamic components. It is shown by the explicit dynamical calculations that the hydrodynamic radial flow and the jet quenching of hard jets are the keys to understand the differences among the hadron spectra for pions, kaons, and protons. This leads to the natural interpretation for N_p/N_π~ 1, R_{AA} >~ 1 for protons, and v_2^p > v_2^πrecently observed in the intermediate transverse momentum region at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed excess of protons over pions at intermediate transverse momentum (p_T ~ 2-3 GeV/c) and the larger elliptic flow for protons than pions arise naturally from the interplay between a soft hydrodynamic component (with radial flow) and a hard pQCD minijet component (with jet quenching), without requiring new hadronization mechanisms such as coalescence or baryon junction.

## Method

The authors combine a fully three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic model for the soft (thermalized) sector with pQCD minijets generated via PYTHIA 6.2 for the hard sector, using an independent fragmentation scheme. Minijets with p_T,jet > 2 GeV/c are propagated through the hydrodynamic fluid on straight-line trajectories, losing energy according to the first-order GLV formula in opacity expansion, with the energy loss parameter C = 0.45 fixed by fitting the neutral pion suppression factor R_AA from PHENIX. The hydrodynamic model uses a bag model equation of state (B^{1/4} = 247 MeV) with phase transition temperature T_c = 170 MeV, partial chemical equilibrium with chemical freeze-out T^{ch} = 170 MeV, and kinetic freeze-out T^{th} = 100 MeV, with initial conditions at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c and e_max = 40 GeV/fm^3 tuned to reproduce BRAHMS pseudorapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density e_max = 40 GeV/fm^3 at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c, giving average initial energy density ~5 GeV/fm^3 in the transverse plane at eta_s = 0 and tau = 1 fm/c
- Crossing point p_T,cross (where soft and hard yields are equal) in central collisions: ~1.8 GeV/c for pions, ~2.5 GeV/c for kaons, ~3.5 GeV/c for protons
- Minijet spectra recover dominance at p_T ~ 3.4 GeV/c for pions, ~4.0 GeV/c for kaons, ~5.0 GeV/c for protons
- Chemical potentials at T^{th} = 100 MeV: mu_pi = 83 MeV, mu_K = 181 MeV, mu_p = mu_anti-p = 349 MeV
- Jet quenching parameter C = 0.45, screening scale mu = 0.5 GeV, effective path length L = 3 fm
- K-factor K = 2.5 and scale Q = p_T,jet/2 used in PYTHIA for pp baseline
- Primordial transverse momentum <k_T^2>_{NN} = 1 GeV^2/c^2

## Context

This work extends the hydro+jet approach previously developed by the authors (Hirano and Nara, 2003) to identified hadrons, building on earlier two-component models by Wang (2001) and Gyulassy et al. (2001, 2000) that combined pQCD with hydrodynamic features. The results contradict pure hydrodynamic descriptions (which overpredict elliptic flow at high p_T) and pure pQCD approaches (which cannot explain the baryon excess), while producing similar behavior to baryon junction models (Vitev, 2002; Topor Pop, 2002) and quark coalescence models (Hwa, 2002; Fries, 2003; Greco, 2003) without invoking those mechanisms. The model enables systematic study of the transition from soft to hard physics as a function of hadron mass, predicting inflection points in the p_T spectra at ~3 GeV/c for kaons and ~4 GeV/c for protons as indicators of this transition.
