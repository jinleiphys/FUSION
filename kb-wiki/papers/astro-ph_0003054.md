# Mu and Tau Neutrino Thermalization and Production in Supernovae: Processes and Timescales

> We investigate the rates of production and thermalization of $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ neutrinos at temperatures and densities relevant to core-collapse supernovae and protoneutron stars. Included are contributions from electron scattering, electron-positron annihilation, nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, and nucleon scattering. For the scattering processes, in order to incorporate the full scattering kinematics at arbitrary degeneracy, the structure function formalism developed by Reddy et al. (1998) and Burrows and Sawyer (1998) is employed. Furthermore, we derive formulae for the total and differential rates of nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung for arbitrary nucleon degeneracy in asymmetric matter. We find that electron scattering dominates nucleon scattering as a thermalization process at low neutrino energies ($ε_ν\lesssim 10$ MeV), but that nucleon scattering is always faster than or comparable to electron scattering above $ε_ν\simeq10$ MeV. In addition, for $ρ\gtrsim 10^{13}$ g cm$^{-3}$, $T\lesssim14$ MeV, and neutrino energies $\lesssim60$ MeV, nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung always dominates electron-positron annihilation as a production mechanism for $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ neutrinos.

## Key claim

The authors find that nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung dominates electron-positron annihilation as a production mechanism for muon and tau neutrinos under specific conditions (density above 10^13 g cm^-3, temperature below 14 MeV, and neutrino energies below 60 MeV), while nucleon scattering competes with or surpasses electron scattering as a thermalization process above neutrino energies of approximately 10 MeV, contradicting earlier assumptions that nucleon scattering was unimportant for neutrino equilibration.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation for neutrino distribution functions in a homogeneous, isotropic thermal bath of scatterers and absorbers, evolving the neutrino distribution in energy and time alone. They employed the structure function formalism of Reddy et al. (1998) and Burrows and Sawyer (1998) for scattering processes to incorporate full kinematics at arbitrary degeneracy, and derived new formulae for nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung rates at arbitrary nucleon degeneracy in asymmetric matter. The four processes considered were neutrino-electron scattering, neutrino-nucleon scattering, electron-positron annihilation, and nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- Electron scattering dominates nucleon scattering as a thermalization process at neutrino energies below approximately 10 MeV; nucleon scattering is faster than or comparable to electron scattering above approximately 10 MeV.
- For densities above 10^13 g cm^-3, temperatures below 14 MeV, and neutrino energies below 60 MeV, nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung always dominates electron-positron annihilation as a production mechanism for muon and tau neutrinos.
- The total volumetric pair emission rate from electron-positron annihilation is approximately 2.09 x 10^24 (T/MeV)^9 f(eta_e) ergs cm^-3 s^-1, where f(eta_e) is a function of the electron degeneracy parameter.
- Muon and tau neutrinos carry away 50-60% of the approximately 2-3 x 10^53 ergs liberated during core collapse and explosion.
- At temperatures around 10 MeV, the fractional energy exchange per collision for neutrino-nucleon scattering is approximately 10-20% due to thermal nucleon velocities, compared to the approximately 1% fractional energy loss from the stationary-nucleon approximation.
- Nucleon-nucleon interactions may suppress the neutrino-nucleon interaction rate by a factor of approximately 2-3.
- The matrix element for nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung includes a factor zeta, assumed of order unity but possibly as low as 0.1, reflecting uncertainty in the momentum-transfer dependence.

## Context

This work extends the structure function formalism developed by Reddy et al. (1998) and Burrows and Sawyer (1998) for neutrino-nucleon scattering, and provides the first comprehensive comparison of all four relevant processes for muon and tau neutrino thermalization and production in supernovae. Prior work by Lamb and Pethick had held that neutrino-nucleon scattering was unimportant for equilibration, and full supernova calculations by Bruenn (1985) and others had neglected nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung as a neutrino source, including only electron-positron annihilation and plasmon decay. The results enable more accurate treatment of muon and tau neutrino transport in core-collapse supernova simulations and protoneutron star cooling calculations, particularly in the semi-transparent regime where multiple processes compete.
