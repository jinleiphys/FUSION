# Remeasuring the anomalously enhanced $B(E2; 2^+ \rightarrow 1^+)$ in $^8\mathrm{Li}$

> The large reported $E2$ strength between the $2^+$ ground state and $1^+$ first excited state of $\isotope[8]{Li}$, $B(E2; 2^+ \rightarrow 1^+)= 55(15)\,e^2\fm^4$, presents a puzzle. Unlike in neighboring $A=7\text{--}9$ isotopes, where enhanced $E2$ strengths may be understood to arise from deformation as rotational in-band transitions, the $2^+\rightarrow1^+$ transition in $^8$Li cannot be understood in any simple way as a rotational in-band transition. Moreover, the reported strength exceeds \textit{ab initio} predictions by an order of magnitude. In light of this discrepancy, we revisited the Coulomb excitation measurement of this strength, now using particle-$γ$ coincidences, yielding a revised $B(E2; 2^+ \rightarrow 1^+)$ of $19(^{+7}_{-6})(2)$~e$^2$fm$^4$. We explore how this value compares to what might be expected in the limits of rotational models. While the present value is about a factor of three smaller than previously reported, it remains anomalously enhanced.

## Key claim

The paper reports a revised $B(E2; 2^+ \rightarrow 1^+)$ value of $19(^{+7}_{-6})(2)$ e$^2$fm$^4$ for $^8$Li, which is about a factor of three smaller than the previously reported $55(15)$ e$^2$fm$^4$ but remains anomalously enhanced compared to theoretical expectations; the authors note that while the new measurement resolves some of the discrepancy, the strength still cannot be understood as a simple rotational in-band transition and exceeds _ab initio_ predictions.

## Method

The authors performed a Coulomb excitation measurement of $^8$Li using a radioactive beam produced via the $^9$Be($^7$Li,$^8$Be)$^8$Li reaction at 22.7(5) MeV, delivered through the TwinSol apparatus at the University of Notre Dame Nuclear Science Laboratory. They used a $^{197}$Au secondary target and detected inelastically scattered $^8$Li nuclei with an annular Si detector in coincidence with deexcitation gamma rays measured by an array of 10 LaBr$_3$ detectors from the HAGRiD array, enabling particle-gamma coincidence gating to suppress background. The $B(E2)$ value was extracted by matching the experimental gamma-ray yield to Geant4 simulations incorporating Winther-De Boer Coulomb excitation calculations.

## Key numbers

- Revised $B(E2; 2^+ \rightarrow 1^+) = 19(^{+7}_{-6})(2)$ e$^2$fm$^4$ for $^8$Li, extracted from Coulomb excitation with particle-gamma coincidences
- Previously reported value was $55(15)$ e$^2$fm$^4$ (Brown 1991), about a factor of three larger than the present result
- The measured gamma-ray peak at 981 keV contained $31^{+13}_{-11}$ counts from the Bayesian analysis
- Beam rate was $4.1(3) \times 10^5$ pps over 5 days of data collection
- The LaBr$_3$ array had total gamma-ray efficiency of 0.82% at 1 MeV
- A $B(E2)$ value below $5.2$ e$^2$fm$^4$ is excluded at 97.7% confidence based on the data
- Systematic uncertainty of 2 e$^2$fm$^4$ from M1 and E1 dipole polarizability contributions

## Context

This work revisits the Coulomb excitation measurement of Brown (1991) that reported an anomalously large $B(E2)$ in $^8$Li, which exceeded _ab initio_ Green's Function Monte Carlo predictions (Pastore 2013) by nearly two orders of magnitude and could not be understood as a rotational in-band transition unlike neighboring $A=7-9$ isotopes. The new measurement brings the $^8$Li strength into the range of $E2$ strengths observed in $^7$Li, $^7$Be, and $^9$Be (up to $\approx 42$ e$^2$fm$^4$ in $^9$Be), but the value remains enhanced relative to the Weisskopf single-particle estimate ($\approx 0.95$ e$^2$fm$^4$ for $A=8$) and still exceeds _ab initio_ No-Core Shell Model predictions, motivating further theoretical work to understand the structure of this nucleus.
