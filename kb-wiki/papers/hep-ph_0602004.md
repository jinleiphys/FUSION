# Pentaquark state in pole-dominated QCD sum rules

> We propose a new approach in QCD sum rules applied for exotic hadrons with a number of quarks, exemplifying the pentaquark Theta^{+} (I=0,J=1/2) in the Borel sum rule. Our approach enables reliable extraction of the pentaquark properties from the sum rule with good stability in a remarkably wide Borel window. The appearance of its valid window originates from a favorable setup of the correlation functions with the aid of it chirality of the interpolating fields on the analogy of the Weinberg sum rule for the vector currents. Our setup leads to large suppression of the continuum contributions which have spoiled the Borel stability in the previous analyses, and consequently enhances importance of the higher-dimensional contributions of the OPE, which are indispensable for investigating the pentaquark properties. Implementing the OPE analysis up to dimension 15, we find that the sum rules for the chiral-even and odd parts independently give the Theta^{+} mass of 1.68 pm 0.22 GeV with uncertainties of the condensate values. Our sum rule indeed gives rather flat Borel curves almost independent of the continuum thresholds both for the mass and pole residue. Finally, we also discuss possible isolation of the observed states from the KN scattering state on view of chiral symmetry.

# Pentaquark state in pole-dominated QCD sum rules

## Key claim
The authors propose a new QCD sum rule approach for the pentaquark Theta+ (I=0, J=1/2) that achieves reliable pole dominance and Borel stability for the first time, yielding a mass of 1.68 ± 0.22 GeV, but this result depends on the specific linear combination of correlation functions (t ≈ 1) and the inclusion of OPE terms up to dimension 15, and does not directly address whether the experimentally observed state exists.

## Method
The authors construct two interpolating fields for the pentaquark Theta+ using diquark operators (pseudoscalar and scalar diquarks) and take a linear combination of their correlation functions with a mixing parameter t, analogous to the Weinberg sum rule for vector currents. They perform an operator product expansion (OPE) analysis up to dimension 15, including quark condensates, gluon condensates, and mixed condensates, and apply Borel sum rules to extract the mass and residue from the chiral-even and chiral-odd parts independently, with careful determination of the Borel window based on pole dominance (≥50%) and OPE convergence criteria.

## Key numbers
- Theta+ mass from chiral-even sum rule: 1.64 ± 0.03 GeV (with sqrt(s_th) = 2.2 GeV, t = 0.9)
- Theta+ mass from chiral-odd sum rule: 1.72 ± 0.05 GeV (with sqrt(s_th) = 2.1 GeV, t = 1.1)
- Combined mass including QCD parameter uncertainties: 1.68 ± 0.22 GeV
- Pole residue from chiral-even sum rule: lambda_0^2 = (3.0 ± 0.1) × 10^(-9) GeV^12
- Pole residue from chiral-odd sum rule: lambda_1^2 / m_Theta+ = (3.4 ± 0.2) × 10^(-9) GeV^12
- Borel window for even part: 2.5 ≤ M^2 ≤ (2.9-3.0) GeV^2 at sqrt(s_th) = 2.1-2.3 GeV
- Borel window for odd part: 0.7 ≤ M^2 ≤ (1.2-1.3) GeV^2 at sqrt(s_th) = 2.0-2.2 GeV

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing problem in prior pentaquark QCD sum rule analyses (Sugiyama et al., Oganesian et al., and the finite energy sum rule work by Eidemuller et al.) where pole contributions were less than 20% and Borel stability could not be established. The authors' approach extends the method of Ioffe's optimization criteria and the Weinberg sum rule technique to exotic hadrons, enabling reliable extraction of pentaquark properties. The results enable future studies of the Theta+ mass shift in nuclear matter and comparisons with lattice QCD calculations, while also providing a framework for analyzing other exotic hadrons with many quark fields.
