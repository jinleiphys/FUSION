# Predictions of alpha-decay half-lives based on potentials from self-consistent mean-field models

> We present a microscopic model for the calculation of alpha-decay half lives employing potentials obtained from relativistic and non-relativistic self-consistent mean-field models. The nuclear and Coulomb potentials are used to obtain the tunneling probability and, in one model variant, also the knocking frequency. The model contains only one parameter. We compare this approach employing several modern mean-field parametrizations to experimental data and to the semi-empirical Viola-Seaborg systematics. We extrapolate our model to superheavy nuclei where assumptions entering semi-empirical approaches might lose validity.

# Predictions of alpha-decay half-lives based on potentials from self-consistent mean-field models

## Key claim
The authors present a one-parameter microscopic model for alpha-decay half-lives that uses nuclear and Coulomb potentials from self-consistent mean-field calculations (relativistic and non-relativistic), claiming accuracy comparable to or slightly better than the semi-empirical Viola-Seaborg systematics, but with the advantage of being more reliable for extrapolation to superheavy nuclei where the assumptions of semi-empirical approaches may lose validity. The hedge is that the model's single parameter (an average pre-formation factor) absorbs all errors and approximations in the calculations of tunneling probability and knocking frequency, and the model currently assumes spherical symmetry, losing validity for deformed nuclei.

## Method
The alpha-nucleus potential is constructed as the sum of two proton and two neutron potentials taken from spherical self-consistent mean-field calculations using relativistic mean-field (RMF) models and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approaches. The transmission probability (Gamow factor) and knocking frequency are computed via numerical integration of the WKB formulas using these potentials, and the alpha-decay constant is obtained as the product of a fitted pre-formation factor, the knocking frequency, and the transmission probability. Only even-even nuclei are considered, and the model is adjusted to experimental data using a logarithmic chi-squared fit with one free parameter (the average pre-formation factor), with an alternative variant that absorbs both pre-formation and knocking frequency into a single combined parameter.

## Key numbers
- The model achieves chi-squared values per point ranging from 0.14 to 0.24 across different mean-field forces, compared to 0.16 for the authors' own adjustment of the Viola-Seaborg systematics to the same data.
- The best-performing force is SLy6 (Skyrme-Hartree-Fock) with chi-squared = 0.14 (combined parameter variant) and 0.15 (explicit knocking frequency variant).
- The average pre-formation factors range from 0.006 (PC-F1 folded) to 0.087 (SkI3), all below 0.1 as expected from prior estimates.
- The original Viola-Seaborg systematics yields chi-squared = 2.57, and the improved Viola-Seaborg yields chi-squared = 1.27, when evaluated against the same experimental data.
- Experimental half-lives span from 1.1 x 10^{-7} s to 2.2 x 10^{23} s, and the model achieves deviations |chi| < 0.3-0.6 in most cases (well below one order of magnitude).

## Context
This work extends the Gamow (1928) tunneling picture and the Viola-Seaborg (1966) semi-empirical systematics by replacing the simplified uniform-plus-Coulomb potential with microscopically calculated potentials from self-consistent mean-field models. It builds on prior mean-field parametrizations including NL3, NL-Z2, PC-F1, SkI3, and SLy6, and references calculations of pre-formation factors using Nilsson model wave functions and cluster models. The approach enables future work on deformed nuclei, microscopic pre-formation factor calculations, fine-structure alpha decay, and applications to superheavy and bubble nuclei where semi-empirical formulas may be unreliable.
