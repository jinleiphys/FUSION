# Electrical conductivity tensor of dense plasma in magnetic fields

> Electrical conductivity of finite-temperature plasma in neutron star crusts is studied for applications in magneto-hydrodynamical description of compact stars. We solve the Boltzmann kinetic equation in relaxation time approximation taking into account the anisotropy of transport due to the magnetic field, the effects of dynamical screening in the scattering matrix element and corre- lations among the nuclei. We show that conductivity has a minimum at a non-zero temperature, a low-temperature decrease and a power-law increase with increasing temperature. Selected numerical results are shown for matter composed of carbon, iron, and heavier nuclei present in the outer crusts of neutron star.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of finite-temperature plasma in neutron star crusts exhibits a minimum at a non-zero temperature, with a low-temperature decrease and a power-law increase at higher temperatures, but this behavior is derived under the relaxation time approximation and assumes the hard-thermal-loop approximation for the polarization tensor, which is appropriate only at not very high temperatures.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation for relativistic electrons scattering off correlated ions, incorporating the anisotropy of transport due to magnetic fields, dynamical screening of the electron-ion interaction via longitudinal and transverse components of the polarization tensor, and ionic correlations through the structure function of a one-component plasma. The relaxation time was computed using the scattering matrix element that includes both longitudinal and transverse photon exchange, with the ionic structure function taken from Monte-Carlo calculations for strongly coupled plasmas and analytical expressions for weakly coupled plasmas. The components of the conductivity tensor were then obtained by integrating the relaxation time over electron energy weighted by the Fermi-Dirac distribution.

## Key numbers

- The scalar conductivity has a minimum as a function of temperature in the range 0.1-1 MeV (MeV = 1.16 x 10^10 K) for densities from 10^6 to 10^10 g cm^-3.
- The zero-temperature Drude formula is valid from low temperatures up to 0.1-1 MeV, where the lower bound corresponds to density rho ~ 10^6 g cm^-3 and the upper bound to rho ~ 10^10 g cm^-3.
- The magnetic field was fixed at B_12 = 1, where B_12 is the magnetic field in units of 10^12 G.
- The melting temperature of the lattice is defined by the Coulomb plasma parameter Gamma_m ~ 160, with Gamma = 22.73 (Z^2/T_6) (rho_6/A)^(1/3), where T_6 is temperature in units of 10^6 K and rho_6 is density in units of 10^6 g cm^-3.
- The nuclear form factor uses charge radius r_c = 1.15 A^(1/3) fm.

## Context

This work extends the transport calculations of Flowers and Itoh (1976) and Nandkumar and Pethick (1984) for dense matter in neutron star crusts to finite temperatures and includes dynamical screening effects that were not fully treated in earlier studies. The results enable improved magneto-hydrodynamical descriptions of proto-neutron stars and hypermassive remnants from neutron star mergers, where the warm, non-degenerate regime is relevant. The formalism also provides a framework for future calculations of thermal conductivity and shear viscosity where electron-electron scattering may dominate.
