# Spin and dynamics in relativistic quantum theories

## Key claim

The spin and orbital angular momentum content of hadronic systems is not uniquely defined in relativistic theories; rather, it depends on the choice of boost used to define the spin observable, and the momentum-dependence of the hadronic wave function, which is determined by the dynamics, necessarily enters the relation between partonic and hadronic spins through Poincaré group Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Method

The authors derive the general relation between hadronic spin and partonic spin and orbital angular momentum using Poincaré group Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, which depend on the choice of boost (helicity, light-front, canonical, or constituent spin). They show that different spin observables are related by momentum-dependent Melosh rotations, and that the associated orbital angular momentum operators are defined by partial derivatives that hold a specific spin observable constant. The equivalence between Poincaré covariant (relativistic quantum mechanics) and Lorentz covariant (field theory) representations is demonstrated by absorbing Wigner rotations of boosts into the state vectors, at the cost of making the Hilbert space inner product dynamical.

## Key numbers

- The relation between different spin observables is given by Melosh rotations: \( j_a^l = R_{ab}(p)^l{}_m j_b^m \) where \( R_{ab}(p)^l{}_m = B_a^{-1}(p)^l{}_{\mu} B_b(p)^{\mu}_{m} \), with \( a \) and \( b \) indexing different boost types.
- The angular momentum decomposition in any representation takes the form \( J^j = (\mathbf{X}_a \times \mathbf{P})^j + C_{2a}^{jk}(\mathbf{V}) j_a^k \), where \( C_{2a}^{jk}(\mathbf{V}) \) is a function of the Poincaré generators given by \( C_{2a}^{jk}(\mathbf{V}) = \frac{1}{2} \mathrm{Tr}[B_a(\mathbf{V})^{-1}\sigma_j B_a(\mathbf{V})\sigma_k] + i \epsilon_{jlm} \mathrm{Tr}[B_a(\mathbf{V})^{-1}\frac{\partial}{\partial V_l} B_a(\mathbf{V})\sigma_m] \).
- The Lorentz covariant inner product kernel is \( D^j_{bb'}[P^{\mu}\sigma_{\mu}/M] \) for the spinor representation, and becomes a \( 2 \times (2j+1) \) component spinor with block-diagonal kernel when including space reflection.

## Context

This paper extends the formal treatment of spin in relativistic few-body systems, building on the Poincaré group Clebsch-Gordan coefficient formalism developed by Moussa, and by Bierter, Klink, and Polyzou. It directly addresses the ambiguity in defining spin observables noted by Melosh (1974) for light-front spin, and shows that the dynamical dependence of spin content is unavoidable in both relativistic quantum mechanics and QCD. The work enables a systematic framework for interpreting spin and orbital angular momentum contributions in hadronic structure calculations, while emphasizing that experimental interpretation requires specifying which spin observable couples to electroweak currents.
