# Baryon interactions from lattice QCD with physical masses -- Overview and $S = 0, -4$ sectors --

> Nuclear forces and hyperon forces are studied by lattice QCD. Simulations are performed with (almost) physical quark masses, $m_π\simeq 146$ MeV and $m_K \simeq 525$ MeV, where $N_f=2+1$ nonperturbatively ${\cal O}(a)$-improved Wilson quark action with stout smearing and Iwasaki gauge action are employed on the lattice of $(96a)^4 \simeq (8.1\mbox{fm})^4$ with $a^{-1} \simeq 2.3$ GeV. In this report, we give the overview of the theoretical framework and present the numerical results for two-nucleon forces ($S=0$) and two-$Ξ$ forces ($S=-4$). Central forces are studied in $^1S_0$ channel, and central and tensor forces are obtained in $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ coupled channel analysis.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD simulations with nearly physical quark masses (mπ ≈ 146 MeV, mK ≈ 525 MeV) yield baryon-baryon potentials for the NN (S=0) and ΞΞ (S=-4) systems, but the results for NN central forces in both the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels suffer from large statistical fluctuations and are presented as preliminary, with the relativistic correction term (∂²/∂t²) neglected in the analysis.

## Method
The authors employ the time-dependent HAL QCD method, which extracts energy-independent baryon-baryon potentials from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) correlators without requiring ground-state saturation. Simulations use Nf=2+1 nonperturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quark action with stout smearing and Iwasaki gauge action on a (96a)⁴ ≈ (8.1 fm)⁴ lattice with a⁻¹ ≈ 2.3 GeV. Central forces are extracted in the ¹S₀ channel, while central and tensor forces are obtained via coupled-channel analysis in the ³S₁-³D₁ channel using a velocity expansion to leading order.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: mπ ≈ 146 MeV; kaon mass: mK ≈ 525 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a⁻¹ ≈ 2.333 GeV (a ≈ 0.0846 fm); lattice volume: (96a)⁴ ≈ (8.1 fm)⁴
- Statistics: 414 configurations × 4 rotations × 48 wall sources, binned by 46 configurations
- ΞΞ (¹S₀) central force: strong mid- and long-range attraction with repulsive core observed at t = 15-18; phase shifts indicate interaction is "strongly attractive at low energies" but insufficient to form a bound state
- ΞΞ (³S₁-³D₁) tensor force: opposite sign and weaker compared to NN tensor force
- NN tensor force (³S₁-³D₁): strong tensor force with long-range tail observed at t = 7-10; tail structures similar to bare OPEP and AV18 phenomenological potential
- NN central force (¹S₀): repulsive core more enhanced than ΞΞ (¹S₀), consistent with one-gluon exchange picture

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's previous studies (Doi 2015) by performing calculations at nearly physical quark masses, moving beyond earlier simulations with unphysically heavy ud-quark masses. The results enable first-principles QCD-based studies of nuclear and hyperon forces relevant for neutron star structure and dense matter equations of state, as previously explored by Inoue (2013) and McIlroy (2017). The time-dependent HAL QCD method avoids the ground-state saturation issues that affect "direct" lattice calculations (Yamazaki 2015, Orginos 2015, Berkowitz 2015), which have been shown to suffer from fake plateaux or "mirage" signals (Iritani 2016). The ΞΞ (¹S₀) results probe whether the 27-plet SU(3) interaction forms a bound state, following theoretical work by Haidenbauer (2014).
