# Study of internal structures of 9,10Be and 10B in scattering of 4He from 9Be

> A study of inelastic scattering and single-particle transfer reactions was performed by an alpha beam at 63 MeV on a 9$Be target. Angular distributions of the differential cross sections for the 9Be(4He,4He')9Be*, 9Be(4He,3He)10Be and 9Be(4He,t)10B reactions were measured. Experimental angular distributions of the differential cross sections for the ground state and a few low-lying states were analyzed in the framework of the optical model, coupled channels and distorted-wave Born approximation. An analysis of the obtained spectroscopic factors was performed.

## Key claim

The internal structures of ⁹Be, ¹⁰Be, and ¹⁰B were studied via inelastic scattering and single-particle transfer reactions using a 63 MeV alpha beam on a ⁹Be target, but the optical model parameters were found to be generally not sensitive to the cluster structures of the excited states, and unambiguous preference between possible cluster configurations (e.g., α+α+n versus α+⁵He) could not be given.

## Method

Angular distributions of differential cross sections were measured for the ⁹Be(α,α')⁹Be*, ⁹Be(α,³He)¹⁰Be, and ⁹Be(α,t)¹⁰B reactions using Si-Si(Li) detector telescopes with ΔE-E particle identification at the K130 Cyclotron facility. The experimental data were analyzed within the optical model, coupled-channels (CC) formalism using the ECIS06 code, and finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) using the DWUCK5 code, with spectroscopic factors extracted for populated states.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 63 MeV for ⁴He ions on ⁹Be target; average beam current 3 nA; target thickness 7 μm; overall energy resolution ~200 keV.
- Q-values: ⁹Be(α,³He)¹⁰Be = −13.8 MeV; ⁹Be(α,t)¹⁰B = −13.2 MeV; ⁹Be(α,d)¹¹B = −8.0 MeV; ⁹Be(α,p)¹²B = −6.9 MeV.
- Deformation parameter β₂ = 0.64 and deformation length β₂R_V = 1.574 fm for the ⁹Be rotational band (Kπ = 3/2⁻); additional hexadecapole term β₄ = 0.27 needed for the 11.28 MeV state.
- Spectroscopic factor S_f for ¹⁰Be ground state (0⁺): 1.65 (present) vs. 1.58 from Ref. [4]; for ¹⁰Be 3.368 MeV (2⁺) state: 1.00 (present) vs. 0.38 from Ref. [4].
- Spectroscopic factor S_f for ¹⁰B ground state (3⁺): 0.59 (present) vs. 0.89 from Ref. [4]; for ¹⁰B 0.781 MeV (1⁺) state: 1.0 (present) vs. 1.0 from Ref. [4].
- Quadrupole moment Q₂₀ of ⁹Be: +53 mb, indicating prolate deformation.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements by Ref. [4] (alpha scattering at 65 MeV) and Ref. [5], providing improved particle identification via ΔE-E telescopes that allowed unambiguous separation of p, d, t, ³He, and ⁴He. The results confirm the rotational band structure of ⁹Be (Kπ = 3/2⁻) and assign spin-parity 9/2⁻ to the 11.28 MeV state, consistent with Ref. [18]. The extracted spectroscopic factors for ¹⁰Be and ¹⁰B are compared with those of Ref. [4], and the data enable future experiments on cluster decay of excited states, though distinguishing break-up into ⁴He and ⁵He remains a challenging kinematical problem.
