# The landscape of two-proton radioactivity

> Ground-state two-proton (2p) radioactivity is a decay mode found in isotopes of elements with even atomic numbers located beyond the two-proton drip line. So far, this exotic process has been experimentally observed in a few light and medium-mass nuclides with Z less than or equal to 30. In this study, using state-of-the-art nuclear density functional theory, we globally analyze 2p radioactivity and for the first time identify 2p decay candidates in elements heavier than strontium. We predict a few cases where the competition between 2p emission and alpha decay may be observed. In nuclei above lead, the alpha decay mode is found to be dominating and no measurable candidates for the 2p radioactivity are expected.

## Key claim

Using nuclear density functional theory, the authors globally identify two-proton (2p) radioactivity candidates in elements heavier than strontium for the first time, but after correcting an error in proton pairing gaps, the energy criterion for true simultaneous 2p emission is relaxed to \(Q_{2p} > 0, Q_p < 0.2 Q_{2p}\), and such candidates are found only up to tellurium.

## Method

The study employs nuclear density functional theory with several mass models (SV-min, UNEDF1, SLy4, SkM*) to calculate \(Q_p\), \(Q_{2p}\), and \(Q_{\alpha}\) values across the nuclear chart. Candidates for true simultaneous 2p emission are identified using the energy criterion \(Q_{2p} > 0, Q_p < 0.2 Q_{2p}\) combined with half-life constraints from both direct and diproton models, while sequential pp emission is identified for cases satisfying \(Q_{2p} > 0, Q_{2p} > Q_p > 0.2 Q_{2p}\).

## Key numbers

- The predicted average path of sequential pp radioactivity coincides with the \(T_{2p} = 0.1\) s limit given by the diproton model.
- The 2p-decay candidates closest to experimental reach beyond \(^{54}\)Zn are \(^{57}\)Ge (3 neutrons beyond known isotopes), \(^{62,63}\)Se (2 and 1 neutrons beyond), \(^{66}\)Kr (3 neutrons beyond), and \(^{103}\)Te (2 neutrons beyond).
- The distance from known isotopes reaches 14 neutrons for \(^{165}\)Pb, predicted to be the pp-emitting lead isotope closest to the drip line.
- The two best candidates for competition between two-proton emission and alpha decay, predicted by at least two mass models, are \(^{103}\)Te and \(^{145}\)Hf.

## Context

This work extends prior experimental observations of two-proton radioactivity in light and medium-mass nuclides with Z ≤ 30 (e.g., references in the original Letter) to heavier elements, using the theoretical framework of nuclear density functional theory. The corrected analysis shows that true simultaneous 2p emission candidates exist only up to tellurium, while sequential pp emission candidates appear in every even-Z isotope above tellurium except xenon, where alpha decay dominates. The results enable experimental searches for 2p radioactivity in the region beyond \(^{54}\)Zn, particularly for \(^{57}\)Ge, \(^{62,63}\)Se, \(^{66}\)Kr, and \(^{103}\)Te, and highlight \(^{103}\)Te and \(^{145}\)Hf as cases where competition with alpha decay may be observed.
