# Beam Energy dependence of Light Nuclei Production in Au+Au Collisions

> We study the collision energy dependence of (anti-)deuteron and (anti-)triton production in the most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=$ 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV, using the nucleon coalescence model. The needed phase-space distribution of nucleons at the kinetic freeze-out is generated from a new 3D hybrid dynamical model (\texttt{iEBE-MUSIC}) by using a smooth crossover equation of state (EoS) without a QCD critical point. Our model calculations predict that the coalescence parameters of (anti-)deuteron ($B_2(d)$ and $B_2(\bar{d})$) decrease monotonically as the collision energy increases, and the light nuclei yield ratio $N_t N_p/N_d^2$ remains approximately a constant with respect to the collision energy. These calculated observables fail to reproduce the non-monotonic behavior of the corresponding data from the STAR Collaboration. Without including any effects of the critical point in our model, our results serve as the baseline predictions for the yields of light nuclei in the search for the possible QCD critical points from the experimental beam energy scan of heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using a nucleon coalescence model fed by phase-space distributions from the iEBE-MUSIC hybrid model with a smooth crossover equation of state (no QCD critical point), the calculated coalescence parameters and light-nuclei yield ratios fail to reproduce the non-monotonic behavior observed by STAR; the results therefore serve as a non-critical baseline for the search for a QCD critical point in the beam energy scan.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-MUSIC (3+1)D hybrid model with a 3D Monte-Carlo Glauber initial condition and a crossover equation of state (NEOS-BQS) to simulate the dynamical evolution of Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies. Nucleon phase-space distributions at kinetic freeze-out were extracted from the hybrid model and fed into a nucleon coalescence model to compute yields of (anti-)deuterons and (anti-)tritons, including both 2-body (d+p) and 3-body (p+n+n) coalescence channels for triton production.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV in 0-10% most central Au+Au collisions
- The specific shear viscosity was set to eta T/(e+P) = 0.08
- Particlization was performed on a hyper-surface with constant energy density e_sw = 0.26 GeV/fm^3
- The model overestimates B_2(d) and B_2(bar-d) by approximately 50% for sqrt(s_NN) = 20-62.4 GeV
- The calculated d/p and bar-d/bar-p ratios are overestimated by 15% and 20%, respectively
- The t/d ratios from the 3-body process are underestimated by 10%
- The yield ratio N_t N_p/N_d^2 from both 2-body and 3-body processes is almost flat across collision energies, in contrast to the STAR data showing a peak around sqrt(s_NN) = 20 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior coalescence model studies (Sun 2017, Sun 2018, Yu 2018, Liu 2019, Sun 2020) by using a more realistic 3D hybrid dynamical model (iEBE-MUSIC) that includes both partonic and hadronic phases, rather than thermal or transport models without a partonic phase. The results contradict the non-monotonic energy dependence observed by the STAR Collaboration (Adam 2019, Zhang 2019) for coalescence parameters and yield ratios, and provide a baseline that enables future studies to isolate the effects of a QCD critical point by comparing with models that include critical fluctuations.
