# Valence quark ratio in the proton

> Beginning with precise data on the ratio of structure functions in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) from $^3$He and $^3$H, collected on the domain $0.19 \leq x_B \leq 0.83$, where $x_B$ is the Bjorken scaling variable, we employ a robust method for extrapolating such data to arrive at a model-independent result for the $x_B=1$ value of the ratio of neutron and proton structure functions. Combining this with information obtained in analyses of DIS from nuclei, corrected for target-structure dependence, we arrive at a prediction for the proton's valence-quark ratio: $\left. d_v/u_v \right|_{x_B\to 1} = 0.230 (57)$. Requiring consistency with this result presents a challenge to many descriptions of proton structure.

## Key claim

The paper uses MARATHON data on the ratio of structure functions from \(^3\)He and \(^3\)H targets, combined with a model-independent extrapolation method, to predict the proton's valence-quark ratio at \(x_B \to 1\) as \(d_v/u_v = 0.230 (57)\), a result that challenges many existing descriptions of proton structure.

## Method

The authors apply the statistical Schlessinger point method (SPM) to the MARATHON data on the cross-section ratio \(\sigma_t/\sigma_h\) (tritium to helium-3) over \(0.19 \leq x_B \leq 0.83\), generating form-unbiased continued-fraction interpolations and extrapolations to \(x_B = 1\). They then use the relation \(F_2^n/F_2^p = (1 + 4 d_v/u_v)/(4 + d_v/u_v)\), valid for \(x \gtrsim 0.2\) where sea-quark contributions are negligible, to convert the extracted structure-function ratio into the valence-quark ratio, incorporating corrections from nuclear DIS analyses for target-structure dependence.

## Key numbers

- \(\left. d_v/u_v \right|_{x_B\to 1} = 0.230 \pm 0.057\): the proton valence-quark ratio at the endpoint, the central result of the paper.
- \(\left. F_2^n/F_2^p \right|_{x_B\to 1} = 0.437 \pm 0.085\): the SPM-extracted structure-function ratio from MARATHON data.
- \(\left. \sigma_t/\sigma_h \right|_{x_B\to 1} = 0.754 \pm 0.052\): the SPM-extracted cross-section ratio from MARATHON data.
- \(\left. F_2^n/F_2^p \right|_{x_B\to 1}^{\text{SPM \& DIS-A}} = 0.454 \pm 0.047\): the average of the SPM result and the nuclear DIS analysis of Segarra (2019).
- The SPM result excludes the Nachtmann lower bound \(F_2^n/F_2^p = 1/4\) (corresponding to \(d_v/u_v = 0\)) with a 99.7% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends the MARATHON experiment (Abrams 2021) by providing a model-independent extrapolation to \(x_B = 1\), and it is consistent with the nuclear DIS analysis of Segarra (2019) that accounts for short-range correlations. The result contradicts models that include only scalar diquark correlations (Close 1988, Anselmino 1992), which predict \(d_v/u_v \approx 0\) at \(x_B = 1\), and instead supports predictions from Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation treatments (Segovia 2014, Xu 2015) that include both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, as well as the QCD parton model prediction of Farrar (1975) and Brodsky (1994). The finding enables discrimination between competing descriptions of proton structure and provides a benchmark for future theoretical and experimental work on valence-quark distributions.
