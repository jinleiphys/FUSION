# Nuclear Structure Calculations with Low-Momentum Potentials in a Model Space Truncation Approach

> We have calculated the ground-state energy of the doubly magic nuclei 4He, 16O and 40Ca within the framework of the Goldstone expansion starting from various modern nucleon-nucleon potentials. The short-range repulsion of these potentials has been renormalized by constructing a low-momentum potential V-low-k. We have studied the connection between the cutoff momemtum Lambda and the size of the harmonic oscillator space employed in the calculations. We have found a fast convergence of the results with a limited number of oscillator quanta.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the cutoff momentum Lambda used in the V-low-k renormalization procedure can be directly mapped to a maximum number of harmonic oscillator quanta N_max, providing a simple criterion to truncate the two-nucleon model space while preserving fast convergence of the Goldstone expansion for doubly magic nuclei.

## Method

The authors renormalized modern nucleon-nucleon potentials (CD-Bonn, N3LO, Bonn A) by constructing a low-momentum potential V-low-k that integrates out high-momentum components above a cutoff Lambda. They introduced a relation linking Lambda to the maximum number of harmonic oscillator quanta N_max via the equation (N_max + 3/2) hbar omega = hbar^2 Lambda^2 / M, and used this criterion to define the two-nucleon model space. Ground-state energies of 4He, 16O, and 40Ca were calculated using the Goldstone expansion up to fourth order in the two-body interaction, with the Hartree-Fock basis expanded in harmonic oscillator wave functions and Padé approximants used to estimate convergence.

## Key numbers

* 4He ground-state energy with CD-Bonn potential: -25.92 MeV (converged with hbar omega = 36 MeV), compared to Faddeev-Yakubovsky result; discrepancy 0.39 MeV for N3LO potential
* 4He ground-state energy with N3LO potential: -25.02 MeV (hbar omega = 29 MeV)
* 4He ground-state energy with Bonn A potential: -27.78 MeV (hbar omega = 34.5 MeV)
* 16O ground-state energy with CD-Bonn potential: -118.1 MeV (hbar omega = 27 MeV), discrepancy of 9.5 MeV from experimental value
* 40Ca ground-state energy with CD-Bonn potential: -307.8 MeV (hbar omega = 25.5 MeV, using Padé approximant [2|1] up to third order), discrepancy of 34.2 MeV from experimental value
* Exact 4He ground-state energy from hyperspherical harmonic approach with V-low-k from CD-Bonn: -27.95 MeV
* Padé approximant [3|1] and [2|2] for 4He in largest model space gave -27.79 MeV and -27.84 MeV respectively, within 45 keV of each other

## Context

This work extends the V-low-k renormalization approach previously developed by Bogner, Kuo, and Coraggio (2001, 2002) for nuclear structure calculations, and applies it within the Goldstone expansion framework following earlier work by Coraggio et al. (2003, 2005). The key advance is establishing a quantitative relation between the momentum-space cutoff Lambda and the harmonic oscillator basis truncation, enabling systematic convergence studies. The results for 16O are compared with the unitary model-operator approach of Fujii et al. (2004), who obtained -115.61 MeV for CD-Bonn including two-body correlations, with three-body cluster effects estimated at about -4 MeV. The rapid convergence with model space size (not exceeding N_max = 10) suggests the approach can be extended to heavier nuclei.
