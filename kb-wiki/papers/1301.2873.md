# Net quark number probability distribution near the chiral crossover transition

> We investigate properties of the probability distribution of the net quark number near the chiral crossover transition in the quark-meson model. The calculations are performed within the functional renormalization group approach, as well as in the mean-field approximation. We find, that there is a substantial influence of the underlying chiral phase transition on the properties of the probability distribution. In particular, for a physical pion mass, the distribution which includes the effect of mesonic fluctuations, differs considerably from both, the mean-field and Skellam distributions. The latter is considered as a reference for a non-critical behavior. A characteristic feature of the net quark number probability distribution is that, in the vicinity of the chiral crossover transition in the O(4) universality class, it is narrower than the corresponding mean-field and Skellam function. We study the volume dependence of the probability distribution, as well as the resulting cumulants, and discuss their approximate scaling properties.

## Key claim

Near the chiral crossover transition in the quark-meson model with physical pion mass, the net quark number probability distribution that includes mesonic fluctuations via the functional renormalization group (FRG) is narrower than both the mean-field and Skellam distributions, with the difference reflecting O(4) criticality rather than merely quantum statistics effects.

## Method

The authors computed the thermodynamic potential of the two-flavor quark-meson model using both the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach in the local potential approximation and the mean-field (MF) approximation. They then obtained the canonical partition function and net quark number probability distribution P(N) via a contour integral over imaginary chemical potential, using the grand canonical partition function at imaginary chemical potential. The FRG approach included mesonic fluctuations, while the MF approximation omitted them, and both were compared to the Skellam distribution (non-critical reference) and a free Fermi gas distribution.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature T_pc = 214 MeV in the FRG approach and 190 MeV in the mean-field approximation, defined by the peak of the chiral susceptibility.
- Pion mass m_pi = 135 MeV and sigma mass m_sigma = 640 MeV used to fix model parameters in vacuum.
- Yukawa coupling g = 3.2, fixed by constituent quark mass M_q = 300 MeV with sigma vacuum expectation value f_pi = 93 MeV.
- Ultraviolet cutoff scale Lambda = 1.0 GeV used for the flow equations.
- At T/T_pc = 0.98 in the FRG approach, the ratio M_q/T is approximately 0.6, indicating that the Boltzmann approximation is not justified.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Morita et al. (2012) on net baryon number probability distributions near the chiral phase transition, moving from mean-field and scaling theory to the more realistic quark-meson model with FRG treatment of fluctuations. It builds on lattice QCD results by Ejiri et al. (2009) and Bazavov et al. (2011) showing O(4) scaling in the magnetic equation of state, and on earlier work by Skokov et al. (2011) and Friman et al. (2011) on cumulants of conserved charges. The findings enable more precise predictions for event-by-event fluctuation measurements in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for interpreting STAR Collaboration data on net proton cumulants.
