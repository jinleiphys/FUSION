# Schwinger functions and light-quark bound states

> We examine the applicability and viability of methods to obtain knowledge about bound-states from information provided solely in Euclidean space. Rudimentary methods can be adequate if one only requires information about the ground and first excited state and assumptions made about analytic properties are valid. However, to obtain information from Schwinger functions about higher mass states, something more sophisticated is necessary. A method based on the correlator matrix can be dependable when operators are carefully tuned and errors are small. This method is nevertheless not competitive when an unambiguous analytic continuation of even a single Schwinger function to complex momenta is available.

## Key claim

Rudimentary methods (Padé approximants, effective masses, exponential fits) can reliably extract ground and first excited state masses from Euclidean-space Schwinger functions only when assumptions about the Schwinger function's analytic structure (specifically, a positive definite spectral density) are valid; for higher states or when the spectral density is not positive definite, these methods fail or give misleading residues, and even the correlator matrix method is not competitive when an unambiguous analytic continuation of a single Schwinger function to complex momenta is available.

## Method

The authors solve the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for pseudoscalar and scalar quark-antiquark vertices in a rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), obtaining the three-point Schwinger functions at spacelike momenta. They then apply diagonal Padé approximants of order N=3 to the inverse vertex function 1/E(0;P²) to extract pole masses and residues, and compare these with results from direct solution of the homogeneous BSE. Separately, they analyze the Fourier-transformed Schwinger function in configuration space using effective mass plots and least-squares fits of sums of exponentials, and also solve the inhomogeneous BSE directly at timelike momenta via matrix inversion to check for unanticipated zeros.

## Context

This work extends the study of meson spectroscopy via Dyson-Schwinger equations (reviewed by Roberts (2000) and others) by critically examining methods to extract bound-state masses from Euclidean-space Schwinger functions, a problem shared with lattice-QCD practitioners (e.g., Michael (1985), Luscher (1990)). The authors find that the rainbow-ladder interaction model of Maris and Tandy (1999) produces a vertex with a zero at timelike momenta, limiting its reliability to bound systems with mass below about 1.1 GeV; this finding enables future work to develop improved interaction kernels that avoid such unphysical analytic structure, and cautions against overinterpreting Padé or exponential fits to Euclidean data for excited states.
