# Dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities of the pion

> Data on pion polarizabilities obtained in different experiments are reviewed. The values of the dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities of the pi^0 and pi^{+-} mesons found are compared with predictions of dispersion sum rules (DSRs) and two-loop calculations in the framework of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). Possible reasons of a difference between the predictions of DSRs and ChPT are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental data on pion polarizabilities and compares them with predictions from dispersion sum rules (DSRs) and chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), finding that while dipole polarizabilities generally agree within errors, the sum of the quadrupole polarizabilities of the neutral pion from ChPT is positive and contradicts the experimental negative value, though this ChPT result is a leading-order two-loop approximation expected to receive substantial three-loop corrections.

## Method

The authors review experimental determinations of pion dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities from three processes: radiative pion scattering off nuclei (π⁻A → γπ⁻A), radiative pion photoproduction from the proton (γp → γπ⁺n), and two-photon fusion (γγ → ππ). For the neutral pion, dispersion relations at fixed t with one subtraction at s = μ² were constructed for the helicity amplitudes of γγ → π⁰π⁰ and used to fit total cross section data from 270–2250 MeV. For charged pions, similar dispersion relations with subtractions were applied to γγ → π⁺π⁻ data from 280–2500 MeV, and an independent analysis of γp → γπ⁺n data from MAMI used a constrained χ² fit.

- Neutral pion dipole polarizability sum (α₁ + β₁)π⁰: fit value 0.98 ± 0.03 (10⁻⁴ fm³), DSR prediction 0.802 ± 0.035, ChPT prediction 1.1 ± 0.3

## Context

This review extends earlier work on pion polarizabilities by comparing experimental results from multiple experiments (including the MAMI experiment by Ahrens et al. and the Serpukhov experiment by Antipov et al.) with theoretical predictions from DSRs by Fil'kov (2005) and two-loop ChPT calculations by Bélanger et al. (1997) and Gasser et al. (2006). The paper identifies a key discrepancy between DSR and ChPT predictions for charged pion polarizabilities, attributing it to the incomplete treatment of the σ-meson in ChPT and to an additional subtraction constant in the dispersion relation used by ChPT that was not accounted for, suggesting that three-loop ChPT calculations may resolve the differences.
