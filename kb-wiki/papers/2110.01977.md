# Analytic scattering parameters at low energies

> The scattering of two and more particles at low energies is described by the so called effective-range expansion. The leading terms of this expansion are the scattering length and effective range. The analytic expressions for both of the aforementioned scattering parameters are presented for the inverse-power potential and the Woods-Saxon potential. A technique for calculating the approximate scattering parameters is proposed. Approximate analytic formulas representing the scattering length and effective range are obtained for the Yukawa potential. The corresponding figures demonstrate a few interesting features of the effective range. All analytic formulas, both exact and approximate, were verified by comparing with the corresponding results obtained by direct numerical calculations. Wolfram Mathematica is heavily used. The presented results can be used with advantage in the fields of nuclear physics, atomic and molecular physics, quantum chemistry and many others.

## Key claim

The paper presents exact analytic expressions for both the scattering length and effective range for the inverse-power potential (with real parameters, not just integer exponents) and the Woods-Saxon potential, and proposes an approximate technique for potentials like the Yukawa potential where exact solutions are unavailable. The author hedges that the Woods-Saxon effective range formula is derived under the condition that the parameter \(x_0\) is very small and \(\alpha\) is close to 1, with relative error less than \(10^{-6}\) for the considered mass number range.

## Method

For the inverse-power potential \(V(r)=\alpha/r^\beta\) with \(\alpha>0\) and \(\beta>3\), the radial Schrödinger equation at zero energy is transformed into a modified Bessel equation, yielding exact solutions for the scattering length and effective range in terms of gamma functions and hypergeometric functions. For the Woods-Saxon potential \(V(r)=-U_0/[1+\exp((r-R)/\eta)]\), the zero-energy Schrödinger equation is solved exactly using Gauss hypergeometric functions, and the effective range is expressed via integrals that are evaluated by splitting the integration range and using series expansions. For the Yukawa potential \(V(r)=-\lambda e^{-r}/r\), an approximate method is proposed: the wave function is expanded in a basis of exponentials \(e^{-\beta r}r^n\), the scattering length is obtained via quasilinearization, and the effective range is computed from the resulting approximate wave function.

## Key numbers

- For the inverse-power potential, the limit values as \(\beta\rightarrow\infty\) are \(a=1\) and \(r_e=2/3\).
- For the inverse-power potential, the effective range has zeros at \(\beta=10/3\) and \(\beta=6\), and discontinuities at \(\beta=3, 3.5, 4, 5\).
- For the Woods-Saxon potential with parameters \(R=1.285 A^{1/3}\) fm, \(\eta=0.65\) fm, \(U_0=40.5+0.13A\) MeV, and mass number \(40\leq A\leq 72\), the scattering length shows a discontinuity at \(A\simeq 52.2504\) and the effective range at \(A\simeq 46.2708\).
- For the Woods-Saxon example, the parameter \(x_0\) is less than 0.0012 and \(\alpha\) is between 0.95 and 1.
- The approximate analytic formula for the Yukawa potential is verified by comparison with direct numerical calculations, but no specific numerical results for the Yukawa case are given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the classic treatment of the inverse-power potential by Landau and Lifshitz (1977), who gave exact scattering lengths only for integer \(\beta>3\), to real parameters and also provides the effective range. For the Woods-Saxon potential, it provides exact analytic scattering parameters, contrasting with the approximate Nikiforov-Uvarov method used by C. Berkdemir, A. Berkdemir, and J. Han (2006). The results enable accurate low-energy parametrization for nuclear, atomic, and molecular scattering, and can be used in effective field theory calculations of nuclear matter and finite nuclei.
