# Few-body calculations of $η$-nuclear quasibound states

> We report on precise hyperspherical-basis calculations of $ηNN$ and $ηNNN$ quasibound states, using energy dependent $ηN$ interaction potentials derived from coupled-channel models of the $S_{11}$ $N^{\ast}(1535)$ nucleon resonance. The $ηN$ attraction generated in these models is too weak to generate a two-body bound state. No $ηNN$ bound-state solution was found in our calculations in models where Re $a_{ηN}\lesssim 1$ fm, with $a_{ηN}$ the $ηN$ scattering length, covering thereby the majority of $N^{\ast}(1535)$ resonance models. A near-threshold $ηNNN$ bound-state solution, with $η$ separation energy of less than 1 MeV and width of about 15 MeV, was obtained in the 2005 Green-Wycech model where Re $a_{ηN}\approx 1$ fm. The role of handling self consistently the subthreshold $ηN$ interaction is carefully studied.

# Few-body calculations of η-nuclear quasibound states

## Key claim
The ηN attraction derived from coupled-channel models of the N*(1535) resonance is too weak to bind the ηN two-body system or the ηNN three-body system for models where Re aηN ≲ 1 fm, but a near-threshold ηNNN quasibound state with η separation energy less than 1 MeV and width about 15 MeV exists in the Green-Wycech (2005) model where Re aηN ≈ 1 fm, provided the momentum scale parameter Λ is chosen at the larger value of 4 fm⁻¹ rather than 2 fm⁻¹.

## Method
Energy-dependent local ηN potentials vηN were constructed from subthreshold ηN scattering amplitudes in the GW (Green-Wycech 2005) and CS (Cervera-Sarkar 2013) coupled-channel models, using a Gaussian form factor with scale parameter Λ = 2 or 4 fm⁻¹. Hyperspherical-basis solutions of the Schrödinger equation were obtained for ηNN (three-body) and ηNNN (four-body) systems with realistic NN potentials (Minnesota MN and Argonne AV4'), applying a self-consistency requirement that the subthreshold energy argument of vηN matches the expectation value ⟨δ√s⟩ derived from the few-body solution.

## Key numbers
- Re aηN ≈ 0.96 fm for the GW model (Green-Wycech 2005), the largest value among models studied
- Re aηN values range from 0.2 fm to almost 1.0 fm across N*(1535) resonance models
- ηNNN η separation energy after self-consistency: 0.95 MeV (MN NN potential) and 0.04 MeV (AV4' NN potential)
- ηNNN total binding energy after self-consistency: −9.33 MeV (MN) and −9.03 MeV (AV4')
- ηNNN width Γ ≈ 13.5 MeV (MN) and 15.75 MeV (AV4')
- Self-consistent subthreshold energy shift δ√s_s.c. = −13.52 MeV (MN) and −15.83 MeV (AV4')
- Self-consistency reduction factor x_s.c. = 0.914 (MN) and 0.895 (AV4')
- 3N binding energy: −8.38 MeV (MN) and −8.99 MeV (AV4')
- No ηNN bound state found for any model with Re aηN ≲ 1 fm; multiplicative factors of 1.1 (Λ=4 fm⁻¹) or 1.3 (Λ=2 fm⁻¹) on the GW threshold strength needed to produce binding
- Momentum scale parameter Λ from separable models: 3.9 fm⁻¹ (KWW/KSW 1995), 3.2 fm⁻¹ (GW 2005), 6.6 fm⁻¹ (CS 2013)

## Context
This work extends the hyperspherical-basis methodology previously applied to K̄-nuclear systems (Barnea, Friedman, Gal 2012) to η-nuclear few-body systems. The negative result for ηNN binding agrees with earlier Faddeev calculations (Deloff 2000, Garcilazo and Pena 2000, Faddeev and Adam 2000, Wycech and Green 2001, Garcilazo 2003) that ruled out ηd quasibound states. The only prior ηNNN Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation (Faddeev and Adam 2002) found no η³H quasibound state but used Re aηN = 0.75 fm, below the GW value. The present results support the Krusche-Wilkin (2015) conjecture that ηd is unbound and η³He is ambiguous, while enabling future calculations of ηNNNN to test whether η⁴He is bound, and motivating inclusion of three-body ηN forces to resolve the scale-parameter ambiguity.
