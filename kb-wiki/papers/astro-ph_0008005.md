# Phase Diagram for Spinning and Accreting Neutron Stars

> Neutron star configurations are considered as thermodynamical systems for which a phase diagram in the angular velocity (Omega) - baryon number (N) plane is obtained with a dividing line N_{crit}(Omega) for quark core configurations. Trajectories of neutron star evolution in this diagram are studied for different scenarios defined by the external torque acting on the star due to radiation and/or mass accretion. They show a characteristic change in the rotational kinematics when the star enters the quark core regime. For isolated pulsars the braking index signal for deconfinement has been studied in its dependence on the mass of the star. Model calculations of the spin evolution of accreting low-mass X-ray binaries in the phase diagram have been performed for different values of the initial magnetic field, its decay time as well as initial mass and mass accretion rate. Population clustering of these objects at the line N_{crit}(Omega) in the phase diagram is suggested as an observable signal for the deconfinement phase transition if it exists for spinnning and accreting neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a deconfinement phase transition from hadronic to quark matter in neutron stars can be detected observationally through a characteristic change in rotational kinematics, specifically a braking index deviation for isolated pulsars or a population clustering of accreting low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs) along a critical line in the angular velocity versus baryon number phase diagram. The authors hedge that this signal is model-dependent and that for their particular equation of state the braking index signal is weak for most candidate pulsars, only becoming significant for periods below 1.5 ms which have not yet been observed.

## Method

The authors construct a phase diagram for rotating neutron star configurations in the angular velocity (Omega) versus baryon number (N) plane, using a two-phase equation of state with a deconfinement transition and treating rotation within general relativity via a perturbation expansion to second order in Omega. They compute the moment of inertia for each configuration and solve the rotational evolution equation dOmega/dt = (K_ext - K_int)/(I + Omega(dI/dOmega)_N) for both isolated pulsars (magnetic dipole spin-down) and accreting stars in LMXBs, incorporating magnetic field decay and mass accretion.

## Key numbers

- The critical line N_crit(Omega) for the model equation of state has endpoints at N_min = 1.49 N_sun and N_max = 1.78 N_sun, with a maximum baryonic mass on the critical line of N_crit(Omega_K) = 1.8 N_sun
- For a star with N_B = 1.8 N_sun, the quark core radius remains approximately unchanged at R_core ~ 7 km across the whole frequency range
- Maximal eccentricities for configurations with N_B = 1.3 N_sun, 1.55 N_sun, and 1.8 N_sun are epsilon(Omega_max) = 0.7603, 0.7655, and 0.7659 respectively
- The braking index signal for deconfinement in isolated pulsars is significant only for periods P < 1.5 ms (Omega > 4 kHz)
- For accreting stars with high accretion rate (dot N = 10^-8 N_sun/yr) and long-lived magnetic field (tau_B = 10^9 yr), the spin frequency plateau occurs between 2.1 and 2.3 kHz; for tau_B = 10^7 yr, the plateau is between 0.4 and 0.5 kHz
- The waiting time peak for population clustering in LMXBs occurs in the frequency interval 225 to 375 Hz for initial magnetic fields in the range 1.0 to 0.6 TG
- Accretion rates for Z sources are dot N ~ 10^-8 N_sun/yr and for Atoll sources dot N ~ 10^-10 N_sun/yr
- Magnetic field decay times considered range from 10^7 to 10^9 yr, with initial magnetic fields from 0.2 to 4.0 TG and remnant field B_infinity = 10^-4 TG

## Context

This work extends the concept of a phase diagram for rotating neutron stars introduced by the authors in prior work (Blaschke et al., 1999) and builds on the braking index signal for deconfinement proposed by Glendenning et al. (1997). The paper enables a search strategy for quark matter in compact stars by performing population statistics among LMXBs exhibiting quasiperiodic brightness oscillations (QPOs), as observed by the Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer and analyzed by van der Klis (2000) and Miller et al. (1998). The results contradict the expectation that deconfinement signals would be easily observable, showing instead that for a realistic equation of state the signal is subtle and requires specific conditions of accretion rate and magnetic field decay time.
