# Jet quenching in a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma

> The jet quenching parameter of an anisotropic plasma depends on the relative orientation between the anisotropic direction, the direction of motion of the parton, and the direction along which the momentum broadening is measured. We calculate the jet quenching parameter of an anisotropic, strongly coupled N=4 plasma by means of its gravity dual. We present the results for arbitrary orientations and arbitrary values of the anisotropy. The anisotropic value can be larger or smaller than the isotropic one, and this depends on whether the comparison is made at equal temperatures or at equal entropy densities. We compare our results to analogous calculations for the real-world quark-gluon plasma and find agreement in some cases and disagreement in others.

## Key claim
The jet quenching parameter of a strongly coupled anisotropic N=4 plasma can be either larger or smaller than the isotropic value, depending on whether the comparison is made at equal temperatures or at equal entropy densities, and on the relative orientation between the anisotropic direction, the parton direction of motion, and the momentum broadening direction.

## Method
The authors used the gauge/gravity duality to compute the jet quenching parameter from the Nambu-Goto action of a string whose endpoints move at the speed of light along a boundary direction, with the string worldsheet descending to the black hole horizon. They numerically evaluated the metric functions of the anisotropic gravity solution from Chernicoff et al. (2012) and integrated the string equations of motion for arbitrary orientations of the parton motion and momentum broadening relative to the anisotropy axis.

## Key numbers
- The isotropic jet quenching parameter is given by \(\hat{q}_{\text{iso}}(T) = \frac{\pi^{3/2} \Gamma(3/4)}{\Gamma(5/4)} \sqrt{\lambda} \, T^3\).
- The entropy density of the anisotropic plasma at large anisotropy scales as \(s = c_{\text{ent}} N_c^2 a^{1/3} T^{8/3}\) with \(c_{\text{ent}} \simeq 3.21\).
- The transition between small and large anisotropy behavior occurs approximately at \(a/T \simeq 3.7\).
- The jet quenching parameter takes the functional form \(\hat{q}(a,T) = T^3 f(a/T)\).

## Context
This work extends the holographic calculation of the jet quenching parameter from the isotropic N=4 plasma (Liu et al., 2006; Liu et al., 2007) to the anisotropic plasma described by the gravity dual of Chernicoff et al. (2012). It generalizes previous anisotropic calculations by Sadeghi et al. and Giataganas (2012) to arbitrary orientations and arbitrary values of the anisotropy. The results enable comparison with real-world quark-gluon plasma jet quenching measurements at RHIC and LHC, finding agreement in some cases and disagreement in others.
