# From eV to EeV: Neutrino Cross Sections Across Energy Scales

> Since its original postulation by Wolfgang Pauli in 1930, the neutrino has played a prominent role in our understanding of nuclear and particle physics. In the intervening 80 years, scientists have detected and measured neutrinos from a variety of sources, both man-made and natural. Underlying all of these observations, and any inferences we may have made from them, is an understanding of how neutrinos interact with matter. Knowledge of neutrino interaction cross sections is an important and necessary ingredient in any neutrino measurement. With the advent of new precision experiments, the demands on our understanding of neutrino interactions is becoming even greater. The purpose of this article is to survey our current knowledge of neutrino cross sections across all known energy scales: from the very lowest energies to the highest that we hope to observe. The article covers a wide range of neutrino interactions including coherent scattering, neutrino capture, inverse beta decay, low energy nuclear interactions, quasi-elastic scattering, resonant pion production, kaon production, deep inelastic scattering and ultra-high energy interactions. Strong emphasis is placed on experimental data whenever such measurements are available.

## Key claim

This review surveys neutrino interaction cross sections across all known energy scales (from eV to EeV), but the authors emphasize that theoretical approximations which work well in one energy regime completely break down elsewhere, and that the delineations between energy regimes are only approximate in nature, meant as a guide for the reader.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review of neutrino cross sections organized by energy regime: threshold-less processes (0-1 MeV), low energy (1-100 MeV), intermediate energy (0.1-20 GeV), high energy (20-500 GeV), and ultra-high energy (0.5 TeV-1 EeV). For each regime, they describe the relevant interaction mechanisms (coherent scattering, neutrino capture, inverse beta decay, quasi-elastic scattering, resonant pion production, deep inelastic scattering, and ultra-high energy interactions) and compile experimental data where available, emphasizing the Standard Model electroweak formalism as the underlying theoretical framework.

## Key numbers

- Fermi constant: \(G_F = 1.1663788(7) \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\), measured to a precision of 0.6 ppm by the MuLan experiment
- For inverse muon decay (\(\nu_l e \rightarrow \nu_e l\)) at high energy: \(\sigma \simeq \frac{2 m_e G_F^2 E_\nu}{\pi} = \frac{G_F^2 s}{\pi}\), with the cross-section growing linearly with energy
- The anti-neutrino cross-section for inverse lepton decay is approximately a factor of 3 lower than the neutrino cross-section due to helicity considerations
- Coherent scattering cross-section scales as the square of the atomic number \(A^2\) when the coherence condition \(QR \ll 1\) is satisfied
- Maximum recoil energy for coherent scattering: \(T_{\rm max} = \frac{E_\nu}{1 + \frac{M_A}{2E_\nu}}\)

## Context

This review extends and systematizes the understanding of neutrino cross sections that has developed since the postulation of the neutrino by Pauli in 1930, covering the full energy range from coherent scattering (first proposed by Freedman, Schramm and Tubbs) to ultra-high energy interactions. The work builds upon foundational experiments including Gargamelle (Hasert 1973), which first observed neutral current interactions, and precision measurements from CHARM II (Vilain 1996), CCFR (Mishra 1990), NuTeV (Formaggio 2001), and others. The review enables future precision neutrino experiments by providing a unified framework for understanding cross sections across energy regimes where different approximations apply.
