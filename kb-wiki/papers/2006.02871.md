# Zero-pairing limit of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov reference states

> The variational Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field theory is the starting point of various (ab initio) many-body methods dedicated to superfluid systems. While taking the zero-pairing limit of HFB equations constitutes a text-book problem when the system is of closed-(sub)shell character, it is typically, although wrongly, thought to be ill-defined whenever the naive filling of single-particle levels corresponds to an open-shell system. The present work demonstrates that the zero-pairing limit of an HFB state is mathematically well-defined, independently of the closed- or open-shell character of the system in the limit. Still, the nature of the limit state strongly depends on the underlying shell structure and on the associated naive filling reached in the zero-pairing limit for the particle number A of interest. All the analytical findings are confirmed and illustrated numerically. While HFB theory has been intensively scrutinized formally and numerically over the last decades, it still uncovers unknown and somewhat unexpected features. From this general perspective, the present analysis demonstrates that HFB theory does not reduce to Hartree-Fock theory even when the pairing field is driven to zero in the HFB Hamiltonian matrix.

## Key claim

The zero-pairing limit of a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) state is mathematically well-defined for any average particle number A, regardless of whether the system is closed-shell or open-shell in that limit; however, the nature of the limit state depends critically on the shell structure, and for open-shell systems the limit state is a finite linear combination of Slater determinants rather than a single Slater determinant.

## Method

The authors analytically investigated the zero-pairing limit by constraining the HFB variational determination using a parameter δ that rescales the pairing field in the HFB Hamiltonian matrix, then taking δ → 0. They performed constrained HFB calculations using a two-nucleon interaction derived from chiral effective field theory, solving the constrained HFB eigenvalue equation while maintaining the average particle number constraint. The analytical results were confirmed through numerical illustrations of the limit state properties, including particle-number variance, pairing energy, and degeneracy of elementary excitations.

## Key numbers

- For closed-shell systems in the zero-pairing limit: the particle-number variance is zero, the pairing energy is zero, and elementary excitations are non-degenerate.
- For open-shell systems in the zero-pairing limit: the particle-number variance is non-zero, with an analytical formula derived that depends on the number of valence nucleons and the degeneracy of the valence shell; the pairing energy remains non-zero; elementary excitations are degenerate.
- The limit state for open-shell systems is only reached for a zero value of the pairing interaction (no BCS collapse), whereas for closed-shell systems it is reached for a finite value of the inter-nucleon interaction.

## Context

This work extends the textbook understanding of the zero-pairing limit of HFB theory, which was previously thought to be ill-defined for open-shell systems. The analysis demonstrates that HFB theory does not reduce to Hartree-Fock theory even when the pairing field is driven to zero in the HFB Hamiltonian matrix, contrary to common assumptions. The findings have direct implications for Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT) calculations: BMBPT calculations of closed-shell nuclei are well-defined in the zero-pairing limit, while those of open-shell nuclei are ill-defined in this limit. This work builds on the foundational HFB formalism of Ring and Schuck (1980) and prior BMBPT developments by Tichai et al. (2018) and Arthuis et al. (2018).
