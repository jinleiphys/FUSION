# New Relations and Constraints on Quark Spin-flavor Contents in Symmetry-breaking Chiral Quark Model

> New relations between the quark spin-flavor contents of the nucleon and axial weak coupling constants are obtained in the chiral quark model with both SU(3) and U(1)-breaking effects. Using the nonsinglet spin combinations, $Δ_3$ and $Δ_8$, all spin-flavor observables are functions of only one parameter $a$ $-$ probability for the chiral pionic fluctuation. The upper and lower bounds of these observables are given. The optimal range of $a$, determined by NMC data $\bar d-\bar u$, gives a constraint to the cutoff of the chiral quark field theory. The model predictions are in good agreement with the existing data in this range of $a$. The roles of kaon, $η$ and $η'$ are also discussed.

# New Relations and Constraints on Quark Spin-flavor Contents in Symmetry-breaking Chiral Quark Model

## Key claim

The chiral quark model with both SU(3) and U(1) breaking effects can describe all nucleon spin-flavor observables as functions of a single parameter *a* (the probability for chiral pionic fluctuation), provided the nonsinglet axial charges Δ₃ and Δ₈ from hyperon beta-decay are used as inputs; the model achieves good agreement with existing data only when the parameter *a* lies in the narrow optimum range 0.144–0.155, which implies strong suppression of kaon and η fluctuations and near-decoupling of the η′.

## Method

The author reformulates the chiral quark model by expressing all quark spin and flavor observables in terms of the nonsinglet axial charges Δ₃ = Δu − Δd and Δ₈ = Δu + Δd − 2Δs, which are precisely known from hyperon beta-decay data. Using the experimentally determined values Δ₃ = 1.2573 ± 0.0028 and Δ₈ = 0.579 ± 0.025, the model reduces to a one-parameter description where the probability *a* for the chiral fluctuation u → dπ⁺ (or d → uπ⁻) is the sole free parameter. The physically allowed range of *a* is further narrowed by requiring consistency with the NMC measurement of the flavor asymmetry \(\bar d - \bar u = 0.147 \pm 0.024\), yielding the optimum range 0.144 < *a* < 0.155.

## Key numbers

- Δ₃ = 1.2573 ± 0.0028 and Δ₈ = 0.579 ± 0.025 from hyperon beta-decay (experimental inputs)
- Δ = (3/5)Δ₃ − Δ₈ = 0.175 ± 0.025, measuring deviation from the naive quark model
- Optimum range of the chiral fluctuation probability: 0.144 < *a* < 0.155, determined from NMC data \(\bar d - \bar u = 0.147 \pm 0.024\)
- Upper bound of strange quark polarization magnitude: |Δs|_max ≈ 0.08
- Predicted quark spin contents in the optimum range: Δu = 0.85–0.86, Δd = −0.41 to −0.40, Δs = −0.07 to −0.06
- SU(3) breaking parameter ε (kaon suppression) in the range 0.39–0.43
- Relative probabilities of chiral fluctuations: π : K(∼η) : η′ ≈ 1 : 0.43 : 0.05 at a = 0.153
- Cutoff parameter Λ constrained to 2.30–2.58 GeV from the optimum *a* range
- Predicted ratio \(\bar u/\bar d \approx 0.65\) (integrated), compared to NA51 measurement \([\bar u(x)/\bar d(x)]_{x=0.18} = 0.51 \pm 0.06\)
- Predicted total antiquark to total quark ratio \(\sum \bar q / \sum q = 0.235\)–0.238, compared to CCFR data 0.245 ± 0.005
- Predicted strange sea fraction f_s = 0.09–0.10, consistent with phenomenological value 0.10 ± 0.06 from GLS sum rule analysis

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model of Eichten, Hinchliffe and Quigg (1988) and the U(1)-breaking formulation of Cheng and Li (1995) by incorporating SU(3) breaking from kaon suppression, previously introduced by Song (1996). It improves upon earlier treatments by using the precisely measured nonsinglet axial charges Δ₃ and Δ₈ from hyperon beta-decay as inputs, eliminating the need for multiple free parameters. The results contradict the SU(3)-symmetric chiral quark model, which predicts Δ₃/Δ₈ = 5/3 and f₃/f₈ = 1/3 inconsistent with data, and show that the η′ fluctuation contributes negligibly (probability ∼0.008) unlike the earlier U(1)-breaking SU(3)-symmetric treatment where η′ contributions were unreasonably large. The constrained cutoff range 2.30–2.58 GeV provides a testable prediction for chiral field theory calculations, and the model enables future comparisons with polarized semi-inclusive data and lattice QCD calculations of the strange quark content.
