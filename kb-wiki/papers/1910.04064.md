# Triangular symmetry in cluster nuclei

> In this contribution, we present evidence for the occurrence of triangular symmetry in cluster nuclei. We discuss the structure of rotational bands for 3-alpha and 3-alpha+1 configurations with triangular D(3h) symmetry by exploiting the double group D'(3h), and study the application to 12C and 13C. The structure of rotational bands can be used as a fingerprint of the underlying geometric configuration of alpha-particles.

# Triangular symmetry in cluster nuclei

## Key claim
The paper presents evidence that the rotational band structures and electromagnetic form factors in 12C and 13C are consistent with an underlying triangular D3h symmetry of the alpha-particle configuration, but the evidence is strongest for the ground-state band while the Hoyle band and excited bands with E' symmetry remain less completely observed.

## Method
The authors classify rotational bands in even-cluster (3-alpha) and odd-cluster (3-alpha+1) nuclei according to irreducible representations of the D3h point group and its double group D'3h, respectively. For 13C, they apply the Cluster Shell Model (CSM) in which an odd neutron moves in the deformed field generated by the three-alpha cluster with triangular symmetry, and they compare the predicted rotational energy formula (including Coriolis and decoupling terms) and longitudinal E2 form factors to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For 13C, the longitudinal E2 form factors for the transitions 1/2-1 → 5/2-1 and 1/2-1 → 3/2-1 are predicted to have identical shapes and identical B(E2;↑) values of 9.6 W.U.
- The ground-state band of 12C shows a rotational sequence with L^P = 0+, 2+, 3-, 4±, 5-, ... all of which have been observed.
- For 13C, the ground-state band has K^P = 1/2- and is assigned to the Ω = E1/2(-) representation of D'3h, which also contains K^P = 5/2+ and 7/2+ bands that appear to have been observed.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing study of alpha-cluster degrees of freedom in light nuclei, building on early work by Wheeler, Hafstad and Teller, and later by Brink and Robson. It contrasts with the axially symmetric two-center shell model description used by Nuhn for 13C bandheads, where in the CSM with triangular D3h symmetry the K^P = 1/2- and 5/2+ bands belong to the same configuration rather than representing separate rotational bands. The results enable a unified description of both even and odd cluster nuclei and predict strong correlations between quadrupole and octupole transitions in 12C and 13C.
