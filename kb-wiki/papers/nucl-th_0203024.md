# Nucleon dynamics in effective field theory of nuclear forces

> Low energy nucleon dynamics in the effective field theory (EFT) of nuclear forces is investigated by using the formalism of the generalized quantum dynamics (GQD). This formalism is based on a generalized dynamical equation derived as the most general equation of motion consistent with the current concepts of quantum theory and allows one to extend quantum dynamics to the case where the fundamental interaction in a quantum system is nonlocal in time. The Schr{ö}dinger equation follows from this equation in the case where the interaction in a system is instantaneous. We show that the effective theory of nuclear forces gives rise to low energy nucleon dynamics which is governed by the generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator. This operator is shown to provide a natural parametrization of the interaction of nucleons and can be derived order by order by using methods of subtractive EFT's. We show that the use of the GQD for describing nucleon dynamics allows one to formulate the EFT of nuclear forces as a completely consistent theory with a well-defined equation of motion which does not require renormalization.

## Key claim

The paper claims that low-energy nucleon dynamics in effective field theory (EFT) of nuclear forces is governed by a generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator, rather than by the Schrödinger equation. This formulation is presented as a way to make the EFT of nuclear forces a completely consistent theory that does not require renormalization, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the proof is carried out only in leading order and partially in next-to-leading order of the EFT expansion.

## Method

The authors use the formalism of generalized quantum dynamics (GQD), which is based on a generalized dynamical equation derived from the Feynman and canonical approaches to quantum theory. They analyze the two-nucleon T-matrix in the EFT of short-range forces developed by van Kolck, first in leading order where only the contact term C0 is kept, and then in next-to-leading order. The T-matrix obtained by summing bubble diagrams in leading order is shown to coincide with the T-matrix of a separable interaction model previously studied within GQD, and is demonstrated to satisfy the generalized dynamical equation rather than the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Context

This work extends the generalized quantum dynamics formalism previously developed by Gainutdinov (1999) to the specific case of low-energy nucleon dynamics in effective field theory. It addresses the well-known problem in EFT approaches to nuclear forces, as discussed by Weinberg and others, that effective Lagrangians yield divergent graphs and singular quantum mechanical potentials requiring renormalization. The paper shows that the leading order contact component of the NN interaction, which cannot be parametrized by a Hamiltonian or satisfy the Lippmann-Schwinger equation after renormalization, can be naturally described by a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator within GQD. This enables a formulation where the dynamics is governed by an integral equation that does not require regularization and renormalization, potentially allowing practical calculations when long-range components of the NN interaction are included.
