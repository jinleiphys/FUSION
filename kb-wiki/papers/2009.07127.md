# Proton fraction in neutron star matter: Dynamical mean-field approach

> Dynamical mean field theory (DMFT) is used to study neutron matter, both with and without admixture of the proton fraction. The system is approximated by the lattice Habbard model. The corresponding equation of state as a function of temperature/density/asymmetry is investigated. The results are compared with the standard mean field (MF) approach where the effect of local correlations is neglected. Whereas the influence of the correlations on the properties of a pure neutron matter is found to be moderate, it becomes strong when the proton admixture is taken into account. In particular, we calculate the proton fraction, energy density and pressure in outer core of neutron stars, taking into account the beta equilibrium condition. The DMFT predicts that the proton fraction is several times the MF based calculations, whereas the DMFT results for energy density and pressure are 30-40\% lower then the corresponding MF estimates. Physical implications of our findings for a neutron star dynamics are discussed.

## Key claim

Dynamical mean-field theory (DMFT) predicts that the proton fraction in beta-equilibrated neutron star matter is several times larger than standard mean-field (MF) estimates, while the DMFT energy density and pressure are 30-40% lower than the corresponding MF results; however, these conclusions are drawn from a lattice Hubbard model that includes only s-wave interactions and is applied at densities up to approximately nuclear saturation, beyond which the description becomes progressively less reliable.

## Method

The authors map continuous neutron/proton matter onto a lattice Hubbard model with local on-site interactions, then solve the resulting impurity problem using a continuous-time interaction-expansion quantum Monte Carlo (CT-INT) algorithm within the DMFT framework, which treats the lattice self-energy as momentum-independent (local). The coupling constants are extracted from experimental nucleon-nucleon phase shifts at a typical relative momentum of (3/4)k_F, and the lattice discretisation is chosen via a Debye-like prescription with an effective spherical Brillouin zone to balance kinetic and correlation approximations. The DMFT self-consistency condition equates the impurity Green's function to the local part of the lattice Green's function, and the procedure is iterated until convergence.

## Key numbers

* Bertsch parameter for unitary Fermi gas: DMFT gives ξ_DMFT = 0.44, compared to the reference lattice result ξ_UFG = 0.37 (difference ~15%).
* Energy gap in unitary limit for pure neutron matter: MF gives ε = 0.86, DMFT gives ε = 0.70 (in units of the Fermi energy E_F).
* DMFT results for realistic neutron matter energy density are close to quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations using the AV4 potential up to densities k_F ≳ 0.5 fm⁻¹.
* Pairing gap of realistic pure neutron matter from DMFT is approximately 1-2 MeV, while MF gives too large values.
* The lowest temperature reachable in the Monte Carlo impurity solver is T = 0.15 E_F due to statistical errors.

## Context

This work extends the application of DMFT, previously used in condensed matter physics (Georges et al., 1996) and for dilute Fermi gases (Barnea, 2015), to neutron star matter with a proton admixture. It contrasts with standard mean-field treatments that neglect local correlations, and it aims to provide a more reliable estimate of the proton fraction needed for the URCA process (minimum ~12%) and the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which are open questions in neutron star physics. The results enable future studies of neutron star cooling and exotic phases such as pasta structures at the crust-core transition.
