# Toward Initial Conditions of Conserved Charges Part I: Spatial Correlations of Quarks and Antiquarks

> In this paper, we study the spatial correlations among quarks and antiquarks produced at mid-rapidity by gluon pair production in the color glass condensate framework. This paper is the first part of a series in which we calculate a complete set of quark/quark, quark/antiquark, and antiquark/antiquark spatial correlation functions in heavy-light ion collisions, with the goal of incorporating their conserved charges into the initial conditions of hydrodynamics. The physical mechanisms captured in this calculation include geometric, entanglement, and interaction-mediated correlations. In this first paper, we construct the building blocks for the correlations arising from single- and double-pair production, studying in detail the single-pair case and the general features of the double-pair case. We find a rich correlation structure in transverse coordinate space, with different mechanisms dominating over different length scales, and we present explicit results for the quark-antiquark correlations in the single-pair production regime. We reserve a detailed discussion of the double-pair production regime for the next paper in this sequence.

## Key claim

The paper calculates spatial correlations among quarks and antiquarks produced at mid-rapidity in heavy-light ion collisions within the color glass condensate (CGC) framework, with the explicit goal of providing initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations that conserve baryon number and other charges; the abstract's claim of "rich correlation structure" is hedged by the fact that this first paper only presents explicit results for quark-antiquark correlations from single-pair production, reserving double-pair production and the full set of correlation functions for subsequent work.

## Method

The authors construct coordinate-space two-particle-inclusive cross sections for quark-antiquark pair production by Fourier-transforming the standard momentum-space amplitudes to transverse coordinate space, using the eikonal approximation for valence quarks from the light projectile nucleus and the CGC effective theory for the heavy target nucleus. They build the correlation functions from single- and double-pair production amplitudes, where single-pair production involves one active nucleon from the light projectile radiating a soft q-qbar pair, and double-pair production involves two active nucleons independently radiating two q-qbar pairs, with the nuclear density profile T_a(b) encoding the combinatoric enhancement from independent nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The net baryon number production at mid-rapidity is suppressed as ~ O(1/s), where s is the center-of-mass energy squared, corresponding to baryon stopping
- The nuclear density profile T_a(b) is normalized such that ∫ d²b T_a(b) = a, where a is the number of nucleons in the light projectile nucleus, implying T_a(b) ~ a^{1/3}
- The regime of interest for heavy-light ion collisions satisfies α_s ≪ α_s² a^{1/3} ≪ 1, meaning density-enhanced corrections from the light ion are more important than genuine quantum loops but not large enough to require simultaneous resummation
- The target nucleus satisfies α_s² A^{1/3} ~ O(1), requiring all-order resummation of density-enhanced corrections

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism for dilute-dense collisions to the "semi-dilute-dense" regime developed by Kovchegov and Wertepny (2012) for asymmetric heavy-light ion collisions, and builds on earlier single-pair production calculations in proton-nucleus collisions by Kovchegov (2006), Gelis (2004), Blaizot (2004), Fujii (2006), and others. The paper explicitly contrasts its approach with Altinoluk et al. (2016), who studied quark-quark correlations via double-pair production but omitted contributions where a radiated gluon first scatters in the target field and later fragments into a q-qbar pair; the present work shows these interference terms generate the dominant contribution to quark-antiquark correlations. The results enable coupling of conserved charge fluctuations (baryon number, flavor, electric charge) to new hydrodynamic codes such as Shen (2017) that can preserve these charges throughout evolution.
