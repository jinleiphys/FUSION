# The domain of soft hadrons in transverse momentum space in high energy collisions

> By studying experimental data for invariant transverse momentum distribution $f(p_{T})$ of hadrons in high energy $pp$, $p$A and AA collisions, we find two characteristic $p_{T}$ points relating to the behavior of $\left[\ln f(p_{T})\right]^{''}$, i.e., the second derivative of the logarithm of hadronic $p_{T}$ spectrum with respect to $p_{T}$. One point is $p_{0}$ at which $\left[\ln f(p_{T})\right]^{''}$ is zero and another point is $p_{1}$ at which $\left[\ln f(p_{T})\right]^{''}$ reaches maximum. \bz{The hadronic distribution can be classified into three kinetic regions, i.e., soft region $0<p_{T}<p_{0}$ dominated by hadrons from soft parton system, hard region $p_{T}>p_{1}$ dominated by hadrons from high energy partonic jet, and the transition region $p_{0}<p_{T}<p_{1}$ of above two sources of hadron production.} Using rich data of hadronic $p_{T}$ spectra at RHIC and LHC, we carry out a systematical analysis for $p_{0}$ and $p_{1}$ of kaon, $K^{*}$, $ϕ$ and baryons such as $p$, $Λ$, $Ξ$, $Ω$ in $pp$, $p$-Pb and heavy-ion collisions, and show their dependence on hadron species, collision energy, collision centrality and/or charged-particle multiplicity. We also study the correlation between $p_{0}$ of hadrons and their average transverse momentum $\left\langle p_{T}\right\rangle $, and find a difference between $p_{0}$-$\left\langle p_{T}\right\rangle $ correlation of baryons and that of mesons which can be understood by quark combination mechanism at hadronization. The systematic comparison for $p_{0}$-$\left\langle p_{T}\right\rangle $ correlation of kaon, $ϕ$, $p$ and $Λ$ in $pp$, $p$-Pb, Pb-Pb and Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies indicates that $p_{0}$ is a sensitive physical quantity of reflecting the production property of soft hadrons in high energy collisions.

## Key claim

The paper identifies two characteristic transverse momentum points, \(p_0\) and \(p_1\), derived from the second derivative of the logarithm of hadronic \(p_T\) spectra, which define the boundaries of soft, transition, and hard hadron production regions; the authors hedge that this classification is based on the behavior of the Levy-Tsallis fit function and its variants, and that the precise values depend on hadron species, collision system, and centrality.

## Method

The authors fit experimental invariant transverse momentum distributions \(f(p_T)\) of identified hadrons (kaon, \(K^*\), \(\phi\), proton, \(\Lambda\), \(\Xi\), \(\Omega\)) in \(pp\), \(p\)-Pb, and AA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies using the Levy-Tsallis function and two modified variants. They compute the second derivative \(\left[\ln f(p_T)\right]''\) from the fitted functions, then numerically solve for \(p_0\) where this derivative equals zero and for \(p_1\) where it reaches its maximum, taking weighted averages over the three fit functions.

## Key numbers

* In peripheral (60-80%) Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, \(p_0 \approx 2.0\) GeV/\(c\) and \(p_1 \approx 3.5\) GeV/\(c\) for protons.
* In central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, \(p_0 \approx 3.5\) GeV/\(c\) and \(p_1 \approx 5.0\) GeV/\(c\) for protons, with transition region width \(p_1-p_0 \approx 1.5\) GeV/\(c\).
* In inelastic \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, both \(p_0\) and \(p_1\) increase with hadron mass, roughly linearly for \(K^*\), \(\phi\), \(p\), \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma^*\), \(\Xi\), \(\Xi^*\), and \(\Omega\).
* In \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, \(p_0\) and \(p_1\) increase with charged-particle multiplicity in the range \(5 \lesssim \langle dN_{ch}/d\eta \rangle_{|\eta|<0.5} \lesssim 25\).
* In \(p\)-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, the linear increase of \(p_0\) with multiplicity holds over a wider range \(5 \lesssim \langle dN_{ch}/d\eta \rangle_{|\eta|<0.5} \lesssim 45\).
* In Au+Au and Pb-Pb collisions, \(p_0\) of hadrons increases with \(\langle N_{part}\rangle\) at each collision energy, with values in central collisions significantly larger than in \(pp\) and \(p\)-Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends phenomenological studies of hadronic \(p_T\) spectra that previously used Boltzmann-like distributions (NA49 Collaboration, 2006, 2007) or blast-wave models (STAR Collaboration, 2003; ALICE Collaboration, 2013) to extract temperatures and flow velocities, and perturbative QCD fragmentation calculations (de Florian et al., 2007; Albino et al., 2008) for high-\(p_T\) hadrons. The proposed \(p_0\) and \(p_1\) points provide a data-driven method to quantitatively separate soft and hard hadron production regions without relying on specific model assumptions. The baryon-meson difference in the \(p_0\)-\(\langle p_T\rangle\) correlation can be understood via the equal-velocity combination model of hadronization (Song et al., 2017; Gou et al., 2017; Li et al., 2021), enabling future systematic studies of soft hadron production mechanisms across collision systems.
