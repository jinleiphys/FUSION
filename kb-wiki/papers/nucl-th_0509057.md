# Auxiliary Field Meson Model at Finite Temperature and Density

> Starting from many quark interactions, we construct a nonlinear sigma-omega model at finite temperature and density. The mesons are introduced as auxiliary fields. Effective quark-meson couplings are strongly related to effective meson masses, since they are derived simultaneously from the original many quark interactions. In this model, even if the effective omega-meson mass decreases due to the partial chiral restoration, the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter can become soft.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when meson fields are introduced as auxiliary fields built from quark and anti-quark degrees of freedom, the effective omega-meson–quark coupling decreases as the effective omega-meson mass decreases, which can soften the nuclear matter equation of state even under conditions of partial chiral restoration, contrary to the expectation that a reduced omega mass alone stiffens the EOS.

## Method

Starting from a general many-quark interaction vertex, the authors introduce sigma and omega mesons as auxiliary fields via an identity inserted into the path integral, then integrate out the quark fields in the mean-field approximation. The resulting effective action yields quark self-energies that are determined by the meson potential, leading to relations between effective meson masses and effective meson–quark couplings at finite temperature and density. The model is applied to uniform, rotationally invariant nuclear matter at zero temperature to compute the binding energy as a function of baryon density.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density \(\rho_{\rm B0}\), the nonlinear model (NLM) gives \(g_{\rm Nv\omega}^*/g_{\rm N\omega} = {m_\omega^*}^2/m_\omega^2 \sim 0.94\), whereas in the linear model (LM) this ratio is exactly 1.
- The effective omega-meson mass squared is proportional to the effective omega–quark coupling when mixing interactions are neglected, so a decrease in \(m_\omega^*\) directly reduces the coupling.

## Context

This work extends the auxiliary-field approach to meson models (see Refs. [3,4,5] in the paper) by explicitly deriving the relation between effective meson masses and effective couplings at finite temperature and density. It directly addresses a tension noted in earlier work (Ref. [1]) where a reduced omega mass was thought to stiffen the EOS, and provides a mechanism (Ref. [2]) by which the coupling can also decrease, softening the EOS. The result enables more consistent treatments of dense nuclear matter in which chiral restoration and the omega-meson mass evolution are coupled to the equation of state.
