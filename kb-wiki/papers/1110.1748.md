# Microscopic Composition of Ion-Ion Interaction Potentials

> We present a new method to calculate the total ion-ion interaction potential in terms of building blocks which we refer to as "single-particle interaction potentials". This allows also to compose the separate contributions from neutrons and protons to the interaction potentials. The method is applied to nuclear collisions via the use of time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a method to decompose the total ion-ion interaction potential into single-particle interaction potentials, which allows separate identification of neutron and proton contributions; this decomposition is valid provided Koopmans' theorem applies and rearrangement corrections remain small, as is the case for nuclear mean-field calculations using Skyrme forces.

## Method

The authors use the density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) approach, where TDHF time-evolution proceeds without restrictions and instantaneous densities are used to perform a static Hartree-Fock minimization holding neutron and proton densities constrained to those instantaneous TDHF densities. The total ion-ion interaction potential is expressed as a sum over single-particle interaction potentials, each defined as half the difference between the single-particle energy (including kinetic, single-particle, and rearrangement terms) at separation R and its asymptotic value. The method is applied to head-on collisions of 16O+16O at Ec.m.=12 MeV, 16O+24O at Ec.m.=12 MeV, and 40Ca+40Ca at Ec.m.=55 MeV using the Skyrme SLy4 effective nuclear interaction.

## Key numbers

- For 16O+16O at Ec.m.=12 MeV: the outer part of the potential barrier is primarily determined by the proton interaction potential, while the neutron potential is essentially zero in that region; inside the barrier, neutrons provide all of the attraction and the proton potential remains positive for all R values.
- For 16O+24O at Ec.m.=12 MeV: the potential minimum is considerably lower than for 16O+16O due to the presence of eight extra neutrons, and the neutron potential starts its dip to negative values at larger R values than the 16O+16O case, bringing the total barrier maximum to a lower value.
- For 40Ca+40Ca at Ec.m.=55 MeV: inside the barrier the proton potential is essentially constant and only rises as the minimum value of R is reached, attributed to stronger Coulomb potential counter-weighting nuclear binding.

## Context

This work extends the density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) method previously introduced by Umar and Oberacker (2006) for calculating ion-ion potentials, which had been successful in describing fusion cross-sections for several systems. The new decomposition into single-particle interaction potentials provides microscopic insight beyond the total potential, revealing bonding and anti-bonding states analogous to molecular physics (Weissbluth, 1978) and clarifying the separate roles of neutrons and protons. The approach enables future studies of how individual single-particle contributions affect fusion barriers and reaction outcomes, and is generally applicable to any fermion system where Koopmans' theorem holds, including electronic systems with self-interaction corrections (Perdew, 1981).
