# Critical evaluation of reference charge radii and applications in mirror nuclei

> I present a critical review of absolute root-mean-square charge radii of stable nuclei from $Z=3$ to $Z=32$, which includes a previously overlooked uncertainty in the combined analysis of muonic x-ray and electron scattering experiments. From these \textit{reference radii} and isotope shift measurements, I obtain those of 12 mirror pairs with a traceable and realistic uncertainty budget. The difference in radii between mirror nuclei is found to be proportional to the isospin asymmetry, confirming recent calculations by Novario \emph{et al}~[PRL~130, 032501]. Assuming that this linear relation holds across the nuclear chart, the fitted proportionality constant, combined with the revised known radii, predicts the radii of 73 previously unknown mirror partners. These are useful e.g., for benchmarking atomic and nuclear theory, calibrating entire chains, and as an input to nuclear beta-decay calculations. The radii of $(T=1,T_z=0)$ nuclei are interpolated assuming negligible isospin symmetry breaking. This completes a model-independent, high-precision extraction of the charge and weak radii of all nuclei involved in the testing of the unitarity of the CKM matrix.

## Key claim

The paper argues that previously compiled reference charge radii for stable nuclei from Z=3 to Z=32 have systematically underestimated uncertainties because they omitted the contribution from the nuclear charge distribution shape (the "v factor"), which becomes the dominant uncertainty for elements from phosphorus onward.

## Method

The author reevaluates absolute root-mean-square charge radii by combining Barrett equivalent radii from muonic x-ray spectroscopy with charge distribution information from elastic electron scattering experiments, introducing a new uncertainty parametrization for the shape factor v = 1 - sqrt(3/5) * V2 based on the maximal momentum transfer achieved in each scattering experiment. For nuclei without reliable scattering data, v factors are interpolated from neighboring elements with generous uncertainties. These reference radii are then combined with isotope shift measurements from optical spectroscopy and atomic theory to obtain radii of mirror nuclei, and a linear fit is performed between the mirror radius difference and the isospin asymmetry I = (N-Z)/A.

## Key numbers

* The mirror shift fit yields Δ_I = 1.382(34) × I fm, with a reduced chi-square of 11.5/11 = 1.04 for 12 mirror pairs
* For ²⁰Ne, the reference radius is r_20 = 3.001(6) fm, with the uncertainty equally from experiment, nuclear polarization, and charge distribution parametrization
* The g-factor measurement in hydrogen-like neon gives δr²_20,22 = -0.3171(24) fm², an improvement by factor 8 over the previous value of -0.310(16)_exp(5)_NP(9)_v fm²
* The v factor increases from 2.2 × 10⁻³ for ¹²C to approximately 9 × 10⁻³ at Z=32, with uncertainties σ_v = (1.7 - q/r) × 10⁻³
* For the A=38 triplet, the constraint on isospin symmetry breaking is |ΔM_B^(1)(38)| ≤ 1.5 fm²
* For the A=26 triplet, ΔM_B^(1)(26) = -3.5(0.7) fm², showing a 5-standard-deviation deviation from theoretical predictions
* The predicted radius of ²¹Na is r_21 = 3.029(7) fm, with uncertainty an order of magnitude smaller than the literature value of 2.97(1)[6] fm
* The mass shift factor for Na is K_D1 = 384.7(6) GHz·u, agreeing with the calculated value of 388(3) GHz·u
* The radii of 73 previously unknown mirror partners are predicted

## Context

This work extends the approach of Novario et al. (PRL 130, 032501, 2023) who predicted a linear relation between mirror radius differences and isospin asymmetry from ab initio calculations, confirming it experimentally for 12 mirror pairs. It corrects the earlier compilations of Angeli (2004) and Angeli and Marinova (2013) by adding previously overlooked uncertainties from charge distribution shape, following the methodology of the ²⁷Al analysis by Al (2021). The results enable benchmarking of atomic many-body theory calculations (e.g., for Na chains as in 2022-NaMg), calibration of entire isotopic chains, and provide input for beta-decay calculations relevant to testing CKM matrix unitarity, particularly for the superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ transitions used in extracting |V_ud|.
