# Impacts of symmetry energy slope on the oscillation frequencies of neutron stars with short-range correlation and admixed dark matter

> Oscillation modes of compact stars, in general, can serve as a fingerprint in determining the equation of state (EOS) of dense matter. In this study, we examine the impact of symmetry energy slope ($L$) on the oscillation frequencies of neutron stars (NSs) with nucleon-nucleon short range correlation (SRC) and admixed dark matter (DM) for the first time within the relativistic mean-field theory. By adjusting the $L$, we revise the EOS and coupling parameters in light of the SRC and DM effects, and construct the new sets. The results reveal that NSs containing SRC and DM inside are more likely to satisfy the observational constraints, and we find that smaller $L$ exhibits larger fundamental non-radial and radial frequencies, and that the effect on Large Separation (LG) is also mainly concentrated in the low-mass region. Moreover, we update the linear relationship between the non-radial frequency and mean density, and we further give empirical relations between non-radial and radial frequencies and tidal deformability at different $L$ for 1.4$M_{\odot}$ and 2$M_{\odot}$. These findings will enable us to more effectively confine the NS EOSs, in turn, also provide a strategy to place constraints on the $L$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron stars containing both nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation (SRC) and admixed dark matter (DM) are more likely to satisfy observational constraints than models with either effect alone, and that a smaller symmetry energy slope \(L\) leads to larger fundamental non-radial and radial oscillation frequencies, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of the relativistic mean-field parameter set GM1 and the assumed DM properties (neutralino mass 200 GeV, Higgs-mediated interaction).

## Method

The authors adopt relativistic mean-field theory with the \(\sigma\omega\rho\) model, incorporating nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation via a \(1/k^{4}\) high-momentum tail in the nucleon momentum distribution (with 25% of nucleons in the high-momentum region and a cutoff \(\lambda = 2.75\)) and admixed fermionic dark matter (neutralino, \(m_\chi = 200\) GeV) interacting with nucleons through Higgs boson exchange. They start from the GM1 parameter set, revise the coupling parameters to include SRC and DM effects, then adjust the isovector coupling \(g_\varrho\) and the \(\Lambda_\omega\) term to vary the symmetry energy slope \(L\) from 50 to 90 MeV while keeping isoscalar saturation properties fixed. Using these EOSs, they solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure and compute non-radial oscillation frequencies in the Cowling approximation and radial oscillation frequencies from the linearized perturbation equations.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy slope \(L\) values studied: 50, 60, 70, 80, and 90 MeV, with the original GM1+SD set having \(L = 94\) MeV.
* At \(1.4 M_\odot\), the neutron star radius varies from 11.5 km (for \(L = 50\) MeV) to 12.5 km (for \(L = 90\) MeV).
* At \(2 M_\odot\), the neutron star radius varies from 11.87 km (for \(L = 50\) MeV) to 12.16 km (for \(L = 90\) MeV).
* Dark matter mass \(m_\chi = 200\) GeV, Higgs boson mass \(M_h = 125\) GeV, Higgs vacuum expectation value \(v = 246\) GeV, Higgs-nucleon formation factor \(f = 0.3\), and DM-Higgs coupling \(y = 0.07\).
* The DM number density is assumed to be about 1000 times lower than the nucleon number density, giving a DM Fermi energy \(k_F^{\mathrm{DM}} \approx 0.033\) GeV at nuclear saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The high-momentum cutoff parameter \(\lambda = 2.75\) and the high-momentum fraction is 25% of nucleons.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star oscillations by incorporating both short-range correlation and dark matter effects simultaneously for the first time, building on prior RMF-based calculations of mass-radius relations and tidal deformability that considered SRC (Hong 2022, Li 2019) or DM separately (Quddus 2020). The study is motivated by the ongoing uncertainty in the symmetry energy slope \(L\), with values ranging from \(L = 64.29 \pm 11.84\) MeV (PhysRevC.95.034324) to \(L = 106 \pm 37\) MeV from the PREX-2 experiment (PhysRevLett.126.172503), and aims to provide empirical relations linking oscillation frequencies to \(L\) and tidal deformability that could be tested by future gravitational wave detectors.
