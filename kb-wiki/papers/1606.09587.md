# Hadronic EDM and New physics beyond standard model

> The nuclear electric dipole moment is a very sensitive probe of CP violation beyond the standard model, and for light nuclei, it can be evaluated accurately using the few-body calculational methods. In this talk, we present the result of the evaluation of the electric dipole moment of 3-body and 4-body systems using the Gaussian expansion method in the ab initio approach and in the cluster model. We also give the future prospects for the discovery of new physics beyond it within the sensitivity of prepared experiments.

# Hadronic EDM and New physics beyond standard model

## Key claim
The nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM) of light nuclei can be accurately evaluated using few-body methods, and if measured at the level of O(10⁻²⁹) e·cm, it can probe new physics at the PeV energy scale, well beyond the reach of LHC, though the actual sensitivity depends on the specific new physics model and its coupling constants.

## Method
The wave functions of the deuteron, ³He, and ³H were calculated ab initio using the Gaussian expansion method with the Av18 potential as the realistic nuclear force and a phenomenological one-pion exchange CP-odd nuclear force. For ⁶Li, ⁹Be, and ¹³C, the wave functions were evaluated in the α-cluster model using the Gaussian expansion method, with the CP-odd α-N potential obtained by folding the bare CP-odd N-N interaction. The Schrödinger equation was solved by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a Gaussian basis with range parameters following a geometric progression.

## Key numbers
- The EDM coefficients of the pion exchange CP-odd nuclear force (aπ⁽ⁱ⁾) in units of 10⁻² e·fm: deuteron aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.45; ³He aπ⁽⁰⁾ = 0.59, aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.08, aπ⁽²⁾ = 1.68; ³H aπ⁽⁰⁾ = -0.59, aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.08, aπ⁽²⁾ = -1.70; ⁶Li aπ⁽¹⁾ = 2.2; ⁹Be aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.4; ¹³C aπ⁽¹⁾ = -0.20.
- The linear coefficients of the nucleon EDM contribution (Kn, Kp): deuteron Kn = 0.914, Kp = 0.914; ³He Kn = 0.88, Kp = -0.04; ³H Kn = -0.05, Kp = 0.88; ⁶Li Kn = 0.86, Kp = 0.86; ⁹Be Kn = 0.75; ¹³C Kn = -0.33.
- If the nuclear EDM is measured at O(10⁻²⁹) e·cm, it can probe new physics with energy scale M_NP ~ PeV for g_NP = O(0.1).
- For supersymmetric models, the nuclear EDM can probe CP phases at O(10⁻²) for M_SUSY ~ TeV at the same sensitivity.
- The 1/2₁⁻ and 1/2₁⁺ states of ¹³C are separated by 3.1 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical evaluation of nuclear EDMs using the Gaussian expansion method (Hiyama 2003) to both ab initio calculations (deuteron, ³He, ³H) and cluster model calculations (⁶Li, ⁹Be, ¹³C). The results are consistent with phenomenological and chiral effective field theory analyses (Liu 2004, Bsaisou 2015). The finding that ⁶Li shows enhanced sensitivity to the isovector CP-odd nuclear force due to constructive interference of the deuteron subsystem and α-N polarization suggests that cluster structure may enhance nuclear EDMs, while the suppression in ¹³C demonstrates that close-lying opposite parity levels do not always guarantee EDM enhancement. The work enables future experimental searches for nuclear EDMs using storage rings, which can probe new physics at the PeV scale.
