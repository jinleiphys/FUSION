# Relativistic disc line: a tool to constrain neutron star equation of state models

> Relativistic iron K$α$ spectral emission line from the inner disc of a neutron star low-mass X-ray binary (LMXB) was first detected in 2007. This discovery opened up new ways to probe strong gravity and dense matter. The past decade has seen detections of such a line from many neutron star LMXBs, and confirmation of this line from the same source with several X-ray satellites. These have firmly established the new field of relativistic disc line from neutron star systems in only ten years. Fitting the shape of such a line with an appropriate general relativistic model provides the accretion disc inner edge radius to the stellar mass ratio. In this review, we briefly discuss how an accurate measurement of this ratio with a future larger area X-ray instrument can be used to constrain neutron star equation of state models.

## Key claim

The relativistic iron Kα line from neutron star LMXBs can be used to constrain neutron star equation of state models, but only if the dimensionless inner disc edge radius (r_in c^2/GM) is measured with an accuracy better than 0.1, which could be achieved with future larger-area X-ray instruments such as eXTP and Athena.

## Method

The author reviews how fitting the shape of a broad relativistic iron Kα spectral emission line (near 6.4-6.97 keV) from the inner accretion disc of a neutron star LMXB with a general relativistic model provides the accretion disc inner edge radius to stellar mass ratio (r_in c^2/GM). For a neutron star, the disc terminates either at the stellar radius R or at the innermost stable circular orbit (ISCO), whichever is larger; both depend on the stellar equation of state, mass, and spin rate. The author demonstrates how numerically computed curves of M versus r_in c^2/GM and Rc^2/GM versus r_in c^2/GM for different EoS models (using a field theoretical chiral sigma model and the Argonne v_18 model) can be compared with observational constraints to rule out EoS models.

## Key numbers

* The first detection of a broad relativistic iron Kα line from a neutron star LMXB (Ser X-1) was reported in 2007 using XMM-Newton data (Bhattacharyya and Strohmayer 2007).
* For the accretion-powered millisecond pulsar SAX J1808.4-3658, assuming a stellar mass of 1.4 M_sun, the inferred inner disc radius was r_in = 18.0_{-5.6}^{+7.6} km, less than the pulsar corotation radius of 31 km (Papitto et al. 2009).
* Cackett et al. (2010) reported r_in c^2/GM = 6-15 for most of 10 neutron star LMXBs, with best-fit values pegged at the lower limit of 6 for many spectra.
* The relativistic nature of the broad iron Kα line from Ser X-1 detected with NuSTAR was 5 sigma significant (Miller et al. 2013).
* Barret (2013) reported a time lag of 15-40 microseconds for 3-8 keV photons relative to 8-30 keV photons for kHz QPO frequencies in 4U 1608-522, consistent with light travel time across a few Schwarzschild radii.
* The precise mass measurement of PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 M_sun) rules out EoS models that cannot support such a high mass (Antoniadis et al. 2013).

## Context

This review extends the relativistic disc line technique, previously established for black hole systems (Fabian et al. 2000; Miller 2007), to neutron star LMXBs following the first detection by Bhattacharyya and Strohmayer (2007). The work builds on numerical methods for computing rapidly spinning neutron star structure (Bhattacharyya et al. 2000, 2001a,b,c; Cook et al. 1994; Datta et al. 1998) and specifically on the procedure detailed by Bhattacharyya (2011) for constraining EoS models using relativistic disc lines. The method enables future missions like eXTP and Athena to measure r_in c^2/GM with sufficient accuracy to distinguish between different EoS models, complementing mass measurements from binary orbital motion (Steeghs and Casares 2002) and radius measurements from thermonuclear burst oscillations (Bhattacharyya et al. 2005).
