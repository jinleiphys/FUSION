# Low energy nuclear scattering and sub-threshold spectra from a multi-channel algebraic scattering theory

> A multi-channel algebraic scattering theory, to find solutions of coupled-channel scattering problems with interactions determined by collective models, has been structured to ensure that the Pauli principle is not violated. Positive (scattering) and negative (sub-threshold) solutions can be found to predict both the compound nucleus sub-threshold spectrum and all resonances due to coupled channel effects that occur on a smooth energy varying background.

## Key claim

The multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) theory can simultaneously predict both the compound nucleus sub-threshold spectrum and all resonances in low-energy nucleon-nucleus scattering, but only when an orthogonalizing pseudo-potential (OPP) approximation is used to prevent violation of the Pauli principle, which had been neglected in all prior collective-model coupled-channel calculations.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space for nucleon-nucleus scattering, using Sturmian-state expansions of the nucleon-nucleus potential matrix to obtain finite-rank separable representations. The Pauli principle is enforced via an orthogonalizing pseudo-potential (OPP) method in the determination of the Sturmians. Resonances are identified by diagonalizing a complex-symmetric matrix derived from the elastic S-matrix and tracking its complex eigenvalues as functions of energy.

## Key numbers

- Neutron scattering from $^{12}$C was studied for nucleon energies up to 6 MeV, coupling to the ground $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$ (4.4389 MeV), and $0^+_2$ (7.64 MeV) states of $^{12}$C.
- For the $p+{}^6$He system, the target structure included the $0^+$ ground state and two $2^+$ states at 1.78 MeV and 5.68 MeV.
- In $n+{}^{12}$C scattering, a prominent $\frac{5}{2}^+$ resonance was found at 2 MeV, and two $\frac{3}{2}^+$ resonances spanning 2.8 - 4.0 MeV were identified with very good widths (narrow and broad respectively).
- The dynamic polarization potential (DPP) for 2.73 MeV neutrons on $^{12}$C was evaluated for three partial waves ($\ell = 0, 1, 2$), and was found to be purely real as the energy is below the first inelastic threshold.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS approach previously reported by Amos et al. (2003) and Canton et al. (2005), correcting a long-standing deficiency in collective model prescriptions for nucleon-nucleus scattering that had violated the Pauli principle in all such calculations prior to those references. The method enables simultaneous fitting of sub-threshold compound nucleus spectra and low-energy scattering cross sections, and also allows construction of non-local, energy-dependent optical potentials from the coupled-channel equations. The results for $p+{}^6$He are new to this paper, and the authors note that a large diffuseness in the base interaction potential was required, consistent with the target having an extended neutron matter distribution.
