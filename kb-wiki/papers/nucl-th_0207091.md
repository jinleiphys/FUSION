# Resonance states below pion-nucleon threshold and their consequences for nuclear systems

> Regular sequences of narrow peaks have been observed in the missing mass spectra in the reactions pp --> p pi^+ X and pd --> ppX_1 below pion-production threshold. They are interpreted in the literature as manifestations of supernarrow light dibaryons, or nucleon resonances, or light pions forming resonance states with the nucleon in its ground state. We discuss how existence of such exotic states would affect properties of nuclear systems. We show that the neutron star structure is drastically changed in all three cases. We find that in the presence of dibaryons or nucleon resonances the maximal possible mass of a neutron star would be smaller than the observational limit. Presence of light pions does not contradict the observed neutron star masses. Light pions allow for the existence of extended nuclear objects of arbitrary size, bound by strong and electromagnetic forces.

# Resonance states below pion-nucleon threshold and their consequences for nuclear systems

## Key claim

If the narrow peaks observed in missing mass spectra below pion-production threshold are interpreted as supernarrow light dibaryons or nucleon resonances, the existence of such states would contradict observed neutron star masses (which must be at least 1.4 solar masses); however, if the peaks are interpreted as light pions with mass around 22 MeV, the existence of such states would not contradict observed neutron star masses and would allow for extended nuclear objects of arbitrary size.

## Method

The authors model the equation of state of nuclear matter using the parameterization of Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (1999), which fits the Urbana-Argonne group's optimal EoS up to four times nuclear saturation density and incorporates the causality limit at higher densities. They introduce hypothetical exotic states (dibaryons with mass 1904 MeV, nucleon resonances with masses 966 and 986 MeV, and light pions with mass 22 MeV) into the EoS and compute the resulting neutron star structure by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations under conditions of charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium. The unknown couplings of exotic states to nucleons are parameterized by a small factor ζ (constrained to be ≤ 0.1 by production cross-section data) and repulsive self-interactions are modeled via scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- Three narrow peaks (width ~5 MeV) observed in missing mass spectra: at M_X = 1004, 1044, and 1094 MeV in pp → pπ⁺ X reactions; at M_{pX₁} = 1904 ± 2, 1926 ± 2, and 1942 ± 2 MeV in pd → ppX₁ reactions, with corresponding M_{X₁} = 966 ± 2, 986 ± 2, and 1003 MeV
- Light pion mass: m_π̃ = 22 MeV (from m_π̃ ≃ 21 ± 2.6 MeV)
- Dibaryon mass: m_{D'} = 1904 MeV, with δm_{D'} = m_{D'} − 2m_N = 28 MeV
- Nucleon resonance masses: m_{N'₁} = 966 MeV (δm = 28 MeV), m_{N'₂} = 986 MeV (δm = 48 MeV)
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Saturation energy: ε₀ = −15.8 MeV; symmetry energy: S₀ = 32 MeV; compressibility modulus: K ≃ 200 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass without exotic states: 2.2 M_⊙
- Observational lower limit on neutron star mass: 1.4 M_⊙
- For dibaryons with f_{D'D'} = 1 fm, maximum NS mass is significantly less than 1.4 M_⊙; even f_{D'D'} ~ 10 fm does not raise maximum mass to 1.4 M_⊙
- For nucleon resonances with f_{N'N'} ≤ 1 fm, maximum NS mass is below 1.4 M_⊙; f_{N'N'} ≥ 2 fm is needed to reach 1.4 M_⊙
- For light pions, maximum NS mass remains above 1.4 M_⊙
- Critical ζ for dibaryon appearance in atomic nuclei: ζ_c ≃ 0.8 for ⁵⁶Fe
- Critical ζ' for N' resonance appearance in atomic nuclei: ζ'_c ≃ 0.9
- Binding energy per particle for π̃-nucleus: ≃ −5.7 MeV (compared to −7 MeV for α particle)

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical analysis of three competing interpretations of narrow peaks observed by Tatischeff et al. (1997) and Filkov et al. (2001) in missing mass spectra below pion-production threshold: supernarrow dibaryons predicted in bag models and chiral soliton models (Kopeliovich, 1995), new nucleon resonances, or bound states of nucleons with light pions of mass ~22 MeV (Wong, 2001). The authors apply methods previously used for hyperon-containing neutron star matter (Glendenning, 1997) and pion condensate systems (Migdal, 1971; Migdal et al., 1990) to assess whether each interpretation is compatible with observed neutron star masses. The work enables experimental discrimination between the three interpretations by showing that dibaryon and nucleon resonance interpretations are ruled out by neutron star observations, while the light pion interpretation remains viable and predicts the possible existence of "nuclei-stars", extended nuclear objects bound by strong and electromagnetic forces.
