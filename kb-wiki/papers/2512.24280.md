# Nuclear structure properties of $^{184-194}$Pb isotopes and isomers

> In the present work, we study nuclear structure properties of the $^{184-194}$Pb isotopes within the framework of the nuclear shell-model. We have performed shell-model calculations using KHH7B and KHHE interactions. We have reported results for energy spectra, electromagnetic properties such as quadrupole moment ($Q$), magnetic moment ($μ$), $B(E2)$, and $B(M1)$ transition strengths, and compared the shell-model results with the available experimental data. The shell-model results for the half-lives and seniority quantum numbers ($v$) are also reported for the isomeric states.

## Key claim
The shell-model calculations using KHH7B and KHHE interactions can reproduce many energy levels and electromagnetic properties of $^{184-194}$Pb isotopes, but the interactions fail to capture the experimentally observed low-lying $0^+_2$ states because proton excitations across the Z=82 shell gap are not included in the model space.

## Method
Shell-model calculations were performed using the KSHELL code with two effective interactions: KHH7B (14 orbitals, 7 proton and 7 neutron) and KHHE (11 orbitals, 5 proton and 6 neutron), both based on a $^{208}$Pb core. For KHH7B, proton orbitals below Z=82 were fully filled and valence neutrons were restricted to orbitals below N=126; for KHHE, all proton orbitals were fully filled and neutron orbitals were restricted to specific partitions. Results for energy spectra, electromagnetic properties (quadrupole moments, magnetic moments, $B(E2)$ and $B(M1)$ transition strengths), half-lives, and seniority quantum numbers were computed and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Energy ratio $R_{4/2}=E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ is approximately 1.5 for all $^{184-194}$Pb isotopes using KHH7B interaction, indicating less collective behavior between spherical and deformed shapes.
- Calculated magnetic moment for the $12^+_1$ isomeric state in $^{192}$Pb is -3.51 $\mu_N$, which is 1.7 times greater than the experimental value of -2.076(24) $\mu_N$.
- Calculated quadrupole moment for the $11^-_1$ isomeric state in $^{194}$Pb is -0.07 eb from KHH7B interaction, while the experimental value is 3.6(4) eb with opposite sign.
- $B(E2;2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ for $^{186}$Pb: experimental 5.6(16) W.u., shell-model 1.0 W.u.
- $B(E2;4^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ for $^{186}$Pb: experimental $5.1\times10^2(12)$ W.u., shell-model 0.3 W.u.
- $B(E2;8^+_1 \rightarrow 6^+_1)$ for $^{188}$Pb: experimental $3.3\times10^2(6)$ W.u., shell-model 0.04 W.u.
- $B(E2;11^-_1 \rightarrow 9^-_1)$ for $^{194}$Pb: experimental $1.70\times10^{-5}$ W.u., shell-model 1.2 W.u.
- $B(E2;8^+_4 \rightarrow 6^+_4)$ for $^{194}$Pb: experimental $5.1\times10^2$ W.u., shell-model 0.1 W.u.

## Context
This work extends previous shell-model studies of Pb isotopes for mass ranges A=196-206 by Shukla and Srivastava (2025) and A=211-215 by Zamick, and complements experimental studies of neutron-deficient Pb isotopes including in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of $^{184}$Pb by Ojala (JUROGAM II/RITU), isomer studies in $^{188}$Pb by Dracoulis, and shape coexistence investigations in $^{190}$Pb by Plaza. The calculations enable comparison with upcoming experimental data for electromagnetic moments and transition strengths, though the inability to reproduce low-lying $0^+$ states highlights the need for future calculations that include proton excitations across the Z=82 shell gap.
