# Alpha-Particle Clustering from Expanding Self-Conjugate Nuclei within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Approach

> The nuclear equation of state is explored with the constrained HFB approach for self conjugate nuclei. It is found that beyond a certain low, more or less universal density, those nuclei spontaneously cluster into A/4 alpha particles with A the nucleon number. The energy at the threshold density increases linearly with the number of alpha particles as does the experimental threshold energy. Taking off the spurious c.o.m. energy of each alpha particle almost gives agreement between theory and experiment. The implications of these results with respect to alpha clustering and the nuclear EOS at low density are discussed.

# Alpha-Particle Clustering from Expanding Self-Conjugate Nuclei within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Approach

## Key claim
Using constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction, self-conjugate nuclei spontaneously cluster into A/4 alpha particles when expanded beyond a critical low density of approximately one-third of the equilibrium density, but the alpha particles form a static lattice rather than a quantum gas due to the mean-field treatment; agreement with experimental threshold energies is achieved only after heuristically removing approximately 7 MeV of spurious center-of-mass energy per alpha particle.

## Method
The authors performed constrained HFB calculations for self-conjugate nuclei from 12C to 40Ca using the Gogny D1S interaction, imposing a spherical constraint on the nuclear radius via a Lagrange multiplier term (H - λr²) to expand the nucleus beyond its ground-state radius. The 3D numerical code allowed spontaneous cluster formation to emerge without pre-imposed cluster configurations, and the resulting density distributions were analyzed for alpha-particle clustering patterns. A heuristic correction of 7 MeV per alpha particle was applied to remove spurious center-of-mass energy, calibrated from a separated two-alpha calculation in 8Be.

## Key numbers
- The critical density for alpha cluster formation is approximately ρ/ρ_eq ~ 1/3 (corresponding to r/r_GS ~ 1.45)
- The theoretical threshold energy increases by approximately 16 MeV per alpha particle at the threshold density
- After removing 7 MeV of spurious center-of-mass energy per alpha particle, the corrected threshold energies show approximately 7.6 MeV increase per alpha particle, matching experimental values
- The theoretical binding energy of an isolated alpha particle in the HFB calculation is -7.5 MeV
- The barrier height between the Fermi gas phase and the alpha cluster phase is approximately 2.5 MeV
- The alpha particles become clearly separated at approximately ρ/ρ_eq ~ 1/5

## Context
This work extends the study of alpha clustering in self-conjugate nuclei from the Hoyle state in 12C (Horiuchi, Kamimura, Uegaki, THSR) and the predicted 4-alpha analogue in 16O (Funaki) to heavier systems up to 40Ca using a fully microscopic mean-field approach. It contrasts with the THSR wave function approach (Tohsaki) which describes alpha condensates as quantum gases, while the HFB approach produces static alpha lattices. The results connect to studies of low-density nuclear matter instability against cluster formation (Ropke, Takemoto) and predictions of a critical temperature for macroscopic alpha condensation (Sogo). The work enables future investigations of cluster formation as a function of neutron excess and comparisons with Relativistic Mean Field approaches (Vretenar).
