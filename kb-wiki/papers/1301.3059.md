# Microscopic study of neutron-rich Dysprosium isotopes

> Microscopic studies in heavy nuclei are very scarce due to large valence spaces involved. This computational problem can be avoided by means of the use of symmetry based models. Ground-state, gamma and beta-bands, and their B(E2) transition strengths in 160-168Dy isotopes, are studied in the framework of the pseudo-SU(3) model which includes the preserving symmetry Q.Q term and the symmetry-breaking Nilsson and pairing terms, systematically parametrized. Additionally, three rotor-like terms are considered whose free parameters, fixed for all members of the chain are used to fine tune the moment of inertia of rotational bands and the band-head of gamma and beta-bands. The model succesfully describes in a systematic way rotational features in these nuclei and allows to extrapolate toward the midshell nucleus 170Dy. The results presented show that it is possible to study full chain of isotopes or isotones in the region with the present model.

# Microscopic study of neutron-rich Dysprosium isotopes

## Key claim
The pseudo-SU(3) model with a systematically parametrized Hamiltonian and only three free parameters (fixed for the entire isotopic chain) can describe ground-state, gamma, and beta bands in 160-168Dy and extrapolate to 170Dy, but the model predicts lower collectivity in 170Dy than in lighter isotopes, contrary to the expectation that it would be the most collective nucleus in the region.

## Method
The authors employed the pseudo-SU(3) shell model with a Hamiltonian containing spherical Nilsson single-particle terms, a quadrupole-quadrupole (Q·Q) interaction, and pairing terms, all parametrized systematically as functions of mass number A. Three rotor-like terms (a K², b J², and c C₃) were added with free parameters determined by a best-fit procedure to experimental data for all five even-even isotopes 160-168Dy simultaneously, using states from Jπ = 0⁺ to 6⁺ in ground, gamma, and beta bands. The Hilbert space included normal parity states with pseudo-spin S̃ = 0 and S̃ = 1 for both protons and neutrons, while nucleons in abnormal parity orbitals were treated as renormalizing the dynamics.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit parameters for the rotor-like terms: a = 20 keV, b = -3.2 keV, c = 0.033 keV, with a root-mean-square deviation of 111 keV for the fitted states (Jπ = 0⁺ to 6⁺)
- In 162Dy, the b J² term (b = -3.2 keV) reduces the Jπ = 2⁺ ground-state band energy from 94 keV (b=0) to 74 keV
- In 164Dy, the Jπ = 8⁺ gamma-band state changes from 1845 keV (b=0) to 1606 keV (b = -3.2 keV)
- The Kπ = 6⁺ isomeric state in 170Dy is predicted at 1048 keV, compared to 1200 keV from Regan et al. (2002)
- Intra-band B(E2) values for ground-state band 0⁺ → 2⁺ transitions range from 591 to 723 (×10⁻² e²b²) across the chain; experimental values (in parentheses) range from 500 to 557 (×10⁻² e²b²)
- Effective charges used: eπ = 2.3 and eν = 1.3 for pseudo-SU(3) calculations; realistic charges eπ = 1.4 and eν = 0.6 used with quasi-SU(3) intruder sector treatment
- Pseudo + quasi-SU(3) B(E2; 0⁺ → 2⁺) values range from 565 to 741 (×10⁻² e²b²), similar to the pseudo-SU(3) results

## Context
This work extends the pseudo-SU(3) model approach previously applied to individual nuclei (Beuschel et al. 2000, Hirsch et al. 2002, Vargas et al. 2004) by using a single set of free parameters for an entire isotopic chain, enabling systematic study and extrapolation to unmeasured nuclei. The results contradict earlier expectations from Bohr and Mottelson (1975), Casteel et al. (1993), and Zhang et al. (2001) that 170Dy would be the most collective nucleus in the region, instead finding a loss of collectivity consistent with the decrease in moment of inertia observed in 162-164Gd isotopes by Jones et al. (2004). The work enables future extensions incorporating the quasi-SU(3) scheme for intruder states, as suggested by Zuker et al. (1995) and Martinez et al. (1997), which would allow realistic effective charges and a complete description of abnormal parity bands.
