# Chiral effects and Joule heating in hot and dense matter

> Initial states of dense matter with nonzero electron chiral imbalance could potentially give rise to strong magnetic fields through chiral plasma instability. Previous work indicated that unless chiral chemical potential is as large as the electron vector chemical potential, the growth of magnetic fields due to the instability is washed out by chirality flipping rate enabled by electron mass. We re-examine this claim in a broader range of parameters and find that at higher temperatures the hierarchy is reversed supporting a growing magnetic field for an initial electron chiral chemical potential much smaller than the electron vector chemical potential. Further, we identify a qualitatively new effect relevant for magnetized hot and dense medium where chiral magnetic effect (CME) sourced by density fluctuation acts as a powerful source of Joule heating. Remarkably, even modest chiral chemical potentials (keV) in such environment can deposit energy densities set by the QCD scale in a relatively short time of the order of a few milliseconds or seconds. We speculate how this mechanism makes CME-driven Joule heating a potentially critical ingredient in the dynamics of turbulent density fluctuation of supernovae and neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The authors find that at higher temperatures, the chiral plasma instability (CPI) growth rate can exceed the chirality-flipping rate even when the initial electron chiral chemical potential is much smaller than the electron vector chemical potential, reversing the hierarchy previously reported for cold degenerate matter where the two were required to be comparable.

## Method

The authors compute the CPI growth rate from the modified Maxwell equations including the chiral magnetic effect (CME) current, and compare it to the chirality-flipping rate from electron mass, evaluated via Boltzmann equations for electron-proton scattering in both degenerate and non-degenerate proton regimes. They consider parameter ranges with electron vector chemical potential \(\mu_e \sim 200\) MeV, temperatures up to \(T=60\) MeV, and chiral chemical potentials \(\mu_5\) from keV to tens of MeV, computing electrical conductivity and chirality flip rates for both \(T \gg \mu_5\) and \(T \ll \mu_5\) limits. Separately, they derive the Joule heating power density from the CME current in a pre-existing strong magnetic field using the steady-state Maxwell equation with finite conductivity.

## Key numbers

* For non-degenerate protons, the ratio of CPI rate to chirality flip rate is \(\Gamma_{\text{CPI}}/\Gamma_m \approx 0.0278 \, \mu_5^2\) MeV\(^{-2}\), so CPI dominates when \(\mu_5 \gtrsim 6\) MeV, allowing \(\mu_5 \ll \mu_e\) (e.g., \(\mu_5 \sim \mathcal{O}(10)\) MeV \(\ll \mu_e \sim \mathcal{O}(100)\) MeV).
* For degenerate protons with \(\mu_5 \gg T\), the CPI rate exceeds the chirality flip rate when \(\mu_5 \gtrsim 0.3\) MeV, again allowing \(\mu_5 \ll \mu_e\).
* The Joule heating power density is \(J_E = (\alpha_{\text{EM}}^2 / 2\pi^2) (\mu_5^2 / \sigma) |\mathbf{B}_0|^2\); for \(\mu_5 \sim \text{keV}\) and magnetic fields of order \(10^{17}\) Gauss, the deposited energy density can reach the QCD scale \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^4\) in a few milliseconds to seconds.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of chiral plasma instability in dense matter from Grabowska (2014) and Kaplan (2016), who found that CPI was ineffective unless \(\mu_5 \sim \mu_e\) in cold degenerate matter. The authors show that at higher temperatures relevant to supernovae and neutron star mergers, CPI can operate with \(\mu_5 \ll \mu_e\). They also identify a qualitatively new effect: CME-driven Joule heating in pre-existing strong magnetic fields, which Sigl (2015) had suggested could sustain keV-scale chiral chemical potentials via density fluctuations. This heating mechanism provides a potentially critical energy dissipation channel for simulations of supernovae and neutron star mergers.
