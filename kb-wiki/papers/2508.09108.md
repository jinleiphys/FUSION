# Quarkonium in a QCD medium with momentum-dependent relaxation time

> In this study, we explore the properties of quarkonia in a hot QCD medium using a newly proposed collision kernel that consistently incorporates the particle's momentum dependence into the relaxation time scale of the medium. The longitudinal component of the gluon self-energy, along with the Debye screening mass, is computed within the one-loop hard thermal loop framework by incorporating non-equilibrium corrections. A modified kinetic theory with an extended relaxation time approximation is employed to model the non-equilibrium dynamics of the QCD medium. The sensitivity of the heavy quarkonia potential to the momentum dependence of the relaxation time is studied. Further, we studied the binding energy and thermal width of quarkonia states within this new kinetic theory. Sizable variations in the temperature behavior of these quantities are observed in comparison with the standard relaxation time approximation method due to the particle momentum dependence on the relaxation timescale of the QCD medium. Our findings highlight that accounting for the microscopic nature of the collision timescale is crucial for understanding the quarkonium behavior in a QCD medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating a momentum-dependent relaxation time into the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA) framework leads to sizable variations in the temperature behavior of quarkonium binding energy and thermal width compared to the standard relaxation time approximation (RTA), but this conclusion is conditional on the specific parameterization of the relaxation time as \(\tau_{\rm R}(X,P) = t_{\rm R}(X) (U\cdot P / T)^\ell\) with the analysis confined to the range \(0 \leq \ell \leq 1\) and \(\ell\) treated as temperature-independent.

## Method

The authors computed the longitudinal component of the gluon self-energy and Debye screening mass within the one-loop hard thermal loop (HTL) framework, incorporating non-equilibrium corrections from a modified kinetic theory using the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA). They modeled the non-equilibrium dynamics of a QCD medium undergoing Bjorken expansion, derived the shear viscosity coefficient, and used the resulting modified screening mass to calculate the real and imaginary parts of the heavy quarkonium potential, from which the binding energy and thermal width of quarkonium states were obtained.

## Key numbers

* The Debye screening mass squared including non-equilibrium corrections is given by \(m_D^2 = \frac{g_s^2 T^2}{6} (N_f + 2N_c) + \frac{4 g_s^2 t_\mathrm{R} T^2 }{3\pi^2 \tau} \left[N_c + N_f(1-2^{-(\ell+1)})\right] \Gamma(\ell+3) \zeta(\ell+2)\), where \(N_f=3\) and \(N_c=3\).
* The shear viscosity is expressed as \(\eta = t_\mathrm{R}\beta^{\ell+1} J_{3+\ell,2}\), with \(\beta = 1/T\) and the thermodynamic integral \(J_{3+\ell,2}\) defined in the text.
* The non-equilibrium correction to the distribution function is \(\delta f_{(1)} = \frac{\beta^{1 + \ell} t_{\rm R} (X)}{\left(U\cdot P\right)^{1-\ell}} P^\mu P^\nu \sigma_{\mu\nu} f_0 \Tilde{f}_0\), where \(\sigma_{\mu\nu}\) is the shear velocity tensor and \(\ell\) characterizes the momentum dependence of the relaxation time.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Karsch, Mehr, and Satz (1987) and Matsui and Satz (1986) on quarkonium suppression in a QCD medium by incorporating non-equilibrium effects through a modified kinetic theory. It builds upon the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA) developed by Dash et al. (2021) to address the limitations of the standard Anderson-Witting relaxation time approximation (RTA) when the relaxation time is momentum-dependent. The results enable more realistic modeling of quarkonium properties in the pre-equilibrium phase of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and can be compared with future studies that allow the parameter \(\ell\) to be temperature-dependent.
