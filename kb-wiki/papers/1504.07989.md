# Thermal photon radiation in high multiplicity p+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> The collective behaviour of hadronic particles has been observed in high multiplicity proton-lead collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), as well as in deuteron-gold collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). In this work we present the first calculation, in the hydrodynamic framework, of thermal photon radiation from such small collision systems. Owing to their compact size, these systems can reach temperatures comparable to those in central nucleus-nucleus collisions. The thermal photons can thus shine over the prompt background, and increase the low $p_T$ direct photon spectrum by a factor of 2-3 in 0-1% p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. This thermal photon enhancement can therefore serve as a clean signature of the existence of a hot quark-gluon plasma during the evolution of these small collision systems, as well as validate hydrodynamic behavior in small systems.

## Key claim

Thermal photons from a hot quark-gluon plasma can increase the low transverse momentum direct photon spectrum by a factor of 2-3 in 0-1% p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but this result depends on using a kinetic freeze-out temperature of 165 MeV and only counting thermal photons emitted above that temperature; including radiation below this threshold would make the enhancement even larger.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the iEBE-VISHNU code package with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions. Thermal photon production rates at leading order in QCD were folded with the dynamically evolving medium, using separate rates for the quark-gluon plasma phase (above 180 MeV) and hadron gas phase (below 180 MeV), with shear viscous corrections included in both phases. Prompt photon backgrounds were calculated using next-to-leading order perturbative QCD scaled by the number of binary collisions, with two different estimates of N_coll used to define the theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Thermal photons increase the low p_T direct photon spectrum by a factor of 2-3 in 0-1% p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, for p_T less than or equal to 2.5 GeV
- The inverse slope of the direct photon spectrum in 0-1% p+Pb collisions is 325 MeV, fitted in the range p_T between 1.0 and 2.0 GeV
- The average number of binary collisions in 0-1% p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV is 15.4 plus or minus 0.03
- The charged hadron multiplicity at midrapidity in 0-1% p+Pb collisions is 57.6 plus or minus 0.3
- The charged hadron elliptic flow v_2{2} in 0-1% p+Pb collisions is 0.056 plus or minus 0.001, integrated from 0.3 to 3.0 GeV
- The charged hadron triangular flow v_3{2} in 0-1% p+Pb collisions is 0.018 plus or minus 0.001, integrated from 0.3 to 3.0 GeV
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio is set to 0.08 for temperatures above 180 MeV
- The hydrodynamic evolution begins at proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm
- The kinetic freeze-out temperature used is 165 MeV

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic calculations of thermal photon production, previously applied to large nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC, to small collision systems where collective behavior has recently been observed. The calculations build on prior photon rate work by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) for the QGP phase and Turbide, Rapp, and Gale (2003) for the hadron gas phase, as well as earlier hydrodynamic modeling by Song and Heinz (2007). The results suggest that direct photon measurements in p+Pb and d+Au collisions can serve as a clean signature for quark-gluon plasma formation in small systems, complementing hadronic observables that cannot easily distinguish initial-state from final-state effects. Future work should consider three-dimensional hydrodynamics, IP-Glasma initial conditions, bulk viscosity, and semi-QGP effects.
