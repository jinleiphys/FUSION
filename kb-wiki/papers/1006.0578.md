# RQM description of PS meson form factors, constraints from space-time translations, and underlying dynamics

> The role of Poincaré covariant space-time translations is investigated in the case of the pseudoscalar-meson charge form factors. It is shown that this role extends beyond the standard energy-momentum conservation, which is accounted for in all relativistic quantum mechanics calculations. It implies constraints that have been largely ignored until now but should be fulfilled to ensure the full Poincaré covariance. The violation of these constraints, which is more or less important depending on the form of relativistic quantum mechanics that is employed, points to the validity of using a single-particle current, which is generally assumed in calculations of form factors. In short, these constraints concern the relation of the momentum transferred to the constituents to the one transferred to the system. How to account for the related constraints, as well as restoring the equivalence of different relativistic quantum mechanics approaches in estimating form factors, is discussed. Some conclusions relative to the underlying dynamics are given in the pion case.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Poincaré covariant space-time translations impose constraints beyond standard energy-momentum conservation that have been largely ignored in relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) calculations of pseudoscalar-meson form factors; these constraints require that the current contain many-particle terms except in the front-form approach with \(q^+=0\), and their implementation can restore the equivalence of different RQM forms for form-factor predictions.

## Method

The authors extend previous work on scalar constituents to pseudoscalar mesons (pion and kaon) treated as quark-antiquark systems with spin-1/2 constituents, using a quadratic mass operator rather than a linear one. They derive constraints from the double commutator of the current with the momentum operator, leading to a relation between the squared momentum transferred to the system (\(q^2\)) and to the constituents (\((p_i-p_f)^2\)), and implement these constraints by introducing a factor \(\alpha\) that multiplies the momentum transfer to account for missed interaction effects. Form factors are calculated in different RQM forms (instant, front, point) both without and with these constraints, and compared to a dispersion-relation approach that automatically satisfies the constraints.

## Key numbers

* The normalization condition for the charge form factor at \(Q^2=0\) is given by \(F_1(0) = \frac{16\pi^2}{N} \int \frac{d\vec{k}}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{(e_{1k}+e_{2k})}{2 e_{1k} e_{2k}} \tilde{\phi}^2(\vec{k}^2) = 1\), where \(e_{ik} = \sqrt{m_i^2 + k^2}\).
* The wave function for the quadratic mass operator is \(\tilde{\phi}(\vec{k}^2) = 1/[(e_{1k}+e_{2k})^2 - M^2]\), while for a linear mass operator it is \(\phi'_0(\vec{k}) = 1/[2\sqrt{e_{1k}e_{2k}}(e_{1k}+e_{2k}-M)]\), leading to different asymptotic behavior at large \(k\).
* The sum of positive-energy and negative-energy contributions to the charge current in the center of mass gives \(\Delta J^+ + \Delta J^- = 2M \frac{16\pi^2}{N} \int \frac{d\vec{k}}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{e_{1k}+e_{2k}}{2 e_{1k} e_{2k} ((e_{1k}+e_{2k})^2 - M^2)^2} = 2M\), which factorizes into the norm times \(2M\).
* For the front-form approach with \(q^+=0\), the constraint equation \(q^2 = \alpha^2 q^2 - 2\alpha (\Delta_i - \Delta_f) q \cdot \xi + (\Delta_i - \Delta_f)^2 \xi^2\) is satisfied with \(\alpha=1\), making the single-particle current assumption valid.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on constraints from Poincaré covariant space-time translations for scalar constituents (Desplanques 2004, Desplanques 2008) to the physically relevant case of pseudoscalar mesons with spin-1/2 quarks. It addresses the long-standing problem that RQM calculations of form factors using the same mass-operator solution give strongly form-dependent results (Amghar 2002, He 2004, Julia-Diaz 2004, He 2005), and that fitting to data yields quite different wave functions depending on the form used. The paper enables a consistent determination of the pseudoscalar-meson mass operator by removing the form dependence, and provides a framework to extract information about underlying dynamics (confinement, one-gluon exchange) from comparison with experimental data on pion and kaon form factors.
