# Jet-medium photons as a probe of parton dynamics

> Photons resulting from jet-medium interactions offer the opportunity of studying the evolving quark distribution in a heavy ion collision. The spectra of jet-medium photons is presented within the JETSCAPE framework for two different energy loss models, MARTINI and CUJET. Jet-medium photons can contribute significantly to the spectrum of direct photons in the intermediate $p_T$ range.

## Key claim

Jet-medium photons can contribute approximately 30% to the total direct photon yield at intermediate transverse momentum, peaking near 7 GeV, but this result is conditional on using the MARTINI energy loss model; the CUJET comparison is currently limited to conversion photons only, as CUJET does not yet include bremsstrahlung photons.

## Method

The authors implemented the CUJET energy loss module into the JETSCAPE framework and simulated Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 ATeV using both CUJET and MARTINI as low-virtuality energy loss modules, with all other parameters held fixed. Hard scattering events were generated with PYTHIA, high-virtuality partons were evolved with MATTER, and the hydrodynamic background was provided by a (2+1)D VISHNU viscous hydrodynamic simulation with temperature-dependent viscosities. Jet-medium photon production was calculated through two mechanisms: jet bremsstrahlung and jet-photon conversion, with the MARTINI module providing both channels while CUJET currently only provides conversion photons.

## Key numbers

- Jet-medium photons contribute approximately 30% to the total direct photon yield at intermediate photon pT, peaking at approximately 7 GeV
- In the 0-5% centrality class, conversion photon yield from MARTINI is nearly 40% larger than that from CUJET
- Including jet-medium photons brings the theoretical curve into much better agreement with data for the intermediate pT range of 5-8 GeV
- The 30-40% centrality curves for conversion photons from CUJET and MARTINI are nearly identical
- Calculations were performed for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 ATeV

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework (JETSCAPE Collaboration, 2019) by implementing CUJET (Xu, 2014) as an additional low-virtuality energy loss module alongside MARTINI (Schenke, 2009), enabling systematic comparison of two different energy loss formalisms. The calculation of jet-medium photons in a realistic dynamical plasma environment enables a new probe of parton energy loss mechanisms, as the difference in conversion photon yield between the two modules (up to 40% in central collisions) motivates using jet-medium photons as clean probes to discriminate between energy loss models. The prompt, pre-equilibrium, and thermal photon contributions were taken from Gale (2021).
