# Exploring skewed parton distributions with two body models on the light front II: covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach

> We explore skewed parton distributions for two-body, light-front wave functions. In order to access all kinematical regimes, we adopt a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach, which makes use of the underlying equation of motion (here the Weinberg equation) and its Green's function. Such an approach allows for the consistent treatment of the non-wave function vertex (but rules out the case of phenomenological wave functions derived from ad hoc potentials). Our investigation centers around checking internal consistency by demonstrating time-reversal invariance and continuity between valence and non-valence regimes. We derive our expressions by assuming the effective qq potential is independent of the mass squared, and verify the sum rule in a non-relativistic approximation in which the potential is energy independent. We consider bare-coupling as well as interacting skewed parton distributions and develop approximations for the Green's function which preserve the general properties of these distributions. Lastly we apply our approach to time-like form factors and find similar expressions for the related generalized distribution amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that skewed parton distributions (SPDs) for two-body light-front models can be computed in all kinematical regimes using a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach, provided the effective quark-quark potential is assumed independent of the mass squared; this assumption sacrifices exactness but allows access to non-valence regions without explicit higher Fock-space components.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a scalar meson composed of two equally massive scalar quarks, perform a light-front projection to obtain the Weinberg equation and its Green's function, and evaluate the covariant triangle diagram for the electromagnetic form factor in the deeply virtual limit. They treat the non-wave function vertex in the Z-graph by crossing the interaction using the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and derive expressions for the SPDs in both valence and non-valence regions, including the effects of interactions at the photon vertex via the T-matrix and its light-front reduction.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken variable for deeply virtual Compton scattering is defined as ζ = Q²/(2P·q), where Q² = -q² is taken large in the deeply virtual limit.
- The plus-momentum fraction X = k⁺/P⁺ is used, with the SPD defined for X in (0,1) for valence contributions and for X in (ζ-1,0) for non-valence contributions.
- The sum rule ∫₀¹ [(2X-ζ)/(2-ζ)] F(X,ζ,t) dX = F(t) is verified in a non-relativistic approximation where the potential is energy independent.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-body light-front model study (Tiburzi and Miller, 2001) by adopting a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach to access all kinematical regimes, building on the method of Einhorn (1976) for handling higher Fock components. It provides a framework for calculating SPDs that maintains time-reversal invariance and continuity between valence and non-valence regimes, enabling future applications to time-like form factors and generalized distribution amplitudes, and complements the spin-1/2 analysis of Choi (2001).
