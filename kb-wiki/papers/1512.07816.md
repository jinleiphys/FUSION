# Unified description of rotational-, $γ$-, and quasiparticle-band structures in neutron-rich mass $\sim$ 110 region

> Band structures of the neutron-rich Mo- and Ru-isotopes around A $\sim $ 110 are investigated using the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach employing multi-quasiparticle configuration space. The mass region under investigation depicts a rich variety of band structures with well developed $γ$- and $γγ$-bands, and quasiparticle excitations based on them. It is demonstrated that TPSM provides a reasonable description of most of the observed properties, in particular, detailed structure variations observed in Mo-isotopes are well reproduced in the present work.

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) with multi-quasiparticle configuration space provides a reasonable description of rotational, gamma, and gamma-gamma bands in neutron-rich Mo and Ru isotopes around A ~ 110, but the agreement is conditional on the triaxial deformation parameter gamma being chosen specifically to reproduce the gamma-band head energy for each nucleus, rather than being determined solely from potential energy surface minima.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial projected shell model calculations using a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian, with basis states constructed from triaxially-deformed Nilsson + BCS quasiparticle vacuum and multi-quasiparticle configurations. For even-even systems, the basis included 0-quasiparticle, two-proton, two-neutron, and four-quasiparticle states; for odd-neutron systems, one- and three-quasiparticle states were used. Three-dimensional angular-momentum projection was applied to all basis states, followed by diagonalization of the shell model Hamiltonian. The axial deformation parameters were taken from measured quadrupole moments or tabulated values, while triaxial deformations were adjusted to reproduce the gamma-band head energy.

## Key numbers

* Axial deformation epsilon = 0.315, gamma = 25 degrees for 102Mo; epsilon = 0.320, gamma = 22 degrees for 104Mo; epsilon = 0.310, gamma = 20 degrees for 106Mo; epsilon = 0.294, gamma = 25 degrees for 108Mo
* Axial deformation epsilon = 0.280, gamma = 29 degrees for 108Ru; epsilon = 0.290, gamma = 28 degrees for 110Ru; epsilon = 0.289, gamma = 25 degrees for 112Ru
* For odd-neutron systems: epsilon = 0.345, gamma = 23 degrees for 103Mo; epsilon = 0.316, gamma = 20 degrees for 105Mo; epsilon = 0.300, gamma = 30 degrees for 107Mo
* For odd-neutron Ru: epsilon = 0.281, gamma = 22 degrees for 109Ru; epsilon = 0.263, gamma = 23 degrees for 111Ru
* In 102Mo, the ground-state band is crossed by the two-neutron aligning state at angular momentum I = 12; bandheads of gamma- and gamma-gamma-bands are at excitation energies of 0.83 MeV and 2.11 MeV, respectively
* Calculated Qt transition probabilities for gamma-band in 102Mo range from 188.82 efm^2 for (4,2)_i to (2,2)_f transition to 321.25 efm^2 for (20,2)_i to (18,2)_f transition
* Experimental Qt for 106Mo gamma-band: 278(+52/-37) efm^2 for (6,2)_i to (4,2)_f transition; 292(+37/-31) efm^2 for (7,2)_i to (5,2)_f transition
* Inter-band transitions from gamma to yrast band are 3-5 times weaker than in-band transitions
* For odd-neutron 105Mo, the ground-state is the projected 1-quasiparticle state with K = 5/2; two possible gamma-bands have K = 9/2 and K = 1/2
* Predicted B(E2) values for 105Mo yrast band: 12.36 W.u. for (9/2,5/2)_i to (5/2,5/2)_f; 153.84 W.u. for (27/2,5/2)_i to (23/2,5/2)_f

## Context

This work extends the multi-quasiparticle TPSM approach previously applied to Er-isotopes and the A ~ 130 region (Bhat 2008, Sheikh 2009) to the neutron-rich mass ~ 110 region, where earlier studies using total routhian surface models (Jin 1995, Xu 2004, Shen 2005) had investigated yrast bands but provided no systematic treatment of gamma-bands. The calculations reproduce the detailed structure variations observed in Mo-isotopes, including the drop in BE2 transitions around I = 8-10 attributed to two-quasineutron alignment, and enable predictions of gamma- and gamma-gamma-band properties in odd-neutron systems where experimental data remain limited.
