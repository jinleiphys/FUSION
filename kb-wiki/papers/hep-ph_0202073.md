# Multiplicative renormalizability and quark propagator

> The renormalized Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator is studied, in Landau gauge, in a novel truncation which preserves multiplicative renormalizability. The renormalization constants are formally eliminated from the integral equations, and the running coupling explicitly enters the kernels of the new equations. To construct a truncation which preserves multiplicative renormalizability, and reproduces the correct leading order perturbative behavior, non-trivial cancellations involving the full quark-gluon vertex are assumed in the quark self-energy loop. A model for the running coupling is introduced, with infrared fixed point in agreement with previous Dyson-Schwinger studies of the gauge sector, and with correct logarithmic tail. Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is investigated, and the generated quark mass is of the order of the extension of the infrared plateau of the coupling, and about three times larger than in the Abelian approximation, which violates multiplicative renormalizability. The generated scale is of the right size for hadronic phenomenology, without requiring an infrared enhancement of the running coupling.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a truncation of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation which preserves multiplicative renormalizability can generate a phenomenologically acceptable dynamical quark mass using a running coupling with an infrared fixed point, without requiring any infrared enhancement of the coupling, contrary to the common assumption in earlier Abelian-approximation studies.

## Method

The author reformulates the renormalized Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator in Landau gauge, eliminating the renormalization constants by using the universality of the strong coupling and the relation between the quark-gluon vertex renormalization constant and the ghost-gluon vertex renormalization constant. A truncation is then constructed by assuming that the combination of the full quark-gluon vertex and the factor \(Z^2/G^2\) in the self-energy integrals reduces to the bare vertex, which preserves multiplicative renormalizability and reproduces the correct leading-order perturbative behavior. A model running coupling with an infrared fixed point and a perturbative logarithmic tail is introduced to decouple the quark equations from the gluon-ghost system.

## Key numbers

* The infrared fixed point of the running coupling is taken as \(\alpha_0 \approx 2.6\) (specifically \(5\pi/6\)), derived from the constraint \(2\pi/3 < \alpha_0 < 4\pi/3\) for SU(3) and lattice results giving the exponent \(\kappa \approx 0.5\).
* The dynamically generated quark mass in the multiplicative-renormalizability-preserving truncation is about three times larger than in the Abelian approximation with the same running coupling.
* The generated mass scale is of the order of the extension of the infrared plateau of the coupling, and is of the right size for hadronic phenomenology.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of multiplicative renormalizability in the gluon and ghost propagator DSEs (Bloch 2001) to the quark sector, and contradicts the common assumption in earlier DSE studies (e.g., Brown 1988, Frank 1996, Maris 1997, Hawes 1994, Jain 1993) that an infrared-enhanced effective coupling is necessary to generate large enough constituent quark masses. The results also address the conundrum found by Ahlig (thesis) where a coupled quark-gluon-ghost DSE study generated a fermion scale a factor two too small for phenomenology. The new truncation enables future self-consistent studies of the coupled quark-gluon-ghost system while respecting multiplicative renormalizability.
