# Efimov Physics in 6Li Atoms

> A new narrow 3-atom loss resonance associated with an Efimov trimer crossing the 3-atom threshold has recently been discovered in a many-body system of ultracold 6Li atoms in the three lowest hyperfine spin states at a magnetic field near 895 G. O'Hara and coworkers have used measurements of the 3-body recombination rate in this region to determine the complex 3-body parameter associated with Efimov physics. Using this parameter as the input, we calculate the universal predictions for the spectrum of Efimov states and for the 3-body recombination rate in the universal region above 600 G where all three scattering lengths are large. We predict an atom-dimer loss resonance at (672 +/- 2) G associated with an Efimov trimer disappearing through an atom-dimer threshold. We also predict an interference minimum in the 3-body recombination rate at (759 +/- 1) G where the 3-spin mixture may be sufficiently stable to allow experimental study of the many-body system.

## Key claim

Using the complex 3-body parameter determined from measurements of the 3-body recombination rate near 895 G by Williams et al. and Jochim and coworkers, the authors calculate universal predictions for the Efimov trimer spectrum and recombination rate in the region above 600 G, predicting an atom-dimer loss resonance at (672 ± 2) G and an interference minimum at (759 ± 1) G where the 3-spin mixture may be stable enough for many-body studies.

## Method

The authors solved coupled Skorniakov–Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equations for the 3-body transition amplitudes, using a complex-valued ultraviolet cutoff Λ exp(iη*/s0) to incorporate the inclusive effects of deep dimers. They applied these equations to the three lowest hyperfine spin states of 6Li, using the three scattering lengths as functions of magnetic field and the complex 3-body parameter (κ* and η*) extracted from experimental data near 895 G. For the case of three equal scattering lengths, they used analytic expressions for the recombination rate derived from the identical-boson case.

## Key numbers

- Atom-dimer loss resonance predicted at (672 ± 2) G, where an Efimov trimer disappears through an atom-dimer threshold.
- Interference minimum in the 3-body recombination rate predicted at (759 ± 1) G.
- Discrete scaling factor λ0 = eπ/s0 ≈ 22.7, with s0 ≈ 1.00624.
- Ratio of binding energies of successive Efimov trimers in the unitary limit is approximately 1/515.
- Numerical constants for the equal-scattering-length recombination formula: C = 29.62(1) and D = 0.6642(2).
- Relation between Efimov parameter and ultraviolet cutoff: κ* = 0.17609(5) Λ, modulo integer powers of λ0.
- In the analytic expression for K3shallow for a > 0, a*0 ≈ 0.32 κ*−1.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for Efimov physics from identical bosons (Kraemer 2006, Knoop 2008) and other bosonic species (Zaccanti 2008, Gross 2009) to fermionic 6Li atoms with three spin states, where the Efimov effect occurs with the same discrete scaling factor as for identical bosons (Efimov 1973). The predictions enable experimental searches for the atom-dimer loss resonance and the interference minimum, and they identify a magnetic field region where the 3-spin mixture may be stable enough to allow experimental study of the many-body system, including possible new superfluid phases (Bedaque 2006, Paananen 2006, He 2006, Zhai 2006, Ma 2009).
