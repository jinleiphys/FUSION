# Dark energy effects on realistic neutron stars

> By considering realistic equations of state (EoSs) to describe the ordinary matter of the stellar crust, in this study, we explore the effect of a dark energy core, made of Chaplygin Dark Fluid (CDF), on neutron stars (NSs). To accomplish this purpose, we solve the stellar structure equations and investigate the impact of the CDF parameters on the several macroscopic properties of NSs such as mass-radius ($M-R$) relation, and tidal deformabilities of a single star and of a binary system, the latter being of great importance when analyzing gravitational-wave signals coming from the merger of such compact objects. We also present an analysis of the radial oscillation modes for the rapid phase transition, with the aim of distinguishing regions consisting of dynamically stable stars from those of unstable ones. Specifically, our outcomes reveal that an increase in the energy density jump (controlled by a parameter $α$) leads to an increase in the radial stability of the NS with a CDF core. Furthermore, our theoretical results are consistent with the observational $M-R$ measurements of millisecond pulsars from NICER data and tidal deformability constraints from the GW170817 event.

## Key claim
The paper claims that neutron stars with a dark energy core made of Chaplygin Dark Fluid (CDF) can produce mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities consistent with observational data from NICER and GW170817, but only when the CDF parameter \(A\) and the density jump parameter \(\alpha\) are chosen within specific ranges that also satisfy the causality condition \(v_s^2 < 1\).

## Method
The authors construct hybrid neutron star models with a CDF core described by the Chaplygin equation of state and a hadronic crust described by either the relativistic mean-field (RMF) BSR8 parametrization or the Skyrme SLy4 parametrization, including the BPS outer crust. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for equilibrium configurations, compute tidal deformabilities via the perturbation function \(y(r)\) with junction conditions at the phase-splitting surface, and analyze radial oscillation modes for rapid phase transitions to determine dynamical stability.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass for SLy4 crust with \(A=0.4\), \(\alpha=1.0\): \(M = 2.134\,M_\odot\) at central density \(\rho_c = 2.236 \times 10^{15}\,\mathrm{g/cm^3}\), radius \(R = 10.853\,\mathrm{km}\), tidal deformability \(\Lambda = 9.015\)
- Maximum mass for BSR8 crust with \(A=0.4\), \(\alpha=1.0\): \(M = 2.227\,M_\odot\) at central density \(\rho_c = 2.035 \times 10^{15}\,\mathrm{g/cm^3}\), radius \(R = 11.480\,\mathrm{km}\), tidal deformability \(\Lambda = 9.443\)
- Causality upper limit for SLy4 crust with \(\alpha=0.8\): \(A \lesssim 0.530\)
- Causality upper limit for BSR8 crust with \(\alpha=0.8\): \(A \lesssim 0.542\)
- Inner energy density at the discontinuous surface: \(\rho_{\mathrm{dis}}^+ = 0.8 \times 10^{15}\,\mathrm{g/cm^3}\)

## Context
This work extends the hybrid star model of Pretel (2024), which used a simple polytropic EoS for the crust, by adopting realistic hadronic EoSs (BSR8 and SLy4) that reproduce nuclear properties and are consistent with neutron star observations. The results are compared with NICER mass-radius constraints from pulsars PSR J0030+0451 (Riley 2019, Miller 2019) and PSR J0740+6620 (Riley 2021, Miller 2021), and with tidal deformability constraints from the GW170817 event. The study enables future investigations of dark energy cores in neutron stars using realistic microphysics and gravitational-wave observations.
