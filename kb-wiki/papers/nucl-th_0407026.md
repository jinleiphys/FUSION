# Momentum distributions in stripping reactions of radioactive projectiles at intermediate energies

> The theory of one-nucleon removal in the stripping reaction (inelastic breakup) on a light target is extended to cover two-dimensional momentum distributions of the reaction residues with the use of realistic profile functions for the core-target and nucleon-target interactions. Examples of the calculated projected parallel- and transverse momentum distributions are given. The transverse momentum distributions, projections on a Cartesian axis perpendicular to the beam direction, show an interesting intermingling of the stripping reaction with elastic scattering of the reaction residue on the target. We also obtain doubly-differential distributions of the cross section on the parallel- and transverse-momentum variables. The distributions depend strongly on the value of the magnetic quantum number $m$. They will be of importance for evaluating acceptance corrections in experiments, and they lead to alignment with the possibility of anisotropic emission of subsequent gamma rays, an interesting spectroscopic tool. Experimental data for proton stripping of $^{8}$B agree with our calculations.

## Key claim

The paper extends the theory of one-nucleon stripping reactions to calculate two-dimensional momentum distributions of the reaction residues using realistic profile functions, showing that transverse momentum distributions are significantly influenced by elastic scattering of the core fragment on the target, while longitudinal distributions remain close to the simpler black-disk approximation.

## Method

The authors used the eikonal approximation with S-matrices for core-target and nucleon-target interactions, derived from optical potentials and the Glauber theory in the optical limit, to calculate differential stripping cross sections. They evaluated the integrals in the expressions for longitudinal and transverse momentum distributions using Gaussian expansions of the S-matrices, and compared results for various projectile-target combinations at intermediate energies (60-103 MeV/nucleon) with different orbital angular momenta and separation energies.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{9}$Be($^{11}$Be,$^{10}$Be$_{gs}$)X at 60 MeV/nucleon, the transverse momentum distribution is lower in intensity than the longitudinal one by about 10%
- The $^{12}$C($^{8}$B,$^{7}$Be$_{gs}$)X reaction at 76 MeV/nucleon involves a proton halo state with binding energy 0.137 MeV
- The $^{9}$Be($^{34}$Ar,$^{33}$Ar($\frac{3}{2}^+$)) reaction at 68.2 MeV/nucleon has a neutron separation energy of 18.42 MeV for the $l=2$ knockout
- The $^{9}$Be($^{15}$C,$^{14}$C$_{gs}$) reaction at 103 MeV/nucleon involves a neutron bound by 1.218 MeV
- The $l=0$ neutron knockout from $^{34}$Ar at 68 MeV/nucleon has a separation energy of 17.06 MeV
- The theoretical uncertainty on the integral single-particle cross section is of the order of 5% for a halo state and 15% for a very deeply bound $l=2$ state with $S_n=22$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Hencken, Bertsch and Esbensen (1996) for knockout reactions, and builds on the calculations of Tostevin (1999, 2001) for single-particle cross sections. The paper provides the first treatment of three-dimensional momentum distributions at the same level of approximation as those cross sections, enabling more accurate acceptance corrections for experiments and opening the possibility of using anisotropic gamma-ray emission as a spectroscopic tool. The results confirm the validity of the black-disk approximation for longitudinal momentum distributions used in earlier work by Hansen (1995, 1996) and Esbensen (1996), while revealing that transverse distributions require the more complete treatment presented here.
