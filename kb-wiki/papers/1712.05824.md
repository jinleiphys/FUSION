# Structure of the exotic $^9$He nucleus from the no-core shell model with continuum

> The exotic $^9$He nucleus, which presents one of the most extreme neutron-to-proton ratios, belongs to the $N=7$ isotonic chain famous for the phenomenon of ground-state parity inversion with decreasing number of protons. Consequently, it would be expected to have an unnatural (positive) parity ground state similar to $^{11}$Be and $^{10}$Li. Despite many experimental and theoretical investigations, its structure remains uncertain. Apart from the fact that it is unbound, other properties including the spin and parity of its ground state and the very existence of additional low-lying resonances are still a matter of debate. In this work we study the properties of $^9$He by analyzing the $n+^8$He continuum in the context of the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) formalism with chiral interactions as the only input. The NCSMC is a state-of-the-art approach for the ab initio description of light nuclei. With its capability to predict properties of bound states, resonances, and scattering states in a unified framework, the method is particularly well suited for the study of unbound nuclei such as $^9$He. Our analysis produces an unbound $^9$He nucleus. Two resonant states are found at the energies of ${\sim}1$ and ${\sim}3.5$ MeV, respectively, above the $n+^8$He breakup threshold. The first state has a spin-parity assignment of $J^π = {1/2}^-$ and can be associated with the ground state of $^9$He, while the second, broader state has a spin-parity of ${3/2}^-$. No resonance is found in the ${1/2}^+$ channel, only a very weak attraction. We find that the $^9$He ground-state resonance has a negative parity and thus breaks the parity-inversion mechanism found in the $^{11}$Be and $^{10}$Li nuclei of the same $N=7$ isotonic chain.

## Key claim

The authors find that the ground-state resonance of \(^9\)He has negative parity (\(J^\pi = 1/2^-\)), breaking the parity-inversion mechanism observed in the \(N=7\) isotones \(^{11}\)Be and \(^{10}\)Li, but they note that their calculations discard three-nucleon forces and that the \(^6\)He + \(2n\) channel (open above 2.14 MeV) is not included, introducing uncertainties especially for the \(3/2^-\) resonance.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) calculations of \(n+^8\)He scattering, using a similarity renormalization group (SRG)-evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential at N\(^4\)LO with cutoff \(\Lambda = 500\) MeV and SRG parameter \(\lambda_{\rm SRG} = 2.4\) fm\(^{-1}\), discarding both induced and initial three-nucleon forces. The NCSMC wave function combines square-integrable NCSM eigenstates of \(^9\)He (up to \(N_{\rm max}=11\) for positive parity, \(N_{\rm max}=10\) for negative parity) with continuous channel states describing \(^8\)He + \(n\) relative motion, including the two lowest eigenstates of \(^8\)He (\(0^+\) and \(2^+\)). The coupled-channel equations were solved by the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, and resonance centroids and widths were extracted from eigenphase shifts.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of \(^4\)He: \(-28.36\) MeV (NCSM, converged to keV precision), compared to experimental \(-28.30\) MeV.
* Ground-state energy of \(^6\)He: \(-28.94(20)\) MeV (extrapolated NCSM), compared to experimental \(-29.27\) MeV.
* Ground-state energy of \(^8\)He: \(-30.23(30)\) MeV (extrapolated NCSM), compared to experimental \(-31.41\) MeV.
* \(^9\)He \(1/2^-\) ground-state resonance: centroid \(E_R = 0.69\) MeV, width \(\Gamma = 0.83\) MeV (NCSMC); with phenomenological adjustment of \(^8\)He \(2^+\) energy to 3.1 MeV, \(E_R = 0.68\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 0.37\) MeV.
* \(^9\)He \(3/2^-\) first excited-state resonance: centroid \(E_R = 4.70\) MeV, width \(\Gamma = 0.74\) MeV (NCSMC); with phenomenological adjustment, \(E_R = 3.72\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 0.95\) MeV.
* No resonance found in the \(^2\)S\(_{1/2}\) channel (corresponding to a \(1/2^+\) state); only a very weak attraction with negative scattering length approaching zero.

## Context

This work extends ab initio NCSMC calculations (Navratil et al., 2013; Phys. Rev. C 87, 034326) to the unbound \(^9\)He nucleus, addressing the long-standing debate over its ground-state parity. The results contradict experiments by Chen et al. (2001) and Al Falou et al. (2007, 2011) that reported a \(1/2^+\) ground state, and instead agree with earlier experiments by Seth et al. (1987) and Bohlen et al. (1988, 1999) finding a \(1/2^-\) ground state. The calculations also disagree with the broad \(1/2^-\) width (\(\Gamma \sim 2\) MeV) reported by Golovkov et al. (2007) and with theoretical variational Monte Carlo (Phys. Rev. C 86, 044330) and continuum shell model (Volya, 2014) results. The work enables future studies including three-nucleon forces via normal-ordering approximations and extension to \(p+^8\)He scattering with charge-exchange channels to access \(T=5/2\) isobaric analog states.
