# Perturbative correction to the adiabatic approximation for $(d,p)$ reactions

> The adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) is widely used by the nuclear community to analyse deuteron stripping ($d$,$p$) experiments. It provides a quick way to take into account an important property of the reaction mechanism: deuteron breakup. In this work we provide a numerical quantification of a perturbative correction to this theory, recently proposed in [R.C. Johnson, J. Phys. G: Nucl. Part. Phys. 41 (2014) 094005] for separable rank-one nucleon-proton potentials. The correction involves an additional, nonlocal, term in the effective deuteron-target ADWA potential in the entrance channel. We test the calculations with perturbative corrections against continuum-discretized coupled channel predictions which treat deuteron breakup exactly.

# Perturbative correction to the adiabatic approximation for $(d,p)$ reactions

## Key claim

The first-order perturbative correction to the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) for deuteron stripping reactions, previously proposed for separable rank-one nucleon-proton potentials, brings ADWA cross sections closer to exact continuum-discretized coupled channel (CDCC) results, but the correction is significant only for non-peripheral reactions and for partial waves with angular momentum less than the product of deuteron momentum and nuclear radius.

## Method

The authors solved the non-homogeneous integral-differential Schrodinger equation for the effective distorted wave using an iterative method, where the correction term involves a Green function evaluated at an energy shifted by the threshold energy $\bar{\epsilon} = 114$ MeV and a nonlocal potential $\delta V_A(R)$ derived from the Yamaguchi rank-1 separable $n$-$p$ interaction. They compared the resulting $(d,p)$ angular distributions to standard ADWA calculations using the Johnson-Tandy potential and to CDCC calculations performed with FRESCO, where the CDCC coupling matrix elements were computed externally using Yamaguchi continuum wave functions and read into FRESCO.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for the Yamaguchi potential is $\bar{\epsilon} = 114$ MeV, calculated using the Hulth\'en wave function.
- For $d+^{10}$Be scattering at $E_d = 40.9$ MeV, the absolute value of the $S$-matrix element $|S_l|$ for $l=0$ changes from 0.2421 (ADWA) to 0.2298 (ADWA with perturbative correction); for $l=7$ it changes from 0.6059 to 0.5594.
- The perturbative correction affects $|u_l|^2$ in their maxima by less than 5% for $l=7$, 2% for $l=8$, and 1% for $l=10$ in the $d+^{10}$Be system at 40.9 MeV.
- The iterative solution for the effective distorted wave converges in less than 10 iterations, with the first iteration achieving more than 95% accuracy, and typical computational time is a couple of seconds when 40 partial waves are included.
- The Yamaguchi phase shifts agree within better than 1.5% with those from a simplified Gaussian $n$-$p$ model for $n$-$p$ relative energies up to 25 MeV.

## Context

This work provides the first numerical assessment of the perturbative correction to ADWA proposed by Johnson (2014) for separable rank-one nucleon-proton potentials. It extends the ADWA formalism, which typically differs from more exact three-body solutions such as CDCC or Faddeev approaches by 10%-20%, but can show larger discrepancies in cases identified by Chazono et al. and earlier studies by Nunes and Deltuva (2011) and Upadhyay et al. (2012). The perturbative method offers a fast correction that could be extended to include nonlocal nucleon-target optical potentials within three-body dynamics, for which no CDCC developments currently exist, and could also treat explicit energy-dependence of optical potentials and induced three-body forces as proposed by Johnson (2014) and Deltuva (2019).
