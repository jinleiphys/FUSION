# Large-scale second RPA calculations with finite-range interactions

> Second RPA (SRPA) calculations of nuclear response are performed and analyzed. Unlike in most other SRPA applications, the ground state, approximated by the Hartree-Fock (HF) ground state, and the residual couplings are described by the same Hamiltonian and no arbitrary truncations are imposed on the model space. Finite-range interactions are used and thus divergence problems are not present. We employ a realistic interaction, derived from the Argonne V18 potenial using the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM), as well as the simple Brink-Boeker interaction. Representative results are discussed, mainly on giant resonances and low-lying collective states. The focus of the present work is not on the comparison with data, but rather on technical and physical aspects of the method. We present how the large-scale eigenvalue problem that SRPA entails can be treated, and demonstrate how the method operates in producing self-energy corrections and fragmentation. The so-called diagonal approximation is conditionally validated. Stability problems are traced back to missing ground-state correlations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that large-scale, self-consistent Second RPA (SRPA) calculations using finite-range interactions are technically feasible without arbitrary truncations, but the method suffers from stability problems at low energies that are traced back to missing ground-state correlations, meaning the approach is not yet fully predictive for low-lying states despite working for giant resonances.

## Method

The authors perform SRPA calculations of nuclear response in closed-shell nuclei using a Hartree-Fock (HF) ground state and the same Hamiltonian for both ground state and residual couplings, employing finite-range interactions (the realistic V_UCOM interaction derived from Argonne V18 via unitary correlation operator method, and the simple Brink-Boeker interaction). They construct the full SRPA matrix in the space of particle-hole (ph) and two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) configurations without arbitrary truncations, solve the large-scale eigenvalue problem using an Arnoldi iteration procedure from the ARPACK package, and examine the diagonal approximation where 2p2h-2p2h couplings are neglected. They also introduce a heuristic renormalized SRPA (RSRPA) to assess the role of ground-state correlations.

## Key numbers

- The SRPA matrix dimension N (sum of ph and 2p2h configurations) reaches up to 10^6 for the cases studied.
- For the 2+ response of ⁴⁰Ca with V_UCOM in reasonably converged spaces, about one million configurations are present, requiring 0.5-1 GB of storage in the diagonal approximation and potentially 100 GB for the full problem.
- The diagonal approximation for A_22 uses unperturbed 2p2h energies given by (e_p1 + e_p2 - e_h1 - e_h2), with a finite constant η2 > 0 introduced to smoothen poles.
- The reduction method solves the non-symmetric N×N problem (A-B)(A+B)R^λ = E_λ^2 R^λ, where eigenvectors R^λ = E_λ^{-1/2}(X^λ + Y^λ).

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent SRPA approach introduced in Papakonstantinou and Roth (2009) by focusing on technical and physical aspects rather than comparison with data, and by using finite-range interactions (V_UCOM and Brink-Boeker) to avoid divergence problems that plague zero-range interactions in second-order RPA methods. The paper addresses the long-standing difficulty of performing self-consistent extended-RPA calculations without arbitrary truncations, which was previously hindered by the lack of effective interactions fitted to beyond-RPA methods and by technical challenges in solving large eigenvalue problems. The results enable future work on including ground-state correlations (e.g., through extended SRPA built on a correlated ground state or self-consistent Green's function methods as in Barbieri and Dickhoff (2003)) and on optimizing the single-particle basis using convergence behavior of SRPA solutions.
