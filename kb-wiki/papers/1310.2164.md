# Transport Equations for Oscillating Neutrinos

> We derive a suite of generalized Boltzmann equations, based on the density-matrix formalism, that incorporates the physics of neutrino oscillations for two- and three-flavor oscillations, matter refraction, and self-refraction. The resulting equations are straightforward extensions of the classical transport equations that nevertheless contain the full physics of quantum oscillation phenomena. In this way, our broadened formalism provides a bridge between the familiar neutrino transport algorithms employed by supernova modelers and the more quantum-heavy approaches frequently employed to illuminate the various neutrino oscillation effects. We also provide the corresponding angular-moment versions of this generalized equation set. Our goal is to make it easier for astrophysicists to address oscillation phenomena in a language with which they are familiar. The equations we derive are simple and practical, and are intended to facilitate progress concerning oscillation phenomena in the context of core-collapse supernova theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives generalized Boltzmann equations based on the density-matrix formalism that incorporate neutrino oscillations, matter refraction, and self-refraction, presented as straightforward extensions of classical transport equations that supernova modelers already use, without requiring wave functions or imaginary quantities.

## Method

The authors start from the Heisenberg-Boltzmann equation for the Wigner phase-space density matrix, then expand the density matrix in SU(2) basis for two flavors and SU(3) basis for three flavors using Dirac and Gell-Mann matrices respectively. They write the transport equations in a "transport basis" that keeps diagonal terms in the flavor basis while placing off-diagonal terms in the SU(N) basis, and also derive the corresponding angular-moment equations (0th and 1st moments) for both two and three flavors.

## Context

This work extends the density-matrix approach to neutrino transport previously developed by Strack and Burrows (2005) and Sigl and Raffelt (1993), providing explicit two- and three-flavor formulations in a form accessible to supernova modelers who use classical Boltzmann transport algorithms such as multi-group flux-limited diffusion and two-moment closures (Zhang 2013, Vlasov 2011). The formalism enables astrophysicists to incorporate neutrino oscillation phenomena including spectral swapping/splitting and collective oscillations into existing supernova simulation codes without adopting the more quantum-heavy approaches common in the oscillation literature.
