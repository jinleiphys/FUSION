# Variational Calculations of the $^{12}C$ Nucleus Structure in a 3$α$ Model Using a Deep Potential with Forbidden States

> The energy spectrum of the $^{12}C$ nucleus with $(J^π, T)=(0^+,0)$ and $(2^+,0)$ is investigated in the framework of the multicluster dynamical model by using a deep $αα$-potential with forbidden states in the S and D waves. A very high sensitivity of the compact ground and first excited $2^+_1$ states energy levels to the description of the two-body forbidden states wave functions has been estabilished. It is shown also that the chosen method of orthogonalizing pseudopotentials yields convergent results for the energies of the excited $(0^+_2,0)$ and $(0^+_3,0)$ states of the $^{12}C$ nucleus with a well developed cluster like structure.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the method of orthogonalizing pseudopotentials (OPP) yields convergent results for the excited $(0^+_2,0)$ and $(0^+_3,0)$ states of $^{12}C$, but does **not** give convergent results for the compact ground $(0^+_1,0)$ and first excited $(2^+_1,0)$ states, because the latter require three-body Pauli forces that the OPP method neglects.

## Method

The author solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for the $^{12}C$ nucleus in a 3α cluster model using the deep Buck-Friedrich-Wheatley (BFW) αα potential, which has three forbidden states in the S and D waves. Forbidden states were eliminated via the OPP method, using a variational expansion of the three-body wave function on a large symmetrized Gaussian basis (80 functions for $0^+$ states, 437 for $2^+$ states). The convergence of the orthogonalization procedure was tested by varying the projecting constant λ and by improving the Gaussian expansion of the two-body forbidden state wave functions across four sets of approximations.

## Key numbers

- Ground state $(0^+_1,0)$ experimental energy: $E_{exp} = -7.27$ MeV (from Ajzenberg-Selove 1988).
- For the excited $(0^+_2,0)$ state, the theoretical energy at large λ ($10^7$–$10^8$ MeV) is $E_{theor}(0^+_2) = 1.551$ MeV; for $(0^+_3,0)$, $E_{theor}(0^+_3) = 4.055$ MeV.
- The theoretical spacing between the $(0^+_2,0)$ and $(0^+_3,0)$ states is $\Delta_{theor} = 2.50$ MeV, in good agreement with the experimental value $\Delta_{exp} = 2.65$ MeV (Ajzenberg-Selove 1988).
- The experimental energy of the astrophysically significant $0^+_2$ resonance is $E_{exp}(0^+_2) = 0.3796$ MeV; the theoretical value overestimates it by about 1.2 MeV.
- The BFW potential parameters: $V_0 = -122.6225$ MeV, $\eta = 0.22$ fm$^{-2}$; Coulomb interaction uses $b = 0.75$ fm$^{-1}$; $\hbar^2/m_\alpha = 10.4469$ MeV fm$^2$.
- Forbidden state energies in the αα subsystem: $E(0^+_1) = -72.6249149$ MeV, $E(0^+_2) = -25.6174$ MeV, $E(2^+) = -21.9991037$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends macroscopic 3α cluster model calculations of $^{12}C$ using the BFW deep potential, following earlier studies that used the Ali-Bodmer repulsive-core potential (Ali and Bodmer 1966) and the BFW potential (Buck, Friedrich, and Wheatley 1977). Prior microscopic calculations using the resonating group method (RGM) and orthogonality condition model (OCM) overbound the ground and $2^+_1$ states by 7–10 MeV (references from the 1970s–1980s), while a later RGM calculation (Csoto 1997) described the low-lying spectrum with good accuracy. The present work shows that the OPP method fails for compact shell-model-like states due to neglected three-body Pauli forces, as also suggested by Oryu (1994), but works for cluster-like resonance states, enabling future study of the $0^+_2$ resonance relevant to the $α + ^8Be \rightarrow ^{12}C + γ$ capture reaction.
