# Freezeout systematics due to the hadron spectrum

> We investigate systematics of the freezeout surface in heavy ion collisions due to the hadron spectrum. The role of suspected resonance states that are yet to be confirmed experimentally in identifying the freezeout surface has been investigated. We have studied two different freezeout schemes - unified freezeout scheme where all hadrons are assumed to freezeout at the same thermal state and a flavor dependent sequential freezeout scheme with different freezeout thermal states for hadrons with or without valence strange quarks. The data of mean hadron yields as well as scaled variance of net proton and net charge distributions have been analysed. We find the freezeout temperature $T$ to drop by $\sim5\%$ while the dimensionless freezeout parameters $μ_B/T$ and $VT^3$ ($μ_B$ and $V$ are the baryon chemical potential and the volume at freezeout respectively) are insensitive to the systematics of the input hadron spectrum. The observed hint of flavor hierarchy in $T$ and $VT^3$ with only confirmed resonances survives the systematics of the hadron spectrum. It is more prominent between $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim10 - 100$ GeV where the maximum hierarchy in $T\sim10\%$ and $VT^3\sim40\%$. However, the uncertainties in the thermal parameters due to the systematics of the hadron spectrum and their decay properties do not allow us to make a quantitative estimate of the flavor hierarchy yet.

## Key claim

The freezeout temperature \(T\) drops by \(\sim 5\%\) when unconfirmed resonance states are added to the hadron spectrum, but the dimensionless parameters \(\mu_B/T\) and \(VT^3\) are insensitive to this systematic change. The observed hint of flavor hierarchy in \(T\) and \(VT^3\) survives the addition of these resonances, but the uncertainties in thermal parameters due to the hadron spectrum and decay properties prevent a quantitative estimate of the hierarchy.

## Method

The authors performed thermal fits within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model using two hadron spectra: PDG-2016 (only confirmed resonances) and PDG-2016+ (including unconfirmed 1- and 2-star baryons and unmarked mesons from PDG 2016). They extracted freezeout parameters \((T, \mu_B/T, VT^3)\) in two schemes: a unified freezeout (1CFO) where all hadrons freeze out at the same thermal state, and a flavor-dependent sequential freezeout (2CFO) with different thermal states for hadrons with and without valence strange quarks. Fits were performed to data on mean mid-rapidity hadron yields and to scaled variances \(\sigma^2/M\) of net proton and net charge distributions from STAR.

## Key numbers

* Freezeout temperature \(T\) drops by \(\sim 5\%\) when moving from PDG-2016 to PDG-2016+ hadron spectrum.
* Maximum flavor hierarchy in \(T\) is \(\sim 10\%\) and in \(VT^3\) is \(\sim 40\%\), most prominent between \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 10 - 100\) GeV.
* The ratio \(\mu_S/\mu_B\) computed from HRG with PDG-2016+ flanks the continuum lattice estimate, correcting the underestimation seen with PDG-2012.
* Freezeout temperatures from PDG-2016 are below 165 MeV (central values), consistent with the lattice QCD crossover region bounded by \(T=163\) MeV (upper) and \(T=145\) MeV (lower) at \(\mu_B=0\).
* For \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 10\) GeV, \(\mu_B/T\) reaches up to \(\sim 4\) with no abrupt change in curvature.

## Context

This work extends earlier thermal model fits by Chatterjee (2013) and others that used PDG-2012, and addresses the influence of missing resonances highlighted by lattice QCD studies (Bazavov 2014, Alba 2017). The finding that dimensionless parameters \(\mu_B/T\) and \(VT^3\) are stable while \(T\) drops confirms and quantifies the systematics suggested by those lattice comparisons. The survival of flavor hierarchy in 2CFO with PDG-2016+ supports the need for flavor-dependent freezeout scenarios, and the results enable future studies using higher moments of net strangeness and correlations to discriminate between hierarchical freezeout and flavor-dependent interactions (Alba 2016).
