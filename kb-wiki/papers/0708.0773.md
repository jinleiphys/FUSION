# S=-1,-2 baryon-baryon interactions in chiral effective field theory

> We have constructed the leading order strangeness S=-1,-2 baryon-baryon potential in a chiral effective field theory approach. The chiral potential consists of one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms. The potential, derived using SU(3) symmetry constraints, contains six independent low-energy coefficients. We have solved a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation and achieved a good description of the available scattering data. Furthermore a correctly bound hypertriton has been obtained.

## Key claim
The leading-order chiral effective field theory potential, constrained by SU(3) flavor symmetry with six independent low-energy coefficients, provides a good description of the available strangeness S=-1,-2 baryon-baryon scattering data and yields a correctly bound hypertriton, but only for cutoff values between 550 and 700 MeV and natural values of the low-energy coefficients.

## Method
The authors constructed the leading-order strangeness S=-1,-2 baryon-baryon potential using chiral effective field theory, consisting of one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms derived from SU(3) flavor symmetry constraints. They solved a regularized coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a cutoff regulator function exp[-(p'^4 + p^4)/Lambda^4] and fitted the five low-energy coefficients determinable from the 35 available low-energy YN scattering data points. The sixth low-energy coefficient, present only in isospin zero S=-2 channels, was varied based on the assumption of moderate attraction in the Lambda-Lambda system with no bound states or near-threshold resonances.

## Key numbers
- The chiral EFT potential contains six independent low-energy coefficients: C^1_S, C^1_T, C^2_S, C^2_T, C^3_S, and C^3_T.
- Cutoff values of Lambda = 550 to 700 MeV were used for the regulator function.
- The fit was performed to 35 low-energy YN scattering data points.
- The sixth LEC in the S=-2 channels was varied in the range of 2.0 to -0.05 times the natural value, for a cutoff of Lambda = 600 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach, previously applied successfully to the nucleon-nucleon interaction by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum et al. (2005), to the strangeness S=-1,-2 baryon-baryon channels for the first time. The results are compared to the Julich '04 meson-exchange model by Haidenbauer et al. (2005) and the Nijmegen NSC97f meson-exchange model by Rijken et al. (1999). This enables future next-to-leading order calculations combining NN and YN interactions, with work in that direction reported as in progress.
