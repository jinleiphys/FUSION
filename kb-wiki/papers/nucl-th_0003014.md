# Observing Quark-Gluon Plasma with Strange Hadrons

> We review the methods and results obtained in an analysis of the experimental heavy ion collision research program at nuclear beam energy of 160-200A GeV. We study strange, and more generally, hadronic particle production experimental data. We discuss present expectations concerning how these observables will perform at other collision energies. We also present the dynamical theory of strangeness production and apply it to show that it agrees with available experimental results. We describe strange hadron production from the baryon-poor quark-gluon phase formed at much higher reaction energies, where the abundance of strange baryons and antibaryons exceeds that of nonstrange baryons and antibaryons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the collective evidence from strange hadron production at SPS energies (158-200A GeV) is consistent with quark-gluon plasma formation, but the analysis requires introducing chemical non-equilibrium parameters (light quark occupancy gamma_q and strange quark occupancy gamma_s) to achieve precise agreement with data, yielding a freeze-out temperature of 143 +/- 5 MeV that is about 30 MeV lower than values obtained from simpler thermal models.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical model analysis (Fermi-2000 model) of hadronic particle yields from SPS heavy ion collisions, using six parameters: freeze-out temperature T_f, strange quark fugacity lambda_s, light quark fugacity lambda_q, strange quark phase space occupancy gamma_s, light quark phase space occupancy gamma_q, and a collective flow velocity parameter. They applied this framework to data from the NA49 and WA97 experiments for Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV and S-induced reactions at 200A GeV, allowing for chemical non-equilibrium in both light and strange quark sectors.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 143 +/- 5 MeV for strange hadron formation in Pb-Pb collisions at SPS
- Nuclear beam energy range studied: 160-200A GeV
- Strangeness enhancement factor of 1.5-3 at SPS conditions, depending on baseline and rapidity region
- Onset of strangeness enhancement occurs when participating baryon number exceeds B = 40-50
- Initial temperature range for QGP formation consistent with data: 260 < T_ch < 320 MeV
- Energy density in SPS collisions exceeds normal nuclear matter by more than a factor of 20
- Early universe QGP temperature: T = 150 MeV approximately 1.7 x 10^12 K, surviving for about 30 microseconds

## Context

This review extends the statistical hadronization approach originally developed by Fermi (1950) and Hagedorn, applying it to the SPS heavy ion program at CERN. The work builds on earlier strangeness signatures for QGP proposed by Rafelski and collaborators (1982, 1986, 1991) and incorporates recent experimental results from WA97, NA49, NA35, WA85, and NA44 collaborations. The analysis enables predictions for strange baryon and antibaryon production at RHIC energies, where the authors predict that most baryons and antibaryons produced will be strange (hyperon dominance), which they consider a unique signature of sudden QGP hadronization.
