# Neutron star interiors and the equation of state of ultra-dense matter

> There has been much recent progress in our understanding of quark matter, culminating in the discovery that if such matter exists in the cores of neutron stars it ought to be in a color superconducting state. This paper explores the impact of superconducting quark matter on the properties (e.g., masses, radii, surface gravity, photon emission) of compact stars.

## Key claim

If quark matter exists in neutron star cores, it should be in a color superconducting state, and this paper explores the impact of such matter on compact star properties, showing that color-flavor locked (CFL) quark stars may have surface redshifts and surface gravities up to 50% higher than conventional neutron stars.

## Method

The study compares three sample models for the nuclear equation of state (EOS): the HV model treating core matter as hadronic particles (nucleons and hyperons) in chemical equilibrium with leptons; the G300 model additionally accounting for non-superconducting quark matter; and a third model accounting for quark matter in the superconducting color-flavor locked (CFL) phase. The authors compute mass-central energy density relations, frame dragging effects, and stellar properties for both non-rotating stars and stars rotating at their respective Kepler frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Central energy density for non-rotating stars: 361.0 MeV/fm^3 (HV), 814.3 MeV/fm^3 (G300), 2300.0 MeV/fm^3 (CFL)
- Mass for non-rotating stars: 1.39 solar masses (HV), 1.40 solar masses (G300), 1.36 solar masses (CFL)
- Radius for non-rotating stars: 14.1 km (HV), 12.2 km (G300), 9.0 km (CFL)
- Surface gravitational redshift for non-rotating stars: 0.1889 (HV), 0.2322 (G300), 0.3356 (CFL)
- Surface gravity for non-rotating stars: 1.1086 x 10^14 cm/s^2 (HV), 1.5447 x 10^14 cm/s^2 (G300), 3.0146 x 10^14 cm/s^2 (CFL)
- Kepler frequencies: 850 Hz (HV), 940 Hz (G300), 1400 Hz (CFL)
- Rotational velocity at equator for stars at Kepler frequency: 0.336 c (HV), 0.353 c (G300), 0.424 c (CFL)

## Context

This work extends the line of research on compact star interiors reviewed by Glen (1997) and Weber (1999), incorporating the theoretical discovery by Rajagopal (2001) and Alford (2001) that quark matter in neutron star cores should be a color superconductor. The study enables future investigations of binary millisecond neutron stars in their final accretion stages, where the enhanced frame dragging effect of CFL stars may be important when the accretion disk is closest to the neutron star.
