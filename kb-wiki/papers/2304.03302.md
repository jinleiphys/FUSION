# TMD Handbook

> This handbook provides a comprehensive review of transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions, commonly referred to as transverse momentum distributions (TMDs). TMDs describe the distribution of partons inside the proton and other hadrons with respect to both their longitudinal and transverse momenta. They provide unique insight into the internal momentum and spin structure of hadrons, and are a key ingredient in the description of many collider physics cross sections. Understanding TMDs requires a combination of theoretical techniques from quantum field theory, nonperturbative calculations using lattice QCD, and phenomenological analysis of experimental data. The handbook covers a wide range of topics, from theoretical foundations to experimental analyses, as well as recent developments and future directions. It is intended to provide an essential reference for researchers and graduate students interested in understanding the structure of hadrons and the dynamics of partons in high energy collisions.

## Key claim

This handbook provides a comprehensive review of transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions (TMDs), covering theoretical foundations, nonperturbative lattice QCD calculations, and phenomenological analyses, but it is a review intended as a reference rather than presenting a single new result or measurement.

## Method

The authors compiled a multi-chapter review synthesizing the theoretical definition of TMDs in quantum field theory and soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), their evolution equations (Collins-Soper-Sterman and rapidity renormalization group), nonperturbative extraction methods including lattice QCD and large-momentum effective theory (LaMET), and phenomenological extractions from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS), Drell-Yan (DY), and e+e- annihilation data. The handbook also covers TMD fragmentation functions, gluon TMDs, subleading-power corrections, and applications to jet substructure and heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The cusp anomalous dimension for quark TMD PDFs at one loop is \(\gamma_K^{(1)} = 2 C_F = 8/3\), with \(C_F = 4/3\).
- The non-cusp anomalous dimension for quark TMD PDFs at one loop is \(\gamma_q^{(1)} = (3/2) C_F = 2\).
- The two-loop cusp anomalous dimension coefficient for quarks is \(\gamma_K^{(2)} = C_F \left[ C_A (67/18 - \pi^2/6) - (10/9) T_F n_f \right]\), with \(C_A = 3\), \(T_F = 1/2\), and \(n_f\) the number of active flavors.
- The Fourier transform convention for TMD PDFs uses \(\tilde f(\mathbf{b}_T) = \int d^2\mathbf{p}_T \, e^{-i \mathbf{b}_T \cdot \mathbf{p}_T} f(\mathbf{p}_T)\), while for TMD FFs the phase sign is reversed: \(\tilde D(\mathbf{b}_T) = \int d^2\mathbf{k}_T \, e^{+i \mathbf{b}_T \cdot \mathbf{k}_T} D(\mathbf{k}_T)\).

## Context

This handbook extends and systematizes the theoretical framework developed in earlier works such as Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1985) for TMD factorization, the CSS evolution formalism, and the SCET-based TMD formulation by Echevarria, Idilbi, and Scimemi (2012) and by Becher and Neubert (2011). It also incorporates lattice QCD approaches from Musch, Hagler, Engelhardt, Negele, and Schafer (2011) and the Ji-Ma-Yuan (2005) scheme. The handbook enables future precision extractions of TMDs from upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) data and provides a unified reference for comparing different theoretical and phenomenological approaches.
