# Thermal Width of the $Υ$ at Large t' Hooft Coupling

> We use the AdS/CFT correspondence to show that the heavy quark (static) potential in a strongly-coupled plasma develops an imaginary part at finite temperature. Thus, deeply bound heavy quarkonia states acquire a small nonzero thermal width when the t'Hooft coupling $λ=g^2 N_c \gg 1$ and the number of colors $N_c \to \infty$. In the dual gravity description, this imaginary contribution comes from thermal fluctuations around the bottom of the classical sagging string in the bulk that connects the heavy quarks located at the boundary. We predict a strong suppression of $Υ$'s in heavy-ion collisions and discuss how this may be used to estimate the initial temperature.

# Thermal Width of the $\Upsilon$ at Large t' Hooft Coupling

## Key claim
The heavy quark (static) potential in a strongly-coupled plasma develops an imaginary part at finite temperature due to thermal fluctuations around the classical string configuration in the dual gravity description, but only when the bottom of the string is sufficiently close to the black brane horizon (specifically when $\zeta > 3^{-1/4} \approx 0.76$); otherwise the imaginary part vanishes.

## Method
The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the expectation value of a rectangular Wilson loop in strongly-coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ Supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory at finite temperature, which is dual to a classical Nambu-Goto string in an AdS$_5$ background with a black brane horizon. They analyze thermal fluctuations $\delta U(x)$ around the classical U-shaped string profile $U_c(x)$ connecting the heavy quark sources at the boundary, and evaluate the path integral in the saddle-point approximation near the bottom of the string where the argument of the Nambu-Goto square root can become negative, generating an imaginary contribution to the action.

## Key numbers
- The imaginary part of the heavy quark potential for $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM at large $\lambda$ is $\mathrm{Im}~\mathcal{V}_{Q\bar{Q}} = -\frac{\pi}{24\sqrt{2}}\,\sqrt{\lambda}\,T \, \frac{3\zeta^4-1}{\zeta}$, where $\zeta \equiv U_h/U_* < 1$ and the expression applies only when $\zeta > 3^{-1/4} \approx 0.76$.
- The thermal width of the $\Upsilon$ ground state is $\Gamma_\Upsilon \simeq 48$ MeV for $m_Q = 4.7$ GeV, $T = 0.3$ GeV, and $\sqrt{\lambda} = 3$.
- The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for $\Upsilon \to \ell^+ \ell^-$ is approximately $R_{AA} \simeq 0.3$ for a plasma lifetime of $t = 5$ fm/c and $\bar\Gamma_\Upsilon = 48$ MeV.
- The relation between separation $L$ and temperature at small $LT$ is $LT = b\,\zeta$ with $b = 2\Gamma(3/4)/\sqrt{\pi}\,\Gamma(1/4) \approx 0.38$.

## Context
This work extends the AdS/CFT calculation of the heavy quark potential in strongly-coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM, originally computed by Maldacena (1998) and Rey (1998), to include an imaginary part arising from thermal fluctuations. The imaginary part is analogous to that found in perturbative QCD at order $\sim g^4$ due to Landau damping of static gluons (ImV_HTL), but appears at the same order in $\sqrt{\lambda}$ as the vacuum potential rather than suppressed by powers of the coupling. The result enables experimental estimates of the initial temperature in heavy-ion collisions via $\Upsilon$ suppression measurements at RHIC and LHC, and complements earlier work on quarkonium breakup via tunneling into heavy-light bound states by KMcLS and Cotrone (2005).
