# Fixed point behavior of cumulants in the three-dimensional Ising universality class

> High-order cumulants and factorial cumulants of conserved charges are suggested to study the critical dynamics in heavy-ion collision experiments. In this paper, using the parametric representation of the three-dimensional Ising model which is believed to belong to the same universality class with the Quantum chromo-dynamics, temperature dependence of the second- to fourth-order (factorial) cumulants of the order parameter is studied. It is found that the values of the normalized cumulants are independent of the external magnetic field at the critical temperature, which results in a fixed point in the temperature dependence of the normalized cumulants. In finite-size systems simulated by Monte Carlo method, the fixed point behavior still exists at the temperature near the critical one. The fixed point behavior is also appeared in the temperature dependence of normalized factorial cumulants at least from the fourth-order one. With a mapping from the Ising model to QCD, the fixed point behavior is also found in the energy dependence of the normalized cumulants (or fourth-order factorial cumulants) along different freeze-out curves.

### Key claim
The paper claims that normalized second- to fourth-order cumulants of the order parameter in the three-dimensional Ising model are independent of the external magnetic field at the critical temperature, creating a fixed point in their temperature dependence; this fixed point behavior persists in finite-size systems near the critical temperature and, after a linear mapping from the Ising model to QCD, appears in the energy dependence of normalized cumulants along different freeze-out curves.

### Method
The authors used the parametric representation of the three-dimensional Ising model, with critical exponents approximated as beta = 1/3 and delta = 5, to derive analytic expressions for second- to fourth-order cumulants and factorial cumulants of the order parameter. They then performed Monte Carlo simulations using the Wolff cluster algorithm on cubic lattices with helical boundary conditions, generating 48 million independent configurations per parameter set and applying Ferrenberg-Swendsen reweighting to calculate observables at intermediate temperatures. Finally, they applied a linear mapping from the Ising variables (reduced temperature t, external magnetic field H) to QCD variables (temperature T, baryon chemical potential mu_B) to convert the temperature dependence into energy dependence along assumed freeze-out curves.

### Key numbers
*   Normalized second-order cumulant at the critical temperature: kappa_2^Norm(t=0) approximately 0.58, independent of external magnetic field H.
*   Normalized third-order cumulant at the critical temperature: kappa_3^Norm(t=0) approximately -0.51, independent of external magnetic field H.
*   Normalized fourth-order cumulant at the critical temperature: kappa_4^Norm(t=0) approximately 0.49, independent of external magnetic field H.
*   In Monte Carlo simulations, the fixed point for normalized cumulants occurs at a temperature about one percent lower than the critical temperature T_c/J approximately 4.51, with lattice sizes L=14, 12, and 10 for H=0.05, 0.07, and 0.1 respectively.
*   For the QCD mapping, the critical energy is sqrt(s)_c = 15.5 GeV, corresponding to mu_Bc = 0.25 GeV, with the width of the critical regime set as Delta mu_B = 0.1 GeV, Delta H = 0.4, and Delta t = 2.

### Context
This work extends studies of critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the use of high-order cumulants and factorial cumulants of conserved charges to search for the QCD critical point, as suggested by Stephanov (2009) and others. It builds on the parametric representation of the three-dimensional Ising model equation of state (e.g., linear parametric model) and the linear mapping from Ising variables to QCD variables (e.g., linearmap1, linearmap2, linearmap3). The fixed point behavior identified here provides a potential signature for locating the QCD critical point in experimental data, complementing other proposed signatures such as the sign change of the fourth-order cumulant (Stephanov 2011) or peak structures (Eur. Phys. J. C.79.245).
