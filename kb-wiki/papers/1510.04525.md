# Bohr Hamiltonian with Hulth?en plus Ring shaped potential for triaxial nuclei

> In this paper, we solve the eigenvalues and eigenvectors problem with Bohr collective Hamil- tonian for triaxial nuclei. The ? beta part of the collective potential is taken to be equal to Hulth?en potential while the gamma part is defined by a new generalized potential obtained from a ring shaped one. Analytical expressions for spectra and wave functions are derived by means of a recent version of the asymptotic iteration method and the usual approximations. The calculated energies and B(E2) transition rates are compared with experimental data and the available theoretical results in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian for triaxial nuclei using a Hulthén potential in the β variable and a new generalized ring-shaped potential in the γ variable, with the condition that the γ potential has a minimum at γ = π/6 and the approximation that the screening parameter δ is small (δ << 1) and the ratio s/c << 1 for harmonic γ-vibrations.

## Method

The authors separate the Bohr Hamiltonian into β and γ parts, using the Hulthén potential for the β-part and a generalized ring-shaped potential u₂(γ) = (c + s cos²(3γ))/sin²(3γ) for the γ-part. They solve the resulting differential equations using a recent version of the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) with an approximation for the centrifugal term valid for small β deformation, obtaining analytical expressions for energy spectra and wave functions. The model parameters δ, c, and s are fitted to experimental data for eight isotopes (¹²⁶,¹²⁸,¹³⁰,¹³²,¹³⁴Xe and ¹⁹²,¹⁹⁴,¹⁹⁶Pt) using a least-squares procedure.

## Context

This work extends the Z(5) model of triaxial nuclei (Z5, 2005) by replacing the infinite square well potential in β with the Hulthén potential, providing a more flexible description that can cover entire isotopic chains. The model also generalizes previous treatments of triaxial shapes using Davidson (Yigitoglu, 2011), sextic oscillator (Setix), and Morse potentials (Inci), as well as the algebraic collective model (Rowe) and Davydov-Chaban model (Davydov). The results enable systematic study of shape phase transitions in triaxial nuclei and can be tested against experimental data for Xe and Pt isotopes, with the γ-band staggering behavior providing a signature for triaxial deformation.
