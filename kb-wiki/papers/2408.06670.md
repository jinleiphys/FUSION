# The triton lifetime from nuclear lattice effective field theory

> In this work, we present a calculation of the triton $β$-decay lifetime using Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. By incorporating a non-perturbative treatment of the higher-order corrections, we achieve consistent predictions for the Fermi and Gamow-Teller matrix elements, which are crucial for determining the triton lifetime. Our results are consistent with earlier theoretical calculations, confirming the robustness of our approach. This study marks a significant advancement in the systematic application of NLEFT to nuclear $β$-decay processes, paving the way for future high-precision calculations in more complex nuclear systems. Additionally, we discuss potential improvements to our approach, including the explicit inclusion of two-pion exchange mechanisms and the refinement of three-nucleon forces. These developments are essential for extending the applicability of NLEFT to a broader range of nuclear phenomena, including neutrinoless double-$β$ decay.

## Key claim

The authors present a calculation of the triton beta-decay lifetime using Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) at N3LO in the chiral expansion, but the results are obtained with a specific set of smeared three-nucleon force operators that prevent direct comparison with continuum low-energy constants, and the predicted lifetime of 1105.1(74) s is slightly lower than the empirical value of 1132.1(25) s.

## Method

The authors use the same lattice action as in Elhatisari (2022) but extend beyond first-order perturbation theory by applying fully non-perturbative calculations to the three-nucleon system using the Lanczos eigenvector method on a lattice with spacing a=1.32 fm. They solve the Schrodinger equation for the three-nucleon system to obtain realistic wave functions, then compute the Fermi and Gamow-Teller matrix elements using the plateau method from lattice QCD, and vary the overall strength factors f_D and f_E of the smeared three-nucleon force operators while keeping the three-body binding energies invariant.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energies: 8.33(2) MeV for triton and 7.62(2) MeV for helium-3 (infinite-volume extrapolated), compared to empirical values of 8.48 MeV and 7.72 MeV respectively
- Fermi matrix element: ⟨F⟩ = 0.99949(11)
- Gamow-Teller matrix element: ⟨GT⟩ = 1.6743(58)
- Triton lifetime comparative half-life: (1+δ_R) t_{1/2} f_V = 1105.1(74) s
- Empirical Gamow-Teller matrix element derived from averaged data: ⟨GT⟩_emp = 1.6497(23)
- Gamow-Teller matrix element calculated using the computed Fermi matrix element and empirical half-lives: ⟨GT_F⟩ = 1.6498(29)

## Context

This work extends the NLEFT framework of Elhatisari (2022) from energy calculations to weak decay processes, enabling consistent predictions of both Fermi and Gamow-Teller matrix elements within the same theoretical framework. The results are consistent with earlier continuum calculations by Gazit (2008) and Baroni (2016), and the approach is intended to pave the way for future calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay in heavier nuclei such as calcium-48 and germanium-76.
