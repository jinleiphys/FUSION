# Regulator scheme dependence of the chiral phase transition at high densities

> A common feature of recent functional renormalization group investigations of effective low-energy QCD is the appearance of a back-bending behavior of the chiral phase transition line at low temperatures together with a negative entropy density in the symmetric regime. The regulator scheme dependence of this phenomenon and the necessary modifications at finite densities are analyzed within a two-flavor quark-meson model. The flows at finite densities for three different regulators of three- or four-dimensional momenta are confronted to each other. It is found that the back-bending behavior and the negative entropy density can be traced back to the explicit momentum dependence of the regulator shape function. While it persists for the often-used three-dimensional flat regulator, it vanishes for Callan-Symanzik type regulators. This points to truncation artifacts in the lowest order of the derivative expansion. A careful theoretical as well as numerical exploration is given.

## Key claim
The back-bending behavior of the chiral phase transition line at low temperatures and the associated negative entropy density in the symmetric regime are truncation artifacts of the lowest-order derivative expansion (LPA), specifically caused by the explicit momentum dependence of the regulator shape function; while the phenomenon persists for the three-dimensional flat (Litim) regulator, it vanishes for Callan-Symanzik type regulators.

## Method
The authors analyze the regulator scheme dependence of the chiral phase transition within a two-flavor quark-meson model using the functional renormalization group (FRG) in the local potential approximation (LPA). They compare flow equations at finite densities for three different regulator functions: a four-dimensional mass-like (Callan-Symanzik type) regulator, a three-dimensional mass-like regulator, and the three-dimensional flat (Litim) regulator. The flows are solved numerically with parameters fixed via an approximate partial IR fixed point procedure, and the resulting phase diagrams are confronted to identify the origin of the back-bending phenomenon.

## Key numbers
- The Yukawa coupling is fixed to \(g = 6.5\) at the chiral symmetry breaking scale \(k_\chi\).
- The explicit chiral symmetry breaking parameter is set to \(c = (120.73 \, \mathrm{MeV})^3\), yielding a pion mass \(m_\pi = 138 \, \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The pion decay constant is fixed to \(\sigma_\mathrm{min} \approx f_\pi = 92.4 \, \mathrm{MeV}\) in the infrared.
- The compositeness scale \(k_\phi\) is an additional free parameter for the mass-like regulators, with values tabulated but not specified in the truncated text.

## Context
This work extends previous FRG studies of the chiral phase transition at high densities that reported a back-bending transition line and negative entropy densities, first discussed by Tripolt (2017) and also observed in other models by Braun (2017) and Tripolt (2021). The present analysis demonstrates that these features are regulator-dependent artifacts of the LPA truncation, not physical phenomena related to diquark condensates or inhomogeneous phases as previously speculated. The results enable future studies to avoid these artifacts by using Callan-Symanzik type regulators or by going beyond LPA in derivative expansions.
