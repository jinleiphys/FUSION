# The Beauty of Spin

> I review recent developments in theoretical spin physics. Topics include pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, the implications of heavy quark spin symmetry for heavy hadron molecules, the nucleon electric dipole form factors and ab initio calculations of the width of hadron resonances. A few spin physics high-lights from experiments at the COSY accelerator are also discussed.

# The Beauty of Spin

## Key claim
This review argues that spin degrees of freedom provide essential tools for testing non-perturbative QCD dynamics across light and heavy quark sectors, but many of the presented results remain preliminary or dependent on future experimental confirmation. The author explicitly notes that existing data for the three-nucleon low-energy constant D are "not precise enough to pin down D accurately," and that lattice calculations of the Δ resonance width require "higher statistics" and "larger lattices" before definitive conclusions can be drawn.

## Method
The paper surveys theoretical spin physics using chiral perturbation theory for light-quark systems (up and down quarks) and heavy quark effective field theory for charm and bottom quarks, combined with lattice QCD simulations. For pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, a combined analysis at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the chiral expansion is presented, parameterized by the three-nucleon low-energy constant D. For hadron resonances, the Lüscher method is applied to finite-volume lattice data to extract scattering phase shifts and resonance parameters from the volume dependence of energy levels.

## Key numbers
- The predicted mass of the Yη state (ηc′f0(980) bound state) is 4616+5−6 MeV, derived from the Y(4660) mass using heavy quark spin symmetry.
- The COSY beam momentum was determined with precision Δp/p < 10−4 at 3 GeV beam momentum using artificially induced spin resonance depolarization.
- The current experimental limit on the neutron electric dipole moment is dn ≤ 2.9 × 10−26 e cm.
- A bound on the QCD vacuum angle is given as |θ0| ≲ 2.5 × 10−10 from chiral perturbation theory analysis.
- The chiral expansion of the neutron electric dipole radius is ⟨r²ed⟩n = −20.4 [1 − 0.67 + O(δ²)] θ0 e fm².
- The chiral expansion of the proton electric dipole radius is ⟨r²ed⟩p = +20.9 [1 − 0.70 + O(δ²)] θ0 e fm².
- The Δ(1232) resonance pole is located at (E, iΓ) = (1210, i50) MeV.
- Lattice calculations used 32³×64 and 40³×64 lattices at β=5.29, κ=0.13632, a=0.075 fm, and 40³×64 and 48³×64 lattices at β=5.29, κ=0.1364, a=0.06 fm.

## Context
This review extends the author's and collaborators' ongoing program applying chiral effective field theory to nuclear and hadronic physics, building on the framework reviewed by Bernard (2007) and Epelbaum (2008). The pion production analysis at NNLO (Baru, 2009) aims to determine the low-energy constant D that appears in multiple processes including Nd→Nd, NN→NNπ, and γd→NNπ. The heavy meson molecule predictions (Guo, 2009) extend heavy quark spin symmetry to molecular states, predicting a spin partner of the Y(4660) that could be tested at BESIII and future PANDA experiments. The nucleon electric dipole moment calculations (Ottnad, 2009) provide chiral extrapolation formulas needed to connect lattice QCD results at unphysical quark masses (Shintani, 2008) to physical values, while the Δ resonance width calculations (Bernard, 2007, 2008, 2009) develop finite-volume methods for extracting resonance parameters from lattice data that are now being applied by the QCDSF collaboration.
