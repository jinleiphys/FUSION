# Semi-classical and boson descriptions of scissors states

> A two interacting rotors Hamiltonian is alternatively treated semi-classically and by a Dyson boson expansion method. The linearized equations of motion lead to dispersion equation for the wobbling frequency. One defined a ground band with energies consisting in a rotational part and one half of the vibrational wobbling energy. Adding to each state energy the corresponding wobbling quanta one obtains the first excited band. Phonon amplitudes are used to calculate the reduced probability for the inter-band M1 transitions. The states exhibit a shears character. One points out a chiral symmetry which is broken by the interaction term, leading to a pair of twin chiral bands. Applications are made for $^{156}$Gd. One outlines the ability of the two rotor model to account for the wobbling and chiral motion in nuclei. Although the chosen trial function has not a definite total angular momentum, for two particular ansatz of the pairs $I_p,I_n$ the average value of the total angular momentum approximates, to a certain accuracy, the partial angular momentum $I_p$ In this context, the rotational bands defined throughout this present paper could be labeled by the total I.

# Semi-classical and boson descriptions of scissors states

## Key claim
The two-rotor model Hamiltonian, treated semi-classically and via Dyson boson expansion, can simultaneously describe wobbling and chiral motion in nuclei, but the rotational bands defined in the paper can only be labeled by the total angular momentum I because the chosen trial function does not have a definite total angular momentum, and the average value of the total angular momentum approximates the partial angular momentum I_p only for two particular ansatz of the pairs (I_p, I_n).

## Method
A two-interacting-rotors Hamiltonian was treated using two approaches: (1) a semi-classical method employing a time-dependent variational principle with coherent states for the SU(2) groups of proton and neutron angular momenta, leading to linearized equations of motion and an RPA-like dispersion equation for the wobbling frequency; and (2) a Dyson boson expansion method where the classical angular momenta were mapped onto boson operators, followed by a Bogoliubov transformation and linearization to obtain the phonon operator and wobbling frequency. The phonon amplitudes from both methods were used to calculate reduced M1 transition probabilities between the ground band and the first excited band.

## Key numbers
- For $^{156}$Gd: proton moment of inertia ${\cal J}_p = 9.3075\ \hbar^2\text{MeV}^{-1}$, neutron moment of inertia ${\cal J}_n = 13.6649\ \hbar^2\text{MeV}^{-1}$, interaction strength $V = 0.186\ \text{MeV}$.
- The nuclear quadrupole deformation for $^{156}$Gd is $\beta = 0.266$.
- The interaction strength V was fixed such that the energy difference between the $2^+$ states of the yrast and first excited bands equals $0.1\ \text{MeV}$.
- Stationary coordinates for the minimum of the energy surface for (I_p, I_n) = (1,1): $\stackrel{\circ}{r} = 0.888889$, $\stackrel{\circ}{t} = 0.888889$.
- Stationary coordinates for (I_p, I_n) = (10,1): $\stackrel{\circ}{r} = 9.9012$, $\stackrel{\circ}{t} = 0.769031$.
- Stationary angles satisfy $\stackrel{\circ}{\psi} - \stackrel{\circ}{\varphi} = \pi$, with the choice $\stackrel{\circ}{\psi} = \pi$ and $\stackrel{\circ}{\varphi} = 0$.
- The Dyson boson method yields a single positive wobbling frequency $\omega_b^{I_p I_n} = |a| V/2$, where $a = -\sqrt{I_p(I_p+1)I_n(I_n+1)} - I_p I_n - \frac{1}{2}(I_p + I_n)$.

## Context
This work extends the Two Rotor Model (TRM) originally proposed by Lo Iudice and Palumbo (1984) for describing the scissors mode in nuclei, and the Generalized Coherent State Model (GCSM) of Raduta et al. (1987) which described scissors modes alongside ground, beta, and gamma bands. The paper provides a new semi-classical and boson expansion treatment of the two-rotor Hamiltonian, enabling simultaneous description of wobbling and chiral motion in nuclei. The numerical application to $^{156}$Gd connects to the experimental identification of the M1 scissors state in that nucleus by Richter's group (1983) and subsequent nuclear resonance fluorescence experiments (1984). The approach enables calculation of wobbling frequencies and M1 transition probabilities for ground and one-phonon excited bands, and identifies a chiral symmetry broken by the interaction term leading to twin chiral bands.
