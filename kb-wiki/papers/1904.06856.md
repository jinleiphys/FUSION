# Equation of State for Neutron Stars in the Quark-Meson Coupling Model with the Cloudy Bag

> Using the quark-meson coupling model with the cloudy bag, we construct the equation of state for neutron stars with hyperons in SU(3) flavor symmetry. The hyperfine interaction due to the gluon exchange and the pion-cloud effect inside a baryon is taken into account in vacuum and nuclear matter. We investigate how the quark degrees of freedom and the exchanges of gluon and pion between two quarks affect the properties of nuclear and neutron-star matter. It is found that the variation of baryon substructure in matter plays an important role in supporting massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

Using the quark-meson coupling model with the cloudy bag, the authors find that the variation of baryon substructure in matter plays an important role in supporting massive neutron stars, with the pion-cloud effect being particularly vital to solve the 2 solar mass neutron-star problem.

## Method

The authors construct the equation of state for neutron stars with hyperons in SU(3) flavor symmetry using the quark-meson coupling model with the cloudy bag model. They incorporate the hyperfine interaction due to gluon exchange and the pion-cloud effect inside a baryon, both in vacuum and nuclear matter, and parametrize the field-dependent scalar polarizabilities to account for the variation of baryon substructure. The coupling constants are determined to reproduce saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, and the vector meson couplings to baryons are set using SU(3) flavor symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{\rm sym}(\rho_0) = 32.5\) MeV (fitted in all cases)
- Effective nucleon mass ratio \(M_N^{\ast}/M_N\): 0.800 (QHD+NL and QMC), 0.782 (QMCg), 0.754 (CQMC)
- Incompressibility \(K_0\): 285 MeV (QHD+NL and QMC), 294 MeV (QMCg), 309 MeV (CQMC)
- Third-order incompressibility \(J_0\): \(-571\) MeV (QHD+NL), \(-459\) MeV (QMC), \(-407\) MeV (QMCg), \(-320\) MeV (CQMC)
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy \(L\): 88.2 MeV (QHD+NL and QMC), 89.0 MeV (QMCg), 90.3 MeV (CQMC)
- Hyperon potentials at saturation: \(U_{\Lambda}(\rho_0) = -28\) MeV, \(U_{\Sigma}(\rho_0) = +30\) MeV, \(U_{\Xi}(\rho_0) = -18\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1987) and Saito (1994, 2005) by including the cloudy bag model of Thomas (1982) with gluon and pion exchange effects, building on prior calculations by Nagai (2008) and Miyatsu (2010, 2013). The results show that including quark degrees of freedom and hyperfine interactions increases the maximum mass of neutron stars compared to standard Quantum Hadrodynamics (Serot, 1984), and the pion-cloud effect is essential to reach the 2 solar mass threshold required by observations of massive neutron stars (Demorest, 2010; Antoniadis, 2013) and gravitational wave constraints from LIGO/Virgo (2017).
