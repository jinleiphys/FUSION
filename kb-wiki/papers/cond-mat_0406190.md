# Exactly-solvable models for atom-molecule hamiltonians

> We present a family of exactly-solvable generalizations of the Jaynes-Cummings model involving the interaction of an ensemble of SU(2) or SU(1,1) quasi-spins with a single boson field. They are obtained from the trigonometric Richardson-Gaudin models by replacing one of the SU(2) or SU(1,1) degrees of freedom by an ideal boson. Application to a system of bosonic atoms and molecules is reported.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new family of exactly-solvable models for atom-molecule systems by replacing one SU(2) or SU(1,1) degree of freedom in the trigonometric Richardson-Gaudin models with an ideal boson in the limit of infinite degeneracy, thereby extending integrability to Hamiltonians that include a single bosonic mode coupled to an ensemble of quasi-spins.

## Method

Starting from the trigonometric family of Richardson-Gaudin integrable models, the authors replace one SU(2) or SU(1,1) degree of freedom (labeled i=0) by an ideal boson via the limit Omega_0 -> infinity, mapping the generators K_0^0 and K_0^+ onto boson operators. They expand the trigonometric amplitudes in inverse powers of Omega_0, introducing new parameters epsilon_l to replace the original eta_l, and obtain new integrals of motion R_0 and R_j that remain hermitian, mutually commuting, and define a completely new set of exactly-solvable models. The seniority-zero eigenstates are constructed as products of collective operators B_alpha^dagger, with parameters x_alpha satisfying a set of coupled nonlinear Richardson-Gaudin equations, and the eigenvalues of any Hamiltonian written in terms of the R_i are obtained directly from the eigenvalues of these integrals of motion.

## Key numbers

* For a system of M=500 bosonic pairs in a 3D isotropic trap with L=50 harmonic oscillator shells and positive molecular energy omega=10, a quantum phase transition occurs at V approximately -0.45, where the occupation of the atomic condensate becomes negligible for V < -0.45.
* For negative molecular energy omega=-10, the system is purely molecular for weak coupling, with no phase transition observed; the occupation of the lowest trap level remains negligible for all V.
* The single-atom energies are taken as epsilon_j = j (j=0,1,...) and the level degeneracies as Omega_j = (j+1)(j+2)/2 for the 3D isotropic trap.

## Context

This work extends the trigonometric Richardson-Gaudin integrable models (Dukelsky, Esebbag, Schuck 2001) by applying a bosonization trick originally proposed by Gaudin (1976) to create new exactly-solvable Hamiltonians for atom-molecule systems. The resulting models generalize the Tavis-Cummings model (Tavis, Cummings 1968) and the atom-molecule model of Links et al. (2003) to multi-level systems with a single bosonic mode. The exact solutions enable detailed study of quantum phase transitions in atom-molecule mixtures, such as the transition at V approximately -0.45 previously studied using mean-field and renormalization group techniques (Radzihovsky, Park 2003; Romans et al. 2003), and can be applied to ultracold fermionic systems (Greiner, Regal, Jin 2003; Jochim et al. 2003) to explore the BCS-to-BEC crossover.
