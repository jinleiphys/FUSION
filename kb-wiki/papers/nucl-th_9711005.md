# Relativistic covariance of quasipotential equations

> We argue that most of the relativistic 3-D (quasipotential) equations used in hadron physics are inconsistent with the discrete symmetries like charge conjugation and CPT, yielding an incorrect Lorentz structure for the calculated Green's functions. An exception to this is the equal time approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation. We present a covariant quasipotential model for the pion-nucleon interaction based on hadronic degrees of freedom and satisfying the full covariance.

## Key claim

Most relativistic 3-D (quasipotential) equations used in hadron physics violate discrete symmetries such as charge conjugation and CPT, yielding an incorrect Lorentz structure for calculated Green's functions; the equal-time approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation is an exception that preserves full covariance.

## Method

The authors analyze the Lorentz structure of the nucleon self-energy in the center-of-mass frame, testing whether the identity \(\Sigma_{r}(P_0) = \Sigma_{-r}(-P_0)\) holds for various quasipotential reduction schemes. They construct a unitary dynamical model for the pion-nucleon interaction based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the equal-time approximation, using tree-level exchanges of \(\rho\), \(\sigma\), nucleon, \(N^{*}(1470)\), and \(\Delta(1232)\) as the driving force, with parameters fitted to \(\pi N\) phase-shifts up to 400 MeV pion kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- \(g_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 13.5\) (pion-nucleon coupling constant)
- \(f_{\pi N\Delta}^2/4\pi = 0.33\), off-shell parameter \(z = -0.2\) (pion-nucleon-Delta coupling)
- \(g_{\sigma NN}g_{\sigma\pi\pi}/4\pi = 0.1\) (sigma meson coupling)
- \(g_{\rho NN}g_{\rho\pi\pi}/4\pi = 2.5\), \(\kappa_\rho = 3.7\) (rho meson coupling)
- Cutoff masses: \(\Lambda_N = 1.5\) GeV, \(\Lambda_\pi = 1.5\) GeV, \(\Lambda_{N^*} = 1.9\) GeV, \(\Lambda_\Delta = 1.4\) GeV, \(\Lambda_\sigma = 1.1\) GeV, \(\Lambda_\rho = 1.1\) GeV
- Bare masses: \(m_{N^*} = 1.54\) GeV, \(m_\Delta = 1.24\) GeV, \(m_\sigma = 0.55\) GeV, \(m_\rho = 0.77\) GeV
- Fit to S- and P-wave \(\pi N\) phase-shifts up to 400 MeV pion kinetic energy

## Context

This work extends the analysis of relativistic 3-D reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, directly comparing the equal-time approximation with the spectator (Gross) equation used by Gross and Surya (1993) and the Blankenbecler-Sugar propagator used by Pearce and Jennings (1991). The authors demonstrate that the equal-time approximation preserves the correct Lorentz structure \(\Sigma(\slashed{P}) = \slashed{P} A(P^2) + B(P^2)\) and satisfies charge conjugation and CPT symmetries, whereas other quasipotential schemes violate these symmetries due to asymmetric treatment of positive and negative energy states. This enables construction of a covariant \(\pi N\) model suitable for extension to few-body systems without the need for non-covariant renormalization procedures.
