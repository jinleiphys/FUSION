# Model for the Glauber-type calculations of beam fragmentation at low energies

> In the present paper momentum distributions of nuclei produced in the heavy ion beam fragmentation at the relatively low energies (below 100 $A\cdot$MeV) are studied. For this study, a new theoretical approach is developed on the basis of the Glauber model modified for taking into account the energy and momentum conservation laws. In this approach, the longitudinal momentum of the most neutron rich nuclei, $^{10}$Be, $^9$Li, $^8$He, produced in a few neutron removal reactions in the $^{11}$B fragmentation in the Be target at a beam energy of 35 $A\cdot$MeV are calculated. The region of applicability of the new approach is discussed. This approach gives the asymmetric longitudinal momentum distributions at low energies, and the asymmetry is defined by the kinematical locus and geometry of the reaction (central of peripheral reactions). We analyze the changes of the phase volume and the longitudinal momentum distributions with the beam energy and number of the removed nucleons. The results of the calculations are compared to the parametrizations widely used for estimates of nuclear production in fragmentation for planning of nuclear experiments.

# Model for the Glauber-type calculations of beam fragmentation at low energies

## Key claim
The authors develop a modified Glauber model that incorporates energy and momentum conservation laws to calculate longitudinal momentum distributions of fragments produced in heavy-ion beam fragmentation at energies below 100 A·MeV, where the Q-value of the reaction is comparable to the beam energy and traditional sudden-removal approaches (such as the Serber model and standard eikonal approximation) are not precise.

## Method
The approach replaces the T-matrix in the differential cross section with the inelastic scattering amplitude multiplied by the Lorentz-invariant phase volume. The projectile is treated as a pre-formed heavy core and valence nucleons; the inelastic scattering amplitude is approximated by the elastic scattering amplitude of the core multiplied by form factors for the core and nucleons, where the core-target interaction is calculated via a folding model using Gaussian density distributions and nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The longitudinal momentum distributions of fragments are computed using Monte Carlo sampling of the reaction kinematics.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy studied: 35 A·MeV for ¹¹B fragmentation on a ⁹Be target.
- Energy range of applicability: below 100 A·MeV.
- Proton RMS distance in ¹¹B used as input: ⟨r_p⟩ = 2.43 fm.
- Default RMS radii of heavy fragments inside ¹¹B: 0.2426 fm for ¹⁰Be, 0.4852 fm for ⁹Li, 0.7278 fm for ⁸He.
- Range of RMS radii varied for sensitivity study: minimum 0.1213 fm and maximum 0.4852 fm for ¹⁰Be; minimum 0.2426 fm and maximum 0.9705 fm for ⁹Li; minimum 0.3639 fm and maximum 1.4557 fm for ⁸He.
- Comparison with Goldhaber (1974) model performed at beam energies of 10, 30, and 100 A·MeV.
- Standard Glauber model provides good description within momentum transfer 100–150 MeV/c; the new approach extends beyond this range.

## Context
This work extends the Glauber model (Glauber 1987) to low-energy fragmentation where the Q-value is comparable to the beam energy, a regime where the Serber model (Serber 1963) and traditional eikonal approximation (which assume Q=0) give only qualitative descriptions. The results are compared to the Goldhaber (1974) statistical model and the parametrizations of Tarasov (2004) used for planning nuclear experiments. The approach enables more accurate predictions of fragment yields and momentum distributions for facilities operating at energies below 100 A·MeV, particularly for planning experiments at fragment separators where the longitudinal emittance of the beam (2% leading to 20% increase after fragmentation) determines acceptance conditions.
