# Observation of Strong $ϕ$-meson Directed Flow at High Baryon Density

> We report the first observation of a large directed flow ($v_1$) of $ϕ$ mesons in mid-central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=3.0$-4.5 GeV, together with the $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=7.7$ GeV collider-energy result for the collision-energy dependence, measured by the STAR experiment at RHIC. Despite its mesonic nature, the $ϕ$-meson $v_{1}$ exhibits a magnitude comparable to that of protons and $Λ$ baryons, and is significantly larger (by a factor of $\gtrsim3$) than that of $K^0_S$ mesons at the lowest energies. The midrapidity slope $dv_1/dy$ shows a pronounced energy dependence similar to that observed for protons and $Λ$ baryons, in contrast to lighter mesons, in the high-baryon-density region. Comparisons with hadronic transport calculations indicate that the observed $ϕ$-meson directed flow is consistent with production via high-mass baryon resonances and the collective motion of baryons influenced by baryonic mean-field interactions. Due to its long lifetime and relatively weak effective $ϕ$-$N$ interaction, the $ϕ$ meson can retain sensitivity to collective dynamics established during the dense baryonic stage of the collision. These results provide new experimental constraints on baryon-resonance production and strange-hadron transport dynamics in dense QCD matter.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of a large directed flow of phi mesons in mid-central Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies 3.0-4.5 GeV, claiming that despite being mesons, phi mesons exhibit directed flow comparable in magnitude to protons and Lambda baryons and significantly larger than that of K0_S mesons. The hedge is that this behavior is observed specifically in the high-baryon-density regime and is interpreted as consistent with production via high-mass baryon resonances and collective baryonic motion influenced by baryonic mean-field interactions, rather than being a universal property of phi mesons at all collision energies.

## Method

The STAR experiment reconstructed phi mesons via the K+K- decay channel using the Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight detectors in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0, 3.2, 3.5, 3.9, 4.5, and 7.7 GeV, with the 3.0-4.5 GeV data from the fixed-target program and 7.7 GeV from collider mode. The directed flow v1 was measured using the invariant-mass method with respect to the first-order event plane reconstructed from the Event Plane Detector, and the midrapidity slope dv1/dy was extracted from linear fits to the v1(y) distributions in the rapidity interval -1 < y < 0 for 10-40% centrality collisions.

## Key numbers

* Phi-meson directed flow magnitude is comparable to that of protons and Lambda baryons, and significantly larger (by a factor of at least 3) than that of K0_S mesons at the lowest collision energies (3.0-4.5 GeV)
* The first-order event-plane resolution ranges from 55% to 75% for sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0-4.5 GeV
* Total systematic uncertainties on the midrapidity slope dv1/dy for phi mesons range from 2.2% at 3.0 GeV to 11.6% at 4.5 GeV
* The phi-meson acceptance window for the directed flow analysis is pT = 0.6-1.8 GeV/c and rapidity y = -1.0 to 0.0
* Without the baryonic mean-field potential in UrQMD calculations, the magnitude of dv1/dy for phi mesons is underestimated by up to a factor of three at the lowest collision energies

## Context

This work extends the STAR Beam Energy Scan program's measurements of directed flow for protons, Lambda baryons, and K0_S mesons (STAR 2014, STAR 2017, STAR 2021) into the phi-meson channel at high baryon density, where previous transport model studies (Steinheimer 2015, Steinheimer 2025) had suggested phi mesons could be produced via high-mass baryon resonance decays. The results provide new experimental constraints on baryon-resonance production and strange-hadron transport dynamics in dense QCD matter, and enable future comparisons with models such as UrQMD, PHQMD, and JAM2 to refine understanding of baryonic mean-field interactions and in-medium phi-N interaction strength.
