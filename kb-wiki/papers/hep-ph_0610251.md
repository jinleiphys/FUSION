# Heavy Quarkonium Physics from Effective Field Theories

> I review recent progress in heavy quarkonium physics from an effective field theory perspective. In this unifying framework, I discuss advances in perturbative calculations for low-lying quarkonium observables and in lattice calculations for high-lying ones, and progress and lasting puzzles in quarkonium production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective field theories (EFTs) provide a rigorous, scale-by-scale framework for heavy quarkonium physics that distinguishes between low-lying states (where perturbative calculations are reliable) and high-lying states (where non-perturbative lattice methods are required), but the author notes that the transition point between these regimes is not clearly established and there is no agreement in the literature.

## Method

The author reviews recent progress in heavy quarkonium physics by organizing results according to the hierarchy of energy scales (heavy quark mass m, soft scale mv, ultrasoft scale mv²) and the corresponding EFTs: non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) for integrating out the mass scale, and potential NRQCD (pNRQCD) for integrating out the soft scale. The review covers perturbative calculations for low-lying states, lattice calculations for high-lying states, and phenomenological models for threshold states, with separate sections for doubly heavy baryons and quarkonium production.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass determinations in the MS scheme: $\overline{m}_b(\overline{m}_b) = 4.210 \pm 0.090 \pm 0.025$ GeV (NNNLO*) from Pineda (2001) and $4.190 \pm 0.020 \pm 0.025$ GeV (NNLO + charm) from Brambilla (2001)
- Charm quark mass: $\overline{m}_c(\overline{m}_c) = 1.24 \pm 0.020$ GeV (NNLO) from Brambilla (2001)
- $B_c$ mass: $M_{B_c} = 6326 \pm 29$ MeV (NNLO) from Brambilla (2000), with experimental measurement $M_{B_c} = 6276.5 \pm 4.0 \pm 2.7$ MeV from CDF (2006)
- $\eta_b$ mass prediction: $M_{\eta_b} = 9421 \pm 10^{+9}_{-8}$ MeV from NLL hyperfine splitting calculation by Kniehl (2003)
- $B_c$ hyperfine splitting: $M_{B_c^*} - M_{B_c} = 65 \pm 24^{+19}_{-16}$ MeV (NLL) from Penin (2004)
- Ratio of electromagnetic decay widths: $\Gamma(\eta_b\to\gamma\gamma) /\Gamma(\Upsilon(1S)\to e^+e^-) = 0.502 \pm 0.068 \pm 0.014$ (NNLL) from Penin (2004)
- Magnetic dipole transition widths: $\Gamma(J/\psi \to \gamma \, \eta_c) = (1.5 \pm 1.0)$ keV (NNLO) and $\Gamma(\Upsilon(1S) \to \gamma\,\eta_b) = (k_\gamma/39$ MeV)$^3 (2.50 \pm 0.25)$ eV (NNLO) from Brambilla (2005)
- Doubly charmed baryon hyperfine splitting: $M_{\Xi^*_{cc}}-M_{\Xi_{cc}} = 120 \pm 40$ MeV (NLO) from Brambilla (2005)
- Doubly bottom baryon hyperfine splitting: $M_{\Xi^*_{bb}}-M_{\Xi_{bb}} = 34 \pm 4$ MeV (NLO) from Brambilla (2005)
- Double charmonium production cross section: $\sigma(e^+e^-\to J/\psi+\eta_c) = 16.7 \pm 4.2$ fb (preliminary estimate including NLO and v² corrections) from Lee (2006)

## Context

This review extends the EFT framework for quarkonium developed by Brambilla, Pineda, Soto, and Vairo (2000-2004) and contrasts with traditional potential models that treat the whole spectrum with the same interaction. The paper enables future work by identifying open problems: the need for a systematic EFT treatment of threshold states (such as the X(3872) and Y(4260) discovered at B-factories), the unresolved discrepancy in inclusive double charm production at BELLE, and the puzzling charmonium polarization data from CDF that disagree with NRQCD predictions.
