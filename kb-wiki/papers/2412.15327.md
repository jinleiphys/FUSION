# Global parametrizations of $ππ$ scattering with dispersive constraints: Beyond the S0 wave

> We provide new global parametrizations of $ππ\to ππ$ scattering for the S2, P, D, F, and G partial waves up to at least 1.8 GeV, easy to implement for phenomenological use. With earlier S0-wave parametrizations, slightly updated here, they reproduce previous partial wave dispersion analyses up to the $πω$ threshold. In addition, these new parametrizations have improved their description of recent P-wave data, the inelasticity in various waves, and their fulfillment of Roy-like and forward dispersion relations. The latter now test very high partial waves and have an improved matching with the Regge regime, extending those with P-wave contributions up to 1.6 GeV. Above 1.6 GeV and up to 1.8 GeV, or sometimes somewhat beyond, the parametrizations are simple unconstrained fits to data.

## Key claim

The paper provides new global parametrizations of ππ → ππ scattering for the S2, P, D, F, and G partial waves up to at least 1.8 GeV that are easy to implement for phenomenological use, but above 1.6 GeV and up to 1.8 GeV (or sometimes somewhat beyond) the parametrizations become simple unconstrained fits to data rather than dispersively constrained results.

## Method

The authors constructed analytic parametrizations for each partial wave using conformal expansions, Chebyshev polynomials, and elastic-plus-inelastic factorization of the S-matrix, fitting to experimental scattering data from the CERN-Munich collaboration and other experiments. They imposed forward dispersion relation constraints up to 1.6 GeV for P-wave contributions (200 MeV higher than previous work) with improved matching to the Regge regime, and used Roy-like equations below 1.1 GeV. For the S0 wave, they slightly updated earlier parametrizations from Pelaez (2019) to account for indirect effects of changes in other waves.

## Key numbers

- The P-wave inelasticity is now allowed to start at the πω threshold at √s = 922.23 MeV (mπ + mω = 139.57 + 782.66 MeV)
- The parametrizations cover energies up to at least 1.8 GeV for all waves, with some extending to 2.1 GeV
- Forward dispersion relations with P-wave contributions are now tested up to 1.6 GeV, compared to 1.42 GeV in previous work (GarciaMartin 2011)
- The matching point between partial-wave and Regge representations is set at √s = 1.4 GeV for the P-wave
- Pion mass used: mπ = 139.57 MeV; kaon mass: mK = 495.7 MeV; omega mass: mω = 782.66 MeV

## Context

This work extends the global parametrization approach of Pelaez (2019), which covered only S0 and P waves, to include S2, D0, D2, F, G0, and G2 partial waves. It improves upon the dispersive constrained fits of GarciaMartin (2011) by extending the energy range of forward dispersion relation constraints and improving the treatment of P-wave inelasticity, which was neglected in that earlier work. The parametrizations enable phenomenological applications requiring wider energy ranges and higher partial waves, such as resonance pole searches in the complex plane, and provide input for experimental analyses at facilities like ALICE, BABAR, Belle, LHCb, FAIR, and the EIC.
