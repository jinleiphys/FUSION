# Higher-order cumulants of net-charge distributions from local charge conservation

> In studies of heavy-ion collisions, fluctuations of conserved quantities are considered as an important signal of the transition between the hadronic and partonic phases of nuclear matter. In this paper, it is investigated how the local charge conservation affects higher-order cumulants of net-charge distributions at LHC energies. Simple expressions for the cumulants are derived under the assumption that particle-antiparticle pairs are produced in local processes from sources that are nearly uncorrelated in rapidity. For calculations with these expressions, one needs to know only the second cumulant of net-charge distribution and low-order cumulants of particle number distribution, which are directly measurable experimentally. It is argued that if one wishes to relate susceptibilities with cumulants of net-proton distributions, the developed model provides a better baseline than the conventional Skellam limit or models based on monte-carlo simulations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that local charge conservation, modeled by assuming particle-antiparticle pairs are produced from nearly uncorrelated sources in rapidity, provides a more realistic baseline for higher-order cumulants of net-charge distributions than the conventional Skellam limit, but this baseline depends on the assumption that the width of the balance function of a source is significantly narrower than the width of the rapidity distribution of the sources.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the fourth and sixth cumulants of net-charge distributions under the assumption that each source produces exactly one particle-antiparticle pair, with sources being nearly uncorrelated in rapidity. The cumulant ratios are expressed in terms of the second cumulant of the net-charge distribution and the robust quantities R2 and R3 of the negative particle (or antiproton) number distribution, which are directly measurable experimentally. The validity of the assumptions is tested by comparing direct calculations from HIJING and EPOS LHC event generators for Pb-Pb collisions at 5 TeV with the analytic expressions.

## Key numbers

- In PYTHIA8 pp collisions at 2.76 TeV, 77% of sources produce only one charged particle visible within acceptance, 10% give single particle-antiparticle pairs, and approximately 13% contain non-resonance sources producing more than two charged particles (for all charged daughters with pT 0.6-2.0 GeV/c and |eta|<2)
- For net-charge analysis in HIJING at 60-90% centrality, the difference between direct calculation of kappa4/kappa2 and the analytic expression is about 4%
- For net-proton fluctuations in HIJING and EPOS LHC at 60-90% centrality, the analytic expression for kappa4/kappa2 shows better agreement with direct calculations than for all charged particles
- In HIJING with 65-75% centrality interval and centrality bin width correction, the kappa4/kappa2 ratio converges to a value around 1.3

## Context

This work extends previous studies on how charge conservation laws affect net-charge cumulants (Bzdak 2013, PBM_AR_JS_NPA, PBM_AR_JS_2019) and volume fluctuation effects (PBM_AR_JS_NPA, Nonaka 2019). It provides a baseline that improves upon the Skellam distribution limit and Monte Carlo simulation baselines (Netrakanti 2014) by incorporating local charge conservation effects. The derived expressions enable experimental measurements of higher-order cumulants to be compared with lattice QCD susceptibilities, with deviations from these baselines potentially indicating critical phenomena. The approach is complementary to the binomial sampling model of Vovchenko (2020) which considers global charge conservation in a fixed volume.
