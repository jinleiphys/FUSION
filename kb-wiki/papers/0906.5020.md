# Neutron Charge Density from Simple Pion Cloud Models

> The physical nucleon is modeled as a bare nucleon surrounded by a pion cloud using a psuedoscalar pion-nucleon coupling to examine its implications for the neutron form factor, F_1, and the corresponding transverse charge density in the infinite momentum frame. Two versions, one with a tunable Pauli-Villars parameter, and one with light-front cloudy-bag pion-nucleon form factors, are examined. A qualitative agreement with the experimental form factor (negative definite for all Q^2) and transverse charge density are achieved when the nucleon is treated as having a finite extent. The bare nucleon must have a finite extent if this model is to account for a negative definite F_1, and consequently a negative transverse charge density of the neutron at its center.

## Key claim
Simple pion cloud models can produce a neutron form factor \(F_1\) that is negative for all \(Q^2\) and a negative transverse charge density at the neutron's center, but only if the bare nucleon is given a finite spatial extent; without this finite extent, the model yields \(F_1 > 0\) for some \(Q^2\), disagreeing with experiment.

## Method
The physical nucleon is modeled as a bare nucleon surrounded by a pion cloud using pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling to one-loop order. Two versions are examined: Model 1 uses a Pauli-Villars regulator with a tunable mass parameter \(\Lambda\) to handle divergences; Model 2 uses light-front cloudy-bag pion-nucleon form factors and includes an explicit dipole factor \(\chi = 1/(1+Q^2 r_s^2/12)^2\) to give the bare nucleon a finite extent. Numerical integration over Feynman parameters (Model 1) or over light-front variables (Model 2) yields the form factor \(F_1(Q^2)\), and the transverse charge density \(\rho(b)\) is obtained via a two-dimensional Fourier transform.

## Key numbers
- Neutron mass \(M = 0.93957\) GeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.13957\) GeV, coupling \(\frac{g_0^2}{4\pi} = 13.5\).
- Model 1: Pauli-Villars regulator mass \(\Lambda = 1.0\) GeV yields \(F_2(0) = -1.9091\), close to the neutron magnetic moment; \(F_1(0) = 0\) (charge conservation).
- Model 2: bare nucleon radius parameter \(r_s^2 = 10.0\ \text{GeV}^{-2} \approx 0.39\ \text{fm}^2\) and \(\Lambda = 1.25\) GeV produce a form factor qualitatively matching the Kelly (2004) parameterization.

## Context
This work extends the finding by Miller (2007) that the neutron transverse charge density is negative at the center, which contrasted with earlier meson cloud models and arguments from Fermi and Marshall (1947), Thomas (1981), Friar (1972), Carlitz (1977), and Isgur (1981). The toy models here show that a finite bare nucleon size is essential for reproducing the observed negative-definite \(F_1\) within a pion cloud framework. The authors note the model is not realistic because the finite-extent factor is not derived from an underlying theory and proton properties are not studied; future work should seek a more rigorous formalism.
