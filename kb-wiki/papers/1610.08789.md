# Recent progress in understanding deconfinement and chiral restoration phase transitions

> Paradigme shift in gauge topology, from instantons to their constituents -- instanton-dyons -- has recently lead to very significant advances. Like instantons, they have fermionic zero modes, and their collectivization at sufficiently high density explains the chiral symmetry breaking. Unlike instantons, these objects have electric and magnetic charges. Their back reaction on the mean value of the Polyakov line (holonomy) allows to explain the deconfinement transition. The talk summarizes recent works on the dyon ensemble, done in the mean field approximation (MFA), and also by direct numerical statistical simulation. Introduction of non-trivial quark periodicity conditions leads to drastic changes in both deconfinement and chiral transitions. In particulaly, in the so called Z(N_c)-QCD model the former gets much stronger, while the latter does not seem to occur at all.

## Key claim

The shift from instantons to their constituents (instanton-dyons) has enabled a unified semiclassical explanation of both deconfinement and chiral restoration transitions, but the specific behavior depends critically on quark periodicity conditions: in the so-called Z(N_c)-QCD model the deconfinement transition becomes much stronger while chiral restoration does not seem to occur at all.

## Method

The paper reviews two complementary approaches to studying instanton-dyon ensembles: a mean field approximation (MFA) in which the partition function is bosonized and certain fields are treated as x-independent parameters whose free energy is minimized via gap equations, and direct numerical statistical simulations of the dyon ensemble with classical dyon-antidyon interactions and fermionic determinants evaluated in the zero mode approximation. Both approaches incorporate the back reaction of dyons on the holonomy (Polyakov line) and the collectivization of fermionic zero modes into a quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2) gauge theory with N_f=2 light flavors, the chiral condensate scales as |<bar q q>|/T^3 approximately 1.25 (n_L/T^3)^1.63, where n_L is the L-dyon density.
- The gap equation for the quark condensate has nonzero solutions only if N_f < 2N_c, giving a critical number of flavors N_f=6 for N_c=3.
- For SU(2) with N_f=2, both deconfinement and chiral restoration transitions occur at the same dyon density, corresponding to action S=7-7.5 (where S=8pi^2/g^2 relates to temperature).
- In the Z(2) QCD model with N_c=N_f=2, the deconfinement transition shows a clear first-order jump in the Polyakov line, unlike the smooth crossover in ordinary QCD with the same N_c,N_f.

## Context

This work extends the instanton-dyon framework pioneered by Diakonov (2009) and the semiclassical confining regime of Poppitz (2011, 2012) to include dynamical fermions and direct numerical simulations. It builds on the Interacting Instanton Liquid Model of Schafer (1996) and addresses the long-standing puzzle of why deconfinement and chiral transitions occur at similar temperatures for fundamental quarks but not for adjoint ones, as noted by Karsch (1998). The results enable lattice tests of instanton-dyon predictions, including the predicted "internal phase transitions" from hopping of quark zero modes between dyon types, and systematic searches for instanton-dyons on the lattice to quantify their density and parameters directly.
