# Renormalising NN scattering: is power counting powerless?

> The renormalisation of NN scattering in theories with zero-range interactions is examined using a cut-off regularisation where the cut-off is taken to infinity, dimensional regularisation (DR) with minimal subtraction, and DR with power-divergence subtraction. In the infinite cut-off limit power counting breaks down: terms of different orders in the potential contribute to the scattering amplitude at the same order. Minimal subtraction does yield a systematic expansion, but with a very limited range of validity for systems that have unnaturally large scattering lengths. For a finite cut-off, the behaviour of the couplings as the cut-off is lowered shows that a theory with a natural scattering length approaches an IR fixed point. In the corresponding effective theory, loop corrections can be treated perturbatively. In contrast, if there is an IR fixed point for systems with an infinite scattering length it must be a nonperturbative one, with no power counting. For such systems, power-divergence subtraction appears to yield a systematic expansion, but with a different power counting from Weinberg's. However the scheme omits IR divergent terms that would otherwise lead to nonperturbative behaviour and so the interpretation of the fixed point remains unclear.

## Key claim

For systems with unnaturally large scattering lengths (like s-wave NN scattering), a systematic power counting may be possible using dimensional regularisation with power-divergence subtraction (PDS), but the scheme omits infrared divergent terms that would otherwise lead to nonperturbative behaviour, leaving the interpretation of the fixed point unclear.

## Method

The paper examines renormalisation of NN scattering in a simplified effective field theory with zero-range contact interactions (pions integrated out), using three regularisation schemes: cut-off regularisation with the cut-off taken to infinity, dimensional regularisation (DR) with minimal subtraction, and DR with power-divergence subtraction (PDS). The s-wave K-matrix is solved from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for a separable potential with form factor, and the behaviour of the couplings is analysed as the cut-off or renormalisation scale is varied.

## Key numbers

- For cut-off regularisation with infinite cut-off, the bare couplings scale as C0 ~ 1/(MΛ) and C2 ~ 1/(MΛ³), but the resulting on-shell K-matrix has zero effective range unless energy-dependent counterterms are introduced.
- For a finite cut-off with natural scattering length (a ~ r_e), the couplings scale as C0 ~ r_e/M + O(Λ⁻¹) and C2 ~ r_e²/(MΛ) + O(Λ⁰), with loop corrections suppressed by powers of r_eΛ.
- In the PDS scheme, the loop integrals give I_n(E) = -A₀ M μ p^{2n} with A₀ = 1/4π, and the couplings scale as C0 ~ 1/(Mμ) and C2 ~ r_e/(Mμ²), where μ is the renormalisation scale.
- The minimal subtraction scheme yields the K-matrix equal to the first Born approximation, with validity only for p < √(2/ar_e), which is too small for s-wave NN scattering.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of effective field theory for nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990) and examines the renormalisation problems identified by the Maryland group (1996-1997) and by Birse, McGovern and Richardson (1998). The paper contrasts the behaviour of cut-off regularisation with the PDS scheme proposed by Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998), and shows that while PDS yields a systematic expansion with a different power counting from Weinberg's, the omission of infrared divergent terms from the loop integrals means the apparent fixed point may not correspond to a well-defined low-energy effective theory. The renormalisation-group analysis suggests that for systems with unnaturally large scattering lengths, the fixed point is nonperturbative with no power counting when using cut-off regularisation.
