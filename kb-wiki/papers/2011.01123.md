# Decipher the $R_{Ψ_{m}}$ correlator in search for the chiral magnetic effect in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Background: The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is extensively studied in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. An azimuthal correlator called $R_{Ψ_{m}}$ was proposed to measure the CME. By observing the same $R_{Ψ_{2}}$ and $R_{Ψ_{3}}$ (convex) distributions from A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, by contrasting data and model as well as large and small systems and by event shape engineering (ESE), a recent preprint (arXiv:2006.04251v1) from STAR suggests that the $R_{Ψ_{m}}$ observable is sensitive to the CME signal and relatively insensitive to backgrounds, and their Au+Au data are inconsistent with known background contributions. Purpose: We examine those claims by studying the robustness of the $R_{Ψ_{m}}$ observable using AMPT as well as toy model simulations. We compare $R_{Ψ_{m}}$ to the more widely used $Δγ$ azimuthal correlator to identify their commonalities and differences. Methods: We use AMPT to simulate Au+Au, p+Au, and d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 \text{ GeV}$, and study the responses of $R_{Ψ_{m}}$ to anisotropic flow backgrounds in the model. We also use a toy model to simulate resonance flow background and input CME signal to investigate their effects in $R_{Ψ_{2}}$. Additionally we use the toy model to perform an ESE analysis to compare to STAR data as well as predict the degree of sensitivity of $R_{Ψ_{2}}$ to isobar collisions with the event statistics taken at RHIC. ...

## Key claim

The authors argue that the $R_{\Psi_{m}}$ correlator, contrary to claims by the STAR collaboration (arXiv:2006.04251v1), is sensitive to both CME signals and flow backgrounds, and that the $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ and $\Delta\gamma$ observables are essentially equivalent in their sensitivity and vulnerability to backgrounds.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (v2.25t4cu2) to simulate Au+Au, p+Au, and d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 \text{ GeV}$, generating 25.8 million 30-50% centrality Au+Au events and 300 million minimum bias events each for p+Au and d+Au. They also constructed a toy model incorporating primordial pions and $\rho$ meson decay daughters with input parameters from real STAR data, using it to perform event shape engineering (ESE) analysis and to study the response of $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ to varying elliptic flow ($v_2$) and CME signal ($a_1$) parameters.

## Key numbers

* In AMPT Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 \text{ GeV}$ (30-50% centrality, 25.8 million events), the $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ and $R_{\Psi_{3}}$ distributions are both concave and apparently different, contradicting STAR's claim of identical convex distributions.
* In AMPT p+Au and d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 \text{ GeV}$ (300 million MB events each, multiplicity range $18 \le dN/d\eta \le 22$), the $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ distributions are slightly concave, unlike STAR's flat or convex results.
* The toy model ESE analysis used 10.9 billion events for centrality 20-50% Au+Au collisions, showing that $\kappa$ (the squared inverse width of $R_{\Psi_{2}}$) depends on both $q_2$ and $v_2$.
* In toy model simulations with no CME signal, $\kappa$ shows a linear dependence on $v_2$; the $\kappa(v_2)$ slope is roughly constant across centrality 10-50%, while the $\Delta\gamma(v_2)$ slope multiplied by multiplicity is inversely proportional to multiplicity.

## Context

This work directly challenges the findings of the STAR collaboration (Magdy et al., arXiv:2006.04251v1), who claimed that $R_{\Psi_{m}}$ is sensitive to CME and relatively insensitive to backgrounds based on AMPT simulations showing identical convex $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ and $R_{\Psi_{3}}$ distributions. The authors' AMPT results contradict those findings and instead support earlier background studies by Bozek (2018) and Feng (2018), which showed concave $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ distributions from pure backgrounds. The paper demonstrates that $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ and the widely used $\Delta\gamma$ correlator have similar sensitivities to both CME signals and flow backgrounds, suggesting that $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ does not offer a significant advantage over existing methods for CME searches.
