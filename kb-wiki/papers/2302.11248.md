# Signatures of deuteron synthesis on the modified combinants in nuclear collisions

> The mechanism of deuteron production in heavy-ion collisions is shown to have a significant impact on the shape of the modified combinants of their multiplicity distribution. In light of this observation, an experimental study is proposed to tackle the long-standing problem of nuclei synthesis in hadronic and nuclear collisions. The proposed approach has the potential to provide stringent constraints on the models of deuteron synthesis.

## Key claim

The mechanism of deuteron production (thermal vs. coalescence models) leaves distinct and observable signatures in the modified combinants of the deuteron multiplicity distribution, and this approach can potentially distinguish between competing models that produce similar multiplicity distributions.

## Method

The authors constructed modified combinants \(C_j\) from the deuteron multiplicity distributions \(P(n_d)\) for three models: a Poisson distribution representing thermal production, and two coalescence models (Model-A with fully correlated proton-neutron fluctuations, Model-B with independent fluctuations) as described in PhysRevC.93.054906. They calculated \(\langle n \rangle C_j\) using the recurrence relation derived from the generating function formalism, and compared the oscillation patterns and amplitudes across the three scenarios for Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.6\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The mean deuteron multiplicity for all three distributions is \(\langle n_d \rangle = 15.05\).
- For Model A (correlated coalescence): \(\sigma^2/\langle n_d \rangle = 1.61142\), \(S\sigma = 2.21540\), \(\kappa\sigma^2 = 6.61484\).
- For Model B (independent coalescence): \(\sigma^2/\langle n_d \rangle = 1.31173\), \(S\sigma = 1.63349\), \(\kappa\sigma^2 = 3.33974\).
- For the Poisson (thermal) distribution, all three moment ratios (\(\sigma^2/\langle n_d \rangle\), \(S\sigma\), \(\kappa\sigma^2\)) equal unity.
- The period of \(\langle n \rangle C_j\) oscillations for Model A remains two; for Model B, slight variation in oscillation period is observed for small \(n\).

## Context

This work extends the method of modified combinants, previously applied to charged particle multiplicity distributions in pp collisions by Wilk (2017) and others, to the problem of deuteron synthesis in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the thermal and coalescence models reviewed in PhysRevC.93.054906 and Andronic (2019), and addresses the long-standing question of light nuclei production from AGS to LHC energies. The proposed experimental study of deuteron multiplicity distributions and their modified combinants at RHIC and LHC could provide stringent constraints on coalescence parameters and potentially falsify thermal Poissonian production, with implications for understanding matter-antimatter asymmetry.
