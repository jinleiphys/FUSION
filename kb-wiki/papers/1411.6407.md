# Vector meson spectral function and dilepton production rate in a hot and dense medium within an effective QCD approach

> The properties of the vector meson current-current correlation function and its spectral representation are investigated in details with and without isoscalar-vector interaction within the framework of effective QCD approach, namely Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and its Polyakov Loop extended version (PNJL), at finite temperature and finite density. The influence of the isoscalarvector interaction on the vector meson correlator is obtained using the ring resummation known as the Random Phase Approximation (RPA). The spectral as well as the correlation function in PNJL model show that the vector meson retains its bound property up to a moderate value of temperature above the phase transition. Using the vector meson spectral function we, for the first time, obtained the dilepton production rate from a hot and dense medium within the framework of PNJL model that takes into account the nonperturbative effect through the Polyakov Loop fields. The dilepton production rate in PNJL model is enhanced compared to NJL and Born rate in the deconfined phase due to the suppression of color degrees of freedom at moderate temperature. The presence of isoscalar-vector interaction further enhances the dileption rate over the Born rate in the low mass region. Further, we also have computed the Euclidean correlation function in vector channel and the conserved density fluctuation associated with temporal correlation function appropriate for a hot and dense medium. The dilepton rate and the Euclidean correlator are also compared with available lattice data and those quantities in PNJL model are found to agree well in certain domain.

## Key claim

The vector meson retains its bound property up to a moderate value of temperature above the phase transition within the PNJL model, and the dilepton production rate in the PNJL model is enhanced compared to the NJL and Born rates in the deconfined phase due to the suppression of color degrees of freedom at moderate temperature, with the isoscalar-vector interaction further enhancing the dilepton rate over the Born rate in the low mass region.

## Method

The authors computed the vector meson current-current correlation function and its spectral function using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and its Polyakov Loop extended version (PNJL) at finite temperature and finite density. The influence of the isoscalar-vector interaction was incorporated via ring resummation known as the Random Phase Approximation (RPA). The dilepton production rate was then obtained from the vector spectral function, and the Euclidean correlation function and conserved density fluctuation (quark number susceptibility) were also calculated.

## Key numbers

- The dilepton production rate in the PNJL model is enhanced compared to the NJL and Born rates in the deconfined phase due to suppression of color degrees of freedom at moderate temperature.
- The presence of isoscalar-vector interaction further enhances the dilepton rate over the Born rate in the low mass region.
- The resummed quark number susceptibility in ring approximation is given by chi_q^R(T, tilde_mu) = chi_q(T, tilde_mu) / (1 + G_V chi_q(T, tilde_mu)), where chi_q is the one-loop susceptibility.
- In the limit q -> 0 and tilde_mu = 0, the imaginary part of the temporal correlator becomes Im Pi_00(omega) = -2 pi T^2 omega delta(omega) for massless quarks (M_f = 0).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of mesonic spectral functions in the scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector channels using the NJL model (Kunihiro 1991, He 2003) by incorporating the Polyakov Loop to account for nonperturbative effects of the semi-QGP above T_c, following the PNJL model framework (Fukushima 2003, Ratti 2005). It provides the first calculation of the dilepton production rate within the PNJL model, enabling comparison with lattice QCD data (Ding 2010, Kaczmarek 2011, Francis 2011) and with perturbative approaches such as Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation (Braaten 1990, Karsch 2000). The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton measurements at RHIC, LHC, and future FAIR experiments.
