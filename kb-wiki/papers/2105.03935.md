# Low-rank matrix decompositions for ab initio nuclear structure

> The extension of ab initio quantum many-body theory to higher accuracy and larger systems is intrinsically limited by the handling of large data objects in form of wave-function expansions and/or many-body operators. In this work we present matrix factorization techniques as a systematically improvable and robust tool to significantly reduce the computational cost in many-body applications at the price of introducing a moderate decomposition error. We demonstrate the power of this approach for the nuclear two-body systems, for many-body perturbation theory calculations of symmetric nuclear matter, and for non-perturbative in-medium similarity renormalization group simulations of finite nuclei. Establishing low-rank expansions of chiral nuclear interactions offers possibilities to reformulate many-body methods in ways that take advantage of tensor factorization strategies.

## Key claim

Low-rank matrix decompositions (specifically truncated singular value decomposition) can significantly reduce computational storage and cost in ab initio nuclear many-body calculations by approximating chiral two-nucleon interactions, at the price of a moderate, systematically controllable decomposition error that does not spoil the reproduction of nuclear observables.

## Method

The authors performed singular value decomposition (SVD) on momentum-space partial-wave matrix elements of chiral two-nucleon (NN) potentials, truncating the decomposition at rank r_SVD by discarding the smallest singular values. The low-rank potentials were then used as input for deuteron ground-state calculations, two-nucleon scattering phase shifts via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) calculations of symmetric nuclear matter up to second order, and non-perturbative in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) calculations at the normal-ordered two-body level for closed-shell medium-mass nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the EMN 500 NN interaction, singular values fall below 10^{-4} to 10^{-5} at SVD rank r_SVD ≈ 20 across all partial-wave channels.
- Deuteron ground-state energy is reproduced at the sub-percent level at r_SVD ≈ 6, with differences below the keV level beyond r_SVD = 10.
- For NN scattering phase shifts at r_SVD = 5, the deviation from exact results is below Δδ < 1° in all partial-wave channels up to laboratory energies of 300 MeV.
- For symmetric nuclear matter at densities up to n = 0.2 fm^{-3}, the energy per particle is quantitatively reproduced at both Hartree-Fock and second-order MBPT levels when keeping r_SVD = 5 singular values.
- For medium-mass nuclei (oxygen and nickel isotopes) at r_SVD = 3, the decomposition error on ground-state energy per particle is about three percent; at r_SVD = 5, the error on the IMSRG(2) binding energy for the heaviest nucleus studied is 2.5 MeV total.
- A compression ratio of C^{100}_{5} ≈ 10 is achieved for matrix dimension N = 100 with r_SVD = 5 without significant loss of accuracy.

## Context

This work extends proof-of-principle tensor factorization applications by Tichai et al. (2019) that used compression on final single-particle matrix elements, by instead applying SVD directly to the initial momentum-space representation of chiral nuclear interactions from Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017), Jiang et al. (2020), and Gezerlis et al. (2013). The approach complements earlier uses of SVD in sensitivity studies of energy density functionals by Bertsch et al. (2005), for shell-model interactions by Brown and Richter (2006), and for Weinberg eigenvalue expansions by Bogner et al. (2006) and Hoppe et al. (2017). The results enable future application of tensor factorization to three-nucleon forces, potentially achieving compression ratios exceeding 10^2 for three-body partial-wave channels and enabling calculations of heavier nuclei with superior scaling.
