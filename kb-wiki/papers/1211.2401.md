# Soft Photons from transport and hydrodynamics at FAIR energies

> Direct photon spectra from uranium-uranium collisions at FAIR energies (E(lab) = 35 AGeV) are calculated within the hadronic Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics transport model. In this microscopic model, one can optionally include a macroscopic intermediate hydrodynamic phase. The hot and dense stage of the collision is then modeled by a hydrodynamical calculation. Photon emission from transport-hydro hybrid calculations is examined for purely hadronic matter and matter that has a cross-over phase transition and a critical end point to deconfined and chirally restored matter at high temperatures. We find the photon spectra in both scenarios to be dominated by Bremsstrahlung. Comparing flow of photons in both cases suggests a way to distinguish these two scenarios.

## Key claim

The direct photon spectra from U+U collisions at FAIR energies are dominated by Bremsstrahlung in both purely hadronic matter and matter with a phase transition to deconfined matter, and the total spectra are very similar between the two scenarios; however, the elliptic flow v₂ of direct photons differs by about 20% between the two scenarios, suggesting a way to distinguish them experimentally.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model in a hybrid mode that couples a microscopic transport description to an intermediate macroscopic hydrodynamic phase. Photon emission was calculated perturbatively from both the transport and hydrodynamic stages, using parametrizations from Turbide et al. for the hydrodynamic stage and Kapusta et al. for the transport stage, with Bremsstrahlung and QGP emission included only in the hydrodynamic description. Two equations of state were compared: a Hadron Gas EoS (HG-EoS) with only hadronic degrees of freedom, and a Deconfinement EoS (DE-EoS) that includes a first-order phase transition to chirally restored and deconfined matter with a critical end point.

## Key numbers

- Central U+U collisions at E_lab = 35 AGeV were simulated.
- Direct photon elliptic flow v₂ peaks about 20% higher in calculations with a phase transition (DE-EoS) compared to purely hadronic matter (HG-EoS).
- The dominant photon source in both scenarios is Bremsstrahlung (ππ → ππγ).
- In DE-EoS calculations, QGP emission nearly exactly compensates for the reduction in Bremsstrahlung photons, making the total direct photon spectra very similar between the two scenarios.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of direct photon production using UrQMD by Dumitru et al. and Bäuchle et al., and with hadron-string dynamics by Bratkovskaya et al., as well as hydrodynamic approaches by Turbide et al. and others. It also relates to experimental measurements by the WA98 Collaboration at CERN-SPS and the PHENIX Collaboration at BNL RHIC. The results enable future experiments at the FAIR facility to distinguish between purely hadronic matter and matter with a phase transition to deconfined matter by measuring direct photon elliptic flow, which shows a sizeable difference between the two scenarios even when the spectra are nearly identical.
