# Bonn Potential and Shell-Model Calculations for 206,205,204Pb

> The structure of the nuclei 206,205,204Pb is studied interms of shell model employing a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential. The energy spectra, binding energies and electromagnetic properties are calculated and compared with experiment. A very good overall agreement is obtained. This evidences the reliability of our realistic effective interaction and encourages use of modern realistic potentials in shell-model calculations for heavy-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential, when used to derive a realistic effective interaction via a G-matrix folded-diagram method, reproduces the experimental energy spectra, binding energies, and electromagnetic properties of 206,205,204Pb with very good overall agreement, though the abstract's claim of "very good overall agreement" glosses over specific discrepancies such as the sign of the magnetic moment of the 6- state in 206Pb and the sign of the quadrupole moment of the 2+ state in 204Pb.

## Method

The authors assumed 208Pb as a closed core and treated valence neutron holes in six single-hole orbits (2p1/2, 1f5/2, 2p3/2, 0i13/2, 1f7/2, 0h9/2) with experimental single-hole energies from 207Pb. A hole-hole effective interaction was derived from the Bonn A free NN potential using a G-matrix formalism with a Pauli exclusion operator Q2, including renormalizations from core polarization and folded diagrams, with the Q-box composed of G-matrix diagrams through second order in G. Shell-model calculations were performed for 206Pb (two holes), 205Pb (three holes), and 204Pb (four holes), and the results were compared with experimental energy spectra, binding energies, magnetic moments, and electric multipole transition rates.

## Key numbers

- Single-hole energy spacings in 207Pb (in MeV): epsilon(f5/2)-epsilon(p1/2)=0.570, epsilon(p3/2)-epsilon(p1/2)=0.898, epsilon(i13/2)-epsilon(p1/2)=1.633, epsilon(f7/2)-epsilon(p1/2)=2.340, epsilon(h9/2)-epsilon(p1/2)=3.414
- Shell-model oscillator hbar omega = 6.88 MeV for A=208
- RMS deviation sigma for 206Pb: 207 keV; for 204Pb: 216 keV; for 205Pb: 74 keV (excluding three unidentifiable states)
- Discrepancy for the 2+1 states in 206Pb and 204Pb: about 40 keV
- Ground-state binding energies relative to 208Pb: Eb(206Pb)=-14.240 MeV (experimental -14.106(6) MeV), Eb(205Pb)=-22.147 MeV (experimental -22.194(6) MeV), Eb(204Pb)=-28.927 MeV (experimental -28.925(6) MeV)
- Effective neutron hole charge e_n^eff = 0.82 e, obtained from observed B(E2; 5/2- -> 1/2-) in 207Pb
- Calculated B(M1; 6- -> 7-) in 206Pb: 0.132 W.u. (experimental 0.045(13) W.u.)
- Largest discrepancy for high-spin states in 204Pb: about 400 keV for the 16+2 state

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous realistic shell-model calculations using the Bonn A potential for medium-mass nuclei around 100Sn and 132Sn (Andreozzi 1996, Andreozzi 1997, Covello 1997) to the heavy-mass lead region (A~208). It contrasts with earlier realistic calculations by McGrory and Kuo (1975) that used the Hamada-Johnston potential with only bare interaction and core polarization diagrams, where bubble diagram matrix elements were multiplied by an empirical constant of 0.75. The results demonstrate that modern realistic NN potentials can successfully describe heavy-mass nuclei without phenomenological adjustments, enabling future shell-model studies of other heavy nuclei near closed shells.
