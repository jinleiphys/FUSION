# Dissipation, hydrodynamics and the fireball

> We investigate the hydrodynamics of the QCD plasma using dimensionless numbers built from the thermodynamics and transport theory of the plasma and characteristic dimensions of the fireball produced in heavy-ion collisions. We find that by the usual measures, dissipation is strong, and the fireball is on the borderline of equilibrium. As a result, the system is richer in phenomena than ideal hydrodynamics would predict. One general implication is that it may be possible to get a direct view of the QCD plasma phase rather than having to infer its existence indirectly from signals that come from the freezeout isotherm after the fireball has cooled into the hadronic phase.

## Key claim

The QCD plasma fireball produced in heavy-ion collisions is on the borderline of equilibrium, meaning dissipation is strong enough that ideal hydrodynamics is quantitatively incorrect but may not be terribly wrong in some ways; the system is richer in phenomena than ideal hydrodynamics would predict, and it may be possible to observe the QCD plasma phase directly rather than inferring it indirectly from freezeout signals.

## Method

The author constructs dimensionless numbers from QCD thermodynamics and transport theory, specifically the liquidity (mean free time times entropy density to the one-third power), Reynolds number, Knudsen number, Mach number, and sound attenuation length, using lattice QCD results, AdS/CFT bounds, and RHIC data to characterize the fireball's dissipative properties. These numbers are evaluated at temperatures around 2T_c (approximately 350 MeV) and at chemical freezeout (approximately 175 MeV) to assess the validity of ideal hydrodynamics and the stability of Bjorken flow under dissipation.

## Key numbers

- Liquidity ℓ = 0.8 at 2T_c, computed from mean free time τ = 0.3 fm and entropy density s/T³ = 5.7 ± 0.2
- Reynolds number upper bound R < 18 using freezeout values (r = 7 fm, T ≈ 175 MeV); R ≈ 14 when extrapolated back to 2T_c using Bjorken flow
- Knudsen number K = λ/r ≈ 0.032–0.055, corresponding to isotherm smearing over 3–6% of the fireball radius
- Sound attenuation length Γ_s T ≥ 1/(3π) from the AdS/CFT bound; at 2T_c, an e-fold decrease in sound intensity occurs over less than 0.4 fm
- Entropy production ratio s'/s ≈ 0.0014 near freezeout using η/s = 1/(4π), T = 150 MeV, r = 7 fm
- Bulk-to-shear viscosity ratio ζ/η = 0.45 at T = 0.9T_c, 0.35 at 1.1T_c, and 0.1 at 1.5T_c, becoming negligible at 2T_c
- Pressure anisotropy A = 1.37 when Bjorken flow is propagated back to 2T_c; A = 1.28 using freezeout values

## Context

This work extends the emerging concordance between AdS/CFT estimates (Son), lattice gauge theory (Gupta, Nakamura), and RHIC data analysis (Teaney) that the QCD plasma has small transport coefficients. The analysis contradicts the notion of an "ideal liquid" by showing that Reynolds numbers are large enough to cause significant dissipation, yet Bjorken flow remains stable. The results enable a program of using transport equations rather than ideal hydrodynamics to extract QGP properties, and suggest that elliptic flow v₂ must be at least 20% smaller than ideal hydro predictions (Molnar), requiring re-examination of the v₂ vs. s trend (NA49) for extracting η/s.
