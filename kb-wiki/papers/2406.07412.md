# Helium-4 gravitational form factors: exchange currents

> We evaluate the leading exchange corrections to the Helium-4 gravitational form factors (GFFs) upto momenta of the order of the nucleon mass. We use both the K-harmonic method with simple pair nucleon potential, and a Jastrow trial function using the Argonne $v_{14}$ potential, to evaluate the Helium-4 GFFs. The exchange current contributions include the pair interaction, plus the seagull and the pion exchange interactions, modulo the recoil corrections. To estimate the off-shellness of the pion nucleon coupling in this momenta range, we discuss the results using either the pseudo-scalar (PS) or pseudo-vector (PV) pion-nucleon couplings. When the PV coupling is used, the pair diagram contribution is higher order in the non relativistic expansion. The results for the Helium-4 A-GFF are comparable to those given by the impulse approximation, especially for the PS coupling using both the K-Harmonic method and variational method. The exchange current contributions with the PS coupling for the charge form factor of Helium-4, yield better agreement with the existing data over a broad range of momenta, especially when the Argonne $v_{14}$ potential including the D-wave admixture is used.

## Key claim

The leading exchange current corrections to Helium-4 gravitational form factors (GFFs) are evaluated up to momenta of the order of the nucleon mass, with the results depending significantly on whether pseudo-scalar (PS) or pseudo-vector (PV) pion-nucleon coupling is used; the exchange contributions with PS coupling yield better agreement with existing charge form factor data, especially when the Argonne v14 potential including D-wave admixture is employed.

## Method

The authors compute the gravitational form factors of Helium-4 using two complementary nuclear wavefunction approaches: the K-harmonic method with a simple pair nucleon potential (Reid potential, no D-wave), and a Jastrow variational wavefunction using the Argonne v14 potential (including D-wave admixture). Exchange current contributions from one-pion exchange are evaluated for four diagram types: pair interaction, seagull, pion exchange, and recoil plus wavefunction renormalization, using both pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector pion-nucleon couplings in a non-relativistic reduction.

## Key numbers

* The K-harmonic method wavefunction reproduces the correct binding energy and charge radius of Helium-4, and describes the empirical charge form factor up to momenta of order half the nucleon mass (approximately 470 MeV).
* The Coulomb potential used in the K-harmonic method is V_C(R) = 2.23 MeV fm / R.
* The pair nucleon potential used in the K-harmonic method is V(r) = +1310.21 exp[-(r/0.7)^2] - 467.97 exp[-(r/1.16)^2] (units not specified but context implies MeV).
* The spin-isospin matrix element for the pair contribution in the 0++ Helium-4 ground state is ⟨Φ|τ1·τ2 σ1·σ2|Φ⟩ = -315/(32π^4).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous impulse approximation analysis of Helium-4 GFFs (He 2023) and their exchange current analysis for the deuteron (He 2024). It addresses the known importance of meson exchange currents for electromagnetic form factors established by Hockert (1973), Chemtob (1974), Kloet (1973), and Jackson (1975). The results enable comparison with future experimental extractions of nuclear gravitational form factors, complementing recent lattice QCD calculations of nucleon GFFs by Hackett (2023) and Wang (2024), and empirical estimates from JLab charmonium photoproduction by GlueX (2019) and Duran (2022).
