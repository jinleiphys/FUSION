# The systematic study of the influence of neutron excess on the fusion cross sections using different proximity-type potentials

> Using different types of proximity potentials, we have examined the trend of variations of barrier characteristics (barrier height and its position) as well as fusion cross sections for 50 isotopic systems including various collisions of C, O, Mg, Si, S, Ca, Ar, Ti and Ni nuclei with $1\leq N/Z < 1.6$ condition for compound systems. The results of our studies reveal that the relationships between increase of barrier positions and decrease of barrier heights are both linear with increase of $N/Z$ ratio. Moreover, fusion cross sections also enhance linearly with increase of this ratio.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for 50 isotopic fusion systems with compound-nucleus N/Z ratios between 1 and 1.6, the barrier positions increase linearly and barrier heights decrease linearly with increasing N/Z ratio, and fusion cross sections also increase linearly with N/Z. The hedge is that these linear relationships are demonstrated using four specific proximity-type potentials (AW 95, Bass 80, Prox. DP, and Prox. 2010), and the linear parametrizations are given for the percentage changes relative to the N=Z case, not for absolute values.

## Method

The authors calculated the total interaction potential (Coulomb plus nuclear) for 50 isotopic systems across 13 groups of colliding nuclei (C+Si, O+Mg, O+Si, Si+Si, Mg+S, Si+Ni, Ca+Ca, S+Ni, Ar+Ni, Ca+Ni, Ni+Ni, Ti+Ni, Ca+Ti) using four proximity-type nuclear potentials: AW 95, Bass 80, Prox. DP, and Prox. 2010. Barrier heights and positions were extracted from the total potential by finding the maximum. Fusion cross sections were computed using the sharp cutoff formula at energies above the barrier (E_cm = 1.125 V_B^0 and 1.375 V_B^0). The percentage differences of barrier height, barrier position, and fusion cross section relative to the N=Z case were then plotted against (N/Z - 1) and fitted with linear functions.

## Context

This work extends the systematic studies of Puri et al. (2004) who first examined isotopic dependence of fusion cross sections using the Skyrme energy density model for Ca+Ca, Ca+Ni, and Ni+Ni systems with 1 ≤ N/Z ≤ 2, finding linear variations. It also builds on later work by Puri and Dhiman (2008, 2010) who found non-linear (second-order) variations of barrier heights and positions but linear variations of fusion cross sections for Ca and Ni isotopes with 0.5 ≤ N/Z ≤ 1. The present study covers a wider range of isotopic systems (13 groups instead of 3) and uses four proximity-type potentials rather than the Skyrme energy density model, confirming linear trends for all quantities over the range 1 ≤ N/Z < 1.6. The results enable future work using dynamic approaches such as the improved quantum molecular dynamics model of Wang et al. to examine how dynamic corrections affect the barrier near the Coulomb barrier.
