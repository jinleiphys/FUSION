# Inclusive heavy meson photoproduction in $pPb$ and $PbPb$ collisions

> The inclusive photoproduction of heavy mesons in ultraperipheral $pPb$ and $PbPb$ collisions at the LHC energies is investigated considering the color dipole $S$-matrix formalism and assuming distinct models for the unintegrated gluon distribution, based on different assumptions for the description of the QCD dynamics. In particular, predictions for the $B^0$-meson photoproduction are presented here for the first time. The study of the $D^0$-meson photoproduction is revisited by estimating the impact of the treatment of the heavy charm fragmentation on the predictions and extended for $pPb$ collisions. Moreover, the contribution associated with the $b \rightarrow D^0$ transition is estimated. Our results indicate that a future experimental analysis of the heavy meson photoproduction will provide important constrains on the description of the hadronic structure at high energies.

# Inclusive heavy meson photoproduction in pPb and PbPb collisions

## Key claim
The inclusive photoproduction of D0 and B0 mesons in ultraperipheral pPb and PbPb collisions at LHC energies can provide important constraints on the description of hadronic structure at high energies, but the current data cannot yet discriminate between different models of the unintegrated gluon distribution (UGD) or the magnitude of electromagnetic dissociation effects. Predictions for B0-meson photoproduction are presented for the first time, and the study indicates that future experimental analysis of this channel is feasible.

## Method
The authors employ the color dipole S-matrix formalism to compute differential cross sections for inclusive heavy meson photoproduction, factorizing the process into an effective nuclear photon flux and a photon-hadron interaction cross section. The calculations use distinct models for the unintegrated gluon distribution of the target: CCFM (linear evolution), EPPS (linear with nuclear effects), rcBK (nonlinear evolution), and KS linear/nonlinear solutions. For fragmentation, the Peterson model (scale-independent) is compared with the KKSS parametrization (DGLAP-evolved), and the KKSS model is adopted for the main results. The analysis includes the effect of electromagnetic dissociation of the photon-emitting nucleus through a survival probability factor P(b).

## Key numbers
- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.36 TeV, total cross sections for D0 photoproduction range from 31.11 mb (rcBK) to 225.1 mb (CCFM) for central rapidity (-2.0 ≤ Y ≤ +2.0), and from 1.867 mb to 14.69 mb for forward rapidity (2.0 ≤ Y ≤ 4.5)
- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.36 TeV, total cross sections for B0 photoproduction range from 0.284 mb (rcBK) to 2.138 mb (CCFM) for central rapidity, and from 0.0046 mb to 0.0310 mb for forward rapidity
- For pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.1 TeV, total cross sections for D0 photoproduction range from 1.33 mb (rcBK) to 2.48 mb (KS linear) for central rapidity, and from 0.236 mb to 0.656 mb for forward rapidity
- For pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.1 TeV, total cross sections for B0 photoproduction range from 0.0149 mb (rcBK) to 0.0261 mb (CCFM) for central rapidity, and from 0.00162 mb to 0.00286 mb for forward rapidity
- Quark masses assumed: m_c = 1.4 GeV, m_b = 4.5 GeV
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: epsilon_c = 0.05, epsilon_b = 0.006, n(D0) = 0.308377, n(B0) = 0.0528288
- Electromagnetic dissociation parameter S = (17.4 fm)^2 used for PbPb calculations

## Context
This work extends the analysis of inclusive D0 photoproduction in PbPb collisions previously performed by Goncalves et al. (2025) by incorporating DGLAP-evolved fragmentation functions (KKSS) instead of the scale-independent Peterson model, and presents the first predictions for B0 photoproduction and for pPb collisions. The study is motivated by recent CMS data (CMS Collaboration, 2025) and preliminary ALICE data (Nese et al., 2025) for D0 photoproduction. The results indicate that future experimental data can provide constraints complementary to those from exclusive processes, and that the contribution from b → D0 transitions is small compared to direct charm fragmentation, consistent with findings by Bolzoni et al. (2013).
