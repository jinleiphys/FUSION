# Neutral Color Superconductivity Including Inhomogeneous Phases at Finite Temperature

> We investigate neutral quark matter with homogeneous and inhomogeneous color condensates at finite temperature in the frame of an extended NJL model. By calculating the Meissner masses squared and gap susceptibility, the uniform color superconductor is stable only in a temperature window close to the critical temperature and becomes unstable against LOFF phase, mixed phase and gluonic phase at low temperatures. The introduction of the inhomogeneous phases leads to disappearance of the strange intermediate temperature 2SC/g2SC and changes the phase diagram of neutral dense quark matter significantly.

## Key claim

The uniform two-flavor color superconducting (2SC/g2SC) phase is stable against chromomagnetic instability and density fluctuations only in a temperature window close to the critical temperature; at low temperatures it becomes unstable toward inhomogeneous phases (LOFF, mixed, or gluonic), and the inclusion of these inhomogeneous phases eliminates the strange intermediate-temperature 2SC/g2SC region from the phase diagram.

## Method

The authors use an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a diquark interaction channel, imposing color and electric charge neutrality via chemical potentials \(\mu_8\) and \(\mu_e\). They compute the Meissner masses squared for the 4–7th and 8th gluons and the gap susceptibility \(\kappa_\Delta\) at finite temperature, and then construct the single-plane-wave LOFF phase and the gluonic phase to map the stable regions of the phase diagram.

## Key numbers

* For the toy two-species model with coupling \(G=0.095G_0\) (\(G_0=4\pi^2/\Lambda^2\)), the gapless state (\(\delta\mu > \Delta\)) appears at low and high temperature but disappears at intermediate temperature; for \(G=0.073G_0\) superfluidity exists only at intermediate temperature.
* At \(\mu=400\) MeV in the NJL model, the critical coupling ratio \(\eta_c = G_D/G_S \simeq 0.63\) below which the uniform color superconducting phase disappears.
* For weak coupling \(\eta=0.75, 0.70, 0.66\) at \(\mu=400\) MeV, the Meissner mass squared \(m_4^2\) for the 4–7th gluons is negative for \(0<T<T_4\) and positive for \(T_4<T<T_c\); \(m_8^2\) for the 8th gluon is negative for \(0<T<T_8\) and positive for \(T_8<T<T_c\).
* For strong coupling \(\eta>0.8\) at \(\mu=400\) MeV, the 8th gluon is free from chromomagnetic instability at all temperatures, but the 4–7th gluons have negative Meissner mass squared for \(0<T<T_4\).
* The gap susceptibility \(\kappa_\Delta\) (scaled by \(\kappa_0=2\bar{\mu}^2/\pi^2\)) is positive in a temperature window near \(T_c\) for all coupling values studied, indicating stability against phase separation in that window.

## Context

This work extends the instability analysis of uniform color superconductivity, previously studied at zero temperature by Huang (2004), Casalbuoni (2004), and Alford (2004), to finite temperature, showing that the chromomagnetic instability (negative Meissner mass squared) and the density-fluctuation instability (negative gap susceptibility) are both cured near the critical temperature. The results contradict the earlier expectation that the gapless 2SC (g2SC) phase could be stable at low temperature under charge neutrality, and instead support the scenario where inhomogeneous phases (LOFF, mixed, gluonic) dominate at low \(T\), as argued by Giannakis (2005) and Gorbar (2005). The findings enable a more complete phase diagram for neutral dense quark matter relevant to neutron-star cores.
