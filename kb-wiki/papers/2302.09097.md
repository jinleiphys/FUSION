# Third-order relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics from the method of moments

> We derive a linearly causal and stable third-order relativistic fluid-dynamical theory from the Boltzmann equation using the method of moments. For this purpose, we demonstrate that such theory must include novel degrees of freedom, corresponding to irreducible tensors of rank 3 and 4. The equations of motion derived in this work are compared with numerical solutions of the Boltzmann equation, considering an ultrarelativistic, classical gas in the highly symmetric Bjorken flow scenario. These solutions are shown to be in good agreement for a wide range of values of shear viscosity and initial temperatures.

## Key claim

The authors derive a linearly causal and stable third-order relativistic fluid-dynamical theory from the Boltzmann equation using the method of moments, demonstrating that such a theory must include novel degrees of freedom corresponding to irreducible tensors of rank 3 and 4, which were neglected in previous second-order formulations.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann equation and expand the nonequilibrium part of the single-particle distribution function using a complete basis of irreducible tensors constructed from the 4-momenta, following the method of moments. They derive equations of motion for irreducible moments up to rank 4, including for the first time the equations for rank 3 and rank 4 moments. A new minimal truncation scheme is proposed that increases the number of independent fields from 14 (the Israel-Stewart 14-moment approximation) to 30, incorporating the new rank-3 and rank-4 tensors as dynamical variables. The resulting third-order fluid-dynamical equations are then compared with numerical solutions of the Boltzmann equation in the highly symmetric Bjorken flow scenario for an ultrarelativistic, classical gas.

## Key numbers

- The new minimal truncation scheme increases the number of independent fields from 14 to 30, with each new tensor of rank ℓ contributing 2ℓ+1 degrees of freedom.
- The equations of motion for irreducible moments of rank 3 and 4 are derived; these equations contain no terms of first order in Knudsen number, with the dominant contribution being at least of second order.
- The solutions of the third-order fluid-dynamical formulation are shown to be in good agreement with solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a wide range of values of shear viscosity and initial temperatures in the Bjorken flow scenario.

## Context

This work extends the second-order Israel-Stewart theory (1970s) and the 14-moment approximation developed by Israel and Stewart, as well as the method-of-moments framework of Denicol, Niemi, Molnar, and Rischke (DNMR). It addresses the recently discovered linear acausality and instability of the third-order formalism developed by El, Muronga, and others, which was based on a Chapman-Enskog-inspired approach. The ad hoc modification proposed by Brito and Denicol (2023) to fix that instability is here replaced by a more fundamental derivation from kinetic theory. This work enables the construction of causal and stable third-order relativistic fluid dynamics applicable to the description of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC.
