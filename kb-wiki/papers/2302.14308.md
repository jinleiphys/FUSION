# Effects of $N(2080){3/2}^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$ molecules on $K^\ast ÎŁ$ photoproduction

> In the present work, we re-analyze the available data for $Îłp\to K^{\ast +}ÎŁ^0$ and $Îłp \to K^{\ast 0}ÎŁ^+$ by considering the contributions from the $N(2080){3/2}^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$ molecules instead of any nucleon resonances in the $s$ channel, where the $N(2080)3/2^-$ was proposed to be a $K^\ast ÎŁ$ molecule as the strange partner of the $P_c^+(4457)$ hadronic molecular state, and the $N(2270)3/2^-$ was assumed to be a $K^*ÎŁ^*$ molecule as the strange partner of the $\bar{D}^\ast ÎŁ^\ast_c$ bound states that are predicated as members in the same heavy-quark spin symmetry multiplet as the $P_c$ states. It turns out that all the available cross-section data can be well reproduced, indicating that the molecular structures of the possible $N(2080){3/2}^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$ states are compatible with the available data for $K^\astÎŁ$ photoproduction reactions. Further analysis shows that for both $Îłp\to K^{\ast +}ÎŁ^0$ and $Îłp \to K^{\ast 0}ÎŁ^+$ reactions, the $N(2080){3/2}^-$ exchange provides dominant contributions to the cross-sections in the near-threshold energy region, and significant contributions from the $N(2270)3/2^-$ exchange to the cross-sections in the higher energy region are also found. Predictions of the beam asymmetry $ÎŁ$, target asymmetry $T$, and recoil baryon asymmetry $P$ are presented and compared with those from our previous work. Measurements of the data on these observables are called on to further constrain the reaction mechanisms of $K^\astÎŁ$ photoproduction reactions and to verify the molecular scenario of the $N(2080){3/2}^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$ states.

## Key claim

The available cross-section data for $\gamma p\to K^{\ast +}\Sigma^0$ and $\gamma p \to K^{\ast 0}\Sigma^+$ can be well reproduced by considering the $N(2080){3/2}^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$ states as hadronic molecules ($K^\ast\Sigma$ and $K^\ast\Sigma^\ast$ bound states, respectively) rather than conventional nucleon resonances in the $s$ channel, but the molecular interpretation is conditional on future measurements of polarization observables (beam asymmetry $\Sigma$, target asymmetry $T$, and recoil baryon asymmetry $P$) to further constrain the reaction mechanisms.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach to construct the reaction amplitudes for $\gamma p\to K^{\ast +}\Sigma^0$ and $\gamma p \to K^{\ast 0}\Sigma^+$, including $s$-channel $N$, $\Delta$, $N(2080){3/2}^-$, and $N(2270)3/2^-$ exchanges; $t$-channel $K$, $\kappa$, and $K^\ast$ exchanges; $u$-channel $\Sigma$, $\Lambda$, and $\Sigma^\ast$ exchanges; and a generalized contact term. The $N(2080){3/2}^-$ coupling constant was fixed model-independently using the Weinberg compositeness criterion with a binding energy of 6 MeV relative to the $K^\ast\Sigma$ threshold, while the $N(2270)3/2^-$ coupling constant multiplied by the corresponding electromagnetic coupling was fitted to the data. The widths of both molecular states and additional electromagnetic coupling parameters were treated as free fit parameters.

## Key numbers

- $N(2080){3/2}^-$ mass taken as $M_R = 2080$ MeV, with a fitted width of $\Gamma_{N(2080)} = 70.1 \pm 9.7$ MeV
- $N(2270)3/2^-$ mass taken as $M_R = 2270$ MeV, with a fitted width of $\Gamma_{N(2270)} = 361.3 \pm 8.9$ MeV
- Coupling constant $g_{K^\ast\Sigma R} = 1.72$ for $N(2080){3/2}^-$ as a $K^\ast\Sigma$ molecule, derived from the Weinberg compositeness criterion
- Fitted electromagnetic coupling $g^{(1)}_{N(2080)N\gamma} = -0.12 \pm 0.04$
- Fitted product $g^{(1)}_{N(2270)N\gamma} \, g_{K^\ast\Sigma N(2270)} = 0.28 \pm 0.06$
- Chi-squared per data point $\chi^2 = 1.648$, indicating good fitting quality
- Cutoff parameters: $\Lambda_R = 1607 \pm 118$ MeV, $\Lambda_s = 1862 \pm 31$ MeV, $\Lambda_t = 1064 \pm 26$ MeV, $\Lambda_u = 715 \pm 35$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis of Wang et al. (2018) (Phys. Rev. C 98, 045209), which described $K^\ast\Sigma$ photoproduction data using an $s$-channel $\Delta(1905){5/2}^+$ resonance exchange. The present study replaces that resonance with the $N(2080){3/2}^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$ molecular states, proposed as strange partners of the hidden-charm $P_c$ pentaquark states discovered by LHCb (2015, 2019). The $N(2080){3/2}^-$ was previously suggested as a $K^\ast\Sigma$ molecule by He (2017) and Lin et al. (2018), while the $N(2270)3/2^-$ as a $K^\ast\Sigma^\ast$ molecule follows from heavy-quark spin symmetry predictions of $\bar{D}^\ast\Sigma^\ast_c$ bound states by Liu et al. (2019). The results enable future experimental tests of the molecular scenario through measurements of single-spin observables (beam asymmetry $\Sigma$, target asymmetry $T$, recoil baryon asymmetry $P$), which are predicted to differ from those of the conventional resonance model.
