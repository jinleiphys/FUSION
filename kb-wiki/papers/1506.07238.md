# Impurity effects of $Λ$ particle on the 2$α$ cluster states of $^{9}$Be and $^{10}$Be

> The low-lying structure of $^{10}_Λ$Be and $^{11}_Λ$Be are investigated within the framework of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. We focus on the modifications of the excitation spectra and dynamical changes of the 2$α$ cluster structure caused by a $Λ$ particle as an impurity in these hypernuclei. It is found that the excitation energies of well-pronounced cluster states are largely shifted up by the addition of a $Λ$ particle. Furthermore, we also find that the 2$α$ cluster structure is significantly changed in the excited states, whereas it is almost unchanged in the ground states.

## Key claim

The excitation energies of well-developed 2α cluster states in ⁹Be and ¹⁰Be are shifted upward by 1.5–3.1 MeV when a Λ particle is added, but the magnitude of this shift depends on the degree of clustering in the core state and the resulting difference in Λ binding energy.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for hypernuclei (HyperAMD) combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to calculate the low-lying states of ¹⁰_ΛBe and ¹¹_ΛBe. The Gogny D1S interaction was used for nucleon-nucleon forces, and three different YN G-matrix interactions (ND, NSC97f, ESC08c) derived from Nijmegen potentials were employed for the ΛN interaction, with the spin-orbit part always taken from ESC08c. The nuclear quadrupole deformation parameter β was constrained during variational calculations, and angular momentum projection was performed before GCM superposition.

## Key numbers

- Λ binding energy B_Λ for the ⁹Be(3/2⁻) ⊗ Λ ground state doublet in ¹⁰_ΛBe: 9.54 MeV (centroid), overestimating observed values of 9.11 ± 0.22 MeV and 8.55 ± 0.18 MeV
- B_Λ for the ⁹Be(1/2⁺) ⊗ Λ excited state doublet in ¹⁰_ΛBe: 7.63 MeV (centroid), which is 1.9 MeV smaller than the ground state doublet
- B_Λ for the ¹⁰Be(0⁺₁) ⊗ Λ ground state in ¹¹_ΛBe: 10.70 MeV
- B_Λ for the ¹⁰Be(0⁺₂) ⊗ Λ excited state in ¹¹_ΛBe: 7.56 MeV, which is 3.1 MeV smaller than the ground state
- B_Λ for the ¹⁰Be(1⁻) ⊗ Λ doublet in ¹¹_ΛBe: 8.84 MeV (centroid), which is 1.9 MeV smaller than the ground state
- Reduction of inter-cluster distance r_{α-α} in the ⁹Be(1/2⁺) ⊗ Λ state of ¹⁰_ΛBe: about 7%, compared to about 5% in the ground state
- Reduction of r_{α-α} in the ¹⁰Be(0⁺₂) ⊗ Λ and ¹⁰Be(1⁻) ⊗ Λ states of ¹¹_ΛBe: about 8%, while the ground state r_{α-α} is almost unchanged
- Intra-band B(E2) for the K^π = 0⁺₂ band in ¹¹_ΛBe: 29 e²fm⁴ (3/2⁺₃ → 1/2⁺₂) and 25 e²fm⁴ (5/2⁺₃ → 1/2⁺₂), reduced from 119 e²fm⁴ in the corresponding ¹⁰Be band
- Intra-band B(E2) for the K^π = 0⁺₁ band in ¹¹_ΛBe: 8 e²fm⁴, reduced from 19 e²fm⁴ in ¹⁰Be

## Context

This work extends previous studies of impurity effects in hypernuclei, particularly the reduction of inter-cluster distances observed in ⁷_ΛLi (α + d clusters) and ²¹_ΛNe (α + ¹⁶O clusters) by earlier authors (1985, 1984, 2011). It also builds on the authors' prior work on ¹²_ΛBe (2015), where a smaller shrinkage was found. The results contradict the expectation that the ¹/2⁺ state of ⁹Be (a Hoyle-analogue state with dilute 2α + n structure) would remain unbound when a Λ is added; instead, the ⁹Be(1/2⁺) ⊗ Λ states in ¹⁰_ΛBe are bound despite the large shift up. The findings enable future experimental searches for these hypernuclear states, which could provide spectroscopic information on the controversial nature of the ⁹Be(1/2⁺) state (debated as virtual state by Descouvemont (1999) and others versus three-body resonance by Garrido (2010) and others).
