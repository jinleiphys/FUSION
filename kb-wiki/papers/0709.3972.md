# $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni fusion reaction calculated with the density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock formalism

> We study fusion reactions of the $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni system using the density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) formalism. In this formalism the fusion barriers are directly obtained from TDHF dynamics. In addition, we incorporate the entrance channel alignments of the slightly deformed (oblate) $^{64}$Ni nuclei due to dynamical Coulomb excitation. We show that alignment leads to a fusion barrier distribution and alters the naive picture for defining which energies are actually sub-barrier. We also show that core polarization effects could play a significant role in fusion cross section calculations.

## Key claim

The density-constrained TDHF method, which contains no free parameters, accurately reproduces measured fusion cross sections for the 64Ni+64Ni system at most energies, but at deep sub-barrier energies (E_cm ≤ 87 MeV) the agreement requires accounting for core polarization effects where the nuclear core orientation differs from the total nuclear density.

## Method

The authors performed unrestricted three-dimensional TDHF calculations for the 64Ni+64Ni system using the Skyrme SLy5 effective interaction, then applied a density constraint to extract the ion-ion interaction potential V(R,β1,β2) for various orientations of the two oblate nuclei. They incorporated dynamical alignment of the deformed nuclei due to Coulomb excitation using a semiclassical calculation of collective levels, and computed fusion cross sections by averaging over orientation angles with the alignment probability distribution. The Schrödinger equation was integrated with incoming wave boundary conditions to obtain barrier penetrabilities for each orientation.

## Key numbers

- The 64Ni nucleus has a quadrupole moment of -0.45 b (oblate deformation) with the SLy5 Skyrme force
- The lowest potential barrier corresponds to the β1=β2=90° orientation at about E_cm = 92 MeV
- Experimental data were measured down to the 10 nb level for the 64Ni+64Ni system
- The 2+ state in 64Ni is at 1.346 MeV, the 4+ state at 2.610 MeV, and the M(E2, 0+ → 2+) transition matrix element is -27.0 e·fm²
- TDHF calculations were initialized at E_cm = 98 MeV and separation R(t0) = 16 fm
- The outer turning point for the β1=β2=90° orientation at E_cm ≤ 86 MeV occurs at R = 13.1 fm, the inner turning point at R = 11.0 fm, and the potential minimum at R = 8.0 fm
- The SIII interaction has a nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient of 356 MeV, compared to modern Skyrme parametrizations around 230 MeV

## Context

This work extends the density-constrained TDHF method previously developed by the same authors (Umar and Oberacker, 2006) for calculating heavy-ion interaction potentials, and applies it to the 64Ni+64Ni system where experimental data from Jiang et al. (2004) showed fusion hindrance at extreme sub-barrier energies. Earlier coupled-channels calculations by Esbensen et al. (2004, 2005) failed to reproduce the data at extreme sub-barrier energies, while later work by Esbensen (2006) attributed hindrance to nuclear incompressibility effects and Ichikawa et al. (2007) proposed a two-step model with neck formation. The present results enable microscopic investigation of core polarization effects and the role of the effective interaction in deep sub-barrier fusion.
