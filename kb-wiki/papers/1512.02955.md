# Faddeev-type calculation of (d,n) transfer reactions in three-body nuclear systems

> Exact Faddeev-type three-body equations are applied to the study of the proton transfer reactions $(d,n)$ in the system consisting of a nuclear core and two nucleons. The integral equations for the three-body transition operators are solved in the momentum-space framework including the Coulomb interaction via the screening and renormalization method. For a weakly bound final nucleus the calculation of the $(d,n)$ reaction is more demanding in terms of the screening radius as compared to the $(d,p)$ reaction. Well converged differential cross section results are obtained for $^{7}{Be}(d,n)^{8}{B}$, $^{12}{C}(d,n)^{13}{N}$, and $^{16}{O}(d,n)^{17}{F}$ reactions. A comparison with the corresponding $(d,p)$ reactions is made. The calculations fail to reproduce the shape of the angular distribution for reactions on $^{12}{C}$ but provide quite successful description for reactions on $^{16}{O}$, especially for the transfer to the $^{17}{F}$ excited state $1/2^+$ when using a nonlocal optical potential.

## Key claim

The paper extends the rigorous Faddeev/AGS three-body framework to proton-transfer (d,n) reactions, demonstrating that while the calculations fail to reproduce the angular distribution shape for reactions on 12C, they provide a successful description for reactions on 16O, particularly for transfer to the 17F excited state 1/2+ when using a nonlocal optical potential.

## Method

The Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for three-body transition operators were solved in momentum-space partial-wave representation for the proton+neutron+nuclear core system. The Coulomb interaction between the proton and the core was included via the screening and renormalization method, with the screened Coulomb potential added to the nuclear interaction. The CD Bonn potential was used for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, while nucleon-core interactions employed several optical potential parametrizations (Watson, Chapel Hill 89, Koning-Delaroche, and a nonlocal potential by Giannini and Ricco).

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 8B(2-) valence proton: 0.137 MeV (1p3/2 state)
- Binding energy of 13N(1/2-) valence proton: 1.944 MeV (1p1/2 state)
- Binding energy of 17F(5/2+) valence proton: 0.600 MeV (1d5/2 state)
- Binding energy of 17F(1/2+) valence proton: 0.105 MeV (2s1/2 state)
- For 16O(d,n)17F(5/2+) at Ed=12 MeV, R-independence established at R > 12 fm
- For 16O(d,n)17F(1/2+) at Ed=12 MeV, convergence reached only at R >= 20 fm
- Differential cross section results presented for 7Be(d,n)8B at center-of-mass energies of 4.5 and 5.8 MeV
- Differential cross section results presented for 12C(d,n)13N at deuteron energies of 7, 12, 18, and 25 MeV
- Differential cross section results presented for 16O(d,n)17F at deuteron energies of 7.7, 9.3, 11, and 12 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev/AGS three-body framework, previously applied to (d,p), (p,d), (p,p), (d,d), (d,pn), (p,pn), and (p,n) reactions by Deltuva (2007, 2009), to the (d,n) reaction for the first time. The calculations enable rigorous study of weakly bound core-plus-valence-proton systems such as the one-proton halo nucleus 8B, and the successful description of 16O(d,n)17F(1/2+) supports the conjecture of Ref. PhysRevC.59.1211 that this state is a one-proton halo. The failure for 12C targets mirrors similar discrepancies observed in 12C(d,p)13C reactions by Deltuva (2009).
