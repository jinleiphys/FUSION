# Pion Production in Neutrino-Nucleon Reactions

> We construct a model for the weak pion production off the nucleon, which in addition to the weak excitation of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance and its subsequent decay into $Nπ$, it includes also some background terms required by chiral symmetry. We re-fit the $C_5^A(q^2)$ form factor to the flux averaged $ν_μp \to μ^-pπ^+$ ANL $q^2-$differential cross section data, finding a substantially smaller contribution of the $Δ$ pole mechanism than traditionally assumed in the literature. We also show that the interference between the Delta pole and the background terms produces parity-violating contributions to the pion angular differential cross section.

## Key claim

The authors construct a model for weak pion production off the nucleon that includes both the Delta(1232) resonance excitation and chiral-symmetry-required background terms, and they find that refitting the C5^A form factor to ANL data yields a substantially smaller contribution from the Delta pole mechanism than traditionally assumed, with the caveat that the correction to the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation would be smaller if BNL data were used instead.

## Method

The model combines a SU(2) nonlinear sigma model for chiral non-resonant background terms with a parametrization of the W+ N to Delta hadron matrix element using form factors from the literature. The authors compute the flux-averaged q^2-differential cross section for the reaction nu_mu p to mu- p pi+ and perform a chi-squared fit to the ANL bubble chamber data to extract the C5^A(0) value and the axial mass M_A_Delta. The model includes seven Feynman diagrams: direct and crossed Delta(1232) pole terms, direct and crossed nucleon pole terms, contact term, pion pole contribution, and pion-in-flight term.

## Key numbers

* C5^A(0) = 0.867 +/- 0.075 from a chi-squared fit to ANL data, representing a correction of about 30% to the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation.
* M_A_Delta = 0.985 +/- 0.082 GeV from the same fit.
* chi-squared/dof = 0.4 and correlation coefficient r = -0.85 for the fit.
* The previous (unfitted) C5^A form factor used C5^A(0) = 1.2 and M_A_Delta = 1.05 GeV.
* The ANL data were taken with a pi N invariant mass cut W <= 1.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus reactions in the quasielastic region (Refs. ccjuan1 and ncjuan) to higher excitation energies above pion production threshold up to the Delta(1232) peak. It contrasts with earlier models (LS72 through Paschos) that described pion production solely through Delta resonance excitation without background terms, and with works by FN and SUL03 that included some background but broke chiral counting or used a small axial mass. The model enables improved treatment of neutrino-nucleus reactions for oscillation experiments and provides a potential tool to distinguish nu_tau from anti-nu_tau via parity-violating contributions to the pion angular differential cross section.
