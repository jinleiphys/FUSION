# The Ter-Mikayelian Effect on QCD Radiative Energy Loss

> The color dielectric modification of the gluon dispersion relation in a dense QCD medium suppresses both the soft and collinear gluon radiation associated with jet production. We compute both the longitudinal and transverse plasmon contributions to the zeroth order in opacity radiative energy loss. This QCD analog of the Ter-Mikayelian effect in QED leads to $\sim 30%$ reduction of the energy loss of high transverse momentum charm quarks produced in a QCD plasma with a characteristic Debye mass $μ\sim 0.5$ GeV.

## Key claim

The Ter-Mikayelian plasmon effect reduces the zeroth-order-in-opacity radiative energy loss of high-transverse-momentum charm quarks in a QCD plasma by approximately 30%, but this reduction is negligible for bottom quarks due to the dead-cone effect; the longitudinal plasmon contribution to the energy loss is significant only near the Debye mass scale and can be neglected for the energy range of experimental interest.

## Method

The authors computed the zeroth-order-in-opacity (associated) radiative energy loss for heavy quarks by evaluating the one-loop hard thermal loop (HTL) gluon self-energy in the temporal axial gauge, using the optical theorem to extract the medium-modified gluon radiation spectrum. Both transverse and longitudinal plasmon contributions were included via the frequency- and wavenumber-dependent dielectric functions epsilon_T and epsilon_L, with the angular integration performed analytically and the running coupling treated using the frozen-alpha model with beta_0 = 28/3 and Lambda_QCD ≈ 0.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks with energy 15 GeV in a QCD plasma with Debye mass μ = 0.5 GeV, the medium reduces the associated radiative energy loss by approximately 30% compared to the vacuum case.
- The transverse plasmon mass m_g(k) starts at ω_pl = μ/√3 at low |k| and asymptotically approaches ω_∞ = μ/√2 at large |k|, consistent with Rebhan.
- For bottom quarks at 15 GeV with μ = 0.5 GeV, the Ter-Mikayelian effect is negligible due to the dead-cone suppression.
- The longitudinal plasmon contribution to the fractional energy loss is negligible for both charm and bottom quarks at μ = 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Ter-Mikayelian effect from QED to QCD, computing the full 1-loop HTL dispersion relation rather than using a constant plasmon mass as in earlier estimates by Kampfer and Pavlenko, Zakharov, and Biro, Levai, and Muller. The results show that the simplifying assumption of a k-independent effective plasmon mass is accurate to about 10% if the asymptotic mass ω_∞ = μ/√2 is used instead of the static ω_pl. This calculation provides the zeroth-order baseline for subsequent first-order-in-opacity (induced) radiative energy loss calculations by Gyulassy and Djordjevic, and helps explain the PHENIX observation that prompt single electrons from open charm show no significant suppression, in contrast to the factor-of-5 suppression seen for light hadrons.
