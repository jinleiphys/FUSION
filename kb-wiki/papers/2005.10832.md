# Exploring $J/ψ$ production mechanism at the future Electron-Ion Collider

> We propose to use transverse momentum $p_T$ distribution of $J/ψ$ production at the future Electron Ion Collider (EIC) to explore the production mechanism of heavy quarkonia in high energy collisions. We apply QCD and QED collinear factorization to the production of a $c\bar{c}$ pair at high $p_T$, and non-relativistic QCD factorization to the hadronization of the pair to a $J/ψ$. We evaluate $J/ψ$ $p_T$-distribution at both leading and next-to-leading order in strong coupling, and show that production rates for various color-spin channels of a $c\bar{c}$ pair in electron-hadron collisions are very different from that in hadron-hadron collisions, which provides a strong discriminative power to determine various transition rates for the pair to become a $J/ψ$. We predict that the $J/ψ$ produced in electron-hadron collisions is likely unpolarized, and the production is an ideal probe for gluon distribution of colliding hadron (or nucleus). We find that the $J/ψ$ production is dominated by the color-octet channel, providing an excellent probe to explore the gluon medium in large nuclei at the EIC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the transverse momentum (\(p_T\)) distribution of single inclusive \(J/\psi\) production at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), without tagging the outgoing electron, can discriminate between different sets of non-perturbative long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) extracted from world data, thereby testing the universality of the NRQCD factorization approach. The authors hedge that while NRQCD factorization is applied as an ansatz, there is no all-order proof for this process, though it is found valid up to two-loop calculations.

## Method

The authors apply QCD and QED collinear factorization to the production of a \(c\bar{c}\) pair at high \(p_T\) in electron-hadron collisions, and non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization to the hadronization of the pair into a \(J/\psi\). They calculate the short-distance hard parts at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\), using the dipole subtraction method developed for heavy quarkonium production to handle QCD divergences. The calculation includes contributions from four leading color-spin states of the \(c\bar{c}\) pair: \(^3S_1^{[1]}\), \(^1S_0^{[8]}\), \(^3S_1^{[8]}\), and \(^3P_J^{[8]}\), with the cross section evaluated using four different sets of LDMEs from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The center-of-mass energy used for numerical analysis is \(\sqrt{s} = 141.4\) GeV, with a \(J/\psi\) pseudo-rapidity cut \(|\eta| < 4\) and transverse momentum cut \(3 < p_T < 15\) GeV.
- The factorization and renormalization scales are set to \(\mu_r = \mu_f = \sqrt{p_T^2 + M^2}\) with the \(J/\psi\) mass \(M = 3.1\) GeV.
- The LDME values from the four groups (Bodwin, Butenschoen, Chao, Gong) are given in Table 1; for example, the Bodwin set has \(\langle \mathcal{O}(^1S_0^{[8]})\rangle = 9.9 \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\), \(\langle \mathcal{O}(^3S_1^{[8]})\rangle = 1.1 \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\), and \(\langle \mathcal{O}(^3P_0^{[8]})\rangle = 1.1 \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^5\).
- The predicted differential cross section shows almost an order of magnitude difference in production rate between the Bodwin/Chao sets and the Butenschoen set.
- The quasi-real photon channel contributes approximately 70% of the total cross section at lower \(p_T\).
- With a \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\) cut on the exchanged photon, the gluon-initiated fraction \(R_g\) of the total differential cross section is large over a wide \(p_T\) region.

## Context

This work extends the study of \(J/\psi\) production from hadron-hadron collisions (e.g., by Acosta (2004), Adare (2009), Abelev (2009), Khachatryan (2015), Aad (2015), Abelev (2012), Aaij (2013)) and photon-hadron collisions (e.g., by Chekanov (2002), Adloff (2002), Aaron (2010)) to the future Electron-Ion Collider. It builds on the NRQCD factorization approach of Bodwin (1994) and addresses the unresolved challenge of LDME universality, as highlighted by Lansberg (2019) and Chung (2018). The proposed observable enables a test of NRQCD factorization via \(J/\psi\) polarization measurements and provides a new probe of the gluon distribution in nucleons and nuclei, supporting one of the main science goals of the EIC.
