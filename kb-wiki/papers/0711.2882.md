# Ultraperipheral Collisions at RHIC and LHC

> A brief introduction to the physics of ultraperipheral collisions at collider energies is given. Photon-hadron (proton/ nucleus) and photon-photon interactions can be studied in a hitherto unexplored energy regime.

## Key claim

Ultraperipheral collisions (UPC) at hadron colliders enable the study of photon-hadron and photon-photon interactions in a previously unexplored energy regime, but only when the impact parameter is large enough (b > R1 + R2) to suppress the dominant strong interactions.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of UPC, where the time-dependent electromagnetic field of a fast-moving charged particle is treated as a spectrum of equivalent (Weizsäcker-Williams) photons. The probability of a specific photon-hadron reaction at impact parameter b is given by P(b) = N(ω, b) σγh(ω), with the equivalent photon spectrum approximated as N(ω, b) = Z²α/(π²b²) for ω < γ/b. The impact parameter is restricted to b > b_min ∼ R1 + R2, and for heavy ions the Coulomb parameter η = Z²e²/(ħv) ∼ Z²/137 is much larger than unity, allowing multiphoton processes to occur.

## Key numbers

- Maximum photon energy at RHIC (Au-Au, γ ∼ 100): about 3 GeV in the collider system
- Maximum photon energy at LHC (Pb-Pb, γ ∼ 3000): about 100 GeV in the collider system
- The nuclear giant dipole resonance is excited with probabilities of order one third
- At LHC, dijet production could measure the gluon density in the proton and/or nucleus down to x ∼ 3 × 10⁻⁵
- Coherent heavy quarkonium production (γ + A → J/Ψ(Υ) + A) can be studied at x ≲ 10⁻²

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of equivalent photon spectra (Fermi 1924, Weizsäcker-Williams) to collider energies at RHIC and LHC, building on earlier work on UPC at lower energies (Aumann 1990s). The paper discusses interference effects in vector meson photoproduction (Klein and Nystrand 2000) that lead to a dip in the transverse momentum spectrum, which was observed in preliminary STAR/RHIC results. The work enables future studies of low-x QCD at LHC, extending HERA photoproduction measurements to invariant γp energies exceeding the maximal HERA energy by a factor of 10, and allows investigation of small dipole propagation through nuclear matter (Frankfurt, Kopeliovich, Machavariani).
