# Particle-dimer approach for the Roper resonance in a finite volume

> We propose a new finite-volume approach which implements two- and three-body dynamics in a transparent way based on an Effective Field Theory Lagrangian. The formalism utilizes a particle-dimer picture and formulates the quantization conditions based on the self-energy of the decaying particle. The formalism is studied for the case of the Roper resonance, using input from lattice QCD and phenomenology. Finally, finite-volume energy eigenvalues are predicted and compared to existing results of lattice QCD calculations. This crucially provides initial guidance on the necessary level of precision for the finite-volume spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new finite-volume quantization condition for three-body systems based on a particle-dimer effective field theory, where the Roper resonance self-energy is expressed in terms of stable meson-baryon loops and dressed dimer propagators; the approach is tested specifically for the Roper resonance using phenomenological input rather than first-principles lattice QCD, providing only "initial guidance" on the necessary precision for future lattice calculations.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant non-relativistic effective field theory Lagrangian with pion and nucleon fields, then introduce auxiliary dimer fields for the Delta(1232) resonance, the sigma meson (f0(500)), and the Roper resonance itself. The quantization condition is formulated by computing the Roper self-energy from one-loop diagrams involving pion-nucleon intermediate states, and from particle-dimer loops where the Delta and sigma dimer propagators are dressed by their own self-energies to incorporate three-body (pi pi N) dynamics. The finite-volume energy eigenvalues are obtained by evaluating the self-energy integrals with momentum sums over the discrete lattice momenta rather than continuous integrals, using phenomenological input to fix the low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

* Roper resonance physical mass m_R and width Gamma_R defined via pole position z = m_R - i*Gamma_R/2, with m_R = m_R0 - (1/(2*m_R0))*Re{Sigma_R(z)} and Gamma_R = (1/m_R0)*Im{Sigma_R(z)}
* The one-loop pion-nucleon self-energy integral evaluates to J_{N pi}(E) = i*lambda^{1/2}(E^2, m_N^2, M_pi^2)/(16*pi*E^2) in the rest frame, where lambda is the Kallen triangle function
* The sigma dimer self-energy contains a symmetry factor of 1/2 and evaluates to Sigma_sigma(p) = i*h_2^2*lambda^{1/2}(p^2, M_pi^2, M_pi^2)/(8*pi*p^2)
* The Kallen function for two equal masses simplifies to lambda(p^2, M_pi^2, M_pi^2) = p^2*(p^2 - 4*M_pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the particle-dimer framework of Kaplan (1996), Bedaque (1998), and Braaten (2004) to finite-volume three-body systems involving non-identical particles (pions and nucleons), building on the authors' prior work Severt (2020). The formalism is designed to address the Roper resonance, whose complex analytic structure from strong coupling to the pi pi N channel was previously studied phenomenologically by Krehl (1999), Arndt (2006), Doring (2009), and Alvarez-Ruso (2010). The predicted finite-volume spectrum is compared to existing lattice QCD results from Lang (2016), Kiratidis (2016), Liu (2016), and Guo (2018), and the approach aims to enable future lattice QCD calculations of three-body resonances by providing a transparent quantization condition that separates power-law finite-volume effects from exponentially suppressed ones.
