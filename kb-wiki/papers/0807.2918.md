# Wigner symmetry, Large Nc and Renormalized One Boson Exchange Potential

> Wigner symmetry in Nuclear Physics provides a unique example of a non-perturbative long distance symmetry, a symmetry strongly broken at short distances. We analyse the consequences of such a concept within the framework of One Boson Exchange Potentials in NN scattering and keeping the leading Nc contributions. Phenomenologically successful relations between singlet 1S0 and triplet 3S1 scattering phase shifts are provided in the entire elastic region. We establish symmetry breaking relations among non-central phase shifts which are successfully fulfilled by even-L partial waves and strongly violated by odd-L partial waves, in full agreement with large Nc requirements.

## Key claim

Wigner SU(4) symmetry holds for the NN interaction at long distances even though it is strongly broken at short distances, and this long-distance symmetry can be reconciled with the large \(N_c\) expansion by using renormalization to separate short-distance boundary conditions from the long-distance potential.

## Method

The authors use a coordinate-space renormalization approach where the scattering length is fixed independently of the potential via a short-distance boundary condition, while the long-distance potential is taken from the leading \(N_c\) One Boson Exchange (OBE) model including \(\pi\), \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) mesons. They solve the Schrödinger equation for S-waves and compute phase shifts from universal functions that depend only on the potential, with the scattering length entering as an explicit parameter. For non-central partial waves, they analyze symmetry-breaking relations from spin-orbit and tensor interactions.

## Key numbers

- Singlet scattering length: \(\alpha_s = -23.74\ \mathrm{fm}\); triplet scattering length: \(\alpha_t = 5.42\ \mathrm{fm}\) (experimental values used as input).
- Effective range from OPE+\(\sigma\) potential: \(r_0 = 2.6989\ \mathrm{fm}\) for \(\alpha_0 = \alpha_s\) (experimental: \(2.770\ \mathrm{fm}\)) and \(r_0 = 1.5221\ \mathrm{fm}\) for \(\alpha_0 = \alpha_t\) (experimental: \(1.753\ \mathrm{fm}\)).
- Fitted \(\sigma\)-meson mass: \(m_\sigma = 510(1)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from a fit to \(^1S_0\) pn data, differing by about \(10\%\) from the Roy-equation value of \(441^{+16}_{-8}\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Universal function coefficients for OPE+\(\sigma\): \(r_0 = 2.4567 - 5.5284/\alpha_0 + 5.7398/\alpha_0^2\) (all in fm).

## Context

This work extends the concept of Wigner SU(4) symmetry from earlier studies by Wigner (1936) and Hund (1937) and connects it to the large \(N_c\) expansion of QCD developed by 't Hooft (1973) and Witten (1979), as applied to nuclear forces by Kaplan and Savage (1995, 1996). The renormalization approach builds on previous coordinate-space renormalization by Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2005, 2007). The results enable a consistent interpretation of Wigner symmetry for physical pion masses, reconciling the large scattering lengths with the symmetry, and provide a framework for testing SU(4) breaking patterns in higher partial waves that agree with large \(N_c\) expectations.
