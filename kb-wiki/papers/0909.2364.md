# Covariant density functional theory: The role of the pion

> We investigate the role of the pion in Covariant Density Functional Theory. Starting from conventional Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory with a non-linear coupling of the $σ$-meson and without exchange terms we add pions with a pseudo-vector coupling to the nucleons in relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation. In order to take into account the change of the pion field in the nuclear medium the effective coupling constant of the pion is treated as a free parameter. It is found that the inclusion of the pion to this sort of density functionals does not destroy the overall description of the bulk properties by RMF. On the other hand, the non-central contribution of the pion (tensor coupling) does have effects on single particle energies and on binding energies of certain nuclei.

## Key claim

Adding the pion via a pseudo-vector coupling in relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation does not destroy the overall description of bulk nuclear properties by Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory, but the optimal fit to bulk data is actually achieved for vanishing pion field; the tensor part of the pion force does produce shifts in single-particle energies and binding energies of certain nuclei, but only at the cost of a worse global fit.

## Method

Starting from conventional RMF theory with non-linear sigma-meson coupling and no exchange terms, the authors add the pion Fock term with a pseudo-vector coupling to nucleons in relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation. The pion coupling constant is treated as a free parameter, scaled by a factor lambda relative to the free-pion value, and the remaining six parameters (sigma mass, three coupling constants, and two nonlinear sigma parameters) are refitted for each fixed lambda using a chi-squared minimization to bulk properties of infinite nuclear matter and spherical finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is fixed to its experimental value of 138.0 MeV.
- For the fit with full free pion coupling (lambda=1.0), chi-squared = 310.00; for lambda=0.5, chi-squared = 168.00; for lambda=0 (no pion, pure RH), chi-squared = 75.00.
- The RMF result without pion overbinds the nucleus 100Sn by 5.7 MeV; including the pion with lambda=0.5 brings the binding energy close to the experimental value.
- From 116Sn to 132Sn, the energy gap between the 1h11/2 and 1g9/2 single-particle levels increases by 2-3 MeV when the pion is included.

## Context

This work extends conventional RMF theory (e.g., the NL3 parameter set of Lalazissis et al. 1997) by adding the pion on the Hartree-Fock level, in contrast to earlier relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations that were limited to nuclear matter or a few doubly magic nuclei (e.g., HS 1984, BMM 1985, BMG 1987, MNQ 1991, BFG 1993, MSF 2004). It also differs from the density-dependent RHF approach of Long et al. (2006, 2007) by keeping the nonlinear sigma coupling and treating the pion coupling as a free parameter. The observed shifts in single-particle energies follow the tensor-force trends predicted by Otsuka et al. (2005) and are compared to experimental data from the Argonne group (Schiffer et al. 2004). The work enables future inclusion of coupling to low-lying surface phonons, which is stated to be in progress.
