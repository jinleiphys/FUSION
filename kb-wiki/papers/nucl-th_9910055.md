# Excitation of multiple giant dipole resonances: from spherical to deformed nuclei

> The effect of deformation on the excitation of multiple giant dipole resonances is studied. Analytical expressions are derived in the framework of the interacting boson model for the energies and E1 properties of giant dipole resonances in spherical and deformed nuclei, and a numerical treatment of transitional nuclei is proposed. Coulomb-excitation cross sections are calculated in $^{238}$U and in the samarium isotopes.

## Key claim

Deformation modifies the excitation of multiple giant dipole resonances (GDRs) in a way that can be treated analytically in the interacting boson model (IBM) for spherical and well-deformed nuclei, but requires numerical diagonalization for transitional nuclei; the calculated Coulomb-excitation cross sections for the double GDR in ²³⁸U show a three-peak structure that could be observed in exclusive experiments, in contrast to the structureless peak predicted by Ponomarev et al. (1998) using second-order perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors model low-energy collective states using the IBM (s and d bosons) and couple them to multiple GDR excitations represented as p bosons, with the total Hamiltonian including monopole, dipole, and quadrupole interactions between the sd and p boson spaces. Analytical energy eigenvalues and E1 transition strengths are derived for the weak-coupling U(5) limit and the strong-coupling SU(3) rotational limit, while numerical diagonalization is used for transitional nuclei. Coulomb-excitation cross sections are calculated by solving the time-dependent Schrödinger equation along classical trajectories for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, using the E1 matrix elements from the model.

## Key numbers

- For ²³⁸U + ²⁰⁸Pb at 0.5 GeV/A: integrated cross sections are σ^GDR = 3.5 b and σ^DGDR = 0.3 b (coupled-channel calculation); using perturbation theory gives σ^GDR = 4.2 b.
- The minimum impact parameter used is b_min = 1.34 [A₁^{1/3} + A₂^{1/3} – 0.75 (A₁^{–1/3} + A₂^{–1/3})] fm.
- For ²³⁸U, a spreading width Γ_i = 0.007 E_i^{2.5} (E_i and Γ_i in MeV) is assigned to each eigenstate; for the Sm isotopes, Γ_i = 0.029 E_i^{1.81}.
- In the strong-coupling rotational limit for large boson number N, the B(E1) values from the 0⁺ ground state to the two 1⁻ GDRs are 1 and 2; for the single-to-double GDR excitation the B(E1) values are 2, 2, 1, and 3.

## Context

This work extends the IBM treatment of the single GDR by Rowe and Iachello (1983) and earlier numerical studies of the double GDR by Scholten (1990) to multiple GDR excitations in both spherical and deformed nuclei, including transitional cases. The results contradict the structureless DGDR peak predicted by Ponomarev et al. (1998) using the particle–phonon model with second-order perturbation theory, and instead predict a three-peak structure in well-deformed nuclei that could be tested in planned experiments. The approach enables quick estimates of excitation cross sections to the triple GDR, for which experiments are currently planned.
