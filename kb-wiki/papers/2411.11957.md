# Casimir effect in magnetic dual chiral density waves

> We theoretically investigate the Casimir effect originating from Dirac fields in finite-density matter under a magnetic field. In particular, we focus on quark fields in the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase as a possible inhomogeneous ground state of interacting Dirac-fermion systems. In this system, the distance dependence of Casimir energy shows a complex oscillatory behavior by the interplay between the chemical potential, magnetic field, and inhomogeneous ground state. By decomposing the total Casimir energy into contributions of each Landau level, we elucidate what types of Casimir effects are realized from each Landau level: the lowest or some types of higher Landau levels lead to different behaviors of Casimir energies. Furthermore, we point out characteristic behaviors due to level splitting between different fermion flavors, i.e., up/down quarks. These findings provide new insights into Dirac-fermion (or quark) matter with a finite thickness.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase, the Casimir energy from Dirac fields exhibits complex oscillatory behavior as a function of distance, arising from the interplay between chemical potential, magnetic field, and the inhomogeneous ground state; however, this behavior is contingent on the presence of Fermi points in the dispersion relations, and the total Casimir energy is a superposition of contributions from each Landau level, with different Landau levels producing qualitatively different oscillatory patterns.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective theory of QCD to describe two-flavor quark matter in the MDCDW phase under a uniform magnetic field parallel to the density wave vector. They compute the Casimir energy by imposing periodic boundary conditions at z=0 and z=L_z, applying the Lifshitz formula to regularize the ultraviolet divergence in the zero-point energy, and separately decomposing the total Casimir energy into contributions from the lowest Landau level (LLL) and higher Landau levels (HLLs). They also employ a lattice regularization scheme as a cross-check, discretizing the momentum in the z direction with lattice spacing a and taking the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- For the one-flavor case with parameters (M/Λ, b/Λ, μ/Λ) = (0.05, 0.25, 0.5) at eB/Λ² = (0.05)², the LLL oscillation period from Fermi points is L_z^osc = 5.89 fm.
- At L_z → 0, the Casimir coefficient for the LLL approaches C_Cas,LLL^[1] = N_c × (|q_f B|/(2π Λ²)) × (π/3), giving 0.00125 for the chosen parameters.
- At L_z → 0, the Casimir coefficient for each HLL approaches C_Cas,HLL^[1] = N_c × (|q_f B|/(2π Λ²)) × (2π/3), giving 0.0025 for the chosen parameters.
- In the massless-quark vacuum (M = b = μ = B = 0), the total Casimir coefficient is C_Cas^[3] = N_f N_c × 2π²/45 ≈ 2.63, corresponding to E_Cas ≈ 519 MeV/fm² at L_z = 1 fm and E_Cas ≈ 0.519 MeV/fm² at L_z = 10 fm.
- The total Casimir coefficient in the MDCDW phase is approximately C_Cas^[3] ∼ 5 even at longer L_z, comparable to the massless-quark vacuum value.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the oscillating Casimir effect in the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase without a magnetic field (Fujii 2024) by incorporating a magnetic field, which makes the DCDW phase more robust and removes the Landau-Peierls instability at finite temperature as shown by Ferrer (2019). The results enable future investigations of Casimir effects in realistic quark matter with finite thickness, such as in heavy-ion collision fireballs or neutron star interiors, and provide a classification scheme for dispersion relations in the MDCDW phase that can be compared with the homogeneous chiral condensate phase under magnetic fields.
