# Evaluation of fission fragment moments of inertia for spontaneous fission of Cf-252

> Current work discusses methods for estimating the moments of inertia of fission fragments for spontaneous fission of the isotope Cf-252, in particular, two main approaches are mentioned: statistical and microscopic. In addition, the methods of the classical and superfluid approaches to the calculation of the moments of inertia are discussed, as well as their application to different models of nuclei. Within this framework, the influence of different oscillation modes and nucleon exchange on the moments of inertia and spin distributions of fission fragments is evaluated. The authors emphasizes the need for a comparative analysis of theoretical predictions with experimental data for a deeper understanding of the internal structure of nuclei and fission mechanisms.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the superfluid model with an oscillatory potential provides the best agreement with experimental moments of inertia for fission fragments from spontaneous fission of Cf-252, but this agreement is limited by the use of simplified potentials (oscillatory and rectangular well) and by the absence of a direct experimental method to measure the quadrupole nonequilibrium deformation parameters of highly deformed pre-fragments.

## Method

The authors calculate fission fragment moments of inertia using classical (rigid-body and hydrodynamic) approaches and the superfluid model of Migdal (1960) for both oscillatory and rectangular potential wells, normalizing all values to the solid-state moment of inertia. They then develop an indirect method to estimate non-equilibrium deformations of pre-fragments at the scission point by relating excitation energies (derived from neutron multiplicities via the formula U = 5 + 4ν + ν²) to deformation energies using the Strutinsky (1967) liquid-drop model framework, with neutron multiplicities obtained from the FREYA code and the Grudzevich (2000) method.

## Key numbers

- For spontaneous fission of Cf-252, the superfluid model with oscillatory potential yields moments of inertia (in units of ħ²/MeV) ranging from 7.76 (for ⁹⁴Sr) to 12.10 (for ¹⁵⁸Nd), while the hydrodynamic model gives values from 5.35 to 9.10 for the same fragment pairs.
- The superfluid model with rectangular potential gives moments of inertia from 6.33 (⁹⁴Sr) to 10.50 (¹⁵⁸Nd).
- The deformation parameter β for the fragment pairs ranges from 0.444 (¹³⁰Sn) to 0.997 (¹²²Cd).
- Neutron pairing gaps Δₙ range from -0.64 MeV (¹⁵⁸Nd) to -1.55 MeV (¹³²Sn), and proton pairing gaps Δₚ from -0.62 MeV (¹⁵⁴Nd) to -1.85 MeV (¹³²Sn).
- The function Φ₁(χ) for the oscillatory potential superfluid model ranges from 0 (χ=0) to 0.87 (χ=3.0), and Φ₂(χ) for the rectangular potential ranges from 0 to 0.50.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of statistical (FREYA by Randrup and Vogt, 2021) and microscopic (TDDFT by Bulgac et al., 2021, 2022) approaches to fission fragment spin generation by focusing specifically on the moments of inertia, which both models identify as a key quantity. The paper tests the "at hot" sawtooth parameterization of moments of inertia introduced by Randrup and Vogt (2021) and finds that the superfluid model with oscillatory potential best reproduces experimental data from Lovchikova (1983) and Sheikh (2020), though the use of simplified potentials limits accuracy. The indirect method for estimating non-equilibrium deformations from neutron multiplicities (using data from Walsh, 1977, and the Grudzevich, 2000, approach) enables future work to connect fragment deformation to spin distributions without requiring direct experimental measurement of scission-point deformations.
