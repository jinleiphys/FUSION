# Relativistic Descriptions of Final-State Interactions in Quasielastic Electron and Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> Models developed within the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation for quasielastic (QE) electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering are discussed. Different descriptions of final-state interactions (FSI) in the inclusive scattering are compared: the relativistic Green's function (RGF) and the relativistic mean field (RMF).The results of the models are compared with the recently measured double-differential charged-current QE (CCQE) neutrino MiniBooNE cross sections.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, which includes the imaginary part of the optical potential to account for inelastic channels, can produce charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino-nucleus cross sections of the same magnitude as the MiniBooNE data without increasing the nucleon axial mass from its standard value of 1.03 GeV/c², whereas the relativistic mean field (RMF) model generally underpredicts the data.

## Method

The authors compare two relativistic descriptions of final-state interactions (FSI) within the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) for inclusive quasielastic electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering: the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, which uses a complex optical potential and its spectral representation to recover the contribution of non-elastic channels, and the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, which uses the same purely real mean-field potentials for both bound and scattering states. For the RGF model, calculations are performed with different parameterizations of the relativistic optical potential (EDAD1, EDAD2, and EDAI). The models are applied to compute differential cross sections for ¹²C(e,e') and ¹²C(ν_μ,μ⁻) reactions, and the results are compared with the MiniBooNE CCQE double-differential and total cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C(e,e') at incident electron energy ε = 1 GeV and momentum transfer q = 1000 MeV/c, the RGF1 cross section at the peak overshoots the RMF cross section by up to 40%.
- The standard value of the nucleon axial mass used in all calculations is M_A = 1.03 GeV/c².
- The MiniBooNE data require M_A = 1.35 GeV/c² in the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model to match the measured cross section.
- The RGF-EDAI model yields total CCQE cross sections per neutron in good agreement with the shape and magnitude of the MiniBooNE unfolded experimental data, while the RGF-EDAD1 results are clearly below the data.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of RGF and RMF models previously applied to inclusive electron scattering (Giusti et al., 2009) and CC neutrino scattering (Meucci et al., 2011) to the MiniBooNE CCQE data (MiniBooNE Collaboration, 2010). The results challenge the conclusion from earlier RFG analyses that a larger axial mass is required, and instead suggest that proper treatment of FSI via the RGF model can account for the observed cross section enhancement. The authors note that multi-nucleon emission (Martini et al., 2009; Nieves et al., 2011) and non-nucleonic contributions (Leitner et al., 2010) should also be evaluated to fully clarify the origin of the enhancement.
