# Multi-photon interactions in lepton photo-production on nuclei at high energies

> We study multi-photon effects in quantum electrodynamics in lepton photo-production on heavy nuclei and nuclear medium at high energies. We are interested in energy, charge density and nuclear geometry dependence of the cross sections. We use the impact parameter representation that allows us to reduce the problem of photo-production to the problem of propagation of electric dipoles in the nuclear Coulomb field. In the framework of the Glauber model we resum an infinite series of multiphoton amplitudes corresponding to multiple rescattering of the electric dipole on the nucleus. We find that unitarity effects arising due to multi-photon interactions are small and energy-independent for scattering on a single nucleus, whereas in the case of macroscopic nuclear medium they saturate the geometric limit of the total cross section. We discuss an analogy between nuclear medium and intense laser beams.

## Key claim

Multi-photon (unitarity) effects in lepton photo-production are small and energy-independent for scattering on a single heavy nucleus, but for a macroscopic nuclear medium they can saturate the geometric (black disk) limit of the total cross section, with the transition between these regimes depending on the nuclear charge density and geometry.

## Method

The author uses the impact parameter (dipole) representation to separate the photon fluctuation into a lepton-antilepton pair from the subsequent scattering of that electric dipole off the nucleus. The Glauber model for multiple scattering resums an infinite series of multiphoton amplitudes, expressing the dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude through the dipole-proton amplitude under the assumption of independent scattering centers. The dipole-proton amplitude is calculated in the Weizsacker-Williams approximation, and the formalism is applied separately to the cases of a single finite nucleus and an extended nuclear medium (thin film).

## Key numbers

- For electron pair production on a single nucleus at high energy, the Born-level total cross section is \(\sigma^{\gamma Z \to e^+e^-X}_{\mathrm{tot},B}(s) = \frac{28}{9}\frac{\alpha^3 Z^2}{m_e^2} \ln \left(\frac{\omega}{m_e}\sqrt{\frac{1-v}{1+v}}\right)\), reproducing the Bethe-Heitler formula.
- For electron production at \(\sqrt{s}=10\) GeV, the maximum impact parameter for dipole-proton scattering is \(b'_\mathrm{max} \sim 20\) nm, while the radius of a Gold nucleus is 7 fm.
- For a nuclear medium with longitudinal extent \(L=1\) nm and transverse extent \(d=100\) nm at \(\sqrt{s}=10\) GeV, deviation from the linear Born regime for electrons begins at about \(Z \sim 10^{12}\) charges, and the cross section becomes essentially black (Z-independent) at \(Z \sim 10^{13}\).
- The minimum transverse momentum cutoff for the Weizsacker-Williams approximation is \(l_\mathrm{min} = M(2m)^2 / s\), where \(M\) is the proton mass and \(m\) the lepton mass.

## Context

This work extends the traditional Bethe-Heitler (1934) and Bethe-Maximon (1954) approach to lepton pair photo-production by explicitly incorporating finite nuclear size and geometry via the Glauber model. It provides a unified QED framework connecting scattering on single nuclei to scattering on dense nuclear media, and draws an analogy to the problem of positronium propagation through matter discussed by Lyuboshits and Podgoretsky (1981). The formalism enables future study of photo-production on intense laser beams, where beam size can be tuned to explore both the single-nucleus and medium limits.
