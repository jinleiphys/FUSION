# The charge radii of calcium isotopes within relativistic density functional theory: nucleon's finite-size and quadrupole shape fluctuation effects

> The anomaly in the charge radii of Ca isotopes has been puzzling for nuclear theory for decades. We present the first self-consistent solution to this puzzle within the density functional theory without resorting to local parameter adjustment. By taking into account both the intrinsic electromagnetic structure of nucleons and the zero-point motions of nuclear shape, which have been often neglected in previous studies, the similar charge radii of $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca as well as an inverted parabolic behavior between them are reproduced. It is found that these effects also play crucial roles in the description of the isotonic shift between the charge radii of Sn and Cd isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper claims to solve the long-standing puzzle of calcium isotope charge radii within density functional theory (DFT) for the first time without local parameter adjustment, by including both the intrinsic electromagnetic structure of nucleons and zero-point shape fluctuations. The hedge is that the reproduction of the parabolic behavior and similar radii of ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca depends on including both effects simultaneously; mean-field calculations alone (point-proton radii) give a monotonic increase with neutron number.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic density functional PC-PK1 with the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) method on the mean-field level to compute spherical mean-square charge radii, including contributions from proton and neutron finite sizes and spin-orbit charge radii. Beyond-mean-field quadrupole shape fluctuations were incorporated via a microscopically mapped five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH), from which the quadrupole deformation parameter β² was extracted using the Kumar-Cline sum rule over the 30 lowest 2⁺ states.

## Key numbers

* The calculated charge radius for ⁴⁰Ca is 3.4893 fm, compared to the experimental value of 3.4776(19) fm.
* The calculated charge radius for ⁴⁸Ca is 3.4866 fm, compared to the experimental value of 3.4771(20) fm.
* The differential mean-square charge radius δ⟨r²⟩_ch^(28,20) (⁴⁸Ca minus ⁴⁰Ca) is -0.019 fm² when including both nucleon structure and zero-point motions, versus 0.088 fm² using point-proton radii alone.
* The root-mean-square deviation for charge radii of even ⁴⁰⁻⁴⁸Ca isotopes is 0.0134 fm.
* The proton mean-square charge radius used is R_p² = (0.8414 ± 0.0019)² fm²; the neutron mean-square charge radius used is R_n² = -0.110 ± 0.008 fm².

## Context

This work extends prior DFT studies (e.g., Fayans functional Fy(Δr) from Fayans (2000) and Miller (2019); relativistic functional with Cooper-pair correction from PhysRevC.102.024307) that required local parameter adjustments to fit the Ca charge radii. It also builds on earlier proposals by Barranco and Broglia (1985) and work using experimental B(E2) values with the SV-min functional (PhysRevC.106.L011304), but achieves a self-consistent treatment without external inputs. The approach enables future systematic studies of charge radii across the nuclear chart and is tested against isotonic shifts between Sn and Cd isotopes.
