# Clustered Quark Matter Calculation for Strange Quark Matter

> Motivated by the need for a solid state strange quark matter to better explain some observational phenomena, we discussed possibility of color singlet cluster formation in cold strange quark matter by a rough calculation following the excluded volume method proposed by Clark et al (1986) and adopted quark mass density dependent model with cubic scaling. It is found that 70% to 75% of volume and 80% to 90% of baryon number is in clusters at temperature from 10MeV to 50MeV and 1 to 10 times nuclear density.

# Clustered Quark Matter Calculation for Strange Quark Matter

## Key claim
Color-singlet three-quark clusters can occupy 70% to 75% of volume and carry 80% to 90% of baryon number in cold strange quark matter at densities 1 to 10 times nuclear density and temperatures 10 to 50 MeV, but this result depends on a rough excluded-volume approximation that treats interactions only through phase-space constraints, not through a full dynamical treatment.

## Method
The author applied the excluded-volume method of Clark et al. (1986) to a three-flavor quark matter system modeled with a quark mass density dependent (QMDD) model using cubic scaling. Color-singlet spin-1/2 three-quark clusters were treated as a new component of the Fermi sea with chemical equilibrium condition μ_c = 3μ_q, and the cluster mass was either fixed at 1000 MeV or taken as the sum of density-dependent quark masses. The MIT bag model was used to relate cluster mass to volume, and the system was solved self-consistently for chemical potential, cluster mass, and available volume fraction at temperatures 10 to 50 MeV and baryon densities 1 to 10 times nuclear density.

## Key numbers
- Cluster mass M_cl ranges from 500 to 1100 MeV, increasing with density, in the excluded-volume treatment.
- Quark chemical potential μ_q ranges from 200 to 400 MeV, increasing with density.
- Available volume fraction is 15% to 30% (meaning 70% to 75% of volume is occupied by clusters).
- 80% to 90% of baryon number is in clusters at temperatures 10 to 50 MeV and densities 1 to 10 times nuclear density ν_0 = 0.159 fm^{-3}.
- The bag constant used was B = 161.5 MeV·fm^{-3} = (187.7 MeV)^4, from Saito and Thomas (1995).
- The cluster mass-volume relation used c ≈ 3.15, derived from M_Λ = 1115 MeV as the average baryon octet mass.

## Context
This work extends the excluded-volume approach of Clark et al. (1986) for two-flavor quark matter to three-flavor strange quark matter, motivated by astrophysical hints of a solid phase in pulsar interiors (Xu 2009). The calculation suggests that clustered quark matter could form a lattice-like structure analogous to positive ions in a metal, with clusters immersed in a small fraction of free quarks. The author notes that for heavier clusters such as H-dibaryons (Jaffe 1977) or 18-quark "quark alpha" clusters (Michel 1988), classical behavior and lattice formation become possible at temperatures above approximately 7 to 9 MeV for densities of 3 to 4 ν_0.
