# Role of dynamic pairing correlations in fission dynamics

> We study the role of dynamic pairing correlations in fission dynamics by considering intrinsic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions that are obtained by minimizing the particle number projected energy. For the restricted variational space, the set of self-consistent wave functions with different values of proton and neutron number particle fluctuations are considered. The particle number projected energy is used to define potential energy surface for fission whereas collective inertias are computed within the traditional formulas for the intrinsic states. The results show that the effect of the restricted variation after particle number projection in the potential energy surface is small while collective inertias substantially decrease. On the other hand, we show that this quenching is strongly mitigated when Coulomb antipairing is considered. In the light of these beyond mean-field calculations, the validity of traditional fission calculations is discussed.

## Key claim

The restricted variation after particle number projection (RVAP-PNP) has a small effect on the potential energy surface for fission but substantially decreases collective inertias; however, this quenching is strongly mitigated when Coulomb antipairing is considered, and the two opposite effects tend to cancel each other, yielding results similar to traditional calculations that omit both effects.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny D1M force for three actinide nuclei (²³⁶U, ²⁴⁰Pu, ²⁵²Cf), using a restricted variation after particle number projection (RVAP-PNP) method where intrinsic wave functions are obtained by minimizing the projected energy as a function of proton and neutron number fluctuations. The potential energy surfaces for fission were computed from the particle number projected energy with a constraint on the axial quadrupole moment, while collective inertias were computed using the traditional perturbative adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) and Gaussian overlap approximation (GCM) formulas applied to the intrinsic states.

## Key numbers

- For ²³⁶U, the HFB$_t$ spontaneous fission half-life is $3.0\times10^{43}$ s (ATDHFB) and $2.4\times10^{32}$ s (GCM); including Coulomb antipairing (HFB$_\mathrm{Cep}$) increases these to $3.1\times10^{63}$ s and $1.2\times10^{46}$ s, respectively; the full RVAP-PNP calculation gives $1.0\times10^{42}$ s (ATDHFB) and $1.4\times10^{32}$ s (GCM).
- For ²⁴⁰Pu, the HFB$_t$ spontaneous fission half-life is $7.4\times10^{38}$ s (ATDHFB) and $7.5\times10^{29}$ s (GCM); HFB$_\mathrm{Cep}$ gives $2.0\times10^{54}$ s and $2.4\times10^{39}$ s; RVAP-PNP gives $2.8\times10^{37}$ s and $1.2\times10^{29}$ s.
- For ²⁵²Cf, the HFB$_t$ spontaneous fission half-life is $2.3\times10^{22}$ s (ATDHFB) and $1.7\times10^{18}$ s (GCM); HFB$_\mathrm{Cep}$ gives $7.6\times10^{24}$ s and $2.9\times10^{18}$ s; RVAP-PNP gives $1.9\times10^{21}$ s and $6.2\times10^{16}$ s.
- The inner fission barrier height $B_I$ for ²³⁶U is 9.07 MeV (HFB$_t$), 9.82 MeV (HFB$_\mathrm{Cep}$), and 9.74 MeV (PNP); for ²⁴⁰Pu it is 10.23 MeV (HFB$_t$), 10.91 MeV (HFB$_\mathrm{Cep}$), and 10.83 MeV (PNP); for ²⁵²Cf it is 11.18 MeV (HFB$_t$), 11.60 MeV (HFB$_\mathrm{Cep}$), and 11.22 MeV (PNP).
- Coulomb antipairing reduces proton pairing gaps by 20-30% (cited from prior work by Lesinski (2009) and Nakada (2011)).

## Context

This work extends beyond mean-field studies of fission by addressing the role of dynamic pairing correlations through particle number projection, which had been computationally prohibitive for fission calculations. Prior work by Samyn (2005) found that particle number projection changes fission barrier heights by at most ±0.5 MeV, consistent with the small PES changes observed here. The results show that for Gogny-type forces where proton and neutron pairing strengths are linked by isospin invariance, the traditional HFB approach (omitting both Coulomb antipairing and dynamic pairing) yields spontaneous fission half-lives similar to the more complete RVAP-PNP calculation, validating the traditional approach for such forces. For interactions where proton and neutron pairing strengths are fitted separately to experimental data (as noted by Baran (2015)), the dynamic pairing effect alone would be relevant and a reduction of collective inertias by a factor of two is expected.
