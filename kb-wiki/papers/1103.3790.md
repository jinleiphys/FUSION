# Nonextensive statistical effects in protoneutron stars

> We investigate the bulk properties of protoneutron stars in the framework of a relativistic mean field theory based on nonextensive statistical mechanics, characterized by power-law quantum distributions. We study the relevance of nonextensive statistical effects on the beta-stable equation of state at fixed entropy per baryon, in presence and in absence of trapped neutrinos, for nucleonic and hyperonic matter. We show that nonextensive statistical effects could play a crucial role in the structure and in the evolution of the protoneutron stars also for small deviations from the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

## Key claim

Nonextensive statistical mechanics, characterized by power-law quantum distributions with a small deviation parameter \(q\) near 1, can significantly alter the bulk properties and evolution of protoneutron stars (PNS) even for small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, especially during the maximum heating phase at high entropy per baryon and in the presence of hyperons.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field theory (RMF) with the GM3 parameter set, including the full baryon octet and leptons, to compute the beta-stable equation of state (EOS) at fixed entropy per baryon. They use \(q\)-deformed Fermi-Dirac distributions derived from Tsallis nonextensive statistics to describe particle occupation numbers, and solve the mean-field equations for the meson fields (\(\sigma, \omega, \rho\)) self-consistently. The analysis covers three PNS evolutionary stages: a lepton-rich phase with trapped neutrinos (\(s=1, Y_L=0.4\)), a maximum heating phase with free neutrinos (\(s=2, Y_{\nu_e}=0\)), and a cold catalyzed phase (\(s=0, Y_{\nu_e}=0\)), for \(q\) values from 0.97 to 1.03.

## Key numbers

- For the maximum heating phase (\(s=2, Y_{\nu_e}=0\)) with hyperons at baryon density \(\rho_B = 5\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0 = 0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\) is the saturation density), the temperature is \(T \cong 37\) MeV for \(q=1\), \(T \cong 51\) MeV for \(q=1.03\), and \(T \cong 21\) MeV for \(q=0.97\).
- Maximum gravitational mass for nucleons-only stars (\(np\)) in the lepton-rich phase (\(s=1, Y_L=0.4\)): \(M_{\rm max} = 1.96\, M_\odot\) (baryonic 2.20 \(M_\odot\)) for \(q=0.97\); \(1.97\, M_\odot\) (2.21 \(M_\odot\)) for \(q=1.00\); \(1.98\, M_\odot\) (2.23 \(M_\odot\)) for \(q=1.03\).
- Maximum gravitational mass for nucleons-plus-hyperons stars (\(npH\)) in the maximum heating phase (\(s=2, Y_{\nu_e}=0\)): \(M_{\rm max} = 1.55\, M_\odot\) (baryonic 1.69 \(M_\odot\)) for \(q=0.97\); \(1.59\, M_\odot\) (1.74 \(M_\odot\)) for \(q=1.00\); \(1.71\, M_\odot\) (1.90 \(M_\odot\)) for \(q=1.03\).
- For cold catalyzed stars (\(s=0, Y_{\nu_e}=0\)) with \(q=1\): \(M_{\rm max} = 2.05\, M_\odot\) (baryonic 2.39 \(M_\odot\)) for \(np\); \(M_{\rm max} = 1.57\, M_\odot\) (baryonic 1.76 \(M_\odot\)) for \(npH\).
- Central baryon density for \(npH\) stars in the maximum heating phase: \(\rho_c = 5.59\,\rho_0\) for \(q=0.97\); \(5.44\,\rho_0\) for \(q=1.00\); \(5.05\,\rho_0\) for \(q=1.03\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of protoneutron star structure using relativistic mean-field models (e.g., Glendenning 1985, Prakash et al. 1997) by incorporating Tsallis nonextensive statistics, which had previously been applied to high-energy physics and nuclear matter EOS (e.g., Wilk and Wlodarczyk 2000, Pereira et al. 2009). The results show that small deviations from Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (\(q\) near 1) can alter the temperature, particle composition, and maximum mass of PNS, particularly during the deleptonization era, with implications for neutrino opacity and cooling timescales. This enables future studies of neutrino luminosity and the possible formation of black holes during PNS evolution, as the metastability window for gravitational collapse depends on the nonextensive parameter \(q\).
