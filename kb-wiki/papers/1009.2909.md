# Binding energy per nucleon and hadron properties in nuclear matter

> We investigate the binding energy per nucleon and hadron properties in infinite and homogeneous nuclear matter within the framework of the in-medium modified Skyrme model. We first consider the medium modifications of the single hadron properties by introducing the optical potential for pion fields into the original Lagrangian of the Skyrme model. The parameters of the optical potential are well fitted to the low-energy phenomenology of pion-nucleus scattering. Furthermore, the Skyrme term is also modified in such a way that the model reproduces bulk properties of nuclear matter, in particular, the binding energy per nucleon. The present approach is self-consistent: the single hadron properties in a nuclear medium, their effective in-medium interactions, and the bulk matter properties are treated on the same footing.

## Key claim

The in-medium modified Skyrme model can reproduce the binding energy per nucleon of nuclear matter only if the quartic Skyrme term is also modified via a density-dependent functional, not just the kinetic and mass terms as in earlier work; the best fit to the ground state of nuclear matter is achieved with an exponential form of that functional.

## Method

The authors start from the Skyrme Lagrangian and introduce three medium functionals: α_s and α_p (fitted to low-energy pion-nucleus scattering data) modify the mass and kinetic terms, and a new functional γ(ρ) modifies the quartic Skyrme term. They solve the classical field equation for a single skyrmion in homogeneous nuclear matter of constant density, then collectively quantize the skyrmion to obtain the in-medium nucleon mass, Δ–N mass splitting, and πNN coupling constant. The form of γ(ρ) is fitted so that the binding energy per nucleon (defined as the difference between the in-medium and free nucleon masses) has a minimum at normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.5 m_π³ and reproduces the volume term of the semiempirical mass formula.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: ~15.7 MeV (minimum at ρ₀ = 0.5 m_π³).
- Compressibility K: ranges from 1647 MeV (c₀ = 0.21 m_π⁻³) down to 580 MeV (c₀ = 0.09 m_π⁻³), with b₀ = −0.024 m_π⁻¹ and g′ = 0.7.
- Effective Δ–N mass difference at ρ₀: 105.21 MeV (c₀ = 0.21 m_π⁻³) to 170.34 MeV (c₀ = 0.09 m_π⁻³), for b₀ = −0.024 m_π⁻¹.
- Symmetry energy at ρ₀: 14.19 MeV (c₀ = 0.09 m_π⁻³) and 8.71 MeV (c₀ = 0.21 m_π⁻³), estimated from E_sym = (1/12) m*_{Δ–N}.
- Critical density for skyrmion stability with c₀ = 0.21 m_π⁻³: ρ_crit ≈ 2.54 ρ₀, above which the πNN coupling constant drops to zero and no stable soliton exists.
- Free-space parameters: F_π = 108.78 MeV, e = 4.85, m_π = 134.98 MeV (neutral pion mass).

## Context

This work extends the in-medium modified Skyrme model of Rakhimov (1996) and Rakhimov (1998) by additionally modifying the quartic Skyrme term, which earlier approaches had left intact. It contrasts with classical crystalline Skyrmion studies (Klebanov 1985, Lee 2003) and with many-body approaches such as Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Ter Haar 1986, Brockmann 1990) and the Walecka model (Serot 1984). The self-consistent treatment enables future studies of finite nuclei and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, and the results suggest that smaller values of the P-wave scattering volume c₀ than those from pionic atom analysis are preferred for realistic compressibility.
