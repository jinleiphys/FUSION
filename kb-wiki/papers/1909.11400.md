# Recent progress on hypernuclei

> Some of last year's progress made in hypernuclear physics is reviewed as follows: (i) resolving the $_╬ø^5$He overbinding problem in single-$╬ø$ hypernuclei [1]; (ii) arguing that the onset of binding double-$╬ø$ hypernuclei is most likely at $A$=5, with the neutral systems $_{╬ø╬ø}^{\,\,3}$n and $_{╬ø╬ø}^{\,\,4}$n unbound by a large margin [2]; and (iii) revising the calculated value of the loosely bound $_╬ø^3$H lifetime to a level of $\sim$20% shorter than the free $╬ø$ lifetime [3], given recent claims from relativistic heavy ion experiments that $Žä(_╬ø^3$H) is shorter than $Žä_╬ø$ by as much as $\approx$(30$\pm$8)%. Also discussed briefly in this context is the lifetime expected for the questionable $_╬ø^3$n hypernucleus.

## Key claim

The paper reviews three recent advances in hypernuclear physics, each with specific hedges: (i) the overbinding problem of $_\Lambda^5$He is resolved within pionless effective field theory (EFT) by fitting four three-body low-energy constants (LECs) to $s$-shell hypernuclei, but only for cutoff values $\lambda \gtrsim 1.5$ fm$^{-1}$ where the $\Lambda NN$ components become repulsive; (ii) the onset of binding in double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei is argued to be most likely at $A=5$, with the neutral systems $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\,\,3}$n and $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\,\,4}$n unbound by a large margin, though $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\,\,4}$H is not definitively excluded; (iii) the calculated $_\Lambda^3$H lifetime is revised to $\sim$20% shorter than the free $\Lambda$ lifetime, but reproducing the $\approx$30% enhancement suggested by some heavy-ion experiments remains inconceivable.

## Method

The author reviews three topics using leading-order (LO) pionless EFT ($\nopieft$) with two-body and three-body $s$-wave contact interactions, regularized by a Gaussian cutoff $\lambda$, and solved via the stochastic variational method (SVM) for $s$-shell hypernuclei. For the $_\Lambda^5$He overbinding problem, four two-body LECs (from $NN$ and $\Lambda N$ scattering lengths) and four three-body LECs (one $NNN$ and three $\Lambda NN$) are fitted to binding energies of $^3$H, $_\Lambda^3$H, $_\Lambda^4$H$_{\rm g.s.}$, and $_\Lambda^4$H$_{\rm exc.}$, then used to predict $B_\Lambda(_\Lambda^5$He). For double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei, the $\Lambda\Lambda$ LEC is fitted to the $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length $a_{\Lambda\Lambda}$, and the $\Lambda\Lambda N$ LEC is fitted to $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\,\,6}$He)=0.67$\pm$0.17 MeV. For the $_\Lambda^3$H lifetime, three-body Faddeev equations are solved to produce the wavefunction, and the decay rate is computed including pion final-state interactions (FSI).

## Key numbers

- $B_\Lambda^{\rm exp}(_\Lambda^5$He) = 3.12$\pm$0.02 MeV; LO $\chi$EFT with cutoff 600 MeV gives $B_\Lambda(_\Lambda^5$He) = 5.82$\pm$0.02 MeV (overbinding), while LO $\chi$EFT with cutoff 700 MeV gives 4.43$\pm$0.02 MeV.
- $B_\Lambda^{\rm exp}(_\Lambda^3$H) = 0.13$\pm$0.05 MeV; $B_\Lambda^{\rm exp}(_\Lambda^4$He$_{\rm g.s.}$) = 2.39$\pm$0.03 MeV; $E_x^{\rm exp}(_\Lambda^4$He$_{\rm exc.}$) = 1.406$\pm$0.003 MeV.
- $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\,\,6}$He) = $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\,\,6}$He) $-$ 2$B_\Lambda(_\Lambda^5$He) = 0.67$\pm$0.17 MeV (Nagara event).
- For $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -0.8$ fm and cutoff $\lambda=4$ fm$^{-1}$, $B_\Lambda(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\,\,5}$H) = 1.21 MeV and $B_\Lambda(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\,\,6}$He) = 3.28 MeV (constrained by $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}=0.67$ MeV).
- Free $\Lambda$ lifetime $\tau_\Lambda = 263\pm2$ ps; recent heavy-ion experiments report $\tau(_\Lambda^3$H) shorter by $\approx(30\pm8)\%$; the 2019 calculation by Gal and Garcilazo gives $\Gamma(_\Lambda^3$H)/$\Gamma_\Lambda = 1.23$ (i.e., $\sim$20% shorter lifetime).
- Estimated $_\Lambda^3$n lifetime $\approx 368$ ps, compared to 181$^{+30}_{-24}\pm$25 ps or 190$^{+47}_{-35}\pm$36 ps from HypHI claims.

## Context

This review extends the author's prior work on pionless EFT for hypernuclei (Gal and Garcilazo, 2018; Contessi et al., 2018; Contessi et al., 2019) and addresses the long-standing overbinding problem first stated by Dalitz et al. (1972) for $_\Lambda^5$He. The double-$\Lambda$ binding onset analysis builds on the Nagara event (2001; Ahn et al., 2013) and earlier Faddeev calculations (e.g., Filikhin and Gal, 2002). The $_\Lambda^3$H lifetime puzzle is motivated by recent relativistic heavy-ion measurements (STAR, 2010; HypHI, 2013; ALICE, 2016, 2018, 2019) that suggest a shorter lifetime than the free $\Lambda$, contradicting earlier calculations by Rayet and Dalitz (1966) and Congleton (1992). The results enable future shell-model studies of $p$-shell hypernuclei and may inform neutron star matter calculations addressing the "hyperon puzzle" (Lonardoni et al., 2014).
