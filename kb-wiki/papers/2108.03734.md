# Singularities and Accumulation of Singularities of $π$N Scattering amplitudes

> It is demonstrated that for the isospin $I=1/2$ $π$N scattering amplitude, $T^{I=1/2}(s,t)$, $s={(m_N^2-m_π^2)^2}/{m_N^2}$ and $s=m_N^2+2m_π^2$ are two accumulation points of poles on the second sheet of complex $s$ plane, and are hence accumulation of singularities of $T^{I=1/2}(s,t)$. For $T^{I=3/2}(s,t)$, $s={(m_N^2-m_π^2)^2}/{m_N^2}$ is the accumulation point of poles on the second sheet of complex $s$ plane. The proof is valid up to all orders of chiral expansions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for the isospin \(I=1/2\) \(\pi\)N scattering amplitude, the points \(s=(m_N^2-m_\pi^2)^2/m_N^2\) and \(s=m_N^2+2m_\pi^2\) are accumulation points of poles on the second sheet of the complex \(s\)-plane, and for \(I=3/2\) only the first of these is an accumulation point; the proof is valid to all orders of chiral perturbation theory, but relies on the specific structure of the \(u\)-channel nucleon-exchange term whose coefficient is immune to chiral corrections.

## Method

The authors analyze the analytic structure of partial-wave \(\pi\)N scattering amplitudes by constructing the \(S\)-matrix from helicity amplitudes and parity eigenstates. Using the explicit forms of the scalar functions \(A^I\) and \(B^I\) at leading order in chiral perturbation theory, they extract the logarithmic singularities arising from the \(u\)-channel nucleon pole and show that the \(S\)-matrix elements contain terms proportional to \(\ln[(s-c_L)/(s-c_R)]\). By examining the behavior of the \(S\)-matrix near the points \(c_L\) and \(c_R\) for arbitrarily high total angular momentum \(J\), they prove that zeros of the \(S\)-matrix (and hence poles on the second sheet) accumulate at these points.

## Key numbers

- For \(I=1/2\): \(c_L = (m_N^2-m_\pi^2)^2/m_N^2\) and \(c_R = m_N^2+2m_\pi^2\) are the two accumulation points of poles on the second sheet.
- For \(I=3/2\): \(c_L = (m_N^2-m_\pi^2)^2/m_N^2\) is the accumulation point of poles on the second sheet.
- The branch points of the partial-wave amplitudes are at \(s_L = (m_N-m_\pi)^2\) and \(s_R = (m_N+m_\pi)^2\).
- The kinematic factor \(\rho(s) = \sqrt{(s-s_L)(s-s_R)}/s\).
- The coefficient of the \(1/(u-m_N^2)\) term in the \(B\) functions is proportional to \(m_N^2 g^2 / F^2\), where \(F\) is the pion decay constant and \(g\) the axial coupling.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of virtual poles in the \(S_{11}\) channel (Li, 2021) to all partial waves \(L_{2I,2J}\). It builds on the observation by Kennedy and Spearman (1962) that the \(u\)-channel nucleon exchange produces a cut \([c_L, c_R]\) in the partial-wave amplitude. The proof of accumulation of singularities follows the approach of Martin (1970) for \(\pi\pi\) scattering, but is here carried out within the framework of chiral perturbation theory to all orders. The results imply that dispersion representations for \(\pi\)N partial waves must be modified to account for these virtual poles, though the net effect on phase shifts is vanishingly small when the poles are taken at the accumulation points.
