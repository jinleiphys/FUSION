# Mott-hadron resonance gas and lattice QCD thermodynamics

> A unified equation of state for quark-hadron matter is presented in the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck form. It follows from a $Φ-$derivable approach to the thermodynamic potential where the ansatz for the $Φ$ functional contains all 2PI diagrams at two-loop order formed with quark cluster Green's functions for quark, diquark, meson and baryon propagators. We present numerical results using an effective model for the generic behaviour of hadron masses and phase shifts at finite temperature which shares basic features with recent developments within the PNJL model for correlations in quark matter. We obtain the transition between a hadron resonance gas phase and the quark gluon plasma where the Mott dissociation of hadrons is encoded in the hadronic phase shifts. The resulting thermodynamics is in very good agreement with recent lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified equation of state for quark-hadron matter that reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics across the crossover region, but the agreement depends on including both a collisional width from quark-hadron scattering and virial corrections from parton rescattering at two-loop order; without these contributions, strong discrepancies remain.

## Method

The authors construct a thermodynamic potential using a Phi-derivable approach with two-loop 2PI diagrams formed from quark, diquark, meson, and baryon propagators. They model the hadron sector with temperature-dependent masses and phase shifts that encode Mott dissociation, using a generic phase shift function that satisfies the Levinson theorem. The quark sector is described within a Polyakov-loop improved Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, with temperature-dependent quark masses obtained from lattice QCD data for the chiral condensate, and the total pressure is the sum of hadronic, PNJL, and perturbative contributions.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature from hadron dominance to quark-gluon dominance: T_trans = 156 MeV, where both partial pressures are equal.
- Scaled pion and kaon pressure components both peak at T = 153 MeV, related to Mott dissociation of these states.
- Universal cross section for quark-hadron scattering: sigma = 150 mb, used in the collisional broadening parameter.
- Light quark mass at zero temperature: m_0 = 5.5 MeV; strange quark mass: m_s = 100 MeV.
- Parameters for hadron width ansatz: a = 2.5 GeV, b = 8.0 (dimensionless).

## Context

This work extends the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach previously applied to nuclear matter (Ropke 2012, Typel 2009) and quark matter (Blaschke 2015) to a unified description of QCD thermodynamics. It improves upon an earlier version of the model (Blaschke 2015) that showed discrepancies with lattice QCD data, and it provides a basis for extending the QCD phase diagram to finite chemical potentials where lattice QCD cannot currently reach due to the sign problem. The results are compared directly to lattice QCD simulations by Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2014).
