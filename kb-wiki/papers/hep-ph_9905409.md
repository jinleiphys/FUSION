# High-Energy Hadron-Induced Dilepton Production from Nucleons and Nuclei

> We review the production of high-mass lepton pairs in fixed-target experiments, including both Drell-Yan (DY) and heavy quarkonium (J/Psi and Upsilons) production.

## Key claim

This review synthesizes fixed-target dilepton production data to show that the Drell-Yan process provides a clean probe of parton distributions in nucleons and nuclei, while quarkonium production exhibits large, poorly understood nuclear effects that do not scale with the target momentum fraction \(x_2\).

## Method

The authors review experimental results from fixed-target dimuon spectrometers at Fermilab (E605, E772, E789, E866) and CERN SPS (NA10, NA50, NA51), covering both continuum Drell-Yan pairs and heavy quarkonium states (\(J/\psi\), \(\psi'\), \(\Upsilon(1S)\), \(\Upsilon(2S)\), \(\Upsilon(3S)\)). They compare data to next-to-leading-order QCD calculations and to parton distribution function parameterizations such as CTEQ4M, MRS(R2), and MRST. For nuclear targets, they examine factorization, shadowing, multiple scattering, and energy loss effects.

## Key numbers

* The E772 measurement of the Drell-Yan angular distribution in 800 GeV/c \(p + Cu\) collisions yields \(\lambda = 0.96 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.06\) (systematic) in the Collins-Soper frame, consistent with the parton-model prediction of \(\lambda = 1\).
* The NA51 experiment using 450 GeV/c protons finds \(\bar u / \bar d = 0.51 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.05\) at \(\langle x \rangle = 0.18\).
* The E866 experiment determines the integral \(\int_0^1 [\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)] dx = 0.100 \pm 0.007 \pm 0.017\) at \(Q^2 = 54\) GeV\(^2\)/c\(^2\), roughly 2/3 of the NMC value \(0.148 \pm 0.039\).
* The NMC measurement of the Gottfried integral gives \(I_G = 0.235 \pm 0.026\) over \(0.004 \le x \le 0.8\), significantly below the Gottfried Sum Rule value of 1/3.

## Context

This review extends the comprehensive 1990 review of Freudenreich by covering new experimental results from the 1990s, particularly the E866 measurement of the \(\bar d/\bar u\) asymmetry and nuclear dependence studies. The Drell-Yan data have become standard input to global parton distribution fits (e.g., MRST), while the quarkonium results inform searches for the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions. The review enables future work on polarized Drell-Yan and \(W^\pm\) production at RHIC, and on improved theoretical understanding of the Lam-Tung relation violation and higher-twist effects in pion-induced Drell-Yan.
