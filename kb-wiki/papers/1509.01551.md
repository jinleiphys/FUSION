# Toward an understanding of the $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ puzzle for heavy quarks

> One of the primary aims of the ongoing nuclear collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies is to create a Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP). The heavy quarks constitutes a unique probe of the QGP properties. Both at RHIC and LHC energies a puzzling relation between the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}(p_T)$ and the elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ related to heavy quark has been observed which challenged all the existing models.\\ We discuss how the temperature dependence of the heavy quark drag coefficient can address for a large part of such a puzzle. We have considered four different models to evaluate the temperature dependence of drag and diffusion coefficients propagating through a quark gluon plasma (QGP). All the four different models are set to reproduce the same $R_{AA}(p_T)$ experimentally observed at RHIC energy. We have found that for the same $R_{AA}(p_T)$ one can generate $2-3$ times more $v_{2}$ depending on the temperature dependence of the heavy quark drag coefficient.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the puzzling simultaneous description of the heavy-quark nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) and elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) at RHIC and LHC can be largely resolved by the temperature dependence of the heavy-quark drag coefficient, but only under the condition that different models are tuned to reproduce the same \(R_{AA}\); the resulting \(v_2\) can then vary by a factor of 2–3 depending on that temperature dependence.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic Langevin (Fokker-Planck) equation to simulate heavy-quark propagation through a quark-gluon plasma, with the bulk medium evolved via a 3+1D relativistic transport code. They considered four different modelings of the heavy-quark drag coefficient: perturbative QCD (pQCD) with a Debye-screened running coupling; gauge/string duality (AdS/CFT); a quasi-particle model (QPM) with temperature-dependent masses and coupling; and a variant with QPM coupling but massless light partons. All four models were rescaled to reproduce the same \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) measured at RHIC for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) AGeV. For comparison, a subset of cases was also treated with a full Boltzmann collision integral.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) AGeV, Au+Au minimum bias), for the same \(R_{AA}(p_T)\), the elliptic flow \(v_2\) can differ by a factor of 2–3 depending on the temperature dependence of the heavy-quark drag coefficient.
* The drag coefficient from gauge/string duality is given by \(\Gamma_{conf}= C\, T_{QCD}^2 / M_c\) with \(C=2.1\pm0.5\).
* The QPM coupling is parameterized as \(g^2(T)=48\pi^2 / [(11N_c-2N_f)\ln[\lambda(T/T_c - T_s/T_c)]^2]\) with \(\lambda=2.6\) and \(T/T_s=0.57\).
* The Peterson fragmentation parameter for charm quarks is \(\epsilon_c=0.04\) and for bottom quarks \(\epsilon_c=0.005\).
* The comparison of \(R_{AA}\) vs \(v_2\) is shown at a fixed transverse momentum \(p_T=1.3\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to describe heavy-quark observables using Fokker-Planck and Boltzmann approaches, such as those by Rapp (2010), Das (2015), and Gossiaux (2012), which struggled to simultaneously reproduce \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\). The paper shows that the temperature dependence of the drag coefficient, rather than its overall magnitude, is a key missing ingredient, and that the Boltzmann approach yields larger \(v_2\) than the Fokker-Planck approximation for the same \(R_{AA}\). This enables future studies to constrain the temperature dependence of heavy-quark energy loss by combining \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) measurements.
