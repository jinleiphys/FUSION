# High energy solar neutrinos and p-wave contributions to ^3He(p,\nue^+)^4He

> High energy solar neutrinos can come from the hep reaction ^3He(p,\nue^+)^4He with a large end point energy of 18.8 MeV. Understanding the hep reaction may be important for interpreting solar neutrino spectra. We calculate the contribution of the axial charge transition $^3P_0\to ^1S_0$ to the hep thermonuclear S factor using a one-body reaction model involving a nucleon moving in optical potentials. Our result is comparable to or larger than previous calculations of the s-wave Gamow Teller contribution. This indicates that the hep reaction may have p-wave strength leading to an enhancement of the S factor.

# High energy solar neutrinos and p-wave contributions to ^3He(p,\nu e^+)^4He

## Key claim
The axial-charge transition ^3P_0 to ^1S_0 in the hep reaction may contribute a thermonuclear S factor comparable to or larger than the previously calculated s-wave Gamow-Teller contribution, indicating that p-wave strength could enhance the total S factor. This result is based on a simple one-body reaction model and the author notes that the total S factor requires coherent addition of several other p-wave transitions, other operators, and the original s-wave strength.

## Method
The author models the ^4He final state as a neutron moving in a real Wood-Saxon optical potential with parameters adjusted to reproduce the charge radius and neutron separation energy of ^4He. The ^3P_0 initial state is modeled as a p-wave scattering solution in a Coulomb potential of a uniform sphere plus the same Wood-Saxon potential, with the strength adjusted to approximately reproduce p+^3He phase shifts. The one-body axial charge operator is evaluated between these states to compute the cross section and S factor at 7.5 keV center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers
- The hep reaction end point energy is 18.8 MeV
- The present estimate for the thermonuclear S factor is S_0 = 2.3 x 10^{-20} keV-b, based on calculations by Carlson et al. (1991)
- The calculated ^3P_0 axial-charge contribution yields S_{^3P_0} = 1.67 x 10^{-20} keV-b at 7.5 keV center-of-mass energy
- The cross section at 7.5 keV is sigma = 5.28 x 10^{-30} b
- The Fermi constant used is G = 1.151 x 10^{-11} MeV^{-2}
- The lepton phase space factor is f = 2.544 x 10^6
- The axial coupling constant is g_a = 1.262
- The Wood-Saxon potential parameters: diffuseness a = 0.6 fm, radius R_0 = 2 fm, bound state strength V_0 = -63.9 MeV, scattering state strength V_0 = -30 MeV
- The neutron separation energy of ^4He is 20.58 MeV
- Setting the p-wave optical potential to zero (V_0 = 0) reduces the axial-charge matrix element by 30%
- Using V_0 = -63.9 MeV for the scattering state produces a strong p-wave resonance and the S factor rises by a factor of 37 to 62.5 x 10^{-20} keV-b (noted as probably unrealistic)

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Carlson et al. (1991) and Schiavilla et al. (1992), who found a small S factor due to cancellations and destructive interference between one-body and meson exchange current contributions, but only considered a single partial wave and the axial-three-vector part of the weak current. Earlier work by Werntz and Brennan (1967, 1973) estimated p-wave resonance contributions, but no nonresonant p-wave calculations existed previously. The result suggests that future microscopic four-body calculations should include all s and p partial waves, all vector and axial parts of the weak current, and the spatial dependence of lepton wave functions, and that solar neutrino experiments such as SuperKamiokande, SNO, and Icarus should search for hep neutrinos at energies near 14 MeV and above.
