# Different pole structures in line shapes of the $X(3872)$

> We introduce a near-threshold parameterization that is more general than the effective-range expansion up to and including the effective-range because it can also handle with a near-threshold zero in the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ $S$-wave. In terms of it we analyze the CDF data on inclusive $p\bar{p}$ scattering to $J/ψπ^+π^-$, and the Belle and BaBar data on $B$ decays to $K\, J/ψπ^+π^-$ and $K D\bar{D}^{*0}$ around the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold. It is shown that data can be reproduced with similar quality for the $X(3872)$ being a bound {\it and/or} a virtual state. We also find that the $X(3872)$ might be a higher-order virtual-state pole (double or triplet pole), in the limit in which the small $D^{*0}$ width vanishes. Once the latter is restored the corrections to the pole position are non-analytic and much bigger than the $D^{*0}$ width itself. The $X(3872)$ compositeness coefficient in $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ ranges from nearly 0 up to 1 in the different scenarios.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the $X(3872)$ line shapes from CDF, Belle, and BaBar data can be reproduced with similar quality whether the $X(3872)$ is a bound state, a virtual state, or both simultaneously, and even suggests it might be a higher-order virtual-state pole (double or triplet pole) in the limit of a vanishing $D^{*0}$ width. The key hedge is that the compositeness coefficient in $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ ranges from nearly 0 up to 1 across these different scenarios, meaning the data alone cannot uniquely determine its nature.

## Method
The authors introduce a near-threshold parameterization for the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ $S$-wave that is more general than the standard effective-range expansion (ERE) because it can explicitly account for a near-threshold zero (a Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson pole). They fit this parameterization to the CDF data on inclusive $p\bar{p}$ scattering to $J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ and the Belle and BaBar data on $B$ decays to $K J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ and $K D\bar{D}^{*0}$ around the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold, analyzing the resulting pole structures.

## Key numbers
- The $X(3872)$ nominal mass relative to the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold is $E_X = -0.12 \pm 0.20$ MeV, as given by the PDG.
- The $D^{*0}$ width is $\Gamma_*$, and the energy delivered in a $D^{*0}$ decay at rest is $\delta = M_{D^{*0}} - M_{D^0} - M_{\pi^0} \simeq 7.2$ MeV.
- The $X(3872)$ total width is constrained to be $\Gamma_X < 1.2$ MeV by the PDG.
- The compositeness coefficient for the $X(3872)$ in $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ ranges from nearly 0 up to 1 across the different fitted scenarios.

## Context
This work extends the analyses of the $X(3872)$ using effective-range-expansion (ERE) parameterizations by Hanhart (2014) and Braaten (2014), and Flatté parameterizations by Zheng, Kalashnikova (2014), and Qiang (2014). It directly addresses a criticism by Baru (2016) regarding the necessity of three shallow poles for a near-threshold zero, showing that such a zero can occur with only two poles. The new parameterization enables a systematic exploration of the $X(3872)$'s pole structure, including the possibility of higher-order virtual-state poles, which has implications for applying Weinberg's compositeness theorem.
