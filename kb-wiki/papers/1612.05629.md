# A Blast Wave Model With Viscous Corrections

> Hadronic observables in the final stage of heavy ion collision can be described well by fluid dynamics or blast wave parameterizations. We improve existing blast wave models by adding shear viscous corrections to the particle distributions in the Navier-Stokes approximation. The specific shear viscosity $η/s$ of a hadron gas at the freeze-out temperature is a new parameter in this model. We extract the blast wave parameters with viscous corrections from experimental data which leads to constraints on the specific shear viscosity at kinetic freeze-out. Preliminary results show $η/s$ is rather small.

# A Blast Wave Model With Viscous Corrections

## Key claim
The specific shear viscosity eta/s at kinetic freeze-out is extracted from experimental data using a blast wave model with viscous corrections in the Navier-Stokes approximation, yielding a rather small value; however, the result is preliminary and conflates the effects of shear viscosity on freeze-out only, not on the full dynamical evolution.

## Method
The authors construct a blast wave parameterization of the freeze-out hypersurface at constant proper time tau = 9 fm/c, with a parameterized transverse flow velocity field including an elliptic deformation. They compute the shear stress tensor in the first-order Navier-Stokes approximation from the velocity field, then incorporate the viscous correction delta f into the Cooper-Frye freeze-out formula, making the particle distribution directly proportional to eta/s. A Bayesian analysis using the MADAI Gaussian process emulator is performed to fit the model parameters to ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV in the 40%-50% centrality bin, using transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow v2 for protons, kaons, and pions.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature T = 0.119 GeV from the best fit to ALICE data.
- Average surface velocity alpha_0 = 0.838 c from the best fit.
- Velocity profile power n = 1.07 from the best fit.
- Ratio of event plane radii R_y/R_x = 1.31 from the best fit.
- Velocity deformation alpha_2 = 0.0372 c from the best fit.
- Specific shear viscosity eta/s = 1.13/(4 pi) from the best fit.
- Fit ranges: 0.325-2.75 GeV/c for protons, 0.225-1.95 GeV/c for kaons, and 0.425-1.25 GeV/c for pions.
- Freeze-out hypersurface at constant proper time tau = 9 fm/c; product R_x R_y = 12 fm^2.

## Context
This work extends existing blast wave parameterizations by adding shear viscous corrections, similar to models developed by previous authors (referenced as [1] and [2] in the paper). Typical values extracted for eta/s from full viscous fluid dynamics or fluid dynamics plus hadronic transport are (1-2)/(4 pi), averaged over all temperatures and including freeze-out. This blast wave approach is complementary because it isolates the specific shear viscosity at kinetic freeze-out only, rather than averaging over the entire dynamical evolution. The results enable future studies of eta/s across more centrality bins and applications to other phenomena such as quark recombination.
