# Is there a pion-Lambda-nucleon bound state?

> We have searched for bound states in the pion-Lambda-nucleon system by solving the nonrelativistic Faddeev equations, as well as a relativistic version, with input separable pion-nucleon, pion-Lambda, and Lambda-nucleon interactions. A bound-state solution, driven by the Delta(1232) and Sigma(1385) p-wave meson-baryon resonances, was found in the channel (I,JP)=(3/2,2+), provided the Lambda lab momentum at which the Lambda-nucleon S=1,L=0,J=1 phase shift becomes negative is larger than about 750-800 MeV/c. Other strange and charmed pion-baryon-baryon systems that might have bound states of a similar nature are listed.

# Is there a pion-Lambda-nucleon bound state?

## Key claim
A bound state in the $\pi\Lambda N$ system with quantum numbers $(I,J^P)=(3/2,2^+)$ is likely to exist, but only if the $\Lambda$ laboratory momentum at which the $\Lambda N$ $^3S_1$ phase shift becomes negative is larger than about 750-800 MeV/c; the existence is conditional on the short-range repulsion in the $\Lambda N$ interaction being sufficiently soft.

## Method
The authors solved nonrelativistic and relativistic three-body Faddeev equations for the $\pi\Lambda N$ system using separable pairwise interactions. The $\pi N$ and $\pi\Lambda$ interactions were modeled as rank-one separable potentials reproducing the $\Delta(1232)$ and $\Sigma(1385)$ $p$-wave resonances, while the $\Lambda N$ $^3S_1$ interaction was modeled as a rank-two separable potential with both attractive and repulsive components, fitted to a scattering length of -1.86 fm and effective range of 3.13 fm. The bound-state search was performed by locating zeros of the Fredholm determinant on the real energy axis.

## Key numbers
- $\Lambda N$ $^3S_1$ scattering length: $a = -1.86$ fm; effective range: $r_0 = 3.13$ fm (from model ESC04d)
- Nonrelativistic binding energies $B_{\pi\Lambda N}$ range from 140 MeV (with no repulsion) down to unbound, depending on the $\Lambda N$ repulsion parameters; for example, with $\alpha_a = 1.8$ fm$^{-1}$, $\alpha_r = 4.0$ fm$^{-1}$: $B_{\pi\Lambda N} = 72$ MeV (Eq. 4) or 67 MeV (Eq. 5)
- Relativistic binding energies range from 152 MeV down to unbound; for example, with $\alpha_a = 2.0$ fm$^{-1}$, $\alpha_r = 4.0$ fm$^{-1}$: $B_{\pi\Lambda N} = 152$ MeV
- Bound state persists when $\Lambda$ laboratory momentum $p_{\rm lab}^{(0)} > 750-800$ MeV/c (nonrelativistic) or $> 750$ MeV/c (relativistic)
- In the limit of vanishing $\Lambda N$ interaction, a robust bound state at $B_{\pi\Lambda N} = 110$ MeV is found
- For a fictitious $\pi NN$ system with symmetric baryons (Pauli-forbidden), binding energy $B_{\pi NN} = 29$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the search for $S=-1$ dibaryons beyond the $H$ dibaryon (Jaffe, 1977) and the $L=1$ $\Lambda N$ resonances predicted by Mulders et al. (1980) near the $\Sigma N$ threshold, which were not confirmed experimentally (JHK, 1992; CDG, 1992). The calculation uses Nijmegen $YN$ potential models (Rijken and Yamamoto, 2006) and compares with the Julich '04 model (Haidenbauer and Meissner, 2005) and chiral EFT (Polinder et al., 2006), which predict $p_{\rm lab}^{(0)} > 900$ MeV/c and thus favor binding. The results enable experimental searches via $(K^-,\pi^+)$ or $(\pi^-,K^+)$ reactions on deuteron targets at J-PARC, and suggest extensions to other $\pi BB'$ systems including strange ($\pi\Xi N$, $\pi\Lambda\Xi$) and charmed ($\pi N\Lambda_c$, $\pi N\Xi_c$, etc.) dibaryons.
