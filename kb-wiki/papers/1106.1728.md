# Opportunities to constrain astrophysical reaction rates for the s-process through determination of the ground state cross sections

> Modern models of s-process nucleosynthesis in stars require stellar reaction rates with high precision. Most of the neutron capture cross sections in the s-process have been measured and for an increasing number of reactions the required precision is achieved. This does not necessarily mean, however, that the stellar rates are constrained equally well because only capture on the ground state of a target is measured in the laboratory. Captures on excited states can considerably contribute to stellar rates already at typical s-process temperatures. We show that the ground state contribution X to a stellar rate is the relevant measure to identify reactions which are or could be well constrained by experiments and apply it to (n,gamma) reactions in the s-process. It is further shown that the maximally possible reduction in uncertainty of a rate through determination of the g.s. cross section is directly given by X. An error analysis of X is presented and it is found that X is a robust measure with overall small uncertainties. Several specific examples (neutron capture on 79Se, 95Zr, 121Sn, 187Os, and 193Pt) are discussed in detail. The ground state contributions for a set of 411 neutron capture reactions around the s-process path are presented in a table. This allows to identify reactions which may be better constrained by experiments and such which cannot be constrained by only measuring ground state cross sections (and thus require supplementary studies). General trends and implications are discussed.

## Key claim

The ground state contribution \(X\) to a stellar reaction rate, rather than the stellar enhancement factor (SEF), is the relevant measure to identify which \((n,\gamma)\) reactions in the \(s\)-process can be well constrained by laboratory measurements of ground-state cross sections; the maximally possible reduction in the uncertainty of a stellar rate through determination of the ground-state cross section is directly given by \(X\), and this holds even when the SEF is close to unity.

## Method

The authors computed the ground state contribution \(X = w_0 R_0 / R^*\) (where \(w_0\) is the ground-state population weight, \(R_0\) the ground-state reactivity, and \(R^*\) the total stellar reactivity) for 411 neutron capture reactions on nuclei along the \(s\)-process path, using the reaction rate code SMARAGD version 0.8.1s with level schemes from ENSDF. They performed an error analysis showing that \(X\) is a robust measure with small overall uncertainties, because the uncertainties in the ratios of excited-state to ground-state reactivities are small (typically better than 10% for \(s\)-process nuclei) and because the uncertainty in \(X\) scales with \(X\) itself. They applied this analysis to five specific examples (neutron capture on \(^{79}\)Se, \(^{95}\)Zr, \(^{121}\)Sn, \(^{187}\)Os, and \(^{193}\)Pt) and provided a table of \(X\) values at \(kT = 30\) keV and \(kT = 80\) keV for all 411 nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the 411 nuclei studied, the ground state contribution \(X\) at \(kT = 30\) keV ranges from as low as \(X = 0.04\) (for \(^{166}\)Ho) to values near unity; the lowest values found are \(X = 0.04\) for \(^{166}\)Ho, \(X = 0.16\) for \(^{193}\)Pt, and \(X = 0.2\) for \(^{142}\)Pr.
- At \(kT = 30\) keV, most nuclei in the mass range \(150 \le A \le 190\) have \(X \approx 0.5\) to \(0.8\).
- For \(^{79}\)Se at \(kT = 30\) keV: \(G_0 = 1.027\), \(f_\mathrm{SEF} = 0.99\), and \(X = 0.98\); at \(kT = 100\) keV: \(f_\mathrm{SEF} = 0.85\) and \(X = 0.78\).
- For \(^{95}\)Zr, the first excited state is at 954 keV, so \(G_0(T) = 1\) and both \(f_\mathrm{SEF} = 1\) and \(X = 1\) over the entire \(s\)-process temperature range.
- The uncertainty in the ratio \(R_i/R_0\) for \((n,\gamma)\) reactions at \(s\)-process energies is estimated to be better than 10% for nuclei with known low-lying level schemes; a conservative uncertainty factor \(\overline{u} = 1.3\) (30%) was used for the error bars on \(X\) in the table and figures.

## Context

This work extends the standard use of the stellar enhancement factor (SEF), as compiled in Bao et al. (2000) and the KADoNiS compilations, by introducing the ground state contribution \(X\) as a more diagnostic quantity for identifying which reactions can be constrained by laboratory measurements. It enables experimentalists to prioritize targets where a measurement of the ground-state cross section will significantly reduce the uncertainty in the stellar rate (those with \(X \ge 0.8\)), and alerts modelers that for many measured reactions (especially in the deformed mass region \(150 \le A \le 190\)) the stellar rate uncertainty remains dominated by theoretical predictions of excited-state contributions even when the ground-state cross section is known to high precision. The analysis also shows that for higher temperatures (e.g., \(kT = 80\) keV or \(T = 2.5\) GK typical of the \(\gamma\)-process), \(X\) can become very small, meaning that extrapolating experimental rates from \(s\)-process temperatures to higher temperatures requires careful treatment of the increasing theory contribution.
