# The K/pi ratio from condensed Polyakov loops

> We perform a field-theoretical computation of hadron production in large systems at the QCD confinement phase transition associated with restoration of the Z(3) global symmetry. This occurs from the decay of a condensate for the Polyakov loop. From the effective potential for the Polyakov loop, its mass just below the confinement temperature T_c is in between the vacuum masses of the pion and that of the kaon. Therefore, due to phase-space restrictions the number of produced kaons is roughly an order of magnitude smaller than that of produced pions, in agreement with recent results from collisions of gold ions at the BNL-RHIC. From its mass, we estimate that the Polyakov loop condensate is characterized by a (spatial) correlation scale of 1/m_\ell ~ 1/2 fm. For systems of deconfined matter of about that size, the free energy may not be dominated by a condensate for the Polyakov loop, and so the process of hadronization may be qualitatively different as compared to large systems. In that vein, experimental data on hadron abundance ratios, for example K/pi, in high-multiplicity pp events at high energies should be very interesting.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of kaons relative to pions in heavy-ion collisions arises naturally from the decay of a Polyakov loop condensate at a hadronization temperature very close to the confinement temperature \(T_c\), because the mass of the Polyakov loop just below \(T_c\) lies between the vacuum masses of the pion and the kaon, restricting phase space for kaon production. The authors hedge that the nearly perfect agreement with experimental data is "certainly a numerical coincidence" and that variations of coupling constants and \(T_h\) would change the result.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory coupling the Polyakov loop \(\ell\) (with a \(Z(3)\)-symmetric potential fitted to lattice data for pressure, energy density, and string tension) to the \(SU(3)_R \times SU(3)_L\) linear sigma model for the pseudoscalar meson octet (pions, kaons, eta). They perform a dynamical computation in which the Polyakov loop condensate is quenched to a temperature \(T_h = 0.96 T_c\) and then decays, converting its energy into mesonic excitations; the equations of motion for 1000 momentum modes of the meson fields are solved in a mean-field factorization, and the occupation numbers of pions, kaons, and etas are extracted as functions of time.

## Key numbers

* The Polyakov loop mass just below \(T_c\) is \(m_\ell \approx 400\) MeV, which lies between the vacuum pion mass and the vacuum kaon mass.
* The spatial correlation scale of the Polyakov loop condensate is \(1/m_\ell \simeq 1/2\) fm.
* At hadronization temperature \(T_h = 0.96 T_c\), the computed charged kaon to charged pion ratio is \(K/\pi \approx 0.15\).
* When the hadronization temperature is artificially lowered to \(T_h = 0.87 T_c\), the Polyakov loop mass becomes \(m_\ell \approx 740\) MeV and the \(K/\pi\) ratio rises to about 0.8.
* The coupling between the Polyakov loop and the chiral field is set to \(h^2 \simeq 20\) to reproduce \(m_\pi(T)\) from lattice data.
* The UV cutoff for the mode sum is \(\Lambda = 10 f_\pi \simeq 1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov loop model of Pisarski (2000) and Dumitru and Pisarski (2001) by coupling it dynamically to the linear sigma model of mesons, providing a first-principles dynamical computation of hadron abundances rather than assuming statistical hadronization as in the approaches of Becattini (1996) and Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (1996). The result that hadronization occurs very close to \(T_c\) is consistent with the "limiting temperature" interpretation of statistical hadronization fits. The paper predicts that in small systems (size \(\lesssim 1/m_\ell \sim 0.5\) fm), such as high-multiplicity \(pp\) collisions, the Polyakov loop condensate may not dominate hadronization, potentially leading to different \(K/\pi\) ratios than in heavy-ion collisions; this could be tested by comparing data from the CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC with high-multiplicity \(pp\) events at the Fermilab-Tevatron (E735 collaboration).
