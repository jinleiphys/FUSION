# Towards a qualitative hadron-parton correspondence in the nuclear modification factor

> In this work, we propose a method to show the correspondence between hadron and its quark component nuclear modification factors. A parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE based on the PYTHIA6.4 is employed to calculate the hadron and its quark component nuclear modification factors in the 0-5\% most central $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV. It turns out that the hadron nuclear modification factor is usually smaller than that of its quark component. On the other hand, it is shown in our study that the "dead cone effect" is more likely to be identified with the quarks and mesons but not with the baryon states obviously.

## Key claim

The authors propose a method to establish a correspondence between the hadron nuclear modification factor and its quark component nuclear modification factor, showing that the hadron \(R_{AA}\) is generally smaller than that of its quark component in the \(p_T\) region above about 2 GeV/c. The "dead cone effect" mass hierarchy is observed for quarks and mesons but not obviously for baryons, which requires further study.

## Method

The parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE, based on PYTHIA6.4, was used to simulate 0-5% most central \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV. The hadron nuclear modification factor in the final hadronic state (FHS) and its quark component nuclear modification factor in the partonic state after parton-parton rescattering (PSw/R) were calculated separately. A correspondence between the two was constructed using the normalized transverse momentum distributions weighted by the ratio of hadron to quark multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- The hadron \(R_{AA}\) is generally smaller than its quark component \(R_{AA}\) in the \(p_T\) region above \(p_T\sim 2\) GeV/c.
- The fitted parameters for the PYTHIA model in \(Pb+Pb\) collisions were: \(K=2.7\), \(\alpha=1.3\), \(\beta=0.09\), and \(\omega=0.575\); for \(p+p\) collisions: \(K=0.7\), \(\alpha=0.3\), \(\beta=0.58\), and \(\omega=0.36\).
- A factor of 1.8 was applied to the simulated primary hadron \(p_T\) for \(p_T\) larger than 2.5 GeV/c in the PYTHIA model for \(Pb+Pb\) collisions.
- The "dead cone effect" mass hierarchy \(R_{AA}^g < R_{AA}^u < R_{AA}^s < R_{AA}^c < R_{AA}^b < R_{AA}^t\) is globally held for quarks (in PSw/R) and mesons (in FHS) but not obviously for baryons (in FHS).

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching and the nuclear modification factor in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, building on the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE (Sa et al., 2012) based on PYTHIA6.4 (Sjostrand et al., 2006). The correspondence principle approach differs from the recombination/coalescence model (Yang et al., 2003; Ko et al., 2004; Bass et al., 2003) by avoiding complications in dealing with flavor composition. The results enable a potential method to distinguish partonic and hadronic energy losses and provide a reference for reliable evaluation of hadron nuclear modification factors, while the baryon sector's deviation from the dead cone effect remains an open question for further study.
