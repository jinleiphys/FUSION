# QCD phase transitions from relativistic hadron models

> The models of translationally invariant infinite nuclear matter in the relativistic mean field models are very interesting and simple, since the nucleon can connect only to a constant vector and scalar meson field. Can one connect these to the complicated phase transitions of QCD ? For an affirmative answer to this question, one must consider models where the coupling constants to the scalar and vector fields must depend on density in a non-linear way, since as such the models are not explicitly chirally invariant. Once this is ensured, indeed one can derive a quark condensate indirectly from the energy density of nuclear matter which goes to zero at large density and temperature. The change to zero condensate indicates a smooth phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that relativistic hadron models can be connected to QCD phase transitions, but only if the coupling constants to scalar and vector fields depend on density in a non-linear way, since the standard linear models are not explicitly chirally invariant; the resulting quark condensate derived from the energy density of nuclear matter goes to zero at large density and temperature, indicating a smooth phase transition.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium quark condensate using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem applied to the energy density of infinite nuclear matter in three relativistic mean-field models: the linear Walecka model, the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) model, and the ZM3 variant. The condensate is evaluated as a function of baryon density at zero temperature and as a function of temperature at zero baryon density, with the models parameterized to reproduce the nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy -15.75 MeV at density 0.15 fm⁻³). The temperature dependence of the pion decay constant is included to account for thermal pions in the vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate at normal nuclear matter density: ⟨q̄q⟩_ρ₀ / ⟨q̄q⟩₀ = 0.69 for all three models, with vacuum condensate ⟨q̄q⟩₀ = -(230 ± 30 MeV)³
- Effective sigma commutator at ρ₀: σ_eff = 44.245 MeV for all models (using σ_N = 45 MeV)
- Binding energy per particle at saturation: -15.75 MeV at ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Incompressibility: K(ρ₀) = 156 MeV for ZM3 model, 220 MeV for ZM model
- Critical density for chiral symmetry restoration in ZM3 model: ρ_c ≈ 3.8 ρ₀
- Critical temperature for ZM3 model: T_c = 260 MeV without pions, T_c = 230 MeV with pions included
- Critical temperature for Walecka model: T_c ≈ 180 MeV (first-order transition)
- Contribution of δE to condensate at ρ₀: ⟨q̄q⟩_ρ^δE ≈ -(40 MeV)³, reducing condensate to -(203 MeV)³

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1992) who used the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to extract the quark condensate from relativistic nuclear matter models, but only considered densities up to about 1.5 ρ₀. The paper addresses the problematic behavior found by Li and Ko (1995) using the Bonn potential in the Dirac-Brueckner approach, where the condensate increases at high density contrary to chiral symmetry restoration expectations. The ZM and ZM3 models of Zimanyi and Moszkowski (1990) are shown to produce a decreasing condensate at high density while keeping the effective nucleon mass non-zero, enabling a connection between hadronic models and QCD phase transitions. The results suggest that softer equations of state with lower incompressibility favor chiral symmetry restoration, and that the nature of the phase transition (first-order versus higher-order) depends on the model.
