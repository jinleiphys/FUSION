# Gauge Invariance and the Electromagnetic Current of Composite Pions

> The Global Color-symmetry Model of QCD is extended to deal with a background electromagnetic field and the associated conserved current is identified for composite $\bar{q}q$ pion modes of the model. Although the analysis is limited to tree level in the bilocal fields that bosonize the model, the identified photon-pion vertex produces the charge form factor associated with ladder Bethe-Salpeter pion amplitudes. A Ward-Takahashi identity for this vertex is derived in terms of the effective inverse propagator for the equivalent local pion field and the intrinsic ladder Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. This identity is then used to illustrate gauge invariance by showing that identical vertex information is produced from the gauge change of the free action once proper account is taken of the gauge transformation properties of the bilocal pion fields. Comments are made on the location of the vector dominance mechanism in this treatment.

## Key claim

The Global Color-symmetry Model (GCM) of QCD can be extended to include a background electromagnetic field, and the resulting conserved current for composite pions yields the charge form factor associated with ladder Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, but only at tree level in the bilocal fields that bosonize the model.

## Method

The authors bosonize the GCM partition function in the presence of an external electromagnetic field using bilocal auxiliary fields, then localize the bilocal pion fields through an eigenfunction expansion truncated to the ground state, producing effective local pion fields with internal structure factors that become ladder Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes on the mass shell. The photon-pion vertex is derived from the quadratic pion action by functional differentiation with respect to the electromagnetic field, yielding an impulse approximation with dressed quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. A Ward-Takahashi identity for the photon-pion vertex is derived in terms of the effective inverse propagator for the local pion field and the intrinsic Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and gauge invariance is demonstrated by showing that the gauge change of the free action produces identical vertex information when the gauge transformation properties of the bilocal pion fields are properly accounted for.

## Key numbers

- The pion charge form factor \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) is obtained from the vertex \(\Lambda_\nu(P;Q) = 2P_\nu F_\pi + 2Q_\nu P \cdot Q H_\pi\), with on-mass-shell condition \((P+Q/2)^2 = (P-Q/2)^2 = -m_\pi^2\) and \(P \cdot Q = 0\).
- At \(Q=0\) on the mass shell, \(\Lambda_\nu(P;Q=0) = 2P_\nu\) and \(F_\pi(0) = 1\).
- The chiral limit (\(m_\pi = 0\)) yields \(\Gamma(q;0) \rightarrow B(q^2)/f_\pi\), where \(f_\pi\) is the pion decay constant determined so that the wavefunction renormalization \(Z(P^2)\) is unity on the mass shell.
- The PCAC result \(f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2 = -m \langle \bar{q}q \rangle\) is reproduced to first order in the current quark mass.

## Context

This work extends the Global Color-symmetry Model (GCM) previously developed by Praschifka et al. (1987) and Roberts et al. (1988) to include electromagnetic interactions, providing a quark-level foundation for the electromagnetic current of composite pions that goes beyond the contact Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Nambu, 1961). The NJL model in mean field theory produces only qualitatively correct pion charge form factors (Blin et al., 1988), whereas the GCM incorporates finite-range interactions, dynamical quark self-energies, and ladder Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes that can accommodate confinement and avoid spurious threshold effects. The localization procedure follows Cahill (1989), and the resulting framework enables a gauge-invariant treatment of the pion electromagnetic vertex that can be extended to nucleon models (Frank and Tandy, 1991) and provides a basis for investigating vector dominance mechanisms in composite meson systems.
