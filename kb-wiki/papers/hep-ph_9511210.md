# Resurrection of the Sigma Meson

> It is shown from a very general model and an analysis of data on the lightest 0++ meson nonet that the f0(980) and f0(1200) resonance poles are two manifestations of the same ss state. Similarily the a0(980) and the a0(1450) are likely to be two manifestations of the same qq state. On the other hand, the uu+dd state, when unitarized and strongly distorted by hadronic mass shifts, becomes an extremely broad Breit-Wigner-like background, m=860 MeV, Gamma=880 MeV, with its pole at s=(0.158-i0.235) GeV^2. This we identify with the sigma meson required by models for spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The sigma meson exists as an extremely broad Breit-Wigner-like background in the \( u\bar u+d\bar d \) channel, with its pole at \( s=(0.158-i0.235) \) GeV\(^2\), and the \( f_0(980) \) and \( f_0(1200) \) are both manifestations of the same \( s\bar s \) state rather than distinct quark-model states.

## Method

The authors used a unitarized model incorporating Adler zeros from chiral symmetry, all light two-pseudoscalar thresholds with flavor-symmetric couplings, analyticity, and unitarity, with six physically interpretable parameters. They analyzed the pole structure of the scalar nonet amplitudes, searching for all relevant poles including "image" or "companion" poles that emerge when the coupling to S-wave thresholds becomes very strong. The model was applied to data on the lightest \( 0^{++} \) meson nonet, and decoupling of the \( s\bar s \) and \( u\bar u+d\bar d \) channels was performed to identify the origin of each pole.

## Key numbers

- The sigma meson Breit-Wigner mass \( m_{BW}=860 \) MeV and width \( \Gamma_{BW}=880 \) MeV, with its pole at \( s=(0.158-i0.235) \) GeV\(^2\)
- The overall coupling constant used in the model is \( \gamma=1.14 \)
- The bare mass of the \( u\bar u \) or \( d\bar d \) state is \( m_0=1.42 \) GeV
- The extra mass for a strange quark is \( m_s=100 \) MeV
- The cutoff parameter is \( k_0=0.56 \) GeV/c
- The Adler zero parameter for \( K\pi \) is \( s_{A_{K\pi}}=-0.42 \) GeV
- The phenomenological parameter enhancing \( \eta\eta' \) couplings is \( \beta=1.6 \)
- Using the simplified model with \( \bar\gamma=1.14 \), the predicted \( (Re s_{pole})^{1/2}=1329 \) MeV and \( m_{BW}=1129 \) MeV, compared to the actual \( f_0(1200) \) values of 1202 MeV and 1186 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of Tornqvist (1995) which showed that the lightest scalars could be understood with a unitarized approach, and it directly addresses the longstanding controversy over the sigma meson that was discarded after the \( f_0(980) \) and \( f_0(1200-1300) \) were established. The paper contradicts the assumption that the sigma could be replaced by the two heavier resonances, and instead identifies the sigma as the broad \( u\bar u+d\bar d \) state required by the linear sigma model and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. The finding that two resonance poles can emerge from a single \( q\bar q \) state resolves the puzzle of the \( f_0(980) \) and \( f_0(1200) \) being two manifestations of the same \( s\bar s \) state, and similarly for the \( a_0(980) \) and \( a_0(1450) \).
