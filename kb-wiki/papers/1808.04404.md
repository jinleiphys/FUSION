# In medium properties of $K^0$ and $ϕ$ mesons under an external magnetic field

> The one loop polarization insertions for the kaon and the $ϕ$ mesons are evaluated in a hadronic medium as functions of the field intensity and the baryonic density. For this purpose an effective chiral model of the hadronic interaction is used, supplemented with a phenomenological $K-ϕ$ interaction. The propagators of the charged particles include the full effect of the coupling to the magnetic field which induces the quantum Landau levels. Consequently, the vacuum diagrams are defined within a zeta function regularization scheme. The effective masses as well as the decay widths are introduced and examined as functions of matter density and the magnetic intensity.

# In medium properties of $K^0$ and $\phi$ mesons under an external magnetic field

## Key claim

The effective mass of the neutral kaon increases monotonically with baryonic density (reaching a 10% enhancement at three times normal nuclear density) while strong magnetic fields produce a moderate opposing reduction of only 1-4%, meaning density effects dominate under the conditions studied. The $\phi$ meson decay width does not depend on matter density in the absence of a kaon condensate, but the charged kaon decay channel becomes blocked above a threshold magnetic intensity of approximately $2.45 \times 10^{18}$ G.

## Method

The one-loop polarization insertions for kaons and $\phi$ mesons were evaluated using an effective chiral SU(3) model with a phenomenological $K$-$\phi$ derivative coupling, treating the magnetic field as a classical external field. Charged particle propagators included the full Landau level quantization, baryon propagators incorporated anomalous magnetic moments, and the calculations used zeta function regularization for charged particles and dimensional regularization for neutral particles, with a Dyson-Schwinger approach to define effective masses as poles of the corrected propagators. The hadronic medium was taken as electrically neutral matter in beta equilibrium at zero temperature, with baryonic densities below $7.5 \times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$ and magnetic intensities from $10^{15}$ G to $10^{19}$ G.

## Key numbers

- The threshold magnetic intensity for blocking the $K^-K^+$ decay channel of the $\phi$ meson is $B_t \simeq 2.45 \times 10^{18}$ G, given by $e B_t = m_\phi^2/4 - m_K^2$.
- The neutral kaon effective mass increases by approximately 10% at three times normal nuclear density ($3 n_0$).
- The reduction of the kaon effective mass due to magnetic field effects is about 4%, 2%, and 1% for baryonic densities $n/n_0 = 0.5$, 1, and 2 respectively at $B = 10^{18}$ G.
- The $\phi$ meson decay width from the neutral kaon channel contributes a constant value of 1.92 MeV.
- For magnetic intensities below $4 \times 10^{17}$ G, the total $\phi$ decay width oscillates around 1.92 MeV.
- The saturation density of nuclear matter in the model is $n_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$, with binding energy $E_B = -15.3$ MeV and symmetry energy $E_s = 31.6$ MeV.
- The kaon mass is taken as $m_K = 495.5$ MeV, the $\phi$ meson mass as $m_\phi = 1020$ MeV, and the $K$-$\phi$ coupling constant as $g = 6.55$.

## Context

This work extends studies of hadronic properties under strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetar astrophysics, where surface fields are estimated around $10^{15}$ G. It contrasts with the quark-meson coupling model of Yue (2014) which found a monotonous decrease of kaon mass with density reaching 20% reduction at $n = 0.45$ fm$^{-3}$, whereas the present chiral model finds density increases the kaon mass. The results for the $\phi$ meson decay width are relevant to the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star structure, where the $\phi$ meson may provide additional repulsion in hyperon-hyperon channels, and to heavy ion collision experiments where magnetic fields in the range predicted for RHIC ($B > B_t$) would make the $\phi$ meson a faithful probe of hadronization.
