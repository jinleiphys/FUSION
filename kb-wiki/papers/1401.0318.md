# Particle diagrams and embedded many-body random matrix theory

> We present a new method which uses Feynman-like diagrams to calculate the statistical quantities of embedded many-body random matrix problems. The method provides a promising alternative to existing techniques and offers many important simplifications. We use it here to find the fourth, sixth and eighth moments of the level density for k fermions or bosons interacting through a random hermitian potential in the limit where the number of possible single-particle states is taken to infinity. All share the same transition, starting immediately after 2k = m, from moments arising from a semi-circular level density to gaussian moments. The results also reveal a striking feature; the domain of the 2n'th moment is naturally divided into n subdomains specified by the points 2k = m, 3k = m, ..., nk = m.

# Particle diagrams and embedded many-body random matrix theory

## Key claim

The paper introduces a Feynman-like diagrammatic method to calculate statistical properties of embedded many-body random matrix ensembles, and uses it to derive the fourth, sixth, and eighth moments of the level density for the embedded Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE) in the limit where the number of single-particle states \(l \to \infty\). The moments all share a transition from semi-circular to Gaussian behavior starting immediately after \(2k = m\), and the domain of the \(2n\)'th moment is naturally divided into \(n\) subdomains specified by the points \(2k = m, 3k = m, \ldots, nk = m\).

## Method

The authors represent ensemble-averaged products of matrix elements of a \(k\)-body random hermitian potential \(V_k\) acting on \(m\)-particle states using "particle diagrams," where solid bonds connect many-particle states sharing \(m-k\) single-particle states and dashed bonds connect states sharing \(k\) single-particle states. They evaluate the argument (power in \(l\) as \(l\to\infty\)) of each combinatorial term using Stirling's formula, retaining only terms with maximal argument to obtain the limiting values of the normalized moments. The method is applied to fermionic and bosonic systems with \(k \le m\) in the limit \(l \to \infty\) and \(m \ll l\).

## Key numbers

* Fourth moment (kurtosis) of the level density: \(\lim_{N\to\infty} \kappa = 2 + \frac{{{m-k}\choose k}}{{m\choose k}}\), with the term \(\frac{{{m-k}\choose k}}{{m\choose k}}\) set to zero if \(m-k < k\).
* Sixth moment: \(\lim_{N\to\infty} h = 5 + \frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}{{m-2k}\choose{k}}}{{m\choose k}^2} + 6\frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}}{{m\choose k}} + 3\frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}^2}{{m\choose k}^2}\).
* Eighth moment: \(\lim_{N\to\infty} \tau = 14 + \frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}{{m-2k}\choose{k}}{{m-3k}\choose{k}}}{{m\choose k}^3} + 4\frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}{{m-2k}\choose{k}}^2}{{m\choose k}^3} + 8\frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}{{m-2k}\choose{k}}}{{m\choose k}^2} + 8\frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}^2{{m-2k}\choose{k}}}{{m\choose k}^3} + 12\frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}^3}{{m\choose k}^3} + 28\frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}^2}{{m\choose k}^2} + 28\frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}}{{m\choose k}} + 2 \frac{{{m-k}\choose{k}}^2}{{m\choose k}^3} \sum_{\alpha} \frac{{{k}\choose{\alpha}}^2{{m-2k}\choose{k-\alpha}}}{{{m-k}\choose{\alpha}}}\).
* Semi-circular moment values: \(\kappa = 2\), \(h = 5\), \(\tau = 14\) for \(k > m/2\).
* Gaussian moment values: \(\kappa = 3\), \(h = 15\), \(\tau = 105\) for \(k = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the embedded random matrix ensembles introduced by Mon and French (1975) for studying many-body interactions with \(k < m\), and provides a simpler alternative to the eigenvector expansion method of Benet, Rupp, and Weidenmuller (around 2000) which was difficult to implement for moments beyond the fourth. The particle diagram method enables calculation of the sixth and eighth moments for the first time, and reveals that the domain of each \(2n\)'th moment is partitioned at the points \(2k = m, 3k = m, \ldots, nk = m\). The results also show that fermionic and bosonic systems yield identical limiting moments, consistent with observations by Benet and Weidenmuller for the fourth moment. The method is expected to be applicable to embedded orthogonal and symplectic ensembles as well, and the appearance of Hahn polynomials in the eighth moment raises open questions about their role in many-body random matrix theory.
