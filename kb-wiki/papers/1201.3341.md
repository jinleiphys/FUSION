# Odd-particle systems in the shell model Monte Carlo: circumventing a sign problem

> We introduce a novel method within the shell model Monte Carlo approach to calculate the ground-state energy of a finite-size system with an odd number of particles by using the asymptotic behavior of the imaginary-time single-particle Green's functions. The method circumvents the sign problem that originates from the projection on an odd number of particles and has hampered direct application of the shell model Monte Carlo method to odd-particle systems. We apply this method to calculate pairing gaps of nuclei in the iron region. Our results are in good agreement with experimental pairing gaps.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a method to calculate ground-state energies of odd-particle systems in the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach by using the asymptotic behavior of imaginary-time single-particle Green's functions of an even-particle system, thereby circumventing the sign problem that arises from projecting on an odd number of particles. The method is validated by calculating pairing gaps of nuclei in the iron region, with results in good agreement with experimental pairing gaps.

## Method

The method uses the asymptotic exponential decay of the imaginary-time single-particle Green's function \(G_{\nu}(\tau)\) for an even-even nucleus to extract the energy difference \(\Delta E_j(\ma_{\pm})\) between the ground state of the even-even nucleus and the lowest state of a given spin \(j\) in a neighboring odd-even nucleus. The Green's function is computed in SMMC for several inverse temperatures \(\beta\) in the range \(3 \text{ MeV}^{-1} \leq \beta \leq 4 \text{ MeV}^{-1}\), and a linear fit to \(\ln G_{\nu}(\tau)\) is performed in the asymptotic region \(0.5 \text{ MeV}^{-1} \leq \tau \leq 2 \text{ MeV}^{-1}\) to extract the slope, which gives \(\Delta E_j(\ma_{\pm})\). The ground-state energy of the odd-even nucleus is then obtained by adding the minimum of these energy differences over possible \(j\) values to the ground-state energy of the even-even nucleus.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{29}\)Si in the \(sd\) shell, the method gave a ground-state energy of \(-133.98 \pm 0.04\) MeV, compared with the exact diagonalization result of \(-133.95\) MeV (deviation less than \(0.1\%\)).
- For \(^{57}\)Fe at \(\beta = 4\) MeV\(^{-1}\), the statistical error in the direct SMMC method was about 20 times larger than in the Green's function method.
- The neutron pairing gaps calculated for nuclei in the \(fp+g_{9/2}\) shell (isotopes of Ti, Cr, Fe, Ni, Zn, Ge) agreed with experimental values to within less than \(15\%\) deviation in most cases.
- The asymptotic region for the linear fits was chosen as \(0.5 \text{ MeV}^{-1} \leq \tau \leq 2 \text{ MeV}^{-1}\), with \(\beta\) values in the range \(3 \text{ MeV}^{-1} \leq \beta \leq 4 \text{ MeV}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the SMMC approach, previously limited to even-particle systems due to a sign problem for odd particle numbers (Alhassid 2001, Koonin 1997), by introducing a Green's function method conceptually similar to techniques used in lattice quantum chromodynamics to extract hadron masses (Gupta 1997). The method enables accurate calculation of ground-state energies for odd-A nuclei, which are essential for determining level densities and pairing gaps (odd-even staggering of binding energies). It was applied to nuclei in the complete \(fp+g_{9/2}\) shell model space using the isospin-conserving Hamiltonian of Nakada (1997), and the results enable future calculations of pairing gaps and other properties for medium-mass nuclei where direct diagonalization is intractable.
