# $ω$ and $ρ$ Photoproduction with an Effective Quark Model Lagrangian

> An unified approach for vector meson photoproduction is presented in the constit quark model. The s- and u-channel resonance contributions are generated using an effective quark vector-meson Lagrangian. In addition, taking into account $π^0$ and $σ$ t-channel exchanges for diffractive production, the available total and differential cross section data for $ω$, $ρ^0$, $ρ^+$, and $ρ^-$ photoproduction can be well described with the same quark model parameter set. Our results clearly indicate that polarization observables are essential to identify so-called "missing" resonances.

# ω and ρ Photoproduction with an Effective Quark Model Lagrangian

## Key claim
The same quark model parameter set, with coupling constants a = -1.7 and b' = 2.5, can simultaneously describe total and differential cross section data for ω, ρ^0, ρ^+, and ρ^- photoproduction, but only when π^0 and σ t-channel exchanges are added for the diffractive component in neutral meson production. The authors state that polarization observables, not cross sections, are essential to identify "missing" resonances such as the F_{15}(2000) state.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective quark model Lagrangian where quarks couple directly to vector meson fields via two coupling constants a and b. They computed tree-level s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel contributions to vector meson photoproduction, treating s-channel resonances below 2 GeV explicitly using harmonic oscillator quark model wavefunctions and higher states as degenerate continuum. For ω and ρ^0 production, additional π^0 and σ t-channel exchanges were included to account for diffractive scattering at small angles, with form factors determined by the harmonic oscillator strength parameters α_π and α_σ.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses: m_u = m_d = 0.33 GeV; harmonic oscillator parameter α = 410 MeV
- Best-fit coupling constants: a = -1.7, b' = b - a = 2.5
- Harmonic oscillator strength parameters: α_π = 300 MeV, α_σ = 250 MeV
- s- and u-channel resonance contributions account for only 20 to 40 percent of the total cross section for ω and ρ^0 photoproduction
- Cross section for charged ρ production is smaller by about a factor of three compared to ρ^0 production
- Resonance structure appears around 1.7 GeV photon lab energy in ω photoproduction from the F_{15}(2000) state

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous quark model approach to pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (Zhao, Li, Bennhold, 1997) to vector meson photoproduction. It builds on the t-channel exchange framework of Friman and Soyeur (1996) for diffractive ω and ρ^0 production. The paper addresses the long-standing puzzle of "missing" resonances predicted by quark models but not seen in πN channels, following earlier work by Simon (1980) and Close (1988). The results enable future polarization measurements at TJNAF to probe the structure of the F_{15}(2000) resonance and other missing states.
