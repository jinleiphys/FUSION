# Equation of state in (2+1) flavor QCD at high temperatures

> We calculate the Equation of State at high temperatures in (2+1) flavor QCD using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action. We study the lattice spacing dependence of the pressure at high temperatures using lattices with temporal extent $N_τ=6,~8,~10$ and $12$ and perform continuum extrapolations. We also give a continuum estimate for the Equation of State up to temperatures $T=2\,$GeV, which are then compared with results of the weak-coupling calculations. We find reasonable agreement with the HTL calculation at the highest temperatures and a tension with the EQCD calculation of a few percent, with the lattice results in the middle between both.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD equation of state for (2+1) flavors at high temperatures shows reasonable agreement with hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory at the highest temperatures, but a tension of a few percent with electrostatic QCD (EQCD) calculations, with the lattice results falling in between both weak-coupling predictions.

## Method

The authors used the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on lattices with temporal extents Nτ = 4, 6, 8, 10, and 12. They calculated the trace anomaly from gauge action and fermion condensate differences between zero and finite temperature, then obtained the pressure via the integral method. Continuum extrapolations were performed using multiple strategies including direct extrapolation of the pressure at fixed temperature and a correction scheme based on quark number susceptibilities, with a continuum estimate extended up to T = 2 GeV using rescaled data from coarser lattices.

## Key numbers

- Continuum estimate for the equation of state provided up to T = 2 GeV
- Lattice results for pressure at T = 500 MeV are one and a half standard errors larger than the 2+1 flavor stout action result from Borsanyi (2013)
- For T > 800 MeV, data with Nτ = 6 or 4 are smaller than the continuum estimate by factors of 1.4 or 1.2 respectively
- Reference pressure values for different Nτ: p(T0=135 MeV, Nτ=6) = 0.189(54), p(T0=120 MeV, Nτ=8) = 0.145(22), p(T0=125 MeV, Nτ=10) = 0.226(23), p(T0=135 MeV, Nτ=12) = 0.344(29)
- Lattice scale parameters: r1 = 0.3106 fm, r2 = 0.1413 fm
- Pion mass for new ensembles: mπ = 320 MeV (compared to mπ = 160 MeV in previous HotQCD work)
- Light quark mass used: ml = ms/5

## Context

This work extends the previous 2+1 flavor HISQ equation of state calculations by Bazavov (2014) to higher temperatures, using ensembles with larger light quark mass (ml = ms/5 instead of ml = ms/20). It enables a robust comparison with weak-coupling calculations (HTL at three-loop by Haque (2014) and EQCD at order O(g^6) by Laine (2006)) to test the applicability of perturbative QCD at high temperatures. The results also provide a baseline for determining the charm quark contribution to the equation of state, which is needed for phenomenological applications at temperatures above 400 MeV.
