# Traces of non-equilibrium dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The impact of non-equilibrium effects on the dynamics of heavy-ion collisions is investigated by comparing a non-equilibrium transport approach, the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD), to a 2D+1 viscous hydrodynamical model, which is based on the assumption of local equilibrium and conservation laws. Starting the hydrodynamical model from the same non-equilibrium initial condition as in the PHSD, using an equivalent lQCD Equation-of-State (EoS), the same transport coefficients, i.e. shear viscosity $η$ and the bulk viscosity $ζ$ in the hydrodynamical model, we compare the time evolution of the system in terms of energy density, Fourier transformed energy density, spatial and momentum eccentricities and ellipticity in order to quantify the traces of non-equilibrium phenomena. In addition, we also investigate the role of initial pre-equilibrium flow on the hydrodynamical evolution and demonstrate its importance for final state observables. We find that due to non-equilibrium effects, the event-by-event transport calculations show large fluctuations in the collective properties, while ensemble averaged observables are close to the hydrodynamical results.

## Key claim

Non-equilibrium effects in heavy-ion collisions cause large event-by-event fluctuations in collective properties, but when averaged over many events the observables become close to the results from a viscous hydrodynamical model that assumes local equilibrium.

## Method

The authors compared the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) non-equilibrium transport approach to the 2D+1 viscous hydrodynamical model VISHNew for peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. They initialized the hydrodynamical model at \(t=0.6\) fm/c using initial conditions extracted from PHSD via a Landau-matching procedure, and used the same lattice QCD equation of state and the same temperature-dependent shear viscosity \(\eta/s(T)\) as obtained from PHSD simulations. The time evolution of energy density, spatial eccentricity, and momentum anisotropy was then compared between the two approaches.

## Key numbers

* Initial condition for hydrodynamics extracted at \(t=0.6\) fm/c for a peripheral (\(b=6\) fm) Au+Au collision at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.
* The PHSD energy-momentum tensor was evaluated on a space-time grid with cell size \(\Delta x = 1\) fm, \(\Delta y = 1\) fm, and \(\Delta z \propto 0.5 \times t/\gamma_{NN}\).
* The inverse Reynolds number \(R^{-1}_\pi = \sqrt{\pi^{\mu\nu}\pi_{\mu\nu}}/P\) was predominantly below 1 at the initialization time of 0.6 fm/c, justifying the choice of starting time for hydrodynamics.
* The spatial eccentricity \(\epsilon_2\) and momentum eccentricity \(\epsilon_p\) were averaged over 100 PHSD events and 100 VISHNew events for the peripheral Au+Au collision at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of coarse-grained hydrodynamical evolution with PHSD dynamics previously studied by Kodama et al. (2011) and deSouza et al. (2015), who found that ensemble averages of PHSD are close to the hydrodynamical limit despite large event-by-event fluctuations. The study also builds on earlier PHSD work by Cassing (2008) that showed a linear correlation of elliptic flow \(v_2\) with initial spatial eccentricity \(\varepsilon_2\). The results enable future development of observables with enhanced sensitivity to non-equilibrium components of bulk QCD matter evolution and the study of how non-equilibrium effects impact hard probe observables such as heavy quarks, electromagnetic probes, and jets.
