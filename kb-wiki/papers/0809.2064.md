# Large-N Summation of Chiral Logs for Generalized Parton Distributions

> We demonstrate that in the region of Bjorken x ~m_π^2/(4πF_π)^2 and/or x ~|t|/(4πF_π)^2 the standard ChPT for the pion GPDs fails and one must perform all order resummation of ChPT. We perform such resummation in the large-N limit of the O(N+1) extension of the chiral theory. Explicit resummation allows us to reveal novel phenomena -- the form of the leading chiral correction to pion PDFs and GPDs depends on the small-x asymptotic of the pion PDFs. In particular, if the pion PDF in the chiral limit has the Regge-like small x behaviour q(x)~1/x^ω, the leading large impact parameter ($b_\perp\to\infty$) asymptotics of the quark distribution in the transverse plane has the form ($m_π=0$) $q(x,b_\perp)\sim 1/x^ω \ln^ω(b_\perp^2)/b_\perp^{2{(1+ω)}}$. This result is model independent and it is controlled completely by the all order resummed ChPT developed in this paper. This asymptotic interweaves with small-$x$ behaviour of usual PDFs, hence it depends on the scale, at which the corresponding PDF is defined. This is a new and interesting result in which the chiral expansion meets the QCD evolution.

## Key claim

Standard chiral perturbation theory (χPT) for pion generalized parton distributions (GPDs) fails in the kinematic region where the Bjorken variable x_Bj is of order m_π²/(4πF_π)² or |t|/(4πF_π)², requiring all-order resummation; the authors perform this resummation in the large-N limit of the O(N+1) extension of the chiral theory, finding that the leading chiral correction to pion PDFs and GPDs depends on the small-x asymptotic behavior of the pion PDFs in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors extend the O(4) σ-model (equivalent to SU(2)×SU(2) chiral theory for pions) to O(N+1) and solve it in the large-N limit using semiclassical methods, which performs a partial summation of χPT diagrams to all orders while respecting chiral symmetry. They compute the leading-logarithm contributions to the isovector and isoscalar pion PDFs and GPDs by evaluating the 1/N-order diagrams (tree contribution plus bubble-chain insertions) and applying the R-operation for UV renormalization, reducing the calculation to a sum over bubble-chain counterterms. The singular contributions (derivatives of δ-functions for PDFs, 1/ξ^k terms for GPDs) are summed explicitly, yielding closed-form expressions for the resummed chiral corrections.

## Key numbers

- Chiral expansion parameter: a_χ = (m_π / (4π F_π))², with F_π ≈ 93 MeV
- For GPDs, the small expansion parameter is b_χ = |t| / (4(4π F_π)²)
- The one-loop coefficients for the isovector PDF singular terms are D₁ = -1, D₂ = -5/3 ⟨x⟩, D₃ = -25/108 ⟨x²⟩ (from three-loop calculations in prior work)
- The resummed singular contribution to the isovector PDF is q^sing(x) = -(2θ(|x|<ε)/N) ∫_{|x|/ε}^{1} (dβ/β) q̃(β), where ε = (N/2) a_χ ln(1/a_χ)
- For the isoscalar PDF: Q^sing(x) = 4 sign(x) θ(|x|<ε)/N ∫_{|x|/ε}^{1} (dβ/β) Q̃(β) (3 - 2|x|/(εβ))
- For the isovector GPD in the ξ→0 limit: H^{I=1}(x,ξ=0,t=-Δ²_⊥) = -(2/N) θ(|x|<ε) ∫_{x/ε}^{1} (dβ/β) q̃(β) √(1 - x/(εβ)), with ε = (N/2) b_χ ln(1/b_χ)
- Large-b_⊥ asymptotics for the isovector quark distribution (m_π=0): q(x,b_⊥) ~ (1/x^ω) ln^ω(b_⊥²) / b_⊥^{2(1+ω)}
- Large-b_⊥ asymptotics for the isoscalar quark distribution (m_π=0): Q(x,b_⊥) ~ (1/x^ω) ln^ω(b_⊥²) / b_⊥^{2(1+ω)}
- For the isovector case with m_π≠0 and m_π b_⊥ → ∞: q(x,b_⊥) ~ (1/N) e^{-2m_π b_⊥} / b_⊥² × (N/|x|) (m_π b_⊥ ln[b_⊥²(4πF_π)²] / (b_⊥²(4πF_π)²))^ω

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kivel, Polyakov, and Vladimirov (2008) who showed that chiral expansions of pion PDFs contain singular δ-function terms requiring reorganization, and that GPDs contain singular 1/ξ^k contributions. The large-N resummation solves the problem conjectured in that prior work that the infinite sum of δ-function derivatives yields a contribution of the form f(x/a_χ)/a_χ. The results enable computation of the correct large-impact-parameter asymptotics of parton distributions in the transverse plane, which previously from one-loop χPT (Kivel 2002) gave q(x,b_⊥) ~ δ(x)/b_⊥⁴ and from two-loop gave Q(x,b_⊥) ~ δ'(x) ln(b_⊥²)/b_⊥⁶, both artifacts of finite-order calculations. The finding that the transverse size of the pion grows as a power of 1/x at small x parallels similar phenomena discussed for the nucleon by Mueller and Schäfer (2006).
