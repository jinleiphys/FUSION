# Formation of correlations at short time scales and consequences on interferometry methods

> The formation of correlations due to collisions in an interacting nucleonic system is investigated shortly after a disturbance. Results from one-time kinetic equations are compared with the Kadanoff and Baym two-time equation with collisions included in second order Born approximation. A reasonable agreement is found for a proposed approximation of the memory effects by a finite duration of collisions. The formation of correlations and the build up time is calculated analytically for the high temperature and the low temperature limit. This translates into a time dependent increase of the effective temperature on time scales which interfere with standard fire ball scenarios of heavy ion collisions. The consequences of the formation of correlations on the two- particle interferometry are investigated and it is found that standard extracted lifetimes should be corrected downwards.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard extraction of source lifetimes from two-particle interferometry in heavy-ion collisions yields values that are too high for short-lived sources and too low for long-lived sources, because the finite time required to build up two-particle correlations (the "transient time" or "formation time" is neglected in conventional Markovian kinetic simulations.

## Method
The authors compare one-time kinetic equations (of Levinson type) with the full Kadanoff-Baym two-time Green's function equations, including collisions in second-order Born approximation. They derive an analytical expression for the time-dependent increase of correlation energy (and thus kinetic energy) in the high-temperature and low-temperature limits, and propose a universal scaling factor that can be applied to the Wigner function from Markovian (BUU-type) simulations to incorporate the transient time effect. This scaling is then used to compute the two-particle correlation function for a parameterized spherical source with lifetime tau, and the result is compared to the standard formula without transient effects.

## Key numbers
- The build-up time (correlation time) is given by tau_c approximately equal to hbar / epsilon_F, where epsilon_F is the Fermi energy.
- For a model of two counter-flowing streams of nuclear matter (densities n1 = n0/60, T1 = 0.5 MeV and n2 = n0/20, T2 = 0.1 MeV, relative velocity 1 hbar/fm, collision energy 21 MeV/nucleon), the correlation time is tau_c = 9 fm/c and the equilibrated temperature (neglecting correlations) is T = 11.6 MeV.
- The Padé formula for the scaled correlation energy uses x = 4 epsilon_F t / hbar; the Kadanoff-Baym result is fit by zeta_K/B(t) = 1 - [1.5225 (1 - cos(0.7337 x) + sin(0.6568 x))] / [exp(0.5469 x) x].
- The critical freeze-out density where the transient effect produces only suppression (no enhancement) of the correlation function is found to be around 0.0036 fm^{-3}.

## Context
This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym (1962) two-time formalism and the Levinson (1965, 1969) non-Markovian kinetic equations to the short-time regime of correlation formation, which had previously been studied numerically by Danielewicz (1984) and Kohler (1995). The authors provide an analytical approximation for the build-up time that is universal for any binary interaction at the Born or T-matrix level. The result enables a simple correction to standard BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) simulations and molecular-dynamics simulations (e.g., QMD) for interferometry analyses, and it shows that the standard extracted lifetimes from experiments (e.g., using the formalism of Bertsch et al. (1994) or Pratt et al. (1987)) should be corrected downwards for short-lived sources.
