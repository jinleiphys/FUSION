# Nuclear interaction modeled with a simple piston-gas model

> A simple one-dimensional gas-piston kinetic model gives the interaction potential between two colliding heavy ions. In the frame of the classical, thermodynamical approach, the colliding heavy ions are not submitted to friction, but produces an irreversible phenomena with cause at the difference of pressure $p$ "felt" by the nucleon gas when ions collide with the target when compared with the pressure that nuclear matter exert on their boundaries when in thermodynamical equilibrium, and offers a straightforward way to calculate interacting potentials.

# Nuclear interaction modeled with a simple piston-gas model

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the interaction potential between two colliding heavy ions arises not from friction but from an irreversible thermodynamic process driven by a pressure difference experienced by the nucleon gas during collision, under the condition that the total energy of the incident ion is much higher than the interaction potential (relativistic heavy-ion collisions).

## Method
The author models two colliding heavy ions as a one-dimensional piston-gas system, treating the nucleons as a non-interacting Fermi gas. The interaction potential is derived from the pressure difference between the nucleon gas during collision and at thermodynamic equilibrium, using the grand thermodynamic potential for a Fermi gas. The resulting integral equation is solved analytically in the relativistic limit where the incident ion energy greatly exceeds the interaction potential, yielding an exponential form for the potential.

## Key numbers
- The touching point distance for all three reactions studied is a = 6.9 fm.
- For the reaction 16O + 16O, the potential at the touching point is phi(a) = -7.0 MeV, with fitting parameter C = 0.26 MeV/fm.
- For the reaction 90Zr + 90Zr, the potential at the touching point is phi(a) = -1330.0 MeV, with fitting parameter C = 0.26 MeV/fm.
- For the reaction 90Zr + 208Pb, the potential at the touching point is phi(a) = -40.0 MeV, with fitting parameter C = 0.26 MeV/fm.
- The critical distance r_c = 1/|lambda| = 1 fm, interpreted as the surface thickness parameter.
- The reduced wavelength of the relative momentum is lambdabar = 1/k with k approximately 10-40 fm^-1, giving lambdabar much less than 1 fm.

## Context
This work extends the piston-gas model originally proposed by Gross (1975) for nuclear collisions, but differs by rejecting the friction interpretation in favor of an irreversible thermodynamic mechanism based on pressure differences. The calculated potentials are compared with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the SkM* parameter set of the Skyrme force from Denisov (2002), showing good agreement. The model enables fast analytical calculation of ion-ion interaction potentials for heavy-ion reactions with mass number A above 40, particularly useful for relativistic collisions.
