# Universality in the neutron$-^{19}$C scattering using finite range separable interactions

> We report a study on the low-energy properties of the elastic $s-$wave scattering of a neutron ($n$) in the carbon isotope $^{19}$C near the critical condition for the occurrence of an excited Efimov state in the three-body $n-n-^{18}$C system. For the separation energy of the two halo neutrons in $^{20}$C we use the available experimental data. We also investigate to which extent the universal scaling laws, strictly valid in the zero-range limit, will survive when using finite-range interactions. By allowing to vary the $n-^{18}$C binding energy, a scaling behavior for the real and imaginary parts of the $s-$wave phase-shift $δ_0$ is verified, emerging some universal characteristics given by the pole-position of $k\cot(δ_0^R)$ and effective-range parameters.

# Universality in the neutron-¹⁹C scattering using finite-range separable interactions

## Key claim

The low-energy s-wave elastic scattering of a neutron from ¹⁹C exhibits universal scaling behavior, specifically in the pole position of k cot(δ₀ᴿ) and effective-range parameters, that survives when finite-range interactions replace the zero-range limit, provided the two-neutron separation energy in ²⁰C is fixed to 3.5 MeV and the n-¹⁸C binding energy is varied between -400 and -800 keV.

## Method

The authors model the n-¹⁹C scattering as a three-body n-n-¹⁸C system using separable Yamaguchi-type finite-range potentials for both the n-n and n-c interactions. The n-n interaction is fixed by the virtual-state energy E_nn = -143 keV, and the n-c bound-state energy E_nc is varied from -400 to -800 keV while keeping the three-body ground-state energy of ²⁰C fixed at -3.5 MeV. The s-wave scattering amplitude is obtained by solving a single-channel Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation for the n-(nc) channel amplitude, with the coupled (nn)-c channel contribution included in the kernel. Range parameters β_nn and β_nc are varied between high-range (low β, large effective range) and low-range (high β, small effective range) regimes.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy in ²⁰C fixed to E_nnc = -3.5 MeV (experimental value).
- n-n virtual-state energy fixed to E_nn = -143 keV.
- n-c bound-state energy E_nc varied from -400 keV to -800 keV, covering the experimental range of -160±110 keV to -530±130 keV.
- For high-range n-n interaction: β_nn = 1.34 fm⁻¹, giving effective range r_nn = 2.372 fm.
- For low-range n-n interaction: β_nn = 24.50 fm⁻¹, giving effective range r_nn = 0.1228 fm.
- For E_nc = -200 keV, high-range β_nc = 0.971 fm⁻¹ (r_nc = 2.736 fm); low-range β_nc = 18.970 fm⁻¹ (r_nc = 0.157 fm).
- For E_nc = -800 keV, high-range β_nc = 0.477 fm⁻¹ (r_nc = 4.255 fm); low-range β_nc = 14.395 fm⁻¹ (r_nc = 0.205 fm).
- The ¹⁸C root-mean-square matter radius is r_m[¹⁸C] = 2.82 ± 0.04 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier zero-range studies by Yamashita et al. (2008) on n-¹⁹C elastic s-wave phase shifts, which had demonstrated a pole in the effective-range expansion qualitatively similar to that found in n-d low-energy scattering and atom-dimer systems. The present paper tests whether those universal features survive when finite-range potentials are used, motivated by the Efimov physics framework for mass-imbalanced halo nuclei as reviewed by Frederico et al. (2012). The results enable future studies of universal scaling in the continuum for neutron-rich halo nuclei near the critical condition for an excited Efimov state, particularly for systems where experimental data on ²⁰C and ¹⁹C binding energies are available.
