# Effects of multiple single-particle basis states in scattering systems

> Low-lying baryon resonances have been explored using Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT), in a formalism where resonances with a three-quark component are described by both two-particle meson-baryon states and a bare basis state. Here, we investigate the use of multiple bare states in the Hamiltonian, to extend the formalism to higher energy ranges, and represent a larger portion of the low-lying baryon spectrum. Introducing a second bare state into a toy model extension of the low-energy $Δ(1232)$ system, we explore the influence of the second bare state on the position of poles in the infinite-volume $T$-matrix. Considering the same system in a finite-volume, we analyse the finite-volume energy spectrum in the presence of a second bare state, providing insight into the interplay between two bare basis states, representing quark-model states, and the relationship between infinite-volume poles and finite-volume eigenstates.

## Key claim

Introducing a second bare basis state into a Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT) model of the Delta(1232) system causes the two bare states to exchange roles in the physical poles and finite-volume eigenstates when their couplings to the scattering channel become comparable, with the turning point occurring when the two couplings are equal.

## Method

The authors extend the HEFT formalism to include multiple bare single-particle basis states, representing quark-model-like states, in addition to two-particle meson-baryon scattering states. They construct a toy model based on a single pi-N scattering channel and two bare states, using separable potentials with a dipole regulator (Lambda = 0.8 GeV), and solve for the infinite-volume T-matrix poles and the finite-volume Hamiltonian eigenvalues and eigenvectors at L = 3 fm. The coupling of the second bare state is smoothly varied while holding all other parameters fixed to values previously fit to the Delta(1232) system.

## Key numbers

*   The single-bare-state fit to the Delta(1232) gives a bare mass of m_Delta0 = 1.359 GeV, couplings g_piN^Delta = 0.176 and v_piNpiN = -0.0127, and a pole at E_pole = 1.211 - 0.049 i GeV, consistent with the PDG value of 1.210 - 0.050 i GeV.
*   For a second bare state at 1.6 GeV with large coupling, the original pole drops below the pi-N threshold (m_pi + m_N = 1.078 GeV) and becomes a bound state, while the second pole approaches the real axis at the first bare mass of 1.359 GeV.
*   For a second bare state at 1.2 GeV with large coupling, the second pole becomes a bound state below threshold, while the original pole tends to the real axis at the first bare mass of 1.359 GeV.
*   The finite-volume analysis at L = 3 fm shows that at approximately g_piN^Delta1 = g_piN^Delta2, the identities of the three lowest energy eigenstates are shuffled, with the bare-state-dominant eigenstates exchanging positions.

## Context

This work extends previous HEFT studies of single resonances such as the Delta(1232) (Hall 2013, Abell 2021), the Roper N*(1440) (Liu 2016, Wu 2017), the Lambda*(1405) (Hall 2014), and the N*(1535) (Liu 2015), which all used only a single bare basis state. The results demonstrate that future HEFT studies of higher-lying baryon resonances will require multiple bare states, and that the couplings of these bare states must be of similar order to avoid generating unphysical bound states or poles. The finite-volume eigenvector analysis provides a method to track which bare state dominates each lattice QCD eigenstate, enabling interpretation of lattice results from three-quark interpolating fields.
