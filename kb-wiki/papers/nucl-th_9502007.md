# The Disappearance of Flow

> We investigate the disappearance of collective flow in the reaction plane in heavy-ion collisions within a microscopic model (QMD). A systematic study of the impact parameter dependence is performed for the system Ca+Ca. The balance energy strongly increases with impact parameter. Momentum dependent interactions reduce the balance energies for intermediate impact parameters $b\approx4.5$ fm. Dynamical negative flow is not visible in the laboratory frame but does exist in the contact frame for the heavy system Au+Au. For semi-peripheral collisions of Ca+Ca with $b\approx6.5$ fm a new two-component flow is discussed. Azimuthal distributions exhibit strong collectiv flow signals, even at the balance energy.

# The Disappearance of Flow

## Key claim

The balance energy at which attractive and repulsive forces cancel in the reaction plane depends strongly on impact parameter, contrary to earlier claims that it was nearly independent; furthermore, collective flow signals persist in azimuthal distributions even at the balance energy, so the term "energy of vanishing flow" is misleading.

## Method

The Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model was used to simulate Ca+Ca and Au+Au collisions, employing a hard equation of state (compressibility κ=380 MeV) with and without momentum-dependent interactions. The in-plane transverse momentum p_x was plotted versus normalized rapidity, and the balance energy E_bal was extracted by linear fits to the energy dependence of the reduced flow (slope of p_x(y)) at the point where it passes through zero. For Au+Au, the initial pre-contact rotation due to Rutherford trajectories was subtracted to reveal negative flow in the contact frame.

## Key numbers

- For Ca+Ca without momentum-dependent interactions, the balance energy varies from 65 to 150 AMeV depending on impact parameter; with momentum-dependent interactions, it varies from 75 to 115 AMeV.
- The balance energy for Ca+Ca at b=0.5b_max≈4 fm is approximately 80-130 AMeV (the energy range where the p_x(y) slope changes sign).
- For Au+Au in the rotated contact frame, the balance energy is E_bal=55±5 AMeV at b=0.25b_max≈3.3 fm and E_bal=65±5 AMeV at b=0.5b_max≈6.5 fm.
- The experimental balance energy for Ar+Sc (A=85) is 87±12 AMeV at estimated impact parameter b≈0.4b_max≈3 fm, compatible with the Ca+Ca calculations.
- The two-component flow for Ca+Ca at b=0.85b_max≈6.5 fm is observed at E=350 AMeV without momentum-dependent interactions, and also at 170 AMeV with momentum-dependent interactions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of collective flow disappearance by Krofcheck (1992), Zhang (1990), and the BUU calculations of Welke (1993), which had proposed a power-law mass dependence E_bal ~ A_tot^{-1/3} but neglected impact parameter dependence. The finding that balance energy increases strongly with impact parameter contradicts claims by Zhou (1993) that the dependence was weak. The demonstration that negative flow exists in the contact frame for Au+Au resolves the experimental puzzle of why only an upper bound E_bal≤60 AMeV had been observed by Zhang (1990). The two-component flow and the persistence of azimuthal anisotropies at the balance energy provide new observables for constraining the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and momentum-dependent interactions.
