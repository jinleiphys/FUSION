# Classification of the pairing transition in finite fermionic systems

> In this work we employ a simple pairing interaction model in order to study and classify an eventual pairing phase transition in finite fermionic systems. We show that systems with as few as 10-16 fermions can exhibit clear features reminiscent of a phase transition. To classify the nature of the transition we apply two different numerical methods, one based on standard thermodymanics, and another based on a recently proposed scheme by Borrmann et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 84, 3511 (2000)]. The transition is shown to be of second order, in agreement with results for infinite fermionic systems.

## Key claim

Systems with as few as 10-16 fermions can exhibit clear features reminiscent of a phase transition, and the pairing transition in these finite fermionic systems is of second order, in agreement with results for infinite fermionic systems. However, this transition is not a phase transition in the ordinary thermodynamical sense, as the system develops several intermediate steps where different numbers of broken pairs can coexist rather than showing an abrupt transition from a purely paired phase to a nonpaired phase.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a simple pairing Hamiltonian with equidistant single-particle levels and fixed spacing d, for systems with up to 16 particles in 16 doubly degenerate levels at half-filling, obtaining all eigenstates and the exact density of states. They applied two numerical methods to classify the transition: a free-energy analysis based on the microcanonical ensemble following Lee and Kosterlitz (1990), and a distribution of zeros analysis of the canonical partition function in the complex temperature plane following Borrmann et al. (2000).

## Key numbers

- For d/G = 0.5 and 16 particles in 16 doubly degenerate levels, the free energy develops two clear minima at temperatures T = 0.5, 0.85, and 1.0 MeV
- At T = 0.85 MeV, the free-energy minima where two and three pairs are broken equal, with Delta F/N approximately constant at 0.5 MeV for N = 10 (0.531 MeV), 12 (0.505 MeV), 14 (0.501 MeV), and 16 (0.495 MeV)
- For d/G greater than 1.5, the free energy develops only one minimum for all temperatures for N less than or equal to 16, indicating no sign of a phase transition
- The parameter tau_1, a measure of discreteness from the distribution of zeros analysis, shows a dependence of tau_1 approximately proportional to N^{-1.4 +/- 0.12}

## Context

This work extends studies of pairing phase transitions from infinite systems (such as neutron stars, where the transition is well established as second order) to finite fermionic systems, addressing the question of the lower size limit for superconductivity raised by experiments on ultrasmall aluminum grains by Tinkham et al. (1995-1997). The results agree with the infinite-system expectation of a second-order transition and contradict interpretations of negative heat capacities as signs of first-order phase transitions, supporting the view of Moretto et al. (2001). The findings enable future work with more realistic interactions to investigate the paired-phase and mixed seniority phases demonstrated in this model.
