# Stability and Representation Dependence of the Quantum Skyrmion

> A constructive realization of Skyrme's conjecture that an effective pion mass ``may arise as a self consistent quantal effect'' based on an ab initio quantum treatment of the Skyrme model is presented. In this quantum mechanical Skyrme model the spectrum of states with $I=J$, which appears in the collective quantization, terminates without any infinite tower of unphysical states. The termination point depends on the model parameters and the dimension of the SU(2) representation. Representations, in which the nucleon and $Δ_{33}$ resonance are the only stable states, exist. The model is developed for both irreducible and reducible representations of general dimension. States with spin larger than 1/2 are shown to be deformed. The representation dependence of the baryon observables is illustrated numerically.

## Key claim

The paper provides a constructive realization of Skyrme's conjecture that an effective pion mass "may arise as a self consistent quantal effect," but only for states with sufficiently small spin values; the spectrum of states with equal spin and isospin terminates at a finite value that depends on the model parameters and the dimension of the SU(2) representation, and representations exist in which the nucleon and Delta_33 resonance are the only stable states.

## Method

The authors perform an ab initio quantum mechanical treatment of the Skyrme model using collective coordinate quantization in SU(2) representations of arbitrary dimension, both irreducible and reducible. They derive the quantum Hamiltonian including a negative quantum correction term Delta M_j(F) that depends on the representation dimension, then minimize the full quantum mass expression to obtain an integro-differential equation for the chiral angle F(r). The parameters f_pi and e are determined iteratively by fitting to the nucleon mass and isoscalar radius, and the resulting solutions are used to compute baryon observables for different representations.

## Key numbers

- For nucleons (ell=1/2), stable soliton solutions exist when e < 7.5; the largest value of e for stable solutions decreases with increasing dimensionality of the representation.
- For Delta_33 resonances (ell=3/2), no stable soliton solutions exist in the fundamental representation (j=1/2) nor in the representation with j=1.
- In representations with j=3/2 and j=2, only stable soliton solutions exist for baryons with spin ell=1/2 and 3/2.
- For the irreducible representation with j=1, the proton magnetic moment calculated is within 10% of the empirical value.
- The calculated values of both the neutron magnetic moment and the axial coupling constant agree with the corresponding empirical values to within 1%.

## Context

This work extends the ab initio quantized version of the Skyrme model developed in Norvaisas (1997) and the quantization method for the fundamental representation suggested by Fujii et al. (1987). It addresses the instability problem in the semiclassical approach noted by Bander and Broniowski (1986) and Braaten (1986), where the rotational energy introduced by projection onto states of good spin and isospin destroys stable soliton solutions. The paper enables a representation-dependent description of baryons where the tower of I=J states terminates naturally, avoiding the infinite tower of unphysical states that appears in the collective quantization approach of Napolitano and Witten (1984), and provides a more general argument than the self-consistent dynamical truncation method of Blaizot (1989).
