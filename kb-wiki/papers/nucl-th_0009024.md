# Eta Production in Hadronic Interactions

> A short, and definitely not a complete representation of ηproduction processes on hadrons is given. First of all, the different ways of obtaining the πN \to ηN and πN \to ηN amplitudes are presented. After that, an overview of results obtained using these amplitudes as input for calculating processes like: NN \to ηNN, pd \to η^{3}He, πd \to ηNN and ηd, η^{3}He and η^{4}He as well as η-light nuclei bond states, will be given. The experimental and theoretical results will be reviewed. The opened problems and the way how to solve them will be presented.

# Eta Production in Hadronic Interactions

## Key claim

The paper argues that a multichannel, unitary coupled-channel approach is essential for reliably extracting N* resonance parameters, because some resonances (such as the second S11(1650) and a fourth P11 resonance) couple weakly or not at all to photoproduction channels and are therefore invisible in single-channel analyses. The authors present a personal star-rating system to rank the reliability of different models, acknowledging that the assessment is subjective.

## Method

The authors review and compare several classes of models for the πN → ηN and ηN → ηN amplitudes: (i) multiresonance, coupled-channel unitary models based on the Cutkosky (1979) formalism, as implemented by Manley (1992), Batinic (1998), and Vrana (1999); (ii) quark-model predictions combined with coupled-channel analysis by Capstick (1999); and (iii) simpler single-resonance or K-matrix approaches. They then survey how these elementary amplitudes are used as input to calculate more complex processes: NN → ηNN, pd → η³He, πd → ηNN, and η bound states on light nuclei (ηd, η³He, η⁴He). The paper also compares resonance parameters from the Zagreb group's phenomenological analysis with quark-model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Real part of the ηN S-wave scattering length: previously ambiguous range 0.2 fm ≤ Real(a_ηN) ≤ 0.98 fm; now resolved by overlapping results from Batinic (1998) and Green (1997) using different formalisms.
- S11(1535) resonance from Zagreb group: mass 1553 MeV, width 182 MeV, with branching fractions x_π = 46%, x_η = 50%, x_π² = 4%.
- S11(1650) resonance from Zagreb group: mass 1652 MeV, width 202 MeV, with branching fractions x_π = 79%, x_η = 13%, x_π² = 8%.
- Fourth P11 resonance from Zagreb group: mass 1740 MeV, width 140 MeV, with branching fractions x_π = 28%, x_η = 12%, x_π² = 60%.
- P11(1710) from Zagreb group: mass 1729 MeV, width 180 MeV, with branching fractions x_π = 22%, x_η = 6%, x_π² = 72%.
- P11(2100) from Zagreb group: mass 2157 MeV, width 355 MeV, with branching fractions x_π = 16%, x_η = 83%, x_π² = 1%.

## Context

This review extends the coupled-channel analysis program initiated by Cutkosky (1979) and the KH80 group (Hohler 1983), and compares phenomenological results with quark-model predictions from Capstick (1998, 1999). The resolved value of the ηN scattering length enables more reliable predictions for η-light nuclei bound states, as discussed by Wycech (1996). The paper identifies open problems in NN → NNη and πd → NNη processes, where experimental data from Berger (1993), Chiavassa (1994), and Nefkens (E890) are still being analyzed, and where theoretical agreement on reaction mechanisms has not yet been reached.
