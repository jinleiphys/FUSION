# Analysing powers and spin correlations in deuteron-proton charge exchange at 726 MeV

> The charge exchange of vector polarised deuterons on a polarised hydrogen target has been studied in a high statistics experiment at the COSY-ANKE facility at a deuteron beam energy of Td = 726 MeV. By selecting two fast protons at low relative energy E_{pp}, the measured analysing powers and spin correlations are sensitive to interference terms between specific neutron-proton charge-exchange amplitudes at a neutron kinetic energy of Tn ~ 1/2 Td =363 MeV. An impulse approximation calculation, which takes into account corrections due to the angular distribution in the diproton, describes reasonably the dependence of the data on both E_{pp} and the momentum transfer. This lends broad support to the current neutron-proton partial-wave solution that was used in the estimation.

## Key claim

The measured analysing powers and spin correlations in deuteron-proton charge exchange at 726 MeV are broadly consistent with the current SAID neutron-proton partial wave solution at 363 MeV, but the agreement is not perfect: there are slight discrepancies near the forward direction that could originate from either the partial wave solution or experimental uncertainties.

## Method

Vector-polarised deuterons at 726 MeV were incident on a polarised hydrogen target in the COSY-ANKE magnetic spectrometer, and two fast protons at low relative energy \(E_{pp}\) were detected in the forward detector. The azimuthal dependence of the count rates for four combinations of beam and target polarisations was used to extract the deuteron vector analysing power \(A_y^d\), the proton analysing power \(A_y^p\), and the spin correlations \(C_{y,y}\) and \(C_{x,x}\). The data were compared to plane-wave impulse approximation calculations that used the SAID neutron-proton partial wave solution as input and included corrections for the angular distribution in the diproton.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron beam energy: \(T_d = 726\) MeV; corresponding neutron kinetic energy: \(T_n \approx 363\) MeV.
- Deuteron beam polarisation values: \(p_d^{\uparrow} = +61 \pm 4\%\) and \(p_d^{\downarrow} = -50 \pm 3\%\) at injection energy 75.6 MeV.
- Target proton polarisation: \(|p_p| = 69\% \pm 2\% (\text{stat}) \pm 3.5\% (\text{syst})\).
- Product of beam and target polarisation magnitudes: \(|p_d||p_p| = 0.373 \pm 0.015\).
- The \(\delta\) amplitude in the \(np\) charge-exchange amplitude has a zero at \(q \approx (140 - 10i)\) MeV/\(c\).
- The spin correlation \(C_{x,x}(0)\) predicted by the SAID solution is approximately \(-0.62\); the experimental value extracted for \(E_{pp} < 2\) MeV is approximately \(-0.57\).
- Systematic uncertainties: below 0.01 for analysing powers and 0.03 for spin correlations, plus 4% from the product of beam and target polarisations.
- The SAID single-energy solution uncertainty in \(C_{x,x}(0)\) is \(\pm 0.024\).
- Double scattering correction to \(C_{x,x}(0)\) is estimated at 0.003.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of analysing powers and spin correlations in the \(\vec{d}\vec{p} \to \{pp\}_s n\) reaction at deuteron beam energies of 1.2 GeV and 2.27 GeV (Mchedlishvili et al., 2013), which were consistent with modern partial wave solutions at 600 MeV but failed at 1135 MeV. The current high-statistics experiment at 726 MeV provides a more precise test at 363 MeV, where the \(np\) partial wave amplitudes are expected to be reliable. The results enable quantitative validation of the theoretical modelling of deuteron charge exchange (Carslon, 1991) and the SAID partial wave solution (Arndt et al., 2000), and can help constrain the \(np\) amplitudes near 180 degrees where direct measurements are scarce.
