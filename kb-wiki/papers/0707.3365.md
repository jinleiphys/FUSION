# Generalized Wick's theorem for multiquasiparticle overlaps as a limit of Gaudin's theorem

> We are able to rederive in a very simple way the standard generalized Wick's theorem for overlaps of mean field wave functions by using the extension of the statistical Wick's theorem (Gaudin's theorem) in the appropriate limits.

## Key claim

The authors show that the standard generalized Wick’s theorem for overlaps of mean field wave functions can be rederived as a limiting case of Gaudin’s statistical Wick’s theorem, provided the statistical probabilities in the density operator are taken to zero. This derivation is simpler than previous proofs and naturally extends to multiquasiparticle overlaps that are combinatorially cumbersome with standard techniques.

## Method

The authors start from Gaudin’s theorem for traces of products of quasiparticle operators weighted by a statistical density operator \(\hat{\rho}\) that satisfies a linear commutation relation with the operators. They then replace \(\hat{\rho}\) by the product \(\hat{\rho}\hat{\mathcal{T}}^{-1}\), where \(\hat{\mathcal{T}}\) performs a canonical transformation, and take the limit in which the statistical probabilities \(p_m\) (the eigenvalues of the density operator in its diagonal basis) go to zero. In this limit the trace reduces to an overlap between two product wave functions, and the contractions of Gaudin’s theorem become the standard contractions of the generalized Wick’s theorem. The same limiting procedure is applied to more general cases, including overlaps where the quasiparticle operators are not related to the wave functions and where an additional canonical transformation operator separates the operator string.

## Key numbers

* The matrix of contractions for the standard overlap is \(\overline{\mathbb{C}} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & -X^{-1}Y \end{pmatrix}\), where \(X\) and \(Y\) are blocks of the canonical transformation matrix \(\mathbb{T}\) (Eq. 28).
* The contraction for two quasiparticle annihilation operators is zero: \(\langle\tilde{\phi}|\beta_{m_1}\beta_{m_2}|\phi\rangle / \langle\tilde{\phi}|\phi\rangle = 0\) (Eq. 29).
* The contraction for one annihilation and one creation operator is the identity: \(\langle\tilde{\phi}|\beta_{m_1}\beta_{m_2}^+|\phi\rangle / \langle\tilde{\phi}|\phi\rangle = \delta_{m_1 m_2}\) (Eq. 30).
* The contraction for two creation operators is \(\langle\tilde{\phi}|\beta_{m_1}^+\beta_{m_2}^+|\phi\rangle / \langle\tilde{\phi}|\phi\rangle = -(X^{-1}Y)_{m_1 m_2}\) (Eq. 31).

## Context

This work extends the statistical Wick’s theorem of Gaudin (1960) and the generalized Wick’s theorem for fermion overlaps previously derived by Balian and Brezin, Hara (1979), and Onishi (1966). By providing a simpler derivation based on a limiting procedure, the authors enable straightforward computation of overlaps involving multiquasiparticle excitations and of overlaps where the quasiparticle operators are not tied to the reference vacuum. The new compact expressions reduce the combinatorial complexity that arises in standard calculations of such overlaps, which is relevant for beyond-mean-field nuclear structure methods that restore broken symmetries or include quantum fluctuations.
