# Bulk viscosity coefficient of hadronic matter

> The bulk viscosity coefficient of hadronic matter has been estimated in this present work, where the thermodynamical equilibrium quantity like speed of sound in the medium has been obtained by using standard hadron resonance gas model. Whereas, the non-equilibrium quantity like thermal widths of medium constituents have been calculated in the framework field theory at finite temperature. Our values of bulk viscosity coefficient are in agreement with some earlier estimations.

# Bulk viscosity coefficient of hadronic matter

## Key claim
The bulk viscosity coefficient of hadronic matter is estimated by combining the equilibrium speed of sound from the hadron resonance gas model with non-equilibrium thermal widths of pions and nucleons calculated from finite-temperature field theory. The authors claim their values agree with some earlier estimations, but note that while their ratio ζ/s decreases with temperature at zero baryon chemical potential, some other investigations have reported it to increase with temperature or show a peak structure near the transition temperature.

## Method
The equilibrium thermodynamics of all hadrons in the medium is described by the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, using hadrons and resonances with masses up to 2 GeV from the Particle Data Book. The non-equilibrium thermal widths of pions and nucleons are calculated from the imaginary parts of their self-energies at finite temperature, considering mesonic and baryonic loops for pions and pion-baryon intermediate states for nucleons. These widths are then used in standard expressions for the bulk viscosity coefficient derived from either the relaxation time approximation in kinetic theory or the one-loop Kubo framework, with separate contributions for pions and nucleons summed to obtain the total bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers
- Bulk viscosity ζ and entropy density s both increase with temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB; the ratio ζ/s is a decreasing function of T but increases with μB.
- At μB = 0, 0.250 GeV, and 0.500 GeV, the temperature dependence of ζ, s, and ζ/s is shown (see Fig. 1 left panel).
- ζ and ζ/s decrease with increasing center-of-mass energy √sNN, qualitatively agreeing with earlier results from Kadam (2015).
- The decreasing trend of ζ and ζ/s with √s is attributed to μB decreasing with √s while T remains fairly constant in the analyzed √s range.

## Context
This work extends the calculation of bulk viscosity for hadronic matter by combining the HRG model for equilibrium properties with thermal field theory for non-equilibrium widths, following the approach of Gavin (1985) for the Kubo framework. The results at μB = 0 agree with most earlier works summarized in Ghosh (2015), but contrast with some investigations that report ζ/s increasing with temperature or having a peak structure near the transition temperature, as noted by Kinkar (2017). The beam energy dependence uses the parameterization from Karsch (2003) for temperature and chemical potential from fits to hadron yields. This enables further studies of transport coefficients in heavy-ion collisions across different beam energies.
