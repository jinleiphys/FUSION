# A-dependence of hadronization in nuclei

> The A-dependence of models for the attenuation of hadron production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on a nucleus is investigated for realistic matter distributions. It is shown that the dependence for a pure partonic (absorption) mechanism is more complicated than a simple $A^{2/3}$ ($A^{1/3}$) behavior, commonly found when using rectangular or Gaussian distributions, but that the A-dependence may still be indicative for the dominant mechanism of hadronization.

## Key claim

The A-dependence of hadron attenuation in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering is more complicated than a simple A^(2/3) or A^(1/3) power law when realistic nuclear matter distributions are used, but the A-dependence may still be indicative for the dominant mechanism of hadronization.

## Method

The authors evaluated two schematic models: model I assumes a purely partonic mechanism where attenuation arises from energy loss of the struck quark due to multiple scattering and gluon bremsstrahlung, depending on the squared density-averaged path length ⟨t_I^2⟩; model II assumes attenuation comes entirely from absorption of the produced hadron after formation, depending on the density-averaged path length ⟨t_II⟩. They calculated these integrals using a 2-parameter Fermi (Saxon-Woods) distribution with fixed parameters ρ0=0.170 nucleons/fm^3 and a=0.5 fm, and also using parameterizations of measured charge distributions for 11 real nuclei from ^2H to ^208Pb.

## Key numbers

- For a 2-parameter Fermi distribution, the power-law exponent α for ⟨t_I^2⟩ is well in excess of 2/3 (the value expected from rectangular or Gaussian distributions).
- For model II with a 2-parameter Fermi distribution, α ranges from about 0.40 for L_f=0 fm to 0.60 for L_f=4 fm.
- For real nuclei (excluding ^2H), fitting the A-dependence gives α=0.74 for ⟨t_I^2⟩.
- For real nuclei (excluding ^2H), α for ⟨t_II⟩ ranges between 0.40 and 0.60 depending on L_f.
- When ^4He is excluded from the fit, α for ⟨t_I^2⟩ increases from 0.74 to 0.79.
- The formation length L_f is estimated to be in the range 1-10 fm for virtual-photon energies 5-30 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronization in nuclei (Air01, Air03) that often used simple rectangular or Gaussian matter distributions, showing that such approximations are inadequate. The results enable more accurate comparisons between experimental data and models of hadronization, such as those by Wang (2001), Arleo (2001), Accardi (2005), Kopeliovich (2004), and Falter (2004). The authors demonstrate that using experimentally established density functions for the specific nuclei in an experiment is essential, and that even the inclusion or exclusion of ^4He affects the extracted A-dependence.
