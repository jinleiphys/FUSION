# Medium-Induced Quarkonium Dissociation at Finite Chemical Potential and Weak Magnetic Field

> We investigate the in-medium modification and dissociation of heavy quarkonium in a hot QCD medium at finite quark chemical potential and in the weak magnetic-field regime. Starting from the one-loop resummed gluon propagator in the imaginary-time formalism, and incorporating non-perturbative effects through a phenomenological correction to the HTL description, we compute the real and imaginary parts of the dielectric permittivity. This, in turn, leads to a complex heavy-quark potential: the real part is used to determine binding energies by solving the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation, while the imaginary part generates thermal decay widths, dominated by Landau damping. Within the explored parameter range, temperature has the greatest control over Debye screening, potential modification, and quarkonium stability, whereas finite density and weak magnetic fields introduce comparatively smaller quantitative changes. As the temperature increases, binding energies decrease and thermal widths grow, giving rise to the expected hierarchy between ground and excited states and a sequential suppression pattern in the dissociation temperatures. Overall, our results indicate that while finite chemical potential and weak magnetic fields can shift quarkonium properties in a measurable way, thermal effects remain the primary driver of dissociation, with direct relevance for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.

# Medium-Induced Quarkonium Dissociation at Finite Chemical Potential and Weak Magnetic Field

## Key claim

Temperature is the primary driver of Debye screening, potential modification, and quarkonium stability, while finite chemical potential and weak magnetic fields introduce comparatively smaller quantitative changes; the authors find that within their explored parameter range, thermal effects remain the dominant mechanism for quarkonium dissociation, with finite density and magnetic fields producing only modest shifts in binding energies, thermal widths, and dissociation temperatures.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop resummed gluon propagator using the imaginary-time formalism in the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, then incorporate non-perturbative effects through a phenomenological dimension-two gluon condensate correction to the HTL propagator following Guo (2019). From the static limit of the 00-component of the resummed propagator, they obtain the complex dielectric permittivity, which yields a complex heavy-quark potential; the real part is used to determine binding energies by solving the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation numerically, while the imaginary part generates thermal decay widths dominated by Landau damping, with dissociation temperatures estimated using the criterion that the thermal width exceeds twice the binding energy.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium quarkonium properties by incorporating both finite chemical potential and weak magnetic fields simultaneously, building on the non-perturbative propagator approach of Guo (2019) that accounts for dimension-two gluon condensates. The authors build upon prior investigations of heavy quark bound states in magnetic backgrounds by Hasan (2020) and Khan (2023), and complement lattice QCD studies by Braguta (2014) and Bonati (2015, 2016, 2017) by providing an analytic framework for the combined effects. The results enable more realistic modeling of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collision phenomenology at RHIC and LHC, where both finite baryon density and transient magnetic fields are present, and are relevant for future experiments at FAIR that will explore baryon-rich QCD matter.
