# The effect of strong electric field on the evolution of charmonium in quark gluon plasma

> In ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the strong electric field can be produced by the colliding nuclei. The magnitude of the electric field $E$ is on the order of $eE\sim m_π^2$ at the early stage of the collision. In quark gluon plasma (QGP), such a strong electric field can have a significant impact on the evolution of charmonia. We employ the time-dependent Schrödinger equation to study the evolution of charmonium states in the strong electric field generated by the moving charges. The electric field can result in transitions between charmonium states with different angular momenta. In order to see this effect, we make comparisons between the yields of $ J/ψ$, $ψ'$ and $χ_c$ with and without the electric field. The results show that in the early stage of the collision the electric field induces significant dissociation of $J/ψ$. In the meantime, $χ_c$ is generated via the transition from $J/ψ$ by the electric field.

## Key claim
The strong electric field (peak magnitude ~m_pi^2) present in the early stage of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions induces significant additional dissociation of J/psi beyond that caused by the hot QGP medium alone, while simultaneously generating chi_c states via electric dipole transitions from J/psi.

## Method
The authors solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for charmonium states in a screened Cornell potential that models the QGP at finite temperature, with an additional time-dependent electric field term from two moving heavy ions. The wave function is expanded in spherical harmonics, and the radial Schrodinger equation is solved using the MSD2 method, tracking the projection of the evolved state onto vacuum Cornell potential eigenstates (1S, 2S, 1P, 2P, 1D) over the first 2 fm/c of evolution.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass taken as 1.25 GeV; reduced mass mu = m_c/2 = 0.625 GeV
- Vacuum binding energy of J/psi: epsilon_B^{J/psi} = 640 MeV
- J/psi mean radius: <r>_{J/psi} ~ 0.5 fm
- Critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV; QGP temperature taken as 1.5 T_c = 255 MeV
- For Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (gamma ~ 100), electric field lifetime ~ 0.2 fm/c; J/psi fraction drops to 0.5 in 2 fm/c with electric field present
- For Pb-Pb at gamma ~ 1000, electric field lifetime ~ 0.02 fm/c; J/psi fraction drops significantly in first 0.02 fm/c
- Vacuum masses from Cornell potential: m_{J/psi} = 3.09 GeV, m_{psi'} = 3.70 GeV, m_{chi_c} = 3.48 GeV, m_{2P} = 3.98 GeV, m_{1D} = 3.78 GeV

## Context
This work extends the standard Matsui-Satz (1986) picture of J/psi suppression in QGP via color screening by adding the effect of the strong electric field generated by the colliding nuclei, which was previously studied in the context of chiral magnetic effects. The authors use the non-relativistic Schrodinger approach with screened Cornell potential (Karsch 1988) and the electric field formula from Jackson (1999). The results enable future studies with non-uniform electric fields, realistic initial states from pQCD, and extension to bottomonium systems.
