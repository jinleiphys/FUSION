# The contributions of $qqqq\bar{q}$ components to the axial charges of proton and its resonances

> We calculate the axial charges of the proton and its resonances in the framework of the constituent quark model, which is extended to include the $qqqq\bar{q}$ components. If 20% admixtures of the $qqqq\bar{q}$ components in the proton are assumed, the theoretical value for the axial charge in our model is in good agreement with the empirical value, which can not be well reproduced in the traditional constituent quark model even though the $SU(6) \bigotimes O(3)$ symmetry breaking or relativistic effect is taken into account. We also predict an unity axial charge for $N^{*}(1440)$ with 30% $qqqq\bar{q}$ components constrained by the strong and electromagnetic decays.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including 20% admixtures of five-quark (qqqqq̄) components in the proton wave function brings the theoretical axial charge into agreement with the experimental value, which the traditional constituent quark model cannot achieve even with SU(6) ⊗ O(3) symmetry breaking or relativistic effects. The authors also predict a unity axial charge for N*(1440) with 30% five-quark components, but note this prediction depends on the specific configuration assumed.

## Method

The authors extended the constituent quark model by including five-quark (qqqqq̄) components in the wave functions of the proton and its resonances, using spin-flavor hyperfine interactions to classify configurations. They calculated diagonal axial charges as sums of configuration-specific coefficients multiplied by their probabilities, using the nonrelativistic axial charge operator which is independent of spatial wave function parameters. For negative parity resonances N*(1535) and N*(1650), they considered mixing angles between the ²P_M and ⁴P_M states and compared results with lattice QCD data.

## Key numbers

- Proton axial charge experimental value: g_A = 1.2670 ± 0.0030 (in units of vector charge g_V)
- Traditional SU(6) ⊗ O(3) quark model predicts proton axial charge = 5/3 (approximately 1.667), about 24% larger than experiment
- With 20% five-quark components in the proton, the extended model gives g_A(N(938)) in the range 1.25 to 1.45, bracketing the experimental value 1.267
- For N*(1440) with 30% five-quark components (80% in [4]_FS[22]_F[22]_S and 20% in [4]_FS[31]_F[31]_S), the predicted axial charge is 1.04
- For N*(1535), the extended model gives axial charge in the range -0.05 to +0.06, consistent with lattice QCD result of approximately 0
- For N*(1650) with 10% to 30% five-quark components, the axial charge falls in the range 0.39 to 0.54, including the lattice value of approximately 0.55
- The sum g_A(N(1535)) + g_A(N(1650)) = 4/9 exactly, independent of mixing angle

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Zou and Riska (2005) and An et al. (2008) who suggested that puzzles in baryon resonances may be solved by including multiquark configurations. The paper addresses the long-standing problem that the traditional SU(6) ⊗ O(3) quark model overpredicts the proton axial charge by about 24%, a discrepancy not resolved by symmetry breaking or relativistic effects (MIT bag model gives 1.09). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations to constrain the proportion of sea-quark components in the Roper resonance N*(1440) and other nucleon resonances, and suggest that photoproduction of kaon mesons and pp → pK⁺Λ reactions can probe the five-quark content of N*(1650).
