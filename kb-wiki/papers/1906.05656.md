# Polarization of tau in quasielastic (anti)neutrino scattering: the role of spectral functions

> We present a study of the $τ$ polarization in charged-current quasielastic (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering. The spectral function formalism is used to compute the differential cross section and the polarization components for several kinematical setups, relevant for neutrino-oscillation experiments. The effects of the nuclear corrections in these observables are investigated by comparing the results obtained using two different realistic spectral functions, with those deduced from the relativistic global Fermi gas model, where only statistical correlations are accounted for. We show that the spectral functions, although they play an important role when predicting the differential cross sections, produce much less visible effects on the polarization components of the outgoing $τ$.

## Key claim

The spectral function formalism, which incorporates realistic nuclear dynamics including nucleon-nucleon correlations, produces significantly different predictions for the quasielastic differential cross section compared to the simpler Relativistic Global Fermi Gas model, but the effects on the longitudinal and transverse polarization components of the outgoing tau lepton are much less visible, with the polarization observables being largely insensitive to the details of the nuclear model.

## Method

The authors computed the double-differential cross section and the longitudinal (P_L) and transverse (P_T) polarization components of the tau lepton produced in charged-current quasielastic (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering on oxygen-16, using the spectral function formalism within the Impulse Approximation. They compared results from three nuclear models: the Relativistic Global Fermi Gas (RGFG) model, a semi-phenomenological spectral function based on nucleon self-energies in the Local Density Approximation (LDA), and a spectral function derived from Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory. The calculations were performed for neutrino energies between 4 and 10 GeV and for small lepton scattering angles where the cross section is largest.

## Key numbers

- For oxygen-16 in the RGFG model, the effective Fermi momentum is taken as \(\bar{p}_F = 225\) MeV, with a shift energy \(\epsilon \sim 15\) MeV for neutrino-induced reactions and \(\epsilon \sim 11\) MeV for antineutrino-induced reactions.
- The tau lepton mass is \(m_\tau \approx 1776.8\) MeV, which limits the available phase space and produces a maximum allowed LAB tau scattering angle of approximately \(11.4^\circ\) at \(E_\nu = 4\) GeV, \(20.3^\circ\) at \(E_\nu = 6\) GeV, \(23.6^\circ\) at \(E_\nu = 8\) GeV, and \(25.5^\circ\) at \(E_\nu = 10\) GeV.
- The threshold neutrino energy for tau production is approximately \(E_\nu = m_\tau + m_\tau^2/2M \sim 3.46\) GeV, at which the energy transfer at the QE peak is \(q^0_{\rm min} = q^0_{\rm max} \sim 0.58\) GeV.
- At \(E_\nu = 4\) GeV and forward scattering (\(\theta = 0^\circ\)), the two QE peaks occur at \(q^2 = -0.36\) GeV\(^2\) (\(q^0 = 0.19\) GeV) and \(q^2 = -2.88\) GeV\(^2\) (\(q^0 = 1.53\) GeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tau polarization in quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering that used free nucleon targets (Hagiwara 2003) or the Random Phase Approximation with an effective nucleon mass (Graczyk 2004). It improves upon an earlier attempt to include spectral functions (Valverde 2006) that was restricted to a narrow phase-space region due to non-relativistic nuclear calculations. The results are a natural continuation of the authors' prior work on electroweak scaling properties (Sobczyk 2017) and strange hyperon production in nuclei (Sobczyk 2019), and they enable future experimental studies of tau polarization in neutrino oscillation experiments such as SHiP, which will measure tau neutrino cross sections with statistics 100 times larger than the DONuT experiment.
