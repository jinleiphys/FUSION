# A Quasiparticle Transport Explanation for Collectivity in the Smallest of Collision Systems (p + p and e+e-)

> The field of heavy ion physics is at a crossroads in understanding experimental signatures of collectivity in small collision systems, p + p and p(d/3He) + A, at RHIC and the LHC. A wealth of data obtained in the latter class of asymmetric systems indicate the existence of particle emission patterns similar to those observed in larger A+A collisions [1], raising the question of whether the same physics is at play in both cases, lest the cruelty of nature be somehow exposed. In this talk, we present an extension of earlier studies using the quasiparticle transport model AMPT to predict particle emission patterns in the smallest of collision systems, namely p + p and e+e-. The e+e- results have been previously published [2] and we thus focus here on an extended set of calculations, as shown at the Quark Matter 2018 Conference.

## Key claim
The AMPT quasiparticle transport model can reproduce qualitative features of collective flow coefficients (v₂, v₃) measured in p+p collisions at 13 TeV, but the model's applicability is questionable because the Boltzmann equation's dilute-gas condition (interparticle spacing much smaller than mean free path) is violated at early times, with dilution parameter D ≥ 1.

## Method
The authors used a modified version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) kinetic transport code, which solves the Boltzmann equation numerically for 2→2 parton scattering after a formation time. For p+p collisions, they implemented a constituent quark framework where each proton consists of three constituent quarks, with initial collisions modeled via a Monte Carlo Glauber approach. They computed flow coefficients v₂ and v₃ using both the true initial geometry and the ATLAS template fitting method for non-flow subtraction, and compared results to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- In e⁺e⁻ collisions at the Z boson mass, parton initial positions are at r ≈ 0.1 fm
- Two-string control test used a constant string separation of 0.5 fm
- Partonic cross sections tested in the range 0.75–3.0 mb produced isotropic angular distributions of scattered parton pairs
- The dilution parameter D (interparticle spacing divided by mean free path) was found to be D ≥ 1 at early times in AMPT, violating the Boltzmann equation requirement D << 1

## Context
This work extends earlier AMPT studies of collectivity in small systems (He, 2015) to the smallest possible collision systems: p+p and e⁺e⁻. The e⁺e⁻ results were previously published by Nagle (2017). The calculations are compared to ATLAS data from Aaboud (2016) and CMS data from Khachatryan (2016) for p+p collisions at √s = 13 TeV. The paper raises fundamental questions about whether kinetic transport models are appropriate for systems where the Boltzmann equation's dilute-gas limit is not satisfied, and highlights that both ATLAS and CMS non-flow subtraction methods fail closure tests in AMPT because jet-associated partons do interact in high-multiplicity events.
