# Radial oscillations of quark stars from perturbative QCD

> We perform the general relativistic stability analysis against radial oscillations of unpaired quark stars obtained using the equation of state for cold quark matter from perturbative QCD, the only free parameter being the renormalization scale. This approach consistently incorporates the effects of interactions and includes a built-in estimate of the inherent systematic uncertainties in the evaluation of the equation of state. We also take into account the constraints imposed by the recent gravitational wave event GW 170817 to the compact star masses and radii, and restrict their vibrational spectrum.

# Radial oscillations of quark stars from perturbative QCD

## Key claim

The fundamental and first-excited radial oscillation frequencies of unpaired quark stars, computed using an equation of state from perturbative QCD with the renormalization scale as the only free parameter, produce a band of possibilities rather than a single curve; only renormalization scale values between approximately 3 and 3.2 simultaneously satisfy the GW170817 constraints on both maximum mass (2.01–2.16 solar masses) and radius (12–13.45 km for a 1.4 solar mass star).

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for hydrostatic equilibrium using the FKV pocket formula for cold, beta-equilibrated, electrically neutral quark matter from perturbative QCD, with the renormalization scale parameter X_FKV varied between 1 and 4. They then solved the Chandrasekhar-Gondek first-order coupled differential equations for linear adiabatic radial oscillations to obtain eigenfrequencies for the fundamental (n=0) and first-excited (n=1) modes, imposing the boundary condition that the Lagrangian pressure perturbation vanishes at the stellar surface.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses for pQCD quark stars range from 0.404 solar masses (X_FKV=1) to 3.042 solar masses (X_FKV=4); for X_FKV=3 the maximum mass is 2.038 solar masses with radius 11.75 km, and for X_FKV=3.2 it is 2.236 solar masses with radius 12.93 km
- For a canonical 1.4 solar mass star, the fundamental mode frequency is approximately 3.5 kHz (period 0.29 ms) for both X_FKV=3 and X_FKV=3.2, and approximately 3.3 kHz (period 0.30 ms) for X_FKV=4
- For a 1.4 solar mass star, the first-excited mode frequency is approximately 9 kHz (period 0.11 ms) for X_FKV=3 and X_FKV=3.2, and approximately 8 kHz (period 0.13 ms) for X_FKV=4
- For stellar configurations satisfying the GW170817 mass constraint (2.01–2.16 solar masses), the fundamental mode period for X_FKV=3.2 ranges from approximately 0.5 ms to 1.0 ms
- The maximum gravitational redshift Z for pQCD quark stars accumulates between 0.42 and 0.48 across different X_FKV values
- The bag model with B^(1/4)=145 MeV yields a maximum mass of 2.02 solar masses with radius 10.99 km

## Context

This work extends the radial oscillation analysis of quark stars beyond the MIT bag model framework used by Benvenuto (1989, 1991), Vath (1992), and Lugones (1995), which gave misleading precision by omitting systematic uncertainties from interactions. The pQCD approach, following the equation of state of Kurkela (2009) and the pocket formula of Fraga (2013), consistently incorporates running coupling and strange quark mass effects. The results enable future comparisons with non-radial oscillation modes detectable in gravitational waves, and provide a foundation for studying hybrid stars with hadronic mantles and crusts, as well as discriminating between hadronic and quark stars near the two-solar mass limit.
