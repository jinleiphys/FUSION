# Relativistic Mean Field Calculations of $Λ$ and $Σ$ Hypernuclei

> Single--particle spectra of $Λ$ and $Σ$ hypernuclei are calculated within a relativistic mean--field theory. The hyperon couplings used are compatible with the $Λ$ binding in saturated nuclear matter, neutron-star masses and experimental data on $Λ$ levels in hypernuclei. Special attention is devoted to the spin-orbit potential for the hyperons and the influence of the $ρ$-meson field (isospin dependent interaction).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic mean-field calculations of Lambda and Sigma hypernuclei, using hyperon couplings constrained by neutron-star masses, Lambda binding in saturated nuclear matter, and experimental Lambda levels, produce single-particle spectra that are mutually compatible and narrowly constraining, contrary to earlier fits that treated the couplings as independent parameters and found correlation errors up to plus or minus 65 percent.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac-Hartree equations for hyperons only once, using as input the meson fields of the corresponding nucleonic system that were self-consistently determined within a relativistic Thomas-Fermi (RTF) approximation, a technique similar to the expectation-value method. For the nucleonic sector they used a Lagrangian including couplings to sigma, omega, and rho meson fields plus phenomenological sigma self-interactions, and for the Sigma hyperons they wrote a Lagrangian that includes coupling to the rho-meson field and the electromagnetic field, treating the three charge states (Sigma minus, Sigma zero, Sigma plus) explicitly.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of spin-orbit splitting for Sigma zero to Lambda hyperons is given by M_Lambda squared over M_Sigma squared = 0.88, derived from the Foldy-Wouthuysen reduction.
* For the asymmetric hypernucleus Zr-90, switching off the rho-Sigma coupling changed the binding of Sigma minus (Sigma plus) states by 2.1 to 3.5 MeV; for Pb-208 the corresponding range was 4.4 to 6.9 MeV.
* The binding energy of the lowest Sigma zero level in saturated nuclear matter is -28 MeV under the assumption of universal hyperon coupling.
* For Ca-40, the rms-radii of the Sigma minus s, p, d, and f states were 2.65, 3.31, 3.89, and 4.76 fm respectively, compared with the experimental charge rms-radius of about 3.48 fm for Ca-40.
* The 1s_{1/2} Sigma minus state in Zr-90 had an rms-radius of 3.09 fm with the rho field switched on, and in Pb-208 the 1s_{1/2} Sigma minus state had an rms-radius of 3.68 fm.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Rufa et al. (1990) for Lambda hypernuclei by incorporating the constraints from neutron-star masses and Lambda binding in nuclear matter established by Glendenning and Moszkowski (1991), and extends the same framework to Sigma hypernuclei under the assumption of universal hyperon coupling. The calculations provide predictions for Sigma hypernuclear spectra that could be tested at facilities such as CEBAF, PILAC, and KAON, and the inclusion of the rho-meson field offers a natural way to incorporate the isospin-dependent Lane potential for Sigma hyperons, which was identified by Dover (1992) as an important open question.
