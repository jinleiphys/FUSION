# Elliptic flow ($v_2$) in pp collisions at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider: A hydrodynamical approach

> At Large Hadron Collider energy, the expected large multiplicities suggests the presence of collective behavior even in pp collisions. A hydrodynamical approach has been applied to estimate the expected elliptic flow measured by the azimuthal asymmetry parameter $v_2$, in pp collisions at $\surd$s = 14 TeV. $v_2$ of $π^-$ is found to be strongly dependent on the parton density profile inside a proton [e.g., surface diffuseness parameter ($ξ$)]. For $ξ$ = 0.105, $v_2$ is found to be positive while at $ξ$ = 0.25, $v_2$ is close to zero and approaches negative values at large $p_t$. The impact parameter dependence of $v_2$ has also been studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that elliptic flow ($v_2$) in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 14 TeV can be estimated using a hydrodynamical approach, but the magnitude of $v_2$ depends strongly on the assumed parton density profile inside the proton, specifically the surface diffuseness parameter ($\xi$). The abstract glosses over the condition that for a more diffuse proton ($\xi$ = 0.25 fm), $v_2$ is close to zero and can approach negative values at large $p_t$, while only for a sharper density profile ($\xi$ = 0.105 fm) is $v_2$ positive and large.

## Method

The authors applied a 2+1-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model (AZYHYDRO code) with longitudinal boost invariance to simulate the space-time evolution of the system formed in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 14 TeV. Initial energy density distributions were obtained from a Glauber model using a Woods-Saxon profile for the proton with two values of the surface diffuseness parameter ($\xi$ = 0.105 fm and 0.25 fm), and the initial energy density was tuned to reproduce the extrapolated charged-particle pseudorapidity density of 5.69 at midrapidity. The hydrodynamic evolution used a lattice QCD + hadron resonance gas equation of state, and particle spectra and elliptic flow were computed via the Cooper-Frye formalism at freeze-out temperatures of 130, 140, and 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated minimum bias charged-particle pseudorapidity density at y = 0 for pp at $\sqrt{s}$ = 14 TeV: 5.69
- For $\xi$ = 0.105 fm and $\tau_i$ = 0.6 fm, $T_F$ = 140 MeV: minimum bias $p_T$ integrated $v_2$ = 1.38%; for $\xi$ = 0.25 fm under same conditions: $v_2$ = 0.37%
- Minimum bias differential $v_2(p_T)$ reaches up to 24% at $p_T$ = 3 GeV/c for $\xi$ = 0.105 fm, versus up to 6% at $p_T$ = 3 GeV/c for $\xi$ = 0.25 fm
- For 20-50% centrality with $\xi$ = 0.105 fm ($\tau_i$ = 0.6 fm, $T_F$ = 140 MeV): $p_T$ integrated $v_2$ = 1.34%; for $\xi$ = 0.25 fm: $v_2$ = 0.34%
- Charged-particle rapidity density at b = 0: 13.79 for $\xi$ = 0.105 fm, 12.01 for $\xi$ = 0.25 fm
- Initial energy density required for $\xi$ = 0.105 fm at $\tau_i$ = 0.6 fm, $T_F$ = 140 MeV: 40.0 GeV/fm$^3$; for $\xi$ = 0.25 fm: 26.2 GeV/fm$^3$

## Context

This work extends the application of hydrodynamic models to small systems, specifically pp collisions at LHC energies, following earlier suggestions that hydrodynamics might be applicable in pp collisions after an isotropization time scale (A. K. Chaudhuri, 2008) and predictions of elliptic flow in pp collisions from multiparton interactions (d'Enterria, 2009). The study enables experimental searches for collective flow in high-multiplicity pp events at the LHC, and proposes that the centrality dependence of $v_2$ could be used to extract the surface diffuseness parameter of the proton, complementing earlier predictions of large $v_2$ (10-20%) from hot-spot-like initial conditions (Urs, 2009).
