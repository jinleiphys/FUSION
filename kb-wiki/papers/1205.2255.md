# Pion- and photo-induced transition amplitudes to $ΛK$, $ΣK$, and $Nη$

> Pion and photo-induced inelastic reactions off protons are studied in a multichannel partial-wave analysis. Properties of nucleon and $Δ$ resonances are derived and compared to previous analyses. Amplitudes are shown for transitions to $Nη$, $ΛK$, and $ΣK$ final states.

## Key claim

The paper presents transition amplitudes for pion- and photo-induced reactions to ΛK, ΣK, and Nη final states derived from a multichannel partial-wave analysis, but the authors emphasize that the fits do not converge to a well-defined minimum; instead, two classes of solutions (BnGa2011-01 and BnGa2011-02) are found, defined by whether one or two resonances with J^P = 7/2^+ are assumed, and within each class multiple solutions exist.

## Method

The Bonn-Gatchina (BnGa) group performed a multichannel partial-wave analysis fitting a large fraction of world data on nucleon resonances, including pion-induced reactions (π⁻p → ΛK⁰, π⁺p → K⁺Σ⁺, π⁻p → K⁰Σ⁰, π⁻p → nη) and photo-induced reactions (γp → ΛK⁺, γp → Σ⁰K⁺, γp → Σ⁺K⁰, γp → pη). The πN elastic scattering amplitudes from the GWU analysis (Arndt 2006) were used as input to avoid biasing toward a multitude of resonances. The resulting amplitudes are presented as multipole decompositions and compared to earlier results from Candlin et al. (1983), the Bonn-Juelich group (Doring 2010), KAON-MAID (Bennhold 1999), SAID, and MAID.

## Key numbers

- The reaction π⁻p → ΛK⁰ was studied from threshold up to 2.32 GeV in invariant mass (Baker 1978).
- The reaction π⁺p → K⁺Σ⁺ was studied from threshold to 2.35 GeV centre-of-mass energy (Candlin 1982).
- Multipoles for photoproduction are given in units of 10⁻³/M_π⁺.
- The double polarization variable G data from ELSA at low energies (M < 1.65 GeV) are compatible with BnGa2011-01 and BnGa2011-02 predictions, while sizable discrepancies are seen to MAID and SAID predictions.
- At medium energies (M ≈ 1.65 – 1.80 GeV), all predictions are reasonably close to the data.

## Context

This work extends the Bonn-Gatchina group's previous analyses (Anisovich 2010, 2011) by comparing their transition amplitudes for hyperon and η production with other partial-wave analysis models, including those from Candlin et al. (1983), the Bonn-Juelich group (Doring 2010), and KAON-MAID (Bennhold 1999). The paper reveals severe discrepancies between different analyses, attributed to the lack of polarization data, and enables future improvements as ongoing experiments with polarized photon beams, polarized targets, and recoil polarization measurements at Bonn, Mainz, and JLab are completed and analyzed.
