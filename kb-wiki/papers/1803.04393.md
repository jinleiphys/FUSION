# Lattice Calculation of Parton Distribution Function from LaMET at Physical Pion Mass with Large Nucleon Momentum

> We present a lattice-QCD calculation of the unpolarized isovector parton distribution function (PDF) using ensembles at the physical pion mass with large proton boost momenta $P_z \in \{2.2,2.6,3.0\}$~GeV within the framework of large-momentum effective theory (LaMET). In contrast to our previous physical-pion PDF result, we increase the statistics significantly, double the boost momentum, increase the investment in excited-state contamination systematics, and switch to $γ_t$ operator to avoid mixing with scalar matrix elements. We use four source-sink separations in our analysis to control the systematics associated with excited-state contamination. The one-loop LaMET matching corresponding to the new operator is calculated and applied to our lattice data. We detail the systematics that affect PDF calculations, providing guidelines to improve the precision of future lattice PDF calculations. We find our final parton distribution to be in reasonable agreement with the PDF provided by the latest phenomenological analysis.

## Key claim

The authors present a lattice-QCD calculation of the unpolarized isovector parton distribution function (PDF) at physical pion mass, finding their final distribution to be in reasonable agreement with the PDF provided by the latest phenomenological analysis, but with the caveat that the central values shift noticeably from 2.2 to 3.0 GeV nucleon momentum, moving the antiquark distribution toward the experimentally measured asymmetry.

## Method

The calculation uses clover valence fermions on an ensemble of gauge configurations with lattice spacing 0.09 fm, box size 5.8 fm, and pion mass 135 MeV, with Nf=2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) generated by the MILC Collaboration. The quasi-PDF is calculated with nucleon boost momenta of 2.2, 2.6, and 3.0 GeV using the gamma_t operator to avoid operator mixing, with nonperturbative renormalization in the RI/MOM scheme and one-loop LaMET matching to convert to the MS-bar lightcone PDF. Four source-sink separations (0.72, 0.81, 0.90, 1.08 fm) are used with a simultaneous fit to control excited-state contamination.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon boost momenta used: P_z = 2.2, 2.6, and 3.0 GeV (corresponding to n = 10, 12, 14 times 2pi/L)
- Lattice spacing a = 0.09 fm, box size L = 5.8 fm, pion mass M_pi = 135 MeV
- Four source-sink separations: 0.72, 0.81, 0.90, 1.08 fm
- Renormalization scales used: mu_R = 2.3 and 3.7 GeV
- Maximum z used in Fourier transform: z_max = 20a (approximately 1.8 fm)
- Largest z_max * P_z product: approximately 27.5
- The renormalization constant Z(z) becomes stable at large p^R_z, with central value chosen at p^R_z = 10 * 2pi/L

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous physical-pion PDF result (Lin 2017) by significantly increasing statistics, doubling the boost momentum, and switching to the gamma_t operator to avoid mixing with scalar matrix elements. It provides a state-of-the-art lattice calculation that agrees with global analysis PDFs such as CT14, NNPDF3.1, and CJ15, and improves upon the ETMC result (Alexandrou 2018) by having larger M_pi*L, larger nucleon boost momentum, bigger z*P_z range, and multistate fitting for excited-state removal. The results enable future calculations of polarized PDFs (helicity and transversity) and can provide constraints for global PDF analyses, particularly in the large-x region where experimental data are contaminated by nuclear effects.
