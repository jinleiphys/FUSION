# Hints on the power corrections from current correlators in x-space

> We consider an interpretation of the recent lattice data on the current-current correlators in the x-space. The data indicate rather striking difference between (axial)vector and (pseudo)scalar channels which goes beyond the predictions of the standard non-perturbative models. We argue that if the difference is to be explained by power corrections, there is a unique choice of the form of the correction. We discuss the emerging picture of the power corrections.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed difference between (axial)vector and (pseudo)scalar current correlators in lattice data can be uniquely explained by a specific power correction, the tachyonic gluon mass term proportional to \(\lambda^2\), provided one assumes that the difference is indeed due to power corrections and that the (axial)vector channels are well described by the instanton liquid model.

## Method

The authors compare the operator product expansion (OPE) predictions for current-current correlators in coordinate space with lattice data from DeGrand (2001) for the \((S+P)\) and \((V+A)\) channels. They use three sets of QCD condensate parameters (SET 1, SET 2, SET 3) where SET 3 includes a quadratic \(x^2\) term corresponding to a tachyonic gluon mass \(\lambda^2\), and they fit the lattice data at moderate \(x\) values to assess the role of this term. Higher-dimension (\(D=8\)) condensate contributions are also included to extend the fit to larger \(x\).

## Key numbers

- The quadratic correction parameter used in SET 3 is \((\alpha_s/\pi)\lambda^2 = -0.12\) GeV\(^2\).
- The gluon condensate value in SET 2 and SET 3 is \(\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle = 0.07\) GeV\(^4\) (twice the SVZ value of 0.04 GeV\(^4\)).
- The four-quark condensate in SET 2 and SET 3 is \(\alpha_s \langle \bar{q}q \rangle^2 = 5.8 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^6\).
- For the \((S+P)\) channel, the \(D=8\) condensate contribution needed to fit larger \(x\) is parametrized as \(+(x/0.58)^8\).
- For the \((V+A)\) channel, the fitted \(D=8\) contribution is parametrized as \((x/0.7)^8\).
- The convergence radius of the standard OPE is stated to be no larger than 0.3 fm based on lattice data in the \((V-A)\) channel.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal of quadratic power corrections by Chetyrkin, Narison, and Zakharov (1999) and applies it to new lattice data from DeGrand (2001) and Chu (1993). It contrasts with the instanton liquid model of Shuryak (1994), which describes the \((V\pm A)\) channels well but fails for the \((S+P)\) channel. The analysis enables a unified picture where a tachyonic gluon mass \(\lambda^2\) dominates intermediate distances (0.1–0.5 fm) and can be understood within effective Higgs-like theories of confinement, as discussed by Gubarev (2001) and Shevchenko (2001).
