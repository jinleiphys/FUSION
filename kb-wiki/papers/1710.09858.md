# First Monte Carlo global analysis of nucleon transversity with lattice QCD constraints

> We report on the first global QCD analysis of the quark transversity distributions in the nucleon from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS), using a new Monte Carlo method based on nested sampling and constraints on the isovector tensor charge $g_T$ from lattice QCD. A simultaneous fit to the available SIDIS Collins asymmetry data is compatible with $g_T$ values extracted from a comprehensive reanalysis of existing lattice simulations, in contrast to previous analyses which found significantly smaller $g_T$ values. The contributions to the nucleon tensor charge from $u$ and $d$ quarks are found to be $δu = 0.3(2)$ and $δd = -0.7(2)$ at a scale $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, contrary to previous analyses which found significantly smaller values, the SIDIS Collins asymmetry data are compatible with lattice QCD values of the isovector tensor charge \(g_T\) near unity when a Monte Carlo method based on nested sampling is used, resolving the apparent "transverse-spin puzzle."

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis of quark transversity distributions using a Monte Carlo method with nested sampling, fitting 106 data points from HERMES and COMPASS SIDIS Collins asymmetry data for \(\pi^\pm\) production on proton and deuteron targets. They incorporated constraints from a reanalysis of existing lattice QCD simulations of the isovector tensor charge \(g_T\), using a weighted average of results from the PNDME, RQCD, and LHPC collaborations with multiple extrapolation formulas combined via the Akaike information criterion. The transversity PDFs and Collins FFs were parametrized with factorized Gaussian transverse momentum dependence, and the analysis extracted 23 parameters from the data.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD averaged isovector tensor charge: \(g_T^\text{latt} = 1.01(6)\) at the physical point, used as a constraint in the global fit.
- For the SIDIS+lattice fit at \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\): \(\delta u = 0.3(2)\) and \(\delta d = -0.7(2)\).
- For the SIDIS+lattice fit at \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\): \(g_T = 1.0(1)\), compared to \(g_T = 0.9(8)\) for the SIDIS-only fit.
- Total \(\chi^2/\text{datum}\) for the Collins asymmetry fit: \(68.9/106 \approx 0.65\).
- Unpolarized valence quark PDF transverse width: \(\avkperp^q_{f_1} = 0.59(1)\) GeV\(^2\); sea quark PDF width: \(0.64(6)\) GeV\(^2\).
- Unpolarized favored FF width: \(\avpperp^{\pi/q}_{D_1} = 0.116(2)\) GeV\(^2\); unfavored FF width: \(0.140(2)\) GeV\(^2\).
- Transversity valence PDF width: \(\avkperp^q_{h_1} = 0.5(2)\) GeV\(^2\); sea transversity width: \(1.0(5)\) GeV\(^2\).
- Collins favored FF width: \(\avpperp^{\pi/q}_{H_1^\perp} = 0.12(4)\) GeV\(^2\); unfavored Collins FF width: \(0.06(3)\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous transversity analyses by Anselmino et al. (2007, 2008, 2013) and Kang et al. (2014, 2015), which used maximum likelihood methods and found \(g_T\) values around 0.5-0.6, in apparent tension with lattice QCD. The new Monte Carlo approach reveals that the SIDIS-only distribution has a long tail extending to higher \(g_T\) values, resolving the discrepancy. The results enable future work incorporating TMD evolution via the Collins-Soper-Sterman framework, inclusion of \(K^\pm\) SIDIS and \(e^+ e^-\) data for better flavor separation, and upcoming high-precision Jefferson Lab measurements at intermediate \(x\) and \(z\).
