# Photo- and electroproduction of $K^{+}Λ$ with unitarity-restored isobar model

> Exploiting the isobar model, kaon photo- and electroproduction on the proton in the resonance region comes under scrutiny. An upgrade of our previous model, comprising higher-spin nucleon and hyperon exchanges in the consistent formalism, was accomplished by implementing energy-dependent widths of nucleon resonances, which leads to a different choice of hadron form factor with much softer values of cutoff parameter for the resonant part. For a reliable description of electroproduction, the necessity of including longitudinal couplings of nucleon resonances to virtual photons was revealed. We present a new model whose free parameters were adjusted to photo- and electroproduction data and which provides a reliable overall description of experimental data in all kinematic regions. The majority of nucleon resonances chosen in this analysis coincide with those selected in our previous analysis and also in the Bayesian analysis with the Regge-plus-resonance model as the states contributing to this process with the highest probability.

## Key claim

The paper presents an upgraded isobar model (BS3) for kaon photo- and electroproduction on the proton that achieves a reliable overall description of experimental data, but the authors acknowledge that the model's success depends on the inclusion of energy-dependent resonance widths and longitudinal couplings of nucleon resonances to virtual photons, and that the selection of hyperon resonances remains ambiguous.

## Method

The authors constructed a single-channel isobar model using effective Lagrangians with tree-level Feynman diagrams for s-, t-, and u-channel exchanges of ground-state hadrons and their excited states, employing a consistent formalism for high-spin baryon resonances to ensure only physical degrees of freedom contribute. They implemented energy-dependent decay widths for nucleon resonances to approximately account for unitarity corrections, introduced electromagnetic form factors using the GKex(02S) parametrization for baryons and monopole forms for K* and K1 mesons, and added longitudinal couplings of virtual photons to nucleon resonances. Free parameters (coupling constants and cutoff parameters) were adjusted via least-squares fitting using MINUIT to 3554 data points (3383 photoproduction and 171 electroproduction) from multiple experiments.

## Key numbers

* The model (BS3) achieved a χ²/n.d.f. of 1.74 when fitting to both photo- and electroproduction data (3554 data points total), compared to 1.51 when fitting only to photoproduction data.
* The hadron form factor cutoff parameters were 1.235 GeV for background terms and 0.892 GeV for resonant terms, much softer than in the previous BS2 model.
* The electromagnetic form factor cutoff parameters for K* and K1 were 0.709 GeV and 1.503 GeV, respectively.
* The coupling constant for the S11(1535) resonance in the K+Λ channel was 0.227, consistent with the range 0.16–0.29 from an independent analysis by Mart (2013).
* The most important nucleon resonances for reducing χ² were P13(1900) (Δχ² = 826%) and D13(1875) (Δχ² = 844%), where Δχ² measures the percentage increase in χ²/n.d.f. when the resonance is omitted.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BS1 and BS2 models for kaon photoproduction (Skoupil and Bydžovský, 2016) by adding energy-dependent resonance widths, electromagnetic form factors, and longitudinal couplings to handle electroproduction data. The model overlaps with the Bayesian analysis using the Regge-plus-resonance model in identifying the most probable contributing nucleon resonances, and the S11(1535) coupling agrees with the isobar model analysis by Mart (2013). The results enable more reliable predictions for hypernucleus electroproduction calculations, though the authors note that the selection of hyperon resonances remains unsettled and that data at higher energies (W > 2.4 GeV) would require additional high-spin resonances.
