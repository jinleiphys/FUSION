# Time-Reversal Violating Schiff Moment of 225Ra

> We use the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method, allowing all symmetries to be broken, to calculate the time-reversal-violating nuclear Schiff moment (which induces atomic electric dipole moments) in the octupole-deformed nucleus 225Ra. Our calculation includes several effects neglected in earlier work, including self consistency and polarization of the core by the last nucleon. We confirm that the Schiff moment is large compared to those of reflection-symmetric nuclei, though ours is generally a few times smaller than recent estimates.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that the Schiff moment of 225Ra is large compared to reflection-symmetric nuclei, but find it is generally a few times smaller than recent estimates from the particle-rotor model, with the reduction attributed to core spin polarization and self-consistency effects neglected in earlier work.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations using the HFODD code, which allows simultaneous breaking of rotational, reflection, and time-reversal symmetries, to compute the intrinsic Schiff moment and the expectation value of the T-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction in 225Ra. They used four different Skyrme force parameterizations (SIII, SkM*, SLy4, and SkO'), with SkO' being the only one whose time-odd spin-density terms were adjusted to reproduce Gamow-Teller resonances. The laboratory Schiff moment was obtained by multiplying the intrinsic Schiff moment by the rigid-rotor factor J/(J+1) = 1/3 and the expectation value of the T-violating potential, evaluated in the zero-range approximation with exchange terms included.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic Schiff moment for SkO': 41.7 e fm³
- Intrinsic Schiff moment for SIII(0): 34.6 e fm³
- Intrinsic Schiff moment for SkM*(0): 46.6 e fm³
- Intrinsic Schiff moment for SLy4(0): 43.4 e fm³
- Intrinsic Schiff moment from Ref. Spevak (1997): 24 e fm³
- Octupole deformation β₃ for SkO' in 225Ra: 0.146
- Quadrupole deformation β₂ for SkO' in 225Ra: 0.190
- Energy denominator ΔE between parity doublet states: 55 keV
- Ground-state angular momentum of 225Ra: J = 1/2

## Context

This work extends the particle-rotor model calculations of Spevak (1997) and Auerbach (1996), which predicted large Schiff moments in octupole-deformed nuclei, by incorporating self-consistency and core polarization effects that were previously neglected. The authors note that Ref. Engel (1999) had suggested these many-body effects might reduce the enhancement, and the present calculation confirms that the Schiff moment is smaller than the earlier estimates, though still large compared to spherical nuclei like 199Hg. The results enable more reliable interpretation of ongoing and planned electric dipole moment experiments in 225Ra, such as those by Holt (2003), by providing improved theoretical limits on the relationship between atomic EDM measurements and fundamental T-violating couplings.
