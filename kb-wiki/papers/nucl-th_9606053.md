# The hadron-quark transition with a lattice of nonlocal confining solitons

> We use a lattice of nonlocal confining solitons to describe nuclear matter in the Wigner-Seitz approximation. The average density is varied by changing the size of the Wigner-Seitz cell. At sufficiently large density quark energy bands develop. The intersection of the filled valence band with the next empty band at a few times standard nuclear density signals a transition from a color insulator to a color conductor and is identified with the critical density for quark deconfinement.

# The hadron-quark transition with a lattice of nonlocal confining solitons

## Key claim
The intersection of the filled valence band with the next empty band at a few times standard nuclear density signals a transition from a color insulator to a color conductor, identified with the critical density for quark deconfinement. The authors note that the model does not include meson dressing and that the calculated root mean square radius should be compared with caution to the experimental proton charge radius.

## Method
The authors model nuclear matter as a lattice of nonlocal confining solitons in the Wigner-Seitz approximation, using the Global Color Model (GCM) hadronized from a truncation of QCD. They solve coupled Dirac and nonlinear Klein-Gordon equations iteratively for the quark and sigma fields with periodic boundary conditions on the Wigner-Seitz cell, varying the cell radius to change the average density. The bottom and top energies of the lowest quark energy bands are calculated, and the band crossing is identified with the deconfinement transition.

## Key numbers
- Critical density for deconfinement with parameter alpha=1.35 GeV: rho_c = (2.6 ± 0.2) rho_0, where rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3 is standard nuclear matter density.
- For alpha=1.25 GeV: rho_c/rho_0 = 2.2 ± 0.2; for alpha=1.45 GeV: rho_c/rho_0 = 3.1 ± 0.3.
- Root mean square radius of the isolated soliton: 0.67 fm for alpha=1.35 GeV (range 0.64-0.71 fm for alpha=1.25-1.45 GeV).
- Wigner-Seitz radius at minimum energy for alpha=1.35 GeV: R_min = 1.26 ± 0.02 fm.
- Critical Wigner-Seitz radius for alpha=1.35 GeV: R_c = 0.82 ± 0.02 fm.

## Context
This work extends earlier soliton matter studies by Banerjee, Glendenning, and Soni (1985) and Birse, Rehr, and Wilets (1988), using a model where meson fields and their self-interactions are generated entirely from the underlying quark-quark interaction rather than being put in by hand. The approach is based on the Global Color Model (GCM) developed by Cahill and Roberts (1985), which has been successful in describing low-energy QCD phenomena including chiral perturbation theory, meson form factors, and soliton descriptions of the nucleon. The model enables a unified description of both hadronic and quark phases using the same basic degrees of freedom, in contrast to the common practice of using separate hadronic and bag-model equations of state with Gibbs phase coexistence conditions.
