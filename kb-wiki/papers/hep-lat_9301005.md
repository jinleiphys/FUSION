# The Heavy Quark Self-Energy in Nonrelativistic Lattice QCD

> The heavy quark self-energy in nonrelativistic lattice QCD is calculated to $O(α_s)$ in perturbation theory. An action which includes all spin-independent relativistic corrections to order $v^2$, where $v$ is the typical heavy quark velocity, and all spin-dependent corrections to order $v^4$ is used. The standard Wilson action and an improved multi-plaquette action are used for the gluons. Results for the mass renormalization, wavefunction renormalization, and energy shift are given; tadpole contributions are found to be large. A tadpole improvement scheme in which all link variables are rescaled by a mean-field factor is also studied. The effectiveness of this scheme in offsetting the large tadpole contributions to the heavy quark renormalization parameters is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The heavy quark self-energy in nonrelativistic lattice QCD is calculated to $O(\alpha_s)$ in perturbation theory, but the perturbative expansion using the bare lattice coupling is a poor choice; much better series result when re-expressed in terms of a renormalized coupling defined from a physical quantity and running with the relevant length scale.

## Method

The self-energy was computed from one-loop diagrams (a quark-gluon vertex diagram and a tadpole diagram) using Feynman rules derived from the lattice NRQCD action that includes all spin-independent corrections to order $v^2$ and spin-dependent corrections to order $v^4$. The integrals were evaluated numerically by approximating four-dimensional momentum integrals with discrete sums, using a change of variables to improve convergence, and deforming the contour for the temporal momentum integral to avoid the quark propagator pole. Both the standard Wilson gluon action and an improved multi-plaquette gluon action were used, and a tadpole improvement scheme rescaling all link variables by a mean-field factor $u_0$ was also studied.

## Key numbers

- For bottomonium, $v_b^2 \sim 0.1$ and $M_b \sim 5$ GeV, requiring lattice spacing $a \ll 2.0$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- For charmonium, $v_c^2 \sim 0.3$ and $M_c \sim 1.7$ GeV, requiring $a \ll 3.4$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- At $\beta = 5.7$, the renormalized coupling squared $g_r^2(\pi/a) \approx 1.9$; at $\beta = 6.0$, $g_r^2(\pi/a) \approx 1.7$.
- The mean-field parameter $u_0$ is defined as $u_0 = \langle \frac{1}{3} \text{Tr} U_{\text{plaq}} \rangle^{1/4}$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Davies et al. (1992) on heavy quark renormalization in lattice NRQCD, providing the first perturbative calculation of the self-energy including spin-dependent corrections to order $v^4$. The results enable lattice simulations of heavy quarkonia (such as $\psi$ and $\Upsilon$ mesons) to determine the coupling coefficients $c_j$ and renormalization factors needed for matching to continuum QCD, and demonstrate that tadpole improvement (rescaling link variables by $u_0$) substantially reduces the large tadpole contributions to the renormalization parameters.
