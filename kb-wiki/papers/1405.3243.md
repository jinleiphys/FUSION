# Dynamical collisional energy loss and transport properties of on- and off-shell heavy quarks in vacuum and in the Quark Gluon Plasma

> In this study we evaluate the dynamical collisional energy loss of heavy quarks, their interaction rate as well as the different transport coefficients (drag and diffusion coefficients, $\hat{q}$, etc). We calculate these different quantities for i) perturbative partons (on-shell particles in the vacuum with fixed and running coupling) and ii) for dynamical quasi-particles (off-shell particles in the QGP medium at finite temperature $T$ with a running coupling in temperature as described by the dynamical quasi-particles model). We use the perturbative elastic $(q(g) Q \rightarrow q (g) Q)$ cross section for the first case, and the Infrared Enhanced Hard Thermal Loop cross sections for the second. The results obtained in this work demonstrate the effects of a finite parton mass and width on the heavy quark transport properties and provide the basic ingredients for an explicit study of the microscopic dynamics of heavy flavors in the QGP - as formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions - within transport approaches developed previously by the authors.

# Dynamical collisional energy loss and transport properties of on- and off-shell heavy quarks in vacuum and in the Quark Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the finite mass and width of partons (as described by the dynamical quasi-particle model) significantly affect heavy quark transport properties such as drag and diffusion coefficients, with the off-shell treatment yielding different results than on-shell perturbative calculations; however, these effects are model-dependent and the authors explicitly note that the derivation of explicit expressions for off-shell rates depends on the choice of the parton spectral function (Lorentzian-omega versus Breit-Wigner-mass form).

## Method

The authors evaluate the dynamical collisional energy loss, interaction rate, and transport coefficients (drag, diffusion, and $\hat{q}$) for heavy quarks using two approaches: (1) perturbative on-shell partons with fixed and running coupling, using elastic $q(g)Q \rightarrow q(g)Q$ cross sections, and (2) off-shell dynamical quasi-particles in the QGP at finite temperature using Infrared Enhanced Hard Thermal Loop (IEHTL) cross sections with running coupling in temperature as described by the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM). The off-shell calculations are performed using two spectral function forms (Lorentzian-omega and Breit-Wigner-mass), with the Breit-Wigner-mass form allowing factorization of the on-shell to off-shell transition.

## Key numbers

- The heavy quark degeneracy factor $g_Q = 6$ is used throughout the calculations
- The light quark degeneracy factor $g_p = 6$, massless gluon degeneracy $g_p = 16$, and massive gluon degeneracy $g_p = 24$ are specified
- The infrared regulator parameter $\kappa \approx 0.2$ is determined by requiring that the energy loss $dE/dx$ from the effective propagator reproduces the running coupling result in extended HTL calculations
- The Debye mass is parameterized as $m_D \approx \xi g_s T$ with $\xi = 1$ for the fixed coupling case ($\alpha = 0.3$)
- The effective gluon propagator uses a global $\mu^2 = \kappa \tilde{m}_D^2$ where $\tilde{m}_D^2 (T) = \frac{N_c}{3} \left(1 + \frac{N_f}{6} \right) 4 \pi \alpha (- \tilde{m}_D (T)) T^2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Berrehrah 2013) on elementary cross sections for heavy quark interactions with QGP constituents, now computing the resulting transport coefficients. It builds on the HTL-GA approach developed by Gossiaux and Aichelin (2008, 2009) and the DQPM framework by Cassing (2009) which reproduces lattice QCD results at finite temperature. The paper provides the necessary input for microscopic transport approaches to heavy flavor dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, enabling comparison between different model treatments (pQCD, HTL-GA, IEHTL, DpQCD) and with lattice QCD correlators. The results are intended to be embedded in models of QGP expansion, where different expansion scenarios can change $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ values by a factor of two (Gossiaux 2011).
