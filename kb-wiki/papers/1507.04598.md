# Some properties of convection in hybrid stars

> It is shown that the unusual thermodynamic properties of matter within the region of two-phase coexistence in hybrid stars result in a change of the standard condition for beginning of convection. In particular, the thermal flux transported by convection may be directed towards the stellar center. We discuss favorable circumstances leading to such an effect of "inverse convection" and its possible influence on the thermal evolution of hybrid stars.

# Some properties of convection in hybrid stars

## Key claim

Within the two-phase coexistence region of hybrid stars, the standard Schwarzschild criterion for convection is reversed: a positive entropy gradient (dS/dr > 0) triggers convective instability, and the convective heat flux can be directed inward toward the stellar center rather than outward. This "inverse convection" arises specifically because the derivative (∂P/∂T)_ρ becomes negative in the phase-coexistence region, contrary to the behavior in single-phase matter.

## Method

The authors derive the general relativistic Ledoux criterion for convection onset, showing that the sign of the factor (∂ε/∂S)_{P,Y} multiplying dS/dr reverses when (∂P/∂T)_S becomes negative, as occurs in the phase-coexistence region of a Maxwellian phase transition. They then compute the temperature profiles and the derivative (∂ε/∂S)_{P,Y} as functions of baryon density for a hybrid equation of state combining the STOS hadronic EOS (Shen 1998, 2011) with the quark bag model EOS (Sagert 2009, Fischer 2011), using a Gibbs (non-congruent) phase transition description with global charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: ρ_n ≈ 2.6 × 10^14 g/cm^3
- Quark bag constant used in calculations: B^{1/4} = 165 MeV
- Lepton fraction fixed for calculations: Y_L = 0.4
- The region of inverse convection is maximal for symmetric matter with Y_e = 0.5 and shrinks as Y_e decreases with increasing neutronization

## Context

This work extends prior observations by Yudin (2013), Hempel (2013), and Iosilevskiy (2014) that pressure can decrease with temperature at constant density in the phase-coexistence region of hybrid stars. It contradicts the standard Schwarzschild criterion for convection (Thorne 1966) which requires dS/dr < 0 for instability. The authors note that for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, convection retains its ordinary form, unlike the deconfinement transition studied here. The paper enables future investigation of how inverse convection might affect the thermal evolution of hybrid stars, the neutrino signal from proto-neutron stars (following Roberts 2012), and possibly the explosion dynamics of core-collapse supernovae if inverse convection appears in the early post-bounce phase.
