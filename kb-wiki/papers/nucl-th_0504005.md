# Influence of the Electronic Chaotic Motion on the Fusion Dynamics at Astrophysical Energies

> We perform semi-classical molecular dynamics simulations of screening by bound electrons in low energy nuclear reactions. In our simulations quantum effects corresponding to the Pauli and Heisenberg principle are enforced by constraints. In addition to the well known adiabatic and sudden limits, we propose a new "dissipative limit" which is expected to be important not only at high energies but in the extremely low energy region. The dissipative limit is associated with the chaotic behavior of the electronic motion. It affects also the magnitude of the enhancement factor. We discuss also numerical experiments using polarized targets. The derived enhancement factors in our simulation are in agreement with those extracted within the $R$-matrix approach.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new "dissipative limit" for electron screening in low-energy nuclear fusion, driven by chaotic electronic motion and electron ejection, which can produce enhancement factors smaller than the adiabatic limit and even cause hindrance (enhancement factor less than 1) in some cases.

## Method

The authors performed semi-classical constrained molecular dynamics (CoMD) simulations for the reactions D+d, D+D, ³He+d, ³He+D, ⁶Li+d, and ⁶Li+D. Quantum effects from the Pauli and Heisenberg principles were enforced via Lagrange multiplier constraints on phase-space distances, and the tunneling process was treated by switching to imaginary-time evolution when the collective momentum of the two nuclei becomes zero. Enhancement factors were computed as the ratio of the penetrability with electrons to the penetrability of the bare Coulomb barrier, averaged over an ensemble of initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Screening potential for D+d: U_e = 15.9 ± 2.0 eV (from fit of average enhancement factors)
- Screening potential for D+D: U_e = 21.6 ± 0.3 eV (from fit of average enhancement factors)
- Screening potential for ³He+d: U_e = 82.4 ± 1.9 eV (from fit of average enhancement factors)
- Screening potential for ³He+D: U_e = 102.8 ± 3.0 eV (from fit of average enhancement factors)
- Screening potential for ⁶Li+d: U_e = 152.0 ± 9.9 eV (from fit of average enhancement factors)
- Screening potential for ⁶Li+D: U_e = 214.4 ± 18.5 eV (from fit of average enhancement factors)
- Adiabatic limit screening potentials: 22.0 eV for D+d, 37.1 eV for D+D, 119 eV for ³He+d, 110 eV for ³He+D, 186 eV for ⁶Li+d (from references cited in the paper)
- For D+d at E_cm = 0.25 keV, the distribution of enhancement factors peaks between the sudden and dissipative limits, rather than near the adiabatic limit

## Context

This work extends prior time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) studies of electron screening (e.g., by S. Kimura et al. and N. Takigawa et al.) which were limited to systems with few bound electrons, to reactions with many bound electrons. The paper addresses the long-standing puzzle that experimentally extracted screening potentials often exceed the adiabatic limit (Rolfs 1995) or are smaller than the sudden limit, and it provides a dynamical explanation through chaotic electron motion and electron ejection. The results enable future experiments using polarized targets and coincidence measurements of fusion residues with energetic electrons, and the derived enhancement factors agree with those extracted using the R-matrix approach (Barker).
