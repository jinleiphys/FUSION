# On final conditions in high energy heavy ion collisions

> Motivated by the recent experimental observations, we discuss the freeze-out properties of the fireball created in central heavy ion collisions. We find that the freeze-out conditions, like temperature, velocity gradient near center of the fireball, are similar for different colliding systems and beam energies. This means that the transverse flow is stronger in the collisions of heavy nuclei than that of the light ones.

## Key claim

The freeze-out temperature and the transverse velocity gradient near the center of the fireball are approximately constant across different colliding systems and beam energies above 10 A·GeV, implying that the stronger transverse flow observed in heavier nuclei arises from their larger freeze-out size rather than from different local freeze-out conditions.

## Method

The authors used a thermal model with a Bjorken-like longitudinal expansion and a parameterized transverse velocity profile, where the transverse flow intensity is characterized by a parameter α related to the velocity gradient at the fireball center. They fitted the experimentally measured slope parameters (effective temperatures) of transverse mass spectra for pions, kaons, and protons as a function of particle mass, using data from NA44 and other experiments, to extract the intrinsic freeze-out temperature T and the flow intensity α. The transverse sideward radius R_S from two-particle correlation measurements was then used to evaluate the Gaussian-like freeze-out radius and the velocity gradient at the center.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb central collisions, α = 6.57 and the average transverse velocity ⟨v_T⟩ = 0.49c (at m_T ≈ 0.5 GeV).
- For S+S central collisions, the average transverse velocity ⟨v_T⟩ = 0.33c.
- The freeze-out temperature T is approximately constant for different colliding systems at beam energies ≥ 10 A·GeV, with no specific numerical value given in the text.
- The transverse velocity gradient at the center v_T'(0) is also approximately constant across systems, with no specific numerical value given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the thermal and hydrodynamic analysis of freeze-out conditions in heavy-ion collisions, building on the Bjorken model (Bjorken, 1983) and Landau hydrodynamics (Landau, 1953), as well as earlier work by Sinyukov et al. (1994, 1996) on transverse flow parameterization. The finding of a constant freeze-out temperature supports the limiting temperature hypothesis of Hagedorn (1965) and Stöcker (1986), while the constant velocity gradient is a new result. The linear relationship between average transverse velocity and freeze-out radius enables future extraction of collective flow from slope parameter data, and the approach can be extended to study deviations for particles like hyperons that may freeze out earlier, as observed by WA97 (1997) and NA49 (1997).
