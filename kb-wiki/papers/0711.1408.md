# Particle-number conservation in static-path approximation for thermal superfluid systems

> By applying particle-number projection to the static-path approximation (SPA), the heat capacity and the breakdown of pairing correlations are investigated in the thermally excited, superfluid systems 172Yb, 94Mo, and 56Fe. For the heavy nucleus 172Yb, the heat capacities in both the SPA and the number-projected SPA (NPSPA) exhibit an S shape; the difference between the SPA and NPSPA heat-capacity curves is not very large and the particle-number projection thereby enhances the S shape already seen in the SPA. The temperature at which the S-shape of heat capacity curve occurs parallels the temperature of the breakdown of pairing correlations as indicated by the effective pairing gap. However, for the comparatively lighter nuclei 94Mo and 56Fe, the SPA does not produce an S-shaped heat capacity on its own; only after particle-number projection the S shape appears in the heat-capacity curve. For 94Mo, we compare the NPSPA result with thermal odd-even mass differences, which are regarded as a direct measure of the pairing gap.

## Key claim
Particle-number projection within the static-path approximation (SPA) enhances or creates the S-shaped heat capacity curve in thermally excited superfluid nuclei, but the S shape is not necessarily a unique signature of a pairing phase transition; it may also arise from particle-number conservation effects, especially in lighter nuclei where the SPA alone does not produce it.

## Method
The authors applied exact particle-number projection to the static-path approximation (NPSPA) using a monopole pairing Hamiltonian and thermo field dynamics (TFD) formalism. They evaluated the canonical partition function via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, integrated over the pairing gap, and used an M-point formula for the number projection. Heat capacities were computed as the temperature derivative of the thermal energy for three nuclei: 172Yb, 94Mo, and 56Fe, using single-particle energies from a deformed Woods-Saxon potential.

## Key numbers
- For 172Yb, the S-shaped heat capacity occurs around T = 0.5 MeV, with the number projection decreasing the SPA heat capacity for T < 0.35 MeV and increasing it in the region 0.35 MeV < T < 0.7 MeV.
- For 94Mo, the inflection point of the effective pairing gap is 0.65 MeV, close to the local maximum of the heat capacity curve.
- For 56Fe, the inflection point of the effective pairing gap is 0.9 MeV, close to the local maximum of the heat capacity curve.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters for 172Yb include V0 = 51.0 MeV, a = 0.67 fm, kappa = 0.67, lambda = 22.0, and r0 = 1.27 fm.

## Context
This work extends the static-path approximation (SPA) by incorporating exact particle-number projection, building on prior studies of thermal pairing in finite Fermi systems by Rossignoli (2000), Rombouts (2002), Liu (2001), and Alhassid (2000). It contrasts with BCS-based approaches such as the variation-after-projection (VAP) method of Essebag (1996) and Nakada (2003), and the projection-after-variation (PAV) method of Esashika (2006), which can produce S-shaped heat capacities even without a pairing phase transition. The results enable future application of combined particle-number and angular-momentum projection to study spin distributions in nuclear level densities, as suggested by Tanabe (2005).
