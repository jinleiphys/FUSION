# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron-alpha scattering

> We describe a new method to treat low-energy scattering problems in few-nucleon systems, and we apply it to the five-body case of neutron-alpha scattering. The method allows precise calculations of low-lying resonances and their widths. We find that a good three-nucleon interaction is crucial to obtain an accurate description of neutron-alpha scattering.

# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron-alpha scattering

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a new quantum Monte Carlo method for low-energy scattering, applied to the five-body neutron-alpha system, can reproduce experimental phase shifts and resonance parameters, but only when a sufficiently sophisticated three-nucleon interaction (the Illinois-2 model) is included; the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential alone, or with the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, yields insufficient spin-orbit splitting of the 3/2- and 1/2- resonances.

## Method
The authors use variational Monte Carlo (VMC) to construct trial wave functions that satisfy a logarithmic derivative boundary condition at a fixed surface radius R0 = 9 fm, then evolve these states in imaginary time using Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC). The boundary condition is enforced by mapping contributions from outside the boundary to image points inside, allowing a fixed R0 independent of scattering energy. Phase shifts are extracted from the asymptotic form of the wave function using the computed energies and the imposed logarithmic derivative.

## Key numbers
- The 3/2- resonance pole for AV18+IL2 is at 0.83 - 0.35i MeV, compared with 0.798 - 0.324i MeV from R-matrix analysis of data.
- The 1/2- resonance pole for AV18+IL2 is at 2.3 - 2.6i MeV, compared with 2.07 - 2.79i MeV from data.
- The 3/2- pole for AV18 alone is at 1.19 - 0.77i MeV; for AV18+UIX it is at 1.39 - 0.75i MeV.
- The 1/2- pole for AV18 alone is at 1.7 - 2.2i MeV; for AV18+UIX it is at 2.4 - 2.5i MeV.
- All three potentials give a scattering length of 2.4 fm, compared with the experimental value of 2.46 fm.
- The GFMC energy depends on R0 at the level of about 100 keV when varying from 7 fm to 9 fm, with no further change from 9 fm to 10 fm; the total energy is approximately -28 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier quantum Monte Carlo bound-state calculations of light nuclei by Pieper, Wiringa, and collaborators (2001, 2002), which had treated narrow resonances as bound states but could not address broad resonances or non-resonant scattering. The method improves on earlier nodal boundary condition approaches used by Carlson, Pandharipande, and others (1984, 1987, 1991), which required different R0 for each energy. The results show that scattering calculations can provide sensitive tests of three-nucleon interaction models, complementing bound-state level data. The method can be extended to include Coulomb interactions, nucleus-nucleus scattering, and multi-channel problems.
