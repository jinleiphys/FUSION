# New developments in fission studies within the time-dependent density functional theory framework

> We have extended significantly the microscopic description of the fission process by examining a larger set of observables. We extract neutron and proton numbers of fission fragments, their spins and fission fragment relative orbital angular momentum and their correlations, investigate neutrons emitted at or shortly after scission, excitation energy sharing mechanism, total kinetic energy of fission fragments, and the entanglement entropy. I will present a short overview of our simulations obtained with two independent nuclear energy density functionals.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) provides a fully microscopic description of fission dynamics that predicts total kinetic energy (TKE), total excitation energy (TXE), and average fission fragment proton and neutron numbers with roughly 1% accuracy, but only for the dynamics from the outer fission barrier until the fragments are well separated; the formation of the compound nucleus and its evolution up to the outer barrier remains too long to simulate numerically.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) simulations on a spatial cartesian lattice with a 1 fm lattice constant, using two independent nuclear energy density functionals (SkM* and SeaLL1) as the only input besides the compound nucleus proton and neutron numbers, excitation energy, and initial spin. From the time evolution they extracted fission fragment neutron and proton numbers, intrinsic spins, relative orbital angular momentum, excitation energies, total kinetic energy, neutron emission patterns, and the orbital entanglement entropy by diagonalizing the one-body density matrix to obtain canonical occupation probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Average TKE of fission fragments is approximately 170-175 MeV for induced fission of 235U(n_th,f)
- Mass difference between compound nucleus and final products is approximately 210 MeV, leaving roughly 40 MeV of excitation energy to share between fragments
- Average intrinsic spin of light fission fragment: S^L = 0.0168 E_n' + 9.197 (in units of hbar) for 239Pu(n,f) with SkM* NEDF
- Average intrinsic spin of heavy fission fragment: S^H = 0.0732 E_n' + 4.933 (in units of hbar) for 239Pu(n,f) with SkM* NEDF
- Light fragment excitation energy: E^L_int = 0.4505 E_n' + 13.25 MeV for 239Pu(n,f)
- Heavy fragment excitation energy: E^H_int = 0.5676 E_n' + 13.40 MeV for 239Pu(n,f)
- Collective flow energy during descent from outer barrier is only 1-2 MeV, despite potential energy surface gain of approximately 20 MeV
- Time from outer barrier to scission is approximately 1500 fm/c
- Period of rotation of an emerging fragment with spin 10 hbar is approximately 3400 fm/c
- Momentum cutoff from lattice spacing: p_cut = hbar*pi/1 fm approximately 600 MeV/c
- Entanglement entropy difference between particle-projected and unprojected wave functions shows S(t) increases after scission

## Context

This work extends the microscopic description of fission beyond earlier mean-field approaches by including pairing correlations through the TDSLDA framework, which is an extension of the Kohn-Sham LDA to superfluid systems. The results contradict the prevailing adiabatic assumption used in collective potential energy surface models by Ring (2004), Schunck (2016), and Pomorski (2012), demonstrating instead that large amplitude collective motion is strongly damped. The predictions for fragment spin correlations disagree with the classical FREYA model by Vogt (2021) and Randrup (2021), and the microscopic spin distributions can be used as input for post-processing codes such as CGMF by Becker (2013), FREYA by Vogt (2009), and FIFRELIN by Litaize (2012) to improve neutron multiplicity and gamma spectra predictions.
