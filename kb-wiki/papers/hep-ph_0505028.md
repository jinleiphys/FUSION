# Estimate of neutrino masses from Koide's relation

> We apply Koide's mass relation of charged leptons to neutrinos and quarks, with both the normal and inverted mass schemes of neutrinos discussed. We introduce the parameters $k_ν$, $k_u$ and $k_d$ to describe the deviations of neutrinos and quarks from Koide's relation, and suggest a quark-lepton complementarity of masses such as $ k_{l}+k_{d} \approx k_ν+k_{u} \approx 2$. The masses of neutrinos are determined from the improved relation, and they are strongly hierarchical (with the different orders of magnitude of $10^{-5} eV$, $10^{-3} eV$, and $10^{-2} eV$).

## Key claim

The authors propose that Koide's relation, which holds with high precision for charged leptons, can be extended to neutrinos and quarks by introducing deviation parameters \(k_{\nu}\), \(k_u\), and \(k_d\). They then use a conjectured quark-lepton complementarity of masses (\(k_{l}+k_{d} \approx k_{\nu}+k_{u} \approx 2\)) to estimate the absolute neutrino masses, noting that the results are only primary estimates due to current experimental inaccuracies.

## Method

The authors define a parameter \(k_f = (m_1+m_2+m_3) / [\frac{2}{3}(\sqrt{m_1}+\sqrt{m_2}+\sqrt{m_3})^2]\) for each fermion family. They compute the allowed ranges of \(k_{\nu}\) for both normal and inverted neutrino mass schemes using the best-fit mass-squared differences from oscillation experiments (\(\Delta m_{\mathrm{sol}}^2 = 6.9\times10^{-5}~\mathrm{eV}^2\), \(\Delta m_{\mathrm{atm}}^2 = 2.6\times10^{-3}~\mathrm{eV}^2\)). Similarly, they compute \(k_u\) and \(k_d\) for quarks using PDG mass ranges. They then impose the complementarity condition \(k_{\nu}+k_{u}\approx 2\) to fix \(k_{\nu}\approx 0.75\) and solve for the neutrino mass eigenstates.

## Key numbers

*   For the normal mass scheme with \(k_{\nu}=0.75\), the neutrino mass eigenstates are \(m_1 = 1.0 \times 10^{-5}~\mathrm{eV}\), \(m_2 = 8.4 \times 10^{-3}~\mathrm{eV}\), and \(m_3 = 0.05~\mathrm{eV}\).
*   The effective flavor eigenstate masses are \(\langle m\rangle_{e} = 6.0 \times 10^{-3}~\mathrm{eV}\), \(\langle m\rangle_{\mu} = 3.6 \times 10^{-2}~\mathrm{eV}\), and \(\langle m\rangle_{\tau} = 3.6 \times 10^{-2}~\mathrm{eV}\).
*   The sum of neutrino mass eigenstates is \(\sum m_i = 0.058~\mathrm{eV}\).
*   The allowed range for the neutrino deviation parameter is \(0.50 < k_{\nu} < 0.85\) for both mass schemes.
*   The allowed range for the u-type quark deviation parameter is \(1.1 < k_u < 1.4\), and for d-type quarks it is \(0.9 < k_d < 1.2\).

## Context

This work extends Koide's relation (Koide, 1981; Koide, 1983), originally formulated for charged leptons, to neutrinos and quarks. The authors note that their results for \(m_2\) and \(m_3\) are strongly consistent with those obtained by Koide (2004) using a different interpretation. The estimated neutrino masses are consistent with current experimental upper bounds from tritium beta decay, pion decay, tau decay, and cosmological observations (WMAP and 2dF Galaxy Redshift Survey). The work enables future tests of the quark-lepton mass complementarity hypothesis with more precise neutrino and quark mass data.
