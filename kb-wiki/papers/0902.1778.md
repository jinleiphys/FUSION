# How many phases meet at the chiral critical point?

> We explore the phase diagram of NJL-type models near the chiral critical point allowing for phases with spatially inhomogeneous chiral condensates. In the chiral limit it turns out that the region in the mean-field phase diagram where those phases are energetically preferred very generically reaches out to the chiral critical point. The preferred inhomogeneous ground state in this vicinity possibly resembles a lattice of domain wall solitons. This raises the question of their relevance for the phase diagram of QCD.

## Key claim

In the mean-field phase diagram of NJL-type models in the chiral limit, the region where spatially inhomogeneous chiral condensates are energetically preferred very generically reaches out to the chiral critical point, replacing the expected first-order transition between homogeneous broken and unbroken phases by two second-order transitions.

## Method

The author performs a generalized Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion of the mean-field thermodynamic potential for NJL-type models, allowing for spatially varying order parameters. The expansion is carried out to sixth order in the order parameter and second order in gradients, with the coefficients evaluated using a regularization scheme where total derivative terms vanish. The resulting GL functional is then minimized for one-dimensional modulations of the order parameter, using known solutions expressed in terms of elliptic Jacobi sn-functions.

## Key numbers

- The second-order phase transition from homogeneous to inhomogeneous phase occurs at $\alpha_4 = -\sqrt{\frac{36}{5}\alpha_2 \alpha_6}$, where $\alpha_2$, $\alpha_4$, $\alpha_6$ are the GL coefficients.
- The second-order phase transition from the inhomogeneous to the unbroken phase occurs at $\alpha_4 = -\sqrt{\frac{8}{3}\alpha_2 \alpha_6}$, with modulation wavevector $|{\bf{q}}| = \sqrt{-\frac{3\alpha_4}{2\alpha_6}}$.
- For the homogeneous first-order transition (when restricting to homogeneous phases only), the transition occurs at $\alpha_4 = -\sqrt{\frac{16}{3}\alpha_2 \alpha_6}$, with the broken solution having $M_0^2 = -\frac{3}{4}\frac{\alpha_4}{\alpha_6}$.

## Context

This work extends the known analytical results from the one-dimensional Gross-Neveu model (Schnetz 2004, Thies 2006) to NJL-type models in three spatial dimensions, showing that the ratios of GL coefficients within each order are identical to those in the one-dimensional case. The finding contrasts with the situation in superconductors (Buzdin 1997) where the underlying particle-particle scattering dynamics gives different coefficient ratios. The results suggest that inhomogeneous chiral phases may be relevant near the QCD critical point, going beyond previous studies of chiral crystalline phases at vanishing temperatures (Deryagin 1992, Rapp 2000) where modulations were not small.
