# Nuclear polarization effects in atoms and ions

> In heavy atoms and ions, nuclear structure effects are significantly enhanced due to the overlap of the electron wave functions with the nucleus. This overlap rapidly increases with the nuclear charge $Z$. We study the energy level shifts induced by the electric dipole and electric quadrupole nuclear polarization effects in atoms and ions with $Z \geq 20$. The electric dipole polarization effect is enhanced by the nuclear giant dipole resonance. The electric quadrupole polarization effect is enhanced because the electrons in a heavy atom or ion move faster than the rotation of the deformed nucleus, thus experiencing significant corrections to the conventional approximation in which they `see' an averaged nuclear charge density. The electric nuclear polarization effects are computed numerically for $1s$, $2s$, $2p_{1/2}$ and high $ns$ electrons. The results are fitted with elementary functions of nuclear parameters (nuclear charge, mass number, nuclear radius and deformation). We construct an effective potential which models the energy level shifts due to nuclear polarization. This effective potential, when added to the nuclear Coulomb interaction, may be used to find energy level shifts in multi-electron ions, atoms and molecules. The fitting functions and effective potentials of the nuclear polarization effects are important for the studies of isotope shifts and nonlinearity in the King plot which are now used to search for new interactions and particles.

## Key claim

The paper provides numerical calculations and fitting formulas for energy level shifts due to electric dipole and electric quadrupole nuclear polarization in atoms and ions with Z >= 20, but the tensor nuclear polarizability contribution to hyperfine splitting is found to be nearly four orders of magnitude smaller than the quadrupole nuclear moment effect and may be neglected in many cases.

## Method

The authors computed energy level shifts for 1s, 2s, 2p1/2 and high ns electrons in hydrogen-like ions using the two-photon exchange formalism from QED, considering contributions from nuclear giant dipole resonance (E1) transitions and nuclear rotational (E2) transitions separately. They numerically evaluated the radial integrals and continuum energy integrals using Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size, then fitted the results with elementary functions of nuclear charge Z, mass number A, nuclear radius R, and deformation parameter beta2. An effective potential was constructed to model these shifts for use in multi-electron systems.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear polarization formalism developed by Plunien (1989, 1991, 1995) and Nefiodov (1996) for hydrogen-like ions to provide simple interpolation formulas applicable across all medium and heavy nuclei. The results are important for studies of isotope shifts and nonlinearity in the King plot, which are used to search for new interactions and particles beyond the Standard Model as discussed by Flambaum (2018), Berengut (2018, 2020), and Yerokhin (2020). The effective potentials constructed here enable calculation of nuclear polarization contributions in multi-electron atoms and molecules, which must be subtracted from experimental data to identify possible new physics signals.
