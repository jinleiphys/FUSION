# Radiative and Collisional Energy Loss of Heavy Quarks in Deconfined Matter

> We extend our recently advanced model on collisional energy loss of heavy quarks in a quark gluon plasma (QGP) by including radiative energy loss. We discuss the approach and present calculations for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76 TeV$. The transverse momentum spectra, RAA, and the elliptic flow $v_2$ of heavy quarks have been obtained using the model of Kolb and Heinz for the hydrodynamical expansion of the plasma.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously developed model of collisional energy loss for heavy quarks in a quark-gluon plasma by including radiative energy loss, and find that perturbative QCD calculations with a running coupling constant and an infrared regulator based on hard thermal loop calculations can reproduce LHC data on heavy meson suppression and elliptic flow, provided a K-factor of 0.6 is applied to all cross sections.

## Method

The authors compute the five matrix elements contributing to gluon bremsstrahlung in scalar QCD, regroup them into three gauge-invariant combinations (two QED-like and one genuine QCD diagram), and evaluate them in light-cone gauge keeping only the leading term in sqrt(s). The resulting squared matrix element factorizes into an elastic cross section term and a gluon distribution function, which is then embedded into the Kolb-Heinz hydrodynamical model for plasma expansion, with the charged particle multiplicity adjusted to the LHC value of dN/dy = 1600.

## Key numbers

* For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, the model uses a K-factor of 0.6 for combined elastic and radiative cross sections, compared to a K-factor of 2 for elastic-only collisions.
* The charged particle multiplicity at LHC is set to dN/dy = 1600.
* For mesons with p_t > 6 GeV, the model shows good agreement with Alice data on R_AA as a function of centrality.
* The color factor for the QCD diagram is D^{QCD} = 2/3.
* Below 40% centrality, R_AA does not change substantially.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier collisional energy loss model (Gossiaux 2008, 2009) by adding radiative energy loss, following earlier perturbative QCD approaches that required large K-factors (Moore 2004, Svetitsky 1987) to match data. The model uses the hydrodynamical expansion approach of Kolb and Heinz (2003), which described midrapidity light mesons at RHIC. The results enable future experimental verification through predicted differences in v_2 between D-mesons and B-mesons, and an increase of R_AA in central collisions for p_t above 15 GeV.
