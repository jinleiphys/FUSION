# Hypothesis of quark binding by condensation of gluons in hadrons

> Hypothesis of quark binding through condensation of gluons inside hadrons is formulated in the context of a renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) in the light-front (LF) Hamiltonian approach to QCD. At the momentum scales of relative motion of hadronic constituents that are comparable with Lambda_QCD, the hypothetical boost-invariant constituent dynamics is identified using gauge symmetry. The resulting picture of mesons and baryons closely resembles constituent quark models with harmonic oscillator potentials, shares some features of AdS/QCD, and can be systematically studied using RGPEP in QCD.

# Hypothesis of quark binding by condensation of gluons in hadrons

## Key claim

The paper hypothesizes that quark binding in hadrons arises from gluon condensation inside hadrons rather than from a nontrivial QCD vacuum, and that this mechanism can be formulated using the renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) in light-front Hamiltonian QCD. The claim is conditional on the RGPEP framework producing effective Hamiltonians at momentum scales comparable to Λ_QCD that yield harmonic oscillator potentials resembling constituent quark models.

## Method

The author applies the RGPEP to light-front QCD, constructing a unitary transformation between bare canonical quark and gluon operators and effective particle operators parameterized by size s = 1/λ, where λ is a momentum cutoff. Gauge symmetry is used to identify the structure of effective Hamiltonians for mesons and baryons at scales s ≲ s_c = 1/Λ_QCD, with the gluon field strength expectation value in the hadronic gluon component (rather than the vacuum) generating harmonic oscillator potentials through minimal coupling in a non-relativistic expansion. The resulting mass-squared operators for mesons and baryons are expressed in terms of relative momenta and the gluon condensate parameter φ_G².

## Key numbers

- Meson harmonic oscillator frequency: ω_M = π φ_G / (3m), where m is the constituent quark mass and φ_G² is the gluon condensate parameter
- Baryon harmonic oscillator frequency: ω_B = √(5/8) ω_M, giving ω_B/ω_M ≈ 0.791
- Ratio of soft-wall parameters in AdS/QCD: κ_M/κ_B ∼ 1.15 ± 0.5 (phenomenological), compared to the theoretical result (8/5)^(1/4) ∼ 1.125
- The gluon condensate parameter φ_G² is identified numerically with the vacuum gluon condensate parameter ⟨Ω| (α_s/π) G^{μνc} G_{μν}^c |Ω⟩/⟨Ω|Ω⟩ from QCD sum rules (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov 1979)

## Context

This work extends the RGPEP approach developed by Glazek (2011) and earlier similarity renormalization group work by Wilson (1994) to address the long-standing problem of vacuum structure in light-front QCD. The resulting harmonic oscillator potentials match phenomenologically accurate constituent quark models (e.g., those cited as CQM1, CQM2, CQM3) and share features with the soft-wall AdS/QCD models of Brodsky and de Teramond (2006-2008). The paper enables a systematic framework for connecting constituent quark model descriptions of hadrons with parton distribution functions and hard scattering processes through scale-dependent RGPEP transformations, and suggests that gluon saturation mechanisms in QCD may be studied using the same tools.
