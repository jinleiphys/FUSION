# Realtime dynamics of hyperon spin correlations from string fragmentation in a deformed four-flavor Schwinger model

> Self-polarizing weak decays of $Λ$-hyperons provide unique insight into the role of entanglement in the fragmentation of QCD strings through measurements of the spin correlations of $Λ{\bar Λ}$-pairs produced in collider experiments. The simplest quantum field theory representing the underlying parton dynamics is the four-flavor massive Schwinger model plus an effective spin-flip term, where the flavors are mapped to light (up/down) and heavy (strange) quarks and their spins. This construction provides a novel way to explore hyperon spin-correlations in 1+1-dimensions. We investigate the evolution of these correlations for different string configurations that are sensitive to the rich structure of the model Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a deformed four-flavor massive Schwinger model, augmented with an effective spin-flip Hamiltonian, can serve as a minimal 1+1-dimensional quantum field theory to model the real-time dynamics of hyperon spin correlations from QCD string fragmentation, but only under the simplifying assumption that the strange quark spin is a proxy for the Lambda hyperon spin.

## Method

The authors map the four-flavor massive Schwinger model onto a spin chain via a multiflavor Jordan-Wigner transformation, with the four flavors reinterpreted as two species (light and heavy) each with two spin states. They simulate the real-time evolution of an expanding QCD string, created by a separating quark-antiquark pair, using exact diagonalization for longer time evolution on small lattices (N=24 sites) and tensor network methods (DMRG and TDVP) for shorter time evolution on larger lattices (N=40 sites), computing local charge densities, electric fields, and heavy-fermion spin correlators.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing parameters: \(a \cdot g = 2\), \(a \cdot m_l = 0.5\), \(a \cdot m_h = 7.5\), chosen such that the mass ratio \(m_h/m_l = 15\) is of order \(m_s/(m_u+m_d)\).
- Spin coupling parameters explored: \(a \cdot g_{ll} = 0, 4\) and \(a \cdot g_{lh} = 0, 2\).
- System sizes: N=24 sites (6 staggered sites, 4 flavors each) for exact diagonalization; N=40 sites for tensor network simulations.
- Evolution time for tensor network simulations: \(t = a\) (one lattice time step).
- External field charge: \(Q = 1\), with the field extending between lattice sites 9 and 32 for the static case.

## Context

This work extends the static string configuration study of Gong (2021) by introducing a dynamical 1+1-dimensional quantum field theory that models the real-time production and evolution of light and heavy quark flavors in a QCD string for the first time. It provides a framework that could enable first-principles quantum simulations of hadronization dynamics, complementing semi-classical approaches like the Lund string model implemented in PYTHIA (Sjostrand, 2014), and is motivated by upcoming measurements of hyperon spin correlations at the future Electron Ion Collider (Accardi, 2012).
