# Heavy Exotic Molecules

> We briefly review the formation of pion-mediated heavy-light exotic molecules with both charm and bottom, under the general strictures of chiral and heavy quark symmetries. The charm isosinglet exotic molecules with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ binds, which we identify as the reported neutral $X(3872)$. The bottom isotriplet exotic with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ binds, and is identified as a mixed state of the reported charged exotics $Z^+_b(10610)$ and $Z^+_b(10650)$. The bound bottom isosinglet molecule with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ is a possible neutral $X_b(10532)$ to be observed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that pion-mediated heavy-light exotic molecules with both charm and bottom can form bound states under chiral and heavy quark symmetries, but the binding depends critically on the isospin channel and the cutoff parameter Lambda (set to 1 GeV), with the coupling between the (0-,1-) and (0+,1+) multiplets causing molecules in the former to bind about twice more while unbinding those in the latter.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-relativistic Hamiltonian for heavy-light meson molecules using a leading-order effective Lagrangian that incorporates chiral and heavy quark symmetries for the (0-,1-) and (0+,1+) multiplets, with interactions mediated by one-pion exchange regulated by a monopole form factor with cutoff Lambda = 1 GeV and coupling g_H = 0.6. They solved the coupled-channel bound state problem for J=1 systems by projecting the wavefunctions onto the appropriate tensor spherical harmonics for each J^PC sector, including D-wave admixtures analogous to the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- Charm isosinglet molecule with J^PC = 1++: binding energy E = 3.867 GeV, identified as X(3872)
- Bottom isosinglet molecule with J^PC = 1++: binding energy E = 10.532 GeV, identified as candidate X_b(10532)
- Bottom isotriplet molecule with J^PC = 1+ -: binding energy E = 10.592 GeV, identified as mixed state of Z_b+(10610) and Z_b+(10650)
- One-pion exchange cutoff Lambda = 1 GeV, coupling g_H = 0.6
- Isospin factor C = +1/4 for I=1 (isotriplet) and C = -3/4 for I=0 (isosinglet)

## Context

This work extends earlier molecular estimates by Tornqvist (1991, 1994) and others (Karliner and Lipkin, 2008; Close and Thomas, 2008) by incorporating the strictures of chiral and heavy quark symmetry and retaining most coupled channels between the (0-,1-) multiplet and its chiral partner (0+,1+). The analysis complements the authors' recent work (Liu and Zahed, 2016) and enables predictions for the yet-unobserved X_b(10532) as the bottom analogue of X(3872), while suggesting that the reported Z_c(3900)+- is at best a threshold enhancement rather than a true bound state.
