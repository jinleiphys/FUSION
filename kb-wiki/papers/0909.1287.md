# Time Modulation of Orbital Electron Capture Decays of H-like Heavy Ions

> According to experimental data at GSI, the rates of the number of daughter ions, produced by the nuclear K-shell electron capture (EC) decays of the H-like 140Pr58+, 142Pm60+ and 122I52+ ions, are modulated in time with periods T_{EC} of the order of a few seconds, obeying an A-scaling T_{EC} = A/20 s, where A is the mass number of the mother nuclei, and with amplitudes a^{EC}_d ~ 0.21. In turn, the positron decay mode of the H-like 142Pm60+ ions showed no time modulation of the decay rates. As has been shown in Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 062502 (2009) and Phys. Rev. Lett. 101, 182501 (2008), these data can be explained by the interference of two massive neutrino mass-eigenstates. In this letter we give a reply on the comments on our paper Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 062502 (2009) by A. Gal (arXiv:0809.1213v2).

## Key claim

The time modulation of orbital electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions observed at GSI can be explained by the interference of two massive neutrino mass-eigenstates, but only if one introduces arbitrary phases for neutrinos of different lepton flavors and defines the transition amplitude as a coherent superposition of amplitudes for all neutrino flavors, contrary to the claim by Gal that such interference terms cannot appear.

## Method

The authors calculate the amplitude for electron capture decay of H-like heavy ions as a coherent superposition of amplitudes for decay channels producing different massive neutrino mass-eigenstates, using the standard weak interaction Hamiltonian with Gamow-Teller nuclear matrix elements. They show that the energy and momentum uncertainties introduced by the time-differential detection of daughter ions (with time resolution τ_d ≈ 0.32 s) make the decay channels into different neutrino mass-eigenstates experimentally indistinguishable, allowing interference. To counter Gal's argument that summing over all neutrino flavors eliminates interference, the authors introduce arbitrary phases φ_α for each flavor neutrino and define the total transition amplitude as a coherent superposition over flavors, then constrain these phases to reproduce the observed decay rate and modulation amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Periods of time modulation obey T_EC = A/20 s, where A is the mass number of the mother nucleus
- Modulation amplitude for all observed decays: a_EC ≈ 0.21
- For 140Pr58+ EC decay: Q-value Q_EC = 3348(6) keV
- Time resolution of daughter ion detection: τ_d ≈ 0.32 s
- Lorentz factor of H-like mother ions: γ = 1.432
- Energy uncertainty from time resolution: δE_d ≈ 1.85 × 10^(-14) eV
- Momentum uncertainty for 140Pr daughter: |δq_d| ≈ 7.21 × 10^(-10) eV
- Neutrino mass-squared difference used: Δm²_21 = 2.19 × 10^(-4) eV²
- Frequency difference for two neutrino mass-eigenstates in 140Pr decay: ω_21 = 8.40 × 10^(-16) eV
- Momentum difference for neutrinos: |k_21| = 3.27 × 10^(-11) eV
- Mixing angle θ_12 = 34°
- Phase differences solving constraints: φ_μe ≈ 1.75 rad, φ_τe ≈ 0.73 rad

## Context

This paper is a reply to comments by Gal (2008) on the authors' earlier work (Ivanov and Kienle, Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 062502, 2009) proposing that the "GSI oscillations" observed in electron capture decays of H-like 140Pr58+, 142Pm60+, and 122I52+ ions arise from interference of massive neutrino mass-eigenstates. The authors extend their earlier analysis by addressing Gal's argument that summing over all neutrino flavors (electron, muon, tau) eliminates interference terms, introducing arbitrary phases to restore coherence. The work also responds to alternative explanations involving magnetic fields (Faestermann) and mass-splitting of mother ions, and notes that the positron decay mode of 142Pm60+ shows no time modulation, which the authors argue rules out neutrino magnetic moment explanations.
