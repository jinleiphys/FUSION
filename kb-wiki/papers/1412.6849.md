# Signals of Deconfinement Phase Transition and Possible Energy Range of Its Detection

> Here we thoroughly discuss the present status of the deconfinement phase transition signals outlined in the NICA White Paper 10.01. It is argued that none of the signals outlined in the NICA White Paper is prepared for experimental verification. At the same time we discuss the new irregularities and new signals of the deconfinement transition found recently within the realistic version of the hadron resonance gas model. All new findings evidence that the mixed quark-gluon-hadron phase can be reached at the center of mass energy of collision 4.3-4.9 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that none of the deconfinement phase transition signals outlined in the NICA White Paper are ready for experimental verification, but presents new irregularities found within a realistic hadron resonance gas model that collectively indicate the mixed quark-gluon-hadron phase can be reached at center-of-mass collision energies of 4.3–4.9 GeV.

## Method

The author critically analyzes the traditional deconfinement signals (Kink, Strangeness Horn, Step) and the signals proposed in the NICA White Paper, including spinodal decomposition, chiral magnetic effect, and critical endpoint searches. New irregularities are identified using a multicomponent hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) with different hard-core radii for pions, kaons, other mesons, and baryons, fitted to 111 independent multiplicity ratios measured at 14 center-of-mass energies from 2.7 GeV to 200 GeV. The chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from these fits are then examined for anomalous behavior as a function of collision energy.

## Key numbers

- The HRGM achieved global fit quality of χ²/dof ≈ 1.16 (Ref. HRGM:13) and χ²/dof ≈ 1.06 (Ref. KAB:gs) for 111 independent multiplicity ratios at 14 values of √sNN from 2.7 GeV to 200 GeV.
- The chemical freeze-out pressure jumps by a factor of 4–7 when √sNN changes from 4.3 GeV to 4.9 GeV, depending on the resonance width parameterization.
- The number of effective degrees of freedom increases by about 70% for the model with multicomponent hard-core repulsion in the same energy range.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature increases by about 1.35 times, and the entropy density jumps by about 4.2 times, between √sNN = 4.3 GeV and 4.9 GeV.
- The best-fit hard-core radii are: baryons Rb = 0.2 fm, other mesons Rm = 0.4 fm, pions Rπ = 0.1 fm, and kaons RK = 0.38 fm.
- The mixed phase is located within a narrow energy range between √sNN = 3.8 GeV and √sNN = 4.9 GeV at most.
- A local minimum in the chemical freeze-out volume exists at √sNN = 4.9 GeV (Elab = 11.6 GeV).
- The finite-size scaling analysis by Roy (2014) located the QCD phase diagram endpoint at √sNN = 47.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior development of the hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) with multicomponent hard-core repulsion (Oliinychenko 2012, HRGM 2013, KAB:gs, BWGwidth) and the shock adiabat model of central nuclear collisions (SA1, SA2). It critically evaluates the signals proposed in the NICA White Paper and argues that traditional irregularities (Kink, Strangeness Horn, Step) cannot be explained within a single framework or reproduced by transport models like UrQMD and HSD or hybrid hydro-cascade models. The new findings enable future experiments at NICA, FAIR, RHIC BES, and SPS CERN to locate the mixed phase, provided energy scan steps are about 100 MeV in the laboratory frame and experimental accuracy improves by about an order of magnitude.
