# Therminator: Thermal heavy-Ion generator

> THERMINATOR is a Monte Carlo event generator designed for studying of particle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions performed at such experimental facilities as the SPS, RHIC, or LHC. The program implements thermal models of particle production with single freeze-out. It performs the following tasks: 1) generation of stable particles and unstable resonances at the chosen freeze-out hypersurface with the local phase-space density of particles given by the statistical distribution factors, 2) subsequent space-time evolution and decays of hadronic resonances in cascades, 3) calculation of the transverse-momentum spectra and numerous other observables related to the space-time evolution. The geometry of the freeze-out hypersurface and the collective velocity of expansion may be chosen from two successful models, the Cracow single-freeze-out model and the Blast-Wave model. All particles from the Particle Data Tables are used. Experimental cuts may be introduced in a very straightforward manner. The code is written in the object-oriented c++ language and complies to the standards of the ROOT environment.

## Key claim

THERMINATOR is a Monte Carlo event generator that implements thermal models of particle production with single freeze-out, providing full information on space-time positions and momenta of produced particles, but it explicitly does not include secondary rescatterings after freeze-out, meaning it assumes no further interactions among decay products.

## Method

The program generates primordial particles on a chosen freeze-out hypersurface (either the Cracow single-freeze-out model or the Blast-Wave model) using Bose-Einstein or Fermi-Dirac statistical distributions, then simulates the space-time evolution and cascade decays of hadronic resonances using particle data from the Particle Data Tables. Particles move along classical trajectories with velocities composed of thermal motion and collective expansion, while unstable resonances decay after a randomly generated lifetime according to the exponential decay law, with two-body and three-body decays implemented using simple kinematic formulas in the rest frame of the decaying particle before boosting to the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature of approximately 165 MeV used as typical input parameter
- Default thermodynamic parameters: Temperature = 0.1656 GeV, MiuB = 0.0285 GeV, MiuS = 0.0069 GeV, MiuI = -0.0009 GeV
- Default geometric parameters: Tau = 9.74 fm, RhoMax = 7.74 fm
- Generation of 500 events from scratch takes about 1 hour 15 minutes on a PC with Athlon-Barton 2.5 GHz; each subsequent 500 events take about 1 hour
- Storage requirement: 1.1 GB for each 500 events; after converting to ROOT TTree format, 900 MB may be freed
- RAM required to execute with typical data: 50 Mbytes
- Package size: 324 KB directory, 40 KB compressed distribution archive
- Program contains 6516 lines of code

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach previously implemented in codes like SHARE (Torrieri, 2004) and THERMUS (Wheaton, 2004), which only compute particle abundances, by adding full space-time information and transverse-momentum spectra calculation. The program implements the Cracow single-freeze-out model from Broniowski (2001) and the Blast-Wave model from Schnedermann (1993) and Retiere (2003), enabling future studies of HBT correlation radii, correlations of non-identical particles, balance functions, and elliptic flow coefficients within the statistical framework.
