# Relativistic Nuclear Energy Density Functionals: adjusting parameters to binding energies

> We study a particular class of relativistic nuclear energy density functionals in which only nucleon degrees of freedom are explicitly used in the construction of effective interaction terms. Short-distance (high-momentum) correlations, as well as intermediate and long-range dynamics, are encoded in the medium (nucleon density) dependence of the strength functionals of an effective interaction Lagrangian. Guided by the density dependence of microscopic nucleon self-energies in nuclear matter, a phenomenological ansatz for the density-dependent coupling functionals is accurately determined in self-consistent mean-field calculations of binding energies of a large set of axially deformed nuclei. The relationship between the nuclear matter volume, surface and symmetry energies, and the corresponding predictions for nuclear masses is analyzed in detail. The resulting best-fit parametrization of the nuclear energy density functional is further tested in calculations of properties of spherical and deformed medium-heavy and heavy nuclei, including binding energies, charge radii, deformation parameters, neutron skin thickness, and excitation energies of giant multipole resonances.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic nuclear energy density functional can be accurately parameterized by fitting exclusively to binding energies of axially deformed nuclei, rather than to spherical closed-shell nuclei, with the goal of producing a functional that does not implicitly contain symmetry-breaking corrections and quadrupole fluctuation correlations.

## Method

The authors construct a point-coupling relativistic Lagrangian with density-dependent coupling functionals α_S, α_V, and α_TV, plus a derivative term δ_S, and adjust the parameters to binding energies of 64 axially deformed nuclei in the mass regions A≈150–180 and A≈230–250. The density dependence of the couplings follows a phenomenological ansatz guided by microscopic nucleon self-energies from Hartree-Fock tree-level calculations of modern NN potentials, and the nuclear matter equation of state is anchored to the microscopic EoS of Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (1998) at two densities. Pairing correlations are treated in the BCS approximation with empirical pairing gaps from the 5-point formula, and the self-consistent relativistic mean-field equations are solved by expanding nucleon spinors in a deformed axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with 16 major shells.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density fixed at ρ_sat = 0.152 fm⁻³, taken from the DD-ME1 and DD-ME2 interactions
- Dirac effective nucleon mass fixed at m*_D = 0.58 m, within the allowed interval 0.57 ≤ m*_D/m ≤ 0.61
- Nuclear matter compression modulus fixed at K_nm = 230 MeV
- Volume asymmetry fixed at a_4 = 33 MeV
- Eight sets of interactions generated with volume energy coefficients a_v ranging from -16.02 MeV (set A) to -16.16 MeV (set H)
- Derivative coupling parameter δ_S constrained to the range -0.84 fm⁴ ≤ δ_S ≤ -0.80 fm⁴, consistent with the microscopic estimate of -0.85 fm⁴ to -0.7 fm⁴ from in-medium chiral perturbation theory
- The set of 64 deformed nuclei covers mass numbers from 154 to 250, with asymmetry coefficient α² ranging from 0.018 to 0.054

## Context

This work extends the relativistic density-dependent point-coupling approach introduced by Finelli, Kaiser, Vretenar and Weise (2004, 2006), but replaces the low-energy QCD constraints on the medium dependence with a phenomenological ansatz adjusted exclusively to deformed nuclei masses. The motivation is to produce a functional suitable for the generator coordinate method with angular-momentum and particle-number projection developed by Niksic, Vretenar and Ring (2006), where correlations to be treated explicitly should not be implicitly included in the functional. The approach contrasts with standard fits to spherical closed-shell nuclei used for interactions such as TW-99, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, PKDD, and PK01, and enables future systematic subtraction of rotational energy corrections from experimental masses following the method of Bender, Bertsch and Heenen (2006).
