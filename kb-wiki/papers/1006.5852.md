# Fulop-Tsutsui interactions on quantum graphs

> We examine scale invariant Fulop-Tsutsui couplings in a quantum vertex of a general degree $n$. We demonstrate that essentially same scattering amplitudes as for the free coupling can be achieved for two $(n-1)$-parameter Fulop-Tsutsui subfamilies if $n$ is odd, and for three $(n-1)$-parameter Fulop-Tsutsui subfamilies if $n$ is even. We also work up an approximation scheme for a general Fulop-Tsutsui vertex, using only $n$ $δ$ function potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a quantum vertex of general degree \(n\), the scattering amplitudes of the free coupling can be reproduced by two distinct \((n-1)\)-parameter Fulop-Tsutsui subfamilies when \(n\) is odd, and by three such subfamilies when \(n\) is even, with the additional condition that the scattering matrix is momentum-independent (scale invariant).

## Method

The authors classify Fulop-Tsutsui couplings on a star graph of degree \(n\) using the \(ST\)-form of boundary conditions, where the rank \(m\) of the matrix \(B\) indexes \(n-1\) subfamilies. They derive the scattering matrix \(\mathcal{S}\) from the boundary conditions and prove that free-like couplings (those with reflection and transmission moduli equal to \(1-2/n\) and \(2/n\), respectively) correspond to specific choices of the parameter matrix \(T\) and the rank \(m\). An approximation scheme is then constructed using \(n\) delta-function potentials and connecting edges with varied lengths and vector potentials to realize a general Fulop-Tsutsui vertex.

## Key numbers

* For a vertex of degree \(n\), the free coupling has reflection amplitude modulus \(|{\cal R}_j| = 1 - 2/n\) and transmission amplitude modulus \(|{\cal T}_{ij}| = 2/n\) for all \(i,j\).
* The Fulop-Tsutsui subfamily of rank \((m, n-m)\) offers \(2m(n-m)\) real parameters from the complex matrix \(T\); this number is maximized at \(m = [n/2]\) giving \([n^2/2]\) parameters.
* The free-like vertex coupling exists for two \((n-1)\)-parameter subfamilies when \(n\) is odd, and for three \((n-1)\)-parameter subfamilies when \(n\) is even.
* The approximation scheme uses \(n\) delta-function potentials, compared to the general construction requiring \(n^2\) parameters (Cheon and Turek, 2010).

## Context

This work extends the classification of Fulop-Tsutsui interactions from the \(n=2\) case (Fulop and Tsutsui, 2000) and the \(n=3\) case (Cheon, Exner, and Turek, 2009) to general degree \(n\). It identifies free-like connection conditions that generalize the standard free coupling (Exner and Seba, 1989) and provides a simplified finite approximation using only \(n\) delta potentials, which is a significant reduction from the general \(n^2\)-parameter construction (Cheon, Exner, and Turek, 2010). The results enable the design of quantum graph vertices with prescribed scale-invariant scattering properties.
