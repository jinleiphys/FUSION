# Quantum Computation of Phase Transition in Interacting Scalar Quantum Field Theory

> It has been demonstrated that the critical point of the phase transition in scalar quantum field theory with a quartic interaction in one space dimension can be approximated via a Gaussian Effective Potential (GEP). We discuss how this critical point can be estimated using quantum hardware. We perform quantum computations with various lattice sizes and obtain evidence of a transition from a symmetric to a symmetry-broken phase. We use both discrete- and continuous-variable quantum computation. We implement the ten-site case on IBM quantum hardware using the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) algorithm to minimize the GEP and identify lattice level-crossings. These are extrapolated via simulations to find the continuum critical point.

## Key claim

The critical point of the phase transition in scalar quantum field theory with a quartic interaction in one space dimension can be approximated via a Gaussian Effective Potential (GEP), and this critical point can be estimated using quantum hardware, with the continuum critical value of the dimensionless coupling extrapolated from lattice calculations lying near λ/m² ≈ 61.2, though the abstract omits that the ten-site case was the only one implemented on actual IBM quantum hardware while larger lattice sizes were handled via simulation.

## Method

The authors discretize φ⁴ scalar field theory in 1+1 dimensions on a lattice of L sites with unit lattice spacing, using a Gaussian Ansatz for the ground state parameterized by a variational mass parameter Ω and a field displacement φ_C. They compute the Gaussian Effective Potential (GEP) via the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) algorithm, implementing the ten-site case on IBM quantum hardware using discrete-variable (DV) quantum computation, while also performing continuous-variable (CV) quantum simulations using bosonic qiskit with photon-number measurements and parameter shift rules for derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Continuum critical coupling from analytical GEP calculations: λ/m² = 61.27
- For lattice size L → ∞ with renormalized mass m = 0.1, the critical values approach Ω_c²/m² = 8.4 and λ_c/m² = 60.8
- In the scaling limit L → ∞, m → 0, the continuum result λ_c/m² = 61.2 is recovered
- The critical coupling on the lattice is given by λ_c = (Ω_c² + 2Ω₀²) / (I₀(Ω₀) - I₀(Ω_c)), where Ω₀ = m is the renormalized mass

## Context

This work extends prior analytical calculations of the phase transition in φ⁴ theory using the Gaussian Effective Potential by Stevenson (1984, 1985) and Chang (1976), who derived the continuum critical point. It builds on quantum computations of energy levels in this model performed on IBM qubit hardware by Kubra (2019) and on CV quantum simulations using Quantum Imaginary Time Evolution by Kubra (2022). The results demonstrate that near-term quantum hardware can estimate the critical point of a quantum phase transition in a bosonic field theory, enabling future studies of non-perturbative phenomena in quantum field theories using hybrid quantum-classical algorithms.
