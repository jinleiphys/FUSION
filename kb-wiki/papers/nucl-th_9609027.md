# The inclusive transverse response of nuclear matter

> The electromagnetic inclusive transverse response of nuclear matter at saturation density is studied within the correlated basis function perturbation theory for momentum transfers q from 300 to 550 MeV/c. The correlation operator includes a Jastrow component, accounting for the short range repulsion, as well as longer range spin, tensor and isospin ones. The Schiavilla- Pandharipande-Riska model for the two-body electromagnetic currents, satisfying the continuity equation with realistic v_14 potentials, is adopted. Currents due to intermediate Delta-isobar excitations, are also included. The global contribution of the two-body currents turns out to be positive and provides an enhancement of the one-body transverse response ranging from about 20% for the lower momenta to about 10% for the higher ones. This finding is in agreement with the Green's Function Monte Carlo studies of the transverse Euclidean response in A=3,4 nuclei and contradicts previous results obtained within the Fermi gas and shell models. The tensor-isospin component of the correlation is found to be the leading responsible for such a behavior. The nuclear matter response is compared to recent experimental data on 40Ca and 56Fe.

# The inclusive transverse response of nuclear matter

## Key claim
The global contribution of two-body currents to the transverse response of nuclear matter at saturation density is positive and provides an enhancement of the one-body transverse response, but this result depends critically on the inclusion of tensor-isospin correlations; scalar Jastrow correlations alone produce a quenching rather than an enhancement.

## Method
The electromagnetic inclusive transverse response of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density was computed using correlated basis function (CBF) perturbation theory, with correlated one particle-one hole intermediate states and a realistic optical potential model to account for spreading into two particle-one hole states. The correlation operator included Jastrow, spin, tensor, and isospin components, and the Schiavilla-Pandharipande-Riska model for two-body electromagnetic currents was adopted, satisfying the continuity equation with realistic v14 potentials, along with currents from intermediate Delta-isobar excitations.

## Key numbers
- Momentum transfers studied: q from 300 to 550 MeV/c
- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3, corresponding to Fermi momentum kF = 1.33 fm^-1
- Enhancement of one-body transverse response due to two-body currents: approximately 20% at q = 300 MeV/c and approximately 10% at q = 550 MeV/c
- Dipole parametrization scale parameter for nucleon form factor: 839 MeV
- Delta form factor cutoff masses: Λ1 = 1196 MeV and Λ2 = 843 MeV
- Transition magnetic moment μγNΔ = 3 μ0

## Context
This work extends CBF studies of the longitudinal response in nuclear matter (Fabrocini 1996) and spin responses (Fabrocini 1996) to the transverse electromagnetic response. The finding of a positive enhancement from two-body currents agrees with Green's Function Monte Carlo studies of the transverse Euclidean response in A=3,4 nuclei (Carlson 1993) but contradicts previous results obtained within the Fermi gas and shell models (Amaro 1994). The nuclear matter response is compared to experimental data on 40Ca and 56Fe from Saclay (Meziani 1984), Bates (Yates 1993), and Jourdan (1996), with the CBF results closer to the Bates and Jourdan data.
