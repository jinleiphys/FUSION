# Adiabatic projection method for scattering and reactions on the lattice

> We demonstrate and test the adiabatic projection method, a general new framework for calculating scattering and reactions on the lattice. The method is based upon calculating a low-energy effective theory for clusters which becomes exact in the limit of large Euclidean projection time. As a detailed example we calculate the adiabatic two-body Hamiltonian for elastic fermion-dimer scattering in lattice effective field theory. Our calculation corresponds to neutron-deuteron scattering in the spin-quartet channel at leading order in pionless effective field theory. We show that the spectrum of the adiabatic Hamiltonian reproduces the spectrum of the original Hamiltonian below the inelastic threshold to arbitrary accuracy. We also show that the calculated s-wave phase shift reproduces the known exact result in the continuum and infinite-volume limits. When extended to more than one scattering channel, the adiabatic projection method can be used to calculate inelastic reactions on the lattice in future work.

## Key claim

The adiabatic projection method provides a general framework for calculating scattering and reactions on the lattice that becomes exact in the limit of large Euclidean projection time, with errors that are exponentially small in the projection time. The method reproduces the low-energy spectrum of the original microscopic Hamiltonian below the inelastic threshold to arbitrary accuracy, but only when the number of initial cluster states is chosen appropriately.

## Method

The method begins by constructing initial cluster states labeled by their separation vector, then evolves these states in Euclidean time using the full microscopic Hamiltonian to produce dressed cluster states. The adiabatic Hamiltonian is defined as the projection of the original Hamiltonian onto the subspace spanned by these dressed cluster states, using the inverse of the norm matrix to handle non-orthogonality. For the specific test case of fermion-dimer scattering, the authors use a lattice Hamiltonian with nearest-neighbor hopping terms and zero-range attractive interactions, corresponding to neutron-deuteron scattering in the spin-quartet channel at leading order in pionless effective field theory. The s-wave phase shift is extracted from the finite-volume spectrum using Luscher's method.

## Key numbers

- Fermion mass set to m = 939 MeV, with dimer binding energy tuned to B = 2.2246 MeV
- Lattice spacing values used: b = 1/100 MeV^-1, 1/150 MeV^-1, and 1/200 MeV^-1
- Cubic periodic box sizes up to L ≤ 16, corresponding to maximum physical box sizes of 32 fm, 21 fm, and 16 fm respectively
- For lattice spacing b = 1/100 MeV^-1 and box size L = 7 (physical length 13.8 fm), the three-body continuum threshold starts at E = 8.0 MeV
- Approximately 30 initial cluster states (N_R ~ 30) used for phase shift calculations
- Dimer breakup momentum at 52.7 MeV, indicated by vertical dashed line in phase shift comparison

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic projection formalism introduced in Rupak (2013) for calculating nuclear reactions on the lattice, and is motivated by recent studies of alpha-particle clusters in carbon-12 by Epelbaum (2011, 2012). The method is similar in structure to the Hamiltonian matrix used in no-core shell model calculations combined with the resonating group method by Navratil (2011) and Quaglioni (2013). The results for s-wave phase shift are compared with exact continuum infinite-volume results from the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation as developed by Bedaque (1997, 1998) and Braaten (2004). Future work will extend the method to inelastic reactions including radiative capture processes relevant to astrophysics, such as lithium-7 neutron capture and beryllium-7 proton capture.
