# Conformal Bjorken flow in the general frame and its attractor: Similarities and discrepancies with the Müller-Israel-Stewart formalism

> We investigate the implications of the general frame approach for conformal Bjorken flow beyond the earlier studies. We show that the power series solution at late times is not unique and is accompanied by an exact solution of the form $1/τ$, which becomes unphysical if taken on shell. In contrast to the Müller-Israel-Stewart formalism, a matching between $\NFSYM$ results and the hydro expansion is only possible up to the first order, which gives rise to $η/s=1/4π$. Matching the results to the next order gives rise to causality/stability-violating values. Furthermore, we show that the pressure anisotropy in the general frame cannot capture the hydrodynamization, and we introduce an alternative measure to find the attractor. Using slow-roll expansion, we find an analytical approximation form for the attractor. We also show that the early-time behavior of attractors is related to stability and causality conditions. The attractor solutions outside the stable and causal regime give rise to reheating and negative longitudinal pressures in early times, in contrast to the stable and causal ones. We also comment on the violation of the second law of thermodynamics by the off-shell parameters. We show that for the stable and causal choice of parameters, the off-shell canonical entropy of the attractors, which is not a physical quantity, has a negative divergence in early times before tending to its on-shell limit. On the other hand, the unstable and acausal attractors have non-negative entropy divergence. We speculate that the violation of the second law by stable and causal off-shell parameters is required for stability of the first-order hydrodynamics. We investigate the analytical structure of the Borel-transformed series and find the proper relation between the poles and nonhydro modes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for conformal Bjorken flow in the general frame approach, the late-time power series solution is not unique and is accompanied by an exact solution of the form \(1/\tau\) that becomes unphysical when taken on shell; matching with \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM results is only possible up to first order, giving \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\), while matching to next order yields causality- or stability-violating values.

## Method

The authors solve the ordinary differential equation for the temperature in conformal Bjorken flow derived from the general frame energy-momentum tensor, using a late-time gradient expansion ansatz \(T = \Lambda \mathcal{T}(\rho)\) with \(\rho \equiv (1/(\Lambda\tau))^{1/3}\). They analyze the attractor by introducing a hydrodynamization measure \(f = 1 + \dot{T}/(T \nabla\cdot u)\) that coincides with the MIS measure, and apply a slow-roll expansion to find an analytical approximation for the attractor. The analytical structure of the Borel-transformed series is investigated to relate poles to nonhydrodynamic modes.

## Key numbers

* \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\): the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio obtained from matching the first-order gradient expansion to \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM results.
* \(\mathcal{C}_\pi > 1\) and \(\mathcal{C}_\theta > 1\): the stability and causality conditions for the off-shell parameters in the general frame approach, translated from the conditions of Kovtun (2019) and Bemfica (2019).
* \(T\tau_G > 4\eta/s\): the causality criterion for the general frame, where \(\tau_G = \pi_1/(Ts)\) and \(\pi_1 = 16\bar{p}\eta\mathcal{C}_\pi T^3\).
* The nonhydrodynamic mode in the local rest frame: \(\omega = -i sT/\pi_1\), which is exact.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Bjorken flow in the general frame approach by Bemfica (2017) and Das (massless case), which suggested that stable and causal first-order hydrodynamics may exist but left open questions about attractor significance and matching to microscopic theory. The paper contrasts the general frame results with the Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) formalism, where matching to \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM is possible to higher orders (Heller 2007, 2008), and shows that the pressure anisotropy in the general frame cannot capture hydrodynamization, unlike in MIS. The findings enable further investigation of attractor behavior in first-order hydrodynamics and its relation to stability and causality conditions.
