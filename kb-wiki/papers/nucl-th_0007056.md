# Partial Dynamical Symmetries in Nuclei

> Partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) are shown to be relevant to the interpretation of the $K=0_2$ band and to the occurrence of F-spin multiplets of ground and scissors bands in deformed nuclei. Hamiltonians with bosonic and fermionic PDS are presented.

## Key claim
Partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) provide a framework where a Hamiltonian is not fully symmetric under a given group, yet a subset of states (e.g., ground and gamma bands) retain exact symmetry while other states (e.g., the K=0₂ band) are mixed, offering a more realistic description of deformed nuclei than exact dynamical symmetries.

## Method
The paper constructs bosonic IBM Hamiltonians with partial SU(3) symmetry (Eq. 1) and partial F-spin symmetry (Eq. 2), where specific solvable states have good symmetry while others are mixed. For the fermionic case, a symplectic shell-model Hamiltonian with partial SU(3) symmetry (Eq. 3) is presented and compared to the exact quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in ²⁰Ne. Analytic expressions for E2 rates between solvable states are derived and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For the SU(3) PDS Hamiltonian with N=16, the double-phonon admixture in the K=0₂ band wave function is at most ~15% for bandhead ratios in the range 0.8–1.65; in ¹⁶⁸Er the admixture is 12.5%.
- In ¹⁶⁸Er, the PDS calculation gives B(E2) values for K=0₂→g transitions: 0.65 W.u. (2⁺→0⁺_g), 1.02 W.u. (2⁺→2⁺_g), 2.27 W.u. (2⁺→4⁺_g), compared to experimental ranges of 0.06–0.94, 0.07–1.27, and 0.4–5.1 W.u. respectively.
- For F-spin multiplets, the ratio R = ΣB(M1)↑/(C_{F,F₀})² is fairly constant across partners; e.g., for ¹⁶⁰Gd (F=7, F₀=0) R=6.36±0.26 and for ¹⁶⁰Dy (F=7, F₀=1) R=5.29±0.39.
- The summed M1 strength to the scissors mode ΣB(M1)↑ ranges from 0.43±0.12 μ_N² (¹⁴⁸Sm) to 3.30±0.12 μ_N² (¹⁶⁸Er) across the listed F-spin multiplets.

## Context
This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetries, previously applied in other contexts, to nuclear structure by showing that the K=0₂ band in deformed nuclei can be understood as a single-phonon excitation with small double-phonon admixture, contrary to pure beta-vibration models. It also addresses the long-standing puzzle of F-spin symmetry in IBM-2, where earlier work by Lipas (1990) showed F-spin impurities of 2–4% in low-lying states, by providing Hamiltonians where F-spin is good only for ground and scissors bands. The results enable systematic tests of PDS predictions against lifetime and Coulomb excitation measurements, and suggest the need for independent lifetime measurements of the 0⁺_{K=0₂} state in ¹⁶⁸Er to resolve experimental discrepancies.
