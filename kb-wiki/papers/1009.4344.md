# Troubles of describing multiple pion production in chiral dynamics

> Generalized Hidden Local Symmetry (GHLS) model as the chiral model of pseudoscalar, vector, and axial vector mesons and their interactions containing also the couplings of strongly interacting particles with electroweak gauge bosons, is confronted with the ALEPH data on the decay $τ^-\toπ^-π^-π^+ν_τ$ and BABAR and CMD data on the reaction $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-π^+π^-$. It is shown that both the invariant mass spectrum of final pions in $τ$ decay calculated in the GHLS framework with the single $a_1(1260)$ resonance and the cross section $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-π^+π^-$ calculated in the above framework with the single $ρ(770)$ resonance, disagree with the experimental data. The modifications of GHLS model based on inclusion of two additional heavier axial vector mesons $a_1^\prime$, $a_1^{\prime\prime}$ in the $τ$ decay and the vector mesons $ρ^\prime$, $ρ^{\prime\prime}$ in $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-π^+π^-$ are shown to be necessary for the good description of the above data.

## Key claim

The Generalized Hidden Local Symmetry (GHLS) model, when restricted to a single \(a_1(1260)\) resonance for \(\tau\) decay or a single \(\rho(770)\) resonance for \(e^+e^-\to 4\pi\), fails to describe the experimental data; the inclusion of two additional heavier axial vector mesons \(a_1^\prime\) and \(a_1^{\prime\prime}\) (for \(\tau\) decay) and two additional vector mesons \(\rho^\prime\) and \(\rho^{\prime\prime}\) (for \(e^+e^-\) annihilation) is necessary to achieve good agreement, but even then the fit must be further constrained to reproduce the measured radiative width \(\Gamma_{a_1^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma}\).

## Method

The authors computed the invariant mass spectrum of \(\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\) in \(\tau^-\to\pi^-\pi^-\pi^+\nu_\tau\) and the cross section of \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-\) using the GHLS Lagrangian, expanding in pion momentum over the pion decay constant \(f_\pi=92.4\) MeV. They fitted the free GHLS parameters \((a,b,c,d,\alpha_4,\alpha_5)\) and the masses and couplings of additional resonances \(a_1^\prime\), \(a_1^{\prime\prime}\) (for \(\tau\) decay) and \(\rho^\prime\), \(\rho^{\prime\prime}\) (for \(e^+e^-\)) to the ALEPH, BABAR, and CMD-2 data, imposing constraints such as universality (\(a=2\)) and the measured radiative width of \(a_1^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma\).

## Key numbers

* The "canonical" GHLS parameter set \((a,b,c,d,\alpha_4,\alpha_5,\alpha_6)=(2,2,2,0,-1,1,1)\) yields a branching fraction \(B_{\tau^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\nu_\tau}\approx 6\%\), which is too low compared to the data.
* The unconstrained fit (variant A) gives \(m_{a_1}=1.332\pm0.015\) GeV, \(a=1.665\pm0.011\), and \(B_{\tau^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\nu_\tau}=(9.05\pm0.16)\%\) with \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f}=79/102\).
* The fit constrained to universality (\(a\equiv2\)) and the measured \(\Gamma_{a_1^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma}=640\pm246\) keV (variant C) yields \(m_{a_1}=1.368\pm0.006\) GeV, \(m_{a_1^\prime}=1.422\pm0.007\) GeV, \(m_{a_1^{\prime\prime}}=1.800\pm0.007\) GeV, and \(B_{\tau^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\nu_\tau}=(8.97\pm0.13)\%\) with \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f}=45/102\).
* For \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-\) at \(\sqrt{s}\approx1\) GeV, the joint contribution of \(\rho^\prime\) and \(\rho^{\prime\prime}\) resonances is a factor of thirty greater than the contribution of \(\rho(770)\) alone.

## Context

This work extends the GHLS model of Bando et al. (1988) by confronting it with high-precision data from ALEPH (2005) on \(\tau\) decay and BABAR and CMD-2 on \(e^+e^-\) annihilation. It contradicts the earlier assumption that a single \(a_1(1260)\) or \(\rho(770)\) resonance suffices within GHLS, and it demonstrates that additional heavier resonances (predicted by Isgur and collaborators in the 1980s) are required. The results enable future fits that incorporate the radiative decay width of \(a_1\) and constrain the parameters of the heavier axial vector and vector mesons, providing a more complete chiral description of multiple pion production.
