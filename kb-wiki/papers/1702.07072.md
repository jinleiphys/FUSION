# 12 GeV CEBAF - The Physics and Experiments

> In this talk the role of spin and polarization is discussed in experimental search for new excited baryon states and in the study of the internal quark-gluon structure of the proton and neutron. Also the perspective of spin physics at the 12 GeV CEBAF electron accelerator is discussed and what we hope to learn about fundamental properties of hadrons such as their multi-dimensional structure through the momentum and spatial imaging, and about the forces on the quarks in the proton and how quark confinement may be realized through the spatial distribution of such forces.

# 12 GeV CEBAF - The Physics and Experiments

## Key claim
The 12 GeV CEBAF upgrade and its experimental equipment are well matched to a scientific program that can provide fundamentally new insights into the multi-dimensional internal structure of the nucleon, including direct experimental access to quark orbital angular momentum and the distribution of confinement forces, but the paper presents projected sensitivities and planned experiments rather than completed measurements, and many results depend on the successful execution of multi-channel partial wave analyses and the inclusion of disconnected interactions in lattice QCD calculations.

## Method
The paper reviews the experimental program at the 12 GeV CEBAF electron accelerator, which uses superconducting rf technology to deliver beams up to 11 GeV to existing end stations and 12 GeV to the new Hall D. The program employs the CLAS12 detector in Hall B with polarized solid-state targets (NH3 and ND3), the GlueX detector in Hall D with linearly polarized photon beams, and the Super High Momentum Spectrometer in Hall C, to measure deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS), and photoproduction of hyperons and mesons for baryon spectroscopy.

## Key numbers
- The CEBAF accelerator reaches a maximum energy of nearly 11 GeV at existing end stations and 12 GeV in Hall D, achieved by adding five accelerating cryomodules with four times higher gradients per unit length to both linacs.
- The Bonn-Gatchina multi-channel partial wave analysis of photo-produced KY final states claimed a set of eight states that are either newly discovered or have significantly improved evidence, which entered recent editions of the Review of Particle Properties.
- Lattice QCD calculations that include disconnected interactions yield net orbital angular momentum of approximately 45% for the proton spin, overturning previous calculations that obtained negligible net OAM.
- The projected data for the beam spin asymmetry A_LU for DVCS-BH interference show statistical uncertainties too small to be visible at 11 GeV beam energy.
- The Boer-Mulders asymmetry for pions as a function of Q^2 and P_T is calculated for Q^2 > 2 GeV^2 from 2000 hours of running with CLAS12 at 11 GeV.

## Context
This paper extends the CLAS N* program at Jefferson Lab from 6 GeV operation to 12 GeV, building on prior work in baryon spectroscopy using the CLAS detector and CB-ELSA facility. The program aims to address the "missing resonance" crisis identified in elastic pi N scattering, where few high-mass states were found, by using photoproduction processes with polarization observables. The work enables future experimental searches for hybrid baryons in the mass range from 2.0 to 3.0 GeV, and will complement global analyses of polarized parton distributions from DIS and SIDIS data as presented by Leader at this conference.
