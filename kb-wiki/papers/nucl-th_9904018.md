# Kinetic Freeze-out and Radial Flow in 11.6 A GeV Au+Au Collisions

> We study the kinetic freeze-out conditions of hadrons in Au+Au collisions at 11.6 A GeV/c using different parametrizations of an expanding thermal fireball. We take into account the available double differential momentum spectra of a variety of particle species, covering a large fraction of the total momentum space. The overall fit to the data is very good and indicates a relatively low kinetic freeze-out temperature of about 90 MeV with an average transverse expansion velocity at midrapidity of about 0.5 c.

## Key claim

The paper finds a kinetic freeze-out temperature of about 90 MeV and an average transverse expansion velocity at midrapidity of about 0.5 c, but this result depends on the specific fireball geometry and flow profile used; the best fit requires an elliptic (cigar-shaped) freeze-out geometry rather than a cylindrical one, and the temperature and flow parameters are stable across different model variations.

## Method

The authors fit a Cooper-Frye thermal freeze-out model to double differential hadron spectra (pions, kaons, protons, and Lambda hyperons) from 11.6 A GeV Au+Au collisions at the AGS. They assume longitudinal boost-invariant Bjorken flow, a linear transverse flow rapidity profile, and compare cylindrical versus elliptic freeze-out geometries, with constant or Gaussian temperature profiles. Resonance decays up to the Delta(1232) are included, and normalization factors are allowed to vary per particle species to account for deviations from chemical equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature at midrapidity: T0 = 90.5 MeV (Model 1), 91.6 MeV (Model 2), 93.3 MeV (Model 3)
- Average transverse flow velocity at midrapidity: ⟨v⊥⟩(η=0) = 0.449 c (Model 1), 0.484 c (Model 2), 0.483 c (Model 3)
- Baryon chemical potential at midrapidity: μB(η=0) = 548 MeV (Model 1), 549 MeV (Model 2), 536 MeV (Model 3)
- Fireball volume: V = 1.74 × 10^4 fm^3 (Models 1 and 2), 1.82 × 10^4 fm^3 (Model 3)
- Maximal longitudinal flow rapidity: ηmax = 1.05 (Model 1), 1.66 (Model 2), 1.71 (Model 3)
- Maximal transverse flow rapidity: ρ0 = 0.726 (Model 1), 0.793 (Model 2), 0.792 (Model 3)
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: χ^2/DOF = 6.64 (Model 1), 3.16 (Model 2), 2.40 (Model 3)
- Entropy per baryon from the thermal parameters: S/A ≈ 12–13

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of Si+Au collisions at the AGS by Chapman and Nix (1993), who found a similar low freeze-out temperature (about 90 MeV) but a slightly smaller average transverse flow velocity of about 0.45 c. It contradicts the earlier analysis by Stachel (1996) which reported a freeze-out temperature of 130 MeV based on rapidity spectra alone; the present work shows that the full set of transverse mass spectra requires the lower temperature. The results are consistent with chemical freeze-out at Tchem ≈ 130 MeV followed by isentropic hydrodynamic expansion to kinetic freeze-out at Tkin ≈ 90 MeV, and enable comparison with higher-energy SPS data from NA49 which suggest T = 100–120 MeV and ⟨v⊥⟩ = 0.45–0.5 c.
