# Charged and neutral vector meson under magnetic field

> The vector meson $ρ$ in the presence of external magnetic field has been investigated in the framework of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, where mesons are constructed by infinite sum of quark-loop chains by using random phase approximation. The $ρ$ meson polarization function is calculated to the leading order of $1/N_c$ expansion. It is found that the constituent quark mass increases with magnetic field, the masses of the neutral vector meson $ρ^{0}$ with spin component $s_z=0,\,\pm1$ and the charged vector meson $ρ^{\pm}$ with $s_z=0$ also increases with magnetic field. However, the mass square of the charged vector meson $ρ^{+}$ ($ρ^{-}$) with $s_z=+1$ ($s_z=-1$) decreases linearly with magnetic field and drops to zero at the critical magnetic field $e B_c \simeq 0.2 {\rm GeV}^2$, which indicates the possible condensation of charged vector meson in the vacuum. This critical magnetic field is much lower than the value $eB_c=0.6 {\rm GeV}^2$ predicted by a point-like vector meson. We also show that if we use lowest Landau level approximation, the mass of the charged vector meson $ρ^{\pm}$ for $s_z=\pm1$ cannot drop to zero at high magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The mass square of the charged vector meson $\rho^{+}$ ($\rho^{-}$) with spin component $s_z=+1$ ($s_z=-1$) decreases linearly with magnetic field and drops to zero at the critical magnetic field $e B_c \simeq 0.2 {\rm GeV}^2$, which is much lower than the value $eB_c=0.6 {\rm GeV}^2$ predicted by a point-like vector meson; however, if one uses the lowest Landau level approximation, the mass of the charged vector meson $\rho^{\pm}$ for $s_z=\pm1$ cannot drop to zero at high magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors investigated vector mesons in the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model under a constant external magnetic field, where mesons are constructed as quark-antiquark bound states via infinite sums of quark-loop chains using the random phase approximation. The $\rho$ meson polarization tensor was calculated to leading order in $1/N_c$ expansion from a single quark loop, using the Ritus fermion propagator method to handle the magnetic field. The masses of charged $\rho^{\pm}$ and neutral $\rho^{0}$ mesons for different spin components $s_z=0,\pm1$ were solved from the gap equations $1+2G_V A_i^2=0$, with the constituent quark mass determined self-consistently from the gap equation.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for charged $\rho^{\pm}$ with $s_z=\pm1$: $e B_c \simeq 0.2 {\rm GeV}^2$, where the mass square drops to zero.
- Slope of mass square decrease for charged $\rho^{\pm}$ with $s_z=\pm1$: $\kappa \approx 3$ from full numerical calculation (linear fit $M_{\rho^\pm}^2(B) = m_{\rho^\pm}^2 - \kappa e B$).
- Weak magnetic field expansion gives slope $\kappa_w \approx 1.92$ for the same mode.
- Point-like vector meson prediction for critical field: $eB_c = m_\rho^2 \simeq 0.6 {\rm GeV}^2$, using vacuum mass $m_\rho = 775.5 {\rm MeV}$.
- Model parameters: $\Lambda = 582 {\rm MeV}$, $G_S \Lambda^2 = 2.388$, $G_V \Lambda^2 = 1.73$, giving vacuum $f_\pi = 95 {\rm MeV}$, $m_\pi = 140 {\rm MeV}$, $M_\rho = 768 {\rm MeV}$, vacuum quark mass $M = 458 {\rm MeV}$, current quark mass $m_0 = 5 {\rm MeV}$, and vacuum condensate $<\bar{u}u> = -(252)^3 {\rm MeV}^3$.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of a "vacuum superconductor" based on point-like vector meson models (Chernodub 2010, 2011), which gave $eB_c=0.6 {\rm GeV}^2$, and addresses conflicting results from lattice QCD (Hidaka 2012) and Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Wangkunlun 2013) that found the charged $\rho$ mass does not drop to zero. The authors show that the lowest Landau level approximation, used in some prior calculations (Andreichikov 2013), prevents the mass from reaching zero, while full Landau level summation yields a critical field $eB_c \simeq 0.2 {\rm GeV}^2$, supporting the possibility of charged vector meson condensation. This enables further investigation into whether a vacuum superconductor exists in QCD under strong magnetic fields.
