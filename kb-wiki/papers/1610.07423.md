# Directed flow of $Î›$, $\barÎ›$, $K^{\pm}$, $K_S^0$ and $Ď•$ mesons from Beam Energy Scan Au+Au collisions using the STAR experiment

> We report the results of $v_1$ and $dv_1/dy$ near mid-rapidity for $Î›$, $\barÎ›$, $K^{\pm}$, $K_s^0$ and $Ď•$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27 and 39~GeV using the STAR detector at RHIC. The $dv_1/dy$ of $Î›$ is found to be consistent with that of the proton and shows a change in sign near $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 11.5$~GeV. The $v_{1}$ slope for $\barÎ›$, $\bar{p}$ and $Ď•$ shows a similar trend for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ $>$ 14.5~GeV, while below 14.5~GeV, $Ď•$ $v_{1}$ is consistent with zero but with a large uncertainty. The $dv_{1}/dy$ for net protons and net kaons is similar for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} >$ 14.5~GeV, but they deviate at lower beam energies.

## Key claim

The directed flow slope ($dv_1/dy$) of $\Lambda$ baryons is consistent with that of protons across all beam energies and shows a sign change near $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 11.5$ GeV, but the $\phi$ meson slope is consistent with zero at 11.5 GeV with large uncertainty, limiting the strength of the claimed softening signature.

## Method

The STAR detector at RHIC measured directed flow ($v_1$) and its slope near mid-rapidity ($dv_1/dy$) for $\Lambda$, $\bar{\Lambda}$, $K^{\pm}$, $K_S^0$, and $\phi$ mesons in 10-40% centrality Au+Au collisions at six beam energies. Particle identification used the Time Projection Chamber and Time of Flight detectors, with neutral species reconstructed via weak decay topologies ($K_s^0 \rightarrow \pi^{+}\pi^{-}$, $\Lambda \rightarrow p\pi$) and $\phi$ via $\phi \rightarrow K^{+}K^{-}$. The $v_1$ slope was extracted from a linear fit within $|y| < 0.8$, using the BBC detectors for event plane reconstruction with a large $\eta$-gap to reduce non-flow effects.

## Key numbers

* $\Lambda$ $dv_1/dy$ shows a sign change near $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 11.5$ GeV in 10-40% Au+Au collisions
* $\phi$ meson $v_1$ slope is consistent with zero at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 11.5$ GeV with large uncertainty, while showing negative slope for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} > 14.5$ GeV
* $K^+$ $dv_1/dy$ is closer to zero than $K^-$ for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} > 11.5$ GeV, with the trend reversed at 7.7 GeV
* Net-proton and net-kaon $dv_1/dy$ follow a similar trend for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 14.5$ to 200 GeV, but deviate strongly at 7.7 GeV
* All kaons and $\bar{\Lambda}$ show negative $v_1$ slope at all beam energies studied (7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the STAR directed flow measurements from protons and net protons (STAR Collaboration, 2014) to multiple strange hadron species, providing new constraints on the QCD equation of state and the search for a first-order phase transition. The results are compared with UrQMD and HSD model calculations, which qualitatively describe the data at higher beam energies but fail at lower energies. The measurements enable separation of produced versus transported quark contributions through net-particle flow analysis, and the kaon results test predictions of kaon-nucleon potential effects at high baryon density (potential model calculations, 2015).
