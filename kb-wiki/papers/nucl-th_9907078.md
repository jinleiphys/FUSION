# Saturation from nuclear pion dynamics

> We construct an equation-of-state for nuclear matter based on the chiral Lagrangian. The relevant scales are discussed and an effective chiral power expansion scheme, which is constructed to work around the nuclear saturation density, is presented. A realistic equation-of-state is obtained by adjusting one free parameter, when the leading and subleading terms in the expansion are included. The saturation mechanism is due to correlations induced by the one-pion-exchange interaction. Furthermore, we find a substantial deviation from the Fermi-gas estimate of the quark condensate in nuclear matter already at the saturation density.

# Saturation from nuclear pion dynamics

## Key claim
The paper constructs an equation-of-state for nuclear matter where saturation is driven by correlations from one-pion-exchange interactions, but the authors hedge that this result depends on adjusting a single free parameter (the effective coupling constant g = g₀ + g₁) and on the assumption that the density dependence of the coupling constants g₀ and g₁ is weak, which is tested rather than proven.

## Method
The authors develop a chiral power expansion scheme for nuclear matter around saturation density, combining a density expansion with the chiral expansion by identifying the Fermi momentum k_F as a small scale. They compute the energy per particle from an effective Lagrangian with s-wave contact interactions and pion exchange, evaluating diagrams up to chiral order Q⁴ (leading and subleading terms) while treating the short-range part of the one-pion-exchange interaction non-perturbatively through renormalized local two-nucleon vertices. The parquet resummation for the local interaction is assumed to be close to its high-density limit at normal nuclear matter density, allowing the density dependence to be governed primarily by pion dynamics.

## Key numbers
- Saturation Fermi momentum: k_F⁽⁰⁾ ≃ 265 MeV, corresponding to density ρ₀ ≃ 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Binding energy at saturation: 16 MeV per nucleon.
- Incompressibility: κ ≃ 218 MeV, compatible with the empirical value (210 ± 30) MeV from Blaizot.
- Effective coupling constant: g ≃ 3.23 (with g_A ≃ 1.26, m_π ≃ 140 MeV, f_π ≃ 93 MeV) yields the realistic equation-of-state.
- Leading chiral order Q² (kinetic energy) contributes +22.5 MeV to energy per particle at saturation; Q³ terms contribute -93.5 MeV; Q⁴ terms contribute +55.0 MeV.
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: Σ_πN ≃ 45 MeV used in the quark condensate calculation.

## Context
This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Weinberg 1990, Bira 1994) into the nuclear many-body problem, identifying new scales that require modification of the free-space expansion scheme. The saturation mechanism differs from the Skyrme model (where saturation comes from density and velocity dependence of zero-range interactions) and the Walecka model (where it is a relativistic effect), and is instead reminiscent of the Brueckner approach (Bethe 1971) where saturation is largely due to second-order tensor contributions. The results enable computation of the quark condensate in nuclear matter, showing that pionic interaction effects counteract the reduction predicted by the Fermi-gas estimate (Drukarev 1988, Lutz 1992, Cohen 1992), with implications for chiral symmetry restoration at finite baryon density.
