# Continuity equation and local gauge invariance for the N3LO nuclear Energy Density Functionals

> Background: The next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) nuclear energy density functional extends the standard Skyrme functional with new terms depending on higher-order derivatives of densities, introduced to gain better precision in the nuclear many-body calculations. A thorough study of the transformation properties of the functional with respect to different symmetries is required, as a step preliminary to the adjustment of the coupling constants. Purpose: Determine to which extent the presence of higher-order derivatives in the functional can be compatible with the continuity equation. In particular, to study the relations between the validity of the continuity equation and invariance of the functional under gauge transformations. Methods: Derive conditions for the validity of the continuity equation in the framework of time-dependent density functional theory. The conditions apply separately to the four spin-isospin channels of the one-body density matrix. Results: We obtained four sets of constraints on the coupling constants of the N3LO energy density functional that guarantee the validity of the continuity equation in all spin-isospin channels. In particular, for the scalar-isoscalar channel, the constraints are the same as those resulting from imposing the standard U(1) local-gauge-invariance conditions. Conclusions: Validity of the continuity equation in the four spin-isospin channels is equivalent to the local-gauge invariance of the energy density functional. For vector and isovector channels, such validity requires the invariance of the functional under local rotations in the spin and isospin spaces.

## Key claim

The validity of the continuity equation in all four spin-isospin channels of the N3LO nuclear energy density functional is equivalent to the local-gauge invariance of the functional, with the scalar-isoscalar channel requiring standard U(1) gauge invariance and the vector and isovector channels requiring invariance under local rotations in spin and isospin spaces.

## Method

The authors derived conditions for the continuity equation within time-dependent density functional theory by analyzing the time-evolution equation of the one-body density matrix for the N3LO quasilocal functional. They multiplied the Schrödinger-type equation with Pauli matrices in spin and isospin spaces and summed over indices to separate the four spin-isospin channels, then imposed that the pseudopotential terms vanish when setting the two spatial coordinates equal. The resulting linear constraints on the coupling constants were obtained using symbolic programming and recoupling methods for differential operators acting on the two spatial variables.

## Key numbers

- The N3LO functional contains 376 total coupling constants across all orders (zero through sixth), of which 22 are unrestricted, 254 vanish, 20 are independent, and 80 are dependent in the scalar-isoscalar channel.
- In the scalar-isovector channel, 13 coupling constants are unrestricted, 267 vanish, 18 are independent, and 78 are dependent.
- In the vector-isoscalar channel, 10 coupling constants are unrestricted, 290 vanish, 12 are independent, and 64 are dependent.
- In the vector-isovector channel, 4 coupling constants are unrestricted, 293 vanish, 4 are independent, and 75 are dependent.
- For zero-order terms in the vector-isovector channel, the constraints are C_{00,0000}^{0000,1} = (1/sqrt(3)) C_{00,0011}^{0011,1} and C_{00,0011}^{0011,0} = (1/sqrt(3)) C_{00,0011}^{0011,1}.

## Context

This work extends the standard Skyrme functional analysis of the continuity equation, which was explicitly checked for the NLO functional by Engel (2007), to the N3LO functional introduced by Carlsson et al. (2008). The results confirm and generalize the link between the continuity equation and local gauge symmetry established by Blaizot and Ripka (1986) and Dobaczewski et al. (2007), now applied to higher-order derivative terms up to sixth order. The derived constraints on coupling constants are a necessary preliminary step before adjusting the parameters of the N3LO functional for nuclear many-body calculations.
