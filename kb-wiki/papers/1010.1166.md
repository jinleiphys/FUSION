# Critical behavior of Binder ratios and ratios of higher order cumulants of conserved charges in QCD deconfinement phase transition

> Binder liked ratios of baryon number are firstly suggested in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Using 3D-Ising model, the critical behavior of Binder ratios and ratios of high order cumulants of order parameter are fully presented. Binder ratio is shown to be a step function of temperature. The critical point is the intersection of the ratios of different system sizes between two platforms. From low to high temperature through the critical point, the ratios of third order cumulants change their values from negative to positive in a valley shape, and ratios of fourth order cumulants oscillate around zero. The normalized ratios, like the Skewness and Kurtosis, do not diverge with correlation length, in contrary with corresponding cumulants. Applications of these characters in search critical point in relativistic heavy ion collisions are discussed.

## Key claim

Binder-like ratios of baryon number are proposed as a direct observable to locate the QCD critical point in relativistic heavy ion collisions, based on the 3D-Ising model universality class. The authors show that the Binder ratio behaves as a step function of temperature, with the critical point identified as the intersection of curves from different system sizes between the two plateaus, but note that this behavior is demonstrated only in the 3D-Ising model and not yet in experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the 3D-Ising model using the Wolff algorithm for four different lattice sizes. They computed the Binder ratios \(B_3 = \langle M^3\rangle / \langle M^2\rangle^{3/2}\) and \(B_4 = \langle M^4\rangle / \langle M^2\rangle^2\) of the magnetization order parameter \(M\), as well as the skewness, kurtosis, and the un-normalized cumulant ratios \(R_{3,2}\) and \(R_{4,2}\), as functions of reduced temperature near the critical point. Finite-size scaling analysis was used to estimate the critical exponents and the system-size dependence of these observables.

## Key numbers

- The Binder ratio \(B_4\) at high temperature (disordered phase) is 3 times larger than its low-temperature (ordered) plateau value of 1; for \(B_3\) the high-temperature plateau is 1.6 times larger than the low-temperature plateau.
- The third-order cumulant ratio \(R_{3,2}\) diverges with correlation length as \(\xi^{2.5}\).
- The fourth-order cumulant ratio \(R_{4,2}\) diverges with correlation length as \(\xi^{5}\).
- The critical exponents for the third and fourth order susceptibilities are \(\gamma_3/\nu = 4.5\) and \(\gamma_4/\nu = 7\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling analysis of cumulant ratios previously studied in lattice QCD by Karsch et al. (2005) and in effective models by Stokic, Friman, and Redlich (2009) and Fu, Liu, and Wu (2010). The authors argue that the 3D-Ising model belongs to the same universality class as QCD deconfinement, allowing model-independent predictions for the critical behavior of baryon number cumulants. The proposed Binder ratio method enables experimental searches for the QCD critical point at RHIC, SPS, and FAIR by scanning incident energies and looking for the characteristic step-function behavior.
