# High-energy $η^{(\prime)}π$ photoproduction and the nature of exotic waves

> The observation of hybrid mesons in photoproduction experiments can provide essential insight into the inner workings of quantum chromodynamics in the strong coupling regime. In particular, the study of final $η^{(\prime)}π$ states is of great interest due to the presence of the lowest lying hybrid candidate with manifestly exotic quantum numbers, the $π_1(1600)$. In this work, a double-vector exchange model with Reggeized $ρ$ and $ω$ trajectories is developed to describe the photoproduction of $η^{(\prime)}π$ in the high-mass region. Results are presented for the differential cross sections and forward-backward asymmetries in the energy region of interest to the GlueX experiment. The model contains no free parameters, and reproduces the magnitude and $t$-dependence of existing CLAS data at $E_γ=5$\gev. The model predicts a stronger asymmetry in the $η^\prime π$ channel than in the $ηπ$ channel, consistent with what has previously been observed in pion beam experiments. This suggests that the sizeable production of exotic odd waves in $η'π$ is not necessarily related to the presence of gluon-rich environments. Confirmation of these predicted asymmetries from forthcoming GlueX data would enable further predictions of the low-energy spectrum.

## Key claim
The paper develops a double-vector exchange model with Reggeized ρ and ω trajectories for η(′)π photoproduction that contains no free parameters, and finds that the larger forward-backward asymmetry in the η′π channel compared to ηπ persists even without Pomeron exchange, suggesting that sizeable production of exotic odd waves in η′π is not necessarily related to gluon-rich environments.

## Method
The authors constructed a fixed-spin double-vector exchange model for γ + p → η(′) + π + p, considering four configurations of ρ and ω exchanges at the top and bottom vertices. They then Reggeized the amplitudes by replacing vector propagators with full Regge trajectories (slope α′ = 0.9 GeV−2) and including signature factors and a vertex function, with all couplings fixed from independent experimental data on radiative decays, nucleon couplings, and SU(3)F symmetry. The model was compared to CLAS data at Eγ = 5 GeV and used to predict differential cross sections and forward-backward asymmetries at GlueX energies.

## Key numbers
- The model reproduces the magnitude and t-dependence of existing CLAS data at Eγ = 5 GeV
- The ρ and ω Regge trajectories share a common slope α′ = 0.9 GeV−2
- The scale parameter s0 is chosen as 1 GeV2, varied between 0.95 and 1.05 GeV2 for uncertainty estimation
- The γωπ coupling gγωπ/m0 = 0.701, the ωρπ coupling gωρπ/m0 = 14.27, and the η-η′ mixing angle φη = 41.4°
- The nucleon couplings are g1ω = 5.75, g2ω = 1.31, g1ρ = 1.15, g2ρ = 9.2
- The Reggeized amplitudes are suppressed by more than one order of magnitude compared to the fixed-spin exchange model

## Context
This work extends the Regge-based analysis of η(′)π final states from pion beam experiments (COMPASS, 2014) to photoproduction, where Pomeron exchange is forbidden due to the photon's quantum numbers. It provides a theoretical framework for interpreting upcoming GlueX data, complementing recent upper limits on π1(1600) photoproduction cross sections (GlueX, 2024). The model enables future application of analyticity constraints in partial-wave extractions for a more robust determination of the π1 resonance parameters.
