# Correlations between charge radii differences of mirror nuclei and stellar observables

> The correlation between the charge radii differences in mirror nuclei pairs and the neutron skin thickness has been studied with the so-called finite range simple effective interaction over a wide mass region. The so far precisely measured charge radii difference data within their experimental uncertainty ranges in the 34Ar-34S, 36Ca-36S, 38Ca-38Ar, and 54Ni-54Fe mirror pairs are used to ascertain an upper limit for the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy L $\approx$ 100 MeV. This limiting value of L is found to be consistent with the upper bound of the NICER PSR J0740+6620 constraint at 1$σ$ level for the radius R$_{1.4}$ of 1.4 M$_\odot$ neutron stars. The lower bound of the NICER R$_{1.4}$ data constrains the lower limit of L to $\approx$ 70 MeV. Within the range for L = 70-100 MeV the tidal deformability $Λ^{1.4}$ constraint, which is extracted from the GW170817 event at 2$σ$ level, and the recent PREX-2 and CREX data on the neutron skin thickness are discussed.

# Correlations between charge radii differences of mirror nuclei and stellar observables

## Key claim
The charge radii differences measured in four mirror nuclei pairs (34Ar-34S, 36Ca-36S, 38Ca-38Ar, and 54Ni-54Fe) constrain the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy to an upper limit of approximately 100 MeV, while the lower bound from NICER pulsar PSR J0740+6620 data at 1-sigma level sets a lower limit of approximately 70 MeV. This L range of 70-100 MeV is derived specifically within the finite range simple effective interaction (SEI) model and is consistent with the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint at 2-sigma level.

## Method
The authors used the finite range simple effective interaction (SEI) with a Gaussian form factor and a zero-range spin-orbit interaction, employing the Quasi-local Density Functional Theory (QLDFT) to describe finite nuclei. They computed charge radii differences for the four mirror pairs by varying the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy while keeping other nuclear properties stable, then correlated these results with neutron star radii from solving the TOV equations using the SEI equation of state combined with the BPS-BBP crust EOS.

## Key numbers
- The characteristic SEI equation of state has saturation density rho_0 = 0.1584 fm^{-3}, energy per nucleon e(rho_0) = -16 MeV, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, effective mass m*/m = 0.711, symmetry energy E_s(rho_0) = 35.5 MeV, and slope L = 76.71 MeV
- Experimental charge radii differences: 34Ar-34S = 0.082(9) fm, 36Ca-36S = 0.150(4) fm, 38Ca-38Ar = 0.063(3) fm, 54Ni-54Fe = 0.049(4) fm
- The upper limit for L from mirror pair data is approximately 100 MeV; the lower limit from NICER PSR J0740+6620 data is approximately 70 MeV
- The NICER 1-sigma constraint on the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is R_{1.4} = 12.45 +/- 0.65 km
- The tidal deformability Lambda^{1.4} predicted by SEI within L = 70-100 MeV lies in the range 300-560, within the GW170817 2-sigma constraint of 190^{+390}_{-120}
- The linear fit between neutron skin Delta R_np and proton radii difference Delta R_p gives slope a = 0.881 +/- 0.036 and intercept b = -0.049 +/- 0.017 fm
- The PREX-2 result for neutron skin in 208Pb is Delta R_np^{208} = 0.283 +/- 0.071 fm; the CREX result for 48Ca is Delta R_np^{48} = 0.121 +/- 0.026(exp) +/- 0.024(model) fm

## Context
This work extends previous correlations between mirror nuclei charge radii differences and neutron skin thickness found using Skyrme forces (Brown 2017, Gaidarov 2020) and chiral EFT calculations (Sammarruca 2018), now using the SEI model which accurately reproduces charge radii of 86 even-even nuclei and binding energies of 161 even-even nuclei. The derived L range of 70-100 MeV is consistent with the range 106 +/- 37 MeV obtained from PREX-2 analysis with covariant EDFs (Reed 2021) and with the range 70-101 MeV from nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering analyses (Danielewicz 2017). The correlation between charge radii differences in the 54Ni-54Fe mirror pair and neutron star radii enables using this precisely measured terrestrial quantity as an alternative to the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb for constraining neutron star properties, complementing gravitational wave observations from GW170817 and NICER pulsar data.
