# TMDs in the bag model

> Leading and subleading twist transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) are studied in a quark model framework provided by the bag model. A complete set of relations among different TMDs is derived, and the question is discussed how model-(in)dependent such relations are. A connection of the pretzelosity distribution and quark orbital angular momentum is derived.

## Key claim

The bag model yields a complete set of 9 linear and 2 non-linear relations among the 14 T-even twist-2 and twist-3 transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs), but these relations are model-dependent and would not hold in full QCD; the connection between pretzelosity and quark orbital angular momentum is derived at the level of matrix elements of operators, not as an operator identity.

## Method

The authors compute all T-even leading-twist and subleading-twist TMDs in the MIT bag model using SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry for the proton wave function, expressing the quark-quark correlation functions in terms of S-wave and P-wave components of the quark wave functions. They then derive linear and non-linear algebraic relations among the 14 TMDs by treating the five independent momentum-space structures as linearly independent, and compare these relations to results from spectator models, light-cone constituent models, and covariant parton models.

## Key numbers

- The bag model yields 9 linear relations among the 14 T-even twist-2 and twist-3 TMDs, plus 2 independent non-linear relations.
- For spin-independent TMDs of definite flavor, the flavor factor is N_u = 2, N_d = 1; for spin-dependent TMDs, the spin-flavor factor is P_u = 4/3, P_d = -1/3.
- The relation g_1^q(x) - h_1^q(x) = g_T^q(x) - h_L^q(x) involves only collinear PDFs and is the only such relation in the bag model.
- The pretzelosity distribution h_{1T}^{⊥(1)q}(x,p_T) is related to the quark orbital angular momentum L_q^3(x,p_T) by L_q^3(x,p_T) = (-1) h_{1T}^{⊥(1)q}(x,p_T) in the bag model.
- The relations among polarized TMDs (Eqs. IV-IX) are satisfied in both the bag model and the spectator model of Jakob (1997), while relations connecting polarized and unpolarized TMDs (Eqs. I-III) require strong model assumptions and may hold in the valence-x region with an accuracy of 20-30%.

## Context

This work extends previous bag model studies of TMD relations by Jaffe (1991), Signal (1997), and Barone (2001), and provides the first complete set of relations including subleading-twist TMDs. The results enable comparison with spectator models (Jakob 1997), light-cone constituent models (Pasquini 2008), and covariant parton models (Efremov 2009), showing which relations are more model-independent. The connection between pretzelosity and orbital angular momentum may inspire attempts to establish a rigorous connection between TMDs and quark orbital angular momentum in QCD, and the relations can be tested in lattice QCD calculations of Mellin moments, such as those for g_T^q reported by Gockeler (2000).
