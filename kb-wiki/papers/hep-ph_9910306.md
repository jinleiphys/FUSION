# Parton Degrees of Freedom from the Path-Integral Formalism

> We formulate the hadronic tensor $W_{μν}$ of deep inelastic scattering in the path-integral formalism. It is shown that there are 3 gauge invariant and topologically distinct contributions. Besides the valence contribution, there are two sources for the sea -- one in the connected insertion and the other in the disconnected insertion. The operator product expansion is carried out in this formalism. The operator rescaling and mixing reveal that the connected sea partons evolve the same way as the valence, i.e. their evolution is decoupled from the disconnected sea and the gluon distribution functions. We explore the phenomenological consequences of this classification in terms of the small x behavior, Gottfried sum rule violation, and the flavor dependence. In particular, we point out that in the nucleon $\bar{u}$ and $\bar{d}$ partons have both the connected and disconnected sea contributions; whereas, $\bar{s}$ parton has only the disconnected sea contribution. This difference between $\bar{u} + \bar{d}$ and $\bar{s}$, as far as we know, has not been taken into account in the fitting of parton distribution functions to experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the hadronic tensor of deep inelastic scattering can be decomposed into three gauge-invariant and topologically distinct contributions in the path-integral formalism: a valence contribution, a "connected sea" from connected insertions, and a "disconnected sea" from disconnected insertions. The key hedge is that this separation into connected and disconnected sea depends on the momentum frame of the nucleon, although the sum (the full physical hadronic tensor) does not.

## Method

The authors formulate the hadronic tensor Wμν of deep inelastic scattering using Euclidean path-integral four-point functions, where the nucleon is represented by an interpolation field and the currents are inserted at different Euclidean times. They classify the quark skeleton diagrams into three topologically distinct types: connected insertions with a quark propagator between currents (Fig. 1a), connected insertions with an antiquark propagator between currents (Fig. 1b), and disconnected insertions where the currents self-contract (Fig. 1c). The operator product expansion is then carried out in this formalism by Taylor-expanding the quark propagator between currents in the short-distance limit, and the resulting forward matrix elements are separated into connected-insertion and disconnected-insertion parts.

## Key numbers

- The valence number sum rules give ∫₀¹ dx u_v(x, Q²) = 2 and ∫₀¹ dx d_v(x, Q²) = 1 for the proton, with ∫₀¹ dx (q_ds(x, Q²) - \bar{q}_ds(x, Q²)) = 0 for the disconnected sea.
- The momentum fraction ⟨x⟩_{C.I.} from lattice calculations at μ ~ 2 GeV is larger than the experimental valence-only ⟨x⟩_v, indicating the connected sea contributes to the connected-insertion matrix element A_f^{n=even}(C.I.).
- The operator product expansion yields moment sum rules for even n: A_f^{n=even}(C.I.) = ∫₀¹ dx x^{n-1} (q_{v+cs}(x, Q²) + \bar{q}_{cs}(x, Q²)) and A_f^{n=even}(D.I.) = ∫₀¹ dx x^{n-1} (q_{ds}(x, Q²) + \bar{q}_{ds}(x, Q²)).

## Context

This work extends the path-integral classification of parton degrees of freedom previously developed by Liu and Dong (1994, 1999) for hadronic form factors and masses, now applied to deep inelastic scattering. The separation into connected and disconnected sea contributions provides a framework to understand the violation of the Gottfried sum rule (where NMC (1992) found \bar{u} ≠ \bar{d}) as arising solely from the connected sea at the flavor SU(2) limit, and to explain the small quark spin content discovered by EMC (1988). The paper enables future parton distribution function fits to distinguish between \bar{u}+\bar{d} (which has both connected and disconnected sea contributions) and \bar{s} (which has only disconnected sea), a distinction the authors note has not been taken into account in experimental extractions.
