# Isospin Dependence of 1S0 Proton and Neutron Superfluidity in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> We investigate the $^1S_0$ neutron and proton superfluidity in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. We have concentrated on the isospin dependence of the pairing gaps and the effect of a microscopic three-body force. It is found that as the isospin asymmetry goes higher, the neutron $^1S_0$ superfluid phase shrinks gradually to a smaller density domain, while the proton one extends rapidly to a much wider density domain. The three-body force turns out to weaken the neutron $^1S_0$ superfluidity slightly, but it suppresses strongly the proton $^1S_0$ superfluidity at high densities in nuclear matter with large isospin asymmetry.

## Key claim

The isospin dependence of neutron and proton \(^1S_0\) superfluidity in asymmetric nuclear matter is opposite: as isospin asymmetry increases, the neutron superfluid phase shrinks to a smaller density domain while the proton superfluid phase extends to a much wider density domain, but this behavior is strongly modified by a microscopic three-body force (TBF) which suppresses the proton pairing gaps at high densities, especially at large asymmetries.

## Method

The authors solved the standard BCS gap equation for the \(^1S_0\) channel using the Argonne \(V18\) two-body interaction plus a microscopic three-body force (TBF) based on the meson-exchange current approach. Single-particle energies for neutrons and protons in asymmetric nuclear matter were calculated within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach under the continuous choice for the single-particle potential, with the TBF reduced to an effective two-body interaction. The isospin asymmetry parameter \(\beta = (\rho_n - \rho_p)/\rho\) was varied from 0.2 to 0.8.

## Key numbers

- Neutron \(^1S_0\) superfluid phase exists only at low densities (\(\rho \le 0.13\) fm\(^{-3}\)) for all isospin asymmetries, with peak gaps located around \(\rho = 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Without TBF, the neutron superfluid density domain shrinks from \(\rho \le 0.13\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(\beta = 0.2\) to \(\rho \le 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(\beta = 0.8\).
- For protons without TBF, as asymmetry rises from \(\beta = 0.2\) to \(\beta = 0.8\), the density domain extends from \(\rho \le 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) to \(\rho \le 0.435\) fm\(^{-3}\), and peak values lower from about 2.0 MeV to about 1.4 MeV.
- For protons with TBF, the density domain extends from \(\rho \le 0.13\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(\beta = 0.2\) to \(\rho \le 0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(\beta = 0.8\).
- At \(\beta = 0.8\), inclusion of the TBF reduces the proton superfluid density domain by about 50%, from \(\rho \le 0.435\) fm\(^{-3}\) down to \(\rho \le 0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(^1S_0\) nucleon superfluidity in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter (Baldo 1990, Zuo 2002c) to the full range of isospin asymmetries relevant for neutron star interiors. It also incorporates a microscopic three-body force, which earlier work (Grange 1989, Zuo 2002a) had shown to be crucial for reproducing empirical nuclear matter saturation properties. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling and rotational dynamics by providing pairing gaps that depend on both density and isospin asymmetry, including the strong suppression of proton superfluidity by three-body forces at high densities.
