# Particle ratios at RHIC: Effective hadron masses and chemical freeze-out

> The measured particle ratios in central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC-BNL are investigated within a chemical and thermal equilibrium chiral SU(3) σ-ωapproach. The commonly adopted noninteracting gas calculations yield temperatures close to or above the critical temperature for the chiral phase transition, but without taking into account any interactions. Contrary, the chiral SU(3) model predicts temperature and density dependent effective hadron masses and effective chemical potentials in the medium and a transition to a chirally restored phase at high temperatures or chemical potentials. Three different parametrizations of the model, which show different types of phase transition behaviour, are investigated. We show that if a chiral phase transition occured in those collisions, ''freezing'' of the relative hadron abundances in the symmetric phase is excluded by the data. Therefore, either very rapid chemical equilibration must occur in the broken phase, or the measured hadron ratios are the outcome of the dynamical symmetry breaking. Furthermore, the extracted chemical freeze-out parameters differ considerably from those obtained in simple noninteracting gas calculations. In particular, the three models yield up to 35 MeV lower temperatures than the free gas approximation. The in-medium masses turn out differ up to 150 MeV from their vacuum values.

## Key claim

The measured particle ratios at RHIC can be described equally well by a chiral SU(3) model with temperature- and density-dependent effective hadron masses as by a noninteracting gas model, but the extracted freeze-out temperatures are up to 35 MeV lower in the interacting model. If a chiral phase transition occurred in these collisions, freezing of the relative hadron abundances in the chirally symmetric phase is excluded by the data; therefore either very rapid chemical equilibration must occur in the broken phase, or the measured hadron ratios are the outcome of dynamical symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors fit measured particle ratios from central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s)=130 GeV) using a chiral SU(3) sigma-omega model in the mean-field approximation, which predicts temperature- and density-dependent effective hadron masses and effective chemical potentials. Three different parametrizations of the model are investigated: CI (crossover), CII (weak first-order phase transition), and CIII (strong first-order phase transition). The fits are performed by minimizing chi-squared for eight experimental particle ratios, with the strange chemical potential chosen to enforce zero net strangeness, and the results are compared to a noninteracting gas calculation using the same feeding procedure from resonance decays.

## Key numbers

* Freeze-out temperatures: CI (crossover) yields T = 170.8 MeV, CII yields T = 155.0 MeV, CIII yields T = 153.3 MeV, compared to the noninteracting gas (ig_FFM) value of T = 187.6 MeV and the BMRS fit value of T = 174.0 MeV.
* Freeze-out baryon chemical potentials: CI gives mu_B = 48.3 MeV, CII gives 54.6 MeV, CIII gives 51.0 MeV, compared to ig_FFM at 44.1 MeV and BMRS at 46.0 MeV.
* The three chiral models yield up to 35 MeV lower temperatures than the free gas approximation.
* In-medium masses differ up to 150 MeV from their vacuum values; the strongest modifications are for nonstrange baryons (Delta m_i*/m_i approximately 15%), with mesons and strange baryons showing smaller changes (about 10% for Lambda, pi, K*; about 5% for kaons; nearly no change for Xi).
* Chi-squared values for the fits: CI = 5.50, CII = 5.73, CIII = 5.40, compared to ig_FFM = 5.66 and BMRS = 5.7.
* Phase transition temperatures at mu_q = mu_s = 0: CII at 156.3 MeV, CIII first transition at 153.4 MeV and second at 155.5 MeV.
* Correlation lengths at freeze-out: xi_sigma = 0.37 fm (CI), 0.41 fm (CII), 0.40 fm (CIII); xi_zeta = 0.20 fm for all three cases.

## Context

This work extends the standard noninteracting gas model analyses of hadron abundances at RHIC (Braun-Munzinger et al., 2001; Becattini et al., 2000; Rafelski et al., 2001) by incorporating in-medium effects through a chiral SU(3) model previously developed by the authors (Zschiesche et al., 2001; Schramm et al., 2001). The results show that the extracted freeze-out parameters are strongly model-dependent, with the interacting models placing the freeze-out point near or at the phase transition boundary, consistent with the "unified freeze-out condition" of constant energy per particle (E/A approximately 1 GeV) proposed by Cleymans et al. (1998). The finding that freeze-out from the chirally restored phase is excluded enables future work on the dynamics of chiral symmetry breaking and chemical equilibration in heavy-ion collisions, and suggests that comparisons with small collision systems (pp or deuteron collisions) could test the role of correlation lengths in the symmetry breaking process.
