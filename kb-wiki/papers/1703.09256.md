# Probing subnucleon scale fluctuations in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions

> We show that introducing subnucleon scale fluctuations constrained by HERA diffractive $J/Ψ$ production data significantly affects the incoherent diffractive $J/Ψ$ production cross section in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. We find that the inclusion of the additional fluctuations increases the ratio of the incoherent to the coherent cross section approximately by a factor of $2$, and modifies the transverse momentum spectra of the produced $J/Ψ$ at momenta larger than the scale that corresponds to the distance scale of the subnucleonic fluctuations. We present predictions for $J/Ψ$ production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\,{\rm TeV}$ at the LHC and $200\,{\rm GeV}$ at RHIC.

## Key claim

Introducing subnucleon scale fluctuations (geometric "hot spots" and saturation scale fluctuations) constrained by HERA diffractive J/Psi production data increases the ratio of incoherent to coherent diffractive J/Psi production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions by approximately a factor of 2, but this result depends on the choice of vector meson wave function and the model uncertainties are comparable to the effect for absolute cross sections.

## Method

The authors calculate coherent and incoherent diffractive J/Psi production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions using the dipole picture within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. They employ the impact parameter dependent saturation model (IPsat) dipole amplitude with a Monte Carlo method to explicitly average over target configurations, replacing the proton's smooth Gaussian thickness function with a sum of three Gaussian "hot spots" whose positions and saturation scales fluctuate independently, with parameters fitted to HERA diffractive J/Psi data. The dipole-nucleus amplitude is constructed using an independent scattering approximation over nucleon positions sampled from a Woods-Saxon distribution.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of incoherent to coherent cross section increases by approximately a factor of 2 when subnucleonic fluctuations are included
- The incoherent cross section becomes sensitive to subnucleonic fluctuations at |t| greater than about 0.25 GeV^2, corresponding to a distance scale of approximately 0.4 fm
- The hot spot root mean square radius is sqrt(2 B_q) ≈ 0.24 fm, with B_q = 0.7 GeV^{-2} and B_qc = 3.3 GeV^{-2}
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, the integrated coherent cross section with subnucleon fluctuations is 106 μb and incoherent is 62 μb (Boosted Gaussian wave function); without fluctuations, coherent is 121 μb and incoherent is 33 μb
- At midrapidity and sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the ALICE coherent cross section datapoint is overestimated by 1.3 sigma and the incoherent by 2 sigma when using the Gaus-LC wave function with subnucleon fluctuations
- Replacing the Boosted Gaussian by the Gaus-LC parametrization reduces both coherent and incoherent cross sections by approximately 20%

## Context

This work extends the CGC framework of Lappi (2013) by including subnucleon scale fluctuations previously constrained by Mantysaari (2016) using HERA diffractive J/Psi data, and improves over that prior work by using a Monte Carlo method to avoid factorizing the impact parameter dependence. The results explain previous tension in Lappi (2013) where the dipole model overestimated coherent and underestimated incoherent cross sections. The framework enables future studies of energy-dependent proton shape evolution via JIMWLK evolution equations, as discussed in Schlichting (2014) and Cepila (2016).
