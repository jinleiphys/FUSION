# Deduction of $M(Sigma pi)$ for $Lambda(1405)$ with mixed $T_{ Sigma pi leftarrow K^-p} and T_{ Sigma pi leftarrow Sigmapi}$ from Hemingway's data

> We formulated the $Lambda(1405)$ (abbreviated as $Lambda^*$) $rightarrow (Sigmapi)^0$ invariant-mass spectra produced in the $K^- + p rightarrow Sigma^+ (1660) + pi^-$, followed by $Sigma^+ (1660) rightarrow Lambda(1405) + pi^+ rightarrow Sigmapi + pi^+$, processes at $p(K^-) = 4.2$ GeV/$c$, in which both the incident channel for a quasi-bound $K^-p$ state and its decay process to $(Sigma pi)^0$ were taken into account realistically. We calculated $M(Sigma pi)$ spectral shapes using mixed transition matrices, $T_{21} = T_{ Sigmapi leftarrow K^-p}$ and $T_{22} = T_{ Sigma pi leftarrow Sigma pi}$, for various theoretical models involving $Lambda^*$. The asymmetric spectra were compared to old experimental data of Hemingway, and it was found that the mixing of the two channels, written as $(1-f) , T_{21} + f, T_{22}$, gave a better result than considering the individual channels, yielding $f = 0.376_{-0.019}^{+0.021}$, $M(Lambda^*) = 1406.6_{-3.3}^{+3.4}$ MeV/$c^2$ and $Gamma = 70 pm 2$ MeV, nearly consistent with the 2014 PDG values.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the asymmetric invariant-mass spectrum of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance from Hemingway's old data can be reproduced significantly better by a *mixture* of two transition channels, $T_{\Sigma\pi \leftarrow K^-p}$ and $T_{\Sigma\pi \leftarrow \Sigma\pi}$, rather than by either channel alone, yielding a mass and width nearly consistent with the 2014 PDG values but with a notably larger width of $70 \pm 2$ MeV.

## Method

The authors formulated the $\Lambda(1405) \rightarrow (\Sigma\pi)^0$ invariant-mass spectra produced in the reaction $K^- + p \rightarrow \Sigma^+(1660) + \pi^-$, followed by $\Sigma^+(1660) \rightarrow \Lambda(1405) + \pi^+ \rightarrow \Sigma\pi + \pi^+$, at a kaon momentum of $p(K^-) = 4.2$ GeV/$c$. They used a coupled-channel separable-potential model with a Yukawa-type form factor to calculate the transition matrices $T_{21}$ and $T_{22}$, and then constructed a mixed transition matrix as $(1-f) T_{21} + f T_{22}$, where $f$ is a real mixing parameter. The theoretical spectral shapes were compared to the 11 data points of Hemingway via a $\chi^2$ minimization over three free parameters: the mass $M$ and width $\Gamma$ of the $\Lambda(1405)$, and the mixing fraction $f$.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit mixing parameter: $f = 0.376_{-0.019}^{+0.021}$, corresponding to approximately 27% $T_{22}$ and 73% $T_{21}$ contribution.
- Best-fit mass of $\Lambda(1405)$: $M = 1406.6_{-3.3}^{+3.4}$ MeV/$c^2$.
- Best-fit width of $\Lambda(1405)$: $\Gamma = 70 \pm 2$ MeV.
- Minimum $\chi^2$ value: $\chi^2_{\rm MIN} = 3.3$ for $n_{\rm DF} \approx 8$ degrees of freedom.
- The mass range of the spectrum is bounded by the $\Sigma\pi$ threshold at 1328 MeV/$c^2$ and the $K^-p$ threshold at 1432 MeV/$c^2$.
- For comparison, a Breit-Wigner fit to the same data gave $M = 1391 \pm 1$ MeV/$c^2$ and $\Gamma = 32 \pm 1$ MeV, while a $K$-matrix method gave $M = 1411.4 \pm 2.0$ MeV/$c^2$ and $\Gamma = 79.6$ MeV (Dalitz and Deloff).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance by re-examining the classic Hemingway bubble-chamber data from CERN, which had previously been fitted with Breit-Wigner functions, $K$-matrix methods, or single-channel $T_{22}$ transitions, all yielding unsatisfactory results. The authors build on their own prior coupled-channel formalism (Hassanvand 2013) and the Feshbach resonance treatment of the $K^-p$ quasi-bound state (Akaishi and Yamazaki 2002; Esmaili 2010, 2011). The result, with a width of $70 \pm 2$ MeV, is larger than the 2014 PDG value of $50.5 \pm 2.0$ MeV, and the authors note that the chiral two-channel model of Hyodo and Weise (2008) yields $\chi^2 = 326$ for $T_{21}$ and $\chi^2 = 80$ for $T_{22}$, neither agreeing with the data. The finding supports the "deep" $\bar{K}N$ binding scenario relevant to predictions of deeply bound $\bar{K}$ nuclear systems such as $K^-pp$ (Yamazaki 2002; Akaishi and Yamazaki 2007) and to the question of kaon condensation (Kaplan and Nelson; Brown 1994).
