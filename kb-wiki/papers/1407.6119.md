# Toward modelization of quark and gluon transversity generalized parton distributions

> Quark and gluon helicity flip generalized parton distributions (GPDs) encode the information on the nucleon structure in the transversity sector. In order to build a theoretically consistent phenomenological parametrization for these hadronic matrix element within the framework of the dual parametrization of GPDs (or with the equivalent approach of the SO(3) partial waves (PW) expansion with the Mellin-Barnes integral techniques) we establish the set of combinations of parton helicity flip GPDs suitable for the expansion in the cross channel SO(3) PWs.

## Key claim

The authors establish the specific combinations of quark and gluon helicity flip GPDs that are suitable for expansion in cross-channel SO(3) partial waves, which is a necessary step for building theoretically consistent phenomenological parametrizations; the abstract glosses over the fact that the work is purely formal and does not yet provide a complete numerical model.

## Method

The authors analyze the form factor decomposition of Mellin moments of quark and gluon helicity flip GPDs analytically continued to the cross channel (t > 0), projecting out combinations with definite helicity J3 = ±1. They compute the spin-tensor structures for nucleon spinors in the N̅N center-of-mass frame and decompose the matrix elements into partial waves using Wigner d-functions. An alternative covariant method using t-channel resonance exchange contributions is also presented, which recovers the same combinations and additionally yields a simple f2(1270) meson exchange model for gluon helicity flip GPDs.

## Key numbers

- The combinations of quark helicity flip GPDs to be expanded in P'_J(1/ξ) are: τ H̃_T^q − ½ E_T^q and −H_T^q + τ H̃_T^q − ½ E_T^q, where τ ≡ Δ²/(4m²).
- The combinations H_T^q + τ H̃_T^q ± τ Ẽ_T^q are to be expanded in P'_J(1/ξ) + (1 ∓ ξ)/ξ P''_J(1/ξ).
- For the aligned helicity configuration (|λ'−λ| = 0) with J3 = ±1, the angular dependence is governed by d^J_{±1,0}(θ) = (±1) sin θ P'_J(cos θ)/√(J(J+1)).
- For the opposite helicity configuration (|λ'−λ| = 1) with J3 = ±1, the expansion uses d^J_{±1,1}(θ) = [1/(J(J+1))] (1 ± cos θ)[P'_J(cos θ) + cos θ P''_J(cos θ) ∓ P''_J(cos θ)].
- Within DVCS kinematics, cos θ → 1/(ξβ) + O(1/Q²), with β ≡ √(1 − 4m²/t); the authors set β = 1 for massless hadrons.

## Context

This work extends the dual parametrization approach of Polyakov (2002) and the equivalent SO(3) partial wave expansion with Mellin-Barnes integral techniques of Mueller (2005) to the transversity sector, which had previously been treated only for unpolarized GPDs (Diehl, 2001). The selection rules derived coincide with those from the general method of Ji and Lebed (2000). The proposed f2(1270) meson exchange model for gluon helicity flip GPDs parallels the b1 meson exchange model for quark helicity flip GPDs suggested by Enberg (2006), and enables future phenomenological parametrizations for analyzing COMPASS and HERMES data on transverse target spin asymmetries.
