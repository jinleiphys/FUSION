# Relativistic Elastic Differential Cross Sections for Equal Mass Nuclei

> The effects of relativistic kinematics are studied for nuclear collisions of equal mass nuclei. It is found that the relativistic and non-relativistic elastic scattering amplitudes are nearly indistinguishable, and, hence, the relativistic and non-relativistic differential cross sections become indistinguishable. These results are explained by analyzing the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the first order optical potential that was employed in the calculation

## Key claim

The relativistic and non-relativistic elastic scattering amplitudes and differential cross sections are nearly indistinguishable for nuclear collisions of equal mass nuclei when using the first order optical potential, because the relativistic propagator's pole structure becomes similar to the non-relativistic case near the elastic cut and the optical potential decays rapidly away from on-shell momenta.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the transition amplitude using relativistic and non-relativistic kinematics with a first order optical potential that factorizes into nucleon-nucleon transition amplitudes and nuclear density form factors. They derived analytic expressions showing that the only difference between the relativistic and non-relativistic scattering amplitudes is a factor h(k,k'') which equals 2 in the non-relativistic case and approaches 2 near the elastic cut in the relativistic case. Numerical calculations were performed using three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger (LS3D), partial wave (PW), and eikonal (Eik) solution methods.

## Key numbers

- For equal mass systems (e.g., ⁴He + ⁴He, ²⁰Ne + ²⁰Ne, ⁵⁶Fe + ⁵⁶Fe), no significant differences were observed between relativistic and non-relativistic elastic differential cross sections at lab projectile energies of 150, 500, 1000, and 20000 MeV/n.
- For unequal mass systems, the largest relativistic difference occurred in the ⁴He + ⁵⁶Fe reaction at 1 GeV/n lab projectile energy, where the mass difference between projectile and target was largest.
- The non-relativistic case has h(k,k'') = 2, while the relativistic case has h(k,k'') = 1 + sqrt((M² + k''²)/(M² + k²)), which approaches 2 only near the elastic cut.

## Context

This work extends studies of relativistic effects in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation by Pickle (1989), Pickle (1990), Wolfe (1991), and Werneth (2014) to systematically compare relativistic and non-relativistic kinematics for equal mass nucleus-nucleus scattering. The results explain why earlier calculations found negligible relativistic effects for symmetric systems, and they enable the use of non-relativistic codes for equal-mass heavy-ion reactions at energies up to 20 GeV/n without loss of accuracy. The findings contradict the expectation that relativistic kinematics would produce observable differences at high energies for equal mass systems.
