# Quantum Shock Waves and Domain Walls in the Real-Time Dynamics of a Superfluid Unitary Fermi Gas

> We show that in the collision of two superfluid fermionic atomic clouds one observes the formation of quantum shock waves as discontinuities in the number density and collective flow velocity. Domain walls, which are topological excitations of the superfluid order parameter, are also generated and exhibit abrupt phase changes by $π$ and slower motion than the shock waves. The domain walls are distinct from the gray soliton train or number density ripples formed in the wake of the shock waves and observed in the collisions of superfluid bosonic atomic clouds. Domain walls with opposite phase jumps appear to collide elastically.

## Key claim

Quantum shock waves and domain walls form in the collision of two superfluid fermionic atomic clouds, with domain walls appearing as topological excitations of the superfluid order parameter that are distinct from the gray soliton trains observed in bosonic collisions; the authors argue that dissipation is not required for shock wave formation in a unitary Fermi gas, contrary to the interpretation of a recent experiment by Joseph (2011).

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) simulations of colliding unitary Fermi gas clouds, solving Bogoliubov-de Gennes-like equations for quasiparticle wave functions with a density functional parameterized by Quantum Monte Carlo results. The simulations assumed a quasi-two-dimensional geometry with periodic boundary conditions in one direction and a harmonic confining potential in the perpendicular direction, using a time-dependent trapping potential along the collision axis similar to the experiment by Joseph (2011).

## Key numbers

- Shock wave speed in simulation: \(v_{qsw}/v_F \approx 0.25\), compared to experimental value \(v_{qsw}/v_F \approx 0.35\) (agreement within approximately 25-30%)
- The unitary Fermi gas has a condensation energy approximately 20% of the total interaction energy
- Simulation frames shown at times \(t\varepsilon_F = 30, 350, 690\) (where \(\varepsilon_F = k_F^2/2\) with \(\hbar=m=1\))

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum shock waves from dilute Bose gases, where they were interpreted as dispersive shock waves without dissipation (Dutton, 2001; Hoefer, 2006), to the strongly interacting unitary Fermi gas. The authors challenge the interpretation of a recent experiment by Joseph (2011), which modeled shock waves using a hydrodynamic approach with a phenomenological viscosity term, arguing instead that dissipation plays a negligible role at temperatures near absolute zero. The simulations predict domain walls as topological excitations not observed in the experiment due to spatial resolution limitations, and suggest that the experimental technique used to visualize quantum vortex lattices in a unitary Fermi gas (Zwierlein, 2005) could be implemented to observe domain walls.
