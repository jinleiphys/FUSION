# Splitting rates in QCD plasmas from a non-perturbative determination of the momentum broadening kernel $C(q_{\bot})$

> We exploit a recent non-perturbative determination of the momentum broadening kernel $C(b_{\bot})$ in impact parameter space \cite{Moore:2021jwe}, to determine the momentum space broadening kernel $C(q_{\bot})$ in high-temperature QCD plasmas. We show how to use the non-pertubatively determined kernel $C(q_{\bot})$ to compute the medium-induced splitting rates in a QCD plasma of finite size. We compare the resulting in-medium splitting rates to the results obtained with leading-order and next-to-leading order perturbative determinations of $C(q_{\bot})$, as well as with various approximations of the splitting employed in the literature. Generally, we find that the differences in the splitting rates due to the momentum broadening kernel are larger than the errors associated with approximations of the splitting rate.

## Key claim

The authors use a non-perturbative determination of the momentum broadening kernel \(C(b_{\perp})\) in impact parameter space, previously obtained from lattice simulations of Electrostatic QCD (EQCD), to compute the momentum-space kernel \(C(q_{\perp})\) and then the medium-induced splitting rates in a QCD plasma of finite size. The key finding is that differences in splitting rates due to the choice of broadening kernel (non-perturbative vs. perturbative) are larger than the errors associated with various approximations of the splitting rate itself.

## Method

The authors Fourier transform the non-perturbative broadening kernel \(C_{\mathrm{QCD}}(b_{\perp})\) from impact parameter space to momentum space using a Hankel transform of the derivative of the kernel, subtracting the asymptotic infrared behavior to ensure numerical convergence. They then solve the evolution equation for the in-medium splitting rate in a static QCD plasma using a wave-function formalism in dimensionless variables, discretizing momentum on a logarithmic grid and evolving the wave function with an Euler scheme. The resulting rates are compared to those obtained using leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative kernels, as well as to approximations including the opacity expansion (GLV) and a resummed opacity expansion.

## Key numbers

*   Non-perturbative jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}_0 / (g^6 T^3) = 0.1465(78)\) for \(T = 250\) MeV and \(0.185(10)\) for \(T = 500\) MeV.
*   EQCD string tension \(\sigma_{\mathrm{EQCD}} / (g^4 T^2) = 0.2836(10)\) for \(T = 250\) MeV and \(0.2867(10)\) for \(T = 500\) MeV.
*   Strong coupling \(g^2 = 3.725027\) for \(T = 250\) MeV and \(g^2 = 2.763516\) for \(T = 500\) MeV.
*   The infrared limit of the momentum broadening kernel scales as \(C_{\mathrm{QCD}}(q_{\perp}) \sim 2\pi \sigma_{\mathrm{EQCD}} / q_{\perp}^3\) for \(q_{\perp} \ll g^2 T\).
*   The ultraviolet limit of the momentum broadening kernel scales as \(C_{\mathrm{QCD}}(q_{\perp}) \sim C_R g^4 T^3 \mathcal{N} / q_{\perp}^4\) for \(q_{\perp} \gg m_D\), where \(\mathcal{N} = \frac{\zeta(3)}{\zeta(2)} \left(1 + \frac{N_f}{4}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative determination of the momentum broadening kernel by Moore and Schlichting (2021) from impact parameter space to momentum space, enabling calculations for finite-size media. It directly compares the resulting splitting rates to those from leading-order and next-to-leading order perturbative kernels (Arnold, 2008; Caron-Huot, 2008) and to approximations such as the opacity expansion (Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev, 1999) and the resummed opacity expansion (Andres, 2020). The results enable more accurate modeling of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating non-perturbative medium effects.
