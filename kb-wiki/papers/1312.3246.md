# A first look at Bottomonium melting via a stochastic potential

> We investigate the phenomenon of Bottomonium melting in a thermal quark-gluon plasma using three-dimensional stochastic simulations based on the concept of open-quantum systems. In this non-relativistic framework, introduced in [Phys.Rev. D85 (2012) 105011], which makes close contact to the potentials derived in effective field theory, the $b\bar{b}$ system evolves unitarily under the incessant kicks by the constituents of the surrounding heat bath. In particular thermal fluctuations and the presence of a complex potential in the EFT are naturally related. An intricate interplay between state mixing and thermal excitations emerges as we show how non-thermal initial conditions of Bottomonium states evolve over time. We emphasize that the dynamics of these states gives us access to information beyond what is encoded in the thermal Bottomonium spectral functions. Assumptions underlying our approach and their limitations, as well as the refinements necessary to connect to experimental measurements under more realistic conditions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Bottomonium melting in a quark-gluon plasma can be studied via stochastic simulations of an open-quantum system, where the complex effective field theory potential is reinterpreted as a real potential subject to Gaussian thermal noise. The authors caution that their approach is strictly valid only for infinite quark mass and that the finite-mass simulations cannot reach thermal equilibrium, so results are only meaningful at early times (t ≤ 2 fm).

## Method

The authors solve a stochastic Schrödinger equation for the b-bbar wavefunction on a 3D lattice (256³ points, 7.68 fm box, 0.03 fm spacing) using a Crank-Nicolson scheme with time step 7.5×10⁻⁴ fm. The vacuum potential is a Cornell-type potential (string tension σ = (0.42)² GeV, Coulomb coefficient α = 0.1) with string breaking at r_SB = 1.32 fm, and the in-medium potential uses the perturbative hard-thermal-loop (HTL) potential from Laine (2006) at T = 2.33 T_C = 396 MeV. Ten noise realizations are averaged to compute state admixture probabilities c_nn(t) by projecting onto vacuum eigenstates.

## Context

This work extends the open-quantum systems approach introduced by Akamatsu (2011) and connects to the effective field theory potentials derived by Laine (2006). It contrasts with earlier static approaches using thermal spectral functions (Burnier 2007, Mocsy 2007) and with complex potential models (Margotta 2011, Strickland 2011) by preserving real energies for the two-body system. The method enables future studies of non-thermal Bottomonium evolution using non-perturbative potentials from lattice QCD, though the authors note that quantitative comparison to experiment requires refinements including proper treatment of finite-mass effects and momentum drag.
