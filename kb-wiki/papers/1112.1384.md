# Quantum anomaly, universal relations, and breathing mode of a two-dimensional Fermi gas

> In this Letter, we show that the classical SO(2,1) symmetry of a harmonically trapped Fermi gas in two dimensions is broken by quantum effects. The anomalous correction to the symmetry algebra is given by a two-body operator that is well known as the contact. Taking into account this modification, we are able to derive the virial theorem for the system and a universal relation for the pressure of a homogeneous gas. The existence of an undamped breathing mode is associated with the classical symmetry. We provide an estimate for the anomalous frequency shift of this oscillation at zero temperature and compare the result with a recent experiment by [E. Vogt et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 108, 070404 (2012)]. Discrepancies are attributed to finite temperature effects.

## Key claim
The classical SO(2,1) symmetry of a harmonically trapped two-dimensional Fermi gas is broken by quantum effects, with the anomalous correction to the symmetry algebra given by the two-body contact operator; this anomaly induces a frequency shift in the breathing mode that is estimated at zero temperature but may be washed out by thermal fluctuations at finite temperature.

## Method
The author derives the quantum anomaly by computing the commutator of the dilatation operator D with the Hamiltonian H_osc, showing that the trace of the stress tensor receives an anomalous correction proportional to the contact operator I. The renormalization is performed using dimensional regularization (d = 2 - ε) with a modified minimal subtraction scheme, and the scattering amplitude is calculated via ladder diagrams to determine the relation between the bare coupling and the bound state energy E_b. The anomalous frequency shift of the breathing mode is then extracted by fitting Monte Carlo equation-of-state data to a polytropic model and solving the linearized hydrodynamic equations.

## Key numbers
- The anomalous commutator is [D, H_osc] = 2iH - 2iω₀²C + (i/2πm)I, where I is the contact operator.
- The bound state energy is E_b = -4/(m a_2D² e^{2γ_E}), where γ_E is Euler's constant.
- In the BCS limit (ln k_F a_2D ≫ 0), the frequency shift is δω/ω₀ = 1/(4η²) - κ/(2η³) + O(η⁻⁴), where η = ln k_F a_2D and κ = 0.06 ± 0.02.
- In the BEC limit (ln k_F a_2D ≪ 0), the frequency shift is δω/ω₀ = -1/(4η) + O(η⁻² ln η²).
- In the strongly interacting regime (ln k_F a_2D ≈ 0), the predicted shift is of order 10% of the classical breathing mode frequency 2ω₀.
- The experimental data from Vogt et al. (2012) at T = 0.42 T_F show no significant shift from the classical value.

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum anomalies in ultracold gases, previously examined for Bose gases by Olshanii et al. (2010) and for the unitary Fermi gas in three dimensions by Pitaevskii and Rosch (1997). The author provides a field-theoretical derivation of two Tan relations (the virial theorem and pressure relation) for 2D Fermi gases, which were previously obtained by other methods in Refs. [ref:11a, ref:13]. The predicted zero-temperature breathing mode shift can be tested in future experiments at lower temperatures, and the finite-temperature discrepancy with Vogt et al. (2012) motivates further theoretical work on thermal effects.
