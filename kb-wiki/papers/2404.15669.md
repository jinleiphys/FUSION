# The Coupling Constant of the Pseudovector Pion-nucleon Interaction for the Magnetic Moment of Nucleon

> The role of the suppression of the coupling constant in the pseudovector pion-nucleon interaction is examined to account for the calculation of the magnetic moment of nucleon. Among three kinds of the higher-order corrections the vacuum polarization of the pion propagator contributes to the numerical value considerably. On the other hand the lowest-order vertex correction for the pion-nucleon-nucleon vertex does not give large effect in the approximation of the on-shell momenta for nucleons.

# The Coupling Constant of the Pseudovector Pion-nucleon Interaction for the Magnetic Moment of Nucleon

## Key claim
The suppression of the effective coupling constant in the pseudovector pion-nucleon interaction, needed to explain the nucleon magnetic moment, arises primarily from vacuum polarization of the pion propagator (giving a factor of 0.74 at typical momenta), while the lowest-order vertex correction under the on-shell approximation for nucleons contributes only a 1.3% shift (factor 1.013) and is therefore insufficient to account for the required reduction of about 20% from the standard value.

## Method
The author calculates three higher-order corrections to the pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling constant: the vertex correction for the pion-nucleon-nucleon vertex, the vacuum polarization of the pion propagator, and the nucleon self-energy. The vertex correction is evaluated using dimensional regularization with on-shell nucleon momenta, expanded in powers of q²/M². The vacuum polarization is computed from the lowest-order polarization function Π₀(q) and applied via the Dyson equation to modify the pion propagator. The nucleon self-energy is treated through a rationalized form of the renormalized propagator, with coefficients determined from renormalization conditions and applied to the anomalous magnetic moment calculation via the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers
- Vertex correction factor at q² = M²: f₁(M²) = 1.013, indicating only a 1.3% increase in the effective coupling constant.
- Vacuum polarization factor at q² = M²: f₃(M²) = 0.74, representing a 26% reduction in the effective coupling constant.
- Nucleon self-energy factor at p² = 0: f₂(0) = 0.88 for √α(0), giving a 12% reduction.
- Alternative estimate of vacuum polarization factor at average pion momentum: f₃(¯k²) = 0.69 for ¯k² ≈ 1.23 M².
- Coefficient a₁ in the vertex expansion: a₁ = 0 in the m² ≈ 0 approximation; with finite pion mass (λ ≈ 0.02), a₁′ = −0.023.
- Coefficient a₂ in the vertex expansion: a₂ = 1/15 in the m² ≈ 0 approximation.
- Required vertex correction factors from experimental anomalous magnetic moments: f′ = 0.797 and 0.798 to reproduce the pion-current part a = 0.528 and nucleon-current part b = −0.692, respectively.
- Perturbative vertex correction values without suppression: a′ = 0.831 and b′ = −1.086.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous calculation (Kinpara) showing that the pseudovector coupling constant must be reduced by about 20% from the standard value to fit experimental nucleon magnetic moment data. It examines the source of this suppression through next-order perturbative corrections, finding that vacuum polarization of the pion propagator is the dominant mechanism. The polarization function used corresponds to the zero-density limit of nuclear matter calculations by Serot. The self-energy treatment follows the perturbative method of Feldman for handling complex poles. The results enable a field-theoretical understanding of how the pion cloud modifies nucleon electromagnetic properties, with implications for calculations of pion-nucleon scattering and photoproduction cross sections.
