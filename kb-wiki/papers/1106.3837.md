# The three-boson system at next-to-leading order in an effective field theory for systems with a large scattering length

> We analyze how corrections linear in the effective range, r_0, affect quantities in the three-body sector within an effective field theory for short-range interactions. We demonstrate that observables can be obtained straightforwardly using a perturbative expansion in powers of r_0. In particular, we show that two linear-in-r_0 counterterms are needed for renormalization at this order if scattering-length-dependent observables are considered. We exemplify the implications of this result using various three-body observables. Analytic results for the running of the next-to-leading-order portion of the three-body force in this effective field theory are provided. Expressions which incorporate O(r_0) corrections and relate the positions of features observed in three-atom recombination near a Feshbach resonance are presented.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that at next-to-leading order in the effective field theory for short-range interactions, two linear-in-\(r_0\) counterterms are needed for renormalization when scattering-length-dependent observables are considered, correcting an earlier oversight by Hammer and Mehen (2001) who omitted one contribution from the contour integration and concluded only one counterterm was required.

## Method

The authors employ a perturbative expansion in powers of the effective range \(r_0\) within the short-range effective field theory (SREFT) using a Lagrangian with a dynamic dimer field. They compute the next-to-leading order correction to the three-body t-matrix by inserting a single NLO dimer propagator into the modified Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation, and derive expressions for the NLO K-matrix using principal-value integration. The running of the NLO three-body force is analyzed, and analytic results for the renormalization-group evolution of the counterterms are provided.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of binding energies of two successive three-body bound states in the limit of infinite scattering length is approximately 515.
- The constant \(c\) in the leading-order three-body force running is \(c = 0.879\), determined numerically by Braaten (2011).
- The leading-order recombination rate is given by \(\alpha_0 = \frac{512\pi^2}{\sqrt{3}m} \left|\tilde{t}_0\left(0,\frac{2\gamma}{\sqrt{3}};0\right)\right|^2\).
- The NLO recombination rate near the minimum is quadratic in \(r_0\) and purely determined by LO and NLO t-matrices, with higher-order corrections starting at order \(r_0^3\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order Efimov physics derived by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 1999) to include effective range corrections in a perturbative framework. It corrects the analysis of Hammer and Mehen (2001) by showing that an additional three-body counterterm is required when scattering-length-dependent observables are considered, and supersedes the non-perturbative treatment of Platter, Hammer, and Mehen (2008) which was valid only in the limit \(|r_0| \gg \ell\). The results enable systematic NLO predictions for three-atom recombination features near Feshbach resonances and provide relations between observable positions that incorporate \(O(r_0)\) corrections.
