# Small radius inclusive jet production at the LHC through NNLO+NNLL

> The study of hadronic jets and their substructure at hadronic colliders is crucial for improving our understanding of QCD, and searching for new physics. As such, there has been a significant effort to improve their theoretical description. In the small radius limit, inclusive jet production exhibits a universal factorization, enabling the resummation of logarithms which greatly stabilizes theoretical predictions. In this paper, we show how to combine a recently introduced framework for small-$R$ resummation with the STRIPPER subtraction formalism for fragmentation, enabling next-to-next-to-leading order calculations of small-$R$ inclusive jet production for a wide variety of processes at the LHC. We extract the two-loop constants for the jet functions, enabling for the first time next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic resummation matched to next-to-next-to-leading order perturbative calculation. We compare with CMS data for small-$R$ jet production, and find that our results greatly improve the accuracy of the predictions at small-$R$, and stabilize the perturbative convergence and error estimates at larger $R$. Our approach is applicable to a wide class of jet substructure observables exhibiting similar factorization theorems, opening the door to an NNLO jet substructure program at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that combining a recently developed small-radius jet factorization theorem with the STRIPPER subtraction formalism enables, for the first time, next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) resummation matched to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) perturbative calculations for inclusive jet production at the LHC, but only in the small-radius limit where the factorization holds at leading power.

## Method

The authors extend the STRIPPER subtraction framework, originally designed for hadron fragmentation, to handle inclusive jet functions that depend on jet transverse momentum and radius, including distribution-valued functions with delta and plus-distributions. They extract the previously unknown two-loop jet function constants numerically by comparing fixed-order NNLO calculations with the factorized expression in moment space, using multiple cross section channels and double-differential binning in rapidity and partonic transverse momentum to disentangle quark and gluon contributions. The resummation is performed by solving the renormalization group evolution equation for the inclusive jet function iteratively to NNLL accuracy, using the extracted two-loop constants and known splitting kernels.

## Key numbers

* Two-loop jet function constants were extracted numerically at R=0.1, with deviations of 5-10% compared to the NLL threshold expansion approximation.
* The total contribution from the NNLO jet constants to the NNLO+NNLL cross sections is at most 3% of the total, and typically considerably less.
* The fit was performed using rapidity bin edges at {−∞, −8, −6, −4, −2, 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, +∞} and partonic transverse momentum bin edges at {25, 50, 100, 200, 400, 800, 1600, 3200, 6500} GeV for the 13 TeV LHC.
* The central scale for the extraction was chosen as 25 GeV, with variations by factors of two to verify scale independence.

## Context

This work extends the small-radius jet factorization theorem presented by Lee et al. (2024) from NLL in e+e- colliders to NNLL at hadron colliders, and builds upon the STRIPPER subtraction framework developed by Czakon et al. (2010-2024) for NNLO calculations. It enables precision predictions for inclusive jet production at the LHC that were previously unavailable, and the framework is applicable to a wide class of jet substructure observables exhibiting similar factorization theorems, including energy correlator observables inside high-energy jets as studied by Chen et al. (2020), Komiske et al. (2022), and Lee et al. (2022).
