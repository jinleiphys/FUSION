# $^{10}$Be in the Cluster Shell Model

> The Cluster Shell Model (CSM)describes light nuclei in terms of $k-α$ particles and $x$ extra nucleons, in which the extra nucleon move in the deformed field generated by the geometric configuration of $α$-particles. We present the first study for the case $x=2$ nucleons in application to $^{10}Be$ as a cluster of two $α$-particles and two neutrons.

# ¹⁰Be in the Cluster Shell Model

## Key claim
The Cluster Shell Model (CSM) is extended for the first time to the case of two extra nucleons (x=2), applied to ¹⁰Be as a cluster of two α-particles and two neutrons. The authors find good agreement with available experimental data, but note that a more detailed analysis including other forms of residual interaction and transition probabilities will be published elsewhere.

## Method
The authors use the CSM Hamiltonian with a central potential obtained by convoluting a Gaussian density distribution of the α-particles with the nucleon-α interaction, plus a spin-orbit term. Single-particle levels are calculated in the intrinsic frame for a two-α cluster with axial symmetry, using a harmonic oscillator basis. For the two extra neutrons, a pairing residual interaction is added, and the strength G is adjusted to reproduce the excitation energy of the first excited 0⁺ state. The resulting eigenvalues are obtained by diagonalizing the combined Hamiltonian in a basis of the three lowest available single-particle levels above those occupied by the α-particle neutrons.

## Key numbers
- The distance between the two α-particles is β = 1.82 fm, determined from the moment of inertia of the rotational band in ⁸Be.
- Single-particle energies (CSM eigenvalues) for the five lowest eigenstates: 1s₁/₂ (Kᴾ = 1/2⁺) at -10.38 MeV; 1p₃/₂ (1/2⁻) at -3.81 MeV; 1p₃/₂ (3/2⁻) at -1.75 MeV; 1p₁/₂ (1/2⁻) at 0.35 MeV; 1d₅/₂ (1/2⁺) at 1.38 MeV.
- The pairing strength is determined as G = 2.079 MeV, from the excitation energy of the first excited 0⁺ state.

## Context
This work extends the Cluster Shell Model, previously applied to nuclei with one extra nucleon (x=1) such as ⁹Be and ⁹B (k=2) by Caballero and Bijker, ¹³C (k=3), and ²¹Ne and ²¹Na (k=5), to the case of two extra nucleons. The Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) of Bijker and Iachello (2000) successfully described ¹²C as three α-particles and predicted the 5⁻ state before its experimental discovery. This CSM extension enables the study of neutron-rich beryllium isotopes and other kα + 2 systems, with the authors noting that further work including other residual interactions and transition probabilities is forthcoming.
