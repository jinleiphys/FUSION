# Dynamical Relativistic Effects in Breakup Processes of Halo Nuclei

> The continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method is used to study the breakup of weakly-bound nuclei at intermediate energies collisions. For large impact parameters, the Eikonal CDCC (E-CDCC) method was applied. The effects of Lorentz contraction on the nuclear and Coulomb potentials have been investigated in details. Such effects tend to increase cross sections appreciably. We also show that, for loosely-bound nuclei, the contribution of the so-called close field is small and can be neglected.

## Key claim

Dynamical relativistic corrections (Lorentz contraction of nuclear and Coulomb potentials) increase breakup cross sections of halo nuclei by about 15% at 250 MeV/nucleon, but this effect is significant only at forward scattering angles (θ ≲ 0.2°) and is slightly smaller for ¹¹Be than for ⁸B; the contribution from close-field collisions to the Coulomb breakup can be neglected for loosely-bound nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method with the eikonal approximation (E-CDCC) to describe breakup reactions of ⁸B and ¹¹Be projectiles on a ²⁰⁸Pb target at 250 MeV/nucleon. Dynamical relativistic corrections were incorporated by replacing the nonrelativistic Coulomb and nuclear coupling potentials with their Lorentz-contracted forms, using the far-field approximation for Coulomb interactions and the Feshbach-Zabek conjecture for nuclear interactions. Quantum mechanical corrections were added for impact parameters b ≤ 18 fm by replacing the eikonal partial amplitudes with full quantum mechanical CDCC amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical relativistic corrections increase the total breakup cross section by about 15% for both ⁸B and ¹¹Be projectiles on ²⁰⁸Pb at 250 MeV/nucleon
- The proton separation energy of ⁸B used in the calculation is 137 keV
- The maximum relative wave number for discretized continuum states is k_max = 0.66 fm⁻¹ for both ⁸B and ¹¹Be
- The maximum impact parameter used in calculations is 400 fm for both reactions
- The maximum value for the internal coordinate ξ is 200 fm
- Quantum mechanical corrections were applied for impact parameters b ≤ 18 fm for both reactions
- The number of discretized continuum states is 20 for s-waves and 10 for other partial waves (p, d, f) for both projectiles

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Letter (Ogata and Bertulani, 2009) by providing a more detailed analysis of dynamical relativistic effects on double differential breakup cross sections for ⁸B and ¹¹Be on ²⁰⁸Pb at 250 MeV/nucleon. It builds on the CDCC method developed by earlier authors (CDCC, 1986) and the eikonal CDCC formalism (Ogata, 2003; Ogata, 2006), as well as the relativistic Coulomb interaction forms derived by Bertulani (2005) and the Feshbach-Zabek conjecture (Feshbach and Zabek, 1977). The results enable fully relativistic and quantum mechanical coupled-channels calculations of breakup reactions at intermediate energies, which are necessary for extracting reliable B(E1) values and asymptotic normalization coefficients from breakup experiments.
