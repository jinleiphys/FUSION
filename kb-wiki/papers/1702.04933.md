# Algorithm for the asymptotic nuclear coefficients calculations using phase shift data for charged particles scattering

> A new algorithm for the asymptotic nuclear coefficients calculation, which we call the $Δ$-method, is proved and developed. This method was proposed in Ref. [O. L. Ramírez Suárez and J.-M. Sparenberg, arXiv: 1602.04082 [nucl-th] (2016)] but no proof was given. We apply it to the bound state situated near the channel threshold when the Sommerfeld parameter is quite large within the experimental energy region. As a result, the value of the conventional effective-range function $K_l(k^2)$ is actually defined by the Coulomb term. One of the resulting effects is the wrong description of energy behavior of the elastic scattering phase shift $δ_l$ reproduced from the fitted total effective-range function $K_l(k^2)$. This leads to an improper value of the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) value. No such problem arises if we fit only the nuclear term. The difference between the total effective-range function and the Coulomb part at real energies is the same as the nuclear term. Then we can proceed using just this $Δ$-method to calculate the pole position values and the ANC. We apply it to the vertices $^4\rm{He}+ {^{12}\rm{C}}\longleftrightarrow {^{16}\rm{O}}$ and $^3\rm{He}+ {^4\rm{He}}\longleftrightarrow {^7\rm{Be}}$. The calculated ANCs can be used to find the radiative capture reaction cross sections of the transfers to the $^{16}\rm{O}$ bound final states as well as to the $^7\rm{Be}\longleftrightarrow {^7\rm{Be}}$. ## Key claim

The paper proves that the $\Delta$-method, which fits only the nuclear term of the effective-range function rather than the total function, is strictly valid for calculating asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) of bound states in the effective-range expansion framework, and is necessary when the Sommerfeld parameter is large because the conventional method of fitting the total effective-range function leads to an incorrect description of the elastic scattering phase shift and an improper ANC value.

## Method

The authors define the nuclear term $\Delta_l(k^2) = \frac{\pi}{\exp(2\pi\eta)-1}\cot\delta_l$ and fit this function (using polynomial or Pade approximants) to experimental phase shift data in the physical energy region, then analytically continue it to negative energies where the bound-state pole condition $\Delta_l = 0$ is satisfied. The residue of the renormalized scattering amplitude at the pole is extracted and used to compute the nuclear vertex constant and ANC via standard relations. The method is applied to the $^4\mathrm{He}+{^{12}\mathrm{C}}\longleftrightarrow {^{16}\mathrm{O}}$ and $^3\mathrm{He}+{^4\mathrm{He}}\longleftrightarrow {^7\mathrm{Be}}$ systems.

## Key numbers

- For $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ ground state ($J^\pi=0^+$, binding energy 7.162 MeV): ANC = $21.76\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$
- For $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ excited state ($J^\pi=0^+$, binding energy 1.113 MeV): ANC = $405.7\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$
- For $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ state ($J^\pi=1^-$, binding energy 0.045 MeV): ANC = $2.073\times 10^{14}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$
- For $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ state ($J^\pi=2^+$, binding energy 0.245 MeV): ANC = $5.050\times 10^{4}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$
- For $^7\mathrm{Be}$ ground state ($J^\pi=3/2^-$, binding energy 1.587 MeV): ANC = $3.389 \pm 0.093\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$
- For $^7\mathrm{Be}$ excited state ($J^\pi=1/2^-$, binding energy 1.157 MeV): ANC = $2.647 \pm 0.139\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$

## Context

This work extends the effective-range expansion method for ANC calculations, which was previously developed by Orlov et al. and Sparenberg et al., by providing a rigorous proof of the Delta-method proposed by Ramirez Suarez and Sparenberg (2016) without proof. The method addresses a limitation of the conventional effective-range approach: when the Sommerfeld parameter is large (as in the $^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}$ system), the Coulomb term dominates the total effective-range function, making it impossible to correctly reproduce phase shifts or obtain proper ANCs. The results enable more reliable calculation of radiative capture reaction cross sections for the $^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^3\mathrm{He}(^4\mathrm{He},\gamma)^7\mathrm{Be}$ reactions, which are important for stellar nucleosynthesis.
