# Chiral instabilities & the onset of chiral turbulence in QED plasmas

> We present a first principles study of chiral plasma instabilities and the onset of chiral turbulence in QED plasmas far from equilibrium. By performing classical-statistical lattice simulations of the microscopic theory, we show that the generation of strong helical magnetic fields from a helicity imbalance in the fermion sector proceeds via three distinct phases. During the initial linear instability regime the helicity imbalance of the fermion sector causes an exponential growth(damping) of magnetic field modes with right(left) handed polarization, for which we extract the characteristic growth (damping) rates. Secondary growth of unstable modes accelerates the helicity transfer from fermions to gauge fields and ultimately leads to the emergence of a self-similar scaling regime characteristic of decaying turbulence, where magnetic helicity is efficiently transferred to macroscopic length scales. Within this turbulent regime the evolution of magnetic helicity spectrum can be described by an infrared power-spectrum with spectral exponent $κ$ and dynamical scaling exponents $α,β$, which we determine from our simulations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral instabilities in QED plasmas proceed through three distinct phases (linear instability, secondary growth, and turbulent scaling) and provides the first extraction of far-from-equilibrium scaling exponents for the turbulent regime, but the simulations are performed at a coupling strength of \(e^2 N_f = 64\) which is much larger than in single-flavor QED, and the results are obtained close to the chiral limit with \(m \ll \mu_h, T\).

## Method

The authors perform classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations of QED with \(N_f\) flavors of Dirac fermions coupled to U(1) gauge fields, solving the Dirac equation for fermion fields and Maxwell's equations for electromagnetic fields simultaneously on a spatial lattice. The system is initialized as a chirally imbalanced charge-neutral Fermi gas with a helicity chemical potential \(\mu_h\) and temperature \(T/\mu_h = 1/8\), with vacuum initial conditions for the electromagnetic fields. Simulations use tree-level improved Wilson fermions on \(N_s^3 = 32^3, 48^3\) lattices with lattice spacings \(\mu_h a_s = 2/3, 1, 1.25, 1.5\) and a fermion mass \(m a_s = 5 \times 10^{-4}\).

## Key numbers

* Maximal growth rate for right-handed magnetic field modes in the linear instability regime: \(\gamma_0 \approx 0.07 \mu_h\) at momentum \(|\mathbf{p}| \approx 0.5 \mu_h\)
* Secondary growth rates are enhanced by a factor of 2-3 relative to \(\gamma_0\), i.e., \(\gamma_{\text{secondary}} \sim (2-3) \gamma_0\)
* Saturation of exponential growth occurs at \(\mu_h t_{\text{sat}} \approx 300\)
* Spectral exponent for the magnetic helicity spectrum in the turbulent regime: \(\kappa = 10.2 \pm 0.5\)
* Dynamical scaling exponents: \(\alpha = 1.14 \pm 0.50\), \(\beta = 0.37 \pm 0.13\)
* Initial axial charge density of fermions: \(n_5(t=0) \approx 0.039 \mu_h^3 N_f\)
* Total energy density transferred to electromagnetic fields: \(e^2 \varepsilon_{\text{tot}} \approx 0.033 \, e^2 N_f \mu_h^4\)
* Reference time for transition to turbulent regime: \(\mu_h t^* \approx 375\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral instabilities in QED plasmas that used magneto-hydrodynamics (Campanelli 2007, Brandenburg 2014, Tuchin 2014), compact and non-compact lattice QED simulations (Buividovich 2015, Figueroa 2017, 2019), kinetic theory (Manuel 2015, Dvornikov 2015), linearized perturbation theory (Kojima 2019), and effective action approaches (Grabowska 2014, Kaplan 2016), which had reached different conclusions about the detailed mechanisms. The results enable future investigations of the universality of the scaling exponents by varying the coupling strength \(e^2 N_f\) and exploring dissipative effects from finite fermion mass, as well as comparisons with analogous dynamics in non-Abelian gauge theories relevant to the Chiral Magnetic Effect in QCD.
