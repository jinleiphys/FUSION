# Pion photoproduction in the delta resonance region

> We have updated our multipole analyses to incorporate new data from the low-energy and delta resonance regions. We note a slight decrease in our estimate of the delta photo-decay amplitudes. This agrees with results found in Compton scattering. Our values are also in good agreement with a previous RPI determination. We have reexamined our determinations of the E2/M1 ratio at the resonance energy and at the pole. We find an E2/M1 ratio (at the pole) which is in good agreement with recent the Mainz value.

# Pion photoproduction in the delta resonance region

## Key claim

The updated multipole analysis yields Delta(1232) photo-decay amplitudes about 4% lower than previous VPI determinations, now agreeing with results from Compton scattering and an earlier RPI determination. The E2/M1 ratio at the pole agrees with the Mainz value, but the resonance-energy value remains slightly lower than some recent determinations, suggesting the discrepancy at the resonance point is not yet resolved.

## Method

The authors performed a multipole analysis of pion photoproduction data, updating their previous global solution (SP97) to incorporate new total cross section measurements from MacCormick et al. (1996) and new Mainz data. The pi-N interaction was parameterized using the pion center-of-mass momentum rather than center-of-mass energy to enforce proper threshold behavior across charge channels. The E2/M1 ratio was extracted both at the resonance energy (from the ratio Im E_{1+}^{3/2} / Im M_{1+}^{3/2}) and at the pole (using speed-plot methods and analytic extrapolation of the T-matrix).

## Key numbers

- VPI Delta photo-decay amplitudes: A_{1/2} = -135 +/- 5 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}) and A_{3/2} = -250 +/- 8 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), representing about a 4% decrease from previous VPI values of -141 +/- 5 and -261 +/- 5
- E2/M1 ratio at the resonance energy (VPI): -1.5 +/- 0.5%, compared to the Mainz value of -2.5 +/- 0.2 +/- 0.2% (Beck et al., 1997)
- E2/M1 ratio at the pole (VPI): -0.034 +/- 5 - i 0.055 +/- 5, compared to the Mainz pole value of -0.035 - i 0.046 (Hanstein et al., 1996)
- Single-energy solutions (SES) for E2/M1 at resonance ranged between approximately -1.5% and -1.9% depending on bin size (10 MeV vs 25 MeV bins)
- The low-energy analysis (W500) gave E2/M1 = -1.2% at resonance, with overall chi^2/data = 193/182 for the Mainz cross section and Sigma data

## Context

This work extends the VPI group's previous multipole analyses (Arndt, Strakovsky, Workman, 1996) by incorporating new data from MacCormick et al. (1996) and the Mainz group (Beck et al., 1997). The updated photo-decay amplitudes now agree with the Compton scattering analysis of Peise et al. (1996) and the RPI determination of Davidson, Mukhopadhyay, and Wittman (1991). The pole value for E2/M1 agrees with the dispersion-relation analysis of Hanstein, Drechsel, and Tiator (1996), supporting the view that this quantity is relatively model-independent. The remaining discrepancy between the VPI resonance-energy value (-1.5%) and the Mainz resonance value (-2.5%) motivates proposed cross-checks: extrapolating the Mainz pole value to the resonance point, and applying speed-plot methods to the Mainz multipoles to extract a pole value.
