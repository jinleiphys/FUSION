# Space-time evolution of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions and hadronic spectra

> The space-time evolution of the hot and dense matter formed after the collisions of heavy nuclei at ultra-relativistic energies is investigated using (3+1) dimensional hydrodynamical models. The effects of the spectral shift of the hadronic properties are incorporated in the equation of state (EOS) of the evolving matter. In-medium shift of hadronic properties are considered for Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and universal scaling scenarios. It is found that the EOS for the hadronic matter for universal scaling of hadronic masses (except pseudoscalar mesons) is similar to the recent lattice results. We observe that the space-time volume of the hadronic matter at the freeze-out is considerably different from the one when medium effects on the hadrons are ignored. The sensitivity of the results on the initial radial velocity profile is investigated. The transverse mass spectra of pions and protons of NA49 collaboration are analyzed.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the transverse mass spectra of pions and protons measured by the NA49 collaboration can be reproduced by both a QGP initial state and a hadronic initial state, provided that in-medium modifications of hadronic masses are incorporated into the equation of state. However, due to various uncertainties, it is not possible to determine which equation of state is actually realized in such collisions.

## Method
The authors solved (3+1) dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion, using the flux corrected transport algorithm. The equation of state incorporated in-medium hadronic mass shifts from two scenarios: Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and universal scaling of hadronic masses (except pseudoscalar mesons) with the form m_H*/m_H = (1 - T^2/T_c^2)^λ. The Cooper-Frye formula was used to compute single-particle transverse momentum spectra from the freeze-out surface at T_F = 120 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature for QGP initial state: 196 MeV (for dN_π/dy = 700, τ_i = 1 fm/c)
- Initial temperature for vacuum mass hadronic initial state: 245 MeV
- Initial temperature for QHD hadronic initial state: 220 MeV
- Initial temperature for universal scaling hadronic initial state (T_c = 200 MeV): 205 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature used in all calculations: 120 MeV
- Initial energy density for QGP formation scenario: 2.6 GeV/fm^3
- Initial thermalization time assumed: τ_i ∼ 1 fm/c
- For RHIC energies, initial temperature assumed: ∼ 300 MeV with thermalization time 0.5 fm/c
- Exponent λ in universal scaling formula: λ = 1/2

## Context
This work extends previous hydrodynamic analyses of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions by incorporating in-medium hadronic mass modifications into the equation of state, following the universal scaling scenario of Brown and Rho (1991) and Quantum Hadrodynamics approaches. The authors had previously analyzed WA98 photon data (Alam et al., 2001) and CERES/NA45 dilepton data (Sarkar et al., 2001) using similar in-medium effects. The paper enables a unified description of hadronic spectra, photon, and dilepton data at CERN SPS energies with consistent initial conditions and freeze-out parameters, indicating a thermal source with initial temperature ∼ 200 MeV.
