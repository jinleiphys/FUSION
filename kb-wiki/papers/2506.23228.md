# Direct proton transfer on $^{46}$Ar supports the presence of a charge density bubble linked to a novel nuclear structure below $^{48}$Ca

> The $^{46}$Ar($^3$He,d)$^{47}$K reaction was performed in inverse kinematics using a radioactive $^{46}$Ar beam produced by the SPIRAL1 facility at GANIL and a cryogenic $^{3}$He target. The AGATA-MUGAST-VAMOS setup allowed the coincident measurement of the $γ$ rays, deuterons and recoiling $^{47}$K isotopes produced by the reaction. The relative cross sections towards the proton-addition states in $^{47}$K point towards a depletion of the $πs_{1/2}$ shell. The experimental findings are in good agreement with ab initio calculations, which predict that $^{46}$Ar exhibits a charge density bubble associated with a pronounced proton closed-shell character.

## Key claim

The measured relative cross sections of the 46Ar(3He,d)47K reaction indicate a depletion of the πs1/2 shell in 46Ar, which the authors interpret as evidence for a charge density bubble. The claim is hedged by noting that the experimental findings are in good agreement with ab initio calculations that *predict* such a bubble, and that the bubble is inferred through indirect evidence rather than directly measured.

## Method

The 46Ar(3He,d)47K proton-transfer reaction was performed in inverse kinematics using a radioactive 46Ar beam at 9.9 MeV/u from the SPIRAL1 facility at GANIL impinging on a cryogenic 3He target. The AGATA gamma-ray tracking array, MUGAST silicon detector array, and VAMOS magnetic spectrometer were used in coincidence to detect gamma rays, deuterons, and recoiling 47K isotopes. The angular distributions of emitted deuterons were fitted with finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations to extract spectroscopic factor ratios for different orbital angular momentum transfers (ℓ=0, 2, 3), and the excitation energy spectrum was reconstructed from deuteron kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor ratio C²S(ℓ=2)/C²S(ℓ=0) = 0.10 ± 0.10_stat ± 0.03_sys, indicating strongly suppressed ℓ=2 transfer
- Spectroscopic factor ratio C²S(ℓ=3)/C²S(ℓ=0) = 1.10 ± 0.15_stat ± 0.17_sys
- Ratio of yields Y(ℓ=3)/Y(ℓ=0) = 1.15(42) from the excitation energy spectrum
- Experimental 3-point mass difference 2Δ⁽³⁾_Exp = 5.55(14) MeV for 46Ar, compared to ab initio result of 2.59(28) MeV
- Ab initio charge radius of 46Ar: 3.462(48) fm, close to experimental value of 3.4377(44) fm
- Depletion factor F = (ρ_max − ρ_r=0)/ρ_max = 32(5)% for the charge density of 46Ar
- Ab initio B(E2; 0⁺ → 2⁺) value of 35(1) e²fm⁴, compared to experimental value of 43(4) e²fm⁴

## Context

This work extends the search for charge density bubbles in exotic nuclei, following the pioneering study of 34Si by Mutschler (2016) and Duguet (2017) which inferred a similar bubble from a depleted 1s1/2 orbital. The results contradict standard shell-model calculations using the SDPF-U interaction, which predict an open-shell configuration with mixed 1s1/2 and 0d3/2 occupancies and overestimate the B(E2) value. The findings enable future direct measurements of charge density distributions in unstable isotopes using electron scattering techniques, as demonstrated by Tsukada (2017) with SCRIT, and could reveal other regions of unconventional nuclear structure.
