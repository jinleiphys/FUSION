# Algebraic models for shell-like quarteting of nucleons

> Algebraic models are proposed for the description of the shell-like quarteting of the nucleons both on the phenomenologic and on the semimicroscopic levels. In the previous one the quartet is considered as a structureless object, while in the latter one its constituents are treated explicitely. The excitation spectrum is generated by the SU(3) formalism in both cases. An application to the $^{20}$Ne nucleus is presented.

## Key claim

The paper proposes two algebraic models for shell-like quarteting of nucleons, but the key hedge is that the simpler phenomenological model (PAQM) produces states that are "very highly excited" and thus "hard to find a well-established correspondence between the experimental and model states," whereas the semimicroscopic model (SAQM) is presented as the more viable approach for direct comparison with experiment.

## Method

The author constructs two algebraic models based on the SU(3) formalism of Elliott. In the phenomenological algebraic quartet model (PAQM), structureless quartets occupy harmonic oscillator shells with an excitation quantum approximately four times that of the nucleon shell model, and the model space is built from U(3) group generators. In the semimicroscopic algebraic quartet model (SAQM), the four nucleons of each quartet are treated explicitly with [4] permutational symmetry and [1,1,1,1] spin-isospin symmetry, truncating the L-S coupled no-core shell model space. Both models use the U(3) group chain U(3) superset SU(3) superset SO(3) to generate the excitation spectrum, with physical operators expressed in terms of group generators.

## Key numbers

* The harmonic oscillator term in the SAQM Hamiltonian uses a strength of hbar omega = 13.19 MeV, obtained from systematics.
* The best-fit SAQM Hamiltonian parameters for Ne-20 are: a = -1.065 MeV, b = -0.000360 MeV, d = 0.808 MeV.
* The E2 transition operator parameter alpha is fitted to the experimental value of the 2+_1 to 0+_1 transition of 20.3 Weisskopf units in Ne-20.
* The PAQM excitation quantum is approximately (hbar omega)_q approx 4 (hbar omega), where hbar omega is the nucleon shell model oscillator quantum.

## Context

This work extends the quartet shell model concepts of Arima (1970) and Satpathy (1972) by providing an algebraic framework for calculating detailed spectra, similar to the group-theoretical approach of the interacting boson type quartet models of Dukelsky (1982) and Iachello (1983). The semimicroscopic model is based on Harvey's (1973) quartet definition and is practically a symmetry-dictated truncation of the L-S coupled no-core shell model, focusing on the spin-isospin-zero sector. The application to Ne-20 demonstrates the model's ability to describe experimentally identified bands, and the author suggests the approach could be extended to the mass region A=92-100 of current experimental interest.
