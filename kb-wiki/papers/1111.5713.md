# Temperature dependence of QGP viscosity over entropy ratio from hydrodynamical analysis of ALICE data in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> Within Israel-Stewart's theory of dissipative hydrodynamics, we have analyzed ALICE data for the centrality dependence of charged particles multiplicity, elliptic flow and $p_T$ spectra in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions and obtained the temperature dependence of the QGP viscosity over the entropy ratio ($\etas$). If temperature dependence of $\etas$ is parameterized as $\etas=α\frac{T-T_c}{T_c}+\frac{1}{4π}$, experimental data favor $α$ in the range 0-0.2. $α\geq 0.4$ is not favored by the data. Experimental data in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=200 GeV Au+Au collisions however, prefer $α$=0.4.

# Temperature dependence of QGP viscosity over entropy ratio from hydrodynamical analysis of ALICE data in √sNN=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions

## Key claim

ALICE data from Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN=2.76 TeV favor a temperature-independent or weakly temperature-dependent QGP shear viscosity over entropy ratio (η/s), with the linear temperature-dependence parameter α in the range 0–0.2, while the same analysis applied to RHIC Au+Au data at √sNN=200 GeV prefers α=0.4, indicating that a unique linear temperature-dependent η/s cannot simultaneously describe both collision energies.

## Method

The author solved Israel-Stewart's second-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code, assuming boost-invariance and initializing the fluid at τi=0.6 fm with energy density proportional to the binary collision number density from a Glauber model. The QGP viscosity over entropy ratio was parameterized as η/s = α(T−Tc)/Tc + 1/(4π), with three values α=0, 0.2, and 0.4, and smoothly joined at Tc=174 MeV to a hadronic resonance gas viscosity from Noronha-Hostler (2008). The central energy density was the only free parameter, fixed to reproduce the charged particle multiplicity in 0–5% central collisions, and particle spectra were computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription at freeze-out temperature TF=130 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Central energy density required to reproduce 0–5% multiplicity in Pb+Pb collisions: εi=135±7 GeV/fm³ for α=0, 78±4 GeV/fm³ for α=0.2, and 49±2 GeV/fm³ for α=0.4.
- Corresponding central temperatures: Ti=541±7 MeV (α=0), 473±6 MeV (α=0.2), and 422±4 MeV (α=0.4).
- QGP viscosity over entropy ratio of the central fluid: 0.08 (α=0), 0.43 (α=0.2), and 0.96 (α=0.4).
- For RHIC Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, central energy densities: 32±2 GeV/fm³ (α=0), 22±2.0 GeV/fm³ (α=0.2), and 16±1 GeV/fm³ (α=0.4); central temperatures: 380±6 MeV, 345±6 MeV, and 319±5 MeV respectively.
- In Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, increasing α from 0 to 0.4 reduced simulated elliptic flow at pT=2 GeV in 10–20% collisions by approximately 50%; at RHIC the reduction was approximately 20%.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic analyses of QGP viscosity by incorporating temperature-dependent η/s, contrasting with most earlier studies that assumed a constant value. It directly compares with Niemi (2011), who used a fixed lattice-QCD-based temperature-dependent η/s, and with Song (2011) and Shen (2011), who concluded that temperature dependence could not be constrained from spectra and elliptic flow alone. The present analysis uses a pure hydrodynamic approach rather than the hybrid VISHNU model of Song (2011), and finds that ALICE data favor weaker temperature dependence than RHIC data, suggesting that a simple linear parameterization is insufficient to describe both collision energies simultaneously. The results enable future work to explore more complex temperature dependencies or to incorporate additional initial condition variations.
