# Barrier modification in sub-barrier fusion reactions using Wong formula with Skyrme forces in semiclassical formalism

> We obtain the nuclear proximity potential by using semiclassical extended Thomas Fermi (ETF) approach in Skyrme energy density formalism (SEDF), and use it in the extended $\ell$-summed Wong formula under frozen density approximation. This method has the advantage of allowing the use of different Skyrme forces, giving different barriers. Thus, for a given reaction, we could choose a Skyrme force with proper barrier characteristics, not-requiring extra ``barrier lowering" or ``barrier narrowing" for a best fit to data. For the $^{64}$Ni+$^{100}$Mo reaction, the $\ell$-summed Wong formula, with effects of deformations and orientations of nuclei included, fits the fusion-evaporation cross section data exactly for the force GSkI, requiring additional barrier modifications for forces SIII and SV. However, the same for other similar reactions, like $^{58,64}$Ni+$^{58,64}$Ni, fits the data best for SIII force. Hence, the barrier modification effects in $\ell$-summed Wong expression depends on the choice of Skyrme force in extended ETF method.

## Key claim

The barrier modification effects required in the extended ℓ-summed Wong formula depend on the choice of Skyrme force used in the semiclassical extended Thomas Fermi (ETF) approach with frozen densities; for the ⁶⁴Ni+¹⁰⁰Mo reaction, the GSkI force fits the fusion-evaporation cross section data exactly without additional barrier modifications, while SIII and SV forces require them, but for ⁵⁸,⁶⁴Ni+⁵⁸,⁶⁴Ni reactions the SIII force gives the best fit.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear proximity potential using the semiclassical extended Thomas Fermi (ETF) approach within the Skyrme energy density formalism (SEDF) for three Skyrme forces (SIII, SV, and GSkI). They use the frozen density approximation (sudden without exchange effects) and incorporate deformations up to hexadecapole (β₂–β₄) and orientation degrees of freedom, then apply the ℓ-summed Wong formula with Hill-Wheeler transmission coefficients to calculate fusion-evaporation cross sections for the ⁶⁴Ni+¹⁰⁰Mo reaction.

## Context

This work extends the ℓ-summed Wong formula approach of Kumar et al. (2009) and Bansal et al. (2010) by replacing the standard Blocki proximity potential with a Skyrme-force-dependent nuclear potential from the ETF method. It builds on the density-dependent M3Y interaction with repulsive core used by Misicu and Esbensen (2006) to describe sub-barrier fusion hindrance, and on the dynamical cluster-decay model of Gupta et al. (2008, 2010) where barrier lowering arises naturally. The results enable selecting the appropriate Skyrme force for a given reaction to avoid empirical barrier modifications, and suggest that no single Skyrme force works universally for all sub-barrier fusion reactions.
