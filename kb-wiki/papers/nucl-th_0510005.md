# What can we learn from hydrodynamic analysis of elliptic flow?

> We can establish a new picture, the perfect fluid sQGP core and the dissipative hadronic corona, of the space-time evolution of produced matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. It is also shown that the picture works well also in the forward rapidity region through an analysis based on a new class of the hydro-kinetic model and is a manifestation of deconfinement.

# What can we learn from hydrodynamic analysis of elliptic flow?

## Key claim
A new picture of space-time evolution in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC emerges: a perfect fluid strongly coupled quark gluon plasma (sQGP) core surrounded by a dissipative hadronic corona. This picture works well even in forward rapidity regions when analyzed with a hydro-kinetic model, but the agreement of ideal hydrodynamic simulations with chemical equilibrium assumptions in the hadron phase is merely an accidental reproduction of data that neglects particle ratio constraints.

## Method
The author systematically compared hydrodynamic simulations under three different assumptions for the hadron phase, chemical equilibrium (CE) fluid, chemically frozen (partial chemical equilibrium, PCE) fluid, and non-equilibrium resonance gas via hadronic cascade models (HC), against experimental data for differential elliptic flow v2(pT), transverse momentum spectra, and particle ratios. A hybrid dynamical model was then developed, combining fully three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the QGP phase with the JAM hadronic cascade model for the hadron phase, with a switching temperature of Tsw = 169 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Switching temperature from hydrodynamic to kinetic description: Tsw = 169 MeV
- Critical temperature and chemical freezeout temperature in the hydrodynamic model: Tc = Tch = 170 MeV
- Shear viscosity of both QGP and hadron phases around Tc: η ∼ 0.1 GeV/fm²
- The minimum possible value of η/s (shear viscosity to entropy density ratio) in the QGP phase is comparable to 1/4π

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic analyses of elliptic flow at RHIC (Huovinen 2003, Ackermann 2000, Adcox 2002, Back 2002) by demonstrating that the common assumption of chemical equilibrium in the hadron phase leads to accidental agreement with data. The author develops a hybrid model building on the earlier "CGC+hydro+jet" framework (Hirano and Nara) and the JAM hadronic cascade model (Nara). The key insight, that the perfect fluid property of sQGP arises from a sudden increase in entropy density rather than a reduction in viscosity, connects to lattice QCD predictions of rapid entropy density increase near the QCD critical temperature and enables future extraction of transport properties of QGP from experimental data.
