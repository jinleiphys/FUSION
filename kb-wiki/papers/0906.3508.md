# Extended Skyrme interaction (II): ground state of nuclei and of nuclear matter

> We study the effect of time-odd components of the Skyrme energy density functionals on the ground state of finite nuclei and in nuclear matter. The spin-density dependent terms, which have been recently proposed as an extension of the standard Skyrme interaction, are shown to change the total binding energy of odd-nuclei by only few tenths of keV, while the time-odd components of standard Skyrme interactions give an effect that is larger by one order of magnitude. The HFB-17 mass formula based on a Skyrme parametrization is adjusted including the new spin-density dependent terms. A comprehensive study of binding energies in the whole mass table of 2149 nuclei gives a root mean square (rms) deviation of 0.575 MeV between experimental data and the calculated results, which is slightly better than the original HFB-17 mass formula. From the analysis of the spin instabilities of nuclear matter, restrictions on the parameters governing the spin-density dependent terms are evaluated. We conclude that with the extended Skyrme interaction, the Landau parameters $G_0$ and $G_0^\prime$ could be tuned with a large flexibility without changing the ground-state properties in nuclei and in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The newly proposed spin-density dependent terms, added as an extension of the standard Skyrme interaction, change the total binding energy of odd nuclei by only a few tenths of keV, which is an order of magnitude smaller than the effect from the time-odd components of standard Skyrme interactions; this allows the Landau parameters G0 and G0′ to be tuned with large flexibility without altering ground-state properties in nuclei or nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations for odd nuclei using the "one-spin polarized approximation" (OSPA) to maximize the spin-density and its effects, applying the SLy5 Skyrme parameterization with additional spin-density dependent terms added perturbatively. They then performed a global adjustment of the BSk17 Skyrme force (renamed BSk17st) by refitting the Skyrme and pairing parameters to the entire mass table of 2149 nuclei, including the new spin-density dependent terms. The ground state of infinite nuclear matter was analyzed for spin-polarization stability, and restrictions on the parameters governing the spin-density dependent terms were evaluated from the Landau parameters and the energy of polarized versus non-polarized matter.

## Key numbers

- The root mean square (rms) deviation between experimental data and calculated binding energies for the whole mass table of 2149 nuclei is 0.575 MeV for the adjusted BSk17st force, slightly better than the original HFB-17 rms deviation of 0.581 MeV.
- For the odd nuclei 41Ca and 49Ca, the difference in total energy with and without the spin-density dependent terms (ΔE) is less than 50 keV.
- The total correction from the time-odd terms of the standard Skyrme interaction in 41Ca and 49Ca is of the order of 0.15–0.3 MeV, larger than the effect of the new spin-density terms.
- The new BSk17st force uses parameters t3s = 4×10^4 MeV fm^4, t3st = 3×10^4 MeV fm^4, x3s = -0.5, x3st = -3, and γs = γst = 2, yielding Landau parameters G0 = -0.03 and G0′ = 0.99 in symmetric matter at saturation density.
- The barrier height for the ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter at ρ ≈ 0.19 fm^-3 is about 10 MeV per nucleon for the BSk17st force.
- The parameter x3s is restricted to the range -1 ≲ x3s < 1 to stabilize spin-symmetric matter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal of spin-density dependent terms (Margueron et al., 2008) to remove ferromagnetic instabilities in Skyrme interactions, and applies them to the HFB-17 mass formula of Goriely et al. (2007) based on the BSk17 force. The study enables more realistic tuning of the spin and spin-isospin channels of Skyrme forces, which is relevant for describing Gamow-Teller resonances, magnetic dipole states, and astrophysical applications such as neutron star crust properties and beta-decay half-lives for r-process nucleosynthesis.
