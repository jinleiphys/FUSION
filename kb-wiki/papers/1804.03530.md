# Heavy-quark dynamics in a hydrodynamically evolving medium

> In this talk we will discuss the recent advances in describing heavy-quark dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), which evolves hydrodynamically. Special emphasis is put on the collective flow of the heavy-quarks with the medium constituents, for which we present our latest results obtained within the MC@sHQ+EPOS2 model at $\sqrt{s}=5$~TeV.

# Heavy-quark dynamics in a hydrodynamically evolving medium

## Key claim
The MC@sHQ+EPOS2 model, when tuned to reproduce central nuclear modification factor data at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, shows that a purely collisional energy loss mechanism (with K=1.5) is favored over a collisional plus radiative scenario (with K=0.8) for describing D meson elliptic and triangular flow at sqrt(s)=5 TeV, though the authors note that the radiative contribution may be missing finite path length effects and uses a fixed coupling.

## Method
Heavy quarks are propagated through a 3+1 dimensional ideal fluid dynamical medium from EPOS2 initial conditions using either a Fokker-Planck/Langevin approach or a Boltzmann equation. The in-medium interaction is modeled via collisional energy loss with running coupling in a one-gluon-exchange approximation, optionally supplemented by radiative energy loss with an effective LPM reduction; hadronization proceeds via coalescence at T_c=155 MeV combined with fragmentation.

## Key numbers
- The purely collisional scenario uses an overall scaling factor K=1.5, fitted to reproduce central sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV R_AA data in the intermediate p_T range.
- The collisional plus radiative scenario uses K=0.8, fitted to the same sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV data.
- Lattice QCD calculations yield a spatial diffusion coefficient D_s in the range (2-7)/(2 pi T).
- The hadronization temperature is set to T_c=155 MeV.
- The analysis is performed at sqrt(s)=5 TeV for PbPb collisions at the LHC.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous predictions for triangular flow of heavy quarks at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV (Nahrgang 2014) to the higher LHC energy of 5 TeV. It compares with preliminary CMS collaboration data for D_0 meson flow (CMS 2016). The Bayesian model-to-data analysis approach of Xu (2017) at Duke provides a complementary method for constraining the spatial diffusion coefficient, which the authors note lies at the lower end of the range covered by models. The results suggest that precise measurements of triangular flow v_3 provide additional constraints on the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient beyond those from elliptic flow v_2 alone.
