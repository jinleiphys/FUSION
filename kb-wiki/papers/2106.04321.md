# $\mathbf{{}^{12}{C} + {}^{12}{C}}$ Fusion $\boldsymbol{S^*}$-factor from a Full-microscopic Nuclear Model

> The ${}^{12}\mathrm{C} + {}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ fusion reaction plays a vital role in the explosive phenomena of the universe. The resonances in the Gamow window rule its reaction rate and products. Hence, the determination of the resonance parameters by nuclear models is indispensable as the direct measurement is not feasible. Here, for the first time, we report the resonances in the ${}^{12}\mathrm{C} + {}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ fusion reaction described by a full-microscopic nuclear model. The model plausibly reproduces the measured low-energy astrophysical $S$-factors and predicts the resonances in the Gamow window. Contradictory to the hindrance model, we conclude that there is no low-energy suppression of the $S$-factor.

## Key claim
Using a full-microscopic antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model without adjustable parameters, the authors report the first description of resonances in the Gamow window of the 12C + 12C fusion reaction. Contrary to the hindrance model of Jiang (2007), they conclude there is no low-energy suppression of the astrophysical S-factor, instead predicting enhanced S-factor peaks inside the Gamow window.

## Method
The authors employed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S nuclear density functional to describe the 12C + 12C and alpha + 20Ne channels, including the compound nucleus 24Mg. They superposed channel wave functions with angular momentum projection to describe resonances, calculated reduced width amplitudes (RWAs) for four decay channels (p0, p1, alpha0, alpha1), and evaluated the modified astrophysical S*-factors using R-matrix theory with Breit-Wigner resonance shapes.

## Key numbers
- Three resonances predicted inside the Gamow window: a 2+ state at 0.93 MeV, a 0+ state at 0.94 MeV, and a 2+ state at 1.50 MeV
- The 0+ resonance at 0.94 MeV has a reduced width amplitude (RWA) in the CC channel of 7.3 x 10^-2 (ratio to Wigner limit)
- The 2+ resonance at 2.18 MeV is a candidate for the experimentally observed 2.14 MeV resonance; the 2+ resonance at 3.73 MeV is a candidate for the 3.8 MeV resonance
- Calculated S*-factor magnitude in the p1 channel above the Gamow window (E > 2.0 MeV) is of order 10^14 to 10^15 MeV-barn
- Isoscalar transition matrix elements for all resonances are comparable to or exceed 1 Weisskopf unit (e.g., 1.56 W.u. for the 2+ resonance at 0.93 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the AMD approach previously applied to 12C + 16O and alpha + 28Si systems (Kimura 2004, Taniguchi 2009, Taniguchi 2020, Kimura 2020) to the 12C + 12C fusion reaction. It contradicts the hindrance model of Jiang (2007) which predicts reduced S-factor at low energy, and instead supports the indirect measurement of Tumino (2018) which reported numerous narrow resonances in the Gamow window. The calculation enables quantitative predictions for the 24Mg(alpha,alpha')24Mg* reaction as a bypass to access deep sub-barrier resonances, building on experimental work by Youngblood (2009), Kawabata (2013), Gupta (2015), and Adsley (2021).
