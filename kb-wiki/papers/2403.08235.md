# First identification of a doublet wobbling excitation mode in $^{105}$Pd

> An experimental investigation of $^{105}$Pd has revealed, for the first time, the existence of two wobbling bands, both having one phonon configuration and originating from excitation which is the wobbling from the yrast band with the $h_{11/2}$ quasineutron fully aligned with the short axis, and from an excited band with the same quasineutron but with less alignment along the short axis. These observations have been drawn from the measured ratios of the inter-band and intra-band gamma transition rates. Model calculations based on the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach have been performed and are found to be in good agreement with the experimental energies and relative transition probabilities. The analysis of the TPSM results provides an insight into the nature of the observed structures at a microscopic level.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first identification of a doublet wobbling excitation mode in a nucleus, where two distinct wobbling bands with one-phonon configuration are observed in $^{105}$Pd; one originates from wobbling of the yrast band with the $h_{11/2}$ quasineutron fully aligned with the short axis, and the other from an excited band with the same quasineutron but with less alignment along the short axis, contrary to the earlier speculation that the second band was a double wobbling excitation.

## Method

High-spin states of $^{105}$Pd were populated via a fusion-evaporation reaction using a 63 MeV $^{13}$C beam on a $^{96}$Zr target, and deexciting gamma rays were detected with the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) consisting of 18 Compton-suppressed clover detectors. Gamma-ray multipolarities and mixing ratios were determined using the Ratio of Directional Correlations from Oriented states (R$_\mathrm{DCO}$) method and linear polarization measurements. Triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) calculations were performed and compared with experimental energies and transition probabilities to interpret the observed band structures.

## Key numbers

- Two new gamma transitions were observed: 253.6(5) keV (23/2$^-$ to 21/2$^-$) and 299.6(5) keV (27/2$^-$ to 25/2$^-$) connecting Band 4 to Band 2.
- Mixing ratios for the 254 and 300 keV transitions were measured as 0.3$^{+0.2}_{-0.1}$ for both.
- B(E2)$_\mathrm{out}$/B(E2)$_\mathrm{in}$ ratios for Band 3 to Band 1 transitions: 0.49(15) at 991.1 keV (17/2$^-$ to 15/2$^-$), 0.39(10) at 1033.8 keV (21/2$^-$ to 19/2$^-$), and 0.57(17) at 993.8 keV (25/2$^-$ to 23/2$^-$).
- B(E2)$_\mathrm{out}$/B(E2)$_\mathrm{in}$ ratios for Band 4 to Band 2 transitions: 1.23(75) at 253.6 keV (23/2$^-$ to 21/2$^-$) and 1.45(98) at 299.6 keV (27/2$^-$ to 25/2$^-$).
- Mixing ratios for the 991, 1034, and 994 keV transitions between wobbling bands were determined as 1.9$^{+0.4}_{-0.5}$, 2.2$^{+0.5}_{-0.4}$, and 2.4$^{+0.8}_{-0.5}$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of transverse wobbling (TW) mode in odd-A nuclei, previously reported in nuclei such as $^{163}$Lu (Hagemann et al., 2001), $^{135}$Pr (Garg et al., 2011), and $^{105}$Pd (Karmakar et al., 2023). The authors had earlier speculated that the second $\alpha=-1/2$ band in $^{105}$Pd might be a double TW excitation by analogy to $^{135}$Pr (Sensharma et al., 2020), but the present detailed measurements of mixing ratios and comparison with TPSM calculations demonstrate it is instead a $n=0$ band with reduced projection $j_s=7/2$, with the second $\alpha=1/2$ band being the TW excited on it. This finding enables a more complete understanding of wobbling modes in triaxial nuclei with odd quasiparticles and provides a microscopic interpretation of the band structures.
