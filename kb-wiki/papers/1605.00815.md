# Transverse pion structure beyond leading twist in constituent models

> The understanding of the pion structure as described in terms of transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) is of importance for the interpretation of currently ongoing Drell-Yan experiments with pion beams. In this work we discuss the description of pion TMDs beyond leading twist in a pion model formulated in the light-front constituent framework. For comparison, we also review and derive new results for pion TMDs in the bag and spectator models.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic study of pion transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) beyond leading twist in three constituent models, showing that internally consistent descriptions are possible despite the absence of explicit gluon degrees of freedom, but with the important caveat that none of these models accounts for the pion's nature as a Goldstone boson associated with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors computed the T-even pion TMDs at twist-2, twist-3, and twist-4 in three constituent frameworks: the light-front constituent model (LFCM), the MIT bag model, and the spectator model. For the LFCM, they used a phenomenological light-front wavefunction with parameters fitted to the pion charge radius and decay constant; for the bag model, they used massless quarks confined in a spherical cavity; for the spectator model, they employed an effective pion-quark-spectator vertex with the spectator mass set equal to the constituent quark mass. They tested internal consistency by checking sum rules, positivity inequalities, equations-of-motion relations, and quark-model Lorentz-invariance relations.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass in LFCM: \(m_q = 0.250\) GeV, with \(\beta = 0.3194\) GeV (fitted to pion charge radius and decay constant)
- Bag model uses massless quarks, but a constituent quark mass of \(m_q \sim 120\) MeV is natural from the constituent picture
- In the spectator model, valence quarks carry \(\frac{2}{3}\) of the pion's momentum in a specific parametric limit (the "\(\frac{2}{3}\)-paradox")
- The LFCM satisfies the valence number sum rule \(\int dx\,f_1^q(x) = N_q\) and momentum sum rule \(\sum_q\int dx\,x f_1^q(x) = 1\) by construction
- The bag model satisfies the sum rule \(\int dx\,f_4^q(x) = N_q/2\) (Eq. f4-sum-rule)
- The LFCM yields the relation \(x\,e^q(x,p_T) = (m_q/m_\pi)\,f_1^q(x,p_T)\) with vanishing tilde terms

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of nucleon higher-twist PDFs and TMDs (Lorce, Pasquini, Schweitzer, 2014) to the pion case, focusing on the peculiar aspects of modeling 2-body \(q\bar q\) dynamics versus the 3-body dynamics in the nucleon. The results are relevant for interpreting ongoing Drell-Yan experiments with pion beams at COMPASS (CERN) and earlier Fermilab and CERN experiments (Falciano 1986, Guanziroli 1987, Conway 1989). The study enables future phenomenological analyses of subleading-twist TMDs in pion-induced Drell-Yan processes, complementing a study of twist-2 pion TMDs in a chiral Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Noguera (2015).
