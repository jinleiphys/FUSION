# Hard Thermal Loops from Transport Processes

> This paper discusses attempts to numerically compute the effects of hard thermal loops in non-abelian gauge theories at finite temperature by means of solutions of Heinz' transport equation for an ensemble of classical colored particles interacting with a real-time lattice gauge field. Results for the real-time response functions and the Chern-Simons number diffusion rate in the unbroken SU(2) guage theory are presented.

# Hard Thermal Loops from Transport Processes

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the dynamics of hard thermal loops (HTL) in non-abelian gauge theories at finite temperature can be reproduced numerically by solving Heinz' transport equation for an ensemble of classical colored particles interacting with a real-time lattice gauge field, with the caveat that the method relies on a test-particle approximation that becomes exact only in the limit of an infinite number of test charges.

## Method
The authors replace the nonlocal HTL effective action with a thermal bath of classical, non-abelian charged particles obeying Wong's equations, which describe the motion of colored point particles in an external gauge field. The continuous phase-space distribution is approximated by a sum over test particles, and the Yang-Mills equation is formulated on a spatial lattice with cells of size a^3, where each test particle's charge is associated with the lattice site of its containing cell. The system is evolved using a leap-frog algorithm that conserves phase-space measure, with electric fields acting on particles only when they transit between cells and magnetic fields acting at each time step.

## Key numbers
- Plasma frequency: ω_p^2 = (1/3)(1 + N_f/2) g^2 T^2 for the continuum theory at low momenta (k → 0)
- Transverse gluon effective mass: m*_g^2 = (3/2) ω_p^2
- Energy conservation achieved at the level of 10^-5; Gauss' law conserved up to 10^-10
- Simulations performed on lattices as large as 32^3 with several hundred test particles per cell
- Plasma damping rate γ_p for k = 0: γ_p = 0.176 g^2 T (theory), with numerical values in agreement
- Plasma frequency scaling: ω_p = 0.39 gQ √(⟨n⟩/T) (theory), confirmed by numerical results
- Chern-Simons number diffusion rate scaling: Γ_CS ∝ (g^2 T)^4, with additional suppression factor g^2 as predicted by Arnold, Son, and Yaffe

## Context
This work extends the perturbative understanding of hard thermal loops in finite-temperature gauge theories, which were previously described by the effective action of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) and Taylor and Wong (1990). The numerical approach confirms the Arnold-Son-Yaffe scaling relation for Chern-Simons number diffusion, which predicts suppression beyond the naive (g^2 T)^4 scaling due to strong damping of space-like gauge field modes by HTL dynamics. The method enables future studies of gauge field configurations far off equilibrium, such as those occurring in relativistic heavy ion reactions, and generalization to a non-Abelian Boltzmann equation that includes hard interactions among colored particles.
