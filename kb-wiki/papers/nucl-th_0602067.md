# The CFL phase and m_s: An effective field theory approach

> We study the phase diagram of dense quark matter with an emphasis on the role of the strange quark mass. Our approach is based on two effective field theories (EFTs). The first is an EFT that describes quark quasi-particles near the Fermi surface. This EFT is valid at energies small compared to the chemical potential. The second is an EFT for the Goldstone modes in the paired phase. We find that in response to a non-zero strange quark mass the CFL phase first undergoes a transition to a kaon condensed phase, and then to a gapless phase with a non-zero Goldstone boson current.

## Key claim

The CFL phase of dense quark matter, when perturbed by a non-zero strange quark mass, first transitions to a kaon condensed phase and then to a gapless phase with a non-zero Goldstone boson current, with the chiral expansion breaking down when \(m^2 \sim p_F \Delta\).

## Method

The author employs a two-step effective field theory (EFT) approach. First, a high-density EFT describes quark quasi-particles near the Fermi surface, valid at energies small compared to the chemical potential. Second, this theory is matched to a chiral EFT for the Goldstone modes in the paired CFL phase, where the mass dependence is constrained by chiral symmetry and the effective Lagrangian includes derivative terms, mass terms, and four-fermion operators matched from perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

- The gap in the CFL phase is smaller than in the 2SC phase by a factor \(2^{-1/3}\).
- The condensation energy in the CFL phase is favored over the 2SC phase by a factor \((27/4)^{1/3} \simeq 1.89\).
- The critical strange quark mass for kaon condensation is \(m_s(\text{crit}) = 3.03 \cdot m_d^{1/3} \Delta^{2/3}\), yielding \(m_s(\text{crit}) \simeq 70\) MeV for \(\Delta \simeq 50\) MeV.
- The pion decay constant in the CFL phase is \(f_\pi^2 = \frac{21-8\log(2)}{18} \left(\frac{p_F^2}{2\pi^2}\right)\) and the pion velocity is \(v_\pi^2 = \frac{1}{3}\).
- The electron chemical potential at leading order in \(m_s^2/p_F^2\) is \(\mu_e \simeq \frac{m_s^2}{4p_F}\left(1 - \frac{4\alpha_s}{\pi} \log\left(\frac{2p_F}{m_s}\right)\right)\).
- The gap on the Fermi surface for two-flavor QCD is \(\Delta_0 \simeq 2\Lambda_{BCS} \exp\left(-\frac{\pi^2+4}{8}\right) \exp\left(-\frac{3\pi^2}{\sqrt{2}g}\right)\), with \(\Lambda_{BCS}=256\pi^4(2/N_f)^{5/2}g^{-5}\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the study of the CFL phase from the symmetric massless case (Alford 1999, Schafer 1999, Evans 1999) to include the effects of a non-zero strange quark mass, which is essential for applications to compact stars. The effective field theory approach provides a systematic alternative to Dyson-Schwinger equations used by Alford (2002), Neumann (2002), and Steiner (2002), and enables the calculation of the phase diagram including kaon condensation (Kaplan and Reddy 2001) and eta meson condensation (Kryjevski, Kaplan, and Schafer 2005). The results enable future studies of the phase structure of dense quark matter at densities relevant to neutron star interiors.
