# Effects of extremely strong magnetic field on photon HBT interferometry

> We discuss potential attainability by the Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) interferometry of photons to probe the spacetime geometry of the primordial plasma created by ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. A possible effect to distort the HBT image is due to an interaction between emitted photon and an extremely strong magnetic field induced by the colliding heavy nuclei in the peripheral collisions. We examine the effects of variation of the refraction index in the strong magnetic field that is called the vacuum birefringence.

## Key claim

The vacuum birefringence induced by an extremely strong magnetic field in non-central heavy-ion collisions will distort photon Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) images, meaning that the correlation function is no longer a simple Fourier transform of the static source distribution; this distortion must be accounted for to correctly extract the early-time source geometry.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop photon polarization tensor in a constant external magnetic field, which yields two distinct refractive indices for the two physical photon polarization modes (vacuum birefringence). They then incorporate these refractive indices into the eikonal approximation for photon propagation, deriving a modified HBT correlation function that includes a phase distortion term and an extinction coefficient. To demonstrate the effect, they model the refractive index as a Gaussian spatial profile with parameters σ_x = 10 fm, σ_y = 5 fm, and δn = 0.5, and compare the HBT correlation functions for a Gaussian source with and without the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength at RHIC is |eB| ~ 10^4 MeV^2 ~ m_π^2, which is 10^5 times higher than the electron's critical magnetic field |eB_c| = m_e^2 = 0.25 MeV^2.
- The model uses a Gaussian refractive index profile with σ_x = 10 fm, σ_y = 5 fm, and δn = 0.5.
- The photon energy in the lowest-Landau-level approximation is taken just below the e⁺e⁻ decay threshold at ω²/4m_e² = 0.99.
- The magnetic field strength used in the lowest-Landau-level approximation is B/B_c = 100.

## Context

This work extends the study of nonlinear QED effects in strong magnetic fields, previously recognized in heavy-ion collisions by Kharzeev (2007) and numerically simulated by Skokov (2009), to the domain of photon HBT interferometry. The distortion mechanism is analogous to the distortion of pion HBT images in the presence of a mean-field cloud, as formulated by Hama and collaborators. The results enable future experimental measurements of photon HBT to be correctly interpreted, potentially providing a probe of the pre-equilibrium stages of heavy-ion collisions.
