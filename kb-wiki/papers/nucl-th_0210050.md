> Corrected version of the report for Eleventh Inter. Symp. on Capture Gamma-Ray Spectroscopy and Rel. Topics(Prague, 2-6 September, 2002); misprints are corrected

# Comparison of Analytical Methods of E1 Strength Calculations in Middle and Heavy Nuclei

## Key claim
The MLO (modified Lorentzian) and GFL (generalized Fermi liquid) models are recommended as the best simple analytical models for E1 gamma-decay strength function calculations, but only for energies below about 4.5 MeV do they show clearly closer agreement with experimental data than the EGLO and SLO methods; at higher energies the differences are less pronounced.

## Method
The authors compared four analytical models (SLO, EGLO, GFL, and three variants of MLO) for the E1 radiative strength function by calculating gamma-decay strengths across 16 nuclei for E1 transitions and 30 nuclei for combined E1+M1 transitions. They used chi-squared criteria to evaluate agreement with experimental data from the literature, employing both global parameter sets and nucleus-specific optimal parameters for the damping widths in the MLO models.

## Key numbers
- The MLO and GFL models show closer agreement with experimental data than EGLO and SLO at energies epsilon_gamma less than approximately 4.5 MeV.
- The global parameter set recommended for MLO models uses F = 1, k_s = 0.3, n_s = 0.1.
- The ratio R = f_E1(B_n) / f_M1(B_n) at neutron binding energy B_n approximately 7 MeV is given by R = 0.0588 * A^0.878.
- The wall formula value for fragmentation width is Gamma_w = 36.43 * A^(-1/3) MeV.
- The particle-hole energy E_0 is taken as the harmonic oscillator energy hbar*omega_0 = 41 / A^(1/3) MeV.
- The GFL model uses Landau parameters F'_0 = 1.49 and F'_1 = -0.04, giving K_GFL = 0.63.
- The free-space neutron-proton cross section near the Fermi surface is sigma_f(np) = 5 fm^2.

## Context
This work extends the comparison of analytical E1 strength models beyond the standard SLO (Brink-Axel Lorentzian) and EGLO (enhanced generalized Lorentzian) approaches, which were known to underestimate low-energy gamma-ray spectra (Popov, 1982) and overestimate integral data like capture cross sections in heavy nuclei (Lone, 1986; Mc, 1981). The MLO models (Plujko, 1999-2001) incorporate a detailed-balance scaling factor and include both collisional and fragmentation damping components, while the GFL model (M. Danos, 2000) adds a dipole-quadrupole interaction term for the fragmentation width. The results enable more reliable statistical calculations of gamma-decay strengths and extraction of GDR parameters for heated nuclei from emission data, and also provide a method to estimate M1 strengths using the E1/M1 ratio at the neutron binding energy.
