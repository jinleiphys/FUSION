# Differences between heavy and light quarks

> The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation shows that there are distinct differences between light and heavy quarks. The dynamical mass function of the light quarks is characterised by a sharp increase below 1 GeV, whereas the mass function of the heavy quarks is approximately constant in this infrared region. As a consequence, the heavy-meson masses increase linearly with the current-quark masses, whereas the light pseudoscalar meson masses are proportional to the square root of the current-quark masses.

# Differences between heavy and light quarks

## Key claim

The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation reveals a qualitative distinction between light and heavy quarks: light-quark mass functions exhibit a sharp infrared enhancement below 1 GeV due to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, whereas heavy-quark mass functions remain approximately constant in that region because their current-quark masses are much larger than the QCD scale. This difference causes heavy-meson masses to increase linearly with current-quark mass while light pseudoscalar meson masses follow a square-root dependence.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) numerically using a ladder-approximation truncation, with an effective interaction that combines an integrable infrared singularity (represented by a delta-function) with an ultraviolet tail incorporating asymptotic freedom. The resulting quark propagator solutions were then used as input to the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for pseudoscalar meson bound states, preserving the axial-vector Ward identity. Meson masses and decay constants were computed for increasing current-quark masses spanning the up/down, strange, charm, and bottom flavours.

## Key numbers

- Chiral condensate (renormalisation-point-independent): \(-\langle \bar q q \rangle^0 = (0.227\ \text{GeV})^3\)
- Current-quark masses at renormalisation point \(\mu = 20\ \text{GeV}\): \(m_{u/d} = 3.7\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_s = 82\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_c = 0.59\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_b = 2.0\ \text{GeV}\)
- Ratio of Euclidean constituent-quark mass to current-quark mass \(M^E / m(\mu)\): 150 for u/d, 10 for s, 2.2 for c, 1.4 for b, approaching 1 for t
- Light meson mass regime: \(m_H \propto \sqrt{\hat m_q}\) for \(\hat m_q < 0.15\ \text{GeV}\) (approximately \(\hat m_s\))
- Heavy meson mass regime: \(m_H \propto \hat m_q\) for \(\hat m_q > 0.3\ \text{GeV}\) (approximately \(2\,\hat m_s\))

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework previously applied to light mesons by Roberts and Williams (1994) and Maris and Roberts (1997) into the heavy-quark sector, demonstrating that the same truncation captures both chiral symmetry breaking and the transition to heavy-quark behaviour. The results enable a unified description of pseudoscalar mesons from pions through kaons, D mesons, and B mesons within a single nonperturbative approach, and support the phenomenological approximation of replacing heavy-quark mass functions by a constant as used by Ivanov et al. (1997). The paper also confirms that confinement (absence of a Lehmann representation) applies equally to heavy and light quarks.
