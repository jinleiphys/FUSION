# Unsupervised machine learning correlations in EoS of neutron stars

> Neutron stars are compact objects of large interest in the nuclear astrophysics community. The extreme conditions present in such systems impose big challenges to our current microscopic models of nuclear structure. Equation of states (EoS) are frequently derived from sophisticated quantum mechanical models, such as: relativistic, non-relativistic and many mean-field approaches. Every single model, in general, contains many parameters such as the NN interaction strength, particle compositions, etc. These are particular features of each model and can be represented by numbers and categories in a machine learning context. Different choices of features will affect EoS properties leading to different macroscopic properties of the star. In this work we analyze a selection of EoS containing a variety of different physics models. One of our objectives is to develop tools that enable a better understanding of the correlations among the different model features and the outcome produced by them when employed to model neutron stars.

# Unsupervised machine learning correlations in EoS of neutron stars

## Key claim
Unsupervised machine learning techniques (PCA, MDS, t-SNE) can reveal hidden correlations among different neutron star equations of state based on their physical features, but the clustering patterns show no clear correlation with maximum stellar mass, only with composition and relativistic versus non-relativistic approach.

## Method
The authors applied three dimensionality reduction algorithms, Principal Component Analysis (PCA), Multidimensional Scaling (MDS), and t-distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (t-SNE), to a selected set of 16 equations of state from the literature. The EoS were characterized by their composition (nucleonic, hyperon, meson, quark), model type (variational, BHF, EDF, mean-field, etc.), and approach (relativistic or non-relativistic), and the algorithms projected these high-dimensional features into two-dimensional visualizations to identify clustering patterns.

## Key numbers
- 16 equations of state were analyzed, spanning compositions including npem (nucleonic in beta-equilibrium), meson, hyperon, and quark models
- The t-SNE algorithm used PCA with 6 principal components as input
- Maximum masses ranged across the EoS sample, with blue representing less massive stars and yellow representing the most massive stars in the MDS-PCA projection (Fig. 1)
- The EoS set includes models with densities from a few g/cm³ at the surface to more than 10¹⁵ g/cm³ at the center

## Context
This work extends the ongoing effort to constrain the neutron star equation of state, which remains uncertain despite constraints from nuclear physics experiments and astrophysical observations such as the LIGO-VIRGO binary neutron star merger (Abbott 2017). Previous studies have attempted to constrain EoS parameters near nuclear saturation density (Dutra 2012, 2014; Lourenco 2019) or using gravitational wave observations (Radice 2018; Motta 2019; Margalit 2017; Bauswein 2017). The unsupervised ML approach demonstrated here enables visualization of correlations among the many existing EoS models (over 500 Skyrme and RMF parameterizations exist) and could help categorize models for future use with data from advanced detectors like VIRGO-LIGO-KAGRA and eXTP.
