# Kinetics of Chiral Phase Transitions in Quark Matter

> We study the kinetics of chiral transitions in quark matter using a microscopic framework (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) and a phenomenological model (Ginzburg-Landau free energy). We focus on the coarsening dynamics subsequent to a quench from the massless quark phase to the massive quark phase. The morphology of the ordering system is characterized by the scaling of the order-parameter correlation function. The domain growth process obeys the Allen-Cahn growth law, $L(t)\sim t^{1/2}$. We also study the growth of bubbles of the stable massive phase from the metastable massless phase.

## Key claim
The domain growth process after a quench from the massless to the massive quark phase obeys the Allen-Cahn growth law, L(t) ~ t^(1/2), for both second-order and first-order chiral transitions, but the abstract does not specify that this result holds only for the asymptotic late-stage growth and that the early-stage dynamics differs between spinodal decomposition (unstable quenches) and nucleation-and-growth (metastable quenches).

## Method
The authors used the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation to compute the thermodynamic potential and phase diagram for chiral symmetry breaking in quark matter. They then fitted a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy expanded to M^6 order to the NJL potential, and solved the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) equation numerically on 3D and 2D lattices to simulate the coarsening dynamics after a temperature quench. Both overdamped and inertial TDGL equations were considered, with the order-parameter correlation function and domain size L(t) extracted from the simulations.

## Key numbers
- Tricritical point location: (mu_tcp, T_tcp) = (282.58, 78) MeV for the NJL model with zero current quark mass.
- Vacuum quark mass: M = 312 MeV, obtained with cutoff Lambda = 653.30 MeV and coupling G = 5.0163 x 10^(-6) MeV^(-2).
- At T = 0, first-order transition occurs at mu = 326.321 MeV; at mu = 0, second-order transition occurs at T = 190 MeV.
- For T = 10 MeV and mu = 321.75 MeV, the dimensionless parameter lambda = |a|d/b^2 = 0.14, used in the TDGL simulations.
- Estimated physical length scale: sqrt(K/|a|) = 1.6 fm, assuming surface tension sigma = 50 MeV/fm^2 at T = 10 MeV.
- Estimated physical time scale: tau = 2.6 fm/s (with s of order 1) for the inverse damping coefficient Gamma ~ 2T/s.

## Context
This work extends studies of chiral transition kinetics in quark matter, which previously used the NJL model to examine critical behavior and spinodal decomposition (Sasaki, 2008) and a nonlinear sigma model coupled to quarks (Dumitru, 2000; Fraga, 2005). It also connects to extensive condensed-matter research on phase-ordering kinetics (Bray, 1994; Puri, 2009). The results enable quantitative predictions for domain growth and nucleation dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly near the QCD critical point, and provide a framework for comparing with future experimental data from RHIC and other facilities.
