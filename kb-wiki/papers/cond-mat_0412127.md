# Continuous unitary transformations and finite-size scaling exponents in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model

> We analyze the finite-size scaling exponents in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model by means of the Holstein-Primakoff representation of the spin operators and the continuous unitary transformations method. This combination allows us to compute analytically leading corrections to the ground state energy, the gap, the magnetization, and the two-spin correlation functions. We also present numerical calculations for large system size which confirm the validity of this approach. Finally, we use these results to discuss the entanglement properties of the ground state focusing on the (rescaled) concurrence that we compute in the thermodynamical limit.

## Key claim

The paper analytically computes finite-size scaling exponents for the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model at its quantum critical point using a combination of the Holstein-Primakoff representation and continuous unitary transformations, showing that the leading corrections to observables scale with specific powers of 1/N that are confirmed by numerical calculations for large system sizes.

## Method

The authors first apply a Holstein-Primakoff bosonization of the spin operators around the classical magnetization direction, then use continuous unitary transformations (CUTs) to diagonalize the resulting non-quadratic Hamiltonian order by order in a 1/N expansion. This allows them to compute the leading 1/N corrections to the ground-state energy, the gap, the magnetization, and the two-spin correlation functions in both the symmetric and broken phases. They also perform numerical diagonalizations for large system sizes to verify the analytical predictions.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy per spin in the thermodynamical limit: \( e_0(\infty) = -\frac{1}{2}\sin^2\theta_0 - h\cos\theta_0 \), where \(\theta_0 = \arccos h\) for \(0 \leq h < 1\) and \(\theta_0 = 0\) for \(h \geq 1\)
- Gap in the symmetric phase (random phase approximation): \(\Delta(\infty) = 2[(h-1)(h-\gamma)]^{1/2}\) for \(h \geq 1\)
- Gap in the broken phase: \(\Delta(\infty) = 0\) for \(0 \leq h < 1\) (exponentially small \(\sim \exp(-aN)\) for finite N)
- Renormalized gap from Bogoliubov transformation in broken phase: \(\Delta^{\rm (R)} = 2[(1-h^2)(1-\gamma)]^{1/2}\) (this is the excitation energy \(\Delta'\) between the two lowest and next two states, not the true gap)
- Two-spin correlation function in rotated basis at first order: \(4\langle \tilde{S}_x^2 \rangle/N^2 = \frac{1}{N}\left(\frac{1+\varepsilon}{1-\varepsilon}\right)^{1/2}\) and \(4\langle \tilde{S}_y^2 \rangle/N^2 = \frac{1}{N}\left(\frac{1-\varepsilon}{1+\varepsilon}\right)^{1/2}\), where \(\varepsilon = (1-\gamma)/(2h-1-\gamma)\) for \(h \geq 1\) and \(\varepsilon = (h^2-\gamma)/(2-h^2-\gamma)\) for \(0 \leq h < 1\)
- Special point where the product state is exact ground state: \(h = \frac{N-1}{N}\sqrt{\gamma}\)

## Context

This work extends the mean-field (semiclassical) analysis of Botet and Jullien (1982, 1983) for the LMG model by computing the first quantum corrections beyond the random phase approximation. It provides analytical finite-size scaling exponents that corroborate numerical studies by Botet and Jullien (1983) and Reslen et al. (2004), and enables a detailed analysis of ground-state entanglement properties (specifically the rescaled concurrence) in the thermodynamical limit, which was previously only accessible numerically. The CUT method used here was developed by Wegner (1994) and independently by Glazek and Wilson (1993, 1994).
