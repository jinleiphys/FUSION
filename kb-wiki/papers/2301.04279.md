# Collective flows of protons and deuterons in Au + Au collisions at $E_{beam}$ = 1.23$A$ GeV by the IQMD model

> Collective flows of protons and deuterons for Au + Au collisions at beam energy $E_{beam}$ = 1.23 $A$ GeV were simulated by an Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model. Two coalescence models, namely naive coalescence and dynamical coalescence models, for the formation of deuterons are compared. After reasonable match of rapidity spectra of protons and deuterons to the High Acceptance DiElectron Spectrometer (HADES) data is reached, we apply an event-plane method to calculate the first four-order collective flow coefficients as well as the ratios of $<v_4>/<v_2>^2$ and $<v_3>/<v_1><v_2>$, and observe the number of constituent nucleon scaling among protons and deuterons. In addition, the dependence of $ε_n$ and $v_n$ as well as the ratio $<v_n>$/$ε_n$ on the centrality is obtained. Lastly, we further investigate the Pearson coefficients $corr(v_n,v_m)$ between the first four harmonic flows for protons and deuterons as a function of rapidity and centrality.

## Key claim

The IQMD model, combined with either a naive or dynamical coalescence model for deuteron formation, can reproduce the rapidity spectra of protons and deuterons measured by HADES for Au+Au collisions at 1.23A GeV, but the model systematically underestimates the elliptic flow coefficient v2 compared to experimental data, particularly for protons.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at beam energy 1.23A GeV (center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV) with 1,600,000 events. Deuterons were formed from the nucleon phase-space distributions at freeze-out using two coalescence approaches: a naive coalescence model with momentum and coordinate cuts (p0 = 0.35 GeV/c, r0 = 3.5 fm) and a dynamical coalescence model based on Wigner phase-space density overlap. Collective flow coefficients v1 through v4 were calculated using the event-plane method with charges |Z| as weights, and the Pearson correlation coefficients between different harmonic flows were computed as functions of rapidity and centrality.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: E_beam = 1.23A GeV (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV)
- Total simulated events: 1,600,000
- Wave packet width parameter L = 2.16 fm^2 for Au+Au reactions
- In-medium correction factor eta = 0.2 for nucleon-nucleon cross section
- Naive coalescence parameters: p0 = 0.35 GeV/c, r0 = 3.5 fm
- Spin-isospin statistical factor G = 3/4 for deuteron in dynamical coalescence
- The ratio <v4>/<v2>^2 for protons and deuterons approaches approximately 0.5 in mid-rapidity region
- Asymptotic values of <v4>/<v2>^2 for protons and deuterons (naive or dynamical) approach 0.42 and 0.41 or 0.78, respectively

## Context

This work extends the study of collective flows to light nuclei (deuterons) in the beam energy regime of 1.23A GeV, complementing recent HADES Collaboration measurements of high-order harmonic flows of light nuclei (Adamczewski-Musch et al., 2020) and earlier studies of nucleon-number scaling of elliptic flow proposed by Yan et al. and Ma et al. The results enable further constraints on transport models in the baryon-rich matter regime and contribute to understanding the "ice in the fire" puzzle of light nuclei production in heavy-ion collisions. The paper also compares with UrQMD model results from Hillmann et al. (2020, 2019) which showed similar underestimation of elliptic flow.
