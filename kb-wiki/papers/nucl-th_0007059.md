# (Non)Thermal Aspects of Charmonium Production and a New Look at J/$ψ$ Suppression

> To investigate a recent proposal that J/$ψ$ production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions is of thermal origin we have reanalyzed the data from the NA38/50 collaboration within a thermal model including charm. Comparison of the calculated with measured yields demonstrates the non-thermal origin of hidden charm production at SPS energy. However, the ratio $ψ^{'}$/(J/$ψ)$ exhibits, in central nucleus-nucleus collisions, thermal features which lead us to a new interpretation of open charm and charmonium production at SPS energy. Implications for RHIC and LHC energy measurements will be discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that J/ψ production in nuclear collisions at SPS energy is not of thermal origin, but the ψ'/(J/ψ) ratio in central nucleus-nucleus collisions exhibits thermal features that suggest a new interpretation: charm quarks are produced directly in hard collisions, then all charmed hadrons form via statistical hadronization at the phase boundary.

## Method

The authors modified a thermal statistical model (grand canonical ensemble) to include charmed hadrons (open charm mesons and baryons, plus charmonia states) and compared its predictions to NA38/50 collaboration data for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy. They then introduced a charm enhancement factor g_c to scale thermal densities to match the directly produced charm quark yield from PYTHIA calculations, using this to compute J/ψ yields via statistical hadronization of the directly produced charm quarks.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 168 MeV and baryochemical potential μ_B = 266 MeV used for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy, established from hadron production yields in prior work
- Thermal model underpredicts measured J/ψ yield by more than a factor of 2 for most central collisions, and by about a factor of 3 for N_part = 200
- Thermal model predicts ψ'/(J/ψ) ratio of 3.7% (including feeding from heavier charmonia), compared to ~12% in hadron-proton and p-nucleus collisions
- For central Pb+Pb collisions (N_part = 400), directly produced charm quark pairs N_c\bar{c}^{direct} = 0.173 per collision, yielding g_c = 1.38
- At RHIC energies, dN_c\bar{c}^{direct}/dy = 1.0, leading to g_c = 6.8 and dN^{therm}_{J/ψ}/dy = 10^{-2}
- Thermal charm quark pair production in plasma phase estimated at N_c\bar{c}^{eq} = 0.47, undersaturated by factor ~50 compared to cascade approach

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach of Braun-Munzinger et al. (1999) for hadron production to include charm, directly testing the proposal by Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1999) that J/ψ production is of thermal origin. The paper contradicts that proposal by showing thermal J/ψ yields are far below data, but builds on the observation that ψ'/(J/ψ) ratios in central collisions approach the thermal model value. The new direct-production-plus-statistical-hadronization scenario enables predictions for RHIC and LHC energies, suggesting no J/ψ suppression at RHIC and possible enhancement at LHC, and calls for direct open charm measurements in nucleus-nucleus collisions.
