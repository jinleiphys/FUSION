# Transitions between complex configurations in the excitation of the Double Giant Resonance

> The transitions between complex configurations, to which the giant dipole resonance (GDR) and the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) doorway states are coupled, are taken into account in second order perturbation theory for the reaction amplitude. It is proved that only transitions between GDR and DGDR doorway states play an essential role in the Coulomb excitation of the DGDR.

## Key claim

The paper proves that transitions between complex (non-doorway) configurations of the GDR and DGDR play a marginal role in the Coulomb excitation of the DGDR in relativistic heavy ion collisions, contributing only 1-2.5% to the cross section in a simplified model and 0.5% in a realistic microscopic calculation, despite the total E1 strength between complex configurations being more than two orders of magnitude larger than that between doorway states.

## Method

The authors use second-order perturbation theory for the reaction amplitude describing two-step Coulomb excitation (ground state → GDR → DGDR). They separate the transition amplitude into a term involving only doorway GDR and DGDR states (A term) and a term involving transitions between complex configurations (B term), then test whether the B term contributes significantly. A simplified boson-type Hamiltonian with adjustable parameters (number of doorway states, number of complex configurations, interaction strength U) is diagonalized to obtain wave functions for GDR and DGDR states, and the cross sections with and without the B term are compared. A more realistic calculation uses wave functions from the Quasiparticle Phonon Model (QPM) with 6 doorway GDR states, 21 doorway DGDR states, and two-phonon (GDR) and three-phonon (DGDR) complex configurations.

## Key numbers

- The energy-dependent reaction function χ_E1(E) for 208Pb (640·A MeV) + 208Pb changes by 60% across the GDR energy region.
- In the simplified model, the fractional change Δσ = (σ^(A+B) - σ^A)/σ^A ranges from 1% to 2.5%, with the maximum achieved when using a single doorway GDR state (no Landau damping).
- In the realistic QPM calculation, Δσ equals 0.5%.
- The total B(E1) strength for transitions between complex GDR and DGDR configurations is more than two orders of magnitude larger than that between doorway GDR and DGDR configurations.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic studies by Ponomarev et al. (1996, 1997) and Bertulani et al. (1996) that neglected transitions between complex configurations in DGDR excitation. It addresses the open question of enhanced DGDR excitation cross sections observed in experiments by Schmidt et al. (1993) for 136Xe and by Boretzky et al. (1996) for 208Pb. The negative result confirms that calculations considering only collective doorway states (which are much simpler to perform) require no further corrections, and that the argument by Soloviev et al. (1997) about enhanced DGDR decay via complex configurations does not apply to the inverse excitation process due to phase space differences.
