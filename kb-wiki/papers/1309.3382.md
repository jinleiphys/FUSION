# Microscopic analysis of $^{11}$Li elastic scattering on protons and breakup processes within $^{9}$Li+$2n$ cluster model

> In the paper, the results of analysis of elastic scattering and breakup processes in interactions of the $^{11}$Li nucleus with protons are presented. The hybrid model of the microscopic optical potential (OP) is applied. This OP includes the single-folding real part, while its imaginary part is derived within the high-energy approximation (HEA) theory. For the $^{11}$Li$+p$ elastic scattering, the microscopic large-scale shell model (LSSM) density of $^{11}$Li is used. The depths of the real and imaginary parts of OP are fitted to the elastic scattering data at 62, 68.4, and 75 MeV/nucleon, being simultaneously adjusted to reproduce the true energy dependence of the corresponding volume integrals. The role of the spin-orbit potential is studied and predictions for the total reaction cross sections are made. Also, the cluster model, in which $^{11}$Li consists of $2n$-halo and the $^{9}$Li core having its own LSSM form of density, is adopted. The respective microscopic proton-cluster OP's are calculated and folded with the density probability of the relative motion of both clusters to get the whole $^{11}$Li$+p$ optical potential. The breakup cross sections of $^{11}$Li at 62 MeV/nucleon and momentum distributions of the cluster fragments are calculated. An analysis of the single-particle density of $^{11}$Li within the same cluster model accounting for the possible geometric forms of the halo-cluster density distribution is performed.

## Key claim

The hybrid microscopic optical potential model, which combines a single-folding real part (including exchange terms) with an imaginary part derived from high-energy approximation theory, can describe $^{11}$Li$+p$ elastic scattering data at 62, 68.4, and 75 MeV/nucleon, but only when the renormalization coefficients $N$ are fitted under the constraint that the volume integrals $J_V$ and $J_W$ follow the expected energy dependence from Romanovsky (no year given); otherwise, the fitted $J_W$ values at 62 MeV/nucleon contradict the expected trend, suggesting that data at angles $\theta_{c.m.} > 46^\circ$ may be influenced by breakup channels.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential was calculated via single-folding of the large-scale shell model (LSSM) densities of $^{11}$Li with the CDM3Y6 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, including both direct and exchange terms. The imaginary part was derived from the high-energy approximation (HEA) using the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude averaged over isospin. The depths of the real, imaginary, and spin-orbit components were renormalized by fitted coefficients $N_R$, $N_I$, $N_R^{SO}$, and $N_I^{SO}$ to match elastic scattering data at three energies, with the additional constraint that the volume integrals $J_V$ and $J_W$ follow the expected energy dependence. For breakup calculations, a $^{9}$Li+$2n$ cluster model was adopted, where the $^{11}$Li$+p$ optical potential was constructed by folding the microscopic potentials of each cluster with the density probability of their relative motion, using both 0$s$ and 1$s$ states for the cluster wave function.

## Key numbers

- At 62 MeV/nucleon, the best-fit renormalization coefficients (without spin-orbit term) were $N_R = 0.871$ and $N_I = 0.953$, yielding $\chi^2 = 1.415$ and total reaction cross section $\sigma_R = 456.97$ mb.
- At 68.4 MeV/nucleon (without spin-orbit term): $N_R = 0.625$, $N_I = 0.186$, $\chi^2 = 1.328$, $\sigma_R = 153.44$ mb.
- At 75 MeV/nucleon (without spin-orbit term): $N_R = 0.679$, $N_I = 0.370$, $\sigma_R = 232.62$ mb.
- When the fitting at 62 MeV/nucleon was restricted to $\theta_{c.m.} \leq 46^\circ$, the parameters changed to $N_R = 0.656$, $N_I = 0.164$, with $\chi^2 = 0.788$, $\sigma_R = 154.86$ mb, and volume integrals $J_V = 257.973$ MeV fm$^3$ and $J_W = 57.136$ MeV fm$^3$.
- The two-neutron separation energy used for the cluster model was $\varepsilon = 0.247$ MeV, and the rms radius of the cluster function was chosen as 4.93 fm.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for the 0$s$-state cluster interaction were $R = 1.0$ fm, $a = 0.25$ fm, $V_0 = 32.55$ MeV; for the 1$s$-state: $R = 6.25$ fm, $a = 0.25$ fm, $V_0 = 11.55$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid microscopic optical potential approach previously applied to $^{6,8}$He scattering by Lukyanov (2007, 2009, 2010) to the neutron-halo nucleus $^{11}$Li, using LSSM densities from Karataglidis (1997) that have correct exponential asymptotics rather than Gaussian forms. The analysis addresses the parameter ambiguity problem noted in earlier semi-phenomenological studies by Hassan (2009) and Farag (2012), where the energy dependence of the imaginary volume integral was found to be problematic. The cluster model for breakup calculations follows the method used for $^{6}$He reactions by Lukyanov (2011) and the HEA approach of Hencken (1996) and Bertulani (2004), and enables predictions for fragment momentum distributions that could be compared with future experimental data at these energies.
