# Reexamination of nuclear structure properties and shape coexistence of nuclei around A70

> We reexamine the nuclear structure properties of waiting point nuclei around A70 using the interacting boson model 1 (IBM 1) and the relativistic mean field (RMF) model. Effective density dependent meson exchange functional (DD ME2) and density dependent point coupling functional (DD PC1) were used for the RMF calculations. We calculated the energy levels, the geometric shapes, binding and separation energies of nucleons and quadrupole deformation parameters (\b{eta}2). The shape coexistence phenomena in A 70 nuclei (68Se, 70Se, 70Br, 70Kr, 72Kr, 74Kr, 74Rb, and 74Sr) was later investigated. Spherical and deformed shapes of the selected waiting point nuclei were computed using the IBM 1 and RMF models, respectively. The proton neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn QRPA) model was used to calculate \b{eta} decay properties (Gamow Teller strength distributions, \b{eta} decay half lives, and branching ratios) of selected nuclei as a function of \b{eta}2. The results revealed a significant variation in calculated half lives and Gamow Teller strength distributions as the shape parameter was changed. The \b{eta}2 computed via DD ME2 functional resulted in half lives in best agreement with the measured data.

## Key claim

The paper reexamines nuclear structure and shape coexistence in A~70 waiting-point nuclei, finding that the β-decay half-lives and Gamow-Teller strength distributions vary significantly with the quadrupole deformation parameter (β₂), and that half-lives computed using β₂ from the DD-ME2 functional show the best agreement with measured data, though the authors note that the IBM-1 model predicts spherical shapes for all selected nuclei while the RMF model predicts deformed shapes.

## Method

The authors used the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1) to calculate energy levels and potential energy surfaces (PESs) for eight neutron-deficient nuclei (⁶⁸Se, ⁷⁰Se, ⁷⁰Br, ⁷⁰Kr, ⁷²Kr, ⁷⁴Kr, ⁷⁴Rb, ⁷⁴Sr), and the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with density-dependent DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 functionals to compute binding energies per nucleon, two-nucleon separation energies, and quadrupole deformation parameters (β₂) from PESs. The proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model was then employed to calculate β-decay properties (Gamow-Teller strength distributions, half-lives, and branching ratios) using five different β₂ values from the IBM-1 and RMF models plus a sixth from the FRDM calculation, with pairing gaps computed from separation energies rather than the traditional 12/√A formula.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square errors (RMSE) between model predictions and experimental binding energies per nucleon: 0.061 MeV for RMF+DD-ME2, 0.045 MeV for RMF+DD-PC1, 0.062 MeV for RMF+NL3*, 0.022 MeV for HFN+SLy4, and 0.008 MeV for FRDM
- Maximum deviation of FRDM from experimental binding energy per nucleon data is 0.006 MeV, while other models deviate up to 0.06 MeV
- The characteristic energy ratio (R₄/₂) values for IBM-1 dynamical symmetries are 3.33 for SU(3) (axially deformed), 2.00 for U(5) (spherical), and 2.50 for O(6) (γ-unstable)
- The oscillator constant for nucleons was determined using ℏω = (45A⁻¹/³ − 25A⁻²/³) MeV
- The ratio gₐ/gᵥ = −1.254 and constant C = 6295 s were used in partial half-life calculations

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of waiting-point nuclei along the N=Z chain around A~70 using IBM-1 and pn-QRPA models by Nabi (2016, 2017a) and studies of ⁷⁶Se by Nabi (2017b), and builds on earlier systematic examinations of Gamow-Teller strength dependence on nuclear deformation in neutron-deficient Kr and Sr isotopes by Sarriguren (2011, 2013, 2014) using a deformed QRPA approach with Skyrme forces. The results enable improved predictions of β-decay half-lives for rp-process nucleosynthesis calculations in X-ray bursts, where the shape dependence of decay properties can influence effective half-lives at high temperatures as argued by Schatz (1998).
