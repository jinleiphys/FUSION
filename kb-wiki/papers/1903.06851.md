# Massive Neutron Stars with a Color Superconducting Quark Matter Core

> We construct the equation of state for high density neutron star matter at zero temperature using the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective theory of QCD. We build nuclear matter, quark matter, and the mixed phases from the same NJL Lagrangian, which has been used to model free and in-medium hadrons as well as nuclear systems. A focus here is to determine if the same coupling constants in the scalar diquark and vector meson channels, which give a good description of nucleon structure and nuclear matter, can also be used for the color superconducting high density quark matter phase. We find that this is possible for the scalar diquark (pairing) interaction, but the vector meson interaction has to be reduced so that superconducting quark matter becomes the stable phase at high densities. We compare our equation of state with recent phenomenological parametrizations based on generic stability conditions for neutron stars. We find that the maximum mass of a neutron star, with a color superconducting quark matter core, exceeds $2.01 \pm 0.04\,M_\odot$ which is the value of the recently observed massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432. The mass-radius relation is also consistent with gravitational wave observations (GW170817).

## Key claim

The same scalar diquark coupling constant that reproduces the free nucleon mass can also be used as the pairing interaction in color-superconducting quark matter, but the vector meson coupling constants determined from nuclear matter saturation must be reduced (by a factor of 0.45 to 0.68) in the quark matter phase to obtain a stable hybrid star with a maximum mass exceeding the observed value for PSR J0348+0432.

## Method

The authors construct the equation of state for neutron star matter at zero temperature using a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with proper-time regularization, describing nuclear matter, quark matter, and the mixed phase from the same Lagrangian. Nuclear matter is treated via a mean-field approximation with nucleons as quark-diquark bound states from the Faddeev equation, while quark matter includes two-flavor color superconductivity with a scalar diquark pairing gap; the mixed phase is obtained using the Gibbs construction with global charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hybrid star mass exceeds \(2.01 \pm 0.04\,M_\odot\), the mass of PSR J0348+0432.
- The vector coupling in quark matter must be reduced by a factor \(0.45 \leq c_v \leq 0.68\) relative to its nuclear matter value to satisfy stability conditions.
- The scalar diquark coupling can be used with a scaling factor up to \(c_s = 0.98\) (essentially unchanged from the nucleon sector).
- Nuclear matter saturation density is \(\rho_0 = 0.16\;\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), with binding energy per nucleon \(E_B = -15.7\;\mathrm{MeV}\) and symmetry energy \(a_4 = 32.0\;\mathrm{MeV}\).
- The phase transition from nuclear matter to quark matter occurs in the range \(2\,\rho_0 \leq \rho_{\mathrm{tr}} \leq 4\,\rho_0\) for the acceptable parameter region.

## Context

This work extends the same NJL model framework previously used to describe free hadrons (Cloet 2005, 2007), nuclear matter (Cloet 2006, 2009), and the EMC effect (Cloet 2006) to the hybrid star equation of state. It contrasts with approaches that combine separate hadronic mean-field theories with NJL quark models (Baym 2017, Contrera 2014, Benic 2014) and with phenomenological polytrope parametrizations (Abgaryan 2018). The results enable future extensions to three-flavor quark matter and inclusion of finite-size effects in the mixed phase, while showing consistency with the generic stability conditions of Alford (2013) and with gravitational wave observations (GW170817).
