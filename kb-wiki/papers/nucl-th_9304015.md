# Colour transparency: a novel test of QCD in nuclear interactions

> Colour transparency is a cute and indispensable property of QCD as the gauge theory of strong interaction. CT tests of QCD consist of production of the perturbative small-sized hadronic state and measuring the strngth of its non-perturbative diffraction nteraction in a nuclear matter. The energy depenednce of the final- state interaction in a nuclear matter probes a dynamical evolution from the perturbative small-sized state to the full-sized nonperturbative hadron. QCD observables of CT experiments correspond to a novel mechanism of scanning of hadronic wave functions from the large nonperturbative to the small perturbative size. In these lectures, which are addressed to experimentalists and theorists, I discuss the principle ideas of CT physics and the physics potential of the hadron and electron facilities in the > 10 GeV energy range. The special effort was made to present the material in the pedagigical and self-consistent way, with an emphasis on the underlying rich quantum-mechanical interference phenomena.

## Key claim

Colour transparency (CT) predicts that in hard-scattering exclusive reactions on nuclear targets, produced hadrons may escape a nucleus without being absorbed, so that the nuclear transmission coefficient approaches unity, although with typical hadronic cross sections a nucleus thickness is of the order of few absorption lengths; this effect relies on the production of perturbative small-sized hadronic states whose interaction cross section vanishes as the transverse quark separation goes to zero, a direct consequence of colour gauge invariance and confinement.

## Method

The paper develops the theoretical framework for CT by combining the Glauber-Gribov multiple-scattering theory with the QCD property that the cross section for a quark-antiquark pair of transverse size rho vanishes as rho^2 at small rho. The formalism uses the fixed-rho representation where the transverse separation of quarks is conserved in high-energy scattering, allowing the nuclear attenuation to be computed via the diagonal operator sigma(rho) acting on the wave functions of the ejectile states. The author derives colour transparency sum rules that relate perturbative QCD observables at small rho to non-perturbative diffraction transition amplitudes between hadronic mass eigenstates, and discusses how the formation (coherence) length controls the quantum evolution of the small-size wave packet into full-size hadrons.

## Key numbers

* The total cross section for J/Psi-nucleon scattering is approximately 5 mb, while for Psi'-nucleon scattering it is approximately 12 mb, probing sigma(rho) at rho ~ R_J/Psi and rho ~ R_Psi' respectively.
* The pion-nucleon total cross section receives its leading contribution from rho ~ 0.7-1 fm, yielding sigma_tot(pi N) approximately 25 mb.
* For heavy nuclei, the attenuation law for J/Psi becomes an inverse thickness law: the expectation value of the attenuation factor equals 1 / [1 + (1/2) sigma_tot(J/Psi N) T(b)], where T(b) is the optical thickness.
* The onset of CT and the principal effects of scanning hadronic wave functions take place in the 10-50 GeV energy range.
* The formation length condition for coherence of components |i> and |k> of the wave packet requires gamma / |m_k - m_i| > R_A, where R_A is the nuclear radius.

## Context

This lecture paper reviews and synthesizes the development of colour transparency from seminal works by Zamolodchikov, Kopeliovich and Lapidus (Ref.1), Bertsch, Brodsky, Goldhaber and Gunion (Ref.2), Mueller (Ref.3), and Brodsky (Ref.4), extending the Glauber-Gribov multiple-scattering theory and Gribov's inelastic shadowing formalism (Ref.9) to QCD. The work builds on the Landau-Pomeranchuck-Feinberg concept of formation length (1953-1954) and the QED Chudakov effect (1949/1955) as classical analogues. The paper enables quantitative predictions for nuclear transparency in (e,e'p) scattering and diffractive deep inelastic scattering experiments at hadron and electron facilities in the 10-50 GeV range, and establishes CT sum rules (Refs.5-8) that connect perturbative and non-perturbative QCD observables.
