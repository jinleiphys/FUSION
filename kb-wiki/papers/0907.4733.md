# Universal Flow in the First fm/c at RHIC

> We will show how both elliptic and radial flow generated during the first fm/c at RHIC can be expressed in a form which is quasi-independent of the state of matter and depends only on the initial energy density profile. Descriptions based on partons or classical fields, thermalized or highly anisotropic, all lead to the same collective velocity given a few easily satisfied conditions. This significantly narrows the uncertainty for initializing hydrodynamic prescriptions.

# Universal Flow in the First fm/c at RHIC

## Key claim
Transverse flow (both radial and elliptic) generated during the first fm/c at RHIC is independent of the specific model of the early-time matter, whether partons, classical fields, thermalized or anisotropic, provided the stress-energy tensor is traceless, longitudinal dynamics are boost-invariant, and the anisotropy parameter κ is purely time-dependent. The result holds at the ≈10% level of validity.

## Method
The authors solve the conservation equation ∂^α T_{αβ} = 0 under three assumptions: traceless stress-energy tensor (valid at 10% level at RHIC energies), boost-invariant longitudinal dynamics fixing u_z = z/τ, and a purely time-dependent anisotropy parameter κ ≡ T_{xx}/T_{00} = T_{yy}/T_{00}. They derive an analytic expression for the transverse flow F_i ≡ T_{0i}/T_{00} that eliminates κ, then verify the result numerically for three cases: ideal hydrodynamics (κ=1/3), free-streaming particles (κ=1/2), and coherent fields (κ=1). They also model a sudden thermalization transition via Rankine-Hugoniot-type conservation across a time-like hypersurface.

## Key numbers
- The transverse flow equation F_x = -(∂_x T_{00}/T_{00}) τ is independent of κ, valid for τ from 0.3 fm/c to 1.0 fm/c.
- The energy density falls as ∂_τ T_{00} = -(2/τ)(1-κ) T_{00}.
- The traceless condition fixes T_{zz} = (1-2κ) T_{00}.
- The longitudinal velocity is u_z = z/τ, valid at the ≈10% level for η ≤ 1.
- The assumptions are valid at the ≈10% level at the large energy densities during the first fm/c.

## Context
This work resolves part of the "HBT puzzle" by showing that early-time flow is universal across competing initial-state models such as the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) and parton-based descriptions (Author 2009). It extends the approach of Pratt (2008) by demonstrating that the transverse flow generated before thermalization is independent of the equation of state parameter κ, significantly narrowing the uncertainty for initializing hydrodynamic simulations. The result enables viscous hydrodynamic simulations coupled to resonance cascades to describe particle spectra and HBT radii at the ≈10% level.
