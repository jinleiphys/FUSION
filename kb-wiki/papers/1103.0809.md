# Suppression of the high $p_T$ charged hadron $R_{AA}$ at the LHC

> We present a parameter free postdiction of the high-$p_T$ charged-hadron nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) in two centralities, measured by the CMS collaboration in $Pb$-$Pb$ collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The evolution of the bulk medium is modeled using viscous fluid dynamics, with parameters adjusted to describe the soft hadron yields and elliptic flow. Assuming the dominance of radiative energy loss, we compute the medium modification of the $R_{AA}$ using a perturbative QCD based formalism, the higher twist scheme. The transverse momentum diffusion coefficient $\hat{q}$ is assumed to scale with the entropy density and normalized by fitting the $R_{AA}$ in the most central $Au$-$Au$ collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). This set up is validated in non-central $Au$-$Au$ collisions at RHIC and then extrapolated to $Pb$-$Pb$ collisions at the LHC, keeping the relation between $\hat{q}$ and entropy density unchanged. We obtain a satisfactory description of the CMS $R_{AA}$ over the $p_{T}$ range from 10-100 GeV.

# Suppression of the high $p_T$ charged hadron $R_{AA}$ at the LHC

## Key claim

The paper presents a parameter-free postdiction of the charged-hadron nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) at LHC energies, but the calculation underpredicts the $R_{AA}$ for $p_T \leq 8$ GeV at the LHC, possibly due to non-perturbative effects such as recombination extending up to 8-9 GeV or enhanced non-linear effects in jet quenching that were ignored.

## Method

The authors combine a 2+1D viscous fluid dynamical simulation (tuned to describe soft hadron yields and elliptic flow at $p_T < 2$ GeV) with a perturbative QCD based radiative energy loss calculation using the higher twist scheme. The transverse momentum diffusion coefficient $\hat{q}$ is assumed to scale with the local entropy density, normalized by fitting the $R_{AA}$ in 0-5% central $Au$-$Au$ collisions at RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/nucleon), and then extrapolated to $Pb$-$Pb$ collisions at the LHC ($\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV) with the same $\hat{q}$-to-entropy-density relation. The medium-modified fragmentation function is obtained by solving an in-medium DGLAP evolution equation, and the calculation is performed for two centrality bins (0-5% and 10-30%) at both RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- $\hat{q}_0 = 2.2$ GeV$^2$/fm at the maximum RHIC temperature of $T = 344$ MeV, obtained by requiring $R_{AA}(0-5\%, p_T = 10 \text{ GeV}) \simeq 0.2$ in central $Au$-$Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/nucleon
- The maximum temperature at LHC thermalization is $T_{max} = 436$ MeV, almost twice that at RHIC
- Thermalization time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c for both RHIC and LHC collisions
- Jets decouple from the medium when local temperature reaches $160$ MeV
- For hadron $p_T \sim 10$ GeV in 0-5% collisions at RHIC, the maximum path length $\zeta_{max} = 5$ fm
- The calculation describes CMS $R_{AA}$ data over the $p_T$ range from 10-100 GeV, with satisfactory agreement for $p_T \geq 9$ GeV at LHC
- A 30% lower value of $\hat{q}_0$ would be required to obtain a better fit with the 0-5% LHC data

## Context

This work extends the higher twist formalism for radiative energy loss, previously applied at RHIC energies, to LHC energies by scaling $\hat{q}$ with entropy density rather than $T^3$ as in prior attempts by Bass (2008) and Majumder (2007). The fluid dynamical simulation uses the KLN parametrization for initial conditions, following the approach of Song (2010) and Shen (2010). The calculation enables systematic comparison of jet quenching across RHIC and LHC energies within a single framework, while identifying the need for future improvements in treating the correlation between energy, virtuality, and distance traveled by partons, as well as possible non-perturbative effects at lower $p_T$.
