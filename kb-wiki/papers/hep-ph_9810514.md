# Non-Perturbative Couplings and Color Superconductivity

> Quark matter at sufficiently high density exhibits color superconductivity, due to attractive gluonic interactions. At lower densities of order $Λ_{QCD}^3$, it has been proposed that instanton generated vertices may play an important role in the Cooper pair formation. We study the renormalization group flow to the Fermi surface of the full set of couplings generated by gluonic and instanton interactions. In earlier work we showed that if the gluonic interactions dominate at the matching scale, their running determines the scale of the Cooper pair formation Delta. Here we consider all possibilities, including the one in which the instanton interactions dominate all others at the matching scale. In the latter case we find that a number of additional induced couplings (including the gluonic ones) reach their Landau poles almost simultaneously with the instanton vertex. Presumably all contribute to the Cooper pair formation. The most important consequence of including all the couplings is a large increase in the gap size Delta.

## Key claim

If instanton-generated interactions dominate over gluonic ones at the matching scale near the Fermi surface, then a set of eight induced couplings (including the gluonic ones) reach their Landau poles almost simultaneously with the instanton vertex, and the resulting Cooper pair gap size Delta is considerably larger than estimates that include only the instanton vertex alone.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory of four-fermion operators near the Fermi surface, integrating out gluons and instantons to obtain a set of 14 couplings that close under renormalization group flow. They compute the one-loop beta functions for these couplings in the presence of a Fermi surface, using the restricted momentum structure that allows only bubble diagrams, and solve the RG equations numerically for various matching conditions (gluon-dominated, instanton-dominated, and mixed).

## Key numbers

- The density of states at the Fermi surface is given by \( N = \mu^2 / 2\pi^2 \), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential.
- For the case where the instanton coupling is small (\(\kappa = 0.01 g\)) relative to the gluonic coupling at matching, the gluonic couplings reach their Landau pole before the instanton vertex becomes large, and the instanton vertex only reaches large values (coupling \(\gg 10\)) at much later RG time.
- When all eight couplings (\(g_7\) through \(g_{14}\)) are taken equal at the matching scale, they run together and reach their Landau poles simultaneously, with the scale of the pole (and hence the gap \(\Delta\)) being significantly smaller in \(|t|\) than for the pure instanton case.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same authors (Evans, Hsu, Schwetz, 1998) that considered only gluonic interactions dominating at the matching scale, and by instanton-focused studies (instanton, 1998) that assumed the instanton vertex was the unique important coupling. The paper shows that when instanton effects are dominant, the full set of induced couplings must be included, and this increases the predicted gap size \(\Delta\) relative to the pure-instanton estimates. The analysis enables more complete gap equation calculations for color superconductivity at densities of order \(\Lambda_{QCD}^3\), where non-perturbative instanton effects are expected to be important.
