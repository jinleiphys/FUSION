# Spectral singularities for Non-Hermitian one-dimensional Hamiltonians: puzzles with resolution of identity

> We examine the completeness of bi-orthogonal sets of eigenfunctions for non-Hermitian Hamiltonians possessing a spectral singularity. The correct resolutions of identity are constructed for delta like and smooth potentials. Their form and the contribution of a spectral singularity depend on the class of functions employed for physical states. With this specification there is no obstruction to completeness originating from a spectral singularity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spectral singularities in non-Hermitian Hamiltonians do not obstruct the completeness of biorthogonal eigenfunction sets, contrary to earlier doubts raised in Mostafazadeh (2009) and Longhi (2009); the resolution of identity can be constructed explicitly, but its form and the contribution of the spectral singularity depend on the class of test functions employed for physical states.

## Method

The authors construct explicit resolutions of identity for the one-dimensional non-Hermitian Hamiltonian with an imaginary delta potential, \(h = -\partial^2 + z\delta(x)\) with \(iz \in \mathbb{R}\), and for a related smooth potential obtained via supersymmetric quantum mechanics using a tanh superpotential. They analyze the biorthogonal eigenfunctions, Green functions, and spectral singularities, and derive the resolution of identity in several equivalent forms depending on the space of test functions (e.g., \(CL_\gamma = C_{\mathbb{R}} \cap C^\infty_{(-\infty,0]} \cap C^\infty_{[0,+\infty)} \cap L_2(\mathbb{R}; (1+|x|)^\gamma)\) with different \(\gamma\)).

## Key numbers

- The spectral singularity occurs at \(\lambda = -z^2/4\) for the delta potential Hamiltonian \(h = -\partial^2 + z\delta(x)\).
- The eigenfunction at the spectral singularity is \(\psi_0(x) = e^{z|x|/2}\).
- For test functions in \(CL_\gamma\) with \(\gamma > 1\), the resolution of identity takes the form \(\delta(x-x') = \text{p.v.} \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \psi_+(x;k)\psi_-(x';k)\,dk - (z/4)\,\psi_0(x)\psi_0(x')\).
- For test functions in \(CL_\gamma\) with \(\gamma > -1\), the resolution includes an additional term with an integral over \(\sin t / t\) that provides the missing half of \(\psi_0(x)\).
- The binorm of the smoothed function \(\psi_0(x;\alpha) = \exp(\frac12 (z-\alpha)|x|)\) is \(\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} (\psi_0(x;\alpha))^2 dx = -2/(z-\alpha)\).
- For the smooth potential \(h_\alpha = -\partial^2 - [z/2(z/2-\alpha)]/\cosh^2(\alpha x)\), the resolution of identity is \(\delta(x-x') = \text{p.v.} \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \psi_+(x;k,\alpha)\psi_-(x';k,\alpha)\,dk - (z/4)[\Gamma(1-z/\alpha)/\Gamma^2(1-z/(2\alpha))]\,\psi_0(x;\alpha)\psi_0(x';\alpha)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of spectral singularities in one-dimensional non-Hermitian quantum mechanics, previously studied by Mostafazadeh (2009) and Ahmed (2009) for delta potentials and by Longhi (2009) for periodic complex potentials, who raised concerns about completeness. The authors resolve these puzzles by constructing explicit resolutions of identity for both delta-like and smooth potentials, showing that spectral singularities contribute to completeness in a manner dependent on the test function space. This enables further study of physical phenomena in complex crystals, optical waveguides, and cosmology where non-Hermitian Hamiltonians with real spectra appear, as discussed in Bender (2007), Makris et al. (2008), and Andrianov et al. (2009).
