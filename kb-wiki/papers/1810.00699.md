# Constraining New Muonic Interactions Meditated by Axion-Like-Particles

> ALPs (Axion Like Particle) beyond the standard model are solutions to several important problems of modern physics. One way to detect these particles is to detect the new interactions they meditate. Many experiments have been performed to search for these new interactions in ranges from $\simμ$m to astrophysical range. At present, nearly all known experiments searching for the ALP-meditated long range new interactions use sources or probes containing protons, neutrons and electrons. Constraints for other fermions such as muons are scarce, though muons might be the most suspicious particles which could take part in new interactions, considering their involvement of several well known puzzles of modern physics. In this work, we discuss the possibility of explaining the anomalous magnetic moment of muons by the long range muonic new interactions mediated by ALPs. We also give a constraint for the scalar-pseudo-scalar(SP) type interaction meditated by muonic ALPs. We propose to further search the muonic SP type interaction by muon spin rotation experiments.

# Constraining New Muonic Interactions Meditated by Axion-Like-Particles

## Key claim

The 3.7-sigma discrepancy between the experimental measurement and Standard Model prediction for the muon anomalous magnetic moment can be explained by long-range new interactions mediated by axion-like-particles (ALPs), but only if the coupling products are nonzero at the ~10^-7 level while the scalar-pseudoscalar product is constrained to be zero at that same level by the muon electric dipole moment limit.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop corrections to the electromagnetic vertex from scalar-scalar (SS), pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (PP), and scalar-pseudoscalar (SP) couplings of a new light boson to muons, deriving analytic expressions for the form factors F2(0) (anomalous magnetic moment) and F3(0) (electric dipole moment). They then use the measured 3.7-sigma deviation in the muon g-2 and the experimental upper bound on the muon electric dipole moment to set 95% confidence level constraints on the coupling products g_S^mu g_S^mu, g_P^mu g_P^mu, and g_S^mu g_P^mu as functions of the ALP mass (interaction range). The same method is applied to electrons for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy at 95% C.L.: 1.28 x 10^-9 < |a_mu^exp - a_mu^SM| < 4.48 x 10^-9
- Muon electric dipole moment upper limit at 95% C.L.: |d_mu| < 1.8 x 10^-19 e.cm (from Bennett 2009)
- Electron anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy at 95% C.L.: |a_e^exp - a_e^SM| < 2.66 x 10^-12
- Electron electric dipole moment upper limit at 95% C.L.: |d_e| < 1.2 x 10^-29 e.cm
- The scalar-scalar, pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar, and scalar-pseudoscalar coupling products for muons are all constrained to be at the ~10^-7 level (from figures, for interaction ranges from ~nm to ~um)
- The electron constraints from this method are ~3 orders of magnitude more stringent than a recent experimental work (Rong 2017) at micrometer ranges
- At long ranges (ALP mass below ~keV), atomic and molecular EDM experiments (Dzuba 2018, Stadnik 2018) give constraints on g_S^e g_P^e ~3 times more stringent than the results presented

## Context

This work extends the search for ALP-mediated new interactions beyond the usual probes (protons, neutrons, electrons) to muons, which have been largely neglected despite their involvement in puzzles such as the muonic hydrogen charge radius anomaly (Antognini 2013) and the muonic deuteron puzzle (Pohl 2016). Previous constraints on monopole-dipole interactions from experiments using polarized neutrons or electrons (Terrano 2015, Hoedl 2011, Rong 2017) involved mixtures of couplings to protons, neutrons, and electrons, whereas this method provides pure constraints for interactions between identical fermions. The results enable future experimental searches using muon spin rotation experiments with nonmagnetic mass-sources or polarized electron spin-density sources (such as SmCo5 used by Terrano 2015 and Ji 2018) to directly probe muonic ALP couplings.
