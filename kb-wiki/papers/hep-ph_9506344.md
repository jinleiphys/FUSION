# Update of the stranger story: The strange vector form factors of the nucleon in the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model

> The strange vector form factors are evaluated for $Q^2=0$ and $Q^2=1\ \mbox{GeV}^2$ in the framework of the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model (or semi-bosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model). The rotational $1/N_c$ and $m_s$ corrections are taken into account up to linear order. The mean-square strange radius $\langle r^{2}\rangle^{Sachs}_{s}=-0.35\; \mbox{fm}^2$ and the strange magnetic moment $μ_s = -0.44\;μ_N$ are obtained. The results are compared with several different models.

## Key claim

The strange vector form factors of the nucleon are evaluated in the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model (semi-bosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) with rotational 1/N_c and m_s corrections included up to linear order, yielding a strange magnetic moment of -0.44 μ_N and a mean-square strange Sachs radius of -0.35 fm², though the abstract does not specify that these values depend on the constituent quark mass being fixed at M=420 MeV and that the m_s corrections contribute roughly 50% to the electric form factor and reduce the magnetic form factor by about 40%.

## Method

The strange vector form factors are computed from the matrix elements of the strange quark current in the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model, using the Kahana-Ripka discretized basis method with a box size of about 10 fm. The profile function is determined self-consistently from the Euler-Lagrange equation, and the collective quantization is performed by integrating over rotational and translational zero modes, with the Dirac operator expanded to include rotational 1/N_c corrections and linear m_s corrections. The valence and sea contributions are separated, and the final expressions for the Sachs electric and magnetic form factors are evaluated numerically with four free parameters fixed to the pion mass (139 MeV), pion decay constant (93 MeV), kaon mass (496 MeV), and constituent quark mass (420 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Strange magnetic moment: μ_s = -0.44 μ_N (nuclear magnetons) at Q²=0, with constituent quark mass M=420 MeV and m_s corrections included
- Mean-square strange Sachs radius: ⟨r²⟩^{Sachs}_s = -0.35 fm², with M=420 MeV and m_s corrections
- Mean-square strange Dirac radius: ⟨r²⟩^{Dirac}_s = -0.32 fm², with M=420 MeV and m_s corrections
- Without m_s corrections (M=420 MeV): ⟨r²⟩^{Sachs}_s = -0.25 fm² and ⟨r²⟩^{Dirac}_s = -0.20 fm²
- Baryon radius: ⟨r²⟩_B = 0.48 fm² without m_s corrections, 0.35 fm² with m_s corrections (M=420 MeV)
- Hypercharge radius: ⟨r²⟩_Y = 0.73 fm² without m_s corrections, 0.70 fm² with m_s corrections (M=420 MeV)
- The strange current quark mass used is m_s ≈ 180 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of strange vector form factors in the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model by including rotational 1/N_c corrections that were omitted in the earlier calculation by Weigel et al. (1995), which also used scaling approximations rather than a fully self-consistent approach. The results are compared with the pole-fit analysis of Jaffe (1989) and Hammer et al. (1994), the kaon-loop calculations of Koepf et al. (1992) and Musolf et al. (1994), the vector meson dominance approach of Cohen et al. (1995) and Forkel (1995), and the SU(3) Skyrme model results of Park et al. (1991) and Park and Weigel (1992), as well as the lattice calculation by Leinweber (1995). The paper enables further study of strange axial form factors and suggests that higher-order m_s corrections may be needed given the strong dependence of the form factors on the strange quark mass.
