# Excitation of Triple Giant Resonances in Heavy-Ion Reactions

> We calculate the cross-section for the excitation and subsequent decay of triple giant resonances(TGDR) in several nuclei excited with heavy ions.The recently developed coherent plus incoherent theory for excitation in conjunction with the hybrid decay model of Dias-Hussein-Adhikari are used for the purpose.It is emphasized that the direct decay of TGDR is expected to deviate appreciably from the harmonic limit especially at low bombarding energies,owing to the incoherent contribution.

## Key claim

The direct decay of the triple giant dipole resonance (TGDR) is expected to deviate appreciably from the harmonic limit of three independently decaying phonons, especially at low bombarding energies, because the incoherent (fluctuation) contribution to the excitation cross section becomes substantial relative to the coherent contribution.

## Method

The authors used a three-dimensional generalization of their previously developed coherent-plus-fluctuation (average-plus-fluctuation) model for Coulomb excitation of giant resonances in heavy-ion collisions, solving coupled equations of motion as a function of impact parameter to obtain asymptotic occupation probabilities. They combined this excitation model with the hybrid direct-plus-statistical decay model of Dias-Hussein-Adhikari (DHA) to decompose the decay of the double and triple giant dipole resonances into contributions from direct decay of collective phonons and decay through statistical states. Cross sections were calculated for various projectiles incident on a lead-208 target at bombarding energies of 10 MeV/nucleon, 100 MeV/nucleon, and 1 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

* For 40Ca on 208Pb at 10 MeV/nucleon, the coherent TGDR cross section was 0.01 mb, while the fluctuation contributions were 0.08 mb (two-phonon final state) and 0.11 mb (one-phonon final state).
* For 208Pb on 208Pb at 10 MeV/nucleon, the coherent TGDR cross section was 0.95 mb, while the fluctuation contributions were 12.24 mb (two-phonon final state) and 14.83 mb (one-phonon final state).
* For 208Pb on 208Pb at 100 MeV/nucleon, the coherent TGDR cross section was 5.28 mb, while the fluctuation contributions were 10.78 mb (two-phonon final state) and 3.36 mb (one-phonon final state).
* For 208Pb on 208Pb at 1 GeV/nucleon, the coherent TGDR cross section was 16.68 mb, while the fluctuation contributions were 2.40 mb (two-phonon final state) and 0.18 mb (one-phonon final state).
* For 208Pb on 208Pb at 100 MeV/nucleon, the coherent DGDR cross section was 96.95 mb and the fluctuation contribution was 72.87 mb.
* The coherent two-phonon cross section scales approximately as the projectile charge Z squared, while the three-phonon coherent cross section scales as Z cubed.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coherent-plus-fluctuation model for double giant dipole resonance excitation (Carlson et al., 1999) to the triple giant dipole resonance, and applies the hybrid decay model of Dias-Hussein-Adhikari (1986) to analyze the decay of these multiple phonon states. The paper provides theoretical estimates to guide experimental searches for the triple giant dipole resonance in nuclei, which were being planned at the time (Frascaria, 1999). The results show that at low bombarding energies (10 MeV/nucleon), the fluctuation contributions dominate over the coherent contributions, leading to direct decay cross sections that can be significantly larger than the harmonic limit prediction.
