# The elastic electron-deuteron scattering beyond one-photon exchange

> We discuss the elastic ed scattering beyond Born approximation. It is shown that the reaction amplitude contains six generalized form factors, but only three linearly independent combinations of them (we call them generalized charge, quadrupole and magnetic form factors) contribute to the reaction cross section in the second order perturbation theory. We examine two-photon exchange and find that it includes two types of diagrams, when two virtual photons interact with the same nucleon and when the photons interact with different nucleons. Estimations based on nonrelativistic calculations with the deuteron wave function for realistic NN potential show that the main contribution to the generalized charge, quadrupole and magnetic form factors comes from diagrams of the first type.

## Key claim

The paper shows that beyond the Born approximation, the elastic electron-deuteron scattering amplitude contains six generalized form factors, but only three linearly independent combinations (generalized charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors) contribute to the cross section in second order perturbation theory; the two-photon exchange amplitude is strongly connected with the deuteron wave function at short distances, and its main contribution comes from diagrams where both virtual photons interact with the same nucleon.

## Method

The authors performed a semi-relativistic calculation of the two-photon exchange amplitude for elastic electron-deuteron scattering in the Breit frame, using non-relativistic deuteron wave functions from realistic NN potentials (CD-Bonn and Paris). They separated the two-photon exchange into two diagram types: those where both photons interact with the same nucleon (type I) and those where photons interact with different nucleons (type II), and computed the generalized form factors by contracting the effective deuteron current with the electron current.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron wave function derivative at the origin for the Paris potential: \(u'(0) = 1.1978 \times 10^{-1}\) fm\(^{-3/2}\)
- The deuteron wave function derivative at the origin for the CD-Bonn potential: \(u'(0) = 3.1035 \times 10^{-1}\) fm\(^{-3/2}\)
- The D-wave contribution to the \(\mathcal S\) factor was found to contribute less than 10%
- The \(\mathcal S\) factor was calculated for \(Q^2 = 1\), 2, and 3 GeV\(^2\) for both CD-Bonn and Paris potentials

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of two-photon exchange in electron-deuteron scattering by DeForest (1968), Gunion (1971), Franco (1973), Boitsov (1974), Lev (1993), and Dong and Chen (2004), moving beyond Gaussian wave function approximations to use realistic NN potentials. The results enable more precise extraction of deuteron electromagnetic form factors from experimental data by accounting for two-photon exchange corrections, and the formalism developed can be applied to other light nuclei such as \(^3\)He and \(^4\)He.
