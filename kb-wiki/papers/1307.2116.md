# Angular Functions with Complex Angular Momenta

> In the study of the amplitudes for many-particle processes, and also for processes involving particles with spin, the use is made of matrix elements of the rotation group d^j_{μν}(z). In this paper the generalization of the functions d^j_{μν}(z) to arbitrary arguments and indices is studied. At the same time the functions of the second kind, analogous to Legendre functions of the second kind, are investigated. The results obtained play an important part in the introduction of complex angular momenta in many-particle processes. [Added in 2013: The generalized Legendre functions considered here may be applied as well to many other problems.]

# Angular Functions with Complex Angular Momenta

## Key claim

The paper provides a rigorous mathematical generalization of the rotation-group matrix elements \(d^j_{\mu\nu}(z)\) to arbitrary complex values of the angular momentum index \(j\), the helicity indices \(\mu\) and \(\nu\), and the argument \(z\), thereby enabling the continuation of partial-wave amplitudes for many-particle processes and processes with spin into the complex angular-momentum plane. The author notes that the functions \(d^j_{\mu\nu}(z)\) themselves have nonessential cuts in \(j\), \(\mu\), and \(\nu\), so the study instead focuses on generalized Legendre functions that differ from \(d^j_{\mu\nu}\) by a factor independent of \(z\).

## Method

The paper defines generalized Legendre functions of the first kind \(P^j_{\mu\nu}(z)\) and second kind \(Q^j_{\mu\nu}(z)\) for arbitrary complex indices and argument via hypergeometric functions \(F(a,b;c;x)\), and establishes their connection to Jacobi functions and associated Legendre functions. Recurrence relations, differentiation formulas, relations between functions of the first and second kinds, analytic properties (including cuts and discontinuities), asymptotic expansions for large indices or argument, and integral orthogonality relations are derived systematically. Addition and multiplication theorems are obtained in both integral and series forms, with the functions \(d^j_{\mu\nu}(x)\) for physical integer or half-integer indices recovered as a special normalized case.

## Key numbers

- The Wronskian determinant of \(P^j_{\mu\nu}\) and \(P^j_{\nu\mu}\) is \(\frac{2}{\pi} \frac{\sin\pi(\nu-\mu)}{1-z^2}\).
- The Wronskian determinant of \(Q^j_{\mu\nu}\) and \(Q^{-j-1}_{\nu\mu}\) is \(\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin 2\pi j}{\sin\pi(j+\mu)\,\sin\pi(j-\nu)} \frac{1}{1-z^2}\).
- The norm of \(\widetilde{P}^j_{\mu\nu}(x)\) for orthogonal systems on \([-1,1]\) is \(\int_{-1}^{+1} dx\,[\widetilde{P}^j_{\mu\nu}(x)]^2 = \frac{2}{2j+1} \frac{\Gamma(j+\mu+1)\,\Gamma(j-\nu+1)}{\Gamma(j-\mu+1)\,\Gamma(j+\nu+1)}\).
- The orthogonality relation for \(d^j_{\mu\nu}(x)\) is \(\int_{-1}^{+1} d^j_{\mu\nu}(x)\,d^l_{\mu\nu}(x)\,dx = \frac{2}{2j+1} \delta_{jl}\).
- The asymptotic behavior for \(j\to\infty\) with fixed \(\mu,\nu\) is \(Q^j_{\mu\nu}(\cosh\alpha) \sim e^{i\pi(\mu-\nu)} j^{\mu-\nu-1/2} \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2\sinh\alpha}} e^{-\alpha(j+1/2)}\).
- The limit relation to Bessel functions: \(\lim_{t\to\infty} \left[ t^{(\nu-\mu)/2} P^j_{\mu\nu}\left(\cosh\frac{y}{\sqrt{t}}\right)\right] = J_{\nu-\mu}(y)\), where \(t = (\nu+\mu)^2/4 - j(j+1)\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier treatment of \(d^j_{\mu\nu}(z)\) for physical helicities (integer or half-integer \(\mu,\nu\)) by Hoa (no year given) to fully arbitrary complex indices, motivated by the need to continue many-particle amplitudes with respect to both total angular momentum and helicities as suggested by Gribov, Pomeranchuk, and Ter-Martirosyan (no year given). The generalized Legendre functions are equivalent to hypergeometric functions, and the paper notes that the addition and multiplication theorems derived here correspond to nonlinear relations for hypergeometric functions not found in standard references such as Bateman (1953) or Gradshteyn and Ryzhik (no year given). The results enable the systematic introduction of complex angular momenta in many-particle scattering amplitudes and may be applied to other problems beyond nuclear physics.
