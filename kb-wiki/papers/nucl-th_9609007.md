# Uncertainties In Direct Neutron Capture Calculations Due To Nuclear Structure Models

> The prediction of cross sections for nuclei far off stability is crucial in the field of nuclear astrophysics. For spherical nuclei close to the dripline the statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) approach is not applicable and direct contributions may dominate the cross sections. For neutron-rich, even-even Sn targets, we compare the resulting neutron capture cross sections when consistently taking the input for the direct capture calculations from three different microscopic models. The results underline the sensitivity of cross sections calculated in the direct model to nuclear structure models which can lead to high uncertainties when lacking experimental information.

## Key claim
For neutron-rich Sn isotopes near the dripline, direct neutron capture cross sections calculated using three different microscopic nuclear-structure models (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov, Relativistic Mean Field Theory, and a Shell Model based on folded-Yukawa wave functions) can vary by orders of magnitude, even when the models yield similar values for other nuclear properties such as masses.

## Method
The authors calculated direct neutron capture cross sections in the optical model using folding potentials derived from nuclear target densities, with the densities consistently taken from each of the three microscopic models. Bound state potentials were adjusted to reproduce the given binding energy of the captured neutron, while scattering potentials were normalized to a volume integral of 425 MeV fm³ based on experimental scattering data on stable Sn isotopes. All nuclei were assumed spherical and spectroscopic factors were set to 1 to enable direct comparison between models.

## Key numbers
- Direct capture cross sections at 30 keV for Sn isotopes show variations of "order of magnitudes" between different microscopic models and between neighboring isotopes.
- The r-process path location differs between models: for RMFT and FYSM it goes through A ≈ 132–134, while for HFB it shifts to A ≈ 142–144.
- The neutron separation energy threshold for the r-process path is approximately 2 MeV.
- The volume integral for the scattering potential was fixed to 425 MeV fm³ based on experimental data.

## Context
This work extends a similar study previously performed for neutron-rich Pb isotopes (Rauscher et al., 1995) to the Sn isotopic chain, where the level density around the neutron magic number N=82 is too low for the statistical Hauser-Feshbach model to apply. The results demonstrate that direct capture calculations for nuclei far from stability remain highly uncertain even with state-of-the-art nuclear-structure models, and similar problems may be encountered for proton capture cross sections near the proton dripline.
