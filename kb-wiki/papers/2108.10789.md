# Transversity GPDs of the proton from lattice QCD

> We present the first calculation of the $x$-dependence of the isovector transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the proton within lattice QCD. We compute the matrix elements with non-local operators containing a Wilson line. The calculation implements the Breit symmetric frame. The proton momenta are chosen as $0.83,\,1.25,\,1.67$ GeV, and the values of the momentum transfer squared are $0.69,\,1.02$ GeV$^2$. These combinations include cases with zero and nonzero skewness. The calculation is performed using one ensemble of two degenerate-mass light, a strange and a charm quark of maximally twisted mass fermions with a clover term. The lattice results are renormalized non-perturbatively and finally matched to the light-cone GPDs using one-loop perturbation theory within the framework of large momentum effective theory. The final GPDs are given in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at a scale of 2 GeV. In addition to the individual GPDs, we form the combination of the transversity GPDs that is related to the transverse spin structure of the proton. Finally, we extract the lowest two moments of GPDs and draw a number of important qualitative conclusions.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the \(x\)-dependence of isovector transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the proton, using one ensemble with a pion mass of about 260 MeV and a single lattice spacing, meaning the results are subject to systematic uncertainties from chiral extrapolation and discretization effects that are not yet fully controlled.

## Method

The authors compute proton matrix elements of non-local operators containing a Wilson line in the \(z\)-direction, using the Breit symmetric frame with proton momenta of 0.83, 1.25, and 1.67 GeV and momentum transfers squared of 0.69 and 1.02 GeV\(^2\), including both zero and nonzero skewness cases. The calculation uses one ensemble of \(N_f=2+1+1\) maximally twisted mass fermions with a clover term on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice with spacing 0.093 fm. The bare matrix elements are renormalized non-perturbatively in an RI-type scheme and then matched to light-cone GPDs using one-loop perturbation theory within the framework of large momentum effective theory (LaMET), with final GPDs given in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme at a scale of 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Proton momenta used: 0.83, 1.25, and 1.67 GeV
- Momentum transfer squared values: 0.69 and 1.02 GeV\(^2\)
- Skewness values: 0 and \(\pm 1/3\)
- Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.093\) fm; lattice volume: \(32^3 \times 64\)
- Pion mass: approximately 260 MeV
- Source-sink separation: \(t_s = 1.12\) fm (12 lattice spacings)
- Number of configurations for the highest momentum (1.67 GeV, zero skewness): 1753 configurations with 112192 measurements
- Renormalization scale used for final results: \((a \mu_0)^2 \approx 2.57\)
- Final GPDs given in \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme at scale 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD calculation of chiral-even GPDs (Alexandrou et al., 2020) to the chiral-odd transversity sector, which is experimentally less accessible because transversity GPDs cannot be measured in deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS). The results enable future comparisons with experimental extractions from deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) at facilities such as the Electron Ion Collider, and provide the first lattice-based determination of the \(x\)-dependence of these distributions, complementing earlier work that was restricted to Mellin moments (reviewed by Syritsyn, 2014; Constantinou, 2014; Green, 2018; Lin, 2017; Constantinou, 2020).
