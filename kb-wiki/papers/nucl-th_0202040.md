# Evaluation of Effective Astrophysical S factor for Non-Resonant Reactions

> We derived analytic formulas of the effective S astrophysical S factor,S^eff for a non-resonant reaction of charged particles using a Taylor expension of the astrophysical S factor and a uniform approximation.The formulas will be able to generate generate more accurate approximation to S^eff than previous ones.

## Key claim

The paper derives new analytic formulas for the effective astrophysical S factor \(S_{\rm eff}\) for non-resonant charged-particle reactions, claiming they can generate more accurate approximations than previous stationary-phase methods, but the formulas are valid only if the upper edge of the Gamow window (\(E_0 + \Delta/2\)) lies within the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion of \(S(E)\).

## Method

The authors used a Taylor expansion of the astrophysical S factor \(S(E)\) around either the threshold energy (\(E=0\)) or the Gamow peak energy \(E_0\), combined with a uniform approximation (Brink, 1955) to derive two analytic expressions for \(S_{\rm eff}\). They applied these formulas to the \(^7\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)B reaction at solar interior temperatures, using the S-factor functional form from Jen (1998), and compared the approximate \(S_{\rm eff}\) values to the exact numerical integration.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^7\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)B reaction, the Gamow peak energy is \(E_0 \approx 20\) keV.
- At \(T = 1.3 \times 10^7\) K, the threshold-expanded formula \(S_{\rm eff-thd}\) converges to the exact result with \(n_M = 4\) and \(k_M = 1\), while the Gamow-expanded formula \(S_{\rm eff-Gam}\) converges with \(n_M = 2\) and \(k_M = 1\).
- At \(T = 1.7 \times 10^7\) K, the same convergence orders (\(n_M = 4\) for \(S_{\rm eff-thd}\), \(n_M = 2\) for \(S_{\rm eff-Gam}\), both with \(k_M = 1\)) are observed.

## Context

This work extends the standard stationary-phase approximation for \(S_{\rm eff}\) described in Clayton (1968) by incorporating higher-order corrections via the uniform approximation of Brink (1955). The new formulas are expected to be useful for high-temperature environments and reactions with strong energy dependence of \(S(E)\) around the Gamow peak, such as the \(^7\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)B reaction studied by Jen (1998). The results enable more accurate stellar evolution calculations, though the authors note that systematic confirmation of the uniform approximation's reliability is still needed.
