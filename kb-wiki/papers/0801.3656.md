# Hadronic Uncertainties in the Elastic Scattering of Supersymmetric Dark Matter

> We review the uncertainties in the spin-independent and -dependent elastic scattering cross sections of supersymmetric dark matter particles on protons and neutrons. We propagate the uncertainties in quark masses and hadronic matrix elements that are related to the $π$-nucleon $σ$ term and the spin content of the nucleon. By far the largest single uncertainty is that in spin-independent scattering induced by our ignorance of the $<N | {\bar q} q | N>$ matrix elements linked to the $π$-nucleon $σ$ term, which affects the ratio of cross sections on proton and neutron targets as well as their absolute values. This uncertainty is already impacting the interpretations of experimental searches for cold dark matter. {\it We plead for an experimental campaign to determine better the $π$-nucleon $σ$ term.} Uncertainties in the spin content of the proton affect significantly, but less strongly, the calculation of rates used in indirect searches.

## Key claim

The spin-independent elastic scattering cross section of supersymmetric dark matter on nucleons can vary by almost an order of magnitude depending on the value of the pi-nucleon sigma term, with the dominant uncertainty coming from ignorance of the nucleon matrix elements of scalar quark densities rather than from the supersymmetric model parameters themselves.

## Method

The authors propagated uncertainties in quark masses, the pi-nucleon sigma term (SigmapiN), and the spin content of the nucleon through the calculation of spin-independent and spin-dependent neutralino-nucleon elastic scattering cross sections within the constrained minimal supersymmetric extension of the Standard Model (CMSSM). They used specific CMSSM benchmark scenarios and surveyed strips in the (m_{1/2}, m_0) plane for tan beta = 10 and 50 where stau-neutralino coannihilation maintains the relic density within the range favored by WMAP.

## Key numbers

* Spin-independent cross section varies by almost an order of magnitude for 48 MeV less than SigmapiN less than 80 MeV, the plus or minus 2-sigma range of the pi-nucleon sigma term.
* The pi-nucleon sigma term is taken as SigmapiN = 64 plus or minus 8 MeV, derived from Sigma_CD = 79 plus or minus 7 MeV corrected by minus 15 MeV.
* The strange scalar density parameter sigma_0 = 36 plus or minus 7 MeV, based on octet baryon mass differences.
* The spin-dependent cross section varies by a factor of approximately 2 when propagating the plus or minus 2-sigma uncertainties in Delta_s^(p) = -0.09 plus or minus 0.03.
* The axial-current combinations are a_3^(p) = 1.2695 plus or minus 0.0029 and a_8^(p) = 0.585 plus or minus 0.025.
* Light quark mass ratios: m_u/m_d = 0.553 plus or minus 0.043, m_s/m_d = 18.9 plus or minus 0.8.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronic uncertainties in neutralino-nucleon scattering by Ellis, Olive, and collaborators (2005) and by prior authors, quantifying how uncertainties in the pi-nucleon sigma term and nucleon spin content affect interpretation of direct detection experiments such as CDMS, XENON10, ZEPLIN-II, and KIMS, as well as indirect searches via neutrino telescopes like Super-Kamiokande and AMANDA/IceCube. The authors call for an experimental campaign to better determine the pi-nucleon sigma term, as this uncertainty already impacts the interpretation of experimental searches for cold dark matter and will be critical for identifying the nature of any detected signal through comparison of scattering rates on different nuclear targets.
