# Nonforward Compton scattering in AdS/CFT correspondence

> We study the nonforward Compton scattering in particular the deeply virtual Compton scattering from AdS/CFT. We first calculate the contributions from the s-channel and u-channel supergravity diagrams as well as the four point interaction diagram which correspond to the Compton scatterings on a dilaton target in CFT. Furthermore, we study the Compton scattering on a dilatino target. Assuming that protons can be identified as supergravity modes of dilatino, we compare the calculated DVCS cross section to the low-energy experimental data from the H1 and ZEUS collaborations and find good agreement. We also discuss the t-channel graviton exchange contribution and show that it should be dominant in the high-energy limit.

## Key claim

The authors compute nonforward Compton scattering amplitudes in AdS/CFT for dilaton and dilatino targets, and show that the deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) cross section calculated under the assumption that protons can be identified as supergravity modes of the dilatino agrees with low-energy experimental data from H1 and ZEUS, with the caveat that the t-channel graviton exchange contribution is expected to dominate at high energies and is treated separately.

## Method

The authors compute supergravity diagrams in AdS_5 x S^5 space: s-channel and u-channel exchange diagrams with summation over Kaluza-Klein excitations of intermediate states, plus a four-point contact interaction for the dilaton target, and s-channel and u-channel diagrams for the dilatino target. The virtual photon is represented by a non-normalizable Kaluza-Klein gauge field (R-current) satisfying Maxwell's equations in the bulk, while the targets are normalizable supergravity modes (dilaton as scalar, dilatino as spin-1/2 fermion) with an infrared cutoff at z = z_0 to mimic confinement. The total amplitude is shown to satisfy current conservation, and the DVCS cross section is computed in the target rest frame with the final photon taken as real.

## Key numbers

- The dilaton wave function satisfies the equation of motion with fifth dimension mass mu^2 = Delta(Delta-4)/R^2, and the normalizable solution is phi(z) = C_1 z^2 J_{Delta-2}(Mz) with boundary condition J_{Delta-2}(Mz_0)=0, where M^2 = -p^2 and z_0 ~ 1/Lambda_QCD.
- The real Compton scattering cross section for a dilaton target is proportional to (1+cos^2 theta), identical in angular dependence to scalar electrodynamics.
- The DVCS cross section for a dilaton target is given by dsigma^Un/dcos(theta) = [omega'^2/(16 M^2 q_0^2 (1-x))] Q^4 (c_i^2 c_f^2/z_0^4) C_{1DVCS}^2 [1+cos^2 theta + chi sin^2 theta], where chi = q^2/(4M^2 (1-x)^2) (1+4M^2 x/q^2)^2, and when q^2 >> 4M^2 one finds chi >> 1.
- The DVCS cross section for a dilatino target is compared to low-energy H1 and ZEUS data and found to be consistent.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT approach to deep inelastic scattering (DIS) developed by Polchinski and Strassler (2001, 2002) from forward to nonforward Compton scattering, enabling future extraction of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) which encode the total spin of constituents in hadrons as proposed by Ji (1996) in QCD. The calculation builds on prior four-point function studies in AdS supergravity by Liu (1998), Freedman (1998), and D'Hoker (1999), and on the authors' own previous work on polarized DIS (Gao and Xiao, 2009). The results enable further investigation of spin and orbital angular momentum decomposition of hadrons at strong coupling, and the t-channel graviton exchange calculation provides a framework for studying the high-energy limit where this contribution dominates.
