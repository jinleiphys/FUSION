# Lindblad-driven recombination of the X(3872) tetraquark

> The internal structure of the exotic meson X(3872) remains an open question. We investigate its production in heavy-ion collisions under the hypothesis that it is a compact tetraquark. To this end, we derive a coalescence model from the Lindblad equation, assuming that unbound heavy quarks are thermalized within the quark-gluon plasma and that the adiabatic approximation holds. Using this model, we predict the nuclear modification factor of the X(3872) at LHC energies, with proton-proton baseline cross sections estimated from available experimental data. We also consider the effect of simplifying assumptions on the model, and a complementary approach based on chemical equilibration. Our results indicate that recombination is the dominant production mechanism for a tetraquark X(3872). It leads to a significant yield enhancement in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that the nuclear modification factor is a powerful observable for probing the exotic nature of this state

## Key claim

Recombination (coalescence) is the dominant production mechanism for the X(3872) in heavy-ion collisions if it is a compact tetraquark, leading to a nuclear modification factor potentially larger than one; however, the authors emphasize that their results are order-of-magnitude estimates intended to highlight the discriminating power of the observable, and that the adiabatic approximation and the assumption of thermalized unbound heavy quarks are key conditions for the model.

## Method

The authors derive a coalescence model from the Lindblad equation for a heavy quark-antiquark pair in an octet configuration, assuming unbound heavy quarks are thermalized within the quark-gluon plasma and that the adiabatic approximation holds. They embed this model in a Bjorken-expanding medium using the complex potential from their earlier work (Armesto 2024), with the imaginary part treated as a temperature-dependent constant. The recombination rate is expressed in terms of the Wigner distribution of the bound state, and the final yield is obtained by integrating the product of the recombination rate and the survival probability over the medium evolution.

## Key numbers

- The dissociation temperature of the X(3872) tetraquark is T_d approximately 350 MeV, obtained from solving the Schrodinger equation with the complex potential.
- The survival probability of an initially produced X(3872) in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies is approximately 0.1 to 0.3 at freeze-out, using initial temperature T_0 = 500 MeV and initial time t_0 = 0.6 fm.
- The double ratio R_double = (N_X(3872)/N_psi(2S))_PbPb / (N_X(3872)/N_psi(2S))_pp = 10.8 +/- 7.5, derived from CMS data.
- Using R_AA(psi(2S)) = 0.15 +/- 0.075, the inferred R_AA(X(3872)) = 1.62 +/- 1.38.
- The effective proton-proton cross section for prompt X(3872) production at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV is sigma_pp->X = 0.126 microbarn, measured in rapidity range 2.5-4.5 and transverse momentum 5-20 GeV/c.
- The midrapidity differential cross section for charm quark production at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV is dsigma_pp->c-cbar/dy = 1.165 mb (p_T-integrated down to 0).

## Context

This work extends the potential model for the X(3872) as a compact tetraquark developed by Armesto (2024), which previously only considered dissociation and neglected recombination. The enhancement of the X(3872)/psi(2S) ratio observed by the CMS Collaboration (2021) in Pb-Pb collisions cannot be explained by suppression models alone, motivating the inclusion of regeneration via coalescence. The results should be confronted with future studies assuming a molecular structure for the X(3872) and with forthcoming experimental measurements in heavy-ion collisions, as the nuclear modification factor is proposed as a discriminating observable for the exotic nature of this state.
