# Nuclear polarization effects in muonic atoms

> We illustrate how nuclear polarization corrections in muonic atoms can be formally connected to inelastic response functions of a nucleus. We first discuss the point-nucleon approximation and then include finite-nucleon-size corrections. As an example, we compare our ab-initio calculation of the third Zemach moment in the muonic Helium-4 ion to previous phenomenological results.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nuclear polarization corrections in muonic atoms can be formally connected to inelastic nuclear response functions, and that the non-relativistic nuclear polarization effects can be systematically expanded in powers of the small parameter \(\sqrt{m_r/M_A}\), where \(m_r\) is the muon-nucleus reduced mass and \(M_A\) is the nuclear mass.

## Method

The authors start from second-order perturbation theory on the difference between the muonic Coulomb interaction with a point nucleus and the sum of interactions with individual protons. They derive expressions for nuclear polarization in the point-nucleon approximation, then include finite-nucleon-size corrections by replacing point-proton interactions with convolutions over proton and neutron charge form factors. As an example, they compute the third Zemach moment in muonic Helium-4 using ab-initio nuclear wavefunctions from the AV18/UIX and EFT N\(^3\)LO (NN)/N\(^2\)LO (NNN) potentials.

## Key numbers

- For \(\mu\,^4\)He\(^+\), the phenomenological model gives \(\delta_{Zem} = -6.65(19)\) meV using \(R_c = 1.681\) fm from electron scattering data (Sick, 2008).
- Using the AV18/UIX potential with \(\langle r_p^2\rangle^{1/2} = 0.8775\) fm from electron-hydrogen data, the sum \(\delta^{(1)}_{Z3}+\delta^{(1)}_{Z1} = 6.12\) meV.
- Using the EFT potential with the same proton radius, \(\delta^{(1)}_{Z3}+\delta^{(1)}_{Z1} = 6.53\) meV.
- Averaging the two ab-initio results gives \(\delta^{(1)}_{Z3}+\delta^{(1)}_{Z1} = 6.32(21)\) meV, consistent with the phenomenological value.

## Context

This work extends the formalism connecting nuclear polarization to response functions, building on prior calculations in muonic deuterium and muonic Helium-4 by Ji (2013), Leidemann (1994), Pachucki (2011), and Friar (2013). The results enable more accurate extraction of nuclear charge radii from planned spectroscopy measurements of muonic deuterium, muonic Helium-3, and muonic Helium-4 at PSI (Antognini, 2011), which aim to resolve the proton radius puzzle.
