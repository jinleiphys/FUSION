# Thermal Pions

> I discuss the absorption and dispersion of pions in hot matter. A two-loop calculation in the framework of chiral perturbation theory is presented and its result is compactly written in terms of the two- and three-particle forward $ππ$ scattering amplitudes. At modest temperatures, $T \le 100$ MeV, the change in the pion mass is small and its dispersion law closely resembles the free space one. At these temperatures, all quantities of interest are given to a good degree of accuracy by the first term in the virial expansion which is linear in the density. ## Key claim

At modest temperatures (T ≤ 100 MeV), the change in the pion mass is small and its dispersion law closely resembles the free-space one; all quantities of interest are given to a good degree of accuracy by the first term in the virial expansion which is linear in the density, with second-order (n_B²) effects being tiny.

## Method

The author performed a two-loop calculation of the pion self-energy in the framework of chiral perturbation theory (CHPT), using a real-time approach at finite temperature. The result is expressed compactly in terms of the forward ππ scattering amplitude (for the linear-in-density term) and a properly defined retarded 3→3 forward scattering amplitude (for the quadratic-in-density term). The calculation is correct up to and including order p⁶ in CHPT, and the results are compared with a virial expansion using a phenomenological parametrization of the ππ scattering amplitude that extends to energies of about 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At T = 100 MeV, the effective pion mass M_π(T) is lowered by 2.5 percent; at T = 150 MeV, it is lowered by 14 percent (full two-loop CHPT result).
- The second-order (n_B²) corrections to the pion mass amount to a shift of 2.5 percent at T = 150 MeV.
- The two-loop CHPT result and the phenomenological analysis of the quasi-particle energy ω(p) agree within 20 percent at temperatures and momenta below 150 MeV.
- At T = 150 MeV, scattering with the gas modifies the frequency ω(p) by less than 20 percent compared to free space.
- The mean damping rate from the leading virial expansion term decreases by a factor of two between T = 100 MeV and T = 200 MeV.
- Using the imaginary parts of the one-loop CHPT prediction for the ππ scattering amplitude gives a mean damping rate that agrees within 30 percent with the virial expansion at T < 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop CHPT calculations of Goity and Leutwyler (1990), Shuryak (1990), and Schenk (1991) to two-loop order, and also builds on the finite-temperature CHPT framework developed by Gerber and Leutwyler (1989). The results contradict earlier suggestions by Shuryak (1990) of a substantial change in the dispersion law at low temperatures. The findings enable future experiments at facilities with higher pion densities and lower temperatures to use the virial expansion (linear in density) as an accurate description, with the understanding that above 100 MeV, contributions from massive states (K, ρ, η) must be included.
