# Roles of crust and core in the tidal deformability of neutron stars

> With the recent measurement of GW170817 providing constraints on the tidal deformability of a neutron star, it is very important to understand what features of the equation of state have the biggest effect on it. We therefore study the contribution of the crust to the tidal deformability and the moment of inertia of a neutron star for a variety of well-known equations of state. It is found that the contributions to these quantities from the low density crust are typically quite small and as a result the determination of the tidal deformability provides an important constraint on the equation of state of dense matter.

## Key claim

The low-density crust contributes only a small fraction (typically ≤10%) to the tidal deformability and moment of inertia of neutron stars, meaning that gravitational-wave constraints on tidal deformability directly probe the dense-matter equation of state rather than crust physics. The authors stress that this conclusion holds despite the crust having a large effect on the mass-radius relation and compactness.

## Method

The authors selected ten equations of state (EoS) from an online database and from the Quark Meson Coupling model, covering maximum masses from 1.8 to 2.5 solar masses and radii from 9 to 14 km. For each EoS, they removed the low-density region (LDR, the crust) by fitting a polytropic form to the core EoS above 100 MeV/fm³ and extrapolating to zero density, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius curves and computed the tidal deformability and moment of inertia for both the full and core-only cases. The percentage difference between the full and core-only results was evaluated at fixed neutron-star mass.

## Key numbers

- For neutron stars with mass above 1.0 solar masses, the crustal contribution to the moment of inertia satisfies |ΔI/I| < 9.0%.
- For a typical 1.4-solar-mass neutron star, |ΔI/I| < 4.5% and |ΔΛ/Λ| < 11%.
- For all neutron stars with mass above 1.0 solar masses, the crustal contribution to the tidal deformability satisfies |ΔΛ/Λ| < 15%.
- The GW170817 constraint on the tidal deformability is 70 ≤ Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580 (Abbott 2018, Abbott 2019), and the corresponding constraint on the dimensionless moment of inertia is 8.82 ≤ Ī ≤ 14.74 (Landry 2018).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of GW170817 (Abbott 2018, Abbott 2019) by quantifying how much the crust affects the tidal deformability and moment of inertia across a wide range of EoS, including those with and without hyperons. It contradicts the suggestion by Yagi (2013) that the universal I-Love relation might depend more on the low-density region than on the core; the authors show the I-Love relation remains unchanged when the crust is removed for masses above 1.0 solar masses. The results enable more confident use of tidal deformability measurements to constrain the dense-matter equation of state, while noting that crust modelling remains crucial for radius predictions relevant to the NICER mission (Piekarewicz 2018).
