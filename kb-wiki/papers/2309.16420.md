# Analysis of $Ξ(1620)$ resonance with chiral unitary approach

> Recently, the $Ξ(1620)$ resonance has attracted much attention, thanks to detailed experimental data by the Belle and ALICE collaborations. This experimental progress has prepared us to conduct theoretical analyses based on experimental data. In this study, we analyze the $Ξ(1620)$ resonance using the chiral unitary model and discuss the properties of the $Ξ(1620)$ resonance and the $K^-Λ$ scattering length.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary analysis shows that the Belle and ALICE experimental results for the Xi(1620) resonance cannot be simultaneously reproduced within the same model, even when experimental errors are taken into account, suggesting that the interpretation of Xi(1620) as a shallow quasibound state below the Kbar-Lambda threshold is incompatible with the K- Lambda scattering length determined by ALICE.

## Method

The authors use the chiral unitary model with the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction kernel for S-wave meson-baryon scattering in a coupled-channel framework including pi-Xi, Kbar-Lambda, Kbar-Sigma, and eta-Xi channels. The free parameters are the subtraction constants a_i in the dimensionally regularized loop functions, which are adjusted to reproduce either the assumed pole position from the Belle data (Model 1) or the K- Lambda scattering length from ALICE (Model 2), while fixing a_KbarSigma = a_etaXi = -2 following Ramos (2002).

## Key numbers

* Model 1: pole position assumed at z = 1610 - 30i MeV based on Belle results (mass M_R = 1610 MeV, width Gamma_R = 60 MeV), reproduced with subtraction constants a_piXi = -4.19 and a_KbarLambda = -0.14 to within 1 MeV accuracy
* Model 2: K- Lambda scattering length a_0 = -0.27 - 0.40i fm (central value from ALICE), reproduced with subtraction constants a_piXi = -2.90 and a_KbarLambda = 0.36 to within 0.01 fm accuracy
* The K- Lambda threshold energy is 1609.4 MeV
* The quasivirtual pole in Model 2 is estimated at 1701 + 228i MeV on the [ttbttt] Riemann sheet using effective range expansion

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to Xi(1620) previously used by Ramos (2002), who predicted a pole at 1607 - 140i MeV with all subtraction constants set to -2, and by Feijoo (2023), who included higher-order chiral Lagrangian terms. The Belle collaboration (2019) measured the pi+ Xi- invariant mass distribution in Xi_c -> pi pi Xi decays, reporting a narrower width than earlier theoretical predictions, while the ALICE collaboration (2021) determined the K- Lambda scattering length from correlation functions in Pb-Pb collisions. The present analysis finds these two experimental constraints cannot be simultaneously satisfied, indicating that direct comparison of the model with the pi+ Xi- invariant mass distribution (rather than assuming a Breit-Wigner pole position) is needed for future work.
