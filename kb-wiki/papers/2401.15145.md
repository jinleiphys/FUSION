# Long-lived doubly charged scalars in the left-right symmetric model: catalyzed nuclear fusion and collider implications

> We show that the doubly charged scalar from the $SU(2)_R$-triplet Higgs field in the Left-Right Symmetric Model has its mass governed by a hidden symmetry so that its value can be much lower than the $SU(2)_R$ breaking scale. This makes it a long-lived particle while being consistent with all existing theoretical and experimental constraints. Such long-lived doubly charged scalars have the potential to trigger catalyzed fusion processes in light nuclei, which may have important applications for energy production. We show that it could also bear consequences on the excess of large ionization energy loss ($dE/dx$) recently observed in collider experiments.

## Key claim

The doubly charged scalar $\Delta_R^{\pm\pm}$ from the $SU(2)_R$-triplet Higgs field in the Left-Right Symmetric Model can have its mass decoupled from the $SU(2)_R$ breaking scale due to a hidden global $SU(3)$ symmetry, making it a long-lived particle that could trigger catalyzed nuclear fusion and explain a recent ionization energy loss excess observed at the LHC, provided the quartic coupling $\rho_2$ and other symmetry-breaking parameters are tuned to small values.

## Method

The authors analyze the scalar potential of the minimal Left-Right Symmetric Model based on $SU(3)_c \times SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times U(1)_{B-L}$, showing that when $\rho_2$ and $\alpha_3$ are set to zero, the potential has a global $SU(3)$ symmetry broken by the $\Delta_R$ vacuum expectation value, making the doubly charged scalar a pseudo-Goldstone boson. They compute the dominant decay modes via Yukawa couplings to same-sign leptons and via off-shell $W_R$ bosons, and derive lifetime contours in the Yukawa coupling versus mass plane and in the $W_R$ mass versus $H^{\pm\pm}$ mass plane. For collider implications, they implement the model in FeynRules and MadGraph5aMC@NLO to calculate production cross-sections via Drell-Yan, photon fusion, and gluon fusion through a new neutral scalar $\chi^0$ from an inverse seesaw extension, comparing with ATLAS data on long-lived particle searches.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on $H^{\pm\pm}$ mass from LHC long-lived particle searches: 700 GeV (for $f_R \lesssim 10^{-8}$)
- Lifetime benchmark for catalyzed nuclear fusion: $\tau = 9.5 \times 10^{11}$ sec
- Lifetime benchmark equal to age of Universe: $\tau_U = 4.34 \times 10^{17}$ sec
- For $m_{H^{\pm\pm}} \sim$ TeV and $m_{W_R} \sim 10^{10}$ GeV, the $H^{\pm\pm}$ lifetime exceeds $\tau_U$
- ATLAS observed 7 events with $dE/dx > 2.4$ MeV g$^{-1}$ cm$^2$ against background $0.7 \pm 0.4$ events, local significance $3.6\sigma$
- Preferred cross-section range to explain $dE/dx$ excess at 95% CL: 0.10--0.19 fb
- Allowed $\chi^0$ mass range for $dE/dx$ explanation: 2.2 TeV to 3.1 TeV
- Allowed $H^{\pm\pm}$ mass range for $dE/dx$ explanation: 700 GeV up to $m_{\chi^0}/2$
- Upper limit on mixing angle $\sin\theta < 0.33$ from CMS Higgs signal strength data
- For $v_R \sim 10^7$ GeV, fine-tuning at level $10^{-4}$ is sufficient to keep $H^{\pm\pm}$ mass at TeV scale

## Context

This work extends the analysis of long-lived doubly charged scalars in the Left-Right Symmetric Model, building on the catalyzed nuclear fusion mechanism proposed by Akhmedov (2021) and the ionization energy loss phenomenology discussed by Jager (2018) and Altakach (2022). The paper addresses the recent ATLAS excess in $dE/dx$ searches (ATLAS 2022), which was previously examined by Giudice (2022), and shows that the LRSM with an inverse seesaw extension can explain this excess while maintaining consistency with neutrino mass generation. The results enable future searches for long-lived doubly charged scalars at the high-luminosity LHC and motivate further study of catalyzed nuclear fusion as a potential energy source.
