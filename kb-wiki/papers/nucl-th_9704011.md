# Correlations and the Cross Section of Exclusive ($e,e'p$) Reactions for $^{16}$O

> The reduced cross section for exclusive ($e,e'p$) reactions has been studied in DWIA for the example of the nucleus $^{16}$O using a spectral function containing effects of correlations. The spectral function is evaluated directly for the finite nucleus starting from a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction within the framework of the Green's function approach. The emphasis is focused on the correlations induced by excitation modes at low energies described within a model-space of shell-model configurations including states up to the $sdg$ shell. Cross sections for the $p$-wave quasi-hole transitions at low missing energies are presented and compared with the most recent experimental data. In the case of the so-called perpendicular kinematics the reduced cross section derived in DWIA shows an enhancement at high missing momenta as compared to the PWIA result. Furthermore the cross sections for the $s$- and $d$-wave quasi-hole transitions are presented and compared to available data at low missing momenta. Also in these cases, which cannot be described in a model without correlations, a good agreement with the experiment is obtained.

## Key claim

The reduced cross section for exclusive ($e,e'p$) reactions on $^{16}$O, calculated in DWIA using a spectral function that includes long-range correlations from low-energy excitation modes, shows enhancement at high missing momenta compared to PWIA results; however, this enhancement is attributed primarily to final state interactions rather than to correlation effects, and the agreement with experimental data for $s$- and $d$-wave quasi-hole transitions (which cannot be described in a model without correlations) is achieved specifically at low missing momenta.

## Method

The authors computed the hole spectral function for $^{16}$O by solving the Dyson equation within a Green's function approach, using a model space of harmonic oscillator single-particle states up to the $sdg$ shell (oscillator parameter $b=1.76$ fm) and a $G$-matrix derived from the Reid soft-core potential. Long-range correlations were included via second-order self-energy terms (2p1h and 2h1p configurations) with residual interactions between these configurations. The reduced cross sections for ($e,e'p$) reactions were then calculated in the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) using relativistic optical potentials for the outgoing proton and an effective momentum approach for electron Coulomb distortion.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator parameter $b = 1.76$ fm (corresponding to $\hbar\omega = 13.3$ MeV) used for the harmonic oscillator basis describing bound single-particle states in $^{16}$O.
- Constant starting energy $\omega = -30$ MeV chosen for the Bethe-Goldstone equation solution for the $G$-matrix.
- Experimental data for reduced cross sections were measured at NIKHEF for missing momenta up to around 300 MeV and at MAMI in Mainz for missing momenta up to 700 MeV.
- The Coulomb distortion shift in electron energy was set to about 4 MeV for $^{16}$O.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of short-range correlations in nuclear spectral functions (Pieper, Co', Benhar, and others) which found that momentum distributions at low missing energies are well approximated by the independent-particle model, and addresses the observation by Adam and collaborators that heavy nuclei like $^{208}$Pb show a small enhancement at high missing momenta attributed to long-range correlations. The paper enables a more reliable comparison between correlated spectral functions and experimental ($e,e'p$) data by using DWIA rather than PWIA, and demonstrates that for $^{16}$O the $s$- and $d$-wave quasi-hole transitions (absent in the independent-particle model) can be described when long-range correlations are included.
