# Regulariztion dependence of S=0 and S=-1 meson-baryon system in the chiral unitary model

> We investigate the dependence of s-wave meson-baryon scattering amplitudes on different regularizations within the framework of the chiral unitary model. We employ two different regularization schemes, i.e. dimensional and form-factor regulariaztions to tame the divergences in the model. We also study the analytic structures of T-matrices, using those regularization schemes. We find that while the form-factor regularizaion produces almost the same results as the dimensional regularization did, the on-shell approximation is to some extent limited in the case of the form-factor regularization. Having chosen parameters properly, we show that the regularization dependences can be minimized.

# Regularization dependence of S=0 and S=-1 meson-baryon system in the chiral unitary model

## Key claim
While the form-factor regularization produces almost the same results as dimensional regularization for s-wave meson-baryon scattering amplitudes, the on-shell approximation is to some extent limited in the case of form-factor regularization due to the appearance of an unphysical branch cut; however, having chosen parameters properly, the regularization dependences can be minimized.

## Method
The authors constructed pseudo-potentials from the lowest-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term of the effective chiral Lagrangian and solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation using the on-mass-shell factorization approximation. They employed three regularization schemes to tame divergences: dimensional regularization (DIM), monopole-type form factor (MF), and dipole-type form factor (DF), with the subtraction parameters for the form-factor regularizations fitted at threshold to match the dimensional regularization results. The analytic structure of the T-matrices was studied by extending to the complex energy plane to find poles corresponding to resonances.

## Key numbers

- Renormalization scale for DIM: μ = 1200 MeV for S=0 sector; μ = 630 MeV for S=-1 sector
- Cut-off parameter for form factors: Λ = 1000 MeV for both DF and MF in both sectors
- Meson decay constants used: fπ = 93.0 MeV, fη = 120.9 MeV, fK = 113.46 MeV for S=0; average f = 107.18 MeV for S=-1
- N*(1535) pole positions: DIM at (1516 - 37i) MeV; DF at (1502 - 41i) MeV; MF at (1517 - 41i) MeV
- Λ(1405) region poles in S=-1, I=0 channel: DIM gave poles at (1398 - 74i) MeV, (1429 - 14i) MeV, and (1688 - 22i) MeV; DF gave (1392 - 74i) MeV, (1422 - 18i) MeV, and (1663 - 9i) MeV; MF gave (1394 - 74i) MeV, (1425 - 17i) MeV, and (1671 - 25i) MeV
- Coupling strengths |gi| of N*(1535) to KΣ channel: DIM = 5.17, DF = 3.23, MF = 3.54
- Subtraction parameters for S=0 DIM: aπN = 2.0, aηN = 0.2, aKΛ = 1.6, aKΣ = -2.8

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary model studies of meson-baryon scattering by Kaiser et al. (1995), Oset et al. (1997), and Oller et al. (2000), who used dimensional regularization, by systematically comparing with form-factor regularization schemes. The paper demonstrates that the N*(1535) and Λ(1405) resonances are dynamically generated regardless of regularization choice, consistent with earlier findings by Inoue et al. (2001) and Oset et al. (2001). The results enable future work to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation without the on-shell approximation by employing form factors instead of dimensional regularization, and highlight that regularization dependence becomes significant above 1500 MeV, particularly affecting the π- p → K0 Σ0 cross section and the coupling strengths of resonances.
