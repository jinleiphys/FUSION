# Bayesian approach to long-range correlations and multiplicity fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The number of particles detected in a nucleus-nucleus collision strongly depends on the impact parameter of the collision. Therefore, multiplicity fluctuations, as well as rapidity correlations of multiplicities, are dominated by impact parameter fluctuations. We present a method based on Bayesian inference which allows for a robust reconstruction of fluctuations and correlations at fixed impact parameter. We apply the method to ATLAS data on the distribution of charged multiplicity and transverse energy. We argue that multiplicity fluctuations are smaller at large rapidity than around central rapidity. We suggest simple, new analyses, in order to confirm this effect.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Bayesian method to reconstruct multiplicity fluctuations and correlations at fixed impact parameter in nucleus-nucleus collisions, arguing that the trivial correlation due to impact parameter variation can be removed without relying on any specific model of collision dynamics. The authors claim that multiplicity fluctuations are smaller at large rapidity than around central rapidity, but note this effect requires confirmation through new analyses.

## Method

The method assumes that for fixed impact parameter, the joint distribution of observables (such as transverse energy and charged multiplicity) is a multivariate Gaussian, justified by the central limit theorem due to large particle numbers. The measured distribution is integrated over impact parameter, with the mean values and covariance matrix parametrized as exponentials of polynomials in centrality fraction. A standard chi-squared fit is performed to match the ATLAS data histogram, and the reconstruction is validated using the TRENTo 3D model where impact parameter is known.

## Context

This work extends the single-variable Bayesian reconstruction method of Das (2017) to multiple observables, enabling the study of rapidity correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions. Previous experimental results were limited to forward-backward correlations (PHOBOS 2006, STAR 2009, ATLAS 2016, ALICE 2017) which have reduced sensitivity to impact parameter fluctuations. The method enables reconstruction of the covariance matrix for central collisions and the projection perpendicular to the ridge line for all impact parameters, and could be implemented directly by experimental collaborations.
