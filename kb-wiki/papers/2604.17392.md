# Boost-invariant perfect Fermi-Dirac spin hydrodynamics

> We analyze the effect of using the Fermi-Dirac statistics, rather than its Boltzmann approximation, in numerical simulations of perfect spin hydrodynamics of particles with spin 1/2. The system considered is boost invariant, transversely homogeneous, with corrections to the baryon current and the energy-momentum tensor that are second order in the spin polarization tensor $ω$, and the spin tensor considered is first order in $ω$. The study shows the feasibility of this approach, as the special functions defined by integrals that appear in the coefficients in the Fermi-Dirac case can be conveniently parametrized. For sets of initial conditions used in previous works, the differences in parameter evolution between the two underlying particle statistics are about one order of magnitude smaller than corrections coming from spin feedback. We also discuss when and why the numerical solutions of the equations of perfect spin hydrodynamics break down for very large values of spin polarization in one of the geometric configurations considered.

## Key claim

Using exact Fermi-Dirac statistics instead of the Boltzmann approximation in perfect spin hydrodynamics simulations is feasible because the special functions appearing in the Fermi-Dirac case can be conveniently parametrized, but the differences in parameter evolution between the two statistics are about one order of magnitude smaller than corrections coming from spin feedback, at least for the sets of initial conditions used in previous works.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the equations of perfect spin hydrodynamics for spin-1/2 particles in a boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous (Bjorken) geometry, with the baryon current and energy-momentum tensor expanded to second order in the spin polarization tensor ω and the spin tensor expanded to first order in ω. They constructed interpolating functions for the Fermi-Dirac integrals J_{mnk}(ξ,z) by tabulating their logarithms on a grid with ξ from -100 to 100 and z from 0.1 to 100, and compared the evolution of the spin polarization coefficients C_k and C_ω in two geometric configurations (longitudinal and transverse) against the Boltzmann approximation.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time τ₀ = 1 fm, final proper time τ_f = 10 fm
- Particle mass m = 1.116 GeV (mass of the Λ hyperon)
- Initial temperature T₀ = 155 MeV, initial baryon chemical potential μ₀ = 800 MeV
- Relative differences between Fermi-Dirac and Boltzmann cases reached about 0.085 (8.5%) in one case (transverse configuration, panel (f) of Fig. 2)
- In the longitudinal configuration, numerical solutions developed singularities for initial magnitudes of coefficients of ω above approximately 0.87
- In the longitudinal configuration, the denominator of the single ODE for T goes to zero at C₀ ≈ 2.0568 (Boltzmann case), and configurations with starting conditions in the interval approximately 0.8697 < C₀ < 4.4809 exhibit a singularity within τ = 1 to 10 fm
- The transverse configuration solver reached τ_f for any initial coefficient values tested, even of order 10⁶

## Context

This work extends previous numerical simulations of relativistic spin hydrodynamics that were limited to the Boltzmann approximation (Florkowski 2019, Drogosz 2024, Singh 2024, Sapna 2025). It builds on the hybrid approach to spin hydrodynamics introduced by Florkowski (2024) and Drogosz (2024), and on the Fermi-Dirac case analysis by Drogosz (2025) and Kar (2025). The study enables more accurate simulations of spin-polarized fluids in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for systems where the Boltzmann approximation may be less justified, and provides a detailed investigation of solution breakdown for large spin polarization in the longitudinal configuration, which was only vaguely mentioned in prior work (Drogosz 2024).
