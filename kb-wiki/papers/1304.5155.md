# A beyond-mean-field example with zero-range effective interactions in infinite nuclear matter

> Zero-range effective interactions are commonly used in nuclear physics to describe a many-body system in the mean-field framework. If they are employed in beyond- mean-field models, an artificial ultraviolet divergence is generated by the zero-range of the interaction. We analyze this problem in symmetric nuclear matter with the t0-t3 Skyrme model. In this case, the second-order energy correction diverges linearly with the momentum cutoff. After that, we extend the work to the case of nuclear matter with the full Skyrme interaction. A strong divergence related to the velocity-dependent terms of the interaction is obtained. Moreover, a global fit can be simultaneously performed for both symmetric and nuclear matter with different neutron-to-proton ratios. These results pave the way for applications to finite nuclei in the framework of beyond mean-field theories.

## Key claim

Zero-range effective interactions used in beyond-mean-field models generate an artificial ultraviolet divergence; for the full Skyrme interaction the second-order energy correction diverges as \(\sim\Lambda^5\), much stronger than the linear divergence found in the simplified \(t_0-t_3\) model, and a global fit including the momentum cutoff as a parameter can simultaneously reproduce the mean-field equations of state for symmetric, asymmetric, and pure neutron matter.

## Method

The authors computed the second-order energy correction to the equation of state of infinite nuclear matter using zero-range Skyrme interactions, first with the simplified \(t_0-t_3\) model in symmetric nuclear matter and then with the full Skyrme interaction (including velocity-dependent terms) for symmetric, asymmetric (\(\delta=0.5\)), and pure neutron matter. They regularized the ultraviolet divergence by introducing a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) and performed chi-squared minimizations to refit the Skyrme parameters so that the second-order-corrected equations of state reproduce the corresponding mean-field reference curves (SkP for the simplified model, SLy5 for the full interaction).

## Key numbers

- For the \(t_0-t_3\) model in symmetric nuclear matter, the second-order correction diverges linearly with the cutoff: \(\frac{\Delta E}{A}(\rho,\Lambda\rightarrow\infty) = \frac{1}{105}(-11+2\ln2)\;\chi(\rho) + \frac{\Lambda}{9k_F}\;\chi(\rho) + \mathcal{O}(k_F/\Lambda)\), where \(\chi(\rho) = -\frac{3}{4\pi^6}\frac{m k_F^7}{\hbar^2 \rho}g^2(\rho)\).
- For the full Skyrme interaction, the asymptotic divergence is \(\frac{\Delta E^{(2)}}{A}(\delta,\rho,\Lambda\rightarrow\infty) = a_1(\delta,\rho)\Lambda^5 + a_2(\delta,\rho)\Lambda^3 + a_3(\delta,\rho)\Lambda + a_4(\delta,\rho) + O(k_F/\Lambda)\).
- Refitted parameters for the \(t_0-t_3\) model at \(\Lambda = 0.5\) fm\(^{-1}\): \(t_0 = -2352.900\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(t_3 = 15379.861\) MeV fm\(^{3+3\alpha}\), \(\alpha = 0.217\), \(\chi^2/N = 0.00004\), saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy \(-15.96\) MeV.
- Refitted parameters for the full Skyrme interaction at \(\Lambda = 0.5\) fm\(^{-1}\): \(t_0 = -2022.142\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(\chi^2 = 0.411\), saturation density \(0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), incompressibility \(K_\infty = 236.36\) MeV.
- For the full Skyrme interaction at \(\Lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\): \(t_0 = -718.397\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(\chi^2 = 1.313\), saturation density \(0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), incompressibility \(K_\infty = 222.76\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Moghrabi et al. (2010, 2012) on ultraviolet divergences in zero-range Skyrme interactions, and by Gambacurta et al. on second random-phase-approximation models. It provides a systematic method to refit Skyrme parameters at the beyond-mean-field level, treating the momentum cutoff as an additional parameter, which enables future applications of these adjusted interactions to finite nuclei in beyond-mean-field theories such as particle-phonon coupling models (described by Bertsch and collaborators, 1980; Litvinova and collaborators, 2007).
