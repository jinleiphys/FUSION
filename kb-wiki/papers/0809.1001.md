# Transverse-Mass Spectra in Heavy-Ion Collisions at energies E_{lab} = 2--160 GeV/nucleon

> Transverse-mass spectra of protons, pions and kaons produced in collisions of heavy nuclei are analyzed within the model of 3-fluid dynamics. It was demonstrated that this model consistently reproduces these spectra in wide ranges of incident energies E_{lab}, from 4A GeV to 160A GeV, rapidity bins and centralities of the collisions. In particular, the model describes the "step-like" dependence of kaon inverse slopes on the incident energy. The key point of this explanation is interplay of hydrodynamic expansion of the system with its dynamical freeze-out.

## Key claim

The 3-fluid dynamics (3FD) model reproduces the "step-like" dependence of kaon inverse slopes on incident energy using only a hadronic equation of state (with incompressibility K = 210 MeV) and a single constant freeze-out trigger energy density, without invoking a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma. The key mechanism is the interplay of hydrodynamic expansion with dynamical freeze-out, where the freeze-out front competes with the fluid flow and cannot always reach regions where the freeze-out criterion is met.

## Method

The authors simulated heavy-ion collisions using the 3-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats projectile and target baryon-rich matter as two interacting fluids plus a baryon-free fireball fluid for newly produced particles. They applied a dynamical freeze-out procedure integrated into the hydrodynamic equations, using a hadronic equation of state with incompressibility K = 210 MeV and a freeze-out trigger energy density ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm³ (with lower values at low incident energies). Transverse-mass spectra were computed for protons, pions, and kaons and fitted with the formula d²N/(m_T dm_T dy) ∝ (m_T)^λ exp(-m_T/T) to extract inverse-slope parameters.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out trigger energy density ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm³ for all incident energies except low energies, where ε_frz(2A GeV) = 0.3 GeV/fm³ and ε_frz(1A GeV) = 0.2 GeV/fm³
- Actual average freeze-out energy density ⟨ε_out⟩ saturates at approximately ε_frz/2 = 0.2 GeV/fm³ at SPS energies
- Incident energy range analyzed: 4A GeV to 160A GeV (with some results at 2A GeV)
- Hadronic equation of state incompressibility K = 210 MeV
- Impact parameters: b = 2 fm for Au+Au (AGS) and b = 2.5 fm for Pb+Pb (SPS) central collisions
- Freeze-out baryon density n_frz exhibits a maximum at incident energies E_lab = 10A–30A GeV
- At top SPS energy (158A GeV), the code requires 7.5 GB of RAM memory for central Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the brief letter by Ivanov and Russkikh (2008) that presented only midrapidity inverse-slope parameters, now providing full transverse-mass spectra across rapidity bins and centralities. It contradicts the interpretation by Gorenstein (2003) and Mohanty (2003) that the kaon inverse-slope saturation signals deconfinement, and it succeeds where microscopic transport models based on hadronic degrees of freedom (Bratkovskaya, Wagner) failed to reproduce the kaon behavior. The results enable further investigation of freeze-out dynamics and suggest that post-hydro kinetic evolution (as in the work by Teaney, Lauret, and Shuryak, 2001) could improve the description of low-m_T proton spectra.
