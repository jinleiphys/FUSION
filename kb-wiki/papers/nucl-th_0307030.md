# Role of virtual break-up of projectile in astrophysical fusion reactions

> We study the effect of virtual Coulomb break-up, commonly known as the dipole polarizability, of the deuteron projectile on the astrophysical fusion reaction 3He(d,p)4He. We use the adiabatic approximation to estimate the potential shift due to the E1 transition to the continuum states in the deuteron, and compute the barrier penetrability in the WKB approximation. We find that the enhancement of the penetrability due to the deuteron break-up is too small to resolve the longstanding puzzle observed in laboratory measurements that the electron screening effect is surprisingly larger than theoretical prediction based on an atomic physics model. The effect of the 3He break-up in the 3He(d,p)4He reaction, as well as the 7Li break-up in the 7Li(p,alpha)4He reaction is also discussed.

# Role of virtual break-up of projectile in astrophysical fusion reactions

## Key claim
The virtual Coulomb break-up (dipole polarizability) of the deuteron projectile enhances the tunneling probability for the 3He(d,p)4He reaction, but this enhancement is too small (about 0.2% at 5.8 keV) to resolve the longstanding puzzle where experimentally observed electron screening energies are surprisingly larger than theoretical predictions based on atomic physics models. The authors note that their adiabatic approximation provides the upper limit of the effect, so the true enhancement is even smaller.

## Method
The authors used a three-body model with a coupled-channels formalism, treating the projectile as a bound state coupled to a continuum state via the E1 operator. They employed the adiabatic approximation to diagonalize the coupling matrix at each internuclear distance R, deriving an adiabatic potential shift, then computed the barrier penetrability using the WKB approximation. For the deuteron, the total B(E1)↑ strength was calculated using the Bertulani-Baur-Hussein formula (1991) assuming the E1 strength is exhausted by a single state at Eγ = 8ε/5.

## Key numbers
- For the d+3He system at Ec.m. = 5.8 keV, the enhancement factor of the penetrability due to deuteron break-up is about 0.21%
- The classical turning point for the d+3He reaction at Ec.m. = 10 keV is 288 fm
- The deuteron binding energy ε = 2.22 MeV, yielding a total B(E1)↑ strength of 0.558 e² fm²
- For the 7Li break-up in the p+7Li system, the enhancement factor is about 2.7×10⁻³ %
- The inner turning point R₀ = 4.3 fm was used for the d+3He system

## Context
This work extends the list of small corrections examined for astrophysical fusion reactions at very low energies, following studies of vacuum polarization, relativity, bremsstrahlung, atomic polarization, radiation correction during tunneling, and zero-point fluctuation of nuclei (Balantekin, Bertulani, and Hussein, 1997; Fiorentini, Rolfs, Villante, and Ricci, 2003). The virtual break-up effect had not been previously computed for these reactions, although similar calculations using the continuum-discretized-coupled-channels method existed for transfer reactions at higher energies (Fernandes, Crespo, Nunes, and Thompson, 1999; Ogata, Yahiro, Iseri, and Kamimura, 2003). The authors conclude that the break-up effect alone does not resolve the electron screening puzzle, and suggest that the atomic physics based model itself may need re-examination with more careful treatment of few-body dynamics including electrons.
