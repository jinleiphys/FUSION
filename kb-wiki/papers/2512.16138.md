# Antisymmetrization of composite fermionic states for quantum simulations of nuclear reactions in first-quantization mapping

> I present a first-quantization deterministic algorithm for antisymmetrizing a spatially separated target-projectile system containing $N_T$ and $N_p$ identical fermions, respectively. The method constructs a fully antisymmetric wavefunction from the product of two independently antisymmetrized many-body states, each of which may be a superposition of Slater determinants. The algorithm uses a Dicke-state ancilla register that coherently encodes all one-particle exchange channels between the two subsystems, and, crucially, requires only single-particle swaps to generate the full antisymmetric structure. A total of $O(N_T N_p)$ single-particle exchanges are needed, with up to $N_p$ of them implemented in parallel, if an additional $N_p$ ancillae are used. The correct fermionic phase is incorporated through application of $Z$ gates on $N_T$ ancillae, after which the ancilla register is efficiently uncomputed using a compact sequence of controlled operations. This construction provides a nontrivial and scalable protocol for preparing fully antisymmetric states in reaction and scattering simulations, significantly expanding the range of systems that can be addressed with first-quantized quantum algorithms.

# Antisymmetrization of composite fermionic states for quantum simulations of nuclear reactions in first-quantization mapping

## Key claim

The paper presents a deterministic first-quantization algorithm for antisymmetrizing a product of two independently antisymmetrized fermionic subsystems (target and projectile) that requires only single-particle swap operations, provided the subsystems remain distinguishable by at least one qubit (e.g., a spatial position qubit). The algorithm achieves full antisymmetrization without applying inverse single-particle state preparation circuits, unlike prior recursive approaches.

## Method

The algorithm prepares \(N_T + N_p\) ancilla qubits in the Dicke state \(D^{N_T+N_p}_{N_p}\) (uniform superposition of all states with exactly \(N_p\) qubits in state \(|1\rangle\)), which coherently encodes all one-particle exchange channels between target and projectile. Controlled-swap operations between target and projectile single-particle states are performed, conditioned on the ancilla register, generating all required permutations through only single-particle exchanges. The correct fermionic phase is applied via \(Z\) gates on the first \(N_T\) ancilla qubits, and the ancilla register is uncomputed using \(N_T+N_p\) controlled-NOT operations conditioned on particle labels.

## Key numbers

- Total number of single-particle exchanges required: \(O(N_T N_p)\), with up to \(N_p\) of them implemented in parallel if an additional \(N_p\) ancillae are used.
- Number of distinct permutations for full antisymmetrization: \(N_\mathrm{perm} = \frac{(N_T + N_p)!}{N_T! \, N_p!}\).
- For the case \(N_T = N_p\), the algorithm requires precisely \(N_T N_p\) single-particle swaps.
- For a system with two projectile particles and three target particles, 8 swaps are required (while \(N_T N_p = 6\)).
- Uncomputation of ancilla register consists of \(N_T+N_p\) controlled-NOT operations.

## Context

This work extends the recursive deterministic antisymmetrization algorithm of Rule (2025) to composite target-projectile systems, avoiding the need for full inverse single-particle preparation circuits that would be required by that prior approach. The algorithm enables scalable quantum simulations of nuclear reactions on fault-tolerant quantum hardware, including neutron-, deuteron-, tritium-, and \(^{3,4}\)He-induced reactions for targets up to \(^{12}\)C, and complements existing state preparation techniques such as quantum phase estimation (Kitaev, 1995) and variational methods (Grimsley, 2019; Tang, 2021).
