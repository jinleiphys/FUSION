# Final state interactions in the nuclear response at large momentum transfer

> The convolution approach, widely employed to describe final state interactions in the response of many-body systems, is derived from the expression of the nuclear response in the zeroth-order ladder approximation. Within this framework, the folding function, accounting for the effects of interactions between the struck particle and the spectator system, can be immediately related to the spectral function of particle states.The role of nucleon-nucleon correlations in determining the energy dependence is analyzed.

## Key claim

The convolution approach to final state interactions (FSI) in nuclear response can be rigorously derived from the zeroth-order ladder approximation of many-body theory, rather than relying on heuristic arguments; the folding function is directly related to the particle spectral function, and nucleon-nucleon correlations significantly affect the energy dependence of this folding function, particularly at large energy loss.

## Method

The author derives the convolution formula for the nuclear response from the zeroth-order ladder approximation, expressing the response as an integral over the hole and particle spectral functions. The particle spectral function is then computed within the eikonal approximation, using a parameterized nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude with medium-modified total cross sections and the nuclear matter pair distribution function obtained from Fermi-Hyper-Netted-Chain calculations. The resulting folding function is applied to compute inclusive electron-nucleus scattering cross sections for isospin symmetric nuclear matter at momentum transfers above 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer condition for validity: \(|{\bf q}| \gg 2 k_F \sim 500\) MeV in electron-nucleus scattering
- At beam energies \(\gsim 800\) MeV, the ratio between proton-neutron cross sections in medium and in vacuum is \(\sim 0.8\) and largely energy independent
- The folding function calculation uses nucleon momentum \(|{\bf q}| = 1.9\) GeV, corresponding to quasi-free kinematics at incident energy \(E_e = 3.6\) GeV and electron scattering angle \(\theta_e = 30\) deg
- The differential cross section calculation at beam energy \(E_e = 3.6\) GeV and \(\theta_e = 30\) deg shows FSI provide the dominant contribution in the low energy loss region
- The differential cross section at \(E_e = 4\) GeV and \(\theta_e = 30\) deg is compared to data over a range exceeding five orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the convolution approach previously justified by heuristic arguments (Hohenberg 1966) and applied in earlier studies (Silver 1988, Benhar 1991) by providing a rigorous derivation from many-body theory using the spectral function formalism. The analysis builds on prior calculations of the hole spectral function in nuclear matter using Correlated Basis Function perturbation theory (Benhar 1989) and the self-consistent Green's function approach (Ramos 1989). The results enable quantitative extraction of spectral function information from inclusive electron-nucleus scattering data by properly accounting for FSI, and demonstrate that neglecting nucleon-nucleon correlations leads to overestimating FSI effects.
