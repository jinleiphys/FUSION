# Competing electric and magnetic excitations in backward electron scattering from heavy deformed nuclei

> Important $E2$ contributions to the $(e,e^{\prime})$ cross sections of low-lying orbital $M1$ excitations are found in heavy deformed nuclei, arising from the small energy separation between the two excitations with $I^πK = 2^+1$ and 1$^+1$, respectively. They are studied microscopically in QRPA using DWBA. The accompanying $E2$ response is negligible at small momentum transfer $q$ but contributes substantially to the cross sections measured at $θ= 165 ^{\circ}$ for $0.6 < q_{\rm eff} < 0.9$ fm$^{-1}$ ($40 \le E_i \le 70$ MeV) and leads to a very good agreement with experiment. The electric response is of longitudinal $C2$ type for $θ\le 175 ^{\circ}$ but becomes almost purely transverse $E2$ for larger backward angles. The transverse $E2$ response remains comparable with the $M1$ response for $q_{\rm eff} > 1.2$ fm$^{-1}$ ($E_i > 100$ MeV) and even dominant for $E_i > 200$ MeV. This happens even at large backward angles $θ> 175 ^{\circ}$, where the $M1$ dominance is limited to the lower $q$ region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the commonly assumed dominance of magnetic $M1$ excitations in backward electron scattering from heavy deformed nuclei is not always valid; the accompanying electric $E2$ excitations contribute substantially to the measured $(e,e^{\prime})$ cross sections because the small energy separation (comparable to experimental energy resolution of 20-40 keV) between the $I^\pi K = 1^+1$ and $2^+1$ states in heavy deformed nuclei makes them experimentally unresolvable.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using an axially-symmetric Woods-Saxon mean field with BCS pairing, separable quadrupole and spin-spin residual interactions, and a basis of oscillator eigenfunctions with principal quantum numbers up to $\mathcal{N} \le 11$. Transition densities for $M1$ and $E2$ excitations were computed from the QRPA wave functions and used as input to distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations of $(e,e^{\prime})$ cross sections at $\theta = 165^\circ$ for five rare-earth nuclei ($^{154}$Sm, $^{154}$Gd, $^{156}$Gd, $^{158}$Gd, $^{168}$Er).

## Key numbers

- The energy separation between the $1^+1$ and $2^+1$ states in $^{156}$Gd is 21 keV, comparable to the experimental energy resolution of 20-40 keV
- The ratio of transverse to longitudinal kinematic factors at $\theta = 165^\circ$ is $V_t/V_\ell = 58$
- The $E2$ contribution becomes comparable to the $M1$ contribution in the region $0.7 < q_{\rm eff} < 0.8$ fm$^{-1}$ ($50 \le E_i \le 60$ MeV)
- The $E2$ response contributes substantially for $0.6 < q_{\rm eff} < 0.9$ fm$^{-1}$ ($40 \le E_i \le 70$ MeV)
- The transverse $E2$ response remains comparable with $M1$ for $q_{\rm eff} > 1.2$ fm$^{-1}$ ($E_i > 100$ MeV) and becomes dominant for $E_i > 200$ MeV
- The spin-spin coupling constants used are $c(+)A = 200$ MeV and $c(-) = -0.5 c(+)$
- The $B(E2)$ value for $^{156}$Gd from experiment is reproduced in the calculations

## Context

This work extends earlier QRPA calculations of orbital $M1$ excitations in rare-earth nuclei (Nojarov, Faessler, and collaborators, 1990-1993) that systematically underestimated experimental form factors at higher momentum transfer. The study was motivated by the experimental identification of the accompanying $E2$ transition in $^{156}$Gd by Bohle et al. (1985) and by earlier PWBA estimates by Willey (1963) suggesting that the long-wave approximation ($qR \ll 1$) fails for heavy nuclei where $qR > 1.3$. The results enable improved interpretation of backward-angle $(e,e^{\prime})$ experiments on heavy deformed nuclei and demonstrate that the $M1$ dominance at backward angles is limited to the lower $q$ region, with implications for future experiments at the new $180^\circ$ electron scattering facility in Darmstadt.
