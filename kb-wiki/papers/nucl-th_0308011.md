# A Test of the Calculability of a Three Body Relativistic, Cluster Decomposable, Unitary, Covariant Scattering Theory

> In this work a calculation of the cluster decomposable formalism for relativistic scattering as developed by Lindesay, Markevich, Noyes, and Pastrana (LMNP) is made for an ultra-light quantum model. After highlighting areas of the theory vital for calculation, a description is made of the process to go from the general theory to an eigen-integral equation for bound state problems, and calculability is demonstrated. An ultra-light quantum exchange model is then developed to examine calculability.

# A Test of the Calculability of a Three Body Relativistic, Cluster Decomposable, Unitary, Covariant Scattering Theory

## Key claim

The Lindesay, Markevich, Noyes, and Pastrana (LMNP) formalism for relativistic three-body scattering is shown to be calculable for bound state problems, but only after constructing an ultra-light quantum exchange model in which the light particle mediates the interaction between two equally massive particles and only the a-a-bar channel survives. The demonstration is limited to this specific ultra-light model and does not establish general calculability for arbitrary systems.

## Method

The authors begin by summarizing the LMNP formalism, which yields Faddeev-decomposed Lippmann-Schwinger-like equations with velocity conservation replacing momentum conservation to maintain cluster decomposability and unitarity. They then construct a "light particle model" where two heavy particles (a and a-bar) interact via a much lighter particle l, deriving approximate expressions for pair energies, momenta, and the separable two-body scattering amplitude. An "ultra-light quantum extension" is then developed by assuming the light particle alone mediates the interaction, reducing the problem to a single kernel channel, which is solved numerically using Gaussian quadrature and Newton methods for eigen-integral equations to find bound state energies.

## Key numbers

- Six stable bound state energies were found as the ultra-light quantum mass m_l approached zero, with the first bound state at energy/m = 0.000030 with uncertainty 8 x 10^-6
- Second bound state at energy/m = 0.000020 with uncertainty 8 x 10^-6
- Third bound state at energy/m = 0.000015 with uncertainty 8 x 10^-6
- Fourth bound state at energy/m = 0.000015 with uncertainty 6 x 10^-6
- Fifth bound state at energy/m = 0.000013 with uncertainty 6 x 10^-6
- Sixth bound state at energy/m = 0.000012 with uncertainty 7 x 10^-6

## Context

This work extends the cluster-decomposable relativistic scattering formalism developed by Lindesay, Markevich, Noyes, and Pastrana (LMNP), which was designed to satisfy clustering, Lorentz kinematics, momentum conservation, and unitarity simultaneously. The authors note that alternative approaches such as the Bethe-Salpeter formalism (Salpeter, year not given) and Blankenbecler-Sugar formalism (Blankenbecler and Sugar, year not given) do not satisfy full cluster decomposability due to Lorentz kinematics and off-shell momentum conservation. The demonstrated calculability of the ultra-light quantum model provides a template for future bound state calculations using alternative two-body amplitudes, potentially enabling modeling of a wide range of nuclear and particle interactions.
