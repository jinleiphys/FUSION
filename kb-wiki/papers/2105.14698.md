# Measurement of the Sixth-Order Cumulant of Net-Proton Multiplicity Distributions in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 27, 54.4, and 200 GeV at RHIC

> According to first principle Lattice QCD calculations, the transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadronic matter is a smooth crossover in the region $μ_{\rm B}\leq T_{c}$. In this range the ratio, $C_{6}/C_{2}$, of net-baryon distributions are predicted to be negative. In this paper, we report the first measurement of the midrapidity net-proton $C_{6}/C_{2}$ from 27, 54.4 and 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The dependence on collision centrality and kinematic acceptance in ($p_{T}$, $y$) are analyzed. While for 27 and 54.4 GeV collisions the $C_{6}/C_{2}$ values are close to zero within uncertainties, it is observed that for 200 GeV collisions, the $C_{6}/C_{2}$ ratio becomes progressively negative from peripheral to central collisions. Transport model calculations without critical dynamics predict mostly positive values except for the most central collisions within uncertainties. These observations seem to favor a smooth crossover in the high energy nuclear collisions at top RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports the first measurement of the net-proton sixth-order cumulant ratio C6/C2 in Au+Au collisions at 27, 54.4, and 200 GeV. While the abstract states that the data "seem to favor a smooth crossover," the actual observation is that only at 200 GeV does the ratio become progressively negative from peripheral to central collisions, whereas at 27 and 54.4 GeV the values are consistent with zero within uncertainties, and transport models without critical dynamics also predict mostly positive values except for the most central collisions.

## Method

The STAR detector at RHIC measured net-proton multiplicity distributions at midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) for Au+Au collisions at three beam energies. The sixth-order cumulant C6 and the second-order cumulant C2 were extracted from the net-proton distributions as functions of collision centrality and kinematic acceptance in transverse momentum (0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c) and rapidity. The measured C6/C2 ratios were compared to predictions from the UrQMD and HIJING transport models, which do not include critical dynamics.

## Key numbers

- At 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, the C6/C2 ratio becomes progressively negative from peripheral to central collisions, reaching values near -1.0 in the most central (0-5%) collisions.
- At 27 GeV, the C6/C2 values are consistent with zero within uncertainties across all centrality classes.
- At 54.4 GeV, the C6/C2 values are also consistent with zero within uncertainties across all centrality classes.
- The kinematic acceptance for the reported results is |y| < 0.5 and 0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c.

## Context

This measurement extends the STAR Collaboration's program of higher-order cumulant measurements of net-proton distributions, which previously included C4/C2 ratios at various beam energies (Adamczyk et al., 2014; Luo, 2015). The results are compared to Lattice QCD predictions (Bazavov et al., 2017) that predict negative C6/C2 in the crossover region at low baryon chemical potential. The observation of negative C6/C2 at 200 GeV but not at lower energies suggests that the smooth crossover from quark-gluon plasma to hadronic matter may be realized at top RHIC energy, while the near-zero values at 27 and 54.4 GeV may indicate different physics at higher baryon chemical potential. This work enables future comparisons with Beam Energy Scan II data and more refined theoretical calculations of critical fluctuations.
