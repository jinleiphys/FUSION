# Nuclear Breakup of Borromean Nuclei

> We study the eikonal model for the nuclear-induced breakup of Borromean nuclei, using Li11 and He6 as examples. The full eikonal model is difficult to realize because of six-dimensional integrals, but a number of simplifying approximations are found to be accurate. The integrated diffractive and one-nucleon stripping cross sections are rather insensitive to the neutron-neutron correlation, but the two-nucleon stripping does show some dependence on the correlation. The distribution of excitation energy in the neutron-core final state in one-neutron stripping reactions is quite sensitive to the shell structure of the halo wave function. Experimental data favor models with comparable amounts of s- and p-wave in the Li11 halo.

## Key claim

The eikonal model for nuclear-induced breakup of Borromean nuclei can be made tractable through simplifying approximations (no-recoil and transparent limits) that are accurate for integrated cross sections, but the two-nucleon stripping cross section retains sensitivity to neutron-neutron correlations, and the excitation energy distribution in one-neutron stripping is sensitive to the s-wave versus p-wave composition of the halo wave function.

## Method

The authors applied the eikonal (sudden) approximation to model the breakup of 11Li and 6He on a 12C target at 280 MeV/nucleon, using profile functions derived from nucleon-nucleon cross sections and target densities. They constructed three-body ground state wave functions for 11Li with varying s-wave probabilities (4.5%, 23%, and 50%) and compared full six-dimensional integrals with simplified no-recoil and transparent-limit approximations. The one-neutron stripping differential cross section was computed by integrating the overlap of the ground state wave function with continuum neutron-core final states, including the final-state neutron-core potential.

## Key numbers

- The one-nucleon stripping cross section for 11Li on 12C is reduced to 43% of the unshadowed value by core shadowing; the diffractive cross section is reduced to 44%; the two-nucleon stripping is reduced to 20%.
- The s-wave energy distribution for 11Li one-neutron stripping peaks at approximately 0.08 MeV, corresponding to E_peak = E_B/4 with E_B ≈ 0.32 MeV.
- The p-wave resonance in the 11Li neutron-core system is at E_R = 0.51 MeV with fitted width Γ_0 = 1 MeV and cutoff parameter c = 1.7.
- For 6He, the p-wave peak in the stripping distribution is at 0.83 MeV, close to the p_3/2 resonance energy of 0.89 MeV.
- The 6He two-nucleon stripping cross section is about a factor of two larger than for 11Li.
- The nucleon-carbon reaction cross section at 280 MeV is 29.2 mb (from the NN cross section used).
- The 9Li core model gives an rms charge radius of 2.28 fm and a predicted cross section at 800 MeV/n of 840 mb, 5% larger than the measured 796 ± 6 mb.
- The 4He core model gives a total cross section of 546 mb at 800 MeV/n and 486 mb at 280 MeV/n, comparable to the experimental 503 ± 5 mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier eikonal model studies of single-nucleon halo nuclei (Bertsch and Hencken, 1996) to the Borromean two-neutron halo case, improving on the black disk model of Barranco et al. (1993) and the simplified two-body wave functions used in that work. The authors compare their results to experimental data from GANIL, NSCL, RIKEN, and GSI (Zinser et al., 1997), and to earlier three-body models of 11Li (Bertsch and Esbensen, 1991; Esbensen et al., 1997). The analysis enables extraction of the s-wave versus p-wave content of the halo wave function from one-neutron stripping energy distributions, which is not possible from Coulomb breakup alone.
