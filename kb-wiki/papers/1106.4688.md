# Effect of Coulomb Forces on the Position of the Pole in the Scattering Amplitude and on Its Residue

> Explicit expressions of the vertex constant for the decay of a nucleus into two charged particles for an arbitrary orbital momentum $l$ are derived for the standard expansion of the effective-range function $K_l(k^2)$, as well as when the function $K_0(k^2)$ has a pole. As physical examples, we consider the bound state of the nucleus ${}^3\rm{He}$ and the resonant states of the nuclei ${^2}$He and ${^3}$He in the s-wave, and those of ${}^5\rm{He}$ and ${}^5\rm{Li}$ in the p-wave. For the systems $Np$ and $Nd$ the pole trajectories are constructed in the complex planes of the momentum and of the renormalized vertex constant. They correspond to a transition from the resonance state to the virtual state while the Coulomb forces gradually decrease to zero.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit expressions for the vertex constant (VC) for the decay of a nucleus into two charged particles for arbitrary orbital momentum \(l\), using the effective-range expansion with Coulomb interaction properly included; the authors claim that prior work by Yarmukhamedov (2007) contains a fundamental error by using a formula for the binding energy that neglects Coulomb effects, making those results invalid.

## Method

The authors start from the standard effective-range expansion of the function \(K_l(k^2)\) and its relation to the Coulomb-modified phase shift, using the formalism of Kok (1980) and Mur (1983). They derive the renormalized partial scattering amplitude in the presence of Coulomb repulsion and obtain expressions for the renormalized vertex constant \(\tilde{G}_l^2\) via the residue at the pole of the amplitude. For the \(s\)-wave, they consider both the standard power-series expansion of \(K_0(k^2)\) and the case where \(K_0(k^2)\) has a pole (as in the doublet \(Nd\) system). Pole trajectories in the complex momentum plane and in the complex plane of the renormalized vertex constant are constructed by gradually reducing the Coulomb interaction strength via a parameter \(\xi\) (from 1 to 0), using either the Landau–Smorodinsky–Schwinger relation for the scattering length or direct solution of the Schrödinger equation with a Yukawa potential.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^2\)He nucleus (proton-proton system), the pole position is \(k_{^2\text{He}} = (0.0644 - i0.0871)\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), corresponding to \(E_{^2\text{He}} = (-142 - i465)\ \text{keV}\); the renormalized vertex constant squared is \(\tilde{G}^2 = -(0.060 + i0.051)\ \text{fm}\).
- For the singlet \(np\) system (no Coulomb), the scattering length is \(a_0^{np} = (-23.719 \pm 0.013)\ \text{fm}\) and the effective range is \(r_0^{np} = (2.76 \pm 0.05)\ \text{fm}\); the virtual level energy is \(E_v = -66\ \text{keV}\).
- For the doublet \(pd\) system, the most reliable theoretical scattering length is \(^2a_{pd} = 0.024\ \text{fm}\), and the fitted parameter \(c_2 = {^2a_{pd}}C_2 = -58.1\ \text{fm}^2\) agrees with the Pisa group result \(c_2 = -56.7\ \text{fm}^2\).
- For the \(^3\)He bound state, the renormalized VC from the Malfliet–Tjon potential is \(\tilde{G}_\tau^2 = 1.63\ \text{fm}\) (binding energy \(\epsilon_\tau = 7.87\ \text{MeV}\), ANC \(C_\tau^2 = 3.90\)); from the Reid soft-core potential it is \(\tilde{G}_\tau^2 = 1.12\ \text{fm}\) (binding energy \(\epsilon_\tau = 6.39\ \text{MeV}\), ANC \(C_\tau^2 = 3.14\)).

## Context

This work extends the effective-range approach of Kok (1980) and the earlier preliminary results of Orlov et al. (2007, 2009) by providing explicit formulas for the vertex constant at arbitrary orbital momentum and by constructing pole trajectories for the \(Np\) and \(Nd\) systems as Coulomb forces are reduced. The authors explicitly correct a serious error in Yarmukhamedov (2007), who used a binding-energy relation valid only for neutral particles while attempting to include Coulomb effects. The results enable the extraction of vertex constants and asymptotic normalization coefficients from experimental scattering data, and clarify the transition from resonance to virtual states under varying Coulomb repulsion.
