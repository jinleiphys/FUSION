# Effective QCD and transport description of dilepton and photon production in heavy-ion collisions and elementary processes

> In this review we address the dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion reactions and in particular the information obtained from electromagnetic probes that stem from the partonic and hadronic phases. The out-of-equilibrium description of strongly interacting relativistic fields is based on the theory of Kadanoff and Baym. For the modeling of the partonic phase we introduce a dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) for QCD in equilibrium. The widths and masses of the quasiparticles are controlled by transport coefficients in comparison to lattice QCD results. The resulting off-shell transport approach - denoted by Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) - also includes covariant dynamical hadronization and keeps track of the hadronic interactions in the final phase. We show that PHSD captures the bulk dynamics of heavy-ion collisions from SPS to LHC energies and provides a basis for the evaluation of the electromagnetic emissivity, using the same dynamical parton propagators as for the system evolution. Direct photon production in elementary processes and heavy-ion reactions at RHIC and LHC energies is investigated and the status of the photon v2 puzzle - a large elliptic flow of the direct photons observed in A+A collisions - is addressed. We discuss the roles of hadronic and partonic sources for the photon spectra and the flow coefficients v2 and v3 and also the possibility to subtract the QGP signal from observables. Furthermore, the production of dilepton pairs is addressed from SIS to LHC energies. The low-mass dilepton yield is enhanced due to the in-medium modification of the rho-meson and at the lowest energy also due to a multiple regeneration of Delta-resonances. In addition, a signal of the partonic degrees-of-freedom is found in the intermediate dilepton mass regime (1.2GeV<M<3GeV), which can shed light on the nature of the very early degrees-of-freedom in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

# Effective QCD and transport description of dilepton and photon production in heavy-ion collisions and elementary processes

## Key claim

The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) approach, which uses equilibrium quasiparticle properties from the Dynamical Quasiparticle Model (DQPM) even for nonequilibrium configurations, can capture the bulk dynamics of heavy-ion collisions from SPS to LHC energies and provide a basis for evaluating electromagnetic emissivity; however, the authors acknowledge that using equilibrium quasiparticle properties for out-of-equilibrium configurations is "well motivated, however, not fully equivalent to the exact solution."

## Method

The PHSD approach combines a hadronic transport model (HSD) with a partonic phase based on the Dynamical Quasiparticle Model (DQPM), which describes QCD properties in terms of single-particle Green's functions with temperature-dependent masses and widths fixed by lattice QCD results. The hadronization from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is implemented via local covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion to mesons and three-quark fusion to baryons, using a Gaussian overlap function with width parameter b = 0.66 fm. The initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions start with two nuclei in a semi-classical groundstate, with string melting to partons occurring only when the local energy density exceeds the transition energy density ε_c ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³.

## Key numbers

- Transition energy density for string melting: ε_c ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Width parameter b for hadronization Gaussian overlap: b = 0.66 fm (corresponding to average rms radius of mesons)
- Average quark mass in PHSD simulations: about 0.5 GeV
- Average gluon mass in PHSD simulations: slightly less than 1 GeV
- Grid size in beam direction: Δz = 1/γ_cm fm, with transverse grid Δx = Δy = 1 fm
- Lorentz-γ factor at LHC energies: γ_cm ≈ 1400
- Energy conservation violation in heavy-ion collisions: typically less than 1%
- Coefficient a(T) for electric conductivity dependence on μ_q: a(T) ≈ 11.6 GeV⁻² at T = 0.2 GeV
- Coefficient c_σ₀ for μ_q²/T² dependence of σ₀/T: c_σ₀ ≈ 0.46 in temperature range 170 MeV ≤ T ≤ 250 MeV
- Number of parallel runs for averaging at RHIC energies: typically 50

## Context

This review extends the HSD approach of Ehehalt and Cassing (1999) by incorporating explicit partonic degrees of freedom through the DQPM, which was developed by Peshier (2005), Andre (2005), and Cassing (2006, 2007). The PHSD approach enables the evaluation of electromagnetic probes (photons and dileptons) from both partonic and hadronic phases, addressing the photon v₂ puzzle observed in A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC. The work enables future subtraction of the quark-gluon plasma signal from observables and provides a framework for studying transport coefficients such as shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and electric conductivity of hot QCD matter.
