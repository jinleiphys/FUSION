# Acoustic scaling of linear and mode-coupled anisotropic flow; implications for precision extraction of the specific shear viscosity

> The $\mathrm{n^{th}}$-order linear flow coefficients $\mathrm{v^L_n \, (n=2,3,4,5)}$, and the corresponding nonlinear mode-coupled ($\mathrm{mc}$) coefficients $\mathrm{v^{mc}_{4,(2,2)}}$, $\mathrm{v^{mc}_{5,(2,3)}}$, $\mathrm{v^{mc}_{6,(3,3)}}$ and $\mathrm{v^{mc}_{6,(2,2,2)}}$, are studied for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 2.76$ TeV. Both sets of coefficients indicate a common acoustic scaling pattern of exponential viscous modulation, with a rate proportional to the square of the harmonic numbers and the mean transverse momenta (respectively), and inversely proportional to the cube root of the charged particle multiplicity ($\mathrm{(N_{ch})^{1/3}}$), that characterizes the dimensionless size of the systems produced in the collisions. These patterns and their associated scaling parameters, provide new stringent constraints for eccentricity independent estimates of the specific shear viscosity ($η/s$) and the viscous correction to the thermal distribution function for the matter produced in the collisions. They also give crucial constraints for extraction of the initial-state eccentricity spectrum.

## Key claim

The linear flow coefficients v^L_n (n=2-5) and the nonlinear mode-coupled coefficients v^mc_4,(2,2), v^mc_5,(2,3), v^mc_6,(3,3), and v^mc_6,(2,2,2) all follow a common acoustic scaling pattern of exponential viscous modulation, with a rate proportional to the square of the harmonic numbers and inversely proportional to the cube root of the charged particle multiplicity, but the paper notes that a further calibration via viscous hydrodynamical calculations would be required to map the extracted scaling parameter β^0 onto the actual value of η/s.

## Method

The authors analyzed published ALICE and ATLAS measurements of p_T-integrated and differential flow coefficients for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV. They computed cumulant-defined eccentricities ε'_n and mode-coupled eccentricities ε^mc_n using a Monte Carlo quark-Glauber model with fluctuating initial conditions. They tested acoustic scaling by plotting the eccentricity-scaled linear and mode-coupled flow coefficients versus (N_ch)^{-1/3} and performing simultaneous exponential fits following the acoustic ansatz, and also extracted the p_T-dependent viscous attenuation factor from differential v_3 and v_2 measurements.

## Key numbers

- The quadratic β prefactors confirmed for the linear harmonics are 4, 9, 16, and 25 for n=2, 3, 4, and 5 respectively.
- The quadratic β prefactors confirmed for the mode-coupled harmonics are 8, 13, 18, and 12 for v^mc_4,(2,2), v^mc_5,(2,3), v^mc_6,(3,3), and v^mc_6,(2,2,2) respectively.
- The extracted β^0 value (the viscous coefficient at p_T = 0.0 GeV/c) is β^0 = 0.83 ± 0.04.
- The p_T-dependent viscous attenuation follows β^δf_n = n k p_T^2 with k = 0.169 ± 0.003 GeV^{-2}.
- The simultaneous fit to the linear and mode-coupled data shows agreement within an uncertainty of approximately 2-12%.

## Context

This work extends the acoustic scaling framework previously developed by Lacey et al. (2011, 2013, 2016) and Shuryak (2013) from elliptic and triangular flow to higher-order linear and mode-coupled harmonics. It provides new experimental constraints that could reduce the large eccentricity-driven uncertainties (currently of order 100%) in η/s extractions from viscous hydrodynamic model comparisons, which have been performed by Romatschke (2007), Song (2010), Schenke (2011), and others. The results enable simultaneous extraction of the specific shear viscosity and the first viscous correction to the thermal distribution function, as well as verification of the initial-state eccentricity spectrum from models such as the MC-Glauber model of Miller (2007) and Alver (2006).
