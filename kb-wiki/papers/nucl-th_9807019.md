# Coherent and Non-Coherent Double Diffractive Production of $ Q \bar {Q} $ - pairs in Collisions of Heavy Ions at High Energies

> The double coherent and non-coherent diffractive production of heavy quark - antiquark pairs ($Q \bar{Q}$) in heavy ion scattering at high energies (LHC) is considered. The total and differential cross sections of these processes with the formation of $c \bar{c}$ and $b \bar{b}$ pairs in $pp$, $CaCa$ and $PbPb$ collisions are evaluated. The contribution of the considered mechanisms is a few per cent of the number of heavy quark - antiquark pairs obtained in the processes of hard (QCD) scattering, and it will be taken into account in the registration of $c$, $b$ quarks or, for instance, in the study of the heavy quarkonia suppression effects in Quark - Gluon Plasma, in the search for intermediate mass Higgs bosons and so on. It is shown that the cross section of the coherent scattering process is great enough. This makes it suitable for studying collective effects in nuclear interactions at high energies. An example of such effects is given: large values of the invariant mass of a $Q \bar{Q}$pair, $M_{Q \bar{Q}} \gsim 100$ GeV, in association with a large rapidity gap between diffractive jets $Δη> 5$.

## Key claim

The double diffractive production of heavy quark-antiquark pairs in heavy ion collisions at LHC energies contributes a few percent of the number of pairs from hard QCD scattering, and the coherent scattering cross section is large enough to enable studies of collective nuclear effects, provided the invariant mass of the pair exceeds about 100 GeV with a rapidity gap larger than 5 units.

## Method

The authors calculate total and differential cross sections for double diffractive production of \(c\bar{c}\) and \(b\bar{b}\) pairs in \(pp\), \(CaCa\), and \(PbPb\) collisions using a factorized model where each incident hadron emits a Pomeron (described by Regge theory with a flux factor), and the Pomeron's gluonic structure function (chosen from three parametrizations: soft, hard, and intermediate) drives the hard gluon-gluon fusion to \(Q\bar{Q}\). For non-coherent ion scattering, the proton form factor is replaced by a nuclear form factor and an \(A^{2\alpha}\) scaling is applied; for coherent scattering, the total energy is scaled as \(\sqrt{s_A} = A\sqrt{s_0}\) and the nuclear form factor is used. A survival probability factor of about 10% at \(\sqrt{s}=14\) TeV is noted but not included in the presented cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For \(pp\) at \(\sqrt{s_0}=14\) TeV, the fraction of diffractive \(Q\bar{Q}\) pairs is \(\sim 1 \div 18\%\) of those from hard scattering (Table values, not explicitly listed in text).
- The maximum allowable invariant mass for double diffractive \(Q\bar{Q}\) production at \(\sqrt{s_0}=14\) TeV is approximately 500 GeV.
- For non-coherent double diffractive scattering in \(CaCa\) and \(PbPb\), the fraction of diffractive \(Q\bar{Q}\) pairs averages approximately \(\sim 1\%\) for \(Ca\) and \(\sim 0.01\%\) for \(Pb\) of those from central collisions.
- For coherent double diffractive scattering, the cross section in the region \(M_{Q\bar{Q}} > 100\) GeV and \(\Delta\eta > 3\) is \(\sim 10^{-5} \div 10^{-7}\) mb, allowing up to \(10^4\) events in a 15-day run at LHC luminosities (\(10^{26}\) for \(PbPb\), \(10^{30}\) for \(CaCa\)).
- The differential cross section for the "Soft" Pomeron model is more than one order of magnitude larger than the "Hard" model at \(M_{Q\bar{Q}} \lsim 50\) GeV in \(pp\), with the difference equalizing at \(M_{Q\bar{Q}} \sim 140 \div 180\) GeV.
- For coherent scattering, the cross sections differ by \(2 \div 3\) orders of magnitude between "Soft" and "Hard" Pomeron models over \(20 \le M_{Q\bar{Q}} \le 200\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of double diffractive heavy quark production from proton-proton collisions (previously treated by Donnachie and Landshoff (1988), Berger et al. (1987), and Heyssler (1996)) to heavy ion collisions, specifically for \(CaCa\) and \(PbPb\) systems at LHC energies. The results enable experimental searches for collective nuclear effects (e.g., quark-gluon plasma signatures) and rare states such as intermediate-mass Higgs bosons, and they provide a basis for discriminating between soft and hard Pomeron structure models using the CMS or FELIX detectors. The paper also highlights that diffractive contributions must be accounted for in heavy quark registration at LHC, as they constitute a non-negligible fraction of the total yield.
