# Recent Theoretical Developments in the QCD Phase Diagram

> In this talk I discuss three recent developments in the theoretical understanding of the phase diagram of the strong interaction. The first topic deals with the comparison of model calculations of the quark-hadron transition at vanishing quark chemical potential with state-of-the-art lattice QCD results. In the second relates to the size of a possible 'quarkyonic phase'. The third deals with the occurence of inhomogeneous chiral phases.

# Recent Theoretical Developments in the QCD Phase Diagram

## Key claim

Model calculations of the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, when extended beyond mean-field to include quantum fluctuations via the functional renormalization group (FRG), produce significantly softened chiral and deconfinement transitions that nearly coincide, leaving little room for a distinct "quarkyonic" phase; the existence and size of inhomogeneous chiral phases depend on the inclusion of vector interactions and the dimensionality of the spatial modulations.

## Method

The author compares mean-field Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) and Polyakov–quark-meson (PQM) model predictions for the quark-hadron transition at vanishing chemical potential with lattice QCD results from the HOTQCD and WB collaborations, then includes quantum fluctuations via the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach. For the quarkyonic phase, the author examines the dependence of the Polyakov-loop transition temperature T0 on quark chemical potential using a one-loop running of the QCD beta-function and a hadron resonance gas analysis. For inhomogeneous phases, the author solves the mean-field equations for the Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions and the NJL model in 3+1 dimensions with one-dimensional spatial modulations of the chiral order parameter, including the effects of vector interactions.

## Key numbers

- The Polyakov-loop parameter T0 at zero chemical potential is adjusted to 270 MeV for pure gauge theory (Nf=0), and at μ=0 with dynamical quarks the estimate gives T0=187 MeV with bT0=2.1×10⁻² MeV from the one-loop beta-function analysis.
- The empirical freeze-out line parametrization yields T0(μ)/T0(0)=1−(bT0)(μB/T0)² with bT0=2.78×10⁻² MeV when T0 is fixed at 200 MeV.
- In the 1+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model, a spatially ordered phase occurs for small temperatures and μ>2/π.
- In the 3+1 dimensional NJL model with finite bare quark masses of mq=5 MeV and 10 MeV, the inhomogeneous region shrinks and the Lifschitz point moves to higher μ and lower T compared to the chiral limit.
- Mesonic contributions to the pressure in the three-flavor PNJL model agree well with chiral perturbation theory results up to temperatures of approximately 150 MeV.

## Context

This talk from the Hot and Cold Baryonic Matter 2010 conference reviews three developments in QCD phase diagram theory. The comparison of model calculations with lattice QCD results extends the work of Ratti (2006), Roesner (2007), and Fukushima (2008) on Polyakov-loop potentials, and uses FRG methods from Schaefer and Wambach (2005) and Schaefer, Schaffner-Bielich, and Wambach (2007). The quarkyonic phase discussion builds on large-Nc arguments by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) and model calculations by Sasaki (2010), while the inhomogeneous phase analysis extends exact results from the 1+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model by Schnetz (2006) and Ginzburg-Landau analysis by Nickel (2009) to 3+1 dimensional NJL and QM models. The work enables future studies of two- and three-dimensional spatial ordering near the crust-liquid interface in neutron stars and the quark-hadron transition in their inner cores.
