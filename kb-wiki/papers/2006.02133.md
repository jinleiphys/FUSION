# Inhomogeneous phases in the quark-meson model with explicit chiral-symmetry breaking

> We investigate the existence of inhomogeneous chiral phases in the quark-meson model with explicit chiral-symmetry breaking. We find that the inhomogeneous region shrinks with increasing pion masses but survives for the physical value of m_pi. The instability towards inhomogeneous matter occurs in the scalar channel, while pseudoscalar modes are disfavored.

## Key claim

The inhomogeneous chiral phase in the quark-meson model survives for the physical value of the pion mass (m_pi = 140 MeV), contrary to a previous claim that it disappears already at m_pi around 37 MeV; however, the inhomogeneous region shrinks with increasing pion mass, and the instability occurs only in the scalar channel, while pseudoscalar modes are disfavored.

## Method

The authors performed a stability analysis of the homogeneous phase against small inhomogeneous fluctuations in the scalar (sigma) and pseudoscalar (pion) channels, computing the inverse dressed meson propagators at zero energy and finite momentum. They then computed the full phase diagram using a specific self-consistent ansatz for the order parameter, the real-kink crystal (RKC), which allows for inhomogeneous condensation only in the sigma channel. The model parameters were fitted to vacuum properties (pion decay constant, sigma mass, constituent quark mass) in the chiral limit, then the explicit symmetry-breaking parameter c was tuned to reproduce various pion masses including the physical value.

## Key numbers

* For the chiral limit with Lambda = 5 GeV (renormalized limit), the inhomogeneous phase survives; the instability region in the mu-m_pi plane at T=0 persists for all pion masses considered, including m_pi = 140 MeV.
* The vacuum fit values in the chiral limit were: constituent quark mass M_0 = 300 MeV, sigma mass m_sigma,0 = 600 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi,0 = 88 MeV.
* For Lambda = 757 MeV (the largest cutoff outside the problematic regime where g^2 and lambda remain positive), the instability region at T=0 also persists for m_pi = 140 MeV.
* The pion decay constant at physical pion mass (m_pi ~ 140 MeV) in the Goldberger-Treiman approximation was found to be too small compared with the empirical value of 92.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inhomogeneous chiral phases in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by Nickel (2009) and in the quark-meson model by Carignano (2014) and Nickel (2009), which were mostly performed in the chiral limit. It contradicts the finding of Andersen (2018) that the inhomogeneous phase in the QM model becomes disfavored already at m_pi = 37 MeV when using a chiral density wave (CDW) ansatz; the present work shows that the CDW ansatz is inadequate because it forces equal amplitudes in scalar and pseudoscalar channels, while the instability is purely scalar. The results enable future studies of inhomogeneous phases in QCD-like models with realistic pion masses and suggest that the RKC ansatz is a viable self-consistent modulation for such calculations.
