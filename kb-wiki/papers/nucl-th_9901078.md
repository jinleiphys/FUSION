# Chemical fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Fluctuations of the chemical composition of the hadronic system produced in nuclear collisions are discussed using the $Φ-$measure which has been earlier applied to study the transverse momentum fluctuations. The measure is expressed through the moments of the multiplicity distribution and then the properties of $Φ$ are discussed within a few models of multiparticle production. A special attention is paid to the fluctuations in the equilibrium ideal quantum gas. The system of kaons and pions, which is particularly interesting from the experimental point of view, is discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The Φ-measure, previously used for transverse momentum fluctuations, can be applied to chemical fluctuations (e.g., strangeness yield) in high-energy nuclear collisions; its sign and magnitude depend on quantum statistics (positive for bosons, negative for fermions, zero for classical particles) and on resonance decay correlations, but the experimental value in pp collisions cannot be reliably extracted from existing data.

## Method

The author expresses the Φ-measure in terms of the first and second moments of the multiplicity distribution for a two-component system (particles of type a and b). Φ is then computed analytically for three simple models (independent Poissonian distributions, fixed chemical composition, and charge conservation) and numerically for an equilibrium ideal quantum gas mixture of pions and kaons, including the effect of resonance decays (ρ, ω, K*). The calculations use the grand canonical partition function for ideal quantum gases, with the system volume canceling out in the final intensive Φ.

## Key numbers

- For massless fermions with zero chemical potential: Φ ≅ -0.045 × √(g_a g_b)/(g_a + g_b)
- For massless bosons with zero chemical potential: Φ ≅ 0.170 × √(g_a g_b)/(g_a + g_b)
- For the π⁻K⁻ system with resonances, Φ is significantly reduced compared to the ideal gas case; for the π⁻K⁺ system, Φ becomes negative above T ≅ 110 MeV, approximately independent of μ_K
- From pp data at 205 GeV: ⟨N_-⟩ = 2.84, ⟨N_-²⟩ - ⟨N_-⟩² = 3.63, ⟨N_K⟩ = 0.18, ⟨N_-N_K⟩ - ⟨N_-⟩⟨N_K⟩ = 0.078; assuming Poissonian kaons gives Φ = -0.004, while using the Wróblewski formula gives Φ = -0.02

## Context

This work extends the Φ-measure approach introduced by Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (1992) for transverse momentum fluctuations to chemical composition fluctuations, motivated by the NA49 Collaboration's plans to study chemical fluctuations at CERN SPS. It builds on earlier discussions of equilibrium fluctuations by Stephanov (1999) and the observation that the small p_T fluctuations in central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon (Roland 1998, Appelshauser 1999) provide a restrictive test of collision models. The results enable quantitative predictions for event-by-event strangeness fluctuation measurements, which could help distinguish between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic scenarios, though the author notes that experimental measurements should first establish the pp baseline.
