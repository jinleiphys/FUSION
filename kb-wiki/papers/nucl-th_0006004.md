# Applicability of the orientation average formula in heavy-ion fusion reactions of deformed nuclei

> In heavy-ion fusion reactions involving a well deformed nucleus, one often assumes that the orientation of the target nucleus does not change during the reaction. We discuss the accuracy of this procedure by analyzing the excitation function of the fusion cross section and the fusion barrier distribution in the reactions of $^{154}$Sm target with various projectiles ranging from $^{12}$C to $^{40}$Ar. It is shown that the approximation gradually looses its accuracy with increasing charge product of the projectile and target nuclei because of the effects of finite excitation energy of the target nucleus. The relevance of such inaccuracy in analyzing the experimental data is also discussed.

# Applicability of the orientation average formula in heavy-ion fusion reactions of deformed nuclei

## Key claim
The orientation average formula for fusion cross sections in reactions with a well-deformed target nucleus is only exact in the limit of zero rotational excitation energy; its accuracy degrades with increasing charge product of projectile and target, and the deviation is much larger than predicted by the analytic perturbation formula of Hagino, Takigawa, and Brink (1995), though for light projectiles the formula remains safely applicable because the deviation from exact coupled-channels calculations is smaller than the deviation from experimental data.

## Method
The authors compared fusion cross sections and barrier distributions from the orientation average (sudden tunneling) formula with exact numerical solutions of coupled-channels equations obtained using the computer code CCFULL, keeping the finite excitation energy (0.082 MeV) of the deformed target nucleus ¹⁵⁴Sm. They considered reactions with spherical projectiles ranging from ¹²C to ⁴⁰Ar, included only quadrupole deformation (λ=2), truncated the rotational band at I_max=20⁺, and treated all projectiles as inert.

## Key numbers
- Excitation energy of the first excited 2⁺ state of ¹⁵⁴Sm: 0.082 MeV
- Deformation parameter β₂ of ¹⁵⁴Sm: 0.32 (determined by fitting ¹⁶O+¹⁵⁴Sm fusion data)
- Bare Coulomb barrier for ¹⁶O+¹⁵⁴Sm: V_B = 59.41 MeV
- Bare Coulomb barrier for ⁴⁰Ar+¹⁵⁴Sm: V_B = 127.57 MeV
- Rotational band truncated at I_max = 20⁺ member; convergence achieved by I_max = 12⁺ for ¹⁶O+¹⁵⁴Sm, but higher levels give non-negligible effects for heavy projectiles
- Interval ΔE = 2 MeV used for point-difference calculation of the fusion barrier distribution

## Context
This work extends the earlier analytic study of Hagino, Takigawa, and Brink (1995), who derived a perturbation formula for the finite-excitation-energy correction to the sudden tunneling approximation. The present paper shows that the analytic formula underestimates the deviation because it ignores the radial dependence of the coupling form factor and uses perturbation theory. The results indicate that for systems with large charge product (e.g., ⁴⁰Ar+¹⁵⁴Sm), proper coupled-channels calculations beyond the orientation average are needed to extract reliable nuclear structure information from high-precision barrier distribution data, while for light systems (e.g., ¹⁶O+¹⁵⁴Sm) the orientation average formula remains safely applicable.
