# Critical point signatures in the cluster expansion in fugacities

> The QCD baryon number density can formally be expanded into a Laurent series in fugacity, which is a relativistic generalization of Mayer's cluster expansion. We determine properties of the cluster expansion in a model with a phase transition and a critical point at finite baryon density, in which the Fourier coefficients of the expansion can be determined explicitly and to arbitrary order. The asymptotic behavior of Fourier coefficients changes qualitatively as one traverses the critical temperature and it is connected to the branch points of a thermodynamic potential associated with the phase transition. The results are discussed in the context of lattice QCD simulations at imaginary chemical potential. We argue that the location of a branch point closest to the imaginary chemical potential axis can be extracted through an analysis of an exponential suppression of Fourier coefficients. This is illustrated using the four leading coefficients both in a toy model as well as by using recent lattice QCD data.

## Key claim

The asymptotic behavior of Fourier coefficients of the baryon density cluster expansion changes qualitatively as one traverses the critical temperature, and the location of a branch point closest to the imaginary chemical potential axis can be extracted from an analysis of exponential suppression of these coefficients, as illustrated using the four leading coefficients in a toy model and in recent lattice QCD data.

## Method

The authors construct a "trivirial model" (TVM), a third-order polynomial expansion of the van der Waals equation of state in particle number density, which contains a first-order phase transition with a critical point. They apply the Lagrange inversion theorem to obtain explicit analytic expressions for all cluster expansion coefficients \(b_k(T)\) in terms of Hermite polynomials, then derive the asymptotic large-\(k\) behavior of these coefficients using known asymptotic representations of Hermite polynomials for subcritical, critical, and supercritical temperatures. The extracted asymptotic forms are then used to fit the four leading Fourier coefficients from lattice QCD data on \(N_\tau = 12\) lattices from Vovchenko (2017).

## Key numbers

* Critical point location in the trivirial model: \(T_c = \frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{2} \frac{a}{b}\), \(n_c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3} b}\), \(p_c = \frac{3-\sqrt{3}}{18} \frac{a}{b^2}\)
* Asymptotic behavior at subcritical temperatures (\(T < T_c\)): \(b_k \simeq A_- \, e^{-k \mu_{\rm sp1}/T} / k^{3/2}\)
* Asymptotic behavior at the critical temperature (\(T = T_c\)): \(b_k \simeq A_c \, e^{-k \mu_c/T} / k^{4/3}\)
* Asymptotic behavior at supercritical temperatures (\(T > T_c\)): \(b_k \simeq A_+ \, e^{-k \mu_{\rm crs}^{\rm R}/T} / k^{3/2} \, \sin(k \mu_{\rm crs}^{\rm I}/T + \theta_0/2)\)
* Extracted \(\mu_{\rm br}^R/T\) from fits to four leading TVM coefficients vs exact values: 5.83 vs 5.89 at \(T = 100\) MeV; 4.26 vs 4.40 at \(T = 120\) MeV; 2.79 vs 2.98 at \(T = 150\) MeV
* Fit at \(T \approx T_c = 120\) MeV with \(\alpha = 4/3\) yields \(\mu_{\rm br}^R = 4.34\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of cluster expansion coefficients in the context of QCD phase transitions, building on the Fourier expansion method applied to lattice QCD at imaginary chemical potential by de Forcrand (2002) and D'Elia (2002), and on the calculation of the four leading Fourier coefficients at the physical point by Vovchenko (2017). The results are consistent with the Landau theory analysis of Almasi (2019) for chiral phase transitions, though with a different pre-exponential factor (\(k^{-3/2}\) instead of \(k^{-2}\)) due to the coupling of \(\mu_B\) to both Ising variables at a finite-density critical point. The approach enables extraction of thermodynamic singularities from a limited number of leading Fourier coefficients, which can be applied to ongoing lattice QCD simulations searching for the QCD critical point.
