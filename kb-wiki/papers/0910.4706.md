# Towards Study of Light Scalar Mesons in Polarization Phenomena

> After a short review of the production mechanisms of the light scalars which reveal their nature and indicate their quark structure, we suggest to study the mixing of the isovector a^0_0(980) with the isoscalar f_0(980) in spin effects.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the mixing of the isovector a0(980) with the isoscalar f0(980) can be studied through spin asymmetries in polarized target experiments, specifically by observing a jump in the normalized azimuthal spin asymmetry of the S-wave eta pi0 production cross section near the K anti-K thresholds, which would unambiguously establish the existence of this mixing phenomenon.

## Method

The authors review the production mechanisms of light scalar mesons (sigma(600), kappa(700-900), f0(980), a0(980)) that reveal their four-quark (q2 qbar2) nature, including the K+ K- loop mechanism for phi radiative decays and two-photon decays via rescattering. They then calculate the a0(980)-f0(980) mixing amplitude from the K+ K- and K0 anti-K0 intermediate states, which is dominated by the kaon mass difference, and propose measuring the spin asymmetry in the reaction pi- p_uparrow -> (a0(980) + f0(980)) n -> eta pi0 n using a polarized target at high energy.

## Key numbers

- The a0(980)-f0(980) mixing amplitude magnitude near K anti-K thresholds: approximately 0.03 GeV2, derived from |g_{a0K+K-} g_{f0K+K-}|/(16 pi) times sqrt(2(m_{K0} - m_{K+})/m_{K0}) ≈ 0.127 |g_{a0K+K-} g_{f0K+K-}|/(16 pi)
- The strong coupling product |g_{a0K+K-} g_{f0K+K-}|/(4 pi) ≈ 1 GeV2
- In the chiral shielding model for sigma(600): M_res = 0.43 GeV, Gamma_res(M_res^2) = 0.67 GeV, m_sigma = 0.93 GeV, renormalized width = 0.53 GeV
- Two-photon light scalar widths averaged over resonance mass distributions: <Gamma_{f0 -> gamma gamma}>_{pi pi} ≈ 0.19 keV, <Gamma_{a0 -> gamma gamma}>_{pi eta} ≈ 0.3 keV, <Gamma_{sigma -> gamma gamma}>_{pi pi} ≈ 0.45 keV
- Predicted a0(980) and f0(980) two-photon widths in the four-quark MIT bag model: approximately 0.27 keV each

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical discovery of a0(980)-f0(980) mixing as a threshold phenomenon (Achasov and Shestakov, 1979) and their earlier studies of light scalar meson production mechanisms in phi radiative decays and gamma-gamma collisions. The paper builds on experimental confirmation by the Belle Collaboration (2007-2009) of the four-quark nature of light scalars through gamma-gamma collision data, and on the VES Collaboration's observation (2008) of the first effect of a0(980)-f0(980) mixing. The proposed polarized target experiment would enable unambiguous measurement of the a0(980) and f0(980) coupling constants to K anti-K channels.
