# Neutron-deuteron scattering cross-sections with chiral $NN$ interactions using wave-packet continuum discretization

> In this work we present a framework that allows to solve the Faddeev equations for three-nucleon scattering using the wave-packet continuum-discretization method. We perform systematic benchmarks using results in the literature and study in detail the convergence of this method with respect to the number of wave packets. We compute several different elastic neutron-deuteron scattering cross-section observables for a variety of energies using chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions. For the interaction $\text{N2LO}_{\text{opt}}$ we find good agreement with data for nucleon scattering-energies $E_\text{Lab} \leq 70$ MeV and a slightly larger maximum of the neutron analyzing power $A_y(n)$ at $E_\text{Lab} = 10$ MeV and 21 MeV compared with other interactions. This work represents a first step towards a systematic inclusion of three-nucleon scattering observables in the construction of next-generation nuclear interactions.

# Neutron-deuteron scattering cross-sections with chiral NN interactions using wave-packet continuum discretization

## Key claim
The wave-packet continuum-discretization method can solve the Faddeev equations for three-nucleon scattering with sufficient accuracy for elastic neutron-deuteron observables up to laboratory energies of approximately 70 MeV, but the method's convergence deteriorates at higher energies and for observables sensitive to fine details of the nuclear interaction.

## Method
The authors implement the wave-packet continuum-discretization (WPCD) method to solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of the Faddeev equations for elastic neutron-deuteron scattering. They discretize the continuum of Jacobi momenta using wave-packet basis states defined on a Chebyshev grid, diagonalize the nucleon-nucleon Hamiltonian in a free wave-packet basis to obtain scattering wave-packets, and compute the on-shell transition operator via a Neumann series with Pade extrapolation. The calculations use partial waves with total angular momentum up to 17/2 and nucleon-nucleon channels with J up to 3.

## Key numbers
- Convergence to standard Faddeev results within approximately 1% for all imaginary and real parts of three-nucleon phase shifts for partial waves up to 7/2 using NWP greater than or equal to 125 wave packets
- Good agreement with experimental data for nucleon scattering energies E_Lab less than or equal to 70 MeV using the N2LOopt interaction
- Neutron analyzing power A_y(n) computed at E_Lab = 10 MeV and 21 MeV shows slightly larger maximum with N2LOopt compared with other interactions
- Computational cost scales as approximately NWP^4, with NWP = 125 wave packets used for production calculations
- Solving for all amplitudes with E_Lab less than 100 MeV is approximately 2 times slower than solving at a single scattering energy

## Context
This work extends the WPCD method, previously developed by Rubtsova (2015), to chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions for three-nucleon scattering calculations. It benchmarks against standard Faddeev results from Gloeckle (1995) using the NijmI potential and compares predictions using the N2LOopt interaction from Ekstrom (2013) and the INNNLO interaction from Entem (2003). The calculations address the long-standing A_y-puzzle identified by Huber (1998) and enable future systematic inclusion of three-nucleon scattering observables in the construction of next-generation nuclear interactions, particularly for simultaneous statistical analyses of two-nucleon and three-nucleon data.
