# Chiral vortices and pseudoscalar condensation due to rotation

> We investigate the influence of rotation on the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in strongly interacting matter. We develop a self-consistent Bogoliubov-de Gennes-like theoretical framework to study the inhomogeneous chiral condensate and the possible chiral vortex state in rotating finite-size matter in four-fermion interacting theories. We show that for sufficiently rapid rotation in $2+1$ dimensions, the ground state can be a chiral vortex state, a type of topological defect in analogy to superfluids and superconductors. The vortex state exhibits pion condensation, providing a new mechanism to realize pseudoscalar condensation in strongly interacting matter.

## Key claim

For a finite-size system in 2+1 dimensions, the ground state can become a chiral vortex state, a topological defect with nonzero circulation, when the angular velocity exceeds a critical value, but only if the system size is small enough that this critical velocity remains below the causality bound ωR < 1.

## Method

The authors developed a self-consistent Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) theoretical framework for a four-fermion (Nambu–Jona-Lasinio) model with U(1) chiral symmetry in a rotating finite-size circular box. They solved the BdG eigenvalue equation numerically by expanding the wave functions in a Bessel-function basis that satisfies the no-incoming-flux boundary condition, and simultaneously solved the variational (gap) equation for the inhomogeneous condensate profile M(ρ) at the leading order in 1/N. The study was performed in 2+1 dimensions because the model is renormalizable there, avoiding regularization-scheme dependence.

## Key numbers

* The critical angular velocity ωc for the κ=1 vortex to become the ground state at T=0 lies below the causality bound ωmax = 1/R for system sizes M0R = 4 and M0R = 5; for M0R = 10, ωc exceeds the causality bound and becomes unphysical.
* The vortex core structure obeys M(ρ) ∼ ρ^{|κ|} for ρ → 0, with the healing length ξ estimated from the vortex excitation energy Ev ∝ κ² ln(R/ξ).
* The renormalized vacuum fermion mass M₀ is the only mass scale in the 2+1-dimensional theory; the critical coupling is Gc = π/Λ, and for G > Gc the effective fermion mass in vacuum is M* = M₀.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry breaking under rotation that used the local density approximation (LDA) (Ebihara, Fukushima, and Mameda, 2017; other works from 2015–2018), which treats the condensate as locally homogeneous and cannot describe vortex states. The self-consistent BdG approach reveals that for sufficiently rapid rotation in small systems, a chiral vortex state with angular-stripe pion condensation becomes the ground state, analogous to quantized vortices in rotating Bose-Einstein condensates and fermionic superfluids. The results suggest that similar topological defects could appear in strongly interacting matter produced in heavy-ion collisions and may be testable in planar condensed-matter systems such as graphene.
