# Shell-model studies on exotic nuclei around 132Sn

> The study of exotic nuclei around 132Sn is a subject of current experimental and theoretical interest. Experimental information for nuclei in the vicinity of 132Sn, which have been long inaccessible to spectroscopic studies, is now available thanks to new advanced facilities and techniques. The experimental data which have been now become available for these neutron-rich nuclei may suggest a modification in the shell structure. They are, in fact, somewhat different from what one might expect by extrapolating the existing results for N<82, and as a possible explanation a change in the single-proton level scheme has been suggested. The latter would be caused by a more diffuse nuclear surface, and could be seen as a precursor of major effects which should show up at larger neutron excess. New data offer therefore the opportunity to test the shell model and look for a possible evolution of shell structure when going toward neutron drip line. This is stimulating shell-model studies in this region. Here, we present an overview of recent shell-model studies of 132Sn neighbors, focusing attention on those calculations employing realistic effective interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that realistic shell-model calculations using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, renormalized via either the Brueckner G-matrix or the low-momentum Vlow-k approach, can reproduce experimental spectra of nuclei around 132Sn, but the neutron-proton channel shows a slight dependence on the renormalization technique and the size of the intermediate-state space used in the effective interaction.

## Method

The authors derive shell-model effective interactions for nuclei with valence nucleons outside the doubly-magic 132Sn core, starting from the CD-Bonn realistic nucleon-nucleon potential. The short-range repulsion is renormalized using two approaches: the Brueckner G-matrix method and the Vlow-k renormalization-group technique (with a cutoff momentum Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹). The effective Hamiltonian is obtained via the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion, with the Q-box calculated up to second order in Vlow-k (or third order in G) using a harmonic-oscillator basis (ħω = 7.88 MeV), and single-particle energies are taken from experimental spectra of 133Sb and 133Sn.

## Key numbers

- The Vlow-k cutoff momentum is set to Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹, chosen to preserve two-nucleon physics up to the anelastic threshold while yielding a smooth potential.
- The harmonic-oscillator parameter used is ħω = 7.88 MeV.
- For 134Te, the calculated 2⁺₁ level is 1.33 MeV (Vlow-k) and 1.21 MeV (G-matrix), compared to the experimental value of 1.28 MeV.
- For 134Sn, the calculated 2⁺₁ level is 0.733 MeV (Vlow-k) and 0.775 MeV (G-matrix), compared to the experimental value of 0.726 MeV.
- For 134Sb, the calculated 1⁻₁ level is 0.052 MeV (Vlow-k) and 0.329 MeV (G-matrix), compared to the experimental value of 0.013 MeV.
- For 135Sb, the calculated 5/2⁺₁ level is 0.391 MeV (Vlow-k) and 0.527 MeV (G-matrix), compared to the experimental value of 0.282 MeV.
- The neutron 0i₁₃/₂ single-particle energy is estimated from the 10⁺ state in 134Sb to be 2.694 ± 0.2 MeV (Urban, 1999).
- The proton 2s₁/₂ single-particle energy is estimated from odd N=82 isotones to lie around 2.8 MeV excitation energy (Andreozzi, 1997).

## Context

This work extends the program of realistic shell-model calculations using the Vlow-k approach (Coraggio, 2002; Coraggio, 2004; Coraggio, 2005a; Coraggio, 2006a) to the neutron-rich region around 132Sn, complementing earlier G-matrix calculations by the Oslo group (Shergur, 2005a; Shergur, 2005b; Kartamyshev, 2006; Brown, 2005). The results enable further tests of shell evolution toward the neutron drip line and address the "shell-quenching" phenomenon in N=82 isotones relevant to r-process abundances (Dillmann, 2003), while also showing that the Vlow-k method allows easier inclusion of more intermediate states than the G-matrix approach.
