# Nucleon Energy Correlators as a Probe of Light-Quark Dipole Operators at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We propose nucleon energy correlators (NECs) as a novel framework to probe electroweak light-quark dipole operators in deep inelastic scattering with an unpolarized nucleon. These operators encode chirality-flipping interactions, whose effects are usually quadratically suppressed in unpolarized cross sections. We construct a chiral-odd quark NEC that accesses quark transverse spin via azimuthal angle asymmetries in the energy flow of the target fragmentation region. These asymmetries serve as clean and powerful observables, enabling linear constraints on the quark dipole couplings. Unlike existing methods, our approach requires neither polarized nucleon beams nor final-state hadron identification, relying instead on fully inclusive calorimetric measurements. This work establishes one of the first applications of energy correlator observables to new physics searches and opens a promising direction for precision studies of chirality-flipping effects at electron-ion colliders.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that nucleon energy correlators (NECs) can probe electroweak light-quark dipole operators in deep inelastic scattering with an unpolarized nucleon, where these chirality-flipping effects are usually quadratically suppressed. The key hedge is that this approach works specifically through the target fragmentation region and requires constructing a chiral-odd quark NEC that accesses quark transverse spin via azimuthal angle asymmetries in the energy flow.

## Method
The authors construct a quark transversity NEC, denoted \(h_1^t(x,\theta^2)\), from the chiral-odd component of the NEC correlation matrix for an unpolarized nucleon target. They derive factorization formulas for the DIS energy pattern in the target fragmentation region that include the interference between the Standard Model amplitude and light-quark dipole operators, producing \(\sin\phi\) and \(\cos\phi\) azimuthal modulations. The \(\theta\)-weighted moments of the NECs are related to \(\boldsymbol{k}_\perp\)-moments of transverse momentum dependent parton distributions, specifically the Boer-Mulders TMD, and evolved using DGLAP evolution equations under the approximation that the moments follow the same evolution as the NECs themselves.

## Key numbers
- The center-of-mass energy for the EIC analysis is set to \(\sqrt{s} = 105\ \mathrm{GeV}\) with an inelasticity cut of \(0.1 < y < 0.9\)
- The dimensionful dipole couplings are fixed to \(c_{u\gamma} = v / (\sqrt{2}\,\mathrm{TeV}^2)\) for both asymmetries, with \(c_{uZ} = v / (\sqrt{2}\,\mathrm{TeV}^2)\) for \(|A_{UU}^{\sin \phi}|\) and \(c_{uZ} = 200\,v / (\sqrt{2}\,\mathrm{TeV}^2)\) for \(|A_{LU}^{\sin \phi}|\)
- \(A_{LU}^{\sin \phi}\) from \(c_{u\gamma}\) reaches \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-2})\) at \(Q=30\) GeV
- The most stringent combined constraints from \(A_{UU}^{\sin\phi}\) and \(A_{LU}^{\sin\phi}\) reach values in the range \(\mathcal{O}(0.01)\sim\mathcal{O}(0.1)\) for \(C_{q\gamma}\)
- For the EIC analysis, \(Q\) bins of width 10 GeV are used over \([10,60]\) GeV for \(A_{UU}\), and 5 GeV bins over \([10,50]\) GeV with an additional bin \([50,60]\) GeV for \(A_{LU}\)
- The Boer-Mulders TMD fit parameters are \(\lambda_u = 2.0 \pm 0.1\) and \(\lambda_d = -1.111 \pm 0.001\) from Barone et al. (2009)

## Context
This work extends the NEC framework previously developed for the Standard Model by Chen et al. (2024) to new physics searches, establishing one of the first applications of energy correlator observables to beyond-Standard-Model physics. It provides an alternative to methods requiring polarized nucleon beams or final-state hadron identification, such as the dihadron approach of Wen et al. (2024), and offers advantages over LHC Drell-Yan constraints from Boughezal et al. (2021) by providing linear sensitivity to dipole couplings at \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda^{-2})\) rather than quadratic \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda^{-4})\) suppression, while also enabling separate extraction of real and imaginary parts of the couplings. The approach enables precision studies of chirality-flipping effects at the EIC, HERA, and LHeC without requiring polarized beams or hadron identification.
