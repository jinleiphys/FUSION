# Nuclear models for inclusive lepton-nucleus scattering in the quasi-elastic region and beyond

> High-precision measurements in neutrino oscillation experiments require a very accurate description of the lepton-nucleus scattering process. Several cross-section calculations are available, but important discrepancies are still present between different model predictions. For the quasi-elastic channel, dominated by one particle-one hole excitations, an overview over several nuclear models - specifically Relativistic Fermi Gas, SuperScaling Approach, Spectral Function, Hartree-Fock and Random Phase Approximation - is presented and compared with data for electron-nucleus scattering, a very important process for testing theoretical models validity, highlighting the specific features of each approach. Furthermore an ongoing microscopic calculation of the two particle-two hole excitations contribution to the electromagnetic response is presented, and some preliminary results are shown.

## Key claim

The paper provides a comparative overview of five nuclear models (Relativistic Fermi Gas, SuperScaling Approach, Spectral Function, Hartree-Fock, and Random Phase Approximation) for describing inclusive electron-nucleus scattering in the quasi-elastic region, but notes that no single model is universally adequate, HF and RPA work better at low energy transfer while SuSA and SF better describe the high-energy tail, and the complete 2p2h calculation including exchange terms is still in progress.

## Method

The authors computed double-differential cross sections for electron-carbon scattering at various beam energies and scattering angles using five nuclear models: Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG), SuperScaling Approach (SuSA), Spectral Function (SF), Hartree-Fock (HF), and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) in both ring and fully antisymmetric (aRPA) forms. For the 2p2h channel, they evaluated the transverse nuclear response using Meson Exchange Currents (MEC) within a relativistic Fermi gas ground state, comparing with previous calculations by De Pace et al. (2003). The HF and RPA calculations were performed in infinite nuclear matter using a non-relativistic expansion of a meson-exchange Bonn potential, while the SF used the Rome spectral function.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum for carbon: \(p_F = 225\) MeV
- Fermi momentum for iron: \(p_F = 256\) MeV
- 2p2h transverse nuclear responses computed at momentum transfers \(|{\bf q}| = 600\) MeV and \(|{\bf q}| = 1140\) MeV for iron
- Scaling behavior in SuSA holds for momentum transfer \(q > 400\) MeV/c approximately
- The RFG scaling function is \(f_{RFG}(\psi) = \frac{3}{4}(1-\psi^2)\)
- The spectral function shell-model accounts for 80% of the normalization, with the remaining 20% from NN correlations

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of testing nuclear models against electron scattering data, which serves as a benchmark for neutrino-nucleus calculations needed for DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande experiments. The comparison builds on prior work by Smith (1972) for RFG, Benhar (1994) for the Rome spectral function, Donnelly (1999) for superscaling, and De Pace (2003) for 2p2h MEC calculations. The authors note that many existing RPA-based neutrino cross section calculations (Martini 2009, Nieves 2011) use the ring approximation with phenomenological contact interactions, whereas this work includes a microscopic treatment of exchange terms via continued fraction expansion. The next steps include applying the same models to neutrino-nucleus cross sections and extending to semi-inclusive observables.
