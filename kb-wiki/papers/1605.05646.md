# Sensitivity of Λ single-particle energies to the ΛN spin-orbit coupling and to nuclear core structure in p-shell and sd-shell hypernuclei

> We introduce a mean field model based on realistic 2-body baryon interactions and calculate spectra of a set of p-shell and sd-shell Λ hypernuclei - 13ΛC, 17ΛO, 21ΛNe, 29ΛSi and 41ΛCa. The hypernuclear spectra are compared with the results of a relativistic mean field (RMF) model and available experimental data. The sensitivity of Λ single-particle energies to the nuclear core structure is explored. Special attention is paid to the effect of spin-orbit ΛN interaction on the energy splitting of the Λ single particle levels 0p3/2 and 0p1/2. In particular, we analyze the contribution of the symmetric (SLS) and the anti-symmetric (ALS) spin-orbit terms to the energy splitting. We give qualitative predictions for the calculated hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The energy splitting between the Λ 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 single-particle levels in p-shell and sd-shell hypernuclei is sensitive to both the nuclear core configuration and the specific spin-orbit terms (symmetric SLS and antisymmetric ALS) of the ΛN interaction, with the ALS term acting opposite to the SLS term at roughly 2/3 of its magnitude; however, the calculated splittings depend strongly on the chosen nuclear core configuration in the mean-field model based on realistic interactions, unlike the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach which gives roughly constant splittings.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations for 13ΛC, 17ΛO, 21ΛNe, 29ΛSi, and 41ΛCa using a Hamiltonian with realistic NN interaction (NNLOopt) plus a density-dependent term to simulate three-body forces, and the ESC08c ΛN interaction (YNG force) with symmetric (SLS) and antisymmetric (ALS) spin-orbit terms included via the Scheerbaum approximation. For open-shell cores (12C, 20Ne, 28Si), several configurations of the nuclear core were considered in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis. The results were compared with RMF model (NL-SH parametrization) calculations and available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The charge rms radius of 12C (p3/2^4 configuration) with fitted density-dependent term: 2.50 fm (experimental: 2.47 fm)
- The charge rms radius of 16O with fitted density-dependent term: 2.72 fm (experimental: 2.70 fm)
- The charge rms radius of 40Ca with fitted density-dependent term: 3.48 fm (experimental: 3.48 fm)
- Fermi momentum kF = 1.20 fm^-1 for 13ΛC (p3/2^4 configuration) and for 17ΛO
- Fermi momentum kF = 1.29 fm^-1 for 41ΛCa
- In 13ΛC (p3/2^4 configuration), the Λ energy splitting Δp = (E(0p1/2) - E(0p3/2)) = -0.58 MeV without spin-orbit terms, +0.99 MeV with SLS only, and -0.06 MeV with SLS+ALS (RMF: 0.27 MeV)
- In 17ΛO, Δp = -0.07 MeV without spin-orbit terms, +0.82 MeV with SLS only, and +0.23 MeV with SLS+ALS (RMF: 0.24 MeV)
- In 41ΛCa, Δp = -0.01 MeV without spin-orbit terms, +0.57 MeV with SLS only, and +0.20 MeV with SLS+ALS (RMF: 0.21 MeV)
- In 29ΛSi (d5/2^6 configuration), Δp = -0.64 MeV without spin-orbit terms, -0.25 MeV with SLS only, and -0.50 MeV with SLS+ALS (RMF: 0.29 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies by Millener (2001, 2012) and few-body cluster calculations by Hiyama et al. (2000) on Λ spin-orbit splitting in light hypernuclei, applying a mean-field approach with realistic baryon interactions (ESC08c potential from the Nijmegen group) to a broader set of p-shell and sd-shell hypernuclei. The results enable future experimental exploration at JLab of Λ 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 level splittings in medium-heavy hypernuclei, and motivate theoretical improvements including deformed-basis calculations with configuration mixing, direct implementation of three-body forces, and inclusion of ΛN tensor terms.
