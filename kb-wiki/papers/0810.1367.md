# Characterizations of the medium in jet quenching calculations

> The modification of hard jets in dense matter has so far been described by four different formalisms based on perturbative QCD (pQCD). In these proceedings, we compare the various approximations made in these different schemes, especially those regarding the structure of the medium through which jets propagate. Following this, we highlight some of the major differences in the various physical processes contained in the different approaches.

## Key claim

The paper compares four perturbative QCD-based formalisms for jet quenching (HT, ASW/BDMPS-Z, AMY, and GLV) and argues that while they use different approximations regarding the medium structure and different physical processes, they produce rather similar predictions for experimental observables when applied to an identical medium.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical foundations of the four major jet quenching formalisms, Higher Twist (HT), Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW/BDMPS-Z), Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY), and Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV), focusing on how each scheme treats the medium structure, the hierarchy of scales (jet energy E, virtuality Q, medium scale μ, and medium length L), and the calculation of single gluon emission spectra. The paper then compares how each formalism iterates single emissions to compute multiple gluon emissions and the final medium-modified fragmentation function.

## Key numbers

- The transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) is defined as the transverse momentum picked up by a hard jet per unit length; for a static medium, the characteristic frequency \(\omega_c = \hat{q} L^2/2\), where L is the medium length.
- In the ASW multiple soft scattering limit for a static medium with large length, the radiation distribution scales as \(\omega dI/d\omega \simeq (2\alpha_s C_R/\pi) \sqrt{\omega_c/(2\omega)}\) for \(\omega < \omega_c\) and as \((1/12)(\omega/\omega_c)^2\) for \(\omega > \omega_c\).
- The opacity \(\bar{n} = L/\lambda\) quantifies the number of scattering centers seen by a jet, where \(\lambda\) is the mean free path and L is the medium thickness; the mean free path satisfies \(\lambda \gg 1/\mu\) where \(\mu\) is the color screening length.
- In the AMY formalism, the formation time for collinear radiation \(\sim 1/(g^2 T)\) is of the same order as the mean free time between soft scatterings, with momentum transfers \(\sim gT\) and temperature T.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of the four major jet quenching formalisms (HT, ASW/BDMPS-Z, AMY, GLV) that originated from early attempts by Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff and Zakharov (1996-2000) and subsequent developments by Guo and Wang (2000), Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (2000-2002), and Gyulassy, Levai and Vitev (1999-2001). The paper enables a systematic understanding of how different medium characterizations affect jet modification calculations and sets the stage for the companion publication (Bass, 2008) that compares predictions from these formalisms using an identical medium description.
