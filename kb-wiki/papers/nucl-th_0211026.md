# Quenched Chiral Physics in Baryon Masses

> Recent work has identified that the primary differences between quenched and dynamical spectroscopy can be described by chiral loop effects. Here we highlight the features of this study.

## Key claim

The primary differences between quenched and dynamical baryon spectroscopy can be described by chiral loop effects, with the pion-cloud source (the baryon without its pion dressing) showing remarkably similar behavior in both quenched and full QCD once the appropriate chiral self-energy contributions are incorporated.

## Method

The authors fit lattice data for nucleon and delta masses from Bernard et al. (2001) using a functional form that combines a linear term in m_pi^2 (dominant at heavy quark masses) with meson-loop self-energies evaluated using a dipole regulator. For quenched QCD, the self-energies include modified pion loops and additional eta' loop contributions (axial hairpin and double hairpin diagrams) that arise because the eta' becomes a Goldstone boson in the quenched approximation. The self-energy integrals were evaluated on discretised momentum grids to account for finite-volume effects of the lattice simulations.

## Key numbers

- The leading non-analytic (LNA) term for the nucleon mass is delta m_N^(LNA) = -5.6 m_pi^3 (with m_pi and delta m_N in GeV); at m_pi = 0.5 GeV this gives delta m_N^(LNA) = 0.7 GeV
- The transition scale where non-analytic chiral contributions become important is below pion masses of about 400-500 MeV
- Best fit parameters for physical (dynamical) data with dipole regulator Lambda = 0.8 GeV: alpha_N = 1.27(2) GeV, beta_N = 0.90(5) GeV^-1, alpha_Delta = 1.45(3) GeV, beta_Delta = 0.74(8) GeV^-1
- Best fit parameters for quenched data with dipole regulator Lambda = 0.8 GeV: alpha_N = 1.24(2) GeV, beta_N = 0.85(6) GeV^-1, alpha_Delta = 1.45(4) GeV, beta_Delta = 0.72(11) GeV^-1
- Only about 50 MeV of the observed 300 MeV N-Delta mass splitting arises from pion loops in full QCD

## Context

This work extends the chiral extrapolation method proposed by Leinweber et al. (1999) for full QCD to the quenched approximation, building on the quark-flow analysis of non-analytic contributions to baryon magnetic moments by Leinweber (2001) and the quenched chiral perturbation theory approach for mesons by Sharpe (1990, 1992). The results enable a practical method to obtain more physical results from quenched simulations: one can determine the meson-cloud source structure from quenched data and then incorporate the chiral structures of the physical theory phenomenologically. The authors note that further accurate lattice simulations at light masses will be needed to test the assumption that parameters of the chiral Lagrangian show negligible differences between quenched and 3-flavour dynamical simulations.
