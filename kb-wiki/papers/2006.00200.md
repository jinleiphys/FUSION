# Analysis of the QCD Kondo phase using random matrices

> We propose a novel random matrix model that describes the QCD Kondo phase. The model correctly implements both the chiral symmetry of light quarks and the SU(2) spin symmetry of heavy quarks. We analytically take the large-N limit with N the matrix size and show that the model has three phases: the pure Kondo phase with no chiral condensate, the pure chirally broken phase with no Kondo condensate, and the coexistence phase. The model predicts that the pairing form of the Kondo condensate in the coexistence phase is significantly altered compared to the pure Kondo phase. For each phase, we rigorously derive the low-energy effective theory of Nambu-Goldstone modes and obtain compact closed expressions for the partition function with external sources. We also include a chiral chemical potential into the model and examine the vacuum structure.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a random matrix model for the QCD Kondo effect that correctly implements both chiral symmetry of light quarks and SU(2) spin symmetry of heavy quarks, but the analysis is limited to a single light flavor (N_f=1) in the chiral limit and the model is dimensionless, so it cannot predict quantities in physical units such as GeV or Lambda_QCD.

## Method

The author constructs a random matrix partition function using N x N complex matrices W and V, where W mediates light-light quark interactions and V mediates light-heavy quark interactions, with the Dirac operator for light quarks written as a 2N x 2N non-Hermitian matrix. The model is solved analytically in the large-N limit by performing a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce auxiliary fields sigma (for chiral condensate) and K (for Kondo condensate), then minimizing the free energy to determine the ground state. Low-energy effective theories of Nambu-Goldstone modes are derived by adding external source terms and taking the large-N limit with sources scaled as O(1/N).

## Key numbers

* Three phases are identified as a function of the parameter xi = lambda |g_q|/|g_Q|: pure Kondo phase for 0 <= xi < 0.556, coexistence phase for 0.556 <= xi <= 1, and pure chirally broken phase for xi > 1.
* In the coexistence phase, the chiral condensate sigma = (xi + sqrt(xi^2 + 8))/4 and the Kondo condensate parameter a = (4 - xi^2 - xi sqrt(xi^2 + 8))/8.
* With a chiral chemical potential mu_5, the Kondo condensates K_{R up} and K_{L down} show phase transitions at mu_5 = +/- 1 and at mu_5^C = 0.27846, which is the solution of exp(1+x)x = 1.
* The Kondo scale in quark matter is estimated to be 10 to 100 MeV, while that in normal metals is of order 10 K, a difference by a factor of 10^11.

## Context

This work extends the study of the QCD Kondo effect, which was previously analyzed using mean-field approximations in works by Yasui (2016, 2017), Hattori (2015), and Suenaga (2019), by specifically elucidating the impact of nearly gapless Nambu-Goldstone modes that were dismissed in conventional mean-field treatments. The random matrix approach builds on the established application of random matrix theory to QCD pioneered by Shuryak (1992) and Verbaarschot (1993, 1994), and the results for the pure Kondo phase coincide exactly with the finite-volume partition function of two-flavor QCD in the epsilon-regime from Leutwyler (1992). The work enables future microscopic studies of the QCD phase diagram with heavy quarks and provides predictions for the pairing form of the Kondo condensate in the coexistence phase that should be checked in more realistic NJL-type models.
