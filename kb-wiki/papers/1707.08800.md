# Sanity check for $NN$ bound states in lattice QCD with Lüscher's finite volume formula -- Exposing Symptoms of Fake Plateaux --

> A sanity check rules out certain types of obviously false results, but does not catch every possible error. After reviewing such a sanity check for $NN$ bound states with the Lüscher's finite volume formula[1-3], we give further evidences for the operator dependence of plateaux, a symptom of the fake plateau problem, against the claim in [4]. We then present our critical comments on [5] by NPLQCD: (i) Operator dependences of plateaux in NPL2013[6,7] exist with the $P$-values of 4--5%. (ii) The volume independence of plateaux in NPL2013 does not prove their correctness. (iii) Effective range expansion (ERE) fits in NPL2013 violate the physical pole condition. (iv) Ref.[5] is partly based on new data and analysis different from the original ones[6,7]. (v) A new ERE in Refs.[5,8] does not satisfy the Lüscher's finite volume formula. [1] T. Iritani et al., JHEP 10 (2016) 101. [2] S. Aoki et al., PoS (LATTICE2016) 109. [3] T. Iritani et al., 1703.0720. [4] T. Yamazaki et al., PoS (LATTICE2017) 108. [5] S.R. Beane et al., 1705.09239. [6] S.R. Beane et al., PRD87 (2013) 034506. [7] S.R. Beane et al., PRC88 (2013) 024003. [8] M.L. Wagman et al., 1706.06550.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the binding energies of two-nucleon (NN) systems extracted from temporal correlation functions at t ≈ 1 fm in lattice QCD are unreliable due to contaminations from excited scattering states, and that the "fake plateau problem" undermines claims of NN bound states in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels at heavy pion masses, despite counterclaims by other groups.

## Method

The authors perform a "sanity check" on published NN lattice data by testing three criteria: (i) consistency of results across different source operators, (ii) regularity of the volume dependence, and (iii) satisfaction of the physical pole condition in effective range expansion (ERE) fits. They demonstrate the fake plateau problem using mock-up data for the effective energy shift ΔE^eff_NN(t) with elastic excitation energy δE_el. = 50 MeV and inelastic excitation δE_inel. = 500 MeV, showing that plateaux at t ≈ 1 fm can appear even when the ground state saturation requires t ≥ 4 fm. They also perform one-to-one χ² comparisons of ground state energies between different operator choices in NPL2013 and CalLat2017 data.

## Key numbers

- The typical lowest elastic excitation energy at L ≈ 4 fm and m_N ≈ 2 GeV is δE_el. = 50 MeV, implying ground state saturation requires t ≥ 1/δE_el. ≈ 4 fm.
- The inelastic excitation energy from the heavy pion mass is δE_inel. = 500 MeV.
- For the mock-up data, the coefficient b₁ = ±0.1 (with c₀ = 0.01) produces fake plateaux at t ≈ 1 fm.
- One-to-one χ² comparisons between NPL2013 and CalLat2017 show P-values as low as 4-5% (e.g., χ²/dof = 3.7 with P = 5% for ¹S₀ at L=24 between NPL(0) and NPL(2); χ²/dof = 5.0 with P = 3% for ¹S₀ at L=24 between NPL(0) and CL(d)).
- For the ¹S₀ channel at L=32, the comparison between NPL2013 and CalLat2017 for the 1st excited state gives χ²/dof = 2.6 with P = 11%; for ³S₁ at L=32, the same comparison gives χ²/dof = 8.0 with P = 0.5%.
- The NPL2013 ground state energy for ¹S₀ at L=24 with d=0 is -17.8(3.3) MeV, while the value in Beane (2017) is -19.0(+2.5/-2.2) MeV; for ³S₁ at L=24 with d=0, NPL2013 gives -25.4(5.4) MeV versus -28.5(±1.7) MeV in Beane (2017).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous sanity check (Iritani 2016, Aoki 2016, Iritani 2017) which found that none of the NN bound state claims in the literature passed all three sanity criteria. It directly contradicts the claims of Yamazaki (2017) and Beane (2017) that their data are reliable, and argues against the NPLQCD collaboration's results (Beane 2013, Beane 2013) and the CalLat2017 results (Berkowitz 2015). The paper enables future work by emphasizing that the variational method (Luscher 1990) is mandatory for reliable NN studies, rather than simple plateau fitting at early times.
