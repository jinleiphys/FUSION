# Test of Isospin Conservation in Thermal Neutron-induced Fission of $^{245}$Cm

> We have, recently, shown that the general trends of partition-wise fission fragment mass distribution in heavy ion (HI) induced compound nuclear (CN) fission of heavy nuclei can be reproduced reasonably well by using the concept of isospin conservation, hence providing a direct evidence of isospin conservation in neutron-rich systems [1, 2, 3, 4]. In this paper, we test the concept of isospin conservation to reproduce the fission fragment mass distribution emerging from thermal neutron-induced CN fission reaction, 245Cm(nth, f). As earlier, we use Kelson's conjectures [5] to assign isospin to neutron-rich fragments emitted in fission, which suggest the formation of fission fragments in Isobaric Analog states (IAS). We calculate the relative yields of neutron-rich fragments using the concept of isospin conservation and basic isospin algebra. The calculated results reproduce quite well the experimentally known partition wise mass distributions. This highlights the usefulness of isospin as an approximately good quantum number in neutron-rich nuclei. This also allows us to predict the fragment distribution of the most symmetric Cd-Cd partition and the heavier mass fragment distributions, both not measured so far.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the concept of isospin conservation, using Kelson's conjectures to assign isospin values to fission fragments via Isobaric Analog States (IAS), can reproduce the experimentally known partition-wise fission fragment mass distributions for thermal neutron-induced fission of \(^{245}\)Cm, with the caveat that the calculations take important inputs from experimental data and do not derive distributions from first principles.

## Method
The authors assign isospin to the compound nucleus \(^{246}\)Cm and to each fission fragment mass number using Kelson's conjectures, which state that fission fragments are preferentially formed in Isobaric Analog States. They then calculate relative yields of fission fragments for ten partitions (from symmetric Cd-Cd to asymmetric Zn-Dy) by summing the squared Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for multiple neutron-emission channels (4n, 6n, 8n for the first six partitions; 2n, 4n, 6n for the last four partitions), normalizing the calculated yields to the maximum-yield fragment in each partition for comparison with experimental data from Rochman et al.

## Key numbers
- The target \(^{245}\)Cm has isospin \(T = 26.5\); the compound nucleus \(^{246}\)Cm has unique isospin \(T_{CN} = T_{3_{CN}} = 27\).
- The average neutron multiplicity for the reaction is 3.83, as reported by Gonnenwein.
- The authors estimate at least 5–10% error in the experimental data from Rochman et al.
- The calculations use two sets of neutron-emission channels: 4n, 6n, 8n for the first six partitions and 2n, 4n, 6n for the last four partitions.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous demonstration of isospin conservation in heavy-ion induced compound nuclear fission (Garg and Jain, 2018) to thermal neutron-induced fission, testing the idea on the \(^{245}\)Cm(nth, f) reaction. It builds on Kelson's (1969) conjectures about isospin and IAS in fission, and on theoretical arguments by Lane and Soper (1962) and Sliv and Kharitonov (1965) that isospin mixing decreases with neutron excess, making isospin a nearly good quantum number in neutron-rich systems. The results enable predictions for the unmeasured heavy-mass fragment distributions and the most symmetric Cd-Cd partition, which the authors propose could be tested with modern fragment separators or gamma-ray arrays.
