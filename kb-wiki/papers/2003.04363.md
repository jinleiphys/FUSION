# Electron interaction with the environment in tunnel ionization

> The average dwell time of an electron in a potential barrier formed by an external electric field and the potential of a helium atom is evaluated within a semi classical one-dimensional tunneling approach. The tunneling electron is considered to interact with a nuclear charge screened by the electron in the helium ion. It is found that screening leads to smaller average dwell times of the tunneling electron. The dissipative effect of the environment on the tunneling electron is taken into account within a semiclassical model involving a velocity dependent frictional force and is found to change the average dwell time significantly.

# Electron interaction with the environment in tunnel ionization

## Key claim
The average dwell time of an electron tunneling from a helium atom in an external electric field is reduced when electron screening from the remaining He+ ion is included, and is significantly altered when a velocity-dependent frictional force representing environmental dissipation is added, with the sign of the friction coefficient (energy loss or gain) determining whether the dwell time increases or decreases.

## Method
The authors evaluate the average dwell time of a tunneling electron in a one-dimensional semiclassical JWKB approach, using the Smith dwell time formula applied to potentials in both spherical and parabolic coordinates. The potential includes the nuclear Coulomb term, the external electric field, the ion polarization term, and a screening correction derived from the Poisson equation for the ground-state electron density of the He+ ion. Dissipative effects are incorporated by adding a frictional force γv to the equation of motion, modifying the effective tunneling energy, and recalculating the dwell time with an effective wave number keff(η).

## Key numbers
- The ionization energy of helium at zero field is I_p(0) = 0.904 a.u., with atomic polarizability α^N = 1.38 a.u. and ion polarizability α^I = 9/32 a.u.
- The screening radius is r_0 = a_0/(2Z), where Z is the atomic number and a_0 the Bohr radius.
- For the dissipative case with screening included, a friction coefficient of γ = 0.00575 (dimensionless) produces a dwell time curve that approaches the experimental data from Alex (2014).
- The dwell time in parabolic coordinates is larger than in spherical coordinates for the same field values, with the screening correction shifting the dwell time to smaller values across the field range shown (approximately 0.05 to 0.20 a.u. for the field strength F).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical comparison of tunneling time concepts performed by Alex (2014) for helium tunnel ionization, where the Eisenbud-Wigner, Buettiker-Landauer, Larmor, and Pollack-Miller times were compared with experimental data without including screening or dissipation. The authors show that including these environmental effects changes the dwell time significantly, suggesting that the conclusions of Alex (2014) regarding which time concept best matches experiment may need revision. The semiclassical dissipative approach was previously applied to electron tunneling in solid-state junctions by the same group (Lozano-Gomez and Kelkar, 2019), and the dwell time concept has been successful in describing alpha decay half-lives and bremsstrahlung in alpha decay.
