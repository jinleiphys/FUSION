# The conical flow from quenched jets in sQGP

> Starting with a reminder of what is strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP), we proceed to recent advances in jet quenching and heavy quark diffusion, with a brief summary of various results based on AdS/CFT correspondence. The conical flow is a hydrodynamical phenomenon created by energy and entropy deposited by high energy jets propagating in matter, similar in nature to well known sonic boom from the supersonic planes. After a brief review, we discuss excitations of two hydro modes -- sound and ``diffuson'' -- which can be excited in this way. We also study expanding matter case, with a variable sped of sound, and use adiabatic invariants to show that the parameter $v/T$ ($v$ velocity in the wave, $T$ temperature) is increasing, up to a factor 3, during expansion. At the end we discuss recent results of the Princeton group which derived conical flow from AdS/CFT.

# The conical flow from quenched jets in sQGP

## Key claim

The conical flow pattern observed in two-particle correlations at RHIC is a hydrodynamical sonic boom created by energy and entropy deposited by high-energy jets propagating through strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma, and the amplitude of this sound wave can increase by up to a factor of 3 during fireball expansion due to a variable speed of sound, but the absence of a second peak in experimental data indicates the deconfining phase transition cannot be of first order.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical development of conical flow from quenched jets, beginning with the hydrodynamical description of sound and "diffuson" modes excited by energy deposition along the jet path. The author then studies the effect of an expanding fireball with a variable speed of sound using adiabatic invariants in a Robertson-Walker metric, and finally discusses the recent AdS/CFT calculation by the Princeton group (Friess et al. 2006) which solved the linearized Einstein equation for a dragged string to derive the stress tensor pattern of the conical flow from first principles.

## Key numbers

- The empirical heavy quark diffusion constant from RHIC data is in the range D_c * (2 pi T) = (1-2), while the pQCD result would be an order of magnitude larger
- The AdS/CFT result for heavy quark diffusion constant is D_HQ = 2 / (pi T sqrt(g^2 N_c)), giving D_c * (2 pi T) approximately 1
- The drag force on a heavy quark from AdS/CFT is dP/dt = - (pi T^2 sqrt(g^2 N_c) v) / (2 sqrt(1-v^2))
- The time-averaged speed of sound at RHIC is approximately 0.33, giving a Mach angle of about 70 degrees
- The ratio v/T (velocity in the wave over temperature) increases by up to a factor of 3 during fireball expansion
- The viscosity-to-entropy ratio from RHIC is eta/s = 0.1-0.2, compared to the AdS/CFT bound of 1/(4 pi)
- The AdS/CFT free energy ratio is F(g,N_c,T)/F(g=0,N_c,T) = 3/4 + O((g^2 N_c)^{-3/2}), compared to the lattice value of about 0.8

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Casalderrey-Solana, Shuryak, and Teaney (2005) that jets in sQGP should produce conical flow via sound emission, analogous to sonic booms from supersonic aircraft. The paper contrasts this hydrodynamical explanation with earlier proposals of Cerenkov gluon radiation, and discusses how the AdS/CFT calculation by Friess, Gubser, Michalogiorgakis, and Peshier (2006) confirmed the Mach cone pattern and its disappearance for subsonic jet velocities v < 1/sqrt(3), as previously predicted by Antinori and Shuryak (2005). The analysis of experimental data from STAR and PHENIX collaborations shows that the absence of a reflected wave peak rules out a first-order QCD phase transition, and the work points toward the ultimate goal of constructing a complete gravity dual to the RHIC collision process.
