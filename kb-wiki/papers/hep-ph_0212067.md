# Diffractive η_c and η_b productions by neutrinos via neutral currents

> We report a first theoretical study for neutrino-induced diffractive productions of heavy pseudoscalar mesons, η_c and η_b, off a nucleon. Based on factorization formalism for exclusive processes, we evaluate the forward diffractive production cross section in perturbative QCD in terms of the light-cone Q\bar{Q} wave functions (WFs) of η_{c,b} mesons and the gluon distribution of the nucleon. The light-cone WFs of the η_c (η_b) meson are constructed to satisfy the spin symmetry relations with those of the J/ψ(Υ) meson. The diffractive η_c production is governed by the axial-vector coupling of the longitudinally polarized Z boson to Q\bar{Q} pair, and the resulting η_c production rate is larger than the J/ψone by one order of magnitude. We also discuss the production of bottomonium η_b, which shows up for higher beam energy.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical QCD calculation for neutrino-induced diffractive production of heavy pseudoscalar mesons (η_c and η_b) via the weak neutral current, finding that the η_c production rate is larger than that for J/ψ by about a factor of 20, with the caveat that this result depends on the specific choice of light-cone wave functions and the assumption that the diffractive slope B_η_Q equals that of the corresponding vector meson.

## Method

The authors apply the leading-logarithmic-order factorization formalism for exclusive processes in perturbative QCD, computing the forward diffractive production cross section for the process ν + N → ν' + N' + η_Q (Q=c,b) mediated by Z boson exchange. They construct light-cone wave functions for the virtual Z boson from the ZQ\bar{Q} weak vertex and derive the η_Q light-cone wave functions from those of the corresponding vector mesons (J/ψ, Υ) using heavy-quark spin symmetry relations, then combine these with the unintegrated gluon distribution of the nucleon and the Cornell potential model wave functions for numerical evaluation.

## Key numbers

- η_c production rate is larger than J/ψ production rate by a factor of approximately 20, mainly due to the ratio of weak couplings (c_A/c_V)^2 ≈ 7 and the different 1/Q behavior of the axial-vector amplitude at low Q^2
- Quark masses used: m_c = 1.5 GeV, m_b = 4.9 GeV (from Cornell potential model)
- Meson masses: M_η_c = 2.98 GeV, M_η_b = 9.45 GeV, M_N = 0.94 GeV, M_Z = 91.2 GeV
- Diffractive slopes assumed: B_η_c = 4.5 GeV^{-2} (from J/ψ experimental value), B_η_b = 3.9 GeV^{-2} (from Υ value)
- Gluon distribution used: GRV95 NLO parameterization

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization approach previously developed for diffractive vector meson electroproduction (Ryskin 1993; Brodsky et al. 1994; Frankfurt et al. 1998; Ryskin et al. 1997) to the weak neutral current channel, enabling production of pseudoscalar mesons via the axial-vector coupling of the Z boson. The results suggest that neutrino-induced diffractive production could provide a new experimental method to observe η_b mesons at high-intensity neutrino facilities, complementing existing observations of η_c from J/ψ and B meson decays.
