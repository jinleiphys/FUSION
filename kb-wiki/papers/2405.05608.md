# Electromagnetic observables of open-shell nuclei from coupled-cluster theory

> We develop a new method to describe electromagnetic observables of open-shell nuclei with two nucleons outside a closed shell. This approach combines the equation-of-motion coupled-cluster method for such systems and the Lorentz integral transform technique, expanding the applicability of coupled-cluster theory for these properties beyond closed-shell nuclei. To validate this new approach, we compute the non-energy-weighted dipole sum rule and the dipole polarizability of $^{16,24}$O in both the closed-shell and the new equation-of-motion coupled-cluster frameworks, finding agreement within error bars. We then analyze the evolution of the dipole polarizability along the oxygen and calcium isotopic chains. Our predictions agree well with available experimental data and other available theoretical calculations for the closed-shell $^{16,22}$O and the open-shell $^{18}$O. In the calcium isotopes, we observe that our dipole polarizability predictions for open-shell nuclei are lower than those of closed-shell nuclei. Our predictions for $^{24}$O and $^{54,56}$Ca will motivate future experimental studies at the dripline.

## Key claim

The authors develop a new method combining the equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (EOM-CC) approach for two-particle-attached (2PA) nuclei with the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique, enabling the calculation of electromagnetic observables for open-shell nuclei with two nucleons outside a closed shell. The method is validated by showing that dipole sum rules for 16,24O computed in both the closed-shell and the new 2PA frameworks agree within error bars, but the approach is limited to nuclei that can be treated as two particles attached to a closed-(sub)shell core.

## Method

The method couples the 2PA-EOM-CC formalism, which treats states of A+2 nuclei as excited states of a closed-shell A-body neighbor, with the LIT technique to avoid explicit calculation of continuum states. The 2PA-LIT-CC equations are solved using the Lanczos algorithm, where the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian is tridiagonalized and the Lorentz integral transform is expressed as a continued fraction of Lanczos coefficients. Calculations use the ΔNNLOGO(394) chiral interaction with three-nucleon forces in the normal-ordered two-body approximation, and the electric dipole operator is similarity-transformed including up to 2p-2h excitations.

## Key numbers

* Non-energy-weighted dipole sum rule m0 for 16O: computed in both closed-shell and 2PA-LIT-CC frameworks, with agreement within error bars (model-space convergence at Nmax = 12).
* Non-energy-weighted dipole sum rule m0 for 24O: computed in both closed-shell and 2PA-LIT-CC frameworks, with agreement within error bars (model-space convergence at Nmax = 12).
* Dipole polarizability αD for 16O: computed in both frameworks, with agreement within error bars (model-space convergence at Nmax = 12).
* Dipole polarizability αD for 24O: computed in both frameworks, with agreement within error bars (model-space convergence at Nmax = 12).
* Dipole polarizability predictions for 18O: agree with available experimental data and other theoretical calculations.
* Dipole polarizability predictions for 54,56Ca: presented as predictions for future experimental studies at the dripline.

## Context

This work extends the LIT-CC method, previously limited to closed-(sub)shell nuclei (Bacca 2013, 2014; Miorelli 2016, 2018; Simonis 2019; Sobczyk 2021, 2023), to open-shell nuclei with two nucleons outside a closed shell. It enables comparison with recent calculations using symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (Burrows 2023), Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov quasiparticle RPA (Taudiere 2023), and projected generator coordinate method (Porro 2024a, 2024b), and provides predictions for 24O and 54,56Ca that can be tested by ongoing experimental campaigns at iThemba Labs, South Africa, and RCNP, Japan, as well as future FRIB upgrades.
