# $η$ and $η'$ mesons in the Dyson-Schwinger approach using a generalization of the Witten-Veneziano relation

> The description of the $η$ and $η^\prime$ mesons in the Dyson-Schwinger approach has relied on the Witten-Veneziano relation. The present paper explores the consequences of using instead its generalization recently proposed by Shore. On the examples of three different model interactions, we find that irrespective of the concrete model dynamics, our Dyson-Schwinger approach is phenomenologically more successful in conjunction with the standard Witten-Veneziano relation than with the proposed generalization valid in all orders in the $1/N_c$ expansion.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger approach to describing the $\eta$ and $\eta'$ mesons is phenomenologically more successful when combined with the standard Witten-Veneziano relation than with Shore's generalization valid to all orders in the $1/N_c$ expansion, regardless of which of three different model interactions is used.

## Method

The authors solved Dyson-Schwinger equations for dressed quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter equations for bound-state vertices in the rainbow-ladder approximation, using three different effective gluon propagator models: the Jain-Munczek renormalization-group-improved interaction, an operator-product-expansion-inspired gluon-condensate-enhanced interaction, and a separable interaction. They then incorporated the gluon anomaly contribution to the $\eta$-$\eta'$ mass matrix using both the standard Witten-Veneziano relation (with the topological susceptibility from lattice QCD) and Shore's generalized relations, and compared the resulting predictions for meson masses and decay constants.

## Key numbers

- $M_\eta = 568.2$ MeV (Jain-Munczek model with WV), $577.1$ MeV (gluon-condensate model with WV), $542.3$ MeV (separable model with WV); experimental value is $547.75 \pm 0.12$ MeV
- $M_{\eta'} = 920.4$ MeV (Jain-Munczek model with WV), $932.0$ MeV (gluon-condensate model with WV), $932.6$ MeV (separable model with WV); experimental value is $957.78 \pm 0.14$ MeV
- $\phi$ (NS-S mixing angle) = $41.42^\circ$ (Jain-Munczek), $39.56^\circ$ (gluon-condensate), $40.75^\circ$ (separable); Shore's analysis gives $38.24^\circ$
- $\chi_{\rm YM} = (175.7 \pm 1.5\ {\rm MeV})^4$, the weighted average of lattice results used for the Witten-Veneziano relation
- $f_\pi = 93.1$ MeV (Jain-Munczek), $92.9$ MeV (gluon-condensate), $92.0$ MeV (separable); experimental value is $92.4 \pm 0.3$ MeV
- $f_K = 113.4$ MeV (Jain-Munczek), $111.5$ MeV (gluon-condensate), $110.1$ MeV (separable); experimental value is $113.0 \pm 1.0$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Dyson-Schwinger studies of the $\eta$-$\eta'$ complex (Kekez 2000, Kekez 2005, Horvatic 2007) that relied on the standard Witten-Veneziano relation (Witten 1979, Veneziano 1979) to determine the anomalous $\eta_0$ mass shift from lattice topological susceptibility. It tests the generalization recently proposed by Shore (2006, 2007) that is valid to all orders in $1/N_c$, finding that the standard relation gives better phenomenological agreement. The results enable future finite-temperature calculations where simple modeling of the $\eta$-$\eta'$ system is desirable.
