# Beyond collinearity in the N(e,e'q) process

> We discuss a few examples of structure functions for polarized, semi-inclusive scattering processes to show the richness of structure. Then we indicate how polarization and particle production can be used to study the quark and gluon structure of hadrons going further than the well-known parton densities and fragmentation functions. We also emphasize how single spin asymmetries in leptoproduction may shed light on explanations for single spin asymmetries in pion production in pp collissions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that semi-inclusive leptoproduction, particularly 1-particle inclusive scattering, can access a much richer set of quark and gluon structure functions beyond the standard collinear parton densities and fragmentation functions, including transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) and T-odd functions, but the actual extraction of these functions requires careful disentangling of initial-state (Sivers) and final-state (Collins) effects in single-spin asymmetries.

## Method

The author performs a field-theoretical analysis of the hadronic tensor for 1-particle inclusive leptoproduction, decomposing it into structure functions that depend on the azimuthal angle between the lepton scattering plane and the hadron production plane. Soft parts (quark correlation functions for distributions and fragmentation) are parametrized in terms of Dirac projections, yielding a complete classification of leading-twist (twist-2) and subleading-twist (twist-3) distribution and fragmentation functions, including T-even and T-odd sectors. Cross sections are then computed at leading order in 1/Q and at order 1/Q to identify which combinations of these functions produce specific azimuthal asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized cross section for semi-inclusive scattering is given by \(d\sigma_{OO}/(d\xbj\,dy\,dz_h) = (2\pi \alpha^2 s/Q^4) \sum_{a,\bar a} e_a^2 [1+(1-y)^2] \xbj f_1^a(\xbj) D_1^a(z_h)\), where \(\xbj = Q^2/(2P\cdot q)\) and \(z_h = P\cdot P_h/(P\cdot q)\).
- The \(\cos(2\phi_h^\ell)\) asymmetry for unpolarized targets at leading twist is \(\langle (Q_T^2/(M M_h)) \cos(2\phi_h^\ell) \rangle_{OO} = (4\pi \alpha^2 s/Q^4) 4(1-y) \sum_{a,\bar a} e_a^2 \xbj h_1^{\perp(1)a}(\xbj) H_1^{\perp(1)a}(z_h)\).
- The single-spin asymmetry for transversely polarized targets involving the Sivers effect is \(\langle (Q_T/M_h) \sin(\phi_h^\ell - \phi_S^\ell) \rangle_{OTO} = (2\pi \alpha^2 s/Q^4) |\bm S_T| (1-y - y^2/2) \sum_{a,\bar a} e_a^2 \xbj f_{1T}^{\perp(1)a}(\xbj) D_1^a(z_h)\).
- The single-spin asymmetry for transversely polarized targets involving the Collins effect is \(\langle (Q_T/M_h) \sin(\phi_h^\ell + \phi_S^\ell) \rangle_{OTO} = (4\pi \alpha^2 s/Q^4) |\bm S_T| (1-y) \sum_{a,\bar a} e_a^2 \xbj h_1^a(\xbj) H_1^{\perp(1)a}(z_h)\).

## Context

This work extends the standard parton model framework of Feynman (1972) and the operator product expansion approach of Soper (1977) and Jaffe (1983) by systematically including transverse momentum dependence and T-odd functions in both distribution and fragmentation sectors. It builds on the classification of twist-2 and twist-3 functions by Jaffe and Ji (1992) and the identification of T-odd distribution functions by Sivers (1990) and fragmentation functions by Collins (1993). The paper enables experimental programs at facilities like HERMES, COMPASS, and Jefferson Lab to extract TMD parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions from azimuthal asymmetries, and provides a method to distinguish between the Sivers and Collins mechanisms for single-spin asymmetries observed in pp collisions by Anselmino (1995, 1999).
