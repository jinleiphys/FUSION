# Constraints on the fermionic dark matter from observations of neutron stars

> We study an impact of asymmetric fermionic dark matter on neutron star properties, including tidal deformability, mass, radius, etc. We present the conditions at which dark matter particles tend to form a compact structure in a core of the star or create an extended halo around it. We show that compact core of dark matter leads to a decrease of the total gravitational mass and tidal deformability compared to a pure baryonic star, while presence of a dark matter halo increases those observable quantities. By imposing an existing astrophysical and gravitational wave constraints set by LIGO/Virgo Collaboration together with the recent results on the spatial distribution of dark matter in the Milky Way we determine a new upper limit on the mass and fraction of dark matter particles inside compact stars. Furthermore, we show that the formation of an extended halo around a NS is incompatible with the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the presence of an extended dark matter halo around a neutron star is incompatible with the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint, and it derives a new upper limit on the mass of fermionic dark matter particles of 60 GeV, but only under the specific assumption that the dark matter fraction accumulated in the most central region of the Galaxy is as estimated.

## Method

The authors model dark matter as a relativistic Fermi gas of non-interacting spin-1/2 particles with masses varied from 100 MeV to 200 GeV, and baryonic matter using the Induced Surface Tension (IST) equation of state. They solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for neutron stars admixed with dark matter, treating each component as a perfect fluid interacting only through gravity. The resulting mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities are then compared against the maximum mass constraints from the pulsars PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620, and the tidal deformability bound from GW170817.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of a pure baryonic neutron star using the IST EoS: 2.08 solar masses, with radius for a 1.4 solar mass star of 11.37 km.
- For dark matter particles of mass 1 GeV and fractions of 3.3% and 6.0%, the DM-admixed stars form a compact core and are more compact than the pure baryonic case.
- For light dark matter particles of mass 100 MeV, the DM halo radius reaches 9.4 km for a fraction of 0.3%, and 135.2 km for a fraction of 3.0%.
- Light dark matter particles with mass 0.1 GeV lead to disagreement with the LIGO/Virgo tidal deformability constraint at fractions greater than about 0.5%.
- For dark matter particle mass 0.174 GeV, the lowest value of the maximum mass equals exactly 2 solar masses, making this case consistent with the upper mass constraint for any fraction.
- The combined analysis yields an upper constraint on the mass of fermionic dark matter particles of 60 GeV, based on the estimated dark matter fraction in the central Galaxy.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter effects on neutron stars, such as those by Ellis et al. (2018) and others, which showed that light dark matter particles tend to form halos while heavy particles form cores. The paper uses the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint from LIGO/Virgo and the mass measurements of the heaviest pulsars PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620 to place new limits on dark matter particle mass and fraction. The results enable future measurements of a 2 solar mass neutron star in the Galactic center to further tighten the upper bound on dark matter mass.
