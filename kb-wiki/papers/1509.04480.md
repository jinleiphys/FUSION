# Beta-decay study within multi-reference density functional theory and beyond

> Pioneering study of Gamow-Teller (GT) and Fermi matrix elements (MEs) using no-core-configuration-interaction formalism rooted in multi-reference density functional theory is presented. After successful test performed for 6He -> 6Li beta-decay, the model is applied to compute MEs in the sd- and pf-shell T=1/2 mirror nuclei. The calculated GT MEs and the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to the Fermi branch are found to be in a very good agreement with shell-model predictions in spite of fundamental differences between these models concerning model space, treatment of correlations or inclusion of a core. This result indirectly supports the two-body current based scenarios behind the quenching of axial-vector coupling constant.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first application of a no-core-configuration-interaction (NCCI) formalism rooted in multi-reference density functional theory (MR-DFT) to compute Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements for beta decay; the calculated matrix elements systematically overestimate experimental data when using the free-neutron axial coupling constant, but the level of disagreement is very similar to that of large-scale nuclear shell model calculations, indirectly supporting two-body current scenarios for the quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant.

## Method

The authors use a three-step NCCI approach: first, self-consistent Hartree-Fock (multi)particle-(multi)hole configurations are computed using Skyrme interactions; second, angular momentum and isospin projections are applied to generate states with good quantum numbers; third, these projected states are mixed by solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation. The method is tested on the 6He to 6Li beta decay and then applied to ground-state-to-ground-state transitions in sd-shell and lower pf-shell T=1/2 mirror nuclei from A=17 to A=55, using the SV and SV_SO Skyrme energy density functionals with N=10 or 12 spherical harmonic oscillator shells.

## Key numbers

* For the 6He to 6Li beta decay, the experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element is |M_GT| = 2.1645(43) (assuming g_A = -1.2701(25)); the NCCI calculation yields 2.238, approximately 3% above experiment.
* The SV_SO functional reproduces binding energies of T=1/2 mirror nuclei at the level of ±1% relative to experiment.
* In the sd-shell (excluding A=31-35), linear fits give quenching factors q = 0.77 for MR-DFT and q = 0.78 for NCCI; in the lower pf-shell, q = 0.75 for MR-DFT and q = 0.69 for NCCI.
* For A=39, inclusion of all possible particle-hole excitations within the sd-shell exhausts 99% of the Ikeda sum rule.
* Isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to the Fermi branch range from 0.166(17)% (A=17) to 0.907(91)% (A=37) in the NCCI calculations.

## Context

This work extends the MR-DFT-based NCCI formalism, previously developed by the same group (Satula 2014, 2015), to beta-decay matrix elements for the first time. The results are compared with large-scale nuclear shell model calculations by Brown (1985) for the sd-shell and by Martinez-Pinedo (1996) and Sekine (1987) for the pf-shell; the agreement in Gamow-Teller matrix elements and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections is very good despite fundamental differences in model space and treatment of correlations. The findings support the interpretation that the quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant arises from two-body currents rather than missing correlations, and the calculated isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections are relevant for tests of the conserved vector current hypothesis and determination of the CKM matrix element V_ud via mirror beta decays.
