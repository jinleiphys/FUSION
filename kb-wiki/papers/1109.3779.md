# Constraining off-shell effects using low-energy Compton scattering

> Off-shell effects in proton electromagnetic vertices can be constrained from their effects on known processes. In particular, parameters in models for the off-shell effects can be determined by fitting to the proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities measured in low-energy Compton scattering. There has been recent speculation that off-shell effects contribute enough energy to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift to explain the discrepancy between muonic and electronic measurements of the proton radius. We find that the constraints discussed here make the off-shell effects about two orders of magnitude smaller than needed for this purpose.

## Key claim

Off-shell effects in the proton electromagnetic vertex, when constrained by the measured proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities from low-energy Compton scattering, are about two orders of magnitude smaller than the 310 micro-eV needed to explain the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift discrepancy, contrary to the speculation of Miller et al. (2011).

## Method

The authors computed the off-shell corrections to the forward Compton scattering amplitudes T1 and T2 for three specific off-shell vertex models (O_a, O_b, O_c) proposed by Miller et al. (2011). They matched the Q^2-dependent terms from these off-shell corrections to the known low-energy expansion of the non-Born Compton amplitudes, which are expressed in terms of the electric polarizability alpha_E and magnetic polarizability beta_M. This matching determined the value of the off-shell parameter lambda/b^2 for each model from the polarizability data.

## Key numbers

- The electric plus magnetic polarizability sum used is alpha_E + beta_M = 0.00139(4) fm^3, from Drechsel et al. (2002) or Schumacher (2005).
- The magnetic polarizability used is beta_M = 0.00034(12) fm^3, from Beane et al. (2004).
- For model O_a, the polarizability-constrained value of lambda/b^2 is approximately -5.8 GeV^{-2}, which is 55 times smaller than the value of about 320 GeV^{-2} needed to explain the proton radius discrepancy.
- For model O_b, the polarizability-constrained value is approximately -2.9 GeV^{-2}, which is 67 times smaller than the needed value.
- For model O_c, the polarizability-constrained value is approximately +1.4 GeV^{-2}, which is 340 times smaller than the needed value.
- The dispersive calculation of the two-photon exchange energy correction on the 2S level in muonic hydrogen yields Delta E = -36.9 +/- 2.4 micro-eV, from Carlson et al. (2011).

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of the two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift, which was fully calculated by Pachucki (1999) and updated by Carlson et al. (2011), and contradicts the claim by Miller et al. (2011) that off-shell effects could explain the proton radius discrepancy between muonic hydrogen measurements (Pohl, 2010) and electronic experiments (Bernauer, 2010; Zhan, 2011; Mohr, 2008). The results enable future model builders to constrain off-shell nucleon parameters using low-energy Compton scattering data, and show that the dispersive method already provides a complete and correct result without needing separate off-shell contributions.
