# Axions in Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> I give an overview of recent developments in the study of the coupling of the QCD axion to nucleons and other octet baryons. I demonstrate how axions can be included into heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, and present recent numerical results for the several axion-baryon couplings in SU(2) and SU(3) chiral perturbation theory for the Kim--Shifman--Vainstein--Zakharov (KSVZ) and the Dine--Fischler--Srednicki--Zhitnitsky (DFSZ) axion model.

# Axions in Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim

The QCD axion couples to nucleons and hyperons with comparable strength at leading order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, meaning that hyperons should be included in studies of neutron star cooling via axion bremsstrahlung. However, the numerical results for the axion-baryon couplings at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in SU(3) suffer from poorly known low-energy constants, particularly the isoscalar ones.

## Method

The author includes the QCD axion into heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) by constructing the external axial-vector fields \(a_\mu\) and \(a_{\mu,i}^{(s)}\) from the axion-quark interaction Lagrangian, separating the SU(2) and SU(3) flavor subspaces. The axion-baryon coupling is then computed at leading order (LO) for all octet baryons in SU(3) HBCHPT, and up to NNLO (including one-pion loop contributions and \(1/m_N\) corrections) for nucleons in SU(2) HBCHPT, with the low-energy constants matched to nucleon matrix elements \(\Delta q\) from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- Axion decay constant window: \(10^9\,\text{GeV} \lesssim f_a \lesssim 10^{12}\,\text{GeV}\)
- Neutron electric dipole moment bound: \(|d_n^\text{exp}| < 1.8\times 10^{-26}\, e\,\text{cm}\) (90% C.L.), implying \(\bar{\theta} \lesssim 10^{-11}\)
- Leading-order KSVZ axion-proton coupling: \(g^{(1)}_{app} = -0.430(36)\)
- Leading-order KSVZ axion-neutron coupling: \(g^{(1)}_{ann} = -0.002(30)\)
- Leading-order KSVZ axion-\(\Sigma^+\) coupling: \(g^{(1)}_{a\Sigma^+\Sigma^+} = -0.543(34)\)
- Leading-order KSVZ axion-\(\Xi^0\) coupling: \(g^{(1)}_{a\Xi^0\Xi^0} = 0.267(23)\)
- Leading-order KSVZ axion-\(\Lambda\) coupling: \(g^{(1)}_{a\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.126(25)\)
- Leading-order DFSZ axion-proton coupling: \(g^{(1),\text{DFSZ}}_{app} = -0.577(38) + 0.430(21)\sin^2\beta\)
- Leading-order DFSZ axion-neutron coupling: \(g^{(1),\text{DFSZ}}_{ann} = 0.269(34) - 0.406(21)\sin^2\beta\)
- Quark mass ratios used: \(z = m_u/m_d = 0.485(19)\), \(w = m_u/m_s = 0.025(1)\)
- Nucleon spin fractions from Aoki (2021): \(\Delta u = 0.847(50)\), \(\Delta d = -0.407(34)\), \(\Delta s = -0.035(13)\)

## Context

This work extends the treatment of axion-nucleon couplings in chiral perturbation theory from earlier studies by Kaplan (1985), Srednicki (1985), Georgi (1986), and GrillidiCortona (2015) to include all octet baryons in SU(3) HBCHPT, as developed in Vonk (2020, 2021). The results enable more precise calculations of axion emission from neutron stars, where hyperons may be present (Tolos, 2020), and improve the determination of the traditional axion window from stellar cooling constraints. Future work is needed to determine the unknown low-energy constants that appear at NNLO in the SU(3) case.
