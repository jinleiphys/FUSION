# Initial conditions in AA and pA collisions

> A full understanding of the spacetime evolution of the QCD matter created in a heavy ion collision requires understanding the properties of the initial stages. In the weak coupling picture these are dominated by classical gluon fields, whose properties can also be studied via the scattering of dilute probes off a high energy hadron or nucleus. A particular challenge is understanding small systems, where LHC data is also showing signs of collective behavior. We discuss some recent results of on the initial matter production and thermalization in heavy ion collisions, in particular in the gluon saturation framework.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory provides a consistent weak-coupling description of the initial stages of heavy ion collisions, where the matter production is dominated by classical gluon fields; however, the relevance of this saturation picture for a particular kinematic regime "can always be debated" and "cannot be resolved within the (self consistent) classical approximation alone," though it becomes better justified as collision energy increases.

## Method

The paper reviews calculations of the glasma initial state using Classical Yang-Mills (CYM) simulations starting from Wilson lines describing the color fields of colliding nuclei, with the initial condition at proper time τ=0 given by the sum of transverse pure gauge fields from each nucleus plus a commutator term. It then discusses how the same Wilson line correlators can be probed in dilute-dense control experiments (deep inelastic scattering and forward proton-nucleus collisions) using the dipole picture, and reviews recent progress toward next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy in these calculations. Finally, it examines long-range rapidity correlations in small collision systems, comparing different approximation schemes for computing four-Wilson-line correlators that generate azimuthal anisotropies.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_s is defined via the condition (1/N_c) ⟨Tr V^†(0_T) V(x_T)⟩ = e^{-1/2}, which gives x_T^2 = 2/Q_s^2
- For large momenta p_T ≫ Q_s, the glasma gluon spectrum reduces to a perturbatively calculable form; for small momenta p_T ≪ Q_s, it collapses to a universal form close to a classical thermal distribution ∼ 1/p_T
- The correlation from color field domains in small systems is suppressed by a factor 1/(S_⊥ Q_s^2 N_c^2) compared to uncorrelated particle production
- The "nonlinear Gaussian" approximation for four-Wilson-line correlators was found accurate within at least 10% compared to full JIMWLK results
- The "Glasma graph" calculation linearized in ρ differs from the full JIMWLK result by at most a factor of 2 for the coefficient v_2

## Context

This review extends the CGC framework for initial conditions in heavy ion collisions, building on the classical field calculations of Kovner (1995) and the glasma picture of Lappi (2006). It discusses recent NLO developments including the resummation of large transverse momentum logarithms in the BK equation by Iancu (2015) and the negativity problem in NLO forward particle production cross sections identified by Stasto (2013). The work enables quantitative connections between HERA deep inelastic scattering data, future Electron-Ion Collider measurements, and the initial state description used in heavy ion phenomenology, while identifying the need to understand the interplay between initial-state color field correlations and final-state hydrodynamic correlations in small collision systems.
