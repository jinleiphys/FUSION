# Long-range correlation studies at the SPS energies in MC model with string fusion

> Studies of the ultrarelativistic collisions of hadrons and nuclei at different centrality and energy enable to explore the QCD phase diagram in a wide range of temperature and baryon density. Long-range correlation studies are considered as a tool, sensitive to the observation of phase transition and the critical point. In the present work, a Monte Carlo model of proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions is applied to heavy and light ion collisions at the cms energy range from a few up to several hundred GeV per nucleon. The model describes the nuclear collisions at the partonic level through interaction of color dipoles and takes into account the effects of string fusion, which can be considered as an alternative to relativistic hydrodynamics way of describing the collective phenomena in heavy-ion collisions. The implementing of both the string fusion and the finite rapidity length of strings allowed to consider the particle production at non-zero baryochemical potential. We calculated the long-range correlation functions and correlation coefficients between multiplicities and transverse momentum at several energies for different colliding systems and obtained predictions for the experiment.

# Long-range correlation studies at the SPS energies in MC model with string fusion

## Key claim

The string fusion model, when applied at non-zero baryochemical potential, reproduces a non-monotonic energy dependence of the pt-pt correlation coefficient in Ar+Ca collisions that suggests a phase transition, but only in the middle-forward rapidity configuration (probing higher baryon density), while midrapidity configurations show a smooth monotonic dependence.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte Carlo model of proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the partonic level through interaction of color dipoles, without referring to the Glauber picture of independent nucleon collisions. Strings are formed between interacting partons, and when their centers occupy the same cell on a transverse lattice (with cell area equal to the string transverse area), they fuse according to local string fusion rules: mean charged multiplicity per cell scales as sqrt(k) and mean transverse momentum as the fourth root of k, where k is the number of overlapping strings. The model accounts for finite rapidity length of strings, allowing particle production at non-zero baryochemical potential, and uses valence string asymmetry to describe net baryon distributions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear density parameters used: Woods-Saxon radius R=6.63 fm and diffuseness d=0.545 fm for 208Pb; R=6.38 fm, d=0.535 fm for 197Au; R=3.53 fm, d=0.542 fm for 40Ca and 40Ar; harmonic oscillator parameters r0=1.77 fm, a=0.327 for 7Be; r0=1.791 fm, a=0.611 for 9Be
- Collision energies studied: sqrt(s) from 5 GeV to 62.4 GeV per nucleon
- Three rapidity window configurations used: (-1;0)-(0;1), (0;1)-(1;2), and (0;1)-(2;3)
- For heavier species (Ar+Ca, Au+Au, Pb+Pb), central events defined as Npart > (A+B)/2 and peripheral as Npart <= (A+B)/2
- In Ar+Ca collisions, the pt-pt correlation coefficient shows a non-monotonic dependence with a minimum around sqrt(s)=17 GeV in the middle-forward rapidity configuration
- The dip in pt-pt correlation for peripheral Ar+Ca collisions is found at higher energy than in central collisions

## Context

This work extends the string fusion approach, previously applied to Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (VK1, VK2) and to pp collisions (YF1PoS2), to a broader range of systems and energies relevant to the NA61 experiment at SPS, the BESII program at RHIC, and future detectors at FAIR and NICA. The non-monotonic behavior of pt-pt correlations in Ar+Ca collisions parallels experimental observations of deviations from Poisson of net-proton fluctuations at RHIC in the 10-20 GeV energy range, which have been attributed to the existence of a phase transition and critical point on the QCD phase diagram. The results highlight the importance of the Ar+Ca run in the beam energy and system size scan by NA61 scheduled for 2015, and enable future studies of strongly intensive variables and net-charge and net-proton fluctuations.
