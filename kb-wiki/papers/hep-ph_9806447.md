# Master Formulae Approach to Photon Fusion Reactions

> We analyze the $γγ\to ππ, KK, ηη, πη$ reactions through $\sqrt{s}=2$ GeV, using the master formula approach to QCD with three flavors. In this approach, the constraints of broken chiral symmetry, unitarity and crossing symmetry are manifest in all channels. The pertinent vacuum correlators are analyzed at tree level using straightforward resonance saturation methods. A one-loop chiral power counting analysis at treshold is also carried out and compared to standard chiral perturbation theory. Our results are in overall agreement with the existing data in all channels. We predict the strange meson polarizabilities and a very small cross section for $γγ\toηη$.

# Master Formulae Approach to Photon Fusion Reactions

## Key claim

The master formula approach to QCD with three flavors, which enforces broken chiral symmetry, unitarity, and crossing symmetry, provides a global description of photon fusion reactions to two mesons through √s = 2 GeV, with the specific prediction that the cross section for γγ → ηη is very small due to a near cancellation between scalar-isoscalar and scalar-isotriplet contributions in the 1.25–1.5 GeV range.

## Method

The authors extended the master formula approach from two flavors to three flavors, deriving identities that relate the fusion reaction amplitudes to vacuum correlators of vector currents (VV), axial currents (jAjA), and scalar densities (σ), modulo Born terms. They analyzed these correlators at tree level using resonance saturation, saturating vector currents by ρ, ω, φ mesons, axial-vector currents by A1 and K1 mesons, and scalar channels by f0(500), f0(980), f2(1270) for isoscalar and a0(980), a2(1320) for isotriplet channels, with masses and widths fixed at Particle Data Group values. A one-loop chiral power counting analysis at threshold was also performed and compared to standard chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- fπ = 93 MeV, fK = 115 MeV, fη = 123 MeV (meson weak-decay constants)
- Current quark masses: m̂ = 9 MeV, ms = 175 MeV at some running scale
- Charged pion polarizability from one-loop analysis: αLπ± ≈ 4.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutral pion polarizability from one-loop analysis: αLπ⁰ ≈ 6.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Charged kaon polarizability: αLK± ≈ −2.7 × 10⁻⁵ fm³
- Neutral kaon polarizability: αLK⁰K̄⁰ ≈ +2.8 × 10⁻⁵ fm³
- Eta polarizability: αLη ≈ −4.4 × 10⁻⁶ fm³
- Five dimensionless coupling parameters used in global fit: c₀ = −98208, c₃ = 5c₀, g₁ = 0.9744, g₂ = −13.64, g₃ = −1.5
- The γγ → ηη cross section is predicted to be about four orders of magnitude smaller than other fusion reactions

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor master formula approach of Yamagishi and Zahed (1994) to three flavors, confirming and extending the earlier analysis of Lee and Zahed (1995). It provides a unified alternative to one-loop chiral perturbation theory (Donoghue, 1993), which does well in charged channels but fails in chargeless sectors, and to two-loop calculations that use resonance saturation to fix counterterms. The results are in overall agreement with an early dispersion analysis for γγ → π⁰π⁰ and with coupled-channel analyses by Oller and Oset. The approach enables predictions for strange meson polarizabilities and for the electromagnetic emission rates from a hadronic gas in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
