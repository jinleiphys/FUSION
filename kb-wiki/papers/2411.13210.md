# Novel features of asymmetric nuclear matter from terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations of neutron stars

> The accurate measurement of neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb by the PREX Collaboration suggests a large value of the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter, $L$, whereas the smaller $L$ is preferred to account for the small neutron-star radii from NICER observations. To resolve this discrepancy between nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations, new effective interactions have been developed using relativistic mean-field models with the isoscalar- and isovector-meson mixing. We investigate the effects of $δ$-nucleon coupling and $σ$--$δ$ mixing on the ground-state properties of finite nuclei, as well as the characteristics of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron stars. Additionally, we explore the role of the quartic $ρ$-meson self-interaction in dense nuclear matter to mitigate the stiff equation of state for neutron stars resulting from the large $δ$-nucleon coupling. It is found that the nuclear symmetry energy undergoes a sudden softening at approximately twice the saturation density of nuclear matter, taking into account the PREX-2 result, the recent NICER observation of PSR J0437$-$4715, and the binary neutron star merger, GW170817.

## Key claim

The authors develop new relativistic mean-field effective interactions incorporating isoscalar- and isovector-meson mixing (σ²δ² and ω²ρ²) to resolve the apparent tension between the large symmetry energy slope parameter L inferred from the PREX-2 neutron skin measurement of ²⁰⁸Pb and the smaller L preferred by NICER neutron-star radius observations, finding that the nuclear symmetry energy undergoes a sudden softening at approximately twice saturation density.

## Method

The authors construct relativistic mean-field (RMF) models including σ, ω, δ, and ρ mesons with nonlinear self-interactions and mixing terms (σ²δ², ω²ρ², and quartic δ and ρ self-interactions). They solve the Dirac equation for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for mesons in the mean-field approximation, first for spherical finite nuclei to compute ground-state properties and neutron skin thicknesses, then for infinite nuclear matter to obtain the equation of state. The models are constrained by nuclear saturation properties, heavy-ion collision flow data, the PREX-2 and CREX neutron skin measurements, the mass of PSR J0740+6620 (2.072 M⊙), the tidal deformability from GW170817, and NICER radius observations of PSR J0437−4715.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb from PREX Collaboration: the large value implies L = 106 ± 37 MeV (Reed 2021), while modern density functionals predict L = 54 ± 8 MeV (Reinhard 2021)
- Observed mass of PSR J0740+6620: M = 2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} M⊙
- Dimensionless tidal deformability from GW170817: Λ₁.₄ = 190^{+390}_{-120}
- Current Bayesian estimate of L from NICER and GW170817 within chiral effective field theory: L = (43.7–70.0) MeV (Lim 2023)
- The nuclear symmetry energy softens at approximately twice the nuclear saturation density (ρ₀)

## Context

This work extends the RMF framework of Serot (1984) and Boguta (1977) by incorporating δ-meson coupling and σ–δ mixing, which Zabari (2018) and Miyatsu (2022, 2023) have shown to affect neutron skin thickness and neutron-star radii. The study addresses the tension between the PREX-2 result (Reed 2021) and NICER observations (Miller 2019, 2021; Riley 2019, 2021), as well as the difficulty of simultaneously reconciling PREX-2 and CREX results (CREX 2022). The resulting equations of state enable improved modeling of neutron-star structure and binary merger events such as GW170817.
