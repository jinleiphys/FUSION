# New contribution to WIMP-nucleus scattering

> A weakly-interacting massive particle (WIMP) is perhaps the most promising candidate for the dark matter in the Galactic halo. The WIMP detection rate in laboratory searches is fixed by the cross section for elastic WIMP-nucleus scattering. Here we calculate the contribution to this cross section from two-nucleon currents from pion exchange in the nucleus, and show that it may in some cases be comparable to the one-nucleon current that has been considered in prior work, and perhaps help resolve the discrepancies between the various direct dark-matter search experiments. We provide simple expressions that allow these new contributions to be included in current calculations.

# New contribution to WIMP-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
The two-nucleon current contribution to WIMP-nucleus elastic scattering, arising from pion exchange in the nucleus, may be comparable to the one-nucleon current traditionally considered, but only in certain cases, specifically when cancellations occur among the scalar coupling constants in the MSSM parameter space, making the ratio of two-nucleon to one-nucleon amplitudes potentially of order unity or larger.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective field theory for WIMP-hadron interactions starting from a general WIMP-quark Lagrangian, organized operators by chiral power counting in the small momentum transfer q ~ 100 MeV, and derived the leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) two-nucleon currents from pion-exchange diagrams. They then specialized to the neutralino in the MSSM, evaluated the two-nucleon scattering amplitude for the scalar-scalar interaction channel, and estimated the nuclear matrix element in the independent particle approximation using square-well and harmonic-oscillator potentials with Jastrow correlation functions.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of two-nucleon to one-nucleon scattering amplitudes is given by (0.21 ± 0.08) × r × (𝒩₀^{ππ}/A), where the uncertainty comes from current-quark masses.
- For the special case where all S_q are equal, the factor (0.21 ± 0.08)r lies between 0.002 and 0.006, using m_s = 115 ± 55 MeV.
- The nuclear matrix element ratio 𝒩₀^{ππ}/A is estimated to be approximately 1.5 ± 0.5, with a minimum of ~1 (harmonic oscillator with Jastrow functions) and a maximum of ~2 (square well with 0.5 fm cut-off).
- The pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV and the axial coupling g_A = 1.27 are used in the matching.
- The scalar nucleon matrix element ε_s⁰ = ⟨N|ūu + d̄d|N⟩ ≈ 16 ± 8, and ε_sˢ ≈ 0.1 ε_s⁰.

## Context
This work extends the standard treatment of WIMP-nucleus scattering, which previously considered only one-nucleon currents (Kurylov 2003), by adding two-nucleon currents from pion exchange. The authors show that these exchange currents can be 10–60% of the one-nucleon amplitude, larger than the typical 5–10% seen in processes like p+n→d+γ (Riska 1989) and ν+d→ν+p+n (Butler 1999). This result may help reconcile discrepancies between direct dark-matter search experiments such as DAMA and others, because the two-nucleon contribution varies with nuclear composition and could cancel against the one-nucleon term differently for different target nuclei.
