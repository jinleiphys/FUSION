# Extraction of baryonia from the lightest anti-protonic atoms

> Anti-protonic hydrogen and helium atoms are analyzed. Level shifts and width are expressed in terms of $\bar{p}$-nucleon sub-threshold scattering lengths and volumes. Experimental data are compared to results obtained from the 2009 version of the Paris $N\bar{N}$ interaction potential. Comparison with 1999 version is also made. Effects of $N\bar{N}$ quasi-bound states are discussed. Atomic 2P hyperfine structure is calculated for antiprotonic deuterium and the significance of new measurements is indicated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimental level shifts and widths of antiprotonic deuterium and helium atoms can be used to constrain the energy and width of predicted \(N\bar{N}\) quasibound states (baryonia), specifically the \(^{33}P_1\) state, whose position is not well determined by scattering data alone and differs between the Paris 1999 and Paris 2009 potential models.

## Method

The authors calculated the complex level shifts (\(\Delta E - i\Gamma/2\)) for the \(2P\) and \(3D\) states of antiprotonic deuterium, \(^3\)He, and \(^4\)He using a quasi-three-body formalism. The shifts were expressed in terms of subthreshold \(S\)-wave scattering lengths and \(P\)-wave scattering volumes derived from the Paris 1999 and Paris 2009 \(N\bar{N}\) interaction potentials, averaged over the recoil energy of the \(\bar{p}N\) pair. The results were compared to existing experimental data from x-ray measurements.

## Key numbers

*   For antiprotonic deuterium \(2P\) level: the sum of \(S\)- and \(P\)-wave contributions from the Paris 2009 potential gives a shift of 91 meV and a width of 575 meV, compared to the experimental data of \(243 \pm 26\) meV (shift) and \(489 \pm 30\) meV (width).
*   For antiprotonic deuterium \(2P\) level: the sum from the Paris 1999 potential gives a shift of 171 meV and a width of 341 meV.
*   For antiprotonic \(^3\)He \(2P\) level: the sum from the Paris 2009 potential gives a shift of 0.31 eV and a width of 32.5 eV, compared to the experimental data of \(17 \pm 4\) eV (shift) and \(25 \pm 9\) eV (width).
*   For antiprotonic \(^3\)He \(3D\) level: the sum from the Paris 2009 potential gives a width of 2.15 meV, compared to the experimental data of \(2.14 \pm 0.18\) meV.
*   The Paris 2009 potential predicts a \(^{33}P_1\) quasibound state at energy \(E_B = -4.5\) MeV with a half-width \(\Gamma/2 = 9\) MeV, while the Paris 1999 potential predicts it at \(E_B = -17\) MeV with \(\Gamma/2 = 6.5\) MeV.
*   The Paris 2009 potential predicts an \(^{11}S_0\) quasibound state at energy \(E = -4.8\) MeV and width 52 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of antiprotonic atoms beyond hydrogen, where data were previously used to fix the Paris 2009 potential (Loiseau and Wycech, 2009). It directly compares the predictions of the Paris 1999 and Paris 2009 potentials against experimental data for deuterium (Gotta, 2003; 1999) and helium (Schneider, 1991). The results indicate that the \(^{33}P_1\) quasibound state position in the Paris 2009 model is too close to the \(\bar{p}N\) c.m. energy in deuterium, leading to an unacceptable shift, and that the experimental data suggest its true position lies between the two Paris model predictions. This work enables better constraints on the \(N\bar{N}\) potential, which is important for interpreting future experiments like the PUMA project at CERN, which aims to study antiproton capture on unstable nuclei.
