# Spectral function for overoccupied gluodynamics from real-time lattice simulations

> We study the spectral properties of a highly occupied non-Abelian non-equilibrium plasma appearing ubiquitously in weak coupling descriptions of QCD matter. The spectral function of this far-from-equilibrium plasma is measured by employing linear response theory in classical-statistical real-time lattice Yang-Mills simulations. We establish the existence of transversely and longitudinally polarized quasiparticles and obtain their dispersion relations, effective mass, plasmon frequency, damping rate and further structures in the spectral and statistical functions. Our new method can be interpreted as a non-perturbative generalization of hard thermal loop (HTL) effective theory. We see indications that our results approach leading order HTL in the appropriate limit. The method can also be employed beyond the range of validity of HTL.

## Key claim

The authors establish that classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations can be used as a non-perturbative generalization of hard thermal loop (HTL) effective theory, and they show that their results approach leading-order HTL in the appropriate limit of large scale separation, while the method can also be employed beyond the range of validity of HTL.

## Method

The authors perform classical-statistical real-time lattice Yang-Mills simulations for SU(2) gauge theory in temporal gauge, initialized with an isotropic overoccupied distribution. They compute the spectral function by applying linear response theory: a small perturbation is introduced and the retarded propagator is extracted from the response, which directly gives the spectral function for positive time differences. The statistical correlation function is obtained from classical field averages, and both are Fourier transformed from relative time to frequency space at fixed central time.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic mass at time Q t = 1500 is m_HTL = 0.149 Q, with a spread of about 5 percent depending on the definition of the distribution function used.
* The plasmon frequency in the HTL framework is given by omega_pl^HTL = sqrt(2/3) m.
* The transverse sum rule gives dot_rho_T(Delta t = 0, p) = 1, while the longitudinal sum rule gives dot_rho_L(Delta t = 0, p) = 2m^2 / (2m^2 + p^2).
* The scale separation in the self-similar regime evolves as m / Lambda ~ (Q t)^{-2/7}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quasiparticle dispersion relations in overoccupied gauge theories (Krasnitz 2000, Berges 2012, Lappi 2016, Lappi 2017) by measuring the full spectral function including the Landau cut and damping rate, not just equal-time statistical functions. The method provides a non-perturbative lattice approach to HTL physics that can be applied to thermal equilibrium where no other controlled non-perturbative methods exist, and it generalizes the classic result for the plasmon damping rate from Braaten and Pisarski (1990) to finite momentum.
