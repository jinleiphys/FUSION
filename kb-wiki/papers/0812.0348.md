# $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements and the occupancy of individual orbits

> The measured occupancies of valence orbits in $^{76}$Ge and $^{76}$Se are used as a guideline for modification of the effective mean field energies that results in better description of these quantities. With them, in combination with the selfconsitent renormalized quasiparticle random phase approximation (SRQRPA) method that ensures conservation of the mean particle number in the correlated ground state, we show that the resulting $0νββ$ nuclear matrix element for the $^{76}$Ge $\to$ $^{76}$Se transition is reduced by $\sim$25% compared to the previous QRPA value, and therefore the difference between the present approach and the interacting shell model predictions becomes correspondingly smaller. Analogous modification of the mean field energies for the A=82 system also results in a reduction of $0νββ$ matrix element for the $^{82}$Se $\to$ $^{82}$Kr transition, making it also closer to the shell model prediction.

## Key claim

Using measured occupancies of valence orbits in ⁷⁶Ge and ⁷⁶Se to modify the effective mean field energies, combined with the selfconsistent renormalized quasiparticle random phase approximation (SRQRPA) method that conserves the mean particle number in the correlated ground state, the resulting 0νββ nuclear matrix element for the ⁷⁶Ge → ⁷⁶Se transition is reduced by approximately 25% compared to the previous QRPA value, making the difference between this approach and the interacting shell model predictions correspondingly smaller.

## Method

The authors used the SRQRPA method, which iteratively solves the BCS and QRPA equations to ensure conservation of the average neutron and proton numbers in the correlated ground state, unlike standard QRPA or RQRPA. They modified the input single-particle energies of the Woods-Saxon mean field (primarily for valence orbits) so that the calculated orbit occupancies from SRQRPA matched the experimental occupancies measured via neutron adding and removing transfer reactions by Schiffer (2008) and proton transfer reactions by Schiffer (2008). The pairing interaction used a constant matrix element adjusted to reproduce experimental odd-even mass differences, and the particle-particle coupling constant g_pp was fixed by requiring correct reproduction of the 2νββ decay rate.

## Key numbers

*   The 0νββ nuclear matrix element M⁰ν for ⁷⁶Ge → ⁷⁶Se is reduced from 4.34 (QRPA with Woods-Saxon mean field) to 3.27 (SRQRPA with adjusted mean field and adjusted M²ν denominators), a reduction of approximately 25%.
*   For the ⁷⁶Ge → ⁷⁶Se transition, the calculated half-life T₁/₂⁰ν for a nominal neutrino mass ⟨m_ββ⟩ = 50 meV is 12.3 × 10²⁶ years (SRQRPA with adjusted mean field and adjusted M²ν denominators), compared to 7.0 × 10²⁶ years for the previous QRPA value.
*   The mean M⁰ν value for ⁷⁶Ge with Jastrow short-range correlations is 3.49 ± 0.23 (new SRQRPA with adjusted energies), down from 4.24 ± 0.44 (previous QRPA with Woods-Saxon).
*   The mean M⁰ν value for ⁷⁶Ge with UCOM short-range correlations is 4.60 ± 0.39 (new), down from 5.19 ± 0.54 (previous).
*   In ⁷⁶Ge, the calculated neutron occupancy of the g₉/₂ orbit is 6.35 (SRQRPA with adjusted energies), compared to the experimental value of 6.5 ± 0.3; the previous BCS value was 4.63.
*   In ⁷⁶Se, the calculated neutron occupancy of the g₉/₂ orbit is 5.66 (SRQRPA), compared to the experimental value of 5.8 ± 0.3; the previous BCS value was 2.78.

## Context

This work extends the QRPA calculations of Simkovic (2008) by incorporating experimental orbit occupancy data from Schiffer (2008) as a constraint, reducing the discrepancy with the interacting shell model predictions of Poves (2008). The authors note that previous attempts by SC3 (2008) to modify single-particle energies used only BCS occupancies, whereas the present work uses the more consistent SRQRPA method that accounts for ground-state correlations and conserves particle number. This approach enables more reliable predictions of 0νββ decay matrix elements for ⁷⁶Ge and ⁸²Se, which are crucial for interpreting experimental searches for neutrinoless double beta decay.
