# The Original Mixed Symmetry States - $6^{+}_{1}$ and $6^{+}_{2}$ in $^{48}$Ti

> The $6^{+}_{1}$ and $6^{+}_{2}$ in $^{48}$Ti form a nearly degenerate doublet. In a single j shell calculation with the matrix elements from experiment the $6^{+}_{1}$ changes sign under the interchange of protons and neutron holes (odd signature) while the $6_{2}^{+}$ does not (even signature). As a consequence the calculated B(E2) $6_{1}^{+}\to 4_{1}^{+}$ is much stronger than the $6_{2}^{+}\to 4_{1}^{+}$ and the Gamow-Teller matrix element to the $6_{2}^{+}$ state vanishes. When using some popular interaction e.g. FPD6 in single j shell the ordering of the even signature and odd signature states gets reversed, so that the Gamow-Teller matrix element to the $6^{+}_{1}$ state vanishes and the $6_{2}^{+}\to 4_{1}^{+}$ E2 transition is the strong one. When configuration mixing is introduced, the E2 transition $6_{2}^{+}\to 4_{1}^{+}$ persists in being large. However the Gamow-Teller strengths reverse, with the large matrix element to the $6_{1}^{+}$ state in agreement with experiment. Static properties $μ$ and Q for the two $6^{+}$ states are also considered. The experimental B(E2)'s from the $6^{+}$ states to the $4_{1}^{+}$ state are not well known.

# The Original Mixed Symmetry States - $6^{+}_{1}$ and $6^{+}_{2}$ in $^{48}$Ti

## Key claim
The near-degenerate $6^{+}_{1}$ and $6^{+}_{2}$ doublet in $^{48}$Ti exhibits signature-dependent selection rules in single-j-shell calculations, but configuration mixing reverses the Gamow-Teller strength distribution while preserving the strong B(E2) transition from the $6_{2}^{+}$ state to the $4_{1}^{+}$ state. The experimental B(E2) values from the $6^{+}$ states to the $4_{1}^{+}$ state are not well known, limiting definitive conclusions.

## Method
Single-j-shell ($f_{7/2}$) calculations were performed using two-body matrix elements taken from the experimental spectrum of $^{42}$Sc, and separately using the FPD6 interaction. Configuration mixing was then introduced by allowing up to t=2 particles to be excited from the $f_{7/2}$ shell to higher shells (t=0, 1, 2, and 3 for energy splittings). B(E2) transition rates, magnetic moments, electric quadrupole moments, and Gamow-Teller matrix elements from the beta decay of $^{48}$Sc($6^{+}_{1}$) were computed for each truncation level.

## Key numbers
- Energy splitting between $6^{+}_{2}$ and $6^{+}_{1}$: 0.076 MeV (FPD6 t=0), 0.156 MeV (t=1), 0.229 MeV (t=2), 0.161 MeV (t=3); experimental value is 0.175 MeV.
- B(E2) $6_{1}^{+} \rightarrow 4_{1}^{+}$: 0.935 $e^{2}fm^{4}$ (FPD6 t=0), 26.63 $e^{2}fm^{4}$ (t=1), 16.13 $e^{2}fm^{4}$ (t=2).
- B(E2) $6_{2}^{+} \rightarrow 4_{1}^{+}$: 43.54 $e^{2}fm^{4}$ (FPD6 t=0), 53.07 $e^{2}fm^{4}$ (t=1), 65.56 $e^{2}fm^{4}$ (t=2).
- B(GT) to $6_{1}^{+}$: 0 (FPD6 t=0), 0.09953 (t=1), 0.09569 (t=2).
- B(GT) to $6_{2}^{+}$: 0.1631 (FPD6 t=0), 2.869 $\times 10^{-5}$ (t=1), 0.01104 (t=2).
- Experimental log ft values: 5.563 to $6_{1}^{+}$, 6.010 to $6_{2}^{+}$.
- Experimental half-life limits for $6_{1} \rightarrow 4_{1}$: 0.221 ps $\leq T_{1/2} \leq$ 8.9 ps; for $6_{2} \rightarrow 4_{1}$: 1.4 ps $\leq T_{1/2} \leq$ 2.4 ps.
- Quadrupole moment $Q(6_{2}^{+})$: -0.892 $e fm^{2}$ (t=0), -23.31 $e fm^{2}$ (t=1), -26.15 $e fm^{2}$ (t=2).
- Magnetic moment $\mu(6_{2}^{+})$: 3.326 nm (t=0), 7.392 nm (t=1), 7.451 nm (t=2).

## Context
This work extends the single-j-shell calculations of McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (1963, 1964) and Ginocchio and French (1963) in the $f_{7/2}$ region, which established cross-conjugate symmetry and signature selection rules for $^{48}$Ti. The authors show that the FPD6 interaction (Richter, Van der Merwe, Julies, and Brown, 1991) reverses the signature ordering compared to matrix elements from the $^{42}$Sc spectrum (Kutschera, Brown, and Ogawa, 1978), and that configuration mixing breaks the strict signature quantum number while preserving the near-degeneracy of the $6^{+}$ doublet. The results enable future experimental measurements of B(E2) values to distinguish between the single-j-shell and configuration-mixing predictions, and suggest a quasi-rotational interpretation with K-quantum numbers replacing the signature selection rule.
