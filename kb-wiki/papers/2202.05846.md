# A dispersive analysis of $η'\toπ^+π^-γ$ and $η'\to \ell^+\ell^-γ$

> We present a dispersive representation of the $η'$ transition form factor that allows one to account, in a consistent way, for the effects of $ρ$-$ω$ mixing in both the isoscalar and the isovector contributions. Using this formalism, we analyze recent data on $η'\to π^+π^-γ$ to constrain the isovector part of the form factor, individually and in combination with data for the pion vector form factor. As a first application, we use our results, in combination with the most recent input for the isoscalar part of the form factor, to predict the corresponding spectrum of $η'\to\ell^+\ell^-γ$, in particular we find the slope parameter $b_{η'}=1.431(23)\, \text{GeV}^{-2}$. With forthcoming data on the latter process, our results establish the necessary framework to improve the evaluation of the $η'$-pole contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon using experimental input from both $η'$ decay channels.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dispersive representation of the η' transition form factor that consistently accounts for ρ-ω mixing in both isoscalar and isovector contributions, enabling a prediction of the η' → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻γ spectrum with a slope parameter b_η' = 1.431(23) GeV⁻², under the condition that the isovector part is constrained by η' → π⁺π⁻γ data and the isoscalar part by external input.

## Method

The authors construct a dispersion relation for the η' transition form factor whose spectral function has a vanishing double discontinuity, allowing consistent treatment of ρ-ω mixing. They fit this formalism to BESIII data on η' → π⁺π⁻γ combined with multiple e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ data sets (BaBar, KLOE, CMD-2, SND) using a shared ρ-ω mixing parameter ε_ρω, with the pion vector form factor represented dispersively via an Omnès function. The resulting form factor is then used to predict the η' → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻γ spectrum and extract the slope parameter.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter b_η' = 1.431(23) GeV⁻² for the η' transition form factor
- ρ-ω mixing parameter ε_ρω = 2.007(10) × 10⁻³ from the combined fit
- Normalization sum rule saturated at 91.9(1.1)% from the dispersive representation
- Isovector contribution dominates the slope at 1.160(23) GeV⁻², isoscalar ω contribution at 0.1176(32) GeV⁻², and φ contribution at 0.1526(53) GeV⁻²
- η' → π⁺π⁻γ fit parameters: A = 17.10(32) GeV⁻³, β = 0.720(46) GeV⁻², γ = -0.418(46) GeV⁻⁴
- ω mass from combined fit: 783.00(28) MeV from BESIII data

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework previously applied to the π⁰ transition form factor by Hoferichter et al. (2018) and the η transition form factor by Stollenwerk et al. (2011), Hanhart et al. (2013), and Kubis et al. (2015), specifically addressing the subtle interplay of isoscalar and isovector contributions via ρ-ω mixing. The results establish the necessary framework to improve the evaluation of the η'-pole contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, using forthcoming experimental data from both η' decay channels.
