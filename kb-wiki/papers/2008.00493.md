# Chiral properties of (2+1)-flavor QCD in strong magnetic fields at zero temperature

> We present lattice QCD results for masses and magnetic polarizabilities of light and strange pseudoscalar mesons, chiral condensates, decay constants of neutral pion, and neutral kaon in the presence of background magnetic fields with $eB$ ranging up to around 3.35 GeV$^2$ ($\sim70~M_π^2$) in the vacuum. The computations were carried out in (2+1)-flavor QCD mostly on $32^3 \times 96$ lattices using the highly improved staggered quark action with $M_π \approx $ 220 MeV at zero temperature. We find that the masses of neutral pseudoscalar mesons monotonously decrease as the magnetic field strength grows and then saturate at a nonzero value, while there exists a nonmonotonous behavior of charged pion and kaon masses in the magnetic field. We observe a $qB$ scaling of the up and down quark flavor components of neutral pion mass, neutral pion decay constant as well as the quark chiral condensates at 0.05 $\lesssim eB\lesssim$ 3.35 GeV$^2$. We show that the correction to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation involving the neutral pion is less than 6% and the correction for the relation involving neutral kaon is less than 30% at $eB\lesssim$ 3.35 GeV$^2$. We also derive the Ward-Takahashi identities for QCD in the magnetic field in the continuum formulation including the relation between integrated neutral pseudoscalar meson correlators and chiral condensates.

## Key claim
The paper presents lattice QCD evidence that the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GMOR) relation holds for neutral pions and kaons in strong magnetic fields up to 3.35 GeV², with corrections below 6% for the neutral pion and below 30% for the neutral kaon, but this validity is conditional on the use of a heavier-than-physical pion mass of approximately 220 MeV and on neglecting disconnected quark-line contributions to the neutral pion correlator.

## Method
The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action on 32³ × 96 and 40³ × 96 lattices at a single lattice spacing of 0.117 fm (inverse lattice spacing 1.685 GeV) with a pion mass of 220 MeV at zero temperature. They computed two-point temporal correlation functions for pseudoscalar mesons in external magnetic fields ranging from 0 to 3.35 GeV², extracting masses, decay constants, and chiral condensates, and tested the GMOR relation by comparing the product of quark masses and chiral condensates against the product of decay constants and squared meson masses.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field range: eB from 0 to 3.35 GeV² (approximately 70 M_π²) at zero temperature
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.117 fm (a⁻¹ = 1.685 GeV)
- Pion mass at zero field: M_π = 220.61(6) MeV
- Neutral kaon mass at zero field: M_K = 507.0(7) MeV
- Correction to GMOR relation for neutral pion: less than 6% at eB ≤ 3.35 GeV²
- Correction to GMOR relation for neutral kaon: less than 30% at eB ≤ 3.35 GeV²
- Largest magnetic flux number: N_b = 64, giving N_b/N_σ² ≈ 6% for discretization error control
- Statistics: approximately O(10³) configurations per magnetic field value, ranging from 1993 to 3432 configurations

## Context
This work extends previous quenched QCD studies of meson masses in magnetic fields (Hidaka 2012, Bali 2017) and full QCD studies using stout fermions (Bali 2011, Bali 2017) by providing the first lattice QCD test of the GMOR relation in strong magnetic fields using the HISQ action. The results enable future studies connecting the observed reduction of the chiral crossover temperature in magnetic fields (inverse magnetic catalysis) to the behavior of the neutral pion as a Goldstone boson, and provide a foundation for testing whether the GMOR relation holds at physical pion masses and in the continuum limit.
