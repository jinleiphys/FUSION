# Understanding the impact of nuclear effects on proton decay searches with the GiBUU model

> Proton decay searches in the next generation of water Cherenkov detectors, such as Hyper-Kamiokande, are expected to probe the $10^{35}$-year lifetime regime where atmospheric neutrino backgrounds and systematic uncertainties begin to play an increasingly important role. In this study, we employ the GiBUU framework and reevaluate the proton decay search sensitivity for the $\textrm{p}\rightarrow\textrm{e}^{+}π^{0}$ channel by incorporating a typical event reconstruction performance in water Cherenkov detectors. Using sophisticated models implemented in GiBUU -- most notably the mean-field potential and Boltzmann transport -- which have been benchmarked against accelerator neutrino scattering data, in particular pion production, we find that the resulting proton decay signal detection efficiency and atmospheric neutrino background rate are comparable to those previously evaluated for the current and near future water Cherenkov experiments using $\textit{ad hoc}$ nuclear models. In addition to pion final-state interactions, we evaluate the impact of differences in the Fermi momentum distribution of nucleons in the nucleus, as a source of systematic uncertainty, on the signal detection efficiency and the expected background event rate. We find that the uncertainty associated with pion final-state interactions is moderate, whereas the choice of Fermi momentum distribution can significantly affect the estimated atmospheric neutrino background rate and constitutes the dominant contribution. Our study provides an independent and complementary characterisation of nuclear effects on proton decay searches and helps to refine sensitivity estimates in the regime where systematic uncertainties become more relevant.

## Key claim

Using the GiBUU framework with sophisticated nuclear models (mean-field potential and Boltzmann transport) benchmarked against accelerator neutrino scattering data, the resulting proton decay signal detection efficiency and atmospheric neutrino background rate for the p → e⁺π⁰ channel are comparable to those previously evaluated using ad hoc nuclear models, but the choice of Fermi momentum distribution constitutes the dominant systematic uncertainty, significantly affecting the estimated atmospheric neutrino background rate.

## Method

The authors employed the GiBUU (Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) model, which treats the entire nuclear reaction process in a unified framework from initial interaction to final-state particles exiting the nucleus, using a Thomas-Fermi local Fermi gas (LFG) model for the mean-field description of the nucleus. Proton decay events within oxygen nuclei were simulated by sampling the initial-state proton from the LFG model, generating decay products isotropically in the proton rest frame, then boosting to the laboratory frame and propagating the π⁰ through the nuclear medium via GiBUU's Boltzmann transport. Atmospheric neutrino background events were simulated using the same GiBUU framework, and a water Cherenkov detector response model based on Super-Kamiokande reconstruction performance was applied to both signal and background events.

## Key numbers

- The Super-Kamiokande experiment has set a lower bound on the proton lifetime of τ/B(p → e⁺π⁰) > 2.4 × 10³⁴ years at 90% confidence level
- The signal detection efficiency in the most recent Super-Kamiokande proton decay search is approximately 38%
- The atmospheric neutrino background event rate in Super-Kamiokande is estimated at 1.8 events/(Mton·years) without neutron tagging and 1.0 events/(Mton·years) with neutron tagging
- Hyper-Kamiokande has a fiducial mass of approximately 200 kton and is projected to reach a proton lifetime sensitivity of 10³⁵ years at 90% confidence level with 10 years of operation
- The fraction of proton decays in oxygen nuclei involving short-range correlation effects was estimated in prior work (Yamazaki, 1999) to be approximately 10%
- The Fermi surface in the LFG model goes up to about 280 MeV/c at the nuclear center
- Approximately 40% of pions are absorbed during final-state interactions when their momentum at production lies between 0.4 and 0.5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior proton decay sensitivity studies for water Cherenkov detectors (Super-Kamiokande, 2020; Hyper-Kamiokande, 2018) by providing an independent assessment using the GiBUU model, which differs from intranuclear cascade-based event generators such as GENIE (Andreopoulos, 2009), NuWro (Golan, 2012), and NEUT (Hayato, 2021) that use factorized models of the initial state and cascade-based treatments of final-state interactions. The study enables refined sensitivity estimates for next-generation proton decay searches in the regime where systematic uncertainties become more relevant, particularly by identifying the Fermi momentum distribution as the dominant source of systematic uncertainty compared to pion final-state interactions.
