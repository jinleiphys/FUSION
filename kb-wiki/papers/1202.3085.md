# Kaons production at finite temperature and baryon density in an effective relativistic mean field model

> We investigate the kaons production at finite temperature and baryon density by means of an effective relativistic mean-field model with the inclusion of the full octet of baryons. Kaons are considered taking into account of an effective chemical potential depending on the self-consistent interaction between baryons. The obtained results are compared with a minimal coupling scheme, calculated for different values of the anti-kaon optical potential.

# Kaons production at finite temperature and baryon density in an effective relativistic mean field model

## Key claim
The effective relativistic mean-field model, where kaons are treated as a quasi-ideal Bose gas with an effective chemical potential derived from self-consistent baryon interactions, yields results that agree well with a minimal coupling scheme for moderate values of the anti-kaon optical potential (around -50 MeV), but deviates at very strong attractive potentials (-160 MeV) and high baryon chemical potential, especially at high temperature.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian including the full baryon octet (p, n, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) with GM3 parameter set couplings. Kaons are treated in two ways: (1) a minimal coupling scheme with direct kaon-meson interactions, where the anti-kaon optical potential is set to -50, -100, or -160 MeV; and (2) an effective approach where kaons form a quasi-ideal Bose gas with an effective chemical potential derived from baryon chemical potentials, incorporating self-consistent meson field interactions. The kaon-to-anti-kaon ratio is computed as a function of baryon chemical potential and temperature for both approaches.

## Key numbers
- Anti-kaon optical potential values used: U(K⁻) = -50 MeV, -100 MeV, and -160 MeV, based on theoretical calculations and experimental measurements.
- The scalar kaon coupling constant g_{σK} is determined from the condition that the real part of the anti-kaon optical potential at saturation nuclear density in symmetric nuclear matter is set to the chosen values.
- The vector coupling constants are set to g_{ωK} = g_{ωN}/3 and g_{ρK} = g_{ρN} from the quark model and isospin counting rules.
- For U(K⁻) = -50 MeV, the effective model and minimal coupling scheme show good correspondence; for U(K⁻) = -160 MeV at high baryon chemical potential, a lowering of the K⁺/K⁻ ratio occurs, especially at high temperature.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Glendenning (1985) and the GM3 parameter set to include kaon production at finite temperature and baryon density. It compares two theoretical treatments of kaons: a minimal coupling scheme used in earlier studies (Schaffner et al., 1994; Pal et al., 1997) and a newer self-consistent effective chemical potential method. The results are relevant for interpreting strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the future CBM experiment at FAIR, and suggest that the anti-kaon optical potential near -50 MeV is favored by recent coupled-channel G-matrix calculations and chiral Lagrangian studies.
