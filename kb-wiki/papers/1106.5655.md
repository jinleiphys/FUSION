# Quasiparticle light elements and quantum condensates in nuclear matter

> Nuclei in dense matter are influenced by the medium. In the cluster mean field approximation, an effective Schrödinger equation for the $A$-particle cluster is obtained accounting for the effects of the surrounding medium, such as self-energy and Pauli blocking. Similar to the single-baryon states (free neutrons and protons), the light elements ($2 \le A \le 4$, internal quantum state $ν$) are treated as quasiparticles with energies $E_{A,ν}(P; T, n_n,n_p)$ that depend on the center of mass momentum $\vec P$, the temperature $T$, and the total densities $n_n,n_p$ of neutrons and protons, respectively. We consider the composition and thermodynamic properties of nuclear matter at low densities. At low temperatures, quartetting is expected to occur. Consequences for different physical properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that light nuclei (deuteron, triton, helion, alpha particle) in dense nuclear matter can be treated as quasiparticles with medium-dependent energies, and that at low temperatures and densities a quantum condensate of alpha particles ("quartetting") is expected to occur, but the exact behavior of the internal energy at low temperatures remains an unsolved problem due to competing effects of self-energy shifts and Pauli blocking.

## Method

The author derives an in-medium Schrödinger equation for A-particle clusters (A ≤ 4) within the cluster mean-field approximation, incorporating self-energy shifts and Pauli blocking terms that depend on temperature T, total neutron and proton densities n_n, n_p, and center-of-mass momentum P. The Pauli blocking shift is parametrized using a fit formula with tabulated parameters for each light nucleus, based on model calculations with a separable potential that reproduces empirical binding energies and rms radii. The composition and thermodynamic properties of nuclear matter at low densities are then evaluated using these quasiparticle energies.

## Key numbers

- For the Pauli blocking shift at zero momentum, the parameter c_α,0 for the alpha particle is 150.71 MeV, and c_α,1 is 9772 MeV^3
- The self-energy shift at zero temperature amounts to about -4000 n MeV fm^3, while the Pauli blocking term is larger than 4434 n MeV fm^3 at T=1 MeV
- The kinetic energy of nucleons bound in the alpha particle is estimated as E_α^kin = 51.6 MeV
- The cluster virial expansion gives about -1500 n MeV fm^3 for the total quasiparticle energy shift at T=1 MeV
- The crossover from alpha matter behavior to quasiparticle nucleonic liquids is expected near ρ = 0.05 fm^-3 rather than n = 0.02 fm^-3
- The charge rms radius for the alpha particle is ⟨r^2⟩_α = 2.822 fm^2, and the proton form factor contribution is ⟨r^2⟩_proton = 0.743 fm^2 with neutron contribution ⟨r^2⟩_neutron = -0.116 fm^2
- For a heavy nucleus with point rms radius 6 fm, alpha clustering would increase the charge rms radius by 2.98% compared to a nucleonic surface

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach of Röpke (previous work) to treat light nuclei as quasiparticles in dense matter, building on the cluster mean-field approximation and the in-medium Schrödinger equation. It connects to nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) at low densities and mean-field approaches near saturation density, and discusses applications to alpha clustering in finite nuclei such as the Hoyle state in 12C and the surface of superheavy elements. The paper enables improved equations of state for supernova simulations and provides a framework for understanding the Wigner energy in nuclei near the N=Z line, as well as the enhanced charge radii observed in superheavy nuclei like Cm compared to Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov shell model calculations.
