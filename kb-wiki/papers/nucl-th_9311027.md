# Simple approximation for the starting-energy-independent two-body effective interaction with applications to 6Li

> We apply the Lee-Suzuki iteration method to calculate the linked-folded diagram series for a new Nijmegen local NN potential. We obtain an exact starting-energy-independent effective two-body interaction for a multi-shell, no-core, harmonic-oscillator model space. It is found that the resulting effective-interaction matrix elements can be well approximated by the Brueckner G-matrix elements evaluated at starting energies selected in a simple way. These starting energies are closely related to the energies of the initial two-particle states in the ladder diagrams. The ``exact'' and approximate effective interactions are used to calculate the energy spectrum of 6Li in order to test the utility of the approximate form.

# Simple approximation for the starting-energy-independent two-body effective interaction with applications to 6Li

## Key claim

The starting-energy-independent two-body effective interaction for a no-core harmonic-oscillator model space, obtained via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method for the Nijmegen local NN potential, can be well approximated by Brueckner G-matrix elements evaluated at a state-dependent starting energy given by the sum of the two harmonic-oscillator single-particle energies plus a constant shift Δ = −21 MeV, rather than at a fixed starting energy.

## Method

The authors calculated the Brueckner G-matrix from the Nijmegen II local NN potential using a harmonic-oscillator single-particle basis with ℏΩ = 18 MeV and a no-core model space containing the first four major shells (0s, 0p, 1s-0d, 1p-0f). They applied the Lee-Suzuki iteration method to sum the two-body folded diagrams to all orders, obtaining a starting-energy-independent effective interaction. The mean field generated by spectator nucleons was approximated by a shifted harmonic-oscillator potential. The resulting effective interaction was then used in shell-model calculations for 6Li, allowing up to 4ℏΩ excitations from the lowest-energy configuration.

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation between G-matrix elements and the exact effective interaction matrix elements is 0.13 MeV when using the state-dependent starting energy prescription with Δ = −21 MeV, compared to a minimum rms deviation of about 0.42 MeV for any fixed starting energy (ω′ = 75 MeV).
- For the (0s²₁/₂) J=0, T=1 state, the exact effective interaction matrix element is −8.75 MeV, the G-matrix at ω′ = 30 MeV gives −8.73 MeV, and the approximate prescription gives −8.75 MeV.
- For the (0s²₁/₂) J=1, T=0 state, the exact effective interaction matrix element is −11.78 MeV, the G-matrix at ω′ = 30 MeV gives −11.70 MeV, and the approximate prescription gives −11.83 MeV.
- For the (0p²₃/₂) J=0, T=1 state, the exact effective interaction matrix element is −3.87 MeV, the G-matrix at ω′ = 75 MeV gives −3.86 MeV, and the approximate prescription gives −3.81 MeV.
- For the (0d²₅/₂) J=0, T=1 state, the exact effective interaction matrix element is −1.79 MeV, the G-matrix at ω′ = 110 MeV gives −1.68 MeV, and the approximate prescription gives −1.62 MeV.
- The calculated ground-state energy of 6Li using the exact effective interaction is −35.655 MeV, compared to the Coulomb-corrected experimental value of −33.996 MeV (overbinding by about 1.66 MeV).
- The approximate effective interaction gives a ground-state energy of −36.854 MeV for 6Li, while the G-matrix at constant ω′ = 0 gives −21.497 MeV and at ω′ = 75 gives −48.386 MeV.
- The 3⁺₁(0) excitation energy in 6Li is 2.054 MeV (exact), 1.916 MeV (approximate), and 2.186 MeV (experimental).
- The 0⁺₁(1) excitation energy in 6Li is 4.932 MeV (exact), 5.168 MeV (approximate), and 3.563 MeV (experimental).

## Context

This work extends previous no-core shell-model calculations by Jaqua et al. and Zheng et al. (1993), where the two-body effective interaction was approximated by the G-matrix alone with the starting energy treated as a parameter fitted to nuclear binding energies. The authors improve upon that approach by including the folded diagrams via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method (Lee and Suzuki, 1980) and by introducing a physically motivated state-dependent starting energy prescription. The results are compared with the work of Poppelier and Brussaard, who obtained even higher excitation energies for 6Li using a different effective interaction that retained some starting-energy dependence. The approximation scheme enables simpler calculations of effective interactions for no-core model spaces without explicitly evaluating folded diagrams, though the authors note that effective three-body forces may be needed for light nuclei when using harmonic-oscillator parameters smaller than ℏΩ = 18 MeV.
