# Parton distribution function uncertainties in theoretical predictions for far-forward tau neutrinos at the Large Hadron Collider

> New experiments to measure neutrinos in the far-forward region at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are under design or already in preparation. Two of them, FASER$ν$ and SND@LHC, are expected to be active during Run 3 and have the potential to detect neutrinos that come from high-energy collisions in one of the LHC interaction points, extracted along the direction tangent to the beam line. Tau neutrinos and antineutrinos come predominantly from $D_s^\pm$ production in $pp$ collisions, followed by the leptonic decay of these mesons. Neutrino pseudorapidities in the range of $η>6.9$ and $η>8.9$ are relevant to these future experiments. At such pseudorapidities at high energies, theoretical predictions for the flux of tau neutrinos rely on parton distribution functions (PDFs) in a combination of very small and large parton$-x$ values. We evaluate PDF uncertainties in a next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD calculation of the flux of $ν_τ$ + $\barν_τ$ produced by $D_s^\pm$ decay in the far forward region at the LHC. The theoretical uncertainty associated with the 40 PDF sets of the PROSA19 group amounts to $\pm (20-30)$\% for the ($ν_τ$ + $\barν_τ$) number of charged-current (CC) events. Scale uncertainties are much larger, resulting in a range of CC event predictions from $\sim 70\%$ lower to $\sim 90\%$ higher than the central prediction. A comparison of the predictions with those obtained using as input the central PDFs from the 3-flavour NLO PDF sets of the CT14, ABMP16 and NNPDF3.1 collaborations show that far-forward neutrino energy distributions vary by as much as a factor of $\sim 2-4$ relative to the PROSA19 predictions at TeV neutrino energies. The Forward Physics Facility in the high luminosity LHC era will provide data capable of constraining NLO QCD evaluations with these PDF sets.

## Key claim

The PDF uncertainty from the 40 PROSA19 sets amounts to ±(20–30)% for the number of charged-current (CC) tau neutrino events, but scale uncertainties are much larger, yielding a range from ~70% lower to ~90% higher than the central prediction, and comparisons with other PDF sets (CT14, ABMP16, NNPDF3.1) show far-forward neutrino energy distributions varying by a factor of ~2–4 relative to PROSA19 predictions at TeV energies.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD calculation of D_s± production in pp collisions at the LHC, followed by leptonic decay to tau neutrinos and antineutrinos, using the PROSA19 PDF set with 40 eigenvector sets to evaluate PDF uncertainties. They implemented transverse momentum smearing via a Gaussian function with ⟨k_T⟩ = 0.7 GeV and Peterson fragmentation in the parton center-of-mass frame, then compared predictions for neutrino energy distributions and charged-current event rates across pseudorapidity ranges η > 6.9 and η > 8.9 relevant to FASERν and SND@LHC.

## Key numbers

- PDF uncertainty from the 40 PROSA19 sets: ±(20–30)% for the (ν_τ + ν̄_τ) number of charged-current events
- Scale uncertainty range: from ~70% lower to ~90% higher than the central prediction for CC event predictions
- Far-forward neutrino energy distributions vary by a factor of ~2–4 relative to PROSA19 predictions when using CT14, ABMP16, and NNPDF3.1 central PDFs at TeV neutrino energies
- D_s± branching fraction to τ+ν_τ: B = 0.0548
- Default charm quark pole mass for PROSA19: 1.442 GeV (from MSbar mass 1.242 GeV)
- Default ⟨k_T⟩ = 0.7 GeV for transverse momentum smearing

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous predictions for far-forward tau neutrino events (Bai 2020) by incorporating PDF uncertainties from the PROSA19 fit, which includes LHCb open heavy-flavor hadroproduction data that extend the x-range down to x ~ 10^{-6}. The results highlight that scale uncertainties dominate over PDF uncertainties in current predictions, and that the Forward Physics Facility during the high-luminosity LHC era will provide data capable of constraining NLO QCD evaluations with these PDF sets, enabling direct tests of lepton flavor universality and searches for new physics in tau neutrino oscillations.
