# Neutrino mean free path in neutron stars

> The neutrino propagation in neutron stars is studied in the framework of the linear response method. The medium effects are treated in the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach either in the mean-field approximation or in the RPA. The residual interaction is expressed in terms of the Landau parameters extracted from the equation of state of spin- and isospin-polarized nuclear matter. The Brueckner theory including three-body forces is used for determining the equation of state. Numerical predictions for the response function of nuclear matter in $β$-equilibrium and the neutrino mean free path are presented in a range of baryonic densities and temperatures. The main results are a dominance of the charge-exchange component over the scattering component and an enhancement of the neutrino mean free path induced by nuclear correlations.

# Neutrino mean free path in neutron stars

## Key claim

The neutrino mean free path in β-equilibrium nuclear matter is substantially enhanced by nuclear correlations (both short-range BHF and long-range RPA effects), with the charge-exchange (absorption) component dominating over the scattering component; however, this enhancement is conditional on the specific treatment of three-body forces and the Landau parameters extracted from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state, and the results are sensitive to the density and temperature regime.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear response function to weak interactions using the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the Argonne AV18 two-body force plus a microscopic three-body force, extracting the l=0 Landau parameters from the equation of state of spin- and isospin-polarized nuclear matter. The neutrino mean free path is then computed in two steps: first in the BHF mean-field approximation (including short-range correlations via mass renormalization and Fermi surface depletion), and then in the random phase approximation (RPA) using the Landau-Migdal residual interaction to incorporate long-range particle-hole correlations, for both neutral-current scattering and charged-current absorption processes in β-equilibrium neutron star matter.

## Key numbers

- Proton fraction Yp = 0.077 without three-body forces and Yp = 0.167 with three-body forces at baryonic density ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³
- For scattering process, neutrino mean free path decreases by a factor of 30 from T = 5 MeV to T = 60 MeV (at ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³, β = 0.668, Eν = 40 MeV)
- For absorption process, neutrino mean free path decreases by a factor of only 2.5 over the same temperature range
- Temperature and neutrino energy fixed at T = 20 MeV and Eν = 40 MeV for the density-dependent mean free path calculations
- The compression modulus constraint on Landau parameter F0 is 230-250 MeV from experimental monopole giant resonance data
- Landau parameter G'0 = 1.2 at saturation density from Gamow-Teller resonance on ⁹⁰Ni

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic Brueckner theory calculations of neutrino mean free path in pure neutron matter (Shen, 2003) to β-equilibrium nuclear matter including both protons and electrons, and confirms the earlier findings of Burrows and Sawyer (1998, 1999) that medium correlations increase λν by up to one order of magnitude with increasing density. The results contradict non-relativistic phenomenological approaches using Skyrme forces (Reddy, 1998; Prakash, 1997; Margueron, 2003) which predict a quenching of the RPA neutrino mean free path with density increase, and show partial agreement with relativistic mean field calculations (Mornas, 2001; Reddy, 1998; Yamada, 1993) which also find enhancement but with varying magnitude. The work enables more quantitative estimates of energy release in supernova explosions and protoneutron star cooling, while highlighting the need for temperature-dependent effective interactions in future calculations.
