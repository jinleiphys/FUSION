# R-parity violating supersymmetric contributions to the neutron beta decay

> We investigate the contribution to the angular correlation coefficients of the neutron beta decay within the R-parity violating (RPV) minimal supersymmetric standard model (MSSM). The RPV effects contribute to the scalar interaction at the tree level. The effective scalar interaction of the neutron beta decay is constructed by making use of the relation between isospin asymmetries and the proton-neutron mass difference. On the basis of the recent update of the analyses of the superallowed Fermi transitions and the recent measurement of transverse polarization of the emitted electrons at PSI, we deduce new upper limits on the RPV couplings. We also point out the existence of new types of angular correlations which are sensitive to the RPV interactions.

# R-parity violating supersymmetric contributions to the neutron beta decay

## Key claim

R-parity violating (RPV) interactions in the minimal supersymmetric standard model (MSSM) contribute to the scalar interaction in neutron beta decay at tree level, and new upper limits on the RPV couplings can be deduced from recent data on superallowed Fermi transitions and the transverse polarization of electrons measured at PSI, under the assumptions that the R-parity conserving sfermion sector is flavour diagonal with no CP phases and that the RPV sector contains no soft SUSY breaking terms.

## Method

The authors constructed the tree-level amplitude for the quark beta decay d → u e⁻ ν̄ₑ within the RPV MSSM, considering selectron exchange (Fig. 1b) and squark exchange (Fig. 1c) mechanisms. They built the effective nucleon-level scalar interaction using the relation between isospin asymmetries and the proton-neutron mass difference to determine the scalar form factor g_S. Using the angular correlation formula of Jackson, Treiman and Wyld extended by Ebel and Feldman, they computed the RPV contributions to all angular correlation coefficients and extracted constraints on the combined RPV couplings λ_{1i1} λ'*_{i11} from the Fierz interference term b (from superallowed Fermi transitions) and the R correlation (from the PSI transverse polarization measurement).

## Key numbers

- Scalar form factor: g_S = 0.49 ± 0.17, derived from M_n − M_p = (2.05 ± 0.30) MeV (QCD origin) and quark masses m_d = 9.3 ± 0.9 MeV, m_u/m_d = 0.553 ± 0.043
- Bound on real part of combined RPV couplings from superallowed Fermi transitions: Re(Σ λ_{1i1} λ'*_{i11}) = (7.2 ± 8.5) × 10⁻⁴ × [m_{\tilde e_L}]² / [g_S], where [m_{\tilde e_L}] = m_{\tilde e_L} / (100 GeV) and [g_S] = g_S / 0.49
- Bound on imaginary part from PSI R correlation measurement: Im(Σ λ_{1i1} λ'*_{i11}) = (−0.012 ± 0.017 ± 0.008) × [m_{\tilde e_L}]² / [g_S], based on R = 0.008 ± 0.011 ± 0.005
- Current upper limits on individual RPV couplings: |λ_{121}| < 0.049 [m_{\tilde e_R}], |λ_{131}| < 0.062 [m_{\tilde e_R}], |λ'_{211}| < 0.059 [m_{\tilde d_R}], |λ'_{311}| < 0.11 [m_{\tilde d_R}], |λ'_{111}| < 1.3 × 10⁻⁴ · [m_{\tilde q}]² [m_{\tilde g}]^{1/2}, |λ'_{112}| < 0.021 [m_{\tilde s_R}], |λ'_{113}| < 0.021 [m_{\tilde b_R}]
- Combined product limits from individual couplings: |λ_{121} λ'*_{211}| < 0.0029 [m_{\tilde e_R}] [m_{\tilde d_R}], |λ_{131} λ'*_{311}| < 0.0068 [m_{\tilde e_R}] [m_{\tilde d_R}]

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Herczeg (1990s) on RPV contributions to the d → u e⁻ ν̄ₑ transition, who showed that RPV interactions generate both V-A and scalar/pseudoscalar type interactions and derived bounds from pion decay and atomic EDM data. The new constraints from Hardy and Towner (2009) on superallowed Fermi transitions and from Kozela et al. (2009) at PSI on the R correlation provide complementary limits on the scalar interaction, which is tree-level in RPV MSSM. The results enable future searches for new angular correlations (S, T, U, V, W) involving electron polarization and neutrino momentum that are sensitive to the scalar interaction and could further constrain RPV couplings with improved experimental precision.
