# Correlations of $Q_β$-values with symmetry energy and effective mass studied within Skyrme energy--density functionals

> The $β$-decay half-lives of nuclei are sensitive to the values of $Q_β$. For accurate theoretical predictions, it is essential to develop an effective interaction or an energy density functional (EDF) that can systematically reproduce experimental $Q_β$ values. The challenge lies in identifying an appropriate EDF for an accurate $Q_β$ prediction. To address this, we focus on the bulk properties of nuclei that have correlations with $Q_β$. The primary objective of this study is to determine which nuclear bulk properties are sensitive to $Q_β$, providing information on the key nuclear characteristics that influence $β$-decay calculations. We employ the Skyrme energy-density functionals to find correlations between $Q_β$ and the nuclear bulk properties, assuming spherical symmetry. Using $42$ different Skyrme EDFs, we analyze these correlations by evaluating Pearson linear coefficients, focusing particularly on the relationship between $Q_β$ and various nuclear properties. We found that the symmetry energy at low densities shows a correlation with the $Q_β$ value. In particular, this correlation becomes stronger for functionals with an effective mass close to $1$. However, as the nuclear density increases, the correlation weakens. From our analysis, we found that a symmetry energy of $32.8\pm0.7$~MeV and effective mass of $m^{*}/m\ge0.75$ at the saturation density is the most likely to systematically reproduce the experimental data of $Q_β$ systematically.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the symmetry energy at low densities shows a correlation with the $Q_{\beta}$ value, and this correlation becomes stronger for functionals with an effective mass close to 1; however, the correlation weakens as nuclear density increases, and the authors conclude that a symmetry energy of $32.8\pm0.7$ MeV and effective mass of $m^{*}/m\ge0.75$ at saturation density is most likely to systematically reproduce experimental $Q_{\beta}$ data.

## Method

The authors employed 42 different Skyrme energy-density functionals (EDFs) within a spherical Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Skyrme-HFB) framework to calculate $Q_{\beta}$ values for 215 even-even nuclei from $^{20}$O to $^{246}$Pu with $Q_{\beta}\ge0.5$ MeV, using experimental data from AME2020. They analyzed correlations between $Q_{\beta}$ and nuclear bulk properties (symmetry energy at various densities, effective mass, saturation density, compressibility, and Landau parameters) by evaluating Pearson linear correlation coefficients, and applied a scaling factor to the Gogny-type pairing force to minimize variations in $Q_{\beta}$ arising from pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- 42 different Skyrme EDFs were used to analyze correlations between $Q_{\beta}$ and nuclear bulk properties.
- The optimal symmetry energy minimizing the RMS deviation $\gamma$ was found to be $32.8\pm0.7$ MeV at saturation density ($\rho=1.00\rho_{0}$) for functionals with $m^{*}/m>0.75$.
- The optimal symmetry energy at $\rho=0.34\rho_{0}$ was $16.9\pm0.4$ MeV, at $\rho=0.76\rho_{0}$ was $27.2\pm0.3$ MeV, and at $\rho=1.50\rho_{0}$ was $40.4\pm6.5$ MeV.
- The mean RMS deviation $\langle \gamma \rangle$ for functionals with $m^{*}/m>0.75$ was $1.24\pm0.39$, while for functionals with $m^{*}/m\le0.75$ it was $1.67\pm0.29$.
- The SkT1$^{*}$ functional provided the lowest RMS deviation $\gamma$ among the 42 functionals, with $\gamma=0.767$ and mean deviation $\mu=-0.060$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Wang et al. (2013) and Jianmin et al. (2013) who found correlations between symmetry energy and proton-neutron Fermi energy differences or $Q_{\beta}$ for odd-$A$ nuclei, but with a much larger set of nuclei (215 even-even nuclei) and 42 functionals. The results enable the selection of Skyrme EDFs suitable for systematic $Q_{\beta}$ calculations in $r$-process nucleosynthesis studies, and suggest that experimental $Q_{\beta}$ values can constrain the symmetry energy at low densities when functionals with high effective mass are chosen.
