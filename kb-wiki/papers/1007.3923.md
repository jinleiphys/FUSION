# Couplings in coupled channels versus wave functions in the case of resonances: application to the two $Λ(1405)$ states

> In this paper we develop a formalism to evaluate wave functions in momentum and coordinate space for the resonant states dynamically generated in a unitary coupled channel approach. The on shell approach for the scattering matrix, commonly used, is also obtained in Quantum Mechanics with a separable potential, which allows one to write wave functions in a trivial way. We develop useful relationships among the couplings of the dynamically generated resonances to the different channels and the wave functions at the origin. The formalism provides an intuitive picture of the resonances in the coupled channel approach, as bound states of one bound channel, which decays into open ones. It also provides an insight and practical rules for evaluating couplings of the resonances to external sources and how to deal with final state interaction in production processes. As an application of the formalism we evaluate the wave functions of the two $Λ(1405)$ states in the $πΣ$, $\bar{K} N$ and other coupled channels.

# Couplings in coupled channels versus wave functions in the case of resonances: application to the two Λ(1405) states

## Key claim

The authors develop a formalism to obtain wave functions in momentum and coordinate space for resonances dynamically generated in unitary coupled-channel approaches, showing that the commonly used on-shell factorization of the scattering matrix can be reproduced in quantum mechanics with a separable potential. The key insight is that a resonance can be understood as a bound state of one channel that decays into open channels, but this interpretation holds only approximately when one channel dominates and its coupling is sufficiently strong relative to others.

## Method

The authors start from a separable potential with a sharp momentum cutoff Λ and solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for coupled channels, deriving wave functions in both momentum and coordinate space. They establish relationships between the couplings of dynamically generated resonances to different channels and the wave functions at the origin, and extend the formalism from the bound-state case of Gamermann et al. (2007) to include open channels where scattering states exist. The method is applied to evaluate wave functions for the two Λ(1405) states in ten coupled channels including πΣ and K̄N.

## Context

This work extends the bound-state formalism of Gamermann et al. (2007), which was applied to the X(3872) as a D⁰D̄*⁰/D⁺D*⁻ molecule, to the more general case where open channels are present alongside bound channels. The formalism provides a foundation for studying three-body systems where two particles cluster into a dynamically generated resonance, as in the multi-rho work cited by the authors. It also justifies and clarifies the conditions under which final-state interaction is implemented via Watson's theorem in chiral unitary approaches, as used by Geng and Oset (2009) and Geng and Zou (2009).
