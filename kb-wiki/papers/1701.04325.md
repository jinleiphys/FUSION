# The QCD Equation of State to $\mathcal{O}(μ_B^6)$ from Lattice QCD

> We calculated the QCD equation of state using Taylor expansions that include contributions from up to sixth order in the baryon, strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials. Calculations have been performed with the Highly Improved Staggered Quark action in the temperature range $T\in [135~{\rm MeV}, 330~{\rm MeV}]$ using up to four different sets of lattice cut-offs corresponding to lattices of size $N_σ^3\times N_τ$ with aspect ratio $N_σ/N_τ=4$ and $N_τ=6-16$. The strange quark mass is tuned to its physical value and we use two strange to light quark mass ratios $m_s/m_l=20$ and $27$, which in the continuum limit correspond to a pion mass of about $160$ MeV and $140$ MeV espectively. Sixth-order results for Taylor expansion coefficients are used to estimate truncation errors of the fourth-order expansion. We show that truncation errors are small for baryon chemical potentials less then twice the temperature ($μ_B\le 2T$). The fourth-order equation of state thus is suitable for the modeling of dense matter created in heavy ion collisions with center-of-mass energies down to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim 12$ GeV. We provide a parametrization of basic thermodynamic quantities that can be readily used in hydrodynamic simulation codes. The results on up to sixth order expansion coefficients of bulk thermodynamics are used for the calculation of lines of constant pressure, energy and entropy densities in the $T$-$μ_B$ plane and are compared with the crossover line for the QCD chiral transition as well as with experimental results on freeze-out parameters in heavy ion collisions. These coefficients also provide estimates for the location of a possible critical point. We argue that results on sixth order expansion coefficients disfavor the existence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram for $μ_B/T\le 2$ and $T/T_c(μ_B=0) > 0.9$.

## Key claim
The QCD equation of state can be reliably computed using a fourth-order Taylor expansion in baryon chemical potential for $\mu_B \le 2T$, with sixth-order coefficients serving primarily to constrain truncation errors rather than providing accurate sixth-order contributions. The results disfavor the existence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram for $\mu_B/T \le 2$ and $T/T_c(\mu_B=0) > 0.9$.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark action with physical strange quark mass and two light-to-strange quark mass ratios ($m_s/m_l = 20$ and $27$, corresponding to pion masses of about 160 MeV and 140 MeV in the continuum limit). They computed Taylor expansion coefficients up to sixth order in baryon, strangeness, and electric charge chemical potentials over a temperature range $T \in [135~\text{MeV}, 330~\text{MeV}]$ using four lattice spacings with $N_\tau = 6-16$ and aspect ratio $N_\sigma/N_\tau = 4$. The results were used to construct lines of constant pressure, energy density, and entropy density in the $T$-$\mu_B$ plane.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range of calculations: $T \in [135~\text{MeV}, 330~\text{MeV}]$
- Lattice sizes used: $N_\tau = 6-16$ with aspect ratio $N_\sigma/N_\tau = 4$
- Pion masses: about 160 MeV and 140 MeV for the two quark mass ratios
- Fourth-order Taylor series for pressure deviates by less than 5% from exact HRG result at $\mu_B = 2T$
- At $T = 170$ MeV, baryonic contribution to pressure (energy density) is about 20% (30%)
- Second-order curvature coefficients for lines of constant physics in $T \in [145~\text{MeV}, 165~\text{MeV}]$: $0.0064 \le \kappa_2^P \le 0.0101$, $0.0087 \le \kappa_2^\epsilon \le 0.012$, $0.0074 \le \kappa_2^s \le 0.011$
- Fourth-order curvature coefficients: $|\kappa_4^f| \le 0.00024$ in the temperature interval around $T_c$
- Temperature on a line of constant physics varies by about 7 MeV (5%) between $\mu_B/T = 0$ and $\mu_B/T = 2$
- Entropy in units of $T^3$ changes by about 15% on a line of constant pressure between $\mu_B/T = 0$ and $\mu_B/T = 2$
- Crossover curvature parameter $\kappa_2^c$ ranges from $0.0066(7)$ (Kaczmarek 2011, Endrodi 2011) to $0.020(4)$ (Cea 2015)
- The region $\mu_B/T \le 2$ corresponds to beam energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \ge 11.4$ GeV in the RHIC beam energy scan
- Lines of constant physics at $T_0 = 145, 155, 165$ MeV correspond to energy densities $\epsilon = 0.203(27), 0.346(41), 0.556(57)$ GeV/fm$^3$ respectively

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state from fourth-order to sixth-order in chemical potentials, building on previous results from Bazavov (2014) for the zero-chemical-potential equation of state. The lines of constant physics approach extends earlier phenomenological work by Cleymans (1999, 2005) on freeze-out conditions in heavy ion collisions, and by Rafelski (2009, 2015) and Petran (2013) on constant pressure freeze-out criteria. The results enable more reliable modeling of dense matter created in heavy ion collisions with center-of-mass energies down to $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 12$ GeV, and provide a parametrization suitable for hydrodynamic simulation codes. The comparison with freeze-out parameters from the STAR Collaboration (Das 2014) and ALICE Collaboration (Floris 2014) shows that experimental freeze-out points do not follow any single line of constant physics, suggesting the need for more sophisticated dynamical descriptions of freeze-out.
