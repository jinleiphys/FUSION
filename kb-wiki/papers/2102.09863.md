# Comprehensive investigation of the symmetric space-star configuration in the nucleon-deuteron breakup

> We examine a description of available cross section data for symmetric space star (SST) configurations in the neutron-deuteron (nd) and proton-deuteron (pd) breakup reaction using numerically exact solutions of the three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev equation based on two- and three-nucleon (semi)phenomenological and chiral forces. The predicted SST cross sections are very stable with respect to the underlying dynamics for incoming nucleon laboratory energies below $\approx 25$ MeV. We discuss possible origins of the surprising discrepancies between theory and data found in low-energy nd and pd SST breakup measurements.

## Key claim

The predicted symmetric space-star (SST) cross sections are very stable with respect to the underlying dynamics for incoming nucleon laboratory energies below approximately 25 MeV, yet the authors find surprising discrepancies between theory and data in low-energy nd and pd SST breakup measurements that cannot be resolved by standard two-nucleon or three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev equation numerically exactly using both (semi)phenomenological potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm1, Nijm2) and chiral NN potentials at orders up to N4LO+, combined with chiral 3NFs at N2LO and N3LO. They compared the resulting SST cross sections to available nd and pd breakup data at laboratory energies from 10.25 to 65 MeV, and investigated sensitivity by varying the strengths of the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 NN force components through a multiplicative factor lambda.

## Key numbers

- At low energies, theoretical predictions underestimate nd SST data by approximately 15-30% and overestimate pd SST data by approximately 10%
- At 13 MeV, the band of predictions from AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm1, and Nijm2 potentials is very narrow, reflecting stability of the low-energy SST cross section to the underlying dynamics
- Increasing the 1S0 nn interaction strength by 20-30% (lambda=1.21 or lambda=1.3) brings predictions close to pd SST data but creates a di-neutron bound state with binding energy Eb = -0.144 MeV (lambda=1.21) or Eb = -0.441 MeV (lambda=1.3)
- Reducing the 3S1 component strength by 5% (lambda=0.95) gives deuteron binding energy Ed = -1.412 MeV, drastically different from the experimental value Ed_exp = -2.224575(9) MeV
- At 65 MeV, theory describes the pd SST data, and the bands of predictions broaden significantly indicating loss of stability

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by the Cracow-Bochum group that used (semi)phenomenological potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm1, Nijm2) and standard 3NF models (Urbana IX, TM99) which showed only moderate 3NF effects at low energies (Witala 2001, Kuros-Zolnierczuk 2002). The authors now incorporate chiral forces from Epelbaum (2006), Entem (2017), and Reinert (2018) up to N4LO+, and find that even consistent chiral 2N+3N forces cannot resolve the SST puzzle. The results enable future experimental searches for di-neutron contributions and motivate the LENPIC collaboration's systematic investigation of 3NF beyond N2LO.
