# Recent Developments in one and two Pion Production in Elementary Reactions and Few Body Systems

> In this talk we cover several issues concerning pion production. The first one is the p p ----> p p pi^0 reaction where an alternative explanation based on the off shell extrapolation of the pi N amplitude is offered. A recent model for the gamma N ----> pi pi N reaction is presented and a new kind of exchange current is constructed from it which allows one to address problems like double Delta photoproduction from the deuteron. Finally the (gamma, pi pi) reaction in nuclei is studied and shown to be highly sensitive to the renormalization of the pions in nuclei.

## Key claim

The off-shell extrapolation of the isoscalar πN amplitude, rather than a relativistic exchange current involving σ exchange, can explain the long-standing discrepancy between theory and precise data for the pp → ppπ⁰ reaction near threshold, though uncertainties in the off-shell extrapolation prevent a definitive assertion.

## Method

The authors calculated the pp → ppπ⁰ cross section using two different off-shell extrapolations for the isoscalar πN amplitude: one from Hamilton (1980) based on σ exchange with σππ and σNN vertices plus a short-range piece, and another from Banerjee (1990) based on PCAC with regularization. For the γN → ππN reaction, a model with 67 Feynman diagrams involving N, Δ, N*(1440), and N*(1520) intermediate states was constructed, simplified to 20 terms below 800 MeV. The double Δ photoproduction from the deuteron was modeled by attaching the π⁻ from the elementary γp → π⁺π⁻p reaction to a second nucleon. For the (γ, π⁺π⁻) reaction in nuclei, the cross section was evaluated using a local density approximation with the photon selfenergy formalism, including pion and Δ renormalization in the medium.

## Key numbers

- λ₁ = 0.0075 and λ₂ = 0.053 for on-shell pions at threshold in the πN effective Lagrangian
- Four possible solutions for the N*(1520) → Δπ decay amplitudes: (a) f = -0.19, g = 0.18; (b) f = 1.03, g = -0.18; (c) f = -1.03, g = 0.18; (d) f = 0.19, g = -0.18
- gγ - gσ = 0.157 for the N*(1520) → Nγ helicity amplitudes
- Renormalization effects increase the ¹²C(γ, π⁺π⁻) cross section by a factor of 4 to 5 compared to the impulse approximation
- The off-shell πN amplitude shows large enhancement for momenta around 400 MeV, which are the relevant momenta for the pp → ppπ⁰ reaction

## Context

This work extends the study of pion production mechanisms from elementary reactions to few-body systems and nuclei. For the pp → ppπ⁰ puzzle, it offers an alternative to the relativistic exchange current explanation of Horowitz (1994) based on σ exchange between positive and negative energy components. The γN → ππN model provides a foundation for constructing exchange currents for reactions like γd → Δ⁺⁺Δ⁻, measured by the TAPS collaboration (1995). The predicted large renormalization effects in (γ, ππ) on nuclei, building on predictions by Oset (1992) for (π, 2π) reactions, suggest experiments at Mainz could test pion medium modifications more cleanly than with hadronic probes.
