# Semimicroscopic algebraic description of $α$-clustering in $^{22}$Ne

> The alpha-cluster states of $^{22}$Ne are studied within the framework of the semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model (SACM). The band structure, energy spectrum as well as E2 and E1 transitions are calculated and are compared with the experimental data. The results are also compared with those obtained from two microscopic models: the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (DAMD) approach and the generator-coordinate method (GCM). It is found that the prominent bands obtained in the latter frameworks all have equivalents in the SACM and the agreement between the calculated spectroscopic properties is rather good, especially for positive-parity states.

# Semimicroscopic algebraic description of $\alpha$-clustering in $^{22}$Ne

## Key claim

The semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model (SACM) can reproduce the band structure, energy spectrum, and electromagnetic transitions of $^{22}$Ne in terms of an $^{18}$O+$\alpha$ configuration, and the prominent bands obtained from the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (DAMD) approach and the generator-coordinate method (GCM) all have equivalents in the SACM, with agreement that is "rather good, especially for positive-parity states", though the SACM uses a simplified dynamical symmetry Hamiltonian that is diagonal, whereas realistic spectra deviate from pure rotational patterns.

## Method

The SACM describes $^{22}$Ne as an $^{18}$O core plus an $\alpha$ particle, constructing a fully antisymmetrized model space by coupling the SU(3) representation of the core, $(\lambda_C,\mu_C)=(4,0)$, with the relative-motion representations $(n_\pi,0)$ for $n_\pi=8$ through $12$, then eliminating Pauli-forbidden states by matching to the SU(3) shell model space. A phenomenological Hamiltonian with dynamical SU(3) symmetry, containing harmonic oscillator, SU(3) Casimir, $K^2$, and rotational terms, is fitted to experimental energy levels, and E2 and E1 transition strengths are calculated using SU(3) tensor operators.

## Key numbers

- The oscillator constant for $A=22$ is $\hbar\omega=12.88$ MeV, determined from the formula $\hbar\omega=45A^{-1/3}-25A^{-2/3}$.
- Fitted Hamiltonian parameters (in MeV): $\chi_R=-0.1027$, $\chi=-0.1227$, $\theta_+=101.929$, $\theta_-=-57.713$, $\beta_0=18.862$.
- The ground-state band $8(8,2)0$ has calculated energies: $0^+$ at 0 MeV, $2^+$ at 0.865 MeV, $4^+$ at 2.882 MeV, $6^+$ at 6.053 MeV, $8^+$ at 10.377 MeV; experimental counterparts are $0^+(0)$, $2^+(1.275)$, $4^+(3.358)$, $(6^+)(6.310)$, $(8^+)(11.032)$ MeV.
- The $10(14,0)0$ highly deformed band has calculated $0^+$ at 6.230 MeV, matching the experimental $0^+_2$ at 6.234 MeV.
- The negative-parity $9(11,1)1$ band has calculated $1^-$ at 3.938 MeV, $2^-$ at 4.330 MeV, $3^-$ at 4.918 MeV; experimental counterparts are $1^-(7.051)$, $2^-(7.665)$, $(3)^-(8.376)$ MeV.
- The $9(9,2)2$ band has calculated $2^-$ at 7.194 MeV and $3^-$ at 7.922 MeV; experimental $2^-(5.146)$ and $3^-(5.910)$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SACM, previously applied to core+$\alpha$ systems in the $A=16$ to 20 region (Lévai, 1992; Cseh and Lévai, 1994), to the heavier $^{22}$Ne nucleus, and compares results directly with two microscopic approaches: the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (DAMD) of Kimura (2005) and the generator-coordinate method (GCM) of Descouvemont (1988, 2003). The SACM provides a simpler algebraic framework that still captures the essential cluster and shell-model features, enabling systematic studies of $\alpha$-clustering across the $sd$ shell and potentially connecting to cluster supersymmetry schemes (Lévai and Cseh, 2007).
