# Simultaneous description of high density QCD matter in heavy ion collisions and neutron star observations

> A combined constraint on the QCD equation of state, at high densities, from connecting neutron star observations to data from heavy ion reactions is presented. We use the Chiral Mean Field Model which can describe neutron star and iso-spin symmetric matter and allows the consistent calculation of the density and momentum dependent potentials of baryons which are then implemented in the UrQMD transport model. In contrast to previous studies, the same equation of state constrained from neutron star properties is also able to describe experimental observables in heavy ion reactions at the HADES experiment. Unlike many other approaches our results are not constraint to densities up to nuclear saturation or perturbative results which allows a continuous description of the equation of state over a large range in baryon density.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a single equation of state (EoS) from the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, constrained by neutron star mass-radius observations, can simultaneously describe heavy-ion collision data from the HADES experiment, provided the model's momentum-dependent baryon potentials are implemented consistently in transport simulations; the best-fit parametrization yields a critical endpoint at a much lower temperature (T_CEP = 16 MeV) than predicted by functional renormalization group methods.

## Method

The authors use the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model to generate five parametrizations of the QCD equation of state, each with different bare nucleon masses (m0 = 600, 675, or 759 MeV) and nuclear incompressibilities (K = 250–440 MeV). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for neutron star matter in beta-equilibrium, matching the CMF EoS to a DD2 crust EoS at 0.25 times nuclear saturation density. The density- and momentum-dependent baryon potentials from each CMF parametrization are then implemented in the UrQMD transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1.23A GeV beam energy, and the results are compared to HADES data on proton elliptic flow, strange hadron yields, and pion production.

## Key numbers

*   Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}; nuclear binding energy per baryon: E/A - m_N = -16 MeV.
*   Best-fit parametrization: bare mass m0 = 675 MeV, nuclear incompressibility K = 303 MeV, effective nucleon mass m*/m = 0.82, symmetry energy E_sym = 31 MeV, slope of symmetry energy L = 53 MeV.
*   Critical endpoint for the best-fit parametrization: T_CEP = 16 MeV, mu_CEP = 1410 MeV.
*   Slope parameter alpha for the centrality dependence of strange hadron yields: alpha = 1.41, in agreement with HADES, KaoS, and FOPI results.
*   Symmetry energy slope L for the five parametrizations ranges from 44 MeV to 73 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to combine neutron star and heavy-ion constraints, such as the speed-of-sound approach by Yao (2023), by implementing the full momentum-dependent potentials from the CMF model into the UrQMD transport code. The resulting best-fit EoS simultaneously satisfies the neutron star mass-radius constraints from binary merger observations (Altiparmak, 2022) and NICER (Miller, 2019; Riley, 2019) and describes HADES data on proton elliptic flow and strange hadron production. The predicted critical endpoint at very low temperature (16 MeV) contrasts sharply with predictions from Dyson-Schwinger and functional renormalization group approaches (Fischer, 2014; Fu, 2019; Gao, 2020), which place T_CEP between 80 and 140 MeV, raising questions about missing many-baryon interactions in the latter or missing aspects of the QCD transition in the CMF model. The framework enables future statistical inference combining data from multiple experiments and improved treatment of isospin-dependent observables.
