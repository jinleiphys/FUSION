# The covariant equations of motion of massive spinning particles in a background Yang-Mills field

> A strong classical color field, known as the glasma, is generated in the earliest stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and can significantly influence the momentum and spin dynamics of hard probes such as quarks and jets. Most existing studies based on the classical equations of motion in a background Yang-Mills field, such as Wong equations, may not capture the full range of effects, for example, they neglect the Stern-Gerlach force experienced by spinning particles in non-uniform glasma fields. Although several extensions of Wong equations have been proposed to include spin degrees of freedom, they generally fail to satisfy all the required conditions simultaneously, such as Lorentz covariance, allowance for an arbitrary chromomagnetic moment, and respect for the necessary physical constraints. In this work, we extend the framework of a relativistic classical spinning particle in an electromagnetic field to describe spin one-half quarks propagating in a background non-Abelian Yang-Mills field. By systematically applying the Dirac-Bergmann algorithm, we derive self-consistent equations of motion for the particle's coordinates, momenta, spin, and color charge that satisfy all these requirements. This formalism provides a more complete and physically relevant description for studying momentum diffusion and spin polarization phenomena of hard probes in the glasma.

## Key claim

The authors derive a self-consistent set of covariant equations of motion for massive spin-1/2 quarks in a background non-Abelian Yang-Mills field that simultaneously satisfy Lorentz covariance, allow for an arbitrary chromomagnetic moment, and preserve all necessary physical constraints, conditions that prior extensions of the Wong equations failed to meet simultaneously.

## Method

The authors apply the Dirac-Bergmann algorithm for constrained Hamiltonian systems to a phase space consisting of the particle's canonical coordinate, kinetic momentum, spin tensor (parametrized via canonical spin variables), and color charge. They impose the mass-shell condition modified by spin-field coupling, the Tulczyjew-Dixon spin supplementary condition, spin normalization constraints, and Casimir invariants for the color charge, then derive the Dirac brackets and equations of motion that preserve all constraints.

## Key numbers

- The spin normalization condition is \(S^{\mu\nu}S_{\mu\nu} = 8s^2\) with \(s = 1/2\) for quarks.
- For SU(\(N_c\)) in the fundamental representation, the quadratic Casimir eigenvalue is \(C_2 = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c)\) and the cubic Casimir eigenvalue is \(C_3 = (N_c^2 - 4)(N_c^2 - 1)/(4N_c^2)\).
- The matching condition for classical Casimirs to the fundamental representation gives \(Q_2 = (N_c^2 - 1)/2\) and \(Q_3 = (N_c^2 - 4)(N_c^2 - 1)/(4N_c)\).
- The on-shell constraint including spin is \(P^2 - 2\mu g q^a F^a_{\mu\nu} \omega^\mu \pi^\nu - m^2 = 0\), where \(\mu\) is the chromomagnetic moment.

## Context

This work extends the constrained Hamiltonian framework for spinning particles in electromagnetic backgrounds developed by Deriglazov (2013, 2014) to the non-Abelian case, addressing shortcomings of prior approaches: the Grassmann-variable formulations of Balachandran (1976) and Mueller (2019) cannot describe spin at the semiclassical level, while the formulations of Heinz (1984) and Yang (2021) cannot accommodate an arbitrary chromomagnetic moment while preserving all physical constraints. The derived equations provide a unified basis for studying momentum diffusion and spin polarization of hard probes (heavy quarks and jets) in the glasma fields generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
