# Leptonic and semileptonic decays of heavy mesons

> A heavy-quark limit of the Dyson-Schwinger equations is introduced and used in an exploratory study of leptonic and semileptonic decays of heavy mesons. The application of this framework to heavy-to-light semileptonic decays, which require a good knowledge of light-quark propagation characteristics and light-meson bound state amplitudes, is illustrated.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a heavy-quark limit of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) and demonstrates that this framework can describe leptonic and semileptonic decays of heavy mesons, but only as an exploratory step because representing the charm-quark propagator in the same simplified form as the bottom-quark propagator is at best a first approximation.

## Method

The authors define a heavy-quark limit by approximating the dressed heavy-quark propagator (for b and c quarks) as a free-particle propagator with a Euclidean constituent-quark mass, and they employ an Ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude of the heavy meson. They then compute leptonic decay constants (f_D, f_B) and the Isgur-Wise function ξ(w) for the semileptonic decay B^0 → D^- ℓ ν by fitting two parameters (binding energy E and width Λ of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude) to experimental data on ξ(w) and lattice-QCD results for f_D and f_B. The light-quark propagators are taken from a previous DSE-based parameterization (Burden, Roberts and Thomson, 1996) that was fit to light-hadron observables.

## Key numbers

- f_D = 0.227 GeV (for forms A, B, C) and 0.272 GeV (form D), compared to the lattice-QCD average of 0.216 ± 0.015 GeV used as input.
- f_B = 0.135 GeV (for forms A, B, C) and 0.162 GeV (form D), compared to the lattice-QCD average of 0.206 ± 0.030 GeV used as input.
- f_{D_s}/f_D = f_{B_s}/f_B ≈ 1.07, compared to the heavy-quark limit expectations of √(M_D/M_{D_s}) = 0.97 and √(M_B/M_{B_s}) = 0.99.
- ρ² (slope of ξ(w) at w=1) = 1.55 (forms A, B, C) and 1.21 (form D), compared to the experimental parametrization ρ² = 1.53 ± 0.36 ± 0.14 from CLEO (1996).
- For the B → π e ν decay using φ_A with E=0.47 GeV and Λ=1.1 GeV: f_+(0)=0.48, giving a branching ratio of 2.3 × 10⁻⁴, compared to the experimental value (1.8 ± 0.4 ± 0.3 ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁴ from CLEO (1996).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to heavy mesons by introducing a heavy-quark limit, building on prior DSE studies of light quarks by Maris and Roberts (1997) and Burden, Roberts and Thomson (1996). The framework enables simultaneous calculation of leptonic and semileptonic decay form factors at all q², including heavy-to-light decays that are inaccessible in heavy-quark effective theory and require detailed knowledge of light-quark propagation and light-meson bound-state amplitudes. The authors note that a uniformly good description of all decays requires refitting the two parameters E and Λ.
