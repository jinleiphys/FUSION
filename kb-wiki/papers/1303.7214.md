# Glasma Evolution and Bose-Einstein Condensation with Elastic and Inelastic Collisions

> In this paper we investigate the role of inelastic collisions in the kinetic evolution of a highly overpopulated gluon system starting from Glasma-type initial condition. Using the Gunion-Bertsch formula we derive the inelastic collision kernel under the collinear and small angle approximations. With both numerics and analytic analysis, we show that the inelastic process has two effects: globally changing (mostly reducing) the total particle number, while locally at small momentum regime always filling up the infrared modes extremely quickly. This latter effect is found to significantly speed up the emergence of a local thermal distribution in the infrared regime with vanishing local "chemical potential" and thus catalyze the onset of dynamical Bose-Einstein Condensation to occur faster (as compared with the purely elastic case) in the overpopulated Glasma.

## Key claim

The authors show that inelastic collisions, while globally reducing total particle number, locally fill up infrared modes extremely quickly, thereby speeding up the emergence of a local thermal distribution with vanishing chemical potential and catalyzing the onset of dynamical Bose-Einstein condensation faster than in the purely elastic case, even though inelastic processes alone would prevent condensation in final equilibrium.

## Method

The authors derived the inelastic collision kernel for 2↔3 gluon scatterings using the Gunion-Bertsch formula under collinear and small-angle approximations, then combined it with the previously known elastic 2↔2 kernel. They numerically solved the resulting kinetic equation for a static, isotropic box with a Glasma-type initial condition (step function at saturation scale Q_s) and compared the evolution with and without inelastic processes.

## Key numbers

* Initial occupation f_0 = 1 (overpopulated regime, above critical f_0^c ≈ 0.154) with saturation scale Q_s as the momentum and energy unit and 1/Q_s as the time unit.
* For purely inelastic evolution, the system thermalizes to a Bose-Einstein distribution with vanishing chemical potential after approximately Q_s t ≈ 5.
* The elastic collision rate scales as ∼ Q_s and the inelastic rate also scales as ∼ Q_s in the overpopulated Glasma case (f ∼ 1/α_s), showing they are parametrically at the same order.
* The overpopulation parameter is n ε^{-3/4} = f_0^{1/4} · 2^{5/4} / (3 π^{1/2}), with overpopulation occurring when f_0 > 0.154.

## Context

This work extends the elastic-only kinetic evolution scenario for Glasma thermalization proposed by Blaizot, Liao, and McLerran (2011-2012), which showed that high initial overpopulation drives dynamical Bose-Einstein condensation via elastic collisions. The present paper addresses the previously unexamined role of inelastic processes, which are parametrically as important as elastic ones in the overpopulated regime. The results enable future studies of expanding systems and the post-condensation evolution using a modified kinetic framework that couples condensate and particles.
