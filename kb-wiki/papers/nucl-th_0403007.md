# Effect of hard processes on momentum correlations

> The effect of hard processes to be encountered in HBT studies at the Large Hadron Collider have been studied. A simple simulation has allowed us to generate momentum correlations involving jet particles as well as particles originating from the kinetic freeze out and to compare them to a simple theoretical model which has been developed. The first results on the effect of hard processes on the correlation function for the case of jet quenching are presented.

## Key claim

Hard processes (jet fragmentation) will imprint characteristic non-Gaussian structures on two-particle momentum correlation functions at LHC energies, but these signatures are only visible when the fraction of pions from jets is sufficiently high or when a high transverse momentum cut is applied; the effect is diluted or invisible when the thermal background dominates.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA to simulate p+p collisions at √s = 5.5 TeV, extracted jets within the ALICE pseudorapidity coverage (-1 < η < 1) using a simple clustering algorithm, and selected jets with total transverse momentum between 5 and 10 GeV. They modeled the spatial distribution of jet particles as a Gaussian of width J, placed jets either on the surface of a cylinder (to simulate jet quenching) or with a Gaussian transverse profile (non-quenching case), and added a thermal background source with an exponential transverse momentum spectrum and a Gaussian spatial distribution whose size decreases linearly with p_t. Correlation functions were constructed in the out-side-long coordinate frame using the Lednický weighting algorithm within the ALICE HBT analysis package HBTAN.

## Key numbers

- PYTHIA simulation at √s = 5.5 TeV for p+p collisions
- Jet selection: sum of p_t of all particles in the jet between 5 and 10 GeV
- Cylinder geometry for jet quenching model: radius R = 6 fm, surface thickness δR = 1 fm, length L = 1 fm or L = 2 fm
- Jet radius J = 1 fm
- Background source parameters: T = 210 MeV, B_0 = 10 fm, B_max = 1 fm, p_t^max = 1.2 GeV
- For 100 jets from a cylinder surface with L = 2 fm, fit to theoretical curve gave: R = 5.92 fm, J = 1.06 fm, L = 1.82 fm, Φ = 0.86, λ = 0.77
- Estimated pion yield at ALICE midrapidity: dN/dy ≈ 3000 charged particles, ~90% pions → ~1350 single-charge pions; average 2.4 positive pions per jet in acceptance → 100 jets give ~120 single-charge pions per pseudorapidity unit → jet pion fraction ~120/1350 ≈ 1/11
- When jet pion fraction dropped to 1/6, the p_t-integrated correlation function was completely dominated by background
- High-p_t cut applied: K_t > 1.2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends HBT (Hanbury Brown-Twiss) correlation studies from lower energies (AGS, SPS, RHIC) to predictions for the LHC, addressing the "HBT puzzle" observed at RHIC where interpretation of correlation data has been problematic. The authors provide a toy model and analytical calculations for correlation functions in the presence of jets, building on the standard formalism of Lednický (2001) for correlation function weighting. The results enable future experimental searches for jet-induced non-Gaussian features in correlation functions at ALICE, and suggest that looking at the large-q region beyond the main peak is important for characterizing the source.
