# Momentum dependence of drag coefficients and heavy flavour suppression in quark gluon plasma

> The momentum dependence of the drag coefficient of heavy quarks propagating through quark gluon plasma (QGP) has been evaluated. The results have been used to estimate the nuclear suppression factor of charm and bottom quarks in QGP. We observe that the momentum dependence of the transport coefficients plays crucial role in the suppression of the heavy quarks and consequently in discerning the properties of QGP using heavy flavours as a probe. We show that the large suppression of the heavy quarks observed at RHIC and LHC is predominantly due to the radiative losses. The suppression of $D^0$ in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy - recently measured by the ALICE collaboration has also been studied.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the momentum dependence of the drag coefficient for heavy quarks in quark gluon plasma (QGP) is non-negligible and crucial for reproducing the experimental nuclear suppression factor; the abstract's claim that "large suppression ... is predominantly due to radiative losses" is conditioned on the finding that using a momentum-independent drag (the low-momentum value) overestimates collisional suppression by a factor of about 2.5, and only when the full momentum-dependent drag is used does the radiative contribution become dominant.

## Method

The authors solved the Fokker-Planck equation for the momentum distribution of charm and bottom quarks evolving in an expanding QGP background, using a Bjorken boost-invariant hydrodynamic model for the medium. The drag coefficient was computed from both collisional (elastic scattering with thermal partons) and radiative (gluon bremsstrahlung) processes, with the radiative calculation including dead-cone and LPM effects. The initial heavy quark distributions were taken from the MNR (Mangano-Nason-Ridolfi) perturbative QCD code, and the final hadron spectra were obtained via Peterson fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

*   Drag coefficient for charm due to collisional processes: 0.036 fm\(^{-1}\) at \(p=5\) GeV, reducing to 0.018 fm\(^{-1}\) at \(p=10\) GeV (at \(T=300\) MeV).
*   Initial temperature and thermalization time for RHIC: \(T_i = 300\) MeV, \(\tau_i = 0.5\) fm/c.
*   Initial temperature and thermalization time for LHC: \(T_i = 550\) MeV, \(\tau_i = 0.1\) fm/c.
*   Transition temperature \(T_c\) taken as 175 MeV.
*   Collisional suppression of charm quarks: about 50% with momentum-independent drag, reducing to about 20% with momentum-dependent drag; inclusion of radiative processes increases suppression to about 75%.
*   Average change due to Bose enhancement and Pauli blocking effects on the drag is about 6% for \(p < 5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark energy loss in QGP (Bjorken 1982, Svetitsky 1988, Moore and Teaney 2005) by explicitly incorporating the momentum dependence of the drag coefficient, which previous calculations often neglected or approximated with its low-momentum value. The results enable more precise extraction of QGP properties from experimental nuclear suppression factor (\(R_{AA}\)) data for \(D\) and \(B\) mesons at RHIC and LHC, and the authors show their formalism reproduces the ALICE collaboration's \(D^0\) suppression data in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
