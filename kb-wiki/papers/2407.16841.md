# Ab initio Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory: closed-form constraint on the average particle number

> Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT) relying on the breaking of U(1) global gauge symmetry has been recently formulated and applied to extend the applicability of standard perturbation theory to ab initio calculations of atomic nuclei away from shell closures. So far, practical applications have been limited to second-order calculations due to the lack of a generic algorithm to constrain the average particle number of the symmetry-broken state. This limitation is presently lifted and a general BMBPT formalism is presented that allows to constrain the particle-number expectation value at arbitrary order P. The constraint can be incorporated in closed form by solving a polynomial equation of degree P-1. The numerical procedure is illustrated through BMBPT(3) calculations of calcium isotopes using a nuclear Hamiltonian derived within chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT) formalism that constrains the average particle number at arbitrary truncation order \(P\) by solving a polynomial equation of degree \(P-1\), lifting the previous limitation that practical applications were restricted to second-order calculations. The constraint is incorporated in closed form without iterative adjustment of the reference state, but for third-order calculations the polynomial may lack real solutions in certain isotopes (e.g., \(^{42}\)Ca and \(^{56}\)Ca) when using the traditional partitioning, requiring a global shift \(\Delta_E\) added to the quasi-particle energies.

## Method

The authors formulate BMBPT with an order-dependent chemical potential \(\lambda^{(P)}\) such that the grand potential \(\Omega^{(P)} = H - \lambda^{(P)} A\) is partitioned into an unperturbed part \(\Omega_0^{(P)}\) and a residual part \(\Omega_1^{(P)}\). The particle-number constraint at order \(P\) is enforced by requiring that the shift \(\Delta A^{[P]} = A^{[P]} - A^{[1]}\) equals zero, which yields a polynomial equation of degree \(P-1\) in the chemical-potential shift \(\Delta\lambda^{(P)}\). For third-order calculations, the quadratic equation is solved after adding a global shift \(\Delta_E\) to the quasi-particle energies entering \(\bar{\Omega}^{11}\), with the physically-preferred root selected as the one closest to zero and the optimal \(\Delta_E\) chosen via a stationarity condition on the binding energy. The method is applied to calcium isotopes using a chiral N\(^3\)LO nucleon-nucleon interaction evolved to \(\lambda_{\text{SRG}} = 1.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and a N\(^2\)LO three-nucleon interaction with cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), with the three-body force reduced to an effective two-body operator.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{42}\)Ca, the quadratic polynomial for \(\Delta\lambda^{(3)}\) has no real solution at \(\Delta_E = 0\); a real root first appears for \(\Delta_E \geq 0.5\ \text{MeV}\), and the binding energy minimum occurs at \(\Delta_E \approx 1.4\ \text{MeV}\).
- Unconstrained BMBPT(3)* calculations show particle-number shifts of \(\Delta A \approx 4\)–\(6\) neutrons in \(^{38}\)Ca, \(^{42}\)Ca, \(^{44}\)Ca, and \(^{46}\)Ca, translating into up to \(70\ \text{MeV}\) of extra binding.
- Constrained BMBPT(3) gives an attractive correction of order \(10\%\) of the BMBPT(2) correlation energy across the calcium chain.
- Between \(^{34}\)Ca and \(^{60}\)Ca, only \(^{42}\)Ca and \(^{56}\)Ca lack a real solution for \(\Delta\lambda^{(3)}\) at \(\Delta_E = 0\).
- The HFB reference state captures 60–70% of the total binding energy throughout the calcium chain.

## Context

This work extends the BMBPT formalism of Duguet (2015) and Arthuis (2018) by providing a closed-form particle-number constraint that replaces the costly iterative adjustment or approximate a posteriori correction proposed by Demol (2020). The method enables consistent third-order BMBPT calculations for open-shell nuclei, which were previously limited to second order. The results are compared to Bogoliubov coupled cluster (BCCSD) calculations from Tichai (2023) and valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations from Stroberg (2019); the constrained BMBPT(3) binding energies follow the VS-IMSRG(2) and BCCSD trends, but two-neutron separation energies near shell closures (e.g., \(N=20,28\)) deteriorate, motivating future work on particle-number-projected BMBPT (PBMBPT) as outlined by Duguet (2015).
