# The viscosity of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and the LHC

> The specific shear viscosity (eta/s)_QGP of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can be extracted from elliptic flow data in heavy-ion collisions by comparing them with the dynamical model VISHNU which couples a viscous fluid dynamic description of the QGP with a microscopic kinetic description of the late hadronic rescattering and freeze-out stage. A robust method for fixing (eta/s)_QGP from the collision centrality dependence of the eccentricity-scaled charged hadron elliptic flow is presented. The systematic uncertainties associated with this extraction method are discussed, with specific attention to our presently restricted knowledge of initial conditions. With the (eta/s)_QGP extracted in this way, VISHNU yields an excellent description of all soft-hadron data from Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy. Extrapolations to Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, using both a purely hydrodynamic approach and VISHNU, are presented and compared with recent experimental results from the ALICE Collaboration. The LHC data are again well described by VISHNU, with the same (eta/s)_QGP value as at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The specific shear viscosity of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can be extracted from elliptic flow data, but the extracted value changes by more than a factor of 2 depending on which initial-state model (MC-Glauber versus MC-KLN) is used to provide the fireball ellipticity, so the viscosity cannot be determined to better than a factor of 2 from elliptic flow alone without constraining the initial ellipticity more precisely.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid code VISHNU, which couples (2+1)-dimensional viscous Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) for the QGP phase with the microscopic kinetic transport model UrQMD for the late hadronic rescattering and freeze-out stage. They extracted the specific shear viscosity by comparing the centrality dependence of the eccentricity-scaled charged hadron elliptic flow from VISHNU simulations against experimental data from RHIC (200 A GeV Au+Au) and the LHC (2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb), using both MC-Glauber and MC-KLN initial condition models.

## Key numbers

- For MC-Glauber initial conditions at RHIC, the extracted specific shear viscosity is (eta/s)_QGP = 0.08 (1/(4pi)).
- For MC-KLN initial conditions at RHIC, the extracted specific shear viscosity is (eta/s)_QGP = 0.16 (2/(4pi)).
- The range of extracted values gives 1 < 4pi(eta/s)_QGP < 2.5 for temperatures between T_c and 2T_c probed at RHIC.
- At the LHC, a straightforward extrapolation with fixed (eta/s)_QGP = 0.16 overpredicts the charged hadron elliptic flow by 10-15%; increasing to 0.20 gives better agreement with ALICE data.
- Changing (eta/s)_QGP by 0.08 destroys the agreement between theory and data for identified hadron elliptic flow at RHIC.
- Event-by-event evolution is expected to reduce extracted (eta/s)_QGP values by no more than (0.2-0.3)/(4pi).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid hydrodynamics+kinetic approach of Song (2010) to simultaneously describe soft hadron spectra and elliptic flow at both RHIC and LHC energies, using the VISHNU code. The paper resolves a failure of purely hydrodynamic models (VISH2+1 with constant eta/s=0.2) that incorrectly predicted the centrality dependence of proton elliptic flow, particularly in central collisions. The results enable future simultaneous analyses of elliptic and triangular flow (as suggested by Shen (2011), Qiu (2011), and Adare (2011)) to resolve the ambiguity between initial-state models, and provide a framework for testing whether the QGP viscosity changes with temperature.
