# Spectral sum rules for the quark-gluon plasma

> We derive sum rules involving the spectral density of the stress-energy tensor in N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory and pure Yang-Mills theory. The sum rules come from the hydrodynamic behavior at small momenta and the conformal (in the case of N=4 SYM theory) or asymptotically free (as for the pure Yang-Mills theory) behavior at large momenta. These sum rules may help constrain QGP transport coefficients obtained from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The authors derive spectral sum rules for the stress-energy tensor in hot gauge theories that relate integrals of the spectral density to thermodynamic and hydrodynamic quantities, with the caveat that the bulk sum rule differs from earlier work by Kharzeev and Tuchin (2007) and Karsch, Kharzeev, and Tuchin (2007) due to a subtle non-commutativity of limits.

## Method

The authors use Kramers-Kronig relations for the retarded Green function of the stress-energy tensor, subtracting zero-temperature contributions and constant pieces to ensure convergence. For N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling, they compute the required asymptotic values using AdS/CFT duality and operator product expansion methods, while for pure Yang-Mills theory they use perturbative calculations and hydrodynamic relations. They verify the sum rules numerically by solving the mode equation for metric perturbations in the AdS black hole background using a leapfrog integration algorithm.

## Key numbers

- For N=4 SYM at strong coupling, the shear sum rule gives 2/5 epsilon = (2/pi) integral (d omega/omega) [rho(omega) - rho_{T=0}(omega)], where epsilon is the energy density
- The second shear sum rule gives eta tau_pi - (1/2) kappa = (2/pi) integral (d omega/omega^3) [rho(omega) - rho_{T=0}(omega) - eta omega], where eta is shear viscosity, tau_pi is relaxation time, and kappa is a second-order hydrodynamic coefficient
- Numerical verification of the first shear sum rule achieved agreement to 0.1500008(44) compared to the analytic value 0.15 (five-digit accuracy)
- Numerical verification of the second shear sum rule achieved agreement to 0.30686(2) compared to the analytic value 1-log(2) approximately 0.306853 (four-digit accuracy)
- For pure Yang-Mills theory, the bulk sum rule takes the form 3(epsilon+P)(1-3c_s^2)-4(epsilon-3P) = (2/pi) integral (d omega/omega) [rho^{bulk}(omega) - rho^{bulk}_{T=0}(omega)], where c_s is the speed of sound

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kharzeev and Tuchin (2007) and Karsch, Kharzeev, and Tuchin (2007), who derived a sum rule for the trace of the stress-energy tensor, but corrects the form of the bulk sum rule due to the non-commutativity of limits. The sum rules provide constraints on spectral densities that can help extract transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma from lattice QCD calculations, which currently rely on reconstructing real-time spectral functions from Euclidean correlation functions through fitting ansatz forms. The results also connect to earlier work by Teaney (2006) on sum rules for weakly coupled relativistic theories and by Baier et al. (2007) on second-order hydrodynamic coefficients.
