# On the electrodisintegration of the deuteron in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism

> The (ed -> enp) process in the frame of the Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable kernel of the Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) interaction was considered. This conception keeps the covariance of description of the process. Special attention was devoted to a contribution of the D-states of the deuteron in the cross section of the electrodisintegration. It was shown that the spectator particle (neutron) plays an important role. The factorization of a cross section of this reaction in the impulse approximation was checked by analytical and numerical calculations.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable Graz II kernel can describe the electrodisintegration of the deuteron in a covariant manner, but the factorization of the cross section in the impulse approximation holds only when the neutron is treated as a spectator, the final-state interaction is neglected, and only positive-energy deuteron states (³S₁⁺ and ³D₁⁺) are included; the inclusion of P-waves would destroy the factorization.

## Method

The authors used the Bethe-Salpeter formalism with a separable Graz II rank III kernel of the nucleon-nucleon interaction to calculate the exclusive differential cross section of the (ed → enp) reaction in the plane-wave relativistic impulse approximation. The hadron electromagnetic current was constructed using the Mandelstam technique with one-body currents only, and the final np-pair was described by a plane wave with both nucleons on the mass shell. The cross section was computed for eight sets of experimental kinematic conditions from Saclay and Bonn experiments, with the deuteron amplitude expanded into positive-energy ³S₁⁺ and ³D₁⁺ partial waves.

## Key numbers

- The spectator neutron contribution to the cross section reaches 50% in the Saclay_III kinematic range (from the experiment of Turck (1984)).
- The Graz II kernel parameters include: γ₁ = 28.69550 GeV⁻², γ₂ = 64.9803 GeV⁻², β₁₁ = 0.231384 GeV, β₁₂ = 0.521705 GeV, β₂₁ = 0.794907 GeV, β₂₂ = 0.157512 GeV.
- The coupling constants for the rank III kernel are: λ₁₁ = 2.718930 × 10⁻⁴ GeV⁶, λ₁₂ = -7.16735 × 10⁻² GeV⁴, λ₁₃ = -1.51744 × 10⁻³ GeV⁶, λ₂₂ = 16.52393 GeV², λ₂₃ = 0.28606 GeV⁴, λ₃₃ = 3.48589 × 10⁻³ GeV⁶.
- The D-state probability in the deuteron was varied between 4% and 6% to study its effect on the cross section.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied to the deuteron by Burov (2005) and others, using the separable Graz II interaction kernel. It contrasts with nonrelativistic treatments and earlier relativistic calculations that did not fully account for the spectator neutron contribution. The results enable future inclusion of final-state interactions, negative-energy states, and P-wave contributions, as well as calculations of asymmetries in the (ed → enp) process that could provide new information about deuteron structure.
