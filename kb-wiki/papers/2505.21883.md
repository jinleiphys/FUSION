# Spin polarization of holographic baryon in strongly coupled fluid

> The spin polarization for baryon in a hydrodynamic medium has been extensively studied in the weakly coupled regime using quantum kinetic theory. As a first study of this problem in the strongly coupled regime, we investigate holographically the spectral function of a probe baryon in the fluid-gravity background. This is done by carefully performing gradient expansion of the Dirac equation in the fluid-gravity background. Different contributions in the expansion are understood in terms of density matrices of the probe baryon and the medium. The resulting spectral functions indicate that the holographic baryonic is polarized as responses to fluid acceleration, shear stress and vorticity. The structures of the responses are similar to those found in weakly coupled studies.

## Key claim
This paper provides the first study of baryon spin polarization in the strongly coupled regime using holography, finding that the spectral function of a probe baryon in a fluid-gravity background shows polarization responses to fluid acceleration, shear stress, and vorticity, with structures similar to those found in weakly coupled studies.

## Method
The authors performed a gradient expansion of the Dirac equation for a probe baryon in the fluid-gravity background, modeling the baryon as a bulk Dirac fermion in a bottom-up holographic approach and the medium as a fluid-gravity background. They solved the resulting equations for the retarded correlator and spectral function up to first order in gradients, separating contributions from the local equilibrium state and the steady state deviation. The calculation used the gauge-gravity duality (Maldacena 1997, Aharony 1999, Witten 1998) and followed the holographic prescription for fermionic operators established by Henningson (1998), Mueck (1998), and Henneaux (1998).

## Key numbers
- The particle spectral function ρ₊ is plotted versus ω for p = 0.9, with the unit set by πT = 1, showing a softened singularity compared to the vacuum spectral function ρ₊ = 2 sgn(ω) θ(ω² − p²) ((ω + p)/(ω − p))^(1/2) from Iqbal (2009).
- The calculation uses a probe baryon with mass m = 0, combining two Weyl fermions of opposite helicities.

## Context
This work extends studies of spin polarization in the weakly coupled regime using quantum kinetic theory (QKT), as reviewed by Hidaka (2022), to the strongly coupled regime where quasi-particles may not exist. It provides a first holographic example of the steady state contribution to the spectral function, complementing the local equilibrium state analysis by Lin (2024) in the weakly coupled case. The results enable future studies of spin polarization in strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma using gauge-gravity duality, with potential applications to understanding local polarization measurements in heavy ion collisions.
