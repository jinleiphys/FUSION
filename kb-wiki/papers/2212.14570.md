# Spin-1 quarkonia in a rotating frame and their spin contents

> We propose a new way of studying the spin content of a hadron by looking at its response in a rotating frame. By collecting all responses of quarks and gluons in a rotating frame, we describe the spin-rotation coupling of spin-1 quarkonia and thereby reveal their spin contents in a relativistic formalism. We demonstrate that both the perturbative and non-perturbative contributions in the operator product expansion follow a universal formula that identifies the spin-rotation coupling with unit strength. This allows us to recognize the total spin-1 of the vector and axial vector quarkonia in terms of the total angular momentum of quarks and gluons. Specifically, we find the spin contents of $J/ψ$, $χ_{c1}$, $Υ(1S)$, and $χ_{b1}$ are slightly different from the naive quark model picture. For example, the $J/ψ$ is traditionally considered as an S-wave particle, but we find quarks do not carry all of the total spin.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the spin content of a hadron can be studied by observing its response in a rotating frame, and demonstrates that the spin-rotation coupling strength \(g_\Omega\) equals 1 for spin-1 quarkonia, meaning the total spin of the system equals the total angular momentum of its constituent quarks and gluons. However, the abstract's claim that spin contents are "slightly different from the naive quark model picture" is qualified by the finding that for vector quarkonia like \(J/\psi\), the quark spin contribution is 0.88 rather than 1, while for axial vector states the orbital angular momentum dominates.

## Method

The authors compute the two-point correlation function for vector and axial-vector currents in a rotating frame using the operator product expansion (OPE), including both leading perturbative diagrams and non-perturbative gluon condensate contributions up to dimension 4 operators. They derive a universal formula relating the rotating-frame part of the OPE to the inertial-frame part, and extract the spin-rotation coupling strength \(g_\Omega\) by comparing the OPE result with a phenomenological expression. Using QCD sum rules with Borel transformation, they decompose \(g_\Omega\) into contributions from the quark spin operator \(\hat{S}_q\), the kinetic and potential parts of the orbital angular momentum (\(\hat{L}_k\) and \(\hat{L}_p\)), and the gluon total angular momentum \(\hat{J}_g\), then evaluate these for specific quarkonia states.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-rotation coupling from spin-1/2 Dirac particles, where the coupling was derived from the Dirac equation in a rotating frame (Mashhoon, 1988; de Oliveira and Tiomno, 1962), to massive spin-1 particles composed of quarks and gluons. It addresses a discrepancy found by Kapusta et al. (2020), who derived from the Proca equation that the spin-rotation coupling strength was 1/2 rather than 1. The method enables quantitative analysis of hadron spin contents using QCD sum rules, analogous to the proton spin crisis studies initiated by the European Muon Collaboration (1987), and provides a framework applicable to more complex hadrons including nucleons for future Electron-Ion Collider experiments.
