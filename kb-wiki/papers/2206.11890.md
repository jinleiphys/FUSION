# Tensor polarization and the dissipative damping of vector meson in QCD Medium

> Unexpectedly large and puzzling spin alignment, and thus tensor polarization, of vector mesons has been observed in heavy-ion collisions. Given that tensor polarization represents a fluctuation of spin, we derive, for the first time, a fluctuation-dissipation relation for tensor polarization, where the polarization is proportional to first-order hydrodynamic gradients (e.g., the shear-stress tensor), with dissipative coefficients depending on the particle's damping properties, as characterized by its spectral function. Employing relativistic hydrodynamics at finite density, we find that dissipative contributions can generate substantial spin alignment. We provide illustrative examples (by tuning one coefficients $α_{\rm sh}$) that generate a beam energy, $p_T$, and centrality dependence of spin alignment resembling those observed in experiments, offering insights into these puzzling phenomena and demonstrating its potential as a ``spectrometer" for in-medium vector mesons.

## Key claim

The authors derive, for the first time, a fluctuation-dissipation relation for tensor polarization of vector mesons in a QCD medium, showing that spin alignment is proportional to first-order hydrodynamic gradients (e.g., shear-stress tensor) with dissipative coefficients that depend on the particle's in-medium damping properties. The abstract glosses over the condition that the illustrative examples reproducing experimental trends require tuning one coefficient α_sh, and that the results are qualitative rather than a precise data description.

## Method

The authors develop a "Zubarev response approach" rooted in thermal field theory to systematically calculate dissipative and non-dissipative contributions to tensor polarization on equal footing. They employ relativistic hydrodynamics at finite density using the CLVisc hydrodynamic code with AMPT initial conditions for Au-Au collisions, and evaluate spin alignment via a Cooper-Fry-like freezeout formula. The dissipative coefficient α_sh is computed from the in-medium spectral function of the vector meson, using either physically motivated parametrizations or a 1-loop quark-meson model for the φ meson.

## Key numbers

- The dissipative coefficient α_sh can reach values as large as "1" and as small as "-0.8" for mass shifts ranging from -30 to 50 MeV with Γ₀ = 0.1 GeV.
- Spin alignment magnitudes range from -2% to 4%, which is orders of magnitude larger than previous predictions.
- Using an ideal Bjorken hydrodynamic solution with α_sh ~ 1 and τ ~ 6 fm yields ρ₀₀ ~ 6%.
- For the quark-meson model, α_sh coefficients are evaluated with effective strange quark masses m_s = {0.3, 0.42, 0.5} GeV.
- The hadronic phase lifetime drops from 7 fm (0% centrality) to 2 fm (60% centrality) at top RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions beyond previous non-dissipative relations linking spin alignment to vorticity (Becattini 2007) or strong electromagnetic fields (Sheng 2019, 2022). The authors establish a fluctuation-dissipation connection that was previously unexplored in this context, and their shear-induced tensor polarization (SITP) effect can generate spin alignment magnitudes comparable to the unexpectedly large values observed by STAR (2022) and ALICE (2019, 2022). This enables using spin alignment as a "spectrometer" to extract in-medium spectral properties of vector mesons, such as mass shifts and widths, which are otherwise difficult to measure directly from invariant mass spectra.
