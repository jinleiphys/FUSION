# Bound states in Yukawa theory

> A generalization of the Gell-Mann-Low Theorem is applied to bound state calculations in Yukawa theory. The resulting effective Schroedinger equation is solved numerically for two-fermion bound states with the exchange of a massless boson. The complete low-lying bound state spectrum is obtained for different ratios of the constituent masses. No abnormal solutions are found. We show the consistency of the non-relativistic and one-body limits and discuss the special cases of identical fermions and fermion-antifermion states. To our knowledge, this is the first consistent calculation of bound states in pure Yukawa theory (without UV cutoff).

## Key claim

The authors claim that a generalization of the Gell-Mann–Low Theorem yields a consistent, cutoff-free calculation of two-fermion bound states in pure Yukawa theory (scalar boson exchange between spin-1/2 fermions) to lowest non-trivial order, but only for the case of a massless exchanged boson and for fine structure constants between zero and one.

## Method

The generalized Gell-Mann–Low Theorem is used to construct a Bloch-Wilson effective Hamiltonian that maps the free two-fermion subspace to the exact interacting subspace via an adiabatic evolution operator. The effective Hamiltonian is expanded to second order in the coupling constant, yielding an effective Schroedinger equation with a non-local potential derived from one-boson exchange. This equation is solved numerically in the center-of-mass frame for two-fermion bound states with a massless scalar boson, for various ratios of the constituent fermion masses.

## Key numbers

* The eight lowest-lying bound states are calculated for fine structure constants between zero and one, including states with non-zero relative orbital angular momentum and mixtures of spin singlet and triplet states.
* The calculation covers different ratios of the constituent masses, with the non-relativistic limit (Coulomb potential) used as a benchmark for comparison.
* No abnormal solutions are found in the entire parameter range studied.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the generalized Gell-Mann–Low Theorem to a purely scalar model (Weber and Ligterink, 2002) and addresses a long-standing problem in relativistic bound state theory. Prior attempts using the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation for Yukawa theory were known to produce a continuum of coupling constants rather than a discrete spectrum (Goldstein, 1953; Green, 1955), and all previous numerical calculations in one-boson exchange models required a momentum cutoff. The present formalism enables consistent bound state calculations without a UV cutoff, and the authors plan to extract the lowest-order fine and hyperfine structure from the effective Hamiltonian analytically in the massless (Coulomb) case.
