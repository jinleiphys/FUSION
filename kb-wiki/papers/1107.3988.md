# Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory in a finite volume: scalar meson sector

> We develop a scheme for the extraction of the properties of the scalar mesons f0(600), f0(980), and a0(980) from lattice QCD data. This scheme is based on a two-channel chiral unitary approach with fully relativistic propagators in a finite volume. In order to discuss the feasibility of finding the mass and width of the scalar resonances, we analyze synthetic lattice data with a fixed error assigned, and show that the framework can be indeed used for an accurate determination of resonance pole positions in the multi-channel scattering.

## Key claim

The authors develop a framework based on unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UCHPT) in a finite volume that can extract the pole positions of scalar mesons f0(600), f0(980), and a0(980) from lattice QCD data, but only if the analysis incorporates prior phenomenological knowledge from UCHPT; the avoided level crossing near 1 GeV is shown to be caused by the K̅K threshold rather than the f0(980) resonance itself, meaning that periodic boundary conditions alone cannot reliably distinguish threshold effects from resonance signals.

## Method

The authors place a two-channel chiral unitary model (ππ and K̅K for I=0; πη and K̅K for I=1) into a finite cubic box by replacing the continuous loop integral G with a discrete sum over quantized momenta G̃, using fully relativistic propagators with a smooth cutoff form factor. They generate synthetic lattice data by computing energy levels as functions of box size L and twisting angle θ, then fit the parameters of the chiral potential to these synthetic data to test whether the original resonance pole positions can be recovered. The analysis compares periodic boundary conditions, twisted boundary conditions for the s-quark, and asymmetric lattice geometries as methods to overcome the underdetermination of multi-channel scattering.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff value q_max = 904 MeV used for regularization of the loop function in the infinite-volume UCHPT model
- Smooth cutoff form factor parameters: a = 1.2 GeV, m = 12, with the form factor differing from unity by less than 1 permil for all energies considered
- The f0(980) resonance disappears when the K̅K → K̅K potential strength is reduced to η ≈ 0.8 (i.e., V₁₁ → ηV₁₁ with η = 0.8)
- The UCHPT model reproduces ππ and K̅K scattering data up to √s ≈ 1200 MeV
- Twisted boundary conditions with θ = 0, π/2, and π produce spectra that are strongly sensitive to twisting at energies around 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Lüscher framework (Lüscher, 1991) from elastic scattering to multi-channel scattering for scalar mesons, building on prior generalizations by Liu et al. (2005) using potential scattering theory and by Lage et al. (2009) using non-relativistic effective field theory. The authors demonstrate that the UCHPT approach avoids sub-threshold singularities that can appear in the standard Lüscher decomposition when applied to multi-channel systems with unequal masses. The synthetic data analysis follows the methodology of Bernard et al. (2008) for the Δ-resonance in πN elastic scattering and Torres et al. (2011) for the D_s0*(2317) resonance in meson-meson interaction with charm. The framework enables future lattice QCD calculations to extract scalar meson resonance parameters by fitting the chiral potential parameters to measured finite-volume spectra, with twisted boundary conditions proposed as a practical method to separate threshold effects from resonance signals.
