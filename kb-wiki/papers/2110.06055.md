# X17 discovery potential in the $ő≥N \rightarrow e^+ e^- N$ process at electron scattering facilities

> We propose a direct search for the X17 particle, which was conjectured to explain the ATOMKI $^8$Be and $^4$He anomalies, through the dilepton photoproduction process on a nucleon in the photon energy range below or around the pion production threshold. For the scenarios of either pseudoscalar, vector, or axial-vector quantum numbers of the conjectured X17, we use existing constraints to estimate the X17 signal process. For dilepton resolutions which have been achieved in previous experiments, a signal-to-background ratio of up to an order of magnitude is found for a neutron target, and in particular for the pseudoscalar and vector X17 scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a direct search for the X17 particle via the dilepton photoproduction process on a nucleon (ő≥N ‚Üí e‚Āļe‚ĀĽN) at photon energies between 100 and 150 MeV could achieve a signal-to-background ratio of up to an order of magnitude for a neutron target, but only under the specific scenarios where X17 has pseudoscalar or vector quantum numbers; the axial-vector scenario and all scenarios on a proton target show significantly smaller or negligible signal relative to background.

## Method

The authors estimated the X17 signal in the ő≥N ‚Üí e‚Āļe‚ĀĽN process by deriving nucleon coupling constants from the ATOMKI-reported ratio of the X17 decay rate to the gamma decay rate for the 18.15 MeV state in ‚ĀłBe, using three separate theoretical models for the X17 quantum numbers: a pseudoscalar axion-like particle following Alves and Weiner (2017), a vector particle following Feng et al. (2016), and an axial-vector particle following Kozaczuk et al. (2016). They then computed the differential cross section for the signal process and compared it to the QED background (Bethe-Heitler and Born processes) for both proton and quasi-free neutron targets at a photon energy of 0.15 GeV and momentum transfer ‚ąít = 0.04 GeV¬≤, averaging the signal over a 0.2 MeV bin around the X17 mass.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical interpretation of the ATOMKI anomalies in ‚ĀłBe (Krasznahorkay 2015, 2018, 2019) and ‚ĀīHe (Krasznahorkay 2019, 2021) by proposing a direct photoproduction search at electron scattering facilities like MAMI or MESA, complementing existing direct searches by the NA64 Collaboration at CERN which have not found X17 evidence. The paper builds on specific models for the X17 particle: the pseudoscalar model of Alves and Weiner (2017), the vector model of Feng et al. (2016), and the axial-vector model of Kozaczuk et al. (2016), and uses nuclear form factor calculations from Donnelly et al. (1978). The proposed experiment could enable determination of the X17 quantum numbers through the different angular dependencies of the signal, and could be optimized further by improving the dilepton energy resolution beyond the 0.2 MeV already achieved at MAMI (Merkel 2014).
