# Weak nuclear decays deep-underground as a probe of axion dark matter

> We investigate the time modulation of weak nuclear decays as a method to probe axion dark matter. To this end, we develop a theoretical framework to compute the $θ$-dependence of weak nuclear decays, including electron capture and $β$ decay, which enables us to predict the time variation of weak radioactivity in response to an oscillating axion dark matter background. As an application, we recast old data sets, from the weak nuclear decays of ${^{40}\text{K}}$ and ${^{137}\text{Cs}}$ taken at the underground Gran Sasso Laboratory, in order to set constraints on the axion decay constant, specifically in the axion mass range from few $10^{-23}\;$eV up to $10^{-19}\;$eV. We finally propose a new measurement at the Gran Sasso Laboratory, based on the weak nuclear decay of ${^{40}\text{K}}$ via electron capture, in order to explore even shorter timescales, thus reaching sensitivities to axion masses up to $10^{-9}\;$eV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that time modulation of weak nuclear decays (electron capture and beta decay) can be used to probe axion dark matter, but the actual sensitivity depends on the assumption that the main source of theta-dependence in weak nuclear decays comes from the proton-neutron mass difference, while the theta-dependence of nuclear binding energies cancels out in the difference between initial and final states for nuclei with the same mass number A.

## Method

The authors developed a theoretical framework to compute the theta-dependence of weak nuclear decays by combining the multipole expansion formalism for semi-leptonic weak processes with the known theta-dependence of the pion mass, nucleon masses, and nuclear binding energies. They applied this framework to reinterpret existing gamma-spectroscopy datasets from the Gran Sasso Laboratory for the weak decays of 40K (via electron capture) and 137Cs (via beta decay), using the observed limits on time modulation of decay rates to set constraints on the axion decay constant. They also proposed a new measurement at Gran Sasso based on 40K electron capture decay to explore shorter timescales down to 1 microsecond.

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Zhang et al. (2023) on beta decay time modulation and by Broggini et al. (2024) on alpha decay time modulation (the RadioAxion-alpha experiment) to weak nuclear decays. It recasts data from Bellotti et al. (2012, 2018) on 137Cs and 40K decay rate measurements at the Gran Sasso Laboratory, which previously ruled out decay constant modulations with amplitudes exceeding a few parts per 10^5 for periods between a few hours and a year. The proposed new 40K measurement at Gran Sasso would enable exploration of axion masses up to 10^-9 eV, complementing other approaches such as neutron EDM experiments and the RadioAxion-alpha prototype.
