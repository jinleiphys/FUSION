# Bulk viscosity in 2SC quark matter

> The bulk viscosity of three-flavor color-superconducting quark matter originating from the nonleptonic process u+s <-> u+d is computed. It is assumed that up and down quarks form Cooper pairs while the strange quark remains unpaired (2SC phase). A general derivation of the rate of strangeness production is presented, involving contributions from a multitude of different subprocesses, including subprocesses that involve different numbers of gapped quarks as well as creation and annihilation of particles in the condensate. The rate is then used to compute the bulk viscosity as a function of the temperature, for an external oscillation frequency typical of a compact star r-mode. We find that, for temperatures far below the critical temperature T_c for 2SC pairing, the bulk viscosity of color-superconducting quark matter is suppressed relative to that of unpaired quark matter, but for T >~ 10^(-3) T_c the color-superconducting quark matter has a higher bulk viscosity. This is potentially relevant for the suppression of r-mode instabilities early in the life of a compact star.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of 2SC color-superconducting quark matter is suppressed relative to unpaired quark matter at temperatures far below the critical temperature \(T_c\), but for \(T \gtrsim 10^{-3} T_c\) the 2SC phase actually has a higher bulk viscosity, which is potentially relevant for damping r-mode instabilities in compact stars.

## Method

The authors computed the rate of the nonleptonic weak process \(u+s \leftrightarrow u+d\) in the 2SC phase (where up and down quarks form Cooper pairs while strange quarks remain unpaired) using a kinetic equation derived from the Kadanoff-Baym formalism. They derived the collision integral including subprocesses with zero, one, two, or three gapped quarks, as well as processes involving creation and annihilation of particles in the condensate. The rate was then used to compute the bulk viscosity as a function of temperature for an external oscillation frequency typical of a compact star r-mode, using a linear-response framework analogous to an electric circuit with resistance and capacitance.

## Key numbers

* The bulk viscosity of 2SC quark matter is suppressed relative to unpaired matter for temperatures far below \(T_c\), but becomes higher for \(T \gtrsim 10^{-3} T_c\).
* The coefficient \(B^2/C\) (which determines the maximum bulk viscosity) is \(m_s^4/(6\pi^2)\) for unpaired matter and \(m_s^4/(9\pi^2) + 4\Delta^2 m_s^2/(27\pi^2) + 4\Delta^4/(81\pi^2)\) for 2SC matter, to lowest order in the strange quark mass \(m_s\) and the pairing gap \(\Delta\).
* The external oscillation frequency is taken as typical of a compact star r-mode, in the kHz range.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of bulk viscosity in unpaired quark matter (Wang 1985) and in other color-superconducting phases such as spin-one superconductors (Sa'd 2006) and hybrid/CFL matter (Drago 2003). It provides the first detailed calculation of bulk viscosity in the 2SC phase including all subprocesses and finite-temperature effects, enabling more realistic modeling of r-mode damping in compact stars and helping to distinguish between different color-superconducting phases through astrophysical observations.
