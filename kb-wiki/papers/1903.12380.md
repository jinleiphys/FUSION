# Renormalization of Unitarized Weinberg-Tomozawa Interaction without On-shell Factorization and $I=0$ $\bar K N$ - $πΣ$ Coupled Channels

> We calculate the scattering $T$-matrix of $I=0$ $\bar K N-πΣ$ coupled channels taking a ladder sum of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction without on-shell factorization, regularizing three types of divergent meson-baryon loop functions by dimensional regularization and renormalizing them by introducing counter terms. We show that not only infinite but also finite renormalization is important in order for the renormalized physical scattering $T$-matrix to have the form of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction. The results with and without on-shell factorization are compared. The difference of the scattering $T$-matrix is small near the renormalization point, close to the observed $Λ$(1405). The difference, however, increases with the distance from the renormalization point. The scattering $T$-matrix without on-shell factorization has two poles in the complex center-of-mass energy plane as with on-shell factorization, the real part of which is close to the observed $Λ$(1405). While the difference is small with and without on-shell factorization in the position of the first pole, closer to the observed $Λ$(1405), the difference is considerably large in the position of the second pole: the imaginary part of the center-of-mass energy of the second pole without on-shell factorization is as large as or even larger than twice that with on-shell factorization. Also, we discuss the origin of the contradiction about the second pole between two approaches, the chiral unitary approach with on-shell factorization and the phenomenological approach without on-shell factorization.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a properly renormalized ladder sum of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction without on-shell factorization still produces two poles in the complex center-of-mass energy plane near the observed Λ(1405), contradicting claims from phenomenological approaches that only one pole exists. The difference between results with and without on-shell factorization is small near the renormalization point but increases away from it, with the second pole's imaginary part becoming twice as large or larger without on-shell factorization.

## Method

The authors calculate the scattering T-matrix for I=0 Kbar N - πΣ coupled channels by summing the ladder diagrams of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction without applying the on-shell factorization approximation. They regularize three types of divergent meson-baryon loop functions (G0, G1, G2) using dimensional regularization and renormalize them by introducing counter terms, imposing renormalization conditions that the T-matrix retains the form of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction at the renormalization point. The resulting T-matrix is expressed as a 2n by 2n matrix equation involving renormalized loop functions, and the pole positions are found by analytic continuation into the complex center-of-mass energy plane.

## Key numbers

- The subtraction constants for the πΣ channel without on-shell factorization: a0 = -2.27, a1 = -2.29, a2 = -1.50 (case A) or a2 = -2.05 (case B); for the Kbar N channel: a0 = -1.83, a1 = -1.95, a2 = -1.60 (case A) or a2 = -2.31 (case B); with on-shell factorization (case C): a2 = -1.96 for both channels
- First pole in coupled channels (closer to observed Λ(1405)): case A at 1434 - 7i MeV, case B at 1419 - 19i MeV, case C at 1432 - 17i MeV
- Second pole in coupled channels: case A at 1418 - 160i MeV, case B at 1424 - 146i MeV, case C at 1398 - 73i MeV
- The imaginary part of the second pole without on-shell factorization (cases A and B) is twice as large as or even larger than twice that with on-shell factorization (case C)
- Real parts of the second pole in cases A and B differ from case C by about 20 MeV
- The pion decay constant f = 106.95 MeV and renormalization scale μ = 630 MeV are used, matching Hyodo (2007)

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller (2000), Jido (2003), and Hyodo (2007) by removing the on-shell factorization approximation that those studies employed to obtain the double-pole structure of the Λ(1405). It directly addresses the contradiction raised by Revai (2017), who claimed that without on-shell factorization only one pole appears, but used a nonrelativistic phenomenological separable potential model with cutoff functions rather than a chiral interaction. The present results show that the double-pole structure survives when the chiral Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction is treated without on-shell factorization but with proper renormalization, suggesting the contradiction arises from the different regularization and renormalization schemes rather than from the on-shell approximation itself. The work enables future studies to systematically assess the role of finite renormalization and the choice of renormalization conditions in chiral unitary approaches.
