# Investigating the possibility of extracting neutron-skin thickness in nuclei by their collisions at intermediate energies

> Inspired by various studies on extracting the density distributions of nuclei from their collisions at ultrarelativistic energies, in the present work we investigate the possibility of extracting the neutron-skin thickness $Δr_{np}$ in nuclei by their collisions at intermediate energies. We have analyzed the free neutron-to-proton yield ratio $n/p$ as a candidate probe at both midrapidities and forward rapidities in peripheral and central $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn collisions based on an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, and found that the resulting $n/p$ yield ratio is more sensitive to the symmetry potential in the collision dynamics than to the initial $Δr_{np}$ in colliding nuclei in most cases. The largest effect on the $n/p$ yield ratio from the initial $Δr_{np}$ is observed for nucleons at large transverse or longitudinal momenta in central collisions at the collision energy of a few GeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

The free neutron-to-proton yield ratio in heavy-ion collisions is more sensitive to the symmetry potential in the collision dynamics than to the initial neutron-skin thickness in the colliding nuclei in most cases, with the largest effect from the initial neutron-skin thickness observed only for nucleons at large transverse or longitudinal momenta in central collisions at a few GeV/nucleon.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with an improved momentum-dependent nuclear effective interaction (ImMDI) to simulate peripheral and central collisions of 124Sn+124Sn at beam energies of 600A MeV and 2A GeV. They varied the initial neutron-skin thickness of 124Sn (0.16, 0.19, and 0.23 fm) by adjusting the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations, and separately varied the symmetry potential and neutron-proton effective mass splitting in the collision dynamics while keeping the symmetry energy fixed. Free nucleons were identified after transport simulation and deexcitation of heavy fragments using the GEMINI model.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness values for 124Sn used in the study: 0.16 fm (L=30 MeV), 0.19 fm (L=60 MeV), and 0.23 fm (L=90 MeV), obtained from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with the MSL0 force.
- At 600A MeV in peripheral collisions, about 80% of free nucleons come from deexcitation of residue fragments and about 20% from direct production; approximately 85% of free neutrons and 60% of free protons are from deexcitation.
- The total neutron number at pseudorapidity |eta|>3.13 (forward polar angle range of 5 degrees) in peripheral collisions at 600A MeV is about 7.5, similar to GSI data.
- At 2A GeV in central collisions, the effect of initial neutron-skin thickness on the n/p yield ratio at high transverse momentum becomes visible but remains smaller than or comparable to the effect from the symmetry potential and neutron-proton effective mass splitting.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Liu et al. (2022, 2023) that investigated neutron-skin extraction from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and by Sun et al. (2009) and Yang et al. (2023) who proposed the free neutron-to-proton yield ratio as a probe of neutron-skin thickness at intermediate energies using quantum molecular dynamics approaches. The study distinguishes the effects of initial neutron-skin thickness from those of the symmetry potential in the collision dynamics, showing that the latter dominates in most kinematic regions, and suggests that extracting neutron-skin thickness from intermediate-energy collisions requires careful selection of kinematic windows where the structure effect is maximized relative to the dynamical effect.
