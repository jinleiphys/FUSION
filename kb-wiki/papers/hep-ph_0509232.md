# Selfconsistent calculations of sigma-meson properties at finite temperature

> I study the properties of the scalar sigma-meson [also referred to as f_0(600)] at nonzero temperature in the O(N)-model in the framework of the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis formalism. In the standard Hartree (or large-N) approximation one only takes into account the double-bubble diagrams in the effective potential. I improve this approximation by additionally taking into account the sunset diagrams, which lead to 4-momentum dependent real and imaginary parts of the Dyson-Schwinger equations. By solving these and the equation for the chiral condensate selfconsistently, one obtains the decay width and the spectral density of the sigma-meson. I compare the results in the case with explicit chiral symmetry breaking with the chiral limit. I found that the 4-momentum dependent real part of the selfenergy does not lead to major qualitative changes in the spectral density.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the 4-momentum dependent real part of the sigma-meson selfenergy, which was neglected in the author's prior improved Hartree-Fock approximation (Roder 2005), does not lead to qualitative changes in the spectral density; the quantitative effect is small, with an average relative change of 5.61 ± 2.90% at T=160 MeV and 0.44 ± 0.01% at T=320 MeV for the case studied.

## Method

The author uses the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism at finite temperature in the O(N) linear sigma model, improving the standard Hartree (large-N) approximation by adding sunset diagrams to the double-bubble diagrams in the two-particle irreducible effective potential. The Dyson-Schwinger equations and the chiral condensate equation are solved selfconsistently to obtain the 4-momentum dependent real and imaginary parts of the sigma-meson selfenergy, from which the decay width and spectral density are computed. The calculation is performed for N=4, comparing the chiral limit (m_pi=0, f_pi=90 MeV) with explicit chiral symmetry breaking (m_pi=139.5 MeV, f_pi=92.4 MeV), for sigma vacuum masses of 400, 600, and 800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in the chiral limit: T_chi = sqrt(12/N) f_pi ≈ 160 MeV for the O(4) model
- Vacuum decay width of the sigma-meson: Gamma_sigma = 600-1000 MeV (from the Particle Data Book)
- Pion mass in explicit symmetry breaking case: m_pi = 139.5 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Pion decay constant in chiral limit: f_pi = 90 MeV
- Explicit symmetry breaking parameter: H = (121.6 MeV)^3
- Coupling constant values range from lambda = 8.230 (m_sigma=400 MeV, explicit breaking) to lambda = 39.506 (m_sigma=800 MeV, chiral limit)
- Bare mass squared values range from mu^2 = -(225.41 MeV)^2 (m_sigma=400 MeV, explicit breaking) to mu^2 = -(565.685 MeV)^2 (m_sigma=800 MeV, chiral limit)
- Decay width scaling: Im Sigma ∝ m_sigma^4, with ratios 1:3:16 for m_sigma = 400:600:800 MeV
- Decay width becomes approximately zero for T > ~200 MeV (explicit breaking) or at T_chi ≈ 160 MeV (chiral limit)

## Context

This work extends the author's prior improved Hartree-Fock approximation (Roder 2005) by including the 4-momentum dependent real part of the selfenergy that was previously neglected for simplicity, while working in the simpler Hartree (large-N) limit rather than the Hartree-Fock approximation. The results confirm the second-order phase transition in the chiral limit predicted by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984), and show that the sigma-meson decay width decreases monotonically with temperature due to chiral restoration squeezing the sigma → 2 pi phase space. The next steps identified are including 1/N terms for nonzero pion decay width, and adding baryons (Beckmann 2005) and vector mesons (van Hees 2000, Ruppert 2004) to connect with dilepton observables in heavy-ion collisions.
