# Strange hadron ratios from quark coalescence at RHIC and LHC

> Quark coalescence models have been applied successfully to reproduce measured hadron production data in relativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies, which finding strongly supports the formation of deconfined quark matter in these collisions. The investigation of meson and baryon production is an ideal tool to understand dynamical details of hadronization, especially strange hadron numbers and ratios. We display latest results on the production of strange particles in quark coalescence processes in heavy ion collisions at different collider energies.

# Strange hadron ratios from quark coalescence at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

Quark coalescence models can reproduce bulk hadron ratios at SPS and RHIC energies, but the agreement is not perfect for all channels (notably the Φ/K⁻ ratio at RHIC), and the predictions for LHC energies rely on a linear extrapolation of entropy production with collision energy, assuming strangeness saturation and minimal baryon stopping.

## Method

The ALCOR (A Lattice-inspired COalescence model) was applied to Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (20–158 GeV/n) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC (√s = 200 AGeV), using four input parameters: number of newly produced light quark–antiquark pairs (N_uū = N_dd̄), strangeness fraction f_s = N_s\bar{s}/(N_uū + N_dd̄), effective strong coupling α_s controlling baryon-to-meson ratios, and stopping fraction for baryon number transport. The coalescence cross sections were computed from quantum mechanical rearrangement reactions using a Coulomb-like colour potential with colour factors −4/3 for meson formation and −2/3 for diquark formation.

## Key numbers

- At SPS 158 GeV/n: K⁺/π⁺ = 0.158, \bar{Λ}⁰/\bar{p}⁻ = 0.80, Φ/K⁻ = 0.28, (Ω⁻+Ω⁺)/π⁻ = 0.00244
- At SPS 20 GeV/n: K⁺/π⁺ = 0.213, \bar{Λ}⁰/\bar{p}⁻ = 1.36, Φ/K⁻ = 0.42, (Ω⁻+Ω⁺)/π⁻ = 0.00268
- At RHIC √s = 200 AGeV: K⁺/π⁺ = 0.15 (data 0.16 ± 0.02), Ξ⁻/π⁻ = 0.008 (data 0.007 ± 0.001), Φ/K⁻ = 0.26 (data 0.15 ± 0.03)
- At RHIC: total number of quark–antiquark pairs (Q\bar{Q}) = 1396, f_s = 0.22, α_s = 0.55, stopping = 3%
- LHC prediction (LHC-I): N_uū = 500, total Q\bar{Q} = 2440, K⁺/π⁺ = 0.14, Ξ⁻/π⁻ = 0.013, Φ/K⁻ = 0.25
- LHC prediction (LHC-II, 50% higher entropy): N_uū = 750, total Q\bar{Q} = 3660, K⁺/π⁺ = 0.14, Ξ⁻/π⁻ = 0.015, Φ/K⁻ = 0.25

## Context

This work extends the ALCOR and MICOR coalescence models (Hwa 1977, Bialas 2002) previously applied at SPS and RHIC, and contrasts with thermal/statistical models (Fermi 1950, Hagedorn 1965, Koch 1983, Becattini) which assume full chemical equilibrium and lose information about the deconfined phase. The results enable predictions for LHC Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 5500 AGeV, though the Φ/K⁻ discrepancy at RHIC (0.26 predicted vs 0.15 measured) indicates that improved wave-function setups (Hamar 2007) or inclusion of higher baryon and meson resonance channels may be needed.
