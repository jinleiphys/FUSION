# Five-quark components in $Δ(1232)\to Nπ$ decay

> Five-quark $qqqq\bar q$ components in the $Δ(1232)$ are shown to contribute significantly to $Δ(1232)\to Nπ$ decay through quark-antiquark annihilation transitions. These involve the overlap between the $qqq$ and $qqqq\bar q$ components and may be triggered by the confining interaction between the quarks. With a $\sim$ 10% admixture of five-quark components in the $Δ(1232)$ the decay width can be larger by factors 2 - 3 over that calculated in the quark model with 3 valence quarks, depending on the details of the confining interaction. The effect of transitions between the $qqqq\bar q$ components themselves on the calculated decay width is however small. The large contribution of the quark-antiquark annihilation transitions thus may compensate the underprediction of the width of the $Δ(1232)$ by the valence quark model, once the $Δ(1232)$ contains $qqqq\bar q$ components with $\sim$ 10% probability.

# Five-quark components in Δ(1232) → Nπ decay

## Key claim

Five-quark (qqqq\bar q) components in the Δ(1232) resonance can increase the calculated decay width for Δ(1232) → Nπ by factors of 2–3 over the pure three-quark valence quark model result, provided the Δ(1232) contains a ∼10% admixture of such five-quark components and the confining interaction is treated as a scalar linear potential. The enhancement arises primarily from quark-antiquark annihilation transitions (qqqq\bar q → qqqπ), not from transitions between five-quark components themselves.

## Method

The authors extended the non-relativistic constituent quark model by explicitly including low-lying qqqq\bar q configurations in both the proton and the Δ(1232), classified by their flavor-spin and color symmetries. They calculated the matrix elements for two types of pionic transitions: (1) direct transitions between five-quark components using the chiral quark model pion-quark coupling, and (2) quark-antiquark annihilation transitions, both direct and those triggered by the confining interaction (linear scalar confinement or harmonic oscillator confinement). The spatial wave functions were described by harmonic oscillator functions with oscillator parameters ω₃ = 225 MeV for the three-quark component and ω₅ = 245 MeV for the five-quark component.

## Key numbers

- With a 10% probability of qqqq\bar q components in the Δ(1232) and a pure qqq proton, the direct annihilation mechanism combined with five-quark component transitions increases the calculated decay width by ∼69% (with ω₅ = 245 MeV, m = 340 MeV).
- When the radius of the qqqq\bar q component is increased by 22% (ω₅ reduced to 200 MeV), the enhancement from direct annihilation increases to 81%.
- For the linear confining potential with string tension c = 280 MeV and shift parameter b = 300 MeV, the total enhancement factor reaches 2.5 with a 10% qqqq\bar q component in the Δ(1232).
- The enhancement factor δ ranges from 3.30 (b = 150 MeV) to 1.82 (b = 450 MeV) for linear confinement, and from 2.23 (B = 50 MeV) to 1.59 (B = 200 MeV) for harmonic oscillator confinement.
- The pion momentum for Δ(1232) → Nπ decay is q_π = 227 MeV.
- The function K(q_π) = 0.74 for the linear confinement model with b = 0 MeV and ω₅ = 245 MeV.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing underprediction of the Δ(1232) decay width in the valence quark model, which gives values only about half the empirical width even in sophisticated covariant versions (Bruno, Melde). Previous coupled-channel treatments of the πNΔ system (Sato, Sato) suggested the problem might be cured by "pion cloud" contributions. The present paper provides an alternative mechanism through explicit five-quark sea-quark components, motivated by experimental evidence for such components in the proton (Towell, NA51, NMC, HERMES) and the classification of low-lying qqqq\bar q configurations (Helminen). The results are qualitative and exploratory, relying on a non-relativistic harmonic oscillator model; the authors note that quantitative estimates require a Hamiltonian model constrained by the N-Δ splitting and nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The analysis could be extended to the N(1440) and Δ(1600) resonances, whose widths are also typically underestimated in three-quark models.
