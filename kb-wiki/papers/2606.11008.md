# Gaussian vs. Real Wavefunction of Nuclear Clusters and Hypernuclei

> We compare realistic $N$-body wave functions obtained from solutions of the Schrödinger equation with Gaussian ansätze constrained to the same rms radius. The microscopic wave functions exhibit significantly broader spatial distributions, revealing pronounced non-Gaussian structures. In addition, we investigate possible production channels for $A=4$ clusters using a phenomenological two-body interaction. This study provides a potential mechanism that may help alleviate the underestimation of $A=4$ cluster yields in theoretical models compared to experimental data.

## Key claim

Realistic N-body wave functions for nuclear clusters and hypernuclei, obtained from solving the Schrödinger equation, exhibit significantly broader spatial distributions than Gaussian ansätze constrained to the same root-mean-square radius, revealing pronounced non-Gaussian structures that may affect cluster production in small collision systems.

## Method

The authors solved the N-body Schrödinger equation using the hyperspherical harmonics formalism with the Argonne-18 potential for nucleon-nucleon interactions and the Usmani potential for hyperon-nucleon interactions, neglecting genuine three-body forces. They compared the dominant component of the resulting wave functions with Gaussian wave functions having the same rms radius. Separately, they investigated possible two-body production channels for A=4 clusters by constructing a phenomenological two-range Gaussian potential, tuned to reproduce the empirical 4He mass, and solving the two-body Schrödinger equation for channels such as t-p and 3He-n.

## Key numbers

- For an N-body Gaussian wave function, the rms radius relates to the width parameter sigma by r_rms^2 = (3(N-1)/2) sigma^2.
- The phenomenological two-range Gaussian potential for A=4 channels used parameters: v_r = 2 GeV (short-range repulsion), v_a = -0.31 GeV (intermediate-range attraction), mu_r = 3 GeV^{-1}, and mu_a = 6 GeV^{-1}.
- The combined mass of the d and Lambda system was found to be smaller than that of 3_Lambda H, disfavoring a bound d-Lambda molecular configuration within the present setup.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Zhao 2025) where they solved the N-body Schrödinger equation and computed Wigner phase-space distributions for nuclear clusters and hypernuclei. The finding that realistic wave functions have broader spatial distributions than Gaussian ansätze challenges the common Gaussian assumptions used in coalescence models, particularly for small collision systems like pp, pA, and peripheral heavy-ion collisions studied by STAR and ALICE. The investigation of A=4 production channels via phenomenological two-body interactions provides a potential mechanism to address the long-standing underestimation of A=4 cluster yields in theoretical models compared to experimental data from ALICE (2023) and STAR (2023).
