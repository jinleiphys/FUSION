# When renormalizability is not sufficient: Coulomb problem for vector bosons

> The Coulomb problem for vector bosons W incorporates a known difficulty; the boson falls on the center. In QED the fermion vacuum polarization produces a barrier at small distances which solves the problem. In a renormalizable SU(2) theory containing vector triplet (W^+,W^-,gamma) and a heavy fermion doublet F with mass M the W^- falls on F^+, to distances r ~ 1/M, where M can be made arbitrary large. To prevent the collapse the theory needs additional light fermions, which switch the ultraviolet behavior of the theory from the asymptotic freedom to the Landau pole. Similar situation can take place in the Standard Model. Thus, the renormalizability of a theory is not sufficient to guarantee a reasonable behavior at small distances for non-perturbative problems, such as a bound state problem.

## Key claim

Renormalizability of a gauge theory is not sufficient to guarantee physically sensible behavior at small distances for non-perturbative bound-state problems; in a renormalizable SU(2) model with a heavy fermion doublet, the W⁻ boson collapses onto the heavy fermion F⁺ at distances r ∼ 1/M, and preventing this collapse requires adding light fermions that switch the ultraviolet behavior from asymptotic freedom to a Landau pole.

## Method

The authors analyze the wave equation for a massive vector boson (W⁻) in the Coulomb field of a heavy fermion F⁺, derived from a renormalizable SU(2) gauge theory with a Higgs triplet and a heavy fermion doublet. They examine the short-distance behavior of the radial wave function for the j=0 partial wave, including the effects of vacuum polarization via the Uehling potential and the associated Υ-term from the external current. The sign of the Gell-Mann–Low beta-function coefficient β determines whether the wave function is exponentially suppressed (β>0, stable) or oscillatory and growing (β<0, collapse).

## Key numbers

- The heavy fermion mass M is a free parameter that can be made arbitrarily large; collapse occurs to distances r ∼ 1/M.
- For the pure Coulomb potential with Z=1/2 (charge of F⁺), the radial wave function scales as v(r) ∝ r^{γ−3/2} with γ = (1/4 − Z²α²)^{1/2}, giving charge density ρ_W ∝ r^{2γ−4} which diverges since 2γ < 1.
- In QED, the lowest-order beta-function coefficient is β = 2/(3π) > 0 per Dirac fermion generation, leading to exponential suppression v ∝ (1/r²) exp(−ϕ) with ϕ = Zα(αβ)^{1/2}/(m r).
- In the SU(2) model, β = −22/(3π) < 0, giving oscillatory growth v ∝ (1/r²) cos(|ϕ|+δ) and collapse.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Kuchiev and Flambaum, 2006) of the Coulomb problem for vector bosons in QED, where fermion vacuum polarization creates a barrier preventing collapse. It contradicts the assumption that renormalizability alone guarantees good short-distance behavior, showing that the Corben-Schwinger (1940) modification of Proca theory (with gyromagnetic ratio g=2) still allows collapse for j=0 and l=0 states. The result applies to the Standard Model SU(2)×U(1) with heavy fermions, where the Weinberg mixing angle cancels at small distances, and enables the conclusion that light fermions must be added to prevent collapse, a condition not implied by renormalizability.
