# On the possibility of an eta-meson light nucleus bound state formation

> The resonance and bound--state poles of the amplitude describing elastic scattering of $η$-meson off the light nuclei $^2H$,\,$^3H$,\, $^3He$, and $^4He$ are calculated in the framework of a microscopic approach based on few--body equations. For each of the nuclei, the two--body parameters that enhance the $ηN$--attraction which generate quasi--bound states, are also determined.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an eta-meson can form a quasi-bound state with a light nucleus of atomic number as low as A=2, contradicting earlier estimates that required A≥12; however, the existence of such states depends on the real part of the eta-nucleon scattering length being within the upper end of its experimentally uncertain range, and for systems other than eta-4He the predicted widths are large (around 20 MeV), making experimental detection difficult.

## Method

The authors used the Finite-Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian, a microscopic few-body approach, to calculate the eta-nucleus T-matrix for elastic scattering on 2H, 3H, 3He, and 4He at complex energies. They solved coupled integral equations after a coherent approximation that keeps the nucleus in its ground state during multiple scattering, using Gaussian ground-state wave functions that reproduce experimental root-mean-square radii and a separable eta-nucleon t-matrix dominated by the N*(1535) S11 resonance. The eta-nucleon scattering length was varied by an enhancing factor g to find the critical attraction needed to move poles from the resonance region across the diagonal boundary into the quasi-bound region of the complex momentum plane.

## Key numbers

- For eta-4He with g=1 and alpha=2.357 fm^-1, the pole position is p0 = -0.15056 + i0.18278 fm^-1, giving E0 = -0.43713 - i2.2399 MeV (a quasi-bound state with negative real energy).
- For eta-d with g=1 and alpha=2.357 fm^-1, the pole is at p0 = -0.90254 + i0.35880 fm^-1, giving E0 = 31.448 - i29.698 MeV (a resonance with positive real energy).
- The critical enhancing factors g that place poles exactly on the diagonal (Re E0 = 0) range from g=0.899 (eta-4He, alpha=7.617 fm^-1) to g=1.654 (eta-d, alpha=2.357 fm^-1).
- The experimental root-mean-square radii used were: deuteron 1.956 fm, triton 1.755 fm, 3He 1.959 fm, and alpha particle 1.671 fm.
- The eta-nucleon scattering length was taken as a_etaN = (g * 0.55 + i0.30) fm, with the real part varied via g; the existing uncertainty range for Re a_etaN is [0.27, 0.98] fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations by the same authors (Rakityansky et al., 1994-1995) that found eta-helium scattering lengths with larger negative real parts than previous optical-potential estimates, and directly contradicts the lower bound A≥12 for eta-nucleus bound states derived from first-order optical potential theory (Haider and Liu, 1986). The results enable future experimental searches for eta-nucleus quasi-bound states with A≥2, though the large widths predicted for eta-d, eta-t, and eta-3He (around 20 MeV) suggest that only the eta-4He system offers a narrow enough state for practical detection.
