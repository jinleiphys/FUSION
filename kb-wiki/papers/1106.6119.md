# The Time-Reversal- and Parity-Violating Nuclear Potential in Chiral Effective Theory

> We derive the parity- and time-reversal-violating nuclear interactions stemming from the QCD theta term and quark/gluon operators of effective dimension 6: quark electric dipole moments, quark and gluon chromo-electric dipole moments, and two four-quark operators. We work in the framework of two-flavor chiral perturbation theory, where a systematic expansion is possible. The different chiral-transformation properties of the sources of time-reversal violation lead to different hadronic interactions. For all sources considered the leading-order potential involves known one-pion exchange, but its specific form and the relative importance of short-range interactions depend on the source. For the theta term, the leading potential is solely given by one-pion exchange, which does not contribute to the deuteron electric dipole moment. In subleading order, a new two-pion-exchange potential is obtained. Its short-range component is indistinguishable from one of two undetermined contact interactions that appear at the same order and represent effects of heavier mesons and other short-range QCD dynamics. One-pion-exchange corrections at this order are discussed as well.

## Key claim

The leading-order parity- and time-reversal-violating nuclear potential from the QCD theta term is solely given by one-pion exchange, which does not contribute to the deuteron electric dipole moment; for dimension-6 sources, all non-derivative pion-nucleon couplings appear at leading order, but the relative strength of the isospin-2 coupling and short-range interactions differs among sources.

## Method

The authors derive the parity- and time-reversal-violating nuclear interactions within two-flavor chiral perturbation theory, using a heavy-baryon formalism to treat nucleons non-relativistically. They construct the chiral Lagrangian for the QCD theta term and dimension-6 operators (quark electric dipole moments, quark and gluon chromo-electric dipole moments, and four-quark operators) and organize the nuclear potential according to chiral power counting, computing contributions up to subleading order for the theta term and at leading order for dimension-6 sources.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM bound: |d_n| < 2.9 × 10^{-26} e cm, implying theta-bar ≤ 10^{-10}
- Proton EDM bound: |d_p| < 7.9 × 10^{-25} e cm, extracted from the EDM of the ^{199}Hg atom
- Projected deuteron EDM precision: |d_d| ≤ 10^{-29} e cm from storage-ring experiments
- Pion decay constant: F_π ≈ 186 MeV
- Pion-nucleon axial coupling: g_A ≈ 1.27
- Pion mass splitting: m_{π±}^2 - m_{π^0}^2 = (35.5 MeV)^2
- Nucleon mass splitting: m_n - m_p = 1.29 MeV
- Quark-mass contribution to nucleon mass splitting: δm_N = 2.26 ± 0.57 ± 0.42 ± 0.10 MeV from lattice simulations
- Electromagnetic contribution to nucleon mass splitting: δ̌m_N = -(0.76 ± 0.30) MeV from Cottingham sum rule
- Isospin-violating pion-nucleon coupling: β_1 = (0 ± 9) × 10^{-3} from phase-shift analysis

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory framework for time-reversal violation from the one-nucleon sector (Crewther et al., 1979; Bira et al., 2005) to the multi-nucleon sector, providing a consistent treatment of nuclear forces alongside the nucleon electric dipole moment calculations. The derived potential enables model-independent calculations of nuclear electric dipole moments (such as the deuteron and ^3He) and other time-reversal-violating observables, complementing earlier phenomenological approaches using one-pion exchange (Hax and Henley, 1983; Herczeg) and heavy-meson exchange (Townsend and Hardy, 1994; Gudkov, Halpern, and Melnitchouk, 1993). The framework parallels the successful application of chiral perturbation theory to parity-violating nuclear forces, which provided an alternative to the DDH approach.
