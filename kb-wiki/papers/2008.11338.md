# Curvature-slope correlation of nuclear symmetry energy and its imprints on the crust-core transition, radius and tidal deformability of canonical neutron stars

> Background: The nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$ encodes information about the energy necessary to make nuclear systems more neutron-rich. While its slope parameter L at the saturation density $ρ_0$ of nuclear matter has been relatively well constrained by recent astrophysical observations and terrestrial nuclear experiments, its curvature $K_{\rm{sym}}$ characterizing the $E_{sym}(ρ)$ around $2ρ_0$ remains largely unconstrained. Over 520 calculations for $E_{sym}(ρ)$ using various nuclear theories and interactions in the literature have predicted several significantly different $K_{\rm{sym}}-L$ correlations. Purpose: If a unique $K_{\rm{sym}}-L$ correlation of $E_{sym}(ρ)$ can be firmly established, it will enable us to progressively better constrain the high-density behavior of $E_{sym}(ρ)$ using the available constraints on its slope parameter L. We investigate if and by how much the different $K_{\rm{sym}}-L$ correlations may affect neutron star observables. Method: A meta-model of nuclear Equation of States (EOSs) with three representative $K_{\rm{sym}}-L$ correlation functions is used to generate multiple EOSs for neutron stars. We then examine effects of the $K_{\rm{sym}}-L$ correlation on the crust-core transition density and pressure as well as the radius and tidal deformation of canonical neutron stars. Results:The $K_{\rm{sym}}-L$ correlation affects significantly both the crust-core transition density and pressure. It also has strong imprints on the radius and tidal deformability of canonical neutron stars especially at small L values. The available data from LIGO/VIRGO and NICER set some useful limits for the slope L but can not distinguish the three representative $K_{\rm{sym}}-L$ correlations considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while the curvature-slope correlation of nuclear symmetry energy significantly affects neutron star crust-core transition properties, radii, and tidal deformabilities, especially at small values of the slope parameter L, the current LIGO/VIRGO and NICER data cannot distinguish between the three representative \(K_{\rm{sym}}-L\) correlations considered, though they do set useful limits on L.

## Method

The authors employ a meta-model of nuclear equations of state (EOSs) that parameterizes the symmetric nuclear matter energy and symmetry energy as Taylor expansions around saturation density \(\rho_0\), with coefficients varied within ranges consistent with nuclear theory and experiment. Three representative \(K_{\rm{sym}}-L\) correlation functions from the literature, from Mondal et al. (2017), Tews et al. (2017), and Holt and Lim (2018), are used to generate multiple EOSs, and the thermodynamical method is applied to find the crust-core transition density and pressure by solving for when the effective incompressibility \(K_\mu\) becomes zero. The resulting EOSs are then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to compute neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities for canonical 1.4 \(M_\odot\) stars.

## Key numbers

- The fiducial value of the slope parameter L from a 2013 survey of 28 analyses was \(L = 59 \pm 16\) MeV; a 2016 survey of 53 analyses found \(L = 58.7 \pm 28.1\) MeV.
- A 2020 Bayesian analysis of LIGO/VIRGO and NICER data found the most probable values \(L = 66_{-20}^{+12}\) MeV and \(K_{\rm{sym}} = -120_{-100}^{+80}\) MeV at 68% confidence level.
- The Mondal et al. correlation is \(K_{\rm{sym}} = (-4.97 \pm 0.07)(3E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0)-L) + 66.80 \pm 2.14\) MeV; the Tews et al. correlation is \(K_{\rm{sym}} = 3.50 L - 305.67 \pm 24.26\) MeV; the Holt and Lim correlation is \(K_{\rm{sym}} = 2.76L - 203.07 \pm 21.69\) MeV.
- For the Mondal correlation with \(L = 40\) MeV, \(K_{\rm{sym}} = -205\) MeV, and \(K_0 = 220\) MeV, the second dynamical instability occurs at \(6.82 \rho_0\), limiting the maximum mass to about 1.9 \(M_\odot\), while the Holt correlation supports about 2.1 \(M_\odot\).
- The crust-core transition density is around \(\rho_0/2\) (approximately 0.16 fm\(^{-3}\)) across the parameter space studied.

## Context

This work extends the extensive literature on the nuclear symmetry energy and its role in neutron star structure, particularly building on surveys of over 520 theoretical predictions compiled by Dutra et al. (2012, 2014) and the Bayesian analyses of Xie and Li (2019, 2020) that used LIGO/VIRGO and NICER data. The study uses the EOS meta-modeling approach of Zhang et al. (2018, 2019, 2020) to systematically compare three specific \(K_{\rm{sym}}-L\) correlations from Mondal et al. (2017), Tews et al. (2017), and Holt and Lim (2018). The results enable future work to use more precise independent measurements of neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities to distinguish between these correlations and thereby better constrain the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy.
