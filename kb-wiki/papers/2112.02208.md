# Lattice QCD Determination of the Bjorken-$x$ Dependence of Parton Distribution Functions at Next-to-next-to-leading Order

> We report the first lattice QCD calculation of pion valence quark distribution with next-to-next-to-leading order perturbative matching correction, which is done using two fine lattices with spacings $a=0.04$ fm and $0.06$ fm and valence pion mass $m_π=300$ MeV, at boost momentum as large as $2.42$ GeV. As a crucial step to control the systematics, we renormalize the pion valence quasi distribution in the recently proposed hybrid scheme, which features a Wilson-line mass subtraction at large distances in coordinate space, and develop a procedure to match it to the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme. We demonstrate that the renormalization and the perturbative matching in Bjorken-$x$ space yield a reliable determination of the valence quark distribution for $0.03\lesssim x \lesssim 0.80$ with 5-20\% uncertainties.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first lattice QCD calculation of the pion valence quark distribution using next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) perturbative matching, but the reliable determination is limited to the range \(0.03 \lesssim x \lesssim 0.80\) with 5–20% uncertainties, and the result depends on controlling systematic errors through a hybrid renormalization scheme and a procedure to match it to the \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) scheme.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavor gauge ensembles at lattice spacings \(a = 0.04\) fm and \(0.06\) fm, using a valence pion mass \(m_\pi = 300\) MeV and boost momenta up to \(P^z = 2.42\) GeV. They renormalized the pion valence quasi-PDF in a hybrid scheme that subtracts the Wilson-line mass divergence at large distances in coordinate space, and derived the NNLO matching coefficient to convert the quasi-PDF in the hybrid scheme to the \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) PDF. The renormalized matrix elements were Fourier transformed to obtain the quasi-PDF in Bjorken-\(x\) space, and the matching was applied to extract the valence quark distribution.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacings: \(a = 0.04\) fm and \(0.06\) fm.
* Valence pion mass: \(m_\pi = 300\) MeV.
* Maximum boost momentum: \(P^z = 2.42\) GeV.
* Wilson-line mass subtraction parameters: \(a\delta m = 0.1586(8)\) for \(a = 0.06\) fm and \(a\delta m = 0.1508(12)\) for \(a = 0.04\) fm.
* Hybrid scheme matching parameter: \(\bar{m}_0 = 0.151(1)\) GeV at \(\mu = 2.0\) GeV.
* Power correction parameter: \(\Lambda = 0.041(6)\) GeV\(^2\) at \(\mu = 2.0\) GeV.
* Reliable PDF extraction range: \(0.03 \lesssim x \lesssim 0.80\) with 5–20% uncertainties.
* Estimated power correction at \(P^z = 2.42\) GeV: \(\alpha(x) / [P_z^2 f_v(x)] < 0.10\) for \(0.01 < x < 0.80\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of pion PDFs, which were performed only at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy, such as the BNL20 analysis by Gao et al. (2020) that used a short-distance factorization approach. The new NNLO matching reduces the scale-variation uncertainty and shows better perturbative convergence, enabling a more reliable determination of the PDF in the moderate-\(x\) region. The hybrid renormalization procedure developed here can be applied to future lattice calculations of gluon PDFs, distribution amplitudes, generalized parton distributions, and transverse momentum distributions.
