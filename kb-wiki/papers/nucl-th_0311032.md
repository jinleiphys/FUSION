# A test of X(5) for the gamma degree of freedom

> We present the first extensive test of the critical point symmetry X(5) for the gamma degree of freedom, based in part on recent measurements for the gamma-band in 152 Sm. The agreement is good for some observables including the energies and most intra- and interband transitions, but there is also a serious discrepancy for one transition.

# A test of X(5) for the gamma degree of freedom

## Key claim
X(5) critical point symmetry predictions for the gamma degree of freedom in 152Sm show good agreement for gamma-band energies and most intra- and interband transitions, but there is a serious discrepancy for the 2+_gamma to 2+_2 transition, where the measured B(E2) value is 27(4) W.u. while X(5) predicts 0.20 W.u.

## Method
The authors solved the Bohr Hamiltonian with an infinite square well potential in beta and a harmonic potential in gamma, assuming small oscillations in gamma and an adiabatic separation of beta and gamma variables. They calculated energy eigenvalues using zeros of cylindrical Bessel functions and B(E2) values from matrix elements of the quadrupole operator, normalizing to experimental excitation energies and selected transition rates. The results were compared to recent GRID technique measurements at ILL and polarization measurements at Yale for the gamma-band in 152Sm up to spin 9+_gamma, covering about 15 absolute B(E2) values and multiple branching ratios.

## Key numbers
- The measured B(E2;2+_gamma → 2+_2) value is 27(4) W.u., while X(5) predicts 0.20 W.u.
- The experimental B(E2;4+_gamma → 2+_1) is 0.59(17) W.u. compared to 2.86 W.u. in X(5)
- B(E2) values normalized to B(E2;2_1 → 0_1) = 100 for Delta K=0 transitions and B(E2;2_gamma → 0_1) = 10 for Delta K=2 transitions
- The branching ratio from the 4+_gamma level is less than 0.7 experimentally, compared to 0.84 in X(5) and 2.93 from the Alaga rule
- Experimental branching ratios to the ground band are about 3–6 times larger than X(5) predictions

## Context
This work extends the X(5) critical point symmetry tests by Iachello (2001) beyond the yrast and yrare bands to the gamma degree of freedom, following earlier gamma-band comparisons for 104Mo. The results contradict the Alaga rules for gamma → 0+_2 band transitions and show that the beta-gamma coupling, included in IBA and GCM calculations, may explain the severe discrepancy for the 2+_gamma → 2+_2 transition. The findings motivate further measurements of higher-lying gamma-band transitions and exploration of alternative potentials V(beta) and V(gamma) as proposed by other authors.
