# Phase Transitions in Dense Matter and Recent Topics of Neutron Stars

> Core of neutron star consists of highly dense matter above normal nuclear density, where phase transitions is expected to take place. We review some phase transitions and recent topics of neutron stars.

# Phase Transitions in Dense Matter and Recent Topics of Neutron Stars

## Key claim

Kaon condensation is a plausible mechanism for the delayed collapse of protoneutron stars into low-mass black holes during the deleptonization era, but this conclusion depends on the use of a chiral Lagrangian formulation and the assumption that total baryon number is fixed; the thermal and neutrino-trapping effects largely suppress the occurrence of kaon condensation, and the maximum mass of cold neutron stars should be determined in the neutrino-free and hot stage.

## Method

The authors use a chiral Lagrangian formulation with heavy-baryon limit for nucleons to construct the thermodynamic potential for kaon condensed matter at finite temperature, including thermal and neutrino-trapped effects. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation with the resulting equation of state (EOS) to obtain gravitational mass versus central density and total baryon number for protoneutron stars, comparing neutrino-trapped and neutrino-free cases at different entropies per baryon. For the mixed phase of first-order phase transitions, they compare the Maxwell construction with the Gibbs conditions, reviewing results from Glendenning and Schaffner-Bielich (1998, 1999).

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 \simeq 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$
- Maximum mass of neutron stars using normal matter EOS: about $2.0 M_\odot$
- Observed neutron star masses from radio pulsar systems: $1.35 \pm 0.04 M_\odot$
- Maximum mass with kaon condensation: $M_{max} \simeq 1.4-1.6 M_\odot$
- Critical density for kaon condensation occurrence: $\rho_c \simeq 2.3 \rho_0$ (from Maxwell construction discussion)
- For $S=2$ and neutrino-trapped case, maximum-mass star central density: $\rho_c = 5.4 \rho_0$ (residing in normal branch)
- For $S=0$ or $1$ neutrino-trapped case, maximum-mass star central density: $\rho_c \simeq 10 \rho_0$ (in kaon condensed branch)
- Mixed phase droplet radius for a $1.5 M_\odot$ neutron star: about $3\ \mathrm{km}$
- Magnetar magnetic fields: $B \sim 10^{14-15}\ \mathrm{G}$
- Estimated magnetization in strange stars at $\rho \sim 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$: $B \sim 10^{15-17}\ \mathrm{G}$
- Energy per baryon for non-interacting massless quarks in bag model: $\varepsilon_{u,d} \simeq 934\ \mathrm{MeV} \cdot (B^{1/4}/145)$, $\varepsilon_{u,d,s} \simeq 829\ \mathrm{MeV} \cdot (B^{1/4}/145)$

## Context

This review extends the line of work on kaon condensation in neutron stars initiated by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and developed by Thorsson, Prakash, and Lattimer (1994) and Fujii et al. (1996), and addresses the delayed collapse scenario proposed by Brown and Bethe (1994). The authors present their own chiral-symmetry-based formulation (Tatsumi and Yasuhira, 1998, 1999) for finite-temperature kaon condensation, contrasting their results with those of Pons et al. (2000) who used a meson exchange model. The discussion of the mixed phase problem follows Glendenning (1992) and Glendenning and Schaffner-Bielich (1998, 1999), who showed that the Gibbs conditions rather than the Maxwell construction are required for first-order phase transitions in nuclear matter with two chemical potentials. The work enables future dynamical evolution studies of protoneutron star collapse including neutrino opacity effects, and motivates further investigation of strange stars as magnetar candidates following Tatsumi (2000).
