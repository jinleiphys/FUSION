# The shear and bulk relaxation times from the general correlation functions

> In this paper we present two quantum field theoretical analyses on the shear and bulk relaxation times. First, we discuss how to find Kubo formulas for the shear and the bulk relaxation times. Next, we provide results on the shear viscosity relaxation time obtained within the diagrammatic approach for the massless $λϕ^4$ theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives Kubo formulas for both shear and bulk relaxation times from general correlation functions, but the actual results for the bulk relaxation time are presented only as formal Kubo relations, while the shear relaxation time is computed numerically for the specific case of massless lambda phi^4 theory.

## Method

The authors parametrize the most general forms of the stress-energy tensor retarded Green functions using the gravitational Ward identity and symmetry properties, then match the denominators of these parametrized correlation functions to the hydrodynamic dispersion relations for shear and sound modes. For the shear relaxation time in scalar field theory, they solve the resulting integral equations for the imaginary and real parts of the retarded Green functions numerically within the real-time formalism, using both one-loop and multi-loop resummation approaches.

## Key numbers

* The ratio (epsilon+P) tau_pi / eta varies between 6.11 and 6.55 as a function of m_th/T in the range shown, for massless lambda phi^4 theory.
* The ratio (epsilon+P) tau_pi / eta generally varies between 5 and 7 across different theories.
* The static susceptibility kappa is of order O(lambda^0 T^2) in the weak coupling limit, as shown in prior work by Romatschke (2009) and Moore (2012).

## Context

This work extends the quantum-field-theoretical determination of second-order transport coefficients, building on prior studies of first-order coefficients by Jeon (1992) and Arnold (2002), and on second-order studies by York (2008) and Hong (2010). The Kubo formula for the bulk relaxation time is consistent with the metric perturbation analysis of Hong (2010) but differs from the projection operator method of Huang (2010). The numerical results for the shear relaxation time agree with kinetic theory calculations by York (2008) and Denicol (2014). The derived Kubo formulas enable first-principles computation of both shear and bulk relaxation times in quantum field theories.
