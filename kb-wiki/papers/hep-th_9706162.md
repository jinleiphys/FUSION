# Variational Perturbation Approach to Resonance-State Wave Functions

# Variational Perturbation Approach to Resonance-State Wave Functions

## Key claim
The variational perturbation method for wave functions, previously shown to work for bound states of the anharmonic oscillator, can be generalized to resonance states of the anharmonic oscillator with negative coupling constant by allowing the trial frequency to take complex values, yielding uniformly accurate wave functions starting from bound-state wave functions of the harmonic oscillator.

## Method
The author introduces a trial frequency Ω as an artificial parameter into the Hamiltonian, then performs Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory in a dummy expansion parameter δ. The wave function is optimized by imposing the principle of minimum sensitivity, requiring the derivative with respect to Ω to vanish at δ=1, which yields Ω as a function of position x. For the negative-coupling anharmonic oscillator, the method is combined with the complex-coordinate method, rotating the coordinate x → x e^{iθ} with θ = -π/6, to obtain normalizable resonance wave functions.

## Key numbers
- For λ = -0.2, the squared norm error ‖φ^exact - φ^(1)‖² between the first-order variational wave function and the exact wave function is about 0.0016.
- The asymptotic behavior of the optimized first-order wave function for the negative-coupling anharmonic oscillator is ψ₀^(1)(x;Ω(x)) → exp(∓ i √(|λ|/8) |x|³) as x → ∞, compared to the exact asymptotic form exp(∓ i √(|λ|/9) |x|³).
- The border between the tunneling regime and the sliding regime for the Hamiltonian H(λ) = ½(-d²/dx² + x² + λx⁴) is at λ ≈ -0.3387.
- For λ ≤ -0.12, the physical solutions of the optimization condition with real x form a complex conjugate pair in the whole x domain; for λ ≥ -0.12, there appear domains where the physical solutions split into two different real values.

## Context
This work extends the variational perturbation method for wave functions previously developed by Tanaka for the anharmonic oscillator and double-well potential (Tanaka, 1997) to resonance states, motivated by the demonstration by Karrlein and Kleinert (1997) that complex-valued optimization can extract the imaginary part of energy eigenvalues for the negative-coupling anharmonic oscillator. The method enables perturbative calculation of resonance wave functions for tunneling systems, with potential applicability to Zeeman and Stark effects, and overcomes the limitations of naive Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation which at any finite order yields only finite superpositions of harmonic oscillator wave functions.
