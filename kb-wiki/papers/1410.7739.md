# Principal component analysis of event-by-event fluctuations

> We apply principal component analysis to the study of event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. This method brings out all the information contained in two-particle correlations in a physically transparent way. We present a guide to the method, and apply it to multiplicity fluctuations and anisotropic flow, using ALICE data and simulated events. In particular, we study elliptic and triangular flow fluctuations as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity. This method reveals previously unknown subleading modes in both rapidity and transverse momentum for the momentum distribution as well as elliptic and triangular flows.

# Principal component analysis of event-by-event fluctuations

## Key claim

Principal component analysis (PCA) of the two-particle correlation matrix \(V_{n\Delta}(p_1,p_2)\) can extract previously unknown subleading modes of anisotropic flow fluctuations in both rapidity and transverse momentum, but the method assumes that nonflow correlations are negligible and cannot systematically disentangle flow fluctuations from nonflow without specific additional assumptions.

## Method

The authors construct the two-particle correlation matrix \(V_{n\Delta}(p_1,p_2)\) from event-by-event flow vectors \(Q_n(p)\) binned in transverse momentum and/or pseudorapidity, subtracting self-correlations and mean values. They diagonalize this matrix to obtain eigenvalues \(\lambda^{(\alpha)}\) and eigenvectors \(\psi^{(\alpha)}(p)\), defining principal components \(V_n^{(\alpha)}(p) \equiv \sqrt{\lambda^{(\alpha)}}\psi^{(\alpha)}(p)\) and normalized coefficients \(v_n^{(\alpha)}(p) \equiv V_n^{(\alpha)}(p)/\langle V_0(p)\rangle\). The analysis is applied to \(10^4\) AMPT-simulated Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV in 0-10% centrality and to ALICE experimental data from the same system.

## Key numbers

- Leading multiplicity fluctuation mode \(v_0^{(1)}(\eta)\) is a global 12% relative fluctuation, independent of pseudorapidity, corresponding to total multiplicity variation within the event sample.
- For multiplicity fluctuations in pseudorapidity, the second eigenvalue is \(\lambda^{(2)} \sim \lambda^{(1)}/60\) and the third is \(\lambda^{(3)} \sim \lambda^{(2)}/5\).
- For elliptic and triangular flow fluctuations in pseudorapidity, the subleading mode has \(\lambda^{(2)} \simeq \lambda^{(1)}/13\).
- For ALICE data in transverse momentum, the eigenvalue ratio between leading and subleading modes is \(\lambda^{(1)}/\lambda^{(2)} \sim 400\) for \(n=2\) and \(\sim 300\) for \(n=3\).
- The analysis uses \(10^4\) Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV in the 0-10% centrality range, with pseudorapidity bins of 0.5 over \(-3 < \eta < 3\) (12 bins total).

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic flow fluctuations beyond traditional event-plane and cumulant methods (Poskanzer and Voloshin, 1998; Borghini, Dinh, and Ollitrault, 2001), which were developed before event-by-event fluctuations were recognized as crucial. The PCA approach fully exploits the double-differential structure of two-particle correlations, which previous work had used only to test factorization breaking via the Pearson correlation coefficient (Aamodt et al., 2011; Gardim, Grassi, Luzum, and Ollitrault, 2012). The method enables direct comparison of subleading flow modes with hydrodynamic model calculations, providing additional constraints on the initial-state dynamics and the quark-gluon plasma response to initial geometry.
