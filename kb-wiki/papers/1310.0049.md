# Analytical representations of unified equations of state for neutron-star matter

> Context. A unified equation of state (EoS) should describe the crust and the core of a neutron star using the same physical model. The Brussels-Montreal group has recently derived a family of such EoSs based on the nuclear energy-density functional theory with generalized Skyrme effective forces, fitted to the available mass data. At the same time, these forces were constrained to reproduce microscopic calculations of homogeneous neutron matter based on realistic two- and three-nucleon forces. Aims. We represent basic physical characteristics of the latest Brussels-Montreal EoS models by analytical expressions to facilitate their inclusion in astrophysical simulations. Methods. We consider three EoS models, which significantly differ by stiffness: BSk19, BSk20, and BSk21. For each of them we constructed two versions of the EoS parametrization. In the first version, pressure P and gravitational mass density ρare given as functions of the baryon number density n_b. In the second version, P, ρ, and n_b are given as functions of pseudo-enthalpy, which is useful for two-dimensional calculations of stationary rotating configurations of neutron stars. In addition to the EoS, we derived analytical expressions for several related quantities that are required in neutron-star simulations: number fractions of electrons and muons in the stellar core, nucleon numbers per nucleus in the inner crust, and equivalent radii and shape parameters of the nuclei in the inner crust. Results. We obtain analytical representations for the basic characteristics of the models of cold dense matter, which are most important for studies of neutron stars. We demonstrate the usability of our results by applying them to calculations of neutron-star mass-radius relations, maximum and minimum masses, thresholds of direct Urca processes, and the electron conductivity in the neutron-star crust.

## Key claim

The paper provides analytical fitting expressions for three unified Brussels-Montreal equations of state (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) that describe cold catalyzed neutron-star matter from the outer crust to the core, but these fits are limited to densities above 10^6 g cm^-3 and assume the "minimal model" of nucleon-lepton matter without exotic particles.

## Method

The authors constructed analytical parametrizations for pressure P as a function of gravitational mass density ρ, mass density as a function of baryon number density n_b, and density as a function of pseudo-enthalpy H, using fractional-polynomial functions matched with exponential switching functions. For each of the three EoS models (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21), they also derived analytical expressions for electron and muon number fractions in the core, nucleon numbers per nucleus in the inner crust, and equivalent radii and shape parameters of nuclei in the inner crust, based on the nuclear energy-density functional theory with generalized Skyrme effective forces fitted to 2149 measured atomic masses.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation of the three EDFs to 2149 measured atomic masses: 0.583 MeV (BSk19), 0.583 MeV (BSk20), 0.577 MeV (BSk21)
- Saturation density n_{b,0}: 0.1596 fm^{-3} (BSk19 and BSk20), 0.1582 fm^{-3} (BSk21)
- Symmetry energy coefficient J: 30.0 MeV for all three models
- Symmetry energy slope L: 31.9 MeV (BSk19), 37.4 MeV (BSk20), 46.6 MeV (BSk21)
- Incompressibility K_v: 237.3 MeV (BSk19), 241.4 MeV (BSk20), 245.8 MeV (BSk21)
- Limiting baryonic density n_caus after which EoSs violate causality: 1.45 fm^{-3} (BSk19), 0.98 fm^{-3} (BSk20), 0.99 fm^{-3} (BSk21)
- Typical fit error of pressure P is approximately 1% for log10(ρ/g cm^{-3}) greater than 6, with maximum error 3.7% at ξ=9.51
- Typical fit error of Δ_ρ (deviation from linear law) is 1-2%, with maximum relative error 4.2% at the low end of fitted density range (10^6 g cm^{-3})
- Fit residuals for electron and muon number fractions are typically (1-3)×10^{-4} with maximum 7×10^{-4}

## Context

This work extends the analytical representation approach developed by Haensel and Potekhin (2004) for the FPS and SLy4 unified EoS models to the newer Brussels-Montreal family of EoSs (BSk19-21). The paper enables astrophysical simulations of neutron-star structure, including mass-radius relations, maximum and minimum masses, thresholds of direct Urca processes, and electron conductivity in the crust, by providing publicly available Fortran subroutines. The authors argue against the rejection of BSk19-21 by Dutra et al. (2012), noting that these EDFs remain well-adapted for unified treatment of all neutron-star regions.
