# Hybrid quark-hadron equation of state for multi-messenger astronomy

> We present the recent progress of the hybrid quark-meson-nucleon model for multi-messenger astronomy that unifies the thermodynamics of quark and hadronic degrees of freedom. The obtained equation of state is in accordance with nuclear matter properties at saturation density and with the flow constraint from heavy ion collision experiments. The mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for compact stars are calculated and compared with the latest astrophysical observations. The phase diagram of the isospin-symmetirc matter is studied as well in terms of the higher-order cumulants of the net-baryon number.

## Key claim

The hybrid quark-meson-nucleon (QMN) model unifies hadronic and quark degrees of freedom in a single framework where chiral symmetry is restored within the hadronic phase before quark deconfinement occurs, and the resulting equations of state comply with modern multi-messenger constraints only when the dynamical confinement mechanism provides sufficient stiffening at densities just above saturation.

## Method

The authors construct the thermodynamic potential of the hybrid QMN model in the mean-field approximation, combining baryonic parity doublet nucleons (positive-parity N(938) and negative-parity N(1535) states) with up and down quarks, coupled to chiral fields (sigma, pions), an isoscalar vector field (omega), and an isovector vector field (rho). Statistical confinement is implemented via medium-dependent modified Fermi-Dirac distribution functions that suppress nucleons above a momentum cutoff and quarks below it, controlled by an auxiliary scalar field b and a dimensionless parameter alpha. The model parameters are fixed from prior work (Marczenko 2020), with the quark-vector coupling treated as a free parameter chi times the nucleon-vector coupling, and four representative values of alpha b0 (350, 370, 400, 450 MeV) are studied.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum expectation value of the auxiliary b-field times alpha is chosen from four values: alpha b0 = 350, 370, 400, 450 MeV (the allowed range is 300-450 MeV from Benic 2015 and Marczenko 2017).
- The parity-doublet mass parameter m0 is set to 700 MeV, giving degenerate masses m+ = m- = m0 when chiral symmetry is restored.
- For alpha b0 = 350, 370, and 400 MeV, the maximal speed of sound within a 1.4 solar mass neutron star lies above the conformal limit (c_s^2 = 1/3) and above the constraint from Reed (2019); for alpha b0 = 450 MeV, the maximal speed of sound does not exceed the conformal value.
- The maximal mass of compact stars is always reached within the hadronic branch of the sequence; for chi = 0 this occurs just before the onset of down quarks, while for chi = 0.1 and 0.2 the hadronic branch extends beyond the maximal-mass density but does not produce stable hybrid stars.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid QMN model developed by Benic (2015) and Marczenko (2017-2020) by systematically studying the equation of state under neutron-star conditions and comparing with astrophysical constraints. It contrasts with conventional two-phase approaches (Bastian 2015) that assume a first-order hadron-quark transition with simultaneous chiral and deconfinement transitions, instead embedding both phenomena dynamically. The results enable future studies of higher-order cumulants of net-baryon number in the phase diagram and may inform interpretations of multi-messenger observations from events like GW170817 (Abbott 2018) and NICER data (Miller 2019).
