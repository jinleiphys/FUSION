# Dynamical model for Pion - Nucleon Bremsstrahlung

> A dynamical model based on effective Lagrangians is proposed to describe the bremsstrahlung reaction $ πN \to πN γ$ at low energies. The $Δ(1232)$ degrees of freedom are incorporated in a way consistent with both, electromagnetic gauge invariance and invariance under contact transformations. The model also includes the initial and final state rescattering of hadrons via a T-matrix with off-shell effects. The $πN γ$ differential cross sections are calculated using three different T-matrix models and the results are compared with the soft photon approximation, and with experimental data. The aim of this analysis is to test the off-shell behavior of the different T-matrices under consideration.

## Key claim
A dynamical model based on effective Lagrangians can describe the πN → πNγ bremsstrahlung reaction at low energies, but the model's predictions lie above experimental data and the soft-photon approximation for photon energies above 20 MeV, indicating that off-shell effects from different T-matrix models do not cancel as they do in the soft-photon expansion.

## Method
The authors constructed a gauge-invariant amplitude for πN bremsstrahlung using effective Lagrangians that include nucleon, Δ(1232) isobar, π, ρ, and σ meson degrees of freedom, with electromagnetic currents derived from minimal coupling. They implemented three different T-matrix models (OBQA, SEP, and NEW) for πN rescattering, each with different off-shell behavior, and compared the resulting differential cross sections to experimental data and the soft-photon approximation for the π⁺p → π⁺pγ reaction at incident pion energies of 269, 298, 299, and 324 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Incident pion laboratory energies used: 269, 298, 299, and 324 MeV (from experiments EXP I and EXP II)
- Photon energy range considered: ωγ = 0–150 MeV (EXP I) and ωγ = 0–140 MeV (EXP II)
- Cutoff parameters Λ (in MeV) for the monopole form factor, adjusted per T-matrix model and incident energy: Λ_OBQA = 750, 700, 600; Λ_SEP = 700, 600, 500; Λ_NEW = 550, 500, 450 for T_lab = 269, 298, 324 MeV respectively
- For EXP II at T_lab = 299 MeV, the Λ values from T_lab = 298 MeV were kept
- The SEP interaction provided the closest results to experimental cross sections, with deviations starting for ωγ > 40 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of πN bremsstrahlung within the soft-photon approximation by Liou (1985, 1988, 1992), which depend only on the on-shell πN T-matrix and cannot probe off-shell effects. The dynamical model enables testing the off-shell behavior of three different T-matrix models (OBQA from Schutz, SEP from Nozawa, and NEW) that are indistinguishable in elastic πN scattering. The results are compared to experimental data from Nefkens (EXP I) and Meyer (EXP II), and the analysis shows that the SEP interaction gives the smallest off-shell effects, consistent with a previous study by Nakayama on pion photo-production observables.
