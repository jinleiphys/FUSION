# The Ginzburg-Landau Free Energy Functional of Color Superconductivity at Weak Coupling

> We derive the Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional of color superconductivity in terms of the thermal diagrams of QCD in its perturbative region. The zero mode of the quadratic term coefficient yields the same transition temperature, including the pre-exponential factor, as the one obtained previously from the Fredholm determinant of the two quark scattering amplitude. All coefficients of the free energy can be made identical to those of a BCS model by setting the Fermi velocity of the latter equal to the speed of light. We also calculate the induced symmetric color condensate near $T_c$ and find that it scales as the cubic power of the dominant antisymmetric color component. We show that in the presence of an inhomogeneity and a nonzero gauge potential, while the color-flavor locked condensate dominates in the bulk, the unlocked condensate, the octet, emerges as a result of a simultaneous color-flavor rotation in the core region of a vortex filament or at the junction of super and normal phases.

## Key claim

The Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional of color superconductivity at ultra-high chemical potential can be derived from thermal QCD diagrams in the perturbative regime, and its coefficients become identical to those of a BCS model when the Fermi velocity of the latter is set to the speed of light, despite the non-BCS scaling of the transition temperature.

## Method

The authors employed the Jona-Lasinio formulation, identifying the Ginzburg-Landau free energy as the generating functional of proper vertices, with each coefficient associated with a set of thermal diagrams in the normal phase. They calculated the quadratic and quartic terms of the free energy functional by evaluating the di-quark propagator and its inverse from the Schwinger-Dyson equation, using hard-dense-loop (HDL) resummation for the irreducible two-quark scattering vertex and the single-quark self-energy. The analysis was restricted to s-wave pairing with quarks above the Dirac sea, decomposing the order parameter into color-antisymmetric (attractive) and color-symmetric (repulsive) channels.

## Key numbers

* Transition temperature formula: \(k_B T_C = 512\pi^3 e^\gamma \left(\frac{2}{N_f}\right)^{5/2} \frac{\mu}{g^5} e^{-\sqrt{\frac{6N_c}{N_c+1}} \frac{\pi^2}{g} - \frac{\pi^2+4}{16}(N_c-1)}\), where \(g\) is the QCD running coupling at \(\mu\) and \(\gamma = 0.5772\) is the Euler constant.
* The induced color-symmetric condensate near \(T_c\) scales as the cubic power of the dominant color-antisymmetric condensate.
* The width of the critical region is given by \(|T - T_C|/T_C \sim (k_B T_C / \mu)^4\), which is "exceedingly narrow."
* The Debye mass is \(m_D = N_f g^2 \mu^2 / (2\pi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD approach to color superconductivity previously developed by Son (1999), Schafer and Wilczek (1999), Pisarski and Rischke (2000), Hong (2000), Brown, Liu, and Ren (2000), and Hsu and Schwetz (2000), which yielded the asymptotic formula for the transition temperature. The derived Ginzburg-Landau free energy for a homogeneous system agrees with the leading-order result of Iida and Baym (2001) in the absence of the color-symmetric condensate component. The formalism enables the study of inhomogeneous systems, including vortex filaments and the interface between super and normal phases, where the unlocked octet condensate emerges due to simultaneous color-flavor rotation.
