# New approach to low energy Virtual Compton Scattering and generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon

> Virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon (VCS) is studied in the regime of low energy of the outgoing real photon. This regime allows one to directly access the generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon in a VCS experiment. In the derivation of the low energy theorem for VCS that exists in the literature, the low energy limit taken for virtual initial photons does not match on that for real photons, when one approaches the initial photon's mass shell. While this problem has for a long time been attributed to the non-analyticity of the Compton amplitude with respect to the photon virtuality, I demonstrate that it is merely due to an ill-defined low energy limit for VCS, on one hand, and to a particular way of constructing the VCS amplitude, use in the literature, on the other. I provide a uniform description of low energy Compton scattering with real and virtual photons by defining a Lorentz-covariant operator sub-basis for Compton scattering in that regime, that has six independent structures. Correspondingly, six new generalized polarizabilities are introduced in the Breit frame. These polarizabilities are defined as continuous functions of the photon virtuality and at the real photon point match onto the nucleon polarizabilities known from real Compton scattering.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the long-standing mismatch between the low-energy limits of virtual and real Compton scattering, previously attributed to non-analyticity of the Compton amplitude with respect to photon virtuality, is actually an artifact of an ill-defined low-energy limit and a particular non-covariant construction of the VCS amplitude used in the literature.

## Method

The author rewrites the VCS amplitude in terms of electromagnetic field strength tensors in a Lorentz-covariant form, then defines a new crossing-symmetric low-energy limit path on the Mandelstam plane that ensures the limit lies inside the physical region. By constructing four linear combinations of the original spin-dependent tensors, the author reduces the VCS amplitude to six independent structures that match the six real Compton scattering structures at the real photon point, and introduces six new generalized polarizabilities in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- Six independent structures are identified for the low-energy VCS amplitude, matching the six amplitudes of real Compton scattering (F1, F2, F4, F6, F10, F11)
- Six new generalized polarizabilities are introduced in the Breit frame as continuous functions of photon virtuality Q²
- The low-energy limit point is defined at s = u = M², ν = 0, t + Q² = -2(qq') = 0
- The path for approaching the low-energy limit is given by t(ω, ω₀, t₀) + Q² = (ω²/ω₀²)(t₀ + Q²)
- The determinant of the transformation matrix for the new linear combinations is 2(ν² + Q²)² > 0

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorem for virtual Compton scattering originally developed by Guichon et al. (1995) and addresses the mismatch problem where only four of the ten generalized polarizabilities matched their real Compton scattering counterparts, with two vanishing at the real photon point. The paper builds on the Lorentz-covariant approach of L'vov et al. (1997) and the full low-energy expansion of real Compton scattering by Ragusa (1988), while also connecting to the recent resolution of a similar problem in forward doubly virtual Compton scattering by Gorchtein (2008). This enables a consistent framework for extracting generalized polarizabilities from VCS experiments that continuously connect to the known nucleon polarizabilities from real Compton scattering.
