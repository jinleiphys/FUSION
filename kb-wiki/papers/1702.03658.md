# Decay width of $d^*(2380) \to NN π$ process in a chiral constituent quark model

> The width of three-body single-pion decay process $d^*\to NNπ^{0,\pm}$ is calculated by using the $d^*$ wave function obtained from our chiral SU(3) constituent quark model calculation. The effect of the dynamical structure on the width of $d^*$ is taken into account in both the single $ΔΔ$ channel and coupled $ΔΔ+CC$ two-channel approximations. Our numerical result shows that in the coupled-channel approximation, namely, the hidden-color configuration being considered, the obtained partial decay width of $d^*\to NNπ$ is about several hundred $\rm {KeV}$, while in the single $ΔΔ$ channel it is just about $2\sim 3~\rm{MeV}$. We, therefore, conclude that the partial width in the single-pion decay process of $d^*$ is much smaller than the widths in its double-pion decay processes. Our prediction may provide a criterion for judging different interpretations of the $d^*$ structure, as different pictures for the $d^*$ may result quite different partial decay width.

## Key claim

The partial decay width of the single-pion decay process \(d^* \to NN\pi\) is predicted to be much smaller than the widths of the double-pion decay modes, with the coupled-channel calculation (including the hidden-color CC component) yielding a width of about 670 keV, which is consistent with the current experimental non-observation of this decay mode; this small width contrasts with predictions from models that treat \(d^*\) as a \(\Delta N \pi\) system.

## Method

The authors used the \(d^*\) wave function obtained from a chiral SU(3) constituent quark model calculation within the Resonating Group Method (RGM), considering both a single \(\Delta\Delta\) channel and a coupled \(\Delta\Delta+CC\) (hidden-color) two-channel approximation. They employed a phenomenological effective Hamiltonian for the pseudo-scalar \(qq\pi\) interaction in the non-relativistic approximation and computed the three-body decay width via 12 Feynman diagrams (with pion exchange and intermediate nucleon states) using time-ordered perturbation theory, ignoring final-state interactions between the outgoing nucleons due to the high partial waves required.

## Key numbers

* In the single \(\Delta\Delta\) channel approximation, the total decay width for \(d^* \to pn\pi^0\) is 2.276 MeV.
* In the coupled \(\Delta\Delta+CC\) two-channel approximation, the total decay width for \(d^* \to pn\pi^0\) is 0.670 MeV (670 keV).
* The \(\Delta\Delta\) component constitutes about 31.5% of the \(d^*\) wave function in the coupled-channel case.
* Assuming a total width of about 75 MeV for \(d^*\), the predicted branching ratio for \(d^* \to pn\pi^0\) is 3.0% in the one-channel case and 0.9% in the coupled-channel case.
* The extracted coupling constant \(G = g_{qq\pi} I_o\) is about 5.41 GeV\(^{-1}\), obtained from the experimental \(\Delta \to \pi N\) decay width of \(\sim 117\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of the double-pion decay modes \(d^* \to d\pi\pi\) and \(d^* \to NN\pi\pi\) (Dong et al., 2016) using the same chiral quark model wave function, which had successfully reproduced the observed total width of about 72 MeV. The predicted small single-pion width contradicts the sizable cross section predicted by a \(\Delta N \pi\) model (Gal, 2015) where the \(d^*\) root-mean-square radius is about 1.5 fm, whereas the present model yields a radius of only about 0.8 fm. The result provides a criterion to distinguish between the compact six-quark (hidden-color dominated) interpretation and the \(\Delta N \pi\) molecular picture of the \(d^*\) dibaryon.
