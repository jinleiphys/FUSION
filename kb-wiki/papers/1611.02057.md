# Theoretical photo-disintegration of $^{16}$O

> The photodisintegration of $^{16}$O is predicted to be dominated by $E$2 excitation in the vicinity of the $α$-particle threshold. The reaction rates of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O are expected to be determined from this reaction.

## Key claim

The photodisintegration of $^{16}$O is predicted to be dominated by $E2$ excitation in the vicinity of the $\alpha$-particle threshold, based on a potential model where the direct capture component from the subthreshold $2^+_1$ state (rather than $E1$ transitions) determines the low-energy cross section.

## Method

The author uses a potential model (PM) to generate scattering and bound states of the $\alpha$+$^{12}$C system, with the potential strength adjusted to reproduce the $\alpha$-particle separation energy for the ground state of $^{16}$O. The photoelectric cross section $\sigma_{\gamma\alpha}$ is derived from the capture cross section $\sigma_{\alpha\gamma}$ via the relation $\sigma_{\gamma\alpha}= [k_\alpha^2/(2k_\gamma^2)]\,\sigma_{\alpha\gamma}$, and the effective charge is obtained from $\chi^2$ optimization of astrophysical $S$-factor data from prior experiments. Experimental resonances are temporarily appended in Breit-Wigner form to examine their contribution to reaction rates.

## Key numbers

- The subthreshold $2^+_1$ state lies at $E_{c.m.}=-0.245$ MeV relative to the $\alpha$+$^{12}$C threshold.
- The $E2/E1$ ratio $\sigma_{E2}/\sigma_{E1}$ is 9.0 at $E_\gamma=8.0$ MeV, 2.3 at $E_\gamma=8.5$ MeV, 0.42 at $E_\gamma=9.0$ MeV, and 0.03 at $E_\gamma=9.5$ MeV.
- The $\alpha$-particle threshold for $^{16}$O is at $E_\gamma=7.162$ MeV.
- The resonant contribution to reaction rates is less than 4% difference compared to the direct capture component below the barrier.
- The most important energy for helium burning is $E_{c.m.}=0.3$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous potential model studies of $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O (Katsuma 2008, 2010, 2012) and predicts the photodisintegration cross section for future experiments. It contrasts with the strong coupling mechanism of prior $R$-matrix analyses, arguing instead that weak coupling and the $\alpha$+$^{12}$C molecular states (the $2^+_1$ state at $E_x=6.92$ MeV and the $1^-_2$ state at $E_x=9.59$ MeV) dominate the reaction. The predicted $E2$ dominance and angular distributions enable experimental tests using photodisintegration to determine the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction rates more accurately than direct measurements at helium burning temperatures.
