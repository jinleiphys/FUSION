# Enhanced pion production in DCC dynamics

> In order to elucidate the enhancement of pion production that may occur during dynamical scenarios of interest in connection with disoriented chiral condensates, we study the evolution of boson modes whose frequencies have a given arbitrary time dependence. The quantum-field treatment yields expressions for the time dependence of observables that depend only on the initial state and specific state-independent enhancement coefficients which can be obtained from the given evolution of the frequency. It is shown how these coefficients also can be obtained within an entirely classical framework by judicious usage of the dependence of the resulting enhancement on the phase of the initial state. Illustrative application is made for frequency evolutions obtained from idealized simulations of high-energy heavy-ion collisions and it is shown how the non-equilibrium evolution of the order parameter may lead to significant enhancements in the final pion spectra.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the enhancement of pion production in disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) dynamics can be described by state-independent coefficients that depend only on the time evolution of the effective pion mass, and that these same coefficients can be obtained from a purely classical treatment by exploiting the dependence of the enhancement on the phase of the initial state, rather than requiring a full quantum-field calculation.

## Method

The author diagonalizes the Hamiltonian for boson modes with time-dependent frequencies into independent harmonic oscillators, then applies the Combescure (1987) method using SU(1,1) algebra and squeezing operators to derive the time evolution operator for each mode. The evolution of observables such as particle number, variance, and energy is expressed in terms of state-independent amplification coefficients \(Z_{mm'}(t)\) that depend only on the magnitude of the wave number \(\kappa\) and the time-dependent frequency \(\omega_\kappa^2(t) = \kappa^2 + \mu^2(t)\). The author then shows that these same coefficients can be extracted from a classical treatment by averaging over the initial phase \(\eta_0\) of coherent states.

## Key numbers

- The number amplification coefficient \(X_N = Z_{00}\) satisfies \(Z_{00} \geq 1\) and \(\ddot{Z}_{00}(0) = \omega_-^2\), where \(\omega_- \equiv (\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)/2\omega_0\)
- For an ensemble with random initial phase, the increase in particle number is \(\prec\Delta\nu(t)\succ = (Z_{00}(t)-1)(\nu_0 + \frac{1}{2})\)
- For constant positive \(\omega\), the number amplification oscillates as \(X_N(t) = (\omega_+^2/\omega^2) - (\omega_-^2/\omega^2)\cos 2\omega t\), with \(\omega_\pm \equiv (\omega^2 \pm \omega_0^2)/2\omega_0\)
- For supercritical motion with \(\mu^2 < 0\) and \(\Omega_\kappa^2 \equiv -\kappa^2 - \mu^2 > 0\), the modulus \(g_\kappa\) approaches unity at large times and the particle number exhibits exponential growth

## Context

This work extends the Hartree treatment of the linear sigma model used by Cooper (1994, 1995), Boyanovsky (1995), and Rajagopal (1993) to describe DCC formation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The key advance is showing that the quantum enhancement coefficients can be obtained classically, which enables the use of more tractable classical field simulations for calculating pion production in scenarios where the effective mass evolves rapidly, such as the quench scenario proposed by Rajagopal (1993) and Randrup (1998). This enables future work to incorporate particle production effects into dynamical simulations of the linear sigma model without requiring full quantum-field calculations.
