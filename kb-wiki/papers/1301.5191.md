# Evolution of single-particle states beyond $^{132}$Sn

> We have performed shell-model calculations for the two one valence-neutron isotones $^{135}$Te and $^{137}$Xe and the two one valence-proton isotopes $^{135,137}$Sb. The main aim of our study has been to investigate the evolution of single-particle states with increasing nucleon number. To this end, we have focused attention on the spectroscopic factors and the effective single-particle energies. In our calculations, we have employed a realistic low-momentum two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential that has already proved quite successful in describing the spectroscopic properties of nuclei in the $^{132}$Sn region. Comparison shows that our results reproduce very well the available experimental data. This gives confidence in the evolution of the single-particle states 4 figures predicted by the present study.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using a realistic low-momentum two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential reproduce available experimental data very well, giving confidence in the predicted evolution of single-particle states when adding neutron and proton pairs beyond the doubly magic nucleus $^{132}$Sn, though the study is largely predictive due to scant experimental data.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed for the one-valence-neutron isotones $^{135}$Te and $^{137}$Xe and the one-valence-proton isotopes $^{135,137}$Sb using the OXBASH code. The Hamiltonian employed single-particle energies taken from experiment and a two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized via the $V_{\rm low-k}$ approach, with valence protons occupying the 50-82 shell orbits and valence neutrons the 82-126 shell orbits. Energies, spectroscopic factors, and effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) were calculated and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For $^{135}$Te: calculated $7/2^-$ ground state spectroscopic factor $C^2S = 0.88$; yrast $13/2^+$ state at 2.268 MeV with $C^2S = 0.72$; experimental $13/2^+$ state at 2.109 MeV (calculated overestimated by 150 keV)
- For $^{137}$Xe: calculated $7/2^-$ ground state $C^2S = 0.86$ (experimental 0.94); yrast $9/2^-$ state at 1.327 MeV with $C^2S = 0.72$ (experimental 0.43); $13/2^+$ state at 2.082 MeV with $C^2S = 0.75$ (experimental 1.751 MeV, overestimated by 330 keV)
- For $^{135}$Sb: $7/2^+$ ground state $C^2S = 0.74$; $5/2^+$ state at 0.387 MeV with $C^2S = 0.42$; $11/2^-$ state at 2.652 MeV with $C^2S = 0.52$
- For $^{137}$Sb: $7/2^+$ ground state $C^2S = 0.71$; $5/2^+$ state at 0.186 MeV with $C^2S = 0.53$; $11/2^-$ state at 2.587 MeV with $C^2S = 0.38$
- Neutron ESPE spacing $i_{13/2}-f_{5/2}$ reduces from 700 keV in $^{133}$Sn to 100 keV in $^{137}$Xe
- Proton ESPEs show overall downshift of about 500 keV in $^{137}$Sb relative to $^{133}$Sb
- Monopole matrix element $V^M(\nu i_{13/2} \pi g_{7/2}) = -0.33$ MeV (most attractive for neutron orbits); $V^M(\nu f_{7/2} \pi d_{3/2}) = -0.35$ MeV (most attractive for proton orbits)

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei beyond $^{132}$Sn by Coraggio, Covello, Gargano, and Itaco (2009, 2011) and is motivated by recent ($d$,$p$) transfer reaction experiments at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (Jones 2010, 2011) and the HELIOS spectrometer test at ATLAS (Kay 2011). The study predicts stronger fragmentation of single-particle strengths in Sb isotopes compared to Te and Xe isotones, attributed to weaker neutron pairing beyond $N=82$ versus proton pairing beyond $Z=50$, which the authors trace to core-polarization effects (Covello 2012). The results are meant to guide future experimental studies with radioactive ion beams in the $^{132}$Sn region, which are also relevant for modeling $r$-process nucleosynthesis.
