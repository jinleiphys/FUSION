# Two Skyrmion Dynamics with Omega Mesons

> We present our first results of numerical simulations of two skyrmion dynamics using an $ω$-meson stabilized effective Lagrangian. We consider skyrmion-skyrmion scattering with a fixed initial velocity of $β=0.5$, for various impact parameters and groomings. The physical picture that emerges is surprisingly rich, while consistent with previous results and general conservation laws. We find meson radiation, skyrmion scattering out of the scattering plane, orbiting and capture to bound states.

## Key claim

The authors present first numerical simulations of two-skyrmion scattering using an omega-meson stabilized effective Lagrangian, finding that the dynamics are "surprisingly rich" while consistent with previous results and conservation laws; the abstract's claim of richness is hedged by the fact that results are limited to a single fixed initial velocity of beta=0.5 and only four impact parameters and groomings, making this an exploratory study rather than a comprehensive survey.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Euler-Lagrange equations derived from an effective Lagrangian coupling the nonlinear sigma model to an omega meson field, using the pion field magnitude pi and unit vector pihat (plus their time derivatives) as dynamical variables on a three-dimensional grid with 0.1 fm lattice spacing. They constructed initial states by placing boosted hedgehog skyrmions 9 fm apart in a simulation box with absorbing boundaries, exploiting four-fold spatial symmetry to simulate only one quadrant, and evolved the system using an implicit iterative scheme to cubic order in the timestep. The calculations were performed for skyrmion-skyrmion scattering at a fixed relative velocity of beta=0.5, for four distinct relative groomings (hedgehog-hedgehog, rotation by pi about the incident direction, rotation by pi about the impact parameter direction, and rotation by pi perpendicular to the scattering plane) and impact parameters of 0.4, 0.8, 1.6, and 2.8 fm.

## Key numbers

* Skyrmion mass: 791 MeV, obtained using f_pi = 62 MeV and g = m/(sqrt(2) f_pi) with m = 770 MeV from vector meson dominance.
* Fixed initial relative velocity: beta = 0.5, corresponding to a center-of-mass kinetic energy of 230 MeV.
* Lattice spacing: 0.1 fm, with a physical box size of 18 x 10 x 10 fermi and a mesh of 91 x 101 x 51 regular points plus absorbing boundary layers.
* Timesteps: 0.1 to 0.4 fm/c, corresponding to CFL ratios of 0.1 to 0.4, with calculations stable out to 50 fm/c and up to 5000 timesteps.
* Energy conservation: total energy conserved to within 3% for typically 20 fm/c in the absence of radiation, and to within 4% in long runs (40-60 fm/c) involving bound states.
* Impact parameters studied: 0.4 fm, 0.8 fm, 1.6 fm, and 2.8 fm for each grooming channel.
* Initial skyrmion separation: 9 fm between the two centers.

## Context

This work extends previous Skyrme model studies of baryon number two systems, which had been hampered by numerical instabilities associated with the fourth-order Skyrme term (Livermore group, 1990s; Caltech group, 1990s). By using omega meson stabilization instead of the Skyrme term, the authors achieve numerically stable simulations that reproduce known features such as right-angle scattering in the attractive channel (Verbaarschot et al., 1987) and repulsive behavior for certain groomings (Amado et al., 1990s), while also discovering new phenomena including meson radiation, scattering out of the scattering plane, orbiting, and capture to bound B=2 states. The success of these simulations enables future work on nucleon-antinucleon annihilation dynamics, which the authors identify as a problem ideally suited to the Skyrme approach but previously intractable due to numerical difficulties.
