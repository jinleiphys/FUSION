# Low-energy monopole strength in exotic Nickel isotopes

> Low-energy strength is predicted for the isoscalar monopole response of neutron-rich Ni isotopes, in calculations performed using the microscopic Skyrme HF+RPA and relativistic RHB+RQRPA models. Both models, although based on different energy density functionals, predict the occurrence of pronounced monopole states in the energy region between 10 MeV and 15 MeV, well separated from the isoscalar GMR. The analysis of transition densities and corresponding particle-hole configurations shows that these states represent almost pure neutron single hole-particle excitations. Even though their location is not modified with respect to the corresponding unperturbed states, their (Q)RPA strength is considerably enhanced by the residual interaction. The theoretical analysis predicts the gradual enhancement of low-energy monopole strength with neutron excess.

## Key claim

Both Skyrme HF+RPA and relativistic RHB+RQRPA models predict pronounced low-energy isoscalar monopole states in neutron-rich Ni isotopes between 10 MeV and 15 MeV, but these states are almost pure neutron single hole-particle excitations rather than collective modes, and their location is not modified by the residual interaction even though their strength is enhanced.

## Method

Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation (HF+RPA) calculations were performed for $^{68}$Ni and $^{78}$Ni using the SLy4 and SGII energy density functionals, with RPA equations solved in configuration space and the continuous single-particle spectrum discretized via diagonalization in a harmonic oscillator basis. Fully self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov plus relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RHB+RQRPA) calculations were also performed using the DD-ME2 functional, with the same interactions used in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. Transition densities and particle-hole configurations were analyzed to characterize the low-lying states.

## Key numbers

- Low-energy monopole states predicted between 10 MeV and 15 MeV excitation energy, well separated from the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) calculated at about 20 MeV in $^{68}$Ni
- In $^{68}$Ni with SLy4: three low-lying states at approximately 13.6 MeV, 14.6 MeV, and 15.6 MeV, each with a single neutron configuration contributing more than 98% to the total strength: (2p$_{3/2}$,3p$_{3/2}$), (2p$_{1/2}$,3p$_{1/2}$), and (1f$_{5/2}$,2f$_{5/2}$) respectively
- In $^{68}$Ni with RHB+RQRPA (DD-ME2): low-lying states at 12.16 MeV, 13.38 MeV, and 15.42 MeV, with the GMR at 18.94 MeV
- For $^{78}$Ni: pronounced low-energy strength predicted around 15 MeV by both models, more pronounced than in $^{68}$Ni
- Current experimental resolution for monopole measurements in unstable nuclei is approximately 2 MeV, with expected improvement to 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of low-energy multipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei, including the pygmy dipole resonance observed in $^{68}$Ni (Wieczorek 2009) and theoretical investigations of monopole response in $^{60}$Ca (Sagawa 1997). It contrasts with earlier Gogny-RPA calculations for $^{78}$Ni (Péron 2005) that did not predict pronounced low-lying monopole strength, and improves upon Skyrme-QRPA work (Terasaki 2006) where large smoothing obscured the low-energy pattern. The predictions enable interpretation of ongoing experiments at GANIL measuring monopole strength in $^{68}$Ni (Vandebrouck 2010) and guide future measurements with next-generation active targets such as ACTAR.
