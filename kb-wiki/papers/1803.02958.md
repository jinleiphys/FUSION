# Three-body Faddeev equations in two-particle Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas form with Distorted-Wave-Born-approximation amplitudes as effective potentials

> In this paper, I prove that the sub-Coulomb $A(d,p)B$ reaction amplitude, which is a solution of the three-body Faddeev equations in the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form, is peripheral if a peripheral is the corresponding DWBA amplitude. Hence the Faddeev's reaction amplitude for the sub-Coulomb $A(d,p)B$ reactions can also be parametrized in terms of the ANC of the $(n\,A)$ bound state. First, I consider the original AGS equations with separable potentials and prove that such equations are peripheral at sub-Coulomb energies. After that, the two-particle AGS equations are derived for the general potentials for sub-Coulomb transfer reactions. The effective AGS potentials are expressed in terms of the DWBA amplitudes for the sub-Coulomb reactions. Again, I demonstrate that the amplitude of the $A(d,p)B$ transfer reaction obtained from the AGS equation is peripheral and can be parametrized in terms of the ANC for the $(nA)$ bound state because the corresponding DWBA amplitude is peripheral. Finally, the AGS equations are generalized by including the optical nuclear potentials in the same manner as it is done in the DWBA. The obtained two-particle AGS equations contain the DWBA effective potentials with distorted waves generated by the sum of the nuclear optical and the channel Coulomb potentials. The AGS equation for the $A(d,p)B$ reactions is analyzed above the Coulomb barrier and it is shown again that the reaction amplitude satisfying generalized AGS equation with optical potentials depends on the ANC if a peripheral is the DWBA amplitude. The two-body AGS equations are generalized by including the intermediate three-body continuum and more than one bound state in each channel.

## Key claim
The paper proves that the amplitude for sub-Coulomb $A(d,p)B$ reactions, obtained as a solution of the three-body Faddeev equations in the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form, is peripheral if and only if the corresponding Distorted-Wave-Born-approximation (DWBA) amplitude is peripheral. This allows the Faddeev reaction amplitude to be parametrized in terms of the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the $(nA)$ bound state, a property that is shown to hold for sub-Coulomb energies and is extended to energies above the Coulomb barrier when optical potentials are included.

## Method
The author first analyzes the original AGS equations using separable potentials to demonstrate their peripheral nature at sub-Coulomb energies. The two-particle AGS equations are then derived for general potentials, where the effective AGS potentials are expressed in terms of DWBA amplitudes for sub-Coulomb transfer reactions. Finally, the AGS equations are generalized by incorporating optical nuclear potentials, analogous to the DWBA approach, resulting in effective potentials that use distorted waves generated by the sum of nuclear optical and channel Coulomb potentials.

- The AGS equations are generalized to include intermediate three-body continuum states and more than one bound state in each channel, extending the formalism beyond the single bound-state assumption.

## Context
This work extends the standard DWBA analysis of peripheral $A(d,p)B$ reactions, which is typically used to extract asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs), by proving that the same peripheral nature holds for the exact three-body Faddeev solution in the AGS form. It builds on the foundational Faddeev (1961) and Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (1967) formalisms, as well as the Coulomb-modified AGS equations of Alt (1978). The results enable the rigorous extraction of ANCs from transfer reactions using the full three-body framework, rather than relying solely on the approximate DWBA, and provide a path to include optical potentials and continuum effects in a consistent manner.
