# Bound states of $Θ^+$ in nuclei

> We study the binding energy and the width of the $Θ^+$ in nuclei, associated to the $K N$ and $ K πN$ components. The first one leads to negligible contributions while the second one leads to a sizeable attraction, enough to bind the $Θ^+$ in nuclei. Pauli blocking and binding effects on the $K N$ decay reduce considerably the $Θ^+$ decay width in nuclei and medium effects associated to the $ K πN$ component also lead to a very small width, as a consequence of which one finds separation between the bound levels considerably larger than the width of the states.

## Key claim

The two-meson cloud component of the $\Theta^+$ provides sufficient attraction to bind the $\Theta^+$ in nuclei, but the actual binding energies depend strongly on the chosen cutoff (700–800 MeV) and the assumed potential strength (60–120 MeV at normal nuclear matter density), with large uncertainties in the coupling constants fitted to the $N^*(1710)$ decay widths.

## Method

The authors used SU(3) invariant Lagrangians with two-meson vector and scalar couplings, fitted to the partial decay widths of the $N^*(1710)$ into $N\rho$ and $N\pi\pi$ (s-wave, I=0), to compute the $\Theta^+$ selfenergy in nuclear matter. The selfenergy was evaluated by dressing the pion (allowing $ph$ and $\Delta h$ excitations) and the kaon (using Chiral Perturbation Theory selfenergy), then solving for bound states in $^{12}C$ and $^{40}Ca$ using two different potential depths.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants: $g_{\bar{10}} = 0.72$ and $\tilde{g}_{\bar{10}} = 1.9$, with "quite large" uncertainties
- Regularizing cutoff of 700–800 MeV provides attraction of 60–120 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ($\rho = \rho_0$)
- For binding energies around 20 MeV, the imaginary part of the $\Theta^+$ selfenergy is about 3 MeV
- For a potential $V = -60$ MeV $\rho/\rho_0$ in $^{12}C$: 1s state at -34.0 MeV, 1p at -14.6 MeV, 2s at -0.3 MeV
- For $V = -60$ MeV $\rho/\rho_0$ in $^{40}Ca$: 1s at -42.6 MeV, 1p at -30.9 MeV, 1d at -18.7 MeV, 2s at -17.9 MeV, 1f at -6.3 MeV, 2p at -5.6 MeV
- For $V = -120$ MeV $\rho/\rho_0$ in $^{12}C$: 1s at -87.3 MeV, 1p at -59.5 MeV, 2s at -32.0 MeV, 1d at -31.9 MeV, 2p at -8.6 MeV, 1f at -5.6 MeV
- For $V = -120$ MeV $\rho/\rho_0$ in $^{40}Ca$: 1s at -98.2 MeV, 1p at -83.3 MeV, 1d at -67.5 MeV, 2s at -65.9 MeV, 1f at -50.8 MeV, 2p at -48.5 MeV, 1g at -33.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of the $\Theta^+$ exotic resonance (first observed by Nakano and Hyodo) by calculating its behavior in nuclear matter, building on prior studies of $KN$ and $Kd$ interactions by Gibbs (2004) and Sibirtsev (2004). The two-meson cloud component, previously studied for the antidecuplet by the same group (Cabrera, 2004; Manolo1, Manolo2), provides the novel binding mechanism, while the $KN$ component alone gives negligible attraction (consistent with Kim). The results enable experimental searches using the $(K,\pi)$ reaction in nuclei, as suggested by Nagahiro, with plans at KEK (Imai).
