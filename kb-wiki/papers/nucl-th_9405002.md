# Testing QCD Sum Rule Techniques on the Lattice

> Results for the first test of the ``crude'' QCD continuum model, commonly used in QCD Sum Rule analyses, are presented for baryon correlation functions. The QCD continuum model is found to effectively account for excited state contributions to the short-time regime of two-point correlation functions and allows the isolation of ground state properties. Confusion in the literature surrounding the physics represented in point-to-point correlation functions is also addressed. These results justify the use of the ``crude'' QCD continuum model and lend credence to the results of rigorous QCD Sum Rule analyses.

## Key claim

The "crude" QCD continuum model, which introduces a sharp threshold marking the onset of excited states, is found to effectively account for excited state contributions to the short-time regime of two-point correlation functions and allows the isolation of ground state properties, but the test is performed on lattice data where an artificial enhancement at very short times makes the test more demanding than in typical QCD Sum Rule analyses.

## Method

The author constructs the QCD continuum model for three-momentum-projected Euclidean-time two-point functions following the techniques established in the QCD Sum Rule approach, using the leading terms of the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) for the nucleon interpolating field. Lattice correlation functions from 28 quenched gauge configurations on a 24x12x12x24 lattice at beta=5.9 are fit with a pole plus QCD continuum model in analysis intervals from t=6 to t_f where t_f ranges from 11 through 23, extracting the nucleon mass, coupling strength, continuum threshold, and continuum model strength. The extracted ground state properties are compared with conventional lattice results from the tails of correlation functions and with fits using a two-pole spectral density.

## Key numbers

* The lattice spacing is determined to be a=0.132(4) fm, with a^{-1}=1.49(5) GeV.
* Three values of the Wilson hopping parameter are used: kappa_1=0.152, kappa_2=0.154, and kappa_3=0.156, with linear extrapolation to kappa_cr=0.1598(2) where the squared pion mass vanishes.
* Fitting time slices t=6 and t=7 to functional forms t^{-7}, t^{-6}, and t^{-5} yields chi^2/dof of 80, 4.8, and 230 respectively, confirming the leading 1/t^6 behavior.
* The typical chi^2/dof for pole plus QCD continuum model fits is 0.6 for the heaviest quark mass and 0.53 for the lightest quark mass.
* The optimum value for the ultraviolet cutoff Lambda is 4.55(3) in lattice units.
* The continuum model strength parameter xi is nearly independent of quark mass when mean-field-improved operators are used, but varies significantly if the naive sqrt(2 kappa) normalization is used.

## Context

This work provides the first lattice test of the QCD continuum model commonly used in QCD Sum Rule analyses since the original formulation by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979). It extends earlier lattice studies by Chu et al. (1993) that examined coordinate-space correlation functions, by focusing on three-momentum-projected correlators and including higher-order OPE terms not investigated in those works. The results justify the use of the continuum model in rigorous QCD Sum Rule analyses and enable future applications to correlation functions that become noisy before clear ground state domination, such as heavy-light meson correlators, as well as investigations of nucleon properties from unconventional interpolating fields in a subsequent paper by Leinweber (1994).
