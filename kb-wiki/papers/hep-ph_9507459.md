# A $J^π= 0^-$ Resonance for $πNN \to πNN $ in the Skyrme Model

> The scattering of pions on a dibaryon configuration is analyzed within the $SU(2)$ Skyrme model. It is shown that this model leads to a low-lying $(J^P,I)= (0^-,2)$ resonance. The possibility that this resonance corresponds to one proposed recently in the context of double charge exchange pion scattering on nuclei is discussed. Given the setup used in those experiments we also show that a resonance with isospin assignment $I=0$ cannot be excited according to the description presented here.

# A $J^\pi = 0^-$ Resonance for $\pi NN \to \pi NN$ in the Skyrme Model

## Key claim

The $SU(2)$ Skyrme model predicts a low-lying $(J^P, I) = (0^-, 2)$ resonance in $\pi NN \to \pi NN$ scattering, arising from a separation mode of the toroidal $B=2$ soliton configuration, but only if the resonance has isospin $I=2$; an $I=0$ assignment is excluded by the experimental setup used in double charge exchange pion scattering on nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyze small-amplitude vibrational excitations of the axially symmetric $B=2$ torus solution in the Skyrme model, using a collective quantization procedure for rotational states and a scaling ansatz for displacement fields to describe intrinsic vibrations. They identify the specific vibrational mode that couples to the $L=0$ incoming pion channel with a two-nucleon target in spin $\Sigma=0$ and isospin $T=1$, and estimate its excitation energy using polynomial displacement fields and standard Skyrme model parameters ($f_\pi=93$ MeV, $m_\pi=138$ MeV, $e=4$). The parity and isospin constraints from the experimental double charge exchange setup are then applied to determine which total isospin values are allowed.

## Key numbers

- The estimated energy of the separation mode (the door-way vibration) is $\omega = 250$ MeV, which is $110$ MeV above pion threshold (pion mass $m_\pi=138$ MeV).
- An alternative two-soliton calculation using the Atiyah-Manton ansatz finds the separation mode at $130$ MeV for massless pions.
- The standard Skyrme model parameters used are $f_\pi=93$ MeV, $m_\pi=138$ MeV, and $e=4$.
- The experimental resonance is cited as being only $50$ MeV above pion threshold, with a width estimated to be a few MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach from pion-soliton scattering in the $B=1$ sector (Weigel et al. 1984, Hayashi et al. 1984) to the $B=2$ sector, where the toroidal configuration (Kopeliovich and Stern 1987, Verbaarschot 1987) provides the background. It directly addresses the experimental evidence for a $J^P=0^-$ dibaryon resonance from double charge exchange pion scattering on nuclei (Bilger et al. 1993, 1994), and contrasts with the negative conclusion from non-relativistic quark model calculations (Faessler 1994). The work enables future calculations of pion-torus scattering amplitudes to determine the resonance width, and clarifies that only $I=2$ is compatible with the experimental constraints, while $I=0$ is excluded.
