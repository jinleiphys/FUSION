# QCD in the Infrared with Exact Angular Integrations

> In a previous paper we have shown that in quantum chromodynamics the gluon propagator vanishes in the infrared limit, while the ghost propagator is more singular than a simple pole. These results were obtained after angular averaging, but in the current paper we go beyond this approximation and perform an exact calculation of the angular integrals. The powers of the infrared behaviour of the propagators are changed substantially. We find the very intriguing result that the gluon propagator vanishes in the infrared exactly like p^2, whilst the ghost propagator is exactly as singular as 1/p^4. We also find that the value of the infrared fixed point of the QCD coupling is much decreased from the y-max estimate: it is now equal to 4π/3.

## Key claim

The gluon propagator vanishes in the infrared exactly like \(p^2\) and the ghost propagator is exactly as singular as \(1/p^4\), but only when the angular integrals are treated exactly rather than with the y-max approximation used in prior work; the infrared fixed point of the QCD coupling is \(4\pi/3\), nearly three times smaller than the y-max estimate.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon and ghost form factors in Landau gauge, using bare vertices and performing the angular integrals exactly (via hypergeometric functions) instead of the y-max approximation. They assumed power-law forms \(F(x)=A x^{2\kappa}\) and \(G(x)=B x^{-\kappa}\) for the form factors, substituted them into the ghost and gluon integral equations, evaluated the angular and radial integrals analytically, and determined \(\kappa\) by equating the resulting expressions for \(\lambda A B^2\). The limit \(\kappa \to 1\) was found to satisfy both the ghost and gluon equations simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- The gluon propagator in the infrared behaves as \(D^{ab}_{\mu\nu}(p) \sim -\delta^{ab} [g_{\mu\nu} - p_\mu p_\nu/p^2] \, p^2\).
- The ghost propagator in the infrared behaves as \(G^{ab}(p) \sim -\delta^{ab} \, 1/p^4\).
- The infrared fixed point of the QCD coupling is \(\alpha(0) = 4\pi/3 \approx 4.19\).
- The y-max approximation gave \(\alpha(0) \approx 11.47\) and \(\kappa \approx 0.77\); the exact calculation yields \(\kappa = 1\).
- The product \(\lambda A B^2 = 1/3\) in the limit \(\kappa \to 1\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of von Smekal et al. (1997) and the authors' own prior paper (Atkinson and Bloch, 1997), which used the y-max approximation and found the gluon propagator vanishes in the infrared while the ghost propagator is more singular than a simple pole. The exact angular integration changes the infrared power laws substantially and resolves an ambiguity in the y-max treatment where swapping ghost and gluon momenta gave inconsistent results. The results enable a precise determination of the infrared behavior of QCD propagators and the running coupling, and provide a benchmark for future studies that include improved vertices or gluon-loop contributions.
