# Time-evolution of magnetic field in hot nuclear matter with fluctuating topological charge

> The time-evolution of the magnetic field in hot homogeneous nuclear matter has two qualitatively different stages separated by the sphaleron transition time $τ_c$. At early times the axial chemical potential and the corresponding chiral conductivity $σ_χ$ are slow functions of time. The soft chiral modes $k<σ_χ$ of the magnetic field grow exponentially with time, which is known as the chiral instability. At later times $σ_χ$ fluctuates due to the sphaleron transitions and can be regarded as a random process. It is argued that the average magnetic field is exponentially damped at later times. The time-evolution of the average magnetic energy is more complicated and depends on the electrical conductivity of the chiral matter but does not depend on chirality. It exhibits instability only if the matter is a poor electrical conductor, such as the quark-gluon plasma near the critical temperature. The precise conditions for the instability and the growth rate of the unstable modes are derived.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at times much longer than the sphaleron transition time τ_c, when the chiral conductivity σ_χ fluctuates as a random process, the average magnetic field is exponentially damped, and the average magnetic field energy is unstable only if the quark-gluon plasma is a poor electrical conductor (near the critical temperature) and the dispersion of the chiral conductivity Σ_χ is sufficiently large relative to the electrical conductivity σ.

## Method

The author treats the chiral conductivity σ_χ(t) as a stochastic process with vanishing expectation value and correlation time τ_c, and uses the Van Kampen theory to derive ordinary differential equations from the stochastic harmonic oscillator equation for the field amplitude a_{kλ}(t). The equations for the average amplitude and its second moments are solved separately for underdamped (k > σ/2), overdamped (k < σ/2), and critically damped (k ≈ σ/2) modes, with the electrical conductivity σ distinguishing well-conducting from poorly conducting chiral matter.

## Key numbers

- The sphaleron transition time is estimated as τ_c ∼ 1/(g^4 T), and the chiral conductivity as σ_χ ∼ e^2 μ_5, yielding σ_χ τ_c ≪ 1 since e^2/g^4 ≪ 1 at any reasonable temperature.
- For the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, the electrical conductivity σ ≈ 5 MeV and the correlation time τ_c ≈ 5 fm, giving a = σ τ_c = 0.12.
- The instability in magnetic field energy occurs when the chiral conductivity dispersion Σ_χ ≥ 15 MeV (b_min = 3.1) for the quark-gluon plasma parameters.
- The condition for instability of underdamped modes in poorly conducting matter requires σ < 1/τ_c ∼ T g^4.
- The growth rate of the average field energy for unstable modes is given by ν_0 ω - σ = (Σ_χ^2/(2k))(c_3 - c_2) - σ.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the chiral instability, previously studied in the adiabatic limit with constant chiral conductivity by Tuchin (2014), Manuel (2015), and others, to the regime where topological charge fluctuations are stochastic at times t ≫ τ_c. The results contradict the constant-σ_χ approximation valid at early times t ≪ τ_c, where the magnetic field itself is unstable for soft modes k < σ_χ and the instability is chiral. The paper enables future study of magnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions near the critical temperature, where the quark-gluon plasma is a poor conductor and the average field energy may grow despite the damping of the average field.
