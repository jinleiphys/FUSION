# Reaction Mechanisms at MINER$ν$A

> The MINER$ν$A experiment investigates neutrino interactions with nucleons needed for an understanding of electroweak interactions of hadrons. Since nuclear targets are being used many-body effects may affect the extracted cross sections and the energy reconstruction. The latter is essential for the extraction of neutrino oscillation properties. We investigate the influence of nuclear effects on neutrino interaction cross sections and make predictions for charged current quasielastic (QE) scattering, nucleon-knock-out and pion- and kaon-production on a CH target. The Giessen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model is used for the description of neutrino-nucleus reactions. Integrated and differential cross sections for inclusive neutrino scattering, QE processes and particle production for the MINER$ν$A neutrino flux are calculated. The influence of final state interactions on the identification of these processes is discussed. In particular, energy and $Q^2$ reconstruction for the MINER$ν$A flux are critically examined. The $Q^2$ dependence of the inclusive cross sections is found to be sensitive to the energy reconstruction. Cut-offs in flux distributions have a large effect. Final state interactions affect the pion kinetic energy spectra significantly and increase the kaon cross sections by cross feeding from other channels.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear effects, particularly final state interactions, severely complicate the identification of quasielastic (QE) scattering and the reconstruction of neutrino energy and \(Q^2\) at MINER\(\nu\)A, making the extraction of true neutrino-nucleon cross sections highly model-dependent. The authors hedge that the 2p-2h contribution, which was dominant at lower energies (MiniBooNE/T2K), is comparatively small at MINER\(\nu\)A energies and is instead overshadowed by resonance excitations and deep inelastic scattering.

## Method

The authors used the Giessen Boltzmann–Uehling–Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model to simulate neutrino-nucleus reactions on a CH target, using the MINER\(\nu\)A flux for neutrino energies from 1.5 to 10 GeV. The model includes initial reaction mechanisms (true QE scattering, 2p-2h excitations, resonance pion production, and deep inelastic scattering) and a detailed treatment of final state interactions. The authors generated full events, then reconstructed the neutrino energy and \(Q^2\) using the standard QE-on-neutron-at-rest formulas, and compared the true and reconstructed distributions for 0-pion event samples.

## Key numbers

- The reconstructed event distribution shows significant strength below 1.5 GeV (where no true flux exists), and the shift at the peak of the flux distribution amounts to about 400 MeV.
- At the peak of the flux-averaged \(Q^2\) distribution (\(Q^2 \approx 0.1\) GeV\(^2\)), the reconstructed cross section is about 25% higher than the true distribution.
- The total flux-averaged \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) for all events is about 3.5 times larger than the true QE cross section alone.
- For 0-pion events, the total cross section at its peak is only about 1.5 times larger than the true QE contribution.
- The semi-inclusive proton knockout cross section is dominated by DIS (about 1/2 of the total at 200 MeV) and \(\Delta\) excitation (about 1/3 of the total).
- For pions, final state interactions increase the cross section at the peak (around 0.08 GeV kinetic energy) compared to the spectrum before final state interactions, while creating a dip at around 0.2 GeV.
- Final state interactions increase the kaon cross sections by factors of about 1.5–1.8 for all kaon energies, due to secondary production channels such as \(\pi + N \to K + \Lambda\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GiBUU calculations for the MINOS and NO\(\nu\)A experiments (Lalakulich et al., 2012) to the MINER\(\nu\)A experiment, using its specific flux and CH target. It directly addresses the puzzles raised by MiniBooNE data, where a \(\approx 30\%\) contribution from 2p-2h events was found in the QE-like sample (Martini et al., 2009; Nieves et al., 2011), and where calculated single-pion production cross sections were smaller than measured (Lalakulich et al., 2012). The paper enables future experimental analyses by providing predictions for nucleon knockout, pion, and kaon production, and by highlighting the need for precision generators that include final state interactions to avoid model-dependent biases in the extraction of oscillation parameters.
