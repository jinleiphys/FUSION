# Charge symmetry breaking effect in mirror Λ hypernuclei with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model

> We study the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) effect in mirror hypernuclei using the deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock (DSHF)+ Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) model together with the CSB term and pairing interaction. Our model provides good account for the observations of CSB effect in mirror hypernuclei in the mass region of A = 7~16. We investigate the effect of deformation on the single-Lambda binding energy differences and we found that, in mirror hypernuclei with mass numbers A = 8 and A = 9, deformation has a noticeable impact on the energy difference.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a contact-type charge symmetry breaking (CSB) term in the deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS model reproduces the observed single-Lambda binding energy differences in mirror hypernuclei across A = 7-16, but the agreement depends on the specific Skyrme NN interaction used (SLy4 vs SLy5) and on whether nuclear deformation is included, with deformation having a noticeable impact only for A = 8 and A = 9 systems.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (DSHF) plus BCS calculations for mirror hypernuclei pairs with mass numbers A = 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 16, 32, and 40, using the SLL4 Lambda-N interaction with a contact-type CSB term proportional to v0/2 times tau_z^N. They solved the SHF Schrodinger equation in cylindrical coordinates under axial symmetry, treating pairing with a density-dependent surface-type force and blocking for odd nucleon numbers, and compared results with and without the CSB term and with and without deformation.

## Key numbers

- For A = 7 mirror pair (^7_Lambda He, ^7_Lambda Be): Delta B_Lambda = 0.436 MeV (SLy4 with CSB) compared to experimental 0.39 +/- 0.13 MeV
- For A = 8 mirror pair (^8_Lambda Li, ^8_Lambda Be): Delta B_Lambda = 0.256 MeV (SLy4 with CSB) compared to experimental -0.04 +/- 0.06 MeV
- For A = 9 mirror pair (^9_Lambda Li, ^9_Lambda B): Delta B_Lambda = 0.530 MeV (SLy4 with CSB) compared to experimental 0.65 +/- 0.21 MeV
- For A = 10 mirror pair (^10_Lambda Be, ^10_Lambda B): Delta B_Lambda = 0.220 MeV (SLy4 with CSB) compared to experimental 0.22 +/- 0.25 MeV
- For A = 12 mirror pair (^12_Lambda B, ^12_Lambda C): Delta B_Lambda = 0.225 MeV (SLy4 with CSB) compared to experimental 0.23 +/- 0.19 MeV
- For A = 16 mirror pair (^16_Lambda N, ^16_Lambda O): Delta B_Lambda = 0.148 MeV (SLy4 with CSB) compared to experimental 0.36 +/- 0.43 MeV
- For A = 32 mirror pair (^32_Lambda P, ^32_Lambda S): Delta B_Lambda = 0.125 MeV (SLy4 with CSB), no experimental data available
- For A = 40 mirror pair (^40_Lambda K, ^40_Lambda Ca): Delta B_Lambda = 0.067 MeV (SLy4 with CSB), no experimental data available
- Quadrupole deformation parameters beta_2 for A = 8: 0.297 (^8_Lambda Li) and 0.324 (^8_Lambda Be) with SLy4 and CSB
- Quadrupole deformation parameters beta_2 for A = 9: -0.164 (^9_Lambda Li) and 0.284 (^9_Lambda B) with SLy4 and CSB

## Context

This work extends the study of charge symmetry breaking in hypernuclei beyond the earlier RMF calculations by Sun et al. (2025) which did not include deformation effects, and beyond shell-model calculations by Gal (2015) and Hiyama et al. (2009) which were limited to lighter systems. The DSHF+BCS approach provides a unified framework applicable from light (A=7) to medium-heavy (A=40) hypernuclei, and the authors predict Delta B_Lambda values for A=32 and A=40 mirror pairs that await experimental confirmation. The results show that deformation effects are significant for A=8 and A=9 systems but negligible for other mass numbers, and that the SLy4 interaction gives better agreement with experiment than SLy5 for the heavier systems.
