# Strangeness Enhancement in Heavy Ion Collisions - Evidence for Quark-Gluon-Matter ?

> The centrality dependence of (multi-)strange hadron abundances is studied for Pb(158 AGeV)Pb reactions and compared to p(158 GeV)Pb collisions. The microscopic transport model UrQMD is used for this analysis. The predicted Lambda/pi-, Xi-/pi- and Omega-/pi- ratios are enhanced due to rescattering in central Pb-Pb collisions as compared to peripheral Pb-Pb or p-Pb collisions. A reduction of the constituent quark masses to the current quark masses m_s \sim 230 MeV, m_q \sim 10 MeV, as motivated by chiral symmetry restoration, enhances the hyperon yields to the experimentally observed high values. Similar results are obtained by an ad hoc overall increase of the color electric field strength (effective string tension of kappa=3 GeV/fm). The enhancement depends strongly on the kinematical cuts. The maximum enhancement is predicted around midrapidity. For Lambda's, strangeness suppression is predicted at projectile/target rapidity. For Omega's, the predicted enhancement can be as large as one order of magnitude. Comparisons of Pb-Pb data to proton induced asymmetric (p-A) collisions are hampered due to the predicted strong asymmetry in the various rapidity distributions of the different (strange) particle species. In p-Pb collisions, strangeness is locally (in rapidity) not conserved. The present comparison to the data of the WA97 and NA49 collaborations clearly supports the suggestion that conventional (free) hadronic scenarios are unable to describe the observed high (anti-)hyperon yields in central collisions. The doubling of the strangeness to nonstrange suppression factor, gamma_s \approx 0.65, might be interpreted as a signal of a phase of nearly massless particles.

# Strangeness Enhancement in Heavy Ion Collisions - Evidence for Quark-Gluon-Matter ?

## Key claim

Conventional hadron-string transport (UrQMD) cannot reproduce the experimentally observed high (anti-)hyperon yields in central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, but reducing constituent quark masses to current quark masses (m_s ~ 230 MeV, m_q ~ 10 MeV) or increasing the effective string tension to kappa = 3 GeV/fm brings the calculations into agreement with data, which may indicate a phase of nearly massless particles rather than definitive proof of quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, which includes hadronic and string degrees of freedom with 55 baryon and 32 meson states, to simulate Pb(158 AGeV)Pb and p(158 GeV)Pb collisions. They computed yields of Lambda, Xi-, and Omega- hyperons as a function of centrality (number of participants N_part) and rapidity, comparing standard UrQMD (string tension kappa = 1 GeV/fm) to calculations with reduced quark masses or enhanced string tension (kappa = 3 GeV/fm), and compared results to WA97 and NA49 experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_s increases from approximately 0.3 (standard UrQMD with constituent quark masses m_q = 0.3 GeV, m_s = 0.5 GeV) to approximately 0.65 with reduced masses (m_s ~ 230 MeV, m_q ~ 10 MeV) or enhanced string tension (kappa = 3 GeV/fm)
- Enhancement factors at midrapidity for standard UrQMD: E_Lambda ~ 1.5, E_Xi- ~ 2.2, E_Omega- ~ 4.8; for reduced mass/enhanced string tension: E*_Lambda ~ 2.0, E*_Xi- ~ 6.0, E*_Omega- ~ 13
- (Xi- + anti-Xi-)/(Lambda + anti-Lambda) ratio: 0.07 +/- 0.01 (standard UrQMD), 0.12 +/- 0.01 (reduced masses/enhanced string tension), compared to experimental values 0.11 +/- 0.003 (WA97) and 0.13 +/- 0.04 (NA49)
- Omega/Xi ratio predicted as approximately 0.12 +/- 0.02, compared to WA97 value of about 0.2
- Pion number changes by less than approximately 4% when gamma_s is increased
- Hyperon yields increase with centrality as ln(Y/event) = ln(N_part^alpha) with alpha ~ 1.1 for Lambda and Xi, alpha ~ 2 for Omega
- Threshold enhancement for Xi and Omega occurs between N_part ~ 10 and 25

## Context

This work extends earlier strangeness enhancement studies by Rafelski (1982), Koch (1986, 1988), and the WA97 and NA49 collaborations (Andersen 1998, 1999; Margetis 1999; Gabler 1999; Evans 1999), who observed that hyperon yields increase with strangeness content and that the Omega enhancement factor reaches 15. The UrQMD results contradict conventional hadronic scenarios and support the suggestion that a transition to chirally restored matter may be occurring, similar to arguments made by Biro (1984) and Sorge (1992) about overlapping color flux tubes. The findings enable further investigation of whether the observed enhancement requires a quark-gluon plasma phase or can be explained by in-medium mass modifications and string tension increases in dense hadronic matter.
