# Short-distance production of three particles with large scattering length

> The short-distance production of multi-particle states in high-energy nuclear reactions provides a unique way to study the low-energy properties of few-body systems. In particular, the production amplitude of multi-neutron systems is strongly constrained by an approximate conformal symmetry of the underlying theory. We calculate the full amplitude for the short-distance production of three particles with large scattering length in leading order pionless EFT, focusing on the cases of three neutrons and three spinless bosons. We investigate the signature of low-energy resonances and other correlations in the relative energy distributions. For the case of neutrons, we compare to the predictions from approximate conformal symmetry close to the unitary limit and calculate the range corrections up to next-to-next-to leading order.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the full amplitude for short-distance production of three particles with large scattering length using leading-order pionless effective field theory, focusing on three neutrons and three spinless bosons, and explicitly verifies that the relative energy distribution is constrained by approximate conformal symmetry in the unitary regime where \(1/(ma^2) \ll E \ll 1/(mr^2)\), with the caveat that the source strength normalization must be fitted to experimental data.

## Method

The authors solve Faddeev equations for the point-production amplitude in pionless EFT at leading order, using effective two-body contact interactions with a soft Gaussian regulator for bosons and a sharp cutoff for neutrons. The integral equation for the particle-dimer amplitude is projected onto partial waves up to D-wave, and the full three-body amplitude is constructed by symmetrizing (bosons) or antisymmetrizing (neutrons) the permutations of outgoing particles. The relative energy distribution \(R(E)\) is obtained by phase-space integration of the squared amplitude with an on-shell delta function.

## Key numbers

- Neutron scattering length \(a \approx -18.9\) fm and effective range \(r \approx 2.83\) fm, giving a leading-order theoretical uncertainty of about 15%
- The unitary regime for neutrons spans relative energies \(1/(ma^2) \approx 0.1\) MeV \(\ll E \ll 1/(mr^2) \approx 5\) MeV
- For three bosons, calculations use scattering lengths \(a = -15.12, -12.6, -10.8,\) and \(-9.45\) fm corresponding to ratios \(a_-/a = 1.25, 1.5, 1.75,\) and \(2.0\) with cutoff \(\Lambda = 179.9\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- The spatial region of neutron creation in knock-out reactions is approximately 2.5 fm, roughly the size of \(^8\)He

## Context

This work extends the conformal symmetry approach of Hammer (2021) to explicit three-body calculations in pionless EFT, providing a framework to interpret multi-neutron production experiments such as the tetraneutron resonance candidate reported by Duer (2022). The three-boson results connect to Efimov physics and resonance studies by Dietz (2021) and Bringas (2004), while the three-neutron case enables future extension to four neutrons and extended source distributions, with direct relevance to ongoing experimental searches for multi-neutron states.
