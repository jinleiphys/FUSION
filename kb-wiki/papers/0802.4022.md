# In-medium QCD and Cherenkov gluons

> The equations of in-medium gluodynamics are proposed. Their classical lowest order solution is explicitly shown for a color charge moving with constant speed. For nuclear permittivity larger than 1 it describes emission of Cherenkov gluons resembling results of classical electrodynamics. The choice of nuclear permittivity and Lorentz-invariance of the problem are discussed. Effects induced by the transversely and longitudinally moving (relative to the collision axis) partons at LHC energies are described.

# In-medium QCD and Cherenkov gluons

## Key claim
The classical lowest-order solution of the proposed in-medium gluodynamics equations describes Cherenkov gluon emission for a color charge moving at constant speed, but only when the nuclear permittivity is larger than 1; the Lorentz invariance of the problem is broken by the introduction of permittivity, requiring careful choice of the coordinate system where the medium is at rest.

## Method
The author generalizes the classical Yang-Mills equations to in-medium gluodynamics by replacing the chromoelectric field **E**_a with **D**_a = ε**E**_a in the vector-form equations of motion, analogous to the in-medium Maxwell equations. The classical lowest-order solution is obtained by solving the resulting Abelian-like equations (with color indices only) for a point color charge moving at constant speed, using Fourier transforms and integration over poles. The nuclear permittivity is treated phenomenologically and related to the forward scattering amplitude of gluons via the refractive index formula from scattering theory.

## Key numbers
- The Cherenkov angle is given by tan²θ = εv² - 1, where v is the parton speed and ε is the nuclear permittivity.
- The intensity of Cherenkov gluon radiation is given by the Tamm-Frank formula: dE/dl = 4π α_S ∫ ω dω (1 - 1/(v²ε)).
- The refractive index is expressed as Re n(E) = 1 + (6 m_π³ ν / E²) Re F(E), where m_π is the pion mass, ν is the number of scatterers per nucleon, and Re F(E) is the real part of the forward scattering amplitude.
- The scalar potential solution is Φ^(1)(r,t) = (2g/ε) × θ(vt - z - r_⊥ √(εv² - 1)) / √((vt - z)² - r_⊥²(εv² - 1)).

## Context
This work extends the classical in-vacuum gluodynamics approach of Kovchegov (1996) and others by incorporating medium effects through a phenomenological nuclear permittivity, analogous to how permittivity is introduced in electrodynamics for matter. The author notes that in-vacuum QCD equations admit no radiation in lowest order, while the in-medium equations do. The approach enables interpretation of experimental observations at SPS (rho-meson shape asymmetry), RHIC (azimuthal correlations of in-medium jets), and cosmic ray energies (Cherenkov rings), and predicts new effects at LHC energies. The treatment contrasts with perturbative thermal field theory calculations of the polarization operator by Klimov (1982), Weldon (1982), and others, which compute the medium response to induced currents rather than external currents.
