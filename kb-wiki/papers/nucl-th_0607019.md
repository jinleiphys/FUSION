# Study of the threshold behavior of the $η$N scattering amplitude through the associated photoproduction of $ϕ$- and $η$-mesons

> We suggest that the $γp \to ϕηp$ reaction cross section, in the kinematics where the $ηp$ invariant mass in the final state lies between the threshold value (m$_p$+m$_η$) and the N*(1535) resonance mass, is largely determined by the $ηN$ scattering amplitude close to threshold. The initial photon energy is chosen in the range $4 < E_γ^{Lab} < 5$ GeV, in order to reach low (absolute) values of the squared 4-momentum transfer from the initial photon to the final $ϕ$-meson. In these conditions, we expect the t-channel $π^0$- and $η$-meson exchanges to drive the dynamics underlying the $γp \to ϕηp$ process. We show that the $η$-exchange is the dominating contribution to the cross section while the $π^0$-exchange is negligible. The $η$-$π^0$ interference is of the order of $20 - 30 %$. The sign of this term is not known and alters significantly our results. Data on the $γp \to ϕηp$ process would be therefore very useful to help unravelling the behavior of the $ηp$ scattering amplitude close to threshold and assessing the possibility of producing $η$-nucleus bound states.

## Key claim

The $\gamma p \to \phi \eta p$ reaction cross section, in the kinematics where the $\eta p$ invariant mass lies between threshold and the N*(1535) resonance mass, is largely determined by the $\eta N$ scattering amplitude close to threshold, but the sign of the $\eta$-$\pi^0$ interference term is not known and alters the results significantly.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for $\gamma p \to \phi \eta p$ using a t-channel meson-exchange model with $\pi^0$- and $\eta$-meson exchanges, employing the anomalous interaction Lagrangian for the $\gamma\phi\pi^0$ and $\gamma\phi\eta$ vertices. The $\pi p \to \eta p$ and $\eta p \to \eta p$ s-wave scattering amplitudes are taken from a relativistic, unitary coupled-channel model (Lutz, 2002) that reproduces pion-nucleon and photon-nucleon scattering data in the energy range $1.4 < \sqrt s < 1.8$ GeV. The calculation is performed at a laboratory photon energy of 4 GeV for two values of the $\eta p$ invariant mass, 1.49 GeV and 1.54 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The $\eta$-nucleon scattering length from the model of Lutz (2002) is $a_{\eta N} = (1.03 + i\,0.49)$ fm; from Green and Wycech (2005) it is $a_{\eta N} = (0.91(6) + i\,0.27(2))$ fm.
- The coupling constants derived from measured partial widths are $|g_{\phi\pi\gamma}| \simeq 0.13$ and $|g_{\phi\eta\gamma}| \simeq 0.70$, giving a suppression factor of about 29 for the pion-exchange contribution relative to the $\eta$-exchange contribution.
- The $\eta$-$\pi^0$ interference is of the order of 20-30% of the total cross section.
- At $E_\gamma^{Lab} = 4$ GeV, $|t_{min}|$ is 0.38 GeV$^2$; at $E_\gamma^{Lab} = 5$ GeV, $|t_{min}|$ is 0.26 GeV$^2$.
- The differential cross section at $\bar w = 1.54$ GeV is roughly twice larger than at $\bar w = 1.49$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach of Lutz (2002) and the K-matrix calculation of Green and Wycech (2005) by proposing a new reaction to probe the $\eta N$ scattering amplitude near threshold. The authors note that the range of values for Re $a_{\eta N}$ in the literature (from about 0.3 to about 1 fm) reflects the indirect nature of determinations from reactions such as $\pi N \to \eta N$, $\gamma N \to \eta N$, and $p d \to \eta ^3He$. The proposed $\gamma p \to \phi \eta p$ measurement would help assess the possibility of producing $\eta$-nucleus bound states.
