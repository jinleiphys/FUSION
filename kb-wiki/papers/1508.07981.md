# Sensitivity to Z-prime and non-standard neutrino interactions from ultra-low threshold neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering

> We discuss prospects for probing Z-prime and non-standard neutrino interactions using neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering with ultra-low energy (~ 10 eV) threshold Si and Ge detectors. The analysis is performed in the context of a specific and contemporary reactor-based experimental proposal, developed in cooperation with the Nuclear Science Center at Texas A&M University, and referencing available technology based upon economical and scalable detector arrays. For expected exposures, we show that sensitivity to the Z-prime mass is on the order of several TeV, and is complementary to the LHC search with low mass detectors in the near term. This technology is also shown to provide sensitivity to the neutrino magnetic moment, at a level that surpasses terrestrial limits, and is competitive with more stringent astrophysical bounds. We demonstrate the benefits of combining silicon and germanium detectors for distinguishing between classes of models of new physics, and for suppressing correlated systematic uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ultra-low threshold (~10 eV) silicon and germanium detectors placed ~1 m from a 1 MW research reactor can achieve sensitivity to Z-prime boson masses up to several TeV and to neutrino magnetic moments at levels surpassing terrestrial limits, but only under the condition that the detector recoil threshold reaches the eV scale rather than the keV scale typical of previous proposals.

## Method

The authors model coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) from reactor antineutrinos using the specific parameters of the Texas A&M University TRIGA reactor (1 MW thermal power, ~1 m baseline) and the expected performance of cryogenic Si and Ge detector arrays with thresholds as low as 10 eV. They compute event rates by integrating the differential cross section over the reactor antineutrino spectrum (using tabulated data from Schreckenbach (1985) and Kopeikin (2012)), then derive sensitivity projections for five benchmark Z-prime models (E6 χ, E6 ψ, E6 η, B-L with two coupling strengths, and the sequential standard model) and for neutrino magnetic moment interactions, including both statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Reactor thermal power: 1.00 ± 0.02 MW, yielding an antineutrino flux of 1.5×10¹² cm⁻² s⁻¹ at 1 m from core from fission, plus 7.9×10¹⁰ cm⁻² s⁻¹ from uranium-to-plutonium breeding
- Fission rate: 3.1×10¹⁶ s⁻¹; antineutrino production rate: 1.9×10¹⁷ s⁻¹
- Detector threshold of ~50 eV (Si) and ~20 eV (Ge) required to capture about half of the scattering from fission neutrinos with mean energy 1.5 MeV
- Sensitivity to Z-prime mass is on the order of several TeV for the E6 χ model with 1 kg·yr exposure
- Neutrino magnetic moment sensitivity reaches ~10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (Bohr magneton), competitive with astrophysical bounds and surpassing previous terrestrial limits

## Context

This work extends the CEνNS detection proposals of Barranco (2007) and the TEXONO and CoGeNT collaborations by demonstrating that eV-scale thresholds (as projected by Mirabolfathi (2015)) enable sensitivity to new physics that is complementary to LHC searches for Z-prime bosons. The analysis is motivated by a specific reactor-based experimental proposal at Texas A&M University and shows that combining Si and Ge detectors can distinguish between Z-prime models (e.g., the E6 ψ model with purely axial couplings is invisible to these detectors) and suppress correlated systematic uncertainties. The results enable future searches for non-standard neutrino interactions and neutrino magnetic moments at levels that challenge astrophysical constraints from stellar cooling.
