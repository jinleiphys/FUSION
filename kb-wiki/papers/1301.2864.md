# Lattice QCD study for stringy excitation and role of UV gluons

> In both cases of quark-antiquark (Q-Qbar) and three-quark (3Q) systems, we study ground-state and low-lying excited-state potentials in terms of the gluon-momentum component in the Coulomb gauge in SU(3) quenched lattice QCD. By introducing UV-cut in the gluon-momentum space, we investigate the "UV-gluon sensitivity" of the ground-state and excited-state potentials quantitatively. Such a non-quark-origin excitation is a purely gluonic excitation, which can be interpreted as a stringy excitation in the color flux-tube picture of hadrons. For both Q-Qbar and 3Q systems, the IR part of the ground-state potential is almost unchanged, even after cutting off high-momentum gluon component. On the other hand, we find more significant change of excited-state potential by the cut of UV-gluons. However, even after the removal of UV-gluons, the magnitude of the low-lying gluonic excitation remains to be of the order of 1GeV.

## Key claim

Even after removing high-momentum (UV) gluon components above 1.5 GeV, the low-lying gluonic excitation energy in both quark-antiquark and three-quark systems remains of the order of 1 GeV, indicating that the stringy excitation is not solely driven by UV gluons.

## Method

The authors performed SU(3) quenched lattice QCD simulations on a 16^4 lattice at beta=6.0 (lattice spacing ~0.104 fm) with Coulomb gauge fixing. They introduced a UV-cut in three-dimensional momentum space by replacing high-momentum Fourier components of the link variables with zero, then reconstructed coordinate-space link variables via inverse Fourier transformation and SU(3) projection. Ground-state and even-parity excited-state potentials for both Q-Qbar and 3Q systems were extracted from Wilson loop matrices using the secular equation method.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a ~ 0.104 fm, momentum-space lattice spacing a_p ~ 0.74 GeV
- UV-cut values used: Lambda_UV = 3a_p ~ 2.2 GeV and Lambda_UV = 2a_p ~ 1.5 GeV
- Low-lying gluonic excitation energy remains of the order of 1 GeV after removal of UV-gluons
- 100 gauge configurations used for Q-Qbar systems; 30 gauge configurations used for 3Q systems
- APE smearing parameters: alpha = 2.3, iteration numbers N_smr = 0, 8, 16, 24

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Yamamoto (2008) on UV-gluon contributions to ground-state potentials in the Landau gauge, now examining both ground and excited states in the Coulomb gauge. It builds on earlier lattice calculations of excited-state potentials for Q-Qbar systems by Juge (2002) and for 3Q systems by Takahashi (2002, 2004). The results support the interpretation of gluonic excitations as stringy excitations in the flux-tube picture of hadrons, relevant for understanding hybrid hadrons (q-qbar-G and qqq-G) beyond the quark model.
