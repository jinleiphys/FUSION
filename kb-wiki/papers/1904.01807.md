# Anomalous magnetohydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and chiral magnetic effect

> We study relativistic magnetohydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance in the presence of chiral magnetic effects and finite electric conductivity. With initial magnetic fields parallel or anti-parallel to electric fields, we derive the analytic solutions of electromagnetic fields and the chiral number and energy density in an expansion of several parameters determined by initial conditions. The numerical solutions show that such analytic solutions work well in weak fields or large chiral fluctuations. We also discuss the properties of electromagnetic fields in the laboratory frame.

## Key claim

The paper derives approximate analytic solutions for electromagnetic fields, chiral number density, and energy density in relativistic magnetohydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance, under the condition that the fluid is electrically neutral and the magnetic and electric fields are parallel or anti-parallel; these solutions are valid only when the anomaly-induced terms (proportional to Planck's constant) are treated as perturbations, i.e., in the regime of weak fields or large chiral fluctuations.

## Method

The authors impose Bjorken flow (longitudinal boost invariance) on a charge-neutral fluid and assume the electric and magnetic fields lie in the transverse plane, parallel or anti-parallel. They solve Maxwell's equations coupled with the anomalous chiral charge conservation equation and energy conservation, using the non-conserved charges method and expanding in powers of the anomaly constant (proportional to \(\hbar\)). Two equations of state are considered: one for the dense limit (high chemical potentials) and one for the hot limit (high temperature). The approximate analytic solutions are compared to numerical solutions of the same equations.

## Key numbers

* Initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm/c}\) is used for all numerical calculations.
* Electric conductivity \(\sigma\) is chosen in the range \(\sigma \sim 5-30\ \mathrm{MeV} \simeq 0.04-0.25\ \tau_0\), based on lattice QCD and holographic QCD models.
* For the dense-limit equation of state, the dimensionless constants \(a_1\) and \(a_2\) (proportional to the anomaly constant) are varied; for example, \(a_2 = \pm 0.2\) and \(a_1 = \pm 0.5\) are used in figures.
* For the hot-limit equation of state, the speed of sound squared is set to \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) and \(E_0^2/\varepsilon_0 = 0.1\).
* The magnetic field decays as \(B(\tau) = B_0 (\tau_0/\tau)\), independent of the chiral magnetic effect.
* The energy density correction \(\Delta\varepsilon\) is amplified by a factor of 100 in the figures to show the comparison between analytic and numerical solutions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of ideal magnetohydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and a transverse magnetic field (Pu 2016, Roy 2015) by including the chiral magnetic effect and finite electric conductivity. It also builds on the non-conserved charges method used by Csorgo (2003) and Shokri (2017) for solving MHD equations. The analytic solutions provide a test bed for numerical simulations of anomalous MHD, which can suffer from chirality instabilities (Akamatsu 2013), and are relevant for understanding the evolution of electromagnetic fields and chiral charge in the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
