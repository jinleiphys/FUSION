# Testing the spin-cutoff parameterization with shell-model calculations

> The nuclear level density, an important input to Hauser-Feshbach calculations, depends not only on excitation energy but also on angular momentum J. The J-dependence of the level density at fixed excitation energy E_x is usually parameterized via the spin-cutoff factor sigma. We carefully test the statistical accuracy of this parameterization for a large number of spectra computed using semi-realistic interactions in the interacting shell model, with a nonlinear least-squares fit of sigma and finding the error bar in sigma. The spin-cutoff parameterization works well as long as there are enough states to be statistical. In turn, the spin-cutoff factor can be related to the average value of J^2 at a fixed excitation energy, and we briefly investigate extracting <J^2 (E_x)> from a thermal calculation such as one might do via Monte Carlo.

## Key claim

The spin-cutoff parameterization of the angular-momentum-dependent nuclear level density is statistically accurate for shell-model spectra, provided there are enough states in an energy bin for the distribution to be statistical; the error bars on the extracted spin-cutoff factor decrease systematically as the number of levels increases.

## Method

The authors generated complete nuclear spectra for a range of nuclides in three valence spaces (sd-shell, pf-shell, and p-sd5/2 cross-shell) using configuration-interaction shell-model diagonalization with semi-realistic interactions (USDB, KB3G, and a hybrid Cohen-Kurath/Wildenthal/Millener-Kurath interaction). They binned the resulting level densities by excitation energy in 1 MeV bins and performed a nonlinear least-squares fit of the spin-cutoff parameter sigma to the Ericson formula, using the MPFIT implementation of the Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm and estimating the error on each J-projected density as the square root of the count.

## Key numbers

- The reduced chi-squared from the fits nearly always fell below 1 and frequently much less than 1; after scaling errors to achieve reduced chi-squared = 1, the best-fit spin-cutoff factors changed by less than 5 percent when using an alternative error model of Delta rho_J = rho_J^(1/4).
- The root mean square error (RMSE) of the normalized spin distribution (rho_J/rho) improved with increasing excitation energy for all analyzed nuclides, with the improvement independent of structural differences in the sd and pf shells.
- For the p-sd5/2 cross-shell nuclides, the improvement in RMSE occurred at higher excitation energies than for sd and pf shell nuclides because the level density is lower due to splitting between parities.
- The best-fit spin-cutoff values were insensitive to bin size when tested with 0.5, 1.0, and 2.0 MeV bins, though error bars depended on bin size due to the number of levels per bin.
- The approximation 3 sigma^2 approximately equals <J(J+1)> + 1/4 worked well when tested against the fit values, though the fit sigma tended to be slightly higher than the approximate sigma.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical and experimental investigations of the spin distribution of nuclear levels, such as those by Grimes (1983), Huang (2000), Alhassid et al. (2007), and von Egidy and Bucurescu (2008, 2009), by quantifying the statistical goodness of the spin-cutoff parameterization through explicit error bars on the fitted sigma. The results enable a computationally cheaper alternative to full J-projected shell-model Monte Carlo calculations: by extracting the energy-averaged <J(J+1)(E_x)> from the thermal average <J^2(beta)> via Laplace inversion, one can obtain the spin-cutoff factor without the intensive J-projection scheme developed by Alhassid et al. (2007) and Van Roosbroeck et al. (2009).
