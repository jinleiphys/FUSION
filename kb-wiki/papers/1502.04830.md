# Particle-vibration coupling effect on the $ő≤$-decay of magic nuclei

> Nuclear $ő≤$-decay in magic nuclei is investigated, taking into account the coupling between particle and collective vibrations,on top of self-consistent random phase approximation calculations based on Skyrme density functionals. The low-lying Gamow-Teller strength is shifted downwards and at times becomes fragmented; as a consequence, the $ő≤$-decay half-lives are reduced due to the increase of the phase space available for the decay. In some cases, this leads to a very good agreement between theoretical and experimental lifetimes: this happens, in particular, in the case of the Skyrme force SkM*, that can also reproduce the line shape of the high energy Gamow-Teller resonance as it was previously shown.

## Key claim

The coupling between particles and collective vibrations (PVC), applied on top of self-consistent RPA calculations, systematically shifts low-lying Gamow-Teller strength downward and reduces ő≤-decay half-lives in magic nuclei, without fitting new parameters; the improvement is most notable for the Skyrme force SkM*, which also reproduces the high-energy Gamow-Teller resonance line shape as previously shown.

## Method

The authors start from self-consistent RPA calculations based on Skyrme density functionals to obtain 1‚Āļ states and their GT strengths. They then couple these 1p-1h RPA states to doorway states consisting of a particle-hole excitation coupled to a collective vibration (phonon), where phonons with natural parity JŌÄ = 0‚Āļ, 1‚ĀĽ, 2‚Āļ, 3‚ĀĽ, 4‚Āļ, 5‚ĀĽ, and 6‚Āļ having energies below 20 MeV and carrying more than 5% of the total strength are included. The resulting complex, energy-dependent self-energy is used to compute the GT strength distribution via Gaussian averaging, and the ő≤-decay half-life is calculated in the allowed GT approximation using the phase-space integral up to the Qő≤ value.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent RPA+PVC models previously applied to GT and spin-dipole strength distributions (Niu 2012, Niu 2014) to the study of ő≤-decay half-lives in magic nuclei. It addresses the long-standing problem that self-consistent (Q)RPA models systematically overestimate ő≤-decay half-lives (Engel 1999, Niksic 2005, Marketin 2007, Niu 2013), especially in closed-shell nuclei where isoscalar proton-neutron pairing has little effect. The approach differs from that of Severyukhin (2014) by using a fully self-consistent model with a larger model space and no fitting of new parameters. The results enable future application to electron capture on fp-shell nuclei and to predicting half-lives of r-process bottleneck nuclei with N=82.
