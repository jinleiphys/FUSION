# Particle-vibration coupling effect on the $β$-decay of magic nuclei

> Nuclear $β$-decay in magic nuclei is investigated, taking into account the coupling between particle and collective vibrations,on top of self-consistent random phase approximation calculations based on Skyrme density functionals. The low-lying Gamow-Teller strength is shifted downwards and at times becomes fragmented; as a consequence, the $β$-decay half-lives are reduced due to the increase of the phase space available for the decay. In some cases, this leads to a very good agreement between theoretical and experimental lifetimes: this happens, in particular, in the case of the Skyrme force SkM*, that can also reproduce the line shape of the high energy Gamow-Teller resonance as it was previously shown.

## Key claim

The coupling between particles and collective vibrations (PVC), applied on top of self-consistent RPA calculations, systematically shifts low-lying Gamow-Teller strength downward and reduces β-decay half-lives in magic nuclei, without fitting new parameters; the improvement is most notable for the Skyrme force SkM*, which also reproduces the high-energy Gamow-Teller resonance line shape as previously shown.

## Method

The authors start from self-consistent RPA calculations based on Skyrme density functionals to obtain 1⁺ states and their GT strengths. They then couple these 1p-1h RPA states to doorway states consisting of a particle-hole excitation coupled to a collective vibration (phonon), where phonons with natural parity Jπ = 0⁺, 1⁻, 2⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 5⁻, and 6⁺ having energies below 20 MeV and carrying more than 5% of the total strength are included. The resulting complex, energy-dependent self-energy is used to compute the GT strength distribution via Gaussian averaging, and the β-decay half-life is calculated in the allowed GT approximation using the phase-space integral up to the Qβ value.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷⁸Ni, the reduction factor R (ratio of RPA half-life to RPA+PVC half-life) is about 42 for SAMi, about 10 for SGII, and about 4 for SLy5, Skx, SkM*, and SIII.
- For ⁷⁸Ni, the RPA+PVC half-life with SkM* and SIII falls within the experimental error (experimental value from Hosmer 2005).
- For ¹³²Sn, the lowest RPA state at E = 3.6 MeV (referred to daughter ground state) is moved into the β-decay window by PVC; RPA predicts no strength below Qβ, making the nucleus stable.
- For ⁶⁸Ni, the RPA peak at 1.5 MeV is moved slightly below the experimental ground state energy by PVC.
- For ³⁴Si, PVC fragments the strength into five peaks at E = -2.2, 1.0, 1.7, 2.6, and 3.1 MeV, contributing respectively 15%, 49%, 24%, 3%, and 9% of the total 1/T₁/₂.
- For ⁷⁸Ni, the RPA state at E = 5.6 MeV is shifted to a peak at 4.0 MeV by PVC, and its contribution to 1/T₁/₂ becomes about 3.4 times larger than in RPA.
- The averaging parameter η is set to 0.05 MeV.
- The constant D in the half-life formula is 6163.4 s, with gA = 1.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent RPA+PVC models previously applied to GT and spin-dipole strength distributions (Niu 2012, Niu 2014) to the study of β-decay half-lives in magic nuclei. It addresses the long-standing problem that self-consistent (Q)RPA models systematically overestimate β-decay half-lives (Engel 1999, Niksic 2005, Marketin 2007, Niu 2013), especially in closed-shell nuclei where isoscalar proton-neutron pairing has little effect. The approach differs from that of Severyukhin (2014) by using a fully self-consistent model with a larger model space and no fitting of new parameters. The results enable future application to electron capture on fp-shell nuclei and to predicting half-lives of r-process bottleneck nuclei with N=82.
