# Low-energy M1 states in deformed nuclei: spin-scissors or spin-flip?

> The low-energy $M1$ states in deformed $^{164}$Dy and spherical $^{58}$Ni are explored in the framework of fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) with various Skyrme forces. The main attention is paid to orbital and spin $M1$ excitations. The obtained results are compared with the prediction of the low-energy {\it spin-scissors} $M1$ resonance suggested within Wigner Function Moments (WFM) approach. A possible relation of this resonance to low-energy spin-flip excitations is analyzed. In connection with recent WFM studies, we consider evolution of the low-energy spin-flip states in $^{164}$Dy with deformation (from the equilibrium value to the spherical limit). The effect of tensor forces is briefly discussed. It is shown that two groups of $1^+$ states observed at 2.4-4 MeV in $^{164}$Dy are rather explained by fragmentation of the orbital $M1$ strength than by the occurrence of the collective spin-scissors resonance. In general, our calculations do not confirm the existence of this resonance.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the low-energy $M1$ states observed in deformed $^{164}$Dy at 2.4-4 MeV are explained by fragmentation of the orbital $M1$ strength rather than by the existence of a collective spin-scissors resonance, and that fully self-consistent QRPA calculations do not confirm the existence of the spin-scissors resonance predicted by the Wigner Function Moments approach.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations for deformed $^{164}$Dy and spherical $^{58}$Ni using four different Skyrme forces (SkM*, SG2, SVbas, and SV-tls). For $^{164}$Dy, they computed reduced transition probabilities $B(M1)$ for orbital, spin, and total contributions, as well as $B(E2)$ values and convective current transition densities (CTD), analyzing the evolution of these quantities as the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$ was reduced from the equilibrium value toward the spherical limit. For $^{58}$Ni, they calculated the $M1$ strength in the 0-6 MeV range to demonstrate the existence of low-energy spin-flip states in spherical nuclei.

## Key numbers

* In $^{164}$Dy, the lowest $K^{\pi}=1^+$ state calculated with SkM* lies at 1.93 MeV with $B(M1,\text{spin})=0.37\ \mu_N^2$ and $B(M1,\text{orbital})=0.03\ \mu_N^2$, dominated by the proton 2qp configuration $pp[411\uparrow, 411\downarrow]$.
* For SkM* in $^{164}$Dy, the orbital $M1$ strength summed over 0-4 MeV nearly vanishes as $\beta$ approaches 0, while the spin $M1$ strength summed over the same range increases as deformation decreases.
* In spherical $^{58}$Ni, the lowest $I^{\pi}=1^+$ state calculated with SkM* lies at 3.41 MeV and is 99.5% exhausted by the 2qp spin-flip neutron configuration $nn[2p_{3/2}, 2p_{1/2}]$.
* The experimental energy of the lowest $1^+$ state in $^{58}$Ni is 2.902 MeV, compared to QRPA values of 3.41 MeV (SkM*), 3.42 MeV (SG2), and 3.02 MeV (SVbas).
* The equilibrium quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$ for $^{164}$Dy calculated with SVbas is 0.348, in good agreement with the experimental value of 0.349(3).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QRPA study (Nesterenko et al., 2021) that analyzed spin-scissors predictions in $^{160,162,164}$Dy and $^{232}$Th, and directly contradicts the Wigner Function Moments predictions of Balbutsev et al. (2011-2021) who proposed a triple structure of the scissors mode including two spin-scissors resonances. The paper enables future microscopic studies of low-energy spin-flip $M1$ states including the effects of tensor forces and coupling with complex configurations, and calls for a re-examination of the WFM result that $M1$ strength fully disappears at deformations $\delta < 0.13-0.15$.
