# Nucleon and Delta axial-vector couplings in 1/Nc - Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> In this contribution, baryon axial-vector couplings are studied in the framework of the combined 1/Nc and chiral expansions. This framework is implemented on the basis of the emergent spin-flavor symmetry in baryons at large Nc and HBChPT, and linking both expansions (ξ-expansion), where 1/Nc is taken to be a quantity O(p). The study is carried out including one-loop contributions, which corresponds to O(ξ^3) for baryon masses and O(ξ^2) for the axial couplings. An analysis of the Lattice QCD results for the axial couplings of both N and Δis presented.

# Nucleon and Delta axial-vector couplings in 1/Nc - Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The combined 1/Nc and chiral expansion (ξ-expansion) framework, where 1/Nc is taken to be of order p, can describe the pion-mass dependence of the nucleon and Delta axial-vector couplings from lattice QCD, but only because spin-flavor symmetry enforces cancellations between one-loop contributions that keep the effective theory consistent with natural 1/Nc power counting. The lattice QCD results for gA^NN and C5^A are underestimated by about 10% when extrapolated to the physical pion mass, which the authors argue may be of systematic lattice QCD origin.

## Method
The authors construct a combined 1/Nc and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) framework using the ξ-expansion, where 1/Nc is treated as order p. They evaluate baryon masses at order ξ^3 and axial-vector couplings at order ξ^2, including one-loop contributions, and perform a simultaneous fit to lattice QCD results for N and Delta masses and the three axial couplings gA^NN, C5^A(0), and g1(0) from the ETM Collaboration for pion masses below approximately 500 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon axial coupling extrapolated to the physical point is gA = 1.17(2), compared to the experimental value 1.2701 ± 0.0025
- The Delta-N axial coupling at the physical point is C5^A(0) = 0.91(2), while the value extracted from the Delta width (Gamma_Delta->Npi = 116-120 MeV) gives C5^A ~ 1.05 ± 0.01
- The Delta axial coupling at the physical point is g1(0) = 0.59(2)
- The baryon masses extrapolated to the physical point are mN = 962(15) MeV and mDelta = 1213(14) MeV
- The leading axial coupling constant in the effective theory is mathring{g}_A = 1.55(3)
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the combined fit is 2.0

## Context
This work extends the combined 1/Nc and chiral expansion framework developed by Calle Cordon and Goity (2012) from baryon masses to axial-vector couplings of both nucleon and Delta. It builds on the spin-flavor symmetry approach of Gervais and Sakita (1983) and Dashen and Manohar (1993), and the earlier combined framework of Jenkins (1995). The analysis confronts lattice QCD results from the ETM Collaboration (Alexandrou 2010, 2011) and shows that the small pion-mass dependence of axial couplings can only be reproduced due to cancellations dictated by spin-flavor symmetry between different one-loop contributions. The framework enables future tests with more accurate lattice QCD results and can be extended to other baryon observables.
