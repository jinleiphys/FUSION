# Extracting Essential Non-perturbative Information in Jet Invariant Mass via the Bayesian Analysis

> In this paper, we present a new three-dimensional non-perturbative (NP) function to account for and parameterize the NP contributions in the jet invariant mass spectrum, in addition to the conventional NP mass shift parametrization. By implementing Bayesian analysis on experimental data of the jet invariant mass from exclusive $W/Z+$jet events and inclusive jet events in $pp$ collisions at RHIC and LHC, where collisional energy increases by a factor of up to $65$ from RHIC to LHC, we ensure the analysis covers a wide range of data. For the first time, we simultaneously extract NP contributions from hadronization, initial soft-gluon radiation, and underlying events, based on two different NP prescriptions. We find that the contribution from initial soft-gluon radiation is negligible, and the hadronization effect dominates in the small-$R$ region, while underlying events provide the dominant contribution in the large-$R$ region. Moreover, when only hadronization effects are considered, our results successfully describe the jet mass data measured in $e^+e^-$ collisions, where only hadronization effects are expected to be present. Our work offers quantitative insights into understanding the soft hadronic contribution to jet substructure.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a novel three-dimensional non-perturbative (NP) function for the jet invariant mass spectrum that, when combined with Bayesian analysis of experimental data from RHIC and LHC, allows simultaneous extraction of hadronization, initial soft-gluon radiation, and underlying event contributions; the claim is conditional on the specific NP parametrizations used and the restriction to the low-mass regime where m is much less than P_J R.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling with a Gaussian Process emulator trained on 500 randomly sampled points, fitting resummed cross-section predictions at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy to experimental jet invariant mass data from exclusive W/Z+jet events (CMS at 7 TeV) and inclusive jet events (STAR at 200 GeV, ATLAS at 7 TeV and 5.02 TeV). Two NP prescriptions were tested: a new three-dimensional angular ("theta") prescription where NP effects add a vector angular deflection to the perturbative splitting, and a conventional momentum shift ("mass shift") prescription where NP effects shift the jet mass via a transverse momentum kick. The analysis extracted NP parameters for hadronization, initial soft-gluon radiation, and underlying events, with quark and gluon parameters related by the color factor ratio C_F/C_A.

## Key numbers

- Collisional energy increases by a factor of up to 65 from RHIC (200 GeV) to LHC (7 TeV), covering the experimental data range
- For the theta prescription, the hadronization parameter for gluons a_{h,g} = 2.907 (+0.127/-0.169) from data set 1 and 2.637 (+0.113/-0.178) from data set 2, at 90% credible interval
- For the theta prescription, the underlying event parameter a_UE = 3.712 (+0.129/-0.107) from data set 1 and 3.856 (+0.108/-0.109) from data set 2, at 90% credible interval
- For the theta prescription, the initial soft-gluon radiation parameter a_{i,g} = 0.180 (+0.745/-0.146) from data set 1, consistent with zero within uncertainties
- For the mass shift prescription, the linear hadronization parameter b_{h,g}^{(1)} = 1.530 (+0.086/-0.116) from data set 1 and 1.494 (+0.080/-0.125) from data set 2, at 90% credible interval
- For the mass shift prescription, the underlying event parameter b_UE = 0.815 (+0.054/-0.062) from data set 1 and 0.863 (+0.050/-0.065) from data set 2, at 90% credible interval
- The e+e- validation used ALEPH data at sqrt(s) = 91.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the momentum shift prescription for NP effects in jet mass from Stewart (2014) and the three-dimensional angular hadronization model from Ringer (2019), providing the first simultaneous extraction of hadronization, initial soft-gluon radiation, and underlying event contributions to the jet invariant mass. The analysis covers a wider energy range than previous studies, from RHIC (STAR, 2021) through LHC (ATLAS, 2012 and 2018; CMS, 2013), and demonstrates that the new three-dimensional NP prescription is more robust than the mass shift prescription when including CMS dijet data at 13 TeV. The successful description of ALEPH e+e- data (Chen, 2021) without including it in the fit validates the predictive power of the framework, enabling future precision QCD studies and applications to other jet substructure observables.
