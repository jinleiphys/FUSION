# Parton Physics on Euclidean Lattice

> I show that the parton physics related to correlations of quarks and gluons on the light-cone can be studied through the matrix elements of frame-dependent, equal-time correlators in the large momentum limit. This observation allows practical calculations of parton properties on an Euclidean lattice. As an example, I demonstrate how to recover the leading-twist quark distribution by boosting an equal-time correlator to a large momentum.

# Parton Physics on Euclidean Lattice

## Key claim

Parton physics involving light-cone correlations can be computed from equal-time, frame-dependent matrix elements in the large momentum limit, allowing practical lattice QCD calculations. The equivalence holds only up to power-suppressed corrections of order Λ²QCD/(P^z)² and M²/(P^z)², which vanish asymptotically as the nucleon momentum P^z becomes large.

## Method

The author demonstrates that the leading-twist quark distribution q(x, μ²) can be recovered from a time-independent, non-local quark correlator ⟨P|ψ̄(z)γ^z exp(-ig∫₀ᶻ dz' A^z(z')) ψ(0)|P⟩ evaluated in a nucleon state with large momentum P^z, where x = k^z/P^z. The light-cone distribution is obtained by taking the infinite-momentum limit after renormalization, with the large logarithms ln P^z in the Euclidean correlator transformed into the standard renormalization scale dependence of the light-cone distribution through a perturbative matching condition Z(x, μ/P^z). The method generalizes to generalized parton distributions, transverse-momentum dependent distributions, Wigner distributions, light-cone amplitudes, light-cone wave functions, and higher-twist parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- The power-suppressed corrections to the leading-twist quark distribution are of order Λ²QCD/(P^z)² and M²/(P^z)², where ΛQCD is the QCD scale and M is the nucleon mass.
- The maximum nucleon momentum achievable on a lattice is approximately 1/a, where a denotes the lattice spacing.
- The smallest momentum on a lattice is 1/(L_z a), with L_z the number of lattice sites in the z-direction; to resolve x = 10⁻² requires approximately 100 lattice sites.
- The one-loop correction to the Euclidean quark distribution yields a term (α_s/2π) T(x) (Λ/P^z) with T(x) = C_F x/(1-x)² and C_F = 4/3, plus a term (α_s/2π) P(x) ln(Λ P^z/λ²) with P(x) = C_F (1+x²)/(1-x) (the standard Altarelli-Parisi kernel).
- The matching kernel at one loop is Z^(1)(x, μ/P^z) = T(x)(μ/P^z) + P(x) ln(P^z/μ).

## Context

This work extends the traditional approach of computing only moments of parton distributions through local twist-2 operators on the lattice, which becomes increasingly difficult for higher moments (Negele, year not specified). The method provides a direct alternative to light-front wave function approaches (Brodsky, year not specified) that have not yet yielded a realistic proton wave function despite many years of effort. The paper enables first-principles lattice QCD calculations of the full x-dependence of parton distributions, generalized parton distributions, transverse-momentum dependent distributions, and other light-cone correlations that were previously inaccessible, with the matching condition connecting the Euclidean correlator to the light-cone distribution requiring perturbative calculation (Ji, Zhang, and Zhao, year not specified).
