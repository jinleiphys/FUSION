# Influence of initial size on higher cumulant ratios of net-proton number fluctuations

> With the help of AMPT default model, we study the influence of initial size (centrality of collisions) on higher cumulant ratios of net-proton distributions. If the centrality is presented by impact parameter, there is a strong centrality dependent, in particular, in those peripheral collisions. This dependence is slightly reduced if the centrality is presented by number of participant, or charged multiplicity. However, the dynamical ratios are almost centrality independent. So the centrality dependence of dynamical ratios at RHIC beam energy scan are presented.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT default model, the centrality dependence of higher cumulant ratios of net-proton distributions is strong when centrality is defined by impact parameter, but this dependence is reduced when centrality is defined by number of participants or charged multiplicity; the dynamical ratios (after subtracting statistical fluctuations) are almost centrality independent, serving as a reference for background effects at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) default model to simulate Au+Au collisions at seven RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV). They classified collision centrality by three different measures: impact parameter (b), number of participating nucleons (N_part), and charged multiplicity (N_ch), and computed the skewness (S), kurtosis (kappa), and their dynamical counterparts (S_dyn and kappa_dyn) for net-proton distributions in the pseudorapidity range |eta| < 0.5, applying the centrality weighted method to remove bin-width effects.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at 39 GeV, the kurtosis values for peripheral collisions classified by impact parameter are "obviously higher" than those classified by N_part or N_ch (exact numerical values not given in text).
- Dynamical kurtosis values for Au+Au at 39 GeV are "close to zero in all centralities" when centrality is defined by N_part or N_ch.
- Dynamical skewness at the two lowest incident energies (7.7 and 11.5 GeV) is negative in peripheral collisions and becomes smaller towards peripheral collisions; at five higher energies (19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200 GeV) it is "around zero and almost energy and centrality independent".
- Dynamical kurtosis across all seven energies is "incident energy independent", negative in peripheral collisions, and "close to zero in mid-central to central collisions".

## Context

This work extends studies of net-proton cumulant ratios as probes of the QCD critical point, following theoretical proposals by Stephanov (2003, 2009, 2011) and Asakawa (2009), and experimental measurements by the STAR Collaboration (2010). It addresses the need to separate trivial centrality-dependent effects from critical fluctuations, building on prior work by Luo (2011) on centrality bin-width corrections and by Chen (2011) on statistical fluctuation subtraction. The results provide a baseline from a model without critical fluctuations (AMPT) against which future experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan can be compared.
