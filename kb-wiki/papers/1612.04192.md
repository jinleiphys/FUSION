# A multi-channel model for an α plus $^6$He nucleus cluster

> A multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method has been used to solve coupled sets of Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the $α$+${}^6$He cluster system, so finding a model spectrum for ${}^{10}$Be to more than 10 MeV excitation. Three states of ${}^6$He are included and the resonance character of the two excited states taken into account in finding solutions. A model Hamiltonian has been found that gives very good agreement with the known bound states and with some low-lying resonances of ${}^{10}$Be. More resonance states are predicted than have as yet been observed. The method also yields $S$-matrices which we have used to evaluate low-energy ${}^6$He-$α$ scattering cross sections. Reasonable reproduction of low-energy differential cross sections and of energy variation of cross sections measured at fixed scattering angles is found.

# Multi-channel model for an α plus ⁶He nucleus cluster

## Key claim

The multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method, applied to the α+⁶He cluster system, produces a model spectrum for ¹⁰Be that matches known bound states and low-lying resonances to within a few tens of keV, but only after including a monopole pairing term, orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials (OPP) for Pauli hindrance, and treating the two excited states of ⁶He as resonances with finite widths. The method predicts more resonance states than have been observed, and the calculated scattering cross sections show reasonable but not perfect agreement with measured data, with notable discrepancies at 2.7 MeV and in some angular ranges.

## Method

The authors used the MCAS method to solve coupled sets of Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the α+⁶He cluster system, treating ¹⁰Be as a coupled-channel problem involving three states of ⁶He (ground 0⁺, first excited 2⁺ resonance at 1.797 MeV, and second excited 2⁺ resonance at 5.6 MeV). A rotational collective model defined the matrix of interaction potentials with central, ℓ²-dependent, orbit-nuclear spin, and spin-spin components, plus a monopole term to account for pairing correlations affecting 0⁺ states. Pauli principle effects were included via orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials (OPP) with λ values of 10⁶ for fully blocked orbits and finite values (0.75–11.1 MeV) for Pauli-hindered orbits.

## Key numbers

- ⁶He ground state lies 0.973 MeV below the two-neutron break-up threshold
- First excited state of ⁶He: centroid 1.797 MeV, width 113 keV
- Second excited state of ⁶He: centroid 5.6 MeV, width 12 MeV
- α+⁶He threshold in ¹⁰Be: 7.413 MeV above ground state
- n+⁹Be threshold in ¹⁰Be: 6.812 MeV above ground state
- MCAS calculated ground state of ¹⁰Be: −7.421 MeV relative to α+⁶He threshold (experimental: −7.413 MeV)
- MCAS calculated 2⁺₁ state: −4.059 MeV (experimental: −4.048 MeV)
- MCAS calculated 3⁻₂ resonance: centroid 2.710 MeV, width 0.119 MeV (experimental: 2.734 MeV, 0.296 MeV)
- MCAS calculated 2⁺₃ resonance: centroid 0.429 MeV, width 0.006 MeV (experimental: 0.126 MeV, 0.006 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the MCAS method, previously applied to p+⁶He for ⁷Li (Canton et al., 2006) and other cluster systems (Fraser et al., 2008, 2013, 2016), to the α+⁶He system forming ¹⁰Be. It builds on phenomenological two-cluster models with local interactions (Dubovichenko, 2011) and addresses the need for coupled-channel treatments of exotic nuclei like the halo nucleus ⁶He. The results enable future studies of α capture on ⁶He and provide a framework for analyzing low-energy scattering data from radioactive ion beams (Suzuki et al., 2013), though the model predicts many unobserved resonance states that require experimental verification.
