# Analytical Results for the Classical and Quantum Tsallis Hadron Transverse Momentum Spectra: the Zeroth Order Approximation and beyond

> We derive the analytical expressions for the first and second order terms in the hadronic transverse momentum spectra obtained from the Tsallis normalized (Tsallis-1) statistics. We revisit the zeroth order quantum Tsallis distributions and obtain the corresponding analytical closed form expressions. It is observed that unlike the classical case, the analytical closed forms of the zeroth order quantum spectra do not resemble the phenomenological distributions used in the literature after $q \to q^{-1}$ substitution, where $q$ is the Tsallis entropic parameter. However, the factorization approximation increases the extent of similarity.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical closed-form expressions for the first and second order correction terms to the Tsallis Maxwell-Boltzmann transverse momentum spectra, and shows that unlike the classical case, the zeroth order quantum Tsallis spectra do not become identical to the phenomenological quantum Tsallis distributions under the \(q \to q^{-1}\) substitution, although the factorization approximation increases the similarity.

## Method

The authors start from the Tsallis normalized (Tsallis-1) statistical mechanics and express the hadronic transverse momentum spectra as an infinite series expansion. For the Maxwell-Boltzmann case, they evaluate the first and second order terms using contour-integral representations of the modified Bessel function \(K_2\) and its square, summing residues at poles to obtain closed analytical forms. For the quantum Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac spectra, they compute the zeroth order term analytically by expanding the integrand in a series and expressing the result in terms of the Hurwitz-zeta function \(\zeta(s,a)\).

## Key numbers

* The first order mean occupation number \(\langle n_p^{(1)}\rangle\) is expressed as a sum of residues at poles \(s = 2, 1, 0, -1, -2, \dots, -k\), with the residue at \(s = -k\) containing the digamma function \(\psi^{(0)}\); the condition for convergence is \(\frac{2qT}{(1-q)m} \left(1+\frac{q-1}{q}\frac{\Lambda-\varepsilon_p+2\mu}{T}\right) > 1\).
* The second order mean occupation number \(\langle n_p^{(2)}\rangle\) is expressed as a sum of residues at poles \(s = 2, 1, 0, -1, -2, \dots, -k-2\), with the residue at \(s = 0\) containing the Euler-Mascheroni constant \(\gamma_{\mathrm{E}} = 0.57721\dots\).
* The zeroth order Bose-Einstein spectrum is given by \(\frac{gV}{(2\pi)^2} p_{\mathrm{T}} m_{\mathrm{T}} \cosh y \left[\frac{(1-q)(m_{\mathrm{T}} \cosh y - \mu)}{q T}\right]^{-\frac{1}{1-q}} \zeta\left(\frac{1}{1-q}, 1 + \frac{q T}{(1-q)(m_{\mathrm{T}} \cosh y - \mu)}\right)\).
* The zeroth order Fermi-Dirac spectrum is given by \(\frac{gV}{(2\pi)^2} p_{\mathrm{T}} m_{\mathrm{T}} \cosh y \left[\frac{2(1-q)(m_{\mathrm{T}} \cosh y - \mu)}{q T}\right]^{-\frac{1}{1-q}} \left[ \zeta\left(\frac{1}{1-q}, \frac{1}{2} + \frac{q T}{2(1-q)(m_{\mathrm{T}} \cosh y - \mu)}\right) - \zeta\left(\frac{1}{1-q}, 1 + \frac{q T}{2(1-q)(m_{\mathrm{T}} \cosh y - \mu)}\right) \right]\).

## Context

This work extends the program of deriving phenomenological Tsallis distributions from first-principles Tsallis statistical mechanics, following earlier studies by Parvan (2016, 2017, 2019, 2020) which showed that the zeroth order truncation of the Maxwell-Boltzmann series yields the widely used phenomenological Tsallis distribution after a \(q \to q^{-1}\) substitution. The present paper provides the first and second order analytical corrections needed when the zeroth order approximation is insufficient at certain collision energies, and clarifies that the quantum zeroth order spectra do not map onto the phenomenological quantum Tsallis distributions of Miller and of the Tsallis Fermi-Dirac/ Bose-Einstein literature via the same substitution. The results enable more precise fits to experimental hadron spectra by including higher order terms, and provide a rigorous basis for comparing quantum Tsallis models with data.
