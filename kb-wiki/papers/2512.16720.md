# A finite temperature framework for quark matter with color-superconducting phases

> Current observations of neutron stars and measurements of gravitational waves only provide constraints on the zero temperature ($T=0$) equation of state (EoS) of dense matter. The detection of the post-merger gravitational-wave signal from a binary neutron star merger would additionally provide access to finite-temperature properties of the EoS which contain more information about the composition and the interactions of dense matter than the cold EoS alone. In particular deconfined quark matter may be probed by its characteristic finite temperature effects. This is especially the case for color-superconducting phases, in which the quasiparticle contribution to the thermal pressure is exponentially suppressed at low temperatures. Here we develop a new finite $T$ framework to model the thermal EoS for dense quark matter based on the cold quark matter EoS which is useful for numerical relativity simulations. We test the validity of the framework against a three-flavor NJL mean-field calculation, both with and without diquark pairing. We find that even for the complicated phase diagram of the NJL model including multiple different phases the framework is accurate to the few percent level for temperatures up to $T\sim 50\,$MeV.

## Key claim
The paper develops a finite-temperature framework for quark matter that can accurately approximate the thermal equation of state for color-superconducting phases (2SC and CFL) based only on the cold (T=0) equation of state, with the hedge that the framework is accurate to the few percent level for temperatures up to T~50 MeV and less than ~20% relative error up to T~100 MeV in densities relevant for neutron star cores and merger simulations.

## Method
The authors construct a Taylor expansion in temperature for the pressure of unpaired quarks using derivatives of the pressure at zero temperature, then add an analytic approximation for the thermal pressure contribution of paired quarks derived from the quasiparticle dispersion relation with a diquark gap. They test this framework against a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation with self-consistent treatment of chiral condensates and diquark pairing, using the renormalization-group consistent treatment to avoid cutoff artifacts. For temperatures where the quasiparticle contribution becomes thermodynamically unstable, they linearly extrapolate the pressure using the entropy density at the instability temperature.

## Key numbers
- The framework is accurate to the few percent level for temperatures up to T~50 MeV and less than ~20% relative error up to T~100 MeV in densities relevant for neutron star cores and merger simulations.
- The critical temperature for the 2SC-to-NQM phase transition is estimated via the BCS relation T_c^BCS ≈ 0.57 Δ_3(T=0), with T_c^2SC→NQM = T_c^BCS for 2SC at T=0 and T_c^CFL→2SC = 2^(-1/3) T_c^BCS for CFL at T=0.
- The instability temperature T* for linear extrapolation is approximately 0.9039 T_c for 2SC and 0.8889 T_c for CFL phases.
- The entropy density at the instability temperature is approximately 8.944 (μ̄²/π^(3/2)) T* for 2SC and 12.09 (μ̄²/π^(3/2)) T* for CFL phases.

## Context
This work extends the finite-temperature Taylor expansion framework of Mroczek (2024) which was previously demonstrated for mean-field nucleonic models, now applied to color-superconducting quark matter phases. The framework enables numerical relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers to incorporate finite-temperature effects of deconfined quark matter with color-superconducting phases, which is important because current simulations estimate merger temperatures up to T≈50 MeV. The approach improves upon ideal Fermi gas approximations and expansions that assume only nucleonic degrees of freedom, such as those by Raithel (2019, 2021) and Most (2021), which are not suitable for 2SC or CFL quark matter.
