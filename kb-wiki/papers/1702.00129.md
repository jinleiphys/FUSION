# Recent selected theory developments for NICA

> In this contribution I present a few selected topics of recent theoretical developments of relevance for the NICA facility under construction. In a first part, I discuss new aspects of the QCD phase diagram like the possible existence of a critical endpoint of first-order phase transitions from the perspective of generalizations of the 3-flavor PNJL model including the conjecture of a universal pressure for the onset of deconfinement in heavy-ion collisions and astrophysics. A second part is devoted to first results of the newly constructed event simulator (THESEUS) which is based on the particlization of the former three-fluid hydrodynamics code by Ivanov, Russkikh and Toneev that allows also to study the role of hadronic final state interactions. Possible signals of the mixed phase accessible at NICA are considered. In particular, the robustness of the baryon-stopping signal of deconfinement and the occurrence of antiflow for protons have been investigated for Au+Au collisions in the range of the NICA-MPD energy scan for $\sqrt{s} \sim 6 \dots 8$ GeV. This signal is reflected also in the flow pattern of light nuclear clusters, in particular deuterons. The sharp peak for the $K^+/π^+$ ratio at $\sqrt{s} \sim 8$ GeV (the "horn" effect) is not obtained in the present version of THESEUS and calls for improvement of the equation of state input. I report the recent progress in developing a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to a unified description of quark-hadron matter which includes now strangeness and reveals a new mechanism for explaining the $K^+/π^+$ ratio due to the pronounced occurrence of an anomalous mode in the $K^+$ at finite baryochemical potentials.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the QCD critical endpoint (CEP) may exist at a location accessible to NICA experiments (around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim 6\) GeV), but this conclusion depends on model assumptions such as a low or vanishing vector coupling at low chemical potentials, and the "horn" effect in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio is not yet reproduced by the THESEUS event simulator, requiring improved equation of state input.

## Method

The author reviews several theoretical approaches: nonlocal Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models constrained by lattice QCD to locate the CEP; a 3-flavor color-superconducting PNJL model with a universal transition pressure; and a model enforcing coincident chiral and deconfinement transitions via a bag pressure. A new event simulator, THESEUS, is constructed by coupling a three-fluid hydrodynamics code (Ivanov, Russkikh, Toneev) with UrQMD for hadronic final-state interactions, and is used to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} \sim 6-8\) GeV. A generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck (GBU) approach is developed to describe quark-hadron matter including strangeness, using medium-dependent hadronic phase shifts to capture the Mott effect.

## Key numbers

- The CEP in the nonlocal PNJL model (Set C) is predicted at \(T_{\rm CEP}\sim 130\) MeV and \(\mu_{\rm CEP}=225\) MeV, corresponding to chemical freeze-out parameters for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim 6\) GeV.
- The universal transition pressure for deconfinement is estimated as 80 MeV/fm\(^3\) from the PNJL model, and from astrophysical analysis of high-mass twin stars the limiting pressure of the hadronic phase is 74–90 MeV/fm\(^3\), consistent with the heavy-ion collision value of \(82\pm 8\) MeV/fm\(^3\) from Rafelski and Petran (2013).
- The upper limiting density for the mixed phase in the model of Fischer, Klahn and Hempel (2016) is only about twice nuclear saturation density for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim 6\) GeV, where \(T\sim 130\) MeV when entering the mixed phase and heating to about 150 MeV after completion.
- The antiflow signal for protons and deuterons is observed in THESEUS simulations for the 2-phase EoS in the energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim 6-7\) GeV, with a dip at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6-8\) GeV.
- The NICA-MPD energy scan covers \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4-11\) GeV, with entropy per baryon \(s/n=10-25\).
- The "horn" effect for the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio peaks at \(\sqrt{s} \sim 8\) GeV in experimental data but is not reproduced by the current THESEUS version.

## Context

This work extends the NICA White Paper (European Physical Journal A, 2016) and prior studies of the QCD phase diagram using PNJL models (Contrera et al., 2012; Fischer et al., 2016) and three-fluid hydrodynamics (Ivanov et al., 2015, 2016). The THESEUS simulator builds on the three-fluid hydrodynamics code of Ivanov, Russkikh and Toneev, and the GBU approach extends earlier work by Schmidt (1990), Hufner et al. (1994), and Blaschke et al. (2013) to include strangeness. The results enable future NICA experiments to search for the CEP and mixed-phase signals such as baryon stopping, antiflow of protons and deuterons, and the \(K^+/\pi^+\) horn effect, with improved equations of state under development.
