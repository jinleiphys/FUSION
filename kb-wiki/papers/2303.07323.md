# Constraint of the Nuclear Dissipation Coefficient in Fission of Hypernuclei

> Experimental studies of nuclear fission induced by fusion, transfer, spallation, fragmentation, and electromagnetic reactions in combination with state-of-the-art calculations are successful to investigate the nuclear dissipation mechanism in normal nuclear matter, containing only nucleons. The dissipation mechanism has been widely studied by the use of many different fission observables and nowadays the dissipation coefficients involved in transport theories are well constrained. However, the existence of hypernuclei and the possible presence of hyperons in neutron stars make it necessary to extend the investigation of the nuclear dissipation coefficient to the strangeness sector. In this Letter, we use fission reactions of hypernuclei to constrain for the first time the dissipation coefficient in hypernuclear matter, observing that this coefficient increases a factor of 6 in presence of a single $Λ$-hyperon with respect to normal nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The nuclear dissipation coefficient in hypernuclear matter is constrained for the first time using fission reactions of hypernuclei, and is found to increase by a factor of approximately 6 when a single Λ-hyperon is present compared to normal nuclear matter; however, the authors caution that for each target nucleus only a single experimental data point is available, so more measurements are needed to confirm the result.

## Method

The authors compare hypernuclear fission cross sections measured at the COSY-Jülich facility for proton-induced reactions on ²³⁸U and ²⁰⁹Bi targets with dynamical calculations using the INCL intranuclear-cascade model coupled to the ABLA deexcitation code. The fission decay width is described by a Kramers-modified Bohr-Wheeler model that includes a nuclear dissipation coefficient β as a free parameter, and the time dependence of the fission width is treated via an analytical approximation to the one-dimensional Fokker-Planck equation.

## Key numbers

- For normal nuclear matter (spallation and fragmentation of normal nuclei), the dissipation coefficient is β = (4.5 ± 2.0) × 10²¹ s⁻¹.
- For p(²⁰⁹Bi, hypernuclei) reactions, the dissipation coefficient is β = (16 ± 7) × 10²¹ s⁻¹.
- For p(²³⁸U, hypernuclei) reactions, the dissipation coefficient is β = (40 ± 10) × 10²¹ s⁻¹.
- The average dissipation coefficient for hypernuclear matter with a single Λ-hyperon is (28 ± 12) × 10²¹ s⁻¹, which is a factor of 6 larger than the normal nuclear matter value.
- A linear extrapolation suggests β = 75 × 10²¹ s⁻¹ for hypernuclei containing three Λ-hyperons.
- Uncertainties in the nuclear level densities and fission barrier heights contribute at most 18% of the total hypernuclear fission cross section, with 16% attributed to the fission barrier height.

## Context

This work extends the methodology used to constrain the nuclear dissipation coefficient in normal nuclear matter through fission observables (Rodríguez-Sánchez 2014, Ayyad 2015, Rodríguez-Sánchez 2015, 2016) to the strangeness sector for the first time. The results are relevant for understanding the role of hyperons in neutron star dynamics, particularly for r-mode instabilities and gravitational wave emission, where shear and bulk viscosities from hyperonic matter are important (Andersson 1998, Friedman 1998, Lindblom 2002). The authors call for new measurements at facilities such as FAIR and HIAF to confirm the results and to study the dissipation coefficient for double-Λ hypernuclei and for hypernuclei far from stability.
