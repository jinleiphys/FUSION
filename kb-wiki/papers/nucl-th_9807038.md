# The parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant from a realistic three flavor Skyrme model

> We study the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling $G_π$ in the framework of a realistic three flavor Skryme model. We find a sizeable enhancement of $G_π\simeq 0.8 ... 1.3 \cdot 10^{-7}$ compared to previous calculations in two-flavor models with vector mesons. This strangeness enhancement stems from induced kaon fields of the chiral soliton and the non-monotoneous dependence on symmetry breaking of the nucleon matrix element of the flavor singlet piece of the operator associated with these induced fields. Both features are sensitive to four quark operators including an $\bar s s$ pair.

## Key claim
The parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant \(G_\pi\) is found to be \(0.8 \ldots 1.3 \cdot 10^{-7}\) in a three-flavor Skyrme model, which is a sizeable enhancement compared to previous two-flavor calculations with vector mesons that gave \(G_\pi \simeq 0.3 \cdot 10^{-7}\). This enhancement depends on the inclusion of induced kaon fields and the non-monotoneous dependence on symmetry breaking of the nucleon matrix element of the flavor singlet piece of the operator associated with these fields.

## Method
The authors use a three-flavor Skyrme model with the Lagrange density including the Skyrme term, symmetry breaking terms, and the Wess-Zumino-Witten term. The classical soliton is obtained from a hedgehog ansatz, and physical baryon states are constructed via adiabatic rotation with collective coordinates, including induced kaon fields from the Wess-Zumino action. The parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant is extracted from the current-current interaction by evaluating matrix elements of the weak Hamiltonian between nucleon states, using the collective coordinate quantization and inserting a complete set of baryon eigenstates.

## Key numbers
- \(G_\pi = 0.8 \cdot 10^{-7}\) for the Skyrme parameter \(e = 4.0\) and \(G_\pi = 1.3 \cdot 10^{-7}\) for \(e = 4.5\), at physical kaon mass \(m_K = 495\) MeV.
- The two-flavor generalized Skyrme result is \(G_\pi = 0.2 \ldots 0.3 \cdot 10^{-7}\) (from Kaiser-Meissner (1990) and Kaiser-Meissner (1991)).
- Setting the induced kaon field \(W(r) = 0\) reduces \(G_\pi\) from \(1.1 \cdot 10^{-7}\) to \(0.5 \cdot 10^{-7}\) for \(e = 4.25\).
- The strange moment of inertia \(\beta^2 = 0.27\)/GeV with induced kaon fields, reduced to \(0.24\)/GeV without them, for \(e = 4.25\).
- Input parameters: \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, \(f_K = 113\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, \(m_K = 495\) MeV, \(G_F = 1.16 \cdot 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\sin^2 \Theta_W = 0.23\).

## Context
This work extends previous two-flavor Skyrme model calculations of parity-violating meson-nucleon couplings by Kaiser and Meissner (1990, 1991), which gave a very small \(G_\pi\) and were not sensitive to operators involving strange quarks. The results are in fair agreement with recent quark model calculations by Dubovik and Zenkin (1993) and Feldman, Crawford, and Henley (1993), which gave \(G_\pi = 2.0 \ldots 2.7 \cdot 10^{-7}\). The study enables future work on parity-violating vector meson couplings to the nucleon and vertices involving the \(\Delta\) resonance in a three-flavor vector meson Skyrme model.
