# Photoabsorption spectra in the continuum of molecules and atomic clusters

> We present linear response theories in the continuum capable of describing photoionization spectra and dynamic polarizabilities of finite systems with no spatial symmetry. Our formulations are based on the time-dependent local density approximation with uniform grid representation in the three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate. Effects of the continuum are taken into account either with a Green's function method or with a complex absorbing potential in a real-time method. The two methods are applied to a negatively charged cluster in the spherical jellium model and to some small molecules (silane, acetylene and ethylene).

## Key claim

The paper presents two linear response methods (real-time with complex absorbing potential and Green's function) that can treat continuum photoionization spectra for finite systems without spatial symmetry, but the real-time method requires careful tuning of the absorbing potential parameters to avoid spurious reflections, and the Green's function method requires the self-consistent potential to be negligible outside the computational box.

## Method

The authors implement time-dependent local density approximation (TDLDA) on a uniform three-dimensional Cartesian grid. The real-time method solves the time-dependent Kohn-Sham equation explicitly and uses a complex absorbing potential at the box boundary to mimic continuum escape, with the dynamic polarizability obtained via Fourier transform of the time-dependent dipole moment. The Green's function method solves the linear response equations iteratively using a free-particle Green's function with outgoing boundary conditions, where the asymptotic behavior outside the box is handled by multipole expansion of Coulomb wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Grid spacing of 1.5 Å used for Na₇⁻ cluster calculations, with spherical box radius R=12 Å and 2109 mesh points
- For Na₇⁻, the ionization threshold is 1.57 eV (HOMO at -0.37 eV, Coulomb potential e²/R=1.2 eV at box edge)
- Main photoabsorption peak in Na₇⁻ appears at 2.35 eV above threshold in the TDLDA calculation, compared to 1.4 eV in the independent-particle approximation
- Summed oscillator strengths up to 5 eV correspond to about 95% of the Thomas-Kuhn-Reiche sum rule for valence electrons
- For silane (SiH₄), a spherical box of radius R=7 Å with grid spacing 0.4 Å was used
- The absorbing potential criterion requires 18(Δr)⁻¹ < |W₀| < 0.07Δr (with W₀ in eV and Δr in Å) for electrons at 0.8 eV above threshold

## Context

This work extends the TDLDA continuum response methods previously applied to spherical systems (Zangwill and Soven 1980, Ekardt 1984, Bertsch 1990) to molecules without spatial symmetry, using the three-dimensional grid representation developed by Chelikowsky et al. (1994). The real-time method builds on earlier work by Yabana and Bertsch (1996, 1999) for dynamic polarizability calculations. The Green's function approach adapts the modified Sternheimer method (Mahan 1980) with outgoing boundary conditions. The methods enable calculation of photoionization spectra for small molecules including silane, acetylene, and ethylene, and can be extended to larger systems where symmetry-based approaches are impractical.
