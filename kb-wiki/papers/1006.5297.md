# Observation of isotonic symmetry for enhanced quadrupole collectivity in neutron-rich 62,64,66Fe isotopes at N=40

> The transition rates for the 2_{1}^{+} states in 62,64,66Fe were studied using the Recoil Distance Doppler-Shift technique applied to projectile Coulomb excitation reactions. The deduced E2 strengths illustrate the enhanced collectivity of the neutron-rich Fe isotopes up to N=40. The results are interpreted by the generalized concept of valence proton symmetry which describes the evolution of nuclear structure around N=40 as governed by the number of valence protons with respect to Z~30. The deformation suggested by the experimental data is reproduced by state-of-the-art shell calculations with a new effective interaction developed for the fpgd valence space.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the enhanced quadrupole collectivity observed in neutron-rich Fe isotopes up to N=40 can be interpreted by a generalized valence proton symmetry scheme with respect to Z approximately 30, rather than the canonical magic number Z=28, and that this symmetry explains the similar evolution of collectivity in isotone pairs such as Fe/Se and Cr/Kr.

## Method

The lifetimes of the 2_1^+ states in 62,64,66Fe were measured using the Recoil Distance Doppler-Shift (RDDS) technique applied to intermediate-energy projectile Coulomb excitation reactions at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. Secondary beams of Fe isotopes produced by fragmentation of a primary 76Ge beam at 130 AMeV were directed onto a plunger device with a gold target and niobium degrader, and gamma rays were detected by the Segmented Germanium Array (SeGA) while reaction products were identified by the S800 spectrograph. Lifetimes were determined by line-shape analysis of the Doppler-shifted gamma-ray spectra at multiple target-degrader distances.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of 2_1^+ state in 62Fe: 8.0(10) ps, corresponding to B(E2) = 198(25) e^2fm^4
- Lifetime of 2_1^+ state in 64Fe: 10.3(10) ps, corresponding to B(E2) = 344(33) e^2fm^4
- Lifetime of 2_1^+ state in 66Fe: 39.4(40) ps, corresponding to B(E2) = 332(34) e^2fm^4
- Excitation energies of 2_1^+ states: 877 keV for 62Fe, 746 keV for 64Fe, 575 keV for 66Fe
- Deformation parameter beta_2 approximately 0.28 calculated from measured B(E2) of 64,66Fe
- Beam energies: 97.8 AMeV for 62Fe, 95.0 AMeV for 64Fe, 88.3 AMeV for 66Fe
- Beam velocities before degrader: v/c = 0.368 for 62Fe, 0.364 for 64Fe, 0.346 for 66Fe

## Context

This work extends previous measurements by Ljungvall et al. (2010) that indicated a sudden increase of B(E2) from 62Fe to 64Fe, and provides the first evidence that enhanced collectivity persists in 66Fe at N=40. The results contrast with the doubly magic character of the neighboring N=40 isotone 68Ni, where a steep decrease of B(E2) was observed from 66Ni to 68Ni by Sorlin et al. (2002). The generalized valence proton symmetry scheme proposed here, with respect to Z approximately 30 rather than Z=28, enables predictions of large collectivity for further neutron-rich Fe and Cr isotopes beyond N=40, and the shell model calculations using the new LNPS effective interaction developed by Lenzi, Nowacki, Poves, and Sieja reproduce the experimental data well.
