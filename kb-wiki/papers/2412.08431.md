# Pairing in fission: Mean-field and collective inertias study

> Pairing plays a crucial role in the microscopic description of nuclear fission. Microscopic methods provide access to three quantities related to pairing, namely, the pairing gap ($Δ$), the particle number fluctuations ($ Δ\hat{N}^2 $), and the quenching factor (QF). The aim of this work is to analyse the impact of each of these quantities on the static description of the fission process.

## Key claim

The three traditional measures of pairing correlations in fission, the pairing gap (Δ), particle number fluctuations (ΔN̂²), and the quenching factor (QF), produce qualitatively different effects on potential energy surfaces and fission barriers, despite all reducing collective inertias; specifically, increasing Δ or ΔN̂² raises the fission barrier while increasing QF lowers it, contradicting the common assumption that these methods are interchangeable.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction in an axially symmetric deformed harmonic oscillator basis with 16 shells. They computed potential energy surfaces for ²⁶²Rf, ²⁶⁰Fm, and several actinide isotopes, imposing constraints on quadrupole moment Q₂₀ together with either the pairing gap Δ, the particle number fluctuation ΔN̂², or the quenching factor QF. Spontaneous fission half-lives were estimated via the WKB approximation with perturbative cranking collective inertias.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁶²Rf, increasing the pairing gap Δ raises the potential energy and broadens the fission barrier, while collective inertias decrease as the inverse square of Δ.
- For ²³⁸Pu and ²³⁶U, increasing QF by 0.05 lowers the first symmetric fission barrier by approximately 0.4 MeV; the second asymmetric barrier changes by less than 0.2 MeV.
- For ²⁶²Rf and ²⁶⁰Fm, increasing the pairing strength by 10% (QF = 1.10) reduces the fission barrier by a few MeV and decreases collective inertias, leading to shorter spontaneous fission half-lives.
- The optimal QF value for reproducing experimental half-lives with the D1S parametrization lies between 1.05 and 1.10 for the actinides studied.
- The effective inertias computed with Δ and ΔN̂² as collective variables agree well, especially at higher values of these constraints.

## Context

This work systematizes and reconciles contradictory findings from earlier studies on pairing in fission, such as those by Ledergerber and Pauli (1973), Moretto and Babinet (1974), and the Lublin group (Staszczak 1985, Lojewski 1999), which used macroscopic-microscopic models with BCS pairing, and more recent self-consistent studies by Giuliani (2014) and Rodriguez-Guzman (2014) using Skyrme and Gogny functionals. The authors clarify that the quenching factor method (renormalizing the pairing interaction) produces opposite barrier-height trends compared to constraining Δ or ΔN̂², enabling future work to choose the appropriate method for specific fission applications.
