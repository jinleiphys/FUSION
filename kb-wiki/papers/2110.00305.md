# Constraints on high density equation of state from maximum neutron star mass

> The low density nuclear matter equation of state is strongly constrained by nuclear properties, however, for constraining the high density equation of state it is necessary to resort to indirect information obtained from the observation of neutron stars, compact objects that may have a central density several times nuclear matter saturation density, $n_0$. Taking a meta-modelling approach to generate a huge set of equation of state that satisfy nuclear matter properties close to $n_0$ and that do not contain a first order phase transition, the possibility of constraining the high density equation of state was investigated. The entire information obtained from the GW170817 event for the probability distribution of $\tildeΛ$ was used to make a probabilistic inference of the EOS, which goes beyond the constraints imposed by nuclear matter properties. Nuclear matter properties close to saturation, below $2n_0$, do not allow us to distinguish between equations of state that predict different neutron star (NS) maximum masses. This is, however, not true if the equation of state is constrained at low densities by the tidal deformability of the NS merger associated to GW170817. Above $3n_0$, differences may be large, for both approaches, and, in particular, the pressure and speed of sound of the sets studied do not overlap, showing that the knowledge of the NS maximum mass may give important information on the high density EOS. Narrowing the maximum mass uncertainty interval will have a sizeable effect on constraining the high density EOS.

## Key claim

The high-density equation of state (EOS) above 3 times nuclear saturation density can be meaningfully constrained by the neutron star maximum mass, but only when the EOS is also constrained at low densities by the tidal deformability measurement from GW170817; nuclear matter properties below 2n₀ alone cannot distinguish between EOS that predict different maximum masses.

## Method

The authors used a meta-modelling approach, generating 150,000 EOS by randomly sampling an 8-dimensional multivariate Gaussian distribution of nuclear matter empirical parameters (isoscalar and isovector coefficients up to fourth order in a Taylor expansion around saturation density). Each EOS was required to satisfy thermodynamic stability, causality, and support a maximum mass of at least 1.97 M⊙, with no first-order phase transition allowed. The full posterior probability distribution of the binary tidal deformability \(\tilde{\Lambda}\) from GW170817 was then used to perform a probabilistic inference on the EOS pressure and speed of sound at high densities, conditioning on \(\tilde{\Lambda}\) via multivariate Gaussian conditioning.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass range covered: \(1.97 < M_{\text{max}}/M_\odot \leq 2.66\), with the highest value in the dataset being \(2.66 M_\odot\)
- Only 3162 EOS (2.11% of the dataset) supported \(M_{\text{max}} > 2.5 M_\odot\)
- For set 1 (\(1.97 < M_{\text{max}}/M_\odot \leq 2.20\)): mean radius of a 1.4 M⊙ star \(R_{1.4M_\odot} = 12.34 \pm 0.22\) km; mean tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4M_\odot} = 427.36 \pm 50.37\)
- For set 3 (\(2.40 < M_{\text{max}}/M_\odot \leq 2.66\)): mean \(R_{1.4M_\odot} = 12.61 \pm 0.20\) km; mean \(\Lambda_{1.4M_\odot} = 514.68 \pm 47.29\)
- The 90% credible intervals for the speed of sound squared for set 1 and set 3 do not overlap for densities \(n > 0.4\) fm\(^{-3}\) (approximately \(> 2.6 n_0\))
- Central density of maximum mass stars in set 3: \(5.05 < n_{\text{max}}/n_0 < 6.19\)

## Context

This work extends the constraints from the GW170817 event (Abbott 2018) and previous maximum mass estimates (e.g., Shibata 2017, Rezzolla 2017, Margalit 2017, Alsing 2018) by using the full posterior probability distribution of \(\tilde{\Lambda}\) rather than just its credible interval. It builds on the meta-modelling approach of Margueron (2018) and contrasts with polytropic EOS parametrizations used in the LIGO/Virgo analysis, showing that the Taylor expansion approach produces narrower pressure bands at low densities. The results enable future work to quantify how narrowing the maximum mass uncertainty interval (e.g., from future pulsar observations or gravitational-wave detections) would directly constrain the high-density EOS, particularly the pressure and speed of sound above 3n₀.
