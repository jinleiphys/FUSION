# Chaos and Quantum Chaos in Nuclear Systems

> The presence of chaos and quantum chaos is shown in two different nuclear systems. We analyze the chaotic behaviour of the classical SU(2) Yang--Mills--Higgs system, and then we study quantum chaos in the nuclear shell model calculating the spectral statistics of $A=46$--$50$ atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

Classical chaos in the spatially homogeneous SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system can be suppressed by large values of the Higgs field in the vacuum, and quantum chaos in the nuclear shell model shows significant deviations from Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) predictions for single closed-shell nuclei like calcium isotopes, contrary to the conventional view that such nuclei are chaotic.

## Method

The author analytically studied the order-to-chaos transition in the classical SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system using the curvature criterion of the potential energy surface, deriving the critical energy for the transition. For the quantum chaos study, exact shell-model diagonalizations were performed in the m-scheme using the Lanczos algorithm with the code ANTOINE, assuming a ⁴⁰Ca inert core and using a minimally modified Kuo-Brown realistic force with monopole improvements. The spectral statistics of low-lying states (up to 6 MeV above the yrast line) for A=46-50 nuclei were analyzed by calculating the Brody parameter ω from the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(s).

## Key numbers

- Critical energy for order-to-chaos transition in the SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system: E_c = 6 g² v⁴, occurring at q₁ = ±√2 v
- Brody parameter ω for ⁴⁶V up to 4 MeV above yrast: 1.14
- Brody parameter ω for ⁴⁶Ti up to 4 MeV above yrast: 0.90
- Brody parameter ω for ⁴⁶Sc up to 4 MeV above yrast: 0.81
- Brody parameter ω for ⁴⁶Ca up to 4 MeV above yrast: 0.41
- Brody parameter ω for ⁴⁸Ca up to 4 MeV above yrast: 0.58
- Brody parameter ω for ⁵⁰Ca up to 4 MeV above yrast: 0.67
- Brody parameter ω for ⁴⁶Ca up to 6 MeV above yrast: 0.51
- Brody parameter ω for ⁴⁸Ca up to 6 MeV above yrast: 0.66
- Brody parameter ω for ⁵⁰Ca up to 6 MeV above yrast: 0.62

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies of chaos in classical field theories by Savvidy (1984) and applies the curvature criterion method of Cerjan and Porter (1986) to the SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system. For the nuclear shell model, it builds on the extensive study of neutron and proton resonance statistics by Bohigas and collaborators (1984) and uses the Brody distribution (Brody, 1973) to quantify deviations from GOE predictions. The results for calcium isotopes challenge the assumption that all spherical nuclei exhibit chaotic spectral statistics, and suggest that future work should examine non-homogeneous Yang-Mills-Higgs systems and investigate why single closed-shell nuclei show more regular behavior.
