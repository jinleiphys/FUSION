# Phase Structure of 2-Flavor Quark Matter: Heterogeneous Superconductors

> We analyze the free energy of charge and color neutral 2-flavor quark matter within the BCS approximation. We consider both the homogeneous gapless superconducting phase and the heterogeneous mixed phase where normal and BCS superconducting phases coexist. We calculate the surface tension between normal and superconducting phases and use it to compare the free energies of the gapless and mixed phases. Our calculation, which retains only the leading order gradient contribution to the free energy, indicates that the mixed phase is energetically favored over an interesting range of densities of relevance to 2 flavor quark matter in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the heterogeneous mixed phase (where normal and BCS superconducting phases coexist) is energetically favored over the homogeneous gapless superconducting phase for 2-flavor quark matter, but this conclusion depends on the surface tension being sufficiently small; the authors calculate the surface tension for the first time and find it is indeed small enough to favor the mixed phase.

## Method

The authors analyze the free energy of charge and color neutral 2-flavor quark matter within the BCS approximation using an Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model for the quark-quark interaction. They calculate the free energy for both the homogeneous gapless phase (imposing local charge neutrality) and the heterogeneous mixed phase (imposing global charge neutrality with the Gibbs criteria for phase coexistence). The surface tension between normal and superconducting phases is computed by solving the equation of motion for the spatial profile of the gap parameter \(\Delta(x)\), retaining only the leading order gradient contribution to the free energy, with the gradient coefficient determined by matching to the Meissner masses of gluons.

## Key numbers

* BCS gap \(\Delta_0 = 100\) MeV at quark chemical potential \(\mu = 350\) MeV and zero electric and color chemical potentials, used to fix the coupling \(g(\Lambda)\)
* Light quark masses set to 5 MeV, assumed small compared to \(\mu\)
* For \(\mu\) in the interval 300-500 MeV, the ratio \(\Delta_0/\delta\mu \simeq 1.3\) in the mixed phase
* Surface tension values: \(\sigma = 1.9\) MeV/fm\(^2\) at \(\mu = 350\) MeV; \(\sigma = 3.6\) MeV/fm\(^2\) at \(\mu = 400\) MeV; \(\sigma = 6.8\) MeV/fm\(^2\) at \(\mu = 450\) MeV; \(\sigma = 9.4\) MeV/fm\(^2\) at \(\mu = 475\) MeV
* Interface thickness \(t \simeq (1.5-2) \Delta_0^{-1}\)
* The gapless phase minimum occurs at \(\Delta \simeq 40\) MeV for \(\mu = 350\) MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of gapless superconducting phases in quark matter by Shovkovy and Huang (2003) and mixed phase scenarios by Bedaque (1999, 2003), and addresses the competition between these phases raised by Shovkovy (2003) who noted that the mixed phase is favored only for small surface tension. The authors provide the first calculation of the surface tension between normal and superconducting phases in this context, enabling a quantitative comparison. The results are relevant for determining the phase structure of 2-flavor quark matter in neutron star interiors, and the authors note that a small interval in \(\delta\mu/\Delta\) may also allow crystalline (LOFF) superconductivity as studied by Alford (2000).
