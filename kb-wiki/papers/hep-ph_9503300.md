# SUPPRESSION OF THE NEUTRON-ANTINEUTRON OSCILLATION IN NUCLEI

> The problem of nuclear decays occurring due to the neutron-antineutron oscillation is analyzed in the optical potentil model and within the field-theoretic S-matrix approach. The result of the optical potential model is rederived within the field theoretic S-matrix approach. The neutron- antineutron oscillation in nuclei is suppressed drastically. We discuss an example for the spin precession of an atom in which analogous suppression takes place due to transparent physical reasons.

# Suppression of the Neutron-Antineutron Oscillation in Nuclei

## Key claim
The nuclear decay width due to neutron-antineutron oscillation is suppressed by a factor ξ < 10⁻³¹ relative to the vacuum oscillation rate ε, and this suppression arises from both the energy gap between the nucleus and the antineutron-containing intermediate state and the antineutron annihilation width. The optical potential model result for the decay width is shown to be consistent with the field-theoretic S-matrix approach, contrary to claims in a recent paper (Ref. 11).

## Method
The author analyzes the neutron-antineutron oscillation in nuclei using two complementary formalisms: an optical potential model with a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian containing a constant antineutron annihilation width Γ_A, and a field-theoretic S-matrix approach treating the baryon-number-violating interaction as a perturbation. The S-matrix approach is developed in both infinite-time and finite-time formulations, with the decay probability extracted from the time evolution of the neutron-component S-matrix element. A gedankenexperiment with deuterium atoms passing through Stern-Gerlach magnets is presented as an analog system exhibiting the same suppression mechanism through wavefunction collapse upon measurement of the spin-down component.

## Key numbers
- The suppression parameter ξ = ε Γ_A / [(M − M̄)² + Γ_A²/4] < 10⁻³¹, where ε is the vacuum oscillation rate, Γ_A is the hadron-scale antineutron annihilation width, and (M − M̄) is the mass difference between the nucleus and the antineutron-containing intermediate state.
- The vacuum neutron oscillation time limit is τ = 1/ε > 1 year, derived from nuclear stability and free-neutron experiments.
- For a nucleus with N = 100 neutrons, Γ_A = 100 MeV, and τ = 1 year, the exponential decay law holds over the time interval 10⁻²⁵ s < t < 10³⁸ s.
- The energy release in the nuclear decay process is approximately E ≈ 2 GeV.
- For ultracold neutrons in a copper trap, the antineutron absorption probability per collision is approximately 1/5 for tangent velocities of about 4 m/s, yielding a suppression factor of about 5 ε t₀ < 10⁻⁶ where t₀ (approximately seconds) is the typical time between collisions.

## Context
This work extends the optical potential model results of earlier studies (Refs. 1-8) on neutron-antineutron oscillation in nuclei and directly addresses a controversy raised by a recent paper (Ref. 11) that claimed disagreement between the optical potential and S-matrix approaches. The author demonstrates that both methods yield the same expression for the nuclear decay width, and clarifies that the apparent discrepancy arose from a misinterpretation of the optical theorem for bound and quasistationary states. The paper also provides a physical analogy to atomic spin precession with measurement-induced suppression, which enables intuitive understanding of the nuclear suppression mechanism. The results enable more reliable interpretation of experimental limits on neutron-antineutron oscillation from nuclear stability searches, and the author notes that further refinement of antineutron optical potential parameters would be desirable, though numerical estimates are unlikely to change by more than an order of magnitude.
