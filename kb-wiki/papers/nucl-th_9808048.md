# Radiative capture of protons by deuterons

> The differential cross section for radiative capture of protons by deuterons is calculated using different realistic NN interactions. We compare our results with the available experimental data below $E_x = 20 MeV$. Excellent agreement is found when taking into account meson exchange currents, dipole and quadrupole contributions, and the full initial state interaction. There is only a small difference between the magnitudes of the cross sections for the different potentials considered. The angular distributions, however, are practically potential independent.

## Key claim

Excellent agreement with experimental data for the differential cross section of proton-deuteron radiative capture below \(E_x = 20\) MeV is achieved only when simultaneously including meson exchange currents, dipole (E1) and quadrupole (E2) contributions, and the full initial state interaction; the angular distributions are practically independent of the chosen realistic NN potential, while the magnitude of the cross section shows a small potential dependence.

## Method

The authors solved Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for the proton-deuteron scattering amplitude, using separable representations (EST) of the Paris, Bonn A, and Bonn B realistic NN potentials. The electromagnetic operator included both E1 and E2 contributions via Siegert's theorem, and meson exchange currents were incorporated. The final \(^3\)He state was obtained from Faddeev calculations including partial waves up to \(j \leq 4\) (34 channels), while the initial scattering state included all partial waves with \(j \leq 2\).

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for \(p\)-\(d\) capture shows a small potential dependence, with the three potentials (Paris, Bonn A, Bonn B) yielding slightly different magnitudes; the experimental data of Belt et al. (1970) and King et al. (1984a, 1984b) are reproduced for all potentials.
- Angular distribution coefficient \(a_2\) shows almost no potential dependence, despite the \(D\)-state probability of the \(^3\)He wave function varying between 6% and 8% for the three potentials.
- The calculation incorporated 99.8% of the wave function by including all partial waves with \(j \leq 4\) (34 channels) in the full state.
- The differential cross section is expanded in Legendre polynomials up to \(k = 4\): \(\sigma(\theta) = A_0 (1 + \sum_{k=1}^{4} a_k P_k(\cos\theta))\), with total cross section \(\sigma = 4\pi A_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of \(^3\)He photodisintegration (Schadow 1998a, Sandhas 1998a) to the inverse radiative capture process, comparing with all sufficiently accurate data below \(E_x = 20\) MeV. It addresses inconsistencies among earlier experimental data sets (Belt 1970; King 1984a, 1984b; Matthews 1974; Skopik 1983a) and improves upon prior theoretical approaches such as the plane-wave calculations of Friar et al. (1990) and the variational hyperspherical calculations of Viviani et al. (1996). The results show that the radiative capture cross section magnitude is correlated with the three-body binding energy and does not provide an additional observable for testing different potentials.
