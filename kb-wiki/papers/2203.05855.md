# Accessing the in-medium effects on nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section with collective flows and nuclear stopping

> A systematic study of the in-medium correction factor ($F$) on nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section is performed within the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model. The effects of the beam energy dependence of $F$ on the directed, elliptic flow and nuclear stopping in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions with energy ranging from $0.09$ to $0.8A$ GeV are explored. It is found that the directed, elliptic flow and nuclear stopping at relatively low energies are very sensitive to $F$, and the sensitivity gradually weakens with increasing beam energy. The beam energy dependent in-medium correction factor $F$ is deduced from the comparison of the excitation functions of the directed, elliptic flow and nuclear stopping between the calculated results and the FOPI experimental data.

# Accessing the in-medium effects on nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section with collective flows and nuclear stopping

## Key claim

The in-medium correction factor \(F\) for the nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section is beam-energy dependent, with the sensitivity of directed flow, elliptic flow, and nuclear stopping to \(F\) being strongest at low beam energies and gradually weakening as energy increases; the authors extract a fixed equivalent \(F\) at each energy from a chi-square comparison of UrQMD calculations with FOPI experimental data on collective flows of free protons.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au collisions at beam energies from 0.09 to 0.8\(A\) GeV using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model with a soft momentum-dependent equation of state (incompressibility \(K_0 = 200\) MeV). They introduced a beam-energy-dependent in-medium correction factor \(\mathcal{F}(\rho,p)\) that includes a \(\tanh(E_{\text{lab}}/\varepsilon)\) term (with \(\varepsilon = 0.2\)) into the previously used density- and momentum-dependent parametrizations (FU3FP2 and FU3FP4), and also tested fixed equivalent factors \(F\) at each energy. The directed flow slope \(v_{11}\), elliptic flow at mid-rapidity \(v_{20}\), and nuclear stopping observable \(varxz\) were compared to FOPI experimental data, and \(F\) values were extracted via \(\chi^2\) minimization.

## Key numbers

* Extracted fixed equivalent in-medium correction factor \(F\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.09A\) GeV: \(F = 0.32 \pm 0.07\) (from chi-square analysis of free proton collective flows).
* Extracted \(F\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.15A\) GeV: \(F = 0.57 \pm 0.06\).
* Extracted \(F\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.25A\) GeV: \(F = 0.76 \pm 0.06\).
* Extracted \(F\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.4A\) GeV: \(F = 0.86 \pm 0.07\).
* Extracted \(F\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.6A\) GeV: \(F = 0.87 \pm 0.10\).
* Extracted \(F\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.8A\) GeV: \(F = 0.87 \pm 0.09\).
* At \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.09A\) GeV, the successful collision number with FU3FP2 (FU3FP4) is about 42% (43%) larger than with \(\varepsilon\)FU3FP2 (\(\varepsilon\)FU3FP4).
* The in-medium effect gives a reduction of the NNECS of about 80% at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 0.04A\) GeV and about 24% at \(0.25A\) GeV (citing previous INDRA results).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections by Li et al. (2000, 2003, 2006, 2011, 2018) and Wang et al. (2013, 2020) that used density-, momentum-, and isospin-dependent correction factors in the UrQMD model, but lacked a systematic extraction over a wide beam energy range. It also builds on the phenomenological formula of Cai et al. (1998) and the INDRA Collaboration results of Lopez et al. (2014) that found \(F \approx 0.2\) at 0.035\(A\) GeV and 0.5 at 0.1\(A\) GeV from nuclear stopping. The extracted energy-dependent \(F\) enables a consistent description of both collective flows and nuclear stopping across SIS energies, and the authors provide a simple phenomenological formula that can be incorporated into other transport models for further validation.
