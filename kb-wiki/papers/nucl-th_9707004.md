# Nuclear Quadrupole Effects in Deeply Bound Pionic Atoms

> We have studied nuclear quadrupole deformation effects in deeply bound pionic atoms theoretically. We have evaluated the level shifts and widths of the hyperfine components using the first order perturbation theory and compared them with the effects of neutron skin. We conclude that the nuclear quadrupole deformation effects for deeply bound $1s$ and $2p$ states are very difficult to observe and that the effects could be observed for $3d$ states. We also conclude that the deformation effects are sensitive to the parameters of the pion-nucleus optical potential.

## Key claim

Nuclear quadrupole deformation effects in deeply bound pionic atoms are very difficult to observe for the 1s and 2p states, but could be observed for 3d states, provided that the neutron skin thickness (monopole nuclear density) is first determined from the 1s state and that experimental energy resolution of about 10 keV is achieved.

## Method

The authors used first-order perturbation theory to evaluate the level shifts and widths of hyperfine components of pionic 2p and 3d states in a deformed nucleus (175Lu). They solved the Klein-Gordon equation with a spherical nuclear density and the standard Ericson-Ericson optical potential, then treated the quadrupole deformation as a perturbation. The nuclear density was parametrized using a Woods-Saxon form with a quadrupole deformation parameter beta, and the neutron skin thickness was simulated by varying the neutron radius parameter Delta.

## Key numbers

- For 175Lu (beta = 0.29, Q = +3.49 b), the spherical binding energies and widths are: B.E.(2p) = 4084 keV, Gamma(2p) = 258 keV; B.E.(3d) = 2133 keV, Gamma(3d) = 29 keV.
- The 2p state splits into three levels with spacing of order 10 keV, which is one order of magnitude smaller than the 2p natural width of 258 keV.
- For the 1s state, the energy shift and width change due to neutron skin (Delta = 1.0 fm) are |Delta E| ~ 300 keV and |Delta Gamma| ~ 900 keV; the 1s state has no quadrupole effect within first-order perturbation.
- For the 2p state, sensitivities to neutron skin are |Delta E| ~ 200 keV and |Delta Gamma| ~ 10 keV; sensitivities to deformation are |Delta E| = 10-40 keV and |Delta Gamma| = 10-70 keV.
- For the 3d state, sensitivities to neutron skin are |Delta E| ~ 20 keV and |Delta Gamma| ~ 10 keV; sensitivities to deformation are |Delta E| = 0-20 keV and |Delta Gamma| <= 10 keV.
- With Konijn's potential, the 3d quadrupole shifts are 40-60% larger than with Seki's potential, and the signs of the width changes can differ between the two potentials.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quadrupole effects in shallow pionic atoms (Scheck 1972, Olaniyi 1982) to the newly discovered deeply bound pionic states (Yamazaki 1996). It also builds on predictions that deeply bound pionic atoms are sensitive to neutron skin (Toki 1990). The paper shows that for deformed unstable nuclei, where both neutron skin and deformation are expected, the 1s state can be used to determine neutron skin thickness first, and then the 3d state can be used to extract deformation information. The strong sensitivity of the quadrupole effects to the optical potential parameters (Seki 1983 vs. Konijn 1990) means that systematic observations could help determine the pion-nucleus optical potential more precisely.
