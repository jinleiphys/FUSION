# Charge radii, moments and masses of mercury isotopes across the N = 126 shell closure

> Combining laser spectroscopy in a Versatile Arc Discharge and Laser Ion Source, with Penning-trap mass spectrometry at the CERN-ISOLDE facility, this work reports on mean-square charge radii of neutron-rich mercury isotopes across the $N = 126$ shell closure, the electromagnetic moments of $^{207}$Hg and more precise mass values of $^{206-208}$Hg. The odd-even staggering (OES) of the mean square charge radii and the kink at $N = 126$ are analyzed within the framework of covariant density functional theory (CDFT), with comparisons between different functionals to investigate the dependence of the results on the underlying single-particle structure. The observed features are defined predominantly in the particle-hole channel in CDFT, since both are present in the calculations without pairing. However, the magnitude of the kink is still affected by the occupation of the $1i_{11/2}$ and $2g_{9/2}$ orbitals with a dependence on the relative energies as well as pairing.

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering (OES) of mean-square charge radii and the kink at N=126 in mercury isotopes are defined predominantly in the particle-hole channel of covariant density functional theory (CDFT), as both features appear in calculations without pairing; however, the magnitude of the kink is still affected by the occupation of the ν1i11/2 and ν2g9/2 orbitals with a dependence on their relative energies as well as pairing.

## Method

Laser spectroscopy in a Versatile Arc Discharge and Laser Ion Source (VADLIS) was combined with Penning-trap mass spectrometry at the CERN-ISOLDE facility to measure mean-square charge radii, electromagnetic moments, and masses of neutron-rich mercury isotopes. The odd-even staggering and kink at N=126 were analyzed within covariant density functional theory (CDFT) using multiple functionals (NL3*, DD-PC1, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ) to assess dependence on single-particle structure. Mass measurements employed time-of-flight ion-cyclotron-resonance (ToF-ICR) in a Penning trap and multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometry (MR-ToF MS).

## Key numbers

- Mean-square charge radius δ⟨r²⟩^{207,198} = 0.503(4){38} fm² for ²⁰⁷Hg
- Mean-square charge radius δ⟨r²⟩^{208,198} = 0.625(3){47} fm² for ²⁰⁸Hg
- Magnetic dipole moment μ(²⁰⁷Hg) = -1.373(20) μ_N
- Electric quadrupole moment Q_s(²⁰⁷Hg) = -0.73(37) b
- Mass excess of ²⁰⁶Hg = -20932.1 ± 8.2 keV/c² (first direct determination)
- Mass excess of ²⁰⁷Hg = -16446.2 ± 6.1 keV/c²
- Mass excess of ²⁰⁸Hg = -13279 ± 20 keV/c²
- g-factor of ²⁰⁷Hg = -0.305(6), compared to Schmidt value g_Schmidt = -0.425
- Factor of 2400 decrease in measured ion rate between ²⁰⁷Hg and ²⁰⁸Hg

## Context

This work extends the study of charge radii across the N=126 shell closure from the lead isotopic chain (Tajima 1993, Sharma 1993, Reinhard 1995) to mercury isotopes, following the initial report in Goodacre (2021). The results provide a broader benchmark for testing covariant density functional theory and challenge non-relativistic approaches such as Fayans functionals, where pairing becomes a dominant contributor to the kink and OES (Gorges 2019), in contradiction with the CDFT results presented here. The new mass measurements and electromagnetic moments enable improved tests of core polarization corrections and single-particle structure near the doubly magic ²⁰⁸Pb nucleus.
