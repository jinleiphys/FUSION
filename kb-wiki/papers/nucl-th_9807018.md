# Weak Decay of Lambda Hypernuclei

> We review recent developments concerning the weak decay of Lambda hypernuclei. New studies covering the mesonic decay channel as well as recent models for the non-mesonic one are discussed and compared with experimental data. The puzzle of the neutron- to proton-induced decay ratio, Gamma_n/Gamma_p, is addressed in connection to the two-nucleon induced decay channel and proposals for more efficient experimental analyses of this ratio are made.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent developments in the weak decay of Lambda hypernuclei, arguing that while the discrepancy between theoretical models and experimental data for the neutron-to-proton-induced decay ratio (Gamma_n/Gamma_p) remains a puzzle, the experimental uncertainties for this ratio are very large and the two-nucleon induced decay channel further enlarges the error bars in experimental analyses.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical models for mesonic decay (Lambda to pi N) using a propagator method with a local density approximation, incorporating the pion self-energy in nuclear matter through particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations. For non-mesonic decay (Lambda N to NN), they review models going beyond one-pion exchange, including heavier meson exchange, multimeson exchange, and quark degrees of freedom, and compare these with experimental data on total decay rates, partial rates, and the Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio.

## Key numbers

- The free Lambda decay width is derived from the weak coupling constant G_F mu^2 = 2.21 x 10^{-7}, with empirical constants A_pi = 1.05 and B_pi = -7.15
- The pion momentum from Lambda decay is approximately 100 MeV/c, which is smaller than the nuclear matter Fermi momentum of k_F = 270 MeV/c, causing Pauli blocking that suppresses mesonic decay in bulk nuclear matter
- The local density approximation predicts non-mesonic decay rates within 5% of finite nuclei results, while mesonic width results for medium hypernuclei around carbon-12 lie within 20-30% of the correct answer

## Context

This review extends earlier comprehensive reviews of hypernuclear physics by Gal (1977), Povh (1978), Bando et al. (1985), Dover et al. (1989), Oset et al. (1990), Cohen (1990), Gibson and Hungerford (1995), and Akaishi and Yamazaki (1997). The work addresses the long-standing puzzle of the Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio, where one-pion exchange models gave an order of magnitude smaller ratio than data, and discusses how the two-nucleon induced decay channel (Lambda NN to NNN) was proposed as a possible resolution but instead complicates experimental analyses. The review enables future experiments using proton spectra measurements and back-to-back nucleon coincidence detection to determine Gamma_n/Gamma_p with far more precision than previously possible.
