# Dense-matter equation of state at zero & finite temperature

> At high density, matter is expected to undergo a phase transition to deconfined quark matter. Although the density at which it happens and the strength of the transition are still largely unknown, we can model it to be in agreement with known experimental data and reliable theoretical results. We discuss how deconfinement in dense matter can be affected by both by temperature and by strong magnetic fields within the CMF model. To explore different dependencies in our approach, we also explore how deconfinement can be affected by the assumption of different degrees of freedom, different vector coupling terms, and different deconfining potentials, all at zero temperature. Both zero-net-strangeness and isospin-symmetric heavy-ion collision matter and beta-equilibrated charge-neutral matter in neutron stars are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the deconfinement phase transition to quark matter in dense matter is sensitive to temperature, magnetic field strength, and the specific choice of degrees of freedom and interaction terms within the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, but the results for magnetic field dependence do not agree with those from the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model.

## Method
The authors use the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a relativistic SU(3) sigma-model-based approach extended to include hadrons (nucleons, hyperons, and Delta baryons) and quarks, to compute equations of state for both neutron-star matter (beta-equilibrated, charge-neutral) and heavy-ion collision matter (zero-net-strangeness, isospin-symmetric). They vary the deconfining potential, vector coupling terms (including a higher-order omega^4 term and a mixed isoscalar-isovector omega-rho term), degrees of freedom (including spin-3/2 Delta baryons), temperature, and magnetic field strength (including anomalous magnetic moments for hadrons and leptons) to study how these factors affect the phase transition.

## Key numbers
- The phase transition for neutron-star matter at T=0 occurs at larger energy density epsilon for larger magnetic field strength B, with changes further enhanced by the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM).
- At finite temperature, a weaker phase transition takes place at lower baryon chemical potential mu_B for larger temperatures T.
- The phase transition for heavy-ion collision matter takes place at larger mu_B and is stronger than for neutron-star matter for any T and B in the CMF model.
- Additional interaction terms (omega-rho and omega^4) push the phase transition to higher densities and decrease the size of the jump in density associated with the phase transition.
- The omega-rho term has a softening effect at low density, while the omega^4 term has a stiffening effect at high density.
- The additional interaction terms allow for hybrid stars with masses greater than 2 solar masses, as suggested by observations (Fonseca 2021).
- The omega-rho term decreases tidal deformability and stellar radii, while the omega^4 term increases maximum mass.

## Context
This work extends the CMF model studies of Clevinger (2022) and Peterson (2023) by systematically exploring how different degrees of freedom, vector coupling terms, and deconfining potentials affect the phase transition at zero temperature, and by comparing finite-temperature and magnetic-field results with the PNJL model (Ferreira 2013, 2014; Costa 2015). The results enable future studies of these effects in binary neutron-star merger simulations, and the zero-temperature hybrid equations of state are made available on the CompOSE repository for astrophysical applications.
