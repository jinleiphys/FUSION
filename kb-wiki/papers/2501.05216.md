# Analysis of clustering fragments of $^7$Li and $^7$Be in the microscopic cluster model

> The nuclear structures of $^7$Li($α+n+n+p$) and $^7$Be($α+p+p+n$) are studied within the microscopic cluster model, in which the clustering fragments e.g., triton, $^3$He, and even the single nucleons around the core are studied in the $^7$Li and $^7$Be systems. We obtain the energy spectra and wave functions of $^7$Li and $^7$Be, and the calculated energy spectra of the ground states and some of the excited states are consistent with experimental data. To investigate the cluster-formation probabilities, we calculate the reduced-width amplitudes of various binary partitions. The results show that the $α+t(^3\mathrm{He})$ cluster structure is dominant in the low-lying states of $^7$Li($^7$Be), including the ground state and the three lowest excited states. In some higher states around the single-particle thresholds, the components of $\mathrm{core}+N$ structures, namely $^6\mathrm{Li}+n$ and $^6\mathrm{He}+p$ in $^7$Li and $^6\mathrm{Li}+p$ in $^7$Be, are significantly enhanced.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\alpha+t$ (or $\alpha+{}^3\mathrm{He}$) cluster structure dominates the low-lying states of $^7$Li (or $^7$Be), while $\mathrm{core}+N$ structures (e.g., $^6\mathrm{Li}+n$, $^6\mathrm{He}+p$, $^6\mathrm{Li}+p$) become significantly enhanced in higher excited states near single-particle thresholds; however, the authors note that some higher-energy states may contain large components of non-physical continuum states, and only those states that remain stable after applying the radius constraint method are considered physical.

## Method

The authors performed four-body generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations for $^7$Li ($\alpha+n+n+p$) and $^7$Be ($\alpha+p+p+n$), using the Volkov No.2 central N-N potential and the G3RS spin-orbit potential with an oscillator parameter $b=1.46~\mathrm{fm}$. They solved the Hill-Wheeler equation for the wave functions, applied the radius constraint method (RCM) with a cutoff of $5~\mathrm{fm}$ to remove continuum contamination, and calculated reduced-width amplitudes (RWA) and spectroscopic factors for various binary cluster partitions to quantify cluster-formation probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator parameter: $b = 1.46~\mathrm{fm}$
- Radius cutoff parameter for RCM: $R_{\mathrm{cut}} = 5~\mathrm{fm}$
- Calculated r.m.s. charge radius of $^7$Li: $2.43~\mathrm{fm}$ (experimental value: $2.42~\mathrm{fm}$ from Sanchez (2006))
- Calculated r.m.s. charge radius of $^7$Be: $2.63~\mathrm{fm}$
- $B(E2;3/2^- \to 1/2^-)$ for $^7$Li: $8.91~e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (experimental: $8.3~e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ from Tilley (2002))
- $B(E2;3/2^- \to 1/2^-)$ for $^7$Be: $26.2~e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (experimental: $26~e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ from Henderson (2019))
- Spectroscopic factor for $\alpha+t$ in $^7$Li ground state: $1.03$ (RGM result from Descouvemont (2022): $1.10$)
- Spectroscopic factor for $\alpha+t$ in $^7$Li $1/2^-_1$ state: $1.02$ (RGM: $1.05$)
- Spectroscopic factor for $^6\mathrm{He}+p$ in $^7$Li ground state: $0.509$ (RGM: $0.41$)
- Spectroscopic factor for $[^6\mathrm{Li}(1^+)\otimes n]_{1/2}\otimes1$ in $^7$Li ground state: $0.657$ (RGM: $0.53$; ab initio from Timofeyuk (2009): $0.68$)

## Context

This work extends prior microscopic cluster model studies of $^7$Li and $^7$Be, including the resonant group method (RGM) calculations by Descouvemont (2022) and no-core shell model (NCSM) predictions by Vorabbi (2019) regarding a near-threshold $^6\mathrm{He}+p$ resonance in $^7$Li. The authors find no such $1/2^+$ state near the $^6\mathrm{He}+p$ threshold, contrary to the NCSM prediction, but confirm the absence of a $^6\mathrm{Li}+p$ resonance near threshold in $^7$Be. The results enable future studies of three- and four-body cluster configurations (deuteron, dineutron, diproton) in these nuclei.
