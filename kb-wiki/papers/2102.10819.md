# On nonadditive anisotropic relativistic hydrodynamics

> Non-additive generalisation of relativistic anisotropic anisotropic hydrodynamics is described. In the particular case of 0+1 boost-invariant hydrodynamics additional entropy production due to non-additivity is calculated.

# On nonadditive anisotropic relativistic hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Non-additivity (via Tsallis statistics with parameter q) introduces an additional entropy production term in 0+1 boost-invariant anisotropic hydrodynamics, on top of the entropy production already known from momentum anisotropy alone; the result is conditional on the specific choice of generalized entropy current from Biro (2011, 2012) and the Romatschke-Strikland form of the anisotropic distribution.

## Method

The author constructs a non-additive generalization of relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics by starting from a non-additive Boltzmann kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation, using a Tsallis-type equilibrium distribution and a Romatschke-Strikland anisotropic distribution function. The first two moments of the Boltzmann equation (for particle current and energy-momentum tensor) are taken, with Landau matching condition for energy conservation, to derive evolution equations for the anisotropy parameter ξ and the momentum scale Λ in 0+1 boost-invariant geometry. Entropy production is then separated into an additive part Δ₀ and a non-additive part (q−1)Δ_q.

## Key numbers

- The non-additive equilibrium distribution is of Tsallis form: f_eq(x,p) = [1 − (1−q) p²/Λ_eq²]^{1/(1−q)}, with the limit q → 1 recovering the usual Boltzmann distribution.
- The matching condition between Λ_eq and Λ is Λ_eq = ℛ(ξ)^{1/4} Λ, where ℛ(ξ) = (1/2)[1/(1+ξ) + arctan(√ξ)/√ξ].
- The additive entropy production term is Δ₀(τ) = (1/τ_eq)[ℛ^{3/4}(ξ)√(1+ξ) − 1] S.
- The non-additive entropy production term is Δ_q(τ) = √(1+ξ) (1/τ_eq) ℛ'(ξ) 𝒢(ξ) ∫ dw w^{1/2} Ln_q f_iso(w).

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework of Martinez (2010, 2009) and the non-additive hydrodynamics of Biro (2011, 2012) by combining both momentum anisotropy and non-additivity in a single formalism. The non-additive generalization of statistical mechanics originates from Tsallis (2009) and has been applied to particle production in heavy ion collisions by Cleymans (2013, 2017) and others. The result enables future studies of entropy production in strongly interacting anisotropic matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, where both glasma-induced momentum anisotropy (Lappi, 2006) and non-additive effects from strong interactions may be relevant.
