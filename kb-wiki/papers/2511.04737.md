# Effective Field Theories for Neutron Stars Physics

> There is an increasing interest in the community for the Neutron Stars and what we can learn from them. In this review we show how chiral effective field theory, combined with many-body methods, can provide important results that connect Neutron Star properties at zero temperature to nuclear physics and allows to use these compact objects as laboratories of new physics.

## Key claim

This review argues that chiral effective field theory (chiEFT), when combined with many-body methods, can provide controlled, systematically improvable calculations of the neutron star equation of state at zero temperature, but the approach is limited to densities around nuclear saturation (n_s ~ 0.16 fm^-3) or up to about 2n_s, and requires careful handling of power-counting violations, regulator artifacts, and the choice of whether to include the Delta(1232) resonance explicitly.

## Method

The authors survey how chiEFT-derived nuclear potentials (two-body, three-body, and four-body forces) are combined with four many-body frameworks: Many-Body Perturbation Theory (MBPT), Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) method, Quantum Monte Carlo (VMC and GFMC), and In-medium Chiral Effective Field Theory. Each method starts from the same chiral Lagrangian respecting QCD symmetries, derives the NN potential (e.g., the leading-order potential V_LO containing contact terms and one-pion exchange), and then computes the energy per particle in pure neutron matter (PNM) and symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) to obtain the equation of state. The review also discusses matching EFT results to crustal equations of state and applying them to study dark matter effects on neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_s ~ 0.16 fm^-3
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi ~ 1 GeV, providing the expected convergence scale for the perturbative expansion
- The breakdown scale for NN interactions in vacuum is estimated at Lambda_NN ~ 600 MeV
- The nucleon mass m_N appears in the LS equation denominator as 2*sqrt(p'^2 + m_N^2) - 2*sqrt(k^2 + m_N^2) + i0^+
- The leading-order NN potential includes contact terms C_S and C_T and one-pion exchange with coupling g_A^2/(4 f_pi^2)

## Context

This review extends the line of work connecting chiral effective field theory (first developed for single nucleons by Gasser (1987) and extended to few-nucleon interactions by Weinberg (1990)) to neutron star physics. It contrasts chiEFT approaches with phenomenological methods such as Skyrme-type effective interactions and mean-field models based on meson exchange (Serot, 1992), as well as with other first-principles methods like variational methods (Akmal, 1998) and nuclear lattice EFT (Ren, 2023). The review enables future work using EFT-based equations of state to compute mass-radius diagrams, tidal deformability, gravitational wave signals from binary mergers, and constraints on dark matter particle mass and capture rates in neutron stars.
