# Improved Kelson-Garvey mass relations for proton-rich nuclei

> The improved Kelson-Garvey (ImKG) mass relations are proposed from the mass differences of mirror nuclei. The masses of 31 measured proton-rich nuclei with $7\leq A\leq41$ and $-5\leq (N-Z)\leq-3$ can be remarkably well reproduced by using the proposed relations, with a root-mean-square deviation of 0.398 MeV, which is much smaller than the results of Kelson-Garvey (0.502 MeV) and Isobar-Mirror mass relations (0.647 MeV). This is because many more masses of participating nuclei are involved in the ImKG mass relations for predicting the masses of unknown proton-rich nuclei. The masses for 144 unknown proton-rich nuclei with $6\leq A\leq74$ are predicted by using the ImKG mass relations. The one- and two-proton separation energies for these proton-rich nuclei and the diproton emission are investigated simultaneously.

# Improved Kelson-Garvey mass relations for proton-rich nuclei

## Key claim

The improved Kelson-Garvey (ImKG) mass relations reproduce the binding energies of 31 measured proton-rich nuclei with a root-mean-square deviation of 0.398 MeV, which is smaller than the traditional Kelson-Garvey (0.502 MeV) and Isobar-Mirror (0.647 MeV) relations, but this improvement is conditional on the inclusion of many more participating nuclei in the prediction (Y(Y+1)-1 nuclei versus 2Y+1 for the KG relation).

## Method

The authors derive improved Kelson-Garvey mass relations by summing multiple expressions for the binding energy difference between mirror nuclei, incorporating both ordinary mirror nuclei near the N=Z line and higher-order mirror nuclei far from the beta-stability line. The general relation (Eq. 9) expresses the binding energy difference B(A,-Y)-B(A,Y) as a weighted sum of differences for mirror nuclei with smaller isotopic numbers Y-i, with coefficients following powers of 2. The method is tested against 31 known proton-rich nuclei with mass numbers 7 to 41 and isotopic numbers -5 to -3, using experimental masses from the AME2011 database, and then applied to predict masses for 144 unknown proton-rich nuclei with mass numbers 5 to 74.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation of ImKG predictions for 31 measured proton-rich nuclei: 0.398 MeV, compared to 0.502 MeV for KG and 0.647 MeV for Isobar-Mirror relations
- Mass predictions generated for 144 unknown proton-rich nuclei with mass numbers 5 to 74
- 14 proton-rich nuclei beyond the proton drip-line are observed in experiment: Li-4,5; B-7,9; N-10,11; O-12; F-14,15,16; Na-18,19; Sc-39; As-65
- 10 candidate nuclei identified for diproton decay: Si-22, Ar-31, Ca-34, Ti-38,39, Cr-42, Fe-45, Ni-48,49, Zn-55
- 13 candidate nuclei listed for one-proton emissions: N-11, F-15, Na-18,19, Al-21, P-24,25,26, Cl-30, K-33,34, Sc-38,39, V-41,42, Mn-44,45, Co-48,49, Cu-53,54,55

## Context

This work extends the original Kelson-Garvey mass relations (Kelson and Garvey, 1966, 1988) and the Isobar-Mirror mass relations (Ormand, 1989, 1997) by incorporating mirror nuclei far from the beta-stability line in addition to those near N=Z. The ImKG approach differs from the extended Garvey-Kelson (GK12) relations (Barea, 2007), which predict the mass of a central nucleus using 20 neighboring nuclei, whereas the ImKG method predicts the most proton-rich nuclei using their neutron-rich analogs and participating nuclei in between. The predicted masses and separation energies enable investigation of the rp-process and nu-p process in nuclear astrophysics, and identify candidates for one-proton and diproton emission studies.
