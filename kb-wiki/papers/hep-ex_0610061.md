# Low-x QCD physics from RHIC and HERA to the LHC

> We present a summary of the physics of gluon saturation and non-linear QCD evolution at small values of parton momentum fraction $x$ in the proton and nucleus in the context of recent experimental results at HERA and RHIC. The rich physics potential of low-x studies at the LHC, especially in the forward region, is discussed and some benchmark measurements in pp, pA and AA collisions are introduced.

## Key claim

The paper argues that experimental results from HERA and RHIC are consistent with gluon saturation and non-linear QCD evolution at small x, but notes that at both facilities the saturation scale lies in the transition region between soft and hard regimes (Q_s^2 ~ 0.5-2 GeV^2), meaning non-perturbative effects can obscure a clearcut interpretation based solely on partonic degrees of freedom.

## Method

The paper reviews inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) data from HERA and hadron production measurements from RHIC, comparing them to predictions from DGLAP, BFKL, and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) frameworks. It then surveys the forward detection capabilities of the four LHC experiments (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) and identifies four benchmark measurements (forward dijets, forward heavy-quarks, quarkonium photoproduction in ultra-peripheral AA collisions, and forward Drell-Yan pairs) that can probe parton densities down to x ~ 10^-6 at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- HERA F_2(x,Q^2) growth described by F_2 ~ x^{-lambda(Q^2)} with lambda ~ 0.1-0.3 logarithmically rising with Q^2
- Saturation momentum Q_s^2(x) ~ A^{1/3} x^{-lambda} with lambda ~ 0.25, giving Q_s^2 ~ 2 GeV^2 for Au at RHIC midrapidity and Q_s^2 ~ 5-10 GeV^2 for Pb at LHC
- Central AuAu multiplicity at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: dN_ch/deta ~ 700, lower than minijet-dominated expectations of ~1000
- Nuclear modification factor R_dAu ~ 0.8 for hadrons at p_T ~ 2-4 GeV/c and eta = 3.2 in dAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- LHC collision energies: pp at 14 TeV, pA at 8.8 TeV, AA at 5.5 TeV
- Forward jet production in pp at 14 TeV probes x_2 ~ 10^{-4} to 10^{-5} for jets with p_T ~ 20-200 GeV/c in 3<|eta|<5
- Mueller-Navelet dijet cross section for two jets separated by Delta_eta ~ 9 expected to be suppressed by factor ~2 compared to BFKL predictions
- Maximum photon-nucleus c.m. energy W_gA ~ 1 TeV in ultra-peripheral PbPb collisions at LHC
- x values probed in gamma A -> J/psi A at y=2 can be as low as x ~ 10^{-5}

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of gluon saturation from HERA ep results (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1999) and RHIC heavy-ion results (Kharzeev and Levin, 2000; Armesto, 2004) to the LHC energy regime. It builds on the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory framework developed over the previous fifteen years. The work enables a concrete experimental programme for low-x QCD studies at the LHC, particularly using forward detectors, and notes that current nuclear parton distribution functions at x < 0.01 are essentially unknown, with different parametrizations showing differences as large as a factor of three at x ~ 10^{-4} for Pb over proton gluon ratios.
