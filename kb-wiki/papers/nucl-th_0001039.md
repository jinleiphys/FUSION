# Symmetry Breaking by Proton-Neutron Pairing

> The symmetries of the $t=1$ and $t=0$ pair-fields are different. The consequences for rotational spectra are discussed. For $t=1$, the concept of spontaneous breaking and subsequent restoration of the isospin symmetry turns out to be important. It permits us to describe the proton-neutron pair-correlation within the conventional frame of pairing between like particles. The experimental data are consistent with the presence of a $t=1$ field at low spin in $N\approx Z$ nuclei. For a substantial $t=0$ field, the spectra of even-even and odd-odd $N\approx Z$ nuclei become similar. The possibility of a rotationally induced $ J=1$ pair-field at high spin is considered.

# Symmetry Breaking by Proton-Neutron Pairing

## Key claim

The paper argues that isovector (t=1) proton-neutron pairing can be understood within the conventional framework of like-particle pairing through spontaneous breaking and restoration of isospin symmetry, while a substantial isoscalar (t=0) pair field would cause the spectra of even-even and odd-odd N≈Z nuclei to become similar. The authors present this as consistent with experimental data at low spin, but hedge that the t=0 field possibility and rotationally induced J=1 pair-field at high spin remain speculative.

## Method

The authors analyze the symmetry properties of isovector (t=1) and isoscalar (t=0) pair fields in atomic nuclei, examining how each type of pairing affects rotational spectra. They develop a theoretical framework where spontaneous breaking of isospin symmetry for t=1 pairing allows description of proton-neutron correlations using methods analogous to those for like-particle pairing. The analysis compares predictions with experimental data from N≈Z nuclei.

This work extends the theoretical understanding of proton-neutron pairing in N≈Z nuclei, building on prior studies of like-particle pairing and isospin symmetry in nuclear structure.
