# Radiative corrections in nucleon time-like form factors measurements

> The completely general radiative corrections to lowest order, including the final and initial state radiations, are studied in proton-antiproton annihilation to an electron-positron pair. Numerical estimates have been made in a realistic configuration of the PANDA detector at FAIR for the proton time-like form factors measurements.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete lowest-order QED radiative correction calculation for the process \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow e^+e^-\), including both initial-state and final-state radiation, but the authors note that the proton vertex and box diagram contributions are evaluated in the point-like approximation and that this approximation is adequate because those contributions are small compared to the total correction.

## Method

The authors computed the full set of first-order QED radiative corrections (virtual corrections, soft photon bremsstrahlung, and hard photon emission) for the annihilation process \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow e^+e^-\). The soft and virtual corrections were separated from the hard photon contribution using an infrared cutoff parameter \(\omega\), and the hard photon contribution from \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow e^+e^-\gamma\) was evaluated using two different phenomenological models for the proton electromagnetic form factors (a fit to BaBar data and the Iachello-Wan model). The calculations were performed in the center-of-mass frame for kinematics relevant to the PANDA detector at FAIR.

## Key numbers

* At \(s = 5.4\) GeV\(^2\) with \(\omega = 12\) MeV (\(\omega/E_{e^+} \approx 1\%\)), the total soft+virtual correction \(\delta^{ep}_{SV}\) ranges from \(-0.3108\) at \(\theta_{e^+}=30^\circ\) to \(-0.1713\) at \(\theta_{e^+}=150^\circ\).
* At \(s = 12.9\) GeV\(^2\) with \(\omega = 18\) MeV (\(\omega/E_{e^+} \approx 1\%\)), the total soft+virtual correction \(\delta^{ep}_{SV}\) ranges from \(-0.3965\) at \(\theta_{e^+}=30^\circ\) to \(-0.1627\) at \(\theta_{e^+}=150^\circ\).
* The box diagram (two-photon exchange) contribution is less than 1% at both energies.
* The vacuum polarization contribution \(\delta_{vac}\) is 0.0305 at \(s=5.4\) GeV\(^2\) and 0.0332 at \(s=12.9\) GeV\(^2\).
* The BaBar model (model 1) uses parameters \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 0.3\) GeV and \(A = 98\) GeV\(^4\) for the magnetic form factor modulus.

## Context

This work extends the radiative correction calculations for \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow e^+e^-\) beyond earlier treatments such as that of reference [18], which assumed a point-like proton and omitted hard photon contributions, and reference [19], which omitted initial-state radiation. The calculations are motivated by the planned PANDA experiment at FAIR, where full simulations indicate that cross section precisions of 3%-5% can be achieved. The paper provides the necessary radiative correction framework for extracting the proton time-like electromagnetic form factors from angular distribution measurements, incorporating realistic form factor models rather than the point-like approximation used in prior work.
