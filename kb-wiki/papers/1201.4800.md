# Elliptic flow at varying energy heavy ion collisions: Partonic vs hadronic dynamics

> We examine whether the breakdown in elliptic flow quark number scaling observed at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energy scan is related to the turning off of deconfinement by testing the hypothesis that hydrodynamics and parton coalescence always apply, but are obscured, at lower energies, by variations in the widths of quark and anti-quark rapidity distribution. We find that this effect is enough to spoil quark number scaling in elliptic flow. A lack of scaling in data therefore does not signal the absence of partonic degrees of freedom and hadronization by coalescence. In a coalescing partonic fluid, however, elliptic flow of anti-baryons should be the greater than that of baryons, since antibaryons contain a greater admixture of partons from the highly flowing midrapidity region. Intriguingly, purely hadronic dynamics has a similar dependence of baryon-anti-baryons $v_2$ as purely partonic dynamics, again because antibaryons tend to come from regions where the deviation of the system from hydrodynamic behavior is at its smallest. The opposite trend observed in experiment is therefore an indication that we might be misunderstanding the origin of $v_2$. We finish by discussing possible explanations of this, and suggest experimental measurements capable of clarifying the situation.

## Key claim

The breakdown of quark number scaling in elliptic flow at lower RHIC energies does not necessarily signal the absence of partonic degrees of freedom; it can be explained by variations in the widths of quark and anti-quark rapidity distributions even if hydrodynamics and parton coalescence always apply. However, both partonic and hadronic dynamics predict that anti-baryon elliptic flow should be greater than baryon elliptic flow, which contradicts experimental observations and suggests a misunderstanding of the origin of elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors constructed a simplified model assuming all elliptic flow is produced in a close-to-ideal hydrodynamic stage at all energies, with quarks and antiquarks having the same local flow but different rapidity distributions (flat for quarks, Gaussian for antiquarks). They implemented coalescence hadronization using a finite-width Wigner function in three dimensions, with parameters tuned to RHIC data, and computed the elliptic flow of mesons and baryons as a function of transverse momentum. For comparison, they also performed purely hadronic molecular dynamics calculations using the UrQMD model (version 2.3) at beam energies of 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For a Gaussian width of antiquark rapidity distribution σ_y = 0.25, the proton v_2/n_q is below the meson one by nearly 50% while the antiproton v_2/n_q is above it.
- The Wigner function parameters used are Δ_p^M = 0.23 GeV for mesons and Δ_p^B = 0.35 GeV for baryons.
- The freeze-out temperature for the parton distribution is T = 170 MeV.
- The transverse momentum range studied for hadron v_2 is 0 to 5 GeV.
- UrQMD calculations were performed at center-of-mass energies of 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV for 0-80% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow scaling from top RHIC energy (200 GeV per nucleon) down to lower energies in the RHIC beam energy scan, where a systematic breakdown of coalescence scaling had been observed and interpreted as signaling the turning off of partonic degrees of freedom (scanv2, scanv2paper). The authors challenge this interpretation, following the caution raised by Soren (2009), and show that rapidity-dependent effects can obscure partonic signatures. The paper enables future experimental measurements of baryon versus anti-baryon elliptic flow as a function of strangeness content to distinguish between competing explanations, including transverse hydrodynamic gradients (Steinheimer, 2012) and vector mean field effects (Plumari, 2011; Bratovic, 2012).
