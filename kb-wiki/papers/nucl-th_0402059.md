# A new look at nuclear supersymmetry though transfer experiments

> Nuclear supersymmetry is reviewed and some of its applications and extensions are discussed, together with a proposal for new, more stringent and precise tests to probe the supersymmetry classification, in particular, correlations between nuclei that belong to the same supermultiplet. The combination of these theoretical and experimental studies may play a unifying role in nuclear phenomena

## Key claim

Nuclear supersymmetry (n-SUSY) predicts explicit correlations between one-proton transfer reaction intensities for different pairs of nuclei within the same supermultiplet, specifically that the ratio of intensities for exciting the ground state versus an excited state is identical for the reactions \({}^{194}\)Pt \(\rightarrow\) \({}^{195}\)Au and \({}^{195}\)Pt \(\rightarrow\) \({}^{196}\)Au, but this prediction has not yet been fully verified experimentally.

## Method

The authors derived closed-form expressions for one-proton transfer spectroscopic factors within the \(U(6/12)_{\nu}\otimes U(6/4)_{\pi}\) dynamical supersymmetry framework, using tensor operators under the subgroup chains \(Spin(6)\supset Spin(5)\supset Spin(3)\). They calculated the intensities for allowed transitions from the ground states of \({}^{194}\)Pt and \({}^{195}\)Pt to states in \({}^{195}\)Au and \({}^{196}\)Au, respectively, and established a correlation relation between the two reactions. The formalism was applied to the quartet of nuclei \({}^{194}\)Pt, \({}^{195}\)Au, \({}^{195}\)Pt, and \({}^{196}\)Au, which are the best empirical examples of n-SUSY.

## Key numbers

* For the one-proton transfer reaction \({}^{194}\)Pt \(\rightarrow\) \({}^{195}\)Au, the ground state of \({}^{195}\)Au is excited with spectroscopic factor \(C^{2}S = 0.175\), while the first excited \(J=3/2\) state has \(C^{2}S = 0.019\) (from Munger and collaborators).
* The ratio of intensities for the excited state to the ground state in the one-proton transfer reactions is predicted to be \(R_2 = 1.12\) for the special tensor operator \(P_2^{\dagger}\), using the boson number \(N=5\) for \({}^{196}\)Au.
* The correlation between the two one-proton transfer reactions is given by \(I(^{195}\text{Pt} \rightarrow ^{196}\text{Au}) = \frac{2L+1}{4} \, I(^{194}\text{Pt} \rightarrow ^{195}\text{Au})\), where \(L\) is the angular momentum of the final state in \({}^{196}\)Au.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical supersymmetry classification originally proposed by Iachello (1980) for the Os-Ir-Pt-Au mass region, building on prior studies of the quartet \({}^{194}\)Pt, \({}^{195}\)Au, \({}^{195}\)Pt, and \({}^{196}\)Au by Bijker, Frank, and collaborators. The paper provides specific, testable predictions for transfer reaction correlations that go beyond the existing spectroscopic data from Munger and collaborators and Sheline and collaborators, and it suggests that future experiments using \((^{3}\text{He},d)\) reactions on \({}^{194}\)Pt and \({}^{195}\)Pt targets (proposed by Graw and collaborators) can directly verify the supersymmetry classification. The results also open the possibility of extending n-SUSY to transitional regions of the nuclear chart, as previously explored for Ru and Rh isotopes by Frank and collaborators.
