# Effective field theory of interactions on the lattice

> We consider renormalization of effective field theory interactions by discretizing the continuum on a tight-binding lattice. After studying the one-dimensional problem, we address s-wave collisions in three dimensions and relate the bare lattice coupling constants to the continuum coupling constants. Our method constitutes a very simple avenue for the systematic renormalization in effective field theory, and is especially useful as the number of interaction parameters increases.

# Effective field theory of interactions on the lattice

## Key claim
The paper claims that discretizing the continuum Schrödinger equation on a tight-binding lattice provides a simpler route to renormalizing two-body effective field theory (EFT) interactions than solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, but this simplification is demonstrated only for s-wave and p-wave interactions up to order k^2, and the method's extension to higher orders or higher partial waves is left as an open question.

## Method
The authors discretize the two-body Schrödinger equation on a one-dimensional tight-binding lattice with spacing d, using a cosine dispersion relation and a nearest-neighbor plus next-nearest-neighbor interaction potential. They compute the two-body phase shift analytically from the lattice Hamiltonian, expand it to second order in kd, and match it to the effective range expansion to obtain relations between bare lattice coupling constants (U, V, W) and continuum EFT parameters (g0, g2, g11). For the three-dimensional s-wave case, they discretize the reduced radial Schrödinger equation and map it onto the one-dimensional fermionic problem, then relate the lattice spacing d to the momentum cutoff Λ via comparison with standard cutoff renormalization.

## Key numbers
- In 1D s-wave renormalization, the nearest-neighbor coupling v = V/J scales as v ~ 1/d near the continuum limit, with explicit expression v = 4β/(d - d²α) where α = 1/a and β = -r.
- The 1D s-wave continuum coupling constant g0 scales as g0 ~ d⁻² + O(d⁻¹), and g2 ~ 1 (dimensionless).
- For 1D p-wave interactions, the bare coupling constant g11 = -2ħ²d/[m(1 - dα)], which vanishes linearly as d → 0.
- In 3D, the relation between lattice spacing and cutoff is d = π/(2Λ), obtained by comparing g0 = (2πħ²d/m) × 2/(d/a - 1) with the standard cutoff-renormalized expression g0 = (4πħ²a/m) × 1/(1 - 2aΛ/π).

## Context
This work extends the lattice regularization approach to EFT interactions, building on the foundational lattice methods of Lüscher (1986) and the continuum EFT renormalization work of Cohen (1999), Frederico (2000), and Braaten (2000). The authors note that prior renormalization via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation involves "frustratingly tedious algebra," which their lattice discretization avoids. The method enables systematic inclusion of effective range corrections in one-dimensional many-body calculations using techniques like density matrix renormalization group, and provides a straightforward path to obtaining EFT coupling constants in three dimensions that could be extended to higher-order interactions and higher partial waves.
