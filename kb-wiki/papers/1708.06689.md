# Thermodynamic and transport properties in Au + Au collisions at RHIC energies from the clustering of color strings

> In this work, we have extracted the initial tempearture from the transverse momentum spectra of charged particles in $Au+Au$ collisions using STAR data at RHIC energies from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 to 200 GeV. The initial energy density ($\varepsilon$), shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($η/s$), trace anomaly ($Δ$), the squared speed of sound ($C_s^2$), entropy density, and bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio ($ζ/s$) are obtained and compared with the lattice QCD calculations for (2+1) flavor. The initial temperatures obtained are compared with various hadronization and chemical freeze-out temperatures. The analysis of the data shows that the deconfinement to confinement transition possibly takes place between $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5 and 19.6 GeV.

## Key claim

The analysis of STAR data using the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) suggests that the deconfinement-to-confinement transition in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies possibly takes place between \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 11.5 and 19.6 GeV, based on the percolation density parameter \(\xi\) crossing the critical threshold \(\xi_c \sim 1.2\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 19.6 GeV and above.

## Method

The authors extracted the percolation density parameter \(\xi\) by fitting the transverse momentum spectra of charged particles from STAR data for 0-10% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 to 200 GeV, using a parameterization derived from pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s}\) = 200 GeV. From \(\xi\) they computed the color suppression factor \(F(\xi)\) and then derived the initial temperature, energy density, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (\(\eta/s\)), squared speed of sound (\(C_s^2\)), entropy density, trace anomaly (\(\Delta\)), and bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio (\(\zeta/s\)) using CSPM relations combined with boost-invariant Bjorken hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- Percolation density parameter \(\xi\) ranges from \(0.75 \pm 0.09\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 GeV to \(2.65 \pm 0.14\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV; the critical threshold is \(\xi_c \sim 1.2\).
- Initial temperature \(T\) ranges from \(159.96 \pm 3.82\) MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 GeV to \(190.32 \pm 3.50\) MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV.
- Scaled energy density \(\varepsilon/T^4\) ranges from \(6.90 \pm 1.16\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 GeV to \(12.19 \pm 1.23\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV.
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) ranges from \(0.31 \pm 0.03\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 GeV to \(0.207 \pm 0.004\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV; the AdS/CFT lower bound is \(1/4\pi\).
- Squared speed of sound \(C_s^2\) ranges from \(0.09 \pm 0.04\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 GeV to \(0.24 \pm 0.01\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV; the ideal gas limit is \(1/3\).
- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\zeta/s\) ranges from \(0.26 \pm 0.09\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 GeV to \(0.028 \pm 0.006\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV.
- Trace anomaly \(\Delta\) ranges from \(3.25 \pm 0.30\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 GeV to \(4.82 \pm 0.10\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV.
- Scaled entropy density \(s/T^3\) ranges from \(7.56 \pm 1.30\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7 GeV to \(15.08 \pm 1.53\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the CSPM approach previously applied to pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s}\) = 1.8 TeV by Gutay et al. (2015) and to earlier RHIC analyses, and compares its thermodynamic and transport results with lattice QCD calculations from the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2009) and the Wuppertal collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2010). The results enable a systematic mapping of the QCD phase diagram across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, with the finding that the percolation threshold and the minimum in \(\eta/s\) both point to a crossover transition between 11.5 and 19.6 GeV, consistent with STAR measurements of net-proton fluctuations (net-proton collaboration) and transverse momentum correlations (ptfluc collaboration).
