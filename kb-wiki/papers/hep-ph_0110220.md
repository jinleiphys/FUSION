# Nonequilibrium Phenomena in Quantum Field Theory: From Linear Response to Dynamical Renormalization Group

> This thesis is devoted to studying aspects of real-time nonequilibrium dynamics in quantum field theory by implementing an initial value formulation of quantum field theory. The main focus is on the linear relaxation of mean fields and quantum kinetics in nonequilibrium multiparticle quantum systems with potential applications to ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, cosmological phase transitions and condensed matter systems. We first study the damping of fermion mean fields in a fermion-scalar plasma with a view towards understanding baryon transport phenomena during electroweak baryogenesis. Secondly, we apply and extend the renormalization group method to study nonequilibrium dynamics with the goals of constructing a quantum kinetic description that goes beyond usual Boltzmann kinetics and understanding anomalous relaxation associated with infrared phenomena. The final part of this thesis presents a real-time kinetic analysis of direct photon production from a quark-gluon plasma created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We show that the direct photon yield is significantly enhanced by the lowest order energy-nonconserving processes originated in the transient lifetime of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The thesis develops a first-principles real-time framework for nonequilibrium quantum field theory, showing that the dynamical renormalization group (DRG) method provides a quantum kinetic description that goes beyond semiclassical Boltzmann kinetics by consistently including medium effects, off-shell (energy-nonconserving) processes, and infrared threshold divergences. The claim that direct photon yield is "significantly enhanced" by transient QGP lifetime is hedged by the qualifier that this enhancement arises specifically from lowest-order energy-nonconserving processes originating in the transient lifetime of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The author implements an initial value formulation of quantum field theory using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism to obtain fully renormalized, retarded, and causal equations of motion for nonequilibrium expectation values. The dynamical renormalization group method is applied to self-interacting scalar theory, the O(4) linear sigma model, and hot QED plasma, where perturbative expansions are carried out to short times and secular terms are absorbed into renormalized amplitudes and phases via RG equations that describe slow-motion dynamics. A real-time kinetic analysis of direct photon production from a quark-gluon plasma is performed using this framework.

## Context

This thesis extends the initial value formulation pioneered by Schwinger (1961) for nonequilibrium quantum systems and applies the renormalization group method (traditionally confined to static/equilibrium properties, as in the work of Goldenfeld and Kunihiro) to real-time nonequilibrium dynamics. It provides a field-theoretical alternative to phenomenological transport models such as those by Wang, Geiger, and the UrQMD group, as well as hydrodynamical models, which rely on semiclassical assumptions. The work enables first-principles treatment of nonequilibrium signatures in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions (particularly at RHIC and LHC), cosmological phase transitions (electroweak baryogenesis, inflation), and condensed matter systems (Bose-Einstein condensation, ultrafast semiconductor spectroscopy), where Boltzmann kinetics fails to capture quantum coherence and off-shell effects.
