# Towards a direct lattice calculation of m_d - m_u

> We describe an independent method for determining the strong-isospin breaking mass parameter, 2delta = m_d - m_u, which utilizes the baryon spectrum. We use a prudent partially quenched choice of splitting the valence quark masses symmetrically about the light sea quark mass. This choice has the consequence of mitigating the most severe partial quenching artifacts. We also discuss the most significant hurdle to this method which is determining the electromagnetic self-energy of the neutron-proton mass splitting, a challenge which lacks a satisfactory answer. Despite these issues, the phenomenologically interesting dependence of m_n - m_p on delta can be determined.

# Towards a direct lattice calculation of m_d - m_u

## Key claim
The paper presents an independent method for determining the strong-isospin breaking mass parameter 2δ = m_d - m_u using the baryon spectrum, but the method's success depends on resolving the unresolved issue of the electromagnetic self-energy subtraction constant for the nucleon, which the authors acknowledge "lacks a satisfactory answer."

## Method
The authors use partially quenched lattice QCD with a prudent choice of splitting valence quark masses symmetrically about the degenerate sea quark mass (m_u^valence = m̂ - δ, m_d^valence = m̂ + δ), which mitigates partial quenching artifacts. They compute cascade baryon (Ξ) mass splittings on tree-level clover improved anisotropic lattices generated by the Spectrum Collaboration, using the EigCG deflation algorithm for high statistics, and extrapolate results using an NNLO heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory formula including finite volume corrections.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a_t ≃ 0.0324 fm (a_s ≃ 0.113 fm), set using the Ω baryon mass
- Preliminary lattice determination: Z_m^{latt} δ_phys = 0.000216(15)(sys.)(13) [lattice units]
- Predicted strong contribution to neutron-proton mass splitting: m_n - m_p = 3.40 ± 0.20 ± sys. ± 0.74 MeV
- Ξ mass splittings at various ensembles range from 4.5(1) MeV to 46.8(1.7) MeV depending on δ value and pion mass
- Pion masses studied: 244 MeV, 420 MeV, and 490 MeV; kaon masses: 506 MeV, 592 MeV, and 630 MeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of Gasser and Leutwyler (1982) who provided a comprehensive determination of quark mass parameters using the baryon spectrum but acknowledged the need for a subtracted dispersion relation for electromagnetic self-energies without computing it. The method provides an independent determination of m_d - m_u complementary to meson-based approaches, and the predicted strong contribution to m_n - m_p is comparable to values from Beane et al. (2006) and Blum et al. (2010). If the electromagnetic self-energy subtraction constant can be determined, the method enables a direct lattice calculation of the fundamental isospin-breaking parameter; if not, the lattice results can instead be used to determine the subtraction constant itself, with the cascade mass splitting serving as a consistency check.
