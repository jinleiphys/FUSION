# Pion production in the MiniBooNE

> We investigate one pion production processes within the Giessen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) coupled channel transport model. Our calculations for integrated and differential cross sections for realistic experimental neutrino fluxes are compared to the data recently provided by the MiniBooNE collaboration.

# Pion production in the MiniBooNE

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model, using an impulse approximation with no 2p-2h interactions and form factors fitted to ANL data, underpredicts MiniBooNE charged-current one-pion production cross sections by a factor of approximately 1.5 at 1 GeV and up to a factor of 2 at 2 GeV; the discrepancy persists even without final-state interactions, indicating that the missing strength likely originates in the primary interaction vertex rather than nuclear medium effects.

## Method

The authors employed the GiBUU coupled-channel transport model, which solves the semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation for the phase-space density of each particle species under a mean-field potential with elastic and inelastic collision terms. The initial nucleus was treated as a collection of off-shell nucleons in a local Thomas-Fermi approximation, and the elementary neutrino-nucleon input (resonance production, non-resonant background, quasi-elastic scattering) used the world-average axial mass M_A = 0.999 GeV for QE and parameters tuned to ANL data for resonance and background processes. Final-state interactions were included via the BUU equation, but nuclear medium corrections to resonance widths and spectral functions were neglected.

## Key numbers

- Average neutrino energy of the MiniBooNE flux: 0.7 GeV, with flux becoming very small above 2 GeV
- Axial mass used for QE scattering: M_A = 0.999 GeV (world average value from Kuzmin (2007))
- With FSI included, the calculated 1π⁺ cross section lies 20% below the calculation without FSI
- At 1 GeV neutrino energy, the GiBUU calculation with FSI is a factor of approximately 1.5 below the MiniBooNE data for 1π⁺ production
- At 2 GeV, the discrepancy between GiBUU with FSI and data for 1π⁺ production amounts to a factor of approximately 2
- For 1π⁰ production, the data are higher than calculated values by a factor of approximately 1.5 (from the slope comparison)
- The ratio of 1π⁺ to QE-like cross sections shows reasonable agreement with data up to about 1 GeV, with discrepancy of only about 10% above that energy
- Including 2p-2h contributions in QE would lower the π/QE ratio by about 25% at 1 GeV
- π⁻ production cross sections are approximately 20 times smaller than those for 1π⁺
- For differential distributions, the 1π⁺ data require a scaling factor of 1.6 (with FSI) or 1.2 (without FSI) to match shape; for 1π⁰, the required scaling factor is 2.0
- The ANL and BNL data for pion production differ by about 30%, with BNL data higher

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU model calculations previously applied to QE scattering and NC pion production (Leitner 2009) to charged-current one-pion production, comparing with the first detailed MiniBooNE pion energy and angle data (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010). The results contradict earlier expectations that conventional impulse-approximation models could explain the data, and the authors note that the discrepancies are larger than can be resolved by using the higher BNL elementary cross sections (which are 30% above ANL values). The paper points toward the possibility that 2p-2h interactions, as proposed by Martini (2010), Amaro (2011), and Nieves (2011) for QE scattering, may also contribute to pion production, though even a 25% increase from such mechanisms would not fully close the factor-of-1.5 to 2 gap. The predicted low-energy peak in pion spectra from FSI, similar to that observed in photo-pion production (Krusche 2004, Mertens 2008), is absent in the MiniBooNE data, suggesting either an issue with the neutrino flux normalization or a missing reaction mechanism in the primary interaction.
