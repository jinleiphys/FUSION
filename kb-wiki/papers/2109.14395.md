# Induced fission-like process of hadronic molecular states

> In this work, we predict a new physical phenomenon, induced fission-like process and chain reaction of hadronic molecular states. As a molecular state, if induced by a $D$ meson, the $X(3872)$ can split into $D\bar{D}$ final state which is forbidden due to the spin-parity conservation. The breeding of the $D$ meson of the reaction, such as $D^0X(3872)\to D^0\bar{D}^0D^0$, makes the chain reaction of $X(3872)$ matter possible. We estimate the cross section of the $D$ meson induced fission-like process of $X(3872)$ into two $D$ mesons. With very small $D^0$ beam momentum of 1 eV, the total cross section reaches an order of 1000 b, and decreases rapidly with the increasing of beam momentum. With the transition of $D^*$ meson in molecular states to a $D$ meson, the $X(3872)$ can release large energy, which is acquired by the final mesons. The momentum distributions of the final $D$ mesons are analyzed. In the laboratory frame, the spectator $D$ meson in molecular state concentrates in the low momentum area. The energy from the transition frim $D^*$ to $D$ meson is mainly acquired by two scattered $D$ mesons. The results suggest that the $D$ meson environment will lead to the induced fission-like process and chain reaction of the $X(3827)$. Such phenomenon can be extended to other hadronic molecular states.

# Induced fission-like process of hadronic molecular states

## Key claim

The authors predict that the hadronic molecular state X(3872), when bombarded by a D meson, can undergo a fission-like process into D meson pairs that would otherwise be forbidden by spin-parity conservation, and that the breeding of additional D mesons in the reaction enables a chain reaction in X(3872) matter. The claim is conditional on the existence of a D meson environment and on the binding energy of the molecular state being in a specific range (approximately 0.1 to 50 MeV) where the cross section is appreciable.

## Method

The authors model X(3872) as a superposition of D*0 D0 and D0 D*0 molecular states with a wave function derived from scattering length theory, using a binding energy that is varied from 0.05 to 50 MeV. They compute the cross section for the reaction D0 + X(3872) → 2 D0 + D0 via a Feynman diagram where the incoming D0 exchanges a ρ or ω meson with the D*0 constituent of X(3872), causing the D*0 to transition to a D0 meson. The amplitude combines the X(3872) splitting vertex (related to the scattering length) with the inelastic scattering amplitude for D*0 D0 → D0 D0, using effective Lagrangians under heavy quark and chiral symmetries with parameters β = 0.9, λ = 0.56 GeV−1, and gV = 5.9.

## Key numbers

- At a D0 beam momentum of 1 eV, the total cross section for D0 + X(3872) → 2 D0 + D0 reaches an order of 1000 b (barns).
- The cross section decreases rapidly with increasing beam momentum, reaching a minimum at approximately 100 MeV.
- The binding energy of X(3872) is varied from 0.05 to 50 MeV; the largest cross sections occur for binding energies around 1–10 MeV, corresponding to molecular radii of approximately 1–14 fm.
- For a binding energy of 0.1 MeV, the molecular radius is approximately 14 fm; for 10 MeV binding energy, the radius is approximately 1 fm.
- The spectator D0 meson in X(3872) concentrates at low momentum (near zero) after the reaction, while the D0 meson from the struck D*0 acquires a momentum of approximately 0.5 GeV.
- With an incoming momentum of 0.5 GeV and binding energy 0.1 MeV, the D0 meson off the struck D*0 has a wide distribution around 0.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the concept of induced fission from nuclear physics (Meitner 1939) to hadronic molecular states, following the authors’ earlier study of nucleon-induced fission of the Tcc+ state (He 2022). The X(3872) was first observed by Belle (2003) and proposed as a hadronic molecule by Tornqvist (2004); its wave function follows the formalism of Liu (2008) and Voloshin (2003). The cross section calculation uses the scattering length approach of Braaten (2004) and the effective Lagrangians of Casalbuoni (1996). The results suggest that in environments with many charm mesons, such as quark-gluon plasma produced in nuclear collisions (Zhang 2020), the induced fission-like process and chain reaction of X(3872) could occur, and the phenomenon may be extended to other hadronic molecular states in the strange, charmed, and bottom sectors.
