# Strangeness enhancement at LHC

> We study production of strangeness in the hot QGP fireball in conditions achieved at LHC, and use these results to obtain soft (strange) hadron multiplicities. We compare the chemical equilibrium and non-equilibrium conditions and identify characteristic experimental observables.

# Strangeness enhancement at LHC

## Key claim

Strangeness yield per entropy (s/S) at LHC is not a "deep" observable of the quark-gluon plasma, meaning it is insensitive to the early-stage gluon equilibration history; however, the final specific strangeness content does depend on the unknown initial entropy S0 and the strange quark mass, with the latter uncertainty cancelling nearly exactly in determining whether chemical equilibrium is reached.

## Method

The authors compute strangeness production in a hot QGP fireball using kinetic theory with perturbative QCD rates for gluon fusion and quark-antiquark annihilation into strange pairs, incorporating a running strange quark mass (ms(μ=2 GeV)=0.10 GeV) and a K-factor of 1.7 for higher-order processes. They then apply the statistical hadronization model (SHM) implemented in the SHARE 2 package, comparing chemical equilibrium (γq=γs=1, Tf=161 MeV) and non-equilibrium (γq=1.6, Tf=140 MeV) scenarios constrained by entropy conservation, baryon chemical potential from SPS/RHIC extrapolation, and strangeness balance.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (√sNN=5520 GeV), dS/dy increases by a factor of 1.65 compared to RHIC (√sNN=200 GeV), based on extrapolation as function of ln√sNN
- For the non-equilibrium model with dV/dy=2126 fm³, dS/dy=7457, charged hadron multiplicity dh_ch/dy (PHOBOS)=1150 (fixed input)
- For the equilibrium model with dV/dy=6200 fm³ (fixed), dS/dy=18790, dh_ch/dy (PHOBOS)=2538
- Specific strangeness s/S = 0.034 (non-equilibrium, low entropy) and 0.038 (non-equilibrium, high entropy) compared to 0.0255 (equilibrium)
- Energy per net baryon E/(b−b̄)=423 GeV (non-equilibrium, low entropy), 431 GeV (non-equilibrium, high entropy), 404 GeV (equilibrium)
- Freeze-out temperature Tf=140 MeV (non-equilibrium) versus 161 MeV (equilibrium)
- γq=1.6 (fixed, non-equilibrium), γs=2.35 (low entropy) and 2.8 (high entropy) versus γq=γs=1 (equilibrium)
- Multistrange hadron yields show 40%+ differences: φ=38 (non-equilibrium, high entropy) vs 25 (equilibrium); Ξ⁻=11 vs 6.2; Ω⁻=2.6 vs 0.98
- Resonance yields are suppressed in non-equilibrium: Δ⁰+Δ⁺⁺=9.4 vs 14.6; ρ=74 vs 119; ω=65 vs 109

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Rafelski 2005, Letessier 2006) on strangeness production from SPS to RHIC energies, now projecting to LHC conditions. It contrasts with chemical equilibrium approaches (Andronic 2005) that assume γq=1, showing that non-equilibrium hadronization with γq>1 better reproduces the baryon-to-meson ratios and resonance yields observed at RHIC, where equilibrium fits disagree by up to 50%. The results enable experimental discrimination between sudden QGP breakup and slow hadronization at LHC through measurement of multistrange hadron yields and double resonance ratios, particularly φ/h_ch, Ξ⁻/Λ, and Λ(1520)/Λ.
