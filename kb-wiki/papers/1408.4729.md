# Numerical and Physical Stability of Supernova Neutrino Flavor Evolution

> This paper examines neutrino flavor evolution outside a supernova neutrinosphere using a one-dimensional model that retains the non-linear nature of neutrino-neutrino interactions as well as some aspects of the full geometry. In some limiting cases analytic results can be obtained that display different behavior from their counterparts in (linear) solar neutrino flavor evolution. For more general cases, numerical solutions require extended numerical algorithms to achieve stable solutions, and these solutions exhibit standard chaotic behavior.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for supernova neutrino flavor evolution, the non-linear nature of neutrino-neutrino interactions prevents the MSW-like adiabatic conversion of pure flavor states into pure mass eigenstates, even when the propagation matrix eigenvalues never cross; instead, the asymptotic survival probability for a state originally produced as electron neutrino is given by a different expression than in the linear solar neutrino case.

## Method

The author constructs a one-dimensional model with two groups of neutrinos (A and B), each containing two incoherent neutrinos at distinct energies, evolving under a single space-time variable x. The model includes neutrino-neutrino interaction terms that couple the two groups, electron density falling off as (R/(R+x))^2, and neutrino density falling off as (R/(R+x))^4, with R = 10 km representing the neutrinosphere radius. Numerical integration is performed using both a unitarized Euler method and a fourth-order implicit Gauss-Runge-Kutta algorithm, with convergence tested by comparing results at different step sizes.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino density at neutrinosphere: 10^32 cm^-3; electron density: 10^30 cm^-3
- Neutrinosphere radius R = 10 km
- Mass-squared splitting Δm^2 = 0.003 eV^2; mixing angle θ = 0.1
- For neutrino energies of 10 MeV, the mean-free path for ν-ν scattering is approximately 50,000 km
- Asymptotic survival probability for a pure electron neutrino state: P_e → (cos^4θ + sin^4θ) = 1 - 2 cos^2θ sin^2θ
- For Case 4 (asymmetric populations with E_< = 9 MeV, E_> = 11 MeV), the GRK method converges to O(h^4) until x ~ 50 km; a sixth-order implicit Runge-Kutta algorithm maintained stability up to about x ~ 75 km but could not go further even with step sizes h ~ 1 mm
- For Case 2 (equal populations, E_< = 9 MeV, E_> = 11 MeV), the UE method does not converge at any value of x after x ~ 1 km

## Context

This work extends the study of non-linear neutrino flavor evolution in supernovae, building on the framework of neutrino refractive optics developed by Pantaleone (1992) and Duan et al. (2006). The results are consistent with the one-dimensional model of PhysRevD.88.045031, which found stable results at high neutrino densities but instabilities as densities decrease. The paper distinguishes between numerical stability (achievable with higher-order algorithms) and physical chaotic behavior, which represents genuine sensitivity to initial conditions. This raises questions about whether full three-dimensional simulations using algorithms with proven convergence can yield stable results when initial conditions are asymmetric, and whether the physical sensitivity to initial conditions is a fundamental limitation rather than a numerical artifact.
