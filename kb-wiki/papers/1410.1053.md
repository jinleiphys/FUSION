# BCS-BEC crossovers and unconventional phases in dilute nuclear matter

> We study the phase diagram of isospin-asymmetrical nuclear matter in the density-temperature plane, allowing for four competing phases of nuclear matter: (i) the unpaired phase, (ii) the translationally and rotationally symmetric, but isospin-asymmetrical BCS condensate, (iii) the current-carrying Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell phase, and (iv) the heterogeneous phase-separated phase. The phase diagram of nuclear matter composed of these phases features two tri-critical points in general, as well as crossovers from the asymmetrical BCS phase to a BEC of deuterons plus a neutron gas, both for the homogeneous superfluid phase (at high temperatures) and for the heterogeneous phase (at low temperatures). The BCS-BEC type crossover in the condensate occurs as the density is reduced. We analyze in detail some intrinsic properties of these phases, including the Cooper-pair wave function, the coherence length, the occupation numbers of majority and minority nucleonic components, and the dispersion relations of quasiparticle excitations about the ground state. We show by explicit examples that the physics of the individual phases and the transition from weak to strong coupling can be well understood by tracing the behavior of these quantities.

## Key claim

The paper maps the phase diagram of isospin-asymmetrical nuclear matter in the density-temperature plane, showing that the BCS-BEC crossover in the condensate occurs as density is reduced, and that this crossover exists both for the homogeneous superfluid phase at high temperatures and for the heterogeneous phase-separated phase at low temperatures, with the low-density asymptotic state being a Bose-Einstein condensate of deuterons mixed with a neutron gas.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled finite-temperature gap equation and density equations in mean-field approximation for the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ (isosinglet, spin-triplet) pairing channel, using the bare Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction for the pairing potential and a Skyrme density functional (SkIII and SLy4 parametrizations) for the nuclear mean field. They computed the free energy of four competing phases, unpaired, isospin-asymmetrical BCS condensate, current-carrying Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase, and phase-separated (PS) phase, and assigned the ground state to the phase with lowest free energy at each temperature, density, and isospin asymmetry. They also analyzed intrinsic properties including the Cooper-pair wave function, coherence length, occupation numbers, and quasiparticle dispersion relations across the BCS-BEC crossover.

## Key numbers

- The phase diagram features two tri-critical points for each asymmetry; at a special asymmetry $\alpha_4 = 0.255$, these two points degenerate into a single tetra-critical point at $\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.22$ and $T = 2.85$ MeV, where $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is nuclear saturation density.
- The LOFF phase disappears at the point $\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.65$ and $T=0$ for asymmetry $\alpha = 0.62$.
- The effective mass mismatch $\delta_m = (m^*_n-m^*_p)/(m^*_n+m^*_p)$ lies in the range $0 \le |\delta_m| \le 0.06$, with the upper bound attained for the largest asymmetries and densities studied.
- At constant density $\rho=0.1$ fm$^{-3}$, the gap as a function of asymmetry was computed for temperatures $T=0.5$, $1.0$, $1.5$, and $2.0$ MeV, comparing BCS and LOFF phases.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Stein 2012) by providing a detailed analysis of intrinsic condensate properties, Cooper-pair wave function, coherence length, occupation numbers, and quasiparticle dispersion relations, across the BCS-BEC crossover and into the LOFF phase. It builds on the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink theory (1985) of the BCS-BEC crossover, adapted to isospin-asymmetrical nuclear matter where the low-density asymptotic state becomes a mixture of deuterons and neutrons (as previously discussed by Lombardo 2001). The results are relevant for understanding supernova matter and hot proto-neutron-star matter at sub-saturation densities, where deuterons are a substantial constituent (Typel 2010, Hempel 2009).
