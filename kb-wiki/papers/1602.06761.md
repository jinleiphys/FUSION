# Excited hadrons and the analytical structure of bound-state interaction kernels

> We highlight Hermiticity issues in bound-state equations whose kernels are subject to a highly asymmetric mass and momentum distribution and whose eigenvalue spectrum becomes complex for radially excited states. We trace back the presence of imaginary components in the eigenvalues and wave functions to truncation artifacts and suggest how they can be eliminated in the case of charmed mesons. The solutions of the gap equation in the complex plane, which play a crucial role in the analytic structure of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel, are discussed for several interaction models and qualitatively and quantitatively compared to analytic continuations by means of complex-conjugate pole models fitted to real solutions.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation produces a non-Hermitian interaction kernel for heavy-light mesons (such as D and D_s mesons) when the mass difference between the quark and antiquark becomes large, causing the eigenvalue spectrum for radially excited states to acquire imaginary components; these imaginary parts are truncation artifacts that can be eliminated by including vertex corrections and crossed-box contributions beyond the leading truncation.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for quark propagators and the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for pseudoscalar meson bound states in Euclidean space, using the rainbow-ladder truncation with several effective interaction models (including the Qin-Chang interaction and Maris-Tandy model). They expand the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in Chebyshev polynomials of the second kind and analyze the eigenvalue spectrum of the BSE kernel for ground and radially excited states of light, strange, and charmed mesons. They also fit complex-conjugate pole models with n=3 poles to the numerical DSE solutions on the real axis and compare these to direct numerical solutions of the DSE in a parabola on the complex plane.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hermiticity issues in Bethe-Salpeter kernels first identified for heavy-light mesons by Rojas et al. (2014) and El-Bennich et al. (2015), showing that the rainbow-ladder truncation produces complex eigenvalues for radially excited D and D_s mesons when higher Chebyshev moments (m >= 4) are included, while ground-state eigenvalues remain real. The authors argue that beyond-rainbow-ladder kernels are necessary for heavy-light systems, as the asymmetric dressing of the light and heavy quark-gluon vertices prevents the cancellation of repulsive crossed-box contributions that occurs in equal-mass cases. The complex-conjugate pole model approach, following Bhagwat et al. (2002), provides an economical representation of quark propagators in the complex plane for BSE calculations, but the authors note that such pole representations are too simplistic for a complete description of hadronic observables beyond a restrained data set.
