# Separable RPA for self-consistent nuclear models

## Key claim

The authors propose a self-consistent separable random-phase approximation (SRPA) that factorizes the two-body residual interaction for arbitrary density- and current-dependent energy functionals, claiming that the method considerably simplifies calculations and demonstrates quick convergence to exact RPA results. The hedge is that the method is tested only for two specific Skyrme parameterizations (SkI3 and SkM*) and for two doubly magic nuclei, and the quality of convergence depends on the choice of a small set of collective generators.

## Method

The authors construct a separable residual interaction by expanding it into a sum of weighted products of one-body operators, where the operators are derived from commutators of the full residual interaction with collective generators (multipole operators and their conjugate momenta). The strength constants are determined from double commutators that reproduce the m1 and m3 sum rules. The method is tested by comparing SRPA strength functions for isoscalar E2 and isovector E1 giant resonances in 40Ca and 208Pb against full RPA calculations using the same 1ph space, with the separable basis consisting of four radial operators (one power-law and three spherical Bessel functions) per isospin channel.

## Key numbers

- The SRPA expansion uses four separable operators (k=1-4) per isospin channel, with radial forms: r^λ for k=1, and j_λ(q^k_λ r) for k=2,3,4 with coefficients a_2=0.6, a_3=0.9, a_4=1.2 relative to the diffraction radius.
- Full RPA calculations used a basis covering all 1ph excitations up to 30 MeV above the Fermi energy, resulting in matrices of rank 400 and more, while SRPA requires inversion of 17×17 matrices.
- The smoothing parameter used for strength functions is Γ=1 MeV, with additional high-resolution comparisons at Γ=0.1 MeV.
- SRPA calculations can include particle energies up to at least 600 MeV when using the full coordinate space grid, compared to the 30 MeV cutoff in the restricted full RPA basis.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent separable scheme previously applied to Kohn-Sham functionals for atomic clusters (Nesterenko et al., 1999; Babst et al., 2000) to the more complex nuclear Skyrme-Hartree-Fock functional. It generalizes the self-consistent scheme of Liu and Schuck (1977) to multiple separable operators, and improves upon earlier separable approaches that were limited to phenomenological single-particle potentials (Nesterenko et al., 1996; Kubo et al., 1999) or specific effective forces (Vorobjev et al., 2000). The method enables large-scale RPA calculations for heavy and deformed nuclei where conventional RPA matrices become prohibitively large (typically 10^3-10^6 1ph states).
