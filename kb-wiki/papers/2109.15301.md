# Spin Polarization Formula for Dirac Fermions at Local Equilibrium

> We derive a Cooper-Frye type spin polarization formula for Dirac fermions at local thermal equilibrium described by a grand canonical ensemble specified by temperature, fluid velocity, chemical potential, and spin potential. We discuss the physical meaning of different contributions to spin polarization and compare them with previous works. The present formula provides machinery to convert the spin potential computed in, e.g., relativistic spin hydrodynamics to the spin polarization observable in, e.g., heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Cooper-Frye type spin polarization formula for Dirac fermions at local equilibrium that includes contributions from the spin potential tensor, thermal shear tensor, and gradients of the chemical potential, in addition to the thermal vorticity contribution that appears in the global equilibrium formula. The authors show that at local equilibrium, the spin potential is not necessarily equal to thermal vorticity, and their difference (Delta omega) contributes to the spin polarization.

## Method

The authors derive the spin polarization formula using two independent approaches: first, from spin kinetic theory by solving the Wigner function for both massless and massive Dirac fermions up to first order in hbar, and second, from the local equilibrium density operator formalism using a perturbative expansion in gradients. Both methods yield the same expression for the axial vector in phase space, which is then integrated over the freeze-out hypersurface to obtain the Cooper-Frye type formula for the mean spin vector.

## Key numbers

- The spin polarization vector is given by P_mu = 2 * S_mu for spin-1/2 fermions, where s = 1/2 is the spin quantum number.
- The zeroth component of the spin vector in the low-velocity limit is S^0 approximately (1/2)[n_F(alpha + beta E_p - (omega dot p)/(2E_p)) - n_F(alpha + beta E_p + (omega dot p)/(2E_p))], where omega^i = epsilon^{ijk} omega_{jk}/2 is the spin potential vector.
- The spatial components of the spin vector in the non-relativistic limit include five contributions: S_(omega) from spin potential, S_(Omega) from non-relativistic vorticity Omega = (nabla x v)/2, S_(sigma) from non-relativistic shear tensor sigma_{ij}, S_(T) from temperature gradient, and S_(alpha) from chemical potential gradient.
- The mean spin vector formula contains the factor n_F(1 - n_F) in the numerator and the denominator includes the factor 8E_p times the integral of dXi dot p times n_F.

## Context

This work extends the global equilibrium spin polarization formula of Becattini (2013) and Fang (2016), which showed that the mean spin vector at global equilibrium is determined solely by thermal vorticity. The new formula addresses the "spin polarization puzzle" in heavy-ion collisions, where the global equilibrium formula successfully reproduces global hyperon polarization data from STAR (2017, 2018, 2021) but fails to describe local (azimuthal-angle-dependent) spin polarization measurements. The paper builds on recent proposals by Becattini (2021) and Liu (2021) that the thermal shear tensor contributes to spin polarization at local equilibrium, and provides a framework to convert spin potentials computed in relativistic spin hydrodynamics (Florkowski 2017, Hattori 2019, Montenegro 2018) into observable spin polarization signals.
