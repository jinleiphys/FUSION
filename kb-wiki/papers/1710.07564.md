# Dual Formulation and Phase Diagram of Lattice QCD in the Strong Coupling Regime

> We present the computation of invariants that arise in the strong coupling expansion of lattice QCD. These invariants are needed for Monte Carlo simulations of Lattice QCD with staggered fermions in a dual, color singlet representation. This formulation is in particular useful to tame the finite density sign problem. The gauge integrals in this limiting case $β\rightarrow 0$ are well known, but the gauge integrals needed to study the gauge corrections are more involved. We discuss a method to evaluate such integrals. The phase boundary of lattice QCD for staggered fermions in the $μ_B-T$ plane has been established in the strong coupling limit. We present numerical simulations away from the strong coupling limit, taking into account the higher order gauge corrections via plaquette occupation numbers. This allows to study the nuclear and chiral transition as a function of $β$.

# Dual Formulation and Phase Diagram of Lattice QCD in the Strong Coupling Regime

## Key claim
The authors present a dual representation of lattice QCD with staggered fermions that incorporates gauge corrections beyond the strong coupling limit (β→0), enabling direct Monte Carlo simulations of the phase diagram in the μ_B-T plane. The method claims to tame the finite density sign problem, though the sign problem reappears away from the strong coupling limit even for U(N_c) gauge theory, and the authors restrict to the q=0 sector of SU(N_c) which introduces systematic errors at O(β^N_c).

## Method
The authors expand the lattice action S = S_F + S_G in both fermion hoppings and β = 2N_c/g^2, then analytically integrate out the SU(N_c) link variables first using Weingarten functions, followed by Grassmann integration of the staggered fermions. The resulting partition function is expressed as a sum over monomers, dimers (meson hoppings), N_c-flux baryon world lines, and plaquette occupation numbers n_P, with the constraint that fermion fluxes f_b form self-avoiding loops. The authors sample this dual representation using an extended worm algorithm and adopt a non-perturbative anisotropy ξ(γ) to define temperature.

## Key numbers
- The tri-critical point in the strong coupling limit is located at (aμ_B^{tric}, aT^{tric}) = (1.56(4), 0.73(4))
- The non-perturbative anisotropy relation uses κ ≃ 0.7810(8) in the formula ξ(γ) ≃ κ + 1/(1+λγ^4) with λ = κ/(1-κ)
- The ratio μ_B^{CEP}/T^{CEP} > 2 becomes even larger as a function of quark mass
- The Weingarten function example for SU(N_c) with n=3 gives Wg_{3,N}(21) = -1/[(N^2-1)(N^2-4)] and Wg_{3,N}(1^3) = (N^2-2)/[N(N^2-1)(N^2-4)]

## Context
This work extends the strong coupling expansion of lattice QCD originally formulated by Rossi and Wolff (1984) and the gauge correction approach of de Forcrand et al. (2014) by directly sampling the partition function including next-to-leading order gauge corrections via plaquette occupation numbers. The dual representation builds on methods previously applied to abelian gauge theories such as the massless Schwinger model (Gattringer, 2015) and gauge-Higgs models (Mercado, 2013). The results enable direct numerical study of the nuclear and chiral transitions as functions of β, with crosschecks against hybrid Monte Carlo and mean field theory (Miura, 2016) for SU(3) at μ_B=0.
