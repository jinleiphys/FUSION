# Mass modification of D-meson at finite density in QCD sum rule

> We evaluate the mass shift of isospin-averaged D-meson in the nuclear medium. Borel-transformed QCD sum rules are used to describe an interaction between the D-meson and a nucleon by taking into account all the lowest dimension-4 operators in the operator product expansion (OPE). We find at normal matter density the D-meson mass shift is about 10 times (\sim 50 MeV) larger than that of J/ψ. This originates from the fact that the dominant contribution in the OPE for the D-meson is the nucleon matrix element of m_c\bar{q}q, where m_c is the charm-quark mass and q denotes light quarks. We also discuss that the mass shift of the D-meson in nuclear matter may cause the level crossings of the charmonium states and the D\bar{D} threshold. This suggests an additional mechanism of the J/ψsuppression in high energy heavy-ion collisions.

# Mass modification of D-meson at finite density in QCD sum rule

## Key claim

The isospin-averaged D-meson experiences a mass shift of approximately -50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, which is about 10 times larger than the J/psi mass shift. This large shift originates primarily from the nucleon matrix element of the operator m_c * <bar{q}q>, where m_c is the charm-quark mass and q denotes light quarks, and the result is presented as a qualitative estimate because the analysis uses only dimension-4 operators and does not include isospin decomposition.

## Method

The author applies Borel-transformed QCD sum rules to the forward scattering amplitude of a pseudoscalar current and a nucleon, decomposing the in-medium correlation function into a vacuum part and a static one-nucleon part in the linear density approximation. The operator product expansion is performed including all lowest dimension-4 operators, and the phenomenological side is constructed with double-pole, single-pole, and continuum terms, with two cases considered for incorporating the Born term from sub-threshold charmed baryon resonances (Lambda_c and Sigma_c). The mass shift is extracted by matching the OPE side to the phenomenological side after Borel transformation and taking a first derivative with respect to the Borel mass squared.

## Key numbers

- D-meson mass shift at normal matter density: delta m_D = -48 +/- 8 MeV, obtained by considering both cases (i) and (ii) for the Born term treatment
- D-N scattering length: a_D = -0.72 +/- 0.12 fm
- Contribution of the m_c * <bar{q}q>_N term in the OPE is more than 95% of the total contribution within the plateau regions
- Vacuum D-meson mass: m_D = 1.87 GeV; charm-quark mass: m_c = 1.35 GeV
- Decay constant: f_D = 0.18 GeV; continuum threshold: s_0 = 6.0 GeV^2
- Nucleon matrix elements used: <bar{q}q>_N = 5.3 GeV, <q^dag i D_0 q>_N = 0.34 GeV^2, <(alpha_s/pi) G^2>_N = -1.2 GeV^2, (alpha_s/pi) * <(u.G)^2 - (1/4)G^2>_N = -0.1 GeV^2
- Light-quark mass: m_q = 0.008 GeV
- For comparison: J/psi mass shift is about -5 MeV and J/psi-N scattering length is about -0.1 fm (Hayashigaki 1999; Klingl et al. 1999)
- Plateau regions for delta m_D: solid line (Born=0) 3.5 <= M^2 <= 7.0 GeV^2 giving -63 +/- 3 MeV; doted line (case i) 2.6 <= M^2 <= 7.0 GeV^2 giving -53 +/- 3 MeV; dashed line (case ii) 3.4 <= M^2 <= 8.0 GeV^2 giving -44 +/- 3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule method previously applied to J/psi (Hayashigaki 1999) and to light vector mesons (Hatsuda and Lee 1992; Koike 1995; Jin and Leinweber 1995; Koike and Hayashigaki 1997) to the pseudoscalar D-meson system with unequal quark masses. The result is very close to the -60 MeV mass shift predicted by the quark meson coupling (QMC) model (Tsushima et al. 1999). The large mass shift suggests that at normal matter density the D-bar{D} threshold (3.74 GeV in vacuum) decreases by about 100 MeV, potentially falling below the psi' (3.68 GeV) and chi_c2 (3.55 GeV) states, which could provide an additional mechanism for J/psi suppression in heavy-ion collisions through the decay of excited charmonium states into open charm pairs.
