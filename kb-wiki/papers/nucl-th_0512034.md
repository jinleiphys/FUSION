# Effective mass of free neutrons in neutron star crust

> The inner layers of a neutron star crust, composed of a Coulomb lattice of neutron rich nuclear clusters immersed in a sea of ``free'' superfluid neutrons, are closely analogous to periodic condensed matter systems such as electronic, photonic or phononic crystals. Applying methods from solid state physics to the neutron star context, we study the transport properties of those ``free'' neutrons for the outermost layers of the inner crust, near the drip point $ρ_{\rm drip} \sim 4\times 10^{11}$ g.cm$^{-3}$. In particular, we evaluate the effective neutron mass resulting from Bragg scattering by a band structure calculation. Comparison is made with the case of electrons in solids. The observational consequences are briefly discussed.

# Effective mass of free neutrons in neutron star crust

## Key claim

For the outermost layers of the inner crust near the neutron drip point (ρ ~ 4×10¹¹ g.cm⁻³), the effective mass of "free" superfluid neutrons deviates significantly from the bare neutron mass due to Bragg scattering, but only when the Fermi wavelength becomes comparable to or smaller than the lattice spacing; at very low dripped neutron densities the effective mass remains close to the bare mass.

## Method

The author applied band theory from solid-state physics to the neutron star crust, treating the nuclear clusters as a periodic body-centered cubic lattice and the free neutrons as independent quasiparticles in an effective mean field. The single-particle Schrödinger equation was solved using the linearized augmented plane wave (LAPW) method with Bloch boundary conditions, going beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation used by Negele & Vautherin (1973). The effective neutron mass was computed from the mobility coefficient via Fermi surface integration using the Gilat-Raubenheimer scheme, with nuclear density profiles taken from Negele & Vautherin and Skyrme interactions (SkM and SLy4 parametrizations).

## Key numbers

- Baryon density at the onset of neutron drip: ρ = 4.66 × 10¹¹ g.cm⁻³ (largest Wigner-Seitz cell of Negele & Vautherin, with Wigner-Seitz sphere radius R_WS = 54 fm)
- Density threshold for Fermi sphere touching the first Brillouin zone boundary in a body-centered cubic lattice: n_f ≈ 2.2 × 10⁻⁶ fm⁻³ (for R_WS = 54 fm)
- Effective electron mass of copper computed for validation: m_⋆ ≈ 1.285 m_e (with one conduction electron per atom), in agreement with earlier estimate of 1.3 ± 0.1 m_e by Seagall (1961); with 11 "free" electrons per atom the effective mass becomes m_⋆ ≈ 14.14 m_e
- Energy cutoff for LAPW basis: E_cutoff = 1 MeV; maximum angular momentum: ℓ_max = 6; Slater sphere radius: R_s = 35 fm
- Fermi surface integrations performed with 9920 cubes in the irreducible domain (476160 cubes in the first Brillouin zone)

## Context

This work extends the band-theory approach previously applied by the author and collaborators (Chamel 2005; CCHI) to the deepest layers of the inner crust and to "pasta" phases, now reaching the outermost layers near the neutron drip point. The results contrast with the Wigner-Seitz approximation of Negele & Vautherin (1973), which cannot capture transport properties because it neglects the wave-vector dependence of single-particle states. The findings suggest that in the middle layers of the crust, where nuclear cluster size is comparable to lattice spacing, the effective neutron mass could become very large or even diverge due to band gaps, with implications for pulsar glitch mechanisms and the coupling between the neutron superfluid and the solid crust.
