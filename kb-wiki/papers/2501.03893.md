# Determining the Duration of the Hadronic Stage at RHIC-BES Energies via Resonance Suppression Using a Full Set of Rate Equations

> We present realistic estimates for the duration of the hadronic stage in central Au+Au reactions in the RHIC-BES energy regime. To this aim, we employ a full set of coupled rate equations to describe the time evolution of the system from chemical to kinetic freeze-out. Combined with the recently measured data by the STAR collaboration on $K^*/K$ ratios, we show that the previous estimates substantially underestimated the duration of this stage due to the omission of the regeneration of hadron resonances. We provide an improved relation between the $K^*/K$ ratio at chemical and kinetic freeze-out and the life time of the hadronic phase. The calculated improved life times are now in line with estimates from other methods and are relevant for the NA61 and STAR collaborations and for upcoming experiments at the FAIR facility.

## Key claim

Previous estimates of the hadronic stage duration in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies substantially underestimated the lifetime because they omitted resonance regeneration; when a full set of coupled rate equations including regeneration is used, the \(K^*/K\) ratio decreases linearly rather than exponentially, yielding lifetimes a factor of 2-4 larger than the STAR collaboration's simplified estimates.

## Method

The authors employed a network of 23 coupled rate equations describing the time evolution of stable hadrons (pions, kaons, nucleons, \(\Lambda\)s) and hadronic resonances from chemical to kinetic freeze-out, using thermal-averaged decay rates from Particle Data Tables and detailed balance to relate decay and regeneration rates. The expanding fireball was modeled with a parametrized volume incorporating longitudinal and transverse expansion, with temperature and chemical potentials determined by partial chemical equilibrium (conserving entropy, net baryon number, and net strangeness). The initial conditions at chemical freeze-out were taken from the Cleymans (2006) parametrization of \(T_{\rm ch}\) and \(\mu_{\rm B,ch}\) as functions of \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\), and the volume at chemical freeze-out was parametrized from data summarized by Andronic (2009).

## Key numbers

* The slope parameter for the \(K^*/K\) ratio time evolution is approximately \(-0.02\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), essentially energy-independent across \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 39 GeV.
* The hadronic phase lifetime estimates from the full rate equations are a factor of 2-4 larger than the STAR collaboration's estimates that neglected regeneration.
* The new lifetime estimates are on the order of 4-8 fm/c, consistent with previous estimates from HBT radii and other methods for similar charged-particle multiplicities.
* The chemical freeze-out temperature is parametrized as \(T_{\rm ch}(\mu_{\rm B,ch}) = 0.166\ \text{GeV} - 0.139\ \text{GeV}^{-1} \mu_{\rm B,ch}^{2} - 0.053\ \text{GeV}^{-3} \mu_{\rm B,ch}^{4}\).
* The baryon chemical potential is parametrized as \(\mu_{\rm B,ch}(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}) = 1.308\ \text{GeV} / (1 + 0.273\ \text{GeV}^{-1} \sqrt{s_{\rm NN}})\).
* The volume at chemical freeze-out is parametrized as \(V_{\rm ch}(x) = (2351.7 x^2 - 10009.5 x + 13878.4) x^{-1.36}\ \text{fm}^3\) where \(x \equiv \ln(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}/\text{GeV})\).
* The transverse expansion parameter is fixed at \(t_{\perp} = 6.5\) fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the resonance-suppression method for estimating hadronic stage lifetimes, originally proposed by the STAR collaboration (STAR 2002, STAR 2022), by incorporating regeneration processes that were neglected in the simple exponential-decay model of Eq. (1). The results bring the lifetime estimates into agreement with independent methods such as HBT femtoscopy (Lisa 2016) and previous dynamical calculations (Li 2007, Graef 2012, Knospe 2015). The improved relation between the \(K^*/K\) ratio and the hadronic lifetime is directly applicable to ongoing analyses by the NA61 collaboration and to future experiments at the FAIR facility.
