# Non-Abelian Plasma Instabilities for Extreme Anisotropy

> Thermalization of quark-gluon plasmas in heavy-ion collisions is a difficult theoretical problem. One theoretical goal has been to understand the physics of thermalization in the relatively simplifying limit of arbitrarily high energy collisions, where the running coupling alpha_s is weak. One of the current roadblocks to achieving this goal is lack of knowledge about the behavior of plasma instabilities when particle distributions are highly anisotropic. In particular, it has not been known how the magnetic fields generated by plasma instabilities scale with anisotropy. In this paper, we use numerical simulations in a first attempt to determine this scaling.

## Key claim

The paper uses numerical simulations of the hard-loop effective theory to determine how the limiting magnetic field strength \(B_*\) generated by non-Abelian plasma instabilities scales with the anisotropy parameter \(\theta \equiv |p_z|/p\) in the extreme anisotropy limit, finding that \(B_* \sim m_\infty^2/(\theta g)\), which corresponds to an exponent \(\nu = 1\) in the parametrization \(B_* \sim m_\infty^2/(\theta^\nu g)\).

## Method

The authors simulate the non-Abelian Vlasov equations (hard-loop effective theory) for SU(2) gauge theory on a spatial lattice, discretizing the velocity dependence via a spherical harmonic expansion truncated at maximum \(\ell\). They initialize the simulations with large, non-perturbatively strong gauge fields (rather than tiny seeds) to mimic the conditions relevant to the first stage of bottom-up thermalization, and measure the late-time linear slope \(d\mathcal{E}^B_\text{tot}/dt\) of the total magnetic energy density as a function of anisotropy.

## Key numbers

* For the three candidate exponents \(\nu = 0\) (Ref. [BnewBUP]), \(\nu = 1\) (Ref. [kminus2]), and \(\nu = 2\) (Nielsen-Olesen limit), the measured scaling of \(d\mathcal{E}^B_\text{tot}/dt\) with anisotropy rules out \(\nu \le 1/2\) and \(\nu \ge 3/2\), leaving only \(\nu = 1\) consistent with the data.
* The maximum unstable wavenumber scales as \(k_\text{max} \sim m_\infty/\theta\), with the instability growth rate \(\gamma \sim m_\infty\) (approaching \(m_\infty/\sqrt{2}\) in the extreme anisotropy limit).
* In the bottom-up thermalization context, the anisotropy evolves as \(\theta \sim (\Qs\tau)^{-1/8}\) for \(\nu = 1\), compared to \(\theta \sim (\Qs\tau)^{-1/4}\) for \(\nu = 0\) and \(\theta \sim (\Qs\tau)^{-1/3}\) in the original bottom-up scenario of Baier et al. (2001).

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies of non-Abelian plasma instabilities (Rebhan et al., 2005; Arnold et al., 2005; Bodeker and Rummukainen, 2007) from moderate to extreme anisotropy, motivated by the first stage of the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier et al. (2001). The results distinguish between two competing conjectures for the parametric scaling of the limiting magnetic field: the earlier assumption of \(B_* \sim m_\infty^2/g\) (Arnold et al., 2005) and the later proposal \(B_* \sim m_\infty^2/(\theta g)\) (Arnold and Moore, 2006), favoring the latter. This enables a parametric determination of how plasma instabilities modify the time evolution of anisotropy in weak-coupling bottom-up thermalization.
