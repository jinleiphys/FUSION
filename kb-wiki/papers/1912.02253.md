# Heavy Quarkonium in Extreme Conditions

> In this report we review recent progress achieved in the understanding of heavy quarkonium under extreme conditions from a theory perspective. Its focus lies both on quarkonium properties in thermal equilibrium, as well as recent developments towards a genuine real-time description, valid also out-of-equilibrium. We will give an overview of the theory tools developed and deployed over the last decade, including effective field theories, lattice field theory simulations, modern methods for spectral reconstructions and the the open-quantum systems paradigm. The report will discuss in detail the concept of quarkonium melting, providing the reader with a contemporary perspective. In order to judge where future progress is needed we will also discuss recent results from experiments and phenomenological modeling of quarkonium in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

This review argues that the static "thermometer" picture of sequential quarkonium melting at well-defined temperatures must be replaced by a dynamical understanding where quarkonium melting arises from the interplay of screening and scattering processes, leading to environment-induced decoherence, and that the concept of a melting temperature is not uniquely defined.

## Method

The paper reviews theory tools developed over the last decade for studying heavy quarkonium under extreme conditions, including effective field theories (NRQCD, pNRQCD), lattice QCD simulations, spectral function reconstruction methods, and the open quantum systems framework. It examines both the idealized setting of fully kinetically equilibrated quarkonium in a static medium and the derivation of genuine real-time descriptions from first-principles QCD using Lindblad equations and stochastic Schrodinger equations.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum binding energies of quarkonium states: Upsilon at 1.1 GeV, chi_b1 and J/psi at approximately 0.64 GeV, chi_c1 at approximately 0.2 GeV
- Crossover transition temperature: T_C = 155 MeV
- Maximum QGP temperature in contemporary heavy-ion collisions: T less than or approximately 600 MeV
- Heavy-ion collision energies explored: SPS at sqrt(s_NN) approximately 0.15 TeV, RHIC at sqrt(s_NN) = 0.2 TeV, LHC at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV
- Number of heavy quark pairs produced: at RHIC O(10) c-cbar and O(1) b-bbar pairs; at LHC O(100) c-cbar and O(10) b-bbar pairs
- QGP lifetime: 5-10 fm
- Pre-thermalization phase duration: approximately 1 fm

## Context

This review extends and updates the foundational work of Matsui and Satz (1986) on sequential quarkonium melting as a signal for deconfined QGP, and the more quantitative treatment by Karsch (2005). It builds on previous reviews by Bazavov (2009) and Mocsy (2013) on in-medium quarkonium. The paper enables future work connecting first-principles real-time descriptions of quarkonium dynamics to experimental measurements at RHIC, LHC, and upcoming facilities at NICA, FAIR, and JPARC, particularly for discriminating between current phenomenological models of charmonium and bottomonium production in heavy-ion collisions.
