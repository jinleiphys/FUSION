# Chiral nuclear thermodynamics

> We calculate the equation of state of nuclear matter for arbitrary isospin-asymmetry up to three loop order in the free energy density in the framework of in-medium chiral perturbation theory. In our approach 1π- and 2π-exchange dynamics with the inclusion of the Δ-isobar excitation as an explicit degree of freedom, corresponding to the long- and intermediate-range correlations, are treated explicitly. Few contact terms fixed to reproduce selected known properties of nuclear matter encode the short-distance physics. Two-body as well as three-body forces are systematically included. We find a critical temperature of about 15 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter. We investigate the dependence of the liquid-gas first-order phase transition on isospin-asymmetry. In the same chiral framework we calculate the chiral condensate of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures. The contribution of the Δ-isobar excitation is essential for stabilizing the condensate. As a result, we find no indication of a chiral phase transition for densities ρ\lesssim 2 ρ_0 and at temperatures T \lesssim 100 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the equation of state and chiral condensate of nuclear matter using in-medium chiral perturbation theory up to three-loop order, finding a critical temperature of about 15 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter and no indication of a chiral phase transition for densities up to roughly twice saturation density and temperatures up to 100 MeV, provided the Delta-isobar excitation is included as an explicit degree of freedom.

## Method

The free energy density of nuclear matter is computed up to three-loop order using in-medium chiral perturbation theory, with one-pion and two-pion exchange dynamics treated explicitly and the Delta-isobar included as an explicit degree of freedom for intermediate-range correlations. Short-distance physics is encoded in a few contact terms fixed to reproduce selected bulk properties of nuclear matter, such as the energy minimum of -16 MeV at saturation. Two-body and three-body forces are systematically included via diagrams with two and three medium insertions in the in-medium nucleon propagator, and the extension to isospin-asymmetric matter distinguishes proton and neutron Fermi seas with their specific isospin factors.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: T_c ≈ 15.1 MeV
- Critical temperature for neutron-rich matter with proton fraction x_p = 0.1: T_c ≈ 6.3 MeV
- Saturation density at T = 0: ρ_0 ≈ 0.157 fm^{-3}
- Energy minimum at saturation for symmetric nuclear matter at T = 0: -16 MeV
- Asymmetry energy at saturation point: A(ρ_0) ≈ 34 MeV
- Neutron-rich matter becomes unbound for proton fraction x_p ≲ 0.12 at T = 0
- Liquid-gas phase transition disappears for proton fraction x_p ≲ 0.05
- No chiral phase transition observed for densities ρ ≲ 2 ρ_0 and temperatures T ≲ 100 MeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term used: σ_N = (45 ± 8) MeV

## Context

This work extends the in-medium chiral perturbation theory approach of Kaiser et al. (various years) by computing the equation of state up to three-loop order with explicit Delta-isobar degrees of freedom and systematically including two-body and three-body forces. The calculation of the chiral condensate at finite density and temperature with in-medium two-pion exchange interactions is presented as the first such systematic treatment. The results provide nuclear physics constraints for discussions of the QCD phase diagram relevant to heavy-ion collisions and astrophysics.
