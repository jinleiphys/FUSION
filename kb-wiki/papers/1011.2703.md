# How many of the scaling trends in $pp$ collisions will be violated at sqrt{s_NN} = 14 TeV ? - Predictions from Monte Carlo quark-gluon string model

> Multiplicity, rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of hadrons produced both in inelastic and nondiffractive $pp$ collisions at energies from $\sqrt{s} = 200$\,GeV to 14\,TeV are studied within the Monte Carlo quark-gluon string model (QGSM). Good agreement with the available experimental data up to $\sqrt{s} = 13$\,TeV is obtained, and predictions are made for the collisions at top LHC energy $\sqrt{s} = 14$\,TeV. The model indicates that Feynman scaling and extended longitudinal scaling remain valid in the fragmentation regions, whereas strong violation of Feynman scaling is observed at midrapidity. The Koba-Nielsen-Olesen (KNO) scaling in multiplicity distributions is violated at LHC also. The origin of both maintenance and violation of the scaling trends is traced to short range correlations of particles in the strings and interplay between the multistring processes at ultrarelativistic energies.

## Key claim

The Monte Carlo quark-gluon string model (QGSM) predicts that at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, Feynman scaling and extended longitudinal scaling will remain valid in the fragmentation regions, while strong violation of Feynman scaling will occur at midrapidity and Koba-Nielsen-Olesen (KNO) scaling in multiplicity distributions will be violated; the maintenance or violation of these scaling trends originates from short-range correlations of particles in strings and the interplay between multistring processes at ultrarelativistic energies.

## Method

The authors employed the Monte Carlo realization of the quark-gluon string model (QGSM), based on Gribov's Reggeon field theory, to simulate pp collisions at energies from sqrt(s) = 200 GeV to 14 TeV. The model decomposes the inelastic cross section into multichain, single-diffractive, and double-diffractive processes, with both soft and hard Pomeron exchanges included via an eikonal approach, and uses the Field-Feynman algorithm for string fragmentation into hadrons. Comparisons were made with experimental data from UA5, UA1, CDF, E735, ALICE, CMS, and TOTEM collaborations at energies up to sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, and predictions were generated for sqrt(s) = 14 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle density at midrapidity for inelastic pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: dN_inel/deta|eta=0 = 5.8
- Charged particle density at midrapidity for nonsingle-diffractive pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: dN_NSD/deta|eta=0 = 6.7
- Rise of central particle density from sqrt(s) = 7 TeV to 14 TeV is expected to be about 20%
- Total cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: sigma_tot = 93.07 mb
- Elastic cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: sigma_el = 23.61 mb
- Multichain cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: sigma_P = 50.76 mb
- Single-diffraction cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: sigma_SD = 11.89 mb
- Double-diffraction cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: sigma_DD = 6.80 mb
- Crossover between hard and soft branches in pT spectra shifts from pT approx 2.8 GeV/c at sqrt(s) = 900 GeV to pT approx 2.2 GeV/c at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV
- Fit for mean pT of charged particles in NSD events: <pT> = 0.417 - 0.0035 ln(s) + 0.00059 ln^2(s) (in GeV/c)
- Fit for NSD midrapidity density: dN_NSD/deta|eta=0 (s) = 5.015 - 0.60 ln(s) + 0.036 ln^2(s)

## Context

This work extends the study of scaling trends in pp collisions from lower energies up to the top LHC energy of 14 TeV, building on the quark-gluon string model framework previously developed by the authors and collaborators (mc_qgsm). The results contradict the simple extrapolation method of Busza (2008), which predicted dN_NSD/deta|eta=0 = 4.6 +/- 0.4 at LHC energies, significantly lower than the QGSM prediction of 6.7. The model predictions are compared with other Reggeon field theory based models including QGSJET-2 (Ostapchenko 2011), GLMM (Gotsman et al.), GLM (Gotsman et al.), KMR-1, and KMR-2 (Khoze, Martin, Ryskin), with QGSM results closest to the GLMM model. The work enables future experimental tests at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV to determine which scaling trends are violated and which are maintained, and clarifies the role of multistring processes in generating the observed energy dependence.
