# The early stages of a high energy heavy ion collision

> At high energy, the gluon distribution in nuclei reaches large densities and eventually saturates due to recombinations, that play an important role in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The Color Glass Condensate provides a framework for resumming these effects in the calculation of observables. In this talk, I present its application to the description of the early stages of heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory can describe the early stages of heavy ion collisions, but the leading-order classical Yang-Mills solution yields a negative longitudinal pressure that is far from thermal equilibrium; this result is incomplete due to instabilities that cause a breakdown of the perturbative expansion at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The author applies the CGC framework, which treats fast partons as static color sources and resums large gluon densities via classical Yang-Mills equations at leading order. At next-to-leading order, the single inclusive gluon spectrum is related to the leading-order result via functional derivatives, and the factorization of energy logarithms into JIMWLK evolution equations is demonstrated. To address instabilities in the classical solution, a Gaussian resummation of initial field fluctuations is performed in a scalar \(\phi^4\) toy model, implemented by solving classical field equations repeatedly with random initial conditions drawn from a Gaussian ensemble.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the quark-gluon plasma transition is \(T\sim 170\) MeV.
- The early universe was in a quark-gluon plasma state at times shorter than \(\tau\sim 10^{-5}\) seconds.
- RHIC operates at a maximum center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV per nucleon pair; the LHC operates at 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair.
- The momentum fraction \(x\) of partons involved in bulk particle production ranges from \(x\sim 10^{-2}\) at RHIC to \(x\sim 10^{-4}\) at the LHC.
- The gluon emission probability behaves as \(dP\sim \alpha_s dx/x\), and saturation occurs when the gluon occupation number reaches \(\alpha_s^{-1}\).
- Long-range rapidity correlations are expected over separations \(\Delta y\sim \alpha_s^{-1}\).
- Any process inducing a correlation between two particles with rapidity separation \(\Delta y\) must occur at a time \(\tau\) smaller than \(\tau_{\text{freeze out}}\, e^{-|\Delta y|/2}\).
- In the resummed scalar model, the pressure relaxes to the equilibrium equation of state \(p=\epsilon/3\) for a scale-invariant system in three spatial dimensions.

## Context

This talk extends the CGC framework developed by Iancu, Leonidov, and McLerran (2001) and the classical Yang-Mills approach of Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (2001) to describe the earliest moments of heavy ion collisions. It builds on the JIMWLK evolution equation for the distribution of color sources, previously established for deep inelastic scattering, and demonstrates its applicability to heavy ion collisions via factorization proofs by Gelis, Lappi, and Venugopalan (2008). The work addresses the long-standing puzzle of how the initially far-from-equilibrium matter with negative longitudinal pressure evolves toward thermalization, using a resummation technique previously applied in inflationary cosmology by Son (1996) and in Bose-Einstein condensation by Norrie, Ballagh, and Gardiner (2005). The results enable future studies of thermalization in longitudinally expanding systems with gauge fields, as recently pursued by Blaizot, Gelis, Liao, McLerran, and Venugopalan (2011).
