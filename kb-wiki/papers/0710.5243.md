# Neutrino emissivity and bulk viscosity of iso-CSL quark matter in neutron stars

> We present results for neutrino emissivities and bulk viscosities of a two-flavor color superconducting quark matter phase with isotropic color-spin-locked (iso-CSL) single-flavor pairing which fulfill the constraints on quark matter derived from cooling and rotational evolution of compact stars. We compare with results for the phenomenologically successful, but yet heuristic 2SC+X phase.

## Key claim

The iso-CSL (isotropic color-spin-locked) phase of two-flavor color superconducting quark matter can simultaneously satisfy observational constraints on both the cooling (via suppressed neutrino emissivity) and the rotational evolution (via sufficient bulk viscosity damping of r-mode instabilities) of compact stars, without requiring the heuristic assumptions of the previously studied 2SC+X phase.

## Method

The authors compute direct Urca neutrino emissivities and bulk viscosities for the iso-CSL phase using a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model that self-consistently determines the density and temperature dependence of quark masses, pairing gaps, and chemical potentials under neutron star constraints. They compare these results with those for the phenomenological 2SC+X phase, using the same parameter set with constituent quark mass M(p=0)=380 MeV. The bulk viscosity is calculated for a frequency of ω=1 kHz, typical for r-mode excitations in millisecond pulsars.

## Key numbers

- The lowest excitation energy in the iso-CSL phase is of order 1 MeV, as required from cooling phenomenology.
- The peak value of the bulk viscosity for both phases is located at T = 1–2 MeV, but is up to three orders of magnitude higher than in previous calculations by Sa'd (2006) that did not use selfconsistent treatment.
- The bulk viscosity shows a dramatic change at low quark chemical potentials due to the strongly density-dependent X-gap in the 2SC+X phase.
- The temperature dependence of the pairing gaps follows Δ(T)=Δ₀√(1-(T/T_c)^β) with β=1 used in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of single-flavor spin-one pairing channels by Schafer (2000), Alford (2002), and Schmitt (2004), and provides the first microscopic calculation of direct Urca emissivity and bulk viscosity for the iso-CSL phase. It contrasts with the 2SC+X phase introduced by Grigorian (2004), which had heuristic assumptions for the X-gap, and with the three-flavor 2SC phase considered by Alford (2006) where nonleptonic processes involving strange quarks dominate the bulk viscosity. The results enable future phenomenological applications in compact star cooling and spin evolution simulations, potentially distinguishing quark matter interiors from neutron star interiors.
