# $Ω$-dibaryon production with hadron interaction potential from the lattice QCD in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Recently the HAL QCD Collaboration reported the $Ω-Ω$ and $N-Ω$ interaction potentials by the lattice QCD simulations. Based on these results, $NΩ$ ($^5S_2$) and $ΩΩ$ ($^1S_0$) bound states were predicted with the binding energy about a few MeV. In addition, $N-Ω$ HBT correlation function was also measured by the STAR Collaboration as well as the ALICE Collaboration. These results provide dynamical information whether or not $Ω$-dibaryons exist in the interaction aspects. Another necessary point for the detection of $Ω$-dibaryons is the experimental environment where the bound state could be produced and survived in the system. In this context, there are at least two necessary conditions to constrain the production probability of $Ω$-dibaryons, i.e. the one is the necessary short-range attractive interaction to form the bound state and the another is the experimental environment such as heavy-ion collision provides abundant enough strangeness and multiplicity of nucleons. In this Letter the $Ω-Ω$ and $Ω-$nucleon interaction potentials by the lattice QCD simulations were employed to obtain $ΩΩ$ ($^1S_0$) and $NΩ$ ($^5S_2$) wave functions, and then the productions of $Ω$-dibaryons were estimated by using of a dynamical coalescence mechanism for the relativistic heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV.

## Key claim

The production probabilities of the $N\Omega$ ($^5S_2$) and $\Omega\Omega$ ($^1S_0$) dibaryons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are estimated to be about $10^{-3}$ and $10^{-6}$, respectively, using a dynamical coalescence model that incorporates the hadron interaction potentials from lattice QCD near the physical pion mass ($m_\pi = 146$ MeV). The authors note that these bound states require both a short-range attractive interaction and an experimental environment with sufficient strangeness and nucleon multiplicity.

## Method

The authors solved the radial Schrodinger equation using the $N-\Omega$ and $\Omega-\Omega$ interaction potentials from the HAL QCD Collaboration (Iritani et al., 2019; PhysRevLett.120.212001) to obtain the relative S-wave functions and binding energies for the $N\Omega$ ($^5S_2$) and $\Omega\Omega$ ($^1S_0$) states. These wave functions were then used to construct Wigner density functions for a dynamical coalescence model, which was applied to phase-space distributions from both a Blast-wave model (fitted to experimental $p$, $\Omega$, and deuteron $p_T$ spectra) and the AMPT transport model (version 2.26t7b) for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $p\Omega$ ($^5S_2$): 2.26 MeV (this work) vs. 2.46 MeV from Iritani et al. (2019), with Coulomb attraction included.
- Binding energy of $n\Omega$ ($^5S_2$): 1.38 MeV (this work) vs. 1.54 MeV from Iritani et al. (2019).
- Binding energy of $\Omega\Omega$ ($^1S_0$): 0.6 MeV (this work) vs. 0.7 MeV from PhysRevLett.120.212001, with Coulomb repulsion included.
- Predicted $dN/dy$ for $p\Omega$ at midrapidity in Au+Au at 200 GeV: $7.51 \times 10^{-4}$ (Blast-wave) and $9.5 \times 10^{-4}$ (AMPT).
- Predicted $dN/dy$ for $\Omega\Omega$ at midrapidity in Au+Au at 200 GeV: $0.31 \times 10^{-6}$ (Blast-wave) and $0.81 \times 10^{-6}$ (AMPT).
- Predicted $dN/dy$ for $p\Omega$ at midrapidity in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV: $1.31 \times 10^{-3}$ (Blast-wave) and $1.11 \times 10^{-3}$ (AMPT).
- Predicted $dN/dy$ for $\Omega\Omega$ at midrapidity in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV: $0.79 \times 10^{-6}$ (Blast-wave) and $1.1 \times 10^{-6}$ (AMPT).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of strangeness dibaryons, including the H-dibaryon prediction by Jaffe (1977) and earlier quark model calculations of $N\Omega$ and $\Omega\Omega$ states by Goldman et al. (1987) and others. It directly uses the lattice QCD potentials from the HAL QCD Collaboration (Iritani et al., 2019; PhysRevLett.120.212001) to provide a first-principles-based estimate of dibaryon production yields, going beyond earlier coalescence calculations that used simple harmonic oscillator wave functions. The results enable experimental searches for these bound states at the STAR and ALICE experiments, which have already measured $N-\Omega$ correlation functions, and provide a test of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions from lattice QCD.
