# Deep inelastic and dipole scattering on finite length hot $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM matter

> Deep inelastic scattering of $\mathcal{R}$-currents and the scattering of a small dipole on finite length hot $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM matter are discussed. In each case we find the scale when scattering becomes strong is determined by a saturation momentum $Q^2_s \sim LT^3/x$ where $L$ is the length of the matter. For $\mathcal{R}$-currents we analyze the operator product expansion. For infinite length matter the series generated by the OPE is not Borel summable but we are able to determine the exponential part of the tunneling amplitude determining $F_2$ when $\frac{Q^2}{Q^2_s}\gg 1$ from the position of the singularity closest to the origin on the real axis of the Borel plane. In finite length matter the OPE series is not convergent but it is Borel summable. When a small dipole, and the string connecting the ends of the dipole, pass through hot matter there is an induced motion of the string in the $5^{th}$ dimension. When $T^4 L \cosh η$, with the $η$ the rapidity of the string, is large enough the string would normally break into several parts after leaving the medium, however, this cannot happen in the classical approximation in which we work.

## Key claim

Deep inelastic scattering of ℛ-currents and dipole scattering on finite length hot 𝒩=4 SYM matter become strong when the virtuality Q² is less than a saturation momentum Q²_s ∼ LT³/x, where L is the length of the matter; this x-dependence differs from the infinite matter case where Q²_s ∼ T²/x², but the two formulas agree when L is replaced by the natural coherence length 1/(xT).

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute structure functions F₁ and F₂ for ℛ-current scattering by evaluating the forward scattering amplitude of gravitational waves in an AdS-black hole metric, using an eikonal approximation valid when the coherence time of the current is much larger than the matter length L. For dipole scattering, they treat a string (whose ends are attached to the boundary) passing through a finite-length region of AdS-black hole metric, solving the Nambu-Goto action in two ways: a "one graviton" approximation expanding about the AdS₅ metric, and an exact treatment using energy conservation at the boundaries of the black hole region.

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum: Q²_s ∼ LT³/x, where L is the length of the hot matter and T is the temperature.
- For infinite matter, the tunneling amplitude determining F₂ when Q²/Q²_s ≫ 1 is proportional to exp[-c √(K³/k)], with c = 2Γ²(1/4)/(3√(2π)), K = q/(2πT), and k = q/(2πT).
- In the saturation regime (Q² ≪ Q²_s), F₁ = N²Q²/(128π³ x) ln(Q²_s/Q²) and F_L = F₂ − 2xF₁ = N²Q²/(32π³).
- For the string passing through the medium, the induced change in velocity in the 5th dimension is Δ\dot{u} = −2u₀⁴L/u, where u₀ = πT.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of deep inelastic scattering on infinite hot 𝒩=4 SYM matter by Hatta, Iancu, and Mueller (2007) to finite-length matter, finding that the saturation momentum acquires an x-dependence from graviton exchange (Q²_s ∼ LT³/x) rather than the infinite-matter form (Q²_s ∼ T²/x²). The dipole scattering calculation builds on the string-medium interaction framework pioneered by Albacete, Kovchegov, and Taliotis (2008), but assumes the string has insufficient time to change its shape while passing through the medium, in contrast to their assumption of relaxation to a static configuration. The results enable a parton interpretation where occupation numbers in the saturation region are parametrically of order one, and suggest that when Q_s/u_m > 1 the string would normally break into multiple parts (corresponding to radiation), though this cannot occur in the classical approximation used.
