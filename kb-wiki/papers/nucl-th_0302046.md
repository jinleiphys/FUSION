# Reaction Rates and Nuclear Properties Relevant for Nucleosynthesis in Massive Stars and Far From Stability

> Explosive nuclear burning in astrophysical environments produces unstable nuclei which again can be targets for subsequent reactions. In addition, it involves a large number of stable nuclides which are not fully explored by experiments, yet. Thus, it is necessary to be able to predict reaction cross sections and thermonuclear rates with the aid of theoretical models. Such predictions are also of interest for investigations at radioactive ion beam facilities. An extended library of theoretical cross sections and reaction rates is presented. The problem of alpha+nucleus potentials is addressed and new parametrizations presented. The problem of properly predicting cross sections at low level densities is illustrated by the 62Ni(n,gamma) reaction.

# Reaction Rates and Nuclear Properties Relevant for Nucleosynthesis in Massive Stars and Far From Stability

## Key claim
The authors present an extended library of theoretical cross sections and reaction rates for about 4600 target nuclei and 32000 reactions, but acknowledge that the accuracy of predictions is limited by poorly constrained nuclear inputs such as alpha-nucleus optical potentials and low level densities, particularly far from stability.

## Method
Statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) calculations were performed using the NON-SMOKER code for neutron-, proton-, and alpha-induced reactions on all target nuclei from neon to bismuth, from the proton-dripline to the neutron-dripline. New parametrizations of alpha-nucleus optical potentials were derived by simultaneously fitting data for three reactions in the A ≈ 140 mass region: 143Nd(n,alpha)140Ce, 147Sm(n,alpha)144Nd, and 144Sm(alpha,gamma)148Gd. The 62Ni(n,gamma) reaction was analyzed using direct capture calculations with DWBA including p-wave contributions and sub-threshold resonances.

## Key numbers
- The reaction rate library covers approximately 4600 target nuclei and 32000 reactions from Ne up to Bi.
- For the Brown-Rho energy-dependent alpha-nucleus potential fit, the best-fit parameters were: E0 = 6.35 MeV, Δ = 28.4 MeV, V0 = 162 MeV, rr = 1.27 fm, ar = 0.48 fm, W0 = 19 MeV, rV = 1.57 fm, aV = 0.6 fm.
- For the Fermi-type energy-dependent alpha-nucleus potential fit, the parameters were: E* = 18.74 MeV, a* = 2.1 MeV.
- An energy-independent Saxon-Woods potential was found with real parameters V0 = 162.3 MeV, rr = 1.27 fm, ar = 0.48 fm and imaginary parameters W0 = 25 MeV, rV = 1.4 fm, aV = 0.52 fm.
- For 62Ni(n,gamma), resonances contribute less than 15% to the cross section at 30 keV.

## Context
This work extends the large-scale reaction rate library previously published by Rauscher and Thielemann (2000, 2001), which has been adopted as a standard for nucleosynthesis in stellar evolution and type II supernovae. The alpha-nucleus potential analysis builds on prior work by Somorjai et al. and McFadden and Satchler, while addressing the scarcity of global potentials at astrophysical energies. The 62Ni(n,gamma) result resolves a previously seen overproduction of 62Ni in stellar models of massive stars by providing an enhanced neutron capture rate that increases destruction of this nucleus. Future work requires improved nuclear property predictions far from stability and experimental data from Rare Isotope Accelerators, as well as continued classical experiments with stable nuclei to provide systematics for global descriptions.
