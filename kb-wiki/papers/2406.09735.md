# Spontaneous fission half-life in Fm isotopes with nuclear energy density functional

> A microscopic description of fission dynamics is important to understand the decay properties of neutron-rich heavy nuclei that are relevant to $r$-process nucleosynthesis. To provide a reliable and efficient method to evaluate the spontaneous fission half-life, we develop a method, called the constrained Hartree--Fock--Bogoliubov (CHFB) plus local quasiparticle random-phase approximation (LQRPA), to include dynamical residual effects in the collective inertia. With the CHFB + LQRPA, we evaluate the collective potential and the collective inertia along a mass-symmetric fission path in Fm isotopes with the neutron numbers $N=158$--164. The obtained LQRPA inertia is much larger than the cranking one that ignores dynamical residual effects and shows a remarkable variation along the fission path. We estimate the fission half-life of the Fm isotopes using the action integral with the obtained collective potential and inertia. A large difference between the fission half-lives obtained with the LQRPA inertia and with the cranking inertia is observed. This indicates the importance of evaluating the collective inertia for estimating the fission half-life.

## Key claim

The CHFB + LQRPA method, which includes dynamical residual effects in the collective inertia, produces spontaneous fission half-lives for Fm isotopes that are 4–6 orders of magnitude larger than those obtained with the cranking inertia that ignores these effects; however, the LQRPA results still deviate from experimental data by several orders of magnitude, indicating room for improvement through zero-point energy corrections or inclusion of additional collective variables.

## Method

The constrained Hartree–Fock–Bogoliubov (CHFB) method was used to compute the collective potential along a mass-symmetric fission path (using the axially symmetric mass quadrupole operator Q20 as the collective coordinate) for even–even Fm isotopes with neutron numbers N=158–164. The local quasiparticle random-phase approximation (LQRPA), solved via the finite amplitude method (FAM) with contour integration, was then applied on top of each CHFB state to obtain the collective inertia including dynamical residual effects. The spontaneous fission half-life was estimated using the WKB approximation for the action integral with the collective potential and inertia, employing an assault frequency of n=10^20.38 s^-1.

## Key numbers

- The LQRPA collective inertia is much larger than the cranking inertia at all Q20 values for 258Fm, with a sharp increase at Q20 ≈ 30 b.
- The fission half-life obtained with the LQRPA inertia is 4–6 orders of magnitude larger than that obtained with the cranking inertia for the Fm isotopes studied.
- Adding a zero-point energy correction of E0 = 0.5 MeV to the ground-state energy decreases the fission half-life by about 3 orders of magnitude.
- The fission barrier height decreases as neutron number increases among 258Fm, 260Fm, 262Fm, and 264Fm, with the energy becoming negative at Q20 ≥ 105 b.
- Including triaxial deformations (Q22 > 0) reduces the fission barrier height by about 3 MeV for 258Fm compared to the axially symmetric path.
- The CHFB + LQRPA calculations were performed for Q20 ≤ 110 b with ΔQ20 = 5 b, using a numerical box of 11.5 fm × 11.5 fm × 16.5 fm with 1.0 fm mesh size.
- The single-particle basis consisted of 1820 neutron and 1440 proton Hartree–Fock basis states, achieving maximum quasiparticle energy EQPmax ≈ 60 MeV.
- The most collective LQRPA mode was selected among modes with squared eigen-frequencies Ωi^2 < 16 MeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the CHFB + LQRPA method previously applied to 240Pu and 256Fm (Washiyama, 2021) to a systematic study of Fm isotopes where mass-symmetric fission is experimentally observed for N ≥ 158 (Lane, 1996). The approach contrasts with earlier EDF-based fission studies (Staszczak, 2009; Sadhukhan, 2013; Rodriguez-Guzman, 2014) that used the cranking approximation for collective inertia, which ignores dynamical residual effects (Dobaczewski, 1981). The results enable future improvements by incorporating zero-point energy corrections consistent with the assault frequency, and by extending to multi-dimensional collective spaces including triaxiality (Q22), which former works (Warda, 2002; Staszczak, 2009; Abusara, 2010) showed reduces fission barrier heights by a few MeV in Fm isotopes.
