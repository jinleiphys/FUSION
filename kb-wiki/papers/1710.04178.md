# Wilsonian renormalization group and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a multitude of cutoff parameters

> The Wilsonian renormalization group approach to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a multitude of cutoff parameters is introduced. A system of integro-differential equations for the cutoff-dependent potential is obtained. As an illustration, a perturbative solution of these equations with two cutoff parameters for a simple case of an S-wave low-energy potential in the form of a Taylor series in momenta is obtained. The relevance of the obtained results for the effective field theory approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Wilsonian renormalization group (RG) approach to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation by introducing a multitude of cutoff parameters, and demonstrates that with two cutoffs the resulting potential can obey Weinberg's power counting for nucleon-nucleon scattering, whereas a single cutoff yields the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) counting. The key qualifier is that this result holds for a perturbative solution in momenta and energy, with the potential expanded as a Taylor series, and for a system close to the unitary limit (scattering length \(a \gg \Lambda_{\text{hard}}^{-1}\)).

## Method

The authors start from the fully off-shell Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-particle scattering and decompose the potential and T-matrix into sums of components associated with \(N\) cutoff parameters \(\Lambda_i, \bar\Lambda_i\). They derive a system of \(2N\) integro-differential RG equations for the cutoff-dependent potential matrix \(\mathcal{V}\) by demanding that the low-energy off-shell amplitude remains cutoff-independent. As an illustration, they solve these equations perturbatively for the case of two Hermitean cutoffs (\(\Lambda_1, \Lambda_2\) with \(\Lambda_2 < \Lambda_1\)) using an S-wave potential expanded as a Taylor series in momenta, and match the resulting on-shell amplitude to the effective range expansion.

## Key numbers

* The LO potential matrix element is \(V_{11,\text{LO}} = \frac{4 a \pi^2}{m \left(-a k \ln \frac{\Lambda_1-k}{\Lambda_1+k} - 2 a \Lambda_1 + \pi\right)}\), where \(a\) is the scattering length, \(m\) the particle mass, and \(\Lambda_1\) the larger cutoff.
* The NLO potential includes a term proportional to \(q^2 c_{20} / (a k \ln \frac{\Lambda_1-k}{\Lambda_1+k} + 2 a \Lambda_1 - \pi)\), with \(c_{20}\) an undetermined coefficient of order \(1/\Lambda_{\text{hard}}^3\).
* The matching fixes \(c_{10} = \pi/(2a)\) and \(c_{31} = -\pi/a - \pi^3 r/(c_{20} m)\), where \(r\) is the effective range.
* The LO on-shell amplitude is \(T_{\text{LO}}(k) = -\frac{4\pi}{m} \frac{1}{-1/a - i k}\), and the NLO correction is \(T_{\text{NLO}}(k) = \frac{2\pi}{m} \frac{r k^2}{(-1/a - i k)^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the single-cutoff Wilsonian RG analysis of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation pioneered by Birse (1998) to multiple cutoffs, addressing a limitation noted in the authors' prior paper (Epelbaum, Gegelia, Meißner, 2017) where the standard Wilsonian RG was found incompatible with Weinberg's power counting. The results enable the application of the multi-cutoff formalism to chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering, particularly for potentials with long-range interactions, which the authors state is work in progress.
