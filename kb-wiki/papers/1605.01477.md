# Structure of $^{78}$Ni from first principles computations

> Doubly magic nuclei have a simple structure and are the cornerstones for entire regions of the nuclear chart. Theoretical insights into the supposedly doubly magic $^{78}$Ni and its neighbors are challenging because of the extreme neutron-to-proton ratio and the proximity of the continuum. We predict the $J^π=2_1^+$ state in $^{78}$Ni from a correlation with the $J^π=2_1^+$ state in $^{48}$Ca using chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions. Our results confirm that $^{78}$Ni is doubly magic, and the predicted low-lying states of $^{79,80}$Ni open the way for shell-model studies of many more rare isotopes.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the first excited 2+ state in 78Ni lies between 2.1 MeV and 3.1 MeV, based on a correlation with the same state in 48Ca computed using a family of chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions; this prediction is hedged by noting that the origin of the correlation is not understood theoretically and that only two of the five employed interactions reproduce the 48Ca 2+ energy within uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-cluster singles-doubles (CCSD) and equation-of-motion (EOM) coupled-cluster calculations including perturbative triples corrections (EOM-CCSD(T)) for 48Ca and 78Ni, using five different chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions from Hebeler (2011) and Ekstrom (2015). They employed a Hartree-Fock basis from a harmonic oscillator basis of up to 15 major shells, and for 79Ni used a Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis with a Berggren basis to include continuum effects. The 2+ state energies in 48Ca and 78Ni were correlated via a linear fit to obtain a predicted range for 78Ni.

## Key numbers

- The predicted range for the 2+ state in 78Ni is 2.1 MeV to 3.1 MeV, obtained from a linear fit to the correlation with 48Ca.
- For the interaction "1.8/2.0 (EM)", the computed binding energy of 78Ni is 637(4) MeV, compared to 641 MeV from systematic trends.
- The computed binding energies for 4He, 16O, 40Ca, and 48Ca using the "1.8/2.0 (EM)" interaction are 28.2 MeV, 128 MeV, 348 MeV, and 419 MeV, respectively, close to experimental values of 28.2 MeV, 128 MeV, 342 MeV, and 416 MeV.
- The neutron separation energy for 78Ni is approximately 4.5 MeV, and for 79Ni is approximately 1 MeV, consistent with systematic values of 5450(950) keV and 1650(1130) keV from Wang (2012).
- The two-neutron separation energy for 80Ni is approximately 2 MeV, setting the neutron dripline beyond 80Ni.
- The 2+ state in 80Ni is computed to be 0.7 MeV above its ground state.
- The ratio of the excited 4+ state to the 2+ state in 78Ni is 1.2.

## Context

This work extends first-principles coupled-cluster calculations to the extremely neutron-rich nucleus 78Ni, building on prior studies of calcium isotopes by Hagen (2012, 2015) and nickel isotopes by Soma (2013) and Hergert (2014). The correlation method between 48Ca and 78Ni follows similar approaches used by Bogner (2008), Reinhard (2010), and Hagen (2015), though the authors note the correlation is not theoretically understood. The predictions for 78Ni and its neighbors 77,79,80Ni enable future shell-model studies and experimental comparisons, particularly with recent measurements at RIBF, RIKEN by Taniuchi (2016).
