# anti-K anti-K N molecule state in three-body calculation

> A $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ system with $I=1/2$ and $J^P=1/2^+$ is investigated with non-relativistic three-body calculations by using effective $\bar{K}N$ and $\bar K \bar K$ interactions. The present investigation suggests that a weakly bound state for the $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ system can be formed below the two-body threshold of $\bar{K}$ and quasibound $\bar{K}N$ with a $40 \sim 60$ MeV decay width of $\bar{K}\bar{K}N\to πY\bar{K}$. This corresponds to an excited $Ξ$ baryon with $J^P=1/2^+$ located around 1.9 GeV. Studying the wave function of the $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ system obtained in this formulation, we find that the three-body bound system has a characteristic structure of $\bar K N$+$\bar K$ cluster with spatial extent.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a weakly bound state for the \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\) system can be formed below the two-body threshold of \(\bar{K}\) and quasibound \(\bar{K}N\), but this result depends on the choice of effective \(\bar{K}N\) potential and the binding is only about 1 MeV below threshold for the weaker HW-HNJH potential, making it a marginal bound state.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic three-body calculations for the \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\) system with \(I=1/2\) and \(J^P=1/2^+\) using a variational approach with the Gaussian expansion method. They employed two different effective \(\bar{K}N\) interactions (the HW-HNJH potential derived from chiral unitary approach and the AY potential fitted to the \(\Lambda(1405)\) as a \(\bar{K}N\) bound state) and treated the imaginary parts of the potentials perturbatively to estimate decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The \(\bar{K}N\) quasibound state with the HW-HNJH potential is found at 1423 MeV with a width of 44 MeV, located 11 MeV below the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold (1434 MeV), with a root-mean-square \(\bar{K}\)-\(N\) distance of 1.9 fm.
- The \(\bar{K}N\) quasibound state with the AY potential is found at 1403 MeV with a width of 40 MeV, with a root-mean-square \(\bar{K}\)-\(N\) distance of 1.4 fm.
- For the HW-HNJH potential, the \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\) bound state appears about 1 MeV below the \(\bar{K}\) + quasibound \(\bar{K}N\) threshold, with a \(\bar{K}\) root-mean-square radius of about 4 fm.
- For the AY potential, the \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\) binding energy is 36 MeV (5 MeV below the \(\bar{K}N\)+\(\bar{K}\) threshold of 31 MeV), with a \(\bar{K}\) root-mean-square radius of 2 fm.
- The decay width \(\Gamma(\bar{K}\bar{K}N \rightarrow \pi Y \bar{K})\) is in the range of 51 to 57 MeV for both potentials.
- The \(K^+K^+\) scattering length from lattice QCD is \(a_{K^+K^+} = -0.141 \pm 0.006\) fm, used to calibrate the \(\bar{K}\bar{K}\) repulsive interaction.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of multi-kaon nuclear systems, motivated by the strong \(\bar{K}N(I=0)\) attraction that produces the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance as a quasibound state. It builds on prior Faddeev and variational calculations of the \(K^-pp\) system by Shevchenko (2007), Ikeda (2007), Yamazaki (2007), and Dote (2008), and uses effective potentials from Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and Hyodo and Weise (2007). The results suggest that an excited \(\Xi\) baryon with \(J^P=1/2^+\) around 1.9 GeV could be observed in double strangeness exchange processes at J-PARC, though the binding is weak and sensitive to the \(\bar{K}N\) interaction strength.
