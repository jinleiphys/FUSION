# Microscopic Nuclear Equation of State with Three-Body Forces and Neutron Star Structure

> We calculate static properties of non-rotating neutron stars (NS's) using a microscopic equation of state (EOS) for asymmetric nuclear matter. The EOS is computed in the framework of the Brueckner--Bethe--Goldstone many--body theory. We introduce three-body forces in order to reproduce the correct saturation point of nuclear matter. A microscopic well behaved EOS is derived. We obtain a maximum mass configuration with $M_{max} = 1.8 M_\odot$, a radius $R = 9.7$ km and a central density $n_c = 1.34~fm^{-3}$. We find the proton fraction exceeds the critical value $x^{Urca}$, for the onset of direct Urca processes, at densities $n \geq 0.45~fm^{-3}$. Therefore, in our model, NS's with masses above $M^{Urca} = 0.96 M_\odot$ can undergo very rapid cooling depending on whether or not nucleon superfluidity in the interior of the NS takes place. A comparison with other microscopic models for the EOS is done, and neutron star structure is calculated for these models too.

# Microscopic Nuclear Equation of State with Three-Body Forces and Neutron Star Structure

## Key claim

The authors derive a microscopic equation of state (BHF3) for asymmetric nuclear matter using Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory with three-body forces, and find that neutron stars with masses above 0.96 solar masses can undergo very rapid cooling via direct Urca processes, provided nucleon superfluidity does not suppress those processes.

## Method

The equation of state was computed in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation within the continuous choice, using the Argonne v14 two-body potential plus a set of three-body forces whose parameters were adjusted to reproduce the correct saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter. The resulting EOS was then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star mass and radius as functions of central density, with the outer crust described by the Feynman-Metropolis-Teller and Baym-Pethick-Sutherland EOS and the middle-density regime by Negele and Vautherin results.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: M_max = 1.8 solar masses
- Radius at maximum mass: R = 9.7 km
- Central density at maximum mass: n_c = 1.34 fm^{-3}
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: n_0 = 0.18 fm^{-3} with binding energy E = -15.88 MeV
- Incompressibility at saturation: K_infinity = 240 MeV
- Critical density for direct Urca processes: n^{Urca} = 0.447 fm^{-3}
- Critical proton fraction for direct Urca: x^{Urca} = 13.6%
- Threshold mass for direct Urca cooling: M^{Urca} = 0.96 solar masses
- Proton fraction at center of maximum mass configuration: x = 26%

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body approach by introducing three-body forces to correct the well-known failure of non-relativistic calculations based on purely two-body interactions to reproduce the correct saturation point of nuclear matter, a deficiency previously addressed in variational calculations by Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988). The BHF3 EOS satisfies causality and other physical requirements that distinguish it from other non-relativistic microscopic EOS, and enables future self-consistent microscopic calculations of both neutron star structure and nuclear superfluid properties within the same many-body framework and nuclear interaction.
