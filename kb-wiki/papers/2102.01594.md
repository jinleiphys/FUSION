# First Saturation Correction in High Energy Proton-Nucleus Collisions: I. Time evolution of classical Yang-Mills fields beyond leading order

> In high energy proton-nucleus collisions, the single- and double-inclusive soft gluon productions at the leading order have been calculated and phenomenologically studied in various approaches for many years. These studies do not take into account the saturation and multiple rescatterings in the field of the proton. The first saturation correction to these leading order results (the terms that are enhanced by the combination $α_s^2 μ^2$, where $μ^2$ is the proton's color charge squared per unit transverse area) has not been completely derived despite recent attempts using a diagrammatic approach. This paper is the first in a series of papers towards analytically completing the first saturation correction to physical observables in high energy proton-nucleus collisions. Our approach is to analytically solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the dilute-dense regime using the Color Glass Condensate effective theory and compute physical observables constructed from classical gluon fields. In the current paper, the Yang-Mills equations are solved perturbatively in the field of the dilute object (the proton). Next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-leading order analytic solutions are explicitly constructed. A systematic way to obtain all higher order analytic solutions is outlined

## Key claim

This paper analytically solves the classical Yang-Mills equations in the dilute-dense regime of proton-nucleus collisions to next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-leading order in the proton's color charge density, providing the first complete derivation of the first saturation correction (terms enhanced by α_s² μ², where μ² is the proton's color charge squared per unit transverse area) that has been only partially derived in prior diagrammatic approaches.

## Method

The authors solve the sourceless classical Yang-Mills equations in the forward light cone (x⁺>0, x⁻>0) using the Fock-Schwinger gauge x⁻A⁺ + x⁺A⁻ = 0, with boost-invariant fields parameterized as A⁺ = x⁺α(τ, x), A⁻ = -x⁻α(τ, x), and Aⁱ = αⁱ(τ, x). The initial conditions at τ=0 are expanded perturbatively in the proton's color charge density ρ_P (treating the proton as dilute and the nucleus as dense), with the Weizsäcker-Williams fields of the projectile expanded to order g⁵ and the Yang-Mills equations solved order by order using the method of variation of parameters and Graf's formula for Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The leading order gluon production amplitude is proportional to gN_⊥, where N_⊥ ~ √(A^(1/3)) is the number of color sources per unit area; the amplitude squared scales as g²N_⊥² ~ α_s A^(1/3)
- At order g³, the saturation correction (diagrams proportional to g³N_⊥²) yields an amplitude squared proportional to g⁶N_⊥⁴ ~ α_s³ A^(2/3)
- At order g⁵, the saturation correction (diagrams proportional to g⁵N_⊥³) yields an amplitude squared proportional to g¹⁰N_⊥⁶ ~ α_s⁵ A
- The saturation scale Q_s is related to the color charge density by Q_s² ~ (g²μ)² ~ α_s² A^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the partial calculations of the first saturation correction to single- and double-inclusive gluon production in proton-nucleus collisions that were begun in Chirilli (2015) using light-cone perturbation theory and in McLerran (2016) using classical Yang-Mills equations, both of which only computed the order-g³ production amplitude. The paper is the first in a series aiming to complete the first saturation corrections (f₂ and h₂ in the expansion in projectile saturation momentum) and ultimately enable resummation of all saturation corrections to solve the dense-dense scattering problem analytically, which remains an unsolved problem in high energy QCD.
