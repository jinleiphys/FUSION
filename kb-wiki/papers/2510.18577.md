# $╬▒$-decay systematics for superheavy nucleus: the effect of deformation of daughter nucleus

> Recently, V.Yu. Denisov proposed a new empirical formula incorporating the deformation of the daughter nucleus, which has significantly improved the description of $╬▒$-decay half-lives for even-even nuclei compared to formulas neglecting the deformation of the daughter nucleus. In this work, we generalize the deformation of the daughter nucleus proposed by V.Yu. Denisov to the DUR model, the AKRA model, the New Geiger-Nuttall law, generating three improved versions of these models. We then employ both the original and modified the DUR model, the AKRA model, and the New Geiger-Nuttall law to investigate the $╬▒$-decay half-lives of 400 isotopes. Results show that among the six models, the modified AKRA model provides the closest agreement with experimental $╬▒$-decay half-life data. For comparative analysis, we use the new empirical formulas developed for the DUR model (DUR+D), the AKRA model (AKRA+D) and a new empirical (ND) proposed by V.Yu. Denisov to predict the $╬▒$-decay properties of 71 even-even nuclei with Z = 118, 120, 122, and 124. The predictions from the DUR+D model, the AKRA+D model, and ND are largely consistent overall. Notably, when the neutron number $N>190$, the predictions from the DUR+D model and the AKRA+D model exceed those of ND, which may be attributed to additional physical contributions (e.g., the hexadecapole and the hexacontatetrapole deformation of the deformed daughter nucleus) incorporated in the DUR+D model and the AKRA+D model but not in ND.

# ╬▒-decay systematics for superheavy nucleus: the effect of deformation of daughter nucleus

## Key claim

Incorporating hexadecapole (╬▓Рѓё) and hexacontatetrapole (╬▓Рѓє) deformations of the daughter nucleus into three empirical ╬▒-decay models (DUR, AKRA, and New Geiger-Nuttall law) improves half-life predictions, with the modified AKRA model (AKRA+D) showing the best agreement with experimental data; however, the model does not include deformation-induced changes in the ╬▒-decay energy Q╬▒, and available deformation parameters remain insufficiently accurate for superheavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors extended V. Yu. Denisov's deformation-dependent empirical formula by adding a term incorporating quadrupole (╬▓Рѓѓ), hexadecapole (╬▓Рѓё), and hexacontatetrapole (╬▓Рѓє) deformations of the daughter nucleus into three existing models: the DUR model, the AKRA model, and the New Geiger-Nuttall law (NGN). They fitted the coefficients of all six models (three original and three deformation-extended versions: DUR+D, AKRA+D, NGN+D) to experimental ╬▒-decay half-lives of 400 nuclei partitioned into even-even, even-odd, odd-even, and odd-odd groups, using WS4 deformation parameters. They then applied five models (DUR+D, AKRA+D, ND, Improved+UL, and Improved+EF) to predict half-lives for 71 even-even superheavy nuclei with Z = 118, 120, 122, and 124.

## Key numbers

- The AKRA+D model reduced the root-mean-square deviation between calculated and experimental ╬▒-decay half-lives by 22% for even-even nuclei, 5% for odd-odd nuclei, 4.7% for odd-even nuclei, and 14.7% for another odd-odd subset, compared to the original AKRA model.
- The study analyzed 400 nuclei across four parity types and predicted half-lives for 71 even-even nuclei with Z = 118, 120, 122, and 124.
- Discontinuities between calculated and experimental values were observed at neutron numbers N = 126 and N = 152 for all models.
- Predictions for the 71 even-even nuclei showed distinct behavior at parent neutron numbers N = 180 and N = 186, suggesting possible magic numbers at daughter neutron numbers N_daughter = 178 and N_daughter = 184.
- The deformation term in the empirical formulas takes the form e(|Max(╬▓РѓѓYРѓѓРѓђ + ╬▓РѓёYРѓёРѓђ + ╬▓РѓєYРѓєРѓђ)|)^(1/2) ├Ќ Z/Q^(1/2), where Q is the ╬▒-decay energy in MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of V. Yu. Denisov (2024), who introduced quadrupole deformation into empirical ╬▒-decay formulas and reduced RMS deviations by approximately 23% for even-even nuclei, by adding hexadecapole and hexacontatetrapole deformations. It builds upon the DUR model by Deng et al. (2020), the AKRA model by Akrawy and Poenaru (2017) which includes isospin asymmetry, and the New Geiger-Nuttall law by Ren and Ren (2012). The results enable improved predictions for ╬▒-decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei with Z = 118РђЊ124, providing guidance for future experiments aimed at synthesizing new elements, while also identifying the need for models that couple deformation effects on both the Coulomb barrier and Q╬▒ values.
