# Characterizing the astrophysical S-factor for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C with wave-packet dynamics

> A quantitative study of the astrophysically important sub-barrier fusion of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C is presented. Low-energy collisions are described in the body-fixed reference frame using wave-packet dynamics within a nuclear molecular picture. A collective Hamiltonian drives the time propagation of the wave-packet through the collective potential-energy landscape. The fusion imaginary potential for specific dinuclear configurations is crucial for understanding the appearance of resonances in the fusion cross section. The theoretical sub-barrier fusion cross sections explain some observed resonant structures in the astrophysical S-factor. These cross sections monotonically decline towards stellar energies. The structures in the data that are not explained are possibly due to cluster effects in the nuclear molecule, which are to be included in the present approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the resonant structures observed in the astrophysical S-factor for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C sub-barrier fusion can be qualitatively reproduced by a wave-packet dynamics model that treats specific alignments between the two oblate $^{12}$C nuclei, but only when the fusion imaginary potential is localized around the Pole-Pole dinuclear configuration; the model does not explain all observed structures, and the predicted S-factor monotonically declines towards stellar energies.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation on a five-dimensional grid (radial coordinate plus two sets of spherical angles for the $^{12}$C symmetry axes) using a collective Hamiltonian that includes the Coriolis interaction. The initial wave function is a Gaussian wave-packet representing the two $^{12}$C nuclei in their $0^+$ ground states, propagated with a modified Chebyshev propagator. Energy-resolved transmission coefficients are extracted via a window-operator method, and the fusion cross section is obtained by summing over even partial waves up to $J=6\hbar$, with an imaginary Woods-Saxon fusion potential centered at $R^{PP}_{min}=3.7$ fm (strength $W_0=-50$ MeV, diffuseness $a_{0w}=0.2$ fm).

## Key numbers

- The oblate deformation of $^{12}$C is $\beta_2 = -0.5$ with moment of inertia $I = 0.67 \, \hbar^2 \text{MeV}^{-1}$, derived from the experimental $2^+$ excitation energy of 4.44 MeV.
- The imaginary fusion potential has strength $W_0 = -50$ MeV, diffuseness $a_{0w} = 0.2$ fm, and is centered at $R^{PP}_{min} = 3.7$ fm.
- The initial wave-packet is centered at $R_0 = 400$ fm with width $\sigma = 10$ fm; the time step is $\Delta t = 10^{-22}$ s.
- The energy window operator uses $\epsilon = 50$ keV and $n=2$.
- The theoretical resonant maxima from phase-shift analysis are at 4.45 MeV ($J=0$), 5.10 MeV ($J=2$), and 6.56 MeV ($J=4$).
- The theoretical maxima are shifted by approximately 0.3 MeV relative to experimental maxima; multiplying the potential landscape by a global factor of 0.98 improves the shift by about 0.1 MeV.
- Reducing the curvature of the potential pockets by 15% significantly improves the location of predicted resonant structures.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent wave-packet approach previously applied to heavy-ion fusion (Boselli, 2014; Yabana, 1994) to the specific case of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, addressing the long-standing problem of resonant structures in the sub-barrier fusion cross section. It contrasts with conventional coupled-channels calculations (Esbensen, 2011; Pierre, 2014) which produce smooth excitation functions without resonances, and with the simplified coupled-channels model of Abe (1978) that used weak absorption. The results suggest that molecular doorway states in non-axial symmetric dinuclear configurations are responsible for the observed resonances, and that future inclusion of cluster effects (e.g., $^{20}$Ne+alpha, $^{23}$Na+p) and vibrational degrees of freedom may explain remaining structures not captured by the present model.
