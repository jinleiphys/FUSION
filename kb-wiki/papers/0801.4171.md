# Time-dependent Green's functions approach to nuclear reactions

> Nonequilibrium Green's functions represent underutilized means of studying the time evolution of quantum many-body systems. In view of a rising computer power, an effort is underway to apply the Green's functions formalism to the dynamics of central nuclear reactions. As the first step, mean-field evolution for the density matrix for colliding slabs is studied in one dimension. The strategy to extend the dynamics to correlations is described.

## Key claim

The paper presents a time-dependent Green's functions formalism as a quantal alternative to semiclassical approaches for central nuclear reactions, but the actual work is limited to a one-dimensional mean-field (TDHF) study of colliding slabs as a first step, with the inclusion of correlations beyond mean-field only described as a future strategy.

## Method

The authors solve the Kadanoff-Baym (KB) equations in the mean-field (Hartree-Fock) approximation for one-dimensional nuclear slabs, using a Skyrme-type local density-dependent mean-field with parameters fitted to saturation properties of infinite nuclear matter. The time evolution of the density matrix is implemented via the Split Operator Method (SOM) using fast Fourier transforms to switch between real-space and momentum-space representations. The initial ground-state density matrix for each slab is prepared by adiabatically switching off a harmonic oscillator potential while switching on the mean-field, after which the slabs are boosted toward each other by transforming the density matrices with operators e^(±iPx).

## Key numbers

- The slabs are boosted with a center-of-mass energy of **25 MeV per particle**.
- The normal nuclear matter density used for scaling is **n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³**.
- The spin-isospin degeneracy is **ν = 4**.
- The time evolution is shown at **t = 30 fm/c** (when slabs interpenetrate) and **t = 60 fm/c** (when slabs move apart).

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method (Bonche 1976) by reformulating it in terms of density matrices rather than single-particle wave-functions, as a foundation for including correlations via the KB equations. The approach aims to overcome limitations of semiclassical central-reaction models and TDHF, which cannot describe thermalization or enhanced stopping from correlations beyond mean-field. The authors plan to implement the direct Born approximation for the self-energy using a Gaussian two-body interaction, following prior implementations for uniform matter (Danielewicz 1984, Kohler 1999), and expect to gain insights into how correlations modify dynamics compared to mean-field or semiclassical treatments.
