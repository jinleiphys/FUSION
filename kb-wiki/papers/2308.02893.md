# Candidates for three-quasiparticle $K$-isomers in odd-even Md-Rg nuclei

> We performed a search for three-quasiparticle high-$K$ isomer candidates in odd-even Md - Rg nuclei by considering the lowest lying 1$π$2$ν$ and 3$π$ excitations. Our approach involves calculating the energies of different nuclear configurations using a microscopic-macroscopic model with the Woods-Saxon potential. We explore three pairing scenarios: blocking, quasi-particle BCS method, and particle number projection formalism. The optimal deformations for both ground-states and high-$K$ configurations are determined through a four-dimensional energy minimization process. By analyzing the obtained excitation energies, we discuss the most promising candidates for high-$K$ isomers and compare them, where possible, with existing experimental data. We also discuss a possible isomer $α$-decay hindrance using calculated $Q_α$-hindrances.

## Key claim

The paper identifies candidates for three-quasiparticle high-\(K\) isomers in odd-even Md through Rg nuclei by calculating excitation energies of \(1\pi2\nu\) and \(3\pi\) configurations, but the authors note that a precise prediction would require reliable estimates of electromagnetic transition probabilities, which are beyond the reach of current theory for heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic-macroscopic model with the Woods-Saxon potential and a four-dimensional energy minimization over \(\beta_{20}, \beta_{40}, \beta_{60}, \beta_{80}\) deformations to calculate ground-state and excited configuration energies. They explored three pairing scenarios: the blocking method, the quasiparticle BCS method, and the particle number projection (PNP) formalism, applying these to over 2500 \(1\pi2\nu\) and over 500 \(3\pi\) three-quasiparticle configurations built from single-particle states near the Fermi surfaces.

## Key numbers

- The lowest-lying type-A configuration in \(^{251}\)Md, \(\pi \nu^2 17/2^{+}\{ \pi 1/2^{-}_{\sf 10} \otimes \nu 9/2^{-}_{\sf 3} \otimes \nu 7/2^{+}_{\sf 4}\}\), lies at 1.04 MeV above the ground state.
- The experimentally measured excitation energy of the \(T_{1/2} = 109(6)\) ms isomer in \(^{252}\)No is approximately 1.25 MeV.
- The experimentally measured energy of the \(K^{\pi}=8^{-}\) isomer in \(^{254}\)No is 1293, 1296, 1295(2), or 1297(2) keV from different measurements.
- The lower limit for the isomer excitation energy in \(^{255}\)Lr was established as 720 keV.
- The isomer in \(^{249}\)Md has excitation energy \(\geq 910\) keV, and in \(^{251}\)Md \(\geq 844\) keV.
- The PNP calculation required increasing pairing strengths by approximately 10% relative to the base model, producing smaller nuclear masses (increased binding) by about 3.5 to 4.5 MeV for studied nuclei.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of \(K\)-isomers in even-even superheavy nuclei, such as those by Liu et al. (2014), to the more complex odd-even systems (Md through Rg). The calculations build on the Woods-Saxon microscopic-macroscopic model previously applied to masses and deformations by Kowal et al. (2010), \(Q_\alpha\) energies by Jachimowicz et al. (2014), and fission barriers by Jachimowicz et al. (2012-2021). The results enable comparison with existing experimental data on isomers in \(^{254}\)No, \(^{252}\)No, \(^{250}\)No, \(^{254}\)Rf, \(^{255}\)Lr, \(^{249}\)Md, and \(^{251}\)Md, and provide candidates for future experimental searches for three-quasiparticle isomers in heavier odd-even nuclei.
