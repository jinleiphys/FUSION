# ISI/FSI in Threshold Meson Production - Onshell Approach and Coulomb Problem

> The onshell description of Initial- and Final-State-Interactions (ISI and FSI) of threshold meson production reactions is reviewed. Existing onshell models and their offshell extension are discussed. Unitarity constraints on enhancement factors are formulated. A strategy for the treatment of essential singularities connected to Coulomb-like FSI is given.

# ISI/FSI in Threshold Meson Production, Onshell Approach and Coulomb Problem

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard Watson-Migdal factorisation of the T-matrix into a short-ranged production amplitude and onshell enhancement factors for initial- and final-state interactions is incomplete, because the enhancement factors retain a "reminder" in the short-ranged production amplitude due to offshell effects during the interaction time, and this reminder can be estimated via unitarity constraints rather than ignored.

## Method

The author reviews existing onshell models for initial- and final-state interactions (ISI/FSI) in threshold meson production, formulates unitarity constraints on the enhancement factors by inserting a complete set of relativistically normalised states into the T-matrix unitarity relation, and derives an approximate unitarity relation under the assumption that contributions from Fock states different from the initial and final states are small. For Coulomb-like FSI, the author proposes a regularisation strategy using a regularised Coulomb potential \( V(r) = 2\gamma\kappa \exp(-\mu r)/(r+\varepsilon) \) to avoid essential singularities in the Coulomb-modified effective range expansion, enabling a Taylor expansion of the FSI enhancement factor squared.

## Key numbers

- For the \( pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0 \) reaction near threshold, experiments yield \( \sigma(s) \propto \eta^{\alpha+4} \) with \( \alpha \ge 0 \) for \( \eta < 0.15 \) and \( \alpha \simeq -2 \) for \( 0.2 \le \eta \le 0.5 \), where \( \eta \) is a kinematic variable related to the centre-of-mass energy.
- The Coulomb penetration factor is given by \( C_0^2(\gamma) = 2\pi\gamma (\exp(2\pi\gamma)-1)^{-1} \) with \( \gamma := \alpha \sqrt{(\kappa^2 + (m_{1'} m_{2'}/(m_{1'}+m_{2'}))^2)/\kappa^2} \) and \( \alpha \simeq 1/137 \).
- For a three-particle final state with FSI between particles \( 1' \) and \( 2' \), the FSI-modified phase space integral \( R_3^{FSI}(s) \) is expanded in powers of \( \eta \) with leading term proportional to \( \eta^{\alpha+4} \), where the expansion coefficients depend on the masses and the parameter \( \bar{\mu} = (m_{1'}+m_{2'})/(2m_{3'}) \).

## Context

This work reviews and extends the Watson-Migdal approach (Watson, 1952; Migdal, 1955) for threshold meson production, discussing existing onshell models and their offshell extensions as reviewed by Niskanen (1999). The paper formulates unitarity constraints that challenge common approximations such as \( T_{FSI} = f_\ell^{-1}(\kappa) \) used by Joachain (1975) and Sibirtsev (1999), and identifies the Fermi-Watson theorem as the only existing unitarity-driven approach. The treatment of Coulomb FSI via regularisation builds on work by Austern (1964), Haeringen (1977), and Haeringen and Kok (1982). The analysis of \( pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0 \) cross sections tests these ideas against data, noting that models by Niskanen (1999) and Koltun (1969) struggle to reproduce the observed negative exponent \( \alpha \simeq -2 \) for \( 0.2 \le \eta \le 0.5 \), while the ansatz of Faldt and Wilkin (1999) shows promising behaviour.
