# New precision mass measurements of neutron-rich calcium and potassium isotopes and three-nucleon forces

> We present precision Penning-trap mass measurements of neutron-rich calcium and potassium isotopes in the vicinity of neutron number N=32. Using the TITAN system the mass of $^{51}$K was measured for the first time, and the precision of the $^{51,52}$Ca mass values were improved significantly. The new mass values show a dramatic increase of the binding energy compared to those reported in the atomic mass evaluation. In particular, $^{52}$Ca is more bound by 1.74 MeV, and the behavior with neutron number deviates substantially from the tabulated values. An increased binding was predicted recently based on calculations that include three-nucleon (3N) forces. We present a comparison to improved calculations, which agree remarkably with the evolution of masses with neutron number, making neutron-rich calcium isotopes an exciting region to probe 3N forces at neutron-rich extremes.

## Key claim

Precision Penning-trap mass measurements of neutron-rich calcium and potassium isotopes show that the binding energies of $^{51,52}$Ca and $^{51}$K are significantly larger than previously tabulated values, with $^{52}$Ca more bound by 1.74 MeV. The authors claim that this dramatic increase in binding, which deviates substantially from the atomic mass evaluation, agrees remarkably with improved theoretical calculations that include three-nucleon (3N) forces, though they emphasize that the impact of uncertainties in the leading 3N forces remains to be studied.

## Method

The masses of $^{51,52}$Ca and $^{51}$K were measured using the time-of-flight ion cyclotron-resonance (TOF-ICR) method in the TITAN Penning-trap system at TRIUMF. Singly-charged ions were produced at the ISAC facility using a tantalum target bombarded with 480 MeV protons (for calcium) or a UC$_x$ target with 1.9 $\mu$A protons (for potassium), then delivered to the Penning trap where cyclotron frequencies were measured relative to reference ions of well-known mass. Systematic effects from mass-dependent shifts and ion-ion interactions were quantified and included as uncertainties, with a count class analysis applied where count rates permitted.

## Key numbers

- $^{52}$Ca is more bound by 1.74 MeV compared to the AME2003 value, with a measured mass excess of -34244.6(61.0) keV
- $^{51}$Ca mass excess is -36338.9(22.7) keV, deviating by 5$\sigma$ from AME2003 and more bound by 0.5 MeV
- $^{51}$K mass excess is -22516.3(13.1) keV, more bound by 514.3 keV compared to the AME2003 extrapolation
- For $^{51,52}$Ca, the difference between theory (chiral NN+3N forces) and experiment is only $\lesssim$ 200 keV
- The mass-dependent shift systematic uncertainty was constrained to below 3.5 ppb in the frequency ratio
- Ion-ion interaction systematic uncertainty was 100 ppb for $^{51}$K and 750 ppb for $^{52}$Ca

## Context

This work extends previous mass measurements of neutron-rich potassium and calcium isotopes up to $^{50}$K and $^{52}$Ca (Tu 1990, Audi 2003) by providing the first direct Penning-trap mass measurements to N=32, and the first mass measurement of $^{51}$K. The results confirm predictions by Holt (2010) that chiral 3N forces provide increased binding in the vicinity of N=32, and the improved calculations including 3N forces to third order show excellent agreement with the new data. These findings enable further tests of 3N forces toward the neutron dripline and have implications for nucleosynthesis and neutron-star matter, as similar 3N forces provide repulsive contributions in those contexts.
