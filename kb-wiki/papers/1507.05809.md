# Survival of charged rho condensation at high temperature and density

> The charged vector $ρ$ mesons in the presence of external magnetic fields at finite temperature $T$ and chemical potential $μ$ have been investigated in the framework of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We compute the masses of charged $ρ$ mesons numerically as a function of the magnetic field for different values of temperature and chemical potential. The self-energy of the $ρ$ meson contains the quark-loop contribution, i.e. the leading order contribution in $1/N_c$ expansion. The charged $ρ$ meson mass decreases with the magnetic field and drops to zero at a critical magnetic field $eB_c$, which means that the charged vector meson condensation, i.e. the electromagnetic superconductor can be induced above the critical magnetic field. Surprisingly, it is found that the charged $ρ$ condensation can even survive at high temperature and density. At zero temperature, the critical magnetic field just increases slightly with the chemical potential, which indicates that the charged $ρ$ condensation might occur inside compact stars. At zero density, in the temperature range $0.2-0.5~ {\rm GeV}$, the critical magnetic field for charged $ρ$ condensation is in the range of $0.2-0.6~ {\rm GeV}^2$, which indicates that the high temperature electromagnetic superconductor could be created at LHC.

## Key claim

The charged rho meson condensation (electromagnetic superconductor) can survive at high temperature and high baryon density, not just in the vacuum; the critical magnetic field for condensation increases with both temperature and chemical potential, but remains within the range achievable in heavy-ion collisions at LHC.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with an external magnetic field, computing the charged rho meson mass from the one-quark-loop polarization function (leading order in 1/N_c expansion) using the Ritus eigenfunction method for the quark propagator. The meson masses are obtained by solving the gap equation 1 + 2 G_V Pi_i^2 = 0 for each spin projection, with the constituent quark mass solved self-consistently at finite temperature T and chemical potential mu. The calculation includes Matsubara frequency summations for finite temperature and sums over Landau levels up to 20.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature and for chemical potentials mu below the vacuum constituent quark mass (458 MeV), the critical magnetic field for charged rho condensation remains eB_c = 0.2 GeV^2.
- At zero chemical potential, in the temperature range 200-500 MeV, the critical magnetic field for charged rho condensation is in the range 0.2-0.6 GeV^2.
- The model parameters are: Lambda = 582 MeV, G_S Lambda^2 = 2.388, G_V Lambda^2 = 1.73, m_0 = 5 MeV, reproducing f_pi = 95 MeV, m_pi = 140 MeV, M_rho = 768 MeV, and vacuum quark mass M = 458 MeV.
- At zero chemical potential, when temperature is below T = 250 MeV (near the chiral phase transition critical temperature), the critical magnetic field changes little from its zero-temperature value.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NJL model calculation of charged rho condensation in vacuum (Liu 2014) to finite temperature and density, and contrasts with lattice QCD results (Hidaka 2012) that found no condensation, as well as with other NJL calculations (Chernodub 2011) that obtained a larger critical field eB_c > 1 GeV^2 by neglecting the quark-loop contribution. The finding that the critical field remains modest (0.2-0.6 GeV^2) at high temperatures suggests that the electromagnetic superconductor phase could be probed in non-central heavy-ion collisions at LHC, and the slight increase of critical field with chemical potential indicates possible relevance for compact star interiors.
