# Nuclear system size scan for freeze-out properties in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by using a multiphase transport model

> A system size scan program was recently proposed for the STAR experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider(RHIC). In this study, we employ a multiphase transport (AMPT) model for considering the bulk properties at the freeze-out stage for $\mathrm{^{10}B+^{10}B}$, $\mathrm{^{12}C+^{12}C}$, $\mathrm{^{16}O+^{16}O}$, $\mathrm{^{20}Ne+^{20}Ne}$, $\mathrm{^{40}Ca+^{40}Ca}$, $\mathrm{^{96}Zr+^{96}Zr}$, and $\mathrm{^{197}Au+^{197}Au}$ collisions at RHIC energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ of 200, 20, and 7.7 GeV. The results for $\mathrm{^{197}Au+^{197}Au}$ collisions are comparable with those of previous experimental STAR data. The transverse momentum $p_{T}$ spectra of charged particles ($π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, and $\bar{p}$) at the kinetic freeze-out stage, based on a blast-wave model, are also discussed. In addition, we use a statistical thermal model to extract the parameters at the chemical freeze-out stage, which agree with those from other thermal model calculations. It was found that there is a competitive relationship between the kinetic freeze-out parameter $T_{kin}$ and the radial expansion velocity $β_{T}$, which also agrees with the STAR or ALICE results. We found that the chemical freeze-out strangeness potential $μ_{s}$ remains constant in all collision systems and that the fireball radius $R$ is dominated by $\left\langle \mathrm{N_{Part}}\right\rangle$, which can be well fitted by a function of $a \left\langle \mathrm{N_{Part}}\right\rangle^{b}$ with $b \approx 1/3$. In addition, we calculated the nuclear modification factors for different collision systems with respect to the $ \mathrm{{}^{10}B} + \mathrm{{}^{10}B}$ system, and found that they present a gradual suppression within a higher $p_{T}$ range from small to large systems.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model to simulate seven symmetric collision systems at three RHIC energies, the authors find that the chemical freeze-out strangeness potential remains constant across all system sizes, while the fireball radius scales with the cube root of the average number of participants, with the caveat that these predictions are based on a transport model rather than experimental data for the smaller systems.

## Method

The authors employed the AMPT model with string melting to simulate 0%-5% most central collisions of seven symmetric nuclear systems (10B+10B, 12C+12C, 16O+16O, 20Ne+20Ne, 40Ca+40Ca, 96Zr+96Zr, and 197Au+197Au) at center-of-mass energies of 200, 20, and 7.7 GeV per nucleon pair. They extracted kinetic freeze-out parameters by fitting the transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons using a blast-wave model, and extracted chemical freeze-out parameters by fitting particle yields using a grand-canonical statistical thermal model.

## Key numbers

- The fireball radius R follows R = a * (N_part)^b with b approximately 1/3; at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, a = 1.0 fm, and at 20 and 7.7 GeV, a = 0.8 and 0.7 fm respectively, with b remaining approximately 0.33
- The average number of participants ranges from 14.8 (10B+10B) to 364.1 (197Au+197Au) at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch is approximately 160 MeV across all systems at all three energies
- The nuclear modification factor R*_AB fitting parameter 1/alpha increases monotonically from approximately 0.024 (12C+12C) to 0.24 (197Au+197Au) at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The slope parameter q in the yield fitting function log10(dN/dy) = p + q*log10(N_part) for pions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is 1.08 for both pi+ and pi-

## Context

This work extends the system size scan program proposed for the STAR experiment at RHIC by providing AMPT model predictions for freeze-out properties across seven symmetric collision systems, complementing experimental measurements from the STAR Collaboration (2009, 2017) for Au+Au collisions. The results enable comparison between transport model predictions and future experimental data from the RHIC system size scan, and the finding that the strangeness chemical potential remains constant across system sizes provides a testable prediction for the proposed experimental program. The discrepancies observed between AMPT and STAR data for antiparticle yields at lower energies (20 and 7.7 GeV) indicate areas where the model's treatment of anti-particles requires improvement.
