# Parton shadowing and $J/ψ$-to-Drell-Yan ratio in nuclear collisions at SPS and FAIR

> We have analyzed the data on $J/ψ$-to-Drell-Yan production cross section ratio in proton-nucleus ($p+A$) and nucleus-nucleus ($A+A$) collisions, measured by the NA50 collaboration in the SPS energy domain. Two component QVZ model has been employed to calculate $J/ψ$ production cross sections. For both $J/ψ$ and Drell-Yan production, nuclear modifications to the free nucleon structure functions are taken into account. In heavy-ion collisions, such modifications are assumed to be proportional to the local nuclear density resulting in centrality dependent initial state effects. Differences in quark and gluon shadowing leads to a new source of impact parameter dependence of the $J/ψ$ to Drell-Yan production ratio. For $J/ψ$, final state interaction of the produced $c\bar{c}$ pairs with the nuclear medium is also taken into account. A satisfactory description of the data in both $p+A$ and $A+A$ collisions is obtained. Role of the shadowing corrections are investigated in detail. Model calculations are extrapolated to predict the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$-to-Drell-Yan ratio in the FAIR energy regime.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the NA50 data on the $J/\psi$-to-Drell-Yan ratio in both proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies can be satisfactorily described by cold nuclear matter effects alone (parton shadowing and final-state dissociation of pre-resonant $c\bar{c}$ pairs), without invoking any additional suppression mechanism such as quark-gluon plasma formation, provided that nuclear modifications of parton densities inside both target and projectile nuclei are correctly taken into account.

## Method

The authors employed the two-component QVZ model to calculate $J/\psi$ production cross sections, factorizing the process into perturbative QCD production of $c\bar{c}$ pairs and non-perturbative hadronization to $J/\psi$ mesons using either a Gaussian or power-law form for the transition probability. Nuclear modifications to parton distribution functions were implemented using leading-order MSTW2008 free-proton PDFs and the LO EPS09 nuclear PDF interface, with spatial dependence of shadowing in heavy-ion collisions modeled as proportional to the local nuclear density via a Woods-Saxon profile. Final-state dissociation of $c\bar{c}$ pairs was accounted for by a shift in the relative momentum squared proportional to the average path length traversed in the nuclear medium, with the parameter $\varepsilon^2$ extracted from previous $p+A$ data.

## Key numbers

* For 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions at impact parameter $b=0$, the ratio $R^{AA}_{J/\psi/DY} = B_{\mu\mu}\sigma^{AA}_{J/\psi}(\varepsilon^2=0)/\sigma^{AA}_{DY}$ assumes a value of approximately 53 in the absence of final-state dissociation.
* At SPS energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 17.3$ GeV), the kinematic region probed corresponds to $x_{SPS} \approx 0.18$, where gluons and valence quarks exhibit anti-shadowing while sea quarks show shadowing.
* At FAIR energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 6.9$ GeV), the kinematic region corresponds to $x_{FAIR} \approx 0.45$, where gluon, valence quark, and sea quark densities all become depleted (EMC effect).
* In the FAIR energy regime, initial-state shadowing effects alone lead to a 10-15% suppression of $J/\psi$ production in Pb+Pb collisions.
* For Drell-Yan production at FAIR energies, central Pb+Pb collisions show a reduction of almost 50% compared to $p+p$ collisions.
* The difference between local and global shadowing parameterizations at SPS energies lies within 2-3%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Bhaduri et al., 2014) of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^{J/\psi}$ in In+In and Pb+Pb collisions to the $J/\psi$-to-Drell-Yan ratio measured by the NA50 collaboration, which offers better control over systematic uncertainties since both cross sections are directly measured in the same experimental run. The analysis is motivated by and akin to earlier studies of parton shadowing effects on $J/\psi$ production (Emelyanov et al., 1999; Klein et al., 2003) at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. The model predictions for Au+Au collisions at 25 A GeV provide baseline expectations for the upcoming Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR, where any additional suppression beyond these cold nuclear matter calculations could indicate the presence of a hot and dense secondary medium.
