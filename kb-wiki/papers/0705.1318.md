# Supersymmetry and Nuclear Pairing

> We show that nuclear pairing Hamiltonian exhibits supersymmetry in the strong-coupling limit. The underlying supersymmetric quantum mechanical structure explains the degeneracies between the energies of the N and Nmax-N+1 pair eigenstates. The supersymmetry transformations connecting these states are given.

## Key claim

The nuclear pairing Hamiltonian exhibits supersymmetry in the strong-coupling limit (where the pairing strength |G| is much larger than the single-particle energies epsilon_j), which explains degeneracies between N-pair and (N_max - N + 1)-pair eigenstates; this supersymmetry is broken by the single-particle energies in the full Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors constructed supersymmetric partner Hamiltonians H_1 = B^+ B^- and H_2 = B^- B^+ using operators B^- = T^dagger S^-(0) and B^+ = S^+(0) T, where T is an operator that exchanges the pair creation and annihilation operators and transforms the particle vacuum into the fully occupied shell. They showed that in the strong-coupling limit, both partner Hamiltonians are identical to the separable pairing Hamiltonian S^+(0)S^-(0), and used Bethe ansatz solutions to demonstrate that the supersymmetric partner of an N-pair state is an (N_max - N + 1)-pair state.

## Key numbers

- The one-pair state S^+(0)|0⟩ has energy E = -|G| sum_j Omega_j |c_j|^2, where Omega_j is the degeneracy of orbital j and c_j are the pairing interaction coefficients.
- The N-pair state in Eq. (a7) has energy E = -|G| ( sum_j Omega_j |c_j|^2 - sum_{k=1}^{N-1} 2/z^{(N)}_k ), where z^{(N)}_k satisfy Bethe ansatz equations.
- The completely filled state |bar{0}⟩ has the same energy as the one-pair state: E = -|G| sum_j Omega_j |c_j|^2.
- Zero-energy states exist when the number of pairs N is less than or equal to N_max/2 (the half-filled shell condition).

## Context

This work extends the application of supersymmetry concepts in nuclear physics beyond dynamical supersymmetries relating even-even and odd-even nuclei (Balantekin, 1981; Balantekin, 1982) and the Osp(1/2) supersymmetry of the spherical Nilsson Hamiltonian (Balantekin, 1992). The paper provides a supersymmetric quantum mechanical explanation for degeneracies previously found in the exactly solvable pairing model (Pan, 1997; Balantekin, 2007), and the Bethe ansatz solution framework connects to the broader literature on exactly solvable pairing models (Dukelsky, 2001; Balantekin, 2004).
