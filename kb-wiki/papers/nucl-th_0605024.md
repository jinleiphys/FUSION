# Deuteron Matrix Elements in Chiral Effective Theory at Leading Order

> We consider matrix elements of two-nucleon operators that arise in chiral effective theories of the two-nucleon system. Generically, the short-distance piece of these operators scales as 1/r^n, with r the relative separation of the two nucleons. We show that, when evaluated between the leading-order wave functions obtained in this effective theory, these two-nucleon operators are independent of the cutoff used to renormalize the two-body problem for n=1 and 2. However, for n greater than or equal to 3 general arguments about the short-distance behavior of the leading-order deuteron wave function show that the matrix element will diverge.

# Deuteron Matrix Elements in Chiral Effective Theory at Leading Order

## Key claim

Matrix elements of two-nucleon operators that scale as 1/r^n at short distances, evaluated with leading-order chiral effective theory deuteron wave functions, are cutoff-independent for n=1 and 2 but diverge for n ≥ 3. This conclusion holds specifically for the leading-order wave functions obtained from the one-pion-exchange potential plus a contact interaction, and assumes that higher-order corrections to the wave function are treated perturbatively rather than iterated to all orders.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for the deuteron S- and D-wave radial functions u(r) and w(r) using the leading-order chiral potential consisting of one-pion exchange and a momentum-independent contact interaction in the 3S1 channel. They split the radial integral for ⟨1/r^n⟩ into three regions: a short-distance piece (0 to 1/Λ) where cutoff dependence resides, an intermediate asymptotic region where the analytic short-distance form of the wave function applies, and a long-distance region. The short-distance asymptotic behavior of u(r) and w(r) was analyzed using the analytic forms derived by Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2005), which contain both an exponentially decaying piece and an oscillatory cosine term that does not vanish at the origin.

## Key numbers

- ⟨1/r⟩_LO = 0.478 fm^{-1} (cutoff-independent limit for the 1/r operator)
- ⟨1/r^2⟩_LO = 0.425 fm^{-2} (cutoff-independent limit for the 1/r^2 operator)
- ∫ dr u(r)w(r)/r = 0.141 fm^{-1} (mixed S-D matrix element for n=1)
- ∫ dr u(r)w(r)/r^2 = 0.156 fm^{-2} (mixed S-D matrix element for n=2)
- The length scale R = 3 g_A^2 M / (32 π f_π^2) governs the short-distance oscillatory behavior of the wave function
- The asymptotic D/S ratio η and the wave number γ = sqrt(M B_d) characterize the long-distance wave function

## Context

This work extends the numerical observations of Nogga and Hanhart (2005) on the cutoff dependence of ⟨1/r⟩ in pion-deuteron scattering, providing an analytic explanation for the oscillations observed in that calculation. It also generalizes the analysis to arbitrary powers n, showing that the 1/r^2 operator relevant for triple-scattering contributions to a_πd (discussed by Beane et al., 2002) converges despite requiring higher cutoffs, while operators with n ≥ 3 diverge and require counterterms or cancellations with wave-function corrections. The results enable systematic assessment of when contact interactions involving external probes must be included in chiral effective theory calculations of deuteron processes such as γd → π^0d.
