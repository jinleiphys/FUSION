# Preconditioning the non-relativistic many-fermion problem

> Preconditioning is at the core of modern many-fermion Monte Carlo algorithms, such as Hybrid Monte Carlo, where the repeated solution of a linear problem involving an ill-conditioned matrix is needed. We report on a performance comparison of three preconditioning strategies, namely Chebyshev polynomials, strong-coupling approximation and weak-coupling expansion. We use conjugate gradient (CG) on the normal equations as well as stabilized biconjugate gradient (BiCGStab) as solvers and focus on the fermion matrix of the unitary Fermi gas. Our results indicate that BiCGStab is by far the most efficient strategy, both in terms of the number of iterations and matrix-vector operations.

## Key claim

The paper reports that, for the fermion matrix of the unitary Fermi gas, using BiCGStab with weak-coupling expansion preconditioning is by far the most efficient strategy in terms of both iteration count and matrix-vector operations, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of random auxiliary field configurations rather than thermalized ones (thermalized configurations increase iteration counts by a factor of ~2 across all parameter values, though relative merits of preconditioners remain unaffected).

## Method

The authors compared three preconditioning strategies, Chebyshev polynomials, strong-coupling approximation (SCA), and weak-coupling expansion (WCE), applied to the fermion matrix M arising from a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformed representation of the unitary Fermi gas. They solved the linear problem M†M x = b using conjugate gradient (CG) on the normal equations, and the two-step problem M† y = b followed by M x = y using stabilized biconjugate gradient (BiCGStab), with a convergence tolerance of 10⁻¹². Test runs used an imaginary time step τ = 0.05, couplings g = 2.5, 5.0, and 7.5, and auxiliary field configurations with randomly distributed entries in [−π, π].

## Key numbers

- For lattice size 10³ × 200 at g = 5.0 (A ≈ 0.75), CG with Chebyshev degree 32 required 735 iterations, while BiCGStab with WCE2 required 121 iterations.
- For lattice size 10³ × 200 at g = 5.0, CG with Chebyshev degree 32 required 48,510 matrix-vector operations, while BiCGStab with WCE2 required 2,178 operations, yielding a gain factor of 27×.
- For lattice size 6³ × 50 at g = 5.0, CG with Chebyshev degree 8 required 5,292 matrix-vector operations, while BiCGStab with WCE0 required 768 operations, yielding a gain factor of 7×.
- For g = 7.5 (A ≈ 0.95), the second-order weak-coupling preconditioner (WCE2) required 1,566 matrix-vector operations on a 6³ × 200 lattice; for g = 10.0 (A ≈ 1.14), the method typically failed to converge in less than 15,000 matrix-vector operations.
- The strong-coupling approximation (SCA) required 5,370 matrix-vector operations for 6³ × 50 at g = 5.0, and failed to converge in less than 10,000 iterations for 8³ × 200 and 10³ × 200.

## Context

This work extends preconditioning techniques developed for lattice QCD (where Hybrid Monte Carlo is standard) to non-relativistic many-fermion systems, where determinantal Monte Carlo (DMC) has been the method of choice despite its ~V³ scaling. The strong-coupling approximation was originally proposed by Scalettar et al., and the Chebyshev approach is related to the Polynomial Hybrid Monte Carlo (PHMC) algorithm of Frezzotti and Jansen. The results enable practical HMC implementations for non-relativistic fermions that can scale as ~V^(5/4), potentially competing with DMC calculations in condensed-matter and nuclear-structure physics.
