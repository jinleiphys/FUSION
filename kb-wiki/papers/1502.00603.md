# Collision Geometry and Flow in Uranium+Uranium Collisions

> Using event-by-event viscous fluid dynamics to evolve fluctuating initial density profiles from the Monte-Carlo Glauber model for U+U collisions, we report a "knee"-like structure in the elliptic flow as a function of collision centrality, located near 0.5% centrality as measured by the final charged multiplicity. This knee is due to the preferential selection of tip-on-tip collision geometries by a high-multiplicity trigger. Such a knee structure is not seen in the STAR data. This rules out the two-component MC-Glauber model for initial energy and entropy production. An enrichment of tip-tip configurations by triggering solely on high-multiplicity in the U+U collisions thus does not work. On the other hand, using the Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDCs) coupled with event-shape engineering, we identify the selection purity of body-body and tip-tip events in the full-overlap U+U collisions. With additional constraints on the asymmetry of the ZDC signals one can further increases the probability of selecting tip-tip events in U+U collisions.

# Collision Geometry and Flow in Uranium+Uranium Collisions

## Key claim
Within the two-component Monte-Carlo Glauber model, a "knee" structure appears in elliptic flow versus centrality near 0.5% centrality in U+U collisions due to preferential selection of tip-on-tip geometries by high-multiplicity triggers, but this knee is not seen in STAR data, ruling out the two-component MC-Glauber model for initial energy and entropy production. However, combined cuts using Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDCs) and event-shape engineering can enrich tip-tip and body-body event selection, with further improvement from requiring symmetric ZDC signals.

## Method
The authors used event-by-event viscous fluid dynamics (iEBE-VISHNU code) with specific shear viscosity η/s = 0.08 to evolve fluctuating initial density profiles generated by the two-component Monte-Carlo Glauber model for U+U collisions at 193 A GeV. Initial entropy density was computed as a mixture of wounded nucleon and binary collision sources with mixing ratio α = 0.12, using a deformed Woods-Saxon distribution with quadrupole deformation β20 = 0.28 and hexadecupole deformation β40 = 0.093. The hydrodynamic evolution started at τ0 = 0.6 fm/c and decoupled at Tdec = 120 MeV, with final charged multiplicity estimated from thermal π+ yields scaled by a factor of 4.6.

## Key numbers
- The "knee" structure in elliptic flow appears near 0.5% centrality as measured by final charged multiplicity.
- Deformation parameters used: β20 = 0.28, β40 = 0.093; average radius r0 = 6.86 fm; surface diffusiveness d = 0.44 fm; saturation density ρ0 = 0.1660 fm⁻³.
- Specific shear viscosity η/s = 0.08; hydrodynamic starting time τ0 = 0.6 fm/c; decoupling temperature Tdec = 120 MeV.
- Mixing ratio α = 0.12; κs = 17.16; multiplicity fluctuation parameter θpp = 0.9175.
- For 1% ZDC events (N_s < 19, corresponding to N_s < 19 spectators), cutting on high dS/dy enriches tip-tip fraction to about 50%; cutting on low dS/dy enriches body-body fraction to about 20%.
- Cutting on small ε2 (or v2) enriches tip-tip fraction to about 25% purity; cutting on large ε2 enriches body-body fraction to about 40% purity.
- Selecting the lowest 10% of ΔNpart (difference in participants between the two nuclei) increases tip-tip selection efficiency by a factor of 1.4, boosting tip-tip purity to about 35%.

## Context
This work extends earlier proposals for using U+U collisions to study collision geometry effects from prolate nuclear deformation (Shuryak 1999, Kolb 2000, Heinz 2004, Kuhlman 2005, Voloshin 2010) and directly tests the two-component MC-Glauber model against STAR data (Wang 2014). The failure of the MC-Glauber model in ultra-central U+U collisions contrasts with its success in Au+Au collisions and points toward alternative initial-state models such as IP-Glasma (Schenke 2012) or wounded valence gluon models (Adler 2013). The ZDC-based event selection strategy enables future experimental studies of body-body and tip-tip configurations, and the results motivate testing other initialization models like MC-KLN (Kharzeev 2000, Drescher 2006) and TRENTO (Moreland 2014) against U+U data.
