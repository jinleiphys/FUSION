# Quenching of Hadron Spectra due to the Collisional Energy Loss of Partons in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We estimate the energy loss distribution and investigate the quenching of hadron spectra in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions due to the collisional energy loss of energetic partons from hard parton collisions in the initial stage.

## Key claim

The collisional energy loss of partons in the quark-gluon plasma can produce a quenching of hadron spectra that is of the same order as the quenching from radiative energy loss, with the collisional quenching factor lying between 0.22 and 0.26 for transverse momenta from 6 to 16 GeV under the authors' chosen parameters.

## Method

The authors model the energy loss of hard partons traversing an expanding quark-gluon plasma using a Fokker-Planck equation derived from the Boltzmann equation under the Landau approximation, where most scattering is soft. They solve this equation to obtain a Gaussian probability distribution for the energy loss, using a drag coefficient averaged over parton species and a diffusion coefficient related via the Einstein relation. The quenched hadron spectrum is then computed by convolving the vacuum transverse momentum distribution with this energy-loss probability distribution and averaging over the transverse geometry of a Bjorken-expanding cylindrical fireball.

## Key numbers

- The effective energy loss is 10% after traversing 1 fm and around 50% after 10 fm (for given transverse momentum).
- The collisional quenching factor lies between 0.22 and 0.26 for transverse momenta from 6 to 16 GeV, using parameters T0 = 0.5 GeV, t0 = 0.3 fm, and alpha_s = 0.3.
- The radiative quenching factor varies between 0.17 and 0.24 for the same momentum range, depending on model assumptions.
- The vacuum transverse momentum distribution is parameterized as dN^vac/d^2p_perp = N0 (1 + p_perp/p0)^(-nu) with nu approximately 8 and p0 = 1.75 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Baier et al. (2001) and Muller (2003), who calculated hadron quenching using only radiative energy loss, by instead using the collisional energy loss derived from Hard-Thermal-Loop resummed perturbative QCD by Thoma (1995). The results show that collisional quenching is comparable to radiative quenching, which is important because recent work by Wang and Wang (2002) indicated that radiative energy loss may be reduced at moderate parton energies due to cancellation between stimulated emission and thermal absorption. The authors' simplified one-dimensional Fokker-Planck treatment with Bjorken expansion provides an intuitive picture, but they note that extension to three dimensions would require revision of many considerations.
