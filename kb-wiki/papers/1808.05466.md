# An iterative method to estimate the combinatorial background

> The reconstruction of broad resonances is important for understanding the dynamics of heavy ion collisions. However, large combinatorial background makes this objective very challenging. In this work an innovative iterative method which identifies signal and background contributions without input models for normalization constants is presented. This technique is successfully validated on a simulated thermal cocktail of resonances. This demonstrates that the iterative procedure is a powerful tool to reconstruct multi-differentially inclusive resonant signals in high multiplicity events as produced in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents an iterative method to reconstruct broad resonance signals from combinatorial background in heavy ion collisions that requires no input models for normalization constants, but the method's convergence depends on identifying a transition point between real signal growth and statistical fluctuations, which must be determined bin-by-bin across the multi-differential phase space.

## Method

The method starts from the total same-event invariant mass spectrum and applies an uncorrelating operator (track rotation) to generate a reference distribution. The positive differences between the total and rotated spectra are taken as the first signal estimate, then the uncorrelating operator is applied to this signal estimate and subtracted from the total in an iterative procedure following the Landweber Iterative Method formalism. Convergence is reached when the signal estimate stops changing, but for finite statistics the iteration must be stopped bin-by-bin by identifying the transition from real signal growth to statistical fluctuation regime.

## Key numbers

- Signal-to-background ratio below 5% in the simulated cocktail of resonances decaying into proton and negative pion pairs
- The simulated thermal source temperature was 65 MeV, mimicking particle production in a heavy ion collision at 1.25 GeV per nucleon
- The phase space was binned as 40 bins in invariant mass, 5 in transverse momentum, 5 in opening angle, and 5 in rapidity
- 300 iterations were performed on the simulated data sample
- The transition from real signal growth to statistical fluctuations occurred between the 40th and 80th iteration for the integrated signal
- The reconstructed integrated signal agreed with the input within statistical uncertainties of the order of 10%
- The method was validated on a cocktail containing Delta(1232)0 at 5% abundance, N(1440)0 at 2%, N(1535)0 at 1.5%, Delta(1620)0 at 1%, and Delta(1920)0 at 0.5% relative to protons

## Context

This work extends the Event-Mixing method (Kopylov 1974) and rotation techniques used for narrow peaks (STAR 2005, ALICE 2012) by providing a model-independent approach that avoids the normalization constant problems identified by Higgins (1978), L'Hote (1992), and Kaskulov (2010). The method enables multi-differential inclusive reconstruction of broad resonances like Delta and N* states in high-multiplicity heavy ion events, and the authors report ongoing application to pi±p and pi+pi- channels in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=2.42 GeV with the HADES detector.
