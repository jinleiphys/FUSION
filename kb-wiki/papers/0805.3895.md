# Time Dependent Pairing Equations for Seniority One Nuclear Systems

> When the time dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov intrinsic equations of motion are solved in the case of seniority one nuclear systems, the unpaired nucleon remains on the same orbital. The blocking effect hinders the possibility to skip from one orbital to another. This unpleasant feature is by-passed with a new set of pairing time dependent equations that allows the possibility that the unpaired nucleon changes its single-particle level. These equations generalize the time dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations of motion by including the Landau-Zener effect. The derivation of these new equations is presented in details. These equations are applied in the case of a superasymmetric fission process, that is, in order to explain the fine structure the 14C emission from 233Ra. A new version of the Woods-Saxon model extended for two-center potentials is used in this context.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new set of time-dependent pairing equations that allow an unpaired nucleon in a seniority one nuclear system to change its single-particle level during nuclear dynamics, bypassing the limitation of standard time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations where the blocked nucleon remains trapped on the same orbital. The generalization incorporates the Landau-Zener effect into the superfluid formalism, with the standard TDHFB equations and the single-particle Landau-Zener equations recovered as limiting cases.

## Method

The author starts from a variational principle with a Lagrangian that includes the many-body Hamiltonian with monopole pairing, a Landau-Zener interaction term coupling diabatic states, and the particle number operator. Two approaches are developed: one neglecting blocking effects on densities and pairing moments (low-lying levels), and a more complete one incorporating the blocking effect where each seniority one BCS wave function has its own set of occupation and pairing parameters. The resulting coupled channel equations for densities, pairing moments, occupation probabilities, and unpairing moment components are solved numerically along a least-action trajectory for the superasymmetric fission process of 14C emission from 223Ra, using a Woods-Saxon potential extended for two-center shapes.

## Key numbers

- Pairing parameter G = 0.13 MeV used in calculations
- 31 levels above and 31 levels below the unpaired Fermi level in the initial ground state configuration (total N-1 = 62 active levels)
- Internuclear velocity ∂R/∂t = 1.4 × 10^6 m/s along the minimal action path
- Time required to penetrate the barrier: about 1.4 × 10^{-20} s
- Theoretical branching ratio r = 5 between partial half-life for transitions to ground state versus first excited state of the daughter
- Experimental branching ratio values range between 5.4 and 5.9
- At R ≈ 10 fm, a system formed by two spherical tangent nuclei is obtained

## Context

This work extends the TDHFB approach of Koonin (1976) and Blocki (1978) by solving the problem of level slippage for unpaired nucleons in seniority one systems, which was previously forbidden. The formalism is applied to explain the fine structure in 14C radioactivity from 223Ra, a phenomenon first observed by Brillard (1989), Hussonnois (1989), and Hourany (1989), where 15±3% of decays go to the ground state and 81±6% to the first excited state. The new equations enable dynamical calculations of spectroscopic amplitudes and dissipated energies in different final channels, reproducing experimental data that could not be accounted for by static models such as the M3Y potential approach of Gupta (1993).
