# Importance of isobar density distributions on the chiral magnetic effect search

> Under the approximate chiral symmetry restoration, quark interactions with topological gluon fields in quantum chromodynamics can induce chirality imbalance and parity violation in local domains. An electric charge separation ({\sc cs}) could be generated along the direction of a strong magnetic field ({\bf B}), a phenomenon called the chiral magnetic effect ({\sc cme}). {\sc cs} measurements by azimuthal correlators are contaminated by a major background from elliptic flow anisotropy ($v_2$). Isobaric $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions have been proposed to identify the {\sc cme} (expected to differ between the two systems) out of the background (expected to be almost the same). We show, by using the density-functional calculated proton and neutron distributions, that these expectations may not hold as originally anticipated, because the two systems may have sizable differences in eccentricity and $v_2$ and because their difference in {\bf B} may suffer from large uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the expected advantages of isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions for identifying the chiral magnetic effect (CME) may not hold as originally anticipated, because density functional theory (DFT) calculations reveal that the two systems may have sizable differences in eccentricity and elliptic flow (v2), and their difference in magnetic field may suffer from large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used density functional theory (DFT) with the SLy4 mean field including pairing correlations (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach) to calculate the proton and neutron density distributions of Ru and Zr nuclei. These DFT densities were then implemented in Monte Carlo Glauber (MCG) calculations to determine eccentricities and magnetic fields, and in A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model simulations with string melting to compute final-state elliptic flow (v2) and event-plane-dependent magnetic field quantities.

## Key numbers

- The relative difference in eccentricity with respect to the participant plane, R(ePP), reaches up to approximately 3.7% at impact parameter b ≈ 5 fm for DFT densities
- The average v2 difference between RuRu and ZrZr collisions is R(vEP) ≈ 2.7 ± 0.1% in the 20-60% centrality range from AMPT simulations
- The magnetic field squared difference R(BRP) is slightly smaller for DFT density profiles than for Woods-Saxon at small impact parameter
- The charge radii from DFT are 4.327 fm for Ru and 4.271 fm for Zr; the mass radii are 4.343 fm for Ru and 4.366 fm for Zr
- The 20-60% centrality corresponds to Nch ranges of 62-273 for RuRu and 61-271 for ZrZr

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Deng (2016) that used Woods-Saxon density profiles and found less than ±2% difference in eccentricity between the isobaric systems. The paper contradicts the assumption that isobaric collisions would have nearly identical backgrounds due to equal mass numbers, showing that realistic nuclear structure differences from DFT calculations introduce sizable eccentricity and v2 differences. This enables experimental tests: the authors predict that the v2 difference between RuRu and ZrZr with respect to the reaction plane is larger than that with respect to the participant plane by an absolute 8%, which can be verified by the upcoming isobaric collision program at RHIC planned for 2018.
