# Beam energy dependence of cumulants of the net-baryon, net-charge and deuteron multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.0-5.0$ GeV

> Within the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, in which the Lorentz-covariant treatment of nuclear mean-field potential is considered, the fluctuations of net-baryon, net-charge and deuterons multiplicity distributions in Au+Au head-on collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.0-5.0$ GeV are calculated. The results show that the nuclear mean-field potential can significantly enhance the magnitude of baryon number fluctuations in narrow rapidity windows, and this enhancement rapidly weakens with increasing beam energy. However, for proton and net-charge number fluctuations, the mean-field effects are less noticeable than that for baryon number. In addition, for net-charge number fluctuations, the negative binomial distribution agrees well with the calculated results at mid-pseudorapidity window. Finally, the event-by-event fluctuations of deuteron number in the coalescence production picture are calculated as well, it is found that its cumulant ratios decrease linearly with increasing the average multiplicity of deuterons per event, i.e., increase with increasing beam energy.

## Key claim

The nuclear mean-field potential significantly enhances the magnitude of baryon number fluctuations in narrow rapidity windows, but this enhancement rapidly weakens with increasing beam energy; for proton and net-charge number fluctuations, the mean-field effects are less noticeable than for baryon number.

## Method

The authors used the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model with a Lorentz-covariant treatment of the nuclear mean-field potential (soft equation of state with momentum dependence, SM-EoS) to simulate 15 million Au+Au head-on collisions (impact parameter b=0 fm) at beam energies from 3.0 to 5.0 GeV per nucleon pair. They calculated cumulants (C1 through C4) and their ratios for net-baryon, net-proton, net-charge, and deuteron multiplicity distributions, comparing results from the mean-field mode with those from a cascade mode (no mean-field). Clustering effects were included via an isospin-dependent minimum-span-tree (MST) algorithm to identify deuterons, and a centrality-bin-width correction (CBWC) method was applied to reduce volume fluctuations from varying participant numbers.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0, 3.6, 3.9, 4.5, and 5.0 GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- For net-baryon cumulant ratios, the rapidity window dependence was studied with transverse momentum cut 0.4 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c.
- For net-charge fluctuations, the pseudorapidity window dependence was studied with transverse momentum cut 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c.
- For fixed baryon number C1 ≈ 40 (approximately 10% of total net-baryon number), the difference between SM-EoS and cascade mode results steadily decreases with increasing beam energy.
- For deuteron cumulant ratios, all ratios show a monotonous trend and a fairly good linearity with the averaged deuteron multiplicity per event; more deuterons (larger C1) correspond to lower beam energy.
- The coalescence parameters used for deuteron identification: relative distance between two nucleons smaller than 2.8 fm (proton-proton or neutron-neutron) or 3.8 fm (neutron-proton), and relative momentum smaller than 0.25 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous UrQMD studies by Ye et al. (2018) and Jan et al. (2018) that calculated cumulant ratios at lower beam energy (E_lab = 1.23 GeV/nucleon, measured by HADES) and found that both mean-field potential and clustering influence cumulant ratios. The present study covers the energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0-5.0 GeV, which corresponds to the future CBM experiment at FAIR. The results enable comparison with upcoming experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and the CBM experiment, and suggest that the simple binomial distribution (BD) cannot fully describe the behavior of kappa*sigma^2 for baryons, while the negative binomial distribution (NBD) agrees well with net-charge fluctuations at mid-pseudorapidity.
