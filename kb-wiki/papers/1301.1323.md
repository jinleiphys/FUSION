# Empirical Survey of Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay Matrix Elements

> Neutrinoless double beta decay has been the subject of intensive theoretical work as it represents the only practical approach to discovering whether neutrinos are Majorana particles or not, and whether lepton number is a conserved quantum number. Available calculations of matrix elements and phase-space factors are reviewed from the perspective of a future large-scale experimental search for neutrinoless double beta decay. Somewhat unexpectedly, a uniform inverse correlation between phase space and the square of the nuclear matrix element emerges. As a consequence, no isotope is either favored or disfavored; all have qualitatively the same decay rate per unit mass for any given value of the Majorana mass.

## Key claim

All candidate isotopes for neutrinoless double beta decay have roughly equivalent sensitivity per unit mass, because a uniform inverse correlation between phase space and the square of the nuclear matrix element cancels out individual advantages; this conclusion holds at the level of a factor of about 3.

## Method

The author compiled published nuclear matrix elements from shell-model, generator-coordinate method (GCM), quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA), interacting boson model (IBM), and projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) calculations, renormalizing them to remove the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\). The specific phase space \(H_{0\nu}\) was computed from the phase-space factors of Kotila and Iachello (2012) and combined with the matrix-element ranges to produce decay-rate per unit mass for each isotope, with \(g_A\) treated as an independent uncertainty between 0.6 and 1.269.

## Key numbers

* The specific activity constant \(a_{0\nu}\) is \(10^{2.9 \pm 0.6}\) decays per year per tonne per eV\(^2\) (with \(g_A\) uncertainty included in quadrature).
* For \(^{76}\)Ge, the reported signal is \(246 \pm 40\) decays per tonne per year (Klapdor-Kleingrothaus and Krivosheina, 2006).
* For \(^{136}\)Xe, the 90% CL upper limits are \(<192\) decays per tonne per year (EXO, 2012) and \(<162\) decays per tonne per year (KamLAND-Zen, 2012); combined limit \(<90\) decays per tonne per year.
* Phase-space factors \(G_{0\nu}^{(0)}\) range from \(0.59 \times 10^{-15}\) y\(^{-1}\) for \(^{128}\)Te to \(63.03 \times 10^{-15}\) y\(^{-1}\) for \(^{150}\)Nd.
* Specific phase space \(H_{0\nu}\) ranges from 7.4 Mg\(^{-1}\) y\(^{-1}\) eV\(^{-2}\) for \(^{128}\)Te to 826.2 Mg\(^{-1}\) y\(^{-1}\) eV\(^{-2}\) for \(^{48}\)Ca.

## Context

This survey extends the compilation of matrix elements by Dueck et al. (2011) and uses the phase-space factors of Kotila and Iachello (2012), which include exact screening corrections. The finding of a uniform inverse correlation between phase space and matrix element is new and contradicts the expectation that some isotopes (e.g., \(^{48}\)Ca) would be strongly disfavored. The work enables experimental collaborations to compare isotopes on a common footing and suggests that technical criteria (cost, background, resolution) rather than nuclear theory should drive isotope choice for next-generation tonne-scale experiments.
