# Effective Field Theories for Hadrons and Nuclei

> Hadron structure and nuclear structure are discussed from the common ground of effective chiral Lagrangians modeling QCD at low energy. The topics treated are the chiral bag model in large $N_c$ QCD, its connection to heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\chpt),\ the role of nonabelian Berry gauge connections for baryon excitations and the application of HB\chpt \ to the thermal $n+p\rightarrow d +γ$ process and to the axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

Hadron and nuclear structure can be described from a common framework of effective chiral Lagrangians modeling QCD at low energy, with the caveat that the strange quark is treated as both heavy (for baryon excitations) and light (for kaon condensation), and that a precise derivation of nuclear matter from a chiral Lagrangian does not yet exist.

## Method

The paper reviews the chiral bag model in large N_c QCD and its connection to heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT), using nonabelian Berry gauge connections to describe baryon excitations. It applies HBχPT to compute two-body exchange currents for the thermal n+p→d+γ process and for axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei, organizing the chiral expansion in powers of small momentum Q relative to the chiral scale Λ_χ ~ 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The thermal n+p→d+γ capture cross section: impulse approximation predicts σ_imp = 305.6 mb, experiment gives σ_exp = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb, and theory with short-range cutoff yields σ_th = 334 ± 3 mb
- For axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei, the two-body tree contribution ratio M_2^tree/M_1 ~ 0.6 – 0.8, and the experimental ratio M^exp/M_1 ~ 1.6 – 2
- The Brown-Rho scaling parameter a ≈ 0.15 – 0.2 for densities up to nuclear matter density ρ_0, with Φ(ρ) ≈ 1 – a(ρ/ρ_0)
- For strange hyperons: fit parameters ω_K = 223 MeV, c_K = 0.62; for charmed hyperons: ω_D = 1418 MeV, c_D = 0.14
- The constant c in the heavy-quark scaling c_Φ ~ c/m_Φ has c ~ 262 MeV

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Rho and Brown (1981) on chiral symmetry in nuclear processes and the Cheshire Cat Principle formulated by Damgaard et al. It connects the chiral bag model to heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory as developed by Manohar and by Jenkins and Manohar, and applies the approach to nuclear forces following Weinberg (1990) and Ordonez, Ray and van Kolck. The work enables systematic calculation of exchange currents in nuclei and provides a framework for studying kaon condensation and in-medium hadron properties via Brown-Rho scaling.
