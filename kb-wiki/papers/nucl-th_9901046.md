# Direct Emission of multiple strange baryons in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions from the phase boundary

> We discuss a model for the space-time evolution of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions which employs relativistic hydrodynamics within one region of the forward light-cone, and microscopic transport theory (i.e. UrQMD) in the complement. Our initial condition consists of a quark-gluon plasma which expands hydrodynamically and hadronizes. After hadronization the solution eventually changes from expansion in local equilibrium to free streaming, as determined selfconsistently by the interaction rates between the hadrons and the local expansion rate. We show that in such a scenario the inverse slopes of the $m_T$-spectra of multiple strange baryons ($Ξ$, $Ω$) are practically unaffected by the purely hadronic stage of the reaction, while the flow of $p$'s and $Λ$'s increases. Moreover, we find that the rather ``soft'' transverse expansion at RHIC energies (due to a first-order phase transition) is not washed out by strong rescattering in the hadronic stage. The earlier kinetic freeze-out as compared to SPS-energies results in similar inverse slopes (of the $m_T$-spectra of the hadrons in the final state) at RHIC and SPS energies.

## Key claim

The inverse slopes of the transverse-mass spectra for multiple strange baryons (Ξ, Ω) are practically unaffected by the purely hadronic stage of the reaction, while the flow of protons and Λ's increases; this result depends on the specific assumption of a first-order phase transition and the use of a hybrid model combining hydrodynamics with microscopic transport.

## Method

The authors employed a hybrid model: relativistic hydrodynamics with a first-order phase transition (MIT bag model EoS for the QGP, ideal hadron gas for the confined phase) described the expansion and hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma, while the microscopic transport model UrQMD was used to propagate the hadrons after hadronization. The hadronization hypersurface was determined from the hydrodynamic solution, and the hadrons generated on that surface were then fed into UrQMD, which followed their individual interactions until freeze-out. The model assumed boost-invariant longitudinal flow and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion, with initial conditions tuned to SPS (Pb+Pb at √s=17A GeV) and RHIC (Au+Au at √s=200A GeV) energies.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant fixed to B = 380 MeV/fm³, with critical temperature T_C = 160 MeV.
- For SPS: initial energy density ε_i = 6.1 GeV/fm³, net baryon density ρ_i = 4.5 ρ₀, initial proper time τ_i = 1 fm/c, transverse radius R_T = 6 fm, net baryon rapidity density dN_B/dy = 80, specific entropy s/ρ_B = 45 ± 5.
- For RHIC: initial energy density ε_i = 20 GeV/fm³, net baryon density ρ_i = 2.3 ρ₀, initial proper time τ_i = 0.6 fm, net baryon rapidity density dN_B/dy = 25, specific entropy s/ρ_B = 205, transverse energy on initial hyperbola dE_T/dy = 1.3 TeV, decreasing to 720 GeV on the hadronization hypersurface.
- Collective transverse flow velocity on the hadronization hypersurface increases from ≈0.3 (SPS) to ≈0.35 (RHIC).
- Inverse slopes T* were obtained by fitting dN_i/d²m_T dy to exp(-m_T/T*) in the range m_T - m_i < 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid hydrodynamics-plus-transport approach of Dumitri and Rischke (1999) and addresses the experimental observation by the NA49 and WA97 collaborations that multiple strange baryon spectra at SPS are "softer" than expected from a linear interpolation of proton and deuteron inverse slopes, as noted by van Hecke, Sorge, and Xu (1998). The model enables the interpretation that multiple strange baryons are emitted directly from the hadronization hypersurface, making their spectra a probe of the QGP equation of state and the phase transition temperature, rather than of hadronic rescattering.
