# Reply to Comment [arXiv:0809.2310] on Universal Charge-Radius Relation for Subatomic and Astrophysical Compact Objects [arXiv:0804.2140, PRL100 (2008) 151102]

> A reply to arXiv:0809.2310, "Comment on Universal Charge-Radius Relation for Subatomic and Astrophysical Compact Objects" [arXiv:0804.2140,PRL100(2008)151102]

# Reply to Comment on Universal Charge-Radius Relation for Subatomic and Astrophysical Compact Objects

## Key claim

The universal charge-radius relation for spherical objects with radius \(R \gg m_e^{-1} \approx 4 \times 10^2\) fm is \(Z_\infty(R) \approx 2 m_e R / \alpha = 0.71 R_\text{fm}\), but this holds only in the limit of infinite time for electron-positron pair creation; for finite time and \(R \gg 25 m_e^{-1} \approx 10^4\) fm, the relation changes to \(Z(R) \approx 11.2 m_e^2 R^2 = 7 \times 10^{-5} R_\text{fm}^2\). The Comment by Cheng, Harko, and Shabad (2008) misinterprets the threshold charge \(Z_\text{thr}^{(1)}(R)\) as an attractor in the charge-radius plane, whereas it actually marks only the onset of pair production and does not act as an attractor.

## Method

The author re-examines the Dirac equation solutions for a spherical shell core potential, distinguishing between the infinite-time universal relation \(Z_\infty(R)\) and the finite-time relation for large radii. The analysis addresses the self-consistent screening problem: when the core charge exceeds the threshold for the 1s\(_{1/2}\) state diving into the negative energy continuum, two units of charge are shielded by electrons; for higher thresholds, additional screening by p\(_{1/2}\) electrons becomes available, and the electron charge must be included self-consistently. The author compares the curves from Madsen (2008), Cheng et al. (2008), and Dicus et al. (2008) to show that only for \(Z_\text{core}(R) > Z^\text{Mads}(R)\) does the universal relation hold, while for intermediate charges a self-consistent analysis would map out a sequence of steady-state \(Z(R)\) curves.

## Key numbers

- The universal relation for infinite time: \(Z_\infty(R) \approx 2 m_e R / \alpha = 0.71 R_\text{fm}\), valid for \(R \gg m_e^{-1} \approx 4 \times 10^2\) fm.
- For finite time and \(R \gg 25 m_e^{-1} \approx 10^4\) fm, the relation becomes \(Z(R) \approx 11.2 m_e^2 R^2 = 7 \times 10^{-5} R_\text{fm}^2\).
- The transition from core-specific charge to the universal asymptotic relation occurs smoothly for \(R \approx m_e^{-1}\).

## Context

This reply defends the universal charge-radius relation derived in Madsen (2008) against the Comment by Cheng, Harko, and Shabad (2008), which proposed that the threshold charge \(Z_\text{thr}^{(1)}(R)\) acts as an additional attractor. The author notes that Dicus et al. (2008) previously confirmed the asymptotic behavior of the threshold charges for s- and p-waves, supporting the original universal relation. The work clarifies that self-consistent screening, as discussed in Greiner (1985) and Muller et al. (1975), must be included for intermediate charges, and that the threshold marks only the onset of pair production, not an attractor. This enables future self-consistent calculations of the charge-radius curves for objects in the regime between the threshold and the universal relation.
