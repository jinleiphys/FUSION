# Nuclear multifragmentation induced by electromagnetic fields of ultrarelativistic heavy ions

> We study the disintegration of nuclei by strong electromagnetic fields induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ions. The proposed multi-step model includes 1) the absorption of a virtual photon by a nucleus, 2) intranuclear cascades of produced hadrons and 3) statistical decay of the excited residual nucleus. The combined model describes well existing data on projectile fragmentation at energy 200 GeV per nucleon. Electromagnetic multifragmentation of nuclei is predicted to be an important reaction mechanism at RHIC and LHC energies.

# Nuclear multifragmentation induced by electromagnetic fields of ultrarelativistic heavy ions

## Key claim

Electromagnetic dissociation (ED) of nuclei in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies will produce multifragmentation yields comparable to or larger than nuclear fragmentation cross sections, but only for collisions with impact parameters exceeding the sum of nuclear radii where the Weizsäcker-Williams method applies and single-photon absorption dominates.

## Method

The authors combine three sequential stages: (1) virtual photon absorption via the Weizsäcker-Williams method with a point-charge spectrum integrated over impact parameters above a minimum value; (2) an intranuclear cascade (INC) model for hadron production and propagation, including a version with trawling (local density depletion); and (3) statistical decay of the residual excited nucleus using the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) with microcanonical break-up channels and subsequent secondary decay. The model is applied to projectile fragmentation at 200A GeV for validation and then extrapolated to RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- For 200A GeV oxygen on various targets, the model shows satisfactory agreement with measured isotope yields; for 14.5A GeV the agreement is poor.
- For 238U on 238U at RHIC energy, σ_ED reaches 40 b, compared to a nuclear cross section of 6.9 b.
- For 197Au on 197Au at RHIC energy, σ_ED is 23.8 b, compared to a nuclear cross section of 6.1 b.
- For Pb+Pb at LHC energies, the probability of absorbing two photons with energies above 0.4 GeV is less than 9%, and for three photons less than 1.6%.
- The average pion multiplicity n_π from ED is ~1 even at LHC energies, much lower than in nuclear collisions.
- For Pb+Pb at LHC, σ_ED exceeds σ_nuc by more than a factor of 10; σ_ED^IMF (intermediate mass fragments) is comparable to or greater than σ_nuc.
- The minimum impact parameter is given by b_min = r_0 (A_p^(1/3) + A_t^(1/3) - X(A_p^(-1/3) + A_t^(-1/3))) with r_0 = 1.34 fm and curvature parameter X = 0.75.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of electromagnetic dissociation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, notably the calculations by Baur and Bertulani (1990) for 238U on 238U and by Mercier et al. (1989) for 197Au on 197Au, which focused on giant dipole resonance excitation. The authors incorporate high-energy virtual photons above the pion production threshold, which previous work by Llope and Braun-Munzinger (1995) had shown to be dominated by single-photon absorption above ~10A GeV. The combined INC+SMM approach had been previously applied to nuclear multifragmentation by Bondorf et al. (1995) and Botvina et al. (1990). The predictions enable experimental planning for RHIC and LHC, where ED will affect beam lifetime and produce significant background in particle and fragment measurements.
