# Quark-hadron crossover equations of state for neutron stars: constraining the chiral invariant mass in a parity doublet model

> We construct an equation of state (EOS) for neutron stars by interpolating hadronic EOS at low density and quark EOS at high density. A hadronic model based on the parity doublet structure is used for hadronic matter and a quark model of Nambu--Jona-Lasinio type is for quark matter. We assume crossover between hadronic matter and quark matter in the the color-flavor locked phase. The nucleon mass of the parity doublet model has a mass associated with the chiral symmetry breaking, and a chiral invariant mass $m_0$ which is insensitive to the chiral condensate. The value of $m_0$ affects the nuclear EOSs at low density, and has strong correlations with the radii of neutron stars. Using the constraint to the radius obtained by LIGO-Virgo and NICER, we find that $m_0$ is restricted as $600\,\mathrm{MeV}\lesssim m_0 \lesssim 900\,\mathrm{MeV}$.

## Key claim

The chiral invariant mass \(m_0\) in a parity doublet model for nucleons is constrained to \(600\,\mathrm{MeV}\lesssim m_0 \lesssim 900\,\mathrm{MeV}\) when the resulting unified equations of state for neutron stars are required to satisfy radius constraints from LIGO-Virgo and NICER, as well as the maximum mass constraint from PSR J0740+6620; this range is narrower than the \(500\,\mathrm{MeV}\leq m_0 \leq 900\,\mathrm{MeV}\) allowed by nuclear saturation properties alone.

## Method

The authors construct a unified neutron star equation of state by interpolating a hadronic equation of state from a parity doublet model (PDM) at baryon densities \(n_B < 2n_0\) (with \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)) and a quark matter equation of state from a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio-type model in the color-flavor locked phase at \(n_B > 5n_0\), using a fifth-order polynomial in baryon chemical potential for the crossover region \(2n_0 < n_B < 5n_0\). The PDM parameters are fixed for each chosen \(m_0\) by fitting vacuum hadron masses and nuclear saturation properties (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility \(K_0 = 240\,\mathrm{MeV}\), symmetry energy \(S_0 = 31\,\mathrm{MeV}\)), while the quark model parameters (diquark coupling \(H\) and vector coupling \(g_V\)) are varied subject to thermodynamic stability and causality. The resulting unified equations of state are then used to compute mass-radius relations via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation, including the BPS crust equation of state for \(n_B \leq 0.1\,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

* The chiral invariant mass is restricted to \(600\,\mathrm{MeV}\lesssim m_0 \lesssim 900\,\mathrm{MeV}\) by combining neutron star radius constraints from LIGO-Virgo and NICER with the \(2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09}\,M_\odot\) maximum mass constraint from PSR J0740+6620.
* The nuclear saturation density is taken as \(n_0 = 0.16\,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the binding energy as \(B_0 = 16\,\mathrm{MeV}\), the incompressibility as \(K_0 = 240\,\mathrm{MeV}\), and the symmetry energy as \(S_0 = 31\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
* For \(m_0 = 800\,\mathrm{MeV}\), the parameter set \((H/G, g_V/G) = (1.45, 1.2)\) is excluded because the squared speed of sound exceeds 1 (causality violation), while \((H/G, g_V/G) = (1.45, 1.0)\) is allowed.
* The slope parameter \(L_0\) varies from \(93.76\,\mathrm{MeV}\) at \(m_0 = 500\,\mathrm{MeV}\) to \(80.08\,\mathrm{MeV}\) at \(m_0 = 900\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
* The GW170817 primary radius constraint is \(10.8_{-1.7}^{+2.0}\,\mathrm{km}\) at mass \(1.46_{-0.10}^{+0.12}\,M_\odot\); the secondary radius constraint is \(10.7_{-1.5}^{+2.1}\,\mathrm{km}\) at mass \(1.27_{-0.09}^{+0.09}\,M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model analyses of Motohiro et al. (2015) and Yamazaki et al. (2019) by constructing a unified equation of state that interpolates between hadronic matter at low density and quark matter at high density, rather than extrapolating the hadronic model alone to \(n_B \approx 3n_0\). It follows the interpolation approach of Masuda et al. (2012) and Baym et al. (2017, 2019), who emphasized that pure hadronic descriptions become questionable above \(n_B \gtrsim 2n_0\). The resulting constraints on \(m_0\) are tighter than the \(m_0 \gtrsim 600\,\mathrm{MeV}\) lower bound from Yamazaki et al. (2019) based solely on the tidal deformability from GW170817, and also provide an upper bound from the \(2M_\odot\) constraint and radius lower bounds. The allowed range of quark model couplings \((H, g_V)\) is larger than the Fierz-transformed estimates \((H/G, g_V/G) = (0.5, 0.5)\) used in earlier hybrid equations of state with first-order phase transitions, which tended to yield maximum masses below \(2M_\odot\).
