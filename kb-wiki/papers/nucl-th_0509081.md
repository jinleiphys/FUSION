# Elliptic Flow Analysis at RHIC: Fluctuations vs. Non-Flow Effects

> The cumulant method is applied to study elliptic flow ($v_2$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$AGeV, with the UrQMD model. In this approach, the true event plane is known and both the non-flow effects and event-by-event spatial ($ε$) and $v_2$ fluctuations exist. Qualitatively, the hierarchy of $v_2$'s from two, four and six-particle cumulants is consistent with the STAR data, however, the magnitude of $v_2$ in the UrQMD model is only 60% of the data. We find that the four and six-particle cumulants are good measures of the real elliptic flow over a wide range of centralities except for the most central and very peripheral events. There the cumulant method is affected by the $v_2$ fluctuations. In mid-central collisions, the four and six-particle cumulants are shown to give a good estimation of the true differential $v_2$, especially at large transverse momentum, where the two-particle cumulant method is heavily affected by the non-flow effects.

## Key claim

The hierarchy of elliptic flow values from two-, four-, and six-particle cumulants observed in STAR data can be reproduced by the UrQMD model, but the model's absolute magnitude of \(v_2\) is only 60% of the experimental data. The four- and six-particle cumulants provide good estimates of the true elliptic flow over a wide range of centralities (approximately 10-50% of the total cross section), except in the most central and very peripheral events where event-by-event \(v_2\) fluctuations become important.

## Method

The authors applied the cumulant method to Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) AGeV simulated with the UrQMD transport model (v2.2), using more than \(1.3 \times 10^6\) minimum-bias events plus an additional \(7 \times 10^5\) events for the most peripheral bin (60-70%). The true reaction plane angle is known in the model, allowing direct calculation of the exact elliptic flow from its definition \(v_2 = \langle \cos 2(\phi - \Phi_R) \rangle\). The cumulant results for integral and differential \(v_2\) were compared to the exact \(v_2\) across centrality bins, as a function of transverse momentum (\(p_T\)) and pseudorapidity (\(\eta\)).

## Key numbers

- The UrQMD model's integral \(v_2\) magnitude is only 60% of the experimental STAR data.
- The two-particle cumulant \(v_2\{2\}\) is larger than the exact \(v_2\) by 18% at all centralities.
- In mid-central collisions (10-50% of total cross section), four- and six-particle cumulants agree well with the exact \(v_2\).
- For semi-central collisions (20-30% centrality), the six-particle cumulant \(v_2\{6\}\) agrees with the exact \(v_2\) over the whole \(p_T\) range within statistical error, while \(v_2\{4\}\) remains about 4% larger than the exact \(v_2\).
- The \(g_2\) factor from UrQMD was rescaled by a factor of 0.186 to compare with STAR data at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) AGeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow fluctuations and non-flow effects previously addressed by Miller and Snellings (2003) and by the STAR collaboration (2004). The authors use the UrQMD transport model, which naturally includes non-flow correlations (transverse momentum conservation, resonance decays, jet production, final-state interactions) and event-by-event fluctuations, unlike hydrodynamic models that assume local equilibrium. The results support the use of the ZDC-SMD method at STAR for reliable flow measurements over the whole centrality range, and suggest that the PHENIX reaction plane method may suffer from non-flow effects similar to those affecting the two-particle cumulant method.
