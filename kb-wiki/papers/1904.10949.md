# Influence of finite volume effect on the Polyakov Quark-Meson model

> In the current work, we study the influence of a finite volume on $2+1$ $SU(3)$ Polyakov Quark-Meson model (PQM) order parameters, (fluctuations) correlations of conserved charges and the quark-hadron phase boundary. Our study of the PQM model order parameters and the (fluctuations) correlations of conserved charges indicates a sizable shift of the quark-hadron phase boundary to higher values of baryon chemical potential ($μ_{B}$) and temperature ($T$) for decreasing the system volume. The detailed study of such effect could have important implications for the extraction of the (fluctuations) correlations of conserved charges of the QCD phase diagram from heavy ion data.

## Key claim

The finite volume effect in the 2+1 SU(3) Polyakov Quark-Meson (PQM) model shifts the quark-hadron phase boundary to higher values of baryon chemical potential and temperature as the system volume decreases, but this conclusion is based on a simplified implementation using only a lower momentum cut-off without full inclusion of surface, curvature, and boundary condition effects.

## Method

The study uses the mean-field approximation to compute the grand potential of the PQM model, which couples chiral condensates (non-strange sigma_x and strange sigma_y) to Polyakov loop dynamics. Finite volume effects are introduced via a lower momentum cut-off p_min = pi/R, where R is the length of a cubic volume, following the approximate method of Bhattacharyya et al. (2012, 2015). The equations of motion for sigma_x, sigma_y, phi, and phi* are solved by minimizing the thermodynamic potential, and the phase transition is identified from the peak of dDelta_ls/dT, where Delta_ls is the normalized net-difference condensate.

## Key numbers

- For R = 2.0 fm, the baryon chemical potential at low temperature increased by about 30% from its value at R = infinity.
- For R = 2.0 fm, the temperature at mu_B = 0.0 GeV increased by about 19% from its value at R = infinity.
- The PQM model parameters include: c = 4807.84 MeV, m^2 = (342.52)^2 MeV^2, h_x = (120.73)^3 MeV^3, h_y = (336.41)^3 MeV^3, lambda_1 = 1.40, lambda_2 = 46.48.
- Polyakov loop potential parameters include: a_1 = 0.75, a_2 = 7.5, b_0 = 6.75, b_1 = -1.95, b_2 = 2.625, b_3 = -7.44, k_1 = 0.30, k_2 = 0.25, k_3 = 0.24, k_4 = 0.20.

## Context

This work extends previous finite-volume studies in the PQM model by Magdy (2015) and in NJL and PNJL models by Abreu et al. (2006), Bhattacharyya et al. (2012), and others, which showed conflicting volume dependence of the transition temperature. The PQM model results here show the transition temperature shifts to larger values with decreasing volume, opposite to the NJL/PNJL trend. The calculations are compared to lattice QCD results from Borsanyi et al. (2013) and Bazavov et al. (2012) for thermodynamics and conserved charge fluctuations at zero chemical potential, showing good agreement. The work enables improved extraction of conserved charge fluctuations from heavy-ion collision data by accounting for finite system size.
