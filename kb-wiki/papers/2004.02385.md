# Yield ratio of hypertriton to light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions from $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 4.9 GeV to 2.76 TeV

> We resolve the difference in the yield ratio $\rm S_3$ = $\rm \frac{N_{^3_ΛH}/N_Λ}{N_{^3He}/N_p}$ measured in Au+Au collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV and in Pb-Pb collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV by adopting a different treatment of the weak decay contribution to the proton yield in Au+Au collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. We then use the coalescence model to extract information on the $Λ$ and nucleon density fluctuations at the kinetic freeze-out of heavy ion collisions. We also show from available experimental data that the yield ratio $\rm S_2$ = $\rm \frac{N_{^3_ΛH}}{N_ΛN_ d}$ is a more promising observable than $\rm S_3$ for probing the local baryon-strangeness correlation in the produced medium.

## Key claim

The paper resolves the apparent discrepancy in the yield ratio S3 between STAR (Au+Au at 200 GeV) and ALICE (Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV) by showing that the difference is partially due to different treatments of the weak decay contribution to the proton yield, and that using the PHENIX proton yield reduces the STAR value to approximately the same as the ALICE value within uncertainties.

## Method

The authors use the coalescence model (COAL-SH formula) to relate the yields of hypertriton, helium-3, deuteron, protons, neutrons, and Lambda hyperons to density fluctuations and correlation coefficients at kinetic freeze-out. They extract the proton-neutron density fluctuation correlation coefficient alpha_np from deuteron yields, the proton density fluctuation Delta p from helium-3 yields, and then determine the Lambda-nucleon correlation coefficients from the measured S3 ratio. For the S2 ratio, they treat hypertriton as a bound Lambda-deuteron system and extract the Lambda-deuteron density fluctuation correlation coefficient alpha_Lambda-d.

## Key numbers

- S3 = 0.36 ± 0.26 in central 11.5A GeV/c Au+Pt collisions (AGS)
- S3 = 1.08 ± 0.22 ± 0.16 in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (STAR)
- S3 = 0.60 ± 0.13 ± 0.21 in central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (ALICE)
- S3 = 0.90 ± 0.22 ± 0.15 in Au+Au at 200 GeV using PHENIX proton yield (STAR+PH)
- Extracted alpha_np values range from -0.781 ± 0.026 at sqrt(s_NN) = 4.9 GeV to -0.717 ± 0.023 at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for 0-10% central collisions
- Extracted Delta p values: 0.656 ± 0.049 at sqrt(s_NN) = 4.9 GeV, 0.536 ± 0.083 at 200 GeV, and 0.727 ± 0.085 at 2.76 TeV
- S2/B2 ratio at LHC energy for momentum per constituent p_T/A = 1.4 GeV/c is 0.899 ± 0.171

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model studies of Sun (2018) and Sun (2015) that previously explained the S3 difference by assuming early Lambda freeze-out. It contradicts the statistical model predictions of Andronic (2010), Cleymans (2011), and Steinheimer (2012), which gave smaller S3 values than the original STAR measurement. The paper enables future experimental measurements of S2/B2 across a broad energy range from AGS to RHIC to extract the Lambda-deuteron density correlation coefficient, which shows a stronger energy dependence than the Lambda-nucleon correlation and may probe the QGP to hadronic matter phase transition.
