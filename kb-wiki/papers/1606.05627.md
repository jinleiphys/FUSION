# Anomalous transport model study of chiral magnetic effects in heavy ion collisions

> Using an anomalous transport model for massless quarks and antiquarks, we study the effect of a magnetic field on the elliptic flows of quarks and antiquarks in relativistic heavy ion collisions. With initial conditions from a blast wave model and assuming that the strong magnetic field produced in non-central heavy ion collisions can last for a sufficiently long time, we obtain an appreciable electric quadrupole moment in the transverse plane of a heavy ion collision. The electric quadrupole moment subsequently leads to a splitting between the elliptic flows of quarks and antiquarks. The slope of the charge asymmetry dependence of the elliptic flow difference between positively and negatively charged particles is positive, which is expected from the chiral magnetic wave formed in the produced QGP and observed in experiments at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, only if the Lorentz force acting on the charged particles is neglected and the quark-antiquark scattering is assumed to be dominated by the chirality changing channel.

## Key claim

The slope of the charge asymmetry dependence of the elliptic flow difference between positively and negatively charged particles is positive, as expected from the chiral magnetic wave and observed at RHIC, **only if** the Lorentz force acting on charged particles is neglected and quark-antiquark scattering is assumed to be dominated by the chirality changing channel.

## Method

The authors solved the noncovariant chiral kinetic equation for massless quarks and antiquarks using the test particle method, including scatterings via a geometric cross-section approach with isotropic final-state momenta in the center-of-mass frame. Initial conditions were taken from a blast wave model with a Woods-Saxon temperature profile, boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, and a uniform magnetic field along the y-axis with a time dependence \( eB = eB_0 / [1 + (t/\tau)^2] \). Partons were evolved until kinetic freeze-out at 120 MeV, with quarks converted to pions via quark-hadron duality below the chiral restoration temperature of 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field parameters: \( eB_0 = 7 m_\pi^2 \) and lifetime \( \tau = 6 \) fm/\(c\).
- Blast wave parameters for Au+Au at \( \sqrt{s} = 200 \) GeV, impact parameter \( b = 9 \) fm (30–40% centrality): \( T_0 = 300 \) MeV, \( R = 3.5 \) fm, \( a = 0.5 \) fm, \( c = 1.5 \), thermalization time \( \tau_0 = 0.4 \) fm/\(c\).
- Total number of quarks and antiquarks: approximately 1500 for rapidities \( |y| \le 2 \).
- Parton scattering cross section: \( \sigma = \sigma_0 (T_0/T)^3 \) with \( \sigma_0 = 13.7 \) mb for the full calculation (CKM+LF+CCS), 13.1 mb for CKM+LF, and 15.5 mb for CKM+CCS.
- Integrated \( v_2 \) of pions with \( 0.15 \le p_T \le 0.5 \) GeV/\(c\): 0.036 in all three cases.
- Slope \( r \) of \( \Delta v_2 \) vs. charge asymmetry \( A_\pm \) for CKM+CCS: \( r = 0.0112 \), giving \( r/v_2 = 0.31 \), about a factor of three smaller than the experimental value of 0.85.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) in heavy ion collisions, which were based on anomalous hydrodynamics (Hongo 2013; PhysRevC.89.044909) and schematic wave-equation models (PhysRevLett.107.052303). It provides a microscopic transport treatment using the chiral kinetic equation derived from Berry-phase considerations (PhysRevLett.109.162001; Son 2012; Manuel 2014), enabling the study of nonequilibrium effects and the separate roles of chiral kinetic motion, Lorentz force, and chirality-changing scatterings. The results enable future work to include magnetic-field modifications to the collision term, the effect of collisions on the chiral kinetic equation itself, and the role of mean-field potentials for particles versus antiparticles.
