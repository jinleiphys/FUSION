# Hadron Propagation in Medium: the Exclusive Process A(e,e'p)B in Few-Nucleon Systems

> The mechanism of propagation of hadronic states in the medium is a key point for understanding particle-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering at high energies. We have investigated the propagation of a baryon in the exclusive process A(e,e'p)B in few-nucleon systems using realistic nuclear wave functions and Glauber multiple scattering theory both in its original form and within a generalized eikonal approximation. New results for the processes 3He(e,e'p)2H and 4He(e,e'p)3H are compared with data recently obtained at the Thomas Jefferson Laboratory (JLAB).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that final-state interactions (FSI) in exclusive A(e,e'p)B processes on few-nucleon systems can be described by elastic rescattering within the Glauber multiple scattering approach and its generalized eikonal approximation, but the agreement with data is systematically better for perpendicular kinematics than for parallel kinematics in the 4He case, and the difference between the standard Glauber approximation and the generalized eikonal approximation is very small (a few percent) in the energy-momentum range covered by the data.

## Method

The authors calculated the distorted spectral function and differential cross section for the reactions 3He(e,e'p)2H, 3He(e,e'p)(np), and 4He(e,e'p)3H using realistic nonrelativistic wave functions (AV18 for 3He, RSC V8 for 4He) and the Glauber multiple scattering theory both in its original form (GA) and within a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) that partially removes the frozen approximation by including the excitation energy of the A-1 system. The cross section was computed in a factorized form using the De Forest cc1 electron-nucleon cross section, with the profile function parameters taken from the Particle Data Group. For 4He, the authors also introduced finite formation time (FFT) effects by replacing the step function in the Glauber operator with a damping factor that depends on the proton formation length.

## Key numbers

- For 3He(e,e'p)2H and 3He(e,e'p)(np), the GA and GEA results differ by only a few percent.
- In 4He(e,e'p)3H, single rescattering dominates at missing momentum p_m ≤ 600 MeV/c, while triple rescattering contributes significantly at p_m > 800 MeV/c.
- The JLAB kinematics for 4He(e,e'p)3H had Q^2 = 1.78 (GeV/c)^2 and x ~ 1.8, where FFT effects were too small to be detected.
- FFT effects in 4He(e,e'p)3H could be unambiguously detected in the region 5 ≤ Q^2 ≤ 10 (GeV/c)^2 at p_m ~ 430 MeV/c with x = 1.4.
- The dip predicted by the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) for 4He(e,e'p)3H in parallel kinematics is completely filled by FSI.

## Context

This work extends the generalized eikonal approximation calculations previously applied to 3He(e,e'p)2H and 3He(e,e'p)(np) by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (2005) to the four-body system 4He(e,e'p)3H, using data from JLAB experiments E89044 and E97111. The approach builds on the diagrammatic reformulation of Glauber theory developed by Gribov (1970) and later generalized to A(e,e'p)X by Frankfurt and Strikman (1997) and Miskimen et al. (2005). The finite formation time treatment follows the method of Braun (2000) and is relevant for investigating color transparency effects at high Q^2. The results enable future experimental searches for FFT effects in 4He(e,e'p)3H at Q^2 ≥ 2 (GeV/c)^2 in the dip region around p_m ~ 430 MeV/c.
