# Stochastic number projection method in the pairing-force problem

> A new stochastic number projection method is proposed. The component of the BCS wave function corresponding to the right number of particles is obtained by means of a Metropolis algorithm in which the weight functions are constructed from the single-particle occupation probability. Either standard BCS or Lipkin-Nogami probability distributions can be used, thus the method is applicable for any pairing strength. The accuracy of the method is tested in the computation of pairing energies of model and real systems.

# Stochastic number projection method in the pairing-force problem

## Key claim

The proposed Metropolis-based stochastic number projection method (MCP) can accurately extract the N-particle component of a BCS wave function for any pairing strength, but its validity depends on the assumption that the BCS functional form holds for the ground-state wave function; the method works with either standard BCS or Lipkin-Nogami occupation probabilities.

## Method

The authors construct the N-particle projected BCS wave function as a sum over all combinations of N/2 occupied time-reversed pair states, with weights proportional to products of v_j^2/u_j^2. They evaluate the projected ground-state energy via a Metropolis algorithm that generates ensembles of occupied-pair configurations with probability equal to these weights, starting from the unperturbed ground state with 10^5 thermalization steps and using trial moves that randomly shift one pair from an occupied to an empty level. The acceptance ratio from thermalization determines the total number of Metropolis steps (300 times the decorrelation steps, where decorrelation steps = 50/R).

## Key numbers

- For the symmetric two-level model with Omega=10 and N=4, MCP_LNM gives pairing energy 0.205(3) at G=0.024, compared to exact 0.202 and BCS 0.161.
- For the same model with Omega=10 and N=10 at G=0.060, MCP_LNM gives 0.353(9) while exact is 0.36 and BCS gives 0.000 (no nontrivial solution).
- For the equispaced model with Omega=N=8 at G=0.7, MCP_BCS gives 5.28(5) versus exact 5.309 and BCS 3.89.
- For the 100Zr nucleus with G=0.255, MCP_BCS gives pairing energy 5.1(1) and MCP_LNM gives 4.98(6), compared to DMC benchmark 5.0 and BCS 3.87.
- The Metropolis algorithm uses 10^5 thermalization steps and total Metropolis steps = 300 * N_decorrelation, where N_decorrelation = 50/R.

## Context

This work extends the line of particle-number projection methods for BCS wave functions, following the demonstration by Kerman et al. (1961) that projected BCS functions approximate exact solutions well. It addresses the known failures of standard BCS for small particle numbers or weak pairing (where BCS may have no nontrivial solution, as shown by Rho and Rasmussen (1964)), and the limitations of the diffusion Monte Carlo method of Cerf (1993) which requires strictly positive interaction matrix elements. The method enables exact projection for any pairing interaction, including Coulomb potentials, and has been applied to electron-hole systems in quantum dots and is proposed for ultrasmall superconducting grains and molecular correlation problems.
