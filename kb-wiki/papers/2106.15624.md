# Spin asymmetries in electron-jet production at the future electron ion collider

> We study all the possible spin asymmetries that can arise in back-to-back electron-jet production, $ep\rightarrow e+\text{jet}+X$, as well as the associated jet fragmentation process, $ep\rightarrow e+ \text{jet} (h)+X$, in electron-proton collisions. We derive the factorization formalism for these spin asymmetries and perform the corresponding phenomenology for the kinematics relevant to the future electron ion collider. In the case of unpolarized electron-proton scattering, we also give predictions for azimuthal asymmetries for the HERA experiment. This demonstrates that electron-jet production is an outstanding process for probing unpolarized and polarized transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that back-to-back electron-jet production at the future Electron-Ion Collider can probe all leading-twist transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) and transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions (TMDFFs) separately, unlike semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering where these functions appear in convolution; however, this claim is conditioned on the use of standard jet axis definitions and the back-to-back kinematic limit where the transverse momentum imbalance \(q_T\) is much smaller than the average transverse momentum \(p_T\).

## Method

The authors derive the TMD factorization formalism for the process \(ep\rightarrow e+\text{jet}+X\) and the associated jet fragmentation process \(ep\rightarrow e+\text{jet}(h)+X\) in the back-to-back limit, working in the one-photon exchange approximation and using the narrow jet approximation with \(R\ll 1\). They express the cross section in terms of structure functions for various beam and target polarizations, perform Fourier transforms to \(\bm{b}\)-space, and apply the Collins-Soper-Sterman formalism with the \(b_*\) prescription to handle non-perturbative Sudakov factors. Phenomenological predictions are made for EIC kinematics and, for unpolarized scattering, for HERA kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The non-perturbative Sudakov factor uses parameters \(g_2 = 0.84\), \(g_1^f = 0.106\) GeV\(^2\), and \(Q_0 = \sqrt{2.4}\) GeV, with \(b_{\text{max}} = 1.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The Born cross section for unpolarized scattering is \(\hat{\sigma}_0 = \frac{\alpha_{\text{em}}\alpha_s}{sQ^2}\frac{2(\hat{u}^2+\hat{s}^2)}{\hat{t}^2}\), and for longitudinally polarized scattering \(\hat{\sigma}_L = \frac{\alpha_{\text{em}}\alpha_s}{sQ^2}\frac{2(\hat{u}^2-\hat{s}^2)}{\hat{t}^2}\).
- The NLO hard function includes terms such as \(-\ln^2(\mu^2/Q^2) - 3\ln(\mu^2/Q^2) - 8 + \pi^2/6\) multiplied by \(\alpha_s C_F/(2\pi)\).
- The NLO quark jet function includes terms such as \(\frac{1}{2}\ln^2(\mu^2/(p_T^2R^2)) + \frac{3}{2}\ln(\mu^2/(p_T^2R^2)) + 13/2 - 3\pi^2/4\) multiplied by \(\alpha_s C_F/(2\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for back-to-back electron-jet production previously proposed to study quark Sivers functions (Liu 2018, Arratia 2020) to include all spin asymmetries and jet fragmentation. It contrasts with semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (Bacchetta 2006), where TMDPDFs and TMDFFs appear in convolution and require additional processes like Drell-Yan and \(e^+e^-\) dihadron production to separate them. The results enable simultaneous extraction of TMDPDFs from the \(\bm{q}_T\) dependence and TMDFFs from the \(\bm{j}_\perp\) dependence at the EIC, and provide predictions for azimuthal asymmetries at HERA.
