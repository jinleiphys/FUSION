# Modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions in the few-GeV regime

> Detecting neutrinos and extracting the information they bring along is an ambitions task that requires a detailed understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions over a broad energy range. We present calculations for quasi-elastic neutrino-induced nucleon knockout reactions on atomic nuclei and neutrino-induced pion production reactions. In our models, final-state interactions are introduced using a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) approach. For interactions at low incoming neutrino energies, long-range correlations are implemented by means of a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) approach. As neutrinos are the only particles interacting solely by means of the weak interaction, they can reveal information about e.g. the structure of nuclei or the strange quark content of the nucleon that is difficult to obtain otherwise. We investigated these effects and present results for the sensitivity of neutrino interactions to the influence of the nucleon's strange quark sea.

# Modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions in the few-GeV regime

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a combined analysis of parity-violating electron scattering and neutrino-induced processes offers the best prospects for understanding the nucleon's strange quark sea, but notes that antineutrino-induced processes exhibit a more pronounced strangeness sensitivity than neutrino-induced ones, and that extracting axial strangeness from neutrino scattering requires a thorough prior understanding of vector strangeness effects.

## Method
The authors calculate quasi-elastic neutrino-induced nucleon knockout and neutrino-induced pion production reactions using a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) for final-state interactions, and a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) for long-range correlations at low incoming neutrino energies. Bound-state wave functions are obtained within the Hartree approximation to the sigma-omega model using the W1 parametrization. For resonant and coherent pion production, the matrix element is evaluated in the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation with medium modifications for the Delta resonance.

## Key numbers
- Charged-current cross sections are computed for 12C(nu_mu, mu-) and 56Fe(nu_mu, mu-) as a function of outgoing muon energy epsilon', comparing RMSGA, RDWIA, and RPWIA results.
- Strangeness sensitivity is evaluated for 1 GeV neutrino scattering off 12C, comparing the relative sensitivity |R(s=0)-R(s)|/|R(s=0)| as a function of strangeness form factors g_A^s, mu_s, and r_s^2.
- Resonant pion production off 16O is shown for 1.3 GeV incoming neutrinos.
- Coherent pion production calculations on 12C compare full and local approximation results for charged-current reactions.
- CRPA correlations are shown as a ratio of mean-field to CRPA cross sections as a function of Q^2 for incoming neutrino energies ranging from 200 to 600 MeV with 12C as target.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of neutrino-nucleus interactions by combining RMSGA for final-state interactions with CRPA for low-energy correlations, building on earlier work by the same authors and collaborators (Jachowicz and Vancraeyveld, previous studies; references cited as "us" and "dimi" for RMSGA, "strang" for strangeness, "chris" for pion production, and "crpaus1, crpaus2" for CRPA). The results enable more accurate modeling of exclusive cross sections needed for interpreting neutrino oscillation experiments, and suggest that combined parity-violating electron scattering and neutrino scattering analyses could resolve the nucleon's strange quark content. The paper also notes that at higher energies the Fermi Gas model is remarkably accurate for inclusive cross sections, but at lower energies nuclear effects become preponderant and require the CRPA treatment.
