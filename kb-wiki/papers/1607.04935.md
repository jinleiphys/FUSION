# Heavy meson spectroscopy under strong magnetic field

> Spectra of the neutral heavy mesons, $η_c(1S,2S)$, $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, $η_b(1S,2S,3S)$, $Υ(1S,2S,3S)$, $D$, $D^\ast$, $B$, $B^\ast$, $B_s$ and $B_s^\ast$, in a homogeneous magnetic field are analyzed in a potential model of constituent quarks. To obtain anisotropic wave functions and the corresponding eigenvalues, the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method is applied, where the wave functions for transverse and longitudinal directions in the cylindrical coordinate are expanded by the Gaussian bases separately. Energy level structures in the wide range of magnetic fields are obtained and the deformation of the wave functions is shown, which reflects effects of the spin mixing, the Zeeman splitting and quark Landau levels. The contribution from the magnetic catalysis in heavy-light mesons is discussed as a change of the light constituent quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper systematically computes heavy meson spectra in a homogeneous magnetic field using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model, but the results are limited to neutral mesons at rest (pseudomomentum K=0) and do not include the magnetic-field dependence of the confining potential parameters, which the authors note could be important for future improvements.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Schrödinger equation for heavy mesons in a magnetic field using the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method (CGEM), an extension of the conventional Gaussian expansion method. The wave functions are expanded separately in Gaussian bases for the transverse (ρ) and longitudinal (z) directions in cylindrical coordinates. A coupled-channel basis is employed to handle the spin mixing between the singlet (|00⟩) and longitudinal triplet (|10⟩) states induced by the quark magnetic moment interaction −μ·B. The Cornell potential with a spin-spin interaction is used, and parameters are fitted to experimental meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Mass shifts at eB=0.3 GeV² for transverse charmonia: +12.4 MeV for J/ψ and +50.8 MeV for ψ(2S)
- Mass shifts at eB=0.1 GeV² for mixed charmonia: −9.9 MeV (first state from ηc(1S)), +12.4 MeV (second state from J/ψ), −16.6 MeV (third state from ηc(2S)), +28.8 MeV (fourth state from ψ(2S))
- Crossing magnetic field for second and third charmonium states: eB≈1.0–1.1 GeV²
- Charm quark kinetic mass: mc=1.7840 GeV; bottom quark kinetic mass: mb=4.8078 GeV
- String tension for charmonium: √σ=0.402 GeV; for Ds meson: √σ=0.324 GeV
- Light constituent quark mass: mq=0.3 GeV; strange quark mass: ms=0.656 GeV
- Hyperfine splitting in vacuum: Δm(J/ψ−ηc)=113 MeV; Δm(ψ(2S)−ηc(2S))=47 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on charmonia in magnetic fields (Suzuki 2016) to a systematic survey including bottomonia and heavy-light mesons (D, D*, B, B*, Bs, Bs*). It uses a potential model approach similar to Alford (2013) and Bonati (2015), who applied the finite difference time domain method for ground states, but the CGEM allows simultaneous treatment of ground and excited states. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD simulations and QCD sum rule calculations (Cho 2014, Cho 2014) for spectral functions under magnetic fields, and provide predictions for level crossing phenomena that could be observed in dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions.
