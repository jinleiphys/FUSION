# Parton-distribution functions for the pion and kaon in the gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral-quark model

> We investigate the parton-distribution functions (PDFs) for the positively charged pion and kaon at a low-renormalization scale ~ 1 GeV. To this end, we employ the gauge-invariant effective chiral action from the nonlocal chiral-quark model, resulting in that the vector currents are conserved. All the model parameters are determined phenomenologically with the normalization condition for PDF and the empirical values for the pseudoscalar-meson weak-decay constants. We consider the momentum dependence of the effective quark mass properly within the model calculations. It turns out that the leading local contribution provides about 70 % of the total strength for PDF, whereas the nonlocal one that is newly taken into account in this work for the gauge invariance does the rest. High-Q^2 evolution to 27 GeV^2 is performed for valance-quark distribution function (VQDF), using the DGALP equation. The moments for the pion and kaon VQDFs are also computed. The numerical results are compared with the empirical data and theoretical estimations, and show qualitatively agreement with them.

## Key claim

The paper presents a direct calculation of pion and kaon parton-distribution functions (PDFs) using a gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral-quark model, where the newly included nonlocal contribution (necessary for gauge invariance) provides about 30% of the total PDF strength, whereas the abstract states only that the local contribution provides about 70% without explicitly quantifying the nonlocal remainder.

## Method

The author computes PDFs for the positively charged pion and kaon at a low renormalization scale of approximately 1 GeV using the gauge-invariant effective chiral action derived from the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM), which ensures vector-current conservation. The model parameters are determined phenomenologically by requiring the PDF normalization condition to be unity and using the empirical pion weak-decay constant (93.2 MeV) as input, while the momentum dependence of the effective quark mass is treated using a Lorentzian-type structure function. The PDFs are then evolved to high Q² = 27 GeV² using the DGLAP (Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi) equation at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO).

## Key numbers

* The constituent-quark mass at zero virtuality is determined to be M₀ ≈ 300 MeV for the chosen parameter set (μ = 1.0 GeV, M₀ = 300 MeV).
* The computed kaon weak-decay constant is F_K ≈ 121.7 MeV, which is about 7% larger than the empirical value of 113.4 MeV.
* The local contribution provides about 76% of the total PDF strength, while the nonlocal contribution provides about 24% (ratio approximately 1/3).
* The pion PDF is parameterized as x u_π(x) = 7.60 x^{2.16} (1-x)^{1.13} at the low scale μ = 1 GeV.
* The kaon valence u-quark PDF is parameterized as x u_K(x) = 18.90 x^{2.43} (1-x)^{2.00} at the low scale μ = 1 GeV.
* The kaon anti-strange PDF is parameterized as x \bar{s}_K(x) = 35.67 x^{3.47} (1-x)^{1.83} at the low scale μ = 1 GeV.
* High-Q² evolution is performed to Q² = 27 GeV² for the valence-quark distribution functions.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the same author (Nam 2011, Nam 2012) that computed PDFs via the Drell-Levi-Yan relation from fragmentation functions, but now performs a direct calculation that satisfies gauge invariance and includes momentum-dependent quark masses. The results are compared with empirical data from the Fermilab E-615 experiment (Conway 1989) for the pion and with the fitted ratio u_K/u_π from Holt (2010), showing qualitative agreement; the approach enables future studies of meson structure at higher Q² and can be compared with other theoretical approaches such as the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model (Shigetani 1993), the Dyson-Schwinger equations (Nguyen 2011), and lattice QCD simulations (Detmold 2003).
