# Exact Relations for a Strongly-interacting Fermi Gas from the Operator Product Expansion

> The momentum distribution in a Fermi gas with two spin states and a large scattering length has a tail that falls off like 1/k^4 at large momentum k, as pointed out by Shina Tan. He used novel methods to derive exact relations between the coefficient of the tail in the momentum distribution and various other properties of the system. We present simple derivations of these relations using the operator product expansion for quantum fields. We identify the coefficient as the integral over space of the expectation value of a local operator that measures the density of pairs.

# Exact Relations for a Strongly-interacting Fermi Gas from the Operator Product Expansion

## Key claim

The exact relations derived by Shina Tan for a two-spin-state Fermi gas with large scattering length, including the tail of the momentum distribution, the adiabatic relation, the energy relation, and the virial theorem, can be derived simply and systematically using Wilson's operator product expansion (OPE) for quantum fields, identifying the contact C as the integral over space of the expectation value of a local operator that measures the density of pairs. The derivations hold for any state of the system (few-body or many-body, homogeneous or trapped, superfluid or normal, zero or nonzero temperature), but the OPE approach makes the generalization to more complicated systems (such as those near a Feshbach resonance or with additional spin degrees of freedom) more straightforward.

## Method

The authors construct a nonrelativistic quantum field theory for fermions with two spin states and zero-range interactions, regularized by an ultraviolet cutoff Λ, with the coupling constant g(Λ) chosen to reproduce the scattering length a. They compute the Wilson coefficients for the OPE of the product ψ_σ^†(R − r/2) ψ_σ(R + r/2) by evaluating matrix elements in two-fermion scattering states |±p⟩, matching the nonanalytic r-linear term to the operator ψ_1^† ψ_2^† ψ_1 ψ_2^{(Λ)}. The contact C is identified as the integral of the renormalized contact density operator g^2 ψ_1^† ψ_2^† ψ_1 ψ_2, and the Tan relations are derived by inserting this OPE term into the momentum distribution, by rewriting the Hamiltonian density into ultraviolet-finite pieces, and by applying the Feynman-Hellman theorem and dimensional analysis.

## Key numbers

- The momentum distribution tail falls off as 1/k^4 at large momentum k, with ρ_σ(k) → C/k^4, where C is the contact.
- The number of fermions with momentum larger than K approaches C/(π^2 K) as K → ∞.
- The adiabatic relation: dE / d(1/a) = − (ℏ^2 / (4π m)) C.
- The coupling constant as a function of cutoff: g(Λ) = 4π a / (1 − 2 a Λ/π).
- The two-body scattering amplitude: A(E) = (4π/m) / (−1/a + √(−mE − iε)).
- The Wilson coefficient for the contact density operator in the OPE is C_{σ,12}(r) = − g^2(Λ) r / (8π).
- The virial theorem: E = 2 ∫ d^3R ⟨V⟩ − C / (8π m a).
- The inelastic loss rate: Γ ≈ (ℏ^2 (−Im a) / (2π m |a|^2)) C.

## Context

This work extends the exact relations first derived by Shina Tan (2005, 2008) using generalized functions, which showed that the momentum distribution of a two-spin-state Fermi gas with large scattering length has a 1/k^4 tail and that the coefficient C (the contact) satisfies exact relations with the energy, the derivative of energy with respect to scattering length, and the virial theorem. The authors provide simpler derivations using Wilson's operator product expansion (1969), a standard tool in relativistic quantum field theory, and identify the contact density operator explicitly as g^2 ψ_1^† ψ_2^† ψ_1 ψ_2. This identification enables direct diagrammatic calculation of the contact for homogeneous systems and facilitates generalization to systems near a Feshbach resonance (as described by the resonance model of Kokkelmans, Milstein, Côté, and Holland, 2002) and to systems with additional spin degrees of freedom, though the latter is complicated by the Efimov effect (Braaten, 2004). The results apply equally well to cold neutron matter at sufficiently low densities.
