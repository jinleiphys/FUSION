# Isovector spin-singlet (T=1, S=0) and isoscalar spin-triplet (T=0, S=1) pairing interactions and spin-isospin response

> We review several experimental and theoretical advances that emphasise common aspects of the study of T=1 and T=0 pairing correlations in nuclei. We first discuss several empirical evidences of the special role played by the T=1 pairing interaction. In particular, we show the peculiar features of the nuclear pairing interaction in the low density regime, and possible outcomes such as the BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter and, in an analogous way, in loosely bound nuclei. We then move to the competition between T=1 and T=0 pairing correlations. The effect of such competition on the low-lying spectra is studied in N=Z odd-odd nuclei by using a three-body model; it is shown that the inversion of the 0+ and 1+ states near the ground state, and the strong magnetic dipole transitions between them, can be considered as a clear manifestation of strong T=0 pairing correlations in these nuclei. The effect of T=0 pairing correlations is also quite evident if one studies charge-changing transitions. The Gamow-Teller (GT) states in N=Z+2 nuclei are studied here by using self-consistent HFB+QRPA calculations in which the T=0 pairing interaction is taken into account. Strong GT states are found, near the ground state of daughter nuclei; these are compared with available experimental data from charge-exchange reactions, and such comparison can pinpoint the value of the strength of the T=0 interaction. Pair transfer reactions are eventually discussed: while two-neutron transfer has been long proposed as a tool to measure the T=1 superfluidity in the nuclear ground states, the study of deuteron transfer is still in its infancy, despite its potential interest in revealing effects coming from both T=1 and T=0 interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while isoscalar (T=0, S=1) pairing correlations have been difficult to confirm unambiguously in nuclei, specific observables in N=Z odd-odd nuclei and charge-exchange transitions can provide clear signatures of T=0 pairing, provided one considers nuclei close to the N=Z line rather than neutron-rich systems where isoscalar effects are suppressed.

## Method

The authors review empirical evidence for T=1 pairing from odd-even mass staggering and low-lying collective excitations, then study T=0 versus T=1 competition using a three-body model for N=Z odd-odd nuclei and self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (HFB+QRPA) calculations with Skyrme forces for Gamow-Teller states in N=Z+2 nuclei. Pairing correlation energies are computed for p-shell and f-shell configurations using separable pairing interactions with a scaling factor f varied between 1 and 2 to represent the T=0 pairing strength relative to T=1.

## Key numbers

- The 3-point pairing gap formula gives Δ⁽³⁾(A) ≈ 12/A^(1/2) MeV across the mass range 16 < A < 250
- The T=1 pairing strength in the separable model is G^(T=1) = 24/A MeV when fitted to empirical pairing gaps
- The spin-orbit coupling strength is taken as V_ls = 24/A^(2/3) MeV, reproducing the empirical spin-orbit splitting Δε = 7.0 MeV between 1f_(7/2) and 1f_(5/2) states in ⁴¹Ca
- The ratio f between T=0 and T=1 pairing strengths from realistic shell-model matrix elements is f ≈ 1.6-1.7
- In the tin region, the pairing gap Δ is expected to be about 1.0 MeV from the 12/A^(1/2) formula
- The BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter occurs at densities around ρ/ρ₀ = 1/8, where the Cooper pair wave function becomes compact with short coherence length

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Goodman (1979) on generalized isospin pairing theory and by Bertsch and collaborators (2009) on conditions for T=0 pairing dominance in heavy nuclei. The paper builds on studies by Engel and collaborators (1999) and by Yoshida and collaborators (2008) showing that isoscalar pairing shifts low-energy Gamow-Teller strength downward, and on the work of Matsuo (2006) demonstrating BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter. The results enable future experimental searches for T=0 pairing signatures through deuteron transfer reactions and precise measurements of Gamow-Teller strength distributions in N=Z+2 nuclei, which can pinpoint the strength of the isoscalar pairing interaction.
