# In--Flight ($K^-,p$) Reactions for the Formation of Kaonic Atoms and Kaonic Nuclei in Green function method

> We study theoretically the kaonic atom and kaonic nucleus formations in the in--flight ($K^-,p$) reactions using the Green function method, which is suited to evaluate formation rates both of stable and unstable bound systems. We consider $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O as the targets and calculate the spectra of the ($K^-,p$) reactions. We conclude that a no peak structure due to kaonic nucleus formation is expected in the reaction spectra calculated with the chiral unitary kaon--nucleus optical potential. In the spectra with the phenomenological deep kaon--nucleus potential, we may have possibilities to observe some structures due to kaonic nucleus states. For all cases, we have peaks due to the kaonic atom formations in the reaction spectra.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that no peak structure due to kaonic nucleus formation is expected in the ($K^-,p$) reaction spectra when using the chiral unitary kaon-nucleus optical potential, but that small structures may be observable with the phenomenological deep kaon-nucleus potential; clear signals from kaonic atom formation appear in all cases, though these can take the form of a "resonance dip" rather than a peak for the atomic 1s state.

## Method

The authors used the Green function method to calculate the nuclear response function for in-flight ($K^-,p$) reactions on $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O targets at an incident kaon kinetic energy of 600 MeV. They employed two different kaon-nucleus optical potentials: a chiral unitary model based on microscopic theory and a phenomenological deep potential fitted to kaonic atom data, both with energy dependence included. The Green function formalism allowed simultaneous treatment of bound states, unstable states with large widths, and quasi-free kaon production contributions to the reaction spectra.

## Key numbers

- The forward differential cross section of the elementary process $p(K^-,p)K^-$ in the laboratory frame is evaluated as 8.8 mb/sr at $T_K = 600$ MeV, with a correction reported by Cieplý et al. reducing it to 3.6 mb/sr
- The real part of the chiral unitary optical potential depth varies from approximately -40 MeV to -65 MeV for kaon energies from 0 MeV to -100 MeV
- The imaginary part of the chiral unitary potential reduces from around -55 MeV to -28 MeV for kaon energies from 0 MeV to -100 MeV
- The phenomenological optical potential uses a real effective scattering length parameterized as Re $a_{\rm eff} = -0.15 + 1.66(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.24}$ fm and an imaginary part Im $a_{\rm eff} = [0.62 - 0.04(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.24}] f^{\rm MFG}(E)$ fm
- The phase space factor $f^{\rm MFG}(E)$ assumes branching ratios of 80% for $\bar{K}N \rightarrow \pi\Sigma$ decay and 20% for $\bar{K}NN \rightarrow \Sigma N$ decay

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Yamagata et al., 2005) which used the effective number approach for the same reactions, by adopting the Green function method of Morimatsu and Yazaki (1985) that can handle unstable states with large widths and quasi-free contributions. The calculations use the chiral unitary model of Ramos and Oset (2000) and the phenomenological potential of Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1997) with phase space factors from Mares, Friedman, and Gal (2004). The results enable comparison with experimental data from Kishimoto and collaborators (1999, 2003) and provide theoretical guidance for future experiments seeking to observe kaonic nuclear states, while emphasizing that nuclear structure change effects not included here may be important for definitive conclusions.
