# Fluctuations of Strongly-interacting Matter in Thermal Models at Chemical Freeze-out

> Fluctuations provide a powerful tool for elucidating the nature of strongly-interacting matter in the QCD phase diagram. In heavy-ion-collision systems, the net-particle number fluctuations are captured at the moment of chemical freeze-out. Studies of the chemical freeze-out via susceptibilities from lattice QCD and the Hadron Resonance Gas model contribute to the characterization of the transition region of the QCD phase diagram. This contribution to proceedings will show how susceptibilities can be used to study the interplay between different conserved charges via cross-correlators and to constrain interactions in the hadron gas phase.

## Key claim

Susceptibility ratios from lattice QCD and the Hadron Resonance Gas model can be used to construct experimentally accessible proxies for conserved-charge correlators and to separately constrain the hadronic spectrum and repulsive baryon interactions, but the model's validity breaks down in the QCD transition region above approximately 160 MeV.

## Method

The author uses the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model to compute susceptibilities (derivatives of pressure with respect to chemical potentials) for baryon number (B), electric charge (Q), and strangeness (S). The model is extended to an excluded-volume HRG (EV-HRG) that includes repulsive interactions between baryons via a single excluded volume parameter b. The HRG results are compared to continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD data for diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities, and the decomposition of correlators into contributions from experimentally measurable hadronic species (π±, K±, p, Λ, Ξ, Ω and their antiparticles) is performed to identify proxies for the baryon-strangeness correlator.

## Key numbers

- The excluded volume parameter b is constrained to a range of 0.4–1 fm³ by fourth-to-second order susceptibility ratios from lattice QCD.
- The three fourth-to-second order ratios (χ⁴_B/χ²_B, χ³¹_BS/χ¹¹_BS, χ³¹_BQ/χ¹¹_BQ) are equal for T ≤ 150 MeV in the EV-HRG model, but significant differences appear in lattice data for T ≳ 160 MeV.
- The proposed proxy for the baryon-strangeness correlator χ¹¹_BS/χ²_S uses the variances σ²_K and σ²_Λ; the combination σ²_Λ/(σ²_K + σ²_Λ) reproduces the full result at all temperatures including the QCD transition region.
- The proton-kaon correlator σ¹¹_pK gives a negligible contribution to χ¹¹_BS/χ²_S and is a poor proxy.

## Context

This work extends studies of conserved-charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions using lattice QCD (Borsanyi 2018, Bazavov 2020) and the HRG model (Bellwied 2019). It builds on the baryon-strangeness correlator C_BS proposed by Koch (2005) and on excluded-volume HRG models developed by Vovchenko (2016, 2017) and Karthein (2021). The results enable experimental searches for the baryon-strangeness correlator using only kaon and Lambda fluctuations at RHIC and the LHC, and provide a method to test for flavor-dependent baryon excluded volumes in the hadron gas phase.
