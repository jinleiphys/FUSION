# Spectrum of QCD at Finite Isospin Density

> We study the phase diagram of QCD at finite isospin density using two flavors of staggered quarks. We investigate the low temperature region of the phase diagram where we find a pion condensation phase at high chemical potential. We started a basic analysis of the spectrum at finite isospin density. In particular, we measured pion, rho and nucleon masses inside and outside of the pion condensation phase. In agreement with previous studies in two-color QCD at finite baryon density we find that the Polyakov loop does not depend on the density in the staggered formulation.

## Key claim
The paper presents first lattice QCD results for the meson and baryon spectrum at finite isospin density, finding that the Polyakov loop remains independent of density in the staggered fermion formulation, but the authors caution that their parameter sets are not ideal and that extrapolation to vanishing pion source term is still needed for physically relevant masses.

## Method
The authors use two-flavor staggered quarks with a tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action on 16^3 x 24 lattices. They introduce a pion source term to enable inversion of the Dirac operator in the pion condensation phase and measure correlation functions for pion, rho, and nucleon operators, extracting masses from fits to time-like correlators at finite isospin chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum pion mass for parameter set 1 (beta=4.0, m=0.05, lambda=0.005): am_pi = 0.66(1)
- Vacuum rho mass for parameter set 1: am_rho = 1.04(3)
- Vacuum nucleon mass for parameter set 1: am_N = 1.64(5)
- Vacuum pion mass for parameter set 2 (beta=3.5, m=0.01, lambda=0.001): am_pi = 0.2597(1)
- Ratio m_pi/m_rho = 0.635 for parameter set 1, compared to the physical value of 0.18
- Pion source to quark mass ratio lambda/m = 0.1 for both parameter sets

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the QCD phase diagram at finite isospin density by Kogut (2002), Detmold (2012), and Brandt (2016), and tests predictions from chiral perturbation theory by Kogut (2000). The results enable future studies of lattice cutoff effects at finite density, such as saturation effects, and provide a foundation for investigating whether a rho condensate forms at higher chemical potentials or whether the observed plateau in rho mass is an artifact of the pion source term.
