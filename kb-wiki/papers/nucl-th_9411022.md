# A dynamical model for correlated two-pion-exchange in the pion-nucleon interaction

> A microscopic model for the $N\bar N\toππ$ process is presented in the meson exchange framework, which in the pseudophysical region agrees with available quasiempirical information. The scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ρ$) piece of correlated two--pion exchange in the pion--nucleon interaction is then derived via dispersion integrals over the unitarity cut. Inherent ambiguities in the method and implications for the description of pion--nucleon scattering data are discussed.

# A dynamical model for correlated two-pion-exchange in the pion-nucleon interaction

## Key claim
The paper presents a microscopic meson-exchange model for the $N\bar N\to\pi\pi$ process that reproduces quasiempirical information in the pseudophysical region, but the resulting scalar ($\sigma$) and vector ($\rho$) contributions to the pion-nucleon interaction contain inherent ambiguities, particularly for $\rho$ exchange, that cannot be resolved by low-energy $\pi N$ data alone without including additional mechanisms such as $\eta N$ coupling or higher $N^*$ resonances.

## Method
The authors construct a dynamical model for the $N\bar N\to\pi\pi$ process by solving a Blankenbecler-Sugar reduced scattering equation, using transition potentials built from $N$, $\Delta$, $\Lambda$, and $\Sigma$ exchange plus $\rho$-meson pole diagrams, with input from a coupled-channel $\pi\pi$/$K\bar K$ model. The resulting $N\bar N\to\pi\pi$ amplitudes in the pseudophysical region ($t \ge 4m_\pi^2$) are then used in dispersion integrals over the unitarity cut to derive the effective $\sigma$ and $\rho$ exchange potentials for $\pi N$ scattering, with a subtracted dispersion relation for the scalar channel to enforce chiral symmetry constraints.

## Key numbers
- The $\pi N$ $\Sigma$-term obtained from the model is approximately 65 MeV.
- The bare tensor/vector coupling constant ratio for $\rho NN$ is $\kappa_\rho^{(0)} = 4.136$.
- Cutoff masses for $N\Lambda K$ and $N\Sigma K$ are set to 2.5 GeV (an upper limit for $K\bar K$ contribution).
- The dispersion integral for the scalar channel uses a cutoff $t_c = 50 m_\pi^2$.
- The $\sigma$ potential cutoff parameter is readjusted from 1200 MeV to 1120 MeV when switching from quasiempirical input to the dynamical model.
- For the $N^*$ pole diagram test, parameters used are: bare mass $m^0_{N^*} = 1550$ MeV, coupling $(g^{(0)}_{N^*N\pi})^2/4\pi = 0.1$, and cutoff $\Lambda_{N^*} = 2000$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous meson exchange model for $\pi N$ scattering (Schuetz 1994), which used quasiempirical $N\bar N\to\pi\pi$ amplitudes from analytic continuation by Hohler et al. (1983). The new dynamical model replaces that quasiempirical input with an explicit field-theoretic calculation, enabling future studies of medium modifications in nuclear environments. The $\pi\pi$ interaction model builds on earlier work by Lohse et al. (1990) using time-ordered perturbation theory, here adapted to the Blankenbecler-Sugar framework. The paper highlights that the ambiguity in $\rho$ exchange formulation, comparing the direct dispersion of Frazer-Fulco amplitudes versus the method of Hohler and Pietarinen (1975), cannot be resolved without including $\eta N$ channel coupling and $N^*$ resonances, pointing to necessary extensions for describing higher-energy $\pi N$ data.
