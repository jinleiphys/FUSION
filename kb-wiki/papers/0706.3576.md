# Coulomb effects in polarization transfer in elastic antiproton and proton electron scattering at low energies

> The influence of Coulomb distortion on the polarization transfer in elastic proton and antiproton electron scattering at low energies is calculated in a distorted wave Born approximation. For antiproton electron scattering Coulomb effects reduce substantially the spin transfer cross section compared to the plane wave Born approximation whereas for proton electron scattering they lead to a dramatic increase for kinetic proton lab energies below about 20 keV.

## Key claim

Coulomb distortion substantially modifies the polarization transfer cross section in elastic hadron-electron scattering at low energies, but the effect is opposite for antiprotons (reduction) versus protons (dramatic increase below about 20 keV), and the Horowitz-Meyer approximation underestimates the Coulomb effect at the lowest energies compared to the full distorted wave Born approximation.

## Method

The author calculates the polarization transfer cross section for elastic scattering of an unpolarized proton or antiproton on a polarized electron using a nonrelativistic distorted wave Born approximation (DW) that includes the static Coulomb potential, the electron spin-orbit interaction, and the hyperfine interaction between electron and hadron. The scattering matrix is evaluated by replacing plane waves with Coulomb-distorted scattering waves for the hyperfine interaction, while the spin-orbit contribution is neglected as higher order. The resulting differential and integrated polarization transfer cross sections are compared with the plane wave Born approximation (PW) and the earlier Horowitz-Meyer approximation (HM).

## Key numbers

- For antiproton-electron scattering, the integrated polarization transfer cross section ⟨P_zz σ⟩ in the DW approximation does not exceed about 5 [b] at its maxima for hadron lab kinetic energies between 0.001 and 10 MeV.
- For proton-electron scattering, the ratio of the unpolarized differential cross section (including spin contribution) to the Rutherford cross section becomes larger than one at scattering angles above about 3° at E = 0.001 MeV and above about 60° at E = 0.0016 MeV.
- The DW approximation is expected to fail for proton-electron scattering below about 1 keV due to the strong attractive Coulomb effect.

## Context

This work extends the earlier estimate of Coulomb effects by Horowitz and Meyer (1994), who used a rough approximation multiplying the plane-wave hyperfine amplitude by the Coulomb wave functions at the origin. The present distorted wave calculation provides a more reliable treatment and shows that the HM approximation underestimates Coulomb effects at low energies, particularly for antiprotons. The results are relevant for proposals to polarize antiprotons in storage rings via polarization transfer from polarized electrons or positrons, and the author notes that a forthcoming paper (Arenhoevel, 2007) will extend the calculation to include initial hadron polarization and spin-flip processes.
