# Investigation of the shape of uranium in relativistic $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions with nuclear densities from covariant density functional theory

> Relativistic $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions have recently been used to extract the quadrupole shape of $^{238}$U. In this study, we employ state-of-the-art three-dimensional (3D) lattice covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with pairing correlations to calculate the density of uranium, including its octupole and hexadecaople deformations, as input for hydrodynamic simulations of these collisions. We find that while the CDFT density well describes elliptic flow, a clear mismatch emerges with transverse-momentum-related observables, indicating a tension in the effective quadrupole deformation. Furthermore, constraining the octupole deformation with triangular flow $v_{3}$ proves to be difficult due to significant sensitivity to the uncertain nuclear structure of the gold reference system. Our results underscore the necessity of realistic nuclear densities for both colliding species and highlight the need for further investigation of correlations related to both flow and transverse momentum to fully characterize nuclear deformation.

## Key claim

While covariant density functional theory (CDFT) densities for uranium reproduce the elliptic flow ratio between U+U and Au+Au collisions, a clear mismatch emerges with transverse-momentum-related observables, indicating a tension in the effective quadrupole deformation. Additionally, constraining the octupole deformation with triangular flow proves difficult due to significant sensitivity to the uncertain nuclear structure of the gold reference system.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the iEBE-VISHNU framework with TRENTo initial conditions, incorporating uranium nuclear density profiles from three-dimensional lattice covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with pairing correlations using the PC-PK1 functional. They simulated approximately 10^6 hydrodynamic events each for U+U and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, comparing octupole-deformed (beta_20=0.29, beta_30=0.09) and purely prolate (beta_20=0.29, beta_30=0) uranium configurations, while using two different Woods-Saxon parameter sets for gold. Flow observables were calculated using the standard Q-cumulant method for charged particles with 0.2 < p_T < 2 GeV/c and |eta| < 2.

## Key numbers

- CDFT calculation yields deformation parameters for uranium: beta_20 = 0.29, beta_30 = 0.09, and beta_40 = 0.16
- The octupole-deformed configuration (Uoct) shows an enhancement of approximately 0.1 in the v_3^2 ratio relative to the prolate configuration (Upro) in most central collisions
- The commonly employed parameter set for Au induces a systematic overestimation of the R(v_2^2) magnitude by approximately 0.1
- The ground-state electric transition rate for uranium is B(E2) = 12.09 +/- 0.20 e^2 b^2
- The 3D lattice space for CDFT calculations is L_x x L_y x L_z = 24 x 24 x 30 fm^3
- Preliminary CDFT calculations for gold indicate a triaxial deformation with gamma = 44 degrees

## Context

This work extends the approach of using relativistic heavy-ion collisions to extract nuclear deformation parameters, following the STAR Collaboration's measurement of the quadrupole deformation of uranium (STAR 2024). It addresses the "ultracentral v_2 puzzle" previously noted when using simplified Woods-Saxon parameterizations, as discussed by Ryssens (2023) and Xu (2024). The results highlight the need for realistic nuclear densities for both colliding species and enable future work employing CDFT-based densities for both uranium and gold, as well as investigating observables associated with nuclear multipole deformations.
