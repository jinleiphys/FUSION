# Universal physics of bound states of a few charged particles

> We study few-body bound states of charged particles subject to attractive zero-range/short-range plus repulsive Coulomb interparticle forces. The characteristic length scales of the system at zero energy are set by the Coulomb length scale $D$ and the Coulomb-modified effective range $r_{\mathrm{eff}}$. We study shallow bound states of charged particles with $D\gg r_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and show that these systems obey universal scaling laws different from neutral particles. An accurate description of these states requires both the Coulomb-modified scattering length and the effective range unless the Coulomb interaction is very weak ($D\to \infty$). Our findings are relevant for bound states whose spatial extent is significantly larger than the range of the attractive potential. These states enjoy universality -- their character is independent of the shape of the short-range potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that shallow bound states of charged particles with attractive short-range plus repulsive Coulomb forces obey universal scaling laws that are fundamentally different from those of neutral particles, and that an accurate description requires both the Coulomb-modified scattering length and the effective range unless the Coulomb interaction is extremely weak (D → ∞).

## Method

The authors study two-body bound states analytically using a square-well short-range potential plus Coulomb interaction, deriving model-independent relations in the zero-range limit. For three- and four-body systems, they perform numerical calculations using the Gaussian Expansion Method and Stochastic Variational Method with correlated Gaussians, extrapolating to the zero-range limit by performing sequences of calculations with decreasing range parameters.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb length scale D = ħ²/(k_e μ Q₁ Q₂) sets the characteristic scale; for two free protons D ≃ 57.6 fm, for two alpha particles D ≃ 3.6 fm
- In the "strong Coulomb" region (region I, a_C ≫ D), the two-body binding momentum satisfies κ² ≃ 6/(D a_C) + 18/(5 a_C²)
- In the "weak Coulomb" region (region II, R ≪ a_C ≪ D), κ ≃ (1/a_C)[1 − (2a_C/D) ln(a_C e^γ/D)]
- The maximum root-mean-square radius in the zero-range limit is √⟨r²⟩₀/D = 0.507...
- For the α+α+n system (⁹Be), the number of bound states N ≤ 1.5 using A = 1.25 and R = 1 fm
- The effective-range correction enters at leading order: κ² ≃ 6/[a_C D (1 − 3 r_eff/D)] for 1/a_C → 0

## Context

This work extends the universal few-body physics framework developed for neutral particles (Braaten and Hammer, 2006; Efimov, 1971; Thomas, 1935) to systems with repulsive Coulomb interactions. It contradicts the expectation that zero-range potentials provide a universal starting point for shallow bound states regardless of charge, showing instead that finite-range corrections are generically enhanced for "strong Coulomb" systems. The results are relevant for cluster models of nuclei near the proton dripline (Morlock, 1997; Ryberg, 2015), charged quasiparticles in solids (Adamowski, 1989; Verbist, 1991), and enable the formulation of effective field theories for shallow bound states of charged particles where the effective range enters at leading order.
