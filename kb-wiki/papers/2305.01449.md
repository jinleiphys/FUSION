# Dynamics in near-threshold $J/ψ$ photoproduction

> The study of $J/ψ$ photoproduction at low energies has consequences for the understanding of multiple aspects of nonperturbative QCD, ranging from mechanical properties of the proton, to the binding inside nuclei, and the existence of hidden-charm pentaquarks. Factorization of the photon-$c \bar c$ and nucleon dynamics or Vector Meson Dominance are often invoked to justify these studies. Alternatively, open charm intermediate states have been proposed as the dominant mechanism underlying $J/ψ$ photoproduction. As the latter violates this factorization, it is important to estimate the relevance of such contributions. We analyse the latest differential and integrated photoproduction cross sections from the GlueX and $J/ψ$-007 experiments. We show that the data can be adequately described by a small number of partial waves, which we parameterize with generic models enforcing low-energy unitarity. The results suggest a nonnegligible contribution from open charm intermediate states. Furthermore, most of the models present an elastic scattering length incompatible with previous extractions based on Vector Meson Dominance, and thus call into question its applicability to heavy mesons. Our results indicate a wide array of physics possibilities that are compatible with present data and need to be disentangled.

## Key claim

The data can be adequately described by a small number of partial waves parameterized with generic models enforcing low-energy unitarity, but the results suggest a nonnegligible contribution from open charm intermediate states and call into question the applicability of Vector Meson Dominance to heavy mesons, though the authors note that "a wide array of physics possibilities that are compatible with present data and need to be disentangled."

## Method

The authors analyzed the latest differential and integrated photoproduction cross sections from the GlueX and J/psi-007 experiments, fitting 142 data points covering photon energies from 8.2 to 11.4 GeV. They expanded the scattering amplitude in s-channel partial waves up to l=3, parameterizing the production vector and K-matrix with generic forms that satisfy low-energy unitarity, and considered three S-wave parameterizations: single channel (1C), two channels including an intermediate D* Lambda_c channel (2C), and three channels including both D Lambda_c and D* Lambda_c channels (3C). The fits were performed by minimizing chi-squared with statistical and uncorrelated systematic uncertainties added in quadrature, and uncertainties were determined using a bootstrap approach.

## Key numbers

* The integrated cross section data cover photon energies E_gamma = 8.2 to 11.4 GeV, with 142 total data points fitted across all models.
* The 1C (single channel) model yields chi-squared = 166 with 9 parameters (chi-squared/dof = 1.25); the 2C model yields chi-squared = 144 with 13 parameters (chi-squared/dof = 1.12); the 3C-NR (nonresonant) model yields chi-squared = 141 with 15 parameters (chi-squared/dof = 1.11); the 3C-R (resonant) model yields chi-squared = 143 with 15 parameters (chi-squared/dof = 1.13).
* The interaction radius extracted from the partial wave series is r approximately 0.1 fm, and the description in terms of s-channel partial waves is expected to hold up to about E_gamma approximately 14 GeV.
* The dip in the integrated cross section at E_gamma approximately 9 GeV has an estimated significance of 2.6 sigma (or 1.4 sigma when considering the probability of any two adjacent points having a similar significance).
* The elastic J/psi p scattering length a_psi p from the 1C model is in the range [0.56, 1.00] fm at 90% confidence level, while the 3C-NR model gives [-2.77, 0.35] fm and the 3C-R model gives [-0.04, 0.19] fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of near-threshold J/psi photoproduction using the latest Jefferson Lab data from GlueX (Adhikari 2023) and the J/psi-007 experiment in Hall C (Duran 2022), building on prior studies that invoked Vector Meson Dominance (Gryniuk 2016, Strakovsky 2019, Pentchev 2020, Wang 2022) or open-charm intermediate states (Du 2020). The results challenge the factorization assumption underlying extractions of proton gravitational form factors and mass radius (Ji 2021, Kharzeev 2021, Mamo 2021), and the finding that most models present an elastic scattering length incompatible with previous VMD-based extractions calls into question the applicability of VMD to heavy mesons. The analysis enables future experimental measurements to disentangle the wide array of physics possibilities compatible with current data, including the role of hidden-charm pentaquarks and the nature of the dip at E_gamma approximately 9 GeV.
