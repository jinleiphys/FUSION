# Excitation of the GDR and the Compressional Isoscalar Dipole State by alpha scattering

> The excitation of the isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) by alpha scattering is investigated as a method of probing the neutron excess in exotic nuclei. DWBA calculations are presented for 28O and 70Ca and the interplay of Coulomb and nuclear excitation is discussed. Since the magnitude of the Coulomb excitation amplitude is strongly influenced by the Q-value, the neutron excess plays an important role, as it tends to lower the energy of the GDR. The excitation of the compressional isoscalar dipole state in 70Ca by alpha scattering is also investigated. It is shown that the population of this latter state may be an even more sensitive probe of the neutron skin than the isovector GDR.

## Key claim

Alpha scattering can excite both the isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) and the compressional isoscalar dipole state (IDR) in neutron-rich nuclei, but the IDR may be an even more sensitive probe of the neutron skin than the GDR, provided the correct Q-value (excitation energy) is used because the Coulomb excitation amplitude is strongly Q-value dependent.

## Method

Distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations were performed for alpha inelastic scattering on the neutron-rich nuclei 28O and 70Ca. The hadronic and Coulomb transition potentials were obtained by folding RPA transition densities from Catara et al. with the alpha-nucleon and Coulomb potentials, respectively. For 28O, a Woods-Saxon distorting potential was used; for 70Ca, the distorting potential was obtained by double folding the M3Y potential with a Gaussian alpha-particle density and the Hartree-Fock density of 70Ca.

## Key numbers

- GDR in 28O assumed at 80 A^(-1/3) ≈ 26 MeV (hydrodynamical value), but RPA calculation predicts average excitation energy around 15 MeV.
- GDR in 70Ca assumed at 13.7 MeV; IDR in 70Ca assumed at 9.4 MeV.
- Alpha bombarding energy for 28O: E_L = 68 MeV; for 70Ca: E_L = 40 MeV.
- Woods-Saxon distorting potential parameters for 28O: V_0 = 29 MeV, W_0 = V_0/2, R_0 = 5.05 fm, a_0 = 0.63 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions by Clement, Lane and Rook, and by Satchler that the isovector GDR can be excited by isoscalar hadronic probes when neutron and proton densities differ. It also builds on the identification of the compressional isoscalar dipole state by Harakeh and Dieperink and by Van Giai and Sagawa, and on RPA calculations by Catara et al. that showed neutron excess fragments and shifts the GDR strength to lower energy. The results suggest that alpha scattering on neutron-rich nuclei can directly probe the neutron skin, with the IDR providing a particularly clean signal.
