# Superstatistical random-matrix-theory approach to transition intensities in mixed systems

> We study the fluctuation properties of transition intensities applying a recently proposed generalization of the random matrix theory, which is based on Beck and Cohen's superstatistics. We obtain an analytic expression for the distribution of the reduced transition probabilities that applies to systems undergoing a transition out of chaos. The obtained distribution fits the results of a previous nuclear shell model calculations for some electromagnetic transitions that deviate from the Porter-Thomas distribution. It agrees with the experimental reduced transition probabilities for the 26A nucleus better than the commonly used chi-squared distribution.

# Superstatistical random-matrix-theory approach to transition intensities in mixed systems

## Key claim

The superstatistical generalization of random matrix theory yields a transition-intensity distribution that fits shell-model results and experimental data for mixed regular-chaotic systems better than the commonly used chi-squared distribution, provided the tuning parameter alpha is allowed to vary; the improvement is most visible in the peak position of the logarithmic distribution, which shifts to negative log values for less chaotic systems, unlike the chi-squared distribution whose peak remains fixed at log y = 0.

## Method

The author constructs a superstatistical random-matrix ensemble by averaging Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) matrix-element distributions over a fluctuating inverse-variance parameter eta, whose probability density is obtained by maximizing Shannon entropy subject to constraints on the mean of eta and on the mean level density. The resulting parameter distribution is inserted into an averaged Porter-Thomas expression to produce an analytic formula for the reduced transition probability distribution, expressed in terms of Meijer G-functions. The single tuning parameter alpha controls the degree of chaos, with alpha approaching infinity recovering the GOE (Porter-Thomas) limit.

## Key numbers

- The superstatistical distribution fits shell-model M1 transition intensities for 60Zn (Tz=0, 4356 matrix elements) and 60Co (Tz=1, 3136 matrix elements) from Hamoudi et al. (2001) better than the best-fit chi-squared distribution.
- For experimental reduced electromagnetic transition strengths in 26Al (approximately 1500 transitions, excitation energies up to 8.067 MeV, isospin T=0 and T=1), the superstatistical distribution with alpha = 1.24 agrees better with the data than the best-fit chi-squared distribution.
- The parameter eta_0 is determined by the normalization condition <y> = 1, yielding eta_0 = (alpha/2) * [G_03^30(alpha^3 | 0,0,1/2) / G_03^30(alpha^3 | 0,1/2,1)].
- The experimental histogram for 26Al was normalized to 0.83 to approximately account for upper and lower detection thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the superstatistical random-matrix approach previously applied to level densities and spacing distributions (Abul-Magd, 2005; Abul-Magd, 2006) to the domain of transition intensities. It directly addresses the failure of the chi-squared distribution with fractional degrees of freedom (Alhassid and Novoselsky, 1992; Alhassid and Levine, 1986) to reproduce the peak position of logarithmic transition-intensity histograms observed in experimental data for 26Al (Adams, Mitchell, and Shriner, 1998) and in shell-model calculations for pf-shell nuclei (Hamoudi, Nazmitdinov, and Alhassid, 2001). The superstatistical formalism, based on Beck and Cohen (2003), provides an alternative to Tsallis-based non-extensive RMT approaches (Evans, 2002; Toscano, 2004; Bertuola, 2004; Nobre, 2004; Abul-Magd, 2005; Abul-Magd, 2006) that are restricted to a limited range near the chaotic phase due to divergence constraints.
