# Roper resonances and quasi-normal modes of Skyrmions

> Radial vibrations of charge one hedgehog Skyrmions in the full Skyrme model are analysed. We investigate how the properties of the lowest resonance modes (quasi normal modes) - their frequencies and widths - depend on the form of the potential (value of the pion mass as well as the addition of further potentials) and on the inclusion of the sextic term. Then we consider the inverse problem, where certain values for the frequencies and widths are imposed, and the field theoretic Skyrme model potential giving rise to them is reconstructed. This latter method allows to reproduce the physical Roper resonances, as well as further physical properties of nucleons, with high precision.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the standard Skyrme model with a pion mass potential cannot reproduce the experimentally observed similar widths of the three lowest Roper resonances, because the higher resonance modes have widths an order of magnitude larger than the first; however, by solving the inverse problem, reconstructing the Skyrme model potential from imposed resonance frequencies and widths, the physical Roper resonances can be reproduced with high precision.

## Method

The authors study radial vibrations of charge-one hedgehog Skyrmions by formulating a Sturm-Liouville problem for linear perturbations around the static soliton, deriving an algebraic effective potential. They compute quasi-normal mode frequencies and widths as functions of the pion mass and potential form using both linear perturbation theory and full time-evolution simulations with a finite difference leapfrog method, then solve the inverse problem to reconstruct the Skyrme model potential from desired resonance properties.

## Key numbers

- For the standard Skyrme potential (p=1), the critical pion masses below which the n-th oscillating mode becomes a resonance are: m1=1.59, m2=7.99, m3=19.38.
- For the p=4 Piette potential, the critical mass for the first oscillating mode decreases to m=0.85.
- For the unbinding potential with alpha=0.95 and physical parameters (f_pi=36.1 MeV, m_pi=303 MeV, e=3.76, giving m=4.46), the three lowest resonance modes have complex frequencies: Omega1+i*Gamma1 = 4.6077+0.1169i, Omega2+i*Gamma2 = 8.2293+1.8342i, Omega3+i*Gamma3 = 11.6761+4.1988i.
- For the standard potential at m=0, the first resonance has frequency Omega=0.61 and width Gamma=0.26.
- Lower bounds for higher resonance widths in the standard potential: Gamma2 > 1.41, Gamma3 > 2.98, Gamma4 > 4.17.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vibrational modes in Skyrme models, including the collective mode approximation by Hajduk (1984) and Kaulfuss (1985), linear perturbation theory by Zahed (1986) and Nappi (1985), and the discovery of a resonance in the potential-less Skyrme model by Bizon (1984). The authors systematically analyze how the potential form (including Piette potentials and unbinding potentials) and the sextic term affect resonance properties, and they introduce an inverse problem approach to reconstruct Skyrme model potentials that can reproduce physical Roper resonance data, which previous models failed to do simultaneously for all three lowest Roper states.
