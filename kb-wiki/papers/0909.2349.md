# Electroexcitation of nucleon resonances from CLAS data on single pion electroproduction

> We present results on the electroexcitation of the low mass resonances Delta(1232)P33, N(1440)P11, N(1520)D13, and N(1535)S11 in a wide range of Q2. The results were obtained in the comprehensive analysis of JLab-CLAS data on differential cross sections, longitudinally polarized beam asymmetries, and longitudinal target and beam-target asymmetries for pion electroproduction off the proton. The data were analysed using two conceptually different approaches, fixed-t dispersion relations and a unitary isobar model, allowing us to draw conclusions on the model sensitivity of the obtained electrocoupling amplitudes. The amplitudes for the Delta(1232)P33} show the importance of a meson-cloud contribution to quantitatively explain the magnetic dipole strength, as well as the electric and scalar quadrupole transitions. They do not show any tendency of approaching the pQCD regime for Q2<6 GeV2. For the Roper resonance, N(1440)P11, the data provide strong evidence for this state as a predominantly radial excitation of a 3-quark ground state. Measured in pion electroproduction, the transverse helicity amplitude for the N(1535)S11 allowed us to obtain the branching ratios of this state to the piN and etaN channels via comparison to the results extracted from eta electroproduction. The extensive CLAS data also enabled the extraction of the gamma*p -> N(1520)D13 and N(1535)S11 longitudinal helicity amplitudes with good precision.

## Key claim

The electroexcitation amplitudes for the Delta(1232)P33, N(1440)P11, N(1520)D13, and N(1535)S11 resonances were extracted from CLAS data on single pion electroproduction over a wide Q2 range, but the results carry model sensitivity because two conceptually different approaches (fixed-t dispersion relations and a unitary isobar model) were used, and the amplitudes for the Delta(1232)P33 show no tendency of approaching the pQCD regime for Q2 up to 6 GeV2.

## Method

The analysis used JLab-CLAS data on differential cross sections, longitudinally polarized beam asymmetries, and longitudinal target and beam-target asymmetries for pion electroproduction off the proton. The data were analyzed separately at each Q2 point using two approaches: fixed-t dispersion relations (DR) and a unitary isobar model (UIM), with the real parts of the amplitudes built in conceptually different ways to assess model sensitivity. The analysis proceeded in stages: first using data at Q2=0.3-0.65 GeV2 with the richest polarization measurements, then analyzing data at Q2=1.72-4.16 GeV2 from the en pi+ channel, and finally extracting Delta(1232)P33 amplitudes from low Q2 data and data at Q2=1.15-6 GeV2.

## Key numbers

- Q2 range covered: 0.16 to 6 GeV2 for the electroexcitation of Delta(1232)P33, N(1440)P11, N(1520)D13, and N(1535)S11 resonances
- For the Delta(1232)P33, the amplitudes show no tendency of approaching the pQCD regime for Q2 up to 6 GeV2
- For the N(1520)D13, the transverse amplitudes show a changeover from helicity-3/2 dominance at the real photon point to helicity-1/2 dominance at Q2 greater than 1 GeV2
- Data sets included: 2364 data points for dsigma/dOmega(pi+) at Q2=0.3 GeV2 with chi2/N=2.06 (DR) and 1.93 (UIM); 5778 data points for dsigma/dOmega(pi+) at Q2=4.16 GeV2 with chi2/N=1.1 (both DR and UIM)
- For the N(1535)S11, the transverse helicity amplitude from pion electroproduction was compared to results from eta electroproduction to obtain branching ratios to piN and etaN channels

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of pion photoproduction and low-Q2 electroproduction data using DR and UIM approaches (Aznauryan 2004, 2005) to higher Q2 values up to 6 GeV2, and provides electrocoupling amplitudes that challenge quark models, particularly for the N(1535)S11 where the longitudinal helicity amplitudes may indicate large meson-cloud contributions or representations different from a 3-quark excitation. The results enable improved understanding of nucleon resonance structure and provide constraints for light-front dynamics approaches, dynamical reaction models (such as those by Yang, Kamalov, Sato, and Lee), and lattice calculations (Alexandrou 2007), while also allowing comparison with the MAID2007 analysis which constrains amplitudes using parameterizations for their Q2 dependence.
