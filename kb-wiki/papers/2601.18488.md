# Probability distribution of observables from a Bogoliubov vacuum projected onto good particle number: application to scission configurations of an actinide

> Nuclear fission dynamics described within nuclear energy density functional frameworks (EDF) have seen substantial advances in the last decade. Part of this success stems from projection techniques, which allow the computation f probability distribution functions (pdf) for selected observables such as particle number and angular momentum of the fragments. Predicting the pdf of other observables, such as the total kinetic energy of the fragments, remains undone. This work proposes a method to determine the complete pdf of a new category of observables from a Bogoliubov vacuum projected onto good particle number. It relies on sampling nucleonic configurations in coordinate and intrinsic-spin representation. We assess the feasibility and convergence properties of the method and apply it to states representative of the scission of an actinide. Fluctuations in fragment shapes, inter-fragment Coulomb and nuclear interaction as well as the corresponding torques are analyzed. We find that a significant fraction of the fluctuation of several measured fission observables is already present within the mean-field picture.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to compute the full probability distribution function (pdf) of any observable diagonal in the position/intrinsic spin representation from a Bogoliubov vacuum projected onto good particle number, and demonstrates that a significant fraction of the fluctuation of several measured fission observables is already present within the mean-field picture, without requiring beyond-mean-field dynamics.

## Method

The authors implement a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampler that generates configurations of nucleon positions and intrinsic spins consistent with the many-body density of a particle-number-projected Bogoliubov vacuum. The probability amplitude for each configuration is computed as a determinant of a skew-symmetric matrix built from canonical single-particle wavefunctions and BCS occupation factors. For any observable diagonal in the position/spin representation, the kernel evaluated on each sampled configuration provides an unbiased estimator of the observable's expectation value and its full pdf.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{20}$Ne ground state, the MCMC method reproduces the deterministic expectation value of $\langle \beta_{20} \rangle = 0.565$ within statistical uncertainty, with a standard deviation $\sigma(\beta_{20}) = 13.50$ at cell size $dx=0.5$ fm (deterministic value: 13.37).
- For a $^{252}$Cf pre-scission configuration, the MCMC gives $\langle \beta_{20} \rangle = 4.108 \pm 3\times10^{-4}$ and $\langle \beta_{30} \rangle = 2.138 \pm 8\times10^{-4}$ at $dx=0.5$ fm, with standard deviations $\sigma(\beta_{20}) = 0.098$ and $\sigma(\beta_{30}) = 0.266$.
- The Coulomb energy expectation value for $^{252}$Cf converges to 846.45 $\pm 5\times10^{-2}$ MeV at $dx=0.5$ fm, compared to the deterministic value of 846.94 MeV.
- The required burn-in period is $10^5$ iterations for $^{20}$Ne and $10^6$ iterations for $^{252}$Cf, with a jump parameter of 100 and 2000 respectively to reduce autocorrelation below 0.2.
- The spatial discretization uses a cell size of $dx=0.5$ fm and a box size $L_x=32$ fm for both light and heavy nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the projection techniques previously applied to one-body observables such as particle number and angular momentum (Scamps 2015, Marevic 2021, Bulgac 2022) to a new class of observables including two-body interactions like the inter-fragment Coulomb repulsion. The method builds on the sampling approach of Matsumoto (2022) for visualizing nucleonic configurations and enables future studies of the full pdf of total kinetic energy and fragment excitation energies in fission, which were previously inaccessible. The open-source code NucleoScope is released to facilitate such applications.
