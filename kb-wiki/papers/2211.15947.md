# Radiative corrections to inverse muon decay for accelerator neutrinos

> Inverse muon decay ($ν_μe^- \to ν_e μ^-$) is a promising tool to constrain neutrino fluxes with energies $E_ν \ge 10.9~\mathrm{GeV}$. Radiative corrections introduce percent-level distortions to energy spectra of outgoing muons and depend on experimental details. In this paper, we calculate radiative corrections to the scattering processes $ν_μe^- \to ν_e μ^-$ and $\barν_e e^- \to \barν_μμ^-$. We present the muon energy spectrum for both channels, double-differential distributions in muon energy and muon scattering angle and in photon energy and photon scattering angle, and the photon energy spectrum for the dominant $ν_μe^- \to ν_e μ^-$ process. Our results clarify and extend the region of applicability of previous results in the literature for the double differential distribution in muon energy and photon energy, and in the muon energy spectrum with a radiated photon above a threshold energy. We provide analytic expressions for single, double and triple differential cross sections, and discuss how radiative corrections modify experimentally interesting observable distributions.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections to inverse muon decay introduce percent-level distortions to muon energy spectra and other observable distributions, but the precise effect depends on experimental details such as photon veto thresholds and flux averaging; the authors clarify that previous results from Bardin and Dokuchaeva (1986) for the double-differential distribution in muon energy and photon energy are only valid within a restricted range of photon energies and are not positive-definite outside that range.

## Method

The authors calculated O(alpha) radiative corrections to the processes nu_mu e- -> nu_e mu- and antinu_e e- -> antinu_mu mu- using the low-energy effective four-fermion interaction with the Fermi coupling constant. They combined virtual corrections (vertex corrections to the charged-lepton current) with real photon emission contributions, integrating over the full allowed phase space including photons of arbitrarily large energy. The resulting analytic expressions for single, double, and triple differential cross sections were evaluated numerically and compared to the earlier calculation by Bardin and Dokuchaeva (1986), and flux-averaged predictions were produced for the MINERvA and DUNE experimental configurations.

## Key numbers

- The threshold neutrino energy for inverse muon decay is E_nu^thr = (m_mu^2 - m_e^2)/(2 m_e), corresponding to E_nu >= 10.9 GeV.
- At a fixed neutrino energy of E_nu = 15 GeV, radiative corrections reduce the cross section by 3-4 percent.
- The muon scattering cone is within 0.2 degrees only for incoming neutrino energies E_nu >= 38 GeV.
- For the MINERvA experiment, the reduction in observed rate from vetoing events with real photons is less than 1 percent, with larger effect for higher muon energies.
- The variable F (E_mu * theta_mu^2) for inverse muon decay events is below 10-100 keV at E_nu = 15 GeV.
- All inverse muon decay events from neutrinos with E_nu <= 30 GeV fall in the first bin of the MINERvA F variable (0 to 250 keV).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of radiative corrections to inverse muon decay beyond the ultrarelativistic limit treatment of Sarantakos (1982) and the earlier evaluation by Bardin and Dokuchaeva (1986), providing new results for the antineutrino channel and clarifying the region of applicability of previous double-differential distributions. The results enable percent-level constraints on high-energy tails of neutrino fluxes at experiments like MINERvA (which already used IMD for flux constraints in 2021) and the future DUNE experiment, which is expected to have more than a few thousand IMD events. The authors also compare their predictions to the elastic neutrino-electron scattering calculations of Tomalak (2019) and discuss how experimental photon veto strategies modify the observable cross section.
