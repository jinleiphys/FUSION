# Reexamination of the role of the $־”^{\ast}$ resonances in the $pp\rightarrow nK^{+}־£^{+}$ reaction

> In this work, the role of the $־”^{\ast }$ resonances in the process of $pp\rightarrow nK^{+}־£^{+}$ are systematically investigated with the effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model. We find that a $P_{31}$ state, either $־”^{\ast }(1750)$ or $־”^{\ast }(1910)$, is favored by the data while the $P_{33}$ state, namely $־”^{\ast }(1920)$, has small contribution. Besides, either sub-threshold $S_{31}$ $־”^{\ast }(1620)$ resonance or strong $n־£$ final state interaction or both have possible contribution at near threshold region, depending on the measured cross sections. We demonstrate the invariant mass distributions and the Dalitz Plots in order to investigate whether it is possible to distinguish the controversial $K־£$ production mechanism in these observables.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a $P_{31}$ state, either $\Delta^{\ast}(1750)$ or $\Delta^{\ast}(1910)$, is favored by the data for the $pp\rightarrow nK^{+}\Sigma^{+}$ reaction, while the $P_{33}$ state $\Delta^{\ast}(1920)$ has small contribution; however, the total cross section alone cannot resolve which $P_{31}$ resonance is responsible, and either a sub-threshold $S_{31}$ $\Delta^{\ast}(1620)$ resonance or a strong $n\Sigma$ final state interaction (or both) may contribute near threshold, depending on which experimental data set is used.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model with tree-level Feynman diagrams to compute the $pp\rightarrow nK^{+}\Sigma^{+}$ reaction via intermediate $\Delta^{\ast}$ resonances ($\Delta^{\ast}(1620)$, $\Delta^{\ast}(1750)$, $\Delta^{\ast}(1910)$, $\Delta^{\ast}(1920)$) and single-pion exchange. The coupling constants of the $\Delta^{\ast}$ resonances to the $K\Sigma$ channel were first determined by fitting the $\pi^{+}p\rightarrow K^{+}\Sigma^{+}$ reaction data, then the same parameters were used to calculate the $pp\rightarrow nK^{+}\Sigma^{+}$ cross sections. Two solutions were considered: Solution I with $\Delta^{\ast}(1910)$ as the $P_{31}$ state, and Solution II with $\Delta^{\ast}(1750)$ as the $P_{31}$ state; the effect of a possible $n\Sigma$ final state interaction was also modeled using a Jost function.

## Key numbers

- The $\Delta^{\ast}(1620)$ resonance has mass 1615 MeV and width 140 MeV, with a fitted coupling $g_{\Delta^{\ast}(1620)\Sigma K}^{2}/4\pi = 0.053$ extracted from $\pi^{+}p\rightarrow K^{+}\Sigma^{+}$ data.
- The $\Delta^{\ast}(1750)$ resonance has mass 1750 MeV and width 300 MeV, with a fitted coupling $g_{\Delta^{\ast}(1750)\Sigma K}^{2}/4\pi = 2.96$ giving a branching ratio of 7.1% for $\Delta^{\ast}(1750)\rightarrow K\Sigma$.
- The $\Delta^{\ast}(1910)$ resonance has mass 1875 MeV and width 250 MeV, with a known branching ratio of 14.0% to $K\Sigma$ and coupling $g_{\Delta^{\ast}(1910)\Sigma K}^{2}/4\pi = 0.953$.
- The $\Delta^{\ast}(1920)$ resonance has mass 1910 MeV and width 220 MeV, with a known branching ratio of 2.14% to $K\Sigma$ and coupling $g_{\Delta^{\ast}(1920)\Sigma K}^{2}/4\pi = 0.510$.
- The fit to $\pi^{+}p\rightarrow K^{+}\Sigma^{+}$ data gave $\chi^{2} = 3.6$ for Solution I (with $\Delta^{\ast}(1750)$) and $\chi^{2} = 4.6$ for Solution II (with $\Delta^{\ast}(1910)$).
- The $n\Sigma$ final state interaction parameters were taken as $\alpha = -70$ MeV and $\beta = 280$ MeV, corresponding to a scattering length $a = 2.1$ fm and effective range $r = 1.9$ fm.
- Cut-off parameters for the pion form factors were $\Lambda_{\pi} = 0.8$ GeV for all resonances, and $\Lambda_{\pi}^{\ast} = 0.8$ GeV, 1.0 GeV, 1.2 GeV, and 1.2 GeV for $\Delta^{\ast}(1620)$, $\Delta^{\ast}(1750)$, $\Delta^{\ast}(1910)$, and $\Delta^{\ast}(1920)$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the $pp\rightarrow nK^{+}\Sigma^{+}$ reaction by Tsushima et al. (1994, 1997, 1999), who introduced the $\Delta^{\ast}(1920)$ resonance, and by Xie et al. (2007) and Cao et al. (2008), who suggested the $\Delta^{\ast}(1620)$ resonance or strong $n\Sigma$ final state interaction to explain near-threshold data from COSY-11. The paper addresses the long-standing discrepancy between different coupled-channel analyses of the $\pi^{+}p\rightarrow K^{+}\Sigma^{+}$ reaction, where the Juelich model favors $\Delta^{\ast}(1620)$, the Bonn-Gatchina partial wave analysis identifies $\Delta^{\ast}(1920)$ as essential, and the Giessen model claims a vital role for $\Delta^{\ast}(1750)$. The results enable future measurements of invariant mass distributions and Dalitz plots at beam energies around 2.8 GeV to distinguish between the competing production mechanisms.
