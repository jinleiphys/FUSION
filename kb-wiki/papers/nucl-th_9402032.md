# Coulomb plus Nuclear Scattering in Momentum Space for Coupled Angular-Momentum States

> The Vincent--Phatak procedure for solving the momentum-space Schrodinger equation with combined Coulomb-plus-short-range potentials is extended to angular momentum states coupled by an optical potential---as occurs in spin 1/2 times 1/2 scattering. A generalization of the Blatt--Biedenharn phase shift parameterization is derived and applied to 500 MeV polarized-proton scattering from He3 and C13. The requisite high-precision partial-wave expansions and integrations are described.

# Coulomb plus Nuclear Scattering in Momentum Space for Coupled Angular-Momentum States

## Key claim

The Vincent-Phatak procedure for including the Coulomb potential in momentum-space scattering calculations can be extended to handle coupled angular-momentum states (as in spin-1/2 × spin-1/2 scattering) with sufficient precision for intermediate-energy proton scattering, provided high-precision partial-wave expansions and a sufficient number of partial waves are used; the method yields stable results for cutoff radii between 6 fm and 10 fm, but becomes unstable below 5 fm.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Vincent-Phatak procedure in terms of T-matrix elements rather than phase shifts, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a cutoff Coulomb potential plus short-range nuclear potential. For coupled channels, they diagonalize the non-symmetric S matrix (arising from an optical potential) using a similarity transformation, apply the Vincent-Phatak matching procedure in the diagonal basis to obtain Coulomb-modified phase shifts, then transform back to the original basis to compute observables. They generalize the Blatt-Biedenharn phase shift parameterization to allow two independent mixing parameters for the non-symmetric case.

## Key numbers

- 500 MeV polarized-proton scattering from He-3 and C-13 is the application energy and systems studied.
- 48–64 grid points are required to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation and obtain four-to-five place precision in the point Coulomb phase shifts σ_l.
- Six-place reproduction of the momentum-space potential V(k',k) required l_max = 48 partial waves and 96 integration points in the partial-wave projection; ten-place reproduction demanded l_max = 96.
- Stable results are obtained for cutoff radii in the range 6 fm ≤ R_cut ≤ 10 fm; R_cut ≤ 5 fm produces unstable results.
- 415 MeV protons scattered from the charge distribution of He-3 were used as a test case for pure-Coulomb scattering with finite-size effects.

## Context

This work extends the Vincent-Phatak procedure (Vincent and Phatak, 1974), originally formulated for pion scattering, to intermediate-energy proton scattering from spin-1/2 nuclei where tensor forces couple angular-momentum states. It addresses documented difficulties with the Vincent-Phatak procedure reported by Crespo and Tostevin (1990) and Picklesimer et al. (1984), who found sensitivity to the cutoff radius and several-percent errors in phase shifts. The authors confirm the finding of Arrellano, Brieva, and Love (1990) that the Vincent-Phatak procedure can be made sufficiently accurate if high-precision partial-wave expansions and large numbers of partial waves are used. The generalization of the Blatt-Biedenharn parameterization (Blatt and Biedenharn, 1952) to non-symmetric S matrices enables the treatment of optical potentials in coupled channels.
