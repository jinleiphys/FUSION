# Generalized parton distributions and spin structures of light mesons from a light-front Hamiltonian approach

> We present the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the valence quarks of the pion and the kaon in both momentum space and position space within the basis light-front quantization framework. These GPDs are obtained from the eigenvectors of a light-front effective Hamiltonian consisting of the holographic quantum chromodynamics (QCD) confinement potential, a complementary longitudinal confinement potential, and the color-singlet Nambu-Jona--Lasinio interactions for the valence quarks of mesons. We then calculate the generalized form factors of the pion and the kaon from the moments of these GPDs. Combining the tensor form factors with the electromagnetic form factors, we subsequently evaluate the impact parameter dependent probability density of transversely polarized quarks inside the pion and the kaon. The numerical results for the generalized form factors, tensor charges, as well as those for the probability densities and the transverse shift of the polarized densities are consistent with lattice QCD simulations and with chiral quark models.

## Key claim

The authors compute generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the valence quarks of the pion and kaon using light-front wave functions from the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) framework with an effective Hamiltonian that includes confinement and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) interactions, finding consistency with lattice QCD simulations and chiral quark models, but only within the valence Fock sector and at zero skewness.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a light-front effective Hamiltonian containing holographic QCD confinement, longitudinal confinement, and color-singlet NJL interactions to obtain light-front wave functions for the pion and kaon in the valence quark-antiquark Fock sector. They then compute the unpolarized GPD \(H\) and the chiral-odd GPD \(E_T\) at zero skewness using overlap integrals of these wave functions, and evolve the results using the DGLAP equation up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). From the moments of the GPDs, they extract electromagnetic and tensor form factors, and via Fourier transform obtain impact parameter dependent probability densities for transversely polarized quarks.

## Key numbers

- The model scale for the pion is \(\mu_{0\pi}^2 = 0.240\) GeV\(^2\); for the kaon it is \(\mu_{0K}^2 = 0.246\) GeV\(^2\).
- The confinement strength parameter \(\kappa\) is 227 MeV for the pion (\(u\bar{d}\)) and 276 MeV for the kaon (\(u\bar{s}\)).
- The quark masses used are \(m_q = 337\) MeV for the \(u\) and \(\bar{d}\) in the pion, and \(m_u = 308\) MeV, \(m_{\bar{s}} = 445\) MeV for the kaon.
- The basis truncation parameters are \(N_{\text{max}} = 8\) and \(L_{\text{max}} = 8\) to 32 for both mesons.
- The GPDs are evolved to final scales \(\mu^2 = 1, 10, 100\) GeV\(^2\) and shown at fixed \(-t = 0.11\) GeV\(^2\).
- The electromagnetic form factor \(A_{10}(t)\) of the pion is compared with experimental data from Amendolia (1986), Bebek (1974, 1977), Volmer (2000), and Horn (2006), and with lattice QCD from Brommel (2006).
- The gravitational form factor \(A_{20}(t)\) and tensor form factors \(B_{T10}(t)\) and \(B_{T20}(t)\) for the pion are compared with lattice QCD from Brommel (2007) and with the chiral quark model (\(\chi\)QM) from Nam (2010) at \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).
- The kaon electromagnetic form factor is compared with experimental data from Dally (1980) and Amendolia (1986).
- The kaon tensor form factors \(B_{T10}(t)\) and \(B_{T20}(t)\) are compared with the \(\chi\)QM from Nam (2011) at \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous BLFQ-NJL studies of light mesons by Jia (2018) that successfully predicted decay constants, electromagnetic form factors, charge radii, and parton distribution functions for the pion and kaon. The results are consistent with lattice QCD simulations by the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration (Brommel 2007) and with chiral quark model calculations by Nam (2010, 2011) and Broniowski (2010), while providing a framework that can be extended to study GPD evolution and spin structure for other mesons and for future comparison with deeply virtual Compton scattering data from JLab, COMPASS, and the Electron-Ion Collider.
