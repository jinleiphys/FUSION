# Phenomenological model for the Kbar N --> K Xi reaction

> A phenomenological model for the Kbar N --> K Xi reaction is suggested. The model includes s and u channel exchanges by Lambda, Sigma, Sigma(1385), and Lambda(1520) and s channel exchanges by above-threshold hyperonic resonances. Explicit expression for the propagator for a particle with spin 7/2 is presented. High-mass and high-spin resonances play a significant role in the process. We deal with the whole set of existing experimental data on the cross sections and polarizations in the energy range from the threshold to 2.8 GeV in the center-of-mass system and reach a good agreement with experiments. Applications of the model to other elementary reactions of Xi production and to Xi hypernuclear spectroscopy are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological model for the $\overline{K}N\to K\Xi$ reaction that achieves good agreement with existing experimental data, but the authors explicitly note that the model is restricted to the Born approximation (neglecting channel coupling and initial/final state interactions) and that the choice of which high-mass resonances to include is not unique, with the available data insufficient to determine a fully reliable theoretical picture.

## Method

The authors adopt a standard diagram approach with $s$ and $u$ channel exchanges by $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Sigma(1385)$, and $\Lambda(1520)$ hyperons, plus $s$ channel exchanges by above-threshold $\Sigma(2030)$ and $\Sigma(2250)$ resonances. Effective Lagrangians with pseudovector couplings are used for vertices, and explicit propagators are given for spin-1/2, 3/2, 5/2, and 7/2 particles, with a Gaussian form factor $F(q)=\exp(-\vec{q}^2/\Lambda^2)$ applied to each vertex. The model is fitted to 396 data points (integral and differential cross sections) for reactions $K^-p\to K^+\Xi^-$ and $K^-p\to K^0\Xi^0$, with independent checks against $K^-n\to K^0\Xi^-$ data and polarization measurements.

## Key numbers

* The energy range covered is from threshold to 2.8 GeV in the center-of-mass system.
* The fit without high-mass resonances yields $\chi^2=1121$ with 391 degrees of freedom; including $\Sigma(2030)$ and $\Sigma(2250)$ reduces $\chi^2$ to 985.
* The cutoff parameter for low-mass particles is $\Lambda_1=839$ MeV; for high-mass resonances it is $\Lambda_2=440$ MeV.
* The product of coupling constants $F_Y\equiv f_{NYK}f_{\Xi YK}$ for the final fit are: $F_\Lambda=0.3303$, $F_\Sigma=0.1185$, $F_{\Sigma(1385)}=-0.0057$, $F_{\Lambda(1520)}=-0.4076$, $F_{\Sigma(2030)}=0.0203$, $F_{\Sigma(2250)}=-0.0838$.
* The partial widths of $\Sigma(2030)$ and $\Sigma(2250)$ decays into $K\Xi$ are calculated as 1.2 MeV and 0.3 MeV, respectively, corresponding to branching ratios of roughly 0.7% and 0.3%.
* The reaction threshold is 1812-1819 MeV depending on the charge channel.

## Context

This work provides the first systematic theoretical analysis of the $\overline{K}N\to K\Xi$ reaction across all available charge channels, extending earlier fragmentary studies (e.g., those cited as "theor" in the text) that dealt with only small portions of the data. The model enables improved calculations for $\Xi$ hypernuclear production via the $(K^-,K^+)$ reaction on nuclei, which is a priority at J-PARC (Nagae, 2007), and can inform studies of $\Xi$ photoproduction $\gamma p\to K^+K^+\Xi^-$ investigated by the CLAS collaboration (Price, 2005; Guo, 2007). The results also provide a check on $\Xi$ coupling constants derived from flavor SU(3) symmetry, as used by Nakayama et al. (2006) for photoproduction analysis.
