# Heavy Quark Transport is Non-Gaussian Beyond Leading Log

> We find that heavy quark transport beyond leading logarithm at weak coupling is intrinsically non-Gaussian: the longitudinal momentum transfer distribution has asymmetric exponential tails that are crucial for equilibration dynamics. We show this by computing the leading-order momentum transfer kernel for relativistic heavy quarks in weakly coupled non-Abelian plasmas, matching perturbative momentum transfer on the thermal scale to hard-thermal-loop-resummed soft physics. This is the same structure previously found in strongly coupled holographic plasmas, showing that it is not peculiar to weak or strong coupling, conformality, or supersymmetry. We therefore expect that this is a robust feature that physical quark-gluon plasma should also exhibit.

# Heavy Quark Transport is Non-Gaussian Beyond Leading Log

## Key claim
Heavy quark transport beyond leading logarithm at weak coupling is intrinsically non-Gaussian: the longitudinal momentum transfer distribution has asymmetric exponential tails that are essential for equilibration dynamics. This structure is the same as previously found in strongly coupled holographic plasmas, indicating it is not peculiar to weak or strong coupling, conformality, or supersymmetry, and is therefore expected to be a robust feature of physical quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The authors computed the leading-order momentum transfer kernel for relativistic heavy quarks in weakly coupled non-Abelian plasmas by matching perturbative momentum transfer on the thermal scale (p ~ T) to hard-thermal-loop (HTL) resummed soft physics (p ~ gT), subtracting their overlap. They isolated the strict fixed-order kernel at order g^4 by inserting Heaviside step functions to separate hard and soft contributions, then determined the longitudinal momentum transfer distribution at all momenta via a Legendre transform of the evolution kernel K(x,v).

## Key numbers
- The fixed-order kernel S_FO is computed at order g^4, with the logarithmically enhanced piece proportional to (g^2 C_F m_D^2 / 4π) ln(m_D/T)
- The radius of convergence for the series determining K(x,v) at v=0 is R(v=0) = 1/(2T), with even cumulants scaling as M_n(0) ~ (2T)^n * n!/n^2 for large n
- In the ultra-relativistic limit γ >> 1, the radius of convergence becomes R(v) ~ 1/(4γ^2 T)
- For strongly coupled N=4 SYM, the radius of convergence scales as R(v) ~ 1/(r_0 γ^(3/2) T) with r_0 ≈ 1.7972
- The Debye mass is m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 [N_c/3 + N_c N_X/6 + T_F N_f/3]

## Context
This work extends the conceptual resolution of heavy quark equilibration dynamics established by Rajagopal (2025), who showed that heavy quark evolution is governed by a Kolmogorov equation rather than a Fokker-Planck equation, with equilibration controlled by a universal non-Gaussian condition. It also builds on the strongly coupled N=4 SYM calculation by Rajagopal (2025) which first showed the distinctive non-Gaussian longitudinal momentum transfer distribution. The authors resolve a long-standing issue from Moore (2004) regarding violation of the Einstein relation at nonzero velocity beyond leading logarithmic accuracy. The results enable revisiting heavy-flavor phenomenology with non-Gaussian kernels, organized in terms of the drag coefficient η_D(v), longitudinal diffusion coefficient κ_L(v), and a volatility exponent R(v) characterizing the asymmetric exponential tails.
