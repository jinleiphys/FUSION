# Delta excitation in antiproton-deutron annihilation

> The $Δ$-excitation in $\overline{p}d$ annihilation at rest was studied. The annihilation amplitude from the statistical model and the $πN$ amplitude from the resonance model were adopted in our calculations. We analyze the invariant mass of the $π^+p$ and $π^-p$ systems selecting the protons with momenta above 400 MeV/c and with respect to the different final reaction channels. Our model reproduces reasonably the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed $\Delta^{++}$ resonance peak in the $\pi^+p$ invariant mass spectrum from $\overline{p}d$ annihilation at rest can be reproduced by a model combining a statistical annihilation amplitude with a resonance-model $\pi N$ amplitude, but only when protons with momenta above 400 MeV/c are selected; the $\Delta^0$ resonance in the $\pi^-p$ system remains unobservable due to strong combinatorial background.

## Method

The authors calculate the amplitude for a triangle diagram where a pion from $\overline{p}N \rightarrow n\pi$ annihilation rescatters on a nucleon via the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance. The annihilation amplitude uses a statistical model for the charge configuration and a Gaussian parametrization for the pion multiplicity distribution, while the $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$ amplitude is computed with a resonance model using a fitted cut-off parameter $\Lambda = 0.33$ GeV. The deuteron wave function from the Bonn potential is adopted, and the invariant mass of $\pi^+p$ and $\pi^-p$ systems is computed for various final reaction channels, selecting protons with momenta above 400 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The cut-off parameter $\Lambda$ for the resonance model was fitted to $\pi^+p$ total cross section data and set to $\Lambda = 0.33$ GeV.
- The pion multiplicity distribution uses $\nu = 2.65 + 1.78 \ln s$ and $\sigma = 0.174 \nu s^{0.2}$, with $s$ the squared invariant mass of the $\overline{p}N$ system.
- The $\Delta^{++}$ resonance in the $\pi^+p$ invariant mass spectrum is clearly visible when selecting protons with momenta above 400 MeV/c.
- The dominant contribution to $\Delta^{++}$ excitation comes from reaction channels with one and two neutral pions ($m=1$ and $m=2$) in $\overline{p}d \rightarrow p \pi^+ 2\pi^- m\pi^0$.
- The contribution from the reaction $\overline{p}d \rightarrow p \pi^+ 2\pi^-$ (no neutral pions) is negligible.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of fast proton production in antiproton annihilation, particularly the suggestion by Kudryavtsev and Tarasov that fast protons arise from pion absorption on recoiling nucleons via baryon resonances. It directly addresses the OBELIX collaboration measurement (Ableev, 1994) which found a $\Delta^{++}$ peak but no $\Delta^0$ signal, and explains the absence of $\Delta^0$ as due to combinatorial background. The model enables predictions for specific final channels ($p 2\pi^+ 3\pi^-$ and $p 2\pi^+ 3\pi^- \pi^0$) that are under study at CERN, and clarifies which channels are most promising for observing the $\Delta^{++}$ resonance.
