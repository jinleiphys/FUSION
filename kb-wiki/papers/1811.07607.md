# Quarkonium dissociation in perturbative QCD

> For weakly bound quarkonia, we rederive the next-to-leading order cross sections of quarkonium dissociation by partons that include the hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation. Our results calculated with an effective vertex from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude reduce to those obtained by potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) in the relevant kinematical limit, and they can be used in a wide temperature range applicable to heavy quark systems in heavy ion collisions. Based on the lattice computation of the temperature-dependent binding energy, our numerical analysis on $Υ(1S)$ indicates that at high temperature the dominant mechanism for quarkonium dissociation is inelastic parton scattering as expected in the quasifree approximation, while it is the gluo-dissociation at low temperature. By comparing with the momentum diffusion coefficient of a heavy quark, we discuss possible O(g) corrections to the next-to-leading order thermal width.

# Quarkonium dissociation in perturbative QCD

## Key claim
For weakly bound quarkonia, the next-to-leading order cross sections for quarkonium dissociation by partons (including hard thermal loop resummation) are rederived using an effective vertex from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude; these results reduce to those from potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) in the relevant kinematical limit, but the authors claim their approach can interpolate between different temperature scales applicable to evolving plasmas, whereas the abstract glosses over the condition that the heavy quark-antiquark pair is assumed to be weakly bound and noninteracting after dissociation in the large \(N_c\) limit.

## Method
The authors derive an effective vertex from the leading-order Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for gluon-quarkonium interactions, then use it to calculate inelastic parton scattering (\(p + \Upsilon \rightarrow p + Q + \bar{Q}\)) at next-to-leading order. For gluo-dissociation (\(g + \Upsilon \rightarrow Q + \bar{Q}\)), they incorporate hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation into the gluon dispersion relation to obtain next-to-leading order cross sections. The cross sections are then convoluted with thermal distribution functions to compute thermal widths, using temperature-dependent binding energies from lattice QCD for \(\Upsilon(1S)\).

## Key numbers
- The leading-order gluo-dissociation cross section for a Coulombic bound state is \(\sigma_{LO}(k) = \frac{2^7 g^2 a_0^2}{d_\Upsilon N_c} \frac{E^{7/2}(k-E)^{3/2}}{k^5}\), where \(E = 2m - m_{\Upsilon} > 0\) is the binding energy and \(a_0 = 16\pi/(N_c m g^2)\) is the Bohr radius.
- The next-to-leading order gluo-dissociation cross section is \(\sigma_{NLO}(k) \simeq \left[1 - \frac{m_D^2}{4k^2}\left(\log\left(\frac{8k^2}{m_D^2}\right) - 2\right)\right] \sigma_{LO}(k)\).
- The inelastic parton scattering cross section for (anti)quark-quarkonium scattering is \(\sigma_{NLO}^{(q,\bar{q})}(k_1) \simeq \frac{3 N_f (N_c^2 - 1) g^4 a_0^2}{4 N_c d_\Upsilon d_q \pi} \left[\log\left(\frac{4k_1^2}{m_D^2}\right) - 2\right]\).
- The inelastic parton scattering cross section for gluon-quarkonium scattering is \(\sigma_{NLO}^{(g)}(k_1) \simeq \frac{3 (N_c^2 - 1) g^4 a_0^2}{4 d_\Upsilon d_g \pi} \left[\log\left(\frac{4k_1^2}{m_D^2}\right) - 2\right]\).
- For \(\Upsilon(1S)\), the bottom quark mass is \(m = 4.8\) GeV and the Bohr radius is \(a_0 = 0.14 - 0.18\) fm.
- The Coulombic binding energy is \(243 - 432\) MeV for fixed coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.3 - 0.4\).
- The \(\Upsilon(1S)\) ground state is expected to survive up to \(T \sim 600\) MeV and melts at higher temperature.

## Context
This work extends the partonic picture of quarkonium dissociation developed by Peskin (1979) and Bhanot and Peskin (1979) for gluo-dissociation, and by Rapp (2001) for inelastic parton scattering in the quasifree approximation. It provides a bridge between the effective field theory framework of pNRQCD (as developed by Brambilla et al. (2008, 2010, 2011)) and phenomenological applications in heavy ion collisions, enabling calculations that interpolate between temperature regimes where different dissociation mechanisms dominate. The numerical results for \(\Upsilon(1S)\) using lattice binding energies from Karsch et al. (2001) show that inelastic parton scattering dominates at high temperature (where \(E < T\)) while gluo-dissociation dominates near the phase transition (where \(E > T\)), consistent with earlier quasifree approximation analyses by Rapp (2001) and Rapp and van Hees (2009).
