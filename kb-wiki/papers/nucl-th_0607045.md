# Study of the effect of the tensor correlation in oxygen isotopes with the charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock method

> Recently, we developed a mean-field-type framework which treats the correlation induced by the tensor force. To exploit the tensor correlation we introduce single-particle states with the parity and charge mixing. To make a total wave function have a definite charge number and a good parity, the charge number and parity projections are performed. Taking a variation of the projected wave function with respect to single-particle states a Hartree-Fock-like equation, the charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock equation, is obtained. In the charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock method, we solve the equation selfconsistently. In this paper we extend the charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock method to include a three-body force, which is important to reproduce the saturation property of nuclei in mean-field frameworks. We apply the charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock method to sub-closed-shell oxygen isotopes (14O, 16O, 22O, 24O, and 28O) to study the effect of the tenor correlation and its dependence on neutron numbers. We obtain reasonable binding energies and matter radii for these nuclei. It is found that relatively large energy gains come from the tensor force in these isotopes and there is the blocking effect by occupied neutron orbits on the tensor correlation.

## Key claim

The charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock (CPPHF) method, extended to include a three-body force, yields reasonable binding energies and matter radii for sub-closed-shell oxygen isotopes and shows that relatively large energy gains come from the tensor force, but only for the specific correlations among same-\(j\) orbits allowed by the spherical symmetry assumption, and with the tensor force strength multiplied by a factor \(x_\text{T}=1.0\) or \(1.5\) to compensate for missing 2p-2h configurations.

## Method

The authors extended their previously developed CPPHF method to include a three-body force, which is needed to reproduce nuclear saturation in mean-field frameworks. They applied the method to the ground states of five oxygen isotopes (¹⁴O, ¹⁶O, ²²O, ²⁴O, and ²⁸O) assuming spherical symmetry and sub-closed-shell configurations for neutrons. Single-particle wave functions were expanded in a Gaussian basis (10 Gaussians per orbit, range 0.5–10 fm), and the CPPHF equation was solved self-consistently using a gradient or damped gradient method, with a quadratic constraint potential on charge number for neutron-rich cases.

## Key numbers

* ¹⁶O binding energy: –124.1 MeV in HF (no tensor contribution), –127.1 MeV in CPPHF with normal tensor force (\(x_\text{T}=1.0\)), and –127.6 MeV with strong tensor force (\(x_\text{T}=1.5\)).
* ¹⁶O tensor potential energy \(V_\text{T}\): 0.0 MeV in HF, –11.7 MeV in CPPHF (\(x_\text{T}=1.0\)), and –38.3 MeV in CPPHF (\(x_\text{T}=1.5\)).
* ¹⁶O charge radius \(R_\text{c}\): 2.73 fm in all three calculations (HF, CPPHF \(x_\text{T}=1.0\), CPPHF \(x_\text{T}=1.5\)).
* ¹⁶O kinetic energy \(T\): 230.0 MeV (HF), 237.1 MeV (CPPHF \(x_\text{T}=1.0\)), 253.9 MeV (CPPHF \(x_\text{T}=1.5\)).
* For ¹⁶O, the central+three-body+Coulomb potential energy \(V_\text{C}+V_\text{3B}+V_\text{Coul}\) was –353.2 MeV (HF), –351.6 MeV (CPPHF \(x_\text{T}=1.0\)), and –342.2 MeV (CPPHF \(x_\text{T}=1.5\)).
* The three-body force parameter \(t_3\) in the MV1 force was positive, making the density-dependent force repulsive in the \(^3\)E channel.
* The LS force was multiplied by a factor of 2 to make its strength comparable to Skyrme and Gogny forces (Patyk 1999).
* The quadratic constraint potential used \(\lambda = 1000\) MeV for convergence in neutron-rich cases.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CPPHF studies on the alpha particle and ⁸Be (Sugimoto 2004, 2005) to heavier oxygen isotopes, and incorporates a three-body force following the approach of Vautherin (1972) for density-dependent forces. It contrasts with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) of Neff (2003) and Roth (2006), which absorbs tensor correlations into effective central and LS forces, and with the shell-model treatment of Myo (2005) and the AMD approach of Dote (2005), which treat 2p-2h configurations more completely. The work also relates to Otsuka (2005), who showed that tensor-force-induced particle-hole correlations change single-particle spin-orbit splittings. The present results enable future studies of tensor correlation effects in neutron-rich nuclei beyond oxygen, though the spherical symmetry assumption limits the treatment to same-\(j\) correlations only.
