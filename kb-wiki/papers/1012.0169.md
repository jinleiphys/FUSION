# Light-front transverse charge densities

> We discuss the recent interpretation of quark-distribution functions in the plane transverse to the light-cone direction. Such a mapping is model independent and allows one to build up multidimensional pictures of the hadron and to develop a semi-classical interpretation of the quark dynamics. We comment briefly the results obtained from the form factors of the nucleon. We show that a generalization to a target with arbitrary spin leads to a set of preferred values for the electromagnetic coupling characterizing structureless particles. Finally, we present the Wigner distribution for an unpolarized quark in an unpolarized proton and we propose an interpretation of the observed distortion as due to the orbital angular momentum of the quark.

# Light-front transverse charge densities

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent probabilistic interpretation of hadron charge densities is only possible in the infinite momentum frame (IMF) with the Drell-Yan-West condition \(q^+ = 0\), not in the Breit frame; the transverse charge densities for a transversely polarized target exhibit multipole patterns that are directly linked to quark orbital angular momentum and the composite nature of the target, and for a structureless particle these patterns must vanish, yielding a set of "natural" electromagnetic moments.

## Method

The authors derive transverse charge densities as two-dimensional Fourier transforms of the \(J^+\) current matrix element in the IMF, using the Dirac and Pauli form factors \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) for spin-1/2 hadrons. They generalize the formalism to arbitrary spin \(j\) and compute the natural electromagnetic multipole moments by imposing that a structureless particle shows no distortion (axial symmetry) in its transverse charge density. They also compute the Wigner distribution for an unpolarized quark in an unpolarized proton via the generalized transverse-momentum dependent distribution (GTMD) \(F^e_{11}\), using the Light-Cone Constituent Quark Model and the Chiral Quark-Soliton Model.

## Key numbers

- The induced dipole moment in natural units (\(e/2M\)) is given by \(F_2(0)\), i.e. the anomalous magnetic moment.
- For the Wigner distribution, the equi-amplitude curve is shown for \(k_\perp = 0.45\) GeV (Figure 1) and radial distributions are shown for \(k_\perp = 0.55\) GeV (Figure 2).
- Natural electromagnetic moments for spins \(j \leq 3\) are tabulated: for example, for \(j=1/2\) the natural values are \(G_{E0}(0)=1\), \(G_{M1}(0)=1\); for \(j=1\) they are \(G_{E0}(0)=1\), \(G_{M1}(0)=2\), \(G_{E2}(0)=-1\); for \(j=3/2\) they are \(G_{E0}(0)=1\), \(G_{M1}(0)=3\), \(G_{E2}(0)=-3\), \(G_{M3}(0)=-1\); for \(j=2\) they are \(G_{E0}(0)=1\), \(G_{M1}(0)=4\), \(G_{E2}(0)=-6\), \(G_{M3}(0)=-4\), \(G_{E4}(0)=1\); for \(j=5/2\) they are \(G_{E0}(0)=1\), \(G_{M1}(0)=5\), \(G_{E2}(0)=-10\), \(G_{M3}(0)=-10\), \(G_{E4}(0)=5\), \(G_{M5}(0)=0\); for \(j=3\) they are \(G_{E0}(0)=1\), \(G_{M1}(0)=6\), \(G_{E2}(0)=-15\), \(G_{M3}(0)=-20\), \(G_{E4}(0)=15\), \(G_{M5}(0)=6\), \(G_{E6}(0)=-1\).

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of form factors in impact-parameter space pioneered by Soper (1976), Burkardt (2000, 2002), and the transverse charge density formalism for spin-1/2 hadrons by Carlson and Vanderhaeghen (2007). It generalizes the multipole analysis to arbitrary spin, following earlier work by Carlson et al. (2008), Alexandrou et al. (2008, 2009), and Lorce (2009). The paper also connects GTMDs, introduced by Meissner et al. (2009), to Wigner distributions, providing a semi-classical interpretation of quark orbital angular momentum. The results enable future studies of phase-space distributions in hadrons and constrain the electromagnetic couplings of hypothetical structureless particles of any spin.
