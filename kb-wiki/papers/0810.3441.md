# Spectral functions of argon and calcium

> Precise knowledge of the cross sections for neutrino interactions with nuclei is important not only for existing experiments but also for development of future detectors. When momentum transferred to the nucleus by the probe is high enough, the impulse approximation is valid, i.e. the nucleus can be described as composed of independent nucleons and the interaction happens between neutrino and a single bound nucleon. The cross section of the nucleus is then expressed as an integral of the free cross section (modified by the off-shell effect) folded with the so-called spectral function, describing the distribution of nucleon momenta and energies. I present results obtained for the approximate spectral functions of argon and calcium. The accuracy of the approach is verified by comparison with a broad spectrum of precise electron-scattering data for the calcium target. Some of these data lie in the kinematical region corresponding to neutrino interactions what indirectly shows that a similar level of accuracy is achieved for neutrinos. The calculated Ar(nu_mu,mu) cross section is significantly lower than the one predicted within the Fermi gas model.

## Key claim

The approximate spectral functions constructed for argon and calcium yield neutrino-nucleus cross sections that are significantly lower than those predicted by the Fermi gas model, but the accuracy of the approach is only verified for kinematic regions where the impulse approximation is valid (transferred momentum above ~400 MeV/c), and the claimed 10.9% reduction in total cross section for argon applies only to the quasielastic contribution.

## Method

The author constructs approximate spectral functions for medium-mass nuclei (argon and calcium) by parametrizing the distribution of nucleon momenta and energies within the impulse approximation framework. The accuracy of the calcium spectral function is tested by comparing calculated quasielastic electron-scattering cross sections with experimental data from Williamson (beam energies 130–841 MeV, scattering angles 45.5–140°). The argon spectral function is then used to compute the differential cross section dσ/dEμ for νμ-argon quasielastic scattering, and the result is compared with the Fermi gas model prediction.

## Key numbers

- The total quasielastic cross section for ⁴⁰Ar(νμ,μ⁻) scattering calculated with the spectral function is 10.9% lower than the Fermi gas model prediction.
- The impulse approximation starts to fail when the typical transferred momentum in the quasielastic peak decreases below ~400 MeV/c.
- Electron-scattering data used for validation span beam energies from 130 to 841 MeV and scattering angles from 45.5° to 140°.

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach, previously applied to lighter nuclei, to medium-mass nuclei (argon and calcium) relevant for neutrino detectors. The results contradict the simpler Fermi gas model, which overestimates cross sections in the quasielastic region. The electron-scattering validation against Williamson (2008) data shows that the approach is reliable where the impulse approximation holds, but the author notes that low-momentum-transfer contributions (beyond the impulse approximation) would further increase the discrepancy with the Fermi gas model. This enables more accurate modeling of neutrino interactions for experiments such as those using argon detectors.
