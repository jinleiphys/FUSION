# Charge-Symmetry Breaking, Rho-Omega Mixing and the Quark Propagator

> The momentum-dependence of the $ρ^0$-$ω$ mixing contribution to charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) in the nucleon-nucleon interaction is compared in a variety of models. We focus in particular on the role that the structure of the quark propagator plays in the predicted behaviour of the $ρ^0$-$ω$ mixing amplitude. We present new results for a confining (entire) quark propagator and for typical propagators arising from explicit numerical solutions of quark Dyson-Schwinger equations. We compare these to hadronic and free quark calculations. The implications for our current understanding of CSB experiments is discussed.

# Charge-Symmetry Breaking, Rho-Omega Mixing and the Quark Propagator

## Key claim
The momentum-dependence of the ρ⁰-ω mixing amplitude is strongly model-dependent, but all calculations considered, including a confining (entire) quark propagator, Dyson-Schwinger equation solutions, heavy free quarks, and hadronic models, predict a strong suppression of the ρ⁰-ω mixing contribution to the nucleon-nucleon potential compared to the usual momentum-independent assumption, with nodes appearing in the potential between 0.5 and 0.8 fm for some models.

## Method
The authors compute the ρ⁰-ω mixing self-energy Π(q²) from a quark-antiquark loop insertion, using three types of quark propagators: an analytic confining (entire) propagator with Gaussian form factors, numerical solutions of quark Dyson-Schwinger equations fitted to fπ and the quark condensate, and a free quark propagator with heavy quarks (m_u=600 MeV, m_d=604 MeV). The mixing amplitude is then inserted into the standard one-boson exchange potential with Bonn coupling constants and form factors, and the static central part of the NN potential is obtained by Fourier transformation to coordinate space.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses used: m_u = 450 MeV, m_d = 454 MeV for the confining propagator; m_u = 600 MeV, m_d = 604 MeV for the heavy free quark case; u-d mass difference set to 4 MeV throughout.
- Confining propagator parameters: μ = 523.6 MeV, f₀ = 4.432 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻², determined by fitting to fπ = 93 MeV and ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(225 MeV)³.
- Bonn couplings: gρ²/4π = 0.41, gω²/4π = 10.6, with cutoffs Λρ = 1400 MeV, Λω = 1500 MeV, and Cρ = fρ/gρ = 6.1.
- Quark-vector-meson product coupling: gqρ gqω = 20.0, with Gaussian form factor cutoff Λ = 1 GeV.
- Nodes in the coordinate-space potential V(r) appear between 0.5 and 0.8 fm for the Dyson-Schwinger and hadronic models; the heavy free quark case (600 MeV) removes the node present in the original GHT calculation at about 0.9 fm.

## Context
This work extends the initial investigation by Goldman, Henderson and Thomas (GHT) (1992), who first showed that the ρ⁰-ω mixing amplitude is strongly momentum-dependent and produces a node in the potential at about 0.9 fm using free quark propagators. The present study addresses the criticism that GHT did not include quark confinement, which led to an unphysical qq̄ production threshold at q²=4m_q². The results support the basic conclusions of GHT and are consistent with subsequent hadronic model calculations by Piekarewicz and Williams (1993) and QCD sum rule studies by Hatsuda, Hogaasen, Hjorth-Jensen and Mangano (1993). The findings challenge the previously accepted theoretical understanding of charge-symmetry breaking phenomena, including the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and the neutron-proton analyzing power difference measured at TRIUMF and IUCF, and suggest that new sources of CSB may be needed to explain existing data.
