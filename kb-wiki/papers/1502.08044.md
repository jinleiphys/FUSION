# Linearly resummed hydrodynamics in a weakly curved spacetime

> We extend our study of all-order linearly resummed hydrodynamics in a flat space~\cite{1406.7222,1409.3095} to fluids in weakly curved spaces. The underlying microscopic theory is a finite temperature $\mathcal{N}=4$ super-Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling. The AdS/CFT correspondence relates black brane solutions of the Einstein gravity in asymptotically \emph{locally} $\textrm{AdS}_5$ geometry to relativistic conformal fluids in a weakly curved 4D background. To linear order in the amplitude of hydrodynamic variables and metric perturbations, the fluid's energy-momentum tensor is computed with derivatives of both the fluid velocity and background metric resummed to all orders. We extensively discuss the meaning of all order hydrodynamics by expressing it in terms of the memory function formalism, which is also suitable for practical simulations. In addition to two viscosity functions discussed at length in refs.~\cite{1406.7222,1409.3095}, we find four curvature induced structures coupled to the fluid via new transport coefficient functions. In ref.~\cite{0905.4069}, the latter were referred to as gravitational susceptibilities of the fluid. We analytically compute these coefficients in the hydrodynamic limit, and then numerically up to large values of momenta.

## Key claim

The paper extends all-order linearly resummed hydrodynamics from flat spacetime to weakly curved spacetimes, finding that the fluid's stress-energy tensor acquires four new curvature-induced tensor structures beyond the two viscosity functions previously identified in flat space, with the new transport coefficient functions (gravitational susceptibilities) computed analytically in the hydrodynamic limit and numerically up to large momenta.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to relate black brane solutions of Einstein gravity in asymptotically locally AdS₅ to relativistic conformal fluids in a weakly curved 4D background, working to linear order in both hydrodynamic variables and metric perturbations. They solve the dynamical components of the bulk Einstein equations for metric corrections parameterized by functions h, k, j_i, and α_ij, constructing all possible linear structures from the velocity field u_μ and metric perturbation h_μν to decompose these functions, then solve the resulting ordinary differential equations to extract the transport coefficient functions from near-boundary asymptotics.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is η₀/s = 1/(4π), universal for strongly coupled gauge theories with Einstein gravity duals
- The energy density and pressure satisfy ε = 3P = 3(πT)⁴, consistent with the tracelessness condition T_μ^μ = 0
- In the hydrodynamic limit, the shear viscosity function expands as η = 1 + (1/2)(2 - ln 2)iω - (1/8)q² - (1/48)[6π - π² + 12(2 - 3 ln 2 + ln² 2)]ω² + ...
- The ζ function expands as ζ = (1/12)(5 - π - 2 ln 2) + ...
- The κ function expands as κ = 2 + (1/4)(5 + π - 6 ln 2)iω + ...
- The ρ function has leading term ρ = 2 + ...
- The ξ function has leading term ξ = ln 2 - 1/2 + ...
- The θ function satisfies θ = (3/2)ζ for generic values of momenta

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous flat-space all-order linearly resummed hydrodynamics (Bu and Lublinsky, 2014) to include small metric perturbations, building on the fluid/gravity correspondence framework of Bhattacharyya et al. (2008) and the gravitational susceptibility formalism introduced by Banerjee et al. (2009). The results enable practical simulations of relativistic hydrodynamics in curved backgrounds using memory function formalisms, with the transport coefficient functions provided numerically up to large momenta for applications in strongly coupled plasmas described by N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory.
