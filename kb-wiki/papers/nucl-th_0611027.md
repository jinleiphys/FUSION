# Jet-quenching in a 3D hydrodynamic medium

> We study the radiative energy loss of hard partons in a soft medium in the multiple soft scattering approximation. The soft medium is described by a 3D hydrodynamical model and we treat the averaging over all possible parton paths through the medium without approximation. While the nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ does not reflect the high quality of the medium description (except in a reduced systematic uncertainty in extracting the quenching power of the medium), the hydrodynamical model also allows to study different centralities and in particular the angular variation of $R_{AA}$ with respect to the reaction plane, allowing for a controlled variation of the in-medium path-length. We study the angular dependence of $R_{AA}$ for different centralities, discuss the influence of hydrodynamical expansion and flow and comment on the comparison with preliminary data.

## Key claim

The nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ for central collisions is not sensitive to the detailed form of the medium evolution model, only constraining an overall quenching scale; however, the angular dependence of $R_{AA}$ with respect to the reaction plane in non-central collisions provides systematic control over the average in-medium path-length and reveals sensitivity to the spatial asymmetry of the medium and its removal by hydrodynamic expansion.

## Method

The authors model the soft medium using a 3D relativistic fluid dynamics (RFD) code with initial conditions at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm, with all parameters fixed by fits to soft-sector observables (elliptic flow, pseudorapidity distributions, low-$p_T$ spectra). They compute radiative energy loss of hard partons in the BDMPS formalism using quenching weights from Salgado and Wiedemann (2003), evaluating line integrals of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}(\xi) = K \cdot 2 \cdot \epsilon^{3/4}(\xi)$ along each parton path. The averaging over all possible parton production vertices and paths through the medium is performed without approximation, and the resulting medium-modified hadron spectra are used to compute $R_{AA}$ as a function of azimuthal angle $\phi$ relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic evolution starts at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm.
- For central collisions, three different medium models (2D hydro, 3D hydro, parametrized fireball) require different values of the quenching scale $K$, with a $\pm 50\%$ spread in $K$ values interpreted as the systematic error in tomographic analysis via $R_{AA}$.
- At impact parameter $b = 7.5$ fm, the difference between in-plane and out-of-plane $R_{AA}$ is of order 20\%.
- When comparing a static medium (using the hydrodynamic initial state) to the full hydrodynamic evolution at $b = 7.5$ fm, the hydrodynamic evolution reduces the difference between in-plane and out-of-plane emission by about 7\%.
- For the static medium scenario, the quenching scale must be readjusted from $K = 3.6$ to $K = 0.65$ to describe central collisions.
- Including transverse flow requires an increase of $K$ by about 30\% to describe $R_{AA}$ in central collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior jet-quenching calculations that used oversimplified medium models (e.g., 1D Bjorken expansion) by employing a 3D hydrodynamic evolution code from Nonaka (2006). It builds on earlier estimates of 3D expansion effects on $R_{AA}$ by Hirano (2002, 2003) but uses a more rigorous energy-loss formalism from Salgado and Wiedemann (2003). The study enables systematic extraction of medium properties by comparing angular-dependent $R_{AA}$ with preliminary PHENIX data, and suggests that if the observed trend toward larger angular splitting is confirmed, it would rule out a linear path-length dependence of energy loss and indicate a scaling stronger than $L^2$.
