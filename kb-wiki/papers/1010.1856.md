# Systematic parameter study of hadron spectra and elliptic flow from viscous hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

> Using the (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1, we present systematic studies of the dependence of pion and proton transverse momentum spectra and their elliptic flow in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions on the parameters of the hydrodynamic model (thermalization time, initial entropy density distribution, decoupling temperature, equation of state, and specific shear viscosity eta/s). We identify a tension between the slope of the proton spectra, which (within hydrodynamic simulations that assume a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio) prefer larger eta/s values, and the slope of the p_T-dependence of charged hadron elliptic flow, which prefers smaller values of eta/s. Changing other model parameters does not appear to permit dissolution of this tension

## Key claim

The paper identifies a tension between proton transverse momentum spectra, which within viscous hydrodynamic simulations assuming a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio prefer larger η/s values (0.16–0.24), and the pT-dependence of charged hadron elliptic flow, which prefers smaller η/s values (0.08–0.16). The authors state that changing other model parameters does not appear to permit dissolution of this tension.

## Method

The authors used the (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 A GeV, solving second-order Israel-Stewart equations with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. They varied the specific shear viscosity η/s from 0.08 to 0.24, the thermalization time τ0 between 0.2 and 0.8 fm/c, the decoupling temperature Tdec from 100 to 160 MeV, the equation of state (comparing s95p-PCE, SM-EOS Q, and EOS L), and the initial entropy density profile (Glauber versus CGC-fKLN initializations). Final hadron spectra were computed via the Cooper-Frye procedure on an isothermal freeze-out surface with a quadratic ansatz for the viscous correction δf to the distribution function.

## Key numbers

- η/s values studied: 0.08, 0.16, and 0.24 (constant, temperature-independent specific shear viscosity)
- Thermalization time τ0 varied between 0.2 and 0.8 fm/c
- Decoupling temperature Tdec varied from 100 to 160 MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature Tchem = 165 MeV
- Centrality: 0-5% for spectra (impact parameter b = 2.33 fm) and 20-30% for elliptic flow (b = 7.5 fm)
- Comparison with PHENIX data focused on pT < 1.5 GeV/c for pions and pT < 2.5 GeV/c for protons

## Context

This work extends earlier ideal fluid dynamics studies of 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Hirano 2002, Kolb 2002, Kolb 2003, Huovinen 2005, Huovinen 2006, Huovinen 2007, Eskola 2007, Broniowski 2008) to the case of viscous fluid dynamics, using a state-of-the-art equation of state (s95p-PCE) that matches lattice QCD data (Cheng 2007, Bazavov 2009) at high temperatures to a chemically frozen hadron resonance gas at low temperatures. Related viscous hydrodynamic work had been reported by Luzum (2008) and Bozek (2009). The identified tension between spectra and elliptic flow preferences for η/s motivates future studies with temperature-dependent η/s and transition to a microscopic kinetic description for the late hadronic stage.
