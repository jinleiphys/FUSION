# Indications of the possible observation of the lowest-lying 1^{-+} QCD state

> We discuss properties of 1^{-+} exotic mesons within the framework of the QCD field-theoretic approach. We estimate the mass of the lowest-lying 1^{-+} exotic meson using renormalization-improved QCD sum rules, and find that the mass lies around $1.26\pm 0.15$ GeV, in good agreement with the $π_1(1400)$ data. This state should be expected in QCD. We find that the mass for the lowest-lying strange 1^{-+} meson is $1.31\pm 0.19$ GeV. Our result hints that the K^*(1410) may be the lowest-lying 1^{-+} nonet state.

## Key claim

Using a novel interpolating current (twist-3 rather than twist-4) and renormalization-group-improved QCD sum rules, the mass of the lowest-lying \(1^{-+}\) exotic meson is found to be \(1.26\pm 0.15\) GeV, which agrees with the \(\pi_1(1400)\) data; the result is conditional on the inclusion of RG corrections, without which no stable sum-rule plateau exists.

## Method

The author computed the two-point correlation function for a gauge-invariant local current \(J(x) = n^\beta n^\mu \bar d(x) \sigma_{\alpha\beta} g_s G_\mu^{\ \alpha}(x) u(x)\), which couples to both \(1^{-+}\) and \(0^{++}\) states. A non-diagonal correlation function was used to subtract the scalar \(a_0(980)\) contribution, and the Borel-transformed sum rule was RG-improved by multiplying each operator-product-expansion term by the appropriate factor \(L^{(-2\gamma_J+\gamma_n)/2b}\). The mass was extracted by applying the differential operator \(M^4 \partial/\partial M^2\) to the sum rule and requiring a stable plateau within the Borel window \(1\ \text{GeV}^2 < M^2 < 2\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Key numbers

* Mass of the lowest-lying \(1^{-+}\) exotic meson: \(m_{\pi_1} = 1.26 \pm 0.15\) GeV, with excited-state threshold \(s_0 = 2.5 \pm 0.7\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* Mass of the lowest-lying strange \(1^{-+}\) meson: \(m_{K^*(1^{-+})} = 1.31 \pm 0.19\) GeV, with \(s_0 = 2.3 \pm 0.9\ \text{GeV}^2\) (scenario 2, using \(K^*_0(1430)\) as the lowest scalar).
* World average mass of \(\pi_1(1400)\) from PDG: \(1376 \pm 19\) MeV.
* Mass of the neutral \(\pi_1\) from the E852 experiment: \(1257 \pm 20 \pm 25\) MeV (Adams, 2006).
* Mass of \(a_0(1450)\): \(1.474 \pm 0.019\) GeV (PDG).

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum-rule studies of the \(1^{-+}\) hybrid meson by Govaerts (1984), Balitsky (1986), Chetyrkin and Narison (2000), and Jin and Körner (2000), which used the twist-4 current \(J_\mu\) and obtained masses \(\gtrsim 1.55\) GeV but lacked RG improvement or stable plateaus. By adopting a twist-3 current and including RG corrections, the author obtains a lower mass consistent with the \(\pi_1(1400)\) and suggests that the \(K^*(1410)\) may be the strange member of the \(1^{-+}\) nonet. The results enable further studies of exclusive electroproduction at JLAB, HERMES, or Compass, and of two-body \(B\) decays involving \(\pi_1(1400)\) at \(B\) factories.
