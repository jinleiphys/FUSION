# The Role of the D13 (1520) Resonance in eta Electroproduction

> We investigate the electroproduction of eta mesons below a center of momentum energy of 1.6 GeV, with particular emphasis on the roles of the N*(1535) and N*(1520) resonances. Using the effective Lagrangian approach, we show that the transverse helicity amplitude of the N*(1535) can be extracted with good accuracy from the new eta electroproduction data, under reasonable assumptions for the strength of the longitudinal helicity amplitude. In addition, although the differential cross section is found to to have a small sensitivity to the N*(1520) resonance, it is shown that a recently completed double polarization experiment is very sensitive to this resonance.

# The Role of the D13 (1520) Resonance in eta Electroproduction

## Key claim

The transverse helicity amplitude of the N*(1535) can be extracted with good accuracy from new eta electroproduction data under the assumption that the longitudinal helicity amplitude is small (fixed at zero or roughly 10% of the transverse amplitude); however, the differential cross section data alone show only a weak signal for the D13(1520) resonance, and a recently completed double polarization experiment is predicted to be much more sensitive to this resonance.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree approximation, including s- and u-channel nucleon, S11(1535) and D13(1520) exchanges, and t-channel vector meson (rho and omega) exchanges, to analyze J-Lab differential cross section data for eta electroproduction at Q² values of 2.4 and 3.6 GeV² and center-of-mass energies W from 1490 to 1590 MeV. They performed chi-squared fits using the CERN routine MINUIT with eight free parameters (the S11 helicity amplitude A_{1/2}^s, three D13 helicity amplitudes, and four off-shell parameters) while fixing the eta-nucleon coupling constant g_{eta NN} to 1.88 and the vector meson coupling combinations to fixed values.

## Key numbers

- At Q² = 2.4 GeV², the extracted S11 transverse helicity amplitude A_{1/2}^s = 50 ± 4 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- At Q² = 3.6 GeV², the extracted S11 transverse helicity amplitude A_{1/2}^s = 35 ± 2 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- The D13 total width was taken as 125 MeV with an eta-nucleon branching ratio of 0.1%, giving a partial decay width of 0.125 MeV
- The S11 total width was taken as 150 MeV with eta-nucleon, pion-nucleon, and pion-pion-nucleon branching ratios of 0.5, 0.4, and 0.1 respectively
- The eta-nucleon coupling constant g_{eta NN} = 1.88
- The vector meson coupling combinations: λ_ρ g_v^ρ + λ_ω g_v^ω = 5.9 and λ_ρ g_t^ρ + λ_ω g_t^ω = 17.5

## Context

This work extends earlier effective Lagrangian analyses of eta photoproduction by the same group (Davidson, Mukhopadhyay, and collaborators, 1990s) to the electroproduction regime, and directly addresses the analysis of new J-Lab data from Armstrong et al. (1999) which had ignored the D13 resonance contribution. The paper builds on prior phenomenological knowledge of the Q² dependence of S11 and D13 helicity amplitudes from analyses by Burkert (1990s) and Stoler (1990s), and provides predictions for a double polarization experiment at J-Lab (Stoler, 1999) that should enable much stronger constraints on the D13 helicity amplitudes than differential cross section data alone can provide.
