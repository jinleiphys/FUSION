# Forward virtual Compton scattering and the Lamb shift in chiral perturbation theory

> We compute the spin-independent structure functions of the forward virtual-photon Compton tensor of the proton at one loop using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and dispersion relations. We study the relation between both approaches. We use these results to generalize some sum rules to virtual photon transfer momentum and relate them with sum rules in deep inelastic scattering. We then compute the leading chiral term of the polarizability correction to the Lamb shift of the hydrogen and muonic hydrogen. We obtain -87.05/n^3 Hz and -0.148/n^3 meV for the correction to the hydrogen and muonic hydrogen Lamb shift respectively.

## Key claim

The authors compute the leading chiral (pion-loop) contribution to the polarizability correction of the Lamb shift in hydrogen and muonic hydrogen using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, obtaining parameter-free, model-independent results that are the leading term in the chiral expansion and are subject to corrections of order \(m_{\pi}/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) (with the \(\Delta\)-nucleon mass difference as the dominant uncertainty scale).

## Method

The spin-independent structure functions \(S_1\) and \(S_2\) of the forward virtual-photon Compton tensor of the proton were computed at one loop in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory using Feynman diagrams and also via dispersion relations from the imaginary parts \(W_1\) and \(W_2\). The resulting Euclidean-space expressions were then inserted into the matching coefficient \(c_{3,NR}^{pl_i}\) for the polarizability contribution to the Lamb shift, and the integrals were evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

* Hydrogen Lamb shift polarizability correction: \(-87.05/n^3\) Hz (for the leading chiral term, with the \(\Delta\) contribution taken to infinity).
* Muonic hydrogen Lamb shift polarizability correction: \(-0.148/n^3\) meV (for the leading chiral term, with the \(\Delta\) contribution taken to infinity).
* Muonic hydrogen Lamb shift polarizability correction evaluated at \(m_{\mu}=m_{\pi}\): \(-0.132339/n^3\) meV.
* Logarithmic approximation for hydrogen (from Pineda (2004)): \(-64.4841/n^3\) Hz, compared to the full result \(-87.0488/n^3\) Hz.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory computation of spin-independent forward Compton structure functions to virtual photons, generalizing sum rules (such as the Baldin sum rule) to nonzero \(Q^2\) and connecting them to deep inelastic scattering moments. The polarizability correction to the Lamb shift is a necessary step for a model-independent extraction of the proton radius from muonic hydrogen measurements, as anticipated by Antognini (2005) and partially computed by Pineda (2004). The results are consistent with model-based and dispersion-relation calculations by Friar (1997), Pachucki (1999), Rosenfelder (1999), Faustov (1999), and Martynenko (2005).
