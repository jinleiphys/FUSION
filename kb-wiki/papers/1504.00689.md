# Kaon and pion parton distribution amplitudes to twist-three

> We compute all kaon and pion parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) to twist-three and find that only the pseudotensor PDA can reasonably be approximated by its conformal limit expression. At terrestrially accessible energy scales, the twist-two and pseudoscalar twist-three PDAs differ significantly from those functions commonly associated with their forms in QCD's conformal limit. In all amplitudes studied, SU(3) flavour-symmetry breaking is typically a 13% effect. This scale is determined by nonperturbative dynamics; namely, the current-quark-mass dependence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The heavier-quark is favoured by this distortion, for example, support is shifted to the s-quark in the negative kaon. It appears, therefore, that at energy scales accessible with existing and foreseeable facilities, one may obtain reliable expectations for experimental outcomes by using these "strongly dressed" PDAs in formulae for hard exclusive processes. Following this procedure, any discrepancies between experiment and theory will be significantly smaller than those produced by using the conformal-limit PDAs. Moreover, the magnitude of any disagreement will either be a better estimate of higher-order, higher-twist effects or provide more realistic constraints on the Standard Model.

## Key claim

At energy scales accessible with existing and foreseeable facilities, the twist-two and pseudoscalar twist-three parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) for pions and kaons differ significantly from their conformal-limit forms, and only the pseudotensor twist-three PDA can reasonably be approximated by its conformal limit expression. SU(3) flavour-symmetry breaking is typically a 13% effect, determined by the current-quark-mass dependence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors computed all six kaon and pion two-particle distribution amplitudes to twist-three by solving the quark gap equations and Bethe-Salpeter equations numerically using both rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation and a modern DCSB-improved (DB) kernel. They employed algebraic parametrizations of the dressed-quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes via Nakanishi-like representations, computed up to 50 moments of the PDAs, and reconstructed the pointwise forms using a Gegenbauer-alpha procedure where the order alpha is determined by the moments themselves rather than fixed beforehand.

## Key numbers

- SU(3) flavour-symmetry breaking is typically a 13% effect across all amplitudes studied
- The kaon twist-two PDA peak is shifted 12-16% away from its position in QCD's conformal limit
- The pseudotensor twist-three PDA in the conformal limit equals 6 u (1-u), identical to the twist-two conformal limit
- The pseudoscalar twist-three PDA conformal limit is 1, but the algebraic model yields 1 + (1/2) C_2^{(1/2)}(u - bar u)
- Results are computed at renormalization scale zeta = 2 GeV
- Reconstruction procedure with j_max = 2 produces errors epsilon_m < 1%

## Context

This work extends prior DSE-based calculations of twist-two PDAs by Chang (2013) and Shi (2014) to include all twist-three amplitudes, and provides the first DSE computation of the pointwise behavior of these amplitudes. Previous estimates by Braun (1989) and Ball (2006) used conformal transformation properties combined with QCD sum rules. The results enable more reliable predictions for hard exclusive processes at existing facilities like Jefferson Lab, and provide better constraints on Standard Model parameters in analyses of nonleptonic B-meson decays.
