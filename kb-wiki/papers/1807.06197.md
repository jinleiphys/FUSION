# Z+jet productions in heavy-ion collisions

> We report a systematic study of Z+jet correlation in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC by combining the next-leading-order matrix elements calculations with matched parton shower in Sherpa for the initial Z+jet production, and Linear Boltzmann transport Model for jet propagation in the expanding quark-gluon-plasma. Our numerical results can well explain CMS measurements on Z+jet correlation in Pb+Pb collisions: the shift of $p_T$ imbalance $x_{jZ}=p_T^{jet}/p_T^Z$ and their mean values, the suppression of the average number of jet partners per Z boson $R_{jZ}$, as well as the modification of azimuthal angle correlations $Δϕ_{jZ}$. We also demonstrate that high-order corrections play a significant role in the understanding of Z+jet correlations at high energies.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that combining next-to-leading-order (NLO) matrix elements with matched parton shower (PS) in Sherpa for the initial Z+jet production, together with the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model for jet propagation in the quark-gluon plasma, can explain CMS measurements of Z+jet correlations in Pb+Pb collisions, but they note that high-order corrections play a significant role in understanding these correlations at high energies, implying that leading-order calculations alone are insufficient.

## Method

The authors used Sherpa to generate initial Z+jet events in p+p collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV, with low jet multiplicities calculated at NLO matched to parton shower resummation. Jet propagation and induced medium response in the hot quark-gluon plasma were simulated with the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes both elastic (2 → 2 scattering) and inelastic scattering (described by the High-twist formalism for induced gluon radiation), and follows the propagation of jet shower partons, radiated gluons, and thermal recoil partons. The underlying event background energy was subtracted event-by-event for Pb+Pb collisions following the CMS procedure.

## Key numbers

* The Z boson mass is M_Z = 91.18 GeV.
* The jet transverse momentum threshold is p_T^{jet} = 30 GeV.
* For high energy Z bosons with p_T^Z > 80 GeV, multi-jet processes give approximately 15% contribution to R_{jZ}.
* Multi-jet processes give approximately 50% contribution when x_{jZ} < 0.5.
* For p_T^Z > 60 GeV, the mean value of momentum imbalance ⟨x_{jZ}⟩ in Pb+Pb collisions is reduced by almost 15% compared to p+p.
* The relative shift Δ⟨x_{jZ}⟩ between p+p and Pb+Pb collisions is 0.075 for p_T^Z = 40-50 GeV, 0.106 for 50-60 GeV, 0.128 for 60-80 GeV, and 0.143 for p_T^Z > 80 GeV.
* The strong coupling constant α_s = 0.2 was chosen to best describe experimental data of R_{jZ} in Pb+Pb.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet quenching using Z-tagged jets, which are considered a golden channel because Z bosons do not participate in strong interactions and have no contributions from fragmentation or decay, unlike photons. The authors build on the CMS measurements of Z+jet correlations at 5.02 TeV (Sirunyan 2017) and prior work on the Linear Boltzmann Transport model (Wang 2013, He 2015) and Sherpa Monte Carlo generator (Gleisberg 2008). The results enable more precise comparisons between theoretical models and experimental data for jet energy loss in heavy-ion collisions, and demonstrate the importance of including higher-order corrections beyond leading-order calculations.
