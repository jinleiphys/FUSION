# Microscopic calculations of medium effects for 200-MeV (p,p') reactions

> We examine the quality of a G-matrix calculation of the effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction for the prediction of the cross section and analyzing power for 200-MeV (p,p') reactions that populate natural parity states in $^{16}$O, $^{28}$Si, and $^{40}$Ca. This calculation is based on a one-boson-exchange model of the free NN force that reproduces NN observables well. The G-matrix includes the effects of Pauli blocking, nuclear binding, and strong relativistic mean-field potentials. The implications of adjustments to the effective mass ansatz to improve the quality of the approximation at momenta above the Fermi level will be discussed, along with the general quality of agreement to a variety of (p,p') transitions.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a baseline calculation of conventional medium effects (Pauli blocking, nuclear binding, and relativistic mean-field potentials) for 200-MeV (p,p') reactions, with the explicit caveat that the effective mass ansatz used in the G-matrix method works best for a different choice of effective masses for continuum nucleons than those previously determined from nuclear matter studies.

## Method

The authors start with an updated one-boson-exchange (OBE) representation of the free NN interaction (based on the Bonn-B potential) and solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation in infinite nuclear matter to obtain a density-dependent G-matrix that includes Pauli blocking, nuclear binding, and relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) effects. The resulting G-matrix is converted to a Yukawa-function form for use in Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) calculations using the LEA program from Kelly, which operates in coordinate space and assumes zero-range exchange. The calculations are compared to cross section and analyzing power measurements for natural-parity, isoscalar (p,p') transitions in 16O, 28Si, and 40Ca at 200 MeV (180 MeV for 28Si).

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: 200 MeV for (p,p') reactions on 16O and 40Ca; 180 MeV for 28Si
- On-shell momentum at 200 MeV: 1.55 fm^-1
- Fermi momentum used in G-matrix calculations: 1.0 fm^-1
- The inelasticity parameter for lower partial waves differed from unity by as much as 18% at full nuclear density
- The σ meson mass is 550 MeV in both T=0 and T=1 channels, with coupling constants g^2/4π = 5.966 (T=0) and 8.5 (T=1)
- The π meson mass is 138.03 MeV with coupling g^2/4π = 13.8 and cutoff 1700 MeV
- The ρ meson mass is 769 MeV with coupling g^2/4π = 0.99 (f_ρ/g_ρ = 6.1) and cutoff 1300 MeV
- The ω meson mass is 782.6 MeV with coupling g^2/4π = 22 and cutoff 1500 MeV
- The nucleon mass in the medium is reduced to approximately 70% of its free-space value at nuclear matter density

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner G-matrix approach (Brueckner, Bethe, Goldstone, 1950s-1960s) and its relativistic Dirac-Brueckner extension (Shakin et al., Brockmann and Machleidt, Horowitz and Serot, ter Haar and Malfliet) to inelastic proton scattering at 200 MeV, comparing specifically to the relativistic calculations of Furnstahl and Wallace (where nucleons in the nucleus are described by Dirac bound states). The paper establishes a baseline for future exploration of exotic medium effects (such as changes in meson masses from QCD vacuum modifications) in polarization transfer measurements, using the data of Seifert on 16O and 40Ca and Chen on 28Si as reference points.
