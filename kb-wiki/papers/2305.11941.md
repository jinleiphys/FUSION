# Quantum Simulations of SO(5) Many-Fermion Systems using Qudits

> The structure and dynamics of quantum many-body systems are the result of a delicate interplay between underlying interactions, which leads to intricate entanglement structures. Despite this apparent complexity, symmetries emerge and have long been used to determine the relevant degrees of freedom and simplify classical descriptions of these systems. In this work, we explore the potential utility of quantum computers with arrays of qudits in simulating interacting fermionic systems, when the qudits can naturally map these relevant degrees of freedom. The Agassi model of fermions is based on an underlying $so(5)$ algebra, and the systems it describes can be partitioned into pairs of modes with five basis states, which naturally embed in arrays of $d=5$ qudits (qu5its). Classical noiseless simulations of the time evolution of systems of fermions embedded in up to twelve qu5its are performed using Google's cirq software. The resource requirements of the qu5it circuits are analyzed and compared with two different mappings to qubit systems, a physics-aware Jordan-Wigner mapping and a state-to-state mapping. We find advantages in using qudits, specifically in lowering the required quantum resources and reducing anticipated errors that take the simulation out of the physical space. A previously unrecognized sign problem has been identified from Trotterization errors in time evolving high-energy excitations. This has implications for quantum simulations in high-energy and nuclear physics, specifically of fragmentation and highly inelastic, multi-channel processes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that mapping the SO(5)-symmetric Agassi model onto arrays of five-dimensional qudits (qu5its) can reduce quantum resource requirements and mitigate errors that take simulations out of the physical space compared to qubit-based mappings, though the advantage in entangling gate count depends on whether the hardware natively supports qudit operations.

## Method

The authors constructed quantum circuits for state preparation and Trotterized time evolution of the Agassi model, which describes fermions with pairing and particle-hole interactions governed by an so(5) algebra. They performed classical noiseless simulations of systems with up to twelve qu5its (24 fermionic modes) using Google's cirq software, and compared the qu5it circuit resource requirements against two qubit mappings: a physics-aware Jordan-Wigner mapping using four qubits per mode-pair and a state-to-state mapping using three qubits per mode-pair.

## Key numbers

* Systems with up to twelve qu5its (corresponding to 24 fermionic modes) were simulated using classical noiseless circuits in cirq.
* The qu5it mapping embeds each mode-pair into a 5-dimensional Hilbert space, compared to 2^(2Ω) dimensions for the naive qubit mapping with 2Ω sites.
* The physics-aware Jordan-Wigner mapping requires four qubits per mode-pair, while the state-to-state mapping requires three qubits per mode-pair.
* Five coupling parameter sets were used for demonstrations: set-0 = (1.0, 0.0, 0.0), set-1 = (1.0, 0.5, 0.5), set-2 = (1.0, 1.5, 0.5), set-3 = (1.0, 0.5, 1.5), set-4 = (1.0, 1.5, 1.5) for (ε, V, g).

## Context

This work extends the first pioneering quantum simulations of the Agassi model by Perez Fernandez et al. (2022) and Saiz et al. (2022), which used Jordan-Wigner mappings for two and four modes, by exploiting the underlying SO(5) symmetry to map mode-pairs directly onto qu5its. The approach builds on prior studies of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model on qubit systems (including VQE and ADAPT-VQE work by various authors) and generalizes to qudit systems following the direction of Calixto (2021) for coupled spin-d systems. The techniques developed here can also be applied to other physical interpretations of the SO(5) algebra, including charge-independent pairing models and vibration-rotation models in nuclear physics.
