# Bound states of $^{9}_ϕ$Be and $^{6}_{ϕϕ}$He nuclei with $ϕ$+$α$+$α$ and $ϕ$+$ϕ$+$α$ cluster models

> We investigate the $^{9}_ϕ$Be and $^{6}_{ϕϕ}$He $ϕ$ mesic nuclei within the framework of the three-body cluster model as the $ϕ$+$α$+$α$ and $ϕ$+$ϕ$+$α$ systems, using the Faddeev formalism in configuration space. The $ϕ$-$α$ potential is determined through a folding procedure of the HAL QCD $ϕ$-$N$ interaction in the $^4S_{3/2}$ channel with the matter distribution of $^4$He. The phenomenological $α$-$α$ and $ϕ$-$ϕ$ potentials are taken from the literature. Additionally, we construct a Wood-Saxon (WS) type interaction to simulate the $ϕ$-$α$ potential, also taken from the literature, based on an effective Lagrangian approach that includes $K\bar{K}$ meson loops in the $ϕ$-meson self-energy. A comparison of binding energies obtained for both types of the $ϕ$-$α$ interactions reveals qualitative agreement. %between the obtained approaches. We predict the binding energy for the $^{9}_ϕ$Be and $^{6}_{ϕϕ}$He $ϕ$ mesic nuclei as the mirror $ϕ$+$α$+$α$ and $ϕ$+$ϕ$+$α$ systems in the range of 1-11 MeV and 3-10 MeV, respectively. The range of values of the binding energies relies on the choice of the WS $ϕ$-$α$ interaction parameters.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $^{9}_{\phi}$Be and $^{6}_{\phi\phi}$He $\phi$ mesic nuclei can form bound states as three-body cluster systems, but the binding energy values depend strongly on the choice of the Wood-Saxon $\phi$-$\alpha$ interaction parameters, yielding a range of 1-11 MeV for $^{9}_{\phi}$Be and 3-10 MeV for $^{6}_{\phi\phi}$He rather than a single definitive value.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for the $\phi+\alpha+\alpha$ and $\phi+\phi+\alpha$ three-body systems, treating the $\alpha$ particles and $\phi$ mesons as identical bosons where appropriate. The $\phi$-$\alpha$ potential was obtained through two independent approaches: a folding procedure using the HAL QCD $\phi$-$N$ interaction in the $^{4}S_{3/2}$ channel with a Gaussian matter distribution for $^{4}$He, and a Wood-Saxon type interaction simulated from an effective Lagrangian approach that includes $K\bar{K}$ meson loops in the $\phi$-meson self-energy. The $\alpha$-$\alpha$ potential was taken from the Ali-Bodmer double Gaussian potential, and the $\phi$-$\phi$ potential from a sum of two Yukawa terms fitted to the $f_{2}(2010)$ resonance.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $^{9}_{\phi}$Be ($\phi+\alpha+\alpha$ system): 3.20, 6.13, and 9.69 MeV for the $\widetilde{V}_{\phi\alpha}$ potential with cutoff parameters $\Lambda_c = 2000$, 3000, and 4000 MeV, respectively
- Binding energy of $^{9}_{\phi}$Be using the $V_{\phi\alpha}$ folding potential: 7.03, 9.79, and 10.86 MeV for $^{4}$He $rms$ radii of 1.84, 1.70, and 1.56 fm, respectively
- Binding energy of $^{6}_{\phi\phi}$He ($\phi+\phi+\alpha$ system): 1.23, 5.41, and 8.19 MeV for $\widetilde{V}_{\phi\alpha}$ with $\Lambda_c = 2000$, 3000, and 4000 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{6}_{\phi\phi}$He using $V_{\phi\alpha}$: 5.01, 8.32, and 10.5 MeV for $rms$ radii of 1.84, 1.70, and 1.56 fm
- Depth of the $V_{\phi\alpha}$ folding potential varies from approximately -38 MeV to -54 MeV depending on the $rms$ radius
- Depth of the $\widetilde{V}_{\phi\alpha}$ potential varies from approximately -20 MeV to -35 MeV depending on the cutoff parameter
- Binding energy of the two-body $\phi+\alpha$ system ($^{5}_{\phi}$He): 0.80, 3.19, and 4.71 MeV for $\widetilde{V}_{\phi\alpha}$ with $\Lambda_c = 2000$, 3000, and 4000 MeV; 2.967, 4.780, and 5.976 MeV for $V_{\phi\alpha}$ with $rms$ 1.84, 1.70, and 1.56 fm

## Context

This work extends the three-body cluster model approach previously applied to $p$-shell hypernuclei such as $^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He, $^{6}_{\Xi\Xi}$He, $^{9}_{\Lambda}$Be, and $^{9}_{\Xi}$Be by Motoba (1983), Hiyama (2001, 2009), and others, now applied to $\phi$ mesic nuclei. The study uses the HAL QCD $\phi$-$N$ potential from Lyu (2022) and the effective Lagrangian approach from Cobos-Martinez (2017) to construct $\phi$-$\alpha$ interactions. The results enable future experimental searches for $\phi$ mesic nuclei and provide predictions that can be tested against measurements of $\phi$ meson binding in light nuclear systems.
