# Nature of particles azimuthal anisotropy at low and high transverse momenta in ultrarelativistic A+A collisions

> LHC data on the correlations of the elliptic flow $v_2$ of particles at low and high transverse momenta $p_T$ from Pb+Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV are analyzed in the framework of the HYDJET++ model. This model includes soft and hard components which allows to describe the region of both low and high transverse momenta. The origin of $v_2$ values in different $p_T$ regions is investigated at different centralities. It is shown that the experimentally observed correlations between $v_2$ at low and high $p_T$ in peripheral lead-lead collisions is due to correlation of particles in jets.

## Key claim
The experimentally observed correlations between elliptic flow at low and high transverse momenta in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions arise not solely from jet quenching in an asymmetric medium, but also from the correlation of particles within jets (dijet topology), which becomes a significant source of azimuthal anisotropy for centralities beyond 30–40%.

## Method
The authors used the HYDJET++ model, which combines a soft hydrodynamic component (thermal hadronic state with resonance decays) and a hard component (PYTHIA-based QCD parton scatterings with PYQUEN parton energy loss in a dense medium). They generated Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for seven centrality bins (5–60%) and calculated elliptic flow coefficients v₂ using the scalar product method (with three subevents and large η gaps) and four-particle cumulants, comparing to CMS data and to the true reaction-plane v₂ᴿᴾ.

## Key numbers
- Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, with centrality bins from 5–10% to 50–60%.
- Number of generated events per centrality bin: from 2×10⁶ (5–10%) to 7×10⁶ (50–60%).
- Low-pT region: 1.0 < pT < 1.25 GeV/c; high-pT region: 14 < pT < 20 GeV/c.
- For centralities up to 30–40%, v₂ᴿᴾ at low and high pT remains correlated; beyond 30–40%, v₂ᴿᴾ at high pT decreases.
- In the hard component, non-zero elliptic flow appears at pT > 10 GeV/c due to jet quenching.
- At pT < 4 GeV/c, v₂{4} and v₂{SP} in HYDJET++ are similar to v₂ᴿᴾ and agree with CMS data.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of elliptic flow correlations first observed by ATLAS (2013) and CMS (2017) in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. It uses the two-component HYDJET++ model, which simultaneously describes soft and hard processes, unlike models such as EPOS or QGSJet. The results clarify that for peripheral collisions (centrality > 30–40%), standard flow measurement methods (cumulants and scalar product) become sensitive to dijet correlations rather than global event geometry, enabling better interpretation of experimental flow data at high pT.
