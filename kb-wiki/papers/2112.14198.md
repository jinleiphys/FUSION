# Dark Matter Effects on the Compact Star Properties

> The neutron star properties are generally determined by the equation of state of $β$-equilibrated dense matter. In this work, we consider the interaction of fermionic dark matter (DM) particles with the nucleons via Higgs exchange and investigate its effect on the neutron star properties with the relativistic mean-field model equation of state coupled with DM. We deduce that DM significantly affects the neutron star properties, such as considerably reduce the maximum mass of the star, which depends on the percentage of the DM considered inside the neutron star. The tidal Love numbers both for electric and magnetic cases and surficial Love numbers are also studied for DM admixed NS. It is observed that the magnitude of tidal and surficial Love numbers increase with more DM percentage. Further, we point out that post-Newtonian tidal corrections to gravitational waves decreased by increasing DM percentage. Also, the DM effect on the GW signal is significant during the late inspiral and merger stages of binary evolution for GW frequencies >500 Hz.

# Dark Matter Effects on the Compact Star Properties

## Key claim
Fermionic dark matter particles interacting with nucleons via Higgs exchange can significantly reduce the maximum mass of a neutron star, with the magnitude of reduction depending on the percentage of dark matter present; the tidal and surficial Love numbers increase with higher dark matter percentage, while post-Newtonian tidal corrections to gravitational waves decrease.

## Method
The authors use the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model to compute the equation of state of beta-equilibrated dense matter, coupled with a fermionic dark matter component that interacts with nucleons via Higgs exchange. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass and radius, then compute gravitoelectric and gravitomagnetic tidal Love numbers (up to l=4) and surficial Love numbers by solving the relevant perturbation equations. The tidal corrections to gravitational wave phase are evaluated using post-Newtonian formalism up to 3.5 PN order.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of IOPB-I-U (DM0) neutron star: 2.149 solar masses, radius 11.748 km
- Dark matter Fermi momenta considered: 0.00 GeV (DM0), 0.03 GeV (DM3), and 0.05 GeV (DM5)
- Dark matter particle mass: 200 GeV; Higgs mass: 125 GeV; nucleon mass: 939 MeV
- DM-Higgs coupling y = 0.07; vacuum expectation value of Higgs field v = 246 GeV; nucleon-Higgs form factor f = 0.35
- Spin-independent scattering cross-section for m_chi = 200 GeV: 9.70 x 10^-46 cm^2
- Dark matter Fermi momentum value: approximately 0.033 GeV (based on assumed nucleon-to-DM number density ratio of 10^3)
- GW170817 constraint on dimensionless tidal deformability: Lambda_1.4 = 190 (+390, -70)
- GW190814 constraint (NSBH scenario) on dimensionless tidal deformability: Lambda_1.4 = 616 (+273, -158)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Das et al. 2020, 2021) on fermionic dark matter effects in neutron stars, where they found that DM admixture could explain the secondary component of GW190814 as a massive neutron star and predicted delays in binary neutron star mergers. The present calculation focuses on higher-order tidal Love numbers (electric and magnetic) and surficial Love numbers, which are relevant for upcoming third-generation gravitational wave detectors. The formalism for tidal Love numbers follows Damour and Nagar (2009) and Chamel (2021), while the dark matter interaction model is consistent with constraints from XENON-1T, PandaX-II, PandaX-4T, LUX, and LHC experiments.
