# Towards thermodynamical consistency of qusiparticle picture

> The purpose of the present article is to call attention to some realistic quasiparticle-based description of the quark/gluon matter and its consistent implementation in thermodynamics. A simple and transparent representation of the thermodynamical consistency conditions is given. This representation allows one to review critically and systemize available phenomenological approaches to the deconfinement problem with respect to their thermodynamical consistency. A particular attention is paid to the development of a method for treating the string screening in the dense matter of unbound color charges. The proposed method yields an integrable effective pair potential which can be incorporated into the mean-field picture. The results of its application are in reasonable agreement with lattice data on the QCD thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for a quasiparticle description of quark/gluon matter to be thermodynamically consistent, the effective Hamiltonian must satisfy specific constraints relating its temperature and density dependence, and that many existing phenomenological models violate these constraints.

## Method

The authors derive general thermodynamical consistency conditions for a quasiparticle Hamiltonian that depends on temperature T and particle densities n, obtaining explicit relations (Eqs. ctc1 and ctc1a) that the quasiparticle energies and background field must satisfy. They then apply these conditions to classify common quasiparticle spectral forms (momentum-independent mean field, effective mass, scaled coupling) and identify which are consistent. Finally, they develop a probabilistic model for string screening in dense color-charge matter, deriving the effective pair potential from the probability distribution of string lengths.

## Key numbers

* Average string length in the straight-string scenario: ⟨ℓ⟩ = 1/(π a² n), where a is the effective string radius and n is the color-charge density.
* Average string length in the fluctuating-string scenario: ⟨ℓ⟩ = Γ(1/3) (3/(4π n))^(1/3), with Γ(1/3) being Euler's Gamma function.
* Probability for a string to be shorter than η in the straight-string scenario: Q(η) = 1 - exp(-π a² n η).
* Probability for a string to be shorter than η in the fluctuating-string scenario: Q(η) = 1 - exp(-(4/3)π n η³).

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle consistency framework first derived in Syu (no year given) and Ton (no year given), and critically reviews phenomenological approaches to deconfinement including the bag model and its variants (MIT, SLAC, CLOUDY), temperature-dependent bag constant models (Levai and Heinz, no year; Kampfer, no year), excluded volume corrections (Hagedorn and Rafelski, no year; Gorenstein, no year), and mixed phase models (Ton, no year; Knoll, no year; Biro, Levai and Zimanyi, no year). The string screening model builds on the nearest-neighbor approximation of Ropke (no year) and provides a foundation for constructing thermodynamically consistent mean-field descriptions of the quark-gluon plasma that can be compared with lattice QCD thermodynamics.
