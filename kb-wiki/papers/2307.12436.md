# Polarization of spin-1/2 particles with effective spacetime dependent masses

> Semiclassical expansion of the Wigner function for spin-1/2 fermions having an effective spacetime-dependent mass is used to analyze spin-polarization effects. The existing framework is reformulated to obtain a differential equation directly connecting the particle spin tensor with the effective mass. It reflects the conservation of the total angular momentum in a system. In general, we find that the gradients of mass act as a source of the spin polarization. Although this effect is absent for simple boost-invariant dynamics, an extension to non-boost-invariant systems displays a non-trivial dependence of the spin density on the mass indicating that the spin polarization effects may be intertwined with the phenomenon of chiral restoration.

## Key claim

Gradients of an effective spacetime-dependent mass act as a source of spin polarization for spin-1/2 fermions, but this effect is absent for simple boost-invariant (Bjorken) dynamics; a non-trivial anti-correlation between spin density and mass appears only when boost invariance is abandoned.

## Method

The authors start from a semiclassical expansion of the Wigner function for spin-1/2 fermions with an effective spacetime-dependent mass M(x), originally derived by Florkowski (1995). They reformulate the kinetic equation for the axial current phase-space density into a differential equation for the GLW spin tensor S^{α,γδ}. The equation is then solved under two scenarios: (1) boost-invariant longitudinal expansion with transverse or longitudinal polarization, and (2) a non-boost-invariant one-dimensional expansion along the z-axis with a constant velocity v relating the non-zero spin-tensor components.

## Key numbers

- For boost-invariant expansion, the spin density σ(τ) = σ₀ τ₀/τ, where τ is the longitudinal proper time and τ₀, σ₀ are integration constants.
- For the non-boost-invariant case, the spin density σ(t,z) = (M₀/M(t)) σ₀(z - v(t - t₀)), where M₀ is the mass at initial time t₀, v is a constant with |v| < 1, and σ₀ defines the initial spatial profile.
- The Casimir operator for spin-1/2 particles is spin = 3/4.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical framework of Florkowski (1995), which was restricted to leading order, and complements the higher-order analysis of Wang (2021). It provides a direct differential equation connecting the spin tensor to the effective mass, showing that mass gradients act as a source term only when spin polarization is already present. The results suggest that spin polarization effects may be intertwined with chiral symmetry restoration, and the authors propose future coupling to Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type interactions to self-consistently determine M(x).
