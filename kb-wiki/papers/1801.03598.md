# Strong and radiative decays of DΞmolecular state and newly observed $Ω_c$ states

> In this work, we study strong and radiative decays of S-wave DΞmolecular state, which is related to the Ω^*_c states newly observed at LHCb. The coupling between the DΞmolecular state and its constituents D and Ξis calculated by using the compositeness condition. With the obtained coupling, the partial decay widths of the DΞmolecular state into the Ξ_c^{+}K^{-}, Ξ^{'+}_cK^{-} and Ω^{*}_c(2695)γfinal states through hadronic loop are calculated with the help of the effective Lagrangians. By comparison with the LHCb observation, the current results of total decay width support the Ω^{*}_c(3119) or Ω^{*}_c(3050) as DΞmolecule while the the decay width of the Ω^{*}_c(3000), Ω^{*}_c(3066) and Ω^{*}_c(3090) can not be well reproduced in the molecular state picture. The partial decay widths are also presented and helpful to further understand the internal structures of Ω^{*}_c(3119) and Ω^{*}_c(3050).

# Strong and radiative decays of DΞ molecular state and newly observed Ω_c states

## Key claim
The total decay widths calculated for the S-wave DΞ molecular state can reproduce the LHCb observations for Ω*_c(3119) or Ω*_c(3050), but the decay widths of Ω*_c(3000), Ω*_c(3066), and Ω*_c(3090) cannot be well reproduced in the molecular state picture, suggesting that only two of the five newly observed states are consistent with being DΞ molecules.

## Method
The coupling between the DΞ molecular state and its constituents D and Ξ was calculated using the compositeness condition (Z=0), which relates the binding energy to the coupling strength. With the obtained coupling constants, partial decay widths into Ξ_c^+K^-, Ξ'_c^+K^-, and Ω*_c(2695)γ final states were computed through hadronic loop diagrams using effective Lagrangians, with a monopole form factor containing a cutoff parameter λ varied between 0.91 and 1.00.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constants g_{Ω*_cΞD} for α=1.0 GeV: Ω*_c(3000)=7.08, Ω*_c(3050)=5.90, Ω*_c(3065)=5.52, Ω*_c(3090)=4.89, Ω*_c(3119)=4.08 (units: GeV^{-1})
- For Ω*_c(3050) as DΞ molecule: total decay width 0.61-0.81 MeV (experimental 0.8±0.2 MeV); Γ_{Ξ_cK}=0.61-0.81 MeV; Γ_{Ω*_c(2695)γ}=1.06-1.42×10^{-7} MeV
- For Ω*_c(3119) as DΞ molecule: total decay width 1.29-1.71 MeV (experimental 1.1±0.8 MeV); Γ_{Ξ_cK}=1.20-1.59 MeV; Γ_{Ξ'_cK}=0.094-0.122 MeV; Γ_{Ω*_c(2695)γ}=1.85-2.48×10^{-7} MeV
- Coupling constant g_{D*0D0γ}=2.0 GeV^{-1} from Γ_{D*0→D0γ}=26 keV; g_{D*+D+γ}=-0.5 GeV^{-1} from Γ_{D*+→D+γ}=1.334 keV
- Coupling constant g_{KD_s*D}=1.84 from light-cone QCD sum rules; g=6.6 from Hofmann (2005)

## Context
This work extends the study of hadronic molecular states using the compositeness condition, previously applied to XYZ resonances and the deuteron by Dong (2008, 2017) and Branz (2009). It directly addresses the five narrow Ω*_c states observed by LHCb (Aaij 2017) in the Ξ_c^+K^- mass spectrum. The results are compared with the constituent quark model calculations of Wang (2017) and with molecular interpretations by Montana (2017), Debastiani (2017), and Nieves (2017), as well as pentaquark-like interpretations by Kim (2017). The predicted radiative decay widths (order 0.1 eV) differ dramatically from those in the conventional three-quark picture (order 10^{-3} MeV), offering a clear experimental test to distinguish between molecular and quark-model interpretations.
