# Medium effects on heavy-flavour observables in high-energy nuclear collisions

> The peculiar role of heavy-flavour observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is discussed. Produced in the early stage, $c$ and $b$ quarks cross the hot medium arising from the collision, interacting strongly with the latter, until they hadronize. Depending on the strength of the interaction heavy quarks may or not approach kinetic equilibrium with the plasma, tending in the first case to follow the collective flow of the expanding fireball. The presence of a hot deconfined medium may also affect heavy-quark hadronization, being possible for them to recombine with the surrounding light thermal partons, so that the final heavy-flavour hadrons inherit part of the flow of the medium. Here we show how it is possible to develop a complete transport setup allowing one to describe heavy-flavour production in high-energy nuclear collisions, displaying some major results one can obtain. Finally, the possibility that the formation of a hot deconfined medium even in small systems (high-multiplicity p-Au and d-Au collisions, so far) may affect also heavy-flavour observables is investigated.

## Key claim

Heavy-flavour observables in high-energy nuclear collisions can be described by a complete transport setup, but the comparison between transport calculations and experimental data does not necessarily prove that charm quarks reach full kinetic equilibrium with the quark-gluon plasma during their partonic phase; instead, the observed collective flow signatures may arise from a combination of finite (weak-coupling) transport in the plasma and in-medium hadronization via recombination with light thermal partons.

## Method

The author develops a multi-step transport setup: initial heavy-quark pair production is simulated with the POWHEG-BOX event generator at NLO accuracy interfaced with a parton-shower stage, validated against p-p data and supplemented with nuclear parton distribution functions. Heavy-quark propagation in the plasma is modeled via the relativistic Langevin equation, derived from the Fokker-Planck approximation of the Boltzmann equation, with transport coefficients (momentum-diffusion coefficients \(\kappa_{\|/\perp}\) and friction \(\eta_D\)) evaluated from weak-coupling calculations or lattice-QCD results. The heavy quarks propagate through a hydrodynamically evolving fireball until they reach a fluid cell below the deconfinement temperature, where hadronization is modeled either via vacuum fragmentation functions or via in-medium recombination with light thermal partons.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to \(m_c = 1.3\) GeV, with renormalization and factorization scales \(\mu_R = \mu_F = m_T\) in the POWHEG-BOX calculation.
- The initial pre-equilibrium stage of the fireball is estimated to last \(\tau_0 \lesssim 1\) fm/c, with the QGP phase lasting \(\sim 5-10\) fm/c.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(D^0\) mesons are computed for 0-10% Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV (RHIC) and for 30-50% Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV (LHC), compared to STAR and ALICE data respectively.
- The momentum-diffusion coefficient \(\kappa\) for a static quark in a gluonic plasma is extracted from lattice-QCD force-force correlators, with a large systematic uncertainty band arising from extracting real-time information from imaginary-time correlators.
- The nuclear modification factor of heavy-flavour decay electrons in 0-20% most central d-Au collisions at RHIC (PHENIX) and of \(D\)-mesons in 0-100% p-Pb collisions at the LHC (ALICE) are shown, with the latter compatible with unity within present uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the heavy-flavour transport approach developed by the Torino group (Beraudo et al., 2015) and other groups (such as Rapp and collaborators, 2010-2015) to describe heavy-quark propagation in the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with purely hydrodynamic descriptions of soft observables and with jet-quenching models of high-\(p_T\) partons, instead addressing the intermediate regime where heavy quarks may partially approach kinetic equilibrium. The paper enables future extraction of heavy-flavour transport coefficients from experimental data, particularly through exclusive \(B\)-meson hadronic decays at the LHC, and extends the investigation to small systems (p-Au and d-Au collisions) where the formation of a hot deconfined medium remains an open question.
