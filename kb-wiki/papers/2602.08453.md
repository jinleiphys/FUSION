# Neutrinoless double beta decays of hyperons in covariant chiral perturbation theory

> Neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decays of spin-1/2 hyperons are investigated in a covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory framework, extended by a $ΔL=2$ operator proportional to the Majorana neutrino mass, where $L$ denotes the lepton number. Within the light Majorana neutrino exchange mechanism, the decay amplitudes are found to emerge at the one-loop level, representing the long-range contribution. The extended-on-mass-shell scheme is employed to renormalize the one-loop amplitudes and restore consistent chiral power counting. Consequently, the differential decay rates for all accessible hyperon $0νββ$ channels are predicted and the corresponding branching ratios are more than 20 orders of magnitude smaller than the current experimental upper bounds. Interestingly, it is found that the leading contribution to hyperon $0νββ$ decay is actually from short-range counterterm operators, as required by the renormalization argument. Neutrinoless transition form factors are proposed to determine this leading contribution through future lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The leading contribution to hyperon neutrinoless double beta decay actually comes from short-range counterterm operators at order p^2, not from the one-loop long-range contribution at order p^3, as required by renormalization; however, the numerical predictions for branching ratios reported in the paper include only the subleading one-loop long-range contribution because the short-range counterterms involve unknown low-energy constants that cannot yet be determined.

## Method

The authors extended SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory by including a Delta L=2 operator derived from the dimension-5 Weinberg operator, which generates the Majorana neutrino mass. They computed the one-loop Feynman diagrams for the long-range light-neutrino exchange mechanism, renormalized the amplitudes using dimensional regularization with the MS-1 scheme and the extended-on-mass-shell scheme to restore chiral power counting, and then evaluated the differential decay rates and branching ratios for six kinematically allowed hyperon decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratios for the four electronic modes (Sigma- -> p e- e-, Sigma- -> Sigma+ e- e-, Xi- -> p e- e-, Xi- -> Sigma+ e- e-) are of order 10^{-31} or less, evaluated with a benchmark effective Majorana mass m_ee = 100 meV
- Branching ratios for the two muonic modes (Sigma- -> p mu- mu-, Xi- -> p mu- mu-) are of order 10^{-28}, estimated with a benchmark effective Majorana mass m_mu_mu = 10 eV
- Mapping the experimental upper bound from HyperCP (B(Xi- -> p mu- mu-) < 4.0 x 10^{-8} at 90% C.L.) onto the light-neutrino-exchange parameterization yields an indicative scale m_mu_mu ~ 100 GeV
- Mapping the experimental upper bounds from BESIII (B(Sigma- -> p e- e-) < 6.7 x 10^{-5} and B(Xi- -> Sigma+ e- e-) < 2 x 10^{-5} at 90% C.L.) yields an indicative scale m_ee ~ 1 TeV
- The one-loop amplitudes are of chiral order O(p^3), while the counterterm contribution is of order O(p^2)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of neutrinoless double beta decay, previously applied to nuclear systems (Cirigliano, 2017, 2018) and meson decays (Liao, 2019, 2020; Zhou, 2021; Chen, 2025), to the hyperon sector for the first time. It improves upon earlier estimates by Barbero (2007, 2013) and Hernandez-Tome (2021) by providing a systematic chiral perturbation theory framework with controlled power counting and renormalization. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of the proposed neutrinoless transition form factors, which would determine the leading short-range counterterm contributions and allow complete predictions for hyperon 0nu beta beta decay branching ratios.
