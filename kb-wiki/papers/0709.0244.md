# Neutrino Interactions with Nuclei

> We investigate neutrino-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies incorporating quasi-elastic scattering and the excitation of 13 resonances as elementary processes, taking into account medium effects such as Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, mean-field potentials and in-medium spectral functions. A coupled-channel treatment of final state interactions is achieved with the GiBUU transport model. Results for inclusive reactions, neutrino- and electron-induced, as well as for pion production and nucleon knockout are presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a realistic, quantitative description of neutrino-induced pion production in nuclei is possible using the GiBUU transport model, but the authors hedge that the influence of final state interactions (FSI) must be treated with the same degree of sophistication as the primary production process and nuclear spectral function information, and that in-medium effects in neutrino-nucleus scattering are important for the interpretation of long-baseline (LBL) oscillation experiments.

## Method

The authors model neutrino-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies (0.5-2 GeV) by incorporating quasi-elastic (QE) scattering and the excitation of 13 resonances as elementary processes, using a local Thomas-Fermi Fermi gas for the nucleus with medium effects including Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, mean-field potentials, and in-medium spectral functions. Final state interactions (FSI) are treated via a coupled-channel approach using the GiBUU transport model, which solves the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation to describe the space-time evolution of particles through the nuclear medium, accounting for elastic and inelastic collisions, decays, resonance formation, and Pauli blocking.

## Key numbers

* Neutrino energy range considered: 0.5-2 GeV, where the relevant contributions are QE scattering and pion production.
* Axial dipole mass used: M_A = 1 GeV for the axial form factors in QE scattering.
* For neutrino energy E_nu = 1 GeV and scattering angle theta = 30 degrees, inclusive CC cross sections on Fe-56 and O-16 are presented as a function of energy transfer omega.
* For NC pion production on Fe-56 and O-16 at E_nu = 1 GeV, FSI effects cause strong suppression around pion kinetic energy T_pi approximately 0.13 GeV, where pion absorption and rescattering are most prominent due to Delta resonance excitation.
* For nucleon knockout on Fe-56, both QE scattering and Delta excitation contribute to the cross section above E_nu approximately 1.2 GeV with almost equal amounts.
* Only for neutrino energies up to approximately 0.5 GeV can one neglect the resonance contributions to nucleon knockout.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU model, previously tested on heavy-ion collisions, proton- and pion-induced reactions on nuclei, and photonuclear reactions, to neutrino-nucleus scattering. The authors compare their FSI results with those of Paschos and collaborators (Paschos 2000, Paschos 2007), finding that the two FSI models are quantitatively and even qualitatively different, with the Paschos model using vacuum cross sections and assuming constant pion energy during transport, while GiBUU includes in-medium modifications and energy redistribution. The results enable improved interpretation of long-baseline oscillation experiments by providing a more realistic treatment of nuclear effects, particularly for distinguishing QE-like events from pion production events that become QE-like through pion absorption.
