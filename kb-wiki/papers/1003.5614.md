# Modelling incomplete fusion dynamics of weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies

> The classical dynamical model for reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies is developed further. It allows a quantitative study of the role and importance of incomplete fusion dynamics in asymptotic observables, such as the population of high-spin states in reaction products as well as the angular distribution of direct alpha-production. Model calculations indicate that incomplete fusion is an effective mechanism for populating high-spin states, and its contribution to the direct alpha production yield diminishes with decreasing energy towards the Coulomb barrier. It also becomes notably separated in angles from the contribution of no-capture breakup events. This should facilitate the experimental disentanglement of these competing reaction processes.

## Key claim

The classical dynamical model for reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies is extended to track the time evolution of the surviving breakup fragment and the incomplete fusion product, enabling predictions of asymptotic angular distributions and spin/excitation energy distributions of primary incomplete fusion products. The model calculations are illustrative and do not aim to adjust any measurements, using a simplified test case of a pseudo-⁸Be projectile on ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Method

The model uses classical equations of motion for a projectile-target system, with projectile breakup stochastically sampled along the trajectory using an empirically determined exponential breakup probability density as a function of the distance of closest approach. At the breakup point, the internal energy, angular momentum, and separation of the two projectile fragments are Monte Carlo sampled from the projectile ground-state wave function and uniform/exponential distributions. The three bodies (two fragments and target) are then propagated in time; capture of a fragment occurs when its trajectory brings it within the fragment-target barrier radius. For incomplete fusion events, the three-body propagation transitions to two-body propagation at the moment the captured fragment reaches the target radius, allowing calculation of the primary incomplete fusion product's spin and excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- The s-wave Coulomb barrier for the ⁸Be + ²⁰⁸Pb system is 39.9 MeV.
- At E₀ = 45 MeV, the incomplete fusion cross section is 84.7 mb, the no-capture breakup cross section is 238.8 mb, and the complete fusion cross section is 149.2 mb (with only 1.5% of complete fusion following ⁸Be breakup).
- At E₀ = 65 MeV, the incomplete fusion cross section is 273.6 mb, the no-capture breakup cross section is 259.3 mb, and the complete fusion cross section is 981.5 mb (with 41% of complete fusion following ⁸Be breakup).
- The breakup function parameters are A = 5.98 × 10³ and α = 0.85 fm⁻¹.
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear potential between the alpha particle and the incomplete fusion product ²¹²Po has parameters (V, r, a) = (33.98 MeV, 1.48 fm, 0.63 fm).

## Context

This work extends the classical dynamical model of Diaz-Torres (2007) by adding time propagation of the surviving breakup fragment and the incomplete fusion product, enabling predictions of asymptotic angular distributions and primary product spin/excitation energy distributions. The approach complements quantum mechanical continuum-discretised coupled channels (CDCC) frameworks, which cannot unambiguously calculate integrated incomplete fusion and complete fusion cross sections or follow the evolution of surviving fragments after incomplete fusion. The model enables interpretation of ongoing experimental efforts to disentangle competing reaction mechanisms from alpha-production yields (Souza et al.) and particle-gamma coincidence experiments, and can be applied to gamma-ray spectroscopy and fusion measurements with radioactive nuclei.
