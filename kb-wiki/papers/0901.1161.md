# Coherent $ρ^0$ photoproduction in bulk matter at high energies

> The momentum transfer $Δk$ required for a photon to scatter from a target and emerge as a $ρ^0$ decreases as the photon energy $k$ rises. For $k>3\times10^{14}$ eV, $Δk$ is small enough that the interaction cannot be localized to a single nucleus. At still higher energies, photons may coherently scatter elastically from bulk matter and emerge as a $ρ^0$, in a manner akin to kaon regeneration. Constructive interference from the different nuclei coherently raises the cross section and the interaction probability rises linearly with energy. At energies above $10^{23}$ eV, coherent conversion is the dominant process; photons interact predominantly as $ρ^0$. We compute the coherent scattering probabilities in slabs of lead, water and rock, and discuss the implications of the increased hadronic interaction probabilities for photons on ultra-high energy shower development.

## Key claim

At photon energies above $10^{23}$ eV, coherent $\rho^0$ photoproduction in bulk matter becomes the dominant interaction mechanism, meaning photons interact predominantly as $\rho^0$ mesons rather than electromagnetically; this effect depends on the target material and is most pronounced in thick slabs where the coherence condition $\Delta k L \ll 1$ is satisfied.

## Method

The authors compute the probability for a high-energy photon to coherently convert into a $\rho^0$ meson in bulk matter by treating the target as a homogeneous medium and integrating the scattering amplitude over the target volume, including absorption of both photons and $\rho$ mesons. They use two approaches to determine the hadronic cross sections: a Glauber calculation based on HERA data on $\gamma p \rightarrow \rho p$ with a soft Pomeron extrapolation, and a generalized vector meson dominance (GVMD) model from Engel, Ranft and Roessler (ERR). The calculations are performed for slabs of lead, water, and standard rock, with the $\rho$ width and direct $\pi^+\pi^-$ production included via a Breit-Wigner mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The coherence length $l_f = \hbar/\Delta k$ exceeds the typical internuclear spacing of 0.2 nm for photon energies $k > 3 \times 10^{14}$ eV (for $M_{\pi\pi} = M_\rho = 778$ MeV/c$^2$)
- The amplitude for a photon to fluctuate to a $q\overline q$ pair is $F = 6.02 \times 10^{-2}$
- The $\rho^0$ width is $\Gamma_\rho = 150$ MeV
- In the ERR model, photonuclear interactions predominate over electromagnetic interactions above $5 \times 10^{19}$ eV; in the Glauber model, this crossover occurs above $5 \times 10^{20}$ eV
- Coherently produced $\rho$ interactions dominate above $10^{23}$ eV (ERR model) to $10^{24}$ eV (Glauber calculation)
- The ERR cross section scales as $A^{0.887}$, normalized so $\sigma(\gamma\text{Pb}) = 15$ mb at low energies
- For $W_{\gamma p} < 10^{11}$ eV, the hadronic cross section is constant; at higher energies it rises as $W_{\gamma p}^{0.2}$

## Context

This work extends the understanding of coherent processes in bulk matter, previously seen in kaon regeneration (as studied by others) and coherent neutrino forward scattering (Liu, year not specified), to the case of photon-to-$\rho^0$ conversion. Unlike the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect which decreases cross sections via destructive interference, this constructive interference raises the cross section. The results have direct implications for searches for ultra-high energy astrophysical $\nu_e$ using radio waves or acoustic pulses, as the shorter hadronic showers alter the expected signal characteristics compared to purely electromagnetic calculations that do not include these effects (Klein, Erice proceedings, year not specified).
