# Comparison of inclusive K+ production in proton-proton and proton-neutron collisions

> The momentum spectra of K+ produced at small angles in proton-proton and proton-deuteron collisions have been measured at four beam energies, 1.826, 1.920, 2.020, and 2.650 GeV, using the ANKE spectrometer at COSY-Juelich. After making corrections for Fermi motion and shadowing, the data indicate that K+ production near threshold is stronger in pp- than in pn-induced reactions. However, most of this difference could be made up by the unobserved K0 production in the pn case.

## Key claim

After correcting for Fermi motion and shadowing in the deuteron, inclusive K+ production near threshold is stronger in proton-proton collisions than in proton-neutron collisions, but most of this difference could be made up by unobserved K0 production in the pn case.

## Method

The ANKE spectrometer at COSY-Juelich measured double-differential cross sections for K+ produced at laboratory angles up to 4 degrees in proton-proton and proton-deuteron collisions at four beam energies. The pp data were modeled using three channels (pp to K+p Lambda, K+p Sigma0, and K+n Sigma+) with normalizations fitted to known total cross sections; the pd data were then compared after smearing the pp predictions over the deuteron Fermi momentum and applying a Glauber shadowing correction factor of 0.95.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies used: 1.826, 1.920, 2.020, and 2.650 GeV
- Mean ratio of double-differential cross sections d(sigma)_pd/d(sigma)_pp: 1.28 +/- 0.03 (stat) +/- 0.28 (syst) at 1.826 GeV; 1.38 +/- 0.06 +/- 0.30 at 1.920 GeV; 1.65 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.36 at 2.020 GeV; 1.34 +/- 0.04 +/- 0.23 at 2.650 GeV
- Extracted ratio of total cross sections for K+ production: sigma_pn/sigma_pp = 0.5 +/- 0.2
- Isospin ratio for NN to K N Lambda: sigma(I=0)/sigma(I=1) = 1.0 +/- 0.8
- Glauber shadowing correction factor applied: 0.95

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to extract the pn/pp K+ production ratio from nuclear target data, which had yielded inconclusive or model-dependent values ranging from about 1 to 3-4 (Buscher 2004, Debowski 1996, Schroeder 1989). The result that sigma_pn/sigma_pp = 0.5 +/- 0.2 is much smaller than the ratio of about twelve reported for eta production near threshold (Calen 1997). The measurement enables improved modeling of strangeness production in nucleon-nucleon collisions and in experiments with nuclear beams and targets; future work at ANKE using spectator proton detection in pd to p_sp K+ n Lambda reactions could reduce the uncertainty.
