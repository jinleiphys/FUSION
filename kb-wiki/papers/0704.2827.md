# Systematics of threshold incident energy for deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance

> We systematically evaluate the potential energy at the touching configuration for heavy-ion reactions using various potential models. We point out that the energy at the touching point, especially that estimated with the Krappe-Nix-Sierk (KNS) potential, strongly correlates with the threshold incident energy for steep fall-off of fusion cross sections observed recently for several systems at extremely low energies. This clearly indicates that the steep fall-off phenomenon can be attributed to the dynamics after the target and projectile touch with each other, e.g., the tunneling process and the nuclear saturation property in the overlap region.

## Key claim

The steep fall-off of fusion cross sections at deep sub-barrier energies (fusion hindrance) is strongly correlated with the potential energy at the touching configuration of the colliding nuclei, particularly as calculated with the Krappe-Nix-Sierk (KNS) potential, indicating that the hindrance originates from dynamics after the nuclei touch, such as tunneling and nuclear saturation in the overlap region.

## Method

The authors calculated the potential energy at the touching point (r_touch = R_P + R_T) for 18 heavy-ion systems using four nuclear potential models: Krappe-Nix-Sierk (KNS), Bass, proximity, and Akyüz-Winther (AW). They added the Coulomb potential for two point charges to obtain the total touching energy V_Touch, then compared these values to the experimental threshold incident energy E_s (the energy at which the astrophysical S-factor reaches its maximum) for each system.

## Key numbers

- For 90Zr + 90Zr: E_s = 175 ± 1.8 MeV, V_KNS = 179.9 MeV
- For 58Ni + 58Ni: E_s = 94 ± 0.9 MeV, V_KNS = 93.4 MeV
- For 32S + 89Y: E_s = 72.6 ± 0.7 MeV, V_KNS = 72.2 MeV
- For 64Ni + 64Ni: E_s = 87.3 ± 0.9 MeV, V_KNS = 89.0 MeV
- For 48Ca + 48Ca: E_s = 48.1 ± 0.9 MeV, V_KNS = 42.2 MeV
- For 16O + 16O: E_s = 7.1 ± 0.8 MeV, V_KNS = 2.2 MeV
- For 16O + 208Pb: E_s = 69.6 MeV, V_KNS = 70.5 MeV
- For 40Ca + 90Zr: E_s = 93.2 MeV, V_KNS = 93.6 MeV
- For 50Ti + 208Pb: E_s = 181.2 MeV, V_KNS = 191.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematics of fusion hindrance observed by Jiang et al. (2006) by showing that the threshold energy E_s correlates with the touching energy V_Touch, particularly from the KNS model. The KNS model partly accounts for nuclear matter saturation, unlike the Bass and proximity models which show larger discrepancies. The results provide a constraint for modeling the overlap process after touching, where the sudden and adiabatic approaches (e.g., Balantekin 1998, Hagino and Watanabe 2006) represent opposite limits that remain unresolved.
