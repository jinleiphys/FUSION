# Far-from-equilibrium attractors and nonlinear dynamical systems approach to the Gubser flow

> The non-equilibrium attractors of systems undergoing Gubser flow within relativistic kinetic theory are studied. In doing so we employ well-established methods of nonlinear dynamical systems which rely on finding the fixed points, investigating the structure of the flow diagrams of the evolution equations, and characterizing the basin of attraction using a Lyapunov function near the stable fixed points. We obtain the attractors of anisotropic hydrodynamics, Israel-Stewart (IS) and transient fluid (DNMR) theories and show that they are indeed non-planar and the basin of attraction is essentially three dimensional. The attractors of each hydrodynamical model are compared with the one obtained from the exact Gubser solution of the Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation. We observe that the anisotropic hydrodynamics is able to match up to high numerical accuracy the attractor of the exact solution while the second order hydrodynamical theories fail to describe it. We show that the IS and DNMR asymptotic series expansion diverge and use resurgence techniques to perform the resummation of these divergences. We also comment on a possible link between the manifold of steepest descent paths in path integrals and basin of attraction for the attractors via Lyapunov functions that opens a new horizon toward effective field theory description of hydrodynamics. Our findings indicate that anisotropic hydrodynamics is an effective theory for far-from-equilibrium fluid dynamics which resums the Knudsen and inverse Reynolds numbers to all orders.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that anisotropic hydrodynamics reproduces the far-from-equilibrium attractor of the exact Gubser solution of the Boltzmann equation with high numerical accuracy, while second-order hydrodynamical theories (Israel-Stewart and DNMR) fail to describe it; the attractor is a non-planar one-dimensional manifold embedded in a three-dimensional basin of attraction, unlike the Bjorken flow case.

## Method

The authors study Gubser flow in de Sitter space times a line (dS3 ⊗ R) using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) Boltzmann equation. They apply nonlinear dynamical systems methods, finding fixed points, constructing flow diagrams, and characterizing basins of attraction via Lyapunov functions, to the evolution equations of Israel-Stewart (IS), DNMR, and anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) theories, comparing each attractor to the exact kinetic solution. The IS and DNMR asymptotic series are shown to diverge, and resurgence techniques are used to perform resummation of these divergences.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is set by the conformal relation τ̂_r = c/ T̂(ρ) with c = 5 η/s.
- The anisotropy parameter ξ is constrained to the range ξ ∈ (-1, ∞), corresponding to the physical bounds -1/4 < π̄ < 1/2 for the normalized shear stress.
- The aHydro evolution equation for the normalized shear π̄ contains the term (4/3) tanh ρ (5/16 + π̄ - π̄² - (9/16) F(π̄)), where F(π̄) = R₂₄₀(ξ(π̄))/R₂₀₀(ξ(π̄)).
- The IS and DNMR evolution equations for π̄ include the term (4/3)(η/s T̂) tanh ρ on the right-hand side, with IS having no additional linear term while DNMR adds (10/7) τ̂_π̄ π̄ tanh ρ.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of attractors in Bjorken flow (Heller 2015, 2016; Basar 2015; Aniceto 2015; Strickland 2017; Florkowski 2017) to the more complex Gubser flow geometry, where the attractor is a genuinely three-dimensional dynamical system rather than reducible to one dimension. The results enable a deeper understanding of hydrodynamization in rapidly expanding systems and suggest a connection between the basin of attraction and the manifold of steepest descent paths in path integrals, potentially leading to an effective field theory description of hydrodynamics. The finding that aHydro resums Knudsen and inverse Reynolds numbers to all orders contrasts with the divergent asymptotic series of IS and DNMR theories.
