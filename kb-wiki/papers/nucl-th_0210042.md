# Semi-hard scattering of partons at SPS and RHIC: A study in contrast

> We analyze the contribution of pQCD based semi-hard parton (re)scattering to the reaction dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC. While such processes are able to account for the measured yield of high momentum direct photons at SPS energies, the conditions necessary for jet-quenching are not fulfilled. The situation changes dramatically at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

Semi-hard parton scattering processes can account for the measured yield of high momentum direct photons at SPS energies, but the conditions necessary for jet-quenching are not fulfilled at SPS; the situation changes dramatically at RHIC energies, where multiple scatterings and fragmentations occur in abundance.

## Method

The authors used a parton cascade model (PCM) based on the relativistic Boltzmann equation with perturbative QCD interactions, including processes such as gluon-gluon, quark-gluon, and photon-producing scatterings, as well as parton branchings (e.g., q → qγ). They simulated central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS (158 A GeV) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC (130 A GeV), using a low-momentum transfer cut-off of 0.7 GeV for SPS and 1.0 GeV for RHIC, and compared the resulting photon transverse momentum spectra to WA98 experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Number of hard collisions per event: 255 at SPS, 3618 at RHIC (factor of 14 increase)
- Number of fragmentations per event: 17 at SPS, 2229 at RHIC (more than two orders of magnitude increase)
- Average momentum transfer ⟨Q²⟩: 0.8 GeV² at SPS, 1.7 GeV² at RHIC
- Average center-of-mass energy ⟨√ŝ⟩: 2.6 GeV at SPS, 4.7 GeV at RHIC
- The PCM accounts for the measured direct photon yield for k_T ≥ 2.7 GeV at SPS when intrinsic k_T (with p_0 = 0.44 GeV) is included
- At SPS, fragmentations increase photon production by about 30%; at RHIC, fragmentation processes enhance multiple scatterings by a factor of almost 2.4 (in the absence of LPM suppression)
- Probability for a parton to scatter five times is more than two orders of magnitude larger at RHIC than at SPS

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade model approach of Geiger and Müller (1992) and the authors' earlier studies (Bass, Müller, Srivastava, 2002) on photon production in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts the SPS regime, where the parton medium is dilute and does not form a dense parton plasma, with the RHIC regime where abundant multiple scatterings and fragmentations lead to significant jet quenching. The results enable quantitative understanding of why jet quenching was absent at SPS but observed at RHIC, and support the interpretation that the WA98 photon data at SPS can be explained by semi-hard parton scattering without requiring a dense partonic medium.
