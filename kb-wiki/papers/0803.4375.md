# Electric Mean Squared Radii of Lambda(1405) in Chiral Dynamics

> The electric mean squared radii <r^2>_E of Lambda(1405) are calculated in the chiral unitary model. We describe the Lambda(1405) as a dynamically generated resonance fully in the octet meson and octet baryon scattering. We also consider ``Lambda(1405)'' as a bound state of KbarN. For the later ``Lambda(1405),'' we obtain negative and larger absolute value of electric mean squared radius than that of ordinary baryons, which implies that Lambda(1405) have structure of widely spread K^- around p.

# Electric Mean Squared Radii of Lambda(1405) in Chiral Dynamics

## Key claim

The electric mean squared radius of the Lambda(1405) is negative and has a larger absolute value than that of ordinary baryons, but only when the resonance is treated as a pure KbarN bound state by switching off all other coupled channels; the physical two-pole structure yields complex radii that are not directly interpretable as physical sizes.

## Method

The authors calculate the electric form factor of the Lambda(1405) as the residue of a second-rank pole in the meson-baryon to meson-baryon scattering amplitude with a virtual photon, using the chiral unitary model (ChUM). The scattering amplitude is obtained from an algebraic Bethe-Salpeter equation with an s-wave Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction kernel and meson-baryon loop functions, in coupled channels of strangeness -1 and charge 0. The photon coupling is introduced in a gauge-invariant manner by gauging the kinetic terms and effective interactions of the constituent mesons and baryons.

## Key numbers

* The lower pole of the physical Lambda(1405) lies at z1 = 1390 - 66i MeV, couples strongly to pi Sigma, and has electric mean squared radius (1.8 - 0.2i) x 10^{-2} fm^2.
* The higher pole of the physical Lambda(1405) lies at z2 = 1426 - 17i MeV, couples strongly to Kbar N, and has electric mean squared radius -0.131 + 0.303i fm^2.
* The pure KbarN bound state "Lambda(1405)" sits at 1429 MeV and has electric mean squared radius -2.193 fm^2.
* The root of the absolute value of the bound-state mean squared radius is sqrt(|<r^2>_E|) approximately 1.48 fm.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary model description of the Lambda(1405) as a dynamically generated resonance in meson-baryon scattering, previously developed by Kaiser, Oset, Oller, Lutz, and Jido (various years). The two-pole structure (z1 near 1390 MeV and z2 near 1426 MeV) had been established in earlier coupled-channel calculations. The present calculation enables future experimental searches for the Lambda(1405) via electromagnetic production, where a large spatial extent would cause a rapid falloff of the form factor and strong energy dependence in production cross sections. The method follows the gauge-invariant form factor approach of Borasoy (various years) and Jido (various years) for excited baryons.
