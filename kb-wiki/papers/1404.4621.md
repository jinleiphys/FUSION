# Dirac oscillators and the relativistic R matrix

> The Dirac oscillators are shown to be an excellent expansion basis for solutions of the Dirac equation by $R$-matrix techniques. The combination of the Dirac oscillator and the $R$-matrix approach provides a convenient formalism for reactions as well as bound state problems. The utility of the $R$-matrix approach is demonstrated in relativistic impulse approximation calculations where exchange terms can be calculated exactly, and scattering waves made orthogonal to bound state wave functions.

# Dirac oscillators and the relativistic R matrix

## Key claim
Dirac oscillators provide a stable expansion basis for solving the Dirac equation via R-matrix techniques, enabling exact calculation of exchange terms in relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) calculations and allowing scattering waves to be made orthogonal to bound state wave functions from relativistic mean field theory (RMFT). The improvement from exact exchange over the approximate Brieva-Rook method is significant primarily in matrix elements between negative energy states, even for t-matrices designed to minimize exchange-direct cancellation.

## Method
The authors constructed an R-matrix formalism for the Dirac equation using Dirac oscillator wave functions as the expansion basis within a channel radius. They applied this formalism to RIA calculations for proton-nucleus elastic scattering using the relativistic Love-Franey (RLF) model of Horowitz (1985), computing the exchange term exactly via Eq. (25) rather than using the nuclear matter approximation for the exchange density. The scattering amplitudes were obtained by matching the R-matrix to Dirac Coulomb functions at the channel radius, and the collision matrix was extracted for each partial wave.

## Key numbers
- Calculations performed for protons on 16O, 40Ca, and 90Zr at energies corresponding to the RLF model parameters
- For 40Ca at 181 MeV with a phenomenological potential, the real part of the κ = -1 phase shift was accurate to three figures over a range of about 2 fm in channel radius
- Maximum oscillator quantum number N set to 16 and 18 for phase shift stability tests
- For positive energy states, approximate exchange matrix elements were on average about 1.7 times larger than exact exchange matrix elements
- At Ep = 9.0 MeV for p + 40Ca, projecting out RMFT bound states from the scattering wave function produced a visible effect on the cross section; this effect decreased with increasing energy and was not significant by 50 MeV

## Context
This work extends the R-matrix approach previously developed by Halderson (1988) for the Dirac equation, which had used free-particle Dirac solutions as an expansion basis that lacked the stability seen in nonrelativistic calculations by Philpott (1975). The Dirac oscillators, introduced by Ito et al. (1967) and revived by Moshinsky and Szczepaniak (1989), provide that stability. The exact exchange calculation improves upon the Brieva-Rook (1977) approximation used in prior RIA calculations by Murdock and Horowitz (1987) and Horowitz et al. (1991). The formalism enables future coupled-channel reaction calculations where binary breakup channels satisfy relative Dirac equations, and bound state problems in RMFT.
