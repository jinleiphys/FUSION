# Bayesian approach for many-body uncertainties in nuclear structure: Many-body perturbation theory for finite nuclei

> A comprehensive assessment of theoretical uncertainties defines an important frontier in nuclear structure research. Ideally, theory predictions include uncertainty estimates that take into account truncation effects from both the interactions and the many-body expansion. While the uncertainties from the expansion of the interactions within effective field theories have been studied systematically using Bayesian methods, many-body truncations are usually addressed by expert assessment. In this work we use a Bayesian framework to study many-body uncertainties within many-body perturbation theory applied to finite nuclei. Our framework is applied to a broad range of nuclei across the nuclear chart calculated from two- and three-nucleon interactions based on chiral effective field theory. These developments represent a step towards a more complete and systematic quantification of uncertainties in \emph{ab initio} calculations of nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors develop a Bayesian framework to quantify many-body truncation uncertainties in many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) for finite nuclei, but the error model assumes that MBPT expansion coefficients are normally distributed and treats data across different nuclei as uncorrelated, which the authors acknowledge as a limitation that artificially narrows uncertainty bands when many nuclei are included.

## Method

The authors compute Hartree-Fock (HF) reference energies and second- and third-order MBPT corrections for closed-shell nuclei across the nuclear chart (A=14-208) using three different chiral interactions. They infer the hyperparameters R (convergence rate) and γ̄² (variance of expansion coefficients) from MBPT ratios using Bayesian inference with an inverse-gamma prior on γ̄² and a uniform prior on R ∈ (0,2), then sample the joint posterior via Markov-chain Monte Carlo to construct predictive distributions for ground-state energies.

## Key numbers

- MBPT(2) uncertainty bands reach up to 1.0 MeV per nucleon at the 90% credibility level for the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction
- MBPT(3) uncertainty reduces to below 0.2 MeV per nucleon at the 90% credibility level for heavier systems with the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction
- The ratio E^(2)/E_HF ≈ 0.4 and E^(3)/E^(2) ≈ 0.1 for the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction across the studied nuclei
- The posterior distribution of R peaks at R ≈ 0.15 with negligible weight for R > 0.4 for the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction
- The 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction yields approximately half the uncertainty width compared to the ΔN²LO_GO and 1.8/2.0 (EM7.5) interactions
- The 1.8/2.0 (EM7.5) interaction produces 0.2 MeV uncertainty for MBPT(3) already at the 68% credibility level

## Context

This work extends Bayesian uncertainty quantification methods previously applied to chiral effective field theory interaction truncation errors (Furnstahl et al., 2015; Melendez et al., 2019; Wesolowski et al., 2021) to the many-body expansion itself, which had previously relied on expert assessment. The framework is benchmarked against nonperturbative IMSRG(2) calculations from Arthuis (2024) and enables systematic uncertainty quantification for ab initio nuclear structure calculations that accounts for both interaction and many-body truncation effects. The authors note that including pure neutron matter results significantly changes the uncertainty bands, indicating the error model cannot simultaneously capture MBPT convergence for nuclei and neutron matter.
