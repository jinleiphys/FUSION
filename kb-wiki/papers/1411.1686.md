# Inhomogeneous phases and chiral symmetry breaking

> In the past few years, a growing number of compelling arguments, backed up by model calculations, pointed out that the intermediate-density region of the QCD phase diagram may be characterized by the formation of inhomogeneous condensates which spontaneously break some of the spatial symmetries of the theory. In the following we provide a brief recapitulation of these arguments and describe some recent results in a 3+1-dimensional QCD-inspired NJL model with quark-hole condensation in the form of a plane wave in the scalar and tensor channels. This model exhibits particular features in close analogy to its 1+1-dimensional counterpart, most notably an asymmetric spectral density and the arising of an anomalous contribution to the free energy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a 3+1-dimensional QCD-inspired Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model extended with a tensor channel, a plane-wave inhomogeneous condensate (chiral spiral) produces an asymmetric single-particle energy spectrum, which generates an anomalous contribution to the free energy that favors inhomogeneous condensation at any nonzero chemical potential, provided the scalar and tensor couplings are equal.

## Method

The authors construct an extended NJL model with scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor interaction channels, setting the scalar and tensor couplings equal (\(G'=G\)) to match the Fierz-transformed confining gluon propagator of quarkyonic matter. They adopt a mean-field ansatz for a spatially modulated chiral condensate varying only along the \(z\)-direction, with both scalar and tensor components forming a plane wave \(M(z)=\Delta e^{iqz}\). The eigenvalue problem for the 3+1-dimensional quark Hamiltonian is reduced to a 1+1-dimensional Bogoliubov–de Gennes problem, whose solutions are then used to compute the full spectrum and the anomalous contribution to the free energy via the \(\eta\)-invariant method of Niemi and Semenoff.

## Key numbers

* The anomalous contribution to the free energy is \(\delta\Omega_{\text{anom}} = \frac{1}{2} \mu \, \eta_H\), with \(\eta_H = -\frac{N_f N_c}{12 \pi^2} \left(2 q^3 - 3 \Delta^2 q \right)\) for \(|q| < \Delta\).
* The 3+1-dimensional single-particle energy spectrum is \(E(\Delta, q) = -\frac{q}{2} \pm \sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + \Delta^2}\), where each mode appears twice, showing an asymmetry of \(q/2\) relative to zero.
* The 1+1-dimensional eigenvalues used as input are \(\epsilon(\Delta, q) = \frac{q}{2} \pm \sqrt{p_3^2 + \Delta^2}\).

## Context

This work builds a bridge between quarkyonic matter arguments (Deryagin, Grigoriev, and Rubakov, 1992; Kojo et al., 2010) and quantitative model studies of inhomogeneous phases in QCD. It extends prior NJL-model investigations of chiral density waves (Nickel, 2009; Tatsumi and Karasawa, 2010) by including a tensor channel with coupling strength equal to the scalar channel, motivated by the Fierz transform of the confining gluon propagator. The results are consistent with the quarkyonic chiral spiral predictions and enable future calculations of the full phase diagram, as well as studies of the effects of external magnetic fields on the inhomogeneous condensate.
