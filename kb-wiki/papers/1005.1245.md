# A path integral for heavy-quarks in a hot plasma

> We propose a model for the propagation of a heavy-quark in a hot plasma, to be viewed as a first step towards a full description of the dynamics of heavy quark systems in a quark-gluon plasma, including bound state formation. The heavy quark is treated as a non relativistic particle interacting with a fluctuating field, whose correlator is determined by a hard thermal loop approximation. This approximation, which concerns only the medium in which the heavy quark propagates, is the only one that is made, and it can be improved. The dynamics of the heavy quark is given exactly by a quantum mechanical path integral that is calculated in this paper in the Euclidean space-time using numerical Monte Carlo techniques. The spectral function of the heavy quark in the medium is then reconstructed using a Maximum Entropy Method. The path integral is also evaluated exactly in the case where the mass of the heavy quark is infinite; one then recovers known results concerning the complex optical potential that controls the long time behavior of the heavy quark. The heavy quark correlator and its spectral function is also calculated semi-analytically at the one-loop order, which allows for a detailed description of the coupling between the heavy quark and the plasma collective modes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a model for heavy-quark propagation in a hot plasma as a first step toward describing heavy-quark bound-state dynamics, with the caveat that the medium is approximated by a Gaussian fluctuating field using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, which is the only approximation made and can be improved without altering the basic path-integral structure.

## Method

The heavy quark is treated as a non-relativistic particle coupled to a fluctuating gauge field, with the medium modeled as an Abelian (Coulomb) plasma of light fermions treated within the HTL approximation. The dynamics of the heavy quark is encoded exactly in a quantum mechanical path integral, which is evaluated numerically in Euclidean space-time using Monte Carlo techniques, with the spectral function reconstructed via the Maximum Entropy Method. The path integral is also evaluated exactly in the infinite-mass limit and semi-analytically at one-loop order to describe the coupling between the heavy quark and plasma collective modes.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass \(m_D = 713\) MeV for \(T = 300\) MeV, using \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(N_c = 3\), \(N_f = 2\) (QCD HTL expression)
- Effective coupling \(\alpha = g^2/4\pi = 0.4\) used in numerical results, corresponding to \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) in QCD with Casimir factor \(C_F = 4/3\)
- Optical potential for infinite-mass limit: \(V_{\rm opt} = -\frac{\alpha}{2} m_D - i\frac{\alpha T}{2}\), with real part \(-\alpha m_D/2\) and imaginary part \(-\alpha T/2\)
- Free energy shift from Euclidean correlator at \(\tau = \beta\): \(-T \ln G^>(t=-i\beta,\mathbf{p}) = M - \frac{\alpha}{2} m_D\)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Beraudo, Blaizot, Faccioli, and Garberoglio (2008) and Blaizot, Iancu, and Rebhan (2004) by constructing a path-integral description of heavy-quark dynamics beyond the simple Schrödinger equation with a complex potential, which is valid only for infinitely massive quarks and at large times. The model addresses limitations of prior lattice QCD studies (e.g., Aarts et al., 2007; Asakawa and Hatsuda, 2004) and potential-model comparisons (e.g., Mocsy and Petreczky, 2007; Rapp and van Hees, 2008) by providing a framework that can handle mixed time scales relevant for heavy-quark bound states in heavy-ion collisions. The approach enables future improvements by replacing the HTL approximation with more accurate descriptions of the medium while keeping the path-integral structure intact.
