# Baryon junction effects on charmonium production in pp and pPb collisions

> In this work we study the effects of the proton and $Pb$ internal structure on charmonium production at high multiplicities. To do this, we assume that the nucleon has an Y shape, which means that the effective quarks (quarks + antiquarks + gluons) are in the extremities of the Y, connected by gluon lines. Since the quarks are in the periphery and the gluons are in the center, as $pp$ collisions go from peripheral to more central and then to ultra-central collisions, the interactions change from quark-quark to gluon-gluon. The enhanced growth of $J/ψ$ occurs because $σ( g + g \to c + \bar{c}) \gg σ( q + \bar{q} \to c + \bar{c})$. In the $p-Pb$ collisions the enhancement is caused by the high concentration of partons in the center of the nuclei. These effects can explain the growth seen in the data and give support to the Y picture of the proton.

## Key claim

The enhanced growth of $J/\psi$ relative yields in high-multiplicity $pp$ and $pPb$ collisions can be explained by the "Y picture" (baryon junction) of the proton, where gluons are concentrated in a central core and quarks form a peripheral corona, provided that the isotropic gluon distribution represents the average configuration of the Y shape over different orientations.

## Method

The authors adapted the Glauber model, originally developed for nucleus-nucleus collisions, to $pp$ and $pPb$ collisions by using a "core-corona" representation of the proton: an isotropic gluon distribution for the central core and a quark distribution for the periphery. They combined the Glauber model outputs (number of participants $N_{part}$ and number of binary collisions $N_{coll}$ as functions of impact parameter) with the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) for charm quark production, using the fact that $\sigma(gg \rightarrow c\bar{c}) \gg \sigma(q\bar{q} \rightarrow c\bar{c})$ to compute $J/\psi$ yields at different centralities.

## Key numbers

- For $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV and $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV, the $J/\psi$ relative yields per relative charged multiplicity at midrapidity show an anomalous enhancement in the high-multiplicity (ultra-central) region, growing faster than the linear trend seen in peripheral collisions.
- For $PbPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=2.76$ TeV, the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used is $\sigma_{inel}^{NN}=64$ mb; at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=5.02$ TeV, $\sigma_{inel}^{NN}=70$ mb.
- The proton charge radius from CODATA-2010 is $r_p=0.8775(51)$ fm; the CREMA collaboration found $r_p=0.84184(67)$ fm and $r_p=0.84087(39)$ fm via muonic hydrogen spectroscopy.
- The mass radius of the proton from holographic QCD is $\sqrt{\langle r_m^2 \rangle} = 0.755 \pm 0.035$ fm, and from the GPD approach is $0.472 \pm 0.042$ fm; the scalar radius is $1.069 \pm 0.056$ fm and $0.695 \pm 0.071$ fm respectively.

## Context

This work extends the baryon junction model previously applied by Deb et al. (2020) to explain elliptic flow ($v_2$) in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV, and the author's own prior work explaining enhanced $J/\psi$ yields in ultra-central $pp$ collisions. It builds on the Y-shaped proton picture proposed by Kharzeev (1996) and tested in lattice QCD simulations, and uses the Color Evaporation Model as developed by Vogt (various years). The results support the "core-corona" picture of the proton and suggest that the baryon junction structure can explain charmonium production data from the ALICE collaboration at the LHC, potentially enabling further tests of the proton's internal gluon distribution at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
