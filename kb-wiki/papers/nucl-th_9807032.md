# Sum rule for the backward spin polarizability of the nucleon from a backward dispersion relation

> A new sum rule for $γ_π$, the backward spin polarizability of the nucleon, is derived from a backward-angle dispersion relation. Taking into account single- and multi-pion photoproduction in the s-channel up to the energy 1.5 GeV and resonances in the t-channel with mass below 1.5 GeV, it is found for the proton and neutron that $[γ_π]_p$ = -39.5 +/- 2.4 and $[γ_π]_n$ = 52.5 +/- 2.4, respectively, in units of 10^{-4} fm^4.

# Sum rule for the backward spin polarizability of the nucleon from a backward dispersion relation

## Key claim

The authors derive a new, well-convergent sum rule for the backward spin polarizability γ_π of the nucleon using a backward-angle dispersion relation, and find values of [γ_π]_p = -39.5 ± 2.4 and [γ_π]_n = 52.5 ± 2.4 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4). These results disagree with the experimental estimate of [γ_π]_p = -27.1 ± 3.4 from Tonnison et al. (1998), suggesting that the experimental finding should be checked in further dedicated experiments with polarized particles.

## Method

The authors apply a fixed-angle dispersion relation at θ = π to the invariant amplitude A = A_2 + (1 - t/4m^2)A_5, which determines the spin-dependent part of Compton scattering in backward kinematics. The s-channel contribution is expressed as an integral of spin-dependent photoabsorption cross sections weighted by a parity factor and kinematic factors, evaluated using single-pion photoproduction multipoles from the SAID SP97K solution and the HDT set up to ω_max = 1.5 GeV, plus a model for multi-pion production. The t-channel contribution is computed from pseudoscalar meson exchanges (π^0, η, η') using experimental and constituent quark model couplings, with monopole form factors to account for radial excitations.

## Key numbers

- [γ_π]_p = -39.5 ± 2.4 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4)
- [γ_π]_n = 52.5 ± 2.4 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4)
- π^0 exchange contribution: γ_π^{(π^0)} = (-45.0 ± 1.6) τ_3 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4), where τ_3 = 1 for proton and -1 for neutron
- Non-π^0 part for proton: [γ_π^{(non-π^0)}]_p = 5.5 ± 1.8 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4)
- Non-π^0 part for neutron: [γ_π^{(non-π^0)}]_n = 7.5 ± 1.8 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4)
- Experimental estimate from Tonnison et al. (1998): [γ_π]_p = -27.1 ± 3.4 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4)
- s-channel single-pion contribution (SAID, 150-500 MeV): proton 7.29, neutron 9.22 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4)
- s-channel single-pion contribution (SAID, 500-1500 MeV): proton 0.02, neutron 0.13 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4)
- η exchange contribution: -1.00 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4) for both proton and neutron
- η' exchange contribution: -0.57 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4) for both proton and neutron

## Context

This work extends previous dispersion relation approaches by Drechsel et al. (1998) and Babusci et al. (1998), which used unsubtracted fixed-t dispersion relations for the amplitude A_2 with the strong assumption that high-energy asymptotics are determined entirely by π^0 exchange. The new backward-angle dispersion relation is manifestly free from convergence problems that plagued those earlier estimates. The results are numerically close to leading-order chiral perturbation theory calculations with the Δ-isobar included by Hemmert et al. (1998), but disagree with the experimental extraction from Tonnison et al. (1998), which suggested a much larger non-π^0 contribution. The sum rule enables future experimental checks using polarized Compton scattering measurements.
