# $X$ structures in $B^+\to J/ψϕK^+$ as one-loop and double-triangle threshold cusps

> The LHCb data on $B^+\to J/ψϕK^+$ show four peaks and three dips in the $J/ψϕ$ invariant mass distribution, and the peaks are interpreted as $X(4140)$, $X(4274)$, $X(4500)$ and $X(4685)/X(4700)$ resonance contributions. Interestingly, all the peaks and dips are located at (or close to) $D^*_{s}\bar{D}^{(*)}_{s}$, $D_{s0}^*(2317)\bar{D}^{(*)}_{s}$, $D_{s1}(2536)\bar{D}^{(*)}_{s}$, and $ψ'ϕ$ thresholds. These coincidences suggest a close connection between the structures and the thresholds, which however has not been seriously considered in previous theoretical studies on the $X$ structures. In fact, if we utilize this connection and interpret the $X$ structures as common $s$-wave threshold cusps, we face a difficulty: $X(4274)$ and $X(4500)$ have spin-parity that conflict with the experimentally determined ones. In this work, we introduce double triangle mechanisms that cause threshold cusps significantly sharper than the ordinary one-loop ones of the same spin-parity. We demonstrate that all the $X$ and dip structures are well described by a combination of one-loop and double-triangle threshold cusps, thereby proposing a novel interpretation of the $X$ and dip structures.

## Key claim

The four peaks and three dips in the LHCb $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$ data, previously interpreted as resonances $X(4140)$, $X(4274)$, $X(4500)$, and $X(4685)/X(4700)$, can be described entirely by threshold cusps from one-loop and double-triangle mechanisms, provided that the double-triangle diagrams produce cusps sharp enough to match the observed structures without requiring the spin-parity assignments that would conflict with experiment for $X(4274)$ and $X(4500)$.

## Method

The author constructs a decay model for $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$ including double-triangle diagrams (with $p$-wave $D_{sJ}^{(*)}\bar{D}_s^{(*)}$ pairs such as $D_{s0}^*(2317)\bar{D}_s$, $D_{s1}(2536)\bar{D}_s$, and their excited partners) and one-loop diagrams (with $D_s^*\bar{D}_s$, $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$, and $\psi'\phi$ pairs). The amplitudes are derived using effective Lagrangians and time-ordered perturbation theory, with dipole form factors (cutoff $\Lambda=1$ GeV) and complex coupling constants fitted to the $M_{J/\psi\phi}$ distribution from LHCb. The double-triangle mechanisms produce threshold cusps sharper than ordinary one-loop cusps because they are close to causing the leading kinematical singularity.

## Key numbers

* $X(4140)$ structure: located at the $D_s^*\bar{D}_s$ threshold (approximately 4.14 GeV); described by a one-loop $D_s^*\bar{D}_s(1^+)$ threshold cusp.
* $X(4274)$ structure: located at the $D_{s0}^*(2317)\bar{D}_s$ threshold (approximately 4.27 GeV); formed by a double-triangle $D_{s0}^*\bar{D}_s(1^+)$ cusp interfering with $Z_{cs}(4000)$.
* $X(4500)$ structure: located at the $D_{s1}(2536)\bar{D}_s$ threshold (approximately 4.50 GeV); developed by a double-triangle $D_{s1}\bar{D}_s(0^+)$ cusp.
* $X(4685)/X(4700)$ structure: located at the $\psi'\phi$ threshold (approximately 4.70 GeV); described by a one-loop $\psi'\phi(1^+)$ threshold cusp.
* $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ width: taken as $\Gamma_{D_{s0}^*}=0.1$ MeV (upper limit from experiment is $<3.8$ MeV); results are stable for $\Gamma_{D_{s0}^*}<1$ MeV.
* Scattering length for $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^{(*)}$ interactions: fixed to $a\sim 0.55$ fm (moderately attractive), with a virtual pole at $\sim 20$ MeV below threshold.
* Cutoff values: common $\Lambda=0.8-1.5$ GeV used for form factors; fit quality stable across this range.

## Context

This work extends the threshold-cusp interpretation previously applied to $X(4140)$ by LHCb (2017) and by Liu (2020) for $X(4140)$ and $X(4700)$, and by Dong et al. (2021) for $X(4140)$ as a virtual-state cusp. It addresses the difficulty that $X(4274)$ and $X(4500)$ as $s$-wave threshold cusps would have spin-parity conflicting with LHCb's experimental determination ($1^+$ and $0^+$, respectively). The double-triangle mechanism, first demonstrated in earlier work by the same author (Nakamura, 2021), produces cusps sharp enough to match the data while respecting the LHCb spin-parity assignments. The analysis enables a kinematical interpretation of all $X$ structures without invoking genuine resonances, though the author notes that quark-model and hadron-molecule interpretations are not excluded and require further experimental and lattice QCD input.
