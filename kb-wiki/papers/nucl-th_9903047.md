# Initial Conditions for Parton Cascades

## Key claim
The parton cascade model provides a useful description of the evolution of matter from a formation time until thermalization, but the initial conditions must be derived from a consistent quantum mechanical picture such as the McLerran-Venugopalan model, because at sufficiently early times the cascade description fails due to quantum mechanical and color coherence effects.

## Method
The author reviews the Bjorken space-time picture of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, then discusses the parton cascade model of Kinder-Geiger and Muller, which assumes the momentum-space distribution of partons just after formation is given by parton distribution functions. The author then describes the McLerran-Venugopalan model, which treats the early-stage gluon fields as non-abelian Lienard-Wiechert potentials generated by a Gaussian distribution of color sources, and notes that the Yang-Mills equations can be solved numerically from these initial conditions to compute the phase space density of produced gluons.

## Key numbers
- Initial thermalization time: \(\tau_0 \le 1\) Fm/c
- Decoupling temperature: \(T_{decoupling} \ge 100\) MeV, where pions are still approximately massless with \((m_{pion}/E)^2 \sim 0.1\)
- Initial temperatures at RHIC: \(T \ge 200\) MeV, sufficient to produce a quark-gluon plasma
- The single scale in the classical problem: \(\Lambda^2 = (1/\pi R^2) (dN/dy)\), which is the rapidity density of gluons per unit area

## Context
This talk extends the parton cascade model developed by Kinder-Geiger and Muller and the Bjorken (1970) space-time picture of ultrarelativistic collisions. The author presents the McLerran-Venugopalan model as a first-principles QCD framework for computing initial conditions, which provides an a posteriori justification for the ad hoc cutoffs used in parton cascade computations. The work enables future numerical solutions of the classical Yang-Mills equations for nucleus-nucleus collisions, which can then initialize parton cascade simulations at RHIC energies.
