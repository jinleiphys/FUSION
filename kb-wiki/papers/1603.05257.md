# Impact of the first-forbidden $β$ decay on the production of $A \sim 195$ r-process peak

> We investigated the effects of first-forbidden transitions in $β$ decays on the production of the r-process $A \sim 195$ peak. The theoretical calculated $β$-decay rates with $β$-delayed neutron emission were examined using several astrophysical conditions. As the first-borbidden decay is dominant in $N \sim 126$ neutron-rich nuclei, their inclusion shortens $β$-decay lifetimes and shifts the abundance peak towards higher masses. Additionally, the inclusion of the $β$-delayed neutron emission results in a wider abundance peak, and smoothens the mass distribution by removing the odd-even mass staggering. The effects are commonly seen in the results of all adopted astrophysical models. Nevertheless there are quantitative differences, indicating that remaining uncertainty in the determination of half-lives for $N=126$ nuclei is still significant in order to determine the production of the r-process peak.

## Key claim

Including first-forbidden β-decay transitions for N ~ 126 neutron-rich nuclei shortens β-decay lifetimes and shifts the A ~ 195 r-process abundance peak towards higher masses, while β-delayed neutron emission widens the peak and removes odd-even mass staggering; however, quantitative differences among astrophysical models remain, indicating that uncertainty in half-lives for N=126 nuclei is still significant for determining peak production.

## Method

The authors performed r-process nucleosynthesis calculations using a nuclear reaction network code with over 4000 isotopes, adopting β-decay rates from the spherical quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) method with realistic forces (FBS13) for N=124–128 nuclei. They compared five different networks: MNK97 (allowed transitions only, no neutron emission), MPK03 (gross theory including first-forbidden transitions and neutron emission), FBS13 (QRPA-based rates), FBS(w/o FF) (FBS13 without first-forbidden contributions), and FBS(w/o n.e.) (FBS13 without β-delayed neutron emission). These networks were applied to three astrophysical models: a magneto-rotational supernova (MR-SN), a proto-neutron-star wind (PNSW), and a neutron star merger (NS–NS merger).

## Key numbers

- First-forbidden transitions contribute 20–60% of the total β-decay rate for N=126 nuclei.
- The FBS13 half-lives for N=126 nuclei agree with shell model predictions within a factor of two.
- β-delayed neutron emission probabilities for r-process path even-even nuclei are P_n ~ 0.1–1.
- The MR-SN model uses electron fraction Y_e ~ 0.1–0.3 and entropy ~ 10 k_B per baryon.
- The PNSW model uses Y_e = 0.3 and entropy S ~ 165 k_B per baryon.
- The NS–NS merger model uses Y_e = 0.14.
- For N=82 nuclei, the first-forbidden contribution is about 10% of the total β-decay rate.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of β-decay in r-process nucleosynthesis, specifically addressing the N=126 waiting point region where experimental data are unavailable. Prior calculations by Moller et al. (1997, 2003) using the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) included only allowed transitions or used gross theory for first-forbidden contributions, while more recent QRPA calculations by Borzov (2006), Fang et al. (2013), and shell model approaches by Suzuki et al. (2012) and Zhi et al. (2013) found significant first-forbidden contributions of 20–80% for N=126 nuclei. The results enable improved predictions for the A ~ 195 r-process peak and highlight the need for experimental measurements of N=126 nuclei, which are being pursued through deep-inelastic and fragmentation reactions.
