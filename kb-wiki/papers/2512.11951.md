# Directional dead-cone effect in QCD matter

> We consider the propagation of heavy quarks through a dense, hydrodynamically flowing QCD medium, representative of the quark-gluon plasma formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. Working in the high-energy limit, we identify two novel mass-dependent effects arising from the heavy quark coupling to the local medium flow. The first is the emergence of a tensorial jet transport coefficient, $\hat{q}_{ij}$, which encodes the directional structure of transverse-momentum broadening. The second, named the directional dead-cone effect, corresponds to an anisotropic suppression of medium-induced radiation aligned with the hydrodynamic flow. We discuss how these effects manifest in jet observables and identify distinctive signature of heavy quark dynamics in an evolving medium.

## Key claim

The paper identifies two novel mass-dependent effects for heavy quarks propagating through a hydrodynamically flowing QCD medium: a tensorial jet transport coefficient and a directional dead-cone effect, where the suppression of medium-induced radiation is anisotropic and depends on the relative orientation of the gluon momentum and the medium flow direction.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma as a stochastic color field generated by flowing quasi-particles with a light-like flow field \(u^\mu\), and consider heavy quark propagation in the high-energy limit with leading-power mass effects. They compute the resummed amplitude for heavy quark evolution in the medium using path-ordered exponentials (Wilson lines), and derive the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum in the soft gluon limit, working in axial gauge with the harmonic oscillator approximation for the scattering potential.

## Key numbers

- Heavy quark mass fixed at \(m_Q = 4\) GeV for numerical evaluations of the spectrum
- Jet quenching parameter fixed at \(\hat{q}_0 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\cdot\text{fm}^{-1}\) for numerical evaluations
- Transverse flow magnitude fixed at \(|\u| = 0.7\) for numerical evaluations
- Three gluon energies considered: \(\omega = 0.06\,\omega_c\), \(\omega = 0.12\,\omega_c\), and \(\omega = 0.20\,\omega_c\), where \(\omega_c \equiv \frac{1}{2}\hat{q}_0 L^2\) is the critical medium energy
- The directional dead-cone angle is given by \(\Theta_{\rm dc}^2 (\k\cdot \u) = \theta_{\rm dc}^2 \left(1-\frac{\k\cdot \u}{u^-\omega}\right)\), where \(\theta_{\rm dc} \equiv m_Q/E\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet-flow interactions (Sadofyev 2021, Andres 2022, Kuzmin 2023, 2024) from massless quarks to heavy quarks, and generalizes the dead-cone effect first described by Dokshitzer (1991) for vacuum radiation. The results enable future phenomenological studies of anisotropic jet substructure and hard parton flow coefficients (\(v_n\)) in heavy-ion collisions, and could be probed using jet-tree based observables (Cunqueiro 2022) augmented with azimuthal variables.
