# On the splitting of nucleon effective masses at high isospin density: reaction observables

> We review the present status of the nucleon effective mass splitting $puzzle$ in asymmetric matter, with controversial predictions within both non-relativistic $and$ relativistic approaches to the effective in medium interactions. Based on microscopic transport simulations we suggest some rather sensitive observables in collisions of asymmetric (unstable) ions at intermediate ($RIA$) energies: i) Energy systematics of Lane Potentials; ii) Isospin content of fast emitted nucleons; iii) Differential Collective Flows. Similar measurements for light isobars (like $^3H-^3He$) could be also important.

Based on the abstract and the available full text (which is truncated but contains the paper's core theoretical development and key table), here is the wiki page body.

## Key claim
The sign of the neutron/proton effective mass splitting in asymmetric nuclear matter is a "puzzle" with controversial predictions, but it can be resolved by measuring the energy slope of the Lane Potential and other reaction observables in collisions of asymmetric ions at intermediate energies; the sign of the splitting is directly linked to the sign of the $g_2$ parameter in Skyrme forces, which differs between older forces (e.g., SIII, SGII) and newer Lyon parametrizations (e.g., SLy4).

## Method
The authors perform a theoretical analysis of the isospin-dependent momentum dependence in Skyrme effective interactions, deriving the relation between the $g_2$ parameter and the neutron/proton effective mass splitting. They then compute the energy dependence of the Lane Potential from the Skyrme mean field and compare predictions across six Skyrme forces (SIII, SGII, SkM*, SLya, SLy4, SLy7). The analysis is extended to a relativistic Quantum-Hadro-Dynamics (QHD) model including the isovector-scalar $\delta$ meson, showing its effect on the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers
- For the Skyrme force SLy4, the $g_2$ parameter is $+2.53 \times 10^{-3} \text{ MeV}^{-1} \text{fm}^3$, leading to a positive energy slope of the Lane Potential of $+0.19$.
- For the Skyrme force SIII, the $g_2$ parameter is $-3.14 \times 10^{-3} \text{ MeV}^{-1} \text{fm}^3$, leading to a negative energy slope of the Lane Potential of $-0.22$.
- The saturation density $\rho_0$ for SLy4 is $0.1595 \text{ fm}^{-3}$, with a symmetry energy $a_4$ of $32.01 \text{ MeV}$.
- The asymmetry parameter used for the effective mass splitting plots is fixed at $I=0.2$.

## Context
This work reviews the conflicting predictions for the nucleon effective mass splitting between non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (e.g., Bombiso, Zuo (2000)) and relativistic Dirac-Brueckner calculations (e.g., Hofmann (2001), Schiller (2001), van Dalen (2004)). It extends the analysis of Skyrme forces by comparing older parametrizations (SIII, SGII, SkM* from Krivine (1980)) with the Lyon parametrizations (SLya, SLy4, SLy7 from LyonNPA627, LyonNPA635, LyonNPA665), which were motivated to cure the collapse of polarized neutron matter. The paper proposes that future experiments with radioactive beams at intermediate energies (RIA) can resolve the puzzle by measuring the energy systematics of Lane Potentials, the isospin content of fast emitted nucleons, and differential collective flows.
