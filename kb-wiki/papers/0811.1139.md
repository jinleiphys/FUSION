# Global nuclear structure aspects of tensor interaction

> A direct fit of the isoscalar spin-orbit and both isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constants to the f5/2-f7/2 SO splittings in 40Ca, 56Ni, and 48Ca requires: (i) a significant reduction of the standard isoscalar spin-orbit strength and (ii) strong attractive tensor coupling constants. The aim of this paper is to address the consequences of these strong attractive tensor and weak spin-orbit fields on total binding energies, two-neutron separation energies and nuclear deformability.

## Key claim

A direct fit of the isoscalar spin-orbit and both isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constants to the f5/2-f7/2 spin-orbit splittings in 40Ca, 56Ni, and 48Ca requires a significant reduction of the standard isoscalar spin-orbit strength and strong attractive tensor coupling constants; however, this fit leads to deteriorated nuclear binding energies, and a functional that simultaneously reproduces these splittings and binding energies must have a large effective mass (m* greater than or equal to 0.9).

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using Skyrme energy density functionals, fitting the isoscalar spin-orbit coupling constant (C0^nabla J) in 40Ca, the isoscalar tensor coupling constant (C0^J) in 56Ni, and the ratio of isovector tensor to spin-orbit coupling constants (C1^J / C1^nabla J) in 48Ca to the measured 1f7/2-1f5/2 spin-orbit splittings. They then applied the resulting functionals (SLy4_T, SkO_TX, SkO_T') to compute total binding energies, two-neutron separation energies, and potential energy surfaces as functions of quadrupole deformation.

## Key numbers

* The tensor contribution to binding energy shows tensorial magic numbers at N(Z)=14, 32, 56, or 90, corresponding to maximum spin-asymmetry in the 1d5/2, 1f7/2+2p3/2, 1g9/2+2d5/2, and 1h11/2+2f7/2 configurations.
* The SkO_T' functional achieves binding energy accuracy for the classical set of double-magic nuclei comparable to the standard SLy4 force, outperforming both SLy4_T and SLy4_Tmin.
* In oxygen isotopes, the tensor interaction induces a decrease in the two-neutron separation energy S2n around 26O, directly related to d3/2 sub-shell occupation reducing spin-asymmetry.
* For 80Zr, adding a strong attractive tensor field (SkO_TX) pulls the deformed minimum down in energy, while the subsequent reduction of spin-orbit strength (SkO_T') shifts the deformed minimum back up to its original position obtained with the SkO functional.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior fitting strategy (Zalewski 2008) that directly fit spin-orbit and tensor coupling constants to single-particle data, contradicting conventional fitting methods that use bulk nuclear matter data and binding energies of double-magic nuclei (Klu 2008). The results show that standard Skyrme forces like SLy4 (Chabanat 1997) and SkO (Reinhard 1999) fail to reproduce the evolution of single-particle splittings along isotopic chains, a problem previously addressed in shell-model studies by introducing monopole shifts attributed to tensor interaction (Otsuka 2001, 2005). The paper demonstrates that a functional with large effective mass (m* greater than or equal to 0.9) can simultaneously reproduce spin-orbit splittings and binding energies, enabling future studies of tensor effects on two-neutron separation energies and nuclear deformability.
