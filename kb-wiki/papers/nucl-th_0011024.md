# A renormalized equation for the three-body system with short-range interactions

> We study the three-body system with short-range interactions characterized by an unnaturally large two-body scattering length. We show that the off-shell scattering amplitude is cutoff independent up to power corrections. This allows us to derive an exact renormalization group equation for the three-body force. We also obtain a renormalized equation for the off-shell scattering amplitude. This equation is invariant under discrete scale transformations. The periodicity of the spectrum of bound states originally observed by Efimov is a consequence of this symmetry. The functional dependence of the three-body scattering length on the two-body scattering length can be obtained analytically using the asymptotic solution to the integral equation. An analogous formula for the three-body recombination coefficient is also obtained.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the off-shell three-body scattering amplitude becomes cutoff independent once the three-body force is properly included, allowing the derivation of renormalized integral equations that are exactly invariant under discrete scale transformations. This invariance is exact at leading order in the effective theory and provides an analytic explanation for the Efimov spectrum of bound states and the functional form of three-body observables.

## Method

The authors start from the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation for three-body scattering, regulated by an ultraviolet cutoff Lambda and including a three-body contact interaction H(Lambda)/Lambda^2. They derive a renormalization group equation for the off-shell amplitude a(p) by eliminating the three-body force through subtraction and acting with Lambda d/dLambda, showing that the amplitude is cutoff independent up to power corrections of order 1/(a_2 Lambda). Using the asymptotic solution for a(p) at large momenta, they obtain an analytic expression for the running of H(Lambda) and identify discrete values of the cutoff where H(Lambda)=0, yielding renormalized equations where the cutoff is replaced by the physical three-body parameter Lambda_* in the upper integration limit.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scale factor is exp(pi/s_0) with s_0 approximately 1.006, giving a period of about 22.7 in the limit cycle.
- The cutoff values where H(Lambda)=0 are given by Lambda_n approximately equal to Lambda_* exp[(n+1/4)pi] for integer n.
- The three-body recombination coefficient is approximately alpha = (a_2^4/m) * 68 cos^2[s_0 ln(a_2 Lambda_*) + 1.7].
- The three-body scattering length is approximately a_3 = a_2 (1.4 - 1.8 tan[s_0(a_2 Lambda_*) + 3.2]).
- The first meeting point where a(p) is independent of Lambda_* occurs at p_0 = 1.1/a_2.
- The total number of three-body bound states is N = (s_0/pi) ln(Lambda a_2).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of the three-body system developed by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999), who showed that a three-body contact interaction is leading order and exhibits a limit cycle in its renormalization group flow. The paper provides an analytic derivation of the discrete scale invariance that underlies the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1971) and derives functional forms for the three-body scattering length and recombination coefficient that were previously obtained only from numerical fits. The renormalized equations enable future numerical work without delicate cancellations between kernel terms and provide a framework for understanding the limit a_2 approaches infinity, which is relevant for atomic systems near Feshbach resonances and for testing conjectures about nonrelativistic conformal invariance in the three-body system.
