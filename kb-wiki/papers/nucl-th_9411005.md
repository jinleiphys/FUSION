# Momentum and Energy Distributions of Nucleons in Finite Nuclei due to Short-Range Correlations

> The influence of short-range correlations on the momentum and energy distribution of nucleons in nuclei is evaluated assuming a realistic meson-exchange potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Using the Green-function approach the calculations are performed directly for the finite nucleus $^{16}$O avoiding the local density approximation and its reference to studies of infinite nuclear matter. The nucleon-nucleon correlations induced by the short-range and tensor components of the interaction yield an enhancement of the momentum distribution at high momenta as compared to the Hartree-Fock description. These high-momentum components should be observed mainly in nucleon knockout reactions like $(e,e'p)$ leaving the final nucleus in a state of high excitation energy. Our analysis also demonstrates that non-negligible contributions to the momentum distribution should be found in partial waves which are unoccupied in the simple shell-model. The treatment of correlations beyond the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation also yields an improvement for the calculated ground-state properties.

## Key claim

Short-range and tensor correlations from a realistic meson-exchange potential produce an enhancement of the high-momentum components of the nucleon momentum distribution in finite nuclei, but this enhancement is significantly smaller than predictions from nuclear matter studies using the local density approximation; the high-momentum components should be observable mainly in knockout reactions with energy transfers around 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson equation for the single-particle Green function directly for the finite nucleus 16O, using a self-energy constructed from a G-matrix derived from the One-Boson-Exchange potential B of Machleidt. The self-energy included Hartree-Fock terms plus 2-particle-1-hole (2p1h) and 2-hole-1-particle (2h1p) contributions, with the real parts obtained via dispersion relations and a correction term to avoid double-counting with the nuclear-matter G-matrix. The spectral functions and momentum distributions were extracted from the Green function using a spherical box basis with radius 15 fm or larger.

## Key numbers

- Quasihole occupation probabilities in 16O: 0.78 for s1/2, 0.91 for p3/2, and 0.90 for p1/2 partial waves.
- Only 14.025 out of 16 nucleons occupy the quasihole states; 1.13 nucleons are in the 2h1p continuum with s and p partial waves, and an additional 0.687 nucleons from d and f partial waves.
- The mean energy of the 2h1p continuum ranges from -80 to -150 MeV for momenta up to 3.3 fm^-1, with a parametrization -k^2/(2m*) - C where m* = 2400 MeV and C = 80 MeV.
- The total particle number summed over all partial waves considered is 15.841, slightly below 16 for 16O.
- The 2h1p continuum density of states shows a maximum at -60 MeV and a second local maximum around -85 MeV, with threshold at approximately -40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends microscopic calculations of spectral functions and momentum distributions from light nuclei (A ≤ 4) and nuclear matter to a finite medium-mass nucleus (16O) without using the local density approximation, which had been employed in prior work by Stringari (1982), SFFB, BFFS, and Neck (1990). The results contradict the expectation from nuclear matter studies using the Reid potential (Vonderfecht, 1993; Polls, 1994) that the local density approximation would yield similar high-momentum enhancement; the present calculation shows significantly less high-momentum strength. The approach enables direct comparison with (e,e'p) knockout experiments and provides ground-state properties beyond the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation, though long-range correlations not treated here (Brand, 1993; Rijsdijk, 1992; Skouras, 1991) and center-of-mass effects (Pieper, 1992) would further reduce quasihole strength toward the experimentally observed 63% for the p1/2 state (Leuschner, 1994).
