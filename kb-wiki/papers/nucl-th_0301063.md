# Role of the Coulomb and the vector-isovector $ρ$ potentials in the isospin asymmetry of nuclear pseudospin

> We investigate the role of the Coulomb and the vector-isovector $ρ$ potentials in the asymmetry of the neutron and proton pseudospin splittings in nuclei. To this end, we solve the Dirac equation for the nucleons using central vector and scalar potentials with Woods-Saxon shape and $Z$ and $N-Z$ dependent Coulomb and $ρ$ potentials added to the vector potential. We study the effect of these potentials on the energy splittings of proton and neutron pseudospin partners along a Sn isotopic chain. We use an energy decomposition proposed in a previous work to assess the effect of a pseudospin-orbit potential on those splittings. We conclude that the effect of the Coulomb potential is quite small and the $ρ$ potential gives the main contribution to the observed isospin asymmetry of the pseudospin splittings. This isospin asymmetry results from a cancellation of the various energy terms and cannot be attributed only to the pseudospin-orbit term, confirming the dynamical character of this symmetry pointed out in previous works.

## Key claim

The Coulomb potential has a quite small net effect on the isospin asymmetry of proton and neutron pseudospin splittings, while the vector-isovector ρ potential gives the main contribution; however, this asymmetry results from a cancellation of all energy terms and cannot be attributed only to the pseudospin-orbit term, confirming the dynamical character of pseudospin symmetry.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for nucleons using central vector and scalar potentials with Woods-Saxon shape, with Z-dependent Coulomb and (N-Z)-dependent ρ potentials added to the vector potential. They fitted these potentials to the Σ potentials from a relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculation for Sn isotopes from A=100 to A=170. They then used an energy decomposition based on a Schrödinger-like equation for the lower component of the Dirac spinor to assess the contribution of each term (kinetic, pseudospin-orbit, Darwin, and potential Σ) to the pseudospin energy splittings of the [2d5/2 - 1g7/2] doublet.

## Key numbers

- The central scalar-vector potential Σ_c has a depth Σ_{o c} = -69.94 MeV, with radius R_c = 1.21 A^{1/3} fm and diffusivity a_c = 0.13 A^{1/3} fm.
- The ρ potential depth V_{o ρ} = -[0.12(N-Z) + 3.87] MeV, with radius R_ρ = [0.03(N-Z) + 5.05] fm and diffusivity a_ρ = [0.007(N-Z) + 0.27] fm.
- For A=150, the ρ potential V_{0 ρ} is around -10 MeV, while the Coulomb potential V_Coul is close to 20 MeV at the origin.
- When the Coulomb potential is excluded, the pseudospin-orbit term contribution to the proton energy splitting becomes smaller (about -1 MeV) and essentially the same as for neutrons.
- The kinetic and potential Σ terms each change by approximately 0.5 MeV when the Coulomb interaction is excluded.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Lisboa, Malheiro, Alberto, and collaborators (2002, 2003) that showed pseudospin degeneracy depends on the shape of the Σ potential and that the pseudospin-orbit term is nonperturbative. It also builds on the relativistic interpretation of pseudospin symmetry by Ginocchio (1997) and on RCHB calculations for Sn isotopes by Meng (1998). The paper provides a quantitative explanation for why neutron pseudospin doublets are more degenerate than proton ones, showing that the ρ potential is the main source of this isospin asymmetry, and confirms the dynamical character of pseudospin symmetry in nuclei.
