# Scaling properties of the pairing problem in the strong coupling limit

> We study the excited states of the pairing Hamiltonian providing an expansion for their energy in the strong coupling limit. To assess the role of the pairing interaction we apply the formalism to the case of a heavy atomic nucleus. We show that only a few statistical moments of the level distribution are sufficient to yield an accurate estimate of the energy for not too small values of the coupling $G$ and we give the analytic expressions of the first four terms of the series. Further, we discuss the convergence radius $G_{\rm sing}$ of the expansion showing that it strongly depends upon the details of the level distribution. Furthermore $G_{\rm sing}$ is not related to the critical values of the coupling $G_{\rm crit}$, which characterize the physics of the pairing Hamiltonian, since it can exist even in the absence of these critical points.

## Key claim

The paper provides an analytic expansion for the energies of excited states of the pairing Hamiltonian in the strong coupling limit, showing that only a few statistical moments of the single-particle level distribution are sufficient for accurate energy estimates, but the convergence radius of the expansion depends strongly on the details of the level distribution and is not related to the critical coupling values that characterize the physics of the pairing Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors start from Richardson's equations for n pairs distributed over L levels with degeneracies Omega_mu and energies epsilon_mu, then introduce a strong coupling expansion parameter alpha = 2 sigma / (G Omega) where sigma is the variance of the level distribution. They split the pair energies E_i into two subsets: k "trapped" pairs (of order sigma) and n-k "collective" pairs (of order G), then expand the Richardson equations in powers of alpha to obtain analytic expressions for the total energy up to fourth order, with the trapped pair contributions computed numerically from a decoupled system of equations.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is defined as alpha = 2 sigma / (G Omega), with the strong coupling limit corresponding to alpha << 1
- For the example of Pb-188, the single-particle energies range from -3.47 MeV to 0 MeV, with average energy bar-epsilon = -1.897 MeV and sigma = 1.056 MeV
- The leading-order total energy formula is E = -G (n - v_l/2) (Omega - n - v_l/2 + 1), where v_l is the like-seniority
- For the trapped pairs in the Pb-188 example, the zero-order energies are E_1 = -0.164 MeV, E_2 = (-2.035 + 0.179i) MeV, E_3 = (-2.035 - 0.179i) MeV
- The first-order correction to the collective energy sum is given by sum y_i^(1) = -2(n-k)/(Omega-2k) * zeta_1^(0), where zeta_1^(0) = 3.384 for the example

## Context

This work extends the strong coupling expansion previously developed for the ground state by Barbaro et al. (2006) to excited states of the pairing Hamiltonian, generalizing the approach of Yuzbashyan et al. and Snyman et al. who studied equally-spaced levels with unit degeneracy appropriate for superconducting metallic grains. The formalism is applied to nuclear physics where levels have varying energies and degeneracies, building on the pseudodeformed quasispin SU(2) algebra approach of Baer and the exact solution for two nondegenerate levels by Balantekin and Pehlivan. The results enable analytic estimates of pairing energies for heavy nuclei without full numerical solution of Richardson's equations, and show that the convergence properties of the expansion are determined by the level distribution details rather than the critical coupling values.
