# The applicability of hydrodynamics in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$= 2.4-7.7 GeV

> To assess the degree of equilibration of the matter created in heavy-ion reactions at low to intermediate beam energies, a hadronic transport approach (SMASH) is employed. By using a coarse-graining method, we compute the energy momentum tensor of the system at fixed time steps and evaluate the degree of isotropy of the diagonal terms and the relative magnitude of the off-diagonal terms. This study focuses mostly on Au+Au collisions in the energy range $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.4-7.7 GeV, but central collisions of lighter ions like C+C, Ar+KCl and Ag+Ag are considered as well. We find that the conditions concerning local equilibration for a hydrodynamic description are reasonably satisfied in a large portion of the system for a significant amount of time (several fm/c) when considering the average evolution of many events, yet they are rarely fulfilled on an event by event basis. This is relevant for the application of hybrid approaches at low beam energies as they are or will be reached by the HADES experiment at GSI, the future CBM experiment at FAIR as well as the beam energy scan program at RHIC.

## Key claim
The conditions for local equilibration required for a hydrodynamic description are reasonably satisfied in a large portion of the system for several fm/c when using the average evolution of many events, but they are rarely fulfilled on an event-by-event basis at low beam energies.

## Method
The authors used the SMASH hadronic transport approach (version 2.0.2) in cascade mode to simulate heavy-ion collisions. They computed the energy-momentum tensor at the nodes of a three-dimensional cubic lattice with 1 fm resolution using a coarse-graining method with smearing kernels, then evaluated the pressure anisotropy (X) and off-diagonality (Y) of the tensor in the Landau frame to quantify the degree of local equilibration.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 2.4-7.7 GeV were studied, with focus on Elab = 1.23 AGeV for the 0-5% centrality class (impact parameter b = 0-3.3 fm)
- The lattice had side length 75 fm, space resolution of 1 fm, and time intervals of 0.5 fm
- 1080 events were performed for each configuration
- Energy density thresholds of ε ≥ 1, 100, and 500 MeV/fm³ were used to restrict analysis
- For C+C collisions at 2 AGeV, the energy density in the center never exceeded 300 MeV/fm³
- The pressure anisotropy X and off-diagonality Y were evaluated at thresholds of 0.1, 0.3, and 0.5
- Time evolution was studied up to t = 40 fm, with results displayed up to t = 25 fm for dilute systems

## Context
This work extends the investigations presented in Oliinychenko (2015) by providing a broader and more systematic study of equilibration conditions at low beam energies. It is relevant for hybrid approaches used at the HADES experiment at GSI, the future CBM experiment at FAIR, and the beam energy scan program at RHIC. The study uses SMASH as a benchmark for hydrodynamic models, contrasting with earlier works that examined equilibration in a central cell of length 4-5 fm (Bravina 1998, Bravina 2018, Bravina 2020) by instead analyzing the whole system volume on a three-dimensional lattice.
