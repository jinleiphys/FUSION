# The Equation of State of Neutron-Star Matter in Strong Magnetic Fields

> We study the effects of very strong magnetic fields on the equation of state (EOS) in multicomponent, interacting matter by developing a covariant description for the inclusion of the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons. For the description of neutron star matter, we employ a field-theoretical approach which permits the study of several models which differ in their behavior at high density. Effects of Landau quantization in ultra-strong magnetic fields ($B>10^{14}$ Gauss) lead to a reduction in the electron chemical potential and a substantial increase in the proton fraction. We find the generic result for $B>10^{18}$ Gauss that the softening of the EOS caused by Landau quantization is overwhelmed by stiffening due to the incorporation of the anomalous magnetic moments of the nucleons. In addition, the neutrons become completely spin polarized. The inclusion of ultra-strong magnetic fields leads to a dramatic increase in the proton fraction, with consequences for the direct Urca process and neutron star cooling. The magnetization of the matter never appears to become very large, as the value of $|H/B|$ never deviates from unity by more than a few percent. Our findings have implications for the structure of neutron stars in the presence of large frozen-in magnetic fields.

## Key claim

For magnetic fields above $10^{18}$ Gauss, the softening of the equation of state caused by Landau quantization is overwhelmed by stiffening due to the inclusion of the anomalous magnetic moments of the nucleons, and neutrons become completely spin polarized; this is a generic result across several field-theoretical models, but the magnetization of the matter never becomes very large, with $|H/B|$ deviating from unity by no more than a few percent.

## Method

The authors employed a field-theoretical mean-field approach with $\sigma-\omega-\rho$ meson exchange to describe interacting neutron-star matter, using two classes of Lagrangians (linear scalar coupling in models HS81, GM1-GM3 and nonlinear scalar coupling in model ZM) that differ in high-density behavior. They included Landau quantization for charged particles and developed a covariant description for the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons, solving self-consistently the field equations for the meson fields, chemical potentials, and number densities under charge neutrality and beta equilibrium at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field for electrons is $B_c^e = 4.414 \times 10^{13}$ Gauss; for protons it is $B_c^p = 1.487 \times 10^{20}$ Gauss (or $B^* = 3 \times 10^6$ in units of $B_c^e$).
- Significant changes in the equation of state begin for $B^* \gtrsim 10^5$, corresponding to $B \gtrsim 4.4 \times 10^{18}$ Gauss.
- The electromagnetic field energy density and pressure are $\varepsilon_f = P_f = B^2/(8\pi) = 4.814 \times 10^{-8} B^{*2}$ MeV fm$^{-3}$.
- The energy from the anomalous magnetic moments is $|\kappa_n + \kappa_p| B \simeq 1.67 \times 10^{-5} B^*$ MeV.
- The deviation $|4\pi\mathcal{M}/B|$ does not exceed 4% in all cases considered.
- The magnetic field required for significant proton effects is estimated as $B^* \gtrsim \lbar^2 (2\pi^4 Y_p^2 n_b^2)^{1/3}$, with $\lbar^2 \simeq 1.5 \times 10^5$ fm$^2$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Chakrabarty (1996) and Chakrabarty, Bandyopadhyay, and Pal (1997) on dense matter in strong magnetic fields by including the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons, the electromagnetic field energy density and pressure, and a broader class of field-theoretical models (HS81, GM1-GM3, ZM) to extract generic trends. It also generalizes the magnetization formalism of Blandford and Hernquist (1982) for an electron gas to interacting multicomponent matter. The results enable future self-consistent calculations of neutron star structure incorporating both the direct effects of magnetic fields on the equation of state and general relativistic structure, with implications for magnetar masses and cooling via the direct Urca process.
