# Spin-isospin rotation dynamics

> The equations for the solitons arbitrarily rotating in the ordinary and isotopic space are obtained. The wave functions of the corresponding dynamic states in the quantum case are found. The generalized matrix of the moments of inertia is degenerate for the O(2)-invariant configurations characteristic for the nucleon and delta-isobar. The equation for such configurations is established. It is shown that the spin-isospin rotation prevents the collapse of the soliton states in the SU(2) sigma-model. The entire consideration is based on the variational approach to the method of collective variables.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spin-isospin rotation prevents the collapse of soliton states in the SU(2) sigma-model, but this result is conditional on the existence of a mass term in the Lagrangian to provide damping asymptotics at infinity, and on the use of a quantization scheme with half-integer spin and isospin values.

## Method

The author develops a variational approach to the method of collective variables, introducing collective coordinates for rotations in ordinary and isotopic space while freezing microscopic degrees of freedom. The equations for dynamic soliton configurations are derived from the Hamiltonian formalism by minimizing the collective Hamiltonian with respect to the field variables under orthogonality constraints, yielding equation (B) that determines the shape of rotating solitons. The approach is applied to SU(2) field theories including the sigma-model and Skyrme model, with the field ansatz separating cyclic collective variables from microscopic field degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon ground state corresponds to quantum numbers I=J=1/2, Q=0, with wave function given by equation (O) as a superposition of spin and isospin states
- The eigenvalues of the invariant operators are: Q^2 = Q(Q+1) with |I-J| ≤ Q ≤ I+J; RQ = (1/2)(I(I+1)-J(J+1)); R^2 = (1/2)(I(I+1)+J(J+1)-(1/2)Q(Q+1))
- The wave function components for spin-1/2 states are expressed in terms of SU(2) group parameters a_0, a_1, a_2, a_3 with normalization factor 1/π

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic approximation used in quantizing the hedgehog solution in the Skyrme model by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) and in quantizing soliton states in higher homotopic classes by Ir. The paper addresses the impossibility of the adiabatic approach for problems requiring exact account of dynamic nucleon deformation, description of rotational resonances, and localized states in the SU(2) sigma-model where static soliton solutions degenerate into point states with zero energy due to the Derrick theorem. The approach enables the study of dynamic soliton states with arbitrary spin and isospin values, and provides a framework for going beyond purely collective description by including fluctuations of microscopic variables.
