# Dilepton production in proton-proton collisions at BEVALAC energies

> The dilepton production in elementary ${pp\to e^{+}e^{-}X}$ reactions at BEVALAC energies $T_{lab}=1÷5$ GeV is investigated. The calculations include direct ${e^{+}e^{-}}$ decays of the vector mesons $ρ^{0}$, $ω$, and $ϕ$, Dalitz decays of the $π^{0}$-, $η$-, $% ρ$-, $ω$-, and $ϕ$-mesons, and of the baryon resonances $% Δ(1232),N(1520),$ $... $ . The subthreshold vector meson production cross sections in $pp$ collisions are treated in a way sufficient to avoid double counting with the inclusive vector meson production. The vector meson dominance model for the transition form factors of the resonance Dalitz decays $R\to e^{+}e^{-}N$ is used in an extended form to ensure correct asymptotics which are in agreement with the quark counting rules. Such a modification gives an unified and consistent description of both $R\to Nγ$ radiative decays and $R\to Nρ(ω)$ meson decays. The effect of multiple pion production on the experimental efficiency for the detection of the dilepton pairs is studied. We find the dilepton yield in reasonable agreement with the experimental data for the set of intermediate energies whereas at the highest energy $T_{lab}=4.88$ GeV the number of dilepton pairs is likely to be overestimated experimentally in the mass range $M=300÷700$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors find that their improved theoretical treatment of dilepton production in proton-proton collisions yields reasonable agreement with DLS experimental data at intermediate beam energies, but at the highest energy of T_lab = 4.88 GeV the experimental data likely overestimate the dilepton yield in the mass range M = 300 to 700 MeV, and the results at the lowest energy T_lab = 1.04 GeV also require additional study.

## Method

The authors calculate dilepton production in pp collisions at BEVALAC energies (T_lab = 1 to 5 GeV) by including direct e+e- decays of vector mesons (rho0, omega, phi), Dalitz decays of pi0, eta, rho, omega, and phi mesons, and Dalitz decays of baryon resonances (Delta(1232), N(1520), and others). They treat subthreshold vector meson production cross sections to avoid double counting with inclusive vector meson production, and extend the vector meson dominance model for resonance Dalitz decay transition form factors to ensure correct asymptotic behavior consistent with quark counting rules. They also model the effect of multiple pion production on experimental detection efficiency using a binomial distribution for pion multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Beam kinetic energies studied: T_lab = 1.04, 1.27, 1.61, 2.09, and 4.88 GeV
- At T_lab = 4.88 GeV, the number of dilepton pairs is likely overestimated experimentally in the mass range M = 300 to 700 MeV
- For the N(1520) resonance: B(N(1520) to N rho) = 15 to 25 percent, B(N(1520) to N gamma) = 0.46 to 0.56 percent (p gamma mode) and 0.30 to 0.53 percent (n gamma mode)
- For the N(1440) resonance: B(N(1440) to N gamma) = 0.035 to 0.048 percent (p gamma mode) and 0.009 to 0.032 percent (n gamma mode)
- For the N(1535) resonance: B(N(1535) to N gamma) = 0.15 to 0.35 percent (p gamma mode) and 0.004 to 0.29 percent (n gamma mode)
- Coupling constant f_N(1520)N rho = 7.0 extracted from the mesonic N(1520) to N rho decay
- Inclusive pion production cross section at T_lab = 4.88 GeV: sigma(pi0 X) = 19 mb (interpolated)
- Exclusive rho0 production cross section at T_lab = 4.88 GeV: sigma(rho0 NN) = 0.12 mb, sigma(rho0 X) = 0.94 mb
- Exclusive omega production cross section at T_lab = 4.88 GeV: sigma(omega NN) = 0.20 mb, sigma(omega X) = 0.94 mb
- Exclusive phi production cross section at T_lab = 4.88 GeV: sigma(phi NN) = 0.001 mb, sigma(phi X) = 0.007 mb

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of dilepton spectra in pp collisions at BEVALAC energies by Ernst (1998) and by B. Martemyanov, C. Fuchs, and M. Krivoruchenko (1999), which had revealed a possible reoccurrence of the "DLS puzzle" at the elementary level of nucleon collisions. The DLS puzzle refers to the discrepancy between theoretical estimates and experimental data from the DLS Collaboration at BEVALAC, where in-medium rho-meson scenarios that successfully explain dilepton yields at SPS energies (CERES and HELIOS-3 experiments) fail for the DLS data. The improved treatment of transition form factors and multiple pion production effects presented here enables a more reliable comparison with DLS data and provides essential input for transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions, with future experiments by HADES at GSI expected to study dilepton spectra in the same energy range in greater detail.
