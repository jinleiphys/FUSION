# The cosmological 7Li problem from a nuclear physics perspective

> The primordial abundance of 7Li as predicted by Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) is more than a factor 2 larger than what has been observed in metal-poor halo stars. Herein, we analyze the possibility that this discrepancy originates from incorrect assumptions about the nuclear reaction cross sections relevant for BBN. To do this, we introduce an efficient method to calculate the changes in the 7Li abundance produced by arbitrary (temperature dependent) modifications of the nuclear reaction rates. Then, considering that 7Li is mainly produced from 7Be via the electron capture process 7Be + e -> 7Li + nu_e, we assess the impact of the various channels of 7Be destruction. Differently from previous analysis, we consider the role of unknown resonances by using a complete formalism which takes into account the effect of Coulomb and centrifugal barrier penetration and that does not rely on the use of the narrow-resonance approximation. As a result of this, the possibility of a nuclear physics solution to the 7Li problem is significantly suppressed. Given the present experimental and theoretical constraints, it is unlikely that the 7Be + n destruction rate is underestimated by the 2.5 factor required to solve the problem. We exclude, moreover, that resonant destruction in the channels 7Be + t and 7Be + 3He can explain the 7Li puzzle. New unknown resonances in 7Be + d and 7Be + alpha could potentially produce significant effects. Recent experimental results have ruled out such a possibility for 7Be+d. On the other hand, for the 7Be + alpha channel very favorable conditions are required. The possible existence of a partially suitable resonant level in 11C is studied in the framework of a coupled-channel model and the possibility of a direct measurement is considered.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, after applying a more complete nuclear-reaction formalism that includes Coulomb and centrifugal barrier penetration without relying on the narrow-resonance approximation, the possibility of a nuclear-physics solution to the cosmological 7Li problem is significantly suppressed. It concludes that, given present experimental and theoretical constraints, it is unlikely that any single nuclear reaction rate is underestimated by the factor of about 2.5 needed to resolve the discrepancy between BBN predictions and observed primordial 7Li abundances.

## Method

The authors introduce a linear-response formalism that calculates the change in the primordial 7Li abundance produced by arbitrary temperature-dependent modifications of nuclear reaction rates, using a kernel \(K(T)\) peaked at the 7Be freeze-out temperature (\(\sim 50\) keV). They then apply a Breit-Wigner formalism that includes exact Coulomb and centrifugal penetration factors (rather than the narrow-resonance approximation) to assess the maximum possible cross-section enhancement from hypothetical new resonances in the 7Be + n, 7Be + d, 7Be + t, 7Be + 3He, and 7Be + 4He channels, using the Wigner limit for reduced widths to maximise the effect.

## Key numbers

- The predicted primordial 7Li abundance from BBN is \((5.1^{+0.7}_{-0.6}) \times 10^{-10}\) (Li/H), while the observed value from metal-poor halo stars is \((1.7 \pm 0.06 \pm 0.44) \times 10^{-10}\), a factor \(\sim 3\) discrepancy.
- The total 7Be destruction rate must be increased by a factor \(\sim 2.5\) to obtain a factor 2 reduction in the 7Li abundance (derived from the kernel area \(\simeq 0.7\)).
- The Maxwellian-averaged cross section for 7Be(n,p)7Li at thermal energies is \(3.84 \times 10^4\) barn; at \(E \simeq 50\) keV, \(\sigma_{\rm np} \simeq 9\) barn, close to the unitarity bound \(\sigma_{\rm max} \simeq 15\) barn.
- To achieve a factor 2 reduction in 7Li via the 7Be(n,alpha)4He channel, its cross section would need to be increased \(\sim 60\)-fold to \(\sim 15\) barn; an upper bound from thermal-neutron data gives \(\langle \sigma_{\rm n\alpha} \rangle_{T_{\rm Be,f}} \le 0.02 \, \langle \sigma_{\rm np} \rangle_{T_{\rm Be,f}}\).
- For resonant destruction in charged-particle channels, the required ratios \(\langle \sigma_a v \rangle / \langle \sigma_{\rm np} v \rangle\) at \(T \simeq 10-60\) keV are: \(R_d \ge 0.01\) for 7Be+d, \(R_t \ge 1.5\) for 7Be+t, \(R_{\rm He3} \ge 0.03\) for 7Be+3He, and \(R_{\rm He4} \ge 4 \times 10^{-6}\) for 7Be+4He.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Coc (2003) and Cyburt (2009) on possible resonant enhancements of sub-dominant 7Be destruction channels, and directly addresses the suggestion by Chakraborty (2010) that unknown resonances could resolve the 7Li problem. By using a complete formalism that avoids the narrow-resonance approximation and includes Coulomb and centrifugal barrier penetration, the authors significantly reduce the parameter space for a nuclear-physics solution. The paper enables future experimental searches to focus on the 7Be+alpha channel (forming 11C), where a partially suitable resonant level is studied via a coupled-channel model, and it motivates direct measurements of that reaction.
