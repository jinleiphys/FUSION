# Probing the evolution of heavy-ion collisions using direct photon interferometry

> We investigate the measurement of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) photon correlations as an experimental tool to discriminate different sources of photon enhancement, which are proposed to simultaneously reproduce the direct photon yield and the azimuthal anisotropy measured in nuclear collisions at RHIC and the LHC. To showcase this, we consider two different scenarios in which we enhance the yields from standard hydrodynamical simulations. In the first, additional photons are produced from the early pre-equilibrium stage computed from the \textit{bottom-up} thermalization scenario. In the second, the thermal rates are enhanced close to the pseudo-critical temperature $T_c\approx 155\,\text{MeV}$ using a phenomenological ansatz. We compute the correlators for relative momenta $q_o, \,q_s$ and $q_l$ for different transverse pair momenta, $K_\perp$, and find that the longitudinal correlation is the most sensitive to different photon sources. Our results also demonstrate that including anisotropic pre-equilibrium rates enhances non-Gaussianities in the correlators, which can be quantified using the kurtosis of the correlators. Finally, we study the feasibility of measuring a direct photon HBT signal in the upcoming high-luminosity LHC runs. Considering only statistical uncertainties, we find that with the projected $\sim 10^{10}$ heavy ion events a measurement of the HBT correlations for $K_\perp<1\, \text{GeV}$ is statistically significant.

## Key claim

Direct photon Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlations can discriminate between different sources of photon enhancement that reproduce the same invariant yield and azimuthal anisotropy, but only if the longitudinal correlation is measured; the outward and sideward correlators show only small differences between scenarios.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1D event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of 200 Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV using the VISHNU package with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions, computing thermal photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma and hadron resonance gas. They added two separate enhancement scenarios: pre-equilibrium photons from the first stage of the bottom-up thermalization scenario matched to hydrodynamics at τhydro = 0.6 fm, and a phenomenological Gaussian enhancement of thermal rates centered at Tc ≈ 155 MeV with width d = 50 MeV and height h0 = 3. The HBT correlator C(q,K) was computed for relative momenta qo, qs, and ql at midrapidity for transverse pair momenta K⊥ = 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5 GeV, and homogeneity radii were extracted using the method of moments.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of direct photon interferometry by Srivastava (1993), Timmermann (1994), and Frodermann (2009), and addresses the direct-photon puzzle where standard hydrodynamical models cannot simultaneously describe photon yields and azimuthal anisotropy measured at RHIC and LHC. The pre-equilibrium photon source builds on the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) and recent estimates by Berges (2017), while the late-time enhancement near Tc follows the conjecture of van Hees (2014) that confining modes enhance thermal rates during hadronization. The results enable experimental searches for direct photon HBT signals in upcoming high-luminosity LHC runs, particularly with the ALICE detector, and provide a method to determine whether direct photons originate predominantly from early or late stages of the collision.
