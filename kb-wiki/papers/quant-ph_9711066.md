# Barut-Girardello coherent states for u(p,q) and sp(N,R) and their macroscopic superpositions

> The Barut-Girardello coherent states (BG CS) representation is extended to the noncompact algebras u(p,q) and sp(N,R) in (reducible) quadratic boson realizations. The sp(N,R) BG CS take the form of multimode ordinary Schrödinger cat states. Macroscopic superpositions of 2^{n-1} sp(N,R) CS (2^n canonical CS, n=1,2,...) are pointed out which are overcomplete in the N-mode Hilbert space and the relation between the canonical CS and the u(p,q) BG-type CS representations is established. The sets of u(p,q) and sp(N,R) BG CS and their discrete superpositions contain many states studied in quantum optics (even and odd N-mode CS, pair CS) and provide an approach to quadrature squeezing, alternative to that of intelligent states. New subsets of weakly and strongly nonclassical states are pointed out and their statistical properties (first- and second-order squeezing, photon number distributions) are discussed. For specific values of the angle parameters and small amplitude of the canonical CS components these states approaches multimode Fock states with one, two or three bosons/photons. It is shown that eigenstates of a squared non-Hermitian operator A^2 (generalized cat states) can exhibit squeezing of the quadratures of A.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Barut-Girardello coherent state representation to the noncompact algebras u(p,q) and sp(N,R) in quadratic boson realizations, showing that the sp(N,R) BG CS take the form of multimode ordinary Schrödinger cat states, with the hedge that these states are constructed in reducible representations and the overcompleteness property holds for specific families parameterized by angle parameters.

## Method

The author constructs BG CS for sp(N,R) as eigenstates of the mutually commuting Weyl lowering operators E_ij = a_i a_j in the quadratic boson representation, solving the eigenvalue equations to obtain states that are superpositions of two multimode canonical coherent states |α⟩ and |-α⟩. For u(p,q), the BG CS are obtained as restrictions of the sp(N,R) CS to the subalgebra, with the additional constraint of fixed eigenvalue l of the difference number operator L = Σ a_α† a_α - Σ a_μ† a_μ, and the resolution of unity is derived using the overcompleteness of canonical CS and orthogonality relations between subspaces H_l.

## Key numbers

- The sp(N,R) BG CS are parameterized by N complex numbers α_j (j=1,...,N) with eigenvalues z_ij = α_i α_j for the operators a_i a_j.
- The overcomplete family of states |α;φ⟩ = cos φ |α⟩ + i sin φ |-α⟩ resolves the unity operator as 1 = (1/π^N) ∫ d^2α |α;φ⟩⟨φ;α| for any angle φ.
- Macroscopic superpositions of 2^n canonical CS (or 2^(n-1) sp(N,R) CS) are constructed for n=1,2,... as eigenstates of (a_i a_j)^(2^(n-1)).
- For u(p,q), the resolution of unity in the subspace H_l is given by 1_l = ∫ dμ(z;l,p,q) ||z;l,p,q⟩⟨q,p,l;z|| with measure dμ(z;l,p,q) = F(|z_p|,|z_q|;l,p,q) d^2z, where the weight function F involves an integral over |α_N| with exponent 2(q-1-p-l).
- For the special case u(p,1), the measure weight function is F' = (2|z|^(-l-p+1)/π^p) K_(-l-p+1)(2|z|), where K_ν(z) is the modified Bessel function of the third kind.

## Context

This work extends the Barut-Girardello coherent state construction from su(1,1) (Barut and Girardello, 1971) to the higher-rank noncompact algebras sp(N,R) and u(p,q), building on prior studies of BG CS for su(1,1) by Trifonov (1994, 1996) and others. The sp(N,R) BG CS are shown to include the even/odd coherent states (cat states) studied in quantum optics by Dodonov et al. (1974) and others, while the u(p,q) BG CS generalize the pair coherent states of Agarwal (1988) to N-mode systems. The results enable the construction of multimode squeezed states and macroscopic superpositions with controlled nonclassical properties, providing an alternative to the intelligent state approach to quadrature squeezing.
