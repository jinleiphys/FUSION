# Hadron-Quark Phase Transition at Finite Density in the Presence of a Magnetic Field: Anisotropic Approach

> We investigate the hadron-quark phase transition at finite density in the presence of a magnetic field taking into account the anisotropy created by a uniform magnetic field in the system's equations of state. We find a new anisotropic equilibrium condition that will drive the first-order phase transition along the boundary between the two phases. Fixing the magnetic field in the hadronic phase, the phase transition is realized by increasing the baryonic chemical potential at zero temperature. It is shown that the magnetic field is mildly boosted after the system transitions from the hadronic to the quark phase. The magnetic-field discontinuity between the two phases is supported by a surface density of magnetic monopoles, which accumulate at the boundary separating the two phases. The mechanism responsible for the monopole charge density generation is discussed. Each phase is found to be paramagnetic with higher magnetic susceptibility in the quark phase. The connection with the physics of neutron stars is highlighted through out the paper.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when a uniform magnetic field is present, the hadron-quark phase transition at finite density must be governed by an anisotropic equilibrium condition (separate parallel and perpendicular pressure equalities) rather than the isotropic Maxwell or Gibbs conditions used in prior work; this anisotropy leads to a magnetic field discontinuity at the phase boundary, supported by a surface density of magnetic monopoles.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic phase using the nonlinear Walecka (NLW) model in the mean-field approximation and the quark phase using the MIT bag model for free quarks, both in a uniform background magnetic field. They derive the anisotropic equations of state from the quantum-statistical average of the energy-momentum tensor, obtaining distinct longitudinal and transverse pressures, and then apply the Maxwell construction with local charge neutrality to determine the first-order phase transition conditions at zero temperature. Numerical calculations are performed to show how thermodynamic parameters behave around the phase boundary.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear matter density is given as $\rho_{nuc}=2.8\times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$.
- Magnetar surface magnetic fields are cited as $10^{14}$-$10^{16}$ G.
- Estimates for maximum inner core magnetic fields are $\sim10^{18}$ G (based on equipartition between gravity and nuclear matter) and $\sim 10^{20}$ G (based on quark matter), with a stable maximum of order $eB \sim 10^{17}$ G from hydrodynamic equilibrium considerations.
- The surface tension of the hadron-quark interface at zero magnetic field is estimated in the range $10 - 100$ MeV/fm$^2$; in the presence of a magnetic field of $10^{17}-10^{18}$ G and baryon densities $2n_o - 4n_o$, values are found between $0.2$ MeV/fm$^2$ and $15$ MeV/fm$^2$.
- A critical surface tension $\lambda_{crit}=60$ MeV/fm$^2$ is cited, above which the Maxwell construction is approached.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the hadron-quark phase transition in magnetic fields (e.g., Trans-B, Rabhi) that used isotropic equilibrium conditions, by incorporating the pressure anisotropy demonstrated for magnetized fermion systems by Canuto (1970) and later formalized by Ferrer and collaborators (2010). The anisotropic equilibrium conditions derived here enable future investigations of magnetized phases such as inhomogeneous chiral condensates (Frolov, 2010; MDCDW) or color superconductivity (MCFL), and are directly applicable to modeling the interior of neutron stars and magnetars.
