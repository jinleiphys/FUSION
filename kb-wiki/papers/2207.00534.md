# An introduction to Thermal Field Theory and Some of its Application

> In this article an introduction to the thermal field theory within imaginary time has been discussed in details. The imaginary time formalism has been introduced through both the operatorial and the functional integration method. The prescription to perform frequency sum for boson and fermion has been discussed. Green's function both in Minkowski time as well as in Euclidean time has been derived. The tadpole diagram in $ϕ^4$ theory and the self-energy in $ϕ^3$ theory have been computed. The basic features of general two point functions for both fermions and bosons in presence of a heat bath have been discussed. Then the free partition functions for scalar, fermion and gauge field, and interacting scalar field have been obtained. The quantum electrodynamics (QED) and gauge fixing have been discussed in details. The one-loop self-energy for electron and photon in QED have been obtained in hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation. The dispersion properties and collective excitations of both electron and photon in a thermal medium have been presented. The spectral representation of fermion and gauge boson propagators have been obtained. In HTL approximation, the generalisation of QED results of two point functions to quantum chromodynamics (QCD) have been outlined that mostly involve group theoretical factors. As an effective field theory approach the HTL resummation and the HTL perturbation theory have been introduced. The leading order, next-to-leading order and next-to-next-leading order free energy and pressure for deconfined QCD medium created in heavy-ion collisions have been computed within HTLpt. The general features of the deconfined QCD medium have also been outlined with non-perturbative effects like gluon condensate and Gribov-Zwanziger action. The dilepton production rates from quark-gluon plasma with these non-perturbative effects have been computed.

## Key claim

This pedagogical review claims that thermal field theory, specifically the imaginary-time (Matsubara) formalism, provides a systematic diagrammatic method to compute partition functions and observables for interacting quantum field theories at finite temperature and chemical potential, but the presentation is limited to perturbative expansions and the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, with non-perturbative effects treated only through gluon condensate and Gribov-Zwanziger action.

## Method

The paper develops the imaginary-time formalism through both operatorial and functional-integration methods, deriving Matsubara frequency sums for bosons and fermions, Green's functions in Euclidean and Minkowski time, and Feynman rules at finite temperature. It then computes one-loop self-energies in scalar theories and in QED within the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, generalizes these two-point functions to QCD by introducing group-theoretical factors, and applies HTL perturbation theory (HTLpt) to compute the free energy and pressure of deconfined QCD matter at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order. Finally, it incorporates non-perturbative effects via gluon condensate and Gribov-Zwanziger action to compute dilepton production rates from quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

* The free partition function for one bosonic degree of freedom yields \(\ln \mathcal{Z}_B = -\beta\omega/2 - \ln(1 - e^{-\beta\omega})\), where the first term is the zero-point energy contribution.
* The free partition function for one fermionic degree of freedom yields \(\ln \mathcal{Z}_F = \beta\omega/2 + \ln(1 + e^{-\beta(\omega-\mu)})\), with the zero-point energy term \(\beta\omega/2\).
* The logarithm of the partition function for a non-interacting gas of fermions, antifermions, and bosons is given by \(\ln \mathcal{Z} = \frac{V}{(2\pi)^3} \int d^3p \left[ \frac{\beta\omega}{2} + \ln(1+e^{-\beta(\omega-\mu)}) + \ln(1+e^{-\beta(\omega+\mu)}) - \ln(1-e^{-\beta\omega}) \right]\).
* The paper computes the leading-order (LO), next-to-leading-order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) free energy and pressure for deconfined QCD medium within HTL perturbation theory, though explicit numerical values for these quantities are not provided in the truncated text.

## Context

This review extends the pedagogical tradition of thermal field theory texts such as Kapusta (1989) and Le Bellac (1996), providing a detailed step-by-step introduction to the Matsubara formalism with explicit calculations of tadpole and self-energy diagrams. It enables researchers to compute thermodynamic quantities and collective excitations in QCD plasmas relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and it outlines how non-perturbative effects from gluon condensate and Gribov-Zwanziger action modify dilepton production rates, pointing toward future work at FAIR and NICA experiments.
