# Few-body hierarchy in non-relativistic functional renormalization group equations and a decoupling theorem

> For non-relativistic quantum field theory in the few-body limit with instantaneous interactions it is shown within the functional renormalization group formalism that propagators are not renormalized and that the renormalization group equations of one-particle irreducible vertex functions are governed by a hierarchical structure. This hierarchy allows to solve the equations in the n-body sector without knowledge or assumptions about the m-body sectors where m>n.

## Key claim

For non-relativistic quantum field theory with instantaneous interactions and conserved particle number in the few-body limit, the functional renormalization group equations for one-particle irreducible vertex functions exhibit a hierarchical structure that allows solving the equations in the n-body sector without knowledge of the m-body sectors for m>n, provided the chemical potential is chosen such that the particle density vanishes and the regularized propagator retains all its singularities in the upper half of the complex frequency plane.

## Method

The author considers a non-relativistic bosonic field theory with a microscopic inverse propagator of the form i p0 + f(p) - mu and instantaneous interaction vertices that are independent of frequency. An infrared regulator function Rk is added to the action, and the exact functional renormalization group flow equation for the effective action Gamma_k is used to derive flow equations for one-particle irreducible vertex functions. The proof of the decoupling theorem relies on classifying n-body correlation functions by their "m-closeable" property, which characterizes whether m outgoing lines can be connected to incoming lines to yield a non-vanishing (n-m)-body diagram, and on showing that the propagator is not renormalized at any scale.

## Key numbers

* The microscopic inverse propagator has the form i p0 + f(p) - mu, where f(p) is non-negative and mu is chosen such that the particle density vanishes, implying mu <= 0 and |mu| larger than the maximal binding energy per particle.
* The bound on closeable parts of one-particle irreducible n-body vertex functions is m <= n-3, meaning the one-particle irreducible n-body vertex function can have m-closeable parts only for m up to n-3.
* The constraint from counting lines in a one-loop expression gives n = sum_{j=2}^infinity V_j (j-1), where V_j are the numbers of j-body vertices, implying V_j = 0 for j > n+1.

## Context

This work extends the known decoupling property of non-relativistic quantum mechanics, where n-particle problems can be solved independently, into the functional renormalization group formalism. It builds on earlier perturbative demonstrations that self-energy corrections vanish in such theories, as discussed in Fetter and Walecka (1971), and on the classification of n-particle irreducibility originally proposed by Taylor (1974) and adapted by Thomas and Rinat (1979) and Afnan and Blankleider (1980). The result enables systematic solution of renormalization group equations for few-body sectors successively, starting from the two-body sector, and has been applied to obtain exact results in the three-body sector using composite dimer fields by other authors.
