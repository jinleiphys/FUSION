# The radius of the rho meson determined from its decay constant

> We present a unified model describing electroweak properties of the pi and rho mesons. Using a general method of relativistic parametrization of matrix elements of local operators, adjusted for the nondiagonal in total angular momentum case, we calculate the rho-meson lepton-decay constant f_rho using the same parameters of free constituent quarks that have ensured exclusively good results for the pi meson previously. The only free parameter, characterizing quark interactions, which include additional spin-spin contribution and hence differ from the $π$-meson case, is fixed by matching the decay constant to its experimental value. The mean square charge radius is calculated, <r^2_rho>=(0.56+-0.04) fm^2. This result verifies, for the rho-meson case, the conjecture of equality between electromagnetic and strong radii of hadrons tested previously for proton, pi and K mesons.

# The radius of the rho meson determined from its decay constant

## Key claim
The authors calculate the rho-meson mean square charge radius as (0.56 ± 0.04) fm² using a unified relativistic constituent-quark model, with the only free parameter fixed by matching the calculated decay constant to its experimental value. This result supports the conjecture that electromagnetic and strong-interaction radii are equal for the rho meson, a hypothesis previously verified for the proton, pion, and kaon.

## Method
The authors use the instant form of Relativistic Quantum Mechanics (RQM) with a modified impulse approximation (MIA) that preserves Lorentz covariance and current conservation. They derive an integral expression for the rho-meson lepton decay constant f_rho in terms of the quark wave function, then fix the wave-function parameter b_rho by matching f_rho to its experimental value (152 ± 8 MeV). Using this fixed parameter, they compute the charge form factor and extract the mean square charge radius via the derivative at zero momentum transfer. All quark parameters (mass M = 0.22 GeV, anomalous magnetic moments, quark radius) are taken from a previous successful calculation of pion properties.

## Key numbers
- f_rho = (152 ± 8) MeV (experimental value used to fix the free parameter)
- b_rho = (0.385 ± 0.019) GeV (wave-function parameter determined from matching f_rho)
- ⟨r²_rho⟩ = (0.56 ± 0.04) fm² (calculated mean square charge radius of the rho meson)
- Quark mass M = 0.22 GeV (fixed from pion calculation)
- Rho-meson lifetime ∼ 4.5 × 10⁻²⁴ s (stated as reason for lack of direct experimental measurements)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous successful relativistic quark model of the pion (Krutov et al., 2001, 2009) to the rho meson, using the same constituent-quark parameters. The calculated charge radius agrees with the strong-interaction radius ⟨r²_st,rho⟩ = (0.52 ± 0.05) fm² from Poh et al. (1990), confirming the conjecture of equality between electromagnetic and strong radii for the rho meson. The result is compared with other approaches including Dyson-Schwinger equation (Bhagwat et al., 2008), lattice QCD (Choi et al., 2015; Owen et al., 2015), holographic QCD (Grigoryan et al., 2007), and various constituent quark models, which give values ranging from 0.268 to 0.67 fm². The model enables further predictions of rho-meson electroweak properties that are not directly accessible experimentally.
