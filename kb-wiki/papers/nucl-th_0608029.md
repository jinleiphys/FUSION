# Relativistic predictions of polarization phenomena in exclusive proton-induced proton-knockout reactions

> Whereas a nonrelativistic distorted wave model fails to quantitatively describe analyzing power data for exclusive proton-induced proton-knockout from the 3s_{1/2} state in Pb-208 at 202 MeV, the corresponding relativistic prediction provides a perfect description, thus suggesting that the Dirac equation is the more appropriate underlying dynamical equation. We check the consistency of this rsult by comparing predictions for both dynamical models to new high resolution data for 3s_{1/2} knockout in Pb-208 at a higher incident energy of 392 MeV.

## Key claim

The relativistic distorted wave model provides a superior description of analyzing power data for exclusive proton-induced proton-knockout from the 3s_{1/2} state in Pb-208 at both 202 MeV and 392 MeV compared to the nonrelativistic model, but at 392 MeV both models describe the data equally well at the maximum of the cross section (T_{a'} ≈ 245 MeV and θ_b ≈ 50°), qualifying the claim that relativistic dynamics are essential.

## Method

The authors compare relativistic (RDWIA) and nonrelativistic (NRDWIA) distorted wave predictions for the Pb-208(p,2p)Tl-207 reaction at 392 MeV incident energy, using a zero-range approximation for the NN interaction. The relativistic model employs Dirac equation solutions with global scalar and vector optical potentials (EDAI-fit) and Dirac-Hartree boundstate wave functions, while the nonrelativistic model uses Schrödinger equation solutions with Schrödinger-equivalent optical potentials and the upper component of the relativistic boundstate wave function. Both predictions are corrected for finite angle acceptance via Monte-Carlo simulation and compared to new high-resolution data (250 keV FWHM) from the Research Center for Nuclear Physics.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy of 392 MeV for the Pb-208(p,2p)Tl-207 reaction, compared to previous 202 MeV data
- Energy resolution of 250 keV at FWHM for the 392 MeV data, improved from 310 keV at FWHM for the 202 MeV data
- Coincident coplanar laboratory scattering angles of (θ_{a'} = 32.5°, θ_b = -50.0°) for energy-sharing distributions
- Angular distributions measured at T_{a'} = 250 MeV and θ_{a'} = 32.5°
- Both RDWIA and NRDWIA models describe the shape of unpolarized cross sections satisfactorily at 392 MeV
- RDWIA analyzing power prediction is consistently reduced compared to NRDWIA, except at T_{a'} ≈ 245 MeV and θ_b ≈ 50° where both models describe data equally well

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finding (Hillhouse et al., 2003; Ne et al., 2002) that a relativistic model perfectly describes analyzing power data for 3s_{1/2} knockout at 202 MeV while the nonrelativistic model fails, by testing consistency at a higher incident energy of 392 MeV with improved resolution. The results confirm the importance of relativistic dynamics for (p,2p) reactions and identify D_{nn}, D_{s's}, and D_{ℓ'ℓ} as additional polarization transfer observables sensitive to relativistic effects. The work enables future application of the RDWIA model to study density-dependent modifications of the NN interaction in deep-lying orbitals and to analyze exclusive proton knockout from exotic nuclei at radioactive beam facilities such as RIKEN and GSI.
