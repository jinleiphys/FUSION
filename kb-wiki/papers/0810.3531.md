# Possibility of s-wave pion condensates in neutron stars revisited

> We examine possibilities of pion condensation with zero momentum (s-wave condensation) in neutron stars by using the pion-nucleus optical potential U and the relativistic mean field (RMF) models. We use low-density phenomenological optical potentials parameterized to fit deeply bound pionic atoms or pion-nucleus elastic scatterings. Proton fraction (Y_p) and electron chemical potential (mu_e) in neutron star matter are evaluated in RMF models. We find that the s-wave pion condensation hardly takes place in neutron stars and especially has no chance if hyperons appear in neutron star matter and/or b_1 parameter in U has density dependence.

## Key claim

The authors find that s-wave pion condensation "hardly takes place" in neutron stars and "especially has no chance" if hyperons appear in neutron star matter or if the b1 parameter in the pion-nucleus optical potential has density dependence, qualifying the abstract's conclusion by noting that some parameter sets without density-dependent b1 and without hyperons do allow the condensation condition to be met at high densities.

## Method

The authors evaluate the in-medium pion energy E_pi at zero momentum using eight different phenomenological pion-nucleus optical potentials (six fitted to pionic atom data, two to pion-nucleus scattering data) and two theoretical chiral perturbation theory self-energies (MOW and KKW). They compute the electron chemical potential mu_e and proton fraction Y_p in neutron star matter under beta equilibrium using four relativistic mean field (RMF) models (NL1, NL3, TM1, SCL) and two RMF models including hyperons (TM1-SM, IOTSY), then compare E_pi with mu_e as a function of baryon density to test the condensation condition E_pi = mu_e.

## Key numbers

- At baryon density rho_B ~ 0.3 fm^{-3} (about twice saturation density), mu_e from RMF models exceeds E_pi obtained with some density-independent b1 parameter sets, allowing possible s-wave condensation.
- In asymmetric nuclear matter with Y_p = 0.2 (typical for neutron stars in RMF), the phenomenological pion potentials show 50-100 MeV ambiguities in E_pi at rho_B ~ rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.
- The model dependence of the isospin-dependent potential g_rho*rho from RMF is only around 10 MeV at rho_B = 0.8 fm^{-3}.
- The KY potential uses alpha = 0.367 and the F-W potential uses alpha = 0.391 for the density dependence of b1, where b1 = tilde_b1/(1 - alpha*rho_B/rho_0).
- The MOW chiral self-energy uses coefficients c1 = -0.81, c2 = 3.20, c3 = -4.66 in units of GeV^{-1} and f = 88 MeV.

## Context

This work revisits the long-standing question of pion condensation in neutron stars, first proposed by Migdal (1971) and Sawyer (1972), extending earlier studies that focused on p-wave pion condensation. The authors use recent experimental constraints from deeply bound pionic atoms in Pb and Sn isotopes (Gilg 1999, Itahashi 1999, Suzuki 2002) and low-energy pion-nucleus scattering, as well as theoretical chiral perturbation theory calculations (Thorsson 1995, Meissner 2001, Kolomeitsev 2002). The results contradict earlier suggestions from Glendenning and IOTSY that mu_e could exceed the vacuum pion mass at densities 1-5 times saturation density, and show that hyperon inclusion (as in Schaffner and IOTSY models) suppresses mu_e enough to prevent condensation. The work calls for improved theoretical treatment of the energy-dependent pion self-energy at high densities and for experimental pionic atom data in neutron-rich nuclei to resolve the 50-100 MeV ambiguity in asymmetric matter.
