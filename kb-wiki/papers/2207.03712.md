# Nuclear geometry at high energy from exclusive vector meson production

> We show that when saturation effects are included one obtains a good description of the exclusive $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ production spectra in ultra peripheral lead-lead collisions as recently measured by the ALICE Collaboration at the LHC. As exclusive spectra are sensitive to the spatial distribution of nuclear matter at small Bjorken-$x$, this implies that gluon saturation effects modify the impact parameter profile of the target as we move towards small $x$. In addition to saturation effects, we find a preference for larger nuclear strong-interaction radii compared to the typical charge radius. We demonstrate the role of finite photon transverse momentum and the interference between the cases for which the role of photon emitter and target are switched between the nuclei. We show that these effects are comparable to the experimental precision for $p_T$-differential cross sections and as such need to be included when comparing to LHC data. Finally, the integrated $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ production cross sections from the LHC and preliminary transverse momentum spectra from RHIC are shown to prefer calculations with fluctuating nucleon substructure, although these datasets would require even stronger saturation effects than predicted from our framework.

## Key claim

When saturation effects are included, exclusive J/ψ production spectra in ultra peripheral lead-lead collisions at the LHC are well described, but the ALICE data require even stronger saturation effects than the framework predicts, and the data prefer larger nuclear strong-interaction radii compared to typical charge radii.

## Method

The authors calculate exclusive J/ψ production in ultra peripheral lead-lead and gold-gold collisions within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, using the dipole picture where a photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark pair that scatters off the target nucleus. The dipole-target scattering amplitude is obtained from Wilson lines sampled from the McLerran-Venugopalan model at x_pom=0.01, then evolved to smaller x by solving the JIMWLK equation with running coupling corrections. The calculation includes the interference effect from both nuclei acting as photon emitters and the non-zero photon transverse momentum, using the equivalent photon approximation with a sharp cutoff at B_min=2R_A.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear radius used: R_A=6.62 fm for Pb and R_A=6.37 fm for Au
- Total coherent J/ψ production cross section at the LHC changes by ~3% when the minimum distance B_min is changed by 10%
- Normalization factor of 0.65 applied to match the full CGC calculation to ALICE data in the second-to-lowest pT^2 bin
- Proton size parameters at initial condition x_pom=0.01: B_p=3 GeV^{-2} (no shape fluctuations), B_qc=3.3 GeV^{-2} and B_q=0.3 GeV^{-2} (with shape fluctuations)
- Magnitude of Q_s fluctuations: sigma=0.7 in the log-normal distribution
- Infrared regulators: m = tilde m = 0.4 GeV
- Coordinate space Lambda_QCD: 0.025 GeV (without nucleon shape fluctuations), 0.040 GeV (with shape fluctuations)
- Strong coupling constant at scale r=1/M_J/psi: alpha_s = 0.14 or 0.16 depending on Lambda_QCD value
- Proportionality constant c between color charge density and saturation scale: c=0.638 (no shape fluctuations), c=0.643 (with shape fluctuations)

## Context

This work extends previous CGC-based studies of exclusive vector meson production in ultra peripheral collisions (Mantysaari 2017) by using a full JIMWLK evolution to describe the geometry evolution, rather than a fixed dipole amplitude. It also incorporates interference effects (Klein 1999, Bertulani 2005) and finite photon transverse momentum (Xing 2020) that were previously neglected. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming measurements from the Electron-Ion Collider and provide constraints on nuclear gluon distributions at small Bjorken-x down to x ~ 10^{-5}.
