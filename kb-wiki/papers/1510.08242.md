# Fluctuations of flow harmonics in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$~TeV from the Glauber model

> In the framework of the Glauber model as implemented in GLISSANDO 2, we study the fluctuations of flow harmonics in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC centre-of-mass energy of 2.76 TeV. The model with wounded nucleons and the admixture of binary collisions leads to reasonable agreement for the ellipticity and triangularity fluctuations with the experimental data from the ATLAS, ALICE, and CMS collaborations, verifying the assumption that the initial eccentricity is approximately proportional to the harmonic flow of charged particles. While the agreement, in particular at the level of event-by-event distributions of eccentricities/flow coefficients in not perfect, it leads to a fair (at the level of a few percent for all centralities except the most peripheral collisions) description of the scaled standard deviation and the F measure which involve the four-particle cumulants. We also discuss the case of quadrangular flow. Computer scripts that generate our results from the GLISSANDO 2 simulations are provided.

## Key claim

The Glauber model, as implemented in GLISSANDO 2, provides a fair description of elliptic and triangular flow fluctuations in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, with agreement at the level of a few percent for the scaled standard deviation and the F measure for all centralities except the most peripheral collisions, contrary to earlier claims that the model fails badly for these observables.

## Method

The authors used Monte Carlo simulations with the GLISSANDO 2 code to generate initial entropy distributions in the transverse plane from a mixed model combining wounded nucleons with a 15% admixture of binary collisions. They compared the event-by-event distributions of eccentricity coefficients epsilon_n from the model to experimental flow coefficient v_n distributions from ATLAS, ALICE, and CMS, assuming linear proportionality v_n = kappa_n * epsilon_n for n=2,3. The analysis included two variants: a mixed model with unit weights and a mixed+Gamma model where source strengths fluctuate according to a Gamma distribution with parameter nu=0.9, calibrated to reproduce multiplicity fluctuations in p+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- The admixture of binary collisions is set to alpha = 0.15 for the LHC energy of sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- The smearing parameter for sources is sigma = 0.4 fm
- The Gamma distribution parameter is nu = 0.9, giving sigma(w) = 1/sqrt(nu) = 1.054
- The total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section is 64 mb for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- The excluded distance for nucleon configurations is d = 0.9 fm
- For p+Pb multiplicity calibration, the negative binomial parameters are lambda = 9.3 and sigma(n) = 10.3
- For the most central events, sigma(epsilon_n)/langle epsilon_n rangle approaches sqrt(4/pi - 1)
- Agreement holds at the level of a few percent for all centralities except the most peripheral collisions (N_w less than 100)

## Context

This work extends previous Glauber model studies of flow fluctuations (Niemi 2012, Renk 2014, Fu 2015) and directly corrects claims from ATLAS publications (Aad 2013, Aad 2014) that the Glauber model fails badly for flow fluctuations. The results verify that the initial eccentricity is approximately proportional to harmonic flow for n=2 and n=3, and suggest that quadrangular flow receives its bulk contribution from linear response to epsilon_4 rather than from nonlinear epsilon_2^2 contributions. The authors provide computer scripts for GLISSANDO 2 to allow reproduction and extension of their results.
