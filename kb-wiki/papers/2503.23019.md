# Separating non-collective effects in d-Au collisions

> In this letter, we present the multiplicity and yields of charged hadrons and particle ratios in d-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 200$ GeV using the PYTHIA8/Angantyr. The model reproduces the multiplicity ($N_{ch}$) and pseudo-rapidity distribution reasonably well in minimum-biased d-Au collisions without assuming the formation of a thermalized medium. The invariant yield from Angantyr underpredicts the data in central collisions. We discussed the similarity between the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and data/MC from Angantyr and the possibility of using it as an alternative to check in-medium effects. The data/MC suggests that the central d-Au collisions exhibit signals like baryon enhancement, but no high $p_T$ suppression was found. The d-Au collisions have a much smaller invariant yield than the thermal model calculation for similar $N_{part}$. Based on the available data and simulation results, we argue that a medium may form in the central d-Au collision, but it differs from the perfect QGP formed in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that central d-Au collisions at 200 GeV may form a medium, but one that differs from the perfect quark-gluon plasma observed in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, based on comparisons between PYTHIA8/Angantyr simulations and experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA8/Angantyr event generator with a spatially constrained color reconnection mechanism to simulate 50 million minimum-biased d-Au events at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They compared the simulated charged hadron multiplicity, pseudorapidity distributions, and transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons against STAR, PHOBOS, and PHENIX experimental data. They also introduced a data/MC ratio as an alternative to the nuclear modification factor R_AA to separate non-collective from collective effects.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity N_ch ranges for centrality classes in pseudorapidity interval -3.8 < eta < -2.8: 0-20% centrality has N_ch > 17, 20-40% has N_ch 11-17, 40-60% has N_ch 7-11
- The spatial constraint parameter "ColourReconnection:dipoleMaxDist" was changed from 0.5 to 0.55 to improve multiplicity description
- StringFlav:probStoUD was set to 0.3 and StringFlav:probQQtoQ was set to 0.068 to match pp collision yields at 200 GeV
- 50 million minimum biased d-Au events were simulated
- 5 x 10^5 events were simulated using the HYDJET++ model for peripheral Au-Au collisions at impact parameter b = 1.92 R_Au

## Context

This work extends studies of small collision systems (d-Au, He-Au) that show QGP-like signals despite small system size, using the Angantyr model (Bierlich 2018) which simulates heavy ion events without assuming QGP formation. The spatially constrained color reconnection mechanism (Lonnblad 2023) enhances baryon production through string junctions. The paper compares with the AMPT model (Lin 2003) and HIJING model (Gyulassy 1994) for pseudorapidity distributions, and with HYDJET++ (Lokhtin 2008) thermal model calculations for yield comparisons. The data/MC approach is compared to R_AA studies in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (Singh 2021, ALICE 2013). The work enables future studies using Angantyr as a baseline tool instead of scaled pp results to check in-medium effects in small systems.
