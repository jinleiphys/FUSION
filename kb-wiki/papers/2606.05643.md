# $γW$-exchange correction beyond the forward-angle limit in neutron $β$ decay

> In this work, we discuss the $γW$-exchange contributions in neutron $β$ decay with an elastic intermediate state, beyond the forward-angle limit (FAL). By decomposing the one-$W$-exchange and $γW$-exchange amplitudes in terms of $16$ independent Pauli-spinor structures, we calculate the $γW$-exchange corrections to the relevant coefficients. Our numerical results show that the relative corrections to the Fermi Born term $C_{\text{Born}}^{\text{F}}$ and the Gamow-Teller Born term $C_{\text{Born}}^{\text{GT}}$ are enhanced by about 8\% and 18\%, respectively. In particular, we find a non-zero contribution to $C_{\text{Born}}^{\text{GT}}$ from the axial-vector current, which is identically zero in the FAL. The corresponding effect on the extracted $V_{ud}$ from the neutron lifetime is also analyzed, and we find the correction to be at the $10^{-4}$ level.

# γW-Exchange Correction Beyond the Forward-Angle Limit in Neutron β Decay

## Key claim

The authors show that the forward-angle limit (FAL) approximation, which assumes negligible electron energy and momentum transfer, underestimates the γW-exchange Born corrections to neutron β decay by about 8% for the Fermi term and 18% for the Gamow-Teller term. Crucially, a non-zero contribution to the Gamow-Teller Born term from the axial-vector current is found, which is identically zero in the FAL and has been neglected in all previous studies.

## Method

The one-W-exchange and γW-exchange amplitudes for neutron β decay with an elastic nucleon intermediate state are decomposed into 16 independent Pauli-spinor structures, extending the amplitude-level framework of Cao (2025) beyond the forward-angle limit. The γW-exchange coefficients are computed numerically using FeynCalc, Package-X, and LoopTools, with nucleon electromagnetic form factors fitted from experimental data and the axial-vector form factor parametrized following Towner (1992). The corrections to the Born coefficients C_Born^F and C_Born^GT are extracted by evaluating the coefficients at the kinematic midpoint (E_e = 1.5 m_e, n_e·n_ν = 0), justified by the near-linear energy dependence and symmetric angular dependence of the δ_i coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Relative correction to the Fermi Born term C_Born^F: enhanced by about 8% (from 0.951 to 1.022)
- Relative correction to the Gamow-Teller Born term C_Born^GT: enhanced by about 18% (from 1.277 to 1.507)
- Non-zero axial-vector contribution to C_Born^GT: C_Born^GT,A = 0.240 (identically zero in the FAL)
- Vector-plus-magnetic part of C_Born^GT: changes slightly from 1.277 to 1.267 (0.444 + 0.823)
- Shift in extracted V_ud from neutron lifetime: about -0.00012 (from 0.97433 to 0.97420)
- Particle masses used: m_n = 939.56542 MeV, m_p = 938.27209 MeV, m_e = 0.51100 MeV
- Axial coupling: g_A = -1.26; axial form factor cutoff: Λ_W = 1.09 GeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework for γW-box contributions developed by Seng (2018, 2019, 2021, 2024) and the Born-level calculations of Cao (2025), which were all performed in the forward-angle limit. The FAL was conventionally assumed in earlier treatments by Towner (1992), Hayen (2021), and Seng (2023). The finding of a non-zero axial-vector contribution to the Gamow-Teller Born term resolves a limitation of the FAL and provides the theoretical foundation for next-generation neutron decay experiments aiming at 10^-4 precision in V_ud. The next step requires extending the beyond-FAL treatment to the non-Born γW sector (inelastic hadronic intermediate states), which are currently still taken from Seng (2023, 2024) in the FAL.
