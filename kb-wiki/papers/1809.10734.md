# Cracking the difference of estimating heavy quark transport coefficients in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Heavy flavor observables provide valuable information on the properties of the hot and dense Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) created in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. Various microscopic models have successfully described many of the observables associated with its formation. Their transport coefficients differ, however, due to different assumptions about the underlying interaction of the heavy quarks with the plasma constituents, different initial geometries and formation times, different hadronization processes and a different time evolution of the QGP. In this study we present the transport coefficients of all these models and investigate systematically how some of these assumptions influence the heavy quark properties at the end of the QGP expansion. For this purpose we impose on these models the same initial condition and the same model for the QGP expansion and show that both have considerable influence on $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$.

## Key claim
By imposing identical initial conditions and the same QGP expansion model across six different heavy-quark transport models, the authors show that both the initial geometry and the medium evolution model have considerable influence on the extracted heavy-quark nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\), independent of the microscopic interaction mechanism.

## Method
The authors extracted charm-quark drag coefficients \(\eta_D\) and momentum transport coefficients \(\kappa_L, \kappa_T\) from six microscopic models (PHSD, Catania-pQCD, Catania-QPM, Nantes, CCNU-LBT, Duke) and inserted them into a standard Langevin evolution framework. They then propagated charm quarks through a (2+1)D hydrodynamical medium (VISHNU) using fixed initial conditions from either PHSD or averaged trento, and compared the resulting charm-quark \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) at the end of the QGP phase (\(T_c = 0.155\) GeV) for AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) A GeV.

## Key numbers
- Drag coefficients \(\eta_D\) from all models show a monotonously rising temperature dependence and a decrease for increasing momentum; the Nantes coefficients have the largest gradient in the high-temperature and low-momentum region.
- The Duke transport coefficients feature an isotropy assumption and have the smallest absolute values among the models.
- For the LBT model, a non-constant \(K\)-factor parametrized as \(K = 1 + K_p \exp(-p^2/2\sigma_p^2)\) produces a non-trivial peak in \(\kappa_L\) and \(\kappa_T\) in the low-momentum region.
- In a static medium at \(T = 0.3\) GeV and fixed momentum \(p = 10\) GeV, the gluon radiation contribution (difference between solid and dashed lines in Fig. 5) becomes significant at higher momenta and at temperatures observed at the beginning of the QGP expansion.
- Charm quarks starting from an averaged trento initial condition develop a larger \(v_2\) at the end of the QGP phase than those from PHSD initial conditions, for the same Duke transport coefficients.

## Context
This work extends the systematic comparison of charm-quark transport coefficients in a static medium by the JET-HQ collaboration (Cao 2018) and the broader investigation of heavy-quark evolution modeling components by the EMMI framework (Rapp 2018). By controlling for initial conditions and medium evolution, it isolates the contribution of these components from the intrinsic interaction mechanisms, enabling future studies to disentangle the effects of hadronization and pre-equilibrium dynamics on extracted transport coefficients.
