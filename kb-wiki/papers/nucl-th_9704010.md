# Transverse Photon Spectrum from QGP Fluid

> We calculate the thermal photon distribution from the hot QCD matter produced by high energy nuclear collisions based on a hydrodynamical model, and compare it with the recent experimental data obtained by CERN WA80. Through the asymptotic value of the slope parameter of the transverse momentum distribution, we investigate the characteristic temperature of the QCD fluid.

# Transverse Photon Spectrum from QGP Fluid

## Key claim
The thermal photon distribution from a hydrodynamical model of hot QCD matter can reproduce the CERN WA80 S+Au 200 GeV/nucleon data only when a QGP fluid model with a phase transition is used, while a hot hadron gas model without phase transition fails to match both the absolute value and slope of the experimental spectrum.

## Method
The authors first fixed hydrodynamical model parameters by fitting the hadronic spectrum (pseudo-rapidity distribution and transverse momentum distribution of charged hadrons and neutral pions) to CERN WA80 data. They then computed the thermal photon production rate from a unit space-time volume using a semi-phenomenological approach based on a quantum Langevin equation, and integrated this rate over the full space-time evolution of the hydrodynamical source to obtain the photon momentum distribution for comparison with WA80 photon data.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature for QGP fluid model with phase transition: $T_i = 195$ MeV
- Critical temperature for QGP fluid model: $T_c = 160$ MeV
- Freeze-out temperature for both models: $T_f = 140$ MeV
- Initial temperature for hot hadron gas model without phase transition: $T_i = 400$ MeV
- Maximum effective temperature from QGP fluid model (QGP phase): $T_{eff} = 280.8$ MeV at $T = 157.5$ MeV, $v_T = 0.53$, $v_L = 0.11$
- Slope parameter at $k_T = 20$ GeV for QGP fluid model (QGP phase): $T_s = 273.8$ MeV
- Slope parameter at $k_T = 20$ GeV for QGP fluid model (hadron phase): $T_s = 273.5$ MeV
- Slope parameter at $k_T = 20$ GeV for hot hadron gas model: $T_s = 397.0$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous hydrodynamical analysis of charged hadron pseudo-rapidity distributions (Muroya, 1995) to include thermal photon emission, and compares with the production rate calculations of Kapusta and collaborators. The key finding is that the asymptotic slope parameter of the transverse photon spectrum reflects the critical temperature in the phase transition model but the initial temperature in the hot hadron gas model, providing a potential observable to distinguish between these scenarios. The analysis enables future studies to use thermal photons as a probe of the early-stage temperature and phase structure of QCD matter produced in relativistic nuclear collisions.
