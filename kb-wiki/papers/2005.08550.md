# Two-photon decays and transition form factors of $Ď€^0$, $Î·$, and $Î·'$ in large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory

> We present a calculation of $P\to Îł^{(\ast)}Îł^{(\ast)}$ processes, where $P=Ď€^0,\ Î·,\ Î·'$, at the one-loop level up to and including next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory. The results are numerically evaluated successively at LO, NLO, and NNLO. The appearing low-energy constants are determined through fits to the available experimental data. We investigate the decay widths to real photons, the single-virtual transition form factors, and the widths of $P\toÎłl^+l^-$, where $l=e,\ ÎĽ$. Furthermore, we provide results for the slopes and curvatures of the transition form factors.

## Key claim

The paper calculates two-photon decays and transition form factors of the light pseudoscalar mesons \(\pi^0\), \(\eta\), and \(\eta'\) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in large-\(N_c\) chiral perturbation theory, finding that the NNLO corrections are generally small for the \(\pi^0\) but more significant for the \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\), with the final results depending on fits to experimental data for the low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors perform a one-loop calculation of the \(P \to \gamma^{(\ast)}\gamma^{(\ast)}\) amplitude in large-\(N_c\) chiral perturbation theory, organized in a combined expansion in momenta, quark masses, and \(1/N_c\) with a collective expansion parameter \(\delta\). They construct the effective Lagrangian from the Wess-Zumino-Witten action at leading order and include odd-intrinsic-parity Lagrangians at NLO and NNLO, as well as normal-parity Lagrangians up to NNLO. The \(\eta\)-\(\eta'\) mixing is treated at NNLO, and the low-energy constants are determined through fits to experimental data on decay widths and transition form factors.

## Key numbers

*   \(\Gamma(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma) = 7.93(17)\ \text{eV}\) at NNLO, compared to the experimental value \(7.80(12)\ \text{eV}\).
*   \(\Gamma(\eta \to \gamma\gamma) = 0.516(21)\ \text{keV}\) at NNLO, compared to the experimental value \(0.516(18)\ \text{keV}\).
*   \(\Gamma(\eta' \to \gamma\gamma) = 4.21(18)\ \text{keV}\) at NNLO, compared to the experimental value \(4.35(14)\ \text{keV}\).
*   Slope parameter \(b_{\pi^0} = 2.99(11)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) for the \(\pi^0\) transition form factor at NNLO.
*   Slope parameter \(b_{\eta} = 1.92(10)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) for the \(\eta\) transition form factor at NNLO.
*   Slope parameter \(b_{\eta'} = 1.72(12)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) for the \(\eta'\) transition form factor at NNLO.
*   \(\Gamma(\pi^0 \to \gamma e^+e^-) = 6.46(15)\ \text{eV}\) at NNLO.
*   \(\Gamma(\eta \to \gamma e^+e^-) = 0.232(10)\ \text{eV}\) at NNLO.
*   \(\Gamma(\eta' \to \gamma e^+e^-) = 2.06(9)\ \text{eV}\) at NNLO.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of pseudoscalar two-photon decays in large-\(N_c\) chiral perturbation theory by Kaiser and Leutwyler (2000) from next-to-leading order to next-to-next-to-leading order, and provides a systematic treatment of the \(\eta\)-\(\eta'\) mixing at NNLO following Bickert and Scherer (2016). The results are relevant for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, where pseudoscalar-exchange diagrams play a significant role, and enable improved theoretical predictions for the transition form factors needed in that context.
