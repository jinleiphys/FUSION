# Landau Pomeranchuk Midgal Effect and Charm Production in $pp$ Collisions at Large Hadron Collider Energies using the Parton Cascade Model

> We study the impact of the Landau Pomeranchuk Midgal (LPM) effect on the dynamics of parton interactions in proton proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energies. For our investigation we utilize a microscopic kinetic theory based on the Boltzmann equation. The calculation traces the space-time evolution of the cascading partons interacting via semihard pQCD scatterings and fragmentations. We focus on the impact of the LPM effect on the production of charm quarks, since their production is exclusively governed by processes well described in our kinetic theory. The LPM effect is found to become more prominent as the collision energy rises and at central rapidities and may significantly affect the model's predicted charm distributions at low momenta.

## Key claim

The Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, which suppresses gluon radiation when the formation time of the radiated gluon exceeds the mean free path, significantly reduces the number of parton scatterings, fragmentations, and charm quarks produced in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies; however, even after this suppression, the remaining multiple parton interactions are still substantial enough to indicate the formation of an interacting medium.

## Method

The authors used the Parton Cascade Model (PCM), a microscopic kinetic theory based on the relativistic Boltzmann equation, to simulate the space-time evolution of partons in proton-proton collisions. They included 2-to-2 scatterings among light quarks, heavy quarks, and gluons, as well as 2-to-3 reactions via time-like branchings of final-state partons in a leading logarithmic approximation, with a lower momentum-transfer cutoff of 2 GeV and a running coupling constant alpha_s(Q^2). The LPM effect was implemented by assigning a formation time tau = omega / k_T^2 to each radiated particle, during which the radiated particle has zero cross-section and is removed if the emitter undergoes another interaction.

## Key numbers

- Reduction in number of collisions due to LPM effect: about 2% at 200 GeV, rising to almost 80% at 13.00 TeV (for minimum bias pp collisions with p_T cutoff of 2 GeV)
- Reduction in number of fragmentations due to LPM effect: about 2% at 200 GeV, rising to about 70% at 13.00 TeV
- Reduction in charm quark production due to LPM effect: about 1% at 200 GeV, rising to about 60% at 13.00 TeV
- With LPM effect included, collisions involving primary and secondary partons account for about 2% of total collisions at 200 GeV, increasing to about 45% at 13.00 TeV
- The lower momentum-transfer cutoff used was p_T cutoff = 2 GeV; earlier work used 0.7 GeV
- The virtuality cutoff for terminating time-like branchings was mu_0 = 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study using the Parton Cascade Model (Srivastava 2018) which indicated formation of a medium in pp collisions, and earlier PCM studies of the LPM effect on light partons and photons in gold-gold collisions at RHIC energy (Renk 2005, Bass 2002, Bass 2007). The results are compared with Fixed Order Next to Leading Log (FONLL) calculations (Cacciari 1998) and experimental data from LHCb and ALICE at 5.02, 7.00, and 13.00 TeV. The findings suggest that multiple scatterings provide additional contributions beyond leading-log approximations, and enable future investigation of the dependence on parameters such as charm quark mass, p_T cutoff, and mu_0.
