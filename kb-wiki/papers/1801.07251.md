# Valence-quark structure N* resonances from DSEs

> We present a unified Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD)-based description of elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors involving the nucleon and its resonances. We compare predictions made using a framework built upon a Faddeev equation kernel and interaction vertices that possess QCD-like momentum dependence with results obtained using a confining, symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector$\,\otimes\,$vector contact-interaction in a widely-used leading-order (rainbow-ladder) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. This comparison explains that the contact-interaction framework produces hard form factors, curtails some quark orbital angular momentum correlations within a baryon, and suppresses two-loop diagrams in the elastic and transition electromagnetic currents. Such defects are rectified in our QCD-based approach and, by contrasting the results obtained for the same observables in both theoretical schemes, shows those objects which are most sensitive to the momentum dependence of elementary quantities in QCD.

## Key claim

A QCD-based Dyson-Schwinger equation framework, using a Faddeev equation kernel with QCD-like momentum dependence, can provide a unified description of elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors for the nucleon, Delta, and Roper resonances, but the comparison with a contact-interaction model shows that the latter produces hard form factors, curtails quark orbital angular momentum correlations, and suppresses two-loop diagrams, defects that are rectified in the QCD-based approach.

## Method

The authors solved a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for baryons, incorporating dressed-quark propagators, diquark correlation amplitudes, and interaction vertices with QCD-like momentum dependence. They computed elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors for the nucleon, Delta(1232), and Roper(1440) resonances, and compared these predictions with results from a confining, symmetry-preserving vector⊗vector contact-interaction model in rainbow-ladder truncation.

## Key numbers

- The dressed-quark mass function yields an effective constituent-like quark mass of around 350 MeV at low energies.
- The dominant scalar-diquark component of the nucleon Faddeev amplitude, ψ0, produces 62% of each bound-state's normalization for both the proton and charged-Roper.
- Meson-cloud effects provide up to 35% of the magnetic transition form factor for γ*N→Δ(1232) at x = Q²/m_N² ≲ 2.
- The QCD-based prediction for the γ*N→N(1440) Dirac transition form factor agrees with data on x ≳ 2, while meson-cloud contributions fall to just 20% of their maximum value by x=2.
- The d-quark contribution to the γp→R⁺ Pauli transition form factor satisfies |κ_d*/κ_u*| < 1/6, compared to a value of ~2/5 in the elastic case.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous unified studies of nucleon, Delta, and Roper electromagnetic form factors (Segovia 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016) using Dyson-Schwinger equations. It contrasts a QCD-based framework with a contact-interaction model to identify which observables are most sensitive to the momentum dependence of QCD's running coupling and masses. The results enable future validation through electroproduction experiments on neutron targets and provide a baseline for separating dressed-quark core contributions from meson-cloud effects, which are known to screen baryon cores (Tiator 2003, Kamano 2013).
