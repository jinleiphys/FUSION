# How important is the three-nucleon force?

> By calculating the contribution of the $π-π$ three-body force to the three-nucleon binding energy in terms of the $πN$ amplitude using perturbation theory, we are able to determine the contribution of the different $πN$ partial waves to the three-nucleon force. The division of the $πN$ amplitude into a pole and nonpole gives a unique procedure for the determination of the $πNN$ form factor in the model. The total contribution of the three-body force to the binding energy of the triton is found to be very small.

## Key claim

The total contribution of the π-π three-body force to the triton binding energy is very small (less than 50 keV) when the energy dependence of the πN amplitude and the dressed πNN form factor are included, contrary to the expectation that such a force would bridge the gap between calculated and experimental binding energies.

## Method

The authors used perturbation theory to calculate the contribution of the π-π three-body force to the triton binding energy, expressing the force in terms of the off-energy-shell πN amplitude. They divided the πN amplitude into a pole and nonpole contribution, using the nonpole part for the three-body force and the pole part to define the dressed πNN form factor, with the triton wave function taken from an 18-channel Faddeev equation solution for the Paris-Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (PEST) potential.

## Key numbers

- The triton binding energy from the PEST potential alone is 7.318 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 8.48 MeV
- The total contribution of the three-body force from all πN partial waves is less than 50 keV, with the exact result for potential PJ being -2.3 keV
- The largest partial wave contributions come from P11 (-8.8 keV), P33 (-16.0 keV), and S31 (26.4 keV) for potential PJ
- Fixing the energy in both the πN amplitude and dressed πNN form factor to the nucleon mass increases the total contribution to -36.0 keV (a factor of about 10)
- Taking the πN energy at threshold increases the total contribution to -80.9 keV
- Replacing dressed form factors with a monopole form factor of cutoff mass 800 MeV gives a total contribution of -3897.3 keV, two orders of magnitude larger
- The S-wave πN interaction has scattering lengths such that a1+2a3 = -0.011 mπ^{-1}, consistent with current algebra

## Context

This work extends the debate between the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) group approach (Coon et al., 1979; Coon and Gloeckle, 1981) which emphasizes current algebra and soft pion theorems, and the NN-NΔ coupled channel approach (Hajduk et al., 1983; Sauer, 1986) which focuses on the Δ(1230) resonance. The paper shows that the energy dependence of the πN amplitude and the dressed πNN form factor, constrained by experimental πN data, suppresses the three-body force contribution by a factor of approximately 40 compared to approximations that neglect this dependence, and that the cancellation between repulsive S31 and attractive P11 and P33 contributions further reduces the total. This result suggests that the three-nucleon force alone cannot resolve the triton binding energy discrepancy, and that a more exact treatment is not warranted since higher order contributions cannot substantially change the magnitude.
