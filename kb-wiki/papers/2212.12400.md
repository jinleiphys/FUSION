# Implications of gradient flow on the static force

> We use gradient flow to compute the static force based on a Wilson loop with a chromoelectric field insertion. The result can be compared on one hand to the static force from the numerical derivative of the lattice static energy, and on the other hand to the perturbative calculation, allowing a precise extraction of the $Λ_0$ parameter. This study may open the way to gradient flow calculations of correlators of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields, which typically arise in the nonrelativistic effective field theory factorization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that gradient flow can be used to compute the static force directly from a Wilson loop with a chromoelectric field insertion, and that this method allows a precise extraction of the \(\Lambda_0\) parameter in pure gauge theory, provided the flow time is large enough to suppress discretization artifacts (i.e., \(\sqrt{8\tau_F}/a \gtrsim 1\)).

## Method

The authors generate quenched SU(3) gauge configurations on four lattices with spacings from 0.060 fm down to 0.030 fm. They compute the static force by measuring a Wilson loop with a chromoelectric field insertion at flow time \(\tau_F\), using the Symanzik improved action for the gradient flow evolution. The renormalization constant \(Z_E(\tau_F)\) is determined non-perturbatively by comparing the flowed force to the numerical derivative of the static energy at the same flow time. The static force is then extracted via AIC-based model averaging over plateau ranges in the temporal extent \(T\), and continuum limits are performed at fixed flow time or fixed flow time ratio.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacings used: \(a = 0.060, 0.046, 0.040, 0.030\) fm, with 6000 configurations for the three coarser lattices and 2700 for the finest.
* The renormalization constant \(Z_E\) approaches 1 when the flow radius satisfies \(\sqrt{8\tau_F} \gtrsim a\).
* For large \(r\), a Cornell fit \(r^2F(r) = A + \sigma r^2\) yields string tension \(\sigma\) between \(5.18\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\) and \(5.23\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\), and \(A\) between \(0.2853\) and \(0.2954\).
* At small \(r\), fits of the perturbative force to data at fixed \(r\) give \(\Lambda_0\) in the range \(0.238\) to \(0.256\) GeV.
* Zero-flow-time extrapolation of \(\Lambda_0\) from fixed-flow-time fits yields \(\Lambda_0 = 0.262\) GeV (scale \(\mu=1/r\)) and \(\Lambda_0 = 0.261\) GeV (scale \(\mu=1/\sqrt{r^2+8\tau_F}\)).
* Using AIC model averaging over \(r\)-ranges, the zero-flow-time extrapolated \(\Lambda_0\) values are 0.297 GeV (1-loop), 0.264 GeV (2-loop), and 0.253 GeV (3-loop with ultrasoft logs resummed).
* The result \(\Lambda_0 = 0.253\) GeV is consistent with the value \(0.251(12)\) GeV from the static potential analysis of Brambilla (2010).

## Context

This work extends the direct lattice measurement of the static force introduced by Brambilla (2021) by incorporating gradient flow, which simplifies renormalization and improves the signal. The perturbative flowed force at 1-loop order was calculated by Brambilla (2021) in the continuum; the present paper provides the first lattice implementation and compares to that calculation. The method enables future gradient flow calculations of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic field correlators that appear in nonrelativistic effective field theory factorization, and the extracted \(\Lambda_0\) is consistent with the FLAG average of 261(15) MeV and with the pure gauge result of Brambilla (2010).
