# Postmerger multimessenger analysis of binary neutron stars: Effect of the magnetic field strength and topology

> The oscillation modes of neutron star (NS) merger remnants, as encoded by the kHz postmerger gravitational wave (GW) signal, hold great potential for constraining the as-yet undetermined equation of state (EOS) of dense nuclear matter. Previous works have used numerical relativity simulations to derive quasi-universal relations for the key oscillation frequencies, but most of them omit the effects of a magnetic field. We conduct full general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamics simulations of NSNS mergers with two different masses and two different EOSs (SLy and ALF2) with three different initial magnetic field topologies (poloidal and toroidal only, confined to the interior, and "pulsar-like": dipolar poloidal extending from the interior to the exterior), with four different magnetic field strengths with maximum values ranging from from $5.5\times 10^{15}G$ to $2.2\times 10^{17}G$ at the time of insertion. We find that magnetic braking and magnetic effective turbulent viscosity drives the merger remnants towards uniform rotation and increases their overall angular momentum loss. As a result, the $f_2$ frequency of the dominant postmerger GW mode shifts upwards over time. The overall shift is up to $\sim 200$Hz for the strongest magnetic field we consider and $\sim 50$Hz for the median case and is therefore detectable in principle by future GW observatories, which should include the magnetic field in their analyses. We also explore the impact of the magnetic field on the postmerger electromagnetic emission, and demonstrate that an extremely large magnetic field, or alternatively a significant shear viscosity mechanism, can cause a supramassive NS remnant to collapse to a BH in less than 100ms and lead to jet formation, although we do not expect the conditions for such an outcome to be realistic.

## Key claim
The magnetic field in binary neutron star merger remnants can cause a detectable upward shift of up to ~200 Hz in the dominant postmerger gravitational wave frequency (f2), but this effect is strongest for magnetic field strengths that are likely unrealistically large; the authors note that the conditions required for magnetic-field-driven collapse of supramassive neutron star remnants to black holes are not expected to be realistic.

## Method
The authors performed full general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (GRMHD) simulations using the Illinois GRMHD code, evolving binary neutron star mergers with two different equations of state (SLy and ALF2) and two different binary ADM masses (2.57 and 2.70 solar masses). Magnetic fields were inserted 1 ms before merger with three different topologies (pulsar-like dipolar poloidal, interior-only toroidal, and interior-only poloidal) at four different maximum field strengths ranging from 5.5e15 G to 2.2e17 G, plus a hydrodynamics-only control case for each system.

## Key numbers
- Maximum upward shift of the dominant postmerger GW f2 frequency: ~200 Hz for the strongest magnetic field considered (initial maximum field strength 2.2e17 G)
- Median upward shift of the f2 frequency: ~50 Hz for the median magnetic field case (initial maximum field strength 5.5e16 G)
- Initial magnetic field strengths tested: 5.5e15 G, 1.4e16 G, 5.5e16 G, and 2.2e17 G at insertion time
- Maximum resolution of simulations: Δx_min ~ 90 m, with 193-224 grid points covering the equatorial diameter of each neutron star
- Supramassive neutron star remnants collapsed to black holes in less than 100 ms for the strongest magnetic field case (2.2e17 G)
- Electromagnetic luminosities from magnetically driven outflows reached ~1e54 erg/s, corresponding to isotropic equivalent gamma-ray luminosities of ~1e54-1e56 erg/s

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Ruiz 2021, Bamber 2024) by systematically varying magnetic field strength and topology across multiple equations of state and binary masses. Prior quasi-universal relations for postmerger GW frequencies (Shibata 2005, Bauswein 2014, Rezzolla 2016) were derived from hydrodynamics-only simulations without magnetic fields. The results show that magnetic fields can alter these frequencies detectably, but the unrealistically large fields required for SMNS collapse to black holes mean that constraints on the neutron star maximum mass based on GW170817 (Ruiz 2017) remain valid unless a more realistic efficient angular momentum loss mechanism is established.
