# The attribute of rotational profile to the hyperon puzzle in the prediction of heaviest compact star

> In this theoretical study, we report an investigation of the equations of state (EoSs) ofhyper-nuclear matter and its composition as a function of density within the framework of effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field model. We have used G2 force parameter along with various hyperon-meson coupling ratios by allowing the mixing and the breaking of SU(6) symmetry to predict the EoSs, keeping the nucleonic coupling constant intact. We have estimated the properties of non-rotating and rapidly rotating configuration of compact stars by employing four different representative sets of equations of state. The obtained results of the mass and radius for the compact stars are compared with the recent mass observations. Further, we have studied the stability and sensitivity of rotational frequency (at sub-millisecond period) on the configuration of the compact stars, because the angular frequency is significantly smaller than the mass-shedding (Keplerian) frequency in slow rotation regime. Moreover, the yield of hyperon as a function of density for various hyperon-meson couplings are also estimated.

## Key claim

The inclusion of hyperons softens the equation of state and reduces the maximum mass of a static compact star by approximately 0.4 solar masses compared to nucleonic matter, which fails to reach the observed 2.0 solar mass threshold; however, rapid rotation at sub-millisecond periods can increase the maximum mass of hyperonic stars enough to potentially resolve this "hyperon puzzle," though the study only examines rotation frequencies significantly below the Keplerian mass-shedding limit.

## Method

The authors used the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) model with the G2 force parameter to compute equations of state for hyper-nuclear matter under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They employed four different sets of hyperon-meson coupling ratios (Sets 1, 1a, 2, and 2a) that allow mixing and breaking of SU(6) symmetry, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static configurations and used the RNS code of Stergioulas and Friedman for axisymmetric stationary rotating configurations.

## Key numbers

- Static nucleonic star maximum mass: 2.04 solar masses (radius 11.02 km)
- Static hyperonic star maximum masses: 1.43, 1.48, 1.51, and 1.54 solar masses for Sets 1, 1a, 2, and 2a respectively (radii 11.02-11.03 km)
- Rotating hyperonic star maximum masses at Keplerian frequency: 2.11, 2.14, 2.17, and 2.19 solar masses for Sets 1, 1a, 2, and 2a respectively (radii 13.35-13.36 km)
- Rotating nucleonic star maximum mass: 2.43 solar masses (radius 13.28 km)
- Nuclear saturation density: 0.15 fm^-3; binding energy per particle at saturation: -16.1 MeV; incompressibility K0: 214.7 MeV; symmetry energy: 36.4 MeV; slope parameter L_sym: 100.7 MeV
- Observed masses used for comparison: J1614-2230 at 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses; J0348+0432 at 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Bhuyan et al., 2013) by adding rotational profiles to the analysis of hyperonic compact stars, addressing the "hyperon puzzle" identified in earlier literature (Sashikumar et al., 2007; Krastev et al., 2008; Oertel et al., 2015; Sharma et al., 2015; Haensel et al., 2016). The results are consistent with prior theoretical predictions that hyperon inclusion reduces maximum mass by about 0.4 solar masses (Ellis et al., 1991; Schaffner et al., 1994; Glendenning, 1991; Long et al., 2012; Zhang et al., 2013; Qi et al., 2015), and the rotational enhancement suggests that rapid rotation may help reconcile hyperonic equations of state with observed massive pulsars, motivating further study of differential rotation effects as proposed by Gondek-Rosinska et al. (2017).
