# Comparing the MARTINI and CUJET models for jet-quenching: Medium modification of jets and jet substructure

> Jets produced by the initial hard scattering in heavy ion collision events lose energy due to interactions with the color-deconfined medium formed around them: the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Jet-medium interactions constitute an important theoretical and experimental field for studies of QGP, and various models with different assumptions have been proposed to describe them. A fair and direct comparison of these models requires that all other aspects of the simulation be fixed, which is achieved in this work by relying on the JETSCAPE framework. We employ JETSCAPE to directly and comprehensively compare two successful energy loss models: CUJET and MARTINI. We compare the models with the results of measurements of jet spectra and substructure observables. With the strong coupling tuned separately, we find that the two models broadly agree with each other in nuclear modification factors for charged hadrons and jets with cone size $R=0.4$. Systematic differences are reported in fragmentation functions, jet shape, and cone size dependent jet $R_{\mathrm{AA}}$.

## Key claim

With the strong coupling tuned separately for each model, MARTINI and CUJET broadly agree on nuclear modification factors for charged hadrons and jets with cone size R=0.4, but systematic differences emerge in fragmentation functions, jet shape, and cone-size-dependent jet R_AA.

## Method

The authors used the JETSCAPE framework to fix all simulation aspects except the low-virtuality energy loss module, enabling a controlled comparison of MARTINI (which implements the AMY formalism to all orders of opacity in an infinite thermal medium) and CUJET (which uses the DGLV formalism to first order in opacity with finite medium size). Both models were embedded in a (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamic simulation with event-averaged initial conditions from TRENTO, using PYTHIA for hard scattering generation and MATTER for high-virtuality in-medium showers, with hadronization performed by the JETSCAPE "colorless" module and jet clustering via the anti-kT algorithm in FASTJET.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron nuclear modification factor R_AA for p_T > 10 GeV agrees between models across centrality bins 0-5%, 20-30%, and 30-40% in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- CUJET tuned with alpha_max = 0.68; MARTINI tuned with alpha_s,0 = 0.3, kappa_r = 1.5, kappa_e = 4.5
- Both models use momentum cut p_cut = 2.0 GeV and Lambda_QCD = 0.2 GeV
- In static brick tests, empirical relations found: Delta_p_rad(CUJET) proportional to p^0 T^3 L^2, Delta_p_rad(MARTINI) proportional to p^1 T^1 L^1
- Quark splitting rates compared at temperatures T = 0.2, 0.3, and 0.4 GeV for initial momenta p_ini = 10 and 100 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of pQCD-based radiative energy loss formalisms in a static QGP brick by Armesto et al. (2011) and the JET Collaboration's global fitting of the scaled jet transport parameter q-hat/T^3 (2013). By incorporating CUJET into JETSCAPE as a Monte Carlo module for the first time, the authors enable direct comparison with MARTINI under identical hydrodynamic backgrounds, initial conditions, and hadronization schemes. The results enable future studies of electromagnetic probes (jet-medium photons) in the second paper of this series, and provide a benchmark for testing other energy loss models within the JETSCAPE framework.
