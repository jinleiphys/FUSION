# Pion properties at finite nuclear density based on in-medium chiral perturbation theory

> The in-medium pion properties, {\it i.e.} the temporal pion decay constant $f_t$, the pion mass $m_π^*$ and the wave function renormalization, in symmetric nuclear matter are calculated in an in-medium chiral perturbation theory up to the next-to-leading order of the density expansion $O(k_F^4)$. The chiral Lagrangian for the pion-nucleon interaction is determined in vacuum, and the low energy constants are fixed by the experimental observables. We carefully define the in-medium state of the pion and find that the pion wave function plays an essential role for the in-medium pion properties. We show that the linear density correction is dominated and the next-leading corrections is not so large at the saturation density, while their contributions can be significant in higher densities. The main contribution of the next-leading order comes from the double scattering term. We also discuss whether the low energy theorems, the Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation and the Glashow--Weinberg relation, are satisfied in nuclear medium beyond the linear density approximation. We find also that the wave function renormalization is enhanced as largely as $50\%$ at the saturation density including the next-leading contribution and the wave function renormalization could be measured in the in-medium $π^0\to γγ$ decay.

## Key claim

The authors calculate in-medium pion properties (temporal decay constant, mass, and wave function renormalization) in symmetric nuclear matter using in-medium chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order in the density expansion \(O(k_F^4)\), finding that the linear density correction dominates at saturation density while next-to-leading corrections become significant only at higher densities, and that the wave function renormalization is enhanced by as much as 50% at saturation density.

## Method

The authors use in-medium chiral perturbation theory with a chiral Lagrangian for pion-nucleon interactions whose low-energy constants are fixed by vacuum experimental observables (such as the \(\pi N\) sigma term \(\sigma_{\pi N} \simeq 45\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the isoscalar \(\pi N\) scattering length \(a^+\)). They evaluate the in-medium pion self-energy, wave function renormalization, and decay constant by calculating Feynman diagrams up to \(O(k_F^4)\) in the Fermi momentum expansion, using the Fermi gas propagator for nucleons in symmetric nuclear matter with equal proton and neutron densities. The in-medium pion mass is obtained from the pole of the two-point function, and the decay constants are extracted from the matrix element of the axial-vector current using the reduction formula with the wave function renormalization factor \(Z\).

## Key numbers

* The linear density correction to the chiral condensate is \(\langle \bar{q} q \rangle^* / \langle \bar{q} q \rangle_0 \approx 1 - 0.35 \rho / \rho_0\), where \(\rho_0\) is normal nuclear density.
* The wave function renormalization is enhanced by 50% at saturation density including the next-to-leading contribution.
* The empirical value of the \(\pi N\) sigma term used is \(\sigma_{\pi N} \simeq 45\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with a recent analysis suggesting \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 59(7)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The isoscalar \(\pi N\) scattering length is \(a^+ = (7.6 \pm 3.1) \times 10^{-3}\ m_\pi^{-1}\) at better than 95% confidence level.
* The self-energy contributions are evaluated up to \(O(k_F^4)\), with explicit expressions given for \(\Sigma_1\) through \(\Sigma_5\) in terms of \(\rho\), \(k_F\), \(f\), \(g_A\), \(c_1\), \(c_2\), \(c_3\), and the functions \(F(x^2)\) and \(G(x^2)\).

## Context

This work extends previous linear density approximations for in-medium pion properties (Thoma 1995) and next-to-leading order calculations of the chiral condensate and decay constant (Lacour 2010) by systematically including the wave function renormalization and double scattering terms up to \(O(k_F^4)\). The results are relevant for interpreting deeply-bound pionic atom data (Itahashi 2000, Geissel 2002, Suzuki 2004) and for testing whether low-energy theorems such as the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation and the Glashow–Weinberg relation hold beyond linear density approximation. The predicted enhancement of wave function renormalization could be measured in the in-medium \(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma\) decay.
