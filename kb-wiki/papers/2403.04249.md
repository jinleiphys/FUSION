# Analytic formula for the proton radioactivity spectroscopic factor

> In the present work, we systematically study the spectroscopic factor of proton radioactivity ($S_p$) with $A>100$ using the deformed two-potential approach (D-TPA). It is found that there is a link between the quadrupole deformation parameter of proton emitter and $S_p$. Based on this result, we propose a simple analytic formula for estimating the spectroscopic factor of proton radioactivity. With the help of this formula, the calculated half-lives of proton radioactivity can reproduce the experimental data successfully within a factor of 2.77. Furthermore, we extend the D-TPA with this formula for evaluating the spectroscopic factor to predict the proton radioactivity half-lives of 12 proton radioactivity candidates whose radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified in NUBASE2020. For comparison, the universal decay law for proton radioactivity (UDLP) and the new Geiger-Nuttall law (NG-N) are also used. It turns out that all of the predicted results are basically consistent with each other.

# Analytic formula for the proton radioactivity spectroscopic factor

## Key claim
The authors propose a simple analytic formula linking the spectroscopic factor of proton radioactivity to the quadrupole deformation parameter of the proton emitter, but the formula is derived from fitting experimental data and shows significant deviations for five specific nuclei (108I, 170Au, 170Au^m, 171Au^m, 177Tl^m) which the authors attribute to proton shell effects near magic numbers.

## Method
The authors used the deformed two-potential approach (D-TPA) to calculate proton radioactivity half-lives for nuclei with A>100, initially assuming a spectroscopic factor of unity. They extracted experimental spectroscopic factors as the ratio of calculated to experimental half-lives, then fitted a piecewise linear function of the quadrupole deformation parameter β2' to these extracted values. The resulting formula was then used to recalculate half-lives and predict those of 12 unquantified candidates from NUBASE2020.

## Key numbers
- The calculated half-lives using the proposed formula reproduce experimental data within a factor of 2.77
- The fitted parameters for the spectroscopic factor formula are: a = -4.353, b = -0.398 (for β2' > 0); c = 1.332, d = -0.479 (for β2' < 0)
- The root-mean-square deviation σ between experimental and calculated half-lives is 0.443 for the D-TPA with the new formula, compared to 1.253 with Sp=1, 0.478 for UDLP, and 0.516 for NG-N
- The σ value of 0.443 represents a reduction of 7.32% compared to UDLP and 14.15% compared to NG-N
- Experimental half-lives span the range from 10^-5 to 10^2 seconds

## Context
This work extends the deformed two-potential approach previously applied to alpha decay by Gui et al. (2022) to proton radioactivity. It builds on the observation by Delion et al. (2021) of a linear decrease of the logarithmic spectroscopic factor versus quadrupole deformation, and improves upon the spherical-emitter formula of Chen et al. (2021) which was unsuitable for deformed proton emitters. The results enable predictions for 12 proton radioactivity candidates from NUBASE2020 whose decays are energetically allowed but not yet quantified.
