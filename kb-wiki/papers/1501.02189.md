# The nature of $Z_b$ states from a combined analysis of $ÎĄ(5S)\rightarrow h_b(mP) Ď€^+ Ď€^-$ and $ÎĄ(5S)\rightarrow B^{(\ast)}\bar B^{(\ast)}Ď€$

> With a combined analysis of data on $ÎĄ(5S)\rightarrow h_b(1P,2P)Ď€^+Ď€^-$ and $ÎĄ(5S)\rightarrow B^{(\ast)}\bar B^{(\ast)}Ď€$ in an effective field theory approach, we determine resonance parameters of $Z_b$ states in two scenarios. In one scenario we assume that $Z_b$ states are pure molecular states, while in the other one we assume that $Z_b$ states contain compact components. We find that the present data favor that there should be some compact components inside $Z_b^{(\prime)}$ associated with the molecular components. By fitting the invariant mass spectra of $ÎĄ(5S)\rightarrow h_b(1P,2P)Ď€^+Ď€^-$ and $ÎĄ(5S)\rightarrow B^{(\ast)}\bar B^{\ast}Ď€$, we determine that the probability of finding the compact components in $Z_b$ states may be as large as about $40\%$.

## Key claim

The present data favor that the $Z_b$ and $Z_b^\prime$ states contain some compact (e.g., tetraquark) components in addition to molecular components, with the probability of finding such compact components possibly as large as about 40%; however, the authors caution that when data on $\Upsilon(5S)\rightarrow h_b(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$ are dropped, the pure molecular scenario and the mixed scenario give almost equal fitting qualities, so a conclusive claim is premature.

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit of the invariant mass spectra from Belle for $\Upsilon(5S)\rightarrow h_b(1P,2P)\pi^+\pi^-$ and $\Upsilon(5S)\rightarrow B^{(\ast)}\bar B^{(\ast)}\pi$ using an effective field theory (EFT) approach that incorporates Weinberg's compositeness theorem. They considered two scenarios: one where $Z_b$ states are pure molecular states (setting the elementary-component probability $Z=0$) and one where $Z_b$ states contain both molecular and compact components (treating $Z$ as a free parameter between 0 and 1). The amplitudes included direct and indirect production mechanisms for the $Z_b$ states, and the fits were performed with and without the $h_b(1P)$ data to test the convergence of the EFT expansion.

## Key numbers

* The probability of finding a compact component in $Z_b^{(\prime)}$ is determined to be about $40\%$ (specifically $Z = 0.42 \pm 0.12$ in fit 1b and $Z = 0.42 \pm 0.18$ in fit 2b).
* In the pure molecular scenario (fit 1a), the binding energy of $Z_b$ is $B = 0.11 \pm 0.12$ eV and of $Z_b^\prime$ is $B^\prime = 27 \pm 58$ keV, with $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 110/58$.
* In the mixed scenario (fit 1b), the binding energy of $Z_b$ is $B = 0.19 \pm 0.22$ eV and of $Z_b^\prime$ is $B^\prime = 1.6 \pm 1.8$ eV, with $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 81/58$.
* The fitted width $\Gamma_{Z_b}$ in fit 1b is $5.5 \pm 1.8$ MeV and $\Gamma_{Z_b^\prime}$ is $7.8 \pm 2.2$ MeV.
* The detector energy resolution used for $\Upsilon(5S)\rightarrow h_b(mP)\pi^+\pi^-$ is $\sigma = 5.2$ MeV and for $\Upsilon(5S)\rightarrow B^{(\ast)}\bar B^{(\ast)}\pi$ is $\sigma = 6$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach of Chen (2013) and the compositeness theorem of Weinberg to the $Z_b$ states, building on earlier molecular interpretations by Cleven (2011) and Mehen (2013) and on the observation that pure molecular states struggle to explain large production rates at $B$-factories (as noted for $X(3872)$ by charm1 and charm2). The analysis enables a quantitative comparison of the molecular versus mixed-composition hypotheses for the $Z_b$ states and suggests that the compact-component probability in $Z_b^{(\prime)}$ (about 40%) may be close to that found for $X(3872)$ (26% to 44% in studies by Meng (2013) and Cardoso (2014)).
