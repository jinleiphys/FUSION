# Solving Potential Scattering Equations without Partial Wave Decomposition

> Considering two-body integral equations we show how they can be dimensionally reduced by integrating exactly over the azimuthal angle of the intermediate momentum. Numerical solution of the resulting equation is feasible without employing a partial-wave expansion. We illustrate this procedure for the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pion-nucleon scattering and give explicit details for the one-nucleon-exchange term in the potential. Finally, we show how this method can be applied to pion photoproduction from the nucleon with $πN$ rescattering being treated so as to maintain unitarity to first order in the electromagnetic coupling. The procedure for removing the azimuthal angle dependence becomes increasingly complex as the spin of the particles involved increases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for two-body scattering systems involving only spin-0 and spin-1/2 particles, the azimuthal-angle dependence of the potential factorizes in the co-linear frame, allowing exact analytic integration over the intermediate azimuthal angle without any partial-wave expansion; the authors note that this procedure becomes increasingly complex as the spin of the particles increases.

## Method

The authors work with the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the helicity basis and show that in the co-linear frame (total three-momentum along the z-axis), the two-particle propagator is independent of the intermediate azimuthal angle, while the fully off-shell potential takes the form \(V_{\lambda'\lambda}(\varphi',\varphi) = e^{-i\lambda'\varphi'} v_{\lambda'\lambda}(\varphi'-\varphi) e^{i\lambda\varphi}\). They expand the azimuthal-dependent parts in Fourier series, obtaining decoupled one-dimensional integral equations for each Fourier component \(m\), and then solve the resulting two-dimensional integral equation numerically using Pade approximants and Gauss-Legendre quadrature. For the pion-nucleon system, they use an instantaneous (Salpeter) approximation with a one-nucleon-exchange potential and compare the full solution to truncated partial-wave series.

## Key numbers

- At pion laboratory energy \(E_{\pi}^{LAB} = 300\) MeV, the full calculation of \(|T_{\frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{2}}^{++}|^2\) as a function of angle is compared with partial-wave truncations; 5 partial waves (\(J = 1/2, \ldots, 9/2\)) reproduce the full result to 1% accuracy.
- The numerical integration uses 16 Gauss points for the momentum integration and 8 points for the polar-angle integration; increasing to 32 and 16 points respectively changes results by less than 0.5% in the considered energy range.
- For the azimuthal integration, at least 16 Gaussian points would be needed if done numerically, which slows the calculation by more than an order of magnitude compared to the analytic method.
- For pion photoproduction at photon energy \(E_{\gamma} = 300\) MeV, the method using analytic azimuthal integration with 2-D numerical integration agrees with multipole-expansion results (using five multipoles) to better than 1% over a wide angular range.

## Context

This work extends the method introduced by Glockle and collaborators (1988) for nucleon-nucleon scattering to the pion-nucleon system and to pion photoproduction, establishing general conditions under which the azimuthal-angle integration can be performed analytically. The approach provides an alternative to the standard partial-wave decomposition method used by Kubis (1972) and others, and is particularly beneficial when many partial waves are needed or when the potential is not spherically symmetric. The method enables solving the full Bethe-Salpeter equation for the \(\pi N\) system (previously solved only in partial waves by Nieland and Tjon (1968) and by Lahiff (1999)) with numerical feasibility comparable to the partial-wave expansion approach.
