# Charge state regulation of nuclear excitation by electron capture in $^{229}$Th ions

> Nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) in $^{229}$Th holds significant potential for precise nuclear state manipulation. In this study, we thoroughly investigate NEEC in $^{229}\text{Th}^{q+}$ ions by integrating quantum numbers ($n, l, j$) effects and analyzing key parameters (e.g., resonance energy $E_r$, cross section $σ$, resonance strength $S$, and NEEC transition width $Γ_{\text{NEEC}}$) influences across charge state from $q=1^+$ to $90^+$. Especially, we focus on the charge-state regulation of the isomeric state (IS, 8.36 eV) and second-excited state (SE, 29.19 keV). Our calculations uncover critical charge-state-dependent behaviors of NEEC in $^{229}\text{Th}$ ions: (1) For the IS, valid NEEC channels exhibit threshold migration, where the dominant principal quantum number $n$ increases linearly with $q$ following the relation $n \approx 1.28q + 4.23$; meanwhile, single-$n$-channel $S$ stabilizes between $10^{-2}$ to $10^0$ barn eV via compensatory nucleus-electron coupling, ensuring the total resonance $S$ constant. (2) For the SE, its excitation energy far exceeds nearly all electron binding energies, leading to negligible channel screening and causing the total $S$ to increase monotonically with $q$. This research clarifies the intrinsic mechanisms of charge-state-driven nuclear-electronic interactions in $^{229}\text{Th}^{q+}$ NEEC and provides a critical reference for future experimental efforts to manipulate $^{229}\text{Th}$ nuclear states, particularly via indirect regulation of the SE.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) in $^{229}$Th ions, the charge state $q$ acts as a regulator with two distinct regimes: for the isomeric state (IS) at 8.36 eV, the total resonance strength remains stable across all charge states due to a compensatory balance between channel screening and enhanced coupling, while for the second-excited state (SE) at 29.19 keV, the total resonance strength grows monotonically with $q$ because the excitation energy far exceeds electron binding energies, eliminating channel screening.

## Method

The authors performed Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater (DHFS) calculations of NEEC cross sections, resonance strengths, and transition widths for $^{229}$Th$^{q+}$ ions across charge states from $q=1^+$ to $90^+$. They computed the radial matrix elements and transition probabilities for electric quadrupole (E2) and magnetic dipole (M1) transitions between the ground state (GS, 5/2$^+$), isomeric state (IS, 3/2$^+$), and second-excited state (SE, 5/2$^+$), systematically varying the principal quantum number $n$, orbital angular momentum $l$, and total angular momentum $j$ of the final-state electron orbital.

## Key numbers

- IS excitation energy: 8.36 eV; SE excitation energy: 29.19 keV
- For the IS, the dominant principal quantum number $n$ follows the linear relation $n \approx 1.28q + 4.23$ across charge states
- Single-$n$-channel resonance strength $S$ for the IS stabilizes between $10^{-2}$ to $10^0$ barn eV across all charge states
- For the SE, total resonance strength $S_{\text{total}}$ grows from $4.65 \times 10^{-6}$ barn eV at $q=1^+$ to $3.47 \times 10^{-1}$ barn eV at $q=90^+$
- IS total resonance strength $S_{\text{total}}$ remains stable in the range $10^{-1}$ to $10^0$ barn eV across all charge states
- SE total resonance strength $S_{\text{total}}$ surpasses that of the IS at $q=87^+$
- For ground-state Th$^{1+}$ with final-state $n=8$, the $p_{3/2} \to 8p_{1/2}$ transition shows the largest cross section at $1.25 \times 10^5$ barn and resonance strength $7.89 \times 10^{-4}$ barn eV
- IS-to-GS reduced transition probabilities: $B$(E2) = 27 W.u., $B$(M1) = 0.0076 W.u.; SE-to-GS: $B$(E2) = 39.49 W.u., $B$(M1) = 0.0043 W.u.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of NEEC in $^{229}$Th that focused on electronic bridge processes (Bilous 2018), nuclear excitation by electron transition (Dzyublik 2011), and inelastic electron scattering (PhysRevLett.124.242501), but had not systematically addressed charge-state dependence. The results provide a quantitative framework for designing experiments to observe NEEC in $^{229}$Th, particularly suggesting that for charge states $q \geq 87^+$, indirect population of the isomeric state via the second-excited state becomes more feasible than direct excitation, which could inform the development of nuclear optical clocks (Thirolf 2019) and tests of fundamental constant variations (PhysRevLett.104.200802).
