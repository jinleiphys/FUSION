# Muon number violating processes in nuclei

> The flavour violating neutrinoless muon decays in the presence of nuclei, are discussed. We focus on the theoretical aspects of $μ^-(A,Z)\to e^-(A,Z)^*$ (muon-to-electron conversion), one of the most prominent flavour changing reactions, emphasizing its connection with the physics beyond the standard model. This process offers the most severe limits of the lepton flavour violation. By using the nuclear transition matrix elements calculated with several methods, and the recent experimental data of the branching ratio $R_{μe^-}$, we determine limits for the flavour changing parameters entering the elementary sector part of $R_{μe^-}$. These results are discussed in view of the ongoing experiment at PSI and the designed at Brookhaven, which are expected to push down by some orders of magnitude the experimental sensitivity the next few years with the hope to see ``new physics''.

## Key claim

The paper argues that muon-to-electron conversion in nuclei provides the most severe experimental limits on lepton flavour violation, but the actual constraints on flavour-violating parameters (such as neutrino mixing matrix elements or supersymmetric mass splittings) depend critically on nuclear structure calculations, which introduce model-dependent uncertainties that the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for muon-to-electron conversion at the nucleon level, separating photonic and non-photonic contributions (including W-boson exchange, Z-exchange, and supersymmetric box diagrams with neutralino and s-lepton mixing). They then derive nuclear transition operators in the non-relativistic limit and compute the branching ratio \(R_{\mu e^-}\) using several nuclear models: state-by-state quasi-particle RPA calculations, a local density approximation mapping nuclear matter onto finite nuclei, and a factorization approximation for the muon wave function. The nuclear matrix elements are combined with experimental upper limits on \(R_{\mu e^-}\) to extract limits on the elementary flavour-violating parameters \(\eta_\nu\), \(\eta_N\), and \(\tilde\eta\).

## Key numbers

* The best experimental upper limit on the branching ratio \(R_{\mu e^-}\) for a titanium target is \(R_{\mu e^-} < 7.0 \times 10^{-13}\) (PSI experiment, Walter).
* For a lead target (\(^{208}\text{Pb}\)), the SINDRUM II experiment set the limit \(R_{\mu e^-} < 4.6 \times 10^{-11}\).
* Earlier limits include: \(R_{\mu e^-} < 5 \times 10^{-4}\) (Steinberger and Wolfe, copper target), \(R_{\mu e^-} < 2.6 \times 10^{-8}\) (Bryman et al., TRIUMF, copper), \(R_{\mu e^-} < 1.6 \times 10^{-8}\) (Badertscher et al., SIN, sulfur target), \(R_{\mu e^-} < 1.6 \times 10^{-11}\) then \(< 4.6 \times 10^{-12}\) (Bryman et al., TRIUMF, titanium).
* For the supersymmetric Z-exchange contribution, the parameter \(\xi\) takes the value \(\xi = 2.8 \times 10^{-2}\) in model 2 of Kosmas and Vergados (1995).
* The axial-to-vector nucleon form factor ratio is \(f_A/f_V = 1.24\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Weinberg and Feinberg (1962), who first formulated the photonic mechanism for muon-to-electron conversion, and incorporates non-photonic contributions developed in the post-gauge-theory era by Marciano and Sanda (1977) and others. The nuclear structure calculations build on prior shell-model and quasi-particle RPA studies by Kosmas, Vergados, and collaborators (1990-1997) for isotopes including \(^{48}\text{Ti}\) and \(^{208}\text{Pb}\), which are the targets of ongoing experiments at PSI and planned experiments at Brookhaven. The results enable extraction of limits on neutrino mixing parameters and supersymmetric mass splittings from experimental data, and the authors note that future experiments with two to three orders of magnitude improved sensitivity may either observe the process or further constrain new physics.
