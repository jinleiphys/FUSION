# Quark meson coupling model with $su(3)$ symmetry within the Bogoliubov independent quark model of the nucleon

> We generalize the Bogoliubov quark-meson coupling model to also include hyperons. The hyperon-$σ$-meson couplings are fixed by the model and the hyperon-$ω$-meson couplings are fitted to the hypreon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter. The present model predicts neutron stars with masses above 2$M_\odot$ and the radius of a 1.4$M_\odot$ star equal to 13.83 km.

## Key claim

The Bogoliubov quark-meson coupling model, when generalized to include hyperons, predicts neutron stars with masses above 2 solar masses and a radius of 13.83 km for a 1.4 solar mass star, but the hyperon-omega-meson couplings are fitted to hyperon potentials that have significant experimental uncertainties, particularly for the Sigma and Xi hyperons.

## Method

The authors generalize the Bogoliubov independent quark model of the nucleon to include strange quarks and hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi) by fixing the hyperon-sigma-meson couplings through the model's quark dynamics and fitting the hyperon-omega-meson couplings to the hyperon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter. The equation of state for neutral hyperonic matter in beta equilibrium is obtained by minimizing the energy density with respect to baryon and lepton Fermi momenta and the scalar field, under constraints of fixed baryon density and charge neutrality. Neutron star masses and radii are then calculated by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.15 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: -15.7 MeV
- Quark-sigma coupling constant g_s^q = 3.982
- Nucleon-omega coupling constant g_omega_N = 9.3001
- Nucleon-rho coupling constant g_rho_N = 8.601
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 32.5 MeV
- Hyperon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter: U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = +30 MeV, U_Xi = -18 MeV
- Omega-meson coupling ratios: x_omega_Lambda = 0.73, x_omega_Sigma = 1.1, x_omega_Xi = 0.52
- Maximum neutron star mass for hyperonic matter: 2.03 solar masses
- Radius of maximum mass hyperonic star: 12.67 km
- Radius of 1.4 solar mass star (hyperonic matter): 13.86 km
- Maximum neutron star mass for pure neutron matter: 2.39 solar masses
- Maximum neutron star mass for nucleonic beta-equilibrium matter: 2.24 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the Bogoliubov quark-meson coupling model, previously applied to symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter by Bohr et al., to include hyperonic degrees of freedom for neutron star studies. It follows the quark-meson coupling approach of Guichon, Saito, and Thomas, and the relativistic mean field models of Walecka, Boguta, and Typel, but incorporates nucleon structure through a confining independent quark potential rather than a bag model. The model enables the study of strangeness in dense matter and suggests that at densities around 0.8 fm^-3, baryonic bags strongly overlap, indicating a possible phase transition to quark matter, consistent with the Bodmer-Witten strange matter hypothesis.
