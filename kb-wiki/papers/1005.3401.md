# Recent developments in modeling neutrino interactions in 1 GeV energy region

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of neutrino interaction modeling in the ~1 GeV region, arguing that the discrepancy between older measurements of the quasielastic axial mass (M_A ~ 1.03 GeV) and newer high-statistics measurements (M_A ~ 1.2-1.35 GeV) may be explained by nuclear effects such as 2p-2h excitations rather than a true change in the free nucleon axial form factor, but this explanation remains conditional on future antineutrino data and detailed comparisons with double differential cross-section measurements.

## Method

The paper surveys experimental measurements and theoretical models for quasielastic (CCQE) reactions, neutral current π⁰ production, and coherent pion production in the ~1 GeV neutrino energy region, with particular attention to the role of nuclear effects and final state interactions. It compares results from multiple experiments (K2K, MiniBooNE, SciBooNE, MINOS, NOMAD) and evaluates the performance of Monte Carlo event generators (GENIE, NEUT, NUANCE, NuWro, FLUKA) against these data, including a comparison project from the NuInt09 workshop.

## Context

This review paper summarizes the state of neutrino interaction physics following the NuInt workshop series, addressing tensions between older deuterium-target measurements of the axial mass (converging near 1.03 GeV) and newer high-statistics measurements from K2K, MiniBooNE, and MINOS that yield larger values. The work extends discussions from earlier NuInt workshops and references specific theoretical proposals such as the many-body model of Marteau and Martini that incorporates 2p-2h excitations to explain the discrepancy. The paper enables future improvements in Monte Carlo generators by identifying specific areas needing better models, particularly coherent pion production (where the Rein-Sehgal model implementation varies by over 100% between generators) and the replacement of the Fermi gas model with spectral function approaches developed by Benhar and collaborators.
