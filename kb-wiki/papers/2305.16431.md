# Model independent analysis of femtoscopic correlation functions: An application to the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$

> We face the inverse problem of obtaining the interaction between coupled channels from the correlation functions of these channels. We apply the method to the interaction of the $D^0 K^+$, $D^+ K^0$, and $D^+_s η$ channels, from where the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ state emerges. We use synthetic data extracted from an interaction model based on the local hidden gauge approach and find that the inverse problem can determine the existence of a bound state of the system with a precision of about 20 MeV. At the same time, we can determine the isospin nature of the bound state and its compositeness in terms of the channels. Furthermore, we evaluate the scattering length and effective range of all three channels, as well as the couplings of the bound state found to all the components. Lastly, the size parameter of the source function, $R$, which in principle should be a magnitude provided by the experimental teams, can be obtained from a fit to the data with relatively high accuracy. These findings show the value of the correlation function to learn about the meson-meson interaction for systems which are difficult to access in other present facilities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the inverse problem of extracting hadron interaction parameters from femtoscopic correlation functions can determine the existence of a bound state (the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\)) with a precision of about 20 MeV, but this result is obtained using synthetic data generated from a specific interaction model (local hidden gauge) rather than from actual experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors generate synthetic correlation functions for the coupled channels \(D^0 K^+\), \(D^+ K^0\), and \(D^+_s \eta\) using a model based on vector-meson exchange from the local hidden gauge approach, regularized with a sharp cutoff \(q_\text{max}\). They then treat these synthetic data as "experimental" and perform a simultaneous fit to all three correlation functions using a flexible potential with eight free parameters (three constant terms \(V'_{ij}\), three energy-dependent coefficients \(\alpha,\beta,\gamma\), the cutoff \(q_\text{max}\), and the source size \(R\)). The resampling method is used to generate 50 pseudo-experiments, from which the average values and dispersions of physical observables (binding energy, scattering lengths, effective ranges, couplings, and channel probabilities) are extracted.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) state: \(E = 2314.2 \pm 21.0\) MeV (corresponding to a binding of about 42 MeV below the \(D^0 K^+\) threshold)
* Source size parameter from fit: \(R = 0.984 \pm 0.040\) fm (input value was 1 fm)
* Scattering length for \(D^0 K^+\) channel: \(a_1 = 0.720 \pm 0.131\) fm
* Scattering length for \(D^+ K^0\) channel: \(a_2 = (0.518 \pm 0.051) - i\,(0.120 \pm 0.030)\) fm
* Scattering length for \(D^+_s \eta\) channel: \(a_3 = (0.213 \pm 0.014) - i\,(0.054 \pm 0.025)\) fm
* Probability of \(D^0 K^+\) component in the bound state: \(P_1 = 0.357 \pm 0.133\)
* Probability of \(D^+ K^0\) component: \(P_2 = 0.306 \pm 0.119\)
* Probability of \(D^+_s \eta\) component: \(P_3 = 0.083 \pm 0.070\)
* Sum of probabilities for the three channels: \(P_1 + P_2 + P_3 \approx 0.75\) (indicating a largely molecular state)
* Cutoff parameter from fit: \(q_\text{max} = 689.03 \pm 103.37\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the methodology of using femtoscopic correlation functions to extract hadron interactions, moving beyond the typical approach of comparing experimental data to specific models (as done by Kamiya (2019) and Kamiya (2022)) toward a model-independent inverse problem. The method is applied to the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) resonance, which has been studied in lattice QCD by Mohler (2013), Lang (2014), Bali (2017), and Cheung (2020), with a detailed analysis by Martinez-Torres (2014) finding a \(DK\) probability of about 72%. The results enable future experimental analyses of correlation functions in the charm sector, particularly from ALICE (2022) which has plans to extend correlation studies to \(D\) mesons, and demonstrate that the source size parameter \(R\) can be determined from the data itself rather than requiring external input from experimental teams.
