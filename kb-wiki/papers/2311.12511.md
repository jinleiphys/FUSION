# Proto-strange quark stars from density-dependent quark mass model

> In this paper, we investigate the evolution of strange quark stars (SQS) from birth as proto-strange quark stars to maturity as stable SQSs at a zero temperature. We assume that self-bound free quarks form {entirely the compact} star and study its evolution {through a series of snapshots} using a density-dependent quark mass model. We consider $β$-equilibrated stellar matter at two major stages of the star's evolution: neutrino trapped regime and neutrino transparent regime during the deleptonization and cooling processes of the star. We fix the entropy density per baryon and the lepton fraction to investigate the nuclear equation of state (EoS), particle distribution, temperature profile inside the star, sound velocity, polytropic index, and the structure of the star. Our results show that stars with higher neutrino concentrations are slightly more massive than the neutrino-poor ones along the evolution lines of the SQS. We obtain EoSs in agreement with the conformal boundary set through sound velocity, and also the 2 M$_\odot$ mass constraint for NSs was satisfied at all stages of the star's evolution.

## Key claim

The authors model proto-strange quark stars (PSS) as being composed entirely of self-bound deconfined strange quark matter from birth, rather than undergoing a phase transition from hadronic matter, and show that such stars can satisfy the 2 M$_\odot$ mass constraint at all evolutionary stages from the neutrino-trapped regime to zero temperature.

## Method

The authors use a density-dependent quark mass model (DDQM) with a cubic-root mass scaling law, fixing model parameters to C = 0.7 and sqrt(D) = 130.6 MeV from a previous Bayesian analysis. They solve the equations of state for beta-equilibrated strange quark matter at fixed entropy per baryon and lepton fraction, considering two regimes: neutrino-trapped matter (with electron neutrinos) and neutrino-transparent matter (with muons). The stellar structure is computed via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for four evolutionary snapshots: S/n_B = 1 with Y_L,e = 0.4, S/n_B = 2 with Y_L,e = 0.2, S/n_B = 2 with Y_nu_e = 0, and T = 0 with Y_nu_e = 0.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of 2.20 M_sun at S/n_B = 1, Y_L,e = 0.4 (neutrino-trapped), with radius 13.86 km and central temperature 8.82 MeV
- Maximum gravitational mass of 2.09 M_sun at T = 0, Y_nu_e = 0 (cold catalyzed), with radius 13.24 km and central baryon density 4.49 n_0
- Central baryon density at maximum mass increases from 3.95 n_0 (S/n_B = 1, Y_L,e = 0.4) to 4.49 n_0 (T = 0)
- Strange quark appearance threshold shifts from n_b ~ 0.66 n_0 (first snapshot) to n_b ~ 0.30 n_0 (third snapshot) to n_b ~ 0.59 n_0 (final T = 0 stage)
- For fixed baryonic mass M_b = 1.85 M_sun, gravitational mass decreases from 1.74 M_sun (S/n_B = 1, Y_L,e = 0.4) to 1.64 M_sun (T = 0)
- Model parameters: current quark masses m_u = 2.16 MeV, m_d = 4.67 MeV, m_s = 93.4 MeV; QCD scale Lambda = 280 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proto-neutron stars with exotic baryons (Issifu 2023, Raduta 2020, Sedrakian 2022) and proto-strange quark stars using other quark models (Chu 2021, Bordbar 2020) by applying the density-dependent quark mass model to the full evolutionary sequence from neutrino-rich birth to cold catalyzed strange quark stars. The model parameters were previously constrained by Bayesian analysis (da Silva 2023) to satisfy mass-radius constraints from NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451. The results enable future studies of the possibility of black hole formation during PSS evolution and provide equations of state for use in supernova and neutron star merger simulations.
