# Similarity between nuclear rainbow and meteorological rainbow -- evidence for nuclear ripples

> We present evidence for the nuclear ripples superimposed on the Airy structure of the nuclear rainbow, which is similar to the meteorological rainbow. The mechanism of the nuclear ripples is also similar to that of the meteorological rainbow, which is caused by the interference between the externally reflective waves and refractive waves. The nuclear ripple structure was confirmed by analyzing the elastic angular distribution in $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C rainbow scattering at $E_L$=115.9 MeV using the coupled channels method by taking account of coupling to the excited states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O with a double folding model derived from a density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon force with realistic wave functions for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O. The coupling to the excited states plays the role of creating the external reflection.

## Key claim

The high-frequency oscillations superimposed on the Airy structure of the nuclear rainbow in \(^{16}\)O+\(^{12}\)C scattering are identified as "nuclear ripples" analogous to the ripple structure in meteorological rainbows, generated by interference between refractive waves and externally reflected waves created by coupling to excited states of the target and projectile.

## Method

The authors performed coupled channels calculations for elastic \(^{16}\)O+\(^{12}\)C scattering at \(E_L\)=115.9 MeV using an extended double folding (EDF) model with microscopic wave functions for \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O from cluster models, and a density-dependent DDM3Y-FR nucleon-nucleon interaction. They included coupling to the \(2^+\) (4.44 MeV) and \(3^-\) (9.64 MeV) states of \(^{12}\)C and the \(3^-\) (6.13 MeV) and \(2^+\) (6.92 MeV) states of \(^{16}\)O, with a phenomenological Woods-Saxon volume-type imaginary potential.

## Key numbers

- Incident laboratory energy: \(E_L\)=115.9 MeV for the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{12}\)C system
- Volume integral per nucleon pair of the ground state diagonal real potential: \(J_V\)=317.7 MeV fm\(^3\)
- Imaginary potential parameters in the coupled channels calculation: \(W_V\)=14 MeV, \(R_W\)=5.6 fm, \(a_W\)=0.20 fm, with volume integral \(J_W\)=54.3 MeV fm\(^3\)
- The ripple structure emerges for partial waves with orbital angular momentum \(L_c\)=14-17, corresponding to impact parameters \(b\)=3.6-4.3 fm, which is significantly smaller than the grazing partial wave \(L\)=24 with impact parameter \(b\)=5.9 fm
- The grazing partial wave \(L\)=24 has \(|S_L|\)=0.5

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear rainbow scattering in heavy-ion systems such as \(^{16}\)O+\(^{16}\)O, \(^{16}\)O+\(^{12}\)C, and \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C (Khoa 2007, Brandan 1997) by revealing a ripple structure not predicted by the semiclassical theory of Ford (1959). Previous analyses of \(^{16}\)O+\(^{12}\)C scattering at energies around 100 MeV (Nicoli 2000, Szilner 2001) required unphysically thin-skinned imaginary potentials with diffuseness around 0.1 fm to reproduce high-frequency oscillations; the present work shows that microscopic channel coupling naturally produces these oscillations without such extreme parameters. The finding enables future studies of nuclear rainbows using realistic coupled channels treatments rather than phenomenological sharp-edged potentials.
