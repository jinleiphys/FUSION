# The Study of Noncollectivity by the Forward-Backward Multiplicity Correlation Function

> We propose a forward-backward multiplicity correlation function $C^N_{FB}$, which is experimentally accessible, to measure the noncollectivity contribution. We find that $C^N_{FB}$ is sensitive to the jet contribution for the particle-rich case. Surprisingly, it will automatically decrease for the particle-rare case. Our study indicates that similar decreasing trend observed previously is mainly driven by particle scarcity instead of jets. The function is studied in Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV with a multiphase transport model (AMPT). We find that the jet fraction is about 10% at transverse momentum ($p_T$) around 2.5 GeV/$c$ and reaches up to 30% at 3.5 GeV/$c$. The implication of this study in the investigation of the noncollectivity contribution in elliptic anisotropy parameter $v_2$ is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the previously observed decreasing trend of forward-backward correlation functions at intermediate transverse momentum is mainly driven by particle scarcity (small number statistics) rather than by jet contributions, contrary to earlier interpretations.

## Method

The authors define a forward-backward multiplicity correlation function \(C^N_{FB}\) and study it using the AMPT string melting model for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV with a parton cross section of 10 mb. They compare the AMPT results to a flow-only simulation where particles are generated with the same transverse momentum distribution but without jets, and they derive an analytical expression for the correlation function in the low-yield regime to isolate the effect of finite number statistics.

## Key numbers

* The jet fraction \(g\) is about 10% at transverse momentum \(p_T\) around 2.5 GeV/\(c\) and reaches up to 30% at 3.5 GeV/\(c\) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.
* The forward-backward multiplicity correlation \(\langle\eta^F\eta^B\rangle\) is approximately 0.013 from fitting the AMPT data.
* For \(p_T\) below 1.4 GeV/\(c\), \(C^N_{FB}\) is independent of \(p_T\) and \(\langle\eta^F\eta^B\rangle\) is about 0.01.
* The analytical formula for the small-number-statistics effect gives \(C_{FB}^N(p_T) = -(1 - N(p_T)/2)^2\) for the particle-rare case.

## Context

This work extends the study of forward-backward elliptic correlation functions by Jingfeng (2009) and Han (2011), which attempted to separate jet contributions from collective flow in heavy-ion collisions. The authors show that the decreasing trend observed in those prior studies is largely an artifact of excluding events with empty bins at high \(p_T\), rather than a clean signature of jets. The proposed multiplicity correlation function \(C^N_{FB}\) provides a method to extract the jet fraction more reliably, and the authors suggest that similar analysis at LHC energies, where particle yields are higher, would help clarify the role of jets versus statistical effects.
