# Renormalization of the isovector $πN$ amplitude in pionic atoms

> The extraction of the isovector s-wave pi N amplitude from pionic atoms is studied with special emphasis on uncertainties and their dependence on the assumptions made regarding the neutron density distributions in nuclei and on the size of the data base used . Only `global' analyses of pionic-atom data reveal a discrepancy between the extracted isovector s-wave pi N amplitude b_1=-0.108\pm0.007 m_π^{-1} and its free pi N counterpart b_1^{free}=-0.0885^{+0.0010}_{-0.0021} m_π^{-1}, where the uncertainty in the neutron densities is included in the error analysis. The role of `deeply bound' pionic atom states is discussed and the reason for failure of these states to provide new information is explained.

# Renormalization of the isovector πN amplitude in pionic atoms

## Key claim
Only global analyses of pionic-atom data across the periodic table reveal a discrepancy between the extracted isovector s-wave πN amplitude b₁ = -0.108 ± 0.007 m_π⁻¹ and its free πN counterpart b₁^free = -0.0885^{+0.0010}_{-0.0021} m_π⁻¹, where the uncertainty in neutron density distributions is included in the error analysis. Deeply bound pionic atom states do not provide sufficiently extensive data to constrain b₁ to the required precision, and claims of a discrepancy based on reduced data sets are unfounded.

## Method
The authors performed global fits of the Ericson-Ericson optical potential to pionic atom data using the Klein-Gordon equation, varying six parameters including the s-wave parameters b₀, b₁, and ReB₀, and the complex p-wave absorption parameter C₀. They systematically varied the neutron density distributions across nuclei using a parameterization relating the difference r_n - r_p to the asymmetry (N-Z)/A, exploring three shape options (skin, halo, and average) and a wide range of the asymmetry coefficient α from 0.3 to 2.7 fm. Fits were performed on data bases of 100 and 120 points across the periodic table, and progressively reduced to smaller subsets including only deeply bound states, to compare the resulting uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Extracted isovector s-wave amplitude from global fits: b₁ = -0.108 ± 0.007 m_π⁻¹, including uncertainty from neutron densities
- Free πN isovector amplitude: b₁^free = -0.0885^{+0.0010}_{-0.0021} m_π⁻¹
- RMF fit parameters for r_n - r_p = α(N-Z)/A + β: α = 1.51 ± 0.07 fm, β = -0.03 ± 0.01 fm
- Antiprotonic atom fit parameters: α = 1.01 ± 0.15 fm, β = -0.04 ± 0.03 fm
- Adopted average α = 1.25 ± 0.25 fm for estimating neutron density uncertainties
- Low-density limit values from fits including Weise's density dependence: b₀ = -0.009 ± 0.007 m_π⁻¹, b₁ = -0.088 ± 0.005 m_π⁻¹, ReB₀ = -0.02 ± 0.03 m_π⁻⁴
- χ² per degree of freedom for global fits: 1.8 for 100 data points with RMF skin densities

## Context
This work extends the long-standing discussion of the "anomalous" s-wave repulsion in pionic atoms, first indicated in 1978 by Batty et al., by providing a comprehensive uncertainty analysis that includes neutron density distributions. The authors directly contradict claims by Geissel et al. (2002) and Suzuki et al. (2002) that deeply bound pionic atom states provide precise constraints on b₁, showing that the Seki-Masutani linearization scheme used in those analyses produces artificially small uncertainties. The results enable future work on medium modifications of the πN amplitude, including Weise's suggestion of density dependence from partial chiral symmetry restoration and the alternative approach by Kolomeitsev et al. (2002) using chiral perturbation theory with gauge invariance.
