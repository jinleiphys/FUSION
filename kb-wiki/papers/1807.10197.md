# Reduced hadronic uncertainty in the determination of $V_{ud}$

> We analyze the universal radiative correction $Δ_R^V$ to neutron and superallowed nuclear $β$ decay by expressing the hadronic $γW$-box contribution in terms of a dispersion relation, which we identify as an integral over the first Nachtmann moment of the $γW$ interference structure function $F_3^{(0)}$. By connecting the needed input to existing data on neutrino and antineutrino scattering, we obtain an updated value of $Δ_R^V = 0.02467(22)$, wherein the hadronic uncertainty is reduced. Assuming other Standard Model theoretical calculations and experimental measurements remain unchanged, we obtain an updated value of $|V_{ud}| = 0.97366(15)$, raising tension with the first row CKM unitarity constraint. We comment on ways current and future experiments can provide input to our dispersive analysis.

## Key claim

The paper claims a new value of the universal radiative correction Δ_R^V = 0.02467(22) and a corresponding |V_ud| = 0.97366(15), which raises tension with first-row CKM unitarity; the abstract glosses over the fact that this result depends on a model-dependent relation between the γW interference structure function and the νp+ν̄p structure function, and that the shift relative to the previous Marciano and Sirlin (2006) result is larger than their quoted theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors express the hadronic γW-box contribution to the universal radiative correction Δ_R^V via a dispersion relation, identifying it as an integral over the first Nachtmann moment of the γW interference structure function F_3^(0). They parameterize F_3^(0) using a Born elastic contribution, a parton-model plus pQCD contribution at high Q^2, a chiral perturbation theory plus form-factor single-pion production near threshold, and a Regge-exchange model with vector meson dominance at high W^2. To constrain the Regge coefficient, they fit the analogous structure function F_3^(νp+ν̄p) from existing neutrino and antineutrino scattering data (CCFR, BEBC/Gargamelle, WA25) and relate it to F_3^(0) using isospin and VMD arguments.

## Key numbers

- Δ_R^V = 0.02467(22), representing a reduction in theoretical uncertainty by nearly a factor of 2 compared to the previous Marciano and Sirlin (2006) result of 0.02361(38).
- |V_ud| = 0.97366(15), compared to the previous value of 0.97420(21) from the Particle Data Group (2018).
- The first-row CKM unitarity constraint gives |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_ub|^2 = 0.9983(4) using the new |V_ud| value.
- Contributions to □_γW^(VA) in units of 10^(-3): 2.17(0) from parton+pQCD, 1.06(6) from Born, and 0.56(8) from Regge+resonance+πN.
- The fit to the νp+ν̄p Nachtmann moment data yields A_WW = 5.2 ± 1.5 and B_WW = 1.08^(+0.48)_(-0.28) GeV^(-2) for the linear parametrization C_WW(Q^2) = A_WW(1 + B_WW Q^2).
- The ratio of Regge contributions at Q^2 = 0 and Q^2 = 2 GeV^2 is approximately 1/36.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach previously applied to the γZ-box correction in parity-violating electron scattering (Gorchtein 2008, Blunden 2011, Carlson 2012) to the γW-box in beta decay. It directly challenges the phenomenological piecewise parametrization of Marciano and Sirlin (2006), which used an interpolating function motivated by vector meson dominance at intermediate Q^2 with a 100% uncertainty. The new result enables improved tests of first-row CKM unitarity and identifies parity-violating deep inelastic electron scattering on hydrogen and deuterium as a future experimental avenue to directly measure the needed structure function F_3^(0).
