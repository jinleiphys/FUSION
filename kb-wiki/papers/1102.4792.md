# D-mesons and charmonium states in hot isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter

> We study the properties of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons in hot isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter, arising due to their interactions with the hadrons in the hyperonic medium. The interactions of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons with these light hadrons are derived by generalizing the chiral SU(3) model used for the study of hyperonic matter to SU(4). We also study the mass modifications of the charmonium states $J/ψ$, $ψ(3686)$ and $ψ(3770)$ in the isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter at finite temperatures and investigate the possibility of the decay of the charmonium states into $D\bar D$ pairs in the hot hadronic medium. The mass modifications of these charmonium states arise due to their interaction with the gluon condensates of QCD, simulated by a scalar dilaton field introduced to incorporate the broken scale invariance of QCD within the effective chiral model. We also compute the partial decay widths of the charmonium states to the $D\bar {D}$ pairs in the hadronic medium. The strong dependence on density of the in-medium properties of the $D$, $\bar{D}$ and the charmonium states, as well as the partial decay widths of charmonium states to $D\bar {D}$ pairs, found in the present investigation, will be of direct relevance in observables like open charm enhancement as well as $J/ψ$ suppression in the compressed baryonic matter (CBM) experiments at the future Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research, GSI, where the baryonic matter at high densities is planned to be produced.

## Key claim

The authors study the properties of D and anti-D mesons and charmonium states (J/psi, psi(3686), psi(3770)) in hot isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter, finding that the in-medium modifications depend strongly on density, with D meson properties being more sensitive to isospin asymmetry at high densities while anti-D meson properties are more affected by strangeness fraction, particularly at high densities.

## Method

The authors generalize a chiral SU(3) model to SU(4) to derive interactions of D and anti-D mesons with nucleons, hyperons, and scalar mesons in the hadronic medium, using mean field approximation where meson fields are treated as classical fields. The coupled equations of motion for scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta) and the dilaton field are solved to obtain their values in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter at finite temperatures. The mass modifications of charmonium states are investigated through the medium modification of the scalar gluon condensate, which is related to the dilaton field in the effective chiral model, with finite quark mass effects taken into account in the trace of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- The scalar gluon condensate is related to the dilaton field as: ⟨(α_s/π) G^a_{μν} G^{a μν}⟩ = (24/25)[(1-d)χ^4 + Σ_i m_i \bar{q_i} q_i] for N_f=4
- The explicit chiral symmetry breaking term is given by: Σ_i m_i \bar{q_i} q_i = [m_π^2 f_π σ + (√2 m_K^2 f_K - (1/√2) m_π^2 f_π) ζ + (√2 m_D^2 f_D - (1/√2) m_π^2 f_π) ζ_c]
- The isospin asymmetry parameter is defined as η = -Σ_i I_{3i} ρ_i / ρ_B, where ρ_i is the number density of baryon type i
- The strangeness fraction is defined as f_s = Σ_i s_i ρ_i / ρ_B, where s_i is the number of strange quarks of baryon i

## Context

This work extends previous studies of D meson properties in symmetric/asymmetric nuclear matter at zero and finite temperatures (Kumar and Mishra, 2010; Mishra and Mazumdar, 2009) by including the effects of strangeness through hyperons in the medium. The chiral SU(3) model used here has previously been applied to study vector meson modifications (Zschiesche et al., 2002; Mishra et al., 2004) and kaon/antikaon optical potentials (Mishra et al., 2009; Mishra et al., 2010). The investigation of charmonium state modifications and their decay to D anti-D pairs is relevant for understanding J/psi suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration in Pb-Pb collisions and for future compressed baryonic matter experiments at FAIR, GSI.
