# On the mean free paths of pions and kaons in hot hadronic matter

> Pion and kaon mean free paths within a thermal hadronic background are calculated using relativistic kinetic theory. Free cross sections are used which include contributions from $ρ$, $K^{*}$, $Δ$ and heavier resonances. Given pion to baryon ratios appropriate for the breakup stage of a $200\cdot A$GeV relativistic-heavy-ion collision, we find for temperatures less than 100 MeV, kaons have a shorter mean free path than pions, while for higher breakup temperatures the reverse is true. Since breakup temperatures should be in the neighborhood of 100 MeV, this suggests that kaon interferometry samples the same emission distribution as pion interferometry.

# On the mean free paths of pions and kaons in hot hadronic matter

## Key claim
For breakup temperatures near 100 MeV, pion and kaon mean free paths are remarkably similar, suggesting that kaon interferometry samples the same emission distribution as pion interferometry. However, this conclusion depends on the assumption of pion-to-baryon ratios appropriate for 200 A·GeV collisions at CERN, and the authors caution that rapid expansion can outrun chemical equilibrium, leading to lower breakup temperatures.

## Method
The authors used relativistic kinetic theory to calculate mean free paths of pions and kaons moving through a thermal hadronic background. Free cross sections were employed, dominated by resonant contributions through the ρ meson for pions and the K* resonance for kaons, with additional contributions from s-wave scattering and heavier resonances including the a1(1260), Δ, φ, K1(1270), Λ(1520), and Λ(1800). Densities of hadron species were determined from a relativistic Boltzmann distribution with a chemical potential for baryons chosen to yield a free π+ to proton ratio of 10.

## Key numbers
- For temperatures less than 100 MeV, kaons have a shorter mean free path than pions; for higher breakup temperatures, the reverse is true.
- The width of the K* resonance is 50 MeV, which is one third the width of the ρ resonance.
- The s-wave contribution to cross sections is taken as a constant 8.0 mb.
- A mean free path of 7 fm corresponds to a temperature of approximately 110 MeV.
- Heavier resonances beyond the ρ reduce the pion mean free path by order 30% at low pion momentum and 15% at high momentum.
- The effect of heavier resonances and s-wave contributions matters at the level of 10–20 percent.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of mean free paths and collision times for pions in hadronic matter, such as those by Goity and Leutwyler (1989) and Prakash et al. (1993). The results clarify the interpretation of two-particle interferometry measurements at CERN, where preliminary data from Humanic (1994) showed smaller source sizes for kaons than pions. The calculations provide guidance for transport models by identifying which reaction channels (ρ, K*, and heavier resonances) must be included and which can be neglected, enabling more reliable comparisons between experimental data and models such as those by Sorge et al. (1991), Pang et al. (1992), and Csernai et al. (1994).
