# On the pion-nucleon coupling constant

> In view of persisting misunderstanding about the determination of the pion-nucleon coupling constants in the Nijmegen multienergy partial-wave analyses of pp, np, and pbar-p scattering data, we present additional information which may clarify several points of discussion. We comment on several recent papers addressing the issue of the pion-nucleon coupling constant and criticizing the Nijmegen analyses.

## Key claim

The Nijmegen group's multienergy partial-wave analyses of nucleon-nucleon scattering data yield a pion-nucleon coupling constant near f² ≈ 0.075, consistently lower than the Karlsruhe-Helsinki value of 0.079, and the authors argue that this low value is essentially correct and free of significant systematic errors, with the exception of a possible small systematic effect in the ¹S₀ partial wave.

## Method

The authors performed multienergy partial-wave analyses of all pp, np, and pbar-p scattering data below T_lab = 350 MeV, using 22 model parameters (including the ppπ⁰ coupling constant) to fit 1787 pp data points. They extracted the coupling constant at the pion pole from the asymptotic one-pion-exchange potential, explicitly tested the influence of form factors (both exponential and Feynman-type), and investigated the sensitivity of different observables, partial waves, and energy ranges to the coupling constant value.

## Key numbers

- ppπ⁰ coupling constant from full pp analysis: f²_ppπ⁰ = 0.0750(5) (purely statistical error)
- When fixing the coupling at the old value 0.079, χ²_min increases by Δχ² = 56, corresponding to 7.5 standard deviations
- npπ± coupling constant from np analysis: f²_c = 0.0748(3)
- Difference between f²_c = 0.075 and 0.079 in np analysis: Δχ²_min = 255, corresponding to 16 standard deviations
- When fixing the ¹S₀ wave coupling at 0.075 and determining from remaining partial waves: f²_ppπ⁰ = 0.0746(6)
- From 3-350 MeV analysis (excluding very low-energy data): f²_ppπ⁰ = 0.0743(6)
- Final quoted value: f²_ppπ⁰ = 0.0745(6)
- Pion masses from combined pp+np analysis: m_π⁰ = 135.6(1.3) MeV, m_π± = 139.4(1.0) MeV
- χ²_min = 1786 for N_df = 1612 in pp analysis (χ²_min/N_df = 1.108)
- χ²_min = 3592.5 for pbar-p partial-wave analysis on 3309 observables

## Context

This paper responds to criticisms from several groups (including the Karlsruhe-Helsinki analyses by Hohler (1975) and others) that obtained f² = 0.079 from π±p scattering, and from potential-model studies by Machleidt (1991) and Haidenbauer (1992) that questioned the Nijmegen results. The work extends the Nijmegen group's earlier multienergy analyses (Bergervoet 1990, Klomp 1991, Timmermans 1991) and provides evidence that the low coupling constant is consistent with the Goldberger-Treiman relation and the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule (Workman, Arndt, and Pavan 1992). The authors recommend that future nucleon-nucleon scattering work adopt f²_NNπ = 0.0745 at the pion pole as a starting point.
