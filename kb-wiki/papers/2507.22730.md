# Pionic gluons from global QCD analysis of experimental and lattice data

> We perform the first global QCD analysis of parton distribution functions (PDFs) in the pion, with lattice-QCD data on gluonic pseudo--Ioffe-time distributions fitted simultaneously with experimental Drell-Yan and leading neutron electroproduction data. Inclusion of the lattice results with parametrized systematic corrections significantly reduces the uncertainties on the gluon PDF at parton momentum fractions $x \gtrsim 0.2$, revealing a higher gluon density in the pion at large $x$ than in the proton. The similar gluon momentum fractions in the pion and proton further suggests a relative suppression of the pion gluon density at small $x$.

## Key claim

The first global QCD analysis of pion parton distribution functions (PDFs) that simultaneously fits lattice-QCD gluonic pseudo–Ioffe-time distributions with experimental Drell-Yan and leading neutron electroproduction data reduces uncertainties on the gluon PDF at parton momentum fractions x ≳ 0.2 by more than 50%, revealing a higher gluon density in the pion at large x than in the proton, while the similar gluon momentum fractions in the pion and proton suggest a relative suppression of the pion gluon density at small x.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian global QCD analysis at next-to-leading order (NLO) plus next-to-leading log (NLL) perturbative corrections, fitting 225 experimental data points (56 from NA10 Drell-Yan, 61 from E615 Drell-Yan, 58 from H1 leading neutron electroproduction, and 50 from ZEUS leading neutron to inclusive cross section ratios) simultaneously with 35 lattice RpITD data points from 5 boost momenta P_z between 0.43 and 2.2 GeV and 7 spatial separations z from 3a to 9a (lattice spacing a = 0.1207 fm). The lattice data were incorporated with parametrized systematic corrections for higher-twist and discretization effects (B_1 and P_1 terms), using a matching scale μ² = max[m_c², 4/(z² e^{2γ_E})], and the posterior distribution was sampled over approximately 800 replicas via χ² minimization with bootstrapped data.

## Key numbers

- The reduced χ² for experimental data is 0.86 both before and after inclusion of lattice data, with or without systematic terms in the lattice matching.
- Including lattice data without systematic terms gives reduced χ²_lat = 1.86 (Z-score = 2.96); with systematic terms included, reduced χ²_lat decreases to 1.25 (Z-score = 1.05).
- The lattice data were obtained from approximately 1.3 million two-point correlators across 1,013 configurations with lattice spacing a = 0.1207 fm at valence pion mass M_π ≈ 310 MeV, on a 24³ × 64 volume.
- Boost momenta P_z up to 2.2 GeV were used, with spatial separations z up to 9a; data at z = a and 2a were excluded due to discretization effects.
- The gluon PDF uncertainty reduction exceeds 50% at x ≳ 0.5.
- At μ = 2 GeV, the pion gluon momentum fraction ⟨x⟩_g^π = 0.43(4), while proton gluon momentum fractions are in the range ⟨x⟩_g^p ≈ 0.40–0.42.
- The fraction of gluonic mass in a hadron is (3/4)⟨x⟩_g, giving about 30% for both pion and proton.

## Context

This work extends the JAM collaboration's previous global QCD analyses of pion PDFs (Barry 2021) by incorporating lattice-QCD gluon RpITD data (Good 2024) for the first time, following the methodology developed for including lattice valence-quark data in global fits (Jefferson Lab Angular Momentum JAM 2022). The results contradict recent analyses (Kotz 2023, Kotz 2025) that suggested a zero gluon distribution at the input scale may be consistent with Drell-Yan and restricted leading neutron data. The improved gluon PDF enables quantitative comparisons with proton gluon PDFs from CJ22 (Cerutti 2025), NNPDF4.0 (NNPDF 2021), and JAM25 (Cocuzza 2025), and the similar gluon momentum fractions in pion and proton have implications for the decomposition of hadron masses via the Ji (1994) mass sum rule. Future lattice data with higher statistics and experimental measurements from COMPASS++/AMBER at CERN, tagged DIS at Jefferson Lab, and future electron-ion colliders will further constrain pion PDFs.
