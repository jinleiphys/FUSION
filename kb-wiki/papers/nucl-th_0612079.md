# Precise determination of low-Q nucleon electromagnetic form factors and their impact on parity-violating e-p elastic scattering

> The extraction of the strangeness form factors from parity violating elastic electron-proton scattering is sensitive to the electromagnetic form factors at low Q^2. We provide parameterizations for the form factors and uncertainties, including the effects of two-photon exchange corrections to the extracted EM form factors. We study effect of the correlations between different form factors, in particular as they impact the parity violating asymmetry and the extraction of the strangeness form factors. We provide a prescription to extract the strangeness form factors from the asymmetry that provides an excellent approximation of the full two-photon correction. The corrected form factors are also appropriate as input for other low-Q analyses, although the effects of correlations and two-photon exchange corrections may be different.

## Key claim

The extraction of strangeness form factors from parity-violating electron-proton scattering requires precise knowledge of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors at low momentum transfer, and the authors provide parameterizations that include the effects of two-photon exchange corrections and correlations between form factors, with the caveat that the prescription for extracting strangeness form factors provides an excellent approximation of the full two-photon correction but the effects of correlations and two-photon exchange corrections may differ for other low-Q analyses.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit to world data on elastic electron-proton and electron-neutron scattering using a Continued Fraction expansion for the proton electric and magnetic form factors (G_Ep and G_Mp) and the neutron magnetic form factor (G_Mn), and a modified parameterization for the neutron electric form factor (G_En). They applied Coulomb distortion corrections to the proton data and separately performed fits using the full two-photon exchange calculation of Blunden et al. (2005), then studied how correlations between form factors and two-photon exchange corrections impact the parity-violating asymmetry and the extraction of strangeness form factors.

## Key numbers

- The fit for proton form factors is valid for Q from 0.3 to 1.0 GeV/c, with the Continued Fraction expansion coefficients for G_Ep: b1=3.440, b2=-0.178, b3=-1.212, b4=1.176, b5=-0.284
- The fit for G_Mp/mu_p uses coefficients: b1=3.173, b2=-0.314, b3=-1.165, b4=5.619, b5=-1.087
- The neutron magnetic form factor G_Mn has uncertainties of approximately 1.5%, roughly independent of Q in the range of interest
- The relative uncertainty on G_En is parameterized as the minimum of (5.2 + 12.6*Q^2)% below Q=0.3 GeV/c and (9.2 + 11*exp(-Q^2/0.19))% above Q=0.3 GeV/c
- The difference between Coulomb distortion and full two-photon exchange corrections for proton form factors is typically 0.3-0.4%, never more than 0.5% for G_Ep and 0.7% for G_Mp
- The two-photon exchange correction to the neutron cross section is parameterized as Delta sigma/sigma = 0.8% * Q^2 * (1-epsilon), with Q^2 in (GeV/c)^2, yielding a maximum correction to G_Mn of 0.4% at Q=1 GeV/c
- The combined effect of two-photon exchange on the parity-violating asymmetry is less than 1% for Q below 1 GeV/c
- The linear parameterization for the remaining two-photon exchange correction to the asymmetry uses C0 = 0.013 - 0.022*Q and C1 = -0.010 + 0.018*Q, with Q in GeV/c, providing agreement within 0.2% of the full calculation

## Context

This work extends the low-Q form factor analysis of Sick (2003) to higher momentum transfers up to Q=1.2 GeV/c, and provides the first consistent treatment of two-photon exchange corrections and form factor correlations for parity-violating electron-proton scattering. The results enable improved extraction of strangeness form factors from experiments at Jefferson Lab, Bates, and Mainz, and the parameterizations are also applicable to other low-Q analyses such as neutrino scattering form factor extraction (Budd, 2003), hyperfine splitting calculations (Brodsky, 2004), and DVCS analyses, though the authors caution that the treatment of two-photon exchange corrections and correlations may differ for these applications.
