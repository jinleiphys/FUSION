# FRG Approach to Nuclear Matter at Extreme Conditions

> Functional renormalization group (FRG) is an exact method for taking into account the effect of quantum fluctuations in the effective action of the system. The FRG method applied to effective theories of nuclear matter yields equation of state which incorporates quantum fluctuations of the fields. Using the local potential approximation (LPA) the equation of state for Walecka-type models of nuclear matter under extreme conditions could be determined. These models can be tested by solving the corresponding Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkov (TOV) equations and investigating the properties (mass and radius) of the corresponding compact star models. Here, we present the first steps on this way, we obtained a Maxwell construction within the FRG-based framework using a Walecka-type Lagrangian.

# FRG Approach to Nuclear Matter at Extreme Conditions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the functional renormalization group (FRG) method, applied within the local potential approximation (LPA) to a Walecka-type model, can produce a Maxwell construction in the effective potential, but only as a first step toward a full equation of state for compact star matter, with the semi-finite temperature approximation validated only for low temperatures where the Fermi–Dirac distribution behaves like a step function.

## Method

The authors start from a Walecka-type Lagrangian including sigma, pion, and omega meson fields, treat the omega meson in mean-field approximation, and apply the Wetterich equation for the scale-dependent effective potential under the local potential approximation (LPA). They solve the resulting partial differential equation for the effective potential at zero temperature and finite chemical potential, using a semi-finite temperature approximation that replaces the finite-temperature running with the zero-temperature running on the right-hand side of the flow equation. The integration runs from a UV cutoff scale of Λ = 1.3 GeV down to k = 0, with parameters chosen to reproduce the correct nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- UV cutoff scale: Λ = 1.3 GeV
- Scalar mass parameter: m² = 1.2 GeV²
- Quartic coupling: λ = 7.4
- The semi-finite temperature approximation reproduces the full finite-temperature pressure solution down to β ≈ 0.1 (in scalar-mass units)
- The classical UV-scale potential has the form U(φ) = -m² φ + λ φ², where φ = σ²/2

## Context

This work extends the application of the functional renormalization group method, previously developed by Wetterich (1993) and reviewed by Gies (2006), to nuclear matter models of the type introduced by Walecka and further developed by Glendenning. The authors build on the treatment of the omega meson in mean-field approximation as used by Drews (2014). The key result, obtaining a Maxwell construction within the FRG framework, enables the next step of computing the full equation of state for extreme dense matter in compact stars, including neutron stars, hybrid stars, and quark stars.
