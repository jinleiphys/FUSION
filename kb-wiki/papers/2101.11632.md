# Towards studying the structural differences between the pion and its radial excitation

> We present an exploratory lattice QCD investigation of the differences between the valence quark structure of pion and its radial excitation $π(1300)$ in a fixed finite volume using the leading-twist factorization approach. We present evidences that the first pion excitation in our lattice computation is a single particle state that is likely to be the finite volume realization of $π(1300)$. An analysis with reasonable priors result in better estimates of the excited state PDF and the moments, wherein we find evidence that the radial excitation of pion correlates with an almost two-fold increase in the momentum fraction of valence quarks. This proof-of-principle work establishes the viability of future lattice computations incorporating larger operator basis that can resolve the structural changes accompanying hadronic excitation.

## Key claim

This exploratory lattice QCD study provides evidence that the first excited state observed in the pion correlator is likely the finite-volume realization of the radial excitation π(1300), and under this justified assumption, the valence quark momentum fraction in this excited state is approximately twice that of the ground-state pion.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on a 64^4 lattice with a=0.04 fm and pion mass M_π=0.3 GeV, using Gaussian-smeared sources with momentum smearing to study boosted pions. They extracted the first excited state energy E_1 from two-state and three-state fits to two-point correlation functions, then determined the bilocal quark bilinear matrix elements for the excited state from three-point functions using three different fitting Ansätze (type-1: full three-state; type-2: with prior on ground state matrix element; type-3: with prior on ground state and neglecting the second excited state matrix element). The parton distribution functions were obtained via leading-twist operator product expansion matching in three renormalization schemes (RI-MOM, ratio scheme using excited state reference, and ratio scheme using ground state reference).

## Key numbers

- First excited state mass at zero momentum: E_1(P_z=0)=1.456(92) GeV, compared to the physical π(1300) mass of 1.3 GeV
- Ground state pion mass on this ensemble: M_π=0.3 GeV
- Lattice spacing: a=0.04 fm, lattice size L_t=L=64
- Maximum momentum used: |P|=2.42 GeV (n_z=5, with P_z=2πn_z/(aL))
- Reference momentum for ratio schemes: P_z^0=0.48 GeV
- Factorization scale for PDF determinations: μ=3.2 GeV
- Two-parameter PDF fit for excited state using M_{π'π'} scheme: {α,β}={0.4(3), 1.1(2)}

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the pion valence PDF by the same collaboration (Gao et al., 2020; Izubuchi et al., 2019) to the first radial excitation, and complements prior lattice studies of decay constants of pion and its radial excitation (McNeile, 2006; Mastropas, 2014) and Dyson-Schwinger Equation studies of distribution amplitudes (Li, 2016). The proof-of-principle nature establishes viability for future lattice computations with larger operator bases to resolve structural changes accompanying hadronic excitation, which could help understand mass-gap generation in QCD and provide a contrast for studies of exotic gluon excitations at Jefferson Lab.
