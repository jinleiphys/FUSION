# Breakup Reactions of 11Li within a Three-Body Model

> We use a three-body model to investigate breakup reactions of 11Li (n+n+9Li) on a light target. The interaction parameters are constrained by known properties of the two-body subsystems, the 11Li binding energy and fragmentation data. The remaining degrees of freedom are discussed. The projectile-target interactions are described by phenomenological optical potentials. The model predicts dependence on beam energy and target, differences between longitudinal and transverse momentum distributions and provides absolute values for all computed differential cross sections. We give an almost complete series of observables and compare with corresponding measurements. Remarkably good agreement is obtained. The relative neutron-9Li p-wave content is about 40%. A p-resonance, consistent with measurements at about 0.5 MeV of width about 0.4 MeV, seems to be necessary. The widths of the momentum distributions are insensitive to target and beam energy with a tendency to increase towards lower energies. The transverse momentum distributions are broader than the longitudinal due to the diffraction process. The absolute values of the cross sections follow the neutron-target cross sections and increase strongly for beam energies decreasing below 100 MeV/u.

## Key claim

The authors use a three-body model (n+n+⁹Li) to compute breakup reaction observables for ¹¹Li on light targets, achieving "remarkably good agreement" with measurements, but the model's absolute cross sections are less accurate than desired due to the approximate treatment of shadowing effects and the sensitivity to the shadowing parameter \(r_0\).

## Method

The projectile-target interaction is described by phenomenological optical potentials, with the participant particle (one neutron) either elastically scattered (diffraction) or absorbed (stripping) by the target, while the two spectators (the other neutron and the ⁹Li core) continue undisturbed but interact via the same two-body potentials as in the initial three-body bound state. The initial ¹¹Li wave function is obtained by solving Faddeev equations in hyperspherical coordinates, and shadowing is approximated by excluding the wave function when the participant is within spheres of radii \(r_{nc}\) and \(r_{nn}\) around the spectators. The differential cross sections factorize into a participant-target cross section (from the optical model) and an overlap matrix element from the sudden approximation.

## Key numbers

- The relative neutron-⁹Li \(p\)-wave content in the ¹¹Li three-body wave function is about **40%**.
- A \(p\)-resonance in the neutron-⁹Li system is found at about **0.5 MeV** with a width of about **0.4 MeV**.
- The shadowing parameter \(r_0 \approx 1.26\) fm is adjusted to reproduce the few reported absolute values of two-neutron removal cross sections.
- The absolute values of the cross sections increase strongly for beam energies decreasing below **100 MeV/u**.
- The widths of the momentum distributions show a tendency to increase towards lower beam energies.

## Context

This work extends the sudden approximation approach previously used by Garrido et al. (1996, 1997) by replacing the schematic black-sphere participant-target interaction with a phenomenological optical potential, thereby introducing dependence on beam energy and target, and enabling computation of absolute cross sections and distinction between longitudinal and transverse momentum distributions. The model also accounts for shadowing effects, which were included through profile functions in Glauber calculations by Bertsch et al. (1998). The results enable systematic predictions of correlated observables for ¹¹Li breakup, including momentum distributions, invariant mass spectra, and angular distributions, all from a single parameter set.
