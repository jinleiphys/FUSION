# Conditional recovery of time-reversal symmetry in many nucleus systems

> Propagation of non-topological soliton in many-nucleus systems is studied based on time-dependent density functional calculations with focusing on mass and energy dependence. The dispersive property and the nonlinearity of the system, which are inherently included in the nuclear density functional, are essential factors to form a non-topological soliton. On the other hand the soliton propagation is prevented by the charge equilibration dynamics, and the competition possibly appears. In this article, based on the energy-dependence of the two competitive factors, the concept of conditional recovery of time-reversal symmetry is proposed in many nucleus systems. It clarifies a possibility of preserving nuclear medium inside natural or artificial nuclear reactors, under a suitable temperature. From an astrophysical point of view, the existence of the low-temperature solitonic core of compact stars is suggested.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that time-reversal symmetry can be conditionally recovered in many-nucleus systems through non-topological soliton propagation, but only when the collision energy is high enough that the soliton-forming nonlinearity dominates over the charge equilibration dynamics that would otherwise disrupt the soliton.

## Method
The authors performed time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) calculations using the SV-bas effective interaction for central collisions of six representative ion pairs: (A,Z) = (4,2), (16,8), (40,20), (48,20), (100,50), and (120,50). They tracked nucleon transfer numbers between colliding nuclei across collision energies from 1 to 100 MeV per nucleon to identify energy regimes where perfect transparency (zero nucleon transfer) occurs.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature T_C = 7.2 MeV is used to distinguish low and high temperature regimes in the Bethe-formula temperature estimation
- Soliton property achieves "almost perfect transparency" around 20 to 40 MeV per nucleon collision energy
- For (A,Z) = (4,2) and (16,8) cases, positive neutron transfer and negative proton transfer appear simultaneously at temperatures less than 10 MeV
- Binding energy differences between theoretical and experimental values are less than 4% at most, validating the SV-bas interaction
- The high energy case around E_K = 100.0 MeV corresponds to relative velocity almost equal to 80% of the speed of light

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous research on soliton propagation in light-ion collisions (Iwata 2015) to a broader mass range from 12 to 244, and connects to studies of nuclear symmetry energy by Stone (2017) which examined density and asymmetry parameters for similar systems. The findings suggest applications in preserving nuclear medium in reactors and imply the existence of low-temperature solitonic cores in compact stars, while also relating to experimental searches for the tetra-neutron resonance state (Kisamori 2016, Marques 2002).
