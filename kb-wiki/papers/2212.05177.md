# Toward precision Fermi liquid theory in two dimensions

> The ultra-cold and weakly-coupled Fermi gas in two spatial dimensions is studied in an effective field theory framework. It has long been observed that universal corrections to the energy density to two orders in the interaction strength do not agree with Monte Carlo simulations in the weak-coupling regime. Here, universal corrections to three orders in the interaction strength are obtained for the first time, and are shown to provide agreement between theory and simulation. Special consideration is given to the scale ambiguity associated with the non-trivial renormalization of the singular contact interactions. The isotropic superfluid gap is obtained to next-to-leading order, and nonuniversal contributions to the energy density due to effective range effects, p-wave interactions and three-body forces are computed. Results are compared with precise Monte Carlo simulations of the energy density and the contact in the weakly-coupled attractive and repulsive Fermi liquid regimes. In addition, the known all-orders sum of ladder and ring diagrams is compared with Monte Carlo simulations at weak coupling and beyond.

## Key claim

The paper obtains universal corrections to the energy density of the two-dimensional weakly-coupled Fermi gas to three orders in the interaction strength for the first time, resolving a long-standing tension between theory and Monte Carlo simulations that existed at second order. The agreement holds specifically in the weak-coupling regime where \(|\log(k_F a_2)|\gg 1\), with the caveat that the renormalization scale ambiguity from the singular contact interactions must be carefully handled.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory (EFT) framework with contact interactions, regularized via dimensional reduction and renormalized in the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme. They compute the thermodynamic potential and energy density of the two-dimensional Fermi gas to third order in the universal coupling \(\alpha(\mu) = M C_0(\mu)/(2\pi)\), including both particle-hole and particle-particle diagrams, and compare these results with Monte Carlo simulations of the energy density and contact in both attractive and repulsive Fermi liquid regimes. The superfluid gap is obtained to next-to-leading order, and nonuniversal contributions from effective range effects, p-wave interactions, and three-body forces are computed perturbatively.

## Key numbers

* The dimensionless coupling constant is \(\alpha(\mu) = M C_0(\mu)/(2\pi) = -1/\log(\mu a_2)\), where \(a_2\) is the two-dimensional scattering length.
* The binding momentum of the two-body bound state is \(\gamma_B = \mu \exp(1/\alpha(\mu)) = 1/a_2\), with binding energy \(\varepsilon_B = -\gamma_B^2/M\).
* The noninteracting Fermi gas energy per particle is \(E/N = k_F^2/(4M)\), where \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum.
* The density of a spin-balanced noninteracting Fermi gas in two dimensions is \(\rho = g k_F^2/(4\pi)\), with degeneracy \(g\).
* The effective range parameter for s-wave scattering is \(\sigma_2 = 4 C_2(\mu)/(M C_0(\mu)^2)\).
* The p-wave scattering volume is \(\sigma_p = M C_2'/8\), and the p-wave length scale \(a_p\) satisfies \(a_p = \mu^{-1} \exp(\alpha_p(\mu))\) with \(\alpha_p(\mu) = 4\pi C_4'(\mu)/(M C_2'^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative EFT treatment of weakly-coupled Fermi gases in two dimensions, which previously had only been carried out to second order in the universal coupling by Engelbrecht et al. (1992) and others. The calculation resolves the tension between those second-order results and Monte Carlo simulations by Bertaina et al. (2011, 2015) and Galea et al. (2015, 2017). The results enable systematic comparison with simulations across the weak-coupling regime and provide a foundation for including nonuniversal effects such as effective range, p-wave interactions, and three-body forces in future Monte Carlo studies.
