# Eccentricity fluctuations and elliptic flow at RHIC

> Fluctuations in nucleon positions can affect the spatial eccentricity of the overlap zone in nucleus-nucleus collisions. We show that elliptic flow should be scaled by different eccentricities depending on which method is used for the flow analysis. These eccentricities are estimated semi-analytically. When $v_2$ is analyzed from 4-particle cumulants, or using the event plane from directed flow in a zero-degree calorimeter, the result is shown to be insensitive to eccentricity fluctuations.

## Key claim

Elliptic flow ($v_2$) measured by different experimental methods should be scaled by different definitions of the spatial eccentricity of the overlap zone; when $v_2$ is analyzed from 4-particle cumulants or using the event plane from directed flow in a zero-degree calorimeter, the result is insensitive to eccentricity fluctuations, whereas the standard event-plane method is strongly affected by such fluctuations.

## Method

The authors compute semi-analytically, to leading order in $1/N$ where $N$ is the mean number of participant nucleons, the moments of the participant eccentricity distribution using a Glauber model with Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles. They derive expressions for $\varepsilon\{2\}$ (root-mean-square participant eccentricity), $\varepsilon\{4\}$ (four-particle cumulant eccentricity), and $\langle\varepsilon_{\rm RP}\rangle$ (reaction-plane eccentricity) by expanding the sample averages over participant positions around their ensemble averages and retaining terms to second order in fluctuations. Numerical results are obtained for Au-Au and Cu-Cu collisions as a function of the number of participants.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions, $\varepsilon\{2\}$ rises steeply as the number of participants $N$ decreases due to the $1/N$ contribution from fluctuations, while $\varepsilon\{4\}$ and $\langle\varepsilon_{\rm RP}\rangle$ remain close to the standard eccentricity $\varepsilon_{\rm s}$ down to low $N$.
- The relative difference between $\varepsilon\{4\}$ and $\varepsilon_{\rm s}$ is of order $1/N$ because the $1/N$ term in Eq. (epscumul4) is multiplied by a term of order $\varepsilon_{\rm s}^4$.
- The ratio $\langle r^4\rangle/\langle r^2\rangle^2$ is approximately 1.4–2 for the nuclear density distributions considered.
- Fluctuations in the transverse area of the overlap zone are found to be practically negligible, scaling as $1/(4N)$ times a combination of fourth-order moments.

## Context

This work extends the PHOBOS collaboration's observation (Manly, 2005) that fluctuations in nucleon positions explain the large $v_2$ seen in Cu-Cu collisions at RHIC, and provides a theoretical framework for understanding which eccentricity definition applies to each flow analysis method. It builds on Monte-Carlo Glauber calculations by Voloshin (2006) and earlier work on eccentricity scaling in hydrodynamics by Bhalerao (2005). The results enable quantitative comparison of $v_2$ measurements across different analysis methods (event-plane, 4-particle cumulants, ZDC) and clarify that higher-order estimates ($v_2\{4\}$ and $v_2\{{\rm ZDC}\}$) are insensitive to both nonflow effects and participant eccentricity fluctuations, explaining the observed agreement between these methods in Au-Au collisions.
