# Hydrodynamics of Resonances

> We derive system of equations describing fluidity of the medium consisting of non-relativistic particles with finite mass-widths. For that we use expressions for the kinetic Noether 4-current and the Noether energy-momentum tensor being conserved provided one uses self-consistent approximations to the gradient expanded Kadanoff-Baym equations. Kinetic coefficients entering equations of non-ideal hydrodynamics of resonances are obtained in terms of the real and imaginary parts of the self-energies within a relaxation time approximation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a system of equations for the hydrodynamics of a medium composed of non-relativistic particles with finite mass-widths (resonances), using self-consistent approximations to the gradient-expanded Kadanoff-Baym equations. The derivation relies on the condition that the kinetic Noether 4-current and Noether energy-momentum tensor are conserved only when these self-consistent approximations are employed.

## Method

The author starts from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for the Green's function and performs a gradient expansion to obtain a kinetic equation for the Wigner function. Using the Noether theorem, expressions for the conserved 4-current and energy-momentum tensor are constructed in terms of the real and imaginary parts of the self-energies. Kinetic coefficients for non-ideal hydrodynamics of resonances are then obtained within a relaxation time approximation, expressing transport coefficients (such as shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and thermal conductivity) through the self-energy components.

## Key numbers

* The paper derives kinetic coefficients for non-ideal hydrodynamics in terms of the real and imaginary parts of the self-energies, but no specific numerical values for these coefficients or for any system are provided in the available text.
* No explicit numerical results (e.g., values of viscosity, thermal conductivity, or relaxation times) are given for any particular resonance or energy regime.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of non-equilibrium quantum field theory, specifically the Kadanoff-Baym approach, to describe the fluid behavior of particles with finite lifetimes (resonances). It builds on earlier work by Kadanoff and Baym (1962) on quantum kinetic theory and by Danielewicz (1984) on non-equilibrium Green's functions. The results enable the formulation of hydrodynamic models for nuclear matter and heavy-ion collisions that incorporate the finite width of resonances, going beyond the standard quasi-particle approximation used in previous works such as those by Botermans and Malfliet (1990).
