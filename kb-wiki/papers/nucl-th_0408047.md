# Separation of Scales in the More Effective Field Theory and Moszkowski-Scott Methods

> We compare the momentum-space decimation procedure used to construct the low momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V(low-k) with the configuration-space separation method of Moszkowski and Scott. Each procedure defines a separation of scales in the nuclear many-body problem, and the extent to which these two scales coincide is studied. By studying the effects of the separation method on the relative S-state Kallio-Kolltveit potential, it is found that close agreement with V(low-k) is obtained as the configuration-space cutoff is lowered to ~ 1.0 fm.

## Key claim

The momentum-space decimation procedure used to construct the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V(low-k) and the configuration-space separation method of Moszkowski and Scott predict the same separation of scales in the nuclear interaction, with close agreement obtained when the configuration-space cutoff is lowered to approximately 1.0 fm.

## Method

The authors compared the renormalization group (RG) decimation procedure that produces V(low-k) with a momentum-space cutoff of 2.0 fm^-1 to the Moszkowski-Scott separation method applied to the Kallio-Kolltveit (KK) relative S-state potential. The KK potential was Fourier-transformed into momentum space using the long-distance part V_l integrated from the separation distance d to infinity, and the resulting diagonal matrix elements were compared directly with those of V(low-k) for the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 channels.

## Key numbers

- The momentum-space cutoff for V(low-k) is taken as 2.0 fm^-1, corresponding to a maximum laboratory energy of approximately 350 MeV.
- The Kallio-Kolltveit potential has an infinite hard core for r ≤ 0.4 fm, with singlet parameters A_s = 330.8 MeV and α_s = 2.4021 fm^-1, and triplet parameters A_t = 475.0 MeV and α_t = 2.5214 fm^-1.
- The separation distances for the KK potential are d_s = 1.025 fm for the singlet state and d_t = 0.925 fm for the triplet state.
- Close agreement between V(low-k) and the KK potential is obtained for a configuration-space cutoff of approximately 1.0 fm for both angular momentum states.
- Diagonal matrix elements of the KK potential for momenta above the V(low-k) cutoff of 2.0 fm^-1 are very small.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the more effective field theory (MEEFT) approach of Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators and the classic separation method of Moszkowski and Scott (1960), which was used in constructing the Kuo-Brown interaction (1968). The results show that the S-wave treatment of Kuo and Brown, using the Moszkowski-Scott separation method, was essentially equivalent to the modern V(low-k) effective interaction, explaining why the Kuo-Brown interactions have remained successful for decades. The authors suggest that local potentials constructed with a correct separation distance and a schematic hard core may be useful for schematic nuclear structure calculations.
