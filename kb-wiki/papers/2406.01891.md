# Quasifragmentation functions in the massive Schwinger model

> We introduce the concept of the quark quasifragmentation function (qFF) using an equal-time and spatially boosted form of the Collins-Soper fragmentation function where the out-meson fragment is replaced by the current asymptotic condition. We derive the qFF for a fermion in two-dimensional quantum electrodynamics (QED2) using the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian after a mapping onto spin qubits in a spatial lattice with open boundary conditions. This form is suitable for quantum computations. We compute the qFF by exact diagonalization of the spin Hamiltonian. The results are compared to the qFF following from the Drell-Levy-Yan result for QED2, both at strong and weak coupling, and to two-dimensional quantum chromodynamics in the lowest Fock approximation.

## Key claim

The authors introduce the concept of the quark quasifragmentation function (qFF) using an equal-time and spatially boosted form of the Collins-Soper fragmentation function, where the out-meson fragment is replaced by the current asymptotic condition. The qFF asymptotes the light-front Collins-Soper fragmentation function only in the luminal limit \(v \rightarrow 1\), and the construction is explicitly implemented in massive two-dimensional QED (QED2) as a testbed for quantum computation.

## Method

The authors map the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian for massive QED2 onto spin qubits on a spatial lattice with open boundary conditions using a Jordan-Wigner transformation, then compute the qFF by exact diagonalization of the resulting spin Hamiltonian. The qFF is evaluated as a Fourier transform of a spatial correlator at fixed boost velocity and large time, with the out-going meson sourced by a current condition at asymptotic times. The results are compared to the Drell-Levy-Yan (DLY) approximation for the fragmentation function in QED2, obtained from the solution of the light-front bound-state equation in the two-particle Fock-space approximation using an expansion in Jacobi polynomials.

## Key numbers

* For strong coupling with \(m/m_S = 0.177\) and boost velocity \(v = 0.925\), the spatial correlator \(\mathbb C(Z,v,t)\) is evaluated at times \(t = 0.1\), \(t = 13\), and \(t = 25\) on a lattice with \(N = 22\) sites and \(g = 1\) (units of lattice spacing \(a = 1\)).
* For weak coupling with \(m/m_S = 0.806\) and boost velocity \(v = 0.925\), the same correlator is evaluated at times \(t = 0.1\), \(t = 13\), and \(t = 25\) on a lattice with \(N = 22\) sites and \(g = 1\).
* In the massless limit of QED2, the DLY fragmentation function simplifies to \(d_{DLY}(z,1) \rightarrow 1/z\).
* For light quarks in the strong coupling regime, the DLY fragmentation function scales as \(d_{DLY}(z,1) \sim \bar{z}^{2\beta}/z^{1+4\beta}\) with \(\beta = (\sqrt{3}/\pi)(m/m_S)\); results are shown for \(\beta = 0\) and \(\beta = 0.2\).
* For heavy quarks in QED2 with \(m/m_S \gg 1\), the DLY fragmentation function is \(d_{DLY}(z,1) \rightarrow (16 f_H^2/\alpha^2) z^3 \bar{z}^2 / (z-2)^8\), with the constant \(f_H\) fixed by the momentum sum rule \(\int_0^1 dz\, z d_{DLY}(z,1) = 2 f_H^2/(105 \alpha^2) \rightarrow 1\).
* In the massless limit of QCD2, the DLY fragmentation function simplifies to \(d_{DLY}^0(z,1) \rightarrow 1/(N_c z)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study of quasi-PDFs (qPDFs) using exact diagonalization of the spin Hamiltonian in QED2 (Grieninger, 2024) to the analogous case of fragmentation functions. The construction provides a first-principles, non-perturbative route to the Collins-Soper light-front fragmentation functions that are otherwise inaccessible to standard Euclidean lattice simulations, as reviewed by Metz (2016). The DLY relation, originally proposed by Drell, Levy, and Yan (1969), is used to approximate the fragmentation functions from the parton distribution functions, and the results are compared to the exact diagonalization data. The spin formulation is designed to be directly implementable on quantum hardware, enabling future quantum simulations of fragmentation in gauge theories.
