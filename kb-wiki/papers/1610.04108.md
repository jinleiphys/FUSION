# Hydrodynamic approach to the centrality dependence of di-hadron correlations

> Measurements of di-hadron azimuthal correlations at different centralities for Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV were reported by the PHENIX Collaboration. The data were presented for different ranges of transverse momentum. In particular, it was observed that the away-side correlation evolves from double- to a single-peak structure when the centrality decreases. In this work, we show that these features naturally appear as due to an interplay between the centrality-dependent smooth background elliptic flow and the one produced by event-by-event fluctuating peripheral tubes. To compare with the PHENIX data, we also carry out numerical simulations by using a hydrodynamical code NeXSPheRIO, and calculate the correlations by both cumulant and the ZYAM method employed by PHENIX Collaboration. It is shown that our results are in reasonable agreement with the data. A brief discussion on the physical content of the present model and its difference from other viewpoint is also presented.

## Key claim

The observed centrality-dependent evolution of the away-side di-hadron correlation from double-peak to single-peak structure in Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV can be explained by the interplay between the centrality-dependent smooth background elliptic flow and the flow produced by event-by-event fluctuating peripheral tubes, without invoking higher-order harmonic flow coefficients as the primary mechanism.

## Method

The authors employed the peripheral tube model, where the one-particle distribution is decomposed into a smooth background elliptic flow component and a tube component characterized by v2 and v3 harmonics. They derived analytic expressions for the subtracted di-hadron correlation using both the cumulant method and the ZYAM method. To validate the model, they performed event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations using the NeXSPheRIO code with initial conditions from the NeXuS event generator, generating 180-5000 events per centrality class depending on the method, and compared the resulting correlations to PHENIX data.

## Key numbers

- Background elliptic flow coefficient v2^b = 0.08 for central (0-10%) collisions and v2^b = 0.3 for peripheral (40-60%) collisions, for all charged particles up to 3 GeV
- Tube flow coefficients: v2^t = 0.017, v3^t = 0.015, extracted from hydrodynamic evolution of events studied previously
- Multiplicity variance (N_b^2 - N_b^2)/(2pi)^2 = 31067 for central (0-10%, impact parameter 0-4.871 fm) and 7264 for peripheral (40-60%, impact parameter 9.568-11.718 fm) collisions
- Tube volume scaling: V_t x n_t = 53.7 x 3.7 for central collisions to 48.4 x 2.0 for peripheral collisions
- N_t^2/(2pi)^2 = 1496582 for central collisions, decreasing to 355221 for peripheral collisions
- v2{EP} values from NeXSPheRIO for 0-20% centrality: 0.0399 (pT 0.4-1.0 GeV), 0.0858 (pT 1.0-2.0 GeV), 0.1387 (pT 2.0-3.0 GeV)
- v2{EP} values for 60-92% centrality: 0.0739 (pT 0.4-1.0 GeV), 0.1460 (pT 1.0-2.0 GeV), 0.2369 (pT 2.0-3.0 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the peripheral tube model previously developed by the same group (Castilho et al., 2014, 2015) to explain the trigger-angle dependence of di-hadron correlations in mid-central collisions. It directly addresses the PHENIX Collaboration measurements (Adare et al., 2011) showing the centrality evolution of away-side correlations. The approach offers an alternative to the standard interpretation that attributes the double-peak structure primarily to triangular flow (v3) from initial state fluctuations. The authors argue that their model provides a more intuitive physical picture where localized hot spots in the initial conditions, rather than global harmonic deformations, generate the observed correlation structures. The work enables future studies of event plane correlations and symmetric cumulants to distinguish between different theoretical models.
