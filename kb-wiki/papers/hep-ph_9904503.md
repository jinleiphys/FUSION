# Hard Photons and Neutral Pions from RHIC

> In order to fix the parameters for predictions of hard photon and pion production in $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, proton-proton and proton-nucleus data are analyzed in perturbative QCD in the energy range $\sqrt{s} \approx 20-60$ GeV and a prediction at RHIC energy is given.

# Hard Photons and Neutral Pions from RHIC

## Key claim

The authors use a perturbative QCD model with an intrinsic transverse momentum ($k_T$) parameter to describe existing proton-proton and proton-nucleus data, and predict that at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV in proton-proton collisions, the direct photon cross section will reach 10% of the neutral pion cross section at $p_T \approx 6.5$ GeV, but only if the intrinsic $k_T$ width is extrapolated linearly from lower-energy fits.

## Method

The authors employ a leading-order pQCD parton model with a K-factor of approximately 2 to account for higher-order corrections, and introduce a Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum distribution for partons with a fitted width $\langle k_T^2 \rangle$. They fit this model to existing $pp \rightarrow \pi^0 X$ and $pp \rightarrow \gamma X$ data in the energy range $\sqrt{s} \approx 20-60$ GeV, then extend the analysis to proton-nucleus collisions using a nuclear thickness function and a "saturated" Cronin factor $C^{sat}$ that adds extra transverse momentum from a single semi-hard collision in the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic transverse momentum width $\langle k_T^2 \rangle$ is fitted to $pp$ data; the average $k_T$ is observed to increase linearly with energy, reaching $\langle k_T \rangle \approx 3.5$ GeV at $\sqrt{s}=1800$ GeV (extrapolated from lower-energy fits).
- The threshold where direct photon production surpasses 10% of the neutral pion cross section in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV occurs at $p_T \approx 6.5$ GeV.
- The saturated Cronin factor for proton-nucleus collisions is fitted as $C^{sat} = 1.2$ GeV$^2$, describing data for $pA \rightarrow \pi^0 X$ with targets $Be$, $Ti$, and $W$.
- The data/theory ratios for $pp$ collisions cluster around unity with maximum deviations on the order of approximately 50%.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of intrinsic $k_T$ used by Huston (1995) and Wang (1997-1998) to describe both direct photon and neutral pion production in hadronic collisions. The authors build on earlier Cronin effect measurements in proton-nucleus collisions and provide parameterized predictions for the upcoming RHIC program at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV. The results enable future calculations of hard photon and pion production in gold-gold collisions at RHIC, where the direct photon yield must be at least 10% of the pion yield to be separable from the two-photon decay background.
