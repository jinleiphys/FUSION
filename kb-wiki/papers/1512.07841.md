# Final-state interactions in two-nucleon knockout reactions

> Background: Exclusive two-nucleon knockout after electroexcitation of nuclei ($A(e,e'NN)$ in brief) is considered to be a primary source of information about short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei. For a proper interpretation of the data, final-state interactions (FSI) need to be theoretically controlled. Purpose: Our goal is to quantify the role of FSI effects in exclusive $A(e,e'pN)$ reactions for four target nuclei representative for the whole mass region. Our focus is on processes that are SRC driven. We investigate the role of FSI for two characteristic detector setups corresponding with a "small" and "large" coverage of the available phase space. Results: The transparency $T^{pN}_{A}$, defined as the ratio of exclusive $(e,e'pN)$ cross sections on nuclei to those on "free" nucleon pairs, drops from $ 0.2-0.3 $ for $^{12}$C to $0.04-0.07$ for $^{208}$Pb. For all considered kinematics, the mass dependence of the $T^{pN}_{A}$ can be captured by the power law $T^{pN}_{A} \propto A^{- λ}$ with $ 0.4 \lesssim λ\lesssim 0.5 $. Apart from an overall reduction factor, we find that FSI only modestly affects the distinct features of SRC-driven $A(e,e'pN)$ which are dictated by the c.m. distribution of close-proximity pairs. Conclusion: The SCX mechanisms represent a relatively small (order of a few percent) contribution of SRC-driven $A(e,e'pN)$ processes. The mass dependence of FSI effects in exclusive $A(e,e'pN)$ can be captured in a robust power law and is in agreement with the predictions obtained in a toy model.

## Key claim
Final-state interactions (FSI) in exclusive $A(e,e'pN)$ reactions reduce the cross section by a factor that follows a robust power law in mass number $A$, but they do not significantly distort the characteristic signatures of short-range correlations (SRC), such as the center-of-mass momentum distribution of the knocked-out pair. The single-charge exchange (SCX) contribution to the observed cross section is a small effect, on the order of a few percent.

## Method
The authors used a factorized expression for the $A(e,e'pN)$ cross section, proportional to the two-body center-of-mass (c.m.) momentum distribution of close-proximity nucleon pairs. Elastic attenuation of the outgoing nucleons was modeled using the relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA), and single-charge exchange (SCX) reactions were included semi-classically. Calculations were performed for $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{56}$Fe, and $^{208}$Pb for two distinct kinematic setups: a large-acceptance "4π" detector (KinB) and a narrow solid-angle setup (KinA).

## Key numbers
* The nuclear transparency $T^{pN}_{A}$ drops from $0.2-0.3$ for $^{12}$C to $0.04-0.07$ for $^{208}$Pb.
* The mass dependence of the transparency follows a power law $T^{pN}_{A} \propto A^{- \lambda}$ with $0.4 \lesssim \lambda \lesssim 0.5$ for all considered kinematics.
* The SCX mechanisms represent a relatively small contribution, on the order of a few percent, of SRC-driven $A(e,e'pN)$ processes.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for interpreting exclusive two-nucleon knockout experiments, which are a primary source of information about short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei. It builds on prior factorized cross-section models (Colle 2014) and the RMSGA approach (Ryckebusch 2003, Cosyn 2013) to quantify FSI effects, which are essential for a proper interpretation of data from experiments like those at Jefferson Lab (Hen 2014, Shneor 2007). The results enable a more reliable extraction of SRC properties from measured cross sections and cross-section ratios, such as the $A$-dependence of SRC pair abundances.
