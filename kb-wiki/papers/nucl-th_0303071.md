# Dilepton decays of nucleon resonances and dilepton production cross sections in proton-proton collisions

> Relativistic, kinematically complete phenomenological expressions for the dilepton decay rates of nucleon resonances with arbitrary spin and parity are derived in terms of the magnetic, electric, and Coulomb transition form factors. The dilepton decay rates of the nucleon resonances with masses below 2 GeV are estimated using the extended vector meson dominance (eVMD) model for the transition form factors. The model provides an unified description of the photo- and electroproduction data and of the vector meson and dilepton decays of the nucleon resonances. The constraints on the transition form factors from the quark counting rules are taken into account explicitly. The remaining parameters of the model are fixed by fitting the available photo- and electroproduction data and using results of the multichannel partial-wave analysis of the $πN$ scattering. The results are used to describe dilepton spectra measured at BEVALAC in proton-proton collisions.

# Dilepton decays of nucleon resonances and dilepton production cross sections in proton-proton collisions

## Key claim
The extended vector meson dominance (eVMD) model, constrained by quark counting rules and fitted to photo- and electroproduction data, provides a unified description of dilepton decays of nucleon resonances with masses below 2 GeV; however, the resulting dilepton spectra in proton-proton collisions agree reasonably well with BEVALAC data only at incident proton kinetic energies of 1.27–1.85 GeV, while agreement is not perfect at 1.04, 2.09, and 4.88 GeV.

## Method
Relativistic, kinematically complete phenomenological expressions for dilepton decay rates of nucleon resonances with arbitrary spin and parity were derived in terms of magnetic, electric, and Coulomb transition form factors. The transition form factors for resonances below 2 GeV were modeled using the eVMD approach, with parameters fixed by fitting photo- and electroproduction data and results from multichannel partial-wave analysis of πN scattering, while respecting asymptotic constraints from quark counting rules. The resulting decay widths were then used in a resonance-production model to compute dilepton production cross sections in proton-proton collisions at BEVALAC energies (1–5 GeV incident proton kinetic energy).

## Key numbers
- The dilepton decay rates of nucleon resonances with masses below 2 GeV were estimated using the eVMD model.
- The dilepton spectra in proton-proton collisions were computed at incident proton kinetic energies T = 1.04, 1.27, 1.85, 2.09, and 4.88 GeV.
- Reasonable agreement with BEVALAC data was found at T = 1.27–1.85 GeV; agreement was not perfect at T = 1.04, 2.09, and 4.88 GeV.
- The subthreshold production of ω mesons via nucleon resonance decays was treated with M-dependent coupling constants evaluated at the ω pole mass m_ω.

## Context
This work extends the author’s previous treatment of Δ(1232) dilepton decays (Krivoruchenko 2001) to nucleon resonances of arbitrary spin and parity, correcting earlier incorrect calculations of Δ(1232) → N e⁺ e⁻ decays available in the literature. The eVMD model addresses the “DLS puzzle”, the discrepancy between measured dilepton spectra from the DLS experiment at BEVALAC and theoretical estimates that included ρ-meson mass reduction and broadening (Bratkovskaya 1999). The results provide elementary cross sections needed as input for transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions, and the future HADES experiment at GSI (Friese 1999) will enable more detailed tests in the same energy range.
