# Probing confinement by direct photons and dileptons

> The intensive synchrotron radiation resulting from quarks interacting with the collective confining color field in relativistic heavy ion collisions is discussed. The spectrum of photons with large transverse momentum is calculated and compared with the experimental data to demonstrate the feasibility of this type of radiation. A study of the earlier predicted azimuthal anisotropy in the angular distribution of dileptons with respect to the three-momentum of the pair is performed as well. This boundary-induced mechanism of lepton pair production is shown to possess the features that are distinctly different from the standard mechanisms and can potentially provide an efficient probe of quark-gluon plasma formation.

# Probing confinement by direct photons and dileptons

## Key claim

Synchrotron radiation from quarks interacting with the collective confining color field at the QGP boundary can contribute to the observed direct photon excess and azimuthal anisotropy, but the absolute normalization is highly sensitive to the input hydrodynamic parameters (initial temperature T0 and proper time τ0), and the mechanism is not uniquely separable from other photon sources based on spectra alone.

## Method

The authors model quarks crossing the QGP boundary as experiencing a constant restoring force (string tension σ ≈ 0.2 GeV²) acting along the normal to the plasma surface, causing curved trajectories and synchrotron radiation. They derive the spectral-angular distribution of emitted photons using the strong-field (large χ) limit of synchrotron radiation theory, fold it with a hydrodynamical scaling (Bjorken) expansion of a cylindrically symmetric plasma volume, and compare the resulting high-transverse-momentum photon spectra with ALICE data for Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. They also compute the polarization of the emitted photons and the resulting azimuthal anisotropy in dilepton angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- String tension σ = 0.2 GeV², fixed from the chromoelectric flux tube model.
- For central collisions (0-20% centrality) at nominal input parameters T0 = 0.9 GeV and τ0 = 0.1 fm, the synchrotron radiation contributes to the experimentally measured direct photon rate at the level of 10%.
- For the 0-20% centrality class with a uniform transverse temperature profile, the spectrum is given by d²Nγ/(2πk⊥dk⊥dy) ∝ k⊥^{-13/3} σ^{-1/3} [Γ(7/2; k⊥/T0) − Γ(7/2; k⊥/Tc)].
- For the 0-20% centrality class with a finite boundary temperature Ts = 0.3 GeV, the synchrotron radiation contributes at the level of 5% across the transverse momentum range 1 < kt < 4 GeV/c.
- The primary degree of polarization for the synchrotron photons is P = 1/2, which reduces to about 20% after integration over a cylindrically symmetric plasma volume.
- The intermediate photons can be considered as having small virtuality up to masses √k² ≈ √σ = 0.45 GeV.
- The dimensionless combination (r Tc^{1/3} σ^{1/3})^{-1} that controls the relative importance of the boundary mechanism is ≈ 1 for r ≈ 0.6 fm at Tc = 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Goloviznin et al., 2012 and 2017) proposing synchrotron radiation from quarks at the QGP boundary as an alternative mechanism contributing to the "direct photon flow puzzle", the observed excess of direct photons with large azimuthal anisotropy at RHIC (PHENIX, 2010 and 2011) and LHC (ALICE) that standard thermal QGP radiation calculations underestimate. The mechanism is distinct from the standard volumetric photon production (Compton scattering gq → γq and annihilation qq̅ → γg) as described by Sinha (analytical estimates) and from the Drell-Yan mechanism. The predicted azimuthal anisotropy in dilepton angular distributions (proportional to the eccentricity ε of the QGP system) offers a potential experimental signature to distinguish this mechanism from other sources, building on suggestions by Chatterjee and collaborators for studying dilepton elliptic flow.
