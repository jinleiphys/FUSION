# Longitudinal Conductivity in Strong Magnetic Field in Perturbative QCD: Complete Leading Order

> We compute the longitudinal electrical conductivity in the presence of strong background magnetic field in complete leading order of perturbative QCD, based on the assumed hierarchy of scales $α_s eB\ll (m_q^2,T^2)\ll eB$. We formulate an effective kinetic theory of lowest Landau level quarks with the leading order QCD collision term arising from 1-to-2 processes that become possible due to 1+1 dimensional Landau level kinematics. In small $m_q/T\ll 1$ regime, the longitudinal conductivity behaves as $σ_{zz}\sim e^2(eB)T/(α_s m_q^2\log(m_q/T))$, where the quark mass dependence can be understood from the chiral anomaly with the axial charge relaxation provided by a finite quark mass $m_q$. We also present parametric estimates for the longitudinal and transverse "color conductivities" in the presence of strong magnetic field, by computing dominant damping rates for quarks and gluons that are responsible for color charge transportation. We observe that the longitudinal color conductivity is enhanced by strong magnetic field, which implies that the sphaleron transition rate in perturbative QCD is suppressed by strong magnetic field due to the enhanced Lenz's law in color field dynamics.

## Key claim
The longitudinal electrical conductivity in a strong magnetic field is computed at complete leading order in perturbative QCD, under the hierarchy $\alpha_s eB \ll (m_q^2, T^2) \ll eB$, and the result shows a universal $m_q^2$ suppression in the collision term that arises from the competing chirality and gluon-polarization selection rules.

## Method
The authors formulate an effective kinetic theory for lowest Landau level (LLL) quarks, which have 1+1 dimensional kinematics, interacting with 3+1 dimensional thermal gluons. The leading-order QCD collision term comes from 1-to-2 processes (pair creation/annihilation) that become kinematically allowed due to the LLL dispersion, and the Boltzmann equation is solved linearly for the deviation from equilibrium induced by an applied electric field parallel to the magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- The longitudinal conductivity in the small mass regime ($m_q/T \ll 1$) behaves as $\sigma_{zz} \sim e^2 (eB) T / (\alpha_s m_q^2 \log(T/m_q))$.
- The full result takes the form $\sigma_{zz} = e^2 \, \text{dim} R / C_2(R) \cdot (eB / 2\pi) \cdot 1/(\alpha_s T) \cdot \sigma_L(m_q/T)$, where $\sigma_L$ is a dimensionless function.
- The charge susceptibility of LLL states is $\chi = N_c \cdot (1/2\pi) \cdot (eB / 2\pi)$.
- The inverse relaxation time from a finite quark mass is $1/\tau_{R,m} \sim \alpha_s m_q^2 / T$.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD treatment of transport in strong magnetic fields initiated by Fukushima (2015) and provides the first complete leading-order computation of longitudinal conductivity. It contrasts with the strong-coupling AdS/CFT result of Basar (2012) and with lattice QCD computations by Buividovich (2010). The result enables quantitative predictions for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions and for the suppression of the sphaleron transition rate due to enhanced Lenz's law from increased longitudinal color conductivity.
