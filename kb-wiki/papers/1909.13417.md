# Robust ab initio predictions for nuclear rotational structure in the Be isotopes

> No-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for p-shell nuclei give rise to rotational bands, identified by strong intraband E2 transitions and by rotational patterns for excitation energies, electromagnetic moments, and electromagnetic transitions. However, convergence rates differ significantly for different rotational observables and for different rotational bands. The choice of internucleon interaction may also substantially impact the convergence rates. Consequently, there is a substantial gap between simply observing the qualitative emergence of rotation in ab initio calculations and actually carrying out detailed quantitative comparisons. In this contribution, we illustrate the convergence properties of rotational band energy parameters extracted from NCCI calculations, and compare these predictions with experiment, for the isotopes 7-11Be, and for the JISP16 and Daejeon16 interactions.

## Key claim

No-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for the Be isotopes can provide quantitatively robust predictions for rotational band energy parameters, but the convergence rates differ significantly for different rotational observables and bands, and the choice of internucleon interaction substantially impacts convergence rates, so there remains a gap between observing qualitative emergence of rotation and making detailed quantitative comparisons.

## Method

The authors performed no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for the isotopes 7-11Be using the MFDn code, with the JISP16 and Daejeon16 internucleon interactions including Coulomb interaction between protons. They extracted rotational band energy parameters (the rotational constant A, Coriolis decoupling parameter a, and band excitation energy Ex) from calculated energy eigenvalues at successive Nmax truncations (Nmax=6, 8, 10 for natural parity; Nmax=7, 9, 11 for unnatural parity) and at varying oscillator basis length scales given by hw. They also applied exponential extrapolation in Nmax to estimate full-space results.

## Key numbers

- For the K^P=1/2^- band of 11Be, the experimental rotational constant is A=0.51 MeV and the Coriolis decoupling parameter is a=0.52
- For the K^P=3/2^- band of 11Be, the experimental rotational constant is A=0.26 MeV and the band excitation energy is Ex=2.77 MeV
- For the K^P=1/2^+ band of 11Be, the experimental rotational constant is A=0.40 MeV, the Coriolis decoupling parameter is a=1.80, and the band excitation energy is Ex=0.22 MeV
- The ground state of 11Be is 1/2^+, lying 0.320 MeV below a 1/2^- excited state experimentally
- Calculations were performed at hw=20 MeV for JISP16 and hw=15 MeV for Daejeon16, corresponding to the approximate variational energy minimum
- The excited K^P=3/2^- band has a moment of inertia roughly twice that of the K^P=1/2^- band
- The Coriolis decoupling parameter for the K^P=1/2^+ band shows Nmax dependence at about the 10% level
- The excitation energy for the K^P=1/2^+ band in Daejeon16 calculations approaches convergence at the ~0.1-0.2 MeV level

## Context

This work extends previous NCCI calculations for p-shell nuclei that identified rotational bands through strong intraband E2 transitions and rotational patterns (Caprio 2013, Maris 2015). The authors build on earlier studies that found convergence rates differ for different rotational observables and interactions (Caprio 2015). The results enable quantitative comparison of ab initio predictions with experimental rotational band parameters across the Be isotopic chain, and the consistency between JISP16 (derived from J-matrix inverse scattering by Shirokov 2007) and Daejeon16 (derived from chiral perturbation theory by Shirokov 2016) interactions suggests robust predictions are possible despite remaining convergence challenges.
