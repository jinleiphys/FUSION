# $nd$ Scattering and the $A_y$ Puzzle to Next-to-next-to-next-to-leading Order

> Polarization observables in neutron-deuteron scattering are calculated to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order ($\mathrm{N}^{3}\mathrm{LO}$) in pionless effective field theory ($\mathrm{EFT}_{\notπ}$). At $\mathrm{N}^{3}\mathrm{LO}$ the two-body $P$-wave contact interactions are found to be important contributions to the neutron vector analyzing power, $A_{y}(θ)$, and the deuteron vector analyzing power, $iT_{11}(θ)$. Extracting the two-body $P$-wave $\mathrm{EFT}_{\notπ}$ coefficients from two-body scattering data and varying them within the expected $\mathrm{EFT}_{\notπ}$ theoretical errors provides results that are consistent (at the $\mathrm{N}^{3}\mathrm{LO}$ level) with $A_y$ experimental data at low energies. Cutoff dependence of the $\mathrm{N}^{3}\mathrm{LO}$ correction of the doublet $S$-wave $nd$ scattering amplitude suggests the need for a new three-body force at $\mathrm{N}^{3}\mathrm{LO}$, which is likely one that mixes Wigner- symmetric and Wigner-antisymmetric three-body channels.

## Key claim

The neutron vector analyzing power \(A_y(\theta)\) and deuteron vector analyzing power \(iT_{11}(\theta)\) in neutron-deuteron scattering, calculated to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory, become consistent with low-energy experimental data when the two-body \(P\)-wave contact coefficients are extracted from two-body scattering data and varied within the expected theoretical errors of the EFT, but only at the N\(^3\)LO level.

## Method

The authors used a strictly perturbative approach in pionless effective field theory to calculate neutron-deuteron scattering amplitudes to N\(^3\)LO, projecting the integral equations into a partial-wave basis up to \(L=4\) and solving them via the Hetherington-Schick method along a contour in the complex plane. Two-body \(P\)-wave contact interactions were included by introducing \(P\)-wave auxiliary fields, and the three-body forces in the doublet \(S\)-wave channel were fit to the doublet \(S\)-wave \(nd\) scattering length and the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers

* The LO three-body force is fit to the doublet \(S\)-wave \(nd\) scattering length \(a_{nd} = 0.65\) fm.
* The deuteron bound state momentum is \(\gamma_t = 45.7025\) MeV; the spin-singlet virtual bound state momentum is \(\gamma_s = -7.890\) MeV.
* The asymptotic \(D/S\) mixing ratio of the deuteron wavefunction is \(\eta_{sd} = 0.02543 \pm 0.00007\).
* The two-body \(P\)-wave coefficients fit to \(np\) Nijmegen phase shifts are: \(C^{{}^{3}\!P_0} = 6.27\) fm\(^4\), \(C^{{}^{3}\!P_1} = -5.75\) fm\(^4\), \(C^{{}^{3}\!P_2} = 0.522\) fm\(^4\), and \(C^{{}^{1}\!P_1} = -19.8\) fm\(^4\).
* The shape parameter corrections are \(\rho_{1t} = 0.389\) fm\(^3\) (for the triplet channel) and \(\rho_{1s} = -0.48\) fm\(^3\) (for the singlet channel).
* The N\(^2\)LO parameter for the splitting between \(nn\) and \(np\) spin-singlet scattering lengths is \(\Delta_s^{(\text{N}^2\text{LO})} = -2.02\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous N\(^2\)LO calculations of \(nd\) scattering in pionless EFT (Vanasse 2013, 2015) by including N\(^3\)LO contributions, particularly the two-body \(P\)-wave contact interactions that give the dominant contribution to the \(A_y\) puzzle, as already identified in phenomenological potential model calculations (Witala 1989, Tornow 1991). The cutoff dependence of the N\(^3\)LO correction to the doublet \(S\)-wave amplitude suggests the need for a new three-body force mixing Wigner-symmetric and Wigner-antisymmetric channels at this order, supporting the claim of Birse (2005) over the earlier expectation of Griesshammer (2005). This enables future work to include that missing three-body force and extend the calculation to higher energies or to proton-deuteron scattering.
