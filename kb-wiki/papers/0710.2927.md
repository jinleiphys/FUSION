# Renormalization group analysis of the chiral pion production operator for NN\to dπ

> We are interested in the consistency between the cutoff, chiral symmetry, and the power counting. For this purpose, we apply the Wilsonian renormalization group (RG) to an operator and then decrease the Wilsonian cutoff. As an example, we study the s-wave pion production operator for NN\to dπ, derived in chiral perturbation theory. We find that the renormalized part of the RG effective operator is accurately absorbed by chiral counter terms of higher order with natural coefficients. Thus, the use of the (sharp) cutoff regularization does not require us to introduce chiral-symmetry-breaking counter terms, at least in the case of the NN\to dπreaction.

## Key claim

The use of a sharp cutoff regularization in chiral perturbation theory for the \(NN\to d\pi\) reaction does not require introducing chiral-symmetry-breaking counter terms, at least to the level of precision and order studied; the renormalized part of the Wilsonian RG effective operator is accurately absorbed by chiral counter terms of higher order with natural coefficients.

## Method

The authors applied the Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) equation to the chiral NLO \(s\)-wave pion production operator for \(NN\to d\pi\), starting with the operator derived in chiral perturbation theory. They introduced a cutoff and reduced it using the WRG equation to obtain a low-momentum operator for \(\Lambda = 500\) MeV, using the CD-Bonn \(NN\)-potential and near-threshold kinematics (\(\eta = 0.1\), where \(\eta = q/m_\pi\)). They then attempted to reproduce the RG low-momentum operator using the starting NLO operator plus chiral counter terms of higher order, fitting the counter terms to the renormalized part while omitting the kink part.

## Key numbers

- The RG low-momentum operator was calculated for \(\Lambda = 500\) MeV using the CD-Bonn \(NN\)-potential.
- Near-threshold kinematics were employed with \(\eta = 0.1\), where \(\eta = q/m_\pi\) (emitted pion momentum divided by pion mass).
- The running of the radial part of the \(^3P_1\to {}^3S_1\) transition operator was shown for cutoff values \(\Lambda = 1000\) MeV, 750 MeV, and 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Wilsonian renormalization group to transition operators in chiral perturbation theory, following earlier derivations for the \(NN\) interaction by one of the authors (Nakamura, 2004). It addresses the question of whether cutoff regularization in chiral effective field theory violates chiral symmetry, a concern that had been raised in prior work on nuclear operators. The result enables future calculations of \(NN\to d\pi\) and similar reactions to use sharp cutoff regularization without introducing ad hoc chiral-symmetry-breaking terms, provided the power counting is consistently applied.
