# Modeling anisotropic magnetized strange quark stars

> Compact objects have an intrinsic anisotropy due to the presence of strong magnetic fields that cause considerable modifications on the equations of state (EoS). In this work, we study the impact of this anisotropy in the size and shape of magnetized strange quark stars using an axially symmetric metric in spherical coordinates, the gamma-metric. The results are compared with those obtained with the standard Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for the parallel and perpendicular pressures independently. Differences in the results are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that modeling magnetized strange quark stars with an axially symmetric gamma-metric yields different mass-radius relations and deformation behavior compared to the standard Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations applied separately to the parallel and perpendicular pressures, but the gamma-metric approach is only valid for magnetic fields up to \(5\times10^{17}\) G because at higher fields the anisotropy parameter \(\gamma\) deviates too far from unity.

## Method

The authors used the MIT Bag model to compute the equation of state for strange quark matter under a constant, uniform magnetic field, obtaining separate parallel and perpendicular pressures. They then solved two sets of structure equations: the standard spherically symmetric TOV equations using each pressure component independently, and the axially symmetric gamma-metric structure equations that couple both pressures simultaneously, for a strange quark mass of 150 MeV and bag constant \(B_{\text{bag}} = 75\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- Maximum magnetic field considered for the gamma-metric solutions: \(B = 5\times10^{17}\) G; the field \(B = 10^{18}\) G was excluded because \(\gamma\) fell outside the approximation range \(\gamma \simeq 1\).
- Observed surface magnetic fields of neutron stars range from \(10^{9}\) to \(10^{15}\) G, while inner fields may reach \(5\times10^{18}\) G.
- Critical magnetic fields: for electrons \(B^c_e \sim 10^{13}\) G, for up quarks \(B^c_u \sim 10^{15}\) G, for down quarks \(B^c_d \sim 10^{16}\) G, and for strange quarks \(B^c_s \sim 10^{19}\) G.
- The bag constant \(B_{\text{bag}} = 75\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) guarantees stability of strange quark matter at \(B=0\) for a strange quark mass of 150 MeV and baryon density of 2.18 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The maximum masses obtained with the gamma-metric do not reach \(2\,M_{\odot}\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of magnetized strange quark matter by Felipe et al. (2008) and applies the gamma-metric structure equations previously developed for magnetized Bose-Einstein Condensate stars and white dwarfs (prd, BEC) to strange quark stars. The results highlight that the deformation predicted by the gamma-metric decreases with mass, opposite to the trend from separate TOV solutions, and that the magnetic field increases maximum masses for strange stars unlike other compact objects studied with the same equations. The authors note that the model can be improved by including a magnetic-field-dependent bag parameter and rotation.
