# Theoretical study of the d(d,p)3H and d(d,n)3He processes at low energies

> We present a theoretical study of the processes d(d,p)3H and d(d,n)3He at energies of interest for energy production and for big-bang nucleosynthesis. We accurately solve the four body scattering problem using the ab-initio hyperspherical harmonic method, starting from nuclear Hamiltonians which include modern two- and three-nucleon interactions, derived in chiral effective field theory. We report results for the astrophysical factor, the quintet suppression factor, and various single and double polarized observables. An estimate of the "theoretical uncertainty" for all these quantities is provided by varying the cutoff parameter used to regularize the chiral interactions at high momentum.

## Key claim
The paper presents ab-initio calculations of the d(d,p)3H and d(d,n)3He reactions that reproduce the energy dependence of experimental data, but the theoretical uncertainty is estimated only from the spread between two cutoff values (500 and 600 MeV) rather than a full Bayesian analysis, and the authors note this is a preliminary estimate.

## Method
The four-nucleon scattering problem was solved using the hyperspherical harmonic expansion method, including all partial waves up to L=4. The nuclear Hamiltonians employed chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at N3LO (with cutoffs 500 and 600 MeV) combined with a chiral three-nucleon interaction at N2LO, whose parameters cD and cE were fixed to reproduce A=3 binding energies and the tritium beta-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element.

## Key numbers
- Astrophysical S-factor at zero energy for d(d,p)3H: S(0) = 50.8 ± 1.9 keV b
- Astrophysical S-factor at zero energy for d(d,n)3He: S(0) = 51.0 ± 1.4 keV b
- At Td < 100 keV, the dominant contributions come from L=0 T-matrix elements, with the TME 2T(3,gamma')_{02,21} giving the largest contribution
- At Td > 100 keV, the TME 1T(3,gamma')_{11,11} becomes dominant
- The theoretical error for the double-polarized observables Az,z and Azz,zz is estimated to be of order 5%
- The astrophysical S-factor for d(d,n)3He is larger than that of d(d,p)3H for Ecm > 0.1 MeV

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of deuteron-deuteron fusion reactions using the hyperspherical harmonic method, complementing previous calculations using Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations by Deltuva (2010) and the Correlated Gaussian method by Aoyama (2012). The results for the quintet suppression factor agree well with the Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation of Deltuva (2010) and the R-matrix analysis of Leonard (2006), but differ from earlier analyses by Lemaitre (1993), Fick (1983), Hofmann (1984), and Uzu (2002). The predictions for double-polarized observables Az,z and Azz,zz will be tested by the upcoming PolFusion experiment.
