# Strangeness Neutrality and QCD Thermodynamics

> Since the incident nuclei in heavy-ion collisions do not carry strangeness, the global net strangeness of the detected hadrons has to vanish. We investigate the impact of strangeness neutrality on the phase structure and thermodynamics of QCD at finite baryon and strangeness chemical potential. To this end, we study the low-energy sector of QCD within a Polyakov loop enhanced quark-meson effective theory with 2+1 dynamical quark flavors. Non-perturbative quantum, thermal, and density fluctuations are taken into account with the functional renormalization group. We show that the impact of strangeness neutrality on thermodynamic quantities such as the equation of state is sizable.

## Key claim

Strangeness neutrality has a sizable impact on thermodynamic quantities such as the equation of state of QCD at finite baryon chemical potential, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of a low-energy effective theory (Polyakov loop enhanced quark-meson model with 2+1 flavors) with non-perturbative fluctuations treated via the functional renormalization group, rather than being derived from full QCD.

## Method

The authors constructed a Polyakov loop enhanced quark-meson (PQM) effective theory with 2+1 dynamical quark flavors, incorporating a temporal gluon background field and an effective gluon potential to capture statistical confinement. They derived and solved functional renormalization group equations for this model at finite baryon and strangeness chemical potential, imposing the strangeness neutrality condition (net strangeness density n_S = 0) to determine the T- and mu_B-dependent strangeness chemical potential mu_S.

## Key numbers

* At T = 0 and mu_B = 0, kaon condensation is expected if mu_S is greater than approximately m_K (the kaon mass), but the authors restrict their study to mu_S less than about 200 MeV where their approximation is expected to be valid.
* The ultraviolet cutoff scale Lambda for the effective theory is approximately 1 GeV, below which gluons decouple from the matter sector.
* The Polyakov loop saturates to unity for temperatures T greater than approximately 1.25 T_c (the critical temperature).

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of the 2+1 flavor PQM model at vanishing density (Herbst, 2013) to finite baryon chemical potential, and introduces a finite strange chemical potential to impose strangeness neutrality for the first time beyond mean-field. It builds on mean-field studies of strangeness neutrality in PNJL models (Fukushima, 2009) and lattice QCD studies of freeze-out conditions (Bazavov, 2012; Borsanyi, 2013), while addressing the limitation that lattice methods rely on expansions in mu_B/T that may have large systematic errors for mu_B/T greater than about 1. The results enable more realistic comparisons with heavy-ion collision data from beam-energy scan experiments at RHIC and LHC.
