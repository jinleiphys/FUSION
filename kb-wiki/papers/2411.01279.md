# Proton induced reaction on $^{108}$Cd for astrophysical p-process studies

> The proton capture cross-section of the least abundant proton-rich stable isotope of cadmium, $^{108}$Cd (abundance 0.89\%), has been measured near the Gamow window corresponding to a temperature range of 3-4 GK. The measurement of the $^{108}$Cd(p,$γ$)$^{109}$In reaction was carried out using the activation technique. The cross-section at the lowest energy point of 3T$_9$, E$_p$$^{lab}$= 2.28 MeV, has been reported for the first time. The astrophysical S-factor was measured in the energy range relevant to the astrophysical p-process, between E$_p$$^{cm}$= 2.29 and 6.79 MeV. The experimental results have been compared with theoretical predictions of Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using TALYS-1.96. A calculated proton-optical potential was implemented to achieve better fitting, with different combinations of available nuclear level densities (NLDs) and $γ$-ray strength functions in TALYS-1.96. The calculations provided satisfactory agreement with the experimental results. The reaction rate was calculated using the calculated potential in TALYS-1.96 and compared with the values provided in the REACLIB database.

# Proton induced reaction on $^{108}$Cd for astrophysical p-process studies

## Key claim
The cross-section for the $^{108}$Cd(p,$\gamma$)$^{109}$In reaction has been measured at the lowest energy point of 2.28 MeV (lab) within the Gamow window for 3 GK stellar temperature for the first time, using the stack foil activation technique with a 66.3% enriched target. The experimental results show satisfactory agreement with Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using TALYS-1.96 only when a newly calculated proton-optical potential is implemented, while default potentials overpredict the cross-section at lower energies within the Gamow window.

## Method
The cross-section was measured using the activation technique at the K130 Cyclotron at VECC, Kolkata, with four stack foil setups irradiated by a 7 MeV proton beam for 6 to 17 hours at 150-200 nA. Eight enriched $^{108}$Cd targets (66.3% enrichment, thicknesses 288-659 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ on mylar backing) were used across nine energy points from 2.29 to 6.85 MeV, with offline gamma-ray counting using a HPGe detector. A Gaussian-averaged cross-section correction was applied to account for beam energy uncertainty and energy loss/straggling in the stacked targets, and the experimental results were compared with TALYS-1.96 calculations using 216 combinations of proton-optical potentials, nuclear level densities, and gamma-ray strength functions.

## Key numbers
- Cross-section at the lowest energy point (effective lab energy 2.26 $\pm$ 0.12 MeV): 1.4 $\pm$ 0.2 $\mu$b
- Cross-section at the highest energy point (effective lab energy 6.84 $\pm$ 0.03 MeV): 40433.2 $\pm$ 4071.4 $\mu$b
- Astrophysical S-factor at 2.24 $\pm$ 0.12 MeV (center-of-mass): 11.72 $\pm$ 2.12 $\times$ 10$^7$ MeV-b
- Astrophysical S-factor at 6.78 $\pm$ 0.03 MeV (center-of-mass): 2.06 $\pm$ 0.74 $\times$ 10$^7$ MeV-b
- Target enrichment: 66.3% (compared to 2.05% in prior work by Gyurky et al.)
- Target thickness range: 288 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ to 659 $\mu$g/cm$^2$, with approximately 10% uncertainty
- Reaction rate for $^{108}$Cd(p,$\gamma$) at 3 GK using calculated potential (Fit2): 19.2 cm$^3$ s$^{-1}$ mol$^{-1}$ (REACLIB value: 23.6 cm$^3$ s$^{-1}$ mol$^{-1}$)
- Reaction rate for $^{109}$In($\gamma$,p) at 3 GK using calculated potential (Fit2): 6050 cm$^3$ s$^{-1}$ mol$^{-1}$ (REACLIB value: 5960 cm$^3$ s$^{-1}$ mol$^{-1}$)

## Context
This work extends the measurements of Gyurky et al. (2007), who measured $^{108}$Cd(p,$\gamma$) cross-sections between 2.4-4.8 MeV using only 2.05% enriched targets, and Olivas et al. (2020), who used an in-beam sum-spectrometer technique with thick targets (2.09 mg/cm$^2$) from 3.5-7.0 MeV. The present measurement provides the first cross-section at the lowest energy point within the Gamow window for 3 GK and uses significantly higher target enrichment (66.3%). The newly calculated proton-optical potential, derived from elastic scattering data on 17 p-nuclei and nearby isotopes, enables improved theoretical predictions for p-process reaction rates. The results enable more accurate reaction rate calculations for the $^{108}$Cd(p,$\gamma$) and $^{109}$In($\gamma$,p) reactions in p-process nucleosynthesis networks, with the calculated rates differing from the REACLIB database values by a factor of less than 1.5.
