# Super strong nuclear force caused by migrating Kbar mesons - Revival of the Heitler-London-Heisenberg scheme in kaonic nuclear clusters

> We have studied the structure of K- pp comprehensively by solving this three-body system in a variational method, starting from the Ansatz that the Lambda(1405) resonance (~ Lambda*) is a K-p bound state. The structure of K-pp reveals a molecular feature, namely, the K- in Lambda* as an "atomic center" plays a key role in producing strong covalent bonding with the other proton. We point out that strongly bound Kbar nuclear systems are formed by ``super strong" nuclear force due to migrating real bosonic particles Kbar a la Heitler-London-Heisenberg, whereas the normal nuclear force is caused by mediating virtual pions. We have shown that the elementary process, p + p --> K+ + Lambda* + p, which occurs in a short impact parameter and with a large momentum transfer, leads to unusually large self-trapping of Lambda* by the involved proton, since the Lambda*-p system exists as a compact doorway state propagating to K-pp.

# Super strong nuclear force caused by migrating Kbar mesons - Revival of the Heitler-London-Heisenberg scheme in kaonic nuclear clusters

## Key claim

The authors claim that the K⁻ meson, as a real migrating bosonic particle, produces a "super strong" nuclear force between nucleons via a Heitler-London-Heisenberg covalent bonding mechanism, in contrast to normal nuclear force mediated by virtual pions. This claim is conditional on the Ansatz that the Λ(1405) resonance is a K⁻p bound state, and the analysis is limited to the specific three-body system K⁻pp.

## Method

The authors solved the K⁻pp three-body system using the ATMS variational method with the bare Kbar-N interaction of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and the bare NN interaction of Tamagaki. The variational wave function incorporated isospin eigenstates (I=0 and I=1) for the Kbar-N pairs and included scattering correlation functions for all particle pairs. Effective adiabatic potentials and density distributions were extracted from the total wave function to characterize the molecular structure.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of K⁻p (Λ(1405)): B_K = 27 MeV, with width Γ = 40 MeV
- Predicted mass of K⁻pp: M = 2322 MeV/c², with B_K = 48 MeV and Γ = 61 MeV
- Average density of predicted kaonic nuclei: ρ_av ∼ 0.5 fm⁻³, about 3 times normal nuclear density ρ_0 ∼ 0.17 fm⁻³
- N-N rms distance in K⁻pp: 1.90 fm, compared to 2.2 fm in normal nuclei and 3.90 fm in deuteron
- Kbar-(NN) rms radius: 1.35 fm, close to the Kbar-N rms distance in Λ(1405) of 1.36 fm
- Kbar-N rms distance in K⁻pp: 1.57 fm, broader than in Λ(1405) (1.36 fm)
- Exchange integral from strong I=0 KbarN attraction: -52.6 MeV
- Volume-integrated strength ratio of super strong to normal nuclear force: 4.1
- Predicted binding energy of double-K⁻ cluster K⁻K⁻pp: B_KK = 117 MeV, with inter-nucleon distance R_N-N_rms = 1.3 fm
- Momentum transfer in p+p → K⁺ + K⁻pp reaction: Q ∼ 1.6 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier predictions of kaonic nuclear clusters (Akaishi and Yamazaki, 2002; Yamazaki and Akaishi, 2002; Dote et al., 2004) and provides a molecular interpretation of the K⁻pp system as a revival of the Heitler-London-Heisenberg scheme originally proposed for nuclear force in 1932 but abandoned in favor of Yukawa's virtual pion mechanism (1935). The results are consistent with Faddeev calculations by Shevchenko et al. (2006) and Ikeda and Sato (2006). The work enables experimental searches for K⁻pp in pp collisions, such as the DISTO experiment at Saclay and planned experiments at GSI, and opens the possibility of studying kaon condensation and ultra-dense strange matter.
