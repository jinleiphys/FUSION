# Nucleon-nucleon cross sections in neutron-rich matter

> We calculate nucleon-nucleon cross sections in the nuclear medium with unequal densities of protons and neutrons. We use the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach together with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. We examine the effect of neutron/proton asymmetry and find that, although generally mild, it can be significant in specific regions of the phase space under consideration.

# Nucleon-nucleon cross sections in neutron-rich matter

## Key claim

The effect of neutron/proton asymmetry on in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections is generally mild, but can be significant in specific regions of phase space; the general tendency is for the pp cross section to decrease and the nn cross section to increase with rising asymmetry, indicating that the effective mass effect dominates over Pauli blocking in the regions examined.

## Method

The authors use the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach with the Bonn-B realistic nucleon-nucleon potential to calculate in-medium G-matrices and cross sections for pp, nn, and np scattering in asymmetric nuclear matter. The cross sections are obtained by direct integration of the differential cross section from the scattering matrix elements, avoiding the assumption of in-vacuum unitarity, with the calculation controlled by total density ρ and asymmetry parameter α = (ρ_n − ρ_p)/(ρ_n + ρ_p).

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations of in-medium NN cross sections by Li and Machleidt (1993-1994) and Fuchs, Faessler, and El-Shabshiry (2001), which did not include asymmetry effects, and contrasts with simpler scaling approaches using effective masses as in Persram and Gale (2002). The results are relevant for isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport models used to simulate collisions of neutron-rich nuclei at facilities like the Rare Isotope Accelerator, and for calculating nucleon mean free paths and nuclear transparency in neutron-rich matter. The authors note that their predictions do not confirm the large resonance-like peaks reported by Alm, Ropke, and Schmidt (1994) that were interpreted as precursors of a superfluid phase transition.
