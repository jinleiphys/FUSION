# Hadronic Atoms and Effective Interactions

> We examine the problem of hadronic atom energy shifts using the technique of effective interactions and demonstrate equivalence with the conventional quantum mechanical approach.

# Hadronic Atoms and Effective Interactions

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the effective field theory approach to calculating hadronic atom energy shifts is completely equivalent to the traditional quantum mechanical method, but only when the bound state energy is determined via the presence of a pole in the scattering matrix rather than through simple second-order perturbation theory.

## Method
The author derives the energy shifts and decay widths of hadronic atoms using both a conventional quantum mechanical approach with square-well strong potentials and Coulomb wavefunctions, and an effective field theory approach using a local contact interaction Lagrangian with cutoff regularization. The equivalence is shown by matching the bound state pole conditions in the scattering amplitude for both single-channel and coupled-channel cases, including effective range corrections.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy shift for pionium: ΔE_gs^(0)(π⁺π⁻) = (4π/3m_π⁺)(a₂+2a₀)|Ψ(0)|², where a₀ = -7m_π/(32πF_π²) and a₂ = m_π/(16πF_π²) with F_π ≈ 92.4 MeV
- Ground state decay width for pionium: Γ_gs^(0)(π⁺π⁻) = (16π/9m_π⁺)√(2m_π⁰Δm_π)(a₂-a₀)²|Ψ(0)|²
- First-order correction to pionium decay width: Γ_gs^(1)/Γ_gs^(0) = 8.3%
- First-order correction to pionium energy shift: ΔE_gs^(1)/ΔE_gs^(0) = -8.2×10⁻⁴
- Panofsky ratio: P = Γ_γn/Γ_π⁰n = 1.546 ± 0.009
- Extracted πN scattering lengths from π⁻p experiments: b₁ = 0.096 ± 0.007 m_π⁻¹, b₀ = 0.0105 ± 0.007 m_π⁻¹
- Chiral symmetry prediction range for b₁: 0.088 m_π⁻¹ to 0.096 m_π⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach of Kong and Ravndal (1998) for pionium decay width and pp scattering to the general problem of hadronic atom energy shifts, making explicit contact with the traditional quantum mechanical treatment found in Deser (1954) and Jackson and Blatt (1950). The paper enables the use of effective field theory for ongoing experimental programs at CERN (pionium) and PSI (π⁻p atom), providing a framework for extracting ππ and πN scattering lengths from measured energy shifts and decay widths.
