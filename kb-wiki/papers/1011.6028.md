# Collective Excitations of Supersymmetric Plasma

> Collective excitations of N = 1 supersymmetric electromagnetic plasma are studied. Since the Keldysh-Schwinger approach is used, not only equilibrium but also non-equilibrium plasma, which is assumed to be ultrarelativistic, is under consideration. The dispersion equations of photon, photino, electron and selectron modes are written down and the self-energies, which enter the equations, are computed in the Hard Loop Approximation. The self-energies are discussed in the context of effective action which is also given. The photon modes and electron ones appear to be the same as in the usual ultrarelativistic plasma of electrons, positrons and photons. The photino modes coincide with the electron ones and the selectron modes are as of free relativistic massive particle.

## Key claim

The collective excitations of N=1 supersymmetric electromagnetic plasma are studied using the Keldysh-Schwinger formalism, which allows treatment of both equilibrium and non-equilibrium ultrarelativistic plasmas; the photon and electron modes are identical to those of ordinary ultrarelativistic QED plasma, while photino modes coincide with electron modes and selectron modes behave as free relativistic massive particles.

## Method

The authors computed the retarded self-energies for photons, electrons, photinos, and selectrons in N=1 supersymmetric QED using the Keldysh-Schwinger formalism within the Hard Loop Approximation, assuming a homogeneous but potentially anisotropic ultrarelativistic plasma with neutral charge and equal particle-antiparticle distribution functions. The dispersion equations for each particle type were written down, and the self-energies were evaluated from the relevant Feynman diagrams (electron loop, selectron loop, selectron tadpole for photons; electron-photon and selectron-photino loops for electrons; electron-selectron loops for photinos; and selectron tadpole, photon tadpole, selectron-photon loop, and electron-photino loop for selectrons).

## Context

This work extends the study of collective excitations in supersymmetric plasmas beyond the equilibrium imaginary-time formalism used by Chesler (2009) to non-equilibrium plasmas using the Keldysh-Schwinger approach, which allows investigation of unstable modes that dominate plasma dynamics. The Hard Loop Approximation employed generalizes the equilibrium treatment reviewed by Thoma (1995), Blaizot (2001), and Kraemmer (2003) to anisotropic systems following Mrowczynski (2000, 2004). The results enable comparison between supersymmetric and ordinary electromagnetic plasmas, and provide a foundation for testing whether supersymmetry induces instabilities in the photino sector when photon modes are unstable, a question motivated by the absence of unstable fermion modes in non-supersymmetric plasmas noted by Mrowczynski (2001) and Schenke (2006).
