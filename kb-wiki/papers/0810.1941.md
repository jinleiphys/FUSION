# Electromagnetic two-body currents of one- and two-pion range

> Nuclear electromagnetic currents are derived in time-ordered perturbation theory within an effective-field-theory framework including explicit nucleons, $Δ$ isobars, and pions up to one loop, or N$^3$LO. The currents obtained at N$^2$LO, {\it i.e.} ignoring loop corrections, are used in a study of neutron radiative captures on protons and deuterons at thermal energies, and of $A$=2 and 3 nuclei magnetic moments. The wave functions for $A$=2 are derived from solutions of the Schrödinger equation with the Argonne $v_{18}$ (AV18) or CD-Bonn (CDB) potentials, while those for $A$=3 are obtained with the hyperspherical-harmonics-expansion method from a realistic Hamiltonian including, in addition to the AV18 or CDB two-nucleon, also a three-nucleon potential. With the strengths of the $Δ$-excitation currents occurring at N$^2$LO determined to reproduce the $n$-$p$ cross section and isovector combination of the trinucleon magnetic moments, we find that the cross section and photon circular polarization parameter, measured in $n$-$d$ and $\vec{n}$-$d$ processes, are underpredicted by theory, for example the cross section by (11--38)% as the cutoff is increased from 500 to 800 MeV. A complete analysis of the results, in particular their large cutoff dependence, is presented.

## Key claim

The N²LO electromagnetic currents (ignoring loop corrections) derived in a chiral effective field theory framework that includes nucleons, Δ isobars, and pions do not provide a satisfactory description of experimental data for suppressed radiative capture processes, particularly the ²H(n,γ)³H reaction, and exhibit a large cutoff dependence that indicates the necessity of including loop corrections at N³LO.

## Method

The authors derived nuclear electromagnetic currents using time-ordered perturbation theory within an effective-field-theory framework including explicit nucleons, Δ isobars, and pions, organized by power counting up to N²LO (tree level) and N³LO (one-loop). They applied the N²LO currents to calculate magnetic moments of A=2 and 3 nuclei and thermal neutron radiative captures on protons and deuterons, using wave functions from the Argonne v18 (AV18) or CD-Bonn (CDB) two-nucleon potentials for A=2, and from the hyperspherical-harmonics-expansion method with realistic Hamiltonians including three-nucleon potentials for A=3.

## Key numbers

- The n-p capture cross section at thermal energies is 332.6 ± 0.7 mb (experimental value from Mughabghab 1981)
- The n-d capture cross section at thermal energies is 0.508 ± 0.015 mb (experimental value from Jurney 1982)
- The n-³He capture cross section at thermal energies is 0.055 ± 0.003 mb (experimental value from Wolfs 1989)
- The n-d capture cross section is underpredicted by theory by 11% to 38% as the cutoff is increased from 500 to 800 MeV
- The one-body (impulse-approximation) term of the magnetic dipole operator gives only 45% of the n-d cross-section experimental value and only 10% of the n-³He cross-section experimental value
- The ³H magnetic moment is 2.979 n.m. (experimental value), while the proton magnetic moment μp = 2.793 n.m.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid approach of Park et al. (2003) for weak capture processes to electromagnetic observables, and is similar to the χEFT current derivation of Park et al. (1996) but uses a different formalism and includes Δ-isobar degrees of freedom explicitly. The calculations show that the N²LO currents, unlike the standard nuclear physics approach currents used by Marcucci et al. (2005) which overpredict the n-d capture cross section by about 10%, underpredict the same cross section, pointing to the need for including one-loop corrections at N³LO to resolve the discrepancies with experimental data.
