# Proton-proton hollowness at the LHC from inverse scattering

> Parameterizations of the pp scattering data at the LHC collision energies indicate a hollow in the inelasticity profile of the pp interaction, with less absorption for head-on collisions than at a non-zero impact parameter. We show that some qualitatively unnoticed features may be unveiled by a judicious application of the inverse scattering problem in the eikonal approximation and interpre- tation within an optical potential model. The hollowness effect is magnifed in a 3D picture of the optical potential, and will presumably be enhanced at yet higher energies. Moreover, in 3D it sets in at much smaller energies than at the LHC. We argue that hollowness in the impact parameter is a quantum effect, relying on the build-up of the real part of the eikonal scattering phase and its possible passage through $π/2$. We also show that it precludes models of inelastic collisions where inelasticity is obtained by naive folding of partonic densities.

# Proton-proton hollowness at the LHC from inverse scattering

## Key claim

The hollowness effect in pp collisions, where there is less absorption for head-on collisions (impact parameter b=0) than at non-zero impact parameter, is a quantum effect that relies on the build-up of the real part of the eikonal scattering phase and its possible passage through π/2, and it precludes models where inelasticity is obtained by naive folding of partonic densities.

## Method

The authors apply inverse scattering methods in the eikonal approximation to extract the optical potential and on-shell optical potential from parameterizations of pp elastic scattering data. They use the modified Barger-Phillips (MBP2) parametrization of Fagundes et al. (2013) fitted to differential cross sections at multiple collision energies, then perform numerical inverse Fourier-Bessel transforms to obtain the impact-parameter representation, and finally use Abel integral equations to reconstruct the 3D optical potentials from the eikonal phase.

## Key numbers

- The hollowness in the 3D optical potential emerges already at √s ~ 1 TeV, well below LHC energies
- At √s = 7 TeV, the inelasticity profile n_in(b) develops a shallow minimum at b=0, with the maximum shifting to b>0
- The curvature of n_in(b) at b=0 becomes negative at √s ≃ 3 TeV, marking the onset of the 2D hollowness
- At √s = 7 TeV, the real part of the eikonal amplitude reaches ~15% of the dominant imaginary part
- The criterion for a dip in n_in(b) is 2p Im h(0) > 1/(1+ρ²) ~ 1, which occurs when Re χ(0) increases above π/2
- The eikonal approximation is accurate to better than 0.1% for √s ≥ 17 GeV and b ≤ 3 fm

## Context

This work extends the findings of Arriola (2016) on the on-shell optical potential analysis of TOTEM data at √s = 7 TeV, and confirms the hollowness effect previously noticed by Alkin (2014), Dremin (2014), and Anisovich (2014). The analysis uses the MBP2 parametrization from Fagundes et al. (2013), which is based on the Barger-Phillips analysis motivated by Regge asymptotics. The results contradict folding models such as those of Chou (1968), Cheng (1987), Bourrely (1978), and Block (2015), which predict maximum absorption at b=0. The work enables future studies of the energy dependence of the hollowness effect and its interpretation in terms of the Glauber model of composite scatterers.
