# Photoproduction of $Λ(1520)$ hyperons from nuclei near the threshold

> We present a theoretical investigation of $Λ(1520)$ attenuation in $γA$ reactions near the threshold. It is performed in the framework of a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function. The model accounts for both primary photon-nucleon $γN \to KΛ(1520)$ and secondary pion-nucleon $πN \to KΛ(1520)$ production processes. We calculate the target mass and momentum dependences of the forward $Λ(1520)$ hyperon production from nuclei at photon energy of 2 GeV as well as for two options for its in-medium width. We find that the considered dependences are markedly sensitive to this width. Our studies also demonstrate that the secondary channel $πN \to KΛ(1520)$ plays a substantial role in the intermediate momentum $Λ(1520)$ photoproduction on nuclei in the chosen kinematics and, hence, is to be taken into account in the analysis of $Λ(1520)$ hyperon photoproduction from nuclei with the aim to get information on its width in the matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the secondary pion-nucleon channel πN → KΛ(1520) contributes substantially to Λ(1520) photoproduction on nuclei at intermediate momenta (0.8–1.2 GeV/c) near the threshold photon energy of 2 GeV, and therefore must be included when extracting the in-medium width of the Λ(1520) from nuclear transparency measurements, a nuance the abstract only hints at by stating the secondary channel "plays a substantial role."

## Method

The author extended a spectral function collision model, previously applied to proton-nucleus reactions, to photon-nucleus (γA) reactions at an incident photon energy of 2 GeV. The model incorporates both primary one-step processes (γN → KΛ(1520)) and secondary two-step processes (γN → 2πN followed by πN → KΛ(1520)), using off-shell kinematics, nuclear spectral functions, and Pauli blocking corrections for the final-state nucleon in the pion production step. The in-medium Λ(1520) width was treated in two scenarios: the free vacuum width (15.6 MeV) and a density-dependent collisional width of 55(ρN/ρ0) MeV added to the free width.

## Key numbers

- Incident photon energy: 2 GeV (near threshold; free production threshold ≈ 1.69 GeV)
- Free vacuum width of Λ(1520): Γvac = 15.6 MeV
- Collisional width added in medium: 55(ρN/ρ0) MeV, where ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- For ¹²C, ⁶³Cu, and ¹⁹⁷Au, the cross section in the momentum range ~1.2–1.6 GeV/c with the increased in-medium width is reduced by factors of about 1.4, 1.8, and 2.3, respectively, compared to the free-width case
- Average Fermi momentum used for Pauli blocking: p̄F = 210 MeV/c (from <ρN> = ρ0/2)
- Total cross section of free γN interaction at 2 GeV: σγNtot = 0.15 mb
- Parametrized differential cross section for γp → K⁺Λ(1520): at Eγ = 1.69–1.93 GeV, dσ/dt = 2.53 e^(−5.41|t−t⁺|) μb/GeV²; at 1.93–2.17 GeV, 1.40 e^(−2.20|t−t⁺|) μb/GeV²; at 2.17–2.43 GeV, 0.66 e^(−1.63|t−t⁺|) μb/GeV²; at 2.43–2.65 GeV, 0.49 e^(−1.51|t−t⁺|) μb/GeV²
- At Eγ = 2 GeV on a free proton at rest: t⁺ = −0.21 GeV², t⁻ = −1.38 GeV², t⁺−t⁻ = 1.17 GeV²
- Fits for total cross sections of γp → π⁺π⁻p: 74 (Eγ/GeV)^(−0.90) μb; γp → π⁺π⁰n: 53 (Eγ/GeV)^(−0.85) μb (valid for 1 GeV < Eγ < 4 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach of Paryev (2011, for pA reactions) to electromagnetic γA reactions, complementing earlier calculations by other authors (e.g., the hadronic model of Kaskulov and Oset, 2007) that predicted a fivefold increase of the Λ(1520) width at saturation density. It also addresses the neglect of two-step processes in prior transparency-ratio calculations for Λ(1520) in photon- and proton-induced reactions (e.g., by Cabrera et al., 2004). The results enable more reliable extraction of the Λ(1520) in-medium width from future photoproduction experiments at facilities such as SPring-8/LEPS, CLAS, or JLab, by showing that the secondary pion channel must be accounted for in the intermediate momentum region.
