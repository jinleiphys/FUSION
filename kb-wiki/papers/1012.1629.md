# Partial conservation of seniority in $j=9/2$ shell: Analytic and numerical studies

> Recent studies show that for systems with four identical fermions in the $j=9/2$ shell two special states, which have seniority $v=4$ and total spins I=4 and 6, are eigenstates of any two-body interaction. These states have good seniority for an arbitrary interaction. In this work an analytic proof is given to this peculiar occurrence of partial conservation of seniority which is the consequence of the special property of certain coefficients of fractional parentage. Further calculations did not reveal its existence in systems with other $n$ and/or $I$ for shells with $j\leq15/2$.

# Partial conservation of seniority in $j=9/2$ shell: Analytic and numerical studies

## Key claim

For four identical fermions in the $j=9/2$ shell, two special states with seniority $v=4$ and total spins $I=4$ and $I=6$ are eigenstates of any two-body interaction, meaning they conserve seniority even when the interaction does not satisfy the general seniority conservation condition. This partial conservation of seniority is proven analytically to arise from a special relation among coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp), but no analogous states exist for other particle numbers $n$, total spins $I$, or shell sizes $j \leq 15/2$.

## Method

The author provides an analytic proof of the special relation among one-particle coefficients of fractional parentage (Eq. 2) using the principal-parent procedure and factorization of cfp into isoscalar factors of the group chain $U(2j+1) \supset Sp(2j+1) \supset O(3)$. Numerical shell-model diagonalizations are performed for systems with $n$ up to $(2j+1)/2$ and $j$ up to $15/2$ to search for other states that are eigenstates of any two-body interaction, using the criterion that the energy expression must remain uniquely defined and independent of variations in the two-body matrix elements $V_J$.

## Key numbers

* Overlap of the special $v=4$, $I=4$ state with the normalized principal-parent state $|j^4[22],v=4,I=4\rangle$ is 0.998220.
* Overlap of the special $v=4$, $I=6$ state with the normalized principal-parent state $|j^4[24],v=4,I=6\rangle$ is 0.997704.
* For $j=9/2$, the necessary and sufficient condition for full seniority conservation is $65V_2 - 315V_4 + 403V_6 - 153V_8 = 0$, where $V_J$ are two-body matrix elements.
* No other fermionic system exhibiting partial seniority conservation was found for shells with $j \leq 15/2$, including all configurations listed in Tables V and VI for $(9/2)^4$, $(11/2)^3$ through $(11/2)^6$, $(13/2)^3$, $(13/2)^4$, $(15/2)^3$, and $(15/2)^4$.

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical observations by Escuderos and Zamick (2006, 2007) and Zamick and Van Isacker (2008) that certain $v=4$, $I=4$ and $I=6$ states in the $j=9/2$ shell exhibit partial dynamic symmetry and solvability. The analytic proof resolves the open question of why these states have vanishing non-diagonal matrix elements, using the cfp factorization approach of Sun (1989) and Wang (1995). The negative numerical search for other such states in shells up to $j=15/2$ confirms the uniqueness of this phenomenon, which may explain the existence of seniority isomers in nuclei in the $0g_{9/2}$ shell as discussed by Van Isacker (2008).
