# Ground state properties of the $β$ stable nuclei in various mean field theories

> The separation energies of neutrons and protons, binding energies, mean square charge radii, electric quadrupole moments and deformation parameters of the proton and neutron distributions are evaluated for $β$ stable even-even nuclei with $16 \leq A \leq 256$. We compare the theoretical estimates obtained within the Hartree-Fock plus BCS model with a few sets of Skyrme forces, relativistic mean-field theory and frequently used Saxon-Woods and Nilsson potentials with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares several mean-field theories and macroscopic-microscopic models for describing ground-state properties of beta-stable even-even nuclei, finding that no single parameter set is universally best; the best choice depends on the observable, with the Nilsson potential giving the best charge radii and the Chepurnov Saxon-Woods set giving the best separation energies, while among self-consistent theories the Skyrme forces m* and p and the RMF set NL-3 perform slightly better overall.

## Method

The authors calculated binding energies, neutron and proton separation energies, mean square charge radii, electric quadrupole moments, and deformation parameters for all beta-stable even-even nuclei with mass numbers 16 ≤ A ≤ 256. They used the Hartree-Fock plus BCS model with ten different Skyrme force parameter sets, relativistic mean-field theory with three parameter sets (NL-1, NL-3, NL-SH), and the macroscopic-microscopic approach with Nilsson and five Saxon-Woods potential parameter sets, then compared all results to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Mean square deviation of charge radii from Nilsson potential: <Δ² r_ch>^(1/2) = 0.029 fm
- Mean square deviation of B(E2) values from Nilsson potential: <Δ² B(E2)>^(1/2) = 1.336 e²b²
- Neutron skin thickness in Skyrme mean field: neutron mean square radii larger than proton ones by up to 0.15 fm for the heaviest isotopes
- Neutron skin thickness in RMF theory: neutron mean square radii larger than proton ones by up to about 0.25 fm
- Difference in quadrupole deformation between proton and neutron distributions can exceed 10% of the total deformation magnitude
- Average Fermi level for protons: λ̄_p = -11.8 MeV / (1 + A/208)
- Average Fermi level for neutrons: λ̄_n = λ̄_p - 0.512 MeV
- Spherical harmonic oscillator frequency: ħω₀ = 40 / A^(1/3) MeV

## Context

This work extends systematic comparisons of mean-field theories for ground-state properties, following earlier studies using Skyrme forces (Vautherin 1972, Beiner 1975), RMF theory (Serot and Walecka 1986), and macroscopic-microscopic models with Nilsson (Nilsson 1957) and Saxon-Woods potentials (Chepurnov, Wahlborn, Rost). The results provide a benchmark for parameter sets along the beta-stability line, enabling more reliable extrapolations to exotic nuclei far from stability and to superheavy nuclei, and suggest that macroscopic-microscopic models should treat proton and neutron deformations separately.
