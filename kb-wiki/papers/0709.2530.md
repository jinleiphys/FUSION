# Three-boson problem at low energy and Implications for dilute Bose-Einstein condensates

> It is shown that the effective interaction strength of three bosons at small collision energies can be extracted from their wave function at zero energy. An asymptotic expansion of this wave function at large interparticle distances is derived, from which is defined a quantity $D$ named three-body scattering hypervolume, which is an analog of the two-body scattering length. Given any finite-range interaction potentials, one can thus predict the effective three-body force from a numerical solution of the Schrödinger equation. In this way the constant $D$ for hard-sphere bosons is computed, leading to the complete result for the ground state energy per particle of a dilute Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) of hard spheres to order $ρ^2$, where $ρ$ is the number density. Effects of $D$ are also demonstrated in the three-body energy in a finite box of size $L$, which is expanded to the order $L^{-7}$, and in the three-body scattering amplitude in vacuum. Another key prediction is that there is a violation of the effective field theory (EFT) in the condensate fraction in dilute BECs, caused by short-range physics. EFT predictions for the ground state energy and few-body scattering amplitudes, however, are corroborated.

## Key claim

The effective three-body interaction strength for bosons at low collision energies can be extracted from the zero-energy three-body wave function via a quantity called the three-body scattering hypervolume \(D\), which is an analog of the two-body scattering length \(a\); the paper claims that effective field theory (EFT) predictions for the ground state energy and few-body scattering amplitudes are corroborated, but that there is a violation of EFT in the condensate fraction caused by short-range physics.

## Method

The author derives an asymptotic expansion of the three-boson wave function at zero collision energy for small momenta (or large interparticle distances), from which the three-body scattering hypervolume \(D\) is defined as a parameter analogous to the two-body scattering length. This expansion is then applied to hard-sphere bosons by numerically solving the three-body Schrödinger equation, yielding a precise value for \(D\). The results are used to compute the ground state energy per particle of a dilute Bose-Einstein condensate to order \(\rho^2\) (where \(\rho\) is number density), the three-body energy in a finite cubic box of side \(L\) to order \(L^{-7}\), and the three-body scattering amplitude in vacuum.

## Context

This work extends the low-density expansion of the Bose-Einstein condensate ground state energy beyond the terms discovered by Bogoliubov, Lee-Huang-Yang (1957), and Wu (1959), by fully determining the previously unknown three-body parameter \(\mathcal{E}_3'\). It resolves the undetermined parameter \(g_3(\kappa)\) from the effective field theory approach of Braaten and Nieto (1999) by expressing it in terms of the scattering hypervolume \(D\). The results enable precise predictions for dilute BECs of hard spheres and provide a method to compute three-body forces for any finite-range interaction potential, with implications for future studies of condensate depletion and few-body scattering.
