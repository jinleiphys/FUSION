# Nuclear Structure Functions from Constituent Quark Model

> We have used the notion of the constituent quark model of nucleon, where a constituent quark carries its own internal structure, and applied it to determine nuclear structure functions ratios. It is found that the description of experimental data require the inclusion of strong shadowing effect for $x<0.01$. Using the idea of vector meson dominance model and other ingredients this effect is calculated in the context of the constituent quark model. It is rather striking that the constituent quark model, used here, gives a good account of the data for a wide range of atomic mass number from A=4 to A=204.

# Nuclear Structure Functions from Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The constituent quark model, in which each constituent quark carries its own internal structure, can describe nuclear structure function ratios for a wide range of atomic mass numbers (A=4 to A=204), but only if a strong shadowing effect is included for x < 0.01. The model's agreement with data requires the shadowing correction to be calculated using a vector meson dominance approach, without needing explicit anti-shadowing effects.

## Method

The authors extend the "valon" model of Hwa (1980, 1995), where a nucleon is a system of bound constituent quarks that themselves have perturbatively-calculable internal structure. They modify the constituent quark distribution in a bound nucleon by introducing a distortion factor δ that depends on atomic number A, then convolve this with a Fermi distribution for nucleons in the nucleus including binding energy corrections. For x < 0.1, they add a shadowing correction based on Qiu (1987) but modified using vector meson dominance ideas, with the shadowing factor parameterized in terms of an effective nuclear size A_eff.

## Key numbers

- The scale parameters for the constituent quark structure function evolution are Q₀ = 0.233 (GeV/c)² and Λ = 0.345 (GeV/c)²
- The distortion factor δ for constituent quark distributions in a bound nucleon is given by δ = [0.0104 − 0.014 ln A + 0.014(ln A)²] / [1 − 0.83 ln A + 0.295(ln A)² − 0.024(ln A)³]
- The shadowing factor parameterization is R_s(x, Q², A_eff) = R_s(Q², A_eff) × (0.94 + 0.076 A_eff^0.5) × x^0.5 × ln(x)
- The effective nuclear size is A_eff = −10.97 − 0.704A + 0.0042A² − 0.00019A^2.5 + 8.63A^0.5
- The free parameters for the U-type and D-type constituent quark distributions in a free nucleon are a = 0.65 and b = 0.35
- The model covers nuclei from A=4 to A=204

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark (valon) model previously applied successfully to the free proton structure function by Arash and Khorramian (1998) and by Hwa (1980, 1995). It addresses the nuclear EMC effect across all kinematic regions (shadowing, anti-shadowing, EMC region, and Fermi motion) for a wide range of nuclei. The shadowing correction modifies the approach of Qiu (1987) by incorporating vector meson dominance ideas from Shaw (1993) and sub-asymptotic pomeron exchange from Armesto and Branin (1997). The model enables a unified description of nuclear structure functions from light (A=4) to heavy (A=204) nuclei within a single framework, without requiring explicit anti-shadowing mechanisms.
