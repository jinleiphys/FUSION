# Unbound states in 17C and p-sd shell-model interactions

> Unbound states in 17C were investigated via one-neutron removal from a 18C beam at an energy of 245 MeV/nucleon on a carbon target. The energy spectrum of 17C, above the single-neutron decay threshold, was reconstructed using invariant mass spectroscopy from the measured momenta of the 16C fragment and neutron, and was found to exhibit resonances at Er=0.52(2), 0.77(2), 1.36(1), 1.91(1), 2.22(3) and 3.20(1) MeV. The resonance at Er=0.77(2) MeV [Ex=1.51(3) MeV] was provisionally assigned as the second 5/2+ state. The two resonances at Er=1.91(1) and 3.20(1) MeV [Ex=2.65(2) and 3.94(2) MeV] were identified, through comparison of the energies, cross sections and momentum distributions with shell-model and eikonal reaction calculations, as p-shell hole states with spin-parities 1/2- and 3/2-, respectively. A detailed comparison was made with the results obtained using a range of shell-model interactions. The YSOX shell-model Hamiltonian, the cross-shell part of which is based on the monopole-based universal interaction, was found to provide a very good description of the present results and those for the neighbouring odd-A carbon isotopes - in particular for the negative parity cross-shell states.

## Key claim

The paper identifies six unbound resonances in 17C via one-neutron knockout from 18C, but the assignments for several states remain provisional; the resonance at Er=0.77(2) MeV is only "provisionally assigned" as the second 5/2+ state, and the states at Er=1.36(1) and 2.22(3) MeV could not be unambiguously placed in the level scheme.

## Method

A 245 MeV/nucleon 18C beam was incident on a carbon target, and one-neutron removal populated unbound states in 17C. The relative energy spectrum above the neutron-decay threshold was reconstructed via invariant mass spectroscopy using measured momenta of the 16C fragment and neutron detected in coincidence, with de-excitation gamma rays from 16C bound states measured by the DALI2 array. The spectra were fitted using R-matrix line shapes with energy-dependent widths and a non-resonant continuum, and the longitudinal momentum distributions of the knockout residues were compared with eikonal reaction calculations to determine orbital angular momenta.

## Key numbers

- Six resonances observed at Er = 0.52(2), 0.77(2), 1.36(1), 1.91(1), 2.22(3), and 3.20(1) MeV
- The resonance at Er = 0.77(2) MeV corresponds to Ex = 1.51(3) MeV, provisionally assigned as 5/2_2+ state
- Two resonances at Er = 1.91(1) and 3.20(1) MeV correspond to Ex = 2.65(2) and 3.94(2) MeV, identified as p-shell hole states with spin-parities 1/2- and 3/2-, respectively
- The 1/2- state at Ex = 2.65(2) MeV has width Gamma = 0.23(3) MeV and cross section sigma = 17.69(75) mb
- The 3/2- state at Ex = 3.94(2) MeV has width Gamma = 0.32(9) MeV and cross section sigma = 4.61(50) mb
- The 5/2_2+ candidate at Ex = 1.51(3) MeV has cross section sigma = 0.87(24) mb and spectroscopic factor C2S_exp = 0.035(12)
- The one-neutron separation energy of 17C is Sn = 0.735(18) MeV
- The 18C beam energy was 245 MeV/nucleon with intensity ~2.3x10^3 particles per second

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron-rich carbon isotopes, particularly the odd-A chain 15,17,19C, to investigate cross-shell negative parity states formed by creating a hole in the p-shell orbitals (0nu p1/2 or 0nu p3/2). The results are compared with shell-model calculations using the YSOX Hamiltonian (Yuan 2012), which is based on the monopole-based universal interaction and includes central, pi+rho tensor, and spin-orbit force components, as well as WBT (Warburton 1992), WBT* (Stanoiu 2008), and ab initio CCEI (Jansen 2014) interactions. The YSOX interaction provides a very good description of the present results and those for neighbouring odd-A carbon isotopes, particularly for negative parity cross-shell states, enabling more stringent tests of shell-model interactions through both energies and spectroscopic factors.
