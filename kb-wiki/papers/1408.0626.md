# Selected Issues in Thermal Field Theory

> New developments on hot and dense QCD in effective field theories are reviewed. Recent investigations in lattice gauge theories for the low-lying Dirac eigenmodes suggest survival hadrons in restored phase of chiral symmetry. We discuss expected properties of those bound states in a medium using chiral approaches. The role of higher-lying hadrons near chiral symmetry restoration is also argued from the conventional and the holographic point of view.

## Key claim

Lattice simulations suggest that hadrons may survive as massive bound states in the chirally restored phase, and that deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration occur at essentially the same temperature at vanishing chemical potential, but the paper emphasizes that a conclusive statement on the relation between Dirac eigenmodes and confinement must await computations with dynamical quarks.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice gauge theory results for the static quark potential after removing low-lying Dirac eigenmodes, and for hadron masses as a function of Dirac mode truncation level. It also discusses chiral effective field theory with a dilaton field representing the gluon condensate, and the construction of vector and axial-vector spectral functions via QCD sum rules and Weinberg sum rules at finite temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of Polyakov loop susceptibilities \(R_A = \chi_A/\chi_R\) retains underlying center symmetry fairly well even in full QCD with physical pion mass, serving as a better pseudo-order parameter than the Polyakov loop itself.
- At vanishing chemical potential, the deconfinement temperature equals the chiral symmetry restoration temperature: \(T_{\rm deconf} \simeq T_{\rm chiral}\).
- With low-lying Dirac modes removed, the lowest nucleon mass remains around 1 GeV and the lowest vector meson mass remains around \(m_\rho\) in the chirally restored phase.
- The mixing strength \(C\) between transverse \(\rho\) and \(a_1\) states at saturation density \(n_0\) is given as 1 GeV from an AdS/QCD model and as 0.1 GeV from the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action in four dimensions.
- The mass gap between chiral partners of heavy-light mesons is around 350 MeV, compared to a mass difference between charged D mesons in dense matter of about 50 MeV.

## Context

This review extends lattice work by Gattringer, Bruckmann, Synatschke, Gongyo, and Doi (2010-2013) on the relation between Dirac eigenmodes and the Polyakov loop, and by GLS on hadron masses after Dirac mode removal. It also builds on chiral effective field theory with a dilaton field from Schechter (1980) and the dilaton-limit fixed point approach of DL1 and DL2. The paper identifies open questions including the need for an effective theory that better handles confinement in the presence of light quarks, and the challenge of systematically incorporating higher-lying hadrons into effective theories near the QCD phase transition.
