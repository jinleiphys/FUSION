# Constraints on $ΛN$ Effective Interactions from Mirror Hypernuclei in a Deformed Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Model

> We investigate the ground-state properties of four mirror hypernuclei pairs--$^{10}_Λ$Be-$^{10}_Λ$B, $^{12}_Λ$B-$^{12}_Λ$C, $^{16}_Λ$N-$^{16}_Λ$O, and $^{40}_Λ$K-$^{40}_Λ$Ca--within the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework, analyzing their connection to $ΛN$ effective interactions. Systematic calculations with eight distinct effective interactions reveal linear correlations between mirror hypernuclei in $Λ$ separation energies and charge radii. The charge symmetry breaking effects, quantified through $Λ$ separation energy differences, exhibit a positive correlation with the SU(3) flavor symmetry violation. We emphasize that constraints derived from $A=10$ and $A=12$ hypernuclear pairs must explicitly incorporate rotational energy correction effects. Precision measurements of the (near) spherical $A=16$ and $A=40$ mirror systems are proposed as critical benchmarks for refining the isospin part of the hyperon-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that linear correlations exist between mirror hypernuclei in both Lambda separation energies and charge radii across eight different effective interactions, but they emphasize that constraints from the A=10 and A=12 mirror hypernuclear pairs must explicitly incorporate rotational energy correction effects before fitting the charge symmetry breaking term, a condition not stated in the abstract.

## Method

The authors employed the multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDC-RHB) model with an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis to calculate ground-state properties of four mirror hypernuclear pairs. They used eight distinct effective interactions (PK1-Y1, NLSH-A, DD-ME2-Yi with i=1,2,3, and PKDD-Yi with i=1,2,3) and incorporated both microscopic center-of-mass corrections and rotational energy corrections via the Inglis-Belyaev formula combined with rigid rotor moments of inertia.

## Key numbers

* For the A=10 mirror pair, experimental Lambda separation energies are 9.11 +/- 0.22 MeV for 10_Lambda_Be (emulsion) and 8.89 +/- 0.12 MeV for 10_Lambda_B (emulsion), while calculations with eight interactions yield values ranging from 7.416 to 8.442 MeV for 10_Lambda_Be and 7.377 to 8.366 MeV for 10_Lambda_B
* For the A=12 mirror pair, experimental Lambda separation energies are 11.529 +/- 0.025 MeV for 12_Lambda_B and 11.30 +/- 0.19 MeV for 12_Lambda_C, with calculated values ranging from 9.319 to 10.534 MeV for 12_Lambda_B and 9.323 to 10.500 MeV for 12_Lambda_C
* The charge radius difference between mirror hypernuclei follows the linear relation Delta r_ch = 1.574 I +/- 0.021, where I is the isospin asymmetry parameter, as established by Novario (2023)
* The slopes k of the linear correlation S_Lambda[^A_Lambda (Z+1)] = k * S_Lambda[^A_Lambda Z] + b are 0.961 for A=10, 0.944 for A=12, 0.957 for A=16, and 0.965 for A=40

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic mean-field study (Rong 2021) which found that conventional parametrizations could not adequately describe Lambda separation energies of 12_Lambda_B-12_Lambda_C and 16_Lambda_N-16_Lambda_O mirror pairs. It also builds on their finding (Rong 2023) that center-of-mass and rotational corrections are non-negligible for light nuclear systems. The study enables future refinement of the isospin-dependent part of hyperon-nucleon interactions by proposing precision measurements of the (near) spherical A=16 and A=40 mirror systems as critical benchmarks, and it shows that the phenomenological CSB interaction proposed by Sun (2025) for the 12_Lambda_B-12_Lambda_C pair must account for deformation effects.
