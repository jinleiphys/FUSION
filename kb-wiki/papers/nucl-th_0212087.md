# Landau parameters of nuclear matter in the spin and spin-isospin channels

> The equation of state of spin and isospin polarized nuclear matter is determined in the framework of the Brueckner theory including three-body forces. The Landau parameters in the spin and spin-isospin sectors are derived as a function of the baryonic density. The results are compared with the Gamow-Teller collective modes. The relevance of $G_0$ and $G_0'$ for neutron stars is shortly discussed, including the magnetic susceptibility and the neutron star cooling.

# Landau parameters of nuclear matter in the spin and spin-isospin channels

## Key claim

The Landau parameter G0' in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, calculated with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation including three-body forces, agrees with the experimentally extracted value from Gamow-Teller resonances to within about 3%, but only when three-body forces are included; the value without three-body forces is about 7% higher and in less agreement.

## Method

The equation of state of spin- and isospin-polarized symmetric nuclear matter was calculated within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation with continuous choice for the auxiliary potential, using the Argonne AV18 two-body force and a three-body force from Ref. TBF. The energy per particle was computed for various combinations of neutron and proton spin polarizations at fixed densities, and the results were fitted to a quadratic form in the spin polarization parameters. The zero-order Landau parameters G0 and G0' were then extracted from the coefficients of this quadratic fit together with the level density at the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- G0' at saturation density including three-body forces: approximately 1.22 (dimensionless)
- G0' at saturation density without three-body forces: approximately 1.30 (dimensionless)
- Experimentally extracted G0' from Gamow-Teller resonance on 90Ni: between 1.182 and 1.188 (dimensionless, multiplied by a factor of two according to the authors' definition)

## Context

This work extends Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of nuclear matter to spin-polarized systems, building on prior saturation studies by Song and by Lombardo and collaborators. The results provide a microscopic prediction for the Landau parameters G0 and G0' as a function of density, which are compared to experimental constraints from Gamow-Teller resonances extracted by Sakai and collaborators. The density dependence of these parameters, especially the enhancement driven by three-body forces at high density, is relevant for neutron star magnetic susceptibility and neutrino mean free path, with implications for neutron star cooling models.
