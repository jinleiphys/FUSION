# Acceleration of a polarized neutron by internal weak nuclear forces

> It is proven that a polarized neutron gets accelerated by internal nuclear forces along the coherent rotation of its spin. The net force upon the neutron arises from the weak nuclear interactions between its quarks. It is the result of the simultaneous breaking of parity symmetry by the chiral interactions between the neutron's quarks, and of time-reversal symmetry along the inversion of their spins. The variation of the neutron's kinetic momentum is accompanied with the transfer of an equivalent momentum to the fields of the Z and W bosons that mediate the interactions, in the opposite direction. The effect is linear in Fermi's constant. Using the simplest hadron models, an upper bound of the order of meters per second is estimated for the velocity variation of the polarized neutron along the spin-flip process.

## Key claim

The paper proves that a polarized neutron experiences a net acceleration from internal weak nuclear forces during coherent spin rotation, arising from parity violation in chiral weak interactions between quarks combined with time-reversal symmetry breaking during spin inversion. The effect is linear in Fermi's constant, and the author estimates an upper bound on the velocity variation of the order of meters per second using the simplest hadron models, though this is an estimate rather than a precise prediction.

## Method

The author uses time-dependent perturbation theory within the Standard Model electroweak gauge theory, modeling the neutron as a three-quark system bound by an effective QCD confinement potential. Two simple hadron confinement models are employed for numerical estimation: the MIT-bag model with massless relativistic valence quarks confined in a static spherical cavity, and the harmonic oscillator model with nonrelativistic constituent quarks interacting through pairwise harmonic potentials. The spin-flip is driven by a radio-frequency magnetic field (Rabi oscillation), and the momentum transfer to virtual Z and W bosons is computed from the conservation of total linear momentum, which includes both canonical quark momenta and transverse boson momenta.

## Key numbers

* Velocity variation of the polarized neutron along the spin-flip process: upper bound of the order of meters per second (estimated using the simplest hadron models)
* Variation of neutron velocity from absorption of the RF photon: of the order of 10^-10 to 10^-7 m/s, for typical Larmor frequencies between 10^5 and 10^8 Hz
* The effect is linear in Fermi's constant G_F (no numerical value for G_F given in the text)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of A. Feigel (2000s), who first proposed that vacuum field fluctuations could carry net linear momentum in magnetoelectric media, and later work by B. van-Tiggelen and collaborators on momentum gained by hydrogen atoms in crossed fields and by chiral molecules in magnetic fields (Kawka, PRL Donaire). The author also builds on a prior calculation (JHEP) that used the Fermi four-fermion interaction for an unpolarized proton in a magnetic field but obtained a velocity of order G_F^2, which the author argues was too small because it was a second-order correction rather than the leading-order effect. If confirmed experimentally, this finding would enable probing hadron structure with low-energy techniques and demonstrate momentum transfer to virtual particles in chiral systems.
