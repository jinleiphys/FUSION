# Production of Light Nuclei in Heavy Ion Collisions Within Multiple Freezeout Scenario

> We discuss the production of light nuclei in heavy ion collisions within a multiple freezeout scenario. Thermal parameters extracted from the fits to the observed hadron yields are used to predict the multiplicities of light nuclei. Ratios of strange to non strange nuclei are found to be most sensitive to the details of the chemical freezeout. The well known disagreement between data of $^3_Λ\text{H/}^3\text{He}$ and $\overline{^3_Λ\text{H/}^3\text{He}}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and models based on thermal as well as simple coalescence using a single chemical freezeout surface goes away when we let the strange and non strange hadrons freezeout at separate surfaces. At the LHC energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2700$ GeV, multiple freezeout scenario within a thermal model provides a consistent framework to describe the yields of all measured hadrons and nuclei.

# Production of Light Nuclei in Heavy Ion Collisions Within Multiple Freezeout Scenario

## Key claim

The well-known disagreement between data for the ratios $^3_\Lambda\text{H/}^3\text{He}$ and $\overline{^3_\Lambda\text{H/}^3\text{He}}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and models using a single chemical freezeout surface is resolved when strange and non-strange hadrons are allowed to freezeout at separate surfaces (2CFO), provided the production mechanism is either thermal or simple coalescence.

## Method

The authors use a hadron resonance gas model in the grand canonical ensemble with two separate chemical freezeout surfaces: one for non-strange hadrons and one for strange hadrons and those with hidden strangeness. Thermal parameters (temperature, volume, chemical potentials) are extracted from fits to observed hadron yields across beam energies from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.27$ to 2700 GeV, and these parameters are then used to predict light nuclei multiplicities in both thermal and simple coalescence models. For the coalescence model, the coalescence factor $C_{\overline{A}A}$ is set to 1, and nuclei ratios are expressed in terms of constituent hadron ratios.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, 2CFO fit parameters: $T_S = 164 \pm 3$ MeV, $T_{NS} = 155 \pm 6$ MeV, $\mu_S = 31 \pm 11$ MeV, $\mu_{NS} = 22 \pm 16$ MeV, $V_S = 2.2 \pm 0.4 \times 10^4$ MeV$^{-3}$, $V_{NS} = 2.8 \pm 0.8 \times 10^4$ MeV$^{-3}$ (without nuclei in fit); $\chi^2/N_{df} = 23/6$
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, including light nuclei in the fit yields: $T_S = 163 \pm 3$ MeV, $T_{NS} = 155 \pm 6$ MeV, $\mu_S = 27 \pm 11$ MeV, $\mu_{NS} = 23 \pm 16$ MeV, $V_S = 2.3 \pm 0.4 \times 10^4$ MeV$^{-3}$, $V_{NS} = 2.6 \pm 0.8 \times 10^4$ MeV$^{-3}$; $\chi^2/N_{df} = 24/10$
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2700$ GeV (LHC): $T_S = 158 \pm 3$ MeV, $T_{NS} = 145 \pm 3$ MeV, $\mu_S = 0 \pm 12$ MeV, $\mu_{NS} = 0 \pm 7$ MeV, $V_S = 5.5 \pm 0.6 \times 10^4$ MeV$^{-3}$, $V_{NS} = 9.7 \pm 0.8 \times 10^4$ MeV$^{-3}$; $\chi^2/N_{df} = 3.1/6$
- The ratio $\l {}^3_{\Lambda}\text{H}/{}^3\text{He}\r^{\text{th}} / \l {}^3_{\Lambda}\text{H}/{}^3\text{He}\r^{\text{c}} \simeq (1/3 + 2/3(m_p/m_\Lambda))^{3/2} \exp(2m_p(1 - T_{ns}/T_s)/T_{ns})$, with the prefactor approximately 0.85

## Context

This work extends the 2CFO (two-chemical-freezeout) framework previously introduced by Chatterjee et al. (2013) for hadrons to the production of light nuclei, including hypernuclei. It addresses a known failure of single-freezeout thermal models (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1995; Yen et al., 1998; Becattini et al., 2000) and simple coalescence models (Cleymans et al., 2011; Andronic et al., 2010) to describe the $^3_\Lambda\text{H/}^3\text{He}$ and $\overline{^3_\Lambda\text{H/}^3\text{He}}$ ratios at RHIC. The results enable future studies of freezeout dynamics using strange-to-non-strange nuclei ratios, particularly at FAIR energies where the sensitivity between 1CFO and 2CFO is largest, and motivate inclusion of yet-undiscovered resonances from lattice QCD and quark models.
