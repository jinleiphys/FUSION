# The Unstable Glasma

> We discuss results from 3+1-D numerical simulations of SU(2) Yang--Mills equations for an unstable Glasma expanding into the vacuum after a high energy heavy ion collision. We expand on our earlier work on a non-Abelian Weibel instability in such a system and study the behavior of the instability in greater detail on significantly larger lattices than previously. We establish the time scale for the onset of the instability and demonstrate that the growth rate is robust as one approaches the continuum limit. For large violations of boost invariance, non-Abelian effects cause the growth of soft modes to saturate. At late times, we observe significant creation of longitudinal pressure and a systematic trend towards isotropy. These time scales however are significantly larger than those required for early thermalization in heavy ion collisions. We discuss additional effects in the produced Glasma that may speed up thermalization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a non-Abelian Weibel instability exists in the expanding Glasma produced after a heavy ion collision, but the time scales for the instability to generate significant longitudinal pressure and approach isotropy are much larger than those required for early thermalization observed at RHIC.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1-dimensional numerical simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills equations on a lattice in proper time (τ), transverse coordinates (x,y), and space-time rapidity (η) coordinates, starting from Color Glass Condensate initial conditions with violations of boost invariance introduced as Gaussian random fluctuations in the electric fields. They tracked the growth of Fourier modes of the longitudinal pressure as a function of proper time and fitted the growth rates to extract the average instability growth rate, while also studying the time evolution of the highest unstable mode number ν_max.

## Key numbers

- The fitted average growth rate Γ_fit^av ≈ 0.5, independent of choices for lattice spacing a_η, transverse lattice spacing g^2 μ a_⊥, longitudinal volume L_η, initial time τ_init, and fluctuation amplitude Δ, with weak dependence on g^2 μ L.
- For RHIC collisions of gold nuclei, g^2 μ L ≈ 120; for LHC lead collisions, this is a factor of two larger.
- The highest unstable mode number ν_max increases linearly with proper time as approximately ν_max ≈ 0.06 g^2 μ τ for small violations of boost invariance.
- The deviation from linear growth of ν_max occurs when the maximum amplitude of τ P̃_L(τ,ν) reaches a critical value of 3 × 10⁻⁵ in the dimensionless units used.
- The instability growth follows the functional form exp(Γ √(g^2 μ τ)), with the onset occurring around g^2 μ τ ≈ 28 on a 16×2048 lattice with g^2 μ L = 22.6 and L_η = 102.4.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier results (Romatschke and Venugopalan, 2005) on the non-Abelian Weibel instability in the expanding Glasma, using significantly larger lattices to establish the robustness of the growth rate in the continuum limit. The instability was not seen in previous numerical simulations by Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (2003) and Lappi (2003) that assumed strict boost invariance with 2+1-dimensional dynamics. The results enable future work on thermalization in the Glasma, with the authors noting that their computation represents an essential but not exclusive piece of a complete next-to-leading order calculation of the evolution from Glasma to Quark Gluon Plasma.
