# Understanding parton evolution in matter from renormalization group analysis

> We perform a renormalization group (RG) analysis of collinear hadron production in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei. We consider the limit where the parent parton energy $E$ is large, while the medium opacity $L/λ_g$ remains small. We identify the fixed order and leading $\ln(E/ξ^2 L)$ enhanced medium contributions to the semi-inclusive cross sections and derive RG equations that resum multiple emissions near the endpoints of the splitting functions at first order in opacity. These evolution equations treat the same type of radiation enhancement in matter as the modified Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi approach, but differ in the way one regulates the collinear divergences. They provide a unique analytic insight into the problem of resummation and a faster and more efficient path to phenomenology. The new RG evolution framework is applied to study fragmentation in $e$A reactions.

# Understanding parton evolution in matter from renormalization group analysis

## Key claim
The authors perform a renormalization group (RG) analysis of collinear hadron production in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei, deriving evolution equations that resum leading logarithms of E/ξ²L from medium-induced emissions. The RG equations treat the same radiation enhancement as the modified DGLAP (mDGLAP) approach but differ in how collinear divergences are regulated, providing analytic insight and a faster path to phenomenology, though the analysis is restricted to the limit where medium opacity L/λ_g remains small (first order in opacity) and the parent parton energy E is large.

## Method
The authors compute semi-inclusive hadron production in eA deep inelastic scattering using the opacity expansion approach with soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons (SCET_G). They identify fixed-order and leading ln(E/ξ²L) enhanced medium contributions to the cross sections, extract the 1/ε poles from medium-induced splitting functions using dimensional regularization, and define counter terms M_ij^(1) to cancel these poles. Taking the derivative with respect to ln μ₂² yields RG evolution equations for the flavor non-singlet, flavor-singlet quark, and gluon spectra, which are then solved analytically (traveling wave solution for the non-singlet case) and numerically for the full coupled system.

## Key numbers
- The inverse interaction range squared is set to ξ² = 0.12 GeV², and the effective medium density parameter to ρ_G = 0.4 fm⁻³.
- These parameters yield a quark transport coefficient q̂_F ≈ 0.052 GeV²/fm at ν = 12 GeV.
- The HERMES data comparison uses average ν = 12 GeV and Q² = 2.25 GeV²; the EMC data comparison uses ν = 62 GeV and Q² = 11 GeV².
- The EIC predictions are shown for Q² = 20 GeV² at various x_B values, with x_B > 0.3 producing very large modifications.
- The traveling wave solution for the non-singlet sector gives a shift Δz = -4C_F C_A τ, where τ depends on α_s(μ₂²) and α_s(χ(w)zν/L) via the running coupling.
- The fixed coupling approximation yields τ_fix = A_0 ln(2πE/ξ²L) with A_0 = α_s,fix² ρ_G L² B(w)/(8ν).

## Context
This work extends the SCET_G-based opacity expansion approach of Ovanesyan, Vitev, and collaborators (2011-2015) and the mDGLAP framework of Wang (2009) and Chang, Chien (2014-2015) by providing a first-principles RG treatment of medium-induced logarithms. The new RG framework enables semi-analytic solutions (traveling wave) for parton energy loss in matter, which is not possible with purely numerical mDGLAP methods. It enables systematic improvement of cold nuclear matter phenomenology for SIDIS at HERMES, EMC, and the future Electron-Ion Collider, and can be generalized to initial-state effects in Drell-Yan and to heavy ion collisions.
