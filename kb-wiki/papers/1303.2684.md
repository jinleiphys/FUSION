# Ultrasoft fermion mode and off-diagonal Boltzmann equation in quark-gluon plasma at high temperature

> We derive the generalized Boltzmann equation (GBE) near equilibrium from the Kadanoff-Baym equation for quark excitation with ultrasoft momentum (~g^2T, g: coupling constant, T: temperature) in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at extremely high T, and show that the equation is equivalent to the self-consistent equation derived in the resummed perturbation scheme used to analyze the quark propagator. We obtain the expressions of the dispersion relation, the damping rate, and the strength of a quark excitation with ultrasoft momentum by solving the GBE. We also show that the GBE enables us to obtain the equation determining the n-point function containing a pair of quarks and (n-2) gluon external lines whose momenta are ultrasoft.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized Boltzmann equation (GBE) for quark excitations with ultrasoft momentum (~g²T) in QCD at high temperature, showing that this GBE is equivalent to the self-consistent equation from resummed perturbation theory used to analyze the quark propagator, thereby extending previous work from the Yukawa model and QED to the non-Abelian case.

## Method

The author starts from the Kadanoff-Baym equation for QCD in the background field gauge, performs a Wigner transformation, and applies gradient expansion and weak coupling approximations justified by the ultrasoft momentum scale (~g²T). The resulting equation for the off-diagonal propagator (quark-gluon correlation function) is simplified by including the leading-order diagonal self-energies (quark and gluon asymptotic thermal masses and damping rates) and the off-diagonal self-energy, yielding a generalized Boltzmann equation. The GBE is then solved in the linear response regime to obtain the dispersion relation, damping rate, and residue of the ultrasoft fermion mode.

## Key numbers

- The ultrasoft momentum scale is ~g²T, where g is the coupling constant and T the temperature.
- The quark asymptotic thermal mass is m_f = gT√(C_f)/2, where C_f = (N²-1)/(2N).
- The gluon asymptotic thermal mass is m_b = gT√((N+N_f/2)/6).
- The quantity δm² ≡ m_b² - m_f² = g²T²(N/24 + 1/(8N) + N_f/12).
- The quark damping rate ζ_f and gluon damping rate ζ_b are both of order g²T ln(1/g), and ζ ≡ ζ_f + ζ_b.
- The free spectral function is ρ⁰(k) = 2π sgn(k⁰)δ(k²).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the ultrasoft fermion mode from the Yukawa model and QED (ultrasoft-QED, 2012) to QCD, and also extends the kinetic equation approach from the gluon propagator (Blaizot and Iancu, 2002) to the quark propagator. The derivation establishes the foundation of the resummed perturbation scheme (Lebedev and Smilga, 1992) and enables analysis of n-point functions containing a pair of quarks and (n-2) ultrasoft gluons, not just the quark propagator. The GBE derived here is a generalization of the Boltzmann equation that includes fermionic background fields, in contrast to the usual Vlasov equation used at the soft scale.
