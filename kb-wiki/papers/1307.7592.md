# Fluid/Gravity Correspondence, Second Order Transport and Gravitational Anomaly

> We study the transport properties of a relativistic fluid affected by chiral and gauge-gravitational anomalies. The computation is performed in the framework of the fluid/gravity correspondence for a 5 dim holographic model with Chern-Simons terms in the action. We find new anomalous and non anomalous transport coefficients, as well as new contributions to the existing ones coming from the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly. Consequences for the shear waves dispersion relation are analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper computes new second-order transport coefficients in a strongly coupled relativistic fluid affected by both chiral (gauge) and mixed gauge-gravitational anomalies, finding that the coefficient \(\tilde{\xi}_5\) (which appears in the anomalous current as \(\nu^\mu_{(2)\text{ano}} = \tilde{\xi}_5 \epsilon^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}u_\nu \mathcal{D}_\alpha E_\beta\)) is non-zero in this holographic model, contrary to earlier predictions based on entropy production arguments by Kharzeev et al. (2011).

## Method

The authors use the fluid/gravity correspondence for a 5-dimensional holographic Einstein-Maxwell model with Chern-Simons terms (both gauge and mixed gauge-gravitational) in the action, which realizes a single chiral \(U(1)\) symmetry. They adopt a Weyl-covariant ansatz for the metric and gauge field, solve the Einstein-Maxwell equations order by order in a derivative expansion around an AdS Reissner-Nordström black-brane background, and extract the transport coefficients from the near-boundary expansion of the fields using holographic renormalization. The parameters \(\kappa\) and \(\lambda\) in the action are fixed by matching to the chiral anomaly for fermions.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity: \(\eta = \frac{r_+^3}{16\pi G}\), where \(r_+\) is the outer horizon radius of the black hole.
* Electrical conductivity: \(\sigma = \frac{\pi T^2}{16 G M^2 r_+}\), with \(M\) the black hole mass and \(T\) the temperature.
* Chiral magnetic conductivity: \(\xi_B = -\frac{\sqrt{3} Q (M+3) r_+ \kappa}{8 \pi G M} + \frac{\sqrt{3} \pi Q T^2 \lambda}{G M r_+}\), where \(Q\) is the black hole charge.
* Chiral vortical conductivity: \(\xi_V = -\frac{3 Q^2 r_+^2 \kappa}{4 \pi G M} + \frac{2 \pi (2 Q^2 - 1) T^2 \lambda}{G M}\).
* Relaxation length for the vorticity term: \(\tilde{l}_\omega = \frac{2\pi}{G p}\left[ \frac{\kappa \mu^3}{48\pi^2} + 64\mu\lambda\left( 3 r_+^2 - 2\mu^2 - \frac{\pi T \mu^2}{r_+}\right) \right]\), with \(\mu\) the chemical potential and \(p\) the pressure.
* Relaxation length for the magnetic term: \(\tilde{l}_B = \frac{1}{2\pi G p}\left( \frac{\kappa \mu^2}{8} + \lambda \pi^2 T^2 \right)\).
* Relaxation length for the electric term: \(\tilde{l}_E = -\frac{8 \bar\mu}{\pi^2 T}\left[\kappa \log 2 - 2\lambda (1+2\log 2)\right] + \mathcal{O}(\bar{\mu}^3)\), where \(\bar\mu = \mu/T\).

## Context

This work extends the study of anomalous transport in relativistic fluids beyond first order, building on the discovery of the chiral magnetic effect by Fukushima et al. (2008) and the chiral vortical effect by Erdmenger et al. (2008) and Son and Surowka (2009). It specifically incorporates the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly contributions first identified at first order by Landsteiner et al. (2011, 2012), and computes their effects at second order in the derivative expansion. The results enable future analysis of the entropy current up to second order in this holographic model, which could clarify the role of processes such as the \(\tilde{\xi}_5\) term in the current, and provide new predictions for the dispersion relation of shear waves in anomalous fluids.
