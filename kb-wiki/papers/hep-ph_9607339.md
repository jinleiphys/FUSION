# Hadronic interactions from effective chiral Lagrangians of quarks and gluons

> Effective chiral Lagrangians involving constituent quarks, Goldstone bosons and long-distance gluons are believed to describe the strong interactions in an intermediate energy region between the confinement scale and the chiral symmetry breaking scale. Baryons and mesons in such a description are bound states of constituent quarks. We discuss the combined use of the techniques of effective chiral field theory and of the field theoretic method known as Fock-Tani representation to derive effective hadron interactions. The Fock-Tani method is based on a change of representation by means of a unitary transformation such that the composite hadrons are redescribed by elementary-particle field operators. Application of the unitary transformation on the microscopic quark-quark interaction derived from a chiral effective Lagrangian leads to chiral effective interactions describing all possible processes involving hadrons and their constituents. The formalism is illustrated by deriving the one-pion-exchange potential between two nucleons using the quark-gluon effective chiral Lagrangian of Manohar and Georgi. We also present the results of a study of the saturation properties of nuclear matter using this formalism.

## Key claim

Effective chiral Lagrangians of constituent quarks, Goldstone bosons, and long-distance gluons can describe strong interactions in an intermediate energy region between the confinement scale and the chiral symmetry breaking scale, but the method relies on a unitary transformation (Fock-Tani representation) to recast composite hadrons as elementary-particle field operators, and the formalism is illustrated only for the one-pion-exchange potential between two nucleons using the Manohar and Georgi Lagrangian.

## Method

The author combines effective chiral field theory with the Fock-Tani representation, a field-theoretic method that uses a unitary transformation to replace composite hadron operators (built from quark and antiquark creation operators) with fictitious elementary-particle field operators that satisfy canonical (anti)commutation relations. Starting from the microscopic quark-quark interaction derived from the Manohar and Georgi chiral effective Lagrangian, the transformation is applied to obtain effective hadron-hadron interactions, including direct and quark-exchange terms. The formalism is demonstrated by deriving the one-pion-exchange potential between two nucleons and by computing the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter in the Hartree approximation.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation energy: E/A - M_N = -15.64 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.364 fm^{-1}, using parameters 9C_s/2f_pi^2 = 8.09, 9C_v/2f_pi^2 = 0.56, b = 0.6 fm, m_q = 350 MeV.
* At saturation: effective quark mass ratio m*_q/m_q = 0.81, in-medium nucleon size ratio b*/b = 1.05.
* Compressibility at saturation: 150 MeV.
* Constituent quark mass: m ~ 300 MeV (for u and d quarks).
* Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Lambda_{chiSB} ~ 1 GeV.
* Confinement scale: Lambda_{conf} ~ 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Fock-Tani formalism, originally developed by Girardeau and by Vorob'ev and Khomkin for atomic systems, to hadronic physics within the effective chiral Lagrangian framework of Manohar and Georgi (1984). It enables the study of short-range hadron-hadron interactions and hadron properties in hot or dense medium using standard field-theoretic techniques (Feynman diagrams, Green's functions), without the need for regularization of the scattering equation at tree level due to form factors from baryon wave functions. The approach contrasts with traditional one-boson exchange models and aims to calculate nucleon-nucleon phase shifts by including four-point interactions and one-gluon exchange beyond the tree-level pion exchange presented here.
