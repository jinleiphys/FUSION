# Simple condensation of composite bosons in a number conserving approach to many fermion systems

> We recently derived the Hamiltonian of fermionic composites by an exact procedure of bosonization. In the present paper expand this Hamiltonian in the inverse of the number of fermionic states in the composite wave function and give the necessary and sufficient conditions for the validity of such an expansion. We compare the results to the Random phase Approximation and the BCS theory and perform an illustrative application of the method.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact bosonization procedure for many-fermion systems that respects fermion number conservation, and expands the resulting bosonic Hamiltonian in inverse powers of Omega (the number of fermionic states in the composite wave function). The key condition for the validity of this expansion and for simple condensation is that the total occupation of noncondensed bosons must be much smaller than Omega.

## Method

The method starts from the operator form of the partition function, performs an independent Bogoliubov transformation at each time slice after Euclidean time discretization, and associates the time-dependent transformation parameters with dynamical composite boson fields. The bosonic Hamiltonian is expanded in powers of Omega^{-1/2} under the assumption of simple condensation (one condensed mode with large occupation), and the resulting Hamiltonian is diagonalized by introducing phonon operators and performing a bosonic Bogoliubov transformation. The structure functions of the composites are determined variationally under normalization and decoupling conditions.

## Key numbers

- The index of nilpotency Omega is defined as the number of fermionic states in the composite structure functions; for a single j-shell, Omega = j + 1/2
- The expansion parameter is Omega^{-1/2}; terms of order Omega^{-1/2} are neglected in the leading approximation
- The first term in the expanded Hamiltonian is of order Omega, the second and third terms are of order Omega^{1/2}, and the remaining terms are of order Omega^0
- The coupling constants are assumed to scale as g_K ~ Omega^{-1} * delta e, where delta e is the single fermion energy spreading
- For the illustrative application in a single j-shell with monopole and quadrupole pairing, the phonon energies are given by E_L = Omega * g_0 * |1 - xi| * sqrt(1 + [1/(1-xi)] * [1 - 4*(1/2 - nu)^2]), where xi = g_2/g_0
- The phonon energies vanish for xi = 1 (close to the critical line)
- For xi = 0, the spectrum reproduces the pairing model

## Context

This work extends the author's previous exact bosonization procedure (Palumbo, 2008) from one-dimensional condensation sectors to condensation sectors of arbitrary dimension. The method is compared to Random Phase Approximation and BCS theory, and the BCS energy per particle in quasichemical equilibrium theory is identified with the parameter E_{00}. The formalism is motivated by applications to BCS-BEC transitions in ultracold fermionic atoms, shape transitions and shape coexistence in atomic nuclei, and derivation of the phenomenological Interacting Boson Model (Arima and Iachello, 1975) from an effective nuclear Hamiltonian.
