# Vector meson-Baryon dynamics and generation of resonances

> The purpose of this work is to study vector meson-octet baryon interactions with the aim to find dynamical generation of resonances in such systems. For this, we consider s-, t-, u-channel diagrams along with a contact interaction originating from the hidden local symmetry Lagrangian. We find the contribution from all these sources, except the s-channel, to be important. The amplitudes obtained by solving coupled channel Bethe Salpeter equations for systems with total strangeness zero, show generation of one isospin 3/2, spin 1/2 resonance and three isospin 1/2 resonances: two with spin 3/2 and one with spin 1/2. We identify these resonances with $Δ(1900) S_{31}$, $N^* (2080) D_{13}$, $N^* (1700) D_{13}$, and $N^*(2090) S_{11}$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that vector meson-octet baryon interactions, when including s-, u-channel and contact terms beyond the t-channel alone, dynamically generate four baryon resonances with total strangeness zero, but the identification with specific observed states (Delta(1900) S31, N*(2080) D13, N*(1700) D13, N*(2090) S11) is based on matching masses and quantum numbers from the model, not on direct experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors constructed vector meson-octet baryon interactions using the hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian, deriving amplitudes for t-channel vector exchange, s- and u-channel octet baryon exchange, and a contact interaction. They solved coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations in s-wave for systems with total strangeness zero, using dimensional regularization for loop functions and including vector meson widths via convolution over mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- One resonance identified with Delta(1900) S31: isospin 3/2, spin 1/2
- Three resonances identified with N*(2080) D13 (isospin 1/2, spin 3/2), N*(1700) D13 (isospin 1/2, spin 3/2), and N*(2090) S11 (isospin 1/2, spin 1/2)
- A bound state pole found at 1702 - i0 MeV (without vector meson width convolution) from t-channel only calculations
- The form factor cutoff parameter Lambda set to 650 MeV, corresponding to a hadron size of about 0.6 fm
- The subtraction constant a(mu) set to -2, following the work of Eulogio (2011)
- Pion decay constant f_pi taken as 93 MeV
- Vector meson widths used: Gamma_rho = 149.4 MeV, Gamma_K* = 50.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the t-channel-only vector meson-baryon interaction study by Eulogio (2011), which found spin-degenerate resonances, by adding s-channel, u-channel, and contact interactions that introduce spin dependence. The authors note that the pseudoscalar meson-baryon interaction is well described by chiral symmetry (as in the work on Lambda(1405) by Kaiser, Ramos, and Jido), but vector meson-baryon interactions lack such guidance, making this a systematic exploration of all leading-order diagrams from HLS. The results enable future studies of vector meson photoproduction and further investigation of the role of spin-dependent interactions in dynamically generated baryon resonances.
