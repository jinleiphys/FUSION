# Production rate and ellipticity of lepton pairs from a rotating hot and dense QCD medium

> Using a current-current correlation function (CF), the photon polarization tensor is calculated for a rotating hot and dense QCD medium. The spectral function (SF) and the dilepton rate (DR) are estimated therefrom. Numerical results show that both SF and DR are enhanced in a rotating medium, especially in a low invariant mass region. SF and DR are also explored in the consequences of the interplay among the angular velocity, temperature and chemical potential. We also estimated the electromagnetic screening by calculating the Debye mass and it shows a suppression for a rotating QCD medium. The most interesting observation is the azimuthal anisotropy of the dilepton production, i.e, the elliptic flow $v_{2}$ of the lepton pair induced by the rotation as an external field. The competition between the centrifugal effect and the spin polarization effect due to rotation results in a convex down behaviour of the elliptic flow as a function of the transverse momentum in a relatively large magnitude of angular velocity. It is noticed that quark spin polarization induces a negative $v_2$ in the case of large angular velocity.

## Key claim

The paper claims that rotation enhances the spectral function and dilepton production rate in a hot and dense QCD medium, particularly at low invariant mass, and induces a non-zero elliptic flow \(v_2\) of lepton pairs; however, this enhancement and the sign of \(v_2\) depend on the competition between centrifugal and spin polarization effects, with large angular velocity potentially producing a negative \(v_2\).

## Method

The authors calculate the one-loop photon polarization tensor (vector current-current correlation function) in a rotating QCD medium using a fermion propagator derived in a co-rotating frame with vierbein formalism. The spectral function is obtained from the imaginary part of the polarization tensor, and the dilepton production rate is derived from the spectral function via the standard relation. The elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) is computed from the azimuthal-angle-dependent part of the dilepton rate, and the Debye mass is extracted from the static limit of the temporal component of the polarization tensor.

## Key numbers

* Spectral function and dilepton rate are enhanced by rotation at low invariant mass \(M/T\); for angular velocity \(\Omega = 0.4\) GeV at \(T = 0.2\) GeV and \(\mu = 0\), the ratio of the dilepton rate to the Born rate exceeds unity by approximately \(15-20\%\) in the low invariant mass region.
* The Debye mass is suppressed in a rotating medium compared to the non-rotating case (no explicit numerical value given for the suppression magnitude).
* Elliptic flow \(v_2\) of lepton pairs is induced by rotation; at large angular velocity, quark spin polarization leads to a negative \(v_2\) (no explicit numerical \(v_2\) value reported in the text).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vector current correlation functions and dilepton production in QCD matter, which were performed for non-rotating media (e.g., Islam (2014), Bandyopadhyay (2016)) or under magnetic fields (e.g., Wang (2020), Wang (2021)), by introducing the effect of rotation. It also complements hydrodynamic simulations of dilepton elliptic flow (e.g., Xu (2014), Vujanovic (2013)) by providing a field-theoretic calculation of rotation-induced anisotropy. The results enable future investigations of how vorticity in heavy-ion collisions can be probed via dilepton observables, particularly the elliptic flow.
