# Proton-Proton Scattering Lengths from Effective Field Theory

> Using a recently developed effective field theory for the interactions of nucleons at non-relativistic energies, we calculate the Coulomb corrections to proton-proton scattering. Including the dimension-eight derivative interaction in PDS regularization scheme, we obtain a modified Jackson-Blatt relation for the scattering lengths which is found to be phenomenologically satisfactory. The effective range is not modified by Coulomb effects to this order in the calculation.

## Key claim

Using an effective field theory with the PDS regularization scheme, the authors derive a modified Jackson-Blatt relation for the proton-proton scattering length that includes Coulomb corrections, but the result is scheme-dependent and the hadronic scattering length is not a directly measurable quantity.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Lagrangian for non-relativistic protons in the spin-singlet channel, including a lowest-order contact interaction (C0) and the static Coulomb repulsion. They calculate the proton-proton scattering amplitude as a geometric series of Coulomb-dressed bubbles, regularizing the divergent integrals using the PDS scheme in d = 3 - ε dimensions with renormalization mass μ. They then include the dimension-eight derivative interaction (C2) perturbatively to obtain the effective range and its contribution to the scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb-modified scattering length extracted from phenomenology is a_pp^C = -7.82 fm, with effective range r0 = 2.83 fm.
- The Jackson-Blatt relation gives a_pp = -17.0 fm for the strong scattering length, close to the nn scattering length of a_nn = -18.8 fm.
- For comparison, the pn scattering length is a_pn = -23.75 fm and the nn scattering length is a_nn = -18.8 fm.
- With the C2 interaction included, the inverse scattering length at μ = m_π is -1/a(μ = m_π) = 6.6 MeV, compared to -1/a_pn = 8.3 MeV, -1/a_nn = 10.5 MeV, and the Jackson-Blatt result -1/a_pp = 11.6 MeV.
- The blowup of the hadronic scattering length occurs for a momentum cutoff Λ ≈ 240 MeV when using cutoff regularization.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for nucleon interactions to include Coulomb corrections for proton-proton scattering. It provides a field-theoretic derivation of the Jackson-Blatt relation (Jackson and Blatt, 1950) and shows that the effective range is not modified by Coulomb effects to this order, consistent with experimental observations. The result enables future calculations of three-nucleon systems like 3He within this effective field theory framework.
