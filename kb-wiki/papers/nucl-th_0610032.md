# Hadron spectrum and hadrons in the nuclear medium

> Some recent developments in chiral dynamics of hadrons and hadrons in a medium are presented. Unitary schemes based on chiral Lagrangians describe some hadronic states as being dynamically generated resonances. We discuss how standard quantum many body techniques can be used to calculate the properties of these dynamically generated and other hadrons in the nuclear medium. We present some results for vector mesons ($ρ$ and $ϕ$), scalar mesons ($σ$, $κ$, $a_0(980)$, $f_0(980)$), the $Λ(1520)$ and for the in-medium baryon-baryon interaction.

## Key claim

Unitary chiral models can dynamically generate hadronic resonances, and standard quantum many-body techniques can then calculate how those resonances and other hadrons change in the nuclear medium; however, the results depend sensitively on the chosen antikaon selfenergy, which remains controversial due to conflicting experimental data on kaonic atoms.

## Method

The author starts from chiral unitary models in coupled channels that successfully describe meson-meson and meson-baryon scattering in vacuum, generating resonances as poles of the scattering amplitudes. Nuclear medium effects are then incorporated by modifying the pseudoscalar meson and baryon propagators with density-dependent selfenergies, calculated using standard many-body techniques that account for interactions with nucleons, including vertex corrections and new decay channels opened by the medium.

## Key numbers

- Pion propagator at normal nuclear density (rho = rho_0) for a pion with momentum q = 500 MeV shows three wide peaks in its imaginary part, corresponding to p-h excitation, the pion peak at lower energies than in vacuum, and Delta-h excitation.
- The Lambda(1520) resonance in vacuum couples strongly to the pi Sigma*(1385) channel, and in the nuclear medium its predicted width becomes approximately 5 times larger than in vacuum at normal nuclear matter densities.
- The Lambda and Sigma masses shift downward by -30 MeV at normal nuclear density (rho = rho_0).
- The phi meson width in the medium becomes one order of magnitude larger than in vacuum, with some small mass reduction.
- The dynamically generated Lambda(1405) is strongly diluted at rho = rho_0, remaining only as a small bump close to its vacuum position.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller (1997) and Oset (2001) for dynamically generated resonances, applying it to in-medium properties of hadrons. It contrasts with earlier scaling-law predictions of strong rho meson mass dropping (Hatsuda 1992) by finding very little mass shift for the rho, consistent with other microscopic calculations (Urban 1998, Post 2000) and recent experimental data (Damjanovic 2005). The results enable experimental tests of medium effects through photoproduction reactions, A-dependent production of the Lambda(1520), and studies of two-pion production in nuclei.
