# Machine Learning Approach to Study of Low Energy Alpha-Deuteron Elastic Scattering using Phase Function Method

> Central idea: To obtain the interaction potential using the inverse scattering method, we have employed the Physics-Informed Machine Learning (PIML) approach. In this framework, the machine learning algorithm is guided by the underlying physical laws, enabling the accurate extraction of the inverse scattering potential from the elastic scattering data. Methodology: As a reference potential, a combination of three smoothly joined Morse functions has been utilized, characterized by ten model parameters. These parameters are optimized in an iterative fashion using a Genetic Algorithm to ensure the best fit to the phase shifts extracted from the experimental scattering data. The process of optimization is guided by the computed scattering phase shifts by solving the phase equation using 5th order RK-method for the reference potential in each iteration Results: Our approach yields inverse potentials for both single and multi channel scattering. Using the Scattering Phase Shifts obtained from these inverse potentials, we calculate the partial cross-section to determine the resonance energies and decay width. The obtain values of resonance energies and decay width for 3D1, 3D2 and 3D3 states of alpha-deuteron are in correspondence with the experimental results. Conclusion: It can be concluded that our machine learning-based approach for constructing the inverse potential offers a novel and complementary technique to existing direct methods.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Physics-Informed Machine Learning (PIML) approach, using a Genetic Algorithm to optimize the parameters of a reference potential composed of three smoothly joined Morse functions, can accurately extract inverse scattering potentials for the alpha-deuteron system from elastic scattering phase shift data up to 14 MeV, with the qualification that the resonance energies and decay widths obtained for the \(^3D_1\), \(^3D_2\), and \(^3D_3\) states are in correspondence with experimental results, though some discrepancies remain (e.g., the \(^3D_1\) resonance energy is 6.64 MeV versus the experimental 5.7 MeV).

## Method

The authors constructed a reference potential from three smoothly joined Morse functions characterized by ten free model parameters, with four additional parameters determined by enforcing continuity of the potential and its derivative at the two boundary points. They used a Genetic Algorithm to iteratively optimize these parameters by minimizing the Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) between simulated scattering phase shifts, computed by numerically solving the phase equation (a first-order nonlinear Riccati equation) using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta method, and experimental phase shift data from Schmelzbach et al. (1972) for deuteron energies 3.0 to 5.8 MeV and Jenny et al. (1983) for 6 to 14 MeV. For the coupled-channel case (J=1, involving \(^3S_1\) and \(^3D_1\) states), the method was extended to solve three coupled nonlinear first-order equations using Stapp parametrization, optimizing 30 parameters.

## Key numbers

- The inverse potential for the \(^3D_2\) state has a depth of 57.54 MeV at a distance of 1.1 fm.
- The inverse potential for the \(^3D_3\) state has a depth of 69.83 MeV at a distance of 0.94 fm.
- The inverse potential for the \(^3D_1\) state (from multi-channel scattering) has a depth of 38.22 MeV at a distance of 1.15 fm.
- The resonance energy obtained for the \(^3D_1\) state is \(E_r = 6.64\) MeV with a center-of-mass decay width \(\Gamma_{CoM} = 2.18\) MeV; the experimental values are 5.7 MeV and 1.9 MeV (Tilley 2002).
- The resonance energy obtained for the \(^3D_2\) state is \(E_r = 4.66\) MeV; the experimental value is 4.36 MeV.
- The \(^3P_2\) state potential well depth is 7.21 MeV.
- The \(^3P_1\) state potential well depth is approximately 4.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous methodology for constructing inverse potentials using Morse functions, which was applied to the \(\alpha-\alpha\) system (Sastri 2024) and the n-p system (Awasthi 2024), now applied to the \(\alpha-d\) system to study low-lying excited states of \(^6Li\) with isospin T=0. The approach offers a complementary technique to existing direct methods for obtaining interaction potentials needed for indirect studies of the \(\alpha+d \to ^6Li + \gamma\) radiative capture reaction, which is the only process producing \(^6Li\) in big bang nucleosynthesis but has an extremely low cross section at astrophysically relevant energies (E \(\leq\) 300 keV), making direct measurements virtually impossible (Hammache 2010). The results enable further analysis of the cluster dynamics of \(^6Li\) as a weakly bound alpha-deuteron system and provide inverse potentials that can be used in calculations of the radiative capture process.
