# Antikaons and hyperons in nuclear matter with saturation

> We evaluate the antikaon and hyperon spectral functions in a self-consistent and covariant many-body approach. The computation is based on coupled-channel dynamics derived from the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. A novel subtraction scheme is developed that avoids kinematical singularities and medium-induced power divergencies all together. Scalar and vector mean fields are used to model nuclear binding and saturation. The effect of the latter is striking for the antikaon spectral function that becomes significantly more narrow at small momenta. Attractive mass shifts of about 30 and 40 MeV are predicted for the Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1385) resonances. Once scalar and vector mean fields for the nucleon are switched on the Lambda(1520) resonances dissolves almost completely in nuclear matter. All together only moderate attraction is predicted for the nuclear antikaon systems at saturation density. However, at larger densities we predict a sizable population of soft antikaon modes that arise from the coupling of the antikaon to a highly collective Lambda(1115) nucleon-hole state. This may lead to the formation of exotic nuclear systems with strangeness and antikaon condensation in compact stars at moderate densities.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that scalar and vector mean fields modeling nuclear binding and saturation have a striking effect on the antikaon spectral function, making it significantly more narrow at small momenta, while only moderate attraction is predicted for nuclear antikaon systems at saturation density; at larger densities, a sizable population of soft antikaon modes may arise from coupling to a collective Lambda(1115) nucleon-hole state, potentially enabling antikaon condensation in compact stars.

## Method

The authors generalize a self-consistent and covariant many-body approach based on coupled-channel dynamics derived from the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian, incorporating scalar and vector mean fields for the nucleon to model nuclear binding and saturation. A novel subtraction scheme is developed to avoid kinematical singularities and medium-induced power divergencies in the in-medium meson-baryon loop functions. The antikaon self-energy is computed self-consistently by iterating the in-medium scattering amplitudes derived from free-space partial-wave amplitudes, using a projector algebra that handles s-, p-, and d-wave interactions in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- Attractive mass shifts of about 30 MeV and 40 MeV are predicted for the Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1385) resonances, respectively, in cold nuclear matter.
- Scalar and vector mean fields are parametrized as Sigma_V = 290 MeV * (rho/rho_0) and Sigma_S = 350 MeV * (rho/rho_0), where rho_0 is saturation density.
- The Lambda(1520) resonance dissolves almost completely already at saturation density.
- The Fermi momentum k_F parameterizes the density rho of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter via rho = 2*k_F^3/(3*pi^2) in the rest frame.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent covariant many-body approach of Lutz and Korpa (2002) by incorporating scalar and vector mean fields to model nuclear binding and saturation, an effect previously claimed to be small by Waas, Rho and Weise (1997) and others using non-relativistic approaches with an attractive mean field potential of about 50 MeV for the nucleon. The results differ from those of Tolos, Ramos, Polls and Kuo (2001) and from recent works by Oset and coworkers (2006), and do not support the strong-attraction scenario advocated by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002). The work enables further study of deeply bound exotic nuclear systems with strangeness and antikaon condensation in compact stars at moderate densities.
