# Uncertainties in determining parton distributions at large x

> We critically examine uncertainties in parton distribution functions (PDFs) at large x arising from nuclear effects in deuterium F2 structure function data. Within a global PDF analysis, we assess the impact on the PDFs from uncertainties in the deuteron wave function at short distances and nucleon off-shell effects, the use of relativistic kinematics, as well as the use of less a restrictive parametrization of the d/u ratio. We find that in particular the d-quark and gluon PDFs vary significantly with the choice of nuclear model. We highlight the impact of these uncertainties on the determination of the neutron structure function, and on W boson production and parton luminosity at the Tevatron and the LHC. Finally, we discuss prospects for new measurements sensitive to the d-quark and gluon distributions but insensitive to nuclear corrections.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear corrections in deuterium, particularly from off-shell effects and relativistic kinematics, introduce significant uncertainties in the determination of parton distribution functions (PDFs) at large x (x > 0.5), with the d-quark and gluon PDFs varying substantially depending on the nuclear model chosen, contrary to the common assumption that deuterium nuclear effects are negligible.

## Method

The authors performed a global PDF analysis (the CJ/CTEQ-Jefferson Lab fits) using charged lepton deep inelastic scattering on proton and deuterium targets, lepton pair production, W boson asymmetry, and jet production data from the Tevatron, with cuts Q^2 > 1.69 GeV^2 and W^2 > 3 GeV^2. They systematically varied the treatment of deuterium nuclear corrections by testing different deuteron wave functions (AV18, CD-Bonn, Paris, WJC-1, WJC-2), comparing nonrelativistic weak binding approximation (WBA) with relativistic convolution models (MST, light-cone), and incorporating nucleon off-shell effects through the modified Kulagin-Petti (mKP) model with a range of confinement radius parameters.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is epsilon_d = -2.2 MeV, approximately 0.1% of its mass
- The typical nucleon momentum in the deuteron is |p| ~ 130 MeV, with average nucleon virtuality sqrt(p^2) ~ 2% smaller than the free nucleon mass
- The CD-Bonn wave function gives an approximately 10% smaller ratio R^{d/N} = F_2^d/F_2^N at x ~ 0.9 compared to the AV18 wave function
- Relativistic kinematic effects on R^{d/N} are negligible for x < 0.6, but grow to around 5% at x ~ 0.9
- The MST off-shell model reduces F_2^d by about 2% compared to the on-shell convolution approximation
- The mKP off-shell correction gives R^{d/N} 5% to 15% smaller than the on-shell ratio at x = 0.9
- The shadowing correction ranges from about 1.5% at x = 10^{-2} to about 3% for x < 10^{-5}
- The parameter delta p^2/M^2 for the deuteron ranges from -3.6% to -6.5% depending on the wave function and kinematics
- The confinement radius change for the deuteron is delta R_N/R_N approximately 1.5% to 1.8%

## Context

This work extends the previous CTEQ6X analysis (Accardi et al., 2010) by systematically quantifying the impact of deuterium nuclear corrections on PDFs at large x, which most global fits (such as CTEQ, MSTW, NNPDF, GJR, ABKM, and HERAPDF) had previously ignored or treated as negligible. The study enables more reliable extraction of the d-quark PDF and neutron structure function from deuterium data, and highlights the need for new measurements (such as at Jefferson Lab) that are sensitive to d-quark and gluon distributions but insensitive to nuclear corrections. The paper also identifies anti-correlation between the d-quark and gluon PDFs in jet production data, where variations in the d PDF can be compensated by opposite variations in the gluon PDF.
