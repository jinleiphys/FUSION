# Heavy-Meson Decay Constants: Isospin Breaking from QCD Sum Rules

> Compared with lattice QCD, isospin breaking of heavy-meson decay constants from QCD sum rules agrees for $D$ mesons but disagrees (by a factor of four) for $B$ mesons.

## Key claim

The authors claim that QCD sum rules predict isospin-breaking differences in heavy-meson decay constants that agree with lattice QCD for D mesons but are a factor of four smaller for B mesons, with the B-meson discrepancy representing a tension of about three standard deviations.

## Method

The authors used an improved QCD sum-rule formalism with Borel-parameter-dependent effective thresholds modeled by polynomial ansatze of order n=1, 2, and 3, optimized to reproduce experimental meson masses. They computed the decay constants of heavy-light mesons (D, D*, B, B*) as functions of the light-quark mass, then extracted the slopes of the decay-constant ratios at the average u-d mass point using linear, quadratic, and heavy-meson chiral perturbation theory (HMchiPT) fits. The decay-constant differences were obtained by multiplying these slopes by the u-d quark mass difference.

## Key numbers

- For D mesons: f_{D+} - f_{D0} = (0.95 +/- 0.13) MeV from QCD sum rules, compared to lattice QCD result of 0.94_{-0.12}^{+0.50} MeV
- For B mesons: f_{B0} - f_{B+} = (0.92 +/- 0.13) MeV from QCD sum rules, compared to lattice QCD result of 3.8 +/- 1.0 MeV (factor of four discrepancy)
- Normalized decay-constant differences: (f_{D+} - f_{D0})/f_D = 0.0046(6), (f_{B0} - f_{B+})/f_B = 0.0048(6)
- For vector mesons: f_{D*+} - f_{D*0} = (1.69 +/- 0.27) MeV, f_{B*0} - f_{B*+} = (0.82 +/- 0.11) MeV
- Input quark masses at 2 GeV: m_u = (2.3^{+0.7}_{-0.5}) MeV, m_d = (4.8^{+0.5}_{-0.3}) MeV, m_ud = (3.5^{+0.7}_{-0.2}) MeV
- Input u-d mass difference at 2 GeV: delta m = (2.67 +/- 0.22) MeV from lattice QCD (FLAG)
- Decay constant values used: f_D = (206.2 +/- 8.9) MeV, f_B = (192.0 +/- 14.6) MeV, f_{D*} = (252.2 +/- 22.7) MeV, f_{B*} = (181.8 +/- 13.7) MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of QCD sum-rule accuracy and precision (Lucha et al., 2007-2015) and their extractions of heavy-light meson decay constants (Lucha et al., 2011-2015). The results for D mesons agree with lattice QCD calculations (Lattice 2015), but the B-meson results are a factor of four smaller than lattice claims (Lattice 2015, PDG 2015), creating a three-standard-deviation tension. The authors note their results are similar in size to lattice QCD results for K mesons (Lattice 2014), suggesting consistency across light-quark systems. This work enables further scrutiny of isospin-breaking effects in heavy-meson systems and highlights the need for resolution between sum-rule and lattice approaches for B mesons.
