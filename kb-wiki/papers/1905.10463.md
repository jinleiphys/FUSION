# Quantum Kinetic Theory of Spin Polarization of Massive Quarks in Perturbative QCD: Leading Log

> We present the quantum kinetic equation for spin polarization of massive quarks in leading log order of perturbative QCD, which describes time evolution of the spin density matrix in momentum space of a massive quark interacting with a background QCD plasma. We find that the time evolution operator of the spin density matrix, or the quantum kinetic collision terms, are universally of order $α_s^2\log(1/α_s)$ in terms of the QCD coupling constant $α_s=g^2/(4π)$. Our quantum kinetic equation is valid for an arbitrary quark mass $m\gg m_D\sim gT$, where $m_D$ is the Debye mass, and can be used to study relaxation dynamics of spin polarization of massive quarks in perturbative QCD regime.

## Key claim

The paper derives the quantum kinetic equation for spin polarization of massive quarks in perturbative QCD at leading log order, finding that the relaxation operator for the spin density matrix is universally of order $\alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s)$ in terms of the QCD coupling constant, but this result is restricted to the case of vanishing vorticity and magnetic field (the $\Gamma_0$ term only), and applies only for quark masses satisfying $m \gg m_D \sim gT$.

## Method

The authors use the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism to compute the time evolution of the spin density matrix of a massive quark interacting with a thermal QCD plasma background. They expand the unitary time evolution operators to quadratic order in the QCD interaction Hamiltonian, obtaining cross and self-energy contributions expressed in terms of gluon two-point correlation functions. The computation focuses on the leading log order from soft t-channel gluon exchange, using Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) resummed gluon propagators, and assumes the spatial homogeneous limit where the density matrix is diagonal in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation rate for spin polarization is of order $\Gamma_0 \sim \alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s) T$, where $\alpha_s = g^2/(4\pi)$
- The quark mass condition is $m \gg m_D \sim gT$, where $m_D$ is the Debye mass
- The soft momentum exchange range that produces the leading log is $m_D \sim gT \ll q \ll T$
- The correlation time for the gluon two-point functions is $\tau_c \lesssim (gT)^{-1}$
- The characteristic relaxation time is $\tau_R \sim (\alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s) T)^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of spin polarization in the strongly coupled regime using AdS/CFT correspondence (Li 2018) to the perturbative QCD regime. It provides the collision term for the quantum kinetic equation of massive quarks, complementing recent studies of the free-streaming collisionless quantum kinetic equation by Mueller (2019), Weickgenannt (2019), Gao (2019), and Hattori (2019). The results are applicable to strange quarks and heavier quark species in the deconfined phase of heavy-ion collisions, but not to light (u,d) quarks or gluons, whose treatment is deferred to future work.
