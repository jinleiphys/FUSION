# Ab initio Green's functions approach for homogeneous nuclear matter

> Homogeneous nuclear matter is investigated using the \textit{ab initio} Self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach with nuclear interactions based on chiral effective field theory. The employed method, which combines the state-of-the-art algebraic diagrammatic construction approximation at third order with Gorkov correlations, is capable of computing both the equation of state (EOS) and single-particle properties of nuclear matter. The EOS calculated with our approach and coupled-cluster theory are shown to agree very well. The one-nucleon spectral functions and the momentum distributions are discussed to gain insights into the dynamics of the interacting nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The Self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach using the algebraic diagrammatic construction at third order with Gorkov correlations produces an equation of state for homogeneous nuclear matter that agrees very well with coupled-cluster theory, but the authors note that pairing correlations are approximated only at first order and that twist-averaged boundary conditions are preferred over periodic boundary conditions for single-particle properties.

## Method

The authors solve the Gorkov equations for the one-body Green's function of homogeneous nuclear matter, approximating the normal self-energy using the algebraic diagrammatic construction scheme at third order [ADC(3)] and the anomalous (pairing) self-energy at first order. They simulate the infinite system using a cubic unit cell with a finite number of nucleons: 66 neutrons for pure neutron matter (PNM) and 132 nucleons for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM), employing periodic boundary conditions for the equation of state and twist-averaged boundary conditions for single-particle properties. The chiral interaction Delta NNLOgo(450) from DeltaGo (2020) is used throughout.

## Key numbers

- Equation of state computed at multiple densities for SNM and PNM using ADC(3), ADC(3)-D, and CCD(T) methods with the Delta NNLOgo(450) interaction
- Spectral functions and momentum distributions calculated at density rho = 0.16 fm^{-3} for both SNM and PNM
- SNM calculations use A = 132 nucleons; PNM calculations use N = 66 neutrons
- Spectral function shows fragmentation at momenta k > 1.5 fm^{-1} in SNM, indicating strong correlations
- Momentum distributions show depletion of hole states (k < k_F) and population of particle states (k > k_F), with larger depletion in SNM than PNM at rho = 0.16 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the SCGF approach for nuclear matter previously developed by Barbieri (2017), Marino (2024), and others, and directly compares with coupled-cluster theory as formulated by Hagen (2014). The results validate the Landau quasi-particle picture at the Fermi surface, as discussed by Rios (2012) and Dickhof and Van Neck. The authors plan to apply the method to superfluid neutron matter, quasi-particle properties such as effective mass and lifetimes, and momentum distributions across different densities and isospin asymmetries.
