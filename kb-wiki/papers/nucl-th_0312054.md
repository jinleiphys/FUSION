# Effects of momentum-dependent symmetry potential on heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei

> Using an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model we study effects of the momentum-dependent symmetry potential on heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei. It is found that symmetry potentials with and without the momentum-dependence but corresponding to the same density-dependent symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$ lead to significantly different predictions on several $E_{sym}(ρ)$-sensitive experimental observables especially for energetic nucleons. The momentum- and density-dependence of the symmetry potential have to be determined simultaneously in order to extract the $E_{sym}(ρ)$ accurately. The isospin asymmetry of midrapidity nucleons at high transverse momenta is particularly sensitive to the momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential. It is thus very useful for investigating accurately the equation of state of dense neutron-rich matter.

## Key claim

Symmetry potentials with and without momentum-dependence but corresponding to the same density-dependent symmetry energy \(E_{sym}(\rho)\) lead to significantly different predictions on several \(E_{sym}(\rho)\)-sensitive experimental observables, especially for energetic nucleons; the momentum- and density-dependence of the symmetry potential must be determined simultaneously to extract \(E_{sym}(\rho)\) accurately.

## Method

The authors used an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model (IBUU04) to simulate \(^{132}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. They compared results using a momentum-dependent single-nucleon potential (MDI) parameterized from Gogny Hartree-Fock calculations against a momentum-independent symmetry potential that gives the same \(E_{sym}(\rho)\), for two different density dependences of the symmetry energy (MDI(1) and MDI(0)). The isoscalar potential was kept approximately the same across comparisons using the MDYI interaction.

## Key numbers

* The strength of the symmetry potential at normal density decreases from about 33 MeV at \(E_{kin}=0\) with a slope of about 0.22 for \(E_{kin}\leq 100\) MeV, in agreement with nucleon-nucleus scattering data.
* The compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter \(K_0\) is set to 211 MeV for the MDI interaction and 215 MeV for the MDYI interaction.
* The interaction strength parameters are \(C_{unlike}=-103.4\) MeV and \(C_{like}=-11.7\) MeV for the momentum-dependent part.
* Without the momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential, the dN/dy values for neutrons increase by 10 to 15% relative to results with momentum-dependent symmetry potentials.
* The isospin asymmetry of bound nucleons increases by about 12% in central collisions and 5% in midcentral collisions going from the stiffer to the softer symmetry energy.
* The variation in the rapidity distribution of \(\delta_{free}\) is less than 8% when changing from the MDYI to the soft BKD isoscalar potential with the same symmetry potential.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isospin-dependent transport models (Li 1997, Li 2000) that identified observables like neutron/proton ratio, isoscaling, and neutron-proton differential flow as probes of the symmetry energy, but which had neglected momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential. It builds on the recent parameterization of a momentum-dependent symmetry potential by Das et al. (2003) using Gogny effective interactions. The results enable future experiments at RIA and GSI to simultaneously determine the density- and momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential, and thus uniquely extract the density dependence of the symmetry energy for dense neutron-rich matter.
