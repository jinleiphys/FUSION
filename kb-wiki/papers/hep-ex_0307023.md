# Quark fragmentation to $π^{\pm}$, $π^{0}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$ and $\bar{p}$ in the nuclear environment

> The influence of the nuclear medium on lepto-production of hadrons was studied in the HERMES experiment at DESY in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering of 27.6 GeV positrons off deuterium, nitrogen and krypton targets. The differential multiplicity for krypton relative to that of deuterium has been measured for the first time for various identified hadrons ($π^+$, $π^-$, $π^0$, $K^+$, $K^-$, $p$ and $\bar{p}$) as a function of the virtual photon energy $ν$, the fraction $z$ of this energy transferred to the hadron, and the hadron transverse momentum squared $p_t^2$. The multiplicity ratio is strongly reduced in the nuclear medium at low $ν$ and high $z$, with significant differences among the various hadrons. The distribution of the hadron transverse momentum is broadened towards high $p_t^2$ in the nuclear medium, in a manner resembling the Cronin effect previously observed in collisions of heavy ions and protons with nuclei.

## Key claim

The nuclear medium significantly reduces the hadron multiplicity ratio (krypton relative to deuterium) at low virtual photon energy and high fractional energy transfer, with the magnitude of this attenuation depending strongly on hadron species; the effect is more pronounced for antiprotons than protons and for negative kaons than positive kaons, which the abstract does not detail.

## Method

The HERMES collaboration measured semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering of 27.6 GeV positrons on deuterium, nitrogen, and krypton gas targets internal to the HERA storage ring. Hadrons were identified using a dual-radiator Ring Imaging Cherenkov (RICH) detector for charged pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons in the momentum range 2.5-15 GeV (4-15 GeV for protons/antiprotons), while neutral pions were identified via two-photon invariant mass reconstruction from the electromagnetic calorimeter. The differential multiplicity ratio R_M^h was computed as a function of the virtual photon energy nu, the fractional energy transfer z, and the hadron transverse momentum squared p_t^2, with kinematic cuts Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, W > 2 GeV, y < 0.85, and z > 0.2.

## Key numbers

* Multiplicity ratio for charged hadrons on krypton: R_M^h = 0.802 ± 0.021 (integrated over nu and z, z > 0.2)
* Multiplicity ratio for charged hadrons on nitrogen: R_M^h = 0.954 ± 0.023 (integrated over nu and z, z > 0.2)
* For z > 0.5 on krypton: pi+ = 0.712 ± 0.023, pi- = 0.731 ± 0.031, pi0 = 0.728 ± 0.024, K+ = 0.766 ± 0.024, K- = 0.668 ± 0.036, p = 0.816 ± 0.029, antiproton = 0.705 ± 0.067
* For z > 0.2 on krypton: pi+ = 0.775 ± 0.019, pi- = 0.770 ± 0.021, pi0 = 0.807 ± 0.022, K+ = 0.880 ± 0.019, K- = 0.783 ± 0.021, p = 0.977 ± 0.027, antiproton = 0.717 ± 0.038
* Selected event counts: 8.2 × 10^5 (krypton) and 7.3 × 10^5 (deuterium) deep-inelastic scattering events; charged pions: 11.3 × 10^4 (krypton) and 13.5 × 10^4 (deuterium); neutral pions: 1.9 × 10^4 (krypton) and 2.3 × 10^4 (deuterium); kaons: 2.1 × 10^4 (krypton) and 2.3 × 10^4 (deuterium); protons and antiprotons: 1.1 × 10^4 for both targets
* Transverse momentum broadening observed for p_t^2 ≥ 0.7 GeV^2, with attenuation below that threshold

## Context

This work extends earlier HERMES measurements on nitrogen (Airapetian et al., 2001) by providing the first identified hadron multiplicity ratios for krypton, including neutral pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons. The results are compared to calculations by Accardi et al. (2002), which describe nuclear modification via rescaling of quark fragmentation functions with nuclear absorption, and by Guo and Wang (2004), which incorporate multiple parton scattering and induced energy loss. The observed differences between particle and antiparticle attenuation (especially protons vs. antiprotons) suggest sensitivity to hadron-nucleon cross sections and formation times, while the p_t broadening resembles the Cronin effect seen in heavy-ion and proton-nucleus collisions (Cronin et al., 1975). The data enable tests of QCD-based models of parton energy loss and provide constraints for interpreting ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
