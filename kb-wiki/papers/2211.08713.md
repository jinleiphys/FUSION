# Electric conductivity of hot pion matter

> The determination of transport coefficients plays a central role in characterizing hot and dense nuclear matter. Currently, there are significant discrepancies between various calculations of the electric conductivity of hot hadronic matter. In the present work we calculate the electric conductivity of hot pion matter by extracting it from the electromagnetic spectral function, via its zero energy limit at vanishing 3-momentum, within the Vector Dominance Model (VDM). Since within the VDM the photon couples to the hadronic currents primarily through the $ρ$ meson, we use hadronic many-body theory to calculate the $ρ$-meson's self-energy in hot pion matter, by dressing its pion cloud with thermal $π$-$ρ$ and $π$-$σ$ loops including vertex corrections to maintain gauge invariance. In particular, we analyze the low-energy transport peak of the spectral function, extract its behavior with temperature and compare to (the results of) existing approaches in the literature.

## Key claim

The electric conductivity of hot pion matter is extracted from the electromagnetic spectral function within the Vector Dominance Model, using hadronic many-body theory to calculate the rho-meson self-energy with thermal pion dressing; the calculation requires vertex corrections to maintain gauge invariance and a finite pion width to obtain a finite conductivity, and the results are compared to existing approaches that show discrepancies of up to an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors calculate the rho-meson self-energy at finite temperature using the imaginary-time formalism, dressing the pion cloud with thermal pi-rho and pi-sigma loops. They compute the pion self-energy from resonant S-wave (sigma) and P-wave (rho) pi-pi scattering in a thermal pion gas, constrained by vacuum pi-pi phase shifts and cross sections up to center-of-mass energies of 1 GeV. Vertex corrections are constructed to satisfy Ward-Takahashi identities and maintain gauge invariance when in-medium pion self-energies are introduced, and the conductivity is extracted from the zero-energy limit of the transverse electromagnetic spectral function at vanishing three-momentum.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi = 140 MeV (used throughout the calculation)
* Bare rho mass: m_rho^(0) = 853 MeV, with coupling g_rho = 5.9 and Pauli-Villars cutoff Lambda_0 = 1 GeV (fitted to P-wave pi-pi phase shifts and pion electromagnetic form factor)
* Sigma resonance parameters: bare mass m_sigma = 0.934 GeV, coupling g_sigma = 8.86, with form factor cutoff Lambda_sigma = 0.745 GeV; rho form factor cutoff Lambda_rho = 1.85 GeV (fitted to S- and P-wave pi-pi scattering phase shifts)
* At T = 150 MeV, the on-shell pion optical potential yields pion widths up to 30 MeV and mass shifts up to ±10 MeV
* The calculation is performed for temperatures up to 180 MeV, with the rho and sigma resonance contributions to pi-pi scattering constrained up to CM energies of about 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior hadronic many-body calculations of the rho-meson self-energy in nuclear matter by Urban (1999) and Rapp (1996) to the case of hot pion matter, serving as a first step in adding thermal pi-pi scattering to baryonic effects. It addresses the significant discrepancies (up to an order of magnitude or more) between existing calculations of the electric conductivity of hot hadronic matter by Greif, Huot, Fraile, Finazzo, Aarts, Brandt, Amato, Kadam, Ghosh, and others. The results enable future connections to low-mass dilepton and photon radiation observed in experiments at SPS, RHIC, HADES, and the LHC, and are relevant for planned measurements of the transport peak through very low-mass and low-momentum dileptons at SIS, RHIC, and the LHC.
