# Benchmark Results for Few-Body Hypernuclei

> The Non-Symmetrized Hyperspherical Harmonics method (NSHH) is introduced in the hypernuclear sector and benchmarked with three different ab-initio methods, namely the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo method, the Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach and the Gaussian Expansion Method. Binding energies and hyperon separation energies of three- to five-body hypernuclei are calculated by employing the two-body $Λ$N component of the phenomenological Bodmer-Usmani potential, and a hyperon-nucleon interaction simulating the scattering phase shifts given by NSC97f. The range of applicability of the NSHH method is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The Non-Symmetrized Hyperspherical Harmonics (NSHH) method is introduced for hypernuclear systems and benchmarked against three other ab-initio methods, showing good agreement for binding energies and hyperon separation energies of three- to five-body hypernuclei, but only when using the two-body ΛN component of the Bodmer-Usmani potential or a simulated NSC97f interaction; the omission of three-body hyperon-nucleon forces produces overestimated separation energies compared to experiment.

## Method

The NSHH method was extended to systems with two fermion species (nucleons and hyperons) by introducing a pseudo-Hamiltonian with a Casimir operator term to enforce antisymmetrization without explicit basis symmetrization. Binding energies and Λ separation energies were computed for A=3 to A=5 hypernuclei using the Argonne V4' or V8' NN potentials combined with either the two-body ΛN Bodmer-Usmani potential or a YN interaction simulating NSC97f phase shifts, and results were compared to Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC), Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY), and Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) calculations. A Lee-Suzuki procedure was applied to generate softer effective interactions for improved convergence.

## Key numbers

- ³_ΛH binding energy with AV4'+Usmani: NSHH = -2.530(3) MeV, FY = -2.537(1) MeV, AFDMC = -2.42(6) MeV
- ³_ΛH Λ separation energy B_Λ with AV4'+Usmani: NSHH = 0.290(3) MeV, FY = 0.292(1) MeV, AFDMC = 0.18(6) MeV (experimental B_Λ = 0.13(5) MeV)
- ⁴_ΛH B_Λ with AV4'+Usmani: NSHH = 3.04(1) MeV, AFDMC = 3.02(7) MeV (experimental B_Λ = 2.04(4) MeV)
- ⁵_ΛHe B_Λ with AV4'+Usmani: NSHH = 6.65(1) MeV, AFDMC = 6.67(6) MeV (experimental B_Λ = 3.12(2) MeV)
- ³_ΛH B_Λ with AV8'+NSC97f: NSHH = 0.17(2) MeV, FY = 0.189(1) MeV, GEM = 0.19(1) MeV
- ⁴_ΛH B_Λ with AV8'+NSC97f: NSHH = 2.29(7) MeV, GEM = 2.33(1) MeV
- NSHH basis dimensions: ~10² for three-body, ~10⁴ for four-body, ~10⁶ for five-body systems

## Context

This work extends the NSHH method, previously applied to nuclear systems by Gattobigio et al. (2011) and Barnea et al. (2013), into the hypernuclear sector for the first time. The benchmark against AFDMC (Lonardoni et al., 2013), FY (Yakubovsky, 1967), and GEM (Hiyama et al., 2014) establishes NSHH as a viable ab-initio tool for few-body hypernuclei. The results enable future studies of systems with A=5 to A=7 and strangeness S=-2, and the authors note that improved parallelization will allow treatment of larger basis dimensions.
