# 1-2-3-flavor color superconductivity in compact stars

> We suggest a scenario where the three light quark flavors are sequentially deconfined under increasing pressure in cold asymmetric nuclear matter, e.g., as in neutron stars. The basis for our analysis is a chiral quark matter model of Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type with diquark pairing in the spin-1 single flavor (CSL) and spin-0 two/three flavor (2SC/CFL) channels, and a Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach in the nuclear matter sector. We find that nucleon dissociation sets in at about the saturation density, n_0, when the down-quark Fermi sea is populated (d-quark dripline) due to the flavor asymmetry imposed by beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. At about 3n_0 u-quarks appear forming a two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase, while the s-quark Fermi sea is populated only at still higher baryon density. The hybrid star sequence has a maximum mass of 2.1 M_sun. Two- and three-flavor quark matter phases are found only in gravitationally unstable hybrid star solutions.

## Key claim
The authors propose a scenario where, under increasing pressure in cold asymmetric nuclear matter (e.g., in neutron stars), down quarks are the first to deconfine at about saturation density, forming a single-flavor color-spin-locking (CSL) phase coexisting with nuclear matter, before u-quarks and s-quarks appear at higher densities. The abstract glosses over the condition that two- and three-flavor quark matter phases are found only in gravitationally unstable hybrid star solutions.

## Method
The authors combine a Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach for nuclear matter with a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type chiral quark model including diquark pairing in the spin-1 single-flavor (CSL), spin-0 two-flavor (2SC), and three-flavor color-flavor locking (CFL) channels. They construct a mixed phase of nucleons and down quarks via a chemical equilibrium of the type n+n ↔ p+3d, analogous to the neutron dripline, and solve for mass gaps, pairing gaps, and the equation of state under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon dissociation sets in at about the saturation density, n_0, when the down-quark Fermi sea is populated (d-quark dripline).
- At about 3n_0, u-quarks appear forming a two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase.
- The hybrid star sequence has a maximum mass of 2.1 M_sun.
- Density profiles are shown for stars with masses 1.4 M_sun and 2.0 M_sun.

## Context
This work extends prior hybrid equation of state studies by Klahn et al. (2006) and Blaschke et al. (2007) that obtained stable hybrid stars with masses from 1.2 M_sun up to 2.1 M_sun, and it addresses the sequential deconfinement scenario that cannot be described in bag models. The new single-flavor CSL phase coexisting with nuclear matter at the crust-core boundary could have significant consequences for cooling behavior, r-mode stability, and the superburst phenomenon, and the authors suggest further investigations using these probing tools.
