# Structure of $^{128,129,130}$Xe through multi-reference energy density functional calculations

> Recently, values for the Kumar quadrupole deformation parameters of the nucleus $^{130}$Xe have been computed from the results of a Coulomb excitation experiment, indicating that this xenon isotope has a prominent triaxial ground state. Within a different context, it was recently argued that the analysis of particle correlations in the final states of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions performed at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) points to a similar structure for the adjacent isotope, $^{129}$Xe. In the present work, we report on state-of-the-art multi-reference energy density functional calculations that combine projection on proton and neutron number as well as angular momentum with shape mixing for the three isotopes $^{128,129,130}$Xe using the Skyrme-type pseudo-potential SLyMR1. Exploring the triaxial degree of freedom, we demonstrate that the ground states of all three isotopes display a very pronounced triaxial structure. Moreover, comparison with experimental results shows that the calculations reproduce fairly well the low-energy excitation spectrum of the two even-mass isotopes. By contrast, the calculation of $^{129}$Xe reveals some deficiencies of the effective interaction.

## Key claim

The ground states of all three xenon isotopes (128, 129, 130) display a very pronounced triaxial structure, but the calculation for 129Xe reveals some deficiencies of the effective interaction SLyMR1, particularly its very weak pairing strength and low isoscalar effective mass.

## Method

The authors performed multi-reference energy density functional (MR-EDF) calculations combining projection onto good proton number, neutron number, and angular momentum with shape mixing for 128,129,130Xe using the Skyrme-type pseudo-potential SLyMR1. Reference states were generated by solving Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations subject to constraints on quadrupole deformation parameters (beta, gamma) across an equally-spaced mesh in the first sextant of the beta-gamma plane, using a three-dimensional cartesian Lagrange mesh with 28 points in each direction and 0.8 fm spacing.

## Key numbers

- Minimum of the particle-number restored energy surface for 128Xe: beta = 0.20, gamma = 19 degrees
- Minimum of the angular-momentum and particle-number restored energy surface for 128Xe (Jpi = 0+): beta = 0.21, gamma = 18 degrees
- Calculated binding energy of 128Xe ground state: -1077.956 MeV (experimental: -1080.743 MeV)
- Calculated root-mean-square charge radius of 128Xe ground state: 4.738 fm (experimental: 4.7774(50) fm)
- Calculated B(E2: 2_1+ to 0_1+) for 128Xe: 1664 e^2 fm^4 (experimental: 1839(421) e^2 fm^4)
- Calculated dipole magnetic moment of 2_1+ state in 128Xe: +0.6 mu_N (experimental: +0.68(7) mu_N)
- Maximum of squared collective wave function for 128Xe 0_1+ state: beta = 0.19, gamma = 26 degrees
- Experimental Kumar deformation parameters for 130Xe: beta_k(0_1+) = 0.17(2), gamma_k(0_1+) = 23(5) degrees
- ATLAS Collaboration result for 129Xe ground state from heavy-ion collisions: beta approximately 0.20, gamma approximately 30 degrees

## Context

This work extends the MR-EDF approach previously applied by Bally et al. (2022) to interpret heavy-ion collision data, now providing detailed spectroscopic calculations for three xenon isotopes. The calculations are motivated by recent Coulomb excitation experiments on 130Xe by Morrison et al. (2020) that indicated prominent triaxial deformation, and by ATLAS Collaboration (2022) results from ultra-relativistic 129Xe+129Xe collisions at the LHC. The results enable quantitative comparison between nuclear structure deduced from low-energy Coulomb excitation and from high-energy heavy-ion collision measurements, while also testing the SLyMR1 effective interaction against experimental spectra.
