# Partial dynamical symmetry and the vibrational structure of Cd isotopes

> The recently reported deviations of selected non-yrast states in $^{110}$Cd from the expected spherical-vibrator behaviour, is addressed by means of an Hamiltonian with U(5) partial dynamical symmetry. The latter preserves the U(5) symmetry in a segment of the spectrum and breaks it in other states. The effect of intruder states is treated in the framework of the interacting boson model with configuration mixing.

## Key claim

The observed deviations from spherical-vibrator behavior in selected non-yrast states of \(^{110}\)Cd can be explained by a Hamiltonian with U(5) partial dynamical symmetry (PDS), which preserves the U(5) symmetry in a subset of states (Class A) while breaking it in other states (Classes B and C), without requiring strong mixing between normal and intruder configurations.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}_{\rm PDS}\) consisting of a U(5) dynamical symmetry (DS) term plus an interaction \(\hat{V}_0\) that annihilates states with \(n_d=\tau\) and \(n_{\Delta}=0\) (Class A), thereby preserving U(5) symmetry for those states while mixing others. Intruder states were treated in the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), using an SO(6)-type Hamiltonian in the \(N+2\) boson space and a mixing term between the normal (\(N=7\)) and intruder (\(N=9\)) boson spaces. The combined Hamiltonian was applied to \(^{110}\)Cd, and E2 transition rates were calculated using an E2 operator with separate effective charges for the two boson spaces.

## Key numbers

- U(5)-DS parameters for \(^{110}\)Cd: \(t_1 = 641.5\) keV, \(t_2 = 67.9\) keV, \(t_3 = -26.1\) keV, \(t_4 = 8.7\) keV, with boson number \(N=7\) and E2 effective charge \(e_B = 1.96\) (W.u.)\(^{1/2}\)
- U(5)-PDS-CM parameters: \(t_1 = 767.8\) keV, \(t_2 = -t_3 = 73.6\) keV, \(t_4 = 18.5\) keV, \(r_0 = 2.1\), \(e_0 = -6.9\), \(\kappa = -72.7\) keV, \(\Delta/2 = 4989.5\) keV, \(\alpha = -42.8\) keV, with \(N=7\) and E2 effective charges \(e_B^{(N)} = 1.96\) and \(e_B^{(N+2)} = 1.19\) (W.u.)\(^{1/2}\)
- Experimental upper limits for problematic transitions: \(B(E2;\,0^{+}_3\rightarrow 2^{+}_1) < 7.9\) W.u., \(B(E2;\,2^{+}_5\rightarrow 4^{+}_1) < 5\) W.u., \(B(E2;\,2^{+}_5\rightarrow 2^{+}_2) < 0.7^{+0.5}_{-0.6}\) W.u.
- Calculated PDS-CM values for these transitions: \(B(E2;\,0^{+}_3\rightarrow 2^{+}_1) = 0.25\) W.u., \(B(E2;\,2^{+}_5\rightarrow 4^{+}_1) = 0.19\) W.u., \(B(E2;\,2^{+}_5\rightarrow 2^{+}_2) = 0.12\) W.u.
- U(5)-DS predictions for comparison: 46.29, 11.02, and 19.84 W.u. respectively

## Context

This work addresses the "Cd problem" raised by Garrett (2008, 2012) and Batchelder (2014), who reported deviations from spherical-vibrator behavior in Cd isotopes (A=108-126) and argued for a "breakdown of vibrational motion." Previous attempts to explain these deviations through strong mixing between normal U(5)-like states and intruder SO(6)-like states were found unsatisfactory by those authors. The present study extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry, previously applied to SU(3) (Leviatan, 1996; Leviatan and Sinai, 1999) and SO(6) (Van Isacker, 1999; Leviatan and Van Isacker, 2002; Ramos, 2009) chains of the interacting boson model, to the U(5) chain. The approach enables a description where most low-lying normal states retain vibrational character while specific non-yrast states exhibit departures, and calculations are underway to extend this to other neutron-rich Cd isotopes.
