# A generalized Theory of Diffusion based on Kinetic Theory

> We propose to use spin hydrodynamics, a two-fluid model of spin propagation, as a generalization of the diffusion equation. We show that in the dense limit spin hydrodynamics reduces to Fick's law and the diffusion equation. In the opposite limit spin hydrodynamics is equivalent to a collisionless Boltzmann treatment of spin propagation. Spin hydrodynamics avoids unphysical effects that arise when the diffusion equation is used to describe to a strongly interacting gas with a dilute corona. We apply spin hydrodynamics to the problem of spin diffusion in a trapped atomic gas. We find that the observed spin relaxation rate in the high temperature limit [Sommer et al., Nature 472, 201 (2011)] is consistent with the diffusion constant predicted by kinetic theory.

## Key claim

The paper proposes "spin hydrodynamics" as a generalization of the diffusion equation that correctly interpolates between diffusive behavior in the dense limit and ballistic (collisionless) behavior in the dilute limit, thereby avoiding unphysical spin currents that arise when the standard diffusion equation is applied to trapped atomic gases with a dilute corona.

## Method

The authors derive a two-fluid model from moments of the Boltzmann equation with a relaxation-time (BGK) collision term, obtaining coupled continuity and momentum equations for spin-up and spin-down densities and velocities. They implement this spin hydrodynamics numerically using the piecewise parabolic method (PPM) on a Lagrangian grid, and compare solutions to the standard diffusion equation in both high-temperature (dilute) and low-temperature (dense) limits for harmonically trapped atomic gases.

## Key numbers

- In the high temperature limit, the variational bound on the dimensionless damping constant gives Γ_red(λ=0)=12.1, Γ_red(λ=0.4)=29.2, and Γ_red(λ→0)=λ^{-2}/log(0.13λ^{-2})
- The experimental spin drag coefficient from Sommer et al. (2011) is Γ_sd=0.16 E_F(0)(T_F/T)^{1/2}
- The theoretical prediction for the spin drag coefficient using kinetic theory is Γ_sd=1.81 E_F(0)/Γ_red(λ) (T_F/T)^{1/2}
- For a strongly deformed cloud with λ=0.1, Γ_red≈200, which differs from the experimental value Γ_red≈11.3 by more than an order of magnitude
- In the low temperature (dense) limit, the variational result gives Γ_red=2, independent of λ
- The kinetic theory diffusion constant for a dilute Fermi gas at unitarity is D=9π^{3/2}/(32√2 m) (T/T_F)^{3/2}
- For typical experimental parameters N=2×10^5 and λ=0.045, the dimensionless decay time is Γ^{-1}≈212 (E_F/E_0)^2
- The dimensionless relaxation time at the trap center is τ(0)=0.02 (E_0/E_F)^2 for these parameters

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bruun and Pethick (2011), who identified that the diffusion approximation breaks down in the dilute corona of trapped atomic gases and proposed a transverse cutoff method. The spin hydrodynamics approach is inspired by moment methods used in radiation hydrodynamics (Pons, 2000; O'Connor, 2015) and anisotropic fluid dynamics (Florkowski, 2010; Martinez, 2010; Bluhm, 2015). The method enables quantitatively accurate extraction of spin diffusion constants from experiments on unitary Fermi gases, such as those by Sommer et al. (2011), without requiring phenomenological cutoffs or boundary conditions.
