# Excited bands in odd-mass rare-earth nuclei

> Normal parity bands are studied in 157Gd, 163Dy and 169Tm using the pseudo SU(3) shell model. Energies and B(E2) transition strengths of states belonging to six low-lying rotational bands with the same parity in each nuclei are presented. The pseudo SU(3) basis includes states with pseudo-spin 0 and 1, and 1/2 and 3/2, for even and odd number of nucleons, respectively. States with pseudo-spin 1 and 3/2 must be included for a proper description of some excited bands.

# Excited bands in odd-mass rare-earth nuclei

## Key claim

The pseudo SU(3) shell model can quantitatively describe six to seven low-lying normal parity rotational bands in 157Gd, 163Dy, and 169Tm, but only when the Hilbert space is enlarged to include proton and neutron states with pseudo-spin 1 and 3/2 (not just the previously used pseudo-spin 0 and 1/2), and when realistic single-particle energies are employed.

## Method

The authors constructed a pseudo SU(3) shell model basis for odd-mass rare-earth nuclei, including proton and neutron irreps with the largest second-order Casimir operator eigenvalues and pseudo-spin values of 0 and 1 for even numbers of particles, and 1/2 and 3/2 for odd numbers. They diagonalized a Hamiltonian containing spherical Nilsson single-particle terms, quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, pairing interactions, and three rotor-like terms (a K_J^2, b J^2, and A_sym C_2), with the rotor-like parameters adjusted per nucleus while other strengths were fixed by systematics. Energies and B(E2) transition strengths were calculated for states belonging to six or seven rotational bands in each nucleus and compared with experimental data from the National Nuclear Data Center.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters: beta = 0.250 for 157Gd, 0.258 for 163Dy, 0.267 for 169Tm
- Occupation numbers: 157Gd has 8 normal-parity protons and 7 normal-parity neutrons; 163Dy has 10 and 9; 169Tm has 11 and 12
- Effective charges used: e_pi = 2.3, e_nu = 1.3
- Rotor-like parameters for 157Gd: a = 0.046, b = 0.0020, A_sym = 0.0008; for 163Dy: a = -0.040, b = 0.0040, A_sym = 0.0016; for 169Tm: a = 0.019, b = 0.0007, A_sym = 0
- Pseudo-spin content of band A in 157Gd: 89% S=1/2, 11% S=3/2; band D in 157Gd: 3% S=1/2, 97% S=3/2
- Pseudo-spin content of band A in 163Dy: 100% S=1/2, 0% S=3/2; band E in 163Dy: 0% S=1/2, 100% S=3/2
- Pseudo-spin content of band A in 169Tm: 93% S=1/2, 7% S=3/2; band C in 169Tm: 9% S=1/2, 91% S=3/2
- Inter-band B(E2) transition 3/2_C^- to 7/2_D^- in 157Gd: 77.1 x 10^-2 e^2 b^2
- Inter-band B(E2) transition 3/2_D^- to 7/2_C^- in 157Gd: 77.8 x 10^-2 e^2 b^2
- Inter-band B(E2) transition 3/2_B^- to 7/2_C^- in 163Dy: 97.9 x 10^-2 e^2 b^2
- Inter-band B(E2) transition 3/2_A^+ to 7/2_B^+ in 169Tm: 0.00168 x 10^-2 e^2 b^2 (experiment: 0.0006 +/- 0.0001)

## Context

This work extends previous pseudo SU(3) calculations by Vargas, Hirsch, and Draayer (2000, 2001) on odd-mass rare-earth nuclei, which had restricted the basis to pseudo-spin 0 and 1/2 states. It also builds on the even-even pseudo SU(3) studies of Beuschel, Popa, and Draayer (2000, 2001) on Gd, Dy, and Er isotopes. The inclusion of pseudo-spin 1 and 3/2 states enables description of excited bands that were inaccessible in earlier calculations, and complements the authors' companion study of M1 scissors-mode transitions in the same nuclei (Vargas, Hirsch, and Draayer, 2002). The results enable future work on magnetic properties of these rotational bands and extension to other rare-earth nuclei.
