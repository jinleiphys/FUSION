# Anomalous $ω$-$Z$-$γ$ Vertex from Hidden Local Symmetry

> We formulate the general form of omega-Z-gamma vertex in the framework based on the hidden local symmetry (HLS), which arises from the gauge invariant terms for intrinsic parity-odd (IP-odd) part of the effective action. Those terms are given as the homogeneous part of the general solution (having free parameters) to the Wess-Zumino (WZ) anomaly equation and hence are not determined by the anomaly, in sharp contrast to the Harvey-Hill-Hill (HHH) action where the relevant vertex is claimed to be uniquely determined by the anomaly. We show that, even in the framework that HHH was based on, the omega-Z-gamma vertex is actually not determined by the anomaly but by the homogeneous (anomaly-free) part of the general solution to the WZ anomaly equation having free parameters in the same way as in the HLS formulation: The HHH action is just a particular choice of the free parameters in the general solution. We further show that the omega-Z-gamma vertex related to the neutrino (nu) - nucleon (N) scattering cross section sigma(nu N -> nu N (N')gamma) is determined not by the anomaly but by the anomaly-free part of the general solution having free parameters. Nevertheless we find that the cross section sigma(nu N -> nu N(N')gamma) is related through the Ward-Takahashi identity to Gamma(omega -> pi^0 gamma) which has the same parameter-dependence as that of sigma(nu N -> nu N(N')gamma) and hence the ratio sigma(nu N -> nu N(N')gamma)/Gamma(omega -> pi^0 gamma) is fixed independently of these free parameters. Other set of the free parameters of the general solution can be fixed to make the best fit of the omega -> pi^0 l^+ l^- process, which substantially differs from the HHH action. This gives a prediction of the cross section sigma(nu N -> nu N (N')gamma^*(l^+ l^-)) to be tested at nu-N collision experiments in the future.

## Key claim

The omega-Z-gamma vertex is not determined by the anomaly, contrary to the claim of Harvey-Hill-Hill (HHH), but instead arises from the homogeneous (anomaly-free) part of the general solution to the Wess-Zumino anomaly equation, which contains free parameters; the HHH action represents only a particular choice of those free parameters.

## Method

The authors formulated the general form of the omega-Z-gamma vertex using the hidden local symmetry (HLS) framework, deriving it from the gauge-invariant intrinsic parity-odd terms in the effective action. They then constructed the general solution to the homogeneous Wess-Zumino anomaly equation in the HHH formulation, showing it consists of fourteen independent chiral-invariant terms with free parameters, and demonstrated that the HHH action corresponds to a specific choice of those parameters. The omega-Z-gamma vertex was related to the omega-pi0-gamma vertex through the Ward-Takahashi identity, and the cross section for neutrino-nucleon scattering was computed using experimental input for the omega to pi0 gamma decay.

## Key numbers

- The ratio sigma(nu N -> nu N gamma) / Gamma(omega -> pi0 gamma) is fixed independently of free parameters and equals (g_omega^2 / (10 pi^2)) * (2 m_omega / (m_omega^2 - m_pi0^2))^3 * (G_F^2 F_pi^2 E_nu^6 / m_omega^4)
- The predicted cross section sigma(nu N -> nu N gamma) = 3.0 x 10^(-41) cm^2 * (Gamma(omega -> pi0 gamma) / 0.70 MeV) * (g_omega / 13.4)^2 * (E_nu / GeV)^6
- The best fit of the omega-pi0 transition form factor yields chi^2/d.o.f = 4.3/13 = 0.33 with parameter bar-c = 0.74, compared to the rho meson dominance case with bar-c = 0 giving chi^2/d.o.f = 23/14 = 1.6
- The HHH action corresponds to the particular parameter choice a_10 + a_11 = 1 for the omega-Z-gamma vertex

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry framework of Bando, Kugo, and Yamawaki (1985) and Fujiwara, Kugo, and collaborators (1984) for incorporating vector mesons into chiral Lagrangians, and directly contradicts the claim of Harvey, Hill, and Hill (2007, 2008) that the omega-Z-gamma vertex is uniquely determined by the anomaly. The results enable predictions for neutrino-nucleon scattering cross sections involving photon or dilepton production that can be tested at future neutrino-nucleon collision experiments, including the MiniBooNE anomaly where electron-like events were observed in the energy range 200 MeV to 475 MeV.
