# Kaon properties and cross sections in nuclear medium

> Results for the $π+ N \to Λ, Σ+ K$ reactions in nuclear matter of Ref. nucl-th/0004011 are presented. To evaluate the in-medium modification of the reaction amplitude as a function of the baryonic density we introduce relativistic, mean-field potentials for the initial, final and intermediate mesonic and baryonic states in the resonance model. These vector and scalar potentials were calculated using the quark meson coupling model. Contrary to earlier work which has not allowed for the change of the cross section in medium, we find that the data for kaon production at SIS energies are consistent with a repulsive $K^+$-nucleus potential.

# Kaon properties and cross sections in nuclear medium

## Key claim
Contrary to earlier work that neglected in-medium changes to the cross section, the authors find that kaon production data at SIS energies are consistent with a repulsive K+-nucleus potential, provided one correctly accounts for the in-medium modification of the production amplitude (not just kinematic effects) for the reactions π + N → Λ, Σ + K.

## Method
The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to calculate relativistic mean-field scalar and vector potentials for the initial, final, and intermediate mesons and baryons in nuclear matter. They then applied the resonance model to compute the in-medium reaction amplitudes for π + N → Λ + K and π + N → Σ + K, incorporating the density-dependent modification of hadron masses and the four-momentum of each particle. The in-medium cross sections were averaged over a time-dependent density profile from dynamical simulations of Au + Au collisions at 2 AGeV and over the available invariant collision energy distribution.

## Key numbers
- The maximum downward shift of the π + N → Λ, Σ + K reaction threshold in nuclear matter is roughly 30 MeV, occurring at baryon densities around ρ_B ≈ 0.6 ρ_0 (ρ_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}).
- At baryon densities ρ_B > 0.2 fm^{-3}, thresholds for the π + N → Λ, Σ + K reactions are higher than those in free space.
- The average total kaon production cross section for central Au + Au collisions at 2 AGeV, using the in-medium calculation, is ⟨K⟩ = 65 μb, compared to ⟨K⟩ = 71 μb using the free space cross section.
- The density profile for Au + Au collisions at 2 AGeV and impact parameter b = 0 was parametrized with ρ_max = 3 ρ_0, t̄ = 13 fm, and Δt = 6.7 fm.

## Context
This work extends the resonance model of Tsushima et al. (previous work) by including in-medium modification of hadron properties in both kinematic factors and reaction amplitudes, using the QMC model of Guichon (1988). It addresses a contradiction noted by Senger et al., Li et al., and Bratkovskaya et al., where heavy ion calculations with a repulsive K+ potential substantially underestimated kaon production data. The results enable a consistent interpretation of K+ production at SIS energies while maintaining the theoretically expected repulsive K+-nucleus potential, and they suggest that earlier failures arose from omitting in-medium effects on the reaction amplitudes themselves.
