# Interaction of antiprotons with nuclei

> We report on self-consistent calculations of $\bar{p}$ quasi-bound states in selected nuclei performed within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model employing $\bar{p}$ coupling constants tuned to reproduce $\bar{p}$-atom data. We confirmed considerable polarization of the nuclear core induced by the antiproton. The $\bar{p}$ annihilation was treated dynamically, taking into account the reduced phase space in the nuclear medium as well as the compressed nuclear density. The energy available for the annihilation products was evaluated self-consistently, considering additional energy shift due to particle momenta in the ${\bar p}$-nucleus system. Next, we constructed the $\bar{p}$-nucleus optical potential using scattering amplitudes related to the latest version of the Paris $\bar{N}N$ potential. We explored energy dependence of the potential and the implications for $\bar{p}$-nucleus quasi-bound states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that self-consistent treatment of antiproton annihilation in the nuclear medium, including momentum-dependent energy shifts and reduced phase space, significantly suppresses the calculated widths of antiproton quasi-bound states compared to simpler two-body approximations, though the widths remain sizable.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations of antiproton quasi-bound states in selected nuclei, solving coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations with source terms from both nucleons and the antiproton. They employed two approaches for the antiproton-nucleus interaction: (1) a phenomenological RMF model with G-parity motivated coupling constants scaled by a factor ξ=0.2 to fit antiproton-atom data, with an imaginary absorptive potential; and (2) an optical potential constructed from S-wave in-medium scattering amplitudes derived from the latest version of the Paris \(\bar{N}N\) potential, including Pauli blocking effects via the multiple scattering approach of Wass et al.

## Key numbers

- Scaling factor ξ = 0.2 for antiproton-meson coupling constants, determined by fitting antiproton-atom data
- Parameter Im b₀ = 1.9 fm for the effective scattering length describing antiproton absorption at threshold
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ used for in-medium scattering amplitude calculations
- Nucleon kinetic energy approximation T_N = 23 MeV used in the density-dependent form of √s (Eq. K)
- Binding energies and widths of 1s antiproton states in selected nuclei calculated using TM1 and TM2 models, with widths showing strong dependence on the form of √s (largest for two-body frame M, significantly reduced for momentum-dependent forms Jc, Jr, and K)
- In ¹⁶O, the S-wave Paris potential yields larger 1s and 1p binding energies and widths compared to the phenomenological RMF approach

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of antiproton-nuclear bound states by Mishustin et al. and the authors' own prior work (Hrtánková et al.), incorporating fully self-consistent treatment of antiproton annihilation with momentum-dependent energy shifts. The calculations use the Paris \(\bar{N}N\) potential (Paris 09) for the first time in this context, building on earlier phenomenological optical potentials fitted to antiproton-atom data by Friedman et al. The results enable future experimental searches for antiproton-nuclear bound states at FAIR, though the authors note that P-wave contributions from the Paris potential remain to be included.
