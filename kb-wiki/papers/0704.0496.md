# Fusion process studied with preequilibrium giant dipole resonance in time dependent Hartree-Fock theory

> The equilibration of macroscopic degrees of freedom during the fusion of heavy nuclei, like the charge and the shape, are studied in the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock theory. The pre-equilibrium Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) is used to probe the fusion path. It is shown that such isovector collective state is excited in N/Z asymmetric fusion and to a less extent in mass asymmetric systems. The characteristics of this GDR are governed by the structure of the fused system in its preequilibrium phase, like its deformation, rotation and vibration. In particular, we show that a lowering of the pre-equilibrium GDR energy is expected as compared to the statistical one. Revisiting experimental data, we extract an evidence of this lowering for the first time. We also quantify the fusion-evaporation enhancement due to gamma-ray emission from the pre-equilibrium GDR. This cooling mechanism along the fusion path may be suitable to synthesize in the future super heavy elements using radioactive beams with strong N/Z asymmetries in the entrance channel.

# Fusion process studied with preequilibrium giant dipole resonance in time dependent Hartree-Fock theory

## Key claim

The preequilibrium giant dipole resonance (GDR) energy is lowered compared to the statistical GDR energy due to strong prolate deformation of the fused system during the equilibration phase, and this lowering is observed for the first time by revisiting experimental data. The effect is most pronounced in N/Z asymmetric fusion reactions, with a smaller contribution from mass asymmetry alone.

## Method

The authors performed Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the SLy4d Skyrme interaction to simulate fusion reactions, extracting the preequilibrium GDR properties from the time evolution of the dipole moment and its conjugate momentum. The gamma-ray spectrum was obtained from the Fourier transform of the charge acceleration, with a Gaussian filter (tau = 320 fm/c) applied to remove spurious effects from finite integration time and box reflections. Deformation parameters were extracted from the quadrupole and monopole moments, and the GDR energy was related to deformation via a macroscopic model.

## Key numbers

- Preequilibrium GDR energy in the reaction 12Be+28S → 40Ca at 1 MeV/nucleon center-of-mass energy: 11.64 MeV (from gamma-ray spectrum) and approximately 11.6 MeV (from oscillation period of 107 fm/c)
- GDR energy built on the spherical ground state of 40Ca: 15.5 MeV (oscillation period approximately 80 fm/c)
- Average deformation parameter during preequilibrium stage for 12Be+28S: epsilon_p approximately 0.13
- GDR energy in 32S: 23.0 MeV; in 8Be along its deformation axis: 17.2 MeV
- For the mass-symmetric reaction 20O+20Mg → 40Ca at 1.6 MeV/nucleon: two peaks in the gamma-ray spectrum at 7.7 MeV and 10.8 MeV, with average deformation epsilon approximately 0.2
- Filter width added by Gaussian procedure: Gamma approximately 0.6 MeV (from hbar/tau with tau = 320 fm/c)

## Context

This work extends the pioneering TDHF study of charge equilibration by Bonche and Ngô (1980) and earlier theoretical work on preequilibrium GDR by Chomaz et al. (1990s) and Baran et al. (2000s). The authors compare their results with experimental data from Flibotte et al. (1996) and others who reported first experimental indications of fast GDR gamma-ray emission in fusion reactions. The findings enable quantitative predictions for fusion-evaporation enhancement via preequilibrium gamma-ray emission, which may be relevant for synthesizing superheavy elements using radioactive beams with strong N/Z asymmetries in the entrance channel.
