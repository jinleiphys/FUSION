# Sensitive behavior of $2νββ$-decay amplitude within QRPA and broken SU(4) symmetry in nuclei

> Making use of an identity transformation independent of a nuclear model, we represent the {\bb}-amplitude as a sum of two terms. One term accounts for most of the sensitivity of the original {\bb}-amplitude to $g'_{pp}$ for realistic $g'_{pp}\simeq 1$ (with $g'_{pp}$ being the ratio of the triplet and singlet p-p interaction strengths) and is determined by a specific energy-weighted sum rule. The sum rule depends only on the particle-particle residual interaction (being linear function of $g'_{pp}$ in the QRPA) and passes through zero at the point $g'_{pp}=1$ where the Wigner SU(4) symmetry is restored in the p-p sector of the Hamiltonian. The second term in the decomposition of the {\bb}-amplitude is demonstrated within the QRPA to be a much smoother function for the realistic values of $g'_{pp}$ than the original {\bb}-amplitude. This term is mainly determined by the intensity of the spin-orbit interaction of the nuclear mean field. Thus, the analysis of the present work reveals the reasons for the sensitivity of the {\bb}-amplitude to different components of the nuclear Hamiltonian and thereby can help in constraining nuclear model uncertainties in calculations of the amplitude.

# Sensitive behavior of $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay amplitude within QRPA and broken SU(4) symmetry in nuclei

## Key claim

The sensitivity of the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay amplitude to the particle-particle interaction strength $g'_{pp}$ near the realistic value $g'_{pp}\simeq 1$ arises from the violation of Wigner SU(4) symmetry in the particle-particle sector of the nuclear Hamiltonian, not from shortcomings of the QRPA itself; the amplitude can be decomposed into a strongly $g'_{pp}$-dependent term governed by an energy-weighted sum rule and a smoother term dominated by the spin-orbit mean field.

## Method

The authors perform a model-independent identity transformation of the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay amplitude $M_{2\nu} = M_{2\nu}' + S/\omega_g^2$, separating a term proportional to an energy-weighted sum rule $S$ from a residual term $M_{2\nu}'$. Within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) using a Hamiltonian with a mean field, Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction, and separable particle-particle interaction, they derive an analytical expression for $S$ and compute $M_{2\nu}$, $S$, and $M_{2\nu}'$ for three nuclei ($^{76}$Ge, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te) as functions of $g'_{pp}$.

## Key numbers

- For $^{76}$Ge, $^{100}$Mo, and $^{130}$Te, the sum rule $S$ depends linearly on $g'_{pp}$ and passes through zero at $g'_{pp}=1$, where SU(4) symmetry is restored in the particle-particle sector.
- The analytical expression for $S$ is $S = 3(1-g'_{pp})\frac{\Delta_n\Delta_p}{G_0}$ (for equal proton and neutron pairing strengths), with $\Delta$ the pairing gap and $G_0$ the pairing strength.
- Realistic pairing strengths are $G_{0n}\approx G_{0p}\approx 12$ MeV$/A$ (set I), with an alternative set II using $G_{0n}=G_{0p}=18$ MeV$/A$.
- The spin-orbit mean field contribution to $M_{2\nu}'$ scales approximately quadratically with the spin-orbit strength; reducing the spin-orbit strength to 50% significantly changes $M_{2\nu}'$ for realistic $g'_{pp}$ near unity.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Rumyantsev and Urin (1998), who proposed a model-independent transformation but used a simplified independent-quasiparticle model that could not address QRPA sensitivity. It directly addresses criticism by Civitarese and Suhonen (2000) that the earlier formalism failed to describe double beta decay observables. The analysis reveals that the sensitivity of the $2\nu\beta\beta$ amplitude to $g'_{pp}$ (first noted by Vogel and Zirnbauer, 1986) has a natural physical origin in SU(4) symmetry breaking, and emphasizes the important but previously underappreciated role of the spin-orbit mean field. The sum rule $S$ can be calculated in other nuclear models (e.g., shell model) to test QRPA calculations.
