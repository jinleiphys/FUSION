# Excitation of the electric pygmy dipole resonance by inelastic electron scattering

> To complete earlier studies of the properties of the electric pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) obtained in various nuclear reactions, the excitation of the 1$^-$ states in $^{140}$Ce by $(e,e')$ scattering for momentum transfers $q=0.1-1.2$~fm$^{-1}$ is calculated within the plane-wave and distorted-wave Born approximations. The excited states of the nucleus are described within the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), but also within the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) by accounting for the coupling to complex configurations. It is demonstrated that the excitation mechanism of the PDR states in $(e,e')$ reactions is predominantly of transversal nature for scattering angles $θ_e \approx 90^o-180^o$. Being thus mediated by the convection and spin nuclear currents, the $(e,e')$ like the $(γ,γ')$ reaction, may provide additional information to the one obtained from Coulomb- and hadronic excitations of the PDR in $(p,p')$, $(α,α')$, and heavy-ion scattering reactions. The calculations predict that the $(e,e')$ cross sections for the strongest individual PDR states are in general about three orders of magnitude smaller as compared to the one of the lowest $2^+_1$ state for the studied kinematics, but that they may become dominant at extreme backward angles.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the excitation of the electric pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in $^{140}$Ce by inelastic electron scattering is predominantly mediated by transversal (current-driven) mechanisms for scattering angles $\theta_e \approx 90^\circ-180^\circ$, in contrast to the Coulomb-dominated excitation of the giant dipole resonance (GDR); however, the $(e,e')$ cross sections for the strongest individual PDR states are about three orders of magnitude smaller than those of the lowest $2^+_1$ state for most studied kinematics, becoming potentially dominant only at extreme backward angles.

## Method

The authors calculated $(e,e')$ differential cross sections for 42 one-phonon $1^-$ states in $^{140}$Ce using both plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) and distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), with incident electron energies from 30 to 120 MeV and scattering angles from $40^\circ$ to $180^\circ$ (momentum transfers $q=0.1-1.2$ fm$^{-1}$). Nuclear structure was described within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) for one-phonon states, and within the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) which accounts for coupling to complex two- and three-phonon configurations, yielding 1157 $1^-$ states below 8.5 MeV. The DWBA calculations employed a complex-plane rotation method to handle poor convergence of radial integrals at backward angles.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: $q = 0.1-1.2$ fm$^{-1}$ for the studied kinematics
- Incident electron energies considered: 30 to 120 MeV, with specific focus on 40, 70, and 110 MeV (corresponding to beam passes at the S-DALINAC)
- The $(e,e')$ cross sections for the strongest individual PDR states are about three orders of magnitude smaller than those of the lowest $2^+_1$ state (excitation energy 1.596 MeV) for most studied kinematics
- The $B(E1)$ values of the strongest one-phonon PDR states are almost two orders of magnitude smaller than those forming the GDR
- The strongest states after fragmentation (QPM with complex configurations) have $B(E1)$ values almost one order of magnitude smaller than the doorway one-phonon states
- The PDR excitation cross sections drop by one to three orders of magnitude when changing scattering angle from $40^\circ$ to $180^\circ$, depending on incident energy

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the PDR properties obtained from $(\gamma,\gamma')$ nuclear resonance fluorescence experiments (Heisenberg 1997, Govaert 1998), $(p,p')$ reactions, $(\alpha,\alpha'\gamma)$ reactions, and heavy-ion scattering by proposing inelastic electron scattering as a complementary probe. The calculations use the same QPM framework previously applied to $^{140}$Ce in comparisons with NRF, $(p,p')$, and $(\alpha,\alpha')$ data (Ponomarev 2019), and the results provide estimates for future experiments at the S-DALINAC facility in Darmstadt, which can provide low-energy electron beams and high-resolution magnetic spectrometers (LINTOTT and Q-CLAM) capable of detecting backward-scattered electrons close to $180^\circ$.
