# Time-dependent covariant density functional theory in 3D lattice space: benchmark calculation for 16O + 16O reaction

> Time-dependent covariant density functional theory with the successful density functional PCPK1 is developed in a three-dimensional coordinate space without any symmetry restrictions, and benchmark calculations for the 16O + 16O reaction are performed systematically. The relativistic kinematics, the conservation laws of the momentum, total energy, and particle number, as well as the time-reversal invariance are examined and confirmed to be satisfied numerically. Two primary applications including the dissipation dynamics and above-barrier fusion cross sections are illustrated. The obtained results are in good agreement with the ones given by the nonrelativistic time-dependent density functional theory and the data available. This demonstrates that the newly developed time-dependent covariant density functional theory could serve as an effective approach for the future studies of nuclear dynamical processes.

## Key claim

The newly developed time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TDCDFT) in a full 3D lattice space without symmetry restrictions, using the PC-PK1 density functional, reproduces the dissipation dynamics and above-barrier fusion cross sections for the 16O + 16O reaction in good agreement with nonrelativistic time-dependent density functional theory results and available experimental data, provided the adiabatic approximation and Taylor expansion parameters (time step 0.1 fm/c, expansion order 4) are carefully chosen to ensure conservation laws.

## Method

The time-dependent Dirac equation was solved in a 3D lattice space with mesh size 0.8 fm, using the predictor-corrector strategy with Taylor expansion of the exponential operator up to order 4 and time step 0.1 fm/c. Initial ground-state wavefunctions for 16O were obtained from static covariant density functional theory with the PC-PK1 functional, then boosted via Lorentz transformation to set the nuclei in motion. Head-on collisions and collisions with varying orbital angular momenta were simulated for the 16O + 16O system, with the fusion probability determined by the Hill-Wheeler formula using a Fermi function with decay constant 0.4 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of a boosted 16O nucleus coincides with relativistic kinetic energy M/(1-v^2)^(1/2) - M; nonrelativistic kinetic energy Mv^2/2 deviates dramatically for velocities above 0.3c.
- Relative momentum deviation for a boosted 16O with collective kinetic energy 50 MeV is as small as 10^(-5) with time step 0.1 fm/c and Taylor order 4.
- Relative total energy deviation for 16O + 16O head-on collision at center-of-mass energy 50 MeV is around 10^(-4) with time step 0.1 fm/c and Taylor order 4.
- Relative particle number deviation is around 10^(-7) at 1000 fm/c with time step 0.1 fm/c and Taylor order 4.
- Percentage of energy dissipation for 16O + 16O head-on collisions: 84.5% at E_c.m. = 90 MeV, 70.9% at E_c.m. = 130 MeV, and 54.2% at E_c.m. = 170 MeV.
- The decay constant in the Hill-Wheeler formula for fusion probability is chosen as 0.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent covariant density functional theory framework previously applied to linear-chain cluster states (Ren 2020) to a systematic benchmark of the 16O + 16O reaction, a classic test case for time-dependent Hartree-Fock methods dating back to Koonin (1976) and Flocard (1978). The results address the "fusion window anomaly" originally resolved by including spin-orbit interactions in nonrelativistic calculations (Umar 1986, Reinhard 1988), and demonstrate that the covariant approach automatically incorporates both time-even and time-odd spin-orbit fields. This enables future studies of nuclear dynamical processes where relativistic effects and time-odd fields are important, such as heavy-ion collisions and fission.
