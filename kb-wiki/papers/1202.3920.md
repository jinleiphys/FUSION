# Multistep shell model in the complex energy plane

> We have adopted the multistep shell model in the complex energy plane to study nuclear excitations occurring in the continuum part of the spectrum. In this method one proceeds by solving the shell model equations in a successive manner. That is, in each step one constructs the building blocks to be used in future steps. We applied this formalism to analyze the unbound nuclei $^{12,13}$Li starting from the one-particle states in $^{10}$Li and two-particle states in $^{11}$Li. In the former case the excitations correspond to the motion of three particles partitioned as the product of a one-particle and two-particle systems. The ground state of $^{12}$Li is thus calculated to be an antibound (virtual) state. In the four-particle system $^{13}$Li the states can be constructed as the coupling of two correlated pairs. We found that there is no bound or antibound state in $^{13}$Li.

# Multistep shell model in the complex energy plane

## Key claim
The multistep shell model in the complex energy plane (CXMSM) can describe unbound nuclei by constructing many-particle states from successive couplings of one- and two-particle building blocks, but the method's predictive power is limited by the strong dependence of the four-particle results on the chosen single-particle energy of the $0p_{1/2}$ state, and the authors note that continuum background states omitted from the basis may influence the spectrum.

## Method
The authors extended the multistep shell model (MSM) to the complex energy plane using the Berggren representation, which includes bound, antibound, and Gamow (resonance) states plus discretized scattering states along a complex contour. They solved the shell-model equations successively: first constructing single-particle states in $^{10}$Li, then two-particle states in $^{11}$Li using a separable pairing interaction, then three-particle states in $^{12}$Li as products of one- and two-particle states, and finally four-particle states in $^{13}$Li as couplings of two correlated two-particle states. The overcomplete and non-orthogonal MSM basis was handled by computing the overlap matrix and transforming to an orthonormal set via the Schmidt procedure.

## Key numbers
- The $0p_{1/2}$ single-particle resonance in $^{10}$Li lies at (0.195, -0.047) MeV (energy, width) and the $0d_{5/2}$ resonance at (2.731, -0.545) MeV.
- The $1s_{1/2}$ state in $^{10}$Li appears as an antibound (virtual) state.
- The two-particle ground state of $^{11}$Li has energy $W(0^+_1) = -0.295$ MeV (bound); the $2^+_1$ resonance lies at (2.300, -0.372) MeV.
- In $^{12}$Li, the ground state is an antibound state; predicted resonances include a $1/2^-$ state at about 1 MeV with width about 800 keV and a $5/2^+$ state at about 1.1 MeV with width about 500 keV.
- In $^{13}$Li, the lowest calculated state for $\epsilon_{p_{1/2}} = (0.195, -0.047)$ MeV is a $2^+$ resonance at (0.715, -0.114) MeV; for $\epsilon_{p_{1/2}} = (0.563, -0.252)$ MeV the lowest state is a $0^+$ resonance at (1.505, -0.041) MeV.
- No bound or antibound state was found in $^{13}$Li.

## Context
This work extends the complex-energy shell model (CXSM) of Id Betan et al. (2002) and the multistep shell model of Liotta et al. (1982) and Blomqvist et al. (1984) to treat many-valence-nucleon systems in the continuum. The formalism is applied to the neutron-rich lithium isotopes $^{12,13}$Li, which are unbound systems at the limit of experimental observation. The results for $^{12}$Li are compared to experimental data from Aksyutina et al. (2008), Patronis et al. (2010), and Hall et al. (2010), and to standard shell-model calculations in a harmonic oscillator basis by Qi et al. (2008). The finding that $^{13}$Li has no bound or antibound state agrees with experiment (Aksyutina 2008). The work enables future studies of unstable multi-nucleon systems where continuum effects are essential, though the authors note that including more two-particle continuum background states may be needed for higher precision.
