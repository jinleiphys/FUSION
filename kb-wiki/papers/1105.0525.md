# Study of participant-spectator matter, thermalization and other related phenomena for neutron-rich colliding pair

> We study the participant-spectator matter, density and temperature reached in heavy-ion reactions of neutron-rich systems having N/Z varying from 1.0 to 2.0 at 50 and 250 MeV/nucleon. The N/Z dependence of these quantities is also investigated. Our results show a weak dependence on the N/Z ratio of the system on these quantities. We also shed light on the role of N/Z ratio on the thermalization achieved in a reaction. We find similar weak N/Z dependence on thermalization also.

## Key claim

The participant-spectator matter, density, temperature, and thermalization achieved in heavy-ion reactions of neutron-rich systems show a weak or almost independent dependence on the N/Z ratio of the colliding system, contrary to what might be expected from the increasing role of symmetry energy in more neutron-rich systems.

## Method

The study uses the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of Ca+Ca and Xe+Xe series with N/Z ratios from 1.0 to 2.0 in steps of 0.2 at incident energies of 50 and 250 MeV/nucleon and at impact parameters b/b_max = 0.2-0.4. Participant matter is defined as nucleons that have experienced at least one collision, and spectator matter as the remainder. Density and temperature are extracted within a central sphere of 2 fm radius using a local density approximation and a hot Thomas-Fermi approach, while thermalization is studied via the anisotropy ratio and relative momentum of the two colliding Fermi spheres.

## Key numbers

* For Ca+Ca reactions at 50 MeV/nucleon, the average density reached is about 1.5 times the normal nuclear matter density.
* The maximal value of density decreases with increasing neutron content, attributed to the repulsive symmetry energy pushing matter away from the central dense zone.
* At 250 MeV/nucleon, much higher densities are achieved than at 50 MeV/nucleon due to the more violent nature of the reaction.
* Higher temperatures are achieved in reactions at 250 MeV/nucleon compared to 50 MeV/nucleon.
* For low incident energy (50 MeV/nucleon), temperature remains almost constant for lower neutron content and shows a slight increase for higher neutron content.
* Including momentum-dependent interactions (SMD) decreases the average temperature for all N/Z ratios; reducing the symmetry energy to zero causes a very slight decrease in average temperature.
* The anisotropy ratio <R_a> increases and saturates after the high-density phase, with very little effect from the N/Z ratio.
* The relative momentum <K_R> decreases as the reaction proceeds, with very little effect from neutron content, and the effect diminishes further at higher incident energy.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Puri and collaborators on participant-spectator matter, density, and temperature, which were restricted to systems near the stability line and at the energy of vanishing flow. The present paper investigates these quantities for neutron-rich systems far from the stability line, using the IQMD model which has been validated against data for 58Ni+58Ni and 58Fe+58Fe by Gautam et al. The results enable further exploration of the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections for exotic nuclei accessible at radioactive ion beam facilities.
