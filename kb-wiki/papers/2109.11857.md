# Properties of neutron star crust with improved nuclear physics: Impact of chiral EFT interactions and experimental nuclear masses

> A compressible liquid-drop approach adjusted to uniform matter many-body calculations based on chiral EFT interactions and to the experimental nuclear masses is used to investigate the neutron star crust properties. Eight chiral EFT hamiltonians and a representative phenomenological force (SLy4) are confronted. We show that some properties of the crust, e.g. clusters mass, charge, and asymmetry, are mostly determined by symmetric matter properties close to saturation density and are therefore mainly constrained by experimental nuclear masses, while other properties, e.g., energy per particle, pressure, sound speed, are mostly influenced by low-density predictions in neutron matter, where chiral EFT and phenomenological forces substantially differ.

## Key claim

The paper shows that neutron star crust properties are split into two categories: cluster mass, charge, and asymmetry are mostly determined by symmetric matter properties near saturation density and constrained by experimental nuclear masses, while energy per particle, pressure, and sound speed are mostly influenced by low-density neutron matter predictions where chiral EFT and phenomenological forces differ substantially.

## Method

A compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) with bulk terms fixed by a nuclear meta-model and finite-size terms containing Coulomb, surface, and curvature contributions was adjusted to uniform matter many-body calculations from eight chiral EFT Hamiltonians and the phenomenological SLy4 force. The model was further constrained by fitting to experimental nuclear masses from the AME2016 database, which allowed selection of the best-performing Hamiltonians (H1-H4, with H2-H3 being optimal). Crust properties were then computed under beta-equilibrium conditions within Wigner-Seitz cells.

## Key numbers

- SLy4 predicts up to 30% larger energy per particle compared to the chiral EFT group for Fermi momenta 0.1 < k_FB < 0.9 fm^-1, with largest differences at k_FB approximately 0.4 fm^-1
- The volume fraction u predicted by SLy4 is larger than the chiral EFT group by about 20-30%
- Saturation density n_sat is approximately 0.16 fm^-3 (corresponding to rho_sat approximately 2.6 x 10^14 g cm^-3)
- The odd-even mass staggering correction used was a_oem = -12 MeV with A^-1/2 scaling
- For k_FB < 0.9 fm^-1, dispersion among chiral EFT predictions is less than or about a few percent
- The energy per particle from SLy4 force differs by about 10-30% from the chiral EFT Hamiltonians group for k_F < 1.0 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the compressible liquid-drop model approach of Carreau (2019) and the nuclear meta-model of Margueron (2018), building on earlier CLDM developments by Bethe, Baym, and Pethick (1971) and Douchin and Haensel (2001). It systematically combines chiral EFT predictions from Drischler (2016, 2021) with experimental nuclear mass constraints, following suggestions by Steiner (2008) to fine-tune finite-size terms and by Tews (2016) to adjust the meta-model to chiral EFT predictions. The study enables narrower predictions for neutron star crust properties by reducing uncertainties through the combination of chiral EFT advances and experimental nuclear mass data, and identifies which crust observables could serve as probes of neutron matter predictions.
