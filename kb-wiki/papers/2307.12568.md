# Thermal conductivity of evolving quark-gluon plasma in the presence of a time-varying magnetic field

> The effect of the temperature evolution of QGP on its thermal conductivity and elliptic flow is investigated here in the presence of a time-varying magnetic field. Thermal conductivity plays a vital role in the cooling rate of the medium or its temperature evolution. The magnetic field produced during the early stages of (non-central) heavy-ion collisions decays with time, where electrical conductivity plays a significant role. As the medium expands, the electrical and thermal properties change, reflecting the effect in various observables. In this study, we have calculated the thermal conductivity of the QGP medium, incorporating the effects of temperature and magnetic field evolution. We discovered that conductivity significantly depends on the cooling rate, and its value increases due to temperature evolution. Furthermore, the influence of these evolutions on the elliptic flow coefficient is measured, and elliptic flow decreases due to the evolution. We also extend our study for the case of Gubser flow, where, along with the longitudinal Bjorken expansion, the radially transverse expansion is also present.

# Thermal conductivity of evolving quark-gluon plasma in the presence of a time-varying magnetic field

## Key claim

The thermal conductivity of quark-gluon plasma increases by approximately 10–20% when the temperature evolution (cooling) of the medium is taken into account, compared to the static picture where cooling is neglected. This enhancement is sensitive to the specific cooling rate used (ideal hydrodynamics, ideal magnetohydrodynamics, or first-order dissipative hydrodynamics), and the effect is largest for slower cooling rates.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation (RTA) in the Landau-Lifshitz frame, using a quasiparticle model with thermal masses to incorporate the lattice QCD equation of state. They compute the thermal conductivity tensor components for a time-varying magnetic field of the form B = B₀ exp(−τ/τ_B), considering three cooling rate scenarios: ideal hydrodynamics (T ∝ τ^{−1/3}), ideal magnetohydrodynamics (including magnetic field effects on cooling), and first-order dissipative hydrodynamics. The heat current is extended to include terms proportional to ∇(dT/dτ) arising from temperature evolution, and the elliptic flow coefficient v₂ is evaluated using the resulting conductivities.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T₀ = 0.35 GeV and formation time τ₀ = 0.5 fm used for all cooling rate calculations.
- Initial magnetic field strength eB₀ = 9 m_π², with decay parameters τ_B = 3 fm and τ_B = 7 fm considered.
- Thermal conductivity enhancement of 10–20% in the evolving picture compared to the static picture across all three cases.
- Strong coupling constant α_s = 0.5 and number of quark flavors N_f = 3 used throughout.
- Baryon chemical potential μ_B = 0.3 GeV used for numerical results shown.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of thermal conductivity in quark-gluon plasma that assumed a static medium (Gavin 1985, PhysRevD.106.034008, PhysRevD.108.094007) by incorporating the effect of temperature evolution due to hydrodynamic expansion. It also builds on studies of time-varying magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions (Tuchin 2013, PhysRevD.90.034018, Hongo 2013) and their effect on transport coefficients. The results enable more realistic modeling of dissipative hydrodynamics for QGP evolution, particularly for elliptic flow calculations, and demonstrate that the cooling rate must be self-consistently included when computing thermal transport in evolving heavy-ion collision systems.
