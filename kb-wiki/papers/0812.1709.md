# Many Body Theory for Quartets, Trions, and Pairs in Low Density Multi-Component Fermi-Systems

> A selfconsistent many body approach for the description of gases with quartets, trions, and pairs is presented. Applications to 3D Fermi systems at low density are discussed.

## Key claim

A selfconsistent many-body approach for describing gases of quartets, trions, and pairs in three-dimensional multi-component Fermi systems is presented, but the treatment of quartets relies on a projected product ansatz that simplifies the full four-body gap equation, and the results for alpha-particle condensation are about 25 percent higher than earlier calculations due to a different alpha-particle radius and variational wave function.

## Method

The authors derive a quartet gap equation analogous to the pairing case, using a four-fermion order parameter with single-particle occupation numbers and a four-particle T-matrix in the one-pole approximation. For strongly bound quartets like the alpha particle, they linearize the problem by replacing occupation numbers with Fermi-Dirac distributions and adopt a projected product ansatz (mean-field 0S-wave function) for the quartet wave function, inserting it into the gap equation to obtain a Hartree-Fock-type equation solved for critical temperatures. For trions, they propose an equation-of-motion method using a three-particle creation operator and energy-weighted sum rules, with a THSR-type ansatz for the trion wave function in homogeneous infinite matter. For pairs, they extend the Nozieres Schmitt-Rink approach by deriving two coupled mean-field equations, one fermionic (Bogoliubov transformation) and one bosonic (Bogoliubov transformation for fermion pairs), to account for the mean field created by composite pairs in the strong coupling limit.

## Key numbers

- Alpha-particle binding energy: -28 MeV, radius: 1.71 fm (used to adjust interaction parameters lambda = -991 MeV fm^3 and k0 = 1.43 fm^{-1})
- Deuteron binding energy: -2.2 MeV, radius: 1.95 fm (used to adjust interaction parameters lambda = -1305 MeV fm^3 and k0 = 1.46 fm^{-1})
- Critical temperature for alpha condensation is about 25 percent higher than in an earlier publication (Ropke et al.), where the underlying alpha-particle radius was 2 fm (larger than the experimental value)
- Hoyle state in ^12C at 7.65 MeV is a product state of three alpha particles in identical 0S orbit to over 70 percent
- The 6th 0+ state at 15.1 MeV in ^16O is predicted to be a four-alpha-particle condensate state
- ^6He has an excited state at about 12 MeV interpreted as mainly consisting of two tritons

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for cluster formation in Fermi systems beyond the well-studied two-body pairing case (e.g., BEC-BCS crossover studied by Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink (1985) and in nuclear matter by Ropke et al.) to include quartets (alpha particles) and trions in 3D. The alpha-particle condensation approach builds on earlier work by Ropke et al. and uses the THSR wave function developed by Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Ropke (2001) for finite nuclei like the Hoyle state. The trion treatment is motivated by recent cold-atom experiments trapping three different fermions (Huck et al.) and by exact 1D model solutions (Lecheminant et al.), while the pair formalism provides a fully selfconsistent extension of the Nozieres Schmitt-Rink approach that includes the mean field of composite bosons. The methods enable future studies of cluster condensation in nuclear systems (e.g., alpha condensates in ^16O, ^20Ne) and in cold-atom systems with three or four fermion species.
