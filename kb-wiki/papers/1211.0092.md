# Resonance parameters of the rho-meson from asymmetrical lattices

> We present a lattice QCD calculation of the parameters of the $ρ$ meson decay. The study is carried out on spatially asymmetric boxes using nHYP-smeared clover fermions with two mass-degenerate quark flavors. Our calculations are carried out at a pion mass $m_π=304(2)$ MeV on the set of lattices $V=24^2\times η24\times48$ with $η=1.0,1.25$, and 2.0 with lattice spacing $a=0.1255(7)$ fm. The resonance mass $m_ρ=827(3)(5)$ MeV and coupling constant $g_{ρππ}=6.67(42)$ are calculate using the P-wave scattering phase shifts. We construct a 2$\times$2 correlation matrix to extract the energy of the scattering states and compute the phase shifts using the finite volume formula. By varying the degree of asymmetry, we are able to compute a set of phase shifts that are evenly distributed throughout the spectral region where the $ρ$ decays.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using spatially asymmetric lattices (with one elongated direction) provides finer control over two-pion momenta than the alternative method of varying total momentum, enabling a well-distributed set of phase shifts across the resonance region with only three lattice volumes, at a pion mass of 304(2) MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with nHYP-smeared clover fermions and two mass-degenerate quark flavors on three ensembles of 300 gauge configurations each, with volumes V = 24² × η24 × 48 for η = 1.0, 1.25, and 2.0. They constructed a 2×2 correlation matrix using a two-pion interpolator and a rho-meson interpolator, extracted ground and first excited state energies via the variational method, and computed P-wave scattering phase shifts using the Lüscher method extended to asymmetrical lattices. The resonance mass and coupling constant were obtained by fitting the phase shifts to the effective range formula.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π = 304(2) MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1255(7) fm
- Resonance mass: m_ρ = 827(3)(5) MeV (first uncertainty statistical, second from lattice spacing determination)
- Coupling constant: g_{ρππ} = 6.67(42)
- Decay width: Γ = 76.6(20)(5) MeV
- Physical-pion-mass width computed from the coupling: Γ_ph = 184(23) MeV
- Ground state energies (aE_0): 0.516(10) for η=1.0, 0.511(4) for η=1.25, 0.442(4) for η=2.0
- First excited state energies (aE_1): 0.660(6) for η=1.0, 0.606(10) for η=1.25, 0.549(12) for η=2.0
- Phase shifts δ_1(E_0): 1.71(11) rad for η=1.0, 0.822(65) rad for η=1.25, 0.242(31) rad for η=2.0
- Phase shifts δ_1(E_1): 2.99(10) rad for η=1.0, 2.64(13) rad for η=1.25, 2.04(18) rad for η=2.0

## Context

This work extends the Lüscher method for asymmetrical lattices (Li 2003, Feng 2004) to the rho resonance, offering an alternative to the non-zero total momentum approach used by Feng (2010), Prelovsek (2011), and Aoki (2011). The results agree well with one-loop unitarized chiral perturbation theory predictions (Pelaez 2010) and with other lattice studies, though the authors note that Lang (2011) obtained significantly different results, likely due to insufficient volume. The method enables finer momentum tuning for mapping narrow resonances and can be extended to other resonances and to studies of finite-volume effects.
