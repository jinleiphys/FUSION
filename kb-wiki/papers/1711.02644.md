# Gravitational-wave constraints on the neutron-star-matter Equation of State

> The LIGO/Virgo detection of gravitational waves originating from a neutron-star merger, GW170817, has recently provided new stringent limits on the tidal deformabilities of the stars involved in the collision. Combining this measurement with the existence of two-solar-mass stars, we generate a generic family of neutron-star-matter Equations of State (EoSs) that interpolate between state-of-the-art theoretical results at low and high baryon density. Comparing the results to ones obtained without the tidal-deformability constraint, we witness a dramatic reduction in the family of allowed EoSs. Based on our analysis, we conclude that the maximal radius of a 1.4-solar-mass neutron star is 13.6 km, and that smallest allowed tidal deformability of a similar-mass star is $Λ(1.4 M_\odot) = 120$.

## Key claim

The maximal radius of a 1.4-solar-mass neutron star is 13.6 km, and the smallest allowed tidal deformability of a similar-mass star is Λ(1.4 M⊙) = 120, under the combined constraints from the GW170817 gravitational-wave detection and the existence of two-solar-mass stars.

## Method

The authors constructed a generic family of neutron-star-matter equations of state (EoSs) by interpolating between a chiral effective theory (CET) EoS at low densities (below nuclear saturation density n_s ≈ 0.16 baryons/fm³) and a perturbative QCD (pQCD) result at high densities (μ_B = 2.6 GeV, corresponding to ~40 n_s). The interpolation used polytropic segments (either three or four segments) with randomly sampled polytropic indices γ_i ∈ [0,15] and matching chemical potentials, generating ensembles of 90,000 tritropic and 170,000 quadrutropic EoSs. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and computed tidal deformabilities for non-rotating stars, applying the 2 M⊙ mass constraint and the LIGO/Virgo 90% upper limit Λ(1.4 M⊙) < 800.

## Key numbers

- Maximal radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: R(1.4 M⊙) < 13.6 km (from the Λ(1.4 M⊙) < 800 constraint)
- Minimal radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: R(1.4 M⊙) > 9.9 km (from the 2 M⊙ constraint)
- Smallest allowed tidal deformability for a 1.4 M⊙ star: Λ(1.4 M⊙) = 120 (from combined constraints)
- Range of tidal deformabilities for EoSs supporting a 2 M⊙ star: Λ(1.4 M⊙) ∈ [120, 1504]
- For soft low-density EoSs: Λ(1.4 M⊙) ∈ [120, 1353]; for hard low-density EoSs: Λ(1.4 M⊙) ∈ [161, 1504]
- First polytropic index range after combined constraints: 0.6 < γ₁ < 6.7 (for quadrutropic EoSs with first segment lasting to n ≥ 1.5 n_s)
- Empirical relation between radius and tidal deformability: Λ(R) = 2.88 × 10⁻⁶ (R/km)⁷·⁵

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kurkela et al. (2014) and Fraga et al. (2015), who constructed EoS families interpolating between CET and pQCD without gravitational-wave constraints, finding a ±40% uncertainty in the EoS at intermediate densities. The new LIGO/Virgo measurement of GW170817 (The LIGO Scientific Collaboration, 2017) provides an upper bound on tidal deformability that complements the 2 M⊙ constraint from Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013). The results enable future studies of whether astrophysical constraints can determine the existence of quark matter in neutron-star cores, and can be combined with direct radius measurements from X-ray observations.
