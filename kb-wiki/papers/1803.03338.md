# Probing the density dependence of the symmetry energy by nucleon flow

> In the framework of the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, sensitive regions of some nucleon observables to the nuclear symmetry energy are studied. It is found that the symmetry energy sensitive observable n/p ratio in the $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn reaction at 0.3 GeV/nucleon in fact just probes the density-dependent symmetry energy below the density of $1.5ρ_0$ and effectively probes the density-dependent symmetry energy around or somewhat below the saturation density. Nucleon elliptic flow can probe the symmetry energy from the low-density region to the high-density region when changing the incident beam energies from 0.3 to 0.6 GeV/nucleon in the semi-central $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn reaction. And nucleon transverse and elliptic flows in the semi-central $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au reaction at 0.6 GeV/nucleon are more sensitive to the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy. One thus concludes that nucleon observables in the heavy reaction system and with higher incident beam energy are more suitable to be used to probe the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy. The present study may help one to get more specific information about the density-dependent symmetry energy from nucleon flow observable in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies.

# Probing the density dependence of the symmetry energy by nucleon flow

## Key claim

The n/p ratio in the $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn reaction at 0.3 GeV/nucleon probes the symmetry energy only below $1.5\rho_0$ and effectively around or below saturation density, not at high densities as one might assume from the maximum compression density reached. Nucleon elliptic and transverse flows in heavier systems at higher beam energies (0.6 GeV/nucleon) are more sensitive to the high-density behavior, but even the $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au reaction at 0.6 GeV/nucleon cannot probe the symmetry energy above $1.5\rho_0$; the observable instead mainly probes the region $\rho_0$ to $1.5\rho_0$.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with a Skyrme-type parametrization (compressibility K = 230 MeV). To identify which density regions each observable is sensitive to, they artificially switched on the symmetry energy (taken as $E_{sym}=32(\rho/\rho_0)^{\gamma=2}$) in successive density intervals: $0-0.5\rho_0$, $0-\rho_0$, $0-1.5\rho_0$, and $0-2\rho_0$, and compared the resulting observables to the case with no symmetry energy. They computed free n/p ratios, neutron and proton elliptic flows $v_2$, and the difference between neutron and proton transverse flows for $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn at 0.3 and 0.6 GeV/nucleon and for $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at 0.6 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state compressibility coefficient of nuclear matter used in the model is K = 230 MeV.
- The symmetry energy form used is $E_{sym}=32(\rho/\rho_0)^{\gamma=2}$.
- The n/p ratio in $^{132}$Sn+$124$Sn at 0.3 GeV/nucleon probes the symmetry energy below $1.5\rho_0$ and effectively around or below saturation density $\rho_0$.
- Neutron elliptic flow in $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn at 0.3 GeV/nucleon mainly probes the density region $0.5\rho_0-\rho_0$.
- Changing the beam energy from 0.3 to 0.6 GeV/nucleon in $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn allows nucleon elliptic flow to probe from low density up to the $\rho_0-1.5\rho_0$ region.
- The difference between neutron and proton elliptic flows in $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn at 0.6 GeV/nucleon still cannot probe the symmetry energy in the region $1.5\rho_0-2\rho_0$.
- The difference between neutron transverse flow and proton transverse flow in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at 0.6 GeV/nucleon mainly probes the density region $\rho_0-1.5\rho_0$ and shows some sensitivity to the $1.5\rho_0-2\rho_0$ region.
- For Au+Au reactions at 0.4 GeV/nucleon, prior work (FOPI collaboration, 2016) found the maximum sensitivity of the neutron/proton elliptic flow ratio lies in the 1.4-1.5$\rho_0$ region.

## Context

This work extends the quantitative approach of the FOPI collaboration (2016), who similarly studied the density regimes probed by elliptic flow ratios in Au+Au reactions at 0.4 GeV/nucleon. The present study systematically compares multiple observables (n/p ratio, elliptic flow, transverse flow) across different beam energies and system sizes to map which density regions each observable actually constrains. The results show that nucleon flow observables in heavy-ion collisions cannot effectively probe the symmetry energy above $1.5\rho_0$; to constrain the high-density symmetry energy, one must first constrain the symmetry energy below $1.5\rho_0$. The authors note that short-range correlations (studied in prior work by other groups) may affect the magnitude of sensitivity but not the relative density regions probed. The momentum dependence of the symmetry potential at different densities (raised by Di Toro and collaborators) is identified as a remaining question requiring further study.
