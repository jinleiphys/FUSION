# Effects of neutrino magnetic moment and charge radius constraints and medium modifications of the nucleon form factors on the neutrino mean free path in dense matter

> Effects of neutrino charge radius and magnetic moment constraints obtained from the astrophysical observations and reactor experiments, as well as in-medium modifications of the weak and electromagnetic nucleon form factors of the matter on the neutrino electroweak interaction with dense matter, are estimated. We use a relativistic mean-field model for the in-medium effective nucleon mass and quark-meson coupling model for nucleon form factors. We analyze the neutrino scattering cross section and mean free path in cold nuclear matter. We find that the increase of the cross section relative to that without neutrino form factors results in the decrease of the neutrino mean free path when neutrino form factors and the in-medium modifications of the nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors are simultaneously considered. The quenching of the neutrino mean free path is evaluated to be about 12-58\% for the values of $μ_ν= 3 \times 10^{-12} μ_B$ and $R_ν= 3.5 \times 10^{-5}~\textrm{MeV}^{-1}$ compared with that obtained for the $μ_ν=0$ and $R_ν=0$. The decrease of the neutrino mean free path is expected to decelerate the cooling of neutron stars. Each contribution of the neutrino form factors to the neutrino mean free path is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper finds that including both neutrino electromagnetic form factors (magnetic moment and charge radius) and in-medium modifications of nucleon form factors simultaneously reduces the neutrino mean free path in dense matter by 12-58% for the specific values of $\mu_\nu = 3 \times 10^{-12} \mu_B$ and $R_\nu = 3.5 \times 10^{-5}~\textrm{MeV}^{-1}$, with the caveat that the neutrino charge radius contribution is more significant than the magnetic moment contribution at the astrophysically constrained magnetic moment value.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model to describe nuclear matter and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to calculate in-medium modifications of nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors. They computed neutrino differential cross sections and mean free paths in cold nuclear matter at zero temperature using a linear response approximation for neutrino scattering with protons, neutrons, electrons, and muons, incorporating both neutrino magnetic moment and charge radius constraints from astrophysical observations and reactor experiments.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino mean free path quenching of 12-58% for $\mu_\nu = 3 \times 10^{-12} \mu_B$ and $R_\nu = 3.5 \times 10^{-5}~\textrm{MeV}^{-1}$ compared with $\mu_\nu = 0$ and $R_\nu = 0$
- For $\mu_\nu = 5 \times 10^{-10} \mu_B$ and $R_\nu = 3.5 \times 10^{-5}~\textrm{MeV}^{-1}$, the NMFP reduction is 15-67% compared to $\mu_\nu = 0$ and $R_\nu = 0$
- The contribution of $\mu_\nu = 3 \times 10^{-12} \mu_B$ alone (with $R_\nu = 0$) to NMFP reduction is only 1-2% compared to $\mu_\nu = 0$ and $R_\nu = 0$
- In-medium modifications of nucleon form factors increase NMFP by 9-38% when $\mu_\nu = 0$ and $R_\nu = 0$
- The effective nucleon mass $M_N^*$ is approximately 0.78 $M_N$ at normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.15~\textrm{fm}^{-3}$
- Calculations performed at $|\bm{q}| = 2.5~\textrm{MeV}$ and $E_\nu = 5~\textrm{MeV}$, typical for neutron star cooling phase

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Hutauruk et al. (2018, 2019) that examined in-medium nucleon form factor effects without neutrino form factors, and by Sulaksono et al. (2006) that studied neutrino form factors without in-medium nucleon modifications. The results suggest that the combined effects of neutrino electromagnetic properties and medium-modified nucleon structure could decelerate neutron star cooling by reducing the neutrino mean free path, making neutron stars less transparent to neutrinos. The paper also compares its predictions with proto-neutron star radii from Raduta (2021) and finds that for the largest neutrino magnetic moment considered ($5 \times 10^{-10} \mu_B$), the NMFP becomes comparable to the PNS radius.
