# Rapidity Spectra Analysis in Terms of Non Extensive Statistic Approach

> We provide description of rapidity spectra of particles produced in p-anti-p collisions using anomalous diffusion approach to account for their non-equilibrium character. In particular, we exhibit connection between multiproduction processes and anomalous diffusion described through the nonlinear Focker-Planck equation with nonlinearity given by the nonextensivity parameter q describing the underlying Tsallis q-statistics and demonstrate how it leads to the Feynman scaling violation in these collisions. The q parameter obtained this way turns out to be closely connected to parameter 1/k converting the original poissonian multiplicity distribution to its observed Negative Binomial form. The inelasticity of reaction has been also calculated and found to slightly decrease with the increasing energy of reaction. Keywords: inelasticity, rapidity spectra, nonextensivity

# Rapidity Spectra Analysis in Terms of Non Extensive Statistic Approach

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that rapidity spectra of particles produced in proton-antiproton collisions can be described using a nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation whose nonlinearity parameter q (from Tsallis statistics) is closely related to the parameter 1/k that converts a Poissonian multiplicity distribution into the observed Negative Binomial form, with the inelasticity of the reaction found to slightly decrease with increasing energy.

## Method

The authors model particle production as an anomalous diffusion process in rapidity space, solving a nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation with drift coefficient proportional to sinh(y) and diffusion coefficient determined by the nonextensivity parameter q. They fit the resulting power-law rapidity distribution formula (with parameters T, y_m, and q, each having logarithmic energy dependence) to experimental data from UA5, Tevatron, P238, and UA7 collaborations at center-of-mass energies from 53 to 1800 GeV. The inelasticity K is then computed by integrating the fitted rapidity spectra weighted by transverse mass.

## Key numbers

- The fitted parameters for the logarithmic energy dependence of T, y_m, and q are: a1 = -0.178 ± 0.099, b1 = 0.267 ± 0.016; a2 = 0.451 ± 0.129, b2 = 0.239 ± 0.023; a3 = 0.91 ± 0.012, b3 = 0.072 ± 0.002 (units: T in GeV, y_m and q dimensionless, sqrt(s) in GeV)
- The ratio y_m/Y_max changes only weakly with energy, varying from 0.265 at sqrt(s) = 53 GeV to 0.285 at sqrt(s) = 1800 GeV
- The transverse mass m_T is taken as fixed at 0.4 GeV for the rapidity fits, with an energy-dependent interpolation m_T = 0.3 + 0.044 ln(sqrt(s)/20) used for inelasticity calculations

## Context

This work extends the diffusion model approach to multiparticle production developed by Wolschin (1999) and applied to RHIC data by Biyajima et al., by incorporating anomalous diffusion through the nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation corresponding to Tsallis q-statistics. The approach builds on earlier work by Lavagno (2002) for nuclear collisions and connects the nonextensivity parameter q to fluctuations in mean multiplicity, showing that q-1 follows the same energy dependence as the Negative Binomial parameter 1/k. The decreasing inelasticity with energy confirms previous findings by the same authors, and the successful simultaneous fit to data from different rapidity regions (P238 and UA7 at 630 GeV) enables more reliable extrapolations to LHC energies.
