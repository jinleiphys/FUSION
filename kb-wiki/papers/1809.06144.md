# Microscopic self-consistent description of induced fission dynamics: finite temperature effects

> The dynamics of induced fission of $^{226}$Th is investigated in a theoretical framework based on the finite-temperature time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) in the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA). The thermodynamical collective potential and inertia tensor at temperatures in the interval $T=0 - 1.25$ MeV are calculated using the self-consistent multidimensionally constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) model, based on the energy density functional DD-PC1. Pairing correlations are treated in the BCS approximation with a separable pairing force of finite range. Constrained RMF+BCS calculations are carried out in the collective space of axially symmetric quadrupole and octupole deformations for the asymmetric fissioning nucleus $^{226}$Th. The collective Hamiltonian is determined by the temperature-dependent free energy surface and perturbative cranking inertia tensor, and the TDGCM+GOA is used to propagate the initial collective state in time. The resulting charge and mass fragment distributions are analyzed as functions of the internal excitation energy. The model can qualitatively reproduce the empirical triple-humped structure of the fission charge and mass distributions already at $T=0$, but the precise experimental position of the asymmetric peaks and the symmetric-fission yield can only be accurately reproduced when the potential and inertia tensor of the collective Hamiltonian are determined at finite temperature, in this particular case between $T=0.75$ MeV and $T=1$ MeV.

## Key claim

The finite-temperature time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) can qualitatively reproduce the triple-humped fission fragment distributions of 226Th even at zero temperature, but the precise experimental positions of the asymmetric peaks and the symmetric-fission yield are only accurately reproduced when the collective potential and inertia tensor are determined at finite temperatures between 0.75 MeV and 1.0 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed constrained relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) calculations using the DD-PC1 energy density functional in the two-dimensional collective space of axially symmetric quadrupole (β20) and octupole (β30) deformations for 226Th. The Helmholtz free energy surfaces and perturbative cranking inertia tensors were computed at temperatures from 0 to 1.25 MeV, and the TDGCM in the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA) was used to propagate the initial collective state in time using the FELIX code version 2.0.

## Key numbers

- Fission barrier heights at T=0 MeV: 5.22 MeV (inner), 6.32 MeV (second), and 5.16 MeV (outer) for 226Th
- Internal excitation energies at T=0.5, 0.75, 1.0, and 1.25 MeV: 2.58, 8.71, 16.56, and 27.12 MeV respectively
- At T=0.75 MeV, asymmetric charge yield peaks shift to Z=36 and Z=54, matching experimental values from Schmidt (2000)
- At T=0.0 and 0.5 MeV, asymmetric mass peaks located at A=83 and A=143; at T=0.75 MeV they shift to A=89 and A=137
- Critical temperature for pairing phase transition in 226Th: approximately 0.75 MeV
- Time step for TDGCM evolution: 5×10⁻⁴ zs; total evolution time: 100 zs (2×10⁵ time steps)

## Context

This work extends the TDGCM+GOA framework previously applied by Tao (2017) using relativistic functionals, and by Goutte (2005), Regnier (2016, 2018), and Zdeb (2017) using non-relativistic Skyrme and Gogny functionals, by incorporating finite-temperature effects for the first time in a microscopic description of induced fission dynamics. The study enables systematic investigation of how internal excitation energy influences fragment distributions, and shows that the experimental charge yields from Schmidt (2000) for photoinduced fission of 226Th (with photon energies 8-14 MeV) lie between the predictions at internal excitation energies of 8.71 and 16.56 MeV.
