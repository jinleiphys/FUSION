# Hyperons in a relativistic mean-field approach to asymmetric nuclear matter

> Relativistic mean-field theory with $δ$ meson, nonlinear isoscalar self-interactions and isoscalar-isovector cross interaction terms with parametrizations obtained to reproduce Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations for nuclear matter is used to study asymmetric nuclear matter properties in $β$-equilibrium, including hyperon degrees of freedom and (hidden) strange mesons. Influence of cross interaction on composition of hyperon matter and electron chemical potential is examined. Softening of nuclear equation of state by the cross interactions results in lowering of hyperonization, although simultaneously enhancing a hyperon-induced decrease of the electron chemical potential, thus indicating further shift of a kaon condensate occurence to higher densities.

## Key claim

The inclusion of vector cross interaction (VCI) terms in the relativistic mean-field model softens the nuclear equation of state, which lowers the total hyperonization of matter while simultaneously enhancing the hyperon-induced decrease of the electron chemical potential, thereby shifting the onset of kaon condensation to higher densities.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field theory with isoscalar self-interactions, isovector scalar delta meson, and isoscalar-isovector cross interaction terms, with parametrizations fitted to reproduce Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations for nuclear matter. They solved the mean-field equations for beta-equilibrium matter including protons, neutrons, Lambda and Sigma- hyperons, electrons, muons, and hidden strange mesons (sigma* and phi), with hyperon-meson couplings derived from SU(6) quark model and adjusted to reproduce hypernuclear potential depths.

## Key numbers

- Lambda hyperon potential depth in nuclear matter: U_Lambda^(N) = -28 MeV; Sigma- potential depth: U_Sigma^(N) = +20 MeV (repulsive)
- Lambda hyperon potential in Lambda-hyperonic matter: U_Lambda^(Lambda) = -5 MeV (weak attraction)
- For MA/MB parametrizations: Lambda onset at 0.46 fm^-3 (with VCI) and 0.45 fm^-3 (without VCI); Sigma- onset at 0.48 fm^-3 (with VCI) and 0.50 fm^-3 (without VCI)
- For LA/LB parametrizations: total hyperonization at rho_B = 0.6 fm^-3 is 22.4% (with VCI) and 25.1% (without VCI)
- For HA/HB parametrizations at rho_B = 0.6 fm^-3: muon fraction 0.69% (HA with VCI) vs 4.85% (HB without VCI); electron fraction 2.19% (HA) vs 7.28% (HB)
- For HA/HB parametrizations: total hyperonization at rho_B = 0.6 fm^-3 is 29.8% (with VCI) vs 40.7% (without VCI) when delta meson included
- Muon onset density: 0.14 fm^-3 (MA/LA with VCI) and 0.16 fm^-3 (MB/LB without VCI)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of delta meson and vector cross interaction in asymmetric nuclear matter (Bunta and Gmuca, 2003) to include hyperon degrees of freedom. It builds on DBHF calculations by Machleidt et al. (1992), Lee et al. (1997), and Huber et al. (1995), and addresses the role of hyperons in neutron star interiors as suggested by earlier work (Glendenning, 1985; Weber and Weigel, 1989; Schaffner et al., 1996). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star composition and the conditions for kaon condensation, showing that vector cross interaction is an indispensable physical degree of freedom for describing beta-equilibrium matter.
