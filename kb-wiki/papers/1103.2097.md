# Primakoff production of $π^0$, $η$ and $η'$ in the Coulomb field of a nucleus

> Photoproduction of neutral pseudoscalar mesons $π^0,η(547)$ and $η'(958)$ in the Coulomb field of an atomic nucleus is studied using a model which describes the Primakoff and nuclear parts of the production amplitude. At high energies the nuclear background is dominated by the exchange of $C$-parity odd Regge trajectories. In the coherent production the isospin filtering makes the $ω(782)$ a dominant trajectory. The calculations are in agreement with $π^0$ data from JLAB provided the photon shadowing and final state interactions of mesons are taken into account. The kinematic conditions which allow to study the Primakoff effect in $η$ and $η'$ photoproduction off nuclei are further discussed. We also give predictions for the higher energies available at the JLAB upgrade.

## Key claim

The authors show that a unified VDM-Regge model can describe both the Primakoff (Coulomb) and strong nuclear amplitudes for coherent photoproduction of π⁰, η, and η' off nuclei without introducing free parameters for relative strengths and phases, provided that photon shadowing and final-state interactions are included. The extraction of η and η' radiative decay widths via the Primakoff effect is feasible only under specific kinematic conditions where isospin filtering suppresses the dominant ρ-exchange background.

## Method

The authors construct a model for high-energy coherent photoproduction of neutral pseudoscalar mesons off nuclei using vector meson dominance (VDM) for the anomalous γγ*→P and γV→P vertices, combined with Reggeized ω- and ρ-exchange amplitudes for the strong nuclear background. The elementary photoproduction amplitudes off nucleons are first fitted to existing data, then extended to nuclear targets using a field-theoretical formalism that includes initial-state photon shadowing and final-state interactions of the outgoing mesons via a profile-function approach. The nuclear coherent amplitude for ω-exchange is proportional to the mass number A, while ρ-exchange is suppressed by isospin filtering (proportional to 2Z−A).

## Key numbers

* Γ_{π⁰→γγ} = (7.82 ± 0.14) eV measured via Primakoff effect (PRIMEX), consistent with the anomaly-based value of 7.73 eV from the model using mixing angle ϑ ≈ −16°.
* Γ_{η→γγ} = (0.51 ± 0.026) keV from e⁺e⁻ collisions (PDG), compared to the Cornell Primakoff measurement of (0.32 ± 0.046) keV; the model predicts Γ_{η→γγ} = 0.52 keV using ϑ ≈ −16°.
* Γ_{η'→γγ} = (4.28 ± 0.19) keV from e⁺e⁻ collisions (PDG), compared to the model value of 5.48 keV using ϑ ≈ −16°.
* The ωNN coupling used is G_ω = 19, with Regge trajectory parameters α_ω⁰ = 0.4 and α_ω' = 0.85 GeV⁻²; the ρNN coupling is G_ρ ≈ g/2 with g ≈ 6 from ρ⁰→π⁺π⁻ decay.
* The ρ-Regge trajectory parameters are α_ρ⁰ = 0.53 and α_ρ' = 0.85 GeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the Primakoff effect from the original formulation by Primakoff (1951) and the Glauber-based approaches of Morpurgo (1964) and Fäldt (1972), as well as the more recent model by Gevorkyan et al. (2009) which required target-mass-dependent free parameters to match PRIMEX data. The authors' VDM-Regge framework provides a parameter-free description of both Coulomb and strong amplitudes, enabling predictions for η and η' Primakoff production at JLAB energies (up to 12 GeV). This enables future high-precision measurements of η→γγ and η'→γγ decay widths, planned at JLAB (etaGasp experiment), by identifying kinematic regions where isospin filtering suppresses the nuclear background.
