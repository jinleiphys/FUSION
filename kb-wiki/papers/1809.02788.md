# Augmenting the Gauge-Gravity Correspondence to include Hadron Polarizabilities

> ADS/CFT models have achieved considerable success in describing hadronic properties such as masses and Regge trajectories. Even if the minimal vertex that couples photons to structureless spin-zero fields is used, one still ends up with electromagnetic form factors of hadrons that are in fair to good agreement with experiment. However, contradicting both experiment and intuition, this minimal model gives zero for hadronic electric and magnetic polarizabilities. We show here that if effective vertices are used, and axial and vector mesons are allowed to propagate as intermediate states, then the static polarizabilities can in principle be computed from ADS/CFT.

## Key claim

The minimal AdS/CFT model that successfully describes hadron masses and electromagnetic form factors yields exactly zero for hadronic electric and magnetic polarizabilities, contradicting experiment. The paper shows that by introducing effective vertices and allowing axial and vector mesons to propagate as intermediate states, the static polarizabilities can in principle be computed from AdS/CFT.

## Method

The author supplements the standard 5D bulk action for a scalar field minimally coupled to the electromagnetic field with additional actions for charged axial vector fields (Eq. SA) and charged vector meson fields (Eq. SV), using effective vertices consistent with Lorentz and discrete symmetries. The Compton scattering amplitude is then computed via the GKP-Witten relation, extracting the non-Born contributions that yield the polarizabilities from the spectral decompositions of the axial and vector meson propagators.

## Key numbers

- The electric polarizability alpha_E and magnetic polarizability beta_M are related to the scalar functions b1(0) and b2(0) by: alpha_E = -(1/2m) b1(0) - (m/2) b2(0) and beta_M = (1/2m) b1(0) (Eqs. EP, MP).
- The axial meson contribution to b2(0) is given by b2(0) = e^2 g_A^2 sum_n C_n D_n / (-m_0^2 + M_An^2) (Eq. MA).
- The vector meson contribution to b1(0) is given by b1(0) = -e^2 m^2 g_V^2 sum_n E_n F_n / (-m_0^2 + M_Vn^2) (Eq. b1).
- The scalar wavefunctions asymptotically behave as Phi_n(z) ~ z^a with a = 1/2 + sqrt(4 + mu_S^2 R^2); axial vector wavefunctions have Psi_n(z) ~ z^b with b = 1/2 + sqrt(1 + mu_A^2 R^2).

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up AdS/CFT approach to QCD reviewed by Brodsky et al. and by Kim and Yi, which successfully describes hadron masses and form factors but fails for polarizabilities. The paper directly addresses the zero polarizability result found by Marquet, Roiesnel, and Wallon (2009) in an arbitrary background dilaton field, and the earlier zero result by Gao and Xiao in a hard-wall model. The proposed extension enables future numerical computation of pion polarizabilities once the dilaton profile and parameters mu_S, mu_A, mu_V are specified, using measured decay widths of the A1 and rho mesons to fix the couplings g_A and g_V.
