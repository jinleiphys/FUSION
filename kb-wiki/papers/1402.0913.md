# Theory Summary

> In this review, I show a personal overview of theoretical results shown in the International Conference on the Initial Stages in High-Energy Nuclear Collision, in Illa da Toxa, Galicia, Spain, Sept.~8-14, 2013.

## Key claim

This conference summary argues that the physics of high-energy nuclear collisions has become more sophisticated, with hydrodynamic descriptions now potentially applicable even in small systems like p-p and p/d-A collisions, but this remains a personal overview and readers should consult the original conference slides for complete details.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical results presented at the International Conference on the Initial Stages in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions (IS2013), covering 25 plenary and 33 parallel theory talks. The review highlights topics including p/d-A collisions, isotropization and thermalization, fluctuations, and recent developments in hydrodynamics and transport theory, with a focus on how initial conditions connect to final observables.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow parameter v2 is almost proportional to initial eccentricity of the profile just after collisions, with the discrepancy between data and model calculations coming from larger initial eccentricity from the CGC model than from the Glauber model
- In classical Yang-Mills simulations with CGC initial conditions, longitudinal pressure PL ~ 0.7 PT at approximately 0.4 fm/c, and the system exhibits hydrodynamic behavior even for small coupling alpha_s ~ 10^{-2}
- In viscous hydrodynamic calculations with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.16, final elliptic flow parameter v2 is strongly correlated with initial eccentricity epsilon_2
- A scaling relation epsilon_2/epsilon_3^k = constant is found with k ~ 0.5 for RHIC data and k ~ 0.6 for LHC data
- The difference between including and omitting non-linear terms in second-order hydrodynamics equations is manifested at large eta/s > 0.2

## Context

This review extends the discussion of initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions that began with the discovery of perfect fluidity in 2005, where ideal hydrodynamic calculations with Glauber-type initial conditions reproduced elliptic flow data, while CGC initial conditions did not (Hirano 2005). The work enables further investigation of whether hydrodynamic frameworks can be applied to smaller systems like p-p and p/d-A collisions, and highlights the importance of event-by-event fluctuations in initial conditions, which became prominent after the third-order deformation parameter resolved the ridge and Mach cone problems simultaneously (Alver 2010).
