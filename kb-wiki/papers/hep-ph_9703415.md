# Inelastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering on nuclei and ``strangeness'' of the nucleon

> Possibilities to extract information on the strange form factors of the nucleon from neutrino (antineutrino) inelastic scattering on nuclei, in an energy range from 200 MeV to 1 GeV and more, are investigated in detail. All calculations are performed within two relativistic independent particle models (Fermi gas and shell model); the final state interactions of the ejected nucleon are taken into account through relativistic optical model potentials. We have shown that the values of the cross sections significantly depend on the nuclear model (especially in the lower energy range). However the NC/CC neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry in a medium--high energy range shows a rather small dependence on the model and allows to disentangle different values of the parameters that characterize the strange form factors. We have calculated also the ratio of the cross sections for inelastic NC scattering of neutrinos on nuclei, with the emission of a proton and of a neutron. Our calculations show that this ratio depends rather weakly on the nuclear model and confirm previous conclusions on the rather strong dependence of this ratio upon the axial strange form factors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while absolute cross sections for inelastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering on nuclei depend significantly on the nuclear model (Fermi gas versus shell model), the neutral-current to charged-current neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry at medium-to-high energies (above approximately 500 MeV) shows only a weak dependence on the nuclear model, making it a robust observable for extracting the strange axial and magnetic form factors of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors calculated inelastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering cross sections on nuclei for both neutral-current (NC) and charged-current (CC) processes, using two relativistic independent particle models: a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) and a relativistic shell model (RSM). Final-state interactions of the ejected nucleon were included through relativistic optical model potentials, and Coulomb corrections for the charged lepton in CC processes were applied using an effective momentum approximation. The cross sections were computed for incident neutrino energies from 200 MeV to 1 GeV and above, focusing on the asymmetry between NC neutrino and antineutrino cross sections normalized to CC cross sections, as well as the ratio of proton-emission to neutron-emission NC cross sections.

## Key numbers

* The strange axial constant from deep inelastic scattering data is \(g_A^s = -0.10 \pm 0.03\), as cited from Ellis (no year given in text).
* The effect of strange form factors on cross sections is estimated to be at most of order \(10 \div 15\%\).
* The relativistic Fermi gas model uses a Fermi momentum \(p_F\) and a constant binding energy \(\epsilon_B\) for nucleons inside the Fermi sea, with the struck nucleon on-shell (\(p_0 = \sqrt{\vec{p}^2 + M^2}\)).
* The relativistic shell model uses bound-state wave functions from a self-consistent Hartree procedure with a linear Lagrangian including \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) mesons, with parameters adjusted to reproduce nuclear matter properties and the rms radius of \(^{40}\)Ca.
* The Coulomb correction for charged leptons uses the effective momentum \(\vec{k}'_{\text{eff}} = \vec{k}'\left(1 \pm \frac{3}{2}\frac{Z\alpha}{R|\vec{k}'|}\right)\), where \(R \simeq 1.2 A^{1/3}\) and the plus/minus sign refers to leptons/antileptons.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Horowitz and collaborators (no year given) on neutrino-nucleus scattering within the RFG and by Singh and Oset (no year given) who found RPA corrections to be large only at low momentum transfers. The RSM used here had been previously tested against precise data on coincidence elastic electron-nucleus scattering (references eep1, eep2 in the text). The paper enables future neutrino experiments using complex nuclear targets to extract the strange axial form factor \(F_A^s\) and strange magnetic form factor \(G_M^s\) from measured asymmetries, provided the nuclear model uncertainties are controlled through the use of cross-section ratios rather than absolute cross sections.
