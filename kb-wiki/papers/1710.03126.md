# Hubbard-Stratonovich-like Transformations for Few-Body Interactions

> Through the development of many-body methodology and algorithms, it has become possible to describe quantum systems composed of a large number of particles with great accuracy. Essential to all these methods is the application of auxiliary fields via the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. This transformation effectively reduces two-body interactions to interactions of one particle with the auxiliary field, thereby improving the computational scaling of the respective algorithms. The relevance of collective phenomena and interactions grows with the number of particles. For many theories, e.g. Chiral Perturbation Theory, the inclusion of three-body forces has become essential in order to further increase the accuracy on the many-body level. In this proceeding, the analytical framework for establishing a Hubbard-Stratonovich-like transformation, which allows for the systematic and controlled inclusion of contact three- and more-body interactions, is presented.

# Hubbard-Stratonovich-like Transformations for Few-Body Interactions

## Key claim
The authors present an analytical framework that generalizes the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to systematically include contact three- and more-body interactions through a single auxiliary field, but the transformation generates an infinite tower of induced N-fermion couplings that must be controlled by requiring the magnitude of the auxiliary-field interaction coefficients to be smaller than one.

## Method
The authors generalize the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation by introducing a single auxiliary field distributed according to a probability distribution P_N(phi) proportional to exp(-phi^(2N)), with the fermion density operator rho coupled to the auxiliary field through a polynomial sum of terms c_j phi^j rho. They expand the resulting integral in powers of the density operator and match coefficients order by order to the desired N-body fermion forces lambda^(k), using Faà di Bruno's formula to relate the auxiliary-field coefficients c_j to the induced fermion interactions. The method is tested on a two-site model with up to three fermion species, where the transfer matrix is computed stochastically by sampling the auxiliary field and compared to exact analytical results.

## Key numbers
- The probability distribution for the auxiliary field is P_N(phi) = N / Gamma(1/(2N)) * exp(-phi^(2N)), normalized such that the integral equals 1 when all c_j = 0.
- For the two-site model, two-body force strengths lambda^(2) are set to -1.74, 0.0, and +1.74 (attractive, absent, repulsive) and three-body force strengths lambda^(3) are set to -0.245, 0.0, and +0.245.
- The stochastic computations used 2 x 10^8 measurements for each system.
- For the N=2 distribution, the constant gamma = Gamma(3/4)/Gamma(1/4) is approximately 0.34.

## Context
This work extends the original Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation (Stratonovich 1957, Hubbard 1959), which linearizes two-body interactions, to include three- and higher-body contact interactions relevant for chiral perturbation theory and nuclear lattice effective field theory. The formalism enables non-perturbative inclusion of three-body forces in lattice simulations, with potential applications to systems near the Efimov threshold and studies of halo nuclei. The authors note that certain combinations of interaction coefficients suffer from large statistical fluctuations related to the Hamburger truncated moment problem (Adamyan 2003, Chen 2004), and they plan to investigate signal-to-noise behavior and the sign problem in forthcoming work.
