# Quantum theory of fermion production after inflation

> We show that quantum effects dramatically enhance the production of fermions following preheating after inflation in the early Universe in the presence of high excitations of bosonic quanta. As a consequence fermions rapidly approach a quasistationary distribution with a thermal occupancy in the infrared, while the inflaton enters a turbulent scaling regime. The failure of standard semiclassical descriptions based on the Dirac equation with a homogeneous background field is caused by nonperturbatively high boson occupation numbers. During preheating the inflaton occupation number increases, thus leading to a dynamical mechanism for the enhanced production of fermions from the rescattering of the inflaton quanta. We comment on related phenomena in heavy-ion collisions for the production of quark matter fields from highly occupied gauge bosons.

## Key claim
Quantum corrections dramatically enhance fermion production after inflation beyond what semiclassical (leading-order) descriptions predict, because nonperturbatively high boson occupation numbers cause the standard Dirac-equation approach to fail; the enhancement scales with the ratio ξ = g²/λ, so for physically realistic parameters with ξ ≳ 1 the effect is even more dramatic than the numerical results shown for ξ = 0.1.

## Method
The authors solve the quantum field theory of a Yukawa-coupled inflaton and massless fermions using two complementary nonperturbative methods: (1) a two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action truncated at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the Yukawa coupling g, which includes self-consistently dressed fermion and inflaton propagators; (2) lattice simulations that treat fermions exactly via "low-cost" fermion algorithms while treating the inflaton dynamics as classical-statistical. Both approaches are applied to a linear sigma model with two fermion flavors (N_f = 2) and O(4) symmetry, without cosmic expansion, for parametric resonance in a chaotic inflation scenario.

## Key numbers
- Fermion occupation number at low momenta reaches the thermal value n_eq(0) = 1/2 for massless fermions at time t = 250/σ₀M₀ for ξ = g²/λ = 0.1.
- The inflaton field amplitude φ(t) starts to decrease substantially at time t_φ ≃ (1/γ₀) ln(φ₀/σ₀M₀); for realistic hierarchies φ₀/M₀ ∼ 10⁶ and σ₀ ∼ O(1), this time is of the same order as for the numerical parameters used.
- The fermion production rate after t_φ scales proportional to ξ = g²/λ, verified numerically for small ξ by varying g.
- The inflaton occupation number at time t = 250/σ₀M₀ approaches a nonthermal power-law distribution ∼ 1/|p|⁴ characteristic for strong turbulence.
- The inflaton quartic coupling used in the numerical simulations is λ = 0.1, with the inflaton mass an order of magnitude smaller than the inflaton field value.

## Context
This work extends the semiclassical treatment of fermion production during preheating (Baacke 1998) by including quantum corrections via 2PI effective action techniques (Berges 2002) and lattice simulations (Aarts 1998). It contradicts the standard leading-order (LO) Dirac-equation results, which show only periodic resonant excitations, by demonstrating that NLO quantum corrections produce a quasistationary thermal distribution in the infrared while the inflaton enters a turbulent scaling regime (Berges 2008). The results enable quantitative predictions for fermion production in early-universe cosmology, including possible production of very heavy fermions relevant for dark matter and ultrahigh energy cosmic rays, and suggest analogous dramatic consequences for quark production in heavy-ion collisions where gluon occupation numbers are ∼ 1/α_s.
