# A Method to Constrain Preferential Emission and Spectator Dynamics in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Longitudinal particle production in heavy-ion collisions is influenced both by preferential emission from participating nucleons and by the breakup of spectator matter, yet quantifying these effects experimentally remains challenging. We introduce a Pearson correlation between spectator and charged-particle forward-backward asymmetries as an experimental probe of these phenomena. Using Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV simulated with A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, we validate that this correlator provides a robust, pseudorapidity-differential measure of the influence of preferential emission on the longitudinal structure of particle production. We further demonstrate that the correlation strength is sensitive to fluctuations in spectator number, which in experiments arise from evaporation and fragmentation of the spectator remnants. The proposed observable therefore offers a data-driven handle for constraining models of preferential emission and spectator breakup, thereby improving our understanding of the mechanisms that shape the final-state longitudinal distributions in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a Pearson correlation between spectator asymmetry and charged-particle forward-backward asymmetry as a probe of preferential emission and spectator dynamics, but the key hedge is that the correlator's sensitivity to spectator fragmentation is demonstrated using a data-driven parameterization of free-neutron spectators from PHENIX measurements, not from direct experimental data; the authors state that "in real data and more advanced models, a clear η dependence of these ratios is expected," indicating the current model-based demonstration is a first-order study.

## Method
The authors used the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. They constructed normalized spectator asymmetry (α_sp) from forward/backward spectator counts and normalized charged-particle asymmetry (α_ch,η) from multiplicities in symmetric pseudorapidity intervals ±η. A Pearson correlation coefficient ρ(α_sp, α_ch,η) was calculated with a modified variance for α_ch,η that removes dependence on the pseudorapidity bin width δη. To model spectator fragmentation effects, the authors replaced total spectators with free-neutron spectators using a parameterization based on PHENIX measurements of spectator breakup.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV simulated with AMPT model
- Centrality determined using charged-particle multiplicity within |η| < 0.2; observables calculated using particles with |η| > 0.2
- The correlator magnitude is about 20% smaller for neutron spectators than for total spectators
- The ratio of correlator with total spectators to that with neutron spectators is approximately 20% larger
- The correlator with free-neutron spectators decreases from near unity in central collisions (0-10%) to nearly zero in peripheral collisions (30-40%)
- The modified variance Var(α_ch,η)_mod scales as 1/δη, and the correlator is shown to be insensitive to δη variations for δη = 0.2 and other tested values
- The covariance -Cov(α_sp, α_ch,η) shows weak centrality dependence, while Var(α_sp) increases toward central collisions

## Context
This work extends the fixed-target approach of Gazdzicki (2005), who used correlations between spectator content and produced-particle multiplicities to probe longitudinal stopping in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV. The proposed correlator provides a collider-compatible realization of that idea, formulated in terms of quantities accessible at RHIC and LHC experiments. The method enables systematic investigation of how source asymmetries are imprinted on the pseudorapidity distribution P(η) and provides constraints on spectator fragmentation models, which have been poorly constrained by existing measurements from PHOBOS (2015) and STAR (2014).
