# Evidence for enhanced neutron-proton correlations from the level structure of the $N=Z+1$ nucleus $^{87}_{43}$Tc$^{\ }_{44}$

> The low-lying excited states in the neutron-deficient $N=Z+1$ nucleus $^{87}_{43}$Tc$^{\ }_{44}$ have been studied via the fusion-evaporation reaction $^{54}$Fe($^{36}$Ar, $2n1p$)$^{87}$Tc at the Grand Accélérateur National d'Ions Lourds (GANIL), France. The AGATA spectrometer was used in conjunction with the auxiliary NEDA, Neutron Wall, and DIAMANT detector arrays to measure coincident prompt $γ$-rays, neutrons, and charged particles emitted in the reaction. A level scheme of $^{87}$Tc from the (9/2$^{+}_{g.s.}$) state to the (33/2$^{+}_{1}$) state was established based on 6 mutually coincident $γ$-ray transitions. The constructed level structure exhibits a rotational behavior with a sharp backbending at $\hbarω\approx 0.50$ MeV. A decrease in alignment frequency and increase in alignment sharpness in the odd-mass isotonic chains around $N=44$ is proposed as an effect of the enhanced isoscalar neutron-proton interactions in odd-mass nuclei when approaching the $N=Z$ line.

## Key claim

The observed decrease in alignment frequency and increase in alignment sharpness in the odd-mass isotonic chains around N=44 is proposed as an effect of enhanced isoscalar neutron-proton interactions in odd-mass nuclei when approaching the N=Z line, though the authors note that shape degrees of freedom can muddle the analysis in some cases.

## Method

The low-lying excited states in 87Tc were studied via the fusion-evaporation reaction 54Fe(36Ar, 2n1p)87Tc at GANIL, using the AGATA gamma-ray spectrometer in conjunction with the NEDA, Neutron Wall, and DIAMANT detector arrays to measure coincident prompt gamma-rays, neutrons, and charged particles. A level scheme from the (9/2+ g.s.) state to the (33/2+1) state was established based on six mutually coincident gamma-ray transitions identified through gamma-gamma coincidence matrices sorted with the condition of "2n01p" (two neutrons together with either none or one proton detected in prompt coincidence). The experimental results were compared with large-scale shell-model calculations in the p g and fp g model spaces using the slgmt0 and jun45 interactions.

## Key numbers

- Six gamma-ray transitions were identified: 712.0(1) keV, 886.5(1) keV, 1007.4(1) keV, 845.9(1) keV, 956.3(2) keV, and 1108.1(4) keV, establishing the level scheme from the (9/2+ g.s.) state to the (33/2+1) state.
- A sharp backbending was observed at rotational frequency hbar-omega approximately 0.50 MeV.
- The AGATA spectrometer achieved a 0.095% sigma/E resolution for the 1408.1 keV gamma-ray transition from a 152Eu calibration source.
- The single-proton detection efficiency of DIAMANT was about 39(1)%.
- Reducing the spin-aligned np pair interaction matrix element by 200 keV (slgmt0* calculation) increased the excitation energy of the 25/2+1 state by 133 keV and nearly 200 keV for higher-spin states in 87Tc, while in 89Tc the same reduction changed the 25/2+1 state excitation energy by only 70 keV.
- Relative intensities of the gamma rays: 100(4), 80(3), 65(5), 37(4), 26(3), and 13(3).

## Context

This work extends the known spectroscopy of the neutron-deficient N=Z+1 nucleus 87Tc, for which only two excited states were previously known (Rudolph 1991), to the tentative 33/2+1 state. The results are compared with the systematics of odd-mass N=44 isotones (83Y, 85Nb) and N=43 isotones (81Sr, 83Zr, 85Mo), as well as neighboring technetium isotopes 89,91,93Tc, and with shell-model calculations. The observed decrease in excitation energy of higher-spin states when approaching the N=Z line supports the role of isoscalar neutron-proton pairing correlations, consistent with earlier suggestions for delayed alignments in even-even N=Z nuclei from 72Kr to 88Ru (Fischer 2001, Marginean 2002, Cederwall 2020) and the proposed spin-aligned np pair structure in 92Pd (Cederwall 2011). This work enables further investigation of isoscalar pairing effects in odd-mass N approximately Z nuclei and provides experimental constraints for theoretical models of neutron-proton correlations.
