# A holographic model for large N thermal QCD

> We summarize the dual gravity description for a thermal gauge theory, reviewing the key features of our holographic model of large N QCD and elaborating on some new results.The theory has matter in the fundamental representation and the gauge coupling runs logarithmically with energy scale at low energies. At the highest energies the theory becomes approximately scale invariant, much like what we would expect for large N QCD although not with asymptotic freedom. In this limit the theory has a gravity dual captured by an almost classical supergravity description with a controlled quantum behavior, such that by renormalizing the supergravity action, we can compute the stress tensor of the dual gauge theory. From the stress tensor we obtain the shear viscosity and the entropy of the medium at a temperature T, and the violation of the bound for the viscosity to the entropy ratio is then investigated. By considering dynamics of open strings in curved spacetime described by the supergravity limit, we compute the drag and diffusion coefficients for a heavy parton traversing the thermal medium. It is shown that both coefficients have a logarithmic dependence on momentum, consistent with pQCD expectations. Finally, we study the confinement/deconfinement mechanism for quarks by analyzing open strings in the presence of the flavor seven branes. We find linear confinement of quarks at low temperatures, while at high temperatures the quarkonium states melt, a behavior consistent with the existence of a deconfined phase.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a holographic dual of large N thermal QCD can be constructed by modifying the Klebanov-Strassler geometry with anti-branes and fluxes to achieve a UV-complete theory that is approximately conformal at high energies and confining at low energies, but the model does not exhibit asymptotic freedom in the UV, only approximate scale invariance.

## Method

The authors construct a ten-dimensional supergravity dual by starting from the Ouyang-Klebanov-Strassler (OKS) geometry and modifying it at large radial coordinate r by switching off three-form fluxes, controlling the axio-dilaton via anti D5-branes and anti seven-branes with world-volume fluxes, and embedding flavor seven-branes. They compute the stress tensor of the dual gauge theory by holographically renormalizing the supergravity action, obtaining the shear viscosity and entropy density. They also compute drag and diffusion coefficients for a heavy parton by analyzing open string dynamics in the curved spacetime, and study confinement/deconfinement by analyzing open strings in the presence of flavor seven-branes.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s is given by an expression that includes a term proportional to c_3 kappa / (3 L^2), where c_3 is the coefficient of the Riemann square term from D7 brane back-reaction; for c_3 greater than some critical value the theory becomes acausal.
* The beta functions for the gauge couplings in the IR are: partial derivative with respect to log Lambda of (4pi^2/g_1^2 + 4pi^2/g_2^2) = -3N_f/4, and partial derivative with respect to log Lambda of (4pi^2/g_1^2 - 4pi^2/g_2^2) = 3M(1 + (3g_s N_f/(2pi)) log Lambda).
* The warp factor h at large r behaves as h = L^4/r^4 [1 + sum_i a_i(r_h,psi,theta_1,theta_2)/r^i], and at small r as h = L^4/r^4 [sum_{j,k} b_{jk}(r_h,psi,theta_1,theta_2) log^k(r)/r^j].
* The effective number of colors N_eff at large r is given by N_eff = N (1 + sum_i a_i/r^i), and the condition for consistent UV behavior is sum_k k a_k / r^{k-1} < 0 for large r.

## Context

This work extends the Klebanov-Strassler (KS) model (1998) and the Ouyang-Klebanov-Strassler (OKS) model with fundamental flavors, addressing their lack of UV completion by proposing a geometry that becomes approximately AdS at high energies. The model enables computation of transport coefficients (shear viscosity, drag, diffusion) for a strongly coupled plasma that mimics large N QCD, and it provides a framework to study the confinement/deconfinement transition and the violation of the eta/s bound, building on prior work by Kats and Petrov (2009) on Riemann square corrections.
