# Evidence for Quark Gluon Plasma from Hadron Production in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> The experimental results on the pion, strangeness and J/psi production in high energy nuclear collisions are discussed. The anomalous energy dependence of pion and strangeness production is consistent with the hypothesis that a transition to a deconfined phase takes place between the top AGS (15 AGeV) and the SPS (200 AGeV) energies. The J/psi production systematics at the SPS can be understood assuming that the J/psi mesons are created at hadronization according to the available hadronic phase space. This new interpretation of the J/psi data allows one to establish a coherent picture of high energy nuclear collisions based on the statistical approaches of the collision early stage and hadronization. Surprisingly, the statistical model of strong interactions is successful even in the region reserved up to now for pQCD based models.

# Evidence for Quark Gluon Plasma from Hadron Production in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The anomalous energy dependence of pion and strangeness production is consistent with the hypothesis that a transition to a deconfined phase (Quark Gluon Plasma) takes place between the top AGS (approximately 15 AGeV) and the SPS (approximately 200 AGeV) energies, but this interpretation remains under vivid discussion because the statistical models on which it rests are not commonly recognized as valid tools and their basic assumptions cannot be derived from QCD.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data on pion multiplicity, strangeness-to-pion ratio, and J/psi production in nucleon-nucleon (N+N) and central nucleus-nucleus (A+A) collisions across AGS and SPS energies, interpreting the results within statistical models of the early collision stage and hadronization. Event-by-event fluctuations of mean transverse momentum and kaon-to-pion ratio in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV are analyzed using the Phi measure of fluctuations and compared to mixed-event simulations representing the grand canonical ensemble.

## Key numbers

- The transition to deconfined matter is located between top AGS energy (approximately 15 AGeV) and SPS energy (approximately 200 AGeV).
- The strangeness-to-pion ratio E_S for N+N interactions increases by a factor of about 2 between F approximately 2 (AGS) and F approximately 4 (SPS).
- The temperature parameter needed to reproduce the measured J/psi yield in the statistical hadronization model is T approximately 176 MeV, consistent with the range 160-190 MeV obtained from hadron yield systematics.
- The value of Phi_{P_T} for central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV is consistent with zero, the value calculated in the statistical model in grand canonical ensemble for classical particles.
- Phi_{P_T} for p+p interactions shows a non-zero, positive value attributed to energy-momentum conservation effects significant for small systems.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Fermi (1950) and Landau (1953) for pion production and the statistical hadronization model of Becattini (1996) and Stachel (1999) to create a coherent picture of high energy nuclear collisions. It contradicts the orthodox picture of J/psi suppression as a QGP signal developed by Matsui and Satz (1986) and the NA38/NA50 collaborations, instead proposing that J/psi mesons are created statistically at hadronization. The paper enables future searches for a non-monotonic energy dependence of the strangeness-to-pion ratio in the intermediate energy region between AGS and SPS as a signal of the transition, and establishes event-by-event fluctuation analysis as a test of the statistical approach.
