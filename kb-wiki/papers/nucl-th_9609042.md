# On the Flavor Structure of the Constituent Quark

> We discuss the dressing of constituent quarks with a pseudoscalar meson cloud within the effective chiral quark model. SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking effects are included explicitly. Our results are compared with those of the traditional meson cloud approach in which pions are coupled to the nucleon. The pionic dressing of the constituent quarks explains the experimentally observed violation of the Gottfried Sum Rule and leads to an enhanced nonperturbative sea of quark-antiquark pairs in the constituent quark and consequently in the nucleon. We find 2.5 times more pions and 10-15 times more kaons in the nucleon than in the traditional picture.

## Key claim

The dressing of constituent quarks with a pseudoscalar meson cloud, rather than the traditional coupling of pions to the nucleon as a whole, can explain the violation of the Gottfried Sum Rule; however, the resulting $\bar u / \bar d$ ratio of approximately 0.74 is too large compared to the NA51 experimental value of $0.51 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.05$ at $x = 0.18$, and the predicted $x(\bar d - \bar u)$ difference is concentrated at smaller Bjorken-$x$ than in the traditional meson cloud model.

## Method

The authors used the effective chiral quark theory of Manohar and Georgi (1984), where Goldstone bosons couple directly to constituent quarks via a light-front Fock decomposition. They calculated pion and kaon splitting functions from the chiral Lagrangian, including SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking via different masses for light (313 MeV) and strange (567 MeV) constituent quarks, and fixed the vertex cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ by fitting the pion dressing probability to the NMC value of the Gottfried Sum Rule. The resulting antiquark distributions in the nucleon were obtained by convoluting the constituent quark distributions (parametrized as $Q_N(y) = C_{\alpha\beta} y^{\alpha}(1-y)^{\beta}$ with $\alpha = 1/3$, $\beta = 3$) with the meson-induced quark distributions at the confinement scale, then evolved via leading-order QCD evolution to higher $Q^2$.

## Key numbers

- Gottfried Sum Rule experimental value: $S_G = 0.235 \pm 0.026$ (NMC at CERN, 1994)
- Pion dressing probability per constituent quark: $P_{\pi/Q} = 0.22$ (same for both model A with $g_A = 1$ and model B with $g_A = 0.75$)
- Kaon dressing probability per constituent quark: $P_{K/Q} = 0.051$ (model A) and $0.084$ (model B)
- Number of pions in the nucleon: $P_{\pi/N} = 0.66$; number of kaons in the nucleon: $P_{K/N} = 0.15$ to $0.25$
- Ratio of pions in the nucleon compared to traditional model: 2.5 times more; ratio of kaons: 10-15 times more
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ from Gottfried Sum Rule fit: 1.26 GeV (model A) and 3.31 GeV (model B)
- Pionic contribution to $N-\Delta$ mass splitting: $\delta_{\pi}^{N\Delta} = 222$ MeV (model A) and 140 MeV (model B), compared to the experimental $\delta^{N\Delta} \approx 300$ MeV
- Momentum carried by sea quarks at the initial scale: $\sum_f \int_0^1 x (q_f^{sea} + \bar q_f^{sea}) dx = 0.08$ to $0.09$
- NA51 experimental ratio: $\bar u / \bar d = 0.51 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.05$ at $x = 0.18$ (CERN, 1994)

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach of Eichten, Hinchliffe, and Quigg (1992) by explicitly including SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking effects and calculating the $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry directly rather than using it as motivation for parametrizations. It contrasts with the traditional nucleonic meson cloud model of Henley, Sick, and collaborators (1995), where pions couple to the nucleon rather than to constituent quarks, finding 2.5 times more pions and 10-15 times more kaons in the nucleon. The results enable future experimental tests to distinguish between these pictures, particularly through measurements of the $x$-dependence of the $\bar u / \bar d$ ratio and the strange sea distributions, and provide constraints on the pionic contribution to the nucleon-delta mass splitting.
