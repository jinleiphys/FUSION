# Measurement of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering with a Polarized Proton Target

> The longitudinal target-spin asymmetry A_UL for the exclusive electroproduction of high energy photons was measured for the first time in p(e,e'pγ). The data have been accumulated at Jefferson Lab with the CLAS spectrometer using 5.7 GeV electrons and a longitudinally polarized NH_3 target. A significant azimuthal angular dependence was observed, resulting from the interference of the Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering and Bethe-Heitler processes. The amplitude of the sin(phi) moment is 0.252 +/- 0.042(stat) +/- 0.020(sys). Theoretical calculations are in good agreement with the magnitude and the kinematic dependence of the target-spin asymmetry, which is sensitive to the generalized parton distributions H and H-tilde.

## Key claim

The first measurement of the longitudinal target-spin asymmetry A_UL for exclusive electroproduction of high-energy photons in p(e,e'pγ) shows a significant sin(phi) moment of 0.252 +/- 0.042(stat) +/- 0.020(sys), but this result is conditional on correcting for pi0 contamination in the single-photon sample and on the assumption that higher-twist contributions (sin2phi term) are negligible within the experimental kinematic range.

## Method

The CLAS spectrometer at Jefferson Lab was used with 5.7 GeV electrons incident on a longitudinally polarized NH3 target. Events were selected by requiring exactly one electron, one proton, and one photon in the final state, with deep inelastic kinematics defined by Q2 > 1 GeV2/c2, W > 2 GeV/c2, and -t < 0.6 GeV2/c2. The target-spin asymmetry was extracted from luminosity-normalized and acceptance-corrected event counts at positive and negative target helicity, with a dilution factor f = 0.901 +/- 0.035 accounting for unpolarized material in the target. The remaining pi0 contamination in the single-photon sample was corrected using a Monte Carlo simulation normalized to observed pi0 events, and the final asymmetry was obtained by subtracting the pi0 contribution using the measured pi0 asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Amplitude of the sin(phi) moment of A_UL: 0.252 +/- 0.042 (stat) +/- 0.020 (sys) at <Q2> = 1.82 GeV2/c2, <-t> = 0.31 GeV2/c2, and <xi> = 0.16
- Amplitude of the sin(2phi) moment: -0.022 +/- 0.045 (stat) +/- 0.021 (sys), compatible with zero
- Dilution factor for single-photon events: f = 0.901 +/- 0.035
- Dilution factor for pi0 events: f = 0.782 +/- 0.036
- Target polarization ranged from 60% to 80%
- Kinematic cuts: Q2 > 1 GeV2/c2, W > 2 GeV/c2, -t < 0.6 GeV2/c2
- Missing mass squared cut: -0.12 (GeV/c2)2 < M_X^2 < 0.12 (GeV/c2)2

## Context

This work extends the study of DVCS through spin asymmetries, following earlier beam spin asymmetry measurements by the CLAS collaboration (Step) and HERMES collaboration (Aira). The target-spin asymmetry provides complementary information to beam spin asymmetry, being sensitive to the generalized parton distributions H and H-tilde, while beam spin asymmetry is dominated by H. The results enable constraints on different GPDs when combined with precision beam spin asymmetry data (hep0303006), and similar target-spin asymmetry studies were also underway at HERMES (hermesaul).
