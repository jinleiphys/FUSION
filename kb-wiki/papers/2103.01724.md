# $J/ψ$ polarization in high multiplicity $pp$ and $pA$ collisions: CGC+NRQCD approach

> Quarkonium production mechanism in high multiplicity small collision systems has recently been pursued in the color-glass-condensate (CGC) effective theory combined with non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization, allowing to study initial state interactions. Quarkonium polarization, potentially measured in future experiments, would help elucidate the quarkonium production mechanism at high multiplicities. In this paper, we provide predictions on $J/ψ$ polarization parameters in high multiplicity proton-proton ($pp$) and proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions within the CGC+NRQCD framework. Theoretical predictions are given for $J/ψ$ rapidity $2.5< y_{J/ψ}<4$, charged-particle multiplicity pseudorapidity $|η_{ch} | <1$ and energies $\sqrt{S}=13\mathrm{~TeV}$ for $pp$, $\sqrt{S}=8.16\mathrm{~TeV}$ for $pA$ collisions. Considering two leptonic frame choices (Collins - Soper and helicity) we find a weak polarization of $J/ψ$ that additionally decreases with growing event activities. No significant system size dependence between $pp$ and $pA$ collisions is obtained - this could be a new constraint to initial state interactions in small collision systems.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that J/ψ polarization in high-multiplicity pp and pA collisions is weak (polarization parameters below 0.1 in magnitude) and decreases with growing event activity, with no significant system size dependence between pp and pA collisions, a result that could serve as a new constraint on initial state interactions in small collision systems.

## Method
The authors compute J/ψ spin density matrix elements within the color-glass-condensate (CGC) effective theory combined with non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization, using short-distance coefficients for color-singlet and color-octet c-cbar production in a dilute-dense system. Charged-particle multiplicity is modeled by varying the initial saturation scale Q_s0^2 in the McLerran-Venugopalan initial condition for the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation, with higher multiplicities corresponding to larger Q_s0^2 values. Long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) are taken from a fit to Tevatron high-p_T prompt J/ψ data by Chao et al. (2012).

## Key numbers
- For pp collisions at √S = 13 TeV and pA collisions at √S = 8.16 TeV, with J/ψ rapidity 2.5 < y_J/ψ < 4 and charged-particle pseudorapidity |η_ch| < 1.
- All polarization parameters (λ_θ, λ_φ, λ_θφ) have magnitudes below 0.1, indicating nearly unpolarized J/ψ production.
- λ_θ in the helicity frame equals 0.1 at minimum bias and decreases to negative values with increasing event activity.
- The color singlet LDME is ⟨O^J/ψ(³S₁[1])⟩ = 1.16/(2N_c) GeV³; color octet LDMEs are ⟨O^J/ψ(¹S₀[8])⟩ = 0.089 ± 0.0098 GeV³, ⟨O^J/ψ(³S₁[8])⟩ = 0.0030 ± 0.0012 GeV³, and ⟨O^J/ψ(³P_J[8])⟩/m_c² = 0.0056 ± 0.0021 GeV³ with m_c = 1.5 GeV.
- Initial saturation scale for minimum bias pp events is Q₀² = 0.168 GeV²; for pA collisions the nucleus-to-proton saturation scale ratio ν is taken as 2 or 3.

## Context
This work extends the CGC+NRQCD framework previously applied to J/ψ p_T spectra in pp and pA collisions (Ma et al., 2014, 2015) and to J/ψ polarization in minimum bias pp collisions (Ma et al., 2018). It provides the first predictions for J/ψ polarization as a function of charged-particle multiplicity, motivated by LHC measurements of J/ψ yield versus multiplicity (ALICE, 2012, 2020). The results suggest that future polarization measurements at the LHC and High-Luminosity LHC could distinguish initial-state saturation effects from final-state medium effects in small collision systems.
