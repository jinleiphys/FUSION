# Asymptotics of neutron Cooper pair in weakly bound nuclei

> Asymptotic form of neutron Cooper pair penetrating to the exterior of nuclear surface is investigated with the Bogoliubov theory for the superfluid Fermions. Based on a two-particle Schrödinger equation governing the Cooper pair wave function and systematic studies for both weakly bound and stable nuclei, the Cooper pair is shown to be spatially correlated even in the asymptotic large distance limit, and the penetration length of the pair condensate is revealed to be universally governed by the two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}$ and the di-neutron mass $2m$.

## Key claim

The neutron Cooper pair retains its spatial correlation even in the asymptotic large-distance limit far outside the nuclear surface, and the penetration length of the pair condensate is universally governed by the two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}\) and the di-neutron mass \(2m\), irrespective of whether the Fermi energy is small or large. The authors hedge that this universality holds under the condition that the \(^{1}S\) interaction has a large scattering length; if the interaction is weaker, the spatial correlation will be weakened accordingly.

## Method

The authors used the selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model, formulated in coordinate space, to describe superfluid fermions in nuclei. They solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equation for even-even isotopes of Ca, Ni, Zr, and Sn, covering stable to neutron-rich drip-line nuclei, using Skyrme functionals (SkM*, SLy4, SkI4) and a density-dependent contact pairing force. The asymptotic form of the Cooper pair wave function was derived analytically from a two-particle Schrödinger equation that holds in the asymptotic region, and the exponential decay constant of the pair condensate was extracted by fitting the numerical HFB results at large distances.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{138}\)Zr, the fitted exponential constant \(\tilde{\kappa} = 0.218\) fm\(^{-1}\) agrees with the analytic value \(\kappa_{{\rm d},0} = 0.207\) fm\(^{-1}\) calculated from \(\sqrt{8m|\lambda|}/\hbar\) with Fermi energy \(\lambda = -0.22\) MeV.
- For \(^{92}\)Zr, the fitted exponential constant \(\tilde{\kappa} = 1.131\) fm\(^{-1}\) matches the analytic value \(\sqrt{8m|\lambda|}/\hbar\) with \(\lambda = -6.6\) MeV.
- The Fermi energy varies from \(\lambda = -10.3\) MeV (\(^{44}\)Ca) to \(-0.22\) MeV (\(^{138}\)Zr) across the studied nuclei.
- The \(^{1}S\) neutron-neutron scattering length is \(a = -18.5\) fm.
- The pairing force strength used is \(v_0 = -458.4\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) with an energy cut-off \(E_{\rm cut} = 60\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous HFB studies of di-neutron correlation in medium and heavy mass nuclei (Matsuo 2005, Pillet 2007) and of giant halo structures in neutron-rich Zr isotopes (Meng 2006, Grasso 2006) by providing a universal analytic description of the asymptotic Cooper pair. It contrasts with three-body and cluster models (Bertsch 1991, Zhukov 1993, Jensen 2004) that are limited to light-mass two-neutron halo nuclei. The result enables a general understanding of neutron pair penetration in all self-bound nuclei, from stable to drip-line, and implies that there is no theoretical upper bound on the penetration length of the pair condensate, scaling as \(1/\sqrt{S_{2n}}\).
