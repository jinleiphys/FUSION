# The electrical conductivity and soft photon emissivity of the QCD plasma

> The electrical conductivity in the hot phase of the QCD plasma is extracted from a quenched lattice measurement of the Euclidean time vector correlator for 1.5 < T/T_c < 3. The spectral density in the vicinity of the origin is examined using a method specially adapted to this region, and a peak at small energies is seen. The continuum limit of the electrical conductivity, and the closely related soft photon emissivity of the QCD plasma, are then extracted from a fit to the Fourier transform of the temporal vector correlator.

# The electrical conductivity and soft photon emissivity of the QCD plasma

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity and soft photon emissivity of the QCD plasma are extracted from quenched lattice data for 1.5 ≤ T/T_c ≤ 3, but the extraction relies on a sequence of Bayesian methods and a parametrized model of the spectral density, with the continuum limit obtained by extrapolation in 1/N_t^2; the result is subject to systematic uncertainties from algorithmic parameters and the finite lattice spacing.

## Method

The Euclidean time vector correlator was computed on quenched lattices at T = 1.5 T_c, 2 T_c, and 3 T_c using staggered quarks with masses m/T_c = 0.03 and 0.1, on lattices with N_t = 8, 10, 12, and 14 and two spatial volumes per spacing. The spectral density near zero energy was extracted by two Bayesian approaches: first, a linear regulator method (with ℒ = 1) applied to the subtracted correlator ΔG = G_QCD − G_ideal; second, a parametrized rational form Δρ(z) = (z Σ γ_n z^{2n})/(1 + Σ δ_m z^{2m}) fitted to the Fourier transform of the Euclidean correlator. The electrical conductivity σ was obtained from the slope of the spectral density at ω = 0 via the Kubo formula, and the continuum limit was taken by extrapolating in 1/N_t^2.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 1.5 ≤ T/T_c ≤ 3, with T_c the critical temperature of quenched QCD.
- Lattice spacings correspond to N_t = 8, 10, 12, and 14 (i.e., a = 1/(8T), 1/(10T), 1/(12T), 1/(14T)).
- Quark masses used: m/T_c = 0.03 and 0.1; results are identical within errors for these two values.
- The continuum-limit estimate: σ/T ≈ 7 × C_EM, where C_EM = 4πα Σ_f e_f^2 (the EM vertex factor).
- The dimensionless soft photon emissivity (right-hand y-axis of Fig. 3) equals 6σ/T.
- The extracted quark mean free time τ_q ≈ 0.3 fm, derived from σ = C_EM n_q τ_q / m.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio estimate: η/S ≈ 0.2.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of dilepton rates (Author, Year) by focusing on the soft (ω → 0) limit, where the spectral density is related to transport via the Kubo formula. It contrasts with weak-coupling perturbative computations (Author, Year) which give σ ∝ α T / (g^4 log g^{-1}) but fail in this temperature range because g > 1. The results enable predictions for soft photon emission in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and provide estimates for the quark mean free path and shear viscosity that can be compared with hydrodynamic models (Author, Year) and the conjectured bound on η/S (Author, Year).
