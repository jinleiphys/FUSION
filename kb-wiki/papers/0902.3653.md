# Dynamical Models of the Excitations of Nucleon Resonances

> The development of a dynamical model for investigating the nucleon resonances using the reactions of meson production from πN, γN, N(e,e'), and N(ν,{\it l}) reactions is reviewed. The results for the Δ(1232) state are summarized and discussed. The progress in investigating higher mass nucleon resonances is reported.

## Key claim

The paper reviews a dynamical model for extracting nucleon resonance parameters from meson production reactions, emphasizing that the model's bare baryon masses (which differ from empirical resonance positions due to coupling with meson-baryon scattering states) can potentially be identified with the mass spectrum from hadron structure calculations that do not account for the meson-baryon continuum, though relating these bare masses to Lattice QCD calculations is more difficult due to lattice spacing limitations.

## Method

The authors apply a unitary transformation method (FST-Okubo method) to derive an energy-independent effective Hamiltonian from phenomenological Lagrangians constrained by chiral symmetry, isospin, parity, and gauge invariance. This effective Hamiltonian includes bare N* and meson (rho, sigma) degrees of freedom, non-resonant meson-baryon interactions, and couplings to pi-pi-N three-particle states, leading to a set of coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the scattering T-matrix. The resulting amplitudes separate into non-resonant and resonant parts, where the resonant amplitudes involve dressed vertex interactions and self-energies that account for the coupling between bare states and the meson-baryon continuum.

## Key numbers

* The model is expected to be valid up to W = 2 GeV, below which three-pion production is very weak.
* Two-pion photoproduction cross sections become larger than one-pion photoproduction as the gamma-p invariant mass exceeds W ~ 1.4 GeV.
* KY (K+Lambda, K+Sigma0, K0Sigma+), eta-p, and omega-p production cross sections are a factor of about 10 weaker than the dominant pi+pi-p production in the higher mass N* region.
* The Delta(1232) state is the primary focus of the low-energy results reviewed, with the model also applied to pi-N elastic scattering, gamma-N to pi-N reactions, pi-N to eta-N reactions, and pi-N to pi-pi-N reactions.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical reaction model approach developed by Sato and Lee (1996, 2000) and the unitary transformation method of Kobayashi, Sato, and Ohtsubo (1997) to include multi-channel, multi-resonance descriptions up to 2 GeV. It contrasts with approaches based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which the authors argue is extremely difficult to apply to N* studies due to singularities and complex analytic structure, and with energy-dependent models such as the Julich model (Krehl, 1999) where unitarity is more difficult to satisfy. The model enables systematic analysis of data from JLab, MIT-Bates, LEGS, Mainz, Bonn, GRAAL, and Spring-8 facilities, and provides a framework for interpreting extracted N* parameters in terms of QCD-based hadron structure models and Lattice QCD calculations.
