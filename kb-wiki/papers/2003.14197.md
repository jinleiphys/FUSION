# Expression of relativistic expectation values of powers of $r$ in terms of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients

> It is shown that, in the relativistic hydrogenic approximation, expectation values of the type $\langle n\ell j|r^k|n\ell j\rangle$ can be expressed in terms of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients or $3jm$ symbols. This generalizes the results obtained by Varshalovich and Karpova [Opt. Spectrosc. 118, 1-5 (2015); Opt. Spektrosk. 118, 3-7] in the non-relativistic hydrogenic case.

# Expression of relativistic expectation values of powers of \(r\) in terms of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients

## Key claim

The paper shows that relativistic hydrogenic expectation values \(\langle n\ell j|r^k|n\ell j\rangle\) can be expressed in terms of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients or \(3jm\) symbols, generalizing the non-relativistic results of Varshalovich and Karpova (2015). However, as in the non-relativistic case, some of the \(3j\) coefficients are evaluated in a nonphysical region of their arguments where the triangle condition is not satisfied and the coefficients vanish individually, yet the products of the coefficients with their prefactors remain finite.

## Method

The author starts from known expressions for the relativistic bound-state Dirac wavefunctions \(P_{n\kappa}(r)\) and \(Q_{n\kappa}(r)\) in terms of confluent hypergeometric functions and generalized Laguerre polynomials. The expectation value \(\langle r^k\rangle\) is expressed as an integral of \(r^k\) times the sum of squares of these radial functions, which reduces to a combination of three hypergeometric \(_3F_2\) functions evaluated at unity. Using the van der Waerden form of the \(_3F_2\) function and the relation between \(_3F_2\) functions and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients (or \(3jm\) symbols), each hypergeometric term is rewritten as a product of Gamma functions and a \(3j\) symbol, yielding the final expression for \(\langle r^k\rangle\) in terms of \(3j\) symbols.

## Key numbers

- For \(k = -3\): \(\langle r^{-3}\rangle = \frac{8Z^3\left[6\kappa^2\left(n_r+\gamma\right)^2+2N^2\left(1-\gamma^2\right)-6N\kappa\left(n_r+\gamma\right)\right]}{(2\gamma-2)(2\gamma-1)2\gamma(2\gamma+1)(2\gamma+2)N^5}\)
- For \(k = -2\): \(\langle r^{-2}\rangle = \frac{4Z^2\left[2\kappa^2\left(n_r+\gamma\right)-N\kappa\right]}{(2\gamma-1)2\gamma(2\gamma+1)N^4}\)
- For \(k = -1\): \(\langle r^{-1}\rangle = \frac{Z(\kappa^2+n_r\gamma)}{\gamma N^3}\)
- For \(k = 1\): \(\langle r\rangle = \frac{3N^2\left(n_r+\gamma\right)-N\kappa-\kappa^2\left(n_r+\gamma\right)}{2ZN}\)
- For \(k = 2\): \(\langle r^2\rangle = \frac{N^2\left[5\left(n_r+\gamma\right)^2+1-\gamma^2\right]-6N\kappa\left(n_r+\gamma\right)-4\kappa^2\left(n_r+\gamma\right)^2}{2Z^2}\)
- In the special case \(n_r=0\): \(\langle r^k\rangle = \frac{(2\gamma+1)_k}{\zeta^k}\), where \((a)_n\) is the Pochhammer symbol

## Context

This work extends the approach of Varshalovich and Karpova (2015), who expressed non-relativistic hydrogenic radial integrals \(\langle n\ell|r^k|n\ell'\rangle\) in terms of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, to the relativistic Dirac case for diagonal matrix elements. The connection between radial integrals and angular momentum coefficients arises from the non-compact group O(2,1) symmetry of the radial hydrogen problem, as noted by Judd (1970) and Armstrong (1971). The results provide a compact algebraic form for relativistic expectation values that may simplify calculations in atomic physics and spectroscopy, and the author indicates that extension to relativistic non-diagonal elements is planned for future work.
