# Free energy versus internal energy potential for heavy quark systems at finite temperature

> Using the QCD sum rule with its operator product expansion reliably estimated from lattice calculations for the pressure and energy density of the quark-gluon plasma, we calculate the strength of the wave function of J/psi at the origin and find that it decreases with temperature above the transition temperature. This result is shown to follow exactly that obtained from the solution of the Schrödingier equation for a charm and anticharm quark pair using the free energy from lattice calculations as the potential and is in sharp contrast to that using the deeper potential associated with the internal energy, which shows an enhanced strength of the J/psi wave function at the origin. Our result thus has resolved the long-standing question of whether the free energy potential or the internal energy potential should be used in analyzing the spectrum of heavy quark systems at finite temperature.

# Free energy versus internal energy potential for heavy quark systems at finite temperature

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules with operator product expansion inputs reliably estimated from lattice calculations of the pressure and energy density of hot QCD matter, the authors show that the temperature dependence of the J/psi wave function at the origin follows that obtained from solving the Schrodinger equation with the free energy potential, not the deeper internal energy potential. This resolves the long-standing question of which potential should be used, but the authors note that the heavy quark potential is expected to acquire a gauge-dependent imaginary part at finite temperature, and a stronger potential with a different in-medium charm quark mass might be needed to reproduce gauge-invariant observables.

## Method

The authors used the Borel transformed QCD sum rule for the charm vector current correlation function, with the operator product expansion truncated at dimension-4 operators (scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates) whose temperature dependence was obtained from lattice data on the energy density and pressure of hot QCD matter. They extracted the residue f0 (proportional to |psi(0)|^2) by requiring Borel stability of the sum rule, and compared these results to solutions of the Schrodinger equation for a charm-anticharm pair using either the free energy potential or the internal energy potential extracted from lattice calculations.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum value of the J/psi wave function at the origin: |psi(0)| = 0.211 +/- 0.04 GeV^{3/2}, obtained from the empirical dielectron decay width Gamma_{J/psi}^{e+e-} = 5.55 keV
* Systematic error in |psi(0)| from Borel curve dependence estimated at most 0.001 GeV^{3/2}
* Charm quark bare mass used in Schrodinger equation: m_c = 1.25 GeV
* With the internal energy potential, |psi(0)| increases by almost a factor of two at slightly above the critical temperature
* With the free energy potential, |psi(0)| decreases monotonically with temperature, matching the QCD sum rule result in both behavior and values
* The analysis is valid up to T = 1.1 T_c, beyond which the OPE truncation at dimension-4 operators becomes unreliable

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate following Matsui and Satz (1986) on whether J/psi dissolves immediately above T_c or remains bound to higher temperatures, a question that lattice calculations of the heavy quark potential had not resolved due to ambiguity between free energy and internal energy potentials (Kaczmarek, 2005). The authors' QCD sum rule approach, building on earlier finite-temperature sum rule studies by Morita (2007) and Gubler (2011), provides a gauge-invariant determination that favors the free energy potential. This result implies that all charmonium states would essentially melt in the quark-gluon plasma, with observed J/psi in heavy ion collisions coming either from initial hard collisions outside the QGP or from recombination of charm and anticharm quarks during hadronization.
