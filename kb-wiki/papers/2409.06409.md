# Implication of a negative effective range on the $D\bar{D}^*$ interaction and the nature of $X(3872)$

> A recent analysis of the LHCb data [Phys. Rev. D 105 (2022) L031503] obtained a sizable negative effective range for the $X(3872)$. This has attracted intensive discussions on whether $X(3872)$ can be deemed as a $D\bar{D}^*$ molecular state. This work explicitly demonstrates that the negative effective range of the $X(3872)$ does not contradict the molecular picture, adopting an effective field theory formulation of the $D\bar{D}^*$ interaction that can simultaneously reproduce the binding energy and effective range of the $X(3872)$. We elaborate on the implications of the large negative effective range of $X(3872)$ and the small binding energy on the underlying $D\bar{D}^*$ interaction. Such results are relevant for a better understanding of hadronic molecules and their binding mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a negative effective range for the X(3872) does not contradict the hadronic molecular picture, provided the D̄D* interaction includes a momentum-dependent term (next-to-leading order) rather than only a constant contact interaction. The authors show that the X(3872) can be dynamically generated as a D̄D* molecular state with compositeness greater than 0.5, contrary to claims that a negative effective range implies a non-molecular (elementary) state.

## Method

The authors parameterize the D̄D* interaction in momentum space as V = α + βk², where α and β are low-energy constants. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to find poles and compute the scattering length, effective range, and compositeness for both single-channel (D⁰D̄*⁰) and coupled-channel (D⁰D̄*⁰ and D⁺D*⁻) cases. The potential parameters are adjusted to simultaneously reproduce the binding energy and negative effective range of the X(3872) extracted from LHCb data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of X(3872) relative to D⁰D̄*⁰ threshold: B = -0.05 MeV (from Particle Data Group)
- Effective range of X(3872) from LHCb data: r₀ = -5.34 fm (Esposito 2021), with a reanalysis giving about -3.78 fm (Baru 2021)
- In the single-channel case with V = α + βk², the compositeness X = 0.80 for r₀ = -5 fm and X = 0.86 for r₀ = -3 fm, both with B = -0.05 MeV
- In the coupled-channel case, the compositeness of channel 1 (D⁰D̄*⁰) is p₁ = 0.77, with scattering length a₁ = 15.7 fm, using α₀ = -191 GeV⁻² and β₀ = -1641 GeV⁻²
- For the isospin-averaged case (B ≈ -4 MeV), the compositeness drops to X = 0.17 with scattering length a = 0.88 fm

## Context

This work addresses the debate sparked by Esposito (2021), who used the Weinberg compositeness criterion to argue that the X(3872) cannot be a pure shallow molecule because its effective range is negative. The authors extend studies by Baru (2021), Albaladejo (2022), and Song (2022) that discussed how range corrections and coupled-channel effects modify the Weinberg relations. The results are relevant for understanding the binding mechanism of hadronic molecules and for future studies using contact-range effective field theories to connect the X(3872) with other systems, as done by Liu (2020), Dong (2021), Chen (2021), and Peng (2021).
