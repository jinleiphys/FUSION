# Exploring Particle Production and Thermal-Like Behavior through Quantum Entanglement

> Recent studies have shown a potential correlation between the entanglement of initial state partons in elementary particle collisions, as conceptualized by contemporary quantum and particle theory, and the final state multiplicity distribution of hadrons produced in experiments like those at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). It has been proposed that this relation between states can be demonstrated in a measurement of entropy. By showing equality between entanglement entropy in the initial state and thermodynamic entropy in the final state, we hope to demonstrate that not only is entanglement the driving mechanism behind matter generation, but also the thermal-like behavior seen in high energy particle collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that entanglement entropy calculated from initial-state parton distribution functions (PDFs) quantitatively agrees with thermodynamic entropy derived from final-state charged particle multiplicity distributions in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, but only when quark contributions and an "entropy of ignorance" correction factor (approximately 1.24 at 0.9 TeV) are included, and only in the low-x (high gluon density) regime.

## Method

The authors calculate initial-state entanglement entropy from leading-order PDFs (NNPDF and MSHT20) by summing over gluon and quark parton numbers in the low-x region (x around 10^-4, Q^2 around unity), applying a 2/3 factor to account for unmeasured neutral particles, and correcting by a ratio (approximately 1.24 at 0.9 TeV) between entropy of ignorance and true entanglement entropy from a Color Glass Condensate model. Final-state thermodynamic entropy is computed as the Shannon entropy of charged particle multiplicity distributions measured by ALICE at central pseudorapidity for collision energies from 0.9 to 8 TeV, with half the multiplicity attributed to each proton. The two entropies are compared as a function of collision energy.

## Key numbers

- The ratio between entropy of ignorance and entanglement entropy reaches approximately 1.24 for sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV at LHC energies, with an upper limit of about 1.4 at low Q^2.
- The analysis is restricted to x values around 10^-4, corresponding to a Q^2 range around unity.
- ALICE charged particle multiplicity data are used for four collision energies ranging from sqrt(s) = 0.9 to 8 TeV.
- The coupling constant alpha_s(M_z) = 0.119 (NNPDF) shows stronger agreement between initial and final state entropy than alpha_s(M_z) = 0.130 (MSHT20).
- The cumulant relations for a fully entangled state are given as C_2 = 2 - 1/bar{n}, C_3 = (6(bar{n}-1)bar{n}+1)/bar{n}^2, C_4 = (12bar{n}(bar{n}-1)+1)(2bar{n}-1)/bar{n}^3, and C_5 = ((bar{n}-1)(120bar{n}^2(bar{n}-1)+30bar{n})+1)/bar{n}^4.

## Context

This work extends the entanglement-based approach to particle production proposed by Kharzeev and Levin (2017) and Bellwied (2018), and follows theoretical developments by Hentschinski et al. (2022) who showed the need to include quark contributions at low Q^2. The analysis uses ALICE data (2015) covering a different phase space (low momentum, central rapidity) than previous studies, emphasizing the low Q^2 region conducive to gluon entanglement. The results support the theory that quantum entanglement in the initial state of relativistic hadron collisions can maintain coherence through early evolution and produce the observed thermal-like final state, with future measurements at forward rapidity at the LHC and full phase space at the EIC needed to test gluon saturation predictions at very low x.
