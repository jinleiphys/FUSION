# Nucleon Properties in the Covariant Quark-Diquark Model

> In the covariant quark-diquark model the effective Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equations for the nucleon and the $Δ$ are solved including scalar {\em and axialvector} diquark correlations. Their quark substructure is effectively taken into account in both, the interaction kernel of the BS equations and the currents employed to calculate nucleon observables. Electromagnetic current conservation is maintained. The electric form factors of proton and neutron match the data. Their magnetic moments improve considerably by including axialvector diquarks and photon induced scalar-axialvector transitions. The isoscalar magnetic moment can be reproduced, the isovector contribution is about 15% too small. The ratio $μG_E/G_M$ and the axial and strong couplings $g_A$, $g_{πNN}$, provide an upper bound on the relative importance of axialvector diquarks confirming that scalar diquarks nevertheless describe the dominant 2-quark correlations inside nucleons.

## Key claim

The covariant quark-diquark model, including both scalar and axialvector diquark correlations with quark substructure, can reproduce the electric form factors of proton and neutron and improve magnetic moments, but the isovector magnetic moment remains about 15% too small, indicating that scalar diquarks still describe the dominant two-quark correlations inside nucleons.

## Method

The authors solved effective Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equations for the nucleon and Delta baryon in Euclidean space, using separable two-quark correlations to introduce non-pointlike scalar and axialvector diquarks with a dipole form factor for their quark substructure. Electromagnetic currents were constructed via the gauging technique to maintain current conservation, including impulse-approximate couplings to quark and diquark, photon-induced scalar-axialvector transitions, and exchange-quark and seagull diagrams. Two parameter sets were employed: Set I with a constituent quark mass of 0.360 GeV and Set II fitted to both nucleon and Delta masses.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: 0.939 GeV for both parameter sets; Delta mass: 1.007 GeV (Set I) and 1.232 GeV (Set II)
- Quark mass: 0.360 GeV (Set I) and 0.425 GeV (Set II)
- Scalar diquark mass: 0.625 GeV (Set I) and 0.598 GeV (Set II)
- Axialvector diquark mass: 0.684 GeV (Set I) and 0.831 GeV (Set II)
- Diquark width parameter lambda: 0.95 GeV (Set I) and 0.53 GeV (Set II)
- Scalar diquark contributes 92% to the nucleon norm integral for Set II, versus 66% for Set I
- Anomalous magnetic moment of axialvector diquark kappa is approximately 1 for both sets
- Scalar-axialvector transition constant kappa_sa is approximately 2.1
- Isovector magnetic moment is about 15% too small compared to experiment

## Context

This work extends the covariant quark-diquark model of Oettel (1999) by including non-pointlike axialvector diquarks alongside scalar diquarks, whereas earlier calculations by Hellstern (1997) used only pointlike scalar diquarks. The results improve upon the semi-relativistic Salpeter approximation studied by Oettel (2000), which showed considerable deviations from the fully relativistic treatment. The model enables future studies of nucleon structure at higher momentum transfers and provides an upper bound on the role of axialvector diquark correlations in nucleons.
