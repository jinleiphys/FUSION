# Magnetic structure of few-nucleon systems at high momentum transfers in a $χ$EFT approach

> The five low-energy constants (LECs) in the electromagnetic current derived in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) up to one loop are determined by a simultaneous fit to the $A\,$=$\,2$--3 nuclei magnetic moments and to the deuteron magnetic form factor and threshold electrodisintegration at backward angles over a wide range of momentum transfers. The resulting parametrization then yields predictions for the $^3$He/$^3$H magnetic form factors in excellent accord with the experimental values for momentum transfers ranging up to $\approx 0.8$ GeV/c, beyond the expected regime of validity of the $χ$EFT approach. The calculations are based on last-generation two-nucleon interactions including high orders in the chiral expansion and derived by Entem, Macheleidt, and Nosyk [Phys.\ Rev.\ C {\bf 96}, 024004 (2017)] and by Piarulli {\it et al.} [Phys.\ Rev.\ C {\bf 94}, 054007 (2016)], using different $χ$EFT formulations. In the $A\,$=$\,3$ calculations, (chiral) three-nucleon interactions are also accounted for. The model dependence resulting from these different formulations of the interactions is found to be mild for momentum transfer below $\approx0.8$ GeV/c. An analysis of the convergence of the chiral expansion is also provided.

## Key claim

The five low-energy constants (LECs) in the chiral effective field theory electromagnetic current up to one loop can be determined by a simultaneous fit to magnetic moments and deuteron magnetic form factor and threshold electrodisintegration data, yielding predictions for trinucleon magnetic form factors that agree with experiment up to momentum transfers of approximately 0.8 GeV/c, which is beyond the expected regime of validity of the chiral expansion.

## Method

The authors fit the five LECs (d'_8, d'_9, d'_21, C'_15, C'_16) entering the N3LO one-pion-exchange and non-minimal contact currents using a chi-squared minimization on two data sets: set A includes the magnetic moments of deuteron, tritium, and helium-3 plus deuteron threshold electrodisintegration cross sections up to q_mu^2 = 40 fm^-2; set B adds deuteron magnetic form factor data up to q_mu = 6 fm^-1. Calculations use last-generation two-nucleon interactions from Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017) at N4LO in momentum space and from Piarulli et al. (2016) at N3LO in configuration space, with three-nucleon interactions included for A=3 systems and wave functions obtained via the hyperspherical-harmonic approach.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range of agreement for trinucleon magnetic form factors: up to approximately 0.8 GeV/c (beyond the expected regime of validity of chi-EFT)
- The reduced chi-squared for NV interactions with set A* (removing Rand et al. 1967 data) reaches approximately 3, with total data of order 40 for set A and 65 for set B
- The LEC d'_2^V is too large by a factor of 10-20 for all Hamiltonian models considered
- The deuteron threshold electrodisintegration cross sections span a region of momentum transfers q_mu^2 extending up to 40 fm^-2
- The cutoff Lambda for the EMN interactions takes values of 450, 500, and 550 MeV
- The NV interactions use cutoff values (R_S, R_L) of (0.8, 1.2) fm for models a and (0.7, 1.0) fm for models b

## Context

This work extends previous parametrizations of the chi-EFT current by Piarulli et al. (2013) and Schiavilla et al. (2019), which used resonance saturation arguments and fits to static magnetic properties but failed to describe the diffraction regions of trinucleon magnetic form factors at high momentum transfers. The present approach does not invoke resonance saturation and determines all five LECs simultaneously, enabling predictions for A=3 magnetic form factors that agree with experiment up to 0.8 GeV/c, suggesting the current parametrization is robust in kinematical regimes outside the limits of validity of the chi-EFT expansion.
