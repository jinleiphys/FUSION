# Constructing Inverse Scattering Potentials for α-α System using Reference Potential Approach

> Background: An accurate way to incorporate long range Coulomb interaction alongside short-range nuclear interaction has been a challenge for theoretical physicists. Purpose: In this paper, we propose a methodology based on the reference potential approach for constructing inverse potentials of alpha-alpha scattering. Methods: Two smoothly joined Morse potentials, regular for short-range nuclear interaction and inverted for long range Coulomb, are used in tandem as a reference potential in the phase function method to obtain the scattering phase shifts for the S, D and G states of alpha-alpha scattering. The model parameters are optimized by choosing to minimize the mean absolute percentage error between the obtained and experimental scattering phase shift values. Results: The constructed inverse potentials for S, D and G states have resulted in mean absolute percentage errors of 0.8, 0.5, and 0.4 respectively. The obtained resonances for D and G states closely match the experimental ones. Conclusion: The reference potential approach using a combination of smoothly joined Morse functions is successful in accurately accounting for the Coulomb interaction between charged particles in nuclear scattering studies.

## Key claim

The reference potential approach using two smoothly joined Morse potentials (regular for short-range nuclear interaction and inverted for long-range Coulomb) can construct inverse scattering potentials for the α-α system that reproduce experimental phase shifts with mean absolute percentage errors below 1%, while overcoming the need for arbitrary cutoffs or channel-dependent screening radii required by earlier screened Coulomb ansatzes.

## Method

The authors employed the phase function method, recasting the radial Schrödinger equation into a first-order Riccati-type differential equation for the phase shift δℓ(k,r). They used a reference potential consisting of two smoothly joined Morse functions: a regular Morse (D1 > 0) for short-range nuclear interaction and an inverted Morse (D2 < 0) for the long-range Coulomb tail, with continuity conditions enforced at the joining point X. The six free parameters (V1, α1, r1, α2, r2, X) were optimized by minimizing the mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) between computed and experimental scattering phase shifts for ℓ = 0, 2, 4 channels at energies up to 28 MeV, using a 5th-order Runge-Kutta integrator.

## Key numbers

- MAPE values for the constructed inverse potentials: 0.8 (ℓ = 0), 0.5 (ℓ = 2), and 0.4 (ℓ = 4)
- Resonance for ℓ = 2 channel: peak at 3.25 MeV with full width at half maximum (FWHM) of 2.46 MeV (experimental: 3.03 MeV, FWHM 1.51 MeV)
- Resonance for ℓ = 4 channel: peak at 12.52 MeV with FWHM of 5.91 MeV (experimental: 11.35 MeV, FWHM 3.50 MeV)
- Coulomb barrier heights from RPA: 0.0969 MeV (ℓ = 0), 4.8802 MeV (ℓ = 2), 11.1349 MeV (ℓ = 4)
- Potential depths Vd after adding centrifugal term: -10.3173 (ℓ = 0), -4.5497 (ℓ = 2), 0.8501 (ℓ = 4)
- Integration distance rf = 40 fm for all channels (compared to channel-dependent cutoffs of 5-10 fm for erf() and 3.5-20 fm for Atomic Hulthen)

## Context

This work extends the reference potential approach previously applied by Selg (2003, 2007) for molecular potentials to nuclear scattering of charged particles. It addresses limitations of earlier screened Coulomb treatments for α-α scattering: the erf()-based Coulomb interaction used by Ali-Bodmer (1966), Buck (1977), and Odsuren (2014) required abrupt cutoffs at channel-dependent distances, while the Atomic Hulthen screened Coulomb used by Laha (2017) required varying the screening radius for different ℓ-channels. The method enables construction of inverse potentials without arbitrary cutoffs and can be applied to atomic, molecular, nuclear, and astrophysical scattering studies.
