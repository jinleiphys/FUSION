# Negative heat capacity at phase-separation in macroscopic systems

> Systems with long-range as well with short-range interactions should necessarily have a convex entropy S(E) at proper phase transitions of first order, i.e. when a separation of phases occurs. Here the microcanonical heat capacity c(E)= -\frac{(\partial S/\partial E)^2}{\partial^2S/\partial E^2} is negative. This should be observable even in macroscopic systems when energy fluctuations with the surrounding world can be sufficiently suppressed.

## Key claim

Systems with both long-range and short-range interactions necessarily exhibit a convex entropy \(S(E)\) at first-order phase transitions where phase separation occurs, leading to a negative microcanonical heat capacity \(c(E) = -(\partial S/\partial E)^2 / (\partial^2 S/\partial E^2)\); this negative heat capacity should be observable even in macroscopic systems if energy fluctuations with the surroundings are sufficiently suppressed.

## Method

The paper uses a microcanonical ensemble analysis of the entropy \(S(E)\) as a function of energy, showing that at phase separation the canonical partition sum weight \(e^{S(E)-E/T}\) becomes bimodal with two peaks separated by the latent heat. Microcanonical Monte-Carlo (MMMC) simulations of sodium clusters with \(N=200\), \(1000\), and \(3000\) atoms at 1 atm external pressure are performed to compute the entropy per atom \(s(e)\), the latent heat, and the surface entropy \(\Delta S_{surf}\) associated with the inter-phase boundary.

## Key numbers

- For \(N=1000\) sodium atoms at 1 atm: transition temperature \(T_{tr} = 990\) K, latent heat per atom \(q_{lat} = 0.91\) eV, boiling entropy \(s_{boil} = 10.7\) (units not specified but consistent with entropy per atom), surface entropy \(\Delta s_{surf} = 0.56\), number of surface atoms \(N_{surf} = 98.53\), and \(\sigma/T_{tr} = 5.68\) (surface tension per surface atom divided by transition temperature).
- For \(N=200\): \(T_{tr} = 940\) K, \(q_{lat} = 0.82\) eV, \(s_{boil} = 10.1\), \(\Delta s_{surf} = 0.55\), \(N_{surf} = 39.94\), \(\sigma/T_{tr} = 2.75\).
- For \(N=3000\): \(T_{tr} = 1095\) K, \(q_{lat} = 0.94\) eV, \(s_{boil} = 9.9\), \(\Delta s_{surf} = 0.44\), \(N_{surf} = 186.6\), \(\sigma/T_{tr} = 7.07\).
- Bulk sodium values: \(T_{tr} = 1156\) K, \(q_{lat} = 0.923\) eV, \(s_{boil} = 9.267\), \(\sigma/T_{tr} = 7.41\).
- The surface entropy scales as \(\Delta S_{surf} \propto N^{2/3}\), while the depth of the convex intruder in \(S(E)\) goes to infinity in the thermodynamic limit.

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical treatment of phase transitions previously discussed by Gross (2001) and Gross (2004), and connects to early discussions of macroscopic energy fluctuations and negative specific heat in high-energy physics by Carlitz (1972). The paper enables a direct calculation of surface tension from the convex intruder in the entropy, as demonstrated by comparison with MMMC simulations of sodium clusters and the liquid-drop model parameters from Brechignac et al. (1995). It challenges the conventional canonical assumption that energy fluctuations scale as \(\delta E \propto \sqrt{N}\), showing instead that at phase separation fluctuations can be macroscopic (\(\Delta E \sim E_{lat} \propto N\)).
