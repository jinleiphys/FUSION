# On operator relations for gravitational form factors of a spin-0 hadron

> The gravitational form factors for a hadron, the form factors for the hadron matrix element of the QCD energy-momentum tensor, not only describe the coupling of the hadron with a graviton, but also serve as unique quantities for describing the shape inside the hadron reflecting dynamics of quarks and gluons, such as the internal shear forces acting on the quarks/gluons and their pressure distributions. We consider the quark contribution to the gravitational form factors for a (pseudo)scalar hadron, and derive and clarify the relations satisfied by them as direct consequences of the symmetries and the equations of motion in QCD, and connections to the generalized parton distributions. Our results reveal the connections between the gravitational form factors and the higher-twist quark-gluon correlation effects inside the hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact relations between the gravitational form factors of a spin-0 hadron (such as a pion) and the moments of twist-two, twist-three, and twist-four generalized parton distributions (GPDs), showing that the gravitational form factor associated with the "D-term" corresponds to twist-three quark-gluon interaction effects, with all quark-mass effects and hadron-mass terms retained up to twist four.

## Method

The author considers the quark contribution to the QCD energy-momentum tensor and its matrix elements for a spin-0 hadron state. Using the QCD equations of motion, the divergenceless property of the energy-momentum tensor, and parity/time-reversal invariance, the gravitational form factors are related to matrix elements of higher-twist operators. The nonlocal quark-antiquark vector operator is expanded in light-cone coordinates to define twist-two, twist-three, and twist-four GPDs, and their moments are matched to the gravitational form factor parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The forward-limit sum rule: $\sum_q {\Theta}_{2q}(0)+{\Theta}_{2g}(0)=4$, representing the total energy-momentum of the hadron
- The relation $2 \int_{-1}^1 dx x H^q_{2} (x,\eta,t)= \frac{1}{2}{\Theta}_{2q}(t)-2\eta^2 {\Theta}_{1q}(t)$ connects the second moment of the twist-two GPD to the gravitational form factors
- The relation $2 \int_{-1}^1 dx x H^q_{3}(x,\eta,t) = \eta {\Theta}_{1q}(t)$ shows the twist-three GPD moment completely determines the gravitational form factor ${\Theta}_{1q}(t)$
- The relation $2 \int_{-1}^1 dx x H_{4}^q (x,\eta,t)= \left\{\frac{t}{2}+\left(m_h^2-\frac{t}{4}\right)\eta^2\right\}{\Theta}_{1q}(t) + \frac{1}{4}\left(m_h^2-\frac{t}{4}\right){\Theta}_{2q}(t)+ \Lambda^2\overline{C}_q(t)$ connects the twist-four GPD moment to all three gravitational form factors

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for gravitational form factors of spin-0 hadrons, building on the parameterization by Pagels (1966) and the GPD formalism developed by Ji (1996), Diehl (2003), and Belitsky (2005). The results connect to the recent demonstration by Kumano (2017) that pion gravitational form factors could be extracted from Belle data on $\gamma^* \gamma \rightarrow \pi^0 \pi^0$ through generalized distribution amplitudes. The derived operator identities and moment relations provide exact constraints that can be used to test nonperturbative approaches to pion structure and to guide experimental extraction of gravitational form factors from GPD measurements.
