# Probing neutron-skin thickness with free spectator neutrons in ultracentral high-energy isobaric collisions

> We show that the yield ratio of free spectator neutrons produced in high-energy $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr to $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru collisions is a clean probe of the neutron-skin thickness of colliding nuclei and the slope parameter $L$ of the symmetry energy. The idea is demonstrated based on the proton and neutron density distributions via a state-of-the-art Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculation. Among spectator nucleons given by the Glauber model, free spectator neutrons include those from direct production that survive from clusterization as well as those from deexcitation of heavy clusters described by the popular GEMINI model. More free neutrons are produced in collisions of $^{96}$Zr nucleus due to its larger neutron skin, compared to those produced in collisions of $^{96}$Ru nucleus with a smaller neutron skin. The difference of the free spectator neutron yield is further increased with the increasing difference of the neutron-skin thickness between $^{96}$Zr and $^{96}$Ru with a larger $L$ value, and the increase in ultracentral collisions is particularly insensitive to model details and experimental uncertainties. Since the production of free spectator neutrons is not affected by the complicated dynamics in the mid-rapidity region, the ratio of their multiplicities in ultracentral isobaric collisions is a robust observable for constraining the neutron skin and $L$ value.

## Key claim
The yield ratio of free spectator neutrons in ultracentral (0-0.2% centrality) high-energy isobaric collisions of ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr to ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru is a clean probe of the neutron-skin thickness difference and the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy, provided that the ratio is taken in the ultracentral region where it becomes insensitive to cluster deexcitation uncertainties and nuclear deformation effects.

## Method
The authors performed Monte-Carlo Glauber model simulations of ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr and ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, using proton and neutron density distributions from spherical and deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (SHFB) calculations with varying slope parameter L (30 to 120 MeV). Spectator nucleons were clustered using phase-space coalescence (Δrmax = 3 fm, Δpmax = 300 MeV/c), with heavy clusters (A ≥ 4) deexcited via the GEMINI model and light clusters (A ≤ 3) formed via Wigner function coalescence. The free spectator neutron yield Nn was then calculated as the sum of directly produced neutrons and those from cluster deexcitation.

## Key numbers
- For L = 30 MeV: Δrnp = 0.147 fm in ⁹⁶Zr and 0.028 fm in ⁹⁶Ru; for L = 120 MeV: Δrnp = 0.231 fm in ⁹⁶Zr and 0.061 fm in ⁹⁶Ru.
- The predicted ratio of free spectator neutrons Nn in ultracentral ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr to ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru collisions (Nch > 340) is approximately 1.27 for L = 30 MeV and 1.31 for L = 120 MeV.
- The baseline ratio of total neutron numbers in the two collision systems is approximately 1.08.
- When L increases from 30 to 120 MeV, the isospin asymmetry δspectator in the ultracentral region increases by about 0.040 in ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr and about 0.015 in ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru.
- The influence of nuclear deformation on Δrnp for a given L is less than 8%.
- In the ultracentral region (Nch > 340), the ratio of Nn changes by about 0.01 when nuclear deformation is included, which is about 25% of the difference between L = 30 and 120 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the program of using high-energy heavy-ion collisions to probe nuclear structure, following earlier proposals to use mid-rapidity observables such as elliptic flow v2 and triangular flow v3 (Zhang 2021) or multiplicity distributions (Li 2019) to constrain neutron skin and nuclear deformation in isobaric collisions. The key advantage over prior approaches is that free spectator neutrons measured by zero-degree calorimeters are causally disconnected from the quark-gluon plasma dynamics in the mid-rapidity region, avoiding the need for sophisticated hydrodynamic modeling. The recent STAR measurement (2021) of enhanced Nn in ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr relative to ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru provides the experimental motivation, and the present work enables quantitative extraction of the neutron-skin thickness and symmetry energy slope parameter L from that data.
