# Multi-step effects in subcoulomb breakup

> Following earlier one-step calculations, we explore the contributions of multi-step effects for the breakup of low energy $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb within a coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) formalism. The Coulomb multi-step differential cross section is significantly reduced for all angles, the largest effect being the destructive interference of nuclear couplings. The nuclear peak, at around $80^\circ$ in the one-step calculation for $^{58}$Ni, virtually disappears.

## Key claim

Multi-step effects in subcoulomb breakup of $^8$B are very strong, producing significant reductions of the cross section compared with first-order theory; the prior-form DWBA overestimates nuclear breakup probabilities at sub-Coulomb incident energy, and the reduction is principally due to continuum-continuum couplings, not just depletion of the elastic channel.

## Method

The authors used the coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) formalism to calculate breakup of $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni at 26 MeV and on $^{208}$Pb, including monopole, dipole and quadrupole contributions for both nuclear and Coulomb mechanisms. Because the Born series diverged rapidly at second order, they employed Pade approximants to resum the series for the S-matrix, and verified that this resummation converges to the full coupled-channel result in a reduced subspace where exact calculations were possible. Continuum states were discretised into energy bins for s, p, d, and f partial waves up to 3 MeV excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- For $^8$B breakup on $^{58}$Ni at 26 MeV, the nuclear peak from 1-step calculations at approximately $80^\circ$ virtually disappears in the CDCC calculation, suffering a reduction to 1/6 of its peak value.
- The Coulomb differential cross section peak at approximately $20^\circ$ is reduced by about 10% when multi-step effects are included.
- For the $^{58}$Ni target, calculations used radii up to $R_{coup}=300$ fm and partial waves up to $L_{max}=600$ for convergence, with each energy bin calculated using $R_{bin}=50$ fm.
- For the $^{208}$Pb target, the nuclear contribution is zero up to $50^\circ$ and becomes important only at backward angles.
- Using the Kim model for $^8$B structure produces a 30% increase in the total differential cross section compared to the Esbensen model.

## Context

This work extends earlier one-step calculations by Nunes and Thompson (1998) and Shyam and Thompson (1998) for $^8$B breakup, and addresses the need to include final-state interactions and continuum-continuum couplings that were omitted in previous treatments. The results show that the Coulomb dissociation method for determining the $S_{17}$ astrophysical factor requires careful treatment of multi-step effects, and that for a $^{208}$Pb target the experiment seems more promising than with the lighter target, although strong destructive Coulomb-Coulomb interference must be accounted for. The work enables future quantitative extraction of electromagnetic components from breakup measurements, but with model dependence that cannot be avoided.
