# Nuclear matter, nuclei, and neutron stars in hadron and quark-hadron models

> We develop a unified model of hadrons and quarks. Within this approach we investigate the phase structure of the model as function of temperature and chemical potential. Computing the equation of state of cold matter we determine neutron and hybrid star masses and radii. In an extension of the investigation we consider the cooling behavior of the compact stars and derive a general relation between the star's mass and rotation and its cooling behavior. Finally we study the effect of Delta resonances for star matter, especially with respect to possible solutions of stars with small radii.

## Key claim

The paper develops a unified hadron-quark model that can describe both nuclear matter ground-state properties and the quark-gluon phase, and finds that neutron stars with maximum masses around 2 solar masses can contain mixed baryon-quark cores, while purely baryonic stars including Delta resonances can reproduce small radii (~10 km) suggested by observational data without requiring exotic quark star solutions.

## Method

The authors couple an effective chiral flavor-SU(3) hadron model with a modified Polyakov-loop Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) quark model, solving the equations in mean-field approximation. They fit the model couplings to reproduce nuclear saturation properties (density, binding energy, compressibility, asymmetry energy) and hyperon potentials, then compute the equation of state for cold matter in beta equilibrium. Using this equation of state, they solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for non-rotating spherical neutron stars and perform cooling simulations incorporating neutrino emission processes.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, binding energy per nucleon B/A = -16.00 MeV, nucleon effective mass M*_N = 0.67 M_N, compressibility K = 297.32 MeV, asymmetry energy E_sym = 32.50 MeV
- Hyperon potentials: U_Λ = -28.00 MeV, U_Σ = 5.35 MeV, U_Ξ = -18.36 MeV
- Average binding energy error for nuclei with 50 or more nucleons: ε_E (A > 50) ≈ 0.21%
- Critical end-point temperature: T_c ≈ 167 MeV, critical chemical potential: μ_c ≈ 354 MeV
- Phase transition to quarks at zero temperature occurs at 4 times nuclear matter saturation density
- Maximum neutron star mass excluding quarks: about 2.1 solar masses
- Maximum neutron star mass including quarks: about 2 solar masses
- Good agreement with cooling data for star masses below 1.2 solar masses (assuming non-rotating stars)
- Delta resonance vector coupling strength r_v = 0.9 reproduces observational data for small-radius stars

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral model approaches (Schramm and collaborators, 2000s) by unifying hadronic and quark descriptions with a Polyakov loop potential, building on lattice QCD results for the phase diagram (Fodor and Katz, 2004). The model enables simultaneous study of nuclear structure, neutron star masses and radii, and cooling behavior, including the role of Delta resonances. The results for small-radius stars address observational analyses by Ozel and collaborators (2010) and suggest that Delta baryons can dominate matter above 4 times nuclear saturation density, offering an alternative to quark star interpretations.
