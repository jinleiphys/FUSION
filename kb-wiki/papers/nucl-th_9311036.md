# Polarization of the nuclear medium and RPA-type calculations in $K^+$ scattering from nuclei

> In the calculation of the $K^+$-nucleus cross sections, the coupling of the mesons exchanged between the $K^+$ and the target nucleons to the polarization of the Fermi sea has been taken into account. This polarization has been calculated in the one-loop approximation but summed up to all orders (RPA-type calculation). This effect is found to be rather important but does not improve the agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The coupling of the $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons exchanged between the $K^+$ and target nucleons to the polarization of the Fermi sea, calculated in the one-loop approximation summed to all orders (RPA-type), produces a significant effect on the $K^+$-nucleus cross section ratio $R_T$, but this effect does not improve the agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors constructed an optical potential for $K^+$-nucleus elastic scattering by folding nuclear densities with a density-dependent $K^+$-nucleon amplitude derived from the Bonn boson exchange model (solution B1). The in-medium modification of the $K^+$-nucleon amplitude was implemented by replacing the free meson propagators for $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons with RPA-summed propagators that include one-loop polarization insertions, accounting for $\sigma$-$\omega$ mixing. The $K^+$-nucleus total cross sections were obtained by solving a relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space and applying the optical theorem, with the ratio $R_T = \sigma_{tot}(K^+-^{12}C) / [6 \cdot \sigma_{tot}(K^+-d)]$ computed for comparison with experiment.

## Key numbers

- The real parts of the in-medium masses $m_-$ and $m_+$ are approximately 7% lower than the $\sigma$-mass and 10% higher than the $\omega$-mass, respectively, for densities above 20% of saturation density.
- The imaginary parts of $m_+$ and $m_-$ grow from zero in vacuum to approximately 200 MeV at saturation density.
- The ratio $R_T$ with polarization effects is on average $\sim$5% lower than that calculated with the free-space $K^+N$ interaction, with a reduction of $\sim$10% at 400 MeV/c and $\sim$2% at 900 MeV/c.
- The effective nucleon mass at saturation is decreased by 15% relative to the free mass, following $M^*(\rho) = M (1 - 0.15 \rho/\rho_0)$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Brown and collaborators and by Cohen and collaborators, which showed that $K^+$-nucleus cross sections are sensitive to in-medium effective masses of $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons. The present calculation goes beyond the simple effective-mass approximation by treating the dressing of mesons as a coupling to particle-hole excitations of the Fermi sea using RPA-type summation, following the formalism of Celenza, Pantziris, and Shakin. The result that this more complete treatment does not resolve the longstanding discrepancy between theory and experiment for $K^+$-nucleus scattering suggests that other medium effects, such as modifications of nucleon structure or higher-order correlations, must be explored.
