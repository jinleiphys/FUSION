# Matching Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas Models and Perturbative QCD to Lattice Calculations

> We match three hadronic equations of state at low energy densities to a perturbatively computed equation of state of quarks and gluons at high energy densities. One of them includes all known hadrons treated as point particles, which approximates attractive interactions among hadrons. The other two include, in addition, repulsive interactions in the form of excluded volumes occupied by the hadrons. A switching function is employed to make the crossover transition from one phase to another without introducing a thermodynamic phase transition. A chi-square fit to accurate lattice calculations with temperature $100 < T < 1000$ MeV determines the parameters. These parameters quantify the behavior of the QCD running gauge coupling and the hard core radius of protons and neutrons, which turns out to be $0.62 \pm 0.04$ fm. The most physically reasonable models include the excluded volume effect. Not only do they include the effects of attractive and repulsive interactions among hadrons, but they also achieve better agreement with lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state. The equations of state constructed in this paper do not result in a phase transition, at least not for the temperatures and baryon chemical potentials investigated. It remains to be seen how well these equations of state will represent experimental data on high energy heavy ion collisions when implemented in hydrodynamic simulations.

# Matching Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas Models and Perturbative QCD to Lattice Calculations

## Key claim

The authors construct smooth crossover equations of state by matching hadronic models (including excluded volume repulsive interactions) to perturbative QCD using a switching function, finding that the excluded volume models achieve better agreement with lattice QCD data than a point-particle hadron gas, but the resulting equations of state do not produce a phase transition for the temperatures and baryon chemical potentials investigated, and their success in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions remains to be tested.

## Method

Three hadronic equations of state were constructed: one treating all known hadrons as point particles (approximating attractive interactions via the Dashen-Ma-Bernstein approach), and two implementing repulsive interactions via thermodynamically self-consistent excluded volume models (model I, where excluded volume is proportional to particle energy; model II, where excluded volume is proportional to particle mass). A smooth switching function S(T,μ) = exp(-θ) with θ = [(T/T₀)^r + (μ/μ₀)^r]⁻¹ was used to interpolate between the hadronic and perturbative QCD equations of state without introducing a thermodynamic phase transition. A chi-square fit to lattice QCD results for pressure and trace anomaly at zero chemical potential over the temperature range 100 < T < 1000 MeV determined the parameters: the switching function parameters (r, T₀), the perturbative QCD scale parameters (C_M, C_S), and the excluded volume energy density parameter ε₀.

## Key numbers

- Hard core radius of protons and neutrons: 0.62 ± 0.04 fm, inferred from the excluded volume parameter fits
- Best-fit ε₀ for model I: 1.149 GeV/fm³ (corresponding to proton radius 0.580 fm)
- Best-fit ε₀ for model II: 797 MeV/fm³ (corresponding to proton radius 0.655 fm)
- Best-fit switching function temperature scale T₀ for excluded volume models: approximately 175-178 MeV (for r=4 or r=5)
- Best-fit T₀ for point particle model: approximately 145-157 MeV (for r=4 or r=5)
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for best excluded volume fits: 0.342 (exI, r=5) and 0.343 (exII, r=5)
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for best point particle fit: 0.558 (r=4)
- Perturbative QCD fit parameters (T > 200 MeV only): C_M = 3.293, C_S = 1.509, with χ²/dof = 1.397 (using Λ_MSbar = 290 MeV)
- Switching function parameter μ₀ set to 3πT₀

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas approach by incorporating repulsive interactions via excluded volume models originally formulated by Hagedorn (HR1), Hagedorn and Rafelski (H1), and Gorenstein, Petrov, and Zinovjev (GPZ1), and specifically builds on model I from Kapusta and Olive (KO1) and model II from Rischke, Gorenstein, Stoecker, and Greiner (RGSG1). The authors match these to state-of-the-art perturbative QCD calculations (up to order α_s³ ln α_s) and fit to lattice QCD results from Borsanyi et al. (2010) at zero chemical potential. The resulting equations of state enable parameter-free predictions at finite baryon chemical potential (compared to lattice data from Borsanyi et al. (2012) at μ = 400 MeV) and can be implemented in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions, where the hadronic composition at freeze-out is directly available from the model.
