# Isospin dependence of the three-nucleon force

> We classify $A$--nucleon forces according to their isospin dependence and discuss the most general isospin structure of the three--nucleon force. We derive the leading and subleading isospin--breaking corrections to the three--nucleon force using the framework of chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper classifies A-nucleon forces by their isospin dependence and derives the leading and subleading isospin-breaking corrections to the three-nucleon force using chiral effective field theory, with the hedge that the classification scheme is a quantum-mechanical exercise that does not reveal underlying dynamics, while the explicit calculation is based on specific power-counting rules for strong and electromagnetic isospin violation.

## Method

The authors first generalize the Henley-Miller classification of two-nucleon forces to A-nucleon systems (A ≥ 3), distinguishing class (I) isospin-symmetric, class (II) charge-symmetric but isospin-breaking, and class (III) both isospin- and charge-symmetry-breaking forces. They then derive the leading (order (q/Λ)^4) isospin-violating three-nucleon force contributions from diagrams involving the nucleon mass shift (proton-neutron mass difference) and from subleading vertices in the chiral effective Lagrangian, using the method of unitary transformation to obtain momentum-space expressions.

## Key numbers

- The quark mass ratio parameter ε ≡ (m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) ∼ 1/3, based on light quark masses in a modified MS scheme at 1 GeV.
- The experimentally known pion mass difference M_{π±} - M_{π0} = 4.6 MeV fixes the low-energy constant C = 5.9 × 10^{-5} GeV^4.
- The strong contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is (δm)^{str.} = -4 c_5 ε M_π^2 = (-2.05 ± 0.3) MeV.
- The electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is (δm)^{em.} = -f_2 e^2 F_π^2 = (0.7 ± 0.3) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the classification of isospin dependence from two-nucleon forces (Henley and Miller) to three-nucleon and A-nucleon systems, and fills a gap in chiral effective field theory where three-nucleon forces in the isospin limit had been analyzed (Weinberg, van Kolck, Epelbaum) but isospin violation in the three-nucleon force had not been addressed. The results enable systematic inclusion of isospin-breaking effects in few-nucleon calculations, such as the binding energy difference between ³H and ³He, and complement related work on charge symmetry breaking in the three-nucleon system by Friar, Payne and van Kolck.
