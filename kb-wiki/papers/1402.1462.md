# Flavor Structure of the Nucleon Sea from Lattice QCD

> We present the first direct lattice calculation of the isovector sea-quark parton distributions using the formalism developed recently by one of the authors. We use $N_f=2+1+1$ HISQ lattice gauge ensembles (generated by MILC Collaboration) and clover valence fermions with pion mass 310 MeV. We are able to obtain the qualitative features of the nucleon sea flavor structure even at this large pion mass: We observe violation of the Gottfried sum rule, indicating $\overline{d}(x) > \overline{u}(x)$; the helicity distribution obeys $Δ\overline{u}(x) > Δ\overline{d}(x)$, which is consistent with the STAR data at large and small leptonic pseudorapidity.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first direct lattice QCD calculation of the isovector sea-quark parton distributions, but the authors explicitly state that "the lattice systematics are not yet fully under control" and the results are obtained at a pion mass of 310 MeV rather than the physical pion mass, so the comparison with experimental data is qualitative rather than quantitative.

## Method

The authors use the large-momentum effective field theory (LaMET) formalism to compute Euclidean quasi-distributions on the lattice and then extract physical light-cone parton distributions. They employ \(N_f=2+1+1\) HISQ gauge ensembles from the MILC Collaboration with clover valence fermions on a \(24^3 \times 64\) lattice with spacing \(a \approx 0.12\) fm and box size \(L \approx 3\) fm. The quasi-distributions are calculated at nucleon momenta \(P_z\) from 0.43 to 1.29 GeV, and the physical distributions are obtained by applying one-loop perturbative matching and nucleon-mass corrections, followed by extrapolation in \(1/P_z^2\) to account for dynamical higher-twist effects.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: \(M_\pi \approx 310\) MeV
- Lattice spacing: \(a \approx 0.12\) fm
- Box size: \(L \approx 3\) fm
- Nucleon momenta used: \(P_z = 0.43, 0.86, 1.29\) GeV (corresponding to 1, 2, and 3 units of \(2\pi/L\))
- Gottfried sum rule violation: \(\int_{0}^{\infty} dx\,(\overline{u}(x)-\overline{d}(x)) = 0.14(5)\)
- Polarized sea asymmetry: \(\Delta \overline{u} - \Delta \overline{d} = 0.24(6)\)
- Strong coupling used for matching: \(\alpha_s = 0.20 \pm 0.04\)
- Renormalization scale: \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) \(\mu = 2\) GeV
- Number of measurements: 1383 (among 461 lattice configurations)

## Context

This work extends the LaMET formalism proposed by Ji (2013) to compute full \(x\)-dependence of parton distributions directly from lattice QCD, which was previously limited to lower moments. The results are compared with global analyses by MSTW (2009) and CTEQ-JLab (CJ12, 2012) for unpolarized distributions, and with DSSV09 for polarized distributions. The Gottfried sum rule violation is compared with experimental results from NMC (1994) giving \(0.147(39)\) at \(Q^2=4\) GeV\(^2\) and HERMES (1998) giving \(0.16(3)\) at \(Q^2=2.3\) GeV\(^2\). This work enables future lattice calculations at physical pion mass with improved systematics to make quantitative comparisons with experimental data.
