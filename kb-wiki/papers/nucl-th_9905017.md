# Relativistic Calculations of Induced Polarization in $^{12}C$(e, e' \vec{p})$ Reactions

> Relativistic calculations of the induced proton polarization in quasifree electron scattering on $^{12}C$ are presented. Good agreement with the experimental data of Woo et al. is obtained. The relativistic calculations yield a somewhat better description of the data than the non-relativistic ones. Differences between the two approaches are more pronounced at larger missing momenta suggesting further experimental work in this region.

## Key claim

Relativistic calculations of induced proton polarization in quasifree electron scattering on $^{12}C$ achieve better agreement with the experimental data of Woo et al. than non-relativistic calculations, with differences between the two approaches becoming more pronounced at larger missing momenta, suggesting further experimental work in this region.

## Method

The authors performed full relativistic calculations of the $^{12}C(e, e' \vec{p})$ reaction amplitude using the one-photon exchange model with the de Forest cc2 prescription, without including Coulomb distortion in the leptonic part. Bound state wave functions were generated using Dirac Hartree potentials from Blunden and Iqbal and phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials, while proton optical potentials were taken from Cooper et al. using both energy-dependent and energy-plus-mass-dependent parameterizations. The induced proton polarization along the y-axis was computed and multiplied by -1 to conform with the sign convention of Woo et al.

## Key numbers

- Incident electron energy: 579 MeV, with constant q-omega kinematics
- Missing momentum range explored: 0 to 250 MeV/c (experimental data) and calculations extended beyond to 300 MeV/c
- For $1p_{3/2}$ knockout, the relativistic model provides a 10% smaller polarization at missing momenta around 20 MeV/c compared to non-relativistic calculations
- Non-relativistic calculations with the EEI potential produce a large negative polarization in the region $p_m$ = 250-300 MeV/c for $1s_{1/2}$ knockout, while relativistic calculations indicate the minimum would be at missing momenta larger than 300 MeV/c
- Woods-Saxon potentials reproduce the experimental binding energy and provide an rms radius for the bound state within one percent of that found from Hartree potentials

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic calculations of proton polarization for the $^{16}O(e, e' \vec{p})$ reaction reported by Johansson and Sherif (1997) to the $^{12}C$ target, and directly compares with the experimental data of Woo et al. (1998) and their non-relativistic DWIA calculations using the effective momentum approximation. The relativistic calculations use the same optical potentials as the non-relativistic ones from Cooper et al. (1993), isolating the difference between relativistic and non-relativistic treatments of the reaction. The results suggest that measurements at higher missing momenta could strongly test both models and clarify the role of two-body currents in these reactions.
