# Evolution of elliptic and triangular flow as a function of collision energy in a hybrid model

> We study the collision energy dependence of elliptic flow v_2 and triangular flow v_3 in Au+Au collisions within the energy range sqrt(s_{NN}) = 5-200 GeV, utilizing a transport + hydrodynamics hybrid model. The transport part is described by the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) approach, combined with an intermediate (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamical evolution phase using a chiral model equation of state. We find the decrease of v_2 produced by hydrodynamics at lower collision energies partially compensated by the transport dynamics. This does not apply to v_3, which falls to 0 in midcentral collisions at sqrt(s_{NN}) = 5 GeV. We conclude that the triangular flow provides the clearer signal for the formation of low-viscous fluid in heavy ion collisions.

# Evolution of elliptic and triangular flow as a function of collision energy in a hybrid model

## Key claim

The hybrid model shows that the weak collision-energy dependence of elliptic flow v₂ arises because hadronic transport dynamics compensate for the diminishing hydrodynamic contribution at lower beam energies, but this compensation does not occur for triangular flow v₃, which falls to zero in midcentral collisions at √sNN = 5 GeV. The authors conclude that triangular flow provides a clearer signal than elliptic flow for the formation of low-viscous fluid in heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport approach for the initial non-equilibrium and final hadronic stages with a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution for the intermediate hot and dense phase, employing a chiral model equation of state applicable at finite baryon density. They simulated Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies √sNN from 5 to 200 GeV, computed elliptic flow v₂ and triangular flow v₃ using the event plane method, and compared the contributions from the transport and hydrodynamic phases separately. The transition from hydrodynamics to transport (particlization) was set at a critical energy density of 2ε₀, where ε₀ = 146 MeV/fm³.

## Key numbers

- Triangular flow v₃ falls to approximately 0 in midcentral collisions (b = 6.7-8.2 fm) at √sNN = 5 GeV, while at √sNN ≥ 27 GeV it reaches approximately 0.02.
- In central collisions (b = 0-3.4 fm), v₃ increases only slightly from below 0.01 to 0.015 with increasing collision energy from 5 to 200 GeV.
- The hydrodynamic starting time t_start drops from 5.19 fm at √sNN = 5 GeV to 1.23 fm at √sNN = 19.6 GeV.
- The critical energy density for particlization is 2ε₀ = 292 MeV/fm³, with ε₀ = 146 MeV/fm³ representing nuclear ground state energy density.
- The switching temperature at √sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions is approximately T ≈ 154 MeV.
- At √sNN = 200 GeV, the v₃ values agree with experimental data from STAR (Adamczyk 2013).
- In midcentral collisions (b = 8.2-9.4 fm), the contribution from hadronic rescatterings to v₂ is about 10%.

## Context

This work extends previous hybrid model studies of elliptic flow by Petersen (2009, 2010) by implementing a new Cooper-Frye hypersurface finder and replacing reaction plane analysis with the event plane method. The study addresses the surprisingly weak energy dependence of v₂ observed by STAR (Adamczyk 2012) between 7.7 and 39 GeV, and the clearer energy dependence of v₃ seen in preliminary results by Pandit (2012) between 27 and 200 GeV. The results suggest that the hadron resonance gas is too viscous to produce v₃ from initial state fluctuations at low energies, implying that larger quantities of low-viscous matter may exist at lower collision energies than expected, while also indicating that ideal hydrodynamics may reach its lower limit of applicability at √sNN = 5 GeV.
