# Energy Loss of High $\pT{}$ Hadrons by Final Hadronic State

> In the context of the `jet quenching' phenomena typically materialization of the jet is assumed to take place in vacuum outside the reaction zone. On the other hand quantum mechanical estimates give a hadronization time on the order of only a few fm/c for jets materializing into hadrons with transverse momenta of $\pT{} \leq 10 GeV$, which thus should well take place inside the fireball. Typical (in-)elastic collisions of these high $\pT{}$ particles with the bulk of hadrons of the fireball have a rather low invariant mass and are thus nonperturbative. An analysis within an opacity expansion in the number of collisions by means of the FRITIOF collision scheme for various hadrons will be presented. It shows that late hadronic collisions can substantially account for the modification of the high $\pT{} $-spectrum observed for central collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

Late hadronic collisions of pre-hadrons materializing inside the fireball can substantially account for the modification of the high transverse momentum spectrum observed for central collisions at RHIC, but this finding signals that various different mechanisms (including partonic energy loss in a deconfined phase) can currently explain the same data, so definitive conclusions about a quark-gluon plasma require disentangling these final-state hadronic effects.

## Method

The authors used the FRITIOF Monte Carlo collision scheme within an opacity expansion in the number of collisions to model inelastic interactions of high transverse momentum hadrons with low-momentum target hadrons (primarily rho mesons at rest) in the late hadronic stage of a heavy-ion collision. Initial hadron distributions were generated by PYTHIA v6.2, tuned to UA1 p-pbar data and scaled by binary collision numbers for different centralities, and the resulting spectra after zero, one, two, or three hadronic collisions were compared to preliminary PHENIX data for charged hadrons and neutral pions.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization time estimate: t_F ≈ 1-1.2 × (E/GeV) fm/c for leading hadrons with transverse momentum ≤ 10 GeV
- Fireball transverse size: R ≈ 8 fm + 0.5 × ct, where t is local proper time
- Hadron density in late fireball: changes from about 1 fm^{-3} to 0.1 fm^{-3}
- Mean free path of fast hadron: λ ≈ 1-10 fm
- Total cross section of two mesons: σ_0 ≈ 10-15 mb
- For p_T = 10 GeV projectile on a pion target at rest: √s < 2 GeV; on a rho target: √s > 2.5 GeV for p_T > 3 GeV
- Binary collision numbers used: N_coll = 2.4 for 80-92% centrality, 12.6 for 60-80% centrality, 945 for 0-5% centrality
- One single final inelastic collision via FRITIOF quantitatively explains the modification of the π_0 momentum distribution for p_T > 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the discussion of jet quenching by proposing that final-state hadronic interactions, rather than (or in addition to) partonic energy loss in a deconfined medium, can explain the high-p_T suppression observed by PHENIX at RHIC. It contrasts with the standard jet quenching picture of Wang (1998) and the GLV formalism of Levai (2001), which attribute the suppression to gluon radiation from partons in a colored medium, as well as with the suggestion by Schaffner-Bielich et al. (2001) that the spectra reflect a direct remnant of gluon-saturated nuclear distributions with no final-state interactions. The analysis enables future work to disentangle hadronic from partonic contributions before drawing quantitative conclusions about a quark-gluon plasma phase.
