# Three-Body Analysis of Incoherent Photoproduction of $η$ Mesons on the Deuteron near Threshold

> The importance of three-body dynamics in the $ηnp$ system in elastic and inelastic $η$-deuteron scattering as well as coherent and incoherent $η$ photoproduction on the deuteron in the energy region from threshold up to 30 MeV above has been investigated. It is shown that a restriction to first order rescattering with respect to the $NN$- and $ηN$-final state interactions, i.e., restriction to rescattering in the two-body subsystems, does not give a sufficiently accurate approximation to the s-wave reaction amplitude and that higher order terms, as described by the three-body dynamics give very substantial contributions.

## Key claim

Near-threshold incoherent photoproduction of η mesons on the deuteron requires a full three-body treatment of the ηnp system; restricting final-state interactions to first-order rescattering in the two-body ηN and NN subsystems fails to reproduce the observed enhancement of the total cross section right above threshold.

## Method

The authors solved coupled AGS three-body equations for the ηnp system, including s-wave interactions only, with separable potentials for the NN and ηN channels. The ηN t-matrix was taken in an isobar approach with intermediate excitation of the S₁₁(1535) resonance, and the NN interaction used a Yamaguchi-type separable form derived from the Bonn OBEPQ potential. The three-body calculation was compared to impulse approximation (IA) and first-order rescattering calculations for elastic and inelastic η-deuteron scattering as well as coherent and incoherent η photoproduction on the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- ηN scattering length: a_{ηN} = 0.75 + i 0.27 fm
- S₁₁(1535) resonance mass: M_{N*} = 1535 MeV
- Helicity amplitude for proton: A_{1/2}^p = 0.104 GeV^{-1/2}
- ηNN* coupling constant: g_{ηNN*} = 2.10
- πNN* coupling constant: g_{πNN*} = 1.19
- Energy range studied: from threshold up to 30 MeV above threshold

## Context

This work extends prior studies of η photoproduction on the deuteron by Fix and Arenhoevel (1997) that used only first-order rescattering, and by Sibirtsev et al. (2002) whose first-order rescattering calculation the authors argue is unreliable near threshold. The three-body approach reproduces the near-threshold enhancement seen in experimental data from Krusche et al. (1995) and Hejny et al. (2002), and enables extraction of the ηN interaction from incoherent η production, provided a full three-body treatment is used. Open questions include the inclusion of meson exchange currents and realistic NN interactions with tensor force and deuteron D-wave.
