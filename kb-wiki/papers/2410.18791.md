# Transport coefficients of chiral fluid dynamics using low-energy effective models

> We investigate the first-order transport coefficients of a fluid made of quasiparticles with a temperature-dependent mass extracted from chiral models. We describe this system using an effective kinetic theory, given by the relativistic Boltzmann equation coupled to a temperature-dependent background field determined from the thermal masses. We then simplify the collision term using the relaxation time approximation and implement a Chapman-Enskog expansion to calculate all first-order transport coefficients. In particular, we compute the bulk and shear viscosities using thermal masses extracted from the linear sigma model coupled with constituent quarks and the NJL model.

## Key claim

The paper computes first-order transport coefficients (bulk and shear viscosities) for a fluid of quasiparticles with temperature-dependent masses from chiral models, using an improved relaxation time approximation that respects energy-momentum conservation even when the relaxation time has an energy dependence, a condition that previous Anderson-Witting approximations violated.

## Method

The authors model the quark fluid using the relativistic Boltzmann equation with a temperature-dependent mass extracted from either the linear sigma model coupled with constituent quarks (LSMq) or the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at mean-field level. They simplify the collision term using a recently developed relaxation time approximation that includes counterterms to enforce conservation laws, then perform a Chapman-Enskog expansion to first order in gradients to obtain expressions for the shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and energy deviation coefficient. The relaxation time is parametrized as \(\tau_R = t_R (E_{\mathbf{p}}/T)^\gamma\), with \(\gamma\) a phenomenological parameter.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity: in the massless limit \(M(T)/T \to 0\), the asymptotic normalized shear viscosity is \(\eta / [t_R (\epsilon_0 + P_0)] \sim \Gamma(\gamma+5)/120\), independent of the chiral model.
* Bulk viscosity: in the massless limit, the normalized bulk viscosity scales as \(\zeta / [t_R (\epsilon_0 + P_0)] \sim -(1/864) M(T) (d/dT)[M(T)/T] \times (\gamma^4 + 10\gamma^3 + 11\gamma^2 - 22\gamma + 120) \Gamma(\gamma+1)\), vanishing as chiral symmetry is restored.
* Energy deviation coefficient: in the massless limit, \(\chi / [t_R (\epsilon_0 + P_0)] \sim (1/288) M(T) (d/dT)[M(T)/T] \times (\gamma^4 + 10\gamma^3 + 11\gamma^2 - 22\gamma + 120) \Gamma(\gamma+1)\), also vanishing at high temperature.
* LSMq parameters: pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, sigma mass \(m_\sigma = 600\) MeV, coupling \(g = 3.3\), \(\lambda^2 = 20\).
* NJL model parameters: coupling \(G = 5.496\) GeV\(^{-2}\), current quark mass \(m_0 = 5.5\) MeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 631\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle Boltzmann approach of Rocha (2022) by incorporating temperature-dependent masses from chiral effective models (LSMq and NJL) into transport coefficient calculations, going beyond earlier treatments that neglected phase transition effects or used the Anderson-Witting relaxation time approximation which violates conservation laws for energy-dependent relaxation times (Rocha, 2021). The results enable more realistic fluid-dynamical simulations of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, particularly near the chiral crossover, by providing transport coefficients that reflect the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate.
