# Dilepton and $ϕ$ meson production in elementary and nuclear collisions at the NICA fixed target experiment

> We argue that the NICA fixed target experiment will be able to provide very important new experimental data on dilepton and $ϕ$ meson production in the basically undiscovered energy domain between the SIS and SPS energies. Experimental information about elementary cross sections in this energy region is an essential ingredient of models of nuclear collisions in the same energy range.

## Key claim

The NICA fixed target experiment will provide crucial new experimental data on dilepton and φ meson production in the energy domain between SIS and SPS energies, which is currently undiscovered; however, reliable interpretation of nuclear collision data depends on understanding elementary cross sections, in-medium modifications, and reaction dynamics, making experimental input essential for calibrating theoretical models in this transition regime.

## Method

The authors use a BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) type hadronic transport model to predict dilepton invariant mass spectra and φ meson production probabilities for central Au+Au collisions at 10 AGeV laboratory kinetic energy. Elementary cross sections for hadronic reactions are obtained from one boson exchange models, with contributions from higher baryon resonances included via extrapolation from nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The model separates contributions from direct vector meson decays, Dalitz decays, and various production channels for φ mesons.

## Key numbers

- Central Au+Au collisions at 10 AGeV laboratory kinetic energy: above 0.6 GeV dilepton mass, the spectrum is dominated by direct decay of neutral vector mesons ρ, ω, and φ
- At 10 AGeV, the highest baryon densities reach approximately 6 times normal nuclear density
- Probability of φ meson creation in Au+Au central collisions at 10 AGeV: BB → NNφ = 8.9 × 10⁻³, πB → Nφ = 1.44 × 10⁻³, (ρ,ω)B → Nφ = 6.30 × 10⁻⁴, K⁺K⁻ → φ = 8.57 × 10⁻²
- φ meson production is dominated by K⁺K⁻ annihilations (8.57 × 10⁻² probability)
- At NICA energies, dense medium reaches 6–10 times nuclear matter density

## Context

This work extends dilepton and φ meson studies from lower energies explored by HADES at GSI SIS and higher energies at SPS and RHIC, into the undiscovered energy region between them. The authors note that the resonance dominance model used at SIS energies (Wolf 1990, Wolf 1993) becomes inadequate above that range, and effective field theory models (Zetenyi-Wolf) have been applied to pion-nucleon collisions but are difficult to implement in transport codes. The paper enables future experimental measurements at NICA to test and calibrate theoretical models in this transition regime, and to study kaon and antikaon properties in dense matter relevant to neutron star interiors (Blaschke-Weber).
