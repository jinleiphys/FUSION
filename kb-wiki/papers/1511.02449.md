# Proximity of $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$ to the scalar glueball

> Within a nonlinear chiral Lagrangian framework, the underlying mixings among quark-antiquark, four-quark and glue components of $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$ are studied in a global picture that includes all isosinglet scalar mesons below 2 GeV. The quark components are introduced in the Lagrangian in terms of two separate nonets (a quark-antiquark nonet and a four-quark nonet) which can mix with each other and with a scalar glueball. The free parameters of the Lagrangian are studied by a simultaneous fit to more than 20 experimental data and constraints on the mass spectrum, decay widths, and decay ratios of the isosinglet scalars below 2 GeV. Moreover, constraints on the mass spectrum and decay widths of isodoublet and isovector scalars below 2 GeV as well as pion-pion scattering amplitude are also taken into account. The insights gained in this global picture, due to the complexities of the mixings as well as the experimental uncertainties, are mainly qualitative but are relatively robust, and reveal that the lowest scalar glueball hides between $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$, resulting in a considerable mixing with various quark components of these two states. The overall current experimental and theoretical uncertainties do not allow to pin down the exact glue components of isosinglet states, nevertheless it is shown that the $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$ have the highest glue component. While this global study does not allow precision predictions for each individual state, it provides useful "family" correlations among the isosinglet states that are found insightful in probing the substructure of all scalars, in general, and the isosinglets, in particular. The overall estimate of the scalar glueball mass is found to be $1.58 \pm 0.18$ GeV.

## Key claim

Within a nonlinear chiral Lagrangian framework, the lowest scalar glueball hides between the $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$ states, resulting in considerable mixing with various quark components of these two states, though the overall current experimental and theoretical uncertainties do not allow pinning down the exact glue components of isosinglet states.

## Method

The authors extended previous work on isosinglet mixing by performing a simultaneous fit to mass and decay properties of isosinglet scalar mesons using an iterative Monte Carlo simulation to explore the 14-dimensional parameter space of the model. The Lagrangian includes two separate nonets (a quark-antiquark nonet above 1 GeV and a four-quark nonet below 1 GeV) that can mix with each other and with a scalar glueball, with parameters determined by fitting to more than 20 experimental data points including mass spectrum, decay widths, and decay ratios of isosinglet scalars below 2 GeV, as well as constraints on isodoublet and isovector scalars and pion-pion scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Scalar glueball mass estimate: $1.58 \pm 0.18$ GeV
- Mass of $f_0(1500)$: 1502 MeV (WA102 collaboration value used in simulations I and II)
- Mass of $f_0(1710)$: 1727 MeV (WA102 collaboration value used in simulations I and II)
- Mass of $f_0(500)$: 400--550 MeV (PDG range)
- Mass of $f_0(980)$: $990 \pm 20$ MeV
- Ratio $\Gamma[f_0(1500)\rightarrow\pi\pi] / \Gamma[f_0(1500)\rightarrow\eta\eta] = 5.56 \pm 0.93$ (WA102)
- Ratio $\Gamma[f_0(1710)\rightarrow\pi\pi] / \Gamma[f_0(1710)\rightarrow K\bar{K}] = 0.20 \pm 0.03$ (WA102)
- Ratio $\Gamma[f_0(1710)\rightarrow\eta\eta] / \Gamma[f_0(1710)\rightarrow K\bar{K}] = 0.48 \pm 0.19$ (WA102)
- Bare masses from isodoublet/isotriplet analysis: $m[a_0] = m[a'_0] = 1.24$ GeV, $m[K_0] = 1.06$ GeV, $m[K'_0] = 1.31$ GeV, mixing parameter $\gamma = 0.58$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the mixing framework developed by Mec (2002) for isodoublet and isotriplet scalars, and further elaborated by Fariborz (2004, 2006) for isosinglet states, by performing a simultaneous 14-parameter fit rather than separate fits. The study builds on lattice QCD predictions from Morningstar (1999) that the lowest scalar glueball has mass 1730 (50)(80) MeV in the quenched approximation, and addresses the need to understand unquenching effects through mixing with quark states. The results enable future investigations of "family" correlations among isosinglet states and provide constraints for identifying the scalar glueball in experimental data from LHCb and BES III.
