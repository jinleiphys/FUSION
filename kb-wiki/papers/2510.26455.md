# Finite density QCD phase structure from strangeness fluctuations

> Charting the phase diagram of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at large density is a challenging task due to the complex action problem in lattice simulations. Through simulations at imaginary baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ we observe that, if the strangeness neutrality condition is imposed, both the strangeness chemical potential $μ_S/μ_B$ and the strangeness susceptibility $χ_2^S$ take on constant values at the chiral transition for varying $μ_B$. We present new lattice data to extrapolate contours of constant $μ_S/μ_B$ or $χ_2^S$ to finite baryon chemical potential. We argue that they are good proxies for the QCD crossover because, as we show, they are only mildly influenced by criticality and by finite volume effects. We obtain continuum limits for these proxies up to $μ_B = 400$ MeV, through a next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) Taylor expansion based on large-statistics data on $16^3 \times 8$, $20^3 \times 10$ and $24^3 \times 12$ lattices with our 4HEX improved staggered action. We show that these are in excellent agreement with existing results for the chiral transition and, strikingly, also with analogous contours obtained with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. On the $16^3 \times 8$ lattice, we carry out the expansion up to next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^4$LO), and extend the extrapolation beyond $μ_B=500$ MeV, again finding perfect agreement with the HRG model. This suggests that the crossover line constructed from this proxy starts deviating from the chemical freeze-out line near $μ_B\approx500$ MeV, as expected but not yet observed.

## Key claim

The authors propose that contours of constant strangeness susceptibility χ₂^S or constant strangeness chemical potential ratio μ_S/μ_B, under strangeness neutrality, serve as practical proxies for the QCD crossover line at finite baryon chemical potential, because these observables are only mildly influenced by criticality and finite volume effects, unlike the chiral susceptibility.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2+1 flavors of rooted staggered fermions using 4HEX improved action on 16³×8, 20³×10, and 24³×12 lattices. They constructed a two-dimensional Taylor expansion in μ_B and μ_S to extrapolate contours of constant χ₂^S and μ_S/μ_B from imaginary chemical potential data to real chemical potentials up to μ_B=400 MeV, with continuum extrapolation via N²LO expansion, and extended to μ_B beyond 500 MeV on the coarsest lattice using N⁴LO expansion.

## Key numbers

- The constant value of χ₂^S at the chiral transition is approximately 0.3 (dimensionless), and the constant value of μ_S/μ_B is approximately 0.25
- Continuum limits for the proxies were obtained up to μ_B = 400 MeV
- On the 16³×8 lattice, the expansion was carried out up to N⁴LO order (tenth order conserved charge fluctuations) and extrapolated beyond μ_B = 500 MeV
- The crossover line from the μ_S/μ_B proxy starts deviating from the chemical freeze-out line near μ_B ≈ 500 MeV
- The chiral transition at zero chemical potential is located at T = 156-158 MeV (citing prior work)
- On the 48³×12 lattice, data were obtained for imaginary chemical potentials up to μ_B/T = i·2.75

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the phase diagram from chiral susceptibility-based definitions of the crossover (Borsanyi 2020, Bazavov 2018) to strangeness-based proxies that allow access to higher chemical potentials. The observation that μ_S/μ_B is approximately constant along the crossover was previously noted by Bollweg (2024) from Taylor expansion. The authors compare their results to the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and find perfect agreement, and show that the crossover line from their proxy agrees with the chiral transition line from Borsanyi (2020) up to around 300 MeV. This enables mapping the QCD phase diagram to larger densities where deviation from the chemical freeze-out line (Andronic 2017) can be observed.
