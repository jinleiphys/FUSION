# Relativistic third-order viscous corrections to the entropy four-current from kinetic theory

> By employing a Chapman-Enskog like iterative solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation-time approximation, we derive a new expression for the entropy four-current up to third order in gradient expansion. We show that unlike second-order and third-order entropy four-current obtained using Grad's method, there is a non-vanishing entropy flux in the present third-order expression. We further quantify the effect of the higher-order entropy density in the case of boost-invariant one-dimensional longitudinal expansion of a system. We demonstrate that the results obtained using third-order evolution equation for shear stress tensor, derived by employing the method of Chapman-Enskog expansion, show better agreement with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation as well as with the parton cascade BAMPS, as compared to those obtained using the third-order equations from the method of Grad's 14-moment approximation.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new expression for the entropy four-current up to third order in gradient expansion using a Chapman-Enskog iterative solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation-time approximation, and show that unlike previous second-order and third-order results from Grad's 14-moment approximation, this expression contains a non-vanishing entropy flux even in the absence of bulk viscosity and dissipative charge current.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation in relaxation-time approximation iteratively to obtain the non-equilibrium distribution function as a Chapman-Enskog expansion up to third order in space-time gradients. They then substitute this distribution function into the kinetic-theory expression for the entropy four-current, expand to third order in the deviation from equilibrium, and perform the momentum integrals to obtain the entropy density and entropy flux. The resulting third-order evolution equation for the shear stress tensor is derived and applied to the case of boost-invariant one-dimensional longitudinal (Bjorken) expansion.

## Key numbers

- For the Chapman-Enskog method in Bjorken flow, the transport coefficients in the shear evolution equation are: $\tau_\pi = \eta/\beta_\pi$, $\beta_\pi = 4P/5$, $\lambda = 38/21$, $\chi = 72/245$.
- For Grad's 14-moment approximation in Bjorken flow, the transport coefficients are: $\tau_\pi' = \eta/\beta_\pi'$, $\beta_\pi' = 2P/3$, $\lambda' = 4/3$, $\chi' = 3/4$.
- For initial temperature $T_0=300$ MeV at initial time $\tau_0=0.25$ fm/c and anisotropic pressure configuration $\xi_0=10$, the initial shear pressure is $\pi_0=87$ MeV/fm$^3$.
- For initial temperature $T_0=600$ MeV at initial time $\tau_0=0.25$ fm/c and anisotropic pressure configuration $\xi_0=10$, the initial shear pressure is $\pi_0=1386$ MeV/fm$^3$.
- The third-order Chapman-Enskog method shows better agreement with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation and with BAMPS parton cascade results than Grad's method, with appreciable differences only for $\eta/s=10/4\pi$.

## Context

This work extends the derivation of third-order viscous hydrodynamics beyond the Grad 14-moment approximation used by El et al. (2009) and Muronga (2010, 2014). The Chapman-Enskog approach was previously shown by Jaiswal (2013, 2014) to give better agreement with microscopic Boltzmann simulations and exact solutions of the RTA Boltzmann equation at second order. The present third-order results enable more accurate modeling of the space-time evolution of matter produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the shear relaxation time and pressure anisotropy, and demonstrate faster convergence of the gradient expansion compared to Grad's method.
