# Nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung: An example of the impossibility of measuring off-shell amplitudes

> For nearly fifty years theoretical and experimental efforts in nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung (NN$γ$) have been devoted to measuring off-shell amplitudes and distinguishing among various NN potentials on the basis of their off-shell behavior. New experiments are underway, designed specifically to attain kinematics further off shell than in the past, and thus to be more sensitive to the off-shell behavior. This letter shows that, contrary to these expectations, and due to the invariance of the S-matrix under transformations of the fields, the off-shell NN amplitude is as a matter of principle an unmeasurable quantity in NN$γ$.

# Nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung: An example of the impossibility of measuring off-shell amplitudes

## Key claim
Contrary to nearly fifty years of theoretical and experimental efforts, the off-shell nucleon-nucleon (NN) amplitude is **in principle** unmeasurable in nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung (NNγ), because the S-matrix is invariant under transformations of the fields, which can arbitrarily change the coefficients of off-shell contributions without affecting any physical observable.

## Method
The paper uses a simple field theory model for spin-zero particles (π⁺ + π⁰ → π⁺ + π⁰ + γ) based on chiral perturbation theory through O(p⁴), which allows complete calculation of the contact term that is impossible to compute in standard non-relativistic potential models. The author explicitly constructs the most general Lagrangian including equation-of-motion (EOM) terms with coefficients β₁ and β₂, then applies field transformations that change these coefficients arbitrarily, and demonstrates that the full bremsstrahlung amplitude is independent of β₁ and β₂ due to exact cancellation between the external radiation diagrams and the contact term.

## Key numbers
- The double scattering contribution in typical potential model calculations is a 10–30% contribution to the NNγ amplitude.
- The off-shell NN amplitude is written schematically as T = T_on + (p² − m²) T_off, where the (p² − m²) factor cancels with the propagator when inserted into the bremsstrahlung amplitude.
- In the model, the renormalized irreducible amplitude for elastic π⁺π⁰ scattering contains off-shell terms proportional to β₁ and β₂, with coefficients 16 i m_π² β₂ / (3 F₀⁴) and 16 i β₁ / F₀⁴.
- The electromagnetic vertex for π⁺ → π⁺ + γ contains an off-shell part ∼ β₁ with factor 16 β₁ / F₀² (Λ_i + Λ_f).
- The renormalized pion propagator correction is Δ_R⁻¹(p) = (p² − m_π²)[1 + (16 β₁ / F₀²)(p² − m_π²)] + iε.

## Context
This work directly contradicts the established expectation, dating back to Ashkin (1949), that NNγ can determine off-shell NN amplitudes and distinguish among NN potentials. It extends similar observations by Scherer and Fearing (1995) for Compton scattering on the pion and by Davidson and collaborators (1996) for the pion-nucleon system. The paper implies that future NNγ experiments designed to probe further off-shell kinematics cannot achieve their stated goal, and instead should focus on understanding contact terms that probe the inner details of the NN interaction.
