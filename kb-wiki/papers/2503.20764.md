# High-Precision Ab Initio Radius Calculations of Boron Isotopes

> We perform a precision study of radii in Boron isotopes for multiple realistic interactions from chiral effective field theory. We obtain predictions of radii with combined many-body and interaction uncertainty quantification from ab initio no-core shell model calculations together with machine learning extrapolation methods. An extension to radius differences further allows us to investigate a potential proton halo in $^{8}$B and, moreover, provide predictions that relate directly to the isotope shift, which can be precisely measured in experiments.

# High-Precision Ab Initio Radius Calculations of Boron Isotopes

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ab initio no-core shell model calculations combined with artificial neural network extrapolations can produce radius predictions for boron isotopes with quantified many-body and interaction uncertainties, but the precision is conditional on the choice of chiral interaction family and cutoff, with remaining cutoff dependence and a tendency to underbind ground-state energies.

## Method

The authors perform no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for boron isotopes using two families of chiral NN+3N interactions (nonlocal EMN and semilocal SMS) at multiple chiral orders, with similarity renormalization group evolution to alpha = 0.08 fm^4. They employ fully-connected feed-forward artificial neural networks trained on NCSM data for few-body systems (A <= 4) to extrapolate radii from finite model spaces, and combine many-body truncation uncertainties with chiral interaction uncertainties via Bayesian analysis using the BUQEYE pointwise model.

## Key numbers

- Point-proton radius of B-8 with EMN[500] interaction at N3LO: 2.708_{-0.016}^{+0.021} fm (including many-body uncertainty only)
- Point-proton radius of B-8 with EMN[500] interaction at N3LO including chiral uncertainty: 2.711_{-0.027}^{+0.030} fm
- Point-proton radius of B-8 with SMS[500] interaction at N4LO+: 2.603_{-0.022}^{+0.024} fm (many-body uncertainty only)
- Point-proton radius of B-8 with SMS[500] interaction at N4LO+ including chiral uncertainty: 2.609_{-0.072}^{+0.070} fm
- Proton-neutron radius difference for B-8 with EMN[500] at N3LO including chiral uncertainty: 0.453_{-0.019}^{+0.021} fm
- Proton-neutron radius difference for He-6 with EMN[500] at N3LO including chiral uncertainty: -0.816_{-0.029}^{+0.027} fm
- Squared point-proton radius difference between B-8 and B-10 with EMN[500] at N3LO including chiral uncertainty: 1.630_{-0.079}^{+0.069} fm^2
- Squared point-proton radius difference between B-12 and B-10 with EMN[500] at N3LO including chiral uncertainty: -0.361_{-0.064}^{+0.059} fm^2

## Context

This work extends the machine learning extrapolation approach developed by Knöll et al. (2023, 2024, 2025) for NCSM calculations to boron isotopes, building on prior NCSM studies of light nuclei by Barrett et al. (2013) and Navrátil et al. (2009). The results enable direct comparison with upcoming laser spectroscopy measurements of isotope shifts in boron isotopes planned by Bernhard et al. (2017, 2019), and provide theoretical predictions for the proton halo candidate B-8 that can be tested against experimental charge radius measurements.
