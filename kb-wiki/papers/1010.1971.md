# Holographic Vector Dominance for the Nucleon

> We derive a two-parameter formula for the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon described as an instanton by "integrating out" all KK modes other than the lowest mesons from the infinite-tower of vector mesons in holographic QCD while preserving hidden local symmetry for the resultant vector fields. With only two parameters, the proton Sachs form factors can be fit surprisingly well to the available experimental data for momentum transfers $Q^2\lsim 0.5$ GeV$^2$ with $χ^2$/dof $\lsim 2$, indicating the importance of an infinite tower in the soliton structure of the nucleon. The prediction of the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic dual model is checked against the fit values that we interpret as representing Nature. The nature of the "core" in the nucleon structure is reassessed in light of the results obtained with the infinite-tower structure.

## Key claim

The paper derives a two-parameter formula for the proton Sachs electromagnetic form factors by integrating out all higher Kaluza-Klein (KK) modes from the infinite tower of vector mesons in holographic QCD, preserving hidden local symmetry for the lowest vector mesons. The fit works surprisingly well only for momentum transfers \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) with \(\chi^2/\text{dof} \lesssim 2\), indicating that the infinite tower structure is crucial for describing the nucleon, but the holographic QCD prediction for the magnetic form factor shows significant deviation from experiment.

## Method

The authors start from the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic dual QCD model, where the nucleon emerges as an instanton in the presence of an infinite tower of vector mesons. They integrate out all KK modes except the lowest vector mesons \(V^{(0)} \equiv (\rho, \omega)\) at tree level, using equations of motion for the higher modes to obtain an effective 4D Lagrangian to \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\) that preserves hidden local symmetry and chiral symmetry. The resulting Sachs form factors are expressed in a two-parameter form \(G_{E,M}^p(Q^2)/\beta_{E,M} = (1 - a_{E,M}/2) + z_{E,M} (Q^2/m_\rho^2) + (a_{E,M}/2) [m_\rho^2/(m_\rho^2 + Q^2)]\), where \(\beta_E = 1\) and \(\beta_M = \mu_p\), and the parameters \(a_{E,M}\) and \(z_{E,M}\) encode the infinite tower effects.

## Key numbers

* Best fit to experimental data for \(Q^2 \leq 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) yields: \(a_E^{\text{(best)}} = 4.55\), \(z_E^{\text{(best)}} = 0.45\) with \(\chi_E^2/\text{dof} = 1.5\) for the electric form factor; \(a_M^{\text{(best)}} = 4.31\), \(z_M^{\text{(best)}} = 0.40\) with \(\chi_M^2/\text{dof} = 1.1\) for the magnetic form factor.
* The standard vector dominance (sVD) model gives \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 187\) for \(G_E^p\) and 852 for \(G_M^p\), confirming its failure for the nucleon.
* The holographic QCD (hQCD) prediction using the lowest four vector mesons gives \((a_E, z_E) = (3.01, -0.042)\) and \((a_M, z_M) = (3.14, 0.16)\), with \(\chi^2 = 20.2\) for \(G_E^p\) and 133 for \(G_M^p\).
* The charge contributed from the lowest vector meson \(V^{(0)}\) overshoots the proton charge by more than 40%, compensated by negative contributions from the first excited vector mesons.

## Context

This work extends the holographic vector dominance approach previously applied to the pion form factor by Harada and Rho (2010) to the nucleon case, contradicting the long-standing failure of Sakurai vector dominance for nucleons as noted by Iachello, Jackson, and Lande (1973) and Bijker (1993). The results challenge the standard interpretation of a "core" in the nucleon structure, which had been attributed to a compact microscopic QCD component such as the "Little Bag" model of Brown, Rho, and Weise (1986). The deviation in the magnetic form factor suggests the need for \(1/N_c\) corrections, quark-mass effects, or curvature effects in the bulk sector, which remain open issues for future work.
