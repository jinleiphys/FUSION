# Angular momentum I ground state probabilities of boson systems interacting by random interactions

> In this paper we report our systematic calculations of angular momentum $I$ ground state probabilities ($P(I)$) of boson systems with spin $l$ in the presence of random two-body interactions. It is found that the P(0) dominance is usually not true for a system with an odd number of bosons, while it is valid for an even number of bosons, which indicates that the P(0) dominance is partly connected to the even number of identical particles. It is also noticed that the $P(I_{max})$'s of bosons with spin $l$ do not follow the 1/N ($N=l+1$, referring to the number of independent two-body matrix elements) relation. The properties of the $P(I)$'s obtained in boson systems with spin $l$ are discussed.

## Key claim

The abstract states that $P(0)$ dominance is "usually not true" for odd numbers of bosons, but the full text reveals that this conclusion applies specifically to boson systems *without* an $s$ boson; in systems like $sp$, $sd$, and $sdg$ that contain an $s$ boson, the $s$ boson condensation can produce large $P(0)$ even for odd $n$, contributing around 40% of $P(0)$.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a scalar two-body Hamiltonian for boson systems with spin $l$, where the two-body interaction strengths $G_L$ (for $L=0,2,\dots,2l$) were drawn from a Gaussian two-body random ensemble (TBRE) with distribution $\rho(G_L) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp(-G_L^2/2)$. For each system, 1000 sets of random interactions were used to compute the ground state probabilities $P(I)$ for each angular momentum $I$. The study covered boson numbers $n$ from 3 to 6 and spins $l$ up to 11, with additional calculations for $n=4$ across many $l$ values.

## Key numbers

- For four bosons with spin $l$, $P(0)$'s stagger with an interval of $\delta_l = 3$; $P(0)$ is dominant over other $P(I)$'s except for $l=2$ and $l=8$.
- The number of $I=0$ states $D_0^{(l)}$ for four bosons with spin $l$ follows the sequence 1, 1, 1, 2, 2, 2, 3, 3, 3, 4, 4, 4, $\dots$ for $l=0,1,2,3,\dots$.
- For odd $n$ and $l=3$, $I=0$ states do not exist unless $n\ge 15$; for $l=5$, unless $n\ge 9$; for $l=7$, unless $n\ge 7$; for $l=9$ or 11, unless $n\ge 5$.
- For fifteen bosons with $l=3$, nine bosons with $l=5$, seven bosons with $l=7$, and five bosons with $l=9$ or 11, $P(0) \sim 0\%$ always.
- The gap $(E_{I_{max}-2} - E_{I_{max}})$ for bosons with spin $l=7$ is 2.03, 2.56, and 3.07 for $n=4$, 5, and 6 respectively; for fermions in a $j=15/2$ shell, the corresponding gaps are 0.47, 0.39, and 0.35 for $n=4$, 5, and 6.
- The $P(I_{max})$'s of bosons with spin $l$ are systematically larger than $1/N = 1/(l+1)$, and increase with boson number $n$.

## Context

This work extends the study of ground state spin probabilities from fermion systems (where $0^+$ dominance was discovered by Johnson and others) to boson systems with arbitrary spin $l$, following earlier studies of $d$-boson systems by Zhao et al. (2001) and $sd$ and $sdg$ boson systems by Bijker and collaborators. The results for four bosons are compared directly to the four-fermion single-$j$ shell results of Zelevinsky and Zhao et al. (2001). The paper enables future systematic calculations of $P(I)$ for both even and odd numbers of bosons with many $l$ values, and raises the open question of whether the $1/N$ relation for $P(I_{max})$ holds for general boson systems beyond those with small $l$.
