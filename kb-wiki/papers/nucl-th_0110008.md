# Light Lambda-Lambda Hypernuclei and the Onset of Stability for Lambda-Xi Hypernuclei

> New Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for light Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei are presented in order to assess the self consistency of the Lambda-Lambda hypernuclear binding-energy world data and the implied strength of the Lambda-Lambda interaction, in the wake of recent experimental reports on Lambda-Lambda-4H and Lambda-Lambda-6He. Using Gaussian soft-core simulations of Nijmegen one-boson-exchange model interactions, the Nijmegen soft-core model NSC97 simulations are found close to reproducing the recently reported binding energy of Lambda-Lambda-6He, but not those of other species. For stranger systems, Faddeev calculations of light Lambda-Xi hypernuclei, using a simulation of the strongly attractive Lambda-Xi interactions due to the same model, suggest that Lambda-Xi-6He marks the onset of nuclear stability for Xi hyperons.

## Key claim

New Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations show that the Nijmegen soft-core model NSC97 simulations are close to reproducing the recently reported binding energy of \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~6}\)He, but not those of other species; for stranger systems, Faddeev calculations suggest that \(_{\Lambda\Xi}^{~6}\)He marks the onset of nuclear stability for \(\Xi\) hyperons, though this prediction depends on the assumed \(\Xi\alpha\) potential depth.

## Method

The authors solved differential \(s\)-wave Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations using a cluster reduction method for three- and four-body systems. They used three-range Gaussian potentials simulating the low-energy \(s\)-wave scattering parameters of Nijmegen one-boson-exchange models (ND, ESC00, and NSC97 versions \(b\) and \(e\)) for the hyperon-hyperon interactions, and two-range Gaussian (Isle) potentials for the \(\alpha\alpha\), \(\Lambda\alpha\), and \(\Xi\alpha\) interactions. For \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~6}\)He they solved three-body \(\alpha\Lambda\Lambda\) Faddeev equations, and for \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{10}\)Be they solved four-body \(\alpha\alpha\Lambda\Lambda\) Yakubovsky equations; for \(_{\Lambda\Xi}^{~6}\)He and \(_{\Lambda\Xi}^{~6}\)H they solved three-body \(\alpha\Lambda\Xi\) Faddeev equations.

## Key numbers

- \(B_{\Lambda}(^9_{\Lambda}\rm Be) = 6.67\) MeV calculated, in excellent agreement with measured value \(6.71 \pm 0.04\) MeV.
- For \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~6}\)He: calculated \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 10.7\) MeV (ESC00), 9.10 MeV (ND), 6.82 MeV (NSC97\(e\)), 6.60 MeV (NSC97\(b\)), and 6.27 MeV (\(V_{\Lambda\Lambda}=0\)); experimental values are \(10.9 \pm 0.6\) MeV (old event, Pro66) and \(7.25 \pm 0.19^{+0.18}_{-0.11}\) MeV (new event, Tak01).
- For \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{10}\)Be: calculated \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 19.5\) MeV (ESC00), 17.8 MeV (ND), 15.5 MeV (NSC97\(e\)), 15.3 MeV (NSC97\(b\)), and 14.9 MeV (\(V_{\Lambda\Lambda}=0\)); experimental value is \(17.7 \pm 0.4\) MeV (Dan63).
- The \(\Xi^0\alpha\) binding energy was assumed to be 2.09 MeV, obtained from a Woods-Saxon potential scaled from a depth of \(\sim 15\) MeV in \(^{11}\)B.

## Context

This work extends earlier cluster calculations by Bodmer, Usmani, and Carlson (1984) and by Wang, Tang, and Bodmer (1986) which noted a near-linear correlation between \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}\) values for \(^{~6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)He and \(^{10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)Be that could not simultaneously reproduce the two events reported in the 1960s. The present calculations confirm and aggravate this incompatibility, and show that the new KEK E373 event (Tak01) for \(^{~6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)He is also incompatible with the older \(^{10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)Be data. The work also extends earlier studies by Schaffner et al. (1992) on \(\Xi\) stability in hypernuclei, suggesting that \(_{\Lambda\Xi}^{~6}\)He may be the lightest particle-stable \(S=-3\) hypernucleus, though direct production would require \(\Omega^-\)-initiated reactions beyond present experimental capabilities.
