# Variance Minimisation of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Model on a Quantum Computer

> Quantum computing can potentially provide advantages for specific computational tasks. The simulation of fermionic systems is one such task that lends itself well to quantum computation, with applications in nuclear physics and electronic systems. Here we present work in which we use a variance minimisation method to find the full spectrum of energy eigenvalues of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model; an exactly-solvable nuclear shell model-type system. We perform these calculations using both quantum simulators and real quantum hardware accessed via IBM cloud-based quantum computers. Using these IBM quantum computers we are able to obtain all eigenvalues for the cases of three and seven fermions (nucleons) in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a variance-minimisation variant of the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) can recover the full energy spectrum of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model on current noisy quantum hardware, but only for system sizes where the parity-submatrix dimension is a power of two (here N=3 and N=7). The method requires no additional circuit depth compared to ground-state VQE, only more circuit evaluations.

## Method

The authors encode the LMG Hamiltonian in the quasi-spin basis, exploiting parity symmetry to split the Hamiltonian into two block-diagonal submatrices of dimension (N+1)/2. For N=3 and N=7, these submatrices have dimensions 2 and 4 respectively, allowing direct Pauli decomposition onto 1 and 2 qubits. They then minimise the variance σ² = ⟨H²⟩ − ⟨H⟩² using a parameterised ansatz circuit (single Ry gate for N=3; a three-parameter two-qubit circuit with CNOTs and Ry gates for N=7), with classical COBYLA optimisation, on IBM cloud-based quantum computers (IBMQ_manila for N=3, IBMQ_nairobi for N=7) using 20,000 shots per circuit and readout error mitigation.

## Key numbers

- For N=3 LMG model (V/ε=0.5, W/ε=0) on IBMQ_manila: ground state exact value −1.823 ε, quantum computer result −1.788 ± 0.062 ε; second excited state exact 0.823 ε, result 0.826 ± 0.064 ε; first excited state exact −0.823 ε, result −0.816 ± 0.063 ε; third excited state exact 1.823 ε, result 1.810 ± 0.063 ε.
- For N=7 LMG model (V/ε=0.5, W/ε=0) on IBMQ_nairobi: ground state exact −6.208 ε, quantum computer result −6.067 ± 0.901 ε; first excited state exact −2.944 ε, result −3.151 ± 0.503 ε; second excited state exact 1.208 ε, result 1.184 ± 0.484 ε; third excited state exact 5.944 ε, result 5.902 ± 0.660 ε.
- Overlaps of N=7 quantum-computer wavefunctions with analytic eigenvectors: ground state 0.924, first excited 0.947, second excited 0.991, third excited 0.972.

## Context

This work extends prior applications of VQE to the LMG model, which had previously focused on ground-state calculations (Cervia 2021, Chikaoka 2022) or used a quantum-assisted generalised eigenvalue approach for excited states (Hlatshwayo 2022). The variance-minimisation approach enables excited-state spectra on NISQ hardware without increasing circuit depth, though the authors note that scaling to larger systems would require increasingly complex ansatz circuits. The method is demonstrated only for the special case where the parity-submatrix dimension is a power of two, enabling efficient Pauli decomposition.
