# How the quark self-energy affects the color-superconducting gap

> We consider color superconductivity with two flavors of massless quarks which form Cooper pairs with total spin zero. We solve the gap equation for the color-superconducting gap parameter to subleading order in the QCD coupling constant $g$ at zero temperature. At this order in $g$, there is also a previously neglected contribution from the real part of the quark self-energy to the gap equation. Including this contribution leads to a reduction of the color-superconducting gap parameter $\f_0$ by a factor $b_0'=\exp \big[ -(\p ^2+4)/8 \big]\simeq 0.177$. On the other hand, the BCS relation $T_c\simeq 0.57\f_0$ between $\f_0$ and the transition temperature $T_c$ is shown to remain valid after taking into account corrections from the quark self-energy. The resulting value for $T_c$ confirms a result obtained previously with a different method.

# How the quark self-energy affects the color-superconducting gap

## Key claim

Including the previously neglected real part of the quark self-energy in the gap equation for two-flavor color superconductivity reduces the zero-temperature gap parameter by a factor of approximately 0.177, but the BCS relation between the zero-temperature gap and the transition temperature remains valid to leading order in the coupling constant.

## Method

The authors solved the QCD gap equation for spin-zero Cooper pairing of massless two-flavor quarks to subleading order in the coupling constant g at zero temperature, incorporating the real part of the quark self-energy via a wave-function renormalization factor Z(q0). They performed the Matsubara sum using spectral representations, transformed the resulting integro-differential equation into Airy's differential equation, and solved it with boundary conditions at the Fermi surface. The transition temperature was then determined by extending the gap equation to finite temperature while assuming the shape of the gap function remains unchanged.

## Key numbers

- The reduction factor for the zero-temperature gap parameter is b0' = exp[-(pi^2 + 4)/8] approximately equal to 0.177.
- The BCS relation between the zero-temperature gap phi0 and the transition temperature Tc is Tc approximately equal to 0.57 phi0.
- The constant c in the exponential factor of the gap solution is c/g = pi/(2 gbar), where gbar = g/(3 sqrt(2)).
- The prefactor b0 in the gap solution is b0 = 256 pi^4 (2/Nf)^(5/2) b0', where Nf is the number of quark flavors.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of the color-superconducting gap by Schafer and Wilczek (1999) and Pisarski and Rischke (2000), which neglected the quark self-energy and obtained b0' = 1. It confirms the result for Tc obtained by Brown, Liu, and Ren (2000) using a different method restricted to the normal phase, and demonstrates that the BCS relation Tc approximately 0.57 phi0 remains valid when self-energy corrections are included. The calculation enables a consistent determination of both the zero-temperature gap and the transition temperature within the same gap-equation framework.
