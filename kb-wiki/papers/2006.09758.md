# From few to many bosons inside the unitary window: a transition between universal to non-universal behavior

> Universal behaviour in few-bosons systems close to the unitary limit, where two bosons become unbound, has been intensively investigated in recent years both experimentally and theoretically. In this particular region, called the unitary window, details of the inter-particle interactions are not important and observables, such as binding energies, can be characterized by a few parameters. With an increasing number of particles the short-range repulsion, present in all atomic, molecular or nuclear interactions, gradually induces deviations from the universal behaviour. In the present letter we discuss for the first time a simple way of incorporating non-universal behaviour through one specific parameter which controls the smooth transition of the system from universal to non-universal regime. Using a system of $N$ helium atoms as an example we calculate their ground state energies as trajectories within the unitary window and also show that the control parameters can be used to determine the energy per particle in homogeneous systems when $N \rightarrow \infty$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the universal behavior of few-boson systems inside the unitary window can be characterized by gaussian potential paths, but that for systems larger than approximately 20 particles, short-range repulsion introduces non-universal deviations that can be captured by a single additional parameter, the range of the three-body force, allowing a smooth description of the transition from universal to non-universal regimes.

## Method

The authors used gaussian potentials of variable strength to construct universal trajectories in the energy plane (E₂, E_N) for N=2,3,4 bosons, then extended these calculations to larger N using the hyperspherical harmonic method. They then employed an effective field theory (EFT) framework at leading order, introducing a soft gaussian potential with both two-body and three-body gaussian terms, whose strengths were fixed by reproducing the dimer and trimer energies of the HFD-HE2 helium potential, and whose ranges were optimized to also reproduce the tetramer energy and the finite-range parameter r_B.

## Key numbers

- At unitarity, the dimensionless binding momenta for gaussian interactions are κ*₃ r₀ = 0.4883 and κ*₄ r₀ = 1.1847 for N=3 and N=4, respectively.
- The characteristic gaussian ranges for helium clusters are r₀⁽³⁾ = 11.147 a₀ and r₀⁽⁴⁾ = 11.85 a₀, determined from seven different realistic He-He potentials.
- The predicted unitary-limit energies for helium are E*₃ = 83.05 ± 0.05 mK and E*₄ = 433.0 ± 0.5 mK, within about 1% of the values from realistic potentials (E*₃ ≈ 84.0 mK, E*₄ ≈ 439.0 mK).
- For the HFD-HE2 potential, the dimer energy is E₂ = 0.83012 mK, the scattering length a = 235.547 a₀, and the finite-range parameter r_B = 7.208 a₀.
- The optimized soft gaussian potential at the physical point gives E₄ = 0.536 K, E₅ = 1.251 K, E₆ = 2.216 K, compared to HFD-HE2 reference values of 0.536 K, 1.266 K, and 2.232 K respectively.
- The energy per particle for N=112 at the physical point is E₁₁₂/112 = 3.58(2) K for the SGP versus 3.63(2) K for HFD-HE2; the extrapolated infinite-system values are 7.2(3) K and 7.14(2) K respectively.
- At the unitary point, the extrapolated infinite-system energy per particle is 6.8(3) K for the SGP versus 6.72(2) K for HFD-HE2.

## Context

This work extends the universal few-body studies of Efimov (1970) and the gaussian characterization approach of Kievsky (2014) and Gattobigio (2019) to larger boson numbers, showing that universal behavior holds up to N≈20 before short-range physics becomes important. The soft gaussian potential approach builds on the leading-order EFT framework of van Kolck (1998) and Bedaque (1999), and the results for helium clusters are compared to the Green Function Monte Carlo calculations of Pandharipande (1993) and the hyperspherical calculations of Hiyama (2012, 2014). The work enables a systematic way to predict saturation properties of homogeneous bosonic systems from few-body input, and suggests that all realistic He-He potentials should predict the same saturation energy at the unitary limit, which is verified for the HFD-B potential.
