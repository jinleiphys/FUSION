# Extracting the rho-omega mixing amplitude from the pion form-factor

> We re-examine and extend a recent analysis which showed that the rho-omega mixing amplitude cannot be unambiguously extracted from the pion electromagnetic form-factor in a model independent way. In particular, we focus on the argument that the extraction is sensitive to the presence of any intrinsic omega_I -> 2pi coupling. Our extended analysis confirms the original conclusion, with only minor, quantitative differences. The extracted mixing amplitude is shown to be sensitive to both the intrinsic coupling omega_I -> 2pi and to the value assumed for the mass of the neutral rho meson.

# Extracting the rho-omega mixing amplitude from the pion form-factor

## Key claim
The rho-omega mixing amplitude cannot be unambiguously extracted from the pion electromagnetic form-factor in a model-independent way, because the extraction is sensitive to both the intrinsic omega_I -> 2pi coupling and the value assumed for the neutral rho meson mass. The authors confirm the original conclusion of Maltman, O'Connell and Williams (1996) with only minor quantitative differences, but show that even when all standard approximations are imposed, the extracted mixing amplitude remains ambiguous.

## Method
The authors re-express the pion electromagnetic form-factor in both the isospin-pure basis and the physical basis, using vector meson dominance with Breit-Wigner propagators that include momentum-dependent widths. They perform a chi-squared fit to 70 data points of the pion form-factor between 500 and 975 MeV, treating the intrinsic omega_I -> 2pi coupling (parameterized as G) and the real part of the rho-omega mixing self-energy (tilde-Pi_rhoomega) as free parameters, while fixing the omega mass and width to Particle Data Group values.

## Key numbers
- Extracted real part of the rho-omega mixing amplitude (case A): tilde-Pi_rhoomega(m_rho^2) = -6832 +/- 1252 MeV^2, with G = 0.102 +/- 0.04
- When G is forced to zero (case B): tilde-Pi_rhoomega(m_rho^2) = -3844 +/- 271 MeV^2
- Using PDG input masses (rho mass fixed at 769.1 MeV): tilde-Pi_rhoomega(m_rho^2) = -6298 +/- 788 MeV^2, with G = 0.065 +/- 0.033
- The complex parameter z, which controls the Renard cancellation, is z = 0.9324 + 0.3511 i using Bernicha et al. (1994) parameters, and z = 1.023 + 0.2038 i using Benayoun et al. (1995) parameters
- Fitted rho mass (case A): 764.1 +/- 0.7 MeV; fitted rho width (case A): 145.0 +/- 1.7 MeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: 88/65 (case A), 94/66 (case B), 172/67 (PDG input)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Maltman, O'Connell and Williams (1996), who first argued that the rho-omega mixing amplitude cannot be unambiguously extracted from the pion form-factor. The authors critically re-examine the Renard cancellation argument (Renard, 1973), which traditionally assumed that the intrinsic omega -> 2pi coupling is cancelled by the imaginary part of the mixing amplitude. The results show that the extracted mixing amplitude depends strongly on whether one allows an intrinsic omega_I -> 2pi coupling, and that the traditional extraction method (assuming G=0) is unjustified. This work enables future analyses to properly account for the ambiguity when extracting isospin-violating parameters from vector meson data.
