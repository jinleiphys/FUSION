# Structure of $^8$B and astrophysical $S_{17}$ factor

> Nuclear structure data are of crucial importance in order to address important astrophysical problems such as the origin of chemical elements, the inner working of our Sun, and the evolution of stars. We demonstrate this by investigating the ground state structure of $^8$B and $^7$Be nuclei within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock framework and by calculating the overlap integral of $^8$B and $^7$Be wave functions. The latter is used to calculate the astrophysical S factor ($S_{17}$) for the solar fusion reaction $^{7}$Be($p, γ)^{8}$B.

# Structure of $^8$B and astrophysical $S_{17}$ factor

## Key claim

The Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SkHF) model with a modified SLy4 interaction (spin-orbit term renormalized to reproduce the experimental binding energy of the last proton in $^8$B) can reproduce the ground-state properties of $^8$B and $^7$Be, and the resulting overlap function yields an astrophysical $S_{17}(0)$ factor of 22.0 eV barn, which lies within the adopted limits but is larger than some recent ANC-method results.

## Method

The authors solved spherically symmetric Hartree-Fock equations with the SLy4 Skyrme interaction, incorporating BCS pairing correlations, and renormalized the spin-orbit parameter to match the experimental single-proton separation energy in $^8$B. They calculated rms radii and density distributions for $^7$Be, $^7$B, $^8$Li, $^8$B, and $^9$C, then used the overlap integral of the $^7$Be and $^8$B ground-state wave functions to compute the direct radiative capture cross section for $^7$Be(p,$\gamma$)$^8$B, from which the astrophysical $S_{17}$ factor was obtained.

## Key numbers

- Calculated rms matter radius for $^8$B: 2.57 fm (experimental values: 2.55 ± 0.08 fm and 2.43 ± 0.03 fm)
- Calculated rms proton radius for $^8$B: 2.73 fm (experimental: 2.76 ± 0.08 fm and 2.49 ± 0.03 fm)
- Calculated rms neutron radius for $^8$B: 2.27 fm (experimental: 2.16 ± 0.08 fm and 2.33 ± 0.03 fm)
- Asymptotic normalization coefficient for $p_{3/2}$ orbit: $\bar{c}_{1 3/2} = 0.64$
- Asymptotic normalization coefficient for $p_{1/2}$ orbit: $\bar{c}_{1 1/2} = 0.34$
- Astrophysical $S_{17}(0)$ from direct HF overlap calculation: $S^{A}_{17}(0) = 22.0$ eV b
- Astrophysical $S_{17}(0)$ using Eq. (4) with $\kappa = 36.5$: $S^{B}_{17}(0) = 19.5$ eV b
- Astrophysical $S_{17}(0)$ using Eq. (4) with $\kappa = 37.8$: $S^{C}_{17}(0) = 20.2$ eV b

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach, previously successful for neutron halo nuclei like $^6$He and $^{11}$Li (Mizutori 2000), to the proton-rich $^8$B nucleus relevant to solar neutrino physics. The calculated $S_{17}(0)$ of 22.0 eV b is closer to the direct capture measurements of Junghans (2003) and Hammache (2001) than to the ANC-method result of Trache (2003), and the authors suggest the discrepancy may arise from uncertainties in the optical potentials used in the DWBA analysis of the transfer reaction data. The work enables further refinement of nuclear structure inputs for the $^7$Be(p,$\gamma$)$^8$B reaction, which directly affects predictions of the high-energy solar neutrino flux detected by SNO, Kamiokande, and Homestake experiments.
