# Charged current weak production of the Delta resonance

> The charge changing weak production of Delta in nucleons and nuclei is studied. The reactions e^{-} p -> Delta^{0} ν_{e} and e^{+} p -> Delta^{++} \barν_{e} are considered as a possible source of information about the weak N-Delta transition form factors. The low q^2 BNL data on neutrino production of Delta are used to extract the axial vector N-Delta coupling, taking into account the deuteron structure and the Delta width. Finally, pion production induced by neutrinos in ^{16}O in the Delta region, relevant to atmospheric neutrino experiments, is investigated.

## Key claim

The weak charged-current production of the Delta resonance in electron-proton collisions (e⁻ p → Δ⁰ νₑ and e⁺ p → Δ⁺⁺ ν̅ₑ) could provide a new experimental window into the axial N-Delta transition form factors, but the feasibility depends on cross sections being high enough over a large angular region at MAMI and TJNAF energies. The extraction of the axial vector coupling C₅ᴬ(0) from BNL neutrino data yields 1.22 ± 0.06, consistent with the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation, but only after properly accounting for the Delta width which causes a 30% reduction in cross section.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section for weak electroproduction of the Delta resonance using vector form factors derived from CVC and M1 dominance (with C₃ⱽ from electroproduction data or a quark model) and axial form factors from the Adler model or a non-relativistic quark model, including the Delta width via a Breit-Wigner propagator. For the BNL data analysis, they computed the ratio R(Q²) of ν d → μ⁻ Δ⁺⁺ n to ν d → μ⁻ p p cross sections at E_ν = 1.6 GeV using the impulse approximation with the Paris potential deuteron wave function. For neutrino-induced Delta production in ¹⁶O, they incorporated nuclear medium effects by modifying the Delta propagator with a Pauli-blocked decay width and a Delta selfenergy, and treated pion absorption in the eikonal approximation.

## Key numbers

- C₅ᴬ(0) = 1.22 ± 0.06, extracted from the BNL data ratio R = 0.55 ± 0.05 at Q² ≤ 0.1 GeV² and E_ν = 1.6 GeV
- The off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation gives C₅ᴬ(0) = 1.15, using f_π = 92.4 MeV and g_{ΔNπ} = 28.6
- The Delta width Γ₀ = 120 MeV, with energy-dependent width Γ_Δ = Γ₀ (M_Δ/W) (q³_cm(W)/q³_cm(M_Δ))
- The axial dipole mass M_A = 1.28 GeV from a best fit to BNL μ⁻ Δ⁺⁺ events
- Nuclear medium effects cause an overall reduction of about 40% in dσ/dE_k' for νₑ on ¹⁶O at E_ν = 750 MeV
- The ratio R(E_ν) = σ_Δ(μ)/σ_Δ(e) is unaffected by medium modifications

## Context

This work extends the analysis of weak N-Delta transition form factors from the earlier ANL (Radecky et al.) and BNL (Kitagaki et al.) neutrino experiments, which had poorly known neutrino energy distributions from 0.5 to 6.0 GeV, by proposing electron scattering experiments at MAMI and TJNAF as a cleaner probe of the axial form factors. The extracted C₅ᴬ(0) value addresses discrepancies between the PCAC prediction and quark model estimates (Mukhopadhyay et al.). The study of neutrino-induced Delta production in ¹⁶O with nuclear effects is directly relevant to atmospheric neutrino experiments like Kamiokande, where the mean neutrino energy of about 700 MeV lies above the Delta production threshold, and the analysis shows that using free Delta cross sections overestimates one-pion production by about 40%.
