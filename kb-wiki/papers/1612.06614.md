# Puzzles in eta photoproduction: the 1685~MeV narrow peak

> We claim that a narrow peak in the cross section near 1685~MeV in the $γn\to ηn$ channel can be explained through a peculiar radial behaviour of the $p$-wave quark states with $j=1/2$ and $j=3/2$ in the low lying S11 resonances and the opening of the $KΣ$ threshold rather than by an exotic resonance. We explain the mechanism of its formation in the framework of a coupled channel formalism which incorporates quasi-bound quark-model states corresponding to the two low lying resonances in the S11 partial wave. A relation to the Single Quark Transition Model is pointed out.

# Puzzles in eta photoproduction: the 1685 MeV narrow peak

## Key claim

The narrow peak near 1685 MeV in the $\gamma n \to \eta n$ cross section can be explained by the peculiar radial behavior of $p$-wave quark states with $j=1/2$ and $j=3/2$ in the low-lying S11 resonances combined with the opening of the $K\Sigma$ threshold, rather than by an exotic resonance; however, the model underestimates the strength of the imaginary part compared to the Bonn-Gatchina 2014-2 analysis, so the explanation is qualitative rather than quantitative.

## Method

The authors employed a coupled-channel formalism based on an SU(3)-extended version of the Cloudy Bag Model (CBM) incorporating $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, $\eta$, and $K$ mesons, with ten channels in the scattering calculation. The resonant part of the electroproduction amplitude in the S11 partial wave was constructed from quasi-bound quark-model states corresponding to the $N(1535)$ and $N(1650)$ resonances, with a mixing angle of $30^\circ$ and meson-quark coupling constants fixed by SU(3) symmetry. The electromagnetic amplitude was dominated by photon-quark coupling, and the radial behavior of the $1p_{3/2}$ and $1p_{1/2}$ quark orbits was evaluated separately to determine their distinct contributions to the $E_{0+}$ multipole.

## Key numbers

- The narrow structure appears at $W \approx 1685$ MeV in the $\gamma n \to \eta n$ channel, as observed by the GRAAL Collaboration.
- The mixing angle between the $|N(1535)\rangle$ and $|N(1650)\rangle$ states is set to $\theta = 30^\circ$.
- The $E_{0+}$ amplitudes are presented in units of mfm (millifermi) for both proton and neutron channels in the region of the $K\Sigma$ threshold.
- The total cross-section for $\gamma n \to \eta n$ is multiplied by a conventional factor of $3/2$ when comparing to the $\gamma p \to \eta p$ channel.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel analysis previously developed by the authors (Golli et al., 2005–2013) for scattering and electroproduction amplitudes in various partial waves, now applied specifically to the S11 partial wave in $\eta$ photoproduction. The narrow peak was first discussed by Azimov et al. (2005) as a possible pentaquark partner of the $\Theta^+$, and alternative explanations have included threshold effects from the $K\Sigma$ channel (Doering, 2010), interference of nearby resonances (Shyam, 2008), and analyses within the Giessen model (Shklyar, 2007, 2013) and Bonn-Gatchina framework (2009, 2015). The present quark-model explanation relates to the Single Quark Transition Model (SQTM) of Cottingham (1979) and demonstrates that the Moorhouse selection rule suppresses the $1s \to 1p_{3/2}$ transition in the proton, explaining the absence of the peak in the $\gamma p$ channel. The results enable further investigation of whether a fully relativistic treatment of quark wave functions is essential for understanding narrow structures in baryon spectroscopy.
