# Review of the electric dipole moment of light nuclei

> In this review, we summarize the theoretical development on the electric dipole moment of light nuclei. We first describe the nucleon level CP violation and its parametrization. We then present the results of calculations of the EDM of light nuclei in the ab initio approach and in the cluster model. The analysis of the effect of several models beyond standard model is presented, together with the prospects for its discovery. The advantage of the electric dipole moment of light nuclei is focused in the point of view of the many-body physics. The evaluations of the nuclear electric dipole moment generated by the $θ$-term and by the CP phase of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix are also reviewed.

## Key claim

This review argues that the electric dipole moment (EDM) of light nuclei is a promising observable for discovering new CP-violating physics beyond the Standard Model, but the theoretical evaluation of nuclear EDMs is subject to significant uncertainties, particularly from short-range CP-odd nuclear forces and the lack of systematic error estimates in many ab initio calculations.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical calculations of the EDM of light nuclei (deuteron, ³H, ³He, ⁶Li, ⁹Be, ¹³C) using two main approaches: ab initio methods solving the Schrödinger equation with realistic nuclear potentials (Argonne v18, Reid 93, Nijmegen II, INOY) and the cluster model. The nucleon-level CP violation is parametrized using chiral effective field theory and a phenomenological one-meson-exchange CP-odd nuclear force, with the nuclear EDM decomposed into contributions from the intrinsic nucleon EDM, nuclear polarization due to the CP-odd nuclear force, and exchange current effects.

## Key numbers

- The experimental upper limit for the neutron EDM is d_n < 2.9 × 10⁻²⁶ e cm (Baker).
- The prospective experimental sensitivity for nuclear EDM measurements is d_A ~ O(10⁻²⁹) e cm.
- The pion decay constant is f_π = 93 MeV and the nucleon mass is m_N = 939 MeV.
- The nucleon axial coupling is g_A = 1.27 and the pion mass is m_π = 138 MeV.
- For the deuteron EDM, the isovector pion-exchange coefficient a_π^(1) = (1.42 ± 0.13) × 10⁻² e fm from chiral EFT, and 1.45 × 10⁻² e fm from the Argonne v18 potential.
- For ³He EDM, the isoscalar pion-exchange coefficient a_π^(0) = (0.86 ± 0.10) × 10⁻² e fm and the isovector coefficient a_π^(1) = (1.10 ± 0.15) × 10⁻² e fm from chiral EFT.
- The enhancement factors (spin matrix elements) for the intrinsic nucleon EDM contribution are all less than 1, e.g., ⟨σ_p⟩ = 0.914 and ⟨σ_n⟩ = 0.914 for the deuteron from ab initio (Arv18) calculations.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for nuclear EDM calculations developed by Sushkov (1984), Khriplovich (1997), and Engel (2013), and summarizes recent ab initio results from Yamanaka (2013), Bsaisou (2015), and others. The work enables future experimental searches for nuclear EDMs using storage ring techniques, and provides the theoretical basis for disentangling different CP-violating sources (such as the QCD θ-term and the CKM matrix phase) by comparing EDM measurements across multiple light nuclei.
