# Comment on scalar glueball mass

> We study the interaction of scalar glueball with quark-antiquark and four-quark spinless meson fields within the framework of a generalized linear sigma model in which the trace anomaly of QCD is exactly realized. To determine the pure scalar glueball mass ($m_h$), we consider the decoupling limit in which the scalar glueball decouples from the meson fields. We find the exact relationship $m_h = 2\, h_0$ where $h_0$ is the vacuum expectation value of the scalar glueball field independent of the properties of the framework used.

# Comment on scalar glueball mass

## Key claim
The pure scalar glueball mass $m_h$ is exactly related to its vacuum expectation value by $m_h = 2 h_0$ in the decoupling limit where the glueball does not mix with quark-antiquark or four-quark meson fields, and this relationship is independent of the specific form of the potential used in the generalized linear sigma model.

## Method
The authors study a generalized linear sigma model containing chiral nonet fields for quark-antiquark ($M$) and four-quark ($M'$) mesons, plus scalar ($h$) and pseudoscalar ($g$) glueball fields, with the Lagrangian constructed to exactly satisfy both the U(1)$_{\rm A}$ anomaly and the trace anomaly of QCD. They impose the decoupling limit by setting all mixed second derivatives of the potential between the glueball and meson fields to zero at the vacuum, then derive the glueball mass from the second derivative of the potential with respect to $h$ evaluated at the vacuum expectation values. A model-independent derivation is also given by differentiating the trace anomaly condition with respect to $h$ and taking the vacuum expectation value.

## Key numbers
- The pure scalar glueball mass satisfies $m_h = 2 h_0$, where $h_0$ is the vacuum expectation value of the scalar glueball field.
- Using the gluon condensate from QCD sum-rules, $\langle \alpha_s G^2\rangle = (0.070 \pm 0.013)$ GeV$^4$, the authors obtain $h_x^{\rm min} = 0.057$ GeV and $h_x^{\rm max} = 0.083$ GeV.
- With these inputs and an adjusted cutoff $m_c$, the resulting $h_0$ values are 0.887 GeV and 0.974 GeV, leading to pure scalar glueball masses of 1.77 GeV and 1.95 GeV respectively.

## Context
This work extends the generalized linear sigma model framework developed by the same authors (Fariborz and Jora, previous papers) for studying scalar and pseudoscalar mesons below and above 1 GeV, by focusing specifically on the pure scalar glueball in the decoupling limit. The result provides a bridge between QCD sum-rules and lattice QCD determinations of the gluon condensate and hadronic model predictions for the scalar glueball mass, enabling future work to connect quark-gluon level inputs to low-energy QCD phenomenology. The derived masses overlap with some estimates from lattice QCD and chiral model approaches cited in the paper.
