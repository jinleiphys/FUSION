# Richardson-Gaudin Algebras and the Exact Solutions of the Proton-Neutron Pairing

> Many exactly solvable models are based on Lie algebras. The pairing interaction is important in nuclear physics and its exact solution for identical particles in non-degenerate single-particle levels was first given by Richardson in 1963. His solution and its generalization to Richardson-Gaudin quasi-exactly solvable models have attracted the attention of many contemporary researchers and resulted in the exact solution of the isovector pn-pairing within the so(5) RG-model and the equal strength spin-isospin pn-pairing within the so(8) RG-model. Basic properties of the RG-models are summarized and possible applications to nuclear physics are emphasized. # Richardson-Gaudin Algebras and the Exact Solutions of the Proton-Neutron Pairing

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the exact solution of the isovector proton-neutron (pn) pairing in nuclei can be obtained within an so(5) Richardson-Gaudin (RG) model, and the equal-strength spin-isospin pn-pairing within an so(8) RG model, but only when the single-particle energy splitting that breaks dynamical symmetry still preserves integrability through a spectral Gaudin algebra extension.

## Method

The authors construct Gaudin algebras as infinite-dimensional extensions of Lie algebras, where each generator acquires a spectral parameter dependence, and the integrals of motion are obtained from the residues of the commuting operator K(lambda) at the single-particle energy parameters z_i. They then specialize to fermion realizations of the generators, setting z_i = 2*epsilon_i for single-particle energies epsilon_i, and derive the generalized Richardson equations whose solutions give the pair energies e_{a,alpha}. For the so(5) case, the two sets of spectral parameters (v_alpha and w_gamma) correspond to pair energies and isospin-related parameters, while for the so(8) case, four sets of spectral parameters arise from the rank-4 algebra.

## Key numbers

- For the so(5) T=1 pairing model, the coupling constant g satisfies: 1/g = sum_i [Omega_i / (2*epsilon_i - v_alpha)] + sum_{beta != alpha} [2/(v_alpha - v_beta)] + sum_{gamma=1}^{M-T} [1/(w_gamma - v_alpha)], where Omega_i = (2j+1)/2 is the degeneracy of level i, M is the total number of pairs, and T is the isospin.
- The total energy for the so(5) model is E = sum_{alpha=1}^{M} v_alpha, where v_alpha are the pair energies.
- For the so(8) spin-isospin pairing model, the energy is E = sum_{alpha=1}^{M_1} e_alpha, where M_1 = lambda_1 + lambda_2 + lambda_3 + lambda_4 is the total number of pairs M, with the lambda_i constrained by sum_i Omega_i >= lambda_1 >= lambda_2 >= lambda_3 >= lambda_4 >= 0.
- The so(8) model has four sets of spectral parameters with sizes: M_1 = M, M_2 = lambda_2 + lambda_3 + lambda_4, M_3 = lambda_3 + lambda_4, and M_4 = lambda_4.

## Context

This work extends Richardson's original 1963 exact solution of the pairing interaction for identical particles in non-degenerate single-particle levels (Richardson, 1963; Richardson, 1966) to the proton-neutron case using the so(5) and so(8) Lie algebras. It builds on the Gaudin algebra formalism developed by Ushveridize and the generalized Richardson equations of Links (2002) and Asorey et al. The paper connects to earlier nuclear models including Elliott's su(3) symmetry (Elliott, 1958), Wigner's su(4) spin-isospin symmetry (Wigner, 1937), and the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Models, while enabling exact treatment of pairing in realistic nuclear systems with non-degenerate single-particle levels.
