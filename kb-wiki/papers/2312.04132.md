# Spin-flip gluon GTMD $F_{1,2}$ at small-$x$

> Until recently the spin-flip processes in the deep inelastic scatterings are thought to be suppressed in the high energy. We found a positive intercept for the spin-flip generalized transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution (GTMDs) ${\rm Re}(F_{1,2})$ as, \begin{eqnarray} {\rm Re}(F_{1,2}) \sim \left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^{{\bar α}_s\left(4\ln2-8/3\right)} \left(\cos 3ϕ_{kΔ} +\cos ϕ_{kΔ}\right). \nonumber \end{eqnarray} This is done by analytically solving the integro-differential evolution equation for ${\rm Re}(F_{1,2})$, recently proposed by Hatta and Zhou, in the dilute regime. Interestingly, the surviving solution corresponds to conformal spin $n=2$ and carries an explicit $\cos 3ϕ_{kΔ} + \cos ϕ_{kΔ}$ azimuthal dependence. As the imaginary part of $F_{1,2}$, is related to the spin-dependent odderon or gluon Siver function and scales as ${\rm Im}(F_{1,2}) \sim x^{0}$, the positive intercept for ${\rm Re}(F_{1,2})$, implies that it is expected to dominate over the gluon Siver function in the small-$x$ limit - and may directly impact the modeling of unpolarised GTMDs and associated spin-flip processes.

## Key claim

The authors find that the real part of the spin-flip gluon GTMD \(F_{1,2}\) grows with decreasing Bjorken-\(x\) as a positive power of \(1/x\), contrary to the prior belief that spin-flip processes are suppressed at high energy; this result is derived analytically in the dilute regime where nonlinear saturation effects are neglected.

## Method

The authors analytically solve the integro-differential small-\(x\) evolution equation for \(\mathrm{Re}(F_{1,2})\) recently proposed by Hatta and Zhou (2022), working in the dilute regime where nonlinear terms are dropped. They perform a Fourier expansion in the azimuthal angle \(\phi_{k\Delta}\) between the gluon transverse momentum \(k_\perp\) and the momentum transfer \(\Delta_\perp\), then apply a Mellin transform in \(x\) to find the eigenvalues of the evolution kernel. The saddle-point approximation is used to evaluate the leading small-\(x\) behavior for each conformal spin \(n\).

## Key numbers

* The intercept for \(\mathrm{Re}(F_{1,2})\) is \(\bar{\alpha}_s (4\ln 2 - 8/3)\), where \(4\ln 2 - 8/3 = 0.106\) is positive.
* The imaginary part of \(F_{1,2}\), related to the gluon Sivers function, scales as \(\mathrm{Im}(F_{1,2}) \sim x^{0}\) (zero intercept).
* The surviving solution corresponds to conformal spin \(n=2\) and carries an explicit azimuthal dependence \(\cos 3\phi_{k\Delta} + \cos \phi_{k\Delta}\).
* The unpolarized GTMD \(f_{1,1}\) follows the BFKL intercept \(\bar{\alpha}_s 4\ln 2\).
* The diffusion coefficient for the \(n=2\) mode is \(\tau_2 = 14(\zeta(3) - 16/27)\).

## Context

This work extends the recent finding by Hatta and Zhou (2022) that the spin-flip gluon GPD \(E_g\) exhibits Regge behavior similar to the BFKL pomeron, by solving the evolution equation for the underlying GTMD \(F_{1,2}\). The positive intercept implies that \(\mathrm{Re}(F_{1,2})\) will dominate over the gluon Sivers function (the odderon) at small-\(x\), which may impact modeling of unpolarized GTMDs and spin-flip processes at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider. The result also provides the small-\(x\) asymptotics of the related GTMD \(f_{1,3}\) via its relation to \(f_{1,1}\) and \(f_{1,2}\).
