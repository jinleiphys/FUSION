# Net-baryon number variance and kurtosis within nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics

> We study the variance and kurtosis of the net-baryon number in a fluid dynamical model for heavy-ion collisions. It is based on an effective chiral model with dilatons for the strong coupling regime of QCD. Taking into account spinodal instabilities, we demonstrate that this model exhibits a diverging quark number susceptibility and kurtosis all along the spinodal lines of the first-order phase transition, with a change of universality class at the critical end point. During the (3+1) dimensional expansion of a hot and dense fireball, instabilities are created by fluctuations in the explicitly propagated chiral and dilaton field. We find a clear enhancement of event-by-event fluctuations of the baryon number at the critical point and first-order phase transition in comparison with an evolution through the crossover region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, when spinodal instabilities are included in a nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics model, the quark number susceptibility and kurtosis diverge all along the spinodal lines of the first-order phase transition, not only at the critical end point (CEP); the enhancement of event-by-event fluctuations of the net-baryon number is larger for a first-order phase transition than for a CEP, and both are larger than for a crossover.

## Method

The authors couple a linear sigma model with a dilaton field (representing the gluon condensate) to a fluid of quarks and gluons. The sigma field is evolved via a Langevin equation with dissipation and Gaussian white noise, while the dilaton follows a classical equation of motion; the fluid is described by ideal hydrodynamics with source terms from the fields. A spherical droplet of quark-gluon plasma is initialized with a Woods-Saxon profile, and event-by-event fluctuations of the net-baryon number are extracted from the fluid dynamical density during the (3+1)-dimensional expansion, using both a fixed central volume and a rapidity/transverse-momentum cut.

## Key numbers

- The chiral critical end point is located at temperature \(T_{\rm CEP}=89\) MeV and quark chemical potential \(\mu_{\rm CEP}=329\) MeV.
- At a fixed temperature of \(T=40\) MeV, the model exhibits a first-order phase transition; susceptibilities and kurtosis diverge at the isothermal spinodal points.
- The critical exponent for the quark number susceptibility at the spinodal point is \(\gamma=1/2\); at the CEP it is \(\gamma=2/3\).
- The critical exponent for the kurtosis is \(\zeta=2\) for both the CEP and the spinodal point.
- The variance of net-baryon number fluctuations at a first-order phase transition is enhanced by a factor of 5 to 6 compared to a CEP.
- The total quark number in each event is fixed to \(N_{\rm q}=67\).

## Context

This work extends earlier nonequilibrium fluid dynamical studies of the chiral phase transition (Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2013) by replacing the Polyakov loop with a dilaton field, which avoids the problem of negative pressures seen in the Polyakov-quark-meson model (Steinheimer 2012). It also builds on the demonstration by Sasaki (2007) that quark number susceptibility diverges along spinodal lines in the NJL model. The results suggest that first-order phase transitions may produce stronger fluctuation signals than the CEP, offering a more accessible experimental signature for future experiments at FAIR and NICA.
