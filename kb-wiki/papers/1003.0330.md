# The impact of sharp screening on the Coulomb scattering problem in three dimensions

> The scattering problem for two particles interacting via the Coulomb potential is examined for the case where the potential has a sharp cut-off at some distance. The problem is solved for two complimentary situations, firstly when the interior part of the Coulomb potential is left in the Hamiltonian and, secondly, when the long range tail is considered as the potential. The partial wave results are summed up to obtain the wave function in three dimensions. It is shown that in the domains where the wave function is expected to be proportional to the known solutions, the proportionality is given by an operator acting on the angular part of the wave function. The explicit representation for this operator is obtained in the basis of Legendre polynomials. We proposed a driven Schrödinger equation including an inhomogeneous term of the finite range with purely outgoing asymptotics for its solution in the case of the three dimensional scattering problem with long range potentials.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for a sharply cut-off Coulomb potential \(V_R\), the three-dimensional wave function inside the cut-off radius \(r<R\) is related to the full Coulomb wave function by an operator acting on the angular coordinate, not merely by a multiplicative constant; the asymptotic regularizing factor \(\rme^{-\rmi\eta\log2kR}\) emerges only in the limit \(R\to\infty\) and in the sense of strong operator topology, with an extra delta-function term in the scattering amplitude that was previously unknown.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Schrödinger equation for a Coulomb potential with a sharp cut-off at radius \(R\) by matching partial-wave solutions at \(r=R\): inside the cut-off the solution is proportional to the regular Coulomb function \(F_\ell(\eta,kr)\), and outside it is a free spherical wave plus an outgoing scattered wave. The partial-wave series is summed in three dimensions using distribution theory with test functions, and the relation between the cut-off wave function and the full Coulomb wave function is expressed as an operator \(\mathbf{a}_R\) acting on the angular variable in the space \(L_2(-1,1)\) of square-integrable functions of \(u=\cos\theta\). The complementary problem with the exterior tail potential \(V^R\) is solved analogously, and a driven Schrödinger equation with a finite-range inhomogeneous term is constructed.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic phase shift for the cut-off potential for \(kR \gg \ell(\ell+1)+\eta^2\) is \(\delta_{R\ell} \sim \sigma_\ell - \eta\log 2kR\), where \(\sigma_\ell\) is the Coulomb phase shift.
* The asymptotic regularizing factor for the wave function in the region \(r<R\) as \(R\to\infty\) is \(\rme^{-\rmi\eta\log2kR}\), acting as a multiplicative constant on the Coulomb wave function in the strong operator topology.
* The scattering amplitude for the cut-off potential \(V_R\) asymptotically contains a term \(-\frac{2}{k}\,\rme^{-\rmi\eta\log 2kR}\sin( \eta\log 2kR)\,\delta(u-1)\) with a delta-function singularity in the forward direction.
* The amplitude for the exterior tail potential \(V^R\) satisfies \(A^R \simeq - \rme^{2\rmi \eta \log 2kR} A_R\) for large \(R\).

## Context

This work extends the formal analysis of sharp Coulomb screening by Ford (1964) and Taylor (1974) by providing a rigorous three-dimensional operator relation between the cut-off and full Coulomb wave functions, valid for any screening radius \(R\). It corrects and generalizes the partial-wave summation approach of Glöckle (1998), which was shown by Popov (2000) to be erroneous for all \(r\). The results enable the use of exterior complex scaling methods, as developed by Yakovlev et al. (2007) and Elander et al. (2008), to solve three-dimensional scattering problems with long-range Coulomb interactions via a driven Schrödinger equation with a finite-range inhomogeneous term.
