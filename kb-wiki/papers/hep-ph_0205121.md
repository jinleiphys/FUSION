# Finite width effects in ϕradiative decays

> The decay widths ϕ\to γf_0(980) and ϕ\to γa_0(980) are calculated taking into account the finite widths of the scalar resonances f_0(980) and a_0(980). The latter are shown to be essential in order to obtain meaningful results. Simultaneously we also study the decays ϕ\to γπ^0π^0 and γπ^0ηwhere a good reproduction of the recent experimental data is obtained, pointing out the necessity of a ϕγK^0\bar{K}^0 contact vertex. The calculated decay rates to γf_0(980) and γa_0(980) are in good agreement with the experimental ones without invoking isospin breaking in the couplings of the f_0(980) and a_0(980) resonances to the K^+ K^- and K^0 \bar{K}^0 channels, at odds with recent proposals. The derived formula for calculating these ϕradiative decay widths can be also applied in their own experimental analyses in order to obtain more precise results.

## Key claim

The finite widths of the scalar resonances \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) are essential for obtaining meaningful results for the decay widths \(\phi \to \gamma f_0(980)\) and \(\phi \to \gamma a_0(980)\), and when these widths are properly included, the experimental decay rates can be reproduced without invoking isospin breaking in the couplings of these resonances to the \(K^+ K^-\) and \(K^0 \bar{K}^0\) channels.

## Method

The author calculates the decay widths \(\phi \to \gamma \pi^0 \pi^0\) and \(\phi \to \gamma \pi^0 \eta\) using kaon loop diagrams, including both charged kaon loops (with Bremsstrahlung and contact terms) and a neutral kaon loop contribution parameterized by a contact \(\phi \gamma K \bar{K}\) vertex with coupling \(\zeta_I\). The strong final-state interactions of the pseudoscalar mesons are described by S-wave meson-meson scattering amplitudes from chiral perturbation theory combined with unitarization via the Inverse Amplitude Method or the approach of Oller and Oset (1997). For the \(\phi \to \gamma R\) decays, the finite widths of the scalar resonances are incorporated by using an energy distribution \(f_R(Q^0)\) derived from unitarity, relating it to the imaginary part of the \(K^+ K^- \to K^+ K^-\) T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The pole position of the \(f_0(980)\) is \(\sqrt{s_{f_0}} = (992.6 - i\,11.8)\) MeV, with couplings \(|g_{\pi\pi}^{f_0}| = 1.90\) GeV and \(|g_{K\bar{K}}^{f_0}| = 3.80\) GeV.
- The pole position of the \(a_0(980)\) is \(\sqrt{s_{a_0}} = (1009.2 - i\,56.1)\) MeV, with couplings \(|g_{\pi\eta}^{a_0}| = 3.54\) GeV and \(|g_{K\bar{K}}^{a_0}| = 5.20\) GeV.
- The branching ratio for \(f_0(980) \to \pi\pi\) is \(Br^{f_0}_{\pi\pi} = 0.7\) and for \(a_0(980) \to \pi\eta\) is \(Br^{a_0}_{\pi\eta} = 0.63\).
- The weak pion decay constant is \(f \simeq 93\) MeV, and the vector coupling \(G_V = 55\) MeV reproduces the experimental width \(\Gamma(\phi \to K^+ K^-)\).
- A change of the scalar resonance mass by 10 MeV from 980 MeV changes the photon momentum \(|\mathbf{k}|\) by a factor of 1.7 and \(|\mathbf{k}|^3\) by a factor of five.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Oller (2001) on \(\phi \to \gamma K^0 \bar{K}^0\) decays and the studies of Marco et al. (1999) on \(\phi \to \gamma \pi^0 \pi^0\) and \(\gamma \pi^0 \eta\), connecting these processes with chiral symmetry and unitarity. It contradicts the proposal of Close et al. (2002) that large isospin breaking in the couplings of \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) to kaon channels is necessary to explain the experimental decay widths. The derived formula for \(\Gamma(\phi \to \gamma R)\) can be applied in experimental analyses to obtain more precise results, and the formalism enables a phenomenological parameterization of the invariant mass distributions \(d\Gamma(\phi \to \gamma M^0 N^0)/dm_{M^0 N^0}\) via the relation \(d\Gamma(\phi \to \gamma M^0 N^0)/d|\mathbf{k}| = d\Gamma(\phi \to \gamma R)/d|\mathbf{k}| \cdot Br^R_{M^0 N^0}(Q^2)\).
