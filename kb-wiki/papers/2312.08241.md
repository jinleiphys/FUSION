# Complex-valued in-medium potential between heavy impurities in ultracold atoms

> We formulate the induced potential in a finite temperature cold atomic medium between two heavy impurities, or polarons, which is shown to be \textit{complex-valued} in general. The imaginary part of the complex-valued potential describes a decoherence effect, and thus, the resulting Schrödinger equation for the two polarons acquires a non-Hermitian term. We apply the developed formulation to two representative cases of polarons interacting with medium particles through the $s$-wave contact interaction: (i) the normal phase of single-component (i.e., spin-polarized) fermions using the fermionic field theory, and (ii) a superfluid phase using the superfluid effective field theory, which is valid either for a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) of a single-component Bose gas or for the BEC-BCS crossover in two-component fermions at a low-energy regime. Computing the leading-order term, the imaginary part of the potential in both cases is found to show a universal $r^{-2}$ behavior at long distance. We propose three experimental ways to observe the effects of the universal imaginary potential in cold atoms.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the imaginary part of the in-medium potential between two heavy impurities (polarons) in ultracold atomic gases universally decays as \(r^{-2}\) at long distances for both normal Fermi and superfluid media, but this result is derived under the assumption of weak impurity-medium coupling and is shown to hold when collisions between the polaron and elementary excitations in the medium are nearly elastic.

## Method

The authors formulate the complex-valued in-medium potential using a real-time Green's function approach adapted from hot QCD plasma physics, defining the potential from the long-time behavior of a two-impurity correlation function. For the weak-coupling regime, they derive perturbative formulas for the real and imaginary parts of the potential in terms of the retarded density-density Green's function of the medium. They then evaluate these formulas for two cases: (i) a non-interacting single-component Fermi gas using fermionic field theory, and (ii) a superfluid phase using an effective field theory for superfluid phonons that is valid for both Bose-Einstein condensates and the BEC-BCS crossover in two-component fermions.

## Key numbers

* For Fermi polarons at long distance (\(k_F r \gg 1\)), the imaginary potential scales as \(V_{\mathrm{Im}}(r) / T_F \simeq - \frac{2(k_F a_{\mathrm{IM}})^2}{\pi} \frac{T/T_F}{1+e^{-T_F/T}} \frac{1}{(k_F r)^2}\), where \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum, \(a_{\mathrm{IM}}\) is the impurity-medium scattering length, and \(T_F\) is the Fermi temperature.
* For Fermi polarons at short distance (\(k_F r \ll 1\)), the imaginary potential is \(V_{\mathrm{Im}}(r \ll k_F^{-1}) / T_F \simeq - \frac{4(k_F a_{\mathrm{IM}})^2}{\pi} \left(\frac{T}{T_F}\right)^2 \log(1 + e^{T_F/T})\).
* In the low-temperature limit (\(T/T_F \ll 1\)) for Fermi polarons, the imaginary potential becomes \(V_{\mathrm{Im}}(\mathbf{r}) \xrightarrow{T/T_F \ll 1} - \frac{8 (k_F a_{\mathrm{IM}})^2}{\pi} T f(2 k_F r, 0)\) with \(f(y,0) = (1 - \cos y)/y^2\), showing oscillatory behavior with period controlled by \(k_F\).

## Context

This work extends the study of medium-induced interactions between polarons, which previously focused on real-valued potentials such as the RKKY interaction for fermionic media (PhysRevA.79.013629, Macneill 2011, Endo 2013, Enss 2020) and monotonic attractive interactions for bosonic media (Pethick 2008, Naidon 2018, Fujii 2022). The authors build on the universal long-range real potential found by Fujii (2022) for superfluid media and on the complex potential formalism developed for heavy quarks in QCD plasmas (Laine 2006, Beraudo 2007, Brambilla 2008, Rothkopf 2011). The work enables experimental detection of the imaginary potential through three proposed methods: radio-frequency interferometry, spectral width measurements of bipolarons, and relaxation of density fluctuations induced by a single impurity.
