# On Making Predictions With Effective Field Theories In Nuclear Physics

> Based on the effective field theory previously formulated by us to accurately postdict all low-energy two-nucleon properties as well as predict certain electroweak transitions in heavy nuclei, we make parameter-free predictions for the polarized np capture process \vec{n}+\vec{p} --> d +γpresently being measured at the Institut Laue-Langevin in Grenoble. Other participants of this meeting are invited to make their own predictions using their preferred approaches and join the bet for the best prediction to confront the forthcoming experiment.

## Key claim

The authors make parameter-free predictions for the polarized np capture process \(\vec{n}+\vec{p}\rightarrow d+\gamma\) using an effective field theory (EFT) scheme that combines realistic wave functions (Argonne \(v_{18}\)) with chiral perturbation theory for exchange currents, but they note that the predictions rely on a "hard-core cutoff scheme" (HCCS) or a modified version (MHCCS) to handle short-distance uncertainties, and the results for the isoscalar M1 and E2 matrix elements are not protected by the chiral filter, making them potentially sensitive to unknown counter terms.

## Method

The authors adopt the Weinberg power-counting scheme, iterating the irreducible vertex to all orders by solving the Schrödinger equation with the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential. They compute the single-particle matrix elements using the resulting wave functions and add exchange-current contributions from baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). For the isoscalar M1 and E2 operators, which lack chiral-filter protection, they include one-loop corrections and counter terms, and they handle short-distance physics either by killing zero-range terms with a hard-core cutoff (HCCS) or by replacing delta functions with delta-shell forms to fix the remaining parameter from the deuteron magnetic moment (MHCCS).

## Key numbers

* Unpolarized np capture cross section: \(\sigma_{th} = 334 \pm 3\) mb, with the leading single-particle contribution at 305.6 mb and the remainder from exchange currents dominated by soft one-pion exchange.
* Deuteron magnetic moment \(\mu_d\) in HCCS: ranges from 0.8390 to 0.8443 nuclear magneton for hard-core radii \(r_c = 0.01\) to 0.8 fm, compared to the experimental value 0.8574.
* Deuteron quadrupole moment \(Q_d\) in HCCS: 0.2710 fm\(^2\), independent of \(r_c\) within the range, compared to the experimental value 0.2859 fm\(^2\).
* Ratio \(\mathcal{R}_{\rm M1} \equiv \MV/\MS\) in HCCS: \(-\mathcal{R}_{\rm M1} \times 10^3\) ranges from 0.788 to 0.887 for \(r_c = 0.01\) to 0.8 fm.
* Ratio \(\mathcal{R}_{\rm E2} \equiv \EV/\MS\) in HCCS: \(\mathcal{R}_{\rm E2} \times 10^3 = 0.242\), independent of \(r_c\).
* Photon circular polarization \(P_\gamma\) in HCCS: \(-P_\gamma \times 10^3\) ranges from 1.46 to 1.60 for \(|\vec{P}_n| = 1\) and \(r_c = 0.01\) to 0.8 fm.
* Photon anisotropy \(\eta\) in HCCS for \(pP = 1\): ranges from 0.56 to 0.61 for \(r_c = 0.01\) to 0.8 fm; for \(pP = 0.96\): \(\eta \times 10^5\) ranges from 1.1 to 1.3; for \(pP = (0.5)^2\): \(\eta \times 10^7\) ranges from 1.5 to 1.8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier EFT formulation (Park et al., 1997-1999) that accurately postdicted low-energy two-nucleon properties and predicted electroweak transitions in heavy nuclei, including axial-charge transitions with enhancements from the chiral filter and Brown-Rho scaling. The predictions for polarized np capture are intended to be tested by the ongoing experiment at the Institut Laue-Langevin (Müller et al.), and the authors invite other groups to make their own predictions using alternative approaches. The paper contrasts its Weinberg-scheme-based hybrid approach with the power-divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), which the authors argue is less predictive for this process.
