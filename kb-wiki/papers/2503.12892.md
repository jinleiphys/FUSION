# On the mapping after and before truncation in the boson expansion theory

> Using the norm operator method, which extends and corrects the conventional boson expansion theories, we investigate two boson mappings of the boson expansion theory, the so-called mapping after truncation and the mapping before truncation. The difference between them stems from the treatment of the phonon excitation modes; those not adopted as boson excitation modes in the former mapping are first all adopted as boson excitation modes and then truncated later in the latter mapping. If and only if the commutation relations among the phonon operators are closed among the excitation modes adopted as the boson excitation modes in the mapping after truncation, the mapping after and the mapping before truncation coincide, not depending on the types, Hermitian and non-Hermitian. We also investigate the Park operator, which judges whether a boson state vector is physical, and reveal that the conventional claim, which claims that it is applicable only when the mapping is that of the whole fermion space, is incorrect.

## Key claim

The mapping after truncation (MAT) and the mapping before truncation (MBT) coincide if and only if the commutation relations among the phonon operators are closed among the excitation modes adopted as boson excitation modes in MAT, and this equivalence holds regardless of whether the mapping is Hermitian or non-Hermitian. The conventional claim that the Park operator is applicable only when the mapping is that of the whole fermion space is incorrect.

## Method

The author uses the norm operator method, which extends and corrects conventional boson expansion theories, to compare two boson mappings: MAT, which maps only the fermion subspace constructed by selected phonons onto a boson subspace with the same excitation modes, and MBT, which first adopts all phonon excitation modes as boson excitations and then truncates the non-adopted modes later. The analysis investigates the conditions under which the two mappings coincide by examining the closure of phonon commutation relations within the selected excitation modes, and re-examines the Park operator's applicability in truncated boson subspaces.

## Key numbers

* The norm operator for the whole fermion space satisfies $\hat Z^{(A)} = (2\hat N_B^{(A)}-1)!!\hat T_B^{(A)}$, where $\hat N_B^{(A)}$ is the boson number operator for all modes.
* When the non-adopted modes discarding (NAMD) condition holds, the norm operator for the truncated space satisfies $\hat Z = (2\hat N_B-1)!!\hat T_B$, where $\hat N_B$ is the boson number operator for the selected modes.
* The Dyson boson expansion of the phonon creation operator is $(X_t^\dagger)_D = b_t^\dagger - \frac{1}{2}\sum_{t_1 t_2}\sum_{t'_1} Y(t t_1 t_2 t'_1) b_{t_1}^\dagger b_{t_2}^\dagger b_{t'_1}$.
* The Park operator satisfies $\hat S \hat T_B^{(W)} = 0$ for the whole fermion space mapping, and $\hat S \hat T_B = 0$ when $\hat W = 0$ (NAMD holds), but $\hat S \hat T_B \neq 0$ when $\hat W \neq 0$.

## Context

This work extends the norm operator method developed by Taniguchi (2024) and refines the understanding of boson expansion theories originally formulated by Usui (1960), Marshalek, et al. (1964), and Kishimoto and Tamura (1983). It corrects the conventional claim by Kishimoto and Tamura (1983) and Takada (2001) that the Park operator is only meaningful for the whole fermion space mapping, showing instead that it properly judges physical state vectors in MAT when the closed-algebra approximation holds. The results enable proper treatment of truncated boson subspaces in practical nuclear structure calculations without relying on the incorrect non-adopted modes discarding assumption.
