# The Dynamical Dipole Mode in Dissipative Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the effect of a direct Giant Dipole Resonance ($GDR$) excitation in intermediate dinuclear systems with exotic shape and charge distributions formed in charge asymmetric fusion entrance channels. A related enhancement of the $GDR$ gamma yield in the evaporation cascade of the fused nucleus is expected. The dynamical origin of such $GDR$ extra strength will show up in a characteristic anisotropy of the dipole gamma-emission. A fully microscopic analysis of the fusion dynamics is performed with quantitative predictions of the $GDR$ photon yield based on a dynamics- statistics coupling model. In particular we focus our attention on the energy and mass dependence of the effect. We suggest a series of new experiments, in particular some optimal entrance channel conditions. We stress the importance of using the new available radioactive beams.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a "dynamical dipole" mode, a pre-equilibrium Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) excitation, will produce an extra yield of gamma rays in the evaporation cascade of a fused nucleus formed in charge-asymmetric fusion reactions, with the effect showing a characteristic rise and fall as a function of beam energy, rather than a monotonic increase.

## Method

The authors performed a fully microscopic analysis of fusion dynamics using the Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport equation, which incorporates mean-field and two-body collision dynamics self-consistently, with a Skyrme-like parametrization for the mean field and in-medium reduced nucleon-nucleon cross sections. They simulated central collisions for specific systems (e.g., 16O + 98Mo) at various beam energies (4, 8, 14, and 20 MeV/nucleon) to extract the time evolution of the dipole moment in coordinate and momentum space, the number of GDR phonons, and the compound nucleus formation time. These dynamical results were then used as input for a time-dependent evaporation cascade model (Monte Carlo) to calculate the final gamma-ray yield.

## Key numbers

*   For the 16O + 98Mo system at 8 AMeV beam energy, the number of pre-equilibrium GDR phonons at the time of compound nucleus formation, n_GDR^(0), was dynamically calculated to be 0.07.
*   The compound nucleus formation time t_CN for the 16O + 98Mo system was extracted as 80 fm/c at 8 AMeV, 120 fm/c at 4 AMeV, and around 50 fm/c at 14 and 20 AMeV.
*   For the 40Ca + 100Mo system at 4 AMeV, the best fit to experimental data required n_GDR^(0) = 0.14 and a GDR spreading width Gamma^down = 8 MeV.
*   The initial number of phonons n_i at the touching configuration for the 40Ca + 100Mo system was estimated from the analytical formula to be 1.4 times a factor involving the mass and charge numbers of the projectile and target.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of a dynamical dipole mode by Chomaz et al. (1993) by providing a fully microscopic, dynamical calculation of the pre-equilibrium GDR phonon population, linking it directly to fusion dynamics. It provides a quantitative framework to interpret experimental observations of enhanced GDR gamma-ray yields in charge-asymmetric fusion reactions, such as those reported by Flibotte et al. (1996) for 40Ca + 100Mo and Cinausero et al. (1998) for 16O + 98Mo. The results enable predictions for optimal experimental conditions, including the use of radioactive beams, and suggest that the dynamical dipole effect could be a cooling mechanism to favor the fusion of very heavy nuclear systems.
