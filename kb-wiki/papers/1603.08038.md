# Test of the Glauber Formula for Nucleon-Deuteron Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> A high-precision test of the Glauber formula for the amplitude of nucleon-deuteron scattering is performed. Nucleon-nucleon amplitudes used in the calculations depend on the spins of interacting particles, phase shifts, and mixing parameters. These amplitudes were derived by using the Nijmegen potentials. The differential cross sections for nucleon-deuteron scattering were calculated for the projectile-nucleon energies of 65, 95, 135, 150, 190, and 250~MeV, and the results of these calculations were compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The Glauber formula for nucleon-deuteron scattering works well beyond its traditional validity condition \(q \ll k\) (which corresponds to \(\theta < 10^\circ\)), with the angular range extending to \(\theta < 30^\circ\) for projectile-nucleon energies 65–150 MeV and broadening to \(\theta < 70^\circ\) at 190 and 250 MeV, provided the inverse momentum \(k^{-1}\) becomes commensurate with the deuteron root-mean-square radius rather than much smaller.

## Method

The author calculated differential cross sections for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering using the Glauber eikonal amplitude, which includes both single-scattering (impulse) and double-scattering (shadowing) terms. The nucleon-nucleon amplitudes were expressed in a general spin-dependent form with coefficients derived from phase shifts and mixing parameters obtained from the Nijmegen potentials (Nijm I, Nijm II, Nijm 93, and Reid 93). Cross sections were computed at six projectile-nucleon energies and compared with experimental data from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections were calculated for projectile-nucleon energies of 65, 95, 135, 150, 190, and 250 MeV.
- The traditional validity condition for the Glauber formula gives \(\theta < 10^\circ\).
- The Glauber formalism describes experimental data in the angular range \(\theta < 30^\circ\) for energies 65–150 MeV.
- At 190 and 250 MeV, the angular range broadens to \(\theta < 70^\circ\).

## Context

This work provides a high-precision test of the Glauber method for the simplest nucleon-nucleus system, extending earlier studies that had surprisingly rarely verified the accuracy and applicability range of the Glauber ansatz (as noted in reference [1] of the paper). The author uses realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijm I, Nijm II, Nijm 93, Reid 93) to derive the scattering amplitudes, going beyond earlier treatments that used simpler parameterizations. The results enable more confident application of the Glauber formalism to nucleon-deuteron scattering at intermediate energies and suggest that the method may be valid over a wider angular range than traditionally assumed for other few-body systems.
