# Shell-model-like approach based on cranking covariant density functional theory with a separable pairing force

> The shell-model-like approach (SLAP) based on cranking covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with a separable pairing force is developed. The developed cranking CDFT-SLAP with separable pairing force is applied to investigate the rotational spectra in $^{60}$Fe, including the positive-parity yrast band and two negative-parity signature partner bands, in comparison with the cranking CDFT-SLAP with monopole pairing force calculations. Excellent agreement with the available data is achieved.

## Key claim

The shell-model-like approach (SLAP) based on cranking covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with a separable pairing force achieves excellent agreement with experimental rotational spectra in $^{60}$Fe, with the caveat that the separable pairing force yields better convergence with respect to the many-particle configuration (MPC) space dimension than the monopole pairing force, and that the pairing energies from the separable force decrease more slowly with rotational frequency.

## Method

The cranking CDFT-SLAP with separable pairing force was developed by replacing the monopole pairing force in the existing cranking CDFT-SLAP framework with a finite-range separable Gogny pairing force represented as a sum of separable terms in the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis. The many-body Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a truncated many-particle configuration space with exact particle number conservation, and the occupation probabilities were iterated back into the densities and currents to achieve self-consistency. The method was applied to calculate the positive-parity yrast band and two negative-parity signature partner bands in $^{60}$Fe using the PC-PK1 density functional in the particle-hole channel.

## Key numbers

- For $^{60}$Fe, the separable pairing force parameters were $G = -728$ MeV fm$^3$ and $a = 0.644$ fm, while the monopole pairing force used $G_n = G_p = 0.8$ MeV.
- Increasing the major oscillator shells $N_f$ from 12 to 14 changed the total energy by 0.004% and the alignment by 0.760% at $\hbar\omega = 0.2$ MeV; thus $N_f = 12$ was used.
- Increasing the MPC space dimension from 1000 to 2000 changed the total energy by 0.151% and the alignment by 0.667% at $\hbar\omega = 0.2$ MeV for the separable pairing force; MPC dimensions of 1000 were used for both neutrons and protons.
- The band crossing frequency for band A obtained with the separable pairing force was $\hbar\omega \sim 0.85$ MeV, compared to $\hbar\omega \sim 0.75$ MeV from the monopole pairing force and no-pairing calculations.
- For band B, both separable and monopole pairing force calculations reproduced the experimental band crossing at $\hbar\omega = 1.1$ MeV.
- The deformation parameter $\beta$ for band A jumped from $\beta \approx 0.19$ to $\beta \approx 0.29$ around the band crossing.
- The $\beta$ obtained from the separable pairing force was about 0.02 larger than that from the monopole pairing force near the bandhead of band A.

## Context

This work extends the cranking CDFT-SLAP previously developed by Shi et al. (2018) for $^{60}$Fe, which used a monopole pairing force, by implementing a separable version of the Gogny pairing force introduced by Tian et al. (2009). The separable pairing force avoids ultraviolet divergence and requires less computational time than other finite-range pairing forces, and it has been previously implemented in tilted-axis cranking CDFT for studies of $^{109}$Ag by Wang et al. (2017) and $^{198,199}$Pb by Wang et al. (2018). The present results enable systematic studies of rotational bands in medium-mass and heavy nuclei with improved treatment of pairing correlations that include contributions from pairs with higher angular momentum beyond the $J=0$ Cooper pairs captured by monopole pairing.
