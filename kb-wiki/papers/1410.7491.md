# The leading hadronic contribution to (g-2) of the muon: The chiral behavior using the mixed representation method

> We extend our analysis of the leading hadronic contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon using the mixed representation method to study its chiral behavior. We present results derived from local-conserved two-point lattice vector correlation functions, computed on a subset of light two-flavor ensembles made available to us through the CLS effort with pion masses as low as 190 MeV. The data is analyzed also using the more standard four-momentum method. Both methods are systematically compared as the calculations approach the physical point.

## Key claim
The mixed representation method and the standard four-momentum method yield consistent results for the leading hadronic contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, but only if the long-distance behavior of the vector correlator is accurately modeled; the paper presents preliminary results with pion masses as low as 190 MeV, noting that systematic uncertainties from the two methods remain within quoted errors.

## Method
The authors computed the hadronic vacuum polarization function \(\widehat{\Pi}(Q^2)\) using two approaches on \(N_f=2\) CLS gauge ensembles with O(\(a\))-improved Wilson fermions at a fixed lattice spacing \(a = 0.0631(21)\) fm. The four-momentum method extracted \(\Pi(Q^2)\) from the Fourier transform of the vector current-current correlator at discrete momenta, using twisted boundary conditions and a Padé fit to extrapolate to \(Q^2=0\). The mixed representation method computed \(\widehat{\Pi}(Q^2)\) directly from the Euclidean-time integral of the zero-momentum vector correlator \(G(x_0,\vec k=0)\), extending the correlator beyond \(x_0 \simeq 1.1\) fm by a single exponential ground-state fit.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses: 451 MeV, 324 MeV, 277 MeV, and 190 MeV, on ensembles E5, F6, F7, and G8 respectively (lattice spacing \(a = 0.0631(21)\) fm).
- Lattice sizes: \(64 \times 32^3\) (2.0 fm), \(96 \times 48^3\) (3.0 fm), and \(128 \times 64^3\) (4.0 fm).
- The mixed representation correlator signal is lost beyond \(x_0/a = 19\) (corresponding to \(x_0 \geq 1.1\) fm) at the current statistics.
- The muon mass is taken as \(m_\mu \simeq 105.65\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD analysis of \(a_\mu^{HLO}\) using the mixed representation method (Francis et al., 2013; Bernecker and Meyer, 2011) and the four-momentum method (Della Morte et al., 2012) to study the chiral behavior down to \(m_\pi = 190\) MeV. It systematically compares the two methods as cross-checks of systematic uncertainties, and identifies that precision results require improved determination of the low-lying vector spectrum, e.g., via a GEVP with additional interpolating operators. The results enable future precision determinations of \(a_\mu^{HLO}\) and straightforward inclusion of disconnected contributions.
