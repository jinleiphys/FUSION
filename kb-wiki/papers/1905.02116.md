# Towards baryon-baryon scattering in manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We study baryon-baryon scattering by applying time-ordered perturbation theory to the manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory. We derive the corresponding diagrammatic rules paying special attention to complications caused by momentum-dependent interactions and propagators of particles with non-zero spin. We define the effective potential as a sum of two-baryon irreducible contributions of time-ordered diagrams and derive a system of integral equations for the scattering amplitude, which provides a coupled-channel generalization of the Kadyshevsky equation. The obtained leading-order baryon-baryon potentials are perturbatively renormalizable, and the corresponding integral equations have unique solutions in all partial waves. We discuss the issue of additional finite subtractions required to improve the ultraviolet convergence of (finite) loop integrals on the example of nucleon-nucleon scattering in the $^3P_0$ partial wave. Assuming that corrections beyond leading order can be treated perturbatively, we obtain a fully renormalizable formalism which can be employed to study baryon-baryon scattering.

## Key claim

The authors develop a systematic, manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of time-ordered perturbation theory for SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory applied to baryon-baryon scattering, providing a perturbatively renormalizable framework that generalizes the modified Weinberg approach to the strange sector, assuming that corrections beyond leading order can be treated perturbatively.

## Method

The authors start from the manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian of SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory and derive diagrammatic rules for time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) by integrating over zeroth components of loop momenta in Feynman diagrams, paying special attention to complications from momentum-dependent interactions and propagators of particles with non-zero spin. They define the effective potential as a sum of two-baryon-irreducible TOPT diagrams and derive a system of coupled-channel integral equations for the scattering amplitude, which provides a generalization of the Kadyshevsky equation. The leading-order baryon-baryon potentials are constructed from contact interactions without derivatives and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchange contributions, with the full relativistic energy expressions retained in the denominators of the meson-exchange potentials.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order effective Lagrangian for single-baryon interactions contains coupling constants D and F, with the axial coupling g_A = D + F used in the one-pion exchange expression.
* The leading-order four-baryon contact Lagrangian involves five Dirac structures: Gamma_1 = 1, Gamma_2 = gamma^mu, Gamma_3 = sigma^{munu}, Gamma_4 = gamma^mu gamma_5, and Gamma_5 = gamma_5, with the Gamma_5 term starting to contribute at next-to-leading order.
* The coupled-channel integral equation for baryon-baryon scattering is a 7x7 matrix equation covering strangeness S=0, -1, -2 channels in the isospin limit, with two-body Green functions of the form G^{IJ}(E) = (1/(omega_I omega_J)) * (m_I m_J)/(E - omega_I - omega_J + i epsilon).
* The ultraviolet behavior of the one-loop integral from iterating the leading-order potential scales as 1/k^3 for large loop momentum k, demonstrating ultraviolet finiteness.

## Context

This work extends the modified Weinberg approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering developed by Epelbaum (2012) and applied in subsequent papers to the full SU(3) sector, enabling systematic studies of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions including Lambda N, Sigma N, Lambda Lambda, Xi N, Sigma Sigma, and Sigma Lambda channels. It provides a renormalizable alternative to the non-relativistic chiral EFT framework used by the Bonn-Julich and Munich groups (Haidenbauer, Polinder, and collaborators, 2006-2018) for strangeness S=-1 and S=-2 systems, where finite-cutoff regularization with exponential cutoffs in the range of 500-700 MeV was employed. The formalism enables future chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results from groups including HAL QCD, NPLQCD, Yamazaki, and the Mainz group, and can be applied to study hadronic molecules as in Baru (2015).
