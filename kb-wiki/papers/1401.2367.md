# Hydrodynamic approach to p-Pb

> The formation and collective expansion of the fireball formed in ultrarelativistic p-A and d-A collisions is discussed. Predictions of the hydrodynamic model are compared to recent experimental results. The presence of strong final state interaction effects in the small dense systems is consistent with the observed azimuthal anisotropy of the flow and with the mass dependence of the average transverse momentum and of the elliptic flow. This raises the question of the mechanism explaining such a rapid build up of the collective flow and the large degree of local equilibration needed to justify this scenario.

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic model can semi-quantitatively reproduce the observed azimuthal anisotropy, mass dependence of average transverse momentum, and elliptic flow in p-Pb and d-A collisions, but the agreement is at best semi-quantitative because the small system size and large velocity gradients make the assumption of local equilibration questionable.

## Method

The authors used 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics to model the expansion of the fireball formed in p-Pb and d-A collisions, with initial conditions estimated from a Glauber Monte Carlo model. They compared the calculated two-particle correlation functions, average transverse momenta of identified particles (pions, kaons, protons), and harmonic flow coefficients (v2 and v3) to experimental data from the LHC (CMS and ALICE) and RHIC (PHENIX). They also computed Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry radii for different collision systems.

## Key numbers

- The eccentricity of the fireball in d-Pb collisions can be as large as 0.5 (Fig. 1, left panel).
- The two-particle correlation function coefficients v2 and v3 are extracted from the decomposition C(Δφ) ∝ 1 + C1 cos(Δφ) + 2v2² cos(2Δφ) + 2v3² cos(3Δφ) + ...
- The hydrodynamic model reproduces the pT-dependent flow coefficients for charged particles in central p-Pb collisions (Fig. 4).
- The average transverse momentum ⟨p⊥⟩ shows a mass hierarchy (pions < kaons < protons) that is stronger than predicted by the HIJING superposition model (Fig. 3).
- The predicted femtoscopy radii (Rside, Rout, Rlong) for p-Pb collisions are similar to those in A-A collisions with similar multiplicity (Fig. 5).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic approach previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Heinz 2013, Gale 2013, Luzum 2013) to the smaller p-Pb and d-A systems, following earlier predictions by Bozek (2011) that collective flow could be observable in such collisions. It directly compares to experimental measurements from CMS (2012), ALICE (2013), and PHENIX (2013), and discusses alternative explanations such as color glass condensate initial-state effects (Dusling 2012, 2013) and color reconnection (Ortiz 2013). The results enable further study of collectivity in small systems, including proposed 3He-Au collisions with triangular deformation (Sickles 2013) and tests of isotropization mechanisms using probes sensitive to pressure anisotropy.
