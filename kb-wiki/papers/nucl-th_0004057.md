# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering in a Three Dimensional Approach

> The nucleon-nucleon (NN) t-matrix is calculated directly as function of two vector momenta for different realistic NN potentials. To facilitate this a formalism is developed for solving the two-nucleon Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space without employing a partial wave decomposition. The total spin is treated in a helicity representation. Two different realistic NN interactions, one defined in momentum space and one in coordinate space, are presented in a form suited for this formulation. The angular and momentum dependence of the full amplitude is studied and displayed. A partial wave decomposition of the full amplitude it carried out to compare the presented results with the well known phase shifts provided by those interactions.

## Key claim

The authors develop a formalism for solving the two-nucleon Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space without partial wave decomposition, using a helicity representation for total spin, and demonstrate that this three-dimensional approach can reproduce standard phase shifts from realistic NN potentials, though the method is currently limited to potentials given in operator form rather than those parameterized per angular momentum state.

## Method

The two-nucleon t-matrix is calculated by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space using a helicity basis for the total spin S, where the relative momentum vector is treated directly without angular momentum projection. For S=0 a single integral equation in two variables is solved, while for S=1 the coupled equations are reduced to two coupled integral equations in two variables for helicity values Λ=0 and Λ=1. The Bonn-B potential (defined in momentum space via one-boson-exchange) and the Argonne AV18 potential (defined in coordinate space) are both expressed in terms of Wolfenstein operators and then transformed into the helicity representation for use in the three-dimensional equations.

## Key numbers

- The three-dimensional approach is tested at nucleon laboratory energies between 100-200 MeV, where three-nucleon Faddeev calculations reach the limits of computational resources due to the large number of angular momentum states required.
- The Bonn-B potential uses coupling constants, cutoff functions, and meson masses as given in Ref. [16] (no specific numerical values provided in the text).
- The Argonne AV18 potential omits the electromagnetic part v^EM(NN) in this work, retaining only the one-pion-exchange part v^π(NN) and the phenomenological short-range part v^R(NN).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-dimensional approach for bosonic systems (Fachruddin, Elster, Glöckle, 1995 for bound states; 1998 for scattering) to include spin for nucleon-nucleon scattering, following earlier NN calculations without partial wave decomposition by other groups (Refs. [13,14]) that used individual m-representations for spin states. The helicity representation approach differs from the earlier work of Ref. [15] in the final equations to be solved. The method enables future few-nucleon calculations without angular momentum decomposition, which would avoid the increasingly complex algebra and numerical challenges of partial wave approaches that currently limit three-nucleon calculations at 100-200 MeV laboratory energies.
