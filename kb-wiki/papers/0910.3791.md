# Collimation of average multiplicity in QCD jets

> The collimation of average multiplicity inside quark and gluon jets is investigated in perturbative QCD in the modified leading logarithmic approximation (MLLA). The role of higher order corrections accounting for energy conservation and the running of the coupling constant leads to smaller multiplicity collimation as compared to leading logarithmic approximation (LLA) results. The collimation of jets produced in heavy-ion collisions has also been explored by using medium-modified splitting functions enhanced in the infrared sector. As compared to elementary collisions, the angular distribution of the jet multiplicity is found to broaden in QCD media at all energy scales.

# Collimation of average multiplicity in QCD jets

## Key claim

The angular collimation of average multiplicity inside quark and gluon jets is reduced (jets become broader) when moving from leading logarithmic approximation (LLA) to modified leading logarithmic approximation (MLLA) due to energy conservation and running coupling corrections; in heavy-ion collisions, medium-modified splitting functions with enhanced infrared gluon emission further broaden the angular distribution compared to vacuum, though the authors note these medium predictions are qualitative rather than quantitative given the simplified model used.

## Method

The authors compute the sub-jet opening angle Θ_δ that contains a fraction δ of the total jet average multiplicity by solving the collimation equation using MLLA evolution equations. The colour current of partons caught by the calorimeter is evaluated from DGLAP evolution in Mellin space, and the gluon jet average multiplicity is taken from MLLA solutions with the limiting spectrum approximation. For medium effects, the infrared-sensitive parts of the splitting functions are enhanced by a factor N_s (with N_s = 1.6 and 1.8) following the model of Borghini and Wiedemann (2005), and the same MLLA formalism is applied.

## Key numbers

- At low scales Q/Λ_QCD = 10^2, the normalized angular aperture Θ_c/Θ_0 increases by 50% from LLA to MLLA for δ = 0.25, but only by 15% for δ = 0.5.
- The medium-modified predictions use N_s = 1.6 and N_s = 1.8, corresponding to 60% and 80% enhancement of infrared gluon emission respectively, chosen to reproduce single-inclusive pion suppression data in Au–Au collisions at RHIC.
- The angular aperture Θ_δ/Θ_0 in medium is increased by roughly a factor 0.15–0.2 compared to vacuum jets, depending on Q/Λ_QCD and N_s.
- The MLLA gluon multiplicity formula uses σ_1 ≈ 0.28 and r_1 ≈ 0.185 for n_f = 3 active flavours.

## Context

This work extends the LLA calculation of jet multiplicity collimation by Dokshitzer et al. (1991) to MLLA accuracy, incorporating all terms of order O(√α_s). The medium-modified analysis uses the toy model of Borghini and Wiedemann (2005) which enhances soft gluon radiation; the qualitative finding of reduced collimation in heavy-ion collisions is consistent with the k_T-broadening of jet multiplicity distributions studied by Salgado and Wiedemann (2003). The results enable future quantitative predictions using more realistic medium-modified splitting functions, and could be tested at RHIC, the Tevatron, and the LHC.
