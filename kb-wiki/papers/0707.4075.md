# Statistical hadronization model predictions for charmed hadrons at LHC

> We present predictions of the statistical hadronization model for charmed hadrons production in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model predicts charmed hadron yields and ratios in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC under the assumption that charm quarks thermalize in the quark-gluon plasma and decouple at hadronization, with the results depending on the input charm production cross section which carries large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors apply the statistical hadronization model using chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature T=161±4 MeV, baryochemical potential μb=0.8^{+1.2}_{-0.6} MeV, volume V=6200 fm³ per unit rapidity) and a charm production cross section dσ_{c\bar{c}}^{pp}/dy=0.64^{+0.64}_{-0.32} mb. They compute yields and ratios for charmed hadrons including D mesons, Λ_c, J/ψ, ψ', η_c, and χ_c states, and also calculate transverse momentum spectra assuming thermalized charm quarks with an average expansion velocity β.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T=161±4 MeV
- Baryochemical potential: μb=0.8^{+1.2}_{-0.6} MeV
- Volume per unit rapidity: V=6200 fm³
- Charm production cross section in pp: dσ_{c\bar{c}}^{pp}/dy=0.64^{+0.64}_{-0.32} mb
- D⁺/D₀ ratio: 0.425(18)
- D*⁺/D₀ ratio: 0.387(15)
- D_s⁺/D₀ ratio: 0.349(14)
- Λ_c/D₀ ratio: 0.163(16)
- ψ'/ψ ratio: 0.031(3)
- η_c/ψ ratio: 0.617(14)
- χ_{c1}/ψ ratio: 0.086(5)
- χ_{c2}/ψ ratio: 0.110(8)
- Centrality independence of ratios expected down to Npart≈100

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model previously applied at SPS and RHIC energies (Andronic 2006) to predict charmed hadron production at LHC energies. The predictions enable experimental tests of charm quark thermalization in the quark-gluon plasma at LHC, and a precision measurement of the J/ψ transverse momentum spectrum would allow determination of the QGP expansion velocity. The model uses the measured p p  spectrum from Tevatron (Pauletta 2005) to calculate the corona contribution.
