# Level rearrangement in exotic atoms and quantum dots

> A presentation and a generalisation are given of the phenomenon of level rearrangement, which occurs when an attractive long-range potential is supplemented by a short-range attractive potential of increasing strength. This problem has been discovered in condensate-matter physics and has also been studied in the physics of exotic atoms. A similar phenomenon occurs in a situation inspired by quantum dots, where a short-range interaction is added to an harmonic confinement.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Zel'dovich "level rearrangement" phenomenon, where adding a short-range attractive potential of increasing strength to a long-range attractive well causes atomic levels to suddenly drop and be replaced by higher excitations, to cases where the narrow potential is located anywhere within the wide well, including asymmetric configurations and harmonic confinement relevant to quantum dots.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for S-wave states (and some P-states) of a Coulomb potential supplemented by an attractive square well of radius \(b=0.01\) (in units where \(\hbar^2/(2\mu)=1\) and \(e=1\)), computing eigenenergies as a function of the well depth \(\lambda\). They also study a one-dimensional double square-well model (symmetric and asymmetric) and a two-particle harmonic trap with short-range interaction, using matching conditions at potential boundaries and the Deser–Trueman formula for energy shifts.

## Key numbers

- For the Coulomb-plus-square-well model with \(b=0.01\), the square well alone supports one S-wave bound state at \(\lambda_1 b^2 = \pi^2/4 \approx 2.46\) and two S-wave bound states at \(\lambda_2 b^2 = 9\pi^2/4 \approx 22.2\); these are precisely the strengths where level rearrangement occurs.
- The Deser–Trueman formula gives the fractional energy shift of the \(n\)S level as \((E_n - E_{0,n})/E_{0,n} \simeq -(4/n)(a/B)\), where \(a\) is the scattering length of the short-range potential and \(B=2\) is the Bohr radius in the paper's units.
- For the harmonic oscillator plus square well with radius \(b=0.01\), the first rearrangement occurs at \(\lambda_1 = \pi^2/4\) and the second at \(\lambda_2 = 9\lambda_1\).
- For the one-dimensional double square-well with \(R_2=1\), \(V_2=80\), \(R_1=0.01\), there are six bound states (three even, three odd) at \(V_1=0\); the odd states show rearrangement at the same critical \(\lambda_n b^2\) values as the Coulomb case.

## Context

This work extends the original discovery by Zel'dovich (1959) of level rearrangement in electron–hole systems in semiconductors, and subsequent studies by Shapiro and collaborators (Kudryavtsev 1978, Shapiro 1978) on exotic atoms where a hadron feels both Coulomb and strong nuclear forces. The paper connects these phenomena to quantum dots via the Kustaanheimo–Stiefel transformation (Mavromatis 1998), which maps the Coulomb problem to a harmonic oscillator, and provides a unified treatment using the Deser–Trueman formula (Deser 1954, Trueman 1961). The results enable further study of absorption effects in exotic atoms (Badalyan 1982, Gal 1996) and suggest new applications in layered condensed-matter systems where small voltage changes could trigger sudden spectral shifts.
