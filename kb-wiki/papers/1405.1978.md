# Onset of cavitation in the quark-gluon plasma

> We study the onset of bubble formation (cavitation) in the quark-gluon plasma as a result of the reduction of the effective pressure from bulk-viscous corrections. By calculating velocity gradients in typical models for quark-gluon plasma evolution in heavy-ion collisions, we obtain results for the critical bulk viscosity above which cavitation occurs. Since present experimental data for heavy-ion collisions seems inconsistent with the presence of bubbles above the phase transition temperature of QCD, our results may be interpreted as an upper limit of the bulk viscosity in nature. Our results indicate that bubble formation is consistent with the expectation of hadronisation in low-temperature QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives an upper limit on the bulk viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma by assuming that cavitation (bubble formation) does **not** occur in experimentally observed heavy-ion collisions, and then calculating the critical bulk viscosity above which cavitation would be inevitable. The authors explicitly note that their approach neglects bulk-viscous contributions to the flow profiles themselves, treating them as a linear-response correction.

## Method

The authors compute the effective pressure in relativistic hydrodynamics including bulk-viscous corrections, then define cavitation as the condition where this effective pressure drops below the vapour pressure (taken as zero for a conservative estimate). They calculate velocity gradients and temperature profiles for three models of quark-gluon plasma evolution in heavy-ion collisions: Bjorken flow (1+1D), Gubser flow (with radial expansion), and a full 2+1D numerical simulation (VH2+1). For each flow profile, they solve for the critical value of the bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio (zeta/s) at which cavitation first occurs, using both first-order and second-order hydrodynamic gradient expansions.

## Key numbers

- For Bjorken flow with an ideal equation of state, the critical bulk viscosity zeta/s increases monotonically with temperature; second-order gradient corrections produce only small changes to the first-order result.
- For Gubser flow, the numerical parameters include: 1/q = 4.3 fm, hat_T0 = 5.55, H0 = 0.33.
- At temperatures T < 140 MeV, a bulk viscosity coefficient smaller than that expected from a pion gas leads to bubble formation.
- Around the QCD phase transition temperature, the critical value is zeta/s greater than or approximately 0.1; above this value, cavitation in the QGP will occur.
- The numerical simulation (VH2+1) was initialised for a central Au+Au collision at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV per nucleon pair.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of cavitation in the quark-gluon plasma by Rajagopal and collaborators, Klimek, Sreekanth and collaborators, and Buchel, but uses a different definition of effective pressure (one-third of the trace of the pressure tensor) rather than focusing on single components of the pressure tensor. The results are compared to calculations of zeta/s for a pion gas by Moore and by Torres and collaborators. The paper enables future work including full numerical simulations with bulk-viscous corrections to flow profiles, more realistic hadron gas pressures, and particle spectra calculations from cavitating fluids to test consistency with experimental data.
