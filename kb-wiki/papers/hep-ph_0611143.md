# Quark Number Fluctuations in a Chiral Model at Finite Baryon Chemical Potential

> We discuss the net quark and isovector fluctuations as well as off-diagonal quark flavor susceptibilities along the chiral phase transition line in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The model is formulated at non-zero quark and isospin chemical potentials with non-vanishing vector couplings in the iso-scalar and iso-vector channels. We study the influence of the quark chemical potential on the quark flavour susceptibilities in detail and the dependence of the results on model parameters as well as on the quark mass. The NJL model findings are compared with recent lattice results obtained in two--flavor QCD at finite chemical potential. On a qualitative level, the NJL model provides a consistent description of the dependence of quark number fluctuations on temperature and baryon chemical potential. The phase diagram and the position of the tricritical point in the NJL model are also discussed for different parameter sets.

## Key claim

The Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model provides a qualitative, but not quantitative, description of the dependence of quark number fluctuations on temperature and baryon chemical potential when compared with lattice QCD results, because the model lacks confinement and gluon degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors computed the net quark number susceptibility χ_q, the isovector susceptibility χ_I, and the off-diagonal flavor susceptibility χ_ud in the two-flavor NJL model at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ_q, including vector couplings in both the iso-scalar and iso-vector channels. The susceptibilities were obtained from the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation by solving the gap equation for the dynamical quark mass and the shifted chemical potentials, then taking the appropriate second derivatives with respect to μ_q and μ_I. The results were compared with lattice QCD data from two-flavor calculations using a p4-improved staggered fermion action with quark mass m_q/T = 0.4.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point (TCP) in the chiral limit with zero vector coupling (G_V^(S) = 0) is located at T = 65 MeV and μ_q = 275 MeV.
- For G_V^(S) = 0.3 G_S, the TCP shifts to T = 42 MeV and μ_q = 305 MeV.
- For G_V^(S) > 0.6 G_S, the chiral transition is second-order everywhere (no TCP exists).
- The critical temperature at μ_q = 0 is fixed to T_c = 177 MeV, matching two-flavor lattice QCD results from Paikert (2004).
- The vacuum parameters used are: current quark mass m_u = m_d = 5 MeV, cutoff Λ = 664.3 MeV, and scalar coupling G_S Λ^2 = 2.06, reproducing f_π = 92.4 MeV and m_π = 135 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL studies of the net quark number susceptibility at finite T by Kunihiro (1991) and at finite μ_q by Fujii (2003, 2004) by including isospin chemical potential and vector couplings, and by computing both diagonal and off-diagonal flavor susceptibilities. The results are compared qualitatively with lattice QCD calculations by Ejiri (2005) and the Budapest–Wuppertal group (Fodor, 2004), which found a critical endpoint at T ≈ 164 MeV and μ_q ≈ 120 MeV in (2+1)-flavor QCD. The NJL model predictions can be improved by coupling the quarks to the Polyakov loop (PNJL model), as done by Fukushima (2004) and Ratti, Thaler, and Weise (2005), which mimics confinement and suppresses fluctuations in the broken phase.
