# Charm quarks are more hydrodynamic than light quarks in final-state elliptic flow

> We study the charm quark elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) in heavy ion as well as small system collisions by tracking the evolution history of quarks of different flavors within a multi-phase transport model. The charm quark $v_{2}$ is studied as a function of the number of collisions the charm quark suffers with other quarks and then compared to the $v_{2}$ of lighter quarks. We find that the common escape mechanism is at work for both the charm and light quark $v_{2}$. However, contrary to the naive expectation, the hydrodynamics-type flow is found to contribute more to the final state charm $v_{2}$ than light quark $v_{2}$. This could be explained by the smaller average deflection angle the heavier charm quark undergoes in each collision, so that heavy quarks need more scatterings to accumulate a significant $v_{2}$, while lighter quarks can more easily change directions with scatterings with their $v_{2}$ coming more from the escape mechanism. Our finding thus suggests that the charm $v_{2}$ is a better probe for studying the hydrodynamic properties of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Contrary to the naive expectation that light quarks would be more hydrodynamic, the charm quark elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) has a larger fraction coming from hydrodynamic collective flow and a smaller fraction from the escape mechanism than light quark $v_{2}$, making charm $v_{2}$ a better probe of quark-gluon plasma hydrodynamic properties.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT model (v2.26t5) to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, p+Pb collisions at 5 TeV, and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. They tracked the collision history of quarks of different flavors (light u,d; strange s; charm c) and compared the $v_{2}$ from normal AMPT calculations with that from azimuth-randomized AMPT calculations, where parton azimuthal angles are randomized after each collision to isolate the escape mechanism contribution. A toy model with straightline propagation through an elliptical geometry was also used to illustrate the role of the average scattering deflection angle.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions ($b=6.6$-8.1 fm) at 200 GeV, the average number of collisions $\langle N_{\rm coll} \rangle$ for charm quarks is 8.68, compared to 4.58 for light quarks and 5.45 for strange quarks.
- The ratio of $\langle v_{2} \rangle$ from azimuth-randomized AMPT to normal AMPT (escape mechanism fraction) for charm quarks in Au+Au collisions is 22%, compared to 66% for light quarks and 47% for strange quarks.
- In Pb+Pb collisions ($b=8$ fm) at 2.76 TeV, the escape mechanism fraction for charm quarks is 8.5%, compared to 43% for light quarks and 27% for strange quarks.
- In p+Pb collisions ($b=0$ fm) at 5 TeV, the escape mechanism fraction for charm quarks is 57%, compared to 73% for light quarks and 59% for strange quarks.
- The root-mean-square change of azimuth angle per collision in Au+Au collisions is approximately $\sigma_{\Delta\phi}=1.0$ for light quarks, 0.65 for strange quarks, and 0.25 for charm quarks.

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT studies by He (2015) and Lin (2015) that showed the escape mechanism dominates $v_{2}$ generation at RHIC energies and in small systems. It also builds on studies of mass splitting of hadron elliptic anisotropies by Li (2016). The finding that charm $v_{2}$ is more hydrodynamic than light quark $v_{2}$ is consistent with results from a linearized Boltzmann transport model of heavy quarks coupled with hydrodynamical background by Cao (2016). This work enables using charm quark $v_{2}$ as a cleaner probe of QGP hydrodynamic properties, especially in large collision systems at high energies.
