# Enhanced Stability of Superheavy Nuclei due to High-Spin Isomerism

> Configuration-constrained calculations of potential-energy surfaces in even-even superheavy nuclei reveal systematically the existence at low excitation energies of multi-quasiparticle states with deformed axially symmetric shapes and large angular momenta. These results indicate the prevalence of long-lived, multi-quasiparticle isomers. In a quantal system, the ground state is usually more stable than the excited states. In contrast, in superheavy nuclei the multi-qausiparticle excitations decrease the probability for both fission and $α$ decay, implying enhanced stability. Hence, the systematic occurrence of multi-qausiparticle isomers may become crucial for future production and study of even heavier nuclei. The energies of multi-quasiparticle states and their $α$ decays are calculated and compared to available data.

## Key claim

Multi-quasiparticle excitations in even-even superheavy nuclei can decrease the probability for both fission and alpha decay, implying enhanced stability compared to the ground state, which is the opposite of the usual quantal behavior. The authors hedge that this inversion of stability applies "for a given class of superheavy nuclei with sufficiently short lifetimes."

## Method

Configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface (PES) calculations were performed using a non-axial deformed Woods-Saxon potential with universal parameters, with pairing treated via the Lipkin-Nogami method and approximate particle-number projection. The total energy was obtained from a macroscopic liquid-drop part plus a microscopic Strutinsky shell-correction part including blocking effects, with the PES calculated in the space of quadrupole (β₂, γ) and hexadecapole (β₄) deformations. The calculations were used to determine deformations, excitation energies, and alpha-decay Q-values for multi-quasiparticle states in even-even superheavy nuclei with Z ≥ 100.

## Key numbers

- In ²⁷⁰Ds, the calculated energies of the 9⁻ and 10⁻ two-quasineutron isomers are 1.39 and 1.26 MeV, respectively, agreeing with the experimental estimate of 1.13 MeV.
- The calculated average alpha-particle energy for decays of the ν9⁻ and ν10⁻ isomers to the corresponding isomers of ²⁶⁶Hs is 11.06 MeV, in good agreement with the observed energy of 10.95 MeV.
- The calculated average alpha energy for ν9⁻ and ν10⁻ isomeric decays to the ground state of ²⁶⁶Hs is 11.93 MeV, in agreement with the measured energy of 12.15 MeV.
- In ²⁷⁰Ds, a π10⁻ two-quasiproton state has a calculated excitation energy of 1.07 MeV, giving Eα(π10⁻ → 8⁺) = 11.10 MeV, in good agreement with the observed value of 11.15 MeV.
- In ²⁵⁰Fm, the calculated excitation energy of the 7⁻ configuration is 1.01 MeV, agreeing with the experimental value of 1.0 MeV.
- In ²⁵⁶Fm, the calculated excitation energy of the 7⁻ isomer is 1.1 MeV, about 0.3 MeV less than the experimental value of 1.42 MeV.
- In ²⁵⁶Fm, non-axial PES calculations result in barrier heights being lowered by 2.4 and 2.6 MeV for the ground state and 7⁻ isomer, respectively, compared with axial calculations.
- The observed partial fission half-life of the 7⁻ isomer in ²⁵⁶Fm is 0.8 ms, remarkably longer than the expected value of 2.5 μs assuming no hindrance from unpaired nucleons.

## Context

This work extends configuration-constrained PES calculations (Xu 1998) to systematically predict high-spin isomers in superheavy nuclei, building on experimental observations of rotational gamma-ray transitions in ²⁵²,²⁵⁴No (Herzberg 2001, Reiter 1999) and ²⁵⁴,²⁵⁶Fm (Firestone 1996). The calculations confirm assignments for isomers in ²⁷⁰Ds observed by Hofmann (2001) and provide predictions for isomers in ²⁵⁰Fm, ²⁵⁴No, and ²⁵⁶Fm that can be tested by future experiments. The results suggest that populating high-K states in fusion reactions may enable the extension of the nuclear chart further into the island of superheavy nuclei.
