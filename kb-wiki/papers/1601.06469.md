# Dynamical Coupling of Pygmy and Giant Resonances in Relativistic Coulomb Excitation

> We study the Coulomb excitation of pygmy dipole resonances (PDR) in heavy ion reactions at 100 MeV/nucleon and above. The reactions 68Ni+197Au and 68Ni+208Pb are taken as a practical examples. Our goal is to address the question of the influence of giant resonances on the PDR as the dynamics of the collision evolves. We show that the coupling to the giant resonances affects considerably the excitation probabilities of the PDR, a result that indicates the need of an improved theoretical treatment of the reaction dynamics at these bombarding energies.

## Key claim

The coupling between pygmy dipole resonances (PDR) and giant resonances (particularly the giant quadrupole resonance, GQR) via higher-order electromagnetic processes in relativistic Coulomb excitation modifies the excitation probability of the PDR by up to 18%, indicating that first-order perturbation theory is insufficient for extracting PDR strength from experimental data at bombarding energies around 100 MeV/nucleon and above.

## Method

The authors solved relativistic coupled-channels (RCC) equations for Coulomb excitation of 68Ni projectiles on 197Au and 208Pb targets at energies from 100 MeV/nucleon to 2 GeV/nucleon. They modeled the electromagnetic response functions for the PDR, isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR), and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) as Lorentzian distributions with specified centroid energies and widths, discretized into 35 energy bins each, and included all magnetic substates for a total of 456 coupled channels. The eikonal absorption phase was used to account for nuclear absorption at small impact parameters, and the resulting S-matrix elements were integrated over impact parameter to obtain excitation cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For 68Ni+197Au at 600 MeV/nucleon: first-order PDR cross section = 80.9 mb; coupled-channels PDR cross section = 92.2 mb (a 14% increase)
- For 68Ni+208Pb at 503 MeV/nucleon: first-order PDR cross section = 58.3 mb; coupled-channels PDR cross section = 71.2 mb (an 18.1% increase)
- Dipole polarizability α_D extracted from the 68Ni+208Pb experiment (Ros13) = 3.40 fm^3; value needed to reproduce experimental cross section with dynamical calculations = 3.16 fm^3
- The reduction in α_D implies a reduction in neutron skin from 0.17 fm to 0.16 fm, within the experimental uncertainty of 7% for α_D and 0.02 fm for the neutron skin
- PDR parameters used in the model: E_PDR = 11 MeV, Γ_PDR = 2 MeV; GDR: E_GDR = 17.2 MeV, Γ_GDR = 4.5 MeV; GQR: E_GQR = 15.2 MeV, Γ_GQR = 4.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of higher-order Coulomb excitation effects observed in double giant dipole resonances (DGDR) by Bertulani (1996) and others, applying the relativistic coupled-channels formalism of Bertulani (2005) to the pygmy dipole resonance for the first time. The calculations show that the dynamical coupling between PDR and GQR via E1 fields is the dominant higher-order effect, while PDR-GDR coupling via E2 fields contributes little. The results indicate that experimental extractions of PDR strength from reactions at 100-600 MeV/nucleon, such as those by Wie09 and Ros13, may need correction, and that future precision measurements of the dipole polarizability (important for constraining the symmetry energy and neutron-star properties) should account for these coupling effects, especially at lower bombarding energies.
