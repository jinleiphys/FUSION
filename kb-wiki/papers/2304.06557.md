# Anisotropic flow and the valence quark skeleton of hadrons

> We study transverse momentum anisotropies, in particular, the elliptic flow $v_2$ due to the interference effect sourced by valence quarks in high-energy hadron-hadron collisions. Our main formula is derived as the high-energy (eikonal) limit of the impact-parameter dependent cross section in quantum field theory, which agrees with that in terms of the impact parameter in the classical picture. As a quantitative assessment of the interference effect, we calculate $v_2$ in the azimuthal distribution of gluons at a comprehensive coverage of the impact parameter and the transverse momentum in high-energy pion-pion collisions. In a broad range of the impact parameter, a sizable amount of $v_2$, comparable with that produced due to saturated dense gluons or final-state interactions, is found to develop. In our calculations, the valence sector of the pion wave function is obtained numerically from the Basis Light-Front Quantization, a non-perturbative light-front Hamiltonian approach. And our formalism is generic and can be applied to other small collision systems like proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a sizable elliptic flow \(v_2\) in soft gluon production can arise purely from interference effects sourced by valence quarks in high-energy hadron-hadron collisions, without requiring final-state interactions or saturated dense gluon states. The authors claim this interference effect produces \(v_2\) values comparable to those attributed to hydrodynamic or saturation mechanisms, but note that their calculation is performed in the dilute-dilute limit where both colliding hadrons are treated as color-singlet multiparton states rather than saturated gluon states.

## Method

The authors derive an impact-parameter dependent cross section for gluon production in the high-energy (eikonal) limit of quantum field theory, then apply it to pion-pion collisions where each pion is modeled as a valence quark-antiquark dipole. The valence sector of the pion wave function is obtained numerically from Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ), a non-perturbative light-front Hamiltonian approach, with the only two parameters fixed by light meson masses. The elliptic flow \(v_2\) is calculated from the azimuthal distribution of soft gluons at leading order in dipole-dipole scattering, using light-cone gauge where only one pion radiates gluons to midrapidity.

## Key numbers

- The elliptic flow \(v_2\) calculated for pion-pion collisions is found to be comparable in magnitude to that observed in proton-proton collisions at the LHC and to theoretical results from saturation physics and hydrodynamic models.
- A distinct double-peak structure in \(v_2\) is observed for impact parameters \(b \gtrsim 0.1\) fm, which encodes information on the hadron size.
- The calculations cover a comprehensive range of impact parameter and transverse momentum, with the gluon transverse momentum scale \(p_T \sim 1\) GeV corresponding to a de Broglie wavelength \(\lambda = 2\pi/p\) comparable to the transverse size of colliding hadrons (\(\sim 1\) fm).

## Context

This work extends the study of initial-state correlations in small collision systems beyond the Color-Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, which typically focuses on the dilute-dense limit where one hadron is modeled as a saturated gluon state. The interference mechanism shares conceptual similarity with the classical double-slit experiment and with prior work by Blok et al. (2017, 2018) and Altinoluk et al. (2015) on interference patterns among multiple gluon emitters. The formalism is generic and can be applied to other small collision systems like proton-proton collisions, enabling future studies that incorporate additional Fock state components beyond the valence sector.
