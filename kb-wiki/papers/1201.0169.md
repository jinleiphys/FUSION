# Momentum space evolution of chiral three-nucleon forces

> A framework to evolve three-nucleon (3N) forces in a plane-wave basis with the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) is presented and applied to consistent interactions derived from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). We demonstrate the unitarity of the SRG transformation, show the decoupling of low and high momenta, and present the first investigation of universality in chiral 3N forces at low resolution scales. The momentum-space-evolved 3N forces are consistent and can be directly combined with the standard SRG-evolved two-nucleon (NN) interactions for ab-initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions.

# Momentum space evolution of chiral three-nucleon forces

## Key claim

The paper presents the first SRG evolution of chiral three-nucleon (3N) forces in a plane-wave momentum basis, demonstrating that the evolved 3N matrix elements show approximate universality at low resolution scales (λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹) despite the initial potentials having different cutoff values, though this universality is limited to channels where the three-body contact interaction c_E contributes and is most pronounced for intermediate cutoff values.

## Method

The SRG flow equations for two-nucleon (NN) and 3N interactions are solved in a plane-wave basis using Jacobi momenta and a partial-wave representation, with the NN interactions evolved first and then embedded into the three-body equations via cyclic and anticyclic permutation operators. The initial interactions are derived from chiral effective field theory at N²LO for five different cutoff combinations Λ/Λ̃, and the evolution is performed in a model space of 42 three-body partial-wave channels (J ≤ 5) with the generator η_s = [T_rel, H_s].

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ³H for Λ/Λ̃ = 550/600 MeV shows a maximal variation of 5 keV under SRG evolution, compared to about 400 keV if induced 3N forces are completely neglected
- For Λ = 450 MeV the variation is ≤ 2 keV, while for Λ = 600 MeV it is about 16 keV
- Initial ³H ground state energies are within 12 keV of converged values based on the antisymmetrized interaction
- All calculated ³H ground state energies are 150–260 keV more bound than the experimental value E_gs = -8.481821(5) MeV
- At λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹, evolved 3N matrix elements have significant size only below ξ, ξ' ≲ 2 fm⁻¹ and around the diagonal ξ ≃ ξ'

## Context

This work extends the SRG evolution of nuclear forces from the discrete harmonic oscillator basis approach of Jurgenson (2009) to a plane-wave momentum basis, addressing basis truncation issues that led to scale dependencies in heavier nuclei and infinite matter calculations. The momentum-space-evolved 3N forces can be directly combined with standard SRG-evolved NN interactions for ab-initio calculations, enabling new tests in no-core shell model and coupled cluster calculations, as well as many-body perturbation theory studies of infinite nuclear matter to test whether consistently evolved forces predict empirical saturation properties.
