# Small x Gluons in Nuclei and Hadrons

> We numerically study the effects of high gluon density at small x on the evolution of gluon distribution function in both hadrons and nuclei. Using a newly derived, Wilson renormalization group-based evolution equation which includes n to 1 gluon ladder fusion, we find significant reduction in nuclear gluon distribution function for large nuclei at zero impact parameter at energies relevant for RHIC and LHC experiments.

## Key claim

Using a newly derived Wilson renormalization group-based evolution equation that includes n to 1 gluon ladder fusion, the authors find significant reduction in the nuclear gluon distribution function for large nuclei at zero impact parameter at energies relevant for RHIC and LHC, but note that the results depend on the numerical values of the hadron or nucleus radius and the coupling constant, and that the treatment is not self-consistent at low values of Q.

## Method

The authors numerically solve a general evolution equation for the impact parameter dependent gluon distribution function, derived from a Wilson renormalization group approach, which includes all orders of n to 1 gluon ladder fusion. They use the method of characteristics to convert the partial differential equation into a set of coupled ordinary differential equations, solved with a 4th order Runge-Kutta method. Initial conditions are set at x0=0.06 and Q0=0.7 GeV using CTEQ parameterization of the proton gluon density, with factorization of impact parameter assumed only at the starting point.

## Key numbers

- For a proton (A=1), a 15-20% reduction in the number of gluons at x ~ 10^-4 compared to DLA DGLAP, at Q=2 GeV and Q=5 GeV with b_perp=0
- For a Gold or Lead nucleus (A=200), a 50-55% reduction at x ~ 10^-4 compared to DLA DGLAP, at Q=2 GeV and Q=5 GeV with b_perp=0
- Initial conditions chosen at x0=0.06 and Q0=0.7 GeV, with nuclear radius R_A=5 fm and hadron radius R=1 fm, and alpha_s=0.25
- For the range of x and Q considered, variation of initial Q0 is between 0.8-1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Levin-Ryskin (GLR) and Mueller-Qiu (MQ) approach (1990s), which included only the first non-linear term in the evolution equation, by using a general evolution equation derived by Jalilian-Marian et al. (1997-1998) that includes all orders of n to 1 gluon ladder fusion. The results enable systematic investigation of nuclear gluon shadowing, impact parameter dependence of gluon distributions, and predictions for observables such as mini-jet production and heavy quark production at RHIC and LHC, with the authors noting that a fully self-consistent treatment would require including initial non-perturbative shadowing from parameterizations such as Eskola et al.
