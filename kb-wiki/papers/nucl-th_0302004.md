# Semi-relativistic meson exchange currents in (e,e') and (e,e'p) reactions

> Electron-induced one-nucleon knock-out observables are computed for moderate to high momentum transfer making use of semi-relativistic expressions for the one-body and two-body meson-exchange current matrix elements. Emphasis is placed on the semi-relativistic form of the $Δ$-isobar exchange current and several prescriptions for the dynamical-equivalent form of the $Δ$-propagator are analyzed. To this end, the inclusive transverse response function, evaluated within the context of the semi-relativistic approach and using different prescriptions for the $Δ$-propagator, is compared with the fully relativistic calculation performed within the scheme of the relativistic Fermi gas model. It is found that the best approximation corresponds to using the traditional static $Δ$-propagator. These semi-relativistic approaches, which contain important aspects of relativity, are implemented in a distorted wave analysis of quasielastic $(e,e'p)$ reactions. Final state interactions are incorporated through a phenomenological optical potential model and relativistic kinematics is assumed when calculating the energy of the ejected nucleon. The results indicate that meson exchange currents may modify substantially the $TL$ asymmetry for high missing momentum.

## Key claim

The semi-relativistic (SR) approach for meson exchange currents (MEC), including a static Delta-propagator, provides the best approximation to the fully relativistic calculation of the inclusive transverse response function, despite the fact that none of the tested "dynamical" Delta-propagator prescriptions (SR1 through SR5) can fully recover the exact relativistic result when the propagator is made independent of the nucleon momentum.

## Method

The authors derived semi-relativistic expressions for the one-body and two-body meson-exchange currents (pion-in-flight, contact, and Delta-isobar) and tested them against fully relativistic calculations within the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model for inclusive (e,e') reactions. They then implemented the best-performing static Delta-propagator approximation in a distorted-wave analysis of exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, using a phenomenological optical potential for final state interactions and relativistic kinematics for the ejected nucleon energy.

## Key numbers

- The inclusive transverse response was computed for momentum transfers q = 500 MeV/c and q = 1000 MeV/c, using Fermi momentum k_F = 237 MeV/c for Ca-40.
- The coupling constants used were: G_1 = 4.2, f^2/4pi = 0.079, f_{pi N Delta} = 2.24.
- Monopole form factors with cutoff Lambda = 1300 MeV were applied to the MEC for the exclusive calculations.
- For q = 1 GeV/c, the Delta pole is first reached at omega ~ 511 MeV according to the relativistic relation, while the static approximation gives omega ~ 649 MeV for the same pole condition.

## Context

This work extends the semi-relativistic treatment of MEC developed in Amaro et al. (2002, 2003) and Amaro et al. (1998) by focusing on the Delta-isobar current and testing various dynamical propagator prescriptions. It contrasts with the fully relativistic RDWIA approach of Udias et al. (1993, 1996, 1999, 2001) and the non-relativistic MEC calculations of Van der Sluys et al. (1994) and Ryckebusch et al. (1999, 2001). The results enable the inclusion of MEC effects in distorted-wave analyses of (e,e'p) reactions at moderate to high momentum transfer, showing that MEC may substantially modify the TL asymmetry for high missing momentum.
