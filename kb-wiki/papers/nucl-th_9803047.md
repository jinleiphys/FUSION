# Disappearance of Elliptic Flow: A New Probe for the Nuclear Equation of State

> Using a relativistic hadron transport model, we investigate the utility of the elliptic flow excitation function as a probe for the stiffness of nuclear matter and for the onset of a possible quark-gluon-plasma (QGP) phase-transition at AGS energies 1 < E_Beam < 11 AGeV. The excitation function shows a strong dependence on the nuclear equation of state, and exhibits characteristic signatures which could signal the onset of a phase transition to the QGP.

# Disappearance of Elliptic Flow: A New Probe for the Nuclear Equation of State

## Key claim

Using a relativistic hadron transport model, the elliptic flow excitation function in Au+Au collisions at AGS energies (1-11 AGeV) shows a strong dependence on the nuclear equation of state stiffness and could signal the onset of a quark-gluon plasma phase transition, but only if the characteristic signatures predicted by the model are confirmed experimentally.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic transport model based on a phenomenological Landau theory of quasiparticles, incorporating nucleons, pions, and Delta and N* resonances as hadronic degrees of freedom. They parametrized the mean field with both momentum-independent and momentum-dependent forms, adjusting parameters to produce soft (K=210 MeV) and stiff (K=380 MeV) equations of state, and also introduced an EOS with a weak second-order phase transition by setting the scalar potential constant for densities above 2.3 times normal nuclear density. The elliptic flow was quantified using the Fourier coefficient <cos(2 phi)> for baryons at midrapidity, calculated for Au+Au collisions at impact parameters of 4-6 fm.

## Key numbers

- Transition beam energy E_tr from negative to positive elliptic flow differs by approximately 2 AGeV between soft and stiff equations of state, whether or not momentum-dependent forces are included.
- For cascade models (no mean field), the transition energy E_tr is less than or approximately 0.6 AGeV.
- The soft equation of state has incompressibility K=210 MeV; the stiff equation of state has K=380 MeV.
- Normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}; collisions at AGS energies are expected to reach densities up to approximately 8 rho_0.
- The effective mass at normal density and Fermi momentum is m_N* = 0.65 m_N for both momentum-dependent parameter sets.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of collective flow as a probe for the nuclear equation of state, particularly building on the finding by Danielewicz (1995) that elliptic flow depends on the ratio of sound speed to spectator velocity, and on the work of Ollitrault (1992) and Sorge (1997) showing sensitivity of elliptic flow to pressure at maximum compression. The authors address limitations of directed sideward flow, which merges with the collision axis at relativistic energies and shows strong dependencies on uncertain transport properties (Danielewicz, 1988, 1995). The results suggest that experimental elliptic flow measurements at AGS energies could discriminate between different equations of state and potentially provide evidence for QGP formation, motivating further experimental analysis of reaction data in this energy range.
