# Exploring the nucleon structure through GPDs and TDAs in hard exclusive processes

> Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) offer a new way to access the quark and gluon nucleon structure. We review recent progress in this domain, emphasizing the need to supplement the experimental study of deeply virtual Compton scattering by its crossed version, timelike Compton scattering. We also describe the extension of the GPD concept to three quark operators and the relevance of their nucleon to meson matrix elements, namely the transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) which factorize in backward meson electroproduction and related processes. We discuss the main properties of the TDAs. \

# Exploring the nucleon structure through GPDs and TDAs in hard exclusive processes

## Key claim

Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) and Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) provide a framework for accessing the three-dimensional quark and gluon structure of the nucleon, but extracting universal GPDs from both timelike Compton scattering (TCS) and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) requires careful treatment because the NLO coefficient functions differ significantly between the two processes, particularly in their analytic structure and imaginary parts.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for hard exclusive processes, comparing DVCS (spacelike) with TCS (timelike) at leading and next-to-leading order in α_s, calculating the quark and gluon coefficient functions and their ratios to the Born term. They extend the GPD concept to three-quark operators for baryon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), deriving spectral representations, polynomiality properties, isospin symmetries, and soft pion theorems for πN TDAs, and estimating cross sections for backward meson electroproduction and crossed reactions in proton-antiproton annihilation.

## Key numbers

- For TCS at s = 10^7 GeV² with Q'² = 5 GeV², t = -0.2 GeV², and μ_F² = 5 GeV², the Compton process dominates over Bethe-Heitler and interference contributions at all φ angles
- For TCS at s = 10^5 GeV² with Q'² = 5 GeV², t = -0.2 GeV², and μ_F² = 5 GeV², all three contributions (Compton, Bethe-Heitler, interference) are comparable in magnitude
- The NLO quark coefficient function ratio R^q is shown for η = 0.3 with α_s = 0.25 and μ_F² = |Q²|, demonstrating that the imaginary part of the TCS amplitude is present in both ERBL and DGLAP regions, unlike DVCS where it exists only in the DGLAP region
- The ratio of NLO gluon coefficient functions in TCS to DVCS is plotted for η = 0.05 in the DGLAP region with μ_F² = |Q²|
- Backward electroproduction data are being extracted for Δ²_T < 1 GeV², with Q² of order a few GeV²

## Context

This review extends the GPD framework developed by Burkardt (2000) and the DVCS factorization theorem, emphasizing the complementarity of TCS as proposed by Berger, Diehl, and Pire (2002) with DVCS measurements at JLab@12 GeV, Compass, and future electron-ion colliders. The TDA concept builds on the factorization theorem for backward DVCS by Pire and Szymanowski (2005) and backward meson electroproduction by Frankfurt et al. (1999, 2002), enabling new experimental programs at JLab-12 GeV, Compass, and PANDA at GSI-FAIR to test universality of GPDs and TDAs across different hard exclusive processes.
