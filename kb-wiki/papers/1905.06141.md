# Aspects of the QCD $θ$-vacuum

> This paper addresses two aspects concerning the $θ$-vacuum of Quantum Chromodynamics. First, large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory is used to calculate the first two non-trivial cumulants of the distribution of the winding number, i.\,e. the topological susceptibility, $χ_\mathrm{top}$, and the fourth cumulant, $c_4$, up to next-to-leading order. Their large-$N_c$ scaling is discussed, and compared to lattice results. It is found that $χ_\mathrm{top}=\mathcal{O}(N_c^0)$, as known before, and $c_4=\mathcal{O}(N_c^{-3})$, correcting the assumption of $\mathcal{O}(N_c^{-2})$ in the literature. Second, we discuss the properties of QCD at $θ\simπ$ using chiral perturbation theory for the case of $2+1$ light flavors, i.\,e. by taking the strange quark mass heavier than the degenerate up and down quark masses. It is shown that --- in accordance with previous findings for $N_f=2$ and $N_f=3$ mass-degenerate flavors --- in the region $θ\simπ$ two vacuum states coexist, which become degenerate at $θ=π$. The wall tension of the energy barrier between these degenerate vacua is determined as well as the decay rate of a false vacuum.

## Key claim

Using large-\(N_c\) chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order, the fourth cumulant \(c_4\) of the winding number distribution scales as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{-3})\), correcting the earlier assumption in the literature that it scales as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{-2})\); for the case of \(2+1\) light flavors at \(\theta \sim \pi\), two vacuum states coexist and become degenerate at \(\theta = \pi\), consistent with previous findings for \(N_f=2\) and \(N_f=3\) mass-degenerate flavors.

## Method

The authors used large-\(N_c\) chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) to compute the topological susceptibility \(\chi_\mathrm{top}\) and the fourth cumulant \(c_4\) of the winding number distribution up to next-to-leading order, employing both the \(\delta\)-expansion scheme of Kaiser and Leutwyler and the standard \(p\)-expansion of CHPT. For the study at \(\theta \sim \pi\), they applied chiral perturbation theory with \(2+1\) light flavors (degenerate up and down quarks with a heavier strange quark) to determine the vacuum structure, the wall tension of the energy barrier between degenerate vacua, and the decay rate of a false vacuum.

## Key numbers

- \(\chi_\mathrm{top} = \mathcal{O}(N_c^0)\) at leading order, with the leading-order result \(\chi_\mathrm{top}^\mathrm{LO} = \frac{\Sigma m \tau}{\Sigma m + \tau N_f}\).
- \(c_4^\mathrm{LO} = -\frac{\Sigma m N_f \tau^4}{(\Sigma m + \tau N_f)^4}\) at leading order, scaling as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{-3})\) in the large-\(N_c\) limit.
- At next-to-leading order in the \(\delta\)-expansion, \(\chi_\mathrm{top}^\delta \xrightarrow{N_c \to \infty} \tau + \mathcal{O}(N_c^{-1})\) and \(c_4^\delta \xrightarrow{N_c \to \infty} \mathcal{O}(N_c^{-3}) + \mathcal{O}(N_c^{-4})\).
- The \(\eta^\prime\) mass in the \(2+1\) flavor case is given by \(M_{\eta^\prime}^2 = \frac{2\Sigma}{3F^2}(2m+m_s) + \frac{6\tau}{F^2}\), and the refined Witten–Veneziano formula reads \(M_{\eta^\prime}^2 + M_\eta^2 - 2M_K^2 = \frac{6\tau}{F^2}\).
- The experimental upper bound on the effective vacuum angle is \(|\bar{\theta}| \lesssim 10^{-11}\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order large-\(N_c\) calculations of the topological susceptibility and fourth cumulant by Leutwyler and Smilga (1992) and by Lucini and Meggiolaro (1999) to next-to-leading order, correcting the expected scaling of \(c_4\) from \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{-2})\) to \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{-3})\). The study at \(\theta \sim \pi\) complements previous work by Smilga (1999) for \(N_f=2\) and \(N_f=3\) mass-degenerate flavors and by Tytgat (1999) for one and zero light flavors, now treating the case of \(2+1\) flavors with a heavier strange quark. The results enable more precise comparisons with lattice simulations of topological quantities and further understanding of the QCD vacuum structure and \(CP\)-violating effects near \(\theta = \pi\).
