# Simple function forms and nucleon-nucleus total cross sections

> Total cross sections for neutron scattering with energies between 10 and 600 MeV and from nine nuclei spanning the mass range from 6Li to 238U have been analyzed using a simple function of three parameters. The values of those parameters with which neutron total cross section data are replicated vary smoothly with energy and target mass and may themselves be represented by functions of energy and mass.

# Simple function forms and nucleon-nucleus total cross sections

## Key claim

Neutron total scattering cross sections for energies from 10 to 600 MeV and target masses from 6Li to 238U can be reproduced by a three-parameter function, provided the parameters are allowed to vary smoothly with energy and mass; however, the functional forms for the parameters themselves are chosen by inspection and do not yet meet a ±5% fit criterion for heavy nuclei below 100 MeV, where oscillatory structure remains.

## Method

The authors used a three-parameter function form previously applied to proton reaction cross sections, where partial total cross sections are described by a Fermi-like distribution in angular momentum space with an additional surface-peaked term. Initial parameter values were obtained from g-folding optical potential calculations using the BonnB force, then the parameters a and epsilon were fixed as parabolic functions of energy, and the remaining parameter l0 was adjusted to match measured neutron total cross section data from nine stable nuclei across 24 energies.

## Key numbers

* Neutron total cross sections were analyzed for nine nuclei: 6Li, 12C, 19F, 40Ca, 89Y, 184W, 197Au, 208Pb, and 238U.
* Energy range covered: 10 to 600 MeV.
* The parameters a and epsilon were fixed as parabolic functions of energy: a = 1.29 + 0.00250 E - 1.76 × 10^(-6) E^2, and epsilon = -1.47 - 0.00234 E + 4.16 × 10^(-6) E^2, where E is in MeV.
* For 208Pb, the l0 values range from 6.892 at 10 MeV to 35.749 at 550 MeV.
* The function form for l0(E) used five parameters (c1, c2, c3, E0, beta); for 208Pb these were: c1 = 4.067 × 10^(-2), c2 = 13.43, c3 = 9.402, E0 = 62.51, beta = 1.400 × 10^(-2).
* The chi-squared values for l0 fits (sum of squared deviations) for 208Pb were 5.0 overall and 3.3 for energies below 100 MeV.
* For 6He-hydrogen scattering at 40A MeV, the total reaction cross section was 16-17% larger than standard shell model predictions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Amos (2002) and Deb (2003) that established similar simple function forms for proton total reaction cross sections, and by Amos (2002) who used g-folding optical potentials to reproduce neutron total cross sections up to 300 MeV. The authors compare their results to the phenomenological global optical model potentials of Koning and Delaroche (2003) and to g-folding calculations that fail above 300 MeV. The approach enables rapid estimation of neutron total cross sections without optical potential calculations, though the authors note that special structure effects such as neutron halos (as seen in 6He studies by Lagoyannis, 2001) require separate treatment.
