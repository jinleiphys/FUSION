# Thermodynamic properties of hot nuclei within the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation

> The thermodynamic properties of hot nuclei are described within the canonical and microcanonical ensemble approaches. These approaches are derived based on the solutions of the BCS and self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation at zero temperature embedded into the canonical and microcanonical ensembles. The obtained results agree well with the recent data extracted from experimental level densities by Oslo group for $^{94}$Mo, $^{98}$Mo, $^{162}$Dy and $^{172}$Yb nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermodynamic properties of hot nuclei can be accurately described by embedding zero-temperature BCS and self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation solutions (with Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection) into canonical and microcanonical ensembles, but the agreement with experimental data is conditional on the choice of energy interval for microcanonical entropy and the extrapolation range used in experimental data extraction.

## Method

The authors solve BCS and self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (SCQRPA) equations at zero temperature for each total seniority (number of unpaired particles) using Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection, then embed the resulting eigenvalues into canonical ensemble partition functions and microcanonical ensemble state counting. The calculations use axially deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle energies with quadrupole deformation parameters from experimental B(E2) values, and pairing strengths are adjusted to reproduce experimental odd-even mass differences at zero temperature. For heavy nuclei, the partition function from 22 single-particle levels around the Fermi energy is combined with an independent-particle model partition function for the full space.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gaps at zero temperature: Δ_N ≈ 1.2 MeV (neutrons) and Δ_Z ≈ 1.4 MeV (protons) for ⁹⁴Mo; Δ_N ≈ 1.0 MeV and Δ_Z ≈ 1.3 MeV for ⁹⁸Mo; Δ_N ≈ 0.8 MeV and Δ_Z ≈ 0.9 MeV for both ¹⁶²Dy and ¹⁷²Yb
- Quadrupole deformation parameters: β₂ = 0.15 for ⁹⁴Mo, 0.17 for ⁹⁸Mo, 0.281 for ¹⁶²Dy, and 0.296 for ¹⁷²Yb
- Computation time for N=20 half-filled system: exact diagonalization not feasible, CE-LNBCS takes 3 hours, CE-LNSCQRPA takes 10 hours on RIKEN Integrated Cluster of Clusters system
- Number of eigenstates for N=20 half-filled system: exact = 377,379,369; LNBCS = 524,288; LNSCQRPA = 5,242,880
- Energy interval δE = 1 MeV used for ⁹⁴Mo and 0.7 MeV for ⁹⁸Mo to fit experimental microcanonical entropy data
- Heat capacity peak observed at T ≈ 0.7 MeV for both ⁹⁴Mo and ⁹⁸Mo in experimental data from Kaneko (2008)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CE-BCS and CE-SCQRPA approaches (Hung and Dang, 2009) from ⁵⁶Fe to heavier nuclei (⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁸Mo, ¹⁶²Dy, ¹⁷²Yb) and compares with experimental data extracted by the Oslo group (Chankova 2006, Kaneko 2008). The method addresses the failure of conventional finite-temperature BCS theory for finite systems by including quantal and thermal fluctuations that smooth out the superfluid-normal phase transition. The approach is computationally simpler than exact diagonalization (which becomes impractical for N > 14) and finite-temperature quantum Monte Carlo calculations, enabling application to heavy nuclei where exact solutions are impossible.
