# Dynamical Screening and Radiative Parton Energy Loss in a Quark-gluon Plasma

> Dynamical screening in the magnetic part of the one-gluon exchange interaction is included in the study of radiative energy loss of a fast parton propagating inside a quark-gluon plasma. As a result the final radiative energy loss is about twice as large as when only the electric part of one-gluon exchange interaction is considered. A non-perturbative magnetic screening mass is also used in the estimate of the mean-free-path of parton scattering in a hot QCD matter.

# Dynamical Screening and Radiative Parton Energy Loss in a Quark-gluon Plasma

## Key claim
Including both the electric and magnetic parts of the one-gluon exchange interaction in the calculation of radiative energy loss for a fast parton in a quark-gluon plasma yields a final energy loss about twice as large as when only the electric part is considered; the full result including a non-perturbative magnetic screening mass gives an energy loss about four times larger than the original BDMPS estimate, but this factor depends on the specific value of the strong coupling constant and the mean-free-path treatment.

## Method
The author computes the averaged transverse momentum broadening per unit distance for a fast parton using thermal field theory, including both longitudinal (electric) and transverse (magnetic) contributions to the effective gluon propagator with resummed self-energies. The imaginary part of the transverse self-energy provides Landau damping that regularizes the infrared behavior of the magnetic interaction in the momentum-broadening integral. For the mean-free-path calculation, a non-perturbative magnetic screening mass μ_mag is introduced to regularize a logarithmic singularity in the magnetic part, and the resulting mean-free-path is then substituted into the BDMPS formula for radiative energy loss.

## Key numbers
- For a fast quark with E = 250 GeV in a plasma with T = 250 MeV and L ≈ 10 fm: d⟨p_T²⟩/dL ≈ 1.9 GeV²/fm and dE/dz ≈ 24 GeV/fm × (L/10 fm)
- For α_s = 1/10 (weak coupling limit) with same conditions: d⟨p_T²⟩/dL ≈ 0.18 GeV²/fm and dE/dz ≈ 0.68 GeV/fm × (L/10 fm)
- The magnetic contribution to the momentum-broadening integral is fit as I_T = 0.5 ln(q²_max/μ_D²B²), comparable to the electric contribution I_L = 0.92 ln(q²_max/μ_D²B²)
- The mean-free-path is given by λ_a⁻¹ = 3TC_aα_s ln(1/α_s), with the dominant contribution from the magnetic interaction
- The final radiative energy loss formula is dE_a/dz = 4πN_cα_s³T³L C_a × 1.42 ln[(9/2)C_aL ln(1/α_s)√(2πα_sET)]

## Context
This work extends the BDMPS formalism (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff, 1997) for radiative parton energy loss by including the magnetic part of the one-gluon exchange interaction, which had been neglected in prior studies using the screened static-potential model of Gyulassy and Wang (1994). The paper also builds on the calculation of the gluon damping rate by Pisarski (1989) and the non-perturbative magnetic screening mass estimate from the TBBM collaboration (1993). The results enable more realistic predictions for jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions and highlight the importance of dynamical screening effects beyond static Debye screening.
