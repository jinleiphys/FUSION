# Search for Quasi Bound $Îˇ$ Mesons

> The search for a quasi bound $Îˇ$ meson in atomic nuclei is reviewed. This tentative state is studied theoretically as well as experimentally. The theory starts from elastic $Îˇ$ nucleon scattering which is derived from production data within some models. From this interaction the $Îˇ$ nucleus interaction is derived. Model calculations predict binding energies and widths of the quasi bound state. Another method is to derive the $Îˇ$ nucleus interaction from excitation functions of $Îˇ$ production experiments. The $s$ wave interaction is extracted from such data via final state interaction theorem. We give the derivation of $s$ wave amplitudes in partial wave expansion and in helicity amplitudes and their relation to observables. Different experiments extracting the final state interaction are discussed as are production experiments. So far only three experiments give evidence for the existence of the quasi bound state: a pion double charge exchange experiment, an effective mass measurement, and a transfer reaction at recoil free kinematics with observation of the decay of the state.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the search for quasi-bound $\eta$ mesons in atomic nuclei, concluding that while theoretical models predict such states for nuclei with mass number A âĽ 12, only three experiments have provided evidence for their existence, and the large scatter in theoretical predictions for the $\eta$-nucleon scattering length means the existence of these states remains tentative.

## Method

The review examines two main theoretical approaches: constructing an $\eta$-nucleus optical potential from the $\eta$-nucleon scattering length (derived from production data via K-matrix or chiral unitary models) and solving a wave equation (SchrĂśdinger-like, Klein-Gordon, or Lippmann-Schwinger) to find complex eigenenergies; and extracting the $\eta$-nucleus interaction directly from excitation functions of $\eta$ production experiments using final-state interaction theorems. The paper also discusses experimental searches including pion double charge exchange, effective mass measurements, and transfer reactions at recoil-free kinematics with decay observation.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework first proposed by Bhalerao and Liu (1985) and Haider and Liu (1986), who predicted quasi-bound $\eta$ mesons in nuclei with A âĽ 12 based on an attractive $\eta$-nucleon interaction. The paper contrasts multiple theoretical approaches including the chiral unitary model (Inoue 2002, Garcia-Recio 2002), the chiral doublet model (Jido 2002, Nagahiro 2009), and the TĎ approximation, showing that predictions for binding energies and widths vary dramatically depending on the input scattering length and model assumptions. The review enables future experimental searches by cataloging the three existing experimental claims and highlighting the need for experiments that can distinguish between narrow bound-state peaks and broad structures from background, particularly given the chiral doublet model prediction that widths may exceed binding energies.
