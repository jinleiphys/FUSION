# Framework for Polarized Superfluid Fermion Systems

> I discuss the advantages and disadvantages of several procedures, some known and some new, for constructing stationary states within the mean field approximation for a system with pairing correlations and unequal numbers spin-up and spin-down fermions, using the two chemical potentials framework. One procedure in particular appears to have significant physics advantages over previously suggested in the literature computational frameworks. Moreover, this framework is applicable to study strongly polarized superfluid fermion systems with arbitrarily large polarizations and with arbitrary total particle numbers. These methods are equally applicable to normal systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a two-chemical-potential framework, where spin-up and spin-down fermions are assigned separate chemical potentials, provides a more transparent and computationally efficient method for constructing stationary states of polarized superfluid fermion systems than previously suggested approaches, though the author notes that multiple valid prescriptions exist and the optimal choice should always correspond to the lowest total energy.

## Method

The author formulates the Bogoliubov transformation for systems with even and odd fermion numbers, then introduces a two-chemical-potential framework using the operator S_z = sign(j_z) to separate quasiparticle states into two groups with different chemical potentials. The method is applied to nuclear systems with axial symmetry (including octupole deformation) and to cold atom systems, with the self-consistent equations written explicitly for both cases. The author compares this approach against the cranking operator j_z constraint and the simplex operator method from Bertsch (2009).

## Key numbers

- Maximum orbital angular momentum projection of an odd nucleon in a heavy fissioning nucleus: l_z ≈ ±9 ħ, where p_F is the Fermi momentum and R ≈ 1.1A^(1/3) is the waist radius
- Maximum angular momentum in fission fragments: l_z(H) ≈ ±7.5 ħ for the heavy fragment and l_z(L) ≈ ±6.7 ħ for the light fragment, smaller by roughly 15±5% than in the mother nucleus
- Single-particle energies and chemical potential μ_η are given in units of ħω, with spin-orbit interaction strength V = 0.12 ħω used in the illustrative example of non-interacting neutrons in a spherical harmonic oscillator potential

## Context

This work extends the two-chemical-potential approach introduced in Bulgac (2007) and used in subsequent studies of polarized superfluid systems, including cold atom systems and nuclear structure calculations. The author contrasts this framework with the standard nuclear physics approach from Dobaczewski (1997, 2000a) and Ring (2004), which requires a priori knowledge of the odd fermion's quantum numbers, and notes that Robledo (2011) showed a gradient technique approach is apparently free of this difficulty. The framework enables microscopic studies of nuclear fission dynamics for odd and odd-odd nuclei, particularly addressing the hindrance of fission in these systems compared to even-even neighbors, and is applicable to arbitrarily large spin polarizations beyond the |N_up - N_down| = 1 case.
