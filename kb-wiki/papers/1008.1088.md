# Sum rules for light-by-light scattering

> We derive a set of sum rules for the light-by-light scattering and fusion: $γγ\to all$, and verify them in lowest order QED calculations. A prominent implication of these sum rules is the superconvergence of the helicity-difference total cross-section for photon fusion, which in the hadron sector reveals an intricate cancellation between the pseudoscalar and tensor mesons. An experimental verification of superconvergence of the polarized photon fusion into hadrons is called for, but will only be possible at $e^+ e^-$ and $γγ$ colliders with both beams polarized. We also show how the sum rules can be used to measure various contributions to the low-energy light-by-light scattering.

## Key claim

The paper derives a set of sum rules for light-by-light scattering and photon fusion (γγ → all), with the central result being a superconvergence relation for the helicity-difference total cross-section that integrates to zero; the authors verify these sum rules in leading-order QED but note that experimental verification of the hadronic superconvergence requires polarized e⁺e⁻ and γγ colliders, which have not yet been realized.

## Method

The authors use analyticity, crossing symmetry, unitarity, and the low-energy limit of forward elastic light-by-light scattering amplitudes to derive dispersion relations, then apply the optical theorem to relate the imaginary parts of the helicity amplitudes to total absorption cross-sections with definite photon polarizations. Expanding the dispersion relations in powers of the Mandelstam variable s and matching orders yields sum rules that connect integrals of polarized total cross-sections to low-energy constants of the Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian. The sum rules are verified in scalar QED and spinor QED at leading order in the fine-structure constant α, and the hadronic sector is examined using Breit-Wigner parametrizations of meson contributions.

## Key numbers

- The superconvergence sum rule: ∫₀^∞ dω Δσ(ω²)/ω = 0, where Δσ = σ₂ - σ₀ is the helicity-difference total cross-section for photon fusion.
- π⁰ contribution to the sum rule: -195.0 ± 15.0 nb, using m_π⁰ = 134.98 MeV and Γ_γγ = (7.8 ± 0.6) × 10⁻³ keV.
- η contribution: -190.7 ± 11.2 nb, using m_η = 547.85 MeV and Γ_γγ = 0.51 ± 0.03 keV.
- η′ contribution: -301.0 ± 10.5 nb, using m_η′ = 957.66 MeV and Γ_γγ = 4.30 ± 0.15 keV.
- Sum of η and η′ contributions: -492 ± 22 nb.
- a₂(1320) tensor meson contribution: 134 ± 8 nb (narrow resonance) or 137 ± 8 nb (Breit-Wigner), using m = 1318.3 MeV and Γ_γγ = 1.00 ± 0.06 keV.
- f₂(1270) contribution: 448 ± 52 nb (narrow) or 479 ± 56 nb (Breit-Wigner), using m = 1275.1 MeV and Γ_γγ = 3.03 ± 0.35 keV.
- f₂′(1525) contribution: 7 ± 1 nb (narrow and Breit-Wigner), using m = 1525 MeV and Γ_γγ = 0.081 ± 0.009 keV.
- Sum of f₂ and f₂′ contributions: 455 ± 53 nb (narrow) or 486 ± 57 nb (Breit-Wigner).
- η_c meson contribution to the sum rule in the charm sector: approximately -15.5 nb.
- Low-energy constants from Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian: c₁ = (1/90) α² m⁻⁴, c₂ = (7/360) α² m⁻⁴ in leading-order QED.

## Context

This work extends the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule framework, previously applied to Compton scattering on massive targets, to the case of photon-photon scattering, building on earlier suggestions by Roy (1974) and Brodsky (1995). The sum rules for the linearly-polarized cross-sections are analogs of the Baldin sum rule for nucleon polarizabilities. The paper enables future experimental measurements of polarized γγ fusion cross-sections at colliders with both beams polarized, which would directly test the superconvergence relation and allow extraction of the low-energy constants c₁ and c₂ characterizing photon self-interaction.
