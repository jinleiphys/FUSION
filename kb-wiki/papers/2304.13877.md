# Short-range baryon-baryon potentials in constituent quark model revisited

> We revisit the short-range baryon-baryon potentials in the flavor SU(3) sector, using the constituent quark model. We employ the color Coulomb, linear confining, and color magnetic forces between two constituent quarks, and solve the three-quark Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method to evaluate the wave functions of the octet $( N , Λ, Σ, Ξ)$ and decuplet $( Δ, Σ^{\ast} , Ξ^{\ast} , Ω)$ baryons. We then solve the six-quark equation using the resonating group method and systematically calculate equivalent local potentials for the $S$-wave two-baryon systems which reproduce the relative wave functions of two baryons in the resonating group method. As a result, we find that the flavor antidecuplet states with total spin $J = 3$, namely, $ΔΔ$, $ΔΣ^{\ast}$, $ΔΞ^{\ast}$-$Σ^{\ast} Σ^{\ast}$, and $ΔΩ$-$Σ^{\ast} Ξ^{\ast}$ systems, have attractive potentials sufficient to generate dibaryon bound states as hadronic molecules. In addition, the $N Ω$ system with $J = 2$ in coupled channels has a strong attraction and forms a bound state. We also make a comparison with the baryon-baryon potentials from lattice QCD simulations and try to understand the behavior of the potentials from lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The authors find that flavor antidecuplet states with total spin J = 3 (ΔΔ, ΔΣ*, ΔΞ*-Σ*Σ*, and ΔΩ-Σ*Ξ* systems) and the NΩ system with J = 2 in coupled channels have attractive potentials sufficient to generate dibaryon bound states as hadronic molecules, but these results depend on the specific constituent quark model parameters fitted to reproduce ground-state baryon masses.

## Method

The authors solved the three-quark Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method with color Coulomb, linear confining, and color magnetic forces between constituent quarks to obtain wave functions for octet (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and decuplet (Δ, Σ*, Ξ*, Ω) baryons. They then solved the six-quark equation using the resonating group method (RGM) and systematically calculated equivalent local potentials for S-wave two-baryon systems that reproduce the relative wave functions of two baryons in the RGM.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 336 MeV and m_s = 509 MeV (fixed to reproduce magnetic moments of proton and Λ)
- Model parameters: K = 184 MeV, α_ss = 0.785, k = 0.755 GeV/fm, σ = 3.50 fm^{-1}, D = 381 MeV
- Calculated baryon masses: N = 950 MeV (experimental 939 MeV), Λ = 1111 MeV (1116 MeV), Σ = 1180 MeV (1193 MeV), Ξ = 1322 MeV (1318 MeV), Δ = 1235 MeV (1232 MeV), Σ* = 1382 MeV (1385 MeV), Ξ* = 1530 MeV (1533 MeV), Ω = 1679 MeV (1672 MeV)
- Root mean squared radii of baryons range from 0.41 fm (Ξ) to 0.51 fm (Δ)
- Gaussian expansion used N = 10 with ranges r_min = 0.01 fm and r_max = 2 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark model studies of baryon-baryon interactions by Oka (1981) and Oka (1981) to systematically cover all pairs of ground-state octet and decuplet baryons. The authors compare their results with baryon-baryon potentials from lattice QCD simulations using the HAL QCD method, as applied to ΩΩ by Gongyo (2017), NΩ by HAL QCD Collaboration (2018), and ΔΔ by Gongyo (2020). The calculated local potentials can be used for further investigations of two-baryon systems and dibaryon bound states, and provide theoretical predictions that can be tested against correlation function measurements in relativistic ion collisions.
