# Improving the J/psi Production Baseline at RHIC and the LHC

> We assess the theoretical uncertainties on the inclusive J/psi production cross section in the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) using values for the charm quark mass, renormalization and factorization scales obtained from a fit to the charm production data. We use our new results to provide improved baseline comparison calculations at RHIC and the LHC. We also study cold matter effects on J/psi production at leading relative to next-to-leading order in the CEM within this approach.

## Key claim

The authors provide the first uncertainty band on inclusive J/psi production in the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) by using charm quark mass and scale parameters obtained from a fit to open charm production data, rather than relying on the previous "by-eye" fits.

## Method

The authors fit the factorization and renormalization scale parameters to a subset of fixed-target total charm production data with beam energies between 250 and 920 GeV, together with PHENIX and STAR data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, using the PDG charm quark mass of 1.27 GeV. They then use the same fitted mass and scale parameters to calculate J/psi production cross sections in the CEM, where the J/psi cross section is taken as a fraction F_C of all charm-anticharm pairs below the open charm threshold, with F_C determined from forward J/psi data on light nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

* Best fit parameters: charm quark mass m_c = 1.27 GeV, mu_F/m = 2.1 (+2.55/-0.85), mu_R/m = 1.6 (+0.11/-0.12)
* PDG charm quark mass value used: m(m) = 1.27 +/- 0.09 GeV
* Fixed-target data range used in fit: 250 <= E_beam <= 920 GeV
* For ALICE comparisons at 7 TeV: a <k_T^2> kick of 1.49 GeV^2 applied to p_T distributions; at 2.76 TeV: a <k_T^2> kick of 1.41 GeV^2 applied
* Using the final STAR data point changes upper and lower limits on mu_F/m by 8% and 4% respectively, while limits on mu_R/m change by less than 1%

## Context

This work extends the Color Evaporation Model approach of Gavai (1994) and Vogt (previous work) by replacing the earlier "by-eye" fit parameters (m = 1.2 GeV, mu_F/m = mu_R/m = 2) with a statistically determined fit to charm production data. The results provide improved baseline calculations for J/psi production at RHIC and the LHC, and enable future studies of B meson decay contributions to J/psi production and more detailed next-to-leading order cold nuclear matter effects.
