# Event-by-event physics in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Motivated by forthcoming experiments at RHIC and LHC, and results from SPS, a review is given of the present state of event-by-event fluctuations in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. Fluctuations in particle multiplicities, ratios, transverse momenta, rapidity, etc. are calculated in participant nucleon as well as thermal models. The physical observables, including multiplicity, kaon to pion ratios, and transverse momenta agree well with recent NA49 data at the SPS, and indicate that such studies do not yet reveal the presence of new physics. Predictions for RHIC and LHC energies are given. The centrality dependence with and without a phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma is discussed - in particular, how the physical quantities are expected to display a qualitative different behavior in case of a phase transition, and can be signaled by anomalous fluctuations and correlations in a number of observables.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies can be explained by standard statistical models (participant nucleon and thermal models) without invoking new physics, but that at RHIC and LHC energies the much larger fluctuations expected in proton-proton collisions relative to thermal fluctuations should allow a quantitative measurement of the degree of thermalization in nuclear collisions.

## Method

The author calculates event-by-event fluctuations in particle multiplicities, kaon-to-pion ratios, and transverse momenta using two limiting models: the wounded nucleon model (WNM), where particle production is a superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions, and the thermal model, where strong rescattering brings the system into local thermal equilibrium. These calculations are compared to NA49 data from central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon at the SPS, and predictions are extended to RHIC and LHC energies. The centrality dependence of fluctuations is analyzed both with and without a first-order phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma, including the effects of droplet formation and supercooling.

## Key numbers

- Average charged particle multiplicity in pp collisions at SPS energies (sqrt(s) ~ 20 GeV): <N_ch> ~ 7.3; at RHIC (sqrt(s) ~ 200 GeV): <N_ch> ~ 20; at LHC (sqrt(s) ~ 5000 GeV): <N_ch> ~ 60
- Multiplicity fluctuations in pp collisions: omega_Nch ~ 2.0 at SPS, ~ 6.2 at RHIC, ~ 20 at LHC energies
- NA49 measured multiplicity distribution width omega_N^exp = 2.01 for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, in agreement with the WNM calculation giving omega_N ~ 2.0
- Thermal fluctuations for massless bosons: omega_N^BE = 1.37; for massive pions at T = m_pi: omega_pi = 1.11; thermal fluctuations including resonances: omega_N ~ 1.5
- Number of participants in central Pb+Pb collisions: <N_p> ~ 350 from Glauber theory, with N_p(0) ~ 400 for b=0
- Impact parameter fluctuations for NA49 centrality cut (b_c ~ 3.5 fm): omega_Np ~ 1.1
- Charged particle multiplicity per participant in NA49 acceptance: <n> ~ 0.77
- Critical temperature for QCD phase transition: T_c ~ 150 MeV for 3 or more massless quark flavors; T_c ~ 260 MeV for pure SU(3) gauge theory
- Formation time tau_0 ~ 1 fm/c and freezeout time tau_f ~ 8 fm/c from HBT studies

## Context

This review extends earlier work on event-by-event fluctuations proposed in papers by BFS, PRL91, and sigfluct, which analyzed transverse energy fluctuations in central collisions to extract evidence for color fluctuations. It builds on the NA49 experimental analysis of ~100,000 central Pb+Pb events at SPS (NA49, 1999) and provides theoretical tools for upcoming RHIC experiments (PHOBOS). The paper discusses how fluctuations could signal a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, following theoretical work on critical fluctuations at the QCD phase transition by Stodolsky, Shuryak, SRS, Berdnikov, and GP. The author notes that no anomalous event-by-event fluctuations have been found at CERN within experimental accuracy, indicating either that no transition took place or that signals were erased before freezeout. The proposed "degree of thermalization" measure would allow future experiments at RHIC and LHC to quantify how much rescattering occurs in nuclear collisions by comparing measured fluctuations to the WNM and thermal limits.
