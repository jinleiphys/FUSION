# Anomaly in decay of 8Be and 4He -- can an observed light boson mediate low energy nucleon-nucleon interactions ?

> We present a hypothesis that the anomaly in the folding angle distribution of electron-positron pairs, emitted in the decay of the excited levels of nucleus $^{8}$Be and $^{4}$He can be related to the cluster structure of the decaying state. Furthermore, we present a hypothesis that the potentially observed boson with re st mass $m_{X}$=17 MeV can mediate the nucleon-nucleon interaction at the low-energy regime of QCD, in particular in the weakly bound cluster state p+$^{7}$Li,$^{3}$H. We present a possible equations of state of symmetric nuclear matter corresponding to the vector meson mass $m_{v}$=17 MeV, obtained using relativistic mean field theory of nuclear force, QHD-I in particular, with physically relevant incompessibility K$_{0}$=240-260 MeV and the values of couplings $g_{v}$, $g_{s}$ lower than unity. Based on concepts of chiral symmetry breaking, we show that reduction of the rest mass of pseudoscalar particle from physical value $m_π$=135 MeV to $m_{X}$=17 MeV is equivalent to reduction of the quark mass from dynamical value around 310 MeV down to current quark mass around 5 MeV ($\frac{m_{X}^2} {m_{q,curr}} \simeq \frac{m_π^2} {m_{q,dyn}}$). Corresponding version of Goldberger-Treiman relation leads to the value of coupling close to the results from relativistic mean field theory of nuclear force. Both model approaches thus point towards apparent restoration of chiral symmetry in nucleon-nucleon interaction at large distances, possibly via bounce into false instanton vacuum. Observation of boson with rest mass $m_{X}$=17 MeV in the decay of high lying excited states of $^{8}$Be and $^{4}$He can possibly resolve one of the longest lasting open questions in nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The anomaly in the folding angle distribution of electron-positron pairs from excited states of 8Be and 4He may be explained by a 17 MeV boson that mediates nucleon-nucleon interaction at low-energy QCD, specifically in weakly bound cluster states such as p+7Li and p+3H, rather than requiring an exotic dark matter interpretation.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field calculations using the QHD-I model to derive equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter with a vector boson mass of 17 MeV, scanning over cubic and quartic scalar boson self-interaction couplings to reproduce experimental binding energy and saturation density. They also applied the instanton liquid model of QCD to derive relations between boson mass and quark mass based on chiral symmetry breaking, obtaining a modified Goldberger-Treiman relation for the hypothetical 17 MeV boson.

## Key numbers

- Vector boson mass m_v = 17 MeV, with corresponding coupling g_v = 0.2407-0.2417 for equations of state with incompressibility K_0 = 245-260 MeV
- Scalar boson mass m_s = 25.58 MeV for the same parameter sets, with couplings g_s = 0.4666-0.4703
- Incompressibility range K_0 = 240-260 MeV constrained by experimental data from nuclear reactions and binary neutron star merger GW170817
- Ratio of dynamical quark mass (310 MeV) to current quark mass (5 MeV) satisfies m_X^2 / m_q,curr ≈ m_π^2 / m_q,dyn for m_X = 17 MeV and m_π = 135 MeV
- Coupling of X-boson to current quark g_Xqq = 0.07, giving nucleon coupling g_XNN = 0.21 from the modified Goldberger-Treiman relation
- Quark condensate drops from (120 MeV)^3 for m_π = 135 MeV to (30 MeV)^3 for m_X = 17 MeV

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the anomaly reported by Krasznahorkay et al. (2016) in 8Be and later in 4He, which was previously interpreted as evidence for a dark photon. The authors propose an alternative nuclear physics explanation based on cluster structure of the decaying states, specifically p+7Li for 8Be and p+3H for 4He. The analysis connects to the instanton liquid model of QCD and suggests that observation of such a boson could indicate restoration of chiral symmetry in nucleon-nucleon interactions at large distances, potentially via a bounce into a false instanton vacuum state.
