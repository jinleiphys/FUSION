# The reaction pi N -> pi pi N in chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom

> The reaction pi N -> pi pi N is studied at tree level up to next-to-leading order in the framework of manifestly covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom. Using total cross section data to determine the relevant low-energy constants, predictions are made for various differential as well as total cross sections at higher energies. A detailed comparison of results based on the heavy-baryon and relativistic formulations of chiral perturbation theory with and without explicit Delta degrees of freedom is given.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom in manifestly covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory at complete tree level up to next-to-leading order (epsilon^2) improves the description of total and differential cross sections for pi N -> pi pi N compared to calculations without explicit Delta degrees of freedom, though the improvement is most significant in the Delta resonance region and the relativistic formulation shows better convergence than the heavy-baryon formulation.

## Method

The authors performed a tree-level calculation of the reaction pi N -> pi pi N using manifestly covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, including all terms from the dimension-one and dimension-two effective Lagrangians for pions, nucleons, and Delta isobars. They determined the relevant low-energy constants (LECs) by fitting to total cross section data, then used these to predict differential cross sections and total cross sections at higher energies. A detailed comparison was made between the relativistic formulation and the heavy-baryon formulation, both with and without explicit Delta degrees of freedom, using the small scale expansion (epsilon-expansion) where the Delta-nucleon mass splitting Delta = 293 MeV is treated as a soft scale.

## Key numbers

- Delta-nucleon mass splitting: Delta = 293 MeV, used as the soft scale parameter in the epsilon-expansion alongside M_pi/Lambda_chi
- The Delta(1232) decay width Gamma is of order O(M_pi^3), justifying that using the dressed propagator with constant width affects contributions only at order epsilon^3 and beyond
- The pion decay constant F_pi, nucleon mass m_N, and axial vector coupling g_A are used as input parameters at tree level without differentiation between chiral limit and physical values
- The LECs c_i from L_piN^(2) are taken from pion-nucleon scattering, while h_A is the pi N Delta axial coupling constant

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of pi N -> pi pi N in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory at tree level (Bernard 1995) and one-loop level (Fettes 1999), as well as a relativistic tree-level calculation without explicit Delta degrees of freedom (Bernard 1997). It also extends the earlier work of Jensen (1997) which included Delta and Roper resonances but only with leading-order pion-baryon couplings. By including explicit Delta degrees of freedom systematically up to next-to-leading order for the first time, this calculation enables improved predictions in the Delta resonance region and provides complementary information to pion-nucleon scattering for determining LECs such as d_16, which governs the quark mass dependence of the nucleon axial vector coupling constant and is a major source of uncertainty in chiral extrapolations of nuclear observables.
