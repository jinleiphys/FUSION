# Polarized $pK^-$ scattering in Unitary Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We study $pK^-$ scattering in the energy range from threshold through the $Λ(1520)$ peak in UBChPT, taking into account $\mathcal{O}(q)$ vertices from meson-baryon contact interactions and $s$- and $u$-channel ground-state baryon exchange, $s$- and $u$-channel decuplet- and nonet-baryon exchange and $t$-channel vector-meson exchange, as well as $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$ flavor-breaking vertices. Detailed fits to data are presented, including a substantial body of differential cross-section data with meson momentum $q_\mathrm{lab} > 300$ MeV not considered in previous treatments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Unitary Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (UBChPT) can describe polarized $pK^-$ scattering data from threshold through the $\Lambda(1520)$ peak, including differential cross-section data with meson momentum $q_\mathrm{lab} > 300$ MeV not previously considered in chiral-theoretic treatments, provided that vector-meson exchange contributions are explicitly included in the tree-level amplitudes.

## Method

The authors compute tree-level BChPT partial waves including $\mathcal{O}(q)$ vertices from meson-baryon contact interactions, $s$- and $u$-channel ground-state baryon exchange, $s$- and $u$-channel decuplet- and nonet-baryon exchange, $t$-channel vector-meson exchange, and $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$ flavor-breaking vertices. These tree-level partial waves are then unitarized using the coupled-channels method of Meissner and Oller (2000) and Oller and Meissner (2001), which resums the right-hand cut in the $s$-plane via the matrix equation $\mathcal{F} = (I + f \cdot G)^{-1} \cdot f$. The $3/2^-$ baryon nonet is treated as static (explicit fields in the chiral Lagrangian), while the $1/2^-$ nonet is dynamically generated. A global fit to $pK^-$ data is performed, including $S$, $P$, and $D$ partial waves.

## Key numbers

- The energy range studied is from threshold through the $\Lambda(1520)$ peak, corresponding to laboratory-frame incident-meson momentum $0 \leq q_\mathrm{lab} \lesssim 600$ MeV.
- Differential cross-section data with meson momentum $q_\mathrm{lab} > 300$ MeV are included in the fits, a substantial body not considered in previous chiral-theoretic treatments.
- The vector-meson coupling constant $G_V \sim 60$ MeV is estimated from vector meson decays or pseudoscalar-meson electromagnetic form factors.
- The $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$ contact interaction Lagrangian contains fifteen terms, of which only three are flavor-breaking, with coupling constants denoted $b_{0,D,F}$.

## Context

This work extends the UBChPT approach previously applied to $N\overline{K}$ processes by Oller and Meissner (2001), Jido et al. (2002, 2003), and Oller et al. (2006) to include vector-meson exchange explicitly and to incorporate higher-momentum differential cross-section data. It contrasts with the Bethe-Salpeter unitarization framework of Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2000), which performed a global fit over a similar energy range but did not explicitly include vector-meson exchange. The treatment of the $3/2^-$ baryon nonet as static (rather than dynamically generated) is noted as a necessary preliminary step before a full global fit including a dynamic $3/2^-$ nonet can be attempted.
