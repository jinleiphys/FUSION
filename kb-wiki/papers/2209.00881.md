# Fermi motion effects in electroproduction of hypernuclei

> In a previous analysis of electroproduction of hypernuclei the cross sections were calculated in distorted-wave impulse approximation where the momentum of the initial proton in the nucleus was set to zero (the frozen-proton approximation). In this paper we go beyond this approximation assuming a non zero effective proton momentum due to proton Fermi motion inside of the target nucleus discussing also other kinematical effects. To this end we have derived a more general form of the two-component elementary electroproduction amplitude (Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu like) which allows its use in a general reference frame moving with respect to the nucleus-rest frame. The effects of Fermi motion were found to depend on kinematics and elementary amplitudes. The largest effects were observed in the contributions from the longitudinal and interference parts of the cross sections. The extension of the calculations beyond the frozen-proton approximation improved the agreement of predicted theoretical cross sections with experimental data and once we assumed the optimum on-shell approximation, we were able to remove an inconsistency which was previously present in the calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including Fermi motion of the initial proton in the target nucleus, by using a nonzero effective proton momentum instead of the frozen-proton approximation, improves the agreement of theoretical cross sections with experimental data for electroproduction of hypernuclei, and that using an optimum on-shell approximation removes a previously present inconsistency in the calculations.

## Method

The authors derived a general two-component (CGLN-like) form of the elementary electroproduction amplitude valid in an arbitrary reference frame, allowing them to go beyond the frozen-proton approximation where the initial proton momentum was set to zero. They used this amplitude in distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations with the optimal factorization approximation, assuming an effective proton momentum due to Fermi motion, and applied the amplitude on-shell in the optimum on-shell approximation to avoid off-energy-shell uncertainties. The calculations were performed for the reaction 12C(e,e'K+)12ΛB using the BS3 isobar model and the older SLA Saclay-Lyon model for the elementary amplitude, with shell-model structure calculations by John Millener.

## Key numbers

- Mean proton momenta in 12C: 176.7 MeV/c for the 0p1/2 state and 179.6 MeV/c for the 0p3/2 state, corresponding to mean kinetic energies of 18.15 MeV and 18.76 MeV, respectively.
- The momentum transfer to the Λ is approximately 250 MeV/c, comparable to the proton Fermi momentum of about 150 MeV/c in 12C.
- The difference between single-particle binding energies of the proton and Λ is approximately 10 MeV, which is small compared to the total energy of about 10 GeV.
- The calculations were performed with Q2 = 0.06 (GeV/c)2, ε = 0.7, Eγ in the range 1.5 to 2.5 GeV, and θKe in the range 5° to 14°.

## Context

This work extends previous DWIA calculations of hypernuclear electroproduction cross sections that used the frozen-proton approximation, as in the analyses by the authors themselves (Bydžovský et al., 2018) and others (Cotanch, Bennhold, Lenske, Motoba). The inclusion of Fermi motion effects was previously studied in other reactions, such as pion photoproduction off 10B (Sato, 1998) and (K-, π-) reactions on 12C (Zofka, 1980), where effects were found to be small, and in (π+, K+) and (K-, π-) reactions (Harada, 1998) where optimal Fermi averaging improved data description. The present work enables more accurate theoretical predictions for hypernuclear spectroscopy experiments at Jefferson Laboratory (Hall A and Hall C), particularly for the longitudinal and interference parts of the cross section which show the largest sensitivity to Fermi motion.
