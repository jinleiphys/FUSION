# Tagging emc effects and hadronization mechanisms by semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off nuclei

> The semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of electrons off a nucleus A with detection of a slow nucleus (A-1) in the ground or low excitation states, i.e. the process A(e,e'(A-1))X, can provide useful information on the origin of the EMC effect and the mechanisms of hadronization. The theoretical description of the process is reviewed and the results of several calculations on few-body systems and complex nuclei are presented.

## Key claim

The semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering process A(e,e'(A-1))X can provide information on both the origin of the EMC effect and the mechanisms of hadronization, but only in specific kinematic regions where the spectator mechanism dominates (backward emission angles, approximately θ_p ≳ 80°), and provided that nuclear effects are properly separated from the structure function information.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical description of the A(e,e'(A-1))X process within the spectator mechanism, where the virtual photon interacts with a quark of a nucleon and the spectator (A-1) nucleus is detected. They extend the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) treatment of Scopetta (1999) by including final state interactions (FSI) of the hadronizing debris (leading quark and diquark) with nucleons of the (A-1) nucleus, described by an effective debris-nucleon cross section derived from the hadronization model of Kopeliovich et al. Calculations are performed for ²H, ³He, and ⁴⁰Ca using realistic wave functions (AV18 potential for few-body systems, mean-field for heavier nuclei) and compared to Jefferson Lab data for ²H(e,e'p)X.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy E_e = 5.75 GeV for the ²H(e,e'p)X experiment at JLab, with four-momentum transfer 1.2 (GeV/c)² ≲ Q² ≲ 5.0 (GeV/c)²
- Recoiling proton momentum range: 0.28 GeV/c ≲ |p_p| ≤ 0.7 GeV/c
- Invariant mass of the produced hadronic state: 1.1 GeV ≤ W_X ≤ 2.7 GeV
- For ³He and ⁴⁰Ca calculations: E_e = 12 GeV, Q² = 8 (GeV/c)², W_X² range 2 GeV² ≲ W_X² ≲ 10 GeV²
- At |P_(A-1)| ≈ 0.4 GeV/c, the two medium-dependent structure function models differ by about 40%
- For the EMC-tagging ratio: x_Bj = 0.45, x_Bj' = 0.35, with θ_(A-1) = 145°

## Context

This work extends the spectator mechanism proposal of Scopetta (1999) by incorporating final state interactions as developed by Ciofi degli Atti and Kopeliovich (2005), and provides the first detailed comparison with experimental data from the JLab ²H(e,e'p)X measurements of Klimenko and Kuhn (2007). The process offers advantages over the traditional SIDIS A(e,e'h)X approach (studied by HERMES and JLab, reviewed by Brooks) because it can access the early stage of hadronization at short formation times without contamination from cascading processes. The results enable future experiments on ³He, ⁴He, and heavier nuclei using recoil detectors currently under development.
