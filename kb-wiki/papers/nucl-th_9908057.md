# Sensitivity to the pion-nucleon coupling constant in partial-wave analyses of elastic pi-N and NN scattering and pion photoproduction

> We summarize results obtained in our studies of the pion-nucleon coupling constant. Several different techniques have been applied to pi-N and NN elastic scattering data, and the existing database for single-pion photoproduction. The most reliable determination comes from pi-N elastic scattering. The sensitivity in this reaction was found to be greater, by at least a factor of 3, when compared with analyses of NN elastic scattering or single-pion photoproduction.

## Key claim

The most reliable determination of the pion-nucleon coupling constant comes from pi-N elastic scattering, where the sensitivity is greater by at least a factor of 3 compared to analyses of NN elastic scattering or single-pion photoproduction; however, the extraction carries both a mapping error of 0.01 and a larger extraction error of 0.03 from the dispersion-relation evaluation.

## Method

The authors performed chi-squared mappings as a function of the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant g^2/4pi for energy-dependent partial-wave analyses of three reactions: pi-N elastic scattering up to T_pi = 2.1 GeV, NN elastic scattering up to T_lab = 400 MeV, and single-pion photoproduction up to E_gamma = 500 MeV (with extensions to 2 GeV). For pi-N scattering, fixed-t dispersion-relation constraints on the C+ amplitude and forward dispersion-relation constraints on the E+/- amplitudes were added to facilitate extraction of the sigma term. For NN scattering, the coupling constant sensitivity was built into the representation through peripheral Born amplitudes containing t-channel and u-channel poles, with two different solutions (SP40 with 57 parameters and SG40 with 48 parameters) fitted to the current GW data base below 400 MeV. For pion photoproduction, the representation included Born terms and elastic pi-N scattering amplitudes to satisfy Watson's theorem below the 2-pion production threshold.

## Key numbers

- From pi-N elastic scattering (solution SP99): g^2/4pi = 13.73 with mapping error of 0.01 and extraction error of 0.03
- From the Karlsruhe solution KA84 using the Hamilton dispersion relation (100-600 MeV Tlab): g^2/4pi = 14.40 with RMS deviation of 0.23
- From NN elastic scattering solution SP40 (57 parameters, 0-400 MeV): uncertainty of 0.13
- From NN elastic scattering solution SG40 (48 parameters, 0-400 MeV): uncertainty of 0.09
- From pion photoproduction fit to 500 MeV: g^2/4pi = 13.97 with uncertainty of 0.13
- From pion photoproduction averaged over maximum energies from 500 MeV to 2 GeV: sensitivity of 0.14

## Context

This work extends previous determinations of the pion-nucleon coupling constant from pi-N scattering via dispersion relations (Arndt et al., 1995), tree-level models (Matsinos, 1997), and potential approaches (Timmermans, 1997), as well as from NN scattering analyses (Arndt et al., 1997; Stoks, Timmermans, and de Swart, 1993). The results update the long-standing value of g^2/4pi = 14.28 from Carter, Bugg, and Carter (1973) used in the Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KA84) and Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley analyses of the late 1970s. The finding that pi-N elastic scattering provides the most sensitive and least model-dependent determination enables future extractions to focus on this reaction, while the identification of substantial sensitivity in np spin data suggests their inclusion will alter any extraction from np elastic scattering.
