# Disassembly model for the production of astrophysical strangelets

> Determination of the baryon number (or mass) distribution of the strangelets, that may fragment out of the warm and excited strange quark matter (SQM) ejected in the merger of strange stars (SSs) in compact binary stellar systems of the Galaxy, is attempted by using a statistical disassembly model (SMM). Finite mass of strange quarks is taken into account in the analysis. Strangelet charge and the resulting Coulomb correction are included to get a plausible mass distribution of galactic strangelets at their source.

## Key claim

The paper uses a statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to predict the baryon number (mass) distribution of strangelets that may fragment from warm, excited strange quark matter ejected in strange star mergers, but only under the assumption that the ejected material reaches thermodynamic and chemical equilibrium at freeze-out temperatures between 0.01 and 1.0 MeV.

## Method

The authors apply a statistical disassembly model (SMM) to bulk strange quark matter ejected in strange star mergers, treating the system as evolving in thermodynamic equilibrium until it reaches a freeze-out volume where strong interactions between fragments cease. They compute the multiplicity of each strangelet species using the Helmholtz free energy, which includes volume, surface, curvature, and Coulomb contributions, with up and down quarks treated as massless and strange quarks assigned a mass of 150 MeV. The initial baryon number is set to \(A_b = 1.2 \times 10^{53}\), corresponding to an average tidally released SQM mass of about \(10^{-4} M_\odot\) per merger, and the available volume is taken as five times the initial bulk volume.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark mass set to \(m_s = 150\) MeV; up and down quarks treated as massless.
- Bag pressure \(B = (145)^4\) MeV\(^4\).
- Freeze-out temperature range: \(T = 0.01\) to \(1.0\) MeV.
- Initial baryon number \(A_b = 1.2 \times 10^{53}\), corresponding to an average ejected SQM mass of \(\sim 10^{-4} M_\odot\) per strange star merger.
- Strangelet mass formula: \(m_i = 874 A_i + 77 A_i^{2/3} + 232 A_i^{1/3}\) MeV.
- Debye screening length \(\lambda_D \sim 5\) fm.
- The condition \(E_i/A_i < 930\) MeV for absolute stability of strangelets is satisfied for baryon number \(A_i \gtrsim 10\).
- Radius parameter of initial bulk SQM: \(r_b \approx 0.94\) fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strangelet production from strange star mergers (Madsen 2005, Bauswein 2009) by incorporating finite strange quark mass and Coulomb corrections into a statistical multifragmentation model originally developed for nuclear fragmentation (Bondorf 1995). The predicted mass distribution is intended to serve as input for galactic propagation models to estimate the strangelet flux in primary cosmic rays, which could be tested by ongoing experiments such as AMS-02 (Kounine 2012).
