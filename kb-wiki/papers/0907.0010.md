# The relativistic entrainment matrix of a superfluid nucleon-hyperon mixture. II. Effect of finite temperatures

> We calculate the important quantity of superfluid hydrodynamics, the relativistic entrainment matrix for a nucleon-hyperon mixture at arbitrary temperature. In the nonrelativistic limit this matrix is also termed the Andreev-Bashkin or mass-density matrix. Our results can be useful for modeling the pulsations of massive neutron stars with superfluid nucleon-hyperon cores and for studies of the kinetic properties of superfluid baryon matter.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the relativistic entrainment matrix for a nucleon-hyperon mixture at arbitrary temperature, extending the zero-temperature analysis of GKH09, under the assumption that the unperturbed matter can be treated as a collection of microscopic domains with chaotically directed neutron quantization axis, which allows the matrix elements to be treated as scalars after averaging.

## Method

The authors model the strongly interacting nucleon-hyperon mixture (neutrons, protons, Λ-hyperons, and Σ⁻-hyperons) using the relativistic Landau theory of Fermi liquids generalized for superfluidity, with a Hamiltonian that combines a Fermi-liquid term and a pairing term. They diagonalize this Hamiltonian via Bogoliubov transformations in the presence of superfluid currents (with Cooper pair momentum 2Q_i), expand all quantities linearly in Q_i/p_F, and derive the entrainment matrix Y_ik from the resulting expression for the particle current density j_i. To handle the anisotropic neutron triplet-state (³P₂) pairing, they introduce an effective isotropic energy gap Δ^(n)_eff(T) defined as the minimum of the angle-dependent gap on the neutron Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- The entrainment matrix Y_ik is a 4×4 symmetric matrix (Y_ik = Y_ki) for baryon species i,k = n, p, Λ, Σ.
- The function Φ_i changes from Φ_i = 0 at T = 0 to Φ_i = 1 at T ≥ T_ci (the critical temperature for superfluidity of species i).
- The effective neutron gap is defined as Δ^(n)_eff(T) = min{Δ^(n)(|p|=p_Fn)} on the neutron Fermi surface.
- The matrix G_ik is defined as G_ik ≡ (1/(9π⁴)) p_Fi² p_Fk² f₁^{ik}, where f₁^{ik} are the symmetric Landau parameters (f_l^{ik}=f_l^{ki}).
- The effective mass relation is μ_i/m_i* = 1 - Σ_k (μ_k G_ik)/n_i.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature calculation of the relativistic entrainment matrix for nucleon-hyperon matter presented in GKH09 to finite temperatures, using the framework of relativistic Landau Fermi-liquid theory generalized for superfluidity by Larkin and Migdal (1963) and Leggett (1965). The nonrelativistic analog of this matrix (the Andreev-Bashkin or mass-density matrix) was analyzed for neutron-proton mixtures at zero temperature by Bedaque, Janka, and Kuo (1996), Comer and Joynt (2003), and Carter and Haensel (2006), and at arbitrary temperature by Gusakov and Haensel (2005). The results enable modeling of pulsations of massive neutron stars with superfluid nucleon-hyperon cores and studies of kinetic properties of superfluid baryon matter.
