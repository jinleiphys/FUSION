# Extended RPA with ground-state correlations

> We propose a time-independent method for finding a correlated ground state of an extended time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory, known as the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM). The correlated ground state is used to formulate the small amplitude limit of TDDM (STDDM) which is a version of extended RPA theories with ground-state correlations. To demonstrate the feasibility of the method, we calculate the ground state of 22O and study the first 2+ state and its two-phonon states using STDDM.

## Key claim

The authors propose a time-independent iterative method based on Newton's gradient method to find a correlated ground state for the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), which avoids the spurious oscillations present in the previous time-dependent approach when the time constant cannot be taken sufficiently large in realistic cases.

## Method

The method solves the stationary TDDM equations for the occupation matrix and two-body correlation matrix using iterative Newton's gradient method, starting from the Hartree-Fock ground state. The small amplitude limit of TDDM (STDDM) is then formulated by linearizing the equations of motion for the one-body and two-body density matrices around the correlated ground state, yielding a non-hermitian eigenvalue problem for excitation energies and amplitudes. The approach is demonstrated for the neutron-rich nucleus 22O using a pairing-type residual interaction of density-dependent delta function form, with only the neutron 1d5/2 and 2s1/2 orbits included in the truncated single-particle space.

## Key numbers

- The final value of the sum of absolute values of the matrix elements of F1 and F2 at the 2100th iteration step is 2.5 × 10⁻⁵ MeV.
- The final single-particle energies of the 1d5/2 and 2s1/2 orbits are -6.9 MeV and -2.8 MeV, respectively.
- The occupation probabilities of the 1d5/2 and 2s1/2 orbits are 0.98 and 0.05, respectively.
- The correlation energy Ecor is -0.60 MeV.
- The total energy is decreased by 0.22 MeV from the Hartree-Fock value.
- The reduced transition probability for the first 2+ state is 68 fm⁴; with a neutron effective charge of 0.5e, the B(E2:0+ → 2₁+) becomes 17 e²fm⁴, comparable with the experimental value of 21 ± 8 e²fm⁴.
- The ratios of the transition strengths of the 2+ and 4+ two-phonon states to that of the 0+ two-phonon state are 0.55 and 0.30, respectively.
- The reduced transition probabilities from the 0+, 2+, and 4+ two-phonon states to the one-phonon state are 16, 14, and 12 fm⁴, respectively.
- The imaginary parts of the eigenvalues for 2+ states are less than 0.05 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) and its small amplitude limit (STDDM) developed by the same group (Tohyama and collaborators, 1990s) by providing a time-independent method for obtaining the correlated ground state, improving upon the earlier time-dependent approach that suffered from spurious oscillations (Tohyama, 1995). The results for 22O are compared with second RPA (SRPA) calculations and with QRPA calculations by Khan and collaborators (2002) and Matsuo (2001), showing that STDDM with ground-state correlations produces a more collective and energetically shifted first 2+ state. The method enables the study of two-phonon states and the role of various two-body amplitudes, and the authors note that the self-energy terms missing in STDDM can be included using the extended RPA formalism of Tohyama and Schuck (2001).
