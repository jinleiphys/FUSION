# Transport Estimations of Final State Interaction Effects on Short-range Correlation Studies Using the $(e,e'p)$ and $(e,e'pp)$ Reactions

> Short range correlated (SRC) nucleon-nucleon pairs in nuclei are typically studied using measurements of electron-induced hard nucleon-knockout reactions (e.g. $(e,e'p)$ and $(e,e'pN)$), where the kinematics of the knocked-out nucleons are used to infer their initial state prior to the interaction. The validity of this inference relies on our understanding of the scattering reaction, most importantly how rescattering of the detected nucleons (final state interactions or FSI) distort their kinematical distributions. Recent SRC measurements on medium to heavy nuclei have been performed at high-$x_B$ (i.e., anti-parallel kinematics) where calculations of light nuclei indicate that such distortion effects are small. Here we study the impact of FSI on recent $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ and $^{12}$C$(e,e'pp)$ measurements using a transport approach. We find that while FSI can significantly distort the measured kinematical distributions of SRC breakup events, selecting high-$x_B$ anti-parallel events strongly suppresses such distortions. In addition, including the effects of FSI improves the agreement between Generalized Contact Formalism-based calculations and data and can help identify those observables that have minimal sensitivity to FSI effects. This result helps confirm the interpretation of experimental data in terms of initial-state momentum distributions and provides a new tool for the study of SRCs using lepton-scattering reactions.

## Key claim
While final state interactions (FSI) can significantly distort measured kinematical distributions of short-range correlation (SRC) breakup events in carbon-12, selecting high-$x_B$ anti-parallel events (with $x_B \ge 1.2$) strongly suppresses such distortions, and including FSI effects via a transport approach improves agreement between Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) calculations and experimental data.

## Method
The authors implemented the GCF two-nucleon spectral function into the eGENIE lepton-nucleus scattering event generator, which uses an IntraNuclear Cascade transport model (the GENIE "hA" model) to simulate FSI via hadron-nucleus cross sections. They generated quasielastic scattering events for $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ and $^{12}$C$(e,e'pp)$ reactions with a 5.01 GeV electron beam, applied CLAS detector acceptance and resolution smearing, and compared three calculation types: Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) without FSI, PWIA with transparency and single-charge exchange corrections (T+SCX), and full transport calculations. They also compared GCF-based SRC pair events against mean-field (Fermi gas) events to assess background contamination.

## Key numbers
- Effective transparency for $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$: 0.61 (T+SCX) and 0.58 (Full); for $^{12}$C$(e,e'pp)$: 0.83 (T+SCX) and 0.73 (Full)
- Event selection cuts included $x_B > 1.2$, $Q^2 \gtrsim 1.7$ GeV$^2$, $0.62 < |{\bf p_N}|/|{\bf q}| < 0.92$, $0.4 < |{\bf p_{miss}}| < 1.0$ GeV/c, $|{\bf p_{recoil}}| > 0.35$ GeV/c
- GCF model used a gaussian SRC pair center-of-mass momentum distribution with width $\sigma_{c.m.} = 150$ MeV/c in each cartesian direction
- PWIA/Full and PWIA/T+SCX ratios agreed to within 5-15%

## Context
This work extends previous SRC studies using Glauber and Eikonal approximations (Frankfurt 1996, Sargsian 2001, Colle 2015) that are reliable for light nuclei but cannot be fully applied to medium and heavy nuclei or to the recoil nucleon in two-nucleon knockout reactions. The study directly supports the data analyses of Schmidt (2020) and Cohen (2018) by confirming that their kinematical cuts effectively suppress FSI distortions, and it provides a new tool (GCF in eGENIE) for studying SRCs in both electron and neutrino scattering experiments. The implementation enables future studies of neutrino-nucleus reactions involving SRC pairs.
