# An introduction to the quark model

> This document contains a review on the quark model, prepared for lectures at the Niccolo Cabeo School at Ferrara in May 2012. It includes some historical aspects, the spectral properties of the 2-body and 3-body Schroedinger operators applied to mesons and baryons, the link between meson and baryon spectra, the role of flavour independence, and the speculations about stable or metastable multiquarks. The analogies between few-charge systems and few-quark bound states will be underlined.

## Key claim

This review argues that the quark model, despite its successes in classifying baryon spectra and explaining many regularities, faces serious limitations in reproducing the observed level ordering of light baryons, particularly the Roper resonance, and that these limitations likely arise from missing relativistic effects and chiral dynamics that cannot be mimicked by static, spin-independent potentials.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of the Schrödinger equation to three-quark systems (baryons) using Jacobi coordinates, with methods including harmonic oscillator expansions, first-order perturbation theory around the harmonic oscillator, converged variational methods (Gaussian expansion), Faddeev equations in position space, and hyperspherical expansion. The review covers both equal-mass and unequal-mass cases, discusses permutation symmetry constraints for identical and distinguishable quarks, and examines the link between meson and baryon potentials via the "1/2" rule from colour-octet exchange.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator energy for equal masses is given by \(E=(6+4n_x+2\ell_x+4n_y+2\ell_y)\sqrt{K/m}\), with cumulated excitation number \(N=2n_x+\ell_x+2n_y+\ell_y\)
- For two different masses \((m,m,M)\), the energy is \(E(m,m,M)=\sqrt{K/m}(3+4n_x+2\ell_x)+\sqrt{K/\mu}(3+4n_y+2\ell_y)\) with \(\mu^{-1}=(m^{-1}+2M^{-1})/3\)
- The chromomagnetic hyperfine interaction for \(\rho-\pi\) gives a colour factor \(-16/3\), spin factor \(4\), and splitting of about 0.6 GeV; for \(\Delta-N\) the colour factor is \(-8/3\), spin factor \(6\), giving about 0.3 GeV
- The \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(\Lambda(1520)\) pair shows an unusually large separation attributed to coupling to the nearby \(\bar K N\) threshold
- For the \((QQq)\) system, the level spacing (2S)-(1S) of \((Q\bar Q)\) decreases for quark mass \(M\lesssim 6\) GeV and increases for \(M\gtrsim 6\) GeV

## Context

This review extends the harmonic oscillator classification work of Dalitz (1960s), Isgur and Karl (1978), Gromes et al. (1979), and Stancu et al. (1991), and contrasts with the Goldstone-boson exchange model of Glozman and Riska (1995) which successfully reproduces the Roper resonance ordering. The paper enables future work on double-charm baryons (still unconfirmed experimentally despite SELEX results from 2002-2004), triple-heavy baryons \((ccc)\) as the "ultimate deal of baryon spectroscopy" (Bjorken, 1985), and the ongoing debate about whether diquarks are effective degrees of freedom or fundamental constituents, as used by Maiani et al. (2005) for \(X,Y,Z\) resonances.
