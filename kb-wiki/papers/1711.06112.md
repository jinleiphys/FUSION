# $SU(2N_F)$ symmetry of confinement in QCD and its observation at high temperature

> In this talk we first overview lattice results that have led to the observation of new SU(2)_{CS} and SU(2N_F) symmetries upon artificial truncation of the near-zero modes of the Dirac operator at zero temperature and at high temperature without any truncation. These symmetries are larger than the chiral symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian and contain chiral symmetries SU(N_F)_L \times SU(N_F)_R and U(1)_A as subgroups. In addition to the standard chiral transformations the SU(2)_{CS} and SU(2N_F) transformations mix the right- and left-handed components of the quark fields. It is a symmetry of the confining chromo-electric interaction while the chromo-magnetic interaction manifestly breaks it. Emergence of these symmetries upon truncation of the near-zero modes of the Dirac operator at T=0 means that all effects of the chromo-magnetic interaction are located exclusively in the near-zero modes, while confining chromo-electric interaction is distributed among all modes. Appearance of these symmetries at high T, where the temperature suppresses the near-zero modes, has radical implications because these symmetries are incompatible with the asymptotically free deconfined quarks at increasing temperature. The elementary objects in the high-temperature phase of QCD should be quarks bound by the pure chromo-electric field that is not accompanied by the chromo-magnetic effects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at high temperature (above ~220 MeV), QCD exhibits approximate \(SU(2)_{CS}\) and \(SU(4)\) symmetries that are larger than the chiral symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian, and that these symmetries are incompatible with asymptotically free deconfined quarks; the observed correlators approach the symmetric limit rather than the free quark limit, even at 380 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of spatial (z-direction) correlators for all possible \(J=0,1\) local isovector operators using domain wall fermions at temperatures above \(T_c\) up to 380 MeV. They classified the resulting correlator multiplets according to their transformation properties under \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R\), \(U(1)_A\), \(SU(2)_{CS}\), and \(SU(4)\) symmetries, and compared the dressed correlators with those from non-interacting (free) quarks.

## Key numbers

- The \(SU(2)_{CS}\) and \(SU(4)\) breaking at the highest available temperature of 380 MeV is at the level of 5%.
- The \(U(1)_A\) and \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R\) symmetries are "exactly" restored at temperatures above 220 MeV.
- The study covers temperatures up to 380 MeV, with spatial correlators calculated for all \(J=0,1\) local isovector operators.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior lattice studies at zero temperature (Glozman 2015, 2016) where \(SU(2)_{CS}\) and \(SU(4)\) symmetries were discovered upon artificial truncation of near-zero Dirac modes. At high temperature, the near-zero modes are naturally suppressed, allowing observation of these symmetries without truncation. The results contradict the expectation from asymptotic freedom that quarks become deconfined and free at high temperature; instead they suggest the high-temperature phase consists of quarks bound by a pure chromo-electric string, which the author terms "stringy matter." The work builds on lattice simulations by the JLQCD collaboration and on earlier studies of spatial correlators at high temperature (R 2017, C 2017).
