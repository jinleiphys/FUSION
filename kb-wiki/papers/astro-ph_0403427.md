# Bremsstrahlung photons from the bare surface of a strange quark star

> The photon emissivity from the bremsstrahlung process ee-> eeγoccuring in the electrosphere at the bare surface of a strange quark star is calculated. For surface temperatures T<10^9K, the photon flux exceeds that of e+e- pairs that are produced via the Schwinger mechanism in the presence of a strong electric field that binds electrons to the surface of the quark star. The average energy of photons emitted from the bremsstrahlung process can be 0.5 MeV or more, which is larger than that in e+e- pair annihilation. The observation of this distinctive photon spectrum would constitute an unmistakable signature of a strange quark star and shed light on color superconductivity at stellar densities.

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlung process \(e^-e^- \rightarrow e^-e^-\gamma\) in the electrosphere of a bare strange quark star can produce photon luminosities that exceed those from \(e^+e^-\) pair production via the Schwinger mechanism, but only for surface temperatures \(T < 10^9\) K and specifically for the case where a charge-neutralizing surface electron layer is present (as in the 2SC, gapless CFL, or crystalline color superconducting phases, but not in the pure CFL phase).

## Method

The authors calculated the photon emissivity from electron-electron bremsstrahlung in the degenerate, ultra-relativistic electron layer (electrosphere) at the bare quark star surface using Low's theorem for soft-photon emission, factorizing the bremsstrahlung amplitude into an elastic Møller scattering part and a classical radiation part. They incorporated in-medium effects including the plasma frequency as a low-energy cutoff, screening via the Debye mass from the hard dense loop approximation, and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) suppression factor for low-energy photons due to multiple scattering within the formation time. The emissivity was integrated over the electrosphere thickness using the Thomas-Fermi electron density profile.

## Key numbers

- For surface temperatures \(T < 10^9\) K, the bremsstrahlung photon flux exceeds that of \(e^+e^-\) pairs produced via the Schwinger mechanism.
- The average energy of bremsstrahlung photons can be 0.5 MeV or more, larger than that from \(e^+e^-\) pair annihilation.
- The electrosphere thickness is typically \(10^3\) fm or more, with electron chemical potential \(\mu_e(0) = 10-20\) MeV at the quark surface.
- The electron number density at the surface is \(n_e \sim 3 \times (10^{-5} - 10^{-4})\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The plasma frequency at the surface is of order 20 MeV; the critical electric field for pair production is \(E_{\text{cr}} \simeq 1.3 \times 10^{16}\ \text{V cm}^{-1}\).
- The LPM suppression factor for a photon of energy \(k = 0.5\) MeV and \(\mu_e = 10\) MeV is approximately \(S_{\text{LPM}}(k) \simeq 1/300\).
- The photon mean free path in the electrosphere is \(\sim 1000\) fm for \(T = 10^{11}\) K (8.6 MeV) and \(\mu_e = 20\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Page and Usov (2002), who showed that hard photon emission from \(e^+e^-\) annihilation at the bare surface of a strange quark star produces a distinctive light curve, and Usov (1998), who first proposed thermal emission of hard photons from pair creation in super-critical electric fields. The present paper adds bremsstrahlung from the electrosphere as an additional photon source that can dominate at lower temperatures, and it enables observational discrimination between strange quark stars and neutron stars using the INTEGRAL satellite, as well as constraints on color superconducting phases (2SC vs. CFL) based on the presence or absence of the electron layer.
