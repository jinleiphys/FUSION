# Updated dispersion-theoretical analysis of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> In the light of the new data on the various neutron and proton electromagnetic form factors taken in recent years, we update the dispersion-theoretical analysis of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors from the mid-nineties. The parametrization of the spectral functions includes constraints from unitarity, perturbative QCD, and recent measurements of the neutron charge radius. We obtain a good description of most modern form factor data, with the exception of the Jefferson Lab data on G_E^p/G_M^p in the four-momentum transfer range Q^2=3...6 GeV^2. For the magnetic radii of the proton and the neutron we find r_M^p = 0.857 fm and r_M^n = 0.879 fm, which is consistent with the recent determinations using continued fraction expansions.

## Key claim

The dispersion-theoretical analysis updated with new form factor data provides a good description of most modern data, but it cannot reproduce the Jefferson Lab data on the ratio \(G_E^p/G_M^p\) in the four-momentum transfer range \(Q^2 = 3\ldots 6\) GeV\(^2\), and it does not show the pronounced structure around \(Q^2 \simeq 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) that was found in the phenomenological fit of Friedrich and Walcher (2003).

## Method

The authors use unsubtracted dispersion relations to express the isoscalar and isovector Dirac and Pauli form factors in terms of spectral functions. The isovector spectral function includes a two-pion continuum (with the \(\rho\) resonance) from unitarity plus a series of vector-meson poles; the isoscalar channel contains only poles, with the lowest two being the \(\omega\) and \(\phi\) mesons. The number of poles is minimized using the stability condition of Sabba Stefanescu (1978), and constraints from the neutron charge radius, correct normalizations, and perturbative QCD asymptotic behavior are enforced, leaving 2 free parameters in the isovector channel and 3 in the isoscalar channel plus one QCD parameter.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic radius of the proton: \(r_M^p = 0.857\) fm.
* Magnetic radius of the neutron: \(r_M^n = 0.879\) fm.
* Electric radius of the proton: \(r_E^p = 0.848\) fm.
* \(\chi^2\) per datum of the fit: 2.07.
* Isoscalar pole masses: \(M_\omega = 0.782\) GeV, \(M_\phi = 1.019\) GeV, \(M_{S'} = 1.65\) GeV, \(M_{S''} = 1.68\) GeV.
* Isovector pole masses: \(M_{\rho'} = 1.05\) GeV, \(M_{\rho''} = 1.465\) GeV, \(M_{\rho'''} = 1.70\) GeV.
* QCD parameter \(\Lambda^2 = 18.28\) GeV\(^2\).
* \(\omega NN\) coupling constants: \(g_1^{\omega NN} = 21.4\), \(g_2^{\omega NN} = 0.9\).
* \(\phi NN\) coupling constants: \(g_1^{\phi NN} = -10.3\), \(g_2^{\phi NN} = -3.1\).

## Context

This work updates the dispersion-theoretical analyses of Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel (1996) and Hammer, Meissner, and Drechsel (1996) using the new data basis collected by Friedrich and Walcher (2003), which includes about a quarter of data points not available in 1996 (mainly for the neutron). The results are consistent with recent magnetic radius determinations from continued fraction expansions by Kubon et al. (2001) and Sick (2003), but the proton charge radius remains somewhat smaller than values from low-momentum-transfer analyses and Lamb shift measurements. The analysis enables future improvements such as including explicit \(\pi\rho\) and \(K\bar K\) continuum contributions, incorporating time-like data, and performing a full error analysis.
