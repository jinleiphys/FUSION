# Photoproduction of scalar mesons on protons and nuclei

> We study the photoproduction of scalar mesons close to the threshold of f_0(980) and a_0(980) using a unitary chiral model. Peaks for both resonances show up in the invariant mass distributions of pairs of pseudoscalar mesons. A discussion is made on the photoproduction of these resonances in nuclei, which can shed light on their nature, a subject of continuous debate.

## Key claim

The photoproduction of scalar mesons $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ on protons near threshold (photon energy $E_{\gamma} = 1.7$ GeV) can be described using a unitary chiral model, but the predicted resonant signals for $\pi^+\pi^-$ production are only about 5% of the estimated background, making observation challenging; the $\pi^0\pi^0$ and $\pi^0\eta$ channels offer better signal-to-background ratios.

## Method

The authors use a nonperturbative unitary chiral model in coupled channels, based on a $O(p^2)$ expansion of the inverse of the $K$ matrix, to compute the photoproduction of scalar mesons via $\gamma p \rightarrow p M$ where $M$ is $f_0(980)$ or $a_0(980)$. The calculation includes meson pole diagrams, contact terms required by gauge invariance, and final-state interactions of the meson pairs through iterated loops, with the strong meson-meson $t$ matrix taken from the chiral unitary approach of Oller and Oset (1997). The photon energy is chosen at $E_{\gamma} = 1.7$ GeV to produce the scalar resonances close to threshold.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy used: $E_{\gamma} = 1.7$ GeV (lab frame)
- Estimated background for $\gamma p \rightarrow p \pi^+ \pi^-$ at $E_{\gamma} = 1.7$ GeV: approximately 45 $\mu$b (from experimental data of ABBHHM collaboration)
- Background differential cross section $d\sigma/dM_I$ for $\pi^+\pi^-$: approximately 55 $\mu$b/GeV
- Resonant peak strength for $f_0(980)$ in $\pi^+\pi^-$: approximately 2.5 $\mu$b/GeV
- Signal-to-background ratio for $\pi^+\pi^-$: 5%
- Signal-to-background ratio for $\pi^0\pi^0$: 20% (since $\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^0\pi^0 p$ cross section is about eight times smaller than for $\pi^+\pi^-$)
- Pion decay constant used: $f_{\pi} = 93$ MeV; an average value $f = 1.15 f_{\pi}$ is used for kaon amplitudes

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller and Oset (1997) for meson-meson interactions to photoproduction reactions, building on earlier studies of $\pi\pi$ and $K\bar{K}$ photoproduction by Tejedor et al. (1995), Ochi et al. (1997), and Adam (1997). The technique is analogous to that used for radiative $\phi$ decay ($\phi \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-\gamma$) via $K^+K^-$ loops, which was successfully confirmed by Novosibirsk experiments. The results enable experimental searches for scalar meson production in photon-induced reactions on protons and nuclei, potentially clarifying the long-debated nature of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances as meson-meson molecules or other configurations.
