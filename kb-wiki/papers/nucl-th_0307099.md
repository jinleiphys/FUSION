# Three-Body Interactions in Many-Body Effective Field Theory

> This contribution is an advertisement for applying effective field theory (EFT) to many-body problems, including nuclei and cold atomic gases. Examples involving three-body interactions are used to illustrate how EFT's quantify and systematically eliminate model dependence, and how they make many-body calculations simpler and more powerful.

# Three-Body Interactions in Many-Body Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) can systematically eliminate model dependence in many-body calculations of nuclei and cold atomic gases, but three-body interactions inevitably arise from logarithmic divergences in three-to-three scattering and must be included to avoid spurious model dependence; two-body data alone is insufficient to determine the three-body coupling constant.

## Method

The author applies EFT to a dilute Fermi gas with a local Lagrangian containing two-body and three-body contact interactions, using dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction (DR/MS) and matching to the effective range expansion for two-body scattering. The renormalization group (RG) equations for the coupling constants are derived from logarithmic divergences in three-to-three scattering amplitudes, and the energy density is computed diagrammatically in powers of the Fermi momentum times the scattering length (k_F a_s). Local field redefinitions are used to test whether quantities such as the momentum distribution are genuine observables.

## Key numbers

- The two-body coefficient C_0 = 4π a_s / M, where a_s is the scattering length and M the mass.
- At cutoff Λ = 2.1 fm^{-1} (corresponding to 350 MeV lab energy), all high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials collapse to the same low-momentum potential V_{low k}.
- The energy density expansion: ℰ = ρ (k_F^2 / 2M) [3/5 + (2/3π)(k_F a_s) + (4/(35π^2))(11 - 2 ln 2)(k_F a_s)^2 + ...].
- The three-to-three scattering amplitude contains a logarithmic divergence proportional to (C_0)^4 with coefficient (4π - 3√3)/(8π^3) in dimensional regularization.
- The RG equation for D_0(μ) yields D_0(μ) = D_0(1/a_s) + M^3 (C_0)^4 (4π - 3√3)/(4π^3) ln(a_s μ).

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach to many-body systems pioneered by Hammer (2000) and Braaten, building on the V_{low k} renormalization group treatment of nucleon-nucleon potentials by Schwenk (2003). The analysis of local field redefinitions and their effect on observables follows Furnstahl (2001, 2002a). The paper enables systematic calculations of energy functionals for nuclei far from stability and superfluidity in trapped fermionic atoms, and connects to ongoing work merging density functional theory with EFT by Puglia (2003) and nonperturbative EFT for large scattering lengths by Steele (2000).
