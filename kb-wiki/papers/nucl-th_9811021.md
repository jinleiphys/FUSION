# Effective kaon energy from a novel chiral SU(3) model

> A new chiral SU(3) Lagrangian is proposed to describe the properties of kaons and anti-kaons in the nuclear medium. The saturation properties of nuclear matter are reproduced as well as the results of the Dirac-Brückner theory. After introducing the coupling between the omega meson and the kaon, our results for effective kaon and anti-kaon energy are quite similar as calculated in the one-boson-exchange model.

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel chiral SU(3) Lagrangian that simultaneously describes nuclear matter ground-state properties and kaon-nuclear interactions, and they find that after introducing an omega-kaon coupling, the effective kaon and anti-kaon energies become quite similar to those calculated in the one-boson-exchange model, though the anti-kaon optical potential remains much weaker than predictions from kaon atomic data.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral SU(3)-symmetric Lagrangian where baryon masses are generated by scalar condensates and their splitting is realized through SU(3) symmetry breaking for these condensates. The model parameters are fixed by reproducing the SU(3) vacuum properties and nuclear matter saturation properties, and the kaon-nuclear interaction is described by a Lagrangian that includes a contact term, a scalar-exchange term involving the sigma and zeta fields, and a vector coupling between the omega meson and the kaon introduced by treating the vector field as a gauge field. The effective kaon and anti-kaon energies are then computed from the resulting mean-field equations as a function of nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation properties: effective nucleon mass ratio \(m^{*}/m_{N}=0.641\), binding energy \(E/A(\rho_{0})=-15.93\) MeV, incompressibility \(K=266.2\) MeV, and saturation density \(\rho_{0}=0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Vacuum meson parameters: pion decay constant \(f_{\pi}=93.3\) MeV, kaon decay constant \(f_{K}=122\) MeV, pion mass \(m_{\pi}=139\) MeV, kaon mass \(m_{K}=498\) MeV
- At normal nuclear density, the optical potential for the kaon is \(U_{opt}^{K}=21.53\) MeV and for the anti-kaon is \(U_{opt}^{\bar{K}}=-98.7\) MeV
- The anti-kaon optical potential from kaon atomic data is \(U_{opt}^{\bar{K}}=-200\pm20\) MeV, which is much stronger than the model prediction

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral SU(3) approaches by providing a consistent calculation that simultaneously describes both kaon-nuclear interactions and the ground state of dense matter, which had not been performed previously. The model reproduces the density dependence of mean fields from Dirac-Brückner theory (Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990) up to four times normal density, and the results for effective kaon and anti-kaon energies are compared with chiral perturbation theory and the one-boson-exchange model (Schaffner et al., 1994). The findings enable further investigation into the discrepancy between the model's anti-kaon optical potential and the stronger potential extracted from kaon atomic data (Friedman et al., 1993).
