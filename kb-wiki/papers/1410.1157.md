# The parity-violating nucleon-nucleon force in the 1/N_c expansion

> Several experimental investigations have observed parity violation in nuclear systems-a consequence of the weak force between quarks. We apply the $1/N_c$ expansion of QCD to the P-violating T-conserving component of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential. We show there are two leading-order operators, both of which affect $\vec{p}p$ scattering at order $N_c$. We find an additional four operators at $O(N_c^0 \sin^2 θ_W)$ and six at $O(1/N_c)$. Pion exchange in the PV NN force is suppressed by $1/N_c$ and $\sin^2 θ_W$, providing a quantitative explanation for its non-observation up to this time. The large-$N_c$ hierarchy of other PV NN force mechanisms is consistent with estimates of the couplings in phenomenological models. The PV observed in $\vec{p}p$ scattering data is compatible with natural values for the strong and weak coupling constants: there is no evidence of fine tuning.

# The parity-violating nucleon-nucleon force in the 1/N_c expansion

## Key claim
The parity-violating (PV) nucleon-nucleon (NN) force is dominated by two leading-order operators at order N_c, with pion exchange suppressed by both 1/N_c and sin²θ_W, providing a quantitative explanation for why pion-exchange effects have not been observed experimentally. The PV observed in polarized proton-proton scattering is compatible with natural values for the strong and weak coupling constants, with no evidence of fine tuning.

## Method
The authors apply the 1/N_c expansion of QCD to the parity-violating, time-reversal-conserving component of the NN potential, using the Hartree expansion for the nuclear Hamiltonian in the large-N_c limit. They classify operators by their N_c scaling and isospin transformation properties (ΔI=0,1,2), then match these to the operator structures of the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) meson-exchange potential. The weak couplings are extracted and their N_c and sin²θ_W scalings determined, then compared to experimental constraints from polarized pp scattering at 15, 45, and 221 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Two leading-order operators affect pp scattering at order N_c (Eq. 4 in the paper)
- Four operators appear at O(N_c⁰ sin²θ_W) and six at O(1/N_c)
- sin²θ_W ≈ 0.23, from the Particle Data Group
- G_F = 1.16 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻², cos²θ_C = 0.946
- The naturalness estimate for the leading weak couplings h_ρ⁰ and h_ρ² is G_F f_π Λ_χ ∼ 1.0 × 10⁻⁶, with f_π = 92.4 MeV
- The predicted weak couplings (in units of 10⁻⁶) are: h_ρ⁰ = -1.0, h_ρ¹ = -0.077, h_ρ² = -1.0, h_ω⁰ = -0.33, h_ω¹ = -0.23
- The predicted strong couplings are: g_ω = 12, g_ρ = 4.0, ξ_S = -0.33, ξ_V = 3.0
- The bound on h_π¹ from the large-N_c analysis is |h_π¹| ≲ (0.8 ± 0.3) × 10⁻⁷, consistent with the experimental bound from ¹⁸F experiments of |h_π¹| ≲ 1.3 × 10⁻⁷

## Context
This work extends the large-N_c analysis previously applied to the strong NN potential by Kaplan and Savage (1996) and to the three-nucleon potential by Phillips (2013), now applied to the parity-violating component. It provides a model-independent derivation of scalings that were previously obtained only within the chiral soliton model by Kaiser (1989), Meissner (1998), and Grach (1988), or from consistency relations for PV pion-nucleon scattering by Zhu (2009). The results contradict the DDH framework (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1979), which predicted a large h_π¹ coupling, and instead explain why pion-exchange effects have not been observed in hadronic parity-violation experiments. The analysis enables future lattice QCD calculations of the iso-tensor piece of the PV NN interaction to break degeneracies in the coupling combinations, and suggests that a new ΔI=1 spin-flavor structure (the U¹_D operator) should be included in subleading analyses of the weak NN force.
