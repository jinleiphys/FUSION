# Low energy collective modes of deformed superfluid nuclei within the finite amplitude method

> Background: The major challenge for nuclear theory is to describe and predict global properties and collective modes of atomic nuclei. Of particular interest is the response of the nucleus to a time-dependent external field that impacts the low-energy multipole and beta-decay strength. Purpose: We propose a method to compute low-lying collective modes in deformed nuclei within the finite amplitude method (FAM) based on the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA). By using the analytic property of the response function, we find the QRPA amplitudes by computing the residua of the FAM amplitudes by means of a contour integration around the QRPA poles in a complex frequency plane. Methods: We use the superfluid nuclear density functional theory with Skyrme energy density functionals, FAM-QRPA approach, and the conventional matrix formulation of the QRPA (MQRPA). Results: We demonstrate that the complex-energy FAM-QRPA method reproduces low-lying collective states obtained within the conventional matrix formulation of the QRPA theory. Illustrative calculations are performed for the isoscalar monopole strength in deformed 24Mg and for low-lying K = 0 quadrupole vibrational modes of deformed Yb and Er isotopes. Conclusions: The proposed FAM-QRPA approach allows one to efficiently calculate low-lying collective modes in spherical and deformed nuclei throughout the entire nuclear landscape, including shape-vibrational excitations, pairing vibrational modes, and beta-decay rates.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that low-lying discrete QRPA modes in deformed superfluid nuclei can be computed by performing a contour integration of the finite amplitude method (FAM) amplitudes in the complex frequency plane around the QRPA poles, rather than by directly diagonalizing the large QRPA matrix. The method is demonstrated to reproduce conventional matrix QRPA results, but the abstract's claim of applicability "throughout the entire nuclear landscape" is conditioned on the method being validated only for specific test cases (24Mg and Yb/Er isotopes) and on the assumption that all QRPA frequencies are real (the matrix S is positive definite).

## Method

The authors start from the finite amplitude method (FAM) equations for superfluid systems, which are equivalent to the linear response formalism of the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA). They show that the FAM amplitudes X(ω) and Y(ω) have first-order poles at the QRPA eigenfrequencies Ω_i, and they compute the residues at these poles by performing a contour integration in the complex frequency plane around each pole. The QRPA amplitudes and transition strengths are then extracted from these residues using Cauchy's integral formula, with the QRPA eigenfrequencies determined from an expression involving the integrated induced fields. The method is implemented using the HFBTHO code with Skyrme energy density functionals and a contact volume pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

* For 24Mg at the oblate HFB minimum (β = -0.163), the pairing gaps are Δ_n = 0.666 MeV and Δ_p = 0.654 MeV.
* The first two low-lying K=0 isoscalar monopole QRPA energies in 24Mg are Ω_1 = 1.3183 MeV and Ω_2 = 1.3731 MeV (FAM-QRPA), with corresponding transition strengths |⟨i|F^ISM|0⟩|^2 of 5.773×10^{-4} e^2 fm^4 and 1.511×10^{-2} e^2 fm^4 (FAM-B method).
* The third isoscalar monopole mode in 24Mg has Ω = 2.4581 MeV with strength 0.1782 e^2 fm^4 (FAM-B), and the fifth mode has Ω = 3.6657 MeV with strength 0.5771 e^2 fm^4 (FAM-B).
* The contour integration used circles with radii 0.02 MeV, discretized with 11 points, and the FAM convergence criterion was 10^{-5}, typically reached after about 40 iterations.

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method (FAM), previously applied to giant resonances and strength functions (Nakatsukasa 2007, PhysRevC.80.044301, PhysRevC.84.041305), to the calculation of discrete low-lying QRPA modes. It provides an alternative to the conventional matrix QRPA (MQRPA) approach, which requires constructing and diagonalizing a large QRPA matrix and often imposes truncations that break self-consistency (PhysRevC.71.034310). The method also contrasts with the iterative Arnoldi diagonalization approach (PhysRevC.81.034312, PhysRevC.86.024303) and a recent FAM-based method that stores the full QRPA matrix (PhysRevC.87.014331). The technique enables efficient computation of low-lying collective modes in deformed nuclei without the memory requirements of full matrix diagonalization, and the authors note that the method can be extended to shape-vibrational excitations, pairing vibrational modes, and beta-decay rates.
