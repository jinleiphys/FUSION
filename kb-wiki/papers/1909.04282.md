# Pure spin-3/2 representation with consistent interactions

> We have investigated the use of pure spin-3/2 propagator with consistent interaction Lagrangians to describe the property of spin-3/2 resonance. For this purpose we use the antisymmetric tensor spinor representation. By using the primary and secondary constraints we obtain the interaction fields that have the correct degrees of freedom. To visualize the result we calculate the contribution of spin-3/2 $Δ$ resonance to the total cross section of pion scattering and pion photoproduction off the nucleon. The result confirms that the scattering and photoproduction amplitudes obtained from the pure spin-3/2 representation with consistent interaction Lagrangians exhibit the required property of a resonance. Therefore, the formalism can be used for phenomenological investigations in the realm of nuclear and particle physics.

## Key claim

The pure spin-3/2 representation in the antisymmetric tensor spinor (ATS) formalism can describe the resonance behavior of a spin-3/2 particle like the Δ(1232) baryon, but only if the interaction Lagrangians are constructed to satisfy the same constraints as the free field; otherwise, the amplitude vanishes at the resonance pole instead of peaking.

## Method

The authors constructed the pure spin-3/2 propagator using the antisymmetric tensor spinor representation and derived the primary and secondary constraints via Dirac-Faddeev quantization to identify the conditions for consistent interaction Lagrangians. They then calculated the Δ(1232) resonance contribution to the total cross section of elastic πN scattering and pion photoproduction off the nucleon using three different interaction models: the conventional gauge-invariant Rarita-Schwinger interaction, a modified derivative coupling, and their newly proposed consistent interaction Lagrangian that includes the pure spin-3/2 projection operator.

## Key numbers

- The pure spin-3/2 field in the ATS representation has 8 degrees of freedom, obtained from 24 components reduced by 16 constraints (4 primary and 4 secondary constraints each reducing by 4×4=16 components).
- The resonance peak in πN scattering appears near W ≈ 1.25 GeV for all models, with the consistent interaction model showing moderate background and no divergence at high energies, unlike the modified derivative coupling model.
- The hadronic form factor used a cutoff Λ = 0.5 GeV in the dipole form F = Λ⁴/[Λ⁴ + (q² − m_Δ²)²] to produce a clean resonance structure.
- At the resonance pole (q² = m_Δ²), the Δ-exchange amplitude from the consistent interaction is proportional to that from the gauge-invariant Rarita-Schwinger interaction, while the naive gauge-invariant ATS interaction gives zero amplitude at the pole.

## Context

This work extends the pure spin-3/2 formalism of Acosta et al. (2015), who applied the ATS representation to Compton scattering where the spin-3/2 particle is on-shell and does not resonate. The authors identify a problem not observed in that earlier work: the standard gauge-invariant interaction Lagrangian fails to produce a resonance peak in πN scattering. They resolve this by constructing consistent interaction Lagrangians that preserve the free-field constraints, following the Dirac-Faddeev quantization approach of Pascalutsa (1998, 2001). The formalism enables phenomenological investigations of spin-3/2 resonances in nuclear and particle physics, including applications to pion photoproduction and potentially to spin-3/2 excited leptons in electroweak interactions.
