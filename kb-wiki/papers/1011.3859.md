# Character Expansions in Physics

> Expanding products of invariant functions of a group element as a series in the basis of characters of the irreducible representations of a group is widely used in many areas of physics and related fields. In this contribution a formula to generate such expansions and its various applications are briefly reviewed.

# Character Expansions in Physics

## Key claim
The paper reviews a formula for expanding products of invariant functions of a group element as a series in the basis of characters of irreducible representations, with the caveat that the formula is primarily combinatorial in nature and is presented for U(N) groups, though it can be generalized to orthogonal and symplectic groups and in principle to supergroup U(N/M) covariant representations.

## Method
The author derives a character expansion formula starting from a power series G(x,t) = Σ A_n(x) t^n, assumed convergent for |t|=1, and manipulates it to express products over eigenvalues of the fundamental representation as sums over irreducible representations labeled by partitions. For U(N), the expansion coefficients are given by determinants of the series coefficients A_n, with the representations labeled by integers derived from non-negative integers m_i and n_N. The formula simplifies when all A_n are non-negative, and examples are worked out using generating functions for complete symmetric functions and Chebyshev polynomials.

## Key numbers
- For U(N), characters are labeled by partitions (n₁, n₂, …, n_N) with n₁ ≥ n₂ ≥ … ≥ n_N, visualized as rows in Young tableaux.
- The generating function 1/∏(1 - x_i z) = Σ h_n(x) z^n yields the character expansion 1/det(1 - zU) = Σ χ_(n,0,0,…) z^n.
- For the quantity det(1 - 2xU + U²), the expansion uses A₀ = 1, A₁ = -2x, A₂ = 1, and A_n = 0 for n ≥ 3, with characters corresponding to Young tableaux having at most two boxes per row.
- The inverse quantity 1/det(1 - 2xU + U²) expands using Chebyshev polynomials of the second kind u_n(x) as coefficients, with characters corresponding to single-column Young tableaux having at most two boxes.

## Context
This work extends prior character expansion techniques used in QCD and gauge theories (Balantekin 2000), quantum transport in stochastic cavities (Khor), multiple-input multiple-output communications (MIMO), statistical theory of nuclear reactions (Mello), lattice gauge theory (Bars 1980), and random matrix models (Guhr 1997, Mitchell 2010). The formula enables systematic computation of group integrals that previously required tedious case-by-case expansions, and it opens the door to applications in supergroup U(N/M) representations and further developments in invariant integration over groups and supergroups.
