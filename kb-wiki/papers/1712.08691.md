# Is it possible to study neutrinoless $ββ$ decay by measuring double Gamow-Teller transitions?

> Searches of neutrinoless double-beta decay require information on the value of the nuclear matrix elements that rule the process to plan and interpret experiments. At present, however, even the matrix elements obtained with the most reliable many-body approaches do not agree to each other better than a factor two or three. A usual test of the many-body calculations is the comparison to several nuclear observables, but so far no nuclear structure property has been found to show a good correlation to neutrinoless double-beta decay. Here we propose that double charge-exchange experiments can offer a very valuable tool to provide insights on neutrinoless double-beta decay. Double charge-exchange reactions are being currently performed in various laboratories worldwide and aim to find the novel nuclear collectivity given by double Gamow-Teller excitations. Our results suggest a good linear correlation between double Gamow-Teller transitions to the ground state of the final nucleus and neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements. The correlation seems robust across $pf$-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that double Gamow-Teller (DGT) transitions measured in double charge-exchange experiments can serve as a proxy for neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, based on a linear correlation that appears robust across pf-shell nuclei but is explicitly not found in quasiparticle random phase approximation calculations.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations for 26 even-even nuclei in the pf-shell (mass numbers 42 to 60), computing both neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements and double Gamow-Teller transition matrix elements to the ground state of the final nucleus. They used three different shell model interactions (GXPF1B, KB3G in the pf-shell, and SDPFMU-DB in the extended sd-pf space) and compared fully correlated shell model states with simplified seniority-zero wave functions. The normalized radial and momentum distributions of the matrix elements were analyzed to understand the origin of the correlation.

## Key numbers

- The dominant GT part of the 0νββ decay NME accounts for almost 90% of the total NME.
- The correlation was found to be valid across a very wide range of NME values from 0 to approximately 10, encompassing the factor-of-two-or-three disagreement among different many-body approaches.
- Typical transferred momenta in 0νββ decay amount to p ~ 100 MeV.
- The radial distributions of both processes are dominated by short-range internucleon distances of r ≲ 2 fm.
- The study covered 26 initial even-even nuclei from the lightest 42Ca to the heaviest 60Cr.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ref. [2GT0nbb] which first showed a linear correlation between the energy of the 48Ca DGT giant resonance and the 0νββ decay NME. The results contradict the behavior found in quasiparticle random phase approximation calculations (Simkovic 2011), where DGT transition values tend to be persistently small and do not show the same correlation. The findings enable future double charge-exchange experiments (being performed at various laboratories worldwide, as noted by Cappuzzello, Takaki, Uesaka, and Takahisa) to provide indirect constraints on neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, which currently disagree among the most reliable many-body approaches by a factor of two or three.
