# Scanning the Quark-Gluon Plasma with Charmonium

> We suggest the variation of charmonium suppression with Feynman x_F in heavy ion collisions as a novel and sensitive probe for the properties of the matter created in such reactions. In contrast to the proton-nucleus case where nuclear suppression is weakest at small x_F, final state interactions with the comoving matter create a minimum at x_F=0, which is especially deep and narrow if a quark-gluon plasma is formed. While a particularly strong effect is predicted at SPS, at the higher RHIC energy it overlaps with the expected sharp variation with x_F of nuclear effects and needs comparison with proton-nucleus data. If thermal enhancement of J/Ψproduction takes over at the energies of RHIC and LHC, it will form an easily identified peak, rather than dip in x_F dependence. We predict a steep dependence on centrality and suggest that this new probe is complementary to the dependence on transverse energy, and is more sensitive to a scenario of final state interactions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Feynman \(x_F\) dependence of charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions is a sensitive probe for distinguishing between a quark-gluon plasma and a hadronic comover gas, but the effect at RHIC energies overlaps with nuclear coherence effects and requires comparison with proton-nucleus data to be isolated.

## Method

The authors calculate the ratio \(R_{AB}(x_F)\) of charmonium production in nucleus-nucleus to proton-proton collisions, factorizing the cross section into nuclear suppression \(S_{NUC}\) and final-state interaction (FSI) suppression \(S_{FSI}\). Nuclear suppression is modeled using known \(pA\) data and coherence effects (gluon shadowing) at RHIC, while FSI is implemented via two parameterized models: a comover hadron gas model (Capella et al.) and a QGP phase transition model (Blaizot et al.). The rapidity-dependent density of the produced matter is scaled by the hadron rapidity distribution to give the \(x_F\) dependence of the suppression factors.

## Key numbers

- At SPS (PbPb, \(E_{lab}=158\) GeV), the QGP model produces a "pronounced minimum" at \(x_F=0\) in \(R_{AB}(x_F)\), while the comover model produces only a "slight depression"; the nuclear absorption cross section is \(\sigma_{\Psi N}^{abs} = 6.4\) mb for the QGP model and 4.5 mb for the comover model.
- The critical participant density for QGP formation is \(n_{cr} = 3.4\) fm\(^{-2}\) with smearing factor \(\lambda = 2\) fm\(^2\); the comover freeze-out density is \(n_{fo} = 1.15\) fm\(^{-2}\) with comover cross section \(\sigma_{co} = 1\) mb.
- At SPS, the hadron rapidity distribution is parametrized with width \(\Delta_{SPS} = 1.5\), shift \(Y_{SPS} = 0.75\), and mid-rapidity normalization \(N_{SPS} = 600\).
- At RHIC (AuAu, \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV), the hadron rapidity distribution uses \(\Delta_{RHIC} = 2.4\), \(Y_{RHIC} = 1.75\), and \(N_{RHIC} = 1000\); the energy scaling exponent is \(\delta = 0.18\).
- The nuclear suppression at RHIC forms a "narrow peak" at \(x_F=0\) (dotted curve), which is substantially reduced by FSI, leading to a "rather flat" \(x_F\) dependence.

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions is due to cold nuclear matter effects or final-state interactions with produced matter, specifically addressing the ambiguity between models of comoving hadrons (Capella et al.) and QGP formation (Blaizot et al.). The proposed \(x_F\) scan is complementary to the existing transverse energy (\(E_T\)) dependence measurements from NA50, which have been unable to clearly discriminate between these scenarios. The paper enables future experimental tests at SPS, RHIC, and LHC, and notes that thermal enhancement of \(J/\Psi\) production (Thews et al., Braun-Munzinger and Stachel) could create a peak rather than a dip at \(x_F=0\), requiring further study.
