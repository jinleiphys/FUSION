# A new barrier penetration formula and its application to alpha-decay half-lives

> Starting from the WKB approximation, a new barrier penetration formula is proposed for potential barriers containing a long-range Coulomb interaction. This formula is especially proper for the barrier penetration with penetration energy much lower than the Coulomb barrier. The penetrabilities calculated from the new formula agree well with the results from the WKB method. As a first attempt, this new formula is used to evaluate alpha decay half-lives of atomic nuclei and a good agreement with the experiment is obtained.

# A new barrier penetration formula and its application to alpha-decay half-lives

## Key claim
The authors derive an analytical barrier penetration formula from the WKB approximation that accounts for the long-range Coulomb tail, making it especially suitable for penetration energies much lower than the Coulomb barrier where the standard parabolic approximation fails. The formula reproduces WKB numerical results with an average relative root mean square deviation of 0.28% for the outer part of the integral, and when applied to alpha decay half-lives, 93.90% of 344 nuclei agree with experiment within one order of magnitude.

## Method
Starting from the WKB integral for penetrability, the authors split the potential barrier at the barrier position R_B into an inner region approximated by half a parabola and an outer region where the Coulomb potential dominates. For the outer region, they expand the integrand using the small ratio of nuclear to Coulomb-minus-energy terms and evaluate the Coulomb integral analytically, using a Woods-Saxon form for the nuclear potential. The resulting analytical expression for x_2 (the outer part of the WKB exponent) is combined with the parabolic inner part to give the total penetrability, which is then used to compute alpha decay half-lives for 344 nuclei using the formalism of Denisov (2005) for the assault frequency and preformation factor.

## Key numbers
- Average relative root mean square deviation between the new formula and WKB for x_2: 0.28% (for Po isotopes, Table I)
- For 344 nuclei: 93.90% have |log10(T_cal/T_exp)| ≤ 1; 4.07% have 1 < |S_α| ≤ 2; 2.03% have 2 < |S_α| ≤ 3 (Table II)
- For 159 even-even nuclei: 97.48% have |S_α| ≤ 1 (Table II)
- For 68 spherical daughter nuclei (|β_2| < 0.01): 100.00% have |S_α| ≤ 1, with root mean square deviation of 0.34 in log10(T_1/2/s) (Table II and text)
- Alpha decay half-lives range from 10^{-7} to 10^{24} s across the dataset
- For 212Po: calculated log10(T_1/2/s) = -6.58 vs experimental -6.52; for 144Nd: calculated 23.17 vs experimental 22.86 (Table III)

## Context
This work extends the WKB-based treatment of barrier penetration beyond the parabolic approximation of Kemble (1935) and Hill (1953), which works well near and above the barrier but fails at sub-barrier energies due to the long-range Coulomb interaction. The new analytical formula enables efficient calculation of penetrability for applications requiring energy-dependent one-dimensional potentials or barrier distribution functions, as used in fusion studies by Rowley (1991) and others. The alpha decay application follows the approach of Denisov (2005) and provides a benchmark for spherical nuclei, with the authors noting that deformation effects (not included here) influence both preformation and penetration, as studied by Stewart (1996).
