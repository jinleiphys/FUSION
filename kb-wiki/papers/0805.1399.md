# $δ$ meson effects on neutron stars in the modified quark-meson coupling model

> The properties of neutron stars are investigated by including $δ$ meson field in the Lagrangian density of modified quark-meson coupling model. The $Σ^-$ population with $δ$ meson is larger than that without $δ$ meson at the beginning, but it becomes smaller than that without $δ$ meson as the appearance of $Ξ^-$. The $δ$ meson has opposite effects on hadronic matter with or without hyperons: it softens the EOSes of hadronic matter with hyperons, while it stiffens the EOSes of pure nucleonic matter. Furthermore, the leptons and the hyperons have the similar influence on $δ$ meson effects. The $δ$ meson increases the maximum masses of neutron stars. The influence of $(σ^*,ϕ)$ on the $δ$ meson effects are also investigated.

# δ meson effects on neutron stars in the modified quark-meson coupling model

## Key claim
The δ meson increases the maximum masses of neutron stars in the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model for all studied compositions (pure neutron matter, β-equilibrium nucleonic matter, and hyperonic matter), which is different from the Quantum Hadron Dynamics (QHD) model where the maximum mass decreases for some cases. The effect is conditional on the presence of hyperons: the δ meson softens the equation of state (EOS) of hadronic matter with hyperons, while it stiffens the EOS of pure nucleonic matter.

## Method
The authors extended the MQMC model by including the δ meson field, which couples only to u and d quarks, in the Lagrangian density. The density-dependent couplings between baryons and scalar mesons (σ, σ*, δ) were calculated self-consistently at the quark level using the MIT bag model with mean-field approximation. The model parameters were fixed by reproducing symmetric nuclear matter properties (binding energy 16 MeV, symmetry energy 32.5 MeV, compressibility 289 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³) and by requiring the pure neutron matter EOS to be consistent with experimental heavy-ion collision flow data. Neutron star properties were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, with the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland model used for subnuclear densities.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass for pure neutron matter (nn) with δ meson (gδᵘ = 4.2): 2.275 Mₛ, radius 12.07 km, central density 5.1 ρ₀
- Maximum mass for pure neutron matter (nn) without δ meson (gδᵘ = 0): 2.147 Mₛ, radius 11.50 km, central density 5.6 ρ₀
- Maximum mass for β-equilibrium nucleonic matter (np) with δ meson: 2.045 Mₛ, radius 11.30 km, central density 6.0 ρ₀
- Maximum mass for β-equilibrium nucleonic matter (np) without δ meson: 2.012 Mₛ, radius 10.95 km, central density 6.3 ρ₀
- Maximum mass for hyperonic matter with (σ*, φ) mesons (npH*) with δ meson: 1.556 Mₛ, radius 11.56 km, central density 6.0 ρ₀
- Maximum mass for hyperonic matter with (σ*, φ) mesons (npH*) without δ meson: 1.543 Mₛ, radius 11.10 km, central density 6.5 ρ₀
- Maximum mass for hyperonic matter without (σ*, φ) mesons (npH) with δ meson: 1.509 Mₛ, radius 12.03 km, central density 5.2 ρ₀
- Maximum mass for hyperonic matter without (σ*, φ) mesons (npH) without δ meson: 1.491 Mₛ, radius 11.54 km, central density 5.8 ρ₀
- The δ meson enlarges radii of neutron stars by about 0.5 km for stars with M > Mₛ
- Central density becomes about 0.5 ρ₀ smaller when δ meson is included
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- δ meson-quark coupling constant constrained to range 0 ≤ gδᵘ ≤ 4.2

## Context
This work extends the MQMC model, originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and refined by Fleck and by Jin and Jennings (who introduced the density-dependent bag constant), to include the δ meson field for the first time in this model. It builds on prior investigations of δ meson effects in the QHD model by Kubis, Haddad, and Liu, as well as studies in a chiral SU(3) model, a relativistic point coupling model, and a relativistic transport model. The inclusion of (σ*, φ) meson fields follows the work of Pal to account for the strong attractive ΛΛ interaction observed in hypernuclei. The results enable comparisons between the MQMC and QHD treatments of δ meson effects, and provide predictions for neutron star masses and radii that can be tested against observations from PSR 1913+16, 4U 0614+09, 4U 1636-536, and EXO 0748-676.
