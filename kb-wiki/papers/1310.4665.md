# Lee-Yang cluster expansion approach to the BCS-BEC crossover: BCS and BEC limits

> It is shown that a cluster expansion technique, which is usually applied in the high-temperature regime to calcutate virial coefficients, can be applied to evaluate the superfluid transition temperature of the BCS-BEC crossover à la Lee and Yang. The transition temperature is identified with the emergence of the singularity in the sum of a certain infinite series of cluster functions. In the weak-coupling limit, we reproduce the Thouless criterion and the number equation of Nozières and Schmitt-Rink, and hence the transition temperature of the BCS theory. In the strong-coupling limit, we reproduce the transition temperature of Bose-Einstein condensation of non-interacting tightly bound dimers.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Lee-Yang cluster expansion method, normally restricted to high-temperature virial coefficient calculations, can be extended to evaluate the superfluid transition temperature of the BCS-BEC crossover by identifying the transition with a singularity in an infinite series of cluster functions, but only in the weak-coupling and strong-coupling limits.

## Method

The authors apply the Lee-Yang cluster expansion method to a dilute gas of two-component Fermi particles with zero-range s-wave interaction characterized by the scattering length a. They calculate the second cluster integral for arbitrary s-wave scattering length using the regularized pseudopotential and the Bethe-Peierls boundary condition, then identify a specific infinite series of cluster functions whose sum reproduces the Thouless criterion and the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink number equation in the BCS limit, and the Bose-Einstein condensation of non-interacting tightly bound dimers in the BEC limit.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the bound state for a > 0 is E_b = -hbar^2/(m a^2)
- The thermal de Broglie wavelength is defined as lambda := (2 pi hbar^2 / m k_B T)^{1/2}
- The fugacity at the superfluid phase transition z_c is greater than 10 (citing experimental work from 2010-2011)
- The cluster expansion of Tan's contact is given by C = (8 pi^2 / lambda^4) sum_{n=2}^{infinity} c_n z^n, where c_n := d b_n / d (lambda / a)

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Yang cluster expansion method (Lee and Yang, 1958-1960) beyond its usual high-temperature regime to address the BCS-BEC crossover problem. It reproduces the established results of the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink theory (1985) in the weak-coupling limit and the BEC of dimers in the strong-coupling limit. The approach provides a systematic framework that could be extended to describe the entire BCS-BEC crossover including the unitary regime, where previous perturbative methods (such as Feynman diagrammatic techniques) are unreliable.
