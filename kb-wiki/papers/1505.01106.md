# Hagedorn spectrum and thermodynamics of SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theories

> We present a high-precision lattice calculation of the equation of state in the confining phase of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory. We show that the results are described very well by a gas of massive, non-interacting glueballs, provided one assumes an exponentially growing Hagedorn spectrum. The latter can be derived within an effective bosonic closed-string model, leading to a parameter-free theoretical prediction, which is in perfect agreement with our lattice results. Furthermore, when applied to SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, this effective model accurately describes the lattice results reported by Borsányi et al. in JHEP 07 (2012) 056.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the equation of state in the confining phase of SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theories is accurately described by a gas of non-interacting massive glueballs with an exponentially growing Hagedorn spectrum derived from a closed bosonic string model, but notes that for SU(2) the Nambu-Goto string prediction for the Hagedorn temperature is slightly lower (by less than 3%) than the numerically determined critical deconfinement temperature, suggesting the effective string model may fail very close to T_c.

## Method

The authors performed high-precision lattice Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory using the standard Wilson action, computing the static interquark potential from Polyakov loop correlation functions via the multilevel algorithm to set the scale. They then computed the trace anomaly and pressure from plaquette expectation values at finite temperature on lattices with temporal extents N_t = 5, 6, and 8, and compared these results to a model of a free glueball gas where light glueball masses were taken from Teper (1998) and heavier states were described by a closed Nambu-Goto string spectral density with no free parameters. For SU(3), they applied the same model using lattice data from Borsanyi et al. (2012) and glueball masses from Meyer (2004).

## Key numbers

- String tension values at beta=2.27: sigma a^2 = 0.157(8); at beta=2.60: sigma a^2 = 0.0175(16)
- Nambu-Goto Hagedorn temperature prediction: T_H = sqrt(3 sigma/(2 pi)) approximately 0.691 sqrt(sigma)
- Critical deconfinement temperature for SU(2): T_c / sqrt(sigma) = 0.7091(36) from Lucini (2003)
- Lattice simulations used N_t = 5, 6, and 8 with spatial extents L_s/L_t between 9 and 12
- Configuration sets of 1.5 x 10^5 (for N_t=5 and 6) and 10^5 (for N_t=8) were generated

## Context

This work directly generalizes the lattice study of SU(3) Yang-Mills thermodynamics by Meyer (2009) and extends the analysis to SU(2) where the second-order deconfinement transition makes the Hagedorn temperature constraint more predictive. The results confirm and extend the findings of Borsanyi et al. (2012) for SU(3), showing that the same bosonic string model describes both theories without free parameters. The work enables future studies of how the effective string description breaks down near the deconfinement transition and provides a benchmark for holographic approaches to Yang-Mills thermodynamics.
