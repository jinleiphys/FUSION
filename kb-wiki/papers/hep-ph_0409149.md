# Vacuum polarization radiative correction to the parity violating electron scattering on heavy nuclei

> The effect of vacuum polarization on the parity violating asymmetry in the elastic electron-nucleus scattering is considered. Calculations are performed in the high-energy approximation with an exact account for the electric field of the nucleus. It is shown that the radiative correction to the parity violating asymmetry is logarithmically enhanced and the value of the correction is about -1%.

## Key claim

The vacuum polarization radiative correction to the parity violating asymmetry in elastic electron-nucleus scattering is logarithmically enhanced and has a value of about -1% in the momentum transfer range \(q = 0.2 \div 2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), but this result is obtained within the high-energy small-angle approximation using the Uehling potential and neglecting higher-order \(Z\alpha\) corrections.

## Method

The authors calculate the parity violating asymmetry using the high-energy small-angle (semiclassical) approximation for the scattering amplitude, expressed via eikonal phase shifts. They incorporate the vacuum polarization effect by replacing the proton density \(\varrho_p(r)\) with \(\varrho_p(r) + \frac{1}{Z}\varrho_{vp}(r)\) in the Coulomb amplitude, where \(\varrho_{vp}(r)\) is the charge density induced by the electron-positron vacuum polarization (Uehling potential). Numerical integrations are performed for lead (Pb, Z=82) using a three-parameter Fermi charge density for the proton distribution.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum polarization radiative correction \(\Delta\) to the parity violating asymmetry is about **-1%** for momentum transfers \(q = 0.2 \div 2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (Figure 3).
- The Born approximation estimate of the correction is \(\Delta^{(Born)} \sim -\frac{2\alpha}{3\pi} \ln\left(\frac{\lambda_C}{r_0}\right) \approx \mathbf{-0.7\%}\).
- The vacuum polarization charge inside a sphere of radius \(8\ \mathrm{fm}\) for Pb is **0.47 \(|e|\)**, corresponding to an effective nuclear charge of 82.47.
- The proton density parameters for Pb are: \(r_0 = 6.4\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(a = 0.54\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(w = 0.32\), \(\varrho_0 = 6.95 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The Fermi constant used is \(G_F = 1.16639 \times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(\sin^2\theta_W \approx 0.23\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of parity violating electron-nucleus scattering, previously studied by Horowitz (1998), Vretenar et al. (2000), and Horowitz et al. (2001) using partial wave analysis, by adding the vacuum polarization radiative correction for the first time. The correction is analogous to logarithmically enhanced radiative corrections in atomic parity violation studied by Milstein and Sushkov. The result enables more precise extraction of neutron densities from future parity violation experiments, such as the proposed measurement at 850 MeV, though the authors note that vertex and self-energy corrections (which can be -23% in Born approximation) remain an open and difficult problem for heavy nuclei.
