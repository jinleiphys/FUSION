# $ϕ$ photoprodution with coupled-channel effects

> We study phi photoproduction with various hadronic rescattering contributions included, in addition to the Pomeron and pseudoscalar meson-exchange diagrams. We find that the hadronic box diagrams can explain the recent experimental data in the vicinity of the threshold. In particular, the bump-like structure at the photon energy E_gamma \approx 2.3$ GeV is well explained by the K Lambda(1520) rescattering amplitude in the intermediate state, which is the dominant contribution among other hadronic contributions. We also find that the hadronic box diagrams are consistent with the observed spin-density matrix elements near the threshold region.

## Key claim

The authors claim that hadronic box diagrams, particularly the $K\Lambda(1520)$ rescattering amplitude, can explain the bump-like structure in $\phi$ photoproduction near $E_\gamma \approx 2.3$ GeV, contrary to the earlier conclusion of Ozaki et al. (2009) that coupled-channel effects alone were insufficient. The claim is conditional on tuning the Pomeron amplitude near threshold so that it becomes effective only at higher energies, and on computing only the imaginary parts of the box diagrams via the Landau-Cutkosky rule.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach combined with a Pomeron-exchange model, adding seven hadronic box diagrams with intermediate $\rho N$, $\omega N$, $\sigma N$, $\pi N$, $K\Lambda(1116)$, $K^*\Lambda(1116)$, and $K\Lambda(1520)$ states. Rather than computing the full box diagrams, they used the Landau-Cutkosky rule to obtain only the imaginary parts from the discontinuity across the branch cut, arguing that the real parts can be absorbed into renormalization of coupling constants. The coupling constants and cutoff masses were fixed by fitting to experimental data for the subprocesses $\gamma p \to K\Lambda(1520)$, $\gamma p \to \rho p$, and $\gamma p \to \omega p$, while the Pomeron strength and threshold parameter were tuned to reproduce high-energy behavior.

## Key numbers

* The bump-like structure appears at photon energy $E_\gamma \approx 2.3$ GeV in the differential cross section at forward angle.
* The Pomeron strength parameter $C_p = 8$ GeV$^{-1}$ and threshold parameter $s_{\mathrm{th}}$ were fitted to reproduce high-energy behavior.
* The $KN\Lambda^*$ coupling constant $g_{KN\Lambda^*} = 11$ was taken from Nam (2005).
* The $\phi NN$ coupling constant $g_{\phi NN} = 0.25$ and $\kappa_\phi = 0.2$ were taken from Meissner (1997).
* The $\phi KK$ coupling constant $g_{\phi KK} = 4.7$ was determined from the PDG decay width $\Gamma_{\phi\to KK}$.
* Cutoff masses for the $K\Lambda(1520)$ box form factors: $\Lambda_1 = 0.8$ GeV, $\Lambda_2 = 0.8$ GeV, $\Lambda_3 = 1.0$ GeV, $\Lambda_4 = 1.0$ GeV.
* Pseudoscalar meson coupling constants: $g_{\pi NN} = 13.26$, $g_{\eta NN} = 3.527$, with cutoff masses $\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 0.7$ GeV and $\Lambda_{\eta NN} = 1$ GeV.
* $\phi\gamma\pi$ and $\phi\gamma\eta$ coupling constants: $g_{\phi\gamma\pi} = -0.141$, $g_{\phi\gamma\eta} = -0.707$, with cutoffs $\Lambda_{\phi\gamma\pi} = 0.77$ GeV and $\Lambda_{\phi\gamma\eta} = 0.9$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach of Ozaki et al. (2009), who considered only the $K\Lambda(1520)$ channel within a K-matrix formalism and found it insufficient to explain the LEPS collaboration bump at $E_\gamma \approx 2.3$ GeV. The present authors include six additional hadronic box diagrams and reach the opposite conclusion, showing that the $K\Lambda(1520)$ rescattering is the dominant contribution. The work also builds on the effective Lagrangian description of $\gamma p \to K\Lambda(1520)$ by Nam (2005) and on the Pomeron-exchange model of Titov (2003, 2007). The results enable future studies of spin-density matrix elements near threshold and suggest that explicit $N^*$ resonances may not be required to explain the bump structure.
