# Microcausality in strongly interacting fields

> We study the properties of strongly interacting massive quantum fields in space-time as resulting from a parametric decay of the fields with a large decay width $γ$. The resulting imaginary part of the retarded and advanced propagators in this case is of Lorentzian form and the theory conserves microcausality, i.e. the commutator between the fields vanishes for space-like distances in space-time. However, when considering separately space-like and time-like components of the spectral function in momentum space we find microcausality to be violated for each component separately. This implies that the modeling of effective field theories for strongly interacting systems has to be considered with great care and restrictions to time-like four momenta in case of broad spectral functions have to be ruled out. Furthermore, when employing effective propagators with a width $γ({\bf p}^2)$ depending explicitly on three-momentum ${\bf p}$ the commutator of the fields no longer vanishes for $r>t$ since the related field theory becomes nonlocal and violates microcausality.

# Microcausality in strongly interacting fields

## Key claim

The spectral functions of the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) and similar effective propagators with a constant decay width conserve microcausality (the field commutator vanishes for space-like separations) *only when the full spectral function is considered*; restricting to either time-like or space-like four-momentum components separately violates microcausality, and using a three-momentum-dependent width also breaks microcausality.

## Method

The authors analytically solve the Klein-Gordon equation with a damping term proportional to the decay width γ, obtaining the retarded Green function in closed form. They then numerically evaluate the commutator integral (Eq. 4) by separating the spectral function into time-like and space-like four-momentum regions using Heaviside step functions, subtracting the singular lightcone contribution to isolate the regular part. The numerical integration is performed on a grid and validated against the analytical result for the full spectral function.

## Key numbers

- For M = 1 GeV and γ = 0.3 GeV at t = 15/GeV, the numerical evaluation of the regular part of the commutator agrees with the analytical result within the linewidth and is identically zero for r > t.
- For γ = 0.045 GeV and γ = 0.3 GeV, the full commutator vanishes outside the lightcone; the violation from separate time-like or space-like components becomes sizeable for γ > 0.1 GeV.
- In the aperiodic limit γ → M (μ → 0), the regular part of the commutator vanishes identically and only the lightcone contribution remains.
- For γ = 1.25 GeV (overdamped case, γ > M), the regular part shows no oscillations but only an exponentially decaying signal within the lightcone.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) developed by Peshier and Cassing (2000s), which uses effective propagators with finite widths to model QCD matter above the critical temperature. The DQPM has been used in transport studies (Vitalii 2013, Cassing 2013, Steinert) to compute shear and bulk viscosities close to lattice QCD results. The paper demonstrates that while the DQPM spectral functions are microcausal, common approximations such as restricting to time-like momenta or using momentum-dependent widths (as in Eq. 11) are not, which has implications for effective field theories of strongly interacting systems.
