# Competition between magnetic catalysis effect and chiral rotation effect

> In this work, we explore the competition between magnetic catalysis effect and chiral rotation effect in a general parallel electromagnetic field within the effective Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. For a given electric field $E$ at zero temperature, the mass gap shows three different features with respect to an increasing magnetic field $B$: increasing monotonically, decreasing after increasing and decreasing monotonically. By making use of strong magnetic field approximation, we illuminate that this is due to the competition between catalysis effect and chiral rotation effect induced both by the magnetic field, and a critical electric field $\sqrt{eE_c}=86.4~{\rm MeV}$ is found beyond which the mass gap will eventually decrease at large $B$. As only large magnetic field is relevant for the derivation, the critical electric field does not depend on the temperature $T$ or chemical potential $μ$.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in a parallel electromagnetic field, the mass gap (dynamical quark mass) shows three distinct behaviors with increasing magnetic field depending on the fixed electric field strength: monotonic increase, increase-then-decrease, or monotonic decrease. This competition between magnetic catalysis and chiral rotation is analytically shown to be governed by a critical electric field \(\sqrt{eE_c}=86.4\) MeV, above which the mass gap eventually decreases at large magnetic field; importantly, this critical field does not depend on temperature or chemical potential.

## Method
The authors employ the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in Euclidean space with a parallel electromagnetic field, including a neutral pion condensate via the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. They derive the thermodynamic potential and coupled gap equations for the chiral mass and pion condensate at zero temperature using Schwinger's proper-time method, and extend the formalism to finite temperature and chemical potential by introducing Matsubara frequencies and a polar-coordinate representation of the order parameters. Numerical solutions are obtained with standard NJL parameters (\(G=4.93\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\Lambda=0.653\) GeV, \(m_0=5\) MeV) fitted to pion mass, decay constant, and quark condensate.

## Key numbers
- Critical electric field: \(\sqrt{eE_c} = 86.4\) MeV, derived analytically from the large-magnetic-field limit of the gap equation.
- NJL model parameters: \(G = 4.93\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\Lambda = 0.653\) GeV, \(m_0 = 5\) MeV, fitted to \(m_\pi = 134\) MeV, \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, and \(\langle\bar\psi\psi\rangle = -2\times (0.25\) GeV\()^3\).
- Asymptotic chiral mass in pure magnetic field: \(M^2 = \frac{N_c G}{6\pi^2}(q_u^2+q_d^2)B^2\) for \(B\to\infty\).
- Subcritical electric field example: \((eE)^{1/2} = 0.08\) GeV; supercritical example: \((eE)^{1/2} = 0.10\) GeV.
- De Haas–van Alphen oscillation region where mass gap vanishes: \((eB)^{1/2} \in (0.364, 0.579)\) GeV at \(T=0.15\) GeV and \(\mu=0.2\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of triangle anomaly effects on chiral symmetry breaking in parallel electromagnetic fields (Cao 2015), where only the case \(E=B\) was considered. It also builds on the established magnetic catalysis effect (MCE) from Gusynin (1994, 1995) and Miransky (2015), and the inverse magnetic catalysis effect observed in lattice QCD (Bali 2011, 2012; Bruckmann 2013). The results enable further exploration of Schwinger pair production in heavy-ion collisions, where the magnetic field typically exceeds the electric field, and suggest that de Haas–van Alphen oscillations can greatly enhance pair production even at subcritical electric fields.
