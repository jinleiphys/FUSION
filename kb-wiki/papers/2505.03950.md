# Perturbation theory for a systematic account of the bound-state motion

> We derive a perturbation theory (PT) for the Lorentz boost operator in the space of two-nucleon wave functions. The latter is expressed in terms of the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials, developed so far in great detail for their use in the $NN$ scattering studies. The PT is designed to take into account the boost relativistic corrections in a systematic way and, as such, it is the only missing part in the corresponding approaches developed up to now in the low-energy effective field theories.

## Key claim

The paper derives a perturbation theory (PT) for the Lorentz boost operator in the space of two-nucleon wave functions, enabling systematic inclusion of relativistic boost corrections that are the only missing part in low-energy effective field theory approaches; the PT is expressed directly in terms of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials already developed for scattering studies, rather than requiring new vertices from the Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Schrodinger equation for a moving two-nucleon bound state and expand the wave function around the center-of-mass solution using the nonrelativistic relative momentum as the second three-dimensional variable. The perturbation operator Delta is defined as the difference between the full moving-frame equation and the center-of-mass equation, and the wave function is expressed as a series in powers of the Green function times Delta. A generalized form is also presented where the kinematic part of the Lorentz boost transformation is incorporated into the unperturbed equation, using the approximate boost formula previously proposed in the literature.

## Key numbers

- The v^4-order terms in the kinematic part of the perturbation Delta are (P^2 p^2 + 2(P.p)^2)/M^3 when using nonrelativistic relative momentum (counting p of order v)
- The approximate boost transformation yields the factor (1-v^2)^(1/4) relating moving and rest-frame wave functions at order v^2
- The spin-dependent term in the approximate boost operator is -i v.[(sigma_1 - sigma_2) x p]/(4m)
- The kinematic part of the generalized perturbation reduces to v^2 p^2/M + O(v^6) when the boosted relative momentum is used in the leading-order wave function

## Context

This work extends the development of systematic relativistic corrections for bound-state calculations in chiral effective field theories, particularly for approaches based on time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) and the method of unitary transformation (MUT). The PT completes the program initiated by earlier work on boost operators in the 1980s (Kvinikhidze et al., 1989) and provides the missing systematic framework for higher-order corrections that previous approximate formulas (Schiavilla, 1990; Friar, 1977) could not supply. The approach enables practitioners of chiral EFT to incorporate boost corrections to arbitrary order using the same NN potentials already employed in scattering calculations.
