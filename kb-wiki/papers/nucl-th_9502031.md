# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of $A\leq6$ Nuclei

> The energies of $^{3}H$, $^{3}He$, and $^{4}He$ ground states, the ${\frac{3}{2}}^{-}$ and ${\frac{1}{2}}^{-}$ scattering states of $^{5}He$, the ground states of $^{6}He$, $^{6}Li$, and $^{6}Be$ and the $3^{+}$ and $0^{+}$ excited states of $^{6}Li$ have been accurately calculated with the Green's function Monte Carlo method using realistic models of two- and three-nucleon interactions. The splitting of the $A=3$ isospin $T=\frac{1}{2}$ and $A=6$ isospin $T=1$, $J^π = 0^{+}$ multiplets is also studied. The observed energies and radii are generally well reproduced, however, some definite differences between theory and experiment can be identified.

## Key claim

The Green's function Monte Carlo method can accurately calculate the energies of nuclei up to A=6 using realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions, but the calculated energies for the 6N states are only upper bounds rather than converged ground-state energies, and some definite differences between theory and experiment remain.

## Method

The authors used the Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) method with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon interaction and the Urbana model IX three-nucleon interaction to calculate nuclear states for A=3 through A=6. The calculations were performed using a simplified isoscalar Hamiltonian with an eight-operator interaction (v8), with all remaining terms treated as first-order perturbations. For the A=5 resonances, the calculations were carried out with an external alpha-n potential that vanishes inside 12.5 fm and is infinitely repulsive beyond that radius, allowing extraction of phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- 3H ground state energy: -8.47(2) MeV (experimental: -8.48 MeV)
- 4He ground state energy: -28.3(1) MeV (experimental: -28.3 MeV)
- 5He 3/2- state energy: -26.5(2) MeV (experimental: -27.2 MeV)
- 5He 1/2- state energy: -25.7(2) MeV (experimental: -25.8 MeV)
- 6He ground state energy: -28.2(8) MeV (experimental: -29.3 MeV)
- 6Li 1+ ground state energy: -32.4(9) MeV (experimental: -32.0 MeV)
- 6Li 3+ excited state energy: -28.9(6) MeV (experimental: -29.8 MeV)
- 6Be ground state energy: not directly given, but the 6Be-6He energy difference is 2.5(1) MeV (experimental: 2.35 MeV)
- Calculated splitting between 5He 3/2- and 1/2- states: 0.8(3) MeV (experimental: 1.4 MeV)
- 4He rms proton radius: 1.42(1) fm (experimental: 1.47 fm)
- 6Li 1+ rms proton radius: 2.41(5) fm (experimental: 2.43 fm)

## Context

This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo calculations for A=5 and below (Carlson 1988, 1994) to the first realistic six-nucleon calculations, where previously only three-body models with an alpha core and two nucleons had been used (Schellingerhout 1993). The results enable future calculations for A=7 and A=8 nuclei using larger parallel computers, and the identified discrepancies (particularly the underestimation of the 5He spin-orbit splitting and the overestimation of the isotensor energy difference in the A=6 T=1 multiplet) provide targets for probing relativistic effects and the spin-isospin dependence of three-nucleon interactions.
