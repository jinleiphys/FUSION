# Effective field theory analysis of boson-trimer bond lengths to next-to-leading order

> Cold Helium atoms are a unique system where a single excited three-body Efimov state occurs, naturally, without the need for an external magnetic field. While three-body bound state energies of cold Helium atoms have previously been investigated, recent experimental techniques have allowed their structure to also be studied. The weak interaction between Helium atoms leads to a helium-helium (dimer) scattering length, $a$, much larger than the helium-helium effective range of interaction, $r$. This feature is exploited in a theory that systematically expands observables in powers of $r/a$, known as short range effective field theory (srEFT), which has been used successfully to investigate properties of cold atom systems. Using srEFT we investigate the average bond length of atoms in the three-body ground state and excited Efimov state of cold Helium atoms. At leading-order (next-to-leading order) in srEFT, we find the average bond length of the $^4$He trimer ground state is 8.35(33) Å (10.29(2) Å) and the average bond length of the excited $^4$He trimer Efimov state is 103(4) Å (105.3(2) Å).

## Key claim
Using short-range effective field theory (srEFT) to next-to-leading order, the authors calculate average bond lengths of the ground state and excited Efimov state of the ⁴He trimer, finding that the expansion works well for the excited state but the ground state bond length disagrees with TTY potential model predictions within errors, likely because the ground state is too deeply bound relative to the effective range scale.

## Method
The authors use srEFT, which expands observables in powers of r/a (effective range over scattering length), to calculate the trimer form factor up to NLO. They fit the three-body counterterm to the atom-dimer scattering length from the TTY potential, and determine the trimer radius from the Q² coefficient of the form factor expansion, then convert to bond length by multiplying by √3.

## Key numbers
- Ground state trimer bond length at LO: 8.35(33) Å; at NLO: 10.29(2) Å
- Excited Efimov state trimer bond length at LO: 103(4) Å; at NLO: 105.3(2) Å
- Ground state trimer binding energy at LO: 97.12(7.59) B_d; at NLO: 89.50(55) B_d
- Excited state trimer binding energy at LO: 1.723(135) B_d; at NLO: 1.736(11) B_d
- Effective range used: r = 7.51 Å, with γ = 0.01041 Å⁻¹ from the dimer binding energy of 1.312262 mK
- Expansion parameter γr ≈ 0.0781 (8% at LO, 0.6% at NLO); for ground state κ₃r = 0.58

## Context
This work extends srEFT calculations of Helium trimer properties beyond binding energies (previously calculated by Ji and Phillips (2012) to NNLO) to structural observables, specifically bond lengths. Prior potential model calculations of bond lengths exist from Roudnev and Yakovlev (2000) using the TTY potential, and experimental measurements from matter wave diffraction by Bruhl et al. (2005) and Coulomb explosion imaging by Kunitski et al. (2015). The srEFT approach provides model-independent predictions with systematic error estimates, and the techniques are adapted from earlier work on nuclear charge radii in halo-EFT by Vanasse (2015) and others.
