# Electron transport through nuclear pasta in magnetized neutron stars

> We present a simple model for electron transport in a possible layer of exotic nuclear clusters (in the so called nuclear pasta layer) between the crust and liquid core of a strongly magnetized neutron star. The electron transport there can be strongly anisotropic and gyrotropic. The anisotropy is produced by different electron effective collision frequencies along and across local symmetry axis in domains of exotic ordered nuclear clusters and by complicated effects of the magnetic field. We also calculate averaged kinetic coefficients in case local domains are freely oriented. Possible applications of the obtained results and open problems are outlined.

## Key claim
The paper presents a simple model for electron transport in the nuclear pasta layer of magnetized neutron stars, showing that transport can be strongly anisotropic and gyrotropic due to different effective electron collision frequencies along and across the local symmetry axis of exotic nuclear clusters, combined with magnetic field effects. The model assumes the existence of nuclear pasta domains with clusters of one type in each domain, and that electron mean free paths are larger than spacing between clusters but smaller than domain size.

## Method
The author solves the linearized Boltzmann equation for a strongly degenerate, ultra-relativistic electron gas using a generalized relaxation time approximation with two different effective collision frequencies: one along the symmetry axis (ν_a) and one in the symmetry plane (ν_p). The collision integral is expressed as I{δf} = -ν_a v_z u_z - ν_p v_p · u_p, where the difference between ν_a and ν_p reflects the anisotropy of electron scattering in non-spherical nuclear clusters. The resulting conductivity and resistivity tensors are derived for four possible pasta phases (rods, slabs, rod-like bubbles, and spherical bubbles) at various magnetic field orientations and strengths.

## Key numbers
- Neutron drip density: ρ_ND ∼ (4-6) × 10^11 g cm^-3
- Saturated nuclear matter density: ρ_0 ≈ 2.8 × 10^14 g cm^-3
- Crust-core interface density: ρ_cc ≈ ρ_0/2
- Nuclear pasta layer outer boundary: ρ_cm ≈ 10^14 g cm^-3
- According to model I of Oyamatsu (1993): ρ_mc = 0.973 × 10^14 g cm^-3; transition to phase II at ρ = 1.24 × 10^14 g cm^-3; to phase III at ρ = 1.37 × 10^14 g cm^-3; to phase IV at ρ = 1.42 × 10^14 g cm^-3; phase IV ends at ρ_cc = 1.43 × 10^14 g cm^-3
- In a typical 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star of radius about 12 km, the nuclear pasta layer can be about 100 m thick with mass ∼ 0.01 M_⊙

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of nuclear pasta structure by Ravenhall et al. (1983), Hashimoto (1984), Lorenz, Ravenhall and Pethick (1993), and Oyamatsu (1993), among others, who predicted exotic nuclear phases (rods, slabs, rod-like bubbles, spherical bubbles) at subnuclear densities. The paper provides a theoretical framework for calculating electron transport coefficients in these anisotropic structures, which is needed for modeling thermal and electrical conduction in neutron star mantles. The results enable future calculations of magnetic field evolution and cooling in neutron stars that may contain a nuclear pasta layer, and can be applied to interpret observations from X-ray telescopes such as Chandra.
