# Quasi parton distribution functions at NNLO: flavor non-diagonal quark contributions

> We present a next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) calculation of the quasi parton distribution functions (Quasi-PDFs) in the large momentum effective theory (LaMET). We focus on the flavor non-diagonal quark-quark channel and demonstrate the LaMET factorization at the NNLO accuracy in the modified minimal subtraction scheme. The matching coefficient between the quasi-PDF and the light-cone PDF is derived. This provides a first step towards a complete NNLO analysis of quasi-PDFs and to better understand the nucleon structures from the first principle of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first two-loop (NNLO) calculation of the matching coefficient between quasi-PDFs and light-cone PDFs for the flavor non-diagonal quark-quark channel, demonstrating that LaMET factorization holds at this order. The authors explicitly show that infrared divergences cancel between the quasi-PDF and the convolution of lower-order matching coefficients and splitting functions, which is a nontrivial check of the factorization framework.

## Method

The authors compute the two-loop quasi-PDF for flavor non-diagonal quark-quark splitting (where the incoming and outgoing quarks have different flavors) using dimensional regularization in the modified minimal subtraction (MS-bar) scheme. They evaluate the relevant Feynman diagrams in both covariant R_xi gauge and axial gauge, reduce tensor integrals to master integrals using integration-by-parts (IBP) techniques, and solve the master integrals via the method of differential equations. The matching coefficient C^{(2)}_{qq''} is extracted by subtracting the known infrared-divergent contributions from the one-loop quark-to-gluon matching coefficient convolved with the one-loop gluon-to-quark splitting function, and the two-loop collinear splitting function.

## Key numbers

* The two-loop quasi-PDF in the region 0 < y < 1 contains double and single infrared poles: (1/epsilon_IR^2) Gamma_2(y) + (1/epsilon_IR) Gamma_1(y) + (2/epsilon_IR) Gamma_2(y) log(mu^2 / p_z^2), where Gamma_2(y) = -T_F C_F (4y^3 + 3y^2 - 3y - 6(y+1)y log(y) - 4) / (6y).
* The matching coefficient C^{(2)}_{qq''} in the region y > 1 is given by an analytic expression containing terms proportional to log^2(y), log(y), log^3(y), and polylogarithms, with the leading asymptotic behavior at y -> +infinity being (4/(27 y^2)) T_F C_F [-6(mu^2/p_z^2) + 12 log(2y) - 7].
* The matching coefficient C^{(2)}_{qq''} in the region 0 < y < 1 contains double logarithms Gamma_2(y) log^2(mu^2/p_z^2) and single logarithms (Gamma_1(y) - P^{(1)}_{q/q''}(y)) log(mu^2/p_z^2), where P^{(1)}_{q/q''}(y) is the two-loop splitting function.
* The one-loop matching coefficient C^{(1)}_{qg} has asymptotic behavior at y -> +infinity of (2/(3y)) T_F [1 + 2 log(2y) epsilon].

## Context

This work extends the one-loop matching calculations for quasi-PDFs (Xiong 2013, Ishikawa 2017, Izubuchi 2018, Ji 2018) to two-loop order for the first time in the flavor non-diagonal quark channel. It builds on the LaMET framework (Ji 2013, Ji 2014) and uses known results for the two-loop splitting functions (Vogt 2004) and one-loop gluon-to-quark matching (Wang 2019). The results provide a first step toward a complete NNLO analysis of quasi-PDFs, enabling more precise extraction of sea quark contributions in both nonsinglet and singlet quark distributions from lattice QCD simulations.
