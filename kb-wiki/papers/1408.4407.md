# Stellar $β^{\pm}$ decay rates of iron isotopes and its implications in astrophysics

> $β$-decay and positron decay are believed to play a consequential role during the late phases of stellar evolution of a massive star culminating in a supernova explosion. Recently the microscopic calculation of weak-interaction mediated rates on key isotopes of iron was introduced using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory with improved model parameters. Here I discuss in detail the improved calculation of $β^{\pm}$ decay rates for iron isotopes ($^{54,55,56}$Fe) in stellar environment. The pn-QRPA theory allows a microscopic "state-by-state" calculation of stellar rates as explained later in text. Excited state Gamow-Teller distributions are much different from ground state and a microscopic calculation of decay rates from these excited states greatly increases the reliability of the total decay rate calculation specially during the late stages of stellar evolution. The reported decay rates are also compared with earlier calculations. The positron decay rates are in reasonable agreement with the large-scale shell model calculation. The main finding of this work includes that the stellar $β$-decay rates of $^{54,55,56}$Fe are around 3 -- 5 orders of magnitude smaller than previously assumed and hence irrelevant for the determination of the evolution of $Y_{e}$ during the presupernova phase of massive stars. The current work discourages the inclusion of $^{55,56}$Fe in the list of key stellar $β$-decay nuclei as suggested by former simulation results.

## Key claim

The stellar beta-decay rates of iron isotopes 54, 55, and 56Fe calculated using the improved pn-QRPA model are around 3 to 5 orders of magnitude smaller than previously assumed, making them irrelevant for determining the evolution of the lepton-to-baryon ratio (Ye) during the presupernova phase of massive stars, contrary to earlier simulation results that included 55Fe and 56Fe in lists of key beta-decay nuclei.

## Method

The author used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory with improved model parameters to perform a microscopic "state-by-state" calculation of stellar beta-decay and positron-decay rates for 54,55,56Fe. The calculation employed a Nilsson + BCS model for single-particle states, incorporated experimental deformation parameters for 54,56Fe from Raman et al. (1987) and a calculated deformation for 55Fe, and used interaction strength parameters chi = 0.15 MeV and kappa = 0.07 MeV. Excited state Gamow-Teller strength distributions were calculated for 246 parent states in 54Fe, 297 states in 55Fe, and 266 states in 56Fe, with transitions to up to 150 daughter excited states covering excitation energies up to around 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The total B(GT-) strength for 54Fe calculated with the improved pn-QRPA model was 7.56, compared to the measured value of 7.5 plus or minus 0.7 from Anderson et al. (1990); the earlier pn-QRPA calculation from Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (2004) gave 9.33.
- The stellar beta-decay rates of 54,55,56Fe are around 3 to 5 orders of magnitude smaller than previously assumed.
- The Q-values for beta decay under terrestrial conditions are -8.2430 MeV for 54Fe, -3.4520 MeV for 55Fe, and -4.5660 MeV for 56Fe, taken from Audi et al. (2003).
- The effective ratio of axial to vector coupling constants squared was taken as 0.60 times the bare value, with (gA/gV)_bare = -1.254 from Rodin et al. (2006).
- The pairing gaps were set to Delta_p = Delta_n = 12 divided by the square root of A MeV.

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of weak interaction rates previously reported by Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (1999, 2004) for nuclei in the fp-shell region, using improved model parameters and experimental deformation data. The results contradict the earlier findings of Aufderheide et al. (1994), who included 55,56Fe in the list of key beta-decay nuclei for presupernova evolution, and the simulation results of Heger et al. (2001), who found 55Fe among the top five nuclei increasing Ye via beta-decay using large-scale shell model rates. The paper enables a reassessment of which iron isotopes should be included in presupernova simulation networks, suggesting that 55,56Fe should be removed from the list of key beta-decay nuclei.
