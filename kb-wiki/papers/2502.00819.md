# Kaon Modification in Pion Medium

> Kaon properties in thermal pion-nucleon medium are studied. The dense pion-nucleon medium is produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The consideration is based on the chiral nucleon-kaon-pion Lagrangian. It is found that the pion medium does not produce a substantive contribution but enhances the effect of the baryon matter. Numerical estimate shows that this enhancement factor induced by the pion medium does not exceed 5% at the freeze-out stage of heavy-ion collisions, i.e. it is small. However, the impact of this enhancement can be higher in actual nuclear collisions because the effect of the in-medium (anti)kaon modification is accumulated during the (anti)kaon evolution before the freeze-out when the pion density is higher.

## Key claim

The pion medium does not produce a direct substantive modification of kaon properties but instead enhances the effect of the baryon medium by a factor \(F_\pi\), which at freeze-out does not exceed 5%; however, this enhancement may be larger during the earlier evolution of the collision when the pion density is higher.

## Method

The author starts from a chiral nucleon-kaon-pion Lagrangian containing s-wave kaon-nucleon interaction and a chiral \(\pi K\) Lagrangian that reproduces \(\pi K\) scattering data. Using the mean-field approximation for the nucleon and pion fields, the Klein-Gordon equation for a kaon in the combined nucleon-pion medium is derived, yielding a dispersion relation that includes an enhancement factor \(F_\pi = (1 - \langle\boldsymbol{\pi}^2\rangle/4f_\pi^2)^{-1}\). The pion expectation value \(\langle\boldsymbol{\pi}^2\rangle\) is computed from the thermal pion gas at freeze-out temperatures and baryon chemical potentials parametrized from the statistical model of Cleymans (2005) and Karsch (2010).

## Key numbers

* The enhancement factor \(F_\pi\) induced by the pion medium at freeze-out does not exceed **5%** across the range of center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) of nuclear collisions.
* The freeze-out temperature parametrization used is \(T(\mu_B) = a - b\mu_B^2 - c\mu_B^4\) with \(a = 0.166\) GeV, \(b = 0.139\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c = 0.053\) GeV\(^{-3}\).
* The baryon chemical potential parametrization is \(\mu_B(\sqrt{s_{NN}}) = d/(1 + e\sqrt{s_{NN}})\) with \(d = 1.308\) GeV and \(e = 0.273\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV; the kaon-nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_{KN}\) is taken as 350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of kaon modification in baryon matter, originally motivated by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) regarding kaon condensation in dense matter, by adding the effect of a thermal pion medium. It contrasts with Faessler (2002), who considered kaons in a purely pionic medium and found mainly kaon broadening, and with Kolomeitsev (1995), who studied the influence of a Bose-Einstein pion condensate with nonzero chemical potential. The result suggests that while the direct pionic effect at freeze-out is small (under 5%), it may be more significant in dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions where the pion density is higher before freeze-out, enabling more accurate modeling of (anti)kaon spectra and flow.
