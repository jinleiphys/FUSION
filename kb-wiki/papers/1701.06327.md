# Superheavy toroidal nuclei in the Skyrme energy functional framework

> Using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) self-consistent mean-field theory with the SkM* Skyrme energy-density functional, we study nuclear structure properties of even-even superheavy nuclei (SHN) of $Z=120$ isotopes and $N=184$ isotones. The shape of the nucleus along the lowest energy curve as a function of the quadrupole moment $Q_{20}$ makes a sudden transition from the oblate spheroids (biconcave discs) to the toroidal shapes, in the region of large oblate quadrupole moments.

## Key claim

Using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) self-consistent mean-field theory with the SkM* Skyrme energy-density functional, the authors find a sudden shape transition from oblate spheroids (biconcave discs) to toroidal shapes for even-even superheavy nuclei of Z=120 isotopes and N=184 isotones in the region of large oblate quadrupole moments, but these toroidal configurations are unstable against returning to a sphere-like geometry.

## Method

The constrained Skyrme-HFB approach was used with an augmented Lagrangian method and the symmetry-unrestricted code HFODD, employing a three-dimensional Cartesian deformed harmonic oscillator basis with up to N=26 quanta and not more than 1140 states. In the particle-hole channel the Skyrme SkM* force was applied, and a density-dependent mixed pairing interaction was used in the particle-particle channel. Total HFB energies were calculated as a function of the quadrupole moment Q20 for chains of even-even Z=120 isotopes (neutron numbers 166 to 190) and N=184 isotones (proton numbers 106 to 124).

## Key numbers

- For 304120184, the biconcave disc solutions end at Q20(A) = -202 b and toroidal solutions start at Q20(B) = -158 b; the nuclear density at the toroidal geometrical center is very small at point B but remains about 0.08/fm3 at point A.
- For Z=120 isotopes, the first toroidal solution appears at Q20 = -150 b for N=166 and at Q20 = -160 b for N=190.
- For N=184 isotones, the energy crossing points of biconcave disc and toroidal energy curves occur at Q20 approximately -180 b for all atomic numbers studied.
- The toroidal solution emerges when the oblate Q20 magnitude exceeds 158 b for 304120184.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of toroidal nuclear density distributions at oblate deformation Q20 ≤ -200 b for hypothetical superheavy nuclei 316122, 340130, 352134, and 364138 using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS model (Staszczak and Wong, 2008). It also builds on the liquid drop model and shell correction studies of toroidal nuclei by Wong (1973, 1978) and on earlier investigations of super-deformed-oblate superheavy nuclei at quadrupole moments Q20 = -60 to -55 b (Jachimowicz et al., 2011; Heenen et al., 2015). The results enable future searches for toroidal figures of equilibrium in the superheavy region with Z ≥ 122, where shell effects may stabilize toroidal shapes, and connect to recent findings of toroidal high-spin isomers in both light mass regions and in 304120184 with spin I = Iz = 81 and 208 ħ (Staszczak et al., 2017).
