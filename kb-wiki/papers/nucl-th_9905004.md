# Q^2-Dependence of the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov integral

> The energy and four-momentum (Q^2) dependence of the photo-absorption cross section on the proton is calculated for helicity -1/2 and -3/2 states. An effective Lagrangian model is used, formulated in terms of meson and baryon degrees of freedom, which obeys crossing symmetry, unitarity, Lorentz and gauge invariance. The difference in the cross sections for the two helicity states, the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov integral $I_{DHG}(Q^2)$, is evaluated at different $Q^2$. We find that at small momentum transfer the absolute value of $I_{DHG}(Q^2)$ first increases to reach a maximum at $Q^2 \approx 0.05 GeV^2$ before decreasing at higher $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The absolute value of the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov integral \(I_{DHG}(Q^2)\) for the proton first increases with momentum transfer to a maximum at \(Q^2 \approx 0.05\) GeV\(^2\) before decreasing at higher \(Q^2\), a behavior that is opposite to the dependence obtained in chiral perturbation theory calculations (Bernard et al., 1993).

## Method

The authors used a relativistic effective-Lagrangian model based on a K-matrix approach, formulated in terms of meson and baryon degrees of freedom, which obeys crossing symmetry, unitarity, Lorentz and gauge invariance. The kernel was constructed from direct (s), exchange (u), and meson exchange (t-channel) tree-level amplitudes, including all spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 baryon resonances with masses below 1.7 GeV. Coupling parameters were obtained from a simultaneous fit to pion-nucleon phase shifts, pion-photoproduction multipoles, and Compton scattering cross sections, with the \(\Delta\)-resonance vertex treated using a vector-meson dominance form factor.

## Key numbers

* The absolute value of \(I_{DHG}(Q^2)\) reaches a maximum at \(Q^2 \approx 0.05\) GeV\(^2\).
* At \(Q^2 = 0\), the DHG sumrule value is \(I_{DHG}(0) = -\frac{1}{4}\kappa^2\), where \(\kappa\) is the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon.
* At \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), the multiple-pion emission contribution to \(I_{DHG}(Q^2)\) increases in relative importance to 23%.
* At small \(Q^2\), single-pion production contributes 86% to the integral, compared to 76% found by Karliner (1973) at the real-photon point.
* At \(Q^2 = 10.7\) GeV\(^2\), the experimental moment \(\Gamma_1^p \approx 0.126\), while the Ellis-Jaffe prediction is \(\Gamma_1^p = 0.185\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the DHG sumrule by calculating its full \(Q^2\) dependence in a model that respects fundamental symmetries, contrasting with chiral perturbation theory calculations (Bernard et al., 1993) which give an opposite behavior at low \(Q^2\). The results bridge the non-perturbative regime at \(Q^2=0\), where the DHG sumrule applies (Drell and Hearn, 1966; Gerasimov, 1966), toward the perturbative QCD regime at high \(Q^2\) described by the Ellis-Jaffe sumrule (Ellis and Jaffe, 1974). The model enables future studies of the role of resonance couplings, particularly the \(g_3\) coupling in the \(\gamma N \Delta\) vertex, in determining the sign change of the integral at large \(Q^2\).
