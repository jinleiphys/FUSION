# Bayesian-Wilson coefficients in lattice QCD computations of valence PDFs and GPDs

> We propose an analysis method for the leading-twist operator product expansion based lattice QCD determinations of the valence parton distribution function (PDF). In the first step, we determine the confidence-intervals of the leading-twist $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ Wilson coefficients, $C_n(μ^2 z^2)$, of the equal-time bilocal quark bilinear, given the lattice QCD matrix element of Ioffe-time distribution for a particular hadron $H$ as well as the prior knowledge of the valence PDF, $f(x,μ)$ of the hadron $H$ determined via global fit from the experimental data. In the next step, we apply the numerically estimated $C_n$ in the lattice QCD determinations of the valence PDFs of other hadrons, and for the zero-skewness generalized parton distribution (GPD) of the same hadron $H$ at non-zero momentum transfers. Our proposal still assumes the dominance of leading-twist terms, but it offers a pragmatic alternative to the usage of perturbative Wilson coefficients and their associated higher-loop uncertainties such as the effect of all-order logarithms at larger sub-Fermi quark-antiquark separations $z$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that lattice QCD determinations of valence PDFs and GPDs can be improved by replacing perturbatively computed Wilson coefficients with probabilistically determined coefficients extracted from lattice data combined with phenomenological PDF knowledge, though the method still assumes leading-twist dominance and is presented as a pragmatic alternative rather than a definitive solution to higher-loop uncertainties.

## Method

The authors develop a two-step Bayesian analysis framework: first, they determine confidence intervals for the leading-twist MSbar Wilson coefficients C_n(mu^2 z^2) by fitting lattice QCD matrix elements of the Ioffe-time distribution for a hadron H, using the known valence PDF of that hadron from global fits as fixed input. Second, they apply these numerically estimated Wilson coefficients to extract valence PDFs of other hadrons and zero-skewness GPDs of the same hadron at non-zero momentum transfers, marginalizing over the distribution of Wilson coefficients obtained in the first step. The method is demonstrated using mock-data generated with known corrections to NLO Wilson coefficients and higher-twist effects, as well as real published lattice data for pion and proton.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing a = 0.06 fm with extent L_z = 48 in the z-direction used for mock-data generation
* Mock-data generated for z in [a, 12a] and P_z aL_z/(2pi) in [1, 5], giving 60 data points
* Pion mass range m_pi in [172, 415] MeV cited as showing weak pion mass dependence in prior lattice studies
* Higher-twist effect estimated as approximately 40 MeV effect compared to QCD scale Lambda_QCD approximately 300 MeV
* Diagonal error model for mock-data: sigma_{z,om} = r |om z| with r = 0.0005 for pion and 0.0008 for proton
* Correlation model in mock-data covariance matrix: Sigma_{[z,om],[z',om']} = sigma_{z,om} sigma_{z',om'} / (1 + k |om-om'|^delta) with delta = 0.5 and k = 0.5

## Context

This work extends the perturbative matching framework for lattice QCD calculations of PDFs, specifically the pseudo-PDF approach of Radyushkin (2017) and Orginos (2017), and the operator product expansion formulation of Braun (2007) and Izubuchi (2018). It contrasts with previous approaches that use perturbatively computed Wilson coefficients at NLO or NNLO (Chen 2020, Li 2020) or that include lattice data as input to global analyses (Bringewatt 2020, Del Debbio 2020, Cichy 2019, Lin 2020). The method enables disentangling perturbative QCD systematics from nonperturbative lattice systematics in parton structure calculations, and can be applied to zero-skewness GPDs (Liu 2019, Radyushkin 2019) and helicity PDFs using the same Wilson coefficients.
