# Pseudorapidity distributions of charged particles in pp($\rm\overline{p}$), p(d)A and AA collisions using Tsallis thermodynamics

> The pseudorapidity distributions of charged particles measured in p+p($\rm \overline{p}$) collisions for energies ranging from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=23.6$ GeV to 13 TeV, d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 5.02$ TeV and A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC are investigated in the fireball model with Tsallis thermodynamics. We assume that the rapidity axis is populated with fireballs following q-Gaussian distribution and the charged particles follow the Tsallis distribution in the fireball. The theoretical results are in good agreement with the experimental data for all the collision systems and centralities investigated. The collision energy and centrality dependence of the central position $y_0$ and its width $σ$ of the fireball distribution are also investigated. A possible application of the model to predict the charged particle pseudorapidity distributions for the system size scan program proposed recently for the STAR experiment at RHIC is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the fireball model with Tsallis thermodynamics, using a q-Gaussian distribution of fireballs along the rapidity axis, can simultaneously describe charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions across a wide range of collision systems (pp, pA, AA), energies (from 23.6 GeV to 13 TeV), and centralities, but the model parameters (central position y0 and width sigma) show different energy and centrality dependencies for pp compared to AA collisions, and the linear behavior observed for AA collisions at RHIC energies does not hold at LHC energies for the width sigma.

## Method

The authors model the rapidity axis as populated by fireballs whose distribution is a sum of two q-Gaussian functions (symmetric for AA, asymmetric with a relative weight for pA). Each fireball emits charged particles according to the Tsallis distribution in transverse momentum and rapidity, with the chemical potential set to zero and the Tsallis parameters q and T fixed by fits to mid-rapidity transverse momentum spectra (with a pT cut at 7.5 GeV/c for LHC systems). The pseudorapidity distribution is obtained by numerically integrating the Tsallis distribution over pT and over the fireball rapidity distribution, converting rapidity to pseudorapidity via the Jacobian relation.

## Key numbers

- pp collisions: data from sqrt(s_NN) = 23.6 GeV up to 13 TeV; a valley at eta=0 develops and its depth increases with collision energy.
- d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV; p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for eight centrality bins.
- Cu+Cu at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV; Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV; Xe+Xe at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV; Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV.
- For AA collisions, the total charged particle multiplicity per participant pair versus sqrt(s_NN) is described by a power-law function from Abbas (2013); the fitting power p decreases from central to peripheral collisions.
- For the most central AA collisions, y0 and sigma show a linear dependence on ln(sqrt(s_NN)) up to 2.76 TeV; for sigma, the linear fit is only done up to 2.76 TeV.
- For centrality dependence in AA collisions: y0 shows a linear relation with centrality (c) for energies up to 2.76 TeV; e.g., for Au+Au at 200 GeV: y0 = 0.22c + 1.76; sigma = 0.63c + 1.46.
- For Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV and Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV, sigma shows a U-shaped dependence on centrality rather than linear.

## Context

This work extends the fireball model with Tsallis thermodynamics, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions by Marques (2015) and Gao (2017), to a broader set of systems including pp, pA, and AA collisions across a wider energy range. It also extends the model to asymmetric collision systems (p+Pb, d+Au) by introducing separate central positions and widths for the two fireball q-Gaussians. The results enable predictions for the system size scan program proposed for the STAR experiment at RHIC, and the observed linear trends of model parameters with energy and centrality could be used to interpolate or extrapolate pseudorapidity distributions for unmeasured collision systems.
