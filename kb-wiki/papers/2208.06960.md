# $X(3872)$ and $T_{cc}$: structures and productions in heavy ion collisions

> We argue why the recently observed $T_{cc}$ could either be a compact multiquark configuration or a loosely bound molecular configuration composed of charmed mesons, whereas the $X(3872)$ is most likely a molecular configuration. The argument is based on different short range interactions for these tetraquark states coming from the color-color and color-spin interaction in a quark model, and the presence of a common strong D-wave mixing at larger distance similar to the deuteron case, which for the molecular configurations lead to large sizes. Such an analogy at large distance allows us to calculate the transverse momentum dependence of the loosely bound molecular configuration of tetraquarks produced in heavy ion collisions using the coalescence model that successfully reproduces the deutron data using the proton spectra. The ratio of the integrated $X(3872)$ yield obtained from our method to the $ψ(2S)$ yield obtained from statistical hadronization model method is calculated to be $0.806 \pm 0.234$, which is a factor of 2.47 larger than that obtained by using statistical model predictions for both particles and in line with the data from the CMS experiment. As the previously calculated transverse momentum distribution of the $T_{cc}$ assuming the structure to be a compact multiquark configuration is markedly different, experimental measurements of the transverse distribution of the tetraquark states will discriminate between their two possible structures.

# X(3872) and Tcc: Structures and Productions in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The X(3872) is most likely a molecular configuration of D and D* mesons, while the Tcc could be either a compact multiquark configuration or a loosely bound molecular configuration of D and D* mesons, depending on the balance between short-range attraction and D-wave mixing from pion exchange. The transverse momentum distributions of these tetraquark states produced in heavy ion collisions will be markedly different depending on their internal structure, allowing experimental discrimination.

## Method

The authors used a constituent quark model with color-color and color-spin interactions to estimate short-range attractions for the X(3872) and Tcc tetraquark states, comparing the color-spin factors after subtracting threshold contributions. They then employed a pion-exchange tensor potential to model D-wave mixing at larger distances, similar to the deuteron case, and solved for the molecular wave functions and binding energies. For production in heavy ion collisions, they applied a two-dimensional coalescence model with Gaussian Wigner functions, using measured proton, D meson, and D* meson transverse momentum distributions as inputs, and fitted the model parameters to reproduce deuteron and helium-3 data from ALICE before applying the same formalism to the tetraquark states.

## Key numbers

- The color-spin attraction for X(3872) is estimated at approximately 17.4 MeV, insufficient to confine it into a compact configuration given that the kinetic energy needed to bring two mesons to 0.3 fm separation is larger than 200 MeV
- The color-spin attraction for Tcc from the first diagonal component is approximately 104.4 MeV, much larger than the 17.4 MeV for X(3872)
- The molecular binding energy of X(3872) is 0.33 MeV with a radius of 3.0 fm; for Tcc the binding energy is 0.65 MeV with a radius of 2.2 fm; for comparison the deuteron has binding energy 2.2 MeV and radius 1.9 fm
- The ratio of the integrated X(3872) yield from the coalescence model to the psi(2S) yield from the statistical hadronization model is 0.806 ± 0.234, which is a factor of 2.47 larger than using statistical model predictions for both particles
- The total yield of the molecular DD* configuration is (2.45 ± 0.71) × 10^{-3} per unit rapidity in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV at 0-10% centrality
- The compact multiquark configuration yield is 6.2 × 10^{-4} per unit rapidity under the same conditions

## Context

This work extends the ExHIC collaboration framework (ExHIC 2017, ExHIC 2010, ExHIC 2011) for discriminating molecular from compact multiquark states via production rates in heavy ion collisions, and builds on the pion-exchange potential formalism for tetraquarks developed by Tornqvist (1993, 2004). The authors compare their results to the statistical hadronization model with charm (SHMc) by Andronic (2021) and to the CMS measurement of X(3872) production in heavy ion collisions (CMS 2021). The analysis enables future experimental discrimination of tetraquark structures through high-statistics measurements at lower transverse momentum than currently available from LHC heavy ion experiments.
