# The thermodynamics of heavy light hadrons at freezeout

> In the discussion of hadronization at or close to the freeze-out curve statistical (hadron resonance gas) models play an important role. In particular, in the charmonium sector, regeneration models are considered which rely on the fact that charmonium states can form again already at temperatures well above the QCD transition or hadronization temperature. An important ingredient in these considerations is the regeneration or hadronization of open charm states. In this talk we report on a lattice QCD analysis of correlations of open strange and charm with other conserved quantum numbers like the net baryon number and electric charge. We analyze the temperature range in which an uncorrelated hadron resonance gas (HRG) provides an adequate description of such correlations. This limits the range of validity of HRG based thermodynamics in open flavor channels and provides an estimate for the melting temperature of heavy-light hadrons.

## Key claim

Open charm mesons and baryons melt at the chiral crossover temperature, not at a higher temperature, and this conclusion is independent of the details of the hadron spectrum and lattice cutoff effects.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on 2+1 flavor Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) configurations with a quenched charm quark, using two lattice sizes (spatial extent Ns=32,24 and temporal extent Nt=8,6). They computed correlations between conserved quantum numbers (net baryon number, charm, and electric charge) and their fluctuations up to fourth order at zero chemical potential, then inverted these susceptibilities to extract partial pressures of charmed meson and baryon sectors separately.

## Key numbers

* The chiral crossover temperature is Tc = 154(9) MeV in the continuum limit for physical quarks.
* Susceptibilities were analyzed in the temperature range 156-330 MeV.
* 6000 stochastic sources were used for charm sector fluctuations and 1500 for the light sector; about 16000 configurations were used at the lowest temperature and 1600-6000 at high temperatures.
* The light quark mass was set such that m_pi = 160 MeV in the continuum.
* The departure from unity in the ratio probes for open charm mesons and baryons occurs rapidly beyond approximately 160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD analysis of melting temperatures from the strange sector, where open strange hadrons were shown to melt at Tc (Sharma and collaborators, 2014; other independent analysis, 2014), to the charm sector. It provides a first-principles estimate of the melting temperature for open charm hadrons independent of the hadron content, which is an important input for regeneration models of charmonium states at LHC freezeout temperatures (PBM, 2014). The analysis also enables a comparison between the hadron resonance gas model using only experimentally known states (PDG) versus one including additional charmed baryon states predicted by Quark Model calculations (Isgur, 1980s; Ebert, 2000s) and lattice spectrum studies (Edwards, 2010s), finding support for the existence of these additional states.
