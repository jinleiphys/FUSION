# Low-momentum relativistic nucleon-nucleon potentials I: Nuclear matter

> A series of relativistic one-boson-exchange potentials for two-nucleon system, denoted as OBEP$Λ$, is constructed with a momentum cutoff $Λ$ ranging from $\infty$ to 2 fm$^{-1}$. These potentials are developed by simultaneous fitting to nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) scattering phase shifts, low-energy scattering length, effective range, and the binding energy of the deuteron. The momentum-space matrix elements of the low-momentum OBEP$Λ$ ($Λ\leqslant 3$ fm$^{-1}$) demonstrate consistency with the universal behaviors observed in other realistic $NN$ potentials evolved by renormalization group methods. These OBEP$Λ$s are applied to calculate the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) within either the nonrelativistic (NR) Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) or relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) frameworks. The results show that the saturation properties of SNM are reproduced qualitatively from the RBHF calculation, but not from the NR-BHF calculation. This study highlights the relativistic mechanism in explaining the saturation properties of nuclear matter. The remaining discrepancy in reproducing empirical saturation properties in the RBHF calculation using the OBEP$Λ$s signals the necessity of including three-nucleon correlations or genuine three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

The authors construct a series of relativistic one-boson-exchange potentials (OBEPΛ) with explicit momentum cutoffs Λ from infinity down to 2 fm⁻¹, and show that when used in relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations, these potentials qualitatively reproduce the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, whereas nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (NR-BHF) calculations with the same potentials fail to do so. However, even the RBHF results still exhibit a remaining discrepancy with empirical saturation properties, signaling the necessity of including three-nucleon correlations or genuine three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors fit a series of relativistic one-boson-exchange potentials (OBEPΛ) to nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts (partial waves j ≤ 4, laboratory energy up to 300 MeV), low-energy scattering lengths and effective ranges in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels, and the deuteron binding energy, using a momentum cutoff Λ ranging from infinity down to 2 fm⁻¹. The potentials are then applied to calculate the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter within both the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (NR-BHF) and relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) frameworks, using the subtracted T-matrix scheme to avoid unphysically large self-energies from the one-pion-exchange potential.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy fitted to E_d^(expt) = 2.2246 MeV
- ¹S₀ scattering length fitted to a_(¹S₀)^(expt) = −23.75 fm; ³S₁ scattering length fitted to a_(³S₁)^(expt) = 5.42 fm
- ¹S₀ effective range fitted to r_(¹S₀)^(expt) = 2.75 fm; ³S₁ effective range fitted to r_(³S₁)^(expt) = 1.76 fm
- Deuteron matter radius r_d = 1.967 fm for Λ = ∞, 2, 3, 4, and 5 fm⁻¹ (experimental value 1.975 fm)
- Deuteron D-state probability P_D decreases from 5.498% at Λ = ∞ to 4.246% at Λ = 2 fm⁻¹
- Empirical saturation region quoted as density n = 0.164 ± 0.007 fm⁻³ and energy per nucleon E/A = −15.86 ± 0.57 MeV (from Drischler et al., 2019)

## Context

This work extends the Bonn potential approach (Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990) by introducing explicit momentum cutoffs, analogous to the resolution-scale dependence developed in chiral effective field theory potentials (Epelbaum, 2009; Machleidt and Entem, 2011) and renormalization group evolved potentials (Bogner, 2007, 2010). The results confirm the earlier finding that RBHF with Bonn-type potentials nearly reproduces nuclear matter saturation without explicit three-nucleon forces (Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990; Gross-Boelting et al., 1999), while NR-BHF calculations require three-nucleon forces for saturation (Day, 1981; Bogner, 2005). The remaining discrepancy in RBHF results motivates future inclusion of three-nucleon correlations or genuine three-nucleon forces in the relativistic framework.
