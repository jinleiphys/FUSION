# Phase structure of two-color QCD at real and imaginary chemical potentials; lattice simulations and model analyses

> We investigate the phase structure of two-color QCD at both real and imaginary chemical potentials mu, performing lattice simulations and analyzing the data with the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. Lattice QCD simulations are done on an 8^3 times 4 lattice with the clover-improved two-flavor Wilson fermion action and the renormalization-group improved Iwasaki gauge action. We test the analytic continuation of physical quantities from imaginary mu to real mu by comparing lattice QCD results calculated at real mu with the result of analytic function the coefficients of which are determined from lattice QCD results at imaginary mu. We also test the validity of the PNJL model by comparing model results with lattice QCD ones. The PNJL model is good in the deconfinement region, but less accurate in the transition and confinement regions. This problem is improved by introducing the baryon degree of freedom to the model. It is also found that the vector-type four-quark interaction is necessary to explain lattice data on the quark number density.

## Key claim

The Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model reproduces lattice two-color QCD data well in the deconfinement region, but is less accurate in the transition and confinement regions; this accuracy is improved by introducing baryon degrees of freedom, and the vector-type four-quark interaction is necessary to explain lattice data on the quark number density.

## Method

Lattice simulations were performed on an 8^3 x 4 lattice using the clover-improved two-flavor Wilson fermion action and the renormalization-group improved Iwasaki gauge action. The authors tested analytic continuation of physical quantities from imaginary chemical potential to real chemical potential by comparing lattice results at real mu with analytic functions whose coefficients were determined from lattice data at imaginary mu, using polynomial series in the deconfinement phase and Fourier series in the confinement phase. They also compared lattice results with PNJL model calculations to test the model's validity.

## Key numbers

- The pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential is T_c0 = 146 MeV.
- The ratio m_ps/m_v is 0.8 at T=0, with lattice spacing a about 1.38 to 1.96 GeV^{-1}.
- The RW transition temperature T_RW is located in the range T = 1.12 to 1.14 T_c0.
- The PNJL model parameters include G_0 = 4.6 GeV^{-2}, G_8,0 = 60 GeV^{-8}, G_v/G_0 = 0.15, Lambda = 700 MeV, m = 110 MeV, and alpha = 0.2.
- At beta = 0.75 (T/T_c0 = 1.18), the polynomial series up to hat{mu}^3 reproduces lattice data in the range 0 <= hat{mu}^2 <= (1.2)^2.
- At beta = 0.65 (T/T_c0 = 0.96), Fourier series fail to reproduce lattice data at real mu when hat{mu}^2 >= 0.4.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-color QCD phase structure by Hands et al. (2006) and Cotter et al. (2013) using Wilson fermions, and tests of analytic continuation by Cea et al. (2012) using staggered fermions. The results enable improved determination of vector-type four-quark interaction strength from lattice data, which is relevant for neutron star physics as discussed by Sasaki et al. (2011), and validate the imaginary chemical potential matching approach for effective model parameter determination proposed by Kashiwa et al. (2009).
