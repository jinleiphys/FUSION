# $ϕ(2170)$ decaying to $ϕππ$ and $ϕK\bar{K}$

> Within the framework of dispersion theory, we study the the processes $e^+e^-\to ϕ(2170) \to ϕππ(K\bar{K})$. The strong pion-pion final-state interactions, especially the $K\bar{K}$ coupled channel in the $S$-wave, are taken into account in a model-independent way using the Omnès function solution. Through fitting the experimental data of the $ππ$ and $ϕπ$ invariant mass distributions of the $e^+e^- \to ϕ(2170) \to ϕπ^+π^-$ process, the low-energy constants in the chiral Lagrangian are determined. The theoretical prediction for the cross sections ratio ${σ(e^+e^- \to ϕ(2170)\to ϕK^+ K^-)}/{σ(e^+e^- \to ϕ(2170)\to ϕπ^+π^-)}$ is given, which could be useful for selecting the physical solution when the fit to the $e^+e^- \to ϕK^+ K^-$ cross section distribution is available in the future. Our results suggest that above the kinematical threshold of $ϕK\bar{K}$, the mechanism $e^+e^- \to ϕK^+ K^-$ with the kaons rescattering to a pion pair plays an important role in the $e^+e^- \to ϕπ^+π^-$ transition.

## Key claim

Using dispersion theory with coupled-channel final-state interactions, the author shows that the mechanism $e^+e^- \to \phi K^+ K^-$ with kaons rescattering to a pion pair provides an important contribution to $e^+e^- \to \phi\pi^+\pi^-$ above the $\phi K\bar{K}$ threshold, but the fit quality ($\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f}=4.83$) indicates that the leading-order chiral Lagrangian alone does not fully describe the data.

## Method

The author constructs chiral effective Lagrangians for $\phi(2170)\phi PP$ contact couplings (where $P$ denotes $\pi$ or $K$), then computes partial-wave helicity amplitudes for $\phi(2170) \to \phi\pi\pi$ and $\phi(2170) \to \phi K\bar{K}$. Strong final-state interactions in the $S$-wave are treated model-independently via a two-channel ($\pi\pi$ and $K\bar{K}$) Omnès function solution, while $D$-wave FSI uses a single-channel Omnès function. The chiral contact terms serve as subtraction functions in the dispersive integrals. The free parameters (two coupling constant combinations and two normalization factors) are determined by fitting simultaneously to the BESIII $\pi\pi$ and $\phi\pi$ invariant mass distributions of $e^+e^- \to \phi\pi^+\pi^-$ at center-of-mass energy $E=2.125$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- Fitted coupling constants: $(g_1-\sqrt{2}g_8) = (-0.385 \pm 0.005)$, $(h_1-\sqrt{2}h_8) = (3.120 \pm 0.029)$, with $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f} = 1583.9/(332-4) = 4.83$
- Predicted cross section ratio at $\sqrt{s}=2.125$ GeV: $\sigma(e^+e^- \to \phi(2170)\to \phi K^+ K^-) / \sigma(e^+e^- \to \phi(2170)\to \phi\pi^+\pi^-) = 0.12 \pm 0.01$
- Experimental cross section ratio at same energy: $\sigma(e^+e^- \to \phi K^+ K^-) / \sigma(e^+e^- \to \phi\pi^+\pi^-) = 0.16 \pm 0.02$, using $\sigma(e^+e^- \to \phi K^+ K^-) = (70.6 \pm 7.2 \pm 4.9)$ pb and $\sigma(e^+e^- \to \phi\pi^+\pi^-) = (436.2 \pm 6.4 \pm 30.1)$ pb
- Predicted ratio $\sigma(e^+e^- \to \phi(2170) \to \phi \pi^0\pi^0) / \sigma(e^+e^- \to \phi(2170) \to \phi \pi^+\pi^-) = 0.51 \pm 0.02$, consistent with experimental ratio $0.54 \pm 0.6$
- $\phi(2170)$ mass and width from PDG: $M = 2188 \pm 10$ MeV, $\Gamma = 83 \pm 12$ MeV; author uses $M=2125$ MeV (BESIII center-of-mass energy) and $\Gamma=100$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive treatment of final-state interactions previously applied to similar processes (Moussallam 2000, Leutwyler 2012, Chen 2019) to the $\phi(2170)$ system, which is the strange analogue of the $Y(4230)$ charmoniumlike state. The $\phi(2170)$ was first observed by BaBar (2006) and later confirmed by BESII, Belle, and BESIII; its nature remains controversial with proposed interpretations including hybrid, tetraquark, molecule, and dynamically generated states. The predicted cross section ratio provides a tool to resolve the multi-solution problem in extracting $\phi(2170)$ parameters from $e^+e^- \to \phi\pi^+\pi^-$ data, as previously noted by Shen (2009). Future fits to $e^+e^- \to \phi K^+ K^-$ cross section data will allow selection of the physical solution.
