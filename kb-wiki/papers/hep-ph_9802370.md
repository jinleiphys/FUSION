# Real-time Relaxation and Kinetics in Hot Scalar QED: Landau Damping

> The real time evolution of field condensates with soft length scales k^{-1}>(eT)^{-1} is solved in hot scalar electrodynamics, with a view towards understanding relaxational phenomena in the QGP and the electroweak plasma. We find that transverse gauge invariant non-equilibrium expectation values of fields relax via {\em power laws} to asymptotic amplitudes that are determined by the quasiparticle poles. The long time relaxational dynamics and relevant time scales are determined by the behaviour of the retarded self-energy not at the small frequencies, but at the Landau damping thresholds. This explains the presence of power laws and not of exponential decay. Furthermore, we derive the influence functional, the Langevin equation and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem for the soft modes, identifying the correlation functions that emerge in the classical limit. We show that a Markovian approximation fails to describe the dynamics {\em both} at short and long times. We also introduce a novel kinetic approach that goes beyond the standard Boltzmann equation and incorporates off-shell processes and find that the distribution function for soft quasiparticles relaxes with a power law through Landau damping. We also find an unusual dressing dynamics of bare particles and anomalous (logarithmic) relaxation of hard quasiparticles.

## Key claim

The paper claims that long-time relaxation of soft gauge-field condensates in hot scalar QED proceeds via power laws rather than exponential decay, because the dominant contribution comes from branch-point singularities at the Landau damping thresholds (ω = ±k) rather than from the small-frequency region of the spectral function. This result contradicts the common assumption that the small-frequency region dominates long-time dynamics.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized equations of motion for non-equilibrium expectation values of transverse and longitudinal gauge fields as an initial-value problem, using Laplace transforms and the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism. The self-energies are computed to one-loop order in the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation, and the analytic structure of the retarded propagator in the complex s-plane is analyzed to extract the real-time behavior. The influence functional, Langevin equation, and fluctuation-dissipation theorem are derived for the soft modes, and a novel kinetic equation incorporating off-shell Landau damping processes is introduced.

## Key numbers

- The tadpole contribution to the transverse self-energy gives a hard thermal loop mass of approximately e²T²/6.
- The soft length scales considered are k⁻¹ > (eT)⁻¹, meaning momenta k ≪ eT.
- The long-time relaxation of the condensate is governed by branch-point singularities at ω = ±k (the Landau damping thresholds), not by the ω = 0 region.
- The relaxation exhibits power-law tails in time, not exponential decay, as a consequence of the Landau damping thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the study of real-time relaxation in scalar field theory (Inhomogeneous scalar field condensates, 1997) to the gauge theory case of scalar QED, which shares the same HTL structure as non-Abelian theories to leading order. The results contradict the assumption that small-frequency spectral functions dominate long-time dynamics, a point relevant to recent arguments about damping effects on sphaleron rates (Yaffe, 1996; Son, 1997). The paper enables a consistent microscopic description of dissipative off-shell processes in gauge theories that can serve as a benchmark for lattice simulations of real-time correlation functions, and it provides a kinetic framework beyond the standard Boltzmann equation that incorporates Landau damping.
