# Exploring New Small System Geometries in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Relativistic heavy ion collisions produce nuclei-sized droplets of quark-gluon plasma whose expansion is well described by viscous hydrodynamic calculations. Over the past half decade, this formalism was also found to apply to smaller droplets closer to the size of individual nucleons, as produced in $p$$+$$p$ and $p$$+$$A$ collisions. The hydrodynamic paradigm was further tested with a variety of collision species, including $p$$+$Au, $d$$+$Au, and $^{3}$He$+$Au producing droplets with different geometries. Nevertheless, questions remain regarding the importance of pre-hydrodynamic evolution and the exact medium properties during the hydrodynamic evolution phase, as well as the applicability of alternative theories that argue the agreement with hydrodynamics is accidental. In this work we explore options for new collision geometries including $p$$+$O and O$+$O proposed for running at the Large Hadron Collider, as well as, $^{4}$He$+$Au, C$+$Au, O$+$Au, and $^{7,9}$Be$+$Au at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that new small collision systems (p+O, O+O, 4He+Au, C+Au, O+Au, 7,9Be+Au) can discriminate between hydrodynamic and color-field descriptions of quark-gluon plasma formation, but the authors hedge that full discrimination between different initial conditions will require simultaneous constraints from experimental data on multiplicity distributions, particle pT spectra, and flow coefficients.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber calculations using full N-nucleon configurations for light nuclei (4He, carbon, oxygen) generated from Green's Function Monte Carlo and quantum Monte Carlo simulations with chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians. These initial conditions were fed into the publicly available SONIC viscous hydrodynamic model with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.08 and bulk viscosity ratio zeta/s = 0, and the energy density scale factor was calibrated to match measured charged particle multiplicity in 3He+Au collisions at RHIC and p+Pb collisions at the LHC. An alternative initial condition framework called IP-Jazma was also used for comparison, modeling energy density via multiplication of target and projectile color charge distributions in the dense-dense limit.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio used: eta/s = 0.08
- Gaussian width for nucleon participant energy deposition: sigma = 0.4 fm
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: 42 mb
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV: 71 mb
- RMS three-dimensional nucleon central coordinates for 3He: 1.57 fm; for 4He: 1.46 fm
- Mean charged particle multiplicity dNch/deta for highest 5% multiplicity events: O+O = 72.9, p+Pb = 41.8, p+O = 18.2
- IP-Sat Gaussian width used in IP-Jazma calculations: 0.32 fm
- Triangle side length L for alpha-cluster carbon model: 2.8 fm; for oxygen tetrahedron model: 3.2 fm
- RMS distance of nucleons within alpha-like cluster (rc): 1.1 fm

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic paradigm tests previously applied to p+Au, d+Au, and 3He+Au collisions at RHIC (Nagle 2013, Aidala 2018) and p+Pb at the LHC, where experimental data were well described by viscous hydrodynamics. It directly addresses the ongoing debate between hydrodynamic descriptions and alternative color field calculations (Dusling 2015, Mace 2018) that can also qualitatively describe flow ordering. The paper enables future experimental runs at RHIC and LHC (Citron 2018) by providing specific predictions for new collision systems, and it quantifies the extent to which alpha-clustering hypotheses (Rybczynski 2017, Zhang 2017, Bozek 2014) could be distinguished from realistic nuclear structure calculations.
