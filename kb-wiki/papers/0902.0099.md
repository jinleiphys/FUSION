# Hyperdeformation in the cranked relativistic mean field theory: the Z=40-58 part of nuclear chart

> The systematic investigation of hyperdeformation (HD) at high spin in the $Z=40-58$ part of the nuclear chart has been performed in the framework of the cranked relativistic mean field theory. The properties of the moments of inertia of the HD bands, the role of the single-particle and necking degrees of freedom at HD, the spins at which the HD bands become yrast, the possibility to observe discrete HD bands etc. are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper claims that hyperdeformed (HD) rotational bands in the Z=40-58 region become yrast at spins typically below 65ℏ in proton-rich nuclei, making them potentially observable with current γ-ray detectors, but the crossing spins may be overestimated by 1-2ℏ due to basis truncation effects, and the HD nature of candidate experimental ridge structures in ¹¹⁸Te, ¹²⁴Xe, and ¹²⁴,¹²⁵Cs is supported by the calculated dynamic moments of inertia.

## Method

The authors performed systematic cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) calculations for even-even nuclei in the Z=40-58 region, using a hybrid basis truncation scheme (N_F=12 for normal-deformed and superdeformed states, N_F=14 for hyperdeformed states) with the NL1 parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian. They searched for rotational solutions in multiple local minima by varying the deformation of the harmonic oscillator basis (β₀=0.30, 0.65, 0.8, and 1.0 with various γ values) and defined crossing spins where HD configurations become yrast relative to normal-deformed and superdeformed configurations. The calculated dynamic moments of inertia and transition quadrupole moments were compared with experimental ridge structures from the HLHD experiment at EUROBALL-IV.

## Key numbers

- The minimum crossing spins I^{HD}_{cr} are reached at N≈Z+10 in the Pd, Te, and Ru isotope chains, with values typically below 65ℏ in proton-rich nuclei
- Experimental dynamic moments of inertia J^{(2)} for ridge structures range from 63 MeV⁻¹ (¹²¹Xe) to 111 MeV⁻¹ (¹¹⁸Te, ¹²⁴Cs, ¹²⁴Xe), with typical uncertainty of 10% (e.g., 111±11 MeV⁻¹ in ¹²⁴Xe)
- The HD ridges are observed in the frequency range of about 650 to 800 keV, with approximately 6-10 transitions in the HD cascades and more than 10 bands contributing to the ridges
- The normalized transition quadrupole moment Q_t^{norm}(Z,A) is defined as ZA^{2/3}/100.36 e·b, and a band is classified as hyperdeformed if its Q_t exceeds this value by at least 40% (or 30% for Z=40,42,44 nuclei)
- The reference Q_t value for the yrast SD band in ¹⁵²Dy at I=60ℏ is 18.73 e·b from CRMF calculations with NL1

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent studies of hyperdeformation at high spin, which had been attempted only in ¹⁰⁸Cd within the CRMF method (Afanasjev 2005) and in four A∼40 nuclei within the cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach (Inakura 2002). The study is motivated by experimental ridge structures observed in the HLHD experiment (Herskind 2006) and earlier macroscopic+microscopic calculations of HD minima (Chassman 1987, Dudek 2001). The results enable future experimental searches for discrete HD bands by identifying the most promising nuclei and spin ranges, and test whether configuration assignment methods developed for superdeformed bands (Ragnarsson 1993, Bengtsson 1988) are applicable to hyperdeformed bands.
