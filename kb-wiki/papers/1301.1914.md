# Recent Progress in Lattice QCD

> Recent progress in Lattice QCD is highlighted. After a brief introduction to the methodology of lattice computations the presentation focuses on three main topics: Hadron Spectroscopy, Hadron Structure and Lattice Flavor Physics. In each case a summary of recent computations of selected quantities is provided.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD has matured to the point where it can deliver clear-cut predictions for phenomenologically relevant quantities with fully controlled systematics, but the computational cost of simulating physical light quarks alongside charm quarks remains enormous, requiring careful extrapolation in quark masses and lattice spacing.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD methodology, where spacetime is discretized onto a 4-dimensional hypercubic grid with spacing \(a\) and box size \(L\), and the path integral is stochastically estimated using the Boltzmann weight \(\exp(-S_\mathrm{QCD})\). Simulations are performed with dynamical sea quarks (\(N_f=2\), \(2+1\), or \(2+1+1\) flavors), and results are extrapolated to the continuum limit (\(a\to0\)), infinite volume (\(L\to\infty\)), and physical quark masses by combining multiple ensembles.

## Key numbers

- Average up/down quark mass: \(m_{ud}^\mathrm{phys} \equiv (m_u^\mathrm{phys}+m_d^\mathrm{phys})/2 \simeq 3.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in \((\overline{\mathrm{MS}},2\ \mathrm{GeV})\) conventions
- Proton mass: \(M_p \simeq 940\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Nucleon mass in the 2-flavor chiral limit: \(M_N(m_{ud}\to0) \simeq 880(20)\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: \(\sigma_{\pi N} = \sigma_{ud} = 47(9)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (conservative average from multiple \(N_f=2+1\) determinations)
- Strange sigma term: \(\sigma_s = 48(25)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (conservative average from multiple \(N_f=2+1\) determinations)
- Lattice spacing at physical pion mass with \(128^4\) grid: \(a = 0.047\ \mathrm{fm}\) (derived from requirement \(\Mpi L > 4\))
- Charm quark mass product at that spacing: \(a m_c = 0.26\) (acceptable); at doubled spacing: \(a m_c = 0.52\) (barely acceptable)

## Context

This review summarizes the state of lattice QCD circa 2013, extending the quenched (\(N_f=0\)) results of CP-PACS (Aoki 2000) which failed to reproduce the experimental hadron spectrum, and showing that unquenched \(N_f=2+1\) simulations (Durr 2008) pass that test. It enables future work on precision determinations of Standard Model parameters (quark masses, CKM matrix elements), dark matter searches via nucleon sigma terms, and first-principles nuclear physics from QCD. The FLAG collaboration (Colangelo 2010) provides averaged lattice results for low-energy observables.
