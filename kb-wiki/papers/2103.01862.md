# Searching for axionlike particles with low masses in $pPb$ and $PbPb$ collisions

> The production of axionlike particles (ALPs) with small masses in ultraperipheral $Pb - p$ and $Pb - Pb$ collisions at the LHC is investigated. The cross section and kinematical distributions associated to the diphoton final state produced in the $γγ\rightarrow a \rightarrow γγ$ subprocesses are estimated considering a realistic set of kinematical cuts. A detailed analysis of the backgrounds is performed and the expected sensitivity to the ALP production is derived. Our results demonstrate that a future experimental analysis of the exclusive diphoton production for the forward rapidities probed by the LHCb detector can improve the existing exclusion limits on the ALP - photon coupling in the mass range 2 GeV $\le m_a \le$ 5 GeV.

## Key claim

A future experimental analysis of exclusive diphoton production at forward rapidities probed by the LHCb detector can improve the existing exclusion limits on the ALP-photon coupling in the mass range 2 GeV ≤ m_a ≤ 5 GeV, a region not currently reachable by ATLAS and CMS detectors.

## Method

The authors used the SuperChic 4 Monte Carlo event generator to simulate ALP production via the γγ → a → γγ subprocess in ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV and Pb-p collisions at √s_NN = 8.1 TeV. They applied realistic exclusivity cuts (m_γγ > 1 GeV, p_T(γ,γ) > 0.2 GeV, acoplanarity < 0.01, p_T(γγ) < 0.1 GeV) and selected events in forward (2.0 < |η(γ,γ)| < 4.5) and central (|η(γ,γ)| < 2.5) rapidity ranges. Backgrounds from light-by-light scattering and dielectron production (with 0.5% misidentification probability per lepton) were estimated and subtracted.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV with forward selection and m_a = 2 GeV, g_aγ = 1×10⁻³ GeV⁻¹: signal cross section = 1282.2 pb in the invariant mass window 1.5 < m(γγ) < 2.5 GeV, compared to LbL background = 166.0 pb and dielectron background = 23.5 pb
- For Pb-p collisions at √s_NN = 8.1 TeV with forward selection and m_a = 2 GeV, g_aγ = 1×10⁻³ GeV⁻¹: signal cross section = 396.0 pb in the invariant mass window 1.5 < m(γγ) < 2.5 GeV, compared to LbL background = 53.2 pb and dielectron background = 7.0 pb
- For Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV with forward selection and m_a = 5 GeV, g_aγ = 1×10⁻³ GeV⁻¹: signal cross section = 580.3 pb in the invariant mass window 4.5 < m(γγ) < 5.5 GeV, compared to LbL background = 5.9 pb and dielectron background = 0.8 pb
- Total cross sections for Pb-Pb collisions are of order nb, while for Pb-p collisions they are of order pb, suppressed by a factor ≈ (82)² due to the Z² dependence of the photon-photon luminosity

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exploratory study (Goncalves et al., 2020) that focused on Pb-Pb collisions with four ALP mass-coupling combinations, now including Pb-p collisions and a larger set of parameters. It complements the experimental limits from ATLAS (2020) and CMS (2020) collaborations, which provide the strongest limits for ALP masses 5 GeV ≤ m_a ≤ 100 GeV but cannot access the low-mass region. The results demonstrate that forward detectors like LHCb can probe ALP parameter space unexplored by LEP (OPAL, 2002) and previous LHC analyses, and that Pb-p collisions are competitive despite lower cross sections due to higher expected integrated luminosities.
