# Bottomonia screening masses from $2 + 1$ flavor QCD

> The sequential melting of the bottomonium states is one of the important signals for the existence of a quark gluon plasma. The study of bottomonia spectral functions on the lattice is a difficult task for many reasons. Calculations based on NRQCD, that are commonly used for such purpose, are not applicable at high temperatures. In this work we propose a new method to study this problem by calculating the spatial screening masses of bottomonium states. We calculate the spatial meson correlators and extract the screening masses for mesons in different quantum channels using highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action for bottom quarks and dynamical $2+1$ flavor QCD HISQ gauge configurations. The typical lattices we choose are of size $N_s^3 \times N_τ$ where $N_s=4 N_τ$ and $N_τ=8, 10, 12$. We consider the temperature range $T = 300$-$1000$ MeV. We show that for $T > 500$ MeV the temperature dependence of the screening masses of the ground state bottomonia are compatible with the expectations based on uncorrelated quark anti-quark pairs.

# Bottomonia screening masses from 2+1 flavor QCD

## Key claim
The temperature dependence of bottomonium screening masses, calculated using a relativistic HISQ action for bottom quarks, shows that ground state bottomonia (eta_b and Upsilon(1S)) melt around 450 MeV, while P-wave states (chi_b0, chi_b1) melt at a lower temperature near 350 MeV. For temperatures above 500 MeV, the screening masses become compatible with expectations based on uncorrelated quark-antiquark pairs, but the authors note systematic uncertainties from the bottom quark mass setting and zero-temperature mass interpolation.

## Method
The authors calculated spatial meson correlators for bottomonium states on 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations generated by the HotQCD collaboration, using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action for bottom quarks in the quenched approximation. They extracted screening masses from two-state fits to the spatial correlators using the form C_M(z) = A_NO cosh[M_NO(z - N_s/2)] - (-1)^z A_O cosh[M_O(z - N_s/2)], considering lattices with N_tau = 8, 10, 12 and N_s = 4 N_tau over a temperature range of 300 to 1000 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: T = 300 to 1000 MeV, corresponding to approximately 2 to 7 times the chiral crossover temperature T_c = 156.5(1.5) MeV
- Bottom quark mass set to m_b = 52.5 m_s, close to the physical value
- Light quark masses set to m_l = m_s/20, corresponding to a Goldstone pion mass of 160 MeV in the continuum limit
- The hyperfine splitting between Upsilon(1S) and eta_b(1S) is about 70 MeV from the Particle Data Group
- At T > 900 MeV, the spin-dependent potential gives a mass splitting of 0.3 T between vector and pseudo-scalar screening masses
- The eta_b mass from the HISQ action is larger than the PDG value by 4% at beta = 7.596 and 9.8% at beta = 8.4
- A systematic error of 1% was assigned to the zero-temperature eta_b mass due to interpolation

## Context
This work extends lattice studies of bottomonium in-medium properties beyond the NRQCD approach, which is not applicable at high temperatures due to lattice spacing constraints. The results are consistent with NRQCD-based studies by Aarts (2010, 2014), Kim (2018), and Larsen (2019) for ground state melting temperatures, and with potential model calculations using screened complex potentials by Petreczky (2010) and Burnier (2015). The method enables study of bottomonium at higher temperatures than accessible with NRQCD, and shows a clear mass hierarchy in quarkonium melting when compared to charmonium results from Bazavov (2014), where eta_c screening masses increase linearly with temperature already above 250 MeV.
