# Sea-quark loop contributions to the $\bar d$-$\bar u$ asymmetry in the proton

> QCD interactions for equal-mass fermion flavors are flavor blind. This fact is often used to state that disconnected sea-quark loop contributions are equal for $u$ and $d$ quarks in the mass symmetric case and therefore these disconnected sea-quark loop contributions cannot contribute to the well-known $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry in the proton. Instead, it is argued that one must look to the connected sector of lattice QCD correlation functions to find this difference. In this presentation, we note that these statements are true provided unphysical contributions in the sea-quark loop sector are included, contributions from baryons that do not appear in the physical spectrum. To respect the Pauli principle, these unphysical contributions from the disconnected sea-quark loop sector must cancel equally unphysical contributions in the connected sector. The remaining disconnected sea-quark loop contributions no longer have a balance between $\bar d$ and $\bar u$. Upon considering only physically observed baryons in the loop contributions, we illustrate an important contribution from the sea-quark loop sector to $\bar d - \bar u$ that enhances the leading connected contribution by 12\%.

## Key claim

The naive assertion that disconnected sea-quark loop contributions to the proton's $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry are exactly equal for $u$ and $d$ quarks relies on including contributions from an unphysical baryon (a $p^{++}$ baryon degenerate with the proton) that does not appear in the physical spectrum; once this unphysical contribution is removed via cancellation with the connected sector, the remaining disconnected sea-quark loop contributions do contribute to the asymmetry, enhancing the leading connected contribution by 12%.

## Method

The authors analyze the quark-flow diagrams contributing to the $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry in lattice QCD three-point correlation functions, distinguishing between connected Wick contractions and disconnected sea-quark loop diagrams. They examine the meson-baryon dressings of the proton, focusing on intermediate baryons degenerate with the nucleon, and use the formalism of partially quenched chiral perturbation theory to quantify the axial couplings of each diagram in terms of the SU(3)-flavor couplings $F$ and $D$. The cancellation of unphysical baryon contributions between the connected and disconnected sectors is traced, and the remaining disconnected contributions to the asymmetry are computed.

## Key numbers

- The connected sector contribution to $\bar d - \bar u$ from diagrams (a) and (c) yields a net effect of $1.23$ (in units where the $n\,\pi^+$ dressing is $2\,g_A^2$), favoring $\bar d$.
- The disconnected sea-quark loop contribution from diagram (h) adds $0.15$ to the $\bar d$ excess, raising the total from $1.23$ to $1.38$.
- This corresponds to a 12% enhancement of the pion-nucleon contribution to $\bar d - \bar u$ from the disconnected sea-quark loop sector.
- The axial couplings used are $D = 0.77$ and $F = 0.50$, giving $g_A = 1.27$.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry in the proton, first predicted by Thomas (1983) and later verified experimentally by the New Muon Collaboration (1990) and the NuSea/SeaQuest collaborations (2001, 2021). It contradicts the common assumption that disconnected sea-quark loop contributions cannot contribute to the asymmetry due to flavor-blind QCD interactions, an argument that the authors show relies on unphysical baryon states. The analysis enables improved phenomenological parton distribution function extractions by demonstrating that lattice QCD calculations of disconnected diagrams can provide nontrivial constraints on the sea-quark asymmetry, supplementing experimental data from Drell-Yan processes and deep inelastic scattering.
