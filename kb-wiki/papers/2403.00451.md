# Charge-conjugation asymmetry and molecular content: the $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)^\pm$ in matter

> We analyze the modifications that a dense nuclear medium induces in the $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)^\pm$ and $D_{s1}(2460)^\pm$. In the vacuum, we consider them as isoscalar $D^{(*)}K$ and $\overline{D}{}^{(*)}\overline{K}$ $S$-wave bound states, which are dynamically generated from effective interactions that lead to different Weinberg compositeness scenarios. Matter effects are incorporated through the two-meson loop functions, taking into account the self energies that the $D^{(*)}$, $\overline{D}{}^{(*)}$, $K$, and $\overline{K}$ develop when embedded in a nuclear medium. Although particle-antiparticle [$D^{(\ast)}_{s0,s1}(2317,2460)^+$ versus $D^{(\ast)}_{s0,s1}(2317,2460)^-$] lineshapes are the same in vacuum, we find extremely different density patterns in matter. This charge-conjugation asymmetry mainly stems from the very different kaon and antikaon interaction with the nucleons of the dense medium. We show that the in-medium lineshapes found for these resonances strongly depend on their $D^{(*)}K$/$\overline{D}{}^{(*)}\overline{K}$ molecular content, and discuss how this novel feature can be used to better determine/constrain the inner structure of these exotic states.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the in-medium lineshapes of the $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)^\pm$ and $D_{s1}(2460)^\pm$ develop a strong charge-conjugation asymmetry in dense nuclear matter, even though particle and antiparticle are identical in vacuum; this asymmetry depends on the molecular $D^{(*)}K$/$\overline{D}{}^{(*)}\overline{K}$ content of each state and could be used to constrain the inner structure of these exotic resonances.

## Method

The authors treat the $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)^\pm$ and $D_{s1}(2460)^\pm$ as isoscalar $S$-wave $D^{(*)}K$ and $\overline{D}{}^{(*)}\overline{K}$ bound states generated from effective contact potentials adjusted to produce different Weinberg compositeness scenarios. Nuclear medium effects are incorporated by replacing the vacuum two-meson loop function with an in-medium version that uses density-dependent spectral functions for the $D^{(*)}$, $\overline{D}{}^{(*)}$, $K$, and $\overline{K}$ mesons, computed from self-consistent chiral unitary approaches. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in the on-shell approximation with a sharp momentum cutoff of 0.7 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)^\pm$ and $D_{s1}(2460)^\pm$ lie about 45 MeV below the $DK$ and $D^*K$ thresholds, respectively.
* The LO HMChPT analysis of Ref. Albaladejo (2016) found a pole at a mass of $2315 \pm 17$ MeV with a molecular probability of $70^{+6}_{-10}\%$.
* The vacuum cutoff used to reproduce the LO HMChPT results is $\Lambda = 875 \pm 85$ MeV.
* The $\overline{K}$ quasiparticle peak shifts to lower energies in matter, while the $K$ quasiparticle energy shows very mild density dependence due to the small $KN$ cross section.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ in matter (Montesinos 2023) to the strange-charm sector, and builds on the NLO HMChPT unitarized scheme of Albaladejo (2016) and Du (2017) that successfully describes the $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ as molecular states. A preliminary study of only the $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)^+$ in dense matter was performed by Molina (2009), while Montana (2020) studied thermal effects but found no particle-antiparticle asymmetry because $K\pi$ and $\overline{K}\pi$ interactions are equal in the SU(2) limit. The present work enables future experimental measurements of the density-dependent charge-conjugation asymmetry to discriminate between molecular and compact (tetraquark or $c\overline{s}$) structures for these states.
