# y-scaling in Quasielastic Electron Scattering from Nuclei

> A relativistic single particle model is used to calculate the inclusive $(e,e')$ reaction from $A=$12, 40, 56, 197, and 208 nuclei in the quasielastic region. We have shown that this model provides a very good description of the available experimental cross sections when they are dominated by the quasielastic process. In this paper we use this model to investigate the dependence of $y$-scaling on electron kinematics, particularly the electron scattering angle, for a range of squared four momentum transfer $0.20-0.80$ (GeV/c)$^2$. In this kinematic domain, Coulomb distortion of the electron does not significantly affect scaling, but final state interactions of the knocked out nucleon do affect scaling particularly when the nucleons have lower energies. In general, we find that scaling works for this reaction, but at lower values of the four momentum transfer, the scaling function does have some dependence on the electron scattering angle. We also consider a modification of y-scaling to include small binding energy effects as a function of Z and A and show that there is some improvement in scaling.

## Key claim

The authors use a relativistic single particle model to show that y-scaling in quasielastic electron scattering works for nuclei from A=12 to A=208, but only with the important qualification that at lower four-momentum transfer (0.20-0.80 (GeV/c)^2) the scaling function depends on the electron scattering angle, and final state interactions of the knocked-out nucleon cause significant scaling violations, particularly at lower nucleon energies.

## Method

The authors calculate inclusive (e,e') cross sections using a relativistic single particle model with bound and continuum nucleon wave functions obtained from solving the Dirac equation with strong vector and scalar potentials. For the outgoing nucleons, they use energy-dependent real potentials fitted to elastic proton scattering data, and they include electron Coulomb distortion effects through an approximate treatment that allows separation of longitudinal and transverse responses. They then construct the y-scaling function F(y) as the ratio of the calculated cross section to the off-shell electron-nucleon cross section, and investigate its dependence on electron kinematics, final state interactions, and a modified scaling variable y' that incorporates average binding energy effects.

## Key numbers

- Squared four-momentum transfer range investigated: 0.20 to 0.80 (GeV/c)^2
- Nuclei studied: A = 12, 40, 56, 197, and 208
- At Q^2 approximately 0.2-0.3 (GeV/c)^2 with scattering angle 45 degrees, deviations of the scaling function from the mean across nuclei are less than 10%
- SLAC data comparison uses incident electron energy E = 2.02 GeV and scattering angle theta = 15 degrees
- Bates data comparison for Ca-40 includes three kinematics: E = 739 MeV at theta = 45.5 degrees, E = 372 MeV at theta = 90 degrees, and E = 367 MeV at theta = 140 degrees
- For Pb-208 at higher Q^2 (approximately 0.5-0.8 (GeV/c)^2), scaling is only weakly dependent on scattering angle across five kinematics from E = 1830 MeV at theta = 30 degrees to E = 580 MeV at theta = 150 degrees
- At high incident energy E = 1.5 GeV and theta = 30 degrees, scaling is greatly improved across nuclei
- The standard value for the form factor cutoff parameter Lambda^2 is 0.71 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic single particle model calculations (Kim and Wright, 2003) that successfully described quasielastic (e,e') data from SLAC, and applies it to systematically investigate y-scaling. The study builds on the pioneering scaling work of West (1975) and the theoretical analyses by Ciofi degli Atti, Pace, and Salme (1989, 1991) who examined scaling in few-body systems and complex nuclei using non-relativistic models. The paper also addresses the recent findings by the Madrid group and Donnelly (2007) that strong scalar and vector potentials in relativistic models cause a breakdown of scaling and produce different scaling functions for longitudinal and transverse responses. The results enable using y-scaling at larger four-momentum transfer to subtract quasielastic contributions from inclusive (e,e') cross sections, allowing investigation of inelastic contributions that add incoherently.
