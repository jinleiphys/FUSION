# Valence-quark distributions of pions and kaons in a nuclear medium

> In-medium valence-quark distributions of $π^+$ and $K^+$ mesons in symmetric nuclear matter are studied by combining the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model and the quark-meson coupling model. The in-medium properties of the current quarks, which are used as inputs for studying the in-medium pion and kaon properties in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, are calculated within the quark-meson coupling model. The light-quark condensates, light-quark dynamical masses, pion and kaon decay constants, and pion- and kaon-quark coupling constants are found to decrease as nuclear density increases. The obtained valence quark distributions in vacuum for both the $π^+$ and $K^+$ could reasonably describe the available experimental data over a wide range of Bjorken-$x$. The in-medium valence $u$-quark distribution in the $π^+$ at $Q^2=16~\mbox{GeV}^2$ is found to be almost unchanged compared to the in-vacuum case. However, the in-medium to in-vacuum ratios of both the valence $u$-quark and valence $s$-quark distributions of the $K^+$ meson at $Q^2=16~\mbox{GeV}^2$ increase with nuclear matter density, but show different $x$-dependence. Namely, the ratio for the valence $u$-quark distribution increases with $x$, while that for the valence $s$ quark decreases with $x$. These features are enhanced at higher density regions.

## Key claim

The in-medium valence u-quark distribution in the π⁺ at Q² = 16 GeV² is almost unchanged compared to the in-vacuum case, while the in-medium to in-vacuum ratios of both valence u-quark and valence s-quark distributions of the K⁺ meson increase with nuclear matter density but show opposite x-dependence, with these features enhanced at higher density regions.

## Method

The authors combined the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to study in-medium valence-quark distributions of π⁺ and K⁺ mesons in symmetric nuclear matter. The QMC model provided in-medium current-quark properties (effective masses and mean-field potentials) by solving self-consistently for nuclear matter saturation properties, which were then used as inputs to the NJL model to compute in-medium dynamical quark masses, meson properties, and valence-quark distributions via the operator definition of twist-2 quark distributions evaluated from Feynman diagrams.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, the dynamical u-quark mass decreases by about 30% (from 0.400 GeV in vacuum to 0.270 GeV at ρ₀)
- The magnitude of the u-quark condensate decreases by about 13% at normal nuclear density (from 0.171 GeV to 0.149 GeV for the cube root)
- The pion mass decreases by about 7% at normal nuclear density (from 0.140 GeV to 0.131 GeV)
- The kaon mass decreases by about 20% at normal nuclear density (from 0.495 GeV to 0.386 GeV)
- The ratio f_π*/f_π = 0.87 at normal nuclear density (from 0.093 GeV to 0.081 GeV)
- The pion-quark coupling constant ratio g_πqq*/g_πqq ≈ 0.77 at normal nuclear density (from 4.255 to 3.265)
- The kaon decay constant decreases only a few percent at normal nuclear density (from 0.091 GeV to 0.088 GeV)
- All valence-quark distribution calculations are performed at Q² = 16 GeV²

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson electromagnetic form factors and distribution amplitudes in nuclear matter by de Melo et al. (2014) and Yabusaki et al. (2017), which combined the light-front constituent quark model with the QMC model, but improves upon them by incorporating chiral symmetry constraints through the NJL model. An earlier attempt by Suzuki (1995) combined the NJL model with operator product expansion and found that the in-medium pion structure function decreases at large Bjorken-x at saturation density. The present results enable predictions for meson-induced Drell-Yan processes on nuclear targets, such as those proposed at the COMPASS experiment at CERN SPS, and provide input for understanding hadronization in heavy ion collisions.
