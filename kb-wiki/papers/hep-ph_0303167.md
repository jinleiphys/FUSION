# Spontaneous symmetry breaking in gauge theories via Bose-Einstein condensation

> We propose a mechanism naturally leading to the spontaneous symmetry breaking in a gauge theory. The Higgs field is assumed to have global and gauged internal symmetries. We associate a non zero chemical potential to one of the globally conserved charges commuting with all of the gauge transformations. This induces a negative mass squared for the Higgs field triggering the spontaneous symmetry breaking of the global and local symmetries. The mechanism is general and we test the idea for the electroweak theory in which the Higgs sector is extended to possess an extra global Abelian symmetry. To this symmetry we associate a non zero chemical potential. The Bose-Einstein condensation of the Higgs leads, at tree level, to modified dispersion relations for the Higgs field while the dispersion relations of the gauge bosons and fermions remain undisturbed. The latter are modified through higher order corrections. We have computed some corrections to the vacuum polarizations of the gauge bosons and fermions. To quantify the corrections to the gauge boson vacuum polarizations with respect to the Standard Model we considered the effects on the T parameter. We finally derive the one loop modified fermion dispersion relations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that spontaneous symmetry breaking in gauge theories can be triggered by introducing a nonzero chemical potential associated with a global symmetry of the Higgs field, rather than assuming a negative mass squared. The mechanism is tested for the electroweak theory by extending the Higgs sector with an extra global U_A(1) symmetry, and the authors note that Lorentz breaking effects are confined to the Higgs sector at tree level while affecting gauge bosons and fermions only through radiative corrections.

## Method

The authors extend the Standard Model Higgs sector to include an extra global U_A(1) symmetry and introduce a chemical potential μ_A associated with this symmetry. They then compute the tree-level dispersion relations for the Higgs field and the η Goldstone boson, and evaluate one-loop corrections to the vacuum polarizations of the W and Z gauge bosons and to fermion self-energies. The corrections are quantified using a generalization of the oblique T parameter and by extracting the fermion velocity from the modified dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- The T parameter from custodial symmetry breaking is T ≈ -0.048, computed from one-loop corrections to gauge boson vacuum polarizations.
- The electron velocity correction is v_e ≈ 1 - 5 × 10^{-15}, derived from one-loop fermion self-energy with the electron Yukawa coupling.
- The muon velocity correction is of order 8 × 10^{-10}, from the same one-loop calculation.
- The Higgs energy gap at zero momentum is Δ² = 2(3μ_A² - m²), which for m=0 gives Δ² = 6μ_A², larger than the potential curvature 2μ_A².
- The η field dispersion relation at small momentum is E²_η = p²/3, derived from the tree-level quadratic Lagrangian in the broken phase.

## Context

This work extends the study of relativistic Bose-Einstein condensation (Haber 1981, Kapusta) as a mechanism for spontaneous symmetry breaking in gauge theories, contrasting with technicolor models (Hill 2002) and supersymmetric extensions (Kane 2002) that provide alternative explanations for electroweak symmetry breaking. The mechanism enables predictions for Lorentz-breaking effects in the Higgs sector that could be tested experimentally, and the authors note that similar ideas using chemical potentials at high temperature have been studied for the early universe (Linde 1979, Ferrer, Bajc 1999).
