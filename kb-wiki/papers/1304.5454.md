# Universal behaviour of four-boson systems from a functional renormalisation group

> We apply a functional renormalisation group to systems of four bosonic atoms close to the unitary limit. We work with a local effective action that includes a dynamical trimer field and we use this field to eliminate structures that do not correspond to the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations. In the physical limit, we find three four-body bound states below the shallowest three-body state. The values of the scattering lengths at which two of these states become bound are in good agreement with exact solutions of the four-body equations and experimental observations. The third state is extremely shallow. During the evolution we find an infinite number of four-body states based on each three-body state which follow a double-exponential pattern in the running scale. None of the four-body states shows any evidence of dependence on a four-body parameter.

## Key claim

The authors find an infinite sequence of four-body bound states below each Efimov three-body state, following a double-exponential "super-Efimov" pattern in the running scale, but only three of these states survive as physical bound states in the last Efimov cycle; crucially, none of the four-body states shows any evidence of dependence on a four-body parameter, contradicting some earlier claims.

## Method

The authors apply a functional renormalisation group (FRG) to systems of four nonrelativistic bosonic atoms near the unitary limit, using a local effective action that includes dynamical dimer and trimer fields. They eliminate couplings that do not correspond to the Faddeev-Yakubovsky structure by making the trimer field explicitly scale-dependent, and numerically integrate the coupled flow equations for the four-body couplings \(u_{dd}\), \(u_{dt}\), and \(u_{tt}\) through several Efimov cycles. The physical limit is reached by evolving the regulator scale \(k\) to zero, and finite scattering lengths are introduced by tuning the initial dimer self-energy.

## Key numbers

- The scaling factor in momentum for the Efimov cycles in this truncated action is \(\sim 29.8\), compared to the true value of \(\sim 22.7\).
- The complex scaling exponents for the three-body sector are \(d_{\pm} = -281/125 \pm \mathfrak{i} \sqrt{535}/25\).
- The atom-trimer threshold crossing occurs at \(t = t_3 \simeq -4.85\) in the unitary limit, where \(t = \ln(k/K)\).
- The rescaled couplings are defined with powers of \(k\): \(\hat{u}_{dd}(k) = k^3 u_{dd}(k)\), \(\hat{u}_{dt}(k) = k^{781/250} u_{dt}(k)\), \(\hat{u}_{tt}(k) = k^{406/125} u_{tt}(k)\).
- Poles in the imaginary part of \(\hat{u}_{tt}\) appear at \(t \simeq -3.83\), \(-4.67\), and just below the threshold \(t_3 \simeq -4.85\), with an infinite sequence becoming equally spaced in \(x = \ln(t - t_3)\).

## Context

This work extends the FRG approach to few-body systems previously applied to the three-body sector by Floerchinger et al. (2009) and to the four-boson problem by Schmidt and Moroz (2010), but with a dynamical trimer field that reproduces the structure of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations. The results agree with exact four-body calculations by von Stecher et al. (2009) and Deltuva (2010) for the two deepest four-body states, and with experimental observations in ultracold \(^{133}\)Cs atoms by Ferlaino et al. (2009). The double-exponential pattern of poles resembles the "super-Efimov" behaviour found by Nishida, Moroz, and Son (2013) in a two-dimensional three-body system. The absence of a four-body parameter contradicts results by Hadizadeh et al. (2011, 2012), who found up to three four-body states per cycle with dependence on such a parameter.
