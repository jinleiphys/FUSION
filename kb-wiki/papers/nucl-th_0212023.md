# Fingerprints of entangled states in reactions with rare isotopes

> We study the presence of entangled states of nucleon pairs from nuclear decays and in reactions with exotic nuclei, e.g., 11Li, or 6He. It is shown that the fingerprints of entangled states in these subsystems are visible in correlation measurements and can be accessed with present experimental techniques. This shows that not only atomic and optical systems, but also nuclear systems serve as important tools to obtain dichotomic outcomes for tests of the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen paradox.

## Key claim

Entangled states of nucleon pairs in nuclear decays and reactions with exotic nuclei (e.g., 11Li, 6He) produce distinct fingerprints in momentum correlation measurements that can be accessed with present experimental techniques, but the interpretation depends on the assumption that the final-state wavefunction is entangled and that the correlation function is largely independent of the details of the reaction mechanism.

## Method

The author defines a correlation function R(Δp) = C(Δp)/U(Δp) − 1, where C(Δp) is the coincidence probability for two nucleons with relative momentum Δp and U(Δp) is the uncorrelated product of single-particle probabilities. The spatial part of the two-nucleon wavefunction is symmetrized for singlet (S=0) and triplet (S=1) spin states, leading to analytical expressions for R(Δp) that depend on the average pair momentum p_n, the momentum spread σ_n, and a mixing parameter M for triplet admixture. The method is applied to peripheral reactions with halo nuclei and to two-proton decay, using Gaussian wavepackets for the nucleons and assuming the reaction suddenly detaches the pair from the core.

## Key numbers

- For the breakup of 11Li projectiles at Elab ≈ 300 MeV/nucleon, the neutrons acquire a momentum spread of the order of σ_n ≈ 20 MeV/c.
- In the analysis of the 1H(6He,2He)5H transfer reaction, the source region size for the two protons was taken as R = 4.6 fm.
- The n-n scattering length from the phenomenological potential used for 11Li is a_nn = −17.4 fm, and the n-9Li scattering length is a_n9 = −10.66 fm.
- The most probable values of the Jacobian coordinates for 11Li are ⟨ρ⟩ = 3.3 fm and ⟨λ⟩ = 3.1 fm.
- The purity P of the 11Li wavefunction, calculated from a variational three-body model, is P ≈ 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum entanglement from atomic and optical systems to nuclear systems, specifically using rare isotopes and two-proton radioactivity to test the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen paradox via Bell's inequalities, as previously proposed by Bell (1964) and Einstein, Podolsky, and Rosen (1935). The correlation-function method is applied to experimental data from Simon et al. (1999) on 11Li fragmentation and from Korsheninnikov et al. (2002) on the 1H(6He,2He)5H reaction, and it enables future experiments at the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) facility to disentangle singlet and triplet spin configurations in nucleon pairs without requiring direct spin measurements.
