# Multi-skyrmion states in the Skyrme model with false vaccum potential

> We study the multi-skyrmion states using a Skyrme model with false vacuum potential upto baryon number $B=8$ using the product ansatz. It is found that, both the false vacuum potential and true vacuum potential can yield cluster structure of the multi-skyrmion states. The effect of the explicit chiral breaking on the masses and the contour surfaces of the baryon number density of the multi-skyrmion states are analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both false vacuum potentials and true vacuum potentials can yield cluster structures of multi-skyrmion states up to baryon number B=8, but notes that with certain mass parameters the conclusion may differ from that obtained using the rational map ansatz, an issue that requires further clarification.

## Method

The authors study multi-skyrmion states using the Skyrme model extended with a false vacuum potential term of the form \(-\frac{1}{4}(m_1^2 \text{Tr}[1-U] + m_2^2 \text{Tr}[1-U^2])\). They construct initial configurations via the product ansatz, where two skyrmion solutions with baryon numbers \(B_1\) and \(B_2\) are multiplied together with a relative rotation in isospin space, and then relax the configurations to energy minima using a finite element method with a squeezing procedure to reduce the inter-cluster distance.

## Key numbers

* For the false vacuum case \(m_1=m_2=0.5\), the energies (in units of \(12\pi^2 \cdot f_\pi/4e\)) are: B=1: 1.27, B=2: 2.48, B=4: 4.56, B=8: 8.91 (truncated octahedron) and 9.00 (D\(_{4h}\) solution).
* For the false vacuum case \(m_1=0, m_2=0.5\), the energies are: B=1: 1.29, B=2: 2.51, B=4: 4.62, B=8: 8.95.
* For the massive pion case \(m_1=0.5, m_2=0\), the energies are: B=1: 1.25, B=2: 2.46, B=4: 4.54, B=8: 9.20.
* For the chiral limit \(m_1=0, m_2=0\), the energies are: B=1: 1.23, B=2: 2.42, B=4: 4.47, B=8: 8.86.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Skyrme model with false vacuum potentials, which were introduced by Dupuis (2018) and Ferreira (2021) and applied to multi-skyrmion systems using the rational map ansatz by Livramento (2022). The authors use the product ansatz instead, finding that for B=8 with \(m_1=m_2=0.5\) two distinct configurations exist (truncated octahedron and D\(_{4h}\)), whereas Battye (2006) found the truncated octahedron to have lower energy using the rational map approach. The results enable further investigation of false vacuum effects on nuclear cluster structures and may inform studies of cosmological phase transitions.
