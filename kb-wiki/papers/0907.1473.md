# Confined but chirally symmetric hadrons at large density and the Casher's argument

> Casher's argument, which is believed to be quite general, states that in the confining regime chiral symmetry is necessarily broken. In the large-N_c limit and at moderate and low temperatures QCD is confining up to arbitrary large densities, and there should appear a quarkyonic matter. It has been demonstrated, within a manifestly confining and chirally symmetric model, which is a 3+1 dimensional generalization of the 't Hooft model, that, at zero temperature and at a density exceeding a critical one, the chiral symmetry is restored while quarks remain confined in color-singlet hadrons. This is in conflict with the Casher's argument. Here we explain the reason why the Casher's argument fails and clarify the physical mechanism lying behind such confined but chirally symmetric hadrons.

# Confined but chirally symmetric hadrons at large density and the Casher's argument

## Key claim
Casher's argument, which claims that confinement necessarily implies chiral symmetry breaking, fails at large densities because in a meson the synchronous motion of quark and antiquark allows their spin flips to cancel each other, conserving total angular momentum even when chiral symmetry is restored. This means confined but chirally symmetric hadrons can exist in dense matter, contradicting the presumed generality of Casher's argument.

## Method
The author analyzes a 3+1 dimensional generalization of the 't Hooft model (QCD in the large-N_c limit) with an instantaneous Coulomb-like Lorentz-vector confining potential between quarks. The Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-antiquark bound states are solved in the rainbow and ladder approximations (exact in large-N_c), with Pauli blocking implemented by removing intermediate quark momenta below the Fermi momentum p_f from all integrations. The infrared regularization ensures that color-singlet observables are finite while single quarks remain confined.

## Key numbers
- At a critical Fermi momentum p_f^{cr}, the gap equation exhibits a chiral restoration phase transition, above which the chiral angle φ_p = 0, the quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ = 0, and the dynamical quark mass M(q) = 0.
- The quark condensate is given by ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(N_C/π²) ∫₀^∞ dp p² sin φ_p, which vanishes when φ_p = 0.
- The dynamical quark mass is M(p) = p tan φ_p, which vanishes when φ_p = 0.

## Context
This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007), who suggested a chirally symmetric but confined subphase within quarkyonic matter at large densities in the large-N_c limit. The author had previously demonstrated this phenomenon in a solvable model (Glozman 2007, 2008), but here addresses the conflict with Casher's classic argument (Casher 1979). The analysis shows that Casher's argument fails because it considers only a single quark's motion, whereas in a meson the simultaneous turning of quark and antiquark allows mutual cancellation of spin flips. The Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (PNJL) studies of quarkyonic matter (Fukushima 2004, Abuki 2008, Sasaki 2008) are noted as nonconfining and thus unable to address the problem of confined but chirally symmetric hadrons.
