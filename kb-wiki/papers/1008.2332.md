# Direct photon calculations in heavy-ion collisions at s(NN)**1/2 = 62.4 - 200 AGeV in a (3+1) dimensional hybrid approach

> Direct photon spectra from central Au+Au- and Cu+Cu-collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4, 130 and 200 AGeV are calculated within the microscopic transport model UrQMD and a micro+macro hybrid model. In the latter approach, the high-density part of the transport evolution is replaced by an ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic calculation. We study the impact of viscosity and full local thermalization and compare the calculations to measurements obtained by the PHENIX collaboration. We find a reasonable agreement with the experimental data for calculations involving a Quark-Gluon-Plasma phase.

## Key claim

The authors find that direct photon spectra from Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC energies are reasonably described by a hybrid model that replaces the high-density part of the transport evolution with ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamics, but only when the hydrodynamic stage includes a Quark-Gluon-Plasma phase; the purely transport (cascade) calculation without a hydrodynamic QGP stage does not reproduce the experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD microscopic transport model and a hybrid approach in which the high-density phase of the collision is replaced by ideal (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics, with three different equations of state: a hadron-gas EoS (HG-EoS), a chiral EoS (chi-EoS) incorporating a QGP phase, and a bag-model EoS (BM-EoS) with a first-order phase transition. Direct photon spectra were computed for central (00%-20% and 20%-40% centrality) Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4, 130, and 200 AGeV and compared to PHENIX measurements.

## Key numbers

* Au+Au at 200 AGeV, 00%-20% centrality, chi-EoS: slope parameter T_slope = 261.9 +/- 8.7 MeV, normalization A = 10.13 +/- 2.05 GeV^-2, chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.020
* Au+Au at 200 AGeV, 00%-20% centrality, HG-EoS: T_slope = 246.7 +/- 8.6 MeV, A = 3.63 +/- 0.83 GeV^-2, chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.025
* Au+Au at 200 AGeV, 00%-20% centrality, BM-EoS: T_slope = 251.4 +/- 9.7 MeV, A = 16.37 +/- 4.03 GeV^-2, chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.029
* Au+Au at 200 AGeV, 00%-20% centrality, pure Transport: T_slope = 232.5 +/- 9.8 MeV, A = 1.65 +/- 0.48 GeV^-2, chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.041
* Au+Au at 62.4 AGeV, 00%-20% centrality, chi-EoS: T_slope = 261.8 +/- 8.2 MeV, A = 9.24 +/- 1.78 GeV^-2, chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.018
* Cu+Cu at 200 AGeV, 00%-20% centrality, chi-EoS: T_slope = 251.5 +/- 7.3 MeV, A = 1.77 +/- 0.39 GeV^-2, chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.033

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model calculations of direct photons by Bauchle and Bleicher (2010) to multiple beam energies and system sizes, comparing with PHENIX data from Adler et al. (2005) and Adare et al. (2010). It builds on earlier transport calculations by Dumitru et al. (1998) and thermal photon studies by Kapusta et al. (1991) and Turbide et al. (2004). The results enable systematic tests of QGP formation signatures across RHIC energies and provide a baseline for future viscous hydrodynamic calculations, as suggested by Dusling (2010).
