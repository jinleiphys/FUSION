# Nonlinear causality of Israel-Stewart theory with diffusion

> We present the first fully nonlinear causality constraints in $D = 3 + 1$ dimensions for Israel-Stewart theory in the presence of energy and number diffusion in the Eckart and Landau hydrodynamic frames, respectively. These constraints are algebraic inequalities that make no assumption on the underlying geometry of the spacetime or the equation of state. In order to highlight the distinct physical and structural behavior of the two hydrodynamic frames, we discuss the special ultrarelativistic ideal gas equation of state considered in earlier literature in $D = 1 + 1$ dimensions, and show that our general $D = 3 + 1$ constraints reduce to their results upon an appropriate choice of angles. For this equation of state in both $D = 1 + 1$ and $D = 3 + 1$ dimensions one can show that: (i) there exists a region allowed by nonlinear causality in which the baryon current transitions into a spacelike vector in the Landau frame, and (ii) an analogous argument shows that the solutions of the Eckart frame equations of motion never violate the dominant energy condition, assuming nonlinear causality holds. We then compare our results with those from linearized Israel-Stewart theory and show that the linear causality bounds fail to capture the new physical constraints on energy and number diffusion that are successfully obtained through our nonlinear causality approach.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully nonlinear causality constraints in 3+1 dimensions for Israel-Stewart theory with energy diffusion (Eckart frame) and number diffusion (Landau frame), showing that these algebraic inequalities make no assumption on spacetime geometry or equation of state, but that the linearized causality bounds fail to capture the new physical constraints on energy and number diffusion that the nonlinear approach successfully obtains.

## Method

The authors derived the characteristic determinant for the quasilinear system of conservation laws and Israel-Stewart relaxation equations in both the Landau frame (with baryon number diffusion current) and the Eckart frame (with energy/heat diffusion vector), treating the dissipative currents as independent dynamical degrees of freedom. They imposed causality by requiring that all characteristic vectors defined by the characteristic equation are nontimelike, which yields algebraic inequalities on the equilibrium thermodynamic quantities and the dissipative currents. The analysis was performed for general equations of state in 3+1 dimensions, and then specialized to the ultrarelativistic ideal gas equation of state to compare with prior 1+1 dimensional results from Hiscock (1989) and Hiscock and Lindblom (1988).

## Key numbers

* The characteristic determinant in the Landau frame reduces to a quartic polynomial P_4^{(L)}(x̂;ψ) with coefficients involving the speed of sound c_s, the diffusion coefficient κ, the relaxation time τ_J, and the chemical potential-to-temperature ratio Θ = μ/T.
* For the ultrarelativistic ideal gas in the Landau frame, the 1+1 dimensional constraints from Hiscock (1989) are both necessary and sufficient for the 3+1 dimensional nonlinear causality conditions.
* In the Eckart frame, the causality bounds involve an order 5 polynomial that cannot be solved analytically in general, requiring numerical investigation for a complete specification of the causal region.
* The linear causality bounds fail to capture the new physical constraints on energy and number diffusion that are obtained through the nonlinear causality analysis.

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear causality analysis of Israel-Stewart theory beyond the case of bulk viscosity studied by Bemfica, Disconzi, and Noronha (2019) and the case of shear and bulk viscosity at vanishing chemical potential studied by Bemfica, Disconzi, and Noronha (2020). It complements those analyses by focusing on the diffusive sector, which introduces distinct structural features in the characteristic determinant. The results recover and generalize prior 1+1 dimensional analyses by Hiscock (1989) and Hiscock and Lindblom (1988) for an ultrarelativistic ideal gas. The nonlinear causality constraints directly limit the size of both equilibrium and dissipative dynamics, affecting the choice of initial data and subsequent evolution in simulations of the quark-gluon plasma and neutron star mergers.
