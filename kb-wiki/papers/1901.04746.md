# Sensitivity to sterile neutrino mixing using reactor antineutrinos

> The reactor antineutrinos are used for the precise measurement of oscillation parameters in the 3-neutrino model, and also used to investigate active-sterile neutrino mixing sensitivity in the 3$+$1 neutrino framework. In the present work, we study the feasibility of sterile neutrino search with the Indian Scintillator Matrix for Reactor Anti-Neutrino (ISMRAN) experimental set-up using electron antineutrinos ($\overlineν_e$) produced from reactor as a source. The so-called 3$+$1 scenario is considered for active-sterile neutrino mixing, which leads to projected exclusion curves in the sterile neutrino mass and mixing angle plane. The analysis is performed considering both the reactor and detector related parameters. It is found that, the ISMRAN set-up can observe the active-sterile neutrino mixing sensitivity for $\sin^{2}2θ_{14} \geq$ 0.064 and $Δm^{2}_{41}$ = 1.0 eV$^2$ at 90$\%$ confidence level for an exposure of 1 ton-year by using neutrinos produced from the DHRUVA reactor with thermal power of 100 MW$_{th}$. It is also observed that, there is a significant improvement of the active-sterile neutrino mixing parameter $\sin^{2}2θ_{14}$ to $\sim$ 0.03 at the same $Δm^{2}_{41}$ by putting the ISMRAN detector set-up at a distance of 20 m from the compact proto-type fast breeder reactor (PFBR) facility with thermal power of 1250 MW$_{th}$.

## Key claim

The ISMRAN experimental set-up can achieve sensitivity to sterile neutrino mixing for \(\sin^{2}2\theta_{14} \geq 0.064\) at \(\Delta m^{2}_{41} = 1.0\) eV\(^2\) at 90% confidence level with 1 ton-year exposure at the DHRUVA reactor (100 MW\(_{th}\)), but this sensitivity improves to \(\sin^{2}2\theta_{14} \sim 0.03\) at the same \(\Delta m^{2}_{41}\) if the detector is placed 20 m from the higher-power PFBR facility (1250 MW\(_{th}\)), indicating that the projected reach depends strongly on reactor power and baseline.

## Method

The authors simulated the expected event rate of electron antineutrinos detected via inverse beta decay in the ISMRAN plastic scintillator array, assuming a 3+1 sterile neutrino model. They incorporated a parametrized reactor antineutrino flux from \(^{235}\)U, \(^{239}\)Pu, \(^{241}\)Pu, and \(^{238}\)U, a Gaussian detector energy resolution of \(\sigma/E \sim 20\%/\sqrt{E}\), and a Monte-Carlo treatment of the extended reactor core geometry. A \(\chi^2\) analysis with pull terms for systematic uncertainties (3% normalization, 1% nonlinear energy response, 0.5% energy calibration, and 2% bin-to-bin uncorrelated error) was used to derive 90% C.L. exclusion contours in the \(\Delta m^{2}_{41}\)–\(\sin^{2}2\theta_{14}\) plane for a 1 ton-year exposure.

## Key numbers

- For the DHRUVA reactor (100 MW\(_{th}\), extended core, 13 m baseline, 70% duty cycle, 25% detection efficiency, \(\sigma/E = 20\%/\sqrt{E}\)), the 90% C.L. sensitivity reaches \(\sin^{2}2\theta_{14} \geq 0.064\) at \(\Delta m^{2}_{41} = 1.0\) eV\(^2\).
- For the PFBR facility (1250 MW\(_{th}\), compact core, 20 m baseline), the sensitivity improves to \(\sin^{2}2\theta_{14} \sim 0.03\) at the same \(\Delta m^{2}_{41} = 1.0\) eV\(^2\).
- Reducing the baseline from 13 m to 7 m at the DHRUVA reactor improves the sensitivity on \(\sin^{2}2\theta_{14}\) by \(\sim 53\%\) at \(\Delta m^{2}_{41} = 1.0\) eV\(^2\).
- Improving the detector energy resolution from \(\sigma/E = 25\%/\sqrt{E}\) to \(5\%/\sqrt{E}\) improves the sensitivity on \(\sin^{2}2\theta_{14}\) by \(\sim 23\%\) at \(\Delta m^{2}_{41} = 1.0\) eV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the search for sterile neutrinos motivated by the reactor antineutrino anomaly (Mention 2011) and the Gallium anomaly (Abdurashitov 2005), and it is placed in the context of short-baseline experiments such as DANSS (Alekseev 2018), STEREO (Almazan 2018), and PROSPECT (Ashenfelter 2018), which have already constrained the parameter space. The results enable a quantitative comparison of the ISMRAN set-up's potential at two Indian reactor facilities (DHRUVA and PFBR) and provide guidance for optimizing the detector baseline and exposure to maximize sensitivity to the 3+1 sterile neutrino mixing parameters.
