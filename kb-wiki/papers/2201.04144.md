# Dressed quark-gluon vertex form factors from gauge symmetry

> We present preliminary results on the longitudinal $and$ transverse form factors of the quark-gluon vertex as functions of the incoming and outgoing quark momenta and an angle $θ=2π/3$ between them. The expressions for these form factors were previously derived from Slavnov-Taylor identities, gauge covariance and multiplicative renormalizability that firmly constrain the fermion-boson vertex.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary results for the longitudinal and transverse form factors of the dressed quark-gluon vertex, derived from Slavnov-Taylor identities and multiplicative renormalizability, but only for a single kinematic configuration where the angle between incoming and outgoing quark momenta is fixed at θ = 2π/3.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) in Euclidean space using lattice QCD input for the gluon and ghost dressing functions, with the quark-gluon vertex defined by previously derived expressions for its twelve form factors (four longitudinal λ_i and eight transverse τ_i). The solution for the quark propagator's scalar and vector components, A(p²) and B(p²), is then used to compute the vertex form factors as functions of the incoming and outgoing quark momenta squared.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic configuration studied has angle θ = 2π/3 between incoming and outgoing quark momenta, corresponding to cos θ = -1/2
- The form factors are presented as functions of momenta squared k² and p² in units of GeV²
- The ghost-dressing function G(q²) is renormalized such that G(μ²) = 1 at the renormalization scale μ
- The dominant contributions to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking from the transverse vertex come from form factors τ₄(k,p) and τ₇(k,p)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Serna 2018, Albino 2021) that derived the transverse quark-gluon vertex form factors from transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities but did not present numerical figures of those form factors. The authors note that their longitudinal form factors λ_i show similar functional forms to those found by Aguilar (2016, 2018) but with differences in magnitude, as those earlier studies concentrated exclusively on the longitudinal vertex. A more comprehensive treatment including other angles (such as the soft-gluon and symmetric quark limits) and sub-leading form factors of the quark-ghost scattering amplitude is stated to be underway.
