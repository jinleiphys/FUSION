# A relativistic two-nucleon model for $A(p,K^+){_Λ}B$ reaction

> We investigate the $A(p,K^+){_Λ}B$ reaction within a covariant two nucleon model. We focus on amplitudes which are described by creation, propagation and decay into relevant channel of $N^*$(1650), $N^*$(1710), and $N^*$(1720) intermediate baryonic resonance states in the initial collision of the projectile nucleon with one of its target counterparts. This collision is modeled by the exchange of $π$ as well as $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons. The bound state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with appropriate scalar and vector potentials. Expressions for the reaction amplitudes are derived taking continuum particle wave functions in both distorted wave and plane wave approximations. Detailed numerical results are presented in the plane wave approximation; estimates of the effects of the initial and final state interactions are given in the eikonal approximation. Cross sections of 1 - 2 nb/sr at the peak positions, are obtained for favored transitions in case of heavier targets.

## Key claim

The authors present a fully covariant two-nucleon model for the \(A(p,K^+){_\Lambda}B\) reaction, claiming that cross sections of 1–2 nb/sr at peak positions are obtained for favored transitions in heavier targets, but only within the plane wave approximation; estimates of initial and final state interaction effects are given only in the eikonal approximation, and the full distorted wave treatment is deferred as numerically too demanding for this exploratory study.

## Method

The reaction is modeled as a two-nucleon process where the incident proton collides with a bound target nucleon via exchange of \(\pi\), \(\rho\), and \(\omega\) mesons, exciting intermediate baryonic resonances \(N^*(1650)\), \(N^*(1710)\), and \(N^*(1720)\) which decay into a kaon and a \(\Lambda\) hyperon. Bound state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with scalar and vector potentials, and reaction amplitudes are derived using both plane wave and distorted wave approximations, though detailed numerical results are presented only in the plane wave approximation with distortion estimates from the eikonal approximation. The model uses the same effective Lagrangians and coupling constants as those previously employed to describe the elementary \(pp \to pK^+\Lambda\) reaction.

## Key numbers

- Threshold laboratory beam energy for the \((p,K^+)\) reaction: 0.739 GeV for \(^{12}\)C target and 0.602 GeV for \(^{40}\)Ca target, considerably lower than the 1.582 GeV threshold for the elementary \(pp \to pK^+\Lambda\) reaction.
- Momentum transfer to the residual nucleus ranges from 1 GeV/c to 5 GeV/c for the \((p,K^+)\) reaction, compared to only 0.33 GeV/c at 0° for the \((\pi^+,K^+)\) reaction.
- Cross sections of 1–2 nb/sr at peak positions are obtained for favored transitions in heavier targets within the plane wave approximation.
- Cutoff parameters for the resonance-nucleon-meson form factors are taken as 1.2 GeV in all cases.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic two-nucleon model calculations of the \(A(p,K^+){_\Lambda}B\) reaction by Shyam (1986), Komarov (1995), and Krimer (1995) into a fully covariant framework, addressing the inadequacy of non-relativistic treatments at momentum transfers above 400 MeV/c where the lower component of the Dirac spinor becomes significant. The model builds on the authors' previous effective Lagrangian description of the elementary \(pp \to pK^+\Lambda\) reaction (Shyam 1999, 2001, 2004a) and includes \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) exchange not present in their earlier \(^{40}\)Ca study (Shyam 2004). The results are intended to guide planning of future experiments at the COSY facility, and the approach enables comparison with hypernuclear states populated via the \((\pi^+,K^+)\) reaction, which involves much smaller momentum transfer.
