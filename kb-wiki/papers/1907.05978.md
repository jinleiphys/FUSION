# Finite volume effects on quarkonium dissociation temperature in an impenetrable QGP sphere

> The system of a quarkonium confined by an impenetrable spherical cavity filled with a hot quantum chromodynamics (QCD) medium is studied by solving the Schrödinger equation. This is the first time this issue has been raised for discussion. The Schrödinger equation with an appropriate boundary condition of a quarkonium in an impenetrable cavity filled with a hot medium is derived. The numerical results are obtained with the help of Gaussian Expansion Method. Binding energies and radii of the ground and low-excited states are obtained as a function of the medium temperature and the cavity radius. We find the behaviour of quarkonium in this cavity is different from that in infinite space. Our results show that the quarkonium dissociation temperature decreases as the cavity radius decreases and the finite volume effects on the ground state are more obvious than on the excited states. We also find that the less mass of the constituents and the bigger radius of the quarkonium lead the finite volume effects to become more obvious.

## Key claim

The dissociation temperature of quarkonium decreases as the radius of the confining spherical cavity (representing a finite-size QGP fireball) decreases, and this finite-volume effect is more pronounced for ground states than for excited states, and also more pronounced for lighter constituent quarks and larger quarkonium radii.

## Method

The authors model a quarkonium system confined in an impenetrable spherical cavity filled with a hot QCD medium, using a non-relativistic Schrödinger equation with a temperature-dependent interquark potential derived from lattice QCD free energy via the internal energy prescription (V = F + TS). They solve the two-body Schrödinger equation in independent coordinates r1 and r2 (since center-of-mass separation is invalid due to broken translational invariance) using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) with basis functions that vanish at the cavity boundary. Binding energies and average radii are computed as functions of temperature and cavity radius for two different definitions of the dissociation limit (V(∞,T) and V(2r0,T)).

## Key numbers

- Charmonium 1S (J/ψ) dissociation temperature in infinite space: Td/Tc = 2.06; at cavity radius r0 = 1 fm: Td/Tc = 1.52 (first limit); at r0 = 0.8 fm: Td/Tc = 1.36 (first limit)
- Charmonium 2S (ψ') dissociation temperature in infinite space: Td/Tc = 1.13; at r0 = 1 fm: Td/Tc = 1.107 (first limit); at r0 = 0.8 fm: Td/Tc = 1.098 (first limit)
- Bottomonium 1S (Υ) dissociation temperature: > 2.2 Tc for all cavity radii studied (limited by lattice data range 0.8Tc to 2Tc)
- Bottomonium 2S (Υ') dissociation temperature in infinite space: Td/Tc = 1.56; at r0 = 1 fm: Td/Tc = 1.30 (first limit); at r0 = 0.8 fm: Td/Tc = 1.26 (first limit)
- Bottomonium 3S (Υ'') dissociation temperature in infinite space: Td/Tc = 1.17; at r0 = 0.8 fm: Td/Tc = 1.142 (first limit)
- Charm quark mass: mc = 1.25 GeV; bottom quark mass: mb = 4.65 GeV; string tension √σ = 0.445 GeV; gauge coupling α = π/12
- Basis parameters: nmax = 20, b1 = 0.1 fm, b_{nmax} = 2r0

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of quarkonium dissociation in infinite space (Cheng 2018) by introducing a finite-volume model motivated by the fact that QGP fireballs produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions have finite size, especially at early times. The model adapts techniques developed for hydrogen atoms confined in impenetrable spherical cavities, first studied by Michels (1937) and later refined by the same group (2019), to the quarkonium problem. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations of the free energy at higher temperatures (Bazavov 2018) and may help interpret quarkonium suppression data from RHIC and LHC experiments by accounting for finite fireball volume effects.
