# Predictions of the Generalized Glauber Model for the coherent $ρ$-production at RHIC and the STAR data

> We calculate the rapidity distribution and the total cross section of coherent and incoherent $ρ$-production in the heavy ion ultraperipheral collisions (UPC) at $\surd s_{NN}=130$ GeV using the generalized vector dominance model(GVDM) and the Gribov- Glauber approach. We find the coherent cross section of $ρ$-production $σ_{coh}=490$ mb compared to the $σ_{coh}=370\pm 170\pm 80$ mb recently reported by the STAR collaboration at RHIC. The predicted cross section inside the acceptance of the experiment, $|y|\le 1$, agrees with the data. It is emphasized that measurements of the rapidity distribution will provide a much more stringent test of the model.

## Key claim

The generalized Glauber model (GGM) combined with the generalized vector dominance model (GVDM) predicts a coherent rho-production cross section of 490 mb in gold-gold ultraperipheral collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV, which agrees with the STAR measurement of 370 +/- 170 +/- 80 mb within the experimental uncertainties, but only after accounting for the STAR momentum-transfer cut and the rapidity acceptance.

## Method

The authors calculated the rapidity distribution and total cross section for coherent and incoherent rho-meson production in gold-gold ultraperipheral collisions using the Weizsacker-Williams approximation for the equivalent photon flux and the Gribov-Glauber approach for the gamma-A to rho-A cross section. The nuclear density was described by the Hartree-Fock-Skyrme model, and the elementary scattering amplitudes were fixed using the Landshoff-Donnachie parameterization with a two-channel GVDM that includes an effective rho-prime meson to account for higher-mass hadronic states. The model parameters were previously fitted to rho-meson photoproduction data off lead at 6.2 GeV and then applied without further adjustment to the RHIC energy.

## Key numbers

- Coherent rho-production total cross section predicted by the model: sigma_coh = 490 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV, compared to the STAR measured value sigma_coh = 370 +/- 170 +/- 80 mb.
- After applying the STAR momentum-transfer cut (t_perp <= 0.02 GeV^2), the predicted cross section is reduced by approximately 10% from the total.
- The predicted cross section within the STAR rapidity acceptance (|y| <= 1) is sigma = 170 mb, compared to the STAR value of 140 +/- 60 +/- 30 mb.
- The ratio of the total cross section to that within |y| <= 1 is R = 3.2 in the model, versus R = 2.7 used in the STAR Monte-Carlo extrapolation.
- The total incoherent rho-production cross section is sigma_inc = 120 mb, with sigma_inc = 14 mb in the low t_perp region (t_perp <= 0.02 GeV^2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier predictions for rho-production at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (Frankfurt, Strikman, Zhalov, 2002) to the lower energy of 130 GeV to enable direct comparison with the first STAR data. The model differs significantly from the earlier approach of Klein and collaborators (1999), which the authors argue underestimated the cross section by a factor of approximately 2.5 due to using a classical mechanics expression for the total rho-A cross section instead of the quantum-mechanical Gribov-Glauber formula, and by an additional factor of approximately 2 due to neglecting coherence length effects at this energy. The results demonstrate that the GGM provides a reliable parameter-free description of coherent rho-production in ultraperipheral collisions, and the authors emphasize that future measurements of the rapidity distribution will provide a more stringent test of the model.
