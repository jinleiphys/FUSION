# Sterile neutrinos and supernova nucleosynthesis

> The role of the sterile neutrinos to the r-process nucleosynthesis in supernova explosions is studied. Previously it has been argued that a large part of neutrino mixing can be excluded if the supernovae are the origin of the heavy elements. It is shown that a conversion to sterile neutrinos may evade those limits. The possibility that such conversions can enhance the nucleosynthesis is investigated. The desired mass spectrum is consistent with neutrino masses suggested by other observed phenomena, like the solar neutrino problem, the atmospheric neurino problem, dark matter and the LSND signals.

# Sterile neutrinos and supernova nucleosynthesis

## Key claim
The paper shows that conversion of active neutrinos to sterile neutrinos can evade previously established limits on neutrino mixing derived from r-process nucleosynthesis in supernovae, and may even enhance nucleosynthesis. However, the author notes that a full numerical simulation has not yet been performed, so the quantitative impact remains uncertain.

## Method
The author analytically derives the matter-induced effective potentials for neutrino oscillations in the supernova environment, including sterile neutrinos, and compares the resonance conditions for different transition channels (ν_e → ν_μ, ν_e → ν_S, ν_μ → ν_S). The neutron fraction Y_n is related to the neutrino energy fluxes and average energies, and the effect of flavor conversions on the proton-to-neutron ratio is evaluated using the hierarchy of potential differences in the region outside the core where 0.35 < Y_e < 0.5.

## Key numbers
- Average energies assumed: ⟨E_ν_e⟩ ∼ 11 MeV and ⟨E_ν̅_e⟩ ∼ 16 MeV, yielding Y_n ≃ 0.6 for the standard case without oscillations
- Muon and tau neutrinos have average energies above 20 MeV due to emission from deeper regions of the star
- For the transition ν_μ → ν_S to be adiabatic with neutrino masses in the dark matter range, the mixing angle must satisfy sin² 2θ_μ > 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻³
- To avoid overlap of resonance zones, the largest mixing angle β must satisfy sin² 2β < 0.02
- Light neutrinos with mass m_ν < 10 eV cannot trivially satisfy adiabaticity requirements for the ν_e → ν_S transition

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Qian et al. (1993), who derived forbidden ranges in the neutrino mixing plane from r-process requirements, and addresses the contradiction noted by Fuller, Primack, and Qian (1995) regarding inverted mass spectra. The paper builds on earlier work by Kainulainen, Maalampi, and Peltoniemi (1991) on sterile neutrino production in supernova cores. It enables future numerical simulations of r-process nucleosynthesis including sterile neutrino conversions, and suggests a mass spectrum consistent with the solar neutrino problem, atmospheric neutrino problem, dark matter, and the LSND signal, while noting that cosmological limits from Enqvist, Maalampi, and Semikoz (1995) may be relaxed due to uncertainties in the effective neutrino number constraint.
