# Comparison of Nucleon Form Factors from Lattice QCD Against the Light Front Cloudy Bag Model and Extrapolation to the Physical Mass Regime

> We explore the possibility of extrapolating state of the art lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors to the physical regime. We find that the lattice results can be reproduced using the Light Front Cloudy Bag Model by letting its parameters be analytic functions of the quark mass. We then use the model to extend the lattice calculations to large values of Q^{2} of interest to current and planned experiments. These functions are also used to define extrapolations to the physical value of the pion mass, thereby allowing us to study how the predicted zero in G_{E}(Q^{2})/G_{M}(Q^{2}) varies as a function of quark mass.

## Key claim

The Light Front Cloudy Bag Model (LFCBM) can reproduce lattice QCD results for nucleon electromagnetic form factors when its parameters are treated as analytic functions of the quark mass, enabling extrapolation to the physical pion mass regime and to large momentum transfers beyond the range of current lattice calculations.

## Method

The authors fit the LFCBM to lattice QCD data from the QCDSF Collaboration for three lattice spacings (a = 0.47, 0.34, 0.26 GeV^-1) and multiple pion masses between 0.58 and 1.24 GeV. Two parameters were varied: the chiral-limit constituent quark mass M_chi (varied per lattice spacing) and the wave function exponent gamma (varied per pion mass), while the pion-nucleon cutoff Lambda and the wave function scale beta were held fixed. The fitted parameters were then extrapolated to the physical pion mass using low-order polynomial functions of m_pi^2, and the resulting model was used to compute form factors at arbitrarily large Q^2.

## Key numbers

* Lattice data covered pion masses from 0.58 GeV to 1.24 GeV, with corresponding nucleon masses from approximately 1.38 GeV to 2.20 GeV, and Q^2 values from 0.6 GeV^2 to 2.3 GeV^2.
* Best-fit M_chi values were 0.390(5) GeV for a = 0.47 GeV^-1, 0.280(5) GeV for a = 0.34 GeV^-1, and 0.210(5) GeV for a = 0.26 GeV^-1.
* Extrapolated gamma values at physical pion mass were -4.79(46) for a = 0.47 GeV^-1, -2.91(29) for a = 0.34 GeV^-1, and -2.41(22) for a = 0.26 GeV^-1.
* The rho meson mass was parameterized as m_rho = 0.776 GeV + 0.427 GeV^-1 * m_pi^2.
* The renormalized pi-N coupling constant was fixed at g^2/4pi = 13.5.

## Context

This work extends the LFCBM approach of Miller (2002) to describe lattice QCD data from the QCDSF Collaboration, building on earlier chiral extrapolation studies of baryon magnetic moments by Leinweber (1998), Hackett-Jones (2000), Hemmert (2002), and Young (2004). The method enables extrapolation of lattice form factor calculations to the physical pion mass and to large Q^2 values relevant for experiments at Jefferson Lab, while also allowing study of how the zero in G_E/G_M varies with quark mass. The strong dependence of M_chi on lattice spacing suggests that only results from the smallest lattice spacing (a = 0.26 GeV^-1) should be compared with experimental data, motivating future calculations with improved actions or smaller lattice spacings.
