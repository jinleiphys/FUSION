# Estimation of electric field in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We estimate the spacetime profile of the electric field in head-on heavy-ion collisions at intermediate collision energies $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = {\mathcal O}(3 \;-\; 10\;{\rm GeV})$. Using a hadronic cascade model (JAM; Jet AA Microscopic transport model), we numerically demonstrate that the produced field has strength $eE = {\mathcal O}((30 \;-\; 60\;{\rm MeV})^2)$, which is supercritical to the Schwinger limit of QED and is non-negligibly large compared even to the hadron/QCD scale, and survives for a long time $τ= {\mathcal O}(10\;{\rm fm}/c)$ due to the baryon stopping. We show that the produced field is nonperturbatively strong in the sense that the nonperturbativity parameters (e.g., the Keldysh parameter) are sufficiently large. This is in contrast to high-energy collisions $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \gtrsim 100 \;{\rm GeV}$, where the field is extremely short-lived and hence is perturbative. Our results imply that the electromagnetic field may have phenomenological impacts on hadronic/QCD processes in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions and that heavy-ion collisions can be used as a new tool to explore strong-field physics in the nonperturbative regime.

## Key claim

The authors claim that intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions (√sNN = O(3–10 GeV)) produce an electric field that is both supercritical to the Schwinger limit and long-lived enough to be nonperturbative, in contrast to high-energy collisions where the field is extremely short-lived and therefore perturbative. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on the hadronic cascade model JAM with default settings, and that the field is estimated for head-on collisions only.

## Method

The authors used the hadronic cascade model JAM (Jet AA Microscopic transport model) to simulate head-on (impact parameter b = 0 fm) collisions of gold ions at eleven collision energies between √sNN = 2.4 and 11.5 GeV. They computed the electric current from the JAM phase-space distributions of hadrons, smeared the charge density with a Gaussian width σ = 1 fm, and then calculated the retarded vector potential and the resulting electromagnetic fields via numerical integration over 100 events per energy.

## Key numbers

- The electric field strength is eE = O((30–60 MeV)^2), which is supercritical to the Schwinger limit eE_cr = (0.511 MeV)^2.
- The field lifetime is τ = O(10 fm/c), due to baryon stopping.
- The charge density reaches ρ/e = O(0.5 fm^{-3}), which is about 5 times greater than that of a gold ion at rest (ρ_0/2 ≈ 0.08 fm^{-3}).
- At √sNN = 3.0 GeV, a relatively dense charge state ρ/e ≳ 0.1 fm^{-3} appears around t ≈ 2 fm/c and survives until t ≈ 18 fm/c, giving a lifetime τ ≈ 16 fm/c, much longer than the naive Bjorken estimate of τ ≈ 9 fm/c.
- The maximum charge density is roughly estimated as max ρ/e ≈ (0.08 fm^{-3}) × (√sNN/1 GeV), and the time of maximum as t_max ≈ (26 fm/c)/(√sNN/1 GeV).

## Context

This work extends studies of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions from low energies (where the Landau picture gives eE ∼ O((20 MeV)^2) with long lifetime) and high energies (where the Bjorken picture gives eE = O(100–1000 MeV) but lifetime τ ∼ 0.1–0.01 fm/c) into the previously unexplored intermediate-energy regime. The authors contrast their results with high-energy collisions at RHIC/LHC (√sNN ≳ 100 GeV) where the field is perturbative, as demonstrated by observations of light-by-light scattering (ATLAS 2017) and linear Breit-Wheeler pair production (STAR 2019) that agree with perturbative QED. This work enables future studies of nonperturbative strong-field QED processes using heavy-ion collisions as a novel tool, and suggests that electromagnetic fields may have phenomenological impacts on hadronic/QCD observables such as di-lepton yields in intermediate-energy collisions.
