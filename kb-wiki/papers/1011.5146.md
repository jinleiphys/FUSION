# The extended coupled cluster method and the pairing problem

> We study the application of various forms of the coupled cluster method to systems with paired fermions. The novel element of the analysis is the study of the breaking and eventual restoration of particle number in the CCM variants. We specifically include Arponen's extended coupled cluster method, which describes the normal Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean field at lowest level of truncation. We show that all methods converge to the exact results as we increase the order of truncation, but that the breaking of particle number at an intermediate level means that this convergence occurs in a surprising way. We argue that the most straightforward form of the method seems to be the most stable approach to implement for realistic (large number of particles) pairing problems

## Key claim

The paper shows that all variants of the coupled cluster method converge to exact results for pairing problems as the truncation order increases, but the breaking of particle number at intermediate truncation levels causes this convergence to occur in a surprising, non-smooth way; the authors argue that the most straightforward form (normal CCM) appears to be the most stable approach for realistic large-number-of-particles pairing problems.

## Method

The authors apply normal CCM (NCCM), extended CCM (ECCM) as formulated by Arponen, and a quasiparticle-based Brueckner orbital approach to the single-shell pairing model, which uses the SU(2) quasispin algebra. They analyze the SUB(1) through SUB(4) truncation levels for the particle-based ECCM and SUB(2) for the quasiparticle-based NCCM and ECCM, solving the coupled polynomial equations for the cluster amplitudes and computing ground-state energies and particle-number fluctuations. The reference state is taken as either the empty state (particle operators) or the fully occupied state (hole operators), and the calculations are performed for various degeneracies Omega and particle numbers.

## Key numbers

- For the single-shell pairing model, the exact ground-state energy for even particle number N is E_N = -G (Omega - N/2) (N/2).
- The SUB(1) ECCM (equivalent to BCS) ground-state energy is E = -G (N_0/2) ((Omega-1)/Omega) (Omega - N_0/2), which differs from the exact result by a correction of relative order 1/Omega.
- For Omega=4, SUB(4) ECCM yields exactly four branches of solutions, with each physical solution lying on a different branch; the green branch is exact at half filling (N=4).
- For Omega=10, the authors compare energy differences Delta E = E - E_exact as a function of average particle number for different truncation levels using particle-based ECCM.
- The quasiparticle NCCM and ECCM at SUB(2) level show the physically correct solution (turning to E=0 for <N>=0) collapsing at mid-shell, where Delta N^2 goes negative.

## Context

This work extends the limited prior applications of CCM to pairing forces, notably the forgotten work of Emrich and Zabolitzky (1984), and builds on Arponen's extended CCM formalism (1983) and the subsequent work by Arponen and Bishop (1987-1990). The study uses the single-shell pairing model from Ring and Schuck's textbook as a testbed. The results enable systematic assessment of which CCM variant is most suitable for realistic nuclear pairing problems with large particle numbers, and highlight the need to understand the non-smooth convergence behavior caused by particle-number breaking at intermediate truncation levels.
