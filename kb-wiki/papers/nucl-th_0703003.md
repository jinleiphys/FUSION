# Superscaling in dilute Fermi gas and its relation to general properties of the nucleon momentum distribution in nuclei

> The superscaling observed in inclusive electron scattering is described within the dilute Fermi gas model with interaction between the particles. The comparison with the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model without interaction shows an improvement in the explanation of the scaling function $f(ψ')$ in the region $ψ' < -1$, where the RFG result is $f(ψ') = 0$. It is found that the behavior of $f(ψ')$ for $ψ' < -1$ depends on the particular form of the general power-law asymptotics of the momentum distribution $n(k)\sim 1/ k^{4+m}$ at large $k$. The best agreement with the empirical scaling function is found for $m\simeq 4.5$ in agreement with the asymptotics of $n(k)$ in the coherent density fluctuation model where $m = 4$. Thus, superscaling gives information about the asymptotics of $n(k)$ and the NN forces.

## Key claim

The superscaling observed in inclusive electron scattering can be described by a dilute Fermi gas model with interactions, and the behavior of the scaling function for scaling variable psi' less than -1 depends on the particular power-law asymptotics of the nucleon momentum distribution n(k) at large k, with optimal agreement found for n(k) approximately proportional to 1/k^{8.5}.

## Method

The authors used the hard-sphere dilute Fermi gas (HSDFG) model with a repulsive hard-core potential to calculate the nucleon momentum distribution n(k) and the corresponding scaling function f(psi'). They then replaced the high-momentum part of n(k) (for k greater than k_F) with a general power-law form n(k) proportional to 1/k^{4+m} and determined the value of m that best reproduces the experimental scaling function from inclusive quasielastic electron scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The hard-sphere dilute Fermi gas model uses k_F c = 0.70, corresponding to a core radius c = 0.50 fm and Fermi momentum k_F = 1.40 fm^{-1}
- The optimal fit to the experimental scaling function gives m approximately 4.5 in the power-law asymptotics n(k) proportional to 1/k^{4+m}, yielding n(k) approximately proportional to 1/k^{8.5}
- The coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) gives n(k) proportional to 1/k^8, corresponding to m = 4
- The scaling function for the optimal case follows f(psi') = 0.12 * ((1+m)/(2+m)) * 1/|psi'|^{2+m}

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model of superscaling introduced by Barbaro et al. (1998) and Donnelly and Sick (1999), which gives f(psi') = 0 for psi' less than -1, by showing that an interacting dilute Fermi gas can reproduce the nonzero scaling function observed experimentally in that region. The results are consistent with the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) of Antonov et al. (1988) and with the general power-law asymptotics of n(k) derived by Amado and Woloshyn (1976, 1977). The work enables extraction of information about nucleon-nucleon forces in the nuclear medium from inclusive electron scattering data.
