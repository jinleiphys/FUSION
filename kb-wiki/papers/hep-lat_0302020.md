# Physical Nucleon Properties from Lattice QCD

> We demonstrate that the extremely accurate lattice QCD data for the mass of the nucleon recently obtained by CP-PACS, combined with modern chiral extrapolation techniques, leads to a value for the mass of the physical nucleon which has a systematic error of less than one percent.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a finite-range regulator (FRR) formulation of chiral perturbation theory to extrapolate CP-PACS lattice QCD data for the nucleon mass yields a physical nucleon mass with a systematic error of less than one percent, provided the extrapolation is carried out over a pion mass range where the FRR expansion converges. The abstract's claim of "systematic error of less than one percent" is conditioned on the use of FRR rather than dimensional regularization, which the authors show does not converge over the required mass range.

## Method

The authors fit four-parameter chiral extrapolation formulas to CP-PACS lattice QCD data for the nucleon mass, using either dimensional regularization (with and without the Delta-N pi branch point) or finite-range regularization with four different regulator shapes (sharp cutoff, monopole, dipole, Gaussian). The loop integrals for the pion self-energy contributions are evaluated in the heavy baryon limit, and the regulator parameter Lambda is treated as a free parameter in the fit. The convergence of each expansion is assessed by examining the size and sign of the fitted coefficients, and the extrapolated nucleon mass at the physical pion mass is extracted with statistical error bands determined by chi-squared analysis.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass from dipole regulator fit: 0.951 GeV, with statistical error +0.081/-0.217 GeV
- Nucleon mass from monopole regulator fit: 0.953 GeV, with statistical error +0.063/-0.219 GeV
- Nucleon mass from Gaussian regulator fit: 0.948 GeV, with statistical error +0.080/-0.216 GeV
- Nucleon mass from dimensional regularization (with branch point): 0.823 GeV, with statistical error +/-0.122 GeV
- Sigma commutator from dipole regulator fit: 36 MeV, with statistical error +35/-13 MeV
- Lightest simulated pion mass squared: 0.27 GeV^2, compared to physical value 0.02 GeV^2
- Regulator mass Lambda for dipole fit: 0.732 GeV
- Regulator mass Lambda for monopole fit: 0.443 GeV
- Regulator mass Lambda for Gaussian fit: 0.593 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral extrapolation studies by Leinweber (2000) and resolves a debate between Bernard (2002) and others about whether finite-range regulators introduce significant model dependence. The authors build on the demonstration by Young (2002) that all smooth FRR schemes give equivalent results over the range m_pi^2 less than 0.8 GeV^2, and on the work of Donoghue (1998) showing the advantages of physical regularization schemes. The results enable future high-precision lattice QCD simulations at lower pion masses (m_pi^2 around 0.1 GeV^2) to reduce the current large statistical errors on the extrapolated nucleon mass and sigma commutator.
