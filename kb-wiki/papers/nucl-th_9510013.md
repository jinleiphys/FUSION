# Microscopic study of superdeformation in the A=150 mass region

> The cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method presented in previous studies of superdeformed bands in Hg and Pb isotopes is applied to the study of superdeformed bands in Gd150, Dy152, Dy151 and Tb151. The same density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction used in the A=190 mass region leads to a similarly good agreement with the experimental data. In particular, our results confirm the importance of a correct treatment of pairing correlations in Gd150 to reproduce its experimental dynamical moment of inertia. The quasi-particle spectra obtained for all the nuclei studied here are compatible with the experimental results obtained for excited bands in this mass region. The quality of our results opens the possibility of studying microscopically very subtle phenomena like the properties of identical bands.

## Key claim

The cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction, previously successful for superdeformed bands in the A=190 mass region, also yields good agreement with experimental data for superdeformed bands in the A=150 mass region, but only for 150Gd does pairing play a crucial role; for other nuclei in this region, pairing correlations are much less important, though they still affect the magnitude of the dynamical moment of inertia even at the highest observed spins.

## Method

The authors performed cranked Hartree-Fock (HF) and cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SkM* Skyrme force for the particle-hole channel. In the particle-particle channel, they tested three treatments: no pairing, a seniority (monopole) interaction with two different strengths, and a density-dependent zero-range interaction previously adjusted on 194Pb. The Lipkin-Nogami prescription was used to treat pairing correlations dynamically, and calculations were performed for the yrast superdeformed bands of 150Gd, 152Dy, 151Dy, and 151Tb, as well as several excited bands.

## Key numbers

- For 150Gd, transition energies were reproduced within 10 keV over more than 10 transitions in the four isotopes 190Hg, 192Hg, 194Hg, and 194Pb (from the prior work this method extends).
- The charge quadrupole moment obtained for 150Gd is 16 eb, close to the measured value of 15.6±0.3 eb for band 2 of 149Gd.
- The charge quadrupole moment obtained for 152Dy is 17.5 eb, close to the measured value for the lowest band of 152Dy.
- The charge quadrupole moment predicted for 151Dy is 17 eb, smaller than the 18 eb obtained in the prior work of Nazarewicz et al. (1989).
- For 151Tb, at rotational frequency around 0.7 MeV, the excitation energies of bands 2 and 3 with respect to band 1 are approximately 1.1 MeV and 0.8 MeV, respectively.
- The interaction strength between the [770]1/2 and [761]3/2 quasiparticles in 150Gd is approximately 0.65 MeV for the density-dependent zero-range interaction.

## Context

This work extends the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method previously applied to superdeformed bands in Hg and Pb isotopes (Gal et al., 1994; Heenen et al., 1995; Terasaki et al., 1995) to the A=150 mass region. It confirms the importance of pairing correlations in 150Gd as suggested by earlier studies using the Nilsson-Strutinsky approximation (Bengtsson et al., 1988), the Woods-Saxon-Strutinsky approach (Nazarewicz et al., 1989), and the Lipkin-Nogami prescription within the Woods-Saxon method (Satula et al., 1994). The results enable microscopic study of subtle phenomena such as identical bands, and the authors note that further testing of the density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction is needed for rotational bands at normal deformations and low spins, as well as for cases more sensitive to the isospin degree of freedom.
