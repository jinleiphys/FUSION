# EMC effect, few-nucleon systems and Poincaré covariance

> An approach for a Poincaré covariant description of nuclear structure and of lepton scattering off nuclei is proposed within the relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics in the light-front form. Indeed a high level of accuracy is needed for a comparison with the increasingly precise present and future experimental data at high momentum transfer. Therefore, to distinguish genuine QCD effects or effects of medium modified nucleon structure functions from conventional nuclear structure effects, the commutation rules between the Poincaré generators should be satisfied. For the first time in this paper a proper hadronic tensor for inclusive deep inelastic scattering of electrons off nuclei is derived in the impulse approximation in terms of the single nucleon hadronic tensor. Our approach is based : i) on a light-front spectral function for nuclei, obtained taking advantage of the successful non-relativistic knowledge of nuclear interaction, and ii) on the free current operator that, if defined in the Breit reference frame with the momentum transfer, $\bf q$, parallel to the $z$ axis, fulfills Poincaré covariance and current conservation. Our results can be generalized : i) to exclusive processes or to semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering processes; ii) to the case where the final state interaction is considered through a Glauber approximation; iii) to finite momentum transfer kinematics. As a first test, the hadronic tensor is applied to obtain the nuclear structure function F$_2^A$ and to evaluate the EMC effect for $^3He$ in the Bjorken limit. Encouraging results including only the two-body part of the light-front spectral function are presented.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a Poincaré covariant description of nuclear structure, using light-front Hamiltonian dynamics with a spectral function derived from non-relativistic wave functions, can provide a more reliable baseline for isolating genuine QCD effects in the EMC effect. The claim is hedged by the fact that only the two-body contribution to the light-front spectral function has been evaluated so far, and the full three-body calculation remains as future work.

## Method

The authors derive a hadronic tensor for inclusive deep inelastic scattering off nuclei in the impulse approximation, using a light-front spectral function for nuclei that incorporates the Bakamjian-Thomas construction of Poincaré generators. The spectral function is obtained from non-relativistic wave functions (specifically the Pisa group $^3He$ wave function with the AV18 NN interaction) and a free current operator defined in the Breit reference frame with momentum transfer parallel to the $z$ axis. The nuclear structure function $F_2^A$ is then evaluated in the Bjorken limit and applied to compute the EMC ratio for $^3He$, including only the two-body part of the light-front spectral function where the spectator $(A-1)=2$ system is in the deuteron state.

## Key numbers

- The renormalization factor for the two-body contribution to the EMC ratio is 1/0.679, corresponding to the probability (0.679) of finding a deuteron in $^3He$ using the AV18 NN interaction.
- The minimum value for $\kappa^+$ is given by $\kappa^+_m = \xi_{min} [ (m^2 + |\bm{\kappa}_\perp|^2)/\xi_{min} + (M_S^2 + |\bm{\kappa}_\perp|^2)/(1-\xi_{min}) ]^{1/2}$, with $\xi_{min} = x (m/M_A)$.
- The Bjorken variable for the nucleus is $x_A = Q^2 / (2 P_A \cdot q)$, and for the nucleon $z = Q^2 / (2 p \cdot q)$.
- The EMC ratio $r_{EMC}(x)$ is defined as $R^{He}_2(x) / R^D_2(x)$, where $R^A_2(x) = F^A_2(x) / [Z F^p_2(x) + (A-Z) F^n_2(x)]$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of the Pisa group (Kievsky, 1997) for $^3He$ wave functions and the AV18 NN interaction (Wiringa, 1995) into a Poincaré covariant framework using light-front dynamics, building on the spectral function formalism developed by the same authors in prior work (noi). The results are compared with the convolution approach of Sauer (Sauer, 1991) and experimental data from the JLab E03-103 experiment (Seely, 2009) and the reanalysis by Kulagin and Petti (Kulagin and Petti, 2006). The approach enables future calculations of exclusive processes, semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, and finite momentum transfer kinematics, as well as inclusion of final state interactions through a Glauber approximation.
