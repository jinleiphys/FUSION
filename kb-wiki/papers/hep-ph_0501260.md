# Breakdown of QCD factorization at large Feynman x

> Recent measurements by the BRAHMS collaboration of high-pT hadron production at forward rapidities at RHIC found the relative production rate(d-Au)/(p-p) to be suppressed, rather than enhanced. Examining other known reactions (forward production of light hadrons, the Drell-Yan process, heavy flavor production, etc.), one notes that all of these display a similar property, namely, their cross sections in nuclei are suppressed at large xF. Since this is the region where x2 is minimal, it is tempting to interpret this as a manifestation of coherence, or of a color glass condensate, whereas it is actually a simple consequence of energy conservation and takes place even at low energies. We demonstrate that in all these reactions there is a common suppression mechanism that can be viewed, alternatively, as a consequence of a reduced survival probability for large rapidity gap processes in nuclei, Sudakov suppression, an enhanced resolution of higher Fock states by nuclei, or an effective energy loss that rises linearly with energy. Our calculations agree with data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear suppression observed at large Feynman $x_F$ in multiple hard reactions (including the BRAHMS data on high-$p_T$ hadron production at forward rapidities) is not a manifestation of coherence effects like color glass condensate, but rather a simple consequence of energy conservation that occurs even at low energies. The authors claim this suppression represents a breakdown of QCD factorization that is a leading twist effect and does not disappear with increasing scale.

## Method

The authors employ a light-cone dipole approach to calculate nuclear effects in various hard reactions, including high-$p_T$ hadron production, Drell-Yan process, and heavy flavor production. They model the proton using a quark-diquark configuration with small gluonic spots (radius $r_0=0.3$ fm) and compute multiple scattering through the nucleus using Glauber theory with Abramovsky-Gribov-Kancheli (AGK) cutting rules. The Sudakov suppression factor $S(x_F)=1-x_F$ is derived from the mean number of radiated gluons and applied to multiple inelastic collisions in the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The Sudakov suppression factor is $S(x_F)=1-x_F$, derived from $dn_G/dy=1$ using $\alpha_s=0.4$ in the Gunion-Bertsch formula $dn_G/dy = (3\alpha_s/\pi)\ln(m_\rho^2/\Lambda_{QCD}^2)$
- For the single diffraction cross section $pA\to pX$, the measured $A$-dependence exponent is $\alpha=0.34\pm0.02$ for $x_F$ integrated from 0.925 to 1
- The effective absorption cross section used for $A=40$ is $\sigma_{abs}\approx 20$ mb after Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections
- Gluon shadowing corrections for heavy nuclei are estimated at about $10\%$ using $R_G(b)\sim 1-\exp[-(3\pi/4)r_0^2 T_A(b)]$ with $r_0=0.3$ fm
- The suppression factor for $p_T$-integrated cross sections at large $x_F$ is approximately $A^{-0.3}\approx 0.2$, consistent with BRAHMS data
- The diquark size used in calculations is $0.2-0.3$ fm, with the quark-gluon separation $r_0=0.3$ fm fitted to diffractive data

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear suppression at large $x_F$ observed in multiple experiments including BRAHMS (2004), E866 for $J/\Psi$ production, E772 for Drell-Yan, and earlier data from Barton (1983) and Geist (1987) for light hadron production. The authors argue against interpretations in terms of color glass condensate (as in models by McLerran-Venugopalan or Kharzeev-Kovchegov-Tuchin) and instead attribute the suppression to energy conservation and Sudakov effects. The paper enables a unified description of nuclear suppression across different hard reactions and predicts that QCD factorization breaks down at large $x_F$ as a leading twist effect, contrary to the assumption of factorization validity made by Hoyer (2003) in studies of $J/\Psi$ production.
