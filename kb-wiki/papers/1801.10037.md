# Heavy flavour in high-energy nuclear collisions: theory overview of transport calculations

> Transport calculations are the tool to study medium modifications of heavy-flavour particle distributions in high-energy nuclear collisions. We give a brief overview on their state-of-the art, on the information one can extract, on the questions remaining open and on further analysis to carry out in the near future.

## Key claim

Transport calculations are the tool to study medium modifications of heavy-flavour particle distributions in high-energy nuclear collisions, but the theoretical input for transport coefficients (such as the momentum-diffusion coefficient) is still far from a definite answer for experimentally relevant conditions, and the comparison with data is affected by large systematic uncertainties from both theory and experiment.

## Method

The paper reviews the multi-step theoretical setup for heavy-flavour transport calculations: initial heavy-quark production via QCD event generators, modeling of the background medium through hydrodynamic simulations, and propagation of heavy quarks through the medium using the relativistic Langevin equation with momentum-dependent transport coefficients. The heavy quarks then undergo hadronization (including possible recombination with medium partons) and finally rescattering in the hadronic phase before kinetic freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Momentum-diffusion coefficient κ: NLO weak-coupling calculations and lattice-QCD results for a gluon plasma are shown, with large NLO corrections arising from overlapping scattering processes with light partons
- Spatial diffusion coefficient D_s defined as D_s = 2T²/κ, used to quantify coupling strength with the medium
- POWLANG model results for D-meson R_AA and v₂ in nucleus-nucleus collisions compared to experimental data from Adamczyk (2014) and Abelev (2013)
- POWLANG predictions for triangular flow v₃ of D mesons in Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV compared to preliminary CMS data from Sirunyan (2017)
- Heavy-quark spatial diffusion coefficient in the hadronic phase evaluated within effective chiral Lagrangian from Ozvenchuk (2014), with values joining smoothly to partonic-phase results
- Nuclear modification factor of heavy-flavour decay electrons and D-mesons in d-Au and p-Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC, compared to data from Adare (2012) and Abelev (2014)

## Context

This review extends the line of work on heavy-flavour transport calculations in heavy-ion collisions, covering the full theoretical chain from initial production to final hadronic observables. It discusses specific prior approaches including the POWLANG model from Beraudo (2014), weak-coupling HTL calculations from Alberico (2013), lattice-QCD results from Francis (2015), and hadronization models based on resonant states from He (2014) and coalescence from Oh (2009). The paper enables future refined analyses of heavy-flavour particle distributions and extension to small systems like high-multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, while identifying open questions including charm thermalization at low p_T, D_s and Λ_c hadrochemistry, and beauty measurements as the golden channel for extracting transport coefficients.
