# Wobbling excitations at high spins in A~160

> We found that in 156Dy and 162Yb the lowest odd spin gamma-vibrational states transform to the wobbling excitations after the backbending, associated with the transition from axially-symmetric to nonaxial shapes. The analysis of quadrupole electric transitions determines uniquely the sign of the gamma-deformation in both nuclei after the transition point.

# Wobbling excitations at high spins in A~160

## Key claim

The lowest odd-spin gamma-vibrational states in 156Dy and 162Yb transform into wobbling excitations after the backbending, which is associated with a transition from axially-symmetric to nonaxial shapes; the identification relies on the sign of the gamma-deformation being negative in both nuclei after the transition point, and the claim is hedged by the need for future measurement of interband B(E2) transitions to draw a definite conclusion.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic approach based on the cranked Nilsson model plus random phase approximation (CRPA), where mean-field parameters (beta and gamma deformations) were determined from an energy-minimization procedure on a mesh in the beta-gamma plane at each rotational frequency. The additional term restoring local Galilean invariance of the Nilsson potential was included to achieve a correct description of the kinematic moment of inertia, and the RPA equations of motion were solved for negative signature normal modes to identify wobbling excitations via a criterion involving the coefficients c_nu derived from the redundant mode.

## Key numbers

- Critical rotational frequency for backbending: hbar Omega_c ≈ 0.25 MeV (10 hbar → 12 hbar) for 162Yb and hbar Omega_c ≈ 0.3 MeV (14 hbar → 16 hbar) for 156Dy.
- In 156Dy, the B10 band is proposed as the wobbling band in the range 0.45 MeV < hbar Omega < 0.55 MeV, corresponding to angular momenta 33 hbar ≤ I ≤ 39 hbar.
- At hbar Omega ~ 0.05 MeV, the transition strength from the first one-phonon state to the yrast state is ~ 330 e^2 fm^4 in 162Yb and ~ 500 e^2 fm^4 in 156Dy.
- The ratio R(-) = B(E2; I n_w=1 → I-1 yr) / B(E2; I n_w → I-2 n_w) is predicted to be approximately 0.1 for both nuclei at hbar Omega > 0.35 MeV.
- The equilibrium gamma-deformation after the transition is approximately -20 degrees for 156Dy.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic CRPA approach of Kvasil and Nazmitdinov (2006) to analyze new high-spin data on 156Dy from Konig et al. and 162Yb from the NNDC database, building on the earlier particle-rotor calculations of Hamamoto (2001) and the microscopic approach of Matsuyanagi et al. (2002) which used constant mean-field deformation parameters. The results provide a self-consistent description of wobbling excitations in even-even nuclei near A~160, complementing the first experimental evidence of wobbling in odd Lu nuclei reported by Odegard et al. (2001), and enable future experimental searches for wobbling bands via predicted E2 and M1 transition patterns.
