# Nucleon and $Δ$ resonances in $γp \to K^+ Σ^0(1385)$ photoproduction

> The photoproduction of $γp \to K^+ Σ^0(1385)$ is investigated based on an effective Lagrangian approach using the tree-level Born approximation, with the purpose of understanding the reaction mechanisms and resonance contents and their associated parameters in this reaction. In addition to the $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast(892)$ exchanges, $s$-channel nucleon ($N$) exchange, $u$-channel $Λ$ exchange, and generalized contact term, the exchanges of a minimum number of $N$ and $Δ$ resonances in the $s$ channel are taken into account in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the experimental data. It is found that the most recent differential cross-section data from the CLAS Collaboration can be well reproduced by including one of the $N(1895){1/2}^-$, $Δ(1900){1/2}^-$, and $Δ(1930){5/2}^-$ resonances. The reaction mechanisms of $γp \to K^+ Σ^0(1385)$ are discussed in detail, and the predictions of the beam and target asymmetries for this reaction are given. The cross sections of $γp \to K^0 Σ^+(1385)$ are shown to be able to further constrain the theoretical models and pin down the resonance contents for $γp \to K^+ Σ^0(1385)$.

## Key claim

The CLAS differential cross-section data for γp → K⁺ Σ⁰(1385) can be well reproduced by including only one of three specific resonances, N(1895)1/2⁻, Δ(1900)1/2⁻, or Δ(1930)5/2⁻, in the s-channel, rather than the eight or nine resonances required in prior models, provided the resonance masses and widths are fixed to PDG values and the coupling constants are treated as free fit parameters.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach at tree-level Born approximation, constructing amplitudes from t-channel K and K*(892) exchanges, s-channel nucleon exchange, u-channel Λ exchange, and a generalized contact term to ensure gauge invariance. They systematically tested each three-star and four-star N and Δ resonance with mass below 2200 MeV individually, fixing resonance masses and widths to PDG values while treating the products of electromagnetic and hadronic coupling constants as fit parameters determined by χ² minimization against CLAS differential cross-section data.

## Key numbers

- χ²/ND = 1.6 for the full energy range when including N(1895)1/2⁻; χ²/ND = 0.9 for data with W ≤ 2200 MeV
- χ²/ND = 2.1 for the full energy range when including Δ(1900)1/2⁻; χ²/ND = 1.5 for data with W ≤ 2200 MeV
- χ²/ND = 2.4 for the full energy range when including Δ(1930)5/2⁻; χ²/ND = 1.2 for data with W ≤ 2200 MeV
- χ²/ND = 5.1 when no resonances are included; χ²/ND = 6.4 when resonance couplings are also freed
- N(1895)1/2⁻ mass fixed to 1895 MeV with width 120 MeV; Δ(1900)1/2⁻ mass 1860 MeV with width 250 MeV; Δ(1930)5/2⁻ mass 1950 MeV with width 300 MeV
- Coupling product g_RNγ^(1) g_RΣ*K^(1) = −3.00 ± 0.06 for N(1895), 3.25 ± 0.08 for Δ(1900), and −0.24 ± 0.06 for Δ(1930)
- Cutoff parameter Λ_{K,K*} = 924 ± 1 MeV (Model I), 933 ± 2 MeV (Model II), 950 ± 1 MeV (Model III)

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Yong (2008) and He-Jun (2014), which used eight or nine N and Δ resonances with masses from quark models and common widths of 300 or 500 MeV, respectively, and by Yu (2017), which used a Regge model without resonances. The present analysis demonstrates that only one resonance is needed when PDG masses and widths are adopted, enabling future measurements of γp → K⁰ Σ⁺(1385) cross sections and beam/target asymmetries to distinguish which of the three candidate resonances actually contributes.
