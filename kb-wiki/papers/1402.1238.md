# Impact of resonance regeneration and decay on the net-proton fluctuations in a hadron resonance gas

> We investigate net-proton fluctuations as important observables measured in heavy-ion collisions within the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. Special emphasis is given to effects which are a priori not inherent in a thermally and chemically equilibrated HRG approach. In particular, we point out the importance of taking into account the successive regeneration and decay of resonances below the chemical freeze-out, which lead to a randomization of the isospin of nucleons and thus to additional fluctuations in the net-proton number. We find good agreement between our model results and the recent STAR measurements of the higher-order moments of the net-proton distribution.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the observed net-proton fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions can be reconciled with hadron resonance gas (HRG) model calculations only when including both the probabilistic nature of resonance decays and the isospin-randomization of nucleons via interactions with thermal pions after chemical freeze-out, contrary to the abstract's implication that the HRG model alone already shows good agreement.

## Method

The authors compute net-proton fluctuations within a grandcanonical HRG model containing 103 baryon species and 113 meson species up to masses of approximately 2 GeV, using chemical freeze-out parameters parameterized as functions of collision energy. They systematically incorporate: (1) kinematic acceptance cuts matching STAR detector coverage (rapidity |y| ≤ 0.5, transverse momentum 0.4 GeV ≤ k_T ≤ 0.8 GeV), (2) resonance decay contributions split into average and probabilistic components following the formalism of Begun (2006) and Fu (2013), and (3) isospin-changing scatterings of nucleons with thermal pions via intermediate Δ-resonances using the Kitazawa-Asakawa (KA) formalism.

## Key numbers

- The chemical freeze-out temperature is parameterized as T^fo(μ_B) = a - b(μ_B)^2 - c(μ_B)^4 with a = (0.166 ± 0.002) GeV, b = (0.139 ± 0.016) GeV^{-1}, c = (0.053 ± 0.021) GeV^{-3}
- The baryon chemical potential as function of collision energy is μ_B^fo(√s) = d_B / (1 + e_B √s) with d_B = (1.308 ± 0.028) GeV and e_B = (0.273 ± 0.008) GeV^{-1}
- The probability that a nucleon changes its isospin identity after one cycle of Δ-mediated scattering is 4/9; after two cycles this probability is approximately 50%
- The regeneration time for Δ(1232) resonances is approximately 3-4 fm in the temperature range 150-170 MeV, while their lifetime is about 1.8 fm
- The average branching ratio for Δ+(1232) decay into protons is ⟨n_p⟩_{Δ+} = 0.669, into neutrons is ⟨n_n⟩_{Δ+} = 0.331
- The average resonance decay contributions cause deviations from net-baryon number fluctuations of up to 20% in σ²/M, 10% in Sσ, and 20% in κσ²
- The purity of the STAR proton sample is 98%; the remaining 2% (assumed Poisson-distributed) affect ratios of third- to second-order cumulants by 1% and fourth- to second-order cumulants by 0.1%

## Context

This work extends prior HRG model calculations of net-proton fluctuations by Karsch (2010), Fu (2013), and Garg (2013) by systematically incorporating resonance regeneration and decay effects after chemical freeze-out, as well as isospin-randomization via the Kitazawa-Asakawa formalism (Kitazawa 2011, 2012). The results provide a non-critical baseline for future searches for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions, and demonstrate that the additional fluctuation sources from resonance decays and isospin-changing interactions can reconcile HRG model predictions with STAR beam energy scan data (Adamczyk 2013).
