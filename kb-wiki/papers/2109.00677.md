# Bayesian Monte Carlo extraction of sea asymmetry with SeaQuest and STAR data

> We perform a global QCD analysis of unpolarized parton distributions within a Bayesian Monte Carlo framework, including the new $W$-lepton production data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and Drell-Yan di-muon data from the SeaQuest experiment at Fermilab. We assess the impact of these two new measurements on the light antiquark sea in the proton, and the $\bar{d}-\bar{u}$ asymmetry in particular. The SeaQuest data are found to significantly reduce the uncertainty on the $\bar{d}/\bar{u}$ ratio at large parton momentum fractions $x$, strongly favoring an enhanced $\bar{d}$ sea up to $x \approx 0.4$, in general agreement with nonperturbative calculations based on chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.

## Key claim

The SeaQuest Drell-Yan data significantly reduce the uncertainty on the \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) ratio at large parton momentum fractions \(x\), strongly favoring an enhanced \(\bar{d}\) sea up to \(x \approx 0.4\), in general agreement with nonperturbative calculations based on chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, though some tension exists between the SeaQuest and earlier NuSea data at high \(x\).

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis of unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) within a Bayesian Monte Carlo framework, using the JAM (Jefferson Lab Angular Momentum) methodology. They included new \(W\)-lepton production data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and Drell-Yan di-muon data from the SeaQuest experiment at Fermilab, alongside existing DIS data from fixed-target and HERA experiments, Drell-Yan data from NuSea, and weak boson and jet production data from the Tevatron and LHC. PDFs were parametrized at the input scale \(\mu_0^2 = m_c^2\) using a generic template function, and the posterior distribution was sampled via data re-sampling with over 900 Monte Carlo samples.

## Key numbers

- The global fit achieved a reduced \(\chi^2\) of 1.12 for a total of 4421 data points.
- The SeaQuest Drell-Yan data (6 data points) gave a reduced \(\chi^2\) of 0.82; the STAR \(W\)-lepton data (9 data points) gave a reduced \(\chi^2\) of 2.02.
- The SeaQuest data reduced the uncertainty on the \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) ratio by up to approximately 50% at high \(x\) (\(x \gtrsim 0.3\)).
- The STAR data reduced the uncertainty on \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) by up to approximately 20% at \(x \approx 0.2\).
- The truncated moment \(\int_{0.01}^1 dx (\bar{d}-\bar{u})\) was tightly gathered around 0.1 when all data were included.
- The analysis used a center-of-mass energy of \(\sqrt{S} = 0.51\) TeV for the STAR data, and heavy quark mass thresholds \(m_c = 1.28\) GeV and \(m_b = 4.18\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous global QCD analyses by including the new SeaQuest Drell-Yan data (SeaQuest Collaboration, 2021) and STAR \(W\)-lepton data (STAR Collaboration, 2020), which had not been incorporated into prior extractions. The results confirm and extend the earlier evidence for a \(\bar{d} - \bar{u}\) asymmetry found by the NuSea experiment (NuSea Collaboration, 2001) and the NMC experiment (New Muon Collaboration, 1991, 1993), while resolving some tensions at high \(x\). The extracted asymmetry is compared with nonperturbative calculations based on chiral symmetry breaking and the pion cloud model (Thomas, 1983; Salamu et al., 2014, 2019), and the results enable more precise tests of these nonperturbative mechanisms in the proton sea.
