# Neutron single spin asymmetries from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off transversely polarized $^3$He

> A study of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off transversely polarized $^3$He is presented. The formal expressions of the Collins and Sivers contributions to the azimuthal single spin asymmetry for the production of leading pions are derived, in impulse approximation, and estimated in the kinematics of forth-coming experiments at JLab. The AV18 interaction has been used for a realistic description of the nuclear dynamics; the nucleon structure has been described by proper parameterizations of data or suitable model calculations. The initial transverse momentum of the struck quark has been properly included in the calculation. The crucial issue of extracting the neutron information from $^3$He data, planned to shed some light on the puzzling experimental scenario arisen from recent measurements for the proton and the deuteron, is thoroughly discussed. It is found that a model independent procedure, widely used in inclusive deep inelastic scattering to take into account the momentum and energy distributions of the bound nucleons in $^3$He, can be applied also in the kinematics of the planned JLab experiments, although fragmentation functions, not only parton distributions, are involved. The possible role played by final state interactions in the process under investigation is addressed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, in the kinematics of the planned JLab experiments E-06-010 and E-06-011, a transversely polarized $^3$He target can be used as an effective transversely polarized free neutron target for extracting neutron single spin asymmetries, despite the involvement of fragmentation functions in addition to parton distributions. This conclusion holds within the impulse approximation framework and relies on the specific kinematic conditions where the detected pions are ultra-relativistic and emitted in a narrow cone around the momentum transfer direction.

## Method

The author derived the formal expressions for the Collins and Sivers contributions to the azimuthal single spin asymmetry for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off transversely polarized $^3$He, using an impulse approximation approach with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction for the nuclear dynamics. The nucleon structure was described using parameterizations of existing data and model calculations, with the initial transverse momentum of the struck quark properly included. The nuclear asymmetries were then estimated in the kinematics of the JLab experiments, and the feasibility of extracting neutron information using an effective polarization procedure was evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The neutron effective polarization in $^3$He is $p_n = 0.86 \pm 0.02$, obtained by averaging many few-body calculations.
- The proton effective polarization in $^3$He is $p_p = -0.028 \pm 0.004$.
- The JLab experiments cover the valence quark region $0.19 \le x \le 0.41$ with $Q^2$ values between $1.77$ GeV$^2$ and $2.73$ GeV$^2$.
- The detected leading fragmentation pions carry $z \simeq 0.5$ of the energy transfer, with an energy $E_h \simeq p_h \simeq 2.4$ GeV.
- The maximum angle between the virtual photon direction and the emitted pion is approximately $12^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the effective polarization method developed for inclusive deep inelastic scattering by Ciofi degli Atti, Scopetta, Pace, and Salme (1993) to the semi-inclusive case, addressing the puzzling experimental scenario from recent measurements by the HERMES collaboration (2005) for the proton and the COMPASS collaboration (2006) for the deuteron. The results enable the planned JLab experiments E-06-010 and E-06-011 to extract neutron Collins and Sivers asymmetries, which will help clarify the strong flavor dependence observed in the Sivers effect and test the interpretation by Brodsky and Gardner (2006) regarding gluon contributions to parton orbital angular momentum.
