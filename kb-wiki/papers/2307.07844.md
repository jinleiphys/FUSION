# Studying QGP transport properties in a concurrent minijet+hydro framework

> Minijets are ubiquitous in heavy-ion collision experiments. However, they are often excluded from the hydrodynamic simulations of QGP as they do not thermalize at short time scales and are not treated as part of the collective medium. Using a concurrent jet+hydro framework, we show that the minijets could account for a significant portion of particle multiplicity. Therefore, the energy deposition from minijet-medium interactions can substantially modify the QGP transport properties inferred from model-to-data comparisons.

## Key claim

Minijets, which are typically excluded from hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), can account for a significant portion of particle multiplicity and their energy deposition substantially modifies the QGP transport properties inferred from model-to-data comparisons, but the size of these effects depends on the soft-hard separation scale \(Q_s\), which remains an open parameter.

## Method

The authors used a concurrent jet+hydro framework where the bulk QGP is initialized with the IP-Glasma model and evolved with the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic solver MUSIC, while minijets are initialized using hard processes in PYTHIA8 with the same binary nucleon-nucleon collision positions. Minijet energy loss is treated within the hybrid strong-weak coupling model, with strongly coupled energy loss given by a holographic formula, and the deposited energy is fed into the bulk evolution via a Gaussian-smeared source term. The bulk medium is hadronized using the Cooper-Frye formalism, surviving minijets are hadronized using the Lund string model in PYTHIA, and all hadrons undergo cascading via UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- For \(p^{\text{J}}_{\text{min}} = 4\) GeV, the optimum shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) is 0.02 and the initial state normalization factor \(s_{\text{factor}}\) is 0.45, compared to \(\eta/s = 0.13\) and \(s_{\text{factor}} = 0.915\) for the case without minijets.
- For \(p^{\text{J}}_{\text{min}} = 4\) GeV, the value of \(\eta/s\) and \(s_{\text{factor}}\) need to be adjusted by about 85% and 50%, respectively, relative to the no-jets case.
- For \(p^{\text{J}}_{\text{min}} = 4\) GeV in 0-5% centrality collisions, the average ratio of hadrons from fragmentation of un-thermalized partons to total hadrons is 0.077(1); for 40-50% centrality it is 0.252(3).
- For \(p^{\text{J}}_{\text{min}} = 7\) GeV in 0-5% centrality, the fragmentation ratio is 0.0125(5); for 40-50% centrality it is 0.033(2).
- For \(p^{\text{J}}_{\text{min}} = 10\) GeV in 0-5% centrality, the fragmentation ratio is 0.0042(3); for 40-50% centrality it is 0.014(2).

## Context

This work extends the concurrent jet+hydro framework developed by Pablos (2022) and applies it to study the extraction of QGP transport properties, specifically the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio and initial state normalization. The results contradict the common practice of excluding minijets from hydrodynamic simulations, showing that their inclusion requires substantial retuning of parameters such as \(\eta/s\) and \(s_{\text{factor}}\). This enables future systematic Bayesian studies to optimize the soft-hard separation scale \(Q_s\) and motivates development of energy loss models with a gradual separation between soft and hard modes.
