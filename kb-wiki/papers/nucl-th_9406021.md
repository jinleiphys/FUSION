# A Limited Symmetry Found by Comparing Calculated Magnetic Dipole Spin and Orbital Strengths in $^4\mbox{He}$

> Allowing for $2$$\hbar ω$ admixtures in $^4\mbox{He}$ we find that the summed magnetic dipole isovector orbital and spin strengths are equal. This indicates a symmetry which is associated with interchanging the labels of the spin with those of the orbit. Where higher admixtures are included, the orbital sum becomes larger than the spin sum, but the sums over the low energy region are still nearly the same.

## Key claim

When ground-state correlations are restricted to 2ħω admixtures in ⁴He, the summed isovector magnetic dipole spin and orbital strengths are equal, indicating a symmetry associated with interchanging spin and orbital labels. This equality holds for all combinations of central, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions at the 2ħω level, but breaks down when higher (4ħω and 6ħω) admixtures are included, although the low-energy sums remain nearly equal.

## Method

Shell-model matrix diagonalizations were performed using the OXBASH code for ⁴He, with progressively larger model spaces including up to 2ħω, 4ħω, and 6ħω admixtures. The interaction of Zheng and Zamick (1991) was used, containing central, spin-orbit, and tensor parts, with the spin-orbit and tensor interactions turned on or off by setting parameters x and y to 0 or 1. The summed B(M1) strengths for the isovector spin operator (s t_z) and orbital operator (l t_z) were calculated separately, dropping the factor 9.412 from the spin operator to facilitate comparison.

## Key numbers

- At 2ħω with central interaction (x=0, y=0): summed B(M1)_spin = 0.8546 (10⁻³ μ_N²) and summed B(M1)_orbit = 0.8546 (10⁻³ μ_N²), exactly equal.
- At 2ħω with realistic interaction (x=1, y=1): summed B(M1)_spin = 3.3944 (10⁻³ μ_N²) and summed B(M1)_orbit = 3.3955 (10⁻³ μ_N²), virtually equal.
- At 4ħω with realistic interaction (x=1, y=1): summed B(M1)_spin = 4.8288 (10⁻³ μ_N²) and summed B(M1)_orbit = 10.554 (10⁻³ μ_N²), showing orbital strength substantially larger.
- At 6ħω with realistic interaction (x=1, y=1): summed B(M1)_spin = 5.6052 (10⁻³ μ_N²) and summed B(M1)_orbit = 14.272 (10⁻³ μ_N²).
- Low-energy summed strengths at 6ħω with realistic interaction: B(M1)_spin = 1.8199 (10⁻³ μ_N²) and B(M1)_orbit = 1.6155 (10⁻³ μ_N²), with a deviation of -10.8%.
- For central interaction at 2ħω, the spin strength (0.855 in 10⁻³ μ_N²) goes to a single non-spurious state at 45.3 MeV, and the orbital strength (0.855 in 10⁻³ μ_N²) goes to a different non-spurious state at 56.5 MeV.
- The largest single orbital B(M1) at 6ħω with central interaction is 2.79 (10⁻³ μ_N²) to a state at 61.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Zamick, Abbas, and Halemane (1981) which considered only spin magnetic dipole transitions in closed-shell nuclei, by now including orbital transitions and examining their interrelation. The symmetry found at the 2ħω level is specific to ⁴He; for larger closed-shell nuclei such as ¹⁶O, the orbital B(M1) is substantially larger than the spin B(M1) even at the 2ħω level, as noted by Brown (private communication). The results suggest that multi-probe experimental analyses might be needed to test this symmetry, given the large isovector spin coupling for electromagnetic probes, and the finding may be of theoretical interest for uncovering unexpected symmetries in nuclear structure and possibly in other fields such as atomic physics and baryon structure.
