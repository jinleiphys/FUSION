# Simultaneous Softening of sigma and rho Mesons associated with Chiral Restoration

> Complex poles of the unitarized pi-pi scattering amplitude in nuclear matter are studied. Partial restoration of chiral symmetry is modeled by the decrease of in-medium pion decay constant f*_{pi}. For large chiral restoration (f*_{pi}/f_{pi} << 1), 2nd sheet poles in the scalar (sigma) and the vector (rho) mesons are both dictated by the Lambert W function and show universal softening as f*_{pi} decreases. In-medium pi-pi cross section receives substantial contribution from the soft mode and exhibits a large enhancement in low-energy region. Fate of this universality for small chiral restoration (f*_{pi}/f_{pi} ~ 1) is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for sufficiently large chiral restoration (in-medium pion decay constant \(f^*_\pi / f_\pi \ll 1\)), the complex poles of both the scalar sigma meson and the vector rho meson in nuclear matter are dictated by the Lambert W function and soften universally and in tandem as \(f^*_\pi\) decreases; however, this universality breaks down when the system is close to the vacuum state (\(f^*_\pi / f_\pi \sim 1\)).

## Method

The authors study complex poles of the unitarized pi-pi scattering amplitude in nuclear matter using the N/D method, which respects analyticity and approximately satisfies crossing symmetry. They adopt four complementary chiral models (Model A: rho model; Model B: sigma model; Model C: degenerate rho-sigma model; Model D: leading order chiral Lagrangian) and model partial chiral restoration by replacing the vacuum pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) with an in-medium value \(f^*_\pi\). The denominator function \(D_{IJ}(s)\) is constructed from tree-level amplitudes, and its complex zeros on the second Riemann sheet are tracked as \(f^*_\pi\) decreases.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum rho pole position: \(\sqrt{s_\rho} \simeq 769 - 76 i\) MeV (from Models A, B, C).
* Vacuum pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
* Bare rho mass in Model A: \(\bar{m}_\rho = 0.774\) GeV.
* Bare sigma mass in Model B: \(\bar{m}_\sigma = 0.838\) GeV.
* Bare rho and sigma mass in Model C: \(\bar{m}_{\rho,\sigma} = 0.778\) GeV.
* Coupling constant: \(g_\rho^2/4\pi = 2.72\) (fixed to reproduce the rho width).
* Subtraction scale: \(\mu = 0.77\) GeV.
* For \(f^*_\pi = 0.7 f_\pi\), moderate softening and broadening of the rho resonance is observed in the \(I=J=1\) channel.
* For \(f^*_\pi \rightarrow 0\), sharp peaks develop in both the \(I=J=1\) and \(I=J=0\) channels as a consequence of the soft modes.

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1985) that the sigma meson could serve as a probe of chiral restoration, and by various authors on in-medium vector mesons (e.g., Pisarski, Brown, Rapp, Hatsuda, Lee). It provides the first strong indication that both the sigma and rho mesons act as chiral soft modes simultaneously, using unitarized chiral models with the N/D method. The results enable future studies including finite pion mass effects, coupling to particle-hole excitations of the Fermi sea, and the distinction between temporal and spatial in-medium pion decay constants.
