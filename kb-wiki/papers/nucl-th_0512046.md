# Analytical Special Solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian

> The following special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian are briefly described: 1) Z(5) (approximately separable solution in five dimensions with gamma close to 30 degrees), 2) Z(4) (exactly separable gamma-rigid solution in four dimensions with gamma = 30 degrees), 3) X(3) (exactly separable gamma-rigid solution in three dimensions with gamma =0). The analytical solutions obtained using Davidson potentials in the E(5), X(5), Z(5), and Z(4) frameworks are also mentioned.

## Key claim

The paper presents several analytical special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian (Z(5), Z(4), X(3)) that are obtained by fixing or approximately fixing the gamma variable to specific values (30 degrees or 0 degrees), with the caveat that Z(5) and X(5) are approximate rather than exact solutions, while Z(4) and X(3) are exactly separable.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian by imposing specific conditions on the gamma variable: for Z(5) they allow gamma to vary only around 30 degrees with approximate separation of variables; for Z(4) they freeze gamma exactly at 30 degrees; for X(3) they freeze gamma at 0 degrees (axial symmetry). In all cases, the beta equation is solved with an infinite square well potential, yielding Bessel function eigenfunctions, while the angular equations are solved analytically. The authors also mention solutions obtained by substituting Davidson potentials for the infinite square well.

## Key numbers

- Z(5) and Z(4) models: the projection alpha of angular momentum on the body-fixed x axis is a good quantum number when gamma = 30 degrees
- X(3) model: the projection K of angular momentum on the body-fixed z-axis is a good quantum number when gamma = 0
- X(3) interband B(E2) transition rates: B(E2; 0_2 to 2_1) = 164.0, B(E2; 2_2 to 4_1) = 64.5, B(E2; 2_2 to 2_1) = 12.4, all normalized to B(E2; 2_1+ to 0_1+)
- X(3) interband B(E2) transition rates: B(E2; 0_3 to 2_2) = 209.1, B(E2; 2_3 to 4_2) = 92.0, normalized to B(E2; 2_1+ to 0_1+)
- Experimental examples of Z(4) appear in Xe-128, Xe-130, Xe-132
- Experimental examples of Z(5) appear in Pt-192, Pt-194, Pt-196
- Experimental examples of X(3) appear in Os-172 and Pt-186

## Context

This work extends the critical point symmetries E(5) (Iachello, 2000) and X(5) (Iachello, 2001) by identifying additional special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian for triaxial shapes (gamma near 30 degrees) and axially symmetric shapes (gamma = 0). The Z(4) and X(3) solutions are exactly separable, unlike the approximate X(5) solution, and their algebraic structures remain to be clarified. The paper enables future work on understanding the algebraic structure of these models and extending them to include octupole and dipole degrees of freedom.
