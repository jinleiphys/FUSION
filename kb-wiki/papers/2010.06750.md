# Hybrid Stars with Hyperons and Strange Quark Matter

> We consider the possibility of having hybrid stars with a phase transition from hadrons into strange matter at the core of a neutron star in b equilibrium. For the hadron phase equation of state (EoS) the quark-meson coupling model is used, while the MIT bag model is used to describe the strange matter phase. The phase transition is treated using the Gibbs construction method and results are calculated and checked against the observational constaints imposed on the EoS. The results are also compared with the hadronic EoS including hyperons, F-QMC700

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a hybrid star model incorporating a phase transition from hadronic matter (including hyperons) to strange quark matter at the core can satisfy current observational constraints from gravitational wave measurements, but only when the Gibbs construction (global charge neutrality) is used and the surface tension at the hadron-quark interface is assumed negligible.

## Method

The hadronic phase is described by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, using the parametrized F-QMC700 equation of state (EoS) that includes hyperons above an energy density of 450 MeV/fm³, with the Baym-Bethe-Pethick (BBP) EoS for the crust below 100 MeV/fm³. The quark phase is described by the MIT bag model with a bag constant of 10¹⁴ g/cm³, and the phase transition is treated using the Gibbs construction method, which enforces global charge neutrality and equal baryon chemical potentials and pressures between phases. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and the tidal deformability is computed following the method of Hinderer and Flanagan.

## Key numbers

- The transition from nucleonic matter to hyperonic matter occurs at a baryon number density of approximately 0.45 fm⁻³.
- The mixed phase (hyperon-strange matter) exists between baryon number densities of 0.6 fm⁻³ and 0.65 fm⁻³.
- Above a baryon number density of 0.65 fm⁻³, the matter is pure strange quark matter.
- The hybrid star model predicts a maximum mass above 2 solar masses (M⊙), slightly heavier than the F-QMC700 EoS maximum mass of 1.95 M⊙.
- The radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star is less than 13 km, consistent with gravitational wave constraints.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model calculations of Rikovska Stone (2007), which first showed that neutron stars with hyperons could reach 2 M⊙, by adding a phase transition to strange quark matter at high densities. The results are compared with the purely hadronic F-QMC700 EoS and are checked against the tidal deformability constraints from the GW170817 neutron star merger observation (Abbott 2017). The study enables future investigations into whether hybrid star EoS can explain neutron star cooling, magnetic fields, and glitches.
