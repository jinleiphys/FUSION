# Filter diagonalization of shell-model calculations

> We present a method of filter diagonalization for shell-model calculations. This method is based on the Sakurai and Sugiura (SS) method, but extended with help of the shifted complex orthogonal conjugate gradient (COCG) method. A salient feature of this method is that it can calculate eigenvalues and eigenstates in a given energy interval. We show that this method can be an alternative to the Lanczos method for calculating ground and excited states, as well as spectral strength functions. With an application to the $M$-scheme shell-model calculations we demonstrate that several inherent problems in the widely-used Lanczos method can be removed or reduced.

## Key claim

The filter diagonalization method, based on the Sakurai-Sugiura (SS) method combined with the shifted complex orthogonal conjugate gradient (COCG) method, can serve as an alternative to the Lanczos method for shell-model calculations, but its practical advantage depends on the choice of initial states with good quantum numbers and the integration contour parameters to filter out unwanted angular momentum and isospin components.

## Method

The method uses Cauchy's integral formula to define moments that project out eigenvalues and eigenvectors within a chosen energy interval on the complex plane, with the integration contour taken as a circle. The shifted COCG method reduces the problem to solving a single linear equation at one reference point, from which solutions at other integration points are obtained with minimal additional cost. The moments form Hankel matrices, and solving a generalized eigenvalue problem of dimension equal to the number of eigenvalues inside the contour yields the energies and amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The M-scheme dimension for $^{48}$Cr with $M=0$ is about $2\times 10^{6}$.
- For the $J=0$ ground state of $^{48}$Cr, the COCG method converged in 19 iterations under a residual norm criterion of $10^{-5}$.
- The lowest state with $J=0$ in the 2p2h space has energy $-31.1$ MeV.
- Integration contours with radius $r=1.0$ MeV were used for yrast states, covering intervals such as [$-$34, $-$32] MeV for $J=0$ and [$-$33, $-$31] MeV for $J=2$.
- For the $0_4$ state, the initial state had only 0.03% overlap, causing it to be missed with a single initial state.
- The double Lanczos method required $N_L(H)=163$ main iterations and $N_L(J^2)=668$ additional iterations for the $0_{12}$ state.

## Context

This work extends the Sakurai-Sugiura method (Sakurai and Sugiura, 2003) by combining it with the shifted COCG method (van der Vorst, 1990) to make it feasible for large-scale shell-model calculations. It directly addresses three long-standing problems of the Lanczos method: slow convergence for highly excited states, the need for reorthogonalization of Lanczos vectors, and the difficulty of conserving angular momentum and isospin in M-scheme calculations. The method enables direct calculation of eigenvalues and eigenstates in a specified energy interval without the contamination from states of different quantum numbers that plagues the Lanczos method, and it can also compute spectral strength functions.
