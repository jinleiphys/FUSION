# Physics of Ultra-Peripheral Nuclear Collisions

> Moving highly-charged ions carry strong electromagnetic fields that act as a field of photons. In collisions at large impact parameters, hadronic interactions are not possible, and the ions interact through photon-ion and photon-photon collisions known as {\it ultra-peripheral collisions} (UPC). Hadron colliders like the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), the Tevatron and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) produce photonuclear and two-photon interactions at luminosities and energies beyond that accessible elsewhere; the LHC will reach a $γp$ energy ten times that of the Hadron-Electron Ring Accelerator (HERA). Reactions as diverse as the production of anti-hydrogen, photoproduction of the $ρ^0$, transmutation of lead into bismuth and excitation of collective nuclear resonances have already been studied. At the LHC, UPCs can study many types of `new physics.'

## Key claim

Ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) at hadron colliders enable the study of photonuclear and two-photon interactions at energies and luminosities beyond those accessible at dedicated photon or electron-beam facilities, but the usable photon flux is restricted to impact parameters where the ions do not overlap hadronically, and the equivalent photon method requires careful treatment of nuclear geometry, form factors, and electromagnetic multipolarity for accurate cross-section predictions.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of the equivalent photon (Weizsäcker-Williams) method applied to relativistic heavy-ion and proton-proton collisions, deriving the photon flux from the Fourier transform of the Lorentz-contracted electromagnetic fields of moving charged particles. They present the formalism for calculating cross sections of photonuclear and two-photon processes, including the integration over impact parameters with a minimum cutoff at twice the nuclear radius to exclude hadronic interactions, and discuss the use of Glauber model calculations and vector meson dominance for exclusive vector meson photoproduction. The review also covers experimental techniques for detecting UPC events at RHIC, the Tevatron, and the LHC, emphasizing the distinctive low transverse momentum signatures of coherent interactions.

## Key numbers

- LHC will reach γp center-of-mass energies up to 1 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions and 3.1 TeV for p+p collisions, more than an order of magnitude higher than available at HERA.
- Maximum γA energy at RHIC for Au+Au is 24 GeV; at LHC for Pb+Pb it is 705 GeV.
- Maximum γγ energy at RHIC for Au+Au is 6.0 GeV; at LHC for Pb+Pb it is 178 GeV.
- Cross section for exclusive ρ⁰ production in Au+Au at RHIC: 590 mb (theoretical prediction from KN99); measured value at 130 A GeV is 460 ± 220 ± 110 mb (STAR collaboration).
- Cross section for exclusive ρ⁰ production in Pb+Pb at LHC: 5200 mb (theoretical prediction from KN99).
- Cross section for exclusive J/ψ production in Au+Au at RHIC: 0.29 mb; in Pb+Pb at LHC: 32 mb.
- Deuteron photodissociation cross section in d+Au at RHIC: 1.38 b; in d+Pb at LHC: 2.49 b.
- Maximum photon energy for heavy-ions is about 0.03/A of the ion energy; for protons, approximately 10% of the proton energy.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the equivalent photon method dating back to Fermi (1924) and Weizsäcker and Williams (1934), and builds on previous reviews of UPCs by BB88, KGS97, BHT98, BHTSK02, and Hot02. The paper synthesizes results from low-energy Coulomb excitation studies at GSI, NSCL/MSU, GANIL, and RIKEN with recent high-energy measurements at RHIC (STAR collaboration ρ⁰ production, 2002) and the Tevatron (antihydrogen production, 1998), and provides a roadmap for UPC physics at the LHC including searches for new physics, measurements of gluon distributions in nuclei via vector meson photoproduction, and studies of electroweak processes such as γγ → W⁺W⁻.
