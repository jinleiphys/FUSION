# Kubo estimation of the electrical conductivity for a hot relativistic fluid in the presence of a magnetic field

> We have explored the multi-component structure of electrical conductivity of relativistic Fermionic and Bosonic fluid in presence of magnetic field by using Kubo approach. This is done by explicitly evaluating the thermo-magnetic vector current spectral functions using the real time formalism of finite temperature field theory and the Schwinger proper time formalism. In absence of magnetic field, the one-loop diagramatic representation of Kubo expression of any transport coefficients is exactly same with relaxation time approximation (RTA) based expression, but this equality does not hold for finite magnetic field picture due to lacking of proper implementation of quantum effect in latter approach. We have shown this discrepancy for particular transport coefficient - electrical conductivity, whose starting point in Kubo approach will be electromagnetic current-current correlator and its one-loop skeleton diagram carrying two scalar/Dirac propagators for scalar/Dirac fluid. Through a numerical comparison between RTA and Kubo expressions of conductivity components (parallel and perpendicular), we have attempted to interpret detail quantum field theoretical effect, contained by Kubo expression but not by RTA expression. In classical RTA expression we get magnetic field independent parallel conductivity due to zero Lorentz force but in field theoretical Kubo expression, it decreases and increases with the magnetic field for scalar and Dirac medium respectively due to Landau quantization effect. This parallel component of conductivity can be interpreted as zero momentum limit of quantum fluctuation with same Landau level internal lines. While for perpendicular component of conductivity, fluctuation with Landau level differences $\pm 1$ are noticed, which might be a new realization of transportation in field theoretical sector.

## Key claim

The one-loop Kubo expression for electrical conductivity in a magnetic field differs from the relaxation time approximation (RTA) expression because the RTA lacks proper implementation of quantum effects such as Landau quantization; the parallel conductivity component decreases with magnetic field for a scalar medium and increases for a Dirac medium in the Kubo approach, whereas the classical RTA expression yields a magnetic-field-independent parallel conductivity.

## Method

The authors evaluated the thermo-magnetic vector current spectral functions for both complex scalar (spin-0) and Dirac (spin-1/2) relativistic fluids using the real-time formalism of finite-temperature field theory combined with the Schwinger proper-time formalism. They computed the one-loop skeleton diagram of the electromagnetic current-current correlator, which carries two scalar or Dirac propagators, and extracted the electrical conductivity tensor components (parallel, perpendicular, and Hall) by applying appropriate projectors in the static (long-wavelength) limit. The results were compared numerically with the corresponding RTA expressions to identify the quantum field-theoretical effects encoded in the Kubo approach.

## Key numbers

* For the scalar medium at zero magnetic field, the Kubo conductivity is given by \(\sigma_\text{Scalar} = \frac{2e^2}{3T}\int\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\frac{\vec{k}^2}{\omega_k^2\Gamma}f_+(\omega_k)[1+f_+(\omega_k)]\).
* For the Dirac medium at zero magnetic field, the Kubo conductivity is \(\sigma_\text{Dirac} = \frac{4e^2}{3T}\int\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\frac{\vec{k}^2}{\omega_k^2\Gamma}f_-(\omega_k)[1-f_-(\omega_k)]\).
* In the presence of a magnetic field, the parallel conductivity component for the scalar medium decreases with increasing magnetic field, while for the Dirac medium it increases with increasing magnetic field, due to Landau quantization effects.
* The perpendicular conductivity component involves fluctuations with Landau level differences of \(\pm 1\), as encoded in the Kronecker delta functions from the analytic \(d^2k_\perp\) integrals of the Laguerre-polynomial-containing functions \(\mathcal{A}_{ln}^{(j)}\), \(\mathcal{B}_{ln}^{(j)}\), \(\mathcal{C}_{ln}^{(j)}\), and \(\mathcal{D}_{ln}^{(j)}\).
* The Hall conductivity components \(\sigma^\times\) vanish for both scalar and Dirac media in this calculation: \(\mathcal{N}_{ln;\text{Scalar}}^\times(\vec{k}) = \mathcal{N}_{ln;\text{Dirac}}^\times(\vec{k}) = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical Kubo approach to electrical conductivity in a magnetic field, building on earlier RTA-based calculations by Tuchin (2011), Ghosh et al. (2018), Dey et al. (2019), and Mohanty et al. (2018) that found anisotropic transport with multiple conductivity components. It also goes beyond prior Kubo studies by Hattori et al. (2016) and Fukushima et al. (2017) by revealing new information about the perpendicular component of conductivity, specifically the involvement of Landau level differences of \(\pm 1\) in the quantum fluctuations. The results enable a more complete quantum field-theoretical description of charge transport in hot QCD matter under strong magnetic fields, relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology and neutron star physics.
