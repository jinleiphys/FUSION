# Equation of State table with hyperon and antikaon for supernova and neutron star merger

> We develop a new equation of state (EoS) table involving thermal (anti)kaons, Bose-Einstein condensate of $K^{-}$ mesons and $Λ$-hyperons for core-collapse supernova and neutron star merger simulations. This EoS table is based on a finite temperature density-dependent relativistic hadron field theory where baryon-baryon interaction is mediated by scalar $σ$, vector $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons, using the parameter set DD2 for nucleons. The repulsive hyperon-hyperon interaction is mediated by an additional strange $ϕ$ meson. The EoS for the $K^-$ condensed matter is also calculated within the framework of relativistic mean field model, whereas the low-density, inhomogeneous matter is calculated in the extended Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium model (NSE). The EoS table is generated for a wide range of values of three parameters - baryon density ($10^{-12}$ to $\sim$ 1 fm$^{-3}$), positive charge fraction(0.01 to 0.60) and temperature(0.1 to 158.48 MeV).

## Key claim

The paper constructs a new equation of state (EoS) table that simultaneously includes both Lambda hyperons and antikaon condensate, finding that the early appearance of hyperons delays or suppresses the onset of the K- condensate, particularly at higher charge fractions and temperatures.

## Method

The authors use a finite-temperature density-dependent relativistic hadron field theory with the DD2 parameter set for nucleons, where baryon-baryon interaction is mediated by sigma, omega, rho, and phi mesons. The antikaon sector is treated within a relativistic mean field model with kaon-meson couplings determined from the quark model and a K- optical potential of -120 MeV. The low-density inhomogeneous matter is calculated using the extended Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium model of Hempel and Schaffner-Bielich, and the final EoS table merges these two regimes by comparing free energies.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses for four EoSs: 2.42 Msolar (nucleons-only HS(DD2)), 2.24 Msolar (with K- condensate only), 2.1 Msolar (with Lambda hyperons only), and 2.05 Msolar (with both Lambda hyperons and K- condensate)
- Corresponding radii at maximum mass: 11.89 km, 12.0 km, 11.58 km, and 11.62 km respectively
- Lambda hyperons appear at approximately 2.2 times nuclear saturation density in cold beta-equilibrated matter
- EoS table covers baryon density from 10^-12 to approximately 1 fm^-3, temperature from 0.1 to 158.48 MeV, and positive charge fraction from 0.01 to 0.60
- Grid spacing: Delta log10(n_B) = 0.04, Delta log10(T) = 0.04, Delta Y_q = 0.01, totaling approximately one million data points
- Nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.149065 fm^-3, binding energy 16.02 MeV per nucleon, incompressibility 242.7 MeV, symmetry energy 31.67 MeV with slope coefficient 55.03 MeV
- Lambda hyperon potential depth U_Lambda^N = -30 MeV in normal nuclear matter
- K- optical potential of -120 MeV used at saturation density

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BHB Lambda phi EoS (Malik, Banik, and Bandyopadhyay, prior work) by adding antikaon condensate, and contrasts with the nucleons-only HS(DD2) EoS of Hempel and Schaffner-Bielich (2014). The new BHB Lambda K- phi EoS table is designed for use in core-collapse supernova and binary neutron star merger simulations, enabling studies of how two forms of strange matter (hyperons and antikaon condensate) compete and coexist in dense astrophysical environments.
