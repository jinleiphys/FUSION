# Improvement of functionals in density functional theory by the inverse Kohn--Sham method and density functional perturbation theory

> We propose a way to improve energy density functionals (EDFs) in the density functional theory based on the combination of the inverse Kohn--Sham method and the density functional perturbation theory. Difference between the known EDF and the exact one is treated as the first-order perturbation. As benchmark calculations, we reproduce the theoretical exchange and correlation functionals in the local density approximation. Systems of noble-gas atoms are used for benchmark calculations, and the ground-state energies and densities, as well as the functionals, are reproduced with good accuracies.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the difference between a known approximate energy density functional (EDF) and the exact EDF can be treated as a first-order perturbation within density functional perturbation theory (DFPT), and that this difference can be determined by combining DFPT with the inverse Kohn-Sham (IKS) method, provided the known functional is already close enough to the exact one for the perturbation treatment to be valid.

## Method

The authors combine the inverse Kohn-Sham method (which extracts the Kohn-Sham potential from a given ground-state density) with first-order density functional perturbation theory. They treat the unknown correction to a known Hartree-exchange-correlation functional as a perturbation, derive a functional equation for this correction, and introduce a simple LDA-type ansatz (a power-law form) for the correction term. The parameters in the ansatz are determined by requiring consistency between two independent expressions for the exact ground-state energy, using two different atomic systems as input. As benchmark calculations, the method is applied to reproduce known theoretical functionals: first, the LDA exchange functional starting from the Hartree functional alone, and second, the LDA exchange-correlation functional (Hartree plus LDA exchange plus PZ81 correlation) starting from the Hartree-plus-LDA-exchange functional.

## Key numbers

* For the first benchmark (reproducing the LDA exchange functional from the Hartree functional using He and Ne as the two systems), the extracted parameters lambda*A_1 and alpha_1 were obtained within 7.2% and 1.0% errors, respectively.
* For the same benchmark using Xe and Rn as the two systems, the extracted parameters lambda*A_1 and alpha_1 were obtained within 2.3% and 0.2% errors, respectively.
* The target LDA exchange functional has the form -3/4 (3/pi)^(1/3) integral [rho(r)]^(4/3) dr (in Hartree atomic units).
* The PZ81 correlation functional (Perdew and Zunger, 1981) is used as the target correlation functional in the second benchmark case.

## Context

This work extends the inverse Kohn-Sham method (originally proposed by Wang and Parr (1993) and by Gorling (1992)) by providing an explicit practical scheme for using IKS results to improve approximate functionals, which had been an open question. The method is demonstrated on electron systems using noble-gas atoms, but the authors note that the same approach could be applied to nuclear systems, where accurate densities from ab initio methods (such as the no-core shell model, self-consistent Green's function method, or in-medium similarity renormalization group) could be used to improve phenomenological nuclear energy density functionals. The work enables a systematic pathway for incorporating high-accuracy density information from experiments or ab initio calculations into the construction of improved density functionals.
