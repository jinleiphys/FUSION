# On symmetries of Hamiltonians describing systems with arbitrary spins

> We consider systems where dynamical variables are the generators of the SU(2) group. A subset of these Hamiltonians is exactly solvable using the Bethe ansatz techniques. We show that Bethe ansatz equations are equivalent to polynomial relationships between the operator invariants, or equivalently, between eigenvalues of those invariants.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Bethe ansatz equations for Hamiltonians built from SU(2) generators are equivalent to polynomial relationships among the operator invariants (or their eigenvalues), and this equivalence holds for all representations of SU(2), not only the spin-1/2 case previously established.

## Method

The authors consider a generic Hamiltonian with one- and two-body interactions written in terms of mutually commuting SU(2) algebras. They derive algebraic equations relating the eigenvalues of the conserved charges \(h_p\) by taking successive derivatives of a differential equation for the function \(\Lambda(\lambda)\), which encodes the Bethe ansatz roots. For a given representation with spin \(j_p\), they obtain recurrence relations that ultimately yield a polynomial equation of degree \(2j_p+1\) in \(\epsilon_p\) (the eigenvalue of \(h_p\)) and degree \(2j_p-1\) in the other \(\epsilon_q\). They verify the polynomial equations reproduce the correct eigenvalues in the \(\mu \to 0\) limit.

## Key numbers

* For \(j_p = 1\), the polynomial equation for \(\epsilon_p\) is \(\epsilon_p^3 - (\sum_{q\neq p} C_{pq}^{(0)}\epsilon_q + K_p^{(0)})\epsilon_p + \mu(\sum_{q\neq p} C_{pq}^{(1)}\epsilon_q + K_p^{(1)}) = 0\), which in the \(\mu \to 0\) limit reduces to \(\epsilon_p^3 - \epsilon_p = 0\) with solutions \(\epsilon_p = -1, 0, 1\).
* For \(j_p = 3/2\), the polynomial equation is degree 4 in \(\epsilon_p\); in the \(\mu \to 0\) limit it reduces to \(\epsilon_p^4 - \frac{5}{2}\epsilon_p^2 + \frac{9}{16} = 0\) with solutions \(\epsilon_p = -3/2, -1/2, 1/2, 3/2\).
* For \(j_p = 2\), the polynomial equation is degree 5 in \(\epsilon_p\); in the \(\mu \to 0\) limit it yields \(\epsilon_p = -2, -1, 0, 1, 2\).
* The number of solutions to the polynomial equations is \(d = \prod_{p=1}^N (2j_p + 1)\), matching the dimension of the Hilbert space.

## Context

This work extends the known equivalence between Bethe ansatz equations and polynomial invariants, previously shown for the \(j_p = 1/2\) case by Faribault (2018), to arbitrary SU(2) representations. It builds on the Richardson (1963) solution for the reduced Hamiltonian and the Gaudin algebraic framework, as reviewed by Balantekin (2018). The results enable the calculation of eigenvalues and conserved charges for systems such as collective neutrino oscillations in the single-angle approximation and pairing interactions in nuclear shell models, without explicitly solving the Bethe ansatz equations.
