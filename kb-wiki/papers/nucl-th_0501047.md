# Relativistic approach to neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering

> A relativistic Green's function and a distorted-wave impulse-approximation approach to charged- and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering are developed. Results for the neutrino (antineutrino) reactions on $^{16}$O and $^{12}$C target nuclei are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function approach to inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering conserves flux by compensating the loss from the imaginary part of the optical potential with a corresponding gain, but in practice the cross sections calculated with only the real part of the optical potential are nearly identical to the full Green's function results, indicating that flux conservation rather than the complex nature of the potential is the dominant condition for these inclusive reactions.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic Green's function method for inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering, where the hadron tensor is expressed via a biorthogonal expansion of the particle-hole Green's operator using eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate. For neutral-current semi-inclusive scattering, they employed a relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) treatment, calculating transition matrix elements in first-order perturbation theory with single-nucleon currents that include possible strange-quark contributions to the vector and axial form factors. Calculations were performed for 16O and 12C target nuclei at various incident neutrino energies.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass parameter: M_A ≈ (1.026 ± 0.021) GeV
- For 16O(ν_μ,μ^-) at E_ν = 500 and 1000 MeV and θ_μ = 30 degrees, the PWIA cross section is about 20-30% higher at the peak than the Green's function approach result
- The ratio of proton to neutron total cross sections for ν(ν̄) quasielastic scattering on 12C shows an enhancement of approximately 15% due to final-state interactions
- The strange axial coupling value g^s_A = -0.19 was used in comparisons with BNL 734 experiment data
- The Weinberg angle sin²θ_W ≈ 0.2313 was used for neutral-current calculations
- The vector form factor mass parameter M_V = 0.843 GeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Green's function approach previously applied to electron scattering (Meucci et al., 2001, 2002) to neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering, both for charged-current inclusive and neutral-current semi-inclusive reactions. The results for neutral-current scattering are compared with data from the BNL 734 experiment (Ahrens et al., 1987), and the sensitivity to strange-quark contributions is explored using form factor parametrizations from Garvey et al. (1993). The approach enables more consistent treatment of final-state interactions in inclusive neutrino reactions and provides a framework for interpreting future neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments aimed at extracting nucleon form factors and strange-quark content.
