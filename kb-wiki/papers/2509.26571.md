# Nuclear state and level densities of actinides with the shell-model Monte Carlo

> Actinides are of great interest in astrophysics and technology applications since they can fission. However, the microscopic calculation of their statistical properties in the presence of correlations poses a major theoretical challenge. The configuration-interaction shell-model is a suitable framework to calculate these properties but the required large model spaces are beyond the reach of conventional diagonalization methods. The shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method enables calculations in very large model spaces and was applied to nuclei as heavy as the lanthanides. Here, we extend the SMMC method to the actinides. Fifteen even-even and odd-mass actinides $^{232}$Th, $^{\textrm{234-239}}$U, $^{\textrm{240-243}}$Pu, $^{\textrm{246-248}}$Cm, and $^{250}$Cf are studied using a single-particle model space that is larger than one major shell each for protons and neutrons, with a total dimension of the many-particle space as large as $10^{32}$. We calculate nuclear state densities of these actinides and find they are strongly enhanced in comparison with mean-field densities. We use spin projection methods to calculate nuclear level densities and average $s$-wave neutron resonance spacings, both of which are found to be in good agreement with experiments.

# Nuclear state and level densities of actinides with the shell-model Monte Carlo

## Key claim
The shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method is extended to actinides for the first time, and the calculated nuclear level densities and average s-wave neutron resonance spacings show good agreement with experimental data, but the agreement is better for even-even nuclei than for odd-mass nuclei, where uncertainties are larger due to ground-state energy determination.

## Method
The authors use the shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method with a configuration-interaction shell-model Hamiltonian in a model space based on a doubly-magic 208Pb core, with valence protons in the 82-126 shell plus the 1g9/2 orbital and valence neutrons in the 126-184 shell plus the 1h11/2 orbital. The interaction is a pairing-plus-multipole interaction with good Monte Carlo sign, and thermal averages are computed in the canonical ensemble using particle-number projection via discrete Fourier transforms. Nuclear state densities are obtained from the saddle-point approximation to the inverse Laplace transform of the partition function, and nuclear level densities are computed using spin projection methods with a discrete Fourier transform over the spin component M.

## Key numbers
- Fifteen actinides studied: 232Th, 234-239U, 240-243Pu, 246-248Cm, and 250Cf
- Total many-particle model space dimension greater than 10^32 for the heaviest nuclei
- Thermal energies calculated up to beta = 40 MeV^-1 for even-even nuclei and beta = 8 MeV^-1 for odd-mass nuclei
- Enhancement factors K = rho_SMMC/rho_HFB between 10 and 25 for even-even uranium isotopes at excitation energies of interest
- For 238U: SMMC NLD at neutron separation energy Sn = 19.7 +/- 2.8 x 10^6 MeV^-1 (experiment: 20 +/- 6 x 10^6 MeV^-1); D0 = 3.62 +/- 0.53 eV (experiment: 3.5 +/- 0.8 eV)
- For 232Th: SMMC NLD at Sn = 20.5 +/- 3.2 x 10^6 MeV^-1 (experiment: 30 +/- 8 x 10^6 MeV^-1); D0 = 1.21 +/- 0.20 eV (experiment not given)
- For 240Pu: SMMC NLD at Sn = 24.7 +/- 3.5 x 10^6 MeV^-1 (experiment: 32.7 +/- 6.6 x 10^6 MeV^-1); D0 = 3.01 +/- 0.44 eV (experiment: 2.20 +/- 0.09 eV)
- Spin-cutoff parameters sigma(Sn) within a few percent of rigid-body values

## Context
This work extends the SMMC method, previously applied to lanthanides with A ~ 160 (Alhassid 2008, Ozen 2013, Ozen 2015, Alhassid 2014, Guttormsen 2021, Mercenne 2024, DeMartini 2025), to the actinide region for the first time. The SMMC results are compared with finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the code HF-SHELL (Ryssens 2021), which are found to underestimate state densities by about an order of magnitude due to missing rotational correlations. The calculated level densities agree with Oslo method experimental data (Guttormsen 2013, Laplace 2016, Zeiser 2019) and neutron resonance data from RIPL-3 (Capote 2009). This work enables future microscopic studies of fission-related statistical properties and spin distributions in actinides that are beyond the reach of conventional diagonalization methods.
