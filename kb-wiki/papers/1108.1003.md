# Effect of different Gaussian width on disappearance of flow

> In the present paper, we reduce and enhance the scaled Gaussian width(SGW) by 30% from the normal SGW of the systems and see its effect on balance energy. The scaled Gaussian width can be defined as the ratio of Gaussian width used for any nuclei to the Gaussian width used for Au nuclei(i.e. 8.66 $fm^2$).

## Key claim

Reducing the scaled Gaussian width (SGW) by 20% from its normal value for each system, within the IQMD model using a soft equation of state and reduced isospin-dependent cross-section, yields balance energies that better match experimental data than calculations with normal SGW, except for some lighter nuclei which require an enhanced SGW.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of heavy-ion collisions for five systems (from \(^{64}Zn_{30}+^{58}Ni_{28}\) to \(^{197}Au_{79}+^{197}Au_{79}\)) using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state and a reduced isospin-dependent cross-section (\(\sigma = 0.9\sigma_{NN}\)). They varied the scaled Gaussian width (SGW), defined as the ratio of the Gaussian width used for a given nucleus to the width used for Au (8.66 fm²), by reducing and enhancing it by 30% from the normal value, and extracted the balance energy (energy of vanishing flow) for each system.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{197}Au_{79}+^{197}Au_{79}\) system, the experimental balance energy is in the range of 40-45 MeV/nucleon; a SGW of 0.8 (20% reduction) best explains these experimental findings.
- The power-law fit \(C(A_{TOT})^\tau\) for balance energy versus total system mass yields \(\tau = -0.47 \pm 0.03\) for the present calculation with 20% reduced SGW, compared to the experimental \(\tau = -0.46 \pm 0.01\).
- The QMD+40mb calculation gave \(\tau = -0.49 \pm 0.04\), and the IQMD model with normal SGW gave \(\tau = -0.40 \pm 0.14\).
- Simulations were performed at incident energies between 45 and 200 MeV/nucleon, with impact parameters and Gaussian widths specified for each system (e.g., \(^{86}Kr_{36}+^{93}Nb_{41}\) at b=4 fm, L=0.6L where L=8.66 fm²).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of collective transverse in-plane flow and balance energy, particularly those using the IQMD model (Kumar 2010) and QMD+40mb calculations (Sood 2004). It demonstrates that the choice of Gaussian width in the wave packet description significantly affects the energy of vanishing flow, and that a 20% reduction in SGW improves agreement with experimental balance energy data across a range of system masses. The results enable more refined modeling of nuclear matter equation of state and nucleon-nucleon cross-section effects, while noting that lighter nuclei require further study with enhanced SGW.
