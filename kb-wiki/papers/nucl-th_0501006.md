# On Distribution Functions for Partons in Nuclei

> We suggest that a previously conjectured relation between Structure Functions (SF) for nuclei and nucleons also links distribution functions (df) for partons in a nucleus and in nucleons. The above suggestion ensures in principle identical results for SF $F_2^A$, whether computed with hadronic or partonic degrees of freedom. In practice there are differences, due to different $F_2^n$ input. We show that the thus defined nuclear parton distribution functions (pdf) respect standard sumrules. In addition we numerically compare some moments of nuclear SF, and find agreement between results, using hadronic and partonic descriptions. We present computations of EMC ratios for both, and compare those with hadronic predictions and data. In spite of substantial differences in the participating SF, the two representations produce approximately the same EMC ratios. The apparent correlation between the above deviations is ascribed to a sumrule for $F_2^A$. We conclude with a discussion of alternative approaches to nuclear pdf.

# On Distribution Functions for Partons in Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors propose that a previously conjectured relation between nuclear and nucleon structure functions (SF) can be extended to define nuclear parton distribution functions (pdf) that produce the same nuclear structure function F₂^A whether computed with hadronic or partonic degrees of freedom, but only in principle; in practice, differences arise due to different input for the neutron structure function F₂^n, particularly at larger x values.

## Method

The authors define nuclear pdf by applying the same convolution relation used for hadronic structure functions, linking nuclear SF to nucleon SF via the point-nucleon spectral function f^{PN,A}, directly to parton distributions, with no flavor mixing between valence and sea quarks. They then compute nuclear structure functions F₂^A and EMC ratios in both the hadronic and partonic representations, using the CTEQ6 parametrization for the partonic approach and experimentally extracted F₂^n for the hadronic approach, and compare results with data for deuterium, helium, carbon, iron, and gold targets at Q² = 3.5 and 5.0 GeV².

## Key numbers

- For Q² = 3.5 and 5.0 GeV², the zeroth moments M₀^A of F₂^A for D, He, C, and Fe show differences between hadronic and partonic representations of at most approximately 1-3%, with values ranging from 0.1338 to 0.1506 depending on target and representation.
- The momentum sumrule violation from the convolution approach is found to be no more than approximately 1% for 4 targets at Q² = 3.5 and 5.0 GeV².
- For the pair D and Fe, the difference of integrals in the hadron and pdf representations is approximately -0.03.
- EMC ratios computed in the two representations agree closely up to x ≈ 0.65, beyond which deviations grow with the pdf representation giving larger values than the hadronic representation up to x ≈ 0.90, where the ordering reverses.
- For the smallest x values down to 10⁻⁵, the pdf results produce EMC ratios that slowly reach values of approximately 0.6-0.7 due to screening effects.

## Context

This work extends the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) theory for inclusive scattering, as previously generalized by Gurvitz and Rinat (1995, 2002), to define nuclear pdf without adjustable parameters. It contrasts with alternative approaches such as those of Eskola et al. (1998), who use parametrized input at a reference Q₀² with parameters constrained by data, and Kumano and co-workers (2001, 2004), who assume linear relations between nuclear and nucleon pdf with species-dependent weight functions. The approach also differs from mean-field models by Mineo et al. (2004) and Steffens et al. (2004), which replace quark interactions with scalar and vector mean fields. The results enable comparison of hadronic and partonic descriptions of nuclear structure functions and identify the neutron structure function input as the primary source of discrepancy between the two representations at large x.
