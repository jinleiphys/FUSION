# Relativistic Dynamics of Fluctuations and QCD Critical Point

> We review the recent development on a systematic deterministic formalism describing dynamics of both bulk and fluctuations in an arbitrary relativistic hydrodynamic flow carrying conserved charges. In particular, we discuss the implementation of such general formalism near the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The paper reviews a systematic deterministic formalism for describing both bulk evolution and fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamic flow carrying conserved charges, and discusses its implementation near the QCD critical point. The formalism resolves the "infinite noise" problem by renormalizing divergences into bare hydrodynamic variables, producing cutoff-independent equations suitable for numerical simulation.

## Method

The authors develop a deterministic approach that replaces stochastic Langevin dynamics with evolution equations for ensemble-averaged hydrodynamic variables and two-point correlation functions of fluctuations. They introduce a confluent derivative and confluent Wigner function to handle the frame-dependent nature of equal-time correlators in relativistic flow, then diagonalize the evolution operator to obtain kinetic equations for sound, diffusive, and shear modes. Near the critical point, they modify the specific heat and relaxation coefficients using Ornstein-Zernike and Kawasaki approximations, resulting in an extended formalism called Hydro++.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium two-point function diverges as \(G^{(\text{eq})}_{AB}\sim\Lambda^3\) with wavenumber cutoff \(\Lambda\), requiring renormalization
- Sound mode relaxation rate: \(\Gamma_L = \gamma_L q^2\) where \(\gamma_L\) is the sound attenuation coefficient
- Diffusive mode relaxation rate: \(\Gamma_\lambda = \gamma_\lambda q^2\) where \(\gamma_\lambda\) is related to thermal conductivity
- Shear mode relaxation rate: \(\Gamma_\eta = \gamma_\eta q^2\) where \(\gamma_\eta\) is related to shear viscosity
- Near the critical point: \(\Gamma_\lambda \sim \xi^{-3}\) and \(\Gamma_\eta \sim \xi^{-2}\), with \(\xi\) the equilibrium correlation length
- The critical part of heat conductivity: \(\kappa_\xi = c_p T / (6\pi\eta\xi)\) where \(\eta\) is shear viscosity
- The \(q\)-dependent specific heat follows Ornstein-Zernike form: \(c_p(q) = c_p / [1 + (q\xi)^2]\)

## Context

This work extends the deterministic hydro-kinetic approach previously developed for neutral fluids with arbitrary background (An, 2019) to accommodate charged fluids and critical dynamics. It builds upon the stochastic formalism of Landau and Lifshitz (1950s) and the renormalization approach of Andreev (1978), as well as the Hydro+ framework (Stephanov, 2018) which accounts for the slowest mode near the critical point. The formalism is intended for interpreting experimental results from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan Program, and future work will include higher-point functions and extension to first-order phase transitions.
