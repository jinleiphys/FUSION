# Medium-induced gluon radiation beyond the eikonal approximation

> In this work we improve existing calculations of radiative energy loss by computing corrections that implement energy-momentum conservation, previously only implemented a posteriori, in a rigorous way. Using the path-integral formalism, we compute in-medium splittings allowing transverse motion of all particles in the emission process, thus relaxing the assumption that only the softest particle is permitted such movement. This work constitutes the extension of the computation carried out for x$\rightarrow$1 in Phys. Lett. B718 (2012) 160-168, to all values of x, the momentum fraction of the energy of the parent parton carried by the emitted gluon. In order to accomplish a general description of the whole in-medium showering process, in this work we allow for arbitrary formation times for the emitted gluon. We provide general expressions and their realisation in the path integral formalism within the harmonic oscillator approximation.

## Key claim
This work extends previous calculations of medium-induced gluon radiation beyond the eikonal approximation by rigorously implementing energy-momentum conservation and allowing transverse motion of all particles in the emission process, without assuming small formation times relative to the medium length. The authors provide general expressions for the double differential spectrum in energy and transverse momentum for all values of x (the momentum fraction carried by the emitted gluon), whereas previous work in Phys. Lett. B718 (2012) 160-168 only treated the x→1 limit.

## Method
Using the path-integral formalism within the harmonic oscillator approximation, the authors compute in-medium gluon emission off a quark by summing two contributions: emission vertices occurring outside or inside the medium. The medium averages are computed in the large Nc limit by expanding Wilson lines to second order in the fields, and the dipole cross section is approximated by its small-distance component σ(r) = (1/2) q̂ r². The calculation allows arbitrary formation times for the emitted gluon, unlike the small-formation-time limit employed by Blaizot, Dominguez, Iancu, and Mehtar-Tani (2013) for the g→gg splitting.

## Key numbers
- The dipole cross section is approximated as σ(r) = (1/2) q̂ r², where q̂ is the transport coefficient representing average transverse momentum squared acquired per mean free path.
- The calculation extends the previous work for x→1 (Phys. Lett. B718 (2012) 160-168) to all values of x, the momentum fraction of the parent parton energy carried by the emitted gluon.
- The medium length is denoted L₊, and the formation time t_form is not constrained relative to L₊ (i.e., no assumption that t_form << L₊).

## Context
This work improves upon the calculation by Blaizot, Dominguez, Iancu, and Mehtar-Tani (2013) for the g→gg splitting by removing the assumption of small formation times relative to the medium length, and extends the authors' own previous work (Apolinario, Armesto, Milhano, and Salgado, 2012) from the x→1 limit to all values of x. The results recover both the soft limit from Zakharov (1996), Baier et al. (1996), Salgado and Wiedemann (2003), and Gyulassy et al. (2000), and the hard limit from Apolinario et al. (2012) in the appropriate limits. The general expressions can serve as input for Monte Carlo codes describing the full in-medium showering process.
