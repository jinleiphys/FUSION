# Chiral Phase Transition in QCD and Vector Manifestation

> Spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking is one of the most important features in low-energy QCD. The chiral symmetry is expected to be restored at very high temperature and/or density. Accompanied by the chiral phase transition, properties of hadrons will be changed especially near the critical point. The study of the phenomena associated with the chiral phase transition will give us some clues on the connection between the chiral symmetry and the low-energy hadron dynamics. We develop the theory based on the hidden local symmetry (HLS) at finite temperature, which is an effective field theory of QCD and includes pions and vector mesons as the dynamical degrees of freedom, and study the chiral phase transition in hot matter. We show that the chiral symmetry is restored as the vector manifestation (VM), in which the massless degenerate pion (and its flavor partners) and the longitudinal $ρ$ meson (and its flavor partners) as the chiral partner. We also present several predictions based on the VM. We estimate the critical temperature $T_c$ and show the following phenomena near $T_c$: the vector charge susceptibility becomes equal to the axial-vector charge susceptibility; the vector dominance of the electromagnetic form factor of the pion is largely violated; the pion velocity is close to the speed of light. Furthermore, we show that the remnant of the VM can be clearly seen in the system of heavy mesons. We expect that the VM and its predictions are testable by current and future experiments and the lattice analysis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chiral symmetry restoration in QCD at high temperature occurs via the "vector manifestation" (VM), in which the massless pion and the longitudinal rho meson become chiral partners, but this result depends on the assumption that only pions and vector mesons are the relevant degrees of freedom near the critical temperature and that the phase transition is second order or weakly first order.

## Method

The author develops the hidden local symmetry (HLS) effective field theory at finite temperature, which includes pions and vector mesons as dynamical degrees of freedom, and applies Wilsonian matching to perturbative QCD to determine the intrinsic temperature dependences of the bare parameters. The renormalization group equations are then used to study the behavior of the theory near the critical temperature, incorporating both hadronic thermal corrections from the soft region and intrinsic thermal effects from the hard region integrated out during matching.

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation scenario previously proposed by Harada and Yamawaki (2003) for zero temperature to finite temperature using the HLS framework, and builds on the Brown-Rho scaling proposal (1991) which suggested dropping vector meson masses near chiral restoration. The predictions of the VM, including violation of vector dominance and the pion velocity approaching the speed of light near \(T_c\), are expected to be testable by heavy-ion experiments at RHIC and LHC, as well as by lattice QCD simulations.
