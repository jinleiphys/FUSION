# The astrophysical rate of 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne via the (p,t) reaction in inverse kinematics

> A recoil coincidence technique has been applied to measure the alpha-decay branching ratios of near-threshold states in 19Ne. Populating these states using the (p,t) reaction in inverse kinematics, we detected the recoils and their decay products with 100% geometric efficiency using a magnetic spectrometer. Combining our branching ratio measurements with independent determinations of the radiative widths of these states, we calculate the astrophysical rate of 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne. Using this reaction rate, we perform hydrodynamic calculations of nova outbursts and conclude that no significant breakout from the hot CNO cycles into the rp process occurs in novae via 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne.

## Key claim

The authors measure alpha-decay branching ratios of near-threshold states in 19Ne using a (p,t) reaction in inverse kinematics and, combining these with independent radiative width determinations, calculate the astrophysical rate of 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne; they then conclude that even using an extreme 99.73% confidence level upper limit on this rate, no significant breakout from the hot CNO cycles into the rp process occurs in novae via this reaction.

## Method

The authors populated states in 19Ne via the p(21Ne,t)19Ne reaction at 43 MeV/u using a 910 MeV 21Ne beam on a (CH2)n target at the KVI. They detected both triton ejectiles and 19Ne recoils (or 15O alpha-decay products) with 100% geometric efficiency using the Big-Bite Spectrometer at 0 degrees, identifying particles via phoswich detectors providing energy loss, total energy, and time-of-flight information. Alpha-decay branching ratios were determined from the ratio of 15O-triton coincidences to 19Ne-triton coincidences, with upper limits for states showing no statistically significant alpha decay derived via Bayesian statistical analysis.

## Key numbers

* Upper limit on the alpha-decay branching ratio of the 4.033 MeV 3/2+ state in 19Ne: B_alpha < 4.3 x 10^-4 (90% confidence level)
* Upper limit on the alpha-decay branching ratio of the 4.379 MeV 7/2+ state in 19Ne: B_alpha < 3.9 x 10^-3 (90% confidence level)
* Measured alpha-decay branching ratio of the 4.600 MeV (5/2+) state: B_alpha = 0.32 +/- 0.04
* Measured alpha-decay branching ratio of the 4.712 MeV (5/2-) state: B_alpha = 0.85 +/- 0.04
* Measured alpha-decay branching ratio of the 5.092 MeV 5/2+ state: B_alpha = 0.90 +/- 0.06
* Adopted radiative width of the 4.033 MeV state: Gamma_gamma = 45 (+200, -33) meV, based purely on experimental data
* The 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne reaction rate is less than 0.3% of the 15O beta+ decay rate at all times throughout the whole accreted envelope in hydrodynamic nova simulations

## Context

This work extends prior studies of alpha-decay branching ratios in 19Ne by Magnus (1990) and Laird (2002), which did not yield useful information on the critical 4.033 MeV state that dominates the reaction rate at nova temperatures. The authors provide the first experimental upper limit on the alpha-decay branching ratio of this state, enabling a more definitive calculation of the 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne reaction rate. The hydrodynamic simulations using this rate confirm earlier conclusions by Wiescher (1999) and Iliadis (2002) that this reaction plays no important role in nova explosions, and the results enable future work to focus on other breakout reactions such as 18F(p,gamma)19Ne and 18Ne(alpha,p)21Na.
