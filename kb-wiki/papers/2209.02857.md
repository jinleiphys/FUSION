# Comparative study on charge radii and their kinks at magic numbers

> Isotope dependences of charge radii, i.e., isotope shifts, calculated by the Skyrme Hartree-Fock, the relativistic mean-field, and the relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations are compared against the experimental data of magic and semimagic nuclei. It is found that the tensor interaction plays a role in reproducing the ``kink'' behavior, irregularity of isotope shifts at the neutron magic number, in the relativistic Hartree-Fock approach. With several Skyrme models, it is found that the kink behavior can be reproduced with the spin-orbit interaction having nonzero isovector channel. The single-particle orbitals near the Fermi energy are crucial to determine the kink size. The effects of the symmetry energy and the pairing interaction are also discussed in relation to the kink behavior.

## Key claim

The kink behavior in charge radii at neutron magic numbers can be reproduced by standard mean-field models without additional terms, but the size of the kink depends critically on the spin-orbit interaction (including its isovector channel) and the tensor interaction, with the occupation probability of specific single-particle orbitals (particularly the 1h9/2 orbital) above the shell gap being the decisive factor.

## Method

The authors performed nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using nine Skyrme energy density functionals (including SAMi-T with tensor interaction and SAMi-noT without it) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) and relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) calculations using eight relativistic functionals, all with spherical symmetry and contact pairing interactions. They computed charge radii for Sn, Pb, and Ca isotopes and defined a quantitative kink indicator Δ²Rch as the discretized second derivative of the charge radius at the magic neutron number.

## Key numbers

- Experimental kink indicator Δ²Rch for Sn isotopes at N=82 is +15.0 × 10⁻³ fm
- SAMi EDF gives Δ²Rch = +9.863 × 10⁻³ fm for Sn; SAMi-T gives +7.311 × 10⁻³ fm; SAMi-noT gives +6.217 × 10⁻³ fm
- UNEDF1 gives Δ²Rch = -3.027 × 10⁻³ fm (anti-kink) for Sn; UNEDF2 gives -2.279 × 10⁻³ fm; UNEDF0 gives -2.016 × 10⁻³ fm
- PKO1* RHF gives Δ²Rch = +16.119 × 10⁻³ fm for Sn, closest to the experimental value of +15.0 × 10⁻³ fm
- PKO3 RHF gives Δ²Rch = +14.441 × 10⁻³ fm; PKO1 gives +9.394 × 10⁻³ fm; PKO2 gives +7.050 × 10⁻³ fm
- DD-LZ1 RMF gives Δ²Rch = +11.795 × 10⁻³ fm; DD-PC1 gives +4.451 × 10⁻³ fm; PKDD gives +3.078 × 10⁻³ fm
- Experimental ΔRch(132) for Sn is +7.4 × 10⁻³ fm; ΔRch(134) is +22.4 × 10⁻³ fm
- Pairing strengths V0 for Skyrme EDFs range from 127.6 MeV·fm³ (UNEDF0) to 225.5 MeV·fm³ (SAMi-T)
- Single proton radius used: rEp = 0.8409 fm; single neutron mean-square radius: rEn² = -0.1161 fm²

## Context

This work extends the discussion of charge radius kinks from Reinhard (1995), who showed that Pb isotope kinks require proper adjustment of isoscalar and isovector spin-orbit strengths in Skyrme functionals, and from Perera (2021), who emphasized occupation probabilities above the shell gap in relativistic mean-field calculations. The study also relates to Nakada (2019), who found that three-body spin-orbit interactions reproduce the kink using M3Y-P6a. The paper enables systematic comparison of nonrelativistic and relativistic approaches to identify which features of the spin-orbit field and tensor interaction are essential, and suggests that beyond-mean-field effects are needed for Ca isotopes.
