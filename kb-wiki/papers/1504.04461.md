# A primordial r-process?

> Baryon density inhomogeneities in the early universe can give rise to a floor of heavy elements (up to $A\approx 270$) produced in a primordial r-process with fission cycling. A parameter study with variation of the global baryon to photon ratio $η$, and under inclusion of neutron diffusion effects was performed. New results concerning the dependence of the results on nuclear physics parameters are presented.

## Key claim
Baryon density inhomogeneities in the early universe can produce a floor of heavy elements up to mass number A ≈ 270 through a primordial r-process with fission cycling, but only under specific conditions of the baryon-to-photon ratio η and the comoving length scale of inhomogeneities; the production is a sensitive measure of η and can provide an independent upper limit for the product Ω_b H_0^2.

## Method
The authors performed a parameter study using a coupled two-part reaction network: a general network of 655 nuclei from neutrons and protons up to krypton (Z=36), and an r-process code containing 6033 nuclei from stability to the neutron drip line up to Z=114, including beta-delayed fission. They varied the baryon-to-photon ratio η (expressed as η_10 = η × 10^10) and the comoving length scale d/a of inhomogeneities, incorporating neutron diffusion effects with a diffusive loss rate κ = 4.2×10^4 / (d/a)_(cm MeV) × T_9^(5/4) (1+0.716 T_9)^(1/2) s^(-1) for temperatures 0.2 < T_9 < 1.

## Key numbers
- Four values of η_10 were used: 416, 104, 52, and 10.4, corresponding to (h_50, Ω_b) pairs of (2.5,1), (1.3,1), (1,0.8), and (1,0.16) respectively.
- Four comoving length scales d/a were considered: ∞ (case 0), 10^7.5 cm MeV (case 1), 10^6.5 cm MeV (case 2), and 10^5.5 cm MeV (case 3), corresponding to distances between nucleation sites of ∞, 2700 m, 270 m, and 27 m at the quark-hadron phase transition.
- For η_10 = 416 and no diffusion (case 0), the mass fraction of heavy nuclei beyond Kr was 0.170×10^(-2); for case 1 it was 0.133×10^(-4); for case 2 it was 0.190×10^(-13); case 3 produced no heavy elements.
- For η_10 = 104 and case 0, the mass fraction was 0.227×10^(-11); for case 1 it was 0.735×10^(-13); cases 2 and 3 produced none.
- For η_10 = 52 and case 0, the mass fraction was 0.628×10^(-15); for case 1 it was 0.253×10^(-16); cases 2 and 3 produced none.
- For η_10 = 10.4, no heavy elements were produced in any case.
- Fission cycling enhanced the initial seed amount of heavy elements by 8 orders of magnitude for η_10 = 416 and no diffusion.
- A factor of 3 increase in the ^8Li(α,n)^11B rate and a factor of 10 increase in the ^18O(n,γ)^19O rate together changed heavy element abundances by a total factor of 30.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of inhomogeneous big bang nucleosynthesis by Applegate (1987) and Applegate (1988) by including a detailed r-process network with fission cycling and exploring the dependence on nuclear physics parameters. The results provide constraints on Ω_b h_50^2 from heavy element production that complement limits from light elements (Li, Be, B) studied by Meyer (1993), Kurki-Suonio (1990), Ryan (1990), and Duncan (1992), though the light element constraints remain tighter. The paper enables future work to refine these limits as nuclear reaction rates are improved, particularly the ^8Li(α,n)^11B and ^18O(n,γ)^19O rates.
