# Soft photon bremsstrahlung at next-to-leading power

> The emission of soft radiation provides a fundamental probe of the consistency of the underlying quantum field theory. Correspondingly, the measurement of the soft photon bremsstrahlung, such as the one proposed with the planned future upgrade of the ALICE experiment at the LHC, is of great interest. In this letter we explore the possibility to implement analytic techniques that have been recently developed for soft gluon resummation at Next-to-Leading-Power (NLP) in the context of the soft photon spectrum. We provide a formula for the differential cross-section with shifted kinematics that is particularly suitable for numerical implementations. We also discuss the impact of loop corrections to Low's theorem due to radiative jet functions.

## Key claim

The authors show that analytic techniques recently developed for next-to-leading-power (NLP) soft gluon resummation in QCD can be adapted to soft photon bremsstrahlung, providing a formula for the differential cross-section with shifted kinematics that is suitable for numerical implementations, while noting that loop corrections from radiative jet functions modify Low's theorem at NLP for massless particles.

## Method

The authors derive a shifted-kinematics formulation of the Low-Burnett-Kroll (LBK) theorem for soft photon emission, generalizing the approach of Del Duca (2017) from two-particle to arbitrary-number-of-leg processes. They then compute one-loop QCD corrections to the soft photon spectrum using radiative jet functions, isolating the logarithmic contributions that arise when the soft photon energy satisfies m^2/Q <= omega_k ~ m, which includes the massless limit relevant for hadronic external states.

## Key numbers

* The leading-power (LP) soft photon bremsstrahlung cross-section contains terms of order 1/omega_k, where omega_k is the soft photon energy
* The first NLP corrections are of order (omega_k)^0 ~ 1, corresponding to the LBK theorem
* The condition for virtual collinear contributions to be absent is omega_k << m^2/Q, where m is the mass of the lightest charged particle and Q is the typical energy of the process
* The larger region where radiative jet corrections become important is m^2/Q <= omega_k ~ m
* The one-loop radiative jet function J^{mu(1)} in d=4-2epsilon dimensions contains terms proportional to log(bar{mu}^2 / (2 p_i dot k))

## Context

This work extends the NLP resummation techniques developed for QCD processes with colorless final states by Del Duca (2017) and others to the case of observed soft photon emissions. The authors address the long-standing discrepancy between measured soft photon yields in hadronic collisions (observed by DELPHI (2005, 2007, 2010) and other experiments) and theoretical predictions based solely on the leading-power eikonal approximation. The shifted-kinematics formulation provides a path toward extending QCD resummation at NLP to processes with colored external states, while the radiative jet corrections offer a possible explanation for the observed excess in soft photon production from hadrons.
