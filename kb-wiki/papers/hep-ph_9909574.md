# Patterns of Symmetry Breaking in QCD at High Baryon Density

> We study the structure of QCD at very large baryon density for an arbitrary number of flavors $N_f$. We provide evidence that for any number of flavors larger than $N_f=2$ chiral symmetry remains broken at asymptotically large chemical potential. For $N_c=N_f=3$, chiral symmetry breaking follows the standard pattern $SU(3)_L\times SU(3)_R\to SU(3)$, but for $N_f>3$ unusual patterns emerge. We study the case $N_f=3$ in more detail and calculate the magnitude of the chiral order parameters $<\barψψ>$ and $<(\barψψ)^2>$ in perturbative QCD. We show that, asymptotically, $<\barψψ>^{1/3}$ is much smaller than $<(\barψψ)^2>^{1/6}$. The result can be understood in terms of an approximate discrete symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper provides evidence that for any number of flavors larger than \(N_f=2\), chiral symmetry remains broken at asymptotically large chemical potential, but the pattern of symmetry breaking depends on \(N_f\); for \(N_f=3\) the pattern matches low-density QCD, while for \(N_f>3\) unusual patterns emerge that preclude a continuous connection between high- and low-density phases.

## Method

The author constructs a BCS-like effective potential for QCD at high baryon density using a bosonization method with collective diquark fields, then minimizes the grand canonical potential numerically to determine the color-flavor structure of the superconducting gap matrix for \(N_f=2\) through \(N_f=6\) with \(N_c=3\). For the \(N_f=3\) case, the gap equation is solved perturbatively including both color-flavor anti-symmetric and symmetric components, using the Nambu-Gorkov formalism and Eliashberg-type equations with long-range magnetic gluon exchanges.

## Key numbers

- For \(N_f=3\) in the color-flavor locked phase, the dominant anti-symmetric gap is \(\Delta_A \simeq 2^{-1/3} \cdot 256\pi^4 (2/N_f)^{5/2} \mu g^{-5} \exp\left(-\frac{3\pi^2}{\sqrt{2}g}\right)\).
- The symmetric gap for \(N_f=3\) is suppressed: \(\Delta_S \simeq \frac{g}{\pi} \frac{\sqrt{2}\log(2)}{36} \Delta_A\).
- The condensation energy ratio of the color-flavor locked state to the \(N_f=2\) state is \(\epsilon(CFL)/\epsilon(N_f=2) \simeq 1.9\) in the weak coupling limit.
- The superfluid condensate for the anti-symmetric component is \(\phi_A = 2\left(\frac{\mu^2}{2\pi^2}\right) \frac{3\sqrt{2}\pi}{g} \Delta\).
- The four-fermion condensate \(\langle (\bar\psi\psi)^2 \rangle = 6\phi_A^2\) in the mean field approximation.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity at high baryon density, building on the renormalization group classification of Fermi surface instabilities by Schaefer and Wilczek (1998) and the discovery of the color-flavor locked phase by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998). The paper shows that for \(N_f=3\) the symmetry breaking pattern \(SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R \times U(1)_V \to SU(3)_V\) matches low-density QCD, but for \(N_f>3\) the patterns differ, implying a phase transition between high and low density. The calculation of the chiral order parameters \(\langle\bar\psi\psi\rangle\) and \(\langle(\bar\psi\psi)^2\rangle\) in perturbative QCD enables future work on the low-energy effective action of dense quark matter.
