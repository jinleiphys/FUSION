# Dimensional Reduction for Fermions

> 5 pages using world_sci.sty, which is appended to the file. Full postscript version available via anonymous ftp from risc0.ca.infn.it:/pub/private/lissia/infnca-th-94-17.ps (192.84.132.4) To appear in the Proceedings of the ``Workshop on Quantum Infrared Physics'', Paris, France, 6-10 June 1994

## Key claim

Dimensional reduction for operators involving fermion fields is possible only when the ultraviolet behavior of the running coupling constant generates a new scale smaller than the temperature, such as \(g^2(T)T\) in asymptotically free theories; otherwise, the lack of a kinematic scale hierarchy prevents even partial reduction.

## Method

The authors generalize the concept of dimensional reduction to Green's functions with external fermion lines by splitting each diagram into a light part (where all loop frequencies take their smallest Matsubara values) and a heavy part, then expanding the heavy part at zero external momentum. They illustrate the general criterion by explicitly calculating the screening mass in the 1+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model in the large-\(N\) limit, using the bubble graph at high temperature and renormalizing in a \(T\)-independent \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme.

## Key numbers

- The screening mass in the Gross-Neveu model is \(\tilde{m} = 2\pi T \left[1 - \frac{1}{\pi} g^2(T) + \mathcal{O}(g^4(T))\right]\), where \(g^2(T) = \pi / \ln(T/T_c)\) and \(T_c \equiv \Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}} e^{\gamma_E} / \pi\).
- The renormalized coupling in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme satisfies \(2\pi / g^2(\mu) = \ln(4\mu^2 e^{\gamma_E} / \pi T_c^2)\).
- The leading momentum dependence of the bubble graph is \(-(2N/\pi)(4\pi^2 T^2 / q_1^2)\), with corrections of order \(q_1^2 / T^2\).

## Context

This work extends the standard dimensional reduction formalism, previously applied only to bosonic zero modes (Appelquist, 1975; Landsman, 1989), to fermionic operators where the lowest Matsubara frequencies are \(\pm \pi T\) and no tree-level scale separation exists. The result provides a formal basis for the non-relativistic reduction picture used by Koch (1992), Hansson (1993), and Schramm (1993) to interpret lattice screening data at high temperature. The authors note that in theories with a finite ultraviolet fixed point, such as the 2+1 Gross-Neveu model (Huang and Lissia, 1994), even partial dimensional reduction fails.
