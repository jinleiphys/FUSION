# Relativistic versus Nonrelativistic Optical Potentials in A(e,e'p)B Reactions

> We investigate the role of relativistic and nonrelativistic optical potentials used in the analysis of ($e,e'p$) data. We find that the relativistic calculations produce smaller ($e,e'p$) cross sections even in the case in which both relativistic and nonrelativistic optical potentials fit equally well the elastic proton--nucleus scattering data. Compared to the nonrelativistic impulse approximation, this effect is due to a depletion in the nuclear interior of the relativistic nucleon current, which should be taken into account in the nonrelativistic treatment by a proper redefinition of the effective current operator.

## Key claim

Relativistic calculations of (e,e'p) cross sections produce smaller values than nonrelativistic calculations even when both relativistic and nonrelativistic optical potentials fit elastic proton-nucleus scattering data equally well, due to a depletion of the relativistic nucleon current in the nuclear interior that is not captured by standard nonrelativistic treatments.

## Method

The authors compared fully relativistic Dirac-equation-based calculations of (e,e'p) reduced cross sections for 208Pb and 40Ca with nonrelativistic calculations using the DEEPY code, employing three types of optical potentials: standard phenomenological nonrelativistic potentials, Dirac-equation-based (DEB) potentials derived from relativistic S-V potentials, and DEB potentials multiplied by the Darwin factor K(r). All calculations were performed in plane wave Born approximation (PWBA) with no electron Coulomb distortion, using the upper component of relativistic bound state wave functions for consistency, and the free nucleon current operator with dipole form factors.

## Key numbers

- For the 3s1/2 state in 208Pb, the ratio of nonrelativistic to relativistic reduced cross sections at their maxima is between 1.2 and 1.6 when using standard optical potentials
- The Darwin factor K(r) has a steady interior value of about 0.79 (from A(r) ≈ 0.63), going to unity asymptotically
- The Perey factor with β=1.0 fm produces a depth of 0.83, comparable to the K(r) depth of 0.79, and reproduces the relativistic result well when combined with the DEB potential
- For the 2d3/2 orbital in 208Pb, the nonrelativistic result with DEB potential plus K(r) matches the fully relativistic result
- The calculations used a fixed outgoing proton kinetic energy of 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Udias et al., 1993) identifying two main sources of discrepancy between relativistic and nonrelativistic spectroscopic factors: electron Coulomb distortion and optical potential effects. It addresses the puzzle that relativistic analyses yield spectroscopic factors around 70% for the 3s1/2 state in 208Pb (consistent with theoretical predictions and other methods such as Wagner), while nonrelativistic analyses give about 50% (Quint). The paper builds on earlier work by Sherif (1986) on Dirac-equation-based potentials and by Boffi on nonlocality effects, and shows that the Darwin factor K(r) plays a role analogous to the Perey factor from nonlocality treatments, enabling nonrelativistic calculations to reproduce relativistic results when properly included.
