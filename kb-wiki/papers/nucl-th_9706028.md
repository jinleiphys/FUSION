# Light Front Treatment of Nuclei: Formalism and Simple Applications

> A relativistic light front treatment of nuclei is developed by performing light front quantization for a chiral Lagrangian. The energy momentum tensor and the appropriate Hamiltonian are obtained. Three illustrations of the formalism are made. (1) Pion-nucleon scattering at tree level is shown to reproduce soft pion theorems. (2) The one boson exchange treatment of nucleon-nucleon scattering is developed and shown (by comparison with previous results of the equal time formulation) to lead to a reasonable description of nucleon-nucleon phase shifts. (3) The mean field approximation is applied to infinite nuclear matter, and the plus momentum distributions of that system are studied. The mesons are found to carry a significant fraction of the plus momentum, but are inaccessible to experiments.

## Key claim

A relativistic light front treatment of nuclei, based on quantizing a chiral Lagrangian at equal light cone time, reproduces standard equal-time results for pion-nucleon scattering, nucleon-nucleon phase shifts, and nuclear matter energy, while additionally providing access to plus-momentum distributions that show mesons carry a significant fraction of the nuclear plus momentum but are inaccessible to experiments.

## Method

The author performs light front quantization of a chiral Lagrangian containing nucleons, pions, scalar mesons, and neutral vector mesons, deriving the energy-momentum tensor and the light front Hamiltonian. The formalism is then applied to three test cases: (1) tree-level pion-nucleon scattering to second order in 1/f, (2) one-boson exchange nucleon-nucleon scattering compared with equal-time results, and (3) a mean-field approximation for infinite nuclear matter, from which plus-momentum distributions are extracted.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon scattering threshold amplitude: the isospin-even term is \(2m_\pi^2/f^2\) and the isospin-odd (Weinberg-Tomozawa) term is \(2i\epsilon_{fin}\tau_n m_\pi M/f^2\), to leading order in \(m_\pi/M\).
- The scalar meson mass \(m_s\) and vector meson mass \(m_v\) appear as bare masses in the Lagrangian; the nucleon bare mass is \(M\).
- The pion decay constant \(f\) appears in the Lagrangian; the Goldberger-Treiman relation yields \(g_A = 1\) at tree level, with corrections of order \((M/f)^3\) expected.
- The free-field dispersion relation for all particles is \(k^- = (k_\perp^2 + m^2)/k^+\).

## Context

This work extends the light front quantization techniques of Soper (1971) and Yan and collaborators (1973) to a chiral Lagrangian relevant for nuclear physics, building on earlier light front treatments of two-hadron systems by Coester (1965), Keister (1991), and others. The mean-field treatment of nuclear matter follows the approach of Glazek and Shakin (1990) but includes vector mesons, and the nuclear matter energy is shown to be the same as in the equal-time formalism. The paper enables future studies of high-momentum exclusive and inclusive processes on nuclear targets, where the plus-momentum variable \(k^+\) is directly related to experimental observables such as the Bjorken variable \(x_{Bj}\).
