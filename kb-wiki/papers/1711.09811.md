# (Anti-)Symmetrizing Wave Functions

> The construction of fully (anti-)symmetric states with many particles, when the single particle state carries multiple quantum numbers, is a problem that seems to have not been systematically addressed in the literature. A quintessential example is the construction of ground state baryon wave functions where the color singlet condition reduces the problem to just two (flavor and spin) quantum numbers. In this paper, we address the general problem by noting that it can be re-interpreted as an eigenvalue equation, and provide a formalism that applies to generic number of particles and generic number of quantum numbers. As an immediate result, we find a complete solution to the two quantum number case, from which the baryon wave function problem with arbitrary number of flavors follows. As a more elaborate illustration that reveals complications not visible in the two quantum number case, we present the complete class of states possible for a system of five fermionic particles with three quantum numbers each. Our formalism makes systematic use of properties of the symmetric group and Young tableaux. Even though our motivations to consider this question have their roots in SYK-like tensor models and holography, the problem and its solution should have broader applications.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic formalism for constructing fully antisymmetric (or symmetric) multi-particle states when each particle carries multiple quantum numbers, by reinterpreting the problem as an eigenvalue equation for representations of the symmetric group; the authors claim a complete solution for the two-quantum-number case and present explicit results for up to five fermionic particles with three quantum numbers, but note that the method becomes computationally challenging for higher particle numbers due to the factorial growth of Young tableaux.

## Method

The authors reformulate the problem of constructing fully (anti-)symmetric states of n fermions (or bosons) carrying multiple quantum numbers as an eigenvalue equation acting on tensor products of irreducible representations of the symmetric group S_n, using the Young-Yamanouchi orthonormal basis. For the two-quantum-number case, they solve the eigenvalue equations sequentially for each transposition (i,i+1) and show that the condition reduces to equality of axial distances between numbers i and i+1 in the Young tableaux of the two slots, leading to a unique pairing of tableaux. For the three-quantum-number case, they present explicit solutions up to level n=5 by solving the analogous system of eight coupled linear equations derived from the action of transpositions.

## Key numbers

* For the two-slot fermionic case at level 2, there are exactly two antisymmetric states, corresponding to the paired Young tableaux (row, column) and (column, row).
* For the two-slot fermionic case at level 3, the antisymmetric states include the paired tableaux (single row, single column) and (single column, single row), plus a linear combination of mixed-symmetry tableaux: (1,3/2) paired with (1,2/3) minus (1,2/3) paired with (1,3/2).
* For the two-slot fermionic case at level 4, an example antisymmetric state is given by (1,2,3/4) paired with (1,4/2/3) minus (1,2,4/3) paired with (1,3/2/4) plus (1,3,4/2) paired with (1,2/3/4).
* The authors explicitly verified the uniqueness of the second-slot Young tableau for a given first-slot tableau up to level 6 (six boxes) for both fermionic and bosonic two-slot cases.
* For the three-slot fermionic case, the complete class of states is presented for systems of five fermionic particles with three quantum numbers each (level n=5).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of baryon wave function construction (e.g., Griffiths) from three flavors to arbitrary numbers of flavors, by providing a general solution for the two-quantum-number case that subsumes the flavor-spin problem after imposing the color singlet condition. The formalism is motivated by applications to SYK-like tensor models and holography (Witten, Klebanov) and builds on prior work by the authors (Krishnan et al., finiteN) on solving ungauged tensor models. The method differs from earlier approaches (Chen, Coleman) that require computing characters and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for inner products of symmetric group irreps, by reformulating the problem as a single eigenvalue equation that yields both the allowed representations and the coefficients in one shot.
