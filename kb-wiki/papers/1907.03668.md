# Effect of non-eikonal corrections on azimuthal asymmetries in the Color Glass Condensate

> We analyse the azimuthal structure of two gluon correlations in the Color Glass Condensate including those effects that result from relaxing the shockwave approximation for the target. Working in the Glasma graph approach suitable for collisions between dilute systems, we compute numerically the azimuthal distributions and show that both even and odd harmonics appear. We study their dependence on model parameters, energy of the collision, pseudorapidity and transverse momentum of the produced particles, and length of the target. While the contribution from non-eikonal corrections vanishes with increasing collision energy and becomes negligible at the energies of the Large Hadron Collider, it is found to be sizeable up to top energies at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

Non-eikonal corrections from relaxing the shockwave approximation for the target break the forward-backward symmetry of standard Color Glass Condensate calculations and generate odd azimuthal harmonics, but these effects vanish with increasing collision energy and are negligible at LHC energies while remaining sizeable up to top RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors compute the double inclusive gluon production cross section in the Glasma graph approximation for dilute-dilute collisions, including non-eikonal corrections from a finite target length. They assume a Gaussian distribution of color sources in the projectile (McLerran-Venugopalan model) and a Yukawa-type potential for the target color fields, then expand the azimuthal distribution in Fourier harmonics to extract the coefficients \(v_n\).

## Key numbers

- Odd harmonics \(v_3\) are sizeable at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 20\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 60\) GeV but become negligible at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV (top RHIC energy) and above (LHC energies).
- The non-eikonal cross section shows a sizeable difference between forward (\(\Delta\phi=0\)) and backward (\(\Delta\phi=\pi\)) peaks for pseudorapidity differences up to 1.5–2 units at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 20\) and 60 GeV, with \(k_1 = 1\) GeV and \(k_2 = 1.2\) GeV.
- Odd harmonics scale strongly with target length \(L^+\): for example, \(v_3\) increases significantly when \(L^+\) is varied from 1.5 fm upward, while even harmonics remain almost constant.
- The infrared regulators used to maximize \(v_3\) are \(\mu_T = 0.4\) GeV and \(\mu_P = 0.2\) GeV, with the gluonic size of the projectile \(B_p = 4\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and target length \(L = 12\) fm (Pb nucleus).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of non-eikonal corrections to single, double, and triple gluon production in the CGC (Agostini, Altinoluk, Armesto 2019) by numerically studying their effect on azimuthal harmonics. It contrasts with standard eikonal CGC calculations (Dumitru 2008, Dumitru 2010) that produce only even harmonics, and with other approaches that generate odd harmonics via density corrections (McLerran 2016, Kovner 2016, Kovchegov 2018) or more sophisticated target descriptions (Kovner 2012, Dumitru 2014). The results enable future extension to dilute-dense (pA) collisions and comparison with experimental data at RHIC energies.
