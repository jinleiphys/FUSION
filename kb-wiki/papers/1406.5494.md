# Crossing of large multiquasiparticle magnetic-rotation bands in $^{198}$Bi

> High-spin states in the doubly-odd $^{198}$Bi nucleus have been studied by using the $^{185,187}$Re($^{16}$O, xn) reactions at the beam energy of 112.5 MeV. $γ-γ$ coincidence were measured by using the INGA array with 15 Compton suppressed clover HPGe detectors. The observed levels have been assigned definite spin-parity. The high spin structure is grouped into three bands (B1, B2 and B3), of which two (B1 and B2) exhibit the properties of magnetic rotation (MR). Tilted axis cranking calculations were carried out to explain the MR bands having large multi-quasiparticle configurations. The calculated results explain the bands B1 and B2 very nicely, confirming the shears mechanism and suggest a crossing of two MR bands in both the cases. The crossing is from 6-qp to 8-qp in band B1 and from 4-qp to 6-qp in band B2, a very rare finding. A semiclassical model has also been used to obtain the particle-hole interaction strengths for the bands B1 and B2, below the band crossing.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two magnetic-rotation bands (B1 and B2) in \(^{198}\)Bi each exhibit a crossing between bands with different multi-quasiparticle configurations, a phenomenon rarely observed in magnetic-rotation bands. The claim is hedged by the fact that the band-crossing in B1 could not be extended much after the backbending, so the details about the nature of the band after the alignment are not well known.

## Method

High-spin states in \(^{198}\)Bi were populated via \(^{185,187}\)Re(\(^{16}\)O, xn) fusion-evaporation reactions at 112.5 MeV beam energy, using a thick natural rhenium target. Gamma-gamma coincidences were measured with the INGA array of 15 Compton-suppressed clover HPGe detectors, and directional correlation (DCO) ratios and integrated polarization asymmetry (IPDCO) ratios were used to assign definite spins and parities to the observed levels. Tilted axis cranking (TAC) calculations in the hybrid version (combining Woods-Saxon single-particle energies with the deformed anisotropic harmonic oscillator) were performed to interpret the magnetic-rotation bands, and a semiclassical model (Dönau-Frauendorf) was used to extract particle-hole interaction strengths below the band-crossing.

## Key numbers

* The beam energy used was 112.5 MeV for the \(^{185,187}\)Re(\(^{16}\)O, xn) reactions.
* About 2.4 \(\times\) 10\(^8\) random- and background-subtracted true \(\gamma\)-\(\gamma\) coincidence events were recorded.
* The 345.5-keV \(\gamma\) ray was assigned as an E1 transition based on an IPDCO ratio of 0.18(5) and a DCO ratio of 1.81(27) (gated by an E2 transition).
* The 468.2-keV \(\gamma\) ray was assigned as an E2 transition with a DCO ratio of 0.53(10) (gated by an E1 transition).
* The \(B(M1)/B(E2)\) ratios for two states in band B2 were 6.6(14) and 9.2(21) \(\mu^2/(eb)^2\).
* The g-factor \(g1 = 0.72(10)\) was obtained for both the 20\(^-\) and 21\(^-\) states in band B2 using the Dönau-Frauendorf model.
* The proton and neutron pairing gap parameters used in TAC calculations were 0.74 MeV and 0.71 MeV, respectively, after 20% attenuation.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of \(^{198}\)Bi by Zhou et al. (2000) and Zwartz et al. (2000), who had proposed unconnected magnetic-rotation-type bands without definite spin-parity assignments or excitation energies. The present measurement provides firm spin-parity assignments and places the three bands (B1, B2, B3) in the level scheme, enabling configuration assignments. The observation of band-crossings from 6-qp to 8-qp in B1 and from 4-qp to 6-qp in B2 is a rare finding in magnetic-rotation bands, and the results enable future comparisons with TAC calculations in neighboring odd-A Pb and Bi isotopes, such as those studied by Amita et al. (2000) and by Baldsiefen et al. (1994).
