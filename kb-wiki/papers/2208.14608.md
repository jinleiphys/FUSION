# Nature of excited $Ξ$ baryons with threshold effects

> Spectroscopy of excited baryons with strangeness $S=-2$ is stimulated by recent experimental developments. Here we focus on the $Ξ(1620)$ which locates close to the $\bar{K}Λ$ threshold. To take into account the threshold effects, we construct the coupled-channels meson-baryon scattering amplitude where the $Ξ(1620)$ appears as a resonance. We demonstrate that the $\bar{K}Λ$ threshold effects distort the peak of the $Ξ(1620)$ resonance from the simple Breit-Wigner distribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the peak of the $\Xi(1620)$ resonance is distorted and shifted from a simple Breit-Wigner distribution due to threshold effects from the nearby $\bar{K}\Lambda$ threshold, even when the pole positions are set to the same values.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channels $s$-wave meson-baryon scattering amplitude using the chiral unitary approach, with four channels in the isospin basis ($\pi\Xi$, $\bar{K}\Lambda$, $\bar{K}\Sigma$, $\eta\Xi$) and the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction. Starting from the model of Ramos (2002), they varied the subtraction constants $a_{\pi\Xi}$ and $a_{\bar{K}\Lambda}$ to minimize the deviation $\Delta z$ between the theoretical pole position and the target pole position $z_{\rm ex}=1610-30i$ MeV motivated by the Belle data. The resulting amplitude was compared with a Breit-Wigner amplitude having the same pole position.

## Key numbers

- The target pole position for the $\Xi(1620)$ was set to $z_{\rm ex}=1610-30i$ MeV, motivated by the Belle result of $M_R=1610.4\pm 6.0^{+6.1}_{-4.2}$ MeV and $\Gamma_R=59.9\pm 4.8^{+2.8}_{-7.1}$ MeV.
- The minimized deviation $\Delta z$ was achieved at subtraction constants $(a_{\pi\Xi}, a_{\bar{K}\Lambda}) = (-4.19, -0.14)$, yielding $z_{\rm th}=1610-30i$ MeV.
- The peak of the imaginary part of the scattering amplitude in the model was found at $W\sim 1604$ MeV, shifted from the real part of the pole position at $W=1610$ MeV.
- The previous model of Ramos (2002) gave a pole at $W=1607-140i$ MeV, corresponding to $M_R=1607$ MeV and $\Gamma_R=280$ MeV, with subtraction constants $a_i=-2$ at regularization scale $\mu=630$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach applied to $\Xi$ resonances by Ramos (2002), updating the model to match the narrower decay width reported by the Belle collaboration (2018). The study is motivated by the fact that the $\Xi(1620)$ lies close to the $\bar{K}\Lambda$ threshold (approximately 1611 MeV), and the authors quantitatively show that threshold effects distort the resonance lineshape. This enables future work on extracting the genuine quark component of the resonance and analyzing the $\Xi_c\to\pi\pi\Xi$ decay spectrum, as well as extending the analysis to the $\Xi(1690)$ resonance near the $\bar{K}\Sigma$ threshold.
