# Dissipative Magnetohydrodynamics for Non-Resistive Relativistic Plasmas

> Based on a 14-moment closure for non-resistive (general-) relativistic viscous plasmas, we describe a new numerical scheme that is able to handle all first-order dissipative effects (heat conduction, bulk and shear viscosities), as well the anisotropies induced by the presence of magnetic fields. The latter is parameterized in terms of a thermal gyrofrequency or, equivalently, a thermal Larmor radius and allows to correctly capture the thermal Hall effect. By solving an extended Israel-Stewart-like system for the dissipative quantities that enforces algebraic constraints via stiff-relaxation, we are able to cast all first-order dissipative terms in flux-divergence form. This allows us to apply traditional high-resolution shock capturing methods to the equations, making the system suitable for the numerical study of highly turbulent flows. We present several numerical tests to assess the robustness of our numerical scheme in flat spacetime. The 14-moment closure can seamlessly interpolate between the highly collisional limit found in neutron star mergers, and the highly anisotropic limit of relativistic Braginskii magnetohydrodynamics appropriate for weakly collisional plasmas in black-hole accretion problems. We believe that this new formulation and numerical scheme will be useful for a broad class of relativistic magnetized flows.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new numerical scheme for non-resistive relativistic magnetohydrodynamics that can handle all first-order dissipative effects (heat conduction, bulk and shear viscosities) alongside magnetic-field-induced anisotropies, but only for plasmas where the comoving electric field vanishes (infinite electrical conductivity limit), and the scheme is demonstrated only in flat spacetime.

## Method

The authors derive evolution equations for the dissipative quantities (bulk scalar pressure, heat flux, and shear-stress tensor) from a 14-moment closure of the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation, extending the Israel-Sewart framework to include magnetic field couplings parameterized by a thermal gyrofrequency. They reformulate all first-order gradient terms into flux-divergence form by introducing stiff relaxation terms that enforce the algebraic constraints (such as orthogonality of heat flux to the fluid four-velocity), and treat these stiff terms with implicit time integration schemes to enable the use of high-resolution shock capturing methods.

## Key numbers

- The dissipative system has 9 independent degrees of freedom (bulk scalar pressure, heat flux with 3 components, shear-stress tensor with 5 independent components) out of 14 total degrees of freedom in the energy-momentum tensor and baryon current
- The relaxation time for the constraint-enforcing term in the heat flux equation is ω_q, which must satisfy ω_q ≪ τ_q (the heat flux relaxation time) to properly impose the orthogonality constraint
- In the limit of vanishing relaxation time τ_Π → 0, the bulk viscous scalar Π satisfies an approximate conservation equation ∇_μ[(Π + ζ/τ_Π)u^μ] ≃ 0, where ζ is the bulk viscosity coefficient

## Context

This work extends the second-order hydrodynamic framework of Denicol (2012) and Denicol (2018) for non-resistive relativistic plasmas, which was originally developed for neutral kinetic systems and later extended to include magnetic fields via the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation. The numerical scheme enables applications to both the highly collisional regime found in neutron star mergers and the weakly collisional regime of relativistic Braginskii magnetohydrodynamics appropriate for black-hole accretion problems, addressing a gap left by previous Eulerian implementations of Israel-Stewart formulations that could not treat all first-order dissipative terms in flux-divergence form.
