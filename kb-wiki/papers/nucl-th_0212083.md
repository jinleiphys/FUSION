# Double beta decay of sigma^- hyperons

> We compute the strangeness-conserving double beta decay of $Σ^-$ hyperons, which is the only hadronic system that can undergo such decays. We consider both, the lepton number conserving %$Σ^-\go Σ^++2e^-+2 \barν$ $Σ^-\go Σ^+e^-e^-\barν\barν$ ($ββ_{Σ_{2ν}}$) and the lepton number violating $Σ^-\go Σ^+e^-e^-$ ($ββ_{Σ_{0ν}}$) modes. The branching ratios of these $ββ$ decays are suppressed at the level of $10^{-30}$ considering a light neutrino scenario in the case of the $ββ_{Σ_{0ν}}$ channel. The dynamical origin of such low rates and their possible enhancements are briefly discussed. Given its simplicity those decays can be used also for the purposes of illustrating the main features of double beta decays.

## Key claim

The strangeness-conserving double beta decay of the Sigma- hyperon, the only hadronic system capable of such decays, has branching ratios suppressed at the level of 10^-30 for the neutrinoless mode under a light neutrino scenario, with the dominant contribution coming from a real Sigma0 intermediate state whose large electromagnetic decay width causes the extreme suppression.

## Method

The authors computed the decay rates for both the lepton-number-conserving (Sigma- -> Sigma+ e- e- antineutrino antineutrino) and lepton-number-violating (Sigma- -> Sigma+ e- e-) modes using the standard four-fermion weak Hamiltonian with V-A leptonic currents and baryonic currents described by vector and axial-vector form factors at zero momentum transfer. They employed the non-relativistic impulse approximation for baryonic currents, considered only the Sigma0 and Lambda intermediate states, and treated the singularity from the on-shell Sigma0 state by including its finite width of 8.89 keV. The phase space integrals were evaluated numerically using the known hyperon form factors from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for the two-neutrino mode: B(beta beta_Sigma2nu) = 1.38 x 10^-30 (with the Sigma0 intermediate state alone giving 1.36 x 10^-30)
- Mass splitting between Sigma- and Sigma+: m_Sigma- - m_Sigma+ = 8.08 MeV
- Energy available for the real Sigma0 intermediate state: m_A - m_Sigma0 = 4.8 MeV
- Width of the Sigma0 hyperon: Gamma_Sigma0 = 8.89 keV
- Mass difference for the Lambda intermediate state: m_A - m_Lambda = 81.7 MeV
- Form factors at zero momentum transfer: f_A_Sigma0 = 1.41, g_A_Sigma0 = -0.69, f_B_Sigma0 = -1.41, g_B_Sigma0 = 0.69; f_A_Lambda = 0, g_A_Lambda = -0.60, f_B_Lambda = 0, g_B_Lambda = -0.60

## Context

This work extends the study of double beta decay from nuclear systems to a free baryon system, the Sigma- hyperon, which has not been previously reported in the literature. The authors note that nuclear double beta decay calculations are limited by model-dependent nuclear wavefunctions (as discussed by Doi (1985), Vergados (1986), and others), whereas the Sigma hyperon system has only two well-defined intermediate states (Sigma0 and Lambda) with hadronic matrix elements dominated by form factors computed by Piekarewicz (1985) and Schweiger (1995) with good agreement. The results show that these decays are far below current experimental sensitivity, similar to the situation for rare kaon decays such as K+ -> mu+ mu+ pi- (with branching ratio limits around 10^-9 from the E865 experiment), but the system provides a clean textbook example for illustrating double beta decay formalism.
