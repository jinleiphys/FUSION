# 5-Point 1PI Proper Vertex Function of Gluon and the S-Matrix Element

> As far as the renormalization in perturbative QCD is concerned the n-point one particle irreducible (1PI) proper vertex function is the basic building block where the ultra-violet (UV) divergence occurs when the loop momentum integration limit goes to infinity. In this paper we express the S-matrix element for the $gg \rightarrow ggg$ scattering process at all orders in coupling constant in terms of 5-point, 4-point, 3-point 1PI proper vertex functions and the (full) propagator by using the path integral formulation of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives an expression for the S-matrix element of the gluon scattering process \(gg \rightarrow ggg\) at all orders in the coupling constant, but only after neglecting quarks and absorbing polarization sums and color factors into the cross section rather than the S-matrix element itself.

## Method

Using the path integral formulation of QCD, the author starts from the generating functional and obtains the 5-point connected Green's function of the gluon. This is then expressed in terms of the 5-point, 4-point, and 3-point one-particle irreducible (1PI) proper vertex functions together with the full gluon propagator, first in coordinate space and then in momentum space. The momentum-space result is inserted into the LSZ reduction formula to write the S-matrix element for \(gg \rightarrow ggg\) entirely in terms of these 1PI vertex functions and the propagator.

## Key numbers

* The paper treats the \(2 \rightarrow 3\) gluonic scattering process \(k_1+k_2 \rightarrow k'_1+k'_2+k'_3\) (Eq. 1 in the text), with two incoming and three outgoing gluons.
* The S-matrix element is given by Eq. (h3): \(\langle f|i \rangle = [G(-k'_1)]^{-1} [G(-k'_2)]^{-1}[G(-k'_3)]^{-1}[G(k_2)]^{-1}[G(k_1)]^{-1} G(-k'_1,-k'_2,-k'_3,k_1,k_2)\), where \(G(k)\) is the renormalized full gluon propagator and \(G(k_1,k_2,k_3,k_4,k_5)\) is the renormalized 5-point connected Green's function.
* The final expression for the 5-point connected Green's function in momentum space (Eq. 5gk) contains a delta function \(\delta^{(4)}(k_1+k_2+k_3+k_4+k_5)\) multiplied by a sum of terms involving the 5-point, 4-point, and 3-point 1PI proper vertex functions and the full propagator.

## Context

This work extends the standard relation between connected Green's functions and 1PI vertex functions, previously established for 2-point, 3-point, and 4-point functions (as referenced by the author as "ab1"), to the 5-point gluon vertex. The result enables the simultaneous study of ultraviolet renormalization and the factorization of infrared and collinear divergences in multi-gluon scattering at all orders in the coupling constant. The author notes that the technique can be extended to the closed-time path integral formalism for non-equilibrium QCD, relevant for studying partonic scattering in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC.
