# Kinetics of hydrodynamic pions in chiral perturbation theory

> We determine the kinetic coefficients of ultrasoft pions using chiral perturbation theory at finite temperature close to the chiral limit. This is used to compute the axial charge diffusion and damping coefficients in the hydrodynamic effective theory for these pion waves. We show that to provide a leading order answer for these coefficients one needs to explore the dynamics of hard, soft, and ultrasoft pion modes, which are represented microscopically by the appropriate kinetic and hydrodynamic descriptions..

## Key claim

The paper computes the kinetic coefficients (axial charge diffusion coefficient \(D_A\) and axial damping coefficient \(D_m\)) of ultrasoft pions in chiral perturbation theory at finite temperature close to the chiral limit, showing that a leading-order calculation requires resumming contributions from hard, soft, and ultrasoft pion modes, and that the final result is independent of the arbitrary scale separating these regimes.

## Method

The authors evaluate the imaginary part of the retarded pion self-energy for ultrasoft external momentum using finite-temperature chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. They separate the calculation into a hard contribution (internal momenta \(\sim T\)), where hard pion propagators are dressed with their collisional width, and a soft contribution (internal momenta \(\sim T(T/F)^4\)), which is treated using a sum-rule technique familiar from high-temperature QCD plasmas. The two contributions are combined to yield the kinetic coefficients \(D_A\) and \(D_m\) that appear in the hydrodynamic effective theory for pion waves.

## Key numbers

* The average scattering rate of hard pions is \(\bar{\gamma}_p = 2.33 \left( T^5 / (27 F^4) \right)\), where \(F\) is the pion decay constant in the chiral Lagrangian and \(T\) is the temperature.
* For a hard pion with momentum \(p \ll T\), the scattering rate scales as \(\gamma_p = (p^2 T^3) / (9\pi F^4) \log(1.56 \, T/p)\).
* The squared pion velocity in the chiral limit is \(v_0^2 = 1 - (T^4)/(27 F^4) \log(\Lambda_\Delta / T)\), where \(\log \Lambda_\Delta\) is a combination of low-energy constants \(L_1^r(\mu)\) and \(L_2^r(\mu)\).
* The chiral phase transition temperature is estimated as \(T \simeq 160\) MeV by extrapolating \(v_0\) to zero, consistent with lattice-QCD measurements of \(T_\chi = 151(3)(3)\) MeV (Aoki 2006).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic effective theory for pions developed by Son (1999) and Son and Stephanov (2001, 2002), who derived the ideal and first-order dissipative equations for O(4) superfluid dynamics. It resolves an inconsistency noted by Smilga (1996), who estimated the damping rate at leading-log order but did not compute the constant under the log. The calculation provides the leading contribution to the isospin conductivity and bulk viscosity in chiral perturbation theory, and the results can be extended to the hadron resonance gas at higher temperatures using experimental cross sections and chiral constraints.
