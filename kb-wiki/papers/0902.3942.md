# Quasiparticle and quasihole states of nuclei around 56Ni

> The single-particle spectral function of 56Ni has been computed within the framework of self-consistent Green's functions theory. The Faddeev random phase approximation method and the G-matrix technique are used to account for the effects of long- and short-range physics on the spectral distribution. Large scale calculations have been performed in spaces including up to ten oscillator shells. The chiral N3LO interaction is used together with a monopole correction that accounts for eventual missing three-nucleon forces. The single-particle energies associated with nucleon transfer to valence 1p0f orbits are found to be almost converged with respect to both the size of the model space and the oscillator frequency. The results support that 56Ni is a good doubly magic nucleus. The absolute spectroscopic factors to the valence states on A=55,57 are also obtained. For the transition between the ground states of 57Ni and 56Ni, the calculations nicely agree with heavy-ion knockout experiments.

## Key claim

The authors find that single-particle energies for nucleon transfer to valence $1p0f$ orbits around $^{56}$Ni are almost converged with respect to both model space size and oscillator frequency, supporting the picture of $^{56}$Ni as a good doubly magic nucleus, but they note that the spectroscopic factors are quenched to about 60% of the independent-particle model values, which is common even for good closed shell nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Green's functions calculations of the single-particle spectral function of $^{56}$Ni using the Faddeev random phase approximation (FRPA) method to account for long-range correlations and the G-matrix technique to handle short-range physics. They employed the chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interaction with a monopole correction for missing three-nucleon forces, and performed calculations in harmonic oscillator spaces including up to ten major shells. The self-energy was constructed from a mean-field term and a dynamic correlation term, with the latter obtained by solving Faddeev equations that couple particle-hole and two-particle/two-hole collective excitations at the RPA level.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies for valence $1p0f$ orbits around $^{56}$Ni were found to be almost converged with respect to model space size (up to ten oscillator shells) and oscillator frequency
- Spectroscopic factors for the transition between the ground states of $^{57}$Ni and $^{56}$Ni show quenching to about 60% of independent-particle model values, consistent with heavy-ion knockout experiments
- The calculations were performed in spaces including up to ten oscillator shells, with the G-matrix evaluated for negative energies up to about -5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the FRPA method, previously applied to $^{16}$O by Barbieri and collaborators (2002, 2006), to the $1p0f$ shell region around $^{56}$Ni, which has been the focus of recent experiments by Yurkon and collaborators (2006) and others. The results support the interpretation of $^{56}$Ni as a doubly magic nucleus, consistent with large-scale shell-model calculations by Horoi and collaborators (2006), but the quenched spectroscopic factors and softness of the $^{56}$Ni core (as indicated by large transition matrix elements to the first excited $2^+$ state) suggest that the single-particle picture is significantly fragmented. This work enables future applications of the FRPA method to other medium-mass nuclei and provides a microscopic foundation for constructing valence-space effective interactions for shell-model calculations in the $1p0f$ shell.
