# Pion photoproduction in a dynamical coupled-channels model

> The charged and neutral pion photoproduction reactions are investigated in a dynamical coupled-channels approach based on the formulation of Haberzettl, Huang, and Nakayama [Phys. Rev. C 83, 065502 (2011)]. The hadronic final-state interaction is provided by the Juelich pi-N model, which includes the channels pi-N and eta-N comprising stable hadrons as well as the effective pi-pi-N channels pi-Delta, sigma-N, and rho-N. This hadronic model has been quite successful in describing pi-N to pi-N scattering for center-of-mass energies up to 1.9 GeV. By construction, the full pion photoproduction current satisfies the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity and thus is gauge invariant as a matter of course. The calculated differential cross sections and photon spin asymmetries up to 1.65 GeV center-of-mass energy for the reactions gamma p to pi+ n, gamma p to pi0 p, gamma n to pi- p and gamma n to pi0 n are in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a gauge-invariant dynamical coupled-channels model for pion photoproduction that reproduces experimental differential cross sections and photon spin asymmetries up to 1.65 GeV center-of-mass energy, but the authors note that as a first step toward a more complete calculation they explicitly include only the eta-N and pi-Delta channels and the basic pi-N channel in the final-state interaction loop integration, while the hadronic T-matrices contain information from all channels of the Juelich model.

## Method

The authors combine the Juelich pi-N hadronic model, which includes pi-N, eta-N, pi-Delta, sigma-N, and rho-N channels, with a novel gauge-invariant formulation of the photoproduction current based on the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity. The hadronic final-state interactions are described by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the non-pole amplitude, while the photoproduction current is constructed from s-, u-, and t-channel pole diagrams plus a contact-type interaction current that ensures gauge invariance. The calculations are performed for center-of-mass energies up to 1.65 GeV for the reactions gamma p to pi+ n, gamma p to pi0 p, gamma n to pi- p, and gamma n to pi0 n.

## Key numbers

* The hadronic pi-N model describes pi-N scattering up to center-of-mass energies of 1.9 GeV
* The photoproduction calculations are performed up to a total center-of-mass energy of W = 1.65 GeV
* The model includes eight genuine bare resonances: S11(1535), S11(1650), S31(1620), P31(1910), P13(1720), D13(1520), P33(1232), and D33(1700)
* The P11(1440) (Roper) resonance appears as a dynamically generated resonance from the interaction within and between the pi-N and sigma-N channels

## Context

This work extends the feasibility study of Haberzettl, Nakayama, and Krewald (2006) which was limited to center-of-mass energies up to 1.25 GeV, by employing a more recent version of the Juelich model from Gasparyan (2003) and a novel gauge-invariant formulation from Haberzettl, Huang, and Nakayama (2011). The approach differs from the EBAC group's dynamical coupled-channels model (Matsuyama, 2007) which is not gauge invariant, and from K-matrix approaches such as those of Arndt (2002, 2006) which sacrifice analyticity by omitting the real part of the two-body propagator. The gauge-invariant framework enables future applications to NN bremsstrahlung and provides a consistent method for extracting baryon resonance parameters from photoproduction data.
