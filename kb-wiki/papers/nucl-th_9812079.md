# Quark Delocalization, Color Screening Model and Nucleon-Baryon Scattering

> We apply the quark delocalization and color screening model to nucleon-baryon scattering. A semi-quantitative fit to N-N, N-Lambda and N-Sigma phase shifts and scattering cross sections is obtained without invoking meson exchange. Quarks delocalize reasonably in all of the different flavor channels to induce effective nucleon-baryon interactions with both a repulsive core and with an intermediate range attraction in the cases expected.

## Key claim

The Quark Delocalization, Color Screening Model (QDCSM) can produce a semi-quantitative fit to N-N, N-Lambda, and N-Sigma phase shifts and scattering cross sections without invoking meson exchange, but the authors emphasize they are "not so ambitious as to expect to achieve a quantitative fit" and "only seek to test further whether the QDCSM contains basically the right physics."

## Method

The authors applied the QDCSM to nucleon-baryon scattering using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with a six-quark basis wave function that includes a delocalization parameter epsilon(s), determined variationally for each baryon separation s. The Hamiltonian includes a color-screened confining potential (linear confinement for quarks in the same baryon orbit, exponentially screened for quarks in different baryon orbits) and a Fermi-Breit one-gluon exchange interaction, with all parameters fixed by single hadron properties and lattice QCD results for the screening constant. The Canto-Brink variational method was used to obtain logarithmic derivatives at the boundary, from which phase shifts were extracted.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses: m = 313 MeV (u,d), m_s = 521.7 MeV
- Baryon size parameter: b = 0.625 fm
- Quark-gluon coupling constant: alpha_s = 1.71
- Confinement strength: a_c = 39.1 MeV fm^{-1}
- Color screening constant: kappa = 1.1111 fm^{-1} (from lattice QCD)
- Calculated Lambda mass: 1025 MeV (90 MeV lower than experimental value)
- Calculated Sigma mass: 1103 MeV (90 MeV lower than experimental value)
- For fine-tuned calculations, kappa_s = (4/9) kappa_u was used for the s-quark screening parameter

## Context

This work extends the QDCSM approach previously applied to N-N scattering (Wang et al., 1992; Goldman et al., 1986-1997) to nucleon-hyperon channels, providing an alternative to meson exchange models (de Swart, 1994; Reuber, 1994) and hybrid meson-gluon exchange quark cluster models (Fujiwara, 1996). The model aims to include non-perturbative QCD effects such as quark percolation and color screening that are precluded by stiff-baryon assumptions, and enables future study of exotic quark states, strangelets, and multiquark systems including the predicted d* dibaryon resonance.
