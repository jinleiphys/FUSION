# Effects of high order deformation on superheavy high-$K$ isomers

> Using, for the first time, configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface calculations with the inclusion of $β_6$ deformation, we find remarkable effects of the high order deformation on the high-$K$ isomers in $^{254}$No, the focus of recent spectroscopy experiments on superheavy nuclei. For shapes with multipolarity six, the isomers are more tightly bound and, microscopically, have enhanced deformed shell gaps at $N=152$ and $Z=100$. The inclusion of $β_6$ deformation significantly improves the description of the very heavy high-$K$ isomers.

# Effects of high order deformation on superheavy high-$K$ isomers

## Key claim
Configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface calculations that include $\beta_6$ deformation for the first time show that high-order octupole-like deformation significantly improves the description of high-$K$ isomers in $^{254}$No, but the magnitude of $\beta_6$ deformation may be slightly underestimated because the authors use a standard liquid-drop model with a sharp surface rather than the more realistic Yukawa-plus-exponential model with a diffuse surface.

## Method
Configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface (PES) calculations were performed in a three-dimensional deformation space ($\beta_2$, $\beta_4$, $\beta_6$) using an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential with universal parameters. The total energy combined a macroscopic liquid-drop model with a microscopic Strutinsky shell-correction approach including blocking effects, with approximate particle-number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method. The calculations adiabatically blocked unpaired nucleon orbits to specify each multi-quasiparticle configuration, using average Nilsson quantum numbers and good quantum numbers of parity and $\Omega$ to constrain the configuration in deformation space.

## Key numbers
- Ground state $\beta_6$ deformation in $^{254}$No: -0.029 (smaller in magnitude than the -0.05 calculated by Muntian et al. using the Yukawa-plus-exponential model)
- Extra binding energy of the ground state due to $\beta_6$ deformation: 0.8 MeV
- Depth increase for the $K^\pi=8^-\{\pi7/2^-[514]\otimes\pi9/2^+[624]\}$ state due to $\beta_6$ deformation: 0.856 MeV
- Calculated excitation energy of the $K^\pi=3^+$ state with $\beta_6$ deformation: 0.965 MeV, compared to experimental value 0.988 MeV (Herzberg et al., 2006)
- Calculated excitation energy of the four-qp $K^\pi=16^+$ state with $\beta_6$ deformation: 2.722 MeV, compared to experimental value 2.928 MeV (Clark et al., 2010)
- Calculated excitation energy of the $K^\pi=10^+$ state: 1.479 MeV, compared to experimental value 2.013 MeV (Clark et al., 2010)
- Calculated excitation energy of the six-qp $K^\pi=25^-$ state: 4.522 MeV
- Half-life of the four-qp isomer: approximately 200 $\mu$s (184 $\mu$s from one measurement)
- Half-life of the $K^\pi=8^-$ isomer: 266 ms

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical studies showing that $\beta_6$ deformation is significant in very heavy nuclei (Patyk and Sobiczewski, 1991; Muntian et al., 2001) and applies this to the specific case of high-$K$ isomers in $^{254}$No, which has been the focus of recent spectroscopy experiments by Herzberg et al. (2006), Tandel et al. (2006), Kondev et al. (2007), Hessberger et al. (2010), and Clark et al. (2010). The calculations resolve some of the controversial configuration assignments for the $K^\pi=8^-$ and $K^\pi=16^+$ isomers, favoring the $K^\pi=16^+$ assignment for the four-qp isomer. The results enable more reliable predictions of superheavy nuclear structure and stability, particularly regarding the $Z=100$ and $N=152$ deformed shell gaps that are relevant to the search for the island of stability.
