# Differences and similarities between fundamental and adjoint matters in SU(N) gauge theories

> We investigate differences and similarities between fundamental fermions and adjoint fermions in SU(N) gauge theories. The gauge theory with fundamental fermions possesses ZN symmetry only in the limit of infinite fermion mass, whereas the gauge theory with adjoint fermions does have the symmetry for any fermion mass. The flavor-dependent twisted boundary condition (FTBC) is then imposed on fundamental fermions so that the theory with fundamental fermions can possess ZN symmetry for any fermion mass. We show similarities between FTBC fundamental fermions and adjoint fermions, using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. In the mean-field level, the PNJL model with FTBC fundamental fermions has dynamics similar to the PNJL model with adjoint fermions for the confinement/deconfinement transition related to ZN symmetry. The chiral property is somewhat different between the two models, but there is a simple relation between chiral condensates in the two models. As an interesting high-energy phenomenon, a possibility of the gauge symmetry breaking is studied for FTBC fundamental fermions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the essential difference between ordinary fundamental matter and adjoint matter in SU(N) gauge theories originates from the presence or absence of Z_N symmetry, and that imposing a flavor-dependent twisted boundary condition (FTBC) on fundamental fermions restores this symmetry, making the two theories dynamically similar for the confinement/deconfinement transition, although chiral properties differ.

## Method

The authors construct a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for SU(3) gauge theories with both FTBC fundamental fermions and adjoint fermions, using mean-field approximation to compute thermodynamic potentials. They compare the confinement/deconfinement transition and chiral condensate behavior between the two theories, and also analyze the possibility of gauge symmetry breaking via the Hosotani mechanism using one-loop effective potential calculations.

## Key numbers

* The Polyakov-loop potential parameter T_0 is rescaled from 270 MeV (pure gauge LQCD value) to 195 MeV to reproduce a pseudocritical temperature T_c of approximately 160 MeV from full LQCD.
* For the NJL sector of fundamental fermions with N=3 and N_F,fund=3, the parameters are: current quark mass m_f = 5.5 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G_s Lambda^2 = 1.835, G_D Lambda^5 = 12.36.
* For the NJL sector of adjoint fermions with N=3 and N_F,adj=1, the parameters are: m = 5.5 MeV, Lambda = 651 MeV, G Lambda^2 = 3.607.
* The Polyakov-loop potential parameters for N=3 are: a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, b_3 = -1.75, T_0 = 195 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Kouno and Sakai (2012-2013) on FTBC fundamental fermions in SU(N) gauge theories, and builds on the PNJL model framework developed by Meisinger, Dumitru, Fukushima, Ratti, and others. The paper connects to lattice QCD results for the deconfinement transition temperature (Borsanyi, Soeldner, Kanaya) and to the Hosotani mechanism for gauge symmetry breaking in compactified dimensions. The findings enable further investigation of whether FTBC fundamental fermions can exhibit gauge symmetry breaking similar to adjoint fermions, and clarify the role of Z_N symmetry as the key distinguishing feature between fundamental and adjoint matter.
