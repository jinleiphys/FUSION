# Polarized tau decay and CP violation in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the role of $τ$-lepton polarization in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions (UPCs) as a novel application of the intense electromagnetic fields generated in such processes. In particular, we analyze the decay distributions of polarized $τ$-leptons produced via photon-photon fusion, focusing on both leptonic and semi-leptonic channels. We show that the external magnetic field present in UPCs induces a preferred spin quantization axis, which modifies the angular and energy distributions of $τ$ decay products relative to the standard helicity frame. By formulating the spin polarization along the magnetic field direction, we derive modified polarization-sensitive observables and demonstrate how kinematic selections can retain nonvanishing polarization signals even after ensemble averaging. Furthermore, we propose that the relative polarization of $τ^-$ and $τ^+$, accessible through complementary angular ranges of their decay products, serves as a sensitive observable for potential CP-violating effects. This framework provides a pathway for future experimental studies at the LHC and future colliders to exploit polarized $τ$ decays in UPCs as an application of the strong electromagnetic fields to probe new sources of CP violation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the strong magnetic fields in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions can induce tau-lepton polarization, and that the ratio of tau-minus to tau-plus polarization extracted from decay product energy distributions serves as a CP-violation observable, provided kinematic selections restrict analysis to complementary angular ranges relative to the magnetic field direction to avoid vanishing ensemble-averaged polarization.

## Method

The author derives modified decay distributions for polarized tau leptons produced via photon-photon fusion in ultraperipheral collisions, considering the single-pion, leptonic, and vector-meson (rho and a1) decay channels. The magnetic field in the laboratory frame is Lorentz-transformed to the tau rest frame, and the tau polarization vector is assumed aligned with this magnetic field direction. The polarization is then projected onto the helicity axis (tau momentum direction), and the resulting decay distributions are expressed in terms of energy fractions of visible decay products in the collinear limit for highly boosted taus. Observables sensitive to CP violation are constructed from ratios of polarization extracted from tau-minus and tau-plus decays, using orthogonal polynomial weighting functions to isolate polarization-dependent terms.

## Key numbers

- Tau lepton mass: 1.777 GeV; tau lifetime: 2.9 × 10^{-13} s
- Magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions: 10^{14} - 10^{15} Tesla
- Expected tau polarization at production: P_tau ~ 0.06 to 0.6, based on eB ~ 1-10 GeV^2 and effective timescale t_eff ~ 0.2 fm
- Statistical uncertainties at HL-LHC: delta P_tau ~ 0.01 to 0.03, from expected O(10^4) usable events
- For the rho meson channel, the polarization sensitivity reduction factor (m_tau^2 - 2m_rho^2)/(m_tau^2 + 2m_rho^2) evaluates to about 0.46; for the a1 meson, about 0.02

## Context

This work extends the study of tau polarization from e+e- colliders (ALEPH, DELPHI, Belle) to the novel environment of ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions, where strong transient magnetic fields provide a complementary polarization mechanism. The proposal builds on experimental confirmation of gamma-gamma to tau+tau- production in Pb-Pb UPCs by ATLAS (2022) and CMS (2022), and on the ALICE collaboration's (2025) observation of D*+ spin alignment from magnetic fields. The framework enables future experimental studies at the LHC and HL-LHC to probe CP violation in the leptonic sector through a qualitatively new channel, with sensitivity to CP-odd electromagnetic dipole operators at multi-GeV scales for O(1) Wilson coefficients.
