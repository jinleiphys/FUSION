# Pion-photon exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials

> We calculate in chiral perturbation theory the dominant next-to-leading order correction to the $πγ$-exchange NN-potential proportional to the large isovector magnetic moment $κ_v = 4.7$ of the nucleon. The corresponding spin-spin and tensor potentials $\widetilde V_{S,T}(r)$ in coordinate space have a very simple analytical form. At long distances $r \simeq 2$ fm these potentials are of similar size (but opposite in sign) as the leading order $πγ$-exchange potentials. We consider also effects from virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation as well as other isospin-breaking contributions to the $2π$-exchange NN-potential induced by additional one-photon exchange.

# Pion-photon exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials

## Key claim
The dominant next-to-leading order correction to the πγ-exchange NN-potential, proportional to the large isovector magnetic moment κ_v = 4.7, produces spin-spin and tensor potentials that at long distances r ≈ 2 fm are of similar size but opposite in sign to the leading order πγ-exchange potentials, though the paper notes that the inclusion of the leading order interaction had negligible effects on ¹S₀ low-energy parameters and led only to tiny improvements in fits of NN-scattering data.

## Method
The calculation uses chiral perturbation theory to compute one-loop diagrams for πγ-exchange between nucleons, employing spectral functions obtained via the Cutkosky cutting rule and dispersion relations to derive T-matrices in momentum space, which are then Fourier-transformed to coordinate-space spin-spin and tensor potentials. The analysis focuses on the contribution from the isovector magnetic moment κ_v = 4.7 as the dominant next-to-leading order correction, and separately evaluates effects from virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitation using the Δ → Nγ transition magnetic moment κ* ≈ 4.9, as well as other isospin-breaking contributions to 2π-exchange induced by additional one-photon exchange.

## Key numbers
- The isovector magnetic moment of the nucleon is κ_v = 4.7, used as the dominant low-energy constant for the next-to-leading order correction.
- The Δ → Nγ transition magnetic moment is κ* ≈ 4.9, extracted from the partial decay width Γ(Δ⁺ → pγ) ≈ 0.68 MeV.
- At r = 2.0 fm, the leading order spin-spin potential Ṽ_S^(lo) = −2.35 keV and tensor potential Ṽ_T^(lo) = 8.26 keV; the next-to-leading order corrections are Ṽ_S^(κ_v) = 2.67 keV and Ṽ_T^(κ_v) = −8.64 keV, showing similar magnitude but opposite sign.
- The Δ-isobar contributions at r = 2.0 fm are Ṽ_S^(κ*) = 0.41 keV and Ṽ_T^(κ*) = −0.88 keV, approximately a factor of 5 to 10 smaller than the nucleon-only contributions.
- The leading order central potential for pp-scattering from 2π-exchange with one-photon exchange is Ṽ_C^(lo) = −49.5 keV at r = 1.0 fm and −1.76 keV at r = 2.0 fm.
- The charged-neutral pion mass difference used for comparison is m_π⁺ − m_π⁰ = 4.59 MeV, and the neutron-proton mass difference is M_n − M_p = 1.29 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the leading order πγ-exchange NN-potential calculated in chiral perturbation theory by van Kolck (2000) to next-to-leading order, focusing on the dominant magnetic moment contribution. The results are compared with earlier isospin-breaking 2π-exchange potentials from Friar (1999) and van Kolck (2000) that considered pion mass differences and nucleon mass differences, which the author notes are substantially larger than the one-photon exchange corrections studied here. The analytical expressions for T-matrices and coordinate-space potentials are presented in a form suitable for implementation into NN-phase shift analyses or few-body calculations, enabling quantitative studies of long-range isospin-violating NN-interactions.
