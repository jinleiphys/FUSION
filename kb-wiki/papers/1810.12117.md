# General analysis of polarization effects in coherent pseudoscalar mesons photo-- and electroproduction on deuteron

> General expressions for the unpolarized differential cross section and for various polarization observables in the coherent pseudoscalar meson photo- and electroproduction on the deuteron target have been obtained in one-photon-exchange approximation. The spin structure of the matrix element is explicitly derived in terms of structure functions. The correspondence with the helicity amplitudes is given. The polarization effects have been investigated for the case of a longitudinally polarized electron beam and vector or tensor polarized deuteron target. The polarization (vector or tensor) of the scattered deuteron for the case of unpolarized or a longitudinally polarized electron beam is also considered. In the case of the photoproduction reaction, we consider linearly, circularly or elliptically polarized photon beam. Numerical estimations have been done for the unpolarized differential cross section and for some polarization observables.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general theoretical framework for polarization observables in coherent pseudoscalar meson photo- and electroproduction on the deuteron, but the numerical estimations are limited to the unpolarized differential cross section and only some polarization observables, with the full set of 25 required observables not being experimentally determined.

## Method

The authors derived general expressions for the differential cross section and polarization observables in the one-photon-exchange approximation, using the spin structure of the matrix element parameterized in terms of 13 scalar amplitudes and structure functions. The formalism was applied to cases of longitudinally polarized electron beams with vector or tensor polarized deuteron targets, as well as polarized scattered deuterons, with numerical estimations performed using impulse approximation and an elementary model from Rekalo (2002).

## Key numbers

- The reaction \(e^- + d \to e^- + d + \pi^0\) requires measurement of 25 observables for a complete experiment, based on 9 amplitudes for transverse virtual photon absorption and 4 amplitudes for longitudinal virtual photon absorption
- The exclusive cross section for longitudinally polarized electrons scattering by a tensor polarized deuteron target is determined by 23 independent asymmetries (16 for unpolarized electrons, 7 for longitudinally polarized electrons)
- The structure function \(h_5\) (fifth structure function) vanishes in collinear kinematics at \(\vartheta = 0^\circ\) and \(\vartheta = 180^\circ\) due to conservation of total helicity
- Previous measurements of coherent \(\pi^0\)-meson photoproduction from deuteron covered energy ranges \(140\ \text{MeV} < E_\gamma < 306\ \text{MeV}\) (Siodlaczek, 2001) and \(200\ \text{MeV} < E_\gamma < 792\ \text{MeV}\) (Krusche, 1999)
- Coherent \(\pi^0\)-meson electroproduction data at large \(Q^2\) (\(1.1 < Q^2 < 1.8\ \text{GeV}^2\)) were reported by Tomasi-Gustafsson (2003)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Rekalo (2002) for unpolarized pion electroproduction on deuterons to include comprehensive polarization observables, building on experimental programs at Mainz and JLab that combine polarized electron beams with polarized deuteron targets. The framework enables future measurements of polarization asymmetries that can provide additional information on nucleon resonance properties and reaction mechanisms beyond what unpolarized cross sections alone can reveal, particularly through the fifth structure function \(h_5\) which is sensitive to final-state interaction effects and vanishes in the impulse approximation.
