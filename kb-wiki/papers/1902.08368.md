# Flow and vorticity with varying chemical potential in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the vorticity patterns in relativistic heavy ion collisions with respect to the collision energy. The collision energy is related to the chemical potential used in the thermal - statistical models that assume approximate chemical equilibrium after the relativistic collision. We use the multiphase transport model (AMPT) to study the vorticity in the initial parton phase as well as the final hadronic phase of the relativistic heavy ion collision. We find that as the chemical potential increases,the vortices are larger in size. Using different definitions of vorticity, we find that vorticity plays a greater role at lower collision energies than at higher collision energies. We also look at other effects of the flow patterns related to the bulk viscosity and the shear viscosity at different collision energies. We find that the shear viscosity obtained is almost a constant with a small decrease at higher collision energies. We also look at the elliptic flow as it is related to viscous effects in the final stages after the collision. Our results indicate that viscosity plays a greater role at higher chemical potential and lower collision energies.

## Key claim

The authors find that as collision energy decreases (and baryon chemical potential increases), vortices become larger and more diffuse, and they conclude that bulk viscosity plays a greater role than shear viscosity in the evolution of vorticity patterns at lower collision energies, though this conclusion is qualitative rather than quantitative.

## Method

The authors used the multiphase transport model (AMPT) in its string melting version to simulate Au+Au collisions at a fixed impact parameter of b = 7 fm, for collision energies between 20 GeV and 200 GeV. They calculated classical, relativistic kinetic, and thermal vorticities in the reaction (x-z) plane using a grid-based method with 0.5 fm cell size, averaging over many events to obtain smooth momentum distributions. The shear viscosity was computed separately for neutrons, protons, pions, and Lambda hyperons using the definition from the hadron resonance gas model of Kadam and Mishra, and elliptic flow was extracted from the hadronic phase and compared to STAR collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- Impact parameter fixed at b = 7 fm for all simulations
- Cell size of 0.5 fm in each direction used for grid-based vorticity calculation
- Collision energies studied range from 20 GeV to 200 GeV
- Vorticity values in partonic stage at 200 GeV and 100 GeV range between -0.06 and 0.08 (non-relativistic definition)
- Parameterization of baryon chemical potential: d = 1.308 ± 0.028 GeV and e = 0.273 ± 0.008 GeV⁻¹ in the relation μ_B(√s) = d/(1 + e√s)
- Specific shear viscosity is highest at lower collision energies and becomes nearly constant above 80 GeV
- Elliptic flow (v₂) vs. p_T at 19.6 GeV from AMPT simulations is close to STAR collaboration data at higher p_T range

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vorticity in heavy ion collisions by Jiang et al. (who studied vorticity in the x-η plane) and Deng et al. (who studied the x-y plane at high energies), by focusing on lower collision energies where finite baryon chemical potential becomes significant. The authors compare their AMPT-based results to predictions from hadron resonance gas (HRG) models, noting agreement that bulk viscosity plays a greater role than shear viscosity at higher chemical potentials. The study enables further investigation of vorticity patterns and transport properties of quark-gluon plasma at finite chemical potentials, particularly at lower collision energies accessible at RHIC.
