# Cumulants from fluctuating width of rapidity distribution

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the longitudinal fluctuations of the fireball density caused, e.g., by baryon stopping fluctuations result in event-by-event modifications of the shape of the proton rapidity density distribution. The multiparticle rapidity correlation functions due to the varying distribution width of the proton rapidity density in central Au+Au collisions at low energies are derived. The cumulant ratios are calculated and discussed in the context of the recent STAR Collaboration results. We find that the cumulant ratios for small width fluctuations seem to be universal.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytic expressions for multiparticle rapidity correlation functions and cumulant ratios arising solely from event-by-event fluctuations in the width of the proton rapidity density distribution, and find that for small width fluctuations the cumulant ratios become universal, i.e., independent of the specific probability distribution assumed for the width.

## Method

The authors assume the single-particle proton rapidity density in central Au+Au collisions at low energies is Gaussian, approximated by a quadratic function near midrapidity. They treat the standard deviation (width) of this distribution as a random variable fluctuating event-by-event according to various probability distributions (uniform, triangular, lognormal, truncated normal). They derive analytic expressions for the multiparticle rapidity correlation functions and factorial cumulants by averaging over the fluctuating width, then compute cumulant ratios using standard relations between cumulants and factorial cumulants.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, the authors use N_t ≈ 200 and sigma_0 ≈ 1.5, with Y = 0.5 (the STAR measurement range)
- For sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV, the authors use N_t ≈ 175 and sigma_0 ≈ 0.75, extracted from preliminary STAR data
- The ratio sigma_0(3 GeV)/sigma_0(7.7 GeV) = 0.75/1.5 = 0.5, close to the estimate ln(3)/ln(7.7) ≈ 0.54
- For small width fluctuations (epsilon < 0.05), the cumulant ratios from width fluctuations are of the same order of magnitude as those measured experimentally by STAR
- The leading-order term of the second factorial cumulant for all three distributions is: hat_C_2 ≈ (N_t z / sqrt(2 pi))^2 epsilon^2 (2 - z^2)^2, where z = Y/sigma_0

## Context

This work extends the study of longitudinal fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, previously addressed using orthogonal polynomial expansions by Bzdak (2012) and others, where width fluctuations correspond to the a_2 coefficient. The authors propose a complementary approach focusing directly on width fluctuations of the rapidity density distribution. The results are discussed in the context of recent STAR Collaboration measurements of cumulant ratios at low collision energies (3 GeV and 7.7 GeV), which are potentially sensitive to the predicted phase transition and critical point between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma. The work enables further investigation of how longitudinal fluctuations contribute to proton multiplicity cumulants and should be considered alongside other correlation sources such as global baryon number conservation studied by the ALICE Collaboration (2019) and others.
