# Three-body correlations in few-body nuclei

> A detailed comparison of Faddeev and variational wave functions for $^3$H, calculated with realistic nuclear forces, has been made to study the form of three-body correlations in few-body nuclei. Three new three-body correlations for use in variational wave functions have been identified, which substantially reduce the difference with the Faddeev wave function. The difference between the variational upper bound and the Faddeev binding energy is reduced by half, to typically $<2\%$. These three-body correlations also produce a significant lowering of the variational binding energy for $^4$He and larger nuclei.

# Three-body correlations in few-body nuclei

## Key claim

Three new three-body correlation operators, identified by comparing Faddeev and variational wave functions for tritium, reduce the discrepancy between variational upper bound and Faddeev binding energy by roughly half, to typically less than 2%, but only for configurations where the Faddeev local energy lies within 5% of the exact eigenvalue.

## Method

The authors compared a 34-channel Faddeev wave function for tritium (from the Los Alamos-Iowa group) with variational wave functions built from two-body correlations and existing three-body correlations, using a set of 1,000 spatial configurations where the Faddeev local energy was within 5% of the exact eigenvalue. For each candidate three-body operator, they minimized a configuration-space deviation measure to extract smooth functional forms, then optimized the final parameters by minimizing the variational binding energy of tritium with the Argonne v14 two-nucleon and Urbana VIII three-nucleon interactions using 10,000 variational configurations.

## Key numbers

- Tritium binding energy with Argonne v14 + Urbana VIII: Faddeev = -8.49(1) MeV; old variational = -8.21(2) MeV; new variational = -8.35(1) MeV (difference reduced from 0.28 MeV to 0.14 MeV)
- Helium-4 binding energy with Argonne v14 + Urbana VIII: GFMC = -28.3(2) MeV; old variational = -27.23(6) MeV; new variational = -27.63(5) MeV
- Total deviation E (average squared difference between Faddeev and variational wave functions over 1,000 test configurations): old = 0.00062; new = 0.00028
- Mean excitation energy of admixtures in variational wave functions: approximately 150 MeV for both old and new wave functions
- Parameters of the new central three-body correlation: q^c_1 = 0.19 fm^{-6}, q^c_2 = 0.83 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the variational Monte Carlo approach of Wiringa (1991) for few-body nuclei by adding three-body correlations identified through direct comparison with Faddeev wave functions from Chen, Payne, Friar, and Gibson (1985). The improved variational wave functions reduce the variance in Green's function Monte Carlo calculations and lower variational upper bounds for helium-4 and six-body nuclei such as lithium-6, where the new correlations lower the energy by approximately 0.25 MeV. The method enables more accurate starting wave functions for GFMC calculations of light nuclei and variational studies of heavier systems.
