# Three-body decay of the d* dibaryon

> Under certain circumstances, a three-body decay width can be approximated by an integral involving a product of two off-shell two-body decay widths. This ``angle-average'' approximation is used to calculate the $πNN$ decay width of the $d^*(J^π=3^+, T=0)$ dibaryon in a simple $Δ^2$ model for the most important Feynman diagrams describing pion emissions with baryon-baryon recoil and meson retardation. The decay width is found to be about 0.006 (0.07, 0.5) MeV at the $d^*$ mass of 2065 (2100, 2150) MeV for input dynamics derived from the Full Bonn potential. The smallness of this width is qualitatively understood as the result of the three-body decay being ``third forbidden''. The concept of $\ell$ forbiddenness and the threshold behavior of a three-body decay are further studied in connection with the $πNN$ decay of the dibaryon $d'(J^π=0^-, T=0 or 2)$ where the idea of unfavorness has to be introduced. The implications of these results are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The three-body decay width of the $d^*$ dibaryon into $\pi NN$ is estimated to be very small (on the order of keV to hundreds of keV depending on the $d^*$ mass) because the decay is "third-forbidden" in analogy to nuclear $\beta$ decay, requiring three units of orbital angular momentum change, and because destructive interference between the dominant Feynman diagrams reduces the width by roughly an order of magnitude compared to the leading diagram alone.

## Method

The author develops an "angle-average" approximation that reduces the three-body decay width integral to a product of two off-shell two-body decay widths, then applies this to the leading Feynman diagrams (recoil and retardation diagrams) for pion emission from baryons in a simple $\Delta^2$ model of the $d^*$ dibaryon. The baryon-baryon dynamics are taken from the Full Bonn potential in the Born approximation and from empirical on-shell $NN$ $t$-matrices constructed from experimental $NN$ phase shifts (Love and Franey). The calculation uses nonrelativistic quark model relations to connect the different vertex amplitudes and includes the dominant P-wave pion emission processes from Figs. 1a-c.

## Key numbers

- Decay width of $d^* \rightarrow \pi NN$ is about 0.006 MeV at $d^*$ mass 2065 MeV, 0.07 MeV at 2100 MeV, and 0.5 MeV at 2150 MeV, using dynamics from the Full Bonn potential.
- The dominant diagram (Fig. 1a) alone would give a width roughly an order of magnitude larger than the total after destructive interference: at 2100 MeV, the individual diagram ratios are $\Gamma(a,a):\Gamma(b,b):\Gamma(c,c) \approx 1:(-0.41)^2:(-0.10)^2$.
- The free $\Delta$ decay width used as input is $\Gamma_\Delta = 120$ MeV at $m_\Delta = 1232$ MeV, with $k^* = 227$ MeV and $E_N^* = 966$ MeV in the $\Delta$ center-of-mass frame.
- The Gaussian form factor parameter is $\alpha = r_p^2/6 = 1.54 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$, with proton radius $r_p = 0.6$ fm.
- Energy denominators at $m^* = 2100$ (2200) MeV: $D_a \approx 178$ (224) MeV, $D_b \approx -290$ (-236) MeV, $D_c \approx -578$ (-524) MeV.
- The maximum pion momentum $p_{3\,{\rm max}}$ is 165 MeV at $m^* = 2100$ MeV and 270 MeV at $m^* = 2200$ MeV.
- The maximum off-shell two-body width $\Gamma_{3b\,{\rm max}}$ is 59 MeV at $m^* = 2100$ MeV and 256 MeV at $m^* = 2200$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of dibaryons with exotic quark structures, particularly the $d^*$ ($J^\pi = 3^+, T=0$) which has been of interest due to its possibly low mass indicating unusual structure or dynamics (Goldman 1989, Wang 1992, Goldman 1995). The paper provides a method for estimating three-body decay widths that can be applied to other dibaryons such as the $d'$ ($J^\pi = 0^-, T=0$ or 2), where the concept of "unfavoredness" is introduced. The results suggest that at low dibaryon masses (around 2100 MeV), the decay width of about 70 keV may produce too few pions for experimental identification, which has implications for searches using the quark-delocalization model of $d^*$ (Goldman 1989, Wang 1992, Goldman 1995).
