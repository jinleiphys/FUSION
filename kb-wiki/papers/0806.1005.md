# Viscous damping of r-mode oscillations in compact stars with quark matter

> We determine characteristic timescales for the viscous damping of r-mode oscillations in rapidly rotating compact stars that contain quark matter. We present results for the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense quark matter, in which the up, down and strange quarks are gapped, as well as the normal (ungapped) quark phase. While the ungapped quark phase supports a temperature window between 10^8 K and 5x10^9 K where the r-mode is damped even for rapid rotation, the r-mode in a rapidly rotating pure CFL star is not damped in the temperature range 10^10 K - 10^11 K. Rotating hybrid stars with quark matter cores display an instability window whose width is determined by the amount of quark matter present, and they can have large spin frequencies outside this window. Except at high temperatures T > 10^10 K, the presence of a quark phase allows for larger critical frequencies and smaller spin-periods compared to rotating neutron stars. If low-mass X-ray binaries contain a large amount of ungapped or CFL quark matter, then our estimates of the r-mode instability suggest that there should be a population of rapidly rotating binaries at frequencies greater than 1000 Hz which have not yet been observed.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the r-mode instability window in compact stars containing quark matter depends critically on whether the quark phase is gapped (color-flavor-locked, CFL) or ungapped, and on the amount of quark matter present; however, the authors note that their estimates assume a sharp hadron-quark interface and neglect general relativistic corrections to the r-mode analysis, which could modify the quantitative conclusions.

## Method

The authors compute r-mode frequencies and damping timescales for rotating compact stars using four equations of state: the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) hadronic EoS, a polytropic EoS, an MIT Bag model for ungapped quark matter, and a CFL quark matter EoS. They solve the second-order perturbation equations for the l=m=2 r-mode in Newtonian gravity, including the Cowling approximation for gravitational perturbations, and combine these with temperature-dependent bulk and shear viscosities from the literature for hadronic matter, ungapped quark matter, and CFL quark matter to determine the viscous damping timescale.

## Key numbers

- For ungapped quark matter, the r-mode is damped in a temperature window between 10^8 K and 5x10^9 K even for rapid rotation.
- For a pure CFL star, the r-mode is not damped in the temperature range 10^10 K to 10^11 K.
- The r-mode frequency correction parameter kappa_2 for the Bag EoS is 0.198, and for the CFL EoS is 0.195, for 1.4 solar mass stars.
- For hybrid stars with ungapped quark matter (APR+Bag, 1.7 solar masses), kappa_2 = 0.083; for hybrid stars with CFL quark matter (APR+CFL, 1.7 solar masses), kappa_2 = 0.099.
- The shear viscosity of ungapped quark matter is given by eta_q = 6.99 x 10^17 (0.1/alpha_s)^(3/2) (rho/rho_0)^(5/3) T_9^(-2) g/(cm s).
- The bulk viscosity of ungapped quark matter has a prefactor alpha T^2 = 6.66 x 10^20 (mu_d/MeV)^3 (m_s/MeV)^4 T_9^2 g/(cm s^3).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of r-mode damping in neutron stars (Cutler 1987, Lindblom and Mendell) to include quark matter phases, specifically the CFL superconducting phase and ungapped quark matter. The results suggest that if low-mass X-ray binaries contain significant amounts of quark matter, there should be a population of rapidly rotating binaries at frequencies above 1000 Hz that have not yet been observed, providing a testable prediction for gravitational wave detectors like Advanced LIGO. The paper also connects to studies of neutron star cooling (Jaikumar, Prakash, and Schafer) and transport in color superconductors (Shovkovy and Ellis).
