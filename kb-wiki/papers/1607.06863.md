# Nuclear Physics without High-Momentum Potentials: Direct Construction of the Effective Interaction from Scattering Observables

> The standard approach to nuclear physics encodes phase shift information in an NN potential, then decodes that information in forming an effective interaction, appropriate to a low-momentum Hilbert space. Here we show that it is instead possible to construct the effective interaction directly from continuum phase shifts and mixing angles, eliminating all reference to a high momentum potential. The theory is rapidly convergent and well behaved, yielding sub-keV accuracy.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that an effective interaction for low-momentum nuclear physics can be constructed directly from experimental phase shifts and mixing angles, without ever encoding that information into a high-momentum NN potential. The method yields sub-keV accuracy for the deuteron binding energy, but only when the chiral one-pion exchange is included as an infrared correction.

## Method

The authors use the Bloch-Horowitz equation reorganized so that the effective Hamiltonian \(H^\mathrm{eff}\) is expressed in terms of the free Green's function \(G_{QT}\), which carries the infrared physics including the phase shifts. The short-range part of the interaction is expanded in harmonic oscillator creation and annihilation operators, producing a gradient expansion whose low-energy constants (LECs) are fit by minimizing the self-consistency error between the input energy and the eigenvalue of \(H^\mathrm{eff}\) at that energy. The fit uses phase shifts from 1 to 40 MeV center-of-mass energy, with a P-space defined by oscillator parameter \(b = 1.7\) fm and maximum quanta \(\Lambda = 8\).

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy from pionful HOBET at N\(^3\)LO: \(-2.22464\) MeV, with self-consistency error \(C^2 = 8.4 \times 10^{-9}\).
* Deuteron binding energy from pionless HOBET at N\(^3\)LO: \(-2.23175\) MeV, with self-consistency error \(C^2 = 1.3 \times 10^{-3}\).
* In a square-well-plus-hard-core test model, the self-consistency error improved by approximately two orders of magnitude per order in the expansion.
* The residual energy dependence of the leading LEC \(a_{LO}\) was reduced from about 3% at LO to less than 0.1% after including the NLO correction.
* The S-wave LEC \(a_{LO}^{3S1}\) was determined to be \(-54.8429\) MeV in pionful HOBET and \(-49.9309\) MeV in pionless HOBET at N\(^3\)LO.

## Context

This work extends the harmonic oscillator based effective theory (HOBET) previously developed by Haxton and collaborators (2012), which had been validated by integrating out the Argonne \(v18\) potential. The present paper severs that connection to realistic potentials entirely, making HOBET a true effective field theory. The approach contrasts with the standard two-step method of first constructing an NN potential (e.g., Argonne \(v18\) by Wiringa et al., 1995) and then renormalizing it, and also differs from chiral EFT approaches where the pion is an explicit degree of freedom at all ranges. The method enables direct calculation of bound-state properties from scattering data and is intended to be extended to A-body systems by summing the iterated softened interactions.
