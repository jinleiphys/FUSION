# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of Light Nuclei Using Chiral Potentials

> We present the first Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei with nuclear interactions derived from chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order. Up to this order, the interactions can be constructed in a local form and are therefore amenable to quantum Monte Carlo calculations. We demonstrate a systematic improvement with each order for the binding energies of $A=3$ and $A=4$ systems. We also carry out the first few-body tests to study perturbative expansions of chiral potentials at different orders, finding that higher-order corrections are more perturbative for softer interactions. Our results confirm the necessity of a three-body force for correct reproduction of experimental binding energies and radii, and pave the way for studying few- and many-nucleon systems using quantum Monte Carlo methods with chiral interactions.

## Key claim

The first Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of light nuclei using chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) demonstrate systematic improvement in binding energies with each chiral order, but the N$^2$LO results with only two-body forces still underbind the nuclei, confirming the necessity of a three-body force for correct reproduction of experimental binding energies and radii.

## Method

The authors performed GFMC calculations for nuclei with mass number A less than or equal to 4 using local chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and N$^2$LO, with regulator cutoffs R0 of 1.0, 1.1, and 1.2 fm (corresponding approximately to momentum cutoffs of 500, 450, and 400 MeV). They computed ground-state binding energies and point proton radii for $^3$H, $^3$He, and $^4$He, and also carried out perturbative calculations for the deuteron treating the difference between N$^2$LO and NLO potentials as a perturbation up to third order.

## Key numbers

- $^4$He binding energies at N$^2$LO: -23.72(1) MeV (R0=1.0 fm), -24.13(1) MeV (R0=1.1 fm), -24.86(1) MeV (R0=1.2 fm); experimental value is -28.31 MeV
- $^4$He point proton radii at N$^2$LO: 1.52(1) fm (R0=1.0 fm), 1.50(1) fm (R0=1.1 fm), 1.47(1) fm (R0=1.2 fm); experimental value is 1.45 fm
- $^3$He binding energies at N$^2$LO: -6.78(1) MeV (R0=1.0 fm), -6.90(1) MeV (R0=1.1 fm), -7.01(1) MeV (R0=1.2 fm); experimental value is -7.72 MeV
- $^3$H binding energies at N$^2$LO: -7.55(1) MeV (R0=1.0 fm), -7.63(1) MeV (R0=1.1 fm), -7.74(1) MeV (R0=1.2 fm); experimental value is -8.48 MeV
- For $^4$He, the spread from cutoff variation is approximately 3.8 MeV at LO, 1.4 MeV at NLO, and 1.1 MeV at N$^2$LO
- At LO, $^4$He is overbound by up to approximately 65% of the experimental binding energy (for R0=1.2 fm)
- For the deuteron, the first-order perturbative correction (N$^2$LO minus NLO) is positive and ranges from 12% to 33% of the NLO binding energy for R0=1.2 to 1.0 fm; third-order corrections are 0.46% to 0.93%

## Context

This work extends the GFMC method, previously limited to phenomenological potentials like the Argonne v18 two-body potential (Wiringa, 1995) and Urbana/Illinois three-body forces (Pudliner, 1995; Pieper, 2001), to chiral EFT interactions. It builds on the development of local chiral potentials by Gezerlis (2013, 2014) that are amenable to quantum Monte Carlo methods. The results enable future GFMC studies of nuclei up to A=12 with chiral interactions and provide benchmarks for other methods that require softened interactions, as well as paving the way for auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo simulations of larger systems.
