# Nuclear matter properties from local chiral interactions with $Δ$ isobar intermediate states

> Using two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions derived in the framework of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with and without the explicit $Δ$ isobar contributions, we calculate the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter in the framework of the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. In particular, we present for the first time nuclear matter calculations using the new fully local in coordinate-space two-nucleon interaction at the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order (N3LO) of ChPT with $Δ$ isobar intermediate states (N3LO$Δ$) recently developed by Piarulli et al. [arXiv:1606:06335]. We find that using this N3LO$Δ$ potential, supplemented with a local N2LO three-nucleon interaction with explicit $Δ$ isobar degrees of freedom, it is possible to obtain a satisfactory saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter. For this combination of two- and three-nucleon interactions we also calculate the nuclear symmetry energy and we compare our results with the empirical constraints on this quantity obtained using the excitation energies to isobaric analog states in nuclei and using experimental data on the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei, finding a very good agreement with these empirical constraints in all the considered nucleonic density range. In addition, we find that the explicit inclusion of $Δ$ isobars diminishes the strength of the three-nucleon interactions needed the get a good saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter. We also compare the results of our calculations with those obtained by other research groups using chiral nuclear interactions with different many-body methods, finding in many cases a very satisfactory agreement.

## Key claim

Using the new fully local N3LO chiral two-nucleon potential with explicit Delta isobar intermediate states (N3LOΔ), supplemented with a local N2LO three-nucleon interaction also including Delta isobar degrees of freedom, it is possible to obtain a satisfactory saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter, but only after fitting the three-nucleon force low-energy constants c_D and c_E to achieve this saturation, since no few-body calculations exist yet for this interaction model.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using chiral two-nucleon interactions at N3LO (both with and without explicit Delta isobar contributions) and chiral three-nucleon interactions at N2LO. The three-nucleon forces were converted into effective density-dependent two-body forces by averaging over one nucleon using the momentum-space averaging procedure of Holt et al., and these effective forces were then included in the Bethe-Goldstone equation alongside the bare two-nucleon interaction. Partial wave contributions up to total two-body angular momentum J_max = 8 were included.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1: 0.171 fm^{-3} with energy per particle -15.23 MeV
- Saturation density for N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2: 0.176 fm^{-3} with energy per particle -15.09 MeV
- Saturation density for N3LO+N2LO(500): 0.135 fm^{-3} with energy per particle -12.12 MeV
- Saturation density for N3LO+N2LO(450): 0.156 fm^{-3} with energy per particle -14.32 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation for N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1: 35.39 MeV; for N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2: 36.00 MeV
- Slope parameter L at saturation for N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1: 76.0 MeV; for N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2: 79.8 MeV
- Incompressibility K_infinity at saturation for N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1: 190 MeV; for N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2: 176 MeV
- Contribution of three-nucleon force to energy per particle in symmetric nuclear matter at rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}: 1.80 MeV for N2LOΔ1, compared to 6.22 MeV for N2LO(500) and 6.39 MeV for N2LO(450)
- Contribution of three-nucleon force to energy per particle in pure neutron matter at rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}: 3.49 MeV for N2LOΔ1, compared to 4.20 MeV for N2LO(450)

## Context

This work extends previous BHF calculations by Logoteta et al. (2015, 2016) using chiral nuclear interactions, now incorporating for the first time the fully local N3LOΔ potential developed by Piarulli et al. (2016). The results confirm that Delta-full chiral perturbation theory has improved convergence compared to Delta-less ChPT, as previously argued by Kaiser et al. (1998) and Krebs et al. (2007), since the three-nucleon force contribution is significantly reduced when Delta isobars are included explicitly. The calculated symmetry energy shows good agreement with empirical constraints from isobaric analog state excitation energies and neutron skin thickness data, and the results enable future Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of nuclei and nuclear matter using these local chiral interactions.
