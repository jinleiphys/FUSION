# Remarcs on the shape transition from spherical to deformed gamma unstable nuclei

> Energies and transition probabilities for low lying states in $^{134}$Ba and $^{104$Ru were calculated within a hybrid model.The ground and the first $2^+$ states are described alternatively as a harmonic and anharmonic vibrator states while the remaining states as states with E(5) symmetry. One concludes that a gradual setting of the 'critical' potential yields a better agreement with the experimental data. Very good agreement with the data is obtained for $^{104}$Ru. Comparing the present results with those of E(5) symmetry, it is conspicuous that the present formalism add corrections to the E(5) formalism by bringing the predictions closer to the experimental data. Analytical relationship between the states with U(5) symmetry and those given by the E(5) description is established.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the critical point for the spherical-to-gamma-unstable shape transition is state-dependent: the ground state (0⁺) and first excited 2⁺ state retain spherical vibrator (U(5)) character while higher-lying states exhibit E(5) critical-point symmetry, yielding better agreement with experimental data than the pure E(5) description where all states share the same critical potential.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid model where the 0⁺ and 2⁺ states are described by either harmonic or anharmonic spherical vibrator wave functions (solutions of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with a harmonic β² potential), while all higher-lying states (L ≥ 4) are described by E(5) wave functions (solutions for a five-dimensional infinite square well potential). The width of the square well (β_w) is fixed by maximizing the overlap between the vibrational and E(5) wave functions for the lowest 0⁺ and 2⁺ states. Excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities are calculated for ¹³⁴Ba and ¹⁰⁴Ru and compared with experimental data and pure E(5) predictions.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³⁴Ba with β_w = 3.1 (pure vibrational 0⁺ and 2⁺): E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) = 2.58 (experiment: 2.32); E(0⁺₂)/E(2⁺) = 3.71 (experiment: 3.57); B(E2; 4⁺→2⁺)/B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) = 1.88 (experiment: 1.56(18)); B(E2; 0⁺₂→2⁺)/B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) = 0.28 (experiment: 0.42(12))
- For ¹³⁴Ba with anharmonic 0⁺ and 2⁺ (x = 0.2, β_w = 3.186): E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) = 2.39 (experiment: 2.32); E(0⁺₂)/E(2⁺) = 3.47 (experiment: 3.57); B(E2; 4⁺→2⁺)/B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) = 1.20 (experiment: 1.56(18)); B(E2; 0⁺₂→2⁺)/B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) = 0.55 (experiment: 0.42(12))
- For ¹⁰⁴Ru with anharmonic 0⁺ and 2⁺ (x = 0.15, β_w = 3.17): E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) = 2.40 (experiment: 2.48); E(6⁺)/E(2⁺) = 4.22 (experiment: 4.35); E(8⁺)/E(2⁺) = 6.29 (experiment: 6.48); E(10⁺)/E(2⁺) = 8.60 (experiment: 8.69); B(E2; 4⁺→2⁺)/B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) = 1.18 (experiment: 1.18(15)); B(E2; 0⁺₂→2⁺)/B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) = 0.47 (experiment: 0.42(7))
- Pure E(5) predictions for comparison: E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) = 2.20; B(E2; 4⁺→2⁺)/B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) = 1.68; B(E2; 0⁺₂→2⁺)/B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) = 0.86

## Context

This work extends the E(5) critical-point symmetry introduced by Iachello (2001) for the U(5)-to-O(6) shape transition by incorporating the idea that the critical point may be state-dependent, as previously suggested by Raduta (1986) for Gd isotopes and by studies of angular-momentum-dependent phase transitions in triaxial rotors (Gheorghe) and cranked systems. The hybrid model improves upon pure E(5) predictions for ¹³⁴Ba and ¹⁰⁴Ru, particularly for the B(E2) value of the 0⁺₂ → 2⁺ transition, and provides an analytical relationship between U(5) and E(5) basis states. The approach enables more accurate description of transitional nuclei where the critical behavior is not uniform across all states.
