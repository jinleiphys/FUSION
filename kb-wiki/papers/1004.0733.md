# Abrikosov Gluon Vortices in Color Superconductors

> In this talk I will discuss how the in-medium magnetic field can influence the gluon dynamics in a three-flavor color superconductor. It will be shown how at field strengths comparable to the charged gluon Meissner mass a new phase can be realized, giving rise to Abrikosov's vortices of charged gluons. In that phase, the inhomogeneous gluon condensate anti-screens the magnetic field due to the anomalous magnetic moment of these spin-1 particles. This paramagnetic effect can be of interest for astrophysics, since due to the gluon vortex antiscreening mechanism, compact stars with color superconducting cores could have larger magnetic fields than neutron stars made up entirely of nuclear matter. I will also discuss a second gluon condensation phenomenon connected to the Meissner instability attained at moderate densities by two-flavor color superconductors. In this situation, an inhomogeneous condensate of charged gluons emerges to remove the chromomagnetic instability created by the pairing mismatch, and as a consequence, the charged gluonic currents induce a magnetic field. Finally, I will point out a possible relation between glitches in neutron stars and the existence of the gluon vortices.

## Key claim

In a three-flavor color superconductor, an externally applied magnetic field with strength comparable to the charged gluon Meissner mass can induce a new phase where charged gluons form Abrikosov vortices; these vortices anti-screen the magnetic field due to the anomalous magnetic moment of the spin-1 gluons, producing a paramagnetic effect rather than the diamagnetic Meissner effect seen in ordinary superconductors.

## Method

The author analyzes the Ginzburg-Landau free energy for the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of three-flavor quark matter in an external rotated magnetic field, including the anomalous magnetic moment contribution of charged gluons. By minimizing the Gibbs free energy density with respect to the gluon condensate amplitude and the induced magnetic field, the resulting equations are shown to be analogous to Abrikosov's Ginzburg-Landau equations for type-II superconductors, but with a sign difference in the coupling between the condensate and the magnetic field. The solutions are found following Abrikosov's approach for applied fields near the critical value, yielding a vortex lattice of charged gluons.

## Key numbers

- The threshold field for the CFL to MCFL phase transition is given by \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{H}_{MCFL} = \frac{4}{v_{\bot}^2}\Delta_{CFL}^2 \simeq 12\Delta_{CFL}^2\); for \(\Delta_{CFL} \sim 15\) MeV this yields \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{H}_{MCFL} \sim 10^{16}\) G.
- The hierarchy of scales in the color superconductor at high baryon chemical potential \(\mu\) is \(\Delta \ll m_g \ll \mu\), where \(\Delta\) is the gap and \(m_g\) is the gluon Meissner mass.
- The gluon vortex phase occurs when the applied rotated magnetic field strength satisfies \(\widetilde{H} \geq m_M^2\), where \(m_M\) is the charged gluon Meissner mass.
- The maximum possible magnetic field in neutron stars from the virial theorem is estimated at \(10^{19}\) G.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior studies of the Magnetic Color Flavor Locked (MCFL) phase (Ferrer, 2006) and the paramagnetic CFL (PCFL) phase (Ferrer, 2007), where magnetic fields were shown to affect gap structure and symmetry breaking patterns in color superconductors. The gluon vortex mechanism is analogous to the antiscreening of magnetic fields found in the electroweak theory for fields \(H \geq M_W^2/e \sim 10^{24}\) G (Olesen, 1999). The paper also connects to work on chromomagnetic instabilities in two-flavor color superconductors (Huang, 2004) where an inhomogeneous gluon condensate can be induced without an external field. The results enable new models for magnetar magnetic field generation and suggest a possible mechanism for pulsar glitches through interactions between gluon vortices and superfluid vortices in neutron star cores.
