# Pairing correlations of cold fermionic gases at overflow from a narrow to a wide harmonic trap

> Within the context of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory, we study the behavior of superfluid Fermi systems when they pass from a small to a large container. Such systems can be now realized thanks to recent progress in experimental techniques. It will allow to better understand pairing properties at overflow and in general in rapidly varying external potentials.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when a superfluid Fermi system overflows from a narrow to a wide harmonic trap, the spectral gap (lowest quasiparticle energy) drops sharply at the overflow point, but the pairing field at the center of the trap remains largely unaffected, contradicting earlier BCS-based predictions that the central gap would also drop.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations, equivalent to the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations, in a spherical double-harmonic trap potential with a contact pairing interaction regularized via the scattering length renormalization scheme of Bulgac (2002) and Grasso (2003). They used a basis of eigenfunctions of the trap potential up to a cutoff energy and varied the chemical potential to study the system from below to above the overflow point at the junction between the narrow and wide trap regions.

## Key numbers

- The overflow point occurs at chemical potential mu = 25 in trap units (hbar*omega_1 = 1, oscillator length sqrt(hbar/m*omega_1) = 1), with the trap junction at radius R_0 = sqrt(50) approximately 7.1.
- The coupling constant used is g = -1.56, corresponding to k_F a = -0.88 at the trap center for mu = 25.
- For the ratio r = omega_2/omega_1 = 0.1, the lowest quasiparticle energy drops practically to zero after the overflow point at mu = 25, while for r = 1 (no wide container) it remains large.
- The cutoff energy E_C = 84 (in trap units) is used for convergence around the drip region.
- For a smaller trap with overflow at mu = 12.25 and R_0 = 4.95, the qualitative behavior remains similar but with stronger shell fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier BCS-based studies (Schuck 2011, Schuck 2012) of fermionic superfluids at overflow, correcting the BCS prediction that the gap at the trap center drops alongside the spectral gap. The results are relevant for understanding pairing in neutron-rich nuclei near the neutron drip line (Pastore 2013, Marini 2012) and in the inner crust of neutron stars (Chamel 2008), where Wigner-Seitz cell calculations mimic the narrow-to-wide container geometry. The findings enable predictions for dynamical properties such as the moment of inertia of rotating traps, where the ratio of the gap to the trap frequency determines whether flow is irrotational or rigid-body-like.
