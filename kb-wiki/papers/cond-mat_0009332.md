# Spectral ergodicity and normal modes in ensembles of sparse matrices

> We investigate the properties of sparse matrix ensembles with particular regard for the spectral ergodicity hypothesis, which claims the identity of ensemble and spectral averages of spectral correlators. An apparent violation of the spectral ergodicity is observed. This effect is studied with the aid of the normal modes of the random matrix spectrum, which describe fluctuations of the eigenvalues around their average positions. This analysis reveals that spectral ergodicity is not broken, but that different energy scales of the spectra are examined by the two averaging techniques. Normal modes are shown to provide a useful complement to traditional spectral analysis with possible applications to a wide range of physical systems.

## Key claim

The apparent violation of spectral ergodicity in sparse matrix ensembles is not a genuine breakdown, but arises because ensemble averaging and spectral averaging probe different energy scales of the spectrum; the key to understanding the discrepancy lies in the definition of the smoothing procedure used for spectral averaging, not in a fundamental failure of ergodicity.

## Method

The authors constructed an ensemble of sparse real symmetric matrices of size N with sparsity parameter α (fraction of non-zero off-diagonal elements), where non-zero elements were drawn from a Gaussian distribution. They computed spectral observables (nearest neighbor spacing distribution and number variance) using both ensemble unfolding (where the mean level spacing is determined from an ensemble average) and spectral self-unfolding (where the mean level spacing is obtained by local smoothing of a single spectrum). They then analyzed the collective fluctuations of eigenvalues using normal modes, which are plane-wave-like independent fluctuations of the eigenvalue positions.

## Key numbers

- The transition from Poisson to Wigner-Dyson nearest neighbor statistics occurs at a critical effective dimension d_eff ≈ 2, determined from the crossing of the γ parameter curves for matrix sizes N = 200, 500, and 1000.
- For d_eff > 2, the number variance follows GOE statistics up to a critical scale L_c(N,d_eff), which shows a power-law dependence on N.
- For d_eff = 0, the spectral density is Gaussian: ρ(E) = (N/(2√π)) exp(-E²/4).
- For d_eff = N (the GOE limit), the spectral density is semicircular: ρ(E) = (1/(2π)) √(4N - E²).
- The effective dimension is defined as d_eff = αN, where α is the fraction of non-zero off-diagonal elements.

## Context

This work extends the study of spectral ergodicity from full random matrices (where ergodicity is rigorously proven, as in Guhr (1998) and Pluhař (2000)) to sparse matrix ensembles relevant to disordered mesoscopic systems, the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) for nuclear physics (Bohr 1971, Flambaum 2000), and lattice QCD (Guhrmann 1999). The paper resolves apparent counter-examples to ergodicity found in TBRE and QCD by showing that the discrepancy arises from different energy scales probed by ensemble versus spectral averaging, and introduces normal modes of the eigenvalue spectrum as a tool for understanding these scale-dependent effects. This enables a unified framework for comparing theoretical ensemble predictions with experimental single-spectrum data across diverse physical systems.
