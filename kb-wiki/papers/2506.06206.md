# Initial stage jet momentum broadening in tBLFQ formalism

> We study the momentum broadening of a high-energy quark jet in the large density gluon medium created right after the collision of two ultrarelativistic heavy nuclei, the Glasma. Previous Glasma studies modeled the jet as a classical probe particle, for which position and momentum are simultaneously determined. In this work, we use the light-front QCD Hamiltonian formalism to treat the jet as a fully quantum state. We compute its real-time evolution while propagating through the Glasma classical background fields, which act as an interaction potential in the quantum evolution of the jet. We present results for the momentum broadening and jet quenching parameter of a jet at mid-rapidity, with special emphasis on the anisotropies between the longitudinal and transverse directions relative to the collision axis. In addition, we compare our results to classical calculations, and initiate a study of the distinction between kinetic and canonic momentum in the context of jet momentum broadening.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully quantum treatment of a quark jet propagating through the Glasma initial stage yields momentum broadening and jet quenching parameter values that agree with classical calculations when using canonical momentum, but the distinction between canonical and kinetic momentum becomes important for understanding anisotropies between transverse and beam directions.

## Method

The authors use the time-dependent Basis Light-Front Quantization (tBLFQ) formalism to compute the real-time evolution of a quark jet as a quantum state interacting with classical Glasma background fields. The jet Fock space is truncated to the leading quark sector, and the eikonal limit is taken where only the interaction with the A_+ component of the background field contributes. The jet wavefunction is expanded in free QCD eigenstates, and the time-dependent Schrodinger equation on the light front is solved to extract the expectation value of transverse momentum squared and the jet quenching parameter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum Q_s = 1.5 GeV, corresponding to central collisions at the Large Hadron Collider
- The jet quenching parameter hat_q_y peaks around x^+ ~ 1/Q_s, reaching values much larger than those typical in the Quark-Gluon Plasma
- Momentum broadening is negligible along the beam direction (z-direction) because the Glasma field in the early stages is uniform along the collision axis
- The classical calculation of kinetic momentum predicts larger broadening in the beam direction (z-direction) than in the transverse direction (y-direction), contrasting with the canonical momentum results

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jets in the Glasma or pre-equilibrium phase by Ipp (2020), Avramescu (2023), Boguslavski (2023), and Barata (2024), which treated the jet as a classical probe particle. The tBLFQ formalism was previously developed for jet evolution in cold nuclear matter and quark-gluon plasma by the same group, and this paper applies it for the first time to the initial-stage Glasma fields. The results enable future studies of quantum effects such as gluon radiation and energy loss that cannot be captured by classical calculations.
