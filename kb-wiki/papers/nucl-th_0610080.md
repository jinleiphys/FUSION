# Theory and Phenomenology of Hadrons

> This Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) aperc,u highlights recent applications to mesons. It reports features of, and results for, pseudoscalar and scalar bound-state residues in vacuum polarisations, and exhibits how a restoration of chiral symmetry in meson trajectories could be manifest in a relationship between them. It also touches on nucleon studies, emphasising the importance of both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, and reporting the calculation of mu_n G_E^n(Q^2)/G_M^n(Q^2). The value of respecting symmetries, including Poincare' covariance, is stressed.

# Theory and Phenomenology of Hadrons

## Key claim

The paper argues that Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a systematic, symmetry-preserving nonperturbative framework for studying hadron properties, but acknowledges that the application to baryons remains at a model-building stage comparable to meson studies from over a decade ago, with the Faddeev equation treatment requiring truncation and input about diquark electromagnetic properties.

## Method

The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations with a nonperturbative, systematic, and symmetry-preserving truncation scheme (Munczek 1994, Bender 1996) to derive exact results for meson masses and decay constants, including a mass formula for pseudoscalar mesons and constraints on scalar meson residues. For nucleons, they use a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation truncated by assuming two of three dressed quarks form scalar or axial-vector diquark correlations, with binding from iterated exchange of the bystander and diquark-participant quarks, and compute electromagnetic form factors using a Ward-Takahashi-identity-preserving current.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar residue for ground-state $\bar uu$: $\rho_{\pi_0^{\bar uu}}(\zeta_{19}) = (0.488\ \text{GeV})^2$ at renormalisation scale $\zeta_{19}=19\ \text{GeV}$
- Pseudoscalar residue for first excited $\bar uu$: $\rho_{\pi_1^{\bar uu}}(\zeta_{19}) = -(0.471\ \text{GeV})^2$
- Scalar residue for first excited $\bar uu$: $\rho_{\sigma_1^{\bar uu}}(\zeta_{19}) = -(0.382\ \text{GeV})^2$
- Ground-state scalar meson mass for $\bar uu$: $m_{\sigma_0^{\bar uu}} = 0.675\ \text{GeV}$; for $\bar ss$: $m_{\sigma_0^{\bar ss}} = 1.076\ \text{GeV}$
- Scalar residue for ground-state $\bar uu$: $\rho_{\sigma_0^{\bar uu}}(\zeta_{19}) = (0.529\ \text{GeV})^2$; for $\bar ss$: $\rho_{\sigma_0^{\bar ss}}(\zeta_{19}) = (0.628\ \text{GeV})^2$
- Diquark mass ranges: scalar $[ud]_{0^+}$ mass $0.74$–$0.82\ \text{GeV}$; axial-vector $(uu)_{1^+}$, $(ud)_{1^+}$, $(dd)_{1^+}$ masses $0.95$–$1.02\ \text{GeV}$
- Electromagnetic size of scalar diquark: $r_{[ud]_{0^+}} \approx 0.7\ \text{fm}$
- Proton ratio $\mu_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2)$ passes through zero at $Q^2 \approx 6.5\ \text{GeV}^2$
- In the chiral limit, all excited pseudoscalar mesons ($n \geq 1$) have vanishing leptonic decay constant: $f_{\pi_n}^{\hat m =0} \equiv 0$
- At physical quark masses, $f_{\pi_1}/f_{\pi_0} = -0.02$

## Context

This work extends the DSE program from meson studies (reviewed in Maris 2003) to baryon structure, building on the Faddeev equation approach of Cahill (1988) and the truncation scheme of Munczek (1994) and Bender (1996). The exact results for pseudoscalar and scalar meson residues provide benchmarks for lattice-QCD techniques (McNeile 2006) and constrain analyses of scalar meson production in $D$- and $B$-meson decays (El-Bennich 2006). The nucleon form factor calculations enable comparison with experimental data from Madey (2003) and predict that $\mu_n G_E^n(Q^2)/G_M^n(Q^2)$ will increase steadily up to $Q^2 \simeq 8\ \text{GeV}^2$, while also showing that pion-loop effects (neglected in the quark-core calculation) materially affect the neutron's electric radius at low $Q^2$.
