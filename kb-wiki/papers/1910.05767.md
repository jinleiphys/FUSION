# Speed of sound constraints on maximally rotating neutron stars

> The observation of maximally rotating neutron stars (in comparison to nonrotating ones) may provide more information on the behavior of nuclear matter at high densities. We provide a theoretical treatment concerning the effects of the upper bound of the sound speed in dense matter on the bulk properties of maximally rotating (at mass-shedding limit) neutron stars. In particular, we consider two upper bounds for the speed of sound, $v_s = c$ and $v_s = c/\sqrt{3}$, and the one provided by the relativistic kinetic theory. We investigate to what extent the possible predicted (from various theories and conjectures) upper bounds on the speed of sound constrain the ones of various key quantities, including the maximum mass and the corresponding radius, Keplerian frequency, Kerr parameter and moment of inertia. We mainly focus on the lower proposed limit, $v_{s}=c/\sqrt{3}$, and we explore in which mass region a rotating neutron star collapses to a black hole. In any case, useful relations of the mentioned bulk properties with the transition density are derived and compared with the corresponding nonrotating cases. We concluded that the proposed limit $v_{s}=c/\sqrt{3}$ leads to dramatic decrease on the values of the maximum mass, Kerr parameter and moment of inertia preventing a neutron star to reach values which derived with the consideration of realistic equations of state or from other constraints. Possible measurements of the Kerr parameter and moment of inertia would shed light on these issues and help to reveal the speed of sound bound in dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that imposing a lower upper bound on the speed of sound in dense matter, specifically \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\), dramatically reduces the maximum mass, Kerr parameter, and moment of inertia of maximally rotating neutron stars compared to the causal bound \(v_s = c\), and that this lower bound prevents neutron stars from reaching values predicted by realistic equations of state or other constraints.

## Method

The authors construct equations of state (EoS) by matching a low-density crust EoS and an intermediate-density EoS based on the Momentum-Dependent-Interaction (MDI) model and data from Akmal (1998) to a maximally stiff high-density region where the speed of sound is fixed to either \(c\), \(c/\sqrt{3}\), or the bound from relativistic kinetic theory. They use the public RNS code by Stergioulas and Friedman (1995) to solve the equilibrium equations for uniformly rotating neutron stars at the mass-shedding (Keplerian) limit, and study the dependence of bulk properties on the transition density \(n_{\rm tr}\) for each sound-speed bound.

## Key numbers

- For the \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\) bound at transition density \(n_{\rm tr} = 1.5 n_{\rm s}\), the maximum gravitational mass of a maximally rotating neutron star is reduced compared to the \(v_s = c\) case; the fit coefficients for the mass formula \(M_{\rm max}/M_\odot = \alpha_1 \coth[\alpha_2 (n_{\rm tr}/n_{\rm s})^{1/2}]\) are \(\alpha_1 = 2.069\) and \(\alpha_2 = 0.883\) for the maximally rotating configuration.
- For the \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\) bound, the equatorial radius fit coefficients for the formula \(R_{\rm max}/{\rm km} = \alpha_3 \coth[\alpha_4 (n_{\rm tr}/n_{\rm s})^{0.8}]\) are \(\alpha_3 = 15.110\) and \(\alpha_4 = 1.009\) for the maximally rotating case.
- The Kerr parameter for the \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\) bound at the maximum mass configuration follows \(\mathcal{K}_{\rm max} = \alpha_7 \coth[\alpha_8 (n_{\rm tr}/n_{\rm s})^{1/2}]\) with \(\alpha_7 = 0.645\) and \(\alpha_8 = 1.348\) for maximally rotating stars.
- The moment of inertia for the \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\) bound at the maximum mass configuration is described by \(I_{\rm max} = \alpha_5 \coth[\alpha_6 (n_{\rm tr}/n_{\rm s})^{3/2}]\) with \(\alpha_5 = 3.040\) and \(\alpha_6 = 0.362\) (in units of \(10^{45}\) g cm\(^2\)) for the maximally rotating case.
- For the \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\) bound, the moment of inertia in the transition density range [1.5–3]\(n_{\rm s}\) follows \(I_{\rm max} = k_2 (1/(n_{\rm tr}/n_{\rm s}))^{k_3}\) with \(k_2 = 8.476\) and \(k_3 = 1.150\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Haensel and Zdunik (1989), Glendenning (1992), Lattimer (1990), and Koranda (1997) on causality bounds for rotating neutron stars, and specifically incorporates the lower sound-speed bound \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\) from strongly coupled theories with gravity duals, as discussed by Bedaque and Steiner (2015). The results enable future observational constraints on the sound-speed bound through measurements of the Kerr parameter and moment of inertia of rapidly rotating neutron stars, and provide a framework for predicting the collapse of rotating neutron stars to black holes via constant rest mass sequences.
