# Cumulants of multiplicity distributions in most-central heavy-ion collisions

> I investigate the volume corrections on cumulants of total charge distributions and net proton distributions. The required volume information is generated by an optical Glauber model. I find that the corrected statistical expectations of multiplicity distributions mimic the negative binomial distributions at non-central collisions, and they tend to approach the Poisson ones at most-central collisions due to the "boundary effects," which suppress the volume corrections. However, net proton distributions and reference multiplicity distributions are sensitive to the external volume fluctuations at most-central collisions, which imply that one has to consider the details of volume distributions in event-by-event multiplicity fluctuation studies.

## Key claim

Volume corrections on cumulants of multiplicity distributions are suppressed at most-central collisions due to "boundary effects" from the upper boundary of system volume, but net proton distributions and reference multiplicity distributions remain sensitive to external Gaussian-type volume fluctuations even at most-central collisions, implying that details of volume distributions must be considered in event-by-event fluctuation studies.

## Method

The author uses an optical Glauber model to generate volume distributions for Au+Au collisions, substituting volume by Poisson parameter λ distributions. The general formalism for volume-corrected cumulants from previous work (Xu 2016) is applied, assuming Poisson distributions for total charges and Skellam distributions for net protons in fixed volume. Cumulants are calculated from the conditional probability distribution of multiplicity q given reference multiplicity k, using the ratio of reference multiplicity distributions at different k values.

## Key numbers

- For total charges at non-central collisions, the scale variance ω = σ²/M varies from NBD expectations (ω ≈ 2) to Poisson expectations (ω = 1) as collisions become more central
- For net protons with default parameters (b₊ = 0.04, b₋ = 0.01), the approximate solutions at non-central collisions give ω = 1.697, Sσ = 0.679, and κσ² = 1.211
- The Glauber model fitting parameter is set to h = 400 and the external Gaussian fluctuation strength parameter to a = 0.01 as default
- The strength parameter a is varied to a = 0.10 and a = 0.50 to study effects of external volume fluctuations on net proton cumulants

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Xu 2016) on volume corrections from non-central to most-central heavy-ion collisions, building on the general formalism derived by Skokov (2012). The results show that the transition from NBD-like to Poisson-like multiplicity distributions at most-central collisions is due to "boundary effects" from the finite system volume, which suppresses volume corrections. However, the sensitivity of net proton cumulants to external volume fluctuations at most-central collisions implies that realistic volume distributions must be considered when interpreting experimental data from STAR (Adamczyk 2014) that show non-monotonic behavior in cumulant products at 0-5% centrality, which has been regarded as a potential signature of the QCD critical point.
