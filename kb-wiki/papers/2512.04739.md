# Static Fission Properties of Even-Even Actinides within the Warsaw Macroscopic-Microscopic Model Using Fourier-over-Spheroid Parameterization

> A systematic study of fission barrier heights and static properties of even-even actinide nuclei from Th to Cf has been performed within the Warsaw macroscopic-microscopic model using the five-dimensional Fourier-over-Spheroid (FoS) shape parameterization. The use of a large deformation grid, containing about $1.3\times10^{8}$ points for each nucleus, allows for a refined and numerically complete exploration of the potential energy landscape without dividing the configuration space into subregions or applying interpolation. Barrier heights, extracted via the Immersion Water Flow method, show good agreement with empirical evaluations (including the new IAEA RIPL-4 dataset) with mean deviations below 1 MeV. Special attention is given to the long-debated third, hyperdeformed minimum. For Th isotopes, a shallow but distinct third well appears, whereas it's absent in heavier actinides (U, Pu).

# Static Fission Properties of Even-Even Actinides within the Warsaw Macroscopic-Microscopic Model Using Fourier-over-Spheroid Parameterization

## Key claim

The Warsaw macroscopic-microscopic model using five-dimensional Fourier-over-Spheroid (FoS) shape parameterization reproduces empirical fission barrier heights for even-even actinides from Th to Cf with mean deviations below 1 MeV, but only when triaxiality is neglected; the authors explicitly note that non-axial effects are significant near the inner barrier and will be addressed in future work.

## Method

The authors constructed five-dimensional potential energy surfaces spanning the FoS parameter space (elongation c, reflection asymmetry a3, neck a4, and higher-order a5, a6) on a dense grid of approximately 128.7 million points per nucleus, with no interpolation between mesh points. The macroscopic energy was computed using the Yukawa-plus-exponential model, while microscopic shell corrections were obtained from a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with Strutinsky's method and BCS pairing. Inner and outer fission barrier heights were extracted using the Immersion Water Flow algorithm operating directly on the discrete deformation grid, and the resulting theoretical barriers were compared against three empirical datasets: Smirenkin (1993), IAEA RIPL-3 (2009), and the new IAEA RIPL-4 dataset.

## Key numbers

- Deformation grid contained approximately 1.3 × 10^8 points (128,702,886) for each nucleus, spanning c = 1.00 to 2.00 in steps of 0.01, a3 = 0.00 to 0.25 in steps of 0.01, a4 = -0.15 to 0.35 in steps of 0.01, a5 = -0.15 to 0.15 in steps of 0.01, a6 = -0.15 to 0.15 in steps of 0.01
- Mean deviations between calculated and empirical barrier heights are below 1 MeV
- For ²³⁰Th: calculated inner barrier 4.18 MeV (empirical 6.10 MeV from Smirenkin), outer barrier 6.05 MeV (empirical 6.10 MeV)
- For ²³⁶U: calculated inner barrier 5.39 MeV (empirical 5.60 MeV from Smirenkin), outer barrier 5.65 MeV (empirical 5.60 MeV)
- For ²⁴⁰Pu: calculated inner barrier 6.43 MeV (empirical 5.80 MeV from Smirenkin), outer barrier 5.17 MeV (empirical 5.30 MeV)
- For ²⁴⁶Cm: calculated inner barrier 7.78 MeV (empirical 6.00 MeV from Smirenkin), outer barrier 5.35 MeV (empirical 4.70 MeV)
- For ²⁵²Cf: calculated inner barrier 8.05 MeV (empirical 5.30 MeV from Smirenkin), outer barrier 4.94 MeV (empirical 3.50 MeV)
- Ground-state mass excess for ²³⁶U: calculated 42.55 MeV, experimental 42.45 MeV (AME2020)

## Context

This work extends the Warsaw macroscopic-microscopic model previously validated for heavy and superheavy nuclei by Jachimowicz et al. (2021) to a systematic study of even-even actinides from Th to Cf, using the Fourier-over-Spheroid parameterization developed by Pomorski et al. (2023) as an alternative to the traditional multipole expansion (beta parameterization). The study provides the first comprehensive application of the five-dimensional FoS parameterization to fission barrier calculations across this mass region, and enables future incorporation of triaxiality effects, which the authors identify as significant near the inner barrier and plan to address in subsequent work. The results are compared against empirical barrier sets from Smirenkin (1993), Capote et al. (2009) for RIPL-3, and the new unpublished RIPL-4 dataset, with the calculated barriers showing systematic deviations that increase for heavier actinides, particularly for inner barriers in Pu, Cm, and Cf isotopes.
