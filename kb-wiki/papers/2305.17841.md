# Bottomonium suppression at RHIC and LHC in an open quantum system approach

> We present potential non-relativistic quantum chromodynamics (pNRQCD) predictions for bottomonium suppression in sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV, 2.76 TeV, and 5.02 TeV heavy-ion collisions using an open quantum systems (OQS) description of the reduced heavy-quark anti-quark density matrix. Compared to prior OQS+pNRQCD studies we include the rapidity dependence of bottomonium production and evolution, allowing for a fully 3-dimensional description of bottomonium trajectories in the quark-gluon plasma. The underlying formalism used to compute the ground and excited state survival probabilities is based on a Lindblad equation that is accurate to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the binding energy over temperature. For the background evolution, we make use of a 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics code which reproduces soft hadron observables at all three collision energies. We find good agreement between NLO OQS+pNRQCD predictions and data taken at LHC energies, however, at RHIC energies, there is tension with recent bottomonium suppression measurements by the STAR collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors find that including the full 3D rapidity dependence of bottomonium trajectories improves agreement with experimental data at LHC energies (5.02 TeV and 2.76 TeV), but at RHIC energy (200 GeV) there is tension with STAR collaboration measurements, with the NLO OQS+pNRQCD framework underestimating Upsilon(1S) suppression and slightly overpredicting Upsilon(2S) suppression.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the NLO Lindblad equation for the reduced heavy-quarkonium density matrix using the quantum trajectories method on a 1D lattice with 2048 points and box size 40 GeV^-1, coupling the evolution to a 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics background. Bottomonium trajectories were sampled with Monte Carlo methods including initial transverse positions from binary collision overlap profiles, momentum rapidity from a Gaussian width of 2.3, and transverse momenta from a 1/E_T^4 distribution, then converted to Milne coordinates for coupling to the hydrodynamic evolution. Late-time excited state feed down was accounted for using a feed down matrix with branching fractions from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperatures for hydrodynamics: 630 MeV at 5.02 TeV, 600 MeV at 2.76 TeV, and 455 MeV at 200 GeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratios: 0.179 at 5.02 TeV, 0.159 at 2.76 TeV, and 0.159 at 200 GeV
- Heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient varied: hat-kappa in {3,4} at LHC energies and hat-kappa in {4,5} at RHIC energy; hat-gamma fixed to 0
- Medium initialization time tau_med = 0.6 fm; hydrodynamic initialization at tau = 0.25 fm/c
- Lattice parameters: 2048 points, box size L = 40 GeV^-1, temporal step size 0.001 GeV^-1
- Sampled approximately 200k physical trajectories at 5.02 TeV, 275k at 2.76 TeV, and 500k at 200 GeV, with 40 quantum trajectories per physical trajectory
- Rapidity width for bottomonium production: 2.3
- Integrated pp cross sections at 200 GeV: Upsilon(1S) = 2.35 nb, Upsilon(2S) = 0.775 nb, Upsilon(3S) = 0.277 nb
- Integrated pp cross sections at 5.02 TeV: Upsilon(1S) = 57.6 nb, Upsilon(2S) = 19 nb, Upsilon(3S) = 6.8 nb

## Context

This work extends the NLO OQS+pNRQCD framework previously applied at leading order by Brambilla et al. (2020, 2021) and at NLO by Brambilla et al. (2022, 2023) by including the rapidity dependence of bottomonium production and evolution, enabling a fully 3-dimensional description of bottomonium trajectories in the quark-gluon plasma. The approach uses the same Lindblad equation formalism derived by Brambilla et al. (2022) and the same quantum trajectories method as prior works, but now couples to 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics rather than assuming zero rapidity. The results enable future comparisons with forthcoming sPHENIX data at RHIC and further investigation of cold nuclear matter effects at large rapidity.
