# Shape coexistence and neutron skin thickness of Pb isotopes by the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> We investigate ground states properties of Pb isotopes located between neutron and proton drip-lines estimated by two-neutron (two-proton) separation energies and Fermi energies within the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). First, we report some candidates of nuclear shape coexistence in the isotope chain. They are accessed by calculating total energy as a function of the deformation parameter $β_2$, and for the coexistence candidates we take a couple of the deformation region bringing about minima of the energy within energy difference $ΔE < $ 1 MeV. Second, the Pb isotopes near neutron drip-lines are also investigated and compared to the results by other nuclear mass models. We find out eleven neutron emitters, $^{278 - 296, 300}$Pb, giving rise to the Pb peninsular near the neutron drip-line. Finally, by exploiting the neutron and proton density we deduce the neutron skin thickness (NST) of the Pb isotopes and compare to the available experimental data. The recent data regarding the shape coexistence of $^{184,186,188}$Pb and the NST of $^{208}$Pb are shown to be well matched with the present results.

## Key claim
The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) predicts shape coexistence in \(^{184,186,188}\)Pb when the total binding energy minima for oblate, spherical, and prolate deformations lie within \(\Delta E < 1\) MeV, and reproduces the PREX II neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb within the experimental confidence level, but the claim is conditional on treating \(N=100\) as a sub-magic shell and on the specific pairing strength and basis truncation choices.

## Method
The authors solved the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis using the PC-PK1 density functional, with a density-dependent zero-range pairing force (\(V_0 = -325.0\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(\rho_{sat}=0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\), pairing window 100 MeV). They calculated total binding energy as a function of the quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2\) for \(^{172-302}\)Pb isotopes, extracted ground-state minima, and computed neutron and proton density distributions to obtain neutron skin thickness (NST). The Legendre expansion truncation was \(\lambda_{max}=8\), with energy cutoff \(E_{cut}^+ = 300\) MeV and angular momentum cutoff \(J_{max} = (23/2)\hbar\).

## Key numbers
- NST of \(^{208}\)Pb: \(R_n - R_p = 0.257\) fm (DRHBc), compared to PREX II experimental value \(0.283 \pm 0.071\) fm.
- Eleven neutron emitters identified: \(^{278-296,300}\)Pb, forming a Pb peninsula near the neutron drip-line.
- Spherical nuclei found around neutron numbers \(N=100\), \(126\), and \(184\); \(N=100\) proposed as a sub-magic shell.
- Energy difference for shape coexistence candidates: \(\Delta E < 1\) MeV for \(^{184,186,188}\)Pb.
- Pairing strength: \(V_0 = -325.0\) MeV fm\(^3\); saturation density \(\rho_{sat} = 0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Basis cutoffs: \(E_{cut}^+ = 300\) MeV, \(J_{max} = (23/2)\hbar\), \(\lambda_{max} = 8\).

## Context
This work extends the DRHBc framework, previously applied to deformed halo nuclei (Zhou 2010, Zhou 2012) and heavy nuclei mass tables (Pan 2021, Kaiyuan 2021), to the full Pb isotope chain from proton to neutron drip-lines. It confirms shape coexistence in \(^{184,186}\)Pb previously discussed by Andreyev (2000) and Duguet (2003) via rotational band data, and adds \(^{188}\)Pb as a candidate suggested by the interacting boson model (Hell 2005). The NST result for \(^{208}\)Pb agrees with PREX II (Adhikari 2021) and enables future constraints on nuclear symmetry energy and neutron star properties, as argued by Danielewicz (2003) and others. The predicted neutron drip-line extends beyond earlier RCHB (Meng 1996), FRDM (Moller 2016), and KTUY (Koura 2005) models due to inclusion of deformation and continuum.
