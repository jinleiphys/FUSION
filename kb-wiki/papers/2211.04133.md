# Study of structure and radii for $^{20-31}$Na isotopes using microscopic interactions

> In this work, Na isotopes with mass varying from $A=$ 20 to 31 have been studied using DJ16A, JISP16 and N3LO microscopic effective interactions in the $sd$-shell. These effective interactions are derived for $sd$-shell using no-core shell model wavefunctions and a unitary transformation method. We have also performed calculation with IMSRG effective interactions targeted for a particular nucleus. The studies include a detailed analysis of ground state binding energy, low-lying spectra, and electromagnetic properties such as reduced electric quadrupole transition strengths, quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments of the Na chain. The results obtained from these microscopic effective interactions were compared with the experimental data as well as with the results of phenomenological interaction USDB. The charge radii of Na isotopes are evaluated using shell model harmonic oscillator wave functions. In addition to charge radii, matter radii and neutron skin thickness in the Na chain are discussed with a focus on neutron-deficient Na isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that microscopic effective interactions (DJ16A, JISP16, N3LO, and IMSRG) can reproduce ground-state binding energies, low-lying spectra, and electromagnetic properties of Na isotopes across the sd-shell, but the agreement is conditional on mass region: the interactions perform well for lighter isotopes (A=20-23) and near the island of inversion (A=29-31), while they struggle for medium-mass isotopes (A=24-28) where correlation energy increases.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using the KSHELL code for Na isotopes from A=20 to 31, employing four microscopic effective interactions (DJ16A, JISP16, N3LO) derived from no-core shell model wavefunctions via the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation, plus a nucleus-dependent IMSRG interaction derived from chiral N3LO potentials. They compared results with the phenomenological USDB interaction and experimental data, calculating ground-state binding energies, energy spectra, reduced electric quadrupole transition strengths (B(E2)), quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments, and charge radii evaluated using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions with oscillator length parameters taken from both hbar-omega and experimental charge radii data.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energies: DJ16A interaction slightly underbinds neutron-rich Na isotopes compared to other microscopic interactions, while JISP16 and N3LO overbind as valence nucleon number increases
- In 20Na, the 3+_1 first excited state from DJ16A lies only 31 keV from the experimental value
- In 22Na, the 1+ state appears at 162 keV in DJ16A spectra versus the experimental 583 keV first excited state
- In 28Na, the 1+ state energy shift relative to ground state is 77 keV with DJ16A, compared to 117 keV with USDB
- In 29Na, the 1/2+ second excited state appears at 1228 keV with N3LO, close to the experimental 1249 keV
- In 30Na, the 1+_1 state lies at 157 keV above ground state with N3LO, nearly matching the experimental 150 keV
- In 31Na, the 5/2+ first excited state appears at 332 keV with DJ16A, reasonably agreeing with the experimental 375 keV
- B(E2) for 21Na 5/2_1+ to 3/2_1+ transition: DJ16A gives 119.2 e^2fm^4, experimental is 134(10) e^2fm^4
- B(E2) for 22Na 1+_2 to 3+_1 transition: N3LO gives 9.4 e^2fm^4, experimental is 9.5(32) e^2fm^4
- B(E2) for 23Na 5/2_1+ to 3/2_1+ transition: all interactions give 133-140 e^2fm^4, experimental is 106(4) e^2fm^4

## Context

This work extends the study of microscopic effective interactions previously developed for the sd-shell using NCSM on 18F by the same group (eff_int_method(2015), sdshell_int), and applies them systematically to the full Na isotopic chain for the first time. The results are compared with the phenomenological USDB interaction (usdb_interaction) and experimental data from NNDC and specific measurements (exp_23Na, exp_26-27Na, exp_27-29Na). The paper identifies that NNN forces are important for describing 22Na spectra, as noted in 22Na_3N, and suggests that future work should include complete charge dependency in the effective interactions and extend calculations to the sd-pf model space for neutron-rich isotopes, following the approach of imsrg_sd_pf for Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes in the island of inversion region.
