# Unifying Nucleon and Quark Dynamics at Finite Baryon Number Density

> We present a model of baryonic matter which contains free constituent quarks in addition to bound constituent quarks in nucleons. In addition to the common linear sigma-model we include the exchange of vector-mesons. The percentage of free quarks increases with baryon density but the nucleons resist a restoration of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model of baryonic matter that includes both free constituent quarks and bound constituent quarks in nucleons, claiming that the percentage of free quarks increases with baryon density but that nucleons resist a restoration of chiral symmetry. The hedge is that this resistance occurs because the repulsive vector-meson (omega) field suppresses the chiral symmetric phase, making the transition to a quark phase continuous rather than a sharp phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct a unified Lagrangian based on a linear sigma-model with additional vector-meson (omega) exchange, coupling both nucleon and quark fields to the same scalar sigma and pion fields. In the mean-field approximation, they treat nucleons and quarks as two independent Fermi gases with Fermi momenta linked by the conserved baryon density and a variable quark concentration x, then minimize the energy density to find the ground state at each density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_S = 1.14 rho_0, where rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3} (normal nuclear matter density)
- Binding energy at saturation: E_B = 16 MeV per baryon
- Dirac mass of the nucleon at saturation: m_D* = g_sn * bar_sigma_s = 581 MeV
- Relativistically equivalent effective mass ratio: m_e* / m_n = 0.71, compared to experimental value m* / m_n ≈ 0.74
- Compression modulus: K_infinity = 823 MeV (much larger than empirical value of 210 MeV)
- Quark concentration at saturation: x_s = 50%
- Average quark concentration in Pb-208: ⟨x⟩ ≈ 40%; in O-16: ⟨x⟩ < 30%
- Residue factor for the 3s-orbital in lead: z = 0.67, compared to experimental value z = 0.64 ± 0.06 from (e, e'p) experiments
- Omega mean-field at saturation: g_vn * bar_omega_s = 263 MeV
- Asymptotic quark concentration as rho_B → infinity: x_infinity ≈ 0.9
- Nucleon mass in model: m_n = 901 MeV (less than 938 MeV because no explicit symmetry breaking term included)
- Sigma mass: m_sigma = 720 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Relativistic Quantum Hadron Dynamics (QHD) approach of Walecka (1974) by incorporating quark degrees of freedom, and builds on the scaling ideas of Brown and Rho (1991) who suggested hadron masses scale with the sigma field. The model provides a continuous interpolation between low-density nucleon matter and high-density quark matter, and offers a simple explanation for experimentally measured residue factors from (e, e'p) experiments on lead, which had previously been attributed to ground state correlations by Hasse (1990). The approach enables future studies of how quark substructure affects nuclear matter properties before any phase transition occurs.
