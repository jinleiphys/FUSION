# Direct Test of the Scalar-Vector Lorentz Structure of the Nucleon- and Antinucleon-Nucleus Potential

> Quantum Hadrodynamics in mean field approximation describes the effective nucleon-nucleus potential (about -50 MeV deep) as resulting from a strong repulsive vector (about 400 MeV) and a strong attractive scalar (about -450 MeV) contribution. This scalar-vector Lorentz structure implies a significant lowering of the threshold for $p\bar{p}$ photoproduction on a nucleus by about 850 MeV as compared to the free case since charge conjugation reverses the sign of the vector potential contribution in the equation of motion for the $\bar{p}$ states. It also implies a certain size of the photon induced $p\bar{p}$ pair creation cross section near threshold which is calculated for a target nucleus $^{208}$Pb. We also indicate a measurable second signature of the $p\bar{p}$ photoproduction process by estimating the increased cross section for emission of charged pions as a consequence of $\bar{p}$ annihilation within the nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the scalar-vector Lorentz structure of the nucleon-nucleus potential in Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) mean field approximation can be directly tested by measuring the near-threshold photoproduction of proton-antiproton pairs on a nucleus, specifically 208Pb, where the threshold energy is predicted to be lowered by about 850 MeV compared to the free case due to the reversal of the vector potential sign for antinucleons.

## Method

The authors calculate the S-matrix element for gamma-induced p-pbar pair creation in first-order perturbation theory, using square-well approximations for the scalar and vector mean field potentials (S=400 MeV, V=450 MeV) adapted to 208Pb. The proton is treated as a plane wave continuum state while the antiproton is produced in a bound negative-energy state of the Dirac equation with scalar-vector potentials. The differential and total inclusive cross sections are computed by summing over all energetically possible final states, including contributions from the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton via a corrected transition current.

## Key numbers

- The effective nucleon-nucleus potential depth is about -50 MeV, resulting from a repulsive vector contribution of about 400 MeV and an attractive scalar contribution of about -450 MeV
- The threshold for p-pbar photoproduction on a nucleus is lowered by about 850 MeV compared to the free case (free threshold approximately 1880 MeV = 2M)
- For 208Pb, the nuclear radius is taken as r0 = 7.2 fm (using r0 ≈ 1.2 × A^(1/3) fm)
- The inclusive cross section for p-pbar pair creation at 70 MeV above threshold is 10^(-3) to 10^(-2) mb
- The background cross section for conventional gamma-induced proton emission at k=1 GeV is estimated at 50 mb from BUU transport model calculations
- The background cross section for conventional charged pion emission at k=1 GeV is estimated at 15 mb
- The average multiplicity of charged pions from pbar annihilation in 208Pb is 2.5, based on INC calculations by Botvina et al.
- To isolate the p-pbar signal from statistical fluctuations, at least 4 × 10^8 events of the inclusive reaction gamma + 208Pb → p + X would be required, corresponding to about 30 days of measurement time with a photon flux of 10^5 s^(-1) per 10 MeV bin
- For charged pion detection, at least 3.6 × 10^7 events would be needed, corresponding to about 9 days of measurement time

## Context

This work extends the mean field approximation of Quantum Hadrodynamics (Walecka Model, 1974) to test whether the scalar-vector Lorentz structure derived for nucleon-nucleus potentials also applies to antinucleons inside nuclei. The authors note that Teis et al. question the simple charge conjugation picture for constructing the antinucleon-nucleus potential due to lack of unitarity and absence of Fock terms, while Mishustin et al. and Schaffner et al. argue for N-Nbar pair creation in scalar-vector mean fields in heavy ion collisions. The proposed experiment would test whether the mean field picture is sufficient or whether effects beyond the mean field approach must be included. The authors suggest that facilities such as CEBAF with the GLAS detector, GRAAL with the BGO detector, and ELSA with the SAPHIR detector could provide sufficient photon flux for the measurement.
