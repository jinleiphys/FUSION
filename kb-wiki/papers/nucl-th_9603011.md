# Lifetimes and Sizes from Two-Particle Correlation Functions

> We discuss the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrization of the two-particle correlation function for azimuthally symmetric expanding sources. We derive model-independent expressions for the YKP fit parameters and discuss their physical interpretation. We use them to evaluate the YKP fit parameters and their momentum dependence for a simple model for the emission function and propose new strategies for extracting the source lifetime. Longitudinal expansion of the source can be seen directly in the rapidity dependence of the Yano-Koonin velocity.

# Lifetimes and Sizes from Two-Particle Correlation Functions

## Key claim

The Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrization of the two-particle correlation function allows a more direct and statistically robust extraction of the source lifetime (duration of particle emission) than the standard HBT radius parametrization, provided the approximations relating the YKP parameter R0 to the time variance ⟨t̃²⟩ are justified, which holds best at low transverse pair momentum and for moderate transverse flow.

## Method

The authors derive model-independent expressions for the YKP fit parameters (R⊥, R∥, R0, and the Yano-Koonin velocity v) in terms of second-order space-time moments of the emission function S(x,K), using a Gaussian approximation for the source. They evaluate these parameters numerically for a specific model of a finite expanding thermalized source with boost-invariant longitudinal flow and linear transverse flow, computing pion correlation functions for a fixed set of source parameters (R=3 fm, τ0=3 fm/c, Δτ=1 fm/c, Δη=1.2, T=140 MeV). They compare the YKP parameters with the standard HBT radii and with the directly computed variances ⟨t̃²⟩ and ⟨z̃²⟩.

## Key numbers

- Source model parameters: R=3 fm (transverse Gaussian radius), τ0=3 fm/c (average freeze-out proper time), Δτ=1 fm/c (mean proper time duration of emission), Δη=1.2 (longitudinal extension), T=140 MeV (freeze-out temperature), mπ=139 MeV/c².
- For pion pairs with Y=0 and ηf=0 (no transverse flow), the approximation R0 ≈ √⟨t̃²⟩ is exact; for Y=3 and large transverse flow, R0 overestimates √⟨t̃²⟩ by up to 50% in the most unfavorable case shown.
- For transverse expansion rapidities ηf ≤ 0.3, the relative error on Δt from contamination of R0 is less than 25%.
- Asymptotically at large pair momentum, the effective lifetime √⟨t̃²⟩ approaches the model parameter Δτ = 1 fm/c.
- The Yano-Koonin velocity v shows a linear relationship with the pion pair rapidity Y, directly reflecting boost-invariant longitudinal expansion.

## Context

This work extends the Yano-Koonin parametrization (Yano and Koonin, 1978) and the Podgoretskii formulation (Podgoretskii, 1983) to azimuthally symmetric expanding sources, building on earlier studies of HBT radius parameters by Chapman, Scotto, and Heinz (1995) and Chapman, Nix, and Heinz (1995). The authors show that the YKP parameters are invariant under longitudinal boosts and provide a more intuitive characterization of source geometry and dynamics than the standard Cartesian HBT radii. The results enable experimental extraction of source lifetime from correlation data, addressing the difficulty that standard fits (e.g., from NA35 and NA44 collaborations) had shown no positive evidence for finite emission duration. The predicted linear dependence of YK velocity on pair rapidity was subsequently observed in Mg+Mg collisions at 4.4 A GeV/c by the GIBS collaboration (Anikina et al., 1995).
