# Observation of a hadronic interference effect in annihilation processes

> We present evidence for small oscillations we observe in electron-positron and proton-antiproton annihilation data, with a periodicity of 76+-2 MeV, independent of the beam energy. We discuss some possible scenarios to explain the phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper reports evidence for small oscillations in several electron-positron and proton-antiproton annihilation datasets, with a claimed constant periodicity of 76±2 MeV that is independent of beam energy and flavor, but the authors acknowledge that the effect is small, requires confirmation by experiments with finer binning, and that for some datasets (e.g., the BABAR data on Υ(2S)π⁺π⁻) the collaboration has a very different interpretation, making it questionable whether those data can be used in this context.

## Method

The authors performed fits to published experimental data from the BABAR, CDF, and CMD-2 Collaborations for the processes e⁺e⁻ → J/ψπ⁺π⁻, p̅p → J/ψπ⁺π⁻, e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻, and e⁺e⁻ → Υ(2S)π⁺π⁻, using a simple cosine function with argument linearly proportional to the invariant mass and amplitude proportional to the average magnitude of the signal (except for the CDF data, where the background magnitude in the resonance region was used). The periodicity was extracted from each dataset and then averaged to obtain a combined value.

## Key numbers

- Periodicity of 76±2 MeV, claimed to be independent of the specific annihilation process and flavor (n̅n, c̅c, b̅b).
- For e⁺e⁻ → J/ψπ⁺π⁻ (BABAR data around the Dₛ*D̅ₛ* threshold): periodicity of 74 MeV.
- For p̅p → J/ψπ⁺π⁻ (CDF data around the X(3872) resonance): periodicity of about 79 MeV, with an estimated uncertainty of 4–5 MeV.
- For e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ (CMD-2 data): periodicity of about 75 MeV, with an estimated uncertainty of some 2 MeV.
- For e⁺e⁻ → Υ(2S)π⁺π⁻ (BABAR data): periodicity of 73±3 MeV.
- Amplitude of oscillation: about 10% of the total signal for e⁺e⁻ annihilation, but just 1% for p̅p annihilation.
- The authors infer that full p̅p annihilation occurs in only 1 out of 10 events.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous observation of an interference effect in BABAR data (van Beveren and Rupp, 2009) and relates it to a flavor-independent quarkonium oscillation frequency of 190 MeV they had previously reported (van Beveren and Rupp, 2009). The observed 76 MeV periodicity is interpreted as possibly arising from interference between the quarkonium oscillation and gluon surface oscillations, with the latter yielding candidate frequencies of 342±4 MeV or 38±4 MeV. The paper discusses connections to lattice QCD glueball level spacings (Morningstar and Peardon, 2004; Liu and Chuan, 2001; Gregory et al., 2006), bag-model surface vibrations (DeGrand and Rebbi, 1978; Mulders et al., 1984; Fiebig, 1985), and earlier observations of oscillations in hadronic scattering (Pacetti, 2001; Gauron, Nicolescu, and Selyugin, 1997; Barshay and Heiliger, 1995). The authors suggest that if confirmed, this would represent a second constant of strong interactions for quarkonia, independent of flavor.
