# Charmed and bottomed hadronic cross sections from a statistical model

> In this work, we extended our statistical model with charmed and bottomed hadrons, and fit the quark creational probabilities for the heavy quarks, using low energy inclusive charmonium and bottomonium data. With the finalized fit for all the relevant types of quarks (up, down, strange, charm, bottom) at the energy range from a few GeV up to a few tens of GeV's, the model is now considered complete. Some examples are also given for proton-proton, pion-proton, and proton-antiproton collisions with charmonium, bottomonium, and open charm hadrons in the final state.

## Key claim

The authors extend their statistical bootstrap model to include charmed and bottomed hadrons, fitting the charm and bottom quark creational probabilities to low-energy inclusive charmonium and bottomonium data. The model is now considered complete for non-exotic states in the energy range from a few GeV up to a few tens of GeV, but the fit assumes a simple linear energy dependence for the heavy quark probabilities (P_c = a_c E, P_b = a_b E) rather than a constant value, and the model's validity is limited to energies far below the top quark mass.

## Method

The model assumes a fireball is formed during a collision, which then hadronizes into final-state particles via a statistical bootstrap approach. The hadronization probability combines a fireball decay scheme, a density of states, two- and three-body phase spaces (with Breit-Wigner factors for resonances), and a quark combinatorial factor based on a multinomial distribution of quark-antiquark pairs. The charm and bottom quark creational probabilities were fitted using a least-squares method to inclusive J/Psi production data in pi- N and p p collisions (for charm) and Upsilon(1S) production in pi- p collisions (for bottom), assuming a linear energy dependence P_c,b = a_c,b E.

## Key numbers

*   Fitted charm quark slope parameter: a_c = 8.5 x 10^-4 GeV^-1.
*   Fitted bottom quark slope parameter: a_b = 1.05 x 10^-5 GeV^-1.
*   Hadronization temperature: T_0 = 160 MeV.
*   Cutoff parameter: E_0 = 500 MeV.
*   Fixed quark creational probabilities: P_u = 0.425, P_d = 0.425, P_s = 0.15.
*   For the ratio R_Psi(2S) of direct Psi(3686) to total J/Psi production in pN collisions at sqrt(s) = 23.7 GeV: measured 5.5 +/- 1.6%, model 6.2 +/- 2.4%.
*   For the ratio R_chi_c1+chi_c2 of direct chi_c1, chi_c2 to total J/Psi production in pi N collisions at sqrt(s) = 23.74 GeV: measured 34 +/- 4%, model 36.4 +/- 7.2%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous statistical model (Balassa and Wolf, 2020) which was developed for strange hadrons, by adding charmed and bottomed hadrons and fitting the heavy quark creational probabilities. The model provides estimates for inclusive cross sections of charmonium, bottomonium, and open charm hadrons in proton-proton, pion-proton, and proton-antiproton collisions, which are needed as inputs for transport calculations in heavy ion collisions. The results enable further studies of onium state propagation in dense nuclear matter and the extraction of the gluon condensate from mass shift measurements, and also highlight the need for better knowledge of branching fractions for excited open charm and bottom hadrons.
