# A new class of hybrid EoS with multiple critical endpoints for simulations of supernovae, neutron stars and their mergers

> We introduce a family of equations of state (EoS) for hybrid neutron star (NS) matter that is obtained by a two-zone parabolic interpolation between a soft hadronic EoS at low densities and a stiff quark matter EoS with color superconductivity at high densities within a finite region of baryonic chemical potentials $μ_B^h < μ_B < μ_B^q$. We consider two scenarios corresponding to a cross-over and a strong first-order transition between quark and hadron phases considered at finite and zero temperatures. This allows us to analyze the effects of finite entropy on the EoS and mass-radius relation of NS. We demonstrate that the formation of a color superconducting state of quark matter drives the evolution of matter in supernovae explosions under the condition of entropy conservation to higher temperatures than in the case of deconfinement to normal quark matter. Within the presented hybrid EoS scenario, regions of the QCD phase diagram may be accessible to supernovae and NS mergers that can be reached also in terrestrial experiments with relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a family of hybrid equations of state for neutron star matter that can produce a QCD phase diagram with two critical endpoints (one at high temperature and one at low temperature), but only if the density jump parameter \(n^*\) is set to a nonzero value (e.g., \(0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)) and the critical temperatures are assigned specific values (\(T_{\text{cep1}}=90\ \text{MeV}\), \(T_{\text{cep2}}=15\ \text{MeV}\)).

## Method
The authors construct hybrid equations of state by merging a hadronic EoS (DD2npY-T, which includes hyperons) with a quark matter EoS from a chiral density functional model that includes color superconductivity (2SC phase). They use a two-zone parabolic interpolation scheme (TZIS) between the hadronic and quark phases, which generalizes earlier zero-temperature work to finite temperatures and finite electric charge fractions. The density jump at the merging point is parameterized as a function of temperature to model either a crossover (\(\Delta n_B=0\)) or a first-order transition (\(\Delta n_B>0\)) between two critical endpoint temperatures.

## Key numbers
- The quark matter squared speed of sound varies only by 5% across the density range from two to ten normal nuclear densities, with values \(c_S^2 = 0.57 - 0.60\).
- The two critical endpoint temperatures are assigned as \(T_{\text{cep1}} = 90\ \text{MeV}\) and \(T_{\text{cep2}} = 15\ \text{MeV}\).
- The density jump amplitude parameter is set to \(n^* = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) for the first-order transition scenario, or \(n^* = 0\) for the crossover scenario.
- The critical exponents are fixed to \(\beta_1 = \beta_2 = 0.3265\), corresponding to the 3D Ising universality class.
- The mixed phase boundary from the hadron side uses \(T_0 = 140\ \text{MeV}\) and \(x = 0.01\).
- The pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD for chiral symmetry restoration is cited as \(156.5 \pm 1.5\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the two-zone interpolation scheme previously developed by Ayriyan et al. (2021) for zero temperature to finite temperatures and finite isospin densities, enabling studies of supernova explosions and binary neutron star mergers. It builds on the observation by Hatsuda (2006) and Abuki (2010) that competition between chiral symmetry breaking and diquark condensation can produce a second critical endpoint at low temperatures. The resulting hybrid EoS family is designed to be used in simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, potentially allowing access to regions of the QCD phase diagram that overlap with those probed by heavy-ion collision experiments.
