# Dynamical screening in a quark gluon plasma

> We calculate the screening potential of a fast parton moving through a quark-gluon plasma in the framework of semi-classical transport theory. We found an anisotropic potential showing a minimum in the direction of the parton velocity. Possible consequences of this potential on binary states in a quark-gluon plasma are discussed.

## Key claim
The screening potential of a fast parton moving through a quark-gluon plasma, calculated using semi-classical transport theory, is strongly anisotropic and exhibits a minimum in the direction of the parton velocity, which could give rise to bound states such as diquarks if not destroyed by thermal fluctuations.

## Method
The authors solved the linearized Vlasov and Poisson equations numerically to obtain the screening potential of a moving color charge, using the longitudinal dielectric function from the high-temperature limit of the polarization tensor (hard thermal loop approximation). The potential was evaluated for two cases: the position vector parallel to the velocity and perpendicular to the velocity, for parton velocities between 0 and 1.

## Key numbers
- For a parton with velocity v=0.8, the minimum in the screening potential parallel to the velocity occurs at about 1.5 fm with a depth of about 8 MeV.
- The calculations used a strong fine structure constant α_s = 0.3, temperature T = 0.25 GeV, and number of quark flavors n_f = 2.
- The Debye mass is given by m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 (1 + n_f/6), where g is the strong coupling constant.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of screening potentials in the quark-gluon plasma that assumed the test charge was at rest, such as the Debye-Hückel (Yukawa) potential. It builds on the work of Chu and Matsui, who used the Vlasov equation to investigate dynamic Debye screening for a heavy quark-antiquark pair and found anisotropic screening, but did not report the minimum. The results also relate to claims by Shuryak of colored bound states (diquarks) above the critical temperature from lattice data. The findings enable further investigation of moving J/ψ meson binding and suppression in heavy-ion collisions using hydrodynamical models or event generators.
