# Consistency of Perfect Fluidity and Jet Quenching in semi-Quark-Gluon Monopole Plasmas

> We utilize a new framework, CUJET3.0, to deduce the energy and temperature dependence of jet transport parameter, $\hat{q}(E>10\; {\rm GeV},T)$, from a combined analysis of available data on nuclear modification factor and azimuthal asymmetries from RHIC/BNL and LHC/CERN on high energy nuclear collisions. Extending a previous perturbative-QCD based jet energy loss model (known as CUJET2.0) with (2+1)D viscous hydrodynamic bulk evolution, this new framework includes three novel features of nonperturbative physics origin: (1) the Polyakov loop suppression of color-electric scattering (aka "semi-QGP" of Pisarski et al) and (2) the enhancement of jet scattering due to emergent magnetic monopoles near $T_c$ (aka "magnetic scenario" of Liao and Shuryak) and (3) thermodynamic properties constrained by lattice QCD data. CUJET3.0 reduces to v2.0 at high temperatures $T > 400$ MeV, but greatly enhances $\hat{q}$ near the QCD deconfinement transition temperature range. This enhancement accounts well for the observed elliptic harmonics of jets with $p_T>10$ GeV. Extrapolating our data-constrained $\hat{q}$ down to thermal energy scales, $E \sim 2$ GeV, we find for the first time a remarkable consistency between high energy jet quenching and bulk perfect fluidity with $η/s\sim T^3/\hat{q} \sim 0.1$ near $T_c$.

# Consistency of Perfect Fluidity and Jet Quenching in semi-Quark-Gluon Monopole Plasmas

## Key claim

The CUJET3.0 framework demonstrates that the combination of Polyakov loop suppression of color-electric degrees of freedom (semi-QGP) and enhanced jet scattering from emergent magnetic monopoles near T_c can simultaneously describe both high-p_T jet quenching and bulk perfect fluidity with η/s ∼ T^3/hat{q} ∼ 0.1 near T_c, but only when the jet transport parameter is extrapolated down to thermal energy scales E ∼ 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors extended the pQCD-based CUJET2.0 jet energy loss model by incorporating three nonperturbative features constrained by lattice QCD data: (1) Polyakov loop suppression of color-electric scattering (semi-QGP), (2) enhancement of jet scattering due to emergent magnetic monopoles near T_c, and (3) lattice-QCD-constrained thermodynamic properties. The framework uses (2+1)D viscous hydrodynamic bulk evolution (VISH2+1) with MC-Glauber initial conditions and computes the jet transport parameter hat{q}(E,T) from a generalized scattering rate that includes both electric (quark, gluon) and magnetic (monopole) quasiparticle contributions, with the Polyakov loop L(T) parameterized from lattice data to determine the fraction of electric versus magnetic degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The jet transport parameter hat{q}/T^3 shows a prominent peak near T_c, with CUJET3.0 using parameters (α_c, c_m) = (0.95, 0.3)
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s ∼ 0.1 near T_c, derived from the extrapolation hat{q}(E ∼ 3T, T)
- CUJET3.0 reproduces R_AA(p_T = 12.5 GeV) ≈ 0.3 at LHC for charged hadrons in Pb+Pb √s_NN = 2.76 TeV semi-peripheral collisions
- The Polyakov loop parameterization uses L(T) = [1/2 + 1/2 Tanh[7.69(T − 0.0726)]]^10 with T_c = 160 MeV
- CUJET3.0 reduces to CUJET2.0 at high temperatures T > 400 MeV
- The total number density ρ(T) = ξ p(T)/T with ξ = 1.012 for N_c = 3, N_f = 2.5 Stefan-Boltzmann gas

## Context

This work extends the pQCD-based CUJET2.0 model (Xu, 2014) by incorporating the semi-QGP concept of Pisarski et al. (2008-2013) and the magnetic scenario of Liao and Shuryak (2006-2012), both constrained by lattice QCD data from HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations. It resolves the long-standing inconsistency where pQCD-based models that account for high-energy jet quenching predicted η/s values too large when extrapolated to thermal scales, while strong-coupling models (AdS/CFT, SYM) that give η/s ∼ 1/4π over-predicted high-energy jet quenching. The framework enables a unified microscopic description of both hard and soft QGP transport properties and provides predictions for D and B meson R_AA and v_2 at LHC that can be tested with future measurements.
