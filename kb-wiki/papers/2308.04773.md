# Meson cloud contributions to the Dalitz decays of decuplet to octet baryons

> We study the role of the meson cloud on the electromagnetic transitions from decuplet ($B'$) to octet ($B$) baryons in terms of the squared four-momentum transfer $q^2$. In the quark model framework, the meson cloud dressing of the quark cores gives important contributions to the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ transition form factors. In the present work, we estimate the meson cloud contributions of all decuplet to octet baryon transitions ($γ^\ast B \to B'$ or $B' \to γ^\ast B$). Models that combine valence quark effects with pion and kaon cloud dressing provide a fair description of the radiative decays of decuplet to octet baryons, namely the $Σ^0(1385) \to γΛ(1116)$ and $Σ^+(1385) \to γΣ^+ (1193)$ decays. Previous studies indicated the relevance of the pion cloud effects on the $B^\prime \to γ^\ast B$ transition, but also suggested that the kaon cloud contributions may be important in the timelike region. We combine then the contributions of the bare core, estimated by a covariant quark model, with $q^2$-dependent contributions of pion and kaon clouds. We use the framework to calculate the Dalitz decay rates and the Dalitz decay widths of decuplet baryons in octet baryons with di-electrons ($B' \to e^+ e^- B$) or di-muons ($B' \to μ^+ μ^- B$). We conclude, based on the magnitude of our results, that most estimates of the $B' \to e^+ e^- B$ Dalitz decay widths may be tested at HADES and PANDA (GSI) in a near future. We discuss also the possibility of measuring the $Δ(1232) \to μ^+ μ^- N$ and $Σ^0 (1385) \to μ^+ μ^- Λ(1116)$ decay widths in some facilities, based on the estimated branching ratios.

## Key claim

The meson cloud contributions, including both pion and kaon clouds, increase the Dalitz decay widths of decuplet baryons into octet baryons by 30%–70% depending on the specific transition, with the kaon cloud being particularly important for decays involving strange baryons such as the Σ⁰(1385) → e⁺e⁻Λ(1116) decay where the width increases by about 27% compared to models with only pion cloud effects.

## Method

The authors combined a covariant spectator quark model for the valence quark core with q²-dependent pion and kaon cloud contributions, extending previous calculations that omitted the kaon cloud. The magnetic dipole form factor G_M was decomposed into bare core and meson cloud components, with the dominance of the magnetic dipole form factor assumed for all decuplet-to-octet transitions. The transition form factors were analytically extended to the timelike region and used to compute Dalitz decay rates and widths for both di-electron (B′ → e⁺e⁻B) and di-muon (B′ → μ⁺μ⁻B) decays.

## Key numbers

- Kaon cloud contributions increase the Σ(1385) to Σ(1193) Dalitz decays by about 30%–40%
- Kaon cloud contributions increase the Ξ(1530) to Ξ(1318) Dalitz decays by about 50%–70%
- The Σ⁰(1385) → e⁺e⁻Λ(1116) Dalitz decay width increases by about 27% when kaon cloud effects are included
- The Dalitz decay integration range for di-electrons is 4m_e² ≤ q² ≤ (M_B′ − M_B)², where m_e is the electron mass
- The di-muon decay width is smaller than the di-electron decay width due to the reduced integration interval [2μ, W−M_B] and the weight function C_μ ≤ 1, where μ is the muon mass

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Ramalho and Tsushima (DecupletDalitz) that included only pion cloud effects, motivated by earlier studies (DecupletDecays) showing that kaon cloud effects provide significant contributions to radiative decays of hyperons such as Σ⁰(1385) → γΛ(1116) and Ξ(1530) → γΞ(1318). The results enable experimental tests at HADES and PANDA (GSI) for di-electron decays, and at BESIII and LHCb for di-muon decays of the Δ(1232) → μ⁺μ⁻N and Σ⁰(1385) → μ⁺μ⁻Λ(1116) transitions based on estimated branching ratios.
