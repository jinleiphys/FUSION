# Kaon photoproduction on the nucleon: Contributions of kaon-hyperon final states to the magnetic moment of the nucleon

> By using the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule and an isobaric model of kaon photoproduction, we calculate contributions of kaon-hyperon final states to the magnetic moment of the proton and the neutron. We find that the contributions are small. The approximation of sigma_{TT'} by sigma_{T} clearly overestimates the value of the GDH integral. We find a smaller upper bound for the contributions of kaon-hyperon final states to the proton's anomalous magnetic moment in kaon photoproduction, and a positive contribution for the square of the neutron's magnetic moment.

# Kaon photoproduction on the nucleon: Contributions of kaon-hyperon final states to the magnetic moment of the nucleon

## Key claim

Using the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule and an isobaric model of kaon photoproduction, the contributions of kaon-hyperon final states to the magnetic moment of the proton and neutron are small, but the approximation of the spin-dependent cross section sigma_{TT'} by the unpolarized total cross section sigma_T clearly overestimates the GDH integral value. The calculation yields a smaller upper bound for the proton's anomalous magnetic moment contribution than previous work by Hammer, Drechsel, and Mart (1997), and a positive contribution for the square of the neutron's magnetic moment.

## Method

The authors used the GDH sum rule to relate the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon to the difference of polarized total photoabsorption cross sections, calculating both the exact expression using sigma_{TT'} (the spin-dependent cross section) and the approximate expression using sigma_T (the unpolarized total cross section). They employed a modern isobaric model with tree-level amplitudes that includes s-, u-, and t-channel background terms plus a contact term for gauge invariance, incorporating three nucleon resonances (S11(1650), P11(1710), P13(1720)) for K Lambda production and two Delta resonances (S31(1900), P31(1910)) for K Sigma production, fitted to recent SAPHIR data. The integrals were evaluated up to a cutoff energy of approximately 2 GeV, where the model was found to be reliable.

## Key numbers

- Total contribution to the square of the proton's anomalous magnetic moment from kaon-hyperon final states: kappa_p^2(K) = -0.063 (using sigma_{TT'}) versus 0.105 (using sigma_T approximation)
- Total contribution to the square of the neutron's anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_n^2(K) = 0.031 (using sigma_{TT'}) versus 0.191 (using sigma_T approximation)
- Upper bound for the magnitude of kaon-hyperon contributions to the proton's magnetic moment: |kappa_p(K)|/kappa_p <= 0.14
- Upper bound for the neutron: kappa_n(K)/kappa_n <= 0.094
- Individual channel contributions to kappa_p^2(K) using sigma_{TT'}: gamma p -> K+ Lambda = -0.026, gamma p -> K+ Sigma^0 = -0.024, gamma p -> K^0 Sigma^+ = -0.013
- Individual channel contributions to kappa_n^2(K) using sigma_{TT'}: gamma n -> K^0 Lambda = 0.075, gamma n -> K^+ Sigma^- = -0.025, gamma n -> K^0 Sigma^0 = -0.019

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Hammer, Drechsel, and Mart (1997), who used experimental data and an isobaric model to estimate strange contributions to the proton's magnetic moment, but could not properly calculate the gamma p -> K^0 Sigma^+ channel due to previous models overpredicting its cross section by up to a factor of 100. The present calculation benefits from new SAPHIR data (1998) in three isospin channels, enabling a more reliable model that also predicts contributions for the neutron case. The results enable future experimental proposals at facilities such as MAMI, ELSA, TJNAF, or GRAAL to measure sigma_T in neutron channels and sigma_{TT'} in all six isospin channels, which would strongly suppress the uncertainties in the calculation.
