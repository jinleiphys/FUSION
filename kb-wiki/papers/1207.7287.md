# The hadron resonance gas model: thermodynamics of QCD and Polyakov loop

> We study the hadron resonance gas model and describe the equation of state of QCD and the vacuum expectation value of the Polyakov loop in the confined phase, in terms of hadronic states with light quarks in the first case, and with exactly one heavy quark in the second case. Comparison with lattice simulations is made.

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas model can describe both the QCD equation of state and the Polyakov loop in the confined phase, but only when the hadronic spectrum is extended beyond the lowest-lying states listed in the Particle Data Group to include many excited states predicted by quark models, with the Polyakov loop sum rule requiring hadrons containing exactly one heavy quark.

## Method

The authors compute the trace anomaly (epsilon - 3p)/T^4 using the standard hadron resonance gas model formula with the cumulative number of hadrons from the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Capstick and Isgur (1986) for light u, d, s quarks. For the Polyakov loop, they evaluate the sum rule L(T) = (1/2) sum_alpha g_{h alpha} exp(-Delta_{h alpha}/T) using hadronic states with exactly one heavy quark (charm or bottom) from the same relativized quark model, supplemented by the MIT bag model and an analytic extrapolation for states with Delta up to 5500 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The cumulative number of hadrons with u, d, s quarks from the relativized quark model is fit to an exponential n(M) = A exp(M/T_H) with A = 0.80 and T_H = 260 MeV in the regime 500 MeV < M < 2300 MeV, with chi^2/dof = 0.031.
* The relativized quark model gives a good description of lattice trace anomaly data for temperatures below T = 180 MeV, after applying a temperature shift of T_0 = 15 MeV to the lattice data from Bazavov (2009).
* The relativized quark model predicts 117 meson and 660 baryon states with one charm quark up to Delta = M - m_c about 1700 MeV, and 87 meson and 776 baryon states with one bottom quark up to a similar Delta bound.
* After including missing baryon states via the equal spacing rule, the total number of baryon states considered is 1470 with one charm quark and 1740 with one bottom quark.
* The analytic extrapolation for Delta between 1700 MeV and 5500 MeV uses n(Delta) = A Delta^alpha with A = 5.92 x 10^-9 and alpha = 3.16 for b-mesons, and A = 1.76 x 10^-24 and alpha = 8.23 for b-baryons.

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas model approach of Hagedorn (1984) and others to the Polyakov loop, building on the authors' previous proposals in Megias (2012) and Arriola (2012) for a hadronic representation of the Polyakov loop. The results enable a "Polyakov loop spectroscopy" approach to deduce multiquark states, gluelumps, and other configurations containing one or several heavy quarks from Polyakov loop measurements in fundamental and higher representations. The need to go beyond mean-field treatments of chiral quark models coupled to the Polyakov loop, as stressed in Megias (2004, 2006), is confirmed by the requirement of many excited states beyond the lowest-lying PDG hadrons.
