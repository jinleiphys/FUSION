# Electric Conductivity of QCD Matter and Dilepton Spectra in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The electric conductivity, $σ_{\rm el}$, is a fundamental transport coefficient of QCD matter that can be related to the zero-energy limit of the electromagnetic (EM) spectral function at vanishing 3-momentum in the medium. The EM spectral function is also the central quantity to describe the thermal emission rates and pertinent spectra of photon and dilepton radiation in heavy-ion collisions. Employing a model for dilepton rates that combines hadronic many-body theory with nonperturbative QGP emission constrained by lattice-QCD which describes existing dilepton measurements in heavy-ion collisions, we investigate the sensitivity of low-mass dilepton spectra in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC to $σ_{\rm el}$. In particular, we disentangle the contributions from QGP and hadronic emission, and identify signatures that can help to extract $σ_{\rm el}$ from high-precision experimental data expected to be attainable with future detector systems at the LHC.

## Key claim

The electric conductivity of QCD matter can be extracted from the zero-energy limit of the electromagnetic spectral function, and its value in the hadronic phase is dominated by the light charge carriers (pions) rather than by direct rho-meson interactions with baryons; a small conductivity manifests in dilepton spectra not as a quenching of the peak but as an enhancement from peak broadening at very low invariant mass.

## Method

The author employs in-medium vector-meson spectral functions from hadronic many-body theory (using effective Lagrangians constrained by empirical decay branchings and scattering data) combined with a nonperturbative QGP emission model constrained by lattice-QCD data. The electromagnetic spectral function is computed for hadronic matter via in-medium rho propagators (including a full off-shell treatment of the pion cloud and additional thermal pion widths) and for the QGP via a perturbative q-qbar spectrum augmented with a conductivity peak. Thermal dilepton rates are then convoluted over a schematic cylindrical fireball model with isentropic expansion to compute spectra for central Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic conductivity values drop from sigma ~ 20-40 MeV (without thermal pion widths) to sigma ~ 5-7.5 MeV (with thermal pion widths), a reduction factor of 4-5.
- For the QGP spectral function, sigma/T = e^2 * 0.23 * C_EM, which with C_EM = 6/9 evaluates to 0.014, compatible with lattice-QCD computations for T > 200 MeV.
- Hadronic rho spectral functions without thermal pion widths give sigma/T ~ 0.2; including thermal pion widths drops this to sigma/T ~ 0.04.
- Thermal pion widths are approximately 43, 26, and 17 MeV at T = 180, 140, and 100 MeV, respectively.
- In 3-momentum integrated dilepton spectra, a factor of about 5 reduction in conductivity leads to an enhancement of more than a factor of 2 in the hadronic contribution at M = 50 MeV.
- With an upper cut on pair transverse momentum q_t^ee < 0.2 GeV, the enhancement from the smaller conductivity scenario at M = 50 MeV increases to a factor of 3; with q_t^ee < 0.03 GeV, the enhancement is close to a factor of 4.
- The yield suppression for q_t^ee < 0.2 GeV is a factor of ~3, and for q_t^ee < 0.03 GeV about an additional order of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous in-medium electromagnetic spectral function calculations (Urban 1999, Rapp 1999, Rapp 2000) to the timelike low-energy limit, connecting the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed in heavy-ion collisions to charge transport. It builds on lattice-QCD computations of the electric conductivity (Aarts 2014, Brandt 2015, Ding 2016) and on recent microscopic calculations of thermal pion widths (Atchison 2022). The results enable future experimental extraction of sigma_el from high-precision dilepton data expected from ALICE-3 at the LHC run-5, while identifying the need for improved theoretical understanding of the conductivity in the pseudocritical region.
