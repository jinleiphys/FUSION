# The SU(3) Beta Function from Numerical Stochastic Perturbation Theory

> The SU(3) beta function is computed from Wilson loops to 20th order numerical stochastic perturbation theory. An attempt is made to include massless fermions, whose contribution is known analytically to 4th order. The question whether the theory admits an infrared stable fixed point is addressed.

## Key claim

The SU(3) pure gauge beta function computed to 20th order in numerical stochastic perturbation theory is significantly smaller at large couplings than the analytic four-loop result, and when combined with the known four-loop fermionic contribution for two massless flavors, the beta function develops a zero at a finite coupling, suggesting an infrared fixed point, though the exact position of this zero is subject to change.

## Method

Rectangular Wilson loops W(L,T) of sizes up to L,T=6 were computed on a 12^4 lattice using numerical stochastic perturbation theory to 20 loops in the bare coupling g_0^2 with the Wilson gauge action. The Creutz ratios R(L,T) for T=5 and L=2,3,4 were used to extract the running coupling g_qq^2 in the force (qq) scheme at scales L=√2, √6, and √12. The beta function was obtained from the logarithmic derivative of g_qq^{-2}(L) with respect to ln L, and a [3,3] Pade approximant was fitted to the lattice data. For the fermionic case, the known analytic four-loop fermionic contribution was added to the pure gauge result, and a separate [3,3] Pade fit was performed.

## Key numbers

- The universal one-loop coefficient is β_0 = 11.8/(4π)^2, consistent with the analytic value 11/(4π)^2.
- The two-loop coefficient at the midpoint L=√6 is β_1 = 115/(4π)^4; a weighted linear fit gives β_1 = 141(90)/(4π)^4.
- At g_0^2=1.04, the running coupling reaches g_qq^2(√12) ≈ 16.
- At g_qq^2 = 6.3 (α_qq = 0.5), the full 20-loop beta function is about half the size of the analytic four-loop result; at g_qq^2 = 8.2 (α_qq = 0.65), it is one third the size.
- The running coupling α_qq(μ) hits a wall at μ/Λ_qq ≈ 0.7, meaning μ cannot be taken lower than about 0.7 Λ_qq.
- Λ_qq = 1.048 Λ_MSbar; using r_0 = 0.5 fm gives Λ_qq = 254(2) MeV.
- For N_f=2, the beta function has a zero at g_qq^2 = 6.3 (α_qq ≈ 0.5) followed by a pole at g_qq^2 = 7.3.
- At g_qq^2(L)=6, the corresponding spatial extent is L = 0.32 fm, with lattice constant a between 0.146 fm and 0.093 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous computation of Wilson loops to 20 loops (Horsley 2012) and compares with the analytic four-loop beta function (Donnellan 2010). The pure gauge result shows no evidence for renormalon singularities or factorial asymptotic growth. The inclusion of massless fermions addresses the open question of whether QCD with a small number of massless flavors has an infrared fixed point and a conformal window, a scenario distinct from the mass-gap scenario of pure gauge theory or the pole scenario of supersymmetric Yang-Mills (Kogan 1995). The results suggest that numerical stochastic perturbation theory can be extended to full QCD (Di Renzo 2004) to compute the fermionic contribution to higher loops, which would be needed to confirm the infrared fixed point beyond doubt.
