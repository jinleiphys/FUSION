# The isovector dipole strength in nuclei with extreme neutron excess

> The E1 strength is systematically analyzed in very neutron-rich Sn nuclei, beyond $^{132}$Sn until $^{166}$Sn, within the Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation. The great neutron excess favors the appearance of a deformed ground state for $^{142-162}$Sn. The evolution of the low-lying strength in deformed nuclei is determined by the interplay of two factors, isospin asymmetry and deformation: while greater neutron excess increases the total low-lying strength, deformation hinders and spreads it. Very neutron rich deformed nuclei may not be as good candidates as stable spherical nuclei like $^{132}$Sn for the experimental study of low-lying E1 strength.

# The isovector dipole strength in nuclei with extreme neutron excess

## Key claim

The low-lying E1 strength in very neutron-rich Sn nuclei is determined by the interplay of isospin asymmetry and deformation: while greater neutron excess increases the total low-lying strength, deformation hinders and spreads it, meaning that very neutron-rich deformed nuclei may not be as good candidates as stable spherical nuclei like $^{132}$Sn for experimental study of low-lying E1 strength.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of the E1 strength in $^{132-166}$Sn isotopes using the Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA) for axially deformed systems, based on a deformed Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) ground state with the NL3 parameter set and a monopole-monopole pairing interaction. The RPA matrix was solved by diagonalization in a truncated configuration space of approximately 16-20 thousand quasiparticle pairs out of roughly 60 thousand, with an energy cutoff of 50 MeV for particle-hole pairs and -1600 MeV for antiparticle-hole pairs.

## Key numbers

* Ground state binding energies for $^{132}$Sn and $^{134}$Sn: B/A = -8.355 MeV and -8.276 MeV respectively, in very good agreement with experimental values from Audi (2003)
* Quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$ ranges from 0.00 for spherical $^{132}$Sn up to 0.32 for the most deformed nucleus $^{152}$Sn, with deformation appearing at $^{142}$Sn ($\beta = 0.11$) and spherical symmetry restored at $^{164}$Sn ($\beta = 0.00$)
* GDR centroid energy decreases from 15.1 MeV in $^{132}$Sn to 13.7 MeV in $^{166}$Sn, with the GDR exhausting 110.0% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule in $^{132}$Sn and 94.4% in $^{166}$Sn
* Low-lying (pygmy) strength increases from 2.9% TRK in $^{132}$Sn to 7.1% TRK in $^{166}$Sn, with the threshold energy decreasing from 9.4 MeV to 7.2 MeV along the chain
* The $K^{\pi}=0^{-}$ mode contribution to low-lying strength reaches almost 60% of the total in the most deformed nucleus $^{152}$Sn, compared to one-third in spherical nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous spherical RQRPA studies of low-lying E1 strength in $^{100-132}$Sn by Pena Arteaga et al. (2007) and in $^{114-140}$Sn by Paar et al. (2005, 2007), which showed that low-lying strength increases with neutron excess but decreases near shell closures. The calculations use the NL3 parameter set and compare favorably with deformed HFB calculations using the D1S Gogny force by Hilaire (2002) and Webb et al. (2003), though differences in predicted deformation appear near the valley of beta-stability. The results enable predictions for next-generation RIB facilities (FAIR, Spiral2, RIBF) and suggest that experimental study of low-lying E1 strength may be more feasible in spherical nuclei like $^{132}$Sn than in very neutron-rich deformed isotopes.
