# A limiting velocity for quarkonium propagation in a strongly coupled plasma via AdS/CFT

> We study the dispersion relations of mesons in a particular hot strongly coupled supersymmetric gauge theory plasma. We find that at large momentum k the dispersion relations become omega = v_0 k + a + b/k + ..., where the limiting velocity v_0 is the same for mesons with any quantum numbers and depends only on the ratio of the temperature to the quark mass T/m_q. We compute a and b in terms of the meson quantum numbers and T/m_q. The limiting meson velocity v_0 becomes much smaller than the speed of light at temperatures below but close to T_diss, the temperature above which no meson bound states at rest in the plasma are found. From our result for v_0, we find that the temperature above which no meson bound states with velocity v exist is T_diss(v) \simeq (1-v^2)^(1/4) T_diss, up to few percent corrections.We thus confirm by direct calculation of meson dispersion relations a result inferred indirectly in previous work via analysis of the screening length between a static quark and antiquark in a moving plasma. Although we do not do our calculations in QCD, we argue that the qualitative features of the dispersion relation we compute, including in particular the relation between dissociation temperature and meson velocity, may apply to bottomonium and charmonium mesons propagating in the strongly coupled plasma of QCD. We discuss how our results can contribute to understanding quarkonium physics in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors directly compute meson dispersion relations in a strongly coupled supersymmetric gauge theory plasma and find that the limiting velocity v0 for mesons at large momentum depends only on T/mq and becomes much smaller than the speed of light near the dissociation temperature, confirming indirectly inferred results about velocity-dependent dissociation temperatures.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence, specifically the D3/D7-brane construction, to study mesons in strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills theory with N=2 hypermultiplets in the fundamental representation. They analyze fluctuations of the D7-brane in the dual gravity description to derive meson dispersion relations, obtaining analytic results in three limits: low temperature at fixed k, low temperature at fixed kT, and large k for any temperature. The dispersion relations are expressed in terms of curvature invariants of the D7-brane geometry.

## Key numbers

* The dissociation temperature for mesons at rest is T_diss = 2.166 m_q / sqrt(lambda)
* At large momentum k, the dispersion relation takes the form omega = v0 k + a + b/k + ..., where v0 depends only on T/m_q
* The temperature above which no meson bound states with velocity v exist is T_diss(v) = (1-v^2)^(1/4) T_diss, up to few percent corrections
* The critical velocity beyond which no potential exists between a quark-antiquark pair is v_c^2 = 1 - (lambda^2 T^4)/(16 m_q^4)
* The screening length for a static quark-antiquark pair at rest is L_s = 0.28/T
* The screening length scales with velocity as L_s(v,T) = L_s(0,T) (1-v^2)^(1/4)

## Context

This work extends previous indirect analyses of quarkonium dissociation in moving plasmas, particularly the screening length calculations for static quark-antiquark pairs in a hot plasma wind by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006). The authors confirm that the velocity scaling of the dissociation temperature inferred from screening length calculations is correct, with only few percent corrections. The results provide a foundation for understanding quarkonium propagation in heavy ion collisions, suggesting that mesons near their dissociation temperature move through strongly coupled plasma at velocities much smaller than the speed of light even at arbitrarily high momentum, which has implications for modeling sequential quarkonium suppression patterns at RHIC and LHC experiments.
