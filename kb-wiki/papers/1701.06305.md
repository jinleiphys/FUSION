# Unexpected neutron/proton ratio and isospin effect in low-energy antiproton induced reactions

> The inclusive spectra of preequilibrium nucleons produced in low-energy antiproton-nucleus collisions are thoroughly investigated within the framework of the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics model for the first time. All possible reaction channels such as elastic scattering, annihilation, charge exchange and inelastic scattering in antibaryon-baryon, baryon-baryon and meson-baryon collisions have been included in the transport model. The unexpected neutron/proton ratio in comparison to the pion and proton induced reactions is caused from the isospin effects of pion-nucleon collisions and the density dependence of symmetry energy. It is found that the $π^{-}$-neutron collisions enhance the neutron emission in the antiproton annihilation in a nucleus. Different to heavy-ion collisions, the isospin effects are pronounced at the low kinetic energies. A soft symmetry energy with the stiffness of $γ_{s}=$0.5 at subsaturation densities is constrained from the available data of the neutron/proton spectra.

# Unexpected neutron/proton ratio and isospin effect in low-energy antiproton induced reactions

## Key claim

The unexpected enhancement of the neutron/proton ratio in low-energy antiproton-nucleus collisions, which had puzzled physicists for decades, is explained by the isospin effects of pion-nucleon collisions and the density dependence of symmetry energy; specifically, the soft symmetry energy with stiffness γs = 0.5 at subsaturation densities is constrained from the LEAR data, but the claim is conditional on the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) model reproducing the available data for the first time.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate antiproton-nucleus collisions, incorporating all relevant reaction channels including elastic scattering, annihilation, charge exchange, and inelastic scattering in antibaryon-baryon, baryon-baryon, and meson-baryon collisions. The dynamics of resonances below 2 GeV, hyperons, and mesons were coupled to hadron-hadron collisions and antibaryon-baryon annihilations, with the temporal evolution of all particles described by Hamilton's equations of motion under self-consistently generated mean-field potentials constructed from the Skyrme energy-density functional. Annihilation reactions in antibaryon-baryon collisions were described by a statistical model with SU(3) symmetry of pseudoscalar and vector mesons, considering final states from two to six mesons.

## Key numbers

- The compression modulus of isospin symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density (ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3) is K = 230 MeV, using parameters α = −226.5 MeV, β = 173.7 MeV, and γ = 1.309.
- The symmetry energy parameter Csym is 38 MeV, with the stiffness γs adjustable to values of 0.5 (soft), 1 (linear), and 2 (hard); the three kinds cross at saturation density with the value of 31.5 MeV.
- The mean-field potential of antinucleons is VN̅ = −164 MeV at normal nuclear density.
- Maximum cross sections for pion-nucleon scattering at the Δ-resonance are 200 mb for π+ + p → Δ++, 133.3 mb for π0 + p → Δ+, and 66.7 mb for π− + p → Δ0.
- The π− multiplicity on 12C is 1.5 and the π+ multiplicity is 0.6 at 200 MeV/c incident antiproton momentum.
- The elastic scattering cross section for π−n reactions has a maximal value of 200 mb at the Δ-resonance energy (E = 0.19 GeV, p = 0.298 GeV/c), compared to 25 mb for π−p collisions at the same pion energy.
- The mean n/p value of the target nucleus 65Cu is 1.24.

## Context

This work extends the intranuclear cascade model approach of Pshenichnov et al. (1995), which could explain n/p spectra in pion- and proton-nucleus collisions but failed to reproduce the antiproton-induced n/p spectra observed at the LEAR facility at CERN (Polf et al., 1993, 1995). The LQMD model provides the first successful explanation of these long-puzzling data. The results enable future measurements at the PANDA facility (Antiproton Annihilation at Darmstadt, Germany) to further constrain the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities, complementing constraints from Fermi-energy heavy-ion collisions where isospin effects appear at different kinetic energy regimes.
