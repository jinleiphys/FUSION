# $α$ Decays in Superstrong Static Electric Fields

> Superstrong static electric fields could deform Coulomb barriers between $α$ clusters and daughter nuclei, and bring up the possibility of speeding up $α$ decays. We adopt a simplified model for the spherical $α$ emitter $^{212}$Po and study its responses to superstrong static electric fields. We find that, superstrong electric fields with field strengths $|\mathbf{E}|\sim0.1$ MV/fm could turn the angular distribution of $α$ emissions from isotropic to strongly anisotropic, and speed up $α$ decays by more than one order of magnitude. We also study the influences of superstrong electric fields along the Po isotope chains, and discuss the implications of our studies on $α$ decays in superstrong monochromatic laser fields. The study here might be helpful for future theoretical studies of $α$ decay in realistic superstrong laser fields.

## Key claim

Superstrong static electric fields with field strengths around 0.1 MV/fm can deform the Coulomb barrier between alpha clusters and daughter nuclei, making alpha emission angular distributions strongly anisotropic and speeding up alpha decays by more than one order of magnitude, but only under the condition that the field strength remains well below the over-barrier, relativistic, and emission-energy-change thresholds (E_ob, E_rel, and E_ΔQ respectively).

## Method

The authors adopt a simplified model from Delion (2009) where the alpha-core potential is a shifted harmonic oscillator joined to a Coulomb potential at the touching radius, applied to the spherical alpha emitter 212Po. They solve the Stark-like Hamiltonian in the center-of-mass frame using the two-potential method to compute the differential barrier penetrability as a function of emission angle, then integrate over angles to obtain the total penetrability. The study is restricted to field strengths below 0.5 MV/fm to maintain the quantum-tunneling picture with approximately unchanged emission energy.

## Key numbers

- For 212Po, the effective charge of the alpha cluster in the center-of-mass frame is Z_eff = 0.4151, clearly different from the bare charge Z_c = 2.
- The critical field strength marking the onset of wild growth in alpha-decay widths for 212Po is E_cr ~ 0.05 MV/fm.
- At |E| ~ 0.1 MV/fm, the enhancement of total penetrability is over one order of magnitude; at |E| ~ 0.5 MV/fm, the enhancement is about five orders of magnitude.
- The anisotropy in angular distribution increases from a factor of about six orders of magnitude at E = 0.1 MV/fm to more than sixteen orders of magnitude at E = 0.5 MV/fm.
- For |E| > E_Θ = 0.17 MV/fm, alpha emissions are forbidden for angles Θ > Θ_E, with Θ_E values of 2.59, 2.17, 2.01, and 1.92 rad for |E| = 0.2, 0.3, 0.4, and 0.5 MV/fm respectively.
- For 212Po in monochromatic laser fields with amplitude 0.1 MV/fm, the average barrier penetrability is 7.433 × 10^{-15} for both photon energies of 1 eV (optical) and 100 eV (X-ray).

## Context

This work extends the simplified alpha-decay model of Delion (2009) to include external electric fields, building on earlier studies of laser-assisted alpha decay by Cortes (2011, 2012), Misicu (2013, 2016), Kopytin (2014), and Delion (2017). The static-field results serve as a benchmark for understanding alpha decays in monochromatic laser fields via the adiabatic (low-frequency) approximation, with potential applications to decontaminating transuranium nuclear wastes. The study enables future theoretical work on alpha decay in realistic superstrong laser fields, particularly through laser-nucleus collision schemes that could magnify laboratory field strengths by factors of 15000-100000 at facilities like the Large Hadron Collider or the proposed Super Proton-Proton collider.
