# Exact Solution of the Isovector Proton Neutron Pairing Hamiltonian

> The complete exact solution of the T=1 neutron-proton pairing Hamiltonian is presented in the context of the SO(5) Richardson-Gaudin model with non-degenerate single-particle levels and including isospin-symmetry breaking terms. The power of the method is illustrated with a numerical calculation for $^{64}$Ge for a $pf+g_{9/2}$ model space which is out of reach of modern shell-model codes.

## Key claim

The paper presents the complete exact solution of the isovector (T=1) neutron-proton pairing Hamiltonian using an SO(5) Richardson-Gaudin model that includes non-degenerate single-particle levels and isospin-symmetry breaking terms, going beyond the incorrect earlier solution by Richardson (1966) for more than two nucleon pairs.

## Method

The authors construct a set of L commuting integrals of motion for the SO(5) algebra, from which the Hamiltonian is obtained as a linear combination. The eigenvalues are derived from coupled Richardson equations involving two sets of spectral parameters: pair energies e_alpha and isospin parameters w_beta. The Bethe ansatz wavefunction is a product of pair-creation and isospin-raising operators acting on a lowest-weight state, with quantum numbers including seniority v_i, reduced isospin t_i, number of T=1 pairs M, and total isospin projection T_0.

## Key numbers

- For 64Ge with a 40Ca core: 12 valence neutrons and 12 valence protons in the pf+g9/2 model space
- Single-particle energies (in MeV): epsilon_f7/2 = 0.00, epsilon_p3/2 = 6.00, epsilon_f5/2 = 6.25, epsilon_p1/2 = 7.1, epsilon_g9/2 = 9.60
- Two pairing strengths considered: g = -0.05 (weak) and g = -0.5 (strong)
- For a test system of two neutrons and two protons in two shells (j0=1/2 and j1=3/2) with seniority 0: interaction strength g = -1, single-particle energies epsilon_0 = 0 and epsilon_1 = 1

## Context

This work extends the Richardson-Gaudin integrable models from rank-1 SU(2) algebras to the rank-2 SO(5) algebra, correcting an earlier incorrect solution by Richardson (1966) for more than two nucleon pairs and generalizing the isospin-invariant solution of Links et al. (2002). The method enables exact calculations for 64Ge in a model space beyond the reach of modern shell-model codes, and opens the possibility of treating higher-rank algebras like Sp(6) and SO(8) for more realistic nuclear structure calculations, as well as applications to high-Tc superconductivity models.
