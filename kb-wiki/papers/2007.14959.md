## Key claim

This paper provides a snapshot of the current state of the art in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and related phenomena through 18 sections, but the specific calculation of neutral pion mass in a magnetic field using the linear sigma model coupled to quarks (LSMq) shows a non-monotonic behavior that the abstract does not detail: the pion mass *decreases* with increasing magnetic field at weak field but *increases* at large field values.

## Method

The neutral pion mass in an external arbitrary magnetic field at zero temperature was calculated within the linear sigma model coupled to quarks (LSMq) by evaluating one-loop pion self-energy contributions from quark-antiquark loops and charged pion loops, incorporating one-loop effective corrections to the boson self-coupling, the fermion coupling, and the minimum of the potential. The resulting gap equation for the magnetic-field-dependent pion mass was solved numerically. Separately, the chiral transition and chiral susceptibility for a chirally imbalanced plasma in a magnetic field were studied using the Wigner function approach within the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a medium separation regularization scheme.

## Key numbers

- Neutral pion mass shows a non-monotonic dependence on magnetic field: it *decreases* at weak field and starts to *increase* at large field values (no specific numerical field strength or mass values given for the turning point in the truncated text).
- For the chiral transition study: current quark masses were set to m_u = m_d = 6 MeV, three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 590 MeV, and coupling g = 2.435 / Lambda^2.
- For the flow correlations study: the bag constant B was adjusted to yield a critical temperature T_c = 164 MeV for the first-order phase transition equation of state.
- For the flow correlations study: initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm and Gaussian smearing sigma = 0.7 fm were used for initial energy density profiles.
- For centrality 0-5% in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the Pearson correlation coefficient c(epsilon_2, v_2) showed approximately 15% decrease for a first-order phase transition equation of state compared to a crossover transition equation of state.

## Context

This work extends studies of magnetic field effects on QCD matter properties, which have generated substantial theoretical activity in the last decade following the realization that very strong transient magnetic fields (approximately 10^19 Gauss) are created in peripheral heavy-ion collisions (Skokov, 2009). The neutral pion mass calculation qualitatively agrees with lattice QCD studies (Bali, 2017) up to moderate magnetic field strengths. The chiral transition study using the Wigner function approach within the NJL model extends prior work by Fukushima, Ruggieri, and Gatto (2010) and Yu, Liu, and Huang (2016) who found that chiral transition temperature decreases with chiral chemical potential, while also addressing the regularization scheme dependence noted by Farias, Duarte, Krein, and Ramos (2016). The flow correlations study enables a potential observable to differentiate between crossover and first-order phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions, building on the finding by Niemi and collaborators (2012) that the Pearson correlation between initial eccentricity and final flow is insensitive to shear viscosity and initial conditions.
