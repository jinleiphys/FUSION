# Realizing Fulde-Ferrell Superfluids via a Dark-State Control of Feshbach Resonances

> We propose that the long-sought Fulde-Ferrell superfluidity with nonzero momentum pairing can be realized in ultracold two-component Fermi gases of $^{40}$K or $^{6}$Li atoms by optically tuning their magnetic Feshbach resonances via the creation of a closed-channel dark state with a Doppler-shifted Stark effect. In this scheme, two counterpropagating optical fields are applied to couple two molecular states in the closed channel to an excited molecular state, leading to a significant violation of Galilean invariance in the dark-state regime and hence to the possibility of Fulde-Ferrell superfluidity. We develop a field theoretical formulation for both two-body and many-body problems and predict that the Fulde-Ferrell state has remarkable properties, such as anisotropic single-particle dispersion relation, suppressed superfluid density at zero temperature, anisotropic sound velocity and rotonic collective mode. The latter two features can be experimentally probed using Bragg spectroscopy, providing a smoking-gun proof of Fulde-Ferrell superfluidity.

# Realizing Fulde-Ferrell Superfluids via a Dark-State Control of Feshbach Resonances

## Key claim

The authors propose that Fulde-Ferrell (FF) superfluidity with nonzero momentum pairing can be realized in ultracold two-component Fermi gases without spin-population imbalance, by using a dark-state optical control of magnetic Feshbach resonances that creates a center-of-mass momentum-dependent interatomic interaction. The scheme avoids the heating problems of spin-orbit coupling approaches, and the FF state is predicted to be the true ground state across the BEC-BCS crossover because the zero-momentum pairing state carries a nonzero current and suffers from severe instability.

## Method

The authors develop a field theoretical formulation for both two-body and many-body problems, starting from an atom-molecule theory of magnetic Feshbach resonances and adding two optical fields that couple two ground molecular states to an excited molecular state. They compute the off-shell T-matrix for atom-atom scattering to characterize the two-body interaction, and evaluate the many-body partition function in the mean-field approximation using a Fulde-Ferrell ansatz with pairing momentum Q. The superfluid ground state is determined by minimizing the thermodynamic potential with respect to the gap parameter Delta, the pairing momentum Q, and the chemical potential mu.

## Key numbers

- For 40K atoms near the broad resonance at B0 = 202.02 G: background scattering length a_bg = 174 a0, resonance width Delta B = 7.04 G, magnetic moment difference Delta mu = 2 mu_B.
- Typical Rabi frequencies: Omega_1 = 2 pi x 120 MHz, Omega_2 = 2 pi x 20 MHz; one-photon detuning Delta_e = -2 pi x 500 MHz; excited state decay rate gamma_e = 2 pi x 6 MHz.
- Recoil energy E_R = k_R^2/(2m) ~ 2 pi x 10 kHz; wave vector k_R = 8.138 x 10^6 m^{-1}.
- Atom density n = 1.82 x 10^13 cm^{-3}, giving Fermi momentum k_F = (3 pi^2 n)^{1/3} ~ k_R.
- The two-body Stark shift imaginary part (decay rate) is about 10^{-5} gamma_e ~ 2 pi x 100 Hz, corresponding to dimer lifetime ~ 0.01-0.1 s.
- The many-body Stark shift imaginary part at the BCS side is < 10^{-6} gamma_e; near resonance the system lifetime is estimated to be 100 ms.
- The FF momentum Q reaches a sizable value Q ~ k_F around the magnetic Feshbach resonance.

## Context

This work extends the concept of dark-state optical control of Feshbach resonances demonstrated by Jagannathan (2016) and the center-of-mass momentum-dependent interaction proposed by Jie (2016). It contrasts with previous approaches to realize FFLO states using spin-population imbalance (Sheehy 2006, Sheehy 2007), which gave a narrow stability window, and spin-orbit coupling (Zheng 2013, Wu 2013, Liu 2013, Hu 2013), which suffered from heating problems (Zhai 2015). The proposal enables experimental probing of anisotropic phonon dispersion and rotonic collective modes via Bragg spectroscopy, providing smoking-gun evidence of FF superfluidity.
