# Carbon-12 in the generalized Skyrme model

> We study properties of the $^{12}$C nucleus within the generalized Skyrme model where, in addition to the standard massive Skyrme model, the sextic term and the pionic potential squared are included. We find that the model continues to accurately describe the rotational bands of the $^{12}$C nucleus. In addition, at variance with the case of the standard Skyrme model, it provides the correct energy ordering of the classical solutions which correspond to the ground state and the Hoyle state, respectively.

## Key claim

The generalized Skyrme model, which includes the sextic term and the pionic potential squared in addition to the standard massive Skyrme terms, can produce the correct energy ordering of the classical solutions corresponding to the ground state and the Hoyle state of carbon-12, provided the coupling constant \(m_2\) is at least 0.5; for smaller values the desired \(D_{3h}\) symmetric soliton is not a stable minimum.

## Method

The authors minimized the static energy functional of the generalized Skyrme model (containing kinetic, quartic Skyrme, pion mass, sextic baryon density squared, and pion potential squared terms) on a cubic lattice using fourth-order finite differences for spatial derivatives and a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method for time evolution, with the motion frozen when the static energy increased. They performed an exhaustive sweep of the three coupling constants \(c_4, c_6, m_2\) to find a region where both the \(D_{3h}\) triangular and \(D_{4h}\) chain Skyrmions are stable and the triangular solution has lower energy. The optimal parameters were then refined on a finer lattice of \(148^3\) points with spacing \(\Delta x = 0.1\) and time step \(\Delta t = 0.05\). Semiclassical rigid-rotor quantization of the spin zero modes and a multipole expansion of the Coulomb energy were applied to obtain the physical excitation spectra.

## Key numbers

*   The optimal classical energy splitting between the ground state (\(D_{3h}\)) and Hoyle state (\(D_{4h}\)) is about \(0.08\%\) in Skyrme units, comparable to the experimental value of \(0.07\%\).
*   The classical energies in Skyrme units (normalized by the Skyrme-Faddeev bound) are \(E = 17.3418\) for the ground state and \(E = 17.3553\) for the Hoyle state.
*   The spin moment of inertia tensor eigenvalues (in Skyrme units) are \(V_{11} = 12540.6\), \(V_{33} = 18363\) for the ground state, and \(V_{11} = 26399.3\), \(V_{33} = 5462.2\) for the Hoyle state.
*   After calibration, the physical energy of the Hoyle state is \(E_{\rm Hoyle} = 11186.1\;{\rm MeV}\), with the ground state energy fitted to \(11177.9\;{\rm MeV}\).
*   The calibrated pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 116.256\;{\rm MeV}\) and the Skyrme coupling is \(e = 5.34383\), giving a pion mass \(m_\pi = 163\;{\rm MeV}\).
*   The root mean square matter radii are \(2.14\;{\rm fm}\) for the ground state and \(2.44\;{\rm fm}\) for the Hoyle state, giving a ratio of 1.14 compared to the experimental ratio of 1.19.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model description of carbon-12 previously developed by Lau and Manton (2014) and Battye et al. (2009), which correctly reproduced the slopes of the rotational bands but gave the wrong energy ordering of the ground and Hoyle states (the chain solution had lower energy than the triangle). The inclusion of the BPS part (sextic term and squared pion potential) fixes this ordering while preserving the alpha-particle clustering structure and the good agreement with excitation spectra. The results enable a more quantitatively precise description of light nuclei within the Skyrme framework and connect to studies of nuclear matter at high densities, where the sextic term is essential for a stiff equation of state supporting neutron stars (Naya et al., 2018; various authors on Skyrme equation of state).
