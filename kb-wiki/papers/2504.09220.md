# Nuclear Structure Properties of even-even Chromium Isotopes and the E ect of Deformation on Calculated Electron Capture Cross Sections

> In this study, we investigate the role of nuclear deformation on the calculated electron capture cross section (ECC) of even-even chromium (Cr) isotopes. We first determined the nuclear structure properties of these nuclei within the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1). The energy spectra and E2 transition probabilities were calculated by fitting the parameters in the model formalism. The analysis of the potential energy surface was also performed to predict the geometric shape of the Cr nuclei by plotting their contour plot in the plane of (beta, gamma) deformation parameters. Later, we calculated the ECC within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model. In particular, we studied how the calculated ECC changed with different values of the nuclear deformation parameter. The calculated Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions were widely spread among the daughter states. The total GT strength decreased with increasing value of the beta parameter. The computed ECC values, however, increased with increasing beta values of the Cr isotopes.

# Nuclear Structure Properties of even-even Chromium Isotopes and the Effect of Deformation on Calculated Electron Capture Cross Sections

## Key claim

The computed electron capture cross sections (ECC) on even-even Cr isotopes increase with increasing nuclear deformation parameter β, while the total Gamow-Teller (GT) strength decreases with increasing β; however, the authors caution that this correlation warrants further investigation and they are not in a position to generalize the statement.

## Method

The authors first calculated nuclear structure properties (energy spectra and E2 transition probabilities) for even-even ⁴⁶–⁶⁶Cr isotopes using the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1), fitting Hamiltonian parameters to experimental data from the National Nuclear Data Center. They then computed Gamow-Teller strength distributions and electron capture cross sections using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a Nilsson basis, employing four different sets of deformation parameters (β_IBM-1, β_B(E2), β_GM, and β_Mac-mic) to study the effect of deformation on the calculated ECC.

## Key numbers

- Total GT strength decreased with increasing mass number; for each Cr isotope, with decrease in β value, the calculated GT strength increased (no explicit numerical values given for individual isotopes in the truncated text).
- ECC values increased by up to a factor of 50 when stellar temperature rose from 0.5 MeV to 1.0 MeV; further increase from 1.0 MeV to 1.5 MeV produced less than a factor of 2 increase.
- The particle-hole interaction strength χ was set to 4.2/A, showing a 1/A dependence; the particle-particle interaction strength κ was set to 0.10.
- Pairing gaps were computed using Δ_p = Δ_n = 12/√A MeV.
- The constant D was taken as 6143 s and g_A/g_V = −1.254.
- The oscillator constant was computed using ħω = 41A^(1/3).
- ECC was calculated for incident electron energies in the range 0–30 MeV at stellar temperatures T = 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous pn-QRPA studies of ⁴⁶,⁴⁸,⁵⁰Cr isotopes by Nabi et al. (2019) and Ullah et al. (2020) to a broader isotopic chain (⁴⁶–⁶⁶Cr), systematically investigating deformation effects on ECC. The IBM-1 structure calculations complement earlier shell model studies by Kaneko et al. (2008), Togashi et al. (2015), and others who studied Cr isotopes using large-scale shell model and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approaches. The results enable improved modeling of electron capture rates in presupernova stellar evolution, where EC on fp-shell nuclei significantly affects the core collapse dynamics, and the authors state they are studying a broader suite of nuclei to further probe the correlation between ECC and nuclear deformation.
