# Relativistic Chiral Theory of Nuclear Matter and QCD Constraints

> We present a relativistic chiral theory of nuclear matter which includes the effect of confinement. Nuclear binding is obtained with a chiral invariant scalar background field associated with the radial fluctuations of the chiral condensate Nuclear matter stability is ensured once the scalar response of the nucleon depending on the quark confinement mechanism is properly incorporated. All the parameters are fixed or constrained by hadron phenomenology and lattice data. A good description of nuclear saturation is reached, which includes the effect of in-medium pion loops. Asymmetry properties of nuclear matter are also well described once the full rho meson exchange and Fock terms are included.

## Key claim
Nuclear matter saturation and asymmetry properties can be described within a relativistic chiral framework that includes confinement effects, provided the scalar response of the nucleon (parameterized by a dimensionless constant C) is properly incorporated to cancel otherwise destabilizing three-body forces and to match lattice QCD constraints on the nucleon scalar susceptibility.

## Method
The authors construct a relativistic chiral effective theory where the attractive scalar mean field is identified with the chiral-invariant radial fluctuation of the chiral condensate. They introduce a phenomenological scalar nucleon response parameter κ_NS (or its dimensionless form C) to account for the nucleon’s internal quark structure adjustment in the medium, which modifies the nucleon mass expansion and generates a repulsive three-body force. The model is solved at the Hartree level, then extended to include in-medium pion loops via a RPA approach with short-range correlations, and finally a full relativistic Hartree-Fock calculation including rho meson exchange and tensor couplings is performed.

## Key numbers
- Lattice data gives (a_4)_latt ≈ −0.5 GeV⁻³ for the nucleon scalar susceptibility coefficient, while the chiral model without confinement gives (a_4)_Chiral ≈ −3.5 GeV⁻³.
- The dimensionless scalar response parameter C that reproduces the lattice a_4 value is C ≈ 1.25; in the full calculation, C = 1.33 (close to the lattice estimate) is used.
- The correlation energy from pion loops is modest: −8 MeV from the longitudinal channel and −7 MeV from the transverse channel.
- In the relativistic Hartree-Fock calculation, the parameters used are: g_S = 10, m_σ = 800 MeV, g_ρ = 2.6 (vector dominance model value), g_ω = 6.4 (close to the VDM value 3g_ρ), and tensor coupling κ_ρ = 3.7 (VDM) or κ_ρ = 5 (strong rho scenario).
- The rho Hartree contribution to the asymmetry energy is 7 MeV, insufficient alone; the Fock tensor term is necessary to reach the accepted asymmetry energy around 30 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Walecka by connecting the scalar background field to chiral condensate fluctuations, building on the authors’ earlier work (Chanfray, Ericson, Guichon 2002) and incorporating lattice QCD constraints from Leinweber, Thomas, Young (2004). It addresses the failure of naive chiral models to produce nuclear saturation, which was traced to the absence of confinement effects. The approach enables a parameter-free or nearly parameter-free description of both symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, and raises the question of how confinement generates the large positive scalar nucleon response, linking to studies of partial chiral symmetry restoration inside the nucleon.
