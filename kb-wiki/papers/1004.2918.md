# Pion Number Fluctuations and Correlations in the Statistical System with Fixed Isospin

> The statistical system of pions with zero total isospin is studied. The suppression effects for the average yields due to isospin conservation are the same for $π^0$, $π^+$ and $π^-$. However, a behavior of the corresponding particle number fluctuations are different. For neutral pions there is the enhancement of the fluctuations, whereas for charged pions the isospin conservation suppresses fluctuations. The correlations between the numbers of charged and neutral pions are observed for finite systems. This causes a maximum of the total pion number fluctuations for small systems. The thermodynamic limit values for the scaled variances of neutral and charged pions are calculated. The enhancements of the fluctuations due to Bose statistics are found and discussed.

## Key claim

For a statistical system of pions with fixed zero total isospin, the suppression of average yields is identical for all three pion charge states, but the fluctuations behave differently: neutral pions show enhanced fluctuations while charged pions show suppressed fluctuations, and these effects are finite-volume phenomena that vanish in the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors use the group projection technique for SU(2) isospin to construct the partition function of an ideal pion gas with fixed total isospin I=0, first within Boltzmann statistics for analytical transparency and then including Bose statistics. They compute mean multiplicities, scaled variances, and correlation coefficients for π⁰, π⁺, and π⁻ by taking derivatives of the partition function with respect to auxiliary fugacity parameters, and compare results to the grand canonical ensemble (GCE) and the canonical ensemble (CE) with fixed electric charge Q=0.

## Key numbers

- In the thermodynamic limit (z→∞) for Boltzmann statistics with I=0: ω₀ → 1 and ω± → 1/2.
- For Bose statistics with m/T→0 and I=0 in the thermodynamic limit: ω₀ → 1.368 and ω± → 0.684.
- For small systems (z≪1) with I=0 and Boltzmann statistics: ω₀ ≈ 2 − z/2 and ω± ≈ 1 − z²/30.
- The correlation coefficient ρⁱ₀± between neutral and charged pions at I=0 reaches a maximum value of approximately 0.19 at z≈1.
- The scaled variance of total pion number ω_N at I=0 has a maximum near z≈0.5, reaching ω_N ≈ 2 + z/2 − z²/4 for small z.

## Context

This work extends the study of non-Abelian charge conservation effects on fluctuations, building on the pioneering isospin conservation work of Bethe and the group projection formalism developed by Turko. It contrasts with the Abelian case of fixed electric charge Q=0 studied by Begun et al. (2005), where neutral pions show no fluctuation enhancement and no correlations with charged pions. The results enable predictions for fluctuations in the colorless quark-gluon gas with SU(3) color symmetry, where analogous differences between color states are expected to survive even in the thermodynamic limit.
