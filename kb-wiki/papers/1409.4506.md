# Recent Advances in Shell Evolution with Shell-Model Calculations

> Shell evolution in exotic nuclei is investigated with large-scale shell-model calculations. After presenting that the central and tensor forces produce distinctive ways of shell evolution, we show several recent results: (i) evolution of single-particle-like levels in antimony and cupper isotopes, (ii) shape coexistence in nickel isotopes understood in terms of configuration-dependent shell structure, and (iii) prediction of the evolution of the recently established $N=34$ magic number towards smaller proton numbers. In any case, large-scale shell-model calculations play indispensable roles in describing the interplay between single-particle character and correlation.

# Recent Advances in Shell Evolution with Shell-Model Calculations

## Key claim

Shell evolution in exotic nuclei is driven by the central and tensor forces in the proton-neutron channel, but the actual single-particle-like states in antimony and copper isotopes are strongly mixed with core-coupled states (spectroscopic factors 0.5-0.7 in mid-shell), meaning the evolution of energy spacings is dominated by shell evolution only because of cancellation between neutron-neutron and proton-neutron correlations.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) for nickel and copper isotopes in the full pf shell plus the 0g9/2 and 1d5/2 orbits, and conventional shell-model diagonalization for antimony isotopes using the SNBG3 neutron-neutron interaction and a density-dependent V_MU proton-neutron interaction. The tensor force was varied between zero, the pi+rho meson exchange potential, and a version scaled by 1.3 to include three-nucleon force effects, while the central force was described by a one-range Gaussian form.

## Key numbers

- In antimony isotopes, the 11/2^-_1 to 7/2^+_1 energy spacing is approximately constant at ~2 MeV when the tensor force is omitted, but agrees with experiment when the pi+rho tensor force is included (scaled by 1.3 for three-nucleon force effects)
- Calculated proton spectroscopic factors for the 11/2^-_1 and 7/2^+_1 levels in antimony are 0.5-0.7 in the mid-shell region (N ~ 64)
- The N=34 shell gap in calcium isotopes is estimated to be 2.66 MeV using the GXPF1Br interaction, which modifies the GXPF1B interaction by delta v^{T=1}_{1p3/2,0f5/2} = -0.15 MeV
- The modified SDPF-MU interaction predicts the N=34 shell gap enhances to 3.9 MeV at 48Si
- The 2^+_1 level in 48Si is predicted to be located at ~3 MeV
- The central monopole matrix elements between proton 1s and neutron 1p orbits were shifted by +0.3 MeV to reproduce experimental energies in potassium isotopes

## Context

This work extends the concept of shell evolution beyond the conventional one-body potential picture, building on the monopole-based universal interaction (V_MU) developed by Otsuka et al. (2005) and the identification of tensor-force-driven shell evolution. It addresses the breakdown of traditional magic numbers such as the disappearance of N=20 (island of inversion) and emergence of N=16, and predicts the persistence of the recently established N=34 magic number (observed in 54Ca by Steppenbeck et al., 2013) towards lower proton numbers. The paper introduces "Type II shell evolution" where shell gaps depend on configuration within a single nucleus, contrasting with conventional Type I shell evolution based on filling configurations, and enables predictions for shape coexistence in nickel isotopes and new doubly magic nuclei such as 48Si to be tested at current and future radioactive-ion beam facilities.
