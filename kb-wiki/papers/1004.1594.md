# Early anisotropic hydrodynamics and the RHIC early-thermalization and HBT puzzles

> We address the problem if the early thermalization and HBT puzzles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions may be solved by the assumption that the early dynamics of the produced matter is locally anisotropic. The hybrid model describing the purely transverse hydrodynamic evolution followed by the perfect-fluid hydrodynamic stage is constructed. The transition from the transverse to perfect-fluid hydrodynamics is described by the Landau matching conditions applied at a fixed proper time. The global fit to the RHIC data reproduces the soft hadronic observables (the pion, kaon, and the proton spectra, the pion and kaon elliptic flow, and the pion HBT radii) with the accuracy of about 20%. These results indicate that the assumption of the very fast thermalization may be relaxed. In addition, the presented model suggests that a large part of the inconsistencies between the theoretical and experimental HBT results may be removed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the early-thermalization and HBT puzzles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be resolved by assuming the produced matter is initially only locally isotropic in the transverse direction, with a delayed transition to full three-dimensional equilibrium, rather than requiring instantaneous thermalization at very early times.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid model with two stages: first, a "transverse hydrodynamics" stage (proper time τᵢ ≤ τ ≤ τₜᵣ) where only transverse degrees of freedom are thermalized and longitudinal pressure is zero; second, a perfect-fluid hydrodynamic stage (τ > τₜᵣ) with full three-dimensional isotropy. The transition at fixed proper time τₜᵣ is implemented via Landau matching conditions that conserve energy flux. The model uses boost-invariant initial conditions from the Glauber model (with a mixed wounded-nucleon and binary-collision source term), a 2D equation of state for gluons, and a modern 3D equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD and hadron-gas calculations. Freeze-out occurs on a constant-temperature hypersurface, and observables are computed using the Monte-Carlo code THERMINATOR.

## Key numbers

* The transition time from transverse to perfect-fluid hydrodynamics is τₜᵣ = 1.0 fm, with initialization time τᵢ = 0.25 fm.
* The freeze-out temperature is fixed at T₃f = 140 MeV.
* The global fit to RHIC data (pion, kaon, and proton spectra; pion and kaon elliptic flow; pion HBT radii) reproduces observables with an accuracy of about 20%.
* Pion spectra and elliptic flow v₂ are described with accuracy of about 10% and 20%, respectively; HBT radii are reproduced at the level of about 10%.
* The proton elliptic flow is 50% larger than the measured value.
* The parameter κ (binary-collision admixture) is set to 0.14, following PHOBOS studies.
* The normalization constant n₀ = 0.43 is used.
* Chemical potentials at freeze-out: μ_B = 28.5 MeV, μ_S = 6.9 MeV, μ_{I₃} = -0.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier transverse-hydrodynamics models by Bialas (2007) and Chojnacki (2007), which showed that purely transverse thermalization could reproduce RHIC transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow, and by Ryblewski and Florkowski (2009) on HBT radii. It contrasts with standard perfect-fluid hydrodynamics (e.g., Kolb 2003, Huovinen 2003) that assume very fast thermalization (τᵢ ~ 0.2 fm) and with approaches using modified initial conditions for the HBT puzzle (Broniowski 2008). The model enables a relaxation of the early-thermalization assumption and suggests that a large part of the HBT puzzle can be resolved without invoking viscous hydrodynamics at very early stages, though the authors note that neglecting the hadronic rescattering phase likely causes the overprediction of proton elliptic flow.
