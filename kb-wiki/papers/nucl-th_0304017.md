# Near-threshold $蠅$ and $蠒$ meson productions in $pp$ collisions

> Using a relativistic effective Lagrangian at the hadronic level, near-threshold $蠅$ and $蠒$ meson productions in proton proton ($pp$) collisions, $p p \to p p 蠅/蠒$, are studied within the distorted wave Born approximation. Both initial and final state $pp$ interactions are included. In addition to total cross section data, both $蠅$ and $蠒$ angular distribution data are used to constrain further the model parameters. For the $p p \to p p 蠅$ reaction we consider two different possibilities: with and without the inclusion of nucleon resonances. The nucleon resonances are included in a way to be consistent with the $蟺^- p \to 蠅n$ reaction. It is shown that the inclusion of nucleon resonances can describe the data better overall than without their inclusion. However, the SATURNE data in the range of excess energies $Q < 31$ MeV are still underestimated by about a factor of two. As for the $p p \to p p 蠒$ reaction it is found that the presently limited available data from DISTO can be reproduced by four sets of values for the vector and tensor $蠒NN$ coupling constants. Further measurements of the energy dependence of the total cross section near threshold energies should help to constrain better the $蠒NN$ coupling constant.

## Key claim

The authors show that including nucleon resonances improves the description of $pp \to pp\omega$ data compared to calculations without resonances, but the SATURNE data at excess energies $Q < 31$ MeV remain underestimated by about a factor of two even with resonances included. For $pp \to pp\phi$, the limited DISTO data can be reproduced by four different sets of vector and tensor $\phi NN$ coupling constants, meaning the coupling is not uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic effective Lagrangian within the distorted wave Born approximation, including both initial and final state $pp\) interactions, to calculate the $pp \to pp\omega/\phi$ reaction amplitudes. For the $\omega$ production, they consider two scenarios: one with only nucleonic and $\omega\rho\pi$ meson-exchange currents, and another that additionally includes contributions from four nucleon resonances ($S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $D_{13}(1700)$, $P_{13}(1720)$) constrained by the $\pi^- p \to \omega n$ reaction. For $\phi$ production, only nucleonic and mesonic currents are considered, with the $\phi NN$ coupling constants treated as free parameters fitted to the DISTO data.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for $pp \to pp\omega$ at excess energy $Q = 173$ MeV: $30.8\ \mu$b (used to normalize the model)
- The $\omega NN$ tensor-to-vector coupling ratio $\kappa_\omega \simeq -2.0$ is required to reproduce the $\omega$ angular distribution at $Q = 173$ MeV without resonances
- Cut-off parameters without resonances: $\Lambda_\rho = \Lambda_\pi = 1000$ MeV, $\Lambda_N = 1190$ MeV
- The Bonn $NN$ potential value $(g_{\omega NN})^2/4\pi = 24$ (corresponding to $g_{\omega NN} = 17.37$) can also fit the angular distribution when $\Lambda_N$ is changed to $1020$ MeV
- Using different cut-offs for vector ($\Lambda_{Nv}=1300$ MeV) and tensor ($\Lambda_{Nt}=1600$ MeV) form factors with $\kappa_\omega = -0.5$ and $\Lambda_\rho = \Lambda_\pi = 1450$ MeV enhances the near-threshold cross section by only $10$ to $20\%$
- The SATURNE data cover excess energies $Q < 31$ MeV; the model underestimates these data by about a factor of two

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of near-threshold vector meson production in $pp$ collisions by Kanzo (2000) and the T眉bingen group (Fuchs, 1998), using new COSY-TOF data at $Q = 92$ and $173$ MeV to constrain the reaction mechanism. The study is motivated by the OZI rule violation observed in $\phi/\omega$ production ratios by the DISTO Collaboration and the "missing resonances" problem in constituent quark models. The results show that nucleon resonances are needed to describe the energy dependence of the $pp \to pp\omega$ cross section, and that more data are required to determine the $\phi NN$ coupling constants uniquely. The finite $\omega$ meson width is critical for excess energies below $30$ MeV and may affect the extraction of cross sections from raw data.
