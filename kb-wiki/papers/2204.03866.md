# Configuration interaction projected density functional theory: effects of four-quasiparticle configurations and time-odd interactions

> The effects of four-quasiparticle configurations and time-odd interactions are investigated in the framework of configuration interaction projected density functional theory by taking the yrast states of 60Fe as examples. Based on the universal PC-PK1 density functional, the energies of the yrast states with spin up to 20\hbar and the available B(E2) transition probabilities are well reproduced. The yrast states are predicted to be of four-quasiparticle structure above spin I = 16\hbar. The inclusion of the time-odd interactions increases the kinetic moments of inertia and delays the appearance of the first band crossing, and, thus, improves the description of the data.

## Key claim

The inclusion of four-quasiparticle configurations and time-odd interactions in configuration interaction projected density functional theory (CI-PDFT) improves the description of high-spin yrast states in ⁶⁰Fe, but the improvement is conditional: the time-odd interactions increase kinetic moments of inertia and delay the first band crossing, while four-quasiparticle configurations become dominant only above spin I = 16ħ.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 and solve the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equation to obtain the intrinsic ground state and quasiparticle orbits for ⁶⁰Fe. They construct a configuration space of zero-, two-, and four-quasiparticle states, perform angular momentum projection, and diagonalize the Hamiltonian in the space of projected states. The time-odd components of the vector and isovector channels are included in the Hamiltonian, and the finite-range separable pairing force with strength G = 728 MeV·fm³ is used.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of ⁶⁰Fe has quadrupole deformation β = 0.24.
- The configuration space includes 30 two-quasiparticle states (21 neutron, 9 proton) and 27 four-quasiparticle states, all with a quasiparticle energy cutoff E_cut = 5.0 MeV.
- The yrast state at I = 20ħ is predicted at 25.5 MeV excitation energy without time-odd interactions, which is about 7.5 MeV higher than the experimental data.
- The 0qp state contributes 0.90 of the probability amplitude for the I = 0ħ yrast state, decreasing to 0.13 at I = 8ħ.
- The first band crossing occurs at I = 8ħ, and the second (weaker) band crossing from four-quasiparticle configurations is predicted near I = 14ħ.

## Context

This work extends the CI-PDFT framework previously introduced by Zhao (2016), which omitted four-quasiparticle configurations and time-odd interactions. The present results improve upon those earlier calculations for high-spin states and are compared with the projected shell model (PSM) calculations of Sun (2009) based on a phenomenological pairing-plus-quadrupole interaction. The study enables future applications of CI-PDFT to other neutron-rich nuclei where four-quasiparticle configurations and time-odd effects are expected to be important for describing rotational bands up to high spin.
