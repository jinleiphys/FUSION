# Proton halo of $^8$B in a microscopic model

> The experimentally predicted existence of a proton halo in $^8$B is confirmed by dynamical microscopic multiconfiguration cluster model calculations. The thickness of the proton halo in $^8$B is 0.5 $fm$ while in $^8$Li a 0.4 $fm$ thick neutron halo has been found. It is shown that the huge quadrupole moment of $^8$B partly comes from the very distortable $^7$Be core.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the existence of a proton halo in $^8$B with a thickness of 0.5 fm, but qualifies that this value is smaller than earlier claims (0.8-0.9 fm) because those earlier works did not account for core distortion; the huge quadrupole moment of $^8$B is partly due to the highly distortable $^7$Be core rather than solely the halo.

## Method

The author performed dynamical microscopic multiconfiguration cluster model calculations of the resonating group type, treating $^8$B as a three-cluster system ($\alpha + h + p$, where $h = {}^3$He) and $^8$Li as $\alpha + t + n$. All possible cluster arrangements and relevant angular momentum configurations were included, with intercluster relative motion functions expanded in tempered Gaussian basis functions and solved variationally. The Minnesota effective nucleon-nucleon interaction was used with a modified spin-orbit force, and all model parameters were fixed to independent subsystem data.

## Key numbers

- Proton halo thickness in $^8$B: 0.52 fm (present model)
- Neutron halo thickness in $^8$Li: 0.39 fm (present model)
- Three-cluster separation energy in $^8$B: 1.645 MeV (theory) vs. 1.725 MeV (experiment)
- Three-cluster separation energy in $^8$Li: 4.192 MeV (theory) vs. 4.501 MeV (experiment)
- Quadrupole moment of $^8$B: 6.58 $e$ fm$^2$ (present model)
- Quadrupole moment of $^8$Li: 2.25 $e$ fm$^2$ (present model)
- Point nucleon rms radius of $^8$B: 2.57 fm (present model)
- Point proton rms radius of $^8$B: 2.74 fm (present model)
- Point neutron rms radius of $^8$B: 2.25 fm (present model)

## Context

This work addresses a dispute between Minamisono (1992), who claimed a 0.8 fm proton halo in $^8$B, and Tanihata (1992), who predicted no proton halo or one thinner than 0.2 fm based on a relativistic mean field model. It also contrasts with Kitagawa and Sagawa (1993), who found a 0.9 fm proton halo in a shell model calculation. The present results support the suggestion by Riisager and Jensen (1993) that core distortion contributes to the large quadrupole moment. The model enables future work to investigate why the 0.5 fm proton halo in $^8$B does not fit the systematic trend of halo thickness versus Fermi energy difference established by Tanihata (1992).
