# Quadrupolar correlations and deformation effect on two neutrino $\varepsilon β^{+}$ and $\varepsilon \varepsilon $ modes of $^{156}$Dy isotope

> The two-neutrino positron double-$β$ decay modes of $^{156}$Dy isotope are studied in the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework for the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ transition. Theoretically calculated half-lives of electron-positron conversion and double-electron capture modes are presented. The effect of the quadrupolar deformation on nuclear transition matrix element $M_{2ν}$ is also investigated.

# Wiki Page: Quadrupolar correlations and deformation effect on two neutrino εβ⁺ and εε modes of ¹⁵⁶Dy isotope

## Key claim

The Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model, using a Hamiltonian with only pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions and employing the summation method to avoid explicit treatment of intermediate odd-odd nuclei, predicts half-lives for the two-neutrino electron-positron conversion and double-electron capture modes of ¹⁵⁶Dy that are consistent with an inverse correlation between the nuclear transition matrix element M₂ν and the quadrupole deformation, but the calculated NTME is smaller by a factor of approximately 4.4 than the pseudo-SU(3) model result.

## Method

The authors generate nuclear wave functions for the parent ¹⁵⁶Dy and daughter ¹⁵⁶Gd nuclei in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework using a Hamiltonian with single-particle energies, pairing interaction (strength Gₚ = Gₙ = 30/A MeV), and quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interaction, with the proton-neutron component χₚₙ fixed by reproducing the excitation energy E₂⁺ of the 2⁺ state. The nuclear transition matrix element M₂ν for the 0⁺ → 0⁺ transition is evaluated using the summation method, which avoids explicit summation over intermediate odd-odd nuclear states by exploiting the commutation of the Gamow-Teller operator with the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. Half-lives are then obtained by combining M₂ν with phase space factors calculated following the formalism of Doi et al. (1992).

## Key numbers

- Phase space factor for the εβ⁺ mode: G₂ν(εβ⁺) = 4.723 × 10⁻²³ y⁻¹
- Phase space factor for the εε mode: G₂ν(εε) = 2.962 × 10⁻²⁰ y⁻¹
- Calculated NTME M₂ν = 0.0138 (dimensionless)
- Half-life for εβ⁺ mode: T₁/₂²ⁿ(εβ⁺) = (1.115 – 2.819) × 10²⁶ y for gₐ = (1.261 – 1.0)
- Half-life for εε mode: T₁/₂²ⁿ(εε) = (1.778 – 4.496) × 10²³ y for gₐ = (1.261 – 1.0)
- Deformation parameters: β₂ = 0.2929 ± 0.0016 for parent ¹⁵⁶Dy; β₂ = 0.3376 ± 0.0018 for daughter ¹⁵⁶Gd
- Ratio D₂ν (M₂ν at zero deformation to full deformation) = 13.64
- Extracted closure double Gamow-Teller matrix element M_DGT²ⁿ = 0.3975
- Average energy denominator E_d = 14.72 MeV (compared to 13.99 MeV recommended by Haxton and Stephenson)

## Context

This work extends the PHFB model, previously applied successfully to β⁻β⁻ decay modes by Chandra et al. (2005), Shukla et al. (2005), Raina et al. (2006), and Singh et al. (2007), to the less-studied e⁺ββ decay modes of ¹⁵⁶Dy. The only prior theoretical calculation for the εε mode of ¹⁵⁶Dy was in the pseudo-SU(3) model by Cerenius et al. (1999), which gave an M₂ν larger by a factor of about 4.4. The study confirms the inverse correlation between M₂ν and quadrupole deformation previously observed in β⁻β⁻ decay, and shows that the NTME is maximized when parent and daughter nuclei have similar deformations, consistent with findings in the Interacting Shell Model by Caurier et al. (2008) and Menendez et al. (2008). The results provide testable predictions for future experimental searches, as no experimental half-lives for these modes in ¹⁵⁶Dy currently exist.
