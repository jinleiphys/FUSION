# Suppression of the repulsive force in nuclear interactions near the chiral phase transition

> We introduce an effective chiral Lagrangian with a dilaton responsible for the trace anomaly in QCD. As the "dilaton limit" is taken, which drives a system to near chiral restoration density, a linear sigma model emerges from the highly non-linear structure. A striking prediction is that the vector-meson--nucleon interaction gets strongly suppressed when the dilaton limit is approached. Its phenomenological implications for the thermodynamics of dense baryonic matter are briefly discussed.

# Suppression of the repulsive force in nuclear interactions near the chiral phase transition

## Key claim

The vector-meson–nucleon interaction is strongly suppressed when the system approaches the "dilaton limit" (κ = g_A = g_V = 1), which drives the system toward chiral restoration density; this suppression occurs for any finite value of the hidden local symmetric gauge coupling g, and the effect is independent of whether nucleons are assigned chirality in the "naive" or mirror scheme.

## Method

The author constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian that includes a dilaton field to implement the QCD trace anomaly, using both a "soft" dilaton (locked to the quark condensate) and a "hard" dilaton (representing the non-vanishing gluon condensate). Starting from a hidden local symmetric Lagrangian with nucleons, the dilaton limit is taken by requiring κ = g_A = g_V = 1, which transforms the non-linear chiral Lagrangian into a linear sigma model and eliminates singularities in the chiral symmetric phase. The resulting Lagrangian is then analyzed for its predictions on vector-meson–nucleon couplings and their density dependence.

## Key numbers

- The Yukawa coupling for vector mesons (ρ, ω) to nucleons scales as g_VN^2 = (g (1 − g_V))^2, which vanishes as g_V → 1 in the dilaton limit.
- The ratio g_VN^2 / m_V^2, which governs the two-body repulsive force, goes as (1 − g_V)^2.
- The symmetry energy S_sym is proportional to g_ρN^2 and thus also gets suppressed.
- The chiral-invariant nucleon mass m_0 is estimated at 210 MeV from thermodynamic considerations and lattice gluon condensate calculations.
- The paper references a neutron star mass of 1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙ as a constraint that a softened equation of state must satisfy.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Beane and van Kolck (1994) for embedding conformal invariance in chiral Lagrangians, and builds on the dilaton-implemented hidden local symmetric framework developed by Sasaki, Lee, Paeng, and Rho (2011). It also connects to the half-skyrmion phase at high density studied by Park, Min, Rho, and Vento (2011). The prediction of suppressed repulsive forces offers a mechanism to soften the equation of state at high density without invoking exotic matter such as kaon condensation or strange quark matter, potentially reconciling nucleon-only equations of state with the observed 1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙ neutron star data.
