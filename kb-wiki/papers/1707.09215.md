# Multicomponent van der Waals equation of state: Applications in nuclear and hadronic physics

> A generalization of the quantum van der Waals equation of state for a multi-component system in the grand canonical ensemble is proposed. The model includes quantum statistical effects and allows to specify the parameters characterizing repulsive and attractive forces for each pair of particle species. The model can be straightforwardly applied to the description of asymmetric nuclear matter and also for mixtures of interacting nucleons and nuclei. Applications of the model to the equation of state of an interacting hadron resonance gas are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalization of the quantum van der Waals equation of state for multi-component systems in the grand canonical ensemble, allowing independent specification of repulsive and attractive interaction parameters for each pair of particle species, but the actual applications shown are limited to toy models and illustrative scenarios rather than fully realistic nuclear matter descriptions.

## Method

The authors start from the classical multi-component van der Waals pressure in the canonical ensemble and reconstruct the free energy, then replace the classical ideal gas free energies with Fermi-Dirac or Bose-Einstein quantum statistical expressions. They transform the formulation to the grand canonical ensemble by solving systems of transcendental equations for effective chemical potentials, using the Gibbs criterion to select the physical solution when multiple solutions exist. The formalism is then applied to three examples: asymmetric nuclear matter as a proton-neutron mixture, a mixture of nucleons and alpha particles, and a hadron resonance gas with flavor-dependent van der Waals interactions.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: binding energy per nucleon epsilon/n_N - m_N = -16 MeV at saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, with van der Waals parameters a_NN = 329 MeV fm^3 and b_tilde_NN = 3.42 fm^3
- For the fully symmetric scenario (a_pp = a_pn = a_NN and b_tilde_pp = b_tilde_pn = b_tilde_NN), the symmetry energy J = 18 MeV, which underestimates the empirical range J = 25-35 MeV
- For the nucleon-alpha mixture at the critical point: T_c = 19.90 MeV, mu_c = 907.56 MeV, n_c = 0.0733 fm^{-3}, p_c = 0.562 MeV fm^{-3}, with alpha mass fraction X_alpha = 0.013 (1.3%)
- For pure nucleon matter at the critical point: T_c = 19.68 MeV, mu_c = 907.67 MeV, n_c = 0.0723 fm^{-3}, p_c = 0.525 MeV fm^{-3}
- For strange baryons in the hadron resonance gas model: a_S = a_NS/8 = 41 MeV fm^3 and b_tilde_S = b_tilde_NS/8 = 0.43 fm^3, with effective hard-core radius half that of non-strange baryons

## Context

This work extends the single-component quantum van der Waals model for nuclear matter developed by Vovchenko (2015) to multi-component systems, and generalizes the quantum van der Waals hadron resonance gas model from Vovchenko (2016) to include flavor-dependent interactions. The formalism enables future studies of asymmetric nuclear matter with isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions, mixtures of nucleons with light nuclei such as alpha particles, and improved comparisons with lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential. The excluded volume interactions for clusters had previously been used in relativistic mean field models by Lattimer (1991), Shen (1998), and Typel (2016), but the present work provides a unified van der Waals framework.
