# Probing Vector Mesons in Deuteron Break-up Reactions

> We study vector meson photoproduction from the deuteron at high momentum transfer, accompanied by break-up of the deuteron into a proton and neutron. The large $-t$ involved allows one of the nucleons to be identified as struck, and the other as a spectator to the $γN\rightarrow VN$ subprocess. Corrections to the plane wave impulse approximation involve final state interactions (FSIs) between the struck nucleon or the vector meson, either of which is energetic, with the slow spectator nucleon. In this regime, the eikonal approximation is valid, so is employed to calculate the cross-section for the reaction. Due to the high-energy nature of the FSIs, the maxima of the rescatterings are located at nearly transverse directions of the fast hadrons. This results in two peaks in the angular distribution of the spectator nucleon, each corresponding to either the $V$-$N$ or the $p$-$n$ rescattering. The $V$-$N$ peak provides a new means of probing the $V$-$N$ interaction. This is checked for near-threshold $ϕ$ and $J/Ψ$ photoproduction reactions which demonstrate that the $V$-$N$ peak can be used to extract the largely unknown amplitudes of $ϕ$-$N$ and $J/Ψ$-$N$ interactions. Two additional phenomena are observed when extending the calculation of $J/Ψ$ photoproduction to the sub-threshold and high-energy domains. In the first case we observe overall suppression of FSI effects due to a restricted phase space for sub-threshold production in the rescattering amplitude. In the second, we observe cancellation of the $V$-$N$ rescattering amplitudes for vector mesons produced off of different nucleons in the deuteron.

## Key claim

The authors propose that vector meson photoproduction from the deuteron with subsequent break-up into a proton and neutron, at high momentum transfer, can be used to extract the largely unknown amplitudes of vector meson-nucleon (V-N) interactions. This is achieved by identifying a distinct peak in the angular distribution of the spectator nucleon that corresponds specifically to V-N rescattering, provided the eikonal approximation is valid and the principal value contributions to the rescattering amplitudes are negligible.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross-section for the reaction γ + d → V + p + n within the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), expanding the Feynman amplitude into plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and single and double rescattering terms. They use the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) to relate the covariant d→pn transition amplitude to the deuteron wave-function, and evaluate the pole contributions of the fast hadron propagators to express the rescattering amplitudes through on-shell hadronic scattering amplitudes. The method is applied numerically to near-threshold φ(1020) and J/Ψ photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer threshold for establishing the eikonal regime is set at -t ≥ 1 GeV² for light vector mesons, with a specific calculation shown for t = -1.2 GeV² at a photon energy of 5 GeV for φ meson photoproduction.
- For J/Ψ photoproduction, the near-threshold calculation uses a photon energy of 10 GeV (threshold is 8.2 GeV) and t = t_thr. = -2.23 GeV².
- The VNA is considered reliable for deuteron internal momenta up to approximately 700 MeV/c, which serves as a kinematical limit for the calculations.
- The spectator neutron momentum values used in the kinematic figures are 200, 400, and 600 MeV, demonstrating the dependence of the struck nucleon and vector meson momenta on the recoil nucleon direction.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of coherent vector meson photoproduction from the deuteron at large -t (Anderson 1971, Frankfurt 1997, Rogers 2005, Mibe 2007), which used the γd→Vd' reaction to probe ρ-N and φ-N cross-sections but had limited success in disentangling parameters such as the total cross-section, slope factor, and the ratio of real to imaginary parts of the amplitude. The new approach using deuteron break-up reactions builds on the GEA formalism previously applied to deuteron electrodisintegration (Sargsian 2009) and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (Cosyn 2010), and the predicted p-n rescattering peak was experimentally confirmed in high momentum transfer deuteron break-up reactions (Egiyan 2007, Boeglin 2011). The method enables future extraction of V-N scattering amplitudes without relying on vector meson dominance assumptions.
