# Neutron skins: A perspective from dispersive optical models

> An overview is presented of neutron skin predictions obtained using an empirical nonlocal dispersive optical model (DOM). The DOM links both scattering and bound-state experimental data through a subtracted dispersion relation which allows for fully-consistent, data-informed predictions for nuclei where such data exists. Large skins were predicted for both ${}^{48}$Ca ($R^{48}_\textrm{skin}=0.25 \pm 0.023$ fm in 2017) and $^{208}$Pb ($R^{208}_\textrm{skin}=0.25 \pm 0.05$ fm in 2020). While the DOM prediction in $^{208}$Pb is within 1$σ$ of the subsequent PREX-2 measurement, the DOM prediction in $^{48}$Ca is over 2$σ$ larger than the thin neutron skin resulting from CREX. From the moment it was revealed, the thin skin in ${}^{48}$Ca has puzzled the nuclear-physics community as no adequate theories simultaneously predict both a large skin in ${}^{208}$Pb and a small skin in ${}^{48}$Ca. The DOM is unique in its ability to treat both structure and reaction data on the same footing, providing a unique perspective on this $R_\textrm{skin}$ puzzle. It appears vital that more neutron data be measured in both the scattering and bound-state domain for ${}^{48}$Ca to clarify the situation.

## Key claim

The dispersive optical model (DOM) predicts a neutron skin thickness for ⁴⁸Ca that is over 2σ larger than the CREX measurement, while its prediction for ²⁰⁸Pb falls within 1σ of the PREX-2 result; no existing theory simultaneously reproduces both a thick skin in ²⁰⁸Pb and a thin skin in ⁴⁸Ca, and the DOM's unique ability to treat structure and reaction data on the same footing suggests that additional neutron scattering and bound-state data for ⁴⁸Ca are needed to resolve the puzzle.

## Method

The authors employed a nonlocal dispersive optical model (DOM) that constrains the nucleon self-energy by fitting elastic differential cross sections, analyzing powers, reaction cross sections, total cross sections, charge density, energy levels, particle number, and root-mean-square charge radius through weighted χ² minimization. The self-energy is parametrized with nonlocal Hartree-Fock, spin-orbit, and wine-bottle-shaped terms plus nonlocal volume, surface, and spin-orbit imaginary terms, with asymmetry-dependent terms added for ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb; the Dyson equation then yields the single-particle propagator from which bound-state and scattering observables are deduced. For ⁴⁸Ca, the isoscalar part was determined from the nearby ⁴⁰Ca nucleus, while for ²⁰⁸Pb both isoscalar and isovector parameters were varied starting from the ⁴⁸Ca parameters.

## Key numbers

- ⁴⁸Ca neutron skin thickness: R_skin = 0.25 ± 0.023 fm (DOM prediction from 2017)
- ²⁰⁸Pb neutron skin thickness: R_skin = 0.25 ± 0.05 fm (DOM prediction from 2020)
- PREX-2 measurement for ²⁰⁸Pb: R_skin = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- CREX measurement for ⁴⁸Ca: R_skin = 0.121 ± 0.026 (exp) ± 0.024 (model) fm

## Context

This work extends the dispersive optical model approach of Mahaux and Sartor (1991) and the nonlocal DOM developments by Mahzoon et al. (2014) and Dickhoff and Charity (2017) to address the neutron skin puzzle created by the PREX-2 and CREX parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab. The DOM predictions are in tension with mean-field approaches that predict a strong positive correlation between the neutron skins of ²⁰⁸Pb and ⁴⁸Ca, and with ab initio results such as those by Hagen et al. (2016) that agree with CREX but show mild tension with PREX-2; the paper calls for additional neutron scattering and bound-state measurements on ⁴⁸Ca to clarify the situation.
