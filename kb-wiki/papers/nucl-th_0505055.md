# Momentum spectra of charmonium produced in a quark-gluon plasma

> We calculate rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of charmonium formed in high energy heavy ion collsions from incoherent recombination of charm quarks. The results are very sensitive to the corresponding distributions of the charm quarks, and thus can serve as a probe of the state of matter produced in the heavy ion collision. At one extreme we generate a set of charm pair momenta directly from pQCD amplitudes, which are appropriate if one can neglect interaction of the quarks with the medium. At the other extreme we generate momenta of charm quarks in thermal equilibrium with the expanding medium, appropriate for an extremely strong interaction. Explicit predictions are made for J/Psi formation in Au-Au interactions at RHIC. We find that for the case in which charm quark momenta are unchanged from the pQCD production calculation, both the rapidity and transverse momentum spectra of the formed J/Psi are substantially narrower than would be anticipated in scenarios which do not include the in-medium formation. In particular, the average transverse momentum of the J/Psi will exhibit a non-monotonic behavior in the progression from p-p to p-A to A-A interactions.

## Key claim

The rapidity and transverse momentum spectra of J/psi formed via incoherent recombination of charm quarks in a quark-gluon plasma are very sensitive to the corresponding distributions of the charm quarks, and can thus serve as a probe of the state of matter produced in heavy ion collisions; the authors find that for the case where charm quark momenta are unchanged from pQCD production, both the rapidity and transverse momentum spectra of the formed J/psi are substantially narrower than would be anticipated in scenarios which do not include in-medium formation.

## Method

The authors calculate J/psi formation via a kinetic model using a Boltzmann equation with competing formation and dissociation reactions, where formation involves recombination of c and anti-c quarks and dissociation involves gluon absorption. They generate charm quark momentum distributions from two extremes: NLO pQCD amplitudes (appropriate if quarks do not interact with the medium) and thermal equilibrium with transverse flow (appropriate for extremely strong interaction with the medium). The differential J/psi spectra are obtained by summing over all ccbar pairs weighted by the formation cross section derived via detailed balance from an OPE-based gluon dissociation cross section, integrated over the lifetime of the deconfined region.

## Key numbers

* Average transverse momentum squared of individual charm quarks from pQCD: 2.45 GeV^2 at RHIC energy
* Average transverse momentum squared of all ccbar pairs from pQCD: 4.88 GeV^2 at RHIC energy
* Average transverse momentum squared of diagonal ccbar pairs from pQCD: 1.20 GeV^2 at RHIC energy
* Rapidity width (Delta y) for individual charm quarks from pQCD: 1.46
* Rapidity width for diagonal ccbar pairs from pQCD: 1.25
* Rapidity width for all ccbar pairs from pQCD: 1.03
* Average transverse momentum squared of thermal charm quarks without flow at T=128 MeV: 0.47 GeV^2
* Average transverse momentum squared of thermal charm quarks with flow (beta_max=0.65, T=128 MeV): 1.35 GeV^2
* Average transverse momentum squared of thermal ccbar pairs without flow at T=128 MeV: 0.93 GeV^2
* Average transverse momentum squared of thermal ccbar pairs with flow (beta_max=0.65, T=128 MeV): 2.70 GeV^2
* Binding energy used for J/psi in the OPE cross section: approximately 600 MeV
* Hadronization temperature used in thermal model: 128 MeV (from fit to light hadrons) or 170 MeV (from coalescence study)

## Context

This work extends the kinetic formation model for charmonium in a quark-gluon plasma previously developed by Thews et al. (2001) and Thews et al. (2003), which contrasts with the statistical hadronization model of Andronic et al. (2003) and Kostyuk et al. (2003) that assumes chemical equilibrium ratios at hadronization. The calculations are motivated by the need to distinguish between these formation scenarios using momentum spectra, since initial PHENIX data from RHIC could not differentiate between them. The results enable future comparison with J/psi rapidity and transverse momentum measurements from RHIC Au-Au collisions to test whether in-medium recombination occurs, and provide predictions for the non-monotonic behavior of average transverse momentum in the progression from p-p to p-A to A-A interactions.
