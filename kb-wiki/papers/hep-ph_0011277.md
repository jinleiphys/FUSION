# Goldstone boson-nucleon dynamics: Working group summary and outlook

> We highlight some of the recent results in chiral dynamics for systems with one nucleon/baryon presented at Chiral Dynamics 2000. We outline the most urgent experimental and theoretical challenges to be tackled in the coming years.

## Key claim

The working group summary reports that recent analyses of the pion-nucleon sigma term are converging on values between 71 and 84 MeV, implying an 18-24% strange quark content in the proton, but this convergence is conditional on the choice of partial wave input and the resolution of remaining discrepancies between different analysis methods.

## Method

The working group reviewed theoretical calculations in chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) and its extensions, including heavy baryon CHPT and infrared regularization, applied to processes such as pion-nucleon scattering, nucleon form factors, electromagnetic meson production, Compton scattering, and muon capture. Experimental results were presented from low-energy pion-nucleon scattering experiments at TRIUMF and PSI measuring analyzing powers and differential cross sections, as well as parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Bates, JLab, and Mainz probing strange form factors.

## Key numbers

- Sigma term values from different analyses: Stahov reported 72 ± 2 MeV using fixed-t dispersion relations with VPI/GWU partial waves; Pavan reported 84 ± 5 MeV from the latest VPI/GWU analysis; Olsson reported 71 ± 5 MeV from a new sum rule using fixed-t dispersion relations; Sainio reported 93 MeV using VPI/GWU phases as input, compared to 60 MeV using KH80 phases
- The old KH80 value for the sigma term was 64 ± 8 MeV; the previous VPI/GWU value was 92 ± 3 MeV
- The Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy analysis favors a smaller pion-nucleon coupling constant f^2 = 0.075
- The TRIUMF radiative muon capture experiment gave a value of g_P exceeding the CHPT prediction by 50%
- The SAMPLE experiment at Bates and HAPPEX at JLab have published results on strange form factors; experiments will cover Q^2 from 0.1 to 1.0 (GeV/c)^2
- The A1 group at Mainz measured p(e,e' pi+)n and confirmed a predicted 5% axial mass discrepancy between antineutrino scattering and pion electroproduction
- The Jefferson Lab experiment on 3He(e,e') studied Q^2 from 0.03 to 1.1 GeV/c^2 for spin structure functions
- The TRIUMF CHAOS experiment covers pion kinetic energies from 15 to 67 MeV and angles from 6 to 180 degrees

## Context

This working group summary from the Chiral Dynamics 2000 conference reviews progress in chiral dynamics for single-nucleon systems, extending the earlier work of the KH80 and VPI/GWU partial wave analyses for pion-nucleon scattering. The results enable future experimental programs at JLab, Bates, and MAMI to extract strange form factors and anapole moments, and motivate complete fourth-order CHPT calculations for processes like Compton scattering and electromagnetic pion production.
