# Magnetars and the Chiral Plasma Instabilities

> We propose a possible new mechanism for a strong and stable magnetic field of compact stars due to an instability in the presence of a chirality imbalance of electrons---the chiral plasma instability. A large chirality imbalance of electrons inevitably occurs associated with the parity-violating weak process during core collapse of supernovae. We estimate the maximal magnetic field due to this instability to be of order 10^{18} G at the core. This mechanism naturally generates a large magnetic helicity from the chiral asymmetry, which ensures the stability of the large magnetic field.

# Magnetars and the Chiral Plasma Instabilities

## Key claim
The authors propose that the chiral plasma instability, driven by a chirality imbalance of electrons inevitably produced during supernova core collapse, can generate magnetic fields up to approximately 10^18 G at the core of compact stars. This estimate assumes no dissipation of energy and perfect conversion efficiency, and the authors note that the actual magnetic field may be smaller due to conductivity, nuclear dynamics, and other complications.

## Method
The authors estimate the chiral chemical potential μ₅ of electrons from the neutron density produced during core collapse via the parity-violating weak process p + e_L⁻ → n + ν_L^e, assuming the chiral density equals the increased neutron density. They then apply energy conservation between the chiral asymmetry energy density and magnetic field energy density, and helicity conservation via the anomaly relation ∂_μ j^{μ5} = (2α/π) E·B, to compute the maximal magnetic field and magnetic helicity generated by the chiral plasma instability.

## Key numbers
- Maximal magnetic field at the core: B_max ∼ Λ² ∼ 10^18 G, where Λ = 200 MeV is the QCD scale
- Surface magnetic field estimate: B_surface ∼ 10^16 G for a core-to-star radius ratio R_core/R_star ∼ 10⁻¹
- Neutron density increase during core collapse: Δn_n ∼ 0.1–1 fm⁻³
- Chiral chemical potential estimate: μ₅ ∼ Λ ∼ 200 MeV
- Chiral plasma instability time scale: τ_inst ∼ (α² μ₅)⁻¹, where α is the fine structure constant
- Chirality flipping rate due to electron mass: Γ_mass ∼ α² (m_e/μ)² μ₅, with (μ/m_e)² ≫ 1 making this effect minor

## Context
This work extends the chiral plasma instability concept from Akamatsu and Yamamoto (2013) and earlier electroweak theory work by Redlich and Wijewardhana (1984) and Rubakov and Tavkhelidze (1985), as well as Joyce and Shaposhnikov (1997) on primordial magnetic fields. It addresses the long-standing problem in magnetar formation that purely poloidal magnetic fields are unstable (Wright, 1973; Braithwaite and Spruit, 2005) and that previous mechanisms like the fossil field or dynamo hypothesis (Harding and Lai, 2006; Spruit, 2007) cannot sustain strong fields. The mechanism provides a microscopic origin for magnetic helicity, which Spruit (2007) suggested could stabilize magnetic fields, and does not require exotic phases such as ferromagnetic nuclear matter, ferromagnetic quark matter (Tatsumi, 1999), or pion domain walls (Son and Stephanov, 2007). Future work requires magnetohydrodynamic simulations for chiral plasmas to reach a definite conclusion.
