# Improved constraints on chiral SU(3) dynamics from kaonic hydrogen

> A new improved study of K^- - proton interactions near threshold is performed using coupled-channels dynamics based on the next-to-leading order chiral SU(3) meson-baryon effective Lagrangian. Accurate constraints are now provided by new high-precision kaonic hydrogen measurements. Together with threshold branching ratios and scattering data, these constraints permit an updated analysis of the complex barK N and pi Sigma coupled-channels amplitudes and an improved determination of the K^- p scattering length, including uncertainty estimates.

## Key claim

The new high-precision SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurements now provide consistent constraints that, together with threshold branching ratios and scattering data, permit an improved determination of the K^-p scattering length and reduce previous uncertainties; the analysis shows that the kaonic hydrogen data are now consistent with low-energy K^-p elastic, charge exchange, and strangeness exchange cross sections, resolving a possible inconsistency indicated by earlier studies.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter calculations using the next-to-leading order (NLO) chiral SU(3) meson-baryon effective Lagrangian as the driving interaction kernel for ten strangeness S = -1 channels. They carried out chi-squared fits to the empirical data set in consecutive steps: starting with leading order Tomozawa-Weinberg terms, then adding direct and crossed Born terms, and finally using the complete NLO Lagrangian, with physical meson and baryon masses and empirical decay constants as inputs. The resulting T-matrix was used to compute the K^-p scattering length, threshold branching ratios, and cross sections, with systematic uncertainty analysis performed by varying input parameters within experimental error ranges.

## Key numbers

- Kaonic hydrogen energy shift: Delta E = 283 +/- 36 (stat) +/- 6 (syst) eV (SIDDHARTA measurement)
- Kaonic hydrogen width: Gamma = 541 +/- 89 (stat) +/- 22 (syst) eV (SIDDHARTA measurement)
- Best-fit NLO result for K^-p scattering length: a(K^-p) = -0.70 + i 0.89 fm
- Deduced K^-p scattering length from SIDDHARTA: Re a(K^-p) = -0.65 +/- 0.10 fm, Im a(K^-p) = 0.81 +/- 0.15 fm
- Upper pole position (Kbar N-dominated): 1424 - i 26 MeV
- Lower pole position (pi Sigma-dominated): 1381 - i 81 MeV
- Best-fit chi-squared per degree of freedom: 0.96 (NLO), compared to 1.12 (TW only) and 1.15 (TW + Born)
- Input decay constants: f_pi = 92.4 MeV, f_K = 110.0 MeV, f_eta = 118.8 MeV
- Threshold branching ratios from NLO fit: gamma = 2.37, R_n = 0.19, R_c = 0.66

## Context

This work extends previous chiral SU(3) coupled-channels analyses by Kaiser (1995) and Borasoy et al. (2005, 2006), which gave strong indications of a possible inconsistency between the DEAR kaonic hydrogen data (Beer 2005) and low-energy K^-p elastic scattering cross sections. The new SIDDHARTA measurements (Bazzi 2011) provide significantly more accurate constraints that resolve this inconsistency. The analysis confirms the two-poles scenario of the Lambda(1405) resonance proposed by Jido et al. (2003) and Hyodo and Weise (2008), and enables improved predictions for subthreshold extrapolations of the Kbar N amplitude and pi Sigma invariant mass spectra.
