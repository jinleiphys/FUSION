# Composite bosons and quasiparticles in a number conserving approach

> I recently proposed a method of bosonization valid for systems of an even number of fermions whose partition function is dominated at low energy by bosonic composites. This method respects all symmetries, in particular fermion number conservation. I extend it to treat odd systems and excitations involving unpaired fermions.

# Composite bosons and quasiparticles in a number conserving approach

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously proposed bosonization method, which respects fermion number conservation, from systems with an even number of fermions to also treat odd systems and excitations involving unpaired fermions, but only for states where the number of composites is much smaller than the index of nilpotency Omega and the number of quasiparticles is much smaller than Omega.

## Method

The author starts from the partition function of a fermion system and introduces Bogoliubov transformations with time-dependent expansion coefficients b_K,t to define new coherent states built from quasiparticle and composite boson creation operators. The trace over fermion Fock space is evaluated to produce an effective action containing both bosonic variables b*,b and Grassmann variables alpha*,alpha, from which effective Hamiltonians for composite bosons alone and for composite bosons plus quasiparticles are derived by expanding in inverse powers of the index of nilpotency Omega.

## Key numbers

- The index of nilpotency Omega is defined as the largest integer such that (hat_B_K)^Omega is nonzero, and is assumed independent of the quantum number K.
- For states with number of composites n much less than Omega, the non-canonical term in the composite commutation relations is of order n/Omega.
- The noncanonical temporal terms in the action are of order Omega^{-1/2} when the number of quasiparticles is much smaller than Omega and gradient terms are of order 1.
- The structure functions F_K are normalized such that tr(F_{K1}^dagger F_{K2}) = 2 Omega delta_{K1 K2}.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier number-conserving bosonization method (Palumbo 2007) for even-fermion systems, which was previously applied to relativistic field theories at zero (Cara) and nonvanishing (Palumbo 2007) fermion number. The approach contrasts with most bosonization methods that violate fermion number conservation, an important symmetry in atomic nuclei and other finite systems as noted by Duke. The formalism enables variational determination of the composite boson structure functions and provides a Hamiltonian formalism for composite bosons plus quasiparticles that respects fermion number conservation term by term.
