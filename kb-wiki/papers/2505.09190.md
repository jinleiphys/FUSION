# Neutron Decay Anomaly and Its Effects on Neutron Star Properties

> We investigate the effects of dark matter (DM) on neutron star (NS) properties using the neutron decay anomaly model within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework. Three nucleonic models (HCD0-HCD2) are developed, satisfying astrophysical constraints such as the maximum NS mass ($\geq 2 M_\odot$), the NICER mass-radius limits, and the tidal deformability constraint from the GW170817 event. The equation of states of the NS admixed with DM (DMANS) are calculated by incorporating the self-interactions between them. The macroscopic properties, such as mass, radius, and tidal deformability of the NSs, are obtained for HCD models along with five others by varying self-interaction strength. By combining NS observations with scattering cross-section constraints from galaxy clusters, we explore model-dependent trends in the DM self-interaction parameter space. While the quantitative bounds may vary with hadronic model choice, our analysis offers insights into the interplay between DM interactions and NS observables within the RMF framework.

## Key claim
The neutron decay anomaly model can be used to incorporate dark matter into neutron star calculations within the relativistic mean-field framework, but the quantitative bounds on dark matter self-interaction strength are model-dependent and vary with the choice of hadronic model.

## Method
The authors developed three new nucleonic models (HCD0-HCD2) within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework that satisfy astrophysical constraints. They then calculated the equation of state for neutron stars admixed with dark matter (DMANS) by incorporating self-interactions between the dark matter and the nuclear matter, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain macroscopic properties for these models and five others.

## Key numbers
- The three HCD models (HCD0-HCD2) were developed to satisfy the astrophysical constraint of a maximum neutron star mass of at least 2 solar masses ($\geq 2 M_\odot$).
- The models also satisfy the NICER mass-radius limits and the tidal deformability constraint from the GW170817 event.
- The analysis combines neutron star observations with scattering cross-section constraints from galaxy clusters to explore the dark matter self-interaction parameter space.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of dark matter in neutron stars by specifically incorporating the neutron decay anomaly model within the RMF framework, building on the approach of using multiple hadronic models to explore model dependence. The results enable future observational constraints from neutron star data to be used to probe the properties of dark matter self-interactions, though the quantitative bounds will depend on the chosen hadronic model.
