# Analysis of dilepton production in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach

> We address dilepton production in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV by employing the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach. Within the PHSD one goes beyond the quasiparticle approximation by solving generalized transport equations on the basis of the off-shell Kadanoff-Baym equations for the Green's functions in the phase-space representation. The approach consistently describes the full evolution of a relativistic heavy-ion collision from the initial hard scatterings and string formation through the dynamical deconfinement phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) as well as hadronization and to the subsequent interactions in the hadronic phase. {With partons described in the PHSD by the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) - matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium} - we calculate, in particular, the dilepton radiation from partonic interactions through the reactions q+qbar->gamma^*, q+qbar->gamma^*+g and q+g->gamma^*+q (qbar+g->gamma^*+qbar) in the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. By comparing our results to the data from the PHENIX Collaboration, we study the relative importance of different dilepton production mechanisms and point out the regions in phase space where partonic channels are dominant. Furthermore, explicit predictions are presented for dileptons within the acceptance of the STAR detector system and compared to the preliminary data.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport approach, which includes partonic dilepton sources from quark-gluon plasma interactions, can account for the PHENIX data in the intermediate mass region (1–4 GeV) but fails to reproduce the observed excess in the low mass region (0.2–0.6 GeV) for central Au+Au collisions, contrary to earlier expectations that partonic signals would dominate there.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach, which solves generalized transport equations based on the Kadanoff-Baym equations for Green's functions, going beyond the quasiparticle approximation. Partons were described by the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) matched to lattice QCD results, and dilepton production was calculated from partonic channels (q+qbar→γ*, q+qbar→γ*+g, q+g→γ*+q) and hadronic sources including vector meson decays, Dalitz decays, and multi-meson channels (π+ω, π+a1, ρ+ρ). The calculations were compared to PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV under specific acceptance cuts.

## Key numbers

- The partonic channels (dominated by q+qbar→e+e-) constitute about half of the dilepton yield in the intermediate mass region 1–4 GeV, with the partonic yield dominating over D-meson contributions for M > 2.5 GeV
- The PHSD calculations underestimate the PHENIX data in the mass region 0.2–0.6 GeV by approximately a factor of 5 for central Au+Au collisions
- The partonic QGP phase in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV dominates for about 5–7 fm/c
- The missing dilepton yield for masses 0.15–0.6 GeV is due to a severe underestimation of data at low pT by up to an order of magnitude
- For the mass bins [0,100 MeV] and [810 MeV, 990 MeV], the PHSD can well describe the dilepton spectra

## Context

This work extends previous HSD transport calculations by Bratkovskaya (2008) that underestimated PHENIX data in the low mass region by a factor of 5, and earlier PHSD studies by Linnyk (2011) for In+In collisions at SPS energies. The results contradict the suggestion by Shuryak (1978) that thermal QGP dileptons would be observable at masses 0.3–0.6 GeV, as the PHSD calculations show partonic channels are subleading there. The work enables future studies to identify the missing low-pT dilepton source at RHIC energies, potentially requiring new physics beyond the current transport description.
