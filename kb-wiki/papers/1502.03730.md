# Parton energy loss and momentum broadening at NLO in high temperature QCD plasmas

> We present an overview of a perturbative-kinetic approach to jet propagation, energy loss, and momentum broadening in a high temperature quark-gluon plasma. The leading-order kinetic equations describe the interactions between energetic jet-particles and a non-abelian plasma, consisting of on-shell thermal excitations and soft gluonic fields. These interactions include 2<->2 scatterings, collinear bremsstrahlung, and drag and momentum diffusion. We show how the contribution from the soft gluonic fields can be factorized into a set of Wilson line correlators on the light cone. We review recent field-theoretical developments, rooted in the causal properties of these correlators, which simplify the calculation of the appropriate Wilson lines in thermal field theory. With these simplifications lattice measurements of transverse momentum broadening have become possible, and the kinetic equations describing parton transport have been extended to next-to-leading order in the coupling g.

# Parton energy loss and momentum broadening at NLO in high temperature QCD plasmas

## Key claim

The paper presents a perturbative-kinetic framework showing that jet propagation, energy loss, and momentum broadening in a high-temperature quark-gluon plasma can be described by a Boltzmann equation at both leading and next-to-leading order in the coupling g, provided that the contributions from soft gluonic fields are factorized into Wilson line correlators on the light cone and that the separation scale dependence between hard 2-to-2 scatterings and soft diffusion processes cancels exactly.

## Method

The authors formulate a Boltzmann equation for high-momentum gluons (p >> T) traversing a weakly coupled QGP, incorporating three leading-order processes: 2-to-2 collisions between jet and hard thermal particles, drag and momentum diffusion induced by soft classical gluonic fields (described via a Fokker-Planck or Langevin equation), and collinear bremsstrahlung induced by transverse momentum diffusion. The soft field contributions are expressed as correlators of Wilson lines on the light cone, which are evaluated using Hard Thermal Loop effective theory. At next-to-leading order, corrections to the diffusion coefficients and collinear rate are computed, along with a new semi-collinear emission process, with all scale-dependent terms canceling to yield a mu-independent total rate.

## Key numbers

- Asymptotic mass squared for gluons: m_infinity^2 = (1/6) g^2 C_A T^2, derived from the integral m_infinity^2 = 2 g^2 C_A integral d^3p/(2pi)^3 n_B/p
- Debye mass squared: m_D^2 = (1/3) g^2 C_A T^2, from m_D^2 = 2 g^2 C_A integral d^3p/(2pi)^3 n_p (1+n_p)/T
- Leading-log transverse diffusion coefficient from hard 2-to-2 collisions: qhat_UV(mu) = g^2 C_A T (m_D^2/(2pi)) log(T/mu)
- Leading-log longitudinal diffusion coefficient from hard 2-to-2 collisions: qhat_{L,UV}(mu) = g^2 C_A T (m_infinity^2/(2pi)) log(T/mu)
- Leading-order soft diffusion coefficients: qhat(mu) = g^2 C_A T (m_D^2/(2pi)) log(mu/m_D); qhat_L(mu) = g^2 C_A T (m_infinity^2/(2pi)) log(mu/m_infinity)
- Hard particle momentum scaling in lightcone coordinates: P^mu = (p^+, p^-, p_perp) ~ (T, g^2 T, gT), with virtuality P^2 ~ g^2 T^2
- Soft gluonic excitation momentum scaling: P^mu ~ (gT, gT, gT), with virtuality P^2 ~ (gT)^2
- Soft mode occupation number: n_B(p^0) ~ T/p^0 ~ 1/g
- Hard particle contribution to energy density: integral cutoff at p ~ gT, with soft mode contribution of order g^3 T^4 versus leading contribution of order T^4

## Context

This review extends the perturbative-kinetic approach to jet propagation developed by Blaizot and Iancu (2001) using background field gauge and gradient expansions for non-abelian Vlasov equations, and builds on the AMY formalism for collinear bremsstrahlung rates (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2002). The work enables lattice measurements of transverse momentum broadening via EQCD effective theory, as first performed by Panero, Schafer, and Ratti (2013), and provides the theoretical foundation for implementing NLO corrections into Monte Carlo event generators such as MARTINI (Schenke, Gale, Jeon, 2009). The lightcone simplifications described here make possible calculations that would otherwise be extremely intricate, and open the possibility of a factorized approach where perturbation theory handles hard scales while 3D lattice methods treat soft and ultrasoft scales.
