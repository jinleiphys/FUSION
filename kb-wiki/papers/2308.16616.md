# Revisit spin effects induced by thermal vorticity

> We revisit the spin effects induced by thermal vorticity by calculating them directly from the spin-dependent distribution functions. For the spin-1/2 particles, we give the polarization up to the first order of thermal vorticity and compare it with the usual result calculated from the spin vector. For the spin-1 particles, we find that all the non-diagonal elements vanish and there is no spin alignment up the first order of thermal vortcity. We present the spin alignment at second-order contribution from thermal vorticity. We also find that the spin effects for both Dirac and vector particles will receive extra contribution when the spin direction is associated with the particle's momentum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the standard formula for spin polarization from thermal vorticity, based on the spin vector approach, differs from the result obtained directly from spin-dependent distribution functions; for spin-1/2 particles the new result lacks a 1/m singularity and has a different structure for the acceleration term, while for spin-1 particles there is no spin alignment at first order in thermal vorticity, only at second order.

## Method

The authors compute spin-dependent distribution functions for Dirac (spin-1/2) and vector (spin-1) fields in global thermodynamic equilibrium with constant thermal vorticity, using the density operator that includes angular momentum contributions. They expand the distribution functions in powers of the thermal vorticity and acceleration, then extract the polarization (for spin-1/2) and spin density matrix elements (for spin-1) directly from the diagonal and off-diagonal components of these distributions.

## Key numbers

- For spin-1/2 particles, the local polarization along a fixed direction n₃ is given by P(x,k) = -(f'_F / 2f_F) Ω_k · n₃, where Ω_k = ω - (ε × k)/(E_k + m), with no 1/m factor present in the earlier spin-vector result.
- For spin-1 particles, all non-diagonal elements of the spin density matrix vanish at first order in thermal vorticity, meaning no spin alignment appears at O(ϖ).
- The spin alignment for vector mesons receives only second-order contributions from thermal vorticity (acceleration or vorticity squared terms).
- When the spin quantization direction is aligned with the particle's momentum (helicity basis), an extra term δΛ^{sr}_{p∥} = (E_p/2p) ε_p · (-e_φ cotθ s δ^{rs} + e_φ δ^{-r,s} - i e_θ s δ^{-r,s}) appears in the commutator, modifying the distribution.

## Context

This work revisits the theoretical foundation of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, where recent measurements of hyperon local longitudinal polarization (Becattini 2017) and vector meson spin alignment (Liang 2004) contradict predictions based on the standard spin Cooper-Frye formula. The authors derive spin-dependent distribution functions directly, rather than using the spin-vector approach of Becattini (2013), and find discrepancies that may help resolve these spin puzzles. The results enable improved calculations of polarization and spin alignment in relativistic hydrodynamic models, and complement alternative mechanisms such as shear contributions (Liu 2021, Fu 2021, Becattini 2021) and strong force fields (Sheng 2019, 2020, 2022) that have been proposed to explain the observed anomalies.
