# Baseline Cold Matter Effects on J/psi Production in AA Collisions

> We present baseline calculations of initial-state shadowing and final-state absorption effects on J/psi production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. We show predictions for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at \sqrt{S_{NN}}=200 GeV and Cu+Cu collisions at \sqrt{S_{NN}} = 62 GeV as a function of the rapidity, y, and the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions, N_{\rm coll}.

## Key claim

The paper provides baseline predictions for cold nuclear matter effects (initial-state shadowing and final-state absorption) on J/psi production in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC, under the condition that if no quark-gluon plasma is formed, these predictions should describe the rapidity and centrality dependence of J/psi production in AA collisions.

## Method

The author calculates J/psi production in the color evaporation model at leading order, using the MRST LO parton densities with m_c = 1.2 GeV and Q = M. The nuclear parton densities incorporate shadowing via the EKS98 parameterization (primarily) and three FGS parameterizations (FGSo, FGSh, FGSl), with spatial dependence modeled through inhomogeneous nuclear density profiles. Final-state absorption is treated with both color octet and color singlet absorption models, using asymptotic absorption cross sections of 0, 1, 3, and 5 mb.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(S_NN) = 200 GeV and Cu+Cu at sqrt(S_NN) = 62 GeV are studied as a function of rapidity y and number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions N_coll
- At midrapidity at 200 GeV, average x_2 ~ 0.01, near the point where the gluon shadowing ratio S_P^g <= 1
- At forward rapidity y ~ 2 at 200 GeV, average x_2 ~ 10^-3, in the low-x regime; at negative rapidity y ~ -2, average x_2 ~ 0.1, in the antishadowing region
- At 62 GeV, midrapidity average x_2 ~ 0.05, in the antishadowing region
- The color octet to singlet conversion formation time is 0.25 fm at negative rapidity
- PHENIX d+Au data suggest sigma_abs <= 3 mb at RHIC
- At 200 GeV, the AA/pp ratio shows two peaks at y ~ +/- 1.5, the location of the antishadowing peak at x ~ 0.1
- At 62 GeV, the AA/pp ratio exceeds unity at y ~ 0 due to coincident antishadowing regions
- For Au+Au at 200 GeV, the EKS98 and FGS parameterizations can be directly compared since A = 197 is available for all (except FGSo which uses A = 206)
- For Cu+Cu, FGS results use A = 40 since A = 63 is not available from FGS

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations for d+Au collisions (Vogt, 2005) which agreed relatively well with preliminary PHENIX d+Au data. It provides the necessary cold matter baseline for interpreting J/psi suppression in AA collisions at RHIC, building on fixed-target studies by NA50 where cold matter effects were attributed solely to nuclear absorption. The predictions enable experimental discrimination between cold nuclear matter effects and possible quark-gluon plasma suppression, particularly noting that lattice-based potential models suggest J/psi might not break up until T ~ 2.5 T_c, while the psi' and chi_c states (40% of yield) should break up at 0.7 < T/T_c < 1.1.
