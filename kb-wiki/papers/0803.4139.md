# Baryon enhancement in high-density QCD and relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We argue that the collinear factorization of the fragmentation functions in high energy nuclear collisions breaks down at transverse momenta $p_T \lesssim Q_s/g$ due to high parton densities in the colliding hadrons and/or nuclei. We find that gluon recombination dominates in that $p_T$ region. We calculate the inclusive cross-section for $π$ meson and nucleon production using the low energy theorems for the scale anomaly in QCD, and compare our quantitative baryon-to-meson ratio to the RHIC data.

# Baryon enhancement in high-density QCD and relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that collinear factorization of fragmentation functions breaks down at transverse momenta \(p_T \lesssim Q_s/g\) due to high parton densities, and that gluon recombination dominates in that region. The baryon-to-meson ratio is calculated using low-energy theorems for the scale anomaly in QCD, with the hedge that the model describes cold nuclear effects from gluon saturation but that thermal equilibration is achieved only through final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors model hadron production in two stages: first, production of a gluon pair in the \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\) and colour singlet state; second, recombination of this pair into final-state hadrons via anomaly matching. The inclusive cross-section for pion and nucleon production is calculated by replacing the perturbative spectral density for double-gluon production with hadronic spectral densities derived from chiral perturbation theory, including contributions from pions, nucleons, and pion-nucleon interactions. The single hadron production cross-section at high \(p_T\) is added via convolution with fragmentation functions, and the baryon-to-meson ratio is computed as a function of transverse momentum and centrality.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at RHIC: \(Q^2_s \approx 2 \times 0.284 \, N_{\rm part}^{1/3}\) GeV\(^2\) at midrapidity, based on the KLN model (Kharzeev and Levin; Kharzeev and Nardi).
- The single gluon production cross-section decreases as \(1/p_T^2\) and dominates at high \(p_T\), while the soft production channel drops as \(1/p_T^6\).
- The spectral density for pions: \(\rho_{\pi\pi}(M^2) = \frac{3}{32\pi^2} M^4\).
- The spectral density for nucleons: \(\rho_{N \bar N}(M^2) = \frac{4}{32\pi^2} m^2_N M^2\).
- The spectral density for pion-nucleon interactions scales as \(M^4\) at high invariant masses, with a full expression given in Equation (6).
- The baryon-to-meson ratio is plotted for AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV as a function of transverse momentum (0-12% central collisions) and centrality (integrated over 3 GeV \(< p_T <\) 4 GeV), with data from STAR (2006) and PHENIX preliminary results.

## Context

This work extends the Colour Glass Condensate framework and the gluon fusion mechanism proposed by Li and Tuchin (2007) for the \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\) channel, and applies anomaly matching as developed by Kharzeev and Levin. It provides a quantitative description of the baryon-to-meson ratio observed in RHIC AuAu collisions, suggesting that cold nuclear effects from gluon saturation contribute to baryon enhancement and fast chemical equilibration in the initial state. The model enables further study of how final-state interactions in the hot medium achieve thermal equilibration and may further increase the baryon-to-meson ratio.
