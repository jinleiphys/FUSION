# Entropy Production and Effective Viscosity in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Entropy production and an effective viscosity in central Au+Au collisions are estimated in a wide range of incident energies 3.3 GeV $\le \sqrt{s_{NN}}\le$ 39 GeV. The simulations are performed within a three-fluid model employing three different equations of state with and without deconfinement transition, which are equally good in reproduction of the momentum-integrated elliptic flow of charged particles in the considered energy range. It is found that more that 80\% entropy is prodused during a short early collision stage which lasts $\sim$1 fm/c at highest considered energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\ge$ 20 GeV. The estimated values of the viscosity-to-entropy ratio ($η/s$) are approximately the same in all considered scenarios. At final stages of the system expansion they range from $\sim$0.05 at highest considered energies to $\sim$0.5 lowest ones. It is found that the $η/s$ ratio decreases with the temperature ($T$) rise approximately as $\sim 1/T^4$ and exhibits a rather weak dependence on the net-baryon density.

# Entropy Production and Effective Viscosity in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The viscosity-to-entropy ratio (η/s) estimated from three-fluid dynamics simulations of central Au+Au collisions is approximately the same across three different equations of state (purely hadronic, first-order phase transition, and smooth crossover) that equally reproduce the momentum-integrated elliptic flow data, ranging from ~0.05 at the highest considered energies to ~0.5 at the lowest ones, with the caveat that this is an effective viscosity derived from entropy production in a model that does not include viscosity explicitly.

## Method

The authors performed three-fluid dynamics (3FD) simulations of central Au+Au collisions without freeze-out, using three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, and two versions involving deconfinement transition (first-order phase transition and smooth crossover). They calculated the total entropy production from the thermodynamic relation using proper entropy densities of each fluid, then estimated an effective shear viscosity by associating the entropy production rate with the viscous dissipation term in first-order (Landau-Lifshitz) dissipative hydrodynamics, assuming zero bulk viscosity and zero thermal conductivity.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: 3.3 GeV ≤ √sNN ≤ 39 GeV
- More than 80% of entropy is produced during a short early collision stage lasting ~1 fm/c at highest energies (√sNN ≥ 20 GeV)
- η/s at final expansion stages ranges from ~0.05 at highest energies to ~0.5 at lowest energies
- η/s decreases with temperature approximately as ~1/T^4
- Formation time for the f-fluid: τ = 2 fm/c for the hadronic scenario, τ = 0.17 fm/c for scenarios involving deconfinement transition
- Eulerian cell sizes used: from dx=dy=dz=0.192 fm at √sNN=3.3 GeV to dx=dy=dz=0.0261 fm at √sNN=39 GeV
- Systematic error of the 3FD entropy definition amounts to ~10% at all collision energies and scenarios
- Number of hadrons per net baryon (NB=2A=394) produced in central Au+Au collisions is presented as a function of collision energy

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-fluid dynamics study (Ivanov 2014) which found that different equations of state equally describe STAR elliptic flow data (Adamczyk 2012) at energies from √sNN=7.7 to 39 GeV, but could not explain why. The present paper quantifies the dissipation in terms of η/s to compare with other approaches such as the hybrid model analysis by Karpenko (2015), which found η/s up to 0.2 at √sNN=7.7 GeV. The results enable comparison with theoretical expectations that viscosity should rise at lower energies due to the system spending more time in the hadronic phase (Kestin 2008), and with findings that hadronic systems with large net-baryon density are closer to the ideal fluid limit (Itakura 2007, Khvorostukhin 2010, Denicol 2013, Kadam 2015).
