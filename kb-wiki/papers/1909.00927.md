# Chiral-spin symmetry of the meson spectral function above $T_c$

> Recently, via calculation of spatial correlators of $J=0,1$ isovector operators using a chirally symmetric Dirac operator within $N_F=2$ QCD, it has been found that QCD at temperatures $T_c - 3 T_c$ is approximately $SU(2)_{CS}$ and $SU(4)$ symmetric. The latter symmetry suggests that the physical degrees of freedom are chirally symmetric quarks bound by the chromoelectric field into color singlet objects without chromomagnetic effects. This regime of QCD has been referred to as a Stringy Fluid. Here we calculate correlators for propagation in time direction at a temperature slightly above $T_c$ and find the same approximate symmetries. This means that the meson spectral function is chiral-spin and $SU(4)$ symmetric.

## Key claim

The meson spectral function in Minkowski space at temperatures slightly above T_c (approximately 1.2 T_c) exhibits approximate SU(2)_CS chiral-spin and SU(4) symmetries, based on direct observation of these symmetries in temporal correlators rather than only spatial correlators as in prior work.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with N_F=2 dynamical domain wall fermions on 48^3 x 12 lattices at T=220 MeV (1.2 T_c), using the Symanzik-improved gauge action with inverse gauge coupling beta_g=4.3 (a=0.075 fm). They computed temporal correlation functions of local isovector J=0 and J=1 meson operators with momentum projection, and compared the multiplet structure of correlators from full QCD with those from free noninteracting quarks on the same lattice.

## Key numbers

- Temperature: T = 220 MeV, corresponding to approximately 1.2 T_c
- Lattice size: 48^3 x 12, with additional checks on N_s=24 and 32 volumes
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.075 fm
- Quark mass: m_ud = 0.001
- Fifth dimension length for domain wall fermions: L_5 = 16
- Symmetry breaking parameter kappa at tT=0.5: |kappa| < 0.05 for both t-correlators and z-correlators

## Context

This work extends previous findings by Rohrhofer et al. (2017, 2019) that observed approximate SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetries in spatial correlators for temperatures T_c - 3 T_c, by demonstrating the same symmetries directly in temporal correlators which are connected to the Minkowski-space spectral function via an integral transform. The results confirm that the "Stringy Fluid" regime identified in earlier spatial correlator studies (Rohrhofer 2017, 2019) also characterizes the spectral functions, and are consistent with prior observations of U(1)_A restoration in t-correlators by Cossu et al. (2015) and Tomiya et al. (2016). The findings enable future studies of QCD at finite chemical potential, since the chemical potential term shares the same symmetries.
