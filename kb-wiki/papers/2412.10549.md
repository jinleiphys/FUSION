# Bottomoniumlike states in proton collisions: Fragmentation and resummation

> We study the semi-inclusive hadroproduction of doubly bottomed tetraquarks ($X_{b\bar{b}q\bar{q}}$) as well as fully bottomed ones ($T_{4b}$), to which we collectively refer as "bottomoniumlike" states. We rely upon the variable-flavor number-scheme fragmentation at leading power, where a single parton perturbatively splits into the corresponding Fock state, which then hadronizes into the color-neutral, observed tetraquark. To this end, we build new sets of DGLAP/HF-NRevo consistent, hadron-structure oriented collinear fragmentation functions, which we name TQHL1.1 and TQ4Q1.1 parametrizations. They extend and supersede the corresponding 1.0 versions recently derived in previous works. The first family describes the fragmentation of doubly heavy tetraquarks and is based on an improved version of the Suzuki model for the heavy-quark channel. The second family depicts the fragmentation of fully heavy tetraquarks and embodies initial-scale inputs for gluon and heavy-quark channels, both of them calculated by the hands of potential nonrelativistic QCD. As a phenomenological application, we provide novel predictions for tetraquark-plus-jet high-energy distributions, computed within the NLL/NLO$^+$ hybrid factorization from (sym)JETHAD, at 14 TeV and 100 TeV FCC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the fragmentation mechanism, modeled via new collinear fragmentation functions (TQHL1.1 and TQ4Q1.1) evolved with DGLAP/HF-NRevo, provides a viable description of bottomoniumlike tetraquark production in proton collisions, but this claim is conditional on the assumption that the leading-power variable-flavor number-scheme fragmentation approach is valid and that the initial-scale inputs from the Suzuki model and potential nonrelativistic QCD are reliable, given the absence of experimental confirmation for bottom tetraquarks.

## Method

The authors constructed two families of collinear fragmentation functions (FFs) for doubly bottomed tetraquarks (X_{b\bar{b}q\bar{q}}) and fully bottomed tetraquarks (T_{4b}) using the variable-flavor number-scheme fragmentation at leading power. The TQHL1.1 FFs for doubly heavy tetraquarks were based on an improved Suzuki model for the heavy-quark channel, while the TQ4Q1.1 FFs for fully heavy tetraquarks used initial-scale inputs for gluon and heavy-quark channels calculated with potential nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD). These FFs were evolved via DGLAP equations using the HF-NRevo setup, and predictions for tetraquark-plus-jet high-energy distributions were computed within the NLL/NLO+ hybrid factorization using the (sym)JETHAD framework at 14 TeV and 100 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Predictions for tetraquark-plus-jet distributions were computed at center-of-mass energies of 14 TeV (LHC) and 100 TeV (FCC).
- The gluon collinear FF was noted to dominate in the semihard regime where proton PDFs are accessed in the range 10^{-4} less than x less than 10^{-2}.
- The production-rate hierarchy between B_c mesons and singly bottomed B mesons was confirmed to not exceed 0.1%, based on prior LHCb estimates.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TQHL1.0 and TQ4Q1.0 FF determinations (Celiberto 2023, Celiberto 2024) by improving the Suzuki model treatment and incorporating NRQCD inputs for fully heavy tetraquarks. It builds on the NRQCD-inspired fragmentation approach used for vector quarkonia (ZCW19+ FFs) and charmed B mesons (ZCFW22 FFs), and it enables future phenomenological studies of tetraquark production at the LHC and FCC, particularly in the high-energy regime where fragmentation dominates over short-distance production.
