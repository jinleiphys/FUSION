# Muonic atoms with extreme nuclear charge

> Bound muons (also pions, kaons, etc) increase the fission barrier and produce some stabilizing effects for highly charged nuclei. If the binding energy of the muon exceeds $mc^2$, it becomes stable. The $1s$ state of a muon inside an exotic nucleus with atomic number $A= 5Z/2$ and such large charge $Z$ that the $1s$ energy $E$ is in the range $0\le E<-mc^2$ is considered.

# Muonic atoms with extreme nuclear charge

## Key claim
For muons bound to extremely large nuclei (with charge Z up to ~2000), the 1s wave function becomes localized inside the nucleus and becomes practically independent of Z when the total relativistic energy E is negative, because the electrostatic potential is approximately constant in the region where the wave function resides. The authors note that these critical charges are so large that there is hardly any chance of actual stabilization of normal nuclear matter by muons, making this primarily a theoretical exercise in relativistic quantum mechanics.

## Method
The authors solved the Dirac equation for a muon in the field of a finite nucleus modeled with a standard nuclear charge density of radius R = 1.15 A^(1/3) fm and atomic number A = 5Z/2. They computed radial wave functions and densities for 1s, 2s, 3s, and 4s states for nuclear charges up to Z ≈ 2000, where the 1s energy reaches the critical value E = -mc^2. The electrostatic potential inside the nucleus was treated as having an oscillator shape V(r) = -(Ze^2/R)(3/2 - r^2/(2R^2)).

## Key numbers
- The muon-to-electron mass ratio is m_mu/m_e = 200.
- The 1s muon energy reaches E = 0 for nuclear charge Z ≈ 840.
- The critical charge for the muon 1s state (E = -mc^2) is Z_c ≈ 2000.
- For the electron case with Z = 166 (close to critical charge Z_c ≈ 170), the 1s electron energy is E = -0.796 mc^2.
- The critical charge for a very small nucleus (point-like) in the Dirac case is Z_c = 138, while for a finite nucleus it is Z_c ≈ 170.
- For spin-0 and spin-1 bosonic particles (pions, kaons), the critical charge is Z_c = 68 for a very small nucleus.
- The nuclear radius for Z = 2000 is R = 19.2 fm; for Z = 840 it is R = 14.6 fm.
- The bound muon contributes approximately 100 MeV to the nuclear fission barrier.

## Context
This work extends the well-studied problem of electron-positron pair creation in supercritical Coulomb fields (reviewed by Zeldovich and Popov, and by Greiner) to the case of heavier particles like muons. The electron case for finite nuclei gives a critical charge Z_c ≈ 170, relatively close to the point-nucleus value of 138. The muon case produces much larger critical charges (Z_c ≈ 2000) because the wave function is confined inside the nucleus where the potential is oscillator-like rather than Coulombic. The authors note that this analysis provides hints for the more interesting cases of pions and kaons, which would require additional calculations including the strong interaction. Experimental investigations of vacuum breakdown in heavy-ion collisions have been performed at GSI, with new experiments in preparation.
