# Multifragmentation at the balance energy of mass asymmetric colliding nuclei

> Using the quantum molecular dynamics model, we study the role of mass asymmetry of colliding nuclei on the fragmentation at the balance energy and on its mass dependence. The study is done by keeping the total mass of the system fixed as 40, 80, 160, and 240 and by varying the mass asymmetry of the ($η$ = $\frac{A_{T}-A_{P}}{A_{T}+A_{P}}$; where $A_{T}$ and $A_{P}$ are the masses of the target and projectile, respectively) reaction from 0.1 to 0.7. Our results clearly indicate a sizeable effect of the mass asymmetry on the multiplicity of various fragments. The mass asymmetry dependence of various fragments is found to increase with increase in total system mass (except for heavy mass fragments). Similar to symmetric reactions, a power law system mass dependence of various fragment multiplicities is also found to exit for large asymmetries.

## Key claim

The mass asymmetry of colliding nuclei has a sizeable effect on fragment multiplicities at the balance energy, with the asymmetry dependence increasing for larger total system masses (except for heavy mass fragments); a power-law system mass dependence of fragment multiplicities exists for large asymmetries, similar to that previously found for symmetric reactions by Dhawan and Puri (2008).

## Method

The quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model was used to simulate central collisions of mass-asymmetric nuclei at their theoretical balance energies, calculated using a momentum-dependent soft equation of state with an energy-dependent Cugnon cross-section. The total system mass was fixed at 40, 80, 160, and 240, while the mass asymmetry parameter η = (A_T - A_P)/(A_T + A_P) was varied from 0.1 to 0.7 for each total mass. The phase space was clusterized using the minimum spanning tree (MST) method, and reactions were followed up to 500 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Total system masses studied: A_TOT = 40, 80, 160, and 240
- Mass asymmetry parameter η varied from 0.1 to 0.7
- Gaussian width parameter L = 1.08 fm² used in the QMD model
- Yukawa potential parameters: m = 1.5 fm, t₃ = -6.66 MeV
- Momentum-dependent interaction parameters: t₄ = 1.57 MeV, t₅ = 5 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻²
- Average binding energy per nucleon for light charged particles (LCPs, 2 ≤ A ≤ 4) is approximately -4 MeV/nucleon
- Average binding energy per nucleon for intermediate mass fragments (IMFs, 4 ≤ A ≤ 30% of largest partner) is approximately -8 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the study of mass asymmetry effects on collective flow, nuclear stopping, and multifragmentation at fixed energies previously conducted by Puri and collaborators (2008-2010) to the specific case of fragmentation at the balance energy. It also builds on the analysis of symmetric colliding nuclei by Dhawan and Puri (2008), which was limited to symmetric reactions only. The results enable future studies of how the balance energy fragmentation pattern depends on both system mass and asymmetry, and may help constrain the nuclear equation of state through comparison with experimental data.
