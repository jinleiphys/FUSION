# Direct local parametrization of nuclear state densities using the back-shifted Bethe formula

> Level densities are often parametrized using the back-shifted Bethe formula (BBF) for nuclei that possess experimental data for s-wave neutron resonance average spacings and a complete discrete level sequence at low excitation energies. However, these parametrizations require the additional modeling of the dependence of the spin-cutoff parameter on excitation energy. Here we avoid the need to model the spin distribution of level densities by using the experimental data to parametrize directly the state densities, for which the BBF does not depend on the spin-cutoff parameter. This approach allows for a local parameterization of state densities that is independent of the spin-cutoff parameter. We provide these parameters in a tabulated form for applications in nuclear reaction calculations and for testing microscopic approaches to state densities.

## Key claim

The authors parametrize nuclear state densities directly using the back-shifted Bethe formula (BBF) for 294 nuclei, avoiding the need to model the energy dependence of the spin-cutoff parameter that is required in standard level-density parametrizations. The method works only for nuclei with both measured s-wave neutron resonance average spacings and a complete set of low-lying discrete levels with known spins.

## Method

The authors solve two equations simultaneously for each nucleus: one equating the BBF state density at the neutron separation energy to the experimental state density inferred from the average s-wave resonance spacing (using a spin-cutoff model with rigid-body moment of inertia), and another matching the integrated BBF state density over a chosen low-energy interval to the cumulative number of experimentally observed states (including magnetic degeneracy). The lower and upper bounds of the counting interval are adjusted until satisfactory agreement is reached between the BBF and the binned experimental state density in the level counting regime.

## Key numbers

- Parameters are provided for 294 nuclei with known neutron resonance data and complete low-lying level sequences.
- The maximum order of magnitude deviation (max-OMD) between the authors' state densities and those of Koning (2008) is below 0.25 for 185 nuclei, between 0.25 and 0.5 for 79 nuclei, and exceeds 0.5 for only 23 nuclei, evaluated over the excitation energy interval 0 to 10 MeV.
- The rigid-body moment of inertia formula used is I_rig/hbar^2 approximately 0.0137 A^(5/3) MeV^(-1).

## Context

This work extends the local parametrization approach of Dilg (1973) and von Egidy (1988) by using state densities rather than level densities, eliminating the need to model the spin-cutoff parameter's energy dependence. It provides a direct comparison with the local parametrization of Koning (2008), which uses an energy-dependent level density parameter and a Grossjean-Feldmeier modification to handle low-energy divergence. The tabulated parameters enable testing of microscopic state density calculations from shell-model Monte Carlo or other approaches, and serve as input for Hauser-Feshbach compound nucleus reaction calculations.
