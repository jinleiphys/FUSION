# Cluster virial expansion for nuclear matter within a quasiparticle statistical approach

> Correlations in interacting many-particle systems can lead to the formation of clusters, in particular bound states and resonances. Systematic quantum statistical approaches allow to combine the nuclear statistical equilibrium description (law of mass action) with mean-field concepts. A chemical picture, which treats the clusters as distinct entities, serves as an intuitive concept to treat the low-density limit. Within a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach, the quasiparticle virial expansion is extended to include arbitrary clusters, where special attention must be paid to avoid inconsistencies such as double counting. Correlations are suppressed with increasing density due to Pauli blocking. The contribution of the continuum to the virial coefficients can be reduced by considering clusters explicitly and introducing quasiparticle energies. The cluster-virial expansion for nuclear matter joins known benchmarks at low densities with those near saturation density.

# Cluster virial expansion for nuclear matter within a quasiparticle statistical approach

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a cluster-virial expansion can systematically join the low-density nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) description with the high-density quasiparticle mean-field picture, provided that double counting between bound-state and scattering-state contributions is avoided through a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach. The subdivision of correlated density into bound and scattering parts is fundamentally ambiguous, as part of the continuum contribution can be transferred to the bound state contribution by redefining the single-cluster term.

## Method

The authors extend the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to include arbitrary clusters by decomposing the nucleon self-energy into cluster contributions and introducing medium-dependent quasiparticle energies for clusters. They derive a cluster-virial expansion where the total baryon density is split into a one-body term (containing nucleons and bound clusters with Mott-screened momenta) and a two-body scattering term (containing continuum correlations between clusters). Using a Yamaguchi separable interaction potential as a model, they show analytically that the Born approximation contribution from two-body scattering is fully accounted for by Hartree-Fock self-energy shifts in the quasiparticle picture, reducing the continuum contribution to higher-order terms.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range considered: T ≤ 20 MeV, baryon density range: n ≤ n₀ with n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ (nuclear saturation density)
- Medium effects become operative when density exceeds about 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³; Pauli blocking dissolves clusters at densities about 10⁻² fm⁻³
- Yamaguchi interaction parameters used for nn scattering: γ = 1.3943 fm⁻¹, λ = 704.76 MeV·fm³; a weaker interaction with λ/2 was also tested
- The deuteron binding energy B₂,₁ appears in the single-deuteron contribution as exp(B₂,₁/T) in the NSE term, with an alternative formulation subtracting 1 to give exp(B₂,₁/T) - 1

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach to nuclear matter previously developed for two-nucleon correlations by Ropke and collaborators (2011), which gave in-medium quasiparticle energies for light nuclei A ≤ 4. It generalizes the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach originally formulated for electron-hole plasmas by Zimmermann (1985) and applies it to nuclear systems. The cluster-virial expansion builds on earlier work by Horowitz and Schwenk (2006) for the proton-neutron-alpha system and by O'Connor et al. (2007) for additional light clusters. The approach enables a unified equation of state for warm dilute nuclear matter that connects the NSE description at low densities with quasiparticle mean-field models near saturation density, relevant for astrophysical applications such as supernova simulations and neutron star crusts.
