# Direct Quark Mechanism for Weak $\Lam N \leftrightarrow NN$ Processes

> Two body weak processes $ΛN \leftrightarrow NN$ are studied from the viewpoint of quark substructure of baryons.They can be studied in nonmesonic weak decays of hypernuclei and also hyperon production in the $NN$ scattering. The direct quark mechanism in contrast to the one-pion exchange mechanism is shown to give sizable $\di=3/2$ contribution in the J=0 channel. Nonmesonic decay rates of $^4_Λ$H, $^4_Λ$He, and $^5_Λ$He agree with the current experimental data fairly well. Especially the $n/p$ ratio is enhanced by the direct quark contribution. The cross sections for $pn\to \Lam p$ scattering are studied in the quark cluster model approach.

# Direct Quark Mechanism for Weak ΛN ↔ NN Processes

## Key claim

The direct quark (DQ) mechanism, in contrast to the conventional one-pion exchange (OPE) mechanism, gives a sizable ΔI=3/2 contribution in the J=0 channel for ΛN ↔ NN processes, and this contribution significantly enhances the neutron-to-proton ratio in nonmesonic weak decays of light hypernuclei, though the agreement with experimental data is qualified by the fact that the proton-induced decay rate remains dominated by J=1 amplitudes from the OPE tensor component.

## Method

The author computed two-body weak transition potentials for ΛN (L=0) to NN (L=0,1) states using a direct quark mechanism based on an effective weak Hamiltonian for quarks with one-loop perturbative QCD corrections, evaluated in six-quark wave functions of the two-baryon systems. These DQ potentials were compared with one-pion exchange potentials, and the nonmesonic decay rates of ⁴ΛH, ⁴ΛHe, and ⁵ΛHe were calculated using realistic Λ-nucleus wave functions from YN G-matrix interactions with harmonic oscillator wave functions and short-range correlations. For the pn → Λp scattering cross sections, the author employed a full six-quark cluster model with DQ+OPE weak transitions in a distorted wave Born approximation.

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological ΛNπ weak coupling constant is |g_w| = 2.3 × 10⁻⁷ (dimensionless); the quark model calculation gives g_w = −3.5 × 10⁻⁷, about 30% larger than the phenomenological value
- For ⁵ΛHe with OPE+DQ: Γ_p = 0.421 Γ_Λ, Γ_n = 0.206 Γ_Λ, Γ_nm = 0.627 Γ_Λ, and R_np = 0.489 (compared to OPE-only values: Γ_p = 0.191, Γ_n = 0.025, R_np = 0.132)
- For ⁴ΛH with OPE+DQ: Γ_p = 0.047 Γ_Λ, Γ_n = 0.126 Γ_Λ, Γ_nm = 0.174 Γ_Λ (compared to OPE-only: Γ_p = 0.004, Γ_n = 0.017)
- The pn → Λp cross sections are of order 10⁻¹⁶ barn, with the parity-violating/parity-conserving ratio reaching as large as 60% at E_cm ≈ 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier meson-exchange studies of nonmesonic weak decays (including K, ρ, ω, K* exchanges and correlated two pions) by incorporating quark substructure effects, building on prior quark model analyses by the author and collaborators (Inoue, Takeuchi, Motoba, Itonaga). The direct quark mechanism addresses the long-standing ΔI=1/2 rule problem in nonleptonic weak interactions, showing that the ΔI=3/2 component of the weak Hamiltonian can give significant contributions in two-baryon processes even though it is suppressed in single-baryon decays. The results enable future experimental tests through high-quality measurements of ⁴ΛH decays and through soft π⁺ emissions in hypernuclear weak decays, which the author proposes as a new probe of the ΔI=3/2 component.
