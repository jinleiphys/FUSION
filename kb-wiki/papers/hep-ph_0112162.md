# Color Superconductivity with Non-Degenerate Quarks

> In dense quark matter, the response of the color superconducting gaps to a small variation, $δμ$, in the chemical potential of the strange quark was studied. The approximation of three massless flavors of quarks and a general ansatz for the color flavor structure of the gap matrix was used. The general pole structure of the quasi-particle propagator in this ansatz is presented. The gap equation was solved using both an NJL interaction model and perturbative single gluon exchange at moderate densities and results are presented for varying values of $δμ$. Quantitative and qualitative differences in the dependence of the gaps on $δμ$ were found.

## Key claim

The response of color superconducting gaps to a small shift in the strange quark chemical potential (δμ) depends on the interaction model used, with quantitative and qualitative differences between the NJL model and perturbative single gluon exchange, though the paper presents numerical results only for the NJL model and states that perturbative results will be presented in a subsequent section.

## Method

The author studied three massless quark flavors with the strange quark given a different chemical potential (μ_s = μ - δμ) to mimic the effect of a strange quark mass. The gap equation was solved using a general ansatz for the color-flavor structure of the gap matrix, with the interaction modeled both by a four-fermion NJL interaction and by perturbative single gluon exchange at moderate densities. The quasiparticle propagator poles were derived analytically, and the coupled gap equations were solved iteratively using numerical integration with an ultraviolet cutoff Λ = 1000 MeV at μ = 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The ultraviolet cutoff used in the NJL model calculation was Λ = 1000 MeV, chosen so that the magnitude of the NJL results would be comparable to the perturbative results.
- The chemical potential was set to μ = 500 MeV for the numerical solutions of the gap equations.
- The Debye mass used in the perturbative gluon propagator is given by m_D^2 = N_f g^2 μ^2 / (2π^2), with g^2 = 4π (12π / ((11N_c - 2N_f) log[μ^2/Λ^2_QCD])).
- The gap Δ_{3̄3̄}(ud) was found to increase linearly with δμ, with an additional quadratic component at larger δμ, while Δ_{3̄3̄}(us) decreased linearly with δμ with a larger magnitude of decrease than the increase in Δ_{3̄3̄}(ud).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity with non-degenerate quarks previously studied by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and by Schäfer and Wilczek (1999) using NJL models, and by Pisarski and Rischke (1999) for perturbative single gluon exchange. The paper provides a more general ansatz for the color-flavor structure of the gap matrix than in Schäfer and Wilczek (1999) and presents the quasiparticle propagator poles in this generalized framework. The results enable future comparison between NJL and perturbative approaches to the three-flavor non-degenerate case, and test the prediction by Bedaque (2001) that number densities of the three quark flavors are constrained equal up to δμ = Δ_{3̄3̄}/√2.
