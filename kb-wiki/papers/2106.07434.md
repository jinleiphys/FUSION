# Fully heavy multiquarks

> The existence of stable multiquarks made of heavy quarks and antiquarks is discussed in the framework of potential models. It is stressed that the few-body problem should be handled seriously and accurately. No bound state is found within the current models. But resonances are likely present in the spectrum above the lowest threshold.

## Key claim

Within standard potential models, no bound state is found for fully heavy multiquarks made solely of charm and bottom quarks, but resonances are likely present in the spectrum above the lowest threshold; the abstract's claim of "no bound state" is conditional on using consistent, accurate few-body calculations and avoiding unjustified approximations such as the point-diquark simplification.

## Method

The author reviews potential-model calculations for tetraquarks, pentaquarks, and hexaquarks composed entirely of heavy quarks (c and b), using a pairwise interaction with color-octet exchange (Eq. 1) optionally supplemented by a spin-spin term (Eq. 2). For tetraquarks, the four-body Hamiltonian is recast as a function of a parameter λ measuring asymmetry in the distribution of potential energy among pairs, and the ground-state energy E(λ) is analyzed variationally. For hexaquarks, a six-body calculation is performed with the same potential model, and the results are compared to lattice QCD simulations.

## Key numbers

- For the equal-mass tetraquark QQ\bar Q\bar Q in the pure chromoelectric limit (Eq. 1), no bound state is found; the configuration is unstable unless the mass ratio m(Q')/m(Q) is "very large, actually much larger than m_b/m_c".
- For the tetraquark Hamiltonian H(λ) (Eq. 3), the parameter λ takes values: λ_s = 1/3 for the Ps+Ps or Q\bar Q+Q\bar Q threshold, λ_mol = -2/3 for the Ps_2 molecule, λ_3 = 1/12 for QQ\bar Q\bar Q in color 3\bar 3, and λ_6 = -7/24 for QQ\bar Q\bar Q in color 6\bar 6; the ground-state energy E(λ) is maximal at λ=0 and decreases on both sides.
- For hexaquarks QQQQQQ, a careful six-body calculation using the potential model of Eqs. 1-2 found "no bound state" (Richard 2020), while a lattice team (Junnarkar 2019) reported several fully-heavy hexaquark states lighter than their QQQ+QQQ threshold; a later lattice estimate of the Ω_cccΩ_ccc system finds it "at the edge of binding" (Lyu 2021).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on whether fully heavy multiquarks can be bound, contrasting with earlier claims of stability from lattice QCD (Junnarkar 2019) and from simplified diquark models (Karliner 2016). The author emphasizes that the point-diquark approximation artificially produces extra attraction, and that relativistic kinematics works against tetraquark binding (Richard 2021). The paper enables future work on resonances using methods from atomic and nuclear physics, such as real or complex scaling (Meng 2020), and calls for resolving the discrepancy between potential models and lattice simulations.
