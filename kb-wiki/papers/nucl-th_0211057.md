# Q-boson interferometry and generalized Wigner function

> Bose-Einstein correlations of two identically charged $Q$-bosons are derived considering these particles to be confined in finite volumes. Boundary effects on single $Q$-boson spectrum are also studied. We illustrate the effects on the spectrum and on the two-$Q$-boson correlation function by means of two toy models. We also derive a generalized expression for the Wigner function depending on the deformation parameter $Q$, which is reduced to its original functional form in the limit of $Q \to 1$.

## Key claim

The paper derives Bose-Einstein correlation functions for two identically charged Q-bosons confined in finite volumes, showing that the deformation parameter Q reduces the intercept parameter λ below unity for 0 ≤ Q ≤ 1, but only under the specific assumption that the density matrix includes an explicit energy dependence; the naive classical limit C₂ → 1 is recovered only for single modes, not for multi-mode systems.

## Method

The authors model pions as Q-bosons governed by deformed commutation relations with parameter Q in the interval [-1,1], confined in finite volumes with reflecting boundaries. They compute the single-particle spectrum and two-particle correlation function using a grand-canonical density matrix, then illustrate the effects through two toy models: a one-dimensional box of length L and a spherical cavity of radius R. They also derive a generalized Wigner function that reduces to the standard form only in the Q → 1 limit.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter Q is assumed to lie in the interval [-1,1], with the analysis focused on 0 ≤ Q ≤ 1.
- For Q = 1, the intercept parameter λ = C₂(K,K) − 1 equals 1 (i.e., C₂ = 2), recovering the ideal bosonic result.
- For Q = 0 in the multi-mode case with a constant density matrix, the intercept parameter λ approaches 1/3 regardless of system size.
- For Q = 0 in the multi-mode case with energy-dependent density matrix, λ → 0 for very small system sizes and λ → 1/3 for very large systems.
- The toy model uses a one-dimensional box of length L = 4 fm and L = 8 fm, with chemical potential μ = 0.
- The spherical cavity model uses radius R as the freeze-out radius, with wave functions determined by solutions of the Klein-Gordon equation with boundary condition ψ(r)|_{r=R} = 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of boundary effects on pion interferometry (Zhang and Padula, 2002) by incorporating Q-deformed algebras, following the suggestion by Greenberg (1991) that quons can encapsulate complex dynamics. The Q parameter is interpreted as an effective measure of internal degrees of freedom of composite particles, as proposed by Gastao (1999), where Q² ≈ 1 − L³/V relates boson volume to system volume. The results generalize earlier single-mode analyses by Agop et al. (1996) and enable future comparisons with Monte Carlo event generators for multi-particle Bose-Einstein correlations.
