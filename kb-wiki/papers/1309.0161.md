# Effect of in-medium spectral density of $D$ and $D^*$ mesons on the $J/ψ$ dissociation in hadronic matter

> The one-loop self-energy of the $D$ and $D^*$ mesons in a hot hadronic medium is evaluated using the real time formalism of thermal field theory. The interaction of the heavy open-charm mesons with the thermalized constituents $(π,K,η)$ of the hadronic matter is treated in the covariant formalism of heavy meson chiral perturbation theory. The imaginary parts are extracted from the discontinuities of the self-energy function across the unitary and the Landau cuts. The non-zero contribution from the latter to the spectral density of $D$ and $D^*$ mesons opens a number of subthreshold decay channels of the $J/ψ$ leading to a significant increase in the dissociation width in hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the in-medium spectral density of \(D\) and \(D^*\) mesons, specifically the non-zero contribution from the Landau cut, opens subthreshold decay channels for the \(J/\psi\) and leads to a significant increase in its dissociation width in hadronic matter; however, this effect is primarily due to spectral broadening rather than a shift of the pole masses of the charmed mesons.

## Method

The one-loop self-energy of \(D\) and \(D^*\) mesons in a hot hadronic medium was evaluated using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory, with interactions treated in the covariant formalism of heavy meson chiral perturbation theory. The imaginary parts of the self-energy were extracted from discontinuities across the unitary and Landau cuts in the complex energy plane. The resulting in-medium spectral functions were then used to calculate the \(J/\psi\) dissociation width in the \(D\overline{D}\), \(D\overline{D}^*\), and \(D^*\overline{D}^*\) channels, incorporating a form factor with a cut-off \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant \(g = 1.177\) GeV was used for the heavy-light pseudoscalar-vector interaction, obtained by reproducing the experimental \(D^* \rightarrow D\pi\) decay width of approximately 65 keV.
* The nominal mass of the \(D\) meson is 1867 MeV, and the \(D^*\) meson pole is at 2008 MeV.
* The coupling \(g_{\psi} = 7.8\) was taken for the \(J/\psi\) interactions, as in Friman (1995).
* The total \(J/\psi\) dissociation width was plotted as a function of temperature, with results shown for a form factor cut-off \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV, which appears in reasonable agreement with Fuchs (2006).
* The mass shift of the \(J/\psi\) at \(T = 200\) MeV was found to be negligible at the pole.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of drag and diffusion coefficients of \(D\) mesons in hadronic matter by Ghosh (2013) and Mitra (2012), which indicated substantial interaction of heavy mesons with the thermal bath. It also builds on earlier theoretical efforts to understand in-medium behavior of charmed mesons using models such as QCD sum rules by Hayashigaki (2000) and Kampfer (2000), and the Quark-Meson Coupling model by Tsushima (1999). The results enable a more detailed understanding of \(J/\psi\) suppression in a hadronic environment, complementing studies of \(J/\psi\) absorption by comoving hadrons by Haglin (2000) and Liu (1998).
