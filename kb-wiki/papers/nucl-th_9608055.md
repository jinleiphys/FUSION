# Evaluation of the mean intensity of the P-odd mixing of nuclear compound states

> A temperature version of the shell-optical-model approach for describing the low-energy compound-to-compound transitions induced by external single-particle fields is given. The approach is applied to evaluate the mean intensity of the P-odd mixing of nuclear compound states. Unified description for the mixing and electromagnetic transitions allows one to evaluate the mean intensity without the use of free parameters. The valence-mechanism contribution to the mentioned intensity is also evaluated. Calculation results are compared with the data deduced from cross sections of relevant neutron-induced reactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a temperature-modified shell-optical-model approach that can evaluate the mean intensity of P-odd mixing of nuclear compound states without free parameters, provided that a single adjustable parameter (describing the strength of particle-hole coupling to many-quasiparticle configurations) is first fixed by fitting the calculated total radiative width to experimental data for each nucleus.

## Method

The authors extend a semiempirical shell-optical-model approach by including a complementary term in the strength function that ensures invariance under ω → -ω, which gives an essential contribution at small transition energies. They calculate the strength function for weak transitions at zero energy using a temperature-dependent modification of occupation numbers and quasiparticle damping, with the temperature determined from the mean excitation energy of final compound states via the level-density formula. The single adjustable parameter α is fixed for each nucleus by fitting the calculated total radiative width Γγ^tot to experimental values, after which the root-mean-squared weak matrix element M_w is computed without further free parameters.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵⁷Fe: M_w^(I) = 54 meV, M_w^(II) = 92 meV, compared to experimental M_w^exp = 46^{+15}_{-12} meV; r = 19 × 10⁻³
- For ¹¹⁴Cd: M_w^(I) = 1.5 meV, M_w^(II) = 3.1 meV, compared to experimental M_w^exp = 2.0^{+1.6}_{-0.9} meV; r = 0.7 × 10⁻³
- For ¹¹⁶In: M_w^(I) = 0.6 meV, M_w^(II) = 1.4 meV, compared to experimental M_w^exp = 0.59^{+0.25}_{-0.15} meV; r = 0.8 × 10⁻³
- For ²³³Th: M_w^(I) = 1.4 meV, M_w^(II) = 2.2 meV, compared to experimental M_w^exp = 1.39^{+0.55}_{-0.38} meV; r = 1.1 × 10⁻³
- For ²³⁹U: M_w^(I) = 1.5 meV, M_w^(II) = 2.5 meV, compared to experimental M_w^exp = 0.56^{+0.41}_{-0.20} meV; r = 1.6 × 10⁻³
- The calculated ratios r = (M_w)_val / M_w are typically about 10⁻³, indicating the valence-mechanism contribution is small
- The parameter α ranges from 0.03 MeV⁻¹ (¹¹⁸Sn) to 0.18 MeV⁻¹ (⁸²Br) across the nuclei studied

## Context

This work extends the semiempirical approach of Urin and collaborators (1990s) for evaluating weak matrix elements by including the complementary term in the strength function and by fixing the single adjustable parameter through fitting to experimental total radiative widths, enabling parameter-free predictions. The results are compared with experimental weak matrix elements deduced from polarized neutron scattering by Bowman (1990s), Sharapov (1990s), and Krupchitsky (1990s) for compound nuclei including ²³³Th, ²³⁹U, ¹¹⁴Cd, and ¹¹⁶In. The approach enables systematic evaluation of P-odd mixing intensities across many nuclei with A > 50 and quantitatively demonstrates that the valence-mechanism contribution is negligible compared to the statistical mechanism.
