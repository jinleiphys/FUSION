# Collisional jet quenching becomes probable

> It was argued recently that loop corrections to tree-level amplitudes are essential in the discussion of the collisional energy loss of energetic partons in the hot quark gluon plasma: Instead of $dE_{\rm coll}^B/dx \sim α^2 T^2 \ln(ET/m_D^2)$, as first derived by Bjorken (assuming a constant $α$), the mean energy loss actually behaves as $dE_{\rm coll}/dx \sim α(m_D^2)T^2$. Here we calculate, within this resummation-improved framework, the probability distribution functions (`quenching weights') of the collisional energy loss. First results from a Monte Carlo implementation of this probabilistic collisional quenching shed new light on the interpretation of jet suppression in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when loop corrections are included by using a running coupling, the collisional energy loss of energetic partons in the quark-gluon plasma behaves as \(dE_{\rm coll}/dx \sim \alpha(m_D^2)T^2\) rather than the previously assumed Bjorken-type formula \(dE_{\rm coll}^B/dx \sim \alpha^2 T^2 \ln(ET/m_D^2)\), and that this revised framework yields probability distribution functions for collisional energy loss that can be implemented in Monte Carlo simulations of jet quenching.

## Method

The author calculates the probability distribution functions (quenching weights) for collisional energy loss by evaluating the collision rate integral with a running coupling \(\alpha(t) = b_0^{-1}/\ln(|t|/\Lambda_{QCD}^2)\) and an infrared cutoff related to the Debye mass. The angular integral in the probability density expression is performed analytically, yielding closed-form results for quarks and gluons. These probability distributions are then implemented in a Monte Carlo simulation of jet quenching in a boost-invariant Bjorken-expanding QGP, where the hard parton spectrum evolves due to collisions with thermal partons as the medium cools.

## Key numbers

- For a quark at temperature \(T = 3T_c \approx 0.5\) GeV, the mean collisional energy loss per unit length is \(\Delta E_q/\Delta x \approx 3\) GeV/fm.
- The probability density function \(P_{\Delta x}^{q,g}(\omega; T)\) for collisions is peaked at energy transfers \(\omega\) of order \({\cal O}(0.1)\) GeV for typical temperatures.
- The quark mean free path is of the same order of magnitude as the typical particle distance \(r_0 = \rho_0^{-1/3} \sim T^{-1}\) in the medium; the gluon mean free path becomes smaller than \(r_0(T)\) at \(\hat T \approx 0.5\) GeV (approximately \(4T_c\)).
- For a model with parameters \(R = 5\) fm, \(T_0 = 0.5\) GeV, \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm, the QGP lifetime is \(\tau_c = 6.1\) fm (for \(T_c = 0.16\) GeV).
- The quenching factor \(Q_q\) for quarks increases with \(p_t\), in contrast to previous predictions based on Bjorken-type formulae.
- With an estimated light quark radiative quenching factor \(\sim 0.6\), a collisional quenching factor \(\sim 0.5\) (averaged over near and far side) is in the expected range to match the observed nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA} \sim 0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier argument (Peshier, 2006) that loop corrections are essential for collisional energy loss, contradicting the long-standing Bjorken-type formula (Bjorken, 1982) and its refinements by Thoma and Gyulassy (1991) and Braaten and Thoma (1991). The probabilistic treatment parallels the quenching weights approach used for radiative energy loss by Salgado and Wiedemann (2003) within the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier, 1996; Zakharov, 1996; Gyulassy, 2000). The results suggest that a collisional component is necessary to reconcile data-adjusted model parameters with general expectations, as advocated by Gyulassy (2005), and that the moderate increase of \(R_{AA}\) at large \(p_t\) seen by the PHENIX collaboration could be a signature of collisional quenching.
