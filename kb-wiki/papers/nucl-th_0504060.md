# Pi-meson as the quanta of non-newtonian hadronic fluid

> It occurs that when we attempt to melt the nucleon in order to obtain the free quark gas, or QGP fluid the mass of the heat quantum (heaton) is equal to the pi-meson mass. Key words: Heat quanta; Quantum heat transport; Quantum diffusion coefficient.

# Pi-meson as the quanta of non-Newtonian hadronic fluid

## Key claim

When the nucleon is thermally excited to the point of "melting" into a free quark gas or quark-gluon plasma fluid, the energy of the heat quantum (heaton) equals the pi-meson mass of approximately 139 MeV, which the authors interpret as a manifestation of quark confinement. This result depends on the specific choice of the strong quark-quark fine structure constant α_s^q ≈ 1, derived from baryon resonance decay widths.

## Method

The authors generalize the quantum hyperbolic heat transport (QHT) equation, previously developed for electron and nucleon Fermi gases, to a quark gas system. They compute the heaton energy E_h^q = (m_q/3)(α_s^q)²c² using an average quark mass m_q = 417 MeV (averaged over up, down, and strange quark masses) and a strong quark-quark coupling α_s^q ≈ 1 estimated from the ratio of electromagnetic to strong decay lifetimes of the Σ⁰(1385 MeV) resonance. The resulting heaton energy is compared to the pi-meson mass.

## Key numbers

- For electron gas: heaton energy E_h^e = 9 eV, comparable to the Rydberg energy, with electron mass m_e = 0.51 MeV/c² and fine structure constant α = 1/137.
- For nucleon gas: heaton energy E_h^N ≈ 7 MeV, described as the average binding energy per nucleon in the nucleus, with nucleon mass m = 938 MeV/c² and strong fine structure constant α_s = 0.15.
- For quark gas: heaton energy E_h^q ≈ 139 MeV, equal to the pi-meson mass m_π, obtained using average quark mass m_q = 417 MeV (from m_u = 350 MeV, m_d = 350 MeV, m_s = 550 MeV) and α_s^q ≈ 1.
- The strong quark-quark fine structure constant α_s^q ≈ 1 is estimated from the ratio of electromagnetic decay lifetime τ_e ≈ 10⁻¹⁹ s to strong decay lifetime τ_s ≈ 10⁻²³ s for the Σ⁰(1385 MeV) resonance.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior development of hyperbolic heat transport equations for fermionic systems (Kozlowski and Marciak-Kozlowska, 2005) to the subnucleon level, building on Nelson's (1966) derivation of the Schrodinger equation from quantum Brownian motion with diffusion coefficient D = ħ/2m. The result suggests that standard approaches to melting nucleons into quarks through heating processes may not succeed, as the thermal energy quanta at the quark level are pi-mesons themselves, implying that the hadronic fluid at excitation energies around 140 MeV becomes a non-Newtonian fluid where pi-mesons act as "phonon-like" excitations.
