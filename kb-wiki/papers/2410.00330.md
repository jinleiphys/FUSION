# Enhancing DUNE's solar neutrino capabilities with neutral-current detection

> We show that the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) has the potential to make a precise measurement of the total active flux of 8B solar neutrinos via neutral-current (NC) interactions with argon. This would complement proposed precise measurements of solar-neutrino fluxes in DUNE via charged-current (CC) interactions with argon and mixed CC/NC interactions with electrons. Together, these would enable DUNE to make a SNO-like comparison of rates and thus to make the most precise measurements of $\sin^2θ_{12}$ and $Δm^2_{21}$ using solar neutrinos. Realizing this potential requires dedicated but realistic efforts to improve DUNE's low-energy capabilities and separately to reduce neutrino-argon cross section uncertainties. Comparison of mixing-parameter results obtained using solar neutrinos in DUNE and reactor antineutrinos in JUNO (Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory) would allow unprecedented tests of new physics.

## Key claim

DUNE can measure the total active flux of 8B solar neutrinos via neutral-current (NC) interactions with argon, but realizing this potential requires dedicated but realistic efforts to improve DUNE's low-energy capabilities and separately to reduce neutrino-argon cross section uncertainties.

## Method

The authors compute the NC neutrino-argon cross section using an empirical approach that extracts nuclear matrix elements from measurements of magnetic dipole strength in 40Ar, focusing on 1+ excited states in the 5-10 MeV range that decay via single gamma-ray emission. They convolve the unoscillated solar neutrino flux from the BS05(OP) solar model with this cross section to predict event rates for a 100 kton-year DUNE exposure, then apply particle identification techniques and angular cuts to suppress backgrounds from charged-current and elastic scattering events.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 2200 NC events predicted above 4.5 MeV detectable energy for 100 kton-year exposure after particle identification and angular cuts
- Approximately 15000 CC+ES events predicted above 4.5 MeV for the same exposure after cuts
- Including NC events improves the uncertainty on sin^2 theta_12 from 4.5% to 1.7%
- Including NC events improves the uncertainty on the total solar neutrino flux from 5% to 1.6%
- Particle identification cut at 3.1 MeV/cm retains 95% of NC events and only 16% of CC+ES events
- The NC cross section for 5-10 MeV 1+ excitations uses measurements with total uncertainty reduced from 40% to within 20%

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Capozzi (2018) for solar neutrino measurements in DUNE using CC and ES channels, adding the NC channel to enable a SNO-like comparison of rates. The authors build on recent measurements of nuclear excitations in argon by Gayer (2019) and Tornow (2022), and on improved prospects for detecting MeV-range gamma rays in liquid argon demonstrated by ArgoNeuT (2018) and studied by Castiglioni (2020) and Gardiner (2020). The results would enable comparison of mixing parameters measured with DUNE solar neutrinos and JUNO reactor antineutrinos for unprecedented tests of new physics.
