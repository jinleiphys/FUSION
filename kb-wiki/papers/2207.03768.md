# On the proton-deuteron backward scattering

> The article is devoted to the relativistic study of elastic $pD$ backward scattering based on the one-nucleon exchange diagram. Calculations were performed using relativistic deuteron wave functions obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Minkowski space with relativistic separable potentials. The unpolarized differential cross section as well as some polarization observables of the reaction for initial proton momentum up to 7.3 GeV were calculated. The obtained results are compared with the calculations of other authors.

## Key claim

The one-nucleon exchange approximation, using relativistic deuteron wave functions from the Bethe-Salpeter equation with separable potentials Graz-II and MY6, fails to describe experimental data for unpolarized cross section, tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\), and polarization transfer \(\kappa_{D \rightarrow p}\), indicating the inadequacy of this simple reaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors calculated the covariant amplitude for elastic proton-deuteron backward scattering using the one-nucleon exchange Feynman diagram, with deuteron vertex functions obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space with relativistic separable potentials (Graz-II and MY6). They computed the unpolarized differential cross section and several polarization observables (tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\), polarization transfer \(\kappa_{D \rightarrow p}\), vector-vector and tensor-tensor polarization transfer coefficients) for initial proton momenta up to 7.3 GeV/c, and compared results with previous calculations from Karmanov and Dorkin (1998), Karmanov and Trubnikov (1982), and Laptev and Andreev (2020). The effect of the zeroth component of the relative momentum in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude was also investigated by comparing calculations with \(q_0 = 0\) and \(q_0 = \tilde{q}_0\).

## Key numbers

- Unpolarized differential cross section: at final proton momentum \(p_f = 0.6\) GeV/c, the result from Karmanov and Dorkin (1998) and the current calculation differ by a factor of 3.
- Tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\): significant differences between approaches start at \(p_f \approx 0.3\) GeV/c, with opposite sign at \(p_f = 0.6\) GeV/c.
- For \(T_{20}\) with \(q_0 = 0\) vs. \(q_0 = \tilde{q}_0\): difference reaches 0.1 for Graz-II and 0.3 for MY6 at \(p_f = 0.6\) GeV/c.
- Polarization transfer \(\kappa_{D \rightarrow p}\): difference between approaches starts at \(p_f = 0.20-0.25\) GeV/c.
- Kinematic limit: as initial proton momentum \(|{\bm p_i}| \to \infty\), final proton momentum \(|{\bm p_f}| \to (M_d^2 - m^2)/(2 M_d) \approx 0.702\) GeV/c.
- Calculations performed for initial proton momentum up to 7.3 GeV/c and final proton momentum up to 0.6 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic one-nucleon exchange approach to proton-deuteron backward scattering, comparing results from Bethe-Salpeter solutions with separable potentials (Graz-II and MY6) to earlier calculations by Karmanov and Dorkin (1998) and Karmanov and Trubnikov (1982) who used one-meson exchange kernels in pseudo-Euclidean space, and to Laptev and Andreev (2020) who used non-relativistic CD-Bonn wave functions with Lorentz transformations. The poor description of experimental data suggests that the one-nucleon exchange mechanism is insufficient, and the authors note that further improvements require including additional diagrams (pion exchange, Delta isobars) or solving the relativistic Faddeev equation, as well as potentially including \(P\)-wave partial states beyond the \(S\) and \(D\) states considered here.
