# The role of S_11 resonance in pi N Scattering

> We analyze Pion Nucleon Scattering up to 700 MeV using a simple, relativistic, unitary model\cite{1}. The kernel of the integral equation includes nucleon, roper, delta, D_13 as well as S_11 poles with their corresponding crossed pole terms approximated by contact interactions. The s- and p- wave phase shifts are calculated from the model and shown to agree very well with the values derived from pi N scattering data \cite {2}. All parameters which involve S_{11} are presented.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed relativistic, unitary model of pion-nucleon scattering to include the S_11 resonance, and finds that the model's calculated s- and p-wave phase shifts agree well with data up to 700 MeV, but the extracted S_11 width (57.4 MeV) is smaller than the Particle Data Group range of approximately 100 to 250 MeV, possibly due to the smaller eta-nucleon-S_11 coupling constant (1.19) compared to other model results.

## Method

The authors solve a relativistic wave equation with pions and etas restricted to their mass shell in all intermediate states, using a kernel that includes undressed nucleon, delta, roper, D_13, and S_11 poles plus their crossed terms approximated as contact interactions, along with sigma- and rho-like exchange terms. The pi-NN coupling is a superposition of pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling, while the pi-N-S_11 coupling mixes scalar and vector forms; the eta-N-S_11 coupling is taken as purely scalar. The model treats the S_11 self-energy as a coupled-channel problem involving pi-pi, pi-eta, eta-pi, and eta-eta intermediate states, and fits phase shifts separately for spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 channels.

## Key numbers

* The pi-NN coupling constant g_piNN is set to 13.5.
* The S_11 resonance mass (m_s11) is 1540.6 MeV, with an effective mass of 1567.0 MeV.
* The S_11 width (Gamma_s11) is 57.4 MeV, smaller than the Particle Data Group range of approximately 100 to 250 MeV.
* The eta-N-S_11 coupling constant g_etaNS11 is 1.19.
* The analysis covers pion laboratory kinetic energies up to 700 MeV.
* The baryon cutoff mass Lambda is 1205.8 MeV; the S_11 cutoff mass Lambda_s11 is 2666.3 MeV.
* The roper resonance mass (m_N*) is 1458.0 MeV, with effective mass 1463.5 MeV and width 244.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic unitary model of Gross and Surya (1999) by adding the S_11 resonance and its coupled eta-N channel to the analysis of pion-nucleon scattering. The model's smaller S_11 width and coupling constants compared to Particle Data Group values and to results from effective models and QCD sum rules using interpolating fields suggest that further quark-model calculations are needed to clarify the couplings. The approach enables future studies of pion-nucleon interactions at the Thomas Jefferson National Lab facility, particularly for energies above 600 MeV where the S_11 contribution becomes important.
