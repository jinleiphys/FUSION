# On The Origin of the OZI Rule in QCD

> The OZI rule is prominent in hadronic phenomena only because OZI violation is typically an order of magnitude smaller than expected from large N_c arguments. With its standard ^3P_0 pair creation operator for hadronic decays by flux tube breaking, the quark model respects the OZI rule at tree level and exhibits the cancellations between OZI-violating meson loop diagrams required for this dramatic suppression. However, if the quark model explanation for these cancellations is correct, then OZI violation would be expected to be large in the nonet with the same quantum numbers as the pair creation operator: the 0^{++} mesons. Experiment is currently unable to identify these mesons, but we report here on a lattice QCD calculation which confirms that the OZI rule arises from QCD in the vector and axial vector mesons as observed, and finds a large violation of the rule in the scalar mesons as anticipated by the quark model. In view of this result, we make some remarks on possible connections between the ^3P_0 pair creation model, scalar mesons, and the U_A(1) anomaly responsible for the large OZI violation which drives the η' mass. In particular, we note that our result favors the large N_c and not the instanton interpretation of the solution to the η' mass problem.

## Key claim

The OZI rule is prominent in hadronic phenomena only because OZI violation is typically an order of magnitude smaller than expected from large N_c arguments; the paper reports a lattice QCD calculation confirming that the OZI rule arises from QCD in vector and axial vector mesons as observed, while finding a large violation in scalar mesons as anticipated by the quark model, with the caveat that experiment is currently unable to identify the scalar meson nonet.

## Method

The authors performed a quenched lattice QCD calculation on 300 gauge configurations on a 12^3 x 24 lattice at beta=5.7 using the clover improved quark action with coefficient C_sw=1.57, employing the allsource method to compute double-hairpin correlators for pseudoscalar, vector, axial vector, and scalar sources. The double-hairpin correlators were Fourier transformed over 3-dimensional time slices at zero 3-momentum, and the OZI-violating amplitudes were extracted by fitting the time-dependence to a double-pole form with a p^2-independent insertion between meson propagators, with chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar double-hairpin amplitude: A_OZI^(0-+) = +(407 ± 11)^2 (MeV)^2, corresponding to A_OZI^(0-+) ≃ +290 MeV
- Vector double-hairpin amplitude: A_OZI^(1--) < (220)^2 (MeV)^2, corresponding to A_OZI^(1--) < 30 MeV (one standard deviation upper bound)
- Axial vector double-hairpin amplitude: A_OZI^(1++) < (380)^2 (MeV)^2, corresponding to A_OZI^(1++) < 60 MeV (one standard deviation upper bound)
- Scalar double-hairpin amplitude: A_OZI^(0++) = -(1350 ± 90)^2 (MeV)^2, corresponding to A_OZI^(0++) ≃ -520 MeV
- Lattice spacing set using physical charmonium 1S-1P splitting: a^(-1) = 1.18 GeV at beta=5.7
- Pseudoscalar double-hairpin from clover action gave A_OZI^(0-+) = (0.41 GeV)^2, compared to (0.49 GeV)^2 from weak-SU(3)-breaking mass formulas

## Context

This work extends the quark model analysis of Geiger and Isgur (1990s) which proposed that cancellations between OZI-violating meson loop diagrams, mediated by the ^3P_0 pair creation operator, could explain the suppression of OZI violation beyond simple 1/N_c expectations. The lattice results confirm the pattern of OZI violation seen empirically in vector and axial vector mesons, and support the prediction that scalar mesons should exhibit large OZI violation. The findings favor the large N_c interpretation over the instanton interpretation of the U_A(1) anomaly solution to the eta' mass problem, and the method enables future lattice studies of OZI-violating amplitudes in other channels.
