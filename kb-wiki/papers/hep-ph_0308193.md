# Learning about the chiral structure of the proton from the hyperfine splitting

> The complete analytical $O(m_l^3α^5/m_p^2\times (\log{m_π}, \logΔ, \log{m_l}))$ contribution to the Hyperfine splitting is given. It can explain about 2/3 of the difference between experiment and the pure QED prediction when setting the renormalization scale at the $ρ$ mass. The suppression of the polarizability piece with respect the Zemach one seems to be, to a large extent, a numerical accident. We give an estimate of the matching coefficient of the spin-dependent proton-lepton operator in heavy baryon effective theory.

# Learning about the chiral structure of the proton from the hyperfine splitting

## Key claim
The complete analytical $O(m_l^3\alpha^5/m_p^2 \times (\log m_\pi, \log\Delta, \log m_l))$ contribution to the hyperfine splitting can explain about 2/3 of the difference between experiment and the pure QED prediction when the renormalization scale is set at the $\rho$ mass, but the remaining discrepancy indicates the expected size of a counterterm from short-distance physics.

## Method
The author computed the logarithmically enhanced hadronic contributions to the hyperfine splitting using potential NRQED matched to heavy baryon effective theory. The calculation involved evaluating the spin-dependent proton-lepton operator coefficient $c^{pl}_{4,NR}$ from the forward virtual-photon Compton tensor of the proton, separating the Zemach (Born) contribution from polarizability effects, and expressing the result in terms of chiral logs from pion and Delta baryon loops.

## Key numbers
- The total logarithmic hadronic contribution to the hyperfine splitting at scale $\nu = m_\rho$ is $E_{\rm HF,logarithms}(m_\rho) = -0.031$ MHz.
- This accounts for approximately 2/3 of the difference between pure QED theory and experiment.
- The matching coefficient in the heavy baryon effective Lagrangian is estimated as $c^{pl}_{4,R}(m_\rho) \simeq -16\alpha^2$.
- The polarizability correction constant $C = \pi^3/12 - 7\pi/8 = -0.165037$ shows a numerical cancellation compared to the natural size of each term.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory treatment of hadronic effects in atomic hyperfine splitting, building on the potential NRQED framework of Pineda and collaborators and the heavy baryon effective theory approach. The calculation provides a model-independent separation of long-distance chiral physics (pion and Delta contributions) from short-distance effects encoded in a matching coefficient, enabling future lattice QCD or experimental determinations of the remaining counterterm. The result contrasts with earlier dipole parameterizations of form factors (such as those used by Bodwin and Yennie, 1988) which cannot reproduce the correct chiral logarithmic behavior.
