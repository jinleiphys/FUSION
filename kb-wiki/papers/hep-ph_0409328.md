# Rapidity distribution of gluons in the classical field model for heavy ion collisions

> The rapidity distribution of gluons produced in heavy ion collisions is studied by a numerical computation in 2+1-dimensional classical Yang-Mills theory. By assuming that the classical source strength g^2 mu depends on rapidity as g^4 mu^2 ~ exp(+- lambda y) and studying collisions of two nuclei with different g^2 mu we find that the rapidity distribution of produced gluons at central rapidities is very broad. The transverse energy is seen to decrease even more slowly as a function of y than the multiplicity. We discuss these results and the range in y and sqrt(s) where they are applicable in the light of experimental results and other theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The rapidity distribution of gluons produced in heavy ion collisions, computed numerically in 2+1-dimensional classical Yang-Mills theory by assuming the classical source strength varies as \(g^4 \mu^2 \sim e^{\pm \lambda y}\), is very broad at central rapidities, but the calculation is limited to small rapidities where the approximation that the average transverse momentum \(\langle p_T \rangle\) is independent of \(y\) holds (roughly \(\lambda y \lesssim 1\)).

## Method

The paper performs a numerical solution of the classical Yang-Mills equations in 2+1 dimensions, using the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the nuclear color charge densities. The initial conditions for the gauge fields at proper time \(\tau=0\) are constructed from pure gauge fields corresponding to two nuclei with different source strengths \(g^2 \mu_{(1)}\) and \(g^2 \mu_{(2)}\), which are varied with rapidity as \(\mu^2_{(1)} = \mu^2 e^{\lambda y}\) and \(\mu^2_{(2)} = \mu^2 e^{-\lambda y}\). The transverse energy and gluon multiplicity per unit rapidity are extracted from the classical fields after fixing Coulomb gauge in the transverse plane, and the results are fitted with a Gaussian form in rapidity.

## Key numbers

* For \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV (corresponding to \(g^4 \mu^2 \pi R_A^2 = 14400\)), the fitted Gaussian width for the multiplicity is \(\lambda \sigma = 1.66 \pm 0.02\) on a \(512^2\) lattice.
* For \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV (\(g^4 \mu^2 \pi R_A^2 = 5184\)), the fitted width is \(\lambda \sigma = 1.96 \pm 0.03\) on a \(512^2\) lattice.
* For \(\mu = 0.8\) GeV (\(g^4 \mu^2 \pi R_A^2 = 36864\)), the fitted width is \(\lambda \sigma = 1.35 \pm 0.02\) on a \(512^2\) lattice.
* Taking \(\lambda \approx 0.25 \dots 0.3\) and \(\lambda \sigma \approx 1.66\) gives a Gaussian width \(\sigma \approx 5.5 \dots 6.6\) for the rapidity distribution.

## Context

This work extends the classical field model for gluon production in heavy ion collisions, previously developed by Krasnitz, Nara, and Lappi (2001, 2003), to study the rapidity dependence by varying the source strengths of the two nuclei. The results are compared to the perturbative saturation-based calculations of Kharzeev and Levin (2001) and Hirano and Nara (2004), which include a \((1-x)^4\) factor from large-\(x\) parton distributions and yield a narrower distribution (\(\sigma \approx 3\)). The broader distribution found here suggests that at RHIC energies, the rapidity dependence may be dominated by large-\(x\) physics rather than saturation dynamics, but at LHC energies the situation could be different. The calculation also enables future full 3+1-dimensional classical field computations with more detailed quantum evolution of the sources.
