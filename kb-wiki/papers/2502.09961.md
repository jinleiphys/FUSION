# Chiral symmetry and peripheral neutron-$α$ scattering

> We propose and demonstrate that peripheral neutron-$α$ scattering at low energies can serve as a sensitive and clean probe of the long-range three-nucleon forces. To this aim, we perform {\it ab initio} quantum Monte Carlo calculations using two- and three-nucleon interactions derived in chiral effective field theory up to third expansion order. We show that the longest-range three-nucleon force stemming from the two-pion exchange plays a crucial role in the proper description of the neutron-$α$ $D$-wave phase shifts. Our Letter reveals the predictive power of chiral symmetry in the few-body sector and opens a new direction for probing and constraining three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

Peripheral neutron-alpha scattering at low energies can serve as a sensitive and clean probe of the long-range three-nucleon forces, specifically the two-pion exchange component, but the N2LO prediction slightly underestimates empirical phase shifts, indicating that the repulsive contribution of the three-nucleon force at this order is too large.

## Method

The authors performed Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of neutron-alpha D-wave phase shifts using local chiral effective field theory interactions at LO, NLO, and N2LO with a cutoff of R0 = 1.2 fm. They converted the scattering problem to an eigenvalue problem by placing nucleons in a harmonic oscillator trap, then used the BERW formula to extract phase shifts from the calculated 5He eigenstate energies. Neural-network wave functions with generalized backflow transformations were employed as trial wave functions for the Green's function Monte Carlo propagation.

## Key numbers

- The three-nucleon force LECs used were c1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c3 = -3.40 GeV^-1, and c4 = 3.40 GeV^-1 (from order-Q^3 piN analysis), with cD = 3.5 and cE = 0.09 (fitted to alpha binding energy and low-energy neutron-alpha P-wave phase shifts)
- Harmonic oscillator lengths b = 4-6 fm were used, with the BERW formula accuracy verified to within approximately 10 percent for extracting phase shifts
- The effective order-Q^5 values of piN LECs used to improve the description were c1 = -0.37 GeV^-1, c3 = -2.71 GeV^-1, and c4 = 1.41 GeV^-1
- The D-wave scattering length a2 from the R-matrix analysis of experimental data (Bond 1977) served as the empirical reference value

## Context

This work extends earlier speculations by Barreiro (1998) and Higa (1999) that peripheral neutron-alpha scattering could probe the long-range tail of three-nucleon forces, but provides the first ab initio calculations of such scattering. Previous ab initio studies of neutron-alpha scattering using quantum Monte Carlo (Carlson 1987), Faddeev-Yakubovsky (Lazauskas 2018), no-core shell model (Kravvaris 2020), and stochastic variational approaches (Bagnarol 2023) were limited to S and P partial waves dominated by short-range mechanisms. The results enable experimental testing of the chiral symmetry predictions for three-nucleon forces and can be extended to higher partial waves and other few-nucleon processes like p-alpha and alpha-alpha scattering.
