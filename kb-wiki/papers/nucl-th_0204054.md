# Partonic Effects on Pion Interferometry at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model that includes both initial partonic and final hadronic interactions, we study the pion interferometry at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. We find that the two-pion correlation function is sensitive to the magnitude of the parton scattering cross section, which controls the parton density at which the transition from the partonic to hadronic matter occurs. Also, the emission source of pions is non-Gaussian, leading to source radii that can be more than twice larger than the radius parameters extracted from a Gaussian fit to the correlation function.

# Partonic Effects on Pion Interferometry at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

## Key claim
The two-pion correlation function is sensitive to the magnitude of the parton scattering cross section, which controls the parton density at which the transition from partonic to hadronic matter occurs; however, the emission source of pions is non-Gaussian, so the source radii extracted from a Gaussian fit to the correlation function can be more than twice smaller than the true source radii obtained directly from the emission function.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, a hybrid model that includes initial partonic interactions via the ZPC parton cascade and final hadronic interactions via the ART model, to simulate central Au+Au collisions at √s = 130A GeV. They compared two versions: the default AMPT model (without string melting) and an extended AMPT model with string melting, where excited strings are converted to valence quarks and antiquarks that then interact via parton scattering before coalescing into hadrons. The two-pion correlation functions were evaluated from the freezeout space-time coordinates using the Correlation After Burner program, and the source radii were extracted both directly from the emission function and from Gaussian fits to the correlation function.

## Key numbers
- Parton scattering cross sections of 6–10 mb in the extended AMPT model with string melting reproduce both the centrality and transverse momentum (below 2 GeV/c) dependence of elliptic flow measured in Au+Au collisions at √s = 130A GeV at RHIC.
- A parton scattering cross section of about 10 mb is required in the extended AMPT model with string melting to reproduce the measured one-dimensional correlation functions from the STAR collaboration.
- The radius parameters from the Gaussian fit to the correlation function are about a factor of 2 to 3 smaller than the source radii obtained directly from the emission function for the mT values shown.
- For pions with 125 < pT < 225 MeV/c, the value of the xout–t correlation term D_{xout, β⊥ t} is 168 fm², compared to 185 fm² and 431 fm² for the first and last terms in the Rout² expression.
- The ratio Rout/Rside obtained from the emission function has a value between 1.0 and 1.3, while the ratio from the Gaussian fit to the correlation function is much closer to 1.
- The λ parameter from the Gaussian fit has a value of about 0.5 at low mT and increases to about 1 at large mT.

## Context
This work extends the AMPT model studies of Lin, Ko, and collaborators (2001) by applying pion interferometry to probe partonic dynamics at RHIC, addressing the surprising experimental finding from the STAR collaboration (Adler 2001) that the Rout/Rside ratio from Gaussian fits is close to one, contrary to hydrodynamical model predictions of a larger ratio (Rischke 1996, Soff 2000). The results show that the non-Gaussian nature of the emission source, including strong xout–t correlations and a shell-like shape with a halo, explains why Gaussian-fit radius parameters are much smaller than true source sizes, similar to findings from the RQMD model at SPS (Sullivan, Fields). This work enables future use of imaging methods (Brown and Danielewicz) to extract the true emission function from correlation data and confirms that pion interferometry can probe partonic matter formation at RHIC.
