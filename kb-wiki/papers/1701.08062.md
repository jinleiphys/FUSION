# Binding energies and pairing gaps in semi-magic nuclei obtained using new regularized higher-order EDF generators

> We present results of the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations performed using nuclear energy density functionals based on regularized functional generators at next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order. We discuss properties of binding energies and pairing gaps determined in semi-magic spherical nuclei. The results are compared with benchmark calculations performed for the functional generator SLyMR0 and functional UNEDF0.

## Key claim
The new regularized higher-order energy density functionals (EDFs) at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) describe binding energies of semi-magic nuclei better than the zero-range pseudopotential SLyMR0 but worse than the standard Skyrme-like EDF UNEDF0, and the authors note that these parametrizations are "not good enough to embark for them on massive mass-table calculations" due to low effective mass and overestimated pairing strength.

## Method
Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were performed for all bound semi-magic spherical nuclei across the mass chart using the finite-range NLO REG2c.161026 and N2LO REG4c.161026 EDFs, the zero-range pseudopotential SLyMR0, and the quasi-local functional UNEDF0. For UNEDF0, the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) method was used for approximate particle-number restoration. The non-local self-consistent equations for the finite-range generators were solved using the newly developed code FINRES4, while SLyMR0 solutions used the spherical solver LENTEUR and UNEDF0 solutions used the code HOSPHE.

## Key numbers
- For SLyMR0, the differences between calculated and experimental ground-state energies were "significantly larger" than for the other three EDFs, requiring division by a factor of five in the figures.
- The effective masses for the NLO and N2LO EDFs are close to 0.4, while SLyMR0 has an effective mass of 0.47.
- For the NLO and N2LO EDFs, pairing correlations were adjusted to values "largely overestimating experimental data."
- Statistical uncertainties for UNEDF0 "significantly increase when going towards neutron-rich nuclei," whereas those for the NLO and N2LO EDFs depend on the neutron excess "much less."

## Context
This work extends the development of regularized higher-order EDF generators presented in Bennaceur et al. (2017) and compares them against the benchmark functional SLyMR0 from Sadoudi et al. (2013) and the functional UNEDF0 from Kortelainen et al. (2010). The results show no clear improvement from NLO to N2LO, suggesting that the penalty function based on spherical doubly magic nuclei did not properly constrain the additional degrees of freedom at N2LO. The next step identified is to increase the effective mass by introducing zero-range three-body terms in the spirit of Onishi and Negele (1978).
