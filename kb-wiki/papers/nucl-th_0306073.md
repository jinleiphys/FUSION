# Investigating scalar meson mixing in nucleon--nucleon and deuteron--deuteron collisions

> Invited talk at the international High Energy Physics Workshop 'Scalar Mesons: an Interesting Puzzle for QCD', May 16-18, 2003, SUNY Institute of Technology, Utica, New York

## Key claim

The paper argues that nucleon–nucleon and deuteron–deuteron collisions near the kaon threshold are uniquely suited to extract the charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) \(f_0\)–\(a_0\) mixing matrix element in a model-independent way, because the isospin of initial and final states can be manipulated so that CSB leads to observables that vanish in its absence. The claim is hedged by the fact that no unambiguous experimental signal of this mixing has yet been seen, and the analysis assumes that CSB in the production operator can be neglected relative to CSB in the propagation of the scalar mesons.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical formalism for \(f_0\)–\(a_0\) mixing, showing that the leading contribution arises from kaon-loop unitarity cuts and is enhanced between the \(K^+K^-\) and \(\bar K^0 K^0\) thresholds (an 8 MeV window). He then analyzes the selection rules for \(pn \to d \pi^0\eta\) and \(dd \to \alpha \pi^0\eta\) to identify observables (forward–backward asymmetry, total cross section, analyzing power) that are zero under isospin conservation and therefore signal CSB. Experimental data from the reaction \(pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+\) at 46 MeV excess energy are fitted with a partial-wave expansion to determine the relative contributions of \(K\bar K\) \(s\)-waves and \(p\)-waves.

## Key numbers

* The \(K^+K^-\) threshold lies at 987.35 MeV and the \(\bar K^0 K^0\) threshold at 995.34 MeV, defining an 8 MeV window where the mixing amplitude is enhanced.
* The CSB cross section for \(\pi^+\pi^- \to \pi^0\eta\) was predicted in a microscopic model to be of order a few hundred \(\mu\)b.
* The scalar resonance width \(\Gamma_R = 50\) MeV is used to estimate that CSB in the production operator is kinematically suppressed by a factor \(\Gamma_R / M_N \sim 1/20\) relative to CSB in the propagation of the scalars.
* In the reaction \(pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+\) at 46 MeV excess energy, the fit yields 83 % of the total cross section from \(K\bar K\) \(s\)-waves and 17 % from \(K\bar K\) \(p\)-waves.
* The same data, re-expressed in \(\bar K d\) coordinates, give 54 % \(\bar K d\) \(s\)-waves and 46 % \(\bar K d\) \(p\)-waves.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal of Achasov (1980s) that \(f_0\)–\(a_0\) mixing should be enhanced near the \(K\bar K\) threshold, and responds to the recent analysis by Close and Kirk (2003) which claimed a mixing matrix element five times larger than the leading-loop prediction and independent of invariant mass, a result criticized by Achasov and by Oller. The paper proposes that the planned experimental program at the COSY accelerator (Buscher) can provide the first unambiguous signal of this mixing. It also notes that the same mixing mechanism may affect \(\eta \to 3\pi\) decay (Schechter), but in a different kinematic regime, so both regimes must be understood for a complete picture.
