# Point symmetries in the Hartree-Fock approach: Symmetry-breaking schemes

> We analyze breaking of symmetries that belong to the double point group D2h(TD) (three mutually perpendicular symmetry axes of the second order, inversion, and time reversal). Subgroup structure of the D2h(TD) group indicates that there can be as much as 28 physically different, broken-symmetry mean-field schemes --- starting with solutions obeying all the symmetries of the D2h(TD) group, through 26 generic schemes in which only a non-trivial subgroup of D2h(TD) is conserved, down to solutions that break all of the D2h(TD) symmetries. Choices of single-particle bases and the corresponding structures of single-particle hermitian operators are discussed for several subgroups of D2h(TD).

## Key claim

The paper classifies all possible symmetry-breaking schemes in Hartree-Fock (HF) mean-field calculations for nuclear systems, showing that up to 28 physically distinct broken-symmetry configurations exist within the double point group D2h(TD), ranging from fully symmetric to fully broken. The authors emphasize that this classification is a complete enumeration of possibilities, not a prediction of which symmetries will actually be broken in a given physical system.

## Method

The authors analyzed the subgroup structure of the single group D2 (16 elements) and the double group D2h(TD) (32 elements), which include three mutually perpendicular twofold rotation axes, inversion, and time reversal. They enumerated all nontrivial subgroups by identifying sets of subgroup generators, classifying them by number of generators (1, 2, or 3), number of Cartesian axes involved (0, I, or III), and number of signature operators (A, B, or D). For each subgroup, they derived the corresponding matrix structure of single-particle hermitian operators in a basis of time-reversed states, showing how conserved symmetries impose block-diagonal or reality conditions on the matrices.

## Key numbers

* The single group D2 has 65 nontrivial subgroups: 15 one-generator, 35 two-generator, and 15 three-generator subgroups.
* The double group D2h(TD) has 10 additional subgroups beyond those inherited from D2, giving a total of 75 nontrivial subgroups.
* There are 28 physically different broken-symmetry mean-field schemes: one fully symmetric solution, 26 generic schemes with a nontrivial conserved subgroup, and one fully broken solution.
* The D2 group contains 16 elements: identity, inversion, time reversal, their product, three signatures, three simplexes, three T-signatures, and three T-simplexes.
* The D2h(TD) double group contains 32 elements, including the 16 from D2 plus their partners obtained by multiplication with the 2π rotation operator.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of point symmetries in nuclear mean-field theory presented in Dobaczewski et al. (2000), which introduced the D2 and D2h(TD) groups and their representations. The classification enables systematic construction of HF and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov algorithms adapted to any desired symmetry-breaking pattern, building on earlier implementations by Umar et al. (1991), Yamagami et al. (1998), and Matsuyanagi et al. (1998) that performed calculations without explicit symmetry assumptions. The results provide the mathematical framework for choosing optimal conserved symmetries based on the Jahn-Teller mechanism, allowing future calculations to exploit the simplifications from known symmetry properties while still describing deformed, rotating, or paired nuclear systems.
