# Learning the relations between neutron star and nuclear matter properties with symbolic regression

> The equation of state (EOS) of dense matter in neutron stars (NSs) remains uncertain, particularly at supra-nuclear densities where complex nuclear interactions and the potential presence of exotic matter, like hyperons, come into play. The complex relationships existing between nuclear matter and neutron star properties are investigated. The focus is on their nonlinearities and interdependencies. In our analysis, we apply a machine learning algorithm known as symbolic regression, paired with principal component analysis, to datasets generated from Bayesian inference over relativistic mean-field models. A systematic Principal Component Analysis has allowed to break down the percentage contribution of each element or feature in the relationships obtained. This study examines two main models (datasets): the NL model, which includes nucleonic degrees of freedom; and the NL-hyp model, which includes hyperons in addition to nucleons. Our analysis confirms a robust correlation between the tidal deformability of a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star and $β$-equilibrium pressure at twice the nuclear saturation density. This correlation remains once hyperons are included. The contribution of the different nuclear matter properties at saturation to the radius and tidal deformability was calculated. It was shown that the isovector properties have the largest impact, with a contribution of about 90\%. We also studied the relationship between the proton fraction at different densities and various symmetry energy parameters defined at saturation density. For the hyperon data set, we took into account the effects of the negatively charged hyperon $Ξ$ in order to recover the relationships. Our study reveals the individual impact of various symmetry energy parameters on proton fractions at different densities.

## Key claim

The paper confirms a robust correlation between the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star and the beta-equilibrium pressure at twice the nuclear saturation density, and this correlation persists even when hyperons are included in the model; however, the correlations between proton fraction and symmetry energy parameters become weaker or disappear at higher densities when hyperons are present, requiring inclusion of the negatively charged Xi hyperon fraction to recover the relationships.

## Method

The authors applied symbolic regression (using the PySR algorithm) combined with principal component analysis to datasets generated from Bayesian inference over relativistic mean-field models. Two main datasets were examined: the NL model containing only nucleonic degrees of freedom, and the NL-hyp model which includes Lambda and Xi hyperons in addition to nucleons. The symbolic regression framework processed datasets with 55 features (NL) and 61 features (NL-hyp), running approximately half a million iterations to identify the most robust mathematical expressions linking nuclear matter parameters to neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- The isovector properties contribute about 90% to the radius and tidal deformability of neutron stars
- For the NL dataset at 2 times saturation density, the slope of the symmetry energy (L_sym,0) contributes 58.53% to the proton fraction, followed by J_sym,0 at 22.29% and K_sym,0 at 19.18%
- At 4 times saturation density in the NL dataset, K_sym,0 becomes dominant at 42.81%, Z_sym,0 contributes 20.04%, Q_sym,0 accounts for 18.64%, and L_sym,0 decreases to 14.01%
- For the NL-hyp dataset at 4 times saturation density, the Xi- fraction contributes 37.58%, K_sym,0 contributes 22.37%, Z_sym,0 contributes 21.23%, L_sym,0 contributes 7.41%, J_sym,0 contributes 6.70%, and Q_sym,0 contributes 4.71%
- The best-fit equation for proton fraction at 2 times saturation density using symmetry energy at that density has a Pearson correlation coefficient of 0.99 and mean absolute percentage error of 0.72%
- The saturation density used is rho_0 = 0.153 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends prior correlation analyses between nuclear matter parameters and neutron star properties, building on the Bayesian inference framework of Malik (2023) and the relativistic mean-field models used in that study. The symbolic regression approach complements earlier neural network studies by Ferreira (2019), Krastev (2021), and others, while providing interpretable analytic equations rather than black-box predictions. The findings enable future precise measurements of neutron star properties to constrain the equation of state at densities two to three times greater than saturation density, and the identified correlations can guide observational campaigns targeting specific neutron star masses to extract particular nuclear matter parameters.
