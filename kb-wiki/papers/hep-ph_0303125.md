# Two-loop Improved Truncation of the Ghost-Gluon Dyson-Schwinger Equations: Multiplicatively Renormalizable Propagators and Nonperturbative Running Coupling

> The coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon and ghost propagators are investigated in the Landau gauge using a two-loop improved truncation that preserves the multiplicative renormalizability of the propagators. In this truncation all diagrams contribute to the leading order infrared analysis. The infrared contributions of the nonperturbative two-loop diagrams to the gluon vacuum polarization are computed analytically, and this reveals that infrared power behaved propagator solutions only exist when the squint diagram contribution is taken into account. For small momenta the gluon and ghost dressing functions behave respectively like (p^2)^{2κ} and (p^2)^{-κ}, and the running coupling exhibits a fixed point. The values of the infrared exponent and fixed point depend on the precise details of the truncation. The coupled ghost-gluon system is solved numerically for all momenta, and the solutions have infrared behaviors consistent with the predictions of the infrared analysis. For truncation parameters chosen such that κ=0.5, the two-loop improved truncation is able to produce solutions for the propagators and running coupling which are in very good agreement with recent lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a two-loop improved truncation of the ghost-gluon Dyson-Schwinger equations, which preserves multiplicative renormalizability, yields infrared power-law solutions for the propagators only when the squint diagram contribution is included; the infrared exponent \(\kappa\) and fixed-point coupling depend on truncation details, and for \(\kappa=0.5\) the solutions agree well with lattice simulations.

## Method

The coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge were truncated by replacing the full vertex dressing functions inside the integrals with tree-level kernels, while preserving multiplicative renormalizability through a specific elimination of renormalization constants. The infrared contributions of all one-loop and two-loop diagrams (ghost loop, gluon loop, three-gluon loop, four-gluon loop, sunset, and squint) to the gluon vacuum polarization were computed analytically. For the numerical solution over all momenta, the two-loop integrals were replaced by effective one-loop integrals with the same infrared behavior and consistent subleading ultraviolet behavior.

## Key numbers

* Infrared exponent \(\kappa = 0.5\) was chosen for the truncation parameters that produce solutions in very good agreement with recent lattice simulations.
* The gluon dressing function behaves as \((p^2)^{2\kappa}\) and the ghost dressing function as \((p^2)^{-\kappa}\) for small momenta.
* The running coupling exhibits an infrared fixed point \(\alpha_0\) whose value depends on \(\kappa\) and is proportional to \(1/N_c\).
* Earlier truncations yielded \(\kappa = 0.92\) with fixed point \(\alpha_0 = 9.5\) (vonSmekal 1998) and \(\kappa = 0.77\) with \(\alpha_0 = 11.5\) (Atkinson 1998), while an angular averaging method gave \(\kappa = 1\) and \(\alpha_0 = 4.2\) (Atkinson 1998).

## Context

This work extends the multiplicatively renormalizable truncation proposed by Bloch (2001) by including the two-loop sunset and squint diagrams, which were previously neglected. It contrasts with earlier truncations by vonSmekal (1998) and Atkinson (1998) that violated multiplicative renormalizability and found only the ghost loop contributing to the leading infrared behavior. The results enable direct comparison with lattice simulations (Bloch 2002) and provide a framework for studying nonperturbative QCD phenomena such as confinement and chiral symmetry breaking.
