# Competition Between Induced Symmetry Breaking, Cooper Pairing and Chiral Condensate at Finite Density

> We study the competition between induced symmetry breaking (ISB), Cooper pairing (superconductivity) and chiral condensation at finite non-asymptotic density. Using a quantum mechanical model studied recently by Ilieva and Thirring, we analyze the expectation value of the fermion number density $\la a^*a\ra$ in the presence of chemical potential and discuss relevance of the ISB phenomenon in the model. By Bogoliubov transformation, we obtain the ground state energy and find that a phase with both superconducting and mean field gap is a true ground state. We also study the effective potentials of a two-dimensional field theory model for competitions between $\la\barψψ\ra$, $\laψψ\ra$ and $\laψ^\daggerψ\ra$. We find that in the regime $μ<μ_c$, where $μ_c$ is found to be around $0.4 m_F$ with $m_F$ being the renormalizaton point of chiral condensate, we have a chiral symmetry-breaking phase with almost zero fermion number density and in the case of $μ>μ_c$ the system sits in the mixed phase characterized by $\la ψ^\daggerψ\ra\neq 0$ and $\la ψψ\ra\neq 0$ as long as the renormalization point, $M_0$, corresponding to a superconducting gap is not zero, $M_0\neq 0$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that at finite density, a mixed phase with both a superconducting gap (Cooper pairing) and a mean-field gap can be the true ground state, but the induced symmetry breaking (ISB) phenomenon observed in earlier work by Langfeld and Rho (1999) may not be a relevant notion in the Ilieva-Thirring model; the chiral condensate and the fermion number density cannot coexist in the global minimum of the effective potential, whereas a stable mixed phase of Cooper pairing and fermion number density is possible at high density.

## Method

The authors first analyze a solvable quantum mechanical model due to Ilieva and Thirring, performing a Bogoliubov transformation to obtain the ground-state energy and gap equations for a pairing gap Δ_B and a mean-field gap Δ_M, with interactions concentrated about the Fermi surface. They then study a (1+1)-dimensional field theory model (extending the work of Chodos et al.) using a large-N approximation and Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations to derive effective potentials for the chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩, the Cooper pair condensate ⟨ψψ⟩, and the fermion number density ⟨ψ†ψ⟩, renormalizing the logarithmic divergences.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential μ_c for the transition between chiral symmetry-breaking and mixed phases is found to be around 0.4 m_F, where m_F is the renormalization point of the chiral condensate.
- In the Ilieva-Thirring model with coupling constants λ_B = -5 and λ_M = -5, the critical chemical potential μ_c = 5 (in arbitrary energy units).
- For λ_B = -5 and λ_M = -10, μ_c = 7.6; for λ_B = -5 and λ_M = -2, there are two critical values: μ_c^1 = 2 and μ_c^2 = 5.
- In all three parameter sets for the Ilieva-Thirring model, the normal phase is recovered at high chemical potential: S1 at μ ≥ 8.5, S2 at μ ≥ 12.3, S3 at μ ≥ 9.7.
- In the field theory model, the renormalized effective potential at the global minimum for the chiral symmetry-breaking phase at zero density is V_eff^R = -(1/4π) m_F^2 exp(2 - 2π/λ_R).

## Context

This work extends the study of competition between chiral condensation and color superconductivity at finite density, following the semi-realistic NJL-type model analysis of Langfeld and Rho (1999) which suggested that induced symmetry breaking (ISB) could postpone color superconductivity. The authors use a simpler solvable quantum mechanical model by Ilieva and Thirring and a two-dimensional field theory model by Chodos et al. to gain qualitative understanding. The results suggest that the ISB phenomenon may not be relevant in the Ilieva-Thirring model, and that a mixed phase of Cooper pairing and fermion number density can exist at high density, which could be relevant to the interior of compact stars.
