# Physics with high-luminosity proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC

> The physics case for the operation of high-luminosity proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions during Run 3 and 4 at the LHC is reviewed. The collection of $\mathcal{O}$(1-10 pb$^{-1}$) of proton-lead ($p$Pb) collisions at the LHC will provide unique physics opportunities in a broad range of topics including proton and nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs and nPDFs), generalised parton distributions (GPDs), transverse momentum dependent PDFs (TMDs), low-$x$ QCD and parton saturation, hadron spectroscopy, baseline studies for quark-gluon plasma and parton collectivity, double and triple parton scatterings (DPS/TPS), photon-photon collisions, and physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM); which are not otherwise as clearly accessible by exploiting data from any other colliding system at the LHC. This report summarises the accelerator aspects of high-luminosity $pA$ operation at the LHC, as well as each of the physics topics outlined above, including the relevant experimental measurements that motivate -- much -- larger $pA$ datasets.

## Key claim

High-luminosity proton-lead (pPb) collisions at the LHC, collecting O(1-10 pb⁻¹) of data in Runs 3 and 4, will provide unique physics opportunities across a broad range of topics that are not otherwise clearly accessible from other colliding systems, though the actual integrated luminosity targets are 1.2 pb⁻¹ for ATLAS/CMS and 0.6 pb⁻¹ for ALICE/LHCb, and achieving these targets may require up to four one-month runs with current performance projections.

## Method

The paper reviews the accelerator aspects of high-luminosity pA operation at the LHC, including beam dynamics with asymmetric proton and lead beams requiring different RF configurations and luminosity levelling for ALICE. It surveys the physics case across multiple domains: parton distribution functions (PDFs and nPDFs), generalised parton distributions (GPDs) via exclusive photoproduction in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs), transverse momentum dependent PDFs (TMDs), low-x QCD and saturation, hadron spectroscopy, quark-gluon plasma baseline studies, double and triple parton scatterings (DPS/TPS), photon-photon collisions, and beyond-Standard-Model (BSM) searches. The report summarises projected event yields and statistical sensitivities for specific final states, and compares the reach of pA collisions with that of pp, AA, fixed-target LHC, and the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).

## Key numbers

- Target integrated luminosity for Run 3 and Run 4 pPb collisions: 1.2 pb⁻¹ at ATLAS and CMS, 0.6 pb⁻¹ at ALICE and LHCb
- Total integrated luminosity delivered in Run 1 and Run 2 pPb collisions: 220 nb⁻¹ (ATLAS/CMS), 75 nb⁻¹ (ALICE), 36 nb⁻¹ (LHCb)
- Projected integrated luminosity per one-month pPb run: 0.47 pb⁻¹ (ATLAS/CMS), 0.33 pb⁻¹ (ALICE), 0.15 pb⁻¹ (LHCb)
- With 1.2 pb⁻¹ of data and γ-meson invariant mass squared above 2 GeV², about 16,000 γρ⁰_L pairs and 1,700 γρ⁰_T pairs are expected, probing chiral-even and chiral-odd GPD sectors respectively
- In the small ξ range 5·10⁻⁵ < ξ < 5·10⁻³, about 800 γρ⁰_L pairs are expected with the same integrated luminosity
- Gluon PDF constraints via Shuvaev transform are accessible for Q² ≈ 2.4–22 GeV² and x ≈ 3·10⁻⁶–10⁻³
- Photoproduction in pA collisions accesses photon-nucleon centre-of-mass energies W from 6 GeV to 3 TeV, corresponding to 10⁻⁶ < x < 0.1
- For J/ψ production in LHCb SMOG fixed-target mode, x range is 0.075 < x < 0.44
- In pPb collisions, DPS and TPS yields are enhanced by factors of about 3×A and 9×A compared to pp collisions (A=208)
- Triple charm production is almost 15% (pp) and 20% (pPb) of all inclusive charm production
- 6-jet TPS contribution can be up to 20% of total cross section above pT_jet ≈ 20 GeV in pp, further increased in pPb
- HL-pPb mode outperforms PbPb UPCs in γγ luminosity for m_γγ ≈ 50–300 GeV mass range
- Same-sign W boson production in pPb would allow σ_eff precision of 10% with a few pb⁻¹ of data

## Context

This review extends the physics case for pA collisions at the LHC beyond the initial Run 1 and Run 2 programmes (Jowett 2006, Salgado 2011), which primarily focused on cold nuclear matter effects as baselines for AA collisions (Accardi 2009). The paper builds on prior work on UPC photoproduction measurements (Baltz 2007, ZEUS 2002, H1 2005, LHCb 2018), DPS/TPS phenomenology (d'Enterria 2017, Rinaldi 2018), and BSM searches in photon-photon collisions (d'Enterria 2009, Knapen 2016). The projected sensitivities enable next-step measurements that complement the EIC (which covers x ≈ 0.0002–0.2) by accessing lower x values for saturation studies, and complement fixed-target LHC (SMOG) for high-x PDF constraints. The paper also identifies open questions regarding the muon puzzle in cosmic-ray physics (Pierre Auger 2020, 2021) and the origins of parton collectivity in small systems (Noronha 2024, Grosse-Oetringhaus 2024).
