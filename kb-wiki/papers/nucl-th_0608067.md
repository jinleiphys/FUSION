# On the necessity to include event-by-event fluctuations in experimental evaluation of elliptical flow

> Elliptic flow at RHIC is computed event-by-event with NeXSPheRIO. We show that when symmetry of the particle distribution in relation to the reaction plane is assumed, as usually done in the experimental extraction of elliptic flow, there is a disagreement between the true and reconstructed elliptic flows (15-30% for $η$=0, 30% for $p_\perp$=0.5 GeV). We suggest a possible way to take into account the asymmetry and get good agreement between these elliptic flows.

# On the necessity to include event-by-event fluctuations in experimental evaluation of elliptical flow

## Key claim

The standard experimental method for extracting elliptic flow, which assumes particle distributions are symmetric with respect to the reaction plane, systematically overestimates the true elliptic flow by 15-30% at mid-rapidity and 30% at transverse momentum 0.5 GeV. The authors propose a modified reconstruction formula that accounts for the asymmetry of event-by-event distributions, yielding good agreement between reconstructed and true elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors used the NeXSPheRIO hydrodynamic code, which combines the NeXus event generator for fluctuating initial conditions with the SPheRIO smoothed particle hydrodynamics solver, to simulate Au+Au collisions at RHIC. They computed the true elliptic flow using the known impact parameter angle and compared it to the experimentally reconstructed elliptic flow obtained via the standard hit-based reaction plane method (as used by PHOBOS), both with and without the usual reaction plane resolution correction. They then derived and tested an alternative reconstruction formula that accounts for the sine terms in the Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal distribution.

## Key numbers

- True elliptic flow $v_2^b$ is overestimated by 15-30% at $\eta=0$ when using the standard reaction plane method, depending on centrality
- True elliptic flow $v_2^b$ is overestimated by 30% at $p_\perp=0.5$ GeV for charged hadrons with $0<\eta<1.5$ in the 50% most central collisions
- The freeze-out temperature parameter was set to 150 MeV, which gave good agreement with PHOBOS charged particle pseudorapidity and transverse momentum distributions for all centrality windows
- The disagreement between true and reconstructed elliptic flow was observed for all three PHOBOS centrality windows and was more pronounced in the most central window

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether event-by-event fluctuations or non-flow correlations are responsible for discrepancies between true and reconstructed elliptic flow, following studies by Miller (using Monte Carlo Glauber calculations) and Zhu (using the UrQMD model), which reached conflicting conclusions about the importance of fluctuations versus non-flow effects. The authors show that with a hydrodynamic model that reproduces RHIC data, the assumption of symmetry around the reaction plane, rather than fluctuations or non-flow effects per se, is the primary cause of the discrepancy. This finding has implications for interpreting elliptic flow data to constrain initial conditions and thermalization, as previous work by Hirano (2005, 2006) used $v_2(\eta)$ data to draw conclusions about the need for viscosity or specific initial condition models; the authors argue those conclusions would be affected if $v_2(\eta)$ data are systematically overestimated as their results suggest.
