# Recent Developments in Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering (Theory)

> 6 pages, 5 figures, Plenary talk at 18th International Workshop on Neutrino Factories, Super beams and Beta beams (NuFact2016), August 21-27 2016, Quy Nhon, Vietnam

# Recent Developments in Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering (Theory)

## Key claim
Selected theoretical developments in neutrino-nucleus scattering from 2015-2016 show that ab initio Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of inclusive electron scattering on 12C, when including both one- and two-body currents, agree excellently with data and are significantly enhanced over one-body-only calculations, but this approach is limited to light nuclei due to computational cost. For the resonance region, a dynamical coupled-channels model provides the first treatment of neutrino-induced double-pion production that accounts for relevant resonance contributions, though its axial-current Q2-dependence relies on a simple dipole ansatz with MA=1.02 GeV due to scarce experimental data.

## Method
For the quasi-elastic region, the authors used the Green's function Monte Carlo method to solve the nuclear Schrodinger equation with two- and three-nucleon potentials, then evaluated the Laplace transform of response functions for inclusive electron scattering on 12C and inverted it using the maximum entropy method. For the resonance region, they extended a dynamical coupled-channels model previously fitted to 23,000 data points of pion-nucleon and photon-induced reactions to neutrino reactions by determining vector current form factors from electroproduction data and axial current matrix elements from chiral Lagrangian with PCAC constraints, then calculated cross sections for neutrino-induced single- and double-pion production. A separate study used the SL dynamical model with impulse, NN rescattering, and piN rescattering terms to compute final-state interaction effects on neutrino-deuteron pion production reactions.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino beam at T2K peaks at approximately 0.6 GeV
- Transverse response functions for 12C calculated at momentum transfers |q| = 300, 380, and 570 MeV
- The DCC model was fitted to approximately 23,000 data points of piN, gamma p -> piN, etaN, KLambda, KSigma reactions
- Axial mass assumed as MA = 1.02 GeV for all axial-coupling Q2-dependence
- The bare axial N-Delta(1232) coupling constant from PCAC was multiplied by 0.8 to better fit ANL data for nu_mu p -> mu- pi+ p
- For neutrino-deuteron calculations, muon kinematics chosen at E_nu = 1 GeV, theta_mu = 25 degrees, with E_mu = 550, 600, 650 MeV

## Context
This review extends the line of work on neutrino-nucleus scattering models needed for next-generation oscillation experiments like T2K and DUNE, which require accurate understanding of reactions from quasi-elastic through resonance regions. The ab initio GFMC calculations by Lovato et al. (2016) validate predictive power for light nuclei up to A=12, providing a benchmark for other many-body models that must be applied to heavier nuclei. The DCC model by Nakamura et al. (2015) overcomes limitations of previous models that lacked multi-channel unitarity and did not treat double-pion production, while the FSI study by Wu et al. (2015) shows that previous analyses of ANL and BNL bubble chamber data ignored sizable final-state interactions in extracting elementary cross sections from deuteron targets, motivating reanalysis of those datasets.
