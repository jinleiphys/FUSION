# Delta--Excitation and Exchange Corrections for NN--Bremsstrahlung

> The role of the relativistic amplitudes for a number of ${\cal O}(k)$ processes usually neglected in potential model calculations of NN--bremsstrahlung is investigated. In particular, we consider the $Δ$--excitation pole contributions related to the one--pion and one--rho exchange and in addition include the exchange contributions induced by the radiative $ω,\,ρ\to πγ$ decays. The contributions are calculated from relativistic Born amplitudes fitted to $Δ$--production and absorption data in the energy range up to 1 GeV and then used to supplement potential model and soft photon calculations for nucleon--nucleon bremsstrahlung. The effects on $NNγ$--observables, although moderate in general, are found to be important in some kinematic domains.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the role of relativistic amplitudes for Δ-excitation and radiative ω/ρ→πγ decay processes, which are usually neglected in potential model calculations of NN-bremsstrahlung, and finds that while these corrections are moderate overall, they become important in certain kinematic domains, with the Δ-excitation dominating but being partly cancelled by the radiative decay contributions.

## Method

The authors calculated relativistic Born amplitudes for Δ-excitation pole contributions (via one-pion and one-rho exchange) and for exchange contributions from radiative ω and ρ decays, using interaction Lagrangians fitted to Δ-production and absorption data up to 1 GeV. These amplitudes were then rescaled by an energy-dependent factor to compensate for neglecting higher-order Born terms, and added coherently to potential model and soft photon approximation (SPA) amplitudes for ppγ and npγ observables. Two different coupled-channel parameter sets (model A from Ter Haar (1987) and model B from Machleidt (1989)) were compared to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The Δ-excitation and radiative decay corrections increase the 280 MeV ppγ integrated cross section d²σ/dΩ₁dΩ₂ by a maximum of roughly 7.5% at symmetric proton angles θ₁∼θ₂∼20°.
- The relative effect on the triple differential cross section d³σ/dΩ₁dΩ₂dθ_γ at 280 MeV is typically ≤20%, maximum for photon emission around θ_γ=90°.
- At 730 MeV, the corrections reach about 20% of the SPA cross section at photon energy k=150 MeV, but are not sufficient to fit experimental data.
- The Δ-excitation amplitude was scaled by factors g=1.95 at 280 MeV and g=1.65 at 730 MeV, based on comparison with coupled-channel Δ-absorption cross sections below 300 MeV and Δ-production data at 800 MeV and 970 MeV.
- The radiative decay widths used are Γ(ω→πγ)=716±75 keV, Γ(ρ⁰→πγ)=121±31 keV, and Γ(ρ^±→πγ)=68±7 keV.
- The Δ-excitation contribution to npγ observables is negligible, confirming the result of Bohannon (1977).

## Context

This work extends earlier Born-level calculations of Δ-excitation in NN-bremsstrahlung by Szyjewicz (1977) and Tiator (1978), and the radiative ω→πγ decay treatment by Kamal (1977), by incorporating more recent experimental constraints on Δ-production and absorption cross sections and using two modern coupled-channel parameter sets. The results are complementary to the full coupled-channel Δ calculation by Jonsson (1993), with good agreement for low-energy cross sections and analyzing powers, but the present approach can be extended to higher energies beyond the pion production threshold. The findings enable improved estimates of the theoretical uncertainties in NNγ observables and show that these corrections cannot resolve the discrepancy between SPA predictions and 730 MeV ppγ data.
