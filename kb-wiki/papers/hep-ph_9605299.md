# RPA-Approach to the Excitations of the Nucleon, Part I: Theory

> In this paper we develop a theoretical framework which allows us to study excitations of the nucleon. Assuming an effective two-body interaction as a model for low-energy QCD, we derive a relativistic TDHF equation for a many-body system of quarks. To render the Dirac-sea contribution tothe mean field finite, we introduce a symmetry conserving regularization scheme. In the small amplitude limit we derive an RPA equation. The structure of the $ph$ interaction and modifications due to the regularization scheme are discussed. We give a prescription to obtain a nucleon state with good angular momentum (J) and isospin (T) quantum numbers on mean-field level. To study excitations, we develop a tensor-RPA approach, which is an extension of the conventional RPA techniques to systems with a nonscalar ground state. This allows us to construct excited states with good (J/T) quantum numbers. We discuss a method to reduce the overcomplete $ph$-space and compute the tensor-RPA interaction matrix elements. Finally we extend our scheme to include ({3/2}^+, {3/2})-states.

## Key claim

The paper develops a theoretical framework for studying nucleon excitations using a relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA) derived from a time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation for a many-quark system, but the approach is conditional on introducing a symmetry-conserving regularization scheme to render the Dirac-sea contribution to the mean field finite, and on extending conventional RPA to a tensor-RPA method for systems with a nonscalar ground state to construct states with good angular momentum and isospin quantum numbers.

## Method

Starting from an effective two-body interaction as a model for low-energy QCD, the authors derive a relativistic TDHF equation for a many-body system of quarks using the Martin-Schwinger hierarchy for time-ordered Green's functions. They introduce a regularization scheme based on a cut-off function that acts on the mean-field Hamiltonian itself, preserving the symmetries of the underlying interaction. In the small-amplitude limit, they derive an RPA equation and develop a tensor-RPA approach, which extends conventional RPA to systems with a nonscalar ground state, allowing construction of excited states with good angular momentum (J) and isospin (T) quantum numbers. They also discuss a method to reduce the overcomplete particle-hole space and compute the tensor-RPA interaction matrix elements, and extend the scheme to include (3/2^+, 3/2)-states.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon ground state is constructed with quantum numbers (J^p, T) = (1/2^+, 1/2), using a wavefunction that involves a sum over three-quark configurations with coupling coefficients of the group SU(2)_J ⊗ SU(2)_T.
* The valence shell is assumed to be a κ = 1 or κ = -1 state, carrying an angular momentum of J = 1/2.
* The regularization cut-off function is exemplified as R_l[ε_i] = exp(-exp(-l(ε_i + Λ))), where l has dimension [MeV^{-1}], and in the limit l → ∞ this approaches θ(ε_i + Λ).
* The gap equation for the chiral condensate in the NJL model is given as ⟨Ψ̄Ψ⟩ ∝ ∫_m^∞ dE √(E² - m²) m R²[E], where m is the constituent quark mass.

## Context

This work extends relativistic many-body methods developed in nuclear-structure physics, such as the Dirac-RPA approaches of Dawson and Furnstahl (1990) and others, to the study of nucleon excitations in quark models. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic quark model of Isgur and Karl (1978) and the hedgehog symmetry approach used in the Skyrme model (Skyrme, 1961) and NJL soliton models (e.g., Alkofer, Reinhardt, and Weigel, 1995; Christov et al., 1995), by constructing states as tensors of the full symmetry group SU(2)_J ⊗ SU(2)_T rather than relying on grand-spin symmetry. The tensor-RPA method, originally developed for nuclear structure with odd numbers of protons or neutrons (Rowe and Ngo-Trong, 1975), is generalized and adapted to Dirac-RPA theory for the nucleon, enabling computation of wavefunctions and excitation energies for any (J/T)-channel. A subsequent paper (Part II) applies the method with a specific choice of interaction.
