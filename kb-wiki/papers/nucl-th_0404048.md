# Traces of Thermalization at RHIC

> I argue that measurements of Au+Au collisions at 20, 130 and 200 GeV of the centrality dependence of the mean p_t together with p_t and net-charge fluctuations reflect the approach to local thermal equilibrium.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the similar centrality dependence of mean transverse momentum and dynamic p_t fluctuations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies reflects the approach to local thermal equilibrium, but this interpretation depends on the assumption that the survival probability S decreases with centrality and that initial correlations are independent of those near local equilibrium.

## Method
The author uses the Boltzmann transport equation with Langevin noise terms to model the evolution of the phase space distribution, deriving expressions for mean p_t and dynamic p_t fluctuations as functions of the survival probability S = exp(-integral of collision rate over time). The model parameters are fit to STAR and PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at 20, 130, and 200 GeV, using the wounded nucleon model and HIJING simulations to estimate initial correlations and the spatial correlation function near local equilibrium.

## Key numbers
- Mean p_t follows ⟨p_t⟩ = ⟨p_t⟩_o S + ⟨p_t⟩_e (1-S), with α = 4 and γ = 0.15 in central collisions
- Dynamic p_t fluctuations follow ⟨δp_t1 δp_t2⟩ = ⟨δp_t1 δp_t2⟩_o S^2 + ⟨δp_t1 δp_t2⟩_e (1-S^2)
- ISR measurements imply ⟨δp_t1 δp_t2⟩_pp / ⟨p_t⟩_pp^2 ≈ 0.015
- The dimensionless quantity F = 0.046 for ξ_t/R_t = 1/6 in the near-equilibrium calculation
- The survival probability S ≈ (τ_0/τ_F)^α with α = ν_0 τ_0, where n(τ) ∝ τ^{-1} from longitudinal expansion

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Gavin 2004) by incorporating new STAR data on p_t fluctuations and net-charge fluctuations at 20, 130, and 200 GeV. The model builds on the Boltzmann transport approach and uses the wounded nucleon model for initial conditions, while HIJING simulations (without rescattering) are used to estimate correlation parameters. The results suggest that thermalization can explain the centrality dependence of both one-body and two-body p_t observables, and the approach is extended to net-charge fluctuations using balance function data. The work enables further study of how local equilibrium is approached in heavy-ion collisions and how radial flow or jets may affect the most central collisions at the highest beam energies.
