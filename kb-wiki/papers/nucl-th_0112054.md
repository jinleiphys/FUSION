> The outline of the presentation is as follows: I. Nucleon Distribution in the Deuteron from $e-D$ and $p-D$ Processes. II. Quark Distribution in Deuteron from its Fragmentation to Pions and Deep Inelastic $e-D$ scattering. III.Difference of These Two Distributions and Its Possible Understanding.

# Deuteron Structure at Small N-N Distances from Inelastic e-D and p-D Reactions

## Key claim

The nucleon distribution extracted from deuteron stripping and elastic electron scattering agrees at low momenta, but the high-momentum tail (k > 0.65 GeV/c) from stripping data cannot be described by standard non-relativistic nucleon degrees of freedom alone, requiring non-nucleon components such as a 2(3q) admixture or NN*, Delta-Delta configurations. Additionally, the quark distribution in the deuteron extracted from fragmentation to pions differs from that extracted from deep inelastic scattering, and this difference can be understood by distinguishing constituent quarks (participating in soft hadron processes) from current valence quarks (interacting in DIS), which have different x-distributions in the nucleon.

## Method

The authors compare the nucleon momentum distribution in the deuteron, |Psi_D(k)|^2, extracted from elastic e-D scattering data and from D p -> p X stripping data (Ableev et al., 1983), analyzing discrepancies at intermediate momenta (0.3-0.45 GeV/c) as due to secondary triangle graph contributions with virtual meson exchange. They then analyze the inclusive pion spectrum from D p -> pi X fragmentation at large light-front variable z > 1, extracting the valence quark distribution in the deuteron q_D(z), and compare this to the deuteron structure function F_2D(x) from deep inelastic scattering data (BCDMS and CCFR collaborations) at x > 1, interpreting the difference via a quark model (Benhar et al., 1997) that calculates the relativistic invariant phase space volume available to a quark when quark distributions of different nucleons overlap.

## Key numbers

- The discrepancy between calculated and experimental nucleon distribution in the deuteron appears at nucleon momenta 0.3 (GeV/c) < k < 0.45 (GeV/c), attributable to secondary interactions rather than non-nucleon degrees of freedom.
- The high-momentum tail of the nucleon distribution at k > 0.65 (GeV/c) from D p -> p X stripping data (Ableev et al., 1983) cannot be described by standard non-relativistic nucleon degrees of freedom.
- The probability of the non-nucleon component (2(3q) admixture) in the deuteron wave function is 2% to 4%, as used in calculations of the backward pion spectrum.
- The constituent quark distribution in the deuteron at z > 1 follows q^cons_D(z) ~ (1/sqrt(z)) (2 - z)^4.5, while the current valence quark distribution follows q^cur_D(z) ~ (1/sqrt(z)) (2 - z)^(8.5 to 9.5).
- The current quark exponent b_N is 3.5 to 4.5 at Q^2 = Q_0^2 ~ 2 to 3 (GeV/c)^2 from DIS data, while the constituent quark exponent b_N = 3/2 from Regge asymptotic.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of deuteron structure beyond the conventional non-relativistic nucleon picture, building on earlier studies of non-nucleon components in the deuteron wave function by Burov et al. (1984), Kobushkin and Vizireva (1982), and Efremov et al. (1988). The approach enables a unified description of both soft hadron fragmentation processes (D p -> pi X) and deep inelastic scattering (e-D DIS) by distinguishing constituent quarks from current quarks, following the quark model of Benhar et al. (1997). The results suggest that the deuteron fragmentation to pions provides complementary information about short-range N-N structure that cannot be obtained from elastic scattering or DIS alone.
