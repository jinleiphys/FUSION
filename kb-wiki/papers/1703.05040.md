# Light fragment production at CERN Super Proton Synchrotron

> Recent data on the deutron and $^3$He production in central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) energies measured by the NA49 collaboration are analyzed within the model of the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) complemented by the coalescence model for the light-fragment production. The simulations are performed with different equations of state---with and without deconfinement transition. It is found that scenarios with the deconfinement transition are preferable for reproduction rapidity distributions of deuterons and $^3$He, the corresponding results well agree with the experimental data. At the same time the calculated transverse-mass spectra of $^3$He at midrapidity do not that nice agree with the experimental data. The latter apparently indicates that coalescence coefficients should be temperature and/or momentum dependent.

# Light fragment production at CERN Super Proton Synchrotron

## Key claim

Scenarios with a deconfinement transition (first-order phase transition or smooth crossover) are preferable for reproducing the rapidity distributions of deuterons and ³He in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, but the calculated transverse-mass spectra of ³He at midrapidity do not agree as nicely with the experimental data, indicating that coalescence coefficients should be temperature and/or momentum dependent.

## Method

The authors simulated central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies using the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats projectile, target, and fireball matter as interpenetrating fluids coupled by friction terms. Light fragment formation (deuterons and ³He) was incorporated via a coalescence model applied locally in six-dimensional phase space at the freeze-out stage, using a single phenomenological parameter \(P_{NZ}\) for each fragment species fitted to reproduce the normalization of the experimental spectra. Simulations were performed with three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, a first-order phase transition EoS, and a smooth crossover EoS involving deconfinement.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence parameter for deuterons \(P(\mathrm{d})\) ranges from 513 MeV/c at 20\(A\) GeV to 425 MeV/c at 158\(A\) GeV in the crossover scenario.
- Coalescence parameter for ³He \(P(^3\mathrm{He})\) ranges from 474 MeV/c at 20\(A\) GeV to 409 MeV/c at 158\(A\) GeV in the crossover scenario.
- Mean freeze-out baryon density \(\langle n_c/n_0\rangle\) decreases from 0.61 at 20\(A\) GeV to 0.43 at 158\(A\) GeV in the crossover scenario.
- Reduced coalescence parameters \(P(\mathrm{d})\langle n_c/n_0\rangle^{-1/3}\) are constant within approximately 2% across the energy range (values 606, 563, 562, 550, 563 MeV/c from 20 to 158\(A\) GeV), except at 20\(A\) GeV.
- The QGSM recipe \(p_{NZ} r_{NZ} = \hbar\) yields \(P^{\mathrm{QGSM}}(\mathrm{d}) = 512\) MeV/c for deuterons, comparable to the fitted values.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of NA49 light-fragment data by Anticic et al. (2016) and theoretical studies by Sun et al. (2017) and Mrowczynski (2016), which used schematic fireball-like models focusing on total or midrapidity yields. The present study uses full three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations (3FD) previously applied to proton rapidity and transverse-momentum distributions by Ivanov et al. (2013). The results enable future comparisons with the thermodynamical approach to fragment production within the THESEUS event generator (Batyuk et al., 2016; Bastian et al., 2016) and motivate introduction of temperature- and momentum-dependent coalescence coefficients.
