# Gluon EMC effects in nuclear matter

> We investigate the gluonic structure of nuclei within a mean-field model of nuclear structure based upon the modification of the structure of a bound nucleon, with the nucleon described by the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. This approach has been shown to reproduce the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect, involving the ratio of the spin-independent structure functions of a heavier nucleus to that of the deuteron. It also predicts a significant nuclear modification for the spin structure functions, known as the polarized EMC effect. Here we report sizeable nuclear modifications of the gluon distributions (a "gluon EMC effect") for the ratios of both the unpolarized and polarized gluon distributions in nuclear matter to those of a free nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper predicts sizeable nuclear modifications of both unpolarized and polarized gluon distributions in nuclear matter (a "gluon EMC effect") for the first time, but these effects are generated indirectly through QCD evolution of medium-modified quark distributions rather than from direct gluon-nuclear interactions, providing a baseline for future studies.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to describe nucleons as quark-diquark bound states, then self-consistently modify the nucleon structure in nuclear matter through Lorentz scalar and vector mean-fields. Gluon distributions, which vanish at the model scale (Q0^2 = 0.16 GeV^2), are generated dynamically by QCD evolution at both NLO and NNLO to a scale of Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 using the APFEL program. The nuclear gluon distributions inherit medium modifications from the altered quark distributions that serve as input to the evolution.

## Key numbers

- Model scale: Q0^2 = 0.16 GeV^2, with Q0 of order the constituent quark mass (approximately 400 MeV)
- Results evolved to Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 for comparison with phenomenological PDFs
- The unpolarized gluon EMC ratio g_A(x)/g_p(x) shows a sizeable suppression in the valence region, comparable in magnitude to the familiar quark EMC effect for F_2A(x)/F_2N(x)
- The polarized gluon EMC ratio Δg_A(x)/Δg_p(x) is predicted to be larger than the unpolarized gluon EMC effect

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NJL model studies that successfully reproduced the unpolarized EMC effect across the periodic table (Cloet 2006, Cloet 2009, Cloet 2012) and predicted a significant polarized EMC effect (Cloet 2006, Cloet 2005). The only experimental indication of nuclear gluon behavior came from the CMS Collaboration (2018) reporting strong suppression of gluon PDFs at large x in lead ions, while earlier New Muon Collaboration studies of J/Psi production suggested possible gluon enhancement. The predictions are directly relevant to planned measurements at Jefferson Lab (polarized EMC effect in 7Li) and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
