# Separable character of ab initio No-Core Shell Model one-body densities

> Motivated by recent findings on the separability of optical potentials that are derived from folding off-shell densities with off-shell nucleon-nucleon amplitudes, we study the off-shell character of one-body density matrices created within the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM). Concentrating on nuclei with a 0$^+$ ground state from $^4$He through $^{48}$Ca, we investigate the off-shell character of their one-body density matrices in momentum space when using the momentum transfer and the average momentum as variables. A singular value decomposition of the one-body density matrices reveals that they can be characterized by only very few terms, depending on the mass number of the nucleus. These findings are independent of the nucleon-nucleon interactions employed, as well as from computational specifics as grid spacing and size of the model space.

## Key claim

The off-shell one-body density matrices from NCSM calculations for nuclei with 0⁺ ground states are separable in momentum transfer \(q\) and average momentum \(\mathcal{K}\), with the rank of separability increasing with the number of filled nuclear shells (rank-2 for p-shell, rank-3 for sd-shell, rank-4 for pf-shell), and this behavior is independent of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, the harmonic oscillator parameter \(\hbar\omega\), and the model space truncation \(N_{\rm max}\).

## Method

The authors computed translationally-invariant off-shell one-body density matrices in momentum space from NCSM wavefunctions for 0⁺ ground-state nuclei from ⁴He through ⁴⁸Ca, using the momentum transfer \(q\) and average momentum \(\mathcal{K}\) as variables. They performed a singular value decomposition (SVD) on the dominant \(l_q = l_{\mathcal{K}} = 0\) partial wave of the density matrix, discretized on an \(N \times N\) grid with \(N=301\) and a grid spacing of \(0.0\bar{3}\) fm⁻¹, and determined the minimum rank \(R\) needed to achieve a tolerance \(\eta(R) = 1 - \text{PV}(R) < 10^{-5}\), where PV is the preserved variance.

## Key numbers

*   For ⁴He at \(N_{\rm max}=16\) with NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) and \(\hbar\omega = 20\) MeV, a rank-2 separable function is needed to reach \(\eta(R) < 10^{-5}\); at \(N_{\rm max}=0\) a rank-1 function suffices.
*   For ¹⁶O at \(N_{\rm max}=10\) with NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) and \(\hbar\omega = 20\) MeV, \(\eta(R=2)\) drops below the \(10^{-5}\) tolerance threshold, indicating a rank-2 separable function.
*   For ⁴⁰Ca at \(N_{\rm max}=6\) with NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) and \(\hbar\omega = 20\) MeV, \(\eta(R=3)\) falls below the \(10^{-5}\) tolerance threshold, indicating a rank-3 separable function.
*   For ⁴⁸Ca at \(N_{\rm max}=2\) with NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) and \(\hbar\omega = 20\) MeV, a rank-4 separable function is required to reach \(\eta(R) < 10^{-5}\).
*   For the open sd-shell nuclei ²⁸Si and ³²S at \(N_{\rm max}=4\) with NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) and \(\hbar\omega = 20\) MeV, \(R=3\) is needed for \(\eta(R) < 10^{-5}\); for ²⁰Ne at \(N_{\rm max}=4\), \(\eta(R=2)\) is slightly below the threshold.
*   The SVD analysis used a grid size \(N=301\) with a grid spacing of \(0.0\bar{3}\) fm⁻¹ and a momentum cutoff of 10 fm⁻¹; results were stable for grid spacing of 0.1 fm⁻¹ and cutoff at least 10.0 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Arellano and Blanchon (2024), who discovered a separable character in folding optical potentials for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering from 0⁺ nuclei in the mass range \(40 \leq A \leq 208\) at beam energies between 40 and 400 MeV. By demonstrating that the off-shell one-body density matrices themselves are separable with low rank, the authors provide a microscopic explanation for the separability observed in the folded optical potentials. The results enable the construction of low-rank separable representations of nuclear densities for use in reaction calculations, potentially simplifying the computation of folding optical potentials for nuclei up to ⁴⁸Ca and beyond.
