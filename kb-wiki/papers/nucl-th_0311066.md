# General Formulae of Invariant Functions of the Generalized Reaction γN - γN in the Effective Lagrangians Method

> The crossed channels of generalized reaction γN - γN have been considered. The transformation coefficients from the independent helicity amplitudes to the invariant functions are calculated. The explicit expressions for invariant functions have been obtained with the subject to the contribution of Born diagrams in s-, u-, and t-channel and six resonances in s- and u-channel. It has been shown that the obtained invariant functions meet the requirements of crossing-symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper derives general analytical formulae for the six invariant functions of the generalized reaction γN → γN (covering Compton scattering on nucleons and antinucleons, and nucleon-antinucleon annihilation into two photons) by including Born diagrams in all three channels and six nucleon resonances, with the hedge that the resulting invariant functions satisfy crossing-symmetry requirements only when the specific transformation properties of the invariant combinations under photon and nucleon crossing are applied.

## Method

The authors constructed a P-, C-, T-, and gauge-invariant amplitude for the s-channel reaction γN → γN using six independent invariant combinations built from four-vectors K, Q, P', and N. They computed the 6×6 transformation matrix between the six independent helicity amplitudes and the six invariant functions f_i(s,t) using the Mathematica symbolic package, then obtained explicit expressions for the invariant functions by summing contributions from Born diagrams in s-, u-, and t-channels and from six resonances (P33(1232), P11(1430), S11(1500), D13(1505), S31(1620), D33(1700)) in s- and u-channels, using effective Lagrangians written in the most general form compatible with hermiticity and P-, T-, C-invariance.

## Key numbers

- The total number of helicity amplitudes for the reaction γ⁻ + 1/2⁺ → γ⁻ + 1/2⁺ is 16, reduced to 8 by P-invariance and further to 6 by T-invariance.
- The transformation matrix ‖u_ij‖ has 8 zero elements out of 36, with the remaining 28 nonzero elements divided into 4 groups; for example, u_{1,1} = -½ u_{1,3} = u_{1,5} = -u_{3,1} = -½ u_{3,3} = -u_{3,5} = M u_{6,1} = -M u_{6,5} = -M² sec(θ/2)/(s - M²).
- The scattering angle θ in the center-of-mass system of the s-channel satisfies cos(θ) = 1 + 2 s t / (M² - s)².
- Six resonances are included: P33(1232), P11(1430), S11(1500), D13(1505), S31(1620), D33(1700).

## Context

This work extends the approach used for pion photoproduction γN → πN by Garcilazo and Drechsel (cited as gar, drech) to the generalized γN → γN reaction, providing explicit invariant functions that those earlier papers did not calculate. The availability of these analytical formulae enables faster numerical simulation of Compton scattering processes and is indispensable for analytic calculation of reaction amplitudes on nuclei, covering energies from threshold up to 1 GeV where higher nucleon resonances become important.
