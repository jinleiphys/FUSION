# Probing the structural evolution along the fission path in the superheavy nucleus $^{256}$Sg

> The evolution of structure property along the fission path in the superheavy nucleus $^{256}$Sg is predicted through the multi-dimensional potential-energy(or Routhian)-surface calculations,in which the phenomenological deformed Woods-Saxon potential is adopted. Calculated nuclear deformations and fission barriers for $^{256}_{106}$Sg$_{150}$ and its neighbors, e.g., $^{258,260}$Sg, $^{254}$Rf and $^{252}$No are presented and compared with other theoretical results. A series of energy maps and curves are provided and used to evaluate the corresponding shape-instability properties, especially in the directions of triaxial $Îł$ and different hexadecapole deformations (e.g., $Î±_{40}$, $Î±_{42}$ and $Î±_{44}$). It is found that the triaxial deformation may help the nucleus bypass the first fission-barrier of the axial case. After the first minimum in the nuclear energy surface, the fission pathway of the nucleus can be affected by $Îł$ and hexadecapole deformation degrees of freedom. In addition, microscopic single-particle structure, pairing and Coriolis effects are briefly investigated and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that triaxial deformation may help the superheavy nucleus \(^{256}\)Sg bypass the first fission barrier of the axial case, and that after the first minimum in the nuclear energy surface, the fission pathway can be affected by both \(\gamma\) and hexadecapole deformation degrees of freedom; these conclusions are drawn from multi-dimensional potential-energy-surface calculations using a phenomenological deformed Woods-Saxon potential, and the authors note that it is impossible to calculate in the full deformation space.

## Method

The authors performed multi-dimensional potential-energy (or Routhian)-surface calculations using the macroscopic-microscopic model with a phenomenological deformed Woods-Saxon potential. The macroscopic energy was given by the standard liquid-drop model with Myers-Swiatecki parameters, while the microscopic part included Strutinsky shell corrections (sixth-order Hermite polynomial, smoothing range \(\gamma = 1.20\hbar\omega_0\)) and Lipkin-Nogami pairing correlations with monopole and doubly stretched quadrupole pairing forces. The deformation space included quadrupole (\(\beta_2\), \(\gamma\)) and hexadecapole (\(\beta_4\)) degrees of freedom, and one-dimensional cranking calculations were performed for rotating nuclei.

- The inner fission barrier height for \(^{256}\)Sg in the axial case is approximately 6.5 MeV (from Fig. 3, as described in the text).
- The inclusion of triaxial deformation (\(\gamma\)) lowers the first fission barrier of \(^{256}\)Sg by about 0.5 MeV compared to the axial case (from Fig. 3).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group on triaxial effects on inner fission barriers in transuranium nuclei (Chai 2018) and on octupole correlation properties in superheavy nuclei (Wang 2012). It specifically focuses on the lightest even-even Sg isotope not yet synthesized, \(^{256}\)Sg, and investigates the role of non-axial hexadecapole deformations (\(\alpha_{40}\), \(\alpha_{42}\), \(\alpha_{44}\)) along the full fission path, going beyond earlier work that focused only on the first barrier. The results enable predictions for the fission properties of \(^{256}\)Sg as a candidate for future synthesis via fusion-evaporation reactions, and test the validity of Woods-Saxon model parameters in the superheavy region without preconceived knowledge from measured densities or single-particle energies.
