# Beta-decay implications for the W-boson mass anomaly

> We point out the necessity to consider $β$-decay observables in resolutions of the $W$-boson anomaly in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory that go beyond pure oblique corrections. We demonstrate that present global analyses that explain the $W$-boson mass anomaly predict a large, percent-level, violation of first-row CKM unitarity. We investigate what solutions to the $W$-boson mass anomaly survive after including $β$-decay constraints.

## Key claim

Global analyses of electroweak precision observables (EWPO) that explain the W-boson mass anomaly using Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) operators beyond oblique parameters predict a large, percent-level violation of first-row CKM unitarity, which is inconsistent with precision beta-decay data; this tension can be resolved by including the CKM unitarity constraint and an additional operator, but the resulting scenario is testable at the LHC.

## Method

The authors performed a SMEFT fit at dimension-six to the EWPO data used in de Blas (2022), assuming U(3)^5 flavor symmetry (Minimal Flavor Violation). They then computed the predicted shift to first-row CKM unitarity, quantified by Delta_CKM = |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 - 1, from the fitted Wilson coefficients. They compared this prediction to the PDG average of Delta_CKM and then performed a combined fit of EWPO and Delta_CKM, also incorporating LHC constraints on the operator C_lq^(3) from pp to ll data.

## Key numbers

* The CDF measurement of the W-boson mass is m_W = 80433.5 +/- 9.4 MeV.
* The EWPO-only fit (de Blas 2022) gives C_Delta = -(0.19 +/- 0.09) TeV^{-2}, which implies Delta_CKM = -(0.012 +/- 0.005) (a percent-level violation).
* The PDG average of Delta_CKM is -0.0015(7), a much smaller violation.
* Including Delta_CKM in the fit shifts C_Delta to -(0.027 +/- 0.011) TeV^{-2}.
* The single-coupling LHC bound on C_lq^(3) from 8 TeV pp to ll data is -(0.028 +/- 0.028) TeV^{-2} at 95% C.L.
* The global-fit LHC bound on C_lq^(3) from the same data is -(0.05 +/- 0.1) TeV^{-2} at 95% C.L.

## Context

This work extends the EWPO analyses of de Blas (2022), Bagnaschi (2022), and Balkin (2022), which explained the W-boson mass anomaly using SMEFT but did not include hadronic beta-decay constraints. The authors show that those fits are severely disfavored by CKM unitarity tests from superallowed nuclear beta decays and kaon decays (Hardy 2020, Seng 2021). The paper demonstrates that including the CKM unitarity constraint and the operator O_lq^(3) can reconcile the W-boson mass anomaly with beta-decay data, and that this scenario can be tested with existing 13 TeV LHC data on pp to ll and pp to l nu processes.
