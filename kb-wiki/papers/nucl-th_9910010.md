# Threshold Behaviour of Meson-Nucleon-$S_{11}$(1535) Vertexfunctions and Determination of the $S_{11}$(1535) Mixing Angle

> A new method for the determination of the spin-1/2-3/2-mixing angle and the range parameter of the quarkmodel wavefunction of the resonance $S^-_{11}(1535)$ is presented. The method ist based on a quantitative calculation of the total cross section of $pp\to ppη$ at threshold. The quantitative on-shell treatment of ISI and FSI is discussed.

## Key claim

A new method determines the spin-1/2-3/2 mixing angle and range parameter of the quark-model wavefunction of the S11(1535) resonance by calculating the total cross section of pp → ppη at threshold, but the extracted numbers are preliminary due to uncertainties in bare meson-nucleon-S11(1535) couplings and the still improvable treatment of initial and final state interactions.

## Method

The total cross section of pp → ppη at threshold was calculated within a relativistic meson-exchange model, where the virtual S11(1535) is excited and deexcited by exchange of δ, σ, π, η, ρ, and ω mesons. The real parts of the meson-nucleon-S11(1535) couplings were derived from a nonrelativistic quark model using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions for the nucleon and resonance, while imaginary parts were calculated from lowest-order meson loop corrections. Initial and final state interactions were treated using an on-shell Watson-Migdal approach with effective range expansions, and the mixing angle and range parameter were obtained by adjusting the calculated cross section to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle θ ≈ -5° (preliminary)
- Inverse range parameter b⁻¹ ≈ 0.5 fm (preliminary)
- For the pp final state interaction, the s-wave scattering length a ≈ -17.1 fm and effective range r ≈ 0 to 2.84 fm
- The ρ-meson tensor coupling constant Kρ ≈ 6.1, while Kω ≈ 0

## Context

This work extends spectroscopic model calculations of baryon mixing angles by providing an independent determination method based on threshold meson production, rather than relying solely on spectroscopic models which have unresolved problems. The approach follows earlier investigations of pp → ppη by Kleefeld (1999) and references therein, and enables future application to other resonances beyond the S11(1535). An extension of the effective range formalism to include Coulomb interactions is in progress.
