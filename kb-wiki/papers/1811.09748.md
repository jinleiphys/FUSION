# New Insights on Low Energy $πN$ Scattering Amplitudes: Comprehensive Analyses at $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ Level

> A production representation of partial-wave $S$ matrix is utilized to construct low-energy elastic pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes from cuts and poles on complex Riemann sheets. Among them, the contribution of left-hand cuts is estimated using the $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ results obtained in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory within the extended-on-nass-shell scheme. By fitting to data on partial-wave phase shifts, it is indicated that the existences of hidden poles in $S_{11}$ and $P_{11}$ channels, as conjectured in our previous paper~\citep{Wang:2017agd}, are firmly established. Specifically, the pole mass of the $S_{11}$ hidden resonance is determined to be $(895\pm81)-(164\pm23)i$ MeV, whereas, the virtual pole in the $P_{11}$ channel locates at $(966\pm18)$ MeV. It is found that analyses at the $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ level improves significantly the fit quality, comparing with the previous $\mathcal{O}(p^2)$ one. Quantitative studies with cautious physical discussions are also conducted for the other $S$- and $P$-wave channels.

## Key claim

The existence of hidden poles in the \(S_{11}\) and \(P_{11}\) channels, previously conjectured at \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\) level, is firmly established at \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) level using a production representation of the partial-wave \(S\) matrix, with the left-hand cuts estimated from one-loop covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory within the extended-on-mass-shell scheme.

## Method

The authors constructed low-energy elastic pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes using the Peking University (PKU) representation, which separates partial-wave \(S\) matrices into contributions from poles and branch cuts. The left-hand cut contributions were estimated from \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory amplitudes renormalized in the extended-on-mass-shell scheme, including the full circular cut structure that appears at this order. The resulting phase shifts were fitted to Roy-Steiner equation partial-wave phase shift data to determine hidden pole positions.

## Key numbers

- The \(S_{11}\) hidden resonance pole mass is \((895\pm81)-(164\pm23)i\) MeV, determined from fits with systematic errors from cut-off parameter variation and statistical uncertainties added in quadrature.
- The \(P_{11}\) virtual pole is located at \((966\pm18)\) MeV, with the pole position varying between 948(1) MeV and 983 MeV depending on the cut-off parameter \(s_c\).
- The \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) analysis improves fit quality significantly compared to the previous \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\) analysis, with \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f}\) values ranging from 1.14 to 1.46 for the \(S_{11}\) channel depending on the cut-off parameter.
- The cut-off parameter \(s_c\) was varied from \(-0.08\) GeV\(^2\) to \(-25.0\) GeV\(^2\) to assess systematic uncertainties in pole positions.

## Context

This work extends the previous \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\) analysis by Wang, Yao, and Zheng (2018) by including the full circular cut structure of the left-hand cuts at \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) level, providing stronger evidence for the hidden poles in the \(S_{11}\) and \(P_{11}\) channels. The analysis uses Roy-Steiner equation phase shifts from Hoferichter et al. (2015) rather than the GWU partial wave analysis used previously, and the chiral amplitudes are renormalized using the extended-on-mass-shell scheme following Gegelia (1999) and Fuchs (2003). This work enables more precise determination of subthreshold pole positions and provides reliable inputs for related physics such as the \(\pi N\) sigma term used in dark-matter searches.
