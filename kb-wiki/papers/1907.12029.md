# Hybrid star within the framework of a lowest-order constraint variational method

> The hadron-quark phase transition in the core of heavy neutron star (NS) has been studied. For the hadronic sector, we have used the lowest-order constraint variational method by employing $ AV_{18} $, $ AV_{14}$, $ UV_{14}$, and Reid $ 68 $ two-body nucleon-nucleon forces supplemented by the phenomenological Urbana-type three-body force. We have adopted the MIT bag model as well as three-flavor version of the Nambu- Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to describe the quark phase. The equation of state (EOS) of a hybrid star (HS) is presented by combining two EOS of the hadronic sector and quark sector of a star, which are derived from independent models or theories. The hadron-quark transition is constructed by considering a sharp phase transition, i.e., Maxwell construction. The structure of the HS is calculated and reported by solving Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. Finally the radii and tidal deformability of purely NS and HS for the mass of $ 1.4M_{\odot} $ is computed and new constraints on these quantities are checked. The maximum mass of HS is found more than $ 2 M_{\odot} $ for both the NJL and MIT bag models. However, the maximum mass of $ 1.796 M_{\odot} $ ($ 1.896 M_{\odot} $) was the best result that would be calculated for a stable HS with the pure quark core within the MIT (NJL) model. All the hybrid EOS fulfill the constraints on radii and tidal deformability extracted from the binary GW170817 for HSs. A comprehensive analysis on the structure of purely NS and HS and also compactness, tidal Love number, and tidal deformability for the star with the mass of 1.4 $ M_{\odot} $ has been conducted for various EOS of the hadron sector and several parameter sets of the quark EOS. The results achieved in this study are in good concurrence with the other calculations reported on this subject.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hybrid stars (with a quark core) can achieve maximum masses exceeding 2 solar masses using both the MIT bag model and the NJL model for quark matter, but the best results for stable hybrid stars with a pure quark core are limited to 1.796 solar masses (MIT bag model) and 1.896 solar masses (NJL model), indicating that the maximum mass depends critically on the specific quark model parameters and the hadronic equation of state used.

## Method

The authors employed the lowest-order constraint variational (LOCV) method with AV18, AV14, UV14, and Reid 68 two-body nucleon-nucleon forces supplemented by Urbana-type three-body forces to describe the hadronic phase. For the quark phase, they used both the MIT bag model (with one-gluon-exchange corrections) and the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with three parameter sets (RKH, HK, LKW). The hadron-quark phase transition was constructed using the Maxwell construction (sharp phase transition), and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain hybrid star structure, including tidal deformability calculations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hybrid star mass exceeds 2 solar masses for both NJL and MIT bag models, but the best stable hybrid star with pure quark core gives 1.796 solar masses (MIT bag model) and 1.896 solar masses (NJL model)
- For the MIT bag model with AV18 plus three-body force, phase transition occurs at densities above 1.5 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3) for bag constants B >= 90 MeV fm^-3
- With only two-body forces (no three-body force), phase transition occurs at about 6 times nuclear saturation density with B = 90 MeV fm^-3
- Bag constants below 78 MeV fm^-3 produce no intersection between hadron and quark pressure curves, meaning no phase transition occurs
- For bag constants between 78 and 84 MeV fm^-3, hadron transition densities are less than nuclear saturation density
- The radii and tidal deformability for stars of mass 1.4 solar masses were computed and checked against constraints from the binary neutron star merger GW170817

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars by combining the LOCV method (previously used by Goudarzi (2015, 2016) for neutron star structure) with both MIT bag model and NJL model descriptions of quark matter. The study builds on the discovery of massive neutron stars by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), and addresses the constraints from the GW170817 gravitational wave event detected by LIGO and Virgo (Abbott, 2018). The results are consistent with other calculations in the field and demonstrate that all hybrid equations of state considered satisfy the tidal deformability constraints from GW170817, enabling further investigation of the hadron-quark phase transition in dense matter.
