# Microscopic Theory of the Nuclear Equation of State and Neutron Star Structure

> The Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone many-body theory of the Nuclear Equation of State is reviewed in some details. In the theory, one performs an expansion in terms of the Brueckner two-body scattering matrix and an ordering of the corresponding many-body diagrams according to the number of their hole-lines. Recent results are reported, both for symmetric and for pure neutron matter, based on realistic two-nucleon interactions. It is shown that there is strong evidence of convergence in the expansion. Once three-body forces are introduced, the phenomenological saturation point is reproduced and the theory is applied to the study of neutron star properties. One finds that in the interior of neutron stars the onset of hyperons strongly softens the Nuclear Equation of State. As a consequence, the maximum mass of neutron stars turns out to be at the lower limit of the present phenomenological observation.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone (BBG) hole-line expansion for the nuclear equation of state shows strong evidence of convergence when three hole-line diagrams are included, but only after three-body forces are introduced does the theory reproduce the phenomenological saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter; the maximum mass of neutron stars then falls at the lower limit of present observational constraints.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations at the two hole-line level using the Argonne v14 nucleon-nucleon potential, then extended the expansion to include three hole-line diagrams by solving the Bethe-Fadeev integral equation for the three-body scattering matrix T(3). They compared results for two choices of the single-particle potential U(k), the "gap choice" (zero above the Fermi momentum) and the "continuous choice" (extended smoothly above kF), to assess convergence. Three-body forces were introduced phenomenologically to correct the saturation density.

## Key numbers

- The difference in energy per particle between the gap and continuous choices at the BHF level is about 4-5 MeV in the considered density range, with the potential energy part of the binding energy around -40 MeV at saturation density (about 10% discrepancy).
- The binding energy per particle at the minimum of the saturation curve from BHF plus three hole-line diagrams is close to the empirical value of about -16 MeV, but the corresponding density comes out about 30-40% larger than the empirical saturation density.
- The saturation curves extend from about one half of saturation density to about five times saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the BBG hole-line expansion approach previously developed by Day (1980) and others, applying it to both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter with the Argonne v14 potential. The finding that the BHF continuous choice already gives results close to the full three hole-line calculation supports using BHF with continuous choice as a first approximation. The need for three-body forces to reproduce empirical saturation confirms earlier findings by Day (1980). The application to neutron star structure enables predictions of maximum mass that can be compared with upcoming observational data from new satellite missions.
