# A Bottom-Up EFT Approach To Superdense Baryonic Matter

> How to arrive at the densest matter in massive compact stars starting from Walecka's linear $ω$-$σ$ mean-field model is described in a series of arguments anchored on hidden local symmetry, hidden scale symmetry and emergent parity-doublet symmetry. I follow the bottom-up approach from chiral symmetry with pions, coupled to hidden local and scale symmetry degrees of freedom. Exploiting the renormalization-group treatment à la Shankar and Polchinski of the fermionic interactions on the Fermi sphere, leading to Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid, one obtains a sort of generalized ``Density Functional" that allows via a topology change hadrons transform to quarks without phase changes at the center of massive stars. The highly dense matter is ``pseudo-conformal" with the sound velocity $v_{pcs}^2/c^2\approx 1/3$ but the trace of the energy-momentum tensor is not equal to zero, hence the matter is non-conformal.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a bottom-up effective field theory (G$n$EFT), built from hidden local symmetry, hidden scale symmetry, and emergent parity-doublet symmetry, can describe superdense baryonic matter up to the center of massive compact stars via a topology change from hadrons to quarks without a phase transition, yielding a "pseudo-conformal" sound velocity near $v^2/c^2 \approx 1/3$ that is not actually conformal because the trace of the energy-momentum tensor remains nonzero.

## Method

The author constructs a Lagrangian $\mathcal{L}_{\rm GnEFT}$ containing nucleons, pions, hidden local symmetry vector mesons ($\omega$, $\rho$), and a hidden scalar dilaton field $\chi_d$ (a pseudo-Goldstone boson of broken scale symmetry), treated via a renormalization-group approach on the Fermi sphere à la Shankar and Polchinski to obtain a Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid fixed-point approximation. This framework is combined with a topology change at density $n_{HQ} \sim (2-3)n_0$ where skyrmions on a crystal lattice fractionalize into half-skyrmions, leading to the decoupling of the $\rho$ meson ($g_\rho \to 0$) and the dilaton approaching the dilaton-limit fixed point, which modifies the equation of state at high density.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter equilibrium density: $n_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- Hadron-quark crossover density: $n_{HQ} \sim (2-3)n_0$, with a specific working value $n_{HQ} = 2.5 n_0$ used for sound velocity predictions
- Pseudo-conformal sound velocity: $v_{pcs}^2/c^2 \approx 1/3$ at densities above $n_{HQ}$ in the center of massive stars
- Maximum star mass estimate for $n_{1/2}=4n_0$: $M_{\rm max} \sim 2.3 M_\odot$
- Predicted tidal deformability: $\Lambda_{1.4} \sim 550$ (with caveat that it depends sensitively on $n_{HQ}$)
- Dilaton mass in free space: $\sim 600$ MeV
- Effective axial coupling in heavy nuclei: $g_A^{\rm eff} \approx 1$ at nuclear matter density, with a possible more quenched value $g_A^{\rm eff} \sim (0.6-0.8)$ from recent $^{100}$Sn Gamow-Teller measurements at RIKEN

## Context

This work extends the Walecka linear $\omega$-$\sigma$ mean-field model (Walecka, 1974) by incorporating hidden local symmetry, hidden scale symmetry, and parity-doublet symmetry into a generalized covariant density functional theory, building on the Shankar/Polchinski renormalization-group treatment of fermions on the Fermi surface. The approach contrasts with chiral effective field theory ($\chi$EFT$_\pi$) which breaks down above $\sim n_0$, and with phenomenological covariant density functionals (such as those by Holt and collaborators) that do not explicitly incorporate a hadron-quark crossover mechanism. The paper enables future systematic corrections beyond the Fermi-liquid fixed-point approximation using coadjoint-orbit techniques from condensed matter physics, and highlights unresolved issues including the role of the anomalous dimension $\beta^\prime$ in the trace anomaly and its impact on quenched $g_A$ in nuclear beta decay.
