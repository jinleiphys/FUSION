# Flux-Bubble Models and Mesonic Molecules

> It has been shown that the string-flip potential model reproduces most of the bulk properties of nuclear matter, with the exception of nuclear binding. Furthermore, it was postulated that this model with the inclusion of the colour-hyperfine interaction should produce binding. In some recent work a modified version of the string-flip potential model was developed, called the flux-bubble model, which would allow for the addition of perturbative QCD interactions. In attempts to construct a simple $q\bar q$ nucleon system using the flux-bubble model (which only included colour-Coulomb interactions) difficulties arise with trying to construct a many-body variational wave function that would take into account the locality of the flux-bubble interactions. In this paper we look at a toy system, a mesonic molecule, in order to understand these difficulties. {\it En route}, a new variational wave function is proposed that may have a sufficient impact on the old string-flip potential model results that the inclusion of perturbative effects may not be needed.

## Key claim

The string-flip potential model, even when extended with colour-Coulomb interactions via the flux-bubble model, does not produce nuclear binding; however, the authors propose that a new variational wave function for the mesonic-molecule toy system may alter the old string-flip potential model results sufficiently that the inclusion of perturbative effects may not be needed.

## Method

The authors study a toy mesonic-molecule system (Q₂) consisting of two heavy quarks and two light antiquarks, using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. They solve the Schrödinger equation variationally via the Metropolis Monte Carlo algorithm for several potential models: the linear string-flip potential, the flux-bubble model with fixed colour (SU′_ℓ(2)), the flux-bubble model with moving colour (SU_ℓ(2)), and a semi-relativistic Dirac-Hartree-Fock treatment. They then introduce a new bond-centred variational wave function analogous to the H₂ molecular wave function, consisting of a superposition of two direct-product terms.

## Key numbers

- The string-flip potential model for nuclear matter gives anti-binding of about 25 MeV at nuclear density instead of the expected binding of about 8 MeV.
- For the Q₂ system with the old wave function, the variational parameters yield α ≈ (1.88 ± 0.13), β ≈ (1.32 ± 0.05) fm, and total energy Ū(R) ≈ (1710 ± 4) MeV.
- The well depth for the Q₂ system with the old wave function at α=2 is D ≈ O(8) MeV.
- The average well depth for the string-flip potentials is Ḏ ≈ (2.986 ± 0.030) MeV.
- A reduced heavy quark mass of μ_Q ≳ (53.7 ± 1.9) GeV is required to obtain binding for these potentials.
- The effective exchange mass from fitting the asymptotic potentials to a Yukawa form is m̄_ex ≈ (636 ± 41) MeV, about 4.5 times too large.
- For the flux-bubble model with moving colour, a linear fit to the potential for R ≤ 1 fm yields a slope of (909.8 ± 1.0) MeV/fm.

## Context

This work extends the string-flip potential model studies of Horowitz (1985) and Watson (1988) and the flux-bubble model of Boyce (1996), which attempted to include perturbative QCD effects into the many-body quark description of nuclear matter. The authors find that the flux-bubble extensions do not produce nuclear binding and that the string-flip potentials do not yield a long-range interaction similar to pion exchange. The proposed new bond-centred wave function, inspired by the Heitler-London treatment of the H₂ molecule, may enable future calculations that could produce binding without adding perturbative interactions.
