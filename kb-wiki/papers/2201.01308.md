# The $^1$S$_0$ pairing gap in neutron matter

> We report ab initio calculations of the S wave pairing gap in neutron matter calculated using realistic nuclear Hamiltonians that include two- and three-body interactions. We use a trial state, properly optimized to capture the essential pairing correlations, from which we extract ground state properties by means of auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo simulations. We extrapolate our results to the thermodynamic limit by studying the finite-size effects in the symmetry-restored projected Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (PBCS) theory and compare our results to other ab initio studies done in the past. Our quantum Monte Carlo results for the pairing gap show a modest suppression with respect to the mean-field BCS values. These results can be connected to cold atom experiments, via the unitarity regime where fermionic superfluidity assumes a unified description, and they are important in the prediction of thermal properties and the cooling of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors report that their quantum Monte Carlo results for the $^1$S$_0$ pairing gap in neutron matter show a modest suppression relative to mean-field BCS values, but this conclusion depends on the quality of the trial wavefunction used to model the system and the constrained-path approximation employed to control the fermion sign problem.

## Method

The authors performed auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations using realistic nuclear Hamiltonians including the Argonne AV8' two-body potential and the Urbana-IX (UIX) three-body potential. They used a pfaffian trial wavefunction optimized to capture pairing correlations, extracted ground-state energies for systems with even and odd particle numbers, and computed the pairing gap via the odd-even staggering formula. Finite-size effects were studied using projected Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (PBCS) theory to extrapolate results to the thermodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-neutron scattering length used is $a \approx -18.7$ fm (with a value of $-18.5$ fm also cited in the text) and the effective range is $r_{\rm e} \approx 2.7$ fm.
- Finite-size effects for the energy and pairing gap were studied at Fermi momenta $k_{\rm F} = 0.2$, $0.3$, and $0.4$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The pair probability distribution and pairing function were compared for $N=40$ particles at $k_{\rm F}=0.4$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier ab initio calculations of the $^1$S$_0$ pairing gap in neutron matter using diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) by Gezerlis and Carlson (2008) and previous AFDMC studies by Fabrocini et al. (2005) and Gandolfi et al. (2008, 2009). The results can be connected to cold atom experiments via the unitarity regime, where fermionic superfluidity assumes a unified description, and are important for predicting thermal properties and cooling of neutron stars.
