# Time-dependent Internal DFT formalism and Kohn-Sham scheme

> We generalize to the time-dependent case the stationary Internal DFT / Kohn-Sham formalism presented in Ref. [14]. We prove that, in the time-dependent case, the internal properties of a self-bound system (as an atomic nuclei) are all defined by the internal one-body density and the initial state. We set-up a time-dependent Internal Kohn-Sham scheme as a practical way to compute the internal density. The main difference with the traditional DFT / Kohn-Sham formalism is the inclusion of the center-of-mass correlations in the functional.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for self-bound quantum systems (such as atomic nuclei or helium droplets), the time-dependent internal many-body wave function and all internal observables can be written as functionals of the internal one-body density and the initial state, provided the center-of-mass correlations are explicitly included in the functional, a condition that distinguishes this formalism from traditional time-dependent DFT.

## Method

The author generalizes the stationary Internal DFT/Kohn-Sham formalism to the time-dependent case by first applying Jacobi coordinates to decouple the center-of-mass motion from the internal dynamics of the N-body system. The proof adapts the Runge-Gross theorem (1984) to the internal Schrodinger equation, using the internal one-body density and internal probability current defined in the center-of-mass frame, and applies the integral mean value theorem to show that two different internal potentials (differing by more than a time-dependent constant) produce different internal densities.

## Key numbers

- The internal one-body density is normalized to N (the number of particles), as defined in Eq. (rho_int0).
- The local part of the two-body internal density matrix is normalized to N(N-1)/2, as defined in Eq. (gamint0).
- The reduced mass for Jacobi coordinate alpha is given by mu_alpha = m * alpha/(alpha+1), where m is the particle mass.
- The total mass M = Nm appears in the center-of-mass Hamiltonian H_CM = -hbar^2 Delta_R / (2M).

## Context

This work extends the stationary Internal DFT formalism established by Messud (2009) and builds on the time-dependent DFT theorems of Runge and Gross (1984) and the time-dependent Kohn-Sham scheme. It provides a fundamental justification for using density functionals in time-dependent mean-field calculations of nuclei (such as time-dependent Hartree-Fock, Neg 1982) and helium droplets, where the spurious center-of-mass motion problem in time-dependent Hartree-Fock (Irv 1980, Uma 2009) cannot be corrected by projection methods. The formalism enables future development of practical time-dependent internal Kohn-Sham schemes that include center-of-mass correlations in the functional.
