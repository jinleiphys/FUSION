# Constraints on the Skyrme Equations of State from Properties of Doubly Magic Nuclei

> I use properties of doubly-magic nuclei to constrain nuclear matter and neutron matter equations of state. I conclude that the data determined the value of the neutron equation of state and the symmetry energy near a density of $ρ_{on}$ = 0.10 nucleons/fm$^{3}$. The slope at that point is constrained by the value of the neutron skin.

# Constraints on the Skyrme Equations of State from Properties of Doubly Magic Nuclei

## Key claim
The data from doubly-magic nuclei determine the neutron equation of state and symmetry energy most precisely near a density of 0.10 nucleons/fm³, but the slope at that point is constrained by the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb, and the results depend on the assumed value of the neutron effective mass.

## Method
The author starts with 11 Skyrme energy-density functionals from the CSkP set (plus SLy4 and SkM*) and refits the parameters t₀, t₁, t₂, x₀, and x₃ to ground-state properties (binding energies, rms charge radii, single-particle energies) of 11 doubly-magic nuclei: ¹⁶O, ²⁴O, ³⁴Si, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁴⁸Ni, ⁶⁸Ni, ⁸⁸Sr, ¹⁰⁰Sn, ¹³²Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb. The fits are performed with constraints on the neutron skin thickness R_np of ²⁰⁸Pb (0.16, 0.20, and 0.24 fm) and, in some cases, with the neutron effective mass fixed to 0.90 at ρ_on = 0.10 nucleons/fm³.

## Key numbers
- The neutron equation of state at ρ_on = 0.10 nucleons/fm³ is F_n(ρ_on) = 11.3(8) MeV for the full CSkP set; restricting to fits with nuclear matter effective mass near unity gives 10.8(3) MeV.
- The symmetry energy at ρ_on = 0.10 nucleons/fm³ is S(ρ_on) = 25.4(8) MeV.
- The nuclear matter incompressibility K_m ranges from 219 to 240 MeV across the fitted interactions.
- The neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb from PREX is R_np = 0.302 ± 0.175 (experimental) ± 0.026 (model) ± 0.005 (strange) fm.
- The crossing point where neutron EOS curves for different R_np values intersect is at ρ = 0.096 to 0.104 nucleons/fm³, with an average of ρ_on = 0.10 nucleons/fm³.
- The derivatives at ρ_on = 0.10 are linear with R_np: S'(ρ_on) = 850 × R_np and F'_n(ρ_on) = 525 × R_np (in units where R_np is in fm).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the CSkP Skyrme functional set from Dutra et al. (2012) by adding constraints from doubly-magic nuclear ground-state data, reducing the spread in the equation of state compared to that earlier study. The results provide an analytical parametrization (Eq. 1) that connects the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb to the neutron equation of state and symmetry energy, enabling future constraints from the PREX-II experiment (which will reduce the error bar on R_np to about 0.06 fm) and from dipole resonance studies and nuclear reaction data.
