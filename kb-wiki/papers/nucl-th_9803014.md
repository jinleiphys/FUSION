# New technique for phase shift analysis: multi-energy solution of inverse scattering problem

> We demonstrate a new approach to the analysis of extensive multi-energy data. For the case of d + He-4, we produce a phase shift analysis covering for the energy range 3 to 11 MeV. The key idea is the use of iterative perturbative data-to-potential inversion which can produce potentials which reproduce the data simultaneously over a range of energies. It thus effectively regularizes the extraction of phase shifts from diverse, incomplete and possibly somewhat contradictory data sets. In doing so, it will provide guidance to experimentalists as to what further measurements should be made. This study is limited to vector spin observables and spin-orbit interactions. We discuss alternative ways in which the theory can be implemented and which provide insight into the ambiguity problems. We compare the extrapolation of these solutions to other energies. Majorana terms are presented for each potential component.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that multi-energy phase shift analysis can be regularized by using iterative perturbative data-to-potential inversion (GIP) to find a single energy-dependent potential that fits data simultaneously over a range of energies, but the study is limited to vector spin observables and spin-orbit interactions, with tensor forces neglected.

## Method

The authors apply the generalized iterative perturbative (GIP) method, which expands potential components (central, spin-orbit, Wigner and Majorana terms) in a Gaussian basis and linearizes the response of the S-matrix to potential changes via Equation (2). They then minimize a chi-squared function that includes cross sections and vector analyzing powers across multiple energies simultaneously, iterating until convergence. For the d + He-4 system, they fit data from 3 to 11.5 MeV using two approaches: Approach A starts with a simple Gaussian potential and adds components in stages, while Approach B starts from a potential derived by inversion of RGM S-matrix elements from Kanada et al. (1985).

## Key numbers

* The energy range covered is 3 to 11 MeV for d + He-4 scattering.
* Approach A achieved chi-squared per degree of freedom (chi-squared/F) of 18.7 with about 20 independent parameters.
* Approach B achieved chi-squared/F of 5.84 but with significant unitarity breaking in the S wave.
* The inelastic threshold is at E_th = 3.3 MeV.
* Potential A gives a bound state energy of E_B = -2.26 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -1.472 MeV.
* The bound state energy is extremely sensitive to the form of the potentials and to the energy dependence of the d-He-4 S-3_1 phase shifts.

## Context

This work extends the established IP S-matrix-to-potential inversion method (Mackintosh and Kobos, 1982; Kukulin et al., 1990; Cooper and Mackintosh, 1991) to direct data-to-potential inversion for multi-energy data. It addresses the well-known problems of incomplete and inconsistent data sets in phase shift analysis, particularly for spin-1 projectiles, which were previously tackled by elaborate PSA methods (Kuznetsova and Kukulin, 1997; Krasnopolsky et al., 1991). The approach enables extraction of more reliable odd-parity phase shifts for d + He-4, which are crucial for testing supersymmetrical aspects of composite particle interactions and tensor interaction structure (Kukulin and Pomerantsev, 1992), and the resulting potentials can be compared with double folding procedures or RGM calculations.
