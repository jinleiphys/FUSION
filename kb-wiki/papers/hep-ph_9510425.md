# Vector Meson Mixing and Charge Symmetry Violation

> We discuss the consistency of the traditional vector meson dominance (VMD) model for photons coupling to matter, with the vanishing of vector meson-meson and meson-photon mixing self-energies at q^2=0. This vanishing of vector mixing has been demonstrated in the context of rho-omega mixing for a large class of effective theories. As a further constraint on such models, we here apply them to a study of photon-meson mixing and VMD. As an example we compare the predicted momentum dependence of one such model with a momentum-dependent version of VMD discussed by Sakurai in the 1960's. We find that it produces a result which is consistent with the traditional VMD phenomenology. We conclude that comparison with VMD phenomenology can provide a useful constraint on such models.

# Vector Meson Mixing and Charge Symmetry Violation

## Key claim
The vanishing of vector-vector mixing amplitudes at $q^2=0$ (required by gauge invariance for couplings to conserved currents) is fully consistent with traditional vector meson dominance phenomenology, provided the momentum dependence of the photon-rho coupling is not too strong. The authors demonstrate this consistency using a nucleon-loop model, but note that universality (the assumption that $g_\rho \sim g_{\rho\pi\pi}$) is violated at the 30-40% level, which limits the precision of the comparison.

## Method
The authors compute the $\rho-\gamma$ mixing amplitude $\Pi_{\rho\gamma}(q^2)$ using a one-loop nucleon (proton) loop model within the Walecka model, following the approach of Piekarewicz and Williams (1992) for $\rho-\omega$ mixing. They include both vector-vector and vector-tensor couplings for the $\rho NN$ vertex, regularize dimensionally, and renormalize with a counter-term chosen to reproduce the extracted value of $g_\rho(0)$. The resulting momentum-dependent $g_\rho(q^2)$ is then compared to the constant-$g_\rho$ assumption of traditional VMD (VMD2) using experimental extractions from $\rho^0$ photoproduction and $\rho^0 \to e^+e^-$.

## Key numbers
- The extracted VMD2 coupling constants from Bauer et al. (1978): for three fits, $g_\rho^2(q^2=0)/4\pi = 2.43 \pm 0.10$, $2.27 \pm 0.23$, $2.18 \pm 0.22$; and $g_\rho^2(q^2=m_\rho^2)/4\pi = 2.21 \pm 0.017$, $2.20 \pm 0.06$, $2.11 \pm 0.06$, with corresponding $\rho$ masses $m_\rho = 767, 774, 776$ MeV and widths $\Gamma_\rho = 135, 145, 155$ MeV.
- The PW nucleon-loop model yields $g_\rho^2(0)/(4\pi) = 2.14$ and $g_\rho^2(m_\rho^2)/(4\pi) = 2.6$, compared to experimental averages of 2.3 and 2.17 respectively.
- The tensor-to-vector coupling ratio for the $\rho NN$ vertex is fixed at $C_\rho \equiv f_{\rho NN}/g_{\rho NN} = 6.1$.
- The counter-term parameter $\beta = 8.32$ is chosen to reproduce the extracted $g_\rho(0)$.
- Universality is violated at the level of 30-40% in $g_\rho^2$, corresponding to approximately 20% in $g_\rho$.

## Context
This work extends the debate on momentum-dependent vector meson mixing initiated by Goldman et al. (1992), who first questioned the constant-mixing assumption using a quark-loop model, and by Piekarewicz and Williams (1992), who used a nucleon-loop calculation. The authors apply the same class of models to $\rho-\gamma$ mixing and compare with the traditional VMD framework of Sakurai (1960s). The result enables future reanalysis of experimental data using VMD1 (momentum-dependent coupling) rather than VMD2 (constant coupling), and provides a phenomenological constraint for any effective theory of vector meson mixing.
