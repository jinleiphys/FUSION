# Multipolar transitions excited by twisted photons in heavy quarkonia

> In this study, we investigate the excitation of multipole transitions in quarkonium systems by high-energy photons carrying orbital angular momentum. We derive explicit expressions for the amplitude and probability of photoexcitation incorporating the dynamics of the quarkonium center of mass. It is shown that the leading contribution to the transitions induced by twisted photons comes from the multipole $j=|m_γ|$ and $m_γ=l'-l$ in the long-wave approximation. Despite the fact that similar transitions can be excited by plane-wave photons, twisted photons with $|m_γ|>1$ enable the isolation of higher multipoles offering a unique spectroscopic tool. The fulfillment of the derived selection rules imposes constraints on the parameters of the wave packet of the quarkonium center of mass, since, in general, the angular momentum can be transferred to the center of mass. As a case study, we examine the octupole transition $1^{3}G_3 \rightarrow 3^{3}P_0$ in the charmonium system. We also compare the probabilities of excitation of this multipole transition by plane-wave and twisted photons with different polarizations.

## Key claim

Twisted photons with orbital angular momentum can selectively excite higher multipole transitions in heavy quarkonia that are suppressed for plane-wave photons, but the fulfillment of the derived selection rules imposes constraints on the wave packet parameters of the quarkonium center of mass because angular momentum can be transferred to the center of mass.

## Method

The authors derived explicit expressions for the amplitude and probability of photoexcitation of quarkonium by twisted photons using a non-relativistic Hamiltonian with the Cornell potential and Breit corrections, incorporating the dynamics of the quarkonium center of mass. They performed a multipole expansion of the transition matrix elements and applied the long-wave approximation to identify the leading contributions. As a case study, they examined the octupole transition \(1^{3}G_3 \rightarrow 3^{3}P_0\) in the charmonium system and compared excitation probabilities for plane-wave and twisted photons with different polarizations.

## Key numbers

- The octupole transition \(1^{3}G_3 \rightarrow 3^{3}P_0\) in charmonium was studied as a case example; the photoexcitation energy \(\epsilon\) is of the order of 100 MeV for such transitions in quarkonia.
- The conditions for the perturbative calculation require \(\frac{k^0_\perp \sigma_\perp}{(k^0_3)^2} \ll 1\), \(\frac{k^0_\perp \sigma_\perp}{\sigma_c^2} \ll 1\), \(\sigma_\perp b_\perp \ll 1\), and \(\frac{2(|m_\gamma| + 1)\sigma_\perp}{k^0_\perp} \ll 1\).
- The leading contribution to transitions induced by twisted photons comes from the multipole \(j = |m_\gamma|\) and \(m_\gamma = l' - l\) in the long-wave approximation.

## Context

This work extends the application of twisted photons from atomic systems (Grinter 2008, Matula 2013, Afanasev 2013, Afanasev 2016, Solyanik-Gorgone 2019, Schulz 2019, Schulz 2020, Peshkov 2023, KKR 2025) and nuclear physics (KS 2024, LuZhi 2023) to heavy quarkonia, building on the approach used for Wannier excitons by KR 2023. The paper enables a new spectroscopic tool for studying higher multipole transitions in charmonium and bottomonium systems, which are typically dominated by dipole transitions when using plane-wave photons.
