# Constraints on spin observables in antiproton-proton to antiLambda-Lambda

> It is recalled that spin-observables in the strangeness-exchange reaction $\bar{p}{p} \to \barΛΛ$ are not independent but are related to each other by simple algebraic relations. This provides constraints on the existing data on polarization and spin-correlation coefficients, and also on the forthcoming data obtained using a polarized proton target.

## Key claim

The spin observables in the reaction \(\bar{p}p \to \bar{\Lambda}\Lambda\) are not independent but are related by simple algebraic relations, providing constraints on existing data and on forthcoming data obtained using a polarized proton target. The paper derives linear and quadratic inequalities that must be satisfied by the measured observables, serving as consistency checks for experimental results.

## Method

The authors decompose the transition matrix \(\mathcal{M}\) for the reaction into six complex amplitudes (\(a, b, c, d, e, g\)) in the center-of-mass system, following the formalism of Bystricky (1978) and LaFrance (1980). They then express the differential cross-section \(I_0\), polarization \(P_n\), asymmetry \(A_n\), and spin-correlation coefficients \(C_{ij}, D_{ij}, K_{ij}\) in terms of these amplitudes. By exploiting positivity constraints on combinations of squared amplitudes, they derive linear inequalities (e.g., \(2|A_n| - C_{nn} \le 1\)) and quadratic inequalities (e.g., \(C_{zz}^2 + D_{nn}^2 \le 1\)) that the observables must obey.

## Key numbers

- The inequality \(2|A_n| - C_{nn} \le 1\) is derived, along with analogous relations \(2|P_n| - C_{nn} \le 1\) and \(2|C_{xz}| - C_{nn} \le 1\).
- The quadratic inequality \(C_{zz}^2 + D_{nn}^2 \le 1\) is proven, with similar relations for other pairs of observables listed in Table I of the paper.
- For the pure spin-triplet case, the relations include \(D_{xx} = -K_{xx}\), \(D_{zz} = -K_{zz}\), \(D_{xz} = -K_{xz}\), and \(D_{nn} = K_{nn}\).
- In the forward production limit (\(\theta_{cm} = 0\)), the relations include \(C_{xx} = -C_{nn}\), \(D_{xx} = D_{nn}\), and \(K_{xx} = -K_{nn}\).
- The spin-singlet fraction is given by \(F_0 = \frac{1}{4}(1 + C_{xx} - C_{yy} + C_{zz}) = \frac{1}{2I_0}|b-c|^2\), with \(F_0 = 1/4\) in the absence of spin-dependent interaction.

## Context

This work extends the general formalism for spin-1/2 scattering developed by Bystricky (1978), LaFrance (1980), and Delaney (1975) to the specific case of \(\bar{p}p \to \bar{\Lambda}\Lambda\). It provides consistency checks for data from the PS185 collaboration at CERN (Barnes, 1991; Barnes, 1996) and for upcoming results from the PS185/3 run using a polarized proton target (Johansson, 1999). The inequalities enable testing of competing models for strangeness production, such as kaon-exchange models (Haidenbauer, 1992) and quark-pair creation or polarized strange sea-quark models (Alberg, 1995), and allow accurate testing of the pure spin-triplet hypothesis suggested by early LEAR data.
