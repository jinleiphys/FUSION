# Coupling constant corrections in a holographic model of heavy ion collisions

> We initiate a holographic study of coupling-dependent heavy ion collisions by analysing for the first time the effects of leading-order, inverse coupling constant corrections. In the dual description, this amounts to colliding gravitational shock waves in a theory with curvature-squared terms. We find that at intermediate coupling, nuclei experience less stopping and have more energy deposited near the lightcone. When the decreased coupling results in an 80% larger shear viscosity, the time at which hydrodynamics becomes a good description of the plasma created from high energy collisions increases by 25%. The hydrodynamic phase of the evolution starts with a wider rapidity profile and smaller entropy.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first dynamical (real-time) holographic computation of heavy ion collisions with leading-order inverse coupling constant corrections, finding that at intermediate coupling nuclei experience less stopping and hydrodynamisation times increase, but the authors stress that the perturbative expansion in the Gauss-Bonnet coupling parameter λ_GB may break down for the largest corrections studied (λ_GB = -0.2), as evidenced by unphysical entropy decreases.

## Method

The authors solve the collision of gravitational shock waves in Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet theory in five-dimensional anti-de Sitter space, treating the Gauss-Bonnet coupling λ_GB perturbatively to first and second order. They expand the metric functions in the characteristic formulation of general relativity as A = A_0 + λ_GB A_1 + λ_GB^2 A_2 (and similarly for B, F, S), then solve nested sets of ordinary differential equations: first the standard Einstein equations for the zeroth-order functions, followed by non-homogeneous equations for the first-order and second-order corrections. The dual stress-energy tensor is extracted from the normalisable modes of the metric, and the authors simulate collisions of planar sheets of energy with widths μw = 0.1 (narrow, high-energy) and μw = 1.5 (wide, low-energy).

## Key numbers

- For λ_GB = -0.2, the maximum energy density on the lightcone is 88% higher at the end of the simulation compared to λ_GB = 0 (narrow shocks).
- For wide shocks at λ_GB = -0.2, the maximum energy density is 2.17 times the initial maximum, compared to 2.71 times at infinite coupling.
- The hydrodynamisation time in units of temperature for narrow shocks is t_hyd T_hyd = 0.41 - 0.52 λ_GB; for λ_GB = -0.2 this gives a 25% increase.
- For wide shocks, t_hyd T_hyd = 0.43 - 6.3 λ_GB; for λ_GB = -0.2 this gives a 290% increase (though the authors note this is sensitive to the hydrodynamisation criterion).
- The shear viscosity at λ_GB = -0.2 is η/s = (1/4π)(1 - 4λ_GB) = (1/4π)(1.8), an 80% increase over the infinite-coupling value.
- Total entropy reduction at the start of the simulation is 27% (narrow) and 25% (wide) for λ_GB = -0.2; at the end of the simulation the reductions are 15% and 12% respectively.
- The rapidity profile at its maximum is 11% wider for narrow shocks and 23% wider for wide shocks at λ_GB = -0.2.

## Context

This work extends the holographic heavy ion collision framework of Chesler and Yaffe (2010) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2013) from infinite coupling to include leading-order inverse coupling constant corrections via curvature-squared terms. It builds on prior studies of coupling constant corrections to thermodynamics (Gubser, 1998), hydrodynamics (Kats and Petrov, 2007; Brigante et al., 2007-2008; Buchel et al., 2008; Grozdanov and Starinets, 2014-2015), and linear spectrum analysis (Grozdanov et al., 2016). The results enable future comparisons with perturbative α'^3 corrections in N=4 SYM theory and provide input for interpolations between weak and strong coupling descriptions of the initial stages of heavy ion collisions, as discussed by Keegan et al. (2015).
