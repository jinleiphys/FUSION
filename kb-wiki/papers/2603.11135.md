# Phase structure and observables at high densities from first principles QCD

> We provide a short review of the progress made in the past decade with functional QCD in the description of the phase structure of QCD. We summarise the most important technical aspects of the framework, discuss strategies for truncations and address the problem of systematic error estimates. We detail efforts to gauge the approach systematically with lattice QCD at zero chemical potential, also including the physics of the Columbia plot at non-physical quark masses. Our main focus is, however, the high density regime of QCD. We address the predictive power of the functional approach for the appearance of new phases beyond the chiral crossover regime for chemical potentials $μ_B/T\geq 4.5$. The onset of this regime may be signalled by a critical end point of the crossover line but may also involve a moat regime or the emergence of an instability that indicates an inhomogeneous phase. Respective results include estimates for the location of the onset of new phases, and predictions for their experimental signatures.

## Key claim

Functional QCD methods (fRG and DSE) can now provide quantitative first-principles predictions for the QCD phase structure up to baryon chemical potentials μB/T ≤ 4.5, and with systematic errors below 10% for chiral dynamics in the range 4.5 ≤ μB/T ≤ 7, but the estimate for a critical end point at (T, μB) ≈ (115-105, 600-650) MeV is only valid under the assumption that no new phases (such as a moat regime or inhomogeneous phase) alter the nature and location of the CEP.

## Method

The authors review the combined functional QCD approach using Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) and functional renormalisation group (fRG) flows, which form an infinite tower of coupled integral or integro-differential equations for n-point correlation functions of quarks, gluons, and emergent composite fields (scalar and pseudoscalar mesons). The system is truncated using systematic expansion schemes with modular decomposition of diagrams into pure-glue, quark-gluon, and quark-meson subclasses, enabling quantifiable systematic error estimates through comparison of results from different functional methods and cross-validation with lattice QCD at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The combined functional estimate for a chiral critical end point (CEP) lies in the interval (T, μB)CEP ∈ (115-105, 600-650) MeV, corresponding to μB/T ≈ 5.5-6 for μS = 0
- The chiral crossover temperature at vanishing density is Tχ ≈ 155 MeV
- The systematic error for chiral dynamics remains below 10% in the range 4.5 ≤ μB/T ≤ 7
- The regime beyond the chiral crossover where new phases may appear is identified for chemical potentials μB/T ≥ 4.5
- The chiral scale (onset scale of chiral symmetry breaking in the chiral limit) is kχ = 388 MeV, computed in Ihssen (2024)

## Context

This review extends and systematises the functional QCD programme initiated by Braun (2008, 2009) for one- and two-flavour phase structures, and by Fischer (2014) for the first 2+1 flavour DSE study at real chemical potential that passed qualitative finite-density benchmarks. The quantitative milestone was achieved by Fu (2019) using fRG, which met all available lattice benchmarks for the chiral phase structure and provided the first estimate for the CEP location, corroborated by Gao (2020) and Gunkel (2021) using DSE. The work enables direct QCD computations of experimental signatures for the onset of new phases (moat regime, inhomogeneous phases) that can be tested in heavy-ion collision experiments at STAR (RHIC), CBM (FAIR, 2028), MPD (NICA, 2026), and CEE+ (HIAF, 2028).
