# Configuration mixing description of the nucleus $^{44}$S

> We study the structure of the neutron rich $^{44}$S isotope with modern configuration mixing methods based on the Gogny interaction including beyond-mean-field effects. Restoration of particle number and rotational symmetries are taken into account as well as shape mixing in the whole triaxial $(β_{2},γ)$ plane. We obtain a qualitative agreement between the calculated spectrum and the experimental data reported recently. Very extended collective wave functions in the $(β_2,γ)$ plane are found for the lowest states, corresponding to very flat potentials, indicating strong configuration mixing and supporting the weakening of the N=28 magic number.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the low-lying states of $^{44}$S exhibit wide configuration mixing rather than shape coexistence, supporting the erosion of the N=28 magic number; however, the agreement with experimental data is only qualitative, and the calculated excitation energies are systematically larger than the measured ones.

## Method
The authors performed Symmetry Conserving Configuration Mixing (SCCM) calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction, including particle number and angular momentum projection of intrinsic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states. The HFB-type states were obtained with the variation after particle number projection method (PN-VAP) and included triaxial shapes in the whole (β₂,γ) plane. The generator coordinate method (GCM) was used to mix these projected states, with the Hill-Wheeler equation solved for each angular momentum value to obtain energy levels and collective wave functions.

## Key numbers
- The first excited 2⁺ state has an experimental energy of E(2⁺)=1329(1) keV, with the 0⁺₂ isomeric state at 1365 keV.
- The experimental γ-rays observed in coincidence with the 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁ ground state transition are 949(5), 1128(6), and 1929(7) keV, populating levels at 2278, 2457, and 3248 keV respectively.
- The theoretical calculation used a regular triangular mesh with N_GCM=83 HFB-type states, with integration points N_a=8, N_b=N_c=16, and N_φ=9 for angular momentum and particle number projection.
- The minimum of the PN-VAP potential energy surface is located at (β₂=0.2, γ=46°), indicating erosion of the N=28 shell closure.
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the 2⁺₁ state is -9.2 e·fm², and for the 2⁺₂ state it is 1.2 e·fm².

## Context
This work extends beyond-mean-field studies of $^{44}$S using Gogny functionals (Egido 2000, Rodriguez 2002) by including triaxial shapes and exact GCM mixing without gaussian overlap approximation, and by using PN-VAP instead of plain HFB. The results are compared to experimental data from recent measurements (PRL 2010, PRC 2011) and to shell model calculations (PRL 2010). The calculations support the erosion of the N=28 magic number and provide a different interpretation of the shape coexistence picture, finding instead wide configuration mixing. The method enables future studies of transitional nuclei where triaxial degrees of freedom and shape mixing are important.
