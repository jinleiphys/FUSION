# Cavitation in holographic sQGP

> We study the possibility of cavitation in the nonconformal N=2^* SU(N) theory which is a mass deformation of N=4 SU(N) Yang-Mills theory. The second order transport coefficients are known from the numerical work using AdS/CFT by Buchel and collaborators. Using these and the approach of Rajagopal and Tripuraneni, we investigate the flow equations in a 1+1 dimensional boost invariant set up. We find that the string theory model does not exhibit cavitation before phase transition is reached. We give a semi-analytic explanation of this finding.

## Key claim

The authors find that the nonconformal N=2* SU(N) theory, studied via holography, does **not** exhibit cavitation before the phase transition temperature is reached, contrary to the phenomenological QCD-based analysis of Rajagopal and Tripuraneni (2010). The primary reason is the lower bulk viscosity in the N=2* model, with the sharp rise in relaxation times near T_c playing a secondary role.

## Method

The authors solve the 1+1 dimensional boost-invariant second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations for the N=2* plasma, using transport coefficients (bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, relaxation times) obtained from numerical AdS/CFT calculations by Buchel and collaborators. They compare the evolution of longitudinal pressure against the phenomenological QCD model of Rajagopal and Tripuraneni (2010), which uses lattice QCD input for the equation of state and bulk viscosity but conformal AdS/CFT values for relaxation times.

## Key numbers

- The N=2* model has a bulk viscosity to shear viscosity ratio of approximately 0.8 near T_c, compared to the lattice QCD fit used by Rajagopal and Tripuraneni (2010) which rises much more sharply.
- The effective relaxation time in N=2* scales as tau_eff * T_c proportional to |1 - T_c/T|^{-1/2} near T_c, with a fitted coefficient C = 0.08 in the formula tau = C / (T_c sqrt(1 - T_c/T)).
- For the QCD-RT model, cavitation occurs near tau approximately 2.5 fm/c; for N=2* the longitudinal pressure remains positive all the way to T_c = 190 MeV.
- Cavitation in the QCD-RT model is prevented only when the relaxation time coefficient C is increased to values greater than or equal to 2, far above the N=2* value of 0.08.
- The initial energy density is taken as epsilon = 15.8 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to T = 305 MeV for the lattice equation of state and T = 245 MeV for the N=2* equation of state, with initialization time tau_i = 0.5 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the cavitation analysis of Rajagopal and Tripuraneni (2010) to a holographic model (N=2* SU(N) theory) where all transport coefficients are consistently computed via AdS/CFT by Buchel and collaborators, rather than mixing lattice QCD inputs with conformal AdS/CFT results for relaxation times. The finding that cavitation does not occur in this string theory model contrasts with the phenomenological QCD analysis and suggests that critical slowing down (relaxation times rising near T_c), as anticipated by Song and Heinz (2008), can hinder cavitation, though a larger effect than seen in N=2* would be needed to prevent it in QCD. The work enables future studies using AdS/CFT to identify when the hydrodynamic gradient expansion breaks down, potentially via gravitational instabilities.
