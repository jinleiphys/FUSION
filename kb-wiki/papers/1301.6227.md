# Chiral soliton model at finite temperature and density

> In mean field approximation, we study a chiral soliton of the linear sigma model with two flavors at finite temperature and density. The stable soliton solutions are calculated with some appropriate boundary conditions. Energy and radius of the soliton are determined in a hot medium of constituent quarks. It is found that for $T<T_c$, the energy of the soliton $E^*$ is less than the energy of three constituent quarks $3M_q$, but with the increasing of temperature, the difference between $E^*$ and $3M_q$ becomes smaller and smaller, once $T>T_c$, there is a sharp delocalization phase transition from hadron matter to quark matter coincident with the restoration of chiral symmetry. In the transition region, the thermodynamic properties show large discontinuities which is an indication for a first-order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, within the mean-field approximation of the linear sigma model, a chiral soliton (representing a nucleon) undergoes a sharp delocalization phase transition from hadron matter to quark matter at a critical temperature \(T_c\), coinciding with the restoration of chiral symmetry; this transition is first-order, but the authors note that the critical temperature and the order of the transition are strongly dependent on the coupling constant \(g\), and for some critical value of \(g\) the order would change from first-order to crossover.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Euler-Lagrange equations of motion for the quark fields and the meson fields (\(\sigma\) and \(\pi\)) in the linear sigma model with two flavors, using the hedgehog ansatz for spherical symmetry. At finite temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\), the classical meson potential is replaced by a modified thermodynamical potential \(\Omega'(T,\mu)\) that includes the contribution of a uniform constituent quark medium, and the gap equations are solved self-consistently to obtain the chiral order parameter \(\sigma_v\). The soliton solutions are then obtained by solving the radial equations with boundary conditions that require the meson fields to approach their thermal vacuum values at infinity, and the normalization condition for the valence quarks is adjusted to maintain baryon number one in the presence of a homogeneous medium density.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the delocalization phase transition at zero chemical potential is \(T_c = 116\) MeV.
- The critical temperature at \(\mu = 200\) MeV is \(T_c = 80\) MeV.
- The total energy of the soliton in vacuum (at \(T=0\), \(\mu=0\)) is \(E = 1170\) MeV, with a proton charge radius \(R = 0.877\) fm.
- The critical value of the Yukawa coupling constant for soliton stability (with \(m_\sigma = 472\) MeV) is \(g_c = 4.24\); the chosen working parameters are \(g = 4.5\) and \(m_\sigma = 472\) MeV.
- The proton magnetic moment in vacuum is \(\mu_p = 0.319\, e\cdot\text{fm}\) and the axial-vector coupling ratio is \(g_A/g_V = 1.24\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model of the nucleon, previously studied in vacuum by Birse (1983) and Kahana (1984), to finite temperature and density using a mean-field treatment of the linear sigma model. It contrasts with the approach of Abu-Shady and Mansour (2012), who used a one-loop mesonic potential and found a different temperature dependence of the nucleon mass, and with studies using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by Berger (1996) and Schleif (1997), where the Dirac sea is treated with a cutoff. The results enable a simultaneous description of chiral symmetry restoration and the dissolution of the soliton (delocalization), providing a framework for studying the hadron-quark phase transition that can be extended beyond mean field using the CJT effective potential.
