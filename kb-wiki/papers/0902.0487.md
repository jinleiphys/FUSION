# Determination of QCD phase diagram from the imaginary chemical potential region

> We test the reliability of the the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, comparing the model result with the lattice data at nonzero imaginary chemical potential. The PNJL model with the vector-type four-quark and scalar-type eight-quark interactions reproduces the lattice data on the pseudocritical temperatures of the deconfinement and chiral phase transitions. The QCD phase diagram in the real chemical potential region is predicted by the PNJL model. The critical endpoint survives, even if the vector-type four-quark interaction is taken into account.

## Key claim

The Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, when augmented with both vector-type four-quark and scalar-type eight-quark interactions, can reproduce lattice QCD data on pseudocritical temperatures of deconfinement and chiral phase transitions in the imaginary chemical potential region, and the critical endpoint survives in the real chemical potential region even when the vector-type interaction is included.

## Method

The authors performed mean-field calculations of the two-flavor PNJL model, first fitting parameters (cutoff Lambda = 631.5 MeV, scalar coupling G_s = 5.498 GeV^{-2}, current quark mass m_0 = 5.5 MeV, and Polyakov potential scale T_0 = 212 MeV) to reproduce the pion mass (138 MeV) and decay constant (93.3 MeV) at zero temperature and chemical potential, and the pseudocritical temperature T_c = 173 MeV from lattice data at zero chemical potential. They then introduced a scalar-type eight-quark interaction (strength G_s8 = 452.12 GeV^{-8}) to bring the chiral pseudocritical temperature closer to the deconfinement one at zero chemical potential, and finally added a vector-type four-quark interaction (strength G_v = 4.673 GeV^{-2}) to match lattice data on the chiral pseudocritical temperature at imaginary chemical potential theta = pi/3. The model was then used to predict the phase diagram in the real chemical potential region.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass M_pi = 138 MeV and pion decay constant f_pi = 93.3 MeV at T = mu = 0, used to fix the NJL sector parameters.
- Pseudocritical temperature T_c = 173 +/- 8 MeV from lattice QCD (Karsch et al., 2001), used to set the Polyakov scale T_0 = 212 MeV.
- Scalar-type eight-quark coupling strength G_s8 = 452.12 GeV^{-8}, chosen to make T_c(sigma)/T_c = 1.05 at mu = 0, consistent with lattice data within 10% error.
- Vector-type four-quark coupling strength G_v = 4.673 GeV^{-2}, chosen so that at theta = pi/3 the chiral pseudocritical temperature T_c(sigma) falls within the lattice band T = (1.1 +/- 0.05) T_c.
- Roberge-Weiss endpoint location (theta_RW, T_RW) = (pi/3, 1.09 T_c) from the PNJL model with set A.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model approach of Fukushima (2004) and Ratti et al. (2006) by systematically determining the vector coupling strength from lattice data in the imaginary chemical potential region, following the proposal of Sakai et al. (2008). The method exploits the Roberge-Weiss periodicity (Roberge and Weiss, 1986) to test effective models against lattice QCD without the sign problem. The results enable quantitative predictions of the QCD phase diagram at real chemical potential, showing that the critical endpoint survives even with repulsive vector interactions, contrary to some earlier NJL model studies (Kitazawa et al., 2002; Kashiwa et al., 2007) where the vector interaction moved the endpoint toward lower temperatures and higher chemical potentials.
