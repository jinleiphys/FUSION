# Calculation of the transport coefficients in neutron star

> In this work, we have calculated the transport coefficients: shear viscosity and thermal conductivity inside the neutron star core. Our calculation is based on the relativistic kinetic theory approach using a modified BUU equation for quasi-particles whose mass and the chemical-potential and thus in turn the Fermi surface varies with the baryonic density $ρ_{B}$ and the temperature of the medium, and we have used the relaxation time approximation. For the description of the hadronic matter inside the neutron star, we consider the relativistic mean field model with three different kinds of parameterizations. We have found that the shear viscosity is predominantly influenced by neutrons, while thermal conductivity is primarily dominated by electrons.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity and thermal conductivity of neutron star core matter are calculated using a relativistic kinetic theory approach with a modified Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation for quasi-particles, where the effective mass and chemical potential vary with baryonic density and temperature; the results depend on the choice of relativistic mean-field (RMF) parameterization and the relaxation time approximation.

## Method

The authors model the neutron star core as consisting of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons in beta equilibrium, described by three RMF parameter sets (IUFSU, FSU2, FSUGold). They solve the relativistic BUU transport equation using the relaxation time approximation, where the relaxation time at the Fermi surface is computed from nucleon-nucleon cross-sections (using a parameterized vacuum form), lepton-lepton cross-sections (from QED), and beta-decay rates. The shear viscosity and thermal conductivity are then obtained from integrals over the Fermi surface involving the relaxation time and thermodynamic quantities derived from the RMF equation of state.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity is predominantly influenced by neutrons, while thermal conductivity is primarily dominated by electrons (no numerical values for the coefficients themselves are given in the truncated text).
* The three RMF models used have saturation densities: IUFSU at 0.155 fm^-3, FSU Gold at 0.148 fm^-3, and FSU2 at 0.1505 fm^-3.
* The nucleon effective mass to bare mass ratio (m*/m) at saturation is 0.61 for IUFSU and FSU Gold, and 0.593 for FSU2.
* The symmetry energy at saturation (E_sym) is 31.30 MeV (IUFSU), 32.59 MeV (FSU Gold), and 37.62 MeV (FSU2).
* The slope of the symmetry energy (L_sym) at saturation is 47.2 MeV (IUFSU), 60.5 MeV (FSU Gold), and 112.8 MeV (FSU2).
* The neutron decay rate used is 1/972 s^-1, taken from Shapiro (1983).
* The nucleon-nucleon cross-section parameterization uses 5.5 fm^2 for sqrt(s) below 1.8993 GeV, and a decreasing function above that threshold.
* A very low temperature of 0.1 MeV is used in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic kinetic theory approach of Albright (2015) to include quasi-particle effects where the effective mass and chemical potential vary with density and temperature, building on earlier transport calculations by Flowers and Itoh (1979) and Prakash (1993). The results are intended for use in modeling dissipative processes in neutron star mergers, particularly r-mode damping and gravitational wave emission, following the framework used by Cutler and Lindblom (1987) and more recent studies of post-merger remnants. The approach enables future calculations of transport coefficients for more complex compositions including hyperons or quark matter.
