# D4 brane probes in gauge/gravity duality

> We propose a DBI vertex brane + $N_c$ fundamental strings configuration for a probe baryon in the finite-temperature thermal gauge field via AdS/CFT correspondence. In particular, we investigate properties of this configuration in QCD_4 and warped AdS_6\times S^4. We find that, in D4-D8 system, a holographic probe baryon can be described as N_c fundamental strings connecting through a vertex D4 brane wrapped on S^4. In QCD_4 background, a closed vertex can exist in confined phase and can not exist in deconfined phase. In the low temperature region, screening effect still exist in confined phase like meson and the vertex D4 brane dominates the baryon mass. The lower energy state corresponds to vertex brane closer to the radial cut off position (r=r_c) and the higher energy state corresponds to vertex brane a little far away from the cut off position. The high energy limit of this configuration is just like the unclosed vertex brane configuration in a higher temperature deconfined phase. In warped AdS_6\times S^4 background, a closed vertex can exist in deconfined phase and the vertex contains a spike, while fundamental strings are relatively short. Screening length should be defined through the distance between top position of the vertex spike and the boundary.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a holographic probe baryon in the D4-D8 system can be described by a DBI vertex D4 brane wrapped on S⁴ with N_c fundamental strings attached, and that in the QCD₄ confined phase a closed vertex brane solution exists while in the deconfined phase it does not, whereas in the warped AdS₆×S⁴ background a closed vertex brane solution does exist in the deconfined phase.

## Method

The author constructs a configuration consisting of a D4 brane wrapped on S⁴ (the vertex brane) with N_c fundamental strings attached, using the DBI plus Chern-Simons action for the D4 brane and the Nambu-Goto action for the strings. The embedding function r(θ) for the D4 brane is solved numerically from the Legendre-transformed energy functional, and the force balance condition at the junction between the vertex brane and the strings is imposed to determine the string embedding ρ(r). The analysis is performed in two backgrounds: the QCD₄ background (confined and deconfined phases of the D4-D8 Sakai-Sugimoto model) and the warped AdS₆×S⁴ background dual to an N=2, D=5 super-conformal Yang-Mills theory.

## Key numbers

* In the QCD₄ confined background, the Kaluza-Klein mass is defined as M_KK = (3/2)(r_c/R³)^(1/2), where r_c is the radial cut-off position and R³ = π g_s N_c l_s³.
* In the QCD₄ deconfined background, the Euclidean time period is δt_E = (4π/3)(R³/r_0)^(1/2) = 1/T, where r_0 is the black hole horizon radius.
* In the warped AdS₆×S⁴ background, the black hole metric function is f(r) = g²r² - μ/r³, with g and μ being parameters independent of r.
* The D4 brane tension is T_4 = 1/(g_s (2π)⁴ l_s⁵).

## Context

This work extends the holographic baryon program initiated by Witten (1998), where a baryon is a compact D-brane wrapped on a transverse sphere with N_c strings attached, and follows earlier attempts to find closed vertex brane solutions from DBI+CS actions by CGST (1999, 2000). It contrasts with the point-brane treatment of the vertex in Liu (2008) and Li (2008), where the D5 brane in AdS₅×S⁵ was treated as a massive point, and instead provides a full DBI brane description. The results enable future study of baryon melting and screening in non-conformal, non-supersymmetric holographic QCD models, and may have signals for heavy-quark bound states in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC.
