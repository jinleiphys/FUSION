# Three-loop formula for quark and gluon contributions to the QCD trace anomaly

> In the QCD energy-momentum tensor $T^{μν}$, the terms that contribute to physical matrix elements are expressed as the sum of the gauge-invariant quark part and gluon part. Each part undergoes the renormalization due to the interactions among quarks and gluons, although the total tensor $T^{μν}$ is not renormalized thanks to the conservation of energy and momentum. Recently it has been shown that, through the renormalization, each of the quark and gluon parts of $T^{μν}$ receives a definite amount of anomalous trace contribution, such that their sum reproduces the well-known QCD trace anomaly, $T^μ_μ= (β/2g)F^{μν}F_{μν}+ m (1+γ_m)\barψψ$, and the corresponding formulas have been derived up to two-loop order. We extend this result to the three-loop order, working out all the relevant three-loop renormalization structure for the quark and gluon energy-momentum tensors in the (modified) minimal subtraction scheme in the dimensional regularization. We apply our three-loop formula of the quark/gluon decomposition of the trace anomaly to calculate the anomaly-induced mass structure of nucleons as well as pions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the decomposition of the QCD trace anomaly into separate quark and gluon contributions from two-loop order to three-loop order, working within the (modified) minimal subtraction scheme in dimensional regularization; the decomposition is scheme-dependent beyond one-loop order and the three-loop results depend on the renormalization scale.

## Method

The author determines the three-loop renormalization mixing structure for the quark and gluon parts of the energy-momentum tensor by using the known three-loop anomalous dimension matrix for twist-two flavor-singlet operators (the second moment of the DGLAP kernel) and the three-loop beta function and mass anomalous dimension, without explicit evaluation of three-loop Feynman diagrams. The renormalization constants for the twist-four scalar operators \(F^2\) and \(m\bar{\psi}\psi\) are derived from the RG invariance of the total trace anomaly. The coefficients \(x_1, y_1, x_3, y_3\) that appear in the trace anomaly formulas for the gluon and quark parts are then fixed by requiring that the renormalization constants have the standard MS pole structure.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop coefficients for the gluon trace anomaly are \(x_1 = \frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi} \left(-\frac{11C_A}{6}\right)\) for the \((F^2)_R\) term and \(y_1 = \frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi} \left(\frac{14C_F}{3}\right)\) for the \((m\bar{\psi}\psi)_R\) term, with \(C_A=3\) and \(C_F=4/3\) for \(N_c=3\).
* The one-loop coefficients for the quark trace anomaly are \(x_3 = \frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi} \left(\frac{n_f}{3}\right)\) for the \((F^2)_R\) term and \(y_3 = \frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi} \left(\frac{4C_F}{3}\right)\) for the \((m\bar{\psi}\psi)_R\) term.
* The three-loop beta function coefficient is \(\beta_2 = \frac{2857 C_A^3}{54} - \frac{1}{2} n_f \left(\frac{1415 C_A^2}{27} + \frac{205 C_A C_F}{9} - 2 C_F^2\right) + \frac{1}{4} n_f^2 \left(\frac{158 C_A}{27} + \frac{44 C_F}{9}\right)\).
* The three-loop mass anomalous dimension coefficient is \(\gamma_{m2} = n_f \left[(-48\zeta(3) - \frac{556}{27}) C_A C_F + (48\zeta(3) - 46) C_F^2\right] - \frac{129}{2} C_A C_F^2 + \frac{11413}{54} C_A^2 C_F - \frac{70}{27} C_F n_f^2 + 129 C_F^3\), with \(\zeta(3) = 1.202056903\ldots\).

## Context

This work extends the two-loop decomposition of the QCD trace anomaly into quark and gluon parts derived by Hatta et al. (2018) to three-loop order, using the same renormalization-mixing framework. The three-loop results enable more precise model-independent determinations of the gravitational form factor \(\bar{C}_{q,g}\) at the forward limit, which constrains the pressure distributions inside hadrons extracted from generalized parton distributions and deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments. The decomposition also provides a refined understanding of the anomaly-induced mass structure of nucleons and pions, relevant for interpreting near-threshold \(J/\psi\) photoproduction data and for the physics program of the future Electron-Ion Collider.
