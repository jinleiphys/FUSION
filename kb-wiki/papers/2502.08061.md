# Scale Setting and Strong Coupling Determination in the Gradient Flow Scheme for 2+1 Flavor Lattice QCD

> We report on the determination of the gradient flow scales in $N_f=2+1$ QCD using highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) ensembles generated by the HotQCD Collaboration for bare gauge couplings ranging from $β= 6.423$ to $8.400$. Using bottomonium splittings, kaon decay constant, the decay constant of unmixed $η_s$ meson and the $ϕ$ meson mass we obtained the values of the gradient flow scales in physical units, $\sqrt{t_0} = 0.14428(48)$~fm and $w_0 = 0.17391(52)$~fm. Using the same physical inputs we revisit the determination of the potential $r_1$ scale and find $r_1 = 0.3112(24)$~fm. As a byproduct of our study we obtain the running of the gauge coupling in the gradient flow scheme. We find that within the uncertainties the running of the gradient flow coupling obtained on the lattice is compatible with the perturbative results up to flow radius $\sqrt{8 τ_F}=0.15$ fm.

## Key claim

The paper determines the gradient flow scales \(\sqrt{t_0}\) and \(w_0\) in physical units for 2+1 flavor QCD using HISQ ensembles, finding that within uncertainties the running of the gradient flow coupling obtained on the lattice is compatible with perturbative results only up to a flow radius of \(\sqrt{8 \tau_F}=0.15\) fm, implying a limited range of perturbative applicability.

## Method

The authors used 2+1 flavor HISQ ensembles generated by the HotQCD Collaboration at seventeen values of the bare gauge coupling \(\beta = 10/g_0^2\) ranging from 6.423 to 8.400, with light quark masses set to \(m_s/m_l = 20\) or \(m_s/m_l = 5\). They computed the gradient flow scales \(t_0\) and \(w_0\) from the flowed gauge action density using both clover and improved (Symanzik) discretizations of the field strength tensor, then performed continuum extrapolations of scale ratios and fitted the lattice-scale dependence to an Allton-type ansatz. The physical values of the flow scales were obtained by matching to bottomonium splittings, the kaon decay constant, the \(\eta_s\) meson decay constant, and the \(\phi\) meson mass.

## Key numbers

* \(\sqrt{t_0} = 0.14428(48)\) fm, the gradient flow scale defined by \(\tau^2\langle E(\tau)\rangle = 0.3\) at \(\tau = t_0\)
* \(w_0 = 0.17391(52)\) fm, the gradient flow scale defined by \(\tau \frac{d}{d\tau}[\tau^2\langle E(\tau)\rangle] = 0.3\) at \(\tau = w_0^2\)
* \(r_1 = 0.3112(24)\) fm, the potential scale defined by \(r^2 dV(r)/dr = 1\) at \(r = r_1\)
* \(\sqrt{t_0}/w_0 = 0.8291(8)(6)\), the ratio of gradient flow scales with statistical and systematic uncertainties
* \(r_1/\sqrt{t_0} = 2.1572(91)\), the ratio of the potential scale to the gradient flow scale
* \(r_1/w_0 = 1.7904(81)\), the ratio of the potential scale to the gradient flow scale
* The running of the gradient flow coupling is compatible with perturbative results up to flow radius \(\sqrt{8 \tau_F} = 0.15\) fm

## Context

This work extends previous scale-setting efforts by the HotQCD Collaboration (2014) and the TUMQCD Collaboration (2022) by providing independent determinations of both gradient flow scales and the \(r_1\) scale using the same HISQ ensembles, enabling cross-checks between different scale-setting approaches. The results enable improved scale setting for high-temperature QCD calculations with the HISQ action, where the scale was previously fixed almost exclusively by \(r_1\), and provide input for determining the QCD \(\Lambda\) parameter via the gradient flow coupling scheme as suggested by Hasenfratz (2023) and Wong (2023). The study also addresses tensions between different determinations of \(t_0\) and \(w_0\) noted by the Flavor Lattice Averaging Group (FLAG, 2024) and compares with results from the ETM Collaboration (2021) and HPQCD Collaboration (2013).
