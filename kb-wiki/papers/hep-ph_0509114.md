# From confining fields back to power corrections

> We overview the issue of the power corrections to the parton model, mostly within the context of QCD sum rules. There are a few sources of the power corrections responsible for various qualitative effects. The basic idea that the power corrections are related to confining fields seems to be realized in case of $1/Q^2$ corrections which are dual to high orders of perturbation theory. The evidence comes from lattice studies of the confinement which allowed to identify the confining field configurations as monopoles and vortices. We comment also on the role of the condensate of dimension two, $<(A_μ^a)^2>_{min}$.

## Key claim

Power corrections of order \(1/Q^2\) in QCD sum rules are directly related to confining field configurations (monopoles and vortices) and are dual to high orders of perturbation theory, meaning they should not be added to a long perturbative series but rather replace the asymptotic tail of that series; this duality is specific to the quadratic corrections and is not shared by other power corrections such as those from instantons.

## Method

The paper reviews the status of power corrections in QCD sum rules, comparing the standard Operator Product Expansion (OPE) approach with models that introduce a short-distance tachyonic gluon mass \(m_g^2\) to generate \(1/Q^2\) corrections. It connects these corrections to lattice-identified confining field configurations (monopoles and vortices) and examines the role of the dimension-two condensate \(\langle (A_\mu^a)^2 \rangle_{\text{min}}\) as a gauge-invariant quantity that may serve as a Higgs field in dual-superconductor models of confinement.

## Key numbers

- The hierarchy of critical scales at which parton model violations become sizable is: \((M^2_{\text{crit}})_\pi \approx 4 \cdot (M^2_{\text{crit}})_\rho\) and \((M^2_{\text{crit}})_{\text{glueball }0^+} \approx 4 \cdot (M^2_{\text{crit}})_\pi\).
- The instanton contribution to sum rules starts as a power correction \(\sim (\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/M)^9\).
- For confining vortices on the lattice: total area \(A \approx 24 \, (\text{fm})^{-2} V_4\) and non-Abelian action \(S \approx 0.5 \, A / a^2\), where \(a\) is the lattice spacing and \(V_4\) the total volume.
- The difference between the total gluon condensate and its perturbative value is of order \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^4\), but only after subtracting more than ten orders of perturbation theory; for shorter series the quadratic correction dominates.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule framework of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) by addressing the long-standing puzzle of quadratic \(1/Q^2\) corrections, which are not captured by the standard OPE. It builds on lattice studies of confinement (Greensite, 2003; monopole reviews) that identified monopoles and vortices as confining configurations, and on the model of Chetyrkin et al. (1998) that introduced a short-distance tachyonic gluon mass to reproduce the hierarchy of scales. The paper enables a dual interpretation where confining fields are fundamental variables in a string-theoretic dual formulation of Yang-Mills theory, as suggested by Maldacena (1998).
