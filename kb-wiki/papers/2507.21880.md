# Constraining neutron-proton effective mass splitting through nuclear giant dipole resonance within transport approach

> Based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation, we investigate the effects of the isovector nucleon effective mass $m^*_{v,0}$ and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section $σ^*$ on the isovector giant dipole resonance~(IVGDR) in $^{208}{\rm Pb}$, employing a set of representative Skyrme energy density functionals. We find that the energy-weighted sum rule $m_1$ of the IVGDR is highly sensitive to $m^{*}_{v,0}$ and only mildly dependent on $σ^*$, while the width $Γ$ of the IVGDR is primarily governed by $σ^*$ with a moderate sensitivity to $m^*_{v,0}$. From a Bayesian analysis of both $m_1$ and $Γ$, we infer the isovector effective mass $m^{*}_{v,0}/m$ = $0.731^{+0.027}_{-0.023}$, where $m$ is the bare nucleon mass. Furthermore, by incorporating the isoscalar effective mass $m^*_{s,0}/m = 0.820 \pm 0.030$, extracted from the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance in $^{208}{\rm Pb}$, the linear neutron-proton effective mass splitting coefficient at saturation density $ρ_0$ is determined to be $Δm^*_1 (ρ_0)/m = 0.200 ^{+0.101}_{-0.094}$.

## Key claim

The authors infer the isovector effective mass at saturation density from a Bayesian analysis of both the energy-weighted sum rule and width of the isovector giant dipole resonance in 208Pb, but the extracted linear neutron-proton effective mass splitting coefficient carries large uncertainties and represents a momentum-independent average over the dominant momentum region relevant to nuclear collective motions, not a definitive value for all momenta.

## Method

The Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation was solved using the test particle ansatz with 10000 parallel ensembles to simulate the time evolution of the Wigner function for neutrons and protons in 208Pb. The isovector giant dipole resonance was excited by perturbing the initial momenta of test particles, and the strength function was obtained via Fourier transform of the response function. A set of 55 representative Skyrme energy density functionals was employed to calculate the energy-weighted sum rule and width for various values of the isovector effective mass and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, with a Gaussian process emulator trained on 20 Skyrme interactions to enable Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling for Bayesian inference.

## Key numbers

- Isovector effective mass at saturation density: m*_{v,0}/m = 0.731^{+0.027}_{-0.023} (68.3% confidence level), inferred from Bayesian analysis of both m1 and Gamma of IVGDR in 208Pb
- In-medium correction factor for NN cross section: k = 0.341^{+0.077}_{-0.074} (68.3% confidence level)
- Linear neutron-proton effective mass splitting coefficient at saturation density: Delta m*_1(rho_0)/m = 0.200^{+0.101}_{-0.094}, derived by combining the inferred m*_{v,0}/m with the isoscalar effective mass m*_{s,0}/m = 0.820 +/- 0.030 from ISGQR in 208Pb
- Experimental width of IVGDR in 208Pb used as constraint: Gamma = 4.08 +/- 0.09 MeV
- Experimental energy-weighted sum rule used as constraint: m1 = 883.05 +/- 27.03 MeV fm^2
- Experimental value of 10^4/m1 used for correlation: 11.32 +/- 0.35 fm^{-2} MeV^{-1}, deduced from centroid energy E_{-1} = 13.46 MeV and electric dipole polarizability alpha_D = 19.6 +/- 0.6 fm^3

## Context

This work extends previous BUU-based analyses of giant resonances (Zhang and collaborators, 2020; Song and collaborators, 2021) by simultaneously constraining the isovector effective mass and in-medium cross section through Bayesian inference on both the energy-weighted sum rule and width of the IVGDR. The result for the linear neutron-proton effective mass splitting coefficient (0.200^{+0.101}_{-0.094}) falls within the range of -0.13m to 0.56m spanned by prior constraints from nuclear structure and reaction experiments, including RPA analyses (Zhang and collaborators, 2014; Zhang and collaborators, 2021), optical potential model studies (Li and collaborators, 2015), and heavy-ion collision analyses (Morawetz and collaborators, 2019; Tsang and collaborators, 2024). The positive value obtained from nuclear collective motions contrasts with negative values favored by heavy-ion collision analyses, suggesting a possible momentum dependence of the effective mass splitting that the standard Skyrme EDF cannot capture.
