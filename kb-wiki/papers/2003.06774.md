# Identify the hidden charm pentaquark signal from non-resonant background in electron-proton scattering

> We study the electroproduction of the LHCb pentaquark states with the assumption that they are resonant states. The main concern here is to investigate the final state distribution in the phase space in order to extract the feeble pentaquark signal from the large non-resonant background. Our results show that the ratio of the signal to background would increase significantly with proper kinematic cut, which would be very helpful for future experimental analysis.

## Key claim

The signal-to-background ratio for electroproduction of LHCb pentaquark states can be increased significantly (e.g., from 0.3 to 19 for Pc(4312) at JLab12) by applying kinematic cuts on the final proton's three-momentum and angle, even though the total cross section for pentaquark production is orders of magnitude smaller than the non-resonant background.

## Method

The authors calculated the electroproduction process ep → e'p'J/ψ using two contributions added incoherently: a t-channel Pomeron exchange (soft dipole Pomeron model) for the non-resonant background, and an s-channel pentaquark resonance with spin-parity 3/2−, where the γp → Pc vertex was modeled via vector meson dominance (VMD). The final-state distributions of the proton in the laboratory frame were obtained by numerical integration using VEGAS and RAMBO event generators for two energy configurations: JLab12 (12 GeV electron on fixed proton target) and EicC (3.5 GeV electron colliding with 20 GeV proton).

## Key numbers

- At JLab12, the total cross section for non-resonant background is 1.4 pb, while for pentaquarks it is 0.0016 pb (a factor of ~875 smaller).
- At EicC, the total cross section for non-resonant background is 111 pb, while for pentaquarks it is 0.013 pb (a factor of ~8500 smaller).
- For Pc(4312) at JLab12, the signal-to-background ratio increases from 0.3 to 19 with a kinematic cut of p > 3 GeV on the final proton.
- The masses used for the three pentaquarks are: Pc(4312) at 4311.9 ± 0.7^{+6.8}_{-0.6} MeV, Pc(4440) at 4440.3 ± 1.3^{+4.1}_{-4.7} MeV, and Pc(4457) at 4457.3 ± 0.6^{+4.1}_{-1.7} MeV.
- The decay widths used are: Pc(4312) at 9.8 ± 2.7^{+3.7}_{-4.5} MeV, Pc(4440) at 20.6 ± 4.9^{+8.7}_{-10.1} MeV, and Pc(4457) at 6.4 ± 2.0^{+5.7}_{-1.9} MeV.
- The center-of-mass energy is about 4.8 GeV for JLab12 and about 16.7 GeV for EicC.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pentaquark photoproduction (Wang 2015, Cao 2019, Wu 2019) by considering electroproduction at JLab12 and the proposed EicC facility, where the GlueX Collaboration (Ali 2019) found no evidence for pentaquark photoproduction and set model-dependent upper limits on branching fractions. The key advance is demonstrating that kinematic cuts on the final proton can dramatically enhance the signal-to-background ratio, enabling future experimental searches at JLab12, EicC, and the US EIC to identify pentaquark signals that would otherwise be buried in the large non-resonant background.
