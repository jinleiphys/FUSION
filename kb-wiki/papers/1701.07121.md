# Novel scenario for production of heavy flavored mesons in heavy ion collisions

> The observed strong suppression of heavy flavored hadrons produced with high $p_T$, is caused by final state interactions with the created dense medium. Vacuum radiation of high-pT heavy quarks ceases at a short time scale, as is confirmed by pQCD calculations and by LEP measurements of the fragmentation functions of heavy quarks. Production of a heavy flavored hadrons in a dense medium is considerably delayed due to prompt breakup of the hadrons by the medium. This causes a strong suppression of the heavy quark yield because of the specific shape of the fragmentation function. The parameter-free description is in a good accord with available data.

# Wiki Page: Novel Scenario for Production of Heavy Flavored Mesons in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The observed strong suppression of high-pT heavy flavored hadrons in heavy ion collisions is caused not primarily by induced energy loss in the hot medium, but by a delayed hadronization process: heavy mesons produced early are repeatedly broken up by the medium, forcing the production point to the dilute surface and increasing energy loss before the final meson escapes, which suppresses the yield due to the steeply falling fragmentation function at small z.

## Method

The authors calculate the production length distribution of heavy mesons (B and D) by relating the vacuum fragmentation function to the rate of radiational energy loss of the heavy quark, using the coherence length formalism for gluon radiation. For in-medium propagation, they model the heavy quark as repeatedly forming and breaking up Q\bar q dipoles, with energy loss from both perturbative radiation and a temperature-dependent string tension κ(T)=κ(1−T/T_c)^{1/3} with T_c=280 MeV. The survival probability of the final dipole is computed using a path-integral Green function approach with an imaginary potential proportional to the transport coefficient \hat q, which is the only fitted parameter.

## Key numbers

- The mean production length l_p of B-mesons shrinks with rising pT; at pT=10 GeV the B-meson formation length l_f^B = 0.8 fm.
- The mean free path of a B-meson in the hot medium is λ_B ≈ 0.04 fm for \hat q = 1 GeV^2/fm.
- The transport coefficient extracted from light hadron quenching is \hat q_0 = 1.6 GeV^2/fm at √s=200 GeV and \hat q_0 = 2 GeV^2/fm at √s=2760 GeV.
- For B-meson production at √s=2.76 TeV, calculations with \hat q_0 = 2.5 and 3 GeV^2/fm are compared to CMS data for indirect J/ψ production.
- For B-meson production at √s=5.02 TeV, calculations with \hat q_0 = 2.6 and 3.2 GeV^2/fm are compared to ATLAS data.
- The string tension in vacuum is κ ≈ 1 GeV/fm; the critical temperature is T_c = 280 MeV.
- The charge radius squared of the B-meson is ⟨r_ch^2⟩_B = 0.378 fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kopeliovich et al. (2015) on energy loss of light hadrons in heavy ion collisions to heavy flavors, contradicting the earlier expectation from the dead-cone effect (Dokshitzer and Kharzeev, 2001) that heavy quarks would be much less suppressed than light ones. The novel scenario explains the similar suppression magnitudes observed for heavy and light hadrons. The analysis enables extraction of the transport coefficient \hat q from heavy flavor data, with values consistent with those from light hadron analyses (Kopeliovich et al., 2015). The model relies on lattice simulations of the temperature-dependent string tension (string1, string2, string3) and on the Cornell-type potential for heavy meson wave functions (Yang et al., 2015).
