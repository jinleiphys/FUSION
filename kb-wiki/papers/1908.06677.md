# Intertwined quantum phase transitions in the Zr chain

> We introduce the notion of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), for which a crossing of two configurations coexists with a pronounced shape-evolution of each configuration. A detailed analysis in the framework of the interacting boson model with configuration mixing, provides evidence for this scenario in the Zr isotopes. The latter exhibit a normal configuration which remains spherical along the chain, but exchanges roles with an intruder configuration, which undergoes first a spherical to prolate-deformed [U(5)$\to$SU(3)] QPT and then a crossover to $γ$-unstable [SU(3)$\to$SO(6)].

## Key claim

The paper introduces the concept of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), where a crossing of two nuclear configurations (Type II QPT) coexists with a pronounced shape-evolution of each individual configuration (Type I QPT), and provides evidence for this scenario in the Zr isotopes using the interacting boson model with configuration mixing.

## Method

The authors employed the Interacting Boson Model with Configuration Mixing (IBM-CM), using Hamiltonians for a normal configuration (N boson space) and an intruder configuration (N+2 boson space) with a coupling term. They performed a global fit to energy and E2 data for the entire chain of Zr isotopes from neutron number 52 to 70, calculating spectra, B(E2) values, isotope shifts, two-neutron separation energies, and classical potential surfaces via matrix-coherent-states.

## Key numbers

- In the region between neutron number 52 and 56, the ratio R4/2 for configuration A is approximately 1.6 and for configuration B is approximately 2.3
- In 104Zr, the intruder configuration shows E(2_1+) = 139.3 keV and R4/2(B) = 3.24
- In 106Zr, E(2_2+) = 607.0 keV and E(4_1+) = 476.5 keV, indicating gamma-unstable behavior
- In 110Zr, E(4_1+) = 565 keV and E(2_2+) = 485 keV, a signature of SO(6) symmetry
- Ground state wave function purity: a^2 = 98.2% for 98Zr, b^2 = 87.2% for 100Zr, and b^2 = 99.9% for 102Zr
- Boson effective charges: e(A) = 0.9 (W.u.)^(1/2) and e(B) = 2.24 (W.u.)^(1/2)
- Isotope shift parameters: alpha = 0.235 fm^2, eta = 0.264 fm^2
- Two-neutron separation energy parameters: tilde-A = -16.5 MeV, tilde-B = 0.758 MeV, Delta_n = 2 MeV for neutron numbers 58-70

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions in nuclei beyond single-configuration Type I QPTs (observed in the neutron number 90 region for Nd-Sm-Gd isotopes, as discussed by Cejnar) and Type II QPTs with strong mixing (observed in light Pb-Hg isotopes by Ramos et al. 2014). The results resemble those obtained in Monte-Carlo shell-model calculations by Togashi et al., but differ from mean-field based calculations by Delaroche, Mei, and Nomura et al., which smooth out the phase transitional behavior. The algebraic approach enables both quantum and classical analysis of QPTs, and the findings enable future studies of IQPTs in other nuclear chains such as Mo and Ge.
