# Hadronic Decays of N and ΔResonances in a Chiral Quark Model

> πand ηdecay modes of light baryon resonances are investigated within a chiral quark model whose hyperfine interaction is based on Goldstone-boson exchange. For the decay mechanism a modified version of the 3P0 model is employed. Our primary aim is to provide a further test of the recently proposed Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model. We compare the predictions for πand ηdecay widths with experiment and also with results from a traditional one-gluon-exchange constituent quark model. The differences between nonrelativistic and semirelativistic versions of the constituent quark models are outlined. We also discuss the sensitivity of the results on the parametrization of the meson wave function entering the 3P0 model.

## Key claim

The paper tests the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model (GBE CQM) by comparing its predictions for π and η decay widths of N and Δ resonances against experiment and against a traditional one-gluon-exchange (OGE) CQM, finding that the GBE CQM reproduces many decay widths correctly but with significant sensitivity to whether the model is treated semirelativistically or nonrelativistically and to the parametrization of the meson wave function.

## Method

The authors solved a Schrödinger-type three-quark equation using the stochastic variational method to obtain baryon wave functions for both GBE and OGE CQMs in semirelativistic and nonrelativistic versions. They then calculated partial decay widths for π and η decays of N and Δ resonances up to about 1.8 GeV using a modified 3P0 decay model, adjusting the pair-creation constant γ separately for π and η decays to match the Δ1232 → Nπ and N1535 → Nη widths respectively.

## Key numbers

- Δ1232 → Nπ decay width: 120 MeV for all four CQM versions (GBE SR, GBE NR, OGE SR, OGE NR), fitted to the experimental value of (119±1)+5−5 MeV
- N1440 → Nπ: GBE SR gives 517 MeV, GBE NR gives 258 MeV, OGE SR gives 1064 MeV, OGE NR gives 161 MeV; experiment is (227±18)+70−59 MeV
- N1535 → Nπ: GBE SR gives 336 MeV, GBE NR gives 75 MeV, OGE SR gives 462 MeV, OGE NR gives 109 MeV; experiment is (67±15)+55−17 MeV
- N1535 → Nη: all four CQM versions give 64 MeV, fitted to the experimental value of (64±19)+76−15 MeV
- N1680 → Nπ: GBE SR gives 85 MeV, GBE NR gives 85 MeV, OGE SR gives 149 MeV, OGE NR gives 313 MeV; experiment is (85±7)+6−6 MeV
- Mean square radius of nucleon: GBE SR = 0.092 fm², GBE NR = 0.134 fm², OGE SR = 0.076 fm², OGE NR = 0.219 fm² (for pointlike constituent quarks)
- Pair-creation constant γ for π decays: GBE SR = 15.365, GBE NR = 14.635, OGE SR = 18.015, OGE NR = 11.868
- Pair-creation constant γ for η decays: GBE SR = 5.929, GBE NR = 6.682, OGE SR = 6.572, OGE NR = 4.937

## Context

This work extends the test of the GBE CQM proposed by Glozman et al. (1998) beyond baryon mass spectra to dynamical observables, specifically hadronic decay widths. It contrasts the GBE CQM with the traditional OGE CQM of Bhaduri, Cohler, and Nogami (1980), finding that certain resonances (like N1535 and N1650) are "structure-dependent" with widths varying by orders of magnitude between models, while others (like N1520, N1675, N1700) are "structure-independent." The results enable further refinement of constituent quark models by identifying which decay channels discriminate between GBE and OGE dynamics, and highlight the need for improved meson wave function parametrizations beyond the Gaussian and Yukawa forms tested here.
