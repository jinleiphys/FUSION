# Ground-State Electromagnetic Moments of Calcium Isotopes

> High-resolution bunched-beam collinear laser spectroscopy was used to measure the optical hyperfine spectra of the $^{43-51}$Ca isotopes. The ground state magnetic moments of $^{49,51}$Ca and quadrupole moments of $^{47,49,51}$Ca were measured for the first time, and the $^{51}$Ca ground state spin $I=3/2$ was determined in a model-independent way. Our results provide a critical test of modern nuclear theories based on shell-model calculations using phenomenological as well as microscopic interactions. The results for the neutron-rich isotopes are in excellent agreement with predictions using interactions derived from chiral effective field theory including three-nucleon forces, while lighter isotopes illustrate the presence of particle-hole excitations of the $^{40}$Ca core in their ground state.

## Key claim

The paper reports that for neutron-rich calcium isotopes with N >= 27, shell-model calculations using interactions derived from chiral effective field theory including three-nucleon (3N) forces provide excellent agreement with measured magnetic and quadrupole moments, while lighter isotopes near N=20 require large-scale calculations that allow particle-hole excitations of the 40Ca core to reproduce the data.

## Method

High-resolution bunched-beam collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at ISOLDE/CERN on the 4s 2S1/2 to 4p 2P3/2 ionic transition in Ca+ at 393 nm. Hyperfine structure spectra were recorded for 43-51Ca isotopes, and the magnetic hyperfine constants A(2S1/2), A(2P3/2), and quadrupole hyperfine constants B(2P3/2) were extracted by fitting Voigt profiles. Magnetic moments were determined relative to the known reference isotope 43Ca, and quadrupole moments were extracted using a calculated electric field gradient of 151.3(7) MHz/b from relativistic coupled-cluster theory.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic moment of 49Ca: mu = -1.3799(8) mu_N (first measurement)
* Magnetic moment of 51Ca: mu = -1.0496(11) mu_N (first measurement)
* Quadrupole moment of 47Ca: Q = +0.084(6) b (first measurement)
* Quadrupole moment of 49Ca: Q = -0.036(3) b (first measurement)
* Quadrupole moment of 51Ca: Q = +0.036(12) b (first measurement)
* Ground state spin of 51Ca: I = 3/2 (determined model-independently)
* Magnetic moment of 45Ca: mu = -1.3264(13) mu_N
* Magnetic moment of 47Ca: mu = -1.4064(11) mu_N
* Quadrupole moment of 43Ca: Q = -0.028(9) b
* Quadrupole moment of 45Ca: Q = +0.020(7) b

## Context

This work extends previous laser spectroscopy measurements on calcium isotopes and provides the first electromagnetic moment data for 47,49,51Ca. The results test shell-model calculations using phenomenological interactions (KB3G, GXPF1A, SDPF.SM) and microscopic interactions from chiral effective field theory with three-nucleon forces (Holt 2012, Holt 2014). The data reveal that cross-shell excitations across N=32 are important for 51Ca, with the ratio of (p1/2)1(p3/2)2 to (p3/2)3 configurations being 3.5% with NN+3N and 4.0% with GXPF1A versus 2.0% with KB3G. The results enable future tests of two-body currents from chiral effective field theory and improved calculations of effective g-factors and magnetic operators.
