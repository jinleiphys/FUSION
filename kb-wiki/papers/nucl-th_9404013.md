# The $η$NN Coupling in Eta Photoproduction

> Low-energy eta photoproduction on the nucleon is studied in an effective Lagrangian approach that contains Born terms, vector meson and nucleon resonance contributions. The resonance sector includes the $S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ states whose couplings are fixed by independent electromagnetic and hadronic data. The available $(γ,η)$ data are employed to discuss the difference between pseudoscalar and pseudovector Born terms and to determine the magnitude of the $ηNN$ coupling constant. We present multipoles that are most sensitive to the various model ingredients and demonstrate how these multipoles may be accessed in polarization observables. Cross section calculations are presented for eta photoproduction on light nuclei.

# The $\eta$NN Coupling in Eta Photoproduction

## Key claim

The $\eta NN$ coupling constant can be extracted from low-energy eta photoproduction data, but the total cross section alone cannot uniquely determine it because a pseudoscalar coupling with a small constant (around $g_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi = 0.1$) and a pseudovector coupling with a large constant (around $g_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi = 6.0$) produce nearly identical total cross sections; the angular distribution and polarization observables are required to distinguish between the two coupling schemes.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian model for $\gamma N \rightarrow \eta N$ including Born terms (both pseudoscalar and pseudovector $\eta NN$ couplings), $t$-channel vector meson exchange ($\rho$ and $\omega$), and three nucleon resonances ($S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{11}(1440)$, $D_{13}(1520)$) whose parameters were fixed by independent fits to $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$, $\pi N \rightarrow \pi\pi N$, and $\pi^- p \rightarrow \eta n$ data from Bennhold and Tanabe (1991). The electromagnetic vertices for the resonances were determined from pion photoproduction data, leaving no free parameters in the $(\gamma,\eta)$ process except the $\eta NN$ coupling. The model was then applied to eta photoproduction on light nuclei ($d$, $^3$He, $^3$H, $^4$He) using plane wave impulse approximation with realistic nuclear wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance decays 45-55% into $\eta N$, while the nearby $S_{11}(1650)$ has a branching ratio of only 1.5% in the $\eta$ channel.
- The $\eta$ photoproduction threshold on the nucleon is at 707 MeV photon energy (center-of-mass energy $W_{thr.}=1486$ MeV).
- The $\eta NN$ coupling constant $g_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi$ from SU(3) flavor symmetry lies between 0.8 and 1.9 for commonly used values of $\alpha$ between 0.6 and 0.65.
- $NN$ forward dispersion relations give $g_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi + g_{\eta' NN}^2/4\pi \leq 1.0$.
- Piekarewicz (1993) constrained the $\eta NN$ coupling to the range 0.32-0.53 from $\pi$-$\eta$ mixing calculations.
- The two-pion decay width parameters are $\gamma(S_{11})=4.3$ MeV, $\gamma(P_{11})=80.3$ MeV, and $\gamma(D_{13})=24.2$ MeV.
- For a pseudoscalar coupling of $g_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi = 0.33$, the background Born terms vanish.
- The momentum transfer in $\eta$ photoproduction at threshold is $Q^2 = 7.8$ fm$^{-2}$.
- The coherent cross section for $^4$He reaches roughly the 10 nb level in a small angular region, falling below 1 nb for most angles.
- The isoscalar to isovector ratio of the $E_{0+}$ multipole predicted by the model is $E_{0+}^{(0)}/E_{0+}^{(p)}=0.15$.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model of Bennhold and Tanabe (1991) by adding Born terms and vector meson exchange contributions that were previously neglected. It addresses the long-standing uncertainty in the $\eta NN$ coupling constant, which varies between 0 and 7 in one-boson exchange potential fits (Bonn potential, Brockmann 1990) and between 0.6 and 1.7 from $\pi^- p \rightarrow \eta n$ analyses (Penner 1987). The paper enables future extraction of small resonance multipoles (particularly $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$) through polarization experiments at LEGS (Brookhaven), GRAAL (Grenoble), and CEBAF, and provides predictions for nuclear targets that can test the $\eta N$ scattering length suggested by Wilkin (1993) to explain large near-threshold production in $pd \rightarrow \eta^3$He.
