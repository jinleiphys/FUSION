# Scaling Properties of Pion Production

> The scaling of pion multiplicities with system size in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions at 200 A GeV is investigated. A nuclear geometry calculation of incoherent multiple scattering including effects of deceleration of the participating particles is presented. Pion multiplicities at high transverse momenta agree relatively well with an overall scaling with the number of binary collisions. The deviations from this gross scaling for p+A and S+Au reactions at 200AGeV are compared to model predictions and the implications are discussed. Within this picture no universal scaling law for high pT particle production is obtained. In addition to the conceptual problems of such an incoherent model, it is shown that the implied kinematic relation between a possible pT-broadening and deceleration leads to contradictions. The scaling of pion production at intermediate pT with system size does not indicate a multiple scattering enhancement.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while pion production at high transverse momentum approximately scales with the number of binary collisions, no universal scaling law for high pT particle production exists; the incoherent multiple scattering model used to describe the nuclear enhancement requires parameters that contradict momentum conservation in individual scatterings.

## Method

The author performs a nuclear geometry calculation using a Glauber-type Monte Carlo simulation with Woods-Saxon nucleon distributions, randomly distributing nucleons and counting binary collisions based on a straight-line trajectory criterion with an interaction distance of 1.025 fm. The model includes deceleration effects via a constant rapidity shift per collision and calculates an effective number of prescatterings to control initial-state multiple scattering, using a hydrodynamically inspired formula to relate transverse rapidity shifts to particle production. Scaled pion multiplicities are computed for p+A and S+Au reactions at 200 A GeV and compared to data from Antreasyan (1979) and Albrecht (1998).

## Key numbers

- The interaction distance for nucleon-nucleon collisions is set to d_int = 1.025 fm, with a hard core radius R_hc = 0.6 fm.
- The Woods-Saxon density profile uses r_0 = 1.16 A^(1/3) - 1.35 A^(-1/3) with a diffuseness parameter of 0.54 fm.
- For high pT production, the attenuation factor scales as (1 - x_T)^9 with x_T = 2p_T / sqrt(s); for low pT, a logarithmic scaling in s is used with coefficients 0.88 + 0.44 ln(s) + 0.118 ln^2(s).
- The slope parameter T for the hydrodynamical fit to p+p data at high pT is T = 240 MeV.
- For delta y = 1, a transverse rapidity shift of delta rho ≈ 7 would be required to account for the rise in p+A data, which the author calls "completely unphysical."
- Data are compared at p_T = 0.77 GeV/c and p_T = 3.08 GeV/c from Antreasyan (1979) and at rapidity 2.1 ≤ y ≤ 2.9 from Albrecht (1998).

## Context

This work extends studies of the Cronin effect in p+A collisions at Fermilab (Antreasyan 1979) and recent high-precision neutral pion data from 200 A GeV S+Au collisions (Albrecht 1998). The author tests whether a random-walk multiple scattering model, similar to that used by Leonidov (1996) to describe pT-broadening at intermediate pT, can explain the nuclear enhancement across both p+A and S+Au systems. The results contradict such incoherent models and point toward the need for coherent multiple scattering treatments, such as the perturbative QCD calculations by Wang (1998) that successfully describe central S+S and Pb+Pb data.
