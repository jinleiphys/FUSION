# Semiclassical Description of Exotic Nuclear Shapes

> Exotic nuclear structures such as bubbles and tori are analyzed through semiclassical extended Thomas-Fermi calculations with the Skyrme force SkM$^*$. The variational equations for neutron and proton densities are solved fully self-consistently in spherical (bubbles) and cylindrical (tori) symmetries. The possible existence of bubble configurations in some astrophysical scenarios is discussed. The stability of toroidal structures against change of quadrupole moment is studied. ## Key claim
The paper claims that spherical bubble and toroidal nuclear shapes containing several hundred nucleons can be described by semiclassical extended Thomas-Fermi calculations, but the stability of these exotic shapes against deformation ultimately requires shell corrections that are not included in the present semiclassical treatment.

## Method
The authors solved the variational Euler-Lagrange equations derived from the Skyrme SkM* energy density functional using the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) method up to order hbar^2. The equations were solved fully self-consistently using the imaginary time-step method in spherical coordinates for bubble configurations and in cylindrical coordinates for toroidal configurations. For toroidal nuclei, calculations were performed with a constraint on the mass quadrupole moment Q2 to map the potential energy surface.

## Key numbers
- For the Z=240 isotopic chain, true bubble and semi-bubble solutions coexist in the neutron range N=524 to N=576; the ground state is the true bubble solution with energies per nucleon ranging from -4.635 MeV (N=524) to -4.219 MeV (N=620)
- The limiting temperature for spherical bubbles is about 5 MeV, smaller than the 8-10 MeV range found in normal nuclei
- At the limiting temperature, the excitation energy per nucleon is 3.40 MeV/A for the semi-bubble 500180 and 2.06 MeV/A for the true bubble 900274
- For the neutron star crust scenario with Z=250 and average density 5.77 x 10^-3 fm^-3, the Wigner-Seitz cell radius is 107 fm and contains A=29608 nucleons with energy per nucleon 1.846 MeV
- For the supernova collapse scenario at average density 0.05 fm^-3 and proton concentration Ye=0.3, the semi-bubble solution has free energy per particle 28.86 MeV and the true bubble solution has 28.73 MeV
- For toroidal nuclei, the system 320132 becomes the ground state in toroidal configuration; for 416164 the ground state occurs at Q2 = -990 b
- At T=1 MeV, the energy of the 416164 system shifts upward by about 55-60 MeV along the entire potential energy surface

## Context
This work extends earlier liquid drop model studies by Wong (1973) and Dietrich and Pomorski (1997) on bubble and toroidal nuclei, and complements mean-field Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Decharge et al. (1999, 2003) using the Gogny D1S force and by Nazarewicz et al. (2002) using Skyrme forces. The semiclassical ETF approach provides the smooth, shell-effect-free part of the nuclear energy, which is a necessary step toward a full quantal description that would include shell corrections computed from the semiclassical single-particle potentials. The astrophysical applications extend the work of Bonche, Levit and Vautherin (1984) on hot nuclei and of Suraud (1987) on thermal Thomas-Fermi calculations.
