# Neutrino mean free path in neutron stars in the presence of hyperons

> We investigate the neutrino elastic differential cross-section (NDCS) and corresponding mean free path for neutral current scattering in the dense matter of a neutron star. A wide range of observed neutron star (NS) masses is considered, including the presence of $Λ$, $Ξ^{-}$, and $Ξ^{0}$ hyperons in the heaviest stars. Their presence significantly decreases the total neutrino mean free path in the heavier stars.

## Key claim

The presence of Λ, Ξ⁻, and Ξ⁰ hyperons in the densest cores of neutron stars significantly decreases the total neutrino mean free path, with the effect becoming pronounced only in stars massive enough (above approximately 1.8 M☉) to contain hyperons; the paper's abstract glosses over the condition that this decrease is density-dependent and only occurs at baryon densities where hyperons appear.

## Method

The authors computed the neutrino differential cross-section (NDCS) and neutrino mean free path (NMFP) for neutral-current elastic scattering off neutrons, protons, Λ, Ξ⁻, and Ξ⁰ baryons in neutron star matter, using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to describe the equation of state and baryon effective masses. They imposed β-equilibrium and charge neutrality, included a phenomenological repulsive baryon-overlap term, and evaluated the polarization insertions within linear response theory at zero temperature for neutrino energies of 5 MeV and 30 MeV across a range of neutron star masses from 1.4 M☉ to the maximum 2.14 M☉.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass predicted by the QMC model: 2.14 M☉, with central baryon density exceeding 6 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is saturation density).
- Threshold baryon number density for Λ hyperon appearance: n_B ≈ 0.55 fm⁻³ (≈ 3.4 n₀); for Ξ⁻: n_B ≈ 0.58 fm⁻³; for Ξ⁰: n_B ≈ 0.87 fm⁻³.
- For a 1.4 M☉ neutron star, central baryon density is n_B ≈ 2.5 n₀ (≈ 0.40 fm⁻³); for a 1.6 M☉ star, central density is n_B ≈ 3.0 n₀.
- The NDCS for neutrino-Ξ⁻ scattering begins to dominate beyond n_B ≈ 4.0 n₀.
- For E_ν = 30 MeV, the NDCS increases by a factor of approximately 50 at higher nuclear matter density compared to lower densities.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of neutrino mean free paths in neutron stars, which mostly considered only nucleons (protons and neutrons) or occasionally the Λ hyperon, as in the studies by Reddy (1997) and Mornas (2004). The authors use the QMC model, which self-consistently determines hyperon-meson couplings without additional free parameters, unlike many relativistic mean-field models. The results enable more realistic input for supernova and neutron-star merger simulations, where neutrino transport depends on the mean free path, and they highlight that the Ξ⁻ hyperon, rather than the Λ, dominates the reduction in mean free path at the highest densities.
