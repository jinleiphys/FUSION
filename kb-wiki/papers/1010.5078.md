# Structural evolution in Pt isotopes with the Interacting Boson Model Hamiltonian derived from the Gogny Energy Density Functional

> Spectroscopic calculations are carried out, for the description of the shape/phase transition in Pt nuclei in terms of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) Hamiltonian derived from (constrained) Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the finite range and density dependent Gogny-D1S Energy Density Functional. Assuming that the many-nucleon driven dynamics of nuclear surface deformation can be simulated by effective bosonic degrees of freedom, the Gogny-D1S potential energy surface (PES) with quadrupole degrees of freedom is mapped onto the corresponding PES of the IBM. Using this mapping procedure, the parameters of the IBM Hamiltonian, relevant to the low-lying quadrupole collective states, are derived as functions of the number of valence nucleons. Merits of both Gogny-HFB and IBM approaches are utilized so that the spectra and the wave functions in the laboratory system are calculated precisely. The experimental low-lying spectra of both ground-state and side-band levels are well reproduced. From the systematics of the calculated spectra and the reduced E2 transition probabilities $B$(E2), the prolate-to-oblate shape/phase transition is shown to take place quite smoothly as a function of neutron number $N$ in the considered Pt isotopic chain, for which the $γ$-softness plays an essential role. All these spectroscopic observables behave consistently with the relevant PESs and the derived parameters of the IBM Hamiltonian as functions of $N$. Spectroscopic predictions are also made for those nuclei which do not have enough experimental E2 data.

## Key claim

The prolate-to-oblate shape/phase transition in Pt isotopes occurs quite smoothly as a function of neutron number, with γ-softness playing an essential role, but the IBM Hamiltonian used cannot produce a triaxial minimum, so the γ-softness is simulated by choosing χπ and χν such that their sum is nearly zero.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1S energy density functional to obtain potential energy surfaces (PES) in the β-γ plane for even-even Pt isotopes 172-200. They then mapped these fermionic PES onto the corresponding PES of the proton-neutron Interacting Boson Model (IBM-2) using a wavelet analysis procedure, deriving the IBM Hamiltonian parameters (ε, κ, χπ, χν, Cβ) for each nucleus. The mapped IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized using the NPBOS code to compute excitation spectra and reduced E2 transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

* The IBM PESs are shown within 0.00 ≤ β ≤ 0.40 and 0° ≤ γ ≤ 60° up to 2 MeV excitation from the minimum, with contour spacing of 100 keV
* The HFB quasiparticle operators were expanded in a Harmonic Oscillator basis containing Nshell = 13 major shells
* The ratio R1 = B(E2;4₁⁺→2₁⁺)/B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) is close to 10/7, the O(6) and SU(3) limit of IBM, rather than the U(5) limit value of 2
* The triaxial minimum point in the HFB PES differs by at most several hundred keV in energy from either prolate or oblate saddle point
* The parameter κ is almost constant and somewhat larger compared to the phenomenological value from BijkerOs

## Context

This work extends the fermion-to-boson mapping procedure previously developed by Nomura and Otsuka (2010) for Skyrme EDFs to the Gogny-D1S EDF, following mean-field studies of Pt isotopes by Rodriguez-Guzman et al. (2010) that used Gogny-D1S, D1N, and D1M parameterizations. The results enable spectroscopic predictions for Pt nuclei lacking experimental E2 data, and the approach can be extended to other isotopic chains where the five-dimensional collective Bohr Hamiltonian approach (e.g., from pairing-plus-quadrupole models or Gogny-HFB calculations) has been applied.
