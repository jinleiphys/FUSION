# In-Medium Bottomonium Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study bottomonium production in heavy-ion collisions using a transport model which utilizes kinetic-rate and Boltzmann equations to calculate the energy, centrality and transverse-momentum ($p_T$) dependence of the yields. Both gluo-dissociation and inelastic parton-induced break-up including interference effects are improved over previous work by using in-medium binding energies from a thermodynamic $T$-matrix approach. A coalescence model with bottom-quark spectra from Langevin simulations is implemented to account for thermal off-equilibrium effects in the $p_T$-spectra of the regeneration contribution. We also update the equation of state for the bulk medium by extracting it from lattice-QCD results. A systematic analysis of bottomonium observables is conducted in comparison to RHIC and LHC data. In particular, the off-equilibrium bottom-quark spectra are found to play an important role in the bottomonium $p_T$ spectra.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a transport model combining rate equations, Boltzmann equations, and a coalescence model with in-medium binding energies from a thermodynamic T-matrix approach can reproduce bottomonium suppression patterns at RHIC and LHC, but the quantitative role of regeneration and its impact on interpreting current data remains an open issue, with the agreement depending on the choice of a rescaling parameter eta=1.1 rather than eta=1.0.

## Method

The authors employ a rate equation for bottomonium production in an expanding fireball background, using in-medium binding energies extracted from thermodynamic T-matrix calculations with a rescaling parameter eta to account for theoretical uncertainties. They compute inelastic reaction rates including both gluo-dissociation and quasi-free parton-induced break-up mechanisms, and calculate transverse-momentum spectra using a Boltzmann equation for the primordial component and a coalescence model with bottom-quark spectra from Langevin simulations for the regeneration component. The bulk medium evolution uses an equation of state extracted from lattice-QCD results, with total entropy adjusted to reproduce experimental hadron yields at a chemical freezeout temperature of 170 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Charged-particle density dN_ch/dy at 5.02 TeV is increased by 22.5% over the value at 2.76 TeV
- Chemical freezeout temperature set to T_c = 170 MeV
- Feeddown on Upsilon states is about 30% at low p_T and 50% at high p_T
- A 30% feeddown from higher excited states to the Upsilon(2S) is assumed to be fully melted
- Input cross sections for bottom/onium production in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV assume a 50% increase relative to 2.76 TeV
- Nuclear absorption cross sections sigma_Y^abs varied between 0 and 3 mb for cold-nuclear matter uncertainty bands
- Shadowing effects represented by a 0-15% range from Eskola (2009)

## Context

This work extends previous transport model calculations by Grandchamp (2005) and Emerick (2011) by using improved in-medium binding energies from T-matrix calculations by Riek (2010) and implementing a coalescence model with bottom-quark spectra from Langevin simulations by He (2014) to account for thermal off-equilibrium effects. The results are compared to new experimental data from STAR at 0.2 TeV and CMS at 5.02 TeV, showing fair agreement, and the calculations indicate that regeneration plays a sub-dominant but significant role for Upsilon(1S) while being the dominant production source for the strongly suppressed Upsilon(2S) at LHC energies. The work enables future quantitative determination of in-medium modifications of the QCD force once other model uncertainties such as fireball evolution, cold-nuclear matter effects, and the role of regeneration are better controlled.
