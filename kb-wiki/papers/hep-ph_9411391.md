# Characteristics of QCD Phase Transitions in an Extended Skyrme Model on $S^3$

> We study the characteristics of the QCD phase transitions in dense hadronic matter using the Skyrme model constructed on S^3. We find numerically the localized solutions on $S^3$ using the extended Skyrme model which implements correctly the scale symmetry of QCD. The transition from the localized phase to the delocalized phase is found to be of first order at the critical radius of the hypersphere, $L_c$. The chiral restoration and the gluon decondensation also take place at the same critical size.

# Characteristics of QCD Phase Transitions in an Extended Skyrme Model on $S^3$

## Key claim

The paper claims that the QCD phase transition from localized to delocalized hadronic matter on a hypersphere is of first order at a critical radius $L_c$, with chiral restoration and gluon decondensation occurring simultaneously at that same critical size. This result is conditional on the inclusion of a dilaton/glueball field $\chi$ that implements scale symmetry, in contrast to earlier models without this field which found a second-order transition.

## Method

The authors place a single skyrmion (baryon number $B=1$) on a three-dimensional hypersphere $S^3(\rho)$ using the hedgehog ansatz for the chiral field and a classical field for the glueball/dilaton field $\chi$. They extremize the static energy functional derived from an extended Skyrme Lagrangian that includes a kinetic term for $\chi$ and a scale-anomaly potential $B\chi^4(\ln\chi^4-1)/4$, then solve the coupled Euler-Lagrange equations numerically for the profile functions $f(\mu)$ and $\chi(\mu)$ as functions of the dimensionless hypersphere radius $L = e_s f_\pi \rho$.

## Key numbers

- The critical radius for the first-order phase transition is $L_c \cong 1.9$ (dimensionless), where the energy of the localized solution equals that of the delocalized (identity map) solution.
- The localized solutions exist only for $L > L_t \cong 1.9$; below this radius, only the delocalized identity map solution is found numerically.
- The parameter set used is: $e_4 = 4.5$, $f_\pi = 93$ MeV, $C = 3.29 \times 10^2$ (MeV)$^2$, $B = (220$ MeV$)^4/e$.
- In the model without the $\chi$ field, the phase transition is of second order at $L_c = \sqrt{2}$.

## Context

This work extends the hypersphere approach to dense matter pioneered by Manton and Ruback (1986) and earlier studies of skyrmions on $S^3$ by Jackson, Wirzba, and collaborators. It directly addresses a limitation of Reinhardt and Dang (1989), who used the same extended Lagrangian but did not clearly determine the order or critical radius of the phase transition. The inclusion of the glueball field $\chi$ via the scale-anomaly potential, following Gomm, Jain, and Johnson (1986), enables the simultaneous study of gluon decondensation and chiral restoration. The results enable future work on metastable baryonic states in the region $L_t < L < L_c$ and provide a framework for studying first-order QCD phase transitions in curved-space effective models.
