# Short-Range Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay Mechanisms

> Neutrinoless double beta decay can significantly help to shed light on the issue of non-zero neutrino mass, as observation of this lepton number violating process would imply neutrinos are Majorana particles. However, the underlying interaction does not have to be as simple as the standard neutrino mass mechanism. The entire variety of neutrinoless double beta decay mechanisms can be approached effectively. In this work we focus on a theoretical description of short-range effective contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay, which are equivalent to 9-dimensional effective operators incorporating the appropriate field content. We give a detailed derivation of the nuclear matrix elements and phase space factors corresponding to individual terms of the effective Lagrangian. Using these, we provide general formulae for the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life and angular correlation of the outgoing electrons.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete theoretical framework for short-range neutrinoless double beta decay mechanisms, showing that the half-life and angular correlation can be expressed in terms of effective couplings and nuclear matrix elements, but the results depend on the specific Lorentz structure of the dimension-9 operators and the chiralities of the currents involved.

## Method

The authors construct a complete basis of 24 linearly independent dimension-9 effective operators invariant under SU(3)C × U(1)Q that contribute to neutrinoless double beta decay, categorized into five types (O1 through O5) with different combinations of scalar, vector, and tensor quark currents paired with scalar or axial-vector electron currents. They derive the nuclear matrix elements by performing a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation to reduce quark currents to non-relativistic nucleon currents, including q²-dependent form factors parametrized in dipole form, and evaluate the resulting two-body operators in the closure approximation. The leptonic phase space factors are calculated separately using electron wave functions evaluated at the nuclear surface, and the results are combined to give general formulae for the differential decay rate and angular correlation.

## Key numbers

- The effective Lagrangian is normalized by G_F²/(2m_p) with the Fermi constant G_F and proton mass m_p, making the effective couplings ε_i dimensionless
- The vector form factor uses g_V = 1 and m_V = 0.84 GeV; the axial form factor uses g_A = 1.269 and m_A = 1.09 GeV
- The weak magnetism form factor uses g_W = μ_p - μ_n = 3.70 with m_W = 0.84 GeV
- The induced pseudoscalar form factor at zero momentum transfer is g_P = 231, derived from PCAC with pion mass m_π = 0.138 GeV
- The current experimental lower limits used are T_1/2(¹³⁶Xe) ≥ 1.07×10²⁶ y and T_1/2(⁷⁶Ge) ≥ 5.3×10²⁵ y at 90% CL
- The planned nEXO experiment has a 3σ discovery sensitivity of T_1/2(¹³⁶Xe) = 4.1×10²⁷ y
- The effective Majorana neutrino mass limit from current experiments is m_ν ≲ 0.06 - 0.17 eV, with future experiments probing m_ν ≈ 0.02 eV

## Context

This work extends the effective operator approach to neutrinoless double beta decay previously developed by Pas et al. (1999, 2000) and del Aguila et al. (2011, 2012), providing the first complete derivation of nuclear matrix elements and phase space factors specifically for short-range dimension-9 operators. The results complement earlier calculations for long-range mechanisms by Doi et al. (1981, 1983) and Tomoda (1990), and for dimension-6 operators by Ali et al. (2007). The framework enables experimental searches to constrain individual short-range mechanisms and distinguish them from the standard light neutrino mass mechanism through measurements of the electron angular correlation.
