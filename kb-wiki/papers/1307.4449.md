# Polynomial Supersymmetry for Matrix Hamiltonians

> We study intertwining relations for matrix one-dimensional, in general, non-Hermitian Hamiltonians by matrix differential operators of arbitrary order. It is established that for any matrix intertwining operator Q_N^- of minimal order N there is a matrix operator Q_{N'}^+ of different, in general, order N' that intertwines the same Hamiltonians as Q_N^- in the opposite direction and such that the products Q_{N'}^+Q_N^- and Q_N^-Q_{N'}^+ are identical polynomials of the corresponding Hamiltonians. The related polynomial algebra of supersymmetry is constructed. The problems of minimization and of reducibility of a matrix intertwining operator are considered and the criteria of minimizability and of reducibility are presented. It is shown that there are absolutely irreducible matrix intertwining operators, in contrast to the scalar case.

# Polynomial Supersymmetry for Matrix Hamiltonians

## Key claim

For any matrix intertwining operator \(Q_N^-\) of minimal order \(N\) that links two matrix Schrödinger Hamiltonians \(H_+\) and \(H_-\), there exists a "conjugate" matrix operator \(Q_{N'}^+\) of generally different order \(N'\) that intertwines the same Hamiltonians in the opposite direction, and the products \(Q_{N'}^+Q_N^-\) and \(Q_N^-Q_{N'}^+\) are identical polynomials of the respective Hamiltonians, but this construction requires that the Wronskian of the transformation vector-functions does not vanish on the real axis, a condition that was missing in prior work by Govorkov (1998) and that invalidates that author's existence theorem.

## Method

The paper constructs matrix \(n \times n\) differential intertwining operators \(Q_N^-\) of arbitrary order \(N\) with nondegenerate constant matrix coefficient at \(\partial^N\) using a canonical basis of formal vector-eigenfunctions and formal associated vector-functions of the initial Hamiltonian \(H_+\). The operator is expressed via a determinant formula involving the Wronskian of these vector-functions, and the potential of the final Hamiltonian \(H_-\) is obtained from the coefficient \(X_{N-1}^-(x)\). Criteria for minimizability (removal of polynomial factors in \(H_+\)) and for reducibility (factorization into lower-order intertwining operators) are derived from the Jordan structure of the matrix \(T^+\) that represents \(H_+\) on the kernel of \(Q_N^-\).

## Key numbers

- The order \(N'\) of the conjugate operator \(Q_{N'}^+\) is given by \(N' = 2(\varkappa_1 + \ldots + \varkappa_L) - N\), where \(\varkappa_l\) is the maximal Jordan block order for eigenvalue \(\lambda_l\) of \(T^+\) and \(L\) is the number of distinct eigenvalues.
- The polynomial supersymmetry algebra satisfies \(\{ \mathbf{Q}, \mathbf{\bar Q} \} = \mathcal{P}_{(N+N')/2}(\mathbf{H})\), where \(\mathcal{P}_{(N+N')/2}\) is a polynomial of degree \((N+N')/2\).
- A minimizable operator can be factored as \(Q_N^- = P_M^- \prod_{l=1}^s (\lambda_l I_n - H_+)^{k_l}\) with \(M = N - 2\sum_{l=1}^s k_l\), where \(k_l\) is the minimal Jordan block order for eigenvalue \(\lambda_l\).
- The example of an absolutely irreducible matrix \(2 \times 2\) intertwining operator of order \(N=2\) is constructed from two scalar Hamiltonians \(h_1\) and \(h_2\) sharing a spectral value \(\lambda_0\), using four vector-functions whose Wronskian equals \(-W_1(x)W_2(x)\) (product of two non-vanishing scalar Wronskians).

## Context

This work extends the theory of Darboux transformations and supersymmetry for matrix Hamiltonians beyond the first-order and second-order cases treated by Andrianov et al. (1997) and others. It corrects a flawed existence theorem in Govorkov (1998) by adding the necessary Wronskian non-vanishing condition, and provides explicit determinant formulas (simpler than the quasideterminant approach of Govorkov 1998) that generalize the partial results of Samsonov and Pegas (2003). The paper also contrasts with Tanaka (2011), which required both intertwining operators to have the same order and used an unnatural conjugation operation. The criteria for minimizability and reducibility enable systematic simplification of intertwining operators, while the discovery of absolutely irreducible matrix operators (impossible in the scalar case per Andrianov and Sokolov 2003) opens new questions for classification and spectral design in multi-channel quantum systems.
