# Possible duality violations in tau decay and their impact on the determination of alpha_s

> We discuss the issue of duality violations in hadronic tau decay. After introducing a physically motivated ansatz for duality violations, we estimate their possible size by fitting this ansatz to the tau experimental data provided by the ALEPH collaboration. Our conclusion is that these data do not exclude significant duality violations in tau decay. This may imply an additional systematic error in the value of alpha_s(m_tau), extracted from tau decay, as large as δalpha_s(m_tau) \sim 0.003-0.010 .

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to previous claims by Davier et al., the ALEPH tau decay data do not exclude significant duality violations (DVs), and that these could introduce an additional systematic error in the determination of \(\alpha_s(m_\tau)\) as large as \(\delta\alpha_s(m_\tau) \sim 0.003-0.010\), depending on the fit parameters.

## Method

The authors introduce a physically motivated ansatz for the duality-violating part of the vector and axial-vector spectral functions, parametrized as an exponentially damped oscillation: \(\frac{1}{\pi}\mathrm{Im}\Delta_{V,A}(s)= \kappa_{V,A} e^{-\gamma_{V,A} s}\sin(\alpha_{V,A}+\beta_{V,A} s)\). They fit this ansatz, together with fixed-order perturbation theory up to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^5)\), to the ALEPH spectral data in the window \(1.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \leq s \leq m_\tau^2\), using the full covariance matrix provided by ALEPH. The fitted DV parameters are then used to compute the contribution to \(R_\tau^{V,A}\) via a contour integral, and the resulting shift in \(\alpha_s\) is estimated by comparing the perturbative expression for \(R_\tau^{V+A}\) with the phenomenological value.

## Key numbers

*   Vector channel fit parameters: \(\kappa_V = 0.018 \pm 0.004\), \(\gamma_V = 0.15 \pm 0.15\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\beta_V = 2.0 \pm 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 10/79 \simeq 0.13\).
*   Axial-vector channel fit parameters: \(\kappa_A = 0.20 \pm 0.06\), \(\gamma_A = 1.7 \pm 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\beta_A = -3.0 \pm 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 17/78 \simeq 0.22\).
*   DV contribution to \(R_\tau\): \(\delta R_\tau^V = (-0.022 \pm 0.013)\ S_{EW}|V_{ud}|^2\) and \(\delta R_\tau^A = (0.0003 \pm 0.0003)\ S_{EW}|V_{ud}|^2\), corresponding to \(\delta R_\tau^V/R_\tau^V \simeq -(1.5 \pm 0.9)\%\) and \(\delta R_\tau^A/R_\tau^A \simeq (0.02 \pm 0.02)\%\).
*   Estimated shift in \(\alpha_s(m_\tau)\) from DVs: \(\delta\alpha_s(m_\tau) \sim 0.003-0.010\).
*   For comparison, the estimate of Davier et al. for the V+A channel gave \(\delta R_\tau^{V+A} \simeq 0.0021\ S_{EW}|V_{ud}|^2\), while the authors find a value ten times larger.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of duality violations in tau decay beyond the combined V+A analysis of Davier et al., which concluded DVs were negligible. By fitting the vector and axial-vector channels separately, the authors find that DVs may be significantly larger than previously estimated, particularly in the vector channel. The results suggest that the systematic error from DVs should be considered in future extractions of \(\alpha_s\) from tau decay, and the authors note that a full analysis of moments with pinched weights in the presence of DVs, along the lines of their iterative method in Cata, Golterman, and Peris (2005), would be required for a more precise determination.
