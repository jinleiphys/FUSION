# Local suppression and enhancement of pairing condensate under rotation

> The rotation induced inhomogeneous problem is non-trivial and inevitable. In this paper a generic framework is developed to investigate the inhomogeneous condensate in a system of fermions under the presence of rotation. It is a self-consistent method basing on a set of relativistic BdG equations solved with typical iteration algorithm. Taking the chiral condensate for example we study rotational effects numerically and discover two inhomogeneous effects, the local rotational suppression effect and centrifugal effect. They may have significant impacts on the phase structure of various kinds of matter. Several systems in different physics branches have been discussed in the paper.

## Key claim

The paper develops a self-consistent relativistic framework to study inhomogeneous fermion pairing under rotation, and discovers two distinct effects, local rotational suppression and centrifugal enhancement, that go beyond the local density approximation (LDA) used in prior work. The key qualifier is that these effects are demonstrated for a 2+1D system with a specific choice of rotation profile (gaussian-like or Woods-Saxon), and the results depend on the radial derivative of the angular velocity, not just its magnitude.

## Method

The authors adopt the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in 2+1D, introduce rotation via curved metrics expanded to linear order in velocity, and solve the resulting Dirac equation self-consistently using a relativistic Bogoliubov–de Gennes (BdG) iteration algorithm. The eigenstates are expanded in Bessel functions of the first kind on a cylindrical domain of radius R = 30 M₀⁻¹, and the chiral condensate σ(r) = -G⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ is computed as a function of radial coordinate r for different rotation profiles ω(r). The coupling is renormalized as G(Λ) = π/(Λ - M₀), with M₀ the vacuum condensate value.

## Key numbers

- System radius: R = 30 M₀⁻¹, chosen to mimic an infinite system (the condensate is nearly uniform at ω = 0 for R > 10 M₀⁻¹).
- For the gaussian-like rotation profile, the peak angular velocity is fixed at ω₀ = 0.25 M₀, large enough to produce significant suppression.
- For the Woods-Saxon rotation profile, the saturated angular velocity is ω = 0.06 M₀, with a plateau covering 10 M₀⁻¹ from the center.
- The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration is T ≈ 2 M₀, consistent with Appelquist (1986).
- Convergence criterion for the BdG iteration: Manhattan norm tolerance ε = 10⁻², typically achieved in dozens of steps.
- The typical correlation length of the system is roughly 1 M₀⁻¹, comparable to the width of the rotation peak.

## Context

This work extends prior LDA-based studies of rotating fermion systems (Jiang 2016, Ebihara 2016, Chen 2017) which treated the condensate as locally constant and found that uniform rotation in a finite system with sharp boundary conditions has no effect at zero temperature. The self-consistent framework reveals that inhomogeneous rotation profiles produce local suppression (from the rotational polarization effect) and centrifugal enhancement (driven by |∂ω/∂r|), effects that cannot be captured by LDA. The results are relevant for heavy-ion collisions, where local vorticity can reach 0.1–0.2 GeV (Jiang 2016), and for neutron star physics, where the centrifugal effect may contribute to pulsar glitch behavior (Link 1992, Melatos 2007). The framework is also applicable to cold atom systems (Bausmerth 2008) and can be extended to study vortex states and other pairing types.
