# A highly resistive layer within the crust of X-ray pulsars limits their spin periods

> The lack of X-ray pulsars with spin periods > 12 s raises the question of where the population of evolved high magnetic field neutron stars has gone. Unlike canonical radio pulsars, X-ray pulsars are not subject to physical limits to the emission mechanism nor observational biases against the detection of sources with longer periods. Here we show that a highly resistive layer in the innermost part of the crust of neutron stars naturally limits the spin period to a maximum value of about 10-20 s. This highly resistive layer is expected if the inner crust is amorphous and heterogeneous in nuclear charge, possibly due to the existence of a nuclear pasta phase. Our findings suggest that the maximum period of isolated X-ray pulsars can be the first observational evidence of an amorphous inner crust, which properties can be further constrained by future X-ray timing missions combined with more detailed models.

# A highly resistive layer within the crust of X-ray pulsars limits their spin periods

## Key claim
The observed maximum spin period of isolated X-ray pulsars (about 10-20 s) is a natural consequence of a highly resistive layer in the innermost crust of neutron stars, provided that the inner crust is amorphous and heterogeneous in nuclear charge, possibly due to the existence of a nuclear pasta phase. The claim is conditional on the inner crust being disordered rather than crystalline, and the authors note that alternative models with low impurity in the inner crust would predict longer periods (20-100 s) that are not observed.

## Method
The authors performed 2D magneto-rotational evolution simulations of neutron stars over 10^6 years using a code developed in Viganò (2012), adopting an impurity parameter formalism to model electrical resistivity in the crust. They considered six models (A through E and J) with different impurity parameter profiles in the inner crust, particularly in the pasta region above 10^13 g/cm^3, and integrated the spin-down equation using the time-dependent magnetic field from the simulations to produce evolutionary tracks in the P-Ṗ diagram.

## Key numbers
- The maximum spin period of isolated X-ray pulsars is about 10-20 s, consistent with the observed lack of sources with periods > 12 s.
- For models with high impurity parameter (Q_imp) in the pasta region, the magnetic field dissipates by one or even two orders of magnitude between 0.1 and 1 Myr.
- The initial dipolar magnetic field used in simulations was B = 3 × 10^14 G at the pole.
- In models D and E, the luminosity at an age of a few Myrs is ≈ 10^32 erg/s, high enough for sources a few kpc away to be detectable.
- The inner crust is about 1 km thick (10% of the star radius) and contains 1% of the neutron star mass.
- The pasta region is defined at densities ρ > 10^13 g/cm^3.
- The evolution was followed for 10^6 years.
- During the first ~ 30000 years, the field is dissipated by a factor ~ 2 for all models.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing question raised by Psa (2002) about the missing population of evolved high magnetic field neutron stars with long periods, and provides a quantitative physical mechanism to explain the observed upper limit. It builds on prior work on magnetic field evolution in neutron star crusts (Pons 2007, Viganò 2012) and on the nuclear pasta phase (Rav 1983, Hor 2005, Hor 2008), as well as studies of amorphous inner crusts (Jon 2004, Jon 2004b). The results enable future X-ray timing missions such as LOFT (Large Observatory For X-ray Timing) combined with population synthesis studies to constrain the transport properties of the inner crust and, consequently, the equation of state of dense matter.
