# Holographic Thermalization

> Using the AdS/CFT correspondence, we probe the scale-dependence of thermalization in strongly coupled field theories following a quench, via calculations of two-point functions, Wilson loops and entanglement entropy in d=2,3,4. In the saddlepoint approximation these probes are computed in AdS space in terms of invariant geometric objects - geodesics, minimal surfaces and minimal volumes. Our calculations for two-dimensional field theories are analytical. In our strongly coupled setting, all probes in all dimensions share certain universal features in their thermalization: (1) a slight delay in the onset of thermalization, (2) an apparent non-analyticity at the endpoint of thermalization, (3) top-down thermalization where the UV thermalizes first. For homogeneous initial conditions the entanglement entropy thermalizes slowest, and sets a timescale for equilibration that saturates a causality bound over the range of scales studied. The growth rate of entanglement entropy density is nearly volume-independent for small volumes, but slows for larger volumes.

## Key claim

Using the AdS/CFT correspondence, the authors probe the scale-dependence of thermalization in strongly coupled field theories following a quench, finding that for homogeneous initial conditions the entanglement entropy thermalizes slowest and sets a timescale for equilibration that saturates a causality bound over the range of scales studied, with the caveat that these results apply specifically to translationally invariant plasmas modeled by an infalling thin mass shell in the Vaidya metric.

## Method

The authors model thermalization via gravitational collapse in asymptotically Anti-de Sitter (AdS) spacetimes of dimensions d=2,3,4, using the Vaidya metric with a mass function m(v)=M/2*(1+tanh(v/v0)) to describe an infalling shell of tensionless null dust. They compute three nonlocal probes of thermalization in the dual field theory: equal-time two-point functions via the geodesic approximation, Wilson loop expectation values via minimal surfaces, and entanglement entropy via minimal volumes, all evaluated in the saddlepoint approximation in the time-dependent Vaidya background.

## Key numbers

- For two-dimensional field theories (AdS3 dual), the thermal contribution to the equal-time Wightman function is proportional to the Bose distribution: G_sub(k,T) = -i/(2pi) * k/(e^{k/T}-1)
- The Hawking temperature of the black brane formed after collapse is T = d*M^{1/d}/(4pi)
- The shell thickness is parametrized by v0, with the zero thickness limit v0 -> 0 studied analytically for AdS3
- The entanglement entropy growth rate is nearly volume-independent for small volumes but slows for larger volumes

## Context

This work extends the study of holographic thermalization beyond the near-equilibrium hydrodynamic regime (Policastro 2001, Janik 2005) and beyond local observables, which are only sensitive to phenomena near the AdS boundary. It builds on earlier studies of gravitational collapse in AdS (Chesler 2008, Bhattacharyya 2009) and thin shell models (Lin 2008), while also complementing studies of quasi-normal modes and small perturbations around equilibrium. The results enable comparison of thermalization dynamics across different dimensions and provide a framework for understanding the "top-down" thermalization mechanism in strongly coupled theories, in contrast to the perturbative "bottom-up" paradigm (Baier 2000).
