# J/$ψ$ measurements in the STAR experiment

> In this paper, we present recent STAR J/$ψ$ results. J/$ψ$ nuclear modification factors ($R_{AA}$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 200, 62.4 and 39 GeV and in U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 193 GeV are measured and compared to different theoretical calculations. We also report J/$ψ$ elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) results in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 200 GeV and the first $ψ(2S)$ to J/$ψ$ ratio measurement in $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} =$ 500 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper reports that J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions shows no strong dependence on collision energy between 39 and 200 GeV, but this conclusion is qualified by the use of Color Evaporation Model calculations as baselines for the 62.4 and 39 GeV data, which introduce large uncertainties due to the lack of precise p+p measurements at those energies.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured J/ψ production via the dielectron decay channel (B_ee = 5.9%) at mid-rapidity (|y| < 1) in Au+Au collisions at three energies and U+U collisions at one energy. Nuclear modification factors (R_AA) were extracted as a function of the number of participant nucleons (N_part) for different transverse momentum ranges, and J/ψ elliptic flow (v_2) was measured in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. The ψ(2S) to J/ψ ratio was measured in p+p collisions at 500 GeV for the first time.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ R_AA in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200, 62.4, and 39 GeV, and in U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV, all at mid-rapidity
- J/ψ v_2 in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for 0-80% central events, consistent with zero for p_T > 2 GeV/c
- First ψ(2S) to J/ψ ratio measurement in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 500 GeV, consistent with no collision energy dependence within current precision
- Low-p_T J/ψ R_AA (p_T < 5 GeV/c) and high-p_T J/ψ R_AA (p_T > 5 GeV/c) measured separately in Au+Au at 200 GeV, with high-p_T R_AA systematically higher than low-p_T R_AA
- Strong suppression of high-p_T J/ψ observed in central Au+Au collisions (0-30%) at 200 GeV
- U+U collisions at 193 GeV can reach up to 20% higher energy density compared to Au+Au in the same centrality bin

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions across multiple collision energies and systems, building on prior measurements by the STAR Collaboration (Adamczyk 2012, Adamczyk 2013) and other experiments. The results are compared to theoretical models by Zhao and Rapp (2010) and Liu et al. (2009), which include both direct J/ψ production with color screening and recombination of c and anti-c quarks. The measurements enable further constraints on the interplay between color screening, recombination, and cold nuclear matter effects in quarkonium production, and the ψ(2S) to J/ψ ratio in p+p collisions provides new input for understanding charmonium production mechanisms and feed-down contributions.
