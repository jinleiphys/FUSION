# Microscopically based energy density functionals for nuclei using the density matrix expansion

> While ab initio many-body techniques have been able to successfully describe the properties of light and intermediate mass nuclei based on chiral effective field theory interactions, neutron-rich nuclei still remain out of reach for these methods. Conversely, energy density functional approaches can be used to calculate properties of heavy nuclei but rely mostly on phenomenological interactions. A usable form of the nuclear energy density functional that is rooted in the modern theory of nuclear forces was presented recently. The first component of this new set of functionals corresponds to the direct part (Hartree term) of the expectation value of local chiral potentials on a Slater determinant. The exchange term, which is a functional of the non-local density, is transformed into a local functional by applying the density matrix expansion. In order to reduce the computational cost due to the direct implementation of non-separable, local interactions in the Hartree term, we use an approximation to represent the regularized Yukawa functions in terms of a sum of (separable) Gaussian functions. These proceedings analyze the accuracy of such an approximation in terms of the number of Gaussian functions and look for an optimal value that gives an acceptable level of accuracy while maintaining the computational memory requirements in a many-body calculation as low as possible.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that representing the regularized Yukawa functions of chiral potentials with a sum of five Gaussian functions provides an optimal compromise between numerical accuracy and computational memory requirements for implementing microscopically constrained energy density functionals, with negligible improvement in accuracy beyond five Gaussians.

## Method

The authors approximated the central terms of local chiral potentials at NLO and N2LO (with and without Δ excitations) as a sum of Gaussian functions, imposing a constraint that the sum vanishes at r=0 to match the regulator behavior. They adjusted the strength and range parameters for approximations using 3 to 8 Gaussian functions, then computed the root mean square deviation between the chiral potential and the Gaussian expansion on a fine radial grid from 0 to 10 fm with 0.01 fm spacing.

## Key numbers

- For the V_C (N2LO) central term, the root mean square deviation decreases from 0.0229 MeV with 3 Gaussians to 0.0013 MeV with 5 Gaussians, then remains at 0.0010 MeV for 7 and 8 Gaussians
- For the W_C (NLO) central isospin term, the root mean square deviation decreases from 0.0828 MeV with 3 Gaussians to 0.0039 MeV with 5 Gaussians, then to 0.0012 MeV with 8 Gaussians
- The regulator used in the chiral potentials has parameters R_c = 1.0 fm and n = 6
- The optimal number of Gaussian functions identified is N = 5

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of microscopically constrained energy density functionals (Navarro Perez 2018) that combine chiral effective field theory potentials with the density matrix expansion to create semi-phenomenological functionals. The approach enables many-body calculations of heavy neutron-rich systems relevant to the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams and r-process nucleosynthesis, building on earlier studies by Stoitsov (2010) that first proposed determining EDFs from microscopic potentials. The Gaussian expansion technique allows these functionals to be implemented in existing Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov codes like HFBTHOv3 that already handle Gogny-type interactions.
