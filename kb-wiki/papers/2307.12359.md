# Collins-Soper kernel from lattice QCD at the physical pion mass

> This work presents a determination of the quark Collins-Soper kernel, which relates transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) at different rapidity scales, using lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). This is the first lattice QCD calculation of the kernel at quark masses corresponding to a close-to-physical value of the pion mass, with next-to-next-leading logarithmic matching to TMDs from the corresponding lattice-calculable distributions, and includes a complete analysis of systematic uncertainties arising from operator mixing. The kernel is extracted at transverse momentum scales $240\,\text{MeV}\lesssim q_{T}\lesssim 1.6\,\text{GeV}$ with a precision sufficient to begin to discriminate between different phenomenological models in the non-perturbative region.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD determination of the quark Collins-Soper kernel at a close-to-physical pion mass (148.8 MeV), with next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic matching and a complete analysis of systematic uncertainties from operator mixing, achieving sufficient precision to begin discriminating between phenomenological models in the non-perturbative region.

## Method

The Collins-Soper kernel was extracted from ratios of quasi-TMD wavefunction matrix elements computed on a lattice ensemble with 2+1+1 dynamical quark flavors at a pion mass of 148.8 MeV, using next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) perturbative matching within the LaMET framework. Bare quasi-TMD wavefunctions were computed from pion two-point correlation functions with staple-shaped Wilson line operators at multiple hadron momenta (0.86, 1.29, 1.72, and 2.15 GeV), and renormalization was performed using the RI/xMOM scheme with full 16x16 mixing matrices quantified for the first time. The kernel was obtained by forming ratios of Fourier-transformed quasi-TMD wavefunctions at different boost momenta, with perturbative matching corrections applied using both fixed-order and resummed matching coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 148.8 ± 0.1 MeV, corresponding to a close-to-physical value
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.12 fm, with volume V = (48a)^3 × 64a
- Transverse momentum range: 240 MeV ≲ q_T ≲ 1.6 GeV
- Hadron momenta used: P^z = 0.86, 1.29, 1.72, and 2.15 GeV (corresponding to n^z = 4, 6, 8, 10)
- Number of configurations: 469 (n^z=4), 472 (n^z=6), 523 (n^z=8), 481 (n^z=10)
- Transverse displacements computed: b_T/a ∈ {0, ..., 7}
- Hopping parameter: κ = 0.12547, clover coefficient: c_sw = 1.0

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of the Collins-Soper kernel by Shanahan et al. (2019-2021) and the Lattice Parton Collaboration (2020-2022) by using a physical pion mass rather than heavier quark masses, thereby reducing systematic uncertainties from chiral extrapolation and power corrections proportional to m_pi^2/(xP^z)^2. The NNLO/NNLL matching accuracy improves upon previous NLO calculations, and the full quantification of operator mixing effects addresses a previously unquantified systematic uncertainty. The results enable direct comparison with phenomenological parameterizations from Bacchetta et al. (2022) and Moos et al. (2023), and provide first-principles constraints that will be valuable for interpreting data from the Electron-Ion Collider and for precision electroweak measurements such as the W-boson mass.
