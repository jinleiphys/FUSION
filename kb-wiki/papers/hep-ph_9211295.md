# Nuclear Matter Aspects of Skyrmions

> As an alternative approach to the infinite-array description of dense matter in the Skyrme model, we report about the properties of a single skyrmion on a compact 3-sphere of finite radius. The density of this matter can be increased by decreasing the hypersphere radius. As in the array calculations one encounters a transition to a distinct high density phase characterized by a delocalization in energy and baryon charge and by increased symmetries. We will argue that the high density phase has to be interpreted as chirally restored one. The arguments are based on the formation of complete chiral multiplets and the vanishing of the pionic massless Goldstone modes in the high-density fluctuation spectrum. We show that the restoration of chiral symmetry is common to any chirally invariant extension of the usual Skyrme model - whether via higher-order contact terms or via the introduction of stabilizing vector mesons which act over a finite range. (Talk at the workshop on ``Baryons as Skyrme Solitons'', Siegen, Germany, Sept. 27-30, 1992)

## Key claim

The paper argues that the high-density phase of skyrmion matter on a compact 3-sphere, characterized by delocalization of energy and baryon charge, should be interpreted as a chirally restored phase, and that this restoration is common to any chirally invariant extension of the Skyrme model, whether via higher-order contact terms or vector meson stabilization.

## Method

The author studies a single skyrmion (baryon number B=1) placed on a 3-sphere of finite radius L, using the hedgehog parameterization and the strain tensor formalism of Manton. The density of the system is increased by decreasing the hypersphere radius L, and the static energy is minimized as a function of the radial profile function f(μ) subject to the baryon number constraint. The stability of the identity map (uniform solution f(μ)=μ) against small-amplitude perturbations is analyzed analytically, and the bifurcation to a localized solution for L > √2 is identified.

## Key numbers

* The critical hypersphere radius for the transition is L_c = √2 (dimensionless units), corresponding to a second-order phase transition of the standard pitchfork type.
* The topological lower bound on the energy for any skyrmion configuration is E ≥ 12π² B.
* For the identity map (uniform solution) on the 3-sphere, the energy is E_identity = 6π² (L + 1/L).
* Using empirical values f_π = 93 MeV and ε_4 = 0.0743, the critical baryon density is ρ_c ≈ 0.20 fm⁻³, compared to nuclear matter density ρ_nm = 0.16 fm⁻³.
* The critical density from periodic array calculations under the same input parameters is approximately ρ_c = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the periodic array approach pioneered by Klebanov (1990) and the hypersphere formalism introduced by Manton (1987) and Ruback (1987), providing an analytically tractable alternative to the numerical lattice calculations of Wuest, Brown, and Jackson (1989). The results enable a rigorous understanding of the high-density phase transition in Skyrme-type models, showing that the transition to a chirally restored phase is not a lattice artifact but a generic feature, and that the critical densities from the hypersphere and periodic array calculations are quantitatively similar. The paper also generalizes the analysis to variants of the Skyrme model with higher-order terms or vector meson stabilization.
