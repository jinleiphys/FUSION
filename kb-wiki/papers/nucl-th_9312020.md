# Chaos Driven Decay of Nuclear Giant Resonances: Route to Quantum Self-Organization

> The influence of background states with increasing level of complexity on the strength distribution of the isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole resonance in $^{40}$Ca is studied. It is found that the background characteristics, typical for chaotic systems, strongly affects the fluctuation properties of the strength distribution. In particular, the small components of the wave function obey a scaling law analogous to self-organized systems at the critical state. This appears to be consistent with the Porter-Thomas distribution of the transition strength.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the chaotic nature of the background 2p-2h states in $^{40}$Ca drives the strength distribution of the giant quadrupole resonance toward a scaling law $N \sim S_{th}^{\alpha}$ with $\alpha \approx 0.50$, analogous to self-organized critical systems, but this scaling is only observed when the full residual interaction is used (yielding GOE fluctuations) and is more pronounced for the isovector resonance than for the isoscalar one.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a nuclear Hamiltonian in a truncated Hilbert space of 1p-1h and 2p-2h states for $^{40}$Ca, using a mean field and residual interaction from prior work (SW), including two major shells above and below the Fermi level. They computed the quadrupole response by solving the Schrödinger equation in a basis of prediagonalized 1p-1h and 2p-2h subspaces, considering three cases for the 2p-2h subspace: (1) no residual interaction, (2) only particle-particle and hole-hole two-body matrix elements (Poissonian level statistics), and (3) the full residual interaction (GOE level statistics). The transition strength distribution $S_F(E)$ was then analyzed for its fluctuation properties and scaling behavior.

## Key numbers

- The calculation involved more than 11,000 states total, of which 26 were 1p-1h states and 3014 were $2^+$ states from the 2p-2h subspace (including those up to 50 MeV excitation energy).
- Mean excitation energy $\langle E \rangle$ for the isovector quadrupole resonance was 30.84 MeV, and for the isoscalar resonance 24.45 MeV, independent of mixing with 2p-2h states.
- Energy dispersion $\sigma$ for the 1p-1h-only case was 3.41 MeV (isovector) and 4.28 MeV (isoscalar); after coupling to 2p-2h states, these increased to 5.40 MeV and 5.42 MeV, respectively.
- The scaling exponent $\alpha$ for the cumulative number of transitions $N \sim S_{th}^{\alpha}$ was approximately 0.50 for the isovector resonance and approximately 0.48 for the isoscalar resonance in the chaotic (full residual interaction) case.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of level statistics in the 2p-2h subspace by Drozdz et al. (DNSW) and builds on the random matrix theory framework of Bohigas, Flusser, and French (BFF) for chaotic nuclear dynamics. It also connects to the concept of self-organized criticality introduced by Bak, Tang, and Wiesenfeld (BTW) and to the Porter-Thomas distribution of transition strengths (PT). The results enable a quantitative understanding of how collectivity and chaos coexist in nuclear giant resonances, and suggest that the strength distribution of the decaying resonance can be interpreted as an attractor analogous to '1/f' noise in classical chaotic systems.
