# Calculating extremely high energy bremsstrahlung in matter

> Ultra-relativistic electrons initiate electromagnetic showers in ordinary matter that evolve through bremsstrahlung and pair production. At very high energy, the quantum mechanical duration of bremsstrahlung becomes longer than the mean free time to elastically scatter from the medium, leading to a significant suppression known at the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. For some ranges of bremsstrahlung photon and initial electron energies $(k_γ,E)$, the duration becomes so long that it will also overlap with subsequent pair production by the bremsstrahlung photon, disrupting LPM suppression and drastically changing LPM predictions. We have previously calculated this change for extremely high energies ($k_γ\gg 2$ TeV or more, depending on the medium), for which the electron mass and medium-induced photon mass could be ignored. In this paper, we extend that analysis to lower (but still ultra-relativistic) energy by accounting for those masses, leading to a rich map of behavior in different regions of $(k_γ,E)$.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous calculations of the LPMplus effect (where overlapping pair production disrupts LPM suppression of bremsstrahlung) from extremely high photon energies where electron mass and medium-induced photon mass could be ignored, down to lower but still ultra-relativistic energies by including those masses, revealing a rich map of behavior in different regions of photon energy and initial electron energy.

## Method

The authors use Zakharov's formalism, representing bremsstrahlung as three high-energy particles (electron, photon, positron) propagating forward in time with a two-dimensional Schrodinger-like effective Hamiltonian that includes the electron mass and medium-induced photon mass. They compute the bremsstrahlung rate by evaluating the propagator of this effective harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian, subtracting the vacuum rate to handle ultraviolet divergences, and then generalizing the calculation to include overlapping pair production by the bremsstrahlung photon.

## Key numbers

- The medium-dependent energy scale Elpma is approximately 2.5 TeV for Gold and 234 PeV for air
- The Bethe-Heitler bremsstrahlung rate approximation is given by (dGamma/dx_gamma)_BH approximately equals (alpha qhat)/(6 pi m^2) times [2 P_{e to gamma}(x_gamma) + x_gamma]
- The deep LPM bremsstrahlung rate is (dGamma/dx_gamma)_LPM approximately equals (alpha/(2 pi)) P_{e to gamma}(x_gamma) sqrt(x_gamma qhat / ((1-x_gamma)E))
- The dielectric region bremsstrahlung rate is (dGamma/dx_gamma)_LPM approximately equals (alpha qhat)/(3 pi m_gamma^2) times x_gamma^2 P_{e to gamma}(x_gamma)
- The medium-induced photon mass m_gamma is approximately sqrt(4 pi alpha Z n / m) for a medium of atomic number Z and density n

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Arnold et al., 2025) of the LPMplus effect at extremely high energies (k_gamma much greater than 2 TeV) to lower energies by including electron mass and medium-induced photon mass, which were previously ignored. The paper reproduces Migdal's (1956) original LPM results including masses as a warm-up, then generalizes to the LPMplus case, and warns about applicability at extreme energies k_gamma greater than approximately 10^20 eV where photonuclear interactions (gamma A to hadrons) may become relevant.
