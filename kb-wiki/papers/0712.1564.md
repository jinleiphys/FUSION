# Temperature Relaxation in Hot Dense Hydrogen

> Temperature equilibration of hydrogen is studied for conditions relevant to inertial confinement fusion. New molecular-dynamics simulations and results from quantum many-body theory are compared with Landau-Spitzer (LS) predictions for temperatures T from 50 eV to 5000 eV, and densities with Wigner-Seitz radii r_s = 1.0 and 0.5. The relaxation is slower than the LS result, even for temperatures in the keV range, but converges to agreement in the high-T limit.

# Temperature Relaxation in Hot Dense Hydrogen

## Key claim

Temperature relaxation in hot dense hydrogen is slower than the Landau-Spitzer (LS) prediction for temperatures from 50 eV to 5000 eV at densities corresponding to Wigner-Seitz radii r_s = 1.0 and 0.5, even in the kilovolt range, though the LS result is recovered in the high-temperature limit where T/E_F approaches 20.

## Method

Molecular dynamics simulations with quantum-corrected effective potentials (diffraction-corrected Coulomb potentials and Pauli exclusion potentials) were performed for electrons and protons at the physical mass ratio 1:1836, using velocity-Verlet integration with an O(N^{3/2}) Ewald method and N=500 particles. Separately, quantum many-body calculations using the Fermi golden rule (FGR) and a coupled-mode (CM) approach were carried out, where the relaxation rate is expressed in terms of the spectral functions of the electron and proton dynamic response functions. The LS result was computed using the Hansen-McDonald prescription for the momentum cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 50 eV to 5000 eV, corresponding to degeneracy parameters 0.25 < theta < 20
- Two densities: r_s = 0.5 (n_e = 1.5 x 10^25 cm^{-3}) and r_s = 1.0 (n_e = 1.61 x 10^24 cm^{-3})
- MD simulations used N = 500 particles with time steps as small as 0.005 omega_{pe}^{-1}
- The factor f_{ep} used in the diffraction-corrected electron-proton potential is within 5% of unity for all conditions studied
- The relaxation time tau is reported in units of the inverse electron plasma frequency for both densities across the full temperature range

## Context

This work extends earlier molecular dynamics studies by Hansen and McDonald (1976), who concluded that many-body effects are negligible and the LS result holds, and quantum many-body calculations by Dharma-wardana et al. (2001) and Hazak et al. (2001) that predicted slower relaxation. The results contradict the faster relaxation predicted by Gould and DeWitt (1967) and show that LS is inadequate for compressed, partially degenerate plasmas. The findings are directly relevant to inertial confinement fusion targets composed of dense cryogenic fuel rapidly laser-compressed to keV temperatures, and enable improved modeling of burning plasmas where electron-ion nonequilibrium may be more significant than previously expected.
