# Electromagnetic radiation from nuclear collisions at RHIC energies

> The hot and dense strongly interacting matter created in collisions of heavy nuclei at RHIC energies is modeled with relativistic hydrodynamics, and the spectra of real and virtual photons produced at mid-rapidity in these events are calculated. Several different sources are considered, and their relative importance is compared. Specifically, we include jet fragmentation, jet-plasma interactions, the emission of radiation from the thermal medium and from primordial hard collisions. Our calculations consistently take into account jet energy loss, as evaluated in the AMY formalism. We obtain results for the spectra, the nuclear modification factor (R_AA), and the azimuthal anisotropy (v_2) that agree with the photon measurements performed by the PHENIX collaboration at RHIC.

# Electromagnetic radiation from nuclear collisions at RHIC energies

## Key claim

The authors model the hot and dense matter created in RHIC heavy-ion collisions using relativistic hydrodynamics and calculate real and virtual photon spectra, finding agreement with PHENIX measurements for spectra, nuclear modification factor (R_AA), and azimuthal anisotropy (v_2), but only within the large experimental error bars, and with the caveat that the jet-plasma contribution is essential to reproduce the data in the 2–4 GeV transverse momentum window.

## Method

The authors use a (2+1)-dimensional longitudinally boost-invariant hydrodynamic code (AZHYDRO) with EOS Q (matching a non-interacting QGP above T_c = 164 MeV to a chemically equilibrated hadron resonance gas below T_c) to model the medium evolution. They calculate photon production from five sources: prompt (hard primordial) photons, jet fragmentation, jet-plasma interactions (non-collinear and collinear processes with LPM effects using the AMY formalism), thermal QGP radiation, and hadron gas radiation. Jet energy loss is consistently included via the AMY transition rates, and the initial jet distributions are computed from pQCD with isospin and shadowing effects.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 164 MeV used for the QGP-to-hadron gas transition
- Initial hydrodynamic start time τ_i = 0.2 fm/c
- Decoupling temperature T_dec ≈ 130 MeV
- NLO K-factor K = 1.7 used for jet production cross sections (nearly q_T-independent)
- Factorization scale Q = p_T/√2 used for prompt photon contributions (fitted to PHENIX p-p data)
- Jet-plasma photons dominate the yield in the window 2 < p_T < 4 GeV
- Neglecting jet-plasma photons causes a ~30% reduction in R_AA^γ at p_T = 8 GeV
- Photon v_2 from jet-plasma interactions is negative but smaller in magnitude than earlier 1D estimates
- Total photon v_2 is essentially zero for p_T > 4 GeV; thermal contributions bring it to 3–4% at low p_T
- Isospin effects cause a 20% reduction in R_AA^γ at high p_T
- Center-of-mass energy √s = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Turbide et al. (2006) and the TGF2006 study that predicted a negative v_2 from jet-plasma interactions using a simpler 1D Bjorken expansion, by incorporating a realistic (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic evolution with transverse flow and a Woods-Saxon nuclear profile. The approach is constrained by soft hadronic data from Heinz (2005) and reproduces the same set of observables used in those prior studies. The results enable quantitative comparisons with PHENIX photon data and provide predictions for LHC energies; the authors note that more precise measurements of R_AA^γ could discriminate between scenarios with and without inclusive jet-plasma contributions.
