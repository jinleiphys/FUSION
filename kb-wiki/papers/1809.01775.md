# Production cross sections for exotic nuclei with multinucleon transfer reactions

> The main progresses in the multinucleon transfer reactions at energies close to the Coulomb barrier are reviewed. After a short presentation of the experimental progress and theoretical progress,the predicted production cross sections for unknown neutron-rich heavy nuclei and the trans-uranium nuclei are presented.

# Production cross sections for exotic nuclei with multinucleon transfer reactions

## Key claim
Multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions at energies near the Coulomb barrier are reviewed as the only currently available approach to access heavy neutron-rich nuclei and trans-uranium nuclei, but systematic studies to find optimum projectile-target combinations remain critical because no sizable yield of new neutron-rich heavy isotopes has yet been found in recent experiments, and the production of neutron-rich nuclei along the N=126 shell closure may be more favorable via fragmentation reactions according to some comparisons.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental progress from the 1960s onward, including deep inelastic scattering experiments with light and heavy projectiles, and theoretical modeling using three approaches: the dinuclear system (DNS) model, which treats nucleon transfer as a diffusion process along mass asymmetry after capture; the GRAZING model, which describes direct transfer reactions without capture using quantum coupled equations in a mean-field approximation; and the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, a semiclassical microscopic dynamics model including mean-field, nucleon-nucleon collisions, and the Pauli principle. The authors propose combining DNS and GRAZING models to describe both scattering and capture processes in MNT reactions.

## Key numbers
- In the reaction $^{176}$Yb+$^{238}$U at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=600$ MeV, calculated production cross sections for neutron-rich nuclei include: $^{165}$Eu at 2.84 $\mu$b, $^{169}$Tb at 6.90 $\mu$b, $^{173}$Ho at 46.24 $\mu$b, and $^{181}$Yb at 53.61 $\mu$b.
- In the reaction $^{144}$Xe+$^{208}$Pb, production cross sections of unknown neutron-rich nuclei are at least two orders of magnitude larger than those in the reaction $^{136}$Xe+$^{208}$Pb.
- In $^{238}$U+$^{248}$Cm at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=800$ MeV, $^{261}$Md and $^{262}$Md could be synthesized with cross sections of about 1.52 nb and 0.17 nb, respectively.
- In the reaction $^{112}$Sn+$^{238}$U at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=550$ MeV, predicted cross sections include: $^{220}$Np at 0.040 $\mu$b, $^{223}$Np at 0.96 $\mu$b, $^{224}$Np at 2.3 $\mu$b, $^{226}$Pu at 0.29 $\mu$b, $^{227}$Pu at 1.2 $\mu$b, $^{229}$Am at 0.088 $\mu$b, $^{232}$Cm at 0.90 $\mu$b, and $^{233}$Bk at 0.066 $\mu$b.
- Five new neutron-deficient isotopes ($^{216}$U, $^{219}$Np, $^{223}$Am, $^{229}$Am, and $^{233}$Bk) were discovered in the MNT reaction $^{48}$Ca+$^{248}$Cm at GSI in 2015.
- Between 1969 and 2009, 77 new isotopes were found using deep inelastic or multinucleon transfer reactions, including 47 neutron-rich light isotopes discovered between 1969 and 1971 by Artukh et al.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on multinucleon transfer reactions that began with the discovery of deep inelastic scattering at Yale University by Kaufmann and Wolfgang (1959) and the development of the Wilczynski plot for the $^{40}$Ar+$^{232}$Th system at Dubna (1973). It covers experimental studies by Corradi et al. at Legnaro on pair transfer in systems such as $^{40}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn and $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U, and theoretical predictions using the DNS model by Adamian et al. and Zagrebaev et al. The review enables future experimental campaigns to optimize projectile-target combinations for producing neutron-rich nuclei around N=126 and trans-uranium nuclei, while noting that key bottlenecks include the difficulty of identifying high-Z elements due to Coulomb barrier plasma effects and pulse height deficit problems, requiring new detection methods such as selective laser ionization, Penning traps, or multiple reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometers.
