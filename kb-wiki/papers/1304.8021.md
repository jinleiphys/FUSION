# Precise determination of the effective-range parameters up to an arbitrary order

> We propose a method to compute, for a given potential model, an arbitrary coefficient of the effective-range function expanded as a power series in energy. The method is based on a set of recurrence relations at low energy, that allows a compact and general description to any order in energy for neutral and charged cases. By using the Lagrange mesh technique to compute the R-matrix at zero energy, this proposal permits to compute, with a very good precision, the effective-range parameters. We use a potential model for some nuclear systems to illustrate the effectiveness of this method and to discuss its numerical limitations.

## Key claim
The paper presents a method to compute any coefficient of the effective-range expansion (ERE) to arbitrary order for a given potential model, using recurrence relations at low energy and the Lagrange mesh technique for the R-matrix at zero energy. The method works for both neutral and charged cases, but its numerical accuracy is limited by the presence of weakly bound states, virtual states, or low-energy resonances, and by the precision of the hardware and software used (here, double-precision FORTRAN on a desktop computer).

## Method
The method derives recurrence relations for the derivatives of the effective-range function \(K_l\) at zero energy, expressed in terms of the R-matrix and renormalized Coulomb or Ricatti-Bessel functions. The R-matrix and its energy derivatives at zero energy are computed numerically using the Lagrange mesh technique with a shifted Gauss-Legendre quadrature. The approach is applied to several neutral and charged nuclear systems (square well, Woods-Saxon, Bargmann, and Gaussian potentials) to compute the first ten effective-range parameters.

## Context
This work extends the method of Refs. [PRC61, PRC63] (which computed the first three effective-range parameters using the R-matrix and Lagrange mesh) to arbitrary order in the effective-range expansion. It provides a tool to compare potential-model predictions with experimental scattering data via the effective-range parameters, and can be used in inverse-problem approaches to derive interaction models. The paper discusses numerical limitations, particularly for systems with weakly bound states or low-energy resonances, and notes that the choice of channel radius must be large enough to avoid distorting the spectrum.
