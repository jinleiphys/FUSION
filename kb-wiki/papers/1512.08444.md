# Lambda-Lambda interaction from two-particle intensity correlation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate $ΛΛ$ interaction dependence of the $ΛΛ$ intensity correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. By analyzing the correlation data recently obtained by the STAR collaboration based on theoretically proposed $ΛΛ$ interactions, we give a constraint on the $ΛΛ$ scattering length, $-1.25~\text{fm} < a_0 < 0$, suggesting that $ΛΛ$ interaction is weakly attractive and there is no loosely bound state. In addition to the fermionic quantum statistics and the $ΛΛ$ interaction, effects of collective flow, feed-down from $Σ^0$, and the residual source are also found to be important to understand the data. We demonstrate that the correlation data favor negative $ΛΛ$ scattering length with the pair purity parameter $λ=(0.67)^2$ evaluated by using experimental data on the $Σ^0/Λ$ ratio, while the positive scattering length could be favored when we regard $λ$ as a free fitting parameter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ΛΛ scattering length is negative and lies in the range −1.25 fm < a₀ < 0, indicating a weakly attractive interaction with no loosely bound state, but this conclusion depends critically on the assumed value of the pair purity parameter λ; if λ is treated as a free fitting parameter instead of being fixed using the measured Σ⁰/Λ ratio, a positive scattering length becomes favored.

## Method

The authors calculated the ΛΛ correlation function using the Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) analytic model and a full numerical integration of the correlation formula with several theoretical ΛΛ potentials (Nijmegen, Ehime, quark model fss2, and potentials fitted to the Nagara double hypernucleus event). They compared the calculated correlation functions to STAR collaboration data from Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, incorporating effects of collective flow, feed-down from Σ⁰ decay, and a residual source contribution to improve the fit.

## Key numbers

- ΛΛ scattering length constraint: −1.25 fm < a₀ < 0 (corresponding to 1/a₀ < −0.8 fm⁻¹)
- Pair purity parameter λ = (0.67)², evaluated from Σ⁰/Λ ratio of 0.278 (Sullivan 1987) and Ξ/Λ ratio of 0.15
- Bond energy from the Nagara event: ΔBΛΛ(⁶ΛΛHe) = 0.67 MeV
- Binding energy from the Nagara event: BΛΛ(⁶ΛΛHe) = 6.91 MeV
- Scattering parameters for specific potentials: fss2 gives (a₀, r_eff) = (−0.81 fm, 3.99 fm); ESC08c gives (−0.97 fm, 3.86 fm); FG potential gives (−0.77 fm, 6.59 fm); HKMYY gives (−0.58 fm, 6.45 fm); updated fit to Nagara gives (−0.44 fm, 10.1 fm)
- STAR collaboration result with free λ: a₀ = 1.10 ± 0.37⁺⁰·⁰⁸₋₀·₆₈ fm

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of ΛΛ interaction from two-particle intensity correlations (C. Greiner 1989, Ohnishi 2000, Morita-Furumoto-Ohnishi 2015) and directly analyzes the STAR collaboration data (2015) from Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The result is consistent with the Nagara event observation of the double hypernucleus ⁶ΛΛHe and with recently proposed ΛΛ potentials (fss2, ESC08), but contradicts the STAR collaboration's own analysis which found a positive scattering length when λ was treated as a free parameter. The work enables future constraints on ΛΛ interaction from data at the B-factory (Belle 2013), LHC (ALICE 2016), and J-PARC, and suggests that two-particle intensity correlation can be used to study other hadron-hadron interactions such as ΩN.
