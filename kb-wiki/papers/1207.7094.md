# Large Nc Deconfinement Transition in the Presence of a Magnetic Field

> We investigate the effect of a homogeneous magnetic field on the thermal deconfinement transition of QCD in the large $N_c$ limit. First we discuss how the critical temperature decreases due to the inclusion of $N_f \ll N_c$ flavors of massless quarks. Then we study the equivalent correction in the presence of an external Abelian magnetic field. To leading order in $N_{f}/N_{c}$, the fact that the deconfinement critical temperature decreases with the magnetic field depends solely on quarks behaving paramagnetically. Finally, we discuss the effects from a finite quark mass and its competition with magnetic effects.

## Key claim

The deconfinement critical temperature in large Nc QCD decreases with an external magnetic field at leading order in Nf/Nc, provided that the flavor contribution to the pressure behaves paramagnetically with a magnetization sufficiently large to overcome any magnetic effects in the string tension.

## Method

The authors use a pressure-matching argument between the confined and deconfined phases in the large Nc limit, equating the vacuum pressure (expressed in terms of the string tension) to the sum of the gluon and quark pressures at the critical temperature. They treat the leading Nf/Nc corrections to both the string tension and the deconfined pressure, promoting the string tension modification coefficient to a function of the magnetic field. The resulting self-consistent equation for Tc(eB) is solved to leading order in Nf/Nc, and the condition for Tc(eB) to lie below the zero-field value Tc(1) is expressed in terms of the magnetization M(Tc,eB) and the magnetic derivative of the string tension modification.

## Key numbers

- The large Nc pure glue deconfinement critical temperature satisfies lim(Nc→∞) Tc/√σ0 = 0.5949(17) + 0.458(18)/Nc^2, where σ0 ~ (440 MeV)^2 is the string tension of the large Nc pure glue theory (Lucini 2012).
- In the free gas implementation with strong magnetic fields (eB/T^2 >> 1), the magnetic suppression of the deconfinement critical temperature goes like eB Npairs/(Nc σ0).
- For massive quarks, the correction to the quark pressure is ∼ (mq/Tc(0))^2, which is extremely small for mq << √σ0, Tc(0), making the critical temperature essentially flat as a function of quark mass.

## Context

This work extends the large Nc pressure-matching approach to deconfinement (Witten 1979, Lucini 2005) by including the leading Nf/Nc corrections in the presence of a magnetic field. It provides a semi-quantitative explanation for the decreasing Tc(eB) observed in recent lattice simulations with physical masses (Bali 2011), which contradicted earlier model predictions of increasing or flat Tc(eB). The analysis also connects to the MIT bag model description (Fraga 2012) that captured the qualitative behavior of Tc(eB) decreasing and saturating at large fields, and it enables future studies of the Veneziano limit where Nf and Nc both go to infinity.
