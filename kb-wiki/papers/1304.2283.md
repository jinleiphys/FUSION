# The temperature evolution of the shear viscosity in a unitary Fermi gas

> We present an ab initio determination of the shear viscosity for the unitary Fermi gas based on finite temperature quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations and the Kubo linear-response formalism. The results are confronted with the bound for the shear viscosity originating from hydrodynamic fluctuations. Assuming smoothness of the frequency dependent shear viscosity eta(omega), we show that the bound is violated in the low temperature regime and the violation occurs simultaneously with the onset of the Cooper paring in the system. In order to preserve the hydrodynamic bound in QMC eta(omega) has to possess a sharp structure located in the vicinity of zero frequency which is not resolved by an analytic continuation procedure.

## Key claim

Assuming smoothness of the frequency-dependent shear viscosity η(ω), the authors show that the classical hydrodynamic bound for η/n is violated in the low-temperature regime, and this violation occurs simultaneously with the onset of Cooper pairing; however, the QMC data are compatible with the bound if a sharp structure in η(ω) near zero frequency exists but is unresolved by analytic continuation.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) simulations on cubic lattices of sizes N_x = 8, 10, and 12, with average densities n ≈ 0.08, 0.04, and 0.03 respectively, to compute the imaginary-time stress-tensor correlator at zero momentum. The frequency-dependent shear viscosity η(ω) was extracted by inverting the Kubo relation using a combination of singular value decomposition (SVD) and the maximum entropy method (MEM), imposing non-negativity, a sum rule, and the known asymptotic tail η(ω→∞) = C/(15π√ω). The static shear viscosity η ≡ η(ω→0) was then obtained from the zero-frequency limit.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for superfluid-to-normal phase transition: T_c = 0.15(1) ε_F.
- Onset of Cooper pair formation (pseudogap regime): T* ≈ 0.20–0.25 ε_F.
- For lattice size 10³ at T ≈ 0.16 ε_F, static shear viscosity η/n ≈ 0.15(2), 0.17(4), 0.15(2), and 0.18(2) for densities n ≈ 0.04, 0.06, 0.08, and 0.1, respectively.
- Minimum ratio η/s ≈ 0.2, located near the critical temperature, above the KSS bound of 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08.
- Hydrodynamic bound from Chafin and Schäfer (2012) and Romatschke and Young (2013): η/n ≳ 0.3 ħ.
- Effective range parameters: p_F r_eff ≈ 0.54, 0.43, 0.39 for N_x = 8, 10, 12 respectively; momentum cutoffs p_cut/p_F ≈ 2.4, 3.0, 3.3.
- Statistical uncertainties typically ~1% from ensembles of 10⁴ uncorrelated samples per temperature.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QMC determination of shear viscosity (Wlazłowski et al., 2012) with higher accuracy and lower densities, and confronts the results with the classical hydrodynamic bound derived by Chafin and Schäfer (2012) and Romatschke and Young (2013). The findings support theoretical scenarios (Bruun and Smith, 2005; Guo et al., 2013) predicting significant suppression of viscosity due to pairing correlations and pseudogap physics, and enable future studies of transport in strongly correlated fermionic systems, including comparisons with experimental measurements on trapped unitary Fermi gases and with the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions.
