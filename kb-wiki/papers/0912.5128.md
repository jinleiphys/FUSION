# Momentum Dependence of Nuclear Mean Field and multifragmentation in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We report the consequences of implementing momentum dependent interactions (MDI) on multifragmentation in heavy-ion reactions over entire collision geometry. The evolution of a single cold nucleus using static soft equation of state and soft momentum dependent equation of state demonstrates that inclusion of momentum dependence increases the emission of free nucleons. However, no heavier fragments are emitted artificially. The calculations performed within the framework of quantum molecular dynamics approach suggest that MDI strongly influence the system size dependence of fragment production. A comparison with ALADiN experimental data justifies the use of momentum dependent interactions in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Momentum dependent interactions (MDI) in the nuclear mean field enhance the emission of free nucleons from a cold nucleus but do not cause artificial emission of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs), thereby allowing the study of multifragmentation with MDI despite the additional repulsion.

## Method

The authors used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) transport model to simulate heavy-ion collisions, implementing a momentum dependent interaction term parameterized from the real part of the proton-nucleus optical potential (with parameters t4 = 1.57 MeV and t5 = 5.0 × 10⁻⁴ (MeV/c)⁻²). They initialized single cold nuclei (⁵⁸Ni and ¹⁹⁷Au) with a soft equation of state (S) and a soft momentum dependent equation of state (SM) and tracked cluster emission and rms radii over 200 fm/c. They then simulated central collisions of six symmetric systems (⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni, ⁹³Nb+⁹³Nb, ¹³¹Xe+¹³¹Xe, ¹⁶⁸Er+¹⁶⁸Er, ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) at 50 and 400 AMeV, and compared IMF multiplicities for ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at 400 AMeV with ALADiN experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For a single cold ¹⁹⁷Au nucleus with SM interactions, about 19 mass units are emitted, of which approximately 15 are free nucleons; the heaviest fragment ⟨A^max⟩ remains close to 177.
- Only about 0.15 IMFs are emitted on average from a single cold nucleus with MDI, compared to 10-12 IMFs seen in Au+Au reactions.
- The power law scaling of MMF and IMF multiplicities with system size (c A_tot^τ) yields a power factor τ close to 3/2 for the static soft equation of state at 50 and 400 AMeV.
- The maximal average density ⟨ρ/ρ₀⟩ in central collisions is reduced with MDI, with the difference between S and SM diminishing at 400 AMeV compared to 50 AMeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on the role of momentum dependent interactions in collective flow and particle production (Aich (1991), Hartnack (1998)) to the domain of multifragmentation, where few prior attempts existed (Blaich (1993), Skalski (1997)). The authors show that MDI weaken system size effects in fragment production and that the ALADiN experimental data (Schüttauf (1996)) for Au+Au at 400 AMeV are well reproduced only when momentum dependence is included, supporting the use of MDI in heavy-ion collision simulations.
