# Exit Doorway Model for Nuclear Breakup of Weakly Bound Projectiles

> We derive closed expressions for the nuclear breakup cross sections in the adiabatic limit using the Austern-Blair theory. These expressions are appropriate for the breakup of weakly bound nuclei. The concept of an exit doorway that mediates the coupling between the entrance channel and the breakup continuum is used. We prove the validity of the scaling law that dictates that the nuclear breakup cross section scales linearly with the radius of the target. We also compare our results for the nuclear breakup cross section of $^{11}$Be, $^8$B on several targets with recent CDCC calculation.

## Key claim

The nuclear breakup cross section scales linearly with the target radius (and thus as \(A_T^{1/3}\)), but the derived closed-form expression systematically underestimates full CDCC calculations for the halo nuclei \(^{11}\)Be and \(^{8}\)B, indicating that higher-order channel-coupling effects not captured by the DWBA-based exit-doorway model are significant.

## Method

The authors derive an analytical expression for the nuclear breakup cross section by treating breakup as an inelastic multipole excitation through an "exit doorway" state, using the Austern-Blair adiabatic theory within the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA). The radial integrals are evaluated in the adiabatic limit (\(k_i = k_f = k\)) with large orbital angular momenta, and the sum over partial waves is approximated by an integral over the grazing angular momentum. Deformation lengths for dipole and quadrupole transitions are calculated from a cluster model using \(B(E\lambda)\) values, and the resulting cross sections are compared to Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations for \(^{11}\)Be, \(^{8}\)B, and \(^{7}\)Be projectiles on several targets.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{11}\)Be, the dipole deformation length squared is \((\delta^{(N)}_1)^2 = 0.71 \pm 0.04\) fm\(^2\) and the quadrupole deformation length squared is \((\delta^{(N)}_2)^2 = 1.27 \pm 0.25\) fm\(^2\), derived from \(B(E1) = 1.05 \pm 0.06\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\).
* The cluster-model deformation lengths for \(^{8}\)B are \((\delta^{(N)}_1)^2 = 1.62\) fm\(^2\) and \((\delta^{(N)}_2)^2 = 3.24\) fm\(^2\) (with \(N_0S=1\)).
* The cluster-model deformation lengths for \(^{7}\)Be are \((\delta^{(N)}_1)^2 = 0.41\) fm\(^2\) and \((\delta^{(N)}_2)^2 = 1.54\) fm\(^2\) (with \(N_0S=1\)).
* The normalization factor \(c\) in the cross-section formula is 1.37 for \(^{7}\)Be, 0.307 for \(^{8}\)B, and 2.52 for \(^{11}\)Be.
* The breakup Q-values used are 0.504 MeV for \(^{11}\)Be (into \(^{10}\)Be + n), 0.137 MeV for \(^{8}\)B (into \(^{7}\)Be + p), and 1.587 MeV for \(^{7}\)Be (into \(^{4}\)He + \(^{3}\)He).

## Context

This work extends the Austern-Blair adiabatic theory for inelastic scattering (Austern and Blair, 1965; Frahn, 1971) to nuclear breakup of weakly bound projectiles, using the exit-doorway concept previously applied to fusion and giant resonance excitation (Hussein et al., 2006; Canto et al., 2006). The derived linear scaling with target radius contradicts the \(A_T^{1/3}\) dependence established by CDCC calculations (Thompson, 2007) and the Serber model (Serber, 1947), but the authors find their formula underestimates full CDCC results for halo nuclei, suggesting that higher-order couplings and continuum-continuum effects must be included. The model enables a simple analytical estimate of the nuclear breakup contribution, with the normalization factor \(c\) potentially related to the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC) as measured for \(^{8}\)B (Trache et al., 2003).
