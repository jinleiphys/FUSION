# In medium T-matrix for superfluid nuclear matter

> We study a generalized ladder resummation in the superfluid phase of the nuclear matter. The approach is based on a conserving generalization of the usual T-matrix approximation including also anomalous self-energies and propagators. The approximation here discussed is a generalization of the usual mean-field BCS approach and of the in medium T-matrix approximation in the normal phase. The numerical results in this work are obtained in the quasi-particle approximation. Properties of the resulting self-energy, superfluid gap and spectral functions are studied.

# In medium T-matrix for superfluid nuclear matter

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalized ladder resummation approach for superfluid nuclear matter that includes both normal and anomalous (off-diagonal) self-energies and propagators, but the numerical results are obtained only in the quasi-particle approximation starting from mean-field BCS propagators, meaning the full self-consistent solution is not achieved. The additional T-matrix contributions to the superfluid gap are found to be small compared to the BCS mean-field gap, particularly near the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors generalize the standard T-matrix approximation to the superfluid phase by introducing a 2x2 matrix structure in the Nambu formalism, coupling normal and anomalous propagators in the ladder resummation. The generalized T-matrix equation is solved in partial-wave decomposition using a separable Yamaguchi S-wave interaction, with the spectral functions approximated by the BCS quasi-particle ansatz. The diagonal and off-diagonal self-energies are calculated from the T-matrix, with the BCS gap added explicitly as the dominant contribution, and the dispersive corrections are obtained via dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- The BCS gap used as input is 10.1 MeV at a temperature of 3 MeV
- The superfluid gap in nuclear matter from nucleon-nucleon interactions is typically 5-10 MeV for the isospin singlet (T=0) partial-wave ³S₁-³D₁
- The two-particle excitation gap appears at twice the superfluid gap (approximately 20.2 MeV) for zero total momentum of the pair
- The temperature used in the calculations is 3 MeV, below the critical temperature

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Green's function approach (T-matrix approximation) from normal nuclear matter, studied extensively by Dickhoff (1990s) and others, into the superfluid phase below the critical temperature. It builds on the earlier heuristic approach of Jensen (2000) for superfluid T-matrix resummation and provides a more rigorous conserving generalization. The formalism enables future calculations of in-medium corrections to the superfluid gap using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, though the present numerical results are limited to a simplified separable interaction.
