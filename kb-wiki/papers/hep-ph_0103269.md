# Carlson-Goldman modes in the color superconducting phase of dense QCD

> We predict the existence of the gapless Carlson-Goldman modes in the color-flavor locked color superconducting phase of dense QCD. These modes resemble Nambu-Goldstone modes of the superconducting phase below the critical temperature where the Anderson-Higgs mechanism takes place. These modes exist in the broken phase in the vicinity of the critical line. Their presence does not eliminate the Meissner effect. The effect of Landau damping on the width of the Carlson-Goldman modes is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of gapless Carlson-Goldman (CG) modes in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of dense QCD, but only in the near-critical region just below the critical temperature where a large density of thermally excited quasiparticles partially screens the interaction, and only when the presence of two distinct quark gaps (octet and singlet) partially suppresses Landau damping.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective action for the gluon field by integrating out quark degrees of freedom, then derived the current-current correlation function and gluon propagator from the one-loop polarization tensor. They expanded the polarization tensor components in powers of the small parameters \(|\Delta_T|/T\) and \(v = q_0/|\vec{q}|\) in the near-critical region, and solved the resulting dispersion relation for the electric-type collective modes. The spectral density of electric gluons was computed using the BCS temperature dependence of the gap.

## Key numbers

* The CG mode dispersion relation is \(q_0 = \frac{|\Delta_T|}{T}(\pm 0.193 - i\,0.316) |\vec{q}|\), giving a gapless mode with a width-to-energy ratio determined by the imaginary part.
* At temperature \(T^* \approx 0.986 T_c\), the ratio of the width to the energy of the CG mode formally goes to infinity, providing an estimate for the temperature where the mode disappears.
* The NG boson dispersion relation has \(x^* \approx 0.215\) and \(y^* \approx 0.245\), qualitatively similar to the CG mode.
* At \(T = 0.9999 T_c\), the gap is \(|\Delta_T| \simeq 0.031 T_c\).
* The static magnetic component of the polarization tensor is \(\Pi_1 \simeq -\frac{7\zeta(3)|\Delta_T|^2}{12\pi^2 T^2} \omega_p^2\), confirming the Meissner effect persists.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Carlson-Goldman modes, originally discovered in condensed matter superconductors by Carlson and Goldman (1975) and analyzed by Takada and others (1988, 1997), to the color-flavor locked phase of dense QCD. The prediction builds on the understanding of color superconductivity developed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998). The existence of these modes in a clean system is enabled by the partial suppression of Landau damping due to the two distinct quark gaps in the CFL phase, unlike ordinary metals where all quarks have the same gap. The results open the possibility that CG modes could affect thermodynamic and transport properties of compact stars and may have consequences for the hypothetical quark-hadron continuity proposed by Schafer and Wilczek (1999).
