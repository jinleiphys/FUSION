# Evolution of collectivity as a signal of quark gluon plasma formation in heavy ion collisions

> A measurement for studying the mass dependence of the dilepton interferometry in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments as a tool to characterize the quark-gluon phase is proposed. In calculations involving dileptons, we show that the mass dependence of radii extracted from the virtual photon (dilepton) interferometry provide access to the development of collective flow with time. It is argued that the non-monotonic variation of HBT radii with invariant mass of the lepton pairs signals the formation of quark gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions. Our proposal of experimentally measuring the ratio, $R_{\mathrm out}/R_{\mathrm side}$ for dileptons can be used to estimate the average life times of the partonic as well as the hadronic phases.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a non-monotonic variation of HBT radii extracted from dilepton (virtual photon) interferometry as a function of the lepton pair's invariant mass can signal quark-gluon plasma formation in heavy ion collisions, but this signal depends on the presence of collective radial flow in the hadronic phase to produce the characteristic dip in \(R_{\mathrm side}\) near the \(\rho\)-meson mass.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic hydrodynamic model with cylindrical symmetry and boost invariance to simulate the space-time evolution of central 0-5% Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. They computed the Bose-Einstein correlation function for dilepton pairs from both the partonic phase (via \(q\bar{q} \rightarrow l^+l^-\)) and the hadronic phase (including decays of \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\phi\) mesons plus continuum contributions), then extracted HBT radii \(R_{\mathrm side}\) and \(R_{\mathrm out}\) by fitting the correlation function to a Gaussian form \(C_2 = 1 + \lambda \exp(-R_i^2 q_i^2)\) with \(\lambda = 1/3\).

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature \(T_i = 290\) MeV and proper thermalization time \(\tau_i = 0.6\) fm used in the hydrodynamic calculation.
- Critical temperature \(T_c = 175\) MeV, chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{ch} = 170\) MeV, and kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{fo} = 120\) MeV.
- For RHIC running 12 weeks at luminosity \(50 \times 10^{27}\) cm\(^{-2}\) s\(^{-1}\) with \(\sigma = 40\) mb, estimated number of events \(N_{\mathrm event} = 1.45 \times 10^{10}\).
- For the mass window \(810 \leq M \leq 990\) MeV and \(p_T\) bin 1-1.8 GeV, the differential number of pairs is \(\frac{N_{part}}{2} \times 1870\).
- For the mass window \(500 \leq M \leq 750\) MeV and \(p_T\) bin 1.4-1.8 GeV, the differential number of pairs is \(\frac{N_{part}}{2} \times 3240\).
- For 0-10% centrality (about 330 participants), the number of lepton pairs in \(p_T\) range 1.4-1.5 GeV is \(\sim 5.3 \times 10^5\) for the mass window 0.5-0.75 GeV.
- The probability to have two dilepton pairs per event is \(\sim 10^{-9}\), requiring roughly \(10^9\) events for HBT interferometry.
- Increasing the \(\rho\) meson width by a factor of two reduces \(R_{\mathrm side}\) by about 10% for invariant mass windows below the \(\rho\)-peak.

## Context

This work extends the use of two-particle intensity interferometry, previously established for hadrons (Heinz and collaborators, various years), to dilepton pairs as a probe of the space-time evolution of heavy ion collisions. It builds on prior proposals for using real photons and dileptons as QGP probes (Gale and Rapp, various years; NA60 collaboration, 2006) and on PHENIX measurements of dilepton yields at RHIC (PHENIX collaboration, 2009). The proposed measurement of the ratio \(R_{\mathrm out}/R_{\mathrm side}\) for dileptons could enable experimental estimation of the average lifetimes of both the partonic and hadronic phases, and the authors note that increased luminosity at RHIC after 2012 (to about \(10^{29}\) cm\(^{-2}\) s\(^{-1}\)) and higher collision energies at the LHC may make such measurements feasible.
