# Shear viscosity of $β$-stable nuclear matter

> Viscosity plays a critical role in determining the stability of rotating neutron stars. We report the results of a calculation of the shear viscosity of $β$~-~stable matter, carried out using an effective interaction based on a state-of-the-art nucleon-nucleon potential and the formalism of correlated basis functions. Within our approach the equation of state, determining the proton fraction, and the nucleon-nucleon scattering probability are consistently obtained from the same dynamical model. The results show that, while the neutron contribution to the viscosity is always dominant, above nuclear saturation density the electron contribution becomes appreciable.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of beta-stable nuclear matter is calculated using an effective interaction derived from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential, with the key finding that while neutrons dominate the viscosity at all densities, the electron contribution becomes appreciable above nuclear saturation density; however, this electron contribution only becomes dominant if the proton fraction is artificially increased to about 8% at saturation density, which is larger than the proton fraction predicted by the authors' own dynamical model.

## Method

The authors apply the Landau-Abrikosov-Khalatnikov formalism to compute the shear viscosity of beta-stable matter composed of neutrons, protons, and electrons. The nucleon-nucleon scattering probabilities are obtained from a correlated basis functions (CBF) effective interaction based on a truncated Argonne v18 potential, while the proton fraction is determined from beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions using single-particle spectra computed in the Hartree-Fock approximation with the same effective interaction. Electron scattering probabilities and the electromagnetic part of proton-proton scattering follow the approach of Flowers and Itoh (1979).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- At densities rho < rho_0, the total viscosity can be identified with the neutron contribution alone
- When the proton fraction is artificially increased by a factor of two, the electron viscosity exceeds neutron viscosity at baryon density rho ~ 0.2 fm^{-3}, corresponding to an electron fraction of ~8%
- The critical temperature for the 1S0 superfluid gap, computed using the same effective interaction, is T_c ~ 2 x 10^{10} K at density rho ~ 0.04 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the CBF effective interaction approach previously applied to pure neutron matter by Benhar and collaborators (2009) to the more realistic case of beta-stable matter containing protons and electrons. It improves upon the seminal calculations of Flowers and Itoh (1979), who used free-space nucleon-nucleon cross sections and neglected medium modifications, by consistently treating the equation of state and transport properties within the same dynamical model. The results enable more consistent studies of gravitational-wave driven instabilities in rotating neutron stars, as the formalism can be extended to include superfluid pairing effects and muons.
