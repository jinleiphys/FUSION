# Investigation of the $K^{-}pp$ Bound State via the \( K^{-} + {}^{3}\mathrm{He} \) Reaction

> Using the four-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the \( K^{-}ppn \) system, we investigate the possible formation of a \( K^{-}pp \) quasi-bound state through the low-energy \( K^{-} + {}^{3}\mathrm{He} \) reaction. The neutron missing mass spectrum in the final state was calculated for different models of the \( \bar{K}N \) interaction. The results indicate that, irrespective of the specific nature of the \( Λ(1405) \) structure or the details of the \( \bar{K}N \) interaction model, a signal corresponding to the \( K^{-}pp \) quasi-bound state could appear in the \( πΣp \) mass spectrum. This supports the feasibility of observing the \( K^{-}pp \) cluster in low-energy kaon-induced reactions on helium-3.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a signal corresponding to the \(K^{-}pp\) quasi-bound state could appear in the \(\pi\Sigma p\) mass spectrum from the low-energy \(K^{-} + {}^{3}\mathrm{He}\) reaction, irrespective of the specific nature of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) structure or the details of the \(\bar{K}N\) interaction model. However, the peak position is shifted by approximately 10-15 MeV toward the \(\pi\Sigma p\) threshold compared to the calculated \(K^{-}pp\) pole positions, and the appearance of a \(\Lambda(1405)\)-related shoulder depends on the potential model used.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the \(K^{-}ppn\) system to calculate the transition amplitude for the \(K^{-} + {}^{3}\mathrm{He} \rightarrow n + \pi\Sigma p\) reaction. They employed the Energy-Dependent Pole Expansion (EDPE) method to obtain separable representations of the three-body subamplitudes and used the exact optical potential method to simplify the coupled-channel \(\bar{K}NN-\pi\Sigma N\) calculations. The neutron missing-mass spectrum was computed for three different \(\bar{K}N\) interaction models: two phenomenological potentials (SIDD1 and SIDD2) and one chiral-based potential, with the incoming antikaon momentum set to \(p_{K^{-}} = 100~\mathrm{MeV}/c\).

## Key numbers

* The \(K^{-}pp\) pole position for the SIDD1 model is \(2325-i34\) MeV; for SIDD2 it is \(2325-i24\) MeV; for the chiral model it is \(2346-i22\) MeV.
* The \(\bar{K}N\) first pole positions are: SIDD1 at \(1428.1-i46.6\) MeV, SIDD2 at \(1418.1-i56.9\) MeV, and chiral at \(1420.6-i20.3\) MeV.
* The \(\bar{K}N\) second pole positions are: SIDD2 at \(1382.0-i104.2\) MeV and chiral at \(1343.0-i72.5\) MeV; SIDD1 has no second pole.
* The binding energy of helium-3 used in the calculation is \(B_{{}^{3}\mathrm{He}} = 10.7\) MeV, overestimated due to the PEST potential omitting short-range repulsion.
* The incoming antikaon momentum was set to \(p_{K^{-}} = 100~\mathrm{MeV}/c\).
* The number of terms \(N_{r}\) in the separable expansion was fixed to \(N_{r} = 2\).

## Context

This work extends previous three-body Faddeev studies of the \(\bar{K}NN-\pi\Sigma N\) system by Ohnishi et al. (2013) and Marri et al. (2019), which investigated the \(K^{-}pp\) quasi-bound state signature but did not provide absolute cross sections. The present four-body calculation explicitly computes the neutron missing-mass spectrum for the \(K^{-} + {}^{3}\mathrm{He}\) reaction, offering more direct guidance for experiments such as J-PARC E15, which reported a signal with binding energy approximately 42 MeV and decay width 100 MeV. The results enable future experimental discrimination between genuine \(K^{-}pp\) bound states and final-state interaction effects through high-resolution invariant mass reconstruction of the \(\pi\Sigma p\) system combined with neutron kinematics.
