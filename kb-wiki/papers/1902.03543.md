# Nuclear Physics without High-Momentum Potentials: Constructing the Nuclear Effective Interaction Directly from Scattering Observables

> The traditional approach to nuclear physics encodes phase shift information in a nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential, producing a nucleon-level interaction that captures the sub-GeV consequences of QCD. A further reduction to the nuclear scale is needed to produce an effective interaction for soft Hilbert spaces, such as those employed in the shell model. Here we describe an alternative construction of this effective interaction, from QCD directly to the nuclear scale, that is direct and precise. This eliminates the need for constructing and renormalizing the high-momentum NN potential. Instead, continuum phase shifts and mixing angles are used directly at the nuclear scale. The method exploits the analytic continuity in energy of HOBET (Harmonic-Oscillator-Based Effective Theory) to connect bound states to continuum solutions at specific energies. The procedure is systematic, cutoff independent, and convergent, yielding keV accuracy at NNLO or N$^3$LO, depending on the channel. Lepage plots are provided.

## Key claim

The paper describes a direct, one-step procedure for constructing the nuclear effective interaction for soft Hilbert spaces (such as those used in the shell model) from continuum phase shifts and mixing angles, eliminating the need for constructing and renormalizing a high-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential. The method is systematic, cutoff independent, and convergent, yielding keV accuracy at NNLO or N3LO depending on the channel, but the convergence rate depends on the choice of the long-range part of the interaction (pionful vs. pionless).

## Method

The method exploits the analytic continuity in energy of HOBET (Harmonic-Oscillator-Based Effective Theory) to connect bound states to continuum solutions at specific energies, using the energy-dependent Bloch-Horowitz equation to generate the effective interaction within a finite harmonic oscillator P space. The short-range part of the effective interaction is represented by a contact-gradient expansion with constant low-energy constants (LECs), while the long-range part is treated either as the infrared component of a given potential (e.g., Argonne v18), as the infrared component of one-pion exchange (pionful HOBET), or as zero (pionless HOBET). The LECs are determined directly from the variation of phase shifts with energy, without using an NN potential, by matching to the Talmi integrals of the effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron bound state energy used for evaluation is -2.2246 MeV, with oscillator parameter b = 1.7 fm and model space cutoff Lambda_SM = 8.
- The integrand for the highest-order s-wave contribution of the one-pion exchange potential peaks at |r1 - r2| ~ 4.1 fm (taking b = 1.7 fm).
- The implicit dimensionless parameter governing order-by-order convergence of the short-range expansion V_delta is (r_SR / b)^2, where r_SR represents the range of unresolved short-range physics.
- The IR corrections are responsible for approximately 95% of the nuclear Bloch-Horowitz equation's energy dependence.

## Context

This work extends the HOBET framework developed by Haxton, Song, and Luu (2001-2008), which previously determined LECs from the Argonne v18 potential by numerical renormalization. The paper contrasts with the traditional two-step approach that first encodes phase shifts in an NN potential (such as Argonne v18 or chiral EFT potentials) and then renormalizes that potential to produce a soft effective interaction, a procedure that the authors argue is "somewhat unusual" compared to other EFT contexts. The direct method enables the construction of effective interactions without the need for softening procedures like V_low_k or similarity renormalization group (SRG), and avoids issues with intruder states that plagued earlier diagrammatic approaches by Barrett and Kirson (1970) and Shucan and Weidenmuller (1972-1973).
