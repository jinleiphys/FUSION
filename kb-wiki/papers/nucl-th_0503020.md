# Comment on "Constraints on proton structure from precision atomic physics measurements"

> We strongly disagree with the procedure used in a recent paper to extract the Zemach moment for hydrogen.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the Zemach moment extracted by Brodsky et al. (2005) using hyperfine structure data and a theoretical polarization correction is incompatible with the precise value obtained directly from elastic electron scattering, and that the claimed uncertainty on the polarization correction from Faustov et al. (2002) has been substantially underestimated.

## Method

The authors compare the Zemach moment and proton charge rms radius from ten form-factor parametrizations used by Brodsky et al. (2005) against world electron scattering data, recomputing the correct numerical values and evaluating the chi-squared fit quality for each parametrization over the momentum transfer range relevant for the Zemach moment determination (q < 4 fm^{-1}). They also examine the correlation between the rms radius and the Zemach moment, and assess the sensitivity of the Brodsky et al. result to the polarization correction.

## Key numbers

- The precise Zemach moment from electron scattering is 1.086 ± 0.012 fm, from Friar and Sick (2004).
- The momentum transfer range relevant for the Zemach moment determination is q < 4 fm^{-1}.
- Most of the form-factor combinations used by Brodsky et al. (2005) give a "disastrous" chi-squared when compared to world electron scattering data over this range, with only fits 3 and 4 being exceptions.

## Context

This Comment directly contradicts the procedure and conclusions of Brodsky et al. (2005), who used hydrogen and muonium hyperfine structure together with a polarization correction from Faustov et al. (2002) to extract the Zemach moment. The authors' own prior work (Friar and Sick, 2004) determined the Zemach moment directly from elastic electron scattering form factors and inferred the polarization correction, finding consistency with electron scattering data. The Comment argues that reliable determination of the Zemach moment from hyperfine structure must await improved inelastic electron scattering data to calculate the polarization correction, and that for now elastic electron scattering provides the most reliable value.
