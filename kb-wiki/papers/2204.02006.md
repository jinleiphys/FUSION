# Octupole correlations in collective excitations of neutron-rich $N\approx56$ nuclei

> Octupole correlations in the low-energy collective states of neutron-rich nuclei with the neutron number $N\approx56$ are studied within the interacting boson model (IBM) that is based on the nuclear density functional theory. The constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations using a universal energy density functional and a pairing interaction provide the potential energy surfaces in terms of the axially-symmetric quadrupole and octupole deformations for the even-even nuclei $^{86-94}$Se, $^{88-96}$Kr, $^{90-98}$Sr, $^{92-100}$Zr, and $^{94-102}$Mo. The SCMF energy surface is then mapped onto the energy expectation value of a version of the IBM in the boson condensate state, which consists of the neutron and proton monopole $s$, quadrupole $d$, and octupole $f$ bosons. This procedure determines the strength parameters of the IBM Hamiltonian, which is used to compute relevant spectroscopic properties. At the SCMF level, no octupole deformed ground state is obtained, while the energy surface is generally soft in the octupole deformation at $N\approx56$. The predicted negative-parity yrast bands with the bandhead state $3^-_1$ weakly depend on $N$ and become lowest in energy at $N=56$ in each of the considered isotopic chains. The model further predicts finite electric octupole transition rates between the lowest negative- and the positive-parity ground-state bands.

## Key claim

The paper claims that octupole correlations are present in neutron-rich nuclei near N≈56, but no octupole deformed ground state is obtained at the self-consistent mean-field level; instead, the energy surface is generally soft in the octupole deformation at N≈56, and the predicted negative-parity yrast bands with the bandhead state 3⁻₁ become lowest in energy at N=56 in each isotopic chain.

## Method

The author performed constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) framework with the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force of finite range, computing potential energy surfaces as functions of axially-symmetric quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformations for even-even nuclei ⁸⁶⁻⁹⁴Se, ⁸⁸⁻⁹⁶Kr, ⁹⁰⁻⁹⁸Sr, ⁹²⁻¹⁰⁰Zr, and ⁹⁴⁻¹⁰²Mo. The SCMF energy surface was then mapped onto the energy expectation value of an sdf-interacting boson model that distinguishes neutron and proton bosons (sdf-IBM-2), using a boson condensate state with monopole s, quadrupole d, and octupole f bosons. The strength parameters of the sdf-IBM-2 Hamiltonian were determined by fitting the bosonic energy surface to the SCMF one in the neighborhood of the global minimum, and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized to compute excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition rates.

## Key numbers

- The pairing force strength used was V₀ = 837 MeV·fm³, corresponding to a 15% increase from the original value of 728 MeV·fm³.
- The octupole-octupole interaction strength κ₃ was kept constant at 0.12 MeV for all nuclei under study.
- The single f-boson energy εf was kept within the range εf ≈ 2.7–3.0 MeV for most isotopic chains.
- The ratio of the 4⁺₁ to 2⁺₁ excitation energies R₄/₂ = 2.46 was calculated for ⁹⁶Zr.
- The lowest negative-parity state was predicted to be I = 3⁻ at excitation energy E_x ≈ 2 MeV.
- For the N=52 isotones, the nearly spherical minimum showed β₂ ≈ 0.05 for ⁸⁶Se and ⁸⁸Kr, and β₂ ≈ 0.0 for ⁹⁰Sr, ⁹²Zr, and ⁹⁴Mo.

## Context

This work extends previous applications of the mapped sdf-IBM framework, which had been applied to octupole shape phase transitions in light actinides and rare-earth nuclei using relativistic energy density functionals (Nomura 2013, Nomura 2014). The study focuses on the lighter mass region near the octupole magic numbers (N,Z) ≈ (56,34), (56,56), and (34,34), which has not been as extensively investigated as the actinides and lanthanides. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on negative-parity states in neutron-rich Kr isotopes recently obtained at RIKEN (Gerst 2022) and provide a microscopic foundation for spectroscopic calculations in the A≈100 region.
