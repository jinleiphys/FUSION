# Exactly-solvable models of proton and neutron interacting bosons

> We describe a class of exactly-solvable models of interacting bosons based on the algebra SO(3,2). Each copy of the algebra represents a system of neutron and proton bosons in a given bosonic level interacting via a pairing interaction. The model that includes s and d bosons is a specific realization of the IBM2, restricted to the transition regime between vibrational and gamma-soft nuclei. By including additional copies of the algebra, we can generate proton-neutron boson models involving other boson degrees of freedom, while still maintaining exact solvability. In each of these models, we can study not only the states of maximal symmetry, but also those of mixed symmetry, albeit still in the vibrational to gamma-soft transition regime. Furthermore, in each of these models we can study some features of F-spin symmetry breaking. We report systematic calculations as a function of the pairing strength for models based on s, d, and g bosons and on s, d, and f bosons. The formalism of exactly-solvable models based on the SO(3,2) algebra is not limited to systems of proton and neutron bosons, however, but can also be applied to other scenarios that involve two species of interacting bosons.

## Key claim

The paper presents exactly-solvable models of interacting proton and neutron bosons based on the rank-two algebra SO(3,2), but these models are restricted to the transition regime between vibrational (U(5)) and gamma-soft (SO(6)) nuclei and do not represent the most general IBM2 Hamiltonian, as the quadrupole operator is limited to χ=0.

## Method

The authors construct a class of exactly-solvable Richardson-Gaudin models using multiple copies of the SO(3,2) algebra, where each copy represents a bosonic level (s, d, g, or f) containing neutron and proton bosons interacting via a pairing interaction. The integrals of motion are derived from the Cartan decomposition of SO(3,2), and the exact eigenvalues are obtained by solving generalized Richardson equations for two sets of spectral parameters. The Hamiltonian is expressed in multipole-multipole form to facilitate connection with the phenomenological IBM2, and systematic calculations are performed as a function of the pairing strength g for systems with s, d, and g bosons and with s, d, and f bosons.

## Key numbers

- Single-boson energies used: ε_s = 0 MeV, ε_d = 1 MeV, ε_g = 1.6 MeV, ε_f = 1.6 MeV
- System of N = 20 bosons with N_ν = N_π = 10 (F_max = 10)
- Phase transition from vibrational to gamma-unstable system occurs at roughly g = 0.08 MeV
- The sd model uses ε_d = 1 MeV and ε_s = 0 MeV; the sdg model uses the same values plus ε_g = 1.6 MeV
- The sdf model uses ε_s = 0 MeV, ε_d = 1 MeV, and ε_f = 1.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Richardson-Gaudin exact-solvability framework, previously applied to identical-particle pairing with SU(2) for fermions and SU(1,1) for bosons, to a rank-two algebra (SO(3,2)) for two-component boson systems. It builds on prior work by Dukelsky, Esebbag and Schuck (2001) on generalized Richardson-Gaudin models and by Richardson (1960s) on exact solutions of the pairing model. The models enable exact treatment of proton-neutron boson systems with multiple boson degrees of freedom (beyond the standard s,d bosons of IBM2), allowing study of mixed-symmetry states and F-spin symmetry breaking in the vibrational to gamma-soft transition regime, and can also be applied to other two-species boson systems such as mixtures of ⁹⁷Rb atoms in hyperfine states.
