# Classification of initial state granularity via 2d Fourier Expansion

> A new method to quantify fluctuations in the initial state of heavy ion collisions is presented. The initial state energy distribution is decomposed with a set of orthogonal basis functions which include both angular and radial variation. The resulting two dimensional Fourier coefficients provide additional information about the nature of the initial state fluctuations compared to a purely angular decomposition. We apply this method to ensembles of initial states generated by both Glauber and Color Glass Condensate Monte-Carlo codes. In addition initial state configurations with varying amounts of fluctuations generated by a dynamic transport approach are analysed to test the sensitivity of the procedure. The results allow for a full characterization of the initial state structures that is useful to discriminate the different initial state models currently in use.

## Key claim

The paper presents a two-dimensional Fourier expansion method (using Bessel functions on a disk) that includes both angular and radial variation to characterize initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, and claims this approach can discriminate between different initial-state models (Glauber, CGC, and UrQMD) more effectively than purely angular decompositions. The key hedge is that the method's usefulness depends on the radial information revealing "clear differences" between models, but the authors acknowledge that the observables developed "cannot be measured in detectors" and are primarily useful for "apples-to-apples comparison of initial state models."

## Method

The authors decompose the initial-state energy density distribution on a disk of radius r0 = 10 fm using orthogonal basis functions phi_mn(r,theta) = J_m(r*lambda_mn/r0)*exp(im*theta)/J_{|m|+1}(lambda_mn), where lambda_mn are zeros of Bessel functions. They apply this decomposition to ensembles of 100 events each from UrQMD, MC-Glauber, and MC-KLN codes for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV at impact parameters b = 2 fm and b = 7 fm, using coefficients up to |m|_max = 8 and n_max = 8. They compute the L2 norm (total fluctuation mass), H1 Sobolev norm (roughness including gradients), M1 semi-norm (angular variation), and a scale-invariant roughness ratio R^2 = H1^2/L2^2 - 1.

## Key numbers

- The decomposition uses r0 = 10 fm, sufficient for all distributions considered.
- Events are generated for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV at two impact parameters: b = 2 fm and b = 7 fm.
- The decomposition uses coefficients with angular index m in [-8, 8] and radial index n in [1, 8].
- The minimum possible value of R^2 is (lambda_{0,1}*ell/r0)^2 approximately 0.05783, where lambda_{0,1} = 5.783063.
- For a typical UrQMD event, R^2 decreases from 1.20 (0 filtering passes) to 0.12 (4096 filtering passes) under a five-point finite difference smoothing filter.
- Ensemble average eccentricities at b = 2 fm: UrQMD epsilon_2 = 0.096 +/- 0.005, epsilon_3 = 0.079 +/- 0.004; MC-KLN epsilon_2 = 0.084 +/- 0.005, epsilon_3 = 0.046 +/- 0.003; MC-Glauber epsilon_2 = 0.089 +/- 0.005, epsilon_3 = 0.070 +/- 0.004.
- Ensemble average eccentricities at b = 7 fm: UrQMD epsilon_2 = 0.271 +/- 0.010, epsilon_3 = 0.117 +/- 0.006; MC-KLN epsilon_2 = 0.343 +/- 0.010, epsilon_3 = 0.111 +/- 0.006; MC-Glauber epsilon_2 = 0.231 +/- 0.009, epsilon_3 = 0.099 +/- 0.006.

## Context

This work extends the purely angular decomposition approach (e.g., the cumulant expansion of Teaney and Yan (2010)) by adding radial information, motivated by the finding of Gardim et al. (2011) that events with identical angular Fourier moments epsilon_2 and epsilon_3 can have dramatically different energy distributions leading to different final-state flow coefficients. The method enables systematic comparison of initial-state models (Glauber, CGC-based MC-KLN, and transport-based UrQMD) and the authors plan future work to examine how the roughness measure propagates through hydrodynamic evolution to the hadronic final state.
