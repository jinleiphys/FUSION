# Quantum statistics effects near the critical point in systems with different inter-particle interactions

> Equation of state with quantum statistics corrections is derived for systems of the Fermi and Bose particles by using their van der Waals (vdW) and effective density-dependent Skyrme mean-field interactions. First few orders of these corrections over the small quantum statistics parameter, $\varepsilon \approx \hbar^3 n(mT)^{-3/2}g^{-1}$, where $n$ and $T$ are the particle number density and temperature, $m$ and $g$ the mass and degeneracy factor of particles, are analytically obtained. For interacting system of nucleon and $α$ - particles, a small impurity of $α$ - particles to a nucleon system at leading first order in both $α$-particle and nucleon small parameters $\varepsilon$ does not change much the basic results for the symmetric nuclear matter in the quantum vdW consideration. Our approximate analytical results for the quantum vdW and Skyrme mean-field approaches are in a good agreement with accurate numerical calculations.

# Quantum statistics effects near the critical point in systems with different inter-particle interactions

## Key claim
The paper derives analytical quantum statistics corrections to the van der Waals (vdW) and Skyrme mean-field equations of state for Fermi and Bose systems, showing that for a mixed system of nucleons and a small impurity of alpha particles (mass fraction X_alpha ≈ 0.013), the first-order corrections in the small quantum statistics parameter do not significantly alter the critical point parameters compared to pure symmetric nuclear matter, provided the quantum statistics parameter ε ≈ ħ³ n (mT)^{-3/2} g^{-1} remains small (ε ≲ 0.15–0.16).

## Method
The authors expand the pressure of ideal quantum gases (Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein) in powers of the small quantum statistics parameter ε, then incorporate inter-particle interactions through the van der Waals mean-field approach (with parameters a and b) and the density-dependent Skyrme mean-field potential (with parameters a_sk, b_sk, and γ). For the two-component nucleon-alpha system, they apply a first-order expansion in ε for both components, using the vdW exclusion-volume parameters b̃_ij and attractive parameters a_ij, with the alpha-particle mass fraction X_α fixed at approximately 0.013 from thermodynamical equilibrium. The critical point parameters (temperature T_c, density n_c, pressure P_c) are obtained by solving the system of equations (∂P/∂n)_T = 0 and (∂²P/∂n²)_T = 0 at each perturbation order.

## Key numbers
- For pure nucleon matter in the classical vdW model (zero-order): T_c^(0) = 29.2 MeV, n_c^(0) = 0.100 fm⁻³, P_c^(0) = 1.09 MeV·fm⁻³
- For pure nucleon matter in the first-order QvdW approximation: T_c^(1) = 19.0 MeV, n_c^(1) = 0.065 fm⁻³, P_c^(1) = 0.48 MeV·fm⁻³
- For pure nucleon matter in the second-order QvdW approximation: T_c^(2) = 20.0 MeV, n_c^(2) = 0.079 fm⁻³, P_c^(2) = 0.56 MeV·fm⁻³
- For pure nucleon matter in the full numerical QvdW calculation: T_c = 19.7 MeV, n_c = 0.072 fm⁻³, P_c = 0.52 MeV·fm⁻³
- For the mixed nucleon+alpha system (X_α ≈ 0.013) in first-order QvdW: T_c = 19.4 MeV, n_c = 0.072 fm⁻³, P_c = 0.51 MeV·fm⁻³
- For the mixed nucleon+alpha system in full numerical QvdW: T_c = 19.9 MeV, n_c = 0.073 fm⁻³, P_c = 0.56 MeV·fm⁻³
- vdW parameters for nucleon-nucleon interaction: a_NN = 329.8 MeV·fm³, b_NN = 3.35 fm³
- Exclusion-volume parameters: b̃_αα = 16.76 fm³, b̃_αN = 13.95 fm³, b̃_Nα = 2.85 fm³
- Alpha particle mass fraction from thermodynamical equilibrium: X_α ≈ 0.013

## Context
This work extends the analytical quantum statistics correction method developed by Fedotkin, Magner, and Grygoriev (2019) for one-component nuclear matter to include both Fermi and Bose particles with vdW and Skyrme mean-field interactions. It builds upon the quantum vdW model for hadronic matter proposed by Marik (2018) and its extension to multicomponent systems by Vovchenko (2017), as well as the Skyrme mean-field approach for Bose condensates by Satarov (2019). The results enable analytical estimates of critical point shifts due to quantum statistics in mixed hadronic systems, with potential applications to particle number fluctuation studies near the nuclear matter critical point, which the authors indicate will be addressed in a separate forthcoming work.
