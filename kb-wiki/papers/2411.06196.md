# Elliptic and quadrangular flow of protons in the high baryon density region

> The collective flow provides valuable insights into the anisotropic expansion of particles produced in heavy-ion collisions and is sensitive to the equation of the state of nuclear matter in high-baryon-density regions. In this paper, we use the hadronic transport model SMASH to investigate the elliptic flow ($v_2$), quadrangular flow ($v_4$), and their ratio ($v_{4}/v_{2}^{2}$) in Au+Au collisions at high baryon density. Our results show that the inclusion of baryonic mean-field potential in the model successfully reproduces experimental data from the HADES experiment, indicating that baryonic interactions play an important role in shaping anisotropic flow. In addition to comparing the transverse momentum ($p_T$), rapidity, and centrality dependence of $v_{4}/v_{2}^{2}$ between HADES data and model calculations, we also explore its time evolution and energy dependence across $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 2.4 to 4.5 GeV. While the ratio $v_{4}/v_{2}^{2}$ for high-$p_{T}$ particles approaches 0.5, which aligns with expectations from hydrodynamic behavior, we emphasize that this result primarily reflects agreement with the HADES measurements rather than a definitive indication of ideal fluid behavior. These findings contribute to understanding the early-stage dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at high baryon density.

## Key claim

The ratio v4/v2^2 for high-pT protons at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV approaches 0.5, but the authors emphasize this reflects agreement with HADES measurements rather than a definitive indication of ideal fluid behavior, as the system dynamics are dominated by baryonic interactions rather than hydrodynamic expansion.

## Method

The authors used the hadronic transport model SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly Interacting Hadrons) to simulate Au+Au collisions at beam energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 to 4.5 GeV. They calculated elliptic flow (v2) and quadrangular flow (v4) for protons by solving the Boltzmann transport equation with a Hamiltonian that includes Coulomb, Yukawa, and mean-field potentials, using a hard equation of state with nuclear incompressibility kappa = 380 MeV. The model results with and without baryonic mean-field potential were compared to HADES experimental data for the same centrality intervals and kinematic selections.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear ground state density rho0 = 0.168 fm^-3 used in the Skyrme potential
- Hard equation of state parameters: alpha = -124 MeV, beta = 70.5 MeV, gamma = 2, corresponding to nuclear incompressibility kappa = 380 MeV
- The ratio v4/v2^2 for protons at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.1) with pT > 1.0 GeV/c approaches approximately 0.5 in 20-30% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV
- v4 value becomes very small above sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV, reaching approximately 0.001 at 4.5 GeV
- The v4/v2^2 ratio remains approximately constant at 0.5 during early stages of expansion (freeze-out time less than 15 fm/c)
- The ratio v4/v2^2 is approximately 0.5 in 10-40% central collisions, with a flat centrality dependence

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic flow at high baryon density, specifically comparing to HADES experiment measurements (HADES 2020, HADES 2022) at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV and STAR measurements (STAR 2021) at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV. The authors use a hard equation of state motivated by earlier UrQMD model studies (Hillmann 2019) that showed better agreement with HADES data than a soft EOS. The results provide baseline predictions for upcoming facilities including FAIR, NICA, and HIAF, and suggest that baryonic mean-field effects are essential for reproducing flow observables in the high-baryon-density regime, in contrast to the cascade-only approach which fails to describe the data.
