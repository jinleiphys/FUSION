# The consistent analyses for determination of the point-nucleon distributions by electron and proton scatterings

> Electron scattering cross section, as well as proton scattering cross section, observes the point-proton and the point-neutron distributions, but both cross sections are not able to determine them separately. If they are analyzed consistently with each other, there is a possibility to determine them with less ambiguity.The consistency can be examined through the moments of the charge distribution, which linearly depend on the moments of the point-proton and -neutron distributions.

## Key claim

If electron and proton scattering cross sections are analyzed consistently within the same non-relativistic framework, the point-proton and point-neutron distributions can be determined with less ambiguity than when using either probe alone, though neither probe alone can determine them separately.

## Method

The authors examine consistency between electron and proton scattering analyses through the moments of the charge distribution, which depend linearly on the moments of the point-proton and point-neutron distributions. They apply linear regression analysis (least squares analysis) using a set of non-relativistic mean-field models to compare experimental moments of the charge distribution with model predictions, deriving least squares line values for the moments of the point-nucleon distributions.

## Key numbers

- The fourth moment of the charge distribution in 208Pb observed in electron scattering is ⟨r⁴⟩_c = 1171.981 fm⁴
- The mean square radius of the charge distribution in 208Pb from electron scattering is ⟨r²⟩_c = 30.283 fm²
- The least squares analysis on the non-relativistic mean-field models with respect to the fourth moment (LSA(4)) gives δR = 0.160 fm for 208Pb
- The least squares analysis on the relativistic mean-field models with respect to the fourth moment (RLSA(4)) gives δR = 0.279 fm for 208Pb
- The point-proton mean square radius from LSA(4) is ⟨r²⟩_p = 29.738 fm²
- The point-neutron mean square radius from LSA(4) is ⟨r²⟩_n = 31.507 fm²
- The proton scattering analysis (p,p) gives δR = 0.190 fm for 208Pb
- The root mean square radius of the charge distribution in 208Pb is √⟨r²⟩_c = 5.503 fm
- The single-proton charge distribution mean square radius used is r²_p = (0.877)² = 0.769 fm²
- The single-neutron charge distribution mean square radius used is r²_n = -0.116 fm²

## Context

This work extends the approach of Refs. [rein0, roca] who proposed performing linear regression analysis using a set of mean-field models, but applies it specifically to the consistent determination of point-nucleon distributions from both electron and proton scattering. The analysis builds on the non-relativistic framework used in earlier proton scattering analyses by Ray (1979) and collaborators, and on the relationship between charge distribution moments and point-nucleon distribution moments established in Refs. [kss, ks1]. The results enable comparison between experimental determinations of the neutron skin thickness in 208Pb and predictions from non-relativistic mean-field models, showing that the framework choice (relativistic vs. non-relativistic) leads to differences of about 0.12 fm in the extracted δR value.
