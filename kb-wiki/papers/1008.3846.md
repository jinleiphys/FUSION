# Medium-modified Jets and Initial State Fluctuations as Sources of Charge Correlations Measured at RHIC

> We investigate the contribution of medium-modified jets and initial state fluctuations to the asymmetry in charged particle production with respect to the reaction plane. This asymmetry has been suggested as a compelling signature of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in QCD and make a study of "conventional" scenarios for the creation of such charged particle multiplicity fluctuations a timely endeavor. The different pathlength combinations of jets through the medium in non-central heavy ion collisions result in finite correlations of like and different charged particles emitted in the different hemispheres. Our calculation is based on combining jet events from YaJEM (Yet another Jet Energy Loss Model) and a bulk medium evolution. It is found that the jet production probabilities are too small to observe this effect. The influence of initial state fluctuations on this observable is explored using an event-by-event (3+1)d hybrid approach that is based on UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) with an ideal hydrodynamic evolution. In this calculation momentum conservation and elliptic flow are explicitly taken into account. The asymmetries in the initial state are translated to a final state momentum asymmetry by the hydrodynamic flow profile. Dependent on the size of the initial state fluctuations the resulting charged particle asymmetries are in qualitative agreement with the preliminary STAR results. The multi-particle correlation as proposed by the PHENIX collaboration can in principle be used to disentangle the different contributions, however is in practice substantially affected by the procedure to subtract trivial resonance decay contributions.

## Key claim
Medium-modified jets and initial state fluctuations can generate charged particle asymmetries with respect to the reaction plane that mimic a Chiral Magnetic Effect signal, but jet production probabilities are too small to observe this effect, while initial state fluctuations produce asymmetries in qualitative agreement with preliminary STAR results only if the granularity is sufficiently large.

## Method
The authors combined jet events from the YaJEM (Yet another Jet Energy Loss Model) in-medium shower evolution code with a 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic bulk medium evolution to compute charged particle asymmetry coefficients. Separately, they used an event-by-event (3+1)d hybrid approach based on UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) with ideal hydrodynamic evolution, varying the Gaussian width of initial energy density distributions from 0.8 to 2.0 fm to study the effect of initial state fluctuations on the same observables.

## Key numbers
- The opposite charge asymmetry coefficients from initial state fluctuations are on the order of 1e-3 for mid-central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200A GeV, in qualitative agreement with preliminary STAR results.
- The pure hadronic transport (UrQMD) background yields A^{+/-} coefficients on the order of 1e-6, two orders of magnitude smaller than the STAR data.
- The difference between up-down and in-plane asymmetry coefficients from initial state fluctuations is constrained to be smaller than 1e-3 within the limited statistics.
- Three different initial parton energies were used for jet studies: 10, 20, and 40 GeV per jet, embedded into Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200A GeV.
- The Gaussian width for initial state fluctuations was varied from sigma_Gauss = 0.8 fm to 2.0 fm, with hydrodynamic starting times from 0.3 to 1.8 fm depending on the fluctuation size.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of conventional backgrounds to the Chiral Magnetic Effect signal at RHIC, following earlier estimates by Pratt (2010) and Schlichting (2010) that charge and momentum conservation overlaid with elliptic flow could describe observed phenomena. The study uses the same event-by-event hybrid approach previously applied to ridge correlations by Takahashi (2009). The results enable quantitative constraints on initial state granularity from correlation measurements and demonstrate that resonance decay subtraction procedures for the PHENIX multi-particle correlation observable can qualitatively affect the extracted signal shape.
