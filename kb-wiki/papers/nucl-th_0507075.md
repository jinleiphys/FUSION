# Renormalization of NN Interaction with Chiral Two Pion Exchange Potential. Non-Central Phases

> We extend the renormalization of the NN interaction with Chiral Two Pion Exchange Potential to the calculation of non-central partial wave phase shifts with total angular momentum j<=5 . The short distance singularity structure of the potential as well as the requirement of orthogonality conditions on the wave functions determines exactly the number of undetermined parameters after renormalization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that renormalization of the NN interaction with chiral Two Pion Exchange Potential (TPE) can be extended to non-central partial waves up to total angular momentum j <= 5, with the short-distance singularity structure of the potential and orthogonality conditions on wave functions determining exactly how many undetermined parameters (counterterms) remain after renormalization, contrary to naive dimensional power counting assumptions which would predict a different number.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation in coordinate space for the NN system using the chiral TPE potential at LO, NLO, and NNLO, implementing short-distance boundary conditions that enforce orthogonality of wave functions at different energies. They determine the number of independent parameters (scattering lengths) required for each partial wave by analyzing the attractive or repulsive nature of the potential eigenvalues at short distances, then compute phase shifts by integrating the Schrodinger equation from a short-distance cutoff radius (0.1-0.2 fm) to large distances (15 fm) and matching to asymptotic free-wave solutions.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts are computed for all partial waves with total angular momentum j <= 5, covering 27 partial waves
- The short-distance cutoff radius is taken in the range 0.1-0.2 fm to check for cutoff independence
- Integration is performed from large distances of 15 fm for high partial waves
- Four sets of chiral low-energy constants (c1, c3, c4) are considered: Set I from pi-N scattering (c1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c3 = -4.69 GeV^-1, c4 = 3.40 GeV^-1), Set II from NN data (c1 = -0.76 GeV^-1, c3 = -5.08 GeV^-1, c4 = 4.70 GeV^-1), Set III from NN data (c1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c3 = -3.40 GeV^-1, c4 = 3.40 GeV^-1), and Set IV from NN data (c1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c3 = -3.20 GeV^-1, c4 = 5.40 GeV^-1)
- For the ^3S1-^3D1 channel, the deuteron binding energy gamma = 0.231605 fm^-1 is used as input for the TPE(B) calculation
- Scattering lengths for all partial waves are taken from NijmII and Reid93 potentials, e.g., ^1S0 scattering length = -23.727 fm (NijmII) or -23.735 fm (Reid93)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous renormalization program for central waves (Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola, 2005) to all non-central partial waves up to j=5, building on the chiral NN potential framework originally proposed by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and first implemented by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1995). The results confirm the findings of Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) for the OPE case regarding the need for additional counterterms beyond Weinberg's power counting, but go beyond by providing fully renormalized TPE amplitudes. The work enables future studies of power counting in chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces and provides model-independent long-range correlations for NN scattering.
