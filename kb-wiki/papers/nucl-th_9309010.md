# Two-body matrix elements of Pauli-projected excentric single-particle orbits

> We present explicit analytical formulae for two-body matrix elements between Pauli-projected single-particle orbits generated by gaussians shifted from the centre of a nuclear core, which is populated by nucleons occupying harmonic-oscillator orbits. Such shifted gaussian orbits appear in the generator-coordinate model of two-cluster systems and, as ingredients of generating functions, in the resonating group model as well. A Pauli projection is to be understood as a projection onto the subspace not occupied by the core nucleons. This is an exact procedure to take into account the Pauli principle in the limit in which the core is infinitely heavy, thus the procedure to be presented is very useful in the microscopic description of large-core plus small-cluster systems, such as involved in $α$-decay problems.

## Key claim
The paper presents explicit analytical formulae for two-body matrix elements between Pauli-projected single-particle orbits generated by gaussians shifted from the centre of a nuclear core, under the condition that the core is infinitely heavy and populated by nucleons occupying harmonic-oscillator orbits. The method is exact within this infinite-mass limit and avoids infinite summations that would arise from expanding the gaussians in harmonic-oscillator functions.

## Method
The author considers a two-cluster system where a heavy core (A nucleons) is described by a harmonic-oscillator shell-model Slater determinant, and a light cluster (a nucleons, at most four) is described by 0s harmonic-oscillator orbits around a centre displaced from the core centre. The Pauli principle is enforced by projecting the single-particle states of the light cluster onto the subspace orthogonal to the occupied core states, using the operator (1 - P). The two-body matrix element of a gaussian form-factor potential between such Pauli-projected states is expanded into 16 terms, each involving combinations of harmonic-oscillator and shifted gaussian functions. The integrals are evaluated analytically using generating functions for the harmonic-oscillator states, leading to finite-sum expressions in terms of the shift vectors s and s'.

## Context
This work extends the generator-coordinate model (GCM) and resonating group model (RGM) treatments of two-cluster systems, specifically for core-plus-alpha systems such as those involved in alpha-decay problems. The method builds on earlier work by Horiuchi (1977) on the GCM and by Kamimura (1977) on transformations to harmonic-oscillator bases. The Pauli-projected orbits are related to shell-model valence orbits, enabling cluster configurations to be treated similarly to ordinary shell-model configurations, though requiring diagonalization over a non-orthogonal basis. The technique has been applied in alpha-decay descriptions by Wild (1985) and Varga (1992, 1993). The paper enables efficient numerical calculation of two-body matrix elements without infinite summations, facilitating microscopic descriptions of large-core plus small-cluster systems.
