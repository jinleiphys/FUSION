# Three-Body System with Short-Range Interactions

> Within the framework of non-relativistic scalar effective field theory it is shown that the problem of the cutoff dependence of the leading order amplitude for a particle scattering off a two-body bound state can be solved without introducing three-body forces.

## Key claim

Within non-relativistic scalar effective field theory (EFT), the ultraviolet cutoff dependence of the leading-order amplitude for a particle scattering off a two-body bound state can be eliminated without introducing three-body forces, contrary to earlier claims that a three-body counter-term is necessary and sufficient.

## Method

The paper derives the Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (S-TM) integral equation for the scattering amplitude from the leading-order EFT Lagrangian of a self-interacting boson field, using a dummy "dimeron" field to rewrite the two-body interactions. The author then analyzes the asymptotic behavior of the amplitude for large momenta, finding that the general solution of the S-TM equation contains an oscillatory term proportional to \(p^{\pm i s_0}\) with \(s_0 \approx 1\), and argues that the physical amplitude corresponds to the unique solution with non-oscillating asymptotic behavior, which eliminates the arbitrary parameter.

## Key numbers

- The two-particle scattering length \(a_2\) enters the total energy via \(M E = 3k^2/4 - 1/a_2^2\).
- The asymptotic exponent \(s_0\) satisfies \(s_0 \approx 1\) from the equation \(1 - \frac{8\lambda}{\sqrt{3}} \frac{\sin(\pi s/6)}{s \cos(\pi s/2)} = 0\) for \(\lambda = 1\) (boson case).
- The kernel \(K(p,q)\) contains the factor \(4/3\) and a logarithmic term with arguments involving \(p\), \(q\), and \(M E\).

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem of cutoff sensitivity in three-body EFT calculations, previously argued by Bedaque et al. (1998) to require a leading-order three-body force. The paper extends the analysis of the S-TM equation, originally derived by Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (1957), and connects to the Thomas (1935) and Efimov (1970) effects. The result enables consistent leading-order calculations of particle-dimeron scattering without introducing additional counter-terms, simplifying the EFT treatment of three-body systems.
