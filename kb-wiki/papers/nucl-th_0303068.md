# Mass Shifts through Re-scattering

## Key claim

The paper presents a model where a mass shift in a resonance can arise from interference between a direct scattering amplitude and that same amplitude undergoing re-scattering through the resonance, with the magnitude of the shift depending on a parameter α that ranges from one (point-source re-scattering) to zero (diffractive re-scattering).

## Method

The author constructs a unitary scattering formalism for elastic ππ scattering in s-wave and p-wave partial waves, starting from a two-channel K-matrix approach (equation 4.5 of an unspecified reference). The model combines a direct production amplitude D for a di-pion system with a re-scattering amplitude A, where the re-scattering loop between the two channels includes both a real part (parameterized by α) and an imaginary part proportional to q^(2ℓ+1). The final cross section is given by the incoherent sum of the direct and re-scattered amplitudes, each modified by the ππ phase shift and a Blatt-Weisskopf barrier factor for the two-body phase space.

## Key numbers

- The interaction range parameter q₀ is 200 MeV/c, corresponding to a 1 fm interaction distance.
- The α parameter is defined as α = (1.0 - r²/r₀²), where r₀ is 1.0 fermi (the limiting range of the strong interaction).
- The two-body phase space factor PS is given by PS = (2q B_ℓ(q/q₀))/M_{ππ}, where B_ℓ is the Blatt-Weisskopf barrier factor.
- For the ρ meson (ℓ=1), the Blatt-Weisskopf barrier factor is B₁ = (q/q₀)²/(1 + (q/q₀)²).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of coupled-channel scattering (Reference 2) to describe mass shifts in resonances produced in heavy ion collisions, specifically addressing the ρ meson. The model uses ππ phase shifts from a prior source (Reference 1) and incorporates Blatt-Weisskopf barrier factors (Reference 3) for angular momentum dependence. The approach enables calculation of resonance line shapes in heavy ion collision final states where pions emerge from a close encounter and can re-scatter, with the α parameter controlling whether the re-scattering is point-like or diffractive.
