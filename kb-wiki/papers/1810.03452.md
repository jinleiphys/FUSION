# Does the bottomonium counterpart of $X(3872)$ exist?

> A narrow line shape peak at about 10615 MeV, just above the threshold in the $B\bar B^*$ channel, which can be regarded as the signal of bottomonium counterpart of $X(3872)$, $X_b$, is predicted by using the extended Friedrichs scheme. Though a virtual state is found at about 10593 MeV in this scheme, we point out that the peak is contributed mainly by the coupling form factor, which comes from the convolution of the interaction term and meson wave functions including the one from $χ_{b1}(4P)$, but not mainly by the virtual-state pole. In this picture, the reason why $X_b$ signal is not observed in the $Υπ^+π^-$ and $Υπ^+π^-π^0$ channels can also be understood. The $χ_{b1}(4P)$ mass and width are found to be about 10771 MeV and 6 MeV, respectively and a dynamically generated broad resonance is also found with its mass and width at about 10672 MeV and 78 MeV, respectively. The line shapes of these two states are also affected by the form factor effect. Thus, this study also emphasizes the importance of the structure of the wave functions of high radial excitations in the analysis of the line shapes, and provides a caveat that some signals may be generated from the structures of the form factors rather than from poles.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a narrow line-shape peak at about 10615 MeV in the \(B\bar B^*\) channel that could be interpreted as the bottomonium counterpart of \(X(3872)\), but argues that this peak is generated primarily by the coupling form factor (from the convolution of meson wave functions including \(\chi_{b1}(4P)\)) rather than by a virtual-state pole at about 10593 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the extended Friedrichs scheme, coupling the bare \(\chi_{b1}(4P)\) state (with mass from the Godfrey-Isgur model) to the \(B\bar B^*\) and \(B^*\bar B^*\) continuum channels via the quark pair creation (QPC) model. They solved for the poles of the resolvent \(\eta(z)\) on different Riemann sheets to identify bound, virtual, and resonance states, and computed the scattering amplitudes to obtain line shapes. The same \(\gamma\) parameter (about 4.0) that reproduces \(X(3872)\) in the charmonium sector was used.

## Key numbers

- A narrow peak in the \(B\bar B^*\) scattering amplitude appears at about 10615 MeV, with a rough Breit-Wigner fit giving a width of about 15 MeV.
- A virtual-state pole is found on the second Riemann sheet at \(z_v = 10593\) MeV, about 10 MeV below the \(B\bar B^*\) threshold.
- The \(\chi_{b1}(4P)\) state (originating from the bare state) has a pole at \(z_{R1} = 10771 \pm 3i\) MeV, corresponding to a mass of about 10771 MeV and width of about 6 MeV.
- A dynamically generated broad resonance from the \(B^*\bar B^*\) coupling has a pole at \(z_{R2} = 10672 \pm 39i\) MeV, corresponding to a mass of about 10672 MeV and width of about 78 MeV.
- The Godfrey-Isgur model predicts the bare \(\chi_{b1}(4P)\) mass at about 10790 MeV.
- The \(B\bar B^*\) threshold is about 10604 MeV; the \(B^*\bar B^*\) threshold opens above 10649 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the same extended Friedrichs scheme previously applied to \(X(3872)\) in the charmonium sector (Zhou 2017) to the bottomonium sector, providing a unified description. It contradicts earlier predictions of a bound \(X_b\) state from one-pion exchange (Tornqvist 1993, Swanson 2006) and from isospin exchange (Karliner 2015), which assumed a bound state below threshold. The results explain the negative experimental searches for \(X_b\) in \(\Upsilon\pi^+\pi^-\) (CMS 2013, ATLAS 2014) and \(\Upsilon\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\) (Belle 2014) by noting that the peak is in the \(B\bar B^*\) channel and that the OZI-suppressed decay channels would not show a signal. The study enables future experimental searches at SuperKEKB to focus on the \(B\bar B^*\) channel.
