# Stochastic Jet Quenching in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
Energy losses of fast color particles in a random inhomogeneous color medium are estimated, showing that stochastic fluctuations in the medium create an additional source of energy loss beyond conventional mechanisms; the calculation is performed in the quasi-abelian approximation and is reliable only in the regime where the product of fluctuation strength and correlation radius times frequency is small (sigma^2 (w r_c) << 1), with a strong dependence on the ultraviolet cutoff p that limits the result to an order-of-magnitude estimate.

## Method
The authors compute the average energy loss per unit length of an ultrarelativistic quark or gluon moving through a random inhomogeneous medium by solving the abelianized equations of motion for the chromoelectric field with a stochastic chromodielectric permittivity. The random inhomogeneities are modeled as a Gaussian ensemble with an exponential binary correlation function characterized by fluctuation magnitude sigma and correlation radius r_c; the averaged field is obtained via a Dyson equation in the one-loop approximation, yielding an effective dielectric permittivity with nontrivial imaginary parts that produce the stochastic energy loss.

## Key numbers
- Energy loss formula: dW/dz = C_{F(V)} (alpha_s / pi) (1 / r_c^2) [f_t(p r_c | sigma, epsilon_0) + f_l(p r_c | sigma, epsilon_0)], where C_{F(V)} is the quadratic Casimir for fundamental (quark) or adjoint (gluon) representations.
- For a quark with energy up to 20 GeV, alpha_s = 0.2, r_c = 0.2 fm, sigma = 0.2, and epsilon_0 = 0.9, the transverse stochastic loss can become "quite substantial" and exceed values discussed in prior work (GT91, B82); the paper provides a plot (Fig. 5) but no single numerical value in GeV/fm is given in the text.
- The one-loop approximation requires sigma^2 (w r_c) << 1, where w = sqrt(epsilon_0) omega.
- The correlation function is g(r) = sigma^2 exp(-r / r_c), with r_c the correlation radius and sigma the fluctuation magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the standard treatment of jet quenching in dense QCD matter (RHICloss, SPSloss; KW03, GVWZ03) by incorporating the effect of event-by-event spatial inhomogeneities in the partonic medium, which arise from the powerlike nature of partonic cross sections (GRZ97). The mechanism is related to scattering of Weizsacker-Williams modes of the incident particle's proper field on medium inhomogeneities, and is amplified when the effective permittivity exceeds the Cherenkov threshold (T72). The results enable future quantitative analysis of stochastic energy losses at LHC energies, but the strong dependence on the ultraviolet cutoff indicates the need for a consistent microscopic approach that unifies soft and hard mode contributions, as noted in connection with earlier work by GT91 and M91.
