# Four-dimensional QCD equation of state from a quasi-parton model with physics-informed neural networks

> The equation of state (EoS) of strongly interacting matter at finite temperature and chemical potentials (baryon, charge, and strangeness) is a crucial input for hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We construct a four-dimensional EoS using a deep-learning-assisted quasi-particle model (DLQPM) within a physics-informed neural network (PINN) framework, in which the masses of light quarks, strange quarks, and gluons are parameterized as functions of temperature and chemical potentials ($T, μ_B, μ_Q, μ_S$). The model is constrained by lattice QCD data at vanishing chemical potentials and provides a thermodynamically consistent extrapolation to finite $μ_{B,Q,S}$. The DLQPM accurately reproduces the lattice-calculated cumulants $χ^{B,Q,S}_{i,j,k}$ at $μ_{B,Q,S}=0$, and its predicted EoS at various chemical potentials agrees well with results from the generalized $T'$-expansion method in lattice QCD. Furthermore, the calculated baryon-strangeness correlation $C_{BS}$ is consistent, within uncertainties, with preliminary STAR data. This work offers a reliable EoS for exploring the QCD phase structure in the beam energy scan region.

## Key claim

The DLQPM provides a thermodynamically consistent four-dimensional QCD equation of state by using physics-informed neural networks to parameterize quasi-parton masses as functions of temperature and all three chemical potentials (baryon, charge, strangeness), but the extrapolation to the low-temperature, high-density region carries larger systematic uncertainties because the model is currently constrained only by lattice QCD data at vanishing chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors construct three separate deep neural networks (ResNets with 8 hidden layers and 32 neurons per layer) to represent the effective masses of light quarks, strange quarks, and gluons as functions of temperature and the three chemical potentials (T, mu_B, mu_Q, mu_S). These mass functions are fed into the ideal gas partition function for quarks and gluons, with one-dimensional momentum integrals evaluated via 50-point Gauss quadrature to enable backpropagation. Thermodynamic quantities (pressure, energy density, entropy density, number densities, trace anomaly) and generalized susceptibilities chi^{B,Q,S}_{i,j,k} are obtained through automatic differentiation, and the neural network parameters are optimized by minimizing a loss function that includes lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential, a mass constraint from hard-thermal-loop theory, and the condition that number densities vanish at zero chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Training data: 70 temperature points uniformly selected within the range 0.05 to 0.4 GeV at mu_B = 0, sourced from lattice simulations
- Mass constraint temperature cutoff: T_cut = 0.150 GeV
- Degeneracies: gluon degeneracy d_g = 16, quark/antiquark degeneracy d_{q(bar{q})_i} = 6
- The DLQPM results for C_BS are evaluated at beam energies sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 100 GeV for 0-5% centrality; calculations at 3 GeV are not shown due to unavailable freeze-out parameters
- The EoS is presented at mu/T values ranging from 0 to 3.5, where mu = sqrt(mu_B^2 + mu_Q^2 + mu_S^2) under the constraint mu_B/sqrt(2) = mu_Q = mu_S

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential (Borsanyi 2013, HotQCD 2018, Bollweg 2022) and the generalized T'-expansion method (Mondal 2021, Borsanyi 2021, Monnai 2024, Abuali 2025) to a full four-dimensional equation of state using a machine-learning approach. The DLQPM improves upon earlier quasi-particle models that used hard-thermal-loop parametrizations (Peshier 1995, Soloveva 2023) by removing prior biases and increasing representational flexibility. The calculated baryon-strangeness correlation C_BS is compared with preliminary STAR data (STAR-QM), lattice QCD results (Bollweg 2024), the hadron resonance gas model, and the quark-model-inspired HRG model (Bazavov 2014). The resulting EoS is intended as input for hydrodynamic simulations exploring the QCD phase structure in the beam energy scan region.
