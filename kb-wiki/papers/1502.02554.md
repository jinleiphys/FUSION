# Anisotropic Flow and Jet Quenching in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> The exploration of the strong-interaction matter under extreme conditions is one of the main goals of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We provide some of the main results on the novel properties of quark-gluon plasma, with particular focus given to the strong collectivity and the color opaqueness exhibited by such hot and dense matter produced in high-energy nuclear collisions at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that quantitative extraction of two key transport coefficients, the specific shear viscosity η/s and the scaled jet quenching parameter q̂/T³, from systematic phenomenological studies can provide a measure of the interaction strength of quark-gluon plasma, but notes that the extracted value of η/s carries an uncertainty of about a factor of 2-2.5 depending on the choice of initial condition model.

## Method

The author reviews relativistic viscous hydrodynamics simulations (primarily the VISHNU and IP-Glasma+MUSIC models) that solve the conservation equation for the energy-momentum tensor with Israel-Stewart theory for viscous corrections, using initial conditions from MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, and IP-Glasma models. The hydrodynamic evolution is coupled to the Cooper-Frye formalism to obtain final-state particle spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients. For jet quenching, the paper discusses parton energy loss models where hard partons interact with the QGP medium via elastic and inelastic collisions.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the QCD phase transition from lattice QCD is T_c ∼ 155 MeV (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2011, Bhattacharya 2014)
- The highest temperatures reached at RHIC and LHC are ∼ 370-470 MeV (Heinz 2013, Gale 2013, Song 2013, Romatschke 2009, Huovinen 2013)
- The specific shear viscosity used in VISHNU calculations for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV is η/s = 0.16, with relaxation time τ_π = 3η/(sT) and initial time τ_0 = 0.9 fm/c (Song 2013)
- The best description of anisotropic flow data gives average η/s = 0.12 at RHIC (200 AGeV Au-Au) and η/s = 0.2 at the LHC (2.76 ATeV Pb-Pb) from IP-Glasma+MUSIC calculations (Schenke 2010)
- The initial eccentricity ε_2 from MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models differs by about 20%, leading to a factor of 2-2.5 uncertainty in extracted η/s (Song 2010)

## Context

This review extends the program of extracting transport coefficients from heavy-ion collision data that began with the discovery of strong collective flow at RHIC (Gyulassy 2004, Adcox 2004, Arsene 2004, Back 2004, Adams 2005) and the successful application of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (Rischke 1995, Kolb 2000, Teaney 2000, Huovinen 2001, Romatschke 2007). The work builds on lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2011) and initial condition models including MC-Glauber (Miller 2003), MC-KLN (Drescher 2006), and IP-Glasma (Schenke 2012). The paper enables future precise determination of the temperature dependence of η/s(T) and the jet quenching parameter, and motivates systematic comparisons across collision systems (p-p, p-Pb, d-Au, ³He-Au, and A-A) to disentangle initial geometry from final-state viscosity effects.
