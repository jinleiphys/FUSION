# Finite width effects on the azimuthal asymmetry in proton-nucleus collisions in the Color Glass Condensate

> We perform a numerical analysis of the two particle azimuthal correlations at central rapidities generated in $p$A collisions within the framework of the Color Glass Condensate. We extend the standard computations to include the subeikonal corrections which stem from considering the finite longitudinal width of the dense target. For practical reasons, we only consider the next-to-next-to-eikonal corrections instead of using the all-order expressions for the inclusive two gluon production cross section. We show that the subeikonal terms that we account for in the two gluon yields contribute to both even and odd harmonics, the latter being absent in the standard Color Glass Condensate calculations performed at eikonal accuracy. Our analysis confirms the vanishing of the subeikonal effects with increasing collision energy and when going to forward rapidities, as expected.

## Key claim

The authors show that including subeikonal corrections from a finite longitudinal target width in the Color Glass Condensate framework generates non-zero odd azimuthal harmonics (specifically \(v_3\)) in proton-nucleus collisions, which are absent in standard eikonal calculations; however, the analysis is restricted to next-to-next-to-eikonal (NNE) accuracy and is valid only when the non-eikonal parameter \(\epsilon_i \lesssim 1\), corresponding to transverse momenta \(p_\perp \gtrsim 1\) GeV and center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s} \gtrsim 50\) GeV.

## Method

The authors compute the two-gluon production cross section in pA collisions within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, extending beyond the eikonal (shockwave) approximation by including a finite longitudinal target width \(L^+\). They use the Area Enhancement model and the harmonic oscillator approximation to solve the generalized dipole functions, then expand the two-gluon yield to second order in the non-eikonal parameter \(\epsilon_i = Q_s^2 L^+ / (2 k_i^+)\) (the next-to-next-to-eikonal order). The azimuthal harmonics \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) are extracted from the Fourier decomposition of the two-particle spectrum, with an infrared regulator \(m_g = 0.4\) GeV applied to handle divergences.

## Key numbers

* The non-eikonal parameter is defined as \(\epsilon_i = Q_s^2 L^+ / (2 k_i^+)\), and in the center-of-mass frame it scales as \(\epsilon_i \sim 10 \, Q_s^2 A^{1/3} / (|{\bf k}_i| \sqrt{s}) \, e^{-\eta_i}\).
* The analysis is valid for \(p_\perp \gtrsim 1\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s} \gtrsim 50\) GeV, where \(\epsilon_i \lesssim 1\); the relative difference between the NNE expansion and the full non-eikonal single-particle spectrum is smaller than 20% in the kinematic region studied.
* The azimuthal harmonics are computed for \(p_\perp^{\rm ref} = 1\) GeV, with the projectile gluonic transverse area fixed to \(B_p = 4\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* For a target with \(Q_s = 1\) GeV and \(A^{1/3} = 6\), the odd harmonic \(v_3\) is non-zero in the non-eikonal case and decreases with increasing collision energy (e.g., comparing \(\sqrt{s} = 50, 70, 200\) GeV) and with increasing pseudorapidity.

## Context

This work extends the numerical study of finite-width target effects on two-particle azimuthal correlations from pp collisions (Agostini 2019) to pA collisions, using the formalism developed by the same authors (Agostini 2022) that shows equivalence to the standard approach for jet quenching. The results confirm that odd azimuthal harmonics, absent in standard eikonal CGC calculations, arise from subeikonal corrections and vanish at high energies and forward rapidities, consistent with expectations from previous work on pp collisions (Agostini 2019). The analysis is applicable to the low-energy regime of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, and the authors plan to extend it to eA collisions for the Electron Ion Collider.
