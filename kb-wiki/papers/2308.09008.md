# Probing the impact of Delta-Baryons on Nuclear Matter and Non-Radial Oscillations in Neutron Stars

> The presence of heavy baryons, such as $Δ$-baryons and hyperons can significantly impact various properties of Neutron Stars (NSs), like oscillation frequencies, dimensionless tidal deformability, mass, and radii. We explored these effects within the Density-Dependent Relativistic Mean Field formalism. Our analysis considered $Δ$-admixed NS matter in both hypernuclear and hyperon-free scenarios, providing insights into particle compositions and their effects on NS properties. Our study of non-radial $f$-mode oscillations revealed a distinct increase in frequency due to the additional baryons. The degree of increase was significantly influenced by the meson-baryon coupling strengths. Notably, the coupling between $Δ$-resonances and $σ$-mesons played a highly influential role. In some cases, it led to an approximately 20\% increase in the $f$-mode oscillation frequency of canonical NSs. These couplings also affect other bulk properties of NSs, including mass, radii, and dimensionless tidal deformability ($Λ$). Comparing our results with available observational data from pulsars (NICER) and gravitational waves (LIGO-VIRGO collaboration), we found strong agreement, particularly concerning $Λ$.

## Key claim

The inclusion of Δ-baryons in neutron star matter, within the Density-Dependent Relativistic Mean Field formalism, can increase the non-radial f-mode oscillation frequency by up to approximately 20% for canonical neutron stars, but the magnitude of this increase depends strongly on the specific values chosen for the meson-Δ coupling strengths, particularly the σ-Δ coupling.

## Method

The authors used the DD-MEX density-dependent relativistic mean field model to compute equations of state for neutron star matter containing nucleons, leptons, hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ), and Δ-baryons (Δ⁺⁺, Δ⁺, Δ⁰, Δ⁻). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and computed the dimensionless tidal deformability and non-radial f-mode oscillation frequencies using the Cowling approximation, varying the meson-Δ coupling ratios x_{σΔ} (0.8–1.2), x_{ωΔ} (1.0–1.1), and x_{ρΔ} (0.5–1.5) while fixing hyperon couplings via SU(6) symmetry and empirical hyperon potential depths.

## Key numbers

- The f-mode oscillation frequency of canonical neutron stars (1.4 M⊙) can increase by approximately 20% due to the presence of Δ-baryons, depending on the meson-Δ coupling strengths.
- The Δ⁻ baryon is the first Δ species to appear, emerging on average near 2 n₀ (twice nuclear saturation density) in Δ-admixed matter.
- The nucleon effective mass m_N* decreases to zero at baryon densities above 4.5 n₀ for certain combinations of meson-Δ couplings.
- The dimensionless tidal deformability Λ at 1.4 M⊙ decreases when x_{σΔ} is increased, with values falling below the nucleon+hyperon-only case for x_{σΔ} > 1.0.
- The DD-MEX model uses coupling constants g_{σN}=10.7067, g_{ωN}=13.3388, g_{ρN}=7.2380 at saturation density n₀, with meson masses m_σ=547.3327 MeV, m_ω=783 MeV, m_ρ=763 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Δ-baryons in neutron stars by Marquez et al. (2022) and Issifu et al. (2023) by computing, for the first time, non-radial f-mode oscillations in Δ-admixed hypernuclear and hyperon-free matter. It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" by showing that including Δ-baryons with appropriate couplings can soften the equation of state enough to satisfy tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 (Λ < 720 at 1.4 M⊙) while maintaining maximum masses consistent with observations of massive pulsars. The results enable future asteroseismology studies using gravitational wave observations to constrain the presence of Δ-baryons in neutron star interiors.
