# Conformality Lost in Efimov Physics

> A general mechanism for the loss of conformal invariance is the merger and disappearance of an infrared fixed point and an ultraviolet fixed point of a renormalization group flow. We show explicitly how this mechanism works in the case of identical bosons at unitarity as the spatial dimension $d$ is varied. For $d$ between the critical dimensions $d_{\rm 1}=2.30$ and $d_{\rm 2}=3.76$, there is loss of conformality as evidenced by the Efimov effect in the three-body sector. The beta function for an appropriate three-body coupling is a quadratic polynomial in that coupling. For $d<d_{\rm 1}$ and for $d>d_{\rm 2}$, the beta function has two real roots that correspond to infrared and ultraviolet fixed points. As $d$ approaches $d_{\rm 1}$ from below and as $d$ approaches $d_{\rm 2}$ from above, the fixed points merge and disappear into the complex plane. For $d_{\rm 1}<d<d_{\rm 2}$, the beta function has complex roots and the renormalization group flow for the three-body coupling is a limit cycle.

## Key claim

The paper shows explicitly that the loss of conformal invariance in Efimov physics for identical bosons at unitarity occurs via the merger and disappearance of an infrared and an ultraviolet fixed point of the renormalization group flow for a three-body coupling, using the spatial dimension \(d\) as the external control parameter.

## Method

The authors analyze the renormalization group flow of a dimensionless three-body coupling \(\hat{G}\) for identical bosons in \(d\) spatial dimensions at unitarity, using the Bedaque-Hammer-van Kolck effective field theory with an auxiliary diatom field. They derive the beta function for \(\hat{G}\) from the asymptotic solutions of the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation for the s-wave atom-diatom amplitude, obtaining a quadratic polynomial in \(\hat{G}\) whose coefficients depend on \(d\) through a scaling exponent \(s^2\) determined by a transcendental equation.

## Key numbers

* The lower critical dimension for the Efimov effect is \(d_1 = 2.30\) and the upper critical dimension is \(d_2 = 3.76\); for \(d_1 < d < d_2\) the beta function has complex roots and conformality is lost.
* At \(d = 3\), the discrete scaling factor for the Efimov effect is \(e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 22.7\), where \(s_0 = 1.00624\).
* The ratio of binding energies of successive Efimov states in three dimensions approaches a universal factor of approximately \(515 \approx 22.7^2\).
* The lowest branch of \(s^2\) as a function of \(d\) reaches a minimum of \(-1.016\) at \(d = 3.04\).

## Context

This work explicitly realizes the general mechanism for loss of conformality proposed by Kaplan, Lee, Son, and Stephanov (2009) in the context of Efimov physics, which they had suggested but not demonstrated. It extends prior work by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) and Braaten and Hammer on the renormalization group limit cycle for three-body couplings in three dimensions, and by Nielsen (2001) on critical dimensions for the Efimov effect. The results enable interpolation in spatial dimension \(d\) using epsilon expansions around the critical dimensions 2.30 and 3.76, which could be applied to the unitary Bose gas in three dimensions.
