# The reaction ${n} {p} \to {p} {p} π^{-}$ from threshold up to 570 MeV

> The reaction ${n} {p} \to {p} {p} π^{-}$ has been studied in a kinematically complete measurement with a large acceptance time-of-flight spectrometer for incident neutron energies between threshold and 570 MeV. The proton-proton invariant mass distributions show a strong enhancement due to the pp($^{1}{S}_{0}$) final state interaction. A large anisotropy was found in the pion angular distributions in contrast to the reaction ${p}{p} \to {p}{p} π^{0}$. At small energies, a large forward/backward asymmetry has been observed. From the measured integrated cross section $σ({n}{p} \to {\rm p}{p} π^{-})$, the isoscalar cross section $σ_{01}$ has been extracted. Its energy dependence indicates that mainly partial waves with Sp final states contribute. Note: Due to a coding error, the differential cross sections ${d σ}/{d M_{pp}}$ as shown in Fig. 9 are too small by a factor of two, and inn Table 3 the differential cross sections ${d σ}/{d Ω_π^{*}}$ are too large by a factor of $10/2π$. The integrated cross sections and all conclusions remain unchanged. A corresponding erratum has been submitted and accepted by European Physics Journal.

## Key claim

The reaction n p → p p π⁻ has been measured from threshold to 570 MeV, and the isoscalar cross section σ₀₁ has been extracted from the integrated cross section; however, the extraction of σ₀₁ includes a principal model dependence because isospin invariance is only approximate, and the comparison of cross sections cannot be performed at the same beam energy due to different particle masses.

## Method

A kinematically complete measurement was performed using a large acceptance time-of-flight spectrometer with a polarized neutron beam at PSI. The experiment used a liquid hydrogen target, two sets of drift chambers for tracking, a segmented trigger hodoscope, and a large scintillator TOF wall. Events were reconstructed using a kinematical fit of at least six measured observables from 2-prong and 3-prong events, with particle velocities determined from time-of-flight measurements between the hodoscope and TOF wall.

## Key numbers

- Incident neutron energies from threshold (approximately 287 MeV for this reaction) up to 570 MeV
- Neutron beam flux of several 10⁷ n/s for a typical proton beam current of 10 μA
- Neutron beam diameter of 4 cm (FWHM) at 20 m downstream of the production target
- Drift chamber DC8 spatial resolution σ = 0.2 mm for track angles of 0°, increasing to 0.5 mm at 40°
- Average detection efficiency per plane: 97% for DC8, 95% for BD6
- Time resolution of 50 ps for the TQC modules
- Typical triggered event rate of 260 events per second with average event length of 250 Bytes
- About 6 × 10⁷ events recorded with transversely polarized neutrons for spin averaged results
- Vertex resolution of approximately 3 mm

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing uncertainty in the isoscalar cross section σ₀₁, which previous experiments had reported as significant (Hand et al., Rush et al., Dzhgarkava et al., Kleinschmidt et al., Rapperport et al.) or negligible (Thomas et al., Dakhno et al., Tsukamoto et al.) for energies below 600-750 MeV. The measurement improves upon earlier np cross section data that suffered from averaging over large neutron beam energy ranges, and enables better constraints on theoretical models including meson-exchange calculations from the Jülich and Osaka groups and chiral perturbation theory calculations.
