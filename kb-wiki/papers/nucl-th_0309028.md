# Isovector Collective Response Function of Nuclear Matter at Finite Temperature

> We study isovector collective excitations in nuclear matter by employing the linearized Landau-Vlasov equation with and without a non-Markovian binary collision term at finite temperature. We calculate the giant dipole resonance (GDR) strength function for finite nuclei using Steinwedel-Jensen model and also by Thomas-Fermi approximation, and we compare them for 120Sn and 208Pb with experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including a non-Markovian binary collision term in the linearized Landau-Vlasov equation improves the agreement of calculated giant dipole resonance (GDR) strength functions with experimental data for finite nuclei, but only when the calculation is performed using the Thomas-Fermi approximation for finite nuclear matter rather than the infinite nuclear matter Steinwedel-Jensen model.

## Method

The author solves the linearized Landau-Vlasov equation with and without a non-Markovian binary collision term to compute the isovector response function of nuclear matter at finite temperature. The GDR strength function is calculated for finite nuclei using two approaches: the Steinwedel-Jensen model (which treats the nucleus as a sphere with constant density) and the Thomas-Fermi approximation (which uses a Wood-Saxon potential to determine the nuclear density profile). The collisional response function is evaluated numerically by reducing twelve-dimensional integrals to five-fold integrals using total and relative momentum transformations, with the angular anisotropy of the nucleon-nucleon cross section neglected by using an isotropic cross section of 40 mb.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of infinite nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- For \(^{120}\)Sn: nuclear radius \(R = 5.6\) fm, wave number \(k = 0.28\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- For \(^{208}\)Pb: nuclear radius \(R = 6.7\) fm, wave number \(k = 0.23\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Symmetry energy coefficient: \(a_\tau = 28\) MeV, giving isovector Landau parameter \(F'_0(T=0) = 1.33\)
- Wood-Saxon potential depth: \(V_0 = 44\) MeV, thickness parameter \(t_p = 0.67\) fm
- Skyrme force parameters: \(t_0 = -983.4\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\), \(t_3 = 13106\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^6\), \(x_0 = 0.48\)
- Isotropic neutron-proton cross section: \(\sigma_{pn} = 40\) mb

## Context

This work extends previous studies of GDR strength functions in infinite nuclear matter using the extended time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory with a non-Markovian collision term (Ayik et al., 2003) by applying the same formalism within a semi-classical Landau-Vlasov framework. The paper contrasts the Steinwedel-Jensen model, which assumes constant nuclear surface and density oscillations obeying a wave equation, with the Thomas-Fermi approximation that provides a more realistic treatment of finite nuclei. The results enable future inclusion of the coherent damping mechanism (Ormand, Bortignon, and Broglia, 1996) to further improve the description of the temperature dependence of the GDR width.
