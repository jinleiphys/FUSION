# Systematic shell-model study of structure and isomeric states in $^{204-213}$Bi isotopes

> In this work, we have performed systematic shell-model calculations for Bi isotopes with $A=$ 204-213 using KHH7B and KHM3Y effective interactions. We have reported yrast and non-yrast shell-model states corresponding to the available experimental data. From the comparison with the experimental data, we could assign spin and parity of several unconfirmed states. We have also calculated electromagnetic properties and compared them with the available experimental data and predicted where experimental data are not available. This study also includes a detailed discussion of multiple isomeric states based on computed shell-model configurations and their respective half-lives.

## Key claim

The authors perform systematic shell-model calculations for bismuth isotopes with mass numbers 204–213 using two effective interactions (KHH7B and KHM3Y), but the results are limited by model-space truncations, for example, the KHH7B interaction cannot reproduce the 8⁺₁ state in ²⁰⁸Bi because it lacks the ν(f₇/₂) orbital, and positive-parity states in odd-A isotopes are overpredicted because proton orbitals below Z=82 (such as s₁/₂ and d₃/₂) are excluded from the model space.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed using the KHH7B interaction (14 orbitals) and the KHM3Y interaction (24 orbitals), with the KSHELL and NUSHELLX codes respectively. For ²⁰⁴–²⁰⁸Bi, valence neutrons were restricted to orbitals below N=126; for ²¹⁰–²¹³Bi, valence neutrons occupied orbitals above N=126; and for ²⁰⁹Bi, 1p–1h excitations across N=126 were applied. The authors compared calculated yrast and non-yrast energy levels, electromagnetic transition probabilities (B(E2), B(M1)), mixing ratios, and half-lives of isomeric states against available experimental data, and assigned spin-parity to several previously unconfirmed states.

## Key numbers

* ²⁰⁴Bi: ground state 6⁺ is reproduced by KHH7B interaction only; the 10⁻₁ state has configuration π(h₉/₂¹) ⊗ ν(f₅/₂⁴p₃/₂⁴i₁₃/₂¹³) with 49.12% probability.
* ²⁰⁶Bi: experimental mixing ratios δ(E2/M1) for 14⁻₁→13⁻₁ and 18⁺₁→17⁺₁ are −0.14(3) and −0.15(5); shell-model values are −0.09 and −0.05 respectively.
* ²⁰⁸Bi: experimental B(E2;6⁺₁→4⁺₁) = 0.10(3) W.u.; shell-model value is 0.1 W.u. using KHH7B.
* ²¹⁰Bi: the 1⁻ state is the lowest-lying state experimentally; the 0⁻₁–9⁻₁ states are all well reproduced with dominant configuration π(h₉/₂¹)⊗ν(g₉/₂¹).
* ²¹²Bi: experimental lower limits of B(M1) from 1⁻₂ to 0⁻₁ and 1⁻_g.s. are 6.444×10⁻³ and 1.074×10⁻⁴ μ_N²; KHH7B gives 1.847×10⁻² and 1.306×10⁻³ μ_N².
* ²¹³Bi: shell-model B(E2;7/2⁻₁→9/2⁻₁) = 0.2 W.u. (no experimental value available).
* ²⁰⁵Bi: shell-model B(M1;17/2⁻₁→15/2⁻₁) = 0.055 μ_N² (no experimental value available).

## Context

This work extends systematic shell-model studies of the lead region previously performed by the same group for Pb and Po isotopes (Shukla et al., 2023–2024), and builds on earlier calculations by McGrory and Kuo (1970s) and more recent work by Yanase and Lonnroth. The results provide theoretical assignments for unconfirmed spin-parity states and predictions for electromagnetic transitions and half-lives that can guide ongoing and future experiments at RIKEN, GSI/FAIR, and CERN, particularly for beta-decay measurements in the Bi isotopic chain.
