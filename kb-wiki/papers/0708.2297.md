# Densities, Parton Distributions, and Measuring the Non-Spherical Shape of the Nucleon

> Spin-dependent quark densities, matrix elements of specific density operators in proton states of definite spin-polarization, indicate that the nucleon may harbor an infinite variety of non-spherical shapes. We show that these matrix elements are closely related to specific transverse momentum dependent parton distributions accessible in the angular dependence of the semi-inclusive processes electron plus proton goes to electron plus pion plus anything, and the Drell-Yan reaction proton plus proton goes to a lepton anti-lepton pair plus anything. New measurements or analyses would allow the direct exhibition of the non-spherical nature of the proton.

# Densities, Parton Distributions, and Measuring the Non-Spherical Shape of the Nucleon

## Key claim

The non-spherical shape of the nucleon can be revealed through measurements of the transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution function h_{1T}^\perp, which is related to spin-dependent quark densities in the proton rest frame. The author shows that while the abstract states this as a general possibility, the actual connection requires integrating over longitudinal momentum and relies on specific relations between rest-frame density operators and parton correlation functions that hold only under certain kinematic approximations.

## Method

The author defines spin-dependent density operators in both non-relativistic and relativistic forms, then integrates them over longitudinal momentum to obtain transverse densities. These transverse densities are related to specific transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions from the framework of Mulders and Tangerman (1996) by evaluating the parton correlation functions in the nucleon rest frame and integrating over the light-cone momentum fraction x. The functions g_T^\perp and h_{1T}^\perp are identified as the quantities that encode the non-spherical shape, with h_{1T}^\perp being accessible at leading twist in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the proton's electric and magnetic form factors G_E/G_M falls with increasing momentum transfer Q^2 for 1 < Q^2 < 6 GeV^2, indicating non-vanishing orbital angular momentum of quarks.
- Deformation of the transverse nucleon shape is visible for transverse momentum values as small as K_T = 0.25 GeV, and increases as K_T increases up to 2.0 GeV.
- The spectator model of Jakob et al. (1997) yields the relation that the x-integrated functions satisfy \tilde{g}_T = -\tilde{h}^\perp_{1T}, though this is not a general result.
- The transverse shape formula involves a term proportional to (K_T^2 / M^2) * cos(2\phi - \phi_n) * (\tilde{h}_{1T}^\perp / \tilde{f}_1), where M is the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Miller, 2003) of spin-dependent density operators in the proton rest frame, which used the relativistic constituent quark model of Frank et al. (1996) to show that such densities reveal non-spherical shapes. The paper connects these rest-frame densities to the experimentally accessible TMD framework of Mulders and Tangerman (1996), specifically to the function h_{1T}^\perp that appears in the cross section for semi-inclusive leptoproduction (Boer, 1997) and in the Drell-Yan process with transversely polarized protons (Boer, 1999). The approach enables future measurements of the angular dependence of ep -> e\pi X and pp -> l\bar{l}X reactions to directly exhibit the non-spherical nature of the proton.
