# The role of the Heisenberg principle in Constrained Molecular Dynamics model

> We implement the Heisenberg principle into the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model with a similar approach to the Pauli principle using the one-body occupation probability $\bar{f}_i$. Results of the modified and the original model with comparisons to data are given. The binding energies and the radii of light nuclei obtained with the modified model are more consistent to the experimental data than the original one. The collision term and the density distribution are tested through a comparison to p+$^{12}$C elastic scattering data. Some simulations for fragmentation and superheavy nuclei production are also discussed.

## Key claim
Implementing the Heisenberg principle as an additional constraint in the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model improves the binding energies and radii of light nuclei (Z ≤ 4) compared to the original model, but the improvement is conditional: the Heisenberg principle becomes less important than the Pauli principle for heavy nuclei, and deuterium, tritium, and helium-3 remain over bound even with the modification.

## Method
The authors introduced a Heisenberg principle constraint into the CoMD model using a procedure analogous to the existing Pauli principle constraint. For each particle that does not violate the Pauli principle, they search for the nearest non-identical particles in phase space and calculate the average one-body occupation probability; if this average exceeds 25.0/93.0 (the value corresponding to two nucleons with ΔrΔp = ħ/2), the momenta of particles in that ensemble are changed. The same "Heisenberg blocking" is applied in the nucleon-nucleon collision term, where a collision is accepted only if both the Pauli and Heisenberg principles are satisfied. A modified cooling procedure with friction terms is used during initialization to prepare ground states, iterated up to 1000 fm/c.

## Key numbers
- The threshold value for the Heisenberg principle constraint is set to 25.0/93.0, obtained by calculating the occupation probability of two nucleons with ΔrΔp = ħ/2.
- The cooling procedure for ground state preparation is iterated up to 1000 fm/c.
- Ground states are accepted only if the root mean square radius R is stable for at least 1000 fm/c.
- Elastic scattering simulations for p+12C were performed at proton beam energies of 35 MeV, 51.93 MeV, 120 MeV, and 250 MeV.
- For the 197Au+197Au fragmentation reaction at 35 MeV/A, impact parameters ranged from 0 to 3.5 fm with evolution time t = 1500 fm/c.
- For the 238U+238U superheavy reaction at 15 MeV/A with zero impact parameter, evolution time was t = 4000 fm/c.
- One selected superheavy nucleus (Z=116, A=291) survived for an additional 6000 fm/c before undergoing fission into 136La + 127Xe plus one alpha particle with 15.78 MeV kinetic energy.

## Context
This work extends the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model developed by Papa (2001, 2005), which successfully introduced Pauli principle constraints to reproduce stable nuclei in the mass range A=30-208 and experimental fragment distributions in heavy-ion collisions. The original CoMD model failed to reproduce binding energies of light nuclei and the Z=2 yield, a problem shared with other molecular dynamics approaches such as Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) by Aichelin (1986, 1991), Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) by Feldmeier (1990, 1995), and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) by Ono (1992). The Heisenberg principle constraint was inspired by similar applications to Coulomb barrier penetrability studies by Kimura (2004) and atomic structure calculations by Kimura (2005). The modified model enables improved simulations of fragmentation reactions and superheavy nuclei production, though the authors note that (iso)spin-(iso)spin forces are still missing and plan to include them in future work.
