# Particle seismology: mechanical and gravitational properties from parton-hadron duality

> The internal structure of hadrons is characterized by form factors which correspond to matrix elements of currents. Among those, the stress-energy-momentum tensor is a universally conserved quantity providing the gravitational form factors, from which mechanical properties may be derived via the response to the space-time fluctuations. They have received much attention because of their role as moments of the Generalized Parton Distributions, where the stress-energy-momentum tensor couples to two photons, and more recently, due to the explicit lattice QCD determination for the pion and nucleon. In these lectures we attempt a pedagogical review of the topic from a purely hadronic point of view, based on the notion of dispersion relations, meson dominance, and parton-hadron duality. We show that despite the overwhelming simplicity of the approach, a rather successful description of the lattice QCD data is achieved.

## Key claim

The authors show that a purely hadronic approach based on dispersion relations, meson dominance, and parton-hadron duality can successfully describe lattice QCD data for gravitational form factors of the pion and nucleon, despite the overwhelming simplicity of the method, though the description is limited to the space-like region and relies on the spectral function being determined from the known meson spectrum in the scalar and tensor channels.

## Method

The authors derive gravitational form factors (GFFs) from the stress-energy-momentum tensor using dispersion relations and unsubtracted dispersion relations for the D-term form factor, which satisfies an analytic structure with a branch cut along the positive real axis starting at threshold (4m_pi^2 for pion and nucleon). They employ meson dominance and parton-hadron duality to relate the spectral function Im D(s) to virtual gravitational hadron-antihadron production in the scalar 0++ and tensor 2++ quantum number channels. The formalism is applied to both the pion and nucleon, using lattice QCD data from the MIT group (Hackett et al., 2023) as benchmarks.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD data from the MIT group covers the space-like region 0 <= -t <= 2 GeV^2 with an almost physical pion mass of 170 MeV and a benchmarking 5% precision for nucleon and pion GFFs.
- The threshold for the branch cut in the D-form factor is s_0 = 4m_pi^2 for both pion and nucleon; for the nucleon there is an additional subthreshold logarithmic singularity at s_a = 4m_pi^2 - m_pi^2/M_N^2.
- Hadron sizes are typically 0.5–1 fm.
- The D-form factor falls off faster than 1/q^2 in QCD: for mesons D ~ 1/(q^2 log q^2), for baryons D ~ 1/(q^2 log q^2)^2.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing concept of gravitational form factors, first studied by Kobzarev (1962), Pagels (1966), and others, and revived by the deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) proposals of Ji (1994, 1996) and the D-term discovery by Polyakov (1999). It directly builds on the authors' own recent work (Broniowski et al., 2024; Ruiz Arriola et al., 2024; Broniowski et al., 2025) and contrasts with the direct lattice QCD computations of the MIT group (Hackett et al., 2023) and earlier lattice studies by Brommel (2007) and QCDSF (2007). The approach enables a purely hadronic determination of mechanical properties (energy density, pressure, stress distributions) without explicit graviton exchange, and provides a framework for interpreting future experimental data from Super-KEKB, ILC, and JLab.
