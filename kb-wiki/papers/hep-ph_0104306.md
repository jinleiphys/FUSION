# Nonperturbative Effects from the Resummation of Perturbation Theory

> Using the general argument in Borel resummation of perturbation theory that links the divergent perturbation theory to the nonperturbative effect we argue that the nonperturbative effect associated with the perturbation theory should have a branch cut only along the positive real axis in the complex coupling plane. The component in the weak coupling expansion of the nonperturbative amplitude, which usually includes the leading term in the weak coupling expansion, that gives rise to the branch cut can be calculated in principle from the perturbation theory combined with some exactly calculable properties of the nonperturbative effect. The realization of this mechanism is demonstrated in the double well potential and the two-dimensional O(N) nonlinear sigma model. In these models the leading term in weak coupling of the nonperturbative effect can be obtained with good accuracy from the first terms of the perturbation theory. Applying this mechanism to the infrared renormalon induced nonperturbative effect in QCD, we suggest some of the QCD condensate effects can be calculated in principle from the perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nonperturbative amplitude associated with a divergent perturbation series must have a branch cut only along the positive real axis in the complex coupling plane, and that the component of this nonperturbative amplitude giving rise to that branch cut, typically the leading term in weak coupling expansion, can be calculated in principle from the Borel resummation of perturbation theory combined with exactly calculable properties of the nonperturbative effect, though the real part of the nonperturbative amplitude is in general not fully calculable from perturbation theory alone.

## Method

The author uses the general Borel resummation formalism linking divergent perturbation theory to nonperturbative effects, deriving that the imaginary part of the nonperturbative amplitude can be obtained from the Borel resummation of the perturbative series. The mechanism is demonstrated by calculating the leading weak-coupling term of the nonperturbative effect from the first few terms of the perturbation series in two models: the double well potential (instanton–anti-instanton pairs) and the two-dimensional O(N) nonlinear sigma model (infrared renormalon). In each case, the residue of the first Borel-plane singularity is extracted from the perturbative coefficients using conformal mapping to improve convergence, and the result is compared with the known exact nonperturbative amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Double well potential: the normalized residue C_N converges to the exact value C=1; with the first 6 terms (N=5) and an optimal conformal mapping, the series gives C_5 = 0.994 (99.4% accuracy).
- Two-dimensional O(N) nonlinear sigma model: the renormalon residue C_N converges to the exact value C=-1; with the first 6 terms (N=5) and conformal mapping, the series gives -C_5 = 1.052.
- Double well potential: the first singularity in the Borel plane is at b = 1/3, where S_0 = 1/6 is the one-instanton action.
- Two-dimensional O(N) nonlinear sigma model: the first infrared renormalon singularity is at b = 1 in the Borel plane.

## Context

This work extends the general heuristic argument linking Borel resummation to nonperturbative effects, as reviewed in Zinn-Justin (1981) and Zinn-Justin (1982), and builds on earlier speculations by David (1982) and an approach similar to that of Grunberg (1993). The mechanism is demonstrated in the double well potential using instanton–anti-instanton calculations from Zinn-Justin (1981) and in the two-dimensional O(N) nonlinear sigma model using the exact 1/N expansion results of Bardeen, Bander, and Kogut (1976). The paper suggests that for QCD, some condensate effects in the Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov sum rule formalism could in principle be calculated from perturbation theory via the same mechanism, though chirality-violating nonperturbative effects remain inaccessible.
