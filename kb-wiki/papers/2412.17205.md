# Deformation from zinc to zirconium

> Extensive gamma-ray spectroscopy of very neutron-rich nuclei of isotopes between the Ni and Sn isotopic chains was facilitated by the high luminosity LH2 target system, MINOS. Results show a persistence of deformation when going beyond the N = 60 threshold of the transition between spherical to deformed ground states at N < 60 and N > 60, respectively. Close to 78Ni, a more detailed image of the N > 50 Zn isotopes shows an erosion of the N = 50 shell closure, with core-breaking effects needed from theoretical models to replicate observation. As well as the experimental results indicating collective effects, the projected generator coordinate method is discussed in detail within the context of the neutron-rich Ge isotopes.

# Deformation from zinc to zirconium

## Key claim
The paper reports that deformation persists beyond the N=60 threshold in neutron-rich nuclei from Zn to Zr, but the abstract's claim of a "persistence of deformation" is qualified by the observation that the onset and nature of collectivity varies dramatically with proton number from Z=30 to Z=40, with different mechanisms at work in different isotopic chains. Near 78Ni, the erosion of the N=50 shell closure requires core-breaking effects in theoretical models to reproduce experimental observations.

## Method
In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy was performed using the MINOS liquid-hydrogen target system coupled with the DALI2+ gamma-ray spectrometer at the RIBF, following knockout and nucleon removal reactions on liquid hydrogen and beryllium targets. Decay spectroscopy was conducted at the final focal plane of the ZeroDegree spectrometer using the EURICA array of high-purity germanium crystals to tag isomeric decays. Projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) calculations using the Gogny D1S functional with variation after particle number projection were performed, exploring intrinsic quadrupole deformations parametrized by (β₂,γ) as collective generating coordinates.

## Key numbers
- E(2⁺₁) and R₄/₂ = E(4⁺₁)/E(2⁺₁) measured for ⁸⁰,⁸²,⁸⁴Zn, showing reduction in E(2⁺₁) and increase in R₄/₂ beyond N=50
- R₄/₂ ratio reaches 3.1(2) at ¹¹⁰Zr, consistent with transition from harmonic vibrator (R₄/₂=2) toward deformed rotor (R₄/₂=3.3)
- E(2⁺₁) in ⁹⁸,¹⁰⁰Kr shows marked decrease at N=62, demonstrating significant increase of deformation
- 2⁺₂ states observed below or close to 4⁺₁ levels in ⁸⁸,⁹⁰,⁹²,⁹⁴Se, indicating possible shape coexistence
- Background from Bremsstrahlung becomes prominent below approximately 200-300 keV in in-flight spectroscopy

## Context
This work extends systematic studies of shape evolution in the neutron-rich region between Ni and Sn isotopic chains, building on prior investigations of shape coexistence in ⁷⁸Ni by Taniuchi and colleagues, and studies of Ge and Se isotopes by various groups (PRC 96 011301 2017, PRC 95 041302 2017, Lizarazo 2020). The results enable tests of theoretical frameworks including PGCM, five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH), and shell-model calculations, particularly for understanding type-II shape evolution as described by Togashi (2016) and intertwined quantum phase transitions as described by Gavrielov (2019). The measurements of ¹¹⁰Zr test predictions of possible shell stabilization at the harmonic oscillator magic numbers N=70, Z=40, with results favoring deformed rather than spherical ground states.
