# Which Constituent Quark Model Is Better?

> A comparative study has been done by calculating the effective baryon-baryon interactions of the 64 lowest channels consisting of octet and decuplet baryons with three constituent quark models: the extended quark gluon exchange model, the Goldstone boson exchange model and the quark gluon meson exchange hybrid model. We find that these three models give similar results for 44 channels. Further tests of these models are discussed.

## Key claim

The three constituent quark models (Goldstone boson exchange, extended quark gluon exchange, and quark gluon meson exchange hybrid) give similar effective baryon-baryon interactions for 44 out of the 64 lowest channels consisting of octet and decuplet baryons, despite appearing to be very different; the authors hedge that this similarity means "it is hard to say which one is better inspired by QCD."

## Method

The authors calculated adiabatic effective baryon-baryon (BB) interactions for the 64 lowest channels consisting of octet and decuplet baryons using three constituent quark models: the Goldstone boson exchange model (GR) with Glozman's parametrization, the extended quark gluon exchange model (GI) using the quark delocalization and color screening model (QDCSM), and the quark gluon meson exchange hybrid model (MG) with the Fujiwara version. They compared the effective BB interactions channel by channel, classifying them as pure repulsive, weak attraction, or similar/different across models. They also analyzed the spin-flavor-color structure of the gluon and Goldstone boson exchange interactions using Casimir operators to explain the origin of the similarity.

## Key numbers

- 44 out of 64 channels have similar effective BB interactions across all three models.
- 17 channels are pure repulsive core channels where all three models agree.
- 13 channels are weak attraction channels where all three models give similar effective BB interactions.
- 14 channels are weak attraction channels where two of the three models give very similar results and the third gives a little different but still similar result.
- For the H particle channel, the three models all give somewhat different results but are all weakly attractive, with the QDCSM and hybrid models yielding a state with binding energy around zero.
- The QDCSM predicts a strong attraction in the di-Delta (SIJ=003) channel, while the hybrid model predicts a strong attraction in the di-Omega (SIJ=-600) channel.

## Context

This work extends the debate between Glozman and Riska (GR model), Isgur (GI model), and Manohar and Georgi (MG hybrid model) on which effective degrees of freedom are proper for low-energy QCD. Prior work by Furuichi and Shimizu and by Garcilazo et al. had concluded that no definite answer exists from baryon spectroscopy alone. The authors show that even in the more sensitive baryon-baryon interaction sector, the models are largely similar, and they suggest that future tests should focus on hadronic transition matrix elements, exotic quark-gluon systems, dibaryons, and more precise hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interaction data from new facilities.
