# Nuclei with up to $\boldsymbol{A=6}$ nucleons with artificial neural network wave functions

> The ground-breaking works of Weinberg have opened the way to calculations of atomic nuclei that are based on systematically improvable Hamiltonians. Solving the associated many-body Schrödinger equation involves non-trivial difficulties, due to the non-perturbative nature and strong spin-isospin dependence of nuclear interactions. Artificial neural networks have proven to be able to compactly represent the wave functions of nuclei with up to $A=4$ nucleons. In this work, we extend this approach to $^6$Li and $^6$He nuclei, using as input a leading-order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian. We successfully benchmark their binding energies, point-nucleon densities, and radii with the highly accurate hyperspherical harmonics method.

## Key claim

The paper extends variational Monte Carlo with artificial neural network (VMC-ANN) wave functions from nuclei with up to A=4 nucleons to A=6 nuclei (6Li and 6He), using a leading-order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian, and benchmarks the results against the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method; however, the VMC-ANN systematically underbinds A=3 and A=4 nuclei by about 0.2 MeV relative to HH, and for A=6 nuclei the charge radii show larger discrepancies attributed to both slow HH convergence and VMC-ANN underbinding.

## Method

The authors construct ANN variational wave functions as a product of a mean-field state (sum of Slater determinants of single-particle orbitals parametrized by feed-forward ANNs) modulated by permutation-invariant correlator factors based on the Deep Sets architecture, using pair-wise nucleon coordinates as input. The stochastic reconfiguration algorithm minimizes the energy expectation value, with Metropolis-Hastings sampling of spatial and spin-isospin coordinates. The HH method solves the Schrodinger equation using a basis of hyperspherical harmonics combined with spin-isospin functions, with the wave function expanded as a sum of Faddeev-like amplitudes over even permutations of particles.

## Key numbers

- 2H binding energy: VMC-ANN gives -2.242(1) MeV, HH gives -2.242 MeV (experiment: -2.225 MeV)
- 3H binding energy with 3N force: VMC-ANN gives -8.232(1) MeV, HH gives -8.475 MeV (experiment: -8.475 MeV)
- 4He binding energy with 3N force: VMC-ANN gives -27.903(1) MeV, HH gives -28.17 MeV (experiment: -28.30 MeV)
- 6He binding energy with NN only: VMC-ANN gives -37.25(4) MeV, HH gives -37.96(8) MeV (experiment: -29.27 MeV)
- 6Li binding energy with NN only: VMC-ANN gives -42.04(1) MeV, HH gives -42.51(5) MeV (experiment: -31.99 MeV)
- 6Li charge radius with NN only: VMC-ANN gives 2.248(3) fm, HH gives 2.09(2) fm (experiment: 2.54(3) fm)
- 6He charge radius with NN only: VMC-ANN gives 1.895(2) fm, HH gives 1.71(1) fm (experiment: 2.05(1) fm)
- 3N force cutoff radius R3 = 1.0 fm with corresponding low-energy constant cE = 1.0786

## Context

This work extends the VMC-ANN approach of Adams (2020) from A=4 to A=6 nuclei, using the leading-order pionless-EFT Hamiltonian of Schiavilla (2021). The ANN architecture improves on prior work by using pair-wise coordinates instead of single-particle inputs, and the results are benchmarked against the HH method of Gnech (2020) for A=6 systems. The paper finds that at LO in pionless-EFT, 6He is not stable against breakup into 4He plus two neutrons, and 6Li is only barely bound against 4He plus deuteron breakup when the 3N force is included, echoing the behavior seen by Contessi (2017) for 16O. The work enables future application to larger nuclear systems and next-to-leading order Hamiltonians for uncertainty quantification.
