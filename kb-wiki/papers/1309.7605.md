# Resolving the Puzzle of the Pion-Photon Transition Form Factor

> We investigate the form factors for pseudoscalar-meson-photon transitions by means of dispersive QCD sum rules and demonstrate that most of the measurements done so far (in particular, those by BaBar for $η,$ $η',$ and $η_c$ and those by Belle for $π^0$) are, on the one hand, compatible with each other and with the saturation required by factorization theorems obtained from perturbative QCD and, on the other hand, give a hint that saturation is effective already at relatively low momentum transfers $Q^2;$ this hypothesis is supported by experimental data for the charged-pion elastic form factor available in the range $Q^2\approx2-4\;\{GeV}^2.$ The only exception are the BaBar results for the $π^0γ$ transition form factor, which do not fit into such picture. We point out that results expected from SHMS at JLab on the pion elastic form factor in the region $Q^2\approx5-8\;\{GeV}^2$ will provide the ultimate test of saturation and factorization and strongly impact our general view of the form factors up to infinitely large $Q^2.$

## Key claim

Most measurements of pseudoscalar-meson-photon transition form factors (BaBar for η, η′, ηc and Belle for π0) are compatible with each other and with the saturation required by perturbative QCD factorization theorems, suggesting that saturation is effective already at relatively low momentum transfers Q²; the sole exception is the BaBar result for the π0γ transition form factor, which does not fit this picture.

## Method

The authors applied dispersive QCD sum rules in the local-duality (LD) limit to compute the transition form factors for γ*γ* → P (P = π0, η, η′, ηc). They used Borel-transformed correlation functions of axial-vector and vector currents, fixed the effective continuum threshold s_eff(Q²) by matching to the asymptotic factorization theorem at large Q², and compared the resulting LD predictions to experimental data from BaBar, Belle, and earlier experiments.

## Key numbers

- For the ηcγ transition form factor at Q² = 0, the LD model gives F_ηcγ(0) = 0.067 GeV⁻¹ from the ⟨AVV⟩ correlator and 0.086 GeV⁻¹ from the ⟨PVV⟩ correlator; the experimental value is F_ηcγ(0) = (0.08 ± 0.01) GeV⁻¹.
- The mixing angle for η–η′ is φ ≈ 38°; the flavour decay constants are f_n ≈ 1.07 f_π and f_s ≈ 1.36 f_π, with f_π = 0.130 GeV.
- The asymptotic value of the effective threshold for massless quarks is s_eff(Q² → ∞) = 4π² f_π².
- The LD model predicts that the effective threshold for the charged-pion elastic form factor reaches its asymptotic value already at Q² ≈ 4–6 GeV², with forthcoming SHMS at JLab measurements in the region Q² ≈ 5–8 GeV² expected to provide the ultimate test.
- BaBar data for π0γ show deviations from LD predictions up to Q² ≈ 40 GeV², while Belle data for π0γ are compatible with LD saturation setting in at Q² ≳ 5–10 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier QCD sum-rule analyses of transition form factors (Lucha and Melikhov, 2011; Braguta et al., 2011) and addresses the puzzle raised by BaBar's π0γ results (BaBar, 2009, 2010) which contradicted the factorization theorem of Lepage and Brodsky (1980). The analysis reconciles most experimental data (CLEO, Belle, BaBar for η, η′, ηc) with the LD model, and identifies the charged-pion elastic form factor measurements from JLab's SHMS upgrade as the decisive test for whether saturation sets in at Q² ≈ 4–8 GeV².
