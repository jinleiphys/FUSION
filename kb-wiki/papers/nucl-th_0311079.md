# Relativistic approach to (e,e'p) and (e,e') reactions

> A relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation model for electron-induced one proton knockout reactions and a Green's function approach to inclusive scattering are developed. Results for (e,e'p) and (e,e') reactions are presented in various kinematical conditions and compared (when possible) with nonrelativistic calculations.

## Key claim

The paper develops a relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) for exclusive (e,e'p) reactions and a Green's function approach for inclusive (e,e') scattering, showing that relativistic effects are small at low proton energies (around 90 MeV) but become large and essential at higher momentum transfers (Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)²), where nonrelativistic analysis gives unreliable results.

## Method

For exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, the authors solve the Dirac equation directly for bound and scattering nucleon states using scalar and vector potentials from relativistic mean field theory, then compute the nuclear transition amplitude with three different off-shell prescriptions for the electromagnetic current operator (cc1, cc2, cc3). For inclusive (e,e') scattering, they employ a spectral representation of the single-particle Green's function based on a biorthogonal expansion in eigenfunctions of the nonhermitian optical potential, which allows consistent treatment of final-state interactions in both exclusive and inclusive reactions.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors for ¹⁶O(e,e'p)¹⁵N at low energy (90 MeV proton energy): Z(p₁/₂) = 0.64 (nonrelativistic) and Z(p₁/₂) = 0.708 (RDWIA with cc3 current); Z(p₃/₂) = 0.54 (nonrelativistic) and Z(p₃/₂) = 0.602 (RDWIA with cc3 current)
- At Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)² for ¹⁶O(e,e'p)¹⁵N, the same spectroscopic factors reproduce JLab data, demonstrating Q² independence of spectroscopic factors
- Polarization transfer components P'^L and P'^T show sensitivity to off-shell effects at most less than or equal to 15% at Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)²
- For inclusive ¹²C(e,e') at q = 400, 500, and 600 MeV/c, the longitudinal response agrees well with Saclay data, while the transverse response is systematically underestimated
- For inclusive ¹⁶O(e,e') at beam energies 700, 1080, and 1200 MeV with scattering angle approximately 32°, the Green's function approach reproduces ADONE-Frascati data

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic DWIA framework (book, 1993; Kelly, 1996) to a fully relativistic treatment, addressing ambiguities in spectroscopic factors extracted at low energy where relativistic and nonrelativistic analyses give different results. The RDWIA approach builds on earlier relativistic models (Picklesimer and Van Orden, 1989; Jin et al., 1992; Spain, 1997) and the Green's function method for inclusive scattering (Capuzzi et al., 1991). The results enable consistent extraction of spectroscopic factors across a wide range of momentum transfers and provide a framework for future JLab experiments planned to extract response functions with high resolution.
