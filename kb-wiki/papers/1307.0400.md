# Nuclear Level Densities at High Excitation Energies and for Large Particle Numbers

> Starting from an independent-particle model with a finite and arbitrary set of single-particle energies, we develop an analytical approximation to the many-body level density $ρ_A(E)$ and to particle-hole densities. We use exact expressions for the low-order moments and cumulants to derive approximate expressions for the coefficients of an expansion of these densities in terms of orthogonal polynomials. The approach is asymptotically (mass number $A \gg 1$) convergent and, for large $A$, covers about 20 orders of magnitude near the maximum of $ρ_A(E)$ (i.e., about half the spectrum). Densities of accessible states are calculated using the Fermi-gas model.

## Key claim

The authors develop an analytical approximation to the many-body level density that is asymptotically convergent for large mass number (A ≫ 1) and covers about 20 orders of magnitude near the maximum of the level density, but the method fails in the tails of the spectrum and cannot provide a uniformly reliable approximation throughout the entire spectrum for large particle numbers.

## Method

Starting from an independent-particle model with a finite set of single-particle energies, the authors take the Fourier transform of the level density with respect to energy and the Laplace transform with respect to particle number. They use exact expressions for low-order moments and cumulants to derive approximate expressions for the coefficients of an expansion of the level density in terms of orthogonal polynomials (sine and cosine functions). The method is applied to three models of continuous single-particle level densities: constant spacing, linear energy dependence, and quadratic energy dependence.

## Key numbers

- For A = 42 and B = 51 in the constant-spacing model (Fermi energy F = 37 MeV, range V = 45 MeV), the many-body spectrum extends from E1 = 778 MeV to EN = 1112 MeV.
- For A = 100 and B = 148 in the linear single-particle level density model (F = 37 MeV, V = 45 MeV), the many-body spectrum extends from E1 = 2466 MeV to EN = 3620 MeV.
- For A = 200 and B = 360 in the quadratic single-particle level density model (F = 37 MeV, V = 45 MeV), the ratio of total level density to normalized level density is approximately 10^106.
- For the constant-spacing model with 100 particles in 200 single-particle states, the level density takes values between unity and approximately 10^60.
- The approximation covers roughly the leading 20 orders of magnitude of the distribution for the linear and quadratic single-particle level density models.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Palffy and Weidenmuller (2013) from the constant-spacing model to arbitrary single-particle spectra, generalizing the seminal work of Bethe (1936) on nuclear level densities and Ericson (1960) on particle-hole densities. The method belongs to the category of saddle-point approximations for the grand partition function (Bloch 1968, Williams 1971, etc.) but goes beyond earlier works by carrying the approach analytically to the determination of orthogonal polynomial coefficients. The results enable calculations of level densities needed for precompound reactions, heavy-ion reactions at several MeV per nucleon, and nuclear reactions induced by zeptosecond multi-MeV laser pulses at facilities like the Extreme Light Infrastructure.
