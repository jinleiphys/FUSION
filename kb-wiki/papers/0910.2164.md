# One-quasiparticle States in the Nuclear Energy Density Functional Theory

> We study one-quasiproton excitations in the rare-earth region in the framework of the nuclear Density Functional Theory in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov variant. The blocking prescription is implemented exactly, with the time-odd mean field fully taken into account. The equal filling approximation is compared with the exact blocking procedure. We show that both procedures are strictly equivalent when the time-odd channel is neglected, and discuss how nuclear alignment properties affect the time-odd fields. The impact of time-odd fields on calculated one-quasiproton bandhead energies is found to be rather small, of the order of 100-200 keV; hence, the equal filling approximation is sufficiently precise for most practical applications. The triaxial polarization of the core induced by the odd particle is studied. We also briefly discuss the occurrence of finite-size spin instabilities that are present in calculations for odd-mass nuclei when certain Skyrme functionals are employed.

## Key claim

The impact of time-odd fields on calculated one-quasiproton bandhead energies is found to be rather small, of the order of 100-200 keV; hence, the equal filling approximation is sufficiently precise for most practical applications, but only when the time-odd channel is neglected are the equal filling approximation and exact blocking strictly equivalent.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for one-quasiproton excitations in rare-earth nuclei using two DFT solvers: HFBTHO (axial, time-reversal symmetric, using the equal filling approximation) and HFODD (symmetry-unrestricted, with exact blocking and full time-odd fields). They compared the exact blocking prescription against the equal filling approximation, and studied the dependence of blocked states on the orientation of the alignment vector relative to the principal axes of the mass distribution. The time-odd coupling constants were treated in four variants: native, gauge, Landau, and time-even (all time-odd constants set to zero).

## Key numbers

- The impact of time-odd fields on calculated one-quasiproton bandhead energies is of the order of 100-200 keV.
- The equal filling approximation and exact blocking give identical time-even densities and therefore identical results for time-even observables such as radii and multipole moments.
- The two codes (HFBTHO and HFODD) yield the same results within a few eV for spherical or axially deformed even-even nuclei.

## Context

This work extends prior systematic studies of one-quasiparticle excitations in nuclear DFT, such as those by Bonneau et al. (2007) which provided the only global DFT study of ground state spin and parity for odd-mass nuclei, and by Zalewski et al. (2008) and Kortelainen et al. (2008) which indicated that currently used nuclear density functionals give a rather poor description of single-particle states. The study enables improved treatment of odd-mass nuclei in spectroscopic-quality energy density functionals by quantifying the magnitude of time-odd polarization effects and establishing the validity range of the equal filling approximation.
