# Radiative transitions of charmonium states in a constituent quark model

> We study the electromagnetic (EM) transitions of the $nS$, $nP$ ($n\leq 3$), and $nD$ ($n\leq 2$) charmonium states with a constituent quark model. We obtain a reasonable description of the EM transitions of the well-established charmonium states $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, $χ_{cJ}(1P)$, $h_c(1P)$ and $ψ(3770)$. We find that the M2 transitions give notable corrections to some E1 dominant processes by interfering with the E1 transitions. Our predictions of EM decay properties for the higher charmonium states are also presented and compared with other model predictions. In particular, we discuss the EM decay properties of some $"XYZ"$ states, such as $X(3823)$, $X(3872)$, $X(3915)$, $X(3940)$ and $X(4350)$ as conventional charmonium states. Assuming $X(3872)$ as the $χ_{c1}(2P)$ state, our predicted ratio $Γ[X(3872)\to ψ(2S)γ]/Γ[X(3872)\to J/ψγ]\simeq 4.0$ is consistent with BaBar's measurement.

## Key claim

The constituent quark model, using a specific electromagnetic operator that includes binding potential effects, provides a reasonable description of charmonium radiative transitions, but the authors note that strong model dependencies still exist between different theoretical approaches, and their predictions for higher charmonium states and XYZ candidates depend on the specific assignment of quantum numbers.

## Method

The authors employed a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator wave functions (oscillator parameter α = 400 MeV, charm quark mass m_c = 1500 MeV) to calculate electromagnetic transition amplitudes. They used a multipole expansion of the transition operator that includes both electric (El) and magnetic (Ml) multipole contributions, with the electromagnetic coupling derived from the quark-photon interaction Hamiltonian, and computed partial decay widths from helicity amplitudes using the standard formula with experimental masses for well-established states and theoretical masses for missing states.

## Key numbers

- Γ[J/ψ → η_c(1S)γ] ≃ 1.25 keV (compared to world average 1.58 ± 0.37 keV from PDG)
- Γ[ψ(2S) → χ_c0(1P)γ] ≃ 26 keV, Γ[ψ(2S) → χ_c1(1P)γ] ≃ 22 keV, Γ[ψ(2S) → χ_c2(1P)γ] ≃ 14 keV
- Γ[ψ(2S) → η_c(1S)γ] ≃ 2.41 keV, Γ[ψ(2S) → η_c(2S)γ] ≃ 0.10 keV
- Γ[χ_c0(1P) → J/ψγ] ≃ 128 keV, Γ[χ_c1(1P) → J/ψγ] ≃ 275 keV, Γ[χ_c2(1P) → J/ψγ] ≃ 467 keV
- Γ[h_c(1P) → η_cγ] ≃ 587 keV
- Γ[ψ(3770) → χ_c0(1P)γ] ≃ 218 keV, Γ[ψ(3770) → χ_c1(1P)γ] ≃ 70 keV, Γ[ψ(3770) → χ_c2(1P)γ] ≃ 2.6 keV
- Γ[X(3872) → ψ(2S)γ] / Γ[X(3872) → J/ψγ] ≃ 4.0, assuming X(3872) as χ_c1(2P) state, consistent with BaBar measurement
- Γ[h_c(1P) → χ_c0(1P)γ] = 0.39 keV, with branching ratio 5.6 × 10^(-4)

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model previously applied to meson photoproduction reactions and strong decays of baryons and heavy-light mesons (Zhong 2008, Xiao 2014) to charmonium electromagnetic transitions up to n=3 S-wave, n=3 P-wave, and n=2 D-wave states. The results are compared with predictions from the Godfrey-Isgur potential model (Swanson 2005), relativistic quark model (Ebert 2003), lattice QCD calculations (Dudek 2006, Chen 2011), and Coulomb gauge approach (Guo 2014), and enable future experimental tests of the conventional charmonium assignment for XYZ states such as X(3823), X(3872), X(3915), X(3940), and X(4350).
