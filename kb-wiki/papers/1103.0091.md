# A quasi-particle description of $(2+1)$- flavor lattice QCD equation of state

> A quasi-particle model has been employed to describe the $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD equation of state with physical quark masses. The interaction part of the equation of state has been mapped to the effective fugacities of otherwise non-interacting quasi-gluons and quasi-quarks. The mapping is found to be exact for the equation of state. The model leads to non-trivial dispersion relations for quasi-partons. The dispersion relations, effective quasi-particle number densities, and trace anomaly have been investigated employing the model.A Virial expansion for the EOS has further been obtained to investigate the role of interactions in quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Finally, Debye screening in QGP has been studied employing the model.

# A quasi-particle description of (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD equation of state

## Key claim

The authors claim that the (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD equation of state with physical quark masses can be mapped exactly onto non-interacting quasi-gluons and quasi-quarks with effective temperature-dependent fugacities, but only in the deconfined phase beyond the QCD transition temperature T_c, and the mapping is exact only for the equation of state itself, not necessarily for all thermodynamic quantities.

## Method

The authors determine effective gluon fugacity z_g and effective quark fugacity z_q by numerically matching the grand canonical partition function expressions for pressure to lattice QCD data for (2+1)-flavor QCD from Cheng et al. (2008). The gluonic sector pressure P_g fixes z_g, while the remaining pressure (P - P_g) fixes z_q, with strange quarks treated using a mass of 0.1 GeV. The temperature-dependent fugacities are then fitted with piecewise exponential functions, and the resulting quasi-particle distribution functions are used to compute dispersion relations, effective number densities, trace anomaly, and a Virial expansion for the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark mass is taken as 0.1 GeV.
- The crossover temperature between low and high temperature regimes for the fugacity fits is x_g = T_g/T_c approximately 1.68 for gluons and x_q = T_q/T_c approximately 1.70 for quarks.
- The low-temperature fitting parameters for gluon fugacity are a_g = 0.803 plus or minus 0.009 and b_g = 1.837 plus or minus 0.039; for quark fugacity a_q = 0.810 plus or minus 0.010 and b_q = 1.721 plus or minus 0.040.
- The high-temperature fitting parameters for gluon fugacity are a'_g = 0.978 plus or minus 0.007 and b'_g = 0.942 plus or minus 0.035; for quark fugacity a'_q = 0.960 plus or minus 0.007 and b'_q = 0.846 plus or minus 0.033.
- The lattice QCD equation of state is approximately 10 percent away from ideal behavior even at 4 T_c.
- The effective quasi-particle number densities remain roughly 10 percent away from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit even at T = 5 T_c.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle model previously applied to pure SU(3) gauge theory by Chandra and Ravishankar (earlier works) to full (2+1)-flavor QCD with physical quark masses, using lattice data from Cheng et al. (2008) and subsequent refinements by Bazavov et al. (2009) and Cheng et al. (2010). The approach differs from effective mass models (Peshier et al., various years) and Polyakov loop models (various authors) by mapping all interactions into effective fugacities while preserving exact reproduction of the equation of state. The model enables future development of effective kinetic theories for quark-gluon plasma and provides a framework for studying transport properties such as shear viscosity, which earlier work by Chandra and Ravishankar (2009, 2010) showed is highly sensitive to the interaction treatment.
