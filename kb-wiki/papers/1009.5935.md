# QCD at non-zero temperature: bulk properties and heavy quarks

> I review recent progress in lattice QCD at non-zero temperature whith emphasis on the calculations of equation of state and the properties of heavy quar anti-quark pairs at high temperatures. I also briefly discuss the deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoring aspects of the QCD transition at finite temperature

# QCD at non-zero temperature: bulk properties and heavy quarks

## Key claim
Lattice QCD calculations with improved staggered fermion actions (p4, asqtad, HISQ, stout) show that the finite-temperature QCD transition is an analytic crossover, with a chiral transition temperature in the range 150–160 MeV in the continuum limit, but current lattice data still suffer from significant discretization errors that cause discrepancies between different actions, particularly at low temperatures and in the high-temperature tail of the trace anomaly.

## Method
The equation of state was computed from the trace anomaly (interaction measure) using p4 and asqtad actions on lattices with temporal extents Nτ = 4, 6, and 8, with the strange quark mass fixed to its physical value and light quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 220–260 MeV. Fluctuations of conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) were calculated via Taylor expansion coefficients, and the singlet free energy of static quark–antiquark pairs was extracted from temporal Wilson line correlators in Coulomb gauge. Quarkonium spectral functions were reconstructed from Euclidean temporal correlators using the Maximum Entropy Method.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses used in the equation-of-state calculations: 220–260 MeV.
- Energy density reaches about 90% of the ideal gas value at the highest temperatures; pressure is about 15% below the ideal gas value.
- The trace anomaly calculated with the stout action is about 50% smaller than with p4/asqtad in the high-temperature region.
- Chiral transition temperature estimate with p4 action on Nτ = 4 and 6 lattices: Tc = 192(4)(7) MeV.
- Expected continuum-limit transition temperature: 150–160 MeV.
- Quadratic fluctuations of conserved charges show visible cutoff dependence at low temperatures but small cutoff dependence for T > 250 MeV.
- The singlet free energy shows exponential screening at distances rT > 0.8, with screening mass mD ∼ T.
- Quenched charmonium spectral functions show no significant changes up to 1.6 Tc, but potential-model calculations using the singlet free energy indicate all charmonium states dissolve below 1.2 Tc.

## Context
This review extends the lattice QCD thermodynamics program that began with early SU(2) gauge theory calculations by Kuti (1981), McLerran (1981), and Engels (1981), and the pure-glue equation-of-state solution by Boyd (1996). It contrasts the p4/asqtad results with the stout-action calculations of Fodor (2009, 2010) and the HISQ results of the Wuppertal–Budapest collaboration (2010). The discussion of quarkonium survival revises earlier claims from Datta (2004), Umeda (2002), and Asakawa (2004) by showing that Euclidean correlators are insensitive to bound-state dissolution, consistent with potential-model studies by Mocsy (2007–2009). The work enables controlled continuum extrapolations of the equation of state and transition temperature, and provides lattice constraints for resummed perturbative calculations by Blaizot (2001) and Rebhan (2002) as well as for AdS/CFT predictions.
