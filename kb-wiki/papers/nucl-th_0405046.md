# Cronin effect from backward to forward rapidity

> I discuss recent experimental data on the Cronin effect in deuteron-gold collisions at the top RHIC energy, in a pseudorapidity range [-2,3]. Two theoretical approaches are compared and contrasted: the pQCD-based Glauber-Eikonal model and Colour Glass Condensate models. Neither can describe the Cronin effect over the whole pseudorapidity interval up to now explored experimentally, its most mysterious and intriguing part being at negative rapidity.

## Key claim
Neither the pQCD-based Glauber-Eikonal model nor Colour Glass Condensate models can describe the Cronin effect over the full pseudorapidity range [-2,3] explored experimentally at RHIC, with the most mysterious part being the large enhancement at negative rapidity that cannot be explained by standard nuclear effects.

## Method
The paper compares two theoretical approaches to the Cronin effect in deuteron-gold collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV per nucleon. The Glauber-Eikonal model treats multiple 2-to-2 partonic collisions in collinearly factorized pQCD with unitarization via an exponential factor representing the probability of no further semihard scatterings. The Colour Glass Condensate models treat multiple 2-to-1 coherent parton scatterings using a kT-factorized formalism with a saturation scale Qs, where the gluon field is computed as a solution of Yang-Mills equations with random colour sources.

## Key numbers
- The Cronin ratio Rcp reaches approximately 1.8 at pseudorapidity eta=-2 for charged hadrons with 1<pT<3 GeV, integrated over centrality classes
- The Glauber-Eikonal model uses an intrinsic transverse momentum <kT^2>=0.52 GeV^2 to describe pp spectra at intermediate pT=1-5 GeV
- At forward rapidity eta=3.2, the Glauber-Eikonal model with opacity chi=3*chi0 (where chi0=0.95) touches the Rcp data at pT>2 GeV but predicts a strong Cronin peak at pT>3 GeV
- The Cronin peak at mid-rapidity occurs at pT approximately 3 GeV

## Context
This work extends the study of the Cronin effect from mid-rapidity to the full pseudorapidity range at RHIC, building on earlier measurements by Cronin (1975) at lower energies and by the BRAHMS collaboration (2004) at forward rapidity. The paper contrasts the Glauber-Eikonal approach of Accardi and Gyulassy (2003) with Colour Glass Condensate models by Iancu (2002) and Jalilian-Marian (2004). The discrepancy between RdAu and Rcp at forward rapidity and the unexplained large Cronin effect at negative rapidity point to the need for a unified kT-factorized framework that can describe both 2-to-2 and 2-to-1 processes, and for further experimental study using azimuthal two-hadron correlations at STAR and PHENIX.
