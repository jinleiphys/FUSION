# Effects of Meson Mass Reduction on the Properties of Neutron Star Matter

> We investigate the effects of meson-mass reduction on the properties of the neutron star matter. We adopt the Brown-Rho scaling law to take into account density dependence of meson masses in the quantum hadrodynamics, quark-meson coupling and modified quark-meson coupling models. It is found that the equation of state becomes stiff when the mass of meson is reduced in dense medium. We discuss its implication on the properties of the neutron star.

# Effects of Meson Mass Reduction on the Properties of Neutron Star Matter

## Key claim
The reduction of meson masses in dense nuclear matter, implemented via the Brown-Rho scaling law, stiffens the equation of state and enhances the proton fraction at low densities; however, the authors caution that inclusion of hyperons would soften the equation of state and substantially lower the maximum neutron star mass, reversing the stiffening effect.

## Method
The authors incorporated the Brown-Rho scaling law for meson masses (sigma, omega, rho) into three nuclear matter models: quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), quark-meson coupling (QMC), and modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC). They computed the equation of state and particle fractions for neutron star matter under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions, comparing each model with and without the scaling.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}; binding energy at saturation = 16.0 MeV (used to adjust coupling constants in all models).
- Maximum neutron star mass in QHD (without scaling): approximately 1.98 solar masses (M_sun), cited from prior work (Hyun and Moon, 1998).
- The Brown-Rho scaling law used: m*_sigma/m_sigma = m*_omega/m_omega = m*_rho/m_rho = (1 + y * rho/rho_0)^{-1}, where y is a parameter and rho is the nuclear matter density.

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical studies (Brown and Rho, 1991; Hatsuda and Lee, 1992; Saito, Tsushima, and Thomas, 1997) that predicted hadron mass reduction in medium, and responds to experimental hints from CERES/NA45 and KEK-PS E325 suggesting rho and omega meson mass reduction. The results indicate that meson mass reduction via Brown-Rho scaling could enable the direct URCA cooling process at low densities, but the authors note that a full treatment including hyperons would likely soften the equation of state and reduce the maximum neutron star mass, pointing to the need for more complete calculations with the baryon octet.
