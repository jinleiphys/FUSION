# Suppression of bottomonia states in finite size quark gluon plasma in PbPb collisions at Large Hadron Collider

> The bottomonium states due to their varying binding energies dissolve at different temperatures and thus their nuclear modification factors and relative yields have potential to map the properties of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP). We estimate the suppression of bottomonia states due to color screening in an expanding QGP of finite lifetime and size with the conditions relevant for PbPb collisions at LHC. The properties of $Υ$ states and recent results on their dissociation temperatures have been used as ingredient in the study. The nuclear modification factors and the ratios of yields of $Υ$ states are then obtained as a function of transverse momentum and centrality. We compare our theoretical calculations with the bottomonia yields measured with CMS in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. The model calculations explain the data very well.

# Suppression of bottomonia states in finite size quark gluon plasma in PbPb collisions at Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim
The model calculations of bottomonia suppression due to color screening in an expanding QGP of finite lifetime and size reproduce the measured nuclear modification factors of Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S) and the double ratio very well at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, but only after using slightly lower values of the dissociation temperatures than the upper limits from potential model calculations.

## Method
The authors used a dynamical model by Chu and Matsui that accounts for finite QGP lifetime and spatial extent, where bottomonia survival probabilities depend on the competition between resonance formation time and plasma characteristics. The model assumes isentropic Bjorken expansion of the QGP from an initial temperature T0 at time tau0 = 0.1 fm/c, with the initial entropy density profile depending on the transverse radius R determined by collision centrality. Survival probabilities were calculated as a function of transverse momentum and centrality, then converted to nuclear modification factors R_AA by including feed-down corrections from higher bottomonia states using fractions from CDF measurements.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature T0 = 0.65 GeV for 0-5% central collisions, obtained using tau0 = 0.1 fm/c and charged particle multiplicity (dN/deta)_{0-5%} = 1.5 x 1600
- Critical temperature taken as T_C = 0.170 GeV
- Dissociation temperatures used in the work: Upsilon(1S) at 1.8 T_C, chi_b(1P) at 1.15 T_C, Upsilon(2S) at 1.1 T_C, Upsilon(3S) at 1.0 T_C
- Upsilon(1S) radius = 0.28 fm, formation time tau_F = 0.76 fm; Upsilon(2S) radius = 0.56 fm, tau_F = 1.9 fm; Upsilon(3S) radius = 0.78 fm, tau_F = 2.4 fm
- Feed-down fractions for Upsilon(1S): g1 = 0.509 (direct 1S), g2 = 0.27 (chi_b(1P)), g3 = 0.107 (2S), g4 = 0.113 (3S + chi_b(2P))
- Transverse size for 0-5% centrality: R_{0-5%} = 0.92 R0, where R0 = r0 A^{1/3} with r0 = 1.2 fm

## Context
This work extends the Chu and Matsui (1989) model for quarkonia suppression in finite-size QGP to bottomonia at LHC energies, using dissociation temperatures from potential model calculations by Mocsy and Petreczky (2007, 2008). The calculations are compared to CMS measurements of Upsilon suppression in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, including the sequential suppression pattern observed by CMS (2011). The approach differs from earlier work by Strickland (2011) in how the initial temperature varies with centrality. The model enables mapping of QGP properties through the sequential melting of bottomonia states, with the double ratio [Upsilon(2S)/Upsilon(1S)]_{PbPb}/[Upsilon(2S)/Upsilon(1S)]_{pp} serving as a robust probe where cold nuclear matter effects cancel.
