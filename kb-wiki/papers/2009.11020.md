# The lightest flavor--singlet qqq baryons as witnesses to color

> We present a new computation in a field-theoretical model of Coulomb gauge QCD of the first radial and angular excitations of a qqq system in a SU(3) flavor singlet state, Lambda_S. The traditional motivation for the study is that the absence of flavor singlets in the lowest-lying spectrum is a direct consequence of the color degree of freedom. (The calculation is tested with decuplet baryons Delta(1232) and Omega(1672).) We also analyze decay branching fractions of the flavor singlet baryon for various masses with the simplest effective Lagrangians.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the lightest flavor-singlet three-quark baryon must be an excited state (not the ground state) due to the antisymmetry requirement of the color wavefunction, and that this excitation raises the mass of the singlet relative to the octet and decuplet. The authors compute masses and decay branching fractions for such a state using a Coulomb gauge QCD model, but caution that their pure three-quark results should be compared to lattice QCD data rather than directly to experimental resonances, because the experimental states likely contain significant meson-baryon molecular components.

## Method

The authors construct totally antisymmetric spin-spatial wavefunctions for three-quark flavor-singlet baryons with one quantum of excitation, yielding three possible quantum number combinations: \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\), \(3/2^-\), and \(1/2^-\). They apply the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle with a Coulomb gauge QCD Hamiltonian, using four different families of radial wavefunction Ansatze (hydrogen-like, 1D harmonic oscillator, 3D harmonic oscillator, and a numerically tabulated wavefunction from solving the meson problem). For decay branching fractions, they employ effective Lagrangians with both constant Yukawa and derivatively coupled meson vertices, exploiting SU(3) flavor symmetry to relate coupling constants across different decay channels.

## Key numbers

* The Coulomb gauge model predicts the flavor-singlet \(1/2^-\) state mass at \(1800 \pm 200\) MeV
* The Coulomb gauge model predicts the flavor-singlet \(3/2^-\) state mass at \(1700 \pm 200\) MeV
* The Coulomb gauge model predicts the flavor-singlet \(1/2^+\) state mass at \(2400 \pm 150\) MeV
* Lattice QCD data from Lin (2011) and Lin (2008) shows a \(1/2^-\) state at 1600 MeV and a \(3/2^-\) state at 1950 MeV, with pion masses in the 300-700 MeV range extrapolated linearly to physical pion mass
* The Graz Goldstone Boson Exchange model predicts the \(1/2^-\) singlet at 1555 MeV and the \(3/2^-\) at 1555 MeV
* The Graz One Gluon Exchange model predicts the \(1/2^-\) singlet at 1630 MeV and the \(3/2^-\) at 1630 MeV
* The Dyson-Schwinger computation from Qin (2019) predicts a \(1/2^+\) uds excitation around 1475 MeV, rising to 1580 MeV with a weakened kernel

## Context

This work extends the long-standing quark model prediction that the lightest flavor-singlet baryon must be excited, a consequence of color confinement forcing antisymmetric color wavefunctions. The authors compare their Coulomb gauge results to lattice QCD computations by Lin (2011) and Lin (2008), to quark model calculations by the Graz group (Melde 2008), to the Bonn model by Ronniger (2011) and Loring (2001), and to Dyson-Schwinger results by Qin (2019). The paper enables future experimental searches for the flavor-singlet component in the \(\Lambda(1405)\), \(\Lambda(1520)\), \(\Lambda(1600)\), and \(\Lambda(1810)\) resonances, and provides branching fraction predictions as a function of the singlet mass to help identify which experimental states contain significant three-quark singlet admixture.
