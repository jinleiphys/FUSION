# Effective Field Theories of QCD

> These are the proceedings of the workshop on ``Effective Field Theories of QCD'' held at the Physikzentrum Bad Honnef of the Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft, Bad Honnef, Germany from November 26 to 30, 2001. The workshop concentrated on Chiral Perturbation Theory in its various settings, its relations with lattice QCD and dispersion theory and the use of effective field theories in systems with one, two or more nucleons as well as at high baryon densities. Included are a short contribution per talk and a listing of some review papers on the subject.

## Key claim

## Method
The workshop was held at the Physikzentrum Bad Honnef, Germany from November 26 to 30, 2001, with 57 participants. The proceedings contain a short contribution per talk and a listing of review papers on the subject, following the format of previous workshops in this series (Ringberg 1988, Dobogoko 1991, Karrebaeksminde 1993, Trento 1996, Bad Honnef 1998). The meeting devoted substantial time to discussions rather than presentations.

## Key numbers
- The E865 experiment at Brookhaven confirmed that more than 94% of the pion mass stems from the quark condensate (from the talk by Gasser).
- Isospin violating corrections to the pion-kaon scattering length for the charged-to-neutral channel give a shift of about 1% (from the talk by Kubis).
- Low-energy constants from pion-kaon scattering were estimated at scale mu = m_rho: L_1^r = 0.84 +/- 0.15, L_2^r = 1.36 +/- 0.13, L_3^r = -3.65 +/- 0.45, L_4^r = 0.22 +/- 0.30 (from the talk by Buettiker).

## Context
This workshop continued a series of meetings on effective field theories of QCD, following previous workshops at Ringberg (1988), Dobogoko (1991), Karrebaeksminde (1993), Trento (1996), and Bad Honnef (1998). The proceedings cover Chiral Perturbation Theory in its various settings, chiral nucleon dynamics, chiral few-nucleon dynamics, high-density QCD, and lattice QCD. The meeting enabled discussion of recent developments including the determination of the quark condensate from K_e4 decays (E865 experiment), which resolved a debate about whether the quark condensate is the leading order parameter of chiral symmetry breaking, a question raised by Knecht, Moussallam, Stern and Fuchs (1995) and Girlanda, Knecht, Moussallam and Stern (1997).
