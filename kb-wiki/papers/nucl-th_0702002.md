# Examining the chiral geometry in 104Rh and 106Rh

> The criteria for chiral doublet bands based on one particle and one hole coupled to a triaxial rotor have been summarized. Two representative cases in $A\sim100$ odd-odd nuclei, nearly degenerate $ΔI =1$ doublet bands in $^{104}$Rh and $^{106}$Rh are checked against these chiral criteria. It is shown that $^{106}$Rh possesses better chiral geometry than $^{104}$Rh, although the energy near degeneracy is achieved in $^{104}$Rh in comparison with the constant energy separation of doublet bands in $^{106}$Rh.

## Key claim

Although $^{104}$Rh achieves near energy degeneracy (2 keV at spin $17^{-}$) and has been considered the best candidate for chiral nuclei, it fails multiple chiral criteria (identical $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios, identical spin alignments, vanishing interband E2 transitions), whereas $^{106}$Rh with its constant energy separation of approximately 300 keV actually possesses better chiral geometry.

## Method

The authors applied six established criteria for chiral doublet bands (energy degeneracy, spin independence of energy staggering parameter $S(I)$, staggering of $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios, identical $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ values, identical spin alignments, and vanishing interband E2 transitions at high spin) to the experimental data on the $\pi g_{9/2}\otimes\nu h_{11/2}$ doublet bands in $^{104}$Rh and $^{106}$Rh. They compared excitation energies, energy staggering parameters, intraband $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios, and spin alignments from previously published experimental results for both nuclei.

## Key numbers

- $^{104}$Rh: energy separation of 2 keV at spin $17^{-}$ between the doublet bands, the smallest level degeneracy observed in the $A\sim100$ mass region.
- $^{106}$Rh: constant energy separation of approximately 300 keV between the doublet bands up to high spin states.
- $^{104}$Rh: intraband $B(M1)/B(E2)$ values for the main band are a factor of approximately 2 larger than those of the side band.
- $^{104}$Rh: alignments of the side band are larger by approximately $2\hbar$ than those of the main band in a wide frequency interval.
- $^{104}$Rh: assuming identical $B(M1)$ strengths, the $B(E2)$ values for the main band are a factor of 2 larger than those of the side band, giving a quadrupole moment ratio $Q_{0,main}/Q_{0,side} \simeq 1.4$.

## Context

This work extends the search for chiral doublet bands in the $A\sim100$ mass region, following the original prediction by Frauendorf and Meng (1997) and prior experimental studies on $^{104}$Rh by C. D. et al. (2004) and $^{106}$Rh by J. O. et al. (2004). The analysis shows that the near degeneracy criterion alone is insufficient, as $^{104}$Rh fails multiple chiral criteria in ways similar to $^{134}$Pr, where lifetime measurements by T. O. et al. (2006) gave different E2 transition rates for the partner bands. The results enable future lifetime measurements for $^{104}$Rh to clarify whether shape coexistence rather than chirality explains the band doubling, and support potential energy surface calculations by J. O. et al. (2004) and relativistic mean field calculations by J. M. et al. (2006) that predicted $^{106}$Rh as a better chiral candidate.
