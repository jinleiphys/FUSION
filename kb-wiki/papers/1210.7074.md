# Energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of all antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/c

> We present a new energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of all antiproton-proton elastic and charge-exchange scattering data below 925 MeV/c antiproton laboratory momentum. The long-range parts of the chiral one- and two-pion exchange interactions are included exactly. The short-range interactions, including the coupling to the mesonic annihilation channels, are parametrized by a complex boundary condition at a radius of r=1.2 fm. The updated database, which includes significantly more high-quality charge-exchange data, contains 3749 scattering data. The fit results in chi^2_min/N_df=1.048, where N_df=3578 is the number of degrees of freedom. We discuss the description of the experimental data and we present the antiproton-proton phase-shift parameters.

## Key claim

This paper presents a new energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of all antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/c, achieving a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.048, but the quality of the fit depends on including significantly more high-quality charge-exchange data than previous analyses and on allowing the imaginary parts of the short-range square-well potentials to differ between isospin I=0 and I=1 states.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channels radial Schrödinger equation for the antiproton-proton and antineutron-neutron channels, including the long-range chiral one-pion and two-pion exchange potentials exactly. The short-range interactions, including coupling to mesonic annihilation channels, are parametrized by a complex boundary condition (P-matrix) at a radius of 1.2 fm, using state-dependent complex spherical wells with different depths for isospin I=0 and I=1. The fit to 3749 scattering data points requires 46 P-matrix parameters, and the S-matrix is extracted by matching the numerical solution to Coulomb and Bessel wave functions at large radius.

## Key numbers

- Database contains 3749 scattering data points; fit yields chi-squared_min/N_df = 1.048 with N_df = 3578 degrees of freedom
- Boundary condition radius set to r = 1.2 fm
- Charge-exchange threshold occurs at antiproton laboratory momentum p_lab ≈ 99 MeV/c (T_lab ≈ 5.2 MeV)
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant fixed at f^2 = 0.075 for both elastic and charge-exchange OPE potentials
- Low-energy constants for two-pion exchange: c_3 = -5.8/GeV and c_4 = 4.0/GeV, with estimated uncertainties of order 0.5
- Mixing angles for coupled partial waves: theta_01 = 7.6° ± 0.4°, theta_11 = -10.7° ± 0.8°, theta_12 = -8.8° ± 1.6°, theta_03 = -7.4° ± 0.4°, theta_13 = -6.9° ± 1.4°
- Imaginary well depths for the ^1S_0 partial wave: W_0 = -161.7(25.2) MeV, W_1 = -132.8(19.9) MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier Nijmegen partial-wave analyses of antiproton-proton scattering by Timmermans et al. (1991, 1994, 1995), replacing the charge-conjugated heavy-boson exchanges with chiral two-pion exchange potentials as used in the updated nucleon-nucleon PWAs by Rentmeester et al. (1999, 2003). The inclusion of more recent high-quality charge-exchange differential cross sections and analyzing powers from LEAR provides new constraints on the isospin dependence of the short-range interaction. The results enable future studies of antinucleon-nucleon scattering with polarized antiproton beams, as proposed by the PAX collaboration, and allow tests of charge conjugation invariance in the two-pion exchange interaction.
