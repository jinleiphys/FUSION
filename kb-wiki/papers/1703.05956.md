# Role of the direct processes in low-energy deuteron interactions

> An extended analysis of the key role of direct interactions, i.e., breakup, stripping and pick-up processes, has been carried out for deuteron-induced reactions. Particular comments concern the deuteron breakup which is the dominant mechanism involved in surrogate reactions on heavy nuclei, around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the failure of deuteron surrogate reaction validation tests (e.g., for neutron capture cross sections) is primarily due to the neglect of direct interaction mechanisms, breakup, stripping, and pick-up, in the theoretical framework, rather than solely due to the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors performed an extended analysis of deuteron-induced reactions on nuclei from ²⁷Al to ²³⁸U, using an empirical parametrization of breakup cross sections (elastic and inelastic breakup fractions) validated against experimental data and Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations. Direct reaction cross sections for (d,p), (d,n), (d,t), and (d,α) were calculated using the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) within the FRESCO code. The total direct interaction contribution was quantified as a reduction factor on the deuteron flux available for pre-equilibrium and compound-nucleus processes.

## Key numbers

- For ²³⁸U at Ed = 15 MeV, the total breakup fraction f_BU exceeds unity (f_BU = 1.0215 at Ed,max = 32 MeV for A > 200), requiring an additional constraint that f_BU be less than 90%.
- The normalized elastic breakup fraction f_EB^norm is defined for Ed > Ed,max using the ratio f_EB(Ed,max)/f_BU^p(Ed,max), with Ed,max ≈ 25–30 MeV for lighter nuclei and lower for heavier targets.
- For the ²³⁸U(d,pγ) surrogate reaction at Ed = 15 MeV, the protons from breakup have energies up to Ep,max^BU = 12.65 MeV, overlapping with the proton energy range (12.65–11.15 MeV) corresponding to the excitation energy range of interest (Sn = 4.806 MeV to 1.5 MeV above it).
- The breakup neutrons from the same reaction have energies between 0 and 1.5 MeV, matching the incident-neutron energy range (0–1.5 MeV) for the desired ²³⁸U(n,γ)²³⁹U reaction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Avrigeanu et al., 2014, 2015) on the role of direct interactions in deuteron reactions, and addresses the surrogate reaction method used by Escher (2012), Wilson (2015), and Ducasse (2016) for measuring (n,γ) and (n,f) cross sections via (d,pγ) and (d,pf) reactions. The paper demonstrates that the hard approximations leading to the simplified surrogate formula (Weisskopf-Ewing model) are inappropriate for deuteron-induced reactions, and calls for a revised theoretical framework that consistently accounts for breakup, direct reactions, pre-equilibrium, and compound-nucleus mechanisms. The surrogate ratio method is noted as potentially valid due to possible cancellations of breakup effects (Chiba, 2016).
