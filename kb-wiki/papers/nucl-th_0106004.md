# Deuteron Electromagnetic Properties with a Poincaré-Covariant Current Operator within Front-Form Hamiltonian Dynamics

> Deuteron elastic and deep inelastic electromagnetic properties have been studied within the front-form Hamiltonian dynamics, using a Poincaré-covariant current operator. The deuteron elastic form factors are strongly sensitive to different realistic $N-N$ interactions, while the relevance of different nucleon form factor models is huge for $A(Q^2)$, weak for $B(Q^2)$ and negligible for the tensor polarization. The possibility to gain information on the neutron charge form factor from an analysis of $A(Q^2)$ has been investigated. The extraction of the neutron structure functions from the deuteron deep inelastic structure functions at high $x$ is largely affected by the use of our Poincaré-covariant relativistic approach instead of the usual impulse approximation within an instant-form approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Poincaré-covariant current operator within front-form Hamiltonian dynamics largely removes the usual disagreement between theoretical and experimental values for the deuteron magnetic and quadrupole moments at zero momentum transfer, but the authors caution that explicit two-body currents and isobar components must be thoroughly investigated before drawing definite conclusions on the neutron charge form factor extracted from deuteron elastic data.

## Method

The authors constructed a Poincaré-covariant electromagnetic current operator for bound systems of interacting particles within front-form Hamiltonian dynamics, starting from the free current in the Breit frame where initial and final three-momenta are directed along the spin quantization axis. They applied this operator to calculate deuteron elastic form factors using front-form deuteron wave functions from a large set of realistic N-N interactions (RSC, Paris, Argonne V14 and V18, Nijmegen-1, -2, -93, Reid93, and CD-Bonn) and various nucleon form factor models. For deep inelastic scattering, they used the same covariant current to compute the deuteron deep inelastic structure function and extracted the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio via a recurrence relation, comparing results between the front-form approach and the usual impulse approximation within instant-form dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron magnetic moment and quadrupole moment show a linear behavior against the asymptotic normalization ratio η = A_D/A_S across all realistic N-N interactions tested, with the CD-Bonn interaction results shown as full triangle and diamond in Fig. 1
- Large differences between relativistic and nonrelativistic calculations appear even at low momentum transfer values Q² ≃ 0.2 - 0.3 (GeV/c)²
- Different N-N interactions yield huge effects on A(Q²) at Q² ≥ 1 (GeV/c)², while for B(Q²) and tensor polarizations effects are large already at Q² ≥ 0.5 (GeV/c)²
- The experimental values for the position of the minimum of B(Q²) and the second zero of T20(Q²) are approximately 1.8 (GeV/c)² and 1.0 (GeV/c)², respectively
- The extracted neutron charge form factor G_E^n(Q²) from fitting A(Q²) data turns out to be somewhat higher than the Gari-Krümpelmann model around Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)² and smaller beyond
- Differences in the extracted neutron-to-proton structure function ratio r(x) become quite sizeable at x ≥ 0.7 when comparing the front-form covariant approach to the instant-form impulse approximation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous construction of a Poincaré-covariant current operator (Pace, Salmè, 1999) applied to an exactly solvable two-body model and to deuteron elastic form factors. The results contradict the usual disagreement between theoretical and experimental deuteron moments found in nonrelativistic calculations, and suggest that contributions from dynamical two-body currents and isobar components may play a minor role at Q² → 0 when using this relativistic framework. The paper enables future extraction of neutron structure functions from deuteron deep inelastic scattering data up to x = 0.83 as planned at TJNAF, and motivates further study of explicit two-body current and isobar configuration contributions within the same covariant approach.
