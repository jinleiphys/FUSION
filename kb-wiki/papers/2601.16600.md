# Nuclear molecule of heavy nuclei

> The model of a nuclear molecule that composed of two heavy nuclei is proposed. To this aim the Hamiltonian of a dinuclear system is derived and diagonalized in the basis of bipolar spherical functions. Analytical expressions, describing excitations of highly deformed states of a nuclear molecule, are obtained. A remarkable agreement between numerical and analytical results is demonstrated at the description of roto-vibrational excitations in $^{240}$Pu at low energies. We provide the prediction for the spectrum of hyperdeformed states of the nucleus $^{232}$Th, considering it as a nuclear molecule that consists of $^{132}$Sn+$^{100}$Zr nuclei. The angular distribution of fission fragments for the nucleus $^{240}$Pu have been analyzed as well and compared with available experimental data.

# Nuclear molecule of heavy nuclei

## Key claim

The paper proposes that certain heavy nuclei can be modeled as a molecular system composed of two distinct nuclear fragments, and demonstrates that this description reproduces low-energy roto-vibrational excitations in ²⁴⁰Pu, but only under the condition that the radial motion (relative distance between fragments) is treated as a fast degree of freedom within a Born-Oppenheimer approximation, with vibrational energies (~10 MeV) much larger than rotational energies.

## Method

The authors derive a quantum Hamiltonian for a dinuclear system consisting of an axially-symmetric quadrupole-deformed fragment and a spherical fragment, using Euler angles as dynamical variables and the Pauli quantization procedure. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a basis of bipolar spherical functions, with the interaction potential approximated by a Morse-type nuclear potential plus Coulomb repulsion, and the radial coordinate treated adiabatically via the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. Analytical expressions for the excitation spectrum are obtained for two limiting cases: small deformation (rolling limit) and large deformation (sticking limit with vibrational modes).

## Key numbers

- The condition for existence of a pocket in the molecular potential energy is given by Z²/A⁴/³ ≲ 6, derived from parameters b = 2a with a ≈ 0.55 fm, f₀ ≈ 3.3 MeV/fm³, and R_i = 1.2 A¹/³ fm.
- The vibrational frequency of the radial coordinate is estimated as ℏω_R ≈ 10 MeV, which is stated to be sufficiently larger than rotational energies for angular momenta l ≤ (10–12)ℏ.
- The barrier between the two pole-to-pole configurations (ϵ = 0 and ϵ = π) disappears for nearly symmetric molecules with Z > 100, characteristic of actinide and transactinide mass regions.
- The paper provides a prediction for the hyperdeformed spectrum of ²³²Th, modeled as a molecular system consisting of ¹³²Sn + ¹⁰⁰Zr.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear molecule concept previously applied to light systems such as ²⁶Mg+²⁶Mg by Uegaki (1993, 2012), who used an asymmetric rotator Hamiltonian that neglected Coriolis coupling and underestimated the rotational moment of inertia. The present paper derives a self-consistent Hamiltonian treating the interaction between internal vibrations and rotation exactly, enabling application to heavy fragments where the earlier approximation would lead to incorrect conclusions. The approach builds on the dinuclear system (DNS) concept used in fusion-fission reactions by Volkov (1982) and on the description of hyperdeformed states in mean-field calculations by Cwiok (1994), who showed that the HD state of ²³²Th can be described as a binary system of ¹³²Sn and ¹⁰⁰Mo. The results enable spectroscopic studies of nuclei in strongly-elongated configurations using new experimental facilities with intense beams of monochromatic gamma quanta.
