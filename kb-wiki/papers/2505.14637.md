# Left-right splitting of elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions: TRENTo-3D initialization and CLVisc hydrodynamic simulations

> Using the TRENTo-3D initial condition model coupled with (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamic simulations, we systematically investigate the left-right splitting of elliptic flow ($Δv_{2}$) for soft particles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Our study reveals that the final distribution characteristics of $Δv_{2}$ are primarily depend on the odd flow harmonics and $v_{2}$ itself. We find that the parton transverse momentum scale $k_\mathrm{T}$ not only determines the geometric tilt of the QGP fireball but also significantly affects the rapidity dependence of both $v_1$ and $Δv_{2}$, providing new insights into the splitting mechanism of $Δv_{2}$. Furthermore, our results demonstrate that $Δv_{2} (p_\mathrm{T})$ exhibits significant sensitivity to influences such as the sub-nucleonic degrees of freedom (or `hotspots'), transverse momentum scale, and fragmentation region profile. By analyzing the $Δv_{2}$ and $Δv_{2}/v_{2}$ ratio, our findings provide new constraints on the uncertainties of the QGP initial state and provide additional constraints for refining model parameters.

## Key claim

The left-right splitting of elliptic flow ($\Delta v_{2}$) is primarily driven by odd flow harmonics, with directed flow ($v_{1}$) dominating the pseudorapidity dependence, but triangular flow ($v_{3}$) playing a significant and previously unrecognized role in the transverse momentum dependence, particularly in the region $|\eta| < 1.3$.

## Method

The authors used the TRENTo-3D initial condition model to generate three-dimensional energy density profiles for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, then evolved the QGP medium using (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamic simulations with the HotQCD2014 equation of state. They computed the elliptic flow splitting $\Delta v_{2}$ by separating particles into right-side ($\phi^{R} \in ((\psi_{\mathrm{RP}} - \pi/2), (\psi_{\mathrm{RP}} + \pi/2))$) and left-side ($\phi^{L} \in ((\psi_{\mathrm{RP}} + \pi/2), (\psi_{\mathrm{RP}} + 3\pi/2))$) hemispheres relative to the reaction plane. The sensitivity of $\Delta v_{2}$ to initial-state parameters was systematically studied by varying the sub-nucleonic degrees of freedom ($n_c$), transverse momentum scale ($k_\mathrm{T}$), and fragmentation profile parameters ($\alpha$ and $\beta$).

## Key numbers

- The slope $d[\Delta v_{2}/v_{2}]/d\eta$ reaches 4.4% for $|\eta| < 1.0$ in 5-40% centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- $\Delta v_{2}/v_{2}$ crosses zero at $p_\mathrm{T} \sim 1.5$ GeV in the same collision system
- The default TRENTo-3D parameters used include: $n_c = 16.4$, $k_\mathrm{T} = 0.33$ GeV, $\alpha = 4.6$, $\beta = 0.19$, $w = 1.3$ fm, $\chi = 0.5$, $f = 1.0$
- The freeze-out energy density was set to $e_{\mathrm{frz}} = 0.4$ GeV/fm$^3$
- The centrality range studied was 5-40% for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of elliptic flow splitting by Zhang (2021) and Parida (2022), which established that $\Delta v_{2}$ is related to the non-zero angular momentum and longitudinal tilted geometry of the QGP fireball, with $v_{1}$ as the primary contributor. The present study reveals that $v_{3}$ plays a significant role in $\Delta v_{2}(p_\mathrm{T})$, unlike earlier findings. The results demonstrate that $\Delta v_{2}$ can serve as a sensitive probe for constraining the three-dimensional initial state of the QGP and refining parameters in the TRENTo-3D model, complementing measurements of $v_{1}$ and $v_{3}$.
