# Bayesian inference of the fluctuating proton shape in DIS and hadronic collisions

> We determine the likelihood distribution for the model parameters describing the event-by-event fluctuating proton geometry at small $x$ by performing a Bayesian analysis within the Color Glass Condensate framework. The exclusive $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ production data from HERA is found to constrain the model parameters well, and we demonstrate that complementary constraints can be obtained from simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.

# Bayesian inference of the fluctuating proton shape in DIS and hadronic collisions

## Key claim
The exclusive J/ψ production data from HERA constrains most parameters of a hot-spot model for the fluctuating proton geometry at small x, but the data alone cannot determine the number of hot spots or the strength of repulsive short-range correlations between them; complementary constraints can be obtained from simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian analysis within the Color Glass Condensate framework, using Gaussian process emulators and Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to determine the posterior likelihood distribution for model parameters describing event-by-event fluctuating proton geometry. The dipole-proton scattering amplitude was obtained using the same approach as in the IP-Glasma calculation, with the proton density profile written as a sum of Gaussian hot spots whose positions, sizes, and normalizations are sampled event-by-event. The model was constrained by exclusive J/ψ production data from HERA at W = 75 GeV, and then used to generate initial conditions for IP-Glasma + MUSIC + UrQMD simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- HERA J/ψ production data at W = 75 GeV and xℙ ≈ 10⁻³ was used to constrain the model parameters
- Pb+Pb collisions were simulated at √s = 5.02 TeV
- Two analyses were performed: one with fixed N_q = 3 hot spots and one with N_q as a free parameter
- The ALICE data on multiplicity distribution and flow harmonics v₂{2} and v₂{4} in Pb+Pb collisions prefers the N_q = 3 parametrization over N_q = 9

## Context
This work extends the hot-spot model of Mantysaari and Schenke (2016) by performing a full Bayesian inference on the model parameters using HERA data from Alexa et al. (2013). It builds on the IP-Glasma framework of Schenke et al. (2012) and the finding by Albacete et al. (2017) that repulsive short-range correlations between hot spots are necessary to describe high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions. The obtained likelihood distribution enables systematic uncertainty propagation for any observable depending on event-by-event fluctuating proton geometry, and demonstrates that heavy-ion collision data from the LHC can provide complementary constraints beyond what HERA data alone can determine.
