# Nuclear enhancement and suppression of diffractive structure functions at high energies

> We compute diffractive structure functions for both protons and nuclei in the framework of Color Glass Condensate models with impact parameter dependence. These models have previously been shown to provide good agreement with inclusive F_2 measurements and exclusive vector meson measurements at HERA. For nuclei, they provide good (parameter free) agreement with the inclusive F_2 data. We demonstrate good agreement of our computations with HERA measurements on inclusive diffraction. We extend our analysis to nuclei and predict the pattern of enhancement and suppression of the diffractive structures functions that can be measured at an Electron Ion Collider. We discuss how the impact parameter dependence crucially affects our analysis, in particular for large invariant masses at fixed Q^2.

## Key claim

The authors compute diffractive structure functions for protons and nuclei using Color Glass Condensate (CGC) models with impact parameter dependence, and predict that nuclear diffractive structure functions will show both enhancement and suppression relative to the proton scaled by atomic number, with the pattern depending on the specific kinematic region and on whether the nucleus breaks up.

## Method

The authors use the IPsat (impact parameter saturation) and bCGC (b-dependent Color Glass Condensate) dipole models, which incorporate DGLAP evolution and small-x evolution respectively, to compute the q-qbar and q-qbar-g contributions to diffractive structure functions. For nuclei, the dipole cross section is obtained via a Glauber-type averaging over nucleon positions distributed according to the Woods-Saxon distribution, with no additional free parameters beyond those fit to HERA proton data. The q-qbar-g contribution is computed using an interpolation between the large-Q^2 (GBW) and beta-to-0 (MS) limits.

## Key numbers

- The IPsat model fit to combined HERA diffractive data gives chi^2 = 1.3 with alpha_s = 0.14 for the q-qbar-g coupling
- The bCGC model fit to combined HERA diffractive data gives chi^2 = 1.3 with alpha_s = 0.22 for the q-qbar-g coupling
- The IPsat fit to only the ZEUS LPS data (proton intact) gives chi^2 = 0.5 with alpha_s = 0.14
- The bCGC fit to only the H1 FPS data gives chi^2 = 0.8
- The ZEUS FPC data is scaled down by a factor of 1.45 and the H1 LRG data by a factor of 1.23 to correct for proton dissociation
- The total number of experimental points in the four HERA data sets is 343, of which 76 are from FPS-LPS data

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of nuclear inclusive structure functions and elastic q-qbar scattering (Kowalski, Lappi, Venugopalan 2007) by including the q-qbar-g Fock state contribution to diffraction, which is essential for a complete description. It builds on the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff dipole model (1999) and the DGLAP-improved IPsat model (Bartels, Golec-Biernat, Kowalski 2002), and provides predictions for diffractive structure functions at a future Electron Ion Collider. The results contrast with earlier factorized impact-parameter treatments (Golec-Biernat, Wusthoff 1999; Marquet 2007) by showing that the q-qbar-g component has a broader impact parameter distribution than the q-qbar component, which affects the nuclear enhancement/suppression pattern.
