# Strong decays of the higher excited $Λ_Q$ and $Σ_Q$ baryons

> In this work, we preform a systematic study of the strong decays for the higher singly heavy baryon resonances $Λ_Q(3S)$, $Λ_Q(2P)$, $Λ_Q(2D)$, $Λ_Q(1F)$, $Σ_Q(3S)$, $Σ_Q(2P)$, $Σ_Q(2D)$, and $Σ_Q(1F)$ within the $^3P_0$ model. Our results show that most of the $λ-$mode higher excited $Λ_Q$ and $Σ_Q$ states have a relatively narrow width, and mainly decay into two-body final states with one heavy meson plus one light baryon. Our calculations provide abundant theoretical information and demonstrate the general feature for these higher states, which may be valuable for future experimental searches and establishing the singly heavy baryon spectrum.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate strong decay widths for higher excited singly heavy baryons (Λ_Q and Σ_Q with Q=c,b) in the N=3 and N=4 shells using the ^3P_0 model, finding that most λ-mode states have relatively narrow total widths and decay predominantly into a heavy meson plus a light baryon, but these predictions depend on the adopted quark-model masses and the harmonic oscillator parameter α_ρ.

## Method

The authors employ the ^3P_0 quark pair creation model to compute OZI-allowed two-body strong decay widths of Λ_Q(3S), Λ_Q(2P), Λ_Q(2D), Λ_Q(1F), Σ_Q(3S), Σ_Q(2P), Σ_Q(2D), and Σ_Q(1F) states. Harmonic oscillator wave functions are used for the spatial overlap integrals, with the overall pair-production strength γ=9.83 fixed by reproducing the width Γ[Σ_c(2520)^(++) → Λ_c π^+] = 14.78 MeV. The initial baryon masses are taken from the relativistic quark model of Ebert et al. (2011), and final-state meson and baryon masses are from the Particle Data Group review (Tanabashi et al., 2018).

## Key numbers

- Λ_c(3S) total width: 69.03 MeV, with branching ratio Br(Λ_c(3S) → D*N) = 95%
- Λ_b(3S) total width: 51.65 MeV, with branching ratio Br(Λ_b(3S) → B*N) = 89%
- Λ_c(2P) J^P=1/2^- total width: 39.84 MeV; J^P=3/2^- total width: 154.67 MeV (using predicted masses 2983 and 3005 MeV respectively)
- Λ_b(2P) J^P=1/2^- total width: 90.90 MeV; J^P=3/2^- total width: 162.42 MeV (using predicted masses 6326 and 6333 MeV respectively)
- Λ_c(2D) J^P=3/2^+ total width: 57.32 MeV; J^P=5/2^+ total width: 73.00 MeV (using predicted masses 3189 and 3209 MeV respectively)
- Λ_b(2D) J^P=3/2^+ total width: 107.06 MeV; J^P=5/2^+ total width: 106.67 MeV (using predicted masses 6526 and 6531 MeV respectively)
- Partial width ratio Γ[Λ_c2(3/2^+,2D)→DN] : Γ[Λ_c2(3/2^+,2D)→D*N] = 9×10^(-3)
- Partial width ratio Γ[Λ_c2(5/2^+,2D)→DN] : Γ[Λ_c2(5/2^+,2D)→D*N] = 0.63
- Harmonic oscillator parameters: α_ρ = 400 MeV; light quark masses m_u/d = 220 MeV, m_s = 419 MeV, m_c = 1628 MeV, m_b = 4977 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous quark-model studies of low-lying singly heavy baryons (Capstick and Isgur, 1986; Ebert et al., 2007, 2011; Chen et al., 2014) to higher excitations in the N=3 and N=4 shells, where few decay calculations existed. The predictions for dominant decays into heavy meson plus light baryon channels (D(*)N, B(*)N) provide guidance for experimental searches at LHCb and Belle II, complementing the mass-spectrum calculations of Ebert et al. (2011). The results also connect to the interpretation of the observed Λ_c(2940) as a J^P=3/2^- Λ_c(2P) state, as previously argued by Lu and Zhong (2018).
