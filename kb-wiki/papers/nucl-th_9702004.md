# Equation of State of Hadronic matter and Electromagnetic Radiation from Relativisitic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the radiation of thermal photons and dileptons likely to be produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We find that the thermal photon multiplicity scales with the charged pion multiplicity as $dN_{ch}/dy)^α$ with $α\sim 1.2$ for a transversely expanding system, contrary to the general belief of a quadratic dependence. The scaling is shown to be valid, both for real and virtual photons. The coefficient of proportionality at a given energy may help us identify the appropriate equation of state of hot hadronic matter produced in such collisions.

# Equation of State of Hadronic Matter and Electromagnetic Radiation from Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Thermal photon multiplicity scales with charged pion multiplicity as $(dN_{ch}/dy)^\alpha$ with $\alpha \sim 1.2$ for a transversely expanding system, contrary to the general belief of a quadratic dependence; the proportionality constant $K$ in this scaling relation depends on the equation of state of hot hadronic matter, which may help identify the appropriate equation of state, but only if the minimal extension of Bjorken hydrodynamics is reliable.

## Method

The authors performed hydrodynamic calculations assuming a boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric transversely expanding fireball initially in a quark-gluon plasma state, with initial conditions related to particle rapidity density via isentropic expansion. They computed thermal photon and dilepton yields by convoluting production rates (Compton and annihilation in QGP; reactions involving $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, $\eta$ mesons and $\pi\rho \rightarrow a_1 \rightarrow \pi\gamma$ in hadronic matter) with the space-time evolution for two different hadronic equations of state: a "simplified" one containing only $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\eta$ mesons, and a "full" one including all hadrons from the Particle Data Table.

## Key numbers

- Thermal photon multiplicity scales as $(dN_{ch}/dy)^\alpha$ with $\alpha \sim 1.2$ for a transversely expanding system.
- Thermal dilepton multiplicity (invariant mass $M$ between 0.5 and 1 GeV) scales with $\alpha \sim 1.1$.
- The simplified equation of state (only $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, $\eta$) produces about twice as many thermal photons and twice as many thermal dileptons as the full equation of state.
- Transition temperature fixed at 160 MeV; freeze-out temperature fixed at 100 MeV.
- Initial time $\tau_i$ set to the canonical value of 1 fm/$c$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Cleymans, Redlich, and Srivastava (1996) on the equation of state in connection with thermal photon and particle production at CERN SPS lead-lead collisions. The scaling result contradicts the quadratic dependence assumed by Cerny, Lichard, and Pisut (1986) and instead supports the $N_\pi^{4/3}$ scaling argued by Feinberg (1976) from general considerations. The findings enable experimental distinction between different hadronic equations of state by measuring the proportionality constant $K$ in the scaling relation at a given energy, and the authors note that the excess dileptons observed at SPS by the CERES and HELIOS collaborations scale with exponent $\beta > 1$ but lack sufficient statistics to distinguish the equation of state.
