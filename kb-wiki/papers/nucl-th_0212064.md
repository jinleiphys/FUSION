# Medium modifications of kaons in pion matter

> Kaon in-medium masses and mean-field potentials are calculated in isotopically symmetric pion matter to one loop of chiral perturbation theory. The results are extended to RHIC temperatures using experimental data on $πK$ scattering phase shifts. The kaon in-medium broadening results in an acceleration of the $ϕ\to K\bar{K}$ decay. The increased apparent dilepton branching of the $ϕ$-mesons, observed recently by NA50, NA49, and the PHENIX collaborations at RHIC, is interpreted in terms of rescattering of secondary kaons inside of the pion matter.

## Key claim

In isotopically symmetric pion matter at RHIC temperatures, kaons experience a negative mass shift and a positive mean-field potential, leading to an in-medium broadening that accelerates the $\phi \to K\bar{K}$ decay; this broadening, combined with rescattering of secondary kaons, can explain the factor of two to three increase in the apparent dilepton branching of $\phi$-mesons observed by NA50, NA49, and PHENIX, without requiring a large modification of the $\phi$ mass itself.

## Method

The kaon self-energy operator $\Sigma(p^2,E)$ was calculated in isotopically symmetric pion matter using one-loop chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), expressed in terms of $\pi K$ forward scattering amplitudes and integrated over thermal pion distributions. For temperatures up to $T \sim 170$ MeV, the low-temperature ChPT results were extended by replacing the threshold expansion with unitarized amplitudes built from experimental $\pi K$ scattering phase shifts and the $K^*$ resonance. The in-medium $\phi \to K\bar{K}$ decay width was then computed by folding the vacuum decay with a Breit-Wigner distribution for the kaon spectral function, using the calculated kaon mass shift $\delta M_K$, mean-field potential $V_K$, and collision width $\Gamma_K^*$.

## Key numbers

- At $T = 170$ MeV, the kaon mass shift $\delta M_K = -33$ MeV and the mean-field potential $V_K = 21$ MeV.
- At $T = 170$ MeV, the kaon collision width $\Gamma_K^* = 81$ MeV.
- At $T = 170$ MeV, the enhancement factor for the $\phi \to K\bar{K}$ decay is $\eta \sim 3$, giving an in-medium $\phi$ width $\Gamma_\phi^{\rm med} \sim 12$ MeV.
- The apparent dilepton branching ratio $B^{\rm app}/B$ is estimated to be $2 \div 3$ for temperatures between $T = 120$ and $170$ MeV, assuming a $\phi$ survival probability $e^{-\tau \Gamma_\phi} \sim 1/2$ at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of kaon in-medium modifications in dense nuclear matter (Kaplan, Brown, Waas) to the baryon-dilute, meson-rich environment of RHIC heavy-ion collisions, where pions dominate. It builds on one-loop ChPT calculations of the $\pi K$ system (Dobado, Pelaez) and on earlier discussions of $\phi$-meson dilepton branching in a pion gas by Lissauer and Shuryak and by Blaizot and Galain. The results provide a quantitative interpretation of the "$\phi$ puzzle", the increased apparent dilepton branching reported by NA50, NA49, and PHENIX, in terms of kaon rescattering and in-medium broadening, and motivate future refined fireball dynamics studies to resolve the puzzle completely.
