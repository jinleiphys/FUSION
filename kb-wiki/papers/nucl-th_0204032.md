# A simple functional form for proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections

> A simple functional form has been found that gives a good representation of the total reaction cross sections for the scattering of protons from (15) nuclei spanning the mass range ${}^{9}$Be to ${}^{238}$U and for proton energies ranging from 20 to 300 MeV.

## Key claim

A three-parameter functional form can reproduce proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections for 15 nuclei from ⁹Be to ²³⁸U at energies from 20 to 300 MeV, but the integral approximation using linear trends for the parameters a and epsilon can deviate from the full calculation by 10% or more, especially at low energies and for light nuclei.

## Method

The authors first calculated partial reaction cross sections sigma_l(E) for proton scattering from 15 nuclei using g-folding optical potentials built from realistic NN interactions (BonnB) folded with nuclear structure models (shell model for ¹²C, Skyrme-Hartree-Fock for ²⁰⁸Pb). They then fitted these calculated partial cross sections with a three-parameter function (l0, a, epsilon) of the form sigma_l = (2l+1)[1+exp((l-l0)/a)]^{-1} + epsilon(2l0+1) exp((l-l0)/a)[1+exp((l-l0)/a)]^{-2}. The total reaction cross sections were obtained by summing these fitted partial cross sections, and also by evaluating an integral approximation of the sum.

## Key numbers

- The functional form reproduces total reaction cross sections to within 1-6% (ratios R_I in Table I range from 0.99 to 1.06) when using individually fitted parameters for each nucleus and energy.
- The integral approximation using linear trend parameters gives ratios R_II ranging from 0.88 to 2.04 across the 15 nuclei and 10 energies (20-300 MeV).
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at 20 MeV, the integral approximation ratio is 2.04 (overestimating by 104%); for ²³⁸U at 20 MeV it is 2.00.
- The l0 parameter scales as l0 ~ kR + 2.3 with R ~ 1.32 A^{1/3}.
- The a parameter follows a ~ 1.02 k - 0.25, and epsilon ~ -1.5 as mean trend lines.
- Energies studied: 20, 30, 40, 50, 65, 100, 150, 200, 250, and 300 MeV.
- Mass range: ⁹Be (A=9) to ²³⁸U (A=238).

## Context

This work extends the g-folding optical potential calculations of Amos (2000) and Deb (2001) for ¹²C and ²⁰⁸Pb to a simple analytic form covering 15 nuclei across the periodic table. The functional form enables rapid evaluation of proton-nucleus reaction cross sections for applications including radioactive ion beam production (Geissel 1995), cosmic ray abundance studies (Webber 1987), space radiation shielding (Wilson 1982), accelerator-driven transmutation systems (Ikeda 2002, Gudowski 2002, Shubin 2002), and radiation therapy dosimetry (Chadwick 2000). The authors note that the integral approximation with linear parameter trends is unreliable for light nuclei and low energies, recommending instead a tabulated interpolation of the three parameters for best accuracy.
