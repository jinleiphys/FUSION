# Total Prompt Energy Release in the Neutron-Induced Fission of 235-U, 238-U, and 239-Pu

> This study addresses, for the first time, the total prompt energy release and its components for the fission of 235-U, 238-U, and 239-Pu as a function of the kinetic energy of the neutron inducing the fission. The components are extracted from experimental measurements, where they exist, together with model-dependent calculation, interpolation, and extrapolation. While the components display clear dependencies upon the incident neutron energy, their sums display only weak, yet definite, energy dependencies. Also addressed is the total prompt energy deposition in fission for the same three systems. Results are presented in equation form. New measurements are recommended as a consequence of this study.

## Key claim

The total prompt energy release in neutron-induced fission of 235-U, 238-U, and 239-Pu decreases with increasing incident neutron energy over the range 0 to 15 MeV, contrary to intuition, though the sum of components shows only weak energy dependence; the results rely on a combination of existing experimental measurements where available and model-dependent calculation, interpolation, and extrapolation, and the author recommends new measurements to improve the results.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of prompt fission energy release by providing the first systematic evaluation as a function of incident neutron energy for 235-U, 238-U, and 239-Pu, using experimental data from Meadows and Budtz-Jorgensen (1982), Straede et al. (1987), Z├Âller (1995), Akimov et al. (1971), and Frehaut et al. (1982), combined with the Los Alamos model of Madland and Nix. The results enable improved modeling of energy deposition in nuclear reactors and fission-based systems, and the author recommends new measurements of fragment kinetic energies and gamma emission energies, especially for 238-U and 239-Pu at higher incident energies, to reduce the reliance on model-dependent interpolation.
