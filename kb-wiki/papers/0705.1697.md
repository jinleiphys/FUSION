# $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson production at RHIC

> Production of $D_{sJ}$(2317) mesons in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC is studied. Using the quark coalescence model, we first determine the initial number of $D_{sJ}$(2317) mesons produced during hadronization of created quark-gluon plasma. The predicted $D_{sJ}$(2317) abundance depends sensitively on the quark structure of the $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson. An order-of-magnitude larger yield is obtained for a conventional two-quark than for an exotic four-quark $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson. To include the hadronic effect on the $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson yield, we have evaluated the absorption cross sections of the $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson by pion, rho, anti-kaon, and vector anti-kaon in a phenomenological hadronic model. Taking into consideration the absorption and production of $D_{sJ}$(2317) mesons during the hadronic stage of heavy ion collisions via a kinetic model, we find that the final yield of $D_{sJ}$(2317) mesons remains sensitive to its initial number produced from the quark-gluon plasma, providing thus the possibility of studying the quark structure of the $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson and its production mechanism in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The final yield of $D_{sJ}$(2317) mesons in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC remains sensitive to its initial number produced from the quark-gluon plasma even after accounting for hadronic absorption and production, providing a way to distinguish between a conventional two-quark and an exotic four-quark structure of this meson.

## Method

The authors used a quark coalescence model to compute the initial number of $D_{sJ}$(2317) mesons produced at hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, with the Wigner distribution functions derived from harmonic oscillator wave functions for both two-quark and four-quark configurations. They then evaluated absorption cross sections for reactions $\pi D_{sJ}\to KD^*(K^*D)$, $\rho D_{sJ}\to KD$, $\bar K D_{sJ}\to \rho D(\pi D^*)$, and $\bar K^* D_{sJ}\to \pi D$ using a phenomenological hadronic model with coupling constants and form factors determined from QCD sum rules. Finally, they solved a kinetic rate equation to track the time evolution of the $D_{sJ}$(2317) abundance through the mixed and hadronic phases of the expanding fire-cylinder.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence model yields per unit rapidity: $9.8\times 10^{-3}$ for a two-quark $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson and $4.2\times 10^{-4}$ for a four-quark $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson (from Monte Carlo evaluation)
- Statistical model yield at hadronization: $5.2\times 10^{-2}$ $D_{sJ}$(2317) mesons (independent of quark structure)
- Coupling constant $g_{D_{sJ}DK}=9.2$ GeV for a two-quark state and $g_{D_{sJ}DK}=3.15$ GeV for a four-quark state
- Root-mean-square radius: 0.53 fm for a two-quark $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson and 0.62 fm for a four-quark $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson
- Charm quark number $N_c=3$ used in calculations (with range from 1.5 to 7 depending on experimental input)
- Final hadron numbers at freeze-out: 926 pions, 133 kaons, 113 anti-kaons, and 47 nucleons in midrapidity ($|y|\leq 0.5$)
- Freeze-out temperature $T_F=125$ MeV and freeze-out time $\tau_F\approx 17.3$ fm/$c$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of $D_{sJ}$(2317) production in heavy ion collisions by Zhongbiao (2005) and complements the coupled-channel quark model study of two-quark $D_{sJ}$(2317) production in hadronic reactions by George (2005). The paper enables future experimental searches at RHIC to distinguish between the two-quark and four-quark interpretations of the $D_{sJ}$(2317) meson, which was first observed by the BABAR Collaboration (2003) and confirmed by Belle (2003), and whose nature remains debated between conventional $c\bar s$ state, four-quark state, or $DK$ molecule.
