# $J/ψ$ and $ψ$(2S) polarization in proton-proton collisions at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider using PYTHIA8

> The production mechanisms of charmonium states in both hadronic and heavy-ion collisions hold great significance for investigating the hot and dense QCD matter. Studying charmonium polarization in ultra-relativistic collisions can also provide insights into the underlying production mechanisms. With this motivation, we explore the $J/ψ$ and $ψ$(2S) polarization in proton+proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7, 8, and 13 TeV using a pQCD-inspired Monte-Carlo event generator called PYTHIA8. This work considers reconstructed quarkonia through their dimuons decay channel in the ALICE forward rapidity acceptance range of $2.5 < y_{μμ} < 4$. Further, we calculate the polarization parameters $λ_θ$, $λ_ϕ$, $λ_{θϕ}$ from the polar and azimuthal angular distributions of the dimuons in helicity and Collins-Soper frames. This study presents a comprehensive measurement of the polarization parameters as a function of transverse momentum, charged-particle multiplicity, and rapidity at the LHC energies. Our findings of charmonium polarization are in qualitative agreement with the corresponding experimental data.

## Key claim

The PYTHIA8 event generator, using leading-order NRQCD mechanisms, predicts that J/ψ and ψ(2S) mesons are longitudinally polarized at low transverse momentum and transversely polarized at high transverse momentum in both helicity and Collins-Soper reference frames, with results showing only qualitative agreement with experimental data from ALICE and LHCb. The authors note that PYTHIA8 overestimates J/ψ polarization in some transverse momentum bins compared to experimental data, suggesting that additional physical processes beyond those implemented in PYTHIA8 may contribute to charmonium polarization in realistic collisions.

## Method

The authors generated 1.5 billion inelastic, non-diffractive proton-proton collision events at √s = 7, 8, and 13 TeV using PYTHIA8 with the 4C tune, including multi-parton interactions and color reconnection. J/ψ and ψ(2S) mesons were produced through the NRQCD framework and forced to decay into dimuon pairs, with the polarization parameters λθ, λφ, and λθφ extracted from the polar and azimuthal angular distributions of the decay muons in the helicity and Collins-Soper reference frames within the ALICE forward rapidity acceptance (2.5 < yμμ < 4.0).

## Key numbers

- Polarization parameters were studied at three collision energies: √s = 7, 8, and 13 TeV
- The rapidity acceptance window used was 2.5 < yμμ < 4.0, matching the ALICE muon spectrometer coverage
- A transverse momentum cut of 0.5 GeV (PhaseSpace:pTHatMinDiverge = 0.5) was applied to avoid QCD divergences
- Angular distributions were shown for a representative pT bin of 2.0 < pT < 3.0 GeV/c
- The rapidity dependence study used rapidity bins of width 0.3 over the range 2.5 to 4.0
- The λθφ parameter was found to be close to zero for both J/ψ and ψ(2S) as a function of transverse momentum, consistent with LHC results
- For ψ(2S), the degree of longitudinal polarization (λθ) increases with charged-particle multiplicity, while for J/ψ the longitudinal polarization remains approximately constant across multiplicity classes

## Context

This work extends previous PYTHIA8 studies of charmonium production by the same group (Deb 2018, Thakur 2017) and addresses the long-standing "J/ψ polarization puzzle" identified by ALICE (2011) and others, where theoretical predictions from NRQCD and the color singlet model fail to consistently describe experimental polarization measurements. The results are compared with NLO NRQCD calculations from Butenschoen (2012), Gong (2012), and Chao (2012) as well as with experimental data from ALICE (2011, 2018) and LHCb (2013). The study enables future investigations of prompt versus non-prompt charmonium polarization using the upgraded ALICE Muon Forward Tracker in Run 3 and Run 4, and provides a baseline for understanding medium effects in heavy-ion collisions through the charged-particle multiplicity dependence of polarization parameters.
