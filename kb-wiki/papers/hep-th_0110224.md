# Derivation of Transport Equations using the Time-Dependent Projection Operator Method

> We develop a formalism to carry out coarse-grainings in quantum field theoretical systems by using a time-dependent projection operator in the Heisenberg picture. A systematic perturbative expansion with respect to the interaction part of the Hamiltonian is given, and a Langevin-type equation without a time-convolution integral term is obtained. This method is applied to a quantum field theoretical model, and coupled transport equations are derived.

## Key claim

The paper develops a systematic perturbative expansion for a Langevin-type equation without time-convolution integral terms using a time-dependent projection operator in the Heisenberg picture, applicable to quantum field theoretical systems where the irrelevant subsystem may have time dependence and both subsystems can be treated on equal footing rather than one serving as a thermal reservoir.

## Method

The author derives a time-convolutionless (TCL) equation from the Heisenberg equation of motion by introducing time-dependent projection operators P(t) and Q(t) that satisfy the condition P(t1)P(t2) = P(t2) and the condition Q(t2)L0(t1,t0)P(t1) = 0, where L0 is the nonperturbative Liouville operator. The formalism is applied to a model Hamiltonian with two interacting boson fields (sigma and pi bosons) coupled through an interaction term g pi^2 phi, where the projection operator traces over the environment degrees of freedom using a local equilibrium density matrix with time-dependent temperature. Coupled transport equations for the sigma and pi distribution functions are derived by treating each boson as the system and the other as the environment in turn, using thermo-field dynamics techniques to evaluate correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The interaction Hamiltonian is H_I = integral d^3 x g pi^2(x) phi(x) with coupling constant g
- The sigma boson dispersion relation is omega^sigma_k = sqrt(k^2 + m_sigma^2) and the pi boson dispersion relation is omega^pi_k = sqrt(k^2 + m_pi^2)
- The transport equation for the sigma distribution function n^sigma_k contains four cosine terms with arguments (omega^pi_q + omega^pi_{q+k} +/- omega^sigma_k)(s-t) and (omega^pi_q - omega^pi_{q+k} +/- omega^sigma_k)(s-t)
- The transport equation for the pi distribution function n^pi_k contains four cosine terms with arguments (omega^sigma_{l+k} +/- omega^pi_l +/- omega^pi_k)(s-t)

## Context

This work extends the time-independent projection operator methods of Nakajima-Zwanzig and Mori to time-dependent projection operators, building on Robertson's (unknown year) original time-dependent projection operator approach and the improvements by Kawasaki et al. (unknown year) and Willis et al. (unknown year). The formalism enables the derivation of coupled transport equations where both subsystems are treated on equal footing, unlike the usual approach where one subsystem is assumed to be a heat bath in thermal equilibrium as in the work of Renormalization (unknown year). The derived equations contain terms proportional to a^dagger_k a^dagger_{-k} and a_k a_{-k} that form an SU(1,1) symmetry group as noted by Shibata et al. (unknown year), and the fluctuation force term satisfies the fluctuation-dissipation theorem in the Tokuyama-Mori formalism, though the author notes that establishing this theorem within the time-dependent formalism remains an open problem.
