# The Casimir-Polder interaction between two neutrons and possible relevance to tetraneutron states

> We present a summary of our recent publication concerning the derivation of the extended Casimir-Polder dispersive interaction between two neutrons. Dynamical polarizations of the neutrons, recently derived within Chiral Effective Theory are used for the purpose. An account of the higher frequency/energy behavior of these entities related to the opening of one-pion production channel and the excitation of the $Δ$ resonance are taken into consideration in our derivation of the CP interaction. The neutron-neutron system in free space is treated in details so are the neutron-wall and the wall-neutron-wall systems. The case of tetraneutron (a 4 neutron system) in a resonant state is then briefly considered. The 4n CP interaction is evaluated to assess its potential relevance to the ongoing debate concerning the nature of the tetraneutron.

# The Casimir-Polder interaction between two neutrons and possible relevance to tetraneutron states

## Key claim
The Casimir-Polder dispersive interaction between neutrons, derived using dynamical polarizabilities that include the one-pion production channel and the Δ resonance, is appreciable and potentially detectable through careful analysis of low-energy neutron-neutron scattering; however, the authors note that their results are valid only for distances beyond 50 fm, and the expected asymptotic behavior is only reached for separations greater than approximately 1000 fm.

## Method
The authors parametrized the dynamic electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities of the neutron using forms that incorporate the pion production threshold cusp and the Δ resonance contribution, fitting these parametrizations to covariant baryon chiral effective field theory (CB-χEFT) results from Lensky, McGovern, and Pascalutsa (2015). They then computed the Casimir-Polder potential between two neutrons via the standard integral over imaginary frequencies, and extended the calculation to three-neutron and four-neutron systems using the non-additive dispersive potential formalism of Power (1985) for specific geometric configurations (equilateral triangle, linear chain, and tetrahedron).

## Key numbers
- The electric polarizability of the neutron used throughout is α_n = 12.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- For the equilateral triangular configuration of three neutrons: V^{CP}_{3n}(r) = 2.12 × 10⁻⁷ × (1/r¹⁰) MeV, with r in femtometers.
- For the linear chain configuration of three neutrons (inner n-n separation r/2): V^{CP}_{3n}(r) = −2.28 × 10⁻⁵ × (1/r¹⁰) MeV, with r in femtometers.
- For the tetrahedron configuration of four neutrons (edge length r): V^{CP}_{4n}(r) = −1.55 × 10⁻⁹ × (1/r¹³) MeV, with r in femtometers.
- The tetraneutron resonance reported by Kisamori et al. (2016) has energy E_R = 0.83 ± 0.63 (statistical) ∓ 1.25 (systematic) MeV above the four-neutron decay threshold, with width Γ_R = 2.6 MeV (FWHM).
- The effect of dynamical polarizabilities decreases the magnitude of the two-neutron Casimir-Polder potential up to distances as far as 200 fm.

## Context
This work extends the static-limit Casimir-Polder potentials for neutron-neutron and neutron-proton systems derived by Feinberg and Sucher (1970) and Bernabeu and Tarrach (1976) by incorporating frequency-dependent dynamical polarizabilities from chiral effective field theory. The authors apply their results to the ongoing debate about the tetraneutron, following the experimental claim of a resonant tetraneutron state by Kisamori et al. (2016) and the subsequent theoretical challenges from Hiyama (2016), Carbonell (2017), and others who argued that such a resonance would require strong three-nucleon forces with undesirable consequences. The authors suggest that the tiny Casimir-Polder interaction may play a role in the tetraneutron decay width, potentially lowering its value, and state that further work on this point is required.
