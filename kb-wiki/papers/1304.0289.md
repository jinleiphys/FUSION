# Baryonic Matter in the Hidden Local Symmetry Induced from Holographic QCD Models

> Baryonic matter is studied in the Skyrme model by taking into account the roles of $π,$ $ρ$, and $ω$ mesons through the hidden local symmetry up to $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ terms including the homogeneous Wess-Zumino (hWZ) terms. Using the master formulas for the low energy constants derived from holographic QCD models the skyrmion matter properties can be quantitatively calculated with the input values of the pion decay constant $f_π$ and the vector meson mass $m_ρ^{}$. We find that the hWZ terms are responsible for the repulsive interactions of the $ω$ meson. In addition, the self-consistently included $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ terms with the hWZ terms is found to increase the half skyrmion phase transition point above the normal nucleon density.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the homogeneous Wess-Zumino (hWZ) terms and the self-consistently determined $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ terms from holographic QCD increases the half-skyrmion phase transition density above normal nuclear matter density, in contrast to minimal models where the transition occurs at or below that density.

## Method

The authors study baryonic matter by placing skyrmions (topological solitons) into a face-centered-cubic (FCC) crystal, using a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian that includes $\pi$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ mesons up to $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ terms plus the hWZ terms. The low-energy constants (LECs) of the HLS Lagrangian are fixed via master formulas derived from the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, using only the empirical pion decay constant $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV and the rho meson mass $m_\rho = 775.5$ MeV as inputs. The energy per baryon and the average chiral field $\langle \sigma \rangle$ are computed as functions of the FCC lattice size $L$ to locate the half-skyrmion phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The half-skyrmion phase transition density $n_{1/2}$ in the full HLS$(\pi,\rho,\omega)$ model is larger by a factor of **1.7** compared to the minimal HLS$_{\rm min}(\pi,\rho,\omega)$ model.
- The single-skyrmion mass $M_{\rm sol}$ in the full HLS$(\pi,\rho,\omega)$ model is **1184 MeV**; in HLS$(\pi,\rho)$ it is **834 MeV**; in HLS$(\pi)$ it is **922 MeV**; in HLS$_{\rm min}(\pi,\rho,\omega)$ it is **1407 MeV**.
- The $\Delta$-$N$ mass splitting $\Delta_M$ in the full HLS$(\pi,\rho,\omega)$ model is **448 MeV**; in HLS$(\pi,\rho)$ it is **1707 MeV**; in HLS$(\pi)$ it is **1014 MeV**; in HLS$_{\rm min}(\pi,\rho,\omega)$ it is **259 MeV**.
- The winding number rms radius $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_W}$ in the full HLS$(\pi,\rho,\omega)$ model is **0.433 fm**; the energy rms radius $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_E}$ is **0.608 fm**.
- Input values used: $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV and $m_\rho = 775.5$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to baryonic matter by including vector mesons ($\rho$ and $\omega$) via HLS up to $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ terms, building on the authors' previous studies of single-skyrmion properties (Ma et al., 2012). It improves upon the minimal $\omega$-meson model of Meissner (1986) and the earlier skyrmion crystal work of Lee (2003) and Park (2003), where the half-skyrmion transition density could not be pinned down explicitly. The framework enables quantitative predictions for dense baryonic matter using only two empirical inputs, and can be extended to study heavy baryons (Harada, 2012) and the role of the dilaton field in hadron properties at finite density.
