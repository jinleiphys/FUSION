# Equation of State of Dense Matter: Pauli Degeneracy, Pairing Correlations, and Implications for Neutron Stars

> We develop a unified description of dense fermionic matter that consistently incorporates Pauli degeneracy, interaction effects, and pairing correlations. The condition that the temperature is much smaller than the Fermi energy leads to a natural separation between Sommerfeld, Fermi-liquid, and pairing regimes, and how these contributions enter the equation of state. The resulting EOS is applied to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to analyze neutron-star structure. We demonstrate that Pauli degeneracy provides the dominant pressure, interactions determine the stiffness of the EOS, and pairing correlations produce subleading but potentially significant corrections, especially in quark matter. Implications for mass--radius constraints and modern observations are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a unified description of dense fermionic matter that consistently incorporates Pauli degeneracy, interaction effects, and pairing correlations, but the key hedge is that the separation into Sommerfeld, Fermi-liquid, and pairing regimes relies on the condition that the temperature is much smaller than the Fermi energy (T << epsilon_F), which is valid for cold neutron stars but not for all astrophysical contexts.

## Method

The authors extend the van der Waals (vdW) equation of state for hadronic matter by adding a quantum-statistical Pauli correction to the second virial coefficient and a BCS-type pairing correction to the thermodynamic potential. They apply the resulting equation of state to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to analyze neutron-star structure, using scaling arguments to estimate the impact of each contribution on the mass-radius relation.

## Key numbers

- Pauli contribution to the proton-proton virial coefficient at T=10 MeV: B2^Pauli = 5.88 fm^3, with effective B_pp^eff = 7.97 fm^3 (for R_p^0=0.5 fm, g=4)
- Relative pairing correction to degenerate quark pressure: delta P_pair/P_F = 4(Delta/mu_q)^2, ranging from 0.7% to 25% for mu_q=400-600 MeV and Delta=25-100 MeV
- For quark matter with mu_q=400 MeV and Delta=50 MeV: baryon density n_B=0.844 fm^-3, Pauli pressure P_F=253 MeV fm^-3, delta P_pair/P_F=0.063
- For quark matter with mu_q=600 MeV and Delta=100 MeV: baryon density n_B=2.85 fm^-3, Pauli pressure P_F=1281 MeV fm^-3, delta P_pair/P_F=0.111
- Fermi energy for nucleons in neutron stars: epsilon_F ~ 10-100 MeV
- Stellar temperature in neutron stars: T ~ 10^-2 to 10^-1 MeV
- Critical temperature related to pairing gap: T_c ~ 0.57 Delta(0)
- Neutron-star radii from NICER observations: R ~ 11-13 km for stars with masses around 1.4 M_sun

## Context

This work extends the van der Waals model for hadronic matter developed by Krivenko (2023) and Vovchenko (2017) by incorporating quantum-statistical and pairing corrections that were previously neglected. The paper connects to the Landau Fermi-liquid theory for interacting normal-phase matter and to BCS theory (BCS, 1957) for pairing correlations, while applying the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff framework (Tolman, 1939; Oppenheimer, 1939; Lattimer, 2001) to neutron-star structure. The results enable more precise constraints on the equation of state of dense matter from neutron-star mass-radius measurements, particularly for distinguishing between nucleonic and quark-matter phases in stellar cores.
