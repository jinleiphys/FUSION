# Nuclear thermodynamics with isospin degree of freedom: from first results to future possibilities with A and Z identification arrays

> Nuclear thermodynamics studies evidenced the existence of a first order phase transition, namely of the liquid-gas type, without paying attention to the isospin degree of freedom. On the other hand, only few results with the introduction of the isospin variable have been so far obtained. Moreover above all a key question remains. It concerns the origin of the dynamics of the phase transition: spinodal instabilities or not with possible consequences and new signatures related to the introduction of the isospin variable.

## Key claim

Nuclear thermodynamics studies have established the existence of a first-order liquid-gas phase transition in hot nuclei, but the key unresolved question concerns the dynamics of the transition: whether phase separation is produced by spinodal instabilities or occurs at equilibrium, with the introduction of the isospin degree of freedom potentially providing new signatures to distinguish these mechanisms.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental signatures of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition from heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies (32-100 AMeV), including negative heat capacity, bimodal distributions of the largest fragment charge, and caloric curves with backbending. It then examines first results incorporating isospin by comparing reactions with different neutron-proton asymmetries, such as 35 AMeV ⁶⁴Zn+⁶⁴Zn, ⁷⁰Zn+⁷⁰Zn, and ⁶⁴Ni+⁶⁴Ni, using the Landau free-energy approach applied to fragment yields from reconstructed quasi-projectiles. Finally, it compares equilibrium phase separation (Gibbs construction) with spinodal decomposition using mean-field calculations and semi-classical BLOB simulations of ³²AMeV ¹³⁶Xe+¹²⁴Sn collisions.

## Key numbers

- Latent heat for nuclei close to gold estimated around 8 MeV from bimodality in 60-100 AMeV Au+Au semi-peripheral collisions
- Pure gas phase appears above 8.5-10.4 MeV for the same system
- Temperature linearly increases with energy between 2 and 8 AMeV for light quasi-projectiles from 35 AMeV ⁷⁰Zn+⁷⁰Zn, ⁶⁴Zn+⁶⁴Zn, ⁶⁴Ni+⁶⁴Ni, reaching 12 MeV at E* = 8 AMeV
- Most unstable spinodal modes correspond to wavelengths around 10 fm with characteristic times of 30-50 fm/c, producing primitive fragments of size approximately 5 fm corresponding to Z approximately 8
- Extra production of equal-sized fragments has a confidence level of only 3-4 sigma from previous experiments
- BLOB simulations show primitive fragments formed at 150 fm/c (approximately three times the characteristic time) in central 32 AMeV ¹³⁶Xe+¹²⁴Sn collisions

## Context

This review extends earlier work on nuclear thermodynamics that established direct signatures of the phase transition without isospin, such as negative heat capacity observed by the INDRA collaboration for 32-50 AMeV Xe+Sn central collisions (Borderie, 2002) and bimodality observed in Au+Au collisions (Bonnet, 2009). It builds on theoretical predictions by Barranco (1998), Colonna (2002), and Ducoin (2007) regarding isospin effects on spinodal instabilities. The paper identifies the need for future A and Z identification arrays like FAZIA (Bougault, 2014; Sahu, 2016) to enable experimental studies of isospin fractionation as a signature of spinodal decomposition versus equilibrium phase separation.
