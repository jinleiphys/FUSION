# On the Temperature Dependence of the Shear Viscosity and Holography

> We examine the structure of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s in holographic theories of gravity coupled to a scalar field, in the presence of higher derivative corrections. Thanks to a non-trivial scalar field profile, eta/s in this setup generically runs as a function of temperature. In particular, its temperature behavior is dictated by the shape of the scalar potential and of the scalar couplings to the higher derivative terms. We consider a number of dilatonic setups, but focus mostly on phenomenological models that are QCD-like. We determine the geometric conditions needed to identify local and global minima for eta/s as a function of temperature, which translate to restrictions on the signs and ranges of the higher derivative couplings. Finally, such restrictions lead to an holographic argument for the existence of a global minimum for eta/s in these models, at or above the deconfinement transition.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s in holographic theories with a scalar field and higher derivative corrections generically runs with temperature, and the existence of a global minimum for eta/s at or above the deconfinement transition can be argued from geometric conditions on the higher derivative couplings, without requiring a specific string theory embedding.

## Method

The authors consider a five-dimensional Einstein-scalar action with a perturbatively small higher derivative term proportional to the square of the Riemann tensor, coupled to a scalar field via a function G(Phi). Using the near-horizon expansion of dilatonic black brane solutions and the phase variables method, they derive an analytic expression for eta/s in terms of the horizon value of the scalar field, the scalar potential, and the coupling function G(Phi). The temperature dependence of eta/s is then obtained by relating the horizon scalar value to temperature through the thermodynamic properties of the background.

## Key numbers

* For the Chamblin-Reall black brane with exponential potential V(Phi) = (V_0/l^2) exp(alpha Phi), the shear viscosity to entropy ratio takes the form eta/s = (1/4pi)[1 - (2/3)beta V_0 (1 - 3 gamma alpha/4) (T/T_0)^{-6 alpha (gamma+alpha)/(8-3 alpha^2)}], where the exponent depends on the potential exponent alpha and the scalar coupling gamma.
* The condition for thermal stability (positive specific heat) in the Chamblin-Reall model restricts the potential exponent to 0 < alpha < sqrt(8/3).
* For the special case alpha = sqrt(2/3) and gamma = -sqrt(2/3), the CR solution uplifts to a pure AdS black brane in six dimensions, and the temperature dependence of eta/s disappears entirely.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio beyond the universal result eta/s = 1/(4pi) found in Einstein gravity duals (Policastro 2001, Buchel 2003), and builds on earlier analyses of higher derivative corrections in the presence of scalar fields (Cremonini 2011, Buchel 2010). The authors focus on phenomenological QCD-like models (Gursoy and Kiritsis 2008, Gursoy et al. 2009) where the dilaton potential is determined by field-theoretic requirements such as asymptotic freedom and confinement. The results enable systematic study of temperature-dependent eta/s in holographic models, complementing field-theoretic arguments for a lower bound on shear viscosity (Kovtun 2011) and providing a framework for interpreting elliptic flow measurements at RHIC and LHC.
