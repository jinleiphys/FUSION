# The charged-hadron/pion ratio at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> The hadron/pion ratio is calculated in 200 GeV AuAu collisions at midrapidity, applying pQCD and non-universal transverse-momentum broadening. Arguments are presented for such non-universality, and the idea is implemented in a model, which explains the enhancement of the hadron/pion ratio in central AuAu collisions. The model also describes the qualitative difference between the recently-measured dAu nuclear enhancement factors for pions and charged hadrons.

# The charged-hadron/pion ratio at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

## Key claim

The enhancement of the charged-hadron-to-neutral-pion ratio in central AuAu collisions at 200 GeV can be explained by non-universal transverse-momentum broadening in initial-state scattering, where the effective partonic transverse momentum width broadens more for charged hadrons than for pions as a function of the path length through the nuclear medium. The model reproduces the main features of the centrality dependence of the hadron/pion ratio, but the authors note that pQCD underestimates the proton-to-pion ratio in pp collisions by a factor of 3-10, limiting the predictive power of the leading-order calculation.

## Method

The authors apply leading-order pQCD with unintegrated parton distribution functions using a Gaussian transverse momentum distribution, CTEQ5L parton distribution functions, and Kretzer fragmentation functions. They introduce species-dependent transverse-momentum broadening where the width for charged hadrons and pions in nuclear collisions is given by <k_T^2>^h_AB = <k_T^2> + c_h L_AB(b) and <k_T^2>^pi_AB = <k_T^2> + c_pi L_AB(b), with L_AB(b) the effective path length through the nuclear medium calculated using Woods-Saxon density profiles. The parameters c_h and c_pi are extracted from fits to the AuAu hadron/pion ratio data and then applied to predict the dAu nuclear modification factors.

## Key numbers

- The pp baseline transverse-momentum width is <k_T^2> = 1.8 ± 0.3 GeV^2, extracted from fits to pp charged hadron and pion spectra at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- The broadening coefficient for pions is c_pi = 0.13 ± 0.04 GeV^2/fm
- The broadening coefficient for charged hadrons is c_h = 0.45 ± 0.08 GeV^2/fm, approximately 3.5 times larger than for pions
- The hadron/pion ratio in peripheral AuAu collisions is approximately 1.6, close to the pp value
- The calculation covers p_T from 2 GeV to approximately 10 GeV for the h^pm/pi^0 ratio

## Context

This work extends the pQCD approach of Owens (1987) and Zhang, Fai, Papp, Barnafoldi, and Levai (2002) by incorporating non-universal k_T broadening to explain the anomalous enhancement of the hadron/pion ratio observed by PHENIX in AuAu collisions at RHIC. The model provides an alternative to coalescence/recombination models proposed by Greco, Ko, and Levai (2003) and Fries, Muller, Nonaka, and Bass (2003), and builds on the Cronin effect observations in lower-energy pA collisions by Cronin et al. (1975). The results enable predictions for the nuclear modification factor R_dAu that can be tested against the dAu data from PHENIX, and suggest that the different behavior of R_AB for charged hadrons and pions in AuAu collisions warrants further study with less schematic calculations.
