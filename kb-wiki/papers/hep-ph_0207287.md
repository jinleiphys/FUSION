# Decomposition of the QCD String into Dipoles and Unintegrated Gluon Distributions

> We present the perturbative and non-perturbative QCD structure of the dipole-dipole scattering amplitude in momentum space. The perturbative contribution is described by two-gluon exchange and the non-perturbative contribution by the stochastic vacuum model which leads to confinement of the quark and antiquark in the dipole via a string of color fields. This QCD string gives important non-perturbative contributions to high-energy reactions. A new structure different from the perturbative dipole factors is found in the string-string scattering amplitude. The string can be represented as an integral over stringless dipoles with a given dipole number density. This decomposition of the QCD string into dipoles allows us to calculate the unintegrated gluon distribution of hadrons and photons from the dipole-hadron and dipole-photon cross section via kT-factorization.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the non-perturbative QCD string confining a quark-antiquark dipole can be decomposed into an integral over stringless dipoles with a specific dipole number density, and that this decomposition allows the unintegrated gluon distribution of hadrons and photons to be calculated from dipole-hadron and dipole-photon cross sections via kT-factorization, provided one works in the limit of small chi-functions (chi < 1) where the T-matrix simplifies.

## Method

The authors compute the dipole-dipole scattering amplitude in momentum space using the loop-loop correlation model, which combines perturbative two-gluon exchange with non-perturbative contributions from the stochastic vacuum model (SVM). The Wegner-Wilson loops representing the color dipoles are transformed into surface integrals over gluon field strengths using minimal surfaces, and the resulting chi-functions are evaluated analytically in the small-chi limit. The non-perturbative contribution separates into quark-quark interactions (with the same dipole factor structure as the perturbative part) and string-string interactions (with a new structure), and the string is then represented as an integral over stringless dipoles with number density n(xi) = 1/xi^2.

## Key numbers

- Effective gluon mass used as infrared regulator: m_G = 0.77 GeV
- Parameter freezing the running coupling: M^2 = 1.04 GeV^2, giving alpha_s(k_perp^2=0) = 0.4
- SVM correlation length: a = 0.302 fm
- Gluon condensate: G_2 = 0.074 GeV^4
- Relative weight of confining tensor structure: kappa = 0.74
- Reference center-of-mass energy for fixed-energy observables: sqrt(s_0) approximately 20 GeV
- Dipole number density in string decomposition: n(xi) = 1/xi^2, with 0 <= xi <= 1

## Context

This work extends the loop-loop correlation model (LLCM) presented by Shoshi et al. (2002) which combines perturbative and non-perturbative QCD contributions to compute hadron-hadron, photon-hadron, and photon-photon reactions. The non-perturbative part uses the stochastic vacuum model (SVM) developed by Dosch (1987) and collaborators, which gives confinement via flux-tube formation. The decomposition of the QCD string into dipoles enables calculation of the unintegrated gluon distribution, which is the central object in BFKL evolution equations (Kuraev et al.) and CCFM evolution (Ciafaloni et al.), and upon integration over transverse momentum yields the conventional gluon distribution used in DGLAP evolution (Gribov et al.).
