# Microscopic dynamics simulations of heavy-ion fusion reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei

> The heavy-ion fusion reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei are investigated with the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model. With a subtle consideration of the neutron skin thickness of nuclei and the symmetry potential, the stability of nuclei and the fusion excitation functions of heavy-ion fusion reactions $^{16}$O+$^{76}$Ge, $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm, $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr and $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca are systematically studied. The fusion cross sections of these reactions at energies around the Coulomb barrier can be well reproduced by using the ImQMD model. The corresponding slope parameter of the symmetry energy adopted in the calculations is $L \approx 78$ MeV and the surface energy coefficient is $g_{\rm sur}=18\pm 1.5$ MeVfm$^2$. In addition, it is found that the surface-symmetry term significantly influences the fusion cross sections of neutron-rich fusion systems. For sub-barrier fusion, the dynamical fluctuations in the densities of the reaction partners and the enhanced surface diffuseness at neck side result in the lowering of the fusion barrier.

# Microscopic dynamics simulations of heavy-ion fusion reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim

The improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, with careful treatment of neutron skin thickness and symmetry potential, can reproduce fusion excitation functions for neutron-rich systems at energies around the Coulomb barrier, but only when the surface-symmetry term is included; without it, sub-barrier fusion cross sections for neutron-rich systems like 16O+154Sm are significantly suppressed. The slope parameter of the symmetry energy is constrained to L ≈ 78 MeV and the surface energy coefficient to g_sur = 18 ± 1.5 MeV fm², but the model over-predicts sub-barrier fusion for some stable systems and fails to reproduce 58Ni+58Ni data even above the barrier.

## Method

The authors used the ImQMD model, a semi-classical microscopic dynamics approach where each nucleon is represented by a Gaussian wave packet, with the mean field derived from a Skyrme-type energy density functional. They initialized nuclei with neutron skin thickness based on a linear relationship between ΔR_np and isospin asymmetry I = (N−Z)/A, sampled nucleon positions within hard spheres, and evolved the system via Hamilton's equations without a collision term. Fusion cross sections were obtained by simulating 100–200 events per incident energy and impact parameter, counting fusion events when the center-to-center distance fell below the compound nucleus radius, for the reactions 16O+76Ge, 16O+154Sm, 40Ca+96Zr, and 132Sn+40Ca using three parameter sets (SkP*, IQ3a, IQ3b).

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: L ≈ 78 MeV, consistent with the range L = 70 ± 15 MeV from the finite range droplet model and 53 < L < 79 MeV from nuclear mass fits.
- Surface energy coefficient: g_sur = 18 ± 1.5 MeV fm², consistent with most Skyrme parameter sets (g_sur ≈ 20 ± 5 MeV fm² over 103 sets).
- For 132Sn+40Ca at E_c.m. = 115 MeV, the neutron-to-proton density ratio at the neck region reaches ρ_n/ρ_p = 2.7 at t = 400 fm/c, compared to the compound nucleus N/Z = 1.37.
- Average spurious nucleon emission at t = 2000 fm/c: 1.1 for 92Zr and 2.6 for 132Sn with parameter set IQ3a.
- For 16O+46Ti, the average dynamical barrier heights from ImQMD simulations are 25.3, 24.5, and 22.7 MeV at incident energies of 32, 27.5, and 24 MeV, respectively, compared to the most probable barrier height of 25.0 MeV from ETF2 calculations.

## Context

This work extends the ImQMD model, previously applied to stable nuclei and intermediate-energy collisions (ImQMD 2002, 2004, 2010), to neutron-rich fusion systems relevant for super-heavy element synthesis. It contrasts with static models using frozen density approximations (proximity potential, Woods-Saxon) and with TDHF calculations (Umar 2006, 2012) which cannot describe sub-barrier fusion directly due to the tunneling problem. The results enable further constraints on the symmetry energy slope parameter L and surface-symmetry term from fusion data, and suggest that dynamical density fluctuations and enhanced surface diffuseness at the neck region lower the fusion barrier for neutron-rich systems, a mechanism that could inform future studies of super-heavy nucleus formation.
