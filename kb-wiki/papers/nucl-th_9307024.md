# Study of High Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions in a Relativistic BUU-Approach with Momentum-Dependent Mean-Fields

> We introduce momentum-dependent scalar and vector fields into the Lorentz covariant relativistic BUU- (RBUU-) approach employing a polynomial ansatz for the relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The momentum-dependent parametrizations are shown to be valid up to about 1 GeV/u for the empirical proton-nucleus optical potential. We perform numerical simulations for heavy-ion collisions within the RBUU-approach adopting momentum-dependent and momentum-independent mean-fields and calculate the transverse flow in and perpendicular to the reaction plane, the directivity distribution as well as subthreshold K+-production. By means of these observables we discuss the particular role of the momentum-dependent forces and their implications on the nuclear equation of state. We find that only a momentum-dependent parameter-set can explain the experimental data on the transverse flow in the reaction plane from 150 - 1000 MeV/u and the differential K+-production cross sections at 1 GeV/u at the same time.

# Study of High Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions in a Relativistic BUU-Approach with Momentum-Dependent Mean-Fields

## Key claim

Only a momentum-dependent parameter-set can simultaneously explain experimental data on transverse flow in the reaction plane from 150 to 1000 MeV/u and differential K+ production cross sections at 1 GeV/u, but the transverse flow alone does not provide precise information about the nuclear equation of state because it is too insensitive to the incompressibility at high energy.

## Method

The authors introduced momentum-dependent scalar and vector fields into the Lorentz covariant relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) approach using a polynomial ansatz for the relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, with parameters fitted to reproduce the empirical proton-nucleus optical potential up to about 1 GeV/u. They performed numerical simulations for heavy-ion collisions using test-particle methods with 150-1000 test particles per nucleon, adopting the Cugnon parametrization for baryon-baryon cross sections including elastic and inelastic channels with the Delta resonance in the "frozen Delta" approximation, and treated the collision term within the Local Ensemble Method.

## Key numbers

- The momentum-dependent parametrizations are valid up to about 1 GeV/u for the empirical proton-nucleus optical potential
- All parameter-sets yield a saturation density of 0.17 fm^-3 and a binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV at that density
- In Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/u with impact parameter b = 6 fm, the differences between parametrizations are negligible at 150 MeV/u but become larger with increasing beam energy
- At 800 MeV/u in Ar+KCl collisions, the momentum-dependent parametrizations POL6 and POL7 reproduce experimental data while momentum-independent sets NL6 and NL7 overestimate the flow
- The flow perpendicular to the reaction plane shows differences due to incompressibility of about 5-10% of the total strength
- The polynomial approximation reproduces exact results up to bombarding energy of about 1 GeV/u with the biggest difference in longitudinal pressure being less than 10%

## Context

This work extends the RBUU approach of Blattel et al. (1991) and Lang et al. (1992) by introducing explicit momentum-dependent mean-fields that correct the overestimated repulsion at high energies present in the original sigma-omega model. It builds on the Fock-term-like ansatz and parametrizations proposed by the same group in earlier publications (1992-1993) and addresses the problem identified by Gale et al. (1990) and Aichelin et al. (1991) that momentum-dependence plays a dominant role for observables like K+ production. The results enable future determination of the nuclear equation of state by combining transverse flow, directivity distribution, and subthreshold kaon production data, though the authors note that the in-plane transverse flow alone does not provide information on the incompressibility.
