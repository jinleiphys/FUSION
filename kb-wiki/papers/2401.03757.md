# Anomalous diffusion of the heavy quarks through the fractional Langevin equation

> The dynamics of heavy quarks within the hot QCD medium have been revisited, considering the influence of anomalous diffusion. This study has been conducted using the framework of the fractional Langevin equation involving the Caputo fractional derivative. We introduce a numerical scheme for the fractional Langevin equation and demonstrate that the mean square displacement of the particle exhibits anomalous diffusion, deviating from a linear relationship with time. Our analysis calculates various entities, such as mean squared momentum, momentum spread, and the nuclear suppression factor, $R_{AA}$. Notably, our findings indicate that superdiffusion strongly suppresses the $R_{AA}$ compared to normal diffusion in the hot QCD medium. The possible impacts on other parameters are also discussed.

# Anomalous diffusion of the heavy quarks through the fractional Langevin equation

## Key claim

Superdiffusion (fractional parameter α > 1) in the fractional Langevin equation framework strongly suppresses the nuclear suppression factor R_AA of heavy quarks in the hot QCD medium compared to normal diffusion, but the analysis is limited to superdiffusion only and does not consider subdiffusion, which is stated as outside the scope of the current work.

## Method

The authors model heavy quark dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma using a fractional Langevin equation with Caputo fractional derivatives of orders α and β for momentum and position evolution, respectively. They develop an L2 numerical scheme to discretize the Caputo fractional derivative and solve the relativistic fractional Langevin equation for charm quarks, using perturbative QCD transport coefficients with Debye mass screening for elastic processes. The numerical method is validated against analytical solutions for the non-relativistic, purely diffusive case (γ = 0) before applying it to the relativistic case with drag and diffusion.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass M_c = 1.3 GeV and bottom quark mass M_b = 4.5 GeV are given as reference masses
- Diffusion coefficient D = 0.1 GeV²/fm used for calculations
- Temperature T = 250 MeV and T = 350 MeV used for R_AA calculations
- Evolution time τ_f = 6 fm/c used for final momentum distributions and R_AA
- Fractional parameters α and β tested at values 1.001, 1.2, 1.4, and 1.6
- For normal diffusion (α → 1), ⟨p²(t)⟩ = 2Dt in the non-relativistic purely diffusive case
- For α = 1.6, the momentum distribution shows the most pronounced spreading and shift toward lower p_T compared to other α values

## Context

This work extends the standard Langevin equation approach for heavy quark dynamics in QGP, which has been used extensively by Moore and Teaney (2004), Das et al. (2013, 2015), and others, to include anomalous diffusion via fractional derivatives. The study is motivated by the observation that conventional Langevin approaches face challenges in adequately explaining experimental observations of heavy quark energy loss in QGP, as noted by Das et al. (2015). The work connects to recent studies of superdiffusion in QCD matter by Caucal et al. (2021) on transverse momentum broadening. This enables future investigation of whether anomalous diffusion can resolve discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental data for heavy quark observables such as R_AA and elliptic flow v_2.
