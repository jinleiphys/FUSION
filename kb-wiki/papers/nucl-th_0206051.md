# Inflation of fireballs, the gluon wind and the homogeneity of the HBT radii at RHIC

> We solve analytically the ellipsoidally expanding fireball hydrodynamics with source terms in the momentum and the energy equations, using the non-relativistic approximation. We find that energy transport from high pt jets of gluons to the medium leads to a transient, exponential inflation of the fireballs created in high energy heavy ion collisions. In this transient, inflatory period, the slopes of the single particle spectra increase, while the HBT radius parameters decrease exponentially with time. This effect is shown to be similar to the development of the homogeneity of our Universe due to an inflatory period. Independently of the initial conditions, and the exact value of the freeze-out time and temperature, the meausurables (single particle spectra, the correlation functions, the slope parameters, elliptic flow, HBT radii and cross terms) become time independent during the late, non-inflatory stages of the expansion, and they satisfy new kind of scaling laws. If the expansion starts with a transient inflation caused by the gluon wind, it leads naturally to large transverse flows and the simultaneous equality and scaling behaviour of the HBT radius parameters, R_{side} \approx R_{out} \approx R_{long} \approx t_f \sqrt{T_f/m}. With certain relativistic corrections, the scaling limit is τ_f \sqrt{T_f/m_t}, where m_t is the mean transverse mass of the pair.

# Inflation of fireballs, the gluon wind and the homogeneity of the HBT radii at RHIC

## Key claim

Energy transport from high transverse momentum gluon jets to the hydrodynamically behaving medium can cause a transient, exponential inflation of the fireball created in high energy heavy ion collisions, which leads to the simultaneous equality and scaling of the HBT radius parameters (R_side ≈ R_out ≈ R_long) independently of initial conditions and freeze-out time, provided the expansion begins with such an inflatory period driven by the gluon wind.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic hydrodynamic equations (continuity, Euler, and energy equations) analytically for an ellipsoidally expanding fireball, adding source terms in the momentum and energy equations to represent energy and momentum transfer from high-pt gluon jets to the soft medium. They assumed a self-similar Gaussian density profile with ellipsoidal symmetry, a linear velocity field, and an equation of state parameterized by κ = λ_e/λ_p and a bag constant B, with the gluon wind source terms proportional to the local number density and temperature. The time evolution of the principal axes (X, Y, Z) was reduced to a Hamiltonian mechanical problem, and the observables (single-particle spectra slope parameters, HBT radii) were computed from the analytic solution.

## Key numbers

- For the inflating solution with constant energy transfer coefficient j_0 = 0.3 c/fm, the fireball radius inflates as R(t) = R_0 exp(κ/(3+2κ) j_0 t), and the local temperature increases as T(t) = T_i,0 exp(2κ/(3+2κ) j_0 t)
- The effective HBT radius during inflation decreases as R_eff ≈ (1/j_0)(κ/(3+2κ)) sqrt(T_0/m) exp(-κ/(3+2κ) j_0 t)
- For the numerical example with initial conditions (X_0, Y_0, Z_0) = (5, 6, 2) fm, initial temperature T_i,0 = 94 MeV, and m = 940 MeV, the inflation coefficient was j_0 = 0.3 c/fm
- In the asymptotic late stage without source terms, the HBT radii approach R_side ≈ R_out ≈ R_long → t_f sqrt(T_f/m), and with relativistic corrections become τ_f sqrt(T_f/m_t) where m_t is the mean transverse mass of the pair
- For the non-inflating numerical example, initial conditions were (X_0, Y_0, Z_0) = (6, 5, 2) fm with T_i = 470 MeV and m = 940 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analytic solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics from earlier studies of ellipsoidally symmetric expansions without source terms (Csorgo et al., 2002; Csorgo and Lorstad, 1996) to include energy and momentum transfer from high-pt gluon jets, building on the jet quenching framework developed by Gyulassy, Plumer and Wang (1994) and later by Gyulassy, Levai and Vitev (2000). The paper draws an analogy between the transient inflation of fireballs in heavy ion collisions and the inflationary period in early Universe cosmology, and notes qualitative similarity to results from cascade models (Humanic, 2002) and predictions of HBT radius saturation (Dumitru, 2002). The results enable reconstruction of the initial state from final-state observables despite the insensitivity of the final state to the detailed path of the time evolution.
