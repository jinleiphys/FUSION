# Simplified Thermal Evolution of Proto-hybrid Stars

> We study the possibility of a hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of neutron stars, taking into account different schematic evolutionary stages at finite temperature. Furthermore, we analyze the astrophysical properties of hot and cold hybrid stars, considering the constraint on maximum mass given by the pulsars J1614-2230 and J1614-2230. We obtain cold hybrid stars with maximum masses $\geq 2$ M$_{\odot}$. Our study also suggest that during the proto-hybrid star evolution a late phase transition between hadronic matter and quark matter could occur, in contrast with previous studies of proto-neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that during the cooling of a proto-neutron star, a hadron-quark phase transition could occur late in the evolution (between entropies per baryon of s ≈ 2 and s = 0), rather than in the first seconds after core bounce as earlier studies predicted.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid star equations of state by combining a hadronic matter table from Shen (2010) for proto-neutron star simulations with quark matter described by the Field Correlator Method, treating the gluonic condensate G₂ and the static quark-antiquark potential V₁ as free parameters. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for three schematic isentropic evolutionary stages (s = 1 with trapped neutrinos, s = 2 with diffused neutrinos, and s = 0 cold) while keeping baryonic mass constant to track when a quark core appears.

## Key numbers

- Cold hybrid stars reach maximum masses ≥ 2 M⊙, consistent with the pulsars J1614-2230 and J1614-2230 (both ∼2 M⊙).
- The thermal evolution starts with entropy per baryon s = 1 and lepton fraction Y_{L_e} ≈ 0.4; the second stage occurs after ∼15 seconds with s = 2 and Y_ν = 0; the final cold stage has s = 0 after a few minutes.
- The quark core appears for baryonic masses between M_{Bar}^{(q, s=0)} and M_{Bar}^{(f)} during the cooling from s ≈ 2 to s = 0.

## Context

This work extends studies of proto-neutron star evolution by Burrows (1986) and Steiner (2000) to the case of hybrid stars with quark cores. It contradicts earlier predictions by Benvenuto (1989) that deconfinement occurs seconds after core bounce, and by Benvenuto (1999) that the transition happens during the s ≈ 1 stage. The results suggest that massive pulsars like J1614-2230 may contain quark matter in their inner cores, motivating further analysis of the hadron-quark phase transition and neutron star thermal evolution.
