# Lifetime of the eta' meson at low temperature

> This work constitutes one part of an investigation of the low-temperature changes of the properties of the eta' meson. In turn these properties are strongly tied to the U(1)_A anomaly of Quantum Chromodynamics. The final aim is to explore the interplay of the chiral anomaly and in-medium effects. We determine the lifetime of an eta' meson being at rest in a strongly interacting medium as a function of the temperature. To have a formally well-defined low-energy limit we use in a first step Chiral Perturbation Theory for a large number of colors. We determine the pertinent scattering amplitudes in leading and next-to-leading order. In a second step we include resonances that appear in the same mass range as the eta' meson. The resonances are introduced such that the low-energy limit remains unchanged and that they saturate the corresponding low-energy constants. This requirement fixes all coupling constants. We find that the width of the eta' meson is significantly increased from about 200 keV in vacuum to about 10 MeV at a temperature of 120 MeV.

## Key claim

The width of the eta' meson at rest in a strongly interacting medium increases significantly with temperature, from about 200 keV in vacuum to about 10 MeV at a temperature of 120 MeV, based on calculations using Chiral Perturbation Theory for a large number of colors and Resonance Chiral Theory, with the caveat that the low-density approximation and pion-only medium are used.

## Method

The authors calculated the collisional broadening of the eta' meson at rest in a thermal pion gas using the low-density approximation, where the width increase is given by an integral over the inelastic cross sections for eta' pi -> eta pi and eta' pi -> K anti-K processes. They first derived scattering amplitudes from large-N_c Chiral Perturbation Theory at leading and next-to-leading order, then extended the calculation using Resonance Chiral Theory which includes scalar and vector resonances that saturate the low-energy constants while preserving the low-energy limit.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum width of the eta' meson: about 200 keV
- Width at temperature of 120 MeV: about 10 MeV
- Input pion decay constant: F_pi = 92.21 MeV
- Input kaon decay constant: F_K = 110.47 MeV
- Input pion mass: M_pi = 138.04 MeV
- Input kaon mass: M_K = 494.99 MeV
- Input eta mass: M_eta = 547.86 MeV
- Input eta' mass: M_eta' = 957.78 MeV
- Determined low-energy constant F = 90.4 MeV
- Determined quark mass ratio S = m_s/m = 23.3
- Determined mixing angles: theta_8 = -21.9 degrees, theta_0 = -6.8 degrees

## Context

This work extends low-temperature studies of hadronic properties in a thermal medium, building on prior work by Gasser (1986), Gerber (1988), Goity (1989), Leutwyler (1990), and Schenk (1991, 1993) on Chiral Perturbation Theory at finite temperature. It also follows the resonance-saturation approach of Ecker (1988, 1989) and Donoghue (1988) to include resonances. The results suggest that the eta' lifetime becomes comparable to the fireball lifetime in heavy-ion collisions, enabling experimental study of the U(1)_A anomaly in medium, as previously discussed by Kapusta (1995), Kwon (2012), and Sakai (2013).
