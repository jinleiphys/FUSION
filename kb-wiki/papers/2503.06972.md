# Novel concept for low-energy antineutron production and its application for antineutron scattering experiments

> Extensive data of antiproton scattering cross sections with protons and nuclei have advanced our understanding of hadronic interactions with antinucleons. However, low-energy antineutron scattering data are scarce, thereby limiting our understanding of the S-wave antinucleon-nucleon and antinucleon-nucleus interactions. We present a novel production scheme of extremely low-energy antineutrons that could remedy this situation. This method is based on backward charge-exchange reaction ($p\bar{p}\to n\bar{n}$), and can reach the lowest momentum of 9 MeV/c, which would be well suited to study of the S-wave antinucleon-nucleon or antinucleon-nucleus interactions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that extremely low-energy antineutrons (down to 9 MeV/c momentum) can be produced via the backward charge-exchange reaction ($p\bar{p}\to n\bar{n}$) using 300 MeV/c antiprotons from the CERN Antiproton Decelerator, enabling S-wave dominated antineutron-nucleon and antineutron-nucleus scattering studies in a momentum regime below 50 MeV/c that has been previously inaccessible.

## Method

The authors analyze the kinematics of the backward charge-exchange reaction, where antineutrons emitted near 180 degrees in the center-of-mass frame appear forward in the laboratory frame with very low momenta due to the Lorentz antiboost. They evaluate the production rate using measured differential cross sections from Bruckner et al. (1986) at LEAR, assuming a 0.44 g/cm^2 thick liquid hydrogen target that degrades the antiproton beam from 300 MeV/c to 250 MeV/c, with an angular acceptance of theta_lab less than 5 degrees.

## Key numbers

- Minimum antineutron momentum achievable: 9 MeV/c, using 300 MeV/c incident antiprotons
- Backward differential cross section at theta_CM = 175 degrees for 300 MeV/c incident antiprotons: 1.25 +/- 0.50 mb/sr (from Bruckner et al. 1986)
- Estimated laboratory-frame differential cross section at theta_lab = 0 degrees: 4.7 +/- 1.9 microbarn/sr
- Expected antineutron yield: approximately 1 antineutron per AD spill containing 5 x 10^7 antiprotons
- Extracted antineutron momentum range: 8.5 to 10.4 MeV/c
- Lowest antineutron momentum previously used in experiments: 54 MeV/c for antineutron-proton scattering (OBELIX experiment) and 76 MeV/c for antineutron-nucleus scattering (OBELIX experiment)
- Threshold momentum for charge-exchange reaction: 98.7 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the capabilities of antineutron scattering experiments beyond what was achieved at CERN LEAR (1983-1996) and the OBELIX experiment, which measured antineutron-proton and antineutron-nucleus scattering only down to 54-76 MeV/c. The proposed method would enable direct experimental determination of antineutron-nucleus scattering lengths, which have previously only been inferred from antiprotonic atom X-ray data (Batty 1995, Friedman 2005) and optical potential models. This capability is particularly relevant for resolving discrepancies between optical potential predictions and measured annihilation cross sections (Friedman 2014), and for providing input to next-generation neutron-antineutron oscillation experiments (Nesvizhevsky 2019, Gudkov 2020, Protasov 2020).
