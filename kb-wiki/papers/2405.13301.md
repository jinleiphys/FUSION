# Universal separable structure of the optical potential

> Based on a momentum-space in-medium folding model, we disclose the universal separability of the optical potential, revealing its radial and nonlocality features at beam energies in the range 40 - 400 MeV and target mass numbers in the range $40\le A\le 208$. From this microscopic study we find that the nonlocality form factor is inherently complex and of hydrogenic nature, affecting both central and spin-orbit components of the potential. A striking outcome from this study is the consistent appearance of a nodal point in the imaginary radial form factor, notably suppressing surface absorption peaks, in evident contrast with Woods-Saxon's assumption of an absorptive peak at the nuclear surface. Our analysis reveals that the complex radial form factor can effectively be represented as convolutions of uniform spherical distribution with a Gaussian form factor and a Yukawa term. These robust microscopically-driven findings offer new ways for investigating nuclear reactions beyond the restricting Woods-Saxon and Perey-Buck assumptions.

## Key claim
The optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering exhibits a universal separable structure (JvH factorization) across beam energies 40–400 MeV and target mass numbers 40 ≤ A ≤ 208, but this separability holds only when the zeroth-order Legendre expansion term is sufficient and the radial form factor’s weak dependence on mean momentum K is neglected by fixing it to the on-shell value.

## Method
The authors performed momentum-space folding model calculations using the Argonne v18 (AV18) nucleon-nucleon potential, with ground-state densities from Hartree-Fock calculations. They solved the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock integral equation for fully off-shell g matrices, then constructed the nonlocal optical potential for proton elastic scattering off ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb at 40, 80, 200, 300, and 400 MeV. The potential was expressed in terms of momentum transfer q and mean momentum K, and the zeroth-order Legendre term was isolated to define the JvH factorization: volume integral J, radial form factor v(q), and nonlocality form factor H(K).

## Key numbers
- Beam energy range: 40–400 MeV; target mass numbers: 40 ≤ A ≤ 208
- Volume integral per nucleon Re[J_c/A] decreases monotonically by about 25% over 40–400 MeV
- Imaginary volume integral per nucleon Im[J_c/A] increases in magnitude by approximately 17% over the same energy range
- First node of real central radial form factor occurs at q ≈ 1 fm⁻¹; first node of real spin-orbit radial form factor at q ≈ 1.1 fm⁻¹
- Nodal point in imaginary radial form factor v_c(r) occurs at r ∼ 4 fm for ⁴⁰Ca
- Perey-Buck nonlocality parameter β = 0.85 fm used for comparison
- The nonlocality form factor H(s) yields a hydrogenic exponential e^{-s/b} with range b, in contrast to Gaussian form

## Context
This work extends the authors’ previous discovery of bell-shape nonlocality in the optical potential (Arellano 2022) by demonstrating full separability into radial and nonlocal components. It contradicts the Woods-Saxon (Woods 1954) assumption of an absorptive peak at the nuclear surface, finding instead a nodal point in the imaginary radial form factor that suppresses surface absorption. The results also contrast with the Gaussian nonlocality of Perey-Buck (Perey 1962), revealing a hydrogenic (exponential) nonlocality form factor. This enables new global parametrizations of the optical potential beyond phenomenological prescriptions, relevant for nuclear reaction studies in the rare-isotope era.
