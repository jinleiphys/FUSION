# Inclusive omega photoproduction off nuclei

> We investigate inclusive omega photoproduction off complex nuclei, concentrating on the feasibility to examine a possible in-medium change of the omega meson properties by observing the pi^0 gamma invariant mass spectrum. The simulations are performed by means of a BUU transport model including a full coupled-channel treatment of the final state interactions. In-medium changes of the omega spectral density are found to yield a moderate modification of the observables as compared to the situation in free space. Also the effects of a momentum dependence of the strong omega potential are discussed.

# Inclusive omega photoproduction off nuclei

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether in-medium modifications of the omega meson properties can be observed via the pi0 gamma invariant mass spectrum in inclusive omega photoproduction off nuclei, finding that such modifications yield only a *moderate* modification of the observables compared to free space, and that a momentum-dependent strong omega potential produces results very similar to the scenario with no mass shift at all.

## Method

The authors performed simulations using a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with a full coupled-channel treatment of final state interactions, including inclusive elementary photoproduction processes described via vector meson dominance and the FRITIOF event generator. The primary photon-nucleon interaction was modeled for exclusive gamma N -> omega N and gamma N -> omega Delta channels, with in-medium modifications introduced through a scalar off-shell potential proportional to density. The omega meson spectral density was modified according to the low-density theorem for collisional broadening and a density-dependent mass shift, and a momentum-dependent omega mass was estimated using a subtracted dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum omega meson pole mass: 782 MeV
- Predicted in-medium omega mass at normal nuclear matter density (0.17 fm^-3) ranges from 640 MeV to 765 MeV across various theoretical predictions
- Collisional broadening of the omega meson at normal nuclear density and zero momentum: 40 MeV (using the omega-nucleon total cross section parameterization of Lykasov (1998))
- Incident photon beam energies studied: 1.2 GeV and 2.5 GeV
- The density-dependent mass shift parameterization used: m_omega* = m_omega^0 (1 - 0.16 * rho_N / rho^0)
- Momentum-dependent mass parameterization constants: beta = 0.16, gamma = 1.0, delta = 0.11
- Applied kinematic cuts: pi0 kinetic energy > 150 MeV, photon energy > 200 MeV, out-of-plane angle cut sin(phi) <= 0.5
- Experimental mass resolution used for folding: 10 MeV and 25 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson photoproduction off nuclei, particularly the authors' previous study of phi meson photoproduction (Muehlich, 2002) where in-medium modifications were found invisible in the K+K- mass spectrum due to strong final state interactions. It also builds on the work of Cassing (2001) on omega photoproduction, which only considered exclusive production and used a momentum-independent in-medium width. The paper addresses the theoretical predictions of Brown and Rho (1991) for dropping vector meson masses and the QCD sum rule analysis of Leupold (1998) suggesting broadening rather than mass shift. The results enable experimental design for the TAPS/Crystal Barrel experiment at ELSA, showing that measurements at different beam energies and with varying momentum cuts could help disentangle density-dependent and momentum-dependent in-medium self-energies of the omega meson.
