# Extending MCAS to hypernuclei and radiative-capture reactions

> Using a Multi-Channel Algebraic Scattering (MCAS) approach we have analyzed the spectra of two hyper-nuclear systems, Lambda9Be and Lambda13C. We have studied the splitting of the two odd-parity excited levels (1/2- and 3/2-) at 11 MeV excitation in Lambda13C, originated by the weak Lambda-nucleus spin-orbit force. We have also considered the splittings of the 3/2+ and 5/2+ levels in both Lambda9Be and Lambda13C, finding how they originate from couplings to the collective 2+ states of the core nuclei. In both hyper-nuclei, we suggest that there could be additional low-lying resonant states in the Lambda-nucleus continua. From the MCAS approach one can extract also the full coupled-channel scattering wave-function to be used in the calculation of various transition matrix elements. As a first application, we have considered the EM-transition matrix elements for the capture reaction Alpha + 3He -> 7Be + Gamma .

## Key claim

The Multi-Channel Algebraic Scattering (MCAS) approach can describe the spectra of hypernuclei \(^9_\Lambda\)Be and \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C using a phenomenological \(\Lambda\)-nucleus interaction, but the correct ordering of the \(\frac{3}{2}^+\) and \(\frac{5}{2}^+\) doublet in \(^9_\Lambda\)Be requires the introduction of a small spin-spin term (\(V_{sI} = -0.1\) MeV) that the abstract does not mention as a necessary condition.

## Method

The authors applied the MCAS method, which couples single-particle dynamics of the hyperon with low-lying collective excitations of the nuclear core, to two hypernuclear systems. For \(^9_\Lambda\)Be, they scaled a previously determined \(n\)-\(^8\)Be Hamiltonian by reducing the central potential by 30%, the interaction radius by 15%, and the spin-orbit strength by an order of magnitude, while removing Pauli-blocking terms since the hyperon is distinguishable from nucleons. For \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C, they similarly tuned a coupled-channel potential starting from a \(\Lambda\)-nucleus optical potential depth about \(\frac{2}{3}\) that of the nucleon-nucleus one, with a 15% smaller radius and an order-of-magnitude weaker spin-orbit strength. They also extracted coupled-channel scattering wavefunctions to calculate electromagnetic transition matrix elements for the radiative-capture reaction \(\alpha + ^3\text{He} \rightarrow ^7\text{Be} + \gamma\).

## Key numbers

- Central potential depth for \(\Lambda\)-\(^8\)Be interaction: \(V_0 = -26.4\) MeV, with radius \(R = 2.3\) fm, diffuseness \(a = 0.65\) fm, and deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.7\).
- Spin-orbit strength for \(\Lambda\)-\(^8\)Be: \(V_{\ell s} = 0.35\) MeV; spin-spin strength for Case 2: \(V_{sI} = -0.1\) MeV.
- Ground-state binding energy of \(^9_\Lambda\)Be: \(-6.71\) MeV (experimental, from emulsion data), with MCAS prediction \(-6.73\) MeV (Case 1 and Case 2).
- Splitting between \(\frac{3}{2}^+\) and \(\frac{5}{2}^+\) states in \(^9_\Lambda\)Be: experimental values \(-3.64\) MeV and \(-3.69\) MeV respectively; MCAS Case 1 gives \(-3.70\) MeV and \(-3.65\) MeV; Case 2 gives \(-3.63\) MeV and \(-3.70\) MeV.
- Central potential depth for \(\Lambda\)-\(^{12}\)C interaction: \(V_0 = -28.9\) MeV (negative parity) and \(-30.4\) MeV (positive parity), with radius \(R = 2.6\) fm, diffuseness \(a = 0.6\) fm, and deformation \(\beta_2 = -0.52\).
- Splitting between \(\frac{1}{2}^-\) and \(\frac{3}{2}^-\) states in \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C at \(\sim 11\) MeV excitation: experimental value \(152 \pm 54 \pm 36\) keV; MCAS predicts \(\sim 160\) keV for both the \(\frac{1}{2}^-\)-\(\frac{3}{2}^-\) and \(\frac{5}{2}^+\)-\(\frac{3}{2}^+\) doublets.
- Predicted \(\frac{1}{2}^+\) bound state in \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C at \(-4.12\) MeV (MCAS), corresponding to a \(\Lambda\) coupled to the Hoyle state (\(0_2^+\) at 7.65 MeV in \(^{12}\)C); shell model predicts \(-10.95\) MeV for the \(\frac{1}{2}^+\) ground state.
- Radiative-capture cross section for \(\alpha + ^3\text{He} \rightarrow ^7\text{Be} + \gamma\): MCAS calculation reproduces the energy dependence but overestimates normalization by a factor of approximately 1.4.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS method, previously applied to nucleon-nucleus scattering and spectra of normal light-mass nuclei (Amos 2003, Canton 2006), to hypernuclear systems and radiative-capture reactions. It builds on experimental studies of hypernuclear spectra from KEK (Hashimoto and Tamura 2006) and shell-model calculations (Millener 2005, 2009) that include \(\Lambda N\), \(\Sigma N\), and \(\Lambda\Sigma\) couplings. The results enable future experimental searches for predicted low-lying resonant states in the \(\Lambda\)-nucleus continua and provide a framework for calculating electromagnetic transition matrix elements in a gauge-independent Hamiltonian approach, with the \(\alpha + ^3\text{He}\) capture reaction as a first test case.
