# Deuteron production and elliptic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The hadronic transport model \textsc{art} is extended to include the production and annihilation of deuterons via the reactions $BB \leftrightarrow dM$, where $B$ and $M$ stand for baryons and mesons, respectively, as well as their elastic scattering with mesons and baryons in the hadronic matter. This new hadronic transport model is then used to study the transverse momentum spectrum and elliptic flow of deuterons in relativistic heavy ion collisions, with the initial hadron distributions after hadronization of produced quark-gluon plasma taken from a blast wave model. The results are compared with those measured by the PHENIX and STAR Collaborations for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}^{}} = 200$ GeV, and also with those obtained from the coalescence model based on freeze-out nucleons in the transport model.

# Deuteron production and elliptic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The hadronic transport model ART, extended to include deuteron production and annihilation via baryon-baryon to deuteron-meson reactions, can describe the measured deuteron transverse momentum spectrum and elliptic flow reasonably well, but it fails to reproduce the negative elliptic flow of deuterons at low transverse momentum (below 1 GeV/c) reported by the STAR Collaboration, instead giving values close to zero.

## Method

The authors extended the ART hadronic transport model (embedded in the AMPT framework) to include deuteron production and annihilation via reactions BB ↔ dM (where B are baryons and M are mesons) and elastic scattering of deuterons with mesons and baryons, using cross sections parameterized from experimental data for pp → dπ⁺ and its inverse, as well as nucleon-deuteron and pion-deuteron elastic scattering data. Initial hadron distributions after hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma were generated from a blast wave model with parameters fitted to reproduce measured pion and proton transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows, and the results were compared to PHENIX and STAR data for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and to a coalescence model using freeze-out nucleons from the same transport calculation.

## Key numbers

- Initial blast wave parameters: critical temperature T_C = 175 MeV, radial flow β_0 = 0.55, spatial elliptic anisotropy s_2 = −0.05, formation time τ_0 = 2.5 fm/c, transverse size R_0 = 5.0 fm
- Initial number of positively charged pions: N_π⁺ = 100, leading to proton number N_p = 2.5 in the thermal model
- Final midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) deuteron number from transport model: approximately 0.046, somewhat larger than the measured value
- Final midrapidity deuteron number from coalescence model: approximately 0.051
- Final midrapidity pion number after hadronic rescattering: N(π⁺) ≈ 74; final proton number: N(p) ≈ 5.4
- Deuteron binding energy: approximately 2.2 MeV
- Charge root-mean-square radius of deuteron: √⟨r²⟩_d = 1.96 fm, corresponding to oscillator frequency ω = 8.06 × 10⁻³ GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier perturbative approach of Oh and Ko (2007), which used time-dependent perturbation theory with reactions NN → dπ, NNN → dN, and NNπ → dπ but required reaction time and volume as parameters and produced too soft deuteron spectra. The present transport model includes position-momentum correlations through collective flow and final-state rescattering, unlike the earlier coalescence model of Oh and Ko (2007) which used an effective temperature without such correlations. The results are compared to experimental data from the PHENIX Collaboration (2007) and STAR Collaboration (2007), and the failure to reproduce the negative deuteron elliptic flow at low p_T raises questions about the production mechanisms for low-p_T hadrons that require further theoretical and experimental investigation.
