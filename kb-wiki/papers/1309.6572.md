# Impact parameter dependence of the scaling of anisotropic flows in intermediate energy HIC

> The scaling behaviors of anisotropic flows of light charged particles are studied for 25 \,MeV/nucleon $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca collisions at different impact parameters by the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. The number of nucleons scaling of elliptic flow is existed and the scaling of the ratios of $v_{4}/v_{2}^{2}$ and $v_{3}/(v_{1}v_{2})$ are applicable for collisions at almost all impact parameters except for peripheral collisions.

# Impact parameter dependence of the scaling of anisotropic flows in intermediate energy HIC

## Key claim

The number-of-nucleons scaling of elliptic flow and the scaling of the ratios v4/v2^2 and v3/(v1v2) hold for light charged particles in 25 MeV/nucleon 40Ca+40Ca collisions across a broad range of impact parameters, but these scalings break down for peripheral collisions with reduced impact parameter between 0.7 and 0.9, where heavier fragments show deviations at high transverse momentum per nucleon.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate approximately 200,000 40Ca+40Ca collisions at 25 MeV/nucleon with a hard equation of state (incompressibility K = 380 MeV). Light charged particles (protons and fragments with A=2, 3, 4) were identified via a coalescence mechanism requiring spatial distance under 3.5 fm and momentum difference under 300 MeV/c between nucleons. Anisotropic flow coefficients v1, v2, v3, and v4 were extracted from the Fourier expansion of the azimuthal distribution at freeze-out time 200 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 25 MeV/nucleon for 40Ca+40Ca collisions
- Reduced impact parameter bins studied: 0.1<b_red<0.3, 0.3<b_red<0.5, 0.5<b_red<0.7, and 0.7<b_red<0.9
- The ratio v4/v2^2 is approximately constant at 0.5 for all light fragments across all impact parameter bins except peripheral collisions
- The ratio v3/(v1v2) is approximately constant at 0.6 for all light fragments across all impact parameter bins except peripheral collisions
- Coalescence parameters: spatial distance threshold 3.5 fm, momentum difference threshold 300 MeV/c
- Normal nuclear matter density: 0.16 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy strength: C_sym = 32 MeV
- Hard EOS parameters: alpha = 124 MeV, beta = 70.5 MeV, gamma = 2, incompressibility K = 380 MeV

## Context

This work extends the number-of-nucleons scaling of elliptic flow previously found by Yan (2013) for intermediate-energy collisions, analogous to the number-of-constituent-quark scaling observed at RHIC energies by Adams (2005) and others. The study tests whether the scaling relations v4/v2^2 and v3/(v1v2) predicted by Kolb (2004) and others from quark coalescence models at RHIC also apply at intermediate energies. The results enable future comparisons between thermalization signatures at RHIC energies and intermediate energies, and suggest that peripheral collisions may probe different thermalization extents for different fragment sizes.
