# Double Pion Photoproduction in Nuclei

> The inclusive A(gamma,pi+ pi-)X reaction is studied theoretically. A sizeable enhancement of the cross section is found, in comparison with the scaling of the deuteron cross section (sigma_deuteron * A/2). This enhancement is due to the modifications in the nuclear medium of the gamma N ----> pi pi N amplitude and the pion dispersion relation. The enhancement is found to be bigger than the one already observed in the (pi,pi pi) reaction in nuclei.

## Key claim

The inclusive A(γ,π⁺π⁻)X reaction shows a sizeable enhancement of the cross section compared to simple scaling of the deuteron cross section (σ_deuteron × A/2), and this enhancement is larger than that already observed in the (π,ππ) reaction in nuclei. The enhancement arises from modifications of the γN → ππN amplitude in the nuclear medium and from changes in the pion dispersion relation, with the effect being most pronounced because the photon reaction is less peripheral than the pion-induced one.

## Method

The authors calculate the inclusive cross section for the A(γ,π⁺π⁻)X reaction using a model for the elementary γN → ππN amplitude that includes N, N*(1440), N*(1520), and Δ(1232) intermediate baryon states, restricted to photon energies below 600 MeV. They apply the local density approximation to finite nuclei (¹²C and ⁴⁰Ca), incorporating Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, pion absorption via two- and three-body mechanisms, and medium modifications to the Δ propagator and the pion self-energy (both p-wave and s-wave parts). The pion propagator in the medium is renormalized using the Lindhard function for particle-hole and Δ-hole excitation, with the spin-isospin correlation parameter g' = 0.6.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C at low energies, the impulse approximation (curve 1) is larger than the deuteron scaling (curve 2) due to Fermi motion shifting the threshold.
- For ¹²C at high energies, the impulse approximation cross section is almost three times smaller than the deuteron scaling, due to pion absorption.
- For ⁴⁰Ca at high energies, the impulse approximation cross section is four times smaller than the deuteron scaling.
- Changing the spin-isospin parameter g' from 0.5 to 0.7 changes the results by about 10%, with larger cross sections for smaller g'.
- The enhancement from medium effects affects mostly pions with energies around 220 MeV, with much less effect on low and high energy pions.
- Below 500 MeV photon energy, the two-pion cross section is very small compared to the total photonuclear cross section; around 600 MeV, the (γ,π⁺π⁻) cross section is approximately one half of the total.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of medium effects in pion production reactions, following earlier work on the (π,ππ) reaction in nuclei by Vicente-Vacas and Oset (1985) and others. The authors use the model for the elementary γN → ππN reaction developed by Gomez Tejedor and Oset (1994), and incorporate the Δ self-energy from Lora and Lui (1993) and the pion self-energy parameters from Meirav et al. (1987) and Garcia-Recio et al. (1991). The predicted enhancement is larger than that seen in the (π,ππ) reaction because the photon penetrates deeper into the nucleus, probing higher baryon densities. The results call for experimental measurements to test these predictions, as no data for the (γ,π⁺π⁻) reaction in nuclei existed at the time of publication.
