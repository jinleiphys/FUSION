# Heavy Quark State Production via p-p and O-O Collisions

> Here we have considered $J/Ψ$ is a normal charmonium meson, while $Ψ(2S)$ is a mixed hybrid charmonium meson. Similarly $Υ(1S)$ and$Υ(2S)$ are normal upsilon mesons, while $Υ(3S)$ is a mixed hybrid upsilon meson. We discuss the differential rapidity cross sections for $J/Ψ$, $Ψ(2S)$, $Υ(1S)$, $Υ(2S)$, $Υ(3S)$ production via p-p, and O-O collisions at proton-proton energy $\equiv \sqrt{s_{pp}}$= 5.44 TeV. The rapidity taken for the present study goes from y=-1 to 1.

# Heavy Quark State Production via p-p and O-O Collisions

## Key claim
The paper predicts differential rapidity cross sections for heavy quarkonium states (J/Psi, Psi(2S), Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), Upsilon(3S)) in proton-proton and oxygen-oxygen collisions at sqrt(s_pp) = 5.44 TeV, using a mixed hybrid theory where Psi(2S) and Upsilon(3S) are approximately 50% standard quark-antiquark and 50% hybrid (quark-antiquark-gluon) states. The authors explicitly state they do not introduce Quark-Gluon Plasma effects in their calculations, and note that the absolute magnitudes are uncertain while the shapes and relative magnitudes are their main predictions.

## Method
The authors use QCD sum rules with a mixed hybrid formalism where the Psi(2S) and Upsilon(3S) states are constructed as superpositions of standard quark-antiquark and hybrid (quark-antiquark-gluon) components with mixing parameter alpha = 0.7 +/- 0.1. Differential rapidity cross sections are computed using the color octet model, with the gluon distribution function f_g and a nuclear modification factor R^E_pp = 0.005 for p-p collisions and R^E_OO = 0.25 for O-O collisions. The cross sections for Psi(2S) and Upsilon(3S) are enhanced by a factor of pi^2/4 relative to the standard model predictions.

## Key numbers
- sqrt(s_pp) = 5.44 TeV for both p-p and O-O collisions
- Rapidity range: y = -1 to 1
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass m_b = 5 GeV
- Mixing parameter alpha = 0.7 +/- 0.1 for Psi(2S) and Upsilon(3S) hybrid states
- A_Phi = 1.26 x 10^(-6) nb for J/Psi production; A_Phi = 3.4 x 10^(-8) nb for Upsilon(1S) production
- a = 4m^2/s = 3.6 x 10^(-7) for charmonium; a = 4.0 x 10^(-6) for bottomonium
- R^E_pp = 0.005 for p-p collisions; R^E_OO = 0.25 for O-O collisions (set equal to R^E_XeXe/2)

## Context
This work extends the mixed hybrid theory developed by Kisslinger (2009) and Kisslinger and Das (2016) to predict heavy quark state production in small collision systems (p-p and O-O) at LHC energies. It builds on the color octet model of Braaten and Chun (1996) and previous studies of Psi(2S) and Upsilon(3S) suppression in p-Pb collisions (Kisslinger 2016). The calculations provide a baseline for future LHC measurements with O-O collisions, as discussed by Das (2023), and aim to test the mixed hybrid theory's explanation of anomalies in Psi(2S) production at high energies (CDF collaboration) and Upsilon(3S) decay patterns (Vogel).
