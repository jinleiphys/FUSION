# Strong-field magnetohydrodynamics for neutron stars

> We present a formulation of magnetohydrodynamics which can be used to describe the evolution of strong magnetic fields in neutron star interiors. Our approach is based on viewing magnetohydrodynamics as a theory with a one-form global symmetry and developing an effective field theory for the hydrodynamic modes associated with this symmetry. In the regime where the local velocity and temperature variations can be neglected, we derive the most general constitutive relation consistent with symmetry constraints for the electric field in the presence of a strong magnetic field. This constitutive relation not only reproduces the phenomena of Ohmic decay, ambipolar diffusion, and Hall drift derived in a phenomenological model by Goldreich and Reisenegger, but also reveals new terms in the evolution of the magnetic field which cannot easily be seen from such microscopic models. This formulation gives predictions for novel diffusion behaviors of small perturbations around a constant background magnetic field, and for the two-point correlation functions among various components of the electric and magnetic fields.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new formulation of magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) for neutron stars based on effective field theory and one-form global symmetry, which in the regime where local velocity and temperature variations can be neglected reproduces the known phenomena of Ohmic decay, ambipolar diffusion, and Hall drift from Goldreich and Reisenegger (1992) while also revealing new terms in the magnetic field evolution that arise from a nontrivial magnetic permeability and cannot easily be seen from microscopic models.

## Method
The authors formulate MHD as an effective field theory for a system with a one-form global symmetry, using the closed-time-path (Schwinger-Keldysh) formalism. They derive the most general constitutive relation for the electric field in terms of the magnetic field consistent with symmetry constraints, including charge conjugation breaking and parity breaking relevant for neutron star interiors. The constitutive relation is obtained by expanding the hydrodynamic effective action in terms of Stueckelberg fields associated with the one-form symmetry, then solving for the electric field in terms of the magnetic field and its gradients.

## Key numbers
- Neutron star magnetic fields can reach up to 10^11 Tesla, cited as some of the strongest in the universe.
- The constitutive relation yields three independent transport coefficients: c_eta (magnetic diffusivity), c_a (ambipolar coefficient), and c_H (Hall coefficient), with constraints eta = c_eta + c_a B^2 >= 0 and c_eta >= 0.
- The one-form susceptibilities chi_parallel and chi_perp are defined via Kubo formulas: chi_parallel = lim_{k->0} lim_{omega->0} G^R(B_z, B_z) and chi_perp = lim_{k->0} lim_{omega->0} G^R(B_x, B_x).
- The magnetic viscosities sigma^para, sigma^perp_1, and sigma^perp_2 are defined via Kubo formulas with the opposite order of limits: sigma^para = lim_{omega->0} lim_{k->0} (1/i omega) G^R(E_z, E_z), sigma^perp_1 = lim_{omega->0} lim_{k->0} (1/i omega) G^R(E_x, E_x), sigma^perp_2 = lim_{omega->0} lim_{k->0} (1/i omega) G^R(E_x, E_y).
- For perturbations around a constant background magnetic field B_0 along the z direction, the dispersion relations for magnetic diffusion are given by equation (MHD_FullDispRel), with special cases: for k_perp = 0, omega = -(i/chi_perp)(sigma_1^perp +/- i sigma_2^perp) k_z^2; for k_z = 0, omega = -i(sigma^para/chi_perp) k_perp^2 or omega = -i(sigma_1^perp/chi_para) k_perp^2.
- In the holographic MHD model, the ratio chi_para/chi_perp is computed numerically as a function of equilibrium B_0/T^2, with values ranging from approximately 1 to 3 for the parameter choices shown.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological model of Goldreich and Reisenegger (1992) for magnetic field evolution in neutron stars by providing a systematic symmetry-based effective field theory framework. It builds on the formulation of hydrodynamics as an effective field theory by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015) and the observation by Grozdanov, Hofman, and Poovuttikul (2016) that MHD can be formulated as a theory with a one-form global symmetry. The new terms involving gradients of the magnetic permeability a(B^2) were not present in the Goldreich-Reisenegger formulation and have been further clarified by Landry (2022). The results enable more accurate nonlinear simulations of neutron star magnetic field evolution and can be generalized to include flow velocities, temperature variations, superfluidity, and superconductivity.
