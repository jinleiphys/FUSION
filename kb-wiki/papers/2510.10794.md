# Realizing the Scientific Program with Polarized Ion Beams at EIC

> Polarized ion beams at the Electron Ion Collider are essential to address some of the most important open questions at the twenty-first century frontiers of understanding of the fundamental structure of matter. Here, we summarize the science case and identify polarized $^2$H, $^3$He, $^6$Li and $^7$Li ion beams as critical technology that will enable experiments which address the most important science. Further, we discuss the required ion polarimetry and spin manipulation in EIC. The current EIC accelerator design is presented. We identify a significant R\&D effort involving both national laboratories and universities that is required over about a decade to realize the polarized ion beams and estimate (based on previous experience) that it will require about 20 FTE over 10 years (or a total of about 200 FTE-years) of personnel, including graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, technicians and engineers. Attracting, educating and training a new generation of physicists in experimental spin techniques will be essential for successful realization. AI/ML is seen as having significant potential for both acceleration of R\&D and amplification of discovery in optimal realization of this unique quantum technology on a cutting-edge collider. The R\&D effort is synergistic with research in atomic physics and fusion energy science.

# Realizing the Scientific Program with Polarized Ion Beams at EIC

## Key claim
The paper argues that a sustained R&D effort of approximately 200 FTE-years over a decade is required to realize polarized ion beams (specifically $^2$H, $^3$He, $^6$Li, and $^7$Li) for the Electron-Ion Collider, and that this effort must begin soon to meet the 2034 operations target. The abstract's statement that polarized ion beams are "essential" is conditioned on the recognition that adapting existing polarization techniques into a reliable, high-performance ion source for an accelerator requires a decade-long, dedicated R&D program with a critical mass of skilled personnel.

## Method
The paper summarizes the EIC accelerator design, including the Hadron Storage Ring (modified from the RHIC Yellow ring), the Electron Storage Ring with its FODO lattice and super-bends, and the electron injector chain (HVDC gun, linac, Beam Accumulator Ring, Rapid Cycling Synchrotron). It reviews the requirements for polarized electron beams (generation, transport, polarimetry) and presents the conceptual design for polarized ion beams, including the need for Siberian snakes, spin rotators, and polarimeters. The paper also describes the complementary EicC facility in China and its planned polarized beam capabilities.

## Context
This paper summarizes the EPIOS (EIC Polarized Ion Source) scientific consortium perspective arising from a March 2025 workshop. It extends the EIC design described in the Conceptual Design Report (CDR) and the 2018 National Academy Report (NAP25171), which established the five science pillars for the EIC. The paper builds on experience from RHIC (the only polarized proton collider in the world, which achieved approximately 60% polarization at 275 GeV) and from HERA (which operated from 1992 to 2007). It identifies a critical need for R&D in polarized ion sources, noting that the primary source of polarized photocathode material no longer exists and that active programs at Jefferson Lab, Mainz, BNL, and Old Dominion University are developing replacements. The paper enables future work by defining the specific R&D requirements, personnel needs, and timeline for achieving polarized ion beams at the EIC, and recommends a focused multi-week program at the Institute for Nuclear Theory and an annual summer school for young physicists.
