# Non-adiabatic dynamics in 10Be with the microscopic alpha+alpha+N+N model

> The alpha+6He low-energy reactions and the structural changes of 10Be in the microscopic alpha+alpha+N+N model are studied by the generalized two-center cluster model with the Kohn-Hulthen-Kato variation method. It is found that, in the inelastic scattering to the alpha+6He(2+) channel, characteristic enhancements are expected as the results of the parity-dependent non-adiabatic dynamics. In the positive parity state, the enhancement originates from the excited eigenstate generated by the radial excitation of the relative motion between two alpha-cores. On the other hand, the enhancement in the negative parity state is induced by the Landau-Zener level-crossing. These non-adiabatic processes are discussed in connection to the formation of the inversion doublet in the compound system of 10Be.

## Key claim

In the inelastic scattering to the α+⁶He(2₁⁺) channel, characteristic enhancements are expected as results of parity-dependent non-adiabatic dynamics; the enhancement in the positive parity state originates from a radially-excited pole, while the enhancement in the negative parity state is induced by the Landau-Zener level-crossing.

## Method

The generalized two-center cluster model (GTCM) was applied to the ¹⁰Be nucleus in a microscopic α+α+N+N four-body model, using atomic orbitals for valence neutrons around two α-clusters. The Kohn-Hulthén-Kato variation method was employed to solve the scattering problem, and an adiabatic KHK method was formulated to connect adiabatic energy surfaces to scattering channels. The nucleon-nucleon interaction used the Volkov No.2 central force and the G3RS spin-orbit force, with parameters adjusted to reproduce the α+⁶He ground-state threshold and the ⁶He(2₁⁺) excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- The energy splitting at the avoided crossing point in the Jπ=0⁺ adiabatic energy surfaces is about 1.5 MeV.
- The energy splitting at the avoided crossing point in the Jπ=1⁻ adiabatic energy surfaces is about 0.5 MeV.
- In Jπ=0⁺, a strong inelastic scattering peak appears at Ec.m. ∼ 3 MeV due to the radially-excited 0₃⁺ pole.
- In Jπ=1⁻, a strong inelastic enhancement appears at Ec.m. ∼ 6 MeV due to the Landau-Zener transition.
- The harmonic oscillator radius parameter b for α clusters and valence neutrons is 1.46 fm.
- The matching radius RC for scattering wave functions is 11.7 fm.
- The Majorana exchange parameter is M=0.643, and Bartlett-Heisenberg exchange is B−H=0.125.

## Context

This work extends the molecular orbital studies of Be isotopes by Itagaki et al. (2000) and the α+α+N+N few-body model by Arai (2002), providing a unified description of both structure and reactions in ¹⁰Be. The GTCM approach enables microscopic description of the nuclear Landau-Zener transition, which has been discussed theoretically by Abe (1975) and Imanishi (1979) but lacked clear experimental evidence. The results enable future comparison with low-energy α+⁶He scattering experiments using radioactive beams, such as those by Raabe (2004) and Shimoura (2004), and can be extended to heavier systems like ¹²Be and ¹⁴Be.
