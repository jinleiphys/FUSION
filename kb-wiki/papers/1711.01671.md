# Probing electron-argon scattering for liquid-argon based neutrino-oscillation program

> The electron scattering has been a vital tool to study the properties of the target nucleus for over five decades. Though, the particular interest on $^{40}$Ar nucleus stemmed from the progress in the accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments. The complexity of nuclei comprising the detectors and their weak response turned out to be one of the major hurdles in the quest of achieving unprecedented precision in these experiments. The challenges are further magnified by the use of Liquid Argon Time Projection Chambers (LArTPCs) in the short- (SBN) and long-baseline (DUNE) neutrino program, with almost non-existence electron-argon scattering data and hence with no empirical basis to test and develop nuclear models for $^{40}$Ar. In light of these challenges, an electron-argon experiment, E12-14-012, was proposed at Jefferson Lab. The experiment has recently successfully completed collecting data for $(e,e'p)$ and $(e,e')$ processes, not just on $^{40}$Ar but also on $^{48}$Ti, and $^{12}$C targets. While the analysis is running with full steam, in this contribution, we present a brief overview of the experiment.

## Key claim

The E12-14-012 experiment at Jefferson Lab has successfully completed data collection for electron-argon scattering, but the analysis is still ongoing; the paper presents only a brief overview of the experiment, not final results.

## Method

The experiment used a 2.222 GeV electron beam from CEBAF in Hall A at Jefferson Lab, with scattered electrons detected in the Left High-Resolution Spectrometer and knocked-out protons in the Right High-Resolution Spectrometer. Coincidence (e,e'p) measurements were performed on 40Ar, 48Ti, and 12C targets at five exclusive kinematics, plus one inclusive (e,e') kinematics, by varying the electron scattering angle while holding beam energy, scattered electron energy, and proton momentum fixed to scan different missing momentum regions where shell-model dynamics dominate.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 2.222 GeV
- Five exclusive kinematics were measured: for example, kin 1 had scattered electron energy 1799 MeV at 21.5 degrees and proton momentum 915 MeV/c at -50.0 degrees; kin 2 had scattered electron energy 1716 MeV at 20.0 degrees and proton momentum 1030 MeV/c at -44.0 degrees
- One inclusive kinematics was measured at kin 5: beam energy 2222 MeV, electron scattering angle 15.5 degrees
- Data were collected on three target nuclei: 40Ar, 48Ti, and 12C

## Context

This experiment addresses the near-complete absence of electron-argon scattering data, with only one prior inclusive cross section measurement at Frascati (Anghinolfi 1995). The work extends the spectral function formalism developed by Benhar (2005), which has been successful in describing electron scattering across various kinematics (Ankowski 2015, Rocco 2016, Vagnoni 2017), to the argon nucleus needed for the SBN and DUNE neutrino oscillation programs. The extracted spectral functions and cross sections will provide an empirical testbed for nuclear models used in neutrino-argon scattering simulations (Van Dessel 2017, Pandey 2014, Butkevich 2012) and help reduce systematic uncertainties in liquid-argon time projection chamber experiments.
