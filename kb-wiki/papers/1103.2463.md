# Structure of the doublet bands in doubly odd nuclei: The case of $^{128}Cs$

> The structure of the $ΔJ = 1$ doublet bands in $^{128}Cs$ is investigated within the framework of the Interacting Vector Boson Fermion Model (IVBFM). A new, purely collective interpretation of these bands is given on the basis of the used boson-fermion dynamical symmetry of the model. The energy levels of the doublet bands as well as the absolute $B(E2)$ and $B(M1)$ transition probabilities between the states of both yrast and yrare bands are described quite well. The observed odd-even staggering of both $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ values is reproduced by the introduction of an appropriate interaction term of quadrupole type, which produces such a staggering effect in the transition strengths. The calculations show that the appearance of doublet bands in certain odd-odd nuclei could be a consequence of the realization of a larger dynamical symmetry based on the non-compact supersymmetry group $OSp(2Ω/12, R)$.

## Key claim

The doublet bands in $^{128}Cs$ can be explained by a purely collective interpretation based on a boson-fermion dynamical symmetry of the Interacting Vector Boson Fermion Model (IVBFM), rather than requiring a rigid triaxial core as assumed in chiral models. The calculations show that the appearance of doublet bands in certain odd-odd nuclei could be a consequence of a larger dynamical symmetry based on the non-compact supersymmetry group $OSp(2\Omega/12, R)$, though the partner bands are not degenerate as would be required in the ideal chiral situation.

## Method

The authors coupled two fermions occupying the same $h_{11/2}$ single-particle level ($j=11/2$) to an even-even core nucleus $^{128}Xe$ described by the symplectic Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), using the orthosymplectic extension $OSp(2\Omega/12,R)$. The Hamiltonian included boson terms fitted to the core nucleus $^{128}Xe$, fermion terms, and an additional quadrupole-type interaction between the core and the two-particle system that produces odd-even staggering in transition strengths. The energy spectra of the odd-mass neighbors $^{127}Xe$ and $^{129}Cs$ were first calculated with the same parameter set, then the doublet bands in $^{128}Cs$ were obtained by mapping states onto stretched $SU(3)$ multiplets with band head structures determined by $(\lambda_0=0,\mu_0=11)$ for the yrast band and $(\lambda_0=8,\mu_0=9)$ for the side band.

## Key numbers

- Energy splitting between partner bands in $^{128}Cs$ is about 200 keV
- The core nucleus $^{128}Xe$ has $R_{4/2}=2.33$, which is between the $U(5)$ value of 2.00 and the $O(6)$ value of 2.5
- Model parameters for the core Hamiltonian: $a=0.08909$, $b=-0.00459$, $\alpha_3=0.02471$, $\beta_3=0.03123$, $\alpha_1=-0.01989$
- Additional parameters for odd-odd nucleus: $\gamma=0.00738$, $\zeta=0.00986$, $\eta=0.11338$, $k=2.28115$
- Quadrupole moment of the $2^+_1$ state of the ground state band in $^{128}Xe$ is $Q(2)=-0.15$ eb
- Boson quadrupole operator parameter $e=1.46$ and $\theta=0.0014$ for $B(E2)$ calculations in $^{128}Xe$

## Context

This work extends previous applications of the IVBFM to doublet bands in the $A\sim130$ region (Ganev and Brant, 2010) and provides an alternative to chiral interpretations based on the tilted axis cranking model, core-quasiparticle coupling model (CQPCM), particle-rotor model, and two quasiparticle plus triaxial rotor model. The authors note that the partner bands in $^{128}Cs$ were regarded as the best known example revealing chiral symmetry breaking (based on lifetime measurements by the Cs128 collaboration), but their model attributes the doublet structure to a dynamical symmetry rather than static chirality. The approach enables simultaneous description of the even-even core, odd-mass neighbors, and odd-odd nucleus with a single parameter set, and can be extended to other odd-odd Cs isotopes such as $^{126}Cs$ and $^{122}Cs$ where candidate chiral doublet bands have been proposed.
