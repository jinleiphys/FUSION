# Collective phenomena in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions: anisotropic flow and more

> Many features of multiparticle production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions reflect the collision geometry and other collision characteristics determining the initial conditions. As the initial conditions affect to a different degree all the particles, it leads to truly multiparticle effects often referred to as anisotropic collective flow. Studying anisotropic flow in nuclear collisions provides unique and invaluable information about the system evolution and the physics of multiparticle production in general. Being not able to cover all aspects of anisotropic flow in one lecture, I decided in the first part of the lecture to discuss briefly a few important and established results, and in the second part, to focus, in a little more detail, on one recent development -- a recent progress in our understanding of the role of fluctuations in the initial conditions. I also discuss some future measurements that might reveal further details of the multiparticle production processes.

## Key claim

Anisotropic flow fluctuations and the "ridge" structure in two-particle correlations, which had been considered separate phenomena attributable to either flow fluctuations or nonflow, are actually different descriptions of the same underlying physics: the system's response to fluctuations in the initial density distribution of the collision.

## Method

The paper reviews established results from RHIC (elliptic flow of charged particles, number-of-constituent-quark scaling of elliptic flow, and charge-dependent correlations sensitive to the chiral magnetic effect) and compares them to early LHC data. The second part develops the argument that event-by-event fluctuations in the initial collision geometry generate anisotropic flow of multiple harmonics, and that rotating each event to a given harmonic symmetry plane produces smooth initial conditions with a shape corresponding to that harmonic, which can be studied via azimuthally sensitive femtoscopic analysis of HBT radii.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma is of the order of a few times the lower bound \(1/4\pi\).
- Elliptic flow of baryons is expected to be about 3/2 times larger than that of mesons within the coalescence hadronization picture, observed experimentally within about 10-20% accuracy.
- The magnetic field in noncentral nuclear collisions is estimated to be \(\sim 10^{15}\) T.
- The analysis of 10k Au+Au collision events at impact parameter \(b=8\) fm is used to illustrate the participant nucleon density averaged after rotation to different harmonic event planes.

## Context

This lecture extends the established RHIC-era results on elliptic flow (E877 Collaboration at AGS, STAR Collaboration at RHIC) and the number-of-constituent-quark scaling idea (Voloshin, 2002; Molnar, 2003) to include recent developments in understanding flow fluctuations and their relation to the ridge phenomenon. The resolution of the puzzle regarding the relative importance of flow fluctuations versus nonflow builds on work by Mishra (2008), Sorensen (2010), Teaney (2010), Staig (2011), and Shuryak (2009). The paper enables new measurements such as femtoscopic analysis relative to higher harmonic event planes and improved background estimates for chiral magnetic effect studies using uranium-uranium collisions (Voloshin, 2010) or higher harmonic correlators.
