# Global constraint on the magnitude of anomalous chiral effects in heavy-ion collisions

> When searching for anomalous chiral effects in heavy-ion collisions, one of the most crucial points is the relationship between the signal and the background. In this letter, we present a simulation in a modified blast wave model at LHC energy, which can simultaneously characterize the majority of measurable quantities, in particular, the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) observables. Such a universal description, for the first time, naturally and quantitatively unifies the CME and the CMW studies and brings to light the connection with the local charge conservation (LCC) background. Moreover, a simple phenomenological approach is performed to introduce the signals, aiming at quantifying the maximum allowable strength of the signals within experimental precision. Such a constraint provides a novel perspective to understand the experimental data and sheds new light on the study of anomalous chiral effects as well as charge dependent correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the current experimental measurements of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral magnetic wave (CMW) at LHC energy can be fully explained by a local charge conservation (LCC) background combined with collective flow, and that any true signal must be very small: the CME fraction in the observable is at most 13% and the CMW fraction at most 2%, under the most ideal conditions.

## Method

The authors constructed a modified blast wave (BW) model for Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, tuned to reproduce experimental pT spectra, elliptic flow (v2), multiplicity distributions, and charge balance functions from the ALICE experiment. They introduced LCC by emitting particles in charge-conserving pairs with a tunable fraction fLCC, and then phenomenologically added CME and CMW signals by interchanging charges of single-produced particles to create dipole or quadrupole charge patterns, quantifying signal strength by the number of interchanged pairs.

## Key numbers

- The calculated integrated v2 within pT < 2 GeV/c and |η| < 0.8 at 5-10%, 30-40%, 50-60% centrality intervals are 0.035 ± 1×10⁻⁵, 0.081 ± 1.5×10⁻⁵, 0.078 ± 3×10⁻⁵, matching experimental values within 3% relative deviations.
- In 30-40% centrality, fLCC = 0.54 gives a balance function width of 0.64 ± 0.014, consistent with ALICE measurement within 5% relative deviations.
- The calculated Δγ values agree with ALICE data within 5% relative deviations in central and semi-central collisions, and within 10% in peripheral collisions.
- The normalized slope of A_ch-Δv2 matches ALICE measurement within 12% relative deviation.
- In 30-40% centrality, the mean S_CME value is ~1, giving a net CME-induced Δγ of 2×10⁻⁵, compared to the total Δγ of 1.5×10⁻⁴, yielding a maximum allowable CME signal fraction of ~13%.
- The average allowable S_CMW in 30-40% centrality is ~0.5, giving a maximum CMW fraction in the slope of ~2%.
- Without LCC background, natural charge fluctuation produces Δδ and Δγ on the order of ~10⁻⁷ to 10⁻⁶.
- Swapping charge once to create one dipole pair generates Δδ ~ -10⁻⁵ and Δγ ~ 10⁻⁵.
- Creating one quadrupole gives Δv2 ~ 10⁻³ when A_ch ≈ ±0.1.

## Context

This work unifies for the first time the separate analyses of CME and CMW observables, which had previously been treated independently despite sharing the LCC background mechanism (STAR, ALICE, CMS experiments). It extends earlier blast wave model comparisons by ALICE (Acharya 2020) that underestimated Δγ by ~40% due to incomplete treatment of LCC fraction and multiplicity. The results enable future searches at RHIC energy, where signals are expected to be stronger due to a possibly longer magnetic field lifetime, and motivate studies with identified particles for more subtle correlations.
