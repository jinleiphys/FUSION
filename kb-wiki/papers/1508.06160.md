# Is hadronic flow produced in p--Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider?

> Using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics ({\tt UrQMD}) model, we investigate the azimuthal correlations in p--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=5.02$ TeV. It is shown that the simulated hadronic p--Pb system can not generate the collective flow signatures, but mainly behaves as a non-flow dominant system. However, the characteristic $v_{2}(p_{\rm T})$ mass-ordering of pions, kaons and protons is observed in {\tt UrQMD} simulations, which is the consequence of hadronic interactions and not necessarily associated with strong fluid-like expansions.

## Key claim

Using the UrQMD hadron cascade model, the simulated p–Pb system at 5.02 TeV cannot generate collective flow signatures and remains non-flow dominant, yet the characteristic $v_{2}(p_{\rm T})$ mass-ordering of pions, kaons, and protons still appears as a consequence of hadronic interactions alone, not necessarily from strong fluid-like expansions.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD version 3.4) hadron cascade model to simulate p–Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=5.02$ TeV, evolving only hadronic matter without an initial stage or quark-gluon plasma phase. They computed two-particle cumulants $c_{2}\{2\}$ with various pseudorapidity gaps and four-particle cumulants $c_{2}\{4\}$ as a function of multiplicity class, and extracted $v_{2}(p_{\rm T})$ for pions, kaons, and protons with and without meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions.

## Key numbers

- $c_{2}\{2\}$ decreases from low to high multiplicity events in UrQMD, and its magnitude weakens as the pseudorapidity gap increases; with $|\Delta \eta| > 1.0$, $c_{2}\{2\}$ still shows strong centrality dependence, indicating non-flow dominance.
- $c_{2}\{4\}$ from UrQMD remains positive for all available multiplicity classes, including central collisions, so real values of $v_{2}\{4\}$ cannot be extracted; this contrasts with experimental data where $c_{2}\{4\}$ becomes negative for the most central collisions.
- A clear $v_{2}$ mass-ordering among pions, kaons, and protons below 2 GeV/$c$ is observed in UrQMD, qualitatively agreeing with ALICE measurements.
- When meson-baryon and meson-meson interaction channels are switched off in UrQMD, the $v_{2}$ mass-ordering almost disappears.

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether collective flow exists in small systems at the LHC, following experimental observations of unexpected collective behavior in p–Pb collisions by ALICE (Abelev et al., 2013) that were semi-quantitatively described by hydrodynamic simulations from Bozek (2013) and Werner (2013). The study shows that hadronic interactions alone, as implemented in UrQMD, cannot reproduce the flow-like four-particle correlations seen in high-multiplicity events, implying that initial-stage or QGP contributions are necessary. It also demonstrates that the $v_{2}$ mass-ordering pattern, often taken as evidence for collective expansion, can arise purely from hadronic interactions, cautioning against interpreting it as a unique flow signature.
