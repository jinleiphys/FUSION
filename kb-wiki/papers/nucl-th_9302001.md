# Medium corrections to the tensor interaction in nuclei: Energy difference of T=1 and T=0 J=0- states in 16O

> It has been shown in the past that in a shell model calculation, restricted to 1Hbar*Omega 1p-1h configurations, the energy splitting between the lowest J=0-, T=0 and J=0-, T=1 states in 16O is mainly due to the tensor component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. In this work, we extend the calculation to include 3Hbar*Omega 2p-2h and 3p-3h configurations. We use realistic G matrices derived from various One-Boson-Exchange potentials taking into account the effects of the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, i.e., a modification of the Dirac spinors for the nucleons in the medium. It is found that the splitting is rather sensitive to the long-range components of the isovector tensor force. The splitting is reduced considerably by including 2p-2h configurations, in particular.

## Key claim

The energy splitting between the lowest \(J^{\pi}=0^{-}\), \(T=0\) and \(J^{\pi}=0^{-}\), \(T=1\) states in \({}^{16}\mathrm{O}\) is mainly due to the tensor component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, but the splitting is reduced considerably when \(3\hbar\omega\) \(2p\)-\(2h\) configurations are included in the shell model space, bringing the calculated value close to experiment.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations for the \(0^{-}\) states in \({}^{16}\mathrm{O}\) using a \(1\hbar\omega\) \(1p\)-\(1h\) model space and then extended the space to include \(3\hbar\omega\) \(2p\)-\(2h\) and \(3p\)-\(3h\) configurations. They employed realistic \(G\) matrices derived from the Bonn A and Bonn C one-boson-exchange potentials, with and without Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock medium modifications (using an effective nucleon mass \(m^{*}=729\) MeV), as well as a schematic interaction with separate central, spin-orbit, and tensor components. The oscillator length was set to \(b=1.72\) fm, and the starting energy for the \(G\) matrix was \(\omega = -30\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The experimental excitation energies of the lowest \(J^{\pi}=0^{-}\) states in \({}^{16}\mathrm{O}\) are 10.952 MeV (\(T=0\)) and 12.797 MeV (\(T=1\)), giving an energy splitting of **1.845 MeV**.
- With the schematic interaction at the \(1p\)-\(1h\) level, the splitting is **2.932 MeV** (full interaction, \(x=y=1\)); including \(2p\)-\(2h\) and \(3p\)-\(3h\) configurations reduces it to **2.002 MeV**.
- For Bonn A with \(m^{*}=m\) (free nucleon mass), the splitting changes from **3.08 MeV** (\(1p\)-\(1h\)) to **1.87 MeV** (full \(1p\)-\(1h\)+\(2p\)-\(2h\)+\(3p\)-\(3h\)).
- For Bonn C with \(m^{*}=m\), the splitting changes from **2.72 MeV** (\(1p\)-\(1h\)) to **1.65 MeV** (full calculation).
- With a purely central schematic interaction (\(x=y=0\)), the splitting is only **0.017 MeV** at the \(1p\)-\(1h\) level and **0.109 MeV** in the full calculation.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Blomqvist and Molinari, Millener and Kurath, and Barrett, who showed that the \(T=0\) vs \(T=1\) splitting in \({}^{16}\mathrm{O}\) is dominated by the tensor force. The authors demonstrate that including \(2\hbar\omega\) monopole excitations (via \(2p\)-\(2h\) configurations) acts as a key medium correction, effectively weakening the tensor interaction through isovector monopole phonon exchange, an effect previously noted by Zamick. The results contrast with large-basis shell model calculations by Haxton and Johnson and by Warburton, Brown, and Millener, who suppress such monopole excitations. The work enables future studies of whether additional medium modifications (e.g., the universal scaling model of Brown and Rho) are phenomenologically required beyond the configuration-mixing mechanism presented here.
