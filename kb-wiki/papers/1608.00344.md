# Bounds on the speed of sound in dense matter, and neutron star structure

> The accurate determination of the maximum mass of the neutron stars is one of the most important tasks in astrophysics. It is directly related to the identification of the black holes in the universe, the production of neutron stars from the supernovae explosion, and the equation of state (EoS) of dense matter. However, not only the EoS is directly connected with neutron star masses, but also the speed of sound in dense matter is a crucial quantity which characterizes the stiffness of the EoS. The upper bound of the speed of sound imposes strong constraints on the maximum mass of neutron stars. However, this upper bound remains still an open issue. Recent observations, of binary neutron star systems, offer the possibility of measuring with high accuracy both the mass and the tidal polarizability of the stars. We study possible effects of the upper bound of the speed of sound on the upper bound of the mass and the tidal polarizability. We conclude that these kinds of measurements, combined with recent observations of neutron stars with masses close to $2 M_{\odot}$, will provide robust constraints on the equation of state of hadronic matter at high densities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if the speed of sound in dense matter is bounded by the conformal limit \(v_s \leq c/\sqrt{3}\), then many nuclear equations of state that otherwise predict neutron star masses above \(2 M_\odot\) would be ruled out, unless the critical density at which the bound applies is sufficiently high. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on the choice of fiducial density and the specific nuclear model.

## Method

The authors construct maximum-mass neutron star configurations by combining a known low-density equation of state (crust and nuclear matter up to a critical density \(n_0\)) with a maximally stiff high-density segment where the speed of sound is fixed to one of three upper bounds: \(v_s = c\), \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\), or the bound from relativistic kinetic theory. They use five nuclear models (MDI, NLD, HLPS, H-HJ, and Skyrme) to describe the intermediate-density region, solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, and compute the tidal polarizability \(\lambda\) from the Love number \(k_2\).

## Key numbers

- Neutron star masses of \(M = 1.97 \pm 0.04 \, M_\odot\) (PSR J1614-2230) and \(M = 2.01 \pm 0.04 \, M_\odot\) (PSR J0348+0432) are used as observational benchmarks.
- The critical (fiducial) density is set to \(n_0 = 1.5 n_s\) where \(n_s = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is the saturation density.
- For the HLPS model with the stiff parameterization, the speed of sound exceeds \(c\) at relatively low pressure (below \(P \leq 100\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{-3}\)).
- When the speed of sound is bounded by \(v_s = c/\sqrt{3}\), the maximum neutron star mass drops below \(2 M_\odot\) for all models except the stiff HLPS case, regardless of the fiducial density.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bedaque and Steiner (2015), who argued that the conformal bound \(v_s \leq c/\sqrt{3}\) is inconsistent with two-solar-mass neutron stars. It also builds on the variational approach of Rhoades and Ruffini (1974) and the kinetic-theory causality constraints of Olson (2000). The study enables future gravitational-wave measurements of tidal polarizability (e.g., from Advanced LIGO and the Einstein Telescope) to discriminate between different upper bounds on the speed of sound and thus constrain the high-density equation of state.
