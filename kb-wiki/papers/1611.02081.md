# In-medium bound-state formation and inhomogeneous condensation in Fermi gases in a hard-wall box

> The formation of bosonic bound states underlies the formation of a superfluid ground state in the many-body phase diagram of ultracold Fermi gases. We study bound-state formation in a spin- and mass-imbalanced ultracold Fermi gas confined in a box with hard-wall boundary conditions. Because of the presence of finite Fermi spheres, the center-of-mass momentum of the potentially formed bound states can be finite, depending on the parameters controlling mass and spin imbalance as well as the coupling strength. We exploit this observation to estimate the potential location of inhomogeneous phases in the many-body phase diagram as a function of spin- and mass imbalance as well as the box size. Our results suggest that a hard-wall box does not alter substantially the many-body phase diagram calculated in the thermodynamic limit. Therefore, such a box may serve as an ideal trap potential to bring experiment and theory closely together and facilitate the search for exotic inhomogeneous ground states.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a hard-wall box trap does not substantially alter the many-body phase diagram calculated in the thermodynamic limit, suggesting such a box may serve as an ideal trap to bring experiment and theory together for searching inhomogeneous ground states, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that their explicit calculations are performed only for one-dimensional systems and rely on an in-medium two-body approximation rather than a full many-body treatment.

## Method

The authors solve a Schrödinger equation for two distinguishable fermions in the presence of inert Fermi seas confined in a one-dimensional hard-wall box, using a contact interaction with coupling strength g = 1/a_s. The ground-state wave function is expanded in the complete orthonormalized set of single-particle eigenfunctions for a hard-wall box, and the coefficients are obtained from numerical diagonalization. The center-of-mass momentum distribution n_com(Q) of the lowest-lying bosonic state is computed from the Fourier transform of the wave function, and the position Q_0 of its global maximum determines the most probable center-of-mass momentum of the two fermions.

## Key numbers

- The bound state energy in free space (vacuum, no Fermi seas) is E_B = g^2/8, using reduced mass m_r = 1/4
- The Fermi energies are given by ε_F,σ = (2m_σ)^{-1}(π(N_σ - 1)/L)^2, where N_σ is the number of fermions of species σ and L is the box extent
- The parameter measuring relative mass difference is defined as \bar{m} = (m_↓ - m_↑)/(m_↓ + m_↑)
- The polarization is defined as P = (N_↑ - N_↓)/(N_↑ + N_↓)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium bound-state formation in the thermodynamic limit by Roscher (2013) and Roscher (2015) to the case of a hard-wall box, motivated by the observation that the center-of-mass momentum of bound states in those studies showed semi-quantitative agreement with the location of inhomogeneous FFLO-type phases in the many-body phase diagram. The authors contrast the hard-wall box with harmonic traps, where the space-dependent chemical potentials produce a shell structure that complicates detection of inhomogeneous phases, as studied by Bulgac (2007) and others using Density Functional Theory in the Local Density Approximation. The work enables future experimental searches for FFLO-type ground states using hard-wall box traps, which have been explored experimentally by Mukherjee (2016) and by the MIT group (2013).
