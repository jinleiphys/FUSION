# Penetrating probes: Jets and photons in a non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma

> We employ new field-theoretical tools to study photons and jets in a non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma. Jet broadening and photon emission takes place through radiation which is suppressed by repeated and coherent interaction with the medium. We analyze this physics in an anisotropic plasma such as is created in the early stages of heavy-ion collisions. The anisotropy introduces an angular dependence in radiation and reduces its overall rate. This can affect phenomenological predictions of the rapidity dependence and angular flow of jets and photons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (QGP), such as created in the early stages of heavy-ion collisions, the rate of medium-induced bremsstrahlung for both photons and jets is reduced overall and acquires an angular dependence, but this result is derived under the assumption that the anisotropy is small (ξ < g²) and the low-energy part of the collision kernel is cut off to avoid divergences from Weibel instabilities.

## Method

The authors use out-of-equilibrium field theory (real-time formalism) to derive integral equations for the differential rate of photon and gluon emission via medium-induced bremsstrahlung, fully incorporating the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. They solve these equations numerically by expanding the function g(p⊥) in a functional basis, using an anisotropic momentum distribution of the form f(p) = √(1+ξ) f_eq(√(p² + ξ(p·n)²)/Λ) where ξ is the anisotropy parameter and Λ is the hard scale. The collision kernel is obtained from the resummed rr correlator of soft mediating gluons, and for small anisotropy the low-energy divergence is screened as 1/q² → 1/(q² + ξ m²).

- The collision kernel in thermal equilibrium is C(q) = C_F [1/q² - 1/(q² + m_D²)] where m_D² is the Debye mass; in an anisotropic medium it becomes 1/q² → 1/(q² + ξ m²) where q² and m² are of order g²Λ²

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of photon production in non-equilibrium media by Schenke (2006) and Hauksson (2016) for two-to-two scattering, now treating medium-induced bremsstrahlung with the LPM effect fully accounted for. The results are consistent with kinetic theory results from Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY). The calculation enables phenomenological predictions for the rapidity dependence and angular flow of jets and photons in heavy-ion collisions, where the QGP is initially highly anisotropic in the rapidity direction, and could affect elliptic flow calculations in viscous hydrodynamics.
