# Relation of E1 pygmy and toroidal resonances

> A possible relation of the low-lying E1 (pygmy resonance) and toroidal strengths is analyzed by using Skyrme-RPA results for the strength functions, transition densities and current fields in $^{208}$Pb. It is shown that the irrotational pygmy motion can appear as a local manifestation of the collective vortical toroidal dipole resonance (TDR) at the nuclear surface. The RPA results are compared to unperturbed (1ph) ones.

## Key claim

The isoscalar part of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) can be interpreted as a local manifestation of the collective vortical toroidal dipole resonance (TDR) at the nuclear surface, despite the PDR being an irrotational mode and the TDR being a vortical mode; the peripheral motion of the neutron excess in the PDR resembles the peripheral nucleon motions in the TDR, making the difference locally insignificant.

## Method

The analysis was performed for \(^{208}\)Pb using the Skyrme random-phase-approximation (RPA) with the SLy6 force. Strength functions, transition densities (TD), and current transition densities (CTD) were calculated for E1 excitations, with TD and CTD summed over RPA states in the energy interval 6.0–8.8 MeV to focus on gross features of nuclear flow. The RPA results were compared to unperturbed (1ph) results to assess collectivity.

## Key numbers

- The PDR has two peaks at 7.5 and 10.3 MeV in \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The TDR lies at 6–9 MeV, with its energy almost coinciding with the PDR peak at 7.5 MeV.
- The TDR peak at 7.5 MeV embraces two RPA states at 7.4 and 7.7 MeV, emerging from superposition of 1ph components with maximal ones exhausting 67% and 23% of the norm, respectively.
- The low-energy ISGDR branch observed in \((\alpha,\alpha')\) reaction lies at 12–14 MeV, noticeably above the calculated TDR.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous suggestion (Repko 2013) that the low-energy isoscalar part of the PDR can be interpreted as a peripheral fraction of the isoscalar TDR, based on analysis of E1(T=0) strength functions, transition densities, and current fields in \(^{208}\)Pb. It contrasts with the common view of the PDR as a fully irrotational mode built from oscillations of the neutron excess against the N=Z core (Paar 2007). The results enable further systematic calculations for PDR/TDR in different mass regions to verify whether PDR excitations always share the energy region with the TDR and exhibit toroidal current fields.
