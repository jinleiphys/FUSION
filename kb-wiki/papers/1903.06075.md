# Universal scaling of conserved charge in the stochastic diffusion dynamics

> In this paper, we explore the Kibble-Zurek scaling of the conserved charge, using the stachastic diffusion dynamics. After determining the characteristic scales $τ_{kz}$ and $l_{kz}$ and properly rescaling the traditional correlation function and cumulant, we construct universal functions for both the two-point correlation function $C(y_1-y_2;τ)$ and second-order cumulant $K(Δy,τ)$ of the conserved charge in the critical regime, which are insensitive to the initial temperature and a parameter in the mapping between 3D Ising model and the hot QCD system near the critical point.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that, within the stochastic diffusion dynamics (model B) for a 1+1-dimensional Bjorken-expanding system, the two-point correlation function and second-order cumulant of the conserved charge can be rescaled into universal functions that are insensitive to the strength of the critical component \(c_c\) (a parameter in the mapping from the 3D Ising model to QCD) and the initial temperature \(T_0\), but only near the critical point where the relative time \(\tau-\tau_c\) is small.

## Method
The authors analytically solve the stochastic diffusion equation for the conserved charge density \(\delta n(y,\tau)\) in 1+1-dimensional rapidity space under Bjorken expansion. The susceptibility \(\chi(\tau)\) and diffusion coefficient \(D(\tau)\) are parameterized via a linear mapping from the 3D Ising model to hot QCD, using the linear parametric model with Ising critical exponents. The Kibble-Zurek characteristic time \(\tau_{\text{KZ}}\) and length \(l_{\text{KZ}}\) are determined from the point \(\tau^*\) where the relaxation time equals the quench time, and these scales are used to rescale the correlation function \(C(y_1-y_2;\tau)\) and cumulant \(K(\Delta y,\tau)\) into universal forms \(\tilde{C}(\tilde{y}_1-\tilde{y}_2,\tilde{\tau})\) and \(\tilde{K}(\Delta y/l_{\text{KZ}},\tilde{\tau})\).

## Key numbers
- The correlation length \(\xi\) is related to the susceptibility as \(\xi = \xi_0 (\chi^{\text{cr}}/\chi^H)^{1/(2-\chi_\eta)}\) with \(\xi_0 = 0.1\) fm and \(\chi_\eta \simeq 0.04\).
- The diffusion coefficient scales as \(D^{\text{cr}}_C \sim \xi^{-2-\chi_\eta+\chi_\lambda}\) with \(\chi_\lambda \simeq 0.916\); the constant \(d_c = 1\) fm is used.
- The critical temperature is set to \(T_c = 160\) MeV and the width of the critical region to \(\Delta T/\Delta H = 10\) MeV.
- The speed of sound is taken as \(c_s^2 = 0.15\) for the Bjorken cooling law \(T(\tau) = T_0 (\tau_0/\tau)^{c_s^2}\).
- The initial rescaled time is fixed at \(\tilde{\tau}_0 = -2.5\) for all calculations.
- The universal functions are tested for \(c_c = 1, 2, 3\) and initial temperatures \(T_0 = 170, 180, 190\) MeV.
- The cumulant is evaluated at a fixed rescaled window \(\Delta y/l_{\text{KZ}} = 4\).

## Context
This work extends the Kibble-Zurek scaling approach previously applied to the non-conserved order parameter field (model A) in Mukherjee (2016) and Wu (2018) to the conserved charge (model B), which is more directly related to experimental observables like net charge fluctuations. It builds on the stochastic diffusion dynamics developed by Sakaida (2017) and Nahrgang (2018), which showed non-monotonic behavior of the second-order cumulant with rapidity window. The results enable future studies of universal scaling for higher-order cumulants and multi-point correlation functions in more realistic 3+1-dimensional simulations, potentially connecting to experimental measurements from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.
