# Color superconductivity under neutron-star conditions at next-to-leading order

> The equation of state of deconfined strongly interacting matter at high densities remains an open question, with effects from quark pairing in the preferred color-flavor-locked (CFL) ground state possibly playing an important role. Recent studies suggest that at least large pairing gaps in the CFL phase are incompatible with current astrophysical observations of neutron stars. At the same time, it has recently been shown that in two-flavor quark matter, subleading corrections from pairing effects can be much larger than would be naïvely expected, even for comparatively small gaps. In the present Letter, we compute next-to-leading-order corrections to the pressure of quark matter in the CFL phase arising from the gap and the strong coupling constant, incorporating neutron-star equilibrium conditions and current state-of-the-art perturbative QCD results. We find that the corrections are again quite sizable, and they allow us to constrain the CFL gap in the quark energy spectrum to $Δ_{\rm CFL} \lesssim 140~{\rm MeV}$ at a baryon chemical potential $μ_{\rm B} = 2.6~{\rm GeV}$, even when allowing for a wide range of possible behaviors for the dependence of the gap on the chemical potential.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections to the pressure of color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter are sizable and allow a constraint on the CFL gap to Δ_CFL ≲ 140 MeV at a baryon chemical potential μ_B = 2.6 GeV, but this bound depends on allowing a wide range of possible behaviors for the gap's dependence on chemical potential and on marginalizing over astrophysical and low-density nuclear-physics inputs.

## Method

The authors compute the pressure of CFL quark matter at zero temperature by expanding the quantum effective action to two-loop order in the strong coupling α_s, the strange quark mass m_s, and the CFL gap Δ_CFL, using an effective action derived from QCD via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. They impose neutron-star equilibrium conditions (charge neutrality, color neutrality, and beta equilibrium) and combine their NLO CFL pressure with state-of-the-art perturbative QCD results for normal quark matter at order O(α_s^{5/2}). A Bayesian analysis is performed, marginalizing over chiral effective field theory results below 1.1 nuclear saturation density and astrophysical observations (mass measurements of PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1624-2230, NICER mass-radius posteriors for PSR J0740+6620, tidal deformability from GW170817, and the black-hole formation assumption from GW170817), using a power-law scaling Δ_CFL = Δ_CFL^* (μ_B/μ_B^*)^σ for the gap.

## Key numbers

* The NLO coefficient γ_1 in the pressure expansion p = p_free [γ_0 + γ_1 (Δ_CFL/(μ_B/3))^2 + ...] is γ_1(α_s, m_s^2) = 4 - 4 m_s^2/3 + 40.9 α_s.
* The CFL phase is favored over normal quark matter when Δ_CFL > (m_s^2/4)[1 - 5.11 α_s + m_s^2/6] under neutron-star conditions.
* For three massless flavors, the 2SC phase dominates over CFL only if Δ_2SC/Δ_CFL > √3 + 2.90 α_s.
* The upper bound on the CFL gap is Δ_CFL ≲ 140 MeV at μ_B = 2.6 GeV at 95% credibility for the symmetric ensemble (matching density n_L = n_TOV and maximum speed of sound squared c_s^2 = 2/3).
* The NLO corrections tighten the 95% upper bound by about a factor of two compared to leading-order results.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of CFL gap constraints by Kurkela, Steinhorst, and Rajagopal (2024) by including NLO corrections in α_s and m_s, and by allowing the gap to depend on chemical potential via a power-law scaling. It also builds on the two-flavor NLO pairing corrections computed by Geißel, Gorda, and Braun (2024). The results enable more precise first-principles constraints on color-superconducting gaps from neutron-star observations, and suggest that the weak-coupling expansion of the pressure may converge more poorly in the CFL phase than in normal quark matter, motivating further higher-order calculations.
