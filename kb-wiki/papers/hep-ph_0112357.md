# New chiral-symmetry-breaking operators in pseudoscalar QCD sum rules

> Nonperturbative Wilson coefficients associated with the leading chiral-symmetry-breaking operators in the operator product expansion of the pseudoscalar QCD correlation function are derived. Implementation of the new, instanton-induced operators enables the corresponding spectral sum rule to reproduce the small pion mass scale, thereby reconciling it with Goldstone's theorem. The same operators suppress the contributions of pionic resonances. Several predictions and structural insights from the new sum rule are discussed.

# New chiral-symmetry-breaking operators in pseudoscalar QCD sum rules

## Key claim

The conventional operator product expansion (OPE) of the pseudoscalar QCD correlation function is practically blind to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, failing to predict a pion mass below standard hadronic scales. New instanton-induced contributions to the Wilson coefficients of the leading chiral-symmetry-breaking operators (quark condensate and mixed quark-gluon condensate) can reconcile the pseudoscalar sum rule with Goldstone's theorem and reproduce the small pion mass scale, provided the effective quark mass parameter \(\bar{m}_{q,1}\) is approximately 20 MeV.

## Method

The author derives nonperturbative, instanton-generated Wilson coefficients for the \(\bar{q}q\) and \(g\bar{q}\sigma Gq\) operators in the instanton-improved operator product expansion (IOPE) of the pseudoscalar correlation function, using semiclassical techniques around the (anti-)instanton in the background of long-wavelength quark and gluon vacuum fields. A Borel-transformed QCD sum rule is then constructed by equating the IOPE description to a hadronic spectral function containing the pion pole, its first excitation \(\pi'\), and a duality continuum that includes both perturbative and instanton contributions. The sum rule is analyzed with standard condensate values and an instanton distribution approximated by a delta function at average size \(\bar{\rho}=0.33\) fm and density \(\bar{n}=0.5\) fm\(^{-4}\), fixing the pion mass and \(\pi'\) mass from experiment to determine the couplings and continuum threshold.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass fixed at \(m_{\pi^0}=135\) MeV and first excitation mass at \(m_{\pi'}=1300\) MeV from experiment (Groom et al., 2000).
* Resulting pion coupling squared \(\lambda_\pi^2 = 0.078\) GeV\(^4\) and \(\pi'\) coupling squared \(\lambda_{\pi'}^2 = 0.032\) GeV\(^4\) from the optimized sum rule.
* Continuum threshold \(s_0 = 1.84\) GeV\(^2\) from the sum-rule analysis.
* Light quark mass sum \(m_u + m_d \simeq 9.0\) MeV (at \(\mu \sim 1\) GeV), derived from \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV, and \(\lambda_\pi\).
* Effective quark mass for one zero-mode propagator \(\bar{m}_{q,1} \sim 20\) MeV, estimated from the large-\(N_c\) instanton-liquid model at momentum transfer \(\bar{Q}_1 = 1.5\) GeV.
* Effective quark mass for two zero-mode propagators \(\bar{m}_{q,2} \simeq 85\) MeV at average instanton size \(\bar{\rho} \simeq 0.33\) fm, adopted from Faccioli and Shuryak (2001).
* Average instanton size \(\bar{\rho} = 0.33\) fm and density \(\bar{n} = 0.5\) fm\(^{-4}\) used in the analysis.
* Standard condensate values: \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -0.0156\) GeV\(^3\), \(\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle = 0.04\) GeV\(^4\), \(m_0^2 = 0.8\) GeV\(^2\) for the mixed condensate.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum-rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) for the pseudoscalar channel, which previously failed to predict a pion mass below standard hadronic scales. Prior attempts to resolve this puzzle included a chirally invariant power correction proposed by Chetyrkin, Narison, and Zakharov (1999) and direct-instanton corrections to the unit-operator coefficient studied by Steele et al. (1997), but both were chirally invariant and unable to generate the physical pion mass. The new instanton-induced chiral-symmetry-breaking operators enable the sum rule to reproduce the Goldstone nature of the pion for the first time, suppress higher resonance contributions, and provide a framework for improved light quark mass determinations. The results are consistent with lattice simulations of mesonic point-to-point correlators by Chu et al. (1993), which find the pseudoscalar correlator well described by just the ground state pole and duality continuum.
