# A quark-meson coupling model with short-range quark-quark correlations

> Short-range quark-quark correlations are introduced into the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model in a simple way. The effect of these correlations on the structure of the nucleon in dense nuclear matter is studied. We find that the short-range correlations may serve to reduce a serious problem associated with the modified quark-meson coupling model (within which the bag constant is allowed to decrease with increasing density), namely the tendency for the size of the bound nucleon to increase rapidly as the density rises. We also find that, with the addition of correlations, both QMC and modified QMC are consistent with the phenomenological equation of state at high density.

## Key claim

Short-range quark-quark correlations, introduced via a simple geometrical overlap probability and contact interactions, can resolve the problem of the nucleon bag radius growing too rapidly at high density in the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model, while also allowing both the standard QMC and MQMC models to reproduce the phenomenological equation of state at high density.

## Method

The authors add scalar- and vector-type contact interactions between quarks in overlapping nucleon bags to the standard QMC Lagrangian, using a mean-field approximation. The probability of nucleon overlap is estimated from a simple geometrical model of rigid spheres, where the overlap volume between two nucleons of radius \(R\) separated by distance \(d\) is proportional to \(1 - 3y/4 + y^3/16\) with \(y = d/R\). The Dirac equation for a quark in a bag is solved self-consistently with an effective quark mass modified by the scalar correlation term, and the total energy per nucleon is computed as a function of nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon bag radius \(R_0 = 0.8\) fm; free bag constant \(B_0^{1/4} = 170.0\) MeV; zero-point parameter \(z = 3.295\).
- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) with binding energy \(-15.7\) MeV.
- For QMC with correlations (QMCs): \(g_\sigma^2 = 67.80\), \(g_\omega^2 = 66.71\); vector correlation strength \(\bar{f}_v^q = 300\); scalar correlation strength \(\bar{f}_s^q = 200\).
- For MQMC with correlations (MQMCs): \(g_\sigma^2 = 35.69\), \(g_\omega^2 = 80.68\); \(\bar{f}_v^q = 10\); \(\bar{f}_s^q = 200\).
- Nucleon overlap begins at \(\rho_B/\rho_0 \sim 2.7\) for QMCs and at \(\rho_B/\rho_0 \sim 1.3\) for MQMCs.
- In the original MQMC, no solution for the quark eigenvalue could be found beyond \(\rho_B/\rho_0 \sim 3\); with correlations, a solution exists at higher densities.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988) and the modified QMC model of Jin and Jennings (1997) by including short-range quark-quark correlations that become important when nucleon bags overlap at high density. The approach enables the QMC framework to describe nuclear matter up to several times saturation density, consistent with the empirical equation of state from Sano (1999), and addresses a known problem in MQMC where the nucleon bag radius grows unphysically large at high density (Jin and Jennings, 1997; Muller, 1998). The authors suggest future work should formulate the correlations more dynamically (Mahaux, 1985) and apply the model to finite nuclei and hypernuclei.
