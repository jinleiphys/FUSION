# New developments in relativistic magnetohydrodynamics

> Relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) provides an extremely useful description of the low-energy long-wavelength phenomena in a variety of physical systems from quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions to matters in supernovas, compact stars, and early universe. We review the recent theoretical progresses of RMHD, such as a formulation of RMHD from the perspective of magnetic flux conservation using the entropy-current analysis, the nonequilibrium statistical operator approach applied to quantum electrodynamics, and the relativistic kinetic theory. We discuss how the transport coefficients in RMHD are computed in kinetic theory and perturbative quantum field theories. We also explore the collective modes and instabilities in RMHD with a special emphasis on the role of chirality in a parity-odd plasma. We also give some future prospects of RMHD, including the interaction with spin hydrodynamics and the new kinetic framework with magnetic flux conservation.

## Key claim

This review argues that relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) can be formulated as a low-energy effective theory based on the conservation of magnetic flux (via the Bianchi identity) treated as a conserved quantity associated with a one-form magnetic U(1) symmetry, rather than by coupling the Navier-Stokes equations to the full Maxwell equations as in conventional approaches; the authors emphasize that this new formulation applies specifically in the regime of strong magnetic fields where the magnetic field persists as a leading-order hydrodynamic variable.

## Method

The authors review three complementary theoretical approaches to RMHD: (1) a macroscopic entropy-current analysis that derives constitutive relations by requiring the semi-positivity of the entropy-current divergence, treating the magnetic flux as a conserved quantity; (2) a nonequilibrium statistical operator method applied to quantum electrodynamics (QED); and (3) relativistic kinetic theory and perturbative quantum field theory for computing transport coefficients. They also analyze collective modes and instabilities in parity-odd plasmas (chiral MHD) and compare the new formulation with the conventional approach where matter and electromagnetic fields are treated separately.

## Key numbers

- Peak magnetic field strength in Au + Au collisions at RHIC: up to 10^19 Gauss
- Peak magnetic field strength in Pb + Pb collisions at LHC: up to 10^20 Gauss
- Surface magnetic fields of neutron stars: 10^12–10^13 Gauss
- Surface magnetic fields of magnetars: 10^14–10^15 Gauss
- Fine structure constant used: α = e^2/(4π) = 1/137

## Context

This review extends the line of work on relativistic hydrodynamics as an effective field theory (Eckart 1940, Landau and Lifshitz 1959) by incorporating the recent insight that the Bianchi identity for electromagnetic fields can be treated as a conservation law associated with a one-form magnetic U(1) symmetry (Grozdanov 2016, Hattori 2017, Glorioso 2018, Armas 2018, Hongo 2020). It contrasts with conventional MHD formulations (Landau and Lifshitz, Lichnerowicz 1967) that keep the electric field as a dynamical variable, and it enables future work connecting RMHD with spin hydrodynamics and developing new kinetic frameworks with magnetic flux conservation.
