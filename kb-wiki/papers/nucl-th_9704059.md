# Perspective: Atomic Parity Violation and the Nuclear Anapole Moment

> I discuss in qualitative terms the implications of the recent measurement by C. S. Wood et al. of the hyperfine dependence of atomic parity violation in 133Cs. The derived anapole moment constrains the strength of hadronic parity violation.

# Perspective: Atomic Parity Violation and the Nuclear Anapole Moment

## Key claim

The first definitive measurement of a nuclear anapole moment in 133Cs yields a value for the anapole parameter κ of 0.127 ± 0.019, differing from zero by approximately 7σ, which constrains the strength of hadronic parity violation but creates a conflict with prior hadronic parity violation experiments that suggest the pion weak meson-nucleon coupling fπ may be substantially less than one.

## Method

The Colorado group measured the hyperfine dependence of atomic parity violation in 133Cs with 0.35% precision, a seven-fold improvement over their previous 2.2% measurement. By isolating the nuclear-spin-dependent component of atomic parity violation from the much larger coherent Z0 exchange contribution, they extracted the anapole moment parameter κ from the parity-violating atomic Hamiltonian HWA = (GF/√2) κ α·I ρ(r). The nuclear matrix elements from shell model calculations were used to separate the anapole contribution from the nuclear-spin-dependent Z0 exchange contribution.

## Key numbers

- Anapole parameter κ = 0.127 ± 0.019 for 133Cs, a result differing from zero by approximately 7σ
- The nuclear-spin-dependent Z0 exchange contribution κ(Z0) is approximately 0.0132, calculated using GA ≈ 1.26 and sin2θW ≈ 0.223
- The measurement precision is 0.35%, a seven-fold improvement over the previous 2.2% measurement by the same Colorado group
- The anapole moment grows as A2/3, where A is the atomic number, and is expected to exceed direct nuclear-spin-dependent parity violation for nuclei heavier than A ≈ 20
- Theoretical calculations yield κ values of 0.074 (Haxton) and 0.074-0.095 (Flambaum) when the anapole moment is included

## Context

This work extends the earlier tentative identification of the anapole moment by the Colorado group (Noecker, 1988) at 2.2% precision and contrasts with the Seattle group's null result (Vetter, 1995) in 205Tl at 1.2% precision. The measurement provides a new technique for studying hadronic weak interactions, which have proven more elusive than weak interactions involving leptons, and creates a conflict with prior hadronic parity violation experiments (Adelberger, 1995) that suggest the pion weak meson-nucleon coupling fπ may be substantially less than the best values of Donoghue (1992). The 133Cs anapole moment depends primarily on the coupling combination (fπ + 0.52 fρ0), where fπ is generated almost entirely by the neutral current.
