# High energy factorization in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We derive a high energy factorization theorem for inclusive gluon production in A+A collisions. Our factorized formula resums i) all order leading logarithms (g^2 \ln(1/x_{1,2}))^n of the incoming partons momentum fractions, and ii) all contributions (g ρ_{1,2})^n that are enhanced when the color charge densities in the two nuclei are of order of the inverse coupling-- ρ_{1,2}\sim g^{-1}. The resummed inclusive gluon spectrum can be expressed as a convolution of gauge invariant distributions W[ρ_{1,2}] from each of the nuclei with the leading order gluon number operator. These distributions are shown to satisfy the JIMWLK equation describing the evolution of nuclear wavefunctions with rapidity. As a by-product, we demonstrate that the JIMWLK Hamiltonian can be derived entirely in terms of retarded light cone Green's functions without any ambiguities in their pole prescriptions. We comment on the implications of our results for understanding the Glasma produced at early times in A+A collisions at collider energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives a high energy factorization theorem for inclusive gluon production in A+A collisions that resums all leading logarithms of the incoming partons' momentum fractions and all contributions enhanced when color charge densities are of order the inverse coupling, but the authors explicitly note they have not attempted to show the formula is valid for sub-leading logarithms.

## Method

The authors develop a formalism in QCD to compute inclusive gluon production by expressing the next-to-leading order corrections as a linear operator acting on the leading order result, separating initial state physics from the collision dynamics. They derive the JIMWLK Hamiltonian entirely in terms of retarded light cone Green's functions without ambiguities in pole prescriptions, and then extend this treatment to the collision of two nuclei to show that non-factorizable terms are suppressed. The factorized result expresses the resummed inclusive gluon spectrum as a convolution of gauge invariant distributions W[ρ₁,₂] from each nucleus with the leading order gluon number operator, where these distributions satisfy the JIMWLK equation describing evolution of nuclear wavefunctions with rapidity.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the JIMWLK renormalization group equation derived by Jalilian-Marian, Kovner, McLerran, Weigert, Iancu, Leonidov, and Mueller (1997-2001) to provide a rigorous factorization theorem for nucleus-nucleus collisions. The result enables systematic computation of the Glasma produced at early times in heavy ion collisions at collider energies and its subsequent evolution into the Quark Gluon Plasma, while also providing a framework for future extensions beyond leading logarithmic accuracy.
