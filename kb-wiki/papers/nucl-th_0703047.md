# Chiral bands for quasi-proton and quasi-neutron coupling with a triaxial rotor

> A particle rotor model (PRM) with a quasi-proton and a quasi-neutron coupled with a triaxial rotor is developed and applied to study chiral doublet bands with configurations of a $h_{11/2}$ proton and a $h_{11/2}$ quasi-neutron. With pairing treated by the BCS approximation, the present quasi-particle PRM is aimed at simulating one proton and many neutron holes coupled with a triaxial rotor. After a detailed analysis of the angular momentum orientations, energy separation between the partner bands, and behavior of electromagnetic transitions, for the first time we find aplanar rotation or equivalently chiral geometry beyond the usual one proton and one neutron hole coupled with a triaxial rotor.

## Key claim

The paper develops a quasi-particle particle-rotor model (PRM) that, for the first time, demonstrates aplanar rotation (chiral geometry) beyond the usual one-proton-one-neutron-hole configuration, specifically for configurations where the neutron is treated as a BCS quasiparticle rather than a pure hole. The key qualifier is that the best chiral degeneracy (energy differences below 100 keV) occurs only for the pure particle-hole case (λ_n = ε_6), while configurations with the neutron Fermi energy deeper in the shell produce larger energy separations and less ideal chiral behavior.

## Method

A particle-rotor model was developed coupling a quasi-proton and a quasi-neutron with a triaxial rotor, with pairing correlations treated via the BCS approximation. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a symmetrized strong-coupling basis built from quasiparticle states, using a single-j (h_{11/2}) model for the valence nucleons with C = 0.3 MeV (corresponding to β ∼ 0.28) and moment of inertia J = 30 MeV⁻¹. The proton was fixed as a pure h_{11/2} particle (λ_p = ε_1, Δ_p = 0), while the neutron Fermi energy λ_n was varied from ε_6 (pure hole) to ε_1 (pure particle) with Δ_n = 1 MeV, and the resulting energy spectra, electromagnetic transition probabilities, and angular momentum orientations were analyzed as functions of spin I and triaxial deformation γ.

## Key numbers

- For λ_n = ε_6 (pure particle-hole) at γ = 30°, the energy difference between yrare and yrast bands is below 100 keV for spins 13 ≤ I ≤ 17; at I = 15 and I = 17, the energy differences are 7.2 keV and 4.1 keV respectively.
- For λ_n = ε_5 at γ = 30°, the two bands show a nearly constant energy separation of ∼ 200 keV for spins 11 ≤ I ≤ 15.
- For λ_n = ε_4 and ε_3 at γ = 30°, the energy difference between yrast and yrare states is smaller than 250 keV only at low spins (I = 9, 11, 12); for I ≥ 14, the difference increases to ∼ 400 keV at I = 14 and ∼ 700 keV at I = 20.
- For λ_n = ε_1 at γ = 30°, the two bands are separated by an average energy of ∼ 800 keV.
- At γ = 20° and 40° for λ_n = ε_6, the energy difference E₂(I) − E₁(I) varies from 100 to 250 keV; at γ = 15° and 45°, the difference is around 450 keV.
- For γ ∈ (20°, 30°) with λ_n = ε_5, a near constant energy difference of ∼ 200–250 keV is observed; for γ ≤ 15° or γ ≥ 45°, the energy difference exceeds 450 keV.

## Context

This work extends the original chiral rotation concept of Frauendorf and Meng (1997), which was based on one proton particle and one neutron hole coupled to a triaxial rotor, to more realistic configurations where the neutron Fermi energy lies deeper in the h_{11/2} shell. The model was previously applied to the doublet bands of ¹²⁶Cs by Wang et al. (2007), and the present paper provides the full formalism and systematic study. The results show that deviations from the ideal particle-hole configuration (either through Fermi energy changes or γ deformation away from 30°) produce near-constant energy differences rather than perfect degeneracy, explaining the behavior observed in candidate chiral nuclei such as ¹³⁴Pr (Petrache et al., 2006) and the Cs isotopes (Koike et al., 2003; Wang et al., 2006). This enables future studies of chiral bands in nuclei with multiple valence nucleons and provides a framework for interpreting experimental data on electromagnetic transitions and angular momentum orientations.
