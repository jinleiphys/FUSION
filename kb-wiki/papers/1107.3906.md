# Antiproton scattering off $^3 He$ and $^4 He$ nuclei at low and intermediate energies

> Antiproton scattering off $^3\He$ and $^4\He$ targets is considered at beam energies below 300 MeV within the Glauber-Sitenko approach, utilizing the $\bar N N$ amplitudes of the Jülich model as input. A good agreement with available data on differential $\bar p ^4\He$ cross sections and on $\bar p ^3\He$ and $\pbar ^4\He$ reaction cross sections is obtained. Predictions for polarized total $\bar p ^3$He cross sections are presented, calculated within the single-scattering approximation and including Coulomb-nuclear interference effects. The kinetics of the polarization buildup is discussed.

# Antiproton scattering off $^3He$ and $^4He$ nuclei at low and intermediate energies

## Key claim

The Glauber-Sitenko multiple scattering approach, using elementary $\bar N N$ amplitudes from the Jülich models, reproduces available $\bar p ^4He$ differential cross sections and $\bar p ^3He$ and $\bar p ^4He$ reaction cross sections without free parameters, but the predicted polarization buildup efficiency for $\bar p ^3He$ is somewhat smaller than for $\bar p p$ and $\bar p d$ targets, making a polarized $^3He$ target less favorable for producing polarized antiproton beams.

## Method

The authors applied the Glauber-Sitenko multiple scattering theory to antiproton scattering on $^3He$ and $^4He$ targets at beam energies below 300 MeV, using $\bar N N$ amplitudes from two Jülich models (model A and model D) as input. For the polarized $\bar p ^3He$ total cross sections, they employed the single-scattering (impulse) approximation with explicit inclusion of Coulomb-nuclear interference effects, deriving analytic relations showing that the spin-dependent cross sections $\sigma_1$ and $\sigma_2$ are determined only by $\bar p n$ scattering due to the spin structure of the $^3He$ ground state.

## Key numbers

- At $T_{lab}=19.6$ MeV, the calculated $\bar p ^3He$ reaction cross section is 392 mb (model A) and 425 mb (model D), compared to the experimental value of $392\pm 23.8$ mb (Balestra et al.)
- The ratio of single-scattering to full multiple-scattering total cross section for $\bar p ^3He$ is $R\approx 1.45$ at 25 MeV, decreasing to $R=1.33$ at 179.6 MeV
- For $\bar p ^4He$ at 19.6 MeV (beam momentum 192.8 MeV/c), the Glauber-Sitenko calculation reproduces the differential cross section data from Bales et al. (1993) remarkably well, with Coulomb contributions important only at $\theta_{cm} < 2^\circ$
- The predicted polarization degree $P_{||}$ and $P_{\perp}$ for $\bar p ^3He$ at beam energies 0-300 MeV are in the order of five percent for target polarization $P_T=1$
- The Coulomb cross section for $\bar p ^3He$ is comparable to the purely hadronic unpolarized cross section $\sigma_0^h$ at beam energies around 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier Glauber-Sitenko analyses of antiproton scattering on light nuclei (Lakshmatikov 1981, Bendiscioli) by using microscopic $\bar N N$ amplitudes from the Jülich potential models (Hippchen 1990, Mull 1991) rather than phenomenological parameterizations. It is the first analysis of PS179 collaboration data for $\bar p ^4He$ (Bales 1993, Batusov) using such microscopic amplitudes. The study builds on prior work for $\bar p d$ scattering (Haidenbauer 2009, 2011; Salhi 2011) and provides predictions for the PAX collaboration's spin-filtering program at FAIR, suggesting that polarized $^3He$ targets are less favorable than polarized hydrogen or deuteron targets for producing polarized antiproton beams.
