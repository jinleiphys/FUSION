# Quantum Simulation of Cranked Zirconium Isotopes: A Fixed-N Approach with a Structured Number-Conserving Ansatz

> We present a methodological study of quantum simulation of cranking in a Nilsson $+$ pairing Hamiltonian on a fixed deformation grid. The many-body Routhian is mapped to qubits via the Jordan--Wigner transformation and minimized using the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) in a truncated active space $(M)$. We employ a structured, number-conserving singles-and-doubles ansatz: double excitations implement pair transfer, while singles are restricted to the nonzero Coriolis-coupling graph of the active Nilsson basis. For $M=8$, this yields 42 parameters while preserving particle number exactly. Exact number conservation enforces $\langle P_k \rangle = 0$, so the conventional pairing gap $Δ_κ\propto G\left|\sum_k \langle P_k \rangle\right|$ vanishes identically. We instead introduce a fixed-$N$ pairing-coherence diagnostic, \[ Δ_{\mathrm{coh}} = G \sqrt{\sum_{k \neq l} \left| \langle P_k^\dagger P_l \rangle \right|}, \] used as a scalar measure of off-diagonal pair coherence rather than a BCS gap. We study even-even $^{80,82,84}$Zr. $^{80}$Zr shows a stable oblate minimum at $δ^\ast \approx -0.25$; $^{82}$Zr exhibits the strongest rotational evolution; $^{84}$Zr retains a robust prolate minimum with the largest neutron pairing coherence. These results reflect the present truncated model rather than converged spectroscopy. A cranked BCS calculation on the same grid serves as a qualitative baseline. Comparisons between $M=6$ and $M=8$ show stable trends but visible shifts, so no active-space convergence is claimed. The structured fixed-$N$ ansatz thus captures consistent isotope trends and provides a practical framework to analyze pairing via $Δ_{\mathrm{coh}}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a structured number-conserving singles-and-doubles ansatz, restricted to pair-transfer and Coriolis-driven excitations, can capture isotope-dependent rotational and pairing trends in cranked zirconium isotopes within a truncated active-space Nilsson+pairing model, but the results are presented as a methodological study rather than converged spectroscopic predictions.

## Method

The many-body Routhian for a cranked Nilsson+pairing Hamiltonian on a fixed deformation grid (25 points in -0.5 ≤ δ ≤ 0.5, rotational scan 0 ≤ ħω ≤ 1.0 MeV) is mapped to qubits via the Jordan-Wigner transformation and minimized using the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) with L-BFGS-B optimization. The structured ansatz uses double excitations restricted to pair-transfer edges (28 parameters for M=8) and single excitations restricted to nonzero j_x coupling graph (14 parameters), yielding 42 total variational parameters while preserving exact particle number. A fixed-N pairing-coherence diagnostic Δ_coh = G √(∑_{k≠l} |⟨P_k^† P_l⟩|) replaces the conventional BCS gap, which vanishes identically in the number-conserving manifold.

## Key numbers

- For M=8 active space (16 qubits), the structured ansatz uses 42 variational parameters: 28 double-excitation edges from the complete pair-transfer graph and 14 single-excitation edges from the nonzero j_x graph
- ²⁰⁸Zr shows a stable oblate minimum at δ* ≈ -0.25 over the full cranking range 0 to 1.0 MeV
- ²⁰⁸Zr at ω=1.0 MeV: total aligned angular momentum J_x = 12.02, with equal proton and neutron contributions J_x,p = J_x,n = 6.01
- ²⁰⁸Zr at ω=0: neutron pair-coherence Δ_coh,n = 1.798 MeV, decreasing to 1.556 MeV at ω=1.0 MeV
- ²⁰⁸Zr at ω=1.0 MeV: total alignment J_x = 19.38, with J_x,n = 11.59 and J_x,p = 7.79
- ²⁰⁸Zr at ω=0: neutron Δ_coh,n = 1.797 MeV, proton Δ_coh,p = 1.690 MeV
- ²⁰⁸Zr at ω=1.0 MeV: total alignment J_x = 12.61, with J_x,n = 7.18 and J_x,p = 5.43
- ²⁰⁸Zr at ω=0: neutron Δ_coh,n = 1.905 MeV, proton Δ_coh,p = 1.695 MeV
- Pairing strength G = 0.5202 MeV used for both protons and neutrons, calibrated so that mean BCS gap matches 1.878 MeV from NNDC NuDat 3 database
- VQE convergence: final callback step sizes ≤ 1.6×10⁻¹² MeV across production runs

## Context

This work extends quantum simulation approaches to cranked nuclear systems beyond the simplified Nilsson-Strutinsky models of Roy (2025) and the pairing-only treatments of Zhang (2024), by incorporating exact deformation-dependent j_x matrix elements from Nilsson eigenvectors and a structured number-conserving ansatz rather than adaptive pool constructions. The fixed-N pairing-coherence diagnostic Δ_coh provides an alternative to broken-symmetry BCS gaps (Ring 1980) and number-projected methods (Raduta 2012, Petrovici 2025), enabling analysis of pairing correlations in variational manifolds where ⟨P_k⟩ vanishes by symmetry. The results enable future active-space extrapolation studies and comparisons with more sophisticated fixed-N classical methods, though the present calculations use statevector simulation and do not claim quantum advantage.
