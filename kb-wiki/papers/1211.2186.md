# Far-from-equilibrium heavy quark energy loss at strong coupling

> We study the energy loss of a heavy quark propagating through the matter produced in the collision of two sheets of energy [1]. Even though this matter is initially far-from-equilibrium we find that, when written in terms of the energy density, the equilibrium expression for heavy quark energy loss describes most qualitative features of our results well. At later times, once a plasma described by viscous hydrodynamics has formed, the equilibrium expression describes the heavy quark energy loss quantitatively. In addition to the drag force that makes it lose energy, a quark moving through the out-of-equilibrium matter feels a force perpendicular to its velocity.

# Far-from-equilibrium heavy quark energy loss at strong coupling

## Key claim
When expressed in terms of the instantaneous energy density, the equilibrium formula for heavy quark drag force describes most qualitative features of energy loss even in far-from-equilibrium matter, and becomes quantitatively accurate once viscous hydrodynamics applies. A heavy quark moving through out-of-equilibrium matter also experiences a force perpendicular to its velocity, which has no equilibrium analogue.

## Method
The authors study heavy quark energy loss in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory by colliding two planar sheets of energy with Gaussian profiles, using holography to map the collision to gravitational wave dynamics in asymptotically AdS5 spacetime. They insert a classical string attached to the boundary, drag its endpoint at constant velocity through the colliding geometry, and solve the string equations of motion using infalling Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates to compute the force required to maintain constant velocity. The instantaneous momentum loss is obtained from the canonical momentum flux at the string endpoint.

## Key numbers
- The incident sheets have energy density per unit transverse area of μ³(N_c²/2π²), where μ is an arbitrary scale; the Gaussian width of each sheet is σ = 1/(2μ)
- The equilibrium drag force formula is (d\vec p/dt)|_eq = (π/2) √λ T² \vec β/√(1-β²), where λ is the 't Hooft coupling and T is temperature
- For a quark moving with β=0.5 along z=0, the instantaneous momentum loss after collision agrees quantitatively with the equilibrium expectation at late times

## Context
This work extends the equilibrium heavy quark energy loss result of Herzog (2006) in strongly coupled N=4 SYM to far-from-equilibrium conditions, using the colliding sheet setup of Chesler (2010). It provides guidance for applying equilibrium energy loss formulas to heavy ion collisions, where quarks must propagate through initially far-from-equilibrium matter before the plasma thermalizes. The finding that the equilibrium expression with T defined from energy density works reasonably well even before equilibration suggests that uncertainties from this approximation may be smaller than other uncertainties in heavy ion phenomenology.
