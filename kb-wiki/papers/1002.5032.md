# Resource Letter: Quantum Chromodynamics

> This Resource Letter provides a guide to the literature on Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), the relativistic quantum field theory of the strong interactions. Journal articles, books, and other documents are cited for the following topics: quarks and color, the parton model, Yang-Mills theory, experimental evidence for color, QCD as a color gauge theory, asymptotic freedom, QCD for heavy hadrons, QCD on the lattice, the QCD vacuum, pictures of quark confinement, early and modern applications of perturbative QCD, the determination of the strong coupling and quark masses, QCD and the hadron spectrum, hadron decays, the quark-gluon plasma, the strong nuclear interaction, and QCD's role in nuclear physics. The letter {E} after an item indicates elementary level or material of general interest to persons becoming informed in the field. The letter {I}, for intermediate level, indicates material of a somewhat more specialized nature, and the letter {A} indicates rather specialized or advanced material.

## Key claim

This paper is a Resource Letter that provides a curated guide to the literature on Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), rather than presenting new research results. The authors select works useful for professors and students planning courses or independent study, including classic contributions, elementary presentations, and well-cited review articles that provide entry points to the contemporary literature.

## Method

The authors compiled and annotated references covering the full scope of QCD, organized into sections on the theory itself (Lagrangian formulation, asymptotic freedom, confinement), theoretical tools (symmetries, potential models, renormalization, lattice QCD, effective field theories), experimental tests (deep inelastic scattering, jet physics, determination of the strong coupling), and QCD's role in the broader standard model and nuclear physics. Each cited work is marked with a level indicator: E for elementary, I for intermediate, or A for advanced material.

## Key numbers

- The quark color factors for QCD based on SU(3) gauge symmetry are C_F = 4/3 and C_A = 3
- The light-quark condensate is measured as ⟨0|q̄q|0⟩ = [242 ± 4^(+19)_(-18) MeV]^3 in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV, where the first error is Monte Carlo statistics and the second encompasses systematic effects
- The strong CP-violating phase is constrained by the neutron electric dipole moment to be approximately 10^(-11)
- By 2008, lattice-QCD calculations of hadron masses had been carried out with an accuracy of a few percent

## Context

This Resource Letter extends the tradition of pedagogical literature guides in physics, following the format established by the American Physical Society's Resource Letter series. It provides a comprehensive entry point for students and researchers into the vast QCD literature, covering topics from the foundational papers of Gross (1973), Politzer (1973), and Wilczek (1973) on asymptotic freedom through modern lattice QCD calculations reviewed by Kronfeld (2008). The paper enables readers to navigate the field by organizing references by topic and difficulty level, with links to digital versions where available.
