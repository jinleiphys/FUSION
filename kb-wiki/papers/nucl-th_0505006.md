# A derivation of the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation from multiple scattering using the Wigner function

> A derivation is given of the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation beginning from multiple scattering considerations. The motivation for the discussion, which is purely pedagogical in nature, is the current interest in understanding the origins of transport equations in terms of rigorous field-theory descriptions, or, as in this case, exact nonrelativistic formulations.

# A derivation of the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation from multiple scattering using the Wigner function

## Key claim

The paper derives the Boltzmann-Vlasov transport equation from multiple-scattering theory using the Wigner function, but only under the restrictive assumptions of extremely high projectile energy, completely uncorrelated scatterers, and a volume much larger than the scattering support domain, conditions that are acknowledged as necessary to reach the simplest classical transport result.

## Method

Starting from the Watson multiple-scattering series for a projectile passing through N uncorrelated target particles, the author writes the projectile wave function in eikonal form and constructs the Wigner function for the static problem. The action of the longitudinal derivative v ∂/∂z on the Wigner function is then separated into a drift term (involving the optical potential) and a collision term (involving direct scattering from individual target particles), with the collision term evaluated by integrating over the longitudinal coordinate and using profile functions to express the result in terms of scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The target density is taken as ρ_i(r_i) = 1/Ω, where Ω is the volume containing the particles (no numerical value given).
- The collision term yields a factor (2π/k)² dσ_i(p'→p)/dΩ, where k is the projectile momentum and dσ_i/dΩ is the differential cross section for scattering from the i-th target particle.
- The final transport equation contains a factor 1/(mk) in the collision integral, where m is the projectile mass and k its momentum.

## Context

This work extends the complete but intricate treatment of Thies (1990s), who derived the Wigner representation from Watson's multiple-scattering theory in full generality, by providing a simpler pedagogical derivation that focuses only on the lowest-order limit. The approach is rooted in the Watson multiple-scattering series (Goldberger and Watson, 1964) and the Glauber series (Glauber, 1959), and it enables a transparent understanding of the approximations, extreme high energy, no correlations, large volume, required to reach the classical Boltzmann-Vlasov equation from a nonrelativistic quantum many-body formulation.
