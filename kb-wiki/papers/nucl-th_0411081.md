# Nuclear structure of the exotic mass region along the rp process path

> Isomeric states in the nuclei along the rapid proton capture process path are studied by the projected shell model. Emphasis is given to two waiting point nuclei 68Se and 72Kr that are characterized by shape coexistence. Energy surface calculations indicate that the ground state of these nuclei corresponds to an oblate-deformed minimum, while the lowest state at the prolate-deformed minimum can be considered as a shape isomer. The impact of these isomer states on isotopic abundance in x-ray bursts is studied in a multi-mass-zone x-ray burst model by assuming an upper-lower limit approach.

## Key claim

The projected shell model predicts that the ground states of the waiting-point nuclei ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr are oblate-deformed, with a prolate-deformed minimum lying 1.1 MeV (⁶⁸Se) and 0.7 MeV (⁷²Kr) higher in excitation that can be considered a shape isomer; however, the impact of these isomer states on isotopic abundance in x-ray bursts is studied only through an upper-lower limit approach, and the authors note that the actual effect on nucleosynthesis is "relatively modest" under these simplified assumptions.

## Method

The authors performed projected shell model calculations of total energy surfaces as a function of the quadrupole deformation parameter ε₂ for ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr, using the same configuration space and Hamiltonian interaction strengths as in previous work by Sun (2004). They then computed energy levels by exact diagonalization and compared them with available experimental data. Finally, they incorporated the isomer states into a multi-mass-zone x-ray burst model by assuming two extreme cases: neglecting any isomer contribution, and assuming full reaction flow through the isomeric states instead of the ground states.

## Key numbers

- The oblate ground-state minimum for both ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr occurs at ε₂ ≈ -0.25, moving to ε₂ ≈ -0.3 with increasing spin.
- The prolate minimum (ε₂ ≈ 0.4) lies 1.1 MeV above the ground state in ⁶⁸Se and 0.7 MeV above in ⁷²Kr.
- The experimentally observed shape isomer in ⁷²Kr has excitation energy 671 keV and half-life τ = 38 ± 3 ns (Bouchez et al., 2003).
- At the peak x-ray burst temperature of about 1.5 GK, thermal population probabilities for the isomer states are ≤ 0.02% for ⁶⁸Se (1.1 MeV) and ≤ 0.5% for ⁷²Kr (0.67 MeV).
- The Q-values for proton capture feeding the isomers are ≈ 3.19 MeV for ⁶⁷As(p,γ)⁶⁸Se* and ≈ 4.1 MeV for ⁷¹Br(p,γ)⁷²Kr*.
- Hauser-Feshbach estimates give proton-capture lifetimes in the range ≈ 100 ns to 10 μs.
- Estimated lifetimes for ⁶⁹Br and ⁷³Rb are less than 24 ns and 30 ns, respectively (Pfaff et al., 1994).

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model calculations of Sun (2004) for the A ≈ 70–80 mass region to specifically address shape coexistence in the rp-process waiting-point nuclei ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr. The experimental observation of a 671 keV shape isomer in ⁷²Kr by Bouchez et al. (2003) motivated the prediction of a similar isomer in ⁶⁸Se, with analogous calculations also performed by Kaneko et al. (2004). The study enables future experimental searches for the predicted ⁶⁸Se isomer and motivates more detailed measurements of γ-decay patterns and lifetimes to replace the simplified upper-lower limit approach used in the x-ray burst model.
