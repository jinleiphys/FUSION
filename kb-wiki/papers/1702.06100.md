# Off-shell persistence of composite pions and kaons

> In order for a Sullivan-like process to provide reliable access to a meson target as $t$ becomes spacelike, the pole associated with that meson should remain the dominant feature of the quark-antiquark scattering matrix and the wave function describing the related correlation must evolve slowly and smoothly. Using continuum methods for the strong-interaction bound-state problem, we explore and delineate the circumstances under which these conditions are satisfied: for the pion, this requires $-t \lesssim 0.6\,$GeV$^2$, whereas $-t\lesssim 0.9\,$GeV$^2$ will suffice for the kaon. These results should prove useful in evaluating the potential of numerous experiments at existing and proposed facilities.

## Key claim

For a Sullivan-like process to provide reliable access to a meson target as the momentum transfer becomes spacelike, the meson pole must remain the dominant feature of the quark-antiquark scattering matrix and the wave function must evolve slowly; for the pion this requires -t less than or approximately equal to 0.6 GeV^2, whereas for the kaon -t less than or approximately equal to 0.9 GeV^2 will suffice.

## Method

The authors used continuum methods for the strong-interaction bound-state problem, specifically a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the coupled Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. They introduced a virtuality eigenvalue lambda(v) into the inhomogeneous pseudoscalar Bethe-Salpeter equation to measure deviations induced by nonzero pion virtuality, and solved for the dressed quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes at various virtualities. The analysis was extended to the kaon by repeating the procedure for a fictitious s + anti-s pseudoscalar bound-state and interpolating to the kaon mass.

## Key numbers

- For the pion, the condition for a valid target is -t less than or approximately equal to 0.6 GeV^2, corresponding to virtuality v_S approximately equal to 31 (in units of m_pi^2).
- For the kaon, the condition is -t less than or approximately equal to 0.9 GeV^2.
- The on-shell pion mass computed in the framework is m_pi = 0.134 GeV, and the decay constant is f_pi = 0.093 GeV.
- The virtuality eigenvalue follows lambda(v) = 1 + 0.016 v for v less than or approximately equal to 45.
- The quark-antiquark core density at zero virtuality is kappa_pi^zeta(0) = (0.28 GeV)^3, and grows as kappa_pi^zeta(v) approximately equal to kappa_pi^zeta(0)[1 + 0.032 v] on v less than or approximately equal to v_S.
- The inferred PDA exponent at v=0 is p(0)=0.29, and at v=31 is p(31)=0.105.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sullivan processes first suggested by Sullivan (1971) and used in experiments by Volmer (2000), Horn (2006), Tadevosyan (2007), and Blok (2008) to access the pion's elastic form factor and parton distribution functions. The results provide quantitative boundaries for when off-shell pions and kaons can serve as valid targets in experiments at existing and proposed facilities, and should prove useful in planning experiments and evaluating their potential.
