# In-Medium Effective Pion Mass from Heavy-Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, we have calculated all the diagrams up to two-loop order which contribute to the S-wave pion self-energy in symmetric nuclear matter. Some subtleties related to the definition of pion fields are discussed. The in-medium pion mass is turned out to be increased by only (5 - 6) per cents in normal nuclear matter density, without any off-shell ambiguity.

# In-Medium Effective Pion Mass from Heavy-Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The in-medium pion mass in symmetric nuclear matter increases by only 5–6% at normal nuclear matter density, but this result is obtained only when using a "detailed" power-counting scheme that treats the mass shift as order Q^5, thereby eliminating off-shell ambiguities; a naive scheme that solves the full propagator equation yields much larger increases (12–32%) and suffers from strong dependence on the definition of the pion field.

## Method
The authors used heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to compute all diagrams up to two-loop order (order Q^7) plus the one-body order Q^8 contribution to the S-wave pion self-energy in symmetric nuclear matter, employing the Fermi gas approximation for nuclear matter. They evaluated the in-medium pion mass by two procedures: (1) solving the full propagator equation m*^2 = m_pi^2 + Pi(m*^2) non-perturbatively, and (2) expanding the self-energy consistently with a detailed power counting that treats the mass gap (m*^2 - m_pi^2) as order Q^5, which removes dependence on the off-shell parameter zeta.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear matter density (rho_0), the ratio m_pi*/m_pi is 1.20–1.32 with the canonical pion field (zeta = 4/3) and 1.12–1.15 with the background field method (zeta = 4) in the naive scheme.
- Using the detailed power-counting scheme, the in-medium pion mass increases by only 5–6% at normal nuclear matter density, with negligible dependence on the parameter set of low-energy constants.
- The leading non-vanishing contribution to the pion self-energy is order Q^5, and the order Q^7 contribution is zero.
- The low-energy constants used are (c1, c2, c3) = (-1.27 ± 0.12, 3.23 ± 0.19, -5.93 ± 0.08) GeV^{-1} (Fit 1), (-1.47 ± 0.09, 3.21 ± 0.11, -6.00 ± 0.03) GeV^{-1} (Fit 2), and (-1.53 ± 0.18, 3.22 ± 0.25, -6.19 ± 0.09) GeV^{-1} (Fit 3).

## Context
This work extends earlier phenomenological studies of the S-wave pion-nucleus optical potential, which gave widely varying results: Weise and Weise (1997) found a 7% increase in the effective pion mass, while Friedman and Gal (1999) found a 20% increase. The authors' result of a 5–6% increase supports the smaller value of Weise and Weise (1997). The paper also discusses the off-shell ambiguity in defining the in-medium pion mass, noting that the effective mass defined by the propagator pole is not a physical observable independent of the pion field definition. The authors propose a consistent power-counting scheme to resolve this ambiguity, and note that a complete calculation up to order Q^8 (including three-body contributions) would be needed for higher densities. A similar but more extensive work by Kaiser and Weise (2001) appeared concurrently, covering pions and kaons in isospin-asymmetric matter but using the simpler prescription m*^2 = m_pi^2 + Pi(m_pi^2).
