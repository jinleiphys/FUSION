# Transverse Momentum Broadening of a Jet in Quark-Gluon Plasma: An Open Quantum System EFT

> We utilize the technology of open quantum systems in conjunction with the recently developed effective field theory for forward scattering to address the question of massless jet propagation through a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma in thermal equilibrium. We discuss various possible hierarchies of scales that may appear in this problem, by comparing thermal scales of the plasma with relevant scales in the effective field theory. Starting from the Lindblad equation, we derive and solve a master equation for the transverse momentum distribution of a massless quark jet, at leading orders both in the strong coupling and in the power counting of the effective field theory. Markovian approximation is justified in the weak coupling limit. Using the solution to the master equation, we study the transverse momentum broadening of a jet as a function of the plasma temperature and the time of propagation. We discuss the physical origin of infrared sensitivity that arises in the solution and a way to handle it in the effective field theory formulation. We suspect that the final measurement constraint can only cut-off leading infrared singularities and the solution to the Markovian master equation resums a logarithmic series. This work is a stepping stone towards understanding jet quenching and jet substructure observables on both light and heavy quark jets as probes of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors derive and solve a master equation for the transverse momentum distribution of a massless quark jet propagating through a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma, using open quantum systems combined with an effective field theory for forward scattering, but note that the Markovian approximation is only justified in the weak coupling limit and that the solution to the master equation resums a logarithmic series rather than providing a fully infrared-safe result.

## Method

The authors treat the jet as an open quantum system interacting with a thermal quark-gluon plasma bath, starting from the Lindblad equation. They derive a master equation for the jet density matrix at leading order in the strong coupling and in the power counting of the forward-scattering effective field theory (SCET with Glauber modes), where the jet is described by collinear modes and the medium by soft modes. The master equation is solved analytically to obtain the transverse momentum distribution of the jet as a function of plasma temperature and propagation time, with the Glauber exchange mediating the forward scattering between collinear jet partons and soft medium partons.

## Key numbers

* The plasma temperature range relevant for heavy ion collisions is roughly 150-500 MeV.
* The Debye mass \(m_D\) is of order \(gT\) in perturbation theory, where \(g\) is the strong coupling at the scale \(T\).
* The jet energy \(Q\) is assumed to be much larger than all other scales, with the power counting parameter \(\lambda \sim \theta \ll 1\) where \(\theta \sim Q_{\perp}/Q\).
* The smallest transverse momentum exchanged is effectively cut off by the Debye mass \(m_D\).

## Context

This work extends the forward-scattering effective field theory developed by Rothstein (2016) and combines it with open quantum system formalism previously applied to quarkonium in-medium dynamics by Brambilla (2016, 2017, 2019) and Yao (2018). It provides a stepping stone toward computing jet quenching and jet substructure observables for both light and heavy quark jets as probes of the quark-gluon plasma, and enables future inclusion of medium-induced radiation and nonperturbative effects.
