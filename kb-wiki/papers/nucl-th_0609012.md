# Quark Matter in QC(2)D

> Results are presented from a numerical study of lattice QCD with gauge group SU(2) and two flavors of Wilson fermion at non-zero quark chemical potential mu >> T. Studies of the equation of state, the superfluid condensate, and the Polyakov line all suggest that in addition to the low density phase of Bose-condensed diquark baryons, there is a deconfined phase at higher quark density in which quarks form a degenerate system, whose Fermi surface is only mildly disrupted by Cooper pair condensation.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice evidence that Two Color QCD (QC2D) at low temperature and high quark chemical potential exhibits a deconfining transition from a Bose-Einstein condensate of tightly-bound diquarks to a degenerate quark system with a Fermi surface only mildly disrupted by Cooper pair condensation, analogous to the BEC/BCS crossover.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge theory with two flavors of Wilson fermion at non-zero quark chemical potential on an 8^3 x 16 lattice, using a standard Wilson gauge action at beta=1.7 and kappa=0.178. A diquark source term with coupling j=0.04 (with some points at j=0.02 and 0.06) was included to enable ergodic hybrid Monte Carlo sampling and direct measurement of the superfluid condensate. Thermodynamic observables including quark density, pressure, and energy density were computed from local operators and normalized to free-field lattice results.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.220 fm from static quark potential studies at mu=0
- Pion mass m_pi a = 0.79(1) and pion-to-rho mass ratio m_pi/m_rho = 0.80(1) at mu=0
- Onset of baryonic matter expected at mu_o a approximately 0.4, with quark density and pressure beginning to rise from zero at mu a approximately 0.3
- By mu a approximately 0.5, both quark density and pressure scale with mu in general accordance with free-field predictions but at approximately twice the expected value
- Deconfining transition and BCS-like pairing regime indicated at mu a approximately 0.6-0.65, where the superfluid condensate becomes proportional to mu^2 and the Polyakov line rises from zero
- Quark energy density shows a pronounced minimum at mu a approximately 0.8

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory predictions for QC2D by Kogut et al. (2000) and the model equation of state by Hands et al. (2006) to a full lattice simulation, testing whether the predicted first-order deconfining transition from BEC to quark matter at mu_d approximately 2.3 mu_o holds in a systematic calculation. The results suggest a BEC/BCS crossover analogous to that studied in strongly-correlated electron and cold atom systems, and the authors note that while QC2D models nuclear matter unrealistically, its description of quark matter may share features with real QCD. The study enables future work on the hadron spectrum, finer lattice spacings, and the gluon propagator in this system.
