# Study of the $pd\to ^3\textrm{He} K^+K^-$ and $pd\to ^3\textrm{He} ϕ$ reactions close to threshold

> Two--kaon production in proton--deuteron collisions has been studied at three energies close to threshold using a calibrated magnetic spectrograph to measure the final $^3$He and a vertex detector to measure the $K^+K^-$ pair. Differential and total cross sections are presented for the production of $ϕ$--mesons, decaying through $ϕ\to K^+K^-$, as well as for prompt $K^+K^-$ production. The prompt production seems to follow phase space in both its differential distributions and in its energy dependence. The amplitude for the $pd\to ^3${He}$ ϕ$ reaction varies little for excess energies below 22 MeV and the value is consistent with that obtained from a threshold measurement. The angular distribution of the $K^+K^-$ decay pair shows that near threshold the $ϕ$--mesons are dominantly produced with polarization $m=0$ along the initial proton direction. No conclusive evidence for $f_0(980)$ production is found in the data.

## Key claim

The amplitude for the $pd\to ^3\textrm{He}\,\phi$ reaction is nearly constant for excess energies below 22 MeV, and the value is consistent with a prior threshold measurement; however, the prompt $K^+K^-$ production appears to follow phase space in both its differential distributions and energy dependence, with no conclusive evidence for $f_0(980)$ production.

## Method

The experiment used the MOMO facility at the COSY accelerator, with a magnetic spectrograph (Big Karl) to detect the recoiling $^3$He and a vertex detector of scintillating fibers and a hodoscope to measure the $K^+K^-$ pair in coincidence. Data were taken at three beam momenta corresponding to overall excess energies $\varepsilon_{KK}=35.1$, 40.6, and 55.2 MeV, and the $K^+K^-$ invariant mass distribution was fitted with a phase-space background plus a Breit-Wigner resonance for the $\phi$ meson (natural width 4.2 MeV/c$^2$) to separate prompt and $\phi$ production.

## Key numbers

- Total prompt $K^+K^-$ cross sections: $\sigma_{KK}=6.4\pm0.5$ nb at $\varepsilon_{KK}=35.1\pm0.5$ MeV; $8.1\pm0.5$ nb at $\varepsilon_{KK}=40.6\pm0.5$ MeV; $15.8\pm1.0$ nb at $\varepsilon_{KK}=55.2\pm0.8$ MeV.
- Total $\phi$ production cross sections (corrected for the 49.1% $K^+K^-$ branching ratio): $\sigma_{\phi}=2.0\pm0.4$ nb at $\varepsilon_{\phi}=3.0\pm0.5$ MeV; $3.0\pm0.6$ nb at $\varepsilon_{\phi}=8.5\pm0.5$ MeV; $6.4\pm1.8$ nb at $\varepsilon_{\phi}=23.1\pm0.8$ MeV.
- Square of the spin-averaged $pd\to ^3\textrm{He}\,\phi$ amplitude: $|f|^2=3.0\pm0.6$ nb/sr at $\varepsilon_{\phi}=3.0$ MeV; $3.1\pm0.6$ nb/sr at $\varepsilon_{\phi}=8.5$ MeV; $3.4\pm1.0$ nb/sr at $\varepsilon_{\phi}=23.1$ MeV; the mean value across all energies is $(3.0\pm0.2)$ nb/sr.
- The $\phi$ meson is produced dominantly with polarization $m=0$: fitting the Jackson-angle distribution for $Q_{KK}>28$ MeV gives $\rho_{00}=0.82\pm0.05$ (statistical error only).
- Upper limits on $f_0(980)\to K^+K^-$ production are 6%, 7%, and 9% of the prompt $K^+K^-$ cross section at the three energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier inclusive measurements of meson production in $pd\to ^3\textrm{He}\,X^0$ reactions at SATURNE (Wurzinger et al., 1996) by adding exclusive detection of the $K^+K^-$ decay products, which allows separation of $\phi$ and prompt production and measurement of the $\phi$ polarization. The results are consistent with the threshold $pd\to ^3\textrm{He}\,\phi$ amplitude from Wurzinger (1996) and show a constant amplitude up to 22 MeV excess energy, in contrast to the $pd\to ^3\textrm{He}\,\omega$ reaction where the amplitude is suppressed at similar excess energies (Wurzinger et al., 1995). The observed $\phi$ polarization ($m=0$ dominance) provides a new constraint for reaction models, which currently underpredict the $\phi$ production rate by at least a factor of five (Faeldt et al., 1995).
