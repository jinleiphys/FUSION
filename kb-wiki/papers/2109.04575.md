# The momentum broadening of energetic partons in an anisotropic plasma

> The quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions is anisotropic throughout its evolution. This anisotropy changes the physics of jet-medium interaction, making it dependent on the momentum direction of the jet. In this paper we analyze transverse momentum broadening of a jet parton interacting with soft gluons in an anisotropic plasma. Our analysis equally applies to momentum broadening of quasiparticles in kinetic theory. We subtract contribution from instability modes in the deep infrared and discuss how our calculation should be complemented in that regime. The resulting anisotropic collision kernel for momentum broadening is qualitatively different from the equilibrium collision kernel and from the isotropic ansatz used in effective kinetic theory. Because of increased medium screening, there is substantially less transverse broadening at low and intermediate momenta.

## Key claim

The paper derives the collision kernel for transverse momentum broadening of a jet parton in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma, showing that the resulting kernel is qualitatively different from the equilibrium kernel and from the isotropic ansatz used in effective kinetic theory, with substantially less transverse broadening at low and intermediate momenta due to increased medium screening, but only after subtracting the contribution from instability modes in the deep infrared.

## Method

The authors calculate the symmetric (rr) component of the soft gluon propagator in an anisotropic plasma using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, where hard quasiparticles with a Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic momentum distribution source soft gluons. They then evaluate the collision kernel \(\mathcal{C}(\mathbf{q}_{\perp})\) from the rr correlator contracted with the jet parton's velocity vector, subtracting the contribution from unstable modes that would otherwise cause a divergence. The calculation is performed at leading order in perturbation theory for a small anisotropy parameter \(\xi\).

## Key numbers

* The collision kernel in thermal equilibrium is \(\mathcal{C}(\mathbf{q}_{\perp}) = g^2 C_F T \left(\frac{1}{\mathbf{q}_{\perp}^2} - \frac{1}{\mathbf{q}_{\perp}^2 + m_D^2} \right)\), where \(T\) is the temperature and \(m_D^2\) is the Debye mass.
* The rr correlator scales as \(D_{rr} \sim 1/g^3\) in the HTL regime due to high occupation density of soft gluons, while the retarded propagator scales as \(D_{\ret} \sim 1/g^2\).
* The condition for the time evolution of unstable modes to be slower than the typical time scale of momentum broadening requires a small anisotropy satisfying \(\xi^{3/2} \ll g^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous analytic calculations of the collision kernel for momentum broadening, which assumed a medium in local thermal equilibrium or with local isotropy in momentum space (Aurenche 2002, Caron-Huot 2008, Hauksson 2017). It contradicts the isotropic ansatz used in effective kinetic theory simulations (Kurkela 2015, Abraao York 2014) by showing that anisotropy introduces directional dependence in jet evolution. The results enable consistent treatment of momentum broadening in kinetic theory descriptions of the early stages of heavy-ion collisions after the glasma phase and before the hydrodynamic phase (Kurkela 2018), and are also relevant for calculating photon radiation through bremsstrahlung in an anisotropic medium (Arnold 2001, Hauksson 2017).
