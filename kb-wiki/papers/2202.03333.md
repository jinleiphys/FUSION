# Quantum Computing for Heavy Quarkonium Spectroscopy

> We report a first demonstration for the application of quantum computing to heavy quarkonium spectroscopy study. Based on a Cornell-potential model for the heavy quark and antiquark system, we show how this Hamiltonian problem can be formulated and solved with the VQE approach on the IBM cloud quantum computing platform. Errors due to a global depolarizing noise channel are corrected with a zero-noise extrapolation method, resulting in good agreement with the expected value. We also extend the calculation to excited states on a noiseless quantum simulator by orthogonalization with respect to the ground state.

## Key claim

This work demonstrates the first application of quantum computing to heavy quarkonium spectroscopy, using a variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) approach on the IBM cloud quantum computing platform; however, the ground state result of 502 ± 98 MeV from the noisy quantum computer with error mitigation shows substantial uncertainty compared to the exact diagonalization value of 492.6 MeV, and the excited state calculation was performed only on a noiseless quantum simulator rather than on actual quantum hardware.

## Method

The authors map the Cornell-potential Hamiltonian for a charm-anticharm system onto a 3-qubit quantum circuit using the Jordan-Wigner transformation with spherical quantum harmonic oscillator basis states. They apply the VQE algorithm with a unitary coupled cluster (UCC) ansatz to find the ground state on the IBMQ Athens quantum processor, correcting for a global depolarizing noise channel using a zero-noise extrapolation method that scales circuit depth by a factor λ. For the first excited state, they generalize VQE by orthogonalizing the variational ansatz with respect to the previously found ground state and perform the calculation on a noiseless QASM simulator.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy from noisy quantum computer with zero-noise extrapolation: 502 ± 98 MeV (2σ uncertainty band), compared to 493 ± 1 MeV from noiseless QASM simulator and 492.6 MeV from exact diagonalization of H₃
- Ground state wavefunction coefficients: −0.9858 for |001⟩, −0.1369 for |010⟩, −0.09722 for |100⟩
- First excited state (2S) energy from noiseless QASM simulator: 1212 ± 2 MeV, compared to 1210.8 MeV from exact diagonalization
- First excited state wavefunction coefficients: −0.1617 for |001⟩, 0.9298 for |010⟩, 0.3307 for |100⟩
- Cornell potential parameters: κ = 0.4063, √σ = 441.6 MeV, reduced mass μ = 637.5 MeV, oscillator frequency ω = 562.9 MeV
- Each expectation value measurement used 1.024 × 10⁶ circuit repetitions

## Context

This work extends the application of VQE methods, previously used for few-nucleon systems by Dumitrescu (2018) and Lu (2019), to the heavy quarkonium system described by the Cornell potential model. The excited state method based on orthogonalization generalizes the standard VQE approach and is supported by the Hylleraas-Undheim-MacDonald theorem. This demonstration opens the possibility of using quantum computing for more challenging hadron spectroscopy problems, such as exotic states, which the authors identify as future work.
