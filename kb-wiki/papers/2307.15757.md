# Chiral effects: new trends

> By chiral effects one understands manifestations of chiral gauge anomaly and of gravitational chiral anomaly in hydrodynamics. In recent two-three years our understanding of the chiral effects has considerably changed. Here we present mini-review of two topics. First, shift in understanding symmetry which underlies the chiral magnetic effect and, second, interpretation of the chiral kinematical effect uncovered recently.

# Chiral effects: new trends

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME), traditionally understood as a quantum loop effect rooted in chiral gauge anomaly, may instead originate from diffeomorphism symmetry of ideal fluid, with the chiral symmetry effectively embedded into a larger symmetry; the chiral kinematical effect (KVE) is interpreted via the Unruh effect, where acceleration alone determines the sample of particles, not rotation.

## Method

The authors present a mini-review of two recent developments: (1) a shift in understanding the symmetry underlying the chiral magnetic effect, showing that the conserved helicity current of ideal fluid arises from diffeomorphism symmetry rather than chiral symmetry, and that non-relativistic anomalies matching the UV anomalies can be generated by replacing ordinary derivatives with covariant derivatives; (2) an interpretation of the chiral kinematical effect, where in the absence of curvature the axial current is fixed uniquely in terms of acceleration and vorticity 4-vectors, and the KVE is linked to the Unruh effect and the equivalence principle. The duality between statistical and gravitational approaches is examined, showing that evaluation of kinematical effects can be rewritten as infrared-sensitive regularization of the gravitational chiral anomaly via acceleration.

## Key numbers

- The gauge anomaly divergence is given by ∂_α J^α_5 = e^2 C_5^{el} · E · B, where C_5^{el} is a constant depending on fundamental constituents.
- The chiral magnetic effect current is J^{el} = e^2 C_5^{el} · μ_5 B, with μ_5 the axial chemical potential.
- The chiral vortical effect current is J_{5,CVE} = μ^2_{el} C_5^{el} Ω, existing in absence of E and B.
- For spin-1/2 fermions, the kinematical axial current is J_5^{α,kinematic} = -(1/(24π^2)) (3 a_μ^2 + w_μ^2) w^α, where a_μ is acceleration and w_μ is vorticity.
- The Unruh temperature is T_Unruh = a/(2π), where a is acceleration.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of chiral effects in hydrodynamics, building on prior developments by Avdoshkin and Zakharov regarding conservation of the CVE current in ideal fluid, and by Jackiw on fluid helicity conservation. It also connects to the work of Abanov, Abanov, and Mitra on non-relativistic analogs of chiral anomalies, and to Mitkin on frame-dependent 4-volume effects. The kinematical vortical effect was introduced by Prokhorov, Teryaev, and Zakharov (2023), and the duality between statistical and gravitational approaches follows Becattini (2012) and Prokhorov, Teryaev, and Zakharov (2023). The paper enables future investigation into whether acceleration-based regularization of gravitational chiral anomaly can be developed into a novel type of infrared-sensitive regularization scheme.
