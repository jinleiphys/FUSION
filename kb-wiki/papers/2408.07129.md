# Are in-medium quark-gluon showers strongly coupled? Results in the large-$N_f$ limit

> Inside a medium, showers originating from a very high energy particle develop via medium-induced splitting processes such as bremsstrahlung and pair production. During shower development, two consecutive splittings sometimes overlap quantum mechanically, so that they cannot be treated independently. Some of these effects can be absorbed into an effective value of a medium parameter known as $\hat q$. Previous calculations (with certain simplifying assumptions) have found that, after adjusting the value of $\hat q$, the leftover effect of overlapping splittings is quite small for purely gluonic large-$N_c$ showers but is very much larger for large-$N_f$ QED showers, at comparable values of $Nα$. Here, by investigating the same problem for QCD with *quarks* in the large-$N_f$ limit of many quark flavors, we make a first study of whether the small effect in purely gluonic showers (i) was merely an accident or (ii) is more broadly characteristic of such overlap effects in QCD. We also offer a qualitative explanation for the large size of the effect in QED vs. QCD.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the surprisingly small overlap effects from overlapping formation times in purely gluonic large-\(N_c\) QCD showers are an accidental cancellation or a broader characteristic of QCD, by studying QCD with many quark flavors in the large-\(N_f\) limit (\(N_f \gg N_c \gg 1\)) and finding that the overlap corrections remain very small (order \(1\% \times N_f \alpha_s\)) compared to the large effects in large-\(N_f\) QED (order \(100\% \times N_f \alpha\)).

## Method

The authors compute next-to-leading-order (NLO) rates for overlapping formation-time effects in large-\(N_f\) QCD showers by adapting existing large-\(N_f\) QED formulas, using the \(\hat q\) approximation for multiple scattering in a static, homogeneous medium. They then integrate these rates into net splitting rates \([d\Gamma/dx]^{\text{net}}\) and use a moment formalism to calculate the overlap corrections to the ratio \(\sigma / l_{\text{stop}}\) (width over stopping length) for energy and initial-flavor deposition distributions, for both gluon-initiated and quark-initiated showers.

## Key numbers

- For energy deposition from a gluon-initiated shower in large-\(N_f\) QCD (\(N_f \gg N_c \gg 1\)): overlap correction to \(\sigma / l_{\text{stop}}\) is \(-0.4\% \times N_f \alpha_s\) (compared to \(+1.0\% \times N_c \alpha_s\) for purely gluonic large-\(N_c\) QCD and \(+99\% \times N_f \alpha\) for large-\(N_f\) QED).
- For energy deposition from a quark-initiated shower in large-\(N_f\) QCD: overlap correction is \(-0.5\% \times N_f \alpha_s\) (compared to \(+113\% \times N_f \alpha\) for large-\(N_f\) QED).
- For initial-flavor deposition from a quark-initiated shower in large-\(N_f\) QCD: overlap correction is \(+1.1\% \times N_f \alpha_s\) (compared to \(-85\% \times N_f \alpha\) for large-\(N_f\) QED).
- For fermion number deposition from a quark-initiated shower in large-\(N_f\) QCD: the distribution is infrared-unsafe (no numerical result given).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Arnold, Elgedawy, and Iqbal (2023) on purely gluonic large-\(N_c\) showers and by the same authors (2022) on large-\(N_f\) QED showers, which found dramatically different overlap effect sizes. The results show that the small QCD overlap effects are not an accident of the purely gluonic case, and the paper provides a qualitative explanation for the QED vs. QCD difference based on how neutral photons in QED interact less directly with the medium than colored gluons in QCD, making pair production overlap effects much larger in QED. The findings enable future work on whether the \(\hat q\) renormalization from overlap effects is universal across different observables.
