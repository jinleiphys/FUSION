# Relativistic Model of Triquark Structure

> At this point it is still unclear whether pentaquarks exist. While they have be seen in some experiments there are many experiments in which they are not found. On the assumption that pentaquarks exist, several authors have studied the properties of pentaquarks. One description considered is that of pentaquarks which consist of a diquark coupled to a triquark. There is a quite extensive literature concerning the properties of diquarks and their importance in the description of the nucleon has been considered by several authors. On the other hand, there is little work reported concerning the description of triquarks. In the present work we study a model for the triquark in which it is composed of a component which contains a quark coupled to a scalar diquark and another two components in which there is a quark coupled to a kaon. We solve for the wave function of the triquark and obtain a mass for the triquark of 0.81 GeV which is quite close to the value of 0.80 GeV obtained in a QCD sum rule study of triquark properties.

## Key claim

On the assumption that pentaquarks exist, the authors construct a relativistic model of a triquark (a bound state of a quark and a diquark or a quark and a kaon) and obtain a triquark mass of 0.81 GeV, close to the 0.80 GeV value from a QCD sum rule study, but note that the triquark mass is very close to the threshold for decay into a diquark and a strange quark or into a quark and a kaon.

## Method

The authors model the triquark as having three components: a quark coupled to a scalar diquark, a u quark coupled to a K0 meson, and a d quark coupled to a K+ meson. They use a quark propagator that has no on-mass-shell poles (obtained from quark propagation in a gluon condensate) to avoid the threshold problem, and solve coupled integral equations for the triquark vertex functions in momentum space with the kaon and diquark placed on mass shell. The equations are solved for the bound-state eigenvalue P0 with the triquark at rest, using form factors at the kaon and diquark vertices and coupling constants obtained from a normalization condition.

## Key numbers

- Triquark mass eigenvalue: P0 = 0.81 GeV, found using coupling constants g1 = g2 = 11.21, form factor parameter alpha = 0.2, and momentum cutoff kmax = 0.6 GeV.
- The result is compared to a QCD sum rule study that obtained a triquark mass of 0.80 GeV for a udsbar structure, with an additional state at 0.90 GeV.
- Constituent quark masses used: 350 MeV for up and down quarks, 450 MeV for the strange quark; scalar diquark mass taken as 400 MeV; kaon mass taken as 495 MeV.
- The momentum-dependent mass function uses a parameter c = 0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the diquark-triquark model of pentaquarks developed by Karliner and Lipkin (2003) and used previously by the authors in studies of pentaquark widths. It provides a relativistic calculation of triquark structure, complementing the QCD sum rule study of triquark properties by another group (2004). The model enables more detailed descriptions of pentaquark structure in the diquark-triquark framework, though the authors note that whether pentaquarks exist remains experimentally uncertain due to contradictory results.
