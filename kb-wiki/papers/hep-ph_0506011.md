# Strangeness and the discovery of quark-gluon plasma

> Strangeness flavor yield s and the entropy yield S are the observables of the deconfined quark-gluon state of matter which can be studied in the entire available experimental energy range at AGS, SPS, RHIC, and, in near future, at the LHC energy range. We present here a comprehensive analysis of strange, soft hadron production as function of energy and reaction volume. We discuss the physical properties of the final state and argue how evidence about the primordial QGP emerges.

## Key claim

Strangeness flavor yield and entropy yield are the two elementary observables that can reveal the deconfined quark-gluon plasma state across the entire experimental energy range from AGS to LHC, but the authors hedge that there is no valid first-principles answer available today for whether the color frozen nuclear phase can actually melt and turn into QGP in the short timescale of heavy ion collisions, nor will a first-principles simulation of the dynamic heavy ion environment become available in the foreseeable future.

## Method

The authors employ the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) to analyze strange, soft hadron production as a function of reaction energy and reaction volume, using the public SHARE package to fit particle yields. They study 11 centrality bins at top RHIC energy (200 GeV Au-Au) using six particle yields (pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, anti-p) plus K*(892)/K- and phi/K- ratios, and assemble data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC experiments to study energy dependence. The model uses four chemical parameters (gamma_q, gamma_s, lambda_q, lambda_s) controlling absolute and relative chemical equilibrium of quarks, with strangeness conservation, charge-to-baryon ratio, and pion ratio constraints.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature at high energy/central collisions: T = 140 MeV (chemical non-equilibrium model); T = 155 +/- 8 MeV (chemical equilibrium model)
- Baryochemical potential at RHIC: mu_B = 25 +/- 1 MeV across 10 centrality bins
- Strangeness chemical potential at RHIC: mu_S = 5.5 +/- 0.5 MeV
- Lattice QCD chemical equilibrium result: T = 163 +/- 2 MeV when extrapolated to physical quark mass scale
- Hadron pressure at low reaction energy: P = 25 MeV/fm^3, increasing by factor 2-3 at higher energies
- Pressure-to-energy-density ratio: P/epsilon = 0.115 at low reaction energy rising to 0.165 at top available energy
- Entropy density jumps by factor 3-4 when crossing the energy threshold 6.26 GeV < sqrt(s_NN) < 7.61 GeV
- Energy and baryon number density jump by factor 2-3 across the same threshold

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies of strangeness as a QGP signature (Rafelski and Letessier book, 2005) and builds on the entropy enhancement observed at CERN-SPS (experimental heavy ion program, 2000). The analysis contradicts the higher hadronization temperature (T = 175 MeV) advocated by Braun-Munzinger at the same conference, arguing that dynamical effects from parton collective flow and quark chemical equilibration can reduce the effective hadronization temperature by up to 20 MeV. The results enable systematic mapping of the deconfinement phase boundary as a function of both reaction energy and reaction volume, with the "horn" in the K+/pi+ ratio serving as a key observable for identifying the energy threshold of QGP formation.
