# Early time behavior of spatial and momentum anisotropies in a kinetic approach to nuclear collisions

> We derive a general formula for the early time dependence of a phase space distribution evolving according to the kinetic Boltzmann equation. Assuming that the early evolution of the system created in high-energy nuclear collisions can be described by kinetic theory, we calculate the scaling behaviors for the onset of various characteristics of the transverse dynamics. In particular, we show that the scaling behavior of the anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$ at early times does not depend on the details of the collision kernel or the system composition, while at the same time it differs from the prediction of fluid dynamics.

## Key claim

The early-time scaling of anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\) in kinetic theory follows \(v_n(t) \propto t^{n+1}\), independent of the collision kernel details or system composition, but this scaling differs from the fluid-dynamical prediction of \(v_n(t) \propto t^n\). The result holds under the assumption that the early evolution of the system created in high-energy nuclear collisions can be described by kinetic theory.

## Method

The authors derive a general early-time Taylor expansion of the phase space distribution from the relativistic Boltzmann equation, expressing time derivatives in terms of spatial gradients and the collision kernel. They apply this expansion to a two-dimensional system of massless particles with elastic binary collisions, assuming an initial distribution isotropic in momentum but anisotropic in spatial geometry, and compute the leading contributions to the integrated anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\) and spatial eccentricities \(\epsilon_n^{\bf x}\) at early times.

## Key numbers

- For a free-streaming system, the spatial ellipticity \(\epsilon_2^{\bf x}(t)\) decreases quadratically with time: \(\epsilon_2^{\bf x}(t) \simeq \epsilon_2^{\bf x}(t_0)\big( 1 - \frac{\langle v_\perp^2\rangle_0}{\langle r_\perp^2\rangle_0} t^2\big)\) for \(t \ll \big(\langle r_\perp^2\rangle_0 / \langle v_\perp^2\rangle_0\big)^{1/2}\).
- In an interacting system, the anisotropic flow coefficient \(v_n(t)\) grows as \(t^{n+1}\) at early times: \(v_n(t) \sim \mathrm{Kn}^{-1} \big( \mathcal{K}^{(1)}_{n,n}\epsilon_n^{\bf x} + \sum_{k\geq 1} \mathcal{K}^{(1)}_{n,n-k,k}\epsilon_{n-k}^{\bf x}\epsilon_k^{\bf x} \big) t^{n+1}\), where \(\mathrm{Kn}^{-1}\) is the inverse Knudsen number.
- The leading contribution to \(v_n\) appears at order \(t^{n+1}\) in the expansion, with the first non-zero term arising from the \(n\)-th time derivative of the collision kernel.

## Context

This work extends earlier kinetic-theory studies of anisotropic flow in simplified geometries (e.g., Romatschke 2018, Kurkela 2018, Borghini 2018) by providing a general analytic formula for the early-time scaling of \(v_n\) that does not depend on the specific collision kernel or system composition. The result contrasts with fluid-dynamical calculations (e.g., Heinz 2002, Kolb 2002) which predict \(v_n(t) \propto t^n\), and it enables systematic comparison between kinetic and hydrodynamic descriptions of the pre-equilibrium stage in heavy-ion collisions. The paper also generalizes the known linear-in-time growth of transverse flow found by Vredevoogd (2008) to higher harmonic anisotropies.
