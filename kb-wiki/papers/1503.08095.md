# Neutrino-nucleus reactions and their role for supernova dynamics and nucleosynthesis

> The description of nuclear reactions induced by supernova neutrinos has witnessed significant progress during the recent years. At the energies and momentum transfers relevant for supernova neutrinos neutrino-nucleus cross sections are dominated by allowed transitions, however, often with non-negligible contributions from (first) forbidden transitions. For several nuclei allowed Gamow-Teller strength distributions could be derived from charge-exchange reactions and from inelastic electron scattering data. Importantly the diagonalization shell model has been proven to accurately describe these data and hence became the appropriate tool to calculate the allowed contributions to neutrino-nucleus cross sections for supernova neutrinos. Higher multipole contributions are usually calculated within the framework of the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation, which describes the total strength and the position of the giant resonances quite well. This manuscript reviews the recent progress achieved in calculating supernova-relevant neutrino-nucleus cross sections and discusses its verification by data. Moreover, the review summarizes also the impact which neutrino-nucleus reactions have on the dynamics of supernovae and on the associated nucleosynthesis. These include the absorption of neutrinos by nuclei (the inverse of nuclear electron capture which is the dominating weak-interaction process during collapse), inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering and nuclear de-excitation by neutrino-pair emission. We also discuss the role of neutrino-induced reactions for the recently discovered $νp$ process, for the r-process and for the neutrino process, for which neutrino-nucleus reactions have the largest impact. Finally, we briefly review neutrino-nucleus reactions important for the observation of supernova neutrinos by earthbound detectors. (Abridged)

## Key claim

This review paper argues that neutrino-nucleus cross sections at supernova-relevant energies (up to a few tens of MeV) are dominated by allowed Gamow-Teller transitions, with non-negligible contributions from first-forbidden transitions, and that the diagonalization shell model accurately describes the allowed contributions while the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) is the appropriate tool for higher multipole contributions, but the authors note that the exact mechanism of supernova explosions remains "still uncertain and controversial."

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for calculating neutrino-nucleus cross sections using perturbation theory, where the nuclear structure problem reduces to allowed and first-forbidden transitions. They describe how Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions are derived from charge-exchange reactions such as (p,n), (³He,t), (n,p), (d,²He), and (t,³He) at intermediate energies, and how shell model diagonalization reproduces these measured GT and M1 strength distributions. For first-forbidden (dipole) transitions, which require multi-shell studies, the authors describe the use of the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) to calculate the total strength and position of giant resonances, and they explain that neutrino-induced spallation reactions are treated as a two-step process: calculating the neutrino-induced excitation spectrum with shell model and QRPA, followed by determination of decay probabilities into particle or gamma channels using the statistical model.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino spectra from electron capture during collapse are centered around neutrino energies of order 10 MeV at densities of a few 10¹¹ g cm⁻³, shifting to E_ν ≈ 20 MeV at ρ > 10¹² g cm⁻³
- Screening effects reduce the neutrino spectra by about 2 MeV
- Neutrino trapping occurs at ρ_trap ≈ 4 × 10¹¹ g cm⁻³, with the main process being elastic scattering of neutrinos on nuclei
- Neutrinos with energies about 20 MeV have a mean free path of order 0.5 km at densities 10¹² g cm⁻³
- The diffusion time scale at ρ = 10¹² g cm⁻³ is longer than the competing collapse time scale
- Core bounce occurs at nuclear densities ρ_N = 2.5 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³
- About 99% of the gravitational binding energy released in a supernova is carried away by neutrinos
- The delayed neutrino-heating mechanism requires about 1% of the neutrino energy to be converted into nuclear kinetic energy

## Context

This review extends and updates the earlier review by Kolbe et al. (2003) on neutrino-nucleus reactions for supernova applications, focusing on research performed after that work. The paper discusses how modern shell model electron capture rates (LMSH) have replaced the earlier Independent Particle Model approach of Bruenn (1985) that neglected electron capture on nuclei with neutron numbers N>38, and how these improved rates show that capture on heavy nuclei dominates during the entire collapse, contrary to earlier assumptions. The review enables future work by providing a comprehensive framework for calculating neutrino-nucleus cross sections across the nuclear chart, which can be used in nucleosynthesis studies of the νp process, the r-process, and the neutrino process, as well as for interpreting signals from supernova neutrino detectors.
