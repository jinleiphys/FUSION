# Passage of charmed particles through the mixed phase in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We employ a modified cascade hydrodynamics code to simulate the phase transition of an expanding quark-gluon plasma and the passage of a charmed particle through it. When inside the plasma droplets, the charmed quark experiences drag and diffusion forces. When outside the plasma, the quark travels as a $D$ meson and experiences collisions with pions. Additional energy transfer takes place when the quark enters or leaves a droplet. We find that the transverse momentum of $D$ mesons provides a rough thermometer of the phase transition.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum of $D$ mesons can serve as a rough thermometer of the phase transition temperature in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, but the temperature scale contains significant uncertainties due to dynamical assumptions, chiefly the initial size of the plasma droplets.

## Method

The authors employed a modified cascade hydrodynamics code to simulate the phase transition of an expanding quark-gluon plasma and the passage of a charmed particle through it. Inside plasma droplets, the charmed quark experiences drag and diffusion forces modeled by a non-relativistic Langevin equation; outside the plasma, the quark travels as a $D$ meson and experiences collisions with pions. Additional energy transfer occurs when the quark enters or leaves a droplet via hadronization and re-ionization processes modeled with flux-tube fission.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperatures considered: $T^*=150$ MeV and $T^*=200$ MeV, with bag constant $B^{1/4}=208$ MeV and $278$ MeV respectively
- Initial droplet radii tested: 1.0, 1.5, and 2.0 fm, giving mixed-phase durations of 12, 22, and 32 fm/$c$ respectively (independent of transition temperature)
- For $T^*=150$ MeV, the thermal RMS $D$ momentum $p_D^T = 1010$ MeV; for $T^*=200$ MeV, $p_D^T = 1200$ MeV
- The RMS $p_\perp^D$ for $T^*=200$ MeV shows a systematic upward shift of 120 MeV compared to $T^*=150$ MeV for given parameters $r_0$ and $d\Gamma/d\ell$
- $D\pi$ scattering causes a systematic increase in $p_\perp^D$ of about 60 MeV at $T^*=150$ MeV
- Flux-tube fission rates used: $d\Gamma/d\ell = 0.5$ and $2.5$ fm$^{-2}$
- Energy loss from ionization followed by re-hadronization: $\Delta E \geq 370$ MeV
- $D\pi$ scattering cross-section: $\sigma_{D\pi}=9$ mb (from additive quark model)
- Probability of hadronization through light-quark binding at droplet surface: approximately 3%

## Context

This work extends the cascade hydrodynamics model of Bertsch et al. (1988) to include charmed particle transport through the mixed phase of a quark-gluon plasma undergoing a first-order phase transition. It builds on earlier theoretical analyses of charm drag in plasma by Svetitsky (1988) and others, and on studies of charm hadronization at phase boundaries by Svetitsky and Uziel (1995). The results enable experimental measurement of $D$ meson transverse momentum distributions as a diagnostic probe of the phase transition temperature, though the authors note that improvement requires a more detailed model of droplet formation in the phase transition.
