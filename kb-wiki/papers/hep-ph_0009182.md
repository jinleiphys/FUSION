# Fermion Damping Rate Effects in Cold Dense Matter

> We review the non-Fermi or marginal liquid behavior of a relativistic QED plasma. In this medium a quasiparticle has a damping rate that depends linearly on the distance between its energy and the Fermi surface. We stress that this dependence is due to the long-range character of the magnetic interactions in the medium. Finally, we study how the quark damping rate modifies the gap equation of color superconductivity, reducing the value of the gap at the Fermi surface.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a relativistic QED plasma, the long-range character of magnetic interactions (which are only weakly dynamically screened via Landau damping) causes the quasiparticle damping rate to depend linearly on the distance from the Fermi surface, violating Luttinger's theorem; this damping rate then reduces the value of the color-superconductivity gap at the Fermi surface when incorporated into the gap equation.

## Method

The author reviews the computation of the fermion damping rate from the imaginary part of the self-energy, showing that for relativistic QED plasmas it scales as Im Σ₊ ∼ (e²/24π) |E − μ|, in contrast to the quadratic dependence expected for short-range interactions. The real part of the self-energy and the wavefunction renormalization factor are then derived via dispersion relations. Finally, the quark damping rate is inserted into the gap equation for color superconductivity at weak coupling, modifying the integral by a factor (2/π) arctan(ε_q/Γ_q) that acts as an ultraviolet cutoff.

## Key numbers

- Damping rate scaling: Im Σ₊(E, p) ∼ (e²/24π) |E − μ|, where e is the electromagnetic coupling constant.
- Real part of self-energy: Re Σ₊(E, p) ∼ (e²/12π²) (E−μ) ln(M/|E−μ|) + O((E−μ)).
- Wavefunction renormalization: Z⁻¹ ∼ 1 − (e²/12π²) ln(M/|E−μ|), showing the fermion propagator vanishes as E → μ.
- Leading-order color-superconductivity gap without damping: φ₀ ∼ 2 (b₀/g⁵) μ exp(−3π²/(√2 g)), with b₀ = 256π⁴ (2/N_f)⁵/² b′₀ and b′₀ of order one.
- Gap with damping included: φ₀^{damp} ∼ 2 (b₀/g⁵) μ exp(−π/(2 ḡ_eff)), where ḡ_eff² = ḡ² (1 − 2 ḡ²) and ḡ = g/(3√2 π), showing a reduction of order g² relative to the leading-order value.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of non-Fermi liquid behavior in relativistic plasmas previously studied by Le Bellac and Manuel (1997, 2000), and applies it to color superconductivity in QCD at high density, building on the weak-coupling gap calculations by Son (1999), Schäfer (1999), Pisarski and Rischke (2000), Hong (2000), and Brown (2000). The inclusion of fermion damping introduces a physical cutoff in the gap equation, reducing the gap value and suggesting that the critical temperature for the superconducting transition, computed by Pisarski and Rischke (2000) and Brown (2000), should also be affected.
