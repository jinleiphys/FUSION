# The Nonclassical Diffusion Approximation to the Nonclassical Linear Boltzmann Equation

> We show that, by correctly selecting the probability distribution function $p(s)$ for a particle's distance-to-collision, the nonclassical diffusion equation can be represented exactly by the nonclassical linear Boltzmann equation for an infinite homogeneous medium. This choice of $p(s)$ preserves the $true$ mean-squared free path of the system, which sheds new light on the results obtained in previous work.

## Key claim
By correctly choosing the probability distribution function \(p(s)\) for a particle's distance-to-collision, the nonclassical diffusion equation can be represented exactly by the nonclassical linear Boltzmann equation for an infinite homogeneous medium, but only when the second moment of \(p(s)\) preserves the true mean-squared free path of the system; the first moment only approximates the true mean free path.

## Method
The author converts both the nonclassical linear Boltzmann equation (with isotropic scattering) and the nonclassical diffusion equation into integral equations for the scalar flux in an infinite homogeneous medium. By requiring the two integral equations to be identical, the author derives explicit expressions for \(p(s)\) and the corresponding path-length-dependent total cross section \(\Sigma_t(s)\) that make the nonclassical transport equation reproduce the nonclassical diffusion approximation.

## Key numbers
- The derived path length distribution is \(p(s) = \lambda^2 s e^{-\lambda s}\) with \(\lambda^2 = 6 / \langle s^2 \rangle\), where \(\langle s^2 \rangle\) is the true mean-squared free path.
- The corresponding total cross section is \(\Sigma_t(s) = \lambda^2 s / (1 + \lambda s)\).
- For the numerical example of a homogenized 3-D Pebble Bed reactor core (fuel pebble diameter \(d=1\), total cross section \(\Sigma_t=1\), scattering ratio \(c=0.99\), packing fraction 0.5934), the classically homogenized system has total cross section \(\overline{\Sigma}_t = 0.5934\) and the true mean-squared free path is numerically calculated to be \(\langle s^2 \rangle = 6.2898\).
- The first moment of the derived \(p(s)\) is \(\langle s \rangle = \sqrt{6 \langle s^2 \rangle} / 3\), which only approximates the true mean free path.
- The second moment of the derived \(p(s)\) exactly equals the true mean-squared free path \(\langle s^2 \rangle\).

## Context
This work extends the nonclassical linear Boltzmann equation developed by Larsen (2011), Frank (2010), and Vasques (2014) for particle transport in inhomogeneous random media with spatially correlated scattering centers, such as Pebble Bed reactor cores. It also builds on results by Siap (2015) showing that the nonclassical transport equation can reproduce classical diffusion approximations. The paper provides a deeper understanding of why the second moments of path-length distributions for classical diffusion approximations give the exact classical transport value \(2/\Sigma_t^2\), and enables the construction of simple \(p(s)\) and \(\Sigma_t(s)\) that approximate solutions of the nonclassical Boltzmann equation in diffusive systems using only the true mean-squared free path.
