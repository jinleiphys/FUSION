# Pairing transitions in the finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

> We formulate the finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory for spherical nuclei based on a point-coupling functional, with the Gogny or separable pairing force. Using the functional PC-PK1, the framework is applied to the study of pairing transitions in Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains. The separable pairing force reproduces the gaps calculated with the Gogny force not only at zero temperature, but also at finite temperatures. By performing a systematic calculation of the even-even Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes, it is found that the critical temperature for a pairing transition generally follows the rule $T_c =0.6 Δ_n(0)$, where $Δ_n(0)$ is the neutron pairing gap at zero temperature. This rule is further verified by adjusting the pairing gap at zero temperature with a strength parameter.

## Key claim

The critical temperature for the pairing transition in finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory generally follows the rule \(T_c = 0.6 \Delta_n(0)\), where \(\Delta_n(0)\) is the neutron pairing gap at zero temperature; this rule holds over a wide interval of pairing strength parameters and across several isotopic chains, though deviations occur near subshell closures (e.g., for very neutron-rich Sn isotopes).

## Method

The authors formulated the finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (FTRHB) theory for spherical nuclei using a point-coupling functional (PC-PK1) in the particle-hole channel, with either the Gogny force (D1S parametrization) or a separable pairing force in the particle-particle channel. The self-consistent FTRHB equations were solved in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis of 20 major shells, and the temperature dependence of pairing gaps, pairing energies, entropy, radii, and specific heat was computed for even-even Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes. The critical temperature was extracted as the temperature at which the pairing gap vanishes, and the relation \(T_c = 0.6 \Delta_n(0)\) was tested by varying the pairing strength parameter for \(^{124}\)Sn.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{124}\)Sn, the neutron pairing gap at zero temperature is \(\Delta_n(0) = 1.33\) MeV and the critical temperature is \(T_c = 0.8\) MeV, yielding \(T_c = 0.6 \Delta_n(0)\).
* The excitation energy of \(^{124}\)Sn reaches \(E^* = 42.9\) MeV at \(T = 2\) MeV.
* The neutron radius of \(^{124}\)Sn increases by 1% from zero temperature to \(T = 2\) MeV; the charge radius increases by 0.6% over the same range.
* For the Sn isotopic chain, the largest discrepancy between the calculated critical temperature and the empirical value \(0.6 \Delta_n(0)\) is 0.11 MeV for \(^{162}\)Sn.
* The separable pairing force parameters are \(G = 728\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(a = 0.644\) fm, determined by reproducing the Gogny force pairing gap in nuclear matter.
* The relation \(T_c = 0.6 \Delta(0)\) holds for zero-temperature pairing gaps up to approximately 2.5 MeV; above this value, deviations appear.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature BCS and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov studies of pairing transitions (e.g., Sano (1963), Goodman (1981, 1986)) to a covariant density functional framework with point-coupling interactions, specifically using the PC-PK1 functional. It confirms the linear relation between critical temperature and zero-temperature pairing gap found in earlier BCS work (\(T_c = 0.57 \Delta(0)\) for constant pairing force) but finds a slightly higher coefficient (0.6) in the Bogoliubov theory, attributed to additional correlations beyond time-reversed pairs. The formulation enables the future development of the finite-temperature relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (FTRQRPA) for modeling stellar weak-interaction processes (electron capture and beta decay) at temperatures below the pairing critical temperature.
