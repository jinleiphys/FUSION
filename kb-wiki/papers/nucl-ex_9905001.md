# Two-proton correlations from 158 AGeV Pb+Pb central collisions

> The two-proton correlation function at midrapidity from Pb+Pb central collisions at 158 AGeV has been measured by the NA49 experiment. The results are compared to model predictions from static thermal Gaussian proton source distributions and transport models RQMD and VENUS. An effective proton source size is determined by minimizing CHI-square/ndf between the correlation functions of the data and those calculated for the Gaussian sources, yielding 3.85 +-0.15(stat.) +0.60-0.25(syst.) fm. Both the RQMD and the VENUS model are consistent with the data within the error in the correlation peak region.

## Key claim

The NA49 collaboration reports the first measurement of the two-proton correlation function at midrapidity from Pb+Pb central collisions at 158 AGeV, extracting an effective proton source size of 3.85 fm. However, the extracted size is only an effective parameter that does not directly correspond to the full spatial extent of the proton source, as space-time-momentum correlations cause the correlation function to probe a smaller region than the overall source dimensions.

## Method

The NA49 experiment used two independent analyses (dE/dx identification in time-projection chambers and time-of-flight identification) to reconstruct proton pairs from the 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV. The two-proton correlation function was calculated as the ratio of the q_inv distribution of true proton pairs to that of mixed-event pairs, then corrected for contamination from weak decay protons (estimated at 25 ± 10% of the single-proton sample) and finite momentum resolution. The corrected correlation function was compared to calculations from static thermal Gaussian source distributions and from the transport models RQMD (version 2.3) and VENUS (version 4.12), using chi-squared per degree of freedom minimization over the range q_inv < 48 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Effective proton source size: sigma_eff = 3.85 ± 0.15 (stat.) +0.60/-0.25 (syst.) fm, extracted from chi-squared minimization between data and Gaussian source calculations
- Peak amplitude of the raw correlation function at q_inv ≈ 20 MeV/c: 1.14 ± 0.04 (statistical error only)
- Corrected peak height after accounting for 44% weak-decay pair contamination: 1.24 ± 0.07
- RQMD model effective source size: sigma_eff = 4.41 fm, with chi-squared/ndf = 1.53 (20% probability of consistency with data)
- VENUS model effective source size: sigma_eff = 3.55 fm, with chi-squared/ndf = 1.38 (14% probability of consistency with data)
- Combined sample contained about 10^5 pairs with q_inv < 120 MeV/c, 75% from the time-of-flight analysis
- Momentum resolution: 0.25% at momentum about 10 GeV/c; q_inv resolution: 2.5 MeV/c in the range q_inv < 120 MeV/c

## Context

This work provides the first measurement of the two-proton correlation function at midrapidity for high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, extending prior studies of pion source sizes via two-pion interferometry (NA49 1998) and lower-energy proton correlation measurements at GSI and AGS energies. The extracted effective source size of 3.85 fm corresponds to a uniform hard-sphere radius of 8.6 fm, larger than the colliding Pb nuclei, and the comparison with lower-energy results suggests that the two-proton correlation function is rather insensitive to bombarding energy over a wide range. The measurement enables future studies of baryon freeze-out conditions and source shapes, though the observed unexplained structure at q_inv ≈ 70 MeV/c indicates possible breakdowns of the standard Koonin-Pratt formalism assumptions such as the smoothness approximation (Pratt 1997) or independent emission.
