# Phase diagram of the two-fluid Lipkin model: a butterfly catastrophe

> Background: In the last few decades quantum phase transitions have been of great interest in Nuclear Physics. In this context, two-fluid algebraic models are ideal systems to study how the concept of quantum phase transition evolves when moving into more complex systems, but the number of publications along this line has been scarce up to now. Purpose: We intend to determine the phase diagram of a two-fluid Lipkin model, that resembles the nuclear proton-neutron interacting boson model Hamiltonian, using both numerical results and analytic tools, i.e., catastrophe theory, and to compare the mean-field results with exact diagonalizations for large systems. Method: The mean-field energy surface of a consistent-Q-like two-fluid Lipkin Hamiltonian is studied and compared with exact results coming from a direct diagonalization. The mean-field results are analyzed using the framework of catastrophe theory. Results: The phase diagram of the model is obtained and the order of the different phase-transition lines and surfaces is determined using a catastrophe theory analysis. Conclusions: There are two first order surfaces in the phase diagram, one separating the spherical and the deformed shapes, while the other separates two different deformed phases. A second order line, where the later surfaces merge, is found. This line finishes in a transition point with a divergence in the second order derivative of the energy that corresponds to a tricritical point in the language of the Ginzburg-Landau theory for phase transitions.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of the two-fluid Lipkin model contains two first-order phase transition surfaces and one second-order line, but the order of the transition along the vertical plane depends on the value of the asymmetry parameter: for values of y' less than or equal to 1 the line is second order, while for y' greater than 1 it becomes first order, with a tricritical point at the boundary.

## Method

The mean-field energy surface of a consistent-Q-like two-fluid Lipkin Hamiltonian (with an su(2)⊗su(2) algebraic structure) was derived from a product of two boson condensates and analyzed as a function of control parameters x, y1, and y2. Catastrophe theory was applied to classify the critical points of the energy surface by expanding around the most degenerate point and separating essential from non-essential variables. Exact diagonalizations with N1=N2=500 bosons were performed to compare with the mean-field results.

## Key numbers

- The second-order phase transition in the plane y'=0 (y1=y2=0) occurs at x_c = 4/5 = 0.8.
- For y1=y2=y=1 and y'=0, a first-order phase transition occurs at x_c = 5/6 ≈ 0.833, given by the formula x_c = (4+y^2)/(5+y^2).
- For the trajectory y1=1, y2=-1/2 (y=1/4, y'=3/4), a first-order phase transition is observed at x_c ≈ 0.805.
- In the vertical plane y=0 with y1=-y2=y'=1/2, a second-order phase transition occurs at x_c = 0.8.
- In the vertical plane y=0 with y1=-y2=y'=3/2, a first-order phase transition occurs at x_c ≈ 0.81.
- The boundary between first-order and second-order behavior in the vertical plane occurs at y' = 1.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quantum phase transitions in two-fluid algebraic models, particularly the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) studied by Arias (2004), Caprio (2005), and Cejnar and Iachello (2004). Unlike those IBM-2 studies where exact diagonalizations were limited to small boson numbers, the two-fluid Lipkin model allows exact calculations with much larger boson numbers (N1=N2=500), enabling a proper comparison with mean-field results. The phase diagram structure resembles that of the single-fluid Lipkin model in the symmetric plane (y1=y2) but reveals richer behavior including a tricritical point in the vertical plane, which corresponds to a butterfly catastrophe in the language of catastrophe theory.
