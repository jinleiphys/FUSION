# The role of the Euclidean signature in lattice calculations of quasi-distributions and other non-local matrix elements

> Lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) provides the only known systematic, nonperturbative method for first-principles calculations of nucleon structure. However, for quantities such as lightfront parton distribution functions (PDFs) and generalized parton distributions (GPDs), the restriction to Euclidean time prevents direct calculation of the desired observable. Recently, progress has been made in relating these quantities to matrix elements of spatially nonlocal, zero-time operators, referred to as quasidistributions. Even for these time-independent matrix elements, potential subtleties have been identified in the role of the Euclidean signature. In this work, we investigate the analytic behavior of spatially non-local correlation functions and demonstrate that the matrix elements obtained from Euclidean lattice QCD are identical to those obtained using the LSZ reduction formula in Minkowski space. After arguing the equivalence on general grounds, we also show that it holds in a perturbative calculation, where special care is needed to identify the lattice prediction. Finally we present a proof of the uniqueness of the matrix elements obtained from Minkowski and Euclidean correlation functions to all order in perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The matrix elements of spatially nonlocal, zero-time operators (such as those used for quasi-PDFs) obtained from Euclidean lattice QCD are identical to those obtained using the LSZ reduction formula in Minkowski space, despite potential subtleties in the analytic continuation of loop integrals that had suggested a possible discrepancy.

## Method

The authors first present a general argument that single-particle matrix elements of time-local operators carry no intrinsic Euclidean or Minkowski label, because neither the energy eigenstates nor the zero-time operator depend on the spacetime signature. They then demonstrate this equivalence explicitly in a perturbative scalar toy model, showing that the correct Euclidean extraction procedure (identifying the leading large-time behavior of a mixed time-momentum representation correlator) yields the same matrix element as the Minkowski LSZ reduction, resolving an apparent tension identified by Carlson et al. (2017). Finally, they present a proof of the uniqueness of these matrix elements to all orders in perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle states are normalized with the relativistic normalization factor $2 \omega_{\textbf P} (2 \pi)^3 \delta^3(\textbf P' - \textbf P)$, where $\omega_{\textbf P} = \sqrt{\textbf P^2 + \M_{\mathcal Q}^2}$ and $\M_{\mathcal Q}$ is the physical pole-mass.
- Finite-volume corrections to single-particle energies and matrix elements are of order $e^{- m_\pi L}$, which the authors take to be negligible.
- In the perturbative toy model, the difference between naive Euclidean and Minkowski contour integrals arises for $k_1^2 + k_2^2 < \mu^2 \equiv P_z^2 - (1-x)^2 P_z^2 + m^2 - m_\chi^2$, where a pole at $k_4 = i \kappa \equiv i \omega_{\textbf P} - i \omega_\chi$ separates the contours.

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Ji (2013) for extracting parton distribution functions from lattice QCD via quasi-PDFs, and directly addresses concerns raised by Carlson et al. (2017) about potential discrepancies between Euclidean and Minkowski treatments of the infrared behavior of quasi-PDF loop integrals. The authors resolve these concerns by showing that the correct Euclidean extraction procedure yields the same matrix element as the Minkowski calculation, supporting the viability of the quasi-PDF approach. This enables continued lattice calculations of nucleon structure using quasi-distributions, building on exploratory work by Alexandrou et al. (2015), Chen et al. (2016), and Zhang et al. (2017).
