# Predictions for isobaric collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 200 GeV from a multiphase transport model

> The isobaric collisions of $^{96}_{44}$Ru + $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr + $^{96}_{40}$Zr have recently been proposed to discern the charge separation signal of the chiral magnetic effect (CME). In this article, we employ the string melting version of a multiphase transport model to predict various charged-particle observables, including $dN/dη$, $p_T$ spectra, elliptic flow ($v_2$), and particularly possible CME signals in Ru + Ru and Zr + Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 200 GeV. Two sets of the nuclear structure parametrization have been explored, and the difference between the two isobaric collisions appears to be small, in terms of $dN/dη$, $p_T$ spectra, and $v_2$ for charged particles. We mimic the CME by introducing an initial charge separation that is proportional to the magnetic field produced in the collision, and study how the final-state interactions affect the CME observables. The relative difference in the CME signal between the two isobaric collisions is found to be robust, insensitive to the final-state interactions.

## Key claim

The relative difference in the chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV is robust and insensitive to final-state interactions, even though those final-state interactions suppress the absolute magnitude of the initial charge separation by roughly a factor of three.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV. They introduced an initial charge separation mimicking the CME by swapping the transverse momenta of a fraction of up and anti-up quarks (and down and anti-down quarks) moving in opposite directions perpendicular to the reaction plane, with the fraction proportional to the magnetic field strength and the nuclear size. The model included parton cascade via Zhang's parton cascade, hadronization via coalescence, and resonance decays, but hadronic rescatterings were turned off to preserve charge conservation.

## Key numbers

- The relative difference in the magnetic field between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is roughly 10% at large impact parameters, consistent with Ru having 10% more protons than Zr.
- The initial charge separation fraction was parameterized as f(%) = 1146.1 (eB_y / m_pi^2) A^{-4/3}, fitted to reproduce STAR data for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions.
- The final-state interactions suppress the initial charge separation by roughly a factor of 3 in both isobaric systems.
- The relative difference in the charge-dependent correlator Δγ between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is between 10-20% for the 20-70% centrality range when the CME is introduced.
- The relative difference in charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/dη between the two systems is at most 3% for the more deformed nuclear geometry setting (set 2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Deng 2016) that demonstrated isobaric collisions could disentangle CME signal from flow backgrounds when final-state interactions were ignored. It directly addresses the concern raised by Ma and Lin (2016) that final-state interactions significantly suppress the initial charge separation in Au+Au collisions. The results support the experimental proposal by Voloshin (2010) to use Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at RHIC to isolate the CME signal from elliptic-flow-driven backgrounds.
