# Half-vortex states in the rotating outer core of neutron stars

> We probe the superfluid-superconductor dynamics of the rotating outer core of neutron stars through half-vortex states. By means of a generalized hydrodynamic model, where proton and neutron fluids are coupled by both dynamic entrainment and Skyrme SLy4 nucleon-nucleon interactions, we analyze single flux tubes in the proton-superconductor component of the system that thread proton vortices located faraway from neutron vortices. It is shown how they give rise to hydrodynamic perturbations in the coexisting neutron superfluid, and its structure remains unaltered for varying rotation rates and magnetic fields within ranges of observational values.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that isolated proton-superconductor flux tubes (half-vortices) in the outer core of neutron stars produce only small hydrodynamic perturbations in the coexisting neutron superfluid, and that this structure remains unchanged for rotation rates between 10^2-10^3 Hz and magnetic fields between 10^12-10^15 G, contrary to what the abstract's phrasing "remains unaltered for varying rotation rates and magnetic fields" might suggest without emphasizing that these are observational ranges where the effects are negligible compared to nuclear-scale energies.

## Method

The authors solve generalized Ginzburg-Landau (Gross-Pitaevskii) equations for coupled neutron and proton order parameters, derived from an energy functional that includes Skyrme SLy4 nucleon-nucleon interactions and dynamic entrainment between the two fluids. Rotation and magnetic fields are introduced through minimal coupling in the kinetic momentum operators, and the resulting equations are solved numerically for straight vortex lines invariant along the z-coordinate, assuming the proton coherence length matches the proton Fermi wavelength at a nuclear density of 0.16 fm^-3.

## Key numbers

- Proton vortex core size (coherence length) ξ_p = 10.3 fm at nucleon density ρ = 0.16 fm^-3, where λ_Fp ≈ ξ_p
- Neutron Fermi wavelength λ_Fn = 3.8 fm at the same density, showing strong asymmetry (δ = 0.9)
- Bulk proton density ρ_p^0 = 7.66 × 10^-3 fm^-3 and bulk neutron density ρ_n^0 = 0.152 fm^-3 at ρ = 0.16 fm^-3
- Neutron density depletion at the proton vortex core is approximately 1% relative to bulk value
- London penetration depth Λ ≈ 74 fm at ρ = 0.16 fm^-3 (or ~50 fm at ρ = 0.24 fm^-3)
- Magnetic field peak at proton-vortex core B_v ≈ 4 × 10^15 G
- Proton fluid velocity estimate v_p ~ ℏ/(mξ_p) = 0.02 c
- Entrainment current density ratio j_np/j_p ~ -0.24 at ρ = 0.16 fm^-3
- Rotation length scale ℓ_Ω = 2.5 × 10^10 fm and magnetic length scale ℓ_B = 2.6 × 10^3 fm, both much larger than ξ_p
- Neutron intervortex distance d_n ~ 4 × 10^-4 cm for a 1 ms period, and proton intervortex distance d_p ~ 5 × 10^-10 cm for 10^12 G average field

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic description of the outer-core superfluid previously used by Gil (2021) for long-wavelength excitations, now applied to isolated vortex structures. It addresses a gap in the literature where studies of outer-core vortices have focused on collective effects and vortex lattices (Mendell 1991, 1998; Andersson 2005; Glampedakis 2011; Link 2012; Sourie 2020; Wood 2022), while single flux tube features in rotating superconductors were partially explored by Carter (2000) and Prix (2000). The results enable future microscopic characterization using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov models that include both fermionic and bosonic sectors, and may contribute to understanding neutron star thermal history and glitch dynamics.
