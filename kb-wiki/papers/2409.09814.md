# Fuzzy logic for reconstructing arbitrary moments of multiplicity distributions

> The Identity Method is a statistical technique developed to reconstruct moments of multiplicity distributions of particles produced in high-energy nuclear collisions. The method leverages principles from fuzzy logic, allowing for a more nuanced representation of particle identification by assigning degrees of membership to different particle types based on detector signals. In this contribution, a new framework, based on a multivariate moment generation function, is developed that allows the derivation of the formulas used in the Identity Method in a more robust way. Moreover, within the introduced framework, the Identity Method is easily extended to cope with arbitrarily higher-order moments. The techniques developed here offer significant potential for improving the accuracy of multiplicity distribution analyses in high-energy nuclear collisions. While the primary focus of the work presented is on applications in high-energy particle and nuclear physics, it can also be applied in other areas where signal identification is probabilistic and data are noisy, such as in medical imaging, remote sensing, and various other fields of experimental science.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a multivariate moment generating function for the W quantities used in the Identity Method, which allows the derivation of formulas for arbitrarily higher-order moments of multiplicity distributions without the computationally intensive use of the multinomial theorem; the method is presented as a more robust and scalable framework, but its validity for the fourth-order moments has only been verified using a simulation procedure reported in prior work (Rustamov 2012).

## Method

The author constructs a multivariate moment generating function \(M_W(t_1, t_2, ..., t_n)\) for the W quantities, defined as the sum of fuzzy membership weights \(\omega_j(x_i)\) for each particle type per event. This generating function is expressed in terms of the moment generating function of the true multiplicity distribution \(M_N\) and functions \(h_i\) that are cumulant generating functions for the \(\omega\) variables. By taking derivatives of \(M_W\) with respect to auxiliary variables \(t_j\) and evaluating at zero, the moments of the W distributions are related to the unknown moments of the multiplicity distributions through a set of linear equations, with explicit formulas derived up to fourth order.

## Context

This work extends the Identity Method, originally developed by Gazdzicki (2011) and Gorenstein (2011) for second-order moments and later extended to third-order by Rustamov (2012), by providing a unified generating-function framework that avoids the computationally intensive multinomial expansions used previously. The new framework enables the reconstruction of arbitrarily high-order moments of multiplicity distributions, which is crucial for studying higher-order cumulants of net charge distributions in heavy-ion collisions at facilities like the LHC, RHIC, and SPS, and for probing the QCD phase diagram. The approach is also applicable to other fields where signal identification is probabilistic, such as medical imaging and remote sensing.
