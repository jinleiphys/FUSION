# Resonances extracted in truncated partial-wave analysis are effective mixtures of angular momenta

> In truncated partial-wave analysis, one fits observables that are bilinear in the amplitudes rather than the amplitudes themselves. Truncation is therefore not merely a restriction of the amplitude basis, but of the bilinear interference terms admitted in the fit. As a result, the coefficients extracted in a truncated analysis are generally not projections of the coefficients of the full amplitude. Instead, they are determined by a coupled nonlinear fit in bilinear space and depend on combinations of the full coefficient set that contribute to the retained moments. We demonstrate this in a minimal scalar toy model, where a Hermitian bilinear generated by a Legendre expansion truncated at order 2 is approximated by one truncated at order 1. Even in this simplest case, the fitted low-order coefficients depend on bilinear combinations involving higher-order contributions of the original amplitude, providing a concrete mechanism for truncation-induced angular-momentum mixing. The implication is that quantities extracted in truncated partial-wave analyses that carry resonance information should not, in general, be identified with direct projections of the corresponding quantities of the exact infinite problem. They are truncation-dependent effective mixtures generated by the restricted bilinear fit. Although shown here for scalar scattering, the same algebraic mechanism applies to Legendre-moment analyses and to photoproduction observables. The statement is established at the level of fitted bilinear quantities and reconstructed truncated coefficients; the corresponding interpretation in terms of analytically continued resonance poles is not developed here.

# Resonances extracted in truncated partial-wave analysis are effective mixtures of angular momenta

## Key claim
In truncated partial-wave analysis (TPWA), the extracted coefficients that carry resonance information are not direct projections of the corresponding coefficients of the exact infinite problem; they are truncation-dependent effective mixtures generated by the restricted bilinear fit, because observables are bilinear in the amplitudes and truncation changes the set of interference terms admitted in the fit rather than merely restricting the amplitude basis.

## Method
The author formulates the truncation problem directly at the level of bilinear observables, showing that minimizing the Legendre least-squares error between the exact bilinear and the truncated bilinear reduces to a coupled nonlinear optimization problem in bilinear space. A minimal scalar toy model is constructed where a Hermitian bilinear generated by a Legendre expansion truncated at order 2 (with coefficients a0, a1, a2) is approximated by one truncated at order 1 (with coefficients α0, α1), and the explicit algebraic relations between the fitted and original coefficients are derived by matching the first three Legendre coefficients exactly.

## Key numbers
- The exact bilinear expansion for the order-2 scalar amplitude contains Legendre coefficients up to order 4: F0, F1, F2, F3, F4.
- The truncated order-1 bilinear expansion contains Legendre coefficients only up to order 2: G0, G1, G2.
- The fitted low-order coefficient |α1|^2 equals 3 Re(a0 a2-bar) + |a1|^2 + (3/7)|a2|^2, showing explicit dependence on the D-wave coefficient a2.
- The fitted low-order coefficient |α0|^2 equals |a0|^2 - Re(a0 a2-bar) + (2/35)|a2|^2, again depending on the D-wave coefficient a2.
- The fitted low-order coefficient Re(α0 α1-bar) equals Re(a0 a1-bar) + (2/5) Re(a1 a2-bar), mixing S-wave, P-wave, and D-wave contributions.

## Context
This work extends the line of research on the mathematical structure of truncated partial-wave analyses, including studies of discrete ambiguities by Wunderlich (2017) and Svarc (2018), and Legendre-moment analyses by Wunderlich (2017, 2020). It sharpens the distinction between true resonance content and truncation-dependent effective resonance content, which has implications for Bayesian TPWA studies such as Kroenert (2024) that analyze multiple truncation orders in parallel. The same algebraic mechanism applies to Legendre-moment analyses and photoproduction observables, which are also bilinear in the underlying amplitudes.
