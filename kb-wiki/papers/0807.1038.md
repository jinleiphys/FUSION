# Di-Jet Conical Correlations Associated with Heavy Quark Jets in anti--de Sitter Space/Conformal Field Theory Correspondence

> We show that far zone Mach and diffusion wake ``holograms'' produced by supersonic strings in anti--de Sitter space/conformal field theory (AdS/CFT) correspondence do not lead to observable conical angular correlations in the strict $N_c\to\infty$ supergravity limit if Cooper-Frye hadronization is assumed. However, a special {\em nonequilibrium} ``neck'' zone near the jet is shown to produce an apparent sonic boom azimuthal angle distribution that is roughly independent of the heavy quark's velocity. Our results indicate that a measurement of the dependence of the away-side correlations on the velocity of associated identified heavy quark jets at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and CERN LHC will provide a direct test of the nonperturbative dynamics involved in the coupling between jets and the strongly-coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP) implied by AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key claim

In the strict supergravity limit ($N_c \to \infty$), the far-zone Mach and diffusion wake holograms from supersonic strings in AdS/CFT do not produce observable conical angular correlations under Cooper-Frye hadronization; however, a non-equilibrium "neck" zone near the jet generates an apparent sonic-boom azimuthal distribution that is roughly independent of the heavy quark's velocity, which could mimic Mach-cone signals without obeying Mach's law.

## Method

The authors computed the energy-momentum tensor perturbations from the AdS/CFT trailing-string drag model for a heavy quark moving at constant speed through a strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM plasma, using the numerical solutions of Gubser, Pufu, and Yarom (2008) and the analytic near-field Yarom solution (2007). They applied Cooper-Frye freeze-out to convert the flow velocity and temperature fields into mid-rapidity azimuthal hadron distributions, separating the contributions from the far (Mach and diffusion) zone, the neck zone (defined by $\Delta\varepsilon/\varepsilon_{SYM}>0.3$), and the head zone, while subtracting the isotropic background.

## Key numbers

- For $v=0.99$, the Knudsen zone (where local equilibrium fails) extends over $|x_1| < 2/(\pi T_0)$ in the longitudinal direction, with a parametric thickness $\Delta x_1^{Kn} \sim 2/(\pi T_0) \sim 6\Gamma_s$, where $\Gamma_s = 1/(3\pi T_0)$ is the sound attenuation length.
- The head zone boundary has maximal longitudinal thickness $\Delta x_{1,C}\,\pi T_0 \sim 1/\gamma^{3/2}$ and transverse thickness $\Delta x_{\perp}\,\pi T_0 \sim 1/\gamma^{1/2}$ for $v=0.99$.
- For "realistic" parameters ($N_c=3$, $\lambda=5.5$, $T_0=0.2$ GeV), the far-zone energy density deviations remain less than 10% outside the neck region, and the Cooper-Frye freeze-out volume was taken as a cylinder of roughly $L\sim R\sim 5$ fm.
- The associated hadron transverse momentum ranges used were $p_T = 4$–$5$, $5$–$6$, and $6$–$7$ times $\pi T_0$ (approximately 2.5–4.4 GeV) for jet velocities $v/c = 0.9$, $0.75$, and $0.58$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT string-drag calculations of Gubser, Pufu, and Yarom (2008) and the analytic near-field solutions of Yarom (2007) by explicitly computing the azimuthal hadron correlations from the different stress zones. It contradicts the expectation from near-equilibrium hydrodynamic models (Stoecker 2004, Shuryak 2005) that Mach cones from the far zone would produce observable double-peaked correlations; instead, it shows that only the non-equilibrium neck zone yields such structure, with a velocity-independent angle. The results enable a direct experimental test of AdS/CFT modeling by measuring the velocity dependence of away-side correlations from identified heavy quark jets at RHIC and LHC.
