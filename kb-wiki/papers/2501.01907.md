# QCD Phase Diagram and Astrophysical Implications

> I make a brief review about the QCD phases and the equation of state inferred from the neutron star data. Along the temperature axis at low baryon density, the QCD phase transition is a smooth crossover, and it is a natural extension of our imagination to postulate a similar crossover along the density axis at low temperature. Even without phase transitions, the inferred thermodynamic properties of neutron star matter turn out to be highly nontrivial already at twice of the nuclear saturation density. I also give some discussions about the substantiation of quark matter by means of the gravitational wave signals including the multi-messenger prospect.

## Key claim

The QCD phase transition at high baryon density and low temperature is likely a smooth crossover rather than a first-order phase transition, based on the analogy with the well-established crossover at high temperature and low baryon density; however, the author emphasizes this is a "good starting point" or "first approximation" for the equation of state, and weak first-order phase transitions cannot be excluded by current data.

## Method

The author reviews the known nature of QCD phase transitions from heavy-ion collision experiments and lattice QCD, then discusses the inference of the neutron star equation of state (EoS) using machine-learning techniques, specifically a neural network approach with 100 independently trained models that predict EoS candidates from observed neutron star radius-mass data. The analysis examines the speed of sound squared and the conformality measure (trace anomaly) extracted from these inferred EoSs, and discusses how gravitational wave signals from binary neutron star mergers could probe the phase structure.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density energy density is ε₀ = 0.15 GeV/fm³
- The speed of sound squared shows a possible peak around ε ≃ 4ε₀ ≃ 0.6 GeV/fm³
- The conformality measure Δ rapidly decreases already around ε/ε₀ ≳ 2 and may cross zero around ε/ε₀ ≃ 4-5
- The binding energy per nucleon is B ∼ 0.016 GeV
- The typical QCD scale is Λ_QCD ≃ 0.2 GeV
- The gravitational wave frequency range of interest for QCD phase transitions is ≳ 3 kHz
- The conformal limit for the speed of sound squared is c_s² = 1/3

## Context

This review extends the author's earlier work on the QCD crossover picture from Quark Matter 2014 (Fukushima, 2014) and the machine-learning EoS inference (Fujimoto et al., 2021, 2024). It contrasts with the traditional bag model picture that predicts a first-order phase transition at high density, and references the quark-hadron continuity scenario (Schäfer, 1998; Alford, 1999; Hatsuda, 2006) as theoretical justification for a crossover. The work enables future discrimination between crossover and first-order phase transition scenarios using gravitational wave signals from binary neutron star mergers, particularly through the lifetime of the post-merger remnant as a function of total binary mass (Fujimoto et al., 2024).
