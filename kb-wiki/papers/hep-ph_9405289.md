# The Convergence Radius of the Chiral Expansion in the Dyson-Schwinger Approach

> We determine the convergence radius $m_{conv}$ for the expansion in the current quark mass using the Dyson-Schwinger (DS) equation of QCD in the rainbow approximation. Within a Gaussian form for the gluon propagator $D_{μν} ({\bf p}) \sim δ_{μν} χ^2 e^{- {{p^2} \over Δ}}$ we find that $m_{conv}$ increases with decreasing width $Δ$ and increasing strength $χ^2$. For those values of $χ^2$ and $Δ$, which provide the best known description of low energy hadronic phenomena, $m_{conv}$ lies around $2 Λ_{QCD}$, which is big enough, that the chiral expansion in the strange sector converges. Our analysis also explains the rather low value of $m_{conv} \approx 50 \dots 80 \ {\text MeV}$ in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, which as itself can be regarded as a special case of the rainbow DS models, where the gluon propagator is a constant in momentum space.

## Key claim

The convergence radius of the chiral expansion in the Dyson-Schwinger approach depends crucially on the infrared behavior of the gluon propagator; for a parameterization that provides a good description of low-energy hadronic phenomena, the convergence radius lies around 2 Λ_QCD, which is large enough that the chiral expansion in the strange quark sector converges, contrary to the poor convergence found in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

## Method

The author solves the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator in Euclidean space, using a Gaussian form for the gluon propagator with variable strength χ² and width Δ, as well as a more realistic form that includes an asymptotic freedom tail. The convergence radius m_conv is determined by finding the minimum of the curve m_0 = m_0[B(0)], where B(0) is the scalar part of the quark self-energy at zero momentum, and m_0 is the current quark mass extracted from the high-momentum behavior of B(p).

## Key numbers

- For the parameter set that provides the best description of low-energy hadronic phenomena (χ = 1.14 GeV, Δ = 0.002 GeV², Λ_QCD = 190 MeV, τ = 3.0), the convergence radius at a renormalization point μ = 1000 Λ is at least m_conv = 1.6 Λ, which corresponds to m_conv ≈ 3 Λ at a typical hadronic scale of μ ≈ 1 GeV.
- In the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, the convergence radius is m_conv ≈ 50 to 80 MeV, which lies far below the strange quark mass of m_s ≈ 150 MeV.
- For the Gaussian gluon propagator with χ = 8.0 Λ and Δ = 0.2 Λ², the self-consistent iteration procedure fails to converge for values of B(0) deviating too much from the chiral limit, but a minimum value for m_conv can still be extracted from the curve.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral expansion convergence originally performed by Carruthers and Haymaker (1971) in the chiral sigma model and by Hatsuda (1990) within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, which found a low convergence radius of about 50-80 MeV. The author shows that this low value arises from the contact-interaction form of the NJL model rather than being a general feature of QCD, and that a Dyson-Schwinger approach with a more realistic gluon propagator exhibiting infrared slavery yields a convergence radius above the strange quark mass. This enables confidence in the convergence of chiral perturbation theory for strange quarks when using QCD-inspired models with proper infrared behavior.
