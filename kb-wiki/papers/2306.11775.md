# Superfluid Rivers in Spinning-down Neutron Stars

> We study the motion of neutron superfluid vortices in a spinning-down neutron star, assuming axisymmetry of the flow and ignoring motion of vortices about the rotation axis. We find that the vortex array, if initially rectilinear, is soon substantially deformed as the star spins down; vortices are swept outward by the Magnus force, accumulating in regions of the inner crust where they pin, accompanied by significant bending of the vortex array. As the star spins down to below a spin rate of ~20 Hz (twice the spin rate of the Vela pulsar), the Magnus and pinning forces gradually compress the vortex array into dense sheets that follow spherical shells. In some cases, the vortex array bends on itself and reconnects, forming one or more tori of vortex rings that contain superfluid ``rivers" with significant angular momentum. Vortex sheets are likely to form near the base of the inner crust, in the regime of nuclear pasta.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Taylor-Proudman theorem, which would keep superfluid vortex arrays rectilinear in a spinning-down neutron star, is strongly violated when realistic vortex pinning forces are present in the inner crust; the vortex array becomes substantially deformed and compressed into dense spherical sheets, and in some cases forms tori of vortex rings containing superfluid "rivers" with significant angular momentum, but only under the assumptions of axisymmetric flow and neglecting vortex motion about the rotation axis.

## Method

The authors derive the equations of motion for superfluid vorticity in a rotating frame, including Magnus forces, vortex tension, and pinning forces from the nuclear lattice. They develop a global model where vortices are mobile only when the local superfluid velocity exceeds a position-dependent critical unpinning threshold, and they solve for the circulation evolution under steady spin-down of the crust. They simulate two illustrative pinning models: a spherically-symmetric Gaussian distribution of critical velocity, and a Gaussian with superimposed sinusoidal oscillations to represent large spatial gradients in pinning strength.

## Key numbers

- The critical force for vortex unpinning is estimated at 10^16 dyn cm^-1 to 10^17 dyn cm^-1, corresponding to critical superfluid velocities of 10^-5 c to 10^-4 c.
- Vortex tension T_v is approximately 1 MeV fm, and pinning forces dominate bending forces for vortex curvature radii greater than about 10^3 fm (10^-10 cm).
- The transition to vortex sheets begins when the spin period P exceeds approximately 0.2 s, for a characteristic pinning variation scale of 0.05 stellar radii and maximum critical velocity of 3 x 10^6 cm/s.
- In Example Model 2 with k = 100 R^-1, the vortex array becomes significantly deformed before reaching a spin period of 0.03 s (the spin period of the Crab pulsar).
- The drag coefficient gamma = eta/(rho_s kappa) in the inner crust is typically about 10^-3.
- In the outer core, the relaxation time tau_v is approximately P(s) seconds, giving sin(theta) approximately 1/(4 pi) for vortex motion.

## Context

This work extends the classic analysis of Ruderman and Sutherland (1974), who argued that the superfluid vortex array in a spinning-down neutron star remains effectively rigid and rectilinear, precluding turbulence. The authors show that this Taylor-Proudman-like behavior is violated when pinning forces are present, contrary to assumptions in many prior models (e.g., Alpar et al. 1984, 1985; Lee 1996; Link and Levin 2002; Antonelli et al. 2017) that assumed rectilinear vortex arrays. The results enable development of more realistic models for neutron star spin-down, spin glitches, and timing noise, and suggest that the vortex array may evolve into complex, possibly turbulent configurations.
