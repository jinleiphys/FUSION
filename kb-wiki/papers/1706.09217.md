# Bottomonia Suppression in Heavy Ion Collisions from AdS/CFT

> We compute for the first time the suppression of bottomonia in a strongly coupled QGP and compare the results to those from a weakly coupled QGP. Using imaginary time techniques we numerically determine the real and imaginary parts of the ground state binding energy of the bottomonia in one potential computed from AdS/CFT and another computed from pQCD. We then use these binding energies in a suppression model to determine the $Υ$(1S) nuclear modification factor in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=2.76$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions. AdS/CFT significantly overpredicts the suppression compared to data, although the predictive power of our calculation is limited by its significant sensitivity to the exact details of the suppression model.

## Key claim
AdS/CFT significantly overpredicts the suppression of bottomonia compared to CMS data for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, but the predictive power of this calculation is limited by its significant sensitivity to the exact details of the suppression model.

## Method
The authors numerically solved the non-relativistic, time-dependent Schrödinger equation using imaginary time techniques with a Crank-Nicolson scheme to determine the real and imaginary parts of the ground state binding energy of Υ(1S) for two potential models: one from leading-order pQCD (taken from Krouppa et al., 2015) and one from AdS/CFT (taken from Albacete et al., 2008). These complex binding energies were then used in a suppression model following Krouppa et al. (2015) to compute the nuclear modification factor R_AA for √s_NN = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, using the optical limit of the Glauber model for the collision geometry.

## Key numbers
- The AdS/CFT potential with λ = 10 (α_s = 0.27) and T_SYM = T_QCD predicts R_AA values significantly below CMS data for Υ(1S) in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- The strongly coupled Υ(1S) dissociates at approximately 1.9 T_c, where T_c = 192 MeV
- The central temperature T_0 = 522 MeV and initial formation time τ_0 = 0.3 fm were used in the suppression model
- The pQCD potential yields binding energies that keep Υ(1S) bound up to at least T = 3 T_c
- The imaginary part of the AdS/CFT potential diverges with quark separation r, while the imaginary part of the pQCD potential saturates

## Context
This work extends the methodology of Krouppa et al. (2015) and Margotta et al. (2011) by computing for the first time bottomonia suppression in a strongly coupled QGP using AdS/CFT and comparing directly to weakly coupled pQCD results and CMS data (Khachatryan et al., 2016). The authors find that their binding energies for the pQCD potential differ quantitatively from those in Margotta et al. (2011), leading to significantly different suppression predictions. The results enable future work investigating velocity-dependent quarkonia potentials from AdS/CFT versus pQCD, and highlight the need for better understanding of the mapping between N=4 SYM and QCD parameters.
