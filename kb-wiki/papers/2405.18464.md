# Ab-initio electroweak corrections to superallowed $β$ decays and their impact on $V_{ud}$

> Radiative corrections are essential for an accurate determination of $V_{ud}$ from superallowed $β$ decays. In view of recent progress in the single-nucleon sector, the uncertainty is dominated by the theoretical description of nucleus-dependent effects, limiting the precision that can currently be achieved for $V_{ud}$. In this work, we provide a detailed account of the electroweak corrections to superallowed $β$ decays in effective field theory (EFT), including the power counting, potential and ultrasoft contributions, and factorization in the decay rate. We present a first numerical evaluation of the dominant corrections in light nuclei based on Quantum Monte Carlo methods, confirming the expectations from the EFT power counting. Finally, we discuss strategies how to extract from data the low-energy constants that parameterize short-distance contributions and whose values are not predicted by the EFT. Combined with advances in ab-initio nuclear-structure calculations, this EFT framework allows one to systematically address the dominant uncertainty in $V_{ud}$, as illustrated in detail for the $^{14}$O $\to$ $^{14}$N transition.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective field theory (EFT) framework for electroweak radiative corrections to superallowed beta decays that, combined with ab-initio nuclear-structure calculations, can systematically address the dominant uncertainty in the determination of $V_{ud}$, but the framework requires unknown low-energy constants whose values must be extracted from data through a simultaneous fit to different isotopes.

## Method

The authors develop a tower of effective field theories spanning from the electroweak scale down to nuclear scales, matching the Fermi theory onto chiral EFT with nucleons, pions, photons, and leptons. They identify and compute the dominant radiative corrections using a power-counting scheme that separates hard, soft, potential, and ultrasoft photon momentum regions, and evaluate the resulting two-body electroweak currents using Quantum Monte Carlo methods for light nuclei. The framework is applied to the $^{14}\text{O} \to ^{14}\text{N}$ transition as a concrete numerical example.

## Context

This work extends the EFT analysis of radiative corrections from the single-nucleon sector (Cirigliano 2023) to nucleus-dependent effects in superallowed beta decays, which currently dominate the uncertainty in $V_{ud}$ determinations. The framework provides a systematic alternative to the traditional decomposition of radiative corrections into $\delta_R^\prime$, $\delta_\text{NS}$, and $\delta_C$ (Hardy 2020, Gorchtein 2023), and enables comparison with the dispersive approach of Seng (2022) and Gorchtein (2023). The results enable improved tests of CKM unitarity, where a global fit currently shows a $2.8\sigma$ deficit (Cirigliano 2022).
