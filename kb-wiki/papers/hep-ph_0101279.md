# Soft pion theorem for hard near threshold pion production

> We prove new soft pion theorem for the near threshold pion production by a hard electromagnetic probe. This theorem relates various near threshold pion production amplitudes to the nucleon distribution amplitudes. The new soft pion theorem is in a good agreement with the SLAC data for F_2^p(W,Q^2) for W^2 < 1.4 GeV^2 and 7 < Q^2 < 30.7 GeV^2.

## Key claim

The paper proves a new "hard-soft" pion theorem (hSPT) for near-threshold pion production by a hard electromagnetic probe, valid for large photon virtuality \(Q^2 \gg \Lambda^2\) (where \(\Lambda \sim 1\) GeV is a typical hadronic scale), which relates the production amplitudes to nucleon distribution amplitudes; the theorem is in good agreement with SLAC data for the structure function \(F_2^p(W,Q^2)\) in the range \(W^2 \le 1.4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(7 \le Q^2 \le 30.7\) GeV\(^2\), but the agreement relies on using a symmetric form for the nucleon distribution amplitude and the data resolution is insufficient to distinguish the predicted \(W\)-dependence from a simple fit.

## Method

The authors apply the QCD factorization theorem for exclusive processes to express the amplitude for \(\gamma^* + N \to \pi + N'\) at large \(Q^2\) in terms of the distribution amplitudes (DAs) of the nucleon and of the low-mass \(\pi N\) system. They then derive a soft pion theorem using the general relation \(\langle \pi^a(k) N_f(p,S)| O | 0 \rangle = \frac{i}{f_\pi} \langle N_f(p,S)| [Q_5^a, O]|0\rangle + \frac{i g_A}{4 f_\pi (p\cdot k)} \sum_{S',f'} \bar u(p,S) \kslash \gamma_5 \tau^a_{ff'} u(p,S') \langle N_{f'}(p,S')| O | 0\rangle\), where the commutator term gives the threshold amplitude and the second term (nucleon pole) contributes for \(W-W_{\rm th} \sim m_\pi\). The threshold amplitudes for various \(\pi N\) channels are computed explicitly in terms of the symmetric and antisymmetric nucleon DA components \(\phi_S(x)\) and \(\phi_A(x)\), and for the symmetric case (\(\phi_A=0\)) the amplitudes are expressed directly in terms of the nucleon magnetic form factors \(G_{Mp}\) and \(G_{Mn}\).

## Key numbers

*   The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
*   The axial charge of the nucleon used is \(g_A \approx 1.25\).
*   The integral \(\int_{\rm th}^{1.4} dW^2 Q^6 F_2^p(W,Q^2)\) from the SLAC E136 experiment is \(0.10 \pm 0.02\) GeV\(^8\), compared to the hSPT prediction of \(0.11 \pm 0.02\) GeV\(^8\).
*   The input nucleon form factors used are \(Q^4 G_{Mp}(Q^2) = 1.0 \pm 0.1\) GeV\(^4\) at \(Q^2 \ge 10\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^4 G_{Mn}(Q^2) = -(0.5 \pm 0.1)\) GeV\(^4\) at \(Q^2 \approx 10\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The predicted integral for the neutron is \(\int_{\rm th}^{1.4} dW^2 Q^6 F_2^n(W,Q^2) = 0.05 \pm 0.02\) GeV\(^8\).
*   The asymptotic (\(Q^2 \to \infty\)) ratio of neutron to proton structure functions near threshold is \(\lim_{Q^2\to \infty, W\to W_{{\rm th}}} \frac{F_2^{n}(W,Q^2)}{F_2^{p}(W,Q^2)} = \frac{57}{32} + O(m_\pi/M_N)\).
*   The asymptotic ratio of threshold amplitudes for the proton is \(\lim_{Q^2\to \infty} \frac{A(\gamma^*p\to \pi^0 p)|_{{\rm th}}}{A(\gamma^*p\to \pi^+ n)|_{{\rm th}}} = -\sqrt{2} + O(m_\pi/M_N)\).
*   The asymptotic ratio of threshold amplitudes for the neutron is \(\lim_{Q^2\to \infty} \frac{A(\gamma^*n\to \pi^0 n)|_{{\rm th}}}{A(\gamma^*n\to \pi^- p)|_{{\rm th}}} = -\frac{13}{\sqrt{2}} + O(m_\pi/M_N)\).

## Context

This work extends the soft pion theorem (SPT) of earlier work (e.g., the authors cite "old", VZ, and Koch) which was valid only for \(Q^2 \ll \Lambda^3/m_\pi\), to the regime of large virtuality \(Q^2 \gg \Lambda^2\) using QCD factorization (Brodsky and Lepage, Efremov and Radyushkin). The new hSPT enables the extraction of nucleon distribution amplitudes from near-threshold pion production data, complementing studies of nucleon form factors at large momentum transfer (e.g., Chernyak and Zhitnitsky, Carlson and Poor, CGS). The authors note that the formalism can be extended to other hard reactions and that measurements at JLab at 12 GeV would be valuable.
