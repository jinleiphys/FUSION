# Uncertainties on the EFT coupling limits for direct dark matter detection experiments stemming from uncertainties of target properties

> Direct detection experiments are still one of the most promising ways to unravel the nature of dark matter. To fully understand how well these experiments constrain the dark matter interactions with the Standard Model particles, all the uncertainties affecting the calculations must be known. It is especially critical now because direct detection experiments recently moved from placing limits only on the two elementary spin independent and spin dependent operators to the complete set of possible operators coupling dark matter and nuclei in nonrelativistic theory. In our work, we estimate the effect of nuclear configuration-interaction uncertainties on the exclusion bounds for one of the existing xenon-based experiments for all fifteen operators. We find that for operator number 13 the $\pm 1σ$ uncertainty on the coupling between the dark matter and nucleon can reach more than 50% for dark matter masses between 10 and 1000 GeV. In addition, we discuss how quantum computers can help to reduce this uncertainty and how the uncertainties are affected for couplings obtained for the nonrelativistic reductions of the relativistic interactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear configuration-interaction uncertainties can cause the ±1σ uncertainty on dark matter-nucleon coupling exclusion limits to exceed 50% for operator number 13 at dark matter masses between 10 and 1000 GeV, and can reach up to an order of magnitude for some operators, but these results are based on a simplified Monte Carlo procedure that samples one-body density matrices rather than the underlying Hamiltonian matrix elements.

## Method

The authors used two different nuclear configuration-interaction Hamiltonians (GCN and JJ55) to compute one-body density matrices for xenon isotopes in a shell model basis with a fixed ¹⁰⁰Sn core and valence space of five orbitals. They constructed Gaussian distributions for each density matrix element from the mean and standard deviation between the two Hamiltonians, then performed Monte Carlo sampling of these distributions while enforcing particle number conservation sum rules. The randomized density matrices were input into the dmscatter code to calculate differential event rates and 90% confidence level upper limits on the 15 nonrelativistic EFT coupling coefficients for XENON1T detector parameters.

## Key numbers

- For operator number 13, the ±1σ uncertainty on the coupling between dark matter and nucleon can reach more than 50% for dark matter masses between 10 and 1000 GeV
- The ±1σ uncertainty region for upper limits on couplings to some operators can be up to an order of magnitude
- The analysis used a WIMP mass of 150 GeV for the event rate spectra calculations
- The XENON1T detector had a 1 ton-year effective exposure with a recoil energy region of interest from 4.9 keV to 54.4 keV
- The six most abundant xenon isotopes used were ¹³²Xe (26.9%), ¹²⁹Xe (26.4%), ¹³¹Xe (21.2%), ¹³⁴Xe (10.4%), ¹³⁶Xe (8.86%), and ¹³⁰Xe (4.07%), constituting slightly more than 97% of detector mass
- The 90% confidence level required a minimum event rate of 2.3 events per ton-year in the zero background case
- The dark matter velocity distribution used v₀ = 220 km/s and v_esc = 550 km/s

## Context

This work extends the Galilean Effective Field Theory framework developed by Fitzpatrick (2012) and Anand (2013) for dark matter-nucleus interactions by quantifying nuclear structure uncertainties that were previously unaccounted for in experimental exclusion limits. The paper builds on configuration-interaction shell model calculations using the GCN Hamiltonian from Caurier (2007, 2010) and the JJ55 Hamiltonian from Brown (2004), and applies these to XENON1T experiment parameters from the XENON collaboration (2018). The results motivate future work using emulators for more complete uncertainty quantification and suggest that quantum computers could help reduce these nuclear uncertainties.
