# Mixing Angle of $\varXi_Q-\varXi_Q'$ in Heavy Quark Effective Therory

> The Heavy Quark Effective Theory provides a systematic method to estimate a mixing angle of hadron states in a heavy quark, such as the charm quark (c) and the bottom quark (b). By using this method, the mixing angle of the baryons $\varXi_Q-\varXi_Q'$ can be estimated. It is found that the mixing angle between $\varXi_Q$ and $\varXi_Q'$ is given by $θ_b = 4.51^{\circ} \pm 0.79 ^{\circ}$ for $Q = b$ case and $θ_c = 8.12^{\circ} \pm 0.80 ^{\circ}$ for $Q = c$ case.

## Key claim

Using Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) with \(O(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/m_Q)\) corrections and neglecting higher-order \(O(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2/m_Q^2)\) terms, the mixing angle between \(\Xi_Q\) and \(\Xi_Q'\) baryons is estimated to be \(|\theta_b| = 4.51^\circ \pm 0.79^\circ\) for bottom quarks and \(|\theta_c| = 8.12^\circ \pm 0.80^\circ\) for charm quarks; the sign of the mixing angle cannot be determined from the mass relations used.

## Method

The mixing angle \(\theta_Q\) was expressed in terms of the experimental masses of \(\Xi_Q\), \(\Xi_Q'\), \(\Xi_Q^*\), \(\Sigma_Q\), and \(\Sigma_Q^*\) baryons using a formula derived from the HQET mass formula with \(O(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/m_Q)\) corrections. Experimental baryon masses from the Particle Data Group (2020) were inserted into this formula to extract the mixing angle for both the charm and bottom cases.

## Key numbers

- \(|\theta_c| = 8.12^\circ \pm 0.80^\circ\) for the \(\Xi_c - \Xi_c'\) mixing
- \(|\theta_b| = 4.51^\circ \pm 0.79^\circ\) for the \(\Xi_b - \Xi_b'\) mixing
- Experimental masses used (in MeV): \(M(\Sigma_c) = 2453.54 \pm 0.15\), \(M(\Sigma_c^*) = 2518.45 \pm 0.15\), \(M(\Xi_c) = 2469.42 \pm 0.15\), \(M(\Xi_c') = 2578.80 \pm 0.35\), \(M(\Xi_c^*) = 2645.98 \pm 0.17\); \(M(\Sigma_b) = 5813.10 \pm 0.18\), \(M(\Sigma_b^*) = 5832.53 \pm 0.20\), \(M(\Xi_b) = 5794.45 \pm 0.51\), \(M(\Xi_b') = 5935.02 \pm 0.05\), \(M(\Xi_b^*) = 5955.33 \pm 0.13\)

## Context

This work extends previous HQET estimates of the mixing angle for the charm case only (Ito, 1996) to also include the bottom case, and provides a comparison with predictions from QCD sum rules (Aliev, 2010) and quark model calculations (Franklin, 1981; Franklin, 1996). The results for \(\theta_c\) agree within errors with the QCD sum rules prediction of \(5.5^\circ \pm 1.8^\circ\), while the quark model prediction of \(3.8^\circ\) disagrees; for \(\theta_b\), the result of \(4.51^\circ\) lies between the QCD sum rules value of \(6.4^\circ \pm 1.8^\circ\) and the quark model value of \(1.0^\circ\). The analysis enables further tests of HQET predictions for heavy baryon properties and confirms the expectation that the mixing angle decreases with increasing heavy quark mass.
