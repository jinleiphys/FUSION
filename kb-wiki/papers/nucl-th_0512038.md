# alpha-particle photoabsorption with a realistic nuclear force

> The 4He total photoabsorption cross section is calculated with the realistic nucleon-nucleon potential Argonne V18 and the three-nucleon force (3NF) Urbana IX. Final state interaction is included rigorously via the Lorentz Integral Transform method. A rather pronounced giant resonance with peak cross sections of 3 (3.2) mb is obtained with (without) 3NF. Above 50 MeV strong 3NF effects, up to 35%, are present. Good agreement with experiment is found close to threshold. A comparison in the giant resonance region is inconclusive, since present data do not show a unique picture.

## Key claim

The total photoabsorption cross section of 4He, calculated with the realistic Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon force (3NF), shows a giant resonance peak near 3 mb, but the comparison with experiment in the giant resonance region is inconclusive because present experimental data do not show a unique picture.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body continuum problem using the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method to include final state interaction rigorously, reducing it to a bound-state-like problem. The ground state and LIT equations were solved via expansions in hyperspherical harmonics using the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) approach, with the total photoabsorption cross section calculated in the unretarded dipole approximation using the Siegert theorem to incorporate exchange current contributions.

## Key numbers

- Peak cross section of 3 mb with the three-nucleon force (AV18+UIX) and 3.2 mb without the 3NF (AV18 only)
- 3NF effects above 50 MeV: enhancement of 18% at 60 MeV, 25% at 100 MeV, and 35% at 140 MeV
- Binding energy results: 24.27 MeV (AV18) and 28.42 MeV (AV18+UIX)
- 3NF reduces the peak height by about 6% and shifts the peak position by about 1 MeV towards higher energy
- At pion threshold, the 3NF enhancement is about twice the effect found in 3H/3He photoabsorption

## Context

This work extends previous calculations on three-nucleon photodisintegration (Gazit et al., 2005) to the four-body system, where the 3NF is expected to be more significant due to four triplets versus one triplet in three-nucleon systems. It improves upon earlier semi-realistic potential models (Efros et al., 1997; Barnea et al., 2001) that overestimated the giant resonance peak by 10-15% but strongly underestimated the cross section at higher energies (by a factor of three at pion threshold). The results enable future experimental investigations to determine whether nuclear force models constructed from two- and three-nucleon systems can explain four-nucleon photodisintegration.
