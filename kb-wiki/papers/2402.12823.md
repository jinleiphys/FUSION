# The influence of hadronic rescatterings on the net-baryon number fluctuations

> Fluctuations of conserved charges, such as the net-baryon number fluctuations, are influenced by different dynamical evolution processes. In this paper, we investigate the influence of hadronic rescatterings on different orders of cumulants of the net-baryon number distribution. At the start of hadronic rescatterings, we introduce net-baryon number distributions reconstructed based on net-baryon cumulants of different orders obtained from computation in functional renormalization group (FRG), where the distributions were constructed using the maximum entropy method. This way we introduce the critical fluctuations of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) into the AMPT model. Firstly, we find that hadronic rescatterings have distinct effects on cumulant ratios of different orders for the net-baryon number. Secondly, we observe that the effect of hadronic rescatterings is more significant for critical fluctuations than dynamical fluctuations, because the two-, three- and four-particle correlation functions due to critical fluctuations are weakened more significantly by hadronic rescatterings.

## Key claim

Hadronic rescatterings weaken two-, three-, and four-particle correlation functions due to critical fluctuations more significantly than those due to dynamical fluctuations, and this effect modifies cumulant ratios of the net-baryon number distribution in a centrality-dependent manner.

## Method

The authors embed net-baryon number distributions reconstructed from functional renormalization group (FRG) cumulants (using the maximum entropy method) into the AMPT model with string melting at the start of the hadronic rescattering stage. They then simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV and compare cumulant ratios and integrated multiparticle correlation functions before and after hadronic rescatterings, with and without the FRG-sampled critical fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) ranges from 399 MeV (0–5% centrality) to 337 MeV (60–80% centrality) for Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV.
- Pseudo-critical temperature \(T_{\mathrm{pc}}\) ranges from 139 MeV (0–5%) to 146 MeV (60–80%).
- Fireball volume \(V\) ranges from 980 \(\mathrm{fm}^3\) (0–5%) to 48 \(\mathrm{fm}^3\) (60–80%).
- The FRG estimate for the CEP location is \((T_{CEP}, \mu_{B_{CEP}}) = (107, 635)\) MeV.
- The FRG phase boundary curvature \(\kappa = 0.0142(2)\) is reported, consistent with lattice QCD values such as \(\kappa = 0.0145(25)\) from Bonati (2018) and \(\kappa = 0.0153(18)\) from Borsanyi (2020).
- Kinematic cuts: transverse momentum \(0.4 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\) and midrapidity \(|y| < 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Chen (2022) on hadronic rescattering effects on dynamical fluctuations by incorporating critical fluctuations from FRG calculations (Fu 2021, Fu 2023) into the AMPT model. It enables a more realistic comparison with net-proton STAR measurements (STAR 2021) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, showing that including critical fluctuations improves agreement for the \(C_4/C_2\) ratio and four-particle correlation functions. The results also highlight that volume fluctuations (corrected via centrality bin width correction) significantly affect cumulant ratios, consistent with earlier studies by Chen (2022) and Huang (2021).
