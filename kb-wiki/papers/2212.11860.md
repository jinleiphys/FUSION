# Towards a Unified Model of Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions

> The achievement of the goals of the ongoing and future accelerator-based neutrino experiments - notably the determination of CP violating phase in the lepton sector - will require the development of advanced models of neutrino-nucleus interactions. In this review, we summarise the present status of experimental studies of neutrino-nucleus scattering, and discuss the developments and perspectives of the theoretical approach based on factorisation of the nuclear cross section, which has recently emerged as a promising framework for the description of the variety of processes contributing to the detected signals.

## Key claim

The factorisation approach, which separates the lepton-nucleon interaction vertex from nuclear initial and final state amplitudes, is a promising framework for describing neutrino-nucleus interactions, but its success depends on the kinematic regime where the impulse approximation applies (momentum transfer much larger than about 400 MeV) and requires spectral functions that go beyond the independent particle model to include nucleon-nucleon correlations.

## Method

The authors review the factorisation formalism based on the spectral function approach, where lepton-nucleus scattering is treated as the incoherent sum of elementary processes involving off-shell nucleons whose momentum and energy distribution is described by the target spectral function. They compare this approach to alternative models including the relativistic Fermi gas model, superscaling, short-time approximation of Quantum Monte Carlo, and Green's Function Monte Carlo techniques, and survey experimental measurements from multiple neutrino experiments (NOMAD, K2K, MiniBooNE, MINOS, SciBooNE, MINERvA, ArgoNeuT, MicroBooNE, NOvA, T2K) on targets including carbon, oxygen, iron, and argon.

## Key numbers

- Mean neutrino beam energies range from 0.6 GeV (T2K) to 23.4 GeV (NOMAD); antineutrino beam mean energies range from 0.6 GeV (T2K) to 19.7 GeV (NOMAD)
- The axial mass parameter M_A measured by different experiments: NOMAD reports 1.0, K2K reports 1.2, MiniBooNE reports 1.2, MINOS reports 1.2, T2K reports 1.26 (units: GeV)
- Flux uncertainties in the most accurate studies (MINOS, MINERvA) are about 5%
- Total systematic error in T2K and NOvA oscillation results is about 5-7%, mostly from beam flux uncertainty and detector response
- Wrong sign neutrino contamination in antineutrino beams can be as large as 10%
- The threshold for long-range correlation effects is momentum transfer much less than about 400 MeV (π/d where d is average nucleon-nucleon distance)
- MiniBooNE neutrino flux mean energy is 880 MeV
- The electron-carbon cross section data shown span beam energies from 0.730 to 1.501 GeV at scattering angle 37 degrees

## Context

This review extends the factorisation-based spectral function approach previously applied to electron-nucleus scattering (Benhar 2006, Sick 1980, Donnelly superscaling) to neutrino-nucleus interactions, building on work by BFNSS, Leitner (2008), Benhar (2010), Martini (2010), Nieves, Ankowski, and others. The availability of argon and titanium spectral functions (PhysRevD.105.112002, 2022) will be critical for the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE). The paper enables future work on unified models that consistently describe quasi-elastic scattering, resonance production, and deep inelastic scattering within a single framework, addressing the systematic uncertainties that currently dominate neutrino oscillation measurements.
