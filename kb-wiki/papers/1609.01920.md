# Experimental results on charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> We present a subset of experimental results on charge fluctuation from the heavy-ion collisions to search for phase transition and location of critical point in the QCD phase diagram. Measurements from the heavy-ion experiments at the SPS and RHIC energies observe that total charge fluctuations increase from central to peripheral collisions. The net-charge fluctuations in terms of dynamical fluctuation measure $ν_{(+-,dyn)}$ are studied as a function of collision energy (\sqsn) and centrality of the collisions. The product of $ν_{(+-,dyn)}$ and $\langle N_{ch} \rangle$ shows a monotonic decrease with collision energies, which indicates that at LHC energy the fluctuations have their origin in the QGP phase. The fluctuations in terms of higher moments of net-proton, net-electric charge and net-kaon have been measured for various \sqsn. Deviations are observed in both $Sσ$ and $κσ^2$ for net-proton multiplicity distributions from the Skellam and hadron resonance gas model for \sqsn $<$ 39 GeV. Higher moment results of the net-electric charge and net-kaon do not observe any significant non-monotonic behavior as a function of collision energy. We also discuss the extraction of the freeze-out parameters using particle ratios and experimentally measured higher moments of net-charge fluctuations. The extracted freeze-out parameters from experimentally measured moments and lattice calculations, are found to be in agreement with the results obtained from the fit of particle ratios to the thermal model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental results on charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, claiming that the monotonic decrease of the product of dynamical fluctuation measure and mean charged particle multiplicity with collision energy indicates that at LHC energy the fluctuations originate in the QGP phase, but this interpretation is hedged by noting that the observable may not be sensitive enough to QGP physics or that hadronization may have washed out the signal.

## Method

The authors compile and review experimental measurements of total charge fluctuations (scaled variance), net-charge fluctuations (dynamical fluctuation measure nu_{(+-,dyn)} and D-measure), and higher moments (skewness S and kurtosis kappa) of net-proton, net-electric charge, and net-kaon distributions from SPS, RHIC, and LHC experiments. They compare these measurements to theoretical expectations from the hadron resonance gas model, Skellam distributions, and lattice QCD calculations, and discuss extraction of freeze-out parameters from cumulant ratios.

## Key numbers

- Scaled variance omega_ch for Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV increases from central to peripheral collisions, with values above the Poisson expectation of 1.0
- D-measure value for central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV is 2.3 +/- 0.21 for Delta_eta = 1.6, approaching the QGP expectation of 1.0-1.5
- Deviations in S*sigma and kappa*sigma^2 for net-proton distributions from Skellam and hadron resonance gas model are observed for sqrt(s_NN) < 39 GeV, with maximum deviations at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 and 27 GeV
- The product nu_{(+-,dyn)} * <N_ch> shows monotonic decrease with collision energy from SPS through RHIC to LHC energies

## Context

This review extends the line of work on charge fluctuation measurements as probes of the QCD phase transition and critical point, building on theoretical predictions by Stephanov (1998), Jeon (2000), and Asakawa (2000). It compiles results from the SPS experiments (WA98, NA49), RHIC experiments (STAR, PHENIX), and LHC experiments (ALICE), and compares with lattice QCD calculations by Borsanyi (2014) and Alba (2014). The work enables future searches for the QCD critical point in upcoming programs such as RHIC BES-II, FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC.
