# Axial Vector Tetraquark with Two s-quarks

> Possibility of an axial vector isoscalar tetraquark $ud\bar{s}\bar{s}$ is discussed. If a $f_1$ meson in the mass region $1.4-1.5$ GeV consists of four quarks $ns\bar{n}\bar{s}$, the mass of the isoscalar $ud\bar{s}\bar{s}$($\vartheta^+$-meson) state with $J^P=1^+$ is expected to be lower than that of the $f_1$ meson. Within a flux-tube quark model, a possible resonant state of $ud\bar{s}\bar{s}(J^{P}=1^{+})$ is suggested to appear at $\sim$ 1.4 GeV with the width ${\cal{O}}(20\sim 50)$ MeV. We propose that the $\vartheta^+$-meson is the good candidate for the tetraquark search, which would be observed in the $K^+K^+π^-$ decay channel.

## Key claim
If a $f_1$ meson in the mass region 1.4–1.5 GeV is interpreted as a four-quark $ns\bar{n}\bar{s}$ state rather than a conventional $q\bar{q}$ meson, then the isoscalar $ud\bar{s}\bar{s}$ ($\vartheta^+$) tetraquark with $J^P=1^+$ is expected to have a mass lower than that $f_1$ meson, appearing at approximately 1.4 GeV with a width of order 20–50 MeV.

## Method
The authors applied a flux-tube quark model combined with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) to the $4q$ system, restricting the model space to the exotic color configuration $(qq)_{\bar{3}}(\bar{q}\bar{q})_3$ and ignoring coupling to the $(q\bar{q})_1(q\bar{q})_1$ configuration. They used the observed mass of either the $f_1(1420)$ or $f_1(1510)$ meson as an input to determine the mass shift parameter $M_0$ in the string potential, then calculated the $\vartheta^+$ mass and estimated its width by assuming the same coupling to $K\bar{K}^*$ as for the four-quark $f_1$.

## Key numbers
- The $\vartheta^+(1^+)$ mass is predicted to be approximately 1.4 GeV.
- The width of the $\vartheta^+(1^+)$ is estimated to be $\Gamma_{\vartheta} = 20 \sim 50$ MeV.
- The lowest threshold for allowed two-body decays is 1.39 GeV for the $KK^*(895)$ channel.
- In the MIT bag model (Jaffe, 1977), the theoretical mass for the isoscalar $ud\bar{s}\bar{s}(J^P=1^+)$ state was predicted to be 1.65 GeV.
- In the constituent quark model calculation (Isgur, 1985), the masses of the two $1^{++}$ $q\bar{q}$ states in the $P$-wave nonet are 1.24 and 1.48 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the diquark picture applied to the pentaquark $\Theta^+$ (Jaffe and Wilczek, 2003) to the tetraquark sector, proposing a $J^P=1^+$ $ud\bar{s}\bar{s}$ state with unnatural spin-parity that forbids $S$-wave decays to two pseudoscalar mesons. It contrasts with earlier predictions for the same flavor content: the MIT bag model (Jaffe, 1977) gave a mass of 1.65 GeV, and Burns et al. (2005) suggested a $J^P=1^-$ state at approximately 1.6 GeV. The predicted $\vartheta^+$ meson could be searched for in the $K^+K^+\pi^-$ decay channel, which is distinct from the decay channels of the $J^P=1^-$ and $J^P=0^+$ predictions.
