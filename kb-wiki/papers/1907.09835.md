# Spin chemical potential for relativistic particles with spin 1/2

> We analyze algebraic structure of a relativistic semi-classical Wigner function of particles with spin 1/2 and show that it consistently includes information about the spin density matrix both in two-dimensional spin and four-dimensional spinor spaces. This result is subsequently used to explore various forms of equilibrium functions that differ by specific incorporation of spin chemical potential. We argue that a scalar spin chemical potential should be momentum dependent, while its tensor form may be a function of space-time coordinates only. This allows for the use of the tensor form in local thermodynamic relations. We furthermore show how scalar and tensor forms can be linked to each other.

## Key claim

A scalar spin chemical potential, often used in the literature, must be momentum-dependent to be consistent with the relativistic definition of spin polarization in the particle rest frame, whereas a tensor spin chemical potential can depend only on space-time coordinates and is therefore better suited for use in local thermodynamic relations.

## Method

The authors analyze the algebraic structure of the relativistic semi-classical Wigner function for spin-1/2 particles at leading order in the \(\hbar\) expansion, showing that it consistently encodes the spin density matrix in both two-dimensional spin space and four-dimensional spinor space. They then construct and compare equilibrium Wigner functions that incorporate either a scalar or a tensor spin chemical potential, examining the constraints imposed by Lorentz covariance and the physical interpretation of spin polarization measurements.

## Key numbers

- The condition for the scalar spin chemical potential to be valid is \(\mu^\pm/T \ll 1\) (small polarization limit).
- For the Boltzmann statistics, the scalar spin chemical potential relates to the polarization magnitude as \(\mu^\pm = T(x) \zeta^\pm(x,\pv)\).
- The spin polarization magnitude is defined as \(\zeta^\pm(x,\pv) = \sqrt{-\zeta^{\pm \beta}(x,\pv) \zeta^{\pm}_{\beta}(x,\pv)} = |\zetav_\ast^\pm|\), with values ranging from 0 to 1.
- The average polarization vector in the particle rest frame is given by \(\left\langle \zetav_\ast^\pm(x,\pv) \right\rangle = \frac{1}{2} \zetav_\ast^\pm(x,\pv)\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of equilibrium distribution functions for particles with spin, building on prior approaches by Fang et al. (2016), Weickgenannt et al. (2019), and Schaefer et al. (2016) that used a scalar spin chemical potential, and by Becattini et al. (2013) and Florkowski et al. (2017) who introduced a tensor form linked to thermal vorticity or the spin polarization tensor. The results clarify the proper Lorentz structure of spin chemical potentials and are intended to support the development of hydrodynamic and kinetic theories for spin-polarized systems, particularly in the context of spin polarization measurements of \(\Lambda\) hyperons in heavy-ion collisions by the STAR Collaboration (2017).
