# Thermal width and quarkonium dissociation by inelastic parton scattering

> In a weak-coupling effective field theory framework we study quarkonium dissociation induced by inelastic scattering with partons in the medium. This is the dominant dissociation process for temperatures such that the Debye mass is larger than the binding energy. We evaluate the dissociation cross section and the corresponding thermal decay width. At leading order we derive a convolution formula relating the two, which is consistent with the optical theorem and QCD at finite temperature. Bound state effects are systematically included. They add contributions to the cross section and width that are beyond a quasi-free approximation, whose validity is critically reviewed. For temperatures such that the Debye mass is smaller than the binding energy, the dominant dissociation mechanism is gluo-dissociation consisting in quarkonium dissociation induced by the absorbtion of a gluon from the medium. We calculate the gluo-dissociation cross section and width at next-to-leading-order accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the commonly used convolution formula for the quarkonium dissociation width due to inelastic parton scattering, which neglects bound-state effects and uses a quasi-free approximation, does not follow from the optical theorem applied to QCD at finite temperature; instead, a different formula incorporating quantum-statistical effects of Pauli blocking and Bose enhancement is derived from first principles in an effective field theory framework.

## Method

The authors use a hierarchy of effective field theories (NRQCD and pNRQCD) to systematically integrate out thermal and non-relativistic scales from QCD, assuming weak coupling and a heavy-quark mass much larger than the temperature. They compute the dissociation cross section and thermal decay width at leading order by evaluating the imaginary part of the gluon propagator in Coulomb gauge, using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory and the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for the gluon self-energy. The analysis covers several temperature regimes depending on the relative sizes of the temperature T, the Debye mass m_D, the inverse Bohr radius mv, and the binding energy E.

## Key numbers

* Debye mass definition: m_D^2 = (g^2 T^2 / 3) (N_c + n_f / 2), where N_c is the number of colors and n_f is the number of light quark flavors.
* Quark contribution to Debye mass: m_D^2|_quark = g^2 n_f T^2 / 6.
* Gluon contribution to Debye mass: m_D^2|_gluon = g^2 N_c T^2 / 3.
* Dissociation temperature estimate: T_d ~ m g^{4/3}, where m is the heavy-quark mass and g is the QCD coupling.
* Bohr radius for a 1S Coulombic state: a_0 = 2 / (m C_F alpha_s), where C_F = (N_c^2 - 1) / (2 N_c).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to quarkonium in a thermal bath developed by Brambilla, Escobedo, Ghiglieri, and Vairo in previous papers (Brambilla 2008, Escobedo 2008, Brambilla 2010), where singlet-to-octet thermal breakup (gluo-dissociation) and Landau damping were identified as leading-order dissociation mechanisms. The paper critically reviews the quasi-free approximation used in phenomenological studies by Grandchamp (2001, 2002) and others, showing that bound-state effects are essential and cannot be neglected in a weak-coupling setting below the dissociation temperature. The results enable more rigorous calculations of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the Upsilon(1S) at LHC energies where the regime mv >> T >> E >> m_D may be relevant.
