# Mixed leptonic and hadronic corrections to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon

> Higher-order hadronic corrections to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon have been evaluated including next-to-next-to-leading-order insertions of hadronic vacuum polarization and next-to-leading-order corrections to hadronic light-by-light scattering. This leaves a set of mixed leptonic and hadronic corrections in the form of double-bubble topologies as the only remaining hadronic effect at ${\mathcal O}(α^4)$. Here, we estimate these contributions by analyzing the respective cuts of the diagrams, suggesting that the impact is limited to $\lesssim 1\times 10^{-11}$ and thus negligible at the level of the final precision of the Fermilab $g-2$ experiment.

# Mixed leptonic and hadronic corrections to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon

## Key claim

The mixed leptonic and hadronic double-bubble corrections at order α⁴ are estimated to be ≤ 1×10⁻¹¹, which is negligible compared to the final precision of the Fermilab g-2 experiment (Δa_μ^exp[E989] = 16×10⁻¹¹), but this conclusion depends on the assumption that no enhanced effects from e⁺e⁻ pair radiation occur due to experimental cuts or misidentification.

## Method

The authors evaluate the double-bubble topologies by separating the contributions into virtual (two-particle cut) and real (four-particle cut) spectral functions using dispersion relations. For the QED case, they reproduce known results for the three lepton-pair configurations (ee, μe, eμ) by computing the spectral functions from the cuts and integrating via the dispersion relation for the vacuum polarization function. For the hadronic case, they estimate the π⁺π⁻ contribution using scalar QED with the pion vector form factor F_π^V(s), calculating the analogous virtual and real spectral functions for a charged-pion loop with an inner electron loop.

## Key numbers

- The total QED contribution for ℓ₁=ℓ₂=e is 1.44 × (α/π)⁴, where (α/π)⁴ ≃ 2.9×10⁻¹¹, corresponding to approximately 4.2×10⁻¹¹ in a_μ
- The total QED contribution for ℓ₁=μ, ℓ₂=e is 0.16 × (α/π)⁴, corresponding to approximately 0.46×10⁻¹¹ in a_μ
- The total QED contribution for ℓ₁=e, ℓ₂=μ is 0.02 × (α/π)⁴, corresponding to approximately 0.06×10⁻¹¹ in a_μ
- The estimated π⁺π⁻ contribution (scalar QED with inner electron loop) is 0.12 × (α/π)⁴, corresponding to approximately 0.35×10⁻¹¹ in a_μ
- The final precision projected for the Fermilab E989 experiment is Δa_μ^exp[E989] = 16×10⁻¹¹
- The current experimental world average is a_μ^exp = 116 592 061(41)×10⁻¹¹
- The Standard Model prediction is a_μ^SM = 116 591 810(43)×10⁻¹¹

## Context

This work completes the set of hadronic corrections at O(α⁴) for the muon g-2, following the evaluation of NNLO hadronic vacuum polarization by Kurz et al. (2014) and NLO corrections to hadronic light-by-light scattering by Colangelo et al. (2014). The authors use methods developed for higher-order corrections to heavy-quark production by Teubner (1995) and Hoang (1993-1997). The result confirms that the remaining mixed leptonic and hadronic double-bubble topologies are negligible at the precision required for the Fermilab g-2 experiment, but the authors note that experimental verification of no enhanced e⁺e⁻ pair radiation effects would be desirable.
