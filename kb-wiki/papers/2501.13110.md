# The Open Relativistic Two-body Problem

> The open relativistic two-body problem, when two interacting particles also are in external potentials, is considered in terms of the principle of the least action. Based on the consistent modification of the relativistic version Newton's third law in external fields, the exact covariant operator equations, which govern dynamics of either a scalar particle or each of the components of the 16 component spinor of spin-$1/2$ fermions, depending on the particle type, are derived in the center-mass and relative motion variables, beyond the consideration in the Breit frame only. The class of external fields and interaction potentials, when the two-body problem can be covariantly reformulated in (3+1) phase space of relative motion variables, uncoupled from the center-mass motion of such a system, is outlined. In the case of fermions the new $γ$-matrix basis generating the Dirac-like equation for the 16 component spinors in the (3+1) phase space is found. The chosen basis allows us to decouple the derived Dirac-like equation into four independent equations governing the dynamics of the four-component spinors. The developed approach is examined in studying the low-energy positronium states in magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the open relativistic two-body problem (two interacting particles also subject to external potentials) can be covariantly reformulated in a (3+1) phase space of relative motion variables, uncoupled from center-of-mass motion, but only for a specific class of external fields and interaction potentials that satisfy a modified relativistic version of Newton’s third law and certain orthogonality and linearity conditions.

## Method

The author starts from the principle of least action, constructing action integrals for two scalar or spin-1/2 fermion particles with vector and scalar interaction potentials and external vector fields. By choosing specific Lagrange multipliers and introducing center-of-mass and relative motion variables (total momentum P, relative momentum p, center coordinate X, relative coordinate x), the equations of motion are derived. For fermions, a new 16×16 gamma-matrix basis is constructed to transform the resulting Klein-Gordon-like equations for each spinor component into a Dirac-like equation that decouples into four independent four-component spinor equations. The approach is then applied to positronium in a uniform magnetic field in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field strength at RHIC and LHC energies is estimated to be about B ∼ 10^18 – 10^19 G (order of and more than the squared pion mass).
* The magnetic length a = (2√(πα) B)^(-1/2) is required to be much smaller than the positronium Bohr radius a_B = 1/(2πα m_e) for the strong-field approximation (a ≪ a_B).
* The fine structure constant α = e^2/(4π) appears in the interaction potential A^0_int = -4πα/r.
* The positronium ground state decay energy is about 0.511 MeV, corresponding to two-photon decay.

## Context

This work extends the constraint dynamics approach to relativistic two-body systems developed by Crater and Van Alstine (1984, 1987, 1990, 2001) and by Sazdjian (1986, 1989, 1994), which previously focused on closed systems without external fields or only in the Breit frame. The paper goes beyond earlier studies by Droz-Vincent (2000, 2009) and Bijtebier (2000) that considered external fields but did not reconstruct the relative motion spectrum or spin mixing effects. The new gamma-matrix basis and the decoupling of the 16-component spinor equation into four independent four-component equations simplify the analysis of real physical systems such as positronium in strong magnetic fields, relevant for interpreting astrophysical signals from pulsars and neutron stars.
