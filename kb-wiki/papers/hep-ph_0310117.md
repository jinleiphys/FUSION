# Properties of baryon anti-decuplet

> We study the group structure of baryon anti-decuplet containing the $Θ^+$. We derive the SU(3) mass relations among the pentaquark baryons in the anti-decuplet, when there is either no mixing or ideal mixing with the pentaquark octet, as advocated by Jaffe and Wilczek. This constitutes the Gell-Mann--Okubo mass formula for the pentaquark baryons. We also derive SU(3) symmetric Lagrangian for the interactions of the baryons in the anti-decuplet with the meson octet and the baryon octet. Our analysis for the decay widths of the anti-decuplet states suggests that the N(1710) is ruled out as a pure anti-decuplet state, but it may have anti-decuplet component in its wavefunction if the multiplet is mixed with the pentaquark octet.

## Key claim

The paper derives SU(3) mass relations for the baryon anti-decuplet containing the Θ⁺, showing that the N(1710) is ruled out as a pure anti-decuplet state but may have an anti-decuplet component if the multiplet is mixed with the pentaquark octet, as advocated by Jaffe and Wilczek.

## Method

The authors use SU(3) flavor symmetry to construct the Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula for the baryon anti-decuplet, deriving equal spacing rules for both the pure anti-decuplet and the ideally mixed case with the pentaquark octet. They also derive an SU(3) symmetric Lagrangian for the interactions of the anti-decuplet with the baryon octet and pseudoscalar meson octet, and compute decay widths for the anti-decuplet members.

## Key numbers

- Θ⁺ mass: 1540 MeV, decay width less than 25 MeV (experimental input)
- Ξ₃/₂ mass: 1862 MeV from NA49 experiment (experimental input)
- Pure anti-decuplet predictions using Θ⁺ and Ξ₁₀ as input: M(N₁₀) = 1647 MeV, M(Σ₁₀) = 1755 MeV
- Anti-decuplet mass splitting parameter δm̄₁₀ ≈ -107 MeV
- Under ideal mixing with octet and assuming M(N_q) = 1440 MeV (Roper), the predicted M(N_s) = 1751 MeV (close to N(1710))
- Under alternative fit using phenomenological mass term: M(N_q) = 1584 MeV, M(Λ₈) = 1647 MeV, M(Σ_q) = 1495 MeV, M(N_s) = 1679 MeV, M(Σ_s) = 2274 MeV
- Universal coupling constant g ≈ 0.9 if Γ(Θ⁺) = 5 MeV
- Decay widths of anti-decuplet members relative to Θ⁺: N₁₀ ≈ 4 × Γ(Θ⁺), Σ₁₀ ≈ 7 × Γ(Θ⁺), Ξ₁₀ ≈ 10 × Γ(Θ⁺)

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model predictions of Diakonov et al. (1997) and the diquark-diquark-antiquark model of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) by providing explicit SU(3) mass relations and interaction Lagrangians for the pentaquark anti-decuplet. The paper enables future experimental searches for the predicted N₁₀, Σ₁₀, and Ξ₁₀ states, and provides selection rules (such as the prohibition of N₁₀ decay into Δπ) that can distinguish pure anti-decuplet states from mixed configurations.
