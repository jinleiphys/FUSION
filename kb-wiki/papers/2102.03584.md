# Critical behaviour of hydrodynamic series

> We investigate the time-dependent perturbations of strongly coupled $\mathcal{N} = 4$ SYM theory at finite temperature and finite chemical potential with a second order phase transition. This theory is modelled by a top-down Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton description which is a consistent truncation of the dimensional reduction of type IIB string theory on AdS$_5\times$S$^5$. We focus on spin-1 and spin-2 sectors of perturbations and compute the linearized hydrodynamic transport coefficients up to the third order in gradient expansion. We also determine the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic mode in spin-1 sector and the lowest non-hydrodynamic modes in spin-2 sector. Analytically, we find that all the hydrodynamic quantities have the same critical exponent near the critical point $θ= 1/2$. Moreover, we establish a relation between symmetry enhancement of the underlying theory and vanishing the only third order hydrodynamic transport coefficient $θ_1$, which appears in the shear dispersion relation of a conformal theory on a flat background.

## Key claim

All hydrodynamic transport coefficients (shear viscosity, relaxation time, and third-order coefficients) and the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic series in the spin-1 sector exhibit the same critical exponent θ = 1/2 near the second-order phase transition point of the 1RCBH model, but only for the thermodynamically stable branch of solutions.

## Method

The authors study linearized perturbations of the 1RCBH background, a top-down Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton construction dual to N=4 SYM at finite temperature and chemical potential. They use the master equations formalism to compute retarded Green's functions via holographic renormalization and extract transport coefficients up to third order in gradient expansion from Kubo formulas. Quasinormal mode frequencies are computed numerically for complex momenta to determine the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic series in the spin-1 sector.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponent for all hydrodynamic quantities near the phase transition: θ = 1/2
- The ratio μ/T ranges from 0 to π/√2, with the critical point at μ/T = π/√2 (corresponding to Q = √2, y = 0)
- At zero chemical potential (y = 1), the model reduces to the AdS₅-Schwarzschild background with standard N=4 SYM thermodynamics
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at the critical point is η/s = 1/(4π)
- The third-order transport coefficient θ₁ vanishes when the underlying theory exhibits symmetry enhancement

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the 1RCBH model's hydrodynamics by DeWolfe (2011) from first-order transport coefficients to third order in gradient expansion. It builds on the analysis of hydrodynamic convergence in AdS-Reissner-Nordstrom black branes by Withers (2018) and Jansen (2020), and on the study of quasinormal modes in N=4 SYM at zero chemical potential by Grozdanov (2019). The results enable future investigation of the spin-0 sector and the application of Borel-Pade resummation techniques to this holographic model with a second-order phase transition.
