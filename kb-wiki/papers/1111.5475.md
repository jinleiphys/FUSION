# Transport Properties of Strong-Interaction Matter

> The properties of strong-interaction matter are probed in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In the context of measurements of the elliptic flow at RHIC and the LHC the shear viscosity is of particular interest. In this presentation we discuss recent results for eta/s in hadronic matter at vanishing baryo-chemical potential within kinetic theory. Using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, special attention is paid to effects arising from the restoration of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry with increasing temperature.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy-density ratio (η/s) in hadronic matter at vanishing baryo-chemical potential is strongly modified by chiral restoration effects as temperature increases, but the results are extremely sensitive to the applied approximations and remain more than one order of magnitude above the AdS/CFT bound of 1/4π, even in the most reliable model.

## Method

The authors computed the shear viscosity of a pion gas using kinetic theory within the Chapman-Enskog expansion, evaluating the ππ-scattering amplitude in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at the 1/N_c level. They included the in-medium modification of quark and meson masses, the momentum dependence of the σ-meson propagator, and a σ width from two-pion decay via a K-matrix approximation, while neglecting the momentum dependence of quark triangle and box diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The Weinberg vacuum scattering lengths are a⁰_W = 7m_π/(32πf_π²) and a²_W = -2m_π/(32πf_π²), which the NJL model reproduces in vacuum.
- In the intermediate temperature regime, the most reliable approximation yields η/s ≈ 3.
- The AdS/CFT (KSS) bound is η/s = 1/4π.
- The dissociation temperature T_diss is where the σ-meson mass becomes degenerate with the in-medium two-pion threshold; the Mott temperature T_Mott is where the pion dissolves into a quark-antiquark pair.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of η/s in a pion gas using Weinberg scattering lengths (Weinberg, 1960s) by incorporating medium effects from chiral restoration via the NJL model, following earlier NJL studies by Schulze and Quack. The results are motivated by hydrodynamical calculations of elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC (Niemi, 2011) which suggest the hadronic phase is more important than the QGP phase for the p_T-dependence of v_2. The authors identify needed improvements including ρ-meson contributions, three-flavor extensions with kaons and η-mesons, and inclusion of quark scattering above the crossover temperature.
