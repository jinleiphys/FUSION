# Photonuclear Tomography in Ultraperipheral Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present a theoretical investigation of photonuclear tomography as a novel technique for probing the internal structure of nuclei. In this approach, ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions (UPCs) serve as a source of intense fluxes of virtual photons, which induce coherent production of vector mesons. By analyzing the probabilities and cross sections of these photon-induced processes, we propose a methodology for reconstructing the spatial distribution of nucleons within the nucleus. Our framework provides a systematic way to access information on the nuclear geometry probed in UPCs, offering new opportunities for studies of nuclear structure using particle production as a probe. Numerical calculations for selected examples illustrate the feasibility and potential of this method.

## Key claim

The paper proposes photonuclear tomography as a technique for probing nuclear structure via vector meson production in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions, but the authors hedge that the sensitivity to neutron skin effects is modest: a 0.2 fm change in neutron skin thickness produces only a 4% change in the predicted J/psi production cross section, making experimental extraction challenging.

## Method

The authors develop a coordinate-space formulation of photonuclear interactions in UPCs, defining a Photonuclear Tomography Function (PTF) that represents the probability of photon-nucleus interaction at a given transverse distance from the nuclear center. They compute the PTF for J/psi and rho0 meson production in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, using experimental data for the gamma-p to V-p cross section from SLAC, Jefferson Lab, HERA, and ALICE, combined with nuclear density distributions modeled as Fermi functions.

## Key numbers

- Lorentz factor for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV: gamma = 1.73 x 10^7 in the rest frame of one ion
- Nuclear radius of lead: R_Pb approximately 6.5 fm
- For J/psi production at photon energy kappa = 100 GeV, the PTF at the nuclear center reaches approximately 0.1
- For J/psi production at photon energy kappa = 5 GeV, the PTF at the nuclear center reaches approximately 10^-3
- The PTF for rho0 production at the nuclear center reaches approximately 2.9 at W_gamma p = 2 GeV, indicating the one-photon exchange approximation is insufficient
- Total J/psi production cross section in Pb+Pb collisions over 4 < W_gamma p < 305 GeV: 48 +/- 1.5 mb with no neutron skin, decreasing to 46 +/- 1.4 mb with neutron skin thickness R_np = 0.2 fm
- Fit parameters for J/psi photoproduction on protons at low energies (kappa < 25 GeV): (a, b, c) = (0.298 +/- 0.027, -3.429 +/- 0.338, 11.802 +/- 1.336) for sigma (nb) = a*kappa + b*kappa^2 + c*kappa^3
- Fit parameters for J/psi photoproduction at high energies (25 < W_gamma p < 305 GeV): sigma_0 = 3.75 +/- 0.05 nb, delta = 0.677 +/- 0.08 for sigma = sigma_0 * W_gamma p^delta
- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 = 0.17 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space approach to photonuclear interactions in UPCs previously applied to dilepton production by gamma-gamma interactions (Sengul 2015, Xu 2024) and connects to the broader program of nuclear imaging with vector meson photoproduction planned at the Electron-Ion Collider (Asche 2019) and studied theoretically at the LHC and EIC (Maanfa 2022, Maanfa 2023). The method enables systematic extraction of nuclear geometry information from UPC data, though the authors note that rho0 production requires treatment beyond the one-photon exchange approximation due to large PTF values, and that neutron skin sensitivity is limited to approximately 4% cross section changes for a 0.2 fm skin thickness.
