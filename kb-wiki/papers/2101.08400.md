# Giant dipole resonance in Sm isotopes within TDHF method

> In this work, we have studied the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in even-even Sm isotopes within time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) with four Skyrme forces SLy6, SVbas, SLy5 and UNEDF1. The approach we have followed is somewhat similar to the one we did in our previous work in the region of Neodymium (Nd, Z=60) [\href{https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1402-4896/ab73d8}{Physica Scripta (2020)}]. We have calculated the dipole strength of $ ^{128-164}\text{Sm}$, and compared with the available experimental data. An overall agreement between them is obtained. The dipole strength in neutron-deficient $ ^{128-142}\text{Sm}$ and in neutron-rich $^{156-164}\text{Sm}$ isotopes are predicted. Shape phase transition as well as shape coexistence in Sm isotopes are also investigated in the light of IVGDR. In addition, the correlation between the quadrupole deformation parameter $β_{2}$ and the splitting $ΔE/ \bar{E}_{m}$ of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) spectra is studied. The results confirm that $ΔE/ \bar{E}_{m}$ is proportional to quadrupole deformation $β_{2}$

## Key claim
The authors claim that the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method with four Skyrme forces provides an overall agreement with available experimental data for the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in even-even Sm isotopes, but this agreement is qualified by a slight down-shift of about 0.5 MeV for SLy5 and SLy6 in spherical and weakly deformed nuclei, and a slight up-shift of about 0.5 MeV for SVbas.

## Method
The authors performed static Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with 24 fm box size and 1.00 fm grid spacing to obtain ground-state properties of 128-164Sm isotopes. They then applied an instantaneous isovector dipole boost to the ground-state wave function and evolved the system using TDHF for 4000 time steps of 0.2 fm/c (total 800 fm/c), recording the dipole moment and performing a Fourier transform to obtain the GDR strength distribution. Four Skyrme functionals were used: SLy6, SLy5, SVbas, and UNEDF1.

## Key numbers
- For 144Sm (spherical), the calculated excitation energy from the dipole moment period (T = 84.3 fm/c) is 14.70 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 15.3 ± 0.1 MeV from Carlos (1974).
- For 138Sm with SLy6, the excitation energies along the three axes are: Ex = 14.75 MeV, Ey = 16.52 MeV, Ez = 13.40 MeV.
- For 154Sm with SLy6, the excitation energies are: Ex = Ey = 15.62 MeV, Ez = 11.90 MeV.
- For 146Sm with SLy6, the binding energy difference between the prolate minimum (-1999.73 MeV) and oblate minimum (-1999.66 MeV) is approximately 0.07 MeV, with quadrupole deformations of β2 = 0.063 (prolate) and β2 = 0.048 (oblate).
- The quadrupole deformation parameter β2 for 154Sm is 0.341 (experimental) and calculated values range from 0.302 (SLy5) to 0.341 (SLy6); for 164Sm, calculated β2 values range from 0.363 (SVbas) to 0.373 (SLy6).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous TDHF study of GDR in Nd isotopes (Ait Ben Mennana and Oulne, 2020) to the Sm isotopic chain, and complements earlier theoretical studies using Separable Random-Phase-Approximation (Reinhard and collaborators, 2007-2008), Relativistic Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (Ring and collaborators, 2009), and Extended Quantum Molecular Dynamics (Wang and collaborators, 2017). The results confirm the proportionality between the GDR splitting parameter ΔE/Ēm and the quadrupole deformation β2, and enable predictions of dipole strength in neutron-deficient (128-142Sm) and neutron-rich (156-164Sm) isotopes where experimental data are not yet available. The finding of shape coexistence in 146Sm (with a 0.07 MeV energy difference between prolate and oblate minima) extends the discussion of shape coexistence in this region by Wood (1992) and Heyde (2011).
