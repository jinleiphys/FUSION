# Production of the $X(4014)$ as the spin-2 partner of $X(3872)$ in $e^+e^-$ collisions

> In 2021, the Belle collaboration reported the first observation of a new structure in the $ψ(2S) γ$ final state produced in the two-photon fusion process. In the hadronic molecule picture, this new structure can be associated with the shallow isoscalar $D^*\bar{D}^*$ bound state and as such is an excellent candidate for the spin-2 partner of the $X(3872)$ with the quantum numbers $J^{PC}=2^{++}$ conventionally named $X_2$. In this work we evaluate the electronic width of this new state and argue that its nature is sensitive to its total width, the experimental measurement currently available being unable to distinguish between different options. Our estimates demonstrate that the planned Super $τ$-Charm Facility offers a promising opportunity to search for and study this new state in the invariant mass distributions for the final states $J/ψγ$ and $ψ(2S)γ$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the X(4014) candidate observed by Belle in two-photon fusion is an excellent candidate for the spin-2 partner of X(3872) with JPC=2++ in the hadronic molecule picture, but the experimental measurement currently available cannot distinguish between molecular and conventional charmonium interpretations because the nature of the state is sensitive to its total width, which remains poorly constrained.

## Method

The authors evaluate the electronic width of X(4014) using vector meson dominance (VMD) with intermediate ψγ states (ψ = J/ψ, ψ(2S)), treating the X2 as a pure D*D̅* molecule. They compute triangle loop diagrams for X2 → ψγ → e+e- in the MS subtraction scheme, introduce counterterms for ultraviolet divergences, and estimate the short-range contribution by varying the renormalization scale μ from 2.0 to 6.0 GeV. The two-photon decay width is recalculated for the X2(4014) mass using the coupling to its D*D̅* constituents.

## Key numbers

- Mass of X2: M_X2 = (4014.3 ± 4.0 ± 1.5) MeV
- Width of X2: Γ_X2 = (4 ± 11 ± 6) MeV
- Measured product Γ_X2γγ × Br(X2 → ψ(2S)γ) = (1.2 ± 0.4 ± 0.2) eV
- Estimated two-photon decay width for molecular X2: Γ(X2 → γγ) ≃ 0.1 keV
- Estimated two-photon decay width for generic χc2 charmonium: Γ(χc2 → γγ) ≃ 1 keV
- Helicity-0 contribution to two-photon decay: Γ_X2γγ[hel-0] ≲ 12 eV for binding energy around 5 MeV
- Estimated branching fraction Br[X2 → e+e-] ≈ 10^(-9) (order-of-magnitude)
- Estimated cross section for direct X2 production in e+e- collisions: σ_C ≃ 7 pb
- Electronic widths used: Γ_ψ(2S)ee = 2.44 keV, Γ_J/ψee = 5.53 keV
- Decay constants extracted: f_J/ψ = 415.49 MeV, f_ψ(2S) = 294.35 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecule interpretation of X(3872) to its predicted spin-2 partner X2, following predictions by Nieves (2012) and Guo (2013) based on heavy quark spin symmetry. The Belle collaboration (2021) reported a 2.8σ hint of this structure in two-photon fusion. The authors show that if the total width of X2 is small (∼1 MeV or less), the state is only consistent with a molecular interpretation, while a width ∼10 MeV would be consistent with both molecular and conventional charmonium scenarios. The planned Super τ-Charm Facility (STCF) offers a promising opportunity to search for this state in J/ψγ and ψ(2S)γ invariant mass distributions.
