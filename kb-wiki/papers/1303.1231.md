# Baryon-antibaryon production asymmetry in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Experimental data at RHIC and SPS energies suggest a systematic correlation between yield ratio $\bar{p}/p$ and $K^{-}/K^{+}$, which reveal some universality of hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We propose an explanation based on the quark combination mechanism in which the production asymmetry between baryons and antibaryons is focused on especially. We start from the basic ideas of quark combination to carry out a general analysis of the properties of the global production of baryons and mesons and obtain the yields of baryons, antibaryons and mesons as functions of the number of constituent quarks and antiquarks just before hadronization. In particular, we study effects of quark-antiquark asymmetry on the ratio of (anti-)baryons to mesons and that of antibaryons to baryons. We use these formula to well explain the data of $\bar{p}/p$ and $K^{-}/K^{+}$ at different of collision energies and rapidities and suggest further measurements at moderate RHIC energies in the mid-forward rapidity regions to make the decisive test of this universality.

# Baryon-antibaryon production asymmetry in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The observed universal correlation between the antiproton-to-proton yield ratio and the kaon charge ratio across RHIC, SPS, and LHC energies can be explained by a quark combination mechanism, provided that the exponent relating these ratios is approximately 5 rather than the naive quark-counting value of 3. The explanation depends on the assumption that hadronization proceeds via exclusive quark combination with unitarity constraints, and that the parameter a ≈ 5 derived from the baryon-to-meson ratio at zero quark-antiquark asymmetry.

## Method
The authors derived analytic expressions for baryon, antibaryon, and meson yields as functions of the total number of constituent quarks and antiquarks (x) and the quark-antiquark asymmetry parameter z = (N_q - N_antiq)/(N_q + N_antiq), using symmetry properties under charge conjugation, linearity in system size, and unitarity constraints (quark number conservation). They then related the unobservable asymmetry parameter z to the experimentally measured K-/K+ ratio using strangeness neutrality and known resonance decay properties, and compared the predicted pbar/p ratio as a function of K-/K+ with data from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC.

## Key numbers
- The exponent a in the relation R(z) = [(1-z)/(1+z)]^a is determined to be a ≈ 5, derived from the baryon-to-meson ratio at z=0 of about 1/12.
- The naive quark-counting prediction pbar/p = (K-/K+)^3 is shown to lie above the experimental data, while the authors' result gives pbar/p = (K-/K+)^α with α ≈ 5.
- The K-/K+ ratio data span collision energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 5 GeV (AGS) to 2.76 TeV (LHC).
- The phi-to-K- final-state ratio is taken as approximately 0.14, an almost collision-energy-independent value from Abelev (2009).
- The vector-to-pseudoscalar meson ratio R_V/P is taken as 0.45 based on K* production studies.

## Context
This work extends the quark combination model (QCM) approach previously developed by the Shandong Group (Shao et al., 2005) to explain the systematic correlation between pbar/p and K-/K+ first noted by Arsene et al. (2010). It contradicts the predictions of popular event generators AMPT (Lin, 2005) with string fragmentation and UrQMD (Bass et al., 1998), which give an exponent α ≳ 8 and lie below the data in the intermediate K-/K+ region. The results are complementary to statistical model explanations (Becattini, 2001; Tawfik, 2011) that use chemical potential to explain antihadron-to-hadron ratios, but provide a microscopic dynamical mechanism rather than an equilibrium description. The authors suggest that the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC can provide decisive tests in the intermediate asymmetry region at sqrt(s_NN) ≈ 20-60 GeV at midrapidity or at mid-forward rapidities at 62.4 GeV.
