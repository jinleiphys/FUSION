# Direct photons and thermal dileptons: A theoretical review

> We discuss the measurement of electromagnetic radiation produced in heavy ion collisions at SPS energies. We review the low invariant mass dilepton sector, the real photon data, and the spectra of intermediate mass dimuons. Along with this, we discuss the theoretical interpretations of those observables.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while low-mass dilepton data show strong indications of genuine in-medium many-body effects, the theoretical interpretation of real photon and intermediate-mass dilepton data at SPS energies remains inconclusive due to unresolved ambiguities in parton transverse momentum broadening, hadronic reaction rates, and the consistency between real and virtual photon frameworks.

## Method

The author reviews experimental data from the SPS program (CERES, WA80, WA98, HELIOS-3, NA38/NA50) on low-mass dileptons, real photons, and intermediate-mass dimuons, comparing them with theoretical models based on finite-temperature quantum field theory, vector meson dominance, chiral effective Lagrangians, and perturbative QCD. The review critically examines the theoretical assumptions behind each interpretation, including the use of hard thermal loop resummation, in-medium vector meson spectral densities, and transport calculations with mesonic reaction databases derived from e+e- annihilation data.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic transverse momentum from a confinement radius of 0.5 fm gives <k_perp^2> ~ 0.32 (GeV/c)^2
- Early estimates of soft-gluon radiation effects gave <k_perp^2> ~ 0.9 (GeV/c)^2 at sqrt(s) = 20 GeV
- The in-medium vector meson spectral density calculation yields at least ~16% of the WA80 photon signal at p_T = 1.5 GeV/c for S+Au at 200 AGeV
- A LO-pQCD calculation with intrinsic <k_perp^2> ~ 1 (GeV/c)^2 yields ~45% of the WA98 measured yield at p_T = 2.5 GeV/c for Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV
- Photon production rates in a meson gas can differ by factors of ~3 at temperatures and transverse momenta relevant to SPS conditions
- The signal-to-background ratio for real photons is smaller than that for dileptons by roughly two orders of magnitude

## Context

This review extends the theoretical discussion of electromagnetic probes from earlier work by Feinberg (1976) and Shuryak (1978) on QGP diagnostics, and addresses the interpretation of SPS data from CERES, WA80, WA98, HELIOS-3, and NA38/NA50 collaborations. The paper identifies unresolved issues including the need for systematic studies of hadron and photon spectra in pp, pA, and AA collisions at the same energies (which will be possible at RHIC), and the necessity of making the theoretical frameworks for real and virtual photons consistent with each other as advocated by Kampfer (1999). The work enables future high-resolution measurements at vector meson masses and dedicated experiments to distinguish between thermal sources and charm enhancement in the intermediate mass region.
