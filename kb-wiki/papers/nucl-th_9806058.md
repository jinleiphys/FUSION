# Bethe-Salpeter-Approach to Relativistic Two-Fermion-Systems with a Separable Nonstatic Interaction

> To study the characteristic features of relativistic bound systems, the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for two equal mass spin 1/2 particles (like the deuteron) is solved in the cm-frame for a covariant separable interaction kernel. For that purpose the BSE is transformed to an eigenvalue problem which is diagonalized numerically. The Bethe--Salpeter amplitudes (BSAs) are obtained straightforwardly from the resulting eigenvectors. Only positive parity solutions of the eigenvalue problem are considered. To correlate the BSAs to standard quantum mechanical wavefunctions, the corresponding equal-time-wavefunctions (ETWs) are calculated. A decomposition of BSAs and ETWs in partial waves in angular momenta and parity is performed. As a first application elastic electron-deuteron-scattering in the impulse approximation (IA) is considered. The charge, magnetic and quadrupole formfactors $F_C(k^2)$, $F_M(k^2)$, $F_Q(k^2)$ and tensor polarizations $\tilde{t}_{20}(k^2)$, $t_{20} (k^2,θ_e=70^{\circ})$ are obtained from three independent matrix elements of the deuteron current in the Breit-frame of elastic electron-deuteron-scattering. Additionally, the formfactors $A(k^2)$ and $B(k^2)$ of the Rosenbluth formula are calculated.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for two equal-mass spin-1/2 particles can be solved numerically in the center-of-mass frame using a covariant separable interaction kernel that includes scalar, vector, and pseudoscalar terms, but only positive parity solutions are considered, and the resulting form factors depend strongly on the choice of coupling constants and cutoff parameters.

## Method

The homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron (proton-neutron system) was transformed into an eigenvalue problem by introducing a separable interaction kernel with scalar, vector, and pseudoscalar spin structures, each multiplied by a monopole form factor in momentum space. The eigenvalue problem was diagonalized numerically in the deuteron rest frame to obtain Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and corresponding equal-time wavefunctions, which were decomposed into partial waves in angular momentum and parity. Elastic electron-deuteron scattering was then calculated in the impulse approximation using the resulting amplitudes to extract deuteron form factors and tensor polarizations in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy used in calculations: \(E_B = 2.371 \times 10^{-3}\) (in units of nucleon mass)
- Cutoff parameters: \(\Lambda_s = \Lambda_v = \Lambda_p = 0.24\) (in units of nucleon mass) for two parameter sets, and \(\Lambda_s = 0.30\), \(\Lambda_v = \Lambda_p = 0.24\) for a third set
- Resulting deuteron magnetic moment: \(\mu_d = 6.685 \, \mu_K\) (nuclear magnetons) for one parameter set, and \(\mu_d = 0.8570 \, \mu_K\) for another
- Resulting deuteron quadrupole moment: \(Q_d = 1.287 \, \text{fm}^2\) for one parameter set, and \(Q_d = 0.2860 \, \text{fm}^2\) for another
- Coupling constants used: \(g_v = 1.285714\), \(g_0 = 1.0\), \(g_p = 0.897\) for the first set; \(g_v = 1.097139\), \(g_0 = 1.0\), \(g_p = 1.522057\) for the second; \(g_v = 2.309181\), \(g_0 = 1.0\), \(g_p = 2.608756\) for the third

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach to relativistic two-fermion systems by using a separable nonstatic interaction kernel, following earlier studies of the deuteron such as Zuilhof and Tjon (1980) and others cited in the paper. The method enables calculation of deuteron electromagnetic form factors and tensor polarizations from a fully covariant framework, including retardation effects, and can be applied to investigate relativistic effects on bound state wavefunctions and antiparticle contributions in few-body systems at high energies.
