# The Generalized Nuclear Contact and its Application to the Photoabsorption Cross-Section

> Using the zero-range model, it was demonstrated recently that Levinger's quasi-deuteron model can be utilized to extract the nuclear neutron-proton contact. Going beyond the zero-range approximation and considering the full nuclear contact formalism, we rederive here the quasi-deuteron model for the nuclear photoabsorption cross-section and utilize it to establish relations and constrains for the general contact matrix. We also define and demonstrate the importance of the diagonalized nuclear contacts, which can be also relevant to further applications of the nuclear contacts.

## Key claim

The quasi-deuteron (QD) model for nuclear photoabsorption is rederived using the full nuclear contact formalism, going beyond the zero-range approximation; the authors conclude that the QD model cannot be completely accurate because heavy nuclei include two-body channels that do not exist in the deuteron, but it can still provide approximate constraints on the nuclear contact matrix within the accuracy of available experimental data.

## Method

The authors start from the asymptotic factorization of the nuclear wave function when two nucleons are close together, defining generalized nuclear contact matrices \(C_{ij}^{\alpha\beta}\) as the analog of Tan's contact. They then introduce a diagonalized basis for the contact matrices, which ensures orthogonality of the final states in the Born-approximation calculation of the E1 dipole photoabsorption cross-section. The response function is derived as a sum over contact matrix elements multiplied by universal functions \(R_{\beta\beta'}(\omega)\), and this result is compared to Levinger's QD model to extract scaling relations and constraints on the neutron-proton contacts.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic form of the nuclear wave function is valid for \(r_{ij}\) smaller than about 1 to 2 fm, based on variational Monte Carlo results for \(A \leq 12\) nuclei using the Argonne v18 and Urbana X potentials.
* The derived response function is expected to hold for photon energies \(\hbar \omega > \hbar c / d_a \approx 100 - 200\) MeV, where \(d_a \approx 1-2\) fm.
* The Levinger constant \(L \approx 6\) relates the nuclear photoabsorption cross-section to the deuteron cross-section via \(\sigma_A(\omega) = L (NZ/A) \sigma_d(\omega)\).
* The E1 transition considered is the leading effect up to about 140 MeV, and Levinger constant extraction is usually done from experiments with photon energies between 40 MeV and 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the zero-range model derivation of Weiss, Bazak, and Barnea (2014) that first linked the Levinger constant to the neutron-proton contact, by incorporating the full nuclear contact formalism with finite-range effects. It builds on the generalized nuclear contact framework introduced by the same authors (2015) and on Tan's contact relations (2008) originally developed for ultracold atomic gases. The results enable future extraction of nuclear contact values from photoabsorption data and provide a framework for computing corrections to the QD model when more detailed contact information becomes available.
