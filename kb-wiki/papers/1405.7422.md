# BCS-BEC Crossover and Stability in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model with Diquark-Diquark Repulsion

> We investigate the equation of state (EoS) along the BCS-BEC crossover for a quark system described by a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with multi-fermion interactions. Together with attractive channels for particle-antiparticle ($\Gs$) and particle-particle ($\Gd$) interactions, a multi-fermion channel with coupling $λ$ that accounts for the diquark-diquark repulsion is also considered. The chiral and diquark condensates are found in the mean-field approximation for different values of the coupling constants. The parameter values where the BCS-BEC crossover can take place are found, and the EoS is used to identify the stability region where the BEC regime has positive pressure. We discuss how the particle density and the repulsive diquark-diquark interaction affect the stability window in the $\Gs-\Gd$ plane and find the profile of the pressure versus the density for various values of $λ$ and $\Gd$. The effects of $λ$ and $\Gd$ in the BCS-BEC crossover tend to compensate each other, allowing for a feasible region of densities where the crossover can occur with positive pressure. These results, although mainly qualitative, should serve as a preliminary step in the microscopic analysis required to determine the feasibility of the BCS-BEC crossover and its realization in more realistic models of dense QCD that can be relevant for applications to neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a BCS-BEC crossover can occur in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with diquark-diquark repulsion, but only within a specific parameter window where the resulting BEC phase has positive pressure; the results are explicitly described as "mainly qualitative" and intended as a "preliminary step" toward more realistic models of dense QCD relevant to neutron stars.

## Method

The authors study a relativistic NJL model of quarks with scalar fermion-antifermion interactions (coupling Gs), attractive fermion-fermion diquark interactions (coupling Gd), and an eight-fermion diquark-diquark repulsion term (coupling lambda). They compute the mean-field thermodynamic potential at zero temperature, solve the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate (mass m) and diquark condensate (gap Delta) at fixed particle number density, and obtain the equation of state by subtracting the self-consistently determined vacuum contribution. The BCS-BEC crossover condition is identified as the point where the chemical potential mu equals the dynamical mass m.

## Key numbers

* Critical diquark coupling for BCS-BEC crossover at zero diquark-diquark repulsion: hGdcr = 0.81 (normalized units), for tPF=0.10 and hGs=1.20
* At hlam=0, the BEC regime is stable only between hGd=0.81 and hGd~0.9, where pressure remains positive
* The critical diquark-diquark repulsion for which pressure never vanishes: hlam_cr = 11 (normalized units)
* For a two-flavor model with Lambda=664.3 MeV and Gs/4=2.06 Lambda^{-2}, the allowed range of hlam for positive pressure crossover at eta=0.75 is 9.3 < hlam < 38; at eta=0.90 the range is 0.85 < hlam < 10^3
* The stability window in the hGd-hGs plane disappears entirely at hlam=0 when tPF=0.23

## Context

This work extends previous studies of BCS-BEC crossover in QCD-inspired NJL models (Abuki, He, Klimenko) by incorporating a self-consistent treatment of diquark-diquark repulsion, going beyond the mixed treatment of Jason (2013) where the repulsion was only included at tree level and a bag constant was introduced ad hoc. The results contradict the expectation from effective boson theories (BS-2) that repulsive interactions alone guarantee stability, showing instead that the repulsion also affects the gap equation and fermion dispersion relations. The findings enable future work on more realistic models with color and flavor degrees of freedom that could be applied to neutron star cores, where the stability of the BEC phase is essential for supporting the star against gravitational collapse.
