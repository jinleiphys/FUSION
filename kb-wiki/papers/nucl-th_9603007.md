# Realistic Expanding Source Model for Invariant One-Particle Multiplicity Distributions and Two-Particle Correlations in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present a realistic expanding source model with nine parameters that are necessary and sufficient to describe the main physics occuring during hydrodynamical freezeout of the excited hadronic matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. As a first test of the model, we compare it to data from central Si + Au collisions at p_lab/A = 14.6 GeV/c measured in experiment E-802 at the AGS. An overall chi-square per degree of freedom of 1.055 is achieved for a fit to 1416 data points involving invariant pi^+, pi^-, K^+, and K^- one-particle multiplicity distributions and pi^+ and K^+ two-particle correlations. The 99-percent-confidence region of parameter space is identified, leading to one-dimensional error estimates on the nine fitted parameters and other calculated physical quantities. Three of the most important results are the freezeout temperature, longitudinal proper time, and baryon density along the symmetry axis. For these we find values of 92.9 +/- 4.4 MeV, 8.2 +/- 2.2 fm/c, and 0.0222 + 0.0096 / - 0.0069 fm^-3, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper presents a nine-parameter expanding source model that achieves a good fit (χ² per degree of freedom = 1.055) to 1416 data points from central Si + Au collisions at p_lab/A = 14.6 GeV/c, but the authors explicitly note that this is a first test and that the model is limited to describing freezeout properties rather than the full hydrodynamical evolution that preceded freezeout.

## Method

The authors construct a Wigner-function-based model of hydrodynamical freezeout with nine adjustable parameters describing the freezeout temperature, baryon chemical potential, pion incoherence parameter, transverse source radius, transverse flow velocity, radial freezeout profile, source rapidity, longitudinal rapidity extent, and longitudinal proper time. They fit the model simultaneously to invariant one-particle multiplicity distributions for π⁺, π⁻, K⁺, and K⁻ and two-particle correlation functions for π⁺ and K⁺ from experiment E-802 at the AGS, using χ² minimization via Simplex or Levenberg-Marquardt methods, with resonance decays included for mesons below 900 MeV and baryons below 1410 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Freezeout temperature: 92.9 ± 4.4 MeV
- Longitudinal proper time: 8.2 ± 2.2 fm/c
- Baryon density along symmetry axis: 0.0222 +0.0096 / -0.0069 fm⁻³
- Overall χ² per degree of freedom: 1.055 for fit to 1416 data points
- Source rapidity y_s: 1.47 (determined from fit)
- Transverse radius R: 8.0 fm (determined from fit)
- Radial freezeout parameter α_t: -0.86 (determined from fit)

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamical freezeout approach of Cooper and Frye (1974) and Bjorken (1983) by providing a parametrized model that can be efficiently fit to data, unlike full hydrodynamical simulations which make χ² minimization impractical. The model enables simultaneous determination of freezeout properties from both one-particle distributions and two-particle correlations, and the authors note that the baryon chemical potential can be extracted from meson data alone via the K⁺/K⁻ abundance ratio, which is sensitive to the net baryon number through strangeness conservation constraints.
