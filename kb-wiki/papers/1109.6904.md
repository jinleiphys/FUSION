# Holographic baryonic matter in a background magnetic field

> We discuss the effect of baryonic matter on the zero-temperature chiral phase transition at finite chemical potential in the presence of a background magnetic field. The main part of our study is done in the deconfined geometry of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, i.e., at large N_c and strong coupling, with non-antipodal separation of the flavor branes. We find that for not too large magnetic fields baryonic matter completely removes the chiral phase transition: chirally broken matter persists up to arbitrarily large chemical potential. At sufficiently large magnetic fields, baryonic matter becomes disfavored and mesonic matter is directly superseded by quark matter. In order to discuss the possible relevance of our results to QCD, we compute the baryon onset in a relativistic mean-field model including the anomalous magnetic moment and point out the differences to our holographic calculation.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in the Sakai-Sugimoto model at large N_c and strong coupling, baryonic matter completely removes the chiral phase transition for not too large magnetic fields, meaning chirally broken matter persists up to arbitrarily large chemical potential; only at sufficiently large magnetic fields does baryonic matter become disfavored and mesonic matter is directly superseded by quark matter.

## Method

The authors use the deconfined geometry of the Sakai-Sugimoto model (a holographic top-down approach to large-N_c QCD) with non-antipodal separation of flavor branes, working in the probe brane approximation with N_f=1. Baryons are introduced as pointlike D4-branes wrapped on the S^4, described through a pointlike instanton approximation in the abelian gauge theory on the D8-branes. The equations of motion are solved semi-analytically in the zero-temperature limit (f(u) approximately 1) to compute the free energy and determine phase transitions between mesonic, baryonic, and quark matter phases as functions of chemical potential and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* The QCD scale squared is approximately (200 MeV)^2, corresponding to a magnetic field of about 2 x 10^18 G, used as a reference for when magnetic fields become significant for QCD.
* Surface magnetic fields of neutron stars have been measured up to approximately 10^15 G, with core fields possibly reaching up to 10^18 G or, for quark stars, up to 10^20 G.
* The binding energy of real-world nuclear matter at onset is E_0 approximately -16 MeV, and the nuclear ground state density is n_0 approximately 0.15 fm^{-3}, used to contrast with holographic baryonic matter which is not self-bound.
* The baryon mass in the holographic model is given by N_c M_q, where M_q = (R/(2 pi alpha')) (u_c/3) sqrt(f(u_c)), with the constituent quark mass M_q defined from the D4-brane action.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Preis 2010) on inverse magnetic catalysis in the Sakai-Sugimoto model by including baryonic matter, which was previously omitted. It also extends the work of Bergman (2007) on baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model to nonzero magnetic fields, and extends Bergman (2008) from the confined antipodal geometry to the deconfined non-antipodal case. The results are compared to the Walecka model (Walecka 1974) for real-world nuclear matter, where the authors compute the baryon onset including the anomalous magnetic moment to highlight differences between holographic and QCD-like baryonic matter.
