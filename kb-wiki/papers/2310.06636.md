# Fate of critical fluctuations in an interacting hadronic medium using maximum entropy distributions

> We study the evolution of critical fluctuations in an expanding system within a hadronic transport approach. The initialization of the system with critical fluctuations is achieved by coupling the ideal hadron resonance gas cumulants to the ones from the 3d Ising model and generating the net and total particle number distribution from the principle of maximum entropy. These distributions are then evolved using realistic hadronic interactions. We systematically investigate the evolution of the critical fluctuations initialized at various temperatures and chemical potentials along a freeze-out line. We find that resonance regeneration and isospin randomization processes have the strongest influence on the evolution of the fluctuations. Additionally, the sets of particles coupled to the critical mode are modified to assess the strength of the propagation of correlations through interactions. We find that in the scaling region of the critical point correlations are propagated through the whole collisional history and are still present after the kinetic freeze-out of the matter if the coupling strength is large enough.

## Key claim

Critical fluctuations initialized via maximum-entropy distributions in a hadronic transport approach survive the entire collisional history and remain present after kinetic freeze-out, but only if the coupling strength of particles to the critical mode is large enough; resonance regeneration and isospin randomization processes are the dominant mechanisms affecting the evolution of these fluctuations.

## Method

The authors initialize a spherical hadronic system by coupling ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) cumulants to cumulants from the 3d Ising model, then generate net and total particle number distributions using the principle of maximum entropy. These distributions are evolved using the SMASH hadronic transport approach, which implements resonance formation and decay, inelastic 2-to-2 scatterings, and string formation based on experimentally measured cross-sections. The evolution of critical fluctuations is systematically studied for various temperatures and chemical potentials along a freeze-out curve, with different sets of particles coupled to the critical mode and different coupling strengths.

## Key numbers

- Critical point location used: mu_B,cp = 0.39 GeV, T_cp approximately 0.148 GeV, with scaling sizes Delta T_c = 0.02 GeV and Delta mu_B,c = 0.42 GeV
- Coupling strengths studied: g_p = 2 and g_p = 4 for nucleons only; g_c = 2 for a larger set of hadronic species (denoted g_c = 2+)
- System initialized with sphere radius R = 9 fm and expansion parameter u_0 = 0.5 across the energy scan region sqrt(s) = 7 to 50 GeV
- Kinetic freeze-out reached at approximately t = 100 fm in the simplified spherical geometry
- At sqrt(s) = 15 GeV, resonance decays and formations dominate the collision chemistry, with inelastic 2-to-2 scatterings at approximately 1% of total collisions

## Context

This work extends the approach of Pradeep (2022) by using the principle of maximum entropy to convert critical equilibrium fluctuations into particle spectra for transport evolution, addressing the question of how critical fluctuations survive the dilute hadronic phase. It builds on the framework of Bluhm (2016) for coupling 3d Ising model cumulants to HRG cumulants, and on the stochastic isospin randomization approach of Kitazawa (2011, 2012). The study enables future investigations of how critical fluctuations evolve in more realistic heavy-ion collision geometries and how resonance regeneration processes, which had not been explicitly computed for critical fluctuations before, affect net-proton and net-baryon fluctuations measured in experiments.
