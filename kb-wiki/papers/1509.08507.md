# Relativistic, model-independent, multichannel $2\to2$ transition amplitudes in a finite volume

> We derive formalism for determining $\textbf{2} + \mathcal J \to \textbf{2}$ infinite-volume transition amplitudes from finite-volume matrix elements. Specifically, we present a relativistic, model-independent relation between finite-volume matrix elements of external currents and the physically observable infinite-volume matrix elements involving two-particle asymptotic states. The result presented holds for states composed of two scalar bosons. These can be identical or non-identical and, in the latter case, can be either degenerate or non-degenerate. We further accommodate any number of strongly-coupled two-scalar channels. This formalism will, for example, allow future lattice QCD calculations of the $ρ$-meson form factor, in which the unstable nature of the $ρ$ is rigorously accommodated.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic, model-independent relation between finite-volume matrix elements of external currents and infinite-volume \( \textbf{2} + \mathcal J \rightarrow \textbf{2} \) transition amplitudes, but the result holds only for states composed of two scalar bosons and is valid only when the center-of-mass energy is below the lowest multi-particle threshold, up to exponentially suppressed corrections of the form \( e^{-mL} \).

## Method

The authors analyze finite-volume two- and three-point correlation functions in a generic relativistic scalar quantum field theory, using a skeleton expansion summed to all orders in perturbation theory. They group all finite-volume effects into known kinematic functions and express the finite-volume correlator in terms of these functions together with infinite-volume on-shell observables: the \( \textbf{2}\rightarrow \textbf{2} \) scattering amplitude \( \mathcal M \), the \( \textbf{1} + \mathcal J \rightarrow \textbf{1} \) transition amplitude \( w \), and a subtracted divergence-free \( \textbf{2} + \mathcal J \rightarrow \textbf{2} \) transition amplitude \( \mathcal{W}_{\rm df} \). Equating the diagrammatic and spectral representations of the finite-volume correlator yields the relation between finite-volume matrix elements and infinite-volume transition amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The relation holds up to exponentially suppressed corrections of the form \( e^{-mL} \), where \( m \) is the physical mass of the lightest scalar in the theory.
* The finite-volume matrix element squared is given by \( \frac{1}{L^6} \mathrm{Tr}[ \mathcal R(E_{n_i}, \mathbf P_i) \mathcal{W}_{L,{\rm df}}(P_i,P_f,L) \mathcal R(E_{n_f}, \mathbf P_f) \mathcal{W}_{L,{\rm df}}(P_f,P_i,L) ] \), where \( L \) is the linear extent of the cubic periodic volume.
* The formalism accommodates any number of strongly-coupled two-scalar channels, with identical or non-identical particles that can be either degenerate or non-degenerate.

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously developed for \( \textbf{1}+\mathcal J \rightarrow \textbf{2} \) transition processes (Briceno and Hansen, 2014, 2015) to the more complicated \( \textbf{2}+\mathcal J \rightarrow \textbf{2} \) case, which involves kinematic singularities from intermediate on-shell propagators and new finite-volume functions from two-particle loops with current insertions. The result enables future lattice QCD calculations of resonant form factors, such as the \( \rho \)-meson form factor, and can be applied to parity-violating two-nucleon processes and studies of two-particle QCD states in fixed background fields.
