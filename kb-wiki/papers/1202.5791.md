# Equation of State for Proto-Neutron Star

> Physics of the Equation of State (EoS) for proto-neutron star (PNS) concerns properties of neutron rich matter at finite temperature over wide range of densities. In this contribution we discuss the structure of PNS inner crust in a relativisitc mean filed model with spherical Wigner-Setiz approximation, and the composition of matter around neutrino-sphere in PNS in a virial expansion of non-ideal gas composed of nucleons and nuclei. We go on to discuss several new complete EoS for PNS and supernova, whose detailed composition is important for the neutrino dynamics. We focus on one important distinction for various EoS - the density dependence of symmetry energy E'sym, and its interesting correlation with the radii of neutron star, as well as properties of neutron distribution in neutron rich nuclei. Improved understanding of E'sym from terrestrial experiment on neutron distribution of neutron rich nuclei, benchmark calculations via ab initio methods, and statistical analysis on good quality observational data will advance our knowledge on EoS.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new complete equation of state (EoS) for proto-neutron stars and supernovae that combines a relativistic mean field model for non-uniform matter at intermediate densities with a virial expansion for low-density matter, but the spherical Wigner-Seitz approximation used for the inner crust is a simplification that does not capture the full three-dimensional "pasta" phases predicted by more complex models.

## Method

The inner crust structure was calculated using a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with density-dependent scalar meson-nucleon coupling, solving the Dirac equations for nucleons self-consistently inside spherical Wigner-Seitz cells with a body-centered cubic lattice for Coulomb energy. The composition around the neutrino-sphere was treated via a virial expansion of a nonideal gas composed of neutrons, protons, alpha particles, and 8980 species of heavy nuclei (A ≥ 12) with masses from the finite-range droplet model, including second-order virial corrections from scattering phase shift data. The two approaches were matched using a thermodynamically consistent interpolation scheme to generate a complete EoS table covering a wide range of densities, temperatures, and proton fractions.

## Key numbers

- The critical baryon density for transition from normal nucleus to shell state at Yp = 0.4 and T = 1 MeV is 0.049 fm^-3
- The transition density from shell state to uniform matter at Yp = 0.4 and T = 1 MeV is 0.090 fm^-3
- The virial EoS covers density range nB = 10^-8 to 0.1 fm^-3, temperature range T = 0.158 to 15.8 MeV, and proton fraction range Yp = 0.05 to 0.56
- The complete EoS table contains 73,840 data points from virial calculation, 17,021 from nonuniform Hartree calculation, and 90,478 from uniform matter calculations
- The overall calculations required 7,000 CPU days on supercomputer clusters
- For the inner crust and core, the calculation covers over 107,000 grid points in proton fraction range Yp = 0 to 0.56, temperature range T = 0.16 to 15.8 MeV for density range nB = 10^-4 to 1.6 fm^-3, and T = 15.8 to 80 MeV for nB = 10^-8 to 1.6 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the seminal crust calculations of Negele and Vautherin (1973) and the EoS tables of Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and H. Shen et al. (1998) by including full shell structure effects and a distribution of 8980 heavy nuclei species rather than a single-nucleus approximation. The new EoS tables enable more realistic simulations of proto-neutron star evolution and supernova dynamics, particularly for neutrino-matter interactions where the detailed composition around the neutrino-sphere affects emitted neutrino spectra and the subsequent nucleosynthesis in neutrino-driven winds.
