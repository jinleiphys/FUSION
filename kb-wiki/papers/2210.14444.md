# Effects of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations on inclusive electron scattering

> The nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation NN-SRC is one of the key issues of nuclear physics, which typically manifest themselves in high-momentum components of the nuclear momentum distributions. In this letter, the nuclear spectral functions based on the axially deformed relativistic mean-field model are developed to involve the NN-SRC. With the spectral functions, the inclusive electron scattering $ (e,e^{\prime}) $ cross sections are calculated within the PWIA framework, including the quasi-elastic (QE) part and $ Δ$ production part. Especially in the $ Δ$ production region, we reconsider the electromagnetic structures of the nucleon resonance $ Δ$(1232) and the scattering mechanisms, thereby the theoretical calculations are improved effectively and the cross sections are well consistent with the experimental data. The theoretical $ (e,e^{\prime}) $ cross sections are further divided into NN-SRC and mean-field contributions. It is found that, at the kinematics $ 0.5 \,{\rm GeV}^{2}<Q^{2}<1 \,{\rm GeV}^{2} $, the QE peak and $ Δ$ production peak not only reflect the mean-field structure, but also are sensitive to the NN-SRC information. Finally, we provide a new method to extract the strengths of NN-SRC from experimental cross sections for selected nuclei at the suitable kinematics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that inclusive electron scattering cross sections in the quasi-elastic (QE) and Delta production regions at squared four-momentum transfer \(0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{2}<Q^{2}<1\ \mathrm{GeV}^{2}\) are sensitive to nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (NN-SRC), not just mean-field structure, and that this sensitivity provides a new method to extract NN-SRC strengths from experimental data, provided the appropriate form factors and decay width for the Delta(1232) resonance are used.

## Method

The authors constructed nuclear spectral functions by combining a mean-field part from the axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with a correlation part from the light-front dynamics (LFD) method, which rescales the deuteron momentum distribution. Using these spectral functions within the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), they calculated inclusive electron scattering cross sections for the quasi-elastic and Delta production processes, employing form factors for the nucleon and Delta(1232) and a Lorentzian decay width for the Delta. The theoretical cross sections were then separated into mean-field and NN-SRC contributions, and the NN-SRC strength parameter \(C_A\) was extracted by comparing calculations with experimental data for several nuclei at kinematics where \(Q^{2}\) ranges from 0.5 to 1 GeV\(^{2}\).

## Key numbers

*   For \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\), the NN-SRC strength parameter was extracted as \(C_A = 4.5\), corresponding to a high-momentum nucleon proportion of \(Y = 21.4\%\) of the total nucleon number.
*   For \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\), the extracted NN-SRC strength was \(C_A = 4.2\), corresponding to \(Y = 22.4\%\).
*   For \(^{27}\mathrm{Al}\), the extracted NN-SRC strength was \(C_A = 5.3\), corresponding to \(Y = 25.5\%\).
*   For \(^{186}\mathrm{W}\), the extracted NN-SRC strength was \(C_A = 5.0\), corresponding to \(Y = 20.2\%\).
*   The QE peak location was calculated as \(\omega_{QE} = 0.33\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and the Delta production peak location as \(\omega_{\Delta} = 0.62\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for the kinematics considered.
*   At the kinematics \(E_k = 1.108\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(\theta = 37.5^{\circ}\) for \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\), the NN-SRC contribution to the total cross section was about \(16\%\), while the proportion of high-momentum nucleons was \(21.4\%\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of NN-SRC in inclusive electron scattering, which previously extracted SRC strengths from the plateau of cross-section ratios at \(Q^{2}>2\ \mathrm{GeV}^{2}\) and \(1.5<x_B<2\) (Frankfurt 1993, Egiyan 2006, Fomin 2012). The authors propose a complementary method using kinematics at lower \(Q^{2}\) (0.5 to 1 GeV\(^{2}\)) where the QE and Delta peaks are prominent, allowing the extraction of SRC strengths while also probing mean-field structure. The extracted proportions of high-momentum nucleons (approximately 20% for medium and heavy nuclei) verify ab initio calculations by Lyu (2020) and are consistent with results from exclusive electron scattering by Subedi (2008) and Hen (2014). This approach enables future studies to distinguish nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus scattering and to better understand strong interactions in the quark confinement domain.
