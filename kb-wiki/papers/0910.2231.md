# No Radial Excitations in Low Energy QCD. II. The Shrinking Radius of Hadrons

> We discuss the implications of our prior results obtained in our companion paper [arXiv:0910.2229]. Inescapably, they lead to three laws governing the size of hadrons, including in particular protons and neutrons that make up the bulk of ordinary matter: a) there are no radial excitations in low-energy QCD; b) the size of a hadron is largest in its ground state; c) the hadron's size shrinks when its orbital excitation increases. The second and third laws follow from the first law. It follows that the path from confinement to asymptotic freedom is a Regge trajectory. It also follows that the top quark is a free, albeit short-lived, quark. # No Radial Excitations in Low Energy QCD. II. The Shrinking Radius of Hadrons

## Key claim
The paper argues that three laws governing hadron size follow inescapably from the author's prior classification of the hadron spectrum: there are no radial excitations in low-energy QCD; the hadron radius is largest in its ground state; and the radius shrinks with increasing orbital excitation. The claim is conditional on the validity of the author's extended quark model that includes diquarks as building blocks on equal footing with quarks, with no assumptions about interquark interactions.

## Method
The author extends the quark model to include diquarks as building blocks, derived from hadron phenomenology: flavor-antisymmetric diquarks (the 3_f of SU(3)_f) for mesons and color-antisymmetric diquarks (the 3_c of SU(3)_c) for baryons. Using this model, the meson spectrum is reclassified into quark-antiquark and diquark-antidiquark states, and the baryon spectrum is reclassified such that the Roper resonances become two-diquark-plus-antiquark states with orbital excitations. The absence of any radial quantum number in this classification leads to the three laws, which are then compared to atomic physics and applied to Regge trajectories.

## Key numbers
- Pion (pi^+/-) ground state radius: 0.672 fm at mass 140 MeV (from PDG)
- Kaon (K^+/-) ground state radius: 0.560 fm at mass 494 MeV (from PDG)
- Proton ground state radius: 0.87 fm at mass 938 MeV (from PDG)
- Sigma^- ground state radius: 0.78 fm at mass 1197 MeV (from PDG)
- Delta baryon radii from lattice QCD: 0.650 fm at 1382 MeV, 0.632 fm at 1425 MeV, 0.614 fm at 1470 MeV
- Top quark mass: over 170 GeV
- Regge trajectory equation: m^2 = a + sigma L, where sigma is the slope

## Context
This paper extends the author's companion work (Friedmann, 2009, arXiv:0910.2229) which classified the hadron spectrum using diquarks. The results contradict the common assumption in bag models (Johnson, 1975), flux-tube models (Isgur and Paton; Nambu, 1974), and potential models (Badalian, 2002) that orbital excitations increase hadron size via a centrifugal barrier. The author claims consistency with lattice QCD results showing color string breaking around 1 fm (Bali, 2005) and with experimental observations including muonic hydrogen experiments (Pohl; Antognini) showing a smaller proton radius, HERMES data on rho meson shrinkage, and BELLE discovery of Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) states. The work enables a proposed explanation for the transition from confinement to asymptotic freedom along Regge trajectories, and suggests the top quark is a free quark residing in the asymptotically free regime.
