# $I = 3$ three-pion scattering amplitude from lattice QCD

> We analyze the spectrum of two- and three-pion states of maximal isospin obtained recently for isosymmetric QCD with pion mass $M\approx 200\;$MeV in Ref. [1]. Using the relativistic three-particle quantization condition, we find $\sim 2 σ$ evidence for a nonzero value for the contact part of the three-$π^+$ ($I=3$) scattering amplitude. We also compare our results to leading-order chiral perturbation theory. We find good agreement at threshold, and some tension in the energy dependent part of the three-$π^+$scattering amplitude. We also find that the two-$π^+$ ($I=2$) spectrum is fit well by an $s$-wave phase shift that incorporates the expected Adler zero.

## Key claim

The paper finds approximately 2σ statistical evidence for a nonzero contact part of the three-π⁺ (I=3) scattering amplitude, but this significance is marginal and the result is consistent with leading-order chiral perturbation theory predictions within uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of 16 two-π⁺ and 11 three-π⁺ energy levels obtained from lattice QCD with pion mass M≈200 MeV, using the relativistic three-particle quantization condition (RFT formalism). They parametrized the two-particle s-wave phase shift using an Adler-zero form and the three-particle K matrix (Kdf) using an isotropic expansion in Δ = (E*² − 9M²)/(9M²), then fitted these parameters simultaneously to the finite-volume spectrum from both rest and moving frames.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: M ≈ 200 MeV
- Box size: ML ≈ 4.2
- Two-particle scattering length from fit 1: M a₀ = 0.089(6)
- Two-particle effective range from fit 1: M² r a₀ = 2.63(8)
- LO chiral prediction for scattering length: M a₀ = 0.0938(12)
- Constant Kiso from global fit 4: M² Kiso_zero = 270(160)
- d-wave scattering length: (M a₂)⁵ = 0.0006(2)
- LO chiral prediction for Kdf: M² Kdf = 360 + 540Δ

## Context

This work extends the RFT three-particle formalism (Hansen and Sharpe, 2014-2015; Briceno, Hansen, and Sharpe, 2017-2018) to analyze multiple excited states in moving frames for the first time, using lattice data from Horz et al. (2019). It provides the first determination of the three-π⁺ contact interaction from lattice QCD and enables future studies of resonant three-particle systems, such as the Roper resonance and three-nucleon interactions.
