# Analysis of data on low energy piN-->pipiN reaction. I. Total cross sections

> This is the first of a series of papers on a consistent model independent analysis of the complete experimental information on the reaction $πN \rightarrow ππN$ at pion momenta up to 500 MeV/c. The paper summarizes the theoretical approach and details of the computational procedure. The complete database on total cross sections in 5 $ππN$ channels is given together with a critical discussion of their model independent analysis.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent phenomenological analysis of total cross sections for the reaction πN → ππN at pion momenta up to 500 MeV/c, but the authors explicitly caution that the restricted dataset on total cross sections alone cannot quantitatively fix all 21 free parameters of their amplitude; the analysis is instead intended to assess the internal consistency of the database and the comparative influence of different reaction mechanisms.

## Method

The authors decompose the reaction amplitude into three components: a smooth background (B) approximated by linear polynomials with 11 free parameters, the one-pion-exchange (OPE) graph (Π) with 4 free parameters describing ππ interaction, and the Δ-isobar contribution (Δ) with 6 free parameters from effective Lagrangians with Breit-Wigner propagators. They fit this 21-parameter amplitude to 105 total cross section data points across 5 channels (π⁻π⁺n, π⁻π⁰p, π⁰π⁰n, π⁺π⁰p, π⁺π⁺n) using the FUMILI minimization program, performing separate fits with subsets of the amplitude components to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses 105 total cross section data points in 5 channels at pion laboratory momenta P_lab ≤ 500 MeV/c
- The full amplitude contains 21 free parameters: 11 for background (A₁–A₁₁), 4 for OPE (G₀–G₃), and 6 for Δ contribution (g_πNΔ, g_πΔΔ, f₀, f₁, g₀, g₁)
- The πNN coupling constant is expressed as g = (2m/μ)f, with f²/4π ≈ 0.08
- One data point (point no. 12) was excluded from the analysis from the beginning as obviously inconsistent
- A fit is considered good when χ̄² ≈ 1, where χ̄² = (1/M) Σ χ_i² with M = N minus number of fit parameters

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Vereshagin et al. (1994) by explicitly including the Δ-isobar contribution, which becomes important at momenta around 0.3 GeV/c (initial state) and 0.5 GeV/c (final state). The authors aim to address limitations of the Olsson-Turner (OT) model used by the OMICRON group, which found that a single parameter ξ was insufficient to describe low-energy data. The analysis is the first in a planned series toward a complete model-independent analysis of all experimental information on πN → ππN, with subsequent papers intended to include differential cross sections.
