# Pion-nucleon scattering in a Bethe-Salpeter approach

> A covariant model of elastic pion-nucleon scattering based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation is presented. We obtain a good description of the S- and P-wave phase shifts up to 360 MeV laboratory energy. We also compare results from the K-matrix approach and several 3-dimensional quasipotential equations to the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant model of elastic pion-nucleon scattering that solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation without approximations to the relative-energy dependence of the kernel, obtaining a good description of S- and P-wave phase shifts up to 360 MeV laboratory energy, but notes that when the Pascalutsa pi N Delta vertex is used there is some disagreement in the P11 partial wave, suggesting the spin-1/2 components of the Rarita-Schwinger Delta propagator are necessary for a good fit.

## Method

The authors construct a meson-exchange model based on an effective hadronic Lagrangian with pi, N, Delta, sigma, and rho exchanges, then solve the full four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation for elastic pi N scattering without reducing the relative-energy dependence. They expand the nucleon propagator into positive and negative energy components, perform a partial wave decomposition to obtain two-dimensional integral equations, and apply a Wick rotation to avoid kernel singularities. Two different pi N Delta vertices (conventional with off-mass-shell parameter x_Delta and Pascalutsa) and two form factor parameterizations (model I with a cutoff per vertex, model II with a form factor only on the pion propagator) are compared.

## Key numbers

* The pi N N coupling constant was fixed at g_piNN^2/4pi = 13.5 in all fits.
* The conventional pi N Delta vertex gave f_piNDelta^2/4pi = 0.365 for both model I and model II fits.
* The Pascalutsa pi N Delta vertex gave f_piNDelta^2/4pi = 0.741 (model I) and 0.63 (model II), roughly twice the empirical value of 0.36 from Delta -> pi + N decay.
* The sigma meson mass m_sigma was fitted as 0.65 GeV (conventional model I), 0.62 GeV (conventional model II), 0.69 GeV (Pascalutsa model I), and 1.12 GeV (Pascalutsa model II).
* Scattering lengths and volumes (in units of m_pi^{-(2l+1)}) from the BSE with conventional vertex: S11 = 0.177, S31 = -0.101, P11 = -0.083, P13 = -0.032, P31 = -0.041, P33 = 0.178, compared to SM95 values S11 = 0.175, S31 = -0.087, P11 = -0.068, P13 = -0.022, P31 = -0.039, P33 = 0.209.

## Context

This work extends earlier dynamical models of pion-nucleon scattering (e.g., Hung, Yang, and Lee (2001)) that used approximations to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, by solving the full covariant equation without relative-energy approximations. The authors compare their BSE results to the K-matrix approach and four three-dimensional quasipotential equations (Cohen, Salpeter, Blankenbecler-Sugar, and Cooper-Jennings), finding that in the P33 partial wave the Blankenbecler-Sugar, Cooper-Jennings, and Salpeter equations produce so much attraction that the Delta(1232) resonance becomes a bound state, while the Cohen equation agrees well with the BSE. The model can be extended to higher energies by including resonances and inelastic channels, or to pion photoproduction by including the photon.
