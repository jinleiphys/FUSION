# Two-photon exchange correction to the Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting of S levels

> We evaluate the two-photon exchange corrections to the Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting of S states in electronic hydrogen relying on modern experimental data and present the two-photon exchange on a neutron inside the electronic and muonic atoms. These results are relevant for the precise extraction of the isotope shift as well as in the analysis of the ground state hyperfine splitting in usual and muonic hydrogen.

## Key claim

The paper provides a first complete dispersive calculation of the alpha^5 two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift in electronic hydrogen, but the results are conditional on the use of modern experimental data for proton form factors and structure functions, and the main uncertainty remains due to limited knowledge of the forward Compton scattering subtraction function.

## Method

The author evaluates the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the Lamb shift as a sum of Born, subtraction, and inelastic contributions, using dispersive integrals over the unpolarized proton structure functions F1 and F2 and the forward Compton scattering subtraction function. For the hyperfine splitting, the correction is separated into Zemach, recoil, and polarizability terms, with the spin-dependent inelastic structure functions g1 and g2 integrated over photon energy and virtuality. The calculations rely on experimental form factor parametrizations from Bernauer (2010, 2013), structure function fits from Bosted (2007) and Christy (2007), and the proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

- Total TPE correction to the 1S Lamb shift in electronic hydrogen: -109.2(12.0) Hz
- Total TPE correction to the 1S Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen: -248.8(22.3) micro-eV
- Total TPE correction to the 1S Lamb shift from neutron in electronic atoms: -43.5(13.2) Hz
- Total TPE correction to the 1S Lamb shift from neutron in muonic atoms: -57.8(18.1) micro-eV
- Total TPE correction to the 1S hyperfine splitting in electronic hydrogen: -46.27(1.09) kHz
- Total TPE correction to the 1S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen: -1131(24) micro-eV
- Updated absolute value of 1S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen: 182.625 +/- 0.012 meV
- Updated absolute value of 2S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen: 22.8132 +/- 0.0015 meV

## Context

This work extends previous dispersive calculations of TPE corrections by Pachucki (1996), Carlson (2011), and Tomalak (2015, 2017) by providing the first complete evaluation for electronic hydrogen and updating results for muonic hydrogen. The results are relevant for resolving the proton charge radius puzzle and for precise extraction of isotope shifts, as well as for analyzing future measurements of 1S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen planned by Pohl (2016) and others. The main uncertainty comes from the forward Compton scattering subtraction function, which could be improved by chiral perturbation theory predictions from Birse (2012) and Alarcon (2013), and by better measurements of the nucleon magnetic polarizability.
