# Hidden Charm Pentaquark $P_c(4380)$ and Doubly Charmed Baryon $螢_{cc}^*(4380)$ as Hadronic Molecule States

> We study hadronic molecular states in a coupled system of $J/蠄N - 螞_c\bar{D}^{(*)} - 危_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ in $I(J^P) = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^-)$ channel, using the complex scaling method combined with the Gaussian expansion method. We construct the potential including one pion exchange and one $D^{(*)}$ meson exchange with $S$-wave orbital angular momentum. We find that the both mass and width of the pentaquark $P_c(4380)$ can be reproduced within a reasonable parameter region, and that its main decay mode is $螞_c\bar{D}^*$. We extend our analysis to a coupled system of $螞_c D^{(*)} - 危_c^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ in $I(J^P) = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^-)$ channel. We find that there exists a doubly charmed baryon of $ccqq\bar{q}$ type as a hadronic molecule, the mass and width of which are quite close to those of $P_c(4380)$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the hidden charm pentaquark \(P_c(4380)\) can be reproduced as an S-wave hadronic molecular state in a coupled-channel analysis, but only for a specific parameter region (cutoff \(\Lambda = 1000\) MeV and coupling \(g_1 = 0.753\)) where no additional unobserved lighter state appears; for larger cutoff values a second, lighter resonance emerges that has not been seen experimentally.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel Schrodinger equation analysis using the complex scaling method combined with the Gaussian expansion method for the \(I(J^P) = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^-)\) channel. They constructed a \(5 \times 5\) potential matrix including one-pion exchange and one-\(D^{(*)}\) meson exchange with S-wave orbital angular momentum for the channels \(J/\psi N\), \(\Lambda_c \bar{D}^{(*)}\), \(\Sigma_c^{(*)} \bar{D}^{(*)}\). They then extended the same one-pion exchange potential to a \(4 \times 4\) system for \(\Lambda_c D^{(*)} - \Sigma_c^{(*)} D^{(*)}\) to study a doubly charmed baryon.

## Key numbers

* The mass of \(P_c(4380)\) from LHCb is \(4380 \pm 8 \pm 29\) MeV and its width is \(205 \pm 18 \pm 86\) MeV.
* For \(\Lambda = 1000\) MeV and \(g_1 = 0.753\), the calculated \(P_c(4380)\) resonance has mass \(4390.2\) MeV and width \(218\) MeV (from \(4390.2 - i109\)).
* For the same parameters (\(\Lambda = 1000\) MeV, \(g_1 = 0.753\)), the doubly charmed baryon \(\Xi_{cc}^*(4380)\) has mass \(4370.1\) MeV and width \(137.4\) MeV (from \(4370.1 - i68.7\)).
* The \(\Sigma_c^* \bar{D}\) threshold is at \(4385.3\) MeV and the \(\Lambda_c \bar{D}^*\) threshold is at \(4295.1\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecule picture of the \(P_c(4380)\) pentaquark observed by LHCb (Aaij 2015) by including the \(J/\psi N\) channel in a full coupled-channel analysis, which had not been done previously. It builds on prior studies by Shimizu (2016) that considered \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D} - \Sigma_c\bar{D}^*\) coupling, and by Yamaguchi (2017) that showed the importance of \(\Lambda_c\bar{D}^{(*)}\) channels. The paper predicts a new doubly charmed baryon \(\Xi_{cc}^*(4380)\) as a hadronic molecule with mass and width close to those of \(P_c(4380)\), which could be searched for in future experiments.
