# Mass Effect in Single Flavor Color Superconductivity

> The nonzero strange quark mass effect in different types of single flavor color superconductivity and the phase diagram in a magnetic field are studied. We have obtained simple analytical forms of the quasi-particle energies for an arbitrary mass and explored the mass correction to the pressure and the transition temperature. It is found that the mass reduces the pressure and transition temperature of strange quarks, but it doesn't change the ranking $P_n<P_{\rm A}<P_{\rm polar}<P_{\rm planar}<P_{\rm CSL}$ of the pressure for the four canonical single flavor phases. The phase diagram with magnetic field and temperature for a system of three flavors is obtained for two different values of the strange quark mass. The changes from the one obtained previously under the approximation of massless strange quarks are examined.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a nonzero strange quark mass reduces the pressure and transition temperature of strange quarks in single-flavor color superconductivity, but it does not alter the pressure ranking among the four canonical phases (CSL, planar, polar, A, and normal). The phase diagram in a magnetic field is obtained for two values of the strange quark mass, revealing that the region where only u and d quarks condense is tiny for \(m_s \sim 150\) MeV but cannot be ignored for \(m_s \sim \mu\).

## Method

The authors formulate single-flavor color superconductivity for an arbitrary quark mass using an NJL-like effective action and apply the mean-field approximation. They diagonalize the gap matrix analytically for the four canonical phases (CSL, planar, polar, and A) and compute the quasi-particle energies, pressure, and transition temperature as functions of the mass parameter \(\lambda = m/\mu\). The phase diagram in a magnetic field is obtained by minimizing the Gibbs free energy, accounting for the Meissner effect in the CSL phase and the incomplete Meissner effect in nonspherical phases, with constraints from the mixing angle between the electromagnetic and color fields.

## Key numbers

- For \(\mu = 500\) MeV, \(\lambda = 0.3\) corresponds to \(m = 150\) MeV and \(\lambda = 0.6\) to \(m = 300\) MeV.
- The transition temperature for strange quarks is \(T_c^{0.3} = 0.98 T_c^0\) for \(m_s = 150\) MeV and \(T_c^{0.6} = 0.683 T_c^0\) for \(m_s = 300\) MeV, with a UV cutoff \(K = 27\) MeV.
- At \(T = 0\) and \(\lambda = 0\), the scaled condensation energies are \(\rho_{\rm CSL} = 1\), \(\rho_{\rm planar} = 0.98\), \(\rho_{\rm polar} = 0.88\), and \(\rho_{\rm A} = 0.65\).
- In the non-relativistic limit, \(\rho_{\rm polar} = \rho_{\rm planar} = 2\rho_{\rm A} = \frac{2^{4/3}}{3}\rho_{\rm CSL}\).
- The pressure ranking is \(P_n < P_{\rm A} < P_{\rm polar} < P_{\rm planar} < P_{\rm CSL}\) for all \(0 \le \lambda < 1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Wu, Hou, and Ren, 2010) of single-flavor color superconductivity in a magnetic field, which considered only the massless and infinitely massive limits for strange quarks. It also builds on earlier studies of spin-1 color superconductivity by Schmitt, Wang, and Rischke (2002) and by T. Schäfer (2000), which established the pressure ranking in the massless limit. The results enable more realistic modeling of quark matter in compact stars, where the strange quark mass is comparable to the chemical potential, and clarify the phase diagram for three-flavor systems under magnetic fields.
