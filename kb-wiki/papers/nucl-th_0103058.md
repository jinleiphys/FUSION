# Polarization observables in p-d scattering below 30 MeV

> Differential and total breakup cross sections as well as vector and tensor analyzing powers for p-d scattering are studied for energies above the deuteron breakup threshold up to E(lab)=28 MeV. The p-d scattering wave function is expanded in terms of the correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis and the elastic S-matrix is obtained using the Kohn variational principle in its complex form. The effects of the Coulomb interaction, which are expected to be important in this energy range, have been rigorously taken into account. The Argonne AV18 interaction and the Urbana URIX three-nucleon potential have been used to perform a comparison to the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The Kohn Variational Principle (KVP) in its complex form can be extended to calculate elastic p-d scattering observables above the deuteron breakup threshold up to 28 MeV, with the Coulomb interaction rigorously included, but the agreement with experimental data for differential cross sections worsens compared to lower energies and the three-nucleon force (URIX) has negligible impact on the total breakup cross section and only small effects on differential cross sections in this energy range.

## Method

The p-d scattering wave function was expanded in a correlated hyperspherical harmonic (CHH) basis, with the elastic S-matrix obtained via the complex Kohn variational principle. The internal part of the wave function was expanded in Laguerre polynomials plus an auxiliary function matched to asymptotic outgoing solutions at a hyperradius ρ₀ > 100 fm, accounting for Coulomb distortion. Calculations used the Argonne AV18 two-nucleon potential with and without the Urbana URIX three-nucleon potential, and observables were computed from the S-matrix for all partial waves up to J_max = 19/2.

## Key numbers

- Total breakup cross section σ_b(p-d) calculated for energies from just above the deuteron breakup threshold up to E_lab = 28 MeV; the AV18 prediction agrees with data from Gruebler (1959) and Carlson (1973).
- Differential cross sections presented at nine energies: E_lab = 5, 7, 9, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 22.7, and 28 MeV.
- At E_lab = 18 MeV, the χ² per datum for differential cross section is 11 using AV18, and nearly the same value for AV18+URIX.
- Phase-shift and inelasticity parameters for n-d and p-d scattering using the Malfliet-Tjon s-wave potential were tested at E_lab = 14.1 and 42.0 MeV, with 16-20 Laguerre polynomials giving four-digit accuracy.
- The matching radius ρ₀ > 100 fm was required for S-matrix stability; the nonlinear parameter γ was varied in the range 1.5 to 2.5 fm⁻¹ with no change in results.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the complex KVP to p-d scattering at only E_lab = 5 and 10 MeV (Kievsky et al., 1999; Viviani et al., 2000) up to 28 MeV, covering the region where Coulomb effects are important. It provides a comparison to experimental data from Sagara (1994), Bruebler (1983), and Hatanaka (1984), and complements Faddeev equation approaches using screening and renormalization (Alt, 1980) and configuration-space methods (Kievsky et al., 2001). The calculated p-d wave functions above the deuteron breakup threshold enable future studies of radiative capture and photo/electrodisintegration of ³He, relevant to the pp solar chain and the hep process.
