# Pairing effects on vorticity of incident neutron current at quasiparticle resonance energies in $n$-$A$ elastic scattering

> In this study, we analyzed how the incident neutron current is affected by the pairing effect in the neutron-nucleus scattering described within the framework of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory by performing numerical calculations in terms of current, vorticity, and circulation of the incident neutron current. We found that the pairing effect on the incident neutron flux is completely different between particle-type and hole-type quasiparticle resonances. In the case of h-type quasiparticle resonance, the pairing acts to prevent the neutron flux from entering the nucleus, reducing circulation. In the case of p-type quasiparticle resonance, pairing acts to reduce circulation at energies lower than the resonance energy, but at energies higher than the resonance energy, the effect of pairing on the neutron flux is reversed and, conversely, circulation is increased.

## Key claim

The pairing effect on incident neutron flux in neutron-nucleus elastic scattering is completely different between particle-type and hole-type quasiparticle resonances, with the effect depending on whether the incident energy is below or above the resonance energy for p-type resonances, while for h-type resonances pairing consistently reduces circulation by preventing neutron flux from entering the nucleus.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation assuming spherical symmetry, using a Woods-Saxon potential for the mean field and a pair potential with the same parameters as in their prior work (Mizuyama et al., Jost-HFB paper). They computed the neutron current, vorticity, and circulation from the upper component of the HFB scattering wave function for neutron-nucleus elastic scattering, decomposing the current into a Hartree-Fock part and a pairing part. The incident neutron direction was taken as the z-axis, and calculations were performed in the z-x plane with partial waves up to l_max = 20 to represent plane waves up to 20 MeV in a 20 fm region.

## Key numbers

- Chemical potential lambda = -1 MeV was adopted to set the neutron-rich target of the system.
- The target nucleus size is given by r_0 A^(1/3) = 3.66 fm (black solid semicircle in figures).
- The mean pairing gap <Delta> = 3.0 MeV was used for the main calculations shown in Figs. 1 and 3.
- The critical pairing gap for the p-type p_{3/2} resonance is <Delta>_c = 4.08 MeV, above which the K-matrix pole disappears.
- The p-type f_{7/2} quasiparticle resonance has real part of S-matrix pole at epsilon = 1.45 MeV and half width (imaginary part) of 1.16 x 10^(-2) MeV.
- The h-type d_{3/2} resonance energy is epsilon = 3.94 MeV (with lambda = -1.0 MeV and <Delta> = 3.0 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies of resonances in neutron-nucleus scattering within HFB theory (Mizuyama et al., Jost-HFB paper; classres paper), where they discussed formation conditions of quasiparticle resonances and the effects of pair correlation in terms of S- and K-matrix poles. The analysis of vorticity and circulation provides a new diagnostic for pairing effects that complements the earlier pole analysis, and the authors show that the disappearance of the K-matrix pole due to correlation with an independent K-matrix pole (discussed in classres paper) manifests as the disappearance of the energy where Gamma_pair = 0. This work enables future derivation of fluid equations from the HFB equation to discuss quantum pressure terms and violations of Kelvin's circulation theorem.
