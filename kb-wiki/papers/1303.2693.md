# Dyson-Schwinger approach to color superconductivity at finite temperature and density

> We investigate the phases of dense QCD matter at finite temperature with Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator for N_f=2+1 flavors. For the gluon propagator we take a fit to quenched lattice data and add quark-loop effects perturbatively in a hard-thermal-loop--hard-dense-loop approximation. We consider 2SC and CFL-like pairing with chiral up and down quarks and massive strange quarks and present results for the condensates and the phase diagram. We find a dominant CFL phase at chemical potentials larger than 500-600 MeV. At lower values of the chemical potential we find a 2SC phase, which also exists in a small band at higher temperatures for larger chemical potentials. With values of 20-30 MeV, the critical temperatures to the normal phase turn out to be quite small.

## Key claim

The authors find that, contrary to earlier Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Nickel 2006) which predicted the CFL phase dominates down to very low chemical potentials, using a more realistic gluonic input (a fit to quenched lattice data with perturbative quark-loop effects) yields a 2SC phase at intermediate chemical potentials and low temperatures, with the CFL phase only becoming dominant above chemical potentials of 500-600 MeV.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation for N_f=2+1 flavors at finite temperature and chemical potential using the Matsubara formalism, with a dressed quark-gluon vertex ansatz and a gluon propagator taken from a fit to quenched lattice data (Fischer 2010) with quark-loop effects added perturbatively in hard-thermal-loop--hard-dense-loop (HTL-HDL) approximation. They expand the quark propagator and self-energy in color-flavor basis matrices appropriate for 2SC and CFL-like pairing patterns, with massless up and down quarks and massive strange quarks, and solve the coupled gap equations self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral phase transition at μ=0: approximately 150 MeV (used to fit vertex parameter d1=9.6 GeV²)
- CFL phase becomes dominant at chemical potentials larger than 500-600 MeV
- Critical temperatures to the normal phase: 20-30 MeV (described as "quite small")
- Strange-quark mass values used at renormalization point ν=100 GeV: 54 MeV and 30 MeV
- Vacuum mass function M(0) for chiral quarks: 710 MeV
- Vacuum mass function M(0) for strange quarks: 875 MeV (for m_s=30 MeV) and 970 MeV (for m_s=54 MeV)
- Up and down quark masses in CFL-like solutions: less than 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of color superconductivity at zero temperature (Nickel 2006, 2008) to finite temperature, and updates those calculations using a more realistic gluon propagator parametrization from lattice data (Fischer 2010) that yields stronger non-perturbative effects. The results contradict earlier DSE findings that showed no 2SC phase at low temperatures, and instead align more closely with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model studies (Buballa 2001) that found a 2SC phase at intermediate chemical potentials. The work enables future studies of electrically neutral matter and self-consistent treatments where the quark DSE solutions feed back into the gluon polarization tensor.
