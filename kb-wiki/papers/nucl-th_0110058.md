# Modern Dyson-Schwinger Equation Studies

> The dichotomy of the pion as QCD's Goldstone mode and a bound state of massive constituents is easily understood using the Dyson-Schwinger equations. That provides the foundation for an efficacious phenomenology, which correlates the pion's charge radius and electromagnetic form factor with its valence quark distribution function; and simultaneously provides a Poincare' covariant description of the nucleon, its form factors and, more recently, meson photoproduction processes. This well-constrained framework can also be used to eliminate candidates for an extension of the Standard Model by providing the relation between current-quark electric dipole moments and that of the neutron

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a single, symmetry-preserving framework that simultaneously explains the pion as both QCD's Goldstone mode and a bound state of massive constituent quarks, and this framework can be used to correlate observables across low- and high-energy regimes, but the model dependence is restricted to a statement about the infrared behaviour of the quark-quark interaction.

## Method

The authors employ the DSEs for the quark propagator (gap equation) and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, using a systematic, symmetry-preserving truncation scheme that respects Ward-Takahashi identities. For baryons, they use a quark-diquark Faddeev amplitude ansatz with parameters fitted to the proton's electromagnetic form factor, bypassing a full numerical solution of the Faddeev equation. The framework is then applied to calculate meson properties, nucleon form factors, meson photoproduction amplitudes, and the neutron's electric dipole moment.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate at 1 GeV: \(-\langle \bar q q \rangle^0_{1\,{\rm GeV}} = (0.241\,{\rm GeV})^3\), corresponding to a density of \(1.8\,{\rm fm}^{-3}\) and a sphere radius of \(r_{\langle \bar q q\rangle}=0.76\,{\rm fm}\)
- Fitted parameters for the nucleon Faddeev amplitude ansatz: scalar diquark mass \(m_{0^+}=0.62\,{\rm GeV}\), diquark width \(\omega_{0^+}=0.79\,{\rm GeV}\), quark-diquark width \(\omega_\psi=0.23\,{\rm GeV}\), giving length scales \(1/m_{0^+}=0.32\,{\rm fm}\), \(1/\omega_{0^+}=0.25\,{\rm fm}\), \(1/\omega_\psi=0.86\,{\rm fm}\)
- Calculated proton charge radius squared: \((0.78)^2\,{\rm fm}^2\) (observed: \((0.87)^2\,{\rm fm}^2\)); neutron charge radius squared: \(-(0.40)^2\,{\rm fm}^2\) (observed: \(-(0.34)^2\,{\rm fm}^2\))
- Calculated proton magnetic moment: \(2.85\,\mu_N\) (observed: \(2.79\,\mu_N\)); neutron magnetic moment: \(-1.61\,\mu_N\) (observed: \(-1.91\,\mu_N\))
- Calculated \(\pi NN\) coupling constant: \(g_{\pi NN}=13.9\) (observed: \(13.4\))
- Calculated axial coupling constant: \(g_A=0.98\) (observed: \(1.26\))
- Experimental upper bound on neutron electric dipole moment: \(|d_n| < 6.3 \times 10^{-26}\,e\,{\rm cm}\) at 90% C.L.
- Standard Model prediction for neutron EDM: \(d_n^{\rm SM} \lesssim 10^{-34}\,e\,{\rm cm}\)
- DSE-derived bound on the down-quark gyroelectric ratio: \(|h_d| < 7.4 \times 10^{-14}\), using the relation \(h_n \approx -91\,h_d\)

## Context

This work extends the DSE phenomenology developed by Roberts and Williams (1994), Maris and Roberts (1997), and Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1998), which proved Goldstone's theorem in QCD and derived quark-level Goldberger-Treiman relations. The framework contradicts the Drell-Yan experimental result for the pion's valence quark distribution function, which shows \(u^\pi_V(x) \propto (1-x)\) near \(x=1\), while the DSE calculation predicts \((1-x)^2\) in agreement with perturbative QCD expectations from Brodsky, Burkardt, and Schmidt (1995). The results enable quantitative improvement of meson exchange models for photoproduction and provide a model-independent constraint on extensions of the Standard Model via the neutron electric dipole moment, directly threatening the three-Higgs-boson model of spontaneous CP violation proposed by Weinberg.
