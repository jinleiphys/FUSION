# Microscopic Calculations of the Spectra of Light Nuclei

> We perform large-space shell-model calculations for the low-lying energy spectra of a few light nuclei, $^4\mbox{He}$, $^5\mbox{He}$, $^6\mbox{Li}$ and $^7\mbox{Li}$, in a no-core model space with a realistic effective two-body interaction (Brueckner G-matrix). Our G-matrices are calculated for the Reid-soft-core potential in a harmonic oscillator basis. Single-particle ``-U'' insertions are replaced by two-particle ``-U'' insertions and are included in the G matrix calculations, which sum them to all orders. With the starting energy of the G-matrix chosen to give approximately the experimental binding energy, we obtain nuclear energy spectra which are in reasonable agreement with experiment. We also investigate the dependence of our results on the size of the model space and on the harmonic oscillator basis parameter ($\hbarΩ$).

## Key claim

Large-space no-core shell-model calculations using a realistic effective two-body interaction (Brueckner G-matrix) can reproduce low-lying energy spectra of light nuclei in reasonable agreement with experiment, provided the starting energy of the G-matrix is chosen to give approximately the experimental binding energy; the results depend on the size of the model space and the harmonic oscillator basis parameter, but the qualitative features of the spectra are stable against moderate variations in these parameters.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in a no-core model space (up to four major shells) for ⁴He, ⁵He, ⁶Li, and ⁷Li, using a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the Reid-soft-core potential in a harmonic oscillator basis. Single-particle "-U" insertions were replaced by two-particle "-U" insertions and summed to all orders in the G-matrix calculation, and the center-of-mass kinetic energy was explicitly subtracted from the Hamiltonian with a constraint term to keep the system in its lowest center-of-mass state. The effective shell-model interaction was taken to be the G-matrix itself, neglecting three-body and higher-body effective interactions, and calculations were performed for model spaces of increasing size (N=2, 3, and 4 major shells) to study convergence.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of ⁴He in the N=4 calculation: -21.918 MeV (experimental value -28.3 MeV), with starting energy ω = 7 MeV and ℏΩ = 21 MeV.
- First excited 0⁺ state in ⁴He: 33.807 MeV above ground state (experimental value 20.21 MeV).
- Single-particle splitting (0p₁/₂ - 0p₃/₂) in ⁵He: 4.386 MeV (N=2), 5.182 MeV (N=3), 2.500 MeV (N=4), with ℏΩ = 18 MeV and ω = 18 MeV; experimental value is 4 ± 1 MeV.
- First J^π = 1/2⁺ state in ⁵He (N=4): 15.891 MeV above ground state.
- Ground-state energy of ⁶Li changes from -23.044 MeV (ω=20 MeV) to -26.044 MeV (ω=29 MeV) to -29.366 MeV (ω=38 MeV), while excitation energies change by less than 0.4 MeV per step (N=4, ℏΩ=18 MeV).
- First excited state (J^π = 1/2⁻) in ⁷Li: 0.335 MeV above ground state in N=4 calculation (experimental value 0.478 MeV), with ℏΩ = 16 MeV and ω = 24 MeV.
- Lowest T=3/2 state in ⁷Li: 10.322 MeV (N=2) and 11.348 MeV (N=3) (experimental value 11.25 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell-model approach to light nuclei using a realistic NN potential, building on earlier studies of effective interactions and G-matrix calculations by Barrett, Hewitt, and McCarthy (1971) and Vary and Yang (1977). It contrasts with phenomenological shell-model studies such as van Hees and Glaudemans (1984), who used empirically fitted interactions in a (0+1)ℏΩ model space, and with earlier small-space calculations by Barrett (1973) that gave excitation energies 9-15 MeV too high for odd-parity states in ⁴He. The results enable future work toward starting-energy-independent effective interactions via the Lee-Suzuki method and toward studies of the dependence on the choice of NN potential.
