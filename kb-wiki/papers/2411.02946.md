# Tetrahedral shape and Lambda impurity effect in $^{80}$Zr with a multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

> This study investigates the tetrahedral structure in $^{80}$Zr and Lambda ($Λ$) impurity effect in $^{81}_{~Λ}$Zr using the multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. The ground states of both $^{80}$Zr and $^{81}_{~Λ}$Zr exhibit a tetrahedral configuration, accompanied by prolate and axial-octupole shape isomers. Our calculations reveal there are changes in the deformation parameters $β_{20}$, $β_{30}$, and $β_{32}$ upon $Λ$ binding to $^{80}$Zr, except for $β_{32}$ when $Λ$ occupies $p$-orbits. Compared to the two shape isomers, the $Λ$ particle exhibits weaker binding energy in the tetrahedral state when occupying the $1/2^+[000](Λ_s)$ or $1/2^-[110]$ single-particle states. In contrast, the strongest binding occurs for the $Λ$ particle in the $1/2^-[101]$ state with tetrahedral shape. Besides, a large $Λ$ separation energy may not necessarily correlate with a significant overlap between the density distributions of the $Λ$ particle and the nuclear core, particularly for tetrahedral hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The ground states of both $^{80}$Zr and $^{81}_{\Lambda}$Zr exhibit a tetrahedral configuration, but this conclusion depends on the use of a model that fully relaxes both axial and reflection symmetry constraints; when only axial symmetry is imposed, the prolate shape appears as the ground state for some effective interactions.

## Method

The authors employed the multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDC-RHB) model, which self-consistently includes shape degrees of freedom represented by deformation parameters $\beta_{\lambda\mu}$ with even $\mu$ values under the intrinsic $V_4$ symmetry group. They calculated two-dimensional potential energy surfaces (PESs) for $^{80}$Zr and $^{81}_{\Lambda}$Zr under four symmetry classes: axial-reflection symmetry ($K\pi$), non-axial but reflection symmetry ($\slashed{K}\pi$), axial but reflection asymmetry ($K\slashed{\pi}$), and fully non-axial and reflection asymmetry ($\slashed{K}\slashed{\pi}$). For hypernuclei, the $\Lambda$ hyperon was placed in the lowest $s$-orbit and three $p$-orbits, with the equal filling approximation adopted for the single $\Lambda$ hyperon.

## Key numbers

- For $^{80}$Zr with PK1 interaction under $\slashed{K}\slashed{\pi}$ symmetry, the tetrahedral ground state has deformation parameters ($\beta_{20}=0.000$, $\beta_{30}=0.000$, $\beta_{32}=0.192$) and total energy $-668.336$ MeV, compared to the prolate minimum at ($\beta_{20}=0.491$, $\beta_{30}=0.000$, $\beta_{32}=0.000$) with energy $-667.061$ MeV.
- For $^{81}_{\Lambda}$Zr with PK1-Y1 interaction, the $\Lambda$ separation energy $S_\Lambda$ for the tetrahedral state is 22.040 MeV when the $\Lambda$ occupies the $1/2^+[000]$ ($\Lambda_s$) orbit, 15.746 MeV for the $1/2^-[110]$ orbit, and 15.625 MeV for the $1/2^-[101]$ orbit.
- For $^{81}_{\Lambda}$Zr with PK1-Y1 interaction in the prolate state, $S_\Lambda$ is 22.745 MeV for the $\Lambda_s$ orbit, 18.228 MeV for the $1/2^-[110]$ orbit, and 14.690 MeV for the $1/2^-[101]$ orbit.
- The root-mean-square charge radius $r_c$ of $^{80}$Zr in the tetrahedral state is 4.265 fm, compared to 4.326 fm in the prolate state (PK1 interaction).
- For $^{81}_{\Lambda}$Zr with PK1-Y1 in the tetrahedral state with $\Lambda_s$ orbit, $r_c$ is 4.263 fm, showing a shrinkage of 0.002 fm relative to the core.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of tetrahedral shapes in neutron-rich Zr isotopes using the MDC-CDFT framework (Zhao 2017) and hypernuclear impurity effects in axially symmetric and reflection-asymmetric nuclei (Lu 2011, Lu 2014, Rong 2021, Chen 2021). It directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical predictions of a tetrahedral ground state for $^{80}$Zr (Tagami 2014, Tagami 2015) and experimental observations suggesting a highly deformed non-tetrahedral shape (Lister 1987, Llewellyn 2020). The results enable future experimental searches for tetrahedral hypernuclei and provide a theoretical basis for distinguishing nuclear shapes through $\Lambda$ separation energy measurements.
