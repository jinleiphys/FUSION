# Light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions -- new possibilities

> Light-by-light scattering is a relatively new area of experimental physics. Our recent, theoretical research shows that studying two photon measurements in regions with lower transverse momentum ($p_{t,γ}$) and invariant mass ($M_{γγ}$) allows us to observe not only the main contribution of photon scattering, known as fermionic loops but also mechanisms like the VDM-Regge (double-photon hadronic fluctuation). In addition, diphoton measurements at low diphoton masses are crucial for studies of light meson resonance contributions in $γγ\to γγ$ scattering. We also focus on the interference between different contributions. For future experiments with the ALICE FoCal and ALICE-3 detectors, we have calculated background contamination and have explored possibilities to minimize their impact.

## Key claim

Studying light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at lower diphoton invariant mass and transverse momentum thresholds than used by current CMS and ATLAS experiments (which use thresholds of 5 GeV for mass and 2-2.5 GeV for transverse momentum) could reveal not only the dominant fermionic loop contribution but also the VDM-Regge mechanism and light meson resonances, though the VDM-Regge mechanism remains difficult to observe because current and planned detectors avoid the required rapidity region and its interference with the box contribution is destructive.

## Method

The authors calculated elementary cross sections for γγ → γγ scattering using Feynman diagrams for fermionic loops (via FormCalc and LoopTools), a VDM-Regge model with Pomeron and Reggeon exchange, and relativistic Breit-Wigner formulas for light meson resonances (π, η, η'). Nuclear cross sections for PbPb collisions were obtained using the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space with a smooth nuclear absorption factor S²(b) = exp(-σ_NN T_AA(b)) replacing the sharp-edge cutoff.

## Key numbers

- Diphoton invariant mass threshold for future FoCal detector: M_γγ > 500 MeV
- Photon rapidity range for FoCal: 3.4 < y_γ1/2 < 5.8
- Photon energy threshold for FoCal: E_t,γ > 200 MeV
- Photon energy threshold for ALICE-3: E_γ > 50 MeV
- Photon rapidity range for ALICE-3: y_γ1/2 ∈ (-4, 4)
- Interference between quarkish and leptonic loops estimated at around 20%
- Difference between sharp and smooth survival factor approaches: 4% for lower diphoton masses up to 10% for higher masses
- For VDM-Regge analysis: W = 5 GeV, M_γγ > 5 GeV, p_t,γ > 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous light-by-light scattering measurements by CMS (2017) and ATLAS collaborations, which used high thresholds (5 GeV diphoton mass, 2-2.5 GeV transverse momentum), by predicting signals accessible to future ALICE FoCal (Run 4) and ALICE-3 detectors at lower thresholds. The calculations improve on the SuperChic approach (Harland-Lang, 2020) by using a smooth nuclear absorption factor instead of a sharp impact parameter cutoff. The results enable future experimental searches for the VDM-Regge mechanism (not yet observed) and light meson resonance contributions to γγ → γγ scattering, though the destructive interference between box and VDM-Regge contributions and the challenging rapidity coverage required for VDM-Regge observation remain open problems.
