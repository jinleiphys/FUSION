# Theory Viewpoint on Extracting Nucleon Polarisabilities in Low-Energy Compton Scattering

> During the workshop "Compton Scattering off Protons and Light Nuclei: pinning down the nucleon polarisabilities" (ECT*, Trento, Italy, 29 July -- 2 August 2013, http://www.ectstar.eu/node/98), recent developments had been reviewed in experimental and theoretical studies of real and virtual Compton scattering, static and generalized dipole scalar and spin polarisabilities of nucleons, as well as related phenomena in physics of muonic atoms. A vivid topic at the workshop was pathways towards the most precise extraction of the static polarisabilities from low-energy Compton cattering---including pertinent theoretical uncertainties. Being asked by our experimental colleagues, we prepared during the workshop a short letter with conclusions of the discussion, emphasising future prospects.

## Key claim

The static dipole scalar and spin polarisabilities of the nucleon can be extracted from low-energy Compton scattering with minimal theoretical bias, but only when data are taken comfortably below the single pion production threshold; around and above the Δ(1232) resonance, all theoretical approaches must resort to well-motivated but not fully controlled approximations, making extraction less reliable.

## Method

The authors, representing several variants of both Dispersion Relations and Effective Field Theories, convened at a workshop to reach a consensus on the feasibility of extracting static polarisabilities from Compton scattering. They divided the energy range into three regimes, below pion threshold, around pion threshold, and around the Δ(1232) resonance, and assessed the reliability of theoretical approaches in each regime based on the physics captured (e.g., pion-cloud dynamics, two-pion production, convergence of expansion parameters).

## Key numbers

- Scalar polarisabilities should be extractable with "high theoretical accuracy and minimal theory error" from data below the single pion production threshold (energy not specified numerically in text).
- In the third regime, around and above the Δ(1232) resonance, the dimensionless expansion parameter in Effective Field Theories "starts to approach unity," indicating increasingly worse convergence.
- The paper reports no specific numerical values for polarisabilities or cross sections; it is a consensus statement on methodology and feasibility.

## Context

This work extends discussions from the 2013 ECT* workshop "Compton Scattering off Protons and Light Nuclei: pinning down the nucleon polarisabilities," and builds on prior theoretical approaches including Dispersion Relations and Effective Field Theories (e.g., Drechsel (2002), L'vov (1996), McGovern (2012), Pascalutsa (2002), Pasquini (2007, 2010), Griesshammer (2012, 2013), Lensky (2009, 2014), and others). The consensus enables experimental proposals for high-accuracy Compton scattering facilities, as highlighted in the US Long-Range Plans (NSAC 2007, NAS 2012) and European NuPECC (2010), and aims to reduce the dominant theoretical uncertainty in determinations of the proton-neutron mass shift and the Lamb shift of muonic hydrogen.
