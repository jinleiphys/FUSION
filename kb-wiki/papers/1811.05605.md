# $ηn$ photoproduction and nucleon resonances

> We present results of a recent work on the reaction mechanism of $ηn$ photoproduction off the neutron in the range of $ \sqrt{s} \approx 1.5 - 1.9$ GeV and discuss the role of various nucleon resonances listed in the Particle Data Group (PDG). We make use of an effective Lagrangian approach combining with a Regge method. The total and helicity-dependent cross sections $σ_{1/2},\,σ_{3/2}$ are computed and the numerical results are in good agreement with the A2 experimental data.

## Key claim
The narrow bump-like structure near $\sqrt{s} \sim 1.68$ GeV seen only in the neutron channel (the "neutron anomaly") can be described by including the narrow $N(1685,1/2^+)$ resonance alongside fifteen PDG-listed nucleon resonances, but the authors note that a detailed partial-wave analysis of helicity amplitudes is still required to definitively confirm the existence of this resonance.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach combined with a Regge method to compute the $\gamma n \to \eta n$ reaction. They included Born terms (nucleon exchanges in $s$ and $u$ channels, plus $\rho$ and $\omega$ Reggeon exchanges in the $t$ channel) and fifteen $N^*$ resonances from the PDG plus the narrow $N(1685,1/2^+)$ in the $s$-channel, constructing interaction Lagrangians for spins up to 7/2. Resonance parameters were taken from PDG experimental data and quark model predictions by Capstick (1998).

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy range: $\sqrt{s} \approx 1.5 - 1.9$ GeV
- Coupling constants used: $\kappa_N = -1.91$, $g_{\rho NN} = 2.6$, $\kappa_{\rho NN} = 3.7$, $g_{\omega NN} = 10.4$, $\kappa_{\omega NN} = 0.41$, $g_{\eta NN} = 6.34$
- Four dominant resonances near threshold: $N(1520,3/2^-)$, $N(1535,1/2^-)$, $N(1685,1/2^+)$, and $N(1710,1/2^+)$
- Additional important higher-spin resonances: $N(1720,3/2^+)$ and $N(1900,3/2^+)$

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of the neutron anomaly first reported by Kuznetsov (2006) and Jaegle (2008), and later measured by the A2 Collaboration (Werthmuller, 2014; Witthauer, 2017). The results favor the existence of the narrow $N(1685,1/2^+)$ resonance, consistent with the authors' recent work on $\gamma n \to K^0 \Lambda$ (Kim, 2018). The study enables future partial-wave analyses of helicity amplitudes needed to definitively establish the $N^*(1685)$ resonance.
