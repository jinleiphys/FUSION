# Hot hadronic matter and strange anti-baryons

> We demonstrate that both quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadronic gas (HG) models of the central fireball created in S -> W collisions at 200 GeV A are possible sources of the recently observed strange (anti-) baryons. From the theoretical point of view, the HG interpretation we attempt remains more obscure because of the high fireball temperature required. The thermal properties of the fireball as determined by the particle ratios, are natural for the QGP state. We show that the total particle multiplicity emerging from the central rapidity region allows to distinguish between the two scenarios.

# Hot hadronic matter and strange anti-baryons

## Key claim

Both quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadronic gas (HG) models of the central fireball created in S → W collisions at 200 GeV A can reproduce the observed strange (anti-)baryon abundances, but the HG interpretation requires a high fireball temperature that makes it theoretically more obscure, while the thermal properties derived from particle ratios are natural for the QGP state. The total particle multiplicity emerging from the central rapidity region provides a way to distinguish between the two scenarios.

## Method

The authors analyze strange baryon and anti-baryon ratios measured by the WA85 collaboration in S–W collisions at 200 GeV A, using a statistical thermal model with chemical fugacities for u, d, s quarks and a strangeness suppression factor γ_s. They compare the entropy per baryon predicted by a QGP fireball model (with perturbative QCD corrections) against that of a hadronic gas fireball model including strange hadrons up to high masses, imposing the condition of zero net strangeness and using the measured particle ratios to fix the thermal parameters.

## Key numbers

- R_Ξ = (anti-Ξ⁻)/(Ξ⁻) = 0.39 ± 0.07 for S–W collisions at 200 GeV A, for 2.3 < y < 3 and p⊥ > 1 GeV/c
- R_Λ = (anti-Λ)/(Λ) = 0.13 ± 0.03 for S–W collisions at 200 GeV A, for 2.4 < y < 2.8 and p⊥ > 1 GeV/c
- (anti-Ξ⁻)/(anti-Λ + anti-Σ⁰) = 0.6 ± 0.2 at fixed m⊥ > 1.72 GeV
- (Ξ⁻)/(Λ + Σ⁰) = 0.20 ± 0.04 at fixed m⊥ > 1.72 GeV
- γ_s = 0.7 ± 0.1, derived from the product of the above two ratios
- μ_q/T = 0.52 ± 0.1, μ_s/T = -0.03 ± 0.06
- For the QGP interpretation at T = 210 MeV: energy density = 2.2 GeV/fm³, baryon density = 0.27/fm³, strange quark density = 0.45/fm³
- Entropy per baryon for QGP: S_QGP/B = 46.9 (ranging from 41.4 to 50.5 depending on α_s)
- Entropy per baryon for HG: S_HG/B = 21.5 ± 1.5
- Predicted pion multiplicity per participating baryon: QGP gives about 10.5, HG gives about 4 ± 0.5

## Context

This work extends the debate between QGP and hadronic gas interpretations of strange particle production in heavy-ion collisions, following earlier studies by Rafelski (1991) showing agreement with a QGP fireball and by Cleymans and Satz (1992) arguing for compatibility with an equilibrium HG fireball. The paper introduces entropy content per baryon as a model-independent observable to distinguish the two scenarios, building on the observation by the NA35 and NA36 collaborations of central production of strange particles with a common apparent temperature of T = 210 ± 10 MeV. The results enable experimental differentiation between QGP and HG sources through charged particle multiplicity measurements in the central rapidity region.
