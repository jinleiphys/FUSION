# Shell-model study for $^{204-210}$Tl isotopes and core excitations across the $Z = 82$ and $N = 126$ shell gaps

> In the present work, the $^{204-210}$Tl isotopes have been investigated by performing large-scale shell-model calculations, including configurations allowing both neutron and proton core excitations across the $Z$ = 82 and $N$ = 126 shell gaps. Inspired by the recent high-spin experimental data, the structure of Tl isotopes has been studied for a considerably large model space. The KHHE interaction has been used for $^{204-206}$Tl isotopes, KHH7B interaction for $^{204-210}$Tl isotopes, and additionally KHM3Y interaction has been used for $^{208}$Tl isotope. The core excitation has been performed using the KHH7B and KHM3Y interactions. The level spectra of $^{204-210}$Tl isotopes are comprehensively described and explained by multi-nucleon couplings of single-particle-hole orbitals within the valence space and by core excitations across $^{208}$Pb core. The well-known isomeric states are analyzed in terms of the shell model configurations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that large-scale shell-model calculations, including both valence-space configurations and core excitations across the Z=82 and N=126 shell gaps, can comprehensively describe the level spectra of 204-210Tl isotopes, but the agreement is energy-dependent: core-excited states are systematically overestimated in energy (e.g., by several MeV) compared to experiment, indicating that the effective interactions used (KHH7B, KHM3Y) still require refinement to capture the energetics of broken-core configurations.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using three interactions: KHHE for 204-206Tl, KHH7B for 204-210Tl, and KHM3Y for 208Tl, with model spaces that included proton and neutron orbitals both below and above the Z=82 and N=126 shell closures. For core-excited states, one proton was allowed to excite into the 0h9/2, 1f7/2, or 0i13/2 orbitals and one neutron into the 1g9/2, 0i11/2, or 0j15/2 orbitals; calculations were performed with the OXBASH, NUSHELLX, and KSHELL codes, comparing t=0 (no core excitation), t=1 (pure core excitation), and t=0+1 (mixed) results against experimental level energies and electromagnetic properties.

## Key numbers

- 204Tl: The 7+ isomer at 0.902 MeV has a 67.27% contribution from the πs1/2−1 νi13/2−1 configuration; the 12− isomer at 2.008 MeV has 80.54% πh11/2−1 νi13/2−1; the 18+ isomer at 4.030 MeV has 82.03% πh11/2−1 νi13/2−2 p1/2−1; the 22− isomer at 5.978 MeV has 83.78% πh11/2−1 νi13/2−3.
- 206Tl: The 5+ isomer at 1.465 MeV has 71.12% πh11/2−1 νp1/2−1; the 7+ isomer at 1.646 MeV has 75.74% πs1/2−1 νi13/2−1; the 12− isomer at 2.686 MeV has 83.47% πh11/2−1 νi13/2−1.
- 208Tl: The 0− core-excited state is calculated at 2.392 MeV with KHM3Y (90.99% πs1/2−1 νg9/2^2 p1/2−1) versus 4.290 MeV with KHH7B; the experimental value is 1.807 MeV.
- 205Tl: The 25/2+ isomer has 80.8% πh11/2−1 νp1/2−1 i13/2−1; the 35/2− isomer is calculated at 4.667 MeV with 83.68% πh11/2−1 νi13/2−2; the 41/2+ state is calculated at 10.246 MeV (experimentally 7.092 MeV) with 72.22% πh11/2−1 νp1/2−1 i13/2−2 g9/2^1.
- 207Tl: The 35/2− state has 94.44% πh11/2−1 νi13/2−1 i11/2^1 configuration.

## Context

This work extends systematic shell-model studies of Tl isotopes near the doubly magic 208Pb, where prior calculations (e.g., using only the KHHE interaction) were limited to valence-hole spaces and could not describe high-spin states above the maximum angular momentum from valence configurations. The inclusion of core excitations across Z=82 and N=126, using the KHH7B and KHM3Y interactions, enables the description of yrast states up to spin 30 in 204Tl and 41/2 in 205Tl, building on earlier experimental work by Fornal (2001), Broda (2010), and Wilson (2017). The results enable future studies of neutron-rich Tl isotopes and r-process waiting-point nuclei, though the systematic overestimation of core-excited state energies (by 2-3 MeV in many cases) highlights the need for improved effective interactions that better account for pairing and octupole correlations.
