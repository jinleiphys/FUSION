# 3D reduction of the N-body Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We perform a 3D reduction of the two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation, by series expansion around a positive-energy instantaneous approximation of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel, followed by another series expansion at the 3D level in order to get a manifestly hermitian 3D potential. It turns out that this potential does not depend on the choice of the starting approximation of the kernel anymore, and can be written in a very compact form. This result can also be obtained directly by starting with an approximation of the free propagator, based on integrals in the relative energies instead of the more usual delta-constraint. Furthermore, the method can be generalized to a system of N particles, consisting in any combination of bosons and fermions. As an example, we write the 3D equation for systems of two or three fermions exchanging photons, in Feynman or Coulomb's gauge.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a 3D reduction of the N-body Bethe-Salpeter equation can be performed such that the resulting 3D potential does not depend on the choice of the starting instantaneous approximation of the kernel, provided one uses an integrating propagator-based method rather than the more usual constraining delta-function approach; the method works for any combination of bosons and fermions, but the practical convergence of the series expansion is not guaranteed and must be assessed for each specific problem.

## Method

The author starts from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for two fermions and performs a 3D reduction by series expansion around a positive-energy instantaneous approximation of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel, followed by a second series expansion at the 3D level to obtain a manifestly hermitian potential. The method is then reformulated as an integrating propagator-based reduction, where the relative energies are integrated over rather than fixed by delta constraints, and this approach is generalized to N particles (any mixture of bosons and fermions) by computing the integral of the product of N free Green functions over the N-1 relative energies. The 3D free propagator consists of two terms: one where all particles have positive free energies and one where all have negative free energies, which preserves the 3D equation from continuum dissolution.

## Key numbers

* The 3D free propagator for two fermions is given by \(g^0 = 1/(P_0 - S + i\epsilon P_0)\) where \(S = h_1 + h_2\) and the operator \(\tau = \Lambda^{++} - \Lambda^{--}\) appears in the residue, with \(\Lambda^{++} = \Lambda_1^+\Lambda_2^+\) and \(\Lambda_i^\pm = (E_i \pm h_i)/(2E_i)\).
* For N particles with \(f\) fermions and \(b\) bosons (\(f \neq 0\)), the integrated free propagator is \((-2i\pi)^{N-1} \tau g^0 \beta / \omega\) where \(\omega = 2^b E_{f+1} \cdots E_N\) and \(\beta = \beta_1 \cdots \beta_f\).
* For bosons only (\(f=0\)), the 3D propagator becomes \(g^0 = 2E/(P_0^2 - E^2 + i\epsilon)\) with \(E = \sum_{i=1}^N E_i\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous two-fermion kernel-based reduction (Bijtebier, 1999) to N-body systems and provides a unifying framework that recovers results from Phillips and Wallace (order-by-order tuning method) and Logunov and Tavkhelidze (retarded part of the full propagator at equal times). The method avoids the unconnectedness difficulties that arise in Gross' spectator model for three fermions (Gross, 1982), which leads to coupled Faddeev-like equations rather than a single 3D equation with a potential. The approach enables the construction of 3D equations for bound states and scattering of N relativistic particles, with the possibility of indefinite improvement by including higher-order terms, though Lorentz invariance and cluster separability are not manifest in the truncated series.
