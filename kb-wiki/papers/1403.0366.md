# Magicity of the $^{52}$Ca and $^{54}$Ca isotopes and tensor contribution within a mean--field approach

> We investigate the magicity of the isotopes $^{52}$Ca and $^{54}$Ca, that was recently confirmed by two experimental measurements, and relate it to like--particle and neutron--proton tensor effects within a mean--field description. By analyzing Ca isotopes, we show that the like--particle tensor contribution induces shell effects that render these nuclei more magic than they would be predicted by neglecting it. In particular, such induced shell effects are stronger in the nucleus $^{52}$Ca and the single--particle gaps are increased in both isotopes due to the tensor force. By studying $N=32$ and $N=34$ isotones, neutron--proton tensor effects may be isolated and their role analyzed. It is shown that neutron--proton tensor effects lead to increasing $N=32$ and $N=34$ gaps, when going along isotonic chains, from $^{58}$Fe to $^{52}$Ca, and from $^{60}$Fe to $^{54}$Ca, respectively. The mean--field calculations are perfomed by employing one Skyrme parameter set, that was introduced in a previous work by fitting the tensor parameters together with the spin--orbit strength. The signs and the values of the tensor strengths are thus checked within this specific application. The obtained results indicate that the employed parameter set, even if generated with a partial adjustment of the parameters of the force, leads to the correct shell behavior and provides, in particular, a description of the magicity of $^{52}$Ca and $^{54}$Ca within a pure mean--field picture with the effective two--body Skyrme interaction.

## Key claim

Within a mean-field Skyrme-Hartree-Fock description, the like-particle tensor contribution induces shell effects that render $^{52}$Ca and $^{54}$Ca more magic than they would be predicted without it, and the neutron-proton tensor contribution leads to increasing $N=32$ and $N=34$ gaps along isotonic chains; the employed parameter set, even with only partial adjustment of the force parameters, provides the correct shell behavior.

## Method

Hartree-Fock calculations in spherical symmetry were performed in coordinate space using a Skyrme parameter set built on top of the SLy5 force, where the spin-orbit strength was reduced to 101 MeV fm$^5$ and the tensor parameters were adjusted to $\alpha_{\rm T} = -170$ and $\beta_{\rm T} = 122$ MeV fm$^5$ following a three-step fitting procedure from Grasso (2013). Pairing correlations were neglected. Single-particle neutron gaps were computed for Ca isotopes to isolate like-particle tensor effects, and for $N=32$ and $N=34$ isotonic chains to isolate neutron-proton tensor effects.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle neutron gap for $^{48}$Ca increased by 1.3 MeV due to like-particle tensor contribution; experimental value is 5.4 MeV (Volya, year not specified).
- For $^{52}$Ca, the neutron gap increased from 1.24 MeV (without tensor) to 3.06 MeV (with tensor).
- For $^{54}$Ca, the neutron gap increased from 0.45 MeV (without tensor) to 1.86 MeV (with tensor).
- Binding energies with the new Skyrme set: 343.83 MeV ($^{40}$Ca), 415.91 MeV ($^{48}$Ca), 438.62 MeV ($^{52}$Ca), and 445.21 MeV ($^{54}$Ca); largest deviation from original SLy5 is 0.3%.
- Two-neutron shell gap for $^{52}$Ca calculated as 5.1 MeV, compared with experimental value of almost 4 MeV (Wienholtz, year not specified).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field tensor parametrizations introduced by Grasso (2013) and applies them to the recently confirmed magicity of $^{52}$Ca and $^{54}$Ca from experimental measurements by Huck (year not specified), Gade (year not specified), Wienholtz (year not specified) at ISOLTRAP/CERN, and RIKEN (year not specified). The analysis of neutron-proton tensor effects along isotonic chains follows the shell-model arguments presented in the RIKEN study. The results demonstrate that a pure mean-field picture with a Skyrme interaction including tensor terms can describe these shell closures, complementing more sophisticated approaches using chiral interactions with three-body forces (Holt, year not specified; Hagen, year not specified) and shell-model calculations (Honma, year not specified).
