# Nonuniversal Effects in the Homogeneous Bose Gas

> Effective field theory predicts that the leading nonuniversal effects in the homogeneous Bose gas arise from the effective range for S-wave scattering and from an effective three-body contact interaction. We calculate the leading nonuniversal contributions to the energy density and condensate fraction and compare the predictions with results from diffusion Monte Carlo calculations by Giorgini, Boronat, and Casulleras. We give a crude determination of the strength of the three-body contact interaction for various model potentials. Accurate determinations could be obtained from diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of the energy density with higher statistics.

## Key claim

Effective field theory predicts that the leading nonuniversal effects in the homogeneous Bose gas arise from the effective range for S-wave scattering and from an effective three-body contact interaction, but the paper provides only a crude determination of the three-body contact interaction strength for various model potentials, noting that accurate determinations would require diffusion Monte Carlo calculations with higher statistics.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory to calculate the leading and next-to-leading nonuniversal contributions to the energy density and condensate fraction of a homogeneous Bose gas at zero temperature. They compared these predictions with existing diffusion Monte Carlo data from Giorgini, Boronat, and Casulleras (1999) for four different two-body model potentials, fitting the three-body contact parameter to the numerical results.

## Key numbers

- The energy per particle expansion includes a universal mean-field term proportional to \(2 \pi \hbar^2 a n / m\), a universal \(\sqrt{na^3}\) correction with coefficient \(32/(15\pi) \sqrt{16\pi}\), and a nonuniversal \(na^3\) correction containing a logarithmic term with coefficient \((4\pi - 3\sqrt{3})/(6\pi)\) and a nonuniversal constant \(c'\).
- The diffusion Monte Carlo data covered densities ranging from \(na^3 = 10^{-6}\) to \(na^3 = 0.244\).
- The universal \(\sqrt{na^3}\) correction accounted for most deviations from the mean-field prediction across the studied density range.

## Context

This work extends the universal low-density expansion for the homogeneous Bose gas, originally developed by Bogoliubov (1947), Lee, Huang, and Yang (1957), and Wu (1959), by identifying the specific nonuniversal parameters using effective field theory. It builds on the diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of Giorgini, Boronat, and Casulleras (1999) and parallels earlier studies of nonuniversal corrections in Fermi gases by Efimov (1993) and Hammer and Furnstahl (2000). The results enable future accurate determinations of three-body contact interactions from higher-statistics Monte Carlo calculations.
