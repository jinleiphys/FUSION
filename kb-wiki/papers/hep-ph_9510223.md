# Multiplicity Distributions of Squeezed Isospin States

> Multiplicity distributions of neutral and charged particles arising from squeezed coherent states are investigated. Projections onto global isospin states are considered. We show how a small amount of squeezing can significantly change the multiplicity distributions. The formalism is proposed to describe the phenomenological properties of neutral and charged particles anomalously produced in hadronic and nuclear collisions at very high energies.

# Multiplicity Distributions of Squeezed Isospin States

## Key claim

A small amount of squeezing (parameter s = 0.02 or 0.04) can significantly change the multiplicity distributions of charged and neutral particles arising from squeezed coherent states, but only for certain global isospin projections; the effect depends critically on both the isospin state (I, I_z) and the sign of the squeeze parameter nu, not merely on its magnitude.

## Method

The authors construct squeezed coherent states in isospin space by applying a displacement operator D(alpha) and a squeeze operator S(eta) to the vacuum, then project these states onto eigenstates of total isospin I and z-component I_z using spherical harmonics. They compute the probability distributions P^{I,I_z}_{n0,nc}(alpha, s) for neutral (n0) and charged (nc) pion multiplicities by evaluating the scalar product between squeezed isospin states and number eigenstates, expressing the result in terms of Hermite polynomials and Euler beta functions. The calculations are performed for I = 0 and I = 1 states with fixed average total multiplicity <n> = 25, using squeeze parameters s = 0.02 and 0.04 with both positive and negative values of nu.

## Key numbers

- Average total multiplicity is fixed at <n> = 25 for all calculations.
- Squeeze parameter values used: s = 0.02 and s = 0.04, where s = (nu/alpha)^2.
- For I = 0 states with nu < 0, the peak of the charged particle distribution P^0_{nc} shifts to low nc as s increases from 0.02 to 0.04.
- For I = 1, I_z = 0 with nu > 0, the neutral and charged distributions are nearly identical despite n0 being odd and nc being even.
- For I = 0 and I = 1, I_z = 1 states, the neutral distribution P_{n0} peaks at n0 = 0 regardless of the sign of nu.

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent states modified by dynamical constraints (Scalapino and Sugar, 1973; Horn and Silver, 1971; Andreev, 1981) and the use of squeezed states in quantum optics (Yuen, 1976; Caves, 1981) to the description of anomalous charge and neutral particle production in high-energy hadronic and nuclear collisions, particularly Centauro-type events. The formalism is proposed as a phenomenological tool analogous to the negative binomial distribution (Carruthers and Shih, 1987; Giovannini and Van Hove, 1986) but with greater flexibility to accommodate unusual neutral-to-charge ratios, including the possibility of peaking at n0 = 0 relevant to Centauro events. The work does not address the dynamical origin of squeezed states in collisions but provides a mathematical framework for describing multiplicity distributions that could later be compared to experimental data.
