# QED Effective Action in Magnetic Field Backgrounds and Electromagnetic Duality

> In the in-out formalism we advance a method of the inverse scattering matrix for calculating effective actions in pure magnetic field backgrounds. The one-loop effective actions are found in a localized magnetic field of Sauter type and approximately in a general magnetic field by applying the uniform semiclassical approximation. The effective actions exhibit the electromagnetic duality between a constant electric field and a constant magnetic field and between $E(x) = E sech^2 (x/L)$ and $B(x) = B sech^2 (x/L)$.

## Key claim

The paper advances a method using the inverse scattering matrix to compute one-loop QED effective actions in pure magnetic field backgrounds within the in-out formalism, and demonstrates that these effective actions exhibit electromagnetic duality with those for electric fields of the same profile under the analytic continuation \(E = iB\).

## Method

The author introduces the inverse scattering matrix, defined as the ratio of the amplitude for the exponentially increasing part to the amplitude for the exponentially decreasing part of the charged particle wavefunction in a magnetic field, and shows it plays an analogous role to the Bogoliubov coefficient used for electric fields. This method is applied to a constant magnetic field and a spatially localized Sauter-type magnetic field \(B(x) = B \, \mathrm{sech}^2(x/L)\), using gamma-function regularization and the proper-time integral to obtain renormalized effective actions. A uniform semiclassical approximation is also outlined for general magnetic field configurations.

## Key numbers

- For the constant magnetic field, the renormalized one-loop effective action is given by \(\mathcal{L}^{(1)} = \mp \frac{1+2|\sigma|}{2} \frac{(qB)^2}{(2\pi)^2} \int_0^\infty \frac{ds}{s^2} e^{-\frac{m^2 s}{2qB}} F_\sigma(s)\), where the spectral function is \(F_\sigma(s) = \frac{[\cosh(s/2)]^{2|\sigma|}}{\sinh(s/2)} - \frac{2}{s} + (-1)^{2|\sigma|} \frac{(1+2|\sigma|)s}{12}\).
- For the Sauter-type magnetic field \(B(x) = B \, \mathrm{sech}^2(x/L)\), the renormalized effective action is \(\mathcal{L}^{(1)} = \mp \frac{1+2|\sigma|}{2} \int \frac{d\tilde{\omega}}{(2\pi)} \frac{d^2 \mathbf{k}_\perp}{(2\pi)^2} \int_0^\infty \frac{ds}{s} \left( e^{-\frac{\Omega_{(+)}}{2}s} + e^{\frac{\Delta_{(-)}}{2}s} - e^{\frac{\Omega_{(-)}}{2}s} - e^{-\frac{\Delta_{(+)}}{2}s} \right) F_\sigma(s)\), where \(\Omega_{(\pm)}\) and \(\Delta_{(\pm)}\) are functions of the momenta and field parameters.
- The bound state condition for the Sauter-type magnetic field is \(c - b = \frac{L}{2}(\Pi_{1(+)} + \Pi_{1(-)}) - \lambda_\sigma + \frac{1+2\sigma}{2} = -n\) for \(n = 0, 1, \dots\), with \(n + (1+2\sigma)/2 < \lambda_\sigma\).

## Context

This work extends the in-out formalism, previously applied to electric fields by Kim, Lee, and Yoon (2008, 2009, 2010), to magnetic field backgrounds by introducing the inverse scattering matrix as a substitute for the Bogoliubov coefficient. It provides an alternative to the resolvent Green function method used by Chudnovsky and Dunne (1998) and Dunne and Hall (1998) for magnetic fields. The demonstrated electromagnetic duality between constant and Sauter-type electric and magnetic fields enables the analytic continuation of effective actions between these configurations and offers a new scheme for understanding vacuum structure in nontrivial field backgrounds.
