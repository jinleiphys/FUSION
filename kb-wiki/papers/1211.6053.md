# $η(T)/s (T)$ and collective flow in chiral hydrodynamics

> Using relativistic conformal hydrodynamics coupled to the linear $σ$ model we study the evolution of matter created in heavy--ion collisions. We focus the study on the influence of the dynamics of the chiral fields on the charged-hadron elliptic flow $v_2$ for a temperature--independent as well as for a temperature--dependent $η/s$ that is calculated from kinetic theory. We find that $v_2$ is not very sensitive to the coupling of chiral fields to the hydrodynamic evolution, but the temperature dependence of $η/s$ plays a much bigger role on this observable.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the coupling of chiral fields to the hydrodynamic evolution has only a small impact on the charged-hadron elliptic flow \(v_2\), while the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) plays a much larger role; this conclusion is hedged by the specific choice of the linear sigma model coupling \(g=3.2\) and the use of a smooth crossover scenario consistent with lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors couple second-order relativistic conformal hydrodynamics to the linear sigma model (LSM) to simulate the evolution of matter created in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. The classical equations of motion for the chiral fields \((\sigma, \vec{\pi})\) are solved self-consistently with the conservation equations for the quark fluid, including source terms that couple the chiral fields to the energy-momentum tensor. The temperature-dependent shear viscosity \(\eta(T)\) is computed from the linearized Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, using averaged cross sections for quark-quark and quark-antiquark scattering with \(1/N_c\) next-to-leading order corrections.

## Key numbers

- The initial temperature at the center of the fireball is \(T_i = 330\) MeV, with an initialization time \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm/c and impact parameter \(b = 7\) fm.
- The freeze-out temperature is \(T_F = 130\) MeV, using the isothermal Cooper-Frye prescription.
- For a temperature-independent \(\eta/s\), the elliptic flow \(v_2\) is practically independent of the chiral sources for \(p_T < 3\) GeV.
- The chiral coupling constant is set to \(g = 3.2\) to produce a smooth crossover with a speed of sound drop near the critical temperature comparable to lattice QCD; a first-order phase transition is obtained if \(g \simeq 3.8\).
- The second-order transport coefficients are taken as \(\tau_\pi = 2(2-\ln 2)\frac{\eta}{sT}\) and \(\lambda_1 = \frac{\eta}{2\pi T}\), corresponding to \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super Yang-Mills theory.

## Context

This work extends recent studies (Nagle, Shen, Niemi) on the impact of temperature-dependent transport coefficients on momentum anisotropies in hydrodynamic models of heavy-ion collisions. The results show that uncertainties from the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) are much larger than those from coupling to chiral fields, adding to the overall theoretical uncertainty (estimated at roughly 0.1 in \(\eta/s\)) from initial conditions and freeze-out processes. The paper enables future work incorporating bulk viscosity and fluctuations of the chiral fields as noise sources in the classical equations of motion.
