# New representation of orbital motion with arbitrary angular momenta

> A new formulation is presented for a variational calculation of $N$-body systems on a correlated Gaussian basis with arbitrary angular momenta. The rotational motion of the system is described with a single spherical harmonic of the total angular momentum $L$, and thereby needs no explicit coupling of partial waves between particles. A simple generating function for the correlated Gaussian is exploited to derive the matrix elements. The formulation is applied to various Coulomb three-body systems such as $e^-e^-e^+, ttμ, tdμ$, and $αe^-e^-$ up to $L=4$ in order to show its usefulness and versatility. A stochastic selection of the basis functions gives good results for various angular momentum states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a variational method for N-body systems with arbitrary total angular momentum L that uses a single spherical harmonic of the total angular momentum, avoiding explicit partial-wave coupling between particles; the authors hedge that the method's utility is demonstrated for Coulomb three-body systems up to L=4, and that results are "satisfactory" but not necessarily the most precise achievable with larger basis sizes.

## Method

The authors construct correlated Gaussian basis functions where the angular part is given by a single global vector v = Σᵢ uᵢ xᵢ (a linear combination of relative coordinates) raised to power 2K+L times a spherical harmonic Y_LM(v̂), with u treated as a continuous variational parameter. Matrix elements for overlap, kinetic energy, and potential energy are derived analytically using a generating function technique that avoids angular momentum coupling algebra. The basis functions are selected stochastically via the stochastic variational method (SVM), with nonlinear parameters refined stepwise, and applied to Coulomb three-body systems including e⁻e⁻e⁺, ttμ, tdμ, and αe⁻e⁻.

## Key numbers

- For the positronium negative ion e⁻e⁻e⁺ ground state (L=0): energy of -0.262005 a.u. with basis dimension K=200 using case (iii) (full correlated Gaussian with K_max=0), compared to -0.26200507 a.u. from correlated exponential calculations
- For ttμ molecule L=0 state: energy of -362.909 eV with case (iii), reproducing first seven digits of Ref. Alexander (1994) using correlated Slater-type geminals
- For ttμ molecule L=2 (D state): energy of -357.530 eV with case (iii) and K=200, in good agreement with Frolov (1994) using K=2250 basis functions
- For tdμ molecule L=0 state: energy of -319.140 eV with case (iii), reproducing first six digits of most precise variational calculations
- For αe⁻e⁻ (helium atom) L=0 state: energy of -2.90372 a.u. with case (iii) and K=200
- Maximum basis dimension used in all calculations: K=200
- Angular momenta studied: L=0 through L=4 for Coulomb three-body systems

## Context

This work extends the correlated Gaussian approach previously developed by Suzuki and Varga (1996) for L=0 systems to arbitrary angular momenta, providing an alternative to the standard method of vector-coupled solid spherical harmonics used by Kamimura (1988) and Frolov (1996). The new angular representation avoids the complexity of partial-wave decomposition that becomes prohibitive as particle number increases, and the generating function technique yields closed-form analytic matrix elements. The method enables variational calculations for rotational excited states of few-body Coulomb systems, including muonic molecules and exotic atoms, with the stochastic selection procedure allowing efficient basis optimization without requiring explicit angular momentum coupling.
