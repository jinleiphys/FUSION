# Comment on recent Strangeness -2 predictions

> It is pointed out that the Chiral Constituent Quark Model (CCQM) interactions that bind the H dibaryon and Lambda-Lambda-3H overbind Lambda-Lambda-6He by more than 4 MeV, thus outdating the CCQM in the strangeness S=-2 sector.

# Comment on recent Strangeness -2 predictions

## Key claim
The Chiral Constituent Quark Model (CCQM) interactions that predict a bound $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~3}{\rm H}$ hypernucleus and a bound $H$ dibaryon overbind the experimentally observed $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}$ (the NAGARA event) by more than 4 MeV, rendering the CCQM's predictive power for the strangeness S=-2 sector questionable.

## Method
The author compares $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}({_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}})$ values from two sets of $\alpha\Lambda\Lambda$ three-body calculations (Filikhin-Gal 2002 and Carr et al. 1997) that use identical $V_{\Lambda\alpha}$ but different $V_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ interactions. By interpolating within these calculations using the CCQM $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length $a^{\rm CCQM}_{\Lambda\Lambda}=-3.3$ fm, the author estimates the CCQM-predicted $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ and compares it to the experimental value. The analysis also accounts for the additional binding from $\Lambda\Lambda$–$\Xi N$ coupling.

## Key numbers
- The experimental $\Delta B^{\rm exp}_{\Lambda\Lambda}({_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}})=0.67\pm0.17$ MeV from the NAGARA event (Nakazawa 2010).
- The CCQM $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length is $a^{\rm CCQM}_{\Lambda\Lambda}=-3.3$ fm.
- Interpolation within the Filikhin-Gal (2002) set gives $\Delta B^{\rm CCQM}_{\Lambda\Lambda}({_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}})=3.2\pm0.1$ MeV.
- The author's conservative estimate including $\Lambda\Lambda$–$\Xi N$ coupling yields $\Delta B^{\rm CCQM}_{\Lambda\Lambda}({_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}})>4.7\pm0.5$ MeV, overbinding $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}$ by more than $4.0\pm0.5$ MeV.
- Analysis of the in-medium reaction $^{12}{\rm C}(K^-,K^+\Lambda\Lambda X)$ (Yoon 2007) gives $a_{\Lambda\Lambda}=-1.2\pm0.6$ fm (Gal et al. 2012), consistent with $a_{\Lambda\Lambda}\sim -0.5$ fm from $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}$ but in disagreement with $a^{\rm CCQM}_{\Lambda\Lambda}=-3.3$ fm.

## Context
This comment directly challenges the predictions of Garcilazo and Valcarce (2012), who reported a bound $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~3}{\rm H}$ using CCQM interactions in a $\Lambda\Lambda N$–$\Xi NN$ coupled-channel Faddeev calculation. The author argues that these same interactions overbind the well-established $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}$ hypernucleus from the NAGARA event, contradicting earlier ab initio few-body calculations by Nemura (2005) that associated loosely bound $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~4}{\rm H}$ with the onset of $\Lambda\Lambda$ binding. The CCQM predictions also conflict with the latest HAL QCD lattice-simulation analysis (2012), which locates the $H$ dibaryon near the $\Xi N$ threshold rather than below the $\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold. This work calls into question the entire set of CCQM predictions for the S=-2 sector, including the claimed $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~3}{\rm H}$ bound state.
