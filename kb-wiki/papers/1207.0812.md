# Effects of Quark Spin Flip on the Collins Fragmentation Function in a Toy Model

> The recent extension of the NJL-jet model to hadronization of transversely polarized quarks allowed the study of the Collins fragmentation function. Both favored and unfavored Collins fragmentation functions were generated, the latter purely by multiple hadron emissions, with 1/2 moments of opposite sign in the region of the light-cone momentum fraction $z$ accessible in current experiments. Hints of such behavior has been seen in the measurements in several experiments. Also, in the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) hadron emission probabilities, modulations of up to fourth order in sine of the polar angle were observed, while the Collins effect describes just the linear modulations. A crucial part of the extended model was the calculation of the quark spin flip probability after each hadron emission in the jet. Here we study the effects of this probability on the resulting unfavored and favored Collins functions by setting it as a constant and use a toy model for the elementary single hadron emission probabilities. The results of the Monte Carlo simulations showed that preferential quark spin flip in the elementary hadron emission is needed to generate the favored and unfavored Collins functions with opposite sign 1/2 moments. For the TMD hadron emission modulations, we showed that the model quark spin flip probabilities are a partial source of the higher rode modulations, while the other source is the Collins modulation of the remnant quark from the hadron emission recoil.

# Effects of Quark Spin Flip on the Collins Fragmentation Function in a Toy Model

## Key claim

The qualitative features of the Collins fragmentation function's 1/2 moments, specifically the opposite sign between favored and unfavored functions, are generated by the quark spin flip process in the hadronization chain and do not depend on the particular elementary Collins functions used as input, provided the spin flip probability exceeds 0.5. The higher-order sine modulations observed in transverse momentum dependent (TMD) polarized fragmentation functions arise from two sources: the recoil-induced modulation of the remnant quark and, in the full model, the dynamical form of the quark spin flip probabilities themselves.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations within the NJL-jet model framework, using a toy model where the elementary Collins function was taken proportional to the unpolarized fragmentation function with a fixed coefficient (0.9 sin φ modulation) and the quark spin flip probability was set to various constant values (SF = 0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1). They extracted the 1/2 moments of the Collins functions by fitting the φ-dependent polarized fragmentation functions with a linear form c₀ + c₁ sin(φ) for fixed z bins. For the TMD analysis, they fitted the polarized fragmentation functions with polynomials of increasing order in sin(φ) and evaluated the χ²/NDF to determine the required modulation order for simulations with fixed numbers of emitted hadrons (N_Links = 2, 3, 6).

## Key numbers

- The elementary Collins function in the toy model was set such that (H̃₁^{⊥ h/q})(p⊥)/(m_h z) = 0.9 d₁^{h/q}(z, p⊥²), with S_q = 1.
- For unfavored π⁻ fragmentation, the 1/2 moment H₁^{⊥(1/2)} vanishes exactly when SF = 0.5; for SF = 1 it becomes the negative of the result for SF = 0.
- For favored π⁺ fragmentation with SF > 0.5, H₁^{⊥(1/2)} crosses zero and becomes negative at small z, with the minimum negative value increasing as SF approaches 1.
- The TMD fits used 100 discretization points each for z and P⊥² in the interval [0,1) and 100 points for φ in [0, 2π), yielding N_F = 3 × 10⁴ fits across all pion types.
- For N_Links = 2, a quadratic polynomial in sin(φ) provided excellent fits; for N_Links = 3, a cubic polynomial was required; for N_Links = 6, at least a fourth-order polynomial was needed.
- The Monte Carlo simulations used approximately 10¹¹ events (this work) and 10¹² events in the prior full model (Matevosyan 2012), run on 50 Intel Core-i7 cores for about two weeks.

## Context

This work extends the NJL-jet model calculations of the Collins fragmentation function from Matevosyan (2012), where both favored and unfavored Collins functions were first generated with opposite-sign 1/2 moments and fourth-order sine modulations were observed in TMD distributions. The toy model approach isolates the role of quark spin flip from the dynamical spin flip probabilities of Eq. (3) in that prior work. The results explain why the qualitative features of the Collins functions, similar to experimental hints from HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab (Airapetian 2004, Bradamante 2011, Aghasyan 2011), arise from the quark-jet mechanism itself rather than from details of the elementary Collins function input. This enables future studies to focus on the spin flip dynamics as the key mechanism for generating unfavored Collins functions with opposite sign to favored ones.
