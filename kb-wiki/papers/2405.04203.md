# Reconciling light nuclei and nuclear matter: relativistic $ab\ initio$ calculations

> It has been a long-standing challenge to accurately predict the properties of light nuclei and nuclear matter simultaneously in nuclear $ab\ initio$ calculations. In this Letter, we develop the relativistic quantum Monte Carlo methods for the nuclear $ab\ initio$ problem, and calculate the ground-state energies of $A\leq4$ nuclei using the two-nucleon Bonn force with an unprecedented high accuracy. For $A=3,4$ nuclei, the present relativistic results significantly outperforms the nonrelativistic results with only two-nucleon forces. Combining the present results for light nuclei and the previous results for nuclear matter with the same Bonn force, a correlation between the properties of light $A\leq4$ nuclei and the nuclear saturation is revealed, and both systems are well described simultaneously, even without introducing three-nucleon forces. This provides a quantitative understanding of the connection between the light nuclei and nuclear matter saturation properties, which has been an outstanding problem in nuclear $ab\ initio$ calculations for decades.

## Key claim

The paper claims that relativistic *ab initio* calculations using only two-nucleon (Bonn) forces can simultaneously describe the ground-state energies of light nuclei (A ≤ 4) and nuclear matter saturation properties, without introducing three-nucleon forces, provided the relativistic framework is used; the hedge is that this success depends on the specific Bonn potential (Bonn A works best) and that the Bonn force is a phenomenological meson-exchange model that does not allow systematic improvements or consistent three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors developed relativistic quantum Monte Carlo methods: relativistic variational Monte Carlo (RVMC) and relativistic diffusion Monte Carlo (RDMC). They solved the many-body Schrödinger equation in coordinate space using the relativistic kinetic operator and the nonlocal Bonn potential (parameter sets A, B, C) in its coordinate-space representation. The trial wave function used a neural-network ansatz with Jastrow-like correlators, and the ground state was projected out via imaginary-time propagation in the RDMC method.

## Key numbers

- The converged RVMC energy of ²H with Bonn A reproduces the experimental value within 1 keV.
- The RDMC calculation for ⁴He with Bonn A yields a ground-state energy that reproduces the experimental value E = -28.3 MeV within approximately 2%.
- The imaginary-time step in RDMC calculations was Δτ = 5 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻¹.
- The RDMC calculation lowered the RVMC energy for ⁴He by about 2 MeV.
- The nonlocal part of the NN interaction (V̂ - V̄) accounts for about 95% of the potential energy in the RVMC calculations.

## Context

This work extends relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations (Brockmann 1990, Sammarruca 2012, Wang 2021) that described nuclear matter saturation with only NN forces, but were less accurate for light nuclei due to center-of-mass approximations. It contradicts the prevailing view that three-nucleon forces are necessary to simultaneously describe light nuclei and nuclear matter, as seen in nonrelativistic calculations with forces like AV18 (Wiringa 1995) and chiral NN forces (Epelbaum 2015, Entem 2017). The relativistic QMC methods developed here enable future calculations with relativistic chiral forces (Epelbaum 2012, Ren 2018, Lu 2022) and the construction of consistent relativistic three-nucleon forces.
