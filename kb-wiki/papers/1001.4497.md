# Exact flow equation for bound states

> We develop a formalism to describe the formation of bound states in quantum field theory using an exact renormalization group flow equation. As a concrete example we investigate a nonrelativistic field theory with instantaneous interaction where the flow equations can be solved exactly. However, the formalism is more general and can be applied to relativistic field theories, as well. We also discuss expansion schemes that can be used to find approximate solutions of the flow equations including the essential momentum dependence.

## Key claim

The paper develops an exact renormalization group flow equation formalism for bound states in quantum field theory, demonstrating that for nonrelativistic particles with instantaneous interaction the flow equations can be solved exactly, but the formalism is intended as a starting point for approximations in more complicated relativistic or retarded-interaction cases where exact solutions are not expected.

## Method

The author introduces a microscopic action for two nonrelativistic particles interacting via a potential, then modifies the partition function with an infrared cutoff term to derive an exact flow equation for the effective average action. A Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation introduces a composite field representing the bound state, and the flow equation for the bound state propagator is integrated exactly for the nonrelativistic case. The paper then extends this to a scale-dependent Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, using a k-dependent linear field redefinition to keep the Yukawa coupling fixed during the flow, which yields a flow equation whose solution reproduces the Schrödinger equation for the two-body problem.

## Key numbers

- The microscopic action for two nonrelativistic particles uses masses \(M_1\) and \(M_2\) with dispersion relations \(p_0 - \frac{1}{2M_i}\vec p^2\).
- The Coulomb interaction is given by \(V(\vec x_1-\vec x_2) = \frac{-e^2}{4\pi |\vec x_1-\vec x_2|}\).
- The reduced mass is defined as \(\mu = M_1 M_2 / (M_1 + M_2)\).
- The bound state propagator pole condition in the center-of-mass frame reduces to \(\left[p_0 - \frac{1}{2\mu}(\vec q^2 + R_k(\vec q^2))\right] \Phi(\vec q) = \int_{\vec q'} V(\vec q,\vec q') \Phi(\vec q')\), which for \(R_k=0\) becomes the Schrödinger equation.

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group approach to bound states, building on Wetterich's formulation of the effective average action (CWFloweq) and on earlier work by Ellwanger (Ellwanger) who traced the four-point vertex flow to calculate binding energies in the Wick-Cutkosky model. The paper improves upon that approach by introducing auxiliary fields for composite operators via a scale-dependent Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, following a proposal by Gies and Wetterich (GiesWetterich) for the NJL model. The formalism enables systematic approximations for relativistic theories, retarded interactions, or nonzero density without relying on small coupling constants.
