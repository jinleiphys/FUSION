# Spin Force Dependence Of Nucleon Structure Functions

> Deep inelastic structure functions for the nucleon are obtained in a constituent quark model on the light cone. In the Bjorken limit the parton model is derived. Structure functions from the hadronic tensor at the scale $μ\sim 0.25$ GeV are evolved from (0.6 GeV$)^{2}$ to $-q^{2}\sim 15$ GeV$^{2}$. The model incorporates the quark boosts, is designed to describe form factors and structure functions, and to provide a link between the parton and constituent quark models. The main effect of the spin force between quarks is described in terms of smaller (and lighter) scalar $u-d$ quark pairs in the nucleon. To account for the negative slope of $F^{n}_{2}(x)/F^{p}_{2}(x)$, attraction between scalar $u-d$ quark pairs and incorporation of boosts are required. The two-body spin force between quarks from color magnetism also leads to a negative slope. The polarization asymmetry $A^{p}_{1}$ is in fair agreement with the EMC data in the valence quark region. Revised hep-ph/9401342, Phys. Rev. D.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the negative slope of the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio \(F^{n}_{2}(x)/F^{p}_{2}(x)\) and the polarization asymmetry \(A^{p}_{1}(x)\) can be explained in the valence quark region by incorporating attraction between scalar \(u-d\) quark pairs and quark boosts into a light-cone constituent quark model, provided the structure functions are evolved from a low scale where the model is valid rather than from the parton model limit.

## Method

The author constructs a light-cone quark model for the nucleon using a relativistic Gaussian wave function with a deformation parameter \(D\) that simulates the effect of the spin force from color magnetism, making scalar \(u-d\) quark pairs smaller and lighter. Deep inelastic structure functions are computed from the hadronic tensor at a low scale \(\mu \sim 0.25\) GeV and then evolved via perturbative QCD from \((-q^{2})=(0.6\ \text{GeV})^{2}\) to \(-q^{2}\sim 15\ \text{GeV}^{2}\), avoiding the unreliable evolution from the parton model limit. The model also incorporates a mixed \([56,0^{+}]\) and \([70,0^{+}]\) \(SU(6)\) configuration to represent the spin force from color magnetism.

## Key numbers

* The deformation parameter \(D=0.37\) is obtained from fitting the ratio \(F^{n}_{2}(x)/F^{p}_{2}(x)\), corresponding to attraction between scalar \(u-d\) quark pairs.
* For \(m_{q}=0.33\ \text{GeV}/c^{2}\), the model yields nucleon magnetic moments \(\mu_{p}=2.475\) n.m. and \(\mu_{n}=-1.58\) n.m.
* The low energy scale is \(\mu\sim 0.25\) GeV, extracted from the second moment \(\langle F^{N}_{2}(-q^{2}=15\ \text{GeV}^{2})\rangle_{2}=0.127\) of the average nucleon structure function from EMC data for the deuteron.
* Structure functions are evolved from \((-q^{2})=(0.6\ \text{GeV})^{2}\) to \(-q^{2}\sim 15\ \text{GeV}^{2}\).
* The logarithmic ratio \(L=14.7\) is used, based on \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\sim 0.2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone quark model of Weber (1994) to deep inelastic scattering, providing a link between the parton model and constituent quark models. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic quark model of Isgur and Karl, where boosts are missing and the \(SU(6)\)-symmetric wave function gives a constant ratio \(F^{n}_{2}(x)/F^{p}_{2}(x)=2/3\), in disagreement with EMC data. The results enable future studies of spin-dependent structure functions and the role of sea quarks at low \(x\), and suggest that ratios of structure functions are more reliable observables than individual distributions due to smaller QCD evolution uncertainties.
