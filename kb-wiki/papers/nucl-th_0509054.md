# D-mesons: In-medium effects at FAIR

> The $D$-meson spectral density at finite temperature is obtained within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach. For the bare meson-baryon interaction, a separable potential is taken, whose parameters are fixed by the position and width of the $Λ_c (2593)$ resonance. The quasiparticle peak stays close to the free $D$-meson mass, indicating a small change in the effective mass for finite density and temperature. Furthermore, the spectral density develops a considerable width due to the coupled-channel structure. Our results indicate that the medium modifications for the $D$-mesons in nucleus-nucleus collisions at FAIR (GSI) will be dominantly on the width and not, as previously expected, on the mass.

## Key claim

The authors claim that in-medium modifications of D-mesons in hot and dense nuclear matter will be dominantly on the width (broadening of the spectral density) and not on the mass, contrary to previous expectations from mean-field models that predicted large attractive mass shifts of -50 to -200 MeV.

## Method

The authors perform a self-consistent coupled-channel G-matrix calculation for the D-meson spectral density in symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, using a separable s-wave potential for the bare DN interaction whose parameters are fixed by the position and width of the Λc(2593) resonance. The calculation includes Pauli blocking on nucleons, Bose distributions on pionic intermediate states, and the dressing of D-mesons, nucleons, and pions in the self-consistent process, with nucleon properties described by a temperature-dependent Walecka-type σ-ω model.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature and ρ0 = 0.17 fm⁻³, the D-meson quasiparticle peak shift is -23 MeV when only D-mesons and nucleons are dressed, and -5 MeV for the full self-consistent calculation including pion dressing.
- At T = 120 MeV and ρ0, the quasiparticle peak shift is -1 MeV for the first approach and -0.1 MeV for the full calculation.
- At T = 120 MeV, the width of the D-meson spectral density increases from 52 MeV at ρ0 to 163 MeV at 3ρ0 when D-mesons and nucleons are dressed; for the full calculation, the width goes from 36 MeV at ρ0 to 107 MeV at 3ρ0.
- The strength of the spectral density at the quasiparticle peak is reduced by a factor of about three when density changes from ρ0 to 3ρ0.
- The in-medium elastic cross sections at threshold are on the order of 1 mb to 20 mb for the range of densities studied.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature coupled-channel calculation (Tolos 2004) to finite temperature, and contrasts with earlier mean-field predictions from the quark-meson coupling model (QMC) and QCD sum-rules (Arata 2000, Weise 2001) that predicted D-meson mass shifts of -50 to -200 MeV. The results support a more recent coupled-channel calculation (Lutz 2005) that found a repulsive D-meson potential. The findings imply that open-charm enhancement at the CBM experiment at FAIR will depend on the off-shell behavior of D-mesons in hot and dense matter rather than on mass shifts, requiring off-shell transport theory for accurate predictions.
