# Far-from-equilibrium attractors in kinetic theory for a mixture of quark and gluon fluids

> We exactly solve an RTA-Boltzmann equation that describes the dynamics of coupled massless quark and gluon fluids undergoing transversally homogeneous longitudinal boost-invariant expansion. We include a fugacity parameter that allows quarks to be out of chemical equilibrium and we account for the different collision rates of quarks and gluons, which are related by Casimir scaling. Based on these assumptions, we numerically determine the evolution of a large set of moments of the quark and gluon distribution functions and reconstruct their entire distribution functions. We find that both late and early-time attractors exist for all moments of the distribution functions containing more than one power of the squared longitudinal momentum. These attractors emerge long before the system reaches the regime where hydrodynamic approximations apply. In addition, we discuss how the shear viscous corrections and entropy density of the fluid mixture evolve and consider the properties of their respective attractors. Finally, the entropy production is also investigated for different initial values of momentum anisotropy and quark abundance.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that far-from-equilibrium attractors exist for all moments of the quark and gluon distribution functions containing more than one power of the squared longitudinal momentum, even when quarks and gluons have different relaxation times related by Casimir scaling and quarks are initially out of chemical equilibrium; these attractors emerge long before the system reaches the regime where hydrodynamic approximations apply.

## Method

The authors exactly solve the relaxation-time-approximation (RTA) Boltzmann equation for a mixture of massless quark and gluon fluids undergoing transversally homogeneous longitudinal boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion, using separate time-dependent relaxation times for quarks and gluons related by Casimir scaling with ratio \(C_R = \tau_{{\rm eq},g}/\tau_{{\rm eq},q} = 4/9\). They include a fugacity parameter \(\gamma_{q,0}\) to allow quarks to be initially out of chemical equilibrium, couple the two species through a generalized Landau matching condition, and numerically determine the evolution of a large set of moments of the distribution functions as well as reconstructing the full distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of gluon to quark relaxation times is set to \(C_R = \tau_{{\rm eq},g}/\tau_{{\rm eq},q} = 4/9\), based on Casimir scaling from the eigenvalues of the Casimir operator in the fundamental and adjoint representations of \(SU(3)\).
- The gluon relaxation time is defined as \(\tau_{{\rm eq}, g} = 5 (\eta/s) / T(\tau)\), with the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) taken as a time-independent parameter.
- The gluon degeneracy factor is \(g_g = 2 \times 8\) (polarization and color), and the quark degeneracy factor is \(g_q = 2 \times 3 \times 3\) (spin, color, and flavor).
- The initial momentum anisotropy is parametrized by \(\xi_0 \in (-1, +\infty)\) or equivalently \(\alpha_0 = (1+\xi_0)^{-1/2}\), with \(\alpha_0 \in (0,1)\) for the physically relevant case of positive \(\xi_0\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-component systems in kinetic theory, such as Florkowski et al. (2017) who studied a two-component system with a single constant relaxation time for quarks and gluons and found a forward attractor for the pressure anisotropy. The present paper goes beyond by including separate time-dependent relaxation times for quarks and gluons related by Casimir scaling, a fugacity parameter for chemical non-equilibrium, and by examining both forward and pullback attractors for a large set of moments of the distribution functions. This enables a more realistic description of the early-time non-equilibrium dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions and establishes the existence of attractors that could allow hydrodynamic descriptions on sub-fm/\(c\) timescales.
