# Dense Nuclear Matter: Landau Fermi-Liquid Theory and Chiral Lagrangian with Scaling

> The relation between the effective chiral Lagrangian whose parameters scale according to Brown and Rho scaling("BR scaling") and Landau Fermi-liquid theory for hadronic matter is discussed in order to make a basis to describe the fluctuations under the extreme condition relevant to neutron stars. It is suggested that BR scaling gives the background around which the fluctuations are weak. A simple model with BR-scaled parameters is constructed and reproduces the properties of the nuclear ground state at normal nuclear matter density successfully. It shows that the tree level in the model Lagrangian is enough to describe the fluctuations around BR-scaled background. The model Lagrangian is consistent thermodynamically and reproduces relativistic Landau Fermi-liquid properties. Such points are important for dealing with hadronic matter under extreme condition. On the other hand it is shown that the vector current obtained from the chiral Lagrangian is the same as that obtained from Landau-Migdal approach. We can determine the Landau parameter in terms of BR-scaled parameter. However these two approaches provide different results, when applied to the axial charge. The numerical difference is small. It shows that the axial response is not included properly in the Landau-Migdal approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an effective chiral Lagrangian with Brown-Rho (BR) scaling can be mapped to Landau Fermi-liquid theory, providing a unified framework where the BR-scaled mean field defines the background around which fluctuations are weak; the tree-level Lagrangian is sufficient to describe these fluctuations, but the axial charge response from the chiral Lagrangian and the Landau-Migdal approach show a small numerical difference, indicating the axial response is not properly included in the Landau-Migdal approach.

## Method

The author constructs a simple model with BR-scaled parameters (where hadron masses and the pion decay constant scale universally with density) and computes the nuclear ground state at normal nuclear matter density using mean-field theory. The model is then compared to the FTS1 Lagrangian of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (which imposes QCD symmetries and naturalness) to show that the BR-scaled model reproduces the same nuclear matter properties with weak coupling. The vector and axial currents from the chiral Lagrangian are derived and compared to those from Landau-Migdal theory, with the Landau parameter \(F_1^\omega\) expressed in terms of BR-scaled parameters.

## Key numbers

- The compression modulus \(K\) is given by \(K = \frac{3k_F^2}{m_L^\star}(1+F_0)\), where \(m_L^\star\) is the Landau effective mass and \(F_0\) is the Landau parameter.
- The density of states at the Fermi surface is \(N(0) = \frac{\gamma k_F m_L^\star}{2\pi^2}\) with \(\gamma = 4\) for symmetric nuclear matter.
- The universal BR scaling relation is \(\Phi(\rho) \approx \frac{f_\pi^\star(\rho)}{f_\pi} \approx \frac{m_v^\star(\rho)}{m_v} \approx \frac{m_\sigma^\star(\rho)}{m_\sigma} \approx \frac{M^\star(\rho)}{M}\), where the star denotes in-medium quantities.
- The quasiparticle lifetime at \(T=0\) is proportional to \(|\epsilon - \epsilon_F|^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) from meson properties in dense matter (successfully used to describe dilepton enhancement in CERES and HELIOS-3 experiments) to baryon properties and the nuclear matter ground state. It builds on the effective field theory approach of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (FTS1) and the renormalization group understanding of Landau Fermi-liquid theory as a fixed point (Shankar, 1994; Froehlich, 1995). The results enable a consistent treatment of both the ground state and fluctuations in dense hadronic matter, which is necessary for extrapolating to higher densities relevant to neutron stars and kaon condensation.
