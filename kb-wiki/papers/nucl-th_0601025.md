# Schiff Theorem and the Electric Dipole Moments of Hydrogen-Like Atoms

> The Schiff theorem is revisited in this work and the residual $P$- and $T$-odd electron--nucleus interaction, after the shielding takes effect, is completely specified. An application is made to the electric dipole moments of hydrogen-like atoms, whose qualitative features and systematics have important implication for realistic paramagnetic atoms.

## Key claim

The paper revisits the Schiff theorem to completely specify the residual parity- and time-reversal-odd electron-nucleus interaction after electrostatic shielding, finding that the conventional definition of the Schiff moment differs from their operator-level derivation and that this difference can be significant for loosely bound nuclei like the deuteron.

## Method

The authors re-derive the Schiff theorem at the operator level, treating the nuclear electric dipole moment as an operator rather than taking its matrix element before the shielding derivation. They then apply the resulting residual interaction to hydrogen-like atoms by solving the Sternheimer equation for the parity-admixed ground state, separating contributions from the electron EDM, semi-leptonic interactions, the nuclear Schiff moment, and magnetic equivalents.

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron Schiff moment, the three terms in the new definition contribute as 1 : -5/3 : -4/3, while in the traditional definition (including the quadrupole term) the ratio becomes 1 : -0.59 : -0.07.
- Contributions to hydrogen-like atom EDMs from the electron EDM scale roughly as Z^2, from semi-leptonic interactions as Z times A, from the Schiff moment as Z times S, and from the magnetic Schiff moment as Z times S^mag.
- The Schiff moment S and its magnetic equivalent S^mag both involve r^2-weighted nuclear moments and roughly scale with A^(2/3).

## Context

This work extends the original Schiff theorem (Schiff 1963) and subsequent treatments by Sandars (1968), Feinberg (1977), Sushkov (1984), Engel (1999), and Flambaum (2001) by providing a complete operator-level derivation that includes magnetic electron-nucleus interactions and a more careful treatment of finite-size effects. The results enable more reliable calculations of atomic EDMs for paramagnetic atoms, showing that the common assumption of electron EDM dominance in heavy paramagnetic atoms may be questionable if semi-leptonic or hadronic parity- and time-reversal-odd interactions are large, motivating simultaneous constraints from multiple EDM measurements.
