# A closer look at the Yukawa's interaction from a symmetry group perspective

> I investigate the use of the SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients in light of the relations of completeness and closure. I show that in the case of $α_V = F/(F+D)~\neq$ 1, there is an additional interaction: the exchange of a $ρ$ meson between a $Λ$ and a $Σ^0$ hyperon that only affects the symmetric coupling. I then calculate these additional coupling constants and show that this recovers the completeness and closure of the SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for all values of $α_V$. Besides, it increases the symmetry of the theory, once now we can group the baryon octet into four doublets. Finally, I add the new coupling constants to study numerical results in the hyperon onset in dense nuclear matter assuming $α_V$ as a free parameter.

# A closer look at the Yukawa's interaction from a symmetry group perspective

## Key claim
The author shows that the standard set of SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for baryon-vector meson couplings violates the relations of completeness and closure for the symmetric coupling when the parameter α_V = F/(F+D) is not equal to 1, and that including a previously neglected crossed Yukawa coupling (ρ meson exchange between Λ and Σ^0 hyperons) restores completeness for all values of α_V.

## Method
The author calculates the SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for baryon-vector meson couplings using the Speiser method and the isoscalar factor tables from Swart (1963). The completeness and closure relations are checked for both symmetric and antisymmetric coupling channels. The missing crossed coupling constants g_{Σ^0Λρ} and g_{ΛΣ^0ρ} are computed from the SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, then added to a quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) Lagrangian in mean-field approximation to study dense nuclear matter and neutron star properties with α_V treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers
- The standard symmetric coupling sum of squared Clebsch-Gordan coefficients ΣC_8^2 = 0.6, while the antisymmetric sum ΣC'_8^2 = 1 and the singlet sum ΣC_1^2 = 0.75
- The crossed coupling ratio: g_{Σ^0Λρ}/g_{NNρ} = (2/3)√3(1 - α_V)
- For α_V = 0.25 with the eL3ωρ parametrization, the Λ threshold shifts from 0.4114 fm^{-3} to 0.4416 fm^{-3} (approximately 10% increase), while the Ξ^- threshold shifts from 0.5821 fm^{-3} to 0.5168 fm^{-3} (approximately 10% decrease)
- For α_V = 0.25 with the GM1 parametrization, the Λ threshold shifts from 0.3264 fm^{-3} to 0.4405 fm^{-3} (approximately 35% increase), while the Ξ^- threshold shifts from 0.4079 fm^{-3} to 0.3655 fm^{-3} (approximately 10% decrease)
- Maximum neutron star masses: eL3ωρ without crossed term = 2.202 M_⊙, with crossed term = 2.206 M_⊙; GM1 without = 2.223 M_⊙, with = 2.208 M_⊙
- Radius at 2.0 M_⊙: eL3ωρ without = 12.379 km, with = 12.420 km; GM1 without = 13.092 km, with = 13.193 km
- For neutron-Λ matter with eL3ωρ, Λ threshold shifts from 0.3634 fm^{-3} to 0.4164 fm^{-3} (approximately 15% increase); with GM1, from 0.2819 fm^{-3} to 0.3586 fm^{-3} (greater than 25% increase)

## Context
This work extends the flavor SU(3) symmetry approach to baryon-vector meson couplings used in quantum hadrodynamics, which was previously applied by Miyatsu (2013), Lopes (2022), and others to study hyperonic neutron star matter. The standard SU(6) symmetry (which gives α_V = 1.00) was widely used following Ellis (1991), Schaffner (1994, 1996), and Tolos (2017), but the discovery of massive neutron stars like PSR J0740+6620 (2.08 ± 0.07 M_⊙, Miller 2021, Riley 2021) created tension with hyperonic equations of state. The author shows that the previously neglected g_{Σ^0Λρ} coupling, supported by experimental coherent Λ-Σ coupling data (Akaishi 2000, 2002), must be included for consistency when α_V ≠ 1, and demonstrates its effects on hyperon thresholds and neutron star properties. This enables more complete SU(3)-based calculations for hypernuclei, hyperon-nucleon scattering (Rijken 2010), and neutron star modeling.
