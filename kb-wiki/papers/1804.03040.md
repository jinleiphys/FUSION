# Zero-temperature limit and statistical quasiparticles in many-body perturbation theory

> The order-by-order renormalization of the self-consistent mean-field potential in many-body perturbation theory for normal Fermi systems is investigated in detail. Building on previous work mainly by Balian and de Dominicis, as a key result we derive a thermodynamic perturbation series that manifests the consistency of the adiabatic zero-temperature formalism with perturbative statistical mechanics---for both isotropic and anisotropic systems---and satisfies at each order and for all temperatures the thermodynamic relations associated with Fermiliquid theory. These properties are proved to all orders.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the Balian-de Dominicis (BdD) renormalization scheme for many-body perturbation theory yields a thermodynamic perturbation series whose zero-temperature limit reproduces the adiabatic ground-state energy to all orders, and which satisfies Fermi-liquid thermodynamic relations at every order and all temperatures, but only in the thermodynamic limit for truncation orders beyond second order.

## Method

The author revisits the order-by-order renormalization of the reference Hamiltonian in bare grand-canonical many-body perturbation theory, building on the scheme outlined by Balian and de Dominicis. The analysis uses time-independent formulas (direct, cyclic, and reduced forms) for the grand-canonical potential, classifies diagrams as normal, anomalous, or pseudoanomalous, and applies a regularization procedure to handle energy-denominator poles introduced by the BdD scheme. The consistency of the zero-temperature limit with the adiabatic formalism and the Fermi-liquid relations is proved to all orders.

## Key numbers

* The BdD scheme leads to a mean field of functional form \(U[n_{\mathbf{k}}]\) with no explicit temperature dependence apart from the Fermi-Dirac distributions, so the \(T\rightarrow 0\) limit exists.
* For \(N>2\), the BdD scheme exists only in the thermodynamic limit; for \(N\leq 2\) it converges to the same scheme for finite systems.
* At \(T=0\), anomalous contributions involve terms \(\partial^\nu n_{\mathbf{k}}/\partial\mu^\nu \rightarrow \delta^{(\nu)}(\mu-\varepsilon_{\mathbf{k}})\), e.g., \(\beta n_{\mathbf{k}}\bar{n}_{\mathbf{k}} = \partial n_{\mathbf{k}}/\partial\mu \rightarrow \delta(\mu-\varepsilon_{\mathbf{k}})\).
* Pseudoanomalous contributions vanish for \(T\rightarrow 0\), e.g., \(n_{\mathbf{k}}\bar{n}_{\mathbf{k}} = T\,\partial n_{\mathbf{k}}/\partial\mu \rightarrow 0\).

## Context

This work extends the formal analysis of Balian and de Dominicis (1960s) on mean-field renormalization in many-body perturbation theory, and addresses issues raised by Holt and Kaiser (2017) regarding the use of a second-order mean field in nuclear matter calculations. The paper clarifies that the BdD scheme resolves the inconsistency between the adiabatic zero-temperature formalism and grand-canonical perturbation theory for both isotropic and anisotropic systems, which was previously only partially resolved by the modified perturbation series \(F(T,\mu_{\text{ref}})\) of Kohn and Luttinger (1960). The results enable consistent perturbative calculations of thermodynamic properties of normal Fermi systems at low temperatures, including nuclear matter.
