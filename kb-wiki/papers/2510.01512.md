# Nonlinear causality and strong hyperbolicity of baryon-rich Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics

> We present the first set of fully-nonlinear, necessary and sufficient conditions guaranteeing causal evolution of the initial data for the Israel-Stewart hydrodynamic equations with shear and bulk viscosity coupled to a nonzero baryon current. These constraints not only provide nonlinear causality: they also (a) guarantee the existence of a locally well-posed evolution of the initial data (they enforce strong hyperbolicity) when excluding the endpoints of the bounds, (b) arise from purely algebraic constraints that make no underlying symmetry assumptions on the degrees of freedom and (c) propagate the relevant symmetries of the degrees of freedom over the entire evolution of the problem. Our work enforces a mathematically rigorous foundation for future studies of viscous relativistic hydrodynamics with baryon-rich matter including neutron star mergers and heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first set of fully-nonlinear, necessary and sufficient algebraic conditions that guarantee causal evolution of initial data for Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics with shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and a nonzero baryon current; these conditions also enforce strong hyperbolicity (local well-posedness) when the endpoints of the bounds are excluded.

## Method

The authors cast the Israel-Stewart equations (bulk and shear relaxation equations coupled to energy-momentum conservation and baryon number conservation) into a quasilinear first-order matrix form with a 23-component state vector. They analyze the characteristic determinant of the system, factor it into a product of polynomials, and derive algebraic constraints on the coefficients of a cubic polynomial that ensure all characteristic speeds are real and bounded by the speed of light. The constraints are expressed as inequalities involving the transport coefficients, the energy density, pressure, bulk viscous pressure, and the eigenvalues of the shear stress tensor, with no assumptions on the spacetime metric or equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The state vector has 23 components: \( (u^\nu, \varepsilon, n, \Pi, \pi^{\nu 0}, \pi^{\nu 1}, \pi^{\nu 2}, \pi^{\nu 3})^T \).
- The characteristic determinant factor includes a term \( \hat{x}^{15} \) and a cubic polynomial \( \mathcal{P}_3(\hat{x}^2, \hat{v}_a^2) = \hat{x}^6 - \mathcal{A}_2 \hat{x}^4 + \mathcal{A}_1 \hat{x}^2 - \mathcal{A}_0 \).
- The shear mode condition requires \( 0 \leq \frac{\frac{1}{2} (2\eta + \lambda_{\pi\Pi}\Pi) + \frac{1}{4} \tau_{\pi\pi}\Lambda_{\hat{v}^2}}{\tau_\pi E} \leq 1 \).
- The causality conditions require \( 0 \leq \mathcal{A}_2/3 \leq 1 \), \( 0 \leq \mathcal{A}_1 \), \( 0 \leq \mathcal{A}_0 \), and \( 0 \leq 1 - \mathcal{A}_2 + \mathcal{A}_1 - \mathcal{A}_0 \), and \( 0 \leq 1 - \frac{2}{3}\mathcal{A}_2 + \frac{1}{3}\mathcal{A}_1 \), all holding for \( \chi^2, \kappa^2 \in [0,1] \).

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear causality analysis of Bemfica et al. (2020) for Israel-Stewart theory with bulk and shear viscosity to include a nonzero baryon current, and improves upon that work by providing conditions that are simultaneously necessary and sufficient rather than separate necessary and sufficient sets. It also generalizes earlier linear causality and stability results by Hiscock and Lindblom (1983, 1988) and the nonlinear causality constraints for Israel-Stewart theory with number diffusion by Cordeiro et al. (2025). The results enable rigorous numerical simulations of viscous relativistic hydrodynamics in baryon-rich systems such as neutron star mergers and heavy-ion collisions.
