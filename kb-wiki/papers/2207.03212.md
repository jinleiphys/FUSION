# Structure formation during phase transitions in strongly interacting matter

> A broad range of problems associated with phase transitions in systems characterized by the strong interaction between particles and with formation of structures is reviewed. A general phenomenological mean-field model is constructed describing phase transitions of the first and the second order to the $k_0=0$ and $\vec{k}_0\neq 0$ states. Due to fluctuations, the phase transition to the state $\vec{k}_0\neq 0$ is the transition of the first order. Various specific features of the phase transitions to the state $\vec{k}_0\neq 0$ are considered such as the anisotropic spectrum of excitations, a possibility of the formation of various structures including running and standing waves, three-axis structures, chiral waves, pasta phases, etc. A formal transition to hydrodynamical variables is performed. Then focus is made on description of the dynamics of the order parameter at the phase transitions to the states with $\vec{k}_0= 0$ and $\vec{k}_0\neq 0$. Next the non-ideal hydrodynamical description of the phase transitions of the liquid-gas type in nuclear systems is performed. Quasiperiodic structures are developed. Next, pion condensation phase transition to the $\vec{k}_0\neq 0$ state in dense, cold or warm nuclear matter is considered and then the high temperature -- small baryon chemical potential system is studied, when baryons become completely blurred and light bosons, e.g., pions, may condense either in $\vec{k}_0= 0$ or $\vec{k}_0\neq 0$ states. Then, for scalar collective modes phenomena of the Pomeranchuk instability and the Bose condensation in $\vec{k}_0= 0$ or $\vec{k}_0\neq 0$ states are studied. Next, possibility of the condensation of Bose excitations in the $\vec{k}_0\neq 0$ state in moving media is considered. Then Bose-Einstein condensation of pions at dynamically fixed number of particles is studied and specific non-equilibrium effects are demonstrated on an example.

## Key claim

This review argues that phase transitions to inhomogeneous states (with wavevector \(\vec{k}_0 \neq 0\)) in strongly interacting matter are necessarily first-order due to fluctuations, even when mean-field theory would predict a second-order transition, and that such transitions produce a rich variety of structures including running and standing waves, three-axis structures, chiral waves, and pasta phases.

## Method

The paper constructs a general phenomenological mean-field model for phase transitions to both homogeneous (\(k_0 = 0\)) and inhomogeneous (\(\vec{k}_0 \neq 0\)) states, then extends it by incorporating fluctuation effects. It performs a formal transition to hydrodynamical variables and derives dynamical equations for the order parameter. Specific applications are worked out for pion condensation in dense nuclear matter, for the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear systems, for Bose condensation of pions at high temperature, for scalar collective modes exhibiting Pomeranchuk instability, and for Bose-Einstein condensation in moving media and nonequilibrium systems.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3} \simeq 0.5 m_\pi^3\), with pion mass \(m_\pi \simeq 140\ \text{MeV}\)
- Pseudo-critical temperature for QCD crossover from hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma: \(T_c \simeq (155-160)\ \text{MeV}\) from hadron resonance model fits
- Lattice QCD prediction for chiral critical temperature: \(T_{\text{chir}} < 135\ \text{MeV}\)
- Liquid/amorphous phase of pion condensate appears for nucleon density \(n > n_c^{(1)} \sim (0.5-0.7)n_0\)
- Solid-like pion condensate phase appears for \(n > n_c^\pi \sim (1.5-3)n_0\)
- Baryon density in centers of heaviest compact stars may reach \((6-8)n_0\)
- Perturbative QCD description expected valid only for baryon densities \(n \gtrsim 40 n_0\)
- Magnetic field strength in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at energies \(\lesssim\) few GeV/A: \(H \sim (10^{17}-10^{18})\ \text{G}\)
- Rotation frequencies in heavy-ion collisions: \(\omega \lesssim 10^{22}\ \text{Hz}\)
- Critical endpoint bounded by \(\mu_B \gtrsim 2.5 T\) and \(T \lesssim m_\pi\)
- Gas-liquid phase transition occurs at low temperature \(T \lesssim (15-20)\ \text{MeV}\) in nucleon density interval \(0.3 n_0 \lesssim n \lesssim 0.7 n_0\)

## Context

This review extends the line of work by Migdal (1978) on pion condensation in nuclear matter, by Brazovskii (1975) and Dyugaev (1975) on fluctuation-induced first-order transitions to inhomogeneous states, and by Voskresensky (1984, 1993) on dynamics of inhomogeneous condensate formation. It also builds on the Ginzburg-Landau theory for superconductors and the Ginzburg-Pitaevskii theory for neutral superfluids. The review enables future experimental searches for structure formation in heavy-ion collisions at NICA and FAIR facilities, and provides theoretical tools for interpreting gravitational wave signals from neutron star mergers and NICER observations of pulsar mass-radius relations.
