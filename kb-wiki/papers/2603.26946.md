# Signatures from pion condensation and lepton flavor asymmetries in the cosmological gravitational wave background

> Large lepton flavor asymmetries at the QCD epoch could generate a pion condensation phase in the early Universe. For large enough tau lepton flavor asymmetries, the speed of sound can exceed the conformal value, leaving a distinctive imprint on the low-frequency gravitational wave (GW) spectrum from causal sources. Beyond probing the formation of a pion condensation phase, the detection or non-detection of this signature would provide a novel constraint on lepton asymmetries in the early Universe. We estimate the GW signal and compare it with the standard case of vanishing lepton asymmetry. Finally, we discuss the implications for the stochastic GW background reported by Pulsar Timing Arrays, using the NANOGrav 15-year dataset.

# Signatures from pion condensation and lepton flavor asymmetries in the cosmological gravitational wave background

## Key claim

If sufficiently large tau lepton flavor asymmetries existed at the QCD epoch, the speed of sound in the early Universe could exceed the conformal value (w = 1/3) due to pion condensation, leaving a distinctive imprint on the low-frequency gravitational wave spectrum from causal sources; the detection or non-detection of this signature would provide a novel constraint on lepton asymmetries, but the effect depends on the specific model used for the high-isospin QCD equation of state and requires improved PTA data to be conclusive.

## Method

The authors model the cosmic trajectory by imposing charge neutrality and conservation of baryon and lepton numbers, using two benchmark equations of state for high-isospin QCD: an HRG-Lattice interpolation (HRGLatt) for tau lepton asymmetries l_tau = -0.3 and -0.6, and a Gaussian model that mimics a stronger peak in the speed of sound. They compute the gravitational wave spectrum and spectral tilt from the causality tail approximation, assuming a short-lived source generated before the QCD epoch at T_star > 300 MeV, and perform a Bayesian analysis using the NANOGrav 15-year dataset via the PTArcade wrapper to compare model predictions with observations.

## Key numbers

- The minimum tau lepton asymmetry required to reach the conformal value w = 1/3 in the HRGLatt model is |l_tau| ≈ 0.3
- Pion condensation occurs when the charge chemical potential mu_Q exceeds approximately 140 MeV for temperatures below about 160 MeV
- At zero temperature, the QCD speed of sound peaks above the conformal limit at an isospin chemical potential mu_I ~ 1.5 m_pi (with m_pi = 170 MeV used in Ref. Abbott 2024)
- The reference frequency for the GW spectrum is f_yr = 32 nHz
- The Bayes factor comparing the model to a Supermassive Black Hole Background is B = -0.35, showing slight preference for the SMBHB

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ref. Franciolini (2023) on imprints from the Lattice QCD equation of state on the causality tail, which assumed lepton asymmetries of order 10^{-11}. It builds on predictions from Refs. Vovchenko (2020) and Middeldorf-Wygas (2020) that large lepton asymmetries can generate high charge chemical potentials leading to pion condensation, and on Lattice QCD results from Brandt (2022, 2024) and Abbott (2023, 2024) showing that the speed of sound peaks above the conformal limit at high isospin. The work enables future PTA observations to constrain both pion condensation and primordial lepton flavor asymmetries, which are otherwise difficult to probe before BBN as noted by Domcke (2025) and Froustey (2021, 2024).
