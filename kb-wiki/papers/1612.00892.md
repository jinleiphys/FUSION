# Resolving the $R_{AA}$ to $v_n$ puzzle

> After 10 years of struggling to simultaneously describe the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and flow harmonics $v_n$'s at high $p_T$, now theoretical models are able to reproduce experimental data well. The necessary theoretical developments such as event-by-event fluctuations, choice of initial conditions, and the scalar product method to calculate flow harmonics at high $p_T$ are reviewed. Additionally, a discussion of new proposed experimental observables known as Soft Hard Event Engineering (SHEE) that are sensitive to the path length dependence of the energy loss is included.

## Key claim

The decade-old puzzle of simultaneously describing the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and flow harmonics \(v_n\) at high transverse momentum has been resolved, but only when event-by-event fluctuations, realistic initial conditions that reproduce soft-sector flow harmonics, and the scalar product method for calculating high-\(p_T\) flow harmonics are all included.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical calculations using the v-USPhydro+BBMG framework with event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics coupled to jet energy loss models, comparing MCGlauber and MCKLN initial conditions. High-\(p_T\) flow harmonics are computed via the scalar product method, correlating one high-\(p_T\) particle with soft particles to obtain \(v_n\{2\}(p_T)\). The paper also discusses Soft Hard Event Engineering (SHEE), where events within a centrality class are sorted by their integrated soft \(v_2\) and the corresponding high-\(p_T\) \(v_2\) is then calculated in those bins.

## Key numbers

* MCKLN initial conditions produce \(\sim 30\%\) larger eccentricities \(\varepsilon_2\) than MCGlauber, leading to a \(\sim 30\%\) increase in \(v_2\{2\}(p_T)\) at high \(p_T\).
* ATLAS has measured \(v_2\) through \(v_7\) up to \(p_T = 25\) GeV for \(0-5\%\) centrality at LHC PbPb \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
* CMS has measured \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) up to \(p_T \sim 100\) GeV for \(0-5\%\) centrality.
* SHEE of \(v_2\) at \(p_T = 10\) GeV shows a clear splitting depending on the path length dependence of energy loss, where \(dE/dx \sim L^n\) with larger \(n\) produces a steeper slope.
* The ratio \(v_2\{4\}(p_T)/v_2\{2\}(p_T)\) approaches 1 around \(p_T \sim 10\) GeV for \(0-5\%\) centrality, while the integrated ratio is \(v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\} < 0.8\).
* Multiplicity weighing and centrality rebinning have up to a \(5\%\) effect on high-\(p_T\) multiparticle cumulants.

## Context

This work extends the decade-long effort to reconcile \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) measurements, which had previously been described separately (see discussions in Betz (2014) and Xu (2014)). The key advance builds on the finding by Noronha-Hostler (2016) that event-by-event fluctuations and realistic initial conditions solve the puzzle. The paper reviews how initial conditions that fit soft-sector flow harmonics (e.g., EKRT from Niemi (2015)) are now being applied to high-\(p_T\) observables, and proposes SHEE as a new observable to distinguish between energy loss models, following the experimental work by ATLAS (Aad 2015) on soft-hard correlations. This enables future Bayesian analyses that simultaneously fit soft and hard observables, as already underway in the heavy flavor sector.
