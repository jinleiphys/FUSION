# Coulomb Breakup Reactions in Complex-Scaled Solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger Equation

> We propose a new method to describe three-body breakups of nuclei, in which the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved combining with the complex scaling method. The complex-scaled solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (CSLS) enables us to treat boundary conditions of many-body open channels correctly and to describe a many-body breakup amplitude from the ground state. The Coulomb breakup cross section from the 6He ground state into 4He+n+n three-body decaying states as a function of the total excitation energy is calculated by using CSLS, and the result well reproduces the experimental data. Furthermore, the two-dimensional energy distribution of the E1 transition strength is obtained and an importance of the 5He(3/2-) resonance is confirmed. It is shown that CSLS is a promising method to investigate correlations of subsystems in three-body breakup reactions of the weakly-bound nuclei.

## Key claim

The complex-scaled solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (CSLS) can describe three-body breakup amplitudes as explicit functions of subsystem energies, but the method requires switching off the pseudo potential in the scattering-state calculation to avoid numerical instability, which approximately ignores antisymmetrization in the scattering state.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for three-body scattering states by replacing the standard Green's function with a complex-scaled Green's function constructed from the eigenstates of the complex-scaled Hamiltonian. This Green's function is expanded using the extended completeness relation, allowing the outgoing and incoming scattering states to be expressed as the asymptotic plane wave plus a sum over complex-scaled bound, resonant, and continuum states. The method is applied to a ⁴He+n+n three-body model of ⁶He using the orthogonality condition model with Gaussian basis functions, and the E1 transition strength and Coulomb breakup cross section are computed from the resulting scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The ⁶He ground state two-neutron binding energy is 0.975 MeV and the matter radius is 2.46 fm.
- The E1 transition strength of ⁶He shows a sharp peak at around 1 MeV above the ⁴He+n+n threshold.
- The two-dimensional energy distribution of the E1 strength is concentrated at an ⁴He-n relative energy of approximately 0.7 MeV, matching the ⁵He(3/2⁻) resonance energy.
- The calculation uses 15 Gaussian bases for each relative coordinate with a range up to about 20 fm and a complex scaling angle of 18 degrees (or 27 degrees for the 1⁻ spectra).
- The effective αnn three-body interaction uses V₃ = -1.503 MeV and ν = 0.07/b_c² fm⁻², with b_c = 1.4 fm for the ⁴He core oscillator length.

## Context

This work extends the two-body scattering approach of Kruglanski (2007) to three-body systems, building on previous studies of ⁶He and ¹¹Li Coulomb breakup using the response function method combined with complex scaling by Myo et al. (2001, 2003, 2007). The CSLS method enables direct calculation of observables as functions of subsystem energies, which is essential for comparing with experimental reanalyses of breakup data such as that by Chatterjee (2005), and for investigating internal correlations in two-neutron halo nuclei like ⁶He and ¹¹Li.
