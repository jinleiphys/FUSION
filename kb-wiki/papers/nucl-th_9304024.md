# Approximate Particle Number Projection for Rotating Nuclei

> Pairing correlations in rotating nuclei are discussed within the Lipkin-Nogami method. The accuracy of the method is tested for the Krumlinde-Szymański R(5) model. The results of calculations are compared with those obtained from the standard mean field theory and particle-number projection method, and with exact solutions.

## Key claim

The Lipkin-Nogami (LN) method, when extended to rotating nuclei (cranked LN, or LNC), provides a simple approximation that suppresses particle-number fluctuations and yields nontrivial superfluid solutions even where the standard cranked BCS (RBCS) method fails, but its accuracy depends on pairing strength: it works well for strong pairing and in the phase-transition region, while in the weak-pairing limit the LNC wave function must be combined with exact particle-number projection (PNP) to achieve fair agreement with exact results.

## Method

The authors derive cranked Lipkin-Nogami (LNC) equations by minimizing the expectation value of an auxiliary Routhian \(\hat{K}^{\omega} = \hat{H} - \lambda_1 \hat{N} - \lambda_2 \hat{N}^2 - \omega \hat{j}_x\) in a quasiparticle vacuum, with \(\lambda_2\) determined self-consistently from the condition that the second-order particle-number fluctuation is suppressed. They test the method on the exactly solvable two-level Krumlinde-Szymanski R(5) model with monopole pairing, for a half-filled symmetric system (\(n=40\) particles, \(\Omega=20\)) at three pairing strengths (\(G=0.015\), \(0.065\), \(0.1\)), comparing LNC results to those from standard RBCS, variation-after-projection (RFBCS), exact particle-number projection of LNC states (LNC+PNP), and exact solutions.

## Key numbers

- For the half-filled symmetric system (\(n=40\), \(\Omega=20\), single-particle splitting \(2e=2\)), the mean-field BCS critical pairing strength is \(G_{\text{crit}} = 2e/(2\Omega-1) \simeq 0.051\).
- For weak pairing (\(G=0.015\)), the LNC+PNP method gives a maximum relative difference of about 20% compared to exact pairing energies.
- For strong pairing (\(G=0.1\)), the LNC method yields solutions up to angular momentum \(I=16\), while the RFBCS method only gives solutions down to \(I=10\) in the phase-transition region.
- Calculations were performed for rotational frequencies \(\omega\) ranging from 0 to 1.2 (in model units).

## Context

This work extends the Lipkin-Nogami method, originally developed for non-rotating nuclei by Lipkin (1960) and Nogami (1964), to the rotating case, addressing the Mottelson-Valatin effect (1960), the phase transition from superfluid to normal state in rotating nuclei. The authors compare their LNC results to the standard cranked BCS (RBCS) method and to the variation-after-projection (RFBCS) approach of Nazarewicz, Dudek, and Szymanski (1985). The method enables a simple quasiparticle description of pairing correlations in rotating nuclei without the complexity of full projection, and the hybrid LNC+PNP approach is suggested for studying nuclei near shell closures, such as spherical magic nuclei at low spin or superdeformed magic nuclei like \(^{152}\)Dy or \(^{192}\)Hg at high spin.
