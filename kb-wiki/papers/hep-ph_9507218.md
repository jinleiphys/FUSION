# The Nucleon's Virtual Meson Cloud and Deep Inelastic Lepton Scattering

> We address the question whether the nucleon's antiquark sea can be attributed entirely to its virtual meson cloud and, in essence, whether there exists a smooth transition between hadronic and quark-gluon degrees of freedom. We take into account contributions from $π$ and $K$ mesons and compare with the nucleon's antiquark distributions which serve as a non-perturbative input to the QCD evolution equations. We elucidate the different behavior in the flavor singlet and non-singlet channels and study the dependence of our results on the scale $Q^2$. The meson-nucleon cut-offs that we determine give not only an indication on the size of the region within which quarks are confined in a nucleon, but we find that the scale of these form factors is closely related to the four-momentum transfer, $Q^2$, where gluons are resolved by a high energy probe, and that large meson loop momenta, $|{\bf k}| \approx 0.8$ GeV, contribute significantly to the sea quark distributions. While the agreement of our calculations with data-based parametrizations is satisfactory and scale independent for the flavor breaking share of the nucleon's antiquark sea, the flavor singlet component is quite poorly described. This hints the importance of gluon degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The nucleon's antiquark sea cannot be attributed entirely to its virtual meson cloud; while the flavor-breaking components of the sea are well described by pion and kaon cloud contributions, the flavor-singlet component is poorly reproduced, indicating that gluon degrees of freedom are essential and that there is no smooth transition between hadronic and quark-gluon descriptions.

## Method

The authors computed the contribution of virtual π and K mesons to the nucleon's antiquark distributions using a convolution formalism (Sullivan process) with exponential form factors at the meson-nucleon vertices. They separately fitted the cut-off parameters Λ_πNN, Λ_πNΔ, and Λ_KNY to the flavor SU(3) breaking component x[ū+d̄-2s̄], the SU(2) breaking component x[d̄-ū], and the strange quark distribution xs̄ from recent parton distribution functions (MRS(A), CTEQ3M) at Q² values of 1, 2, and 4 GeV², using the NA10 and NA24 pion structure functions.

## Key numbers

- The exponential cut-off masses determined at Q²=1 GeV² are Λ_πNN ≈ 1000 MeV, Λ_πNΔ ≈ 800 MeV, and Λ_KNY ≈ 1180 MeV, averaged over MRS(A) and CTEQ3M PDFs and NA10/NA24 pion structure functions.
- Large meson loop momenta |k| ≈ 0.8 GeV contribute significantly to the sea quark distributions.
- The meson virtualities relevant in the deep inelastic process are t ≈ -0.4 GeV².
- The πNN coupling constant used is g_πNN/√3 = 13.05, and g_πNΔ = 28.6.
- The strange quark suppression factor from CCFR is κ = 0.48 ± 0.06, consistent with κ = 0.5 in the MRS(A') and CTEQ3M PDFs.

## Context

This work extends the Sullivan process analysis of Frankfurt et al. (1989) and Thomas (1983), who used older PDFs and found softer cut-offs (Λ_e ≈ 700 MeV), and contrasts with the Jülich group (Hwang et al. 1991, Szczurek et al. 1993) who used hard vertices (Λ_e ≈ 950 MeV) and an additional wave function renormalization factor. The results show that the meson cloud picture describes flavor-breaking components well but fails for the flavor-singlet component, suggesting that gluon splitting into qq̄ pairs is essential and that chiral QCD Lagrangians may not apply at the large loop momenta probed. This enables future work to incorporate explicit gluon contributions in models of nucleon structure at intermediate Q².
