# Ground State Correlations in 16O and 40Ca

> We study the ground state properties of doubly closed shell nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca in the framework of Correlated Basis Function theory using state dependent correlations, with central and tensor components. The realistic Argonne $v_{14}$ and $v'_{8}$ two-nucleon potentials and three-nucleon potentials of the Urbana class have been adopted. By means of the Fermi Hypernetted Chain integral equations, in conjunction with the Single Operator Chain approximation, we evaluate the ground state energy, one- and two-body densities and electromagnetic and spin static responses for both nuclei. In $^{16}$O we compare our results with the available Monte Carlo and Coupled Cluster ones and find a satisfying agreement. As in the nuclear matter case with similar interactions and wave functions, the nuclei result under-bound by 2--3 MeV/A.

## Key claim

The Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory with Fermi Hypernetted Chain/Single Operator Chain (FHNC/SOC) approximation, using state-dependent correlations and realistic two- and three-nucleon potentials, underbinds both 16O and 40Ca by 2–3 MeV per nucleon, consistent with the accuracy found in nuclear matter calculations.

## Method

The authors used the FHNC integral equations with the SOC approximation to evaluate the ground state energy, one- and two-body densities, and static responses of 16O and 40Ca. They employed a correlated wave function with a product of two-body correlation operators (f6-type, containing central and tensor components but no spin-orbit terms) acting on a Slater determinant of single-particle states from either Woods-Saxon or harmonic oscillator potentials. The realistic Argonne v14 and v'8 two-nucleon potentials were used, along with Urbana VII and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions; spin-orbit and three-body contributions were treated via a local density approximation or by extending the nuclear matter formalism to finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 16O with A14+UVII: -5.97 MeV/A (FHNC/SOC) vs. -6.85 MeV/A (estimated CMC value after subtracting three-body correlations); the FHNC/SOC result differs by ~1 MeV/A from CMC.
- Ground state energy of 16O with A14 two-body potential only: -6.04 MeV/A (FHNC/SOC), close to the coupled cluster result of -6.1 MeV/A from Heisenberg (1999).
- Kinetic energy of 16O with Jastrow correlation: 24.61 MeV (FHNC) vs. 24.33±0.21 MeV (VMC); potential energy: -22.07 MeV (FHNC) vs. -21.56±0.25 MeV (VMC).
- Kinetic energy of 16O with f6 correlation and v6 potential: 31.16 MeV (FHNC/SOC) vs. 29.45±0.33 MeV (CMC); potential energy: -35.47 MeV (FHNC/SOC) vs. -33.03±0.31 MeV (CMC); binding energy: -4.33 MeV/A (FHNC/SOC) vs. -4.59±0.10 MeV/A (CMC).
- Spin-orbit contribution for 16O with A14 and f6 correlation: 0.56 MeV/A (FHNC/SOC W0 term) vs. 0.62 MeV/A (CMC extrapolated).
- Nuclear matter saturation with A18+UIX and A8'+UIX interactions occurs at the empirical density (0.16 fm^-3); A14+UVII minimum is at a slightly higher density.
- One-body density sum rule violated by less than 1%; two-body density normalizations violated by ~9% in the worst case (40Ca with tensor correlations).

## Context

This work extends the FHNC/SOC formalism of Fabrocini (1998) from semi-realistic central interactions to complete realistic potentials including spin-orbit and three-nucleon forces, applied to 16O and 40Ca. It confirms the accuracy of the FHNC/SOC approximation estimated in nuclear matter by Wirzba (1988) and provides a benchmark for comparison with Cluster Monte Carlo calculations by Pieper (1992) and Coupled Cluster results by Heisenberg (1999). The results enable future applications of CBF theory to heavier doubly closed shell nuclei and to dynamical quantities such as electromagnetic responses.
