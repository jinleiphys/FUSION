# New insights into the nature of the $Λ(1380)$ and $Λ(1405)$ resonances away from the SU(3) limit

> Starting from the SU(3) limit, we consider the nature of the dynamically generated resonances $Λ(1380)$, $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ(1680)$ as the pion and kaon masses are tuned to their physical values. We show that the accidental symmetry of the two octets due to the leading order Weinberg-Tomozawa term is broken by the next-to-leading order terms. Most interestingly, we observe an interchange of the two trajectories of the $Λ(1380)$ and the $Λ(1405)$ away from the SU(3) limit at next-to-leading order. This remarkable phenomenon can be investigated using lattice QCD calculations that start from the SU(3) limit.

# New insights into the nature of the Λ(1380) and Λ(1405) resonances away from the SU(3) limit

## Key claim
The trajectories of the Λ(1380) and Λ(1405) poles interchange when going from leading-order (Weinberg-Tomozawa) to next-to-leading order chiral dynamics: the Λ(1380) originates from the singlet (octet) state in the SU(3) limit at LO (NLO), while the Λ(1405) originates from the octet (singlet) state at LO (NLO). This interchange can be investigated using lattice QCD calculations that start from the SU(3) limit.

## Method
The authors use a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with a chiral unitary amplitude for the meson-baryon system (πΣ, K̄N, ηΛ, KΞ) in the S-wave, isospin I=0 sector. The potential includes the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term and next-to-leading order contact terms, with the low-energy constants for the symmetry-breaking terms (b₀, b_D, b_F) determined from baryon masses and the dynamical terms (d₁, d₂, d₃, d₄) fitted to experimental K⁻p scattering cross sections and threshold ratios. The quark mass dependence of meson and baryon masses is implemented using chiral perturbation theory formulas up to NLO, allowing a controlled extrapolation from the SU(3) limit to physical pion and kaon masses.

## Key numbers
- Pole positions at NLO with physical masses: Λ(1380) at 1415.4 − i 165.7 MeV, Λ(1405) at 1417.9 − i 15.7 MeV, Λ(1680) at 1725.9 − i 13.7 MeV
- In the SU(3) limit with LO WT interaction: three poles on the physical Riemann sheet at E¹ = 1704 MeV, E⁸ = 1788 MeV, E⁸′ = 1788 MeV (two octet poles degenerate)
- In the SU(3) limit with NLO terms: three separated poles at E¹ = 1716 MeV (singlet), E⁸ = 1772 MeV (octet), E⁸ = 1787 MeV (octet, on unphysical sheet)
- Subtraction constants at physical point: a_{πΣ} = −0.70, a_{K̄N} = −1.15, a_{ηΛ} = −1.21, a_{KΞ} = −1.13; at SU(3) limit: a_{SU(3)} = −0.92
- Kaonic hydrogen shift and width from NLO fit: ΔE − iΓ/2 = 356 − i 464 eV (experimental: 283 ± 42 − i 542 ± 110 eV)

## Context
This work extends the pioneering two-pole structure study of the Λ(1405) by Jido et al. (2003) and the SU(3) decomposition analysis by Bruns et al. (2021). It contradicts the simple singlet/octet assignment from the earlier extrapolation by Jido (2003) by showing that the pole trajectories interchange when NLO terms are included, a phenomenon analogous to the a₁(1260) pole-flipping observed by Nagahiro et al. (2011) when varying the number of colors. The results enable lattice QCD studies such as those by Bulava et al. (2023) to directly test the chiral dynamics by starting from the SU(3) limit and tracking the pole evolution as quark masses are tuned to physical values.
