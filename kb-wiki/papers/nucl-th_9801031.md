# Exchange Currents in Photoproduction of Baryon Resonances

> We calculate photoexcitation amplitudes for several nucleon and delta resonances. We use a chiral quark model including two-body exchange currents. The two-body currents give important contributions. For the delta (1232) and the D13 (1520) we observe that the individual exchange current contributions considerably cancel each other while in the case of the Roper resonance and the S11 (1535) we get a reinforcement of the two-body amplitudes. In comparison with present experimental data, we obtain both for the S11 (1535) and for the Roper resonance an improvement with respect to the impulse approximation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including two-body exchange currents in a chiral quark model improves agreement with experimental photoexcitation amplitudes for the S11(1535) and Roper (P11(1440)) resonances, but this improvement does not hold as a general tendency; for the Delta(1232) and D13(1520) resonances, the individual exchange current contributions cancel each other and do not reduce the discrepancy with data.

## Method

The authors used a chiral quark potential model (χQPM) with harmonic oscillator wavefunctions to calculate transverse helicity amplitudes for photoexcitation of nucleon and Delta resonances up to center-of-mass energy √s = 1.6 GeV. They constructed the complete electromagnetic current density as a sum of one-body (impulse approximation) and two-body operators, including contributions from one-gluon exchange, one-pion exchange (pair and pion-in-flight), one-sigma exchange, and the confinement potential, with all parameters fixed from previous calculations of nucleon and Delta electromagnetic properties.

## Key numbers

- For the Delta(1232) resonance, the impulse approximation results deviate by 30-40% from experimental values, and this difference is slightly enlarged by including two-body currents and the finite electromagnetic size of constituent quarks.
- The confinement current contributes up to 60% relative to the one-body current for some resonances.
- The finite electromagnetic size of constituent quarks was set to rγq = 0.6 fm, compatible with vector meson dominance predictions.
- The constituent quark mass mq = 939 MeV (nucleon mass) and pion mass μ = 138 MeV were used, with gπN²/4π = 13.845.
- For the S11(1535) proton excitation, the two-body currents strongly damp the helicity amplitude, resolving the long-standing problem that nonrelativistic quark model calculations overestimate this amplitude.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group on two-body exchange currents in the Delta(1232) (Alf1) and Roper resonance (Uli) to all nucleon and Delta resonances up to 1.6 GeV. It contradicts the claims of Robson (1990s) and Perazzi et al. (Boffi) that pion-in-flight contributions are not significant; the authors find important pion-in-flight contributions for positive parity resonances, though they partially cancel with pion-pair contributions. The results enable future work using expanded Hilbert spaces for orbital wavefunctions and testing different radial forms of the confinement potential (linear, color-screened) to improve agreement for the Delta(1232) and D13(1520) resonances.
