# Rotation Symmetry and Nonabelian Berry Potential

> We study the role of rotational symmetry in the systems where nonabelian Berry potentials emerge as a result of integrating out fast degrees of freedom. The conserved angular momentum is constructed in the presence of a non-abelian Berry potential, which is formulated using Grassmann variables. The modifications on conventional angular momentum are discussed in close analogy with monopole systems. The diatomic molecular system discussed by Zygelman is found to have a similar structure to that of a non-abelian $SU(2)$ charge coupled to a 't Hooft - Polyakov monopole. The abelian limit of the Berry potential in diatomic systems is shown to be the same as $U(1)$ monopole and in a large separation limit, we observe the decoupling associated with a vanishing field tensor.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the conserved angular momentum in a system coupled to a nonabelian Berry potential takes the same form as that of a 't Hooft-Polyakov monopole, but with the crucial qualifier that the Berry potential contains a non-quantized parameter κ (the transition amplitude between Σ and π electronic states) which prevents a direct mapping; the conserved angular momentum is found to be L = L₀ + I, independent of κ.

## Method

The authors formulate nonabelian Berry potentials using Grassmann variables instead of the usual matrix representation, which simplifies canonical quantization and makes gauge invariance more transparent. They derive two constraint equations for constructing a conserved angular momentum: one from requiring the angular momentum satisfy the SU(2) algebra, and another from requiring it commute with the Hamiltonian. These constraints are then applied to the diatomic molecular system previously studied by Zygelman, where the Berry potential arises from integrating out electronic degrees of freedom (Σ and π states) coupled to the nuclear coordinate R.

## Key numbers

- The Berry potential for the diatomic system has components A_θ = κ(R)(T_y cosφ - T_x sinφ) and A_φ = T_z(cosθ - 1) - κ(R) sinθ (T_x cosφ + T_y sinφ), where κ(R) = (1/√2)|⟨Σ|L_x - iL_y|π⟩| measures the transition amplitude between Σ and π states.
- The nonvanishing field strength tensor is B = -(1 - κ²)/R² T_z R̂, showing the field vanishes when κ = ±1.
- In the κ = 0 limit, the Hamiltonian becomes H = -(1/(2μR²)) ∂/∂R (R² ∂/∂R) + (1/(2μR²))(L·L - 1), which is the generic form for a system coupled to a U(1) monopole field.
- In the κ = 1 limit, the Berry potential and its field tensor vanish (become pure gauge), corresponding to the restoration of rotational symmetry in the electronic system as R → ∞.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Berry potentials in diatomic molecules previously studied by Zygelman, showing that the structure is analogous to a nonabelian SU(2) charge coupled to a 't Hooft-Polyakov monopole. The authors connect their results to the general theory of spherically symmetric gauge potentials developed by Jackiw, and they note that the gauge structure is generic and independent of the specific interactions, potentially applicable to strongly interacting systems such as light- and heavy-quark baryons as suggested in earlier work by Lee, Nair, Rho, and Zahed. The paper enables future investigation of how rotational symmetry restoration and decoupling of slow and fast variables occur in more complex systems like heavy-quark baryons.
