# Some aspects on four quarks recombination

> We have performed a 3-D Monte Carlo simulation of a system composed of two identical light quarks ($qq$) and two identical antiquarks ($\bar Q\bar Q$) and determined whether it is energetically more favorable to form a tetraquark or two mesons, as a function of the interparticle separation distance which, for a fixed number of particles, can be identified as a particle density. In this proceedings, we highlight the main results and elaborate on the implications in properties like the correlation function for two-mesons and characterize the isolated diquark correlation function. We analize the four-body potential evolution and exhibit its linear behavior as a function of the invariant distance. We track the dynamical flipping among configurations to determine the recombination probability, exhibiting the importance of the tetraquark state.

# Some aspects on four quarks recombination

## Key claim
The authors perform a 3-D Monte Carlo simulation of a system of two identical light quarks and two identical antiquarks to determine whether a tetraquark or two-meson configuration is energetically favored as a function of interparticle separation (density). The abstract's claim of determining which configuration is "more favorable" is hedged by the fact that the study tracks dynamical flipping among configurations and recombination probabilities, rather than establishing a definitive stability boundary.

## Method
The authors use a String-Flip Model with a linear potential to describe the strong interaction among quarks, and implement a variational wave function that reproduces isolated mesons at low density while incorporating four-body correlations and Fermi statistics. A Metropolis Monte Carlo algorithm samples configurations, and for each configuration the potential is chosen as the minimum among two possible meson pairings and a tetraquark (Steiner-tree) configuration. The system is placed in a cubic box of side L, with density defined as ρ = 4/L³, and the authors study three cases: only meson configurations allowed, only tetraquark configurations allowed, and a mixed case where all configurations compete.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of the isolated meson is E₀ = 2.3381 in units where m₁ = m₂ = m = k = 1, corresponding to the first zero of the Airy function.
- The variational wave function reproduces the exact minimum energy value within a 1% error.
- For the tetraquark-only case at zero density, the slope of the linear potential fit is B(ρ ≈ 0) = 0.84 ± 0.02.
- For the mixed case at zero density, the slope of the linear potential fit is B(ρ ≈ 0) = 0.67 ± 0.02.
- Diquark correlation function parameters for the ud system: A₀ = 0.64, A₁ = 1.24, A₂ = 1.51.
- Diquark correlation function parameters for the ub system: A₀ = 1.13, A₁ = 1.1, A₂ = 1.47.
- The meson-meson correlation function is shown at density ρ = 0.01 for the ud system.

## Context
This work extends the seminal approach of Lenz et al. (1986) for four-quark systems using a harmonic potential, now applied with a linear potential and Monte Carlo simulation including quantum correlations. It builds on the String-Flip Model used by Oka (1985) and others for dense matter studies, and connects to experimental searches for tetraquark states at Belle, BES, LHCb, and D0. The results enable future work on observable signatures of tetraquark formation in meson-meson collisions and in WW decay at LEP2 energies, where the spatial separation is around 0.1 fm, as noted by the W-mass collaboration.
