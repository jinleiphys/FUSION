# Identification of a natural fieldlike entanglement resource in trapped-ion chains

> The electromagnetic trapping of ion chains can be regarded as a process of non-trivial entangled quantum state preparation within Hilbert spaces of the local axial motional modes. To begin uncovering properties of this entanglement resource produced as a byproduct of conventional ion-trap quantum information processing, the quantum continuous-variable formalism is herein utilized to focus on the leading-order entangled ground state of local motional modes in the presence of a quadratic trapping potential. The decay of entanglement between disjoint subsets of local modes is found to exhibit features of entanglement structure and responses to partial measurement reminiscent of the free massless scalar field vacuum. With significant fidelities between the two, even for large system sizes, a framework is established for initializing quantum field simulations via "imaging" extended entangled states from natural sources, rather than building correlations through deep circuits of few-body entangling operators. By calculating probabilities in discrete Fock subspaces of the local motional modes, considerations are presented for locally transferring these pre-distributed entanglement resources to the qudits of ion internal energy levels, improving this procedure's anticipated experimental viability.

# Identification of a natural fieldlike entanglement resource in trapped-ion chains

## Key claim

The ground state of local axial motional modes in a trapped-ion chain, produced as a byproduct of conventional trapping, exhibits entanglement structure and responses to partial measurement that are remarkably similar to those of the free massless scalar field vacuum, with significant fidelities even for large system sizes. However, this similarity holds only within the leading-order harmonic approximation of the trapping potential, and the dimensionless covariance matrix describing this entanglement is universal across all ion species and quadratic trap strengths, meaning any modifications to match the scalar field vacuum must be implemented through explicit external quantum operations.

## Method

The authors model N ions in a linear Paul trap with a quadratic trapping potential, expanding the Coulomb and trapping potential about equilibrium positions to leading harmonic order. They construct the covariance matrix of the local axial motional modes in the ground state using the Gaussian continuous-variable formalism, then compute logarithmic negativity between disjoint regions of contiguous ions under three conditions: tracing out external modes, projective measurement in the local position basis, and projective measurement in the local conjugate-momentum basis. Fidelity between the ion-chain motional ground state and the infinite-volume lattice scalar field vacuum is calculated using techniques for mixed Gaussian continuous-variable states, with optimization over a single-mode squeezing parameter applied to each local mode.

## Key numbers

- For a single 171Yb+ ion in a trap with ωz/2π = 1 MHz, the root-mean-square fluctuation scale is ℓnm = 7.7 nm, while the inter-ion separation scale is ℓμm = 2.7 μm.
- The ratio ℓnm/ℓμm for this representative system is 2.8 × 10−3, justifying the harmonic approximation.
- Fidelity between central local axial modes of a 150-ion chain and a same-size region of the infinite-volume massless scalar field vacuum (m = 10−10) remains at order 1 (approximately 0.8-0.9) for regions up to 50 modes after one-parameter single-mode squeezing optimization, whereas without squeezing the fidelity decays catastrophically with system size.
- For a two-ion chain, applying single-mode squeezings to transform the local motional mode covariance matrix into normal form reduces the population external to low-energy Fock states by several orders of magnitude, with the improvement increasing with qudit dimension.

## Context

This work extends the observation that the vacuum state of a quantum field is entangled at spacelike separations (Reeh 1961, Summers and Werner 1985, 1987) and that locally-interacting detectors can transfer that entanglement into basic quantum degrees of freedom (Valentini 1991, Reznik 2002, 2003). It connects to prior proposals for using trapped-ion transverse motional modes as computational bosonic degrees of freedom in lattice gauge theory simulations (Casanova 2011, Davoudi 2021) and to the first proposal for transferring entanglement from ion motional modes to internal energy levels (Retzker 2005). The paper enables a new framework for quantum field vacuum state preparation by "imaging" naturally distributed entanglement from trapped-ion chains rather than building correlations through deep circuits of few-body entangling operators, addressing known challenges in initial state preparation for quantum field simulations (Jordan 2011, 2012; Kempe, Kitaev, and Regev 2004; Oliveira and Terhal 2008).
