# Density-induced suppression of the alpha-particle condensate in nuclear matter and the structure of alpha cluster states in nuclei

> At low densities, with decreasing temperatures, in symmetric nuclear matter alpha-particles are formed, which eventually give raise to a quantum condensate with four-nucleon alpha-like correlations (quartetting). Starting with a model of alpha-matter, where undistorted alpha particles interact via an effective interaction such as the Ali-Bodmer potential, the suppression of the condensate fraction at zero temperature with increasing density is considered. Using a Jastrow-Feenberg approach, it is found that the condensate fraction vanishes near saturation density. Additionally, the modification of the internal state of the alpha particle due to medium effects will further reduce the condensate. In finite systems, an enhancement of the S state wave function of the c.o.m. orbital of alpha particle motion is considered as the correspondence to the condensate. Wave functions have been constructed for self-conjugate 4n nuclei which describe the condensate state, but are fully antisymmetrized on the nucleonic level. These condensate-like cluster wave functions have been successfully applied to describe properties of low-density states near the n alpha threshold. Comparison with OCM calculations in 12C and 16O shows strong enhancement of the occupation of the S-state c.o.m. orbital of the alpha-particles. This enhancement is decreasing if the baryon density increases, similar to the density-induced suppression of the condensate fraction in alpha matter. The ground states of 12C and 16O show no enhancement at all, thus a quartetting condensate cannot be formed at saturation densities.

## Key claim
The condensate fraction of alpha particles in symmetric nuclear matter vanishes near saturation density, but the paper shows that this suppression is driven not only by inter-alpha repulsion but also by medium modifications of the alpha particle's internal structure due to Pauli blocking, which further reduces the condensate. The authors hedge that their perturbative Jastrow-Feenberg calculation only holds in the low-density limit, and that a full treatment of Pauli blocking would push the disappearance of the condensate to around one-third of saturation density.

## Method
The authors model symmetric nuclear matter as a uniform Bose gas of alpha particles interacting via the Ali-Bodmer potential, using a Jastrow-Feenberg variational wave function to compute the condensate fraction at zero temperature via the Penrose-Onsager criterion. For finite nuclei, they construct fully antisymmetrized THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke) wave functions for self-conjugate 4n nuclei and diagonalize the alpha-particle density matrix to extract single-alpha orbital occupancies, comparing with OCM (orthogonality condition model) calculations for 12C and 16O.

## Key numbers
- The critical density for alpha particle dissolution in nuclear matter is about ρ₀/5, where ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is the saturation density.
- The quartetting condensate disappears at a density of about ρ₀/3.
- In the Hoyle state of 12C, the zero-node S-orbit is occupied to more than 70% by the three alpha particles.
- The rms radius of the THSR state for the Hoyle state is 4.3 fm.
- The experimental rms radius of the 12C ground state is 2.56 fm.
- For 16O, the OCM calculation gives the 0₆⁺ state at 16.5 MeV calculated energy, with an rms radius of 5.6 fm, and 61% of alpha particles in the zero-node S-orbit.
- The relative density for the 16O 0₆⁺ state is estimated as (R(0₁⁺)_exp / R(0₆⁺)_OCM)³ = 0.12.

## Context
This work extends the concept of alpha-particle condensation from infinite nuclear matter, previously studied by Röpke (2000) for the critical temperature, to zero temperature and to finite nuclei. It directly builds on the THSR wave function approach introduced by Tohsaki et al. (2001) for describing gas-like cluster states in self-conjugate 4n nuclei. The results contradict a simple shell-model picture for the Hoyle state and show that the density-dependent suppression of the condensate fraction in infinite matter has a direct analogue in the decreasing S-orbital occupancy observed in OCM calculations for 12C by Yamada (2005) and for 16O. This enables systematic searches for Hoyle-like states in heavier self-conjugate nuclei such as 20Ne and beyond.
