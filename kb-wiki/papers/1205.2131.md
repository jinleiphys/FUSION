# Recent progress in the study of fission barriers in covariant density functional theory

> Recent progress in the study of fission barriers of actinides and superheavy nuclei within covariant density functional theory is overviewed.

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory (CDFT), after the inclusion of triaxial deformation, describes inner fission barriers of actinides with an accuracy comparable to that of macroscopic+microscopic methods and Gogny density functional theory, but the similar trends of deviations from experiment across all these approaches suggest that further improvement may require going beyond the mean-field approximation.

## Method

The authors performed systematic triaxial calculations of inner fission barriers for even-even Th, U, Pu, Cm, and Cf nuclei using a triaxial RMF+BCS model with monopole pairing forces, which reduced computational time by at least an order of magnitude compared to full relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations. For superheavy nuclei with Z=112-120 and N-Z=48-62, they used three classes of CDFT models (nonlinear meson-nucleon coupling, density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling, and density-dependent point coupling) to compute fission barriers allowing for triaxial deformation. The results were compared to experimental barrier heights from the RIPL-2 database and to calculations from macroscopic+microscopic methods (Dobrowolski 2007, Moller 2009) and Gogny DFT (2006).

## Key numbers

- Average deviation per barrier δ for inner fission barriers in actinides is comparable across CDFT, macroscopic+microscopic methods, and Gogny DFT; the δ-values are shown in figures but no explicit numerical δ is given in the text for any method.
- In the actinide region, only approximately 33% of one-quasiparticle states are described with an accuracy better than 200 keV in the NL3* and NL1 parametrizations of the RMF Lagrangian.
- Approximately 22% of one-quasiparticle states in actinides are described with an accuracy between 200 and 400 keV in the NL3* and NL1 parametrizations.
- Some one-quasiparticle states show deviations from experiment exceeding 1 MeV, and can be close to 1.4 MeV.
- After rescaling by the Lorentz effective mass, more than 75% of one-quasiparticle states are described with an accuracy better than 400 keV.
- For superheavy nuclei, triaxiality lowers outer fission barriers by approximately 2-3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier CDFT calculations of Afanasjev et al. (2010) that first included triaxiality for fission barriers, and compares results with macroscopic+microscopic methods (Dobrowolski 2007, Moller 2009) and Gogny DFT (2006). The paper highlights that while CDFT now matches the accuracy of these other approaches for actinide fission barriers, the similar systematic deviations from experiment across all methods point to the need for beyond-mean-field treatments such as particle-vibrational coupling, which has been implemented only in spherical nuclei (Litvinova and Ring 2006, Litvinova and Afanasjev 2011). The complex potential energy surfaces found in superheavy nuclei, especially for Z=118 and 120 isotopes near N=184, call for dynamical fission path calculations rather than static barrier estimates.
