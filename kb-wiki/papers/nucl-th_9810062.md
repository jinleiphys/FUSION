# Restoration of Isospin Symmetry in Highly Excited Nuclei

> Explicit relations between the isospin mixing probability, the spreading width $Γ_{IAS}^{\downarrow}$ of the Isobaric Analog State (IAS) and the statistical decay width $Γ_c$ of the compound nucleus at finite excitation energy, are derived by using the Feshbach projection formalism. The temperature dependence of the isospin mixing probability is discussed quantitatively for the first time by using the values of $Γ_{IAS}^{\downarrow}$ and of $Γ_c$ calculated by means of microscopic models. It is shown that the mixing probability remains essentially constant up to a temperature of the order of 1 MeV and then decreases to about 1/4 of its zero temperature value, at higher temperature than $\approx$ 3 MeV, due to the short decay time of the compound system.

## Key claim

The isospin mixing probability in highly excited nuclei remains essentially constant up to a temperature of about 1 MeV and then decreases to roughly one quarter of its zero-temperature value at temperatures above approximately 3 MeV, due to the rapid increase of the compound decay width relative to the spreading width of the Isobaric Analog State.

## Method

The authors extended the Feshbach projection formalism, previously applied at zero temperature, to finite-temperature compound nuclei. They derived an explicit relation connecting the isospin mixing probability, the spreading width of the Isobaric Analog State (IAS), and the statistical decay width of the compound system. The temperature dependence of the isospin mixing was then calculated for \(^{208}\)Pb using microscopic estimates for the spreading widths of the IAS and the isovector giant monopole resonance (IVGMR), combined with a Weisskopf formula for the neutron decay width of the compound nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The isospin mixing probability in \(^{208}\)Pb at zero temperature is \(\alpha^2 = 0.1\), corresponding to a spreading width of the IAS \(\Gamma^\downarrow_{IAS}(T=0) = 100\) keV.
- The spreading width of the IVGMR in \(^{208}\)Bi at the IAS excitation energy is \(\Gamma^\downarrow_M(T=0) \approx 250\) keV, with an escape width of 200 keV.
- The slope parameter \(c\) for the linear temperature dependence of the spreading widths is taken as \(c = 0.025\) (and tested up to \(c = 0.05\)).
- The neutron separation energy for \(^{208}\)Pb is \(B_n = 7.4\) MeV and the nuclear radius is \(R = 7.1\) fm.
- The level density parameter used is \(a = 10\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- At a temperature of 3 MeV, the isospin mixing probability decreases to one fourth of its zero-temperature value.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature Feshbach projection model of Sagawa, Suzuki, and Colò (1996) to finite excitation energies, providing the first quantitative microscopic calculation of the temperature dependence of isospin mixing. The results confirm the conjecture of Wilkinson (1956) and Morinaga (1955) that isospin symmetry is restored in highly excited compound nuclei, and are consistent with experimental observations of decreasing mixing amplitude at high excitation energies in \(^{28}\)Si and \(^{60}\)Zn (Snover, 1993) and in \(^{40}\)Ca (Cinausero, 1995). The derived relation between mixing probability, IAS spreading width, and compound width also parallels the kinetic arguments of Harney, Richter, and Weidenmüller (1986) for self-conjugate nuclei.
