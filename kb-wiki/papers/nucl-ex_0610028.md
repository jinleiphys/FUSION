# Moment Analysis and Zipf Law

> The moment analysis method and nuclear Zipf's law of fragment size distributions are reviewed to study nuclear disassembly. In this report, we present a compilation of both theoretical and experimental studies on moment analysis and Zipf law performed so far. The relationship of both methods to a possible critical behavior or phase transition of nuclear disassembly is discussed. In addition, scaled factorial moments and intermittency are reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews moment analysis and Zipf's law as methods to study nuclear disassembly, arguing that these techniques can indicate critical behavior or phase transitions in finite nuclear systems, but the interpretation is complicated by finite-size effects that can distort the signatures (e.g., the \(\gamma_2 > 2\) criterion alone is insufficient for small systems).

## Method

The review compiles theoretical and experimental studies applying Campi's moment analysis (using moments \(M_k\) of fragment size distributions, normalized moments \(S_k\), and the \(\gamma_2\) parameter) and Zipf's law to multifragmentation data from heavy-ion collisions. It examines EOS collaboration data (1 A GeV Au, La, Kr on C targets) and NIMROD data (47 MeV/nucleon \(^{40}\)Ar on Al, Ti, Ni targets), extracting critical exponents (\(\tau\), \(\beta\), \(\gamma\), \(\sigma\)) from scaling relations and comparing them to percolation and liquid-gas universality classes.

## Key numbers

- For EOS Au data: \(\tau = 2.16 \pm 0.08\), \(\beta = 0.32 \pm 0.02\), \(\beta/\gamma = 0.22 \pm 0.03\) (close to liquid-gas universality class).
- For EOS La data: \(\tau = 2.10 \pm 0.06\), \(\beta = 0.34 \pm 0.02\), \(\beta/\gamma = 0.25 \pm 0.01\).
- For EOS Kr data: \(\tau = 1.88 \pm 0.08\), \(\beta = 0.53 \pm 0.05\), \(\beta/\gamma = 0.50 \pm 0.01\) (deviating from liquid-gas values).
- For NIMROD data (A\(\sim\)36 system): \(\tau = 2.13 \pm 0.10\), \(\beta = 0.33 \pm 0.01\), \(\gamma = 1.15 \pm 0.06\), \(\sigma = 0.68 \pm 0.04\), with critical excitation energy around 5.6 MeV/u and critical temperature \(T_c \sim 8.3\) MeV.
- In percolation model finite-size study, slopes of ln(\(A_{max}\)) vs ln(\(\overline{S_2}\)) for cubic lattices L=4 to 10 approach the thermodynamic limit value 1.23, ranging from 1.582\(\pm\)0.036 (L=4) to 1.242\(\pm\)0.015 (L=10).

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Campi (1986, 1988) and Bauer (1985, 1986) who first applied percolation methods to nuclear multifragmentation, and by the EOS collaboration (Gilkes et al.) who extracted critical exponents from Au data. It also builds on Fisher's droplet model (Fisher) for critical exponents (\(\tau \sim 2.3\)). The paper enables future studies to apply moment analysis and Zipf's law to new experimental data, while cautioning that finite-size effects (as shown in percolation studies by Jaqaman) require careful interpretation, especially for light systems where the \(\gamma_2 > 2\) criterion may fail.
