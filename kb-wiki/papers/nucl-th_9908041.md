# Multi-Phonon $γ$-Vibrational Bands and the Triaxial Projected Shell Model

> We present a fully quantum-mechanical, microscopic, unified treatment of ground-state band and multi-phonon $γ$-vibrational bands using shell model diagonalization with the triaxial projected shell model. The results agree very well with data on the g- and $γ$-band spectra in $^{156-170}$Er, as well as with recently measured $4^+$ 2-phonon $γ$-bandhead energies in $^{166}$Er and $^{168}$Er. Multi-phonon $γ$-excitation energies are predicted.

## Key claim

The Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) provides a fully quantum-mechanical, microscopic, unified description of ground-state bands and multi-phonon γ-vibrational bands in a single shell-model diagonalization, but the γ-bands arise from the introduction of triaxiality in the deformed basis (a single adjustable parameter ε′) rather than from multi-quasiparticle admixtures, which had previously failed to produce low-lying γ-states in the axial Projected Shell Model.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model diagonalization within a basis of angular-momentum-projected triaxial quasiparticle vacuum states (the simplest possible configuration space for even-even nuclei), using a Hamiltonian with monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, and a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction whose coupling constants are fixed by self-consistency and standard systematics, not adjusted to γ-band energies. The triaxial deformation parameter ε′ was the single adjustable parameter, set to 0.13 (corresponding to γ ≈ 25.5°) for all Er isotopes studied, and calculations were carried out for spins up to I=10.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶⁸Er, the triaxial parameter ε′ = 0.13 reproduces the observed γ-band while preserving the good g-band agreement obtained with an axially deformed basis.
- For ¹⁵⁶Er, the same ε′ = 0.13 reproduces both the g-band and γ-band in this transitional nucleus.
- The triaxial parameter ε′ = 0.13 corresponds to γ ≈ 25.5° in terms of the usual gamma parameter, using the rough estimate γ ≈ tan⁻¹(ε′/ε).
- For ¹⁶⁸Er, the second excited theoretical band agrees with the newly measured 4⁺ 2γ-bandhead reported in Ha98.
- For ¹⁶⁶Er, the observed 4⁺ 2γ-bandhead from Fa96 is reproduced.
- The predicted 6⁺ 3γ-bandhead energies lie between 3 and 3.6 MeV for the Er isotopes studied.
- The monopole pairing strength is given by G_M = [21.24 ∓ 13.86(N−Z)/A]/A, with the minus sign for neutrons and plus for protons.
- The quadrupole pairing strength G_Q is proportional to G_M with a proportionality constant in the range 0.16–0.18.

## Context

This work extends the Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) previously introduced by Sheikh and Hara (1999) for describing g-band moments of inertia in transitional nuclei, now applied to multi-phonon γ-vibrational bands. It addresses the failure of the axial Projected Shell Model (reviewed in the paper) to produce low-lying γ-bands, as documented by Sun and by Velázquez (1998), and provides a microscopic alternative to earlier collective models that required explicit anharmonicities (DH82, MM86, MN93) or separate coupling constants for different multipole components of the quadrupole-quadrupole force. The results support the experimental assignment of 4⁺ 2γ-bands in ¹⁶⁶Er and ¹⁶⁸Er and predict 3γ-bandhead energies for future experimental searches.
