# Fermion-Boson Duality of One-dimensional Quantum Particles with Generalized Contact Interaction

> For a system of spinless one-dimensional fermions, the non-vanishing short-range limit of two-body interaction is shown to induce the wave-function discontinuity. We prove the equivalence of this fermionic system and the bosonic particle system with two-body $δ$-function interaction with the reversed role of strong and weak couplings. KEYWORDS: one-dimensional system, $ε$-interaction, solvable many-body problem, exact bosonization

# Fermion-Boson Duality of One-dimensional Quantum Particles with Generalized Contact Interaction

## Key claim
For spinless one-dimensional fermions, the non-vanishing short-range limit of a two-body interaction induces a discontinuity in the wave function itself, and this fermionic system is exactly equivalent to a bosonic system with two-body delta-function interaction where the roles of strong and weak coupling are reversed. The equivalence holds for any number of particles, but requires a careful treatment of boundary conditions: under periodic boundary conditions for bosons, the fermions must satisfy antiperiodic boundary conditions.

## Method
The authors start with two identical fermions in one dimension with a short-range symmetric potential and take the zero-range limit using self-adjoint extension theory, which yields a generalized pointlike interaction (the epsilon-interaction) that produces a discontinuity in the antisymmetric relative wave function. They then apply a transformation that maps the fermionic relative wave function to a symmetric (bosonic) wave function, showing that the epsilon-interaction on fermions is equivalent to a standard delta-function interaction on bosons with coupling strength v = 1/c. The extension to N particles is performed by constructing fully antisymmetric and symmetric wave functions from a single ordered wave function and showing that the same mapping holds for all pairwise interactions.

## Key numbers
- The epsilon-interaction coupling constant c and the delta-interaction coupling constant v are related by v = 1/c (Eq. 24).
- For the two-body case, the epsilon-interaction is explicitly constructed as the limit: ε(x;c)φ(x) = lim_{a→0} (1/(2c) - 1/a) {δ(x+a) + δ(x-a)} φ(x) (Eq. 17).
- Under cyclic boundary conditions on a ring of length L, the fermionic and bosonic boundary phases are related by λ_- = λ_+ + (N-1)π (Eq. 38), so that periodic boundary for bosons (λ_+=0) implies antiperiodic boundary for fermions.

## Context
This work extends the classic bosonization idea of Coleman (1975) and the Tomonaga-Luttinger theory (Tomonaga 1950, Luttinger 1963, Mattis and Lieb 1965, Haldane 1981) to a solvable model of interacting fermions with zero-range interactions. It contrasts with the Tomonaga-Luttinger model, which is exact only for the ground state and does not apply to short-range interactions. The bosonic delta-interaction model solved by Lieb and Linger (1963) provides the exact solution for the equivalent bosonic system, giving a solvable fermion N-body problem. The authors note connections to the Calogero-Sutherland model (Sutherland 1971, Calogero 1971) and to recent work on fermion-boson equivalence in two dimensions by Lee (1997).
