# Breaking of Charge Independence of Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction and Bulk Properties of Nuclear Matter

> The effects of charge independence breaking of nucleon-nucleon interaction on the bulk properties of nuclear matter are investigated. Our results indicate that at high densities, the inclusion of charge dependence in the nucleon-nucleon potential affects the bulk properties of nuclear matter. However, at low densities, this effect is not considerable. It is seen that the change of our results for the nuclear matter calculations due to the breaking of the charge independence increases by increasing density. It is shown that the energy contribution of the $^1S_0$ channel is sensitive to considering the charge dependence in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. It is indicated that the effects of charge independence breaking on the calculated equation of state of nuclear matter are ignorable.

## Key claim
The paper claims that charge independence breaking (CIB) of the nucleon-nucleon interaction has a significant effect on the total energy and potential energy of nuclear matter at high densities (above about 0.2 fm⁻³), but that the effect on the equation of state and saturation properties is negligible. The hedge is that the effect is only considerable at high densities, while at low densities and for the equation of state, the impact is ignorable.

## Method
The author performed nuclear matter calculations using the Reid-93 potential, which explicitly includes charge-dependent proton-proton (pp), neutron-neutron (nn), and neutron-proton (np) interactions. Two cases were compared: a charge-dependent (CD) case using the full Reid-93 potential with distinct pp, nn, and np interactions, and a charge-independent (CI) case where the pp and nn interactions were replaced by the corresponding np interaction. The total energy, potential energy contributions from specific channels (³S₁-³D₁ and ¹S₀), saturation properties, and equation of state were computed as functions of density.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.17 ± 0.02 fm⁻³; energy per nucleon at saturation: E = -16 ± 1 MeV (from introduction, standard values).
- Proton-proton scattering length: a_pp = -17.3 ± 0.4 fm; effective range: r_pp = 2.85 ± 0.04 fm.
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18.8 ± 0.3 fm; effective range: r_nn = 2.75 ± 0.11 fm.
- Neutron-proton scattering length: a_np = -23.75 ± 0.01 fm; effective range: r_np = 2.75 ± 0.05 fm.
- Neutral pion mass: m_π⁰ = 134.977 MeV; charged pion mass: m_π± = 139.570 MeV.
- The difference in total energy between CD and CI cases (ΔE_CIB) increases with density, but the effect on saturation properties (ρ₀, E(ρ₀), incompressibility K₀) is small; specific numerical values for these saturation properties are not given in the text.
- The energy contribution of the ¹S₀ channel shows a significant difference between CD and CI cases, which increases with density; the change is repulsive.

## Context
This work extends earlier nuclear matter calculations using realistic potentials, specifically the Reid-93 potential, by explicitly including charge dependence. It compares its results with those of EHMMP (2000) and MPM (1998), finding good agreement. The paper shows that while CIB affects the total energy at high densities, it has a negligible impact on the equation of state, which is important for astrophysical applications such as neutron star structure and heavy-ion collisions. The finding that the ¹S₀ channel is the primary source of CIB effects aligns with known differences in scattering lengths and effective ranges for pp, nn, and np systems.
