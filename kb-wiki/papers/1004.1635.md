# Summation of Parquet diagrams as an ab initio method in nuclear structure calculations

> In this work we discuss the summation of the Parquet class of diagrams within Green's function theory as a possible framework for ab initio nuclear structure calculations. The theory is presented and some numerical details are discussed, in particular the approximations employed. We apply the Parquet method to a simple model, and compare our results with those from an exact solution. The main conclusion is that even at the level of approximation presented here, the results shows good agreement with other comparable ab initio approaches.

## Key claim

The Parquet summation of diagrams within Green's function theory is presented as a viable ab initio framework for nuclear structure calculations, with the hedge that even at the level of approximation presented here (using a simple model), the results show good agreement with other comparable ab initio approaches, though the method is not yet applied to realistic nuclear systems.

## Method

The authors implement the Parquet summation of diagrams within Green's function theory, which self-consistently sums particle-particle, hole-hole, and particle-hole ladder diagrams as well as ring diagrams. The method is applied to a simple model system (likely a schematic nuclear model with a finite number of single-particle states) and solved iteratively. The self-energy is computed both with energy-dependent and energy-independent approximations, and the spectral function is extracted. Results are compared with exact solutions of the model.

## Key numbers

- For a model with 10 single-particle states and 4 particles (n=10, p=4), the self-energy is plotted over an energy range from approximately -20 MeV to +20 MeV.
- The spectral function for the model shows quasiparticle peaks with spectroscopic factors: State 1 after 1 iteration gives 0.99950, after 15 iterations gives 0.99933; State 2 after 1 iteration gives 0.99851, after 15 iterations gives 0.99732 (for eta=0).
- For the model with n=10, p=4 and eta=0.5, the spectral function shows peaks labeled 1 through 10 over an energy range from -4 MeV to +10 MeV.
- The mixing parameter eta is varied between values of 0, 0.5, 1, and 7 to study the effect of different diagram summations on the spectral function.

## Context

This work extends the application of Parquet diagram summation techniques, previously used in condensed matter physics, to nuclear structure problems. It builds on earlier Green's function approaches to nuclear many-body theory and compares favorably with other ab initio methods such as the coupled-cluster method and self-consistent Green's function approaches. The results on a simple model demonstrate the feasibility of the method, enabling future application to realistic nuclear interactions and heavier nuclei.
