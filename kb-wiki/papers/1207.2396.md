# Hydrodynamic Modeling and the QGP Shear Viscosity

> In this article, we will briefly review the recent progress on hydrodynamic modeling and the extraction of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) specific shear viscosity with an emphasis on results obtained from the hybrid model VISHNU that couples viscous hydrodynamics for the macroscopic expansion of the QGP to the hadron cascade model for the microscopic evolution of the late hadronic stage.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the extraction of the QGP specific shear viscosity from elliptic flow data using the VISHNU hybrid model, concluding that the extracted value is bounded by \(1/(4\pi) < (\eta/s)_{QGP} < 2.5/(4\pi)\) at top RHIC energies, with the width dominated by uncertainties in initial eccentricities from MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models rather than by the hydrodynamic modeling itself.

## Method

The authors developed the hybrid model VISHNU, which couples the 2+1-dimensional Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 for the QGP expansion to the UrQMD hadron cascade model for the microscopic evolution of the late hadronic stage, with a switching temperature of 165 MeV. They performed single-shot hydrodynamic simulations using event-averaged smooth initial entropy density profiles from MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models, then compared the theoretical eccentricity-scaled integrated elliptic flow \(v_2^{ch}/\varepsilon_x\) as a function of multiplicity density per overlap area \(\mathrm{dN_{ch}/(dy S)}\) against corrected experimental data to extract \((\eta/s)_{QGP}\).

## Key numbers

- Extracted QGP specific shear viscosity at top RHIC energies: \(1/(4\pi) < (\eta/s)_{QGP} < 2.5/(4\pi)\), where \(1/(4\pi) \approx 0.08\) is the KSS bound.
- Switching temperature for VISHNU: \(T_{sw} = 165\ \mathrm{MeV}\), chosen from the chemical freeze-out temperature at RHIC.
- Decoupling temperature for pure hydrodynamics: \(T_{\mathrm{dec}} = 100-120\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- At LHC energies (2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb), VISHNU with MC-KLN initial conditions yields a fit to experimental data with \((\eta/s)_{QGP} \sim 0.20-0.24\), compared to \((\eta/s)_{QGP} \sim 0.16\) at RHIC.
- Bulk viscous effects on the extracted \((\eta/s)_{QGP}\) are estimated to be less than 20\%.
- Event-by-event simulations are estimated to reduce the extracted \((\eta/s)_{QGP}\) by O(\(<30\%\)).
- Initial flow effects are estimated to increase the extracted \((\eta/s)_{QGP}\) by O(\(<30\%\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier viscous hydrodynamic studies by Luzum and Romatschke (2008) that used optical Glauber and KLN initial conditions and found a conservative upper limit \((\eta/s)_{QGP} < 5/(4\pi)\), but neglected hadronic rescattering and chemical freeze-out effects. The VISHNU hybrid model improves upon pure hydrodynamic approaches by replacing the hadronic phase with a microscopic cascade, eliminating adjustable parameters for hadronic transport and freeze-out. The results enable future combined analyses of elliptic and triangular flow from event-by-event simulations to reduce initial condition ambiguities and extract a temperature-dependent \((\eta/s)_{QGP}(T)\) from RHIC to LHC energies.
