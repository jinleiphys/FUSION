# Lattice computation of the Kugo-Ojima correlation function

> As of today, color confinement in Quantum Chromodynamics remains a mystery from the theoretical point of view. So far, no analytical proof of color confinement has been found and the mechanism that confines colored states from the space of physical states is still unknown. Taichiro Kugo and Izumi Ojima proposed such confinement mechanism, using as basis the BRST-symmetry and derived the requirements for the realization of this mechanism. One such requirement, which happens to be the non-trivial one, is that a special correlation function, the Kugo-Ojima correlation function $u(p^2)$, approaches $-1$ at the origin ($p^2=0)$. This correlation function can be obtained on the lattice within the lattice formulation of gauge theories. The present work consists on lattice results for this correlation function on the Landau gauge. We present results obtained from 4 symmetric large volume lattices $(32^{4}, 48^{4}, 64^{4}, 80^{4})$ with $β= 6.0$ on the Landau gauge. A test on the transversality of the Kugo-Ojima correlation function is also performed, along with some statistical considerations of the results. The results present further evidence that the Kugo-Ojima confinement scenario is not realized on the lattice and that the Kugo-Ojima correlation function, in the Landau gauge, is transverse. Our findings align qualitatively with existing literature.

## Key claim

The lattice computation provides further evidence that the Kugo-Ojima confinement scenario is not realized on the lattice, specifically that the Kugo-Ojima correlation function $u(p^2)$ does not approach $-1$ at $p^2=0$ in the Landau gauge, contrary to the requirement for the confinement mechanism proposed by Kugo and Ojima.

## Method

The Kugo-Ojima correlation function $u(p^2)$ was computed on four symmetric large-volume lattices with sizes $32^4$, $48^4$, $64^4$, and $80^4$ at fixed $\beta = 6.0$ in the Landau gauge. The computation used the lattice formulation of gauge theories with SU(3) gauge group, and the Landau gauge condition was imposed by maximizing a functional that corresponds to fixing the gauge. A test of the transversality of the Kugo-Ojima correlation function was performed, along with statistical analysis of the results.

## Key numbers

- Four lattice volumes were used: $32^4$, $48^4$, $64^4$, and $80^4$, all with $\beta = 6.0$ (corresponding to bare coupling $g_0 = 1$ for $N_c = 3$).
- The Kugo-Ojima correlation function $u(p^2)$ was computed at all possible momentum values on these lattices.
- The longitudinal part of the correlation function was found to be compatible with zero, as expected for a transverse function.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of the Kugo-Ojima correlation function, aligning qualitatively with the lattice data of Sternbeck (2006) and supporting the estimate provided by Kondo (2009). The results agree with studies using different formalisms, including the renormalization-group dependence of $u(0,\mu^2)$ from Aguilar (2009) and Binosi (2009), the non-enhanced ghost dressing function from Boucaud (2008) and Cucchieri (2008), and BRST-symmetry breaking evidence from Cucchieri (2014) and Weishi (2021). The non-realization of the Kugo-Ojima scenario suggests that the assumption of an unbroken BRST charge may not be valid at the non-perturbative level, which would prevent using it to define the physical subspace.
