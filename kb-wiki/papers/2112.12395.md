# Radiative (anti)neutrino energy spectra from muon, pion, and kaon decays

> To describe low-energy (anti)neutrino fluxes in modern coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments as well as high-energy fluxes in precision-frontier projects such as the Enhanced NeUtrino BEams from kaon Tagging (ENUBET) and the Neutrinos from STORed Muons (nuSTORM), we evaluate (anti)neutrino energy spectra from radiative muon ($μ^- \to e^- \barν_e ν_μ(γ),~μ^+ \to e^+ ν_e \barν_μ(γ)$), pion $π_{\ell 2}$ ($π^- \to μ^- \barν_μ(γ),~π^+ \to μ^+ ν_μ(γ)$), and kaon $K_{\ell 2}$ ($K^- \to μ^- \barν_μ(γ),~K^+ \to μ^+ ν_μ(γ)$) decays. We compare detailed $\mathrm{O} \left( α\right)$ distributions to the well-known tree-level results, investigate electron-mass corrections and provide energy spectra in analytical form. Radiative corrections introduce continuous and divergent spectral components near the endpoint, on top of the monochromatic tree-level meson-decay spectra, which can change the flux-averaged cross section at $6\times 10^{-5}$ level for the scattering on $^{40}\mathrm{Ar}$ nucleus with (anti)neutrinos from the pion decay at rest. Radiative effects modify the expected (anti)neutrino fluxes from the muon decay around the peak region by $3-4$ permille, which is a precision goal for next-generation artificial neutrino sources.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections to (anti)neutrino energy spectra from muon, pion, and kaon decays introduce continuous and divergent spectral components near the endpoint on top of the monochromatic tree-level meson-decay spectra, but the effect on flux-averaged cross sections is at the $6\times 10^{-5}$ level for scattering on $^{40}\mathrm{Ar}$ from pion decay at rest, and radiative effects modify muon-decay fluxes around the peak region by $3-4$ permille.

## Method

The author evaluates leading-order electromagnetic corrections (O($\alpha$)) to (anti)neutrino energy spectra from muon decay ($\mu^- \to e^- \bar{\nu}_e \nu_\mu(\gamma)$, $\mu^+ \to e^+ \nu_e \bar{\nu}_\mu(\gamma)$), pion $\pi_{\ell 2}$ decay ($\pi^- \to \mu^- \bar{\nu}_\mu(\gamma)$, $\pi^+ \to \mu^+ \nu_\mu(\gamma)$), and kaon $K_{\ell 2}$ decay ($K^- \to \mu^- \bar{\nu}_\mu(\gamma)$, $K^+ \to \mu^+ \nu_\mu(\gamma)$). The calculation includes virtual corrections from one-loop self-energies and photon exchange, soft-photon Bremsstrahlung, and hard-photon contributions from inner Bremsstrahlung and structure-dependent amplitudes, with electron-mass corrections investigated and analytical forms provided for the energy spectra.

## Key numbers

* Radiative corrections change the flux-averaged cross section on $^{40}\mathrm{Ar}$ nucleus at the $6\times 10^{-5}$ level for (anti)neutrinos from pion decay at rest, comparing $\sigma^{^{40}\mathrm{Ar}}_{\bar{\nu}_\mu} = (15.1867\pm0.25) \times 10^{-40}~\mathrm{cm}^2$ to the tree-level result $\sigma^{^{40}\mathrm{Ar}}_{\bar{\nu}_\mu,\mathrm{LO}} = (15.1875\pm0.25) \times 10^{-40}~\mathrm{cm}^2$.
* Radiative effects modify the expected (anti)neutrino fluxes from muon decay around the peak region by $3-4$ permille.
* The branching ratio for the muon decay channel containing an $e^+e^-$ pair is $(3.4 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-5}$.
* The dominant leptonic kaon decay branching ratio is $(63.56\pm0.11)~\%$ for $K^\pm \to \mu^\pm \nu_\mu$.
* The $\pi^+ \to e^+ \nu_e \pi^0$ channel branching ratio is $(1.036\pm0.006)\times10^{-8}$.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of radiative corrections to muon decay by Greub (1993) and Broncano (2002) in the approximation of vanishing electron mass, and to total decay widths by Kinoshita (1958), Nir (1989), Bijnens (1992), van Ritbergen (1999), and Pak (2008). The results enable percent-level control of muon-over-electron flavor ratios for incoming (anti)neutrino fluxes, which is needed for precision-frontier projects such as ENUBET (Enhanced NeUtrino BEams from kaon Tagging) and nuSTORM (Neutrinos from STORed Muons), as well as for modern coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments and neutrino oscillation experiments like T2K, NOvA, and DUNE.
