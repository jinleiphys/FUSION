# Deconfinement critical point of lattice QCD with $N_{\rm f}=2$ Wilson fermions

> The ${\rm SU}(3)$ pure gauge theory exhibits a first-order thermal deconfinement transition due to spontaneous breaking of its global $Z_3$ center symmetry. When heavy dynamical quarks are added, this symmetry is broken explicitly and the transition weakens with decreasing quark mass until it disappears at a critical point. We compute the critical hopping parameter and the associated pion mass for lattice QCD with $N_f=2$ degenerate standard Wilson fermions on $N_τ\in\{6,8,10\}$ lattices, corresponding to lattice spacings $a=0.12\, {\rm fm}$, $a=0.09\, {\rm fm}$, $a=0.07\, {\rm fm}$, respectively. Significant cut-off effects are observed, with the first-order region growing as the lattice gets finer. While current lattices are still too coarse for a continuum extrapolation, we estimate $m_π^c\approx 4 {\rm GeV}$ with a remaining systematic error of $\sim 20\%$. Our results allow to assess the accuracy of the LO and NLO hopping expanded fermion determinant used in the literature for various purposes. We also provide a detailed investigation of the statistics required for this type of calculation, which is useful for similar investigations of the chiral transition.

## Key claim
The first-order deconfinement transition in lattice QCD with two degenerate Wilson fermions weakens with decreasing quark mass and disappears at a critical point, but the continuum limit for this critical point is not yet within reach due to significant cut-off effects; the authors estimate the critical pion mass to be approximately 4 GeV with a remaining systematic error of about 20%.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2 degenerate standard Wilson fermions on N_tau = 6, 8, and 10 lattices, corresponding to lattice spacings of 0.12 fm, 0.09 fm, and 0.07 fm, respectively. They used finite-size scaling of the kurtosis of the absolute value of the Polyakov loop, including a leading finite-volume correction term, to locate the critical hopping parameter (kappa_c) where the first-order transition ends. The physical scale was set using the Wilson flow parameter, and the pseudoscalar meson mass was computed to parameterize the critical point.

## Key numbers
- Critical hopping parameter kappa_c for N_tau=6: 0.0877(9).
- Critical pion mass m_pi^c for N_tau=6: approximately 18.1 times the critical temperature T_c.
- Estimated continuum critical pion mass m_pi^c: approximately 4 GeV, with a systematic error of about 20%.
- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.12 fm (N_tau=6), a = 0.09 fm (N_tau=8), a = 0.07 fm (N_tau=10).
- Total trajectories produced: 28 million for N_tau=6, 39.5 million for N_tau=8, and 21.8 million for N_tau=10.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the deconfinement critical point for N_f=2 Wilson fermions on coarser lattices (N_tau=4,6,8) by Saito (2011) and Ejiri (2019), which used a histogram method reweighted with a hopping-expanded fermion determinant. The authors find a large discrepancy (about 50% in the critical pion mass) compared to the NLO hopping-expanded results of Ejiri (2019) on N_tau=6, highlighting the necessity of a full calculation for a reliable continuum limit. This work enables future quantitative comparisons with effective lattice theories and effective Polyakov loop theories, and it documents the growing statistics requirements for approaching the continuum limit in the heavy quark mass regime.
