# Nucleon Strangeness and Unitarity

> The strange-quark vector current form factors of the nucleon are analyzed within the framework of dispersion relations. Particular attention is paid to contributions made by $K\bar{K}$ intermediate states to the form factor spectral functions. It is shown that, when the $K\bar{K}\to N\bar{N}$ amplitude is evaluated in the Born approximation, the $K\bar{K}$ contributions are identical to those arising from a one-loop calculation and entail a serious violation of unitarity. The mean square strangeness radius and magnetic moment are evaluated by imposing unitarity bounds on the kaon-nucleon partial wave amplitudes. The impact of including the kaon's form factor in the dispersion integrals is also evaluated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that one-loop (Born approximation) estimates of the $K\bar{K}$ intermediate state contribution to the nucleon's strange-quark vector current form factors seriously violate unitarity, and that imposing unitarity bounds on the kaon-nucleon partial wave amplitudes significantly modifies predictions for the mean square strangeness radius and magnetic moment, though the analysis remains provisional due to model dependence in the kaon strangeness form factor.

## Method

The authors employ dispersion relations for the nucleon's strange-quark Dirac and Pauli form factors, focusing on the two-kaon intermediate state contribution to the spectral functions. They decompose the $K\bar{K}\to N\bar{N}$ amplitude into helicity partial waves, impose unitarity bounds ($|b_J^{\lambda_1,\lambda_2}|\leq 1$), and evaluate the spectral functions using these bounded partial waves together with a model parametrization of the kaon's strangeness form factor. The mean square strangeness radius and magnetic moment are then computed from the dispersion integrals.

## Key numbers

- The unitarity bound on the $K\bar{K}\to N\bar{N}$ partial waves is $|b_J^{\lambda_1,\lambda_2}|\leq 1$ for $t\geq 4m_N^2$.
- The kaon strangeness form factor satisfies $\fks(0)=-1$.
- The three-pion threshold is at $t_0=9m_\pi^2$, below the $K\bar{K}$ threshold at $t=4m_K^2$.
- The $\phi(1020)$ resonance has a width $\Gamma$ and mass $m_\phi$, used in the vector dominance parametrization $|\fks(t)_{VDM}|=\left\{ {(\xi^2)^2+m_\phi^2\Gamma^2\over [(\xi^2-t)^2 +m_\phi^2\Gamma^2]}\right\}^{1/2}$ with $\xi^2\equiv m_\phi^2-\Gamma^2/4$.
- The $\omega$ meson has a strange-quark admixture at the level of $\epsilon\approx 0.05$.

## Context

This work extends earlier dispersion relation analyses of nucleon form factors by Federbush, Goldberger, and Treiman (1958) and Drell and Zachariasen (1960), and addresses the same unitarity issues previously treated for the $\pi\pi$ contribution to the nucleon's isovector electromagnetic form factors. It contrasts with vector meson dominance predictions for strangeness form factors (Jaffe 1989, Hammer 1996) and with one-loop kaon-cloud calculations (Musolf 1996, Koepf 1992, Forkel 1994, Cohen 1993, Ito 1995), showing that those one-loop estimates violate unitarity. The analysis is provisional and enables future work incorporating kaon-nucleon scattering data and other multi-meson continuum contributions.
