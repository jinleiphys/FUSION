# Roy-Steiner equations for gamma gamma -> pi pi

> Starting from hyperbolic dispersion relations, we derive a system of Roy--Steiner equations for pion Compton scattering that respects analyticity, unitarity, gauge invariance, and crossing symmetry. It thus maintains all symmetries of the underlying quantum field theory. To suppress the dependence of observables on high-energy input, we also consider once- and twice-subtracted versions of the equations, and identify the subtraction constants with dipole and quadrupole pion polarizabilities. Based on the assumption of Mandelstam analyticity, we determine the kinematic range in which the equations are valid. As an application, we consider the resolution of the $γγ\toππ$ partial waves by a Muskhelishvili--Omnès representation with finite matching point. We find a sum rule for the isospin-two $S$-wave, which, together with chiral constraints, produces an improved prediction for the charged-pion quadrupole polarizability $(α_2-β_2)^{π^\pm}=(15.3\pm 3.7)\cdot 10^{-4} {\rm fm}^5$. We investigate the prediction of our dispersion relations for the two-photon coupling of the $σ$-resonance $Γ_{σγγ}$. The twice-subtracted version predicts a correlation between this width and the isospin-zero pion polarizabilities, which is largely independent of the high-energy input used in the equations. Using this correlation, the chiral perturbation theory results for pion polarizabilities, and our new sum rule, we find $Γ_{σγγ}=(1.7\pm 0.4)\,{\rm keV}$.

## Key claim

The paper derives a system of Roy–Steiner equations for pion Compton scattering that fully respects analyticity, unitarity, gauge invariance, and crossing symmetry, but the equations are rigorously valid only up to 1 GeV (extendable further under additional assumptions), and the twice-subtracted version predicts a correlation between the σ two-photon width and isospin-zero pion polarizabilities that is largely independent of high-energy input.

## Method

The authors start from hyperbolic dispersion relations for the invariant amplitudes A and B of γπ → γπ, then perform partial-wave projections onto both the s-channel (γπ → γπ) and t-channel (γγ → ππ) partial waves, obtaining a coupled system of integral equations. They implement once- and twice-subtracted versions by deriving sum rules whose subtraction constants are identified with dipole and quadrupole pion polarizabilities, and determine the domain of validity using Mandelstam analyticity and the boundaries of double spectral functions from box-diagram kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Charged-pion quadrupole polarizability: (α₂ − β₂)^{π^±} = (15.3 ± 3.7) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵, obtained from a sum rule for the isospin-two S-wave combined with chiral constraints.
- Two-photon width of the σ resonance: Γ_{σγγ} = (1.7 ± 0.4) keV, found using the twice-subtracted dispersion relation correlation, chiral perturbation theory results for pion polarizabilities, and the new sum rule.
- The Roy–Steiner equations for γγ → ππ are valid up to 1 GeV under the assumption of Mandelstam analyticity.

## Context

This work extends the Roy-equation approach previously applied to ππ scattering (Roy, 1971; Colangelo, Gasser, and Leutwyler, 2001) to the coupled reactions γπ → γπ and γγ → ππ, using hyperbolic dispersion relations as done for πK scattering (Anisovich and Leutwyler, 1996). It improves upon earlier dispersive treatments of γγ → ππ (García-Martín and Moussallam, 2010) by including a numerically important S-wave/D-wave coupling that had been neglected, and enables model-independent extraction of the σ two-photon width as a function of pion polarizabilities, which can be tested by ongoing COMPASS measurements.
