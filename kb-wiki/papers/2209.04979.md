# Exploration of trace anomaly contribution to proton mass based on light vector meson photoproduction

> In the quantum chromodynamics, the mass source of the proton is decomposed into four parts by the energy momentum tensor : quark energy term, gluon energy term, quark mass term and trace anomaly term. And the trace anomaly term is the most crucial contribution for studying the internal structure of the proton. In this work, under the definition of the vector meson dominant model, the trace anomaly contribution of the proton is extracted from the experimental datas of light vector mesons $ρ$, $ω$ and $ϕ$ photoproduction at the near-threshold, which are ($1.15\pm0.08$)$\%$, ($2.70\pm0.04$)$\%$ and ($5.58\pm0.25$)$\%$, respectively. Eventually, the average trace anomaly contribution of the proton is ($4.36\pm0.40$) $\%$, which only account for a small fraction of the total proton mass. The result of this work will provide theoretical information support for further study of proton internal structure.

## Key claim
The trace anomaly contributes only a small fraction (approximately 4.36%) of the total proton mass, extracted from near-threshold photoproduction data of light vector mesons, with the result depending on the specific meson and the choice of differential versus total cross section.

## Method
The authors used the vector meson dominance (VMD) model to relate the differential cross sections of near-threshold photoproduction of rho, omega, and phi mesons on protons to the trace anomaly parameter b, which appears in the energy-momentum tensor decomposition of the proton mass. For the phi meson, they also employed a two-gluon exchange model with a parameterized gluon distribution function to predict the cross section and extract the trace anomaly contribution. The trace anomaly contribution M_a/M_N was extracted from experimental data from the CLAS, LEPS, and SAPHIR Collaborations.

## Key numbers
- Trace anomaly contribution from rho meson photoproduction: (1.15 ± 0.08)% (average over W = 1.79 to 1.94 GeV)
- Trace anomaly contribution from omega meson photoproduction: (2.70 ± 0.04)% (average over W = 1.73 to 2.38 GeV)
- Trace anomaly contribution from phi meson photoproduction (differential cross section, CLAS and LEPS data): (5.58 ± 0.25)% (average over W = 1.98 to 2.29 GeV)
- Root mean square trace anomaly contribution combining all three mesons: (4.36 ± 0.40)%
- Two-gluon exchange model prediction for phi meson (differential cross section): (6.03 ± 0.76)% over R in [0, 0.66]
- Two-gluon exchange model prediction for phi meson (total cross section): (2.51 ± 0.45)% over R in [0, 0.66]
- Proton mass M_N = 0.938 GeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of Wang (2019) and Kou (2021), who related vector meson photoproduction cross sections to the trace anomaly parameter using VMD, but focuses on light vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) rather than heavy mesons like J/psi. The results contrast with the decomposition of Metz (2020), who found M_a = 0 using independent operators, and with the larger and more energy-dependent values extracted from J/psi photoproduction by Duran (2022). The findings enable further investigation of proton mass decomposition and internal structure, particularly at future facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and JLab.
