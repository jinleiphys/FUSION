# The ridge in proton-proton collisions at the LHC

> We show that the key features of the CMS result on the ridge correlation seen for high multiplicity events in sqrt(s)=7TeV proton-proton collisions at the LHC can be understood in the Color Glass Condensate framework of high energy QCD. The same formalism underlies the explanation of the ridge events seen in A+A collisions at RHIC, albeit it is likely that flow effects may enhance the magnitude of the signal in the latter.

## Key claim
The ridge-like two-particle correlation observed by CMS in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at √s = 7 TeV can be explained by intrinsic angular collimation from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, without requiring hydrodynamic flow; however, the authors note that flow effects may enhance the signal in heavy-ion collisions and cannot be completely ruled out for the highest multiplicity pp events.

## Method
The authors compute the two-particle correlation function C₂ using the CGC effective field theory, employing unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) that evolve via the Balitsky–Kovchegov (BK) equation with running coupling. The double-inclusive gluon production cross section is derived from high-energy factorization theorems, relating multi-particle final states to multi-parton correlations in saturated hadron wavefunctions. The correlation is evaluated for different values of the initial saturation scale Q₀², which corresponds to different impact parameters and multiplicities.

## Key numbers
- The ridge extends at least up to Δη ≈ 4.8, the limit of detector acceptance in the CMS analysis.
- The correlation is seen for charged particle pairs with momenta in the range p⊥, q⊥ ∼ 1–3 GeV, and systematically vanishes for p⊥, q⊥ ≲ 1 GeV and p⊥, q⊥ ≳ 3 GeV.
- The CMS high-multiplicity selection requires N ≥ 110 charged particle tracks.
- The correlation C₂ is computed for p⊥ ≈ q⊥ and shown as a function of Q₀², the saturation scale at the initial condition x = x₀ = 0.01.
- For p⊥ > 6 GeV, the correlation decreases below the value at p⊥ = 0.5 GeV and is smaller for all higher p⊥ values.
- The rapidity dependence is shown for fixed p⊥ = q⊥ = 2 GeV, with yp = 0 and yp = −3, for two different Q₀ values.

## Context
This work extends the CGC-based explanation of the ridge previously applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC by STAR (Adams et al., 2005; Abelev et al., 2009) and PHOBOS (Alver et al., 2007; Alver et al., 2010) to proton-proton collisions at the LHC. The same formalism that described the ridge in Cu+Cu collisions at √s = 62.4 GeV and Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV now predicts a ridge in pp collisions, which the CMS measurement confirmed. The results enable quantitative tests of gluon saturation dynamics and multi-parton correlations in small systems, and the authors note that more detailed comparisons require understanding impact parameter dependence, experimental efficiency, acceptance effects, and the possible contribution of radial flow.
