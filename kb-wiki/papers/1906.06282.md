# Calculation of resonance energies from Q-values

> Resonance energies are frequently derived from precisely measured excitation energies and reaction Q-values. The latter quantities are usually calculated from atomic instead of nuclear mass differences. This procedure disregards the energy shift caused by the difference in the total electron binding energies before and after the interaction. Assuming that the interacting nuclei in a stellar plasma are fully ionized, this energy shift can have a significant effect, considering that the resonance energy enters exponentially into the expression for the narrow-resonance thermonuclear reaction rates. As an example, the rate of the $^{36}$Ar(p,$γ$)$^{37}$K reaction is discussed, which, at temperatures below 1 GK, depends only on the contributions of a single resonance and direct capture. In this case, disregarding the energy shift caused by the total electron binding energy difference erroneously enhances the rate by $\approx$40\% near temperatures of 70 MK.

## Key claim

Resonance energies derived from tabulated Q-values based on atomic mass differences are systematically biased because they ignore the difference in total electron binding energies before and after the nuclear interaction; for fully ionized stellar plasmas, this shift can significantly alter thermonuclear reaction rates, with the effect becoming noticeable for nuclides heavier than oxygen.

## Method

The author derives the relationship between Q-values based on atomic masses and those based on nuclear masses, showing that the correction term is the difference in total electron binding energies of the neutral atoms involved. Using the approximate expression for total electron binding energy from Lunney, Pearson and Thibault (2003), which is based on relativistic Hartree-Fock-Slater calculations by Huang et al. (1976), the author computes the binding energy difference for the 36Ar(p,γ)37K reaction and applies it to recalculate the center-of-mass resonance energy and the resulting thermonuclear reaction rate.

## Key numbers

- Total electron binding energy for neon (Z=10): 3.5 keV; for silicon (Z=14): 7.9 keV; for argon (Z=18): 14.5 keV
- For the 36Ar(p,γ)37K reaction, the tabulated atomic-mass-based Q-value is Q_at = 1857.63 ± 0.09 keV (Wang et al., 2017)
- The excitation energy of the 37K compound level is E_x = 2170.18 ± 0.13 keV (De Esch, 1988)
- The center-of-mass resonance energy using atomic masses is E_r,at^cm = 312.55 ± 0.16 keV; using nuclear masses it is E_r,nu^cm = 314.53 ± 0.16 keV, a shift of 1.98 keV
- Disregarding the electron binding energy difference erroneously enhances the 36Ar(p,γ)37K reaction rate by approximately 40% near temperatures of 70 MK

## Context

This work extends the standard practice of using atomic mass differences for Q-value calculations, as found in mass evaluations such as Wang et al. (2017), by quantifying the systematic bias introduced. The correction becomes important for reactions involving nuclides heavier than oxygen and is more pronounced for alpha-capture reactions compared to proton-induced reactions. The paper enables more accurate thermonuclear reaction rate calculations for stellar nucleosynthesis models, particularly for temperatures below 1 GK where narrow-resonance rates depend exponentially on resonance energy.
