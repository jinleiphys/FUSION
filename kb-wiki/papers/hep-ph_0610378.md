# Dispersive contributions to $e^+p/e^-p$ cross section ratio in forward regime

> Two-photon exchange (TPE) contributions to elastic electron-proton scattering in the forward regime are considered. The imaginary part of TPE amplitude in these kinematics is related to the DIS nucleon structure functions. The real part of the TPE amplitude is obtained from the imaginary part by means of dispersion relations. We demonstrate that the dispersion integrals for the relevant elastic $ep$-scattering amplitude converge and do not need subtraction. This allows us to make clean prediction for the real part of the TPE amplitude at forward angles. We furthermore compare $e^+p$ and $e^-p$ cross sections which depends on the real part of TPE amplitude, and predict the positron cross section to exceed the electron one by a few per cent, with the difference ranging from 1.4% to 2.8% for electron $lab$ energies in the range from 3 to 45 GeV. We furthermore predict that the absolute value of this asymmetry grows with energy, which makes it promising for experimental tests.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the positron-proton cross section exceeds the electron-proton cross section by 1.4% to 2.8% for electron lab energies from 3 to 45 GeV in the forward regime, with the hedge that this result depends on the convergence of unsubtracted dispersion relations for the two-photon exchange amplitude and on a phenomenological model for the t-dependence of the virtual photon cross section.

## Method
The imaginary part of the two-photon exchange amplitude for elastic electron-proton scattering in forward kinematics was related to deep inelastic scattering nucleon structure functions, specifically using the Callan-Gross relation and a phenomenological parametrization of the virtual photon cross section from low-x data (Cvetic, 2000). The real part of the two-photon exchange amplitude was then obtained from the imaginary part using unsubtracted dispersion relations at fixed momentum transfer, exploiting the analyticity and crossing symmetry of the invariant amplitudes. The electron helicity-conserving amplitudes F2 and F3 were computed via phase space integrals over the electron energy, with a threshold regulator function to ensure correct behavior at pion production threshold.

## Key numbers
- The predicted e+p/e-p cross section ratio asymmetry ranges from 1.4% to 2.8% for electron lab energies from 3 to 45 GeV.
- The pomeron trajectory intercept used in the Regge parametrization of the total photoabsorption cross section is alpha_P = 1.097 +/- 0.002.
- The exponential slope parameter for the t-dependence of the Compton cross section is B approximately 7 GeV^-2, valid for -t up to about 0.8 GeV^2.
- The real part of the amplitude delta-tilde-F2 is only about 10% of the real part of delta-tilde-F3 at forward angles.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological approach of Afanasev (2000) and Gorchtein (2005) for beam normal spin asymmetries to the electron helicity-conserving amplitudes that enter the unpolarized cross section and charge asymmetry. It contrasts with the low-energy dispersive approach of Bernabeu (1975) which used once-subtracted dispersion relations in the annihilation channel and nucleon polarizabilities. The results enable experimental tests of two-photon exchange effects at JLab and VEPP-3, where the e+p/e-p cross section ratio will be measured over a wide angular range.
