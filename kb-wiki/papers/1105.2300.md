# Gravity Dual Corrections to the Heavy Quark Potential at Finite-Temperature

> We apply gauge/gravity duality to compute $1/N^2_c$ corrections to the heavy quark potentials of a quark--anti-quark pair ($Q\bar Q$) and of a quark--quark pair ($QQ$) immersed into the strongly coupled N = 4 SYM plasma. On the gravity side these corrections come from the exchanges of supergravity modes between two string worldsheets stretching from the UV boundary of AdS space to the black hole horizon in the bulk and smeared over $S^5$. We find that the contributions to the $Q\bar Q$ potential coming from the exchanges of all of the relevant modes (such as dilaton, massive scalar, 2-form field, and graviton) are all attractive, leading to an attractive net $Q\bar Q$ potential. We show that at large separations $r$ and/or high-temperature $T$ the potential is of Yukawa-type, dominated by the graviton exchange, in agreement with earlier findings. On the other hand, at small-$r T$ the $Q\bar Q$ potential scales as $\sim (1/r) \ln (1/rT)$. In the case of $QQ$ potential the 2-form contribution changes sign and becomes repulsive: however, the net $QQ$ potential remains attractive. At large-$r T$ it is dominated by the graviton exchange, while at small-$r T$ the $QQ$ potential becomes Coulomb-like.

## Key claim

The paper computes the leading $1/N_c^2$ corrections to the heavy quark-antiquark ($Q\bar Q$) and quark-quark ($QQ$) potentials in strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ SYM plasma using gauge/gravity duality, finding that the adjoint (color-octet) potential at strong coupling scales as $\lambda/N_c^2$, the same power of the 't Hooft coupling as at weak coupling, unlike the singlet potential which changes from $\lambda$ to $\sqrt{\lambda}$.

## Method

The authors calculate the exchange of supergravity modes (dilaton, massive scalar $t^0$, NS-NS and RR 2-form fields, and graviton) between two straight string worldsheets stretching from the UV boundary of AdS space to the black hole horizon, with the strings smeared over $S^5$ to restrict to $k=0$ Kaluza-Klein modes. The calculation is performed in Euclidean time using the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole metric, with the adjoint potential extracted from the Polyakov loop correlator at order $1/N_c^2$ via the decomposition of the free energy into singlet and adjoint contributions following McLerran (1981) and Nadkarni (1986).

## Key numbers

* The adjoint potential at strong coupling scales as $V_{adj}(r) \sim \lambda/N_c^2$, where $\lambda = g^2 N_c$ is the 't Hooft coupling and $N_c$ is the number of colors.
* At large separations $r$ and/or high temperature $T$, the $Q\bar Q$ potential is of Yukawa-type, with the exponent determined by the first pole of the momentum-space potential along the imaginary-$q$ axis, corresponding to the glueball mass in QCD$_3$ calculated by Csaki (1998) and Brower (2000).
* At small $rT$, the $Q\bar Q$ potential scales as $\sim (1/r) \ln(1/rT)$, where $r$ is the quark-antiquark separation and $T$ is the temperature.
* The $QQ$ potential at small $rT$ becomes Coulomb-like, without the logarithmic singularity found in the $Q\bar Q$ case.

## Context

This work extends the earlier AdS/CFT calculations of the finite-temperature heavy quark potential by Rey (1998) and Brandhuber (1998), who identified the hanging and straight string configurations but obtained a kink artifact in the large-$N_c$ approximation. The paper follows the suggestion by Bak (2007) that $N_c^2$ configurations of straight strings enhance the otherwise subleading supergravity exchange contributions, and adopts the decomposition into color-singlet and color-adjoint potentials proposed by Albacete (2008). The results enable a complete determination of the adjoint potential at strong coupling, and the authors suggest that the 2-form exchange (which changes sign between $Q\bar Q$ and $QQ$) may correspond to chromo-electric modes, while the scalar and graviton exchanges correspond to chromo-magnetic modes.
