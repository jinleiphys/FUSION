# On coupled-channel dynamics in the presence of anomalous thresholds

> We explore a general framework how to treat coupled-channel systems in the presence of overlapping left and right-hand cuts as well as anomalous thresholds. Such systems are studied in terms of a generalized potential, where we exploit the known analytic structure of t- and u-channel forces as the exchange masses get smaller approaching their physical values. Given an approximate generalized potential the coupled-channel reaction amplitudes are defined in terms of non-linear systems of integral equations. For large exchange masses, where there are no anomalous thresholds present, conventional $N/D$ methods are applicable to derive numerical solutions to the latter. At a formal level a generalization to the anomalous case is readily formulated by use of suitable contour integrations with amplitudes to be evaluated at complex energies. However, it is a considerable challenge to find numerical solutions to anomalous systems set up on a set of complex contours. By a suitable deformations of left-hand and right-hand cut lines we managed to establish a framework of linear integral equations defined for real energies. Explicit expressions are derived for the driving terms that hold for an arbitrary number of channels. Our approach is illustrated in terms of a schematic 3-channel systems. It is demonstrated that despite the presence of anomalous thresholds the scattering amplitude can be represented in terms of 3 phase shifts and 3 in-elasticity parameters, as one would expect from the coupled-channel unitarity condition.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general framework for treating coupled-channel systems with anomalous thresholds, but the key hedge is that the approach requires the inclusion of a specifically constructed "box" term in the generalized potential to restore the Schwarz reflection principle and unitarity, without which the non-linear integral equations would not yield physically consistent amplitudes.

## Method

The authors start from a spectral representation of t- and u-channel exchange processes and deform the left-hand and right-hand cut lines into the complex plane to avoid crossing of cut structures. They then derive a set of linear integral equations defined for real energies by deforming the complex contours back toward the real axis, and construct a minimal second-order box term that restores the Schwarz reflection principle and coupled-channel unitarity. The approach is illustrated with a schematic three-channel model where the generalized potential is defined by anomalous spectral weights on specified contour paths.

## Key numbers

* The schematic model uses three channels with masses M1 = 4.2 m_pi, M2 = 4.5 m_pi, M3 = 4.8 m_pi, all with pion mass m_pi for the lighter particles.
* Anomalous threshold values are set at mu1^2 = 25 m_pi^2 and mu2^2 = 29 m_pi^2, with return points at hat_mu1^2 = 28 m_pi^2 and hat_mu2^2 = 32 m_pi^2.
* The matching scale mu^2 is chosen as 20 m_pi^2.
* The anomalous spectral weight rho^A_{ab}(s) contains a factor 20 m_pi^2 divided by the product of phase-space factors.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts by Ball, Frazer and Nauenberg (1962) and Greben and Kok (1976) on two-channel systems with anomalous thresholds, which the authors note were never numerically implemented and whose conclusions were conflicting. The framework enables treatment of multi-channel systems involving vector mesons and baryon decuplet states relevant to the hadrogenesis conjecture, where anomalous thresholds arise from t- and u-channel exchange forces. The authors state that their Fig. 5 represents the first numerical solution of an anomalous three-channel system in the published literature.
