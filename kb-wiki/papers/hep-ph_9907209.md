# Parton saturation, production, and equilibration in high energy nuclear collisions

> Deeply inelastic scattering of electrons off nuclei can determine whether parton distributions saturate at HERA energies. If so, this phenomenon will also tell us a great deal about how particles are produced, and whether they equilibrate, in high energy nuclear collisions.

# Parton saturation, production, and equilibration in high energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim
If the qualitative features of the Caldwell plot from ZEUS data remain unchanged after improved averaging over a smaller range of $Q^2$, the turnover in $dF_2/d\log(Q^2)$ at small $x$ may demonstrate the onset of parton saturation at HERA energies, which would then be highly relevant for particle production and equilibration in nuclear collisions at LHC and, to a lesser extent, RHIC.

## Method
The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) approach to QCD at small $x$, where the soft gluon modes are described by a classical Yang-Mills field coupled to a color charge source $\rho$ on the light cone. For a single nucleus, the classical gluon distribution is computed by averaging over Gaussian color charge fluctuations, yielding a saturated distribution with a characteristic scale $Q_s$. For nuclear collisions, the classical fields from two colliding nuclei are matched at $\tau=0$ in the forward light cone, and gluon production is computed both perturbatively to $O(\rho^2)$ and non-perturbatively to all orders in $\rho$ via lattice simulations of the SU(2) gauge theory in 2+1 dimensions with an adjoint scalar field.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale $Q_s$ is approximately 1 GeV for RHIC energies and 2–3 GeV for LHC energies.
- The turnover region in the Caldwell plot lies between $\langle Q^2 \rangle \approx 1$–10 GeV$^2$.
- Lattice simulations were performed on transverse lattices ranging from 10$\times$10 to 160$\times$160 for SU(2).
- The energy per unit transverse area per unit rapidity $\epsilon \tau$ scales as $\mu^3$ and reaches a constant at $\tau \sim 1/\alpha_S\mu$.
- The gluon distribution for large $x_t$ falls as $1/x_t^2$ with a $1/\alpha_S$ prefactor.
- The quark saturation scale $Q_s^q$ is related to the gluon saturation scale by the ratio of Casimirs $C_A/C_F$.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to small-$x$ QCD developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Kovchegov (1996) to nuclear collisions, building on the classical gluon distribution formalism. It interprets the Caldwell plot from ZEUS data as a possible signature of saturation, and connects this to the earlier saturation-based multiplicity estimate of Blaizot and Mueller (1987). The results enable future lattice computations of the coefficient $c$ in the relation $dN/dy = c \, 2A \, xG(x,Q_s^2)$ and provide initial conditions for parton cascade models of equilibration, while also motivating the need for an electron-nucleus collider to confirm saturation.
