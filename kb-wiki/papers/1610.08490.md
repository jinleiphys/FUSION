# Constraints on rapidity-dependent initial conditions from charged particle pseudorapidity densities and two-particle correlations

> We study the initial three-dimensional spatial configuration of the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using centrality and rapidity-dependent measurements of charged particle pseudorapidity densities and two-particle correlations. A cumulant-generating function is used to parametrize the rapidity dependence of local entropy deposition and extend arbitrary boost-invariant initial conditions to nonzero beam rapidities. The model is compared to p+Pb and Pb+Pb single-particle distributions and systematically optimized using Bayesian parameter estimation to extract high-probability initial condition parameters. The optimized initial conditions are then compared to a number of experimental observables including two-particle rapidity correlations, the rapidity dependence of anisotropic flow, and event-plane decorrelations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a data-driven method to constrain the three-dimensional (rapidity-dependent) initial conditions of the quark-gluon plasma, using a cumulant-generating function to parametrize longitudinal entropy deposition; the authors hedge that the use of ideal (nonviscous) hydrodynamics is acceptable because they calibrate only on multiplicity observables and two-particle pseudorapidity correlations, which receive little modification from viscosity.

## Method

The authors extend the boost-invariant TRENTo initial condition model to nonzero rapidity by factorizing the entropy density into a transverse midrapidity component and a rapidity-dependent function parametrized via the first three cumulants (mean, standard deviation, skewness) of a local rapidity distribution. They embed this parametric model in 3+1D ideal hydrodynamics with a UrQMD hadronic afterburner, generate 100 parameter configurations via a maximin Latin hypercube design, and train Gaussian process emulators on principal components of the charged-particle pseudorapidity density and the root-mean-square of the first Legendre coefficient of two-particle correlations. Bayesian parameter estimation with Markov chain Monte Carlo is then used to extract posterior distributions for the nine model parameters by fitting to p+Pb and Pb+Pb data.

## Key numbers

*   The TRENTo midrapidity parameters from a previous Bayesian analysis (Bernhard 2016) established 90% credible intervals for the effective nucleon width \(w \approx 0.5 \pm 0.1\) fm and entropy deposition parameter \(p \approx 0.0 \pm 0.2\).
*   The rapidity-dependent model introduces a skewness coefficient \(\gamma_0\) with a range of 0.0–10.0 for the relative-skewness model and 0.0–3.6 for the absolute-skewness model.
*   The rapidity width coefficient \(\sigma_0\) is varied in the range 2.0–4.0, and the rapidity shift mean coefficient \(\mu_0\) in the range 0.0–1.0.
*   The emulator training used \(4 \times 10^3\) Pb+Pb events and \(10^4\) p+Pb events at each of the 100 design points.
*   The first 6 principal components for each of the two pseudorapidity density observables and 4 principal components for the rms \(a_1\) accounted for 99.5% of the observed variance.

## Context

This work extends the boost-invariant Bayesian analysis of TRENTo initial conditions (Bernhard 2016) to include longitudinal structure, addressing the need for realistic three-dimensional initial conditions in small asymmetric collisions (p+Pb) where boost invariance is a poor approximation. It builds on prior rapidity-dependent models such as shifted and tilted initial conditions (Bozek 2010) and the longitudinal extension of IP-Glasma (Schenke 2016), and it enables future systematic quantification of QGP transport properties (e.g., the temperature dependence of shear viscosity) by providing constrained three-dimensional initial condition parameters for use in viscous hydrodynamic simulations.
