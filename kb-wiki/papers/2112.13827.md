# Mass-Energy Equivalence in Bound Three-Nucleon Systems

> The mass defect formula reflects the equivalence of mass and energy for bound nuclear systems. We study three-nucleon systems $^3$H and $^3$He, considering the neutron and proton as indistinguishable particles ($AAA$ model) or taking into account the real masses of neutrons and protons ($AAB$ model). We have focused on conceptual problems of the $AAA$ model, which is widely used for $3N$ calculations. In particular, the $AAA$ model is incompatible with the mass defect formula, which naturally corresponds to the $AAB$ model. In addition, the $AAA$ model has a cyclic permutation symmetry, which is breaking in the natural $AAB$ model. The latter problem cannot be eliminated within the perturbative $AAA$ approach, in which the mass difference effect is simulated by correcting the kinetic energy operator. Earlier it was reported that the accuracy of such $AAA$ calculations is 1~keV. An example of the $AAB$ calculation, we numerically estimate the effect of the difference between the neutron and proton masses on the energy calculated without any approximation with the accuracy of 0.1~keV. Another manifestation of the equivalence of mass $m$ and energy $E$ can be expressed by the formula $dE/dm=Const$. To show this dependence of the three-body energy on the nucleon mass, we performed realistic calculations within the $AAA$ approximation, varying the averaged nucleon mass. The mass-energy compensation effect for the three-body Hamiltonian is shown. According to this, we have determined the effective nucleon mass required to compensate for the perturbative effect of a three-body potential.

## Key claim

The widely used $AAA$ model, which treats neutrons and protons as indistinguishable particles with an averaged mass, is fundamentally incompatible with the mass defect formula for bound three-nucleon systems; this incompatibility is a conceptual problem that cannot be fully resolved by perturbative mass corrections, and rigorous treatment requires the $AAB$ model that uses the physical masses of neutrons and protons.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for the $^3$H and $^3$He systems, comparing the $AAA$ model (identical particles with averaged nucleon mass) with the $AAB$ model (two identical particles and one distinct particle with physical masses). For the $AAB$ calculations, they used a semi-realistic MT-I-III potential modified to reproduce experimental spin-singlet scattering lengths for $nn$, $np$, and $pp$ interactions. For the $AAA$ calculations, they used the charge-independent Argonne AV14 potential and varied the averaged nucleon mass to study the mass-energy compensation effect, where changes in the kinetic energy operator can compensate for the perturbative effect of a three-body potential.

## Key numbers

- The mass defect formula gives the $^3$H ground state energy as -8.48179622 MeV, using free neutron mass 939.56542052 MeV, proton mass 938.27208816 MeV, and triton mass 2808.92113298 MeV.
- The difference between the total mass of particles in the $AAA$ model and the physical masses is about 0.647 MeV for $^3$H.
- Within the $AAB$ model using the MT-I-III potential, the mass difference effect $\Delta E_m$ for $^3$H is 4.7 keV.
- Within the $AAB$ model using the MT-I-III potential, the mass difference effect $\Delta E_m$ for $^3$He is -7.4 keV.
- The $AAA$ calculation with the AV14 potential (up to $J_{max}=7$) gives $^3$H binding energy -7.684 MeV and $^3$He binding energy -7.018 MeV, compared to experimental values -8.481798 MeV and -7.718043 MeV respectively.
- The perturbative mass correction for the $AAA$ model using N2LO+3NFs potentials gives $\langle \Delta H_0 \rangle$ about 6 keV, leading to an effective mass correction $\delta m$ of about 0.3 MeV when the averaged kinetic energy is about 35 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the $AAB$ model for three-nucleon systems (Filikhin et al. 2016, 2019) and addresses conceptual problems in the standard $AAA$ approach used in realistic $3N$ calculations by Nogga et al. (2000) and others. The paper shows that the $AAA$ model's cyclic permutation symmetry is broken in the natural $AAB$ model, and that perturbative corrections cannot fully resolve this. The mass-energy compensation effect introduced here provides a phenomenological mechanism to understand how changes in nucleon mass can mimic the effect of three-body forces, which is relevant for future studies of hypernuclear systems like $\Lambda NN$ where the mass difference is much larger (176.12 MeV).
