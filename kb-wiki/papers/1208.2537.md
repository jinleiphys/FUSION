# From the chiral magnetic wave to the charge dependence of elliptic flow

> The quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy ion collisions contains charged chiral fermions evolving in an external magnetic field. At finite density of electric charge or baryon number (resulting either from nuclear stopping or from fluctuations), the triangle anomaly induces in the plasma the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW). The CMW first induces a separation of the right and left chiral charges along the magnetic field; the resulting dipolar axial charge density in turn induces the oppositely directed vector charge currents leading to an electric quadrupole moment of the quark-gluon plasma. Boosted by the strong collective flow, the electric quadrupole moment translates into the charge dependence of the elliptic flow coefficients, so that $v_2(π^+) < v_2(π^-)$ (at positive net charge). Using the latest quantitative simulations of the produced magnetic field and solving the CMW equation, we make further quantitative estimates of the produced $v_2$ splitting and its centrality dependence. We compare the results with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic wave (CMW) in the quark-gluon plasma, driven by the triangle anomaly in the presence of a magnetic field and finite vector charge density, produces an electric quadrupole moment that translates into a charge-dependent splitting of elliptic flow coefficients, with the specific prediction that \(v_2(\pi^+) < v_2(\pi^-)\) at positive net charge; the effect survives event averaging and is linear in the net charge asymmetry \(A\).

## Method

The authors solve the CMW wave equation for left- and right-handed quark currents in a fixed background, using the Sakai-Sugimoto model to obtain the wave velocity and diffusion constants. They simulate Au-Au collisions with the KLN model for the initial almond-shaped fireball and use event-by-event magnetic field computations from Bzdak and Skokov (2011) and Deng and Huang (2012). The resulting electric charge quadrupole moment is then mapped onto the elliptic flow splitting \(v_2^- - v_2^+ = r_e A\) by assuming the charge asymmetry distribution is carried over by the strong radial flow.

## Key numbers

* The wave velocity is \(v = \frac{N_c e B \alpha}{2\pi^2}\), with \(\alpha\) the susceptibility and \(N_c = 3\).
* For \(\sqrt{s} = 11\) GeV collisions at RHIC, with net charge asymmetry \(A \sim 0.3\) and computed quadrupole \(r_e \sim 0.01\) (using the Deng-Huang magnetic field with lifetime \(t = 4\) fm/c), the predicted splitting is \(v_2^- - v_2^+ \sim 0.003\), about 10% of the overall elliptic flow at that energy.
* The magnetic field magnitude used in the simulation is \(eB = 2.8 m_\pi^2\) for the example shown (impact parameter \(b = 3\) fm, temperature \(T = 165\) MeV, magnetic field lifetime \(\tau = 4\) fm).
* The QCD sphaleron rate is quoted as \(\Gamma \sim 0.3\; T^4\).
* The computed charge quadrupole \(r_e\) is shown as a function of impact parameter \(b\) for magnetic field lifetimes of 2, 3, 4, and 5 fm/c, using both the Bzdak-Skokov and Deng-Huang magnetic field results.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal of the chiral magnetic wave by Kharzeev and Yee (2011) and the initial estimate of the charge quadrupole by Burnier et al. (2011) by incorporating realistic, event-by-event magnetic field simulations. The predicted linear relation between the elliptic flow splitting and the charge asymmetry \(A\) was subsequently tested by the STAR collaboration at RHIC (2012), finding good agreement with the computed quadrupole magnitude \(r_e\) over a wide range of centralities. The results provide evidence for the CMW-induced quadrupole deformation of the quark-gluon plasma and motivate further studies with hydrodynamic simulations, improved knowledge of the plasma's electric conductivity, and experimental tests in U+U collisions and at LHC energies.
