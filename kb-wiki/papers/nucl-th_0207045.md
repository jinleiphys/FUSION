# Low-energy photodisintegration of $^9$Be with the molecular orbit model

> We use a microscopic three-cluster model with the $α+α+n$ configuration to analyze with astrophysical interests the photodisintegration cross section of $^9$Be. The valence neutron in $^9$Be is treated in the molecular orbit model (MO), including continuum states by the box discretization method. It is shown that good agreement is achieved with the recently measured B(E1) transition probability from the ground state to the first 1/2$^+$ state when the box size is enlarged, resulting in reasonable reproduction of the experimental data for the photodisintegration cross section. The Coulomb dissociation of $^9$Be is also discussed.

## Key claim

The molecular orbit model with a sufficiently large model space for the valence neutron (up to 15 fm from the core) reproduces the recently measured B(E1) transition probability from the ground state to the first 1/2+ state of 9Be, but the energy of that state remains sensitive to the box size and could not be converged with controlled numerical accuracy, requiring the use of the experimental value instead.

## Method

A microscopic three-cluster model with an alpha+alpha+n configuration was used, treating the valence neutron in the molecular orbit model with continuum states included via the box discretization method. The total intrinsic wave function was fully antisymmetrized with Gaussian single-particle wave functions, and basis states with different alpha-alpha distances and neutron positions were superposed variationally. The photodisintegration cross sections were computed from E1 transition probabilities using Fermi's Golden rule, with the delta function smeared by a Breit-Wigner function to mimic neutron emission.

## Key numbers

- The ground 3/2- state of 9Be and the ground 0+ state of 8Be were calculated at -56.3 MeV and -54.7 MeV respectively, giving the correct binding energy for 9Be relative to the 8Be+n threshold (1.7 MeV experimentally) within 0.1 MeV accuracy.
- The B(E1) value from the ground 3/2- state to the first 1/2+ state increased from 0.022 e2 fm2 with a 2.5 fm box to 0.048 e2 fm2 with a 15.0 fm box, consistent with the experimental value of 0.054 +/- 0.004 e2 fm2 from Utsunomiya et al. (2000).
- The first 1/2+ state lies above the 8Be+n threshold by only 19 keV, with an excitation energy of 1.68 MeV experimentally; the calculation gave 2.05 MeV for this state.
- The Q-value for 9Be breakup into alpha+alpha+n is -1.57 MeV, and for breakup into alpha+5He is -2.47 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular orbit model previously applied to 9Be by Itagaki and Okabe (2000) and to heavier Be isotopes, and is complementary to the microscopic three-cluster calculations of Descouvemont (2001) which also achieved good agreement with experimental B(E1) values and photodisintegration cross sections. The results enable more accurate reaction rates for the alpha(alpha n,gamma)9Be process in r-process nucleosynthesis in core-collapse supernovae, as discussed by Woosley and Hoffman (1992) and Sumiyoshi et al. (2002), and also address the underestimation of Coulomb excitation probabilities for 9Be + 208Pb reactions reported by Hinde et al. (2002).
