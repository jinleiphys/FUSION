# Study of the core-excited Fano Resonances in the neutron-rich C-isotopes

> Fano Resonances are a new continuum excitation mode in light exotic nuclei. By the QPC model we explain these sharp and asymmetric structures as due to the coupling of a single particle elastic channel to the closed core-excited channels. Theoretical model calculations are compared for 15C, 17C and 19C. The theoretical results for 15C are in qualitative agreement with the experimental spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Fano resonances in neutron-rich carbon isotopes arise from the coupling of a single-particle elastic channel to closed core-excited channels, but the theoretical results for 15C are only in qualitative agreement with the experimental spectrum, and the calculations do not account for reaction dynamics.

## Method

The authors extend the quasiparticle-core coupling (QPC) model into the continuum, incorporating information from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations. The QPC Hamiltonian couples one-quasiparticle (1-QP) and three-quasiparticle (3-QP) core-excited states via a residual interaction V13, and the coupled equations are solved numerically by matching internal and asymptotic radial wave functions to obtain scattering matrix elements and partial wave elastic cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energies Sn for the three isotopes: 1.218 MeV (15C), 0.73 MeV (17C), 0.16 MeV (19C)
- For 15C, four theoretical resonance levels found at excitation energies Ex = 6.67, 7.36, 7.70, 8.92 MeV with widths Gamma = 66, 80, 141, 85 keV respectively
- Experimental resonances in 15C observed at Ex = (6.77 +/- 0.06, 7.30 +/- 0.06, 8.50 +/- 0.06) MeV with widths Gamma = 160, 70, 140 keV respectively
- Core-excited states included for 15C: 6.094 (1-), 6.728 (3-), 7.012 (2+), 8.317 (2+) MeV
- Core-excited states included for 17C: 1.766 (2+), 3.986 (2+), 4.142 (4+) MeV
- Core-excited states included for 19C: 1.620 (2+), 2.967 (4+), 3.313 (2+), 5.502 (1-) MeV
- Coupling strength F0 = 5 MeV used for the results shown

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle-core coupling model of Lenske (2004) into the continuum to explain Fano resonances previously observed in 11Be by Cappuzzello et al. (2001, 2004) and in 15C by Orrigo et al. (2003, 2004). The systematic comparison across 15C, 17C, and 19C shows a progressive lowering of bound-state-embedded-in-continuum (BSEC) energies and enhanced intensity with increasing neutron excess, indicating stronger dynamical correlations. This enables predictions for more exotic neutron-rich systems where sharper and more intense BSEC resonances are expected at lower excitation energies, with implications for astrophysical capture and knockout reactions in neutron-rich stellar environments.
