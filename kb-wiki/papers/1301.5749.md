# Nuclear asymmetry energy and isovector stiffness within the effective surface approximation

> The isoscalar and isovector particle densities and the surface tension coefficients at the average binding energy are used to derive analytical expressions of the neutron skin thickness and the isovector stiffness of sharp edged proton-neutron asymmetric nuclei. For most Skyrme forces these quantities are significantly larger than the well known ones. Using the analytical isovector surface energy constants in the framework of the hydrodynamical and the Fermi-liquid droplet models the mean energies and the sum rules of the isovector giant dipole resonances are in fair agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytical expressions for the neutron skin thickness and isovector stiffness within the effective surface approximation, finding that for most Skyrme forces these quantities are significantly larger than the well-known empirical values from the liquid droplet model, and that the isovector surface energy constants obtained yield fair agreement with experimental giant dipole resonance data when used in hydrodynamical and Fermi-liquid droplet models.

## Method

The authors used the effective surface approximation within the extended Thomas-Fermi approach to nuclear energy density functional theory, minimizing the nuclear energy as a functional of isoscalar and isovector densities under constraints of fixed particle number and neutron excess. They solved the resulting Lagrange equations analytically up to leading order in the small parameter a/R (where a is the diffuse-edge thickness and R is the nuclear radius), obtaining universal density distributions independent of specific nuclear properties. From these solutions they derived analytical expressions for the surface symmetry energy coefficient, neutron skin thickness, and isovector stiffness in terms of Skyrme force parameters.

## Key numbers

- The volume symmetry energy constant J ≈ 30 MeV and the particle separation energy b_V ≈ 16 MeV specify the volume terms in the energy density
- The saturation density of infinite nuclear matter is approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The diffuseness parameter a ≈ 0.5-0.6 fm for the Skyrme forces considered
- The incompressibility modulus K ≈ 220-260 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter
- The isovector stiffness Q values from the effective surface approximation are significantly larger than the empirical values Q ≈ 14-35 MeV from the liquid droplet model
- The experimental IVGDR energy constant D_exp ≈ 80 MeV for heavy nuclei, compared with D_∞ ≈ 88 MeV from the hydrodynamical model
- For the Skyrme force T6 (with C_- = 0), the isovector surface energy constant k_S = 0 and Q diverges
- For RATP and SkI Skyrme forces, the isovector stiffness Q is negative because C_- > 0

## Context

This work extends the effective surface approximation developed by the authors and collaborators (Strutinsky, Magner, and others) to include asymmetry and spin-orbit effects, providing analytical expressions that contrast with the phenomenological liquid droplet model of Myers and Swiatecki (1969, 1980). The results enable calculations of isovector giant dipole resonance properties within hydrodynamical and Fermi-liquid droplet models, and the authors suggest future applications to pygmy resonances and low-lying isovector collective states using the periodic orbit theory.
