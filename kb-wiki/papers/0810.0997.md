# On the definitions of the gamma*N -> N* helicity amplitudes

> We present definitions and formulas that may be useful for a consistent computation of helicity amplitudes for the process gamma* N -> N* in theoretical approaches. Of particular importance is the correct determination of the common sign of the amplitudes and of the relative sign between the transverse (A12,A32) and longitudinal (S12) amplitudes. This clarification is necessary to clear up confusions present in theoretical works. Using the definitions presented in this paper will enable a direct comparison with amplitudes extracted from experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper clarifies that the helicity amplitudes \(A_{1/2}, A_{3/2}, S_{1/2}\) extracted from experimental data on \(\gamma^* N \rightarrow N\pi\) contain an implicit sign from the \(\pi N N^*\) vertex, and that theoretical calculations must include this sign factor \(\zeta = -\text{sign}(g^*/g)\) to enable proper comparison with data; without this correction, the common sign and relative sign between transverse and longitudinal amplitudes are often inconsistent across different theoretical approaches.

## Method

The authors performed covariant calculations of the resonance and Born term contributions to \(\gamma^* p \rightarrow \pi N\) for resonances with \(J^P = 1/2^\pm\) and extended the results to higher spins using the formalism of Devenish. They derived explicit relations linking the helicity amplitudes defined through multipole amplitudes (used in experimental extraction) to those defined through the hadron electromagnetic current and form factors (used in theory), incorporating the sign of the \(\pi N N^*\) coupling constant ratio. They also presented nonrelativistic quark model formulas for the \(\gamma^* p \rightarrow P_{11}(1440)\) and \(\gamma^* p \rightarrow S_{11}(1535)\) transitions, with explicit wave functions and the correct sign conventions.

## Key numbers

- For the \(\gamma^* p \rightarrow P_{11}(1440)\) transition in the nonrelativistic quark model: \(\mathcal{A}_{1/2} = -\sqrt{\frac{2\pi\alpha}{K}} \frac{|\mathbf{k}|^3}{6\sqrt{6} m_q \beta^2} e^{-\frac{\mathbf{k}^2}{6\beta^2}}\) and \(\mathcal{S}_{1/2} = -\sqrt{\frac{2\pi\alpha}{K}} \frac{\mathbf{k}^2}{6\sqrt{3} \beta^2} e^{-\frac{\mathbf{k}^2}{6\beta^2}}\)
- For the \(\gamma^* p \rightarrow S_{11}(1535)\) transition in the nonrelativistic quark model: \(\mathcal{A}_{1/2} = \sqrt{\frac{8\pi\alpha}{K}} \frac{\beta}{6 m_q} \left(1 + \frac{\mathbf{k}^2}{2\beta^2}\right) e^{-\frac{\mathbf{k}^2}{6\beta^2}}\) and \(\mathcal{S}_{1/2} = \sqrt{\frac{4\pi\alpha}{K}} \frac{|\mathbf{k}|}{6\beta} e^{-\frac{\mathbf{k}^2}{6\beta^2}}\)
- The relation between the experimental and theoretical amplitudes is \(A_{1/2,3/2} = \zeta \mathcal{A}_{1/2,3/2}\) and \(S_{1/2} = \zeta \mathcal{S}_{1/2}\), with \(\zeta = -\text{sign}(g^*/g)\)
- For the \(P_{11}(1440)\) resonance, the \(A_{1/2}\) amplitude at \(Q^2=0\) has opposite signs in nonrelativistic versus light-front relativistic quark model calculations, requiring the \(\pi N N^*\) sign correction for \(Q^2 > 0.4\) GeV\(^2\)

## Context

This work addresses confusion in theoretical calculations of \(\gamma^* N \rightarrow N^*\) helicity amplitudes, particularly regarding the common sign and the relative sign between transverse (\(A_{1/2}, A_{3/2}\)) and longitudinal (\(S_{1/2}\)) amplitudes. It extends the formalism of Devenish and provides consistent definitions that enable direct comparison with experimental data from JLab-CLAS measurements of \(\vec{e}p \rightarrow ep\pi^0, en\pi^+\) reactions (Joo et al., 2002-2005; Egiyan et al., 2006; Park et al., 2008; Aznauryan et al., 2004-2008). The paper enables proper comparison of quark model predictions (Capstick, 1992; Aznauryan, 1993; Weber, 1997; Simula, 1998; Warns, 1990; Santopinto, 1998) with each other and with experimental data, and provides explicit formulas for nonrelativistic quark model calculations of the \(P_{11}(1440)\) and \(S_{11}(1535)\) resonances.
