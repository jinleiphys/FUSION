# Neutrino Reactions on Deuteron

> The cross sections for the ν-d and \barν-d reactions are calculated for the incident energy up to E_ν= 170 MeV, with the use of a phenomenological Lagrangian approach. We assess and improve the reliability of the employed calculational method by examining the dependence of the results on various input and approximations that go into the calculation. The main points of improvements over the existing work are: (1) use of the "modern" NN potentials; (2) use of the more accurate nucleon weak-interaction form factors; (3) monitoring the strength of a vertex that governs the exchange-current contribution, with the use of data on the related process, n+p\to d+γ. In addition to the total cross sections, we present various differential cross sections that are expected to be useful for the SNO and other experiments. In the low energy regime relevant to the solar neutrinos, the newly calculated total cross sections agree with the existing literature values. The origins of slight differences found for higher energies are discussed. The ratio between the neutral-current and charged-current reaction cross sections is found to be extremely stable against any variations in the input of our calculation.

## Key claim

The authors calculate neutrino-deuteron and antineutrino-deuteron reaction cross sections up to 170 MeV using a phenomenological Lagrangian approach (PhLA), claiming that the ratio between neutral-current and charged-current cross sections is extremely stable against any variations in input, while the total cross sections at low energies agree with existing literature and show only slight differences (up to 6%) at higher energies.

## Method

The authors compute cross sections for charged-current and neutral-current reactions of neutrinos and antineutrinos with deuterons using a phenomenological Lagrangian approach (PhLA) that includes one-body impulse approximation (IA) terms and two-body meson exchange current (MEX) terms. They employ three modern nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne-v18, Reid93, and Nijmegen II) as input for the nuclear wave functions, use updated nucleon weak-interaction form factors from recent analyses, and monitor the πNΔ vertex strength by simultaneously calculating the n+p→d+γ reaction cross section. The cross sections are evaluated via multipole expansion of the nuclear currents, with the final state treated as a two-nucleon scattering state.

## Key numbers

- Incident neutrino energy range: up to E_ν = 170 MeV
- Fermi coupling constant: G_F = 1.166 × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- Cabibbo angle: cos θ_C = 0.9749
- Weinberg angle: sin^2 θ_W = 0.2312
- Axial-vector form factor at q^2=0: f_A = -1.254
- Cutoff masses for vertex form factors: Λ_π = 1.18 GeV, Λ_ρ = 1.45 GeV
- For higher energies, the present calculation gives ν-d total cross sections larger than those of KN by up to 6%

## Context

This work extends the PhLA calculations of Tatara et al. (1992), Doi et al. (1993), and Kubodera and Nozawa (1994) by using modern NN potentials (Argonne-v18, Reid93, Nijmegen II) and more accurate nucleon weak-interaction form factors. The results are complementary to effective field theory (EFT) calculations by Butler and Chen (2000) and Butler, Chen and Kong (2000), which require an unknown low-energy coefficient L_1A that can be adjusted to match the PhLA results. The detailed differential cross sections provided are intended for use in analyzing Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) experiments, where uncertainties in ν-d cross sections represent the largest ambiguity in most physics conclusions according to Bahcall, Krastev and Smirnov (2000).
