# Modelling the Off-Shell Dependence of $π^{o}- η$ Mixing with Quark Loops

> It is shown that including form factors for the quark-pseudoscalar meson couplings of the Georgi-Manohar chiral quark model allows one to obtain the leading off-shell dependence of $π^{o}- η$ mixing (as predicted by chiral perturbation theory) from the effect of quark loops on the meson propagators. Implications for $ρ^{o} - ω$ mixing and for the effects on meson mixing contributions to few body charge symmetry breaking observables are also discussed.

# Modelling the Off-Shell Dependence of π⁰-η Mixing with Quark Loops

## Key claim
The Georgi-Manohar chiral quark model, when augmented with monopole form factors at quark-pseudoscalar meson vertices, can reproduce the leading off-shell (q²-dependent) behavior of π⁰-η mixing predicted by one-loop chiral perturbation theory, provided the form factor cutoff Λ is fixed by requiring the model to reproduce the leading-order pseudoscalar mass relations. This success strengthens the case that the analogous quark-loop model for ρ⁰-ω mixing (Goldman, Henderson, Thomas) may yield reliable off-shell behavior for that mixing as well, potentially altering previous estimates of charge symmetry breaking contributions in few-nucleon systems.

## Method
The authors employ the Georgi-Manohar chiral quark model, which couples constituent quarks to the pseudoscalar meson octet via pseudovector couplings and includes chiral symmetry breaking terms proportional to current quark masses. They introduce a monopole form factor F(k²) = Λ²/(Λ² − k²) at each quark-meson vertex and compute quark-loop contributions to the π₃-π₈ inverse propagator matrix, consisting of two-vertex loops (from the pseudovector coupling, giving q²-dependent contributions) and tadpole loops (from the symmetry-breaking terms, giving q²-independent contributions). The form factor cutoff Λ is fixed by requiring the tadpole contributions to reproduce the leading-order pseudoscalar mass relations. The resulting mixing angle θ(q²) is expanded to O(q²) and compared to the one-loop chiral perturbation theory result of Maltman (1992).

## Key numbers
- The chiral perturbation theory slope parameter c₁ (defined by θ(q²) = θ(0)[1 + c₁q²]) is 0.279 GeV⁻².
- For constituent quark mass m = 202.5 MeV and δm_s^{con} = 200 MeV, the model gives c₁ = 0.234 ± 0.026 GeV⁻².
- For m = 220 MeV and δm_s^{con} = 200 MeV, the model gives c₁ = 0.222 ± 0.025 GeV⁻².
- Varying δm_s^{con} between 175 and 225 MeV changes c₁ by approximately 5-10%.
- The constituent quark mass m = 220 MeV and δm_s^{con} = 200 MeV are taken from the meson and baryon analyses of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Capstick and Isgur (1986).
- The π⁰-η mixing matrix element from chiral perturbation theory varies by approximately 20% over the range from q² ≈ m_η² to q² ≈ −m_η².

## Context
This work extends the quark-loop modelling approach of Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1991) for ρ⁰-ω mixing to the pseudoscalar sector, where a reliable chiral perturbation theory benchmark exists from Maltman (1992). The one-loop chiral perturbation theory result shows that π⁰-η mixing is q²-dependent, contrary to the common assumption of q²-independence used in standard evaluations of charge symmetry breaking in few-nucleon systems (reviewed by Miller, Nefkens, and Slaus, 1990). The success of the model in reproducing the known pseudoscalar off-shell behavior supports the contention that the ρ⁰-ω mixing matrix element may also have significant q²-dependence, which would require re-evaluation of meson-mixing contributions to observables such as the difference of neutron and proton scattering lengths and the non-Coulombic A=3 binding energy difference. The authors note that the GHT calculation addressed only the off-diagonal inverse propagator element, not the full propagator mixing element needed for few-body calculations, and that the present work suggests a full calculation could provide reliable estimates.
