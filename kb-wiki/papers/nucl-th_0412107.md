# The effects of nuclear structure on generalized parton distributions of 3He

> The effect of the nuclear medium on generalized parton distributions (GPDs) is studied for the 3He nucleus, through a realistic microscopic analysis. In Impulse Approximation, Fermi motion and binding effects, evaluated by modern potentials, are found to be larger than in the forward case and very sensitive to the details of nuclear structure at short distances.

## Key claim

Nuclear medium effects on generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of 3He are larger than in the forward (parton distribution) case and are very sensitive to the details of nuclear structure at short distances, particularly at non-zero momentum transfer and skewedness.

## Method

The author performed an impulse approximation (IA) calculation of the unpolarized quark GPD H_q for 3He using a convolution formula with a realistic non-diagonal spectral function. The spectral function was evaluated using the AV18 interaction with Coulomb repulsion, and the nucleon GPDs were modeled using the Double Distribution representation with the Δ²-dependence given by the quark contribution to the nucleon form factor. The calculation is valid for Δ² ≪ Q², M² and was performed in the nuclear Breit Frame.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear effects in the GPD for x₃ ≤ 0.7 are as large as 15% at most, for Δ² = -0.15 GeV²
- The calculation is valid in the kinematical region -Δ² ≤ 0.25 GeV², where the x-integral of the nuclear GPD gives a good description of form factor data for 3He
- Results are shown for Δ² = -0.15 GeV² and Δ² = -0.25 GeV², with ξ₃ values of 0, 0.1, and 0.2
- The forward limit ratio R_q(x,0,0) shows an EMC-like behavior, recovering well-known results

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear GPDs from the forward (parton distribution) case to the non-forward regime, where Fermi motion and binding effects are found to be larger and more sensitive to nuclear structure details. The calculation tests the accuracy of prescriptions proposed by Cano and Pire (2004) for estimating nuclear GPDs, and the obtained GPDs are being used to estimate cross-sections for future experiments. The study is motivated by the use of 3He as an effective polarized neutron target for accessing the angular momentum content of the free neutron.
