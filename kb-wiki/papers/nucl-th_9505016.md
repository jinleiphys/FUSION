# Formation of superdense hadronic matter in high energy heavy-ion collisions

> We present the detail of a newly developed relativistic transport model (ART 1.0) for high energy heavy-ion collisions. Using this model, we first study the general collision dynamics between heavy ions at the AGS energies. We then show that in central collisions there exists a large volume of sufficiently long-lived superdense hadronic matter whose local baryon and energy densities exceed the critical densities for the hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma transition. The size and lifetime of this matter are found to depend strongly on the equation of state. We also investigate the degree and time scale of thermalization as well as the radial flow during the expansion of the superdense hadronic matter. The flow velocity profile and the temperature of the hadronic matter at freeze-out are extracted. The transverse momentum and rapidity distributions of protons, pions and kaons calculated with and without the mean field are compared with each other and also with the preliminary data from the E866/E802 collaboration to search for experimental observables that are sensitive to the equation of state. It is found that these inclusive, single particle observables depend weakly on the equation of state. The difference between results obtained with and without the nuclear mean field is only about 20\%. The baryon transverse collective flow in the reaction plane is also analyzed. It is shown that both the flow parameter and the strength of the ``bounce-off'' effect are very sensitive to the equation of state. In particular, a soft equation of state with a compressibility of 200 MeV results in an increase of the flow parameter by a factor of 2.5 compared to the cascade case without the mean field. This large effect makes it possible to distinguish the predictions from different theoretical models and to detect the signatures

## Key claim

The paper claims that in central heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies, a large volume of sufficiently long-lived superdense hadronic matter is formed whose local baryon and energy densities exceed the critical densities for the hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma transition, but this conclusion depends crucially on the equation of state used and the size and lifetime of this matter are found to depend strongly on the equation of state.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic transport model (ART 1.0) based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) framework, incorporating baryons (N, Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535), Lambda, Sigma) and mesons (pi, rho, omega, eta, K) with explicit isospin degrees of freedom. They modeled inelastic hadron-hadron collisions through resonance formation and decay, using empirical cross sections from experimental data with double-logarithmic interpolations, and treated both elastic and inelastic collisions among these particles using Monte Carlo methods with detailed balance relations for resonance production and absorption.

## Key numbers

- Baryon and energy densities up to 10 times that in normal nuclei are reached in central collisions at AGS energies
- The difference between results obtained with and without the nuclear mean field for inclusive single particle observables is only about 20%
- A soft equation of state with a compressibility of 200 MeV results in an increase of the flow parameter by a factor of 2.5 compared to the cascade case without the mean field
- The maximum cross section for the process NN to Delta Delta rho at (sqrt(s))_NN = 4.9 GeV is about 3.0 mb
- For pion-nucleon interactions, sigma_max(pi+ + p to Delta++) = sigma_max(pi- + n to Delta-) = 190 mb
- For eta + p to N*+(1535), sigma_max = 74 mb
- The threshold energies for kaon production are 2.56, 2.74, 2.63 and 2.77 GeV for the four final states N Lambda K, Delta Lambda K, N Sigma K and Delta Sigma K, respectively

## Context

This work extends the BUU model (Bertsch and Das Gupta) to higher energies by including more baryon and meson resonances and their interactions, building upon earlier transport models such as RQMD, ARC, and QGSM that have been used to study AGS energy heavy-ion collisions. The model enables systematic studies of superdense hadronic matter formation and its properties, and allows comparison with experimental data from the E866/E802 collaboration and future measurements from the EOS collaboration, with the goal of identifying observables sensitive to the equation of state that could help detect signatures of quark-gluon plasma formation.
