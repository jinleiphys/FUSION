# The Local Potential Approximation for the Brueckner G-matrix

> The Brueckner G-matrix for a slab of nuclear matter is analyzed in the singlet $^1S$ and triplet $^3S+^3D$ channels. The complete Hilbert space is split into two domains, the model subspace $S_0$, in which the two-particle propagator is calculated explicitly, and the complementary one, $S'$, in which the local potential approximation is used. This kind of local approximation was previously found to be quite accurate for the $^1S$ pairing problem. A set of model spaces $S_0(E_0)$ with different values of the cut-off energy $E_0$ is considered, $E_0$ being the upper limit for the single-particle energies of the states belonging to $S_0$. The independence of the G-matrix of $E_0$ is assumed as a criterion of validity of the local potential approximation. Such independence is obtained within few percent for $E_0=10 ÷20$ MeV for both the channels under consideration.

## Key claim

The Local Potential Approximation (LPA) for the Brueckner G-matrix in a slab of nuclear matter is accurate to within a few percent for both the singlet \(^1S\) and triplet \(^3S+^3D\) channels, provided the cut-off energy \(E_0\) defining the model subspace is set to \(10 \div 20\) MeV; the abstract's claim of "few percent" independence from \(E_0\) is conditional on this specific range and on the choice of \(P_{\perp}=0\) as the most stringent test case.

## Method

The Bethe-Goldstone (BG) equation for the G-matrix was solved for a nuclear-matter slab of thickness \(2L=16\) fm placed in a one-dimensional Saxon-Woods potential (depth \(V_0=50\) MeV, diffuseness \(d=0.65\) fm). The complete Hilbert space was split into a model subspace \(S_0(E_0)\), containing all two-particle states with single-particle energies below a cut-off \(E_0\), where the two-particle propagator was calculated explicitly, and a complementary subspace \(S'\) where the LPA was used, replacing the exact BG propagator with that of infinite nuclear matter in a constant potential well \(V(X)\) at the average center-of-mass coordinate \(X\). The separable version of the Paris NN-potential was employed, and the G-matrix was computed for \(E_0 = 0, 10, 20\) MeV at a chemical potential \(\mu = -8\) MeV and total perpendicular momentum \(P_{\perp}=0\).

## Key numbers

- The slab thickness is \(2L = 8\) fm (half-thickness \(L=8\) fm), with a Saxon-Woods potential depth \(V_0 = 50\) MeV and diffuseness \(d = 0.65\) fm.
- The chemical potential used is \(\mu = -8\) MeV, corresponding to a total energy \(E = 2\mu = -16\) MeV.
- The cut-off energies tested are \(E_0 = 0, 10, 20\) MeV; the LPA is found valid for \(E_0 = 10 \div 20\) MeV in both channels.
- For the singlet channel, the strengths of the "big" components of the separable potential (in MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\)) are: \(\lambda_{11} = -3.659 \times 10^3\), \(\lambda_{12} = 2.169 \times 10^3\), \(\lambda_{22} = -1.485 \times 10^3\).
- For the triplet channel, the strengths (in MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\)) include: \(\lambda_{11} = -1.618 \times 10^3\), \(\lambda_{12} = -1.296 \times 10^3\), \(\lambda_{22} = 7.848 \times 10^2\), \(\lambda_{33} = -7.450 \times 10^2\), \(\lambda_{44} = 1.865 \times 10^3\).
- The zero moments of the G-matrix are compared using a cut-off \(t_c = 3\) fm for the Fermi-averaged G-matrix.

## Context

This work extends the LPA, previously developed for the effective pairing interaction in the \(^1S\) channel (Baldo et al., 2000), to the more complex problem of the Brueckner G-matrix, including the coupled triplet \(^3S+^3D\) channel. It addresses the need for ab initio calculations of nuclear properties beyond the local density approximation (LDA), which fails at the nuclear surface. The results enable microscopic evaluation of the Landau-Migdal amplitude for finite nuclear systems, with the LPA providing a computationally efficient alternative to direct solution of the BG equation, which is "very difficult" for the triplet channel (Baldo et al., 2000).
