# The nucleon as a relativistic quark-diquark bound state with an exchange potential

> Treating the quark and diquark as elementary particles, the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nucleon is solved numerically. The dependence of the mass on the diquark mass and on the coupling constants is investigated. The resulting relativistic quark-diquark-nucleon vertex is presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The nucleon mass can be reproduced in a relativistic quark-diquark Bethe-Salpeter calculation with a propagating quark exchange potential, but the result depends on the model parameters (diquark masses and coupling constants) and the nucleon mass is reached for a scalar diquark mass of about 0.6 GeV only for the specific parameter set chosen.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the quark-diquark system was solved numerically with a propagating quark exchange potential, including both scalar and vector diquark channels. The coupled integral equations for the eight scalar vertex functions (two for scalar diquarks, six for vector diquarks) were solved on a grid by repeated application of the BSE kernel after performing a Wick rotation to avoid propagator poles. The vertex functions were normalized using the Bethe-Salpeter norm condition, which was verified by checking that the resulting proton and neutron charges equal 1 and 0 respectively.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass fixed at m_a = 0.45 GeV, with vector diquark mass set to m_v = m_s + 0.2 GeV to reproduce the Delta-N mass difference
- Euclidean cutoff taken as Lambda = 0.9 GeV
- Nucleon mass reached for scalar diquark mass m_s approximately 0.6 GeV (with g_s=2.17, g_v=1.64)
- Norm components: N_qS = 0.80, N_dS = 0.76, N_qV = 0.17, N_dV = 0.14
- Charges carried by scalar and vector diquark channels in the proton: 0.79 and 0.20 respectively, compared to 0.5 each in the non-relativistic SU(2) limit

## Context

This work extends earlier Bethe-Salpeter calculations for the quark-diquark system that used a local (static) quark-diquark interaction (BAR92) by including a propagating quark exchange potential. It also goes beyond the work of BCP89 by including vector diquarks, and complements the three-quark calculation of Ishii, Bentz and Yazaki (1993) by providing properly normalized vertex functions needed for form factor and structure function calculations. The results enable future calculations of nucleon form factors and structure functions using the relativistic quark-diquark-nucleon vertex.
