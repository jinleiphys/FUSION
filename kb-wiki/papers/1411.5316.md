# Thermal evolution of neutron stars with global and local neutrality

> Globally neutral neutron stars, obtained from the solution of the called Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi equations that account for all the fundamental interactions, have been recently introduced. These configurations have a more general character than the ones obtained with the traditional Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff, which impose the condition of local charge neutrality. The resulting configurations have a less massive and thinner crust, leading to a new mass-radius relation. Signatures of this new structure of the neutron star on the thermal evolution might be a potential test for this theory. We compute the cooling curves by integrating numerically the energy balance and transport equations in general relativity, for globally neutral neutron stars with crusts of different masses and sizes, according to this theory for different core-crust transition interfaces. We compare and contrast our study with known results for local charge neutrality. We found a new behavior for the relaxation time, depending upon the density at the base of the crust, $ρ_{\rm crust}$. In particular, we find that the traditional increase of the relaxation time with the crust thickness holds only for configurations whose density of the base of the crust is greater than $\approx 5\times 10^{13}$ g cm$^{-3}$. The reason for this is that neutron star crusts with very thin or absent inner crust have some neutrino emission process blocked which keep the crust hotter for longer times. Therefore, accurate observations of the thermal relaxation phase of neutron stars might give crucial information on the core-crust transition which may aid us in probing the inner composition/structure of these objects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the traditional proportionality between thermal relaxation time and crust thickness holds only for configurations whose density at the base of the crust is greater than approximately \(5\times 10^{13}\) g cm\(^{-3}\); for lower densities the opposite behavior occurs, with thinner crusts producing longer relaxation times due to reduced crustal neutrino emission.

## Method

The authors solved the general relativistic energy balance and energy transport equations numerically for neutron star models with fixed gravitational mass (\(M\approx 1.4 M_\odot\)) but different values of the density at the base of the crust, \(\rho_{\rm crust}\). These models were constructed using the Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi (EMTF) equations with the NL3 parameterization of the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) nuclear model, which yields globally neutral configurations with a positively charged core separated from the crust by a thin electron layer. The thermal evolution was computed including direct and modified Urca processes, Bremsstrahlung, plasmon decay, and electron-positron pair annihilation, with pairing effects initially turned off and later included in a conservative scheme.

## Key numbers

* The critical density separating two regimes of relaxation time behavior is \(\rho_{\rm crust} \approx 5\times 10^{13}\) g cm\(^{-3}\); above this value the traditional increase of relaxation time with crust thickness holds, below it the opposite occurs.
* The neutron drip density is \(\rho_{\rm drip}\approx 4.3\times 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\); configurations with \(\rho_{\rm crust} \leq \rho_{\rm drip}\) possess only an outer crust.
* The nuclear saturation density is \(\rho_{\rm nuc}\approx 2.7\times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\); the core-crust transition occurs at this density in the globally neutral model.
* The configuration with \(\rho_{\rm crust}=2.4\times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) approximates the structure of a locally neutral (TOV) neutron star.
* The thickness of the electron screening layer at the core-crust interface is \(\Delta r \sim\) a few hundred fm, with the electric field reaching \(E \sim (m_\pi/m_e) E_c\) where \(E_c = m_e^2 c^3/(e \hbar)\) is the critical field for vacuum polarization.

## Context

This work extends the globally neutral neutron star models developed by Rotondo et al. (2011), Rueda et al. (2011), and Belvedere et al. (2012), which solve the Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi equations and produce a new mass-radius relation with thinner, less massive crusts than traditional Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) solutions. The paper contrasts its results with the locally neutral cooling calculations of Lattimer et al. (1994), who found \(t_w \propto \Delta R_{\rm crust}^{1.4-1.8}\). The new finding that relaxation time can increase for thinner crusts when \(\rho_{\rm crust} < 5\times 10^{13}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) provides a potential observational test: accurate measurements of the thermal relaxation phase could constrain the core-crust transition density and probe the inner structure of neutron stars. The authors note that the case \(\rho_{\rm crust} = \rho_{\rm drip}\) resembles quark star models with an ultra-high electric field between the core and a thin crust.
