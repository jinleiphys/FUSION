# Backward Elastic p3He Scattering at Energies 1 - 2 GeV

> The two-body transfer amplitude for the rearrangement process i+(jkl) - j+(ikl) is constructed on the basis of technique of 4-dimensional covariant nonrelativistic graphs. The developed formalism is applied to describing backward elastic $p^3He$ scattering in the energy range 0.5 - 1.7 GeV. Numerical calculations performed using the 5- channel wave function of the $^3He$ nucleus show that the transfer of a noninteracting np- pair dominates and explains satisfactorily the energy and angular dependence of the differential cross section at energies 0.9 - 1.7$ GeV. A weak sensitivity to high momentum components of the $~^3He$ wave function in spite of large momentum transfer as well as a very important role of rescatterings in the initial and final states are established.

# Backward Elastic p³He Scattering at Energies 1–2 GeV

## Key claim

The dominant mechanism for backward elastic p³He scattering at 0.9–1.7 GeV is sequential transfer of a noninteracting np-pair, not deuteron or singlet-deuteron exchange as previously assumed; this mechanism shows weak sensitivity to high-momentum components of the ³He wave function despite the large momentum transfer of 2–3 GeV/c, contradicting earlier expectations that the process would probe short-range nucleon–nucleon correlations.

## Method

The authors construct the two-nucleon transfer amplitude for the rearrangement process i+(jkl) → j+(ikl) using the technique of 4-dimensional covariant nonrelativistic Feynman graphs. Numerical calculations employ a 5-channel Faddeev wave function of ³He from Kim and Haftel–Gari–Sauer (RSC NN-potential in ¹S₀ and ³S₁–³D₁ states). The sequential transfer (ST), interacting pair transfer (IPT), and nonsequential transfer (NST) amplitudes are evaluated, and initial- and final-state rescatterings are included via the Glauber–Sitenko diffraction multistep theory.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer in the reaction: Δ = 2–3 GeV/c at energies 0.5–1.5 GeV.
- At Tₚ = 1–1.7 GeV, the sequential transfer contribution exceeds the experimental data by a factor of ~30–40 and is 3–4 orders of magnitude larger than the IPT and NST contributions.
- At θ_c.m. = 180°, the suppression factor from rescatterings reduces the cross section to agree with data over Tₚ = 0.7–1.7 GeV.
- The momenta |Q₁| and |Q₄| in the kinematic region Tₚ = 0.7–1.7 GeV are ~0.5 GeV/c.
- The suppression factor from rescatterings for the analogous one-nucleon exchange in pd → dp is about 2–3 (Uzikov, 1997).

## Context

This work extends earlier two-body approximations for p³He backward scattering (Barry, 1972; Kobyushkin, 1975) that used deuteron and singlet-deuteron exchange and neglected initial/final state interactions. The authors show that those simple exchange mechanisms are negligible compared to sequential transfer, and that the cross section at 180° measures the weight of the antisymmetrized {NN(¹S₀)+N} component of the ³He wave function (channel ν=1 in the Haftel–Gari–Sauer classification). This enables future experimental investigation of spin observables at Tₚ > 1 GeV to probe the spin structure of ³He, which Faddeev calculations (Sauer, 1993) predict is dominated by the neutron through this channel.
