# Hoyle state and rotational features in Carbon-12 within a no-core shell model framework

> By using only a fraction of the model space extended beyond current no-core shell-model limits and a schematic effective many-nucleon interaction, we gain additional insight within a symmetry-guided shell-model framework, into the many-body dynamics that gives rise to the ground state rotational band together with phenomena tied to alpha-clustering substructures in the low-lying states in C-12, and in particular, the challenging Hoyle state and its first 2+ excitation. For these states, we offer a novel perspective emerging out of no-core shell-model considerations, including a discussion of associated nuclear shapes and matter radii. This, in turn, provides guidance for ab initio shell models by informing key features of nuclear structure and the interaction.

# Hoyle state and rotational features in Carbon-12 within a no-core shell-model framework

## Key claim
The Hoyle state and its rotational excitations in Carbon-12 can be described within a no-core shell-model framework using only a fraction of the model space and a schematic many-nucleon interaction with a single adjustable parameter, provided the model space extends to at least Nmax=20. The authors emphasize that this is a guidance study for ab initio approaches, not a full ab initio calculation, and that short-range and tensor forces are excluded from the present analysis.

## Method
The authors employ the no-core symplectic model (NCSpM) with Sp(3,R) symmetry, using a Hamiltonian that includes the harmonic oscillator kinetic energy, a long-range quadrupole-quadrupole interaction with an exponential form containing a single adjustable parameter gamma, and a spin-orbit term. The model space is reduced by selecting the most relevant symplectic irreducible representations from a full Nmax=20 space, yielding a total dimensionality of 6.6 x 10^3 basis states. Calculations are performed for Carbon-12 with empirical estimates of hbar-Omega = 18 MeV and kappa = 3.8 MeV, with the coupling constant chi determined by a self-consistent relation.

## Key numbers
- Model space truncated at Nmax=20, reduced to 6.6 x 10^3 basis states by selecting relevant symplectic irreps
- Single adjustable parameter gamma = 1.7 x 10^-4
- Matter rms radius for ground state: 2.43(1) fm (experimental: 2.43(2) fm)
- Matter rms radius for Hoyle state: 2.93(5) fm (experimentally deduced: 2.89(4) fm)
- Electric quadrupole moment for 2_1^+ state: +5.9(1) e fm^2 (experimental: +6(3) e fm^2)
- Electric quadrupole moment for 2^+ above Hoyle state: -21(1) e fm^2
- Hoyle state radius is 1.2 times larger than ground state radius
- Interband B(E2; 0_2^+ -> 2_1^+) = 8.4 W.u. from 2% mixing (experimental: 8.0(11) W.u.)
- Interband M(E0; 0_2^+ -> 0_1^+) = 2.1 e fm^2 from 2% mixing (experimental: 5.4(2) e fm^2)
- Hoyle state wavefunction peaks around n=8 harmonic oscillator excitations with dominant (16,0) deformation

## Context
This work extends the no-core shell-model framework beyond current Nmax limits (which previously reached only Nmax=8 for Carbon-12, as in Roth et al. 2011) to describe both the ground state rotational band and the cluster-like Hoyle state within a single microscopic framework. The study builds on the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott 1958) and the symplectic Sp(3,R) model of Rosensteel and Rowe (1977), and provides guidance for ab initio approaches such as lattice effective field theory (Epelbaum et al. 2011) and the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) by identifying the dominant deformation configurations and the need for large model spaces. The results enable future ab initio calculations by showing that the Hoyle state requires model spaces extending to Nmax=18-20 and that a 4-particle-4-hole configuration with prolate deformation underlies its structure.
