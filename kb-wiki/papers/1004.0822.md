# Hot and dense hadronic matter in an effective mean field approach

> We investigate the equation of state of hadronic matter at finite values of baryon density and temperature reachable in high energy heavy ion collisions. The analysis is performed by requiring the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of baryon number, electric charge fraction and zero net strangeness. We consider an effective relativistic mean-field model with the inclusion of Delta-isobars, hyperons and lightest pseudoscalar and vector mesons degrees of freedom. In this context, we study the influence of the Delta-isobars degrees of freedom in the hadronic equation of state and, in connection, the behavior of different particle-antiparticle ratios and strangeness production.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how the inclusion of Delta-isobar degrees of freedom modifies the hadronic equation of state at finite baryon density and temperature, under the condition that the system globally conserves baryon number, electric charge fraction, and zero net strangeness. The authors qualify that the existence of stable Delta-excited nuclear matter remains controversial and depends sensitively on the choice of Delta-meson coupling constants, which are constrained within a triangle relation from QCD sum rules.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model including the full baryon octet (p, n, Lambda, Sigma, Xi), the Delta-isobar quartet, and the lightest pseudoscalar and vector mesons treated as a quasi-free Bose gas with effective chemical potentials derived from the self-consistent baryon-meson interactions. The meson fields (sigma, omega, rho, delta) are treated in the mean-field approximation with nonlinear self-interactions for sigma and omega mesons, and the system is solved self-consistently under Gibbs conditions for global conservation of baryon number, electric charge fraction, and zero net strangeness at fixed temperature and baryon density. Three parameter sets (GM3, NLrho-delta, TM1) are used, with hyperon-meson couplings fixed from hypernuclear data and SU(6) quark model relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: rho_0 approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}
- For GM3 parameter set: saturation density 0.153 fm^{-3}, compression modulus K = 240 MeV, effective nucleon mass ratio M_N*/M_N = 0.78, symmetry energy a_sym = 32.5 MeV
- For NLrho-delta parameter set: saturation density 0.160 fm^{-3}, K = 240 MeV, M_N*/M_N = 0.75, a_sym = 30.5 MeV
- For TM1 parameter set: saturation density 0.145 fm^{-3}, K = 281 MeV, M_N*/M_N = 0.63, a_sym = 36.9 MeV
- Hyperon potential depths: U_Lambda^N = -28 MeV, U_Sigma^N = +30 MeV, U_Xi^N = -18 MeV
- For GM3 with r_v=1: second minimum in energy per baryon appears at r_s = 1.41, maximum allowed r_s = 1.50
- For NLrho-delta with r_v=1: second minimum at r_s = 1.32, maximum r_s = 1.41
- For TM1 with r_v=1: second minimum at r_s = 1.27, maximum r_s = 1.33
- Position of the second minimum in energy per baryon appears at approximately rho_B approximately 3 to 4 times rho_0 for temperatures up to about 80 to 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies of dense hadronic matter by Glendenning (1982) and Greiner (1987, 1997) by systematically including Delta-isobars alongside hyperons and mesons under finite temperature and asymmetry conditions relevant to heavy ion collisions. The analysis uses parameter sets (GM3 from Glendenning and Moszkowski (1991), NLrho-delta from Di Toro (2002), TM1 from Toki) that are compatible with experimental flow data constraints from Danielewicz (2002) and are widely used in neutron star astrophysics. The results enable future comparisons with data from the CBM experiment at FAIR and low-energy RHIC scans, and provide a baseline for studying the deconfinement phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at high density and temperature.
