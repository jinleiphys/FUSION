# Re-Hardening of Hadron Transverse Mass Spectra in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We analyze the spectra of pions and protons in heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies from 2 A GeV to 65+65 A GeV by using a jet-implemented hadron-string cascade model. In this energy region, hadron transverse mass spectra first show softening until SPS energies, and re-hardening may emerge at RHIC energies. Since hadronic matter is expected to show only softening at higher energy densities, this re-hardening of spectra can be interpreted as a good signature of the quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Key claim

The re-hardening of hadron transverse mass spectra, which is observed as a rapid increase in the radial flow velocity at RHIC energies after a softening between AGS and SPS energies, can be interpreted as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, but the authors hedge that this interpretation is based on a hydrodynamical picture and that confirmation and further studies are necessary to conclude firmly.

## Method

The authors used the jet-implemented hadron-string cascade model JAM (version 1.009.27, with default parameters set before RHIC experiments began) to simulate gold-on-gold reactions at SIS (2 A GeV), AGS (10.6 A GeV), JHF (25 A GeV), and RHIC (sqrt(s) = 56 and 130 A GeV) energies, and lead-on-lead reactions at SPS (158 A GeV) energy. The impact parameter range was limited to 0 < b < 3.3 fm, corresponding to central 350 mb collisions, and more than 1000 events were generated at each incident energy. The transverse mass spectra of pions and protons at mid-rapidity were fitted with a single exponential over several kinetic energy ranges to extract inverse slope parameters, which were then separated into temperature and radial flow velocity using the non-relativistic relation T' = T + m beta_t^2/2.

## Key numbers

*   The calculated ratio of pseudorapidity density of antiprotons to protons at eta=0 at RHIC sqrt(s)=130 A GeV is 0.63, which agrees with the experimental data of 0.61 +/- 0.06 (stat.) +/- 0.4 (syst.) from the BRAHMS collaboration.
*   The charged particle pseudorapidity distribution at mid-rapidity at RHIC sqrt(s)=130 A GeV is dN/deta|eta=0 approximately 570 for central collisions, which is well reproduced by the model.
*   The inverse slope parameter of protons stays almost constant between AGS and RHIC energies at sqrt(s)=56 A GeV, and rapidly grows at RHIC energy of sqrt(s)=130 A GeV.
*   The radial flow velocity first grows at low energies, subsequently saturates, then decreases between AGS and SPS energies, and increases again at RHIC energies.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hadron spectra softening observed at SIS-AGS-SPS energies, which had been attributed to hadronic degrees of freedom and the Hagedorn gas picture (Hagedorn, 1965), and which Sahu et al. (2000) had reproduced within a hadron-string picture. The paper directly addresses the question of whether the re-hardening seen in preliminary RHIC data (Ohnishi, 2000; Xu, 2001) can be explained by hadronic scenarios or requires QGP formation, and it contrasts its approach with the hydrodynamical model of Bass and Dumitru (2000) by including explicit parton dynamics via mini-jet production. The results suggest that the excitation function between SPS and RHIC energies should be useful to obtain the signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.
