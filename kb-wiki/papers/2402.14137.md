# Finite-energy sum rules at finite chemical potential and zero temperature

> In this article we explore the effect of chemical potential at zero temperature in the implementation of in-medium effects in the perturbative sector in finite energy sum rules. For this purpose, we explore the axial, axial-pseudoscalar and pseudoscalar current correlators involving charged pions. The inclusion of non-normal ordered condensates with chemical potential effects in the operator mixing is considered. As a result, the contribution of the operator mixing with chemical potential dependence cancels all the explicit chemical potential contribution of the perturbative sector, aligned with the so-called "silver blaze problem". We find an abrupt transition when $μ=\sqrt{s_0}/2$, with $s_0$ representing the hadronic continuum threshold. Exploring beyond this critical chemical potential we found similarities with low-energy effective meson models at high chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that when operator mixing from non-normal ordered condensates is included with explicit chemical potential dependence, the resulting contribution cancels all explicit chemical potential dependence in the perturbative sector of finite-energy sum rules at zero temperature, consistent with the "silver blaze problem", but only up to a critical chemical potential \(\mu = \sqrt{s_0}/2\), beyond which an abrupt transition occurs.

## Method

The authors study axial-vector, axial-pseudoscalar, and pseudoscalar current correlators involving charged pions using finite-energy sum rules (FESR) without radiative corrections, up to dimension-4 operators in the operator product expansion (OPE). They incorporate non-normal ordered condensates with chemical potential effects in the operator mixing, using a contour integration in the complex \(p_0\)-plane rather than the usual \(p^2\)-plane, and consider the same chemical potential for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks in the isospin symmetric mass approximation.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential is \(\mu_c = \sqrt{s_0}/2\), where \(s_0\) is the hadronic continuum threshold.
- In vacuum, with pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 0.092\) GeV as inputs, the axial hadronic threshold is \(\sqrt{s_A} = 0.82\) GeV.
- The pseudoscalar hadronic threshold is \(\sqrt{s_P} = 1.24\) GeV, giving a subtraction scale \(\nu = 1.03\) GeV.
- The axial-vector hadronic threshold \(s_A\) is obtained directly from the relation \(2f_\pi^2 = s_A/(4\pi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-energy sum rules framework of Cohen (1991), Furnstahl (1992), and Jin (1992-1994) to the unexplored regime of zero temperature and finite chemical potential, where previous studies introduced baryon density effects only through condensates. The use of non-normal ordered condensates follows the approach of Generalis (1990), Jamin (1993), and Zschocke (2011) to resolve infrared divergences in the chiral limit. The results enable future studies of in-medium hadron properties at finite density and suggest connections to low-energy effective meson models at high chemical potential beyond the critical point.
