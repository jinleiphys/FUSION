# Structural properties and decay modes of Z $=$ 122, 120 and 118 superheavy nuclei

> Structural properties and the decay modes of the superheavy elements Z $=$ 122, 120, 118 are studied in a microscopic framework. We evaluate the binding energy, one- and two- proton and neutron separation energy, shell correction and density profile of even and odd isotopes of Z $=$ 122, 120, 118 (284 $\leq$ A $\leq$ 352) which show a reasonable match with FRDM results and the available experimental data. Equillibrium shape and deformation of the superheavy region are predicted. We investigate the possible decay modes of this region specifically $α$-decay, spontaneous fission (SF) and the $β$-decay and evaluate the probable $α$-decay chains. The phenomena of bubble like structure in the charge density is predicted in $^{330}$122, $^{292,328}$120 and $^{326}$118 with significant depletion fraction around 20-24$\%$ which increases with increasing Coulomb energy and diminishes with increasing isospin (N$-$Z) values exhibiting the fact that the coloumb forces are the main driving force in the central depletion in superheavy systems.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that bubble-like structures in charge density (central depletion) occur in specific superheavy nuclei (³³⁰122, ²⁹²,³²⁸120, and ³²⁶118) with depletion fractions around 20-24%, and that this depletion increases with Coulomb energy but decreases with increasing isospin (N−Z), indicating Coulomb forces as the main driver of central depletion in superheavy systems.

## Method

The authors used two complementary theoretical frameworks: (i) a Relativistic Mean-Field plus state-dependent BCS (RMF+BCS) approach with TMA parametrization, solved via expansion on an axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis with quadrupole constrained calculations; and (ii) a Macroscopic-Microscopic approach using the triaxially deformed Nilsson-Strutinsky method (NSM) to obtain shell corrections, deformations, and shapes. Binding energies, separation energies, shell corrections, density profiles, and decay half-lives (α-decay via the modified Royer formula, spontaneous fission via the Xu formula, and β-decay via the Fiset-Nix formula) were computed for even and odd isotopes of Z = 122, 120, and 118 in the mass range 284 ≤ A ≤ 352.

## Key numbers

*   The last 2p-bound nuclei (proton drip line) predicted by RMF are ²⁹⁸122, ²⁹⁰120, and ²⁸⁴118; the last 1p-bound nuclei are ²⁹⁶122, ²⁸⁷120, and ²⁸²118.
*   Shell correction minima of approximately −5 MeV and −10 MeV are found at neutron numbers N = 164 and N = 228, respectively, indicating magicity.
*   Depletion fractions (DF) for charge density range from about 24.52% (for N=205 in Z=122) down to about 20.69% (for N=227 in Z=118), with specific nuclei ³³⁰122, ²⁹²,³²⁸120, and ³²⁶118 showing significant depletion.
*   For the decay chain of ²⁹⁴118, the experimental Qα is 11.84 MeV, while RMF gives 11.15 MeV and Mac-Mic gives 13.63 MeV; the experimental log₁₀T₁/₂ is −3.16 s, while RMF gives −0.80 s and Mac-Mic gives −5.96 s.
*   α-decay is the dominant decay mode for A ≤ 314 in Z=122, A ≤ 305 in Z=120, and A ≤ 299 in Z=118; beyond these mass numbers, spontaneous fission becomes more favorable.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on Z=121 superheavy nuclei (Saxena, 2018) and provides systematic predictions for Z=122, 120, and 118 isotopes, complementing experimental efforts at GSI, RIKEN, and JINR. The predicted bubble structures and shell closures at N=164 and N=228 (consistent with earlier theoretical works by Zhang (2004) and Patra (1999)) offer guidance for future fusion-evaporation experiments, particularly for candidate nuclei like ²⁹²⁻³⁰²122, ²⁹⁰⁻³⁰⁰120, and ²⁸⁸⁻²⁹⁸118 which are identified as having long α-decay chains and high binding energy per nucleon.
