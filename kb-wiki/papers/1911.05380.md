# Extracting Skyrme energy density functional parameters with Heavy-Ion Collision Data

> The effective Skyrme energy density functionals are widely used in the study of nuclear structure, nuclear reaction and neutron star, but they are less established from the heavy ion collision data. In this work, we find 22 effective Skyrme parameter sets, when incorporated in use the transport model, ImQMD, to describe the heavy ion collision data, such as isospin diffusion data at 35 MeV/u and 50 MeV/u. We use these sets to calculate the neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb based on the restricted density variation method, and obtain the neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb in the range of $δR_{np}=0.18\pm0.04$ fm.

## Key claim

The authors find 22 effective Skyrme parameter sets that can reproduce isospin diffusion data at 35 MeV/u and 50 MeV/u within experimental errors, but note that the isospin diffusion data are not sensitive to the isoscalar effective mass or the sign of the isovector effective mass splitting.

## Method

The authors use the ImQMD-Sky transport model with nuclear matter parameters (incompressibility K0, symmetry energy S0, slope L, isoscalar effective mass m_s*, and isovector effective mass parameter f_I) as independent inputs, removing prior correlations between them. They sample 120 points in a 5-dimensional parameter space using Latin Hypercube Sampling, perform transport model calculations for isospin diffusion in 112,124Sn+112,124Sn reactions at 35 MeV/u and 50 MeV/u, and select the 22 parameter sets that reproduce the experimental isospin transport ratio data within errors. The neutron skin of 208Pb is then calculated for these 22 sets using the restricted density variational method with spherical symmetric Fermi function density distributions.

## Key numbers

- 22 effective Skyrme parameter sets reproduce isospin diffusion data at 35 MeV/u and 50 MeV/u within experimental errors
- Neutron skin of 208Pb from the 22 parameter sets: delta R_np = 0.18 +/- 0.04 fm
- Parameter ranges sampled: K0 in [200,280] MeV, S0 in [25,35] MeV, L in [30,120] MeV, m_s*/m0 in [0.6,1.0], f_I in [-0.5,0.4]
- The slope parameter L from the 22 sets distributes from 30 to about 100 MeV
- The correlation between S0 and L gives partial derivative dS0/dL = 0.061 with standard error 0.022, corresponding to sensitive density rho_s/rho_0 = 0.685-0.946

## Context

This work extends prior constraints on the symmetry energy from heavy-ion collision data, specifically improving upon the 2009 analysis by Tsang (2009) which used isospin diffusion and double neutron-to-proton yield ratios at 50 MeV/u. The inclusion of isospin diffusion data at 35 MeV/u improves constraints at densities below about 0.13 fm^-3. The resulting symmetry energy constraints are consistent with those from nuclear structure studies such as electric dipole polarizability in 208Pb by Zhang (2014), properties of double magic nuclei by Brown (2013), and Fermi-energy differences by Wang (2013). The 22 parameter sets enable future comprehensive tests of Skyrme functionals across nuclear structure, neutron stars, and heavy-ion collisions.
