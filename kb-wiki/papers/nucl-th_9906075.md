# The physics of the centrality dependence of elliptic flow

> The centrality dependence of elliptic flow and how it is related to the physics of expansion of the system created in high energy nuclear collisions is discussed. Since in the hydro limit the centrality dependence of elliptic flow is mostly defined by the elliptic anisotropy of the overlapping region of the colliding nuclei, and in the low density limit by the product of the elliptic anisotropy and the multiplicity, we argue that the centrality dependence of elliptic flow should be a good indicator of the degree of equilibration reached in the reaction. Then we analyze experimental data obtained at AGS and SPS energies. The observed difference in the centrality dependence of elliptic flow could imply a transition from a hadronic to a partonic nature of the system evolution. Finally we exploit the multiplicity dependence of elliptic flow to make qualitative predictions for RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of elliptic flow, specifically the shape of the curve of \(v_2\) versus centrality, can serve as an indicator of the degree of equilibration (thermalization) and the hadronic versus partonic nature of the system evolution, but the authors note that the experimental data available at the time are not adequate to answer these questions convincingly.

## Method

The authors compare the centrality dependence of elliptic flow \(v_2\) (the second Fourier coefficient of the particle azimuthal distribution) to two limiting cases: the hydro limit, where flow is proportional to the initial spatial elliptic anisotropy \(\epsilon\), and the low-density limit, where flow is proportional to \(\epsilon\) times the particle density per unit transverse area. They analyze experimental data from the AGS (E877 Collaboration) and SPS (NA49 Collaboration) and also compare to calculations from the RQMD v2.3 event generator for Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon, the maximal value of the initial spatial elliptic anisotropy \(\epsilon \approx 0.44\).
- In the hydro limit, the ratio \((v_2/\epsilon)_{hydro} \approx 0.27-0.35\) from Ollitrault (1992) calculations, or \(0.21-0.23\) from Sollfrank calculations (partly due to a 15% reduction from resonances).
- The RQMD v2.3 calculation for Pb+Pb at 158 GeV per nucleon yields a normalized hydro-limit curve of \(v_2^{HYDRO} \approx 0.059 \epsilon\).
- At SPS energies, the maximal measured elliptic flow is \(v_2 \leq 0.04\), significantly less than the hydro prediction of about 0.09–0.1.
- The authors estimate that at RHIC and LHC, the rapidity densities will be higher than at SPS by factors of 2 and 8, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Ollitrault (1992) on elliptic flow in the hydro limit and Heiselberg (1999) on the low-density limit, and it analyzes experimental data from the E877 Collaboration at AGS and the NA49 Collaboration at SPS. The observed difference in centrality dependence between AGS and SPS energies is interpreted as a possible signature of a transition from hadronic to partonic dynamics in central collisions at SPS. The paper enables future measurements at RHIC and LHC to test whether the system approaches the hydro limit at higher energies, and it motivates studies of lighter systems (e.g., Cu+Cu) and new SPS data at 40 GeV per nucleon to scan the onset of deconfinement.
