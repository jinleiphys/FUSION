# Extracting the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma from flow in ultra-central heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a method for extracting the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (eta/s) of the quark-gluon plasma from experimental data. We argue that uncertainty due to poor knowledge of the earliest stages of a heavy-ion collision is smallest for ultra-central events. The most precise value of eta/s can thus be obtained from a global fit to p_T-integrated Fourier harmonics of azimuthal correlations. We further outline a method for quantifying the overall uncertainty in the extracted value. Only after a comprehensive and systematic accounting of all sources of uncertainty can a reliable measurement be claimed. In these proceedings we report preliminary results; full and final results will be presented in a separate publication.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (η/s) of the quark-gluon plasma can be extracted most precisely from pT-integrated Fourier harmonics of azimuthal correlations measured in ultra-central heavy-ion collisions, because uncertainty from the earliest stages of the collision is smallest for such events. The authors report a preliminary result of 0.07 ≤ η/s ≤ 0.43, but note this is a conservative estimate that will likely be refined in a final publication.

## Method

The authors performed viscous hydrodynamic calculations with η/s as a free parameter, using multiple models of initial conditions (PHOBOS MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, Glauber discs, MCrcBK with negative binomial fluctuations, DIPSY, and UrQMD) to compute the eccentricities εn and corresponding flow harmonics vn. They then performed a least-squares fit to ATLAS data for v2 through v6 in the 0-1% most central collisions, varying all uncertain aspects of the theory to quantify the systematic uncertainty in the extracted η/s value.

## Key numbers

- Preliminary extracted range for η/s: 0.07 ≤ η/s ≤ 0.43, obtained from a simultaneous fit to v2 through v6 in 0-1% centrality collisions at the highest LHC energies
- The uncertainty in η/s due to uncertain initial anisotropy in the initial state is approximately ±0.05, as estimated from the range of best-fit viscosity values across different initial condition models
- The lower bound of 0.07 indicates that purely ideal (zero viscosity) hydrodynamic calculations cannot describe these data, which is a significant result

## Context

This work extends previous viscous hydrodynamic calculations by Luzum (2008) and Schenke (2011) that successfully reproduced flow measurements but could not place robust error bars on η/s. It addresses the piecemeal approach to uncertainty quantification criticized by Song (2008) and provides the first systematic study of all known sources of uncertainty. The method enables a reliable measurement of η/s with quantified error bars, and the authors note that the lower bound does not represent a lower bound for QCD viscosity over its entire temperature range, only an average value at the typical time when flow is built up.
