# An effective method to accurately calculate the phase space factors for $β^- β^-$ decay

> Accurate calculations of the electron phase space factors are necessary for reliable predictions of double-beta decay rates, and for the analysis of the associated electron angular and energy distributions. We present an effective method to calculate these phase space factors that takes into account the distorted Coulomb field of the daughter nucleus, yet allows one to easily calculate the phase space factors with good accuracy relative to the most exact methods available in the recent literature.

## Key claim

The authors present an effective method that modifies the point-charge formalism of Doi (1985) and Suhonen and Civitarese (1998) by introducing a constant multiplicative "screening factor" for the charge of the daughter nucleus, which replicates the effects of finite nuclear size and atomic electron screening while maintaining computational simplicity; the method achieves maximum deviations of 6.3% for s-wave phase space factors and 18.1% for p-wave phase space factors when using a single screening factor for each class.

## Method

The method replaces the actual charge of the daughter nucleus \(Z_f\) with an effective "screened charge" \(Z_s = (S_f/100) Z_f\), where \(S_f\) is a dimensionless screening factor. The phase space factors are then calculated using the standard point-charge Fermi function and kinematic integrals from Doi (1985) and Suhonen and Civitarese (1998), but with \(Z_s\) substituted for the nuclear charge. The optimal screening factor is determined by minimizing the maximum percentage deviation between the calculated phase space factors and reference values from Kotila (2012), Stoica and Mirea (2013), Mirea and Pahomi (2015), and Stefanik (2015), which use Dirac electron wave functions with finite nuclear size and atomic electron screening.

## Key numbers

- Optimal screening factor for s-wave phase space factors (\(G^{0\nu}_{01}\), \(G^{2\nu}_{g.s.}\), \(G^{2\nu}_{0_1^+}\), \(G^{2\nu}_{2_1^+}\)): \(S_f = 94.5\), yielding a maximum deviation of 6.3% across all s-wave cases.
- Optimal screening factor for p-wave phase space factors (\(G^{0\nu}_{02}\) through \(G^{0\nu}_{09}\)): \(S_f = 92.0\), yielding a maximum deviation of 18.1% across all p-wave cases.
- For \(G^{0\nu}_{01}\) of \(^{150}\)Nd, the point-charge formalism deviates by up to 40% from reference values, while the effective method with \(S_f=94.5\) gives a deviation of 4.5% (calculated value \(66.00 \times 10^{-15}\) yr\(^{-1}\) vs. reference \(63.16 \times 10^{-15}\) yr\(^{-1}\)).
- For \(G^{0\nu}_{08}\) of \(^{150}\)Nd, the point-charge formalism deviates by over 100%, while the effective method with individual screening factors reduces the deviation to 9.3% (calculated value \(7.946 \times 10^{-11}\) yr\(^{-1}\) vs. reference from Stefanik (2015)).
- For \(G^{2\nu}_{g.s.}\) of \(^{48}\)Ca, the effective method gives \(1480.46 \times 10^{-20}\) yr\(^{-1}\) with a 4.7% deviation from the reference value of \(1553.6 \times 10^{-20}\) yr\(^{-1}\) from Mirea and Pahomi (2015).

## Context

This work extends the point-charge formalism of Doi (1985) and Suhonen and Civitarese (1998) by providing a simple correction that reproduces results from more computationally intensive methods that include finite nuclear size and electron screening, such as those of Kotila (2012), Stoica and Mirea (2013), Mirea and Pahomi (2015), and Stefanik (2015). The method enables fast calculations of phase space factors for studies of electron angular and energy distributions in neutrinoless double-beta decay, which are needed for analyzing contributions from right-handed currents and other beyond-Standard-Model mechanisms, as in the work of Horoi and Neacsu (2015). The authors provide predictions for several isotopes where reference values are disputed or unavailable, and note that further improvement could come from a mass-dependent screening factor.
