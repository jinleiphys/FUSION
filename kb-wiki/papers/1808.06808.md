# Relativistic parameterizations of neutron matter and implications for neutron stars

> We construct parameter sets of the relativistic mean-field model fitted to the recent constraints on the asymmetry energy $J$ and the slope parameter $L$ for pure neutron matter. We find cases of unphysical behaviour, i.e.\ the appearance of negative pressures, for stiff parameter sets with low values of the effective mass $m^*/m$. In some cases the equation of state of pure neutron matter turns out to be outside the allowed band given by chiral effective field theory. The mass-radius relations of neutron stars for all acceptable parameter sets shows a maximum mass in excess of $2M_\odot$ being compatible with pulsar mass measurements. Given the constraints on the model in the low-density regime coming from chiral effective theory, we find that the radius of a $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star is nearly independent on the value of $L$. This is in contrast to some previous claims for a strong connection of the slope parameter with the radius of a neutron star. In fact, the mass-radius relation turns out to depend only on the isoscalar parameters of symmetric matter. The constraints of GW170817 on the tidal deformability and on the radius are also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors construct relativistic mean-field parameter sets fitted to recent constraints on the symmetry energy J and slope parameter L, and find that when the low-density regime is constrained by chiral effective field theory, the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star becomes nearly independent of the slope parameter L, contradicting previous claims of a strong connection between L and neutron star radius.

## Method

The authors calibrate relativistic mean-field model parameters by fixing isoscalar couplings to saturation properties (density 0.15 fm^-3, binding energy -16 MeV, incompressibility 240 MeV) while varying the effective nucleon mass between 0.55 and 0.75, then determine isovector couplings g_rho and Lambda_omega from chosen values of the symmetry energy J (30 or 32 MeV) and slope parameter L (40-60 MeV). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating neutron stars using the resulting equations of state for beta-stable matter, with the DD2 parameterization for the crust, and compare the neutron matter equation of state against constraints from chiral effective field theory for densities up to 1.3 times saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density taken as n_0 = 0.15 fm^-3, binding energy E/A(n_0) = -16 MeV, incompressibility K(n_0) = 240 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass varied between m^*/m = 0.55 and 0.75
- Symmetry energy J taken as 30 and 32 MeV; slope parameter L varied between 40 and 60 MeV
- All acceptable parameter sets produce maximum neutron star masses in excess of 2 solar masses
- For m^*/m = 0.60, J = 32, and L = 60, the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is 13.5 km with dimensionless tidal deformability 995
- For m^*/m = 0.75, J = 32, and L = 60, the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is 12.4 km with dimensionless tidal deformability 501
- Radii for 1.4 solar mass neutron stars across all acceptable parameter sets range from 12.1 to 13.7 km
- Maximum masses range from 2.11 solar masses (m^*/m = 0.75) to 2.90 solar masses (m^*/m = 0.55) for J = 32 and L = 60

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field modeling approach of Chen (2014) by imposing constraints from chiral effective field theory calculations by Drischler (2016) on the neutron matter equation of state at low densities. The finding that the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is nearly independent of L when constrained by chiral effective field theory contradicts earlier claims by Fattoyev (2012), Alam (2016), and others who found a strong correlation between L and neutron star radius using wider ranges of L. The results enable future comparisons with simultaneous mass and radius measurements from the NICER and eXTP missions, and the tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 provide additional restrictions on the effective nucleon mass.
