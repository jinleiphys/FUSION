# Antimatter Bounds by Anti-Asteroids annihilations on Planets and Sun

> The existence of antimatter stars in the Galaxy as possible signature for inflationary models with non-homogeneous baryo-synthesis may leave the trace by antimatter cosmic rays as well as by their secondaries (anti-planets and anti-meteorites) diffused bodies in our galactic halo. The anti-meteorite flux may leave its explosive gamma signature by colliding on lunar soil as well as on terrestrial, jovian and solar atmospheres. However the propagation in galaxy and the consequent evaporation in galactic matter gas suppress the lightest (m < 10^(-2)g) anti-meteorites. Anisotropic annihilation of larger anti-meteorites within a narrow mass window, maybe rarely deflected, bounced by the galactic gas disk, escaping detection in our solar system. Nevertheless heaviest anti-meteorites (m > 10^(-1)g up to 10^(6)g) are unable to be deflected by the thin galactic gas surface annihilation; they might hit the Sun (or rarely Jupiter) leading to an explosive gamma event and a spectacular track with a bouncing and even a propelling annihilation on cromosphere and photosphere. Their anti-nuclei annihilation in pions and their final hard gammas showering may be observabe as a "solar flare" at a rate nearly comparable to the observed ones. From their absence we may infer bounds on antimatter-matter ratio near or below 10^(-9) limit: already recorded data in BATSE catalog might be applied.

## Key claim

The absence of observed gamma-ray flashes from anti-meteorite annihilation on Earth, Moon, and the Sun allows one to infer an upper bound on the antimatter-to-matter ratio in the Galaxy near or below \(10^{-9}\), but this bound is conditional on the anti-meteorite mass being above a threshold (roughly milligram to gram scale) because lighter anti-meteorites are evaporated during galactic propagation and cannot reach the inner solar system.

## Method

The authors model the flux of galactic (extra-solar) meteorites hitting Earth using a power-law mass distribution, then scale it by an unknown antimatter-to-matter ratio \(r\). They compute the gamma-ray fluence from anti-meteorite annihilation in Earth's atmosphere, on the lunar surface, and on the Sun, and compare these predicted signals to the non-observation of such events in BATSE data. They also calculate the minimum mass of an anti-meteorite that can survive annihilation and evaporation while crossing the galactic gas disk, using the column density of the disk and the condition that the mass of gas crossed equals the anti-meteorite mass.

## Key numbers

- The flux of galactic meteorites hitting Earth is estimated as \(dN/dt = 10^6 (M/1\,\text{g})^{-1}\) events per year.
- The energy released by annihilation of an anti-meteorite of mass \(M\) is \(E = 10^{18} (M/1\,\text{mg})\) erg.
- The predicted event rate for anti-meteorite annihilation on Earth is \(dN/dt = 10^9 r (M/1\,\text{mg})^{-1}\) year\(^{-1}\).
- The gamma fluence from a milligram anti-meteorite annihilation in Earth's atmosphere is estimated as \(F \simeq 10\,\text{erg/cm}^2\).
- The minimal anti-meteorite mass that survives annihilation in the galactic disk, assuming water density, is \(M_{an} \approx 2.2 \times 10^{-4}\) g; accounting for atomic composition raises this to up to 0.3 g for anti-ice.
- The characteristic relaxation time for deceleration of a millimeter-radius anti-meteorite in galactic gas is \(\tau = 1.3 \eta^{-1} \cdot 10^{2}\) year \((r/1\,\text{mm}) (\rho_a / 1\,\text{g cm}^{-3}) (10^{-24}\,\text{g cm}^{-3} / \rho)\).
- The predicted anti-asteroid rate on the Sun is \(dN/dt = 10^{10} r (g/M)\) year\(^{-1}\), and for \(r = 10^{-9}\) this gives 10 events per year.
- The fluence at Earth from a solar anti-asteroid annihilation is \(3 \times 10^{-7}\) erg/cm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the search for antimatter in the Galaxy beyond cosmic-ray anti-nuclei (targeted by the AMS-II experiment) to macroscopic anti-meteorites, following the hypothesis of antimatter stars in non-homogeneous baryosynthesis models (Dolgov 1992, Khlopov 1998). It provides a new observational test using existing gamma-ray data (BATSE catalog) and suggests that the absence of observed gamma flashes from anti-meteorite annihilation already constrains the antimatter fraction to \(10^{-8}\)–\(10^{-9}\), comparable to the sensitivity planned for AMS-II. The paper also notes that earlier anti-meteorite searches in the USSR (Konstantinov 1974) reported positive but unconfirmed signals, leaving open the possibility of future discovery.
