# Exchange Current Corrections to Neutrino--Nucleus Scattering

> Relativistic exchange current corrections to neutrino--nucleus cross sections are presented assuming non--vanishing strange quark form factors for the constituent nucleons. For charged current processes the exchange current corrections can lower the impulse approximation results by 10\% while these corrections are found to be sensitive to both the nuclear density and the strange quark axial form factor of the nucleon for neutral current processes. Implications on the LSND experiment to determine this form factor are discussed.

## Key claim

Relativistic exchange current corrections to neutrino-nucleus cross sections are presented assuming non-vanishing strange quark form factors for the nucleon; the corrections can lower impulse approximation results by 10% for charged current processes, but their magnitude depends on both nuclear density and the strange quark axial form factor for neutral current processes.

## Method

The authors generalized the soft-pion exchange current method of Chemtob and Rho (1971) from SU(2) to SU(3) to accommodate finite strange quark form factors, constructing fully relativistic two-body exchange current operators for both neutral and charged currents. They evaluated these corrections for quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering using a relativistic Fermi Gas model with zero binding energy, considering only 1p1h final states, and applied the kinematics of the LSND experiment with an incident neutrino beam energy of 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For charged current $^{12}C(\nu_{\mu^-}, \mu^-)X$ at Fermi momentum $k_F = 225$ MeV (the usual value for $^{12}C$), the total cross section is reduced from 24 to 22.7 ($\times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$) by exchange current corrections.
- The LSND collaboration measured a total cross section of $(8.3 \pm 0.7\; \text{stat.} \pm 1.6\; \text{syst.}) \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ for the charged current reaction, which the exchange current corrections cannot explain.
- For charged current processes, exchange current corrections vary from 5% to 10% as Fermi momentum increases from 200 to 300 MeV.
- For neutral current $^{12}C(\nu, \nu' p)$ at $k_F = 300$ MeV, the quasi-elastic peak value drops from $60 \times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$/MeV (impulse approximation) to $50 \times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$/MeV with exchange current corrections.
- For $k_F = 200$ MeV, both impulse approximation and exchange current corrected results give about $70 \times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$/MeV at the quasi-elastic peak.
- The strange quark form factors were assumed to be $F_2^s = -0.21$ and $G_A^s = -0.19$ for the calculations.
- The vector mass was set to $M_V = 840$ MeV and the axial mass to $M_A = 1030$ MeV.
- For the integrated proton-to-neutron yield ratio $R(p/n)$ with the LSND kinematic cut ($50 \leq T_F \leq 120$ MeV), exchange currents change the ratio by about 5% for neutrino scattering and about 15% for anti-neutrino scattering.

## Context

This work extends the soft-pion exchange current approach previously applied to electromagnetic and weak axial processes (such as first forbidden beta-decay studied by Warburton (1991-1994) and deuteron photo-disintegration by Hockert et al. (1973)) to neutrino-nucleus scattering, where both vector and axial currents are involved simultaneously. The calculations are motivated by the LSND experiment's goal to determine the strange quark axial form factor of the nucleon, following earlier work by Garvey et al. (1992) on quasi-elastic neutrino scattering. The results enable future extensions to finite nuclei and suggest that exchange current corrections alone cannot resolve the discrepancy between theory and the LSND measured charged current cross section on $^{12}C$.
