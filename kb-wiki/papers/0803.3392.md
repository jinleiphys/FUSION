# Pionic contribution to relativistic Fermi Liquid parameters

> We calculate pionic contribution to the relativistic Fermi Liquid parameters (RFLPs) using Chiral Effective Lagrangian. The RFLPs so determined are then used to calculate chemical potential, exchange and nuclear symmetry energies due to $π$$N$ interaction. We also evaluate two loop ring diagrams involving $σ$, $ω$ and $π$ meson exchanges and compare results with what one obtains from the relativistic Fermi Liquid theory (RFLT).

## Key claim

The pionic contribution to relativistic Fermi Liquid parameters (RFLPs) is calculated using Chiral Effective Field Theory, going beyond the mean-field level where the pion, being a pseudoscalar, fails to contribute. The paper shows that the pionic contribution to the dimensionless Landau parameters is significantly larger than the combined contributions of sigma and omega mesons, implying that any realistic relativistic calculation must include the pion.

## Method

The author uses Chiral Effective Field Theory (chEFT) to derive the pionic exchange contribution to the quasiparticle interaction near the Fermi surface. The spin-averaged scattering amplitude for pion exchange is evaluated from the relevant Feynman diagram (exchange only, as the direct term vanishes), and the Landau parameters \(f_0\) and \(f_1\) are obtained via Legendre expansion. These are then used to calculate the chemical potential, exchange energy, and symmetry energy for symmetric nuclear matter, and the results are compared with direct evaluation of two-loop ring diagrams involving sigma, omega, and pion meson exchanges.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear matter saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.148\ \mathrm{fm^{-3}}\), the dimensionless Landau parameters from pion exchange are \(F_0^{ex,\pi} = 0.68\) and \(F_1^{ex,\pi} = -0.20\).
* The dimensionless Landau parameters for sigma meson are \(F_0^\sigma = -5.04\) and \(F_1^\sigma = 0.875\); for omega meson they are \(F_0^\omega = 5.44\) and \(F_1^\omega = -0.93\) (at \(\rho = \rho_0\)).
* The exchange energy from pion exchange at saturation density is \(E^{ex}_\pi = 12.49\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from FLT), compared to \(40.48\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for sigma and \(-23.41\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for omega.
* The symmetry energy at saturation density is \(\beta = 14.57\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The coupling constants used are \(g_A^2 = 1.5876\), \(f_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(m_\pi = 139\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Fermi liquid theory of Baym and Chin (1976) and Matsui (1981), who calculated FLPs using the Walecka model but did not include pionic contributions at the mean-field level. It contrasts with non-relativistic calculations such as those by Friman et al. (1996, 1999) and Rho (1980), which showed the importance of pionic contributions. By using Chiral Effective Field Theory (as developed by Furnstahl et al. (1997) and Hu et al. (2007)), the paper provides a systematic framework to include pion exchange and enables more realistic calculations of nuclear matter properties such as chemical potential, exchange energy, and symmetry energy within a relativistic framework.
