# Excitation energy of fission fragments within nuclear time-dependent density functional theory

> The number and properties of the neutrons and photons emitted in nuclear fission are directly related to the excitation energy of the fission fragments when they are formed at scission. Though not observable experimentally because of the extremely short time scales, the excitation energy of fission fragments can be predicted by microscopic theory based on time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT). Initial results on the value of the total kinetic energy of fission reactions were very promising, but could not probe all possible fragmentations. In this work, we perform large-scale TDDFT calculations in $^{240}$Pu enabled by the development of a new TDDFT solver. We obtain TDDFT trajectories covering nearly all possible fragmentations. We find that the total kinetic energy is close to experimental values only for the most likely fission while it is severely underestimated at both small and large asymmetries. This conclusion seems rather independent of the parameterization of the energy functional, both in its particle-hole and particle-particle channels.

## Key claim

The authors find that time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) calculations for $^{240}$Pu reproduce experimental total kinetic energy (TKE) values only for the most likely fission fragmentations, while TKE is severely underestimated by up to 10-20 MeV for both small and large asymmetries, and this conclusion appears largely independent of the energy functional parameterization.

## Method

The authors developed a new TDHFB solver (AxialHOHFB) using an axially-symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with a novel basis selection method that fixes the box size and adjusts oscillator lengths as basis size increases, enabling improved resolution of sharp features. They performed large-scale TDHFB simulations covering nearly all possible fragmentations of $^{240}$Pu, implementing algorithmic improvements including an exact Coulomb potential evaluation via Talmi-Moshinsky brackets and a singular value decomposition technique for computing mean-field matrix elements with O(N^5) complexity instead of O(N^6). The time evolution used a quasiparticle energy cutoff of 60 MeV, resulting in energy conservation within approximately 100 keV.

## Key numbers

- TKE values for most likely fission fragmentations of $^{240}$Pu are within a few MeV of experimental data
- TKE is underestimated by up to 10-20 MeV for both smaller and larger asymmetries compared to experimental values
- A quasiparticle energy cutoff of 60 MeV results in typical energy conservation within 100 keV during time evolution
- When all quasiparticles are retained (no cutoff), energy conservation is observed at the sub-eV level

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHFB calculations by Bulgac (2016, 2019) that showed promising TKE results for most likely fission but could not probe all fragmentations, and confirms findings by Ren (2022) and Li (2025) that TKE values are too small for asymmetric fission. The new large-scale calculations spanning the entire mass range of fragmentations demonstrate that the discrepancy with experimental data is not caused by insufficient trajectory sampling or energy functional choices, pointing to a fundamental limitation of the TDHFB approach for predicting fragment excitation energies.
