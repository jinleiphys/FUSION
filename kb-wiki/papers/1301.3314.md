# Size of the emission source and collectivity in ultra-relativistic p-Pb collisions

> The interferometric radii in the system formed in ultra-relativistic proton-lead collisions are investigated in a framework based on event-by-event 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics. We argue that the most central p-Pb collisions undergoing collective expansion behave similarly to the peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions. The interferometric observables can serve as signatures of the formation of an extended fireball.

# Size of the emission source and collectivity in ultra-relativistic p-Pb collisions

## Key claim

The authors argue that if experimental interferometric radii in the most central p-Pb collisions follow the pattern of A-A collisions (rather than p-p collisions), this would serve as a fingerprint of collective behavior; however, they note that low-multiplicity p-Pb collisions are not expected to display this behavior and should follow the p-p pattern, hinting at different dynamics.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations of p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, using GLISSANDO to generate initial conditions with two variants (standard and compact source) and THERMINATOR2 for statistical emission and resonance decays at freeze-out (Tf = 150 MeV). They generated 450 distinct hydrodynamic evolutions for each source scenario, with 1000 THERMINATOR events per freeze-out hypersurface, and extracted pionic interferometric radii by fitting a three-dimensional Gaussian shape to the same-sign two-pion correlation functions using the Bertsch-Pratt parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The average rms radii for the two sets of initial conditions are 1.5 fm (standard source) and 0.9 fm (compact source)
- For the compact initial distribution, the interferometric radii are above 1.8 fm for Rside and above 2 fm for Rout and Rlong at kT = 400 MeV
- The compact source leads to Rout reduced by 25% compared to the standard source
- The expansion of the compact source causes an increase of the average transverse momentum by 20% relative to the standard source
- The typical size and lifetime of the collective source formed in central p-Pb collisions is 3-4 fm
- The centrality 0-3.4% is defined by Npart ≥ 18 participant nucleons
- The NN inelastic cross section used is σNN = 67.7 mb

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic approach previously applied to A-A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies (Shen 2011, Teaney 2009, Schenke 2011, Luzum 2011) to the p-Pb system, building on the authors' earlier work (Bozek 2012) that used event-by-event hydrodynamics to describe the same-side ridge structure in p-Pb correlation data. The paper contrasts with the color-glass condensate approach (Dusling 2012) that explains the same-side ridge from initial state effects, and aims to provide an independent test of collectivity through femtoscopic measurements. The results enable experimental tests at the LHC to distinguish between collective and elementary dynamics in p-Pb collisions, and if a large fireball is found, it could be used in quenching models for comparison with RAA data.
