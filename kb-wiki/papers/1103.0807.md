# Dynamically generated hadron resonances

> As an example of dynamically generated resonances we mention the interaction of vector mesons with baryons within the local hidden gauge formalism which gives rise to a large amount of such states, many of which can be associated to known resonances, while others represent predictions for new resonances. The width of these states coming from decay into pseudoscalar baryon is also addressed. We also mention recent states coming from $Δρπ$ interaction obtained with Faddeev equations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that many known hadron resonances can be dynamically generated from the interaction of vector mesons with baryons using the local hidden gauge formalism, but the authors qualify that the resulting states are degenerate in spin (J^P = 1/2^- and 3/2^-) under the approximation of neglecting three-momentum compared to mass, and that this degeneracy is only qualitatively followed by the experimental spectrum.

## Method

The authors apply the hidden gauge formalism for vector meson interactions, constructing scattering amplitudes for vector meson - baryon octet interactions in a coupled-channel unitary approach. They compute the interaction by exchanging a vector meson between the vector meson and the baryon, using the SU(3) matrix of vector mesons and the Lagrangian for vector meson coupling to the baryon octet and decuplet. The decay widths of the resulting states into pseudoscalar meson plus baryon are also calculated, and three-body Faddeev equations are used for the Delta-rho-pi system.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f = 93 MeV is used to define the coupling g = M_V/(2f)
- Three degenerate N* states and three degenerate Delta states are found around 1900 MeV, with J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-, from the rho-Delta interaction
- The f_2(1270) and f_0(1370) meson resonances are dynamically generated from rho-rho interaction within the hidden gauge formalism

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to pseudoscalar meson - baryon interactions (Kaiser 1995, Oller and Meissner 2001, Jido 2003) into the vector meson sector. It builds on the hidden gauge formalism developed by Bando, Kugo, and Yamawaki (1985) and the rho-rho interaction work by Geng and Oset (2009) that generated f_2(1270) and f_0(1370). The results enable predictions for new resonances from vector meson - baryon interactions and provide a framework for understanding spin-degenerate states in the experimental spectrum, while also motivating further three-body studies of systems like Delta-rho-pi to clarify the status of Delta states around 2000 MeV.
