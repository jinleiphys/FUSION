# Analytic properties of the scattering amplitude and resonances parameters in a meson exchange model

> The analytic properties of scattering amplitudes provide important information. Besides the cuts, the poles and zeros on the different Riemann sheets determine the global behavior of the amplitude on the physical axis. Pole positions and residues allow for a parameterization of resonances in a well-defined way, free of assumptions for the background and energy dependence of the resonance part. This is a necessary condition to relate resonance contributions in different reactions. In the present study, we determine the pole structure of pion-nucleon scattering in an analytic model based on meson exchange. For this, the sheet structure of the amplitude is determined. To show the precision of the resonance extraction and discuss phenomena such as resonance interference, we discuss the S11 amplitude in greater detail.

## Key claim

The pole positions and residues of the S-matrix provide a well-defined, model-independent parameterization of resonances, free from assumptions about background and energy dependence of the resonance part; however, the separation of the amplitude into pole and non-pole contributions is regularization-dependent, and the non-pole part can itself contain poles that interact non-trivially with the pole part.

## Method

The authors analytically continue the coupled-channel meson-exchange scattering amplitude of the Jülich model (based on time-ordered perturbation theory with Lagrangians obeying chiral constraints) to the various Riemann sheets in the complex plane of the scattering energy \(z = s^{1/2}\). For channels with stable particles (\(\pi N\), \(\eta N\)), the continuation is performed by adding the discontinuity along the righthand cut; for effective \(\pi\pi N\) channels (\(\sigma N\), \(\rho N\), \(\pi\Delta\)), the continuation is achieved through contour deformation of the momentum integration in the complex plane, accounting for the additional branch points induced by the unstable-particle propagators.

## Key numbers

* The \(\sigma(600)\) pole in the \(\pi\pi\) scattering amplitude is found at \(z^0_\sigma = 875 - 232i\) MeV.
* The \(\rho(770)\) pole lies at \(z^0_\rho = 763 - 64i\) MeV, corresponding to a width \(\Gamma = 128\) MeV.
* The \(\Delta(1232)\) pole in the \(\pi\Delta\) propagator lies at \(z^0_\Delta = 1211 - 37i\) MeV, corresponding to a width \(\Gamma = 74\) MeV.
* The bare \(\sigma\) mass used in the model is \(m_\sigma^0 = 900\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich meson-exchange model (Schutz 1998, Krehl 1999, Gasparyan 2003) by performing a full analytic continuation to extract resonance poles, building on earlier analytic continuation studies in meson-baryon scattering (Arndt 2006, Inoue 2001, Jido 2003, Sarkar 2004) and the recent work of Suzuki (2008) within a meson exchange framework. The results enable a comparison of resonance parameters extracted from different reactions using pole positions and residues rather than model-dependent Breit-Wigner parameterizations, and address the question of whether a clean separation of pole and non-pole contributions is possible, as previously investigated for the \(P_{33}\) partial wave with the \(\Delta(1232)\) (Döring 2009).
