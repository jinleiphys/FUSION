# Chiral vortical catalysis constrained by LQCD simulations

> Evidences of vortical effects have been recently found by experiments in heavy ion collisions, instigating new insights into the phase diagram of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Considering the effect of rotations, lattice QCD data shows that the temperatures for deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration should increase with real angular velocity, and the dominant effects are related to gluonic degrees of freedom. These findings could be essential for quark models in rotating systems that lack gluonic interactions, which predicts the decreasing of the chiral temperature transition with the angular velocity. To address this issue properly, in this work we apply the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model to explore the phase diagram in a rotating rigid cylinder with constant angular velocity in the mean field approximation. To circumvent the absence of gluons, we propose the application of an effective coupling dependent of the angular velocity, fitted to match the pseudocritical temperature of chiral phase transition in the model through lattice QCD data. Our results indicate that the running coupling induces the enhancement of the chiral condensate as a function of angular velocity, strengthening the breaking of chiral symmetry, an effect previously dubbed as chiral vortical catalysis. For the chiral susceptibility we observe stronger fluctuations around the transition temperature when we consider the running coupling. The phase diagram is affected by these findings shifting the critical end point (CEP) to higher temperatures and chemical potentials.

# Chiral vortical catalysis constrained by LQCD simulations

## Key claim

The authors show that introducing an angular-velocity-dependent coupling in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, fitted to lattice QCD pseudocritical temperatures, reverses the usual model prediction: instead of rotation suppressing chiral symmetry, it enhances the chiral condensate (chiral vortical catalysis). This reversal is conditional on the running coupling mimicking the missing gluonic degrees of freedom, which lattice QCD indicates dominate the rotational response.

## Method

The authors apply the two-flavor NJL model in the mean-field approximation to a rigid cylinder rotating with constant angular velocity. They fit an exponential function for the effective coupling G(ω) to reproduce the lattice QCD pseudocritical temperature of the chiral transition as a function of angular velocity from Braguta et al. (2022). They then compute the chiral condensate, effective quark masses, chiral susceptibility, and the phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential plane, comparing results with constant coupling versus the running coupling.

## Key numbers

- Angular velocity in heavy ion collisions: ω ≈ (9 ± 1) × 10^21 s^−1 ≈ 7 MeV (STAR 2017); hydrodynamic simulations predict ω ≈ 20–40 MeV.
- Lattice QCD parametrization: T_c(v)/T_c(0) = 1 + B_2 v^2/c^2 with B_2 ≈ 1.13153 for m_ps/m_v = 0.80 (Braguta 2022).
- NJL model parameters: cutoff Λ = 651 MeV, bare quark mass m = 5.5 MeV, coupling G = 5.04 GeV^−2; vacuum pion mass m_π = 141.715 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 94.043 MeV, chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄_u ψ_u⟩^{1/3} = −(251.322) MeV.
- Running coupling fit: G(ω) = G_α + G_β exp(ω/Ω) with G_α = 4.97667 GeV^−2, G_β = 0.05840 GeV^−2, Ω = 0.02457 GeV.
- Maximum angular velocity: ω = 0.2 GeV for cylinder radius r = 5 GeV^−1 to satisfy causality ωr < 1.
- Effective quark mass increase: 22% increase at ω = 60 MeV compared to ω = 30 MeV at low temperature with running coupling; chiral condensate increases 12% for the same values.
- NJL model B_2 coefficient: B_2 = 1.12813, compared to lattice QCD value B_2 = 1.13153 ± 0.03.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Jiang (2021), who first proposed chiral vortical catalysis with a linear ω-dependent coupling in the NJL model, by instead fitting the coupling to lattice QCD data from Braguta et al. (2022). It contradicts earlier NJL model results (Jiang 2016, Chernodub 2016, Wang 2018, Sun 2021, Sun 2023) where rotation reduces the critical temperature, and also disagrees with recent linear sigma model results (Hernandez 2024) and holographic approaches (Zhao 2022, Chen 2024) regarding the location of the critical end point. The work enables future studies of the splitting between deconfinement and chiral pseudocritical temperatures under rotation, and provides a method to incorporate gluonic effects into effective quark models without explicit Polyakov loop extensions.
