# Saturation Physics in Ultra High Energy Cosmic Rays: Heavy Quark Production

> In this work we estimate the heavy quark production in the interaction of ultra high energy cosmic rays in the atmosphere, considering that the primary cosmic ray is a proton or a photon. At these energies the saturation momentum Q_{sat}^2 stays above the hard scale μ_c^2=4m_c^2, implying charm production probing the saturation regime. In particular, we show that the ep HERA data presents a scaling on τ_c = (Q^2+μ_c^2)/Q_{sat}^2. We derive our results considering the dipole picture and the Color Glass Condensate formalism, which one shows to be able to describe the heavy quark production in photon-proton and proton-proton collisions. Nuclear effects are considered in computation of cross sections for scattering on air nuclei. Implications on the flux of prompt leptons at the earth are analyzed and a large suppression is predicted.

# Saturation Physics in Ultra High Energy Cosmic Rays: Heavy Quark Production

## Key claim

The authors claim that at ultra-high energies (above 10^18 eV), the saturation momentum Q_sat^2 exceeds the hard scale for charm production (μ_c^2 = 4m_c^2), meaning charm production in cosmic ray interactions probes the saturation regime for the first time; however, this effect is significant only for charm quarks, while bottom production remains largely unaffected by saturation effects even at the highest energies considered.

## Method

The authors use the color dipole approach and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism to calculate heavy quark production cross sections in photon-proton, photon-nucleus, proton-proton, and proton-nucleus interactions. For nuclear targets, they apply the Glauber-Gribov formalism for nuclear shadowing with an average mass number A = 14.5 to represent air nuclei. They also provide a parametrization for the charm and bottom differential cross sections dσ/dx_F suitable for input into atmospheric shower simulations.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_sat^2 is compared to the charm hard scale μ_c^2 = 4m_c^2, with the condition Q_sat > μ_c occurring at energies E > 10^18 eV
- For charm photoproduction on nuclei, the cross section energy dependence is mild at E_γ ≥ 10^5 GeV, giving σ ∝ E_γ^0.12
- For charm production in proton-proton collisions, the energy dependence is σ ∝ E_p^0.17 at E_p ≥ 10^7 GeV
- The deviation between CGC and color transparency (leading twist) predictions for charm photoproduction is about one order of magnitude at E_γ ≈ 10^12 GeV
- For charm production in proton-nucleus collisions, the deviation between CGC and leading twist approaches is of order six at E_p ≈ 10^12 GeV
- For bottom photoproduction, saturation effects contribute only approximately 10% at very high photon energies (E_γ ≈ 10^12 GeV)
- The profile function for charm production peaks at approximately r ≈ 0.4 GeV^-1, while for bottom production it peaks at r ≈ 0.1 GeV^-1

## Context

This work extends previous prompt lepton flux calculations by Thunman, Ingelman, and Gondolo (TIG) and others that assumed collinear factorization and DGLAP evolution, by incorporating saturation effects from the Color Glass Condensate formalism. The authors show that their saturation-inclusive predictions for charm production are similar to the TIG results, which used a gluon distribution extrapolated with λ = 0.08, implying that saturation physics predicts a suppression of prompt lepton fluxes relative to standard pQCD calculations. The paper also demonstrates geometric scaling of HERA charm data on the variable τ_c = (Q^2 + μ_c^2)/Q_sat^2, extending the scaling property previously observed for inclusive deep inelastic scattering by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Kwiecinski (2001). The results enable improved estimates of atmospheric neutrino and muon backgrounds for neutrino telescopes such as AMANDA, Antares, and Nestor.
