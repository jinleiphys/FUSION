# Statistics of a simple transmission mode on a lossy chaotic background

> Scattering on a resonance state coupled to a complicated background is a typical problem for mesoscopic quantum many-body systems as well as for wave propagation in the presence of a complex environment. On average, such a simple mode acquires an effective damping, the so-called "spreading" width, due to mixing with the background states. Modeling the latter by random matrix theory and employing the strength function formalism, we derive the joint distribution of the reflection and total transmission at arbitrary absorption in the background. The distribution is found to possess a remarkable symmetry between its reflection and transmission sectors, which is controlled by the ratio of the spreading to escape width. This in turn results in a symmetry relation between the marginal densities, despite the absence of the flux conservation at finite absorption. As an application, we study the statistics of total losses in the system at arbitrary coupling to the background.

# Statistics of a simple transmission mode on a lossy chaotic background

## Key claim
The joint distribution of reflection and total transmission for a simple resonance mode coupled to a chaotic background with arbitrary absorption possesses a symmetry between its reflection and transmission sectors under the involution η → η⁻¹, where η = Γ↓/Γ₀ is the ratio of spreading width to escape width, and this symmetry holds despite the absence of flux conservation at finite absorption.

## Method
The author models the chaotic background using random matrix theory (Gaussian orthogonal or unitary ensemble, depending on time-reversal invariance) and employs the strength function formalism to derive the S-matrix for a simple mode coupled to background states. The joint probability density function of reflection R and total transmission T is obtained by computing the Jacobian transformation from the known joint distribution of the local Green's function variables u and v, which describe the background's local density of states and absorption. Marginal distributions and the distribution of total losses (unitarity deficit D = 1 − R − T) are then derived by integration.

## Key numbers
- η = Γ↓/Γ₀: the ratio of spreading width to escape width, the dimensionless parameter controlling coupling strength to the background (dimensionless).
- γ = 2π Γ_abs/Δ: the dimensionless absorption rate, where Γ_abs is the uniform broadening of background states and Δ is the mean level spacing (dimensionless).
- The joint distribution P(r,t) at perfect coupling (S⁽⁰⁾_aa = 0) is given by Eq. (P(r,t)): P(r,t) = (2/π(1−r−t)²√y) P₀((η⁻¹r + ηt)/(1−r−t)), valid for 1−r−t > 0 and y = 1+2rt−(1−r)²−(1−t)² > 0.
- At zero absorption (γ → 0), the marginal transmission distribution is P₀(t) = 1/(π√(t(1−t)) [ηt + η⁻¹(1−t)]), independent of time-reversal symmetry.
- The unitarity deficit D satisfies D ≤ (1−S⁽⁰⁾_aa)/2, with maximum range 0 ≤ D ≤ 1 at S⁽⁰⁾_aa = −1.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous results on transmission intensity fluctuations (Savin 2017) and phase fluctuations (Savin 2018) for a simple mode coupled to a chaotic background, now providing the full joint statistics of reflection and transmission including absorption. The approach builds on the random matrix theory framework for chaotic scattering (Mitchell 2010, Fyodorov 2011) and on exact results for scattering characteristics at finite absorption (Savin 2003, Fyodorov 2003, Fyodorov 2005, Kumar 2013). The symmetry found in the joint distribution relates to earlier symmetries of the local density of states established for ergodic states (Fyodorov 2004, Savin 2005) and generalized to multifractal spectra at Anderson transitions (Mirlin 2006, Gruzberg 2011). The results enable experimental extraction of the background coupling η and absorption strength γ from scattering data, and are applicable to microwave cavity experiments (Kuhl 2013) and microwave graphs (Lawn 2019) for systems with or without time-reversal invariance.
