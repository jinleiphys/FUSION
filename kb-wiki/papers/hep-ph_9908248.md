# On the Perturbative Nature of Color Superconductivity

> Color superconductivity is a possible phase of high density QCD. We present a systematic derivation of the transition temperature, T_C, from the QCD Lagrangian through study of the di-quark proper vertex. With this approach, we confirm the dependence of T_C on the coupling g, namely $T_C \sim μg^{-5} e^{-κ/g}$, previously obtained from the one-gluon exchange approximation in the superconducting phase. The diagrammatic approach we employ allows us to examine the perturbative expansion of the vertex and the propagators. We find an additional O(1) contribution to the prefactor of the exponential from the one-loop quark self energy and that the other one-loop radiative contributions and the two gluon exchange vertex contribution are subleading.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the transition temperature for color superconductivity follows the scaling $T_C \sim \mu g^{-5} e^{-\kappa/g}$ previously obtained from one-gluon exchange in the superconducting phase, but finds an additional $O(1)$ contribution to the pre-exponential factor from the one-loop quark self-energy that was not reported in prior work.

## Method

The authors derive the transition temperature from the normal phase of QCD by studying the di-quark proper vertex function at finite temperature and chemical potential, using a Dyson-Schwinger equation approach analogous to Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov (1961) for non-relativistic Fermi gases. They work in Coulomb gauge, include hard dense loop effects in the gluon propagator (Debye screening for electric gluons and Landau damping for magnetic gluons), and solve the resulting Fredholm eigenvalue problem for the $l=0$ partial wave in the antisymmetric color channel. Higher-order corrections from the one-loop quark self-energy and two-gluon exchange diagrams are evaluated systematically.

## Key numbers

- The transition temperature scaling: $\pi k_B T_C = \mu g^{-5} c c' e^{-\sqrt{6N/(N+1)} \pi^2 / g}$, where for $N=3$ the exponent is $-\sqrt{18/4} \pi^2 / g$
- The factor $c = 1024 \sqrt{2} \pi^4 N_f^{-5/2}$ (up to an $O(1)$ constant)
- The previously unreported factor $c' = \exp[ -(\pi^2 + 4)(N-1)/16 ] \simeq 0.1766$ for $N=3$, arising from the one-loop quark self-energy
- The Debye mass squared: $m_D^2 \simeq N_f g^2 \mu^2 / (2\pi^2)$
- The exponent coefficient $\kappa = 3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}$ from Son (1998) is confirmed

## Context

This work extends the one-gluon exchange calculations of Son (1998), Pisarski and Rischke (1999), and Schafer and Wilczek (1999) by providing a gauge-invariant derivation from the normal phase rather than from gap equations in the superconducting phase. The approach enables a systematic examination of higher-order perturbative corrections, showing that Landau damping preserves the perturbative expansion (unlike static screening, where infrared log-enhancement would spoil it), and identifies a previously unreported $O(1)$ contribution to the pre-exponential factor from the quark self-energy. The method opens the way to an exact determination of the pre-exponential factor in a forthcoming publication.
