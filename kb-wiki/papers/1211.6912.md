# Heavy quark transport in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC within the UrQMD transport model

> We have implemented a Langevin approach for the transport of heavy quarks in the UrQMD hybrid model. The UrQMD hybrid approach provides a realistic description of the background medium for the evolution of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We have used two different sets of drag and diffusion coefficients, one based on a $T$-Matrix approach and one based on a resonance model for the elastic scattering of heavy quarks within the medium. In case of the resonance model we have investigated the effects of different decoupling temperatures of the heavy quarks from the medium, ranging between $130\,\text{MeV}$ and $180\,\text{MeV}$. We present calculations of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$, as well as of the elliptic flow $v_2$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\,\text{GeV}$ and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\,\text{TeV}$. To make our results comparable to experimental data at RHIC and LHC we have implemented a Peterson fragmentation and a quark coalescence approach followed by the semileptonic decay of the D- and B-mesons to electrons. We find that our results strongly depend on the decoupling temperature and the hadronization mechanism. At a decoupling temperature of $130\,\text{MeV}$ we reach a good agreement with the measurements at both, RHIC and LHC energies, simultaneously for the elliptic flow $v_2$ and the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$.

## Key claim

The authors find that a Langevin approach for heavy quark transport within the UrQMD hybrid model can simultaneously describe both the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) at RHIC and LHC energies only when using a quark coalescence hadronization mechanism and a low decoupling temperature of \(130\,\text{MeV}\); with Peterson fragmentation alone, the elliptic flow is too small compared to experimental data.

## Method

The authors implemented a relativistic Langevin equation for charm and bottom quark propagation on the space-time dependent background generated by the UrQMD hybrid model, which combines hadronic transport with (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics. Two sets of non-perturbative drag and diffusion coefficients were used: one from a \(T\)-Matrix approach and one from a resonance model for elastic heavy-quark scattering. Heavy quarks were hadronized either via Peterson fragmentation or a quark coalescence model, followed by semileptonic decay of D- and B-mesons to electrons using PYTHIA routines.

## Key numbers

* Decoupling temperatures investigated for the resonance model ranged between \(130\,\text{MeV}\) and \(180\,\text{MeV}\).
* Calculations were performed for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\,\text{GeV}\) and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\,\text{TeV}\).
* The bottom-to-charm production cross-section ratio was fixed at \(\sigma_{b\bar{b}}/\sigma_{c\bar{c}} \simeq 4.9\times 10^{-3}\).
* Peterson fragmentation parameters were \(\epsilon_Q=0.05\) for charm quarks and \(\epsilon_Q=0.005\) for bottom quarks.
* For the coalescence model, light quark mass was assumed to be \(370\,\text{MeV}\), giving D-meson mass \(1.87\,\text{GeV}\) and B-meson mass \(4.87\,\text{GeV}\).
* At RHIC, with coalescence and \(130\,\text{MeV}\) decoupling temperature, the calculation achieved reasonable agreement with PHENIX data for both \(v_2\) and \(R_{AA}\).
* At LHC, D-meson \(R_{AA}\) results were compared to ALICE data (ALICE, 2012).

## Context

This work extends previous heavy quark transport studies that used simplified thermal fireball models (van Hees et al., 2007; Rapp, 2009) by employing the more realistic UrQMD hybrid model with event-by-event fluctuations and a well-tested hydrodynamic background. It also builds on similar Langevin studies in ideal hydrodynamics with lattice-QCD equation of state (He et al., 2012) and in the BAMPS model (Uphoff et al., 2011, 2012). The results enable future comparisons with direct D- and B-meson measurements from the STAR Heavy Flavor Tracker and demonstrate the importance of the hadronization mechanism and late-stage medium interactions for simultaneously describing \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\).
