# Glueball hunting in $e^+e^- \to J/ψ\to ϕf_0$

> Revtex, 5 pages, 3 figures; An arithmetic error with Eq.(7) was corrected; Relevant changes to Eqs.(8),(10-12) were made; The conclusion does not change; Minor text changes are made; Version to appear on Phys. Lett. B

## Key claim

The authors argue that the method of Brodsky, Goldhaber, and Lee (2003) for producing scalar glueballs in electron-positron annihilation can be rescaled from the 3 GeV mass region to the 1 GeV mass region, making the process accessible at BES and CLEO-c via the reaction \(e^+e^- \to J/\psi \to \phi f_0\). The key hedge is that the numerical estimates depend on the assumption that exchanged gluons are perturbative, which is not well satisfied for light quark systems, so the actual branching ratios may be debatable even though the broad conclusion appears robust.

## Method

The authors rescale the pQCD formalism of Brodsky, Goldhaber, and Lee (2003) from the \(J/\psi\)-glueball system at \(\sqrt{s}=10.6\) GeV to the \(\phi\)-glueball system at \(\sqrt{s}=3.1\) GeV, using the mass ratio \(r=4m_q/\sqrt{s}\) as the scaling variable. They compute the branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to \gamma^* \to \phi {\cal G}_0\) via virtual photon exchange, then estimate the dominant strong decay contribution \(J/\psi \to 3g \to \phi {\cal G}_0\) by normalizing to the experimentally known \(J/\psi \to \phi \eta'\) and \(J/\psi \to \phi \eta\) rates. They also identify a lower-order diagram where the glueball couples directly to a single gluon from \(c\bar{c}\) annihilation, which could enhance the branching ratio by a factor of \(1/\alpha_s^2\).

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to \gamma^* \to \phi {\cal G}_0\) (with \(M_{\cal G}=M_{\eta'}\)) is estimated as \(5.85 \times 10^{-6} (\alpha_s/0.33)^2\).
- The ratio of strong to electromagnetic coupling for \(s\bar{s}\) production is \(br_{3g\to s\bar{s}}/br_{\gamma^*\to s\bar{s}} = 21 \pm 3\).
- The predicted branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to 3g \to \phi (s\bar{s})\) is \((6.5 \pm 1.7) \times 10^{-4} (\alpha_s/0.33)^2\).
- The total predicted branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to \phi (s\bar{s})\) via both strong and EM channels is \(6.5 \times 10^{-4} (\alpha_s/0.33)^2\), which agrees with the experimental estimate of \((8 \pm 1) \times 10^{-4}\) from \(\phi\eta\) and \(\phi\eta'\) data.
- The additional contribution from the lower-order diagram (Fig. 3) gives \(br^{add}_{J/\psi\to 3g\to \phi{\cal G}_0} \simeq 6.0 \times 10^{-3}\), leading to a total \(\bar{br}^{th}_{J/\psi\to \phi{\cal G}_0} \simeq 6.6 \times 10^{-3}\).
- The running coupling constant is taken as \(\alpha_s \sim 0.33\) at \(\sqrt{s}=3.1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the pQCD approach of Brodsky, Goldhaber, and Lee (2003) from the 3 GeV glueball mass region to the 1-1.7 GeV region suggested by Lattice QCD (Morningstar and Peardon, 1999; UKQCD Collaboration, 1997) and phenomenological studies (Close, Farrar, and Li, 1997; Close and Kirk, 2000). The authors show that the method can be normalized using existing data on \(J/\psi \to \phi\eta\) and \(J/\psi \to \phi\eta'\) from the Particle Data Group (2002), and they identify a potentially dominant lower-order diagram that could make glueball production strongly favored in \(J/\psi\) decays. The results motivate experimental searches at BES III and CLEO-c for scalar glueball candidates in the \(f_0(980)\), \(f_0(1370)\), \(f_0(1500)\), and \(f_0(1700)\) mass region.
