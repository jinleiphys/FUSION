# High energy pA collisions in the color glass condensate approach I. Gluon production and the Cronin effect

> We study gluon production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions in the semi-classical framework of the Color Glass Condensate. We develop a general formalism to compute gluon fields in covariant gauge to lowest order in the classical field of the proton and to all orders in the classical field of the nucleus. The use of the covariant gauge makes the diagrammatic interpretation of the solution more transparnt. k_t-factorization holds to this order for gluon production -- Our results for the gluon distribution are equivalent to the prior diagrammatic analysis of Kovchegov and Mueller. We also show that these results are equivalent to the computation of gluon production by Dumitru and McLerran in the Fock-Schwinger gauge. We demonstrate how the Cronin effect arises in this approach, and examine its behavior in the two extreme limits of a) no small-x quantum evolution, and b) fully saturated quantum evolution. In both cases, the formalism reduces to Glauber's formalism of multiple scatterings. We comment on the possible implications of this study for the interpretation of the recent results on Deuteron-Gold collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

Gluon production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions can be computed to lowest order in the proton source density and to all orders in the nuclear source density using a covariant gauge formalism, with the result that k_t-factorization holds at this order and the Cronin effect emerges naturally from multiple scattering; however, the validity of the dilute-proton approximation is restricted to moderate collision energies or to kinematic regions not too far from the proton fragmentation region even at very high energies.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in covariant gauge for a proton-nucleus collision, treating the proton as a dilute source of color charge (to first order in ρ_p) and the nucleus as a dense source (to all orders in ρ_A). They develop a systematic truncation of the field equations, express the solution in terms of Wilson lines, and show that the resulting gluon production cross-section can be written in a k_t-factorized form involving the square of the Lipatov vertex. The equivalence of this covariant-gauge result to prior work in Fock-Schwinger gauge by Dumitru and McLerran is demonstrated explicitly.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon for Deuteron-Gold collisions at RHIC: √s = 200 GeV
- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon for future proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC: √s = 5.5 TeV
- The nuclear enhancement factor for parton density in the transverse plane is ∼ A^{1/3} (where A is the nuclear mass number)

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic analysis of gluon production in covariant gauge by Kovchegov and Mueller (2000) and the Fock-Schwinger gauge computation by Dumitru and McLerran (2002), providing the first explicit proof of equivalence between those two approaches. The formalism enables systematic inclusion of non-Gaussian correlations from quantum evolution via the JIMWLK renormalization group equation, going beyond the limitations of the Glauber multiple scattering model. The results are directly applicable to interpreting Deuteron-Gold collision data from RHIC and will inform future proton-nucleus studies at the LHC.
