# Transposition of a local-density-dependent pion-nucleus potential to an effective density-linear potential - generalized Seki-Masutani relations

> We have shown that a local-density-dependent term, $F[ρ(r)] ρ(r)$, of the pion-nucleus potential with a nuclear density, $ρ(r)$, can be transposed to a conventional density-linear term, $F(ρ_e) ρ(r)$, with an effective nuclear density, $ρ_e$, which is close to $\sim 0.6 ρ(0)$ for most $π^-$ bound states. The presently found relations, {\it generalized Seki-Masutani relations}, for the density-quadratic term, the medium-modified isovector term and the double-scattering isoscalar term of the s-wave pion-nucleus interaction assure that the constant parameters in the conventional pion-nucleus potential are interpreted as being effective ones in the light of density-dependent effects.

# Transposition of a local-density-dependent pion-nucleus potential to an effective density-linear potential - generalized Seki-Masutani relations

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that any local-density-dependent (LDD) term of the form $F[\rho(r)]\rho(r)$ in the pion-nucleus s-wave potential can be replaced by an effective density-linear term $F(\rho_e)\rho(r)$ with a constant effective density $\rho_e \sim 0.6\rho(0)$, meaning the conventional Ericson-Ericson potential with constant coefficients remains valid if those coefficients are interpreted as effective values at this reduced density, rather than at full nuclear density.

## Method
The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation for pionic atoms in $^{16}$O and $^{208}$Pb using LDD potentials for the density-quadratic term, the medium-modified isovector term, and the double-scattering isoscalar term, then compared the resulting binding energies to those calculated with conventional constant-parameter potentials. By equating the two approaches, they extracted the effective nuclear density $\rho_e$ that makes the constant-parameter potential reproduce the LDD results, generalizing the original Seki-Masutani Ansatz which only treated the density-quadratic term.

## Key numbers
- Effective nuclear density for the density-quadratic term ($n=2$): $\rho_e^{(n=2)} \sim 0.088$ fm$^{-3}$ ($\sim 0.59\rho_0$) for $^{208}$Pb and $\sim 0.071$ fm$^{-3}$ ($\sim 0.42\rho_0$) for $^{16}$O
- Effective nuclear density for the density-cubic term ($n=3$): $\rho_e^{(n=3)} \sim 0.062$ fm$^{-3}$ ($\sim 0.41\rho_0$) for $^{208}$Pb and $\sim 0.056$ fm$^{-3}$ ($\sim 0.33\rho_0$) for $^{16}$O
- Effective density for the density-dependent isovector parameter $b_1(\rho)$: $\rho_e \sim 0.090$ fm$^{-3}$ ($\sim 0.60\rho_0$)
- Effective density for the double-scattering term: $\rho_e/\rho_0 \sim 0.40$ for $^{16}$O and $\sim 0.54$ for $^{208}$Pb
- The empirically determined Seki-Masutani combination: $b_0^* = b_0 + 0.215\,\text{Re}B_0 = -0.028 \pm 0.001$
- Deduced empirical value of Re$B_0$ from $b_0^*$ and the double-scattering correction: Re$B_0 \sim -0.038 \pm 0.025$ (compared to Im$B_0 = 0.055$)
- Angle transformation correction to the isoscalar parameter: $\Delta b_0^{\text{AT}} \approx +0.003$
- Angle transformation correction to the imaginary part: $\Delta \text{Im}B_0^{\text{AT}} \approx -0.008$ for $^{16}$O and $-0.014$ for $^{208}$Pb

## Context
This work extends the original Seki-Masutani relation (Seki and Masutani, 1980s), which showed a strong correlation between the isoscalar parameter $b_0$ and the density-quadratic parameter Re$B_0$, to arbitrary functional forms of density-dependent potentials. It directly addresses the long-standing "anomalous s-wave repulsion" problem in pion-nucleus physics (Batty et al., 1997; Konijn et al., 1990) and provides the theoretical justification for interpreting the enhanced isovector parameter $b_1$ observed in deeply bound $\pi^-$ states in $^{205}$Pb (Geissel et al., 2002) as evidence for chiral symmetry restoration at an effective density of $0.6\rho_0$. The results enable future global fits of pionic atom data to use the conventional Ericson-Ericson potential form while correctly interpreting the fitted parameters as effective values at $\rho_e \sim 0.6\rho_0$, and allow extraction of the chiral order parameter reduction at full nuclear density ($R \sim 0.63$) from the measured value at the effective density ($R = 0.78^{+0.13}_{-0.09}$).
