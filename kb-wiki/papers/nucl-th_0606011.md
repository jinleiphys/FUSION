# Sub-barrier Fusion Cross Sections with Energy Density Formalism

> We discuss the applicability of the energy density formalism (EDF) for heavy-ion fusion reactions at sub-barrier energies. For this purpose, we calculate the fusion excitation function and the fusion barrier distribution for the reactions of $^{16}$O with $^{154,}$$^{144}$Sm,$^{186}$W and $^{208}$Pb with the coupled-channels method. We also discuss the effect of saturation property on the fusion cross section for the reaction between two $^{64}$Ni nuclei, in connection to the so called steep fall-off phenomenon of fusion cross sections at deep sub-barrier energies.

## Key claim

The energy density formalism (EDF) using Skyrme functionals can reproduce experimental fusion excitation functions and barrier distributions for several heavy-ion reactions at sub-barrier energies, but only after introducing an overall scaling factor to the potential; the formalism also yields a much shallower potential pocket than the standard double folding model, which may explain the steep fall-off phenomenon at deep sub-barrier energies.

## Method

The authors constructed internuclear potentials using the energy density formalism with the Skyrme energy functional, employing the frozen density approximation and the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation for kinetic energy and spin-orbit densities. They performed full-order coupled-channels calculations with the CCFULL code for reactions of 16O with 154Sm, 144Sm, 186W, and 208Pb, and single-channel calculations for 64Ni+64Ni, using three different Skyrme parameter sets (SkM*, SIII, and SGI). The EDF potentials were normalized by an overall scaling factor to reproduce experimental fusion cross sections at high energies, then simulated with Woods-Saxon forms for the coupled-channels calculations.

## Key numbers

- For 16O+144Sm: normalization factor N=1.07, Woods-Saxon parameters V0=66.57 MeV, r0=1.140 fm, a=0.74 fm, Coulomb barrier height VB=61.73 MeV
- For 16O+154Sm: N=1.31, V0=82.66 MeV, r0=1.144 fm, a=0.75 fm, VB=59.54 MeV; deformation parameters β2=0.33, β4=0.035, β6=0.033
- For 16O+186W: N=1.37, V0=86.86 MeV, r0=1.152 fm, a=0.73 fm, VB=69.02 MeV; deformation parameters β2=0.335, β4=-0.045, β6=0.018
- For 16O+208Pb: N=1.47, V0=95.20 MeV, r0=1.150 fm, a=0.74 fm, VB=74.73 MeV
- Surface diffuseness parameter a was around 0.7 fm for all systems, similar to the double folding model result
- For 144Sm: excitation energies E2=1.66 MeV, E3=1.81 MeV; deformation parameters β2=0.11, β3=0.205
- For 208Pb: E3=2.615 MeV, β3=0.161; E5=3.928 MeV, β5=0.056
- For 64Ni+64Ni: the potential minimum energy was about 80 MeV, comparable to the energy ES~87 MeV where the steep fall-off begins
- The double folding model with M3Y force gave nuclear potential depth of -2500 MeV and total potential depth of -2250 MeV for 64Ni+64Ni, much deeper than the EDF results
- Three Skyrme parameter sets used for 64Ni+64Ni: SIII (K∞=355.4 MeV), SGI (K∞=269 MeV), and SkM* (K∞=216.7 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the energy density formalism by Brueckner et al. (1968) and Brink and Stancu (1978-1980) by applying it to full-order coupled-channels calculations for heavy-ion fusion at sub-barrier energies, rather than only to elastic scattering or simplified calculations. The results directly address the steep fall-off phenomenon reported by Jiang et al. (2002) for 64Ni+64Ni, and the EDF potential minimum is comparable to that obtained by Misicu and Esbensen (2004) who modified the double folding model with a repulsive term. The work enables future studies of the adiabaticity of fusion reactions and the role of the Pauli principle in the frozen density approximation.
