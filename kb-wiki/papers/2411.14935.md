# Ab initio study of the beryllium isotopes $^{7}$Be to $^{12}$Be

> We present a systematic ab initio study of the low-lying states in beryllium isotopes from 7Be to 12Be using nuclear lattice effective field theory with the N3LO interaction. Our calculations achieve good agreement with experimental data for energies, radii, and electromagnetic properties. We introduce a novel, model-independent method to quantify nuclear shapes, uncovering a distinct pattern in the interplay between positive and negative parity states across the isotopic chain. By combining Monte Carlo sampling of the many-body density operator with a novel nucleon-grouping algorithm, the prominent two-center cluster structures, the emergence of one-neutron halo, complex nuclear molecular dynamics such as π orbital and σ orbital, emerge naturally.

## Key claim

Using nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with the N3LO interaction, the authors achieve good agreement with experimental data for energies, radii, and electromagnetic properties across the beryllium isotopic chain from 7Be to 12Be, but note that numerical challenges such as Euclidean time extrapolation and finite volume effects become more pronounced for excited states, making it more difficult to maintain the same level of accuracy as for ground states.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio lattice calculations using nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with a next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) chiral interaction and a simpler SU(4)-symmetric interaction, defined on spatial lattices with spacings of 1.32 fm and 1.64 fm respectively. They employed the wavefunction matching method to mitigate the Monte Carlo sign problem, and used the pinhole algorithm with Monte Carlo sampling of the many-body density operator combined with a novel nucleon-grouping algorithm to extract nuclear shapes, cluster structures, and molecular orbital configurations from the full A-body wave function.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of 11Be: E(1/2+) = -64.6(1) MeV and E(1/2-) = -64.1(1) MeV using the SU(4)-symmetric interaction, compared to experimental values of -65.5 MeV and -65.2 MeV respectively
- Charge radii and point matter radii for all isotopes agree with experimental values within approximately 6%
- Quadrupole moment of 9Be: Q(3/2-) = 7.4(1.0) e fm2 with N3LO interaction, compared to experimental value 5.29(4) e fm2
- B(E2) transition in 10Be: 2_1+ to 0_1+ = 8.5(9) e2 fm4 with N3LO interaction, compared to experimental value 9.2(3) e2 fm4
- B(E1) transition in 11Be: 1/2- to 1/2+ = 0.038(3) e2 fm2 with N3LO interaction, compared to experimental value 0.102(2) e2 fm2
- B(E2) transition in 12Be: 2_1+ to 0_1+ = 9.0(3.1) e2 fm4 with N3LO interaction, compared to experimental value 14.2(1.0)(2.0) e2 fm4

## Context

This work extends previous NLEFT studies of the Hoyle state in 12C (Epelbaum et al., 2011, 2012) and geometric configurations of the 12C spectrum (Shen et al., 2022), and provides the first systematic ab initio study covering energies, radii, electromagnetic properties, and geometric structures across the beryllium isotopes from 7Be to 12Be. The results enable direct comparison with cluster model approaches such as Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (Kanada-En'yo, 2015), Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (Krieger et al., 2012), and the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke wave function approach (Lyu et al., 2021), and the model-independent shape quantification method introduced here can be applied to other light nuclei to study clustering and molecular orbital structures from first principles.
