# Consistent analysis of neutral- and charged-current neutrino scattering off carbon

> Background: Good understanding of the cross sections for (anti)neutrino scattering off nuclear targets in the few-GeV energy region is a prerequisite for correct interpretation of results of ongoing and planned oscillation experiments. Purpose: Clarify possible source of disagreement between recent measurements of the cross sections on carbon. Method: Nuclear effects in (anti)neutrino scattering off carbon nucleus are described using the spectral function approach. The effect of two- and multi-nucleon final states is accounted for by applying an effective value of the axial mass, fixed to 1.23 GeV. Neutral-current elastic (NCE) and charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) processes are treated on equal footing. Results: The differential and total cross sections for the energy ranging from a few hundreds of MeV to 100 GeV are obtained and compared to the available data from the BNL E734, MiniBooNE, and NOMAD experiments. Conclusions: Nuclear effects in NCE and CCQE scattering seem to be very similar. Within the spectral function approach, the axial mass from the shape analysis of the MiniBooNE data is in good agreement with the results reported by the BNL E734 and NOMAD Collaborations. However, the combined analysis of NCE and CCQE data does not seem to support the contribution of multi-nucleon final states being large enough to explain the normalization of the MiniBooNE-reported cross sections.

# Consistent analysis of neutral- and charged-current neutrino scattering off carbon

## Key claim
Nuclear effects in neutral-current elastic (NCE) and charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) scattering off carbon appear very similar, and the axial mass extracted from the shape of MiniBooNE CCQE data agrees with results from BNL E734 and NOMAD when using the spectral function approach. However, the combined NCE and CCQE analysis does not support a multi-nucleon final state contribution large enough to explain the normalization of the MiniBooNE-reported cross sections.

## Method
The author describes neutrino-nucleus interactions using the impulse approximation with the spectral function approach for carbon, employing realistic hole spectral functions obtained in the local density approximation that combine shell structure from experimental (e,e') data with correlation contributions from uniform nuclear matter calculations. The effect of two- and multi-nucleon final states is accounted for by applying an effective axial mass value of 1.23 GeV, determined from the MiniBooNE shape analysis of the reconstructed Q^2 distribution. NCE and CCQE processes are treated on equal footing, with off-shell effects accounted for using the de Forest approximation, and the differential and total cross sections are computed for neutrino energies from a few hundred MeV to 100 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Effective axial mass value fixed to 1.23 GeV to account for two- and multi-nucleon final states
- The difference between proton and neutron removal energies in carbon is Δ = 2.76 MeV
- Neglecting the structure function W̃4 increases the total ¹²C(ν,ν') cross section by 1.4% at Eν = 450 MeV, 3.0% at 100 MeV, and 2.0% at 5 GeV
- The Fermi constant used is GF = 1.16637 × 10⁻⁵/GeV²
- The weak mixing angle is sin²θW = 0.23116
- The axial coupling constant is gA = -1.2673
- The strange quark contribution to the axial form factor is set to Δs = -0.08
- The Cabibbo angle corresponds to cosθC = 0.97418
- The world average axial mass for free nucleons is 1.03 GeV
- The BNL E734 experiment had mean neutrino energy 1.3 GeV and antineutrino energy 1.2 GeV, with 79% of protons bound in carbon and aluminium and 21% free protons
- The BNL E734 normalization uncertainty is 11.2% for neutrinos and 10.4% for antineutrinos
- The MiniBooNE average neutrino energy is 788 MeV
- The MiniBooNE CCQE event normalization is higher by a factor of 1.21 ± 0.24 than the calculated one
- At neutrino energy of 100 GeV, 89.8% of the ¹²C(νμ,μ⁻) cross section comes from momentum transfers |q| ≤ 1.645 GeV, and 97.5% from |q| ≤ 2.747 GeV
- Final state interactions are expected to modify differential cross sections dσ/dQ² at Q² ≲ 0.15 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the spectral function approach previously applied to NCE scattering off oxygen (Ankowski, 2012, in the context of neutrino-induced gamma-ray production) to the carbon target, and extends earlier CCQE results presented with arbitrary normalization (Ankowski, Benhar, and Farina, 2010). The paper addresses the discrepancy between MiniBooNE and NOMAD cross section measurements on carbon, which has been analyzed by various groups including Martini et al. (using random-phase approximation with two-particle-two-hole and three-particle-three-hole processes), Nieves et al. (using effective interactions from photon, electron, and pion scattering data), Amaro et al. (relativistic mean-field approach), Benhar et al. (spectral function with adjusted axial mass), and Meucci et al. (relativistic Green's function model). The results enable cross-checks between NCE and CCQE channels to constrain nuclear effects and multi-nucleon contributions in neutrino oscillation experiments.
