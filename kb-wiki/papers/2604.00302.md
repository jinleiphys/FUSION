# Fluid Acceleration in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the generation and space-time evolution of fluid acceleration in heavy-ion collisions using AMPT and UrQMD transport models combined with a Gaussian smearing method. The peak proper acceleration reaches several hundred MeV, with mild model dependence. Transverse acceleration points outward and is strongest at the fireball boundary due to steep pressure gradients and low enthalpy density--a persistent feature even at early times and low energies. Longitudinal acceleration shows strong collision-energy dependence: low-energy collisions exhibit early deceleration from nuclear stopping, while ultra-relativistic collisions produce sharp acceleration pulses from passing nuclei. The volume-averaged acceleration is nearly centrality independent, as extreme acceleration localizes at boundaries. These strong acceleration fields may have important implications for QGP physics, including the Unruh effect mimicking a thermal bath, potential influences on the chiral phase transition and deconfinement, and contributions to spin polarization beyond vorticity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that fluid acceleration in heavy-ion collisions reaches peak proper accelerations of several hundred MeV, with the transverse component concentrated at the fireball boundary due to steep pressure gradients and low enthalpy density, while the longitudinal component exhibits strong collision-energy dependence, a feature that persists even at early times and low energies, contrary to what the abstract's phrasing about "mild model dependence" might suggest.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT and UrQMD transport models to simulate Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions across a range of energies, extracting the fluid 4-velocity field via a Gaussian smearing method applied to the energy-momentum tensor. The 4-acceleration was then computed from the velocity field using the definition a^μ = u^ν ∂_ν u^μ, with an energy density threshold of ε_c = 50 MeV/fm³ applied to isolate the hot dense region. For AMPT simulations at higher energies (62.4 GeV, 200 GeV, 2.76 TeV), a scaling factor K was applied to the energy-momentum tensor to reproduce observed particle multiplicities, while UrQMD simulations at lower energies (up to 27 GeV) used K=1.

## Key numbers

- Peak proper acceleration reaches several hundred MeV, with transverse components reaching ~150 MeV for √s = 200 GeV and ~50 MeV for √s = 7.7 GeV at later times
- Longitudinal acceleration at √s = 7.7 GeV exceeds 200 MeV at early times due to baryon stopping
- Energy density threshold of ε_c = 50 MeV/fm³ was used to define the fireball region
- Gaussian smearing widths σ_⊥ = σ_z = 0.6 fm were used for both AMPT and UrQMD models
- Scaling factors K = 1.35, 1.45, and 1.6 were used for AMPT at √s = 62.4 GeV, 200 GeV, and 2.76 TeV respectively
- 200 events were simulated per impact parameter and energy below 27 GeV (UrQMD), 1000 events for 62.4 GeV and above (AMPT)

## Context

This work extends the study of fluid acceleration in heavy-ion collisions beyond the PHSD framework used by Prokhorov (2025), which examined Au+Au collisions at √s = 4.5–11.5 GeV and found Unruh temperatures exceeding thermodynamic temperatures. The paper provides systematic AMPT and UrQMD calculations across a wider energy range (3.5 GeV to 2.76 TeV) and connects to the broader literature on vorticity-induced spin polarization (Becattini 2013, Wu 2019, Florkowski 2019) and the Unruh effect in QGP (Kharzeev 2005). These results enable future studies of acceleration effects on the chiral phase transition, deconfinement, and spin polarization beyond vorticity contributions.
