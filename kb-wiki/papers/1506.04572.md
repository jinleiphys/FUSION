# R-mode constraints from neutron star equation of state

> The gravitational radiation has been proposed a long time before, as an explanation for the observed relatively low spin frequencies of young neutron stars and of accreting neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries as well. In the present work we studied the effects of the neutron star equation of state on the r-mode instability window of rotating neutron stars. Firstly, we employed a set of analytical solution of the Tolman-Oppemheimer-Volkoff equations with special emphasis on the Tolman VII solution. In particular, we tried to clarify the effects of the bulk neutron star properties (mass, radius, density distribution, crust size and elasticity) on the r-mode instability window. We found that the critical angular velocity $Ω_c$ depends mainly on the neutron star radius. The effects of the gravitational mass and the mass distribution are almost negligible. Secondly, we studied the effect of the elasticity of the crust, via to the slippage factor $S$ and also the effect of the nuclear equation of state, via the slope parameter $L$, on the instability window. We found that the crust effects are more pronounced, compared to those originated from the equation of state. Moreover, we proposed simple analytical expressions which relate the macroscopic quantity $Ω_c$ to the radius, the parameter $L$ and the factor ${\cal S}$. We also investigated the possibility to measure the radius of a neutron star and the factor ${\cal S}$ with the help of accurate measures of $Ω_c$ and the neutron star temperature. Finally, we studied the effects of the mutual friction on the instability window and discussed the results in comparison with previous similar studies.

## Key claim

The critical angular velocity Ω_c for r-mode instability depends mainly on the neutron star radius, while the effects of gravitational mass and mass distribution are almost negligible; crust elasticity effects (via the slippage factor S) are more pronounced than those originating from the nuclear equation of state (via the slope parameter L).

## Method

The authors employed analytical solutions of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, with special emphasis on the Tolman VII solution, to study the r-mode instability window of rotating neutron stars. They computed the critical angular velocity Ω_c by solving the equilibrium equation where gravitational radiation driving balances various dissipation mechanisms (bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, boundary layer damping at the crust-core interface, and mutual friction). They then derived simple analytical expressions relating Ω_c to the neutron star radius, the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy, and the slippage factor S describing the elastic response of the crust.

## Key numbers

- The critical angular velocity Ω_c depends mainly on the neutron star radius R, with the effects of gravitational mass M and mass distribution being almost negligible.
- The crust-core interface density is taken as ρ_cr = 1.5 × 10^14 g cm^{-3}.
- The mutual friction damping time scale τ̃_MF is treated as a phenomenological parameter varying in the range 5 s ≤ τ̃_MF ≤ 10^4 s.
- The Kepler angular velocity is approximately Ω_K = (2/3)Ω_0, where Ω_0 = √(πGρ̄) and ρ̄ = 3M/(4πR^3) is the mean density.
- The coefficient C(n_s, 1.4 M_⊙) relating radius to pressure at saturation density is 9.52 ± 0.49 km, based on the revised value from Lattimer and Lim excluding EOS that violate the maximum mass constraint from PSR J1614-2230.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of r-mode instabilities (Andersson 1998, Friedman 1998, Owen 1998, Bildsten 2000) by systematically using analytical TOV solutions to separate the effects of bulk neutron star properties from crust elasticity and nuclear EOS parameters. The authors build on the empirical relation between neutron star radius and pressure found by Lattimer and Prakash (2001), and on the revised radius-pressure correlation by Lattimer and Lim (2013) that incorporates the maximum mass constraint from PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010). The results enable future observational constraints on the neutron star radius and the slippage factor S from accurate measurements of Ω_c and neutron star temperature, and also connect macrophysics (mass, radius, critical angular velocity) to microscopic nuclear physics (the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy).
