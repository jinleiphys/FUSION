# Baryon Current Matrix Elements in a Light-Front Framework

> Current matrix elements and observables for electro- and photo-excitation of baryons from the nucleon are studied in a light-front framework. Relativistic effects are estimated by comparison to a nonrelativistic model, where we use simple basis states to represent the baryon wavefunctions. Sizeable relativistic effects are found for certain transitions, for example, to radial excitations such as that conventionally used to describe to the Roper resonance. A systematic study shows that the violation of rotational covariance of the baryon transition matrix elements stemming from the use of one-body currents is generally small.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in baryon electroexcitation amplitudes are sizeable for certain transitions, particularly for radial excitations such as the Roper resonance, but the violation of rotational covariance from using one-body currents is generally small across the transitions studied.

## Method

The authors used light-front relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics to calculate current matrix elements for electro- and photo-excitation of baryons from the nucleon, expanding baryon wavefunctions in a simple harmonic-oscillator basis with unmixed oscillator states. They computed one-body current matrix elements for three constituent quarks with mass 220 MeV, using point-like quarks (F1q = F2q = 1), and performed six-dimensional numerical integrations using the adaptive Monte Carlo method VEGAS. Results were compared to a nonrelativistic model using the same wavefunctions but with quark mass 336 MeV and the standard nonrelativistic impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass used in relativistic calculation: m_u = m_d = 220 MeV
- Quark mass used in nonrelativistic calculation: m_u,d = 336 MeV
- Harmonic oscillator size parameter: alpha_HO = 0.41 GeV
- Statistical uncertainties from Monte Carlo integration: on the order of a few percent for the largest matrix elements
- The relativistic calculation yields a proton charge radius close to that found from the slope near Q^2 = 0 of the dipole fit to the data
- The nonrelativistic calculation falls off too rapidly at larger Q^2, like exp(-q^2 / 6 alpha_HO^2)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nucleon elastic form factors in light-cone models by Chung and Coester, Schlumpf, Weber, and Aznaurian, and extends those models to electroproduction amplitudes for the Delta(1232) resonance and higher-mass baryon states. The authors note that nonrelativistic quark models successfully describe photocoupling systematics but fail for electroproduction amplitudes, as shown by the proton charge radius being too small by a factor of almost two in simple nonrelativistic models (Isgur, 1978). The present work enables systematic study of relativistic effects in baryon transition form factors and provides a baseline for future calculations including two- and three-body currents and more realistic wavefunctions.
