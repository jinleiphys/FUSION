# New scenarios for hard-core interactions in a hadron resonance gas

> The equation of state of a baryon-symmetric hadronic matter with hard-sphere interactions is studied. It is assumed that mesons are point-like, but baryons and antibaryons have the same hard-core radius rB. Three possibilities are considered: 1) the baryon-baryon and antibaryon-baryon interactions are the same; 2) baryons do not interact with antibaryons; 3) the baryon-antibaryon and meson-(anti)baryon interactions are negligible. By choosing the parameter rB=0.3-0.6 fm, we calculate the nucleon to pion ratio as a function of temperature and perform the fit of hadron yields measured in central Pb+Pb collisions at the bombarding energy Ecm=2.76 TeV per nucleon pair. New nontrivial effects in the interacting hadron resonance gas at temperatures 150-200 MeV are found.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nucleon-to-pion ratio in a baryon-symmetric hadron resonance gas with hard-core interactions is a non-monotonic function of temperature with a maximum in the range 150-200 MeV, and that this ratio is sensitive to whether baryon-antibaryon repulsion is included or omitted, contrary to the implicit assumption in earlier excluded volume models that baryon-baryon and baryon-antibaryon interactions are identical.

## Method

The authors model a hadron resonance gas containing all known baryons, antibaryons, and mesons with masses below 2.6 GeV, assuming mesons are point-like while all (anti)baryons share the same hard-core radius r_B. They compare three interaction scenarios: (1) identical BB and B-antibaryon repulsion, (2) no baryon-antibaryon interaction, and (3) negligible B-antibaryon, meson-baryon, and meson-antibaryon interactions. The equation of state is computed using the Carnahan-Starling approximation for the compressibility factor, and final hadron yields include feeding from strong decays of resonances using THERMUS decay tables.

## Key numbers

- Hard-core radius parameter r_B = 0.3-0.6 fm is used for baryons and antibaryons
- Calculations are performed for temperatures T = 150-200 MeV at baryon chemical potential mu_B = 0
- The N/pi ratio maximum shifts from about 350 to 250 MeV when r_N increases from 0.3 to 0.5 fm in the simple N-antiN-pi system
- Fits are performed to hadron yields from central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair
- The maximum of the N/pi ratio in the full hadron gas calculation occurs at T less than or approximately 200 MeV
- Packing fractions for (anti)nucleons in the N-antiN-pi matter at mu_B=0 are eta = 2 n_N v_N less than or approximately 0.1

## Context

This work extends the excluded volume approach of Yen et al. (1997) and the Carnahan-Starling approximation applied by Satarov et al. (2015) by introducing for the first time a possible difference between BB and B-antibaryon short-range repulsion in a multi-component hadron resonance gas. It builds on earlier thermal models by Stoecker (1981, 1987), Cleymans (1993), and Becattini (2001) that assumed ideal gas equations of state, and on the non-diagonal eigenvolume model of Gorenstein et al. (1999). The results enable improved fits to ALICE data from LHC Pb+Pb collisions and provide a framework for comparing hadron resonance gas predictions with lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state at zero baryon chemical potential.
