# Correction to the energy spectrum of $^1S_0$ heavy quarkonia due to two-gluon annihilation effect

> In this work, the non-relativistic asymptotic behavior of the transition $q\overline{q}\rightarrow2g\rightarrow q\overline{q}$ in the $^1S_0$ channel is discussed. Different with the usual calculation which expands the physical amplitude around the quark anti-quark threshold, we take the quark anti-quark pairs as off shell and only expand the expression on the three-dimensional momenta of the quarks and anti-quarks. We calculate the results to order 6. The imagine part of the results after applying the on shell conditions can reproduce the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) results in leading order of $α_s$. The real part of the results can be used to estimate the mass shift of the $^1S_0$ heavy quark anti-quark system due to the $2g$ annihilation effect. The results can also be used to estimate the energy shifts of the positronium system due to the two-photon annihilation.

## Key claim

The paper derives the non-relativistic asymptotic expansion of the two-gluon annihilation transition \(q\overline{q}\rightarrow 2g\rightarrow q\overline{q}\) in the \(^1S_0\) channel by treating the quark-antiquark pairs as off shell and expanding only in three-dimensional momenta, rather than expanding around the physical threshold as is usual. This off-shell treatment allows the real part of the result to estimate the mass shift of heavy quarkonia due to two-gluon annihilation, while the imaginary part reproduces the leading-order NRQCD decay width after on-shell conditions are applied.

## Method

The authors compute the Feynman diagrams for \(q\overline{q}\rightarrow 2g\rightarrow q\overline{q}\) in the \(^1S_0\) channel using the Bethe-Salpeter interaction kernel, with the quark-antiquark pairs taken off shell and momenta chosen in the center-of-mass frame under the instantaneous approximation (\(P\cdot p_i = P\cdot p_f = 0\)). They expand the expression in the three-dimensional momenta \(|\textbf{p}_i|, |\textbf{p}_f|\) and \(\textbf{p}_i\cdot\textbf{p}_f\) to order 6, perform the loop integrals using Feyncalc and FIESTA, and then apply angular averaging to project onto the \(^1S_0\) state. The resulting coefficients are expressed as functions of \(r_s = \sqrt{s}/2m\) and the momenta, and the on-shell limit is taken by setting \(|\textbf{p}_i|^2 = |\textbf{p}_f|^2 = p^2\).

## Key numbers

* The color factor for the two-gluon annihilation is \(c_f^{(2g)} = 2/3\).
* The imaginary part of the leading coefficient after on-shell conditions is \(\textrm{Im}[c_0^{(on)}] = 2\pi\).
* The real part of the leading coefficient after on-shell conditions is \(\textrm{Re}[c_0^{(on)}] = 4(1-\log 2)\).
* For \(\eta_c\), the estimated mass shift from the leading-order real part is approximately \(-3.1\) MeV, using \(\Gamma(\eta_c)\) and the approximation \(r_s(\eta_c) \approx 1\).
* For \(\eta_b\), the estimated mass shift is approximately \(-6.1\) MeV, using \(\Gamma(\eta_b)\) and the approximation \(r_s(\eta_b) \approx 1\).
* For \(\eta_c(2S)\), the estimated mass shift is approximately \(1.0\) MeV, using \(0.09 \times \Gamma(\eta_c(2S))\).

## Context

This work extends the standard NRQCD treatment of two-gluon annihilation (e.g., the operator formalism of Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage) by keeping the quark-antiquark pairs off shell, which allows a consistent calculation of both the imaginary part (decay width) and the real part (mass shift) from the same interaction kernel. The results enable quantitative estimates of the mass shift of \(^1S_0\) heavy quarkonia (such as \(\eta_c\) and \(\eta_b\)) due to annihilation effects, and can also be applied to positronium by replacing the coupling and color factors. The approach contrasts with earlier phenomenological estimates in the quark model (e.g., Barnes 2008) which gave mass shifts of about 600 MeV from decay channels, whereas this perturbative calculation yields shifts on the order of a few MeV.
