# Determination of the Equation of State from Nuclear Experiments and Neutron Star Observations

> With recent advances in neutron star observations, major progress has been made in determining the pressure of neutron star matter at high density. This pressure is constrained by the neutron star deformability, determined from gravitational waves emitted in a neutron-star merger, and measurements of radii of two neutron stars, using a new X-ray observatory on the International Space Station. Previous studies have relied on nuclear theory calculations to provide the equation of state at low density. Here we use a combination of 15 constraints composed of three astronomical observations and twelve nuclear experimental constraints that extend over a wide range of densities. Bayesian Inference is then used to obtain a comprehensive nuclear equation of state. This data-centric result provides benchmarks for theoretical calculations and modeling of nuclear matter and neutron stars. Furthermore, it provides insights on the composition of neutron stars and their cooling via neutrino radiation.

## Key claim
The paper claims that combining 12 nuclear experimental constraints with 3 astronomical observations in a Bayesian analysis yields a comprehensive nuclear equation of state (EOS) that is data-centric and does not rely on nuclear theory calculations at low density, unlike previous studies. The hedge is that this approach is valid for densities from about half to three times saturation density (0.5n0 to 3n0), and the authors assume nucleonic matter with no sharp phase transitions in the neutron star core.

## Method
The authors parameterize the nuclear EOS as the energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter plus a symmetry energy term, using Taylor expansion coefficients. They apply Bayesian inference to simultaneously determine these coefficients from 15 discrete constraints: 12 from nuclear experiments (including heavy-ion collision flow data, nuclear masses, dipole polarizability, neutron skin measurements, and pion ratios) and 3 from astronomical observations (tidal deformability from GW170817 and radii of two pulsars from NICER). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for each EOS in the prior distribution to calculate neutron star properties for comparison with astronomical data.

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of the nuclear EOS by Danielewicz (2002), Tsang (2009, 2012), and the "decoding" collaboration (2022) by adding three astronomical constraints from LIGO/Virgo (GW170817) and NICER (Riley 2019, Miller 2019, Riley 2021, Miller 2021). It contrasts with studies by Legred (2021) that used only astronomical constraints with non-parametric EOS priors, and with Nature (2022) that incorporated chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) calculations at low density and heavy-ion flow data. The data-centric approach enables benchmarking of microscopic nuclear theories like chiEFT (Drischler 2019, 2016) and provides predictions for neutron star composition and the onset of direct Urca cooling, which informs understanding of neutron star thermal evolution and supernova collapse.
