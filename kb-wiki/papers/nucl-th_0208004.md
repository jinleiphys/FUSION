# Time Structure of Anomalous J/psi and psi' suppression in nuclear collisions

> The data for the mean squared transverse momentum P_t as function of transverse energy E_t of J/Psi and Psi' produced in Pb-Pb collisions at the CERN-SPS are analyzed and it is claimed that they contain information about the time structure of anomalous suppression. A transport equation which describes transverse motion of J/Psi and Psi' in the absorptive medium is proposed and solved for a QGP and a comover scenario of suppression. While the comover approach accounts for the data fairly well without adjusting any parameter, the fit to the data within the QGP scenario requires to assume anomalous suppression to become effective rather late, 3-4 fm/c after the nuclear overlap.

# Time Structure of Anomalous J/ψ and ψ' Suppression in Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The mean squared transverse momentum ⟨p²t⟩(Et) of J/ψ and ψ' produced in Pb-Pb collisions at the CERN-SPS contains information about the time structure of anomalous suppression, but the threshold (QGP) scenario requires anomalous suppression to act 3–4 fm/c after nuclear overlap to fit the data, while the comover scenario accounts for the data without adjusting any additional parameter.

## Method
A transport equation describing free streaming of J/ψ and ψ' with transverse velocity and an absorptive term was solved analytically for two scenarios: (I) a threshold (QGP) model where suppression acts when participant density exceeds a critical value, with suppression delayed to a time tA; and (II) a comover model where absorption by comoving partons/hadrons acts continuously with a 1/t density dependence from longitudinal expansion. The calculated ⟨p²t⟩(Et) was compared to CERN-SPS Pb-Pb collision data for both J/ψ and ψ'.

## Key numbers
- For J/ψ in the threshold model: critical participant density nc = 3.75 fm⁻²; absorption cross section σabs = 6.4 mb; ⟨p²t⟩NN = 1.11 (GeV/c)²; gluon rescattering parameter agN = 0.081 (GeV/c²) fm⁻¹
- For ψ' in the threshold model: critical participant density nc = 2.3 fm⁻²; absorption cross section σabs = 7 mb
- In the threshold model, tA ≤ 2 fm/c for Et < 80 GeV; tA ≈ 2.5 fm/c for 80 ≤ Et ≤ 100 GeV; tA ≈ 3.5 fm/c for Et > 100 GeV
- In the comover model: mean absorption time ⟨tA⟩ = 3.5 fm/c for J/ψ and 3.0 fm/c for ψ', independent of Et
- Comover model parameters: σco = 1 mb for J/ψ, σco = 3 mb for ψ'; σabs = 4.5 mb for J/ψ, σabs = 6 mb for ψ'; start time t0 = 1 fm/c; final density nf = 1 fm⁻²

## Context
This work extends the analysis of anomalous J/ψ suppression discovered in 1996 at CERN-SPS, which had been modeled both with QGP (Blaizot et al., 2000) and comover (Capella et al., 2000; Kharzeev et al., 2000) scenarios. The leakage effect, where high-pt charmonia escape the absorbing medium, was proposed more than 10 years earlier but had not been systematically incorporated into these models. The results enable distinguishing between suppression mechanisms by using ⟨p²t⟩ data as a timing probe, and suggest that future work should investigate pt-dependent absorption and transverse plasma expansion.
