# Two-Meson and Multi-Pion Final States from 600 Gev Pion Interactions

> This report describes the transitions pion -> meson_1 + meson_2 and also pion -> multi-pion for high energy pions interacting with target nuclei (Z,A). The physics interests are: A) Nuclear inelastic coherent diffraction cross sections for pions, for studies of size fluctuations in the pion wave function. B) Radiative widths of excited meson states, for tests of vector dominance and quark models. C) Experimental determination of the pi- + rho -> pi- + gamma total reaction rate for gamma production above 0.7 GeV, needed for background studies of quark-gluon plasma formation experiments. D) Investigation of the gamma ->3 pi vertex in pion pair production by a pion, for a significantly improved test of the hypothesis of chiral anomalies. The physics interest and associated bibliography are summarized here; with particular reference to the 200-600 GeV beams available at FNAL and CERN. Complementary GEANT simulations and trigger studies are needed.

# Two-Meson and Multi-Pion Final States from 600 GeV Pion Interactions

## Key claim
The paper argues that improved measurements of soft coherent diffractive dissociation and Primakoff production of two-meson and multi-pion final states at 200-600 GeV can test color fluctuation models of the pion wave function and chiral anomaly predictions, but notes that existing data for the chiral anomaly vertex F₃π disagree with the O(p⁴) chiral perturbation theory expectation by at least two standard deviations when using the updated pion decay constant f = 92.4 ± 0.2 MeV rather than the value used in prior analyses.

## Method
The author reviews four physics objectives for reactions induced by high-energy pions (200-600 GeV) on nuclear targets: (A) measurement of nuclear inelastic coherent diffraction cross sections to test color fluctuation models via the A-dependence of diffractive dissociation into multi-pion and two-meson final states; (B) determination of radiative widths of excited meson states (ρ, a₁(1260), a₂(1320), b₁(1235)) via Primakoff production; (C) measurement of the π⁻ + ρ → π⁻ + γ reaction rate via the inverse Primakoff process for quark-gluon plasma background studies; (D) investigation of the γ → 3π vertex via pion pair production in the nuclear Coulomb field to test chiral anomaly predictions. The paper discusses separation of Primakoff (Coulomb) and diffractive (pomeron exchange) contributions by fitting the t-distribution dσ/dt = F²_A(t)(ZC₁/√t + C₂ exp(at))², where the Coulomb term scales as Z² and the diffractive term follows an exponential form.

## Key numbers
- For the total diffractive cross section of pions on nuclei, FMS calculations predict σ^{π-A}_{diff}(A) ∝ A^{1.05} for A ∼ 16 and A^{0.65} for A ∼ 200.
- The predicted total diffractive cross section is 40-50 mb for π-nucleus and n-nucleus interactions at 200 GeV for A ∼ 100.
- The variance parameter ω_σ(p) ∼ 0.25 and ω_σ(π) ∼ 0.4 for incident momenta near 200 GeV/c.
- The part of the pion total cross section associated with small-size configurations (SSC) is roughly 2-5%, the integral of P(σ) for σ values less than 5 mb.
- For ρ → πγ, measured widths range from 60 to 81 keV.
- For a₁(1260) → πγ, the width is Γ = 640 ± 246 keV.
- For a₂(1320) → πγ, the width is Γ = 295 ± 60 keV.
- For b₁(1235) → πγ, the width is Γ = 230 ± 60 keV.
- The Coulomb cross section for the chiral anomaly reaction is about 0.06 μbarns for a ¹²C target.
- Antipov et al. measured F₃π = 12.9 ± 0.9 (stat) ± 0.5 (sys) GeV⁻³ at 40 GeV.
- Using f = 92.4 ± 0.2 MeV, the O(p⁴) chiral anomaly expectation is F₃π = 9.72 ± 0.06 GeV⁻³.
- Bijnens et al. give the lowest-order value of F₃π as 9.5 GeV⁻³ using f = 93.2 MeV, with higher-order corrections raising the prediction to roughly 11 GeV⁻³.
- The coherent region for three-pion production is defined as t < t* where t* = 0.4 A^{-2/3} GeV² for incident 200 GeV pions.
- For a target with A=208, the maximum nuclear recoil energy is E* = 30 keV.

## Context
This paper extends the theoretical framework of color fluctuations developed by Frankfurt, Miller, and Strikman (1993) and the Good-Walker formulation of diffraction, applying it to proposed measurements at FNAL E781 and other facilities. It directly builds on the experimental work of Zielinski et al. on three-pion diffractive production at 200 GeV and the Serpukhov experiment of Antipov et al. (1986) measuring the chiral anomaly vertex at 40 GeV. The author argues that the existing F₃π measurement disagrees with the O(p⁴) chiral perturbation theory prediction when using updated pion decay constants, motivating a higher-statistics measurement at 600 GeV where strong interaction backgrounds are negligible. The work enables future tests of whether higher-order chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bijnens et al. can resolve the discrepancy, and whether different diffractive channels show different A-dependences that could reveal sensitivity to small-size or large-size configurations in the pion wave function.
