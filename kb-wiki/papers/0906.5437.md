# Calculation of P,T-odd electric dipole moments for diamagnetic atoms $^{129}$Xe, $^{171}$Yb, $^{199}$Hg, $^{211}$Rn, and $^{225}$Ra

> Electric dipole moments of diamagnetic atoms of experimental interest are calculated using the relativistic Hartree-Fock and random-phase approximation methods, the many-body perturbation theory and configuration interaction technique. We consider P,T-odd interactions which give rise to atomic electric dipole moment in the second order of the perturbation theory. These include nuclear Schiff moment, P,T-odd electron-nucleon interaction and electron electric dipole moment. Interpretation of a new experimental constraint of a permanent electric dipole moment of $^{199}$Hg [W. C. Griffith {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 102}, 101601 (2009)] is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper calculates atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) for five diamagnetic atoms using several many-body methods, but the results depend on the specific P,T-odd interaction considered and the level of correlation included; the authors note that for atoms with two valence s electrons (Yb, Hg, Ra), the random-phase approximation corrections increase the EDM by factors of 2.5 to 5 compared to the Dirac-Hartree-Fock approximation, while for noble gases (Xe, Rn) the increase is only about 30%.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Hartree-Fock method followed by the random-phase approximation (RPA) for all five atoms, and for Yb, Hg, and Ra they additionally applied the configuration interaction combined with many-body perturbation theory (CI+MBPT) to treat the two valence electrons more accurately. They computed contributions from four P,T-odd mechanisms: tensor-pseudotensor and pseudoscalar-scalar electron-nucleon interactions, the nuclear Schiff moment, and the interaction of the electron EDM with the nuclear magnetic field. The calculations used two nuclear density models (uniform sphere and Fermi distribution) and two basis set methods (multiplied orbitals and B-splines) to check consistency.

## Key numbers

- For the tensor-pseudotensor electron-nucleon interaction (in units of $10^{-20} C_T \langle \bm{\sigma}_N \rangle |e|$ cm, RPA values): $^{129}$Xe: 0.57; $^{171}$Yb: -3.4; $^{199}$Hg: -5.9; $^{211}$Rn: 5.6; $^{225}$Ra: -17.
- For the pseudoscalar-scalar electron-nucleon interaction (in units of $10^{-23} C_P \langle \bm{\sigma}_N \rangle |e|$ cm, RPA values): $^{129}$Xe: 1.6; $^{171}$Yb: -11.5; $^{199}$Hg: -21.3; $^{211}$Rn: 21.0; $^{225}$Ra: -63.7.
- For the Schiff moment contribution (in units of $10^{-17} [S/(|e|\, \mathrm{fm}^3)]\, |e|$ cm, RPA values): $^{129}$Xe: 0.38; $^{171}$Yb: -1.9; $^{199}$Hg: -3.0; $^{211}$Rn: 3.3; $^{225}$Ra: -8.3.
- For the electron EDM contribution (in units of $d_e \times 10^{-4}$, RPA values): $^{129}$Xe: 1.0; $^{171}$Yb: 5.1; $^{199}$Hg: 12.5; $^{211}$Rn: -13; $^{225}$Ra: -55.
- The new experimental limit for $^{199}$Hg is $|d(^{199}\mathrm{Hg})| < 3.1 \times 10^{-29}\, |e|$ cm, an improvement by a factor of 7 over the previous limit.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Dzuba, Flambaum, and collaborators (2002, 2007) for Schiff moments and by Martensson-Pendrill (1985) for electron-nucleon interactions, providing a consistent set of EDM contributions for five isotopes of experimental interest. The results enable interpretation of the improved experimental limit on the $^{199}$Hg EDM reported by Griffith et al. (2009) in terms of constraints on CP-violating parameters, and provide predictions for ongoing experiments on $^{129}$Xe, $^{171}$Yb, $^{211}$Rn, and $^{225}$Ra. The authors note that a third-order contribution to the atomic EDM proportional to the electron EDM, which is actually larger than the second-order contribution discussed here, will be treated in a separate publication.
