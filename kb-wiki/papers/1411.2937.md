# Two- and three- dimensional few-body systems in the universal regime

> Macro properties of cold atomic gases are driven by few-body correlations, even if the gas has thousands of particles. Quantum systems composed of two and three particles with attractive zero\=/range pairwise interactions are considered for general masses and interaction strengths in two and three dimensions (2D and 3D). The Faddeev decomposition is used to derive the equations for the bound state, which is the starting point for the investigation of universal properties of few\=/body systems, i.e. those that all potentials with the same physics at low energy are able to describe in a model\=/independent form. In 2D, the number of bound states in a three\=/body system increases without bound as the mass of one particle becomes much lighter than the other two. The analytic form of an effective potential between the heavy particles explains the mass\=/dependence on the number of bound energy levels. An exact analytic expression for the large\=/momentum asymptotic behaviour of the spectator function in the Faddeev equation is presented. The spectator function and its asymptotic form define the two- and three\=/body contact parameters. The two\=/body parameter is found to be independent of the quantum state in some specific 2D systems. The 2D and 3D momentum distributions have a distinct sub\=/leading form whereas the 3D term depends on the mass of the particles. A model that interpolates between 2D and 3D is proposed and a sharp transition in the energy spectrum of three-body systems is found.

## Key claim

The paper claims that universal properties of two- and three-body systems with zero-range attractive interactions can be described in a model-independent form using the Faddeev decomposition, but the specific behavior of bound states and momentum distributions depends critically on the dimensionality (2D vs 3D) and the mass ratios of the particles, with the number of bound states in 2D increasing without bound as one particle becomes much lighter than the other two.

## Method

The author uses the Faddeev decomposition to derive bound-state equations for two and three particles with attractive zero-range pairwise interactions in two and three dimensions. The asymptotic large-momentum behavior of the spectator function in the Faddeev equation is derived analytically, and this asymptotic form is used to compute the one-body momentum density and the two- and three-body contact parameters. A model that interpolates between 2D and 3D is also proposed.

## Key numbers

- In 2D, the number of bound states in a three-body system increases without bound as the mass of one particle becomes much lighter than the other two (no specific numerical bound given, but the qualitative behavior is stated).
- For the special case of equal masses (A=1) in 3D, the sub-leading term in the momentum distribution from the n2 contribution is ⟨n2(qB)⟩ = -4π²|cAB|²/(√3 qB⁵).
- For the special case A=1 in 3D, the n3 contribution yields ⟨n3(qB)⟩ = 4π²|cAA|² cosh(sπ/6) / (qB⁵ √3 cosh(sπ/2)).
- The asymptotic form of the spectator function in 3D is given by |χAB(qA)|² = |cAB|³ qA⁻⁴/2 after averaging over the oscillatory term.

## Context

This work extends the universal few-body physics framework developed for cold atomic gases, where macro properties are driven by few-body correlations even in systems with thousands of particles. The Faddeev decomposition approach follows the standard methodology for few-body problems with zero-range interactions. The results enable future studies of the transition between 2D and 3D regimes in cold atomic systems, and the analytic expressions for contact parameters provide tools for connecting few-body correlations to macroscopic observables in ultracold gases.
