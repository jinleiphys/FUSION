# The pressure of strong coupling lattice QCD with heavy quarks, the hadron resonance gas model and the large N limit

> In this paper we calculate the pressure of pure lattice Yang-Mills theories and lattice QCD with heavy quarks by means of strong coupling expansions. Dynamical fermions are introduced with a hopping parameter expansion, which also allows for the incorporation of finite quark chemical potential. We show that in leading orders the results are in full agreement with expectations from the hadron resonance gas model, thus validating it with a first principles calculation. For pure Yang-Mills theories we obtain the corresponding ideal glueball gas, in QCD with heavy quarks our result equals that of an ideal gas of mesons and baryons. Another finding is that the Yang-Mills pressure in the large N limit is of order $\sim N^0$ to the calculated orders, when the inverse 't Hooft coupling is used as expansion parameter. This property is expected in the confined phase, where our calculations take place.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the leading-order strong coupling expansion of the pressure for pure Yang-Mills theories and for QCD with heavy quarks exactly reproduces the hadron resonance gas model predictions, but only in the confined phase and for sufficiently strong coupling (small \(\beta\)) where the series converges; corrections to the ideal gas behavior appear at higher orders.

## Method

The authors perform a combined strong coupling expansion in the inverse gauge coupling \(u\) and a hopping parameter expansion in \(\kappa\) (for heavy quarks) on the lattice, calculating the pressure as the difference between finite-temperature and zero-temperature contributions. For pure Yang-Mills, they sum over polymer diagrams consisting of tubes of fundamental plaquettes winding around the temporal direction, including inner decorations and slits, with group integrals enforcing representation constraints. For QCD with heavy quarks, they integrate out fermions exactly, expand the resulting determinant in powers of \(\kappa\), and keep only the leading Polyakov-loop-type contributions that survive after vacuum subtraction.

## Key numbers

* Leading-order pressure for pure \(SU(N)\) Yang-Mills: \(P = \frac{6}{L_\tau a^3} u^{4N_\tau} (1 + \mathcal{O}(u^2))\), where \(u\) is the fundamental character and expansion parameter.
* For \(SU(3)\) glueballs, masses extracted from the series: \(am_1(A) = -4\ln u - \ln(1+3u+6v_1+8w-18u^2) + \mathcal{O}(u^4)\), with similar expressions for \(m_2(E)\) and \(m_3(T)\).
* Pressure for \(SU(3)\) Yang-Mills expressed as glueball gas: \(P = \frac{1}{L_\tau a^3} [e^{-m_1(A)L_\tau} + 2e^{-m_2(E)L_\tau} + 3e^{-m_3(T)L_\tau}] (1 + \mathcal{O}(u^4))\).
* For QCD with two heavy quark flavours (up and down), pressure at leading order: \(P = \frac{1}{L_\tau a^3} \{4(2\kappa_u)^{2N_\tau} + 8(2\kappa_u 2\kappa_d)^{N_\tau} + 4(2\kappa_d)^{2N_\tau}\} + \frac{1}{L_\tau a^3} \{4(2\kappa_u)^{3N_\tau} + 6[(2\kappa_u)^2 2\kappa_d]^{N_\tau} + 6[2\kappa_u (2\kappa_d)^2]^{N_\tau} + 4(2\kappa_d)^{3N_\tau}\} (e^{3a\mu N_\tau} + e^{-3a\mu N_\tau})\).
* In the large-\(N\) limit, the pressure is of order \(\sim N^0\) when expressed in terms of the inverse 't Hooft coupling \(u = 1/\lambda\), with cancellations of all \(N\)-dependence up to \(\mathcal{O}(u^8)\).

## Context

This work provides a first-principles derivation of the hadron resonance gas model from lattice QCD in the strong-coupling regime, validating the phenomenological model of Karsch et al. (2003) and Meyer (2009) that describes the confined phase as an ideal gas of hadrons. The strong coupling series for the pressure can be used to fix the lower integration constant in the integral method for numerical lattice simulations, as used by Boyd et al. (1996). The large-\(N\) result extends the earlier \(SU(2)\) calculation by Langelage et al. (2009) and supports the expectation that the pressure in the confined phase is finite in the 't Hooft limit. Future work could incorporate \(\beta\) and \(\kappa\) corrections to study deviations from ideal gas behavior due to hadron interactions.
