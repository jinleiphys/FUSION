# Notes on New Narrow N*

> We briefly discuss the most recent evidences for narrow nucleon excitation with mass around 1680 MeV. The data show that the N* should have much stronger photocoupling to the neutron than to the proton. That makes it a good candidate for the anti-decuplet member.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a narrow nucleon excitation (N*) with mass around 1680 MeV, observed primarily in neutron rather than proton channels, is a good candidate for the predicted anti-decuplet member, but the evidence remains circumstantial and alternative explanations (such as threshold cusp effects) have not been fully ruled out.

## Method

The author reviews experimental data from four groups (GRAAL, CBELSA/TAPS, LNS, and Crystal Ball/TAPS) on eta photoproduction off neutrons and protons, as well as Compton scattering on neutrons. The analysis compares observed narrow structures in quasi-free neutron reactions with the absence of such structures in proton reactions, and evaluates the consistency of these observations with theoretical predictions for an anti-decuplet nucleon resonance based on SU(3) flavor symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the putative narrow N*: 1680 ± 15 MeV, extracted from neutron data (GRAAL, CBELSA/TAPS, LNS, Crystal Ball/TAPS, Compton scattering)
- Total width from Compton scattering on neutron: Γ = 28 ± 12 MeV at ~1685 MeV with ~4.6σ significance
- Total width from de-folded quasi-free eta photoproduction on neutron: Γ = 25 ± 12 MeV at W ~ 1670 MeV
- Upper limit on total width: Γ ≤ 40 MeV
- Upper limit on πN decay width: Γ_{πN} ≤ 0.5 MeV
- Photocoupling to neutron (scaled by branching ratio): √(Br_{ηN}) A_{1/2}^n = (12-18) × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- Photocoupling to proton (scaled by branching ratio): √(Br_{ηN}) A_{1/2}^p = (1-3) × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- From partial wave analysis including beam asymmetry data: mass ~1694 MeV, width ~40 MeV, photocoupling to proton ~2.6 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions from 1997-2004 for an anti-decuplet nucleon resonance (Polyakov and collaborators) and reviews experimental evidence from GRAAL, CBELSA/TAPS, LNS, and Crystal Ball/TAPS collaborations. The paper contrasts the narrow N* interpretation with alternative explanations such as the KY threshold cusp effects proposed by Doring (2009), which the author argues are inconsistent with flavor SU(3) and with the absence of the anomaly in pion photoproduction. Future experiments on double polarization neutron observables are needed to confirm the resonance interpretation.
