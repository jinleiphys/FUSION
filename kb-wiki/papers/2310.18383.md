# QCD equation of state and thermodynamic observables from computationally minimal Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> We study the QCD equation of state and other thermodynamic observables including the isentropic trajectories and the speed of sound. These observables are of eminent importance for the understanding of experimental results in heavy ion collisions and also provide a QCD input for studies of the timeline of heavy-ion-collisions with hydrodynamical simulations. They can be derived from the quark propagator whose gap equation is solved within a minimal approximation to the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD at finite temperature and density. This minimal approximation aims at a combination of computational efficiency and simplification of the truncation scheme while maintaining quantitative precision. This minimal DSE scheme is confronted and benchmarked with results for correlation functions and observables from first principles QCD lattice at vanishing density and quantitative functional approaches at finite density.

## Key claim

The authors present a "minimal Dyson-Schwinger Equation (miniDSE) scheme" that aims to reproduce quantitative QCD thermodynamics at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential with significantly reduced computational cost, but they explicitly note that the simplified vertex truncation loses quantitative reliability for baryon chemical potentials with mu_B/T greater than about 3, including a 10 percent reduction in the estimated critical endpoint location compared to full functional QCD.

## Method

The authors solve the quark gap equation within a 2+1 flavour DSE framework at finite temperature and density, using a simplified quark-gluon vertex that retains only two of the twelve possible tensor structures (the classical tensor and the chiral symmetry breaking tensor). The gluon propagator is taken from parametrised fits to lattice and functional QCD data, and the vertex dressings are constructed from combinations of the quark propagator dressing functions and the ghost dressing function, with the coupling and quark masses fixed at an RG-scale of 15 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Light quark condensate at the lattice RG-scale of 2 GeV: Delta_l(mu_lat) = -(274.5 MeV)^3, compared to the functional precision result of (272.0 MeV)^3 from Gao (2021)
- Chiral limit light quark condensate at 2 GeV: Delta_{l,chi}(mu_lat) = -(273.9(8) MeV)^3, compared to the functional precision result (269.3(7) MeV)^3 from Gao (2021) and the lattice FLAG result (272(5) MeV)^3
- Pion decay constant estimated from Pagels-Stokar formula: f_pi = 94.7 MeV, consistent with the physical value of about 93 MeV
- Pion mass from Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation: m_pi = 140.4 MeV
- Gluon screening mass in vacuum: m_scr approximately 850 MeV, with an error of about 20 MeV
- Coupling parameters at RG-scale 15 GeV: alpha_s = 0.235, m_l = 3.0 MeV, m_s = 81 MeV

## Context

This work extends the functional QCD approach to thermodynamics developed in prior studies by Fu (2019), Gao (2020), and Gunkel (2021), which achieved convergent results for the chiral phase structure at finite density. The miniDSE scheme enables computation of the equation of state, isentropic trajectories, and speed of sound across a wide range of temperature and baryon chemical potential, providing input for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions. The results are benchmarked against lattice QCD computations at vanishing chemical potential from Borsanyi (2020) and the HotQCD collaboration (2018), and against functional QCD results at finite density from Gao (2020) and Fu (2019).
