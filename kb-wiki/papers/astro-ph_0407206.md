# Neutrino Interactions in the Outflow from Gamma-Ray Burst Accretion Disks

> We examine the composition of matter as it flows away from gamma ray burst accretion disks, in order to determine what sort of nucleosynthesis may occur. Since there is a large flux of neutrinos leaving the surface of the disk, the electron fraction of the outflowing material will change due to charged current neutrino interactions. We calculate the electron fraction in the wind using detailed neutrino fluxes from every point on the disk and study a range of trajectories and outflow conditions for several different accretion disk models. We find that low electron fractions, conducive to making r-process elements, only appear in outflows from disks with high accretion rates that have a significant region both of trapped neutrinos and antineutrinos. Disks with lower accretion rates that have only a significant region of trapped neutrinos can have outflows with very high electron fractions, whereas the lowest accretion rate disks with little trapping have outflow electrons fractions of closer to one half.

## Key claim

Low electron fractions (Ye < 0.3) conducive to r-process nucleosynthesis only appear in outflows from disks with high accretion rates that have a significant region where both neutrinos and antineutrinos are trapped; disks with lower accretion rates and only trapped neutrinos can produce very high electron fractions, while the lowest accretion rate disks with little trapping yield electron fractions closer to one half.

## Method

The authors parameterize outflow trajectories from gamma-ray burst accretion disks using a two-component approach: initially vertical (cylindrical) flow near the disk, transitioning to spherical flow at larger distances. They calculate the evolution of the electron fraction along these trajectories by summing charged-current neutrino and antineutrino capture rates from every point on the disk, using detailed neutrino fluxes from the disk models of DPN (2002) for high accretion rates and Popham et al. (1999) for lower rates, with the velocity parameterized as |u| = v_infinity (1 - R_0/R)^beta.

## Key numbers

- For high accretion rate disks (Mdot = 10 Msun/s, a=0) with slow acceleration (beta = 2.5) and s=20, the electron fraction reaches approximately 0.1
- For the same high accretion rate model with fast acceleration (beta = 0.8) and s=40, the electron fraction reaches 0.45
- For Mdot = 1 Msun/s, a=0, the neutrino surface is at 40 km and the antineutrino surface at 32 km, compared to 200 km and 140 km respectively for the Mdot = 10 Msun/s model
- For Mdot = 0.1 Msun/s, a=0.95, neutrino capture changes the electron fraction by up to 30% in the upward direction
- The outflow velocity parameter v_infinity ranges from 5 x 10^3 km/s to 5 x 10^4 km/s, with starting radii R_0 from 50 km to 600 km
- Entropy is taken as an input parameter in the range s=10 to s=40

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Pruet et al. (2004) and Surman and McLaughlin (2004) by calculating the electron fraction evolution in disk outflows using detailed neutrino fluxes from every point on the disk, rather than using simplified spherical models. The results enable future studies to determine which disk models and outflow conditions produce different types of nucleosynthesis, such as r-process or iron peak nuclei, and can be combined with future observations of emission lines from gamma-ray bursts to understand both nucleosynthesis and disk conditions.
