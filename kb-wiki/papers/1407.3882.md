# Mean-field potential effects on particle and antiparticle elliptic flows in the beam-energy scan program at RHIC

> The elliptic flow splitting between particles and their antiparticles has recently been observed by the STAR Collaboration in the beam-energy scan program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. In studies based on transport models, we have found that this splitting can be explained by the different mean-field potentials acting on particles and their antiparticles in the produced baryon-rich matter. In particular, we have shown that the experimentally measured relative elliptic flow difference can help constrain the vector coupling constant in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model used in describing the partonic stage of heavy-ion collisions. This information is useful for locating the critical point in the QCD phase diagram and thus understanding the phase structure of QCD.

# Mean-field potential effects on particle and antiparticle elliptic flows in the beam-energy scan program at RHIC

## Key claim

The elliptic flow ($v_2$) splitting between particles and their antiparticles observed by STAR in the beam-energy scan program can be explained by different mean-field potentials acting on particles versus antiparticles in baryon-rich matter, and the measured relative $v_2$ difference can constrain the vector coupling constant ratio $R_V = G_V/G$ in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to about $0.8 \pm 0.3$, which in turn suggests that the QCD critical point may disappear or be located at extremely low temperatures.

## Method

The authors extended the multiphase transport (AMPT) model with string melting to include hadronic mean-field potentials in the ART hadronic transport module (using test particles) and partonic mean-field potentials from a 3-flavor NJL model in the partonic phase. Hadronic potentials for nucleons, antinucleons, kaons, antikaons, and pions were calculated from relativistic mean-field and chiral effective Lagrangian approaches; partonic potentials for quarks and antiquarks were derived from the NJL Lagrangian with scalar and vector coupling terms. The partonic phase ended when the central cell energy density dropped to about 0.8 GeV/fm$^3$, and hadronization was described by a spatial coalescence model using quark Wigner functions.

## Key numbers

- Relative $v_2$ difference between $p$ and $\bar{p}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV: experimentally about 63%; with hadronic potentials only, the model gives qualitatively consistent sign and energy dependence.
- Relative $v_2$ difference between $K^+$ and $K^-$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV: experimentally about 13%; with hadronic potentials only, the model overestimates this value.
- Relative $v_2$ difference between $p$ and $\bar{p}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV: experimentally about 44%; at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 39$ GeV: about 12%.
- Relative $v_2$ difference between $K^+$ and $K^-$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV: experimentally about 3%; at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 39$ GeV: about 1%.
- The vector coupling constant ratio $R_V = G_V/G$ is constrained to $0.8 \pm 0.3$ by simultaneously reproducing the relative $v_2$ differences between $p$ and $\bar{p}$ and between $K^+$ and $K^-$ in mini-bias Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV.
- The partonic phase evolution ends when the effective mass of light quarks in the central cell increases to about 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Xu et al., 2012; Song et al., 2012; Xu et al., 2014; Ko et al., 2014) that first proposed mean-field potentials as the explanation for the $v_2$ splitting observed by STAR (2013). It contradicts the chiral magnetic wave explanation (Burnier et al., 2011) and alternative proposals based on different rapidity distributions (Greco et al., 2012) or conservation laws (Steinheimer et al., 2012). The constrained $R_V$ value enables predictions for the QCD phase diagram location of the critical point, building on phase diagram calculations by Asakawa and Yazaki (1989), Fukushima (2008), and others. Future work can extend this to higher beam energies and centrality dependence to further map the QCD phase structure.
