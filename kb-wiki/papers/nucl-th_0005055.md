# Bosonization of the pairing Hamiltonian

> The problem of the pairing interaction is dealt with even Grassmann variables in the hamiltonian framework. Eigenfunctions of given energy, seniority and zero third component of angular momentum are given in terms of single particle and collective bosonic fields.

# Bosonization of the pairing Hamiltonian

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that eigenfunctions of the pairing Hamiltonian with given energy, seniority, and zero third component of angular momentum can be constructed using even Grassmann variables in the Hamiltonian framework, but only for a single-j shell of one kind of nucleons (e.g., neutrons) and only for the specific case where pairs are coupled to total angular momentum with J_z = 0.

## Method

The authors exploit the isomorphism between the Fock space generated by fermionic creation operators and the Grassmann algebra of anticommuting variables, mapping fermionic states onto elements of the algebra. They introduce even Grassmann variables φ_m = λ_{-m} λ_m to represent pairs of nucleons, then construct the pairing Hamiltonian H_P^(j) = -g_P A*_j A_j in this representation and solve the eigenvalue equation by building the matrix representation of the Hamiltonian in a basis of monomials formed from products of the φ variables. The secular equation is solved for general pair number n and seniority v by combinatorial analysis of the matrix structure.

## Key numbers

- For one pair (n=1) in a level with degeneracy Ω = (2j+1)/2, the lowest eigenvalue is ℰ_0 = -Ω with degeneracy δ = 1, and the next eigenvalue is ℰ_2 = 0 with degeneracy δ = Ω - 1.
- For two pairs (n=2) with Ω = 4, the eigenvalues are ℰ_0 = -6 (δ = 1), ℰ_2 = -2 (δ = 3), and ℰ_4 = 0 (δ = 2).
- The general lowest eigenvalue for n pairs is ℰ = -n(1 + Ω - n) = -n - n(Ω - n).
- The degeneracy for seniority v is given by δ_v = (Ω choose v/2) - (Ω choose v/2 - 1).
- The number of non-zero off-diagonal matrix elements in each row (or column) of the Hamiltonian matrix is n(Ω - n).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous path-integral treatment of the pairing Hamiltonian using even Grassmann variables (Barbaro 1998), which had only addressed the ground state. The spectrum of the pairing Hamiltonian was already known from the quasi-spin formalism (Rowe 1970), but the present approach provides explicit eigenfunction expressions in terms of bosonic fields and clarifies the role of composite fields in nuclear physics. The formalism enables the expression of eigenstates in terms of collective bosons Φ_J of definite angular momentum, connecting to the interacting boson model picture where s-bosons (Φ_0) represent unbroken pairs and d-bosons (Φ_2) represent broken pairs.
