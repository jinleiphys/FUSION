# Theoretical issues in J/psi suppression

> Two decades ago Matsui and Satz suggested that Debye screening in the quark-gluon plasma would result in J/psi suppression in heavy ion collisions. Much has happened in the subsequent years, and the picture of quark-gluon plasma at present is rapidly evolving - what does it imply for the J/psi suppression? What are the recent RHIC and SPS results trying to tell us? What else has to be done? This talk is an attempt to address these questions.

## Key claim

The talk argues that the observed survival of J/psi in quark-gluon plasma up to about 2 T_c, seen in lattice data, can be reconciled with theoretical expectations if a dimension-two operator (related to a tachyonic gluon mass) dominates quarkonium dynamics and its expectation value does not change significantly at T ≤ 2 T_c, implying that residual "short strings" survive into the deconfined phase.

## Method

The paper reviews the operator product expansion (OPE) for the heavy quark potential, introducing a tachyonic mass term λ² into the Coulomb gluon propagator to generate a confining potential. It then applies this modified propagator to finite temperature by including the polarization tensor Π₀₀, and compares the resulting static potential and effective coupling to lattice results from Kaczmarek (2004). The talk also examines the temperature dependence of the gluon condensate using the Hagedorn spectrum of hadron resonances, and discusses the implications for J/psi suppression at RHIC and SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass m_c ≃ 1.3 GeV, with running coupling α_s(m_c) ≃ 0.3
- Saturation scale Q_s²(x;A) ∼ A^{1/3} x^{-0.3}, with Q_s² ≫ Λ_QCD²
- J/psi survival in lattice data up to about 2 T_c (deconfinement transition temperature)
- Critical energy density ε_c ≃ 1 GeV/fm³
- Initial energy density in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC estimated at ε_initial ≃ 20 GeV/fm³
- Survival of J/psi up to energy density ε ≃ 16 ε_c
- Observed suppression of about 40% of J/psi's originating from decays of excited χ and ψ' states
- Gluon fragmentation contribution to J/psi production expected to dominate at p_T ≥ 3–5 GeV

## Context

This talk reviews and extends the original proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) that Debye screening in quark-gluon plasma would cause J/psi suppression. It addresses the contradiction between perturbative expectations and lattice results from Datta (2003) and Asakawa (2003) showing J/psi survival up to 2 T_c. The work builds on earlier calculations of quarkonium dissociation by thermal gluons (Kharzeev 1994, Xu 1995) and the photo-effect analogy (Shuryak 1978). The proposed resolution using a tachyonic gluon mass follows approaches by Chetyrkin (1998) and connects to the Gribov copies problem (Gribov 1977). The talk enables future work on disentangling suppression mechanisms at RHIC and SPS, particularly distinguishing between direct J/psi suppression, feed-down from excited states, and regeneration from charm quark pairs.
