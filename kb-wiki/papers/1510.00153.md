# Nucleon structure functions at small $x$ via holographic Pomeron exchange

> The analysis on nucleon structure functions at small Bjorken $x$ in the framework of holographic QCD is presented. In the model setup, the complicated nonperturbative interaction between the virtual photon and the target nucleon is described via the Pomeron exchange, which corresponds to the reggeized graviton exchange in the AdS space. We show that our calculations for both $F_2$ and $F_L$ structure functions are in agreement with the experimental data measured at HERA.

# Nucleon Structure Functions at Small $x$ via Holographic Pomeron Exchange

## Key claim
The authors show that their holographic QCD calculations for both the $F_2$ and $F_L$ structure functions agree with HERA experimental data in the small-$x$ region ($x < 0.01$, $Q^2$ below a few GeV$^2$), but only when using the soft-wall AdS/QCD model for the nucleon density distribution and the modified (non-conformal) BPST Pomeron kernel; the hard-wall model produces a qualitatively different nucleon density distribution and is not used for the final comparison.

## Method
The structure functions are computed using the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel in AdS space, which represents reggeized graviton exchange. The photon density distribution is obtained from the 5D U(1) vector field wave function in AdS, while the nucleon density distribution is extracted from the gravitational form factor derived from a 5D Dirac fermion action in the soft-wall AdS/QCD model. Three adjustable parameters ($g_0$, $\rho$, and $z_0$) are fixed by fitting to $F_2$ data, and then $F_L$ is predicted without additional parameters.

## Key numbers
- The analysis covers the kinematical region $x < 0.01$ and $Q^2$ below a few GeV$^2$.
- The hard-wall model nucleon density distribution is cut off at $z' \sim 4$ GeV$^{-1}$, corresponding to a nucleon size of $0.8$ fm.
- The longitudinal-to-transverse ratio $R = F_L / F_T$ increases with both Bjorken $x$ and $Q^2$, with substantial $Q^2$ dependence and comparatively weak $x$ dependence.

## Context
This work extends the holographic approach to DIS pioneered by Polchinski and Strassler (2002) and the BPST Pomeron kernel developed by Brower, Polchinski, Strassler, and Tan (2006). It follows the nucleon modeling of Abidin and Carlson (2009) and the authors' own prior studies (Watanabe and Suzuki, 2012, 2013). The framework enables predictions for any two-body high-energy scattering process where Pomeron exchange dominates, and has already been applied to pion structure functions (Watanabe, 2012) and photon structure functions (Watanabe, 2015). Further investigations are needed to clarify the behavior of the ratio $R$ in the small-$x$ region, requiring more precise $F_L$ data.
