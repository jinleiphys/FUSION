# Correlated stopping, proton clusters and higher order proton cumulants

> We investigate possible effects of correlations between stopped nucleons on higher order proton cumulants at low energy heavy-ion collisions. We find that fluctuations of the number of wounded nucleons $N_{\mathrm{part}}$ lead to rather nontrivial dependence of the correlations on the centrality; however, this effect is too small to explain the large and positive four-proton correlations found in the preliminary data collected by the STAR collaboration at $\sqrt{s}=7.7$ GeV. We further demonstrate that, by taking into account additional proton clustering, we are able to qualitatively reproduce the preliminary experimental data. We speculate that this clustering may originate either from collective/multi-collision stopping which is expected to be effective at lower energies or from a possible first-order phase transition, or from (attractive) final state interactions. To test these ideas we propose to measure a mixed multi-particle correlation between stopped protons and a produced particle (e.g. pion, antiproton).

## Key claim

The authors find that fluctuations in the number of wounded nucleons produce nontrivial centrality dependence of proton correlations but are too small by roughly three orders of magnitude to explain the large positive four-proton correlations seen in preliminary STAR data at √s = 7.7 GeV; however, by introducing additional proton clustering (e.g., quartets) they can qualitatively reproduce the experimental results, though they note this clustering mechanism is speculative.

## Method

The authors constructed a minimal model based on independent binomial stopping of nucleons with baryon number conservation and fast isospin exchange, using a Glauber model to generate distributions of wounded nucleons (Npart) and sampling charged particles from a Poisson distribution to define centrality bins. They calculated the integrated correlation functions C2, C3, and C4 from the generating function of the proton multiplicity distribution, both with and without Npart fluctuations, and compared to preliminary STAR data at √s = 7.7 GeV. They then extended the model by allowing protons to be produced in correlated clusters (pairs, quartets, or larger multiplets) rather than independently, and computed the resulting correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 7.7 GeV, the preliminary STAR data show 6C3 ∼ −60 and C4 ∼ 170 for central collisions with Npart ∼ 200
- The minimal independent-stopping model gives C4 ∼ −0.3 after centrality averaging, compared to the data value of ∼ 170, a discrepancy of nearly three orders of magnitude
- For central collisions at √s = 7.7 GeV, ⟨N⟩ ≃ 40, 7C2 ≃ −15, 6C3 ≃ −60, and C4 ≃ 170 from the data
- With 8 proton pairs (M = 8) and Npart = 350, the pair model yields C4 values of order 100 for p2 > 0.5
- With 4 proton quartets (M = 4) and Npart = 350, the quartet model yields C4 values much larger than the pair model for p4 > 0.7

## Context

This work extends the analysis of genuine multi-particle correlations from Bzdak (2016) by studying the specific role of wounded nucleon fluctuations and correlated stopping at low energies, where baryon pair-production is negligible. The authors show that the standard Glauber model with independent stopping (Bialas 1976) fails to explain the large four-proton correlations observed by STAR at √s = 7.7 GeV, and they propose that collective stopping mechanisms or a first-order phase transition could produce proton clustering. The paper enables future experimental tests by proposing to measure mixed multi-particle correlations between stopped protons and produced particles (e.g., pions, antiprotons) to distinguish between clustering from collective stopping, phase transitions, or final-state interactions.
