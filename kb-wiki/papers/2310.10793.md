# Multi-dimensional hadron structure through the lens of gluon Wigner distribution

> In this review, we present the current status of phenomenological research on constraining the multi-dimensional proton (and nucleus) structure at high energies through studies of the so-called gluon Wigner distributions. We provide a brief pedagogical introduction into the corresponding theoretical definitions and modelling of exclusive and diffractive scattering observables in terms of the Wigner distribution. Also, we present a detailed outlook into the existing and planned experimental measurements that attempt to constrain the Wigner distribution. We briefly overview possible interconnections between various manifestations of the gluon Wigner distribution emerging, for instance, in azimuthal-angle correlations in (semi)exclusive reactions and elliptic flow measurements in inclusive processes. We also summarise the current knowledge on the most important processes that would potentially enable one to constrain the elliptic gluon density in the proton and to separate it from the genuine effect of hydrodynamic evolution in the flow measurements.

## Key claim

This review argues that the gluon Wigner distribution, while not directly measurable, can be constrained experimentally through azimuthal-angle correlations in exclusive dijet photoproduction and related diffractive processes, but only if large-statistics, low-pile-up data samples can be collected and if theoretical uncertainties in models predicting non-trivial angular correlations in impact-parameter space can be reduced.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework connecting the gluon Wigner distribution to the dipole S-matrix in the Color Glass Condensate formalism at small Bjorken x (x ≪ 1), and survey existing and planned experimental measurements at the LHC and future facilities that could constrain the elliptic gluon density. They examine exclusive dijet photoproduction in deep inelastic scattering and ultraperipheral collisions as the primary probes, and discuss how azimuthal-angle correlations between the dijet transverse momentum and the recoiled nucleon transverse momentum can be related to the gluon Wigner distribution through Fourier transforms of the dipole S-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The gluon Wigner distribution is defined for momentum fractions x ≪ 1, with the dipole S-matrix depending on rapidity Y ≡ ln(x₀/x) where x₀ = 0.01 is a typical adjustable parameter
- The typical lowest pT threshold for jet reconstruction from calorimeter clusters at ATLAS or CMS is 20 GeV, which can go down to 10 GeV if tracks are considered
- The number of colors in QCD is Nc = 3, used in the relation between the gluon GTMD and the dipole S-matrix
- The skewness variable ξ is related to the momentum transfer squared as Δ² = -(4ξ²m² + Δ⊥²)/(1-ξ²)

## Context

This review extends the pioneering work of Hatta et al. (2016) who proposed probing the gluon Wigner distribution in exclusive dijet photoproduction, and subsequent quantitative studies by Hagiwara et al. (2017) and Reinke Pelicer et al. (2018) on exclusive dijet and heavy quark-pair photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions. It also connects to the Color Glass Condensate framework analysis of diffractive dijets in ep collisions at the Electron-Ion Collider by Mäntysaari et al. (2019). The review enables future experimental programs to design measurements that can separate elliptic gluon density effects from hydrodynamic evolution in flow measurements, and motivates larger-statistics data samples under low pile-up conditions at the LHC and future colliders.
