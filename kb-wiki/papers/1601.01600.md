# Pairing in high-density neutron matter including short- and long-range correlations

> Pairing gaps in neutron matter need to be computed in a wide range of densities to address open questions in neutron star phenomenology. Traditionally, the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approach has been used to compute gaps from bare nucleon-nucleon interactions. Here, we incorporate the influence of short- and long-range correlations in the pairing gaps. Short-range correlations are treated including the appropriate fragmentation of single-particle states, and substantially suppress the gaps. Long-range correlations dress the pairing interaction via density and spin modes, and provide a relatively small correction. We use different interactions, some with three-body forces, as a starting point to control for any systematic effects. Results are relevant for neutron-star cooling scenarios, in particular in view of the recent observational data on Cassiopeia A.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that short-range correlations (SRC) substantially suppress both singlet and triplet pairing gaps in neutron matter, while long-range correlations (LRC) provide only a relatively small correction, with the overall conclusion being that the combined effect of correlations significantly reduces pairing gaps compared to traditional Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) calculations.

## Method

The authors combine self-consistent Green's functions (SCGF) techniques with Fermi liquid theory to include both short- and long-range correlations in the pairing gap equation for neutron matter. Short-range correlations are incorporated by using normal-state spectral functions from finite-temperature ladder resummation calculations, extrapolated to zero temperature, which account for the fragmentation of single-particle strength. Long-range correlations are treated by screening the pairing interaction with particle-hole excitations using Landau parameters from the induced interaction framework. The gap equation is solved for both the singlet \(^1\)S\(_0\) channel and the coupled triplet \(^3\)PF\(_2\) channel using three different nucleon-nucleon interactions (CDBonn, Argonne v18, and N3LO Idaho), with some calculations also including three-neutron forces.

## Key numbers

- Short-range correlations suppress pairing gaps by approximately 20% relative to BCS calculations, as quantified by the effective \(\mathcal{Z}\)-factor \(Z_\text{eff} \approx 0.8-0.9\) across relevant densities.
- The imaginary part of the self-energy at the Fermi surface approaches zero as temperature decreases, with the zero-temperature extrapolation performed at \(k_F = 1.33\) fm\(^{-1}\) for the CDBonn interaction.
- The momentum distribution shows a sharp discontinuity at the Fermi surface at zero temperature, with the discontinuity within a few percent of the \(\mathcal{Z}\)-factor at \(k = k_F\).
- The double convolution energy denominator \(\chi(k)\) shows a linear behavior near the Fermi surface with a non-zero minimum, and increases with density across all momenta for Fermi momenta up to approximately 2.5 fm\(^{-1}\).
- The quasi-particle \(\mathcal{Z}(k)\) factor peaks at \(k=0\) with values of approximately 1.3-1.5, decreases to a minimum near \(k_F\), and rises to approximately 1 at high momenta, while the effective denominator ratio \(Z_\text{eff}\) is always in the range 0.8-0.9.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of short-range correlations presented in Muther (2005) to include triplet pairing in the \(^3\)PF\(_2\) channel, zero-temperature extrapolations, multiple nucleon-nucleon interactions, and three-body forces, while also incorporating long-range correlations via Fermi liquid theory following the approach of Shen (2003), Cao (2006), and Dong (2013). The results are directly relevant for neutron-star cooling scenarios, particularly in light of observational data on Cassiopeia A from Page (2011), and for understanding glitch phenomena in pulsars as discussed by Ho (2015b). The calculations enable more realistic predictions of pairing gaps across a wide density range, which are needed to interpret neutron star phenomenology including neutrino emission rates and superfluid vortex dynamics.
