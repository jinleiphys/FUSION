# Disorienting the Chiral Condensate at the QCD Phase Transition

> I sketch how long wavelength modes of the pion field can be amplified during the QCD phase transition. If nature had been kinder, and had made the pion mass significantly less than the critical temperature for the transition, then this phenomenon would have characterized the transition in thermal equilibrium. Instead, these long wavelength oscillations of the orientation of the chiral condensate can only arise out of equilibrium. There is a simple non-equilibrium mechanism, plausibly operational during heavy ion collisions, which naturally amplifies these oscillations. The characteristic signature of this phenomenon is large fluctuations in the ratio of the number of neutral pions to the total number of pions in regions of momentum space, that is in phase space in a detector. Detection in a heavy ion collision would imply an out of equilbrium chiral transition.

## Key claim

Long-wavelength oscillations of the chiral condensate (pion field) can be amplified during the QCD phase transition, but only if the transition occurs out of equilibrium; in thermal equilibrium, the nonzero pion mass prevents the correlation length from becoming long enough for this phenomenon to arise.

## Method

The author uses classical numerical simulations of the linear sigma model on a 64^3 lattice with spacing a = (200 MeV)^{-1}, solving the nonlinear equations of motion with parameters chosen so that f_pi and m_pi take their zero-temperature values and m_sigma = 600 MeV. Initial conditions are set with the order parameter fluctuating randomly at each lattice site, mimicking a quench from above T_c, and the time evolution of the pion field modes is tracked via Fourier transform. The resulting momentum-space pion numbers are binned in 10-degree by 10-degree angular bins to construct distributions of the neutral pion fraction R.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = (200 MeV)^{-1} used in the classical simulations.
- The longest-wavelength mode's amplitude squared was amplified by more than a factor of 1000 relative to short-wavelength modes, and by about a factor of 50 relative to its equilibrium value at late times.
- The amplification occurs over intermediate times of order 5 to 50 times m_pi^{-1}.
- Modes with |k| less than about m_pi are the ones that become amplified.
- The momentum-space cut used in the analysis of R distributions was |k| < 300 MeV.
- Lattice simulations from the MILC collaboration show the order parameter decreases rapidly but smoothly over a temperature range about 10 MeV wide centered at T_c ~ 140-160 MeV, for quark masses corresponding to m_pi/m_rho ~ 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) and Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) on the O(4) universality class of the chiral phase transition, and proposes a non-equilibrium mechanism distinct from the equilibrium critical phenomena studied by those authors. The paper enables experimental searches for disoriented chiral condensates in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and CERN, with specific analysis strategies for the WA98, NA49, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR collaborations. The simulations build on earlier work by Anselm (1988), Blaizot (1991), and Bjorken (1993) on idealized disoriented regions, but incorporate a more realistic treatment of the full nonlinear dynamics and the amplification of long-wavelength modes.
