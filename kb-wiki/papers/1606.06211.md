# In-medium P-wave quarkonium from the complex lattice QCD potential

> We extend our lattice QCD potential based study arXiv:1509.07366 (JHEP 1512 (2015) 101) of the in-medium properties of heavy quark bound states to P-wave bottomonium and charmonium. Similar to the behavior found in the S-wave channel their spectra show a characteristic broadening, as well as mass shifts to lower energy with increasing temperature. In contrast to the S-wave states, finite angular momentum leads to the survival of spectral peaks even at temperatures, where the continuum threshold reaches below the bound state remnant mass. We elaborate on the ensuing challenges in defining quarkonium dissolution, present estimates of melting temperatures for the spin averaged $χ_b$ and $χ_c$ states and contrast the findings to recent direct lattice NRQCD studies of P-wave quarkonium. As an application to heavy-ion collisions we estimate the contribution of feed down to S-wave quarkonium through the P-wave states after freezeout.

## Key claim

The authors extend their lattice QCD potential-based study of in-medium heavy quark bound states to P-wave bottomonium and charmonium, finding that finite angular momentum leads to the survival of spectral peaks even at temperatures where the continuum threshold reaches below the bound state remnant mass, which challenges conventional definitions of quarkonium dissolution.

## Method

The authors use a pNRQCD approach with a complex-valued in-medium potential extracted from dynamical lattice QCD simulations with Nf=2+1 flavors, parametrized via an extended Gauss Law ansatz with a single temperature-dependent Debye mass parameter mD. They solve the Schrodinger equation directly in frequency space to obtain spectral functions for P-wave states, fitting the resulting peaks with a skewed Breit-Wigner form to extract mass, width, and integrated area as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass mD/T ranges from 0.04(10) at T/Tc=0.86 to 2.29(14) at T/Tc=1.66
- Vacuum masses: chi_b(1P)=9.92597 GeV, chi_b(2P)=10.269 GeV, chi_b(3P)=10.538 GeV, chi_c(1P)=3.5089 GeV, chi_c(2P)=3.7918 GeV
- Mean radii: chi_b(1P)=0.4797 fm, chi_b(2P)=0.7679 fm, chi_b(3P)=1.100 fm, chi_c(1P)=0.8148 fm, chi_c(2P)=5.008 fm
- Distance to open heavy flavor threshold: chi_b(1P)=0.633 GeV, chi_b(2P)=0.29 GeV, chi_b(3P)=0.02 GeV, chi_c(1P)=0.227 GeV, chi_c(2P)=-0.056 GeV
- Centrifugal barrier height: 4 MeV for bottomonium at T=300 MeV, 23 MeV for charmonium at T=300 MeV
- Continuum T=0 parameters: c=-0.1767+/-0.0210 GeV, alpha_s=0.5043+/-0.0298, sqrt(sigma)=0.415+/-0.015 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous S-wave study (Burnier, Kaczmarek, Rothkopf, JHEP 1512 (2015) 101) to P-wave states, providing an independent analysis that addresses discrepancies between MEM-based lattice NRQCD studies (which found chi_b(1P) melting around 1.27Tc) and BR-based reconstructions (which found features up to 1.6Tc). The results enable estimates of feed-down contributions to S-wave quarkonium in heavy-ion collisions after freezeout, and provide a framework for defining quarkonium dissolution in the presence of finite angular momentum where spectral peaks survive below the continuum threshold.
