# Impact of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking on meson structure and interactions

> We provide a glimpse of recent progress in meson physics made via QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations with: a perspective on confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB); a pre'cis on the physics of in-hadron condensates; results for the masses of the π, σ, ρ, a_1 mesons and their first-radial excitations; and an illustration of the impact of DCSB on the pion form factor.

## Key claim

Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) is the dominant mass-generating mechanism for light-quark hadrons, making the Higgs mechanism largely irrelevant for normal matter; however, the paper argues that DCSB has not yet been realized on the light-front formulation of QCD, and proposes that its impact is instead expressed through in-hadron condensates connected with sea-quarks from higher Fock states.

## Method

The authors employed a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) model with a contact-interaction kernel, regularized in a symmetry-preserving manner, to compute meson masses via the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations. They augmented the rainbow-ladder truncation with a spin-orbit repulsion term parameterized by a single constant \(g_{\rm SO}=0.29\), fixed to reproduce the \(a_1\) meson mass. For radial excitations, they implemented the required single zero in the bound-state amplitude using the method of Volkov (1999).

## Key numbers

- Dressed-quark mass in the chiral limit: \(M = 0.40\) GeV (momentum-independent model value).
- Current-quark mass used: \(m = 8\) MeV, yielding a momentum-independent dressed-quark mass \(M = 0.41\) GeV.
- Pion mass in rainbow-ladder truncation: \(m_\pi = 0.141\) GeV (experiment: 0.140 GeV).
- Rho meson mass in rainbow-ladder truncation: \(m_\rho = 0.798\) GeV (experiment: 0.777 GeV).
- Sigma meson mass with spin-orbit repulsion: \(m_\sigma = 1.079\) GeV (experiment: 0.4–1.2 GeV).
- \(a_1\) meson mass with spin-orbit repulsion: \(m_{a_1} = 1.243\) GeV (experiment: 1.243 GeV), used to fix \(g_{\rm SO}=0.29\).
- First radial excitation of the pion: \(m_{\pi^\ast} = 1.434\) GeV (experiment: \(1.3 \pm 0.1\) GeV).
- First radial excitation of the rho: \(m_{\rho^\ast} = 1.434\) GeV (experiment: 1.465 GeV).
- Gluon mass-scale in the model: \(m_G = 0.11\) GeV.
- Confinement length-scale: \(1/\Lambda_{\rm ir} = 0.83\) fm.
- Contact-interaction pion form factor deviates from QCD-based DSE prediction by more than 20% for \(Q^2 > 0.17\) GeV\(^2 \approx M^2\).
- Perturbative QCD behavior in meson form factors is argued to require \(Q^2 > 8\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends DSE studies of meson physics by Maris and Tandy (2000) and Gutierrez-Guerrero et al. (2010), using a contact-interaction model to isolate the effects of a momentum-independent dressed-quark mass. It contrasts with QCD-based DSE calculations that employ a momentum-dependent renormalization-group-improved gluon exchange interaction, and with lattice-QCD results confirming the running mass function (Bowman, 2005). The paper enables future studies that map the running of the dressed-quark mass via baryon elastic and transition form factors, and aims to unify hadron spectrum, form factors, and parton distribution functions through DCSB.
