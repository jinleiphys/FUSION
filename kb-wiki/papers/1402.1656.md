# Solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter Equation in Minkowski space: a comparative study

> The Bethe-Salpeter Equation for a two-scalar, S-wave bound system, interacting through a massive scalar, is investigated within the ladder approximation. By assuming a Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, one can deduce new integral equations that can be solved and quantitatively studied, overcoming the analytic difficulties of the Minkowski space. Finally, it is shown that the Light-front distributions of the valence state, directly obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, open an effective window for studying the two-body dynamics.

## Key claim

The Nakanishi integral representation, originally developed in perturbation theory, can be successfully applied to non-perturbative solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, provided one uses a light-front projection method to derive integral equations for the Nakanishi weight function; the agreement between two different integral equations for the weight function is very close but not exact, with small residual differences.

## Method

The authors solve the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for a bound system of two massive scalars interacting through a massive scalar exchange, using a Nakanishi integral representation ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. They apply the light-front projection method, integrating over the light-front variable k^- to obtain an integral equation for the Nakanishi weight function, and also derive a second, simpler integral equation by assuming the uniqueness theorem of the Nakanishi representation holds non-perturbatively. The two equations are solved numerically and compared for various binding energies and exchanged scalar masses.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant alpha = g^2/(m^2 * 16 pi) is determined for binding energies B/m ranging from 0.20 to 2.0, for exchanged scalar masses mu/m = 0.15 and 0.50 (see Fig. 1 left panel)
- For B/m = 1.0 and mu/m = 0.5, the difference between the two Nakanishi weight functions g_U and g_V is shown to be small (Fig. 1 right panel)
- Light-front longitudinal-momentum fraction distributions phi(xi) are presented for B/m = 0.20, 0.50, 1.0, and 2.0 with mu/m = 0.5 (Fig. 2 left panel)
- Light-front transverse-momentum distributions P(gamma) multiplied by (gamma/m^2)^3 are shown for the same binding energies and mu/m = 0.5 (Fig. 2 right panel)

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of the Nakanishi approach to the Bethe-Salpeter equation by Kusaka and Williams (earlier studies) and by Carbonell and Karmanov (CK1, CK2), providing a detailed comparison between two different integral equation formulations. The results enable future calculations of scattering lengths and cross-box contributions, and open the door to applications in hadronic physics and solid state physics by providing a practical method to obtain Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and light-front wave functions in Minkowski space.
