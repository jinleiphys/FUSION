# Possible Way to Synthesize Superheavy Element Z=117

> Within the framework of the dinuclear system model, the production of superheavy element Z=117 in possible projectile-target combinations is analyzed systematically. The calculated results show that the production cross sections are strongly dependent on the reaction systems. Optimal combinations, corresponding excitation energies and evaporation channels are proposed in this letter, such as the isotopes ^{248,249}Bk in ^{48}Ca induced reactions in 3n evaporation channels and the reactions ^{45}Sc+^{246,248}Cm in 3n and 4n channels, and the system ^{51}V+^{244}Pu in 3n channel.

## Key claim
The production cross sections for superheavy element Z=117 are strongly dependent on the specific projectile-target combination, with the optimal systems being those that maximize both fusion probability and survival probability through a balance of mass asymmetry, neutron richness, and shell effects.

## Method
The authors used the dinuclear system (DNS) model, which describes compound nucleus formation via nucleon transfer from the light to the heavy nucleus, to calculate evaporation residue cross sections for various projectile-target combinations. The model incorporates capture probability via a barrier distribution function, fusion probability by solving a master equation for nucleon transfer in the potential energy surface, and survival probability using statistical evaporation theory with neutron and fission width competition. Calculations were performed for cold fusion reactions with 209Bi targets and hot fusion reactions with 48Ca beams on actinide targets, as well as alternative combinations such as 45Sc+246,248Cm and 51V+244Pu.

## Key numbers
- Maximal production cross section for 82Se+209Bi in the 1n channel: 0.015 pb at excitation energy 12 MeV
- Maximal production cross section for 48Ca+247Bk in the 3n channel: 0.86 pb at excitation energy 37 MeV
- Production cross section for 48Ca+247Bk in the 2n channel: 0.61 pb at excitation energy 26 MeV
- Production cross section for 48Ca+247Bk in the 4n channel: 0.48 pb at excitation energy 47 MeV
- Production cross section for 51V+244Pu in the 3n channel: 0.6 pb
- Maximal angular momentum used in calculations: J_max = 30

## Context
This work extends the DNS model previously developed by the same group (Feng et al., 2007) and applied to other superheavy element syntheses, building on experimental successes at Dubna (Oganessian et al., 2004-2006) for elements 113-116 and 118 using 48Ca-induced reactions, and at GSI using cold fusion with 208Pb and 209Bi targets. The calculations provide specific guidance for future experiments aiming to synthesize element 117, identifying 48Ca+248,249Bk in 3n channels and 45Sc+246,248Cm in 3n and 4n channels as the most promising systems, while noting the practical difficulty of preparing 246,248Cm targets.
