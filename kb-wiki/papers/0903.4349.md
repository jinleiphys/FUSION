# The effect of hidden color channels on Nucleon-Nucleon interaction

> This letter reports the nucleon-nucleon($NN$) interaction obtained from multi-channel, including hidden color channels, coupling quark model calculation. The results show that the hidden color channels coupling provides the intermediate range attraction which is usually assumed to be due to multi-$π$ or $σ$ meson exchange and that the short and intermediate range $NN$ interaction can be described solely by the fundamental quark-gluon degree of freedom of QCD.

# The effect of hidden color channels on Nucleon-Nucleon interaction

## Key claim

Hidden color channel coupling in a quark model calculation can provide the intermediate-range attraction in the nucleon-nucleon interaction that is conventionally attributed to multi-pion or sigma meson exchange, suggesting that the short- and intermediate-range NN interaction may be described solely by quark-gluon degrees of freedom. However, the authors had to adjust the confinement interaction strength per partial wave to achieve quantitative agreement with phase shift data, indicating that the model in its current form does not yet provide a parameter-free description.

## Method

The authors performed a multi-channel resonating-group-method calculation of nucleon-nucleon scattering using a six-quark Hamiltonian with one-gluon exchange, one-pion exchange, and a two-body quadratic confinement potential. They included hidden color channels (color octet nucleon pairs coupled to overall color singlet) in addition to the usual color singlet NN, Delta-Delta, and N-Delta channels, and solved the coupled-channel scattering problem using the Kamimura variational method. The confinement strength was adjusted by a multiplicative factor k, either for hidden-color to color-singlet couplings only (recipe 1) or for all hidden color channels (recipe 2), to fit the SP07 partial wave phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses: m_u = m_d = 313 MeV (set to 1/3 of nucleon mass)
- Nucleon size parameter: b = 0.518 fm
- One-gluon exchange coupling: alpha_s = 0.485
- Confinement strength: a_c = 56.75 MeV fm^{-2} (base value, before adjustment per partial wave)
- Confinement offset: V_0 = -1.3590 fm^2
- Pion mass: m_pi = 0.71 fm^{-1}
- Pion cutoff: Lambda_pi = 4.2 fm^{-1}
- Chiral coupling: alpha_ch = 0.027
- Deuteron binding energy: 1.0 MeV (recipe 1) or 2.2 MeV (recipe 2), compared to experimental value (not quoted in text)
- Deuteron root-mean-square radius: 1.2 fm (recipe 1) or 1.1 fm (recipe 2)
- Deuteron D-state probability: 4% for both recipes
- Confinement adjustment factors k ranged from 1.00 to 2.40 across partial waves (see Table 2)

## Context

This work extends the quark delocalization color screening model (Wang et al., 1992) by directly including hidden color channels rather than modeling their effect through phenomenological color screening. It contrasts with the one-boson exchange model (Machleidt, Holinde, Elster, 1987) and chiral perturbation theory (Epelbaum, Hammer, Meissner, 2008), which fit NN data with 30-40 adjustable parameters but do not explain the similarity between nuclear and molecular forces. The results suggest that lattice QCD calculations (Ishii, Aoki, Hatsuda, 2007) may eventually describe NN interactions from quark-gluon degrees of freedom alone, and the authors identify remaining problems including the need for a more realistic confinement interaction for multi-quark systems and the possible role of color screening from q-qbar excitations.
