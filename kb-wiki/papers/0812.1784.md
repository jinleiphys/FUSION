# Squeezed correlations among particle-antiparticle pairs

> The hadronic correlation among particle-antiparticle pairs was highlighted in the late 1990's, culminating with the demonstration that it should exist if the masses of the hadrons were modified in the hot and dense medium formed in high energy heavy ion collisions. They were called Back-to-Back Correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs, also known as squeezed correlations. However, even though they are well-established theoretically, such hadronic correlations have not yet been experimentally discovered. Expecting to compel the experimentalists to search for this effect, we suggest here a clear way to look for the BBC signal, by constructing the squeezed correlation function of phi-phi and K+K- pairs at RHIC energies, plotted in terms of the average momentum of the pair, K12=(k1+k2)/2, inspired by procedures adopted in Hanbury-Brown & Twiss (HBT) correlations.

# Squeezed correlations among particle-antiparticle pairs

## Key claim

Back-to-Back Correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs, also called squeezed correlations, should exist if hadron masses are modified in the hot and dense medium formed in high energy heavy ion collisions; however, these theoretically well-established correlations have not yet been experimentally discovered, and the authors propose a specific method to search for them by constructing the squeezed correlation function in terms of the average momentum of the pair, K12 = (k1 + k2)/2, inspired by HBT correlation procedures.

## Method

The authors apply a non-relativistic formalism with flow-independent squeezing parameter, using a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation to relate asymptotic free-particle operators to in-medium quasi-particle operators, and compute the squeezed correlation function C2(k1,k2) for bosonic phi-phi and K+K- pairs. They analyze the correlation as a function of the pair average momentum K12 and relative momentum q12, assuming a constant in-medium mass shift, and examine the effects of finite emission time, radial flow, and system size on the BBC signal strength.

## Key numbers

- For phi-phi pairs with in-medium mass fixed to m* = 1.0 GeV, finite emission time of Δt = 2 fm/c reduces the BBC signal by more than two orders of magnitude compared to the static case
- System radii of R = 7 fm and R = 3 fm are compared, with relative momentum fixed to q12 = 0.80 GeV/c and q12 = 1.6 GeV/c, showing that the size of the squeezing region is reflected in the inverse width of the curves plotted as function of 2|K|
- For K+K- pairs with radial flow parameter ⟨u⟩ = 0.5, the in-medium kaon mass is fixed to m* = 650 MeV for the correlation plots
- The squeezed correlation signal is stronger over momentum regions roughly for 0 ≤ |K12| ≤ 60 MeV/c and |q12| ≤ 2000 MeV/c in the presence of flow

## Context

This work extends the theoretical formalism for squeezed correlations originally developed for bosonic systems by Asakawa, Csorgo, and Gyulassy (1999) and for fermion-antifermion pairs by Padula, Csorgo, Huang, Krein, and Papp (2001). The authors build on a later non-relativistic treatment with flow by Padula, Huang, Krein, Papp, and Csorgo (2005) to propose an experimentally accessible variable (the pair average momentum) for searching for the BBC signal at RHIC energies, aiming to motivate experimentalists to search for this effect which has not yet been observed despite being theoretically established.
