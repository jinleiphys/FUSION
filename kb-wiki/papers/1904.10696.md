# Nuclear effects in electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering within a relativistic quantum mechanical framework

> We study the impact of the description of the knockout nucleon wave function on electron- and neutrino-induced quasielastic and single-pion production cross sections. We work in a fully relativistic and quantum mechanical framework, where the relativistic mean-field model is used to describe the target nucleus. The focus is on Pauli blocking and the distortion of the final nucleon, these two nuclear effects are separated and analyzed in detail. We find that a proper quantum mechanical treatment of these effects is crucial to provide the correct magnitude and shape of the inclusive cross section. Also, this seems to be key to predict the right ratio of muon- to electron-neutrino cross sections at very forward scattering angles.

## Key claim

A proper quantum mechanical treatment of Pauli blocking and final-state nucleon distortion is crucial to obtain the correct magnitude and shape of inclusive cross sections and to predict the right ratio of muon- to electron-neutrino cross sections at very forward scattering angles, but the energy-independent relativistic mean-field (RMF) potential used for final-state interactions fails for knockout nucleon momenta above 400-500 MeV, requiring an energy-dependent potential.

## Method

The authors compute quasielastic (QE) and single-pion production (SPP) cross sections for electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering using a fully relativistic quantum mechanical framework based on the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for the target nucleus. They compare four treatments of the outgoing nucleon wave function: relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), Pauli-blocked RPWIA (PB-RPWIA) which restores orthogonality via Gram-Schmidt projection, full RMF with energy-independent potentials, and an energy-dependent RMF (ED-RMF) where the scalar and vector potentials are scaled by a function fitted to inclusive electron scattering data from the SuSAv2 model.

## Key numbers

* For carbon-12, the Fermi momentum cutoff used in a simple Pauli-blocking approach is \(p_F = 230\) MeV.
* The RMF model has only 6 free parameters: coupling constants of the \(\omega\), \(\rho\), and \(\sigma\) mesons, the mass of the \(\sigma\) meson, and two additional coupling constants for non-linear terms.
* Spectroscopic factors extracted from exclusive \((e,e'p)\) experiments are systematically larger in relativistic models (\(\sim 0.75\)) compared to non-relativistic ones (\(\sim 0.55\)) for \(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{208}\)Pb.
* The energy-independent RMF model fails for knockout nucleon momenta reaching 400-500 MeV, where the QE cross sections depart from \((e,e')\) data.
* The ED-RMF scaling function \(f(T_N)\) follows the SuSAv2 behavior for \(T_N \gtrsim 100\) MeV and is close to 1 for \(T_N < 100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) studies of SPP by Fernandez-Ramirez (2008), Praet (2009), and Gonzalez-Jimenez (2018) by including final-state interactions of the knocked-out nucleon via RMF potentials for the first time in a pion-production calculation. It builds on the SuSAv2 model of Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014) and Megias (2016), which used a linear combination of RPWIA and RMF scaling functions fitted to \(^{12}C(e,e')\) data. The results enable improved neutrino energy reconstruction for oscillation experiments like DUNE and T2HK by reducing uncertainties from nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus cross sections.
