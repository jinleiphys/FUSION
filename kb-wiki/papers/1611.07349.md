# Hadron Resonance Gas Model for An Arbitrarily Large Number of Different Hard-Core Radii

> We develop a novel formulation of the hadron-resonance gas model which, besides a hard-core repulsion, explicitly accounts for the surface tension induced by the interaction between the particles. Such an equation of state allows us to go beyond the Van der Waals approximation for any number of different hard-core radii. A comparison with the Carnahan-Starling equation of state shows that the new model is valid for packing fractions 0.2-0.22, while the usual Van der Waals model is inapplicable at packing fractions above 0.1-0.11. Moreover, it is shown that the equation of state with induced surface tension is softer than the one of hard spheres and remains causal at higher particle densities. The great advantage of our model is that there are only two equations to be solved and it does not depend on the various values of the hard-core radii used for different hadronic resonances. Using this novel equation of state we obtain a high-quality fit of the ALICE hadron multiplicities measured at center-of-mass energies of 2.76 TeV per nucleon. Furthermore, using the traditional hadron-resonance gas model with multi-component hard-core repulsion and the novel one we investigate the recently suggested model in which the proper volume of a hadron is proportional to its mass. We find that the high-temperature maximum of $χ^2/ndf$ observed in the latter model always appears in the region located far above the limit of its applicability.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new formulation of the hadron-resonance gas model that goes beyond the Van der Waals approximation by including induced surface tension, and it claims validity for packing fractions up to 0.2-0.22, whereas the usual Van der Waals model is only applicable up to packing fractions of 0.1-0.11. The key hedge is that the model's parameter alpha must be fixed to 1.25 to match the Carnahan-Starling equation of state, and this value is used throughout the work without proof of universality.

## Method

The authors derive a system of two transcendental equations for pressure and induced surface-tension coefficient from the virial expansion for a multi-component mixture, using the Boltzmann approximation for all hadrons except pions (which use Bose-Einstein statistics). They fit the model to 18 independent hadron-multiplicity ratios measured by ALICE at a center-of-mass energy of 2.76 TeV per nucleon in Pb+Pb collisions, treating the chemical freeze-out temperature as the only free parameter while fixing all chemical potentials to zero and the strangeness suppression factor to unity.

## Key numbers

- The IST EOS with alpha=1.25 reproduces the Carnahan-Starling compressibility factor and speed of sound up to packing fraction eta = 0.2-0.22, while the Van der Waals model is only valid up to eta = 0.1-0.11.
- The best fit to the full ALICE data set (18 ratios including light nuclei) using the MHRGM gives chi^2/ndf = 9.71/17 = 0.57 at T_CFO = 154 +/- 7 MeV.
- The best fit to the full ALICE data set using the IST EOS gives chi^2/ndf = 11.32/17 = 0.67 at T_CFO = 152 +/- 7 MeV.
- When excluding light (anti)nuclei ratios (10 ratios), the IST EOS gives chi^2/ndf = 8.04/10 = 0.8 at T_CFO = 152 +/- 7 MeV.
- The new hard-core radii found from fitting the IST EOS to AGS-to-RHIC data are: baryons R_b = 0.365 fm, mesons R_m = 0.42 fm, pions R_pi = 0.15 fm, kaons R_K = 0.395 fm, and Lambda hyperons R_Lambda = 0.085 fm, yielding chi^2/ndf = 57.099/55 = 1.038.
- The Van der Waals model violates causality at a baryonic density of about 0.64 fm^{-3} (eta = 0.17), while the IST EOS remains causal for baryonic densities above 1 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the multi-component hadron-resonance gas model (MHRGM) previously developed by Bugaev et al. (2014) and Veta et al. (2014), which used up to five different hard-core radii and achieved chi^2/ndf = 0.95 for AGS-to-RHIC data. The new IST EOS formulation reduces the computational complexity from solving N transcendental equations (for N different radii) to solving only two equations, enabling application to arbitrarily many hadron species. The paper also re-analyzes the Vovchenko-Stoecker model (2015) which proposed a linear relation between hadron proper volume and mass, finding that its high-temperature chi^2/ndf minimum at 274 MeV lies far above the model's applicability limit. This work enables future hydrodynamic simulations with a causal equation of state at high densities and facilitates inclusion of many hadronic species in thermal fits without prohibitive computational cost.
