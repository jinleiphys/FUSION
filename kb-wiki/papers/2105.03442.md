# The van der Waals interaction as the starting point for an effective field theory

> We consider the system of three ${}^4$He atoms to assess whether a pure van der Waals potential can be used as a starting point for an effective field theory to describe three-body processes in ultracold atomic systems. Using a long-range van der Waals interaction in combination with short-distance two-body counterterms, we analyze the dependence of two- and three-body observables on the short-distance regulator that is required due to the singular nature of the van der Waals interaction. We benchmark our approach with results obtained with the realistic ${}^4$He-${}^4$He LM2M2 interaction and find good agreement. We furthermore show that in this effective field theory approach no three-body force is required at leading order and that universal van der Waals physics leads to a universal correlation between three-body observables in the absence of an Efimov three-body parameter.

## Key claim

The paper assesses whether a pure van der Waals potential can serve as the leading-order starting point for an effective field theory (EFT) of three-body processes in ultracold atomic systems, using the ${}^4$He trimer as a test case. The authors find that no three-body force is required at leading order and that universal van der Waals physics produces a universal correlation between three-body observables without needing an Efimov three-body parameter, but they note that the ground-state trimer binding energy (126.4 mK) is the observable farthest from threshold and estimate a conservative leading-order uncertainty of 30% based on the ratio of momentum scales.

## Method

The authors constructed a momentum-space van der Waals potential using the $C_6$ coefficient from the realistic LM2M2 ${}^4$He-${}^4$He interaction, regulated at short distances with a local regulator function and a nonlocal momentum-space regulator. They introduced a two-body counterterm in each partial wave ($S$, $D$, and $G$ waves) tuned to reproduce specific low-energy observables from the LM2M2 potential, then solved the two- and three-body scattering equations in momentum-space partial-wave representation to compute binding energies and atom-dimer scattering lengths as functions of the short-distance regulator $R$.

## Key numbers

* Two-body scattering length $a_s = 99.9 \pm 0.1$ Å in the $\beta_6/R \rightarrow \infty$ limit, within 0.5% of the LM2M2 result of 100.2 Å
* Two-body effective range $r_s = 7.2 \pm 0.1$ Å in the $\beta_6/R \rightarrow \infty$ limit, within 2% of the LM2M2 result of 7.33 Å
* Ground-state trimer binding energy $B_3^{(0)} = 135 \pm 10$ mK in the $\beta_6/R \rightarrow \infty$ limit, compared to the LM2M2 result of 126.4 mK
* Excited-state trimer binding energy $B_3^{(1)} = 2.3 \pm 0.1$ mK in the $\beta_6/R \rightarrow \infty$ limit, compared to the LM2M2 result of 2.27 mK
* Atom-dimer scattering length $a_{AD} = 105 \pm 10$ Å in the $\beta_6/R \rightarrow \infty$ limit, compared to the LM2M2 result of 115.22 Å
* Atom-dimer effective range $r_{AD} = 85 \pm 10$ Å, compared to the LM2M2 result of 79.0 Å

## Context

This work extends previous studies of van der Waals universality in ultracold atomic systems, particularly the observation that the Efimov three-body parameter can be derived from the van der Waals tail coefficient (reviewed by Naidon and Endo, 2017). It contrasts with the standard short-range EFT (SR-EFT) approach, which requires a three-body parameter at leading order (Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck, 1998), and with the finding by Chapurin et al. (2019) that some caveats to universality exist depending on details of the atom-atom interaction. The results enable systematic improvement of van der Waals EFT calculations for atomic three-body systems and provide uncertainty estimates for leading-order predictions, with the next step being inclusion of higher-order corrections from the $C_8$ and $C_{10}$ terms in the interaction.
