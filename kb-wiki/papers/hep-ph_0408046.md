# Implications of the Present Bound on the Width of the Theta(1540)

> The recently reported Theta(1540) exotic baryon seems to be very narrow: Gamma(Theta)< 1 MeV according to some analyses. Using methods of low energy scattering theory, we develop expectations for the width of the Theta, an elastic resonance in KN scattering in a theory where the characteristic range of interactions is ~1 Fermi. If the Theta is a potential scattering resonance, generated by the forces in the KN channel, its width is hard to account for unless the KN-channel orbital angular momentum, l, is two or greater. If the Theta is a CDD pole, its coupling to the scattering channel is at least an order of magnitude less than the coupling of the Lambda(1520) unless l is two or greater. Either way, if the Theta proves to be in the KN s- or p-wave, new physics must be responsible for its narrow width.

## Key claim

If the Theta(1540) is a potential scattering resonance in the KN channel, its narrow width (Gamma < 1 MeV) is difficult to explain unless the orbital angular momentum is two or greater; if it is a confined-channel (Feshbach) resonance, its coupling to the scattering channel must be at least an order of magnitude weaker than that of the Lambda(1520) unless the orbital angular momentum is two or greater.

## Method

The authors analyze the Theta(1540) as an elastic resonance in non-relativistic KN scattering using two distinct formalisms. For the potential scattering case (Case a), they solve the Schrodinger equation with a square well potential and compute the resonance width from the phase shift derivative at the resonance energy. For the confined-channel case (Case b), they employ the Fano-Feshbach formalism with a two-channel model where a bound state in a closed channel couples to the open KN channel via a separable potential, deriving an analytic expression for the resonance width in terms of the channel-coupling strength and range.

## Key numbers

- The Theta(1540) resonance momentum is k0 = 270 MeV, corresponding to a mass of 1540 MeV
- For a square well potential with l=2, a width of 1 MeV requires a range b ≈ 0.24 fm
- The Lambda(1520) has a partial width into anti-K N of 7 MeV and appears in the d-wave
- At b = 1 fm, the ratio of channel-coupling strengths Omega(Theta)/Omega(Lambda) must be 0.02 for l=0, 0.06 for l=1, and 0.3 for l=2 to produce a 1 MeV width for the Theta
- The nucleon velocity squared over c squared is 0.08 and the kaon velocity squared over c squared is 0.30 at the Theta mass

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of the Theta(1540) width that began with experimental reports from Nakano (2004) and the reanalysis of bubble chamber data by Cahn and Trilling (2003). The authors compare the Theta to the well-established Lambda(1520) resonance, which has a natural suppression mechanism (d-wave and quark pair creation) and a partial width of 7 MeV. The analysis challenges chiral soliton model predictions of a narrow width from Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and constrains quark model approaches such as those by Jaffe and Wilczek, Karliner and Lipkin, and Nussinov, by showing that any model placing the Theta in an s- or p-wave must invoke new physics or extremely suppressed couplings to explain its narrow width.
