# Equation of state of neutron star matter and its warm extension with an interacting hadron resonance gas

> We propose an interpolating equation of state that satisfies phenomenologically established boundary conditions in two extreme regimes at high temperature and low baryon density and at low temperature and high baryon density. We confirm that the hadron resonance gas model with the Carnahan-Starling excluded volume effect can reasonably fit the empirical equation of state at high density up to several times the normal nuclear density. We identify the onsets of strange particles and quantify the strangeness contents in dense matter. We finally discuss the finite temperature effects and estimate the thermal index $Γ_{\rm th}$ as a function of the baryon density, which should be a crucial input for the core-collapse supernova and the binary neutron star merger simulations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an interpolating equation of state for neutron star matter that uses the Carnahan-Starling excluded volume refinement of the hadron resonance gas model, claiming it can reasonably fit empirical equations of state up to several times normal nuclear density, but with the caveat that the model's validity may be limited to densities below approximately 3.7 times normal nuclear density due to causality constraints.

## Method

The authors construct an interacting hadron resonance gas model using the van der Waals prescription with a Carnahan-Starling excluded volume term, fitting the two free parameters (hard-sphere radius R_B and attractive interaction strength a) to reproduce the N^3LO chiral effective field theory equation of state from Drischler (2020). They impose beta-equilibrium and electric charge neutrality conditions to compute the equation of state for neutron star matter at zero and finite temperatures up to 50 MeV, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain mass-radius relations and compute the thermal index from the finite-temperature extension.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Hard-sphere radius for baryons: R_B = 0.511 fm
- Maximal packing density for CS-HRG model: n_B = 11.2 n_0 (at eta=1)
- Causality limit density for CS-HRG model: n_B approximately 3.7 n_0 (for R_B = 0.511 fm)
- Maximal packing density for VDW-HRG model: n_B approximately 2.8 n_0
- Density at superluminal speed of sound in VDW-HRG model: n_B approximately 2.2 n_0
- Maximum mass from CS-HRG model: M = 2.56 M_sun at n_B = 9.32 n_0
- Onset of hyperons occurs slightly below 2 n_0
- Thermal index Gamma_th approximately 1.4 at T = 50 MeV (nearly constant with density)

## Context

This work extends the interacting hadron resonance gas approach, previously applied to high-temperature lattice QCD studies by Andronic (2012) and Vovchenko (2016), to the low-temperature high-density regime relevant for neutron stars. The Carnahan-Starling excluded volume treatment, previously used in astrophysics by Lourenco (2019) and Dutra (2020), is shown to avoid the causality violations that plague the simpler van der Waals excluded volume treatment. The resulting equation of state enables improved modeling of thermal effects in core-collapse supernova and binary neutron star merger simulations, where the thermal index has typically been treated as a free parameter around 1.7-1.8 rather than the density-dependent quantity computed here.
