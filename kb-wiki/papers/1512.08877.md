# The R_AA and v_2 of muons from heavy-quark decays in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV with the ATLAS detector

> The ATLAS experiment measures the production of muons coming from the decays of heavy flavour particles in the kinematic interval 4<p_T<14 GeV and |eta|<1. The measurement is performed in sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV pp collisions and over the centrality range of 0-60% in sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions. The heavy flavour muon differential cross-sections and per-event yields are measured in pp and Pb+Pb collisions, respectively. The nuclear modification factor measured in 0-10% most central collisions is observed to be approximately equal to 0.4 and independent of p_T within uncertainties, which indicates suppressed production of heavy flavour muons in Pb+Pb collisions. The muon yields are also measured as a function of the azimuthal angle with respect to the event plane. Fourier coefficients associated with the second harmonic modulation vary slowly with p_T and show a systematic variation with centrality that is characteristic of other elliptic anisotropy measurements.

## Key claim

The nuclear modification factor (R_AA) of heavy flavour muons in 0-10% most central Pb+Pb collisions is approximately 0.4 and independent of p_T within uncertainties, indicating suppressed production, but the measurement is limited to the kinematic interval 4 < p_T < 14 GeV and |eta| < 1, and the quoted value carries systematic and statistical uncertainties that allow for some p_T dependence.

## Method

The ATLAS experiment measured muons from heavy flavour decays by combining tracks from the inner detector and muon spectrometer in both pp and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. Heavy flavour muons were statistically separated from background muons (from pion and kaon decays and hadronic interactions) using a fractional momentum imbalance variable that compares inner detector and muon spectrometer momentum measurements after accounting for calorimeter energy loss. The nuclear modification factor R_AA was calculated from the ratio of per-event yields in Pb+Pb to the pp differential cross-section scaled by the nuclear overlap function, while the elliptic flow v_2 was measured using the event-plane method with the second-order event plane angle from the forward calorimeters.

## Key numbers

- R_AA in 0-10% most central Pb+Pb collisions is approximately 0.4, independent of p_T within uncertainties over the range 4 < p_T < 14 GeV
- Muon reconstruction efficiency is approximately 0.87 for pp data and 0.84 for Pb+Pb data above p_T = 6 GeV
- Pb+Pb muon trigger efficiency increases from 0.60 at p_T = 4 GeV to approximately 0.75 at 6 GeV, then constant
- pp muon trigger efficiency increases from 0.52 for 4 < p_T < 4.5 GeV to 0.82 for p_T > 5.5 GeV, then constant
- Residual irreducible contamination of non-heavy flavour muons is less than or equal to 1% including contributions from J/psi decays
- Data sample: 0.14 nb^{-1} of Pb+Pb data and 4.0 pb^{-1} of pp data, yielding 9.2 million muons from 8.7 million Pb+Pb events and 1.8 million muons from 1.8 million pp events

## Context

This measurement extends previous heavy flavour suppression and anisotropy results from the ALICE Collaboration (Abelev 2012, Adam 2015) with substantially improved statistical precision, and is consistent with those earlier measurements. The results provide constraints on heavy quark energy loss mechanisms and transport parameters in the quark-gluon plasma, complementing charged hadron elliptic flow measurements from ATLAS (2011, 2012) and heavy flavour meson reconstructions from ALICE (2013, 2014). The combination of R_AA and v_2 measurements over the same kinematic range enables improved discrimination between models of heavy quark interactions in the medium.
