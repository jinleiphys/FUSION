# Testing for coherence and nonstandard neutrino interactions in COHERENT data

> We analyze data from the CsI, liquid Ar and Ge detectors of the COHERENT experiment and confirm within $1.5σ$ that the measured elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section is proportional to the square of the number of neutrons in the nucleus, as expected for coherent scattering in the standard model. We also show how various degeneracies involving nonstandard neutrino interaction parameters are broken in a combined analysis of the three datasets.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the measured elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section is proportional to the square of the number of neutrons in the nucleus, as expected for coherent scattering in the standard model, but only within 1.5 sigma; the combined data from all three COHERENT detectors prefer an exponent of x=1.95 rather than exactly 2, driven by the Ge detector which measured fewer events than the standard model expectation.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of data from the COHERENT experiment's CsI (14.6 kg, 19.3 m from target), liquid Ar (24 kg, 27.5 m), and Ge-Mini (10.66 kg effective mass, 19.2 m) detectors, using neutrino fluxes from the Spallation Neutron Source at Oak Ridge National Laboratory. They constructed chi-squared functions for each detector incorporating nuisance parameters for normalization and background uncertainties, then tested the N-squared dependence by allowing the exponent of the standard model weak charge to be a free parameter x. They also examined nonstandard neutrino interaction (NSI) parameter spaces by fixing the weak mixing angle and allowing various combinations of NSI coupling constants to vary.

## Key numbers

- The combined CsI+LAr+Ge data yield a best-fit exponent x = 1.95 for the N-squared dependence, consistent with x = 2 within 1.5 sigma
- The CsI+LAr data alone give a best-fit exponent x = 1.99
- Standard model flux-weighted cross sections: LAr = 17.6 x 10^-40 cm^2, Ge = 56.1 x 10^-40 cm^2, CsI = 178 x 10^-40 cm^2
- Measured flux-weighted cross sections: LAr = 20.9 (+6.72/-6.86) x 10^-40 cm^2, Ge = 26.37 (+8.98/-8.58) x 10^-40 cm^2, CsI = 172 (+25.8/-24.9) x 10^-40 cm^2
- The Ge detector observed 20.6 (+7.1/-6.3) events compared to a standard model expectation of 35.2 events, a deviation of approximately 2 sigma
- CsI detector: 13.99 GWhr integrated beam power, 3.2 x 10^23 protons on target, 8 energy bins (8-60 PE) and 11 time bins (0-6 microseconds)
- LAr detector: 6.12 GWhr integrated beam power, 1.37 x 10^23 protons on target, 12 energy bins and 10 time bins
- Ge detector: 10.22 GWhr kg exposure, 1.5 keV_ee threshold (corresponding to 6.7 keV_nr nuclear recoil energy), 17 energy bins and 17 time bins

## Context

This work extends the COHERENT collaboration's measurements of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS) first detected in 2017 by COHERENT (2017) with a CsI detector, and subsequently with LAr (2020) and Ge (2024) detectors. The analysis builds on prior studies of NSI degeneracies by showing that combining data from elements with different neutron numbers (Cs, I, Ar, Ge) breaks degeneracies between NSI parameters and the weak mixing angle at the 2 sigma level, which was not possible with reactor-based experiments that detect only electron antineutrinos as noted by Galindo-Uribarri (2020). The results enable future ton-scale COHERENT upgrades (NaI, 750 kg LAr, cryogenic CsI) to place even stronger constraints on nonstandard neutrino interactions.
