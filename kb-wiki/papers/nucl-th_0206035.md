# Folding model analysis of alpha radioactivity

> Radioactive decay of nuclei via emission of $α$ particles has been studied theoretically in the framework of a superasymmetric fission model using the double folding (DF) procedure for obtaining the $α$-nucleus interaction potential. The DF nuclear potential has been obtained by folding in the density distribution functions of the $α$ nucleus and the daughter nucleus with a realistic effective interaction. The M3Y effective interaction has been used for calculating the nuclear interaction potential which has been supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential for exchange along with the density dependence. The nuclear microscopic $α$-nucleus potential thus obtained has been used along with the Coulomb interaction potential to calculate the action integral within the WKB approximation. This subsequently yields microscopic calculations for the half lives of $α$ decays of nuclei. The density dependence and the exchange effects have not been found to be very significant. These calculations provide reasonable estimates for the lifetimes of $α$ radioactivity of nuclei.

# Folding model analysis of alpha radioactivity

## Key claim

The double folding procedure using the M3Y effective interaction alone (without explicit density dependence or exchange corrections) provides reasonable estimates for alpha decay half-lives across a wide range of nuclei, with the density dependence and exchange effects not being very significant for the alpha decay energy regime.

## Method

The alpha-nucleus nuclear potential was obtained by double folding the density distributions of the alpha particle (Gaussian form) and the daughter nucleus (Fermi form) with the M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, supplemented in some calculations by a zero-range pseudo-potential for exchange and a density-dependent term (DDM3Y). This microscopic nuclear potential was combined with the Coulomb interaction (using both spherical and point charge distributions) and the centrifugal barrier to compute the total interaction energy, from which the WKB action integral was evaluated to obtain alpha decay half-lives within the superasymmetric fission model framework.

## Key numbers

- Alpha particle density distribution: Gaussian form with parameters 0.4229 exp(-0.7024 r^2), with volume integral equal to 4 (mass number of alpha particle)
- Daughter nucleus density distribution: Fermi form with diffuseness a = 0.54 fm and radius parameter r_rho = 1.13 A_d^(1/3)
- M3Y effective interaction: v(s) = 7999 exp(-4s)/(4s) - 2134 exp(-2.5s)/(2.5s) (in MeV)
- Zero-range pseudo-potential for exchange: J_00(E) = -276 (1 - 0.005E/A_alpha) MeV.fm^3, taken as -276 MeV.fm^3 for alpha decay
- Density dependent parameter beta(E) fixed at 1.6 for optimum fit
- Zero-point vibration energy proportionality constants: 0.1045*Q for even-even, 0.0962*Q for odd-even, 0.0907*Q for even-odd, 0.0767*Q for odd-odd parent nuclei
- Example calculated half-life for 222Ra (Z=88, A=222): log10 T(s) = 1.39 (M3Y with SCD) vs experimental 1.58
- Example calculated half-life for 226Ra (Z=88, A=226): log10 T(s) = 10.56 (M3Y with SCD) vs experimental 10.703
- Example calculated half-life for 242Cm (Z=96, A=242): log10 T(s) = 7.01 (M3Y with SCD) vs experimental 7.1485

## Context

This work extends the analytical superasymmetric fission model (ASAFM) of Poenaru, Greiner and collaborators (1986, 1991) and the cluster model calculations of Buck, Merchant and Perez (1993), which used phenomenological nuclear potentials, by replacing them with microscopically derived double folding potentials. The calculations provide results comparable to the best available theoretical estimates and are better than the liquid drop model calculations of Royer and Moustabchir (2001) and the preformed cluster model calculation of Mazzocchi et al. (2000) for 242Cm. The approach can be extended to provide estimates of alpha decay lifetimes for exotic nuclei.
