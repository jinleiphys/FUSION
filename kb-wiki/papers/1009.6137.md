# Quarkonium in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma

> We report about a recent calculation of the heavy quarkonium mass and decay width in a quark-gluon plasma, whose temperature T and screening mass m_D satisfy the hierarchy m alpha_s >> pi T >> m alpha_s^2 >> m_D, m being the heavy-quark mass, up to order m alpha_s^5. The calculation may be relevant to understand the behavior of the Y(1S) in a quark-gluon plasma at present-day colliders.

# Quarkonium in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

For a quarkonium state satisfying the hierarchy m alpha_s >> pi T >> m alpha_s^2 >> m_D (where m is the heavy-quark mass, T the plasma temperature, and m_D the screening mass), the complete thermal contribution to the spectrum and thermal width can be computed analytically up to order m alpha_s^5, provided that [m_D/(m alpha_s^2)]^4 << g. This hierarchy may be relevant for the Upsilon(1S) at the LHC, though it is not clear if any quarkonium state at present heavy-ion facilities realizes this specific ordering of scales.

## Method

The calculation uses a sequence of effective field theories (EFTs): QCD is matched to non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) by integrating out the scale m, then to potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) by integrating out the scale m alpha_s, and finally to hard-thermal-loop pNRQCD (pNRQCD_HTL) by integrating out the scale T. The thermal corrections to the color-singlet quark-antiquark potential are computed from a single diagram involving a gluon exchange, with the loop integral separated into contributions from three distinct momentum regions: k_0 ~ k ~ T, k_0 ~ k ~ E (where E ~ m alpha_s^2 is the binding energy), and k_0 ~ k ~ m_D. The Bose-Einstein distribution and HTL gluon propagators are expanded according to the hierarchy, and divergences between scales cancel in the final physical results.

## Key numbers

- The hierarchy of scales for a candidate bottomonium 1S state at the LHC is: m_b ~ 5 GeV > m_b alpha_s ~ 1.5 GeV > pi T ~ 1 GeV > m alpha_s^2 ~ 0.5 GeV >~ m_D
- The leading-order thermal correction to the quarkonium mass increases quadratically with T: delta E_{n,l}^{(thermal)} contains a term (2 pi/9) alpha_s^2 T^2 a_0 [3n^2 - l(l+1) + 8/3], where a_0 = 3/(2 m alpha_s)
- The leading-order thermal width is linear in T: Gamma_{n,l}^{(thermal)} contains a term (4 + 832/(81 n^2)) alpha_s^3 T
- The screening mass is m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 (1 + n_f/6), where n_f is the number of light quarks
- The Bethe logarithm for the 1S state is L_{1,0} = -81.5379; for the 2S state L_{2,0} = -37.6710
- The integral I_{n,l} for the 1S state is I_{1,0} = -0.49673; for the 2S state I_{2,0} = 0.64070

## Context

This work extends the perturbative EFT framework developed by Brambilla, Escobedo, Soto, and Vairo (2008) for studying quarkonium in a weakly-coupled plasma, where the color-singlet to color-octet break-up mechanism was identified as the dominant contribution to the thermal decay width when E >> m_D. It builds on earlier calculations of the quarkonium static potential by Laine, Philipsen, Romatschke, and Tassler (2006-2007) and by Beraudo, Blaizot, and Ratti (2007), which identified the imaginary part of the static potential from Landau damping. The results challenge the long-held view that quarkonium dissociation is primarily driven by color screening, since the melting temperature (where the thermal width equals the binding energy) is parametrically pi T_melting ~ m g^{4/3}, which is lower than the temperature at which screening sets in. The calculation enables predictions for the temperature dependence of lepton and photon yields from electromagnetic decays of quarkonia at LHC energies, with the leading correction to the wavefunction at the origin being of order n^4 T^2/(m^2 alpha_s).
