# Extracting temperature and transverse flow by fitting transverse mass spectra and HBT radii together

> Single particle transverse mass spectra and HBT radii of identical pion and identical kaon are analyzed with a blast-wave parametrization under the assumptions local thermal equilibrium and transverse expansion. Under the assumptions, temperature parameter $T$ and transverse expansion rapidity $ρ$ are sensitive to the shapes of transverse mass $m_\text T$ spectrum and HBT radius $R_\text{s}(K_\text T)$. Negative and positive correlations between $T$ and $ρ$ are observed by fitting $m_\text{T}$ spectrum and HBT radius $R_\text s (K_\text T)$, respectively. For a Monte Carlo simulation using the blast-wave function, $T$ and $ρ$ are extracted by fitting $m_T$ spectra and HBT radii together utilizing a combined optimization function $χ^2$. With this method, $T$ and $ρ$ of the Monte Carlo sources can be extracted. Using this method for A Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) at RHIC energy, the differences of $T$ and $ρ$ between pion and kaon are observed obviously, and the tendencies of $T$ and $ρ$ vs collision energy $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}$ are similar with the results extracted directly from the AMPT model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that temperature $T$ and transverse flow rapidity $\rho_0$ can be extracted simultaneously for a single meson species (pion or kaon) by fitting the shapes of both the transverse mass spectrum and the HBT radius $R_s(K_T)$ together, resolving the degeneracy where $m_T$ spectra alone give a negative $T$-$\rho_0$ correlation and HBT radii alone give a positive correlation.

## Method

The authors use a blast-wave parametrization assuming local thermal equilibrium and transverse expansion to derive analytic expressions for the transverse mass spectrum $\frac{dN}{m_T dm_T}$ and the HBT side-radius $R_s^2(K_T)$. They fit the logarithmic derivatives $\frac{d}{dm_T}\ln\frac{dN}{m_T dm_T}$ and $\frac{d\ln R_s^2}{dK_T}$ (which remove normalization factors) to Monte Carlo simulated data and to A Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) events using a combined $\chi^2$ optimization function derived from the product of Gaussian likelihoods for the two data sets.

## Key numbers

- Monte Carlo simulation parameters: $T = 130$ MeV, $\rho_0 = 0.8$, $R = 10$ fm; also tested with $T = 100$ MeV and $\rho_0 = 0.5$.
- AMPT model analyzed for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ from 7.7 to 200 GeV.
- For AMPT, the directly extracted temperature $T_d$ of pions is lower than that of kaons, and the directly extracted transverse flow rapidity $\rho_d$ of pions is higher than that of kaons; these differences are reproduced by the combined fitting method.

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave analysis approach of previous studies (e.g., LQShan, BlastWaveMtsRs2) that fitted $m_T$ spectra and HBT radii separately to obtain crossing contours on the $T$-$\rho$ plane. The authors introduce a formal combined $\chi^2$ fitting procedure for a single meson species, avoiding the assumption that $T$ and $\rho$ are identical for different hadrons. The method is validated on Monte Carlo blast-wave sources and then applied to the AMPT model at RHIC energies, enabling future extraction of species-dependent freeze-out parameters from experimental data.
