# Sum Rules of the Multiple Giant Dipole States

> Various sum rules for multiple giant dipole resonance states are derived. For the triple giant dipole resonance states, the energy-weighted sum of the transition strengths requires a model to be related to those of the single and double giant dipole resonance states. It is also shown that the non-diagonal matrix elements of the double commutator between the dipole operator and the nuclear Hamiltonian give useful identities for the excitation energy and transition strength of each excited state. Using those identities, the relationship between width of the single dipole state and those of the multiple ones is qualitatively discussed.

# Sum Rules of the Multiple Giant Dipole States

## Key claim

The energy-weighted sum rule for the triple giant dipole resonance (TGDR) cannot be expressed solely in terms of the single and double giant dipole resonance strengths without additional model assumptions, such as the folding model where the DGDR states are composed of dipole bosons. Under the folding model assumption, the TGDR sum rule becomes $S_1(3) = 9S_1(1)S_0(2)$, but this result is not model-independent.

## Method

The authors derived sum rules for multiple giant dipole states by calculating double commutators of the dipole operator $D$ with the nuclear Hamiltonian $H$, assuming the double commutator is a constant (the same approximation used for the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule). They computed the energy-weighted sum $S_1(f)$ for the operator $D^3$ and decomposed it into contributions from transitions to both single and triple giant dipole resonance states. Additionally, they derived identities from non-diagonal matrix elements of the double commutator, which provide constraints on the excitation energy and transition strength of each excited state, and used these to relate the widths of single and multiple giant dipole resonances.

## Key numbers

- The Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule value: $S_1(1) = \frac{1}{2m}\frac{NZ}{A}$ for dipole excitations, where $m$ is the nucleon mass, $N$ the neutron number, $Z$ the proton number, and $A$ the mass number.
- The sum rule for the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR): $S_1(2) = 4S_0(1)S_1(1)$, where $S_0(1)$ is the non-energy-weighted sum of single dipole transition strengths and $S_1(1)$ is the TRK sum rule value.
- The total energy-weighted sum for $D^3$ transitions: $S_1(f) = 9S_1(1)\left(S_0^2(1)+S_0(2)\right)$, where $S_0(2)$ is the non-energy-weighted sum for DGDR transitions.
- Under the folding model assumption: $S_1(3) = 9S_1(1)S_0(2)$ for the triple giant dipole resonance.
- In the folding model, the variance of the DGDR width relates to the SGDR width as $\sigma_2 = \sqrt{2}\sigma_1$, and more generally $\sigma_m = \sqrt{m}\sigma_1$.
- The folding model also yields $S_0(2) = 2S_0^2(1)$ and the mean DGDR energy $\sum_n\omega_{2n}|\langle 2n|D^2|\rangle|^2/S_0(2) = 2\bar{\omega}$, where $\bar{\omega} = S_1(1)/S_0(1)$ is the mean SGDR energy.

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule approach previously applied to single and double giant dipole resonances, building on the TRK sum rule framework and the earlier derivation of the DGDR sum rule by Kurasawa (2004). The authors show that the TGDR sum rule requires additional modeling beyond the double-commutator method, unlike the SGDR and DGDR cases. The non-diagonal matrix element identities provide constraints on individual excitation energies and transition strengths, enabling a qualitative discussion of how the SGDR width propagates to multiple resonances. The folding model result $\sigma_m = \sqrt{m}\sigma_1$ reproduces the well-known stochastic broadening factor previously discussed in the literature on giant resonances. The formalism is applicable beyond nuclear physics to other quantum systems such as atomic clusters and Bose-Einstein condensates.
