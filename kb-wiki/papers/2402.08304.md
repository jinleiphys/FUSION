# Elastic Electron Scattering from Deformed and Oriented Odd-A Nuclei

> Interference terms between monopole and quadrupole Coulomb form factors that contribute to the cross-section of electron scattering from polarized nuclei are studied within the plane wave Born approximation. By experimentally exploring the effect of this nuclear response, valuable insights into the sign of the quadrupole deformation can be obtained. The nuclear structure is evaluated numerically using a self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock approximation with Skyrme effective interactions and accounting for pairing correlations. To illustrate the practical applicability, several examples of odd-A nuclei with different spins and deformations are examined. Comparison of unpolarized and polarized cross-sections becomes a tool to discriminate between the oblate and prolate nature of the nuclear shape, providing a deeper understanding of the nuclear deformation.

# Elastic Electron Scattering from Deformed and Oriented Odd-A Nuclei

## Key claim

By comparing unpolarized and polarized cross-sections in elastic electron scattering from aligned odd-A nuclei, one can obtain a model-independent signature of whether the nuclear deformation is oblate or prolate, based solely on whether the cross-section increases or decreases when the target is polarized. The magnitude of the deviation provides information about the magnitude of the quadrupole deformation, but in a model-dependent way.

## Method

The study uses the plane wave Born approximation (PWBA) to calculate electron scattering cross-sections from deformed, oriented odd-A nuclei, focusing on interference terms between monopole (C0) and quadrupole (C2) Coulomb form factors. Nuclear structure is evaluated using self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme SLy4 effective interactions and BCS pairing correlations, with the axially deformed density expanded in Legendre polynomials to obtain multipole components. The analysis considers nuclei aligned along the momentum transfer direction (θ'=0) with full polarization of the target substates.

## Key numbers

- The interference term F02 is typically between one and two orders of magnitude smaller than (FC0)² and two orders of magnitude larger than (FC2)²
- Statistical tensor α₂ values for aligned nuclei: 1/√5 for I=3/2, √(5/14) for I=5/2, √(7/15) for I=7/2, and √(6/11) for I=9/2
- The coefficient A in the interference term F02 = A·FC0·FC2 takes values: 2/√5 for I=3/2, 5√5/7 for I=5/2, 14/(3√5) for I=7/2, and 12√5/11 for I=9/2
- The study examines nuclei with spins I=3/2, 5/2, 7/2, and 9/2, including ²¹Ne, ⁶³Cu, ¹⁹¹Ir, ²⁷Al, ⁵⁵Mn, ¹⁰⁵Pd, ¹⁶⁷Er, ¹⁷⁷Hf, ¹⁸¹Ta, and ¹⁷⁹Hf
- For ⁵⁵Mn, the differences between oblate and prolate form factors in both (FC2)² and F02 are the largest among the cases studied, corresponding to the largest differences in β₂ between oblate and prolate deformations
- The first peak of F02 in prolate nuclei appears with negative sign (decreasing cross-section), while in oblate nuclei it appears with positive sign (increasing cross-section)

## Context

This work extends a previous study by Sarriguren (2023) on using polarized targets to determine deformation sign, now providing a more comprehensive and systematic investigation across different nuclear spins (I=3/2, 5/2, 7/2, 9/2) and deformation types (oblate, prolate, shape coexistence). The formalism builds on earlier theoretical frameworks by Moya de Guerra (1980s) and Donnelly (1970s) for electron scattering from polarized nuclei. The approach addresses the limitation of standard unpolarized experiments, where only squared C2 multipoles contribute (preventing sign determination), and complements methods based on B(E2) transition probabilities (Pritychenko, 2016) which cannot determine deformation sign due to quadratic dependence on β₂. The results enable future experiments at facilities like SCRIT at RIKEN, ELISe at GSI-FAIR, and the Electron-Ion Collider at Brookhaven to extract deformation sign from cross-section comparisons.
