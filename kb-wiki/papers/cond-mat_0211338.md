# Russian Doll Renormalization Group and Superconductivity

> We show that an extension of the standard BCS Hamiltonian leads to an infinite number of condensates with different energy gaps and self-similar properties, described by a cyclic RG flow of the BCS coupling constant which returns to its original value after a finite RG time.

## Key claim

The paper shows that adding a time-reversal-breaking imaginary part to the BCS pairing potential produces an infinite tower of BCS condensates with self-similar energy gaps, whose existence is explained by a cyclic renormalization group flow of the BCS coupling constant that returns to its original value after a finite RG time; the cyclicity and the infinite condensate spectrum are conditional on the presence of the imaginary coupling theta and hold in the weak-coupling regime where the gaps are much smaller than the cutoff.

## Method

The authors extend the reduced BCS Hamiltonian by taking the scattering potential V_{jj'} to be G + i Theta for epsilon_j > epsilon_{j'} and G - i Theta for epsilon_j < epsilon_{j'}, with G = g delta and Theta = theta delta. They solve the mean-field gap equation in the thermodynamic limit, obtaining an infinite set of gap solutions Delta_n. They then derive RG equations by integrating out the highest energy level via a canonical transformation, obtaining a beta function dg/ds = g^2 + theta^2 for the many-body case. The one-Cooper-pair problem is solved exactly in the large-N limit, and numerical diagonalization for N from 500 down to 50 confirms the predicted scaling and cyclicity.

## Key numbers

- The RG cycle period is lambda = pi / theta for the many-body case, and lambda_1 = 2 pi / theta for the one-Cooper-pair case.
- The infinite set of energy gaps is given by Delta_n = omega / sinh(t_n) with t_n = t_0 + n pi / theta, where n = 0, 1, 2, ... and t_0 satisfies tan(theta t_0) = theta / g with 0 < t_0 < pi/(2 theta).
- For the one-Cooper-pair problem, the bound state energies are E_n = - omega / (e^{t_n} - 1) with t_n = t_0 + 2 pi n / theta, where t_0 satisfies tan(theta t_0 / 2) = theta / g with 0 < t_0 < pi / theta.
- The number of condensates in the many-body case scales as n_C ~ (theta / pi) log N; the number of bound states in the one-Cooper-pair case scales as n_B ~ (theta / (2 pi)) log(N/2).
- For weak couplings, the condensation energy of the n-th BCS eigenstate scales as E_C^{(n)} ~ - (1/2) delta N^2 e^{-2 t_0 - 2 n pi / theta}.
- The ratio Delta_n(0) / T_{c,n} is approximately 3.52 for weak couplings, as in standard BCS theory.

## Context

This work extends the standard BCS model by introducing a complex pairing interaction that breaks time-reversal symmetry, leading to cyclic RG behavior previously observed in nuclear three-body problems (Bedaque, Hammer, and Van Kolck, 1999) and in simple quantum-mechanical Hamiltonians (Glazek and Wilson, 2002), as well as in anisotropic current-current interactions in two-dimensional quantum field theory (Bernard and LeClair, 2002). The model is related to the reduced BCS model used for ultrasmall superconducting grains (von Delft and Ralph, 2001). The results suggest the possibility of "Russian doll superconductors" with multiple condensates and self-similar properties, and indicate that the critical temperature can be raised by varying theta.
