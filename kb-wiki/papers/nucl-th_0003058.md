# Preserving the gauge invariance of meson production currents in the presence of explicit final-state interactions

> A comprehensive formalism is developed to preserve the gauge invariance of currents describing the photo- or electroproduction of mesons off the nucleon when the final-state interactions of mesons and nucleons is taken into account explicitly. Replacing exchange currents by auxiliary currents, it is found that all contributions due to explicit final-state interactions are purely transverse and do not contain a Kroll-Ruderman-type contact current. The relation of the present formulation to tree-level-type prescriptions is shown.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism to preserve gauge invariance in meson photoproduction and electroproduction currents when explicit hadronic final-state interactions are included, finding that all contributions from these final-state interactions are purely transverse and contain no Kroll-Ruderman-type contact current. The result holds provided the auxiliary currents constructed to replace exchange currents satisfy specific Ward-Takahashi identity constraints, but the construction is not unique since arbitrary transverse currents may be added.

## Method

The author starts from the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity for the total photoproduction current and decomposes the interaction current into contributions from bare currents, exchange currents, and the nonpolar πN amplitude mediating final-state interactions. By replacing the exchange currents with auxiliary currents jμ0 and jμ1 that satisfy the four-divergence conditions derived from the Ward-Takahashi identities, the formalism cancels gauge-invariance-violating terms and leaves only transverse contributions from the final-state interaction. The construction is applied to a πNN vertex with a mixture of pseudoscalar and pseudovector couplings, and the resulting gauge-invariant production current is expressed in terms of the Born terms, the auxiliary current jμ0, and the transverse parts of the u- and t-channel currents mediated by the final-state interaction amplitude X.

## Key numbers

- The dressed πNN vertex is written as F = g_ps γ_5 G_ps + g_pv (γ_5 γ·q_π)/(2m) G_pv, where g_ps and g_pv add up to the physical coupling constant g_πNN = g_ps + g_pv.
- The bare Kroll-Ruderman contact current is mμ_bare = -g_pv (γ_5 γμ)/(2m) Q_π τ.
- The auxiliary current jμ0 contains terms with denominators (s - p²), (u - p'²), and (t - q²) corresponding to the Mandelstam variables s = (p+k)², u = (p'-k)², and t = (q-k)².
- For real photons, the transverse pion current Mμ_t,t = 0 because F_π - 1 = 0 and F_1 - 1 = 0, and the u-channel current reduces to the magnetic σμν k_ν term.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier tree-level gauge-invariance-preserving formalism (Haberzettl, 1997) to include explicit final-state interactions mediated by the nonpolar πN amplitude X. It generalizes the approaches of Ohta (1989) and Haberzettl, Bennhold, Mart, and Feuster (1998), showing that both can be understood as different choices for an undetermined function F̂ in the auxiliary current jμ0. The result enables consistent treatment of gauge invariance in phenomenological models of meson photoproduction that include hadronic rescattering, and the author notes that the formalism can be straightforwardly extended to include other baryons and mesons such as Δ and ρ resonances.
