# A closer look at the influence of tubular initial conditions on two-particle correlations

> In a recent paper, the hydrodynamic code NEXSPheRIO was used in conjunction with STAR analysis methods to study two-particle correlations as function of Delta-eta and Delta-phi. The various structures observed in data were reproduced. In this work, we discuss the origin of these structures as well as present new results.

# A closer look at the influence of tubular initial conditions on two-particle correlations

## Key claim
The near-side ridge and away-side double-hump structures observed in two-particle correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be reproduced by hydrodynamic expansion starting from fluctuating tubular initial conditions, without explicitly invoking jets. The ridges originate from the deflection of radial flow by high-energy-density tubes located near the surface of the expanding matter, rather than from jet quenching or Mach-cone mechanisms.

## Method
The authors used the NeXSPheRIO hydrodynamic code, which combines initial conditions from the NeXus event generator with the SPheRIO smoothed-particle hydrodynamics solver, on an event-by-event basis for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon. To isolate the origin of the correlation structures, they also constructed a simplified two-dimensional model consisting of a single high-energy-density spot (tube) embedded in a smooth background, undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and transverse expansion. Two-particle correlations were computed after aligning event planes and subtracting mixed-event contributions to remove flow and pseudorapidity distribution effects.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon (center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair).
- Centrality windows studied: 0-5%, 5-10%, 10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, 40-50%.
- Transverse momentum thresholds used: trigger particle $p_t^{trig} > 2.5$ GeV and associated particle $p_t^{assoc} > 1.5$ GeV (for centrality dependence); $p_t^{trig} > 3.0$ GeV and $p_t^{assoc} > 1.0$ GeV (for event-plane angle dependence).
- The simplified model produces a single-particle angular distribution with two peaks located on both sides of the tube position, leading to a two-particle correlation with a central peak at $\Delta\phi=0$ and two smaller peaks at $\Delta\phi \sim \pm 2$ radians.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous hydrodynamic study using NeXSPheRIO (Takahashi et al., 2009) which reproduced the near-side ridge and away-side double-hump structures seen in STAR data. It provides a mechanistic explanation for those results using a simplified single-tube model, contrasting with jet-quenching and Mach-cone interpretations (Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2006; Ruppert and Renk, 2008). The paper enables future discrimination between models via three-particle correlations and 2+1 correlations, which could distinguish the tube-deflection mechanism from Mach-cone or deflected-jet predictions.
