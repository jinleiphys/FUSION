# Renormalized Effective Hamiltonian Approach to QCD

> Continuing our previous QCD Hamiltonian studies in the gluonic and quark sectors, we describe a new renormalization procedure which generates an effective Hamiltonian. The formulation, which is in the Coulomb gauge, provides an improved framework for investigating hadron structure.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new renormalization procedure for the Coulomb gauge QCD Hamiltonian that generates an effective Hamiltonian, but the procedure is explicitly perturbative and the resulting Hamiltonian is only fully covariant and gauge invariant through some order in perturbation theory, with the cut-off regulator itself violating Lorentz covariance and gauge invariance.

## Method

The authors start with the canonical QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge, which includes transverse gauge fields, their conjugate momenta, and quark fields. They regulate the theory by suppressing matrix elements of the Hamiltonian between free-energy eigenstates using a Gaussian cut-off function exp(-E_nm^2/Lambda^2) based on free energy differences. They then construct a renormalized effective Hamiltonian by determining counterterms through a similarity transformation approach, following Wegner's formulation, where the generator of infinitesimal scale transformations is T(Lambda) = [H_0, H(Lambda)], and they analyze the evolution equation order-by-order in perturbation theory to determine the counterterm structure.

## Key numbers

* The gauge field anomalous dimension is gamma_A = -(g^2/16pi^2)(4C_A/3), where C_A is the Casimir invariant of the adjoint representation
* The cut-off function used for regularization is f_nm(Lambda) = exp(-E_nm^2/Lambda^2), where E_nm is the free energy difference between states n and m

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD Hamiltonian studies (Robertson et al., submitted to Phys. Rev. D; Szczepaniak et al., 1996; Swanson and Szczepaniak, 1998) which had successfully reproduced lattice glueball spectra. The approach builds on Gribov's (1978) suggestion of a natural confinement scenario in Coulomb gauge and Zwanziger's (1997) formalization of that scenario. The renormalization method adapts the cut-off approach of Glazek and Wilson (1993, 1994) and the similarity transformation formalism of Wegner (1994). The resulting effective Hamiltonian enables nonperturbative many-body analyses of hadron structure, including variational BCS vacuum calculations for constituent gluons and future applications to meson spectra, form factors, and decays.
