# Effects of mass renormalization on the surface properties of heavy-ion fusion potential

> We discuss the effects of fast nuclear excitations on heavy-ion fusion reactions at energies near and below the Coulomb barrier. Using the fusion of two $^{40}$Ca nuclei as an example and the inversion method, we show that the mass renormalization induced by fast nuclear excitations leads to a large surface diffuseness in the effective potential for heavy-ion fusion reactions.

## Key claim

The mass renormalization induced by fast nuclear excitations, when added to the adiabatic potential shift, increases the surface diffuseness of the effective fusion potential beyond the bare double-folding value, but this increase alone is not sufficient to explain the empirically observed large diffuseness of about 1.27 fm for the $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca system.

## Method

The authors used the double folding procedure with a Woods-Saxon shape (diffuseness 0.75 fm) to determine the bare nuclear potential for $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca fusion. They then included the effects of three vibrational modes of $^{40}$Ca (3$^-$, 2$^+$, 5$^-$) through a revised adiabatic formula that incorporates both potential and mass renormalizations. The effective fusion potential was extracted from the calculated s-wave barrier penetrability using the inversion method, and its surface diffuseness was compared to exponential potentials with fixed diffuseness parameters.

## Key numbers

- Bare Coulomb barrier for $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca: located at 10.0 fm with height 53.1 MeV and curvature 3.43 MeV
- Surface diffuseness of the bare double-folding nuclear potential: 0.75 fm
- Surface diffuseness of the effective nuclear potential including mass renormalization: about 0.8 fm, compared to the standard value of 0.6 fm
- Excitation energies of the three vibrational modes: 3.737 MeV (3$^-$), 3.905 MeV (2$^+$), 4.492 MeV (5$^-$)
- Adiabaticity parameters for the 3$^-$ mode: $a_1 = 0.9177$, $a_2 = 0.6176$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of potential and mass renormalizations in heavy-ion fusion (Hagino and Takigawa, 1994) by connecting these effects to the empirically observed large surface diffuseness. The large diffuseness had been suggested earlier by inversion analyses of experimental data for $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca (Balantekin et al., 1983) and from analyses of $^{16}$O + $^{154}$Sm fusion (Rowley et al., 1993). The paper enables future studies of how transfer reactions might further modify the surface properties, and leaves open the question of how to treat channel coupling in fast tunneling systems such as $^{16}$O + $^{154}$Sm.
