# Masses of open charm and bottom tetraquark states in a relativized quark model

> We study the masses of open charm and bottom tetraquark states within the diquark-antidiquark scenario in the relativized quark model proposed by Godfrey and Isgur. The diquark and antidiquark masses are firstly solved by relativized quark potential, and then treated as the usual antiquark and quark, respectively. The masses of tetraquark states are obtained by solving the Schrödinger-type equation between the new diquark and antidiquark. We find the masses of $sq\bar b\bar q$ tetraquark configuration are much higher than that of $X(5568)$. This conclusion disfavors the possibility of $X(5568)$ as a tetraquark state within the diquark-antidiquark scenario. Further experimental searches are needed to clarify the nature of the signal observed by D0 collaboration.

## Key claim
The authors find that the masses of the $sq\bar b\bar q$ tetraquark configuration are much higher than that of $X(5568)$, which disfavors the possibility of $X(5568)$ as a tetraquark state within the diquark-antidiquark scenario, but only under the assumption that the diquark is treated as point-like (form factor $F(r)=1$).

## Method
The authors used the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur to first solve for diquark masses using a quark-quark potential taken as half the quark-antiquark interaction, then treated the diquark and antidiquark as point-like constituents and solved a Schrödinger-type equation between them to obtain tetraquark masses. The Gaussian expansion method was employed to solve the Hamiltonian, and all model parameters were taken from Godfrey and Isgur (1985) without refitting.

## Key numbers
- The scalar $[u,d]$ diquark mass is 691 MeV; the axial vector $\{u,d\}$ diquark mass is 840 MeV.
- The scalar $[b,s]$ diquark mass is 5572 MeV; the axial vector $\{b,s\}$ diquark mass is 5585 MeV.
- The lowest $J^P=0^+$ $sq\bar b \bar q$ tetraquark mass is 6150 MeV (from the $A\bar A$ configuration), which is much higher than the $X(5568)$ mass of $5567.8\pm2.9^{+0.9}_{-1.9}$ MeV.
- The lowest $J^P=1^+$ $sq\bar b \bar q$ tetraquark mass is 6210 MeV (from the $S\bar A$ configuration), which is larger than $m(X(5568)) + m(B^*_s)- m(B_s)$ where $m(B^*_s)- m(B_s) = 48.6^{+1.8}_{-1.6}$ MeV.
- The spin-averaged mass $(3M_V+M_P)/4$ of $B^*$, $B$, $K^*$, and $K$ is 6107 MeV, consistent with the calculated 6150 MeV.
- The $bsu$ flavor baryons $\Xi_b$ and $\Xi_b^*$ have masses 5794 and 5945 MeV, respectively.

## Context
This work extends the diquark-antidiquark tetraquark studies of Ebert, Faustov, and Galkin (2010) and applies the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) to the open charm and bottom tetraquark sector, motivated by the D0 Collaboration's observation of $X(5568)$. The results contradict tetraquark interpretations of $X(5568)$ from QCD sum rule and simple quark model studies (e.g., Wang 2016, Maiani 2004) but are consistent with the general arguments of Burns (2016) and Guo (2016) that tetraquark masses should lie above the $\Xi_b$ baryon masses. The predicted tetraquark states are much higher than the $B_s\pi$ and $B\bar K$ thresholds, suggesting they may fall apart immediately, and further experimental searches are needed to clarify the nature of the $X(5568)$ signal.
