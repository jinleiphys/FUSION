# On the products of bipolar harmonics

> The products of the two and three bipolar harmonics ${\cal Y}^{\ell_1 \ell_2}_{LM}({\bf r}_{31}, {\bf r}_{32})$ are represented as the finite sums of powers of the three relative coordinates $r_{32}, r_{31}$ and $r_{21}$. The complete (angular+radial) integrals of the products of the two and three bipolar harmonics in the basis of exponential radial functions are expressed as finite sums of the auxiliary three-particle integrals $Γ_{n,k,l}(α, β, γ)$. The formulas derived in this study can be used to accelerate highly accurate computations of the rotationally excited (bound) states in arbitrary three-body systems. In particular, we have constructed compact (400-term) variational wave functions for the triplet and singlet $2P(L = 1)-$states in light two-electron atoms and ions. Highly accurate calculations (20 - 21 stable decimal digits in the total energy) of the triplet and singlet $2P(L = 1)-$states in the two-electron Li$^{+}$, Be$^{2+}$, B$^{3+}$ and C$^{4+}$ ions are performed for the first time

## Key claim

The paper derives finite-sum analytical formulas for the angular and radial integrals of products of two and three bipolar harmonics, enabling highly accurate computations of rotationally excited bound states in three-body systems; the authors qualify that their preliminary results for the Li⁺, Be²⁺, B³⁺ and C⁴⁺ ions achieve 20–21 stable decimal digits in total energy, but note that more accurate calculations would require larger computational resources than currently available.

## Method

The authors represent the angular integrals of products of two and three bipolar harmonics as finite sums of powers of the three relative coordinates \(r_{32}, r_{31}, r_{21}\), using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, \(3j\)-symbols, \(6j\)-symbols, and Legendre polynomials. They then express the complete six-dimensional (angular plus radial) integrals in a basis of exponential radial functions as finite sums of auxiliary three-particle integrals \(\Gamma_{n,k,l}(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)\), defined in perimetric coordinates. Variational wave functions with 400 exponential basis terms are constructed for the singlet and triplet \(2P(L=1)\) states, and the total energies are computed using a two-stage optimization strategy.

## Key numbers

- Total energies of the singlet \(2^1P\) and triplet \(2^3P\) states in two-electron Li⁺, Be²⁺, B³⁺, and C⁴⁺ ions are computed to 20–21 stable decimal digits (Table III, preliminary results).
- For the bound \(3D\) states in the He atom, the method yields total energies of \(-2.055620\,7328528(4)\) a.u. (singlet) and \(-2.055636\,3094537(4)\) a.u. (triplet), with accuracy comparable to late-1990s results.
- A test calculation for the \(T^{1 2 1}_{3,2,2;1,2,2}\) coefficient yields \(1.9488412125971230 \times 10^{4}\).
- Example coefficients \(b_\lambda\) for \(\ell_1=1, \ell_2=3, \ell_3=2, \ell_4=2, \ell_5=2, \ell_6=2\) and \(L_a=1, L_b=2, L_c=1\): \(b_1 = 0.15921224404155089 \times 10^{-1}\), \(b_3 = -0.67600638318413508 \times 10^{-2}\), \(b_5 = 0.27451117819426586 \times 10^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the Hylleraas-style approach (Hylleraas, 1929) and earlier bipolar harmonic methods (Var, Drake, Efros) by providing closed-form finite-sum expressions for integrals involving products of two and three bipolar harmonics, which previously required more computationally intensive numerical integration. The formulas enable accelerated calculations for rotationally excited states with arbitrary angular momentum \(L\), and the authors demonstrate that for \(L=1\) states the accuracy matches that of their earlier specialized method (Frolov, 2011). The results for Li⁺, Be²⁺, B³⁺, and C⁴⁺ are reported for the first time at this precision, and the approach opens the possibility of highly accurate computations for states with \(L \ge 2\), though improved optimization strategies are needed to reach 20–25 stable decimal digits.
