# Dynamical effects of spin-dependent interactions in low- and intermediate-energy heavy-ion reactions

> It is well known that non-central nuclear forces, such as the spin-orbital coupling and the tensor force, play important roles in understanding many interesting features of nuclear structures. However, their dynamical effects in nuclear reactions are poorly known since only the spin-averaged observables are normally studied both experimentally and theoretically. Realizing that spin-sensitive observables in nuclear reactions may carry useful information about the in-medium properties of non-central nuclear interactions, besides earlier studies using the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach to understand effects of spin-orbital coupling on the threshold energy and spin polarization in fusion reactions, some efforts have been made recently to explore dynamical effects of non-central nuclear forces in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions using transport models. The focuses of these studies have been on investigating signatures of the density and isospin dependence of the form factor in the spin-dependent single-nucleon potential. Interestingly, some useful probes were identified in the model studies while so far there is still no data to compare with. In this brief review, we summarize the main physics motivations as well as the recent progress in understanding the spin dynamics and identifying spin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies. We hope the interesting, important, and new physics potentials identified in the spin dynamics of heavy-ion collisions will stimulate more experimental work in this direction.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that spin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions, such as the spin up-down differential transverse flow, can be used to probe the in-medium properties of non-central nuclear forces (spin-orbit coupling and tensor force), but notes that so far there is still no experimental data to compare with the model predictions.

## Method

The authors review theoretical studies using three transport frameworks: the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) model for low-energy reactions, the spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (SIBUU) transport model, and quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) models for intermediate-energy collisions. These models incorporate spin-dependent single-nucleon potentials with density- and isospin-dependent form factors, and simulate the time evolution of nucleon coordinates, momenta, and spin degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The fusion threshold energy in O-16 + O-16 reactions is increased by about a factor of 2 after including the spin-orbit force in TDHF calculations (Uma86, Rei88).
- The strength of the spin-orbit coupling constant W0 is approximately 80-150 MeV fm^5, from fitting properties of light to heavy nuclei (Les07, Zal08, Ben09).
- The spin up-down differential transverse flow shows a maximum slope at a beam energy of about 100 MeV in non-central Au+Au collisions (SIBUU14).
- The spin-orbit coupling constant χw ≈ 0.1 in a modified Skyrme functional reproduces the kink in charge radii of lead isotopes (Sha95).

## Context

This review extends earlier TDHF studies of spin-orbit effects on fusion thresholds (Uma86, Rei88) and recent SIBUU transport model work (SIBUU13, SIBUU14, SIBUU15) that identified spin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on nuclear structure studies of spin-orbit coupling and tensor force effects on shell evolution (Ots05, Ots06) and the isospin dependence of short-range correlations (Hen14). The work aims to stimulate experimental measurements of spin-dependent observables in heavy-ion reactions, which could help constrain the density and isospin dependence of the in-medium spin-orbit interaction and tensor force.
