# Description of $^{178}$Hf$^{m2}$ in the constrained relativistic mean field theory

> The properties of the ground state of $^{178}$Hf and the isomeric state $^{178}$Hf$^{m2}$ are studied within the adiabatic and diabatic constrained relativistic mean field (RMF) approaches. The RMF calculations reproduce well the binding energy and the deformation for the ground state of $^{178}$Hf. Using the ground state single-particle eigenvalues obtained in the present calculation, the lowest excitation configuration with $K^π=16^+$ is found to be $ν(7/2^-[514])^{-1}(9/2^+[624])^{1}$ $π(7/2^+[404])^{-1}(9/2^-[514])^{1}$. Its excitation energy calculated by the RMF theory with time-odd fields taken into account is equal to 2.801 MeV, i.e., close to the $^{178}$Hf$^{m2}$ experimental excitation energy 2.446 MeV. The self-consistent procedure accounting for the time-odd component of the meson fields is the most important aspect of the present calculation.

## Key claim

The constrained relativistic mean field (RMF) calculation with time-odd fields reproduces the excitation energy of the isomeric state $^{178}$Hf$^{m2}$ to within about 0.36 MeV of the experimental value, but only when both self-consistency and the time-odd component of the meson fields are included; without the time-odd fields the calculated excitation energy is 3.579 MeV, significantly higher than the experimental 2.446 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed adiabatic (occupying lowest levels) and diabatic (configuration-fixed) constrained RMF calculations using the PK1 parameter set with a deformed harmonic-oscillator basis of 20 shells. They constructed candidate $K^\pi=16^+$ configurations from the ground-state single-particle spectra, then computed the self-consistent excitation energy for the lowest configuration both with and without the time-odd (nuclear magnetic) potential arising from unpaired nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of $^{178}$Hf: calculated -1434.0 MeV, compared to experimental -1432.8 MeV (Audi 2003).
- Ground state deformation $\beta_2$ of $^{178}$Hf: calculated 0.283, compared to experimental 0.280 (Raman 2001).
- Excitation energy of $^{178}$Hf$^{m2}$ ($K^\pi=16^+$): calculated 2.801 MeV with time-odd fields, compared to experimental 2.446 MeV.
- Excitation energy of the same configuration without time-odd fields: 3.579 MeV; from single-particle sum: 3.954 MeV.
- For the $8^-$ isomer $^{178}$Hf$^{m}$: excitation energy without current 1.552 MeV, with current 1.315 MeV (experimental 1.147 MeV).
- Deformation of the $16^+_1$ isomer: $\beta_2 = 0.30$, similar to the ground state.
- Neutron energy gap between $\nu(9/2^+[624])$ and $\nu(7/2^-[514])$: 2.00 MeV without current, decreasing to 1.25 MeV with current.
- Proton energy gap for the relevant excitation: 1.34 MeV without current, decreasing to 0.54 MeV with current.

## Context

This work extends the constrained RMF approach, previously applied to ground states and low-lying excitations, to the high-$K$ isomeric state $^{178}$Hf$^{m2}$ which has been studied experimentally by Hayes (2002, 2007), Cline (2005), and others for its potential as an energy storage medium (Collins 1999). The configuration found ($\nu(7/2^-[514])^{-1}(9/2^+[624])^{1}$ $\pi(7/2^+[404])^{-1}(9/2^-[514])^{1}$) agrees with the original assignment by Helmer (1968) and later support from Mullins (1997). The key methodological advance is the self-consistent inclusion of time-odd fields, following the approach used for odd-$A$ and odd-odd nuclei by Warrier (1994) and Yao (2006), which proves essential for reproducing the isomer excitation energy. This enables future RMF studies of other high-$K$ isomers in the $A\approx 180$ region.
