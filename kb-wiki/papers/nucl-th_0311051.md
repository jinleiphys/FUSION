# Spectrum of outgoing nucleons in quasielastic neutrino-nucleus interactions

> We have analyzed the consequences of introducing the local density approximation combined with an effective nuclear momentum-dependent potential into the CC quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering. We note that the distribution of the recoil nucleons momenta becomes smooth for low momentum values and the sharp threshold is removed. Our results may be relevant for Sci-Fi detector analysis of K2K experiments. The total amount of observed recoil protons is reduced because some of them remain bound inside the nucleus. We compare theoretical predictions for a probability of such events with the results given by NUX+FLUKA MC simulations.

## Key claim

The introduction of a local density approximation (LDA) combined with a momentum-dependent nuclear potential removes the unphysical sharp threshold in the recoil nucleon momentum spectrum predicted by the simple Fermi gas model, producing a smooth distribution that extends down to zero momentum; however, the effect is only significant for nucleon momenta below approximately 300 MeV, and the total cross section is reduced by only a few percent.

## Method

The authors constructed a Monte Carlo generator for charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (\(\nu_\mu n \rightarrow \mu^- p\)) on oxygen, argon, and iron targets. They implemented three models: (a) a global Fermi gas with a fixed average Fermi momentum, (b) a local density approximation (LDA) where the Fermi momentum varies with nuclear density, and (c) the LDA combined with an analytic momentum-dependent optical potential fitted to prior calculations. The generator sampled the neutrino energy, selected the interaction point in the nucleus based on density profiles from electron scattering, chose the target nucleon momentum from the local Fermi sphere, and solved for the outgoing nucleon momentum using energy conservation including the potential; Pauli blocking was applied, and nucleons unable to escape the nucleus were counted as bound.

## Key numbers

- Average Fermi momenta: \( \langle k_F^O \rangle = 199\) MeV for oxygen, \( \langle k_F^{Ar} \rangle = 217\) MeV for argon, and \( \langle k_F^{Fe} \rangle = 217\) MeV for iron.
- The analytic potential parameters (all in MeV): \(a = 206\), \(b = 582\), \(c = -322\), \(d = 422\), \(e = 289\).
- For a 700 MeV neutrino on oxygen and argon, the differential cross section for ejected nucleons rises almost linearly for momenta below 450 MeV, reaches a maximum at about 600 MeV, and is only slightly changed above 800 MeV compared to the simple Fermi gas.
- The probability that a proton remains bound in an excited nucleus decreases with neutrino energy and becomes flat at about 1 GeV; this probability is about three times larger than predicted by the NUX+FLUKA Monte Carlo.
- For the K2K near-detector neutrino spectrum, the probability that a proton does not leave the nucleus is about 4%.

## Context

This work extends the Fermi gas model of quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering by incorporating a local density approximation and a momentum-dependent optical potential, addressing the unphysical sharp threshold in the recoil nucleon spectrum noted in prior work (e.g., the simple Fermi gas model). The results are relevant for the K2K experiment's Sci-Fi detector, where proton reconstruction requires momenta above 500 MeV, and the authors compare their bound-proton probabilities with the NUX+FLUKA Monte Carlo simulations. The approach enables more realistic modeling of neutrino event generators for oscillation experiments, though it excludes RPA correlations and final-state interactions.
