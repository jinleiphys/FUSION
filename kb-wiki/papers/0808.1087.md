# Extending Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory to describe hard jets in dense QCD media

> An extension to the Soft-Collinear-Effective Theory (SCET) description of hard jets is motivated to include the leading contributions between the propagating partons within the jet with partons radiated from a dense extended medium. The resulting effective Lagrangian, containing both a leading and a power suppressed (in the hard scale $Q^2$) contribution, arises primarily from interactions between the hard collinear modes in the jet with Glauber modes from the medium. In this first attempt, the interactions between the hard jet and soft and collinear partonic modes have been ignored, in an effort to focus solely on the interactions with the Glauber modes. While the effect of such modes on vacuum cross sections are suppressed by powers of the hard scale compared to the terms from the SCET Lagrangian, such sub-leading contributions are enhanced by the extent of the medium and result in measurable corrections. The veracity of the derived Lagrangian is checked by direct comparison with known results from full QCD calculations of two physical observables: the transverse momentum broadening of hard jets in dense media and a reanalysis of the transverse momentum dependent parton distribution function (TMDPDF).

## Key claim
The authors extend Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory (SCET) to include interactions between hard collinear jet partons and Glauber gluons from a dense extended medium, but only as a first attempt that ignores interactions with soft and collinear partonic modes; the resulting power-suppressed contributions become enhanced by the medium extent and yield measurable corrections.

## Method
The authors derive an effective Lagrangian from full QCD by introducing Glauber gluon modes with momentum scaling \(k \sim Q(\lambda^2, \lambda^2, \lambda)\) and integrating out the non-dynamical field \(\xi_n\) using the tree-level equation of motion. They then compute cross sections for two observables, transverse momentum broadening of hard jets in DIS on large nuclei and the transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution function (TMDPDF), and compare with full QCD results to validate the effective theory.

## Key numbers
- The hard scale \(Q\) is defined by the photon virtuality \(q^2 = -Q^2\) in DIS, with the jet invariant mass \(m \sim \lambda Q\) and total forward energy \(E = q^-/\sqrt{2} \sim Q\).
- The nucleon mass \(M_N \simeq 1\) GeV is assumed to scale as \(\mu Q \lesssim \lambda^2 Q\), with \(\mu\) a new scaling variable.
- The nuclear enhancement factor \(A^{1/3}\) (for mass number \(A \gg 1\)) is expressed in powers of \(\lambda\) as \(A^{1/3} \sim \lambda^{-n}\), where \(n\) is observable-dependent.
- In covariant gauge, the Glauber field components scale as \(A^+ \sim \lambda^2 Q\), \(A^\perp \sim \lambda^3 Q\), and \(A^- \sim \lambda^4 Q\); in light-cone gauge, \(A_\perp \sim \lambda Q\) and \(A^- \sim \lambda^2 Q\).

## Context
This work extends SCET (Bauer et al., 2000–2002; Beneke, 2002; Chay, 2002) to include medium-enhanced higher-twist effects, building on prior full-QCD calculations of jet modification in large nuclei (Luo et al., 1992, 1994) and deconfined matter (Guo and Wang, 2000; Majumder, 2004). It enables systematic effective-field-theory treatments of jet broadening and transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions in dense QCD media, with future extensions needed to incorporate radiative energy loss via soft and collinear modes.
