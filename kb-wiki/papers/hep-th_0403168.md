# The Renormalization Group Limit Cycle for the 1/r^2 Potential

> Previous work has shown that if an attractive 1/r^2 potential is regularized at short distances by a spherical square-well potential, renormalization allows multiple solutions for the depth of the square well. The depth can be chosen to be a continuous function of the short-distance cutoff R, but it can also be a log-periodic function of R with finite discontinuities, corresponding to a renormalization group (RG) limit cycle. We consider the regularization with a delta-shell potential. In this case, the coupling constant is uniquely determined to be a log-periodic function of R with infinite discontinuities, and an RG limit cycle is unavoidable. In general, a regularization with an RG limit cycle is selected as the correct renormalization of the 1/r^2 potential by the conditions that the cutoff radius R can be made arbitrarily small and that physical observables are reproduced accurately at all energies much less than hbar^2/mR^2.

# The Renormalization Group Limit Cycle for the 1/r^2 Potential

## Key claim

For an attractive 1/r^2 potential, the regularization that is selected as the correct renormalization is the one that produces a renormalization group (RG) limit cycle, because it allows the cutoff radius R to be made arbitrarily small while still reproducing physical observables accurately at all energies much less than ℏ²/mR². The delta-shell regularization uniquely forces the coupling constant to be a log-periodic function of R with infinite discontinuities, making an RG limit cycle unavoidable, whereas the square-well regularization admits both continuous and log-periodic choices.

## Method

The authors regularize the attractive 1/r^2 potential at short distances using two different regularization potentials: a spherical square-well potential and a spherical delta-shell potential. For each regularization, they impose boundary conditions at the cutoff radius R that match the zero-energy wavefunction of the true 1/r^2 potential, and derive the resulting coupling constant λ(R) as a function of the cutoff. They then solve for the bound-state energy eigenvalues as functions of R and compare the spectra for continuous versus log-periodic choices of the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling factor for the RG limit cycle is λ = e^{π/ν}, where ν is the positive parameter in the potential coefficient −(1/4 + ν²)ℏ²/(2mr²).
- For ν = 2, the coupling constant λ(R) for the delta-shell regularization is given by λ(R) = 1/2 − ν cot[ν ln(R/r₀)], which is log-periodic with infinite discontinuities at R values given by R^(M) = (e^{−π/ν})^M r₀, where M is an integer.
- The asymptotic spectrum of shallow bound states near threshold follows E_n → −(ℏ²κ_*²/m) (e^{−2π/ν})^(n−n_*), where κ_* is related to r₀ by κ_* e^{nπ/ν} = (2/r₀) e^{arg Γ(1+iν)/ν}.
- For the square-well regularization with continuous λ(R), the coupling constant reaches −∞ at a finite cutoff radius R^(M) = (e^{−π/ν})^M r₀, imposing a lower bound on R.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the 1/r² potential by Beane et al. (2000) and Bawin and Coon (2003), who used square-well regularization and found that both continuous and log-periodic coupling constants are possible. The authors show that the delta-shell regularization uniquely selects the RG limit cycle, and they identify the criterion that the cutoff radius must be arbitrarily small as the condition that selects the limit-cycle regularization as the correct renormalization. This connects to the broader study of RG limit cycles, including the Efimov effect for three identical bosons with large scattering length (Efimov, 1970), where the discrete scaling factor is λ ≈ 22.7, and to effective field theory treatments by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998).
