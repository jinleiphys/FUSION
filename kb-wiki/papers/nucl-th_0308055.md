# Neutrino-nucleus reactions and effective field theory

> Effetive field theory is believed to provide a useful framework for describing low-energy nuclear phenomena in a model-independent fashion. I give here a brief account of the basic features of this approach, some of its latest developments, and examples of actual calculations carried out in this framework.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) provides a model-independent framework for low-energy neutrino-nucleus reactions, but the actual calculations require a hybrid approach (EFT*) that uses SNPA wave functions and fixes unknown low-energy constants from tritium beta-decay data, with the resulting cross sections showing stability against cutoff variation only within the range 500-800 MeV.

## Method

The author describes three calculational frameworks: the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) using phenomenological potentials and exchange currents; nuclear chiral perturbation theory (χPT) in the Weinberg scheme, where irreducible diagrams define an effective potential and transition operators; and a hybrid EFT (EFT*) that uses SNPA wave functions with EFT-derived transition operators, fixing the unknown low-energy constant \(\hat{d}_R\) by fitting the tritium beta-decay rate. The method is applied to calculate Gamow-Teller matrix elements for pp fusion, Hep fusion, and neutrino-deuteron reactions, using a Gaussian cutoff function \(\exp(-\vec{p}^2/\Lambda^2)\) to restrict intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- pp fusion threshold S-factor: \(S_{pp}(0) = 3.94 \times (1 \pm 0.005) \times 10^{-25}\) MeV·b, with only ~0.1% variation against changes in cutoff \(\Lambda\)
- Hep fusion threshold S-factor: \(S_{Hep}(0) = (8.6 \pm 1.3) \times 10^{-20}\) keV·b, for \(\Lambda\) = 500-800 MeV
- The optimal value of the low-energy constant \(L_{1A}\) from Butler, Chen and Kong's EFT calculation: \(L_{1A} = 5.6\) fm\(^3\), consistent with naturalness bound \(|L_{1A}| \le 6\) fm\(^3\)
- Neutrino-deuteron cross sections from EFT* agree within 1% with SNPA results of Nakamura et al. for all four reactions in eq. (1) for solar neutrino energies \(E_\nu \lesssim 20\) MeV

## Context

This paper extends the program of applying effective field theory to low-energy nuclear weak processes, building on Weinberg's (1990) proposal for nuclear χPT and the power divergence subtraction scheme of Kaplan, Savage and Wise. The EFT* approach correlates observables across neighboring nuclei (pp fusion, Hep fusion, ν-d reactions, and tritium beta-decay) using the same low-energy constant \(\hat{d}_R\), enabling parameter-free predictions. The results confirm the reliability of SNPA calculations by Schiavilla et al. (1998) and Marcucci et al. for solar fusion reactions, and establish that the neutrino-deuteron cross sections used in SNO experiments are robust at the 1% precision level, supporting the evidence for neutrino oscillations.
