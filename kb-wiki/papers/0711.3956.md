# Nuclear Effects in Neutrino Structure Functions

> We discuss calculation of nuclear corrections to the structure functions for the deep-inelastic scattering of muon and (anti)neutrino. Our approach includes a QCD description of the nucleon structure functions as well as the treatment of Fermi motion and nuclear binding, off-shell correction to bound nucleon structure functions, nuclear pion excess and nuclear shadowing. We emphasize the dependence of nuclear effects on the type and C-parity of (anti)neutrino structure functions. We also examine the interplay between different nuclear effects in the Adler and the Gross-Llewellyn-Smith sum rules for nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors present a consistent model of nuclear corrections to deep-inelastic neutrino structure functions that includes Fermi motion, nuclear binding, off-shell effects, pion excess, and shadowing; they emphasize that the size and shape of these corrections depend on the C-parity of the neutrino structure function combination, and that the Adler sum rule provides a constraint linking the off-shell and coherent nuclear corrections.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear structure functions for charged-lepton and (anti)neutrino deep-inelastic scattering using an impulse approximation with realistic nuclear spectral functions, adding an off-shell correction to bound nucleon structure functions extracted from fits to charged-lepton EMC data. They include a nuclear pion correction via convolution with the pion light-cone distribution and a coherent nuclear correction (shadowing/antishadowing) using Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering theory with phenomenological effective amplitudes. For neutrino scattering, they separate isoscalar and isovector contributions and treat the C-even and C-odd combinations of structure functions separately, with the coherent correction requiring four effective amplitudes (isoscalar/isovector × C-parity) compared to one for charged leptons.

## Key numbers

- The global fit to charged-lepton data on structure function ratios for targets from D to Pb (about 560 data points, Q² ≥ 1 GeV²) yields χ²/d.o.f. = 459/556.
- The nuclear pion correction to F₂ reaches about 5% at maximum near x ∼ 0.1 for heavy nuclei.
- The nuclear correction to the Gross-Llewellyn-Smith integral δS_GLS is negative and of order 2% at low Q², decreasing progressively with Q², with similar results for different targets.
- The Adler sum rule for a nucleus gives S_A = (Z-N)/A = β, verified to be preserved by Fermi motion and binding corrections and by the pion correction, while off-shell and coherent corrections must cancel.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous charged-lepton analysis (Kulagin and Petti, 2004) to neutrino scattering, emphasizing the dependence on C-parity and the role of isovector contributions. It provides a framework for interpreting neutrino oscillation experiments (such as MINERvA) that require precision cross sections on nuclear targets, and it shows that nuclear corrections must be accounted for in extractions of the strong coupling constant α_s from the Gross-Llewellyn-Smith sum rule. The approach uses the same spectral functions and off-shell parametrization as the charged-lepton analysis, with the coherent correction constrained by the Adler sum rule.
