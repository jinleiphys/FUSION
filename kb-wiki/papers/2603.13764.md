# New nonet scalar mesons and glueballs: the mass spectra and the production yields in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We propose a new nonet scheme for scalar mesons consisting of $f_{0}(980)$, $a_{0}(980)$, $K_{0}^{\ast}(1430)$, and $f_{0}(1770)$, regarding them as quark-antiquark $P$-wave states classified by $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ light flavor symmetry. We investigate their production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and estimate their yields by applying the statistical model and the quark coalescence model. In contrast to these scalar mesons, we regard $f_{0}(1500)$ as a glueball that is not included in the proposed nonet. We quantify the production yields of $f_{0}(1500)$ by accounting for various internal structures of this state, and compare their yields with those of the new nonet scalar mesons. Given the production yields of these hadrons, our results strongly suggests that $f_{0}(1500)$ is a glueball.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new nonet classification for scalar mesons, $f_{0}(980)$, $a_{0}(980)$, $K_{0}^{\ast}(1430)$, and $f_{0}(1770)$, as quark-antiquark $P$-wave states, while identifying $f_{0}(1500)$ as a glueball; the production yields in heavy ion collisions are estimated to support this assignment, but the identification of $f_{0}(1500)$ as a glueball is conditional on comparing yields from the statistical model, coalescence model, and S-matrix formulation, with the conclusion that the glueball interpretation is "strongly suggested" rather than proven.

## Method

The authors applied the statistical model and the quark coalescence model to estimate production yields of scalar mesons and glueballs in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC (2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV). For the coalescence model, they constructed wave functions using a harmonic oscillator potential with frequency parameters $\omega_h$ calibrated to reproduce yields of reference hadrons ($\rho(770)$, $\phi(1020)$, $\Omega(1672)$), and introduced effective frequencies $\omega_s' \approx 2\omega_s$ and $\omega_s'' \approx 3\omega_s$ for the $P$-wave scalar mesons $K_{0}^{\ast}(1430)$ and $f_{0}(1770)$ respectively. They also performed an S-matrix analysis using phase shifts from $\pi\pi$-$K\bar{K}$ and $\pi K$ scattering to compare thermal yields with the other two models.

## Key numbers

- $f_{0}(1500)$ mass: $1522 \pm 25$ MeV, decay width: $108 \pm 33$ MeV (from Particle Data Group)
- Statistical model yields for $f_{0}(1500)$: 0.239 at RHIC, 0.403 at LHC (both 2.76 and 5.02 TeV)
- Coalescence model yields for $f_{0}(1500)$ as glueball ($gg$, $S$-wave): not explicitly given in the truncated text for all scenarios, but S-matrix yield ratio relative to statistical model is 0.57 for $f_{0}(1500)$ at RHIC
- Statistical model yields for $f_{0}(980)$: 3.44 at RHIC, 6.55 at LHC; for $a_{0}(980)$: 10.3 at RHIC, 19.6 at LHC; for $K_{0}^{\ast}(1430)$: 0.738 at RHIC, 1.18 at LHC; for $f_{0}(1770)$: 0.078 at RHIC, 0.125 at LHC
- Coalescence model yields for $f_{0}(980)$ ($\bar{n}n$, $P$-wave): 2.10 (Sc.1) and 2.09 (Sc.2) at RHIC; 4.20 (Sc.1) and 4.14 (Sc.2) at LHC
- Coalescence model yields for $K_{0}^{\ast}(1430)$ ($\bar{s}n$, $P$-wave): 0.93 (Sc.1) and 0.87 (Sc.2) at RHIC; 1.68 (Sc.1) and 1.46 (Sc.2) at LHC
- Constituent gluon mass set to $m_g = 750$ MeV; harmonic oscillator frequency for gluons $\omega_g$: 310 MeV (Sc.1) and 340 MeV (Sc.2) at RHIC; 310 MeV (Sc.1) and 380 MeV (Sc.2) at LHC
- S-matrix yield ratios relative to statistical model: 0.82 for $f_{0}(980)$, 0.57 for $f_{0}(1500)$, 0.79 for $K_{0}^{\ast}(1430)$ at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the study of scalar meson classification by proposing a new nonet scheme that excludes the broad low-mass states $f_{0}(500)$ and $K_{0}^{\ast}(700)$ (traditionally considered part of the scalar nonet in works such as Jaffe (1976) and Pelaez (2015)), and instead assigns $f_{0}(980)$, $a_{0}(980)$, $K_{0}^{\ast}(1430)$, and $f_{0}(1770)$ as $P$-wave quark-antiquark states. The identification of $f_{0}(1500)$ as a glueball builds on lattice QCD studies (Morningstar 1999, Athenodorou 2020) and experimental reviews (Crede 2008). The production yield analysis in heavy ion collisions follows the framework developed for exotic hadrons in ExHIC (2010, 2011, 2017) and uses the statistical model parameters from Andronic (2005) and Stachel (2013). The S-matrix comparison employs phase shifts from coupled-channel analyses (ccm for $\pi\pi$-$K\bar{K}$, and kappa for $\pi K$ scattering). This work enables future experimental tests at RHIC and LHC (particularly ALICE) to distinguish between the glueball and conventional meson interpretations of $f_{0}(1500)$ through yield measurements.
