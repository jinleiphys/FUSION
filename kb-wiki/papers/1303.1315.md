# Dynamics of the inner crust of neutron stars: hydrodynamics, elasticity and collective modes

> We present calculations of the hydrodynamics of the inner crust of neutron stars, where a superfluid neutron liquid coexists with a lattice of neutron-rich nuclei. The long-wavelength collective oscillations are combinations of phonons in the lattice and phonons in the superfluid neutrons. Velocities of collective modes are calculated from information about effective nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from Lattimer and Swesty's microscopic calculations based on a compressible liquid drop picture of the atomic nuclei and the surrounding neutrons.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the velocities of long-wavelength collective modes in the inner crust of neutron stars, where a superfluid neutron liquid coexists with a lattice of neutron-rich nuclei, using effective nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from Lattimer and Swesty's microscopic calculations. The authors show that the mode velocities are strongly affected by the magnitude of the neutron superfluid density, which may be as low as one-tenth of the density of neutrons outside nuclei based on band structure calculations.

## Method

The authors develop a two-fluid hydrodynamic model for the inner crust, treating the superfluid neutrons and the ionic lattice (with entrained normal neutrons) as coupled components. They linearize the equations of motion including conservation laws, the Josephson relation for the superfluid phase, and the momentum flux density tensor with elastic contributions, then solve for the velocities of transverse and longitudinal collective modes. The key input parameters (derivatives of chemical potentials with respect to densities, shear elastic constant, and neutron superfluid density) are evaluated from Lattimer and Swesty's compressible liquid drop model and from Chamel's band structure calculations.

## Key numbers

- Transverse mode velocity: \( v_t^2 = S / \rho^n \), where \( \rho^n = m(n_p + n_n^n) \) is the total normal mass density and \( S \) is the effective shear modulus.
- Shear modulus from Coulomb lattice: \( S_{\rm Coul} = 0.1194 \, n_i Z^2 e^2 / a_i \), with screening correction \( S_{\rm screen} = -0.010 Z^{2/3} S^{\rm Coul} \) for ultrarelativistic electrons.
- Neutron superfluid density may be as low as \( n_n^s \approx n^{\rm out} / 10 \) or less at matter densities 1/10 to 1/3 of nuclear matter density (0.155 fm\(^{-3}\)).
- Table I provides numerical values of \( E_{nn}, E_{np}, E_{pp} \) across densities from \( n = 2.512 \times 10^{-4} \) fm\(^{-3}\) to \( 1.259 \times 10^{-3} \) fm\(^{-3}\); for example, at \( n = 2.512 \times 10^{-4} \) fm\(^{-3}\), \( E_{nn} = 3.937 \times 10^4 \) MeV fm\(^3\), \( E_{np} = -4.281 \times 10^4 \) MeV fm\(^3\), \( E_{pp} = 1.061 \times 10^5 \) MeV fm\(^3\).
- Melting temperature estimate: \( T_m \approx (1/100) (Z/40)^{5/3} (\mu_e / k_B) \), with \( \Gamma_m \approx 175 \).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculation of crustal modes by Epstein and the general hydrodynamic treatment of Carter, as well as the macroscopic entrainment formulation by Carter, Chamel, and Haensel. It builds on the general formulation of crustal mode properties by Pethick, Chamel, and Reddy (2010) and provides a more detailed parameter evaluation using Lattimer and Swesty's microscopic calculations. The results enable more accurate predictions for torsional oscillations observed in giant X-ray flare afterglows (Duncan, Watts, Andersson) and for heat transport mechanisms (Aguilera), and they highlight the need for further investigation of the neutron superfluid density and its reduction due to lattice vibrations via the Debye-Waller factor.
