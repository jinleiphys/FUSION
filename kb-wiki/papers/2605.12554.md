# Spin dynamics and polarization in relativistic systems: recent developments

> We review recent theoretical and experimental developments in spin dynamics and polarization phenomena in relativistic systems, with a particular focus on heavy-ion collisions. The large angular momentum and magnetic field generated in non-central collisions induce vorticity in the quark-gluon plasma, leading to observable spin polarization of emitted hadrons. We discuss the theoretical foundations of spin polarization arising from spin-vorticity coupling, including formulations based on relativistic hydrodynamics, kinetic theory, and quantum statistical approaches such as the Zubarev density operator. A central theme of the review is the role of pseudo-gauge freedom and its implications for defining energy-momentum and spin tensors, which can influence theoretical predictions of polarization observables. We further examine different formulations of spin hydrodynamics, emphasizing the impact of gradient expansions, spin chemical potential, and entropy-current analysis on the structure of the theory and associated transport coefficients. In addition, we discuss the recent developments in heavy flavor spin dynamics within the framework of rotational Brownian motion, where spin degrees of freedom undergo stochastic evolution due to interactions with the medium. This framework provides a complementary perspective on spin relaxation and diffusion by incorporating the effects of strong initial magnetic fields and establishes connections between spin polarization and the initial geometry through the definition of polarization harmonics. This review provides a comprehensive overview of relativistic spin hydrodynamics as well as non-equilibrium spin dynamics, and outlines future directions toward a consistent and predictive description of spin phenomena in strongly interacting matter.

## Key claim

This review argues that theoretical predictions of spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions depend on the choice of pseudo-gauge (the definition of energy-momentum and spin tensors), and that this dependence is not resolved by conservation laws alone; the canonical spin tensor is the only one that satisfies the correct \(SO(3)\) angular momentum algebra, while other choices such as GLW and HW do not.

## Method

The authors review theoretical frameworks for spin dynamics in relativistic systems, focusing on heavy-ion collisions. They examine formulations based on relativistic hydrodynamics, kinetic theory, and the Zubarev density operator approach, with particular attention to pseudo-gauge freedom and its consequences for defining energy-momentum and spin tensors. They also discuss heavy-flavor spin dynamics modeled as rotational Brownian motion, where spin degrees of freedom undergo stochastic evolution due to interactions with the medium.

## Key numbers

* The total angular momentum in non-central heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be on the order of \(10^{3\sim5}\hbar\).
* The decay parameter \(\alpha_{\Lambda}\) for \(\Lambda \rightarrow p + \pi^{-}\) is \(\sim 0.732\).
* The global \(\Lambda\) polarization decreases with increasing center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\), as shown by STAR and ALICE data.
* The relation used to estimate vorticity from polarization is \(\omega = \frac{1}{T} \left( P_{\Lambda} + P_{\bar{\Lambda}} \right)\), where \(T\) is the temperature.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions initiated by the STAR collaboration (STAR, 2007, 2017, 2018) and ALICE (ALICE, 2019), which established that the quark-gluon plasma is the most vortical fluid ever observed. It addresses the theoretical challenge that spin-thermal vorticity coupling models (e.g., Karpenko, 2016; Li, 2017; Vitiuk, 2019; Sun, 2017) can explain global polarization but fail to describe the azimuthal-angle dependence of longitudinal polarization. The review enables future work toward a consistent spin-hydrodynamic framework that resolves pseudo-gauge ambiguities and can simultaneously describe both global and local polarization data.
