# Multi-phase transport model predictions of isobaric collisions with nuclear structures from density functional theory

> Isobaric $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions were performed at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider in 2018. Using the "a multi-phase transport" model with nuclear structures calculated by the density functional theory (DFT), we make predictions for the charged hadron multiplicity distributions and elliptic azimuthal anisotropies in these collisions. Emphases are put on the relative differences between the two collision systems that can decisively discriminate DFT nuclear distributions from the commonly used Woods-Saxon densities.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the ratio of charged hadron multiplicity distributions in Ru+Ru to Zr+Zr collisions will curve upward at high multiplicity when using density functional theory (DFT) nuclear densities, opposite to the downward trend predicted by the commonly used Woods-Saxon (WS) densities; this difference arises because DFT gives Ru a slightly smaller mass radius than Zr, while WS gives Ru a larger radius.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the "a multi-phase transport" (AMPT) model, version 2.26t7, with initial nuclear density distributions from DFT calculations of Ru and Zr proton and neutron densities. They simulated 80 million minimum-bias events each for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions with DFT densities, plus 35 million and 32 million events with two variants of Woods-Saxon densities (WS-R_Q using charge radii, and WS-R_DFT using effective mass radii from DFT). The model includes Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions, parton elastic scatterings with cross section 3 mb, quark coalescence hadronization, and hadronic cascade terminated at 30 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Average midrapidity charged hadron multiplicities (|eta|<0.5, pT>0.2 GeV/c): 65.97 for Ru+Ru and 65.15 for Zr+Zr from AMPT with DFT densities.
- Woods-Saxon thickness parameter a = 0.46 fm used for both Ru and Zr.
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.33 and Debye screening mass mu_D = 2.265/fm, giving total parton scattering cross section sigma = 3 mb.
- DFT charge root-mean-square radii: 4.327 fm for Ru, 4.271 fm for Zr; DFT mass root-mean-square radii: 4.343 fm for Ru, 4.366 fm for Zr.
- WS charge radius parameters R_0: 5.085 fm for Ru, 5.020 fm for Zr.
- Two-component model fit parameters: n_pp approximately 2.1 and x approximately 9% for both systems.
- Average ratio of opposite-sign to same-sign pion pair invariant mass distributions in minimum-bias collisions: 1.013 +/- 0.007.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier DFT nuclear structure calculations (Xu et al., 2017) by implementing those densities into the full AMPT transport model to make testable predictions for the 2018 RHIC isobar run. It contrasts with prior calculations using Woods-Saxon densities (Deng et al., 2016, 2018) that assumed charge radii equal mass radii. The predictions enable experimental discrimination between DFT and Woods-Saxon nuclear density scenarios using the high-statistics (approximately 2 billion events per system) STAR isobar data, which is essential for establishing correct initial conditions before searching for the chiral magnetic effect.
