# "Naked" Cronin effect in A+A collisions from SPS to RHIC

> Baseline computations of the Cronin effect in nuclear collisions at energies spanning the SPS and the RHIC accelerators are performed in the Glauber-Eikonal model, which ascribes the effect to initial-state incoherent multiple parton scatterings. The model accounts very well for the mid-rapidity Cronin effect in hadron-nucleus collisions in the 27-200 GeV center of mass energy range, and will be extended to nucleus-nucleus collisions. The computations are performed under the assumption that the partons do not interact with the medium produced in the collision. Therefore, medium effects such as energy loss in a Quark-Gluon Plasma may be detected and measured as deviations from the presented baseline computation of the "naked" Cronin effect.

## Key claim

The Glauber-Eikonal model provides a baseline computation of the Cronin effect in nucleus-nucleus collisions under the assumption that partons do not interact with the produced medium, so that medium effects such as energy loss in a Quark-Gluon Plasma may be detected as deviations from this "naked" Cronin effect.

## Method

The author computes the Cronin ratio for hadron production in nucleus-nucleus collisions using the Glauber-Eikonal model, which sums multiple 2-to-2 partonic scatterings in collinearly factorized pQCD with unitarization via an exponential factor representing the probability of no further semihard scatterings. The model parameters (infrared regulator p0 and K factor) are fitted to proton-proton data at each energy and then applied to nuclear collisions without additional free parameters. The computation is extended from hadron-nucleus to nucleus-nucleus collisions by allowing partons from both nuclei to rescatter on the other nucleus.

## Key numbers

* The intrinsic transverse momentum used to describe p+p spectra at intermediate pT is ⟨kT²⟩ = 0.52 GeV².
* The infrared regulator p0 is extracted as 0.7 GeV at √s = 27.4 GeV and 1.0 GeV at √s = 200 GeV, with a fitted logarithmic energy dependence: p0 = 0.151 + 0.100 log(s/1 GeV) ± 10% GeV.
* The K factor is approximately 1 and insensitive to energy in the 27-200 GeV range.
* The model describes the Cronin effect in hadron-nucleus collisions over the center-of-mass energy range √s = 27-200 GeV.
* For Au+Au collisions at √s = 62 GeV, comparison with PHOBOS charged hadron data and PHENIX π0 data indicates that hadron quenching starts at around 40% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-Eikonal model computations of Accardi and Gyulassy (2004) from hadron-nucleus collisions to nucleus-nucleus collisions, providing a baseline for detecting jet quenching. The model successfully describes the Cronin effect on pion production in p+A collisions at Fermilab (√s=27.4 GeV) and d+Au collisions at RHIC (√s=200 GeV), but fails for baryon production, where alternative explanations based on parton recombination by Hwa are more successful. The computed baseline enables systematic study of the onset and magnitude of hadron quenching across SPS and RHIC energies, with planned cross-checks using NA60 data for In+In collisions at √s=17 GeV and the multiple-target setup for p+A collisions.
