# Physical features of strength of isoscalar pairing interaction determined by relation between double charge change and double pair transfer

> A new method has been proposed to determine the strength of the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interaction applicable to many nuclei. The principle is the equivalence between the double charge change and the double transfer of like-particle pair, and a constraint is derived to the effective interactions used in approximations. This method was applied to the quasiparticle random-phase approximation for determining that interaction strength. In this paper, detail of this method is explained thoroughly, and applications are made to nuclei of several instances of the double-$β$ decays. The systematics of the strengths determined for those nuclei is understood in terms of a midshell effect. The effect of the new interaction strength is examined in two examples of the Gamow-Teller strength function with comparisons with the experimental data. The nuclear matrix elements of the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay are also calculated.

## Key claim

The strength of the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interaction can be determined by enforcing the equality between two different QRPA expressions for the double-charge-change transition matrix element, under the condition that other interactions are assumed to be established.

## Method

The author derived a constraint from the theoretical equivalence between the double-charge-change transition matrix element calculated via the proton-neutron QRPA and the same matrix element calculated via the like-particle QRPA. This constraint was applied to determine the isoscalar pairing interaction strength in the quasiparticle random-phase approximation for several nuclei involved in double-beta decay. The method uses the equality of the two QRPA expressions as a condition to adjust the isoscalar pairing interaction strength, while the isovector pairing strength is approximated from the average of proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing strengths.

## Key numbers

* The isoscalar-to-isovector ratio of pairing interaction strengths from shell-model interactions is 1.65 for the sd shell and 1.63 for the fp shell (from Ref. Ber10).
* The isoscalar-to-isovector ratio of the contact density-dependent interaction is 1.9 with a cutoff energy of 20 MeV (from Ref. Tan14).
* The masses of odd-odd N=Z nuclei are deviated from the mass systematics of even-even nuclei by 2.5 to 7.5 MeV (from Ref. Mac00a).
* The isoscalar-to-isovector ratio of pairing interaction strengths from Ref. Sat01 is approximately 1.65 at the beginning of the sd shell and approximately 1.4 in the f7/2 subshell.
* The isoscalar-to-isovector ratio of pairing interaction strengths from Ref. Pov98 is 1.56.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on isoscalar pairing correlations, which have shown conflicting results depending on whether one examines mass systematics along the N=Z line (Mac00a) or perpendicular to it (Sat01). The method provides a way to determine the isoscalar pairing interaction strength for N≠Z nuclei relevant to neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments, where experimental data for discussing isoscalar pairing correlations are much less abundant than for N=Z nuclei. This enables more reliable calculations of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay, which are sensitive to the isoscalar pairing interaction strength as shown by Vog86 and Civ87.
