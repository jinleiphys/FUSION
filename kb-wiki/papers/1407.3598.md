# Exciting flavored bound states

> We study ground and radial excitations of flavor singlet and flavored pseudoscalar mesons within the framework of the rainbow-ladder truncation using an infrared massive and finite interaction in agreement with recent results for the gluon-dressing function from lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger equations. Whereas the ground-state masses and decay constants of the light mesons as well as charmonia are well described, we confirm previous observations that this truncation is inadequate to provide realistic predictions for the spectrum of excited and exotic states. Moreover, we find a complex conjugate pair of eigenvalues for the excited $D_{(s)}$ mesons, which indicates a non-Hermiticity of the interaction kernel in the case of heavy-light systems and the present truncation. Nevertheless, limiting ourselves to the leading contributions of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, we find a reasonable description of the charmed ground states and their respective decay constants.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation with an infrared massive interaction that matches lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger equation results for the gluon dressing function can describe ground-state pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants, but it is inadequate for realistic predictions of excited and exotic states; furthermore, a complex conjugate pair of eigenvalues appears for excited $D_{(s)}$ mesons, indicating non-Hermiticity of the interaction kernel in heavy-light systems within this truncation.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) on the complex momentum plane and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for pseudoscalar mesons within the rainbow-ladder truncation, using an effective interaction composed of an infrared Gaussian term and a perturbative-QCD tail. The BSE was solved via Arnoldi factorization with Chebyshev expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and the DSE solutions on the complex plane were obtained using Cauchy's theorem with a contour parametrization bounded by a parabola.

## Context

This work extends the study of the Qin et al. (2011) interaction model to flavored pseudoscalar mesons including charm, building on earlier DSE/BSE studies of heavy-light mesons by Nguyen et al. (2010) and Souchlas (2010) which encountered difficulties with decay constants. The results confirm that the rainbow-ladder truncation, while successful for equal-mass systems like light mesons (Maris, 1997) and charmonia (Maris, 2005; Krassnigg, 2004; Blank, 2011), requires beyond-rainbow-ladder contributions for accurate description of heavy-light excited states, motivating future work with dressed quark-gluon vertices satisfying Ward-Takahashi identities as pursued by Chang (2009), Fischer (2009), and Qin (2013).
