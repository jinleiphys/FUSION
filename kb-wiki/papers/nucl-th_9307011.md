# Space-time Structure of Initial Parton Production in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The space and time evolution of initial parton production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is investigated within the framework of perturbative QCD which includes both initial and final state radiations. Uncertainty principle is used to relate the life time of a radiating parton to its virtuality and momentum. The interaction time of each hard or semihard parton scattering is also taken into account. For central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, most of the partons are found to be produced within 0.5 fm/c after the total overlap of the two colliding nuclei. The local momentum distribution is approximately isotropical at that time. The implication on how to treat correctly the the secondary scattering in an ultimate parton cascading model is also discussed.

## Key claim

For central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, most partons are produced within 0.5 fm/c after complete nuclear overlap, but this result depends on the specific choices of the infrared cutoff p₀ = 2 GeV/c and the minimum virtuality μ₀ = 0.5 GeV/c, and does not include the soft (nonperturbative) component of particle production.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING Monte Carlo model, which employs PYTHIA subroutines to simulate hard parton scatterings and their associated initial and final state QCD radiation via the Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions and Sudakov form factors. The lifetime of each virtual parton was estimated from the uncertainty principle as Δt ≈ q₀/q², where q₀ is the parton energy and q² its virtuality, and the interaction time for each hard scattering was similarly estimated. Parton production vertices and trajectories were then tracked in space and time assuming classical free-streaming without rescatterings, using a three-parameter Wood-Saxon nuclear density distribution boosted to the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, most partons are produced within 0.5 fm/c after total overlap of the two colliding nuclei.
- About 2/3 of the total number of partons are produced between t = -0.5 and 0.5 fm/c.
- About 200 semihard scatterings happen between t = -0.1 and 0.1 fm/c.
- About 2/3 of the total number of partons are produced in initial and final state radiations.
- The spatial spread of partons from initial state radiation is Δz ≈ 0.25 fm at √s = 200 GeV.
- The time for achieving local isotropy in momentum space via free-streaming in a central cell |z| < 0.5 fm is t_iso ≈ 0.7 fm/c.
- For Au+Au collisions, hard scatterings with p_T > 2 GeV/c produce about 13% quarks and anti-quarks; without radiations this fraction rises to 18%.
- In pp collisions, about 28% of partons produced via hard scatterings without radiations are quarks and anti-quarks.
- The infrared cutoff for semihard scatterings is p₀ = 2 GeV/c; the minimum virtuality for final state radiation is μ₀ = 0.5 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of minijet production in heavy ion collisions by Kajantie, Landshoff, and Lindfors (1987) and Blaizot and Mueller (1987), and provides the space-time foundation for parton cascading models such as those by Geiger, Kavoussi, and Müller (1991) and Rancotta (1992). The results enable more realistic initial conditions for rate-equation approaches to thermalization, as in the work of Biró, Dooms, Müller, and Wang (1993), and highlight that the small initial quark fraction (13%) implies chemical equilibration may take longer than the phase transition time, potentially preventing formation of a fully equilibrated quark-gluon plasma.
