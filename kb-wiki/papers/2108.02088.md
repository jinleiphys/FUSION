# Soft-photon radiative corrections to the $e^- p \to e^- p l^- l^+$ process

> We calculate the leading-order QED radiative corrections to the process $e^- p\rightarrow e^- p l^- l^+ $ in the soft-photon approximation, in two different energy regimes which are of relevance to extract nucleon structure information. In the low-energy region, this process is studied to better constrain the hadronic corrections to precision muonic Hydrogen spectroscopy. In the high-energy region, the beam-spin asymmetry for double virtual Compton scattering allows to directly access the Generalized Parton Distributions. We find that the soft-photon radiative corrections have a large impact on the cross sections and are therefore of paramount importance to extract the nucleon structure information from this process. For the forward-backward asymmetry the radiative corrections are found to affect the asymmetry only around or below the 1\% level, whereas the beam-spin asymmetry is not affected at all in the soft-photon approximation, which makes them gold-plated observables to extract nucleon structure information in both the low- and high-energy regimes.

## Key claim

The soft-photon radiative corrections have a large impact on the unpolarized cross sections for the $e^- p \to e^- p l^- l^+$ process, but the forward-backward asymmetry is affected only at or below the 1% level, and the beam-spin asymmetry is not affected at all within the soft-photon approximation, making these asymmetries "gold-plated observables" for extracting nucleon structure information.

## Method

The authors calculated the leading-order QED radiative corrections to the $e^- p \to e^- p l^- l^+$ process using the soft-photon approximation, distinguishing three gauge-invariant types of corrections: one contributing to the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) case, one to the time-like Compton scattering (TCS) case, and a new type specific to the double-virtual case. They modeled the double virtual Compton amplitude in two energy regimes: at low energies using a Born amplitude plus a $\Delta(1232)$-pole contribution, and at high energies using generalized parton distributions (GPDs) via the QCD factorization theorem. The virtual radiative corrections and soft-photon bremsstrahlung were calculated analytically, with the infrared divergences shown to cancel.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of radiative corrections for the VCS process by Vanderhaeghen (2000) and more recently for the TCS process by Heller, Keil, and Vanderhaeghen (2018, 2019, 2020). The low-energy regime is motivated by the need to better constrain the hadronic corrections to precision muonic Hydrogen spectroscopy, following the proton charge radius puzzle from Pohl (2010). The high-energy regime enables access to Generalized Parton Distributions through double deeply virtual Compton scattering (DDVCS), which is relevant for the JLab 12 GeV upgrade and the future Electron-Ion Collider. The paper enables empirical determination of the low-energy nucleon structure constant $\bar{T}_1^{\prime\prime}(0)$ through the forward-backward asymmetry in the $e^- p \to e^- p l^- l^+$ process, as proposed by Pauk (2020).
