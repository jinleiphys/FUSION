# Single- and coupled-channel radial inverse scattering with supersymmetric transformations

> The present status of the coupled-channel inverse-scattering method with supersymmetric transformations is reviewed. We first revisit in a pedagogical way the single-channel case, where the supersymmetric approach is shown to provide a complete solution to the inverse-scattering problem. A special emphasis is put on the differences between conservative and non-conservative transformations. In particular, we show that for the zero initial potential, a non-conservative transformation is always equivalent to a pair of conservative transformations. These single-channel results are illustrated on the inversion of the neutron-proton triplet eigenphase shifts for the S and D waves. We then summarize and extend our previous works on the coupled-channel case and stress remaining difficulties and open questions. We mostly concentrate on two-channel examples to illustrate general principles while keeping mathematics as simple as possible. In particular, we discuss the difference between the equal-threshold and different-threshold problems. For equal thresholds, conservative transformations can provide non-diagonal Jost and scattering matrices. Iterations of such transformations are shown to lead to practical algorithms for inversion. A convenient technique where the mixing parameter is fitted independently of the eigenphases is developed with iterations of pairs of conjugate transformations and applied to the neutron-proton triplet S-D scattering matrix, for which exactly-solvable matrix potential models are constructed. For different thresholds, conservative transformations do not seem to be able to provide a non-trivial coupling between channels. In contrast, a single non-conservative transformation can generate coupled-channel potentials starting from the zero potential and is a promising first step towards a full solution to the coupled-channel inverse problem with threshold differences.

# Single- and coupled-channel radial inverse scattering with supersymmetric transformations

## Key claim

The paper reviews and extends the supersymmetric transformation approach to inverse scattering for coupled-channel radial Schrödinger equations, showing that while conservative transformations provide a complete solution for single-channel problems and for coupled-channel problems with equal thresholds, they cannot generate non-trivial channel coupling when thresholds differ; only non-conservative transformations can circumvent this limitation, though a full practical inversion algorithm for the different-threshold case remains an open problem.

## Method

The authors first revisit single-channel supersymmetric transformations, distinguishing conservative transformations (which preserve boundary behaviour at the origin) from non-conservative transformations (which modify it), and show that for a zero initial potential, a non-conservative transformation is equivalent to a pair of conservative transformations. They then extend this framework to coupled-channel systems, focusing on two-channel examples, and develop iterative algorithms using chains of supersymmetric transformations to construct matrix potentials from scattering data. For equal thresholds, they develop a technique where the mixing parameter is fitted independently of the eigenphases using iterations of pairs of conjugate transformations, and apply this to neutron-proton triplet S-D scattering data. For different thresholds, they analyze non-conservative transformations that generate coupled-channel potentials from the zero potential.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton triplet eigenphase shifts for S and D waves are used as a test case for single-channel inversion, with the S-wave scattering length and effective range from the effective-range expansion providing low-energy constraints.
- For the two-channel equal-threshold case, exactly-solvable matrix potential models are constructed for the neutron-proton triplet S-D scattering matrix, reproducing the mixing parameter and eigenphases from modern data.
- In the different-threshold two-channel case, non-conservative transformations generate exactly-solvable schematic models of atom-atom interactions, including models for the interplay of a magnetically-induced Feshbach resonance with a bound state or virtual state near the elastic-scattering threshold.

## Context

This work extends the supersymmetric (Darboux) transformation approach to inverse scattering, which is equivalent to the Gel'fand-Levitan and Marchenko integral equation methods for separable kernels (Nieto 1984, Sukumar 1985) but offers simpler iterative implementation. The paper builds on prior single-channel inversion work by Sparenberg (1997), Samsonov (2003), and Baye (2004), and on coupled-channel studies by Amado, Cannata and Dedonder (1988) who established that conservative transformations cannot modify coupling between channels with different thresholds. The results enable construction of exactly-solvable coupled-channel potential models for testing numerical methods and for phenomenological applications such as neutron-proton scattering and cold-atom Feshbach resonances.
