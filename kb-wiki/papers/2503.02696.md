# Particle number projection on a spatial domain

> The formalism of particle number on a spatial domain for mean field wave functions with pairing is revisited to account for the case where finite dimensional basis are used. The formulas differ from the ones previously used in the literature. It is shown that the present formalism has the right limit in the well known case of zero pairing whereas the other formalism do not satisfy this basic requirement. By using a simple one-dimensional model we illustrate the differences in the results for particle number distribution probability obtained with the two methods.

# Particle number projection on a spatial domain

## Key claim

The correct formalism for particle number projection on a spatial domain using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions requires accounting for finite basis effects, because the symmetry operator acting on a finite basis produces states that cannot be expressed as linear combinations of the original basis elements. The formulas used in a series of recent papers (Scamps 2013, PhysRevC.100.024612, PhysRevC.100.034612, PhysRevC.101.014310, PhysRevC.103.054602, PhysRevC.104.054601, PhysRevLett.126.142502) are incorrect for finite bases and do not reproduce the correct zero-pairing limit, whereas the present formalism does.

## Method

The author derives the overlap between HFB states under the operator exp(i phi N_D) using the formalism of Robledo (1994, 2022) for bases not closed under the symmetry transformation. The key matrix element R_nm = <phi_n|exp(i phi N_D)|phi_m> is expressed in terms of the eigenvalues theta_l of the domain projection matrix (Theta_D)_nm, giving eigenvalues r_l(phi) = 1 + (exp(i phi) - 1) theta_l. The overlap is computed as sqrt(det A det R) with A = U_0^T (R^T)^{-1} U_1^* + V_0^T R V_1^*, and a Pfaffian formula is also provided to handle sign ambiguities. The formalism is tested on a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator model with 40 basis states and BCS-type occupancies, comparing results for various domain boundaries x_0.

## Key numbers

- The basis contains N_B = 40 elements (20 spatial harmonic oscillator states with spin degeneracy 2), with oscillator length b = 1.
- For the simple BCS model with n_0 = 10.5 and sigma = 1, the average total particle number is 20.
- For the two-fragment DVR model, the total number of particles is 14.4 and the average number in the right fragment is 5.4.
- For x_0 = -10 (domain covering essentially all space), all eigenvalues theta_l = 1 and the correct and incorrect formalisms coincide.
- For x_0 = -5, the correct particle number sumrule gives 19.98 while the incorrect gives 21.08.
- For x_0 = -2, the correct sumrule gives 15.47 and the incorrect gives 21.51.
- For x_0 = 0 in the zero-pairing limit, the correct a(N) peaks at N = 10 while the incorrect peaks at N = 20.
- For the two-fragment case with x_0 = 0, the correct a(N) peaks at N = 6 (close to the average 5.4) while the incorrect distribution differs substantially.

## Context

This work corrects the formalism for particle number projection on a spatial domain that was introduced by Simenel (2009) for Slater determinants and extended by Scamps (2013) to BCS wave functions using Pfaffian techniques. The author shows that the formulas used in a series of subsequent papers (PhysRevC.100.024612 through PhysRevLett.126.142502) are incorrect because they assume the basis is closed under the symmetry operation, which fails for finite bases. The correct approach follows the formalism of Robledo (1994, 2022) originally developed for angular momentum projection. The results call for revision of similar angular momentum projection on a domain formalism used in Marevic (2021) and PhysRevC.104.054601. The work also connects to concepts from quantum information and condensed matter physics (Klich 2006) regarding entanglement between fragment and environment.
